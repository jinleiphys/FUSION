# The 3-flavor A-scaled Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We review the so called A-scaled Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model both in the vacuum and at finite temperature and density in the case of three flavors. Starting from QCD and integrating out the gluons, we show how to reintroduce some of their effects in order to take into account the trace (or scale) anomaly of QCD. The axial U_A(1) anomaly is also present in the model in order to discuss the eta' particle. In the present work we treat the pseudoscalar and scalar sectors, pointing out the defects related to the omission of the vector particles. We use our model to study the restoration of chiral symmetry as a function of temperature, the thermodynamical functions and the equation of state, and we survey the mixing between the scalar isoscalar particles and the glueball.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the A-scaled Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for three flavors, showing how to reintroduce gluon effects to account for the QCD trace anomaly while also incorporating the axial U_A(1) anomaly to discuss the eta' particle; however, the treatment is limited to the pseudoscalar and scalar sectors, explicitly omitting vector particles, which the author notes as a defect.

## Method

The author starts from QCD, integrates out the gluon fields, and expands the resulting gluon functional in powers of quark currents, retaining only the two-point gluon correlation function (the Global Color Model approximation). Fierz transformations are then applied in color, flavor, and Dirac spaces to rewrite the effective four-quark interaction in terms of mesonic (quark-antiquark) and diquark channels. The model is studied in vacuum and at finite temperature and density using the mean-field approximation and the Schwinger-Dyson approach, with the scale anomaly implemented via a scaling factor that modifies the coupling constant and the cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar nonet masses (pions, kaons, eta, eta') are discussed: in the chiral limit, pions, kaons, and eta have vanishing mass as Goldstone bosons, while the eta' acquires its high mass from the axial U_A(1) anomaly.
- The strange quark mass is heavier than the up and down quark masses, leading to a greater explicit chiral symmetry breaking and thus heavier masses for kaons and eta' compared to pions.
- The color Fierz identity yields a factor of 1/2 (1 - 1/N_c) for the color-singlet channel and 1/(2N_c) for the color-antitriplet diquark channel, with N_c = 3.

## Context

This work extends the earlier A-scaled NJL model developed by Jaminon and Van den Bossche (1995) for SU(3) scaled effective Lagrangians at finite temperature, and by Cugnon, Jaminon, and Van den Bossche (1996) for phase transitions and thermodynamics. It contrasts with the full Global Color Model (GCM) of Roberts and collaborators, which retains non-local gluon propagators and confinement, whereas the NJL model uses a local four-fermion interaction and lacks confinement. The paper enables future studies of chiral symmetry restoration, the equation of state, and scalar-glueball mixing in a three-flavor framework, while pointing out the need to include vector mesons for a complete description.
