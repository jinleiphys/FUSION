# Mass splittings of the baryon decuplet and antidecuplet with the second-order flavor symmetry breakings within a chiral soliton model

> We revisit the mass splittings of SU(3) baryons, taking into account the second-order effects of isospin and SU(3) flavor symmetry breakings within the framework of a chiral soliton model. The masses of the baryon decuplet turn out to be improved, compared to those with the first-order corrections. The mass of the N^* as a member of the baryon antidecuplet is obtained as M_{n^*}=1687 MeV, which is in agreement with the recent experimental data. The pion-nucleon sigma term becomes Σ_{πN}=(50.5\pm 5.4) MeV.

## Key claim

Including second-order corrections of isospin and SU(3) flavor symmetry breakings within a chiral soliton model improves the masses of the baryon decuplet compared to first-order corrections alone, and yields a mass for the non-strange antidecuplet member N* of 1687 MeV that agrees with recent experimental data, though the authors note that the nature of this N*(1685) resonance is not yet settled in consensus.

## Method

The authors used the collective Hamiltonian of the SU(3) chiral soliton model, including both first-order and second-order perturbative corrections from isospin and SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking, plus electromagnetic mass corrections. They performed a least-square fit to determine the model parameters (moments of inertia I₁, I₂, and the dynamical parameters α, β, γ) using as input the experimental masses of the whole baryon octet, the Ω⁻, and the Θ⁺ from the LEPS experiment. The second-order corrections were computed perturbatively from the mixing of SU(3) representations (octet, decuplet, antidecuplet, 27-plet, 35-plet, and 35-bar-plet).

## Key numbers

- Mass of the non-strange antidecuplet member n*: M_{n*} = 1687 MeV (with full second-order corrections)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: Σ_{πN} = (50.5 ± 5.4) MeV (full second-order result), compared to (36.4 ± 3.9) MeV from first-order only
- Second moment of inertia: I₂ = 0.431 ± 0.001 fm (full result), compared to 0.420 ± 0.006 fm (first-order)
- Morpurgo quantity T = M_{Ξ*-} - ½(M_{Σ*-} + M_{Ω-}) = (2.04 ± 0.16) MeV, which vanishes at first order and is nonzero only with second-order corrections
- Mass of Θ⁺ used as input: 1524 ± 0.005 MeV (from LEPS experiment Nakano 2008)
- Mass of N*(1685) from GRAAL experiment used for comparison: 1685 ± 0.012 MeV (Kuznetsov 2007)
- Decuplet masses reproduced: Δ masses range from 1235.4 to 1243.9 MeV (full), Σ* masses from 1383.8 to 1390.8 MeV, Ξ* masses 1529.5 and 1533.7 MeV
- Antidecuplet predictions: Σ_{10-bar} masses from 1844.0 to 1852.1 MeV; Ξ_{3/2} masses from 1993.7 to 2003.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the previous first-order analysis by Yang and Kim (2010) and the earlier studies by Diakonov (1997) and Ellis (2004) of baryon mass splittings within chiral soliton models. Unlike those earlier works, the authors include both isospin symmetry breaking and second-order SU(3) breaking, allowing all dynamical parameters to be fixed unambiguously from experimental octet masses, Ω⁻, and Θ⁺. The results enable improved predictions for the baryon antidecuplet masses, particularly the N*(1685) candidate, and provide a more consistent test of the Morpurgo mass formula. The predicted masses of the Ξ_{3/2} states (around 1994-2004 MeV) are larger than the debated NA49 data (Alt 2003) and also larger than the range 1900-1970 MeV derived by Goeke (2009).
