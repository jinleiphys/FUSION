# Influence of nuclear physics inputs and astrophysical conditions on Th/U chronometer

> The productions of thorium and uranium are key ingredients in $r$-process nucleo-cosmochronology. With the combination of improved nuclear and stellar data, we have made detailed investigations on the $r$-process abundance pattern in the very metal-poor halo stars based on the classical $r$-process approach. It is found that the results are almost independent of specified simulations to observed abundances. The influence from nuclear mass uncertainties on Th/U chronometer can approach 2 Gyr. Moreover, the ages of the metal-poor stars HE 1523-0901, CS 31082-001, and BD +17$^\circ$3248 are determined as $11.8\pm 3.7$, $13.5\pm 2.9$, and $10.9 \pm 2.9$ Gyr, respectively. The results can serve as an independent check for age estimate of the universe.

## Key claim
The paper finds that age estimates from the Th/U chronometer are almost independent of which specific set of observed stellar abundances is used to calibrate the r-process simulation (fit I, II, or III), but are highly sensitive to the choice of nuclear mass model, with differences between mass models reaching up to 6.3 Gyr. The abstract's claim of "almost independent" is hedged by the finding that this independence holds only for a given mass model, and that the overall uncertainty is dominated by nuclear physics inputs rather than astrophysical conditions.

## Method
The authors employed the classical r-process model with a multi-component neutron irradiation approach (16 components with neutron densities from 10^20 to 3 x 10^27 cm^-3) to simulate r-process abundances in very metal-poor halo stars. They performed three types of fits to observed abundances: fit I (elements with Z=38-82), fit II (elements with Z=56-82), and fit III (solar r-process isotopic abundances with A=125-209). Eight different nuclear mass models were tested (DZ10, DZ28, ETFSI-2, ETFSI-Q, FRDM, HFB-17, RMF, KTUY), and beta-decay rates from FRDM+QRPA and ETFSI+CQRPA were compared.

## Key numbers
- The influence from nuclear mass uncertainties on the Th/U chronometer can approach 2 Gyr.
- Age of HE 1523-0901: 11.8 +/- 3.7 Gyr (with 1.6 Gyr from nuclear mass models and 3.3 Gyr from observation)
- Age of CS 31082-001: 13.5 +/- 2.9 Gyr
- Age of BD +17 degrees 3248: 10.9 +/- 2.9 Gyr
- Differences in log10(Th/U) due to beta-decay rate variations can be as large as 0.25, corresponding to an age variation of about 5.5 Gyr
- For a fixed fit, the deviation in age using different mass models can be as large as 5.9 Gyr (fit I), 5.0 Gyr (fit II), and 6.3 Gyr (fit III)
- The rms deviation of one-neutron separation energies (Z=26-100) across all mass models is within 0.6 MeV; DZ28 best reproduces experimental S_n values, while RMF has the largest deviation at 0.59 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous r-process chronometer studies by Cowan (1999), Goriely (2001), Schatz (2002), and Kratz (2007) that used solar r-process abundances to predict zero-decay abundances of Th and U. The authors improve upon these earlier approaches by directly simulating the observed abundances in very metal-poor stars (HE 1523-0901, CS 31082-001, BD +17 degrees 3248) rather than relying solely on solar system data. The results enable independent cross-checks of cosmic age estimates from WMAP data and provide constraints on nuclear mass models by requiring that predicted Th/U ratios yield Galactic ages between 4.6 Gyr (solar system age) and 13.7 Gyr (universe age from WMAP).
