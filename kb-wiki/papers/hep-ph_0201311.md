# Higher Twist Effects in Nuclei

> This talk serves as an introduction to higher twist effects in nuclei. We want to discuss how perturbative QCD can be applied to processes involving heavy nuclei by taking into account multiple scattering.

# Higher Twist Effects in Nuclei

## Key claim

Higher twist corrections to perturbative QCD processes in nuclei can be systematically organized by their nuclear-size enhancement, with only those matrix elements that scale as additional powers of A^{1/3} dominating over ordinary power corrections; this rearrangement of the twist expansion into powers of λ²A^{1/3}/Q² allows pQCD to describe multiple scattering in heavy nuclei while omitting subleading twist terms.

## Method

The author reviews the factorization theorem for deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes, then extends the twist expansion to nuclear targets by classifying twist-4 matrix elements according to whether they involve additional parton pairs (quark-gluon or gluon-gluon correlators) that are sensitive to the nuclear size. Double scattering contributions are separated into double-hard and soft-hard components based on whether the pole integration for the gluon momentum fraction yields a large (hard) or small (soft) value, with the interference between these residues explaining the absence of nuclear enhancement at low transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The non-perturbative scale λ is of order Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV, defining the expansion parameter λ/Q for the twist expansion
- Nuclear-enhanced twist-4 matrix elements scale as λ² A^{1/3} / Q², where A is the mass number of the nucleus
- For Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening, the ratio ⟨q_⊥²⟩/⟨q_⊥⁰⟩ = (4π²α_s/3) A^{1/3} λ², showing explicit A^{1/3} dependence
- Model-dependent parameterization of double-hard matrix elements: T^{DH}_{ab}(ξ,x) = C A^{4/3} f_a(ξ) f_b(x), with C of order Λ_QCD
- Model-dependent parameterization of soft-hard matrix elements: T^{SH}_{ab}(ξ) = λ² A^{4/3} f_a(ξ), with λ² of order Λ_QCD
- RHIC calculations shown for 100 GeV Au + 250 GeV p collisions at Q = 5 GeV and q_⊥ = 4 GeV

## Context

This talk extends the work of Luo, Qiu and Sterman (1990s) on nuclear-enhanced higher twist effects in pQCD, providing a pedagogical framework for understanding multiple scattering in e+A, p+A, and A+A collisions. The formalism explains the Cronin effect (Cronin 1975, E772 1990) as nuclear enhancement of production cross sections at large transverse momentum, and accounts for transverse momentum broadening observed in Fermilab data. The approach enables predictions for RHIC observables and connects to medium modification of fragmentation functions (Guo and Wang 2000) and parton distributions (Qiu, LHC era), though factorization for A+A collisions is limited to twist-4 accuracy due to breakdown beyond that order (Dicus, Fried, Teplitz).
