# On the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron II

> The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron is applied to the calculation of the cross sections of the low-energy radiative neutron-proton capture n + p -> D + gamma and the low-energy two-proton fusion p + p -> D + e^+ + nu_e. For the low-energy radiative neutron-proton capture n + p -> D + gamma our result agrees well with both the experimental data and the potential model prediction. In the case of the two-proton fusion the cross section obtained is 2.9 times as much as that given by the potential approach. The obtained result is discussed in connection with the solar neutrino problem. ERRATUM: We correct the value of the cross section for pp-fusion p + p -> D + e^+ + nu_e. We find a contribution to the astrophysical factor δS_pp(0) = 2.01 x 10^{-25} (1 +/- 0.30) MeV b which is obtained only due to weak and strong low-energy interactions of the protons and the deuteron when neglecting the Coulomb repulsion between protons in the intermediate state. Minor misprints are corrected.

## Key claim

The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron, when applied to low-energy two-proton fusion (p + p → D + e⁺ + νₑ), yields a cross section 2.9 times larger than the potential model prediction; however, the erratum specifies that the contribution to the astrophysical factor δS_pp(0) = 2.01 × 10⁻²⁵ (1 ± 0.30) MeV b is obtained only when neglecting Coulomb repulsion between protons in the intermediate state.

## Method

The authors revise their earlier relativistic field theory model of the deuteron, treating it as a bound state of proton and neutron via a one-nucleon loop approximation inspired by the Nambu–Jona–Lasinio approach, with a cutoff Λ_D = 1/r_D = 45.688 MeV. They compute one-nucleon loop diagrams for the radiative neutron-proton capture n + p → D + γ and the weak two-proton fusion p + p → D + e⁺ + νₑ, fixing ambiguities from virtual momentum shifts by imposing electromagnetic gauge invariance on individual loops and gauge invariance under transformations of the deuteron field. The coupling constants g_V and g_T are determined from the experimental electric quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.286 fm² and the condition κ_D = 0, yielding g_V = 11.3 and g_T/g_V = −√(3/8).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the deuteron: ε_D = 2.273 MeV, obtained from the model with g_T/g_V = −√(3/8), matching the experimental value 2.225 MeV to within 2%.
- Cutoff scale: Λ_D = 1/r_D = 45.688 MeV, derived from the effective radius r_D = 4.319 fm.
- Coupling constant g_V = 11.3, fixed from the experimental electric quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.286 fm² and the deuteron mass M_D ≈ 1876 MeV.
- Astrophysical factor contribution from pp-fusion (erratum): δS_pp(0) = 2.01 × 10⁻²⁵ (1 ± 0.30) MeV b, obtained when neglecting Coulomb repulsion between protons in the intermediate state.
- The cross section for two-proton fusion is 2.9 times larger than the potential model prediction.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron introduced by the same authors (Ivanov et al., 1996) by revising the treatment of one-nucleon loop ambiguities through gauge invariance requirements, correcting the earlier relation between g_V and Q_D by a factor of 2/3. The result for radiative neutron-proton capture agrees with experimental data and potential model predictions, while the enhanced two-proton fusion cross section is discussed in connection with the solar neutrino problem. The model enables further low-energy calculations of deuteron interactions but is limited by the lack of a perturbative justification for the one-nucleon loop approximation, unlike the large-N expansion in the Nambu–Jona–Lasinio quark model.
