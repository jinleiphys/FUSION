# On the role of $J/ψ$ production in electron-ion collisions

> Within the framework of non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) effective field theory, we study the leptoproduction of $J/ψ$ at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD for both unpolarized and polarized electron-ion collisions. We demonstrate that the $J/ψ$-tagged deep inelastic scattering in the future Electron-Ion Collider can be served as a golden channel for the reasons including constraining NRQCD long distance matrix elements, probing the nuclear gluon distribution functions, as well as investigating the gluon helicity distribution inside a longitudinal polarized proton.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that $J/\psi$-tagged deep inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider can serve as a golden channel for constraining NRQCD long distance matrix elements, probing nuclear gluon distribution functions, and investigating gluon helicity distributions, but only if the large theoretical uncertainties from different LDME sets are resolved by future data.

## Method
The authors performed a complete next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation in perturbative QCD for $J/\psi$ leptoproduction within the NRQCD effective field theory framework, including contributions from color-singlet and color-octet Fock states up to $\mathcal{O}(v^4)$. They computed analytical short-distance coefficients for both unpolarized and longitudinally polarized electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions, using dimensional regularization and a mixed renormalization scheme (on-shell for heavy quark mass and field, $\overline{\text{MS}}$ for strong coupling). The cross sections were evaluated at a center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s}=100$ GeV with charm quark mass $m=1.55$ GeV and $n_f=3$ light flavors.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass set to $m = 1.55$ GeV; number of light quark flavors $n_f = 3$
- Factorization and renormalization scales chosen as $\mu_F = \mu_R = \sqrt{Q^2 + 4m^2}$
- Center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s} = 100$ GeV for all numerical results
- Five sets of NRQCD LDMEs compared: Bodwin et al. (2014) gives $\langle \mathcal{O}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle = 9.9 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$; Butenschoen et al. (2011) gives $3.04 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$; Chao et al. (2012) gives $8.9 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$; Gong et al. (2012) gives $9.7 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$; Brambilla et al. (2022) gives $-0.63 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$
- The gluon channel contributes 100% to 110% of the NLO differential cross section for $eA$ collisions across all $x_B$ values considered
- Expected integrated luminosity: 10 fb$^{-1}/A$ for $eA$ collisions and 100 fb$^{-1}$ for $ep$ collisions at the EIC

## Context
This work extends NLO calculations of $J/\psi$ production from earlier studies in electron-positron, proton-proton, and electron-proton collisions (Bodwin 2014, Butenschoen 2011, Chao 2012, Gong 2012, Brambilla 2022) to the specific kinematics of the future Electron-Ion Collider. It addresses the unresolved universality problem of NRQCD LDMEs and the $J/\psi$ polarization puzzle by proposing $J/\psi$-tagged DIS as a clean gluon-dominated observable, in contrast to dijet production (Boer 2016, Xing 2012) which suffers from quark channel contamination. The results enable future global analyses of LDMEs and nuclear PDFs using EIC data, and provide the first NLO predictions for double longitudinal spin asymmetry in polarized $ep$ collisions.
