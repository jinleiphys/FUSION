# Relativistic three-particle quantization condition for nondegenerate scalars

> The formalism relating the relativistic three-particle infinite-volume scattering amplitude to the finite-volume spectrum has been developed thus far only for identical or degenerate particles. We provide the generalization to the case of three nondegenerate scalar particles with arbitrary masses. A key quantity in this formalism is the quantization condition, which relates the spectrum to an intermediate K matrix. We derive three versions of this quantization condition, each a natural generalization of the corresponding results for identical particles. In each case we also determine the integral equations relating the intermediate K matrix to the three-particle scattering amplitude, $\mathcal M_3$. The version that is likely to be most practical involves a single Lorentz-invariant intermediate K matrix, $\widetilde{\mathcal K}_{\rm df,3}$. The other versions involve a matrix of K matrices, with elements distinguished by the choice of which initial and final particles are the spectators. Our approach should allow a straightforward generalization of the relativistic approach to all other three-particle systems of interest.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the relativistic three-particle quantization condition from identical or degenerate scalars to three nondegenerate scalar particles with arbitrary masses, deriving three versions of the quantization condition under the assumption that exponentially suppressed finite-volume corrections can be neglected and that the energy range allows only one three-particle state to go on shell.

## Method

The authors work within a relativistic effective field theory with three real scalar fields having a \(\mathbb{Z}_2^3\) symmetry and use time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) to express the finite-volume correlator as a geometric series involving on-shell two- and three-particle K matrices. They then derive three forms of the quantization condition: an asymmetric non-Lorentz-invariant form from TOPT, an asymmetric Lorentz-invariant form using Feynman diagrams, and a symmetric Lorentz-invariant form obtained by algebraic symmetrization of the asymmetric results.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic restriction for the energy in the center-of-mass frame is \(0 < E^* < 3 m_1 + m_2 + m_3\), where \(m_1 \le m_2 \le m_3\) are the particle masses.
- For the \(D_s^+ D^0 \pi^-\) system, the formalism is valid for \(0 < E^* < 2 M(D_0) + M(K_0) = 4224\) MeV, with the lower limit of interest at \(E^* \approx M(D_s) + M(D^0) + M(\pi^-) = 3973\) MeV.
- The finite-volume corrections are exponentially suppressed, typically of the form \(\sim \exp(-m_i L)\), where \(L\) is the box size and \(m_i\) are the particle masses.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic formalism developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) for identical scalars and by Briceno, Hansen, and Sharpe (2017) for nonidentical but degenerate scalars, as well as the isospin generalization by Hansen, Sharpe, and collaborators (2020). The only prior nondegenerate three-particle formalism is the very recent work by Pang and collaborators (2020), which studied the \(DDK\) system using nonrelativistic effective field theory assuming only s-wave two-particle interactions. The present results enable future applications to systems such as \(D_s^+ D^0 \pi^-\) and \(D_s^+ D^0 D^+\), and should allow straightforward generalization to all other three-particle systems of interest.
