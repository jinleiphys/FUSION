# Microscopic calculation of proton and alpha-particle inelastic scattering to study the excited states of $^{6}$He and $^{8}$He

> Elastic and inelastic cross sections of the $p+{}^6\textrm{He}$, $p+{}^8\textrm{He}$, and $α+{}^8\textrm{He}$ reactions were investigated using the Melbourne $g$-matrix folding approach with the theoretical densities of ${}^6\textrm{He}$ and ${}^8\textrm{He}$ obtained by a microscopic structure model of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). Microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations of the $p+{}^6\textrm{He}$ and $p+{}^8\textrm{He}$ reactions were performed to investigate transition properties of the ${}^6\textrm{He}(2^+_1)$ and ${}^8\textrm{He}(2^+_1)$ states. The MCC+AMD calculations reproduced elastic cross sections of the $p+{}^6\textrm{He}$ reaction at $E=40.9$ MeV/A and of the $p+{}^8\textrm{He}$ reaction at $E=32.5$ and 72 MeV/A, which have both been measured by inverse kinematics experiments. For $p+{}^6\textrm{He}$ inelastic scattering to the $2^+_1$ state, the calculated result was in reasonable agreement with the $(p,p')$ data at $E=24.5$ and 40.9 MeV/A and supported the AMD prediction of the neutron transition matrix element $M_n=7.9$ fm$^2$. For the $p+{}^8\textrm{He}$ inelastic scattering to ${}^8\textrm{He}(2^+_1)$, the MCC+AMD calculation overshot the $(p,p')$ cross sections at $E=72$ MeV/A by a factor of three. According to a phenomenological model analysis, $M_n$ values in the range of 4--6 fm$^2$ were favored to reproduce the ${}^8\textrm{He}(2^+_1)$ cross sections of the $p+{}^8\textrm{He}$ reaction at $E=72$ MeV/A. For the $α+{}^8\textrm{He}$ reaction, the MCC+AMD calculation reproduced the elastic cross sections at $E=26$ MeV/A. Theoretical predictions of the ($p,p'$) and ($α,α'$) cross sections to the ${}^8\textrm{He}(0^+_2)$, ${}^8\textrm{He}(1^-_1)$, ${}^8\textrm{He}(2^+_3)$ and ${}^8\textrm{He}(3^-_1)$ states are also given.

## Key claim
The Melbourne g-matrix folding approach combined with AMD densities reproduces elastic scattering data for p+6He and p+8He reactions, but for p+8He inelastic scattering to the 2+1 state the calculation overshoots the measured cross sections at 72 MeV/A by a factor of three, indicating that the AMD-predicted neutron transition matrix element of 7.6 fm² is too large and values in the range 4–6 fm² are favored.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations using the Melbourne g-matrix folding model, which folds an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from the Bonn B potential with diagonal and transition densities of 6He and 8He obtained from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations. For alpha scattering, the nucleon-nucleus potentials were folded with a Gaussian alpha density. The calculations included the 0+1 ground state and 2+1 excited state in a two-channel coupling scheme, with additional DWBA calculations for comparison.

## Key numbers
- For 6He(2+1), the AMD prediction of the neutron transition matrix element is Mn = 7.9 fm², supported by reasonable agreement with (p,p') data at E = 24.5 and 40.9 MeV/A
- For 8He(2+1), the MCC+AMD calculation overshot the (p,p') cross sections at E = 72 MeV/A by a factor of three
- Phenomenological model analysis favored Mn values in the range 4–6 fm² to reproduce the 8He(2+1) cross sections at E = 72 MeV/A
- The MCC+AMD calculation reproduced elastic cross sections for p+6He at E = 40.9 MeV/A, for p+8He at E = 32.5 and 72 MeV/A, and for alpha+8He at E = 26 MeV/A
- AMDm56 calculation gave 6He point-proton radius Rp = 1.92 fm and point-neutron radius Rn = 2.48 fm; for 8He, Rp = 1.92 fm and Rn = 2.63 fm
- AMDm56 gave 6He(2+1) excitation energy Ex = 1.4 MeV and 8He(2+1) Ex = 4.1 MeV

## Context
This work extends the Melbourne g-matrix folding approach, previously tested for stable nuclei and applied to p+6He and p+8He reactions by the same group (Kanada-En'yo 2019, 2020), to investigate transition properties of neutron-rich helium isotopes. The results contradict the AMD prediction for the 8He(2+1) neutron transition strength, consistent with earlier JLM reaction calculations using NCSM densities (Lapoux 2015) that also overshot the data by a factor of five. The findings enable future extraction of neutron transition matrix elements for unstable nuclei from (p,p') and (alpha,alpha') data, and provide theoretical predictions for inelastic scattering to higher excited states of 8He including the 0+2, 1-1, 2+3, and 3-1 states.
