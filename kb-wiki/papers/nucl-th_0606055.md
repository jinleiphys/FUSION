# Kinetic Description of a Finite Temperature Meson Gas

> A transport model based on the mean free path approach for an interacting meson system at finite temperatures is discussed. A transition to a quark gluon plasma is included within the framework of the bag model. We discuss some calculations for a pure meson gas where the Hagedorn limiting temperature is reproduced when including the experimentally observed resonances. Next we include the possibility for a QGP formation based on the MIT bag model. The results obtained compare very well with Lattice QCD calculations. In particular the cross over to the QGP at about 175 MeV temperature is nicely reproduced.

## Key claim

The transport model reproduces the Hagedorn limiting temperature for a pure meson gas and yields a crossover to a quark-gluon plasma at about 175 MeV temperature, in qualitative agreement with Lattice QCD calculations, but the model's equation of state for the QGP phase gives a ratio of energy density to T⁴ of about 12, which is larger than the LQCD value of about 8 due to the neglect of attractive interactions among partons.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic Boltzmann equation (ReB) approach based on the mean free path method, where for each time step the closest pair of particles in phase space is identified and a collision probability is computed from the local density, relative velocity, and elementary cross sections. Two systems of 300+300 pions are boosted and collided in a box with periodic boundary conditions; resonances up to the f₂(2340) are included with parametrized cross sections and experimental lifetimes, and a QGP is introduced via the MIT bag model when local energy density exceeds the critical value of 3B = 0.71 GeV/fm³, with partons then evolved using the same transport method.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn limiting temperature for a pure meson gas with resonances: about 140 MeV.
- Crossover temperature to QGP: about 175 MeV (for N_f = 2).
- Critical energy density for QGP formation: ε_c = 3B = 0.71 GeV/fm³, using B^(1/4) = 0.206 GeV.
- For a 2-flavor QGP, the ratio ε/T⁴ reaches about 12, compared to the LQCD value of about 8.
- Entropy per particle of 3.6 recovered for an ideal Bose gas with zero chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends transport approaches such as the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) and Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) methods to relativistic energies and finite-temperature meson gases, including the Hagedorn resonance scenario. The results are compared to Lattice QCD calculations by Karsch (2002) and the MIT bag model by Wong (1994) and Las (1995). The model enables future application to out-of-equilibrium relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the authors note that a generalization to three flavors and inclusion of attractive parton interactions are in progress.
