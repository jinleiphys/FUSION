# $ϕN$ photoproduction coupled with the $KΛ^*$ channel

> We present in this talk a recent investigation on $ϕ$ photoproduction, emphasizing the rescattering effects of the $KΛ^*$ channel near the threshold region. We discuss the results of the differential cross section and the angular distributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the bump-like structure observed by the LEPS collaboration near photon energy \(E_\gamma \approx 2.3\) GeV in \(\phi\) photoproduction can be explained by the rescattering effects of the \(K\Lambda(1520)\) channel, contrary to an earlier coupled-channel analysis by Ozaki et al. (2009) which found such effects insufficient. The claim is hedged by noting that only the imaginary part of the rescattering amplitude has been computed so far, with the full calculation still under investigation.

## Method

The authors constructed the \(\phi\) photoproduction amplitude by combining Pomeron-exchange, pseudoscalar meson-exchange (\(\pi\) and \(\eta\)), and seven different rescattering amplitudes, with the \(K\Lambda(1520)\) channel found to be dominant near threshold. They used a Blankenbecler-Sugar equation to describe the rescattering, but for this work they extracted only the imaginary part via the two-body unitarity relation (Landau-Cutkosky rule), requiring only on-mass-shell amplitudes for the \(\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda^*\) and \(K^+ \Lambda^* \to \phi p\) processes.

## Key numbers

- The bump-like structure in the total cross section for \(\phi\) photoproduction appears near photon energy \(E_\gamma \approx 2.3\) GeV, as measured by the LEPS collaboration (Mibe et al., 2005).
- The \(K\Lambda(1520)\) rescattering effects start to arise from threshold drastically till around 2 GeV, then fall off fast.
- The angular distribution \(2\pi W(\phi-\Phi)\) was compared with LEPS data at photon energy \(E_\gamma = 2.07\) GeV, where the Pomeron does not contribute.
- The LEPS angular distribution data were taken at forward angles (\(-0.2 < t + |t|_{\mathrm{min}}\)) in two energy regions: \(1.97 < E_\gamma < 2.17\) GeV and \(2.17 < E_\gamma < 2.37\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(\phi\) photoproduction beyond the conventional Pomeron-exchange picture, which alone cannot explain the bump-like structure near threshold seen by the LEPS collaboration (Mibe et al., 2005). It contradicts the earlier coupled-channel analysis by Ozaki et al. (2009), who used the \(K\)-matrix method and found that \(\gamma N \to K\Lambda^*(1520)\) and \(K\Lambda^* \to \phi N\) kernels were insufficient. The present results enable future full coupled-channel calculations including the real part of the rescattering amplitude, which the authors state is under investigation.
