# Method of moments for a relativistic single-component gas

> We derive the equations of motion for all the irreducible moments of the single-particle distribution function. We find that these moment equations of motion are highly coupled, with the dynamics of lower-rank moments always being coupled to those of a higher-rank, leading to an endless tower of equations. Considering a massless gas in Bjorken flow, we investigate how this hierarchy of differential equations can be properly truncated and solved.

## Key claim
The paper derives general equations of motion for all irreducible moments of the single-particle distribution function in a relativistic gas, showing that the hierarchy of moment equations is infinite and highly coupled. The authors then investigate how this infinite tower can be truncated and solved for a massless gas in Bjorken flow, but the convergence properties of this truncation are only explored within the relaxation time approximation.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation and expand the non-equilibrium distribution function using a complete and orthogonal basis of irreducible momenta and orthogonal polynomials. They derive a general equation of motion for an irreducible moment of arbitrary rank ℓ, which couples lower-rank moments to higher-rank ones. For a massless gas undergoing Bjorken flow, they simplify these equations using the symmetries of the flow and the relaxation time approximation for the collision term, obtaining a closed set of differential equations for the moments. They then solve these equations numerically using the Runge-Kutta algorithm after imposing a truncation at a maximum rank ℓ_max.

## Key numbers
- The expansion rate in Bjorken flow is θ = 1/τ, and the shear tensor components are σ_μν = (0, 1/(3τ), 1/(3τ), -2τ/3).
- The shear relaxation time is taken as τ_R = τ_π = 5η/(ε_0 + P_0).
- The equilibrium value of the irreducible moments for a massless gas is ϱ_{n,ℓ}^{eq} = g e^α (n+1)!/(2π²) T^{n+2} δ_{ℓ0}, with g = 1.

## Context
This work extends the method of moments originally developed by Grad (1949) for non-relativistic gases and later extended to relativistic systems by Israel and Stewart (1979). It generalizes the DNMR approach (Denicol, Niemi, Molnar, Rischke, 2012) by deriving equations for all irreducible moments rather than only up to rank 4 as done previously (de Brito, Denicol, 2023). The results are consistent with the moment equations derived directly in Bjorken flow (Denicol, 2014). This enables systematic studies of the convergence of the moment expansion and the development of higher-order fluid-dynamical theories beyond second order.
