# $^7$H ground state as a $^3$H+4n resonance

> We have investigated the possible existence of a $^7$H resonant state, considered as a five-body system consisting of a $^3$H core with four valence neutrons. To this aim, an effective n-$^3$H potential is constructed in order to reproduce the low energy elastic neutron scattering on $^3$H phase shifts and the $^5$H resonant ground state in terms of $^3$H-n-n system. The variational Gaussian Expansion Method is used to solve the 5-body Schrödinger equation, while the resonant state parameters were estimated by means of the stabilization method. We have not found any sign of a narrow low energy resonance in the vicinity of $^3$H+4n threshold. However, we have identified a very broad structure at $E_R\approx 9$ MeV above this threshold, which corresponds to the $^7$H J$^π$=1/2$^+$ ground state. In the vicinity of this state, we have also identified a broad structure corresponding to the ground state of $^6$H isotope with quantum numbers $J^π=2^-$.

## Key claim
The authors find no evidence for a narrow low-energy $^7$H resonance near the $^3$H+4n threshold, contrary to several experimental claims; instead they identify a very broad structure at $E_R\approx 9$ MeV above this threshold as the $^7$H ground state, with a width that would likely become even larger in a more realistic model allowing triton core breakup.

## Method
The $^7$H system was treated as a five-body cluster of a $^3$H core plus four valence neutrons, using the Gaussian Expansion Method to solve the Schrödinger equation. An effective n-$^3$H local potential was constructed to reproduce low-energy n-$^3$H scattering phase shifts and the $^5$H resonant ground state (treated as a $^3$H-n-n system), with a three-body t-n-n force adjusted to match previous ab initio results. Resonant state parameters were estimated via the stabilization method, analyzing avoided crossings in the eigenvalue spectrum as a function of a basis scaling parameter.

## Key numbers
- $^7$H ground state resonance energy: $E_R \approx 9.5$ MeV above the $^3$H+4n threshold, with width $\Gamma \approx 3.5$ MeV (for three-body force parameters $b_3=8$ fm, $V_0=2.5$ MeV reproducing the ab initio $^5$H ground state).
- With $V_0=3$ MeV (producing $^5$H at the lowest point compatible with experiment), $^7$H resonance found at $E_R=8.5$ MeV and $\Gamma=3$ MeV.
- $^6$H ground state (t-n-n-n four-body system) estimated at $E_R=10$ MeV and $\Gamma=4$ MeV, using the same interactions.
- $^5$H ground state reproduced at $E=1.9 - 1.2i$ MeV (i.e., $E_R=1.9$ MeV, $\Gamma=2.4$ MeV) with $b_3=8$ fm and $V_0=2.5$ MeV, in agreement with previous ab initio results.
- $^4$H resonant states from the effective n-t potential: $L=1^-, S=0$ at $1.28-2.61i$ MeV; $L=1^-, S=1$ at $1.33-1.84i$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous ab initio studies of $^4$H and $^5$H (Hiyama et al., 2019) to the heavier hydrogen isotopes $^6$H and $^7$H, using a cluster approximation with a $^3$H core. The results contradict the narrow $^7$H resonance (width $\approx 0.1$ MeV, energy 2-3 MeV) predicted by Li, Michel, and Zuo (2021) using No-core Gamow Shell Model, which the authors argue systematically underestimates widths. The findings are more consistent with earlier AMD calculations by Aoyama and Itagaki (2004) giving $E(^7$H$)\sim 7$ MeV, and conflict with experimental claims from GANIL (2007, 2008) and Dubna (2020) of narrow states near the t+4n threshold. The results enable future comparison with ongoing RIBF experiments (NP1512-SAMURAI134) and suggest that the $^7$H ground state is too broad and high in energy to be a narrow resonance.
