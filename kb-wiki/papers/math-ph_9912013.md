# Decay to bound states of a soliton in a well

> The decay of a soliton in a trapped state inside a well is shown numerically. Bound states of a kink in an attractive well, both centered and off center are found. Their stability is studied. Unstable soliton solutions inside a repulsive barrier are also found.

# Decay to bound states of a soliton in a well

## Key claim

A soliton trapped in an attractive well will decay by radiating its kinetic energy as meson waves, eventually settling into a static bound state; this decay occurs for any initial impinging velocity that leads to trapping, and the bound states exist both centered and off-center with respect to the well, though off-center states are unstable.

## Method

The author numerically integrates the nonlinear wave equation for a kink (φ⁴ soliton) with a bell-shaped impurity potential V(x) = h cosh⁻²(a(x−x_c)), using a finite-difference grid spanning x = −140 to 140 with dx = 0.1. The soliton is launched from x = −3 with initial speed v = 0.025 toward a well at x_c = 3, and the field amplitude at the well center is monitored over time. Static bound state solutions are found by integrating the static equations of motion from the soliton center, and stability is analyzed by perturbing the soliton center and deriving an effective equation of motion.

## Key numbers

- Free soliton mass: M = 0.9428 (used as reference for bound state energies)
- For a well of depth h = −1 and width w = 5, centered soliton energy: 0.633
- For a well of depth h = −1 and width w = 5, off-center soliton at x = 5 energy: 0.9425 (nearly unchanged from free mass)
- For a well of depth h = −5 and width w = 5, centered soliton energy: −0.771 (negative energy bound state)
- For a well of depth h = −5 and width w = 5, off-center soliton at x = 5 energy: 0.1177
- For a well of depth h = −5 and width w = 1, centered soliton energy: 0.533
- Meson dispersion relation for m = λ = 1: ω² = k² + 2, giving maximum meson velocity u_max = 1
- Radiation reabsorption time from boundaries at x = ±140: t_absorption = 280
- Friction coefficient used in dissipative simulation: γ = 0.1

## Context

This work extends the earlier findings of Kivshar et al. (approximately 1990) on soliton trapping by impurities, where a soliton arriving at an attractive potential oscillates inside it indefinitely without reemerging. The author previously generalized the delta-function impurity to a finite-size potential (Kalbermann 1997) and developed a few-degree-of-freedom model for trapping (Kalbermann 1999). The present paper demonstrates numerically that trapped states are not eternal but decay radiatively to static bound states, and it identifies both centered (stable) and off-center (unstable) bound state solutions, which have no classical particle analog. The results are relevant to fluxons in large Josephson junctions and to any soliton propagating in an inhomogeneous medium representable by a potential.
