# Investigating radial flow-like effects via pseudorapidity and transverse spherocity dependence of particle production in pp collisions at the LHC

> Recent observations of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) like signatures in high multiplicity proton-proton (pp) collisions, have compelled the heavy-ion physics community to re-examine small collision systems for proper baseline studies. Event-shape-based studies in pp collisions have succeeded to a certain extent in identifying the rare events mimicking such heavy-ion-like behaviour. In this study, we incorporate PYTHIA8 and AMPT to study radial flow-like signatures in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV as a function of transverse spherocity and pseudorapidity. The selection of softer events possibly carrying heavy-ion-like features is performed using the transverse spherocity event-shape observable. As the particle production mechanism in midrapidity differs greatly from the forward rapidity, a pseudorapidity-dependent study is meaningful. Keeping ALICE 3 upgrades at the LHC in mind, this study aims to demonstrate the transverse spherocity and pseudorapidity dependence of the mean transverse momentum, particle ratios, and kinetic freezeout parameters in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8. We observe that the isotropic events show enhanced radial-flow effects in all multiplicity classes, however, the jetty events show signatures of the radial flow-like effects only in high-multiplicity events. For the first time, we show the transverse spherocity and pseudorapidity dependence of partonic modification factor in pp collisions, which clearly shows that by choosing transverse spherocity, one can directly probe the radial flow-like effects in pp collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that transverse spherocity selection in PYTHIA8 simulations of pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV can isolate events with enhanced radial flow-like effects, but this enhancement is conditional: isotropic events show enhanced radial flow-like effects across all multiplicity classes, while jetty events only show such signatures in high-multiplicity events.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA8 (version 8.308 with the 4C tune including color reconnection and multi-partonic interactions) and the string-melting mode of AMPT (version 2.26t9b) to simulate pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV. They classified events using transverse spherocity (S₀) defined with a pseudorapidity range of |η| < 2.0, selecting the lowest 20% of events as jetty and highest 20% as isotropic. The study examined the pseudorapidity dependence of mean transverse momentum, particle ratios, kinetic freezeout parameters, and a partonic modification factor across six pseudorapidity bins from |η| < 0.2 to 1.5 ≤ |η| < 2.0, using V0M multiplicity classes for event selection.

## Key numbers

- The transverse spherocity distribution peak shifts to higher S₀ values when widening the pseudorapidity range from |η| < 0.8 to |η| < 2.0 for both PYTHIA8 and AMPT simulations
- For (0-100)% V0M class in PYTHIA8, the mean charged particle density ⟨dNch/dη⟩ at |η| < 2.0 is 6.702 ± 0.002, with jetty events in S₀ range 0-0.425 and isotropic events in S₀ range 0.765-1
- For (0-1)% V0M class in PYTHIA8, ⟨dNch/dη⟩ at |η| < 2.0 is 25.323 ± 0.017, with jetty events in S₀ range 0-0.675 and isotropic events in S₀ range 0.865-1
- For (0-100)% V0M class in AMPT, ⟨dNch/dη⟩ at |η| < 2.0 is 6.476 ± 0.002, with jetty events in S₀ range 0-0.435 and isotropic events in S₀ range 0.765-1
- For (0-1)% V0M class in AMPT, ⟨dNch/dη⟩ at |η| < 2.0 is 25.780 ± 0.018, with jetty events in S₀ range 0-0.662 and isotropic events in S₀ range 0.858-1

## Context

This work extends previous studies of radial flow-like effects in small collision systems, building on observations of QGP-like signatures in high-multiplicity pp collisions by ALICE (2013, 2016) and CMS (2015, 2016). It follows the approach of Ortiz et al. (2013, 2016, 2020, 2022) who showed that PYTHIA8 with color reconnection can mimic radial flow effects correlated with multi-partonic interactions, and Cuautle et al. (2014, 2015) who established transverse spherocity as a tool for event classification. The study is motivated by the upcoming ALICE 3 upgrades at the LHC, which will enable particle identification over a wider pseudorapidity coverage (|η| < 4.0), and demonstrates that transverse spherocity defined with |η| < 2.0 can effectively probe radial flow-like effects across different pseudorapidity regions.
