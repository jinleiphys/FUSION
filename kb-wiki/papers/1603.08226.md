# Exploring Dense and Cold QCD in Magnetic Fields

> Strong magnetic fields are commonly generated in off-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Lab and in the Large Hadron Collider at CERN and have been used to probe the topological configurations of the QCD vacua. A strong magnetic field can affect the character and location of the QCD critical point, influence the QCD phases, and lead to anomalous transport of charge. To take advantage of the magnetic field as a probe of QCD at higher baryon densities, we are going to need experiments capable to scan the lower energy region. In this context, the nuclotron-based ion collider facility (NICA) at JINR offers a unique opportunity to explore such a region and complement alternative programs at RHIC and other facilities. In this paper we discuss some relevant problems of the interplay between QCD and magnetic fields and the important role the experiments at NICA can play in tackling them.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields can be used as a probe of QCD at higher baryon densities, but to take advantage of this requires experiments capable of scanning the lower energy region, a niche that the NICA facility at JINR is uniquely positioned to fill by complementing programs at RHIC and other facilities.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical and phenomenological aspects of QCD in strong magnetic fields, focusing on how off-central heavy-ion collisions generate fields of order \(10^{18}-10^{20}\) G. It discusses the influence of magnetic fields on the QCD phase diagram, including the chiral critical point, phase transitions, color superconducting phases, and anomalous transport phenomena such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME). The authors specifically examine the potential of NICA experiments to probe a P-odd inhomogeneous phase of dense quark matter (the Dual Chiral Density Wave phase) that exhibits axion electrodynamics and anomalous Hall currents.

## Key numbers

- Off-central Au-Au collisions at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV) generate magnetic fields of \(10^{18}-10^{19}\) G.
- Off-central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV) generate fields as large as \(10^{20}\) G.
- The inhomogeneity length of the Dual Chiral Density Wave phase is \(\Delta x \sim 0.6\) fm for a modulation wave number \(q \sim \mu = 300\) MeV, much smaller than the QGP characteristic scale \(L \sim 10\) fm.
- NICA will explore collision energies in the range \(2 \le E \le 11\) GeV.
- The CME-induced signal from STAR measurements is reduced at beam energies below 20 GeV, where same-sign and opposite-sign correlations begin to overlap.

## Context

This review extends prior work on QCD phases in magnetic fields, including lattice QCD studies of inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC-Lattice, 2012) and model calculations of color superconducting phases (RevCS, RevCS-B). It builds on theoretical predictions of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (NPA803, PRD78) and the Dual Chiral Density Wave phase (KlimenkoPRD82, PLB743). The paper enables future experimental searches at NICA and BES II at RHIC for anomalous transport phenomena in dense quark matter, particularly the nondissipative Hall current in P-odd phases, and proposes that axionic polariton modes could be tested by shining laser light into the collision region.
