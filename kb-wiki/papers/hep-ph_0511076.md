# $η$ and $η'$ decays and what can we learn from them?

> In this talk a short overview of $η$ and $η'$ decays is given with an emphasis on what can be learned for the strong interaction from them. The talk consists of a short introduction to ChPT, a discussion of $η\to3π$ beyond $p^4$ and some of the physics involved in $η'\toηππ,3π$ as well as an overview of anomalous processes.

## Key claim

The talk argues that studying eta and eta' decays provides unique insights into strong interaction dynamics, particularly the role of the U(1)_A anomaly and the gluonic content of the eta', but that many predictions remain uncertain due to large final-state interactions and the need for higher-order calculations in Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT).

## Method

The talk reviews ChPT calculations for eta and eta' decays, focusing on eta to 3pi beyond p^4 order using dispersion relations that incorporate pi-pi rescattering effects. For eta' decays, the author discusses large N_c chiral Lagrangians that include the singlet degree of freedom and treat the U(1)_A anomaly as a perturbation, comparing lowest-order predictions to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The lowest-order ChPT prediction for Gamma(eta to pi+ pi- pi0) is approximately 66 eV using Q approx 24 from baryon mass differences, or approximately 140 eV using Q = 20.0 +/- 1.5 from kaon mass differences
- The p^4 calculation gives an enhancement factor of 2.4 for the integrated decay rate relative to lowest order
- The experimental ratio r = Gamma(eta to pi0 pi0 pi0) / Gamma(eta to pi+ pi- pi0) = 1.44 +/- 0.04 (PDG 2004)
- For eta' to eta pi0 pi0, the lowest-order prediction is 1.0 keV versus experimental 42 +/- 6 keV
- For eta' to eta pi+ pi-, the lowest-order prediction is 1.9 keV versus experimental 89 +/- 10 keV
- For eta' to pi0 pi0 pi0, the lowest-order prediction is 455 eV versus experimental 311 +/- 77 eV
- For eta' to pi0 pi+ pi-, the lowest-order prediction is 405 eV versus experimental less than or equal to 1005 eV

## Context

This talk was presented at the Eta'05 Workshop and reviews work extending the p^4 ChPT calculation of eta to 3pi by Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) using dispersion relations developed by Anisovich and Leutwyler (1996) and by Kambor, Wiesendanger, and Wacker (1996). The eta' treatment builds on large N_c approaches by Veneziano, DiVecchia, Witten, and Schechter (1979-1980). The talk identifies that eta' to eta pi pi decays are dramatically underestimated by lowest-order ChPT, requiring higher-order 1/N_c suppressed terms, while eta' to 3pi agrees reasonably well. The work enables improved experimental tests at WASA, KLOE, and other facilities to determine the Q parameter from eta to 3pi and to study the flavor-singlet degrees of freedom in eta' decays.
