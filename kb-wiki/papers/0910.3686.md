# Chiral effective-field theory with a resonance and heavy fields

> Several conceptual points concerning the inclusion of the $Δ$(1232) resonance in the framework of chiral effective-field theory are discussed, with an emphasis on the problem of power counting in the baryon sector in general. I also formulate a new dispersion relation in the pion-mass squared (or, the quark mass) and make a link between the power counting and the analytic properties of chiral expansion. A controversy regarding the determination of the proton's magnetic polarizability from Compton-scattering data is stressed here as well.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the power-counting problems in Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory are not severe because lower-order "violating" contributions are analytic in quark masses and can be absorbed into low-energy constants, while higher-order terms are required by analyticity and should be kept; the δ-expansion for including the Δ(1232) resonance provides a more adequate counting of resonant contributions than the small-scale expansion.

## Method

The author discusses conceptual issues in including the Δ(1232) resonance in chiral effective-field theory, comparing the small-scale expansion (SSE) and the δ-expansion schemes for power counting. A dispersion relation in the pion-mass squared is formulated and validated using explicit calculations of the nucleon self-energy at order p³ and the nucleon polarizabilities from Compton scattering. The author contrasts the heavy-baryon (HB) χPT results with manifestly-covariant baryon χPT (BχPT) calculations that include Δ-isobar effects within the δ-counting scheme.

## Key numbers

- Δ-nucleon mass splitting: Δ = M_Δ - M_N ≈ (1232 - 939) MeV = 293 MeV
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λ_χSB ≈ 4πf_π ≈ 1 GeV
- In HBχPT at order p³: α = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- In HBχPT at order p⁴: α = 12.1 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β = 3.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- In BχPT with Δ's at order p³: α = 6.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β = -1.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- In BχPT with Δ's at order p⁴/Δ: α = 10.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β = 4.0 × 10⁻⁴ fm³

## Context

This work extends the discussion of power counting in baryon chiral perturbation theory initiated by Weinberg (1979), Gasser and Leutwyler (1983), and Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1989). It contrasts the heavy-baryon expansion of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) with manifestly-covariant formulations, and compares the small-scale expansion of Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1996) with the δ-expansion of Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003) for including the Δ(1232) resonance. The paper enables a resolution of the controversy regarding the determination of the proton's magnetic polarizability from Compton-scattering data, as demonstrated in the BχPT calculation by Lensky and Pascalutsa (2008).
