# The N*(1520) ---> Delta pi Amplitudes Extracted from the gamma p ---> pi+ pi- p Reaction and Comparison to Quark Models

> The gamma p ---> pi+ pi- p reaction, in combination with data from the pi N ---> pi pi N reaction, allows one to obtain the s- and d-wave amplitudes for the N*(1520) decay into Delta pi with absolute sign with respect to the N*(1520) ---> N gamma helicity amplitudes. In addition one obtains the novel information on the q dependence of the amplitudes. This dependence fits exactly with the predictions of the non-relativistic constituent quark models. The absolute values provided by these models agree only qualitatively, and a discussion is done on the reasons for it and possible ways to improve.

# The N*(1520) → Δπ Amplitudes Extracted from the γp → π⁺π⁻p Reaction and Comparison to Quark Models

## Key claim

The s- and d-wave amplitudes for the N*(1520) decay into Δπ, including their momentum dependence, can be extracted from the γp → π⁺π⁻p reaction with absolute sign relative to the N*(1520) → Nγ helicity amplitudes. The q-dependence of these amplitudes fits exactly with predictions from non-relativistic constituent quark models, though the absolute values from these models agree only qualitatively.

## Method

The authors analyze the γp → π⁺π⁻p reaction using a model that includes the contact gauge term (Δ-Kroll-Ruderman term) and the γN → N*(1520) → Δπ process. They extract the s- and d-wave partial amplitudes A_s and A_d for the N*(1520) → Δπ decay by fitting to experimental data from the γp → π⁺π⁻p reaction combined with πN → ππN data, using a Lagrangian with both a constant term and a term proportional to the square of the pion momentum. The q-dependence of the amplitudes is tested by introducing a parameter ε that modifies the constant term, and the results are compared to predictions from the non-relativistic constituent quark model using the elementary emission model with a pseudovector qqπ interaction.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1520) → Nπ decay width at the resonance mass is Γ = 66 MeV, with on-shell pion momentum q_cm(m_N*) = 456 MeV
- The on-shell pion momentum for N*(1520) → Δπ decay is |q_on-shell| = 228 MeV
- The extracted coupling constants for the two solutions are: (a) f̃_N*Δπ = 0.911, g̃_N*Δπ = -0.552; (b) f̃_N*Δπ = -0.911, g̃_N*Δπ = 0.552
- The helicity amplitude coupling constants extracted from experimental values are g̃_γ = 0.108 and g̃_σ = -0.049
- The NRCQM prediction for the ratio A_d/A_s using the Bhaduri potential is +0.156, while the experimental value is 1.2
- The parameter ε = 0 gives the best agreement with data for the q-dependence of A_s
- Off-shell effects in spin 3/2 propagators produce changes in the cross section of order 1%

## Context

This work extends the model of Gómez Tejedor and Oset (1995) for the γp → π⁺π⁻p reaction, which improved on the earlier model of Lüke and Söding. The extracted amplitudes provide a test for non-relativistic constituent quark models, specifically the Bhaduri et al. (1981) potential used by Silvestre-Brac et al. (1986) for baryons. The results confirm the anti-SU(6)_W sign predicted by Le Yaouanc et al. (1975) using the ³P₀ model, and show that the recoil term in the quark model is crucial for explaining this sign. The work enables further study of the γN → ππN reaction in all isospin channels, and provides a new experimental constraint (the q-independence of A_s + A_d) that future relativistic models must satisfy, as discussed in relation to the approaches of Koniuk and Isgur (1980) and Capstick and Roberts (1994).
