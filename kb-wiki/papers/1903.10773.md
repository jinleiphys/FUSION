# Further Theoretical Analysis on the $K^{-} {}^{3} \text{He} \to Λp n$ Reaction for the $\bar{K} N N$ Bound-State Search in the J-PARC E15 Experiment

> Based on the scenario that a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is generated and it eventually decays into $Λp$, we calculate the cross section of the $K^{-} {}^{3} \text{He} \to Λp n$ reaction, which was recently measured in the J-PARC E15 experiment. We find that the behavior of the calculated differential cross section $d ^{2} σ/ d M_{Λp} d q_{Λp}$, where $M_{Λp}$ and $q_{Λp}$ are the $Λp$ invariant mass and momentum transfer in the $(K^{-} , \, n)$ reaction in the laboratory frame, respectively, is consistent with the experiment. Furthermore, we can reproduce almost quantitatively the experimental data of the $Λp$ invariant mass spectrum in the momentum transfer window $350 \text{ MeV} /c < q_{Λp} < 650 \text{ MeV} /c$. These facts strongly suggest that the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state was indeed generated in the J-PARC E15 experiment.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the peak structure observed in the J-PARC E15 experiment's $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum near the $K^{-} p p$ threshold is consistent with the scenario that a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is generated and decays into $\Lambda p$, but only after applying a momentum-transfer cut of $350 \text{ MeV}/c < q_{\Lambda p} < 650 \text{ MeV}/c$ to suppress the quasi-elastic scattering background.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section $d^{2} \sigma / d M_{\Lambda p} d q_{\Lambda p}$ for the $K^{-} {}^{3} \text{He} \to \Lambda p n$ reaction assuming a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is formed and decays into $\Lambda p$. They improve upon their previous work (Sekihara 2016) by incorporating Fermi motion of nucleons and using a $2 \times 2$ matrix form for the $\bar{K} N \to \bar{K} N$ scattering amplitude at the first collision, with partial-wave amplitudes taken from a dynamical coupled-channels model (Kamano 2014) up to the $F$ wave ($L = 4$). The differential cross section is then transformed from the center-of-mass frame to the laboratory frame momentum transfer $q_{\Lambda p}$ and integrated over the experimental momentum-transfer window.

## Key numbers

* The $\bar{K} N N$ bound-state signal appears near the $K^{-} p p$ threshold at $2.37 \text{ GeV}$ in the $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum.
* The momentum-transfer window used to isolate the bound-state signal is $350 \text{ MeV}/c < q_{\Lambda p} < 650 \text{ MeV}/c$, matching the experimental analysis of Ajimura (2018).
* The calculated $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum with the momentum-transfer cut reproduces almost quantitatively the experimental data throughout a wide range of $\Lambda p$ invariant mass.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of Sekihara (2016) by incorporating Fermi motion and a more complete treatment of the $\bar{K} N$ scattering amplitude, and it directly addresses the second-run data of the J-PARC E15 experiment reported by Ajimura (2018). The results support the interpretation that the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state was generated in the experiment, and they validate the experimental strategy of applying a momentum-transfer cut to suppress the quasi-elastic $\bar{K}$ scattering background. This enables future searches for $\bar{K} N N$ bound states in other reactions and provides a benchmark for few-body calculations of antikaon-nucleon interactions.
