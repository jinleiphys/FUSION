# Sequential Decay Distortion of Goldhaber Model Widths for Spectator Fragments

> Momentum widths of the primary fragments and observed final fragments have been investigated within the framework of an Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics transport model code (AMD-V) with a sequential decay afterburner (GEMINI). It is found that the secondary evaporation effects cause the values of a reduced momentum width, $σ_0$, derived from momentum widths of the final fragments to be significantly less than those appropriate to the primary fragment but close to those observed in many experiments. Therefore, a new interpretation for experiemental momentum widths of projectile-like fragments is presented.

## Key claim

The Goldhaber Model accurately describes the momentum widths of primary fragments produced in spectator fragmentation, but the reduced momentum width derived from final observed fragments is significantly smaller (by about 20 MeV/c) than the intrinsic Fermi momentum width because sequential decay (light particle evaporation) from excited primary fragments distorts the observed widths.

## Method

The authors simulated 200 MeV/u 40Ar + 27Al peripheral collisions (impact parameter 6-8 fm) using the AMD-V transport model with Gogny force to produce primary fragments, then fed those excited primary fragments into the GEMINI statistical decay code as an afterburner to follow the full de-excitation cascade. They extracted momentum widths for both primary fragments (at t=60 fm/c) and final fragments after evaporation, fitting the mass-dependent widths to the Goldhaber Model expression to extract the reduced width parameter sigma_0 and apparent initial mass A_0.

## Key numbers

- The average reduced width sigma_0 for primary fragments was approximately 105 MeV/c for projectile-like fragments and 110 MeV/c for target-like fragments, close to the initial nucleon Fermi momentum widths from AMD-V ground states (105 MeV/c for 40Ar, 101 MeV/c for 27Al)
- After sequential decay, the sigma_0 values for final fragments became significantly smaller, with mean values around 83 MeV/c, approaching the experimentally observed values of about 90 MeV/c
- The mean excitation energy of primary fragments was about 1.7 MeV/u for projectile-like fragments and 2.7 MeV/u for target-like fragments
- The reduced width characterizing the evaporation step, sigma_0^Evap, was generally in the range of 30-70 MeV/c, significantly lower than P_F/sqrt(5) which characterizes the primary break-up

## Context

This work extends the Goldhaber Model (Goldhaber, 1974) by showing that the longstanding discrepancy between the predicted reduced width (112-116 MeV/c from electron scattering) and experimentally observed values (about 90 MeV/c) can be explained by sequential decay effects, rather than requiring the more complex corrections proposed by Bertsch (correlations), Murphy (phase space constraints), or Besliu (temperature dependence). The result enables experimentalists to correct observed width distributions for secondary decay using particle-fragment correlation measurements, potentially allowing Fermi momentum distributions to be probed in low-intensity radioactive beam experiments across a wider range of N/Z ratios.
