# The structure of the f_0(980) from system size dependent hadronic resonance ratios in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> It is shown that the hadronic phase in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions can be used to understand the properties of the $f_0(980)$ resonance. In particular it is shown that the centrality dependence of the $f_0(980)/π$ and $f_0(980)/ϕ$ ratios depends strongly on the $f_0(980)\rightarrow \overline{K}+K$ branching ratio and whether the $f_0(980)$ is produced as a $\left | \overline{q}q \right \rangle$ or $\left | \overline{s}s \right \rangle$ state. These conclusions are drawn from calculations within the partial chemical equilibrium of the HRG model within Thermal-FIST as well as with the fully non-equilibrium hybrid-transport approach UrQMD. Our findings show how the hadronic phase in heavy ion collisions can be used for studies of exotic hadron properties otherwise possible only in dedicated experiments such as PANDA.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of the $f_0(980)/\pi$ and $f_0(980)/\phi$ ratios in hadronic collisions at the LHC can distinguish between a light quark $|\overline{q}q\rangle$ or strange quark $|\overline{s}s\rangle$ composition of the $f_0(980)$, but only if the hadronic rescattering phase is properly accounted for and the experimental mass cut on the invariant mass distribution is applied.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary theoretical approaches: (1) a partial chemical equilibrium (PCE) hadron resonance gas model within the Thermal-FIST package, which assumes chemical freeze-out followed by pseudo-elastic reactions down to a kinetic freeze-out temperature, and (2) the hybrid UrQMD model, where an initial fluid-dynamic evolution is followed by a fully microscopic transport cascade with hadronic rescattering. Both models treated the $f_0(980)$ as an independent quasiparticle degree of freedom, with its quark structure implemented implicitly through either the production mechanism (strangeness suppression in strings or via a strangeness saturation parameter $\gamma_S$) or the decay branching ratios to $\pi\pi$ versus $\overline{K}K$.

## Key numbers

* Chemical freeze-out temperature parametrization: $T_{\rm ch} = 0.176\ {\rm GeV} - 0.0026\ {\rm GeV} \cdot \ln(\langle dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\rangle)$
* Correlation volume per rapidity: $dV/dy = 2.4\ {\rm fm}^3 \cdot \langle dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\rangle$
* Strangeness saturation parameter: $\gamma_S = 1 - 0.25 \cdot \exp[-\langle dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\rangle / 59]$
* Particlization temperature in hybrid UrQMD: approximately 162 MeV at vanishing net baryon density
* ALICE lower reconstruction limit for $f_0(980)$ invariant mass: $0.8\ {\rm GeV}$ in the $\pi^+\pi^-$ channel
* Two branching ratio scenarios explored: 0% and 30% branching ratio of $f_0(980)$ into $\overline{K}K$

## Context

This work extends the study of short-lived resonance yields in hadronic matter, previously applied to $K^{*0}$, $\rho^0$, and $\Delta^{++}$ by Motornenko (2019) and Knospe (2015), to the enigmatic $f_0(980)$ resonance. The authors build on the ALICE measurement of $f_0(980)$ production in p+p collisions (ALICE, 2022) and use the system-size dependence across p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies to probe the $f_0(980)$ internal structure. The approach enables studies of exotic hadron properties that would otherwise require dedicated experiments such as PANDA, and could help distinguish between competing models of the $f_0(980)$ as a $|\overline{q}q\rangle$ state, a $|\overline{s}s\rangle$ state, a tetraquark, or a molecular $\overline{K}K$ state.
