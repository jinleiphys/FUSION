# On the QCD critical point, Lee-Yang edge singularities and Pade resummations

> We analyze the trajectory of the Lee-Yang edge singularities of the QCD equation of state in the complex baryon chemical potential ($μ_B$) plane for different values of the temperature by using the recent lattice results for the Taylor expansion coefficients up to eighth order in $μ_B$ and various resummation techniques that blend in Pade expansions and conformal maps. By extrapolating from this information, we estimate for the location of the QCD critical point, $ T_c \approx 100$ MeV, $μ_c \approx 580$ MeV. We also estimate the crossover slope at the critical point to be $α_1 \approx 9^\circ$ and further constrain the non-universal mapping parameters between the three dimensional Ising model and QCD equations of state.

## Key claim
The paper estimates the QCD critical point location by extrapolating the trajectory of Lee-Yang edge singularities in the complex baryon chemical potential plane, using only four Taylor expansion coefficients from lattice data and assuming the trajectory can be partially captured by the scaling behavior of the critical point.

## Method
The author uses diagonal (2,2) Padé resummation of the QCD equation of state Taylor expanded in μ_B^2 up to eighth order, then improves the estimate by iterating with two conformal maps (a two-cut map and a uniformizing map) that depend on the singularity location itself. The Lee-Yang edge singularities are extracted as poles of the resummations for temperatures between 135 and 144 MeV, and the trajectory is extrapolated to the point where the imaginary part vanishes to locate the critical point.

## Key numbers
- Critical point temperature: T_c ≈ 100 MeV (from two-cut conformal Padé, with 1σ uncertainty +18/-18 MeV)
- Critical point chemical potential: μ_c ≈ 557 MeV (from two-cut conformal Padé, with 1σ uncertainty +175/-150 MeV)
- Crossover slope at critical point: α_1 ≈ 8.69° (from two-cut conformal Padé, with 1σ uncertainty +3.91°/-3.83°)
- Non-universal mapping parameter c_2 ≈ 2.56 MeV^(1-βδ) (from two-cut conformal Padé, with 1σ uncertainty +0.58/-1.21)
- Ising critical exponent βδ ≈ 1.5631 (taken from conformal bootstrap literature)
- Lee-Yang edge singularity scaling variable x_LY ≈ 0.246 (taken from functional renormalization group literature)
- Critical exponent σ_LY ≈ 0.074-0.085 (taken from literature for the singular contribution around the LY singularity)

## Context
This work extends the approach of extracting Lee-Yang edge singularities from Taylor coefficients, previously applied to QCD by Mukherjee (2019), Connelly (2020), and Basar (2021), and uses the most recent HotQCD lattice data from Bollweg (2022). The results can be incorporated into state-of-the-art equation of state parametrizations such as those by Parotto (2018) and An (2021) to assist experimental searches for the critical point at the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and the future CBM experiment at FAIR.
