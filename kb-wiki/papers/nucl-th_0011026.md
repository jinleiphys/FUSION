# Do chemically saturated antihyperon abundancies signal the quark gluon plasma?

> We first review the production and the possible chemical equilibration of strange particles at CERN-SPS energies within a microscopic hadronic transport calculation. It is shown in particular that the strange quarks are produced initially via string excitations in the primary, secondary and ternary interactions. We then further elaborate on a recent idea of antihyperon production by multi-mesonic reactions like $n_1π+ n_2 K \to \bar{Y}+p $ corresponding to the inverse of the strong binary baryon-antibaryon annihilation process. It is argued that by these reactions the (rare) antihyperons are driven towards local chemical equilibrium with pions, nucleons and kaons on a timescale of 1--3 fm/c in the still moderately baryon-dense initial hadronic environment after the termination of the prehadronic string phase. Accordingly this mechanism can provide a convenient explanation for the antihyperon yields at CERN-SPS energies without any need of a deconfined quark gluon plasma phase.

# Do chemically saturated antihyperon abundancies signal the quark gluon plasma?

## Key claim
Antihyperons can be driven towards local chemical equilibrium with pions, nucleons and kaons on a timescale of 1–3 fm/c via multi-mesonic reactions (the inverse of strong binary baryon-antibaryon annihilation) in the moderately baryon-dense initial hadronic environment after the prehadronic string phase, providing an explanation for antihyperon yields at CERN-SPS energies without requiring a deconfined quark gluon plasma phase.

## Method
The author reviews strangeness production within the microscopic hadron-string transport model HSD, which implements elementary cross sections for strangeness production in baryon-baryon, baryon-meson and meson-meson channels. The paper then derives a master equation for antihyperon densities in a hadronic gas, where the effective annihilation rate of antihyperons on baryons (using the thermally averaged cross section and baryon density) determines the chemical equilibration timescale. The analysis employs detailed balance to relate the forward annihilation reactions (e.g., \(\bar{Y} + N \rightarrow n\pi + n_Y K\)) to the multi-mesonic back-reactions that produce antihyperons.

## Key numbers
- The chemical equilibration timescale for antihyperons is approximately 1–3 fm/c, derived from \(\Gamma_{\bar{Y}}^{-1} = 1 / [\langle \sigma_{N\bar{Y}} v \rangle \langle \rho_B \rangle]\) using an annihilation cross section of about 50 mb and an average baryon density of 1–2 times normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\).
- The initial net baryon density for midrapidity in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS starts slightly above \(2\rho_0\) after the disappearance of string-like excitations at 4.7 fm/c.
- The chemical freeze-out point from thermal model analyses occurs at a baryon density of 0.5–0.75 \(\rho_0\), corresponding to a time of 8–10 fm/c.
- The \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio starts at approximately 6–8% from primary string fragmentation at AGS to SPS energies before hadronic rescattering begins.
- The relative enhancement factor for strange particles at SPS energies compared to p+p collisions is 1.75, explained by secondary and ternary string-like interactions producing about the same number of strange quarks as primary p+p collisions.
- In box calculations at baryon density \(\rho_B = \rho_0\) and energy density \(\epsilon = 0.52\) GeV/fm\(^3\), the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio needs about 50–100 fm/c to approach its stationary equilibrium value.

## Context
This work extends and challenges the seminal argument by Koch, Muller and Rafelski (1986) that antihyperons do not approach chemical equilibrium even after 1000 fm/c in a hot baryon-rich hadronic system, making them a signature of quark gluon plasma formation. The paper argues that the earlier work underestimated the equilibration rate by using a smaller annihilation cross section (about 10 mb for \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow 5\pi\) instead of the total annihilation cross section of about 50 mb) and by not considering the higher baryon densities present in the early hadronic fireball. The mechanism enables a purely hadronic explanation for the chemically saturated antihyperon yields observed at CERN-SPS energies, which have been interpreted as evidence for QGP formation in thermal model analyses by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1996), Cleymans and Redlich (2000), and Kaneta and Morita (2000). The paper notes that this mechanism does not apply to multi-strange hyperons (\(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\)), whose enhancement may still require more exotic processes or a deconfined state.
