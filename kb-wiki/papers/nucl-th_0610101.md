# Pairing and alpha-decay

> Nuclear pairing interaction plays a crucial role in both macroscopic-microscopic and fully macroscopic descriptions of nuclei. In the present study we discuss different pairing interactions (monopole and delta pairing forces) and the methods allowing for the particle number symmetry restoration in addition to the customary BCS treatment of pairing correlations in the context of $α$-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei. The calculations are done in the macroscopic-microscopic framework for even-even nuclei with Z > 110.

## Key claim

The choice of pairing interaction (monopole vs. delta) and the method of particle-number restoration (BCS, Lipkin-Nogami, or variation after projection) significantly affects calculated alpha-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei with Z > 110, with the largest discrepancies reaching 4 orders of magnitude for long-lived isotopes near neutron numbers N=162 and N=184.

## Method

The authors used a macroscopic-microscopic framework with the Yukawa+Exponential macroscopic model and a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential. They compared four pairing treatments: seniority BCS with constant monopole pairing (BCSg), state-dependent delta-pairing BCS (BCSdelta), the Lipkin-Nogami approximate particle-number projection method, and the exact variation after projection (VAP) method. Alpha-decay half-lives were computed from the resulting Q-values using a modified Viola-Seaborg formula for even-even nuclei with atomic numbers 112 ≤ Z ≤ 120.

## Key numbers

- Delta-pairing strength set to 225 MeV fm^3 for both neutrons and protons in the superheavy region
- Differences in Fermi-level pairing gaps between BCSg and BCSdelta models are of order 0.1 MeV
- Deviations in pairing energy between BCSg and BCSdelta models exceed 0.5 MeV in some cases
- Average deviation from VAP results: σ_LN = 0.717 MeV for Lipkin-Nogami, σ_BCSg = 0.654 MeV for seniority BCS, σ_delta = 0.438 MeV for delta-pairing BCS
- Largest model discrepancies reach 4 orders of magnitude in log(T_alpha/y) for long-lived isotopes with log(T_alpha/y) > -8
- Calculations performed for 70 nuclei with 112 ≤ Z ≤ 122

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Baran et al. (2005) on the influence of macroscopic energy models on fission and alpha-decay, using the Yukawa+Exponential model from Krappe et al. (1979) and the Woods-Saxon potential from Dudek et al. (1979). The paper demonstrates that the delta-pairing BCS method reproduces VAP results more closely than the Lipkin-Nogami approach, contrary to expectations, and identifies the region of long-lived superheavy nuclei (N > 180) as having the largest theoretical uncertainty in half-life predictions. This enables future work to focus on developing more powerful alpha-decay models for superheavy nuclei.
