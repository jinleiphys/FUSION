# Determination of gluon polarization from deep inelastic scattering and collider data

> We investigate impact of $π^0$-production data at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and future E07-011 experiment for the structure function $g_1$ of the deuteron at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab) on studies of nucleonic spin structure, especially on the polarized gluon distribution function. By global analyses of polarized lepton-nucleon scattering and the $π^0$-production data, polarized parton distribution functions are determined and their uncertainties are estimated by the Hessian method. Two types of the gluon distribution function are investigated. One is a positive distribution and the other is a node-type distribution which changes sign at $x \sim 0.1$. Although the RHIC $π^0$ data seem to favor the node type for $Δg(x)$, it is difficult to determine a precise functional form from the current data. However, it is interesting to find that the gluon distribution $Δg(x)$ is positive at large $x$ ($>0.2$) due to constraints from the scaling violation in $g_1$ and RHIC $π^0$ data. The JLab-E07-011 measurements for $g_1^d$ should be also able to reduce the gluon uncertainty, and the reduction is comparable to the one by RUN-5 $π^0$-production data at RHIC. The reduction is caused by the error correlation between polarized antiquark and gluon distributions and by a next-to-leading-order (NLO) gluonic effect in the structure function $g_1^d$. We find that the JLab-E07-011 data are accurate enough to probe the NLO gluonic term in $g_1$. Both RHIC and JLab data contribute to better determination of the polarized gluon distribution in addition to improvement on polarized quark and antiquark distributions.

## Key claim

The polarized gluon distribution \(\Delta g(x)\) is positive at large \(x\) (\(>0.2\)) due to constraints from scaling violation in \(g_1\) and RHIC \(\pi^0\) data, but it remains difficult to determine a precise functional form from current data; although the RHIC \(\pi^0\) data seem to favor a node-type distribution that changes sign at \(x \sim 0.1\), both positive and node-type solutions remain viable.

## Method

The authors performed global next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD analyses of polarized lepton-nucleon deep inelastic scattering (DIS) spin asymmetry \(A_1\) data and double-spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}^{\pi^0}\) data from \(\pi^0\) production in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC. Polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) were parameterized at an initial scale \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) and evolved via DGLAP equations, with uncertainties estimated by the Hessian method using \(\Delta\chi^2 = 12.65\) for the eleven-parameter space. Three analysis sets were compared: set A (DIS only), set B (DIS plus RHIC RUN-5 \(\pi^0\) data), and set C (DIS plus simulated JLab-E07-011 data for the deuteron structure function \(g_1^d\)).

## Key numbers

* The total \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for all analyses ranged from 0.87 to 0.91, indicating successful fits to the data.
* For the positive gluon distribution at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), the first moment \(\Delta G = \int_0^1 dx\,\Delta g(x)\) changed from \(0.63 \pm 0.81\) (set A) to \(0.40 \pm 0.28\) (set B) when RHIC \(\pi^0\) data were included.
* For the node-type gluon distribution at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), \(\Delta G\) changed from \(0.94 \pm 1.66\) (set A) to \(-0.12 \pm 1.78\) (set B).
* The relative uncertainty on the partial first moment \(\Delta G(x>0.1) = \int_{0.1}^1 dx\,\Delta g(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) for the positive distribution decreased from 1.36 (set A) to 0.71 (set B), a reduction of about 48%.
* For the node-type distribution, the relative uncertainty on \(\Delta G(x>0.1)\) decreased from 1.02 (set A) to 0.77 (set B), a reduction of about 25%.
* The JLab-E07-011 data are expected to provide five data points for \(A_1^d\) at \(x\) values from 0.175 to 0.55 and \(Q^2\) from 1.4 to 3.7 GeV\(^2\), with relative errors \(\delta g_1^d/g_1^d\) ranging from 0.010 to 0.020.
* For the positive distribution, the relative uncertainty on \(\Delta G(x>0.1)\) decreased from 1.36 (set A) to 0.73 (set C) with JLab-E07-011 data, a reduction comparable to that from RHIC \(\pi^0\) data (0.71 in set B).
* The quark spin content \(\Delta\Sigma\) at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) was \(0.24 \pm 0.07\) (set A, positive) and \(0.26 \pm 0.06\) (set B, positive).

## Context

This work extends the previous AAC06 analysis (Hirai et al., 2006) by including updated DIS data from CLAS (2006), HERMES (2007), and COMPASS (2007), as well as published RHIC RUN-5 \(\pi^0\) data from PHENIX. The study investigates whether future JLab-E07-011 measurements of the deuteron structure function \(g_1^d\) can provide constraints on the polarized gluon distribution comparable to those from RHIC \(\pi^0\) data. The results enable improved determination of polarized PDFs, particularly the gluon distribution at large \(x\), and provide a new AAC08 (Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration 2008) PDF library for use in spin physics analyses.
