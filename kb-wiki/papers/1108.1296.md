# Exclusive $c\to s,d$ semileptonic decays of ground-state spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons

> We evaluate exclusive semileptonic decays of ground-state spin-1/2 doubly heavy charmed baryons driven by a $c\to s,d$ transition at the quark level. Our results for the form factors are consistent with heavy quark spin symmetry constraints which are valid in the limit of an infinitely massive charm quark and near zero recoil. Only a few exclusive semileptonic decay channels have been theoretically analyzed before. For those cases we find that our results are in a reasonable agreement with previous calculations.

## Key claim

The authors present the first systematic study of exclusive semileptonic decays of ground-state spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons (\(\Xi_{cc}\) and \(\Omega_{cc}\)) via \(c\to s,d\) transitions, finding that their quark-model results for form factors are consistent with heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) constraints near zero recoil, with moderate deviations of about 10% attributed to finite charm quark mass corrections.

## Method

The authors employed a non-relativistic constituent quark model using the AL1 interquark potential to compute baryon wave functions and masses, then evaluated the hadronic matrix elements for \(1/2\to 1/2\) and \(1/2\to 3/2\) semileptonic transitions. Form factors were extracted from these matrix elements and used to calculate decay widths for all dominant (\(c\to s\)) and sub-dominant (\(c\to d\)) exclusive channels to the lowest-lying single-charm baryons (\(\Lambda_c\), \(\Sigma_c\), \(\Sigma_c^*\), \(\Xi_c\), \(\Xi_c'\), \(\Xi_c^*\), \(\Omega_c\), \(\Omega_c^*\)). HQSS relations among form factors were derived using the trace formalism and checked against the model predictions.

## Key numbers

- \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Xi_c^+ e^+\nu_e\) decay width: \(8.75\times 10^{-2}\) ps\(^{-1}\) (c→s transition)
- \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Xi_c'^+ e^+\nu_e\) decay width: 0.146 ps\(^{-1}\) (c→s transition)
- \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Xi_c^{*+} e^+\nu_e\) decay width: \(3.20\times 10^{-2}\) ps\(^{-1}\) (c→s transition)
- \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Lambda_c^+ e^+\nu_e\) decay width: \(4.86\times 10^{-3}\) ps\(^{-1}\) (c→d transition)
- \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Sigma_c^+ e^+\nu_e\) decay width: \(7.94\times 10^{-3}\) ps\(^{-1}\) (c→d transition)
- \(\Omega_{cc}^{+}\to\Omega_c^0 e^+\nu_e\) decay width: 0.282 ps\(^{-1}\) (c→s transition)
- \(\Omega_{cc}^{+}\to\Omega_c^{*0} e^+\nu_e\) decay width: \(5.77\times 10^{-2}\) ps\(^{-1}\) (c→s transition)
- HQSS form factor ratios at \(w=1\) deviate from unity by about 10% for actual charm mass, tending to unity as heavy quark mass increases (shown for pseudoscalar meson masses up to \(\sim\)6 GeV)
- Using the SELEX mass for \(\Xi_{cc}\) (3518.9 MeV/c\(^2\)) instead of the model mass (3613 MeV) reduces decay widths by about 20%

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Kiselev (2001) and Faessler (2001), who studied only a few exclusive semileptonic channels of \(\Xi_{cc}\), and by Guberina (1999), who computed total semileptonic rates via operator product expansion. The authors find reasonable agreement with those earlier results where comparison is possible (e.g., \(\Xi_{cc}\to\Xi_c' e^+\nu_e\) width of 0.146 ps\(^{-1}\) versus 0.208–0.258 ps\(^{-1}\) from Faessler (2001)). The derived HQSS relations among form factors for doubly charmed baryon decays are new, and the systematic survey of all dominant and sub-dominant channels enables future experimental searches at facilities like LHCb and Belle II to identify and measure these decays, which remain unobserved except for the poorly established \(\Xi_{cc}\) signal from SELEX.
