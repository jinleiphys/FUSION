# Pion production and absorption in heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the isospin dependent transport model IBUU, the pion production and its absorption are thoroughly studied in the central collision of $Au+Au$ at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. It is found that the pions are firstly produced by the hard $Δ$ decay at the average density around $1.75ρ_{0}$, whereas about 18\% of them are absorbed absolutely in the subsequent inelastic collisions. For the free pions observed, more than half of them have been scattered for one or more times before they are free from matter. And the more scattering numbers of pions, the higher the momentum they possess. These pions, due to longer time of their existence in high density nuclear matter, carry more information about the symmetry energy of the nuclear matter at high densities.

# Pion production and absorption in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Based on the IBUU transport model for central Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, pions are first produced by hard Δ decay at an average density around 1.75ρ₀, but about 18% of them are subsequently absorbed absolutely in inelastic collisions and never become free; among the free pions observed, more than half have undergone one or more πN scattering cycles before freeze-out, and those with more scattering carry more information about the high-density symmetry energy.

## Method
The isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model was used to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy. All inelastic collisions (NN → NΔ, NΔ → NN, Δ → Nπ, Nπ → Δ) were recorded and statistically analyzed to track the production, absorption, and rescattering history of pions from first formation through freeze-out. Free charged pions were categorized by the number of πN scattering cycles (absorption into Δ followed by re-decay) they experienced before detection.

## Key numbers
- Pions are first produced by hard Δ decay at an average density of **1.75ρ₀** in central Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- About **18%** of pions from hard Δ decay are absorbed absolutely in subsequent inelastic collisions and never become free.
- Only **33%** of hard Δ resonances decay into pions; the rest are absorbed into nucleons without any decay.
- Less than **40%** of detectable negative pions freeze out without any πN scattering; for positive pions, the fraction without scattering is about **44%**.
- At least **5%** of negative pions have been scattered at least five times before detection.
- For pions with five scattering processes, the sensitivity of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio to the symmetry energy reaches more than **15%**.
- The average density of the last (freeze-out) pion formation is up to **1.5ρ₀**.
- The maximum cross sections used for πN → Δ are **200 mb** (π⁺p → Δ⁺⁺, π⁻n → Δ⁻), **66.67 mb** (π⁻p → Δ⁰, π⁺n → Δ⁺), and **133.33 mb** (π⁰p → Δ⁺, π⁰n → Δ⁰).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of pion production as a probe of the symmetry energy at high densities, following the suggestion by Li (2002) that the charged pion ratio can constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy. It addresses conflicting conclusions from comparisons of π⁻/π⁺ ratios with experimental data (Xiao 2009, Xie 2013, Feng 2010) by providing a detailed statistical analysis of pion absorption and rescattering that is not accessible experimentally. The results enable more refined extraction of the high-density symmetry energy by showing that pions with more scattering cycles carry enhanced sensitivity to the stiffness of the symmetry energy, and that the effect depends not only on the density of pion origin but also on the time spent in high-density matter.
