# Second-order quark number susceptibility of deconfined QCD matter in the presence of a magnetic field

> Considering the strong field approximation we compute the hard thermal loop pressure at finite temperature and chemical potential of hot and dense deconfined QCD matter in lowest Landau level in one-loop order. We consider the anisotropic pressure in the presence of the strong magnetic field i.e., longitudinal and transverse pressure along parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field direction. As a first effort, we compute and discuss the anisotropic quark number susceptibility of deconfined QCD matter in lowest Landau level. The longitudinal quark number susceptibility is found to increase with the temperature whereas the transverse one decreases with the temperature. We also compute the quark number susceptibility in the weak field approximation. We find that the thermomagnetic correction to the quark number susceptibility is very marginal in the weak field approximation.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of anisotropic second-order quark number susceptibility (QNS) of deconfined QCD matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field, using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation in the lowest Landau level (LLL), with the caveat that the results are obtained at one-loop order and are valid only under the specific scale hierarchy \(gT < T < \sqrt{|q_fB|}\).

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop HTL pressure for quarks and gluons at finite temperature and chemical potential in the presence of a strong magnetic field, restricting quarks to the lowest Landau level. They derive the anisotropic pressure (longitudinal and transverse to the magnetic field direction) and obtain the second-order QNS by taking the second derivative of the pressure with respect to chemical potential at \(\mu = 0\). The calculation is also extended to the weak field approximation (\(\sqrt{|q_fB|} < m_{th} \sim gT < T\)) using previously developed HTL free-energy expressions.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal second-order QNS for an ideal (non-interacting) quark-gluon plasma in a strong magnetic field is \(\chi_{sf} = \sum_f N_c N_f \frac{q_fB}{\pi^2}\).
* The ideal transverse second-order QNS in a strong magnetic field vanishes, as only gluons contribute to the transverse pressure.
* The Debye mass in the strong field approximation receives a magnetic correction \((\delta m_{D,f}^2)_s = \frac{g^2 |q_fB|}{4\pi^2}\).
* The QCD coupling constant in the strong field region is given by \(\alpha_s(\Lambda^2,|eB|)=\frac{\alpha_s(\Lambda^2)}{1+b_1\alpha_s(\Lambda^2) \ln\left(\frac{\Lambda^2}{\Lambda^2+|eB|}\right)}\) with \(b_1=\frac{11N_c-2N_f}{12\pi}\) and \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}=176\) MeV.
* The thermomagnetic quark mass in the weak field approximation is \(M_B^2 = \sum_f \frac{q_fB}{16\pi^2}\left[-\frac{1}{4}\aleph(z)-\frac{\pi T}{2m_f}-\frac{\gamma_E}{2}\right]\).

## Context

This work extends previous HTL calculations of quark number susceptibilities in thermal QCD (Haque 2013, 2014) and thermodynamic quantities in magnetized QCD matter (Karmakar 2019, Bandyopadhyay 2017) by computing the anisotropic QNS for the first time. The results qualitatively match lattice calculations of magnetization (Bali 2014) and show that the longitudinal QNS increases with temperature while the transverse QNS decreases, indicating anisotropic expansion dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. The weak field calculation shows that thermomagnetic corrections to QNS are marginal, suggesting that magnetic field effects on fluctuations are most significant in the strong field regime.
