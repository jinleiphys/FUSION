# Predictions of elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor from 200 GeV U + U collisions at RHIC

> Predictions of elliptic flow ($v_2$) and nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) are provided as a function of centrality in U + U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. Since the $^{238}$U nucleus is naturally deformed, one could adjust the properties of the fireball, density and duration of the hot and dense system, for example, in high energy nuclear collisions by carefully selecting the colliding geometry. Within our Monte Carlo Glauber based approach, the $v_2$ with respect to the reaction plane $v_2^{RP}$ in U + U collisions is consistent with that in Au + Au collisions, while the $v_2$ with respect to the participant plane $v_2^{PP}$ increases $\sim$30-60% at top 10% centrality which is attributed to the larger participant eccentricity at most central U + U collisions. The suppression of $R_{AA}$ increases and reaches $\sim$0.1 at most central U + U collisions that is by a factor of 2 more suppression compared to the central Au + Au collisions due to large size and deformation of Uranium nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in U+U collisions at 200 GeV, the elliptic flow measured with respect to the participant plane ($v_2^{PP}$) will be 30-60% larger than in Au+Au collisions for the top 10% most central events, while the elliptic flow with respect to the reaction plane ($v_2^{RP}$) will remain consistent between the two systems. The nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) is predicted to reach approximately 0.1 in the most central U+U collisions, representing a factor of 2 more suppression than in central Au+Au collisions, contingent on the assumption that the path length determines the suppression.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model with a deformed Woods-Saxon nucleon density distribution for uranium (deformation parameters $\beta_2 = 0.28$ and $\beta_4 = 0.093$) and a spherical profile for gold. They generated 1 million U+U collision events at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, randomly rotating the nuclei and sampling impact parameters, then calculated charged particle pseudorapidity density using a two-component model. Elliptic flow predictions were obtained by scaling the measured $v_2/\varepsilon$ scaling curve from Au+Au data (using Eq. 1 from Bhalerao (2005)) with the calculated eccentricities and transverse densities for U+U. The $R_{AA}$ was extrapolated by fitting the Au+Au $R_{AA}$ as a function of the calculated path length $\langle L \rangle$ and applying that fit to the U+U path lengths.

## Key numbers

*   The maximum charged particle pseudorapidity density ($dN_{ch}/d\eta$) in U+U collisions increases by approximately 15% compared to Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.
*   The participant eccentricity $\varepsilon_{PP}\{2\}$ in U+U collisions increases by approximately 60% at top 5% central collisions compared to Au+Au collisions at the same $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.
*   The predicted $v_2^{PP}$ in U+U collisions at top 0-10% centrality is 30-60% larger than in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.
*   The predicted $R_{AA}$ reaches approximately 0.1 at most central U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, which is a factor of 2 more suppression than in central Au+Au collisions.
*   The average path length $\langle L \rangle$ in U+U collisions is slightly larger (approximately 3% in central and 5% in peripheral collisions) than in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the scaling of elliptic flow with transverse density observed by Voloshin (2007) and the suppression of high-$p_T$ hadrons measured by PHENIX (Adare, 2008) to a larger, deformed nuclear system. It provides specific predictions for the upcoming U+U collisions at RHIC, which will become possible when the Beam Ion Source becomes operational around 2012 (Alessi, 2005). The results enable experimental tests of whether the increased system size and deformation from uranium can further probe the hydrodynamic limit of $v_2$ and the density dependence of parton energy loss, and suggest that selecting specific nuclear orientations (e.g., tip-tip vs. body-body) could enhance these effects, as previously discussed by Heinz (2004).
