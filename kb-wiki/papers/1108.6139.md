# Holographic equations of state and astrophysical compact objects

> We solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation using an equation of state (EoS) calculated in holographic QCD. The aim is to use compact astrophysical objects like neutron stars as an indicator to test holographic equations of state. We first try an EoS from a dense D4/D8/\textoverline {D8} model. In this case, however, we could not find a stable compact star, a star satisfying pressure-zero condition with a radius $R$, $p(R)=0$, within a reasonable value of the radius. This means that the EoS from the D4/D8/\textoverline {D8} model may not support any stable compact stars or may support one whose radius is very large. This might be due to a deficit of attractive force from a scalar field or two-pion exchange in the D4/D8/\textoverline {D8} model. Then, we consider D4/D6 type models with different number of quark flavors, $N_f=1,2,3$. Though the mass and radius of a holographic star is larger than those of normal neutron stars, the D4/D6 type EoS renders a stable compact star.

## Key claim

The D4/D8/D8 holographic QCD model does not yield a stable compact star solution to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, likely due to the absence of a scalar field mediating intermediate-range attraction; in contrast, the D4/D6 model, which includes both vector and scalar bulk fields, does produce stable compact stars, though their masses and radii are larger than those of observed neutron stars.

## Method

The authors solved the general-relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation using equations of state (EoS) derived from two top-down holographic QCD models: the D4/D8/D8 model (Sakai-Sugimoto type) and the D4/D6 model. For the D4/D6 model, they computed the energy density and pressure from the on-shell DBI actions of probe D6 branes and compact D4 branes, with force balancing at the brane junction, and extended the single-flavor case to multi-flavor cases with N_f = 1, 2, 3. They integrated the TOV equation using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with a tabulated EoS, patching the holographic EoS to the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland EoS at low baryon number densities.

## Key numbers

- For the D4/D6 model with N_f = 2, 't Hooft coupling λ = 6, and M_KK = 1.04 GeV, the maximum mass was about 3.86 solar masses and the corresponding radius was 45 km.
- For the D4/D6 model with N_f = 2, λ = 17, and M_KK = 1.04 GeV, the maximum mass was 1.36 solar masses and the radius was 17 km.
- The normal nuclear density used was ρ_0 = 1.3 × 10^6 MeV^3.
- The holographic EoS was patched to the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland EoS at ρ ≈ 3 × 10^{-4} · ρ_0 MeV^3.
- The model parameters for the D4/D6 EoS were a ≈ 4.45 × 10^8 MeV^4 and b ≈ 1.07 × 10^6 MeV^3.
- The model parameters for the D4/D8/D8 EoS were a = 3.76 × 10^9 MeV^4 and b = 7.16 × 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}, with λ ≈ 16.71 and M_KK ≈ 950 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of using holographic QCD to describe dense baryonic matter, following earlier studies of the D4/D8/D8 model by Sakai and Sugimoto (2004) and dense matter in that model by Kim (2007). The authors find that the D4/D8/D8 model fails to produce stable compact stars, which they attribute to the missing scalar field that provides intermediate-range attraction in nuclear physics, while the D4/D6 model, previously studied for dense matter by Seo (2008) and Kim (2009), does yield stable solutions. The results enable future work on hybrid stars that combine a conventional low-density EoS with a holographic high-density EoS, and they highlight the need to incorporate proper attraction into holographic models to match observed neutron star masses (around 1.5 solar masses) and radii (around 10 km).
