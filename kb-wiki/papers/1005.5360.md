# Total cross sections for neutron-nucleus scattering

> Systematics of neutron scattering cross sections on various materials for neutron energies up to several hundred MeV are important for ADSS applications. Ramsauer model is well known and widely applied to understand systematics of neutron nucleus total cross sections. In this work, we examined the role of nuclear effective radius parameter (r$_0$) on Ramsauer model fits of neutron total cross sections. We performed Ramsauer model global analysis of the experimental neutron total cross sections reported by W. P. Abfalterer, F. B. Bateman, {\it et. al.,}, from 20MeV to 550MeV for nuclei ranging from Be to U . The global fit functions which can fit total cross section data over periodic table are provided along with the required global set of parameters. The global fits predict within $\pm 8%$ deviation to data, showing the scope for improvement. It has been observed that a finer adjustment of r$_0$ parameter alone can give very good Ramsauer model description of neutron total scattering data within $\pm 4%$ deviation. The required r$_0$ values for Ramsauer model fits are shown as a function of nuclear mass number and an empirical formula is suggested for r$_0$ values as a function of mass number. In optical model approach for neutron scattering, we have modified the real part of Koning-Deleroche potentails to fit the neutron total cross sections using SCAT2 code. The modified potentails have a different energy dependence beyond 200MeV of neutron energy and fit the total cross sections from Al to Pb.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Ramsauer model with twelve global parameters can fit neutron total cross sections from 20 to 550 MeV across the periodic table within ±8% deviation, but that adjusting only the nuclear effective radius parameter r₀ locally improves the fits to within ±4% deviation, revealing that the global constant r₀ = 1.42988 fm is an approximation requiring mass-dependent refinement.

## Method

The authors performed Ramsauer model fits to experimental neutron total cross section data from Abfalterer et al. (2001) for nuclei from ⁹Be to ²³⁸U in the energy range 20-550 MeV, using a parameterization where the total cross section depends on nuclear radius R, reflection coefficient α, and phase shift β. They then used the SCAT2 optical model code with Koning-Deleroche potentials, modifying the real part of the potential with an exponential energy-dependent factor f(E) = exp((E-E₀)/aₑ) beyond a cutoff energy E_c to extend the optical model description up to 550 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Global Ramsauer model fits reproduce experimental data within ±8% deviation for light nuclei and ±6% for heavy nuclei over the energy range 20-550 MeV
- By adjusting only the r₀ parameter locally, fits improve to within ±4% deviation for most systems
- The global fit uses a constant r₀ = 1.42988 fm, while the mass-dependent empirical formula is r₀(A) = 1.0005 + 0.38045 × A^0.02581
- For ²³⁸U the best-fit r₀ = 1.4442 fm; for ²⁰⁸Pb the best-fit r₀ = 1.42689 fm; for ⁹Be the best-fit r₀ = 1.40193 fm
- Modified Koning-Deleroche potentials with parameters E_c ranging from 210 to 350 MeV and a_e from 245 to 300 MeV fit total cross sections from ²⁷Al to ²⁰⁹Bi up to 550 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Ramsauer model systematics of neutron total cross sections previously studied by Grimes et al. (1998, 2000) and Deb and Amos (2004) by providing a complete set of twelve global parameters and demonstrating the sensitivity to the nuclear radius parameter. The modified Koning-Deleroche potentials extend the optical model approach of Koning and Delaroche (2003), which was limited to 200 MeV, up to 550 MeV for applications in accelerator-driven sub-critical (ADS) systems. The empirical r₀(A) formula enables improved Ramsauer model fits for any nucleus without re-optimizing all parameters.
