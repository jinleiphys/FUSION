# Angular distributions of higher order splitting functions in the vacuum and in dense QCD matter

> We study the collinear splitting functions needed for next-to-next-to-leading order calculations of jet production in the vacuum and in dense QCD matter. These splitting functions describe the probability of a parton to evolve into three-parton final state and are generalizations of the traditional DGLAP splitting kernels to a higher perturbative order. Of particular interest are the angular distributions of such splitting functions, which can elucidate the significance of multiple parton branching for jet observables and guide the construction of parton shower Monte Carlo generators. We find that to ${\cal O}(α_s^2)$ both the vacuum and the in-medium collinear splitting functions are neither angular ordered nor anti-angular ordered. Specifically, in dense QCD matter they retain the characteristic broad angular distribution already found in the ${\cal O}(α_s)$ result.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at next-to-next-to-leading order (${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2)$), the collinear splitting functions for $q\rightarrow ggq$ in both vacuum and dense QCD matter are neither angular ordered nor anti-angular ordered; in the medium case, the angular distribution remains broad as already found at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s)$, contradicting the angular-ordering assumption used in parton shower Monte Carlo generators like HERWIG.

## Method

The authors use Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to compute the vacuum $q\rightarrow ggq$ splitting function and verify it reproduces the known result of Catani (1998). They then extend the calculation to dense QCD matter using Soft Collinear Effective Theory with Glauber gluons (SCETG), deriving the first-order-in-opacity medium-induced $q\rightarrow ggq$ splitting function. The angular distributions of these $1\rightarrow 3$ splitting functions are studied and compared to a "cascade approximation" that iterates $1\rightarrow 2$ DGLAP splitting functions.

## Context

This work extends the vacuum $1\rightarrow 3$ splitting function results of Catani (1998) to dense QCD matter, where no such calculations previously existed. It provides the theoretical foundation for next-to-next-to-leading order jet production calculations in heavy ion collisions and guides the construction of parton shower Monte Carlo generators beyond leading order. The results enable improved fixed-order and resummed calculations of jet observables in heavy ion reactions at the LHC, as well as better interpretation of experimental measurements from ATLAS and CMS collaborations.
