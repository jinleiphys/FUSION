# Deuteron Compton scattering below pion photoproduction threshold

> Deuteron Compton scattering below pion photoproduction threshold is considered in the framework of the nonrelativistic diagrammatic approach with the Bonn OBE potential. A complete gauge-invariant set of diagrams is taken into account which includes resonance diagrams without and with NN-rescattering and diagrams with one- and two-body seagulls. The seagull operators are analyzed in detail, and their relations with free- and bound-nucleon polarizabilities is discussed. It is found that both dipole and higher-order polarizabilities of the nucleon are needed for a quantitative description of recent experimental data. An estimate of the isospin-averaged dipole electromagnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon and the polarizabilities of the neutron is obtained from the data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that elastic deuteron Compton scattering below pion photoproduction threshold can provide a viable alternative for extracting neutron electromagnetic polarizabilities, but only if binding corrections including meson-exchange currents and seagulls are carefully evaluated; the isospin-averaged dipole polarizabilities of the nucleon and the polarizabilities of the neutron are estimated from the data, but with the caveat that the sum of polarizabilities is constrained by the Baldin sum rule while the difference remains a free parameter.

## Method

The authors perform a nonrelativistic diagrammatic calculation of deuteron Compton scattering using the Bonn OBE potential (OBEPR), constructing a complete gauge-invariant set of diagrams that includes resonance diagrams with and without NN-rescattering, plus one- and two-body seagull operators. The seagull operators are analyzed in detail, with their relations to free- and bound-nucleon polarizabilities discussed, and the calculation avoids Siegert-like transformations by working directly in momentum representation. The model includes leading relativistic corrections (spin-orbit interaction) in the one-body electromagnetic current and seagull, and covers energies below pion photoproduction threshold.

## Key numbers

- Proton dipole polarizabilities from experiment: \(\bar\alpha_p = 12.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.5\), \(\bar\beta_p = 2.1 \mp 0.8 \mp 0.5\) (in units of \(10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\)), extracted from 1990s experiments under the Baldin sum rule constraint (MacGibbon et al., 1995)
- Neutron electric polarizability from transmission experiments: \(\bar\alpha_n = 12.6 \pm 1.5 \pm 2.0\) (Schmiedmayer et al., 1991) and \(\bar\alpha_n = 0.6 \pm 5\) (Koester et al., 1995), with a relativistic correction of \(+0.62\) included
- First quasi-free \(\gamma d \to \gamma n p\) experiment result: \(\bar\alpha_n = 10.7^{+3.3}_{-10.7}\) (Rose et al., 1990)
- Authors' own Baldin sum rule evaluation gives \(\bar\alpha_p + \bar\beta_p = 14.0\) and \(\bar\alpha_n + \bar\beta_n = 15.2\) (in \(10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\)), with uncertainties \(\pm (0.3{-}0.5)\)
- Adopted isospin-averaged sum: \(\bar\alpha_N + \bar\beta_N = 14.6\) (in \(10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\))
- The correction \(\delta\alpha_0\) to the electric polarizability in the nonrelativistic Hamiltonian: \(-0.85\) for the proton and \(-0.62\) for the neutron (in \(10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\))

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of deuteron Compton scattering by Weyrauch and Arenhoevel (1983, 1990), Wilbois et al. (1995), and the authors' own preliminary work (Levchuk and L'vov, 1995), addressing discrepancies among those results (e.g., the forward-angle cross section at 100 MeV from Wilbois et al. is only 2/3 of that from Weyrauch and Arenhoevel or Levchuk and L'vov). The paper also relates to recent effective field theory approaches by Chen et al. (1998, 1999) and Beane et al. (1999), noting that while those naturally include non-static pion propagation effects, they lose contributions important for neutron polarizability determination (e.g., the Delta-isobar). The analysis enables improved extraction of neutron polarizabilities from elastic \(\gamma d\) scattering data, complementing quasi-free \(\gamma d \to \gamma n p\) measurements and avoiding the strong assumptions about high-energy \(\gamma N\) interactions required by dispersion-theory approaches.
