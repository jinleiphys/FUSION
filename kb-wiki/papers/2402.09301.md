# Exploring semi-inclusive two-nucleon emission in neutrino scattering: a factorized approximation approach

> The semi-inclusive cross section of two-nucleon emission induced by neutrinos and antineutrinos is computed employing the relativistic mean field model of nuclear matter and the dynamics of meson exchange currents. Within this model we explore a factorization approximation based on the product of an integrated two-hole spectral function and a two-nucleon cross section averaged over hole pairs. We demonstrate that the integrated spectral function of the uncorrelated Fermi gas can be analytically computed, and we derive a simple fully relativistic formula for this function, showcasing its dependency solely on both missing momentum and missing energy. A prescription for the average momenta of the two holes in the factorized two-nucleon cross section is provided, assuming that these momenta are perpendicular to the missing momentum in the center-of-mass system. The validity of the factorized approach is assessed by comparing it with the unfactorized calculation. Our investigation includes the study of the semi-inclusive cross section integrated over the energy of one of the emitted nucleons and the cross section integrated over the emission angles of the two nucleons and the outgoing muon kinematics. A comparison is made with the pure phase-space model and other models from the literature. The results of this analysis offer valuable insights into the influence of the semi-inclusive hadronic tensor on the cross section, providing a deeper understanding of the underlying nuclear processes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a factorized approximation, where the semi-inclusive hadronic tensor is expressed as the product of an integrated two-hole spectral function and a two-nucleon cross section evaluated at average hole momenta, can be applied to meson-exchange current (MEC) two-nucleon emission in neutrino scattering, but only under the condition that the average hole momenta are taken perpendicular to the missing momentum in the center-of-mass system.

## Method

The semi-inclusive cross section for two-nucleon emission induced by neutrinos and antineutrinos was computed using the relativistic mean field (RMF) model of nuclear matter with meson exchange currents, including seagull, pion-in-flight, pion-pole, and Delta(1232) isobar currents. The exact semi-inclusive hadronic tensor was reduced to a two-dimensional integral over relative angles of the hole pair in their center-of-mass system, and a factorized approximation was derived by replacing the elementary 2p2h hadronic tensor with its value at average hole momenta, using an analytically computed integrated two-hole spectral function for the uncorrelated Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

* The Fermi constant used is \(G = 1.166 \times 10^{-11}~\mathrm{MeV}^{-2}\) and the cosine of the Cabibbo angle is \(\cos\theta_c = 0.975\).
* The strong form factor cutoff for pion-nucleon vertices is \(\Lambda = 1300~\mathrm{MeV}\).
* The Delta(1232) strong form factor cutoff is \(\Lambda_\Delta = 1150~\mathrm{MeV}\).
* The vector form factor mass for the Delta vertex is \(M_V = 0.84~\mathrm{GeV}\) and the axial form factor mass is \(M_{A\Delta} = 1.05~\mathrm{GeV}\).
* The coupling constants used are \(f_{\pi NN} = 1\), \(g_A = 1.26\), and \(f^* = 2.13\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF-based inclusive 2p2h response calculations (Martinez-Consentino and Amaro, 2021a, 2021b, 2023a, 2023b) to the semi-inclusive channel, and connects with earlier semi-inclusive calculations by Cuy (2016, 2017) using the shell model without Delta currents, and by Sob (2020) using a relativistic Fermi gas with local density approximation. The factorized approximation enables more efficient computation of multi-dimensional integrated cross sections needed for Monte Carlo event generators such as GENIE, NEUT, NUWRO, and GiBUU, and allows comparison with the isotropic symmetric model commonly used in those generators.
