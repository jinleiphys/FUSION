# Anomalous Dimensions from Soft Regge Constants

> Using an effective field theory (EFT) formalism for forward scattering, we reconsider the factorization of $2\to 2$ scattering amplitudes in the Regge limit. Expanding the amplitude in gauge invariant operators labelled by the number of Glauber exchanges, allows us to further factorize the standard impact factors into separate collinear and soft functions. The soft functions are universal, and describe radiative corrections to the Reggeized gluon states exchanged by the collinear projectiles. Remarkably, we find that the one-loop soft function for the single Reggeized gluon state is given to $\mathcal{O}(ε)$ in terms of the two-loop cusp and two-loop rapidity anomalous dimensions. We argue that this iterative structure follows from the simple action of crossing symmetry in the forward scattering limit, which in the EFT allows us to replace the divergent part of a soft loop by a much simpler Glauber loop. We use this correspondence to provide a simple calculation of the two-loop Regge trajectory using the EFT. We then explore its implications at higher perturbative orders, and derive the maximally matter dependent contributions to the Regge trajectory to all loop orders, i.e.~the terms $\sim α_s^{k+1}n_f^k$ for any $k$, where $n_f$ is the number of massless flavors. These simplifications suggests that the EFT approach to the Regge limit will be helpful to explore and further understand the structure of the Regge limit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the one-loop soft function for the single Reggeized gluon state is given to order epsilon in terms of the two-loop cusp and two-loop rapidity anomalous dimensions, and that this iterative structure follows from crossing symmetry in the forward scattering limit, which allows replacing the divergent part of a soft loop with a simpler Glauber loop.

## Method

The authors use the Glauber effective field theory (EFT) built into soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to re-examine the factorization of 2-to-2 scattering amplitudes in the Regge limit. They expand the amplitude in gauge invariant operators labelled by the number of Glauber exchanges, further factorizing standard impact factors into separate collinear and soft functions. They compute one-loop soft corrections for graphs with one and two Glauber exchanges, and use crossing symmetry relations between soft loops and Glauber loops to derive the two-loop Regge trajectory and higher-order maximal matter-dependent contributions.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop soft function for the single Reggeized gluon state is given to order epsilon in terms of the two-loop cusp and two-loop rapidity anomalous dimensions.
* The two-loop Regge trajectory is given by alpha^(2)(epsilon) = beta_0/epsilon^2 C_A + 2 Gamma_cusp^(1)/epsilon C_A + Gamma_R^(1), where Gamma_cusp^(1) = -5n_f/9 + C_A(67/18 - pi^2/6) and Gamma_R^(1) = -56 C_A n_f/27 + C_A^2(404/27 - 2 zeta_3).
* The one-loop Regge trajectory is alpha^(1)(epsilon) = 2C_A/epsilon.
* The authors derive the maximally matter dependent contributions to the Regge trajectory to all loop orders, i.e. the terms ~ alpha_s^(k+1) n_f^k for any k, where n_f is the number of massless flavors.
* The alpha_s^3 n_f^2 Regge trajectory is obtained to order epsilon^0 and agrees with explicit three-loop calculations.
* The alpha_s^4 n_f^3 terms in the Regge trajectory are given to orders epsilon^{-4} through epsilon^0, and alpha_s^5 n_f^4 terms to orders epsilon^{-5} through epsilon^0.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber EFT approach of Rothstein (2016) for forward scattering, which previously demonstrated that the order epsilon^0 term in the one-loop soft function was given by the two-loop cusp anomalous dimension. The paper extends observations by Del Duca (2017) about iterative structures in impact factors by showing that the soft contributions to impact factors are purely given by the Regge trajectory and are independent of whether quarks or gluons are collided. The results enable calculation of maximal-n_f terms in the Regge trajectory to all loop orders and provide new predictions for the order epsilon^0 terms at four and five loops.
