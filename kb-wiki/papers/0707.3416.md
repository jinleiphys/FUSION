# Searching for statistical equilibrium in a dynamical multifragmentation path

> A method for identifying statistical equilibrium stages in dynamical multifragmentation paths as provided by transport models, already successfully tested for for the reaction ^{129}Xe+^{119}Sn at 32 MeV/u is applied here to a higher energy reaction, ^{129}Xe+^{119}Sn at 50 MeV/u. The method evaluates equilibrium from the point of view of the microcanonical multifragmentation model (MMM) and reactions are simulated by means of the stochastic mean field model (SMF). A unique solution, corresponding to the maximum population of the system phase space, was identified suggesting that a huge part of the available phase space is occupied even in the case of the 50 MeV/u reaction, in presence of a considerable amount of radial collective flow. The specific equilibration time and volume are identified and differences between the two systems are discussed.

## Key claim

Statistical equilibrium can be identified in the dynamical multifragmentation path of the reaction 129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/u, but only when fragment kinetic distributions and positions are fitted in addition to fragment partitions and excitation energies; the system phase space is largely occupied even in the presence of considerable radial collective flow, though the degree of equilibration is weaker than at 32 MeV/u.

## Method

The authors used the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model to simulate the reaction 129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/u and then applied the Microcanonical Multifragmentation Model (MMM) to search for a statistically equilibrated source along the dynamical path. They minimized a two-stage error function: first fitting fragment multiplicity, charge distributions, and internal excitation energies (error function E1), then fitting fragment kinetic energies and positions of the three largest fragments after propagating MMM configurations to the dynamical freeze-out volume (error function E2). The MMM source parameters (mass A, excitation energy E, freeze-out volume V, and level-density cutoff parameter tau) were varied over wide ranges, and flow energy and flow exponent were also adjusted.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrated source for 129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/u corresponds to tau=50 MeV, A=183, Z=76, E=6.6 MeV/u, V/V0=5.6, with flow energy 0.6 MeV/u and flow exponent 1.2; the propagation time from this configuration to dynamical freeze-out is 58.8 fm/c.
- An alternative solution with tau=infinity gives A=183, Z=76, E=6.4 MeV/u, V/V0=9.6, flow energy 1 MeV/u, flow exponent 1.4, and propagation time 14.8 fm/c.
- The equilibration time is approximately 140 fm/c after the beginning of the reaction, coinciding with the saturation of the pre-fragment number.
- The dynamical freeze-out time is around 200 fm/c, with a corresponding volume of 16.73 V0.
- For the 32 MeV/u reaction, the equilibrated source was previously identified at 140 fm/c with volume 3.4 V0 and tau=infinity.

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Raduta et al. (2006) for the 129Xe+119Sn reaction at 32 MeV/u to a higher incident energy of 50 MeV/u, where the system exhibits more radial collective flow and smaller fragments. The analysis reveals a qualitative difference: at 32 MeV/u, fragment partitions and excitation energies alone were sufficient to identify the equilibrated source, while at 50 MeV/u a kinematic analysis is necessary. The results show that equilibrium is reached while system constituents are still in a pre-fragment stage, and that an external radial flow constraint is required at 50 MeV/u. This work enables experimental nuclear thermodynamics studies where volume is a key variable for locating the process in the phase diagram.
