# Prompt photon yield and $v_2$ coefficient from gluon fusion induced by magnetic field in heavy-ion collision

> We compute the production of prompt photons and the $v_2$ harmonic coefficient in relativistic heavy-ion collisions induced by gluon fusion in the presence of an intense magnetic field, during the early stages of the reaction. The calculations take into account several parameters which are relevant to the description of the experimental transverse momentum distribution, and elliptic flow for RHIC and LHC energies. The main imput is the strength of the magnetic field which varies in magnitude from 1 to 3 times the pion mass squared, and allows the gluon fusion that otherwise is forbidden in the absence of the field. The high gluon occupation number and the value of the saturation scale also play an important role in our calculation, as well as a flow velocity and geometrical factors. Our results support the idea that the origin of at least some of the photon excess observed in heavy-ion experiments may arise from magnetic field induced processes, and gives a good description of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that prompt photon production via gluon fusion, which is normally forbidden in vacuum but becomes allowed in the presence of an intense magnetic field, can explain at least part of the experimentally observed photon excess at low transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions. The claim is hedged by the condition that the mechanism operates only during the early stages of the collision when the magnetic field reaches its maximum strength (1 to 3 times the pion mass squared) and the gluon occupation number is high.

## Method

The authors compute the photon production rate from gluon fusion in a magnetic field using a perturbative scheme valid at times of order τ_s ≈ 1/Λ_s (about 1.5 fm), when the glasma is highly occupied by gluons described as quasiparticles. They sum the dominant Feynman diagrams with one quark in the Lowest Landau Level and two quarks in the first excited Landau Level, working in the massless quark approximation and assuming the magnetic field is the dominant energy scale. The invariant photon momentum distribution is then shifted by a phenomenological expansion factor with constant flow velocity, and the v_2 coefficient is obtained from a Fourier decomposition combined with a weighted average over the magnetic-induced and direct photon contributions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength varied from 1 to 3 times the pion mass squared (eB = 1-3 m_π²)
- Saturation scale Λ_s = 2 GeV
- Time scale Δτ_s = 1.5 fm
- Strong coupling constant α_s = 0.3
- Flow velocity β = 0.25
- Geometrical factor χ = 0.8
- Nuclear radius R = 7 fm
- Centrality range 20-40% for comparison with PHENIX data
- Sum over three light quark flavors in the calculation

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Ayala et al. (2015, 2016) on photon production from gluon fusion in magnetic fields, and addresses the incomplete agreement between hydrodynamical model calculations and experimental measurements of the photon v_2 coefficient from ALICE and PHENIX. The results support the idea that magnetic field-induced processes during the early stages of heavy-ion collisions contribute to the observed photon excess, and the calculation provides a good description of the difference between PHENIX data and the hydrodynamical calculations of the direct photon yield from Ref. hydro-photons1.
