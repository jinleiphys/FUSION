# Quadrupole deformation of deuterons and final state interaction in $^2 \vec H (e,e'p)$ scattering on tensor polarized deuterons at CEBAF energies

> The strength of final state interaction (FSI) between struck proton and spectator neutron in $^2\vec{H}(e,e'p)$ scattering depends on the alignment of the deuteron. We study the resulting FSI effects in the tensor analyzing power in detail and find substantial FSI effects starting at still low missing momentum $p_m \gsim 0.9 fm^{-1}$. At larger $p_m \gsim 1.5 fm^{-1}$, FSI completely dominates both missing momentum distribution and tensor analyzing power. We find that to a large extent FSI masks the sensitivity of the tensor analyzing power to models of the deuteron wave function. For the transversely polarized deuterons the FSI induced forward-backward asymmetry of the missing momentum distribution is shown to have a node at precisely the same value of $p_m$ as the PWIA missing momentum distribution. The position of this node is not affected by FSI and can be a tool to distinguish experimentally between different models for the deuteron wave function.

# Quadrupole deformation of deuterons and final state interaction in $^2 \vec H (e,e'p)$ scattering on tensor polarized deuterons at CEBAF energies

## Key claim

Final state interaction (FSI) effects in the tensor analyzing power for $^2\vec{H}(e,e'p)$ scattering become substantial starting at missing momentum $p_m \gsim 0.9 fm^{-1}$ and completely dominate both the missing momentum distribution and tensor analyzing power at $p_m \gsim 1.5 fm^{-1}$, largely masking the sensitivity of the tensor analyzing power to different models of the deuteron wave function. However, the forward-backward asymmetry of the missing momentum distribution for transversely polarized deuterons exhibits a node at precisely the same $p_m$ value as the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) missing momentum distribution, and this node position is unaffected by FSI, providing a tool to distinguish experimentally between deuteron wave function models.

## Method

The authors employed a Glauber theory description of FSI between the struck proton and spectator neutron in $^2\vec{H}(e,e'p)$ scattering, treating the photon as a scalar operator for the longitudinal response at high $Q^2 \gsim (1-2) GeV^2$ relevant to CEBAF experiments. They decomposed the deuteron wave function into S- and D-wave components using the Bonn and Paris potentials, computed the tensor analyzing power $A = (\sigma_{++} + \sigma_{--} - 2\sigma_0)/(\sigma_+ + \sigma_- + \sigma_0)$ for tensor polarized deuterons, and analyzed the FSI-modified S- and D-wave amplitudes as functions of missing momentum $p_m$ and angle $\theta$ between the momentum transfer $\vec q$ and $\vec p_m$.

## Key numbers

- FSI effects become substantial starting at $p_m \gsim 0.9 fm^{-1}$ and completely dominate at $p_m \gsim 1.5 fm^{-1}$.
- For the Bonn potential, the S-wave amplitude $s(p_m)$ has a PWIA node at $p_m^{(1)} = 2.22 fm^{-1}$; FSI shifts this node to $p_m^{(4)} = 1.37 fm^{-1}$ in transverse kinematics ($\theta = 90^\circ$).
- The zero in $N_0(p_m, \theta = 90^\circ)$ occurs at $p_m^{(2)} = 1.58 fm^{-1}$ for the Bonn potential and $p_m^{(2)} = 1.50 fm^{-1}$ for the Paris potential in PWIA.
- The tensor analyzing power $A^{PWIA}(p_m, \theta=90^\circ)$ changes sign at $p_m^{(3)} = 2.73 fm^{-1}$ for Bonn and $p_m^{(3)} = 2.52 fm^{-1}$ for Paris potentials.
- The FSI-induced node in $Re \, d_0(p_m, \theta=90^\circ)$ occurs at $p_m^{(5)} = 2.87 fm^{-1}$ for the Bonn potential.
- At small $p_m \lsim 0.5 fm^{-1}$, FSI reduces the S-wave contribution by approximately 3.5% and the momentum distributions $W_\mu$ by approximately 7%.
- The dip in $W_0(p_m, \theta=90^\circ)$ from destructive S-D interference shifts from $p_m^{(2)} \approx 1.58 fm^{-1}$ in PWIA to $p_m^{(7)} = 1.21 fm^{-1}$ with FSI included.
- The tensor analyzing power $A(p_m, \theta=90^\circ)$ has a maximum at $p_m^{(8)} = 1.19 fm^{-1}$ and equals $1/2$ at $p_m^{(9)} = 1.42 fm^{-1}$.
- The first zero of $A(p_m, \theta=90^\circ)$ with FSI occurs at $p_m^{(10)} = 1.70 fm^{-1}$, compared to $p_m^{(3)} = 2.73 fm^{-1}$ in PWIA.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of unpolarized $^2H(e,e'p)$ scattering (Bianconi et al., 1994) which showed FSI overwhelming PWIA distributions at $p_m \gsim 1.5 fm^{-1}$, and similar FSI effects found for $^4He$ (Bianconi et al., 1994) and heavier nuclei. The paper provides a quantitative Glauber theory description of FSI effects in tensor polarized deuteron scattering, which is needed to interpret experiments planned at CEBAF and the HERMES experiment at HERA. The results enable experimental discrimination between deuteron wave function models (specifically Bonn and Paris potentials) through the node position in the forward-backward asymmetry, which remains unaffected by FSI, while also showing that FSI must be carefully accounted for when extracting short-distance properties of the $np$ interaction from tensor analyzing power measurements.
