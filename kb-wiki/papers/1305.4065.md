# The influence of the Chi_b(3P) state on the decay cascade of bottomium in PbPb collisions at LHC energies

> We investigate the decay cascade of the Upsilon(nS) meson including the newly found Chi_b(3P) state in pp and PbPb collisions at LHC energies. The main goal is to quantitatively determine the additional suppression of the Upsilon(nS) states in PbPb collisions relative to pp at LHC energies of sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV when the Chi_b(3P) state is included together with the Upsilon(nS) and Chi_b(1P,2P) states. It is found that the suppression of Upsilon(1S) in PbPb collisions relative to pp is increased by at most 7% through the inclusion of Chi_b(3P), whereas the suppression factors for the Upsilon(2S) and Upsilon(3S) states do not change significantly.

## Key claim

Including the newly discovered χb(3P) state in the decay cascade of bottomium increases the suppression of Υ(1S) in PbPb collisions relative to pp by at most 7%, while the suppression factors for Υ(2S) and Υ(3S) do not change significantly; this result depends on using theoretical radiative decay widths from Daghighian and Silverman (1987) since no experimental branching ratios exist for the χb(3P) state.

## Method

The authors performed an inverted decay cascade calculation using measured final Υ(nS) populations from CMS pp data at √sNN = 2.76 TeV to obtain initial bottomium state populations, then applied preliminary suppression factors from their prior work (Nendzig and Wolschin, 2013) for screening, gluodissociation, and collisional damping in the quark-gluon plasma. They extended the cascade to include the χb(3P) state using theoretical partial radiative decay widths from Daghighian and Silverman (1987) for the E1 transitions to Υ(1S), Υ(2S), and Υ(3S), averaging over the χbj(3P) hyperfine states weighted by (2j+1). The final suppression factors RAA were computed for minimum-bias and centrality-dependent PbPb collisions and compared to CMS data.

## Key numbers

- The minimum-bias suppression factor for Υ(1S) including χb(3P) is RAA = 0.425 (for formation time tF = 0.1 fm/c, QGP lifetime tQGP = 6 fm/c), compared to RAA = 0.455 without χb(3P) from Nendzig and Wolschin (2013)
- The CMS experimental minimum-bias suppression factor for Υ(1S) is 0.56 ± 0.08 ± 0.07 at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- The ground-state suppression factor is lowered by about 6.7% when including χb(3P); the Υ(2S) minimum-bias suppression factor is reduced by only 1.4%
- Theoretical partial radiative decay widths for χb(3P) to Υ(1S) are 3.159 keV total (averaged over hyperfine states), to Υ(2S) are 3.356 keV, and to Υ(3S) are 10.333 keV, from Daghighian and Silverman (1987)
- The upper limit for the fraction of Υ(1S) arising from χb(3P) decays is 6%, based on the assumption that all Υ(3S) come from χb(3P) decays

## Context

This work extends the feed-down cascade model of Nendzig and Wolschin (2013) for bottomium suppression in PbPb collisions at LHC energies, which previously considered five states (Υ(1S,2S,3S) and χb(1P,2P)), by adding the χb(3P) state recently discovered by the ATLAS Collaboration (2013) in 7 TeV pp collisions and confirmed by the D0 Collaboration (2012) from Tevatron data. The results enable more precise theoretical comparisons with CMS suppression measurements (CMS Collaboration, 2012) and indicate that while the χb(3P) effect is below 7% for the ground state, it should be included in future detailed descriptions of bottomium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma.
