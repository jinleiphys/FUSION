# Path integral approach to no-Coriolis approximation in heavy-ion collisions

> We use the two time influence functional method of the path integral approach in order to reduce the dimension of the coupled-channels equations for heavy-ion reactions based on the no-Coriolis approximation. Our method is superior to other methods in that it easily enables us to study the cases where the initial spin of the colliding particle is not zero. It can also be easily applied to the cases where the internal degrees of freedom are not necessarily collective coordinates. We also clarify the underlying assumptions in our approach.

# Path integral approach to no-Coriolis approximation in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The path integral method provides a new derivation of the no-Coriolis approximation that is superior to previous methods because it can be extended to cases where the initial spin of the colliding particle is not zero and where internal degrees of freedom are not necessarily collective coordinates, though the approximation itself assumes the angular part of translational motion is much slower than radial motion and replaces the operator L² by a c-number.

## Method

The authors use the two time influence functional method of the path integral approach to reduce the dimension of coupled-channels equations for heavy-ion reactions. They first replace the operator L² in the time evolution operator of the environmental degrees of freedom by a c-number Λ(Λ+1), then perform a rotational coordinate transformation to a rotating frame where the z-axis is along the direction of the radial vector. This yields an effective Hamiltonian in which the z-component of internal spin m_I is conserved, drastically reducing the number of coupled channels.

## Key numbers

- The fusion cross section formula in the no-Coriolis approximation is given by σ_fus(E) = π/(k_{n_iI_i})² Σ_{JL_i} Σ_K (2L_i+1)/(2I_i+1) |⟨L_i0I_iK|JK⟩|² × [path integral expression with influence functional]
- When initial internal spin is zero, L_i equals J and the summation simplifies, with the coupling strength scaled by a factor √((2λ+1)/(4π)) for each multipole λ
- The effective Hamiltonian in the rotating frame contains the term Λ(Λ+1)ℏ²/(2μR²) where Λ is a c-number often taken to be the total angular momentum J

## Context

This work extends previous derivations of the no-Coriolis approximation (also called the rotating frame approximation) that used properties of Racah coefficients (Takigawa and Ikeda 1986, Nishiguchi et al. 1986, Tanimura 1987, Esbensen et al. 1987), Green's function methods (Takigawa et al. 1992, Abe and Takigawa 1993), and symmetry considerations using tidal spin (Gomez-Camacho and Johnson 1986). A prior path integral approach (Balantekin et al. 1991) did not treat angular momentum coupling explicitly. The method enables future application to cases with non-zero initial spin and to transfer reactions in heavy-ion fusion, though the authors note the no-Coriolis approximation cannot be applied when long-range forces such as Coulomb interaction are involved, and a prescription for renormalizing the coupling strength remains an unsettled problem.
