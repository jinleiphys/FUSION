# Statistical Hadronization of Multistrange Particles

> We study multistrange hadrons produced in NA49 and STAR experiments at center of mass energies varying from \sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.61 GeV to 200 GeV. We show that the yields of Ξ, Ξ-bar and ϕ can help to constrain the physical conditions present in the hot dense fireball source of these multistrange hadrons created in heavy ion collision. We address the question of chemical equilibrium of individual quark flavors before and after hadronization and offer a few predictions for LHC.

## Key claim

The ratio of Xi to phi hadron yields is nearly constant across a wide range of collision energies (from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.61 GeV to 200 GeV) and centralities, which implies that the light quark phase space occupancy gamma_q and hadronization temperature T are universal hadronization conditions; however, the ratio of Xi to K varies by a factor of two or more, showing that strange quark phase space occupancy gamma_s changes significantly between experiments and centralities, ruling out full chemical equilibrium hadronization.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental data from the NA49 collaboration at SPS (Pb-Pb collisions) and the STAR collaboration at RHIC (Au-Au collisions) for multistrange hadron yields including Xi, anti-Xi, and phi mesons. They applied the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) with phase space occupancies gamma_q and gamma_s for light and strange quarks, using the SHARE program to compute particle yields including contributions from resonance decays, and examined ratios such as Xi/phi (proportional to gamma_q) and Xi/K (proportional to gamma_s) to constrain the hadronization conditions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio Xi/phi (defined as sqrt(Xi+ Xi- / phi phi)) remains within the range 0.249 to 0.304 across all systems studied, from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.61 GeV to 200 GeV, with number of participants N_part from about 20 to 350.
- The average value of Xi/phi across all data is 0.281 (straight dashed line in figure).
- For STAR 62 GeV Au-Au collisions, the Xi/phi ratio varies from 0.249 (60-80% centrality) to 0.304 (0-5% centrality).
- The Xi/K ratio (defined as sqrt(Xi+ Xi- / K+ K-)) shows a factor of 1.5 variation between central and peripheral collisions at STAR 62 GeV, and more than a factor of two across all systems.
- For STAR 200 GeV 0-20% centrality: 10*Xi/phi = 3.02, 10^2*Xi/K = 4.06, 10^3*Xi/pi = 6.04, 10*phi/K = 1.34, 10^2*phi/pi = 2.54 (+0.21/-0.09).
- For SPS 80A GeV 7% centrality: 10*Xi/phi = 3.33, 10^2*Xi/K = 3.04, 10^3*Xi/pi = 2.6, 10*phi/K = 0.83, 10^2*phi/pi = 0.88.
- The predicted range for Omega/phi at RHIC 62 GeV is 5.5 x 10^-2 to 7.0 x 10^-2.
- The predicted Xi/phi at LHC is 0.28 plus or minus 10%.
- The critical value of gamma_s for T = 140 MeV is gamma_s^crit = 9.47; for T = 170 MeV it is gamma_s^crit = 6.68.
- The strangeness over entropy ratio at LHC is predicted to be at least s/S = 0.037, implying gamma_s/gamma_q = 1.55.

## Context

This work extends the Statistical Hadronization Model analysis of multistrange particles previously presented by Petran et al. (2009) and builds on global fits by Rafelski et al. (2004, 2009) and Letessier et al. (2005) that showed all systems hadronize at the same temperature in the non-equilibrium model. The finding that Xi/phi is constant while Xi/K varies contradicts the full chemical equilibrium model (gamma_q = gamma_s = 1) and supports a non-equilibrium hadronization picture where the quark-gluon plasma hadronizes rapidly, preserving its strangeness content. The results enable predictions for LHC heavy ion collisions, particularly that Xi/phi will remain in the same range and that phi/pi greater than 0.03 would indicate gamma_s greater than 1, which can only arise from a strangeness-rich initial phase such as chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma.
