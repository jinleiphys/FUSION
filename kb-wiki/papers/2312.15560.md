# The $z_g$ distribution for heavy flavour jets in dense quark-gluon plasma

> We study the $z_g$ distribution for heavy flavour, i.e. bottom and charm quark, jets propagating through the dense QCD medium. We extend the late emission approximation for Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) formula to heavy flavours. % We consider both the normalised and $N_{\rm jet}$ normalised $z_g$ distributions, and the ratio of the latter distributions to the vacuum ones, called the R ratio. We demonstrate that since there is no collinear singularity in the medium, there is no principal need to use the Sudakov safety technique for medium-induced emission (MIE). We see that contrary to the vacuum case, the normalised $z_g$ distribution is sensitive to the dead cone angle value. In particular, for the case of $N_{jet}$ normalised distribution with $θ_g\le θ_{cut}$, it is possible to directly probe the dead cone gluons in the medium. Our results can be useful to guide the experimental measurements of the heavy flavour jet substructure in dense QCD medium.

## Key claim
The authors extend the late emission approximation of the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) formula to heavy flavour jets and show that, contrary to the vacuum case, the normalised $z_g$ distribution is sensitive to the dead cone angle value. They argue that because there is no collinear singularity in the medium, there is no principal need to use the Sudakov safety technique for medium-induced emission, though such a technique is still needed for a smooth massless limit.

## Method
The authors use the late emission approximation (large L limit) of the ASW formula in the harmonic oscillator approximation to calculate the $z_g$ distribution for heavy flavour jets. They split the full shower into vacuum-like emissions (VLE) occurring at times $t \le t_f$ and medium-induced emissions (MIE) occurring at times $t \ge t_f$, where $t_f = \sqrt{\omega/\hat q}$ is the medium formation time. They consider both normalised and $N_{\rm jet}$ normalised $z_g$ distributions, and for the latter they compute the ratio to the vacuum distribution (the R ratio), using a Sudakov factor only for the VLE contribution to handle the collinear singularity in the massless limit.

## Key numbers
- The coherence angle $\theta_c \sim 2/\sqrt{\hat q L^3}$ is 0.04 for $L=4$ fm and 0.02 for $L=6$ fm, using $\hat q = 1.5$ GeV$^2$/fm.
- For b-jets, the dead cone angle is larger than the coherence angle for $p_T \le 125$ GeV at $L=4$ fm and for $p_T \le 70$ GeV at $L=6$ fm.
- The characteristic transverse momentum scale is $k_f^2 = \sqrt{\hat q \omega}$, and the characteristic frequency is $\omega_c = \hat q L^2/2$.
- The dead cone frequency scale is $\omega_{DC} = (\hat q / \theta_0^4)^{1/3}$, below which radiation goes essentially outside the dead cone.

## Context
This work extends the calculation of the $z_g$ distribution for massless partons in dense QCD medium by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2016) and Caucal et al. (2019) to heavy flavour jets. It builds on the ASW formalism developed by Armesto et al. (2003) for heavy quarks, which showed that the dead cone effect is weakened in the medium relative to the earlier dead cone approximation by Dokshitzer et al. (2001). The results can guide experimental measurements of heavy flavour jet substructure in quark-gluon plasma and provide a direct probe of dead cone gluons in the medium.
