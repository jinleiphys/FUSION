# Functional Renormalization Group and Kohn-Sham scheme in Density Functional Theory

> Deriving accurate energy density functional is one of the central problems in condensed matter physics, nuclear physics, and quantum chemistry. We propose a novel method to deduce the energy density functional by combining the idea of the functional renormalization group and the Kohn-Sham scheme in density functional theory. The key idea is to solve the renormalization group flow for the effective action decomposed into the mean-field part and the correlation part. Also, we propose a simple practical method to quantify the uncertainty associated with the truncation of the correlation part. By taking the $φ^4$ theory in zero dimension as a benchmark, we demonstrate that our method shows extremely fast convergence to the exact result even for the highly strong coupling regime.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel method (KS-FRG) that combines functional renormalization group with the Kohn-Sham scheme of density functional theory, claiming that this approach shows extremely fast convergence to exact results even for highly strong coupling regimes, as demonstrated in a zero-dimensional phi^4 theory benchmark.

## Method

The method splits the full effective action into a mean-field (Kohn-Sham) part and a correlation part at each flow parameter lambda, then solves the Kohn-Sham equation for the mean field and the FRG flow equation for the correlation part self-consistently. The correlation part is expanded in a Taylor series around the stationary point of the Kohn-Sham effective action, and a practical uncertainty quantification method is introduced by taking a first iteration of the next-order truncation equations.

## Key numbers

* For weak coupling (omega=1, v=0.01): first-order KS-FRG gives ground-state density 0.99506529 and ground-state energy 0.001241785, already at O(10^-6) and O(10^-7) accuracy respectively.
* For intermediate coupling (omega=1, v=1): third-order KS-FRG gives ground-state energy 0.084529 with O(10^-4) accuracy, compared to conventional FRG which gives only O(10^-2) accuracy even with sixth-order calculation.
* For strong coupling (omega=1, v=100): third-order KS-FRG gives ground-state density 0.1482 and ground-state energy 0.7597, reaching O(10^-2) accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the two-particle point-irreducible FRG (2PPI-FRG) approach developed by Polonyi, Sailer, and Schwenk (2002) and further studied by Kemler and collaborators (2013, 2017), which showed slow convergence in previous case studies including zero-dimensional phi^4 theory and (1+1)-D Alexandrou-Negele nuclei. The KS-FRG method enables systematic and fast-converging calculations of energy density functionals for quantum many-body systems, with potential applications to cold atoms near unitarity and nucleons in finite nuclei.
