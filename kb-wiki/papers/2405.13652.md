# "Soft" interaction parameters setting in the extended quantum molecular dynamics model

> The extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model is one of the few quantum molecular dynamics (QMD)-like transport approaches that can be used to study the effective clustering structure as well as heavily deformed nuclei in both ground state nuclei and nuclear reactions. However, there are only two parameter sets that lead to hard incompressibility for long times. The aim of the present work is to obtain a soft equation of state (EoS) in the EQMD model. In this context, we take the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR), which is sensitive to the EoS, as an example to check our work. By introducing a kind of standard Skyrme energy density functional with different parameter sets, such as SkP, SkT1, and SKXce, whose incompressibility value ranges from 200 to 268 MeV, the ISGMR of $^{208}$Pb and other nuclei are studied. When the SkP parameter sets are adopted, our new soft interaction in the EQMD model gives reasonable agreement with the experimental data in the heavy ion regime.

## Key claim
By introducing standard Skyrme energy density functionals (SkP, SkT1, SKXce) into the EQMD model, the authors obtain a soft equation of state with incompressibility values between 200 and 268 MeV, where only the SkP parameter set (K∞ ≈ 200 MeV) gives reasonable agreement with experimental ISGMR data for heavy nuclei, while the original EQMD model only had parameter sets leading to hard incompressibility.

## Method
The authors replaced the simple Skyrme interaction in the original EQMD model with a standard Skyrme energy density functional including bulk, Coulomb, gradient terms and their symmetry parts, while keeping the Pauli potential with adjusted strength. They used three Skyrme parameter sets (SkP, SkT1, SKXce) with known incompressibility values, initialized nuclei via frictional cooling, and excited the isoscalar giant monopole resonance by applying a scaling perturbation (c = 1.03) to the nuclear density. The GMR peak energies were extracted from the time evolution of the root-mean-square radius using Fourier analysis.

## Key numbers
- SkP parameter set: K∞ = 200 MeV, gives best agreement with experimental ISGMR data for 208Pb
- SkT1 parameter set: K∞ = 236 MeV
- SKXce parameter set: K∞ = 268 MeV
- Original EQMD model gave GMR peak energy for 208Pb of about 20.55 MeV, compared to experimental value of 13.7 MeV (from RCNP experiments by Patel et al.)
- Experimental 208Pb ISGMR: Eγ = 13.7 MeV, Γ = 3.3 MeV (from RCNP 386-MeV inelastic scattering data)
- SkP parameters: t0 = -2931.7 MeV fm⁵, t3 = 18709.0 MeV fm³⁺³α, α = 1/6, cp = 18.6 MeV
- Pauli potential parameters for all three sets: f0 = 1.0, μ = 1.3, a = 0.5 fm, b = 4.0 fm²

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing limitation of the EQMD model developed by Maruyama et al. (1996), which only had two parameter sets both yielding hard incompressibility far above the consensus range of 230 ± 40 MeV (Khan, 2009). The new soft interaction enables the EQMD model to study nuclear reactions involving clustering and deformed nuclei with a realistic equation of state, particularly relevant for ISGMR studies where previous theoretical calculations overestimated peak energies in Sn isotopes (Tin1, Tin2) compared to experimental data. The approach follows similar Skyrme functional implementations in other QMD-like models such as ImQMD (Zhang et al.) and LQMD, and enables future studies of heavy deformed nuclei and neutron-rich isotopes at subsaturation densities.
