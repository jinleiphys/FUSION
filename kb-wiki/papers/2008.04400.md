# $α$-decay properties of even-even superheavy nuclei

> In this paper, we have calculated the $α$-decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei with $106 \leq Z \leq 126$ and a neutron number of $150 \leq N \leq 200$ within proximity potentials and deformed-spherical Coulomb potentials by using $WS4$ $α$-decay energy and the semi-classical Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin approximation for penetration probability. Besides, we have included the preformation factor within the cluster-formation model. We have investigated magic numbers and submagic numbers in the mentioned region; with high probability 162, 178, and 184 are predicted as neutron magic numbers. We also have confirmed that there is good agreement between our predicted half-lives and the ones obtained from semiempirical relationships such as Royer, VSS, UDL, and SemFIS2.

## Key claim

The authors predict that neutron numbers 162, 178, and 184 are probable magic numbers in the superheavy region (Z=106–126, N=150–200), based on systematic trends in calculated α-decay half-lives; the claim is hedged by stating these are predictions "with high probability" and that for Z=126 "we need more proficiency to have a more comprehensive discussion."

## Method

The authors calculated α-decay half-lives for even-even superheavy nuclei using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) semiclassical approximation for penetration probability, with proximity potentials (prox. 66, prox. 03 I, and Ngô 80) for the nuclear potential and a deformed-spherical Coulomb potential from the double-folding model. They used Qα values from the WS4 mass model and included the α-preformation factor from the cluster-formation model (CFM). The calculated half-lives were compared with those from four semiempirical formulas: Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski (VSS), Royer, universal decay law (UDL), and SemFIS2.

## Key numbers

- RMSD for prox. 03 I with preformation factor: 0.5055 (for even-even nuclei from Table 3 of Ref. [0410])
- RMSD for prox. 66 with preformation factor: 0.5802
- RMSD for Ngô 80 with preformation factor: 0.8949
- RMSD for SemFIS2: 0.52059
- RMSD for Royer: 0.4921
- RMSD for VSS: 0.4967
- RMSD for UDL: 0.5250
- Neutron numbers predicted as magic: 162, 178, and 184
- Neutron numbers suggested as submagic: 170, 174, and 182
- Proton range studied: Z=106 to Z=126
- Neutron range studied: N=150 to N=200

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comprehensive investigation of superheavy nuclei (Ghodsi and Hassanzad, 2020) by applying proximity potentials and deformed-spherical Coulomb potentials to a wider range of isotopes. The study confirms and refines earlier predictions of magic numbers, including N=162 (predicted by prior work for Z=110–118), N=184 (predicted for Z=114), and N=178 (suggested by systematic studies of Qα and Pα values for Z=118–128). The results provide guidance for future experiments searching for the island of stability and for nuclear structure studies of superheavy elements.
