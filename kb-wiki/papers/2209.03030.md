# Particle species and energy dependencies of freeze-out parameters in high-energy proton-proton collisions

> We used blast wave model with Tsallis statistics to analyze the experimental data measured by ALICE Collaboration in proton-proton collisions at Large Hadron Collider and extracted the related parameters (kinetic freeze-out temperature, transverse flow velocity and kinetic freeze-out volume of emission source) from transverse momentum spectra of the particles. We found that the kinetic freeze-out temperature and kinetic freeze-out volume are mass dependent. The former increase while the latter decrease with the particle mass which is the evidence of a mass as well as volume differential kinetic freeze-out scenario. Furthermore we extracted the mean transverse momentum and initial temperature by an indirect method and observed that they increase with mass of the particles. All the above discussed parameters are observed to increase with energy. Triton ($t$), hyper-triton (${^3_{\barΛ} H}$) and helion (${^3 He}$) and their anti-matter are observed to freeze-out at the same time due to isospin symmetry.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature and volume in proton-proton collisions are mass-dependent, with temperature increasing and volume decreasing as particle mass increases, which the authors interpret as evidence for a mass and volume differential freeze-out scenario. The claim is hedged by noting that the blast wave model with Tsallis statistics provides only an approximate description, and that in a few cases the fit quality is poor due to low statistics at high transverse momentum and the use of a single-component function.

## Method

The authors fitted the blast wave model with Tsallis statistics to transverse momentum spectra measured by the ALICE Collaboration for pions, kaons, protons, strange particles (K0_S, Lambda, Xi, Omega, phi, K*), and light nuclei (deuteron, triton, hyper-triton, helion) and their antiparticles in proton-proton collisions at center-of-mass energies of 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV. The fit extracted the kinetic freeze-out temperature (T0), transverse flow velocity (beta_T), and kinetic freeze-out volume (V) for each particle species. Mean transverse momentum and initial temperature were derived indirectly from the fitted spectra using the string percolation model relation.

## Key numbers

- For pions at 0.9 TeV: T0 = 0.030 ± 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.440 ± 0.011, V = 1200 ± 103 fm^3
- For protons at 0.9 TeV: T0 = 0.041 ± 0.005 GeV, beta_T = 0.400 ± 0.008, V = 1000 ± 75 fm^3
- For helion (3He) at 0.9 TeV: T0 = 0.066 ± 0.005 GeV, beta_T = 0.190 ± 0.007, V = 639 ± 40 fm^3
- For pions at 7 TeV: T0 = 0.082 ± 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.567 ± 0.010, V = 2270 ± 123 fm^3
- For protons at 7 TeV: T0 = 0.092 ± 0.005 GeV, beta_T = 0.518 ± 0.010, V = 2026 ± 95 fm^3
- For triton (t) at 7 TeV: T0 = 0.129 ± 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.260 ± 0.011, V = 1400 ± 90 fm^3
- For helion (3He) at 7 TeV: T0 = 0.129 ± 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.220 ± 0.011, V = 1400 ± 100 fm^3
- The entropy index q was restricted to the range 1.001 to 1.15 for all fits

## Context

This work extends previous studies of freeze-out scenarios in high-energy collisions, which have included single freeze-out (as in the work of the authors cited as reference 8), double freeze-out where strange and non-strange particles decouple separately (references 9 and 10), and multiple kinetic freeze-out scenarios (references 11, 12, 13). The authors' own prior work (reference 14) observed a triple kinetic freeze-out scenario. The present analysis specifically examines whether isospin symmetry and quark coalescence play a role in freeze-out, finding that triton, hyper-triton, and helion freeze out at the same time due to isospin symmetry. The results enable further investigation of the critical end point of the phase transition between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma by studying specific variations in freeze-out parameters as functions of collision energy.
