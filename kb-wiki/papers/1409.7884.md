# The energy dependence of antiparticle to particle ratios in high energy pp collisions

> The energy dependence of antiparticle to particle ratios in pp collisions of high energy is studied using the PACIEA and DCPC model. The yield ratios of antimatter and matter for different masses are measured at various energies. It is found that the yield ratios of antimatter and matter increase with the increase of the c.m energy of pp collisions until they gradually approach to 1 after the c.m energy is more than 200 GeV. The distribution of transverse momentum also has signiffcant dependence on the energy and mass, i.e the average transverse momentum increase when the c.m energy of pp collisions increase. The model results are compatible with the STAR preliminary datum.

## Key claim

The antiparticle-to-particle yield ratios in pp collisions increase with center-of-mass energy and approach 1 only after the energy exceeds 200 GeV, with the model results being compatible with the STAR preliminary datum, but the paper notes that the ratios are "approximately close to 1 and most of them is slightly less than 1" even at 200 GeV and 7 TeV, rather than exactly reaching unity.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model, based on PYTHIA, to generate the hadronic final state in non-single diffractive pp collisions at various center-of-mass energies. They then applied the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model to form light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypernuclei from the final-state hadrons, imposing constraints on invariant mass and spatial coalescence radius. The model parameters (K factor, parj(1), parj(2), parj(3)) were fitted to STAR data for Xi+, Xi-, Lambda, and anti-Lambda in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Yield ratios of antiparticles to particles (pi-/pi+, K-/K+, anti-p/p, anti-Lambda/Lambda, anti-Xi-/Xi-, anti-Omega-/Omega-, anti-d/d, anti-He3/He3, and anti-hypertritium/hypertritium) were calculated at c.m. energies of 10, 17.3, 50, 100, 200, 1000, 7000, and 14000 GeV.
- The ratios saturate and gradually approach 1 after the c.m. energy exceeds 200 GeV, and tend to 1 when the c.m. energy is more than 1.0 TeV.
- The transverse momentum distributions peak around pT = 0.2 to 0.5 GeV/c for all particles studied.
- Average transverse momentum was studied at c.m. energies of 17.3, 200, 1000, 7000, and 14000 GeV, and increases with increasing c.m. energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the PACIAE+DCPC model to investigate light (anti)nuclei production in relativistic pp collisions (Wang et al., 2013) and Au-Au collisions (Chen et al., 2013). The energy dependence of antiparticle-to-particle ratios in pp collisions is found to be similar to that observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by the STAR and ALICE collaborations. The results enable systematic comparison of antimatter production mechanisms between pp and heavy-ion systems, and provide predictions for future measurements at higher LHC energies.
