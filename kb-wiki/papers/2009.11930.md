# Forward dijets in proton-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading order: the real corrections

> Using the CGC effective theory together with the hybrid factorisation, we study forward dijet production in proton-nucleus collisions beyond leading order. In this paper, we compute the "real" next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections, i.e. the radiative corrections associated with a three-parton final state, out of which only two are being measured. To that aim, we start by revisiting our previous results for the three-parton cross-section presented in our previous paper. After some reshuffling of terms, we deduce new expressions for these results, which not only look considerably simpler, but are also physically more transparent. We also correct several errors in this process. The real NLO corrections to inclusive dijet production are then obtained by integrating out the kinematics of any of the three final partons. We explicitly work out the interesting limits where the unmeasured parton is either a soft gluon, or the product of a collinear splitting. We find the expected results in both limits: the B-JIMWLK evolution of the leading-order dijet cross-section in the first case (soft gluon) and, respectively, the DGLAP evolution of the initial and final states in the second case (collinear splitting). The "virtual" NLO corrections to dijet production will be presented in a subsequent publication.

## Key claim

The paper computes the "real" next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to forward dijet production in proton-nucleus collisions within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, showing that in the soft-gluon limit the result reproduces the B-JIMWLK evolution of the leading-order cross-section, while in the collinear-splitting limit it reproduces the DGLAP evolution of the initial and final states, but only after correcting several errors in the authors' previous work (Iancu, 2018) and reorganizing the expressions for the three-parton cross-section.

## Method

The authors start from the leading-order (tree-level) cross-section for three-parton (trijet) production in the quark channel, previously derived using light-cone wavefunction formalism, and reorganize the expressions while correcting errors. They then obtain the real NLO corrections to inclusive dijet production by integrating out the kinematics of one of the three final partons, working explicitly in the limits where the unmeasured parton is either a soft gluon or the product of a collinear splitting. The calculations are performed in transverse coordinate space using eikonal Wilson lines to describe scattering off the nuclear target, with Fourier transforms relating coordinates to measured momenta.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order dijet cross-section (Eq. LOfinal) contains the factor \(2\alpha_s C_F [1+(1-\vartheta)^2] / [(2\pi)^6 \vartheta q^+]\), where \(\vartheta = k^+/q^+\) is the gluon splitting fraction and \(C_F = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)\).
* The saturation momentum scales as \(Q_s^2(x_g) \sim x_g^{-\lambda_s}\) with \(\lambda_s \simeq 0.20\) (citing Ducloue, 2019).
* The forward dijet kinematics corresponds to pseudorapidities \(\eta_1\) and \(\eta_2\) both positive and larger than 1, giving \(x_g \ll x_p < 1\).
* The longitudinal momentum fractions are given by \(x_p = (p_\perp/\sqrt{s}) e^{\eta_1} + (k_\perp/\sqrt{s}) e^{\eta_2}\) and \(x_g = (p_\perp/\sqrt{s}) e^{-\eta_1} + (k_\perp/\sqrt{s}) e^{-\eta_2}\).

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based hybrid factorization approach for forward dijet production beyond leading order, building on the authors' previous trijet calculation (Iancu, 2018) and correcting several errors in that work. It fits into the broader program of computing NLO impact factors for dilute-dense collisions, alongside similar efforts for single inclusive hadron production (Chirilli, 2011; Chirilli, 2012; Altinoluk, 2014), DIS structure functions (Balitsky, 2012; Beuf, 2016; Beuf, 2017), and exclusive processes (Boussarie, 2014; Boussarie, 2016). The results enable future phenomenological studies of saturation effects in dijet azimuthal correlations at RHIC and LHC, particularly when combined with the companion paper on virtual NLO corrections and the gluon channel.
