# Strangeness production within Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD)

> The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach consistently simulates the full evolution of a relativistic heavy-ion collision from the initial hard scatterings string formation through the dynamical deconfinement phase transition to the quark gluon plasma (QGP), to the hadronization and to subsequent interactions in the hadronic phase. The transport theoretical description of quarks and gluons is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons matched to reproduce recent lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for the fusion of quark-antiquark pairs or three quarks (antiquarks). Studying Pb+Pb reactions from 40 to 158 A GeV, we find that at most 40% of the collision energy is stored in the dynamics of the partons. This implies that a large fraction of non-partonic, i.e. hadronic or string-like matter, which can be viewed as a hadronic corona, is present in these reactions, thus neither hadronic nor purely partonic models can be employed to extract physical conclusions in comparing model results with data. On the other hand, comparing the PHSD results to those of the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) approach without the phase transition to QGP, we observe that the existence of the partonic phase has a sizeable influence on the transverse mass distribution of final kaons due to the repulsive partonic mean fields and initial partonic scattering. Furthermore, we find a significant effect of the QGP on the production of multi-strange antibaryons due to a slightly enhanced s+sbar pair production in the partonic phase from massive time-like gluon decay and to a more abundant formation of strange antibaryons in the hadronization process.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport approach shows that even in central Pb+Pb collisions at top SPS energy (158 A GeV), at most 40% of the collision energy is stored in partonic degrees of freedom, implying a large hadronic corona is always present; the partonic phase nevertheless has a sizeable influence on kaon transverse mass spectra and multi-strange antibaryon yields.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which simulates the full evolution of relativistic heavy-ion collisions from initial hard scatterings and string formation through a dynamical deconfinement phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma (QGP), then to hadronization and subsequent hadronic interactions. The partonic dynamics is based on the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) matched to lattice QCD results, with hadronization described by covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion or three-quark recombination. The authors compared PHSD results to those from the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) approach (which has no phase transition to QGP) and to experimental data from the NA49 and NA57 Collaborations for Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies from 20 to 158 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- At most 40% of the collision energy is stored in partonic dynamics for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV, decreasing to about 20% for very peripheral reactions (impact parameter 13 fm).
- The partonic volume shrinks by about a factor of three when stepping down in bombarding energy from 160 to 20 A GeV for central collisions (b = 1 fm).
- HSD and PHSD both underestimate the NA57 data for (Lambda + Sigma0)/N_wound by about 30% for Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV.
- PHSD calculations for (anti-Lambda + anti-Sigma0)/N_wound lie between the NA49 data and the NA57 data for Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV.

## Context

This work extends the PHSD approach, previously developed by Cassing and Bratkovskaya (2008) and Cassing (2009), to strangeness production at SPS energies (20-160 A GeV). It directly contradicts the "popular picture" that a partonic phase dominates the overlap region at top SPS energies, instead showing a large hadronic corona. The results support an earlier suggestion by Bratkovskaya et al. (2004) that the underestimation of kaon slopes by HSD (and also UrQMD) was a signature for missing partonic degrees of freedom. The work enables further studies of partonic signatures in dileptons (Linnyk, 2009), charm (Linnyk, 2008), and jets (Gallmeister, 2004), and points to the need for examining multi-strange antibaryons as a sensitive probe of QGP effects.
