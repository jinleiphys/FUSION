# Helicity of Quarks and Gluons at Small Bjorken $x$

> The proton spin puzzle is a longstanding problem in high-energy nuclear physics: how the proton spin distributes among the spin and orbital angular momenta of the quarks and gluons inside. Two of the unresolved pieces of the puzzle are the contributions to quark and gluon spins from the region of small Bjorken $x$. This dissertation fills the gap by constructing the evolution of these quantities into the small-$x$ region using a modified dipole formalism. The dominant contributions to the evolution equations resum powers of $α_s\ln^2(1/x)$, where $α_s$ is the strong coupling constant. In general, these evolution equations do not close. However, once the large-$N_c$ or large-$N_c\& N_f$ limit is taken, they turn into a system of linear integral equations that can be solved iteratively. At large $N_c$, the evolution equations are shown to be consistent with the gluon sector of the polarized DGLAP evolution in the small-$x$ limit. We numerically solve the equations in the large-$N_c$ and large-$N_c\& N_f$ limits and obtain the exponential growth in $\ln(1/x)$ for $N_f \leq 5$, with the intercept decreasing with $N_f$. For the large-$N_c$ limit, we have $α_h = 3.66$, which agrees up to the uncertainty with the earlier work by Bartels, Ermolaev and Ryskin. Furthermore, at $N_f=6$, the asymptotic form attains an oscillation in $\ln(1/x)$ on top of the exponential growth, with the period spanning several units of rapidity. Finally, parts of the single-logarithmic corrections to the small-$x$ helicity evolution is also derived, resumming powers of $α_s\ln(1/x)$. There, the effects of the unpolarized small-$x$ evolution and the running coupling are also included for consistency. The complete single-logarithmic corrections can be derived based on the framework established here. Altogether, these equations will provide the most precise small-$x$ helicity evolution to date.

## Key claim

This dissertation constructs the evolution of quark and gluon helicity distributions into the small-Bjorken-\(x\) region using a modified dipole formalism, but the evolution equations only close into a solvable system of linear integral equations after taking the large-\(N_c\) or large-\(N_c\& N_f\) limits, and the complete single-logarithmic corrections are not fully derived here, only partially constructed.

## Method

The author generalizes the dipole framework to incorporate helicity dependence, deriving small-\(x\) helicity evolution equations that resum powers of \(\alpha_s\ln^2(1/x)\) (double-logarithmic approximation). The equations are simplified by taking the large-\(N_c\) and large-\(N_c\& N_f\) limits, turning them into closed systems of linear integral equations solved iteratively. Parts of the single-logarithmic corrections (resumming powers of \(\alpha_s\ln(1/x)\)) are also derived, including effects of unpolarized small-\(x\) evolution and running coupling.

## Key numbers

* Large-\(N_c\) limit intercept: \(\alpha_h = 3.66\), which agrees within uncertainty with the earlier work by Bartels, Ermolaev and Ryskin (1996).
* For \(N_f \leq 5\), the numerical solutions yield exponential growth in \(\ln(1/x)\), with the intercept decreasing as \(N_f\) increases.
* At \(N_f = 6\), the asymptotic form exhibits an oscillation in \(\ln(1/x)\) superimposed on exponential growth, with the period spanning several units of rapidity.

## Context

This work extends the small-\(x\) helicity evolution program initiated by Kovchegov, Pitonyak, and Sievert (2016, 2017) and is consistent with the gluon sector of polarized DGLAP evolution in the small-\(x\) limit at large \(N_c\). It builds on the earlier flavor-singlet calculation by Bartels, Ermolaev and Ryskin (1996) and provides the foundation for deriving complete single-logarithmic corrections, which will yield the most precise small-\(x\) helicity evolution to date. The results are directly applicable to future analyses of polarized deep-inelastic scattering data, including projections for the Electron-Ion Collider.
