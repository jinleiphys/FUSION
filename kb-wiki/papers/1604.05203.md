# Three-flavor chiral effective model with four baryonic multiplets within the mirror assignment

> We study three-flavor octet baryons by using the so-called extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM). Within a quark-diquark picture, the requirement of a mirror assignment naturally leads to the consideration of four spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ baryon multiplets. A reduction of the Lagrangian to the two-flavor case leaves four doublets of nucleonic states which mix to form the experimentally observed states $N(939)$, $N(1440)$, $N(1535)$ and $N(1650)$. We determine the parameters of the nucleonic part of the Lagrangian from a fit to masses and decay properties of the aforementioned states. By tracing their masses when chiral symmetry is restored, we conclude that the pairs $N(939)$, $N(1535)$ and $N(1440)$, $N(1650)$ form chiral partners.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the four experimentally observed nucleon states N(939), N(1440), N(1535), and N(1650) can be described within the extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) using four baryonic multiplets with a mirror assignment, and that chiral symmetry restoration reveals the pairs N(939)/N(1535) and N(1440)/N(1650) as chiral partners. However, the fit produces three acceptable minima with almost equal quality, and the decay N(1535) → Nη cannot be described (a factor 10 too small) for any of them.

## Method

The authors extended the eLSM to three-flavor baryons by constructing four spin-1/2 baryon octets (two standard and two mirror-type) from a quark-diquark picture, then reduced the Lagrangian to the two-flavor case to obtain four nucleonic isodoublets. They performed a χ² fit of twelve model parameters to thirteen quantities: masses and decay widths of N(939), N(1440), N(1535), N(1650), the axial coupling constant of the nucleon, and lattice results for remaining axial coupling constants. The masses of the states were then traced as a function of the chiral condensate to identify chiral partners.

## Key numbers

- The mixing matrix for Minimum 1 shows the largest component of N(939) is B_N with coefficient -0.996, and N(1440) is B_M with coefficient 0.995.
- For Minimum 1, N(1535) has a dominant component from γ⁵B_M* with coefficient -0.867, and N(1650) from γ⁵B_N* with coefficient 0.869.
- The decay width N(1535) → Nη is a factor 10 too small compared to experiment for all three minima.
- The constituent quark mass scale is about 300 MeV, referenced as the effective mass of a dressed quark.

## Context

This work extends the eLSM from the two-flavor baryonic case studied by Gallas (2010) to three flavors, building on the mesonic three-flavor eLSM developed by Denis (2008) and others. The mirror assignment follows the approach of the mirror model (DeTar and Kunihiro, 1989) to allow chirally invariant mass terms. The results enable future full three-flavor fits to all octet baryons, which could inform studies of kaon scattering processes and the role of hyperons in compact star physics.
