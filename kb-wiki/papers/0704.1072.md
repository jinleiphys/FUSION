# A Faddeev Calculation for Pentaquark $Θ^+$ in Diquark Picture with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Type Interaction

> A Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev (BSF) calculation is performed for the pentaquark $Θ^+$ in the diquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek in which $Θ^+$ is a diquark-diquark-${\bar s}$ three-body system. Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is used to calculate the lowest order diagrams in the two-body scatterings of ${\bar s}D$ and $D D$. With the use of coupling constants determined from the meson sector, we find that ${\bar s}D$ interaction is attractive while $DD$ interaction is repulsive, and there is no bound $\frac 12^+$ pentaquark state. A bound pentaquark $Θ^+$ can only be obtained with unphysically strong vector mesonic coupling constants.

## Key claim
Using coupling constants determined from the meson sector, the authors find no bound pentaquark Θ⁺ with Jᴾ = ½⁺; a bound state in the Jᴾ = ½⁻ channel appears only with unphysically strong vector mesonic coupling constants far below the physical value.

## Method
The authors performed a Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev (BSF) calculation for the pentaquark Θ⁺ treated as a three-body system of two scalar, isoscalar, color 3̅ diquarks (D) and a strange antiquark (s̄), following the diquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek. They used the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute the lowest-order diagrams in the two-body scatterings of s̄D and DD, with the Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction scheme to convert the four-dimensional integral equations into three-dimensional ones. The two-body t-matrix elements were then inserted into the homogeneous Faddeev equation to search for a three-body bound state.

## Key numbers
- The physical value of the vector coupling constant G_v is -0.78 GeV⁻², determined from the ρ-ω mass difference.
- A s̄D bound state begins to appear only when G_v becomes less than -5 to -6 GeV⁻², far more negative than the physical value.
- A pentaquark bound state in the Jᴾ = ½⁻ channel appears when G_v becomes less than approximately -8.0 GeV⁻².
- The pentaquark binding energy E_B(5q) grows from 77 to 505 MeV as G_v decreases from -8.0 to -14.0 GeV⁻².
- Constituent quark masses used were M_u,d = 400 MeV and M_s = 600 MeV; diquark mass M_D = 600 MeV.
- The dipole form factor cutoff was Λ = 0.84 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the diquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) by providing a dynamical calculation using the NJL model, which had previously been successful in describing nucleon static properties and valence quark distributions (Mineo et al., 1999). The results contradict the existence of a Jᴾ = ½⁺ pentaquark within the physical parameter range, and suggest that if such a state exists, it would require dynamics beyond the NJL model with standard meson-sector couplings. The calculation enables future work to test alternative interaction mechanisms or different diquark configurations for exotic hadrons.
