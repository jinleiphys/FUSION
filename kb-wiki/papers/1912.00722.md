# The role of the chiral phase transition in modelling the kaon to pion ratio

> A sharp peak in the $K^+/π^+$ ratio in relativistic heavy-ion collision is discussed in the framework of the SU(3) Polyakov-loop extended NJL model with vector interaction. In the model, the $K^+/π^+$ ratio was calculated along the chiral phase transition line for different values of the vector coupling $g_V$. We showed that the value of the vector coupling had no significant effect on the $K^+/π^+$ behaviour.

# The role of the chiral phase transition in modelling the kaon to pion ratio

## Key claim
The sharp peak in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is weakly sensitive to the type of chiral phase transition (first order versus crossover) in the high-density region; replacing the first-order transition with a soft crossover only changes the peak height, not its existence. The peak structure is more sensitive to the slope of the phase transition curve and to matter properties such as the strange chemical potential.

## Method
The authors used the SU(3) Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with vector interaction and the Kobayashi–Maskawa–t'Hooft determinant term. They solved the mean-field gap equations for quark condensates, Polyakov loop fields, and quark densities, then computed meson masses via the Bethe-Salpeter equation at zero momentum. The $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio was calculated as a ratio of number densities along the chiral phase transition line, using the Cleymans parametrization to map collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ to the variable $T/\mu_B$ along the freeze-out line.

## Key numbers
- Current quark masses: $m_{0u} = m_{0d} = 5.5$ MeV, $m_{0s} = 0.131$ GeV
- Cut-off $\Lambda = 0.652$ GeV; couplings $g_D = 89.9$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $g_S = 4.3$ GeV$^{-5}$
- Critical temperature at $\mu_u = 0$: $T_c = 0.216$ GeV (higher than Lattice QCD prediction of $T_c = 154(9)$ MeV)
- Mott transition temperatures at zero chemical potential: $T^\pi_{Mott} = 0.232$ GeV, $T^K_{Mott} = 0.23$ GeV
- Pion chemical potential fixed at $\mu_\pi = 0.135$ GeV
- Vector couplings studied: $g_V = 0$, $g_V = 0.2 g_S$, and $g_V = 0.6 g_S$; at $g_V = 0.5 g_S$ the first-order transition disappears entirely
- The peak in $K^+/\pi^+$ appears at collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 7-10$ GeV for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the "horn" structure in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio observed by the NA49 and NA61/SHINE collaborations. It builds on the microscopic transport model of Bratkovskaya (2011) which showed that partial chiral symmetry restoration drives the rapid increase in the ratio at low energies. The authors' earlier work (Friesen, Kalinovsky, Toneev, 2019) established that the peak appears at low temperatures and high baryon chemical potential, and that setting the strange chemical potential to zero or imposing strangeness neutrality removes the peak structure. This study specifically tests whether the vector coupling strength (which controls the presence of a first-order phase transition) affects the ratio, finding that the peak persists regardless of the transition order. The results enable future work to focus on the phase transition slope and matter composition rather than the transition type when modelling the kaon-to-pion ratio.
