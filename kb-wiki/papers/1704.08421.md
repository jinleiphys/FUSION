# Behaviour of pf shell under RMF+BCS Description

> We have employed RMF+BCS (relativistic mean-field plus BCS) approach to study behaviour of pf shell with the help of ground state properties of even-even nuclei. Our present investigations include separation energies , deformations, single particle energies, wavefunction, potential as well etc density distribution. As per recent experiments showing neutron magicity at N = 32 for Ca isotopes, our results with mass dependent pairing indicate a shell closure at N = 32 in Ca isotopes and a more strong shell closure at N = 34 in proton deficient 48Si because of reorganization of neutron pf shell. In a similar manner, proton pf shell structure is more likely to produce shell closure at Z = 34 with a doubly magic character for 84,116Se. We have also included N = 40 isotones and Z = 40 isotopes for our study and predicted 60Ca and 68Ni as doubly magic nuclei out of which 60Ca is found near dripline of Ca and a potential candidate for future studies in the chain of Ca isotopes next to doubly magic 52Ca.

## Key claim

Using RMF+BCS calculations with mass-dependent pairing and the NL3* parameter set, the authors predict a shell closure at N = 32 in Ca isotopes and a stronger shell closure at N = 34 in proton-deficient 48Si, but find no evidence for a shell closure at N = 34 in 54Ca, contrary to some experimental suggestions.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with a BCS pairing treatment using a delta-force interaction, applying both constant pairing strength (V0 = 350 MeV fm3) and mass-dependent pairing strength (V0 = 25,500/A MeV fm3). They computed ground-state properties including two-neutron separation energies, single-particle energies, occupancies, and pairing energies for even-even nuclei in the pf shell region, using five different non-linear force parameter sets (TMA, NLSH, NL3, PK1, NL3*) and comparing results with experimental data and Skyrme calculations with tensor interaction.

## Key numbers

- Shell gap between 2p3/2 and 2p1/2 states in 52Ca: 1.90 MeV (NL3* with mass-dependent pairing)
- Shell gap between 2p1/2 and 1f5/2 states in 48Si: 3.15 MeV (NL3* with mass-dependent pairing)
- Shell gap between 2p3/2 and 2p1/2 states in 52Ca: 1.48 MeV (TMA) and 1.59 MeV (NLSH) with mass-dependent pairing
- Proton shell gap at Z = 34 in Se isotopes: 3.1 MeV for N = 50 and 4.1 MeV for N = 82
- 60Ca predicted as doubly magic nucleus near the neutron drip-line of Ca

## Context

This work extends previous RMF+BCS studies of pf-shell nuclei (Saxena 2015, Saxena 2016) and responds to experimental evidence for N = 32 magicity in Ca isotopes from high-precision mass measurements at ISOLTRAP/CERN (Wienholtz 2013) and spectroscopic studies at RIKEN (Stepp 2015). The results contradict the suggestion of N = 34 magicity in 54Ca from those same experiments, while supporting the N = 32 shell closure in 52Ca. The predictions for 60Ca and 48Si as doubly magic nuclei provide candidates for future experimental studies near the neutron drip-line.
