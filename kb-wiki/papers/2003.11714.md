# Description of the asymmetric to symmetric fission transition in the neutron-deficient Thoriums -- Role of the tensor force

> In the present study, we have investigated the impact of the tensor force on fission paths, in particular the symmetric and asymmetric barriers in 230 Th, 226 Th, 222 Th and 216 Th isotopes which display an asymmetric to symmetric fission transition. This analysis has been performed within the HFB approach with (Q 20 ,Q 30 ,Q 40 ) as collective variable constraints, using the D1ST2a Gogny+tensor term interaction and comparing to the standard D1S Gogny interaction results. The effects from the tensor term on the potential energy surface landscape, and especially on barrier heights and its topology by opening a new valley in agreement with experimental data, are found to be crucial in the description of exotic actinide fission. We conclude that a tensor term should be integrated to the long range part of the effective interaction for a better description of the fission.

## Key claim

The tensor term in the D1ST2a Gogny interaction, added perturbatively to the standard D1S interaction, is crucial for describing the asymmetric-to-symmetric fission transition in neutron-deficient Thorium isotopes, specifically by reducing the energy difference between symmetric and asymmetric second barriers and making the symmetric path energetically competitive in the lightest isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with constraints on the axially symmetric multipole moments Q20, Q30, and Q40, using both the standard D1S Gogny interaction and the D1ST2a interaction which adds a perturbative long-range tensor term. They computed one-dimensional fission paths (symmetric and asymmetric) and two-dimensional potential energy surfaces for the even-even isotopes 216Th, 222Th, 226Th, and 230Th, analyzing the contributions of the mean-field, pairing, and tensor terms to the total HFB energy.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between symmetric and asymmetric second hump heights in 216Th is approximately 1.5 MeV with D1S, and tends to zero with D1ST2a, rendering the symmetric path competitive.
- In 232Th, the asymmetric second hump height is about 9.3 MeV with D1S, compared to the symmetric height of about 18.8 MeV.
- The tensor contribution to the HFB energy at the first barrier (ETS) ranges from 5.522 MeV in 216Th to 6.649 MeV in 226Th.
- The HFB energy difference ΔE_HFB between D1ST2a and D1S at the ground state ranges from 4.310 MeV in 216Th to 7.968 MeV in 230Th.
- The mean-field energy difference ΔE_MF at sphericity is -6.286 MeV for 216Th and -6.433 MeV for 230Th, while the pairing energy difference ΔE_pair at sphericity is 8.056 MeV for 216Th and 7.310 MeV for 230Th.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic description of fission using Gogny interactions, building on the D1S parameterization developed by the Orsay group (Gogny et al., 1980s) and the perturbative tensor addition proposed by Anguiano et al. (2010s). The study is motivated by SOFIA collaboration experiments (Martin, Pellereau, 2014-2015) that revealed a symmetric bimodal fission mode in neutron-deficient Thorium isotopes, characterized by a decrease in average neutron multiplicity. The results suggest that including a tensor term in the effective interaction is necessary to describe exotic actinide fission and opens the way for future dynamical studies using the Time-Dependent Generator Coordinate Method or Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov.
