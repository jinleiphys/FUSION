# Signature of the Fragmentation of a Color Flux Tube

> The production of quark-antiquark pairs along a color flux tube precedes the fragmentation of the tube. Because of local conservation laws, the production of a $q$-$\bar q$ pair will lead to correlations of adjacently produced mesons (mostly pions). Adjacently produced mesons however can be signalled by their rapidity difference $Δy$ falling within the window of $|Δy | $$\lesssim$$ 1/(dN/dy)$, on account of the space-time-rapidity ordering of produced mesons in a flux tube fragmentation. Therefore, the local conservation laws of momentum, charge, and flavor will lead to a suppression of angular correlation function $dN/(dΔϕ\, dΔy)$ for two mesons with opposite charges or strangeness on the near side at $(Δϕ, Δy)$$ \sim$0, but an enhanced correlation on the back-to-back, away side at $Δϕ$$\sim$$ π$, within the window of $|Δy |$$\lesssim$$ 1/(dN/dy)$. These properties can be used as signatures for the fragmentation of a color flux tube. The gross features of the signature of flux tube fragmentation for two oppositely charged mesons are qualitatively consistent with the STAR and NA61/SHINE angular correlation data for two hadrons with opposite charges in the low-$p_T$ region in high-energy $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

The local conservation laws of momentum, charge, and flavor at quark-antiquark production vertices in a color flux tube produce a suppression of the angular correlation function for two mesons with opposite charges or strangeness on the near side at (Δφ, Δy)∼0, but an enhanced correlation on the back-to-back away side at Δφ∼π, within the rapidity window |Δy|≲1/(dN/dy); these signatures are qualitatively consistent with STAR and NA61/SHINE data for oppositely charged hadrons in the low-pT region in high-energy pp collisions.

## Method

The author derives the two-meson angular correlation function dN/(dΔφ dΔy) from first principles by modeling flux-tube fragmentation as the Schwinger pair-production mechanism of ordered q-anti-q pairs along the tube, with subsequent formation of adjacent mesons via confining interactions. The analysis separates the transverse momentum distributions for adjacent mesons (where the quark of one meson and the antiquark of the other are produced at the same vertex, subject to local momentum conservation) from those for nonadjacent mesons (where constituents are produced independently), and uses space-time-rapidity ordering to identify adjacent pairs by their rapidity difference |Δy| falling within 1/(dN/dy).

## Key numbers

* For a flux-tube radius of 0.6 fm and string tension κ=1 GeV/fm, the transverse momentum width from the tube geometry is σ_u=0.33 GeV/c, the width from the Schwinger mechanism is σ_q=0.25 GeV/c, and the combined width is σ=0.414 GeV/c.
* The rapidity window for identifying adjacently produced mesons is |Δy|≲1/(dN/dy), where dN/dy is the rapidity density of produced mesons.
* The normalized transverse distribution for nonadjacent mesons is dN_T^N/dΔφ = 1/(8π^2), independent of the azimuthal angle difference Δφ.

## Context

This work provides the first microscopic derivation of the two-hadron angular correlation signature for flux-tube fragmentation, extending earlier phenomenological treatments by the STAR Collaboration (2006) that associated the "soft" component in pp collisions with a one-dimensional Gaussian at Δη∼0 independent of Δφ. The results enable experimental separation of flux-tube fragmentation from hard-scattering (minijet) processes in pp collisions, allowing the transverse momentum boundary p_Tb(√s_NN) between the two mechanisms to be mapped as a function of collision energy, with implications for understanding thermalization and jet quenching in nucleus-nucleus collisions.
