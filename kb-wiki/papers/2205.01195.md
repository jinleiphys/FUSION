# Exotic heavy hadrons with a three-body nature

> In this talk we present a summary of our latest results on the investigation of three-body systems with explicit/hidden charm and with explicit/hidden strangeness. To be more concrete, in case of explicit strangeness quantum number, we pay attention on the $K D D $ and $K D\bar D^*$ systems, where, in the former, a charm $+2$, isospin $1/2$ and strangeness $+1$ state is obtained with a mass around 4140 MeV, while in the latter, a $K^*$ state, with hidden charm, and a mass close to 4307 MeV is found. In case of hidden strangeness, the $DK\bar K$ system is studied, while in the hidden charm and no strangeness quantum numbers sector, the $ND\bar D^*$ system is investigated. In the former a meson $D$ with mass around 2900 MeV is found to be generated, while in the latter several $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ states with hidden charm are obtained with masses about $4600$ MeV. All these states constitute predictions of our model and future experimental confirmation of them would be relevant to elucidate the properties of the strong interaction in the presence of heavy quarks.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several exotic heavy hadrons (mesons and baryons) can be understood as three-body molecular states generated by solving Faddeev equations, but these states are predictions that have not yet been confirmed experimentally; for example, the Belle collaboration conducted a preliminary search for the charm +2 state near 4100 MeV and found no clear signal, indicating that more precise data are needed.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for three-body systems using as kernel the two-body t-matrices obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in its on-shell factorization form. The kernel was determined from effective Lagrangians based on chiral and heavy quark symmetries. The systems studied were KDD, KD\(\bar D^*\), DK\(\bar K\), and ND\(\bar D^*\), with the two-body subsystems generating known hadronic molecules such as \(D^*_{s0}(2317)\), \(X(3872)\), \(Z_c(3900)\), and \(f_0(980)\).

## Key numbers

- A state with charm +2, isospin 1/2, strangeness +1, and mass around 4140 MeV is found in the KDD system; its total width is estimated at 2-3 MeV.
- A \(K^*\) state with hidden charm and mass close to 4307 MeV is found in the KD\(\bar D^*\) system; its width is around 18 MeV when the \(Z_c(3900)\) width of about 30 MeV is included.
- A D meson with mass around 2900 MeV and width of 55 MeV is found in the DK\(\bar K\) system.
- Several \(N^*\) and \(\Delta^*\) states with hidden charm are obtained with masses about 4400-4600 MeV in the ND\(\bar D^*\) system.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of exotic hadrons to three-body systems, building on prior studies that used Faddeev equations to generate mesons and baryons with a molecular nature (Martinez Torres 2007, Khemchandani 2008, Martinez Torres 2010). The results are relevant to ongoing experimental searches by LHCb, BESIII, and Belle for multi-quark states with hidden charm and strangeness, and the predicted states could be confirmed through specific decay channels such as \(J/\psi\pi K\) for the \(K^*(4307)\) or \(D^*_{s0}(2317)\bar K\) for the D(2900).
