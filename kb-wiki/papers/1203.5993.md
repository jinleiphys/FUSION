# Local Projections of Low-Momentum Potentials

> Nuclear interactions evolved via renormalization group methods to lower resolution become increasingly non-local (off-diagonal in coordinate space) as they are softened. This inhibits both the development of intuition about the interactions and their use with some methods for solving the quantum many-body problem. By applying "local projections", a softened interaction can be reduced to a local effective interaction plus a non-local residual interaction. At the two-body level, a local projection after similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution manifests the elimination of short-range repulsive cores and the flow toward universal low-momentum interactions. The SRG residual interaction is found to be relatively weak at low energy, which motivates a perturbative treatment.

## Key claim

By applying a "local projection" to similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved nuclear interactions, the softened non-local potential can be separated into a local effective interaction plus a non-local residual interaction; the residual interaction is relatively weak at low energy, which motivates a perturbative treatment, but the projection is not unique and the authors provide no evidence that it is an optimal separation for calculations.

## Method

The authors define a local projection functional that integrates over the non-locality of a two-body potential in each partial wave, producing a local function of the radial coordinate. They apply this projection to SRG-evolved potentials starting from the Argonne v18 local potential (Wiringa, 1995) and the Entem-Machleidt N3LO 500 MeV chiral EFT potential (Entem and Machleidt, 2003), evolving from lambda = infinity (unevolved) to lambda = 1.6 fm^-1. The residual non-local interaction is then tested in perturbation theory using the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) for phase shifts and ordinary perturbation theory for the deuteron binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The unevolved N3LO potential's local projection shows an overall oscillation with wavelength of about 2.5 fm, consistent with a momentum cutoff of 500 MeV.
- The initial N3LO potential's short-range contributions peak in the S-waves at about 140 MeV for the singlet channel and 200 MeV for the triplet channel.
- The Argonne v18 S-wave peaks are off scale at over 2 GeV in the unevolved projection.
- By lambda = 2 fm^-1, both projected potentials are completely attractive except for small long-range oscillations.
- At lambda = 1.6 fm^-1, the second-order perturbative correction brings the deuteron binding energy for both initial potentials within 100 keV of the exact result.
- The first-order DWBA correction restores agreement with exact phase shifts below 100 MeV to the one percent level for the evolved Argonne v18 potential.

## Context

This work extends the study of SRG-evolved nuclear interactions (Bogner, 2009) by providing the first direct coordinate-space visualization of how the repulsive core is eliminated during evolution, which had previously only been inferred. The local projection technique enables comparison of SRG-evolved potentials from different starting points (Argonne v18 and chiral EFT) and demonstrates their flow toward universal low-momentum interactions in coordinate space. The results enable future use of softened interactions with quantum Monte Carlo methods (such as Green's function Monte Carlo and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo) that require local potentials, and suggest that the non-local residual can be treated perturbatively in many-body calculations.
