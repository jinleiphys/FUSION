# Solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space and applications to electromagnetic form factors

> We present a new method for solving the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space. It is based on the Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and on subsequent projection of the equation on the light-front plane. The method is valid for any kernel given by the irreducible Feynman graphs and for systems of spinless particles or fermions. The Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in Minkowski space are obtained. The electromagnetic form factors are computed and compared to the Euclidean results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for solving the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation directly in Minkowski space, based on the Nakanishi integral representation combined with a light-front projection that eliminates the singularities inherent in the Minkowski-space amplitude. The method is claimed to be valid for any kernel given by irreducible Feynman graphs and for systems of spinless particles or fermions, but the numerical demonstrations are limited to specific ladder and cross-ladder kernels and to bound states with zero total angular momentum.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is first expressed via the Nakanishi integral representation, which introduces a non-singular weight function g(γ,z). A light-front projection is then applied to both sides of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, replacing the relative momentum k with k + (ω/ω·p)β and integrating over β in infinite limits, which removes the singularities of the amplitude. This yields a non-singular integral equation for the weight function g(γ,z), which is solved numerically by expanding g on a bicubic spline basis. For the two-fermion case, the amplitude is decomposed into four independent spin structures, and a regularization factor η(k,p) is introduced to smooth the kernel before applying the same light-front projection technique.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kusaka and Williams (1995) and Kusaka, Simpson, and Williams (1997) by adding a light-front projection to the Nakanishi representation method, which removes the singularities that made previous Minkowski-space solutions difficult. The method enables direct calculation of electromagnetic form factors without the static approximation required by Euclidean-space solutions, which becomes increasingly inaccurate at higher momentum transfer. The results for binding energies with ladder and cross-ladder kernels are compared to those from Light-Front Dynamics calculations by Mangin-Brinet and Carbonell (2000) and by Carbonell and Karmanov (2004), showing close agreement, and the cross-ladder contribution is found to be strongly attractive but still only a small part of the total correction from all irreducible diagrams as computed by Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1996).
