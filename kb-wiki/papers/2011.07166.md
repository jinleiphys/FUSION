# Summary of the NuSTEC Workshop on Neutrino-Nucleus Pion Production in the Resonance Region

> The NuSTEC workshop held at the University of Pittsburgh in October 2019 brought theorists and experimentalists together to discuss the state of modeling and measurements related to pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering in the kinematic region where pions are produced through both resonant and non-resonant mechanisms. Modeling of this region is of critical importance to the current and future accelerator- and atmospheric-based neutrino oscillation experiments. For the benefit of the community, links to the presentations are accompanied by annotations from the speakers highlighting significant points made during the presentations and resulting discussions.

# Summary of the NuSTEC Workshop on Neutrino-Nucleus Pion Production in the Resonance Region

## Key claim
The workshop concluded that over 50% of expected DUNE events come from the poorly studied higher-W resonance region (above the Delta resonance), and that understanding of electroproduction data is not sufficient for present needs. While the Delta P33(1232) resonance is the focus of many studies, the kinematic region with mass above the Delta is an unknown mixture of higher resonances, non-resonant pion production, and the start of deep-inelastic scattering.

## Method
The workshop brought together theorists and experimentalists for a 4-day meeting at the University of Pittsburgh in October 2019, featuring presentations on the impact of pion production modeling in neutrino experiments, existing models and new model development, implementation in neutrino event generators, and relevant neutrino, electron, and pion scattering measurements. Several hours per day were dedicated to in-depth questioning and discussions, with links to presentations accompanied by annotations from the speakers highlighting significant points.

## Key numbers
- Over 50% of expected DUNE events come from the poorly studied higher-W regime above the Delta resonance
- T2K and BNB experiments have neutrino beams with mean energy below 1 GeV, where pion production contributes up to about 25% of interactions
- T2K reduced flux systematic uncertainty to 8%-12% using NA61 data
- The SBND near detector will collect seven million nu_mu charged current interactions and 50 thousand nu_e charged current interactions in three years
- For pion absorption on nuclei, the pp final state accounts for only 23% of total absorption, 2pn is 35%, 3p is 13%, and 3pn is 9%
- The Delta-nucleus interaction was parameterized as (V+iW)/T_R = a + b(T_pi/T_R) + c(T_pi/T_R)^2 with T_R=180 MeV, a=0.44-0.77i, b=-11.24+1.24i, c=0.47-0.47i
- A unified model predicted the squared charge radius of the axial pseudo-vector form factor to be r_A^2 = 0.29 +/- 0.03 fm^2, with the dipole approximation leading to 5-10% over-estimation of total cross section

## Context
This workshop summary extends the prior year's NuSTEC workshop on Shallow and Deep Inelastic Scattering (2018), blending seamlessly with that work to cover the full kinematic range. The workshop addressed tensions between MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA pion production results, with GiBUU calculations (Mosel, 2015; Mosel, 2017) showing that MiniBooNE data for pion production are likely incorrect in both absolute cross section and kinetic energy distribution. The GiBUU generator, built on quantum-kinetic transport theory, successfully described MINERvA and T2K data but cannot describe coherent pion production. The workshop identified that the next few years should deliver breakthroughs from the SBN program at Fermilab, and that future work must address missing nuclear effects in generators, as evidenced by tuned GENIE models failing to simultaneously describe CC1pi+ and CC1pi0 measurements from MINERvA.
