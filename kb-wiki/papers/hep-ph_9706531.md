# Magnetic moments of the SU(3) decuplet baryons in the chiral quark-soliton model

> Magnetic moments of baryons are studied within the chiral quark soliton model with special emphasis on the decuplet of baryons. The model is used to identify all symmetry breaking terms proportional to $m_{\rm s}$. Sum rules for the magnetic moments are derived. A ``model-independent'' analysis of the symmetry breaking terms is performed and finally model calculations are presented, which show the importance of the rotational $1/N_{\rm c}$ corrections for cranking of the soliton.

## Key claim

The chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM) can describe decuplet baryon magnetic moments with accuracy comparable to the octet sector, but only when rotational \(1/N_{\rm c}\) corrections are included; the "model-independent" analysis leaves one free parameter \(p\) unconstrained by octet data, making the decuplet predictions dependent on this parameter.

## Method

The authors compute magnetic moments of the SU(3) decuplet baryons within the chiral quark-soliton model (semibosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model), treating baryons as \(N_{\rm c}\) valence quarks coupled to a polarized Dirac sea in a hedgehog background field. They derive the collective magnetic moment operator including chiral-limit terms, rotational \(1/N_{\rm c}\) corrections, and linear \(m_{\rm s}\) symmetry-breaking terms, then sandwich it between octet and decuplet wave functions that include mixing with higher SU(3) representations. A "model-independent" analysis treats the dynamical coefficients \(w_i\) as free parameters fitted to octet magnetic moments, while full model calculations use selfconsistent solitonic profile functions for constituent quark masses \(M = 370, 400, 420, 450\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- \(\mu_{\Delta^{++}} = (4.52 \pm 0.50)\ \mu_{\rm N}\) (experimental, from pion bremsstrahlung)
- \(\mu_{\Omega^{-}} = (-1.94 \pm 0.17 \pm 0.14)\ \mu_{\rm N}\) (experimental, E756 Collaboration)
- World average for \(\Omega^{-}\): \(\mu_{\Omega^{-}} = (-2.02 \pm 0.05)\ \mu_{\rm N}\) (Particle Data Group '96)
- \(\mu_{\Delta^{++}}\) world average: \(3.5 - 7.5\ \mu_{\rm N}\) (much less constrained)
- Chiral-limit model predictions: \(\mu_{\Delta^{++}} = 4.92\ \mu_{\rm N}\), \(\mu_{\Omega^{-}} = -2.46\ \mu_{\rm N}\)
- Full model predictions (with \(1/N_{\rm c}\) corrections): \(\mu_{\Delta^{++}} = 4.73\ \mu_{\rm N}\), \(\mu_{\Omega^{-}} = -2.27\ \mu_{\rm N}\)
- Sum rule: \(\Delta^{++} - \Omega^{-} = \frac{3}{4}(2{\rm p} + {\rm n} + \Sigma^{+} - \Sigma^{-} - \Xi^{0} - 2\Xi^{-})\); left side = 6.43, right side = 7.38 (using experimental octet values)
- Octet fit parameters: \(v = -0.268\), \(w = 0.060\) (from mean of three expressions)
- Model prediction for \(w\): \(w^{\rm NJL} = 0.029\) (for \(M = 420\) MeV), a factor of 2 smaller than the "model-independent" value
- Mixing parameters from mass splittings: \(K_1/I_1 = 0.51\), \(K_2/I_2 = 0.52\)
- \(\Sigma = 48\) MeV, \(m_{\rm s} = 180\) MeV, \(\overline{m} = 6\) MeV, \(\sigma = 2.67\)
- \(M_{\Sigma} - M_{\Lambda} = 77\) MeV, \(M_{\Xi} - M_{\rm N} = 379\) MeV
- Free parameter range from \(I_2 \sim 0.4-0.8\) fm: \(p = 0.17 - 0.22\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of octet magnetic moments in the χQSM (Kim, Praszałowicz, Goeke, 1996) to the decuplet, motivated by recent measurements of \(\mu_{\Delta^{++}}\) and \(\mu_{\Omega^{-}}\). It improves upon the chiral bag model approach of Hong and Brown (1996) by including rotational \(1/N_{\rm c}\) corrections and wave-function mixing effects. The results differ quantitatively from those of Wakamatsu and Kaya (1996), who used the same model but omitted some rotational corrections. The "model-independent" analysis provides sum rules that can be tested by future lattice QCD calculations or more precise experimental measurements of decuplet magnetic moments.
