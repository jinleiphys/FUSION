# Deciphering nonfemtoscopic two-pion correlations in $p+p$ collisions with simple analytical models

> A simple model of nonfemtoscopic particle correlations in proton-proton collisions is proposed. The model takes into account correlations induced by the conservation laws as well as correlations induced by minijets. It reproduces well the two-pion nonfemtoscopic correlations of like-sign and unlike-sign pions in proton-proton collision events at $\sqrt{s} = 900$ GeV analyzed by the ALICE Collaboration. We also argue that similar nonfemtoscopic correlations can appear in the hydrodynamic picture with event-by-event fluctuating nonsymmetric initial conditions that are typically associated with nonzero higher-order flow harmonics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple analytical model that reproduces the nonfemtoscopic two-pion correlations observed in proton-proton collisions at 900 GeV, but the authors caution that similar correlation shapes could also arise from hydrodynamic models with fluctuating initial conditions, meaning the true origin of these correlations remains ambiguous.

## Method

The authors construct a model where N distinguishable pions are produced with correlations from two sources: transverse momentum conservation (enforced via a delta function) and minijet-like clustering (modeled by a Gaussian function Q(p_i,p_j) = exp(-(p_i-p_j)^2/alpha^2) that correlates particles within the same jet). They compute the two-particle correlation function C_NF by separating contributions from pairs belonging to the same minijet versus different minijets, then compare to ALICE data for unlike-sign pions and to PHOJET event-generator simulations for like-sign pions at sqrt(s)=900 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The model fits ALICE data for p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=900 GeV with parameters M=50, T_T=alpha=0.65 GeV, yielding mean transverse momentum <p_T> ~ 0.58 GeV.
- Fitted N values differ: N=20 for like-sign pion pairs and N=11 for unlike-sign pion pairs.
- Auxiliary factors Lambda(|p_T|) range from 0.95 to 0.99 for like-sign pions and from 0.82 to 0.93 for unlike-sign pions across the five p_T bins.
- The correlation function C_NF for the toy hydrodynamic fluctuation model scales as exp(-beta^4/(2(alpha^2+beta^2)) q_T^2), showing a decreasing exponential with q_T.

## Context

This work extends the ALICE Collaboration's (2010) observation that event generators like PHOJET reproduce unlike-sign pion correlations but may misrepresent the correlation baseline for identical pions. The authors show that minijet and conservation-law correlations can be captured analytically, but they also argue that hydrodynamic models with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions (similar to those producing higher flow harmonics in heavy-ion collisions) could produce qualitatively similar nonfemtoscopic correlations. This ambiguity means that extracting femtoscopic radii from p+p collisions depends on which production mechanism is assumed, and the authors suggest that future analyses should fit dynamical models directly to total correlation functions rather than relying on a separate baseline subtraction.
