# Electrical conductivity of a warm neutron star crust in magnetic fields

> We study the electrical conductivity of finite-temperature crust of a warm compact star which may be formed in the aftermath of a supernova explosion or a binary neutron star merger as well as when a cold neutron star is heated by accretion of material from a companion. We focus on the temperature-density regime where plasma is in the liquid state and, therefore, the conductivity is dominated by the electron scattering off correlated nuclei. The dynamical screening of this interaction is implemented in terms of the polarization tensor computed in the hard-thermal-loop effective field theory of QED plasma. The correlations of the background ionic component are accounted for via a structure factor derived from Monte Carlo simulations of one-component plasma. With this input we solve the Boltzmann kinetic equation in relaxation time approximation taking into account the anisotropy of transport due to the magnetic field. The electrical conductivity tensor is studied numerically as a function of temperature and density for carbon and iron nuclei as well as density-dependent composition of zero-temperature dense matter in weak equilibrium with electrons. We also provide accurate fit formulas to our numerical results as well as supplemental tables which can be used in dissipative magneto-hydrodynamics simulations of warm compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper computes the electrical conductivity tensor of warm neutron star crust matter in the liquid state, including dynamical screening via hard-thermal-loop QED and ionic correlations via Monte Carlo structure factors, but it explicitly restricts to non-quantizing magnetic fields below approximately 10^14 G where Landau quantization of electron trajectories is unimportant.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann kinetic equation for electrons in the relaxation time approximation, using electron-ion scattering as the dominant conduction mechanism. The scattering matrix elements incorporate dynamical screening of the photon propagator via the polarization tensor computed in hard-thermal-loop effective field theory, and ionic correlations are included through a structure factor derived from Monte Carlo simulations of a one-component plasma. The electrical conductivity tensor is then obtained as a function of temperature, density, and magnetic field strength for carbon and iron nuclei, as well as for a density-dependent composition of zero-temperature matter in weak equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb plasma parameter is given by Gamma = 22.73 * (Z^2 / T_6) * (rho_6 / A)^(1/3), where T_6 is temperature in units of 10^6 K and rho_6 is density in units of 10^6 g cm^-3.
- Ions form a liquid for Gamma values up to Gamma_m approximately 160, above which they solidify into a lattice.
- The critical magnetic field above which Landau quantization becomes important is B approximately 10^14 G.
- The nuclear charge radius used in the form factor is r_c = 1.15 * A^(1/3) fm.
- In the degenerate limit, the zero-field conductivity reduces to the Drude formula sigma = n_e e^2 tau_F / epsilon_F, where tau_F is the relaxation time at the Fermi energy and epsilon_F is the Fermi energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of electrical conductivity in cold neutron star crusts by Flowers and Itoh (1976) and by Itoh and collaborators (1983, 1984, 1993) to the warm, finite-temperature regime relevant for proto-neutron stars, binary neutron star mergers, and accreting neutron stars. The results provide input for dissipative magneto-hydrodynamics simulations of warm compact stars, such as those by Palenzuela (2013) and Dionysopoulou (2015), and enable studies of magnetic field decay and internal heating in these systems.
