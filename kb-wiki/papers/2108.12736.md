# Quark condensate and chiral symmetry restoration in neutron stars

> Based on an equivparticle model, we investigate the in-medium quark condensate in neutron stars. Carrying out a Taylor expansion of the nuclear binding energy to the order of $ρ^3$, we obtain a series of EOSs for neutron star matter, which are confronted with the latest nuclear and astrophysical constraints. The in-medium quark condensate is then extracted from the constrained properties of neutron star matter, which decreases non-linearly with density. However, the chiral symmetry is only partially restored with non-vanishing quark condensates, which may vanish at a density that is out of reach for neutron stars.

## Key claim

The in-medium quark condensate in neutron stars decreases non-linearly with density, but chiral symmetry is only partially restored because the quark condensate remains non-zero at densities reachable inside neutron stars; it may vanish only at densities beyond those found in neutron stars.

## Method

The authors used an equivparticle model to extract the in-medium quark condensate from neutron star matter. They first obtained a series of equations of state (EOSs) for neutron star matter by performing a Taylor expansion of the nuclear binding energy to order ρ³, with parameters including incompressibility K, skewness J, symmetry energy slope L, curvature K_sym, and skewness J_sym. These EOSs were then confronted with nuclear constraints (saturation properties) and astrophysical constraints (pulsar masses, radii, and tidal deformability from GW170817). For EOSs consistent with these constraints, the in-medium quark condensate was extracted by equating the energy density from the equivparticle model to the nuclear matter energy density, then using the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation to obtain the relative quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- The chiral restoration density n* is 0.985 fm⁻³, obtained from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation with m_π ≈ 140 MeV, f_π ≈ 93.2 MeV, and m_0 = 7.5 MeV.
- For criterion (a) (weaker constraints), the symmetry energy at twice saturation density is S(2ρ₀) ≈ 40.1 to 65.6 MeV.
- For criterion (b) (stronger constraints), the symmetry energy at twice saturation density is S(2ρ₀) ≈ 36.4 to 56.1 MeV.
- The maximum sound velocity for EOSs meeting criterion (b) reaches v_max ≈ c (speed of light) in some cases.
- The maximum central density for neutron stars meeting criterion (b) is ρ_TOV ≲ 1 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium quark condensate in neutron stars, which was recently carried out by Minamikawa (2021) using a parity doublet model for nuclear matter and an NJL model for quark matter with an interpolation at intermediate densities. The present study uses an equivparticle model that bypasses the difficulty of taking a derivative of the system energy density with respect to the quark current mass. The results are consistent with the findings of Minamikawa (2021) and suggest that a full transformation from hadronic matter into a Fermi gas of quarks is unlikely inside neutron stars, as the density required for chiral symmetry restoration is out of reach.
