# Fission of super-heavy nuclei: Fragment mass distributions and their dependence on excitation energy

> The mass and total kinetic energy distributions of the fission fragments in the fission of even-even isotopes of superheavy elements from Hs (Z=108) to Og (Z=118) are estimated using a pre-scission point model. We restrict to nuclei for which spontaneous fission has been experimentally observed. The potential energy surfaces are calculated with Strutinsky's shell correction procedure. The parametrization of the nuclear shapes is based on Cassini ovals. For the just before scission configuration we fix $α$=0.98, what corresponds to $r_{neck}\approx 2$ fm, and take into account another four deformation parameters: $α_1,α_3,α_4,α_6$. The fragment-mass distributions are estimated supposing they are due to thermal fluctuations in the mass asymmetry degree of freedom just before scission. The influence of the excitation energy of the fissioning system on these distributions is studied. The distributions of the total kinetic energy (TKE) of the fragments are also calculated (in the point-charge approximation). In Hs, Ds and Cn isotopes a transition from symmetric to asymmetric fission is predicted with increasing neutron number N (at N$\approx$168). Super-symmetric fission ocurs at N$\approx$160. When the excitation energy increases from 0 to 30 MeV, the peaks (one or two) of the mass distributions become only slightly wider. The first two moments of the TKE distributions are displayed as a function of the mass number A of the fissioning nucleus. A slow decrease of the average energy and a minimum of the width (at N$\approx$162) is found.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that for superheavy nuclei from Hs (Z=108) to Og (Z=118), the mass distributions of fission fragments are quite robust with respect to excitation energy: when the excitation energy increases from 0 to 30 MeV, the peaks of the mass distributions become only slightly wider, meaning the symmetric-to-asymmetric transition features are not washed out at moderate excitation.

## Method

The authors use a pre-scission point model in which nuclear shapes just before scission are described by generalized Cassini ovals with five deformation parameters (α=0.98 fixed, plus α₁, α₃, α₄, α₆). The potential energy surfaces are calculated with Strutinsky's macroscopic-microscopic shell correction procedure, and the fragment mass distributions are obtained from Boltzmann factors for thermal fluctuations in the mass asymmetry degree of freedom at the scission point. The excitation energy dependence is incorporated using a generalization of the Strutinsky shell correction to finite temperature, keeping the same entropy in the original and averaged quantities so that the shell correction decreases exponentially with excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Transition from symmetric to asymmetric fission in Hs, Ds and Cn isotopes occurs at neutron number N ≈ 168.
- "Super-symmetric" fission (extremely narrow symmetric mass distributions with full width at half maximum between 5 and 8 amu) occurs at N ≈ 160.
- Excitation energies studied range from 0 to 30 MeV; no noticeable difference between E* = 0 and 15 MeV, and very small difference between E* = 15 and 30 MeV.
- The damping parameter for the exponential decay of shell correction with excitation energy is E_d = 40 MeV at scission.
- A minimum of the width of the total kinetic energy distribution is found at N ≈ 162 for Hs and Ds isotopes.
- The average total kinetic energy shows a slow decrease with increasing mass number A of the fissioning nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous scission point model (Carjan et al., 2015; Carjan et al., 2017) which had been applied to heavy actinides and to long series of isotopes with Z from 110 to 126. The present study focuses specifically on nuclei for which spontaneous fission has been experimentally detected (isotopes of Hs, Ds, Cn, Fl, Lv, and Og), providing predictions for upcoming experiments at facilities such as the SHE Factory at JINR-Dubna, HIAF+CUBE at ANU-Canberra, and the tandem accelerator at JAEA-Tokai. The results enable comparison with existing experimental indications that certain Cn and Fl isotopes may fission asymmetrically at about 30 MeV excitation (MI, 2015; EK1, 2014; EK2, 2015), and provide guidance for future measurements of spontaneous fission in these superheavy nuclei.
