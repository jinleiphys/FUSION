# On the Klein-Gordon bosonic fields in the Bonnor-Melvin spacetime with a cosmological constant in rainbow gravity: Bonnor-Melvin Domain Walls

> We investigate the effect of rainbow gravity on Klein-Gordon (KG) bosons in the background of the magnetized Bonnor-Melvin (BM) spacetime with a cosmological constant. We first show that the very existence of the sinusoidal term \(\sin^2(\sqrt{2Λ}r)\), in the BM space-time metric, suggests that \(\sin^2(\sqrt{2Λ}r) \in [0,1],\) which consequently restricts the range of the radial coordinate \(r\) to \(r \in [0,π/\sqrt{2Λ}]\). Moreover, we show that at \(r = 0\) and \(r = π/\sqrt{2Λ}\), the magnetized BM-spacetime introduces domain walls (infinitely impenetrable hard walls) within which the KG bosonic fields are allowed to move. Interestingly, the magnetized BM-spacetime introduces not only two domain walls but a series of domain walls. However, we focus on the range \(r \in [0,π/\sqrt{2Λ}]\). A quantum particle remains indefinitely confined within this range and cannot be found elsewhere. Based on these findings, we report the effects of rainbow gravity on KG bosonic fields in BM-spacetime. We use three pairs of rainbow functions: \( f(χ) = \frac{1}{1 - \tildeβ |E|}, \, h(χ) = 1 \); \( f(χ) = (1 - \tildeβ |E|)^{-1}, \, h(χ) = 1 \); and \( f(χ) = 1, \, h(χ) = \sqrt{1 - \tildeβ |E|^\upsilon} \), with \(\upsilon = 1,2\). Here, \(χ= |E| / E_p\), \(\tildeβ = β/ E_p\), and \(β\) is the rainbow parameter. We found that while the pairs \((f,h)\) in the first and third cases fully comply with the theory of rainbow gravity and ensure that \(E_p\) is the maximum possible energy for particles and antiparticles, the second pair does not show any response to the effects of rainbow gravity. We show that the corresponding bosonic states can form magnetized, spinning vortices in monolayer materials, and these vortices can be driven by adjusting an out-of-plane aligned magnetic field.

## Key claim

The Bonnor-Melvin spacetime with a positive cosmological constant inherently introduces domain walls (infinitely impenetrable hard walls) at radial coordinates \(r=0\) and \(r=\pi/\sqrt{2\Lambda}\), which confine Klein-Gordon bosonic fields to move within this range; the commonly used approximation that \(r\ll 1\) (implying \(\sin(r)\sim r\)) is invalid because it eliminates these domain walls and changes the dynamics of the quantum system.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation in the magnetized Bonnor-Melvin spacetime metric with a cosmological constant, modified by rainbow gravity through three pairs of rainbow functions. They transformed the radial equation into a one-dimensional Schrodinger-like form, identified the effective potential \(V_{eff}(r)=(\tilde{m}^2-1/4)/\sin(r)^2\), and obtained exact power-series solutions with boundary conditions \(U(0)=0=U(\pi)\) corresponding to the domain walls. The energy eigenvalues were derived by truncating the power series to polynomials of order \(n\).

## Key numbers

- The radial coordinate is restricted to \(r \in [0, \pi/\sqrt{2\Lambda}]\) due to the sinusoidal term \(\sin^2(\sqrt{2\Lambda}r)\) in the metric.
- For the rainbow function pair \(f(\chi)=(1-\tilde{\beta}|E|)^{-1}, h(\chi)=1\), the energy spectrum is \(E_{nm} = \pm \sqrt{\mathcal{\tilde{G}}_{nm}}/(1 + \tilde{\beta}\sqrt{\mathcal{\tilde{G}}_{nm}})\), with asymptotic convergence \(|E_{\pm}| \sim 1/\tilde{\beta} = E_p/\beta\) as \(\Lambda \gg 1\).
- For the rainbow function pair \(f(\chi)=1, h(\chi)=\sqrt{1-\tilde{\beta}|E|^\upsilon}\) with \(\upsilon=2\), the energy is \(E_\pm = \pm \sqrt{\mathcal{\tilde{G}}_{nm}/(1+\tilde{\beta}\mathcal{G}_{nm})}\), with asymptotic convergence \(|E|_{max} \sim \sqrt{1/\tilde{\beta}} \approx 1.41\) as \(\Lambda \rightarrow \infty\).
- For the rainbow function pair \(f(\chi)=(1-\tilde{\beta}|E|)^{-1}=h(\chi)\), the energies grow with increasing cosmological constant \(\Lambda\) and show no convergence to a maximum energy bound.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of scalar bosonic fields in Bonnor-Melvin spacetime under rainbow gravity previously studied by Mustafa (2024), but corrects the invalid small-angle approximation used in that prior work. The authors show that the magnetized Bonnor-Melvin spacetime with cosmological constant naturally produces domain walls, agreeing with Zofka's (2016) proposal that \(\sqrt{2\Lambda}r=\pi\) is the location of an axis. The results enable modeling of magnetized, spinning vortices in monolayer materials that can be driven by adjusting an out-of-plane magnetic field.
