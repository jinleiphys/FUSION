# An introduction to the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 3.0

> We introduce a parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 3.0 based on PYTHIA 6.428 and the PACIAE 2.2 program series. The simulation framework of C-, B-, and A-loops are designed for the high energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}\geq 3$ GeV) and low energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}<3$ GeV) nuclear collisions, respectively, in PACIAE 3.0. In the C-loop simulation, the parton-parton inelastic scattering processes are added in the partonic rescattering process. The single string structure and multiple string interaction mechanism have been introduced investigating the strangeness enhancement in C- and B-loop. An improved mapping relation between the centrality percentage definition and the impact parameter definition is proposed responding the observation of $b_{max}\approx 20$ fm from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations. We have extensively modified the phenomenological coalescence hadronization model. The PACIAE 3.0 model simulated results of particle yield, transverse momentum distribution, and rapidity distribution well reproduce, respectively, the experimental data measured at FOPI, E895, RHIC, and LHC energies.

## Key claim

PACIAE 3.0 reproduces experimental data on particle yield, transverse momentum distribution, and rapidity distribution across FOPI, E895, RHIC, and LHC energies, but the model's success depends on tuning several free parameters (e.g., the exponent `x` for flavor distribution, the ratio of inelastic to total cross section `x_ratio`, and the deexcitation generation limit `adj1(16)`) and on the specific choice of simulation loop (A, B, or C) appropriate for the collision energy.

## Method

The model describes a heavy-ion collision as a cumulative superposition of hadron-hadron collisions, using three simulation loops: A-loop for low-energy collisions (√sNN < 3 GeV) with two-body elastic and inelastic scattering kinematics; B-loop for high-energy collisions (√sNN ≥ 3 GeV) using PYTHIA 6.428 for hadron-hadron collisions with string fragmentation; and C-loop for high-energy collisions where PYTHIA is forced to produce an initial partonic state, which then undergoes partonic rescattering via LO-pQCD 2→2 processes (including newly added inelastic channels), followed by hadronization via LUND string fragmentation or a modified coalescence model (COCCNU), and finally hadronic rescattering with ~600 inelastic channels.

## Key numbers

- The model is designed for nuclear collisions at √sNN ≥ 3 GeV (high energy) and √sNN < 3 GeV (low energy).
- The maximum impact parameter b_max is extended to approximately 20 fm, responding to observations from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS.
- The NN total cross section adopted is approximately 70 mb at LHC energies and approximately 40 mb at RHIC energy and below.
- The nuclear radius parameter r0 = 1.12 fm and diffusivity parameter d = 0.546 fm are used in the centrality mapping.
- The coefficient f in the b_max formula is approximately 4 for nucleus-nucleus collisions and approximately 2 for proton-nucleus collisions.
- The default ratio of inelastic to total cross section (x_ratio) is 0.85.
- The default maximum number of deexcitation generations (adj1(16)) is 1.
- The exponent x for flavor distribution in inelastic parton scattering has a default value of 3.65.
- The vacuum string tension κ ≈ 1 GeV/fm ≈ 0.2 GeV² is used as reference.
- The phase space constraint volume for coalescence is h³/d with d = 4 (spin and parity degeneracies).

## Context

This work extends the PACIAE model series (PACIAE 2.2 and earlier LUCIAE/JPCIAE models) by adding inelastic parton-parton scattering, strangeness enhancement mechanisms via effective string tension, an improved centrality-impact parameter mapping, and a modified coalescence hadronization model. It enables systematic comparisons with experimental data from FOPI, E895, RHIC, and LHC, and provides an open-source framework (available on GitHub and Gitee) for further studies of QGP phase transition and relativistic nuclear collisions, complementing models such as AMPT, UrQMD, HIJING, and EPOS-LHC.
