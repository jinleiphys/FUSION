# Absence of inhomogeneous chiral phases in 2+1-dimensional four-fermion and Yukawa models

> We show the absence of an instability of homogeneous (chiral) condensates against spatially inhomogeneous perturbations for various 2+1-dimensional four-fermion and Yukawa models. All models are studied at non-zero baryon chemical potential, while some of them are also subjected to chiral and isospin chemical potential. The considered theories contain up to 16 Lorentz-(pseudo)scalar fermionic interaction channels. We prove the stability of homogeneous condensates by analyzing the bosonic two-point function, which can be expressed in a purely analytical form at zero temperature. Our analysis is presented in a general manner for all of the different discussed models. We argue that the absence of an inhomogeneous chiral phase (where the chiral condensate is spatially non-uniform) follows from this lack of instability. Furthermore, the existence of a moat regime, where the bosonic wave function renormalization is negative, in these models is ruled out.

## Key claim

The authors prove that for a large class of 2+1-dimensional four-fermion and Yukawa models with Lorentz-(pseudo)scalar interaction channels, homogeneous condensates are stable against spatially inhomogeneous perturbations at non-zero baryon chemical potential, ruling out inhomogeneous chiral phases and moat regimes under the mean-field approximation where bosonic quantum fluctuations are neglected.

## Method

The authors perform a stability analysis by expanding the effective action around homogeneous field configurations and examining the bosonic two-point function (Hessian) in momentum space at zero temperature. They express the two-point function in a purely analytical form for all considered models, which contain up to 16 Lorentz-(pseudo)scalar fermionic interaction channels, and analyze its sign to determine whether homogeneous condensates are unstable against inhomogeneous perturbations. The analysis is carried out within the mean-field approximation (large-N limit), where fermionic fluctuations are fully included through the functional determinant but bosonic quantum fluctuations are neglected.

## Key numbers

- The bosonic two-point function contains a momentum-dependent contribution with coefficient \(a_{\dbf_j} = 4\) for fields with parity quantum numbers \((+,+)\) and \((-,-)\), and \(a_{\dbf_j} = 0\) for fields with parity quantum numbers \((+,-)\) and \((-,+)\).
- At zero temperature, the integral \(\ell_2\) takes three distinct analytical forms depending on the chemical potential \(\mu\) relative to the mass \(M\) and momentum \(q\): it is zero for \(\mu^2 > M^2 + q^2/4\); it equals \(\frac{2}{\pi q} \arctan\left(\frac{\sqrt{q^2 + 4(M^2-\mu^2)}}{2\mu}\right)\) for \(M^2 \leq \mu^2 \leq M^2 + q^2/4\); and it equals \(\frac{2}{\pi q} \arctan\left(\frac{q}{2|M|}\right)\) for \(\mu^2 < M^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous findings in the 2+1-dimensional Gross-Neveu model (Winstel 2019, Narayanan 2020, Buballa 2020) that inhomogeneous phases are regulator artifacts, to a much broader class of models including those with up to 16 interaction channels and Yukawa-type models. The analysis rules out the moat regime (negative bosonic wave-function renormalization) recently discussed in QCD studies (Fu 2019, Pisarski 2021), and contradicts the existence of inhomogeneous chiral phases found in mean-field studies of 3+1-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Buballa 2014 review). The results enable future work to focus on homogeneous phases in these 2+1-dimensional models without needing to consider spatially modulated condensates.
