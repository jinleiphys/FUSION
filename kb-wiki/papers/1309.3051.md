# Next to leading order analysis of DVCS and TCS

> The study of $O(α_s)$ QCD contributions to the timelike and spacelike virtual Compton scattering amplitudes in the generalized Bjorken scaling regime demonstrates that gluonic contributions are by no means negligible even in the medium energy range which will be studied intensely at JLab12 and in the COMPASS-II experiment at CERN.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that next-to-leading order (NLO) gluonic contributions to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) amplitudes are significant even at medium energies accessible at JLab12 and COMPASS-II, and can change the sign of the real part of the Compton form factor, contrary to the expectation that such contributions might be negligible in this energy range.

## Method

The authors computed the O(α_s) QCD coefficient functions for DVCS and TCS amplitudes in the generalized Bjorken scaling regime, using the analyticity relation between the two processes. They evaluated the Compton form factors (CFFs) using two different generalized parton distribution (GPD) models based on double distributions: the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model and a model based on the MSTW08 parton distribution function parametrization, at a factorization scale μ_F^2 = Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 and t = -0.1 GeV^2 or t = -0.2 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- At μ_F^2 = Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 and t = -0.1 GeV^2, gluonic contributions change the sign of the real part of the CFF H(ξ,t) for almost all values of the skewness parameter ξ.
- The gluonic contribution to the imaginary part of the CFF remains sizeable for skewness parameter values up to 0.3.
- DVCS observables were computed for JLab kinematics at E_e = 11 GeV, μ_F^2 = Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, and t = -0.2 GeV^2.
- COMPASS kinematics were evaluated at ξ = 0.05, Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, t = -0.2 GeV^2.
- TCS cross sections were computed at E_γ = 10 GeV, Q^2 = μ^2 = 4 GeV^2, and t = -0.1 GeV^2, integrated over θ ∈ (π/4, 3π/4).
- The ratio R(η) for TCS was plotted as a function of skewness η at Q^2 = μ^2 = 4 GeV^2 and t = -0.2 GeV^2, showing that NLO corrections induce a change of sign dominated by the gluon contribution.

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculations of coefficient functions for DVCS from early GPD studies and the more recent TCS calculations by Pire, Szymanowski, and Wagner (2013), connected via the analyticity relation established by Muller et al. (2012). The results indicate that current attempts to extract GPD information from DVCS data, such as those by Guidal (2009), Kroll (2012), Guidal (2013), Kumericki (2009), and Kumericki (2013), represent only a first step, and that a full NLO analysis including gluonic contributions is necessary for precise data interpretation from JLab12, COMPASS-II, and future higher-energy facilities like the EIC.
