# Skewness-dependent moments of the pion GPD from nonlocal quark-bilinear correlators

> We present lattice QCD calculations of the odd Mellin moments of pion valence-quark generalized parton distribution (GPD) up to fifth order, $\langle x^4\rangle$, and for the skewness range $[-0.33, 0]$ using operator product expansion of bilocal quark-bilinear operators. The calculations are performed on an ensemble with lattice spacing $a=0.04~\mathrm{fm}$ and valence pion mass $300$ $\mathrm{MeV}$, employing boosted pion states with momenta up to 2.428 GeV and momentum transfers reaching 2.748 GeV$^2$. We employ ratio-scheme renormalization and next-to-leading-logarithmic resummed perturbative matching. At zero skewness, our results are consistent with previous lattice studies. By combining matrix elements at multiple values of skewness and momentum transfer, skewness-dependent moments are obtained through simultaneous polynomiality-constrained fits.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD calculations of odd Mellin moments of the pion valence-quark generalized parton distribution (GPD) up to fifth order ($\langle x^4 \rangle$) for skewness values in the range $[-0.33, 0]$, using operator product expansion of bilocal quark-bilinear operators; the results at zero skewness are consistent with previous lattice studies, while the nonzero-skewness analysis remains at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy rather than the NNLL accuracy used for zero skewness.

## Method

The authors computed bare quasi-GPD matrix elements from boosted pion states on a $64^3 \times 64$ lattice with spacing $a=0.04$ fm and valence pion mass 300 MeV, using Wilson-Clover fermions with HYP smearing and boost-smeared sources. They extracted ground-state matrix elements via two-state fits to ratios of three-point and two-point correlation functions, applied ratio-scheme renormalization by dividing by the rest-frame matrix element, and performed operator product expansion matching with next-to-leading-order perturbative coefficients and next-to-leading-logarithmic resummation for nonzero skewness. The skewness-dependent moments were obtained through simultaneous polynomiality-constrained fits combining matrix elements at multiple values of skewness and momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: $a = 0.04$ fm
- Valence pion mass: 300 MeV
- Boosted pion momenta up to: 2.428 GeV
- Momentum transfers reaching: 2.748 GeV$^2$
- Skewness range: $[-0.33, 0]$
- Zero-skewness boosted pion momenta: $\{0.967, 1.453, 1.937\}$ GeV
- Nonzero-skewness boosted pion momenta: $\{1.937, 2.421\}$ GeV
- Spatial momenta quantized in units of: $2\pi/(aN_s) \simeq 0.484$ GeV
- One-loop anomalous dimension matrix diagonal elements for first five moments: 0.318, 0.601, 0.760, 0.874, 0.962

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of pion GPDs that were limited to zero skewness (e.g., Bhattacharya 2023, HadStruc 2024) by extracting moments at nonzero skewness for the first time, enabling tests of polynomiality constraints directly from lattice data. The approach uses the short-distance factorization framework previously applied to pion PDFs (Gao 2020, Gao 2021, Gao 2022) and extends it to GPDs with off-diagonal matching coefficients, while the nonzero-skewness analysis is limited to NLL accuracy due to the unavailability of three-loop off-diagonal anomalous dimensions. This work enables future comparisons with experimental programs at Jefferson Lab, AMBER at CERN, the Electron-Ion Collider, and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider in China.
