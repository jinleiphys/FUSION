# Recent Results of the Relativistic Green's Function Model in Quasielastic Neutrino and Antineutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> The analysis of quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections requires relativistic theoretical descriptions also accounting for the role of final-state interactions (FSI). In the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model FSI are described by a complex optical potential where the imaginary part recovers the contribution of final-state channels that are not included in other models based on the impulse approximation. The RGF results are compared with the data recently published by the MiniBooNE and MINER$ν$A Collaborations. The model is in general able to give a good description of the data.

# Recent Results of the Relativistic Green's Function Model in Quasielastic Neutrino and Antineutrino-Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim
The Relativistic Green's Function (RGF) model, which uses a complex optical potential to describe final-state interactions, is in general able to give a good description of the MiniBooNE and MINERνA quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino-nucleus scattering data, but the numerical predictions are affected by theoretical uncertainties from the choice of phenomenological optical potential parameterization.

## Method
The RGF model describes final-state interactions in inclusive quasielastic scattering using a complex relativistic optical potential, where the imaginary part recovers the contribution of inelastic channels that are not included in other models based on the relativistic impulse approximation. The hadron tensor components are expressed in terms of the single-particle optical model Green's function, which is evaluated via a biorthogonal expansion using eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian relativistic optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. The flux-averaged cross sections are calculated over the entire energy range relevant to the neutrino flux and compared with data from the MiniBooNE and MINERνA Collaborations.

## Key numbers
- For CCQE \(^{12}\text{C}(\nu_\mu, \mu^-)\) and \(^{12}\text{C}(\bar{\nu}_\mu, \mu^+)\) at MiniBooNE kinematics, the RGF double-differential cross section at the \(\cos\vartheta_\mu = 0.75\) angular bin shows reasonable agreement with data around the peak region, with slight underprediction for large muon kinetic energy \(T_\mu\).
- For MINERνA CCQE scattering on a hydrocarbon target, the RGF-EDAI and RGF-DEM differential cross sections \(d\sigma/dQ^2_{QE}\) are in good agreement with data; the RGF-EDAI cross sections are larger than RGF-DEM for \(Q^2_{QE} \lesssim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), while similar results are obtained for larger \(Q^2_{QE}\).
- For MiniBooNE neutral-current elastic scattering, the RGF-DEM results provide the best agreement with the data; the rROP results (retaining only the real part of the EDAI potential) underestimate the data except for \(Q^2 \geq 0.6\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\).
- The ratio of \(\bar{\nu}\) to \(\nu\) neutral-current elastic cross sections from all models are within experimental errors, with RGF results practically independent of the optical potential parameterization.

## Context
This work extends the RGF model, originally developed and tested for inclusive quasielastic electron scattering (Giusti and Meucci, various years), to charged-current quasielastic and neutral-current elastic neutrino and antineutrino-nucleus scattering. The RGF results are larger than those from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, which underpredicts MiniBooNE data but describes MINERνA data well (Juan et al., 2014). The model enables comparison with MiniBooNE and MINERνA data, showing that contributions beyond direct one-nucleon emission, recovered by the imaginary part of the optical potential, are important at MiniBooNE kinematics but less so at MINERνA kinematics. The RGF does not include two-body currents explicitly but can partially recover rescattering processes, non-nucleonic \(\Delta\) excitations, and some multinucleon processes through the imaginary part of the optical potential.
