# Interplay between core and corona from small to large systems

> We present new results in $p$+$p$ and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energies from the updated dynamical core--corona initialization framework (DCCI2). The fractions of final hadron yields originating from equilibrated and non-equilibrated components are extracted as functions of multiplicity. We find that the contributions from non-equilibrated components are non-negligible even in Pb+Pb collisions and affect $p_T$-integrated multi-particle correlations. These suggest the importance of non-equilibrated components for the sophisticated extraction of properties of the quark gluon plasma from comparisons between dynamical frameworks and experimental data.

# Interplay between core and corona from small to large systems

## Key claim

The fractions of final hadron yields originating from equilibrated (core) and non-equilibrated (corona) components show a clear multiplicity scaling that is independent of system size or collision energy; however, the corona contribution remains non-negligible even in the highest-multiplicity Pb+Pb collisions, meaning that the core fraction never reaches 100% and this residual corona component significantly affects multi-particle correlation observables used to extract QGP properties.

## Method

The authors used the dynamical core-corona initialization framework (DCCI2), which combines initial parton distributions from PYTHIA8 (for p+p) and PYTHIA8 Angantyr (for Pb+Pb) with a dynamical initialization approach that deposits energy and momentum from non-equilibrated partons into QGP fluids via source terms in relativistic hydrodynamic equations. The core (QGP fluids) evolves under (3+1)-D ideal hydrodynamics with equation of state s95p-v1.1, while corona partons hadronize via Lund string fragmentation in PYTHIA8; both components are subsequently processed through the JAM hadronic transport model for rescatterings and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- The core component becomes dominant when the charged-particle multiplicity exceeds ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≈ 20, based on results from p+p collisions at √s = 7 and 13 TeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.
- In 20-40% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, the corona component shows a slight enhancement at very low pT (pT < 1 GeV) in charged pion spectra, contrary to the naive expectation that corona dominates only high pT.
- The four-particle cumulant c2{4} for charged particles with |η| < 0.8 and 0.2 < pT < 3.0 GeV in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV shows negative values from core components, while corona contributions are zero-consistent across the entire Nch range, and the absolute values of c2{4} from core alone are diluted by the corona contributions.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical core-corona initialization framework previously developed by Kanakubo, Tachibana, and Hirano (2018, 2019, 2021) and builds on the core-corona picture introduced by Werner (2007). The results directly address the ongoing discussion about QGP formation in small systems following the ALICE Collaboration's (2017) observation of strangeness enhancement in p+p collisions, and they enable more precise extraction of QGP transport coefficients by demonstrating that non-equilibrated components must be accounted for even in heavy-ion collisions when comparing dynamical frameworks to experimental data.
