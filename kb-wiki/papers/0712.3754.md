# Emergence of symmetry from random n-body interactions

> An ensemble with random n-body interactions is investigated in the presence of symmetries. A striking emergence of regularities in spectra, ground state spins and isospins is discovered in both odd and even-particle systems. Various types of correlations from pairing to spectral sequences and correlations across different masses are explored. A search for interpretation is presented.

## Key claim
Random n-body interactions that conserve angular momentum and isospin produce ground states with extreme (minimum or maximum) quantum numbers with surprisingly high probability, and this effect becomes stronger as the particle rank n of the interaction increases, even though the mechanism differs from the pairing correlations that dominate realistic nuclear forces.

## Method
The author defines an n-body rotationally invariant Hamiltonian built from orthogonal n-body creation operators coupled to total angular momentum L, with interaction strengths V_L^(n)(α,β) drawn from a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) within each symmetry class. For a single-j model space (j=19/2 and j=9/2), the ensemble is sampled numerically for n=1 through n=5, and ground-state spin J, seniority s, and correlations across different particle numbers N are computed from many random realizations.

## Key numbers
- For N=6, j=19/2, the probability P[0_0] (ground state spin zero) is 52.3% in the 2-BRE, 66.4% in the 3-BRE, and 83.0% in the 4-BRE.
- For N=7, j=19/2, the probability P[19/2_0] (minimum spin ground state) is 12.4(4)% in the 2-BRE, while P[13/2_0]=14.5(4)% is larger; for n=3,4,5 the P[19/2_0] is bigger than in the 2-BRE.
- For N=8, j=19/2, the 8-BRE (n=N) gives P[J_0]=41%, 35%, 13%, and 5% for spins J=12, 14, 16, and 10 respectively, corresponding to the highest dimensionalities d(J).
- The joint probability P[J_max(5)_0 ... J_max(10)_0] for j=19/2 is 6.6% in the 2-BRE, while the uncorrelated product is 2.1×10^(-4)%.
- The correlation measure C for J_min across N=5-10 is 1.883 (2-BRE), 1.560 (3-BRE), and 0.435 (4-BRE); for J_max it is 3.806 (2-BRE), 3.266 (3-BRE), and 0.000 (4-BRE).
- For N=6, j=19/2, the probability of the sequence 0,2,4 as the three lowest states is 3.7(2)% in the 2-BRE, 4.2(2)% in the 3-BRE, and 4.4(7)% in the 4-BRE.
- For a degenerate p-shell (j=3/2 and j=1/2) odd-odd 10B, P[(1+0)_0]=19.1(4)% in the 2-BRE, 25.6(5)% in the 3-BRE, and 30.3(6)% in the 4-BRE.

## Context
This work extends the study of Two-Body Random Ensembles (2-BRE) pioneered by Johnson (1998) to n-body interactions, where prior work had focused on understanding the preponderance of J=0 ground states in even-particle systems. The results contradict the expectation that pairing (Mulhall, 2000) or time-reversal symmetry (Bijker, 1999) are the primary causes, and instead support an interpretation based on coherent J^2 and T^2 components of the Hamiltonian that emerge with random sign in each realization. The work opens the possibility of studying alpha-particle four-body clustering and shape properties in random ensembles, and extends ideas from Papenbrock (2004) and Zuker (2003) to higher-rank interactions.
