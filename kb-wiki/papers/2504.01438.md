# Precise Determination of Electric Quadrupole Moments and Isotope Shift Constants of Yb$^+$ in Pursuance of Probing Fundamental Physics and Nuclear Radii

> Contemplating to register signature of a new vector boson unambiguously from the measured non-linear isotope shift (IS) effects in three recent experiments [Phys. Rev. X {\bf 12}, 021033 (2022); Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 128}, 163201 (2022) and Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 134}, 063002 (2025)], very precise values of quadrupole moments and IS constants for the $6s ~ ^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 5d ~ ^2D_{3/2}$ and $6s ~ ^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 5d ~ ^2D_{5/2}$ clock transitions of $^{171}$Yb$^+$ are presented. This is accomplished by incorporating contributions from the computationally challenging triply excited configurations through the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory. Testament of quality atomic wave functions of states of the above transitions, obtained using the RCC theory, are gauged by comparing the calculated energies and magnetic dipole hyperfine structure constants with their measurements. The improved quadrupole moments from this work will be immensely useful to estimate quadrupole shifts of the clock transitions of Yb$^+$. Complementary approaches are employed to ascertain accuracy and comprehend roles of orbital relaxation and correlation effects in evaluating the IS constants. Combining these constants with the IS measurements from Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 128}, 163201 (2022), differential nuclear charge radii of the Yb isotopes are inferred that deviate by 6-7\% from the literature data.

# Precise Determination of Electric Quadrupole Moments and Isotope Shift Constants of Yb⁺ in Pursuance of Probing Fundamental Physics and Nuclear Radii

## Key claim
The paper presents improved values of electric quadrupole moments and isotope shift constants for the 6s ²S₁/₂ → 5d ²D₃/₂ and 6s ²S₁/₂ → 5d ²D₅/₂ clock transitions of ¹⁷¹Yb⁺, achieved by including triply excited configurations in relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory. The inferred differential nuclear charge radii of Yb isotopes deviate by 6-7% from literature data, which the author attributes to large discrepancies in the field shift constants used in prior analyses.

## Method
The author employed the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory with singles, doubles, and triples excitations (RCCSDT method) to calculate wave functions for the 6s ²S₁/₂, 5d ²D₃/₂, and 5d ²D₅/₂ states of Yb⁺. Electric quadrupole moments and magnetic dipole hyperfine structure constants were evaluated using the expectation value evaluation (EVE) approach, while isotope shift constants (field shift, normal mass shift, and specific mass shift) were determined using two complementary approaches: the finite-field (FF) approach and the analytical response (AR) approach. The Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) method provided the mean-field wave functions, with orbitals constructed from 40 Gaussian type orbitals per spinor extended to 500 atomic units, including symmetries up to i₁₁/₂;₁₃/₂.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of quadrupole moments and isotope shift constants for Yb⁺ that showed substantial differences among methods, including configuration interaction (CI), many-body perturbation theory (MBPT), and earlier RCC calculations. The author specifically addresses contrasting experimental quadrupole moment values for the 5d ²D₃/₂ state from Schneider and Lange2, and the lack of experimental values for the 5d ²D₅/₂ state. The improved isotope shift constants are intended for use in analyzing non-linear isotope shift effects from recent experiments (Ono 2022, Counts 2022, Hur 2025) that aim to detect signatures of a new vector boson. The inferred nuclear charge radii differ significantly from the literature values of Angeli, enabling future work on probing beyond-standard-model physics through nonlinear isotope shift effects in Yb⁺.
