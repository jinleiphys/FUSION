# Exploration of hyperfine interaction between constituent quarks via eta productions

> In this work, the different exchange freedom, one gluon, one pion or Goldstone boson, in constituent quark model is investigated, which is responsible to the hyperfine interaction between constituent quarks, via the combined analysis of the eta production processes, $π^{-}p\rightarrowηn$ and $γp\rightarrowηp$. With the Goldstone-boson exchange, as well as the one-gluon or one-pion exchange, both the spectrum and observables, such as, the differential cross section and polarized beam asymmetry, are fitted to the suggested values of Particle Data Group and the experimental data. The first two types of exchange freedoms give acceptable description of the spectrum and observables while the one pion exchange can not describe the observables and spectrum simultaneously, so can be excluded. The experimental data for the two processes considered here strongly support the mixing angles for two lowest S11 sates and D13 states as about -30 and 6 degree respectively.

## Key claim

The Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) and one-gluon exchange (OGE) hyperfine interactions both give acceptable descriptions of the baryon spectrum and eta production observables, while the one-pion exchange (OPE) model cannot simultaneously describe the spectrum and observables and can be excluded. The experimental data for the two eta production processes strongly support mixing angles for the two lowest S11 states and D13 states of about -30 and 6 degrees respectively.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of the eta production processes π⁻p → ηn and γp → ηp using a chiral quark approach with a harmonic oscillator basis. They calculated the mass spectrum of nucleon resonances with mass below 2 GeV and the Δ(1232), along with differential cross sections and polarized beam asymmetries, fitting the results to experimental data and Particle Data Group values using the MINUIT program. Three types of hyperfine interactions were tested: one-gluon exchange (OGE), Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) including pseudoscalar, vector and scalar meson exchanges, and one-pion exchange (OPE), with 19, 25, and 23 free parameters respectively.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle for the two lowest S11 states: about -30 degrees (the text gives -32 degrees as the standard OGE value and 31 degrees for GBE)
- Mixing angle for the D13 states: about 6 degrees (the text gives 6 degrees as the standard OGE value and -6 degrees for GBE)
- Average χ² for mass spectrum: 1.3 for OGE model, 1.6 for GBE model
- Average χ² for γp → ηp: 2.4 for OGE model, 2.7 for GBE model
- Average χ² for π⁻p → ηn: 1.3 for OGE model, 1.6 for GBE model
- Constituent quark mass in OGE model: 310 MeV
- Constituent quark mass in GBE model: 251 MeV
- Harmonic oscillator constant α_ho: 309 MeV for OGE model, 316 MeV for GBE model
- Mass of S11(1535): 1532 MeV (OGE), 1534 MeV (GBE)
- Mass of new S11 state: 1701 MeV (OGE), 1730 MeV (GBE)
- Coupling constant g_ηNN: 0.290 (OGE), 0.146 (GBE)
- OPE model mixing angles: 24 degrees for S11 and -47 degrees for D13

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperfine interactions in constituent quark models, particularly the Isgur-Karl model (Isgur 1978, 1979) using OGE and the Goldstone-boson exchange model developed by Riska and Glozman (1996). It builds on earlier comparative studies by Chizma and Karl (2003) and He (2003) that used mixing angles to test exchange models, and on the authors' previous work (He 2009, 2010) applying OGE to eta production. The results enable further investigation of hyperfine interactions through other reaction channels such as γp → KΛ and π⁻p → KΛ, where positive parity states and missing resonances may provide better discrimination between GBE and OGE models.
