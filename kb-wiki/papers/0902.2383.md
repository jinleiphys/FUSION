# Non-gaussian statistics and the relativistic nuclear equation of state

> We investigate possible effects of quantum power-law statistical mechanics on the relativistic nuclear equation of state in the context of the Walecka quantum hadrodynamics theory. By considering the Kaniadakis non-Gaussian statistics, characterized by the index $╬║$ (Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy is recovered in the limit $╬║\to 0$), we show that the scalar and vector meson fields become more intense due to the non-Gaussian statistical effects ($╬║\neq 0$). From an analytical treatment, an upper bound on $╬║$ ($╬║< 1/4$) is found. We also show that as the parameter $╬║$ increases the nucleon effective mass diminishes and the equation of state becomes stiffer. A possible connection between phase transitions in nuclear matter and the $╬║$-parameter is largely discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that applying Kaniadakis non-Gaussian statistics (parameterized by the index ╬║) to the Walecka quantum hadrodynamics model makes the nuclear equation of state stiffer and the meson fields more intense, but only for temperatures high enough that power-law effects become relevant (e.g., heavy-ion collisions or protoneutron stars); at zero temperature the generalized distribution reduces to the standard Fermi-Dirac one, so no non-Gaussian signature is expected in cold neutron star interiors.

## Method

The authors replace the standard Fermi-Dirac distribution functions for nucleons and antinucleons in the mean-field Walecka QHD-I model with the ╬║-generalized quantum distribution derived by Kaniadakis. They then solve the self-consistency equation for the effective nucleon mass and compute the scalar density, baryon density, energy density, and pressure numerically for pure neutron matter (╬│_N=2) and symmetric nuclear matter (╬│_N=4) at finite temperature, using the coupling constants from Walecka (1986) that give binding energy -15.75 MeV and k_F=1.42 fm^{-1}. The convergence of the integrals imposes the analytical bound ╬║ < 1/4.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Pereira, Silva, Alcaniz, 2007) by applying Kaniadakis non-Gaussian statistics to the Walecka quantum hadrodynamics model (Walecka, 1986). It contrasts with standard treatments that use Fermi-Dirac distributions and connects to the phase transition analysis of Theis et al. (1986) by showing that the order of the phase transition depends not only on the scalar coupling constant C_S^2 but also on the ╬║ parameter. The results enable future studies of compact object masses (especially protoneutron stars) and suggest that non-Gaussian effects could modify the phase structure of nuclear matter at high temperatures relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
