# Spin dependent momentum distributions of proton-deuteron clusters in 3He from electron scattering on polarized 3He: theoretical predictions

> The process $\overrightarrow{^3{\rm He}}(e,e'\vec{p})d $ (or $\overrightarrow{^3{\rm He}}(e,e'\vec{d})p $) is studied theoretically in a Faddeev treatment with the aim to have access to the spin-dependent momentum distribution of ${\vec p} {\vec d} $ clusters in polarized $^3$He. Final state interactions and meson exchange currents turn out to have a strong influence in the considered kinematical regime (below the pion threshold). This precludes the direct access to the momentum distribution except for small deuteron momenta. Nevertheless, the results for the longitudinal and transverse response functions are interesting as they reflect our present day understanding of the reaction mechanism and therefore data would be very useful.

## Key claim

The spin-dependent momentum distribution of polarized proton-deuteron clusters in polarized 3He can be accessed via the \(\overrightarrow{^3{\rm He}}(e,e'\vec{p})d\) or \(\overrightarrow{^3{\rm He}}(e,e'\vec{d})p\) processes, but only for small deuteron momenta (\(p_d \leq 2\ {\rm fm}^{-1}\)) because final-state interactions and meson-exchange currents preclude direct access in most kinematic regimes.

## Method

The authors performed a nonrelativistic Faddeev calculation of the electromagnetic breakup of polarized 3He, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and consistent pi- and rho-like meson-exchange currents. They computed the longitudinal (\(W_L\)), transverse (\(W_T\)), and polarized transverse (\(W_{T'}\)) response functions for parallel kinematics (all spins and momenta aligned with the photon momentum) and compared them to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) predictions. The calculations included full final-state interactions via Faddeev equations and antisymmetrization of the final state.

## Key numbers

- The s-wave component \(H_0(q_0)\) of the momentum distribution has a node around \(q_0 = 400\) MeV/c; above this value, s- and d-wave contributions are comparable.
- The spin-dependent momentum distributions show minima shifting from \(q_0 = 300\) to 500 MeV/c when the proton polarization switches from parallel to antiparallel relative to the 3He spin direction.
- For the \(C_2\) configuration at \(p_d = 300\) and 400 MeV/c, the 3N center-of-mass energy is as low as 3.58 MeV and 3.60 MeV respectively, where final-state interactions are most dramatic.
- The accessible region for extracting the momentum distribution is limited to deuteron momenta \(p_d \leq 2\ {\rm fm}^{-1}\) (approximately 395 MeV/c).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of momentum distributions in polarized 3He by Forest et al. (1996) by including full final-state interactions and meson-exchange currents in a Faddeev framework. The calculations demonstrate that the simple PWIA picture fails for most kinematic conditions, but the predicted response functions represent the current state-of-the-art understanding of the reaction mechanism. Experimental verification of these predictions would test few-nucleon dynamics and the dominant photon absorption process, while also enabling access to spin-dependent cluster momentum distributions at small deuteron momenta.
