# Ab Initio No Core Shell Model Study of Neutron Rich Nitrogen Isotopes

> In the present paper, we have calculated the energy spectra for neutron rich $^{18-22}$N isotopes using no core shell model (NCSM).To calculate the energy spectrum we have used three different $NN$ potentials, inside non-local outside Yukawa (INOY), next-to-next-to-next-leading order (N3LO) from chiral effective field theory and charge-dependent Bonn 2000 (CDB2K). The INOY potential, which is a two body interaction but also have the effect of three body forces by short range and non local character present in it. The calculations have been done at $\hbarΩ$=20 MeV, 14 MeV and 12 MeV using INOY, N3LO and CDB2K potentials, respectively. Apart from this, we have also performed shell model calculations with the YSOX interaction.The results with INOY interaction show good agreement with the experimental data in comparison to other three interactions. We have also shown the occupancy of different orbitals involved corresponding to the largest model space ($N_{max}$= 4) in the present calculations.

## Key claim
The INOY potential, a two-body interaction that incorporates three-body effects through its short-range non-local character, reproduces the experimental energy spectra of neutron-rich nitrogen isotopes (¹⁸–²²N) more accurately than the N3LO or CDB2K potentials, but only for specific isotopes; for ¹⁹N the N3LO interaction yields better agreement.

## Method
The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for ¹⁸–²²N using three different NN potentials: INOY (at ħΩ = 20 MeV), N3LO from chiral effective field theory (at ħΩ = 14 MeV), and CDB2K (at ħΩ = 12 MeV). They also carried out standard shell-model calculations with the YSOX interaction in the full psd model space. The NCSM calculations used the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki renormalization scheme with up to two-body cluster terms and model spaces up to Nmax = 4.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of ¹⁸N: INOY gives −121.782 MeV, YSOX gives −127.344 MeV, experimental value is −126.695 MeV.
- Ground-state energy of ²⁰N: INOY gives −128.788 MeV, YSOX gives −134.556 MeV, experimental value is −134.180 MeV.
- Ground-state energy of ²²N: INOY gives −136.560 MeV, YSOX gives −140.657 MeV, experimental value is −140.052 MeV.
- For ²²N at Nmax = 4, the largest model-space dimension is approximately 6.4 × 10⁷.
- The NCSM calculations used ħΩ = 20 MeV for INOY, ħΩ = 14 MeV for N3LO, and ħΩ = 12 MeV for CDB2K.

## Context
This work extends previous NCSM studies of even carbon isotopes (Carbon_isotopes, 2016) using the same INOY and CDB2K interactions to the neutron-rich nitrogen chain. It also compares with shell-model calculations using the YSOX interaction developed by Yuan and Suzuki (2015) for B to O isotopes. The results show that the INOY potential, which implicitly includes three-body force effects, is necessary to reproduce the correct ground-state spin and level ordering for ²²N, while the N3LO and CDB2K potentials fail for that nucleus; this suggests that explicit three-nucleon forces are important for accurate description of these neutron-rich systems.
