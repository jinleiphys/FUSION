# Dispersion relations applied to double-folding potentials from chiral EFT

> We present a determination of optical potentials using the double-folding method based on chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order combined with dispersion relations to constrain the imaginary part. This approach is benchmarked on $^{16}$O--$^{16}$O collisions, and extended to the $^{12}$C--$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C--$^{16}$O cases. Predictions derived from these potentials are compared to data for elastic scattering at energies up to 1000 MeV, as well as for fusion at low energy. Without adjusting parameters, excellent agreement with experiment is found. In addition, we study the sensitivity of the corresponding cross sections to the nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclear densities used.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that double-folding optical potentials built from chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon interactions at N²LO, when combined with dispersion relations to constrain the imaginary part and realistic nuclear densities from electron scattering, reproduce elastic scattering and fusion data without any adjustable parameters, a significant improvement over the authors' previous work where the imaginary part was simply taken proportional to the real part with a scaling factor \(N_W = 0.6\)–\(0.8\).

## Method

The authors construct double-folding potentials for \(^{16}\)O–\(^{16}\)O, \(^{12}\)C–\(^{12}\)C, and \(^{12}\)C–\(^{16}\)O using local chiral EFT NN interactions at N²LO with cutoffs \(R_0 = 1.2\), 1.4, and 1.6 fm. The real part is obtained from the direct and exchange contributions at the Hartree-Fock level; the imaginary part is derived via dispersion relations from the energy-dependent exchange potential, rather than from a simple proportionality constant. Elastic scattering cross sections are computed within the optical model, and fusion cross sections are calculated using the incoming-wave boundary condition (IWBC) with the code CCFULL, including symmetrization for identical spinless bosons.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering data are compared for laboratory energies up to 1000 MeV for \(^{16}\)O–\(^{16}\)O and \(^{12}\)C–\(^{12}\)C, and up to 300 MeV for \(^{12}\)C–\(^{16}\)O.
- For \(^{12}\)C–\(^{12}\)C at \(E_\text{lab} = 1016\) MeV, the comparison with experimental data deteriorates at large momentum transfer.
- Fusion astrophysical \(S\) factors are computed for center-of-mass energies around 12 MeV (the energy at which \(V_\text{Ex}\) is evaluated, since its energy dependence is negligible in this range).
- The Coulomb radius parameter used in fusion calculations is \(r_C = 1.79\) fm.
- The exchange part of the double-folding potential is one order of magnitude smaller than the direct part.
- The dispersion-relation-derived imaginary potential shows a reversed energy ordering compared to the real part: higher energies produce larger imaginary terms.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous double-folding study (Durant 2017) by replacing the ad hoc imaginary scaling factor \(N_W = 0.6\)–\(0.8\) with dispersion relations, following the approach of Carlson (1989), Brandan (1990), and Gonzalez (2001) for nucleus-nucleus potentials. It also improves upon earlier double-folding calculations that used zero-range contact interactions (Chamon 2002, Pereira 2009) or G-matrix methods (Furumoto 2012, Minomo 2015). The use of realistic electron-scattering densities (de Vries 1987) instead of two-parameter Fermi distributions (Chamon 2002) is shown to be crucial for fusion predictions. The results enable future extension to asymmetric and exotic nuclei, inclusion of three-nucleon forces, and fully consistent chiral EFT descriptions bridging reaction and structure physics.
