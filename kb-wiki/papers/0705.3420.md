# A Model for QCD at High Density and Large Quark Mass

> We study the high density region of QCD within an effective model obtained in the frame of the hopping parameter expansion and choosing Polyakov type of loops as the main dynamical variables representing the fermionic matter. To get a first idea of the phase structure, the model is analyzed in strong coupling expansion and using a mean field approximation. In numerical simulations, the model still shows the so-called sign problem, a difficulty peculiar to non-zero chemical potential, but it permits the development of algorithms which ensure a good overlap of the Monte Carlo ensemble with the true one. We review the main features of the model and present calculations concerning the dependence of various observables on the chemical potential and on the temperature, in particular of the charge density and the diquark susceptibility, which may be used to characterize the various phases expected at high baryonic density. We obtain in this way information about the phase structure of the model and the corresponding phase transitions and cross over regions, which can be considered as hints for the behaviour of non-zero density QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective model for QCD at high density and large quark mass, derived from a hopping parameter expansion up to order κ², which retains a sign problem but permits efficient local algorithms with good Monte Carlo ensemble overlap; the results are presented as "hints for the behaviour of non-zero density QCD" rather than definitive predictions, with the phase diagram described as "tentative."

## Method

The authors derive an effective model from the QCD grand canonical partition function with Wilson fermions by expanding the fermionic determinant in the hopping parameter κ up to next-to-leading order (κ²), keeping the combination ζ = κ exp(μ) fixed in the double limit κ → 0, μ → ∞. They analyze the model using strong coupling expansion and mean field approximation for qualitative orientation, then perform Monte Carlo simulations on 4⁴ and 6⁴ lattices with a reweighting procedure using a local Boltzmann factor B₀ that incorporates part of the μ > 0 effects, employing a Cabibbo-Marinari heat-bath procedure mixed with over-relaxation.

## Key numbers

- κ = 0.12 (corresponding to bare mass M₀ = 0.167) is used for simulations, driving 1/M² effects in baryonic density to about 50%
- Simulations are performed on 4⁴ and 6⁴ lattices for n_f = 1 and n_f = 3 degenerate flavors
- Strong coupling results for ⟨P⟩ at small C show leading behavior ∼ C²(1 + (4/9)βκ²(N_τ − 1))
- Strong coupling results for ⟨P*⟩ at small C show leading behavior ∼ (2/3)C(1 + (1/3)βκ²(N_τ − 1))
- For the 6⁴ lattice at β = 5.5, agreement with strong coupling remains good up to μ ≈ 0.95 for ⟨P⟩, while at β = 5.6 deviations appear at much lower μ, indicating a phase transition in this region
- The mean field phase diagram (Fig. 4) for β = 4 and N_τ = 6 shows three phases: confined (u = 0, v = 0), intermediate (u = 0, v > 0), and deconfined (u > 0, v > 0)
- The tentative phase diagram shows a triple point at μ_c ≈ 400 MeV for small masses, with a first-order transition of pure SU(3) Yang-Mills at T_c ≈ 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the "non-zero density quenched approximation" of Bendtsen et al. (1989) and FKTRE collaboration (1990s), which considered the double limit M → ∞, μ → ∞ with ζ fixed, by including κ² corrections that introduce dynamical effects of heavy but finite-mass quarks. The model provides a systematic extension of the static charged background approach and enables numerical exploration of the high-density, low-temperature region of the QCD phase diagram where color superconductivity and color-flavor locking (Alford et al., 1999) are expected, complementing Taylor expansion methods (TARO collaboration, 1990s) and reweighting approaches (various authors, 2000s) that are limited to small μ.
