# The 16O(e,e'pp)14C reaction to discrete final states

> Differential cross sections for exclusive 16O(e,e'pp)14C processes are computed within a distorted-wave framework which includes central short-range correlations and intermediate Δ+ excitations. The cross sections are compared to high-resolution data from the MAMI facility at Mainz for a central energy and momentum transfer of ω=215 MeV and q=316 MeV respectively. A fair agreement between the numerical calculations and data is reached when using spectroscopic information extracted from a 15N(d,3He)14C experiment. The comparison between the calculations and the data provides additional evidence that short-range correlations exclusively affect nucleon pairs with a small center-of-mass momentum residing in a relative S state.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that short-range correlations (SRC) exclusively affect proton pairs in a relative S state with small center-of-mass momentum, while intermediate Δ⁺ excitations dominate the cross section for other kinematic conditions. The agreement with data is "fair" rather than exact, and relies on using spectroscopic information extracted from hadronic transfer reactions rather than from first-principles nuclear structure calculations.

## Method
The authors compute eightfold differential cross sections for the ¹⁶O(e,e'pp)¹⁴C reaction using a non-relativistic distorted-wave model with partial-wave expansion of the final state into two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) eigenstates of a Skyrme Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian. The nuclear current operator includes both a one-body charge-current density modified by a central correlation function g(r₁₂) and a Δ⁺-isobar current operator. Two-hole overlap amplitudes (spectroscopic factors) for the residual ¹⁴C states are taken from empirical information gathered in ¹⁵N(d,³He)¹⁴C transfer reactions rather than from shell-model calculations.

## Key numbers
- Central energy transfer: ⟨ω⟩ = 215 MeV; central momentum transfer: ⟨q⟩ = 316 MeV
- Pair missing momentum range covered: -100 ≤ P ≤ 400 MeV/c
- For the 1⁺ state at E_x = 11.31 MeV, the overlap amplitude for the |(1p₃/₂)⁻¹(1p₁/₂)⁻¹; 1⁺⟩ configuration is set to 1, which is about 30% larger than the value of 0.76 quoted in Geurts (1996)
- For the 2⁺ doublet at E_x = 7.01 and 8.32 MeV, the fragmentation of two-hole strength is taken as 47% and 36% respectively from Kaschl (1971)
- For the 0⁺ state at E_x = 9.75 MeV, about 17% of the two-hole strength from Cohen (1970) is assumed to be carried by this state
- Core polarization amplitude β = 0.4 is used for the 2⁺ transitions, based on shell-model calculations of True

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical and experimental studies of two-proton knockout reactions, including pioneering work at the AMPS facility in Amsterdam (Zondervan, 1995; Kester, 1995; Gerco, 1998) and complementary measurements at MAMI in Mainz (Guenther, 2000; Kahrau, 1999). The calculations are compared to high-resolution data from the A1 collaboration at MAMI that can separate individual final states in ¹⁴C. The paper contrasts its approach with that of the Pavia group (Giusti, 1998; Kadrev, 2003), which uses advanced shell-model calculations for the two-nucleon overlap functions, and notes that even the most sophisticated two-hole spectral function calculations for ¹⁶O (Barbieri, 2002) still miss key features of low-lying states in ¹⁴C. The results enable future studies of the interplay between short-range correlations and Δ degrees of freedom in exclusive (e,e'pp) reactions.
