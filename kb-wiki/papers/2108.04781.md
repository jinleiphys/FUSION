# Single-Logarithmic Corrections to Small-$x$ Helicity Evolution

> The small-$x$ quark helicity evolution equations at double-logarithmic order, with the kernel $\simα_s\ln^2(1/x)$, have been derived previously. In this work, we derive the single-logarithmic corrections to the equations, to order $α_s\ln(1/x)$ of the evolution kernel. The new equations include the effects of the running coupling and the unpolarized small-$x$ evolution, both of which are parametrically significant at single-logarithmic order. The large-$N_c$ and large-$N_c\& N_f$ approximations to the equation are computed. (Here, $N_c$ and $N_f$ are the numbers of quark colors and flavors, respectively.) Their solutions will provide more precise estimates of the quark helicity distribution at small $x$, contributing to the resolution of the proton spin puzzle.

## Key claim
The paper derives single-logarithmic corrections (order α_s ln(1/x)) to the previously known double-logarithmic (order α_s ln²(1/x)) quark helicity evolution equations at small x, incorporating running coupling effects and unpolarized small-x evolution that are parametrically significant at this order, but the resulting full equations are not closed and require large-N_c or large-N_c & N_f limits to obtain solvable systems.

## Method
The author uses lightcone perturbation theory (LCPT) to compute quark and gluon polarized dipole amplitudes, treating parton splittings that occur outside the target shockwave. The evolution kernels are separated into double-logarithmic (DLA), single-logarithmic longitudinal (SLA_L), and single-logarithmic transverse (SLA_T) contributions, with the SLA_T terms arising from hard-parton emission vertices and requiring careful separation of running coupling effects. The full integral equations for the polarized dipole amplitudes are then closed by taking the large-N_c limit (where quark and gluon amplitudes become proportional) and the large-N_c & N_f limit.

## Key numbers
- The double-logarithmic (DLA) evolution kernel scales as α_s ln²(1/x), while the single-logarithmic corrections scale as α_s ln(1/x)
- At double-logarithmic order, the quark helicity distribution grows as (1/x)^α_h with α_h ≈ 2.31, determined from numerical and analytical solutions at large-N_c (KPS_analytic_soln, 2017) and numerical solutions at large-N_c & N_f (KT_NcNf_soln, 2017)
- In the large-N_c & N_f regime at DLA, the power-law growth is multiplied by an oscillation factor corresponding to periodic sign flips of the helicity distribution as ln(1/x) → ∞, with the oscillation period spanning several units of rapidity

## Context
This work extends the double-logarithmic helicity evolution equations derived previously (QuarkOp_2018, Gluon_2017) by adding the subleading single-logarithmic corrections that include running coupling and unpolarized Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution effects (BK_Balitsky, BK_Kovchegov). The closed large-N_c and large-N_c & N_f equations enable more precise determination of the small-x asymptotic behavior of the quark helicity distribution ΔΣ(x,Q²), which contributes to resolving the proton spin puzzle via the Jaffe-Manohar sum rule (Jaffe_Manohar, 1990). The phenomenological impact of these single-logarithmic corrections requires further numerical study, building on prior DLA phenomenology (pheno_2021).
