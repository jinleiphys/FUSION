# The associated photoproduction of K+ meson off proton within a coupled-channels K-matrix approach

> We investigate the p(gamma,K+)Lambda and p(gamma,K+)Sigma^0 reactions within a coupled-channels effective-Lagrangian method which is based on the K-matrix approach. The two-body final channels included are pi-N, eta-N, phi-N, rho-N, gamma-N, K-Lambda, and K-Sigma. Non-resonant meson-baryon interactions are included in the model via nucleon intermediate states in the s- and u-channels and meson exchanges in the t-channel amplitude and the u-channel resonances. The nucleon resonances S11 (1535), S11 (1650), S31 (1620), P11 (1440), P11 (1710), P13 (1720), P33 (1232), P33 (1600), D13 (1520), D13 (1700), and D33 (1700) are included explicitly in the calculations. With a single parameter set which was derived earlier from our analysis of the eta meson photoproduction, the model describes well all the available cross section and polarization data of the SAPHIR collaboration for the two investigated channels. The description of the data of the CLAS collaboration, however, is not of the same quality. In contrast to some previous studies, we do not find any compelling need for including a D13 state with mass of around 2.0 GeV in order to reproduce the data for the p(gamma,K+)Lambda reaction at photon energies corresponding to the invariant mass around 1.9 GeV.

## Key claim

The model describes the SAPHIR data for both p(gamma,K+)Lambda and p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 reactions well with a single parameter set derived from eta meson photoproduction, but the description of the CLAS data is not of the same quality. The authors find no compelling need for a D13 resonance with mass around 2.0 GeV to reproduce the p(gamma,K+)Lambda data at invariant masses around 1.9 GeV, in contrast to some previous studies.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channels effective-Lagrangian method based on the K-matrix approach, including two-body final channels pi-N, eta-N, phi-N, rho-N, gamma-N, K-Lambda, and K-Sigma. Non-resonant meson-baryon interactions are included via nucleon intermediate states in s- and u-channels and meson exchanges in t-channel amplitudes, while eleven nucleon resonances (S11, S31, P11, P13, P33, D13, D33 states) are included explicitly. The kernel is built from tree-level diagrams with dipole form factors, and gauge invariance is restored using the Davidson-Workman procedure.

## Key numbers

* Total cross sections for p(gamma,K+)Lambda show a steep rise from threshold to about E_gamma = 1.1 GeV (corresponding to invariant mass W approximately 1.7 GeV), with a second peak at E_gamma approximately 1.5 GeV (W approximately 1.9 GeV)
* Total cross sections for p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 peak at E_gamma approximately 1.45 GeV (W approximately 1.9 GeV)
* Cut-off parameter lambda = 1.2 GeV^2 for all form factors except Born contributions in kaon channels where lambda = 1.0 GeV^2
* The D13(1520) resonance has mass 1.515 GeV and width to outside states 0.050 GeV, with coupling constants g_Npi = 1.2, g^1_pgamma = 2.6, g^2_pgamma = 2.5, g_KLambda = 2.0, g_KSigma = 0.0, g_Neta = 1.2
* The P33(1600) resonance has mass 1.855 GeV and width to outside states 0.150 GeV, with coupling constants g_Npi = 0.0, g^1_pgamma = -0.4, g^2_pgamma = -0.6, g_KLambda = (not applicable), g_KSigma = 0.55, g_Neta = (not applicable)

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels K-matrix approach previously applied to eta meson photoproduction (Uso et al., 2005; Uso et al., 2006; Shyam et al., 2008) to simultaneously describe p(gamma,K+)Lambda and p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 reactions. The results contradict studies by Mart and Bennhold (2006), Mart and Bennhold (2001), Janssen et al. (2001), and Sarantsev et al. (2005) who required an additional D13(1895) resonance, instead supporting the coupled-channels analysis of Shklyar et al. (2005) and Penner and Mosel (2002) which attribute the second peak to interference effects. The model enables further investigation of nucleon resonance properties and the role of channel couplings in strangeness photoproduction.
