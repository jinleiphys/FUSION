# Nonlocal Form of Quantum Off-Shell Kinetic Equation

> A new nonlocal form of the off-shell kinetic equation is derived. While being equivalent to the Kadanoff--Baym and Botermans--Malfliet formulations in the range of formal applicability, it has certain advantages beyond this range. It possesses more accurate conservation laws for Noether quantities than those in the Botermans--Malfliet formulation. At the same time the nonlocal form, similarly to the Botermans--Malfliet one, allows application of the test-particle method for its numerical solution, which makes it practical for simulations of heavy-ion collisions. The physical meaning of the time-space nonlocality is clarified.

# Nonlocal Form of Quantum Off-Shell Kinetic Equation

## Key claim

The authors derive a new nonlocal form of the off-shell kinetic equation that is equivalent to both the Kadanoff-Baym and Botermans-Malfliet formulations within the first-order gradient approximation, but provides more accurate conservation laws for Noether quantities than the Botermans-Malfliet form while still allowing application of the test-particle method for numerical solution.

## Method

Starting from the Kadanoff-Baym kinetic equation in the first-order gradient approximation, the authors rewrite the collision term with shifted phase-space variables, introducing a nonlocal form that includes a Poisson bracket term involving the collision term and the retarded Green function. The new equation is shown to coincide with the Kadanoff-Baym equation including second-order gradient terms, while neglecting those second-order terms returns the Botermans-Malfliet equation. The test-particle equations of motion between collisions remain identical to those in the Botermans-Malfliet case, but collisions occur with time-space delays.

## Key numbers

- The effective current from the nonlocal kinetic equation differs from the Noether current only in second-order gradients, whereas the Botermans-Malfliet effective current differs in first-order gradients
- The collision term C is proportional to ∂_X (first order in gradients)
- The nonlocal collision term C^NL equals C + {C/A, Re G^R} + O(∂_X^3), where the Poisson bracket term is first order in gradients
- The spectral function A satisfies the sum rule ∫_0^∞ (dp_0/2π) A(X,p) = 1 for nonrelativistic particles

## Context

This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym (Kad62) and Botermans-Malfliet (Bot90) formulations of quantum kinetic theory, which are two slightly different forms of the gradient-expanded Kadanoff-Baym equations. The Kadanoff-Baym form possesses exact conservation laws for Noether current and energy-momentum (KIV01, IKV03) but does not allow efficient test-particle method application, while the Botermans-Malfliet form allows test-particle methods (Mosel, Cass99, Leupold) but only approximately conserves Noether quantities. The new nonlocal form combines favorable features of both, enabling more accurate conservation laws in numerical simulations of heavy-ion collisions while maintaining practical applicability of the test-particle method.
