# Final State Interactions in Hypernuclear Decay

> We present an update of the One-Meson-Exchange (OME) results for the weak decay of s- and p-shell hypernuclei (Ref. Phys. Rev. C {\bf 56}, 339 (1997)), paying special attention to the role played by final state interactions between the emitted nucleons. The present study also corrects for a mistake in the inclusion of the $K$ and $K^*$ exchange mechanisms, which substantially increases the ratio of neutron-induced to proton-induced transitions, $Γ_n/Γ_p$. With the most up-to-date model ingredients, we find that the OME approach is able to describe very satisfactorily most of the measured observables, including the ratio $Γ_n/Γ_p$.

## Key claim

The One-Meson-Exchange (OME) model, after correcting a sign error in the strange-meson (K and K*) exchange amplitudes and using the most up-to-date strong-interaction models, can describe most measured hypernuclear weak-decay observables satisfactorily, including the neutron-to-proton ratio Γ_n/Γ_p, provided that final-state interactions (FSI) between the emitted nucleons are treated via a proper T-matrix solution rather than through phenomenological approximations.

## Method

The authors computed non-mesonic weak decay rates and asymmetries for s-shell (⁵_ΛHe) and p-shell (¹²_ΛC) hypernuclei using a one-meson-exchange potential that includes π, η, K, ρ, ω, and K* mesons. They corrected a sign error in the parity-violating K and K* amplitudes, employed the new Nijmegen NSC97a and NSC97f baryon-baryon potentials consistently for strong vertices, form factors, pole-model weak couplings, and final NN distorted waves, and compared results from different treatments of FSI (T-matrix, K-matrix, phenomenological correlation function, and no FSI).

## Key numbers

- For ⁵_ΛHe with the NSC97f model and proper T-matrix FSI: non-mesonic decay rate Γ_nm = 0.32 Γ_Λ (where Γ_Λ = 3.8 × 10⁹ s⁻¹ is the free Λ decay rate), Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.34, and hypernuclear asymmetry parameter A_p = -0.68.
- For ¹²_ΛC with the NSC97f model and proper T-matrix FSI: Γ_nm = 0.55 Γ_Λ, Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.29, and A_p = 0.36.
- The corrected sign for K and K* exchange increased Γ_n/Γ_p by almost a factor of 3 compared to the earlier work (Phys. Rev. C 56, 339 (1997)), where it was 0.068 for ¹²_ΛC.
- The non-mesonic decay rate for ¹²_ΛC ranges between 0.55 and 0.73 Γ_Λ across the NSC97a and NSC97f models; Γ_n/Γ_p ranges between 0.29 and 0.34.
- Using a phenomenological correlation function (f_phen = 1 - j_0(q_c r) with q_c = 3.93 fm⁻¹) or no FSI at all gave decay rates differing by more than a factor of two from the T-matrix result, and Γ_n/Γ_p about 20% larger.

## Context

This work updates the OME model of Parreño, Ramos, and Bennhold (1997) by correcting a sign error in the K and K* exchange amplitudes that had suppressed Γ_n/Γ_p, and by incorporating the new Nijmegen NSC97 baryon-baryon potentials (Stoks and Rijken, 1999) consistently throughout the calculation. It extends studies by Oset et al. (2000) and Oka et al. (2000) that also addressed the Γ_n/Γ_p puzzle, and it quantifies the large sensitivity of decay observables to the treatment of FSI, showing that phenomenological or no-FSI approximations are inadequate. The results enable more reliable comparisons with experimental data from Brookhaven, KEK, and TJNAF, and they highlight a new puzzle: the meson-exchange model cannot reproduce the opposite signs of the elementary asymmetry parameter a_Λ extracted from ⁵_ΛHe (small positive, Ajimura 2000) and ¹²_ΛC (large negative, Ajimura 1992).
