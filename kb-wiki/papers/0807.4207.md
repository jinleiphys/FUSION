# High-energy hadron physics at J-PARC

> The J-PARC facility is near completion and experiments will start in 2009 on nuclear and particle physics projects. In this article, the J-PARC facility is introduced, and possible projects are discussed in high-energy hadron physics by using the primary proton beam of 30-50 GeV. There are proposed experiments on charm-production and Drell-Yan processes as well as single spin asymmetries for investigating quark and gluon structure of the nucleon and nuclei. Parton-energy loss could be studied in the Drell-Yan processes. There is also a proposal on hadron-mass modifications in a nuclear medium by using the proton beam. In addition, possible topics include transition from hadron to quark degrees of freedom by elastic pp scattering, color transparency by (p,2p), short-range correlation in nuclear force by (p,2pN), tensor structure functions for spin-one hadrons, fragmentation functions, and generalized parton distributions in the ERBL region although proposals are not written on these projects. If proton-beam polarization is attained, it is possible to investigate details of nucleon spin structure. In the last part of this article, our own studies are explained on parton distribution functions in connection with the J-PARC projects.

## Key claim

The J-PARC facility, with its 30-50 GeV primary proton beam, will enable a wide range of high-energy hadron physics projects that are complementary to RHIC and LHC, particularly for probing the large-\(x\) region (\(x>0.2\)) of parton distribution functions, though many of the discussed topics are speculative and do not yet have formal proposals.

## Method

The paper surveys proposed and potential experiments at the J-PARC hadron experimental facility using the primary 30-50 GeV proton beam, including Drell-Yan processes, charm production, single spin asymmetries, and hadron-mass modification measurements. The author also presents their own global QCD analyses of world data on structure functions and Drell-Yan cross sections to determine nuclear parton distribution functions and polarized parton distribution functions, with uncertainties shown as shaded bands.

## Key numbers

- J-PARC primary proton beam energy: 30 GeV initially, designed 50 GeV later; center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s}=8\) GeV in the beginning and 10 GeV later
- J-PARC beam power: 1 MW in the 3-GeV synchrotron and 0.75 MW in the 50-GeV synchrotron
- Drell-Yan measurements at J-PARC (50 GeV) extend the \(x\) region to \(x_{max} \sim 0.6\), beyond the Fermilab-E866 range
- KEK-E325 experimental results indicated a 9% mass shift for \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons in nuclear medium
- BNL color transparency measurements done up to proton momentum 14 GeV/c, with an interesting turnover at \(p \simeq 9\) GeV/c
- BNL experiment \(A(p,2pN)X\) indicated \(pn\) short-range correlation is twenty times larger than \(pp\) correlation
- Elastic \(pp\) scattering counting rule: \(d\sigma/dt \sim s^{2-n} f(\theta_{c.m.})\) where \(n\) is total number of interacting elementary particles

## Context

This paper surveys the experimental program planned for the J-PARC facility, which extends the work of Fermilab-E866 on flavor asymmetric antiquark distributions (\(\bar u \ne \bar d\)) and the HERMES collaboration's 2005 measurement of tensor structure functions \(b_1\) and \(b_2\). The proposed measurements will complement RHIC-Spin (which probes smaller \(x\)) and enable new studies of parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter, chiral symmetry restoration via vector meson mass shifts, and the transition from hadron to quark degrees of freedom.
