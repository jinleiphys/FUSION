# Reply to the paper of V.D. Efros: Comments in some recent papers on the Hyperspherical approach in few--body system

> Few days ago V.D. Efros submitted a preprint to nucl-th containing criticisms of our recent research activity on bound and scattering states of A=3, 4 nucleons. As a consequence, we are forced to examine the essence of the controversy and of the comments contained in that preprint. These are the motivations of the present paper.

# Reply to the paper of V.D. Efros: Comments in some recent papers on the Hyperspherical approach in few–body systems

## Key claim

The authors defend their use of correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (CHH/PHH) bases and uncorrelated HH expansions for A=3,4 nucleon systems against criticisms by V.D. Efros, asserting that their approach yields accurate results for bound and scattering states, and that the specific selection of HH functions and correlation factors is crucial for achieving convergence, not merely a "minor difference" as Efros claimed.

## Method

The authors expand the three-nucleon wave function as a sum of Faddeev-like amplitudes, each decomposed into channels labeled by angular-spin-isospin quantum numbers and expanded in terms of correlated HH functions (Jastrow-correlated CHH or pair-correlated PHH bases). For bound states, they select the maximum grand angular quantum number K_max per channel to reflect its importance, increasing the number of channels until stability is achieved. For scattering states, the wave function is decomposed into an asymptotic part and an internal part, following the Kohn-Hulthèn variational principle, and the PHH correlated functions are used to perform n-d and p-d scattering calculations.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy obtained with three accurate digits using only 12 channels and 6 correlated HH functions per channel for realistic NN potentials.
- Using the uncorrelated HH expansion, the authors achieved six digits of accuracy for the binding energy and four digits for other quantities of interest for potentials such as MT(I-III) and Argonne-type potentials.
- For the SSC(C) interaction, Mukhtarova (1989) quoted a triton binding energy of B = 7.608 MeV with K_max = 34 and an extrapolated value of 7.65 MeV, and a mixed symmetry percentage P_s' = 1.15%; the authors' corresponding estimates are B = 7.5385 MeV and P_s' = 1.238%, which agree with Faddeev results.

## Context

This reply addresses criticisms from V.D. Efros (1998) regarding the authors' prior work on bound and scattering states of A=3,4 nucleons using the Hyperspherical Harmonic approach. The authors distinguish their correlated HH basis from the uncorrelated HH expansion used by Efros (1973), Demin (1973), Mukhtarova (1989), and Fomin (1981), noting that the uncorrelated expansion requires much larger K_max values and has slower convergence for scattering problems. The paper extends the variational approach pioneered by Delves for realistic NN interactions and enables benchmark comparisons between PHH and Faddeev results for n-d and p-d scattering, as demonstrated by Huber (1995).
