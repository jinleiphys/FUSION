# Deuteron Polarization Observables in Elastic $\vec{d}p$ Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> A calculation of vector and tensor polarization observables of a deuteron in an optical potential framework for elastic proton-deuteron scattering at the incident deuteron energy $E_d=880$ MeV is presented. The main aim is to point out the role of various effects in explaining the wide-range angle distribution of these observables. Under the investigation are a double-scattering mechanism in the optical potential, the influence of various $NN$-partial waves and as well a comparison of calculations with two different deuteron wave-functions derived from the Bonn-CD $NN$-potential model and dressed bag model of Moscow-Tuebingen group.

## Key claim
The paper argues that at an incident deuteron energy of 880 MeV, the double-scattering mechanism in the optical potential and the short-range behavior of the deuteron wave function (specifically whether it has a node from six-quark dynamics or a depletion from meson-exchange models) produce large, distinguishable effects on vector and tensor polarization observables, but a definitive conclusion on which deuteron model is preferable requires including the corresponding three-nucleon forces, which differ between the two concepts.

## Method
The author calculated deuteron polarization observables (A^d_y, A_yy, A_xx, A_xz) for elastic proton-deuteron scattering using an optical potential framework derived from the Faddeev equations, with the NN T-matrix taken on-shell in the Breit frame via the optimal impulse approximation. The scattering equation was solved in the helicity partial-wave basis with total angular momentum up to J=37/2, using NN partial waves from the SAID analysis up to J_NN=7. Two deuteron wave functions were compared: one from the Bonn-CD meson-exchange potential model and one from the dressed bag model of the Moscow-Tuebingen group.

## Key numbers
- Incident deuteron laboratory energy: E_d = 880 MeV.
- Effective NN scattering energy ranges from E_eff = 443 MeV at scattering angle theta = 10 degrees to E_eff = 796 MeV at theta = 170 degrees.
- Convergent result for the pd scattering amplitude was obtained at total angular momentum J = 37/2.
- NN partial waves were included up to total angular momentum J_NN = 7 from the SAID analysis.

## Context
This work extends optical-potential calculations of elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering to intermediate energies above the pion production threshold, where full Faddeev calculations become computationally difficult and new degrees of freedom (isobars, mesons) become relevant. It contrasts with lower-energy calculations using Faddeev equations with three-nucleon forces of Fujita-Miyazawa or Tucson-Melbourne type (Gloekle review; Sekiguchi; Uzikov), and with coupled-channel models including Delta-isobar excitation (Mach). The results enable future measurements of dp elastic scattering analyzing powers at the Nuclotron internal target station at JINR (Ladygin) to discriminate between deuteron wave functions with nodal behavior (from six-quark dynamics, Kukulin) versus conventional depletion at short distances (Bonn-CD, Machleidt).
