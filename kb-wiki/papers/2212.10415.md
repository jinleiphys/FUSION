# New insights into the pole parameters of the $Λ(1380)$, the $Λ(1405)$ and the $Σ(1385)$

> A coupled-channel S- and P-wave next-to-leading order chiral-unitary approach for strangeness $S=-1$ meson-baryon scattering is extended to include the new data from the KLOE and AMADEUS experiments as well as the $Λπ$ mass distribution of the $Σ(1385)$. The positions of the poles on the second Riemann sheet corresponding to the $Σ(1385)$ pole and the $Λ(1380)$ and $Λ(1405)$ poles as well as the couplings of these states to various channels are calculated. We find that the resonance positions and branching ratios are on average determined with about 20\% higher precision when including the KLOE and AMADEUS data. Additionally, for the first time, the correlations between the parameters of the poles are investigated and shown to be relevant. We also find that the $Σ(1385)$ has negligible influence on the properties of the $Λ$ states given the available data. Still, we identify isospin-1 cusp structures in the present solution in light of new measurements of $π^\pmΛ$ line-shapes by the Belle collaboration.

## Key claim

The inclusion of new KLOE and AMADEUS data improves the precision of resonance pole positions and branching ratios by about 20% on average, but the paper's hedge is that the $\Sigma(1385)$ has negligible influence on the $\Lambda(1380)$ and $\Lambda(1405)$ properties given the available data, and that correlations between pole parameters are shown to be relevant for the first time in this meson-baryon system.

## Method

A coupled-channel S- and P-wave next-to-leading order chiral-unitary approach for strangeness $S=-1$ meson-baryon scattering was extended to include new data from KLOE and AMADEUS experiments plus the $\Lambda\pi$ mass distribution of the $\Sigma(1385)$. The Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved with contact interactions from the chiral Lagrangian, and the $\Sigma(1385)$ was incorporated using the two-potential formalism to modify the isovector $f_{1+}$ partial wave. Four fit scenarios ($\mathfrak{F}_1$ through $\mathfrak{F}_4$) were compared to isolate the impact of the new data, with pole positions extracted from the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

* Pole position of $\Lambda(1405)$: $W^* = 1.430(6) - i\,0.023(4)$ GeV (fit $\mathfrak{F}_1$, all data)
* Pole position of $\Lambda(1380)$: $W^* = 1.355(16) - i\,0.038(14)$ GeV (fit $\mathfrak{F}_1$)
* Pole position of $\Sigma(1385)$: $W^* = 1.385(1) - i\,0.019(1)$ GeV (fit $\mathfrak{F}_1$)
* Total $\chi^2$ for fit $\mathfrak{F}_1$ (all data): 677.98, with $\chi^2_{\rm dof} = 1.19$
* Total $\chi^2$ for fit $\mathfrak{F}_4$ (no new data): 705.92, with $\chi^2_{\rm dof} = 1.13$
* The $\Sigma(1385)$ coupling to $\bar{K}N$ channels is of order $10^{-4}$, about two orders of magnitude smaller than couplings to $\pi\Lambda$ and $\pi\Sigma$ channels

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary coupled-channel approach of Mai (2014) and Sadasivan (2018) by incorporating recently measured data from the AMADEUS and KLOE collaborations, which had not been included in previous analyses. The results enable more precise determination of the two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ region, first observed by Oller (2000), and provide the first correlation matrices for pole parameters in a meson-baryon system. The findings can inform studies of the $K^-pp$ bound state and antikaon condensates in neutron stars, as discussed in reviews by Hyodo (2022) and Tolos (2020).
