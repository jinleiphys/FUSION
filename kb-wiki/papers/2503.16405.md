# Acceptance dependence of factorial cumulants, long-range correlations, and the antiproton puzzle

> We analyze joint factorial cumulants of protons and antiprotons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and point out that they obey the scaling $\hat{C}_{nm}^{p,\bar{p}} \propto \langle N_p \rangle^n \langle N_{\bar{p}} \rangle^m$ as a function of acceptance when only long-range correlations are present in the system, such as global baryon conservation and volume fluctuations. This hypothesis can be directly tested experimentally without the need for corrections for volume fluctuations. We show that if correlations among protons and antiprotons are driven by global baryon conservation and volume fluctuations only, the equality $\hat{C}_{2}^{p} / \langle N_p \rangle^2 = \hat{C}_{2}^{\bar{p}} / \langle N_{\bar{p}} \rangle^2$ holds for large systems created in central collisions. We point out that the experimental data of the STAR Collaboration from phase I of RHIC beam energy scan are approximately consistent with the scaling $\hat{C}_{nm}^{p,\bar{p}} \propto \langle N_p \rangle^n \langle N_{\bar{p}} \rangle^m$, but the normalized antiproton correlations are stronger than that of protons, $-\hat{C}_{2}^{\bar{p}} / \langle N_{\bar{p}} \rangle^2 > -\hat{C}_{2}^{p} / \langle N_p \rangle^2$. Existing theoretical baselines, based on global baryon conservation and volume fluctuations, cannot explain the data, to which we refer as the antiproton puzzle. We also discuss high-order factorial cumulants which can be measured with sufficient precision within phase II of RHIC-BES.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that joint factorial cumulants of protons and antiprotons obey a specific scaling with acceptance when only long-range correlations (global baryon conservation and volume fluctuations) are present, and that experimental data from STAR's BES-I show antiproton correlations are stronger than proton correlations, a discrepancy that existing theoretical baselines cannot explain, which the authors term the "antiproton puzzle."

## Method

The authors analyze joint factorial cumulants of protons and antiprotons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, deriving that reduced correlation coefficients \(\hat{c}_{nm}^{p,\bar{p}} = \hat{C}_{nm}^{p,\bar{p}} / \langle N_p \rangle^n \langle N_{\bar{p}} \rangle^m\) should be independent of acceptance when only long-range correlations are present. They test this scaling against STAR BES-I data for second-order cumulants and compare the results to a single-fireball model with global baryon conservation and a two-source model that separates stopped and produced matter.

## Key numbers

- For global baryon conservation in the large-volume limit, second-order reduced correlation coefficients are \(\hat{c}_{2,0}^{B,\bar{B}} = -\hat{c}_{1,1}^{B,\bar{B}} = \hat{c}_{0,2}^{B,\bar{B}} = -1/\mathcal{N}\), where \(\mathcal{N} = \langle N_B \rangle + \langle N_{\bar{B}} \rangle\) (Eq. 14).
- Third-order couplings scale as \(\hat{c}_{3,0}^{B,\bar{B}} = (3 - r_B)/\mathcal{N}^2\) and \(\hat{c}_{0,3}^{B,\bar{B}} = (3 + r_B)/\mathcal{N}^2\), where \(r_B = B/\mathcal{N}\) (Eq. 15).
- Fourth-order couplings include \(\hat{c}_{4,0}^{B,\bar{B}} = -3(5 - 4r_B + r_B^2)/\mathcal{N}^3\) and \(\hat{c}_{0,4}^{B,\bar{B}} = -3(5 + 4r_B + r_B^2)/\mathcal{N}^3\) (Eq. 16).
- In the single-fireball model, \(\hat{c}_2^p = \hat{c}_2^{\bar{p}} = -1/(N_{\text{part}} + 2\langle N_{\text{pairs}}^{B\bar{B}} \rangle)\) (Eq. 22).
- For the two-source model, antiproton coupling is \(\hat{c}_2^{\bar{p}} = -1/(2\langle N_{\text{pairs}}^{B\bar{B}} \rangle)\) (Eq. 27), while proton coupling is \(\hat{c}_2^p = -(1 - R_{\bar{p}p})^2/N_{\text{part}} - R_{\bar{p}p}^2/(2\langle N_{\text{pairs}}^{B\bar{B}} \rangle)\) (Eq. 26), where \(R_{\bar{p}p} = \langle N_{\bar{p}} \rangle / \langle N_p \rangle\).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 27\) GeV, the single-fireball model predicts identical couplings for protons and antiprotons, but data show \(-\hat{C}_2^{\bar{p}} / \langle N_{\bar{p}} \rangle^2 > -\hat{C}_2^p / \langle N_p \rangle^2\) for energies below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 62.4\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the baseline calculations of Braun-Munzinger (2020) using the hadron resonance gas model with canonical baryon conservation and of Vovchenko (2021) using viscous hydrodynamics with global baryon conservation and excluded volume effects. The paper identifies a discrepancy in antiproton correlations that cannot be explained by these existing models, suggesting the need for additional physics such as incomplete equilibration between stopped and produced matter, hadronic re-scattering, or baryon annihilation as discussed by Becattini (2016) and Savchuk (2021). The results enable future tests with higher-order factorial cumulants that could be measured with sufficient precision in phase II of RHIC-BES.
