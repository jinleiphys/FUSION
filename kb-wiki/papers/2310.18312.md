# Mass gaps of a $\mathbb{Z}_3$ gauge theory with three fermion flavors in 1 + 1 dimensions

> We consider a $\mathbb{Z}_3$ gauge theory coupled to three degenerate massive flavors of fermions, which we term "QZD". The spectrum can be computed in $1+1$ dimensions using tensor networks. In weak coupling the spectrum is that of the expected mesons and baryons, although the corrections in weak coupling are nontrivial, analogous to those of non-relativistic QED in 1+1 dimensions. In strong coupling, besides the usual baryon, the singlet meson is a baryon anti-baryon state. For two special values of the coupling constant, the lightest baryon is degenerate with the lightest octet meson, and the lightest singlet meson, respectively.

## Key claim

For a $\mathbb{Z}_3$ gauge theory with three degenerate massive fermion flavors in 1+1 dimensions, the lightest baryon becomes degenerate with the lightest octet meson at one special coupling value and with the lightest singlet meson at another, though the authors note this second degeneracy "may simply be fortuitous" and they have not checked whether it extends to excited states.

## Method

The authors study a $\mathbb{Z}_3$ lattice gauge theory (termed "QZD") with three flavors of staggered fermions, using the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) with tensor networks implemented via the QSpace library to exploit SU(3) flavor symmetry. They compute the mass spectrum as a function of the gauge coupling $g^2$ for fixed fermion mass $m=0.125$ on chains of up to $L=120$ sites with open boundary conditions, targeting different symmetry sectors labeled by total particle number $N_{\text{tot}}$ and SU(3) flavor representation $(n_s,n_a)$. The gauge fields are eliminated in 1+1 dimensions by solving Gauss's law, yielding a long-range Hamiltonian expressed entirely in terms of fermion fields.

## Key numbers

- At weak coupling ($g^2=0$), meson mass is approximately $2m$ and baryon mass is approximately $3m$, where $m$ is the fermion mass.
- The weak-coupling expansion for meson mass follows $M_{\text{meson}}/m = 2m \cdot (1 + 0.40431 \cdot (g/(2m))^{4/3} + O(g^2/m^2))$, with the $g^{4/3}$ scaling indicating confinement.
- At strong coupling, the baryon mass is $m_B = 3m + 3/(4a^3) \cdot 1/g^2$ to leading order in $1/g^2$, with lattice spacing $a=1/2$.
- The singlet meson mass at infinite coupling is $6m$ (a baryon-antibaryon state), while octet mesons have mass $\sim g^2$ and become very heavy.
- The edge mode in the ground state carries a fractional excess particle number $n_{\text{edge}} = 3/4 = N_f/4$ but zero net electric charge $q_{\text{edge}} = 0$, topologically protected for all $g$.

## Context

This work extends the study of confining gauge theories in 1+1 dimensions beyond the Schwinger model (Schwinger, 1962; Banks, 1975; Coleman, 1976) and the 't Hooft model (t'Hooft, 1974), where only mesons appear. The $\mathbb{Z}_3$ model proposed by Pisarski (2021) produces both gauge-invariant baryons and mesons, unlike U(1) theories where Gauss's law forbids net fermion number for a single flavor. The weak-coupling non-analytic $g^{4/3}$ scaling matches predictions for QCD$_2$ (Ziyatdinov, 2010), while the strong-coupling behavior is analyzed via perturbation theory around the product-state vacuum. The topological edge modes generalize the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model (Su, 1979) to the gauged setting. The two degeneracy points between baryon and meson masses suggest possible connections to supersymmetry, though the authors caution this remains unverified for excited states.
