# A Comparison of p-p, p-Pb, Pb-Pb Collisions in the Thermal Model: Multiplicity Dependence of Thermal Parameters

> % An analysis is made of the particle composition (hadrochemistry) of the final state in proton-proton (p-p), proton-lead (p-Pb) and lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions as a function of the charged particle multiplicity ($\dNchdeta$). The thermal model is used to determine the chemical freeze-out temperature as well as the radius and strangeness saturation factor $γ_s$. Three different ensembles are used in the analysis namely, the grand canonical ensemble, the canonical ensemble with exact strangeness conservation and the canonical ensemble with exact baryon number, strangeness and electric charge conservation. It is shown that for high multiplicities (at least 20 charged hadrons in the mid-rapidity interval considered) the three ensembles lead to the same results.

## Key claim

The thermal model's three ensembles (grand canonical, strangeness-canonical, and full canonical) converge to identical chemical freeze-out parameters only when the charged-particle multiplicity exceeds approximately 20 charged hadrons at mid-rapidity; for lower multiplicities, especially in proton-proton collisions, the ensembles give markedly different results, casting doubt on the thermal model's applicability to low-multiplicity p-p collisions.

## Method

The authors fitted the THERMUS thermal-model code to measured hadron yields (pions, kaons, protons, Lambdas, Xis, and Omegas where available) from p-p collisions at 7 TeV, p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, using ALICE data. For each collision system and multiplicity bin, they extracted the chemical freeze-out temperature, the strangeness saturation factor gamma_s, and the freeze-out radius independently in three statistical ensembles: grand canonical (GCE), canonical with exact strangeness conservation (SCE), and full canonical with exact baryon number, strangeness, and charge conservation (FCE). The fits used the Boltzmann approximation with zero chemical potentials and included resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- The three ensembles converge to a common chemical freeze-out temperature close to 160 MeV at the highest multiplicities.
- The convergence threshold is reached when the charged-particle multiplicity dN_ch/deta exceeds 20 at mid-rapidity.
- For p-p collisions at 7 TeV, the lowest multiplicity bin (dN_ch/deta = 2.26) yields chi^2/ndf values of 12.79/2 (SCE), 3.85/2 (FCE), and 6.45/2 (GCE), showing large ensemble dependence.
- For p-p collisions at 7 TeV, the highest multiplicity bin (dN_ch/deta = 21.29) yields chi^2/ndf values of 22.20/2 (SCE), 21.83/2 (FCE), and 21.55/2 (GCE), showing convergence.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the highest multiplicity bin (dN_ch/deta = 1448) yields chi^2/ndf values of 11.08/2 (SCE), 13.07/3 (FCE), and 11.45/3 (GCE).

## Context

This work extends the thermal-model analyses of Abelev et al. (2006) and Becattini et al. (2009, 2010) for p-p collisions at 200 GeV by systematically studying the multiplicity dependence of thermal parameters across three collision systems at LHC energies. It directly compares three statistical ensembles for the first time in this context, showing that the thermodynamic limit is reached only above a multiplicity threshold of about 20 charged hadrons. The results enable future studies of whether even higher beam energies or multiplicities maintain this convergence, and they motivate investigations of finite-volume corrections and alternative ensembles.
