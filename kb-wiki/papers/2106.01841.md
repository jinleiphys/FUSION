# Neutron stars in $f(\mathtt{R,L_m})$ gravity with realistic equations of state: joint-constrains with GW170817, massive pulsars, and the PSR J0030+0451 mass-radius from ${\it NICER}$ data

> In this work we investigate neutron stars (NS) in $f(\mathtt{R,L_m})$ theory of gravity for the case $f(\mathtt{R,L_m}) = \mathtt{R} + \mathtt{L_m} + σ\mathtt{R}\mathtt{L_m}$, where $\mathtt{R}$ is the Ricci scalar and $\mathtt{L_m}$ the Lagrangian matter density. In the term $σ\mathtt{R}\mathtt{L_m}$, $σ$ represents the coupling between the gravitational and particles fields. For the first time the hydrostatic equilibrium equations in the theory are solved considering realistic equations of state and NS masses and radii obtained are subject to joint constrains from massive pulsars, the gravitational wave event GW170817 and from the PSR J0030+0451 mass-radius from NASA's Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (${\it NICER}$) data. We show that in this theory of gravity, the mass-radius results can accommodate massive pulsars, while the general theory of relativity can hardly do it. The theory also can explain the observed NS within the radius region constrained by the GW170817 and PSR J0030+0451 observations for masses around $1.4~M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the specific $f(\mathtt{R,L_m}) = \mathtt{R} + \mathtt{L_m} + \sigma\mathtt{R}\mathtt{L_m}$ gravity, neutron star mass-radius relations can simultaneously satisfy constraints from massive pulsars, the gravitational wave event GW170817, and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, whereas general relativity struggles to do so, but only for certain values of the coupling constant $\sigma$ and only for some equations of state.

## Method

The authors solved the hydrostatic equilibrium equations derived from the $f(\mathtt{R,L_m})$ gravity action for non-rotating, isotropic neutron stars, using a variable change $p' = z$ to handle the system of differential equations. They employed five realistic equations of state (APR4, WFF1, SLy4, MPA1, ALF4) that yield maximum masses near $2.0 M_\odot$ in general relativity, and computed mass-radius curves for four values of the coupling constant $\sigma = 0, 10, 20, 30$. The resulting mass-radius relations were then compared against joint observational constraints from massive pulsars, GW170817, and NICER data.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0740+6620 mass: $2.14_{-0.18}^{+0.20} M_\odot$ (95.4% credibility interval)
- PSR J2215+5135 mass: $\approx 2.27 M_\odot$
- NICER PSR J0030+0451 results: (a) mass $\approx 1.44 M_\odot$, equatorial radius $\approx 13.02$ km (Miller 2019); (b) mass $\approx 1.24 M_\odot$, equatorial radius $\approx 12.71$ km (Riley 2019)
- GW170817 constraint: radius of a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star $\overline{R}_{1.4} = 12.39$ km (Most 2018)
- For APR4 EoS with $\sigma = 30$, maximum mass can surpass $2.5 M_\odot$
- For MPA1 EoS, $\sigma \approx 20$ is the best value to satisfy all constraints; $\sigma = 30$ is disfavored as it lies outside the LIGO-VIRGO region

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron stars in $f(\mathtt{R,L_m})$ gravity by Carvalho (2020) from simple barotropic equations of state to realistic nuclear matter EoS, following the methodology of Lobato (2020) for $f(\mathtt{R,T})$ gravity. It applies joint constraints from the gravitational wave event GW170817 (LIGO-VIRGO 2018, 2019), massive pulsars including PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2020) and PSR J2215+5135 (Linares 2018), and NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 (Miller 2019; Riley 2019). The results enable future work on gravitational wave emission in $f(\mathtt{R,L_m})$ gravity for neutron star mergers, though the authors note that vacuum gravitational wave solutions reduce to general relativity since $\mathtt{L_m} \rightarrow 0$.
