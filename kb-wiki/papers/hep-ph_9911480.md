# New anomalous trajectory in Regge theory

> We show that a new Regge trajectory with α_{f_1} (0) \approx 1 and slope α_{f_1}'(0) \approx 0 explains the features of hadron-hadron scattering and photoproduction of the rho and phi mesons at large energy and momentum transfer. This trajectory with quantum numbers P = C = +1 and odd signature can be considered as a natural partner of the Pomeron which has even signature. The odd signature of the new exchange leads to contributions to the spin-dependent cross sections, which do not vanish at large energy. The links between the anomalous properties of this trajectory, the axial anomaly and the flavor singlet axial vector f_1 (1285) meson are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new Regge trajectory with intercept α_f₁(0) ≈ 1 and slope α_f₁'(0) ≈ 0, associated with the flavor singlet axial vector f₁(1285) meson and the axial anomaly, explains hadron-hadron scattering and vector-meson photoproduction data at large momentum transfer. The hedge is that this trajectory's existence depends on the relation between the axial anomaly and the QCD vacuum holding in real QCD, and that the estimates for the coupling constants and decay constants are likely upper limits due to uncertainties in extrapolating spin-dependent structure functions to low x.

## Method

The authors calculate the contribution of f₁(1285) exchange to elastic proton-proton scattering and to ρ⁰ and φ meson photoproduction using Regge theory with a fixed-pole approximation (α_f₁(t) = 1), employing coupling constants derived from the proton spin analysis and from f₁ → γV decay widths. They compare the resulting differential cross sections and spin asymmetries with experimental data from CHRS (1978), ZEUS, and HERMES collaborations, and extract the intercept of the new trajectory from the E581/704 Collaboration measurement of Δσ_L in polarized pp scattering.

## Key numbers

- Intercept of the new trajectory: α_f₁(0) ≈ 1, with slope α_f₁'(0) ≈ 0.028 GeV⁻² (estimated from the ratio of instanton size to confinement radius, ρ_c/R_conf ≈ 1/3)
- Coupling constant g_f₁NN = 2.5 ± 0.5, extracted from the proton spin analysis using g_A⁰ ≈ 0.3
- Decay constant f_f₁ ≈ 0.11 GeV², derived from the axial vector charge g_A⁰ ≈ 0.3
- Coupling constants for f₁Vγ vertices: |g_f₁ρ⁰γ| = 0.94 GeV⁻², |g_f₁φγ| = 0.18 GeV⁻², from f₁(1285) → γV decay widths (Γ(f₁ → ρ⁰γ) ≈ 1.30 MeV, Γ(f₁ → φγ) ≈ 1.90 × 10⁻² MeV)
- Intercept extracted from E581/704 data on Δσ_L in pp scattering at p = 200 GeV/c: α_f₁(0) = 0.99 ± 0.04, consistent with α_f₁(0) = 0.9 ± 0.2 from E154 Collaboration neutron structure function data
- Pomeron trajectory parameters: α_P(0) ≈ 1.08, α_P' ≈ 0.25 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the Landshoff-Nachtmann model (1987) that relates the soft Pomeron to the gluon condensate and conformal anomaly, proposing a parallel trajectory associated with the axial anomaly and the flavor singlet axial vector current. The new trajectory is presented as a natural partner to the Pomeron (which has even signature) but with odd signature and quantum numbers P = C = +1, and its properties are linked to the topological charge screening in the QCD vacuum as discussed by Veneziano and Shore (1990). The paper enables experimental tests at RHIC through measurements of double spin asymmetries in pp elastic scattering and polarized vector-meson photoproduction, and provides a mechanism for the non-vanishing spin-dependent cross sections at large energy that the Pomeron alone cannot explain.
