# Hindrance in the fusion of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca

> The coupled-channels technique is applied to analyze recent fusion data for $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca. The calculations include the excitations of the low-lying $2^+$, $3^-$ and $5^-$ states in projectile and target, and the influence of mutual excitations as well as the two-phonon quadrupole excitations is also investigated. The ion-ion potential is obtained by double-folding the nuclear densities of the reacting nuclei with the M3Y+repulsion effective interaction but a standard Woods-Saxon potential is also applied. The data exhibit a strong hindrance at low energy compared to calculations that are based on a standard Woods-Saxon potential but they can be reproduced quite well by applying the M3Y+repulsion potential with an adjusted radius of the nuclear density. The influence of the polarization of high-lying states on the extracted radius is discussed.

## Key claim

The fusion hindrance phenomenon, previously established for medium-heavy systems with large negative Q-values, also occurs in the fusion of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, a system with near zero Q-values, but the hindrance is not strong enough to produce an $S$ factor maximum within the measured energy range.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed in the rotating frame approximation, including excitations of the low-lying $2^+$, $3^-$, and $5^-$ states in projectile and target, mutual excitations, and two-phonon quadrupole excitations. The ion-ion potential was obtained either from a standard Woods-Saxon potential or from double-folding nuclear densities with the M3Y+repulsion effective interaction, with the repulsive part constrained by the nuclear incompressibility (K = 223.7 MeV). The radius and diffuseness parameters were adjusted to optimize the fit to fusion data measured down to cross sections below 1 $\mu$b.

## Key numbers

- The standard Woods-Saxon potential (diffuseness $a$ = 0.662 fm, depth $V_0$ = 64.10 MeV) with radius $R_0$ = 8.495 fm gave a poor fit to all data with $\chi^2/N$ = 7.9; a slightly larger radius $R_0$ = 8.562 fm described data above 50 MeV but showed strong hindrance below that energy.
- The best M3Y+repulsion fit (24 channels, M3Y+rep-2 solution) achieved $\chi^2/N$ = 1.66 with parameters $R$ = 3.798 fm, $a_r$ = 0.4295 fm, $v_r$ = 505.6 MeV fm$^3$, producing a Coulomb barrier height $V_{CB}$ = 51.73 MeV and pocket minimum $V_{min}$ = 42.55 MeV.
- The $S$ factor maximum for the M3Y+rep-2 calculation occurs at $E_s$ = 43.2 MeV, with a corresponding fusion cross section of about 0.3 nb.
- The extracted rms matter radius for the M3Y+rep-2 solution is 3.562 fm, compared to the estimated experimental rms matter radius of 3.53(3) fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of fusion hindrance, previously identified in medium-heavy systems with large negative Q-values (Jiang, 2004; Jiang, systematic study), to the symmetric $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca system with near-zero Q-values. The analysis uses the M3Y+repulsion potential approach developed by Misiicu and Esbensen (1975) and applies it to recent data from Stefanini et al. (2010). The results enable a baseline for understanding the symmetric calcium systems, which is necessary before isolating the effect of transfer reactions on the asymmetric $^{40}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca system (measured by Jiang et al., 2010; new measurement for $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca underway by Stefanini et al.).
