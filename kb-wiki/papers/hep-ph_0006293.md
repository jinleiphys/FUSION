# Out-of-equilibrium Photon Production from Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> We study the production of photons through the non-equilibrium relaxation of a disoriented chiral condensate. We propose that to search for non-equilibrium photons in the direct photon measurements of heavy-ion collisions can be a potential test of the formation of disoriented chiral condensates.

## Key claim

Under a "quench" approximation (instantaneous cooling from above the critical temperature to zero temperature), the non-equilibrium relaxation of a disoriented chiral condensate produces photons via parametric amplification that exceed thermal photon yields from a quark-gluon plasma or hadron gas, but only for large initial amplitudes of the neutral pion field (approximately 1 fm^{-1}) and for photon energies around 0.2-0.7 GeV.

## Method

The authors model the disoriented chiral condensate using an O(N+1) linear sigma model with an additional vector meson (omega meson) coupling to the neutral pion and photon fields. They adopt a "quench" scenario where the system is instantaneously cooled from an initial temperature of 220 MeV to zero temperature, then solve the non-equilibrium equations of motion for the mean fields and quantum fluctuations in the large-N limit using Hartree factorization. Photon production is computed from the mode equations of the transverse photon field in Coulomb gauge, which exhibit parametric resonance driven by the oscillating neutral pion mean field.

## Key numbers

- Initial quench temperature: T_i = 220 MeV = 1.1 fm^{-1}
- For initial amplitude zeta(0) = 1 fm^{-1}, photon spectrum peaks at k = 1.35 fm^{-1} and k = 2.7 fm^{-1}
- For initial amplitude zeta(0) = 0.5 fm^{-1}, peak values are almost two orders of magnitude lower than for zeta(0) = 1 fm^{-1}
- The ratio of growth rates between the two peaks (vector meson channel vs. anomalous channel) is approximately 0.5
- Non-thermal photon production exceeds thermal photons for energies around 0.2-0.7 GeV
- Parameters used: m_sigma = 600 MeV, f_pi = 93 MeV, lambda = 4.5, T_c = 200 MeV, h = (120 MeV)^3, m_V = 782 MeV, lambda_V = 0.36

## Context

This work extends the study of photon production from disoriented chiral condensates by Boyanovsky et al. (1997), who considered only the U_A(1) anomalous vertex (pi^0 F tilde F). The authors add a vector meson (omega) channel via the V pi gamma coupling, finding that for large amplitude oscillations this contribution is of the same order as the anomalous interaction. The results suggest that non-equilibrium photons could serve as a cleaner experimental signature of DCC formation than pion distributions, since photons escape the medium with minimal final-state interactions. The work enables future experimental searches at RHIC and LHC for non-thermal photon signals as a test of chiral phase transition dynamics.
