# SU(3) analysis for B(E2) anomaly

> B(E2) anomaly is becoming a hot topic in the field of nuclear structure. Since the B(E2) anomaly was experimentally found, understanding the mechanism of its production has become an important problem. Theoretical studies have found that the SU3-IBM and other extended IBM theories can give explanations, but different mechanisms were found. In this paper, the concept ``SU(3) analysis'' for B(E2) anomaly is proposed, and some new conclusions are obtained. The SU(3) third-order interaction $[L\times Q \times L]^{(0)}$ and level-crossing are both vital for the emergence of the B(E2) anomaly. This technique can help us to better understand the realistic reason of the B(E2) anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the B(E2) anomaly (where the ratio B(E2;4₁⁺→2₁⁺)/B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) is much smaller than 1.0 despite the energy ratio E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) being larger than 2.0) can be understood through "SU(3) analysis" within the SU3-IBM framework, and that both the SU(3) third-order interaction [L×Q×L]⁽⁰⁾ and level-crossing phenomena are vital for its emergence, though whether this relationship is unique remains unclear.

## Method

The authors perform SU(3) analysis by removing the boson number operator (setting α=0) and restoring the SU(3) quadrupole operator Q (with χ=-√7/2) in previously published SU3-IBM Hamiltonians that had successfully described the B(E2) anomaly. They then systematically vary the coefficient η of the [L×Q×L]⁽⁰⁾ interaction and track the evolution of low-lying energy levels and B(E2) values for transitions between 0₁⁺, 2₁⁺, 4₁⁺, and 6₁⁺ states, examining level-crossing patterns and their effect on E2 transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁷⁰Os (N=9 bosons, prolate SU(3) irrep (18,0)): the 4₁⁺ state crosses another 4⁺ state at η=-6.45 keV; the 6₁⁺ state crosses another 6⁺ state at η=-4.25 keV; the 2₁⁺ state crosses another 2⁺ state at η=-12.97 keV (parameters from Wang (2020))
- For ¹⁶⁸Os (N=8 bosons, SU(3) irrep (4,6)) from Zhang et al. (2022): the B₄/₂ ratio is 0.6, larger than the experimental value 0.34
- For ¹⁶⁸Os (N=8 bosons, SU(3) irrep (2,4)) from Zhang et al. (2022): the 4₁⁺ state crosses another 4⁺ state at η=0.023 MeV; the 6₁⁺ state crosses another 6⁺ state at η=0.0301 MeV
- For ¹⁶⁸Os (N=8 bosons, prolate SU(3) irrep (16,0)) from Pan et al. (2024): the 4₁⁺ state crosses another 4⁺ state at η=-44.8 keV; the 6₁⁺ state crosses another 6⁺ state at η=-29.4 keV
- For ¹⁶⁸Os (N=8 bosons, oblate SU(3) irrep (0,8)) from Zhang and Teng (2025): the 2₁⁺ and 2₂⁺ states intersect at η=12.1 keV; the 4₁⁺ and 4₂⁺ states intersect at η=4.6 keV; the 6₁⁺ and 6₂⁺ states intersect at η=2.5 keV

## Context

This work extends the SU3-IBM framework developed by Wang (2020, 2022, 2023) and others (Zhang et al. 2022, 2024; Pan et al. 2024) that had previously provided multiple explanations for the B(E2) anomaly, which was experimentally observed in nuclei such as ¹⁷⁰Os (Grahn et al. 2016) and ¹⁶⁸Os (Cederwall et al. 2018). The paper distinguishes between two mechanisms: level-crossing within the SU(3) symmetry limit (where crossing states belong to different SU(3) irreps, forcing E2 transitions to zero) and the rigid triaxial rotor effect (Zhang et al. 2014). The SU(3) analysis technique enables future work to determine which mechanism operates in realistic nuclei and to search for additional anomalous B(E2) values in higher-spin transitions and 2₁⁺→0₁⁺ transitions, such as those reported for ⁷²Zn (Louchart et al. 2013) and ¹⁶⁶Os (Stolze et al. 2021).
