# Accessing gluon polarization with high-$P_T$ hadrons in SIDIS

> A recent global QCD analysis of jet production and other polarized scattering data has found the presence of negative solutions for the gluon helicity distribution in the proton, $Δg$, along with the traditional $Δg > 0$ solutions. We consider polarized semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering for hadrons produced with large transverse momentum as a means of constraining the dependence of $Δg$ on the parton momentum fraction, $x$. Focusing on the double longitudinal spin asymmetry, we identify the kinematics relevant for future experiments at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider which are particularly sensitive to the polarized gluon channel and could discriminate between the different $Δg$ behaviors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that polarized semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) with high-transverse-momentum hadrons can discriminate between positive and negative solutions for the gluon helicity distribution \(\Delta g\) in the proton, which a recent JAM global QCD analysis found are both allowed by inclusive jet data from RHIC because the double spin asymmetry for inclusive jet production is quadratic in the parton polarization.

## Method

The authors compute the double longitudinal spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}^h\) for charged pion production in polarized SIDIS using collinear factorization at leading order in the strong coupling \(\alpha_s\), with the partonic cross sections calculated from the four tree-level \(2 \to 2\) scattering diagrams (quark-initiated with quark or gluon fragmentation, and gluon-initiated with quark or antiquark fragmentation). They then perform numerical simulations at kinematics relevant for Jefferson Lab (12 GeV and a possible 22 GeV upgrade) and for the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), using the two sets of \(\Delta g\) solutions from the JAM analysis (Zhou 2022) to predict the asymmetry and assess sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The lower bound on the four-momentum transfer squared is set at \(Q^2 > m_c^2 = (1.28\ \text{GeV})^2\).
- The invariant mass squared of the final hadronic state is constrained to \(W^2 > W_{\text{min}}^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For Jefferson Lab kinematics, the center-of-mass energies are \(\sqrt{s} = 4.8\ \text{GeV}\) (12 GeV beam) and \(\sqrt{s} = 6.5\ \text{GeV}\) (22 GeV beam).
- For EIC kinematics, the center-of-mass energy is \(\sqrt{s} = 140\ \text{GeV}\).
- The fragmentation variable is restricted to \(0.2 < z < 0.8\).
- The minimum electron scattering angle for CLAS12 is \(\theta_{\text{min}} \approx 5^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the recent JAM global QCD analysis (Zhou 2022) which found that negative \(\Delta g\) solutions are allowed by inclusive jet data from STAR and PHENIX, and earlier work by Jager et al. who discussed constraints on the sign of \(\Delta g\) from inclusive pion production in polarized \(pp\) collisions. The paper identifies polarized SIDIS with high-\(P_T\) hadrons as a process where the gluon contribution enters at the same order in \(\alpha_s\) as the quark contribution, making it linear in \(\Delta g\) and thus capable of resolving the sign ambiguity that inclusive jet data cannot settle.
