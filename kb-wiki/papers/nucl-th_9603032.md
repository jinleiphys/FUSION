# Influence of Different Collective Coordinates on Spontaneous Fission Process in Fm Isotopes

> We study the role of different collective degrees of freedom in spontaneous fission process of even--even Fm isotopes. To find a proper collective space we examine a reach collection of nuclear shape parameters $\{β_λ\}$, with $λ$=2,3,4,5,6 and 8; as well as the paring degrees of freedom i.e. proton $Δ_{p}$ and neutron $Δ_{n}$ pairing gaps. On the basis of the multidimensional dynamic--programming method (MDPM) the optimal collective space $\{β_{2}, β_{4}, β_{6}, Δ_{p}, Δ_{n}\}$ for Fm isotopes was found.

## Key claim
The optimal collective space for describing spontaneous fission half-lives in even-even Fm isotopes is five-dimensional, comprising shape parameters β₂, β₄, β₆ and proton and neutron pairing gaps Δp, Δn; this conclusion is conditional on the multidimensional dynamic-programming method (MDPM) treating all coordinates as independent dynamical variables, unlike prior work that handled only β₂ and β₄ dynamically.

## Method
The authors calculated spontaneous fission half-lives T_sf for even-even Fm isotopes using a one-dimensional WKB semiclassical approximation, with the action integral minimized via the multidimensional dynamic-programming method (MDPM) extended to four dimensions. The potential energy was computed using the macroscopic-microscopic model with a Yukawa-plus-exponential finite-range macroscopic part and Strutinsky shell correction based on a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with BCS pairing; the inertia tensor was calculated in the cranking approximation. They examined collective spaces including even-multipolarity parameters β₂, β₄, β₆, β₈, a combined odd-multipolarity parameter β₃₅, and the pairing gaps Δp and Δn.

## Key numbers
- Experimental T_sf for even-even Fm isotopes (N = 142, 144, ..., 158) form approximately two sides of an acute-angled triangle with a vertex at N = 152.
- T_sf descends by about ten orders of magnitude when passing from ²⁵⁴Fm to ²⁵⁸Fm.
- The zero-point energy in the fission direction is defined as V(X⁰_λ) + 0.5 MeV.
- Proton and neutron pairing gaps reduce T_sf for Fm isotopes with N > 152 by about 3 orders of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends earlier dynamical calculations by Staszczak and Szymanski (1995) and by Sobiczewski and Kuchta (1987), which treated only β₂ and β₄ dynamically while other degrees of freedom were found by potential energy minimization. The study also builds on the proposal by M. Brack and collaborators (1972) to include pairing-field parameters dynamically, previously applied with Nilsson single-particle potentials by Sobiczewski and Patyk (1992) and by Staszczak and Patyk (1994). The optimal five-dimensional collective space identified here enables improved theoretical predictions of spontaneous fission half-lives for heavy Fm isotopes, particularly the rapid decrease beyond N = 152.
