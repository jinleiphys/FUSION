# The quark gap equation in light-cone gauge

> We calculate the quark self-energy correction in light-cone gauge motivated by distribution amplitudes whose definition implies a Wilson line. The latter serves to preserve the gauge invariance of the hadronic amplitudes and becomes trivial in light-cone gauge. Therefore, the calculation of the distribution amplitudes simplifies significantly provided that wave functions and propagators are obtained in that gauge. In here, we explore the corresponding Dyson-Schwinger equation in its leading truncation and with a dressed vertex derived from a Ward identity in light-cone gauge. The quark's mass and wave renormalization functions, as well as a third complex-valued amplitude, are found to depend on the relative orientation of the quark momentum and a light-like four-vector, which expresses a geometric gauge dependence of the propagator.

## Key claim

The quark propagator in light-cone gauge exhibits a geometric gauge dependence expressed through the relative orientation of the quark momentum and a light-like four-vector, but this exploratory nonperturbative calculation finds that the third complex-valued amplitude C(p,n) is approximately zero across all momenta, while the mass and wave renormalization functions show angular dependence only below 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in light-cone gauge using the rainbow truncation and a dressed quark-gluon vertex derived from a Ward-Fradkin-Green-Takahashi identity (WFGTI) in that gauge. They employed a nonperturbative gluon propagator with a dynamical gluon mass from Cornwall's pinch technique, and projected out three scalar dressing functions A(p,n), B(p,n), and C(p,n) using Dirac traces. The coupled integral equations were solved iteratively on a momentum grid with renormalization conditions imposed at mu = 2 GeV, and the angular dependence was studied by varying the orientation of the quark momentum relative to the light-like vector n.

## Key numbers

- Renormalization point: mu = 2 GeV, with conditions B(mu,n) = m(mu) = 25 MeV and A(mu,n) = 1
- Quark mass function at zero momentum with bare vertex: M(0) approximately 210 MeV
- Dynamical gluon mass parameter: m_g = 0.3 GeV
- Gluon dressing parameter omega = 0.15 GeV
- Gluon dressing parameter b = 2.0 x 10^(-4) for bare vertex and b = 9.0 x 10^(-3) for WFGTI vertex
- Numerical cutoff Lambda = 100 GeV
- Angular dependence studied for angles y_p and z_p in the range -1 to +1, with epsilon approximately 10^(-3) for the principal value treatment of the (n.q)^(-1) pole

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to light-cone gauge, motivated by the simplification of parton distribution amplitudes and parton distribution functions when Wilson lines become trivial in this gauge. Prior work by Serna (2020, 2022), Chang (2013), and Shi (2015) addressed parton distributions within a DSE and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach in Landau gauge assuming the Wilson line effect is negligible, while Mezrag (2016) and Shi (2018) projected Bethe-Salpeter wave functions on the light front to avoid Wilson lines. The results enable future calculations of mesonic distribution amplitudes directly in light-cone gauge using nonperturbative quark propagators, though the authors note that a full treatment requires solving the DSE on a three-dimensional mesh for the angular dependence of the dressing functions in the integral kernels.
