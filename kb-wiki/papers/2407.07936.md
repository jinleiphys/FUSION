# Towards an Interpretation of the First Measurements of Energy Correlators in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Energy correlators have recently been proposed as a class of jet substructure observables that directly link experimental measurements of the asymptotic energy flux with the field theoretic description of the underlying microscopic dynamics. This link holds particular promise in heavy-ion physics, where both experimental measurements and theoretical interpretations are inherently complex. With recent measurements of energy correlators in proton-proton collisions, the first measurement of these observables on inclusive jets in heavy-ion collisions underscores the importance of a theoretical understanding of their behavior in this complex environment. In this manuscript, we extend our previous calculations to account for several effects necessary for a qualitative understanding of the behavior of energy correlators on inclusive jets in heavy-ion collisions. Through a semi-analytic approach implemented in a hydrodynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP), we account for medium-induced radiation with leading broadening effects, selection biases arising from energy loss, and a description of the confinement transition. Our results represent a crucial first step towards interpreting the measurements of energy correlators on inclusive jets in heavy-ion collisions, which marks a significant milestone in connecting heavy-ion experiment and fundamental quantum field theory, in the quest to disentangle the microscopic dynamics of the QGP.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous calculations of energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions to include a hydrodynamically expanding medium, confinement transition effects, and selection biases from energy loss, but emphasize that this work provides only a qualitative understanding and leaves open many areas for future improvement, particularly the incorporation of medium response and further perturbative calculations.

## Method

The authors compute the two-point energy correlator (EEC) on inclusive quark jets in heavy-ion collisions using a semi-analytic approach that incorporates medium-induced radiation with leading broadening corrections, a hydrodynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP) modeled with 2+1 viscous hydrodynamics, selection biases from energy loss via a probability distribution for jet energy loss, and a model for the confinement transition. The calculation averages over jet trajectories within a given centrality class using a nuclear overlap distribution, with the medium information encoded in the local time-dependent jet quenching coefficient \(\hat{q}(t) = k_{\rm HO} T^3(\xi(t))\), where \(k_{\rm HO}\) is the only free parameter.

## Key numbers

* The hydrodynamic simulation begins at an initial proper time of \(\tau_{\rm hydro}=1\,\rm{fm/c}\) and uses a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of \(\eta/s = 0.16\).
* The freeze-out temperature of the hydrodynamic simulation is \(T_{\rm f} = 140\) MeV.
* Results are presented for \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =5.02\) TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, for the 0-10% and 20-30% centrality classes.
* The medium-induced splitting formalism is currently limited to static media with a length of up to \(L=2\) fm for the \(\gamma \rightarrow q \bar q\) channel in a related numerical method.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Andres 2022, Andres 2023) that used a static "brick" description of the QGP and neglected transverse momentum broadening, by incorporating a hydrodynamically expanding medium, broadening corrections, and selection bias effects. It also builds on earlier analytical studies of energy correlators in heavy-ion jets (Andres 2022, Andres 2023, Barata 2023) that relied on simplified models. The results aim to provide a qualitative framework for interpreting the first measurements of energy correlators on inclusive jets in heavy-ion collisions by the CMS experiment, which were presented simultaneously with this paper.
