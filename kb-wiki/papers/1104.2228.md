# One-neutron halo structure by the ratio method

> We present a new observable to study halo nuclei. This new observable is a particular ratio of angular distributions for elastic breakup and scattering. For one-neutron halo nuclei, it is shown to be independent of the reaction mechanism and to provide significant information about the structure of the projectile, including binding energy, partial-wave configuration, and radial wave function of the halo. This observable offers new capabilities for the study of nuclear structure far from stability.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a specific ratio of angular distributions for elastic breakup and elastic scattering of one-neutron halo nuclei is, for all practical purposes, independent of the reaction mechanism and the choice of optical potentials, thereby providing direct access to projectile structure information including binding energy, partial-wave configuration, and radial wave function of the halo.

## Method

The authors model the collision of a one-neutron halo projectile (core plus valence neutron) with a target as a three-body Schrodinger equation. They derive the ratio observable analytically within the Recoil Excitation and Breakup (REB) model, which neglects the neutron-target interaction and uses the adiabatic approximation, obtaining a form factor \(|F_{E,0}|^2\) that depends only on projectile structure. They then test the accuracy of this approximation by comparing the REB prediction to full Dynamical Eikonal Approximation (DEA) calculations for \({}^{11}\)Be on lead and carbon targets at around 69 MeV/nucleon, varying the core-target and neutron-target optical potentials.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{11}\)Be impinging on Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon, the form factor \(|F_{E,0}|^2\) at a c-n relative energy \(E = 0.1\) MeV is computed as a function of scattering angle up to \(10^\circ\).
* The binding energy sensitivity is tested by varying the \(s\)-wave ground state binding energy to 50 keV, 0.5 MeV, and 5 MeV, showing strong dependence of the form factor on this value.
* The partial-wave configuration sensitivity is tested by fitting the 0.5-MeV binding energy in the \(0p_{1/2}\) and \(0d_{5/2}\) orbitals, in addition to the \(1s_{1/2}\) ground state.
* The ratio for \({}^{11}\)Be on Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon is compared to the ratio for \({}^{11}\)Be on C at 67 MeV/nucleon, showing excellent agreement with the REB form factor \(|F_{E,0}|^2\) despite very different reaction mechanisms (Coulomb-dominated on Pb versus nuclear-dominated on C).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Capel, Johnson, and Nunes (2010) which showed that elastic scattering and breakup angular distributions for one-neutron halo nuclei exhibit very similar features. The ratio method directly addresses the problem identified by Capel, Baur, and Nunes (2004) that uncertainties in optical potentials for the core-target interaction hinder quantitative analysis of experimental data. The new observable enables extraction of halo structure information (binding energy, angular momentum, radial wave function) from reaction measurements without relying on poorly known optical potentials, and is applicable to short-lived nuclei such as \({}^{31}\)Ne that are too unstable for usual spectroscopic techniques.
