# On the sensitivity of transverse flow to the isospin-dependent cross-section and symmetry energy

> We study the transverse flow for systems having various N/Z ratios. We find the transverse flow is sensitive to N/Z ratio and, in fact, increases with N/Z of the system. The relative contribution of symmetry energy and isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross section is also investigated. We find the greater sensitivity of symmetry energy in the lighter systems compared to heavier ones.

## Key claim

The transverse flow in heavy-ion collisions increases with the N/Z ratio of the system, and the relative contribution of symmetry energy versus isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section depends on system mass: symmetry energy has greater sensitivity in lighter systems (Ca+Ca) compared to heavier ones (Xe+Xe), where the isospin dependence of the cross section dominates.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model for reactions of Ca+Ca and Xe+Xe series with N/Z ratios of 1.0, 1.6, and 2.0 at an incident energy of 100 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b/b_max = 0.2-0.4. They used a soft equation of state with the standard isospin- and energy-dependent cross section reduced by 20% (sigma = 0.8 sigma_nn_free). To isolate contributions, they separately set the symmetry energy strength to zero, made the cross section isospin independent (sigma_np = sigma_nn), and applied both modifications simultaneously, then decomposed the directed transverse momentum into mean-field and collision contributions.

## Key numbers

- Reactions studied: Ca+Ca series (40Ca+40Ca, 52Ca+52Ca, 60Ca+60Ca) and Xe+Xe series (110Xe+110Xe, 140Xe+140Xe, 162Xe+162Xe) at 100 MeV/nucleon incident energy.
- Impact parameter range: b/b_max = 0.2-0.4.
- Cross section used: sigma = 0.8 sigma_nn_free (standard isospin- and energy-dependent cross section reduced by 20%).
- Saturation time for transverse flow: approximately 100 fm/c for lighter colliding nuclei and 150 fm/c for heavy colliding nuclei in the present energy domain.
- The maximum decrease in flow when both symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross section are removed occurs for 60Ca+60Ca (highest N/Z = 2.0), decreasing as systems become more symmetric.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Gautam and collaborators (2010) on isospin effects in flow and its disappearance for isobaric pairs, where Coulomb repulsion was found to dominate over symmetry energy. It also builds on a study by Gautam and Sood (2010) on the N/Z dependence of the energy of vanishing flow, which pointed to the role of symmetry energy. The results enable future experiments with radioactive ion beams at facilities such as FAIR, RIKEN, and GANIL to use lighter neutron-rich systems as better probes to constrain the symmetry energy, since the sensitivity is greater in lighter systems than in heavier ones.
