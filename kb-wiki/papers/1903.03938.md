# Electrical conductivity and Hall conductivity of hot and dense hadron gas in a magnetic field: a relaxation time approach

> We estimate the electrical conductivity and the Hall conductivity of hot and dense hadron gas using the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann transport equation in the presence of electromagnetic field. We have investigated the temperature and the baryon chemical potential dependence of these transport coefficients in presence of magnetic field. The explicit calculation is performed within the ambit of the hadron resonance gas model. We find that the electrical conductivity decreases in the presence of magnetic field. The Hall conductivity on the other hand shows a non monotonic behavior with respect to the dependence on magnetic field. We argue that for a pair plasma (particle-anti particle plasma) where $μ_B=0$, Hall conductivity vanishes. Only for non vanishing baryon chemical potential Hall conductivity has non zero value. We also estimate the electrical conductivity and the Hall conductivity as a function of the center of mass energy along the freeze out curve as may be relevant for relativistic heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of hot and dense hadron gas decreases in the presence of a magnetic field, while the Hall conductivity shows non-monotonic behavior with respect to magnetic field strength; the Hall conductivity vanishes exactly at zero baryon chemical potential due to exact cancellation between particle and antiparticle contributions, and only becomes non-zero when the baryon chemical potential is finite.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation for a system of charged hadrons in external electric and magnetic fields, using the hadron resonance gas model with all hadrons and resonances up to a mass cutoff of 2.6 GeV. The relaxation time for each hadron species was computed using a thermal-averaged hard-sphere scattering cross section with uniform radius of 0.5 fm for all mesons and baryons, assuming Boltzmann statistics. The electrical and Hall conductivities were then evaluated from the resulting distribution function by integrating over momentum space for each charged species.

## Key numbers

- At zero magnetic field and zero baryon chemical potential, normalized electrical conductivity σ^el/T decreases monotonically with increasing temperature
- At B = 0.05 GeV^2 and μ_B = 0, the normalized electrical conductivity σ^el/T shows non-monotonic temperature dependence, first increasing then decreasing with temperature
- At B = 0.05 GeV^2, the normalized electrical conductivity σ^el/T increases with baryon chemical potential (comparing μ_B = 0, 300 MeV, and 600 MeV)
- At μ_B = 100 MeV, the normalized Hall conductivity σ^H/T shows non-monotonic behavior with temperature for magnetic fields of B = 0.01, 0.03, and 0.05 GeV^2
- At B = 0.05 GeV^2, the normalized Hall conductivity σ^H/T increases with baryon chemical potential (comparing μ_B = 20, 300, and 600 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electrical conductivity in hadron resonance gas models (such as Greif et al. and Kadam et al.) by including the effects of a magnetic field and calculating the Hall conductivity for the hadronic phase, which had not been studied earlier. The authors note that Hall conductivity in QGP at finite baryon chemical potential was recently investigated by Feng (2017), and the present work provides the corresponding calculation for the hadronic phase relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR and NICA where finite baryon density is expected. The results enable magnetohydrodynamic simulations of the hadronic phase evolution in heavy-ion collisions with finite electrical and Hall conductivities.
