# Exclusive reactions in QCD

> We review the theory of hard exclusive scattering in Quantum Chromodynamics. After recalling the classical counting rules which describe the leading scale dependence of form factors and exclusive cross-sections at fixed angle, the pedagogical example of the pion form factor is developped in some detail in order to show explicitely what factorization means in the QCD framework. The picture generalizes to many hard reactions which are at the heart of the ELFE project. We briefly present the concepts of color transparency.

# Exclusive Reactions in QCD

## Key claim

Hard exclusive scattering in QCD can be factorized into a convolution of non-perturbative distribution amplitudes (describing how valence quarks share the hadron momentum) with a perturbatively calculable hard scattering amplitude, but this factorization is strictly valid only when the exchanged gluon virtuality remains large, i.e., when the light-cone momentum fractions x and x' do not approach zero, and transverse degrees of freedom must be reintroduced for intermediate momentum transfers where Sudakov suppression of large transverse sizes becomes important.

## Method

The paper develops the QCD factorization formalism for hard exclusive processes using the pion electromagnetic form factor as the pedagogical example, deriving the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, the light-cone wave function, and the distribution amplitude φ(x) for the pion. It then computes the Born-level hard scattering amplitude in Feynman gauge, performs the color algebra to obtain the form factor expression, and analyzes radiative corrections to show that large collinear logarithms must be resummed into the scale-dependent distribution amplitude via an integro-differential evolution equation with a characteristic splitting kernel V(u,x). The formalism is extended to the proton distribution amplitude, proton magnetic form factor, real and virtual Compton scattering, and meson photoproduction, with discussion of transverse degrees of freedom and Sudakov suppression.

## Key numbers

- Pion form factor scaling: F_π(Q²) ∝ 1/Q² at large Q²
- Nucleon form factor scaling: F_N(Q²) ∝ 1/Q⁴ at large Q²
- Pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 92 MeV, which normalizes the distribution amplitude via ∫₀¹ dx φ(x) = √2 f_π
- Color factor for the pion form factor Born amplitude: C_F/3 = 4/9
- Evolution exponents for the pion distribution amplitude: λ₀ = 0, λ₂ = -0.62, with higher λ_n decreasing
- Evolution exponents for the proton distribution amplitude: λ₀ = 2/27, λ₁ = 20/81, λ₂ = 24/81
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: κ = 1.79
- The Q⁴ G_M(Q²) scaling is observed in the Q² range 5 - 30 GeV²
- The πγ transition form factor data from e⁺e⁻ colliders cover Q² in the range 2 - 8 GeV²
- The Sudakov-improved approach becomes relevant for momentum transfers above about 5 GeV²
- The asymptotic pion distribution amplitude: φ(x, Q→∞) ∼ 6√2 f_π x(1-x)
- The Chernyak-Zhitnitsky distribution amplitude uses Q₀ ≈ 500 MeV as the reference scale

## Context

This lecture reviews the QCD factorization approach to hard exclusive reactions, extending the classical counting rules of Brodsky and Farrar (1973) which predict power-law falloffs for form factors and fixed-angle cross sections. The formalism builds on the work of Farrar and Jackson (1979) on the pion form factor, the Bethe-Salpeter equation of Salpeter (1951), and the light-cone wave function approach of Brodsky. The paper discusses the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky (1984) distribution amplitude derived from QCD sum rules, and addresses the controversy over whether the proton magnetic form factor at accessible Q² is still dominated by long-distance soft physics. The work enables experimental extraction of distribution amplitudes from exclusive scattering data at facilities like ELFE, and provides the theoretical foundation for color transparency measurements where the transparency ratio T_r = σ_Nucleus/(Z σ_Nucleon) should approach unity at asymptotically large Q².
