# Spin-thermal shear coupling in a relativistic fluid

> We show that spin polarization of a fermion in a relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium can be generated by the symmetric derivative of the four-temperature vector, defined as thermal shear. As a consequence, besides vorticity, acceleration and temperature gradient, also the shear tensor contributes to the polarization of particles in a fluid. This contribution to the spin polarization vector, which is entirely non-dissipative, adds to the well known term proportional to thermal vorticity and may thus have important consequences for the solution of the local polarization puzzles observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors show that spin polarization of a fermion in a relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium can be generated by the symmetric derivative of the four-temperature vector (thermal shear), in addition to the well-known contribution from thermal vorticity. This new contribution is entirely non-dissipative but depends on a specific 3D integration hypersurface, breaking local covariance.

## Method

The authors use Zubarev's method of the stationary non-equilibrium density operator to describe a relativistic quantum fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium. They expand the four-temperature vector in a Taylor series around a point x, separating the gradients into antisymmetric (thermal vorticity) and symmetric (thermal shear) parts, and compute the linear response of the Wigner function to these gradients. The spin polarization vector is then obtained from the particle term of the Wigner function, with the thermal shear contribution evaluated by applying Gauss' theorem to convert a 3D hypersurface integral into a 4D volume term and a boundary term.

## Key numbers

- The new contribution to the spin polarization vector is given by equation (main): S_xi^mu(p) = -1/(4m) epsilon^{munu sigma tau} (p_tau p^rho / epsilon) [int_Sigma dSigma dot p n_F (1-n_F) hat{t}_nu xi_{sigma rho}] / [int_Sigma dSigma dot p n_F], where xi is the thermal shear tensor and hat{t} is the time direction in the QGP frame.
- In the non-relativistic limit, the spin polarization from thermal shear reduces to S_xi = (1/8) v x [int d^3 x n_F (1-n_F) nabla(1/T)] / [int d^3 x n_F], with all remaining terms including the spin-shear coupling being purely relativistic.

## Context

This work extends the standard formula for spin polarization from thermal vorticity (Becattini, 2013) which had failed to reproduce momentum-dependent measurements in relativistic heavy ion collisions (Adam, 2019; Niida, 2018; Becattini, 2020). The new thermal shear contribution may help resolve the "local polarization puzzles" observed in those experiments. The authors note a simultaneous similar study by Liu (2021) and state that a forthcoming paper (Becattini, 2021) will show the numerical impact of this new term.
