# EMC and Polarized EMC Effects in Nuclei

> We determine nuclear structure functions and quark distributions for $^7$Li, $^{11}$B, $^{15}$N and $^{27}$Al. For the nucleon bound state we solve the covariant quark-diquark equations in a confining Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, which yields excellent results for the free nucleon structure functions. The nucleus is described using a relativistic shell model, including mean scalar and vector fields that couple to the quarks in the nucleon. The nuclear structure functions are then obtained as a convolution of the structure function of the bound nucleon with the light-cone nucleon distributions. We find that we are readily able to reproduce the EMC effect in finite nuclei and confirm earlier nuclear matter studies that found a large polarized EMC effect.

## Key claim

The authors reproduce the unpolarized EMC effect in finite nuclei and confirm earlier nuclear matter studies that predicted a large polarized EMC effect, but the polarized effect is defined relative to naive expectations using polarization factors from shell-model wavefunctions, and the magnitude depends on the specific nucleus and the choice of multipole (K=1 vs. helicity M=J) definition.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear quark distributions using a convolution formalism: bound-nucleon quark distributions are obtained from a confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with quark-diquark Faddeev equations, while nucleon distributions in the nucleus are determined from a relativistic shell model with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector mean fields (depths S0 = -194 MeV, V0 = 133 MeV, range R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm, diffuseness a = 0.65 fm). Medium effects are incorporated by shifting the Bjorken scaling variable via the vector field and modifying the quark mass via the scalar field, with Fermi motion included through convolution with smearing functions.

## Key numbers

- Scalar field depth S0 = -194 MeV and vector field depth V0 = 133 MeV, taken from earlier nuclear matter calculations.
- For ^7Li, the mass per nucleon Mbar_N = 933 MeV; for ^11B, Mbar_N = 931 MeV; for ^15N, Mbar_N = 929 MeV; for ^27Al, Mbar_N = 930 MeV.
- The first moment of the spin-dependent u-quark distribution in the free proton is 0.97, decreasing to 0.91 in ^7Li, 0.88 in ^11B, 0.87 in ^15N, and 0.87 in ^27Al.
- The nucleon axial charge g_A decreases from 1.267 (free) to 1.19 in ^7Li, 1.16 in ^11B, 1.15 in ^15N, and 1.15 in ^27Al.
- The polarized EMC effect is roughly twice the unpolarized EMC effect for ^11B and ^27Al, except for ^7Li at x > 0.6 where the multipole result is smaller.
- The ^15N polarized EMC ratio goes to zero at x ~ 0.8 because the full g_1A changes sign at that point.

## Context

This work extends earlier nuclear matter studies by Cloet et al. (2005) that found a large polarized EMC effect in infinite nuclear matter, and applies the same covariant quark-diquark NJL model to finite nuclei (^7Li, ^11B, ^15N, ^27Al). It also builds on the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1987) and Saito (1995) and the convolution formalism of Jaffe (1985). The results enable experimental searches for medium modifications of spin structure functions in polarized deep inelastic scattering on light nuclei, and suggest that ^15N may not be a good candidate due to sign-change behavior.
