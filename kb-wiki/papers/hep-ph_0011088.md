# Single particle strength restoration and nuclear transparency in high Q^2 exclusive (e,e'p) reactions

> Quenching of the single particle strength which previously precluded unambiguous measurement of nuclear transparency in quasifree (e,e'p) reactions at high momentum transfer is evaluated from the cross sections of measured processes. We have demonstrated evidence of the single particle strength restoration with increase of the momentum transfer in exclusive (e,e'p) reactions and argue that in practically interesting cases of study the nuclear transparency in these processes quenching is weakly depends on the probability of short range nucleon correlations. It is shown that use of Glauber approximation well describes NE18 and E94-139 (e,e'p) data at the momentum transfers 1 GeV^2 < Q^2 < 3 GeV^2. This gives a further support to our observation that the quenching of nuclear levels strongly depends on the resolution (Q^2)and practically disappears at Q^2>1GeV^2.

# Single particle strength restoration and nuclear transparency in high Q^2 exclusive (e,e'p) reactions

## Key claim

The quenching of single-particle valence strength, which is about 40% at low momentum transfer (Q^2 ≤ 0.3 GeV^2), practically disappears at Q^2 ≥ 1 GeV^2 in exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, with at most a modest 10% quenching possibly surviving. This Q^2 dependence of quenching is a natural consequence of the transition from photon interaction with quasiparticles at low Q^2 to interaction with free nucleons at high Q^2, and the authors argue that the Glauber approximation well describes the data without any significant quenching for Q^2 between 1 and 4 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors performed a joint analysis of exclusive A(e,e'p) and inclusive A(e,e')X data at Q^2 ≥ 1 GeV^2, using Hartree-Fock-Skyrme wave functions and a Glauber-type model for final-state interactions. They calculated the nuclear transparency T from the ratio of measured cross sections to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) cross section, where the PWIA normalization was constrained by inclusive (e,e') data at x ≈ 1 and Q^2 ≈ 1-2 GeV^2. The differential (e,e'p) cross sections for carbon, iron, and gold at Q^2 = 1.8 GeV^2 were compared to calculations using distorted momentum distributions from the Glauber model with no free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor for 12C at low Q^2 (≤ 0.3 GeV^2): η(12C) = 0.57 ± 0.02, determined from low-energy (e,e'p) data
- Quenching factor at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 for carbon: approximately 0.9, extracted from NE18 differential cross sections
- Correction factors f(A) used in prior NE18 and E91-013 analyses: f(12C) = 0.9, f(56Fe) = 0.82, f(197Au) = 0.78
- Short-range correlation contribution to the normalization integral for carbon: approximately 5-7%
- Inelastic correction to inclusive (e,e') data at x=1: about 3% at Q^2 = 1.0 GeV^2 and 6% at Q^2 = 2.0 GeV^2
- Correction factors to renormalize prior transparency values to the authors' spectral function: 1.02 for carbon, 0.896 for iron, 0.83 for gold
- Momentum transfer range analyzed: 1 GeV^2 < Q^2 < 3 GeV^2 for NE18 and E94-139 data; 2 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 4 GeV^2 for E91-013 and NE18 data
- The authors' calculations show agreement with E91-013 differential data for bound proton momenta ≤ 200 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Frankfurt, Strikman, and Zhalov (1999) on the Q^2 dependence of quenching in (e,e'p) reactions. It directly addresses the interpretation of nuclear transparency measurements from the NE18 experiment at SLAC and the E91-013 experiment at Jefferson Lab, which had previously used independent particle shell model spectral functions with additional renormalization factors f(A) as described by the experimental collaborations. The authors demonstrate that the quenching observed at low Q^2 (reviewed by Mogad, SWH, and Kelly (1996)) is not applicable at high Q^2, resolving a discrepancy where using the low-energy quenching factor would predict transparency values of about 0.8 for carbon at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, much higher than Glauber theory predictions. This work enables more reliable searches for color transparency effects in high Q^2 A(e,e'p) reactions, as the nuclear transparency can now be calculated with few-percent accuracy using the Glauber approximation for Q^2 ≥ 2 GeV^2.
