# Staggering of the B(M1) value as a fingerprint of specific chiral bands structure

> Nuclear chirality has been intensively studdied for the last several years in the context of experimental as well as theoretical approach. Characteristic gamma selection rules have been predicted for the strong chiral symmetry breaking limit that has been observed in Cs isotopes. The presented analysis shows that the gamma selection rules cannot be attributed only to chiral symmetry breaking. The selection rules relate to structural composition of the chiral rotational bands, i.e. to odd particle configuration and the deformation of the core.

# Staggering of the B(M1) Value as a Fingerprint of Specific Chiral Bands Structure

## Key claim
The gamma selection rules (staggering of B(M1) values) previously attributed to chiral symmetry breaking cannot be uniquely assigned to that phenomenon alone; they instead depend on the structural composition of the chiral rotational bands, specifically the odd particle configuration and the triaxial deformation of the core. The staggering effect is an additional property that may be absent even when chiral partner bands exist.

## Method
The author presents a theoretical analysis of the chiral symmetry operator R_y^T (T-signature) and its commutation relations with the nuclear Hamiltonian and electromagnetic transition operators. Using a basis of left-handed and right-handed intrinsic states |L⟩ and |R⟩, the formalism derives expressions for inband and interband electromagnetic transition matrix elements in the strong chiral symmetry breaking limit, showing that B(M1) and B(E2) probabilities in both partner bands should be identical when tunneling disappears. The analysis then examines how additional quantum numbers (j_ν, j_π, j_c) from the core and odd nucleons produce the staggering effect, referencing the CQPC model of Droste and the single-j shell Hamiltonian of Hamamoto.

## Key numbers
- Tunneling frequency between left- and right-handed configurations: f ≈ 2.5 × 10^19 Hz, estimated from ΔE = 100 keV typical energy splitting observed in chiral nuclei
- The staggering of B(M1) transition probability is very weak or absent in 104Rh (Suzuki) and 135Nd (Mukhopadhyay 2007), compared to Cs isotopes (Grodner 2006)
- In 135Nd, the corresponding transition probabilities in the partner bands are almost equal, pointing to the strong chiral symmetry breaking limit
- The staggering effect vanishes rapidly when γ-deformation deviates from 30° in the CQPC model (Droste)

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear chirality from the strong symmetry breaking limit observed in Cs isotopes (Grodner 2006, Tonev 2006) to explain why B(M1) staggering is absent in other candidate chiral nuclei. The analysis contradicts the assumption that gamma selection rules uniquely signal chirality, instead building on the Hamiltonian model of Hamamoto for a triaxially deformed core coupled to one proton particle and one neutron hole in the same single-j shell. The paper enables future work to use B(M1) staggering as a sensitive measure of triaxiality in two-quasiparticle single j-shell configurations, and explains observations in 102,103,104,105,106Rh (Timar 2006, Vaman 2004, Joshi) and 135Nd (Mukhopadhyay 2007) where partner bands exist without strong staggering.
