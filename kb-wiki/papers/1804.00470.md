# Medium-induced gluon emission via transverse and longitudinal scattering in dense nuclear matter

> We study the medium-induced gluon emission from a hard quark jet traversing the dense nuclear matter within the framework of deep inelastic scattering off a large nucleus. We extend the previous work and compute the single gluon emission spectrum including both transverse and longitudinal momentum exchanges between the hard jet parton and the medium constituents. On the other hand, with only transverse scattering and using static scattering centers for the traversed medium, our induced gluon emission spectrum in the soft gluon limit reduces to the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev one-rescattering-one-emission formula.

## Key claim

The paper extends the higher-twist (HT) formalism for medium-induced gluon emission to include both transverse and longitudinal momentum exchanges between a hard quark jet and dense nuclear matter, showing that when longitudinal momentum transfer is neglected and static scattering centers are assumed, the spectrum reduces to the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) one-rescattering-one-emission formula in the soft gluon limit.

## Method

The authors compute the single gluon emission spectrum from a hard quark jet in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus at twist-four level, using the light-cone gauge and the collinear emission limit. They evaluate 21 central-cut diagrams contributing to the hadronic tensor, including both transverse and longitudinal momentum transfers via Glauber and longitudinal-Glauber gluon exchanges, and model the medium via a static Yukawa potential for the gluon field correlator. The calculation integrates over internal momentum fractions and coordinates, simplifying the phase factors using the separation of nucleon and nuclear length scales.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order vacuum gluon emission spectrum is given by \( dN_g^{\rm vac}/(dy\, dl_\perp^2) = (\alpha_s/(2\pi)) C_F (1+(1-y)^2)/y \cdot 1/l_\perp^2 \), where \( y = l^-/q^- \) is the gluon forward momentum fraction and \( l_\perp \) its transverse momentum.
- The formation time of the radiated gluon is \( \tau_{\rm form}^- = 2q^- y(1-y)/l_\perp^2 = 2l^-(1-y)/l_\perp^2 \).
- The medium-induced gluon emission spectrum involves an integral over the exchanged momentum \( k_1^- \) and \( \mathbf{k}_{1\perp} \) with a Yukawa potential factor \( 1/[(\sqrt{2}k_1^-)^2 + \mathbf{k}_{1\perp}^2 + \mu^2]^2 \), where \( \mu \) is the exchanged gluon mass.
- The color factor for the hadronic tensor contribution from the diagram with one rescattering on the radiated gluon evaluates to \( C_F C_A \).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist (HT) radiative energy loss approach of Guo (2000) and Wang (2001) by including longitudinal momentum exchange at the emission vertex, building on prior studies of medium-induced photon emission by Qin (2014) and Zhang (2016). It also generalizes the GLV formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 1999-2000) by incorporating longitudinal scattering, and enables more complete calculations of parton energy loss and jet modification in quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC and the LHC.
