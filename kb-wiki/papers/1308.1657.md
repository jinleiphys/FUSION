# Quark deconfinement in high-mass neutron stars

> In this paper, we explore whether or not quark deconfinement may occur in high-mass neutron stars such as J1614-2230 (1.97 \pm 0.04 M_Sun) and J0348+0432 (2.01 \pm 0.04 M_Sun). Our study is based on a non-local extension of the SU(3) Nambu Jona-Lasinio (n3NJL) model with repulsive vector interactions among the quarks. This model goes beyond the frequently used local version of the Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by accounting for several key features of QCD which are not part of the local model. Confined hadronic matter is treated in the framework of non-linear relativistic mean field theory. We find that both the local as well as the non-local NJL model predict the existence of extended regions of mixed quark-hadron (quark-hybrid) matter in high-mass neutron stars with masses of 2.1 to 2.4 M_Sun. Pure quark matter in the cores of neutron stars is obtained for certain parametrizations of the hadronic lagrangian and choices of the vector repulsion among quarks. The radii of high-mass neutron stars with quark-hybrid matter and/or pure quark matter cores in their centers are found to lie in the canonical range of 12 to 13 km.

## Key claim

Both the local and non-local NJL models predict extended regions of mixed quark-hadron (quark-hybrid) matter in high-mass neutron stars with masses of 2.1 to 2.4 solar masses, but pure quark matter in the cores is obtained only for certain parametrizations of the hadronic lagrangian and choices of the vector repulsion among quarks.

## Method

The authors modeled the quark phase using a non-local extension of the SU(3) Nambu Jona-Lasinio (n3NJL) model with repulsive vector interactions, comparing it to the local SU(3) NJL (l3NJL) model. Confined hadronic matter was treated within non-linear relativistic mean field theory using the GM1 and NL3 parametrizations. The mixed quark-hadron phase was constructed under the Gibbs condition for pressure equilibrium with global electric charge neutrality imposed on the constituents.

## Key numbers

- The masses of the two high-mass neutron stars considered are PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses.
- Both local and non-local NJL models predict extended regions of mixed quark-hadron matter in neutron stars with masses of 2.1 to 2.4 solar masses.
- The radii of high-mass neutron stars with quark-hybrid matter and/or pure quark matter cores lie in the range of 12 to 13 km.
- For the local NJL model with GM1 parametrization, deleptonization densities are 6.65 rho_0 (G_V/G_S = 0) and 7.45 rho_0 (G_V/G_S = 0.30), where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- For the non-local NJL model with GM1 parametrization, deleptonization densities are 5.85 rho_0 (G_V/G_S = 0) and 7.03 rho_0 (G_V/G_S = 0.09).
- Maximum neutron star masses for pure hadronic matter are 2.23 solar masses for GM1 and 2.77 solar masses for NL3.
- The non-local NJL model parameters include: current quark mass m_bar = 6.2 MeV, strange quark mass m_s = 140.7 MeV, Lambda = 706.0 MeV, G_S Lambda^2 = 15.04, and H Lambda^5 = -337.71.

## Context

This work extends the investigations of Orsaria (2013) for neutron stars containing deconfined quark matter, building on the non-local NJL model developed by Contrera (2008, 2010). It contrasts with studies using the MIT bag model by Glendenning (1992, 2001) which do not predict the occurrence of the Delta- state, and with local NJL studies by Lenzi (2012) and Bonanno that found masses above 2 solar masses only with very stiff hadronic models like NL3. The results enable further exploration of the QCD phase diagram and the role of vector interactions in determining the equation of state for neutron star matter.
