# Useful relations and sum rules for PDFs and multiparton distribution functions of spin-1 hadrons

> There are two types of polarizations in spin-1 hadrons, and they are vector and tensor polarizations. The latter is a unique one since it does not exist in the spin-1/2 proton. The vector-polarized PDFs are the same for both the proton and spin-1 hadrons; therefore, we mainly investigate the unique PDFs in tensor-polarized hadrons. By using the operator product expansion, the twist-3 PDF $f_{LT}$ can be expressed by two terms in the same way with $g_T$ of the proton. The first term is determined by the twist-2 PDF $f_{1LL}$ (or $b_1$) which was measured by an experiment, and the second term is expressed by twist-3 quark-gluon distributions. If we neglect the higher-twist effects, $f_{LT}$ is simply given by $f_{1LL}$, and this relation is similar to the Wandzura-Wilczek relation of $g_T$. Furthermore, a new sum rule is also obtained for $f_{2LT}=2/3 f_{LT}- f_{1LL}$, which is analogous to the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule, in the tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons. In future, these interesting relations could be studied at accelerator facilities, such as the Jefferson Laboratory, the Fermilab, the nuclotron-based ion collider facility in Russia, the proposed electron-ion colliders in US and China.

# Useful relations and sum rules for PDFs and multiparton distribution functions of spin-1 hadrons

## Key claim

The twist-3 parton distribution function $f_{LT}$ in tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons can be decomposed into a twist-2 contribution from $f_{1LL}$ (or $b_1$) and a twist-3 quark-gluon contribution, yielding a Wandzura-Wilczek-type relation and a Burkhardt-Cottingham-type sum rule, provided higher-twist effects are neglected.

## Method

The authors used the operator product expansion to relate the antisymmetric quark-antiquark operator $\bar{\psi}(0) \, ( \vec\partial^{\,\mu} \gamma^{\,\alpha} - \vec\partial^{\,\alpha} \gamma^{\,\mu} ) \, \psi (\xi)$ to quark-gluon-antiquark operators in the Fock-Schwinger gauge. They parameterized the quark-gluon-quark correlator for tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons at twist-3 level, then derived a differential equation linking $f_{LT}$ and $f_{1LL}$ by equating matrix elements of both sides of the operator relation. Integration of this equation produced the decomposition and the sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The Wandzura-Wilczek relation for the proton contributes 60-85% of $g_T$, as cited from Accardi (2009).
- The WW-type relation for spin-1 hadrons gives $f_{2LT}^+ (x) = -f_{1LL}^+ (x) + \int^1_x \frac{dy}{y} \, f_{1LL}^+(y)$, where $f_{2LT} \equiv \frac{2}{3} f_{LT} - f_{1LL}$.
- The BC-type sum rule is $\int_0^1 dx \, f_{2LT}^+ (x) = 0$.
- If the parton-model sum rule $\int dx f_{1LL}^+ (x) = 0$ (i.e., $\int dx b_1 (x) = 0$) holds with vanishing tensor-polarized antiquark distributions, then $\int_0^1 dx \, f_{LT}^+ (x) = 0$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons, building on earlier studies of the leading-twist PDF $f_{1LL}$ (or $b_1$) by Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1988) and by Kumano (2021), as well as the complete decomposition of the quark-antiquark correlation function by Kumano (2021). The derived relations are analogous to the Wandzura-Wilczek relation (1977) and the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule (1970) for the proton's $g_T$ and $g_2$ distributions. These results enable future experimental tests at Jefferson Laboratory, Fermilab, the nuclotron-based ion collider facility in Russia, and the proposed electron-ion colliders in the US and China, where tensor-polarized deuteron targets are under development.
