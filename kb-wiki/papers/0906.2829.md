# Effective operators from exact many-body renormalization

> We construct effective two-body Hamiltonians and E2 operators for the p-shell by performing $16\hbarΩ$ ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for A=5 and A=6 nuclei and explicitly projecting the many-body Hamiltonians and E2 operator onto the $0\hbarΩ$ space. We then separate the effective E2 operator into one-body and two-body contributions employing the two-body valence cluster approximation. We analyze the convergence of proton and neutron valence one-body contributions with increasing model space size and explore the role of valence two-body contributions. We show that the constructed effective E2 operator can be parametrized in terms of one-body effective charges giving a good estimate of the NCSM result for heavier p-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The effective E2 operator constructed from exact many-body renormalization in the p-shell can be parametrized in terms of j-dependent one-body effective charges that give a good estimate of the full no-core shell model (NCSM) result for heavier p-shell nuclei, but the neutron effective charges are somewhat smaller than standard phenomenological values, and the two-body contributions are significant for neutrons.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio NCSM calculations for A=5 (^5Li and ^5He) and A=6 (^6Li) nuclei in model spaces up to 16ℏΩ using the CD-Bonn potential with ℏΩ=20 MeV. They explicitly projected the many-body Hamiltonians and E2 operator onto the 0ℏΩ space, then separated the effective E2 operator into one-body and two-body contributions using the two-body valence cluster approximation. The one-body part was determined from the A=5 calculations, and the two-body part was extracted by subtracting the one-body contribution from the A=6 results.

## Key numbers

- One-body effective proton charge for the p₃/₂→p₃/₂ transition at N_max=16: e₁^π(3,3)=1.422 (bare value 1.000)
- One-body effective neutron charge for the p₃/₂→p₃/₂ transition at N_max=16: e₁^ν(3,3)=0.183 (bare value 0.000)
- One-body effective proton charge for the p₁/₂→p₃/₂ transition at N_max=16: e₁^π(1,3)=1.693 (bare value 1.000)
- One-body effective neutron charge for the p₁/₂→p₃/₂ transition at N_max=16: e₁^ν(1,3)=0.222 (bare value 0.000)
- Total effective proton charge (one-body plus two-body) for p₃/₂→p₃/₂ at N_max=16: e_t^π(3,3)=1.755
- Total effective neutron charge for p₃/₂→p₃/₂ at N_max=16: e_t^ν(3,3)=0.359
- Extrapolated converged total effective proton charge for p₃/₂→p₃/₂: e_t^π(3,3)=2.179
- Extrapolated converged total effective neutron charge for p₃/₂→p₃/₂: e_t^ν(3,3)=0.400
- Oscillator length parameter: b=1.44 fm for ℏΩ=20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the valence cluster expansion (VCE) approach previously developed by Lisetskiy (2008) for effective Hamiltonians to the construction of effective electromagnetic operators. It contrasts with earlier two-body cluster approximation studies by Stetcu (2005), which showed that the long-range quadrupole operator undergoes insufficiently weak renormalization in the two-body cluster approximation, unlike short-range operators. The results enable systematic construction of effective E2 operators for heavier p-shell nuclei using parametrized effective charges, and the methodology can be generalized to other multipole operators and larger model spaces.
