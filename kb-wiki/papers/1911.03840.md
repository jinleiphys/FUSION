# Transverse-Momentum-Dependent Parton Distribution Functions from Large-Momentum Effective Theory

> We show that transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs), important non-perturbative quantities for describing the properties of hadrons in high-energy scattering processes such as Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering with observed small transverse momentum, can be obtained from Euclidean QCD calculations in the framework of large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). We present a LaMET factorization of the Euclidean quasi-TMDPDFs in terms of the physical TMDPDFs and off-light-cone soft function at leading order in $1/P^z$ expansion, with the perturbative matching coefficient satisfying a renormalization group equation. We also discuss the implementation in lattice QCD with finite-length gauge links as well as the rapidity-regularization-independent factorization for Drell-Yan cross section.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) can be obtained from Euclidean QCD calculations in large-momentum effective theory (LaMET), provided that the off-light-cone soft function is computed as the form factor of a heavy quark-antiquark system, removing the final obstacle to a lattice formulation.

## Method

The authors define a Euclidean quasi-TMDPDF using an equal-time correlator with staple-shaped gauge links at large hadron momentum \(P^z\), and subtract the gauge-link self-energy using the square root of a flat rectangular Euclidean Wilson loop \(Z_E(2L,b_\perp,\mu)\). They then present a LaMET factorization that relates the quasi-TMDPDF to the physical TMDPDF and an off-light-cone soft function at leading order in \(1/P^z\) expansion, with the perturbative matching coefficient \(H(\zeta_z/\mu^2)\) satisfying a renormalization group equation. The off-light-cone soft function is obtained as the large-velocity-transfer form factor of a fixed-separation color-anticolor source pair.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop matching coefficient is \(\ln H^{(1)} = \alpha_s \left[ c_1 + \frac{C_F}{2\pi}\left(\ln\frac{\zeta_z}{\mu^2} - \frac{1}{2}\ln^2\frac{\zeta_z}{\mu^2}\right)\right]\) with \(c_1 = \frac{C_F}{2\pi}\left(-2 + \frac{\pi^2}{12}\right)\).
* The one-loop Collins-Soper kernel is \(K^{(1)}(b_\perp,\mu) = -\frac{\alpha_s C_F}{\pi}\ln \frac{\mu^2 b_\perp^2}{4e^{-2\gamma_E}}\).
* The one-loop off-light-cone soft function is \(S^{(1)}(b_\perp,\mu,Y,Y') = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi}\big[2-2(Y+Y')\big]\ln\frac{\mu^2 b_\perp^2}{4e^{-2\gamma_E}}\).
* The two-loop cusp anomalous dimension is \(\Gamma^{(2)}_{\rm cusp} = C_F C_A\left(-\frac{1}{12} + \frac{67}{36\pi^2}\right) - \frac{5 C_F N_f}{18\pi^2}\).
* The power corrections to the factorization are of order \(\mathcal{O}\left(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2/\zeta_z, M^2/\zeta_z, 1/(b_\perp^2 \zeta_z)\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework (Ji 2013, Ji 2014) to TMDPDFs, which had previously been limited to collinear PDFs. It resolves the difficulty of computing the TMD soft function on the lattice by identifying it with a heavy-quark form factor (Ji 2019), building on earlier exploratory lattice studies (Hagler 2009, Musch 2010, Engelhardt 2015, Yoon 2017) that did not address longitudinal momentum dependence or matching. The formalism enables first-principle lattice QCD calculations of Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering cross sections at small transverse momentum, with the Collins-Soper kernel extractable from the \(P^z\) dependence of quasi-TMDPDFs.
