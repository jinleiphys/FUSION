# Collective effects in neutrino scattering on solid and liquid targets

> Neutrino scattering on liquid and solid targets at low energy transfer can serve both as a tool for searching the physics beyond the Standard Model, for example, such as neutrino electromagnetic interactions, and as a test of the Standard Model at low-energy scale. At the same time, the theoretical apparatus for low-energy elastic neutrino scattering on electrons and nuclei bound in liquids and solids must take into account collective effects in the electron and nuclear subsystems of the target. We develop such an apparatus based on the formalism of the dynamic structure factor. A numerical illustration of the role of the collective effects is provided for the case of a superfluid He-4 target.

# Collective effects in neutrino scattering on solid and liquid targets

## Key claim
The authors develop a theoretical formalism based on the dynamic structure factor to account for collective effects in low-energy elastic neutrino scattering on electrons and nuclei bound in liquids and solids. They show that for superfluid He-4 targets, the differential cross section with collective effects is strongly suppressed (by a factor of 10^6) relative to the free-atom case, qualitatively changing the energy-transfer dependence.

## Method
The authors generalize the single-atom elastic neutrino scattering cross section to a system of N interacting atoms by substituting the vector and axial-vector coupling constants with matrix elements involving sums over atomic positions, then summing over final states and averaging over initial states using the dynamic structure factor S(T,q). For the superfluid He-4 target, they approximate the dynamic structure factor in the energy-transfer range below 1 meV as S(T,q) = <ρ_q ρ_q^+> δ(T - u q), where u is the sound velocity in superfluid He-4, and derive the resulting differential cross section.

## Key numbers
- The differential cross section for tritium antineutrino scattering on superfluid He-4 with collective effects is suppressed by a factor of 10^6 relative to the free-atom case at E_ν = 10 keV.
- The energy-transfer range where collective effects are relevant is ≤ 1 meV for superfluid He-4.
- The sound velocity u in superfluid He-4 appears in the dynamic structure factor approximation S(T,q) = <ρ_q ρ_q^+> δ(T - u q).
- The differential cross section for the superfluid He-4 target is given by dσ_0/dT = N C_V^2 (G_F^2 T)/(2π m u^2) (1 - T^2/(4u^2 E_ν^2)), where N is the number of atoms, m is the atomic mass, and E_ν is the neutrino energy.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of elastic neutrino-atom scattering (Gaponov) to condensed-matter targets by incorporating the dynamic structure factor approach used in solid-state physics (Kittel, Kvasnikov). The results are directly relevant to proposed detectors for low-energy dark matter and neutrino signals using condensed matter targets (Maris 2017, Cadeddu), and can be applied to search for neutrino electromagnetic properties (RMP 2015). The formalism enables future experiments with detectors based on liquid He-4 and other materials such as graphene (Hochberg 2017).
