# Quantum fluctuations of energy in subsystems of a hot relativistic gas

> We derive a formula that defines quantum fluctuations of energy in subsystems of a hot relativistic gas. For small subsystem sizes we find substantial increase of fluctuations compared to those known from standard thermodynamic considerations. However, if the size of the subsystem is sufficiently large, we reproduce the result for energy fluctuations in the canonical ensemble. Our results are subsequently used in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collisions to introduce limitations of the concepts such as classical energy density or fluid element. In the straightforward way, our formula can be applied in other fields of physics, wherever one deals with hot and relativistic matter.

## Key claim
Quantum fluctuations of energy in a subsystem of a hot relativistic gas substantially exceed standard thermodynamic fluctuations for small subsystem sizes, but reproduce canonical-ensemble results when the subsystem is sufficiently large. The authors derive a formula that introduces limitations on the classical concepts of energy density and fluid element in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors model a hot relativistic gas as a free real scalar field in thermal equilibrium, using a Gaussian profile of length scale \(a\) to define a finite subsystem \(S_a\) and avoid boundary effects. They compute the thermal expectation value of the Hamiltonian density operator \(\mathcal{H}_a\) and its variance \(\sigma^2(a,m,T)\) using thermal expectation values of products of creation and annihilation operators with Bose-Einstein statistics. Numerical integration of the resulting expression is performed for various temperatures, particle masses, and subsystem sizes, and the large-\(a\) limit is analytically connected to the thermodynamic specific heat.

## Key numbers
- For massless particles at temperature \(T = 150\) MeV with degeneracy \(g = 40\), the normalized fluctuations become larger than unity for subsystem sizes \(a < 0.4\) fm.
- In the large-\(a\) limit for massless particles, the volume-scaled normalized fluctuation satisfies \(V_a \sigma_n^2 \sim T^5 / a^3\).
- For the massless Boltzmann case, the analytic result for the thermodynamic limit gives \(\lim_{a \to \infty} V_a \sigma_n^2 = \frac{11 \pi^2}{8g} \frac{1}{T^3}\).
- Numerical results show that quantum fluctuations agree with thermodynamic fluctuations for subsystem sizes \(a > 1\) fm.

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum fluctuations in finite subsystems of a thermal field, building on the smeared-operator technique of Chen (2018) and the vacuum fluctuation analysis of Phillips (2000). It directly connects to the use of relativistic hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, where the concepts of energy density and fluid elements are applied to very small volumes; the results suggest that classical hydrodynamic descriptions require coarse-graining over scales larger than about 0.4 fm. The formula can be applied to any hot relativistic system, including quark-gluon plasma and hadron resonance gas.
