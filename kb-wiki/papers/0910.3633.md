# Anisotropic Hydrodynamics, Bulk Viscosities and R-Modes of Strange Quark Stars with Strong Magnetic Fields

> In strong magnetic fields the transport coefficients of strange quark matter become anisotropic. We determine the general form of the complete set of transport coefficients in the presence of a strong magnetic field. By using a local linear response method, we calculate explicitly the bulk viscosities $\zperp$ and $\zpara$ transverse and parallel to the $B$-field respectively, which arise due to the non-leptonic weak processes $u+s\leftrightarrow u+d$. We find that for magnetic fields $B<10^{17}$ G, the dependence of $\zperp$ and $\zpara$ on the field is weak, and they can be approximated by the bulk viscosity for zero magnetic field. For fields $B>10^{18}$ G, the dependence of both $\zperp$ and $\zpara$ on the field is strong, and they exhibit de Haas-van Alphen-type oscillations. With increasing magnetic field, the amplitude of these oscillations increases, which eventually leads to negative $\zperp$ in some regions of parameter space. We show that the change of sign of $\zperp$ signals a hydrodynamic instability. As an application, we discuss the effects of the new bulk viscosities on the r-mode instability in rotating strange quark stars. We find that the instability region in strange quark stars is affected when the magnetic fields exceeds the value $B= 10^{17}$ G. For fields which are larger by an order of magnitude, the instability region is significantly enlarged, making magnetized strange stars more susceptible to $r$-mode instability than their unmagnetized counterparts.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for magnetic fields below \(10^{17}\) G, the bulk viscosities of strange quark matter are essentially isotropic and field-independent, but for fields above \(10^{18}\) G the transverse bulk viscosity \(\zeta_\perp\) can become negative in some regions of parameter space, signaling a hydrodynamic instability that significantly enlarges the r-mode instability region in rotating strange quark stars.

## Method

The authors develop a fully relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamic formalism for magnetized strange quark matter, identifying eight independent viscosity coefficients (six shear and two bulk viscosities) in the presence of a strong magnetic field. They then apply a local linear response method to compute the two bulk viscosities \(\zeta_\perp\) and \(\zeta_\parallel\) arising from the non-leptonic weak processes \(u+s \leftrightarrow u+d\), using the zero-temperature limit for thermodynamic quantities and evaluating the weak interaction rate \(\lambda\) as a function of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* For magnetic fields \(B < 10^{17}\) G, the bulk viscosities \(\zeta_\perp\) and \(\zeta_\parallel\) show weak field dependence and can be approximated by the zero-field bulk viscosity.
* For magnetic fields \(B > 10^{18}\) G, both \(\zeta_\perp\) and \(\zeta_\parallel\) exhibit strong field dependence with de Haas-van Alphen-type oscillations.
* The r-mode instability region in strange quark stars is affected when the magnetic field exceeds \(B = 10^{17}\) G.
* For magnetic fields larger by an order of magnitude (i.e., \(B \sim 10^{18}\) G), the instability region is significantly enlarged compared to unmagnetized strange stars.

## Context

This work extends the study of bulk viscosity in strange quark matter, previously calculated for isotropic systems by Wang (1985), Sawyer (1989), and Madsen (1992), to the anisotropic case with strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars. The results enable more realistic modeling of r-mode instabilities in rotating strange quark stars with magnetic fields up to \(10^{18}\)–\(10^{20}\) G, building on the r-mode instability framework reviewed by Andersson (2001) and Friedman (2001), and on earlier work by Alford (2007) and Manuel (2007) on bulk viscosity in quark matter.
