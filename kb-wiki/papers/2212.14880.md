# Pseudogap effects in the strongly correlated regime of the two-dimensional Fermi gas

> The two-species Fermi gas with attractive short-range interactions in two spatial dimensions provides a paradigmatic system for the understanding of strongly correlated Fermi superfluids in two dimensions. It is known to exhibit a BEC-BCS crossover as a function of $\ln(k_F a)$, where $a$ is the scattering length, and to undergo a Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless superfluid transition below a critical temperature $T_c$. However, the extent of a pseudogap regime in the strongly correlated regime of $\ln(k_F a)\sim 1$, in which pairing correlations persist above $T_c$, remains largely unexplored with controlled theoretical methods. Here we use finite-temperature auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) methods on discrete lattices in the canonical ensemble formalism to calculate thermodynamical observables in the strongly correlated regime. We extrapolate to continuous time and the continuum limit to eliminate systematic errors and present results for particle numbers ranging from $N=42$ to $N=162$. We estimate $T_c$ by a finite-size scaling analysis, and observe clear pseudogap signatures above $T_c$ and below a temperature $T^*$ in both the spin susceptibility and free-energy gap. We also present results for the contact, a fundamental thermodynamic property of quantum many-body systems with short-range interactions.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first controlled theoretical calculation of pseudogap signatures in the two-dimensional Fermi gas in the strongly correlated regime (ln(k_F a) ~ 1), observing a suppression of spin susceptibility and an increase in free-energy gap above the superfluid critical temperature T_c and below a temperature T*.

## Method

The authors used finite-temperature auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) methods on discrete lattices in the canonical ensemble formalism, simulating systems with particle numbers N=42 to N=162. They extrapolated results to continuous time and the continuum limit to eliminate systematic errors, and estimated the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) critical temperature T_c using finite-size scaling analysis of the largest eigenvalue of the two-body density matrix.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 0.11(2) T_F at coupling η = ln(k_F a) = 1.0
- Critical temperature T_c = 0.09(2) T_F at η = 1.3
- Critical temperature T_c = 0.06(2) T_F at η = 1.8
- Spin gap temperature T* = 0.31 T_F at η = 1.0
- Spin gap temperature T* = 0.22 T_F at η = 1.3
- Spin gap temperature T* = 0.12 T_F at η = 1.8
- Particle numbers studied: N = 42, 74, 90, 114, and 162 for each coupling

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the 2D BEC-BCS crossover, including zero-temperature diffusion Monte Carlo calculations by Bertaina (2011) and finite-temperature AFMC calculations by Anderson (2015) that lacked continuum extrapolation. The results provide controlled benchmarks for the pseudogap regime, complementing earlier Luttinger-Ward self-consistent T-matrix calculations by Bauer (2014) and recent AFMC calculations of the BKT critical temperature by He (2022). The findings enable future calculations of spectral functions as dynamical probes of the pseudogap regime.
