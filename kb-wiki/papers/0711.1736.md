# Stability of the Solutions of Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Equations with Confining Interactions

> For two bound-state equations derived as simplified forms of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with confining interaction, stability of all solutions is rigorously shown.

## Key claim
For two instantaneous reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with confining interactions, the reduced Salpeter equation and the exact-propagator bound-state equation, all solutions are rigorously shown to be stable, meaning their mass eigenvalues belong to a real discrete spectrum bounded from below. This stability holds for confining interactions of harmonic-oscillator type in configuration space, provided the propagator functions are positive and well-behaved.

## Method
The authors analyze three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for fermion-antifermion bound states with spin-parity-charge conjugation assignment \(J^{PC}=0^{-+}\). For interactions of harmonic-oscillator type in configuration space, the bound-state equations simplify to ordinary differential equations that can be cast as eigenvalue equations for Schrödinger operators. The stability proof relies on a theorem stating that the spectrum of a Schrödinger Hamiltonian with a locally bounded positive potential rising to infinity in all directions is purely discrete.

## Key numbers
- For the reduced Salpeter equation with free-particle propagators of constant constituent mass \(m\), all solutions are stable for a large class of kernels (no specific numerical mass or energy given).
- For the exact-propagator bound-state equation, stability holds for reasonably well-behaved mass function \(m(\boldsymbol{p}^2) > 0\) and wave-function renormalization \(Z(\boldsymbol{p}^2) > 0\) (no specific numerical values provided).
- For the full Salpeter equation, mass eigenvalues squared \(M^2\) are guaranteed to be real, but the spectrum of mass eigenvalues \(M\) may include imaginary points \(M = -M^\ast\) in addition to real opposite-sign pairs \((M, -M)\).

## Context
This work extends earlier rigorous analyses by Lucha and collaborators (Lucha 2000, Lucha 2001) on instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equations, addressing numerical instabilities observed in studies of the Salpeter equation with confining interactions (StabSal). The results provide a formal foundation for the stability of bound-state solutions in these simplified frameworks, enabling further investigation of the full Salpeter equation where the spectrum may include imaginary eigenvalues.
