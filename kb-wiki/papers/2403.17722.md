# Nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double-beta decay in covariant density functional theory with different mechanisms

> Nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay in candidate nuclei play a crucial role in interpreting results from current experiments and in designing future ones. Accurate NME values serve as important nuclear inputs for constraining parameters in new physics, such as neutrino mass and the Wilson coefficients of lepton-number-violating (LNV) operators. In this study, we present a comprehensive calculation of NMEs for $0νββ$ decay in $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe, using nuclear wave functions obtained from multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT). We employ three types of transition potentials at the leading order in chiral effective field theory. Our results, along with recent data, are utilized to constrain the coefficients of LNV operators. The results demonstrate that the combined NMEs based on the Feynman diagrams at the hadronic scale for the nonstandard mechanisms lead to uncertainty by different nuclear models comparable to that for the standard mechanism. The use of NMEs from various nuclear models does not dramatically change the parameter space intervals for the coefficients, although MR-CDFT yields the most stringent constraint. Furthermore, our NMEs can also be used to perform a more comprehensive analysis with multiple isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper presents comprehensive nuclear matrix element (NME) calculations for neutrinoless double-beta decay using multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) across five candidate nuclei, finding that the uncertainty in combined NMEs from different nuclear models for nonstandard mechanisms is comparable to that for the standard light-neutrino-exchange mechanism, and that using NMEs from various models does not dramatically change the parameter space intervals for lepton-number-violating operator coefficients.

## Method

The authors employed multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) based on the relativistic point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 to generate nuclear wave functions for the ground states of initial and final nuclei in five candidate isotopes. They computed NMEs using three types of leading-order transition potentials from chiral effective field theory: the standard light Majorana neutrino exchange mechanism (type I), the momentum-dependent long-range mechanism (type II), and the short-range contact mechanism (type III), each decomposed into NN, piN, and pipi contributions. The Dirac equation was solved in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis within 10 major shells, with pairing correlations treated by the BCS method using zero-range pairing forces.

## Key numbers

* For 136Xe, the combined NMEs from MR-CDFT show a variation of approximately a factor of 2-4 compared to results from interacting shell model (SM), IBM2, and spherical QRPA (sQRPA) across most combined NMEs
* The Fermi-type NME M_F^VV for 76Ge is -1.924, for 82Se is -1.742, for 100Mo is -1.814, for 130Te is -1.365, and for 136Xe is -1.184
* The Gamow-Teller NME M_GT^AA for 76Ge is 5.743, for 82Se is 5.021, for 100Mo is 6.341, for 130Te is 4.565, and for 136Xe is 4.003
* The short-range Fermi-type NME M_F,sd^VV for 76Ge is -2.226, for 82Se is -2.049, for 100Mo is -2.484, for 130Te is -1.843, and for 136Xe is -1.607
* The short-range Gamow-Teller NME M_GT,sd^AA for 76Ge is 7.141, for 82Se is 6.549, for 100Mo is 7.974, for 130Te is 6.160, and for 136Xe is 5.390
* The tensor-type NME M_T^AP for 76Ge is 0.020, for 82Se is -0.018, for 100Mo is 0.039, for 130Te is 0.062, and for 136Xe is 0.053
* The CKM matrix element V_ud used is 0.97373
* The axial-vector coupling constant g_A is approximately 1.27
* The pairing force strength parameters are -314.550 MeV fm^3 for neutrons and -346.500 MeV fm^3 for protons

## Context

This work extends previous MR-CDFT studies of NMEs for light and heavy Majorana neutrino exchange mechanisms (Song 2014, Yao 2015, Yao 2016, Song 2017, Ding 2023) to include nonstandard mechanisms within the chiral effective field theory framework developed by Cirigliano (2017, 2018). The results provide complementary NMEs for constraining lepton-number-violating operator coefficients, enabling multi-isotope analyses that can be compared with constraints from other nuclear models such as the interacting shell model (Menendez 2017), IBM2 (Deppisch 2020), and spherical QRPA (Hyvarinen 2015), while noting that ab initio calculations (Yao 2020, Belley 2021, Novario 2021) remain limited to the standard mechanism.
