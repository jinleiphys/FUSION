# Rapidity and centrality dependence of azimuthal correlations in Deuteron-Gold collisions at RHIC

> We calculate azimuthal correlations in dAu collisions at different rapidities and centralities and argue that experimentally observed depletion of the back-to-back pick can be quantitatively explained by gluon saturation in the Color Glass Condensate of the Gold nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed depletion of the back-to-back peak in azimuthal correlations in deuteron-gold collisions can be quantitatively explained by gluon saturation in the Color Glass Condensate of the Gold nucleus, but only after correcting the overall normalization of the double-inclusive cross section by a free parameter (the ratio K2/K1) fixed to reproduce the correlation function height in proton-proton collisions.

## Method

The author uses kT-factorization to compute single and double inclusive gluon production cross sections in dAu collisions, employing the quasi-multi-Regge-kinematics (QMRK) amplitude for 2→4 processes (gg→gggg and gg→ggq¯q) at finite rapidity separations. The unintegrated gluon distribution function is modeled after the KLN approach with a saturation scale Qs that depends on x and atomic number, and the correlation function is defined as the number of pairs per trigger particle after subtracting a constant pedestal. Numerical integrations over transverse momenta are approximated by evaluating integrands at the lower momentum cutoff, and the overall normalization is fixed to pp data.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at central rapidity at RHIC: Qs ≈ 1.5 GeV for a Gold nucleus
- Saturation scale parameters: x0 = 3.04 × 10⁻⁴, λ = 0.288 (fixed by fits to DIS data)
- Coupling constant fixed at αs = 0.3
- Kinematic regions studied: trigger pT > 4 GeV, associated pA between 1.5 and pT GeV (forward); trigger pT > 2 GeV, associated pA between 1 and pT GeV (forward-backward); trigger pT between 4 and 6 GeV, associated pA between 2 and pT GeV (central)
- Rapidity values: yT = 3.1, yA = 3 for small Δy; yT = 3, yA = 0 for large Δy (Δy = 3)

## Context

This work extends the qualitative analysis of Kharzeev (2004) on CGC effects in azimuthal correlations to a quantitative calculation using kT-factorization, building on the KLN model of Kharzeev (2000-2002) and earlier work by Kovchegov (2002) on correlations in heavy-ion collisions. The paper enables future studies of finite-rapidity-separation corrections to the multi-Regge kinematics approximation and highlights the need for including valence quark contributions at forward rapidities, which were not addressed here.
