# Single Freeze-Out, Statistics and Pion, Kaon and Proton Production in Central Pb-Pb Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV

> Many data in the high energy physics are, in fact, sample means. It is shown that when this exact meaning of the data is taken into account and the most weakly bound states are removed from the hadron resonance gas, the acceptable fit to the whole spectra of pions, kaons and protons measured at midrapidity in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV [Phys.Rev.Lett.109,252301(2012)] is obtained. The invariant distributions are predicted with the help of the single-freeze-out model in the chemical equilibrium framework. Low $p_{T}$ pions and protons are reproduced simultaneously as well as $p/π$ ratio. Additionally, correct predictions extend over lower parts of large $p_{T}$ data. Some more general, possible implications of this approach are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an acceptable fit to the full transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons in central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV can be obtained within the chemical equilibrium single-freeze-out model only when two modifications are applied simultaneously: the freeze-out temperature is treated as a random variable (averaged over the event sample) and the most weakly bound resonances (full width > 250 MeV, mass below 1600 MeV) are removed from the hadron resonance gas.

## Method

The author uses the single-freeze-out model (SFOM) with a freeze-out hypersurface defined by invariant time τ_f and transverse size ρ_max, and a thermal distribution with freeze-out temperature T_f. The model prediction is redefined as an average over a probability density function for β_f = 1/T_f, using either a log-normal or triangular distribution. The fit is performed simultaneously to the full p_T spectra of pions, kaons, and protons from the ALICE collaboration (Abelev et al., 2012), with resonances having full width > 250 MeV removed from the hadron gas.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature expectation value E[T_f] = 116.7 ± 3.0 MeV (log-normal p.d.f.) and 111.6 MeV (triangular p.d.f.)
- Fluctuation of freeze-out temperature sqrt(V[T_f]) = 20.7 ± 1.6 MeV (log-normal) and 22.6 MeV (triangular), corresponding to ~20% variation
- χ²/n_dof = 1.048 with p-value = 29% (log-normal p.d.f.) and χ²/n_dof = 1.026 with p-value = 38% (triangular p.d.f.) for n_dof = 234
- Invariant time τ_f = 13.80 ± 0.40 fm (log-normal) and 14.42 fm (triangular); transverse size ρ_max = 20.48 ± 0.60 fm (log-normal) and 21.45 fm (triangular)
- Predicted yields: π⁺ = 739.2 (log-normal) vs. data 733 ± 54.0; K⁺ = 107.5 vs. data 109 ± 9.0; p = 32.9 vs. data 34 ± 3.0
- p/π ratio = 0.045 (log-normal) vs. data 0.046 ± 0.003; K/π ratio = 0.145 vs. data 0.149 ± 0.010

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out model previously applied successfully at RHIC energies (Broniowski et al., 2001; Prorok, 2007) to LHC energies, addressing the "proton puzzle" where the statistical model overpredicted the p/π ratio by a factor ~1.5 (Floris, 2014). The approach contrasts with alternative explanations such as incomplete resonance lists, non-equilibrium thermal models, hadronic inelastic interactions after hadronization, or flavor hierarchy at freeze-out. The removal of the f_0(500) resonance finds theoretical justification in Broniowski (2015). The work suggests that the 0-5% centrality class may be significantly inhomogeneous, with freeze-out temperature varying event-by-event while system size remains constant.
