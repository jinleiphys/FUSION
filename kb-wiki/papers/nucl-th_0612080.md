# Global Conservation Laws and Femtoscopy of Small Systems

> It is increasingly important to understand, in detail, two-pion correlations measured in p+p and d+A collisions. In particular, one wishes to understand the femtoscopic correlations, in order to compare to similar measurements in heavy ion collisions. However, in the low-multiplicity final states of these systems, global conservation laws generate significant N-body correlations which project onto the two-pion space in non-trivial ways and complicate the femtoscopic analysis. We discuss a model-independent formalism to calculate and account for these correlations in measurements.

## Key claim

Global conservation laws (energy and momentum) generate significant N-body correlations that project onto two-pion correlation functions in low-multiplicity systems like p+p and d+A collisions, and these correlations must be accounted for before femtoscopic information can be reliably extracted. The authors present a model-independent formalism to calculate these effects, but note that the analytic calculations never reproduce the simulations exactly, and the experimentalist must use educated guesses for unmeasured quantities like total multiplicity and total energy.

## Method

The authors used the GENBOD Monte Carlo event generator to simulate events containing only energy and momentum conservation, with no other physics, and calculated spherical harmonic decomposition (SHD) coefficients of the resulting two-pion correlation functions. They extended the analytic formalism of Danielewicz (1987) and Borghini, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000) from transverse momentum conservation to full four-momentum conservation, deriving a first-order expansion in 1/N that yields an "experimentalist's formula" where the correlation function is expressed as a weighted sum of four two-particle observables (p_T dot product, p_z product, energy product, and energy sum) with four global fit parameters M1 through M4.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Danielewicz (1987) and Borghini, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000), who considered only transverse momentum conservation for two-particle azimuthal correlations (elliptic flow v2), to include full four-momentum conservation applied to femtoscopic correlation functions. The paper addresses preliminary STAR measurements by Chajecki (2005) showing that HBT radii fall with transverse mass in both A+A and p+p collisions at the same sqrt(s_NN), which might signal unexpected universality. The formalism enables direct comparison of femtoscopic measurements between small and large systems at RHIC and LHC by providing a method to disentangle conservation-law correlations from the femtoscopic signal, though the authors note that the number of fit parameters must approximately double to account for both effects.
