# Upper Bound on the Speed of Sound in Nuclear Matter from Transport

> We point out that there is an upper bound on the speed of sound squared given by $c_s^2 \leq 0.781$ valid for all known systems described by relativistic transient hydrodynamics where calculations of certain ratios of hydrodynamic transport coefficients can be performed from first principles. Assuming this bound is valid for ultradense matter implies that the maximum mass of isolated (non-rotating) neutron stars cannot be larger than 2.7 solar masses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that relativistic causality and stability, combined with bounds on transport coefficients from first-principles calculations, impose an upper bound on the speed of sound squared of \(c_s^2 \leq 0.781\) for all systems describable by relativistic transient hydrodynamics, and that assuming this bound holds for ultradense matter limits the maximum mass of non-rotating neutron stars to below 2.7 solar masses.

## Method

The authors derive a thermodynamic inequality from Israel-Stewart transient fluid dynamics that relates the speed of sound squared to ratios of shear and bulk viscosity coefficients and their corresponding relaxation times. They combine this inequality with empirically observed bounds on the shear relaxation time coefficient \(C_\pi\) (ranging from \(2(2-\ln 2)\) to 6.1) from first-principles calculations across many theories, yielding the numerical bound \(c_s^2 \leq 0.781\). They then test this bound against first-principles lattice QCD, perturbative QCD, and two-color QCD results for the speed of sound, and apply it to neutron star equations of state by constructing piecewise EoSs that saturate the bound above a threshold density and solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

* The derived upper bound on the speed of sound squared is \(c_s^2 \leq 0.781\).
* The shear relaxation time coefficient \(C_\pi\) is bounded between \(2(2-\ln 2)\) and 6.1, leading to the inequality \(\frac{4\eta}{3\tau_\pi(\epsilon+P)} \geq \frac{4}{3 \times 6.1}\).
* Assuming the bound holds and chiral effective theory is valid up to two times saturation density, the maximum mass of an isolated non-rotating neutron star is bounded by \(M < 2.7\,M_\odot\).
* The transport bound decreases the maximum neutron star mass by approximately 8% compared to the causal bound \(c_s^2 \leq 1\), and decreases the radius of the maximally massive configuration by \(3.6 \pm 0.3\%\).
* The compact object of mass \(2.6\,M_\odot\) observed in the GW190814 event is compatible with the new bound.

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on the speed of sound in ultradense matter, which were limited to the causal bound \(c_s \leq 1\) from relativistic causality (Hartle 1978) and the conformal limit \(c_s \leq 1/\sqrt{3}\) from lattice QCD at high temperature (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2017). The new transport bound is derived from fundamental thermodynamic principles in relativistic transient hydrodynamics (Israel 1979) and is obeyed by all known first-principles calculations, including lattice QCD at finite isospin density (Detmold 2012, Abbott 2023, Brandt 2018, Brandt 2022), perturbative QCD at finite baryon density (Gorda 2021, Gorda 2023), and two-color QCD (Cotter 2012). The result enables future Bayesian analyses of neutron star observations to assess whether ultradense matter in neutron star cores exhibits exotic transport properties that would violate the bound.
