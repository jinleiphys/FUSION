# Theoretical Review of Charmonium Production with Different $p_T$ in the Hot Medium

> Charmonia with different transverse momentum $p_T$ usually comes from different mechanisms in the relativistic heavy ion collisions. This work tries to review the theoretical studies on quarkonium evolutions in the deconfined medium produced in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions. The charmonia with high $p_T$ are mainly from the initial hadronic collisions, and therefore sensitive to the initial energy density of the bulk medium. For those charmonia within $0.1<p_T<5$ GeV/c at the energies of Large Hadron Collisions (LHC), They are mainly produced by the recombination of charm and anti-charm quarks in the medium. In the extremely low $p_T\sim 1/R_A$ ($R_A$ is the nuclear radius), additional contribution from the coherent interactions between electromagnetic fields generated by one nucleus and the target nucleus plays a non-negligible role in the $J/ψ$ production even in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key claim

This review argues that charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions is dominated by different mechanisms depending on the transverse momentum \(p_T\): high-\(p_T\) charmonia come from initial hadronic collisions, intermediate-\(p_T\) charmonia (0.1–5 GeV/c) from recombination of charm and anti-charm quarks in the QGP, and extremely low-\(p_T\) charmonia (\(p_T \sim 1/R_A\)) from coherent photoproduction, with the latter playing a non-negligible role even in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical transport models that simulate charmonium evolution in the deconfined medium produced in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions. For primordial production, a transport equation with a decay rate \(C_\Psi\) (including parton inelastic scatterings and color screening) is used, while regeneration is modeled via the coalescence of charm and anti-charm quarks with densities \(\rho_{c(\bar c)}\) and cross section \(c+\bar c\rightarrow J/\psi +g\). Photoproduction is treated by approximating the Lorentz-contracted electromagnetic fields as quasi-real photons that scatter coherently with the target nucleus, with cross section proportional to \(Z_e^4\). The contribution from B-hadron decay is included via a non-prompt fraction \(f_B(p_T)\) and a quench factor \(Q(p_T)\).

## Key numbers

*   The mean transverse momentum of regenerated charmonia is estimated as \(\langle p_T^\Psi\rangle \sim 2p_c \sim 4T_{QGP}\), which at QGP temperatures \(T_{QGP} \sim 200-300\) MeV gives \(\langle p_T^\Psi\rangle \sim 1\) GeV/c, below the primordial mean \(2-3\) GeV/c.
*   In p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, the experimental \(R_{AA}^{\psi(2S)}\) in the backward rapidity for central collisions (\(N_{coll}\sim 11\)) is 0.3, while the transport model with hot medium suppression brings it close to data but suppresses \(R_{AA}^{J/\psi}\) to 0.8.
*   The photoproduction contribution becomes visible only for \(p_T < 0.1\) GeV/c, where it can exceed hadroproduction; in ultra-peripheral collisions \(R_{AA} \rightarrow +\infty\) as \(N_p\rightarrow 0\).
*   In pp collisions, the non-prompt fraction is fitted as \(f_B(p_T)=0.04+0.023p_T/(\text{GeV}/c)\) for \(J/\psi\) and \(f_B(p_T)=0.11+0.022p_T/(\text{GeV}/c)\) for \(\psi(2S)\).
*   For prompt charmonia at \(p_T>6.5\) GeV/c in Pb-Pb collisions, \(R_{AA}^{\psi(2S)}/R_{AA}^{J/\psi}\) decreases with \(N_p\); for inclusive yields, this ratio first decreases then increases for \(100<N_p<400\) due to B-hadron decay dominance.

## Context

This review extends prior work on charmonium suppression as a QGP signal (Matsui 1986) and regeneration (Thews 2000) by synthesizing results from transport models (e.g., TAMU Group, Du 2018) and comover models (Ferreiro 2014). It connects to studies of charm quark diffusion via Langevin equations (Chen 2016, He 2011) and photoproduction in ultra-peripheral collisions (Klein 1999, Baur 2001). The work enables a unified picture of charmonium production across all \(p_T\) regions, linking hadroproduction, regeneration, and photoproduction, and highlights open questions about the quantum effects of quarkonium states in thermal medium and the role of electromagnetic fields in chiral/charged particle evolution.
