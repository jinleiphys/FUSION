# Boson Realization of the su(3)-Algebra. II

> On the basis of the Schwinger boson representation for the Lipkin model developed in (I), the Holstein-Primakoff representation for the su(3)-algebra is presented. Including the symmetric case, the representation obtained in this paper contains all the cases.

## Key claim

On the basis of the Schwinger boson representation for the su(3)-algebra developed in the companion paper (I), the authors present a Holstein-Primakoff representation that includes the symmetric case and, they claim, contains all cases. The hedge is that the representation is obtained by regarding the intrinsic state in the Schwinger representation as representing the equilibrium, with fluctuations described by boson operators, rather than by the usual MYT boson mapping method.

## Method

Starting from the Schwinger boson representation of the su(3)-algebra for the Lipkin model (developed in paper (I)), the authors identify the intrinsic state as the equilibrium and introduce a new boson space with operators (α̂+, α̂+*), (α̂−, α̂−*), (α̂, α̂*), and (β̂+, β̂+*). They then replace the original Schwinger boson operators (b̂, b̂*) and (b̂−, b̂−*) with square-root operators √(m₀ − α̂+*α̂+ − α̂−*α̂−) and √(m₁ − α̂*α̂ − β̂+*β̂+), respectively, where m₀ and m₁ are quantum numbers characterizing the intrinsic state. The resulting expressions for the su(3)-generators are given explicitly in terms of these new bosons and the square-root operators.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic state is characterized by two quantum numbers m₀ and m₁, related to the su(3) labels (T, I⁰) by m₀ = 2[(T − 3/2) − I⁰] and m₁ = 2I⁰.
- The physical space is spanned by states where the boson numbers satisfy α̂+*α̂+ + α̂−*α̂− = 0, 1, 2, …, m₀ and α̂*α̂ + β̂+*β̂+ = 0, 1, 2, …, m₁.
- In the symmetric case m₁ = 0, the representation reduces to a form with only two boson types (α̂+, α̂−) and the square root √(m₀ − α̂+*α̂+ − α̂−*α̂−).
- In the case m₀ = 0, the representation reduces to a form with bosons (α̂, β̂+) and the square root √(m₁ − α̂*α̂ − β̂+*β̂+).
- In the simplest (RPA-like) approximation, the unperturbed Hamiltonian K̄₀ contains terms with coefficients (ε₂ − ε₀), (ε₁ − ε₀), and (ε₂ − ε₁) for the different boson excitations.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger boson representation for the su(3)-algebra developed by the same authors in paper (I) (Providencia et al., 2005) to a Holstein-Primakoff form, following the approach used for su(2) where the intrinsic state is treated as the equilibrium. The symmetric case (m₁ = 0) reproduces the well-known Holstein-Primakoff representation for su(3) given by Marshalek (1974). The representation enables a transparent description of fluctuations around the equilibrium in the su(3)-Lipkin model, and the simplest approximation yields a form analogous to the RPA method in the original fermion space, with the number of boson types (two or three) depending on whether m₁ = 0, m₀ = 0, or both are nonzero.
