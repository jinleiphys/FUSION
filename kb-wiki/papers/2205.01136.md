# Role of slow, out-of-equilibrium modes on the dynamic structure factor near the QCD critical point

> The role of slow out of equilibrium modes (OEM), introduced to extend the validity of hydrodynamics near the QCD critical point on the power spectrum of dynamical density fluctuations has been studied. We have used the equation of motion of slow modes for the situation when the extensive nature of thermodynamics is not altered due to the introduction of OEM. We find that the extensivity condition puts an extra constraint on the coupling of OEM with the four divergence of velocity. The dynamic structure factor (Snn) in presence of the OEM shows four Lorentzian peaks asymmetrically positioned about $ω(\text{frequency})=0$, whereas the dynamic structure factor without the presence of any OEM shows three well-known Lorentzian peaks. The width of the peaks are reduced in presence of OEM. We find that the asymmetric peaks originate due to the coupling of the out-of-equilibrium modes with the hydrodynamic modes. It is also shown that the OEM has negligible effects on Snn if first order hydrodynamics (relativistic Navier-Stokes) is used. The introduction of OEM reduces the width of the Rayleigh peak indicating the reduction in the decay rate of the fluctuation which leads to slowing down, a well-known characteristics of the critical end point (CEP).

## Key claim

The paper shows that introducing a slow out-of-equilibrium mode (OEM) to extend hydrodynamics near the QCD critical point changes the dynamic structure factor from three Lorentzian peaks to four asymmetrically positioned peaks, but only when second-order (Israel-Stewart) hydrodynamics is used; the OEM has negligible effects if first-order (relativistic Navier-Stokes) hydrodynamics is employed, and the extensivity condition of thermodynamics imposes an extra constraint on the coupling of the OEM with the four-divergence of velocity.

## Method

The authors start from the Israel-Stewart (IS) second-order causal dissipative hydrodynamics in the Eckart frame and introduce an additional scalar field \(\phi\) representing the slow out-of-equilibrium mode, following the hydro+ formalism of Stephanov (2017). They linearize the coupled hydrodynamic and OEM equations around equilibrium, apply Fourier-Laplace transforms to obtain an \(11 \times 11\) matrix equation in \((\omega,k)\) space, and solve for the density fluctuation correlation function \(\mathcal{S}_{nn}(\vec{k},\omega)\). The equation of state is constructed using the universality hypothesis that the QCD critical point belongs to the same universality class as the 3D Ising model, and the scaling behavior of transport coefficients and thermodynamic response functions near the critical point is implemented using critical exponents (\(\alpha = 0.11\), \(\gamma' = 1.2\), \(a_\zeta = 1.78\), \(a_\kappa = 0.63\)).

## Key numbers

- The dynamic structure factor is evaluated at wave number \(k = 0.1 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\) for two reduced temperature values: \(r = 0.2\) (away from CEP) and \(r = 0.01\) (close to CEP).
- Transport coefficients are set to \(\eta/s = \zeta/s = \kappa T/s = 1/4\pi\) for the calculations.
- The critical exponents used are \(\alpha = 0.11\) and \(\gamma' = 1.2\); the exponent for bulk viscosity scaling is \(a_\zeta = \nu d - \alpha = 1.78\) (with \(d=3\), \(\nu=0.63\)), and for thermal conductivity scaling \(a_\kappa = 0.63\).
- Away from the CEP (\(r=0.2\)), the structure factor shows four Lorentzian peaks: two asymmetric Brillouin peaks and an additional peak from coupling of \(\phi\) with heat flux, with no elastic Rayleigh peak at \(\omega=0\).
- Close to the CEP (\(r=0.01\)), the structure factor shows only two peaks as the Brillouin peaks disappear due to sound absorption.
- The width of the Rayleigh peak is reduced in the presence of the OEM, indicating slower decay of fluctuations (critical slowing down).

## Context

This work extends the hydro+ formalism introduced by Stephanov (2017), which adds a scalar non-hydrodynamic field to extend hydrodynamics near the QCD critical point, by computing the dynamic structure factor \(\mathcal{S}_{nn}\) within relativistic causal hydrodynamics. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Hasanujjaman et al. (no year given in text) that found three symmetric peaks without the OEM, and shows that the OEM produces four asymmetric peaks when transverse modes are included in the second-order theory. The results enable experimental investigation of slow-mode effects in condensed matter systems belonging to the same \(\mathcal{O}(4)\) universality class as the QCD critical point, which can then guide hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA.
