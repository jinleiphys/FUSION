# Anomaly in the charge radii of Pb isotopes

> The anomalous behaviour of the charge radii of the isotopic chain of Pb nuclei has been studied in the relativistic mean field theory. It has been shown that the relativistic mean field provides an excellent description of the anomalous kink in the isotopic shifts about $^{208}$Pb. This contrasts strongly from the Skyrme mean field, where almost all the known and realistic forces fail to reproduce the observed trend in the empirical data on the charge radii. The results have been discussed in the perspective of differences in the ansätze of the relativistic and the Skyrme mean-field theories.

# Anomaly in the charge radii of Pb isotopes

## Key claim
The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, specifically with the NL-SH force, reproduces the anomalous kink in the charge radii isotope shifts about $^{208}$Pb, whereas all tested Skyrme mean-field forces (SkM*, Ska, SIII, SGII) fail to do so, despite being fitted to ground-state properties of spherical nuclei from $^{16}$O to $^{208}$Pb.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic Hartree calculations for even-mass Pb isotopes from A=190 to 214 using the NL-SH parameter set of the nonlinear $\sigma\omega\rho$ model, with nucleon and meson wavefunctions expanded in N=12 oscillator shells. Pairing was included via the BCS formalism with pairing gaps from neighboring nuclei, and charge radii were obtained by folding the proton form factor with the calculated proton density distribution.

## Key numbers
- Charge radius of $^{208}$Pb taken as 5.503 fm from empirical data
- Binding energies reproduced to within at most 0.1% deviation for all Pb isotopes with NL-SH
- Asymmetry energy of NL1 force is about 44 MeV, which causes systematic deviations in binding energies for lighter Pb isotopes
- Calculations performed for even-mass Pb isotopes from A=190 to 214

## Context
This work extends the long-standing problem of the Pb isotope shift kink, previously studied within the Skyrme mean-field framework by various authors (Ref. 7 in the paper), where forces such as SkM*, Ska, SIII, and SGII all failed to reproduce the empirical isotope shifts for nuclei heavier than $^{208}$Pb. The RMF theory with the NL-SH force, which was developed to correct the excessive neutron skin thickness and asymmetry energy of earlier forces NL1 and NL2, succeeds where the Skyrme approach fails. This difference points to fundamental distinctions in the density dependence, saturation mechanism, and spin-orbit interaction between the relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field approaches, with implications for the description of neutron-rich nuclei far from stability, the equation of state of neutron matter, and neutron-star structure.
