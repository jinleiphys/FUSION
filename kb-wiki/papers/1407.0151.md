# Parity mixing of pair at nuclear surface due to spin-orbit potential in $^{18}$F

> We investigate the structure of $^{18}$F with the microscopic wave function based on the three-body $^{16}$O+$p$+$n$ model. In the calculation of the generator coordinate method (GCM) of the three-body model, $T=0$ energy spectra of $J^π=1^+$, $3^+$, and $5^+$ states and $T=1$ spectra of $J^π=0^+$, $2^+$ states in $^{18}$F are described reasonably. Based on the dinucleon picture, the effect of the spin-orbit force on the $T=0$ and $T=1$ $pn$ pairs around the $^{16}$O core is discussed. The $T=1$ pair in the $J^π=0^+$ state gains the spin-orbit potential energy involving the odd-parity mixing in the pair. The spin-orbit potential energy gain with the parity mixing is not so efficient for the $T=0$ pair in the $J^π=1^+$ state. The parity mixing in the pair is regarded as the internal symmetry breaking of the pair in the spin-orbit potential at the nuclear surface.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit potential from the core induces odd-parity mixing in the \(T=1\) proton-neutron pair in the \(J^\pi=0^+\) state of \(^{18}\text{F}\), but this parity mixing is suppressed in the \(T=0\) pair in the \(J^\pi=1^+\) state because the internal energy loss outweighs the spin-orbit energy gain.

## Method

The authors performed a generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation using a microscopic three-body \(^{16}\text{O}+p+n\) model, where the two valence nucleons are described by Gaussian wave packets and the \(^{16}\text{O}\) core is treated as an inert harmonic-oscillator \(p\)-shell closed configuration. The wave functions were projected onto good parity and total angular momentum, and the Hill-Wheeler equation was solved to obtain \(T=0\) (\(J^\pi=1^+,3^+,5^+\)) and \(T=1\) (\(J^\pi=0^+,2^+\)) states using the Volkov No.2 central force plus a G3RS-type spin-orbit force.

## Key numbers

* The \(T=0\) \(J^\pi=5^+\) state gains about 3 MeV from the spin-orbit force relative to the case without spin-orbit, while the \(J^\pi=1^+\) state gains very little energy.
* The \(T=1\) \(J^\pi=0^+\) state gains about 1 MeV from the spin-orbit force, with a slightly smaller gain in the \(2^+\) state.
* The odd-parity component \(\mathcal{P}_{\text{odd}}\) in the \(T=1\) \(J^\pi=0^+\) state is 0.11 (with bh125 interaction), compared to 0.05 in the \(T=0\) \(J^\pi=1^+\) state.
* The spin-orbit force strength was set to \(u_{\text{ls}}=820\) MeV to reproduce the low-lying energy spectra of \(^{17}\text{O}\).

## Context

This work extends the three-body potential model study of Tanimura et al. (2013) by using a fully microscopic wave function with antisymmetrization and recoil effects, and it provides a dinucleon-picture interpretation of the spin-orbit effect on \(pn\) pairs. The results support earlier shell-model and mean-field studies by Poves (1998), Baroni (2009), Bertsch (2009), Gezerlis (2011), and Sagawa (2012) that the spin-orbit force suppresses \(T=0\) \(J=1\) pairing relative to \(T=1\) \(J=0\) pairing. The parity-mixing mechanism is analogous to that discussed for Cooper pairs in noncentrosymmetric superconductors by Fujimoto (2009).
