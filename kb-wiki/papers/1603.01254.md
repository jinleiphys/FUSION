# Thermodynamics, transport and relaxation in non-conformal theories

> We study the equilibrium and near-equilibrium properties of a holographic five-dimensional model consisting of Einstein gravity coupled to a scalar field with a non-trivial potential. The dual four-dimensional gauge theory is not conformal and, at zero temperature, exhibits a renormalisation group flow between two different fixed points. We quantify the deviations from conformality both in terms of thermodynamic observables and in terms of the bulk viscosity of the theory. The ratio of bulk over shear viscosity violates Buchel's bound. We study relaxation of small-amplitude, homogeneous perturbations by computing the quasi-normal modes of the system at zero spatial momentum. In this approximation we identify two different relaxation channels. At high temperatures, the different pressures first become approximately equal to one another, and subsequently this average pressure evolves towards the equilibrium value dictated by the equation of state. At low temperatures, the average pressure first evolves towards the equilibrium pressure, and only later the different pressures become approximately equal to one another.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a holographic non-conformal gauge theory, the dominant relaxation channel for small-amplitude homogeneous perturbations depends on the ratio \(T/\Lambda\): at high temperatures the system first isotropises (pressures become equal) and then "EoSizes" (average pressure approaches the equilibrium value), while at low temperatures the order is reversed, with the system first EoSizing and then isotropising.

## Method

The authors study a five-dimensional holographic model of Einstein gravity coupled to a scalar field with a potential derived from a superpotential, which is dual to a four-dimensional gauge theory exhibiting a renormalisation group flow between an ultraviolet and an infrared fixed point. They compute equilibrium thermodynamic quantities (entropy density, speed of sound, interaction measure) and transport coefficients (shear and bulk viscosity) from black brane solutions obtained via the master function method of Gubser (2008). They then determine the relaxation dynamics by computing the quasi-normal mode spectrum at zero spatial momentum for small-amplitude homogeneous perturbations across a range of temperatures.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of bulk viscosity to shear viscosity, \(\zeta/\eta\), violates Buchel's bound for all temperatures studied.
* At high temperatures, the scalar operator VEV scales as \(\langle \mathcal{O} \rangle_T \sim \Lambda T^2\).
* The entropy density ratio \(s_R = (\kappa_5^2 / 2\pi^4 L^3) (s/T^3)\) approaches 1 at high temperature and \((L_{\rm IR}/L)^3\) at low temperature, where \(L_{\rm IR} = L / (1 + \phi_M^2/6)\).
* The interaction measure \(I = (\epsilon - 3p)/(\epsilon + p)\) vanishes at both high and low temperatures, with maximum deviations at intermediate temperatures that grow with \(\phi_M\).
* The speed of sound squared \(c_s^2\) is less than \(1/3\) at intermediate temperatures, approaching the conformal value \(1/3\) at both high and low temperatures.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of near-equilibrium dynamics beyond conformal field theories such as \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills, where only isotropisation occurs, to non-conformal theories where a second relaxation channel (EoSization) appears. It builds on the master function method of Gubser (2008) and the quasi-normal mode analysis of earlier works including Heller (2013), Janik (2015), Buchel (2015), and Rougemont (2015). The results enable the study of hydrodynamisation in non-conformal plasmas relevant for heavy ion collisions, where bulk viscosity effects become significant at temperatures near the QCD transition, and provide a framework for understanding the ordering of relaxation processes in strongly coupled non-abelian matter.
