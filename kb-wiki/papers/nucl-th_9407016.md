# Can dileptons reveal the in-medium properties of vector mesons?

> Dilepton production from both pion-pion and kaon-antikaon annihilation in heavy-ion collisions is studied using the relativistic transport model. The formation of a rho meson from pion-pion annihilation and a phi meson from kaon-antikaon annihilation, their propagation in the medium, and their decay into dileptons are explicitly treated. Including the medium modifications of the masses and widths of vector mesons as predicted by the QCD sum-rule calculations, we study their effects on the dilepton invariant mass spectra from heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies.

## Key claim

Dilepton invariant mass spectra from heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies can reveal the in-medium modifications of rho and phi meson masses and widths as predicted by QCD sum-rule calculations, provided that the formation, propagation, and decay of the intermediate vector mesons are treated explicitly rather than through a form factor approach, especially for long-lived mesons like the phi.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on the Walecka model to simulate central Ni+Ni collisions at 2.0 GeV/nucleon. Dilepton production from pion-pion annihilation (via an intermediate rho meson) and kaon-antikaon annihilation (via an intermediate phi meson) was calculated by explicitly treating the formation, propagation, and decay of the vector mesons, with their in-medium masses and widths taken from QCD sum-rule parametrizations. The medium modifications of kaon and antikaon masses were included via a mean-field approximation to the chiral Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Rho meson mass in medium: \( m^*_\rho / m_\rho \approx 1 - 0.18(\rho/\rho_0) \); at twice normal nuclear matter density (\(2\rho_0\)), the mass decreases by about 25%.
- Phi meson mass in medium: \( m^*_\phi / m_\phi \approx 1 - 0.0255(\rho/\rho_0) \); at \(2\rho_0\), the mass decreases by about 5%.
- Free rho meson mass: 768.1 MeV; free rho width: 151.5 MeV; free phi mass: 1019.4 MeV; free phi width into \(K\bar{K}\): 3.7 MeV.
- With medium effects, the rho peak in the dilepton spectrum shifts from about 770 MeV to around 550 MeV, and its height increases by about a factor of four compared to the free case.
- With medium effects, the phi peak broadens by about a factor of five and a shoulder appears around 950 MeV, about a factor of two above the pion-pion background.
- Final pion multiplicity in the simulation: about 39, giving a pion-to-nucleon ratio of about 0.33; final kaon multiplicity: about 0.33; final antikaon multiplicity: about 0.009.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dilepton production from pion-pion annihilation using fire-ball models (Gale and Kapusta, 1987; Xia and others, 1988) and transport models (Xiong and others, 1990; Wolf and others, 1991, 1993) by including the explicit dynamical treatment of intermediate vector mesons and their in-medium modifications from QCD sum-rule calculations (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992; Asakawa and Ko, 1993). It contrasts with the form factor approach used in those earlier works, showing that for narrow resonances like the phi meson, the form factor approach overestimates medium effects. The results enable future experimental searches for shifted and broadened vector meson peaks in dilepton spectra at SIS/GSI and higher energies (AGS), and motivate extending the transport model to higher incident energies where the kaon-to-pion ratio is larger.
