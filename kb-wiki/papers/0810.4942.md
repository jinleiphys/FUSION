# Cronin effect for protons and pions in pA collisions

> Pions and protons production cross-sections are analyzed in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the RHIC energy at midrapidity. We employ the pQCD factorization scheme supplemented with the color-dipole formalism to investigate the Cronin effect. We calculate the broadening in the color-dipole approach for different centralities. Our main goal is to investigate, in a parameter-free manner within a unified framework, how much of the cronin effect for both pions and baryons comes from the transverse momentum broadening due to initial partons multi-scatterings. We conclude that final-state effects in pA collisions are important. Uncertainties in nuclear shadowing of various parton distributions and parton fragmentation functions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The observed Cronin effect for pions in d+Au collisions can be fairly described by transverse momentum broadening due to initial parton multi-scatterings, but the same mechanism underestimates the Cronin ratio for protons, indicating that final-state effects are important and that baryon and meson production mechanisms in cold nuclear matter may differ.

## Method

The authors employed pQCD factorization with kT-factorization, supplementing it with the color-dipole formalism to compute transverse momentum broadening of partons propagating through nuclear matter. They calculated inclusive hadron production cross sections for pions and protons in p+p and d+Au collisions at RHIC energy, using the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff dipole cross-section model and three different nuclear parton distribution functions (EKS, HKN, HIJING-new). The broadening was computed in a parameter-free manner, with all phenomenological parameters fixed from p+p collisions, and the Cronin ratio was evaluated for different centralities.

## Key numbers

- The parton primordial transverse momentum squared was set to ⟨k²_T⟩ = 2 GeV² for all calculations.
- The K-factor was taken as K=2.5 for Kretzer fragmentation functions and K=1.5 for AKK08 fragmentation functions.
- The dipole cross-section parameters were σ₀ = 23.03 mb, R₀ = 0.4 fm × (x/x₀)^0.144, with x₀ = 3.04×10⁻⁴.
- The discrepancy between theory and experimental data for baryons in d+Au collisions persists up to p_T = 6.5 GeV.
- The normalization uncertainty on the p+p reference is 9.7%, and the uncertainty in estimating the number of binary collisions in minimum bias is about 17%.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kopeliovich et al. (2008) by calculating the Cronin effect for charged pions and protons at different centralities, whereas the prior study focused only on π⁰. The main difference is that the present work includes nuclear shadowing via nuclear parton distributions in the conventional way, while Kopeliovich et al. used a linear interpolation between short- and long-coherence limits without shadowing. The results suggest that final-state effects, such as recombination of soft and shower partons as studied by other authors, are important for baryon production, and that precise experimental measurement of the Cronin ratio for baryons at p_T > 7 GeV is needed to reveal the underlying hadron production mechanism in cold nuclear matter.
