# Impact of Initial-State Nuclear and Sub-Nucleon Structures on Ultra-Central Puzzle in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Hydrodynamic models fail to describe the near-equal $v_2/v_3$ ratio observed in ultra-central heavy-ion collisions, despite their success in other centrality classes. This discrepancy stems from shear viscosity suppressing higher-order geometric eccentricities, resulting in underestimated $v_3$ when using the conventional QGP viscosity coefficient. We explore two initial-state modifications to resolve this puzzle: (1) enforcing a minimum nucleon separation distance to homogenize distributions, and (2) amplifying sub-nucleon structures to reduce initial eccentricity. Using TRENTo initial conditions and 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc, both approaches significantly lower geometric eccentricity, reduce required viscosity, and narrow the $v_2$-$v_3$ gap in ultra-central collisions. Our results implicate initial-state nuclear and sub-nucleon structures as critical factors in addressing this puzzle. Resolving it would advance nuclear structure studies and improve precision in extracting QGP transport coefficients (e.g., shear viscosity), bridging microscopic nuclear features to macroscopic quark-gluon plasma properties.

# Impact of Initial-State Nuclear and Sub-Nucleon Structures on Ultra-Central Puzzle in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic models fail to reproduce the near-equal $v_2/v_3$ ratio in ultra-central collisions because shear viscosity suppresses higher-order eccentricities; the authors show that modifying initial-state nuclear structure, by enforcing a minimum nucleon separation distance or amplifying sub-nucleon structures, reduces initial geometric eccentricity and narrows the $v_2$-$v_3$ gap, but only when combined with a lower shear viscosity coefficient than conventionally used.

## Method

The authors used the TRENTo initial condition model to generate Pb+Pb collision events at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2.76$ TeV, varying the minimum distance between nucleons ($d_{\mathrm{min}}$ = 0.0, 1.0, 1.4, 1.7 fm) and including or excluding sub-nucleon fluctuations (three constituent quarks per nucleon with Gaussian width 0.3 fm). These initial conditions were evolved with the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc on a $300 \times 300 \times 161$ lattice with thermalization time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm, freeze-out temperature $T = 0.137$ GeV, and shear viscosity to entropy density ratios $\eta/s = 0.16$ or 0.22. Flow harmonics $v_n\{2\}$ were computed from the final-state charged hadron distributions.

## Key numbers

- Minimum nucleon separation distances tested: $d_{\mathrm{min}} = 0.0$, 1.0, 1.4, and 1.7 fm for Pb nuclei (Woods-Saxon parameters $R = 6.62$ fm, $a = 0.546$ fm)
- Sub-nucleon constituent quark Gaussian width: $v = 0.3$ fm; nucleon Gaussian width: $w = 0.5$ fm; number of constituents $N_c = 3$
- Shear viscosity values used: $\eta/s = 0.16$ for semi-central calibrations and $\eta/s = 0.22$ for ultra-central (0-1%) collisions
- Lattice dimensions: $n_x \times n_y \times n_z = 300 \times 300 \times 161$; thermalization time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm; freeze-out temperature $T = 0.137$ GeV
- 1000 hydrodynamic events generated per parameter configuration
- Transverse momentum integration cut for flow harmonics: $p_T = 0.3$–$3$ GeV
- With $d_{\mathrm{min}} = 1.4$ fm and $\eta/s = 0.22$, the $v_2\{2\}$ and $v_3\{2\}$ results in 0-1% centrality show improved agreement with CMS data compared to the case without nucleon-nucleon correlations (IP-glasma + MUSIC model from Denicol (2014))

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing "ultra-central puzzle" where hydrodynamic models fail to describe the near-equal $v_2/v_3$ ratio observed by CMS (2013) in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions, despite succeeding for other centrality classes. Prior attempts to resolve the puzzle include improved initial conditions (e.g., IP-glasma), modified transport coefficients, hydrodynamic fluctuations, and equations of state, but none fully succeeded. The authors extend the approach of Denicol (2014), who showed nucleon-nucleon correlations affect the $v_2/v_3$ ratio, by systematically varying the minimum nucleon separation and including sub-nucleon structure. The results suggest that initial nuclear and sub-nucleon structures are critical factors, and resolving this puzzle would enable more precise extraction of QGP transport coefficients such as shear viscosity.
