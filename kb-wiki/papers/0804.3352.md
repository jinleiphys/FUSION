# Enhanced Production of Delta(1230) and Sigma(1385) Resonances

> Yields of Delta(1230), Sigma(1385) resonances produced in heavy ion collisions are studied within the framework of a kinetic master equation. The time evolution is driven by the process Delta(1230) \leftrightarrow N π, Sigma(1385) \leftrightarrow Λπ. We obtain resonance yield both below and above chemical equilibrium, depending on initial hadronization condition and separation of kinetic and chemical freeze-out.

## Key claim

The yields of Delta(1230) and Sigma(1385) resonances produced in heavy ion collisions can be either enhanced or suppressed relative to chemical equilibrium expectations, depending on whether the initial hadronization condition is above or below chemical equilibrium and on the separation between chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures.

## Method

The authors solve a kinetic master equation for the time evolution of resonance abundances, driven by the processes Delta(1230) ↔ N π and Sigma(1385) ↔ Λ π, assuming a hydrodynamic expansion model with conserved entropy content and fixed total baryon number (Delta + N constant, Sigma(1385) + Λ constant). The pion yield is taken as a fixed background unaffected by the reactions. The initial hadron yields at hadronization are set by a quark recombination model with constituent quark fugacity γ_q, and the system evolves from chemical freeze-out temperature down to kinetic freeze-out at 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Delta(1230) vacuum lifetime: cτ_Δ = 1/Γ_Δ = 1.67 fm (corresponding to Γ_Δ ≈ 120 MeV)
- Sigma(1385) partial decay width to Λπ: Γ_{Σ→Λ} ≈ 35 MeV
- Hadronization temperature values considered: T = 140, 150, 160, 170, 180 MeV with corresponding γ_q values: 1.6, 1.42, 1.27, 1.12, 1.0
- Time between chemical and thermal freeze-out: Δτ ≤ 2.5 fm/c, as short as 1 fm/c
- Final thermal freeze-out temperature: T_max = 120 MeV
- Predicted relative yield range for Delta: 0.16 < Δ^{++}/p = 0.5 Δ/N_tot < 0.26
- Predicted relative yield range for Sigma(1385): 0.35 < Σ(1385)/Λ_tot < 0.43
- Experimental value for charged Sigma(1385): (Σ^±(1385)+anti-Σ^±(1385))/(Λ_tot+anti-Λ_tot) ≈ 0.29
- Delta and Sigma(1385) yields estimated to be underestimated by about 15%–35% in the simplified model

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model (SHM) approach of Torrieri et al. (2004, 2006) by incorporating the time evolution of resonance abundances between chemical and kinetic freeze-out, going beyond the assumption that yields are fixed at hadronization. It contrasts with microscopic transport simulations by Bleicher and collaborators (2002, 2003, 2006) by using a simplified master equation approach. The results enable using the ratio Δ/N_tot as a tool to distinguish chemical non-equilibrium from chemical equilibrium hadronization conditions, and explain the relatively high yield of charged Sigma(1385) reported by STAR (Adams et al., 2006; Salur et al., 2006) as favoring hadronization at T=140 MeV.
