# Antimatter production in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$=200 GeV

> We have used the dynamically constrained phase space coalescence model to investigate the production of light nuclei (anti-nuclei) based on the 1.134$\times 10^7$ hadronic final states generated by the PACIAE model for the 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$=200 GeV with $|y| <$1 and $p_T<$5 acceptances. The STAR data of $\bar{_{\barΛ}^ 3H}$, ${_Λ^3 H}$, $^3{\bar{He}}$, and $^3{He}$ yields and ratios are well reproduced by the corresponding PACIAE results. The transverse momentum distribution of $\bar{^3{He}}$ (${^3{He}}$) and $\bar{_{\barΛ}^3H}$ (${_Λ^3H}$) is also given. It turned out that the transverse momentum distribution of light nuclei is close to that of the corresponding anti-nuclei.

## Key claim

The dynamically constrained phase space coalescence model, applied to 1.134×10^7 hadronic final states from the PACIAE model, reproduces STAR data for light nuclei and anti-nuclei yields and ratios in 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN=200 GeV, but only after fitting the PACIAE model parameters (K factor, parj(1), parj(2), parj(3)) to STAR data on Λ, anti-Λ, Ξ⁻, and anti-Ξ⁻ yields.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model to generate 1.134×10^7 hadronic final states for 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN=200 GeV, with acceptance cuts |y|<1 and p_T<5 GeV/c. They then applied a dynamically constrained phase space coalescence model, which integrates over the six-dimensional phase space of constituent particles (protons, neutrons, Λ hyperons) in units of h^3N, imposing constraints on invariant mass (within Δm of the rest mass) and spatial separation (within the nuclear radius R_0) to identify coalesced light nuclei and hypernuclei.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the dynamically constrained phase space coalescence model to non-single diffractive pp collisions at √s=7 TeV (Yan et al., year not given in text) to the more complex system of central Au+Au collisions at RHIC. It provides a theoretical description that reproduces STAR measurements of light anti-nuclei and anti-hypernuclei (STAR Collaboration, 2010), and the results are compared with calculations from the coalescence+blast-wave method (Ma et al., year not given in text) and the UrQMD-hydro hybrid model with thermal model (Steinheimer et al., year not given in text). The demonstrated agreement enables further predictions for light nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions at different energies and system sizes.
