# Holographic studies of quasi-topological gravity

> Quasi-topological gravity is a new gravitational theory including curvature-cubed interactions and for which exact black hole solutions were constructed. In a holographic framework, classical quasi-topological gravity can be thought to be dual to the large $N_c$ limit of some non-supersymmetric but conformal gauge theory. We establish various elements of the AdS/CFT dictionary for this duality. This allows us to infer physical constraints on the couplings in the gravitational theory. Further we use holography to investigate hydrodynamic aspects of the dual gauge theory. In particular, we find that the minimum value of the shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio for this model is $η/s \simeq 0.4140/(4π)$.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that quasi-topological gravity, a theory with curvature-cubed interactions, can serve as a holographic dual to a non-supersymmetric but conformal gauge theory at large $N_c$, and that the shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio in this model reaches a non-zero minimum value of $\eta/s \simeq 0.4140/(4\pi)$ rather than vanishing, subject to constraints from positivity of energy fluxes and causality.

## Method

The authors construct the AdS/CFT dictionary for five-dimensional quasi-topological gravity by computing the central charges $a$ and $c$ of the dual four-dimensional CFT via the holographic trace anomaly and two-point function of the stress tensor. They then perform holographic calculations of energy flux coefficients $t_2$ and $t_4$ using the thought experiment of Hofman and Maldacena (2008), which involves localized stress tensor insertions and measurements of energy flux at null infinity, exploiting the fact that linearized graviton equations in the AdS$_5$ vacuum match those of Einstein gravity. Finally, they compute the shear viscosity from hydrodynamic transport in the dual CFT using the black hole solutions of quasi-topological gravity.

## Key numbers

* The minimum value of the shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio is $\eta/s \simeq 0.4140/(4\pi)$ for this model.
* The central charge $c$ is given by $c = \pi^2 \frac{L^3}{\ell_P^3} \frac{1}{f_\infty^{3/2}} \left(1 - 2\lambda f_\infty - 3\mu f_\infty^2\right)$, where $f_\infty$ satisfies $1 - f_\infty + \lambda f_\infty^2 + \mu f_\infty^3 = 0$.
* The central charge $a$ is given by $a = \pi^2 \frac{L^3}{\ell_P^3} \frac{1}{f_\infty^{3/2}} \left(1 - 6\lambda f_\infty + 9\mu f_\infty^2\right)$.
* The ratio $(c-a)/c = 4 f_\infty (\lambda - 3\mu f_\infty) / (1 - 2\lambda f_\infty - 3\mu f_\infty^2)$.
* The Hawking temperature of planar AdS black holes is $T = r_0/(\pi L^2 f_\infty^{1/2})$, with entropy density $s = 2\pi r_0^3/(\ell_P^3 L^3)$ and energy density $\rho = 3r_0^4/(2\ell_P^3 L^5 f_\infty^{1/2})$, satisfying $\rho = \frac{3}{4} T s$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic studies of Gauss-Bonnet gravity (which introduced curvature-squared interactions and allowed independent central charges $a$ and $c$) by incorporating curvature-cubed interactions through quasi-topological gravity, as introduced by Myers and Robinson (2010). The new model enables exploration of a broader class of non-supersymmetric conformal gauge theories and provides a framework to test the KSS bound on $\eta/s$ beyond perturbative corrections. The results enable future investigations of black hole instabilities and the precise nature of lower bounds on transport coefficients in holographic plasmas.
