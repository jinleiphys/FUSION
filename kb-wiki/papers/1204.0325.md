# Calculation of the Structure Properties of a Strange Quark Star in the Presence of Strong Magnetic Field Using a Density Dependent Bag Constant

> In this article we have calculated the structure properties of a strange quark star in static model in the presence of a strong magnetic field using MIT bag model with a density dependent bag constant. To parameterize the density dependence of bag constant, we have used our results for the lowest order constrained variational calculation of the asymmetric nuclear matter. By calculating the equation of state of strange quark matter, we have shown that the pressure of this system increases by increasing both density and magnetic field. Finally, we have investigated the effect of density dependence of bag constant on the structure properties of strange quark star.

## Key claim
The paper claims that using a density-dependent bag constant (rather than a fixed one) in the MIT bag model leads to substantially higher maximum gravitational masses for static strange quark stars in the presence of a strong magnetic field, but only for magnetic fields below about \(10^{19}\ \text{G}\) where pressure anisotropy is negligible.

## Method
The authors compute the equation of state of strange quark matter (u, d, s quarks) using the MIT bag model with a density-dependent bag constant parameterized via a Gaussian form. The bag constant’s asymptotic value is fixed by equating the energy density of strange quark matter to that of asymmetric nuclear matter (proton fraction 0.4) at a transition density, using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the \(UV_{14}+TNI\) nucleon-nucleon potential. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are then integrated to obtain the mass-radius relation and maximum gravitational mass for static strange quark stars at magnetic fields \(B = 0\) and \(B = 5 \times 10^{18}\ \text{G}\).

## Key numbers
- Maximum gravitational mass of a strange quark star with density-dependent bag constant at \(B = 5 \times 10^{18}\ \text{G}\): \(1.62\ M_\odot\).
- Maximum gravitational mass of a strange quark star with fixed bag constant (\(90\ \frac{\text{MeV}}{\text{fm}^3}\)) at \(B = 5 \times 10^{18}\ \text{G}\): \(1.33\ M_\odot\).
- Transition density for hadron-quark phase change: \(\rho_B = 0.98\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (corresponding to energy density \(1100\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3}\)).
- Asymptotic bag constant value: \(\mathcal{B}_{\infty} = 8.99\ \frac{\text{MeV}}{\text{fm}^3}\).
- Surface redshift for the maximum-mass star with density-dependent bag constant at \(B = 5 \times 10^{18}\ \text{G}\): \(z_s = 0.534\ \text{ms}^{-1}\).
- For \(\rho > 0.52\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), the pressure of strange quark matter with density-dependent bag constant exceeds that with fixed bag constant at \(B = 5 \times 10^{18}\ \text{G}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors’ prior calculations of strange quark star structure using a fixed bag constant (Bordbar and Peyvand, year not given) by introducing a density-dependent bag constant, motivated by the need for a density-dependent form to describe the hadron-quark transition (Burgio et al., year not given). The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetized strange quark stars, particularly for objects like SWIFT J1749.4-2807 and RX J185635-3754, and suggest that rotation (not considered here) could further increase the maximum mass (Shen et al., year not given).
