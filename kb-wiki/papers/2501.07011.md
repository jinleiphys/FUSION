# Constraint on the magnetic field for the stable strange quark matter

> The quasiparticle model is employed to investigate the quark matter at finite chemical potential. The effective bag constant is derived to be dependent on both the chemical potential and the magnetic field. The self-consistent thermodynamics is fulfilled that the free energy minimum corresponds to the zero pressure. It is shown that the strong magnetic field is helpful for the stabilization of the strange quark matter. However, the increase in the coupling constant and the vacuum bag constant could reduce the stability. For the absolutely stable strange quark matter, there is a lower limit of the allowed magnetic field, which rises with the increase in the coupling constant and the vacuum bag constant.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a strong magnetic field enhances the stability of strange quark matter (SQM), but this stability is conditional: there exists a lower limit on the allowed magnetic field for absolutely stable SQM, and this lower limit rises with increasing coupling constant and vacuum bag constant.

## Method
The authors employ a quasiparticle model with medium-dependent quark masses derived from the hard dense loop approximation, incorporating a running coupling constant that depends on both chemical potential and magnetic field. They derive an effective bag constant self-consistently from the stationarity condition of the pressure with respect to the effective mass, ensuring thermodynamic consistency. The stability of SQM is then evaluated by requiring that the free energy per baryon at zero pressure be less than 930 MeV (the energy per baryon of iron nuclei).

## Key numbers
- The vacuum bag constant is set to \(B_0^{1/4} = 145\) MeV (corresponding to \(B_0 = 58\) MeV·fm\(^{-3}\)).
- The critical chemical potential for stability relative to nuclear matter is \(\mu_c = \mu_B/3 = 310\) MeV.
- The stability condition requires the free energy per baryon to be smaller than 930 MeV (the value for iron nuclei).
- The magnetic field values studied include \(eB_m = 0\), 0.2, 0.3, and 0.4 GeV\(^2\).
- The scale-fixing parameters for the running coupling are \(\Lambda_1 = 120\) MeV and \(\Lambda_2 = 200\) MeV, with free parameters \(\alpha = 2\) and \(\beta = 0.000327\).

## Context
This work extends the quasiparticle model approach of Peshier (1996, 1999) and Schertler (1996, 1997) to include magnetic field effects on the stability of strange quark matter, building on the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis that SQM clusters could be more stable than atomic nuclei. The self-consistent thermodynamic treatment follows the stationarity condition of Gorenstein and Yang (1995). The results provide constraints on the magnetic field strength in SQM that could be relevant for models of strongly magnetized neutron stars, such as those studied by Dexheimer (2016), and for the possibility of SQM composing massive compact objects.
