# Universal Relations and Correlation Analysis of Proto-Neutron Star Properties in Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity

> Proto-neutron stars (PNSs) are the hot, lepton-rich remnants of the core collapse supernovae, which go through a cooling phase and become cold, stable Neutron stars (NSs). Since PNSs are also superdense objects with strong gravitational fields, we can use them to probe general relativity (GR) in the high-curvature regime, similar to NSs. In this study, we analyze the macroscopic properties like mass, radius, compactness, tidal deformability, $f$-mode oscillations and gravitational binding energy of PNSs using four different relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations of state (EOSs) with fixed entropy per baryon ($S$ =1, 2) and varying the lepton fractions ($Y_l$). The variation of $S$ and $Y_l$ has a noticeable effect on these properties. Extending our study beyond GR, we explore these effects within the framework of Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity (EMSG). This modified gravity theory adds the squared energy-momentum terms to the field equations with a free parameter $α$. In the weak-field regimes, EMSG remains indistinguishable from GR, but in the strong-field regimes, such as PNSs or NSs, it shows measurable deviations. Varying the free parameter $α$, we observe significant changes in the macroscopic properties of the PNSs. After that, we focus on the universal relations of the macroscopic properties and the correlations of the universal relations. We find that, despite significant changes in the macroscopic properties induced by the variations of $S$, $Y_l$ and $α$, the correlations remain strong and nearly unaffected.

## Key claim

The study finds that universal relations (URs) connecting macroscopic properties of proto-neutron stars (PNSs), such as tidal deformability, compactness, gravitational binding energy, and f-mode frequency, remain strong and nearly unaffected despite significant changes in those properties induced by variations in entropy per baryon (S = 1, 2), lepton fraction (Y_l), and the Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity (EMSG) coupling parameter α.

## Method

The author generated finite-temperature equations of state (EOSs) using four different relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (NITR, IOPB-I, MODEL I, and IUFSU) at fixed entropy per baryon S = 1 and 2 with varying lepton fractions Y_l, combined with a finite-temperature crust from the HS(DD2) EOS. The modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations in the EMSG framework were solved to obtain mass, radius, compactness, tidal deformability, f-mode oscillation frequencies (using the Cowling approximation), and gravitational binding energy for PNS configurations. Universal relations and their correlations were then fitted with polynomial functions for different values of the EMSG parameter α.

## Key numbers

- Four RMF EOS models were used: NITR, IOPB-I, MODEL I, and IUFSU, all consistent with NS maximum mass constraints to approximately 2 M⊙.
- Entropy per baryon values were fixed at S = 1 and S = 2 (units of kB, the Boltzmann constant), with lepton fractions Y_l varied between 0.2 and 0.4.
- The EMSG coupling parameter α was varied, with specific values shown in fitting tables including α = +5.01 × 10⁻³⁸, 0, and −5.01 × 10⁻³⁸ (units: erg⁻¹ cm³).
- For the f_fM₁.₄–C (compactness) universal relation at S = 0 and α = 0, the fitting coefficients include a₀ = −2.348 × 10⁻², a₁ = 10.01, a₂ = 2.974 × 10¹, a₃ = −0.806 × 10², a₄ = 0.286 × 10³, a₅ = −0.664 × 10³, with reduced chi-squared χᵣ² = 1.375 × 10⁻⁴.
- For the f_fM₁.₄–C relation at S = 2, Y_l = 0.4 and α = 0, the coefficients are a₀ = −41.26 × 10⁻², a₁ = 15.98, a₂ = 1.766 × 10¹, a₃ = −1.380 × 10², a₄ = 0.556 × 10³, a₅ = −1.014 × 10³, with χᵣ² = 1.441 × 10⁻⁴.
- Effective squared sound speeds (c_s(eff)²) for all EOSs, S, Y_l, and α variations remained between 0 and 0.6, satisfying the causality condition (0 ≤ c_s² ≤ 1).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of universal relations for cold neutron stars in EMSG (Ghosh 2025) to the unexplored regime of proto-neutron stars, which are hot, lepton-rich remnants of core-collapse supernovae. The analysis uses four RMF EOSs consistent with nuclear matter constraints from terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations (Lattimer 2013, Hagen 2015, RocaMaza 2015, Oertel 2017) and with observational NS mass-radius constraints from PSR J0740+6620 (Miller 2021, Riley 2021) and PSR J0030+0451 (Miller 2019, Riley 2019). The findings enable future gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observations of PNSs to test EMSG modifications to general relativity, since the universal relations remain robust even when macroscopic properties change significantly.
