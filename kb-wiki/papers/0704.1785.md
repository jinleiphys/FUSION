# Three-body collisions in Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck theory

> Aiming at a microscopic description of heavy ion collisions in the beam energy region of about 10 A GeV, we extend the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model by including a relativistic mean field, in-medium baryon-baryon cross sections and three-body collisions. The model is then compared with experimental data for central Au+Au collisions at 2-10 A GeV and central Pb+Pb collisions at 30 and 40 A GeV on the proton rapidity spectra, the midrapidity yields of $π^+$, $K^\pm$ and $(Λ+Σ^0)$, and the transverse mass spectra of $π^\pm$ and $K^\pm$. The three-body collisions increase the inverse slope parameters of the hadron $m_\perp$-spectra to a good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model to include three-body collisions, a relativistic mean field, and in-medium baryon-baryon cross sections, and show that three-body collisions increase the inverse slope parameters of hadron transverse mass spectra, bringing them into good agreement with experimental data from heavy ion collisions at 2-40 A GeV. The abstract glosses over the condition that the mean field and in-medium reduced cross sections alone lead to less thermalization, and it is the three-body collisions that counterbalance this effect.

## Method

The authors implemented a Walecka-type relativistic mean field (RMF) for baryon propagation using the NL2 parameter set, in-medium baryon-baryon cross sections computed via a corrected invariant energy scheme and a modification factor accounting for Dirac mass reduction, and a geometrical three-body collision algorithm based on a hard-sphere picture with Lorentz-contracted interaction volumes. The three-body collision procedure redistributes momenta microcanonically according to three-body phase space, then simulates a two-body collision of the first two particles using the standard GiBUU collision machinery including resonance excitations and FRITIOF string fragmentation. The model was applied to central Au+Au collisions at 2-10 A GeV and central Pb+Pb collisions at 30 and 40 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Gas parameter estimate: at baryon density rho_B = 10 rho_0 (with rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3), gamma_gas = (sigma/pi)^(3/2) rho_B ~ 2, using sigma ~ 40 mb for the total pp cross section in vacuum.
- Formation time used for all baryons and mesons: tau_f = 0.8 fm/c.
- RMF parameter set NL2: m_sigma = 550.5 MeV, m_omega = 783.3 MeV, g_sigma = 8.50, g_omega = 7.54, g_2 = -50.37 fm^-1, g_3 = -6.26, producing incompressibility K = 210 MeV and nucleon effective mass ratio m_nuc^star/m_nuc = 0.83 at normal nuclear matter density.
- Number of test particles per nucleon: N = 200.
- Gaussian smearing width for test particles: L ~ 1 fm.
- Energy conservation accuracy: better than 3% of the center-of-mass kinetic energy for the studied reactions.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport model beyond previous calculations by Weber (2003), Bratkovskaya (2004), and W. Liu et al. (2005), which systematically overpredicted pion multiplicity and produced too steep kaon transverse mass spectra at beam energies above 2 A GeV. The three-body collisions address the insufficient thermalization in microscopic transport models, providing an alternative explanation to the formation of a nonhadronic phase advocated by Bratkovskaya (2004). The approach enables improved microscopic description of heavy ion collisions in the 10-20 A GeV region relevant for the future Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at FAIR, and builds on earlier N-body collision implementations by Kodama (1982), Bonasera et al. (1992), and others for beam energies below 1-2 A GeV.
