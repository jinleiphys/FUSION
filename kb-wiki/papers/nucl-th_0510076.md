# Analysis of zero-frequency solutions of the pion dispersion equation in nuclear matter

> In this paper we consider instability of nuclear matter which takes place when the frequencies of the collective excitations turn to zero. We investigate collective excitations with pion quantum numbers J^π=0^-. We study the dependence of zero-frequency solutions of the pion dispersion equation on the value of the spin-isospin quasiparticle interaction G'. The solutions of the pion dispersion equation describe the different types of the excitations in the matter, ω_i(k). At the critical density ρ=ρ_c one of solutions of the definite type turns to zero: ω_{i0}(k_c)=0. When ρ>ρ_c, the excitations ω_{i0}(k) become amplified. It is shown that there is such a "transitional" value of G'=G'_{tr} that for G'<G'_{tr} the zero-frequency solutions belong to the type ω_{sd} while for G'>G'_{tr} they pertain to the type ω_c. The solutions of the type ω_{sd} correspond to instability to small density fluctuations of the nuclear matter at G'\le -1. On the other hand, ω_c is responsible for the "pion condensation" at G'\approx 2. For the stable nuclear matter the branches of solutions ω_{sd}(k) and ω_c(k) are located on the unphysical sheets of the complex plane of frequency.

## Key claim

The paper shows that zero-frequency solutions of the pion dispersion equation in nuclear matter belong to two distinct families of collective excitations depending on the value of the spin-isospin quasiparticle interaction parameter \(G'\): for \(G' < G'_{tr}\) they belong to the \(\omega_{sd}\) family (spinodal decomposition instability), while for \(G' > G'_{tr}\) they belong to the \(\omega_c\) family (pion condensation instability), with a transitional value \(|G'_{tr}| \ll 1\).

## Method

The author solves the nonrelativistic pion dispersion equation \(D^{-1} = \omega^2 - k^2 - m_\pi^2 - \Pi^R(\omega,k) = 0\) in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature using the Migdal model, where the pion self-energy \(\Pi^R\) is constructed from particle-hole and isobar-hole loops renormalized by effective quasiparticle interactions \(G'_{NN}\), \(G'_{N\Delta}\), and \(G'_{\Delta\Delta}\). The analysis extends solutions to the unphysical sheets of the complex frequency plane by analytically continuing the logarithmic cuts of the polarization operator, allowing identification of zero-frequency solutions \(\omega_{i0}(k_c)=0\) that satisfy \((\frac{d}{dk}\omega(k))_{k=k_c}=0\) at critical density \(\rho_c\) and wave vector \(k_c\).

## Key numbers

* The equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter is \(\rho_0\), with Fermi momentum \(p_{F0}=0.268\) GeV.
* The effective mass of quasiparticles is \(m=0.8 m_0\), where \(m_0=0.94\) GeV is the vacuum nucleon mass.
* The pion mass is \(m_\pi=0.14\) GeV; the isobar mass is \(\Delta=1.23\) GeV (isobar width neglected).
* The pion-nucleon coupling constant is \(f_{\pi NN}=1\); the pion-nucleon-isobar coupling constant is \(f_{\pi N\Delta}=2\).
* The Landau-Migdal parameters for isobar interactions are fixed at \(G'_{N\Delta}=0.4\) and \(G'_{\Delta\Delta}=1.6\).
* The form factor cutoff parameters are \(\Lambda_N=0.667\) GeV for nucleons and \(\Lambda_\Delta=1.0\) GeV for isobars.
* The experimental isobar width is \(\Gamma=115\) MeV (not included in the calculations).
* The transitional value \(G'_{tr}\) satisfies \(|G'_{tr}| \ll 1\).

## Context

This work extends the Migdal model analysis of pion excitations in nuclear matter (Migdal, 1978) by studying solutions on the unphysical sheets of the complex frequency plane, building on earlier work by Sadovnikova and collaborators (DRS, SR). The paper distinguishes between two instability mechanisms: spinodal decomposition at \(G' \le -1\) (related to long-wavelength density fluctuations, as discussed in Landau and Lifshitz) and pion condensation at \(G' \approx 2\) (predicted by Migdal and collaborators). The results enable identification of which type of collective excitation becomes unstable at the phase transition boundary, which is important for understanding phase transitions in nuclear matter relevant to heavy ion collisions and neutron star interiors.
