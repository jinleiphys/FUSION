# Theory @ Hard Probes 2015

> 8 pages, to appear in the proceedings of the Hard Probes 2015 conference, held at McGill University, Montreal, Canada, June 29 to July 3 2015, reference added

## Key claim

The paper provides an overview of theoretical developments presented at the Hard Probes 2015 conference, covering progress in understanding the quark-gluon plasma through hard probes, but notes that a consistent framework combining all relevant aspects of medium evolution, hard parton production, electromagnetic probes, and heavy quark dynamics remains outstanding.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical work presented at the conference, organized by sub-field: initial state calculations using classical gluon fields (IP-Glasma) and pQCD with saturation (EKRT framework); bulk evolution modeled with viscous relativistic fluid dynamics, including extended theories such as DNMR and anisotropic hydrodynamics; electromagnetic probe calculations including thermal photon and dilepton rates to NLO; heavy flavor physics including quarkonium production and open heavy flavor energy loss; parton shower and jet calculations using multiple formalisms (Higher Twist, BDMPS-Z, AdS/CFT); and small collision system analyses including initial-state and final-state explanations for ridge correlations.

## Key numbers

- The extent of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions does not exceed approximately 20 fm
- Lattice NRQCD shows survival of the Upsilon and chi_b1 states up to temperatures greater than 249 MeV
- The heavy quark diffusion coefficient from lattice calculations is DT = 0.59 to 1.1
- Soft radiated partons in jets are of order approximately 1 GeV when perturbative methods cease to be valid
- The EKRT framework together with second order viscous hydrodynamics was used to constrain the temperature dependence of eta/s

## Context

This conference proceedings extends the line of work on heavy ion collisions and QCD matter characterization presented at previous Hard Probes conferences. It reviews progress in initial state calculations from the IP-Glasma framework (Schenke 2012) and the EKRT model (Eskola 1999), bulk evolution using DNMR theory (Denicol 2012) and anisotropic hydrodynamics (Martinez 2010, 2012), and jet quenching calculations using the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996). The work enables future development of a unified theoretical framework combining all aspects of medium evolution, hard probe production, and heavy flavor dynamics, as well as improved understanding of small collision systems where ridge-like correlations have been discovered.
