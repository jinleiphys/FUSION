# Quantum rearrangement and self-consistent BCS-BEC crossover thermodynamics

> Based on previous works, analytical calculational procedures for dealing with the strongly interacting fermions ground state are further developed through a medium dependent potential in terms of the Bethe-Peierls contact interaction model. The methods are exact in the unitary limit regime and they lead to the self-consistent equations analogous to the Hartree ones. The single particle energy spectrum rearrangement effects on the thermodynamics due to the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem constraint are addressed. These effects lead to an additional instantaneous correlation potential contribution to the system physical chemical potential and pressure, i.e., equation of state, which enforces the classical thermodynamic consistency. The Dyson-Schwinger equations represent implicitly the various Bethe-Goldstone expansion ones. In a thermodynamically self-consistent way, the universal dimensionless factor is analytically calculated to be $ξ=\049$, which defines the ratio of the unitary fermions energy density to that of the ideal non-interacting ones at T=0.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the universal dimensionless factor $\xi$ for unitary fermions at $T=0$ is analytically calculated to be $\xi=0.49$, but this result depends on incorporating single-particle energy spectrum rearrangement effects to enforce thermodynamic consistency via the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, and the authors note that the exact value remains debated in the range $0.42-0.46$.

## Method

The authors develop a non-relativistic Bethe-Peierls contact interaction model with a medium-dependent effective potential $U_{eff}^*[n,T]$, derived through an analogy with the intermediate vector boson theory and a counteracting environment that introduces an anti-screening sign in the interaction denominator. They solve the generalized Dyson-Schwinger equations in the instantaneous approximation, compute the polarization function in the static infrared limit using the non-relativistic random phase approximation, and derive closed algebraic expressions for the number density, energy density, entropy density, pressure, and chemical potential. The rearrangement terms in the pressure and chemical potential are obtained analytically from the density dependence of the Debye screening mass.

## Key numbers

* Universal dimensionless factor $\xi = 0.49$ for the ratio of unitary fermion energy density to that of the ideal non-interacting fermion gas at $T=0$.
* At unitarity and $T=0$, the pressure is $P = \xi P_{ideal}$ with $\xi = 0.49$, and the sound speed is $v = \sqrt{\xi} \, v_{FG} = 0.7 \, v_{FG}$, where $v_{FG} = \sqrt{1/3} \, v_f$ and $v_f = k_f/m$ is the Fermi speed.
* The accepted experimental/theoretical range for $\xi$ is stated as $0.40-0.46$, with updating experiments converging toward $\xi \sim 0.42-0.46$.
* At $T=0$, the in-medium effective scattering length is $a_{eff} = a / (1 - 2 k_f a / \pi)$.
* The Debye screening mass squared at $T=0$ is $m_D^2|_{T=0} = k_f m / \pi^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical approach (Chen et al., 2006) for the unitary limit, which used a relativistic Dirac many-body formalism and a medium-dependent interaction with a counteracting external source, but produced auxiliary pressure and chemical potential that violated the universal hypothesis of Ho (2004). The present paper corrects those quantities by incorporating quantum rearrangement effects of the single-particle energy spectrum, a technique standard in Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone nuclear many-body theory (Brown, 2002; Baker, 1971; Rios, 2005; Nakada, 2006), to enforce the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem. The result $\xi=0.49$ is compared with quantum Monte-Carlo results (Astrakharchik, 2004; Bulgac, 2005) and experiments (Partridge, 2006; Stewart, 2006; Joseph, 2006), and the framework enables analytical calculation of finite-temperature thermodynamics and collective mode frequencies in trapped unitary Fermi gases.
