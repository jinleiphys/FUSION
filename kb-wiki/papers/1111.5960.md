# Hadron Structure in Lattice QCD

> Recent progress in hadron structure calculations within lattice QCD is reviewed. Results on key observables such as the axial charge, the quark momentum fraction and the spin content of the nucleon are discussed with focus on open issues. Lattice QCD studies of the $γ^* N\rightarrow Δ$ transition as well as the $Δ$ form factors are also presented.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that lattice QCD has reached a stage where simulations at near-physical parameters can provide *ab initio* calculations of hadron structure observables, but emphasizes that systematic uncertainties from finite volume, finite lattice spacing, heavier-than-physical pion masses, and excited-state contamination must be carefully controlled before quantitative comparison with experiment is possible.

## Method

The paper reviews the lattice QCD formalism for computing hadron masses from two-point correlation functions and hadron matrix elements from three-point correlation functions using the fixed-sink method. It describes how nucleon generalized form factors (GFFs) and moments of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point functions, with renormalization performed non-perturbatively. The review covers both connected and disconnected diagram contributions, the latter evaluated using stochastic techniques.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon axial charge \(g_A = G_A(0)\) is identified as a benchmark quantity for lattice QCD techniques, precisely determined from neutron \(\beta\)-decay.
- Momentum transfers up to \(Q^2 = -q^2 \sim 2\ \text{GeV}^2\) can typically be achieved in lattice calculations.
- Finite volume effects are small for lattice sizes satisfying \(L_S m_\pi \gtrsim 3.5\).
- The cost of generating \(10^3\) independent \(N_f=2\) twisted mass fermion gauge configurations scales as \(C_{\text{sim}} \propto (300\ \text{MeV}/m_\pi)^{c_m}\) with \(c_m \sim 4\), and at the physical point is estimated to be \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) Teraflop\(\cdot\)year.
- For nucleon form factors at pion masses above about 300 MeV, sink-source separations \(t_f - t_i \gtrsim 1\ \text{fm}\) are sufficient to suppress excited-state contamination.
- The disconnected contribution to the nucleon magnetic form factor is consistent with zero, while the disconnected contribution to the scalar form factor is of the same order as the connected one.

## Context

This review extends the line of lattice QCD hadron structure calculations summarized by Hagler (2009) and Alexandrou (2010), and discusses results from the BMW Collaboration (Durr, 2008) and ETM Collaboration (Alexandrou, 2009) on the low-lying baryon spectrum. It enables future calculations at physical pion masses, studies of disconnected contributions using stochastic methods (Bali, 2009), and extraction of resonance parameters such as the \(\rho\)-meson width via Luscher's relation (Feng, 2010).
