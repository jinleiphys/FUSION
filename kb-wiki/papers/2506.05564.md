# Bayesian Inference of the Landau Parameter $G'_0$ from Joint Gamow-Teller Measurements

> The Landau-Migdal parameter $G'_0$ characterizes the main part of the spin-isospin dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction. Consequently, the $G'_0$ is closely related to the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR), the beta and double-beta decay rates of finite nuclei, the response of hot and dense nucleonic matter that modifies the neutrino-nucleon absorption rates in core-collapse supernovae (CCSNe) and binary neutron star (BNS) mergers, and finally the critical density for pion condensation in neutron stars. In this letter, for the first time, we report the $G'_0$ with quantified uncertainty in the framework of Bayesian inference, using a self-consistent standard Skyrme Random Phase Approximation (RPA) model and joint constraints from experimental GTR measurements on $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$, $^{132}\mathrm{Sn}$, $^{90}\mathrm{Zr}$. Our extracted $G_0'$ is $0.48\pm0.034$, which is close to the prediction of a few existing Skyrme models that consider spin-isospin observables but smaller than the traditional ones extracted from pion-exchange models. We hint to possible reasons for this deviation, like the value of the nucleon effective mass $\frac{m^*}{m}$. The $G_0'$ values extracted in this work may guide the construction of new energy density functionals that aims to self-consistently describe the dense matter properties in the spin-isospin channel.

## Key claim

The Landau-Migdal parameter \(G'_0\) is extracted with quantified uncertainty for the first time using Bayesian inference, yielding \(0.48 \pm 0.034\), which is smaller than traditional values from pion-exchange models; the authors suggest this deviation may be related to the nucleon effective mass \(m^*/m\) being smaller than previously assumed.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using a self-consistent Skyrme Random Phase Approximation (RPA) model, constrained by joint experimental Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) measurements on \({}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\), \({}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}\), and \({}^{90}\mathrm{Zr}\). The inference also incorporated ground-state properties (charge radii and binding energies), spin-orbit splitting constraints, and intrinsic scattering parameters to account for systematic model uncertainties. The posterior distribution of Skyrme parameters was sampled to extract the corresponding \(G'_0\) values.

## Key numbers

- Extracted Landau parameter \(G'_0 = 0.48 \pm 0.034\) from joint GTR measurements on \({}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\), \({}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}\), and \({}^{90}\mathrm{Zr}\).
- Mean effective mass ratio \(m^*/m = 0.67\) in symmetric nuclear matter at \(\rho = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}\) from the posterior distribution.
- Experimental GTR peak energies used as constraints: \(E_\mathrm{GT}(^{90}\mathrm{Zr}) = 15.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (uncertainty 0.32 MeV), \(E_\mathrm{GT}(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}) = 13.97\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (uncertainty 0.28 MeV), \(E_\mathrm{GT}(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}) = 19.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (uncertainty 0.38 MeV).
- Experimental GTR strength fractions used as constraints: \(m_0\%(^{90}\mathrm{Zr}) = 68\%\) (uncertainty 1.4%), \(m_0\%(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}) = 81.0\%\) (uncertainty 16%), \(m_0\%(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}) = 74\%\) (uncertainty 1.48%).

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to determine \(G'_0\) from phenomenological models such as Suzuki (1982) and Towner (1987), which used simplified RPA solutions and assumed larger effective masses (\(0.8 \lessapprox m^*/m \lessapprox 1.0\)). The authors demonstrate that a self-consistent Skyrme RPA approach with Bayesian uncertainty quantification yields a smaller \(G'_0\) than those traditional extractions. The results can guide construction of new energy density functionals that self-consistently describe dense matter properties in the spin-isospin channel, with implications for neutrino-nucleon absorption rates in core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers.
