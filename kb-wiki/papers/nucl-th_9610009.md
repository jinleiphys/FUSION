# Photocouplings of nucleon resonances with gluon and pion exchange currents

> Photocouplings for the nucleon resonances most relevant to total photoabsorption are calculated in a constituent quark model including gluon and pion exchange. The sensitivity of the results to the different ingredients of the model is discussed.

# Photocouplings of nucleon resonances with gluon and pion exchange currents

## Key claim

The inclusion of two-body pion exchange currents in the constituent quark model systematically worsens the agreement with experimental photocoupling data for the P33(1232), D13(1520), and F15(1680) resonances, contrary to the expectation that such currents would improve the description of the three main peaks in total photoabsorption. The pion exchange contribution is large and has the opposite sign to the gluon exchange contribution, driving the calculated photocouplings away from experimental values, whereas omitting pion exchange yields results much closer to data.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator confinement and residual interactions from one-gluon exchange and one-pion exchange potentials. They calculated helicity amplitudes for photon decay of the three resonances using a one-body electromagnetic current plus two-body currents from gluon pair, pion pair, and pionic current operators, with model parameters fixed by fitting the mass spectrum (including the Delta-N mass splitting) as in Buchmann (prior work). Configuration mixing coefficients for the baryon wave functions were taken from several variants of the Isgur-Karl model and from Buchmann (prior work), and the matrix elements were evaluated analytically for the one-body current and numerically for the two-body currents.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: m_q = 313 MeV (one third of the nucleon mass)
- Oscillator spring constant: alpha = 0.41 GeV = 2.0778 fm^{-1} (chosen to match confinement radius)
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: f^2_{pi N}/4pi = 0.08, with f_{pi q} = (3/5) f_{pi N}
- For the P33(1232) resonance, the one-body current contribution to A^p_{3/2} is approximately 0.100 GeV^{-1/2} (with configuration mixing G1), while the total including pion exchange currents drops to approximately 0.050 GeV^{-1/2}, compared to the experimental value of approximately 0.258 GeV^{-1/2}
- For the D13(1520) resonance, the A^p_{1/2} amplitude with configuration mixing G and including pion exchange (B1-G) is approximately 0.010 GeV^{-1/2}, compared to the experimental value of approximately 0.024 GeV^{-1/2}
- For the F15(1680) resonance, the A^p_{1/2} amplitude with configuration mixing G and including pion exchange (B1-G) is approximately -0.001 GeV^{-1/2}, compared to the experimental value of approximately -0.015 GeV^{-1/2}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Buchmann (prior work) on nucleon electromagnetic form factors to the photocouplings of the three resonances dominating total photoabsorption, following earlier studies by Copley (prior work), Isgur and Karl (prior work), Koniuk (prior work), and Warns (prior work) that used only one-body currents. The results contradict the expectation from Robson (prior work) that pion exchange currents would improve the description, and instead show that the nonrelativistic constituent quark model with gluon exchange alone gives better photocouplings. The findings highlight the limitations of nonrelativistic quark models and suggest that relativistic effects or different mechanisms for the second photoabsorption peak (as studied by Braghieri, prior work) may be needed to resolve the discrepancies.
