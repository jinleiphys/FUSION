# Studying the effect of the hadronic phase in nuclear collisions with PYTHIA and UrQMD

> In this work, we couple Pb-Pb events simulated with the PYTHIA Angantyr event generator at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=2.76$ and $5.02$~TeV with the hadronic cascade simulator UrQMD to study the effect of the hadronic phase on observables such as charged-particle multiplicity densities, transverse momentum spectra and identified particle ratios, giving special emphasis to short-lived resonances.

## Key claim

Coupling PYTHIA Angantyr with UrQMD produces a heavy-ion collision simulation chain with no equilibrium assumption, and the hadronic phase alone can generate up to 50-60% of the measured elliptic flow, even in the complete absence of any initial flow from a quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Method

The authors coupled Pb-Pb events generated by the PYTHIA Angantyr event generator (which uses a wounded-nucleon Glauber model with no free parameters beyond those fixed by pp, ep, and e+e- data) with the hadronic cascade simulator UrQMD. Spatial vertices for final-state hadrons were calculated using the string model and passed to UrQMD, which then simulated elastic and inelastic hadronic interactions without assuming any equilibrium. The chain was used to compute charged-particle multiplicity densities, transverse momentum spectra, identified particle ratios, the nuclear modification factor R_AA, and elliptic flow coefficients v2{2} and v2{4}.

## Key numbers

- Charged-particle multiplicity density (dN_ch/deta) from the simulation chain is within 10% of ALICE measurements at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.
- Hadronic interactions reduce high-pT yields most strongly around 5 GeV/c, with a radial-flow-like effect pushing very low momentum particles to about 1.5 GeV/c.
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA from PYTHIA+UrQMD matches ALICE data above a pT of 6 GeV/c at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- Elliptic flow from hadronic interactions alone reaches approximately 50-60% of the values measured by ALICE at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- The v2{4} from 4-particle cumulants matches the v2 from a fit to the dN_ch/dDeltaPhi distribution in 0-50% centrality collisions.

## Context

This work extends the Angantyr framework (Bierlich 2016, 2018) by adding a hadronic cascade stage, providing a baseline for testing models of collectivity that include QGP effects. The finding that hadronic interactions alone generate substantial elliptic flow is consistent with earlier UrQMD studies (Lu 2006). The results enable future disentanglement of hadronic-phase effects from QGP signatures in heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that further studies are needed to determine how much flow must be present at hadronization time to reproduce ALICE measurements.
