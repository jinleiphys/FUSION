# Anisotropic Hydrodynamics: Three lectures

> Anisotropic hydrodynamics is a non-perturbative reorganization of relativistic hydrodynamics that takes into account the large momentum-space anisotropies generated in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. As a result, it allows one to extend the regime of applicability of hydrodynamic treatments to situations that can be quite far from isotropic thermal equilibrium. In this paper, I review the material presented in a series of three introductory lectures. I review the derivation of ideal and second-order viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory. I then show how to extend the methods used to a system that can be highly anisotropic in local-rest-frame momenta. I close by discussing recent work on this topic and then present an outlook to the future.

## Key claim

Anisotropic hydrodynamics extends the regime of applicability of relativistic hydrodynamic treatments to systems far from isotropic thermal equilibrium by non-perturbatively reorganizing the expansion around a momentum-space anisotropic leading-order distribution function, rather than around an isotropic equilibrium distribution as done in standard viscous hydrodynamics.

## Method

The author derives ideal and second-order viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation, then extends this framework by replacing the isotropic equilibrium distribution function with a spheroidally anisotropic leading-order ansatz parameterized by a single anisotropy parameter ξ and a temperature-like scale Λ. The dynamical equations of leading-order spheroidal anisotropic hydrodynamics are obtained from the zeroth and first moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, ignoring the non-spheroidal correction δ̃f. Next-to-leading-order corrections from δ̃f are treated perturbatively, while the spheroidal deformation is treated non-perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC-like initial conditions with T₀ = 400 MeV at τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c and η/S = 1/4π, first-order Navier-Stokes gives (P_L/P_T)_NS ≈ 0.5.
- For LHC-like initial conditions with T₀ = 600 MeV at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c and η/S = 1/4π, first-order Navier-Stokes gives (P_L/P_T)_NS ≈ 0.35.
- At very early times after initial nuclear impact, pressure anisotropies reach P_L/P_T ≲ 0.3 in the center of the fireball for shear viscosities consistent with experimental observations.
- The shear relaxation time in the strong-coupling limit of N=4 SYM is τ_π = (2 - log 2)/2πT, giving τ_π ∼ 0.1 fm/c for RHIC-like and τ_π ∼ 0.07 fm/c for LHC-like initial conditions.
- Strong-coupling AdS/CFT simulations find early-time pressure anisotropies on the order of P_L/P_T ∼ 0.31 or smaller.
- For an ideal gas equation of state in 0+1d boost-invariant expansion, energy density evolves as E = E₀ (τ₀/τ)^{4/3}.

## Context

This review extends the work on relativistic viscous hydrodynamics applied to ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, which has been widely used to model quark-gluon plasma evolution at RHIC and LHC (Huovinen 2001, Heinz 2005, Romatschke 2007, Song 2008, Schenke 2010, Bozek 2011, Niemi 2011, Denicol 2012). The anisotropic hydrodynamics framework was developed by Martinez (2010), Florkowski (2010), and Ryblewski (2010) to address the problem that traditional linearized viscous hydrodynamics can produce negative one-particle distribution functions in regions of phase space when momentum-space anisotropies are large. The approach enables more reliable modeling of early-time dynamics, dynamics near the transverse edges of the nuclear overlap region, and temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratios, and has been benchmarked against exact solutions to the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation.
