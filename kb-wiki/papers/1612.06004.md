# Shell-model calculation of isospin-symmetry breaking correction to superallowed Fermi beta-decay

> We investigate the radial-overlap part of the isospin-symmetry breaking correction to superallowed $0^+\to 0^+$-decay using the shell-model approach similar to that of Refs. [1, 2]. The 8 sd-shell emitters with masses between $A=22$ and $A=38$ have been re-examined. The Fermi matrix element is evaluated with realistic spherical single-particle wave functions, obtained from spherical Woods-Saxon (WS) or Hartree-Fock (HF) potentials, fine-tuned to reproduce the experimental data on charge radii and separation energies for nuclei of interest. The elaborated adjustment procedure removes any sensitivity of the correction to a specific parametrisation of the WS potential or to various versions of the Skyrme interaction. The present results are generally in good agreement with those reported in Refs. [3, 4]. At the same time, we find that the calculations with HF wave functions result in systematically lower values of the correction.

## Key claim

The radial-overlap part of the isospin-symmetry breaking correction to superallowed Fermi beta-decay is calculated using shell-model wave functions combined with realistic spherical single-particle wave functions from Woods-Saxon or Hartree-Fock potentials; the authors find that calculations with Hartree-Fock wave functions yield systematically lower values of the correction than those with Woods-Saxon wave functions, while both sets of results are generally consistent with prior work.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in the full sd shell using the NuShellX@MSU code for eight sd-shell emitters (A=22 to A=38). The Fermi matrix element was evaluated using realistic spherical single-particle wave functions obtained from either Woods-Saxon potentials (with two parametrizations: Schwierz-Wiedenhöver-Volya and modified Bohr-Mottelson) or self-consistent Hartree-Fock potentials (with three Skyrme forces: SGII, SkM*, and SLy5). The potentials were fine-tuned to reproduce experimental charge radii and proton/neutron separation energies for each nucleus, and up to 100 intermediate states of each spin were included in the sum over intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- Eight sd-shell emitters were examined: 22Mg, 26Al, 26Si, 30S, 34Cl, 34Ar, 38K, and 38Ca
- The constant K/(ℏc)^6 = (8120.2716 ± 0.012) × 10^(-10) GeV^(-4) sec appears in the Ft relation
- 14 superallowed transitions have measured ft values known to 0.1% precision or better (from the most recent survey cited)
- For 30S, the correction δ_RO is dominated by the 2s1/2 state, where the centrifugal barrier is absent, making the radial wave function very sensitive to mean-field details
- The SLy5 Skyrme interaction gives a considerably smaller δ_RO value for 30S compared to SGII and SkM*

## Context

This work extends the shell-model approach of Ormand and Brown (1985, 1989) and Towner and Hardy (2002) for calculating isospin-symmetry breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta-decay. The authors re-examine eight sd-shell emitters using an elaborated adjustment procedure that removes sensitivity to specific potential parametrizations. The results enable improved tests of the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis and more precise determinations of the V_ud element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa quark-mixing matrix, though the authors note that work including orbitals outside the valence space (as in Towner and Hardy 2008) is in progress.
