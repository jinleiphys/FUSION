# $η$ meson in strange magnetized matter

> The in-medium properties of $η$ mesons are studied in hot and dense isospin asymmetric strange magnetized matter using the chiral SU(3) hadronic mean-field model. The scalar and vector density of baryons are expressed in terms of thermal distribution functions at finite temperature and magnetic field and have dependence on the scalar fields $σ$, $ζ$, and $δ$ through the effective mass of baryons and the dependence on the vector fields $ω, ρ$, and $ϕ$ through the effective chemical potential. The properties of $η$ mesons get modified in a hot and dense magnetized environment through the medium-modified nucleons and hyperons. The negative mass shift obtained gives rise to optical potential, which is attractive in the medium and suggests a possibility of $η$-mesic bound states formation. The addition of hyperons in the medium enhances the attractive interactions and causes an increased negative mass shift for $η$ mesons.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the in-medium mass of the η meson decreases in hot, dense, isospin-asymmetric strange magnetized matter, producing an attractive optical potential that suggests the possibility of η-mesic bound states; however, this result depends on the inclusion of hyperons (strange baryons) in the medium, which enhances the negative mass shift compared to calculations with nucleons alone.

## Method

The authors employ the chiral SU(3) hadronic mean-field model, a non-perturbative relativistic mean-field framework based on nonlinear realization of chiral SU(3)L × SU(3)R symmetry, to compute the in-medium properties of the η meson. The scalar and vector densities of all baryons in the octet (p, n, Λ, Σ+, Σ0, Σ−, Ξ−, Ξ0) are expressed using thermal distribution functions at finite temperature and magnetic field, with the scalar fields σ, ζ, δ modifying the effective baryon masses and the vector fields ω, ρ, ϕ modifying the effective chemical potentials. The η meson properties are then obtained from the medium-modified nucleon and hyperon interactions through the η-baryon Lagrangian, yielding a dispersion relation from which the mass shift and optical potential are extracted.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density (ρ₀), the optical potential for the η meson in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter without strangeness is reported from prior literature as ranging from -20 MeV (Waas) to -83 ± 5 MeV (Zhong, 2006), with the chiral unitary approach giving -54 MeV (Inoue, 2002) and the QMC model giving +60 MeV (Tsushima, 1998).
- The magnetic field strengths considered are eB = 0 and eB = 3 mπ², where mπ is the pion mass.
- The effective nucleon mass at saturation density is set to approximately 0.65 mN, where mN is the vacuum nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of η meson properties in non-magnetized nuclear matter (Rajesh, 2020) and magnetized nuclear matter (Kumar, 2022) by including the full baryon octet (hyperons) to account for strangeness, which is relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments and magnetar physics. The chiral SU(3) model has previously been applied to finite nuclei (Wang, 2002), hypernuclei (Tsub, 2010), strange hadronic matter (Wang, 2001), and neutron stars (Kumari, 2022), and the present results enable future investigations of η-mesic bound states and photo-production of η mesons in strange magnetized environments.
