# From lattice to observables: Real and virtual experiments for exploring hot and dense QCD

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the lattice QCD Monte-Carlo simulations are important ``experimental'' tools for investigating the properties of the medium described by QCD under extreme conditions. After briefly examining their characteristics, I pick up the study of the critical points in QCD as an example of the research subjects that these two tools play complementary roles and discuss recent topics that have been achieved with the use of each tool.

## Key claim

Relativistic heavy-ion collisions (HIC) and lattice QCD Monte-Carlo simulations (LAT) are complementary "experimental" tools for investigating QCD under extreme conditions, but the paper focuses specifically on how their combined use is effective for studying QCD critical points, noting that the conventional LAT algorithm is applicable only at zero baryon chemical potential due to the sign problem.

## Method

The paper reviews the characteristics of HIC and LAT, then examines two specific applications: constructing the ratio of second-order cumulants of baryon number and electric charge from STAR Collaboration data at 200 GeV and comparing with lattice results, and performing a finite-size scaling analysis of the Binder cumulant around the critical point in the heavy-quark region using lattice simulations with spatial volumes up to aspect ratio LT=12 and lattice spacing a=1/(4T).

## Key numbers

- The phase transition temperature for QCD is expected when T exceeds approximately 150 MeV (about 1.5 x 10^12 K) or baryon-number density exceeds about 10^15 g/cm^3
- The ratio of second-order cumulants of baryon number and electric charge from STAR data at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV yields an extracted temperature T of 134-138 MeV
- For the Z_2 universality class to which QCD critical points belong, the Binder cumulant value b_4 is approximately 1.604 and the critical exponent nu is approximately 0.63
- The lattice fit for large spatial volumes (LT greater than or equal to 9) gives b_4 = 1.630(24) and nu = 0.614(48), consistent with Z_2 universality within 1 sigma

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to locate QCD critical points, building on prior studies of the phase diagram on the quark-mass plane by Gavin (1993), Ejiri (2019), Kuramashi (2020), and Kiyohara (2021). The paper enables direct comparison between lattice QCD results and heavy-ion collision data for conserved-charge cumulants, and demonstrates that large spatial volumes are necessary for reliable finite-size scaling analysis of the Binder cumulant, which will inform future lattice studies and beam-energy scan experiments at RHIC, NA61/SHINE, HADES, FAIR, and J-PARC-HI.
