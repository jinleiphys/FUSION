# Late-Time Relaxation from Landau Singularities

> Nonlinear hydrodynamic interactions can change the relaxation of fluctuations from exponential to power-law decay at late times. Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory provides a standard framework for describing such fluctuation effects, where the nonlinear late-time behavior is encoded in loop corrections. Extracting this behavior requires identifying the singularities of loop integrals, whose structure becomes increasingly intricate beyond simple models. We apply Landau singularity analysis to two-point functions in effective field theories and determine the singularities induced by nonlinear interactions without performing the loop integrations explicitly. From these frequency-space singularities, we extract nonlinear relaxation modes that control the late-time behavior. When gapless modes are present, these modes produce power-law decay at late times. Our results give a systematic singularity-based description of nonlinear late-time relaxation in a broad class of macroscopic effective theories.

# Late-Time Relaxation from Landau Singularities

## Key claim

Nonlinear interactions in Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory generate infinitely many new decay modes that can be identified via Landau singularity analysis without performing explicit loop integrations; when gapless modes are present, these nonlinear modes produce power-law decay at late times, but if all basic modes are gapped (nonzero decay rate at zero momentum), the nonlinear modes decay faster and the late-time behavior remains exponential.

## Method

The authors apply Landau singularity analysis to loop corrections of two-point correlation functions in Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory (SK-EFT). They decompose free propagators into sums over simple poles, substitute these into the loop integral for the self-energy, and solve the Landau equations (algebraic conditions on Feynman parameters and internal momenta) to determine the locations and types of singularities without performing the loop integrations. The singular behavior near each Landau singularity is extracted, and an inverse Fourier transform yields the late-time behavior of the two-point function in the time domain.

## Key numbers

- Leading singularities of banana diagrams take the form $\omega_{(s)} = -i\gamma_{(s)} - c|\mathbf{k}| - i\Gamma_{(s)}|\mathbf{k}|^2$, where $\gamma_{(s)} \equiv \sum_{e=1}^{E} \gamma_{n_e}$ and $\Gamma_{(s)}^{-1} \equiv \sum_{e=1}^{E} \Gamma_{n_e}^{-1}$ for a diagram with $E$ internal edges.
- The singular part of the loop integral near a Landau singularity scales as $(\delta\omega)^{\sigma/2} \ln \delta\omega$ when $\sigma = 0,2,4,\dots$ and as $(\delta\omega)^{\sigma/2}$ otherwise, where $\sigma \equiv Ld - V + \kappa$ with $L$ loops, $V$ vertices, $\kappa$ contracted edges, and $d$ spatial dimensions.
- In the long-wavelength limit $\mathbf{k} \to \mathbf{0}$, the late-time behavior of the two-point function is $\lim_{\mathbf{k}\to\mathbf{0}} G(t,\mathbf{k}) \approx \text{const} + D(\mathbf{0})\, t^{-L_{\text{min}} d/2}$, where $L_{\text{min}}$ is the minimum number of loops for a nonvanishing banana diagram.
- For cubic interactions $\varphi_a \varphi_r^2$, $L_{\text{min}} = 1$, giving power-law decay $t^{-d/2}$.

## Context

This work extends previous explicit loop calculations in nonlinear diffusion models at one-loop (Chen-Lin 2018, Abbasi 2022) and two-loop (Jain 2020) order, as well as arbitrary-loop banana diagram analysis (Grozdanov 2024), by providing a general singularity-based method that bypasses explicit integration. It also generalizes the long-time tail analysis of Arnold (1997) and Kovtun (2003) for charge current correlators, which relied on factorizing the nonlinear current into products of Gaussian fields. The results enable systematic characterization of late-time relaxation in any SK-EFT with simple-pole propagators, including higher-loop diagrams and non-Gaussian noise effects, without requiring explicit construction of nonlinear operator terms.
