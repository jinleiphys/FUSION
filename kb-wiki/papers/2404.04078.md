# Strangeness $+1$ light multiquark baryons

> In view of the renewing experimental interest for searching strangeness $+1$ baryons at J-PARC, we study the existence of light baryon resonances with strangeness +1 generated in the $K$-$(N^*/Δ^*)$ system, where $N^*$ represents either $N^*(1535)$/$N^*(1650)$/$N^*(1700)$, and $Δ^*$ corresponds to $Δ(1620)$. The description of the properties of the aforementioned states requires considering the dynamics involved in the coupled pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon systems with strangeness $S=0$ in the s-wave. For the purpose of our current study, we consider the pseudoscalar-baryon (PB) and vector-baryon channels (VB) to which the mentioned $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ resonances couple and solve the Faddeev equations for the coupled channel system $K$-$\text{PB}$, $K$-$\text{VB}$, with all interactions being in the s-wave. Despite some strong attraction present in two of the subsystems, we do not find clear evidence supporting the formation of strangeness +1 states, with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$, in the energy region $2000-2200$ MeV. However, the case of spin-parity $J^P=3/2^+$ seems more promising, showing the formation of a resonance with a mass around 2167 MeV, with a width of 90-100 MeV. We suggest that a signal of such a state could be found in processes with final states like $KN$, $K^*(892) N$.

## Key claim
The authors find no clear evidence for strangeness +1 baryon resonances with spin-parity \(J^P = 1/2^+\) in the 2000–2200 MeV region, but a state with \(J^P = 3/2^+\) appears promising, with a mass around 2167 MeV and width 90–100 MeV, though the result is conditional on the specific coupled-channel Faddeev treatment and the modeling of the two-body subsystems.

## Method
The authors solve Faddeev equations for the coupled \(K\)-pseudoscalar-baryon and \(K\)-vector-baryon systems in s-wave, using two approaches: (I) a fixed-center approximation treating the \(\rho N\) system as a cluster (\(N^*(1650)\) or \(\Delta(1620)\)) with the kaon rescattering, and (II) a full three-body coupled-channel formalism including channels such as \(K\eta N\), \(KK\Sigma\), \(KK\Lambda\), \(K\rho N\), \(K\omega N\), and \(K\phi N\) for spin 1/2, and the vector-baryon channels for spin 3/2. The two-body \(t\)-matrices for the subsystems are obtained from Bethe-Salpeter equations using effective Lagrangians, generating resonances like \(N^*(1535)\), \(N^*(1650)\), \(N^*(1700)\), \(\Delta(1620)\), and \(K_1(1270)\).

## Key numbers
- For spin-parity \(1/2^+\) in isospin 0: a peak at \(2223 \pm 11\) MeV with width \(122 \pm 7\) MeV (from Method I, \(K\rho N\) with \(\rho N\) clustering as \(N^*(1650)\)).
- For spin-parity \(1/2^+\) in isospin 1: a peak at \(2215 \pm 10\) MeV with width \(58 \pm 17\) MeV (from Method I, coupled \(KN^*(1650)\) and \(K\Delta(1620)\) channels).
- For spin-parity \(3/2^+\): a resonance with mass around 2167 MeV and width 90–100 MeV (from Method II, the more complete coupled-channel treatment).
- The cut-off \(\Lambda\) in the form factor is varied between 650 and 750 MeV to estimate uncertainties; the \(\rho\) width is taken as 150 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of strangeness +1 baryons, which were motivated by the controversial \(\Theta^+(1540)\) pentaquark (LEPS Collaboration, 2003) and subsequent searches at J-PARC (Muramatsu, 2021; Ahn, 2023). Earlier three-body calculations in the \(\pi KN\) system (Khemchandani, 2009) found no state near 1540 MeV but a broad bump around 1700 MeV, while studies of the \(K\rho N\) system are prompted by old data on \(KN \to K^*N\) cross sections (Hirata, 1971) showing peaks near 2100 MeV. The present results suggest that a \(3/2^+\) resonance near 2167 MeV could be searched for in final states like \(KN\) or \(K^*(892)N\) at J-PARC, and the formalism enables future studies of other three-body systems with strangeness +1.
