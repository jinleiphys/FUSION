# The boson number hypothesis and the boson number odd-even effect in $^{196-204}$Hg

> In the SU3-IBM the oblate shape is described by the \textrm{SU(3)} third-order Casimir operator in the large-$N$ limit. However for finite $N$, this interaction can produce a boson number odd-even effect. In this Letter, we find that, the unique odd-even effect really exists in the nuclei $^{196-204}$Hg. This finding implies that realistic low-lying excitations are sensitive to certain boson number $N$. The boson number hypothesis is verified for the first time since the advent of the interacting boson model. This also proves the accuracy and validity of the SU3-IBM directly. The SU(3) symmetry and the higher-order interactions are both indispensable for understanding the nuclear quadrupole deformations.

## Key claim

The SU3-IBM predicts a boson number odd-even effect for the $0_{2}^{+}$ and $2_{2}^{+}$ states in oblate nuclei when the boson number changes gradually, and this effect is found to really exist in the mercury isotopes $^{196-204}$Hg, but only for finite boson number $N$; in the large-$N$ limit the effect disappears.

## Method

The authors performed a quantitative fit of the low-lying energy spectra and B(E2) transition strengths in $^{196-204}$Hg using the SU3-IBM Hamiltonian, which includes the SU(3) second-order Casimir operator, the SU(3) third-order Casimir operator, the square of the second-order Casimir operator, and two additional higher-order terms derived from the SU(3) mapping of the rigid triaxial rotor. The fitting parameters $\eta=0.4$, $\alpha=1.65$, $\beta=0.05$, $\gamma=-1.9$, and $\delta=-12.0$ were fixed across all isotopes, with only the boson number $N$ (ranging from 2 to 6) and the global energy scale parameter $c$ varied per nucleus.

## Key numbers

* The boson numbers for $^{196-204}$Hg are 6, 5, 4, 3, 2 respectively.
* The SU(3) representations of the ground state for the third-order Casimir operator are (0,2), (2,2), (0,4), (2,4), (0,6) for $N=2,3,4,5,6$.
* The global energy scale parameters $c$ are 733.5 keV, 743.1 keV, 602.0 keV, 548.6 keV, 613.2 keV for $^{196-204}$Hg respectively.
* Experimental B(E2) value for $2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+$ in $^{196}$Hg is 33.3(12) W.u.; in $^{198}$Hg is 28.8(4) W.u.; in $^{200}$Hg is 24.56(22) W.u.; in $^{202}$Hg is 17.34(14) W.u.; in $^{204}$Hg is 11.96(9) W.u.
* The $0_{2}^{+}$ state in $^{200}$Hg is much lower in energy than the neighboring isotopes, matching the predicted odd-even pattern.

## Context

This work extends the SU3-IBM framework previously applied to the Hf-Hg region for prolate-oblate shape phase transitions (Wang 2023) and to $^{108-116}$Cd for quadrupole moment trends (Wang 2024). It directly contradicts the traditional description of oblate shapes using the $\overline{\text{SU(3)}}$ symmetry in the IBM (Smirnov 1998) and the O(6) symmetry description of $\gamma$-softness (Iachello 1978, Casten 1978). The odd-even effect was previously noticed as an anomalous phenomenon by Bernards (2013) but not explained; prior explanations based on O(6)-U(5) evolution (Garcia-Ramos and Heyde 2014, Utsuno 2024) could not be generalized to $^{202,204}$Hg. This result enables future tests of the SU3-IBM in other isotopic chains and motivates extensions including proton-neutron distinction, $g$ bosons, and single-particle excitations.
