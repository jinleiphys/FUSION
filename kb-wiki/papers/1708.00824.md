# Intrinsic properties of high-spin band structures in triaxial nuclei

> The band structures of $^{68,70}$Ge, $^{128,130,132,134}$Ce and $^{132,134,136,138}$Nd are investigated using the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. These nuclei depict forking of the ground-state band into several s-bands and in some cases, both the lowest two observed s-bands depict neutron or proton character. It was discussed in our earlier work that this anamoluos behaviour can be explained by considering $γ$-bands based on two-quasiparticle configurations. As the parent band and the $γ$-band built on it have the same intrinsic structure, g-factors of the two bands are expected to be similar. In the present work, we have undertaken a detailed investigation of g-factors for the excited band structures of the studied nuclei and the available data for a few high-spin states are shown to be in fair agreement with the predicted values.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the anomalous observation of two s-bands with similar (negative) g-factors in certain triaxial nuclei can be explained by identifying the second s-band as a gamma-band built on the parent two-neutron aligned state, rather than as a separate proton-aligned band; the authors qualify that the available experimental g-factor data for a few high-spin states show only "fair agreement" with the TPSM predicted values.

## Method

The authors employed the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, constructing basis states from the triaxial Nilsson potential with deformation parameters from Table 1, then performing three-dimensional angular-momentum projection and diagonalizing a Hamiltonian containing pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. They calculated g-factors for the excited band structures of 10 nuclei (68,70Ge, 128,130,132,134Ce, and 132,134,136,138Nd) using the projected wavefunctions, with standard gyromagnetic ratios (g_l^pi=1, g_l^nu=0, g_s^pi=5.586, g_s^nu=-3.826) and an attenuation factor of 0.75 for spin components.

## Key numbers

- For 68Ge band B1 at I=8, the measured g-factor is about 0.1, while TPSM predicts g = -0.26, both indicating neutron character.
- For 68Ge band B2 at I=8, both measured and calculated g-factors are negative and have similar values (exact number not given in text).
- The monopole pairing strength parameters used are G1 = 20.82 and G2 = 13.58.
- Deformation parameters include: 68Ge (epsilon=0.220, epsilon'=0.160, gamma=36 degrees); 70Ge (epsilon=0.235, epsilon'=0.145, gamma=31 degrees); 128Ce (epsilon=0.250, epsilon'=0.120, gamma=26 degrees); 132Nd (epsilon=0.267, epsilon'=0.120, gamma=24 degrees).
- For 70Ge, both band B1 and B2 cross the ground-state band at hbar omega = 0.5 MeV, while cranked shell model predicts neutron crossing at this frequency.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier TPSM studies (Jehangir et al., 2009; Kumar et al., 2014) that explained band forking in Ge, Ce, and Nd isotopes by considering gamma-bands built on two-quasiparticle configurations, but which previously only compared energies. The present g-factor calculations provide a more stringent test of this interpretation, addressing a puzzle where earlier particle-rotor model work (IBM) invoked higher multipole interactions to explain similar g-factors for the two lowest s-bands in 68Ge. The results enable future comparisons with experimental g-factor measurements for the Ce and Nd isotopes studied, where such data are currently lacking.
