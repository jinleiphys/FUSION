# The role of $ν$-induced reactions on lead and iron in neutrino detectors

> We have calculated cross sections and branching ratios for neutrino induced reactions on ^{208}Pb and ^{56}Fe for various supernova and accelerator-relevant neutrino spectra. This was motivated by the facts that lead and iron will be used on one hand as target materials in future neutrino detectors, on the other hand have been and are still used as shielding materials in accelerator-based experiments. In particular we study the inclusive ^{56}$Fe(ν_e,e^-)$^{56}Co and ^{208}$Pb(ν_e,e^-)$^{208}Bi cross sections and calculate the neutron energy spectra following the decay of the daughter nuclei. These reactions give a potential background signal in the KARMEN and LSND experiment and are discussed as a detection scheme for supernova neutrinos in the proposed OMNIS and LAND detectors. We also study the neutron-emission following the neutrino-induced neutral-current excitation of ^{56}Fe and ^{208}Pb.

# The role of ν-induced reactions on lead and iron in neutrino detectors

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the total charged-current cross section on 208Pb for supernova νe neutrinos is significantly smaller (by about 55% for high-energy spectra) than earlier estimates by Fuller (1999), while neutral-current cross sections on 208Pb are smaller by factors of 2–3 across all energies studied; these discrepancies arise because the present work uses a more complete treatment of forbidden transitions via the random phase approximation rather than the Goldhaber-Teller model.

## Method

The authors compute neutrino-induced excitation spectra in 56Fe and 208Pb using a hybrid model: for 56Fe, the Gamow-Teller (1+) response is taken from interacting shell-model calculations in the complete pf shell, scaled by a universal quenching factor (0.74)² and corrected for finite momentum transfer using RPA ratios; for 208Pb, all multipole responses up to λ ≤ 9 are calculated within the continuum random phase approximation using a Woods-Saxon single-particle basis and a Landau-Migdal residual interaction, with the 1+ strength renormalized by a quenching factor (0.7)². The resulting excitation spectra are then folded with neutrino energy distributions (Michel spectra for decay-at-rest neutrinos, Fermi-Dirac spectra for supernova neutrinos), and the decay of the daughter nuclei into particle-unbound channels (neutron, proton, α, γ) is modeled using the statistical model code SMOKER.

## Key numbers

- Total 56Fe(νe,e⁻)56Co cross section for decay-at-rest neutrinos: σ = 2.4 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² (experimental value from KARMEN: (2.56 ± 1.08 ± 0.43) × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²)
- Total 208Pb(νe,e⁻)208Bi cross section for decay-at-rest neutrinos: 3.62 × 10⁻³⁹ cm², with about 91% going to the (νe,e⁻n) channel
- Muon capture rates: 4.46 × 10⁶ s⁻¹ for 56Fe (experiment: (4.4 ± 0.1) × 10⁶ s⁻¹) and 16.1 × 10⁶ s⁻¹ for 208Pb (experiment: (13.5 ± 0.2) × 10⁶ s⁻¹)
- Total photoabsorption cross section on 208Pb: 3.0 MeV·b (classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule value: 2.98 MeV·b)
- Charged-current 208Pb(νe,e⁻) cross section for supernova νe with (T,α)=(3,3) spectrum: 1.6 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²; for (T,α)=(8,0) spectrum: 25 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² (Fuller (1999) estimate: 58 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²)
- Neutral-current cross section on 208Pb for νx with (T=8 MeV, α=0): 1.4 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² per neutrino family
- Neutral-current scaling factor c(T=8 MeV, α=0): 0.77 for 56Fe (open shell) and 0.67 for 208Pb (closed shell)
- Estimated 208Pb(νe,e⁻) events in LSND lead shielding (20 m³, ρ=11.3 g/cm³) over 3 years: 200,000 events, with about 180,000 producing a neutron

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid-model calculation for 56Fe (Kolbe, 1999) to include 208Pb and both charged- and neutral-current reactions for accelerator and supernova neutrino spectra. The results contradict the earlier empirical estimates by Fuller (1999) for 208Pb, which placed the Gamow-Teller strength in a single resonance and used the Goldhaber-Teller model for forbidden transitions; the present RPA calculation finds low-lying GT⁻ strength at about 7.5 MeV in 208Bi that was not considered by Fuller (1999). The calculated cross sections and neutron energy spectra are intended to guide the design of supernova neutrino detectors such as OMNIS and LAND, and to improve background simulations for the KARMEN and LSND experiments. The authors note that the isotope effect on neutral-current cross sections is small (less than 16% deviation from A-scaling for iron isotopes, following To (2000)), while charged-current cross sections scale with (N-Z) via the Fermi and Ikeda sumrules.
