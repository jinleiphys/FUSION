# Hard Thermal Loop -- theory and applications

> In this review, we present the key aspects of modern thermal perturbation theory based on the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, including its theoretical foundations and applications within quantum electrodynamics (QED) and quantum chromodynamics (QCD) plasmas. To maintain conciseness, we focus on scenarios in thermal equilibrium, examining a variety of physical quantities and settings. Specifically, we explore both bulk thermodynamic properties and real-time observables in high-temperature domains relevant to heavy-ion physics.

## Key claim

This review paper systematically presents the theoretical foundations and applications of the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation in modern thermal perturbation theory, focusing exclusively on systems in thermal equilibrium within quantum electrodynamics (QED) and quantum chromodynamics (QCD) plasmas at high temperatures relevant to heavy-ion physics.

## Method

The authors compile and synthesize the key developments of HTL resummation techniques, covering finite temperature field theory formalism, effective field theory at finite temperature, and applications to scalar theory, QED, and QCD. They examine both bulk thermodynamic properties and real-time observables, including electromagnetic particle production rates, mesonic correlation functions, QCD thermodynamics, damping rates, and energy loss, using the HTL approximation to consistently handle soft momentum scales of order gT.

## Key numbers

- The HTL resummed quark propagator yields effective quark masses that are chiral symmetric despite the appearance of an effective mass, as established by Weldon (1982) and Klimov (1982)
- The free meson spectral function for pseudoscalar channel at zero momentum is given by sigma_ps^f(omega, p=0) = (N_c/(4 pi^2)) Theta(omega-2m) omega^2 tanh(omega/(4T)) sqrt(1-(2m/omega)^2) (a_M + (2m/omega)^2 b_M) with (a_M,b_M) = (1,0) for pseudoscalar
- The dimensionless pseudoscalar correlation function for massless free quarks is G_tilde_ps(tilde_tau, p=0) = 2 pi N_c (1-2 tilde_tau) (1+cos^2(2 pi tilde_tau))/sin^3(2 pi tilde_tau)
- The pseudoscalar spectral function in 1-loop HTL approximation decomposes into pole-pole (PP), pole-cut (PC), and cut-cut (CC) contributions, with explicit expressions involving the quark dispersion relations omega_+(k) and omega_-(k) and the thermal quark mass m_th^q

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on HTL resummation by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and its applications to QCD thermodynamics and real-time observables. It builds upon prior calculations of mesonic correlation functions and spectral functions by Karsch et al. (2000), Hashimoto et al. (1992), Boyd et al. (1994), and Shuryak (1993), and connects to lattice QCD studies of temporal correlation functions near the transition temperature T_c. The work enables systematic perturbative calculations of photon and dilepton production rates, damping rates, and energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma, complementing lattice calculations that are limited in extracting spectral functions from Euclidean correlators.
