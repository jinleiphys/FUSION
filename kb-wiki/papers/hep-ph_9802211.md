# Vector mesons in medium

> Based on an effective Lagrangian which combines chiral SU(3) dynamics with vector meson dominance, we have developed a model for the forward vector meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes. We use this as an input to calculate the low energy part of the current-current correlation function in nuclear matter. For the isovector channel we find a significant enhancement of the in-medium spectral density below the $ρ$ resonance while the $ρ$ meson mass itself changes only slightly. The situation is different in the isoscalar channel, where the mass and peak position of the $ω$ meson move downward while its width increases moderately. For the $ϕ$ meson we find almost no mass shift, but the width of the peak increases significantly. We use these spectra as ``left hand side'' of in medium QCD sum rules and observe a remarkable degree of consistency with the operator product expansion ``right hand''side in all three channels. We point out, however, that these results cannot simply be interpreted, as commonly done, in terms of a universal rescaling of vector meson masses in matter. We also compare the resulting thermal dilepton rates of our model with CERES data. We find satisfactory agreement but we point out that the dilepton rates from completely uncorrelated quark-antiquark pairs also fit the data quite well. Considering the strongly attractive potential for the $ω$ together with its comparably small width, we discuss the possible formation of bound $ω$ states in nuclei. Using a Green's function approach we investigate the possibility of detecting these bound states in the $A(d, ^3He) $ pick-up reaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in-medium modifications of vector meson spectral functions are channel-dependent and cannot be interpreted as a universal rescaling of vector meson masses, as commonly done in the Brown-Rho scaling scenario. The authors emphasize that while their hadronic model agrees with CERES dilepton data, dilepton rates from completely uncorrelated quark-antiquark pairs also fit the data quite well, so the present data cannot distinguish between these scenarios.

## Method

The authors construct forward vector meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes using an effective Lagrangian that combines chiral SU(3) dynamics with vector meson dominance, including the complete baryon octet and decuplet. They apply the low density theorem to compute the in-medium current-current correlation function to leading order in nuclear density, then use these spectral functions as the "left hand side" of in-medium QCD sum rules to compare with the operator product expansion "right hand side." For the p-wave interaction of the rho meson, they incorporate an isobar model including the N*(1720) and Delta*(1905) resonances following Friman and Pirner (1994).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- For the rho meson at normal nuclear density, a simple pole ansatz leads to a reduction of m_rho by more than 10 percent, while the full model gives only a small change
- Initial fireball temperature for CERES comparison: T^i = 170 MeV; final temperature: T^infinity = 110 MeV; thermal time constant tau_1 = 8 fm
- Initial density for CERES comparison: rho_i = 2.5 rho_0; density time constant tau_2 = 5 fm
- OPE coefficients used for rho and omega: c_2^{rho,omega} = 0.04 + 0.018(rho/rho_0) GeV^4; c_3^{rho,omega} = -0.07 + 0.036(rho/rho_0) GeV^6
- OPE coefficients for phi: c_1^phi = -0.07 GeV^2; c_2^phi = -0.1 + 0.01(rho/rho_0) GeV^4; c_3^phi = -0.07 + 0.006(rho/rho_0) GeV^6
- Perturbative continuum threshold s_0 lies at about 1.2 GeV^2
- Borel mass parameter should be chosen in the range M >= 1 GeV for OPE convergence

## Context

This work extends hadronic calculations by Rapp et al. (1997) and Chanfray et al. (1996) that suggested only marginal changes of the in-medium rho meson mass but a strongly increased decay width, in contrast to the Brown-Rho scaling scenario (1991) and bag model considerations that predicted strong mass reduction. The authors show that their hadronic model is consistent with QCD sum rule analysis using the full spectrum rather than a delta function caricature as used by Hatsuda et al. (1993). The paper enables future experimental searches for bound omega meson states in nuclei via the A(d,^3He) pick-up reaction, which could provide cleaner signals than heavy-ion dilepton measurements.
