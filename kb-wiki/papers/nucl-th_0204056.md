# Spin-polarized states of nuclear matter

> The equations of state of spin-polarized nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are studied in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory including a three-body force. The energy per nucleon $E_A(δ)$ calculated in the full range of spin polarization $δ = \frac{ρ_{\uparrow}-ρ_{\downarrow}}ρ$ for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter fulfills a parabolic law. In both cases the spin-symmetry energy is calculated as a function of the baryonic density along with the related quantities such as the magnetic susceptibility and the Landau parameter $G_0$. The main effect of the three-body force is to strongly reduce the degenerate Fermi gas magnetic susceptibility even more than the value with only two body force. The EOS is monotonically increasing with the density for all spin-aligned configurations studied here so that no any signature is found for a spontaneous transition to a ferromagnetic state.

# Spin-polarized states of nuclear matter

## Key claim

The energy per nucleon of spin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter follows a parabolic law in the spin polarization parameter over the full range of polarization, and the equation of state increases monotonically with density for all spin-aligned configurations studied, showing no evidence for a spontaneous ferromagnetic phase transition even when three-body forces are included.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) self-consistent calculations using the Argonne V18 two-body force supplemented by a microscopic three-body force transformed into an effective two-body interaction. The Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved for spin-polarized nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, with the continuous choice adopted for the auxiliary single-particle potential, and the spin-symmetry energy, magnetic susceptibility, and Landau parameter G0 were extracted from the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic susceptibility ratio χ/χF is reduced to approximately 0.3 at a density of ρ = 0.8 fm⁻³ when only the two-body force is included, with further reduction from the three-body force.
- The Landau parameter G0 remains positive and monotonically increasing with density up to the highest density calculated, never reaching the instability condition G0 < -1.
- The spin-symmetry energy Esym(ρ) increases monotonically with density, with the three-body force enhancing the slope for densities above the saturation point.

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic Brueckner calculations (Cugnon, 1976; Lejeune, 1986; Vida, 1990; Bombaci, 1995) that did not support a ferromagnetic transition, in contrast to relativistic mean-field approaches (Bernard, 1993; Maruyama, 1991) and Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme forces that predict such a transition. The inclusion of a three-body force, which incorporates important relativistic effects via virtual nucleon-antinucleon excitations (Lejeune, 1986) and improves the empirical saturation density (Zuo, 2000), strengthens the conclusion against ferromagnetism. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutrino propagation in neutron stars and supernovae, where the magnetic susceptibility quenching could significantly affect the neutrino mean free path.
