# Probing nucleon's spin structures with polarized Drell-Yan in the Fermilab SpinQuest experiment

> Although the proton was discovered about 100 years ago, its spin structure still remains a mystery. Recent studies suggest that the orbital angular momentum of sea quarks could significantly contribute to the proton's spin. The SeaQuest experiment, which recently completed data collection, probed the unpolarized light quark sea distributions of the proton using the Drell-Yan process. Its successor, the SpinQuest (E1039), will access the $\bar{u}$ and $\bar{d}$ Sivers functions using polarized NH$_3$ and ND$_3$ targets. A non-zero Sivers asymmetry, observed in SpinQuest, would be a strong indication of non-zero sea-quark orbital angular momentum. The SpinQuest experiment can also probe the sea quark's transversity distribution, which is relevant for the determination of proton's tensor charge. Recent study suggests that sea-quarks might contribute significantly to deuteron's tensor polarized structure functions. This can be further probed in SpinQuest using tensor polarized ND$_3$ target. The current status and future plan of the experiment are presented.

## Key claim
SpinQuest (E1039) will measure the sea-quark Sivers functions via polarized Drell-Yan with 120 GeV proton beam on transversely polarized NH$_3$ and ND$_3$ targets, where a non-zero Sivers asymmetry would indicate non-zero sea-quark orbital angular momentum; the abstract's claim that this would be a "strong indication" depends on the theoretical connection between the Sivers function and orbital angular momentum via helicity-flip amplitudes.

## Method
SpinQuest uses the upgraded SeaQuest dimuon spectrometer with 120 GeV proton beam incident on transversely polarized NH$_3$ or ND$_3$ targets, measuring the left-right azimuthal asymmetry $A_N$ of dimuons produced via the Drell-Yan process. The target system employs Dynamic Nuclear Polarization in a 5 T magnetic field at 1 K to achieve nucleon polarization, with the target moved 1.5 meters upstream from the SeaQuest configuration. New fiber scintillators in stations 1 and 2 improve position resolution and background rejection.

## Key numbers
- Expected average polarization of 80% for the NH$_3$ target
- Target system operates at 5 T magnetic field and 1 K temperature
- The HERMES $b_1$ data show a node at $x \sim 0.3$ in the $xb_1$ distribution
- SpinQuest can cover $x_{target}$ ($x_2$) down to 0.1 for measuring the tensor polarization function
- First physics data-taking expected to begin in September 2019

## Context
This work extends the unpolarized sea-quark measurements of the SeaQuest/E906 experiment (E906Prop) by adding polarized targets to access TMD distributions. It complements polarized SIDIS measurements from HERMES (HERMES 2005, 2009), COMPASS (COMPASS 2005, 2015, 2017), and JLab (JLab Hall A 2011), as well as polarized Drell-Yan measurements at COMPASS (COMPASS) and STAR (STAR 2016). The experiment enables direct measurement of sea-quark Sivers functions, which are difficult to isolate in SIDIS due to valence-quark dominance, and can test the process dependence of TMDs predicted by Brodsky (Brodsky).
