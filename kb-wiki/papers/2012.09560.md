# The nucleon-pair approximation for nuclei from spherical to deformed regions

> In this paper we model low-lying states of atomic nuclei in the nucleon-pair approximation of the shell model, using three approaches to select collective nucleon pairs: the generalized seniority scheme, the conjugate gradient method, and the Hartree-Fock approach. We find the collective pairs obtained from the generalized seniority scheme provides a good description for nearly spherical nuclei, and those from the conjugate gradient method or the Hartree-Fock approach work well for transitional and deformed nuclei. Our NPA calculations using collective pairs with angular momenta 0, 2, and 4 (denoted by $SDG$ pairs) reproduce the nuclear shape evolution in the $N=26$ isotones, $^{46}$Ca, $^{48}$Ti, $^{50}$Cr, and $^{52}$Fe, and yield good agreement with full configuration-interaction calculations of low-lying states in medium-heavy transitional and deformed nuclei: $^{44-48}$Ti, $^{48}$Cr, $^{50}$Cr, $^{52}$Fe, $^{60-64}$Zn, $^{64,66}$Ge, $^{84}$Mo, and $^{108-112}$Xe. Finally, using the $SDGI$-pair approximation we describe low-lying states of $^{112,114}$Ba, cases difficult to reach by conventional configuration-interaction methods.

## Key claim

The nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) using collective pairs selected by the conjugate gradient (CG) method or the Hartree-Fock (HF) approach can describe low-lying states of transitional and deformed nuclei, while the generalized seniority (GS) approach works only for nearly spherical nuclei; the CG method yields numerically optimal solutions but requires many iterations, while the HF approach is more practical for medium-heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors constructed NPA basis states from collective nucleon pairs with angular momenta 0, 2, and 4 (SDG pairs) or including also angular momentum 6 (SDGI pairs), with pair-structure coefficients determined by three methods: the generalized seniority (GS) approach minimizing the Hamiltonian in S-pair condensates and one-broken-pair states; the conjugate gradient (CG) method iteratively optimizing all pair coefficients by minimizing ground-state energy; and the Hartree-Fock (HF) approach extracting pairs from a deformed HF Slater determinant by projecting out pairs of good angular momentum. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian in the resulting truncated spaces and compared excitation energies and B(E2) transition strengths to full shell-model calculations and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For the schematic Hamiltonian in the pf shell with Np=Nn=6, the SDG-pair approximation with the CG approach reproduced full SM results for rotational cases (R4 approximately 3.33, R6 approximately 7 at x close to 0), while the GS approach performed poorly for small x/strong quadrupole-quadrupole interaction.
- For N=26 isotones: B(E2;2+ to 0+) values from the SDG_CG + SDG_GS approach were 0.75 W.u. for 46Ca (experiment 2.59(46) W.u.), 8.1 W.u. for 48Ti (experiment 14.7(4) W.u.), 15.7 W.u. for 50Cr (experiment 19.3(6) W.u.), and 12.5 W.u. for 52Fe (experiment 14.2(19) W.u.).
- For 48Cr, the HF calculation produced an axially symmetric deformed minimum with beta = 0.31; for 50Cr, beta = 0.22; for 52Fe, beta = 0.16.
- For 48Cr, B(E2;2+ to 0+) from SDG_HF was 19.6 W.u. (experiment 31(4) W.u., SM 20.6 W.u.); for 50Cr, B(E2;2+ to 0+) was 18.4 W.u. (experiment 19.3(6) W.u., SM 16.9 W.u.).
- For 84Mo in the pf5g9 shell, B(E2;2+ to 0+) from SDGI_HF was 58.2 W.u. (SM 54.9 W.u.); B(E2;10+ to 8+) was 89.5 W.u. (SM 89.1 W.u.).

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-pair approximation beyond its traditional application to nearly spherical nuclei (reviewed in NPA review by the authors) by introducing CG and HF methods to determine collective pairs for deformed systems. It builds on earlier findings that SDG pairs from HF Slater determinants describe rotational bands (Fu and Johnson, 2020) and contrasts with the pair condensate variational approach of other authors (2020) which optimizes a condensate without enforcing good angular momentum. The results enable NPA calculations for medium-heavy transitional and deformed nuclei that are difficult to reach with full configuration-interaction methods, such as 112,114Ba.
