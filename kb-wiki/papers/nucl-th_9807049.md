# Superallowed Fermi beta decay

> Superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ nuclear beta decay provides a direct measure of the weak vector coupling constant, $\GV$. We survey current world data on the nine accurately determined transitions of this type, which range from the decay of $^{10}$C to that of $^{54}$Co, and demonstrate that the results confirm conservation of the weak vector current (CVC) but differ at the 98% confidence level from the unitarity condition for the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix. We examine the reliability of the small calculated corrections that have been applied to the data, and assess the likelihood of even higher quality nuclear data becoming available to confirm or deny the discrepancy. Some of the required experiments depend upon the availability of intense radioactive beams. Others are possible today.

## Key claim

The world data on nine superallowed Fermi beta decays confirm the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis but differ at the 98% confidence level from the unitarity condition for the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix; however, the discrepancy is described as "provocative, though not yet definitive" and depends on the reliability of small calculated radiative and Coulomb corrections.

## Method

The authors surveyed world data on the nine most accurately determined superallowed \(0^+ \to 0^+\) beta decays, ranging from \(^{10}\)C to \(^{54}\)Co, incorporating a new \(^{10}\)C branching-ratio measurement and a more precise \(^{38m}\)K Q-value determination. They extracted corrected \(\mathcal{F}t\)-values using the formula \(\mathcal{F}t = ft (1 + \delta_R)(1 - \delta_C)\), where \(\delta_R\) is the nucleus-dependent radiative correction and \(\delta_C\) is the isospin-breaking correction, with \(\delta_C\) taken as the average of two independent calculations (Towner-Hardy-Harvey and Ormand-Brown). The average \(\mathcal{F}t\)-value was then used to determine \(V_{ud}\) and test CKM unitarity.

## Key numbers

- Average \(\mathcal{F}t\)-value for the nine transitions: \(3072.3 \pm 2.0\) s (including systematic uncertainty from \(\delta_C\) differences).
- CVC verification at the level of \(3 \times 10^{-4}\) fractional uncertainty on the average \(\mathcal{F}t\)-value, with \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.1\) for the fit.
- Extracted \(V_{ud} = 0.9740 \pm 0.0005\).
- CKM unitarity sum: \(|V_{ud}|^2 + |V_{us}|^2 + |V_{ub}|^2 = 0.9968 \pm 0.0014\), differing from unity at the 98% confidence level.
- Nucleus-independent radiative correction: \(\Delta_R^V = (2.40 \pm 0.08)\%\).
- Typical \(\delta_R\) for \(T_z = 0\) emitters is about 1.4%; typical \(\delta_C\) is about 0.4%.
- Calculated \(\delta_C\) for \(^{30}\)S decay is 1.2%, about a factor of two larger than any other currently known case; for \(^{22}\)Mg it is 0.35%.

## Context

This work extends the survey of Hardy (1990) and the ENAM95 conference update, incorporating new measurements for \(^{10}\)C and \(^{38m}\)K. The results confirm CVC but challenge CKM unitarity, a tension that has motivated further theoretical work on radiative corrections (Sirlin, 1967; Sirlin, 1987; Jaus and Rasche, 1987) and Coulomb corrections (Towner, Hardy, and Harvey, 1977; Ormand and Brown, 1995; Sagawa, van Giai, and Suzuki, 1996; Navratil, Barrett, and Ormand, 1997). The paper identifies specific experimental improvements needed (Q-values, half-lives, branching ratios) and points to future measurements with radioactive beams, particularly for \(T_z = -1\) emitters like \(^{30}\)S and \(^{22}\)Mg, to test the calculated Coulomb corrections.
