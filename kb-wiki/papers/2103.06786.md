# Ab initio computation of the longitudinal response function in $^{40}$Ca

> We present a consistent \emph{ab initio} computation of the longitudinal response function $R_L$ in $^{40}$Ca using the coupled-cluster and Lorentz integral transform methods starting from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions. We validate our approach by comparing our results for $R_L$ in $^4$He and the Coulomb sum rule in $^{40}$Ca against experimental data and other calculations. For $R_L$ in $^{40}$Ca we obtain a very good agreement with experiment in the quasi-elastic peak up to intermediate momentum transfers, and we find that final state interactions are essential for an accurate description of the data. This work presents a milestone towards \emph{ab initio} computations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant for experimental long-baseline neutrino programs.

# Ab initio computation of the longitudinal response function in $^{40}$Ca

## Key claim

The authors present the first consistent ab initio computation of the longitudinal response function $R_L$ for $^{40}$Ca using coupled-cluster and Lorentz integral transform methods, achieving very good agreement with experimental data in the quasi-elastic peak up to intermediate momentum transfers, but only when final state interactions are fully included; the agreement degrades at $q=400$ MeV/c, where the quasi-elastic peak is quenched and the tail enhanced relative to experiment, possibly due to relativistic boost effects or the limits of chiral effective field theory at the regulator cutoff of 450 MeV/c.

## Method

The authors combine the coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) method with the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) approach to compute the longitudinal response function $R_L(\omega,q)$ for $^{40}$Ca. They solve the bound-state-like equation $(H-E_0-\sigma)|\widetilde{\Psi}_{\sigma,q}^{\rho}\rangle = \rho(q)|\Psi_0\rangle$ using equation-of-motion coupled-cluster techniques with a source term, then invert the Lorentzian-kernel transform to obtain $R_L$. Two chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interactions are used: NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ (without explicit $\Delta$-isobars) and $\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$(450) (with explicit $\Delta$-isobars), both at next-to-next-to-leading order with a regulator cutoff of 450 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{40}$Ca: 300.1 MeV (NNLO$_{\rm sat}$) and 322.12 MeV ($\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$(450)) in CCSD
- Proton separation energies $\omega_{th}$: 6.32 MeV (NNLO$_{\rm sat}$) and 6.12 MeV ($\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$(450))
- Bound excited states in $^{40}$Ca: $J^\pi=3^-$ at 4.5 MeV (NNLO$_{\rm sat}$) and 3.8 MeV ($\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$(450)); $J^\pi=5^-$ at 4.7 MeV (NNLO$_{\rm sat}$) and 4.0 MeV ($\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$(450)); experimental values are 3.7 MeV ($3^-$) and 4.5 MeV ($5^-$)
- Convergence of LITs at $\sigma=20$ MeV in the quasi-elastic peak: 2% level for $q\le 350$ MeV/c and 4% for $q\ge 400$ MeV/c, estimated by varying $\hbar\Omega$ from 18 to 22 MeV
- Model space: 15 major oscillator shells ($e_{\rm max}=14$) with three-nucleon force cutoff $e_{3{\rm max}}\le 16$
- PWIA overestimates quasi-elastic peak size by up to 40% and misses the peak position by up to 20 MeV compared to LIT-CC results

## Context

This work extends the LIT-CC method, previously applied to low-energy dipole responses (Bacca et al., 2013, 2014) and the Coulomb sum rule for $^4$He and $^{16}$O (Sobczyk et al., 2020), to compute the full longitudinal response function for a medium-mass nucleus ($^{40}$Ca). It contrasts with Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations for $^4$He and $^{12}$C (Lovato et al., 2014, 2015) and self-consistent Green's function approaches for $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ar (Rocco et al., 2018; Barbieri et al., 2019), which use different treatments of final state interactions. The results enable future ab initio computations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for $^{40}$Ar relevant to the DUNE and T2HK experiments, and the authors note that new experimental data at $q=200$ MeV/c are being pursued at Mainz (Doria, ongoing).
