# Alpha-cluster structure in even-even nuclei around $^{94}$Mo

> $Background$: The $α$-cluster model was successful for describing spectroscopic properties of light nuclei near the shell closures. Evidences of the $α$-cluster structure in $^{94}$Mo were shown, motivating the search for the same structure in other intermediate mass nuclei. $Purpose$: The systematic analysis of the $α$-cluster structure in $^{94}$Mo and four even-even neighbouring nuclei. $Method$: The $α$-cluster model with the local potential approach. $Results$: A good general description of the experimental ground state bands is obtained with only one variable parameter. It is shown that the employed $α+\textrm{core}$ potential is weakly dependent on the angular momentum $L$ and such dependence may be described satisfactorily by a simple $L$-dependent factor for the five nuclei. The radial parameter of the $α+\textrm{core}$ potential reveals a linear trend in relation to the total nuclear radius and the sum of the $α$-cluster and core radii. The calculated $B(E2)$ transition rates reproduce correctly the order of magnitude of almost all experimental data without the use of effective charges. The rms intercluster separations and reduced $α$-widths obtained for the ground state bands point to a reduction of the $α$-cluster intensity with the increasing spin. Negative parity bands are calculated and compared to available experimental levels. The volume integral per nucleon pair and rms radii were calculated for the studied $α+\textrm{core}$ potentials, revealing a similarity with the real parts of the optical potentials used to describe the $α+ ^{90}$Zr and $α+ ^{92}$Mo elastic scattering at several energies. $Conclusions$: This work strongly indicates the presence of similar $α+\textrm{core}$ structures for the $N=52$ even-even nuclei of the Mo region.

## Key claim

The paper systematically analyzes alpha-cluster structure in five even-even N=52 isotones (⁹⁰Sr, ⁹²Zr, ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁶Ru, ⁹⁸Pd) using a local potential model with only one variable parameter per nucleus, finding that the alpha+core potential is weakly dependent on angular momentum L and that similar alpha+core structures exist across these nuclei, though the description of the 0⁺ bandhead and 2⁺ state spacing is rougher than for higher-spin states.

## Method

The authors employed the alpha-cluster model with a local phenomenological potential consisting of a Coulomb term and a nuclear Woods-Saxon plus Woods-Saxon cubed (W.S.+W.S.³) form. The radial parameter R was fitted separately for each nucleus to reproduce the experimental 4⁺ member of the ground state band, while the depth V₀ = 220 MeV, diffuseness a = 0.65 fm, and mixing parameter b = 0.3 were fixed. The Schrödinger radial equation was solved for the alpha+core reduced mass, with the global quantum number G = 2N + L set to G = 14 for ⁹⁰Sr and ⁹²Zr, and G = 16 for ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁶Ru, and ⁹⁸Pd based on the Wildermuth condition.

## Key numbers

- Radial parameter R values: 5.321 fm for ⁹⁰Sr (α+⁸⁶Kr), 5.295 fm for ⁹²Zr (α+⁸⁸Sr), 5.784 fm for ⁹⁴Mo (α+⁹⁰Zr), 5.808 fm for ⁹⁶Ru (α+⁹²Mo), 5.825 fm for ⁹⁸Pd (α+⁹⁴Ru)
- Fixed nuclear potential parameters: V₀ = 220 MeV, a = 0.65 fm, b = 0.3
- Global quantum numbers: G = 14 for ⁹⁰Sr and ⁹²Zr; G = 16 for ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁶Ru, and ⁹⁸Pd
- Linear fit for the five intermediate mass nuclei: R = 1.234 A_T^(1/3) fm, with correlation coefficient r ≈ 0.882
- Linear fit for the set {²⁰Ne, ⁴⁴Ti, ⁹⁴Mo, ²¹²Po}: R = 1.092(A_α^(1/3) + A_core^(1/3)) - 1.041 fm, with correlation coefficient r ≈ 0.993
- Effective potential barrier extension for resonant states nearest to threshold: 204 fm < Δr < 476 fm; for highest spin states: 31 fm < Δr < 114 fm
- Effective potential barrier height relative to resonant energies: 13 MeV < ΔE < 25 MeV for states nearest threshold; 27 MeV < ΔE < 31 MeV for highest spin states
- Depth V₀ variation: approximately ±1.5% from 220 MeV across all fitted states
- L-dependent depth polynomial coefficients: c₁ = -4.6875 × 10⁻³ MeV, c₂ = 0.05625 MeV, with minimum at (L=4, V₀=220 MeV) and maximum at (L=12, V₀=221.2 MeV)
- Calculated rms intercluster separations for ⁹⁴Mo ground state band: 5.109 fm (0⁺) decreasing to 4.765 fm (12⁺)
- Reduced alpha-widths for ⁹⁴Mo: 708.39 eV (0⁺) decreasing to 14.48 eV (12⁺)
- Dimensionless reduced alpha-widths for ⁹⁴Mo: 3261.8 × 10⁻⁶ (0⁺) decreasing to 66.7 × 10⁻⁶ (12⁺)

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster model studies of Buck, Merchant and Perez (1995) on ⁹⁴Mo and ²¹²Po, and the double folding potential analysis of Ohkubo (1995) for ⁹⁴Mo, to a systematic study of five N=52 isotones. The results confirm the linear trend of the radial parameter R with nuclear radius found by earlier authors and demonstrate that the W.S.+W.S.³ potential with fixed parameters gives better results for intermediate mass nuclei than simple Woods-Saxon potentials (which produce inverted spectra) or folding potentials (which produce excessively compressed bands). The work enables future experimental verification through alpha-transfer reactions on ⁹⁰Sr, ⁹⁶Ru, and ⁹⁸Pd, and provides a basis for extending the analysis to negative parity bands and to other mass regions.
