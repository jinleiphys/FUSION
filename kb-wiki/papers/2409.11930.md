# Optimization of Nuclear Mass Models Using Algorithms and Neural Networks

> Taking into account nucleon-nucleon gravitational interaction, higher-order terms of symmetry energy, pairing interaction, and neural network corrections, a new BW4 mass model has been developed, which more accurately reflects the contributions of various terms to the binding energy. A novel hybrid algorithm and neural network correction method has been implemented to optimize the discrepancy between theoretical and experimental results, significantly improving the model's binding energy predictions (reduced to around 350 keV). At the same time, the theoretical accuracy near magic nuclei has been marginally enhanced, effectively capturing the special interaction effects around magic nuclei and showing good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The new BW4 mass model, which incorporates nucleon-nucleon gravitational interaction, higher-order symmetry energy terms, pairing interaction, and neural network corrections, achieves binding energy predictions reduced to around 350 keV RMSD, though this accuracy is contingent on the specific combination of optimization algorithm and neural network type used, with the best performance requiring LSTM networks.

## Method

The authors extended the BW2 mass model by adding three physical terms: a higher-order symmetry energy term derived from the Fermi gas model, a gravitational interaction term between nucleons, and a pairing interaction term with a piecewise function depending on nucleon parity and asymmetry. They then optimized the coefficients of the liquid-drop part (BE_LDM) using BFGS and SLSQP algorithms and compared them to least squares fitting. Finally, they trained neural network models (KAN, GPR, and LSTM) on the residuals between experimental and theoretical binding energies to produce a correction term delta_NN, yielding the complete BW4 model.

## Key numbers

* The full dataset contained 3250 nuclei, split into 2275 training samples and 975 testing samples.
* For the BE_LDM component alone, BFGS achieved RMSD = 1.626 MeV and SLSQP achieved RMSD = 1.627 MeV.
* The best overall BW4 model performance on the full set was Least Squares + LSTM with RMSD = 0.221 MeV; SLSQP + LSTM gave RMSD = 0.236 MeV; BFGS + LSTM gave RMSD = 0.233 MeV.
* On the training set, Least Squares + LSTM achieved the lowest RMSD of 0.124 MeV.
* On the test set, BFGS + LSTM achieved RMSD = 0.352 MeV and SLSQP + LSTM achieved RMSD = 0.364 MeV.
* For the Ca isotope chain at N=20, the Least Squares + KAN correction improved the deviation from -1.579 MeV to 0.009 MeV; at N=28, Least Squares + GPR improved from 1.805 MeV to 0.003 MeV.
* For the Sn isotope chain at N=50, Least Squares + LSTM improved the deviation from 8.137 MeV to 0.018 MeV; at N=82, Least Squares + KAN improved from 9.091 MeV to 6.31e-4 MeV.
* For the Pb isotope chain at N=126, BFGS + GPR improved the deviation from 5.867 MeV to 4.85e-4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BW2 mass model developed by Kirson's team, which introduced six physical terms including exchange Coulomb and surface symmetry terms to improve upon the original Bethe-Weizsacker semi-empirical mass formula. The new BW4 model competes with macroscopic-microscopic approaches such as the Finite-Range Droplet Model (FRDM), the Koura-Tachibana-Uno-Yamada (KTUY) model, and the Weizsacker-Skyrme (WS) mass model, as well as microscopic models like Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory. The demonstrated accuracy around 350 keV enables more reliable predictions for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis calculations and provides a methodology for combining physical models with machine learning corrections.
