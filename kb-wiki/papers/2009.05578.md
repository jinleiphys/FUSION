# Signals of the QCD axion with mass of 17 MeV/c^2: nuclear transitions and light meson decays

> The QCD axion remains experimentally viable in the mass range of O(10 MeV) if (i) it couples predominantly to the first generation of SM fermions; (ii) it decays to $e^+ e^-$ with a short lifetime $τ_a\lesssim 10^{-13}\,$s; and (iii) it has suppressed isovector couplings, i.e., if it is piophobic. Remarkably, these are precisely the properties required to explain recently observed anomalies in nuclear de-excitations, to wit: the $e^+e^-$ emission spectra of isoscalar magnetic transitions of $^{8\!}$Be and $^{4\!}$He nuclei showed a "bump-like" feature peaked at $m_{e^+e^-}\sim 17$ MeV. In this article, we argue that on-shell emission of the QCD axion (with the aforementioned properties) provides an extremely well-motivated, compatible explanation for the observed excesses in these nuclear de-excitations. The absence of anomalous features in other measured transitions is also naturally explained: piophobic axion emission is strongly suppressed in isovector magnetic transitions, and forbidden in electric transitions. This QCD axion hypothesis is further corroborated by an independent observation: a $2-3\,σ$ deviation in the measurement of $Γ(π^0\to e^+e^-)$ from the Standard Model theoretical expectation. This article also includes detailed estimations of various axionic signatures in rare light meson decays, which take into account contributions from low-lying QCD resonance exchange, and, in the case of rare Kaon decays, the possible effective implementations of $ΔS=1$ octet enhancement in chiral perturbation theory. These inherent uncertainties of the effective description of the strong interactions at low energies result in large variations in the predictions for hadronic signals of the QCD axion; in spite of this, the estimated ranges for rare meson decay rates obtained here can be probed in the near future in $η/η^\prime$ and Kaon factories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a QCD axion with a mass of approximately 17 MeV/c², which is "piophobic" (has suppressed isovector couplings), couples predominantly to first-generation Standard Model fermions, and decays to e⁺e⁻ with a short lifetime, provides a well-motivated and compatible explanation for the observed bump-like excesses in the e⁺e⁻ emission spectra of isoscalar magnetic transitions of ⁸Be and ⁴He nuclei, as well as the 2–3σ deviation in the measurement of Γ(π⁰ → e⁺e⁻). The explanation is conditional on the axion having these specific properties, which are required to avoid existing experimental constraints.

## Method

The author uses chiral perturbation theory (χPT) and Resonance Chiral Theory to parametrize the axion's isoscalar and isovector mixing angles with neutral mesons (π⁰, η, η′), remaining agnostic about their magnitudes. The axion-nucleon couplings derived from this parametrization are then fitted to the reported excess rates in the ⁸Be and ⁴He nuclear de-excitation anomalies, and the same parametrization is used to calculate axionic signatures in rare light meson decays (η, η′, and Kaon decays), including contributions from low-lying QCD resonance exchange and octet enhancement in χPT for Kaon decays.

## Key numbers

*   Axion mass benchmark: mₐ = 16.7 MeV/c², with axion decay constant fₐ ≈ 1030 MeV for PQ charges qᵤ/2 = q_d = 1.
*   Axion lifetime: τₐ ≈ 4 × 10⁻¹⁵ s / (qₑ)², with the electron PQ charge qₑ constrained to 1/5 ≲ |qₑ| ≲ 2.
*   Relative branching ratio for ⁸Be*(18.15) → ⁸Be(0) + X → e⁺e⁻: Γ_X/Γ_γ ≈ (6 ± 1) × 10⁻⁶, with the radiative γ width Γ_γ(18.15) ≈ (1.9 ± 0.4) eV.
*   Upper bound for ⁸Be*(17.64) de-excitation via on-shell emission: Γ_X/Γ_γ ≲ O(10⁻⁶).
*   Axion-pion mixing angle from KTeV anomaly fit: θ_{aπ}|_{KTeV} = (-0.6 ± 0.2)/qₑ × 10⁻⁴.
*   Range of axion isoscalar mixing angles favored by the ⁸Be anomaly: -(θ_{aη_ud}(Δu + Δd) + √2 θ_{aη_s}Δs) ≈ (1.1–6.3) × 10⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends the study of a "piophobic" QCD axion model previously investigated by Alves (2017), which showed that a QCD axion with mass O(10 MeV) can evade experimental constraints if it has suppressed isovector couplings. The paper directly addresses the anomalies reported by Krasznahorkay et al. (2015, 2019) in ⁸Be and ⁴He nuclear de-excitations and the KTeV Collaboration (2007) measurement of π⁰ → e⁺e⁻, proposing the QCD axion as a unified explanation. The analysis enables future experimental searches at η/η′ and Kaon factories to probe the predicted ranges for rare meson decay rates, which suffer from large O(1) uncertainties due to the effective description of low-energy strong interactions.
