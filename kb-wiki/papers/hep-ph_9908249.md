# Nothing anomalous about two-loop HBCPT: consistency with the LET for spin-dependent Compton scattering

> The leading order contributions of processes involving anomalous pion-photon vertices to forward spin-dependent Compton scattering from nucleons are considered in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. These all involve the exchange of three pions between one or two photons and the nucleon, and hence are two-loop processes. We find that the sum of these processes vanishes in the manner predicted by the low energy theorem of Low, Gell-Mann and Goldberger as the photon energy goes to zero. This provides the first consistency test of two-loop HBCPT.

# Nothing anomalous about two-loop HBCPT: consistency with the LET for spin-dependent Compton scattering

## Key claim

The sum of all leading-order two-loop diagrams involving anomalous pion-photon vertices in forward spin-dependent Compton scattering from nucleons vanishes exactly as the photon energy goes to zero, consistent with the low-energy theorem of Low, Gell-Mann and Goldberger. This provides the first consistency test of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT) at two-loop order, though the authors note that failure would have indicated a problem with chiral perturbation theory rather than with the low-energy theorem itself.

## Method

The authors calculated the leading (seventh-order) contributions from the anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) Lagrangian to forward spin-dependent Compton scattering in HBCPT, using a transverse gauge with epsilon dot v = 0. They evaluated all two-loop diagrams containing anomalous vertices (proportional to N_c) with pion-nucleon vertices from the lowest-order Lagrangian, separating diagrams proportional to g_A^3 (figs. 1a-c) from those proportional to g_A (figs. 1d-i), and reduced the resulting integrals to scalar form using integration-by-parts identities and dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy theorem value for the spin-dependent forward scattering amplitude at zero energy is f_2(0) = -e^2 kappa^2 / (8 pi M_N^2), where kappa is the anomalous magnetic moment.
- The lowest-order HBCPT prediction for the forward spin-polarisability gamma_0 is 4.4 x 10^{-4} fm^4 for both proton and neutron, from gamma_0 = e^2 g_A^2 / (96 pi^3 f_pi^2 m_pi^2).
- A generalized HBCPT calculation including an explicit Delta gives a total gamma_0 of 2.2 x 10^{-4} fm^4.
- The MAMI experiment covers photon energies between 200 and 800 MeV, with planned extension down to 140 MeV; a future Bonn experiment will extend to 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of testing HBCPT beyond one-loop order, where the theory has been extensively validated against low-energy theorems. Previous two-loop calculations in HBCPT were limited to the imaginary part of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Bernard et al., 1996) and the fifth-order contribution to the nucleon mass (McGovern, 1999). The calculation demonstrates compatibility between the WZW anomalous Lagrangian and HBCPT, and provides a novel test of the structure of the anomalous Lagrangian. The results are relevant to ongoing experimental efforts at MAMI (Mainz) and Bonn to measure polarized Compton scattering, and to sum-rule analyses of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and the forward spin-polarisability gamma_0, where multipole analyses of electroproduction data (Karliner, 1973; Sandorfi et al., 1998) have shown discrepancies with chiral predictions.
