# Probing the Three-dimension Emission Source and Neutron Skin via $π$-$π$ Correlations in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The Richardson-Lucy algorithm is applied to reconstruct the three-dimensional source function of identical pions from their two-particle correlation functions. The algorithm's performance is first evaluated through simulations with Gaussian-type initial source functions. Its imaging quality and robustness are further demonstrated with experimental data from Au+Au collisions at 1.23 A GeV, collected by the HADES Collaboration. Additionally, using UrQMD simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at 1.5 A GeV, we show that the deblurred source functions exhibit sensitivity to the initial neutron skin thickness of the colliding nuclei. This highlights the potential of the Richardson-Lucy algorithm as a tool for probing the neutron density distribution in heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The Richardson-Lucy algorithm can reconstruct three-dimensional source functions from pion-pair correlation functions, and the deblurred source functions show sensitivity to the initial neutron skin thickness of colliding nuclei in UrQMD simulations, though the effect is demonstrated only for two extreme values of the diffuseness scaling parameter (f_n = 1 and f_n = 6) rather than a continuous mapping.

## Method

The authors apply the Richardson-Lucy iterative deconvolution algorithm to reconstruct the three-dimensional source function S(r_o, r_s, r_l) from two-pion correlation functions C(q_o, q_s, q_l) using the Kopylov-Podgoretsky formalism with Coulomb wave functions for identical pions. The method is validated first with Gaussian test source functions (R_o = 4 fm, R_s = 5 fm, R_l = 3 fm) and then applied to experimental data from Au+Au collisions at 1.23 A GeV from the HADES Collaboration. Finally, UrQMD v3.0.5 simulations of central Pb+Pb collisions at 1.5 A GeV with zero impact parameter are used to generate correlation functions for two neutron skin configurations, which are then imaged with the RL algorithm.

## Key numbers

- Gaussian test source radii: R_o = 4 fm, R_s = 5 fm, R_l = 3 fm; spatial domain divided into 5 bins over 0-16 fm
- Convergence threshold for RL iterations: Delta chi^2 < 10^-6
- Experimental data: Au+Au collisions at 1.23 A GeV from HADES; initial Gaussian radii used as starting point: R_o = 5.01 fm, R_s = 4.94 fm, R_l = 3.38 fm
- Pion selection: rapidity window |y - y_cm| < 0.35, transverse momentum 0.2-0.25 GeV/c
- UrQMD simulations: Pb+Pb collisions at 1.5 A GeV, 3 x 10^6 events per neutron skin configuration
- Neutron skin parameter: f_n = 1 and f_n = 6, with fixed proton diffuseness f_p = 1.0 and diffuseness parameter t_i = 2.18 fm
- Momentum components constrained to below 12 MeV when projecting correlation functions

## Context

This work extends one-dimensional source imaging methods (Xu 2024, Tam 2025) to three dimensions, enabling a more complete picture of the spatial source structure in heavy-ion collisions. The approach goes beyond conventional Gaussian HBT radius extraction methods (Bowler-Sinyukov method) used by the HADES Collaboration (2018, 2019) and recent studies on Gaussian HBT radii for neutron skin studies (Li 2025). The sensitivity of the reconstructed source function to neutron skin thickness opens a new probe of neutron density distributions in heavy nuclei, complementing existing constraints from PREX (2021) and CREX (2022) on the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy.
