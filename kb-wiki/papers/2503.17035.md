# Bayesian reconstruction of anisotropic flow fluctuations at fixed impact parameter

> The cumulants of the distribution of anisotropic flow are measured accurately in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC as a function of centrality classifiers (charged multiplicity and/or transverse energy). Using Bayesian inference, we reconstruct from these measurements the probability distribution of anisotropic flow in the ``theorists' frame'' where the impact parameter has a fixed magnitude and orientation, up to $\sim 70\%$ centrality. The variation of flow fluctuations with impact parameter displays direct evidence of viscous damping, which is larger for higher Fourier harmonics, in line with expectations from hydrodynamics. We use intensive measures of non-Gaussian flow fluctuations, which have reduced dependence on centrality. We infer from ATLAS data the magnitude of these intensive non-Gaussianities in each Fourier harmonic. They provide data-driven estimates of response coefficients to initial anisotropies, without resorting to any specific microscopic model of initial conditions. These estimates agree with viscous hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reconstructs the probability distribution of anisotropic flow at fixed impact parameter (the "theorists' frame") from ATLAS data up to 70% centrality, but the reconstruction of centrality fluctuations relies on the TRENTo model of initial conditions to determine the centrality dependence of multiplicity fluctuations, which cannot be inferred from data alone.

## Method

The authors use Bayesian inference to unfold the effects of impact parameter fluctuations from experimental centrality classes, assuming Gaussian fluctuations of the centrality classifier at fixed true centrality. They fit the cumulants of elliptic (v2), triangular (v3), and quadrangular (v4) flow measured by ATLAS in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, using two centrality classifiers (charged multiplicity Nch and transverse energy ET). The probability distribution of anisotropic flow at fixed impact parameter is parametrized using a Gaussian model with next-to-leading corrections (asymmetry, skewness, kurtosis), and intensive cumulants are defined to reduce centrality dependence.

## Key numbers

- Centrality reconstruction extends up to ~70% centrality
- Relative multiplicity fluctuation in central collisions: sigma_Nch(0)/mean_Nch(0) = 0.045, inferred from ATLAS data
- Average multiplicity at b=0: mean_Nch(0) = 3104 from ATLAS
- Gamma parameter (decrease factor of sigma_N^2/mean_N between peripheral and central collisions): gamma ~ 3.5 from TRENTO simulations
- Average number of participants at b=0: Npart = 410, close to total nucleons 2A = 416
- Elliptic-power distribution intensive asymmetry Gamma_02 ~ -1/2 and intensive skewness Gamma_12 ~ -2 for moderate epsilon_0
- Intensive kurtosis of epsilon_3 and C_4 distribution assumed approximately -2 (elliptic-power with epsilon_0=0)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of impact parameter fluctuations that were limited to central collisions (typically 0-10%) by Das (2017) and Alqahtani (2024), now reaching 70% centrality. It uses the Bayesian method introduced by Das (2017) for centrality reconstruction and the intensive non-Gaussianity measures proposed by Alqahtani (2024). The results enable data-driven estimates of hydrodynamic response coefficients to initial anisotropies without relying on specific microscopic models of initial conditions, and these estimates agree with viscous hydrodynamic calculations.
