# On nuclear coalescence in small interacting systems

> The formation of light nuclei can be described as the coalescence of clusters of nucleons into nuclei. In the case of small interacting systems, such as dark matter and $e^+e^-$ annihilations or $pp$ collisions, the coalescence condition is often imposed only in momentum space and hence the size of the interaction region is neglected. On the other hand, in most coalescence models used for heavy ion collisions, the coalescence probability is controlled mainly by the size of the interaction region, while two-nucleon momentum correlations are either neglected or treated as collective flow. Recent experimental data from $pp$ collisions at LHC have been interpreted as evidence for such collective behaviour, even in small interacting systems. We argue that these data are naturally explained in the framework of conventional QCD inspired event generators when both two-nucleon momentum correlations and the size of the hadronic emission volume are taken into account. To include both effects, we employ a per-event coalescence model based on the Wigner function representation of the produced nuclei states. This model reproduces well the source size for baryon emission and the coalescence factor $B_2$ measured recently by the ALICE collaboration in $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that recent ALICE data on deuteron production in $pp$ collisions, which have been interpreted as evidence for collective flow or quark-gluon plasma formation, are naturally explained by conventional QCD-inspired event generators when both two-nucleon momentum correlations and the size of the hadronic emission volume are taken into account, without invoking collective behavior.

## Method

The authors employ a per-event coalescence model (WiFunC) based on the Wigner function representation of produced nuclei states, combining two-nucleon momentum correlations from QCD-inspired event generators (QGSJET II and Pythia 8.2) with a Gaussian ansatz for the spatial distribution of nucleons characterized by a single parameter sigma. They compare model predictions to ALICE measurements of the baryon-emitting source size from femtoscopy and the coalescence factor B2 as functions of multiplicity and transverse momentum in pp collisions at 13 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian emission size parameter sigma is determined to be (0.95 +/- 0.1) fm from fitting the WiFunC model to ALICE source size data
- The deuteron Wigner function uses a Gaussian width d approximately 3.2 fm for analytical estimates, with a more accurate two-Gaussian fit to the Hulthen wave function used for numerical results
- The coalescence factor B2 is measured at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5) in pp collisions at 13 TeV, with data divided into multiplicity classes as percentages of the inclusive cross section
- For astrophysical applications, the main contribution to cosmic ray antideuterons comes from primary protons with energies 20-100 GeV colliding with interstellar matter, producing antideuterons with kinetic energies per nucleon T approximately 2-20 GeV/n

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous WiFunC model (Kachelriess 2019, 2020) and directly addresses the interpretation of ALICE data that had been attributed to collective flow (Nagle 2018). The paper contrasts with the femtoscopy-based approach of Blum (2017, 2019) and Bellini (2020), which assumes the smoothness approximation and neglects two-nucleon momentum correlations. The results enable more reliable predictions for antideuteron production in cosmic ray interactions relevant to AMS-02 and GAPS experiments, while showing that the femtoscopy-derived coalescence parameters from LHC energies overestimate the coalescence factor at the lower energies most relevant for astrophysical applications.
