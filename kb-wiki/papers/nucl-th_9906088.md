# Particle Freeze-out and Discontinuities in Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> Freeze-out of particles in relativistic hydrodynamics is considered across a 3-dimensional space-time hypersurface. The conservation laws for time-like parts of the freeze-out hypersurface require different values of temperature, baryonic chemical potential and flow velocity in the fluid and in the final particle spectra. We analyze this freeze-out discontinuity and its connection to the shock-wave phenomena in relativistic hydrodynamics.

# Particle Freeze-out and Discontinuities in Relativistic Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The paper argues that for time-like parts of the freeze-out hypersurface, the conservation laws force a discontinuity between the fluid parameters (temperature, baryonic chemical potential, flow velocity) and the parameters of the final particle distribution, which the authors call a "freeze-out shock." This discontinuity is required specifically because the cut-off distribution function used for time-like hypersurfaces cannot satisfy the conservation laws with a smooth matching, unlike the space-like case where the standard Cooper-Frye formula permits a continuous transition.

## Method

The authors analyze the freeze-out procedure across a 3-dimensional space-time hypersurface in relativistic hydrodynamics, distinguishing between space-like and time-like parts of the hypersurface. For time-like parts, they employ a cut-off distribution function (Eq. CO) that eliminates negative particle number contributions present in the standard Cooper-Frye formula, and derive local conservation laws (Eqs. BGconserv, BGconserv1) that relate the fluid variables to the gas variables. They then obtain analytical solutions for the freeze-out shock in the simplified case of zero baryonic number and massless particles with a Juttner distribution, comparing the resulting velocity and temperature relations to those of normal relativistic shock waves.

## Key numbers

- The minimal fluid velocity for both freeze-out shocks and normal shock waves is $(v_f)_{min} = p_f/\varepsilon_f$, which equals $1/3$ for the ideal gas equation of state considered.
- The speed of sound in the ideal gas equation of state is $c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}$.
- The maximal decompression in the freeze-out shock reaches a value of about 2 at the edge of the stability region, $v_f = 1/\sqrt{3}$.
- The entropy production in the freeze-out shock is given by Eq. entropy2: $\frac{s_g^{\mu} d\sigma_{\mu} }{s_f^{\mu} d\sigma_{\mu} } = \frac{1}{2 v_f} \left[ \frac{(3 v_f^2 + 1)^2(1 - 3 v_f)}{(1 + v_f)} \right]^{1/4}$, with maximal value at $v_f = 1/\sqrt{3}$.
- For the normal shock wave, the entropy production is given by Eq. entropy1: $\frac{s_g^{\mu} d\sigma_{\mu} }{s_f^{\mu} d\sigma_{\mu} } = \frac{1}{3^{3/4} v_f} \left[ \frac{9 v_f^2 - 1}{1 - v_f^2} \right]^{1/4}$.
- The temperature ratio between fluid and gas for the freeze-out shock is $R \equiv (T_f/T_g)^4 = \frac{4(3v_f^2 - 2v_f +1)^2 (v_f +1)}{(3v_f -1)(3v_f^2 +1)^2}$.
- For the normal shock wave, the temperature ratio is $R \equiv (T_f/T_g)^4 = \frac{3 (1-v_f^2)}{9v_f^2 - 1}$.

## Context

This work extends the freeze-out procedure developed by Bugaev (1996) for time-like hypersurfaces, which itself corrected the Cooper-Frye formula (Cooper and Frye, 1974) that produced unphysical negative particle number contributions on time-like hypersurfaces. The paper connects the freeze-out discontinuity to the theory of relativistic shock waves, building on the classification of space-like and time-like shocks by Csernai (1987) and the mechanical stability criteria for shock transitions developed by Bugaev (1988, 1989, 1991). The results enable self-consistent inclusion of freeze-out in hydrodynamical models of nucleus-nucleus collisions, particularly for hybrid approaches that combine hydrodynamics for the early deconfined stage with microscopic non-equilibrium models for the later hadronic stage, as referenced in Bass (1999).
