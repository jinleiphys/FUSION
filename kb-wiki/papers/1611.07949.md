# Structure of $^{14}$C and $^{14}$O nuclei within a five-cluster model

> Within a five-particle model (three $α$-particles plus two nucleons), the structure functions of mirror nuclei $^{14}$C and $^{14}$O are studied. Using the variational approach with Gaussian bases, the energies and wave functions are calculated for these five-particle systems. Two spatial configurations in the ground-state wave function are revealed. The r.m.s. charge radius of $^{14}$O nucleus is found to be $2.415\pm0.005$ fm. The charge density distributions and the form factors of both nuclei are predicted. The pair correlation functions are analyzed, and the r.m.s. relative distances are calculated. The momentum distributions of particles are found.

## Key claim

The r.m.s. charge radius of the rare isotope \(^{14}\)O is predicted to be \(2.415\pm0.005\) fm, which is smaller than that of its mirror nucleus \(^{14}\)C, even though the mass radius of \(^{14}\)O is larger; this counterintuitive result depends on the fact that the extra protons in \(^{14}\)O reside mainly inside the \(^{12}\)C cluster, and the calculation assumes that the experimental charge radius of \(^{14}\)C is well fitted and that the additional Coulomb repulsion in \(^{14}\)O is accurately accounted for.

## Method

The authors model \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O as five-particle systems consisting of three alpha particles plus two extra nucleons (neutrons for \(^{14}\)C, protons for \(^{14}\)O). They solve the five-body Schrödinger equation using a variational method with a Gaussian basis of up to 400 functions, employing both stochastic and regular variational techniques to fix the nonlinear parameters. The interaction potentials between particles are of a generalized type with local and non-local (separable) terms, with parameters taken from prior work (Grinyuk et al., cited as R1) that reproduce the experimental binding energies of both nuclei and the experimental charge radius of \(^{14}\)C.

## Key numbers

*   Ground-state energy of \(^{14}\)C (subtracting the own energies of alpha particles): \(-20.398\) MeV.
*   Ground-state energy of \(^{14}\)O (subtracting the own energies of alpha particles): \(-13.845\) MeV.
*   Predicted r.m.s. charge radius of \(^{14}\)O: \(2.415\pm0.005\) fm.
*   Calculated r.m.s. charge radius of \(^{14}\)C: \(2.500\) fm.
*   Calculated r.m.s. mass radius of \(^{14}\)C: \(2.433\) fm; of \(^{14}\)O: \(2.461\) fm.
*   Calculated r.m.s. relative distance between the two extra nucleons (\(r_{NN}\)) in \(^{14}\)C: \(2.621\) fm; in \(^{14}\)O: \(2.732\) fm.
*   Calculated r.m.s. relative distance between an extra nucleon and an alpha particle (\(r_{N\alpha}\)) in \(^{14}\)C: \(2.667\) fm; in \(^{14}\)O: \(2.750\) fm.
*   Calculated r.m.s. relative distance between two alpha particles (\(r_{\alpha\alpha}\)) in \(^{14}\)C: \(3.189\) fm; in \(^{14}\)O: \(3.239\) fm.
*   Calculated average kinetic energy of an extra neutron in \(^{14}\)C: \(32.66\) MeV; of an extra proton in \(^{14}\)O: \(31.77\) MeV.
*   Calculated average kinetic energy of an alpha particle in \(^{14}\)C: \(6.83\) MeV; in \(^{14}\)O: \(6.62\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends a five-cluster model (three alphas plus two nucleons) previously applied to lighter nuclei such as \(^{6}\)He, \(^{6}\)Li, \(^{10}\)Be, and \(^{10}\)C (Grinyuk et al., cited as R2, R3, R4, R5, R6). The study predicts the charge radius of the radioactive isotope \(^{14}\)O, which has not yet been measured experimentally, and contrasts its structure with the stable mirror nucleus \(^{14}\)C. The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted charge radius and provide a detailed comparison of the charge form factors, which are shown to differ significantly between the two nuclei due to the contribution of the extra protons in \(^{14}\)O.
