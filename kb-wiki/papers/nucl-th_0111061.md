# Simultaneous Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering, Breakup, and Fusion Cross Section Data for the $^{6}$He + $^{209}$Bi System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies

> Based on an approach recently proposed by us, simultaneous $χ^{2}$-analyses are performed for elastic scattering, direct reaction (DR) and fusion cross sections data for the $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies to determine the parameters of the polarization potential consisting of DR and fusion parts. We show that the data are well reproduced by the resultant potential, which also satisfies the proper dispersion relation. A discussion is given of the nature of the threshold anomaly seen in the potential.

## Key claim

The fusion part of the imaginary optical potential for the $^{6}$He + $^{209}$Bi system exhibits a threshold anomaly (rapid energy variation near the Coulomb barrier) similar to that seen for tightly bound projectiles, but this anomaly is masked in the total imaginary potential because the direct reaction (breakup) part is much larger in magnitude and shows no such anomaly. The authors demonstrate this by decomposing the total polarization potential into separate fusion and direct reaction components through simultaneous fits to elastic scattering, breakup, and fusion data.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous $\chi^{2}$-analyses of elastic scattering, angle-integrated total direct reaction (breakup), and fusion cross section data for the $^{6}$He + $^{209}$Bi system at five energies between 14.3 and 21.4 MeV. They used an optical model with a volume-type Woods-Saxon imaginary potential for fusion ($W_F$) and a surface-derivative Woods-Saxon imaginary potential for direct reactions ($W_D$), treating $W_F(E)$ and the radius parameter $r_D(E)$ as adjustable parameters while keeping other geometrical parameters fixed. The real parts of the polarization potentials were then generated from the imaginary parts using the dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for fusion, where $W_F(E)=0$, is $E_0 = 15.4$ MeV, matching the threshold of $S(E)=\sqrt{E\sigma_F}$ found by Kolata et al. (1998).
- The fusion imaginary strength $W_F(E)$ is parameterized as: 0 for $E \leq 15.4$ MeV, $1.25(E-15.4)$ MeV for $15.4 < E \leq 18.5$ MeV, and 4.0 MeV for $E \geq 18.5$ MeV.
- The direct reaction radius parameter $r_D(E)$ decreases linearly from 1.73 fm at $E=14.0$ MeV to 1.508 fm at $E=21.4$ MeV, with a slope of $-0.03$ fm/MeV.
- At the strong absorption radius $R_{sa}=13.0$ fm, the direct reaction imaginary potential $W_D(R_{sa};E)$ is larger than the fusion part $W_F(R_{sa};E)$ by approximately a factor of 5.
- The elastic probability $P_E$ starts to deviate from unity at an unusually large reduced distance $d_I = 2.2$ fm, compared to the typical value of about 1.6 fm for tightly bound projectiles.
- At the lowest energy $E=14.3$ MeV, a fit using only elastic scattering data gave $r_D=1.93$ fm, but this produced a direct reaction cross section of 540 mb, about 3 times larger than the experimental value.

## Context

This work extends previous optical model analyses of elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections for the $^{6}$He + $^{209}$Bi system by Aguilera et al. (2001) and Mohr (2000), adding simultaneous treatment of breakup and fusion data. The approach builds on the method of Kim, Naito, and Udagawa (1990) for decomposing the polarization potential into direct reaction and fusion parts. The results confirm the argument by Mahaux, Ngo, and Satchler (1986) that the weak energy dependence of the breakup polarization potential masks the threshold anomaly in the total potential for loosely bound projectiles. The work enables future studies where the separate fusion and direct reaction potentials can be used to predict cross sections for other weakly bound projectile systems near the Coulomb barrier.
