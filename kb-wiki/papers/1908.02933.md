# Viscous coefficients and thermal conductivity of a $πK N$ gas mixture in the medium

> The temperature and density dependence of the relaxation times, thermal conductivity, shear viscosity and bulk viscosity for a hot and dense gas consisting of pions, kaons and nucleons have been evaluated in the kinetic theory approach. The in-medium cross-sections for $ππ$, $πK$ and $πN$ scatterings were obtained by using complete propagators for the exchanged $ρ$, $σ$, $K^*$ and $Δ$ excitations derived using thermal field theoretic techniques. Notable deviations can be observed in the temperature dependence of $η$, $ζ$ and $λ$ when compared with corresponding calculations using vacuum cross-sections usually employed in the literature. The value of the specific shear viscosity $η/s$ is found to be in agreement with available estimates.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using in-medium cross-sections (derived from thermal field theory) for ππ, πK, and πN scattering leads to notable deviations in the temperature dependence of shear viscosity η, bulk viscosity ζ, and thermal conductivity λ compared to calculations using vacuum cross-sections, while the specific shear viscosity η/s remains consistent with available estimates.

## Method

The authors used the kinetic theory approach with the Boltzmann transport equation, employing the Chapman-Enskog method for the left-hand side and the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for the coupled collision integral. In-medium scattering cross-sections for ππ, πK, and πN were obtained by replacing vacuum propagators for exchanged ρ, σ, K*, and Δ resonances with complete propagators derived from one-loop self-energies calculated using the Real Time Formalism of thermal field theory. The relaxation times for pions, kaons, and nucleons were then computed from these in-medium cross-sections and used to evaluate the transport coefficients via integral expressions.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constants were fixed from experimental decay widths: gρππ = 6.05, gσππ = 2.5, fπNΔ = 2.8, gπKK* = 10.80.
- Hadronic form factor cutoffs were taken as ΛπN = 600 MeV and ΛπK = 350 MeV.
- The in-medium cross-sections were evaluated at T = 160 MeV, with μπ = μK = 0 and μN = 200 MeV.
- For an interacting pion gas, the ratio of shear viscosities calculated in the Chapman-Enskog and RTA methods varies between 1.18 at T = 100 MeV to about 1.1 at T = 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in hadronic matter, such as those by Gavin (1985) using relaxation time approximation, Davesne (1996) using Chapman-Enskog for bosonic systems, and Dobado (2002) for multicomponent πKη mixtures. It also builds on calculations by Itakura (2008) for πN mixtures and Wiranata (2013) for hadronic resonance gases, but introduces the novel element of medium-modified cross-sections from thermal field theory. The results enable more realistic modeling of dissipative processes in the hadronic phase of heavy-ion collisions, particularly relevant for future CBM experiments at FAIR where finite baryon density effects are important.
