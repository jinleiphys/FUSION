# An alternative way of plotting the data and results of models of J/psi suppression

> We propose an alternative way of looking at data on anomalous J/psi suppression. The proposed method is in principle equivalent to the one used by the NA50 Collaboration, but it permits to visualize separate contributions of individual processes responsible for the disintegration of J/psi's produced by a hard process in nuclear collisions. The method can be used provided that the time sequence of contributing mechanisms is known or assumed. It offers an alternative graphical presentation of the onset of anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb-Pb interactions observed by the NA50 Collaboration at the CERN SPS and might contribute to explain why different mechanisms, such as J/psi suppression by the Quark-Gluon Plasma and by co-movers in the Dual Parton Model or in Monte Carlo microscopic approaches, all lead to an approximate description of anomalous J/psi suppression.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an alternative graphical method for presenting data on anomalous J/psi suppression that is in principle equivalent to the standard NA50 Collaboration approach, but which permits visualization of the separate contributions of individual disintegration mechanisms (nuclear absorption, Quark-Gluon Plasma dissolution, co-mover interactions) provided the time sequence of those mechanisms is known or assumed.

## Method

The authors decompose the total number of produced J/psi mesons into additive components: those surviving (experimentally measured), those disintegrated by nuclear absorption, those dissolved by Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), and those destroyed by other mechanisms (e.g., co-movers). They calculate each term separately using a simplified geometric model of nuclear collisions (hard spheres, strong correlation between impact parameter b and transverse energy E_T) and plot the individual contributions as functions of E_T. The method is illustrated using the KLNS (Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi, Satz) model for QGP suppression, with nuclear absorption cross-sections varied between 0.5 and 0.7 fm^2.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear absorption cross-section sigma_a = 0.7 fm^2 used in the main illustrative calculation (also shown with 0.6 fm^2 and 0.5 fm^2 in Fig. 3).
- Nucleon-nucleon non-diffractive cross-section sigma_nn = 30 mb (3 fm^2).
- Nuclear radii taken as R_A = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm.
- Relationship between transverse energy and number of wounded nucleons: E_T(b) = 0.325 GeV * N_w(b).
- The threshold for anomalous suppression (onset of QGP contribution) appears between E_T = 30 GeV and E_T = 40 GeV in the plotted Delta N = N_prod - N_exp - N_NA.
- For co-mover models, cross-sections sigma_co of about 5 mb are cited from some approaches, while the Dual Parton Model requires sigma_co = 0.6 mb with sigma_a = 6.7 mb, and another approach uses sigma_co = 1 mb with sigma_a = 4.5 mb.

## Context

This work extends the standard analysis of anomalous J/psi suppression performed by the NA50 Collaboration at the CERN SPS, which was originally predicted by Matsui and Satz (1986). The method is designed to clarify why different mechanisms (QGP dissolution as in Blaizot and Ollitrault (1996) and Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi, Satz (1997); co-mover interactions in the Dual Parton Model; and Monte Carlo microscopic approaches) all yield approximate descriptions of the same data. The approach enables future consistency checks by plotting the "accountant's balance" of where all produced J/psi mesons go, and could help resolve whether QGP or hadronic mechanisms are responsible for the anomalous suppression when combined with accurate determinations of nuclear absorption from lighter-ion data, lattice QCD results on the critical temperature (about 173 MeV), and analyses of J/psi transverse momentum data.
