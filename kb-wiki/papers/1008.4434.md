# Baryon Interactions from Lattice QCD

> We report on new attempt to investigate baryon-baryon interactions in lattice QCD. From the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) wave function, we have successfully extracted the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials in quenched QCD simulations, which reproduce qualitative features of modern $NN$ potentials. The method has been extended to obtain the tensor potential as well as the central potential and also applied to the hyperon-nucleon ($YN$) interactions, in both quenched and full QCD.

## Key claim

The authors report that nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials extracted from the Bethe-Salpeter wave function in quenched lattice QCD simulations reproduce qualitative features of modern NN potentials, but only at a pion mass of 529 MeV and a lattice spacing of 0.137 fm, with the method extended to tensor potentials and hyperon-nucleon interactions in both quenched and full QCD.

## Method

The equal-time Bethe-Salpeter (BS) wave function for a two-nucleon system is computed from lattice QCD, and a non-local potential is defined via the relation [E - H0] phi_E(r) = integral U(r,s) phi_E(s) d^3s, where H0 is the non-relativistic kinetic energy operator. This non-local potential is expanded in a derivative expansion, and the leading local terms (central potential V_C and tensor potential V_T) are determined from the BS wave function at a single energy by solving a system of equations involving projections onto L=0 and L=2 states.

## Key numbers

* Central potential for the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 states extracted at m_pi = 529 MeV in quenched QCD with lattice spacing a = 0.137 fm and spatial extension L = 4.4 fm.
* Local potential for the ^1S_0 state shows very small energy dependence between k approximately 0 MeV and k approximately 250 MeV at m_pi = 529 MeV in quenched QCD.
* Central potential for the ^1S_0 state compared at three pion masses: m_pi = 380 MeV, 529 MeV, and 731 MeV in quenched QCD at a = 0.137 fm, showing stronger repulsive core and stronger intermediate attraction as quark mass decreases.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ishizuka (2000) and Aoki, Hatsuda, and Ishii (2008) for defining nuclear potentials from lattice QCD via the Bethe-Salpeter wave function, and it directly confronts phenomenological NN potentials such as Bonn, Reid93, and AV18. The results enable first-principles calculations of nuclear forces from QCD, with future applications to neutron star structure and supernova mechanisms, while open questions include the approach to the physical pion mass and the inclusion of higher-order derivative terms in the potential.
