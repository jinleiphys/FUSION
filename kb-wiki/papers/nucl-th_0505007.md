# Determination of S17 from 8B breakup by means of the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels

> The astrophysical factor for 7Be(p,γ)8B at zero energy, S17(0), is determined from an analysis of 208Pb(8B, p+7Be)208Pb at 52 MeV/nucleon by means of the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) taking account of all nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes. The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method is used to extract S17(0) from the calculated breakup-cross-section. The main result of the present paper is S17(0)=20.9 +2.0/-1.9 eV b. The error consists of 8.4% experimental systematic error and the error due to the ambiguity in the s-wave p-7Be scattering length. This value of S17(0) differs from the one extracted with the first-order perturbation theory including Coulomb breakup by dipole transitions: 18.9 +/- 1.8 eV b. It turns out that the difference is due to the inclusion of the nuclear and Coulomb-quadrupole transitions and multi-step processes of all-order in the present work. The p-7Be interaction potential used in the CDCC calculation is also used in the ANC analysis of 7Be(p,γ)8B. The value of S17(0)=21.7 +0.62/-0.55 eV b obtained is consistent with the previous one obtained from a precise measurement of the p-capture reaction cross section extrapolated to zero incident energy, S17(0)=22.1 +/- 0.6 (expt) +/- 0.6 (theo) eV b, where (theo) stands for the error in the extrapolation. Thus, the agreement between the values of S17(0) obtained from direct 7Be(p,γ)8B and indirect 8B-breakup measurements is significantly improved.

## Key claim

The astrophysical factor S17(0) for the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction is determined to be 20.9 +2.0/-1.9 eV b from 8B breakup data using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method that includes all nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes, which differs from the value of 18.9 ± 1.8 eV b obtained with first-order perturbation theory that only included Coulomb dipole transitions.

## Method

The authors analyzed the 208Pb(8B, p+7Be)208Pb reaction at 52 MeV/nucleon measured at RIKEN using the CDCC method with a p+7Be+208Pb three-body model, incorporating all nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes including multi-step effects to all orders. They used the eikonal-CDCC method for large orbital angular momenta and constructed hybrid scattering amplitudes combining eikonal and fully quantum-mechanical CDCC results. The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method was then applied to extract S17(0) from the calculated breakup cross section, using the same p-7Be interaction potential in both the CDCC calculation and the ANC analysis of the direct capture reaction.

## Key numbers

- S17(0) = 20.9 +2.0/-1.9 eV b from CDCC analysis of 8B breakup, with error consisting of 8.4% experimental systematic error and error from ambiguity in the s-wave p-7Be scattering length
- S17(0) = 21.7 +0.62/-0.55 eV b obtained from ANC analysis of direct 7Be(p,γ)8B using the same p-7Be potential as in the CDCC calculation
- S17(0) = 22.1 ± 0.6 (expt) ± 0.6 (theo) eV b from direct measurement of the p-capture reaction by Junghans et al. (2003) extrapolated to zero energy
- S17(0) = 18.9 ± 1.8 eV b from first-order perturbation theory including only Coulomb dipole transitions (RIKEN analysis)
- S17(0) = 17.8 +1.4/-1.2 eV b from MSU experiment at 44 and 83 MeV/nucleon
- S17(0) = 18.6 ± 1.2 ± 1.0 eV b from GSI experiment at 250 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the analysis of 8B Coulomb dissociation experiments beyond the first-order perturbation theory used in earlier analyses by Davids et al. (2001) and Kikuchi et al. (1998), which only considered electric dipole transitions and yielded S17(0) values around 18-19 eV b that disagreed with direct capture measurements by about 15%. The inclusion of nuclear breakup, Coulomb-quadrupole transitions, and multi-step processes to all orders via CDCC significantly improves the agreement between indirect and direct determinations of S17(0), bringing the breakup-derived value to within uncertainty of the direct measurement by Junghans et al. (2003). This enables more reliable extraction of S17(0) from breakup experiments and reduces the systematic uncertainty in solar neutrino flux predictions.
