# Recent progress in global optimizations of covariant energy density functionals

> The recent progress on global optimizations of covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) and global calculations of binding energies within the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) has been analyzed and reviewed. Recently developed anchor-based optimization approach of Ref. [1] allows global optimizations of CEDFs at a reasonable numerical cost. Moreover, it permits such optimizations in a very large fermionic basis with a proper extrapolation to an infinite one. This allows to accurately estimate global calculation errors due to use of truncated fermionic basis and neglect of some contributions to binding energies (such as total electron binding energy)

# Recent progress in global optimizations of covariant energy density functionals

## Key claim

The anchor-based optimization approach (ABOA) enables global optimizations of covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) at reasonable numerical cost, allowing calculations in very large fermionic bases with proper extrapolation to infinite basis; however, even with this progress, the accuracy of global binding energy descriptions at the mean-field level remains limited by global calculation errors of order 1 MeV when total electron binding energies and infinite basis corrections are neglected.

## Method

The authors review and analyze global optimizations of CEDFs within covariant density functional theory (CDFT), comparing functionals fitted with the traditional fitting protocol (TFP) against those fitted with the recently developed anchor-based optimization approach (ABOA). The ABOA requires only about 10 rounds of global calculations in axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) codes, in contrast to the thousands needed by TFP, enabling calculations with fermionic bases up to N_F=120 and bosonic bases up to N_B=40 that approximate infinite basis results. The study benchmarks the accuracy of the extrapolation procedure and quantifies global calculation errors from basis truncation and neglect of total electron binding energies (TEBE) across the nuclear chart.

## Key numbers

- The DD-MEZ functional achieves ΔB_rms = 1.557 ± 0.028 MeV (nuclear binding energies, including TEBE and infinite basis corrections)
- The NL5(Z) functional achieves ΔB_rms = 2.357 ± 0.023 MeV (nuclear binding energies, including TEBE and infinite basis corrections)
- The PC-Z functional achieves ΔB_rms = 1.901 ± 0.05 MeV (nuclear binding energies, including TEBE and infinite basis corrections)
- Global calculation error δB_rms^{negl} = 0.77 MeV for DD-MEY and 0.84 MeV for NL5(Y) functionals when fitted in finite N_F=N_B=20 basis without TEBE
- The difference between N_B=20 and N_B=40 bosonic bases reaches 300 keV in superheavy nuclei for DDME functionals and 800 keV for NLME functionals
- Adding Wigner energy in the formulation of Goriely et al. (2002) reduces ΔB_rms for DD-MEZ from 1.601 MeV to 1.557 MeV
- Removing nuclei around Z=92, N=126 improves DD-MEZ ΔB_rms from 1.557 MeV to 1.403 MeV
- The PC-PK1 functional with 5DCH corrections gives ΔB_rms = 1.14 MeV (HO basis, Ref. LLLYM.15) and 1.31 MeV (HO basis, Ref. YWZL.21), with estimated δB_rms^{negl} at least 1 MeV

## Context

This work reviews and extends the global optimization program initiated by the authors in Afanasjev and Osei (2023) and the NL5Z-DDMEZ-PCZ study, which introduced the anchor-based optimization approach that made global CEDF fits feasible. The paper contrasts these results with earlier global mass calculations in CDFT, such as those by Agbemava et al. (2016) which used traditional fitting protocols limited to N_F=16 basis, and with non-relativistic approaches that achieve ΔB_rms ≈ 0.5 MeV through phenomenological vibrational+rotational corrections and Wigner energy. The analysis identifies specific regions of the nuclear chart (Z=92, N=126; N=Z line; neutron drip-line regions) where deficiencies in single-particle properties limit accuracy, and recommends future work on tensor interactions, delta-meson channels, and beyond-mean-field effects to improve spectroscopic quality of CEDFs.
