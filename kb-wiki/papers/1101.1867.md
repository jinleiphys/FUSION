# Two-photon exchange in electron deuteron scattering

> It is shown that the amplitude of elastic ed scattering beyond Born approximation contains six generalized form factors, but only three linearly independent combinations of them (generalized charge, quadrupole and magnetic form factors) contribute to the reaction cross section in the second order perturbation theory. We examine two-photon exchange and find that it includes two types of diagrams, when two virtual photons interact with the same nucleon and when the photons interact with different nucleons.We discuss contribution of the two-photon exchange in reaction observables, generalized \mathcal A and \mathcal B structure functions and tensor polarization of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper shows that beyond the Born approximation, the elastic electron-deuteron scattering amplitude contains six generalized form factors, but only three linearly independent combinations (generalized charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors) contribute to the cross section in second-order perturbation theory; the two-photon exchange contribution becomes significant (more than 10%) in the structure function B at momentum transfers above 2 GeV^2, while its effect in the structure function A is not significant.

## Method

The authors calculate the two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitude for elastic electron-deuteron scattering, separating it into two types of diagrams: those where both virtual photons interact with the same nucleon (type I) and those where the photons interact with different nucleons (type II). For type I diagrams, they use the TPE amplitude for a nucleon expressed through effective hadron currents with corrections to Dirac and Pauli form factors plus a new form factor, combined with nonrelativistic deuteron wave functions. For type II diagrams, they employ a hard-photon approximation assuming each intermediate photon carries about half the transferred momentum, reducing the calculation to a one-dimensional integral using the S-component of the deuteron wave function in momentum representation.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 > 2 GeV^2, TPE contribution becomes more than 10% in the structure function B at theta_LAB = 180 degrees
- In the structure function A, TPE contribution is not significant (no specific percentage given)
- In tensor polarization t_22 at theta_LAB = 70 degrees, TPE effect was found significant (no specific percentage given)
- In tensor polarizations t_20 and t_21, TPE contribution is minor (less than 1%)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical calculations by Lev, Gakh and Tomasi, Dong and Chen, and the authors' own prior work (Kobushkin and Krivenko-Emetov, 2010) showing that effects beyond one-photon exchange may significantly change the extraction of deuteron form factors from experimental data. The paper enables improved analysis of deuteron electromagnetic structure by quantifying two-photon exchange contributions to the generalized structure functions A and B and to tensor polarization observables, using CD-Bonn and Paris deuteron wave functions for numerical calculations.
