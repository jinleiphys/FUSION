# Polyakov loop in 2+1 flavor QCD from low to high temperatures

> We study the free energy of a static quark in QCD with 2+1 flavors in a wide temperature region, 116 MeV $< T < $ 5814 MeV, using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action. We analyze the transition region in detail, obtain the entropy of a static quark, show that it peaks at temperatures close to the chiral crossover temperature and also revisit the temperature dependence of the Polyakov loop susceptibilities using gradient flow. We discuss the implications of our findings for the deconfinement and chiral crossover phenomena at physical values of the quark masses. Finally a comparison of the lattice results at high temperatures with the weak-coupling calculations is presented.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the deconfinement crossover temperature, defined by the peak of the static quark entropy, is consistent with the chiral crossover temperature for physical quark masses, contradicting earlier claims that the deconfinement crossover occurs at significantly higher temperatures.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of the bare Polyakov loop using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action on \(N_{\sigma}^3 \times N_{\tau}\) lattices with \(N_{\tau}=4,6,8,10,12\) and aspect ratio 4, covering a temperature range of 116 MeV to 5814 MeV. They renormalized the Polyakov loop using the static quark-antiquark energy at zero temperature, performed continuum extrapolations using both local and global fits, and computed the static quark entropy \(S_Q = -\partial F_Q/\partial T\) from the temperature derivative of the free energy.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range covered: 116 MeV \(< T <\) 5814 MeV
- The peak of the static quark entropy \(S_Q\) occurs at \(T_S = 153^{+6.5}_{-5}\) MeV, with a systematic uncertainty of \(^{+4}_{-2.5}\) MeV from the spread of fits
- The chiral crossover temperature from previous work (Bazavov 2011) is approximately 153-157 MeV, consistent with \(T_S\)
- The inflection point of the renormalized Polyakov loop lies between 180 and 200 MeV for each \(N_{\tau}=12,10,8,6\), significantly above the chiral transition temperature

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of the renormalized Polyakov loop in 2+1 flavor QCD (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2013) to a much wider temperature range reaching 5.8 GeV, enabling direct comparison with weak-coupling calculations. It contradicts earlier findings by Aoki (2006, 2009) that the deconfinement crossover defined by the renormalized Polyakov loop occurs at temperatures significantly higher than the chiral crossover. The results enable future comparisons with next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading order perturbative calculations at high temperatures.
