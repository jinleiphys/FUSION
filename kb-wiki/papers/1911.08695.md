# Refined analysis on the parton distribution functions of the proton

> We explore the application of a two-component model of proton structure functions in the analysis of deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) data at low $Q^2$ and small $x$. This model incorporates both vector meson dominance and the correct photo-production limit. The CJ15 parameterization is applied to the QCD component, in order to take into account effects of order $1/Q^2$ effects, such as target mass corrections and higher twist contributions. The parameters of the leading twist parton distribution functions and higher twist coefficient functions are determined by fitting deep inelastic scattering data. The second moments of the parton distribution functions are extracted and compared with other global fits and lattice determinations.

# Refined analysis on the parton distribution functions of the proton

## Key claim

The two-component model of proton structure functions, which incorporates vector meson dominance and the correct photo-production limit, yields second moments of parton distribution functions that are consistent with previous global-fit determinations and do not explain the significant discrepancy between those global fits and lattice QCD results. The analysis is exploratory and limited to DIS data only, with several parameters fixed from the CJ15 analysis due to the restricted data set.

## Method

The authors fit a two-component model of the proton structure function to deep-inelastic scattering data from BCDMS, SLAC, NMC, E665, and H1 at low Q² and small x. The model separates the structure function into a vector meson dominance (VMD) component and a QCD component, where the QCD part uses the CJ15 parametrization with target mass corrections and higher-twist contributions. Nine free parameters of the leading-twist parton distribution functions and the higher-twist coefficient function were determined by fitting data at Q² bins around 5, 10, 20, 25, and 50 GeV², with statistical and systematic errors added in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 4 GeV², the extracted second moments for the fit with Q₀² = 1.0 GeV² and κ = 0.4 are: ⟨x⟩_{u⁺} = 0.3543, ⟨x⟩_{d⁺} = 0.2039, ⟨x⟩_{s⁺} = 0.0344, ⟨x⟩_g = 0.3993
- The VMD contribution accounts for 10-20% of the total F₂ at x ≤ 0.1 for Q² = 4 GeV²
- The fit with Q₀² = 1.0 GeV² and κ = 0.4 yields χ²_{d.o.f} = 116/(129-9) = 0.97
- The fit with Q₀² = 1.5 GeV² and κ = 0.4 yields χ²_{d.o.f} = 138/(129-9) = 1.15
- The d_v/u_v ratio in the x → 1 limit is 8.80 × 10⁻², consistent with the CJ15 value of 8.89 × 10⁻²
- The gluon momentum fraction ranges from 0.3831 to 0.4033 depending on Q₀² and κ, compared to the CJ15 value of 0.4152

## Context

This work extends the two-component model originally proposed by Badelek and Kwiecinski (1989, 1992) and later used by Martin et al. (1999), applying it with the modern CJ15 parametrization from the Jefferson Lab group. The analysis was motivated by a discrepancy between global-fit determinations and lattice QCD results from ETMC17 (Alexandrou, 2017), where lattice values for quark momentum fractions were significantly larger. The present results confirm the global-fit values rather than the lattice results, suggesting the discrepancy originates from the lattice side. The authors note that a full global fit incorporating this two-component model could remove the constraints used here and determine all parameters more precisely.
