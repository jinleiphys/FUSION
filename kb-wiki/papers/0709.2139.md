# Spectral functions for medium-sized nuclei

> The spectral functions for calcium and argon are constructed. It is verified that their predictions for the quasielastic electron-nucleus cross sections in the energy range ~1 GeV agree with the data. The argon spectral function is then used to obtain the quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross section.

## Key claim

The authors construct spectral functions for calcium and argon that predict quasielastic electron-nucleus cross sections in the ~1 GeV energy range with accuracy comparable to the more systematic Benhar spectral function for oxygen, but only in the region of energy and momentum transfer relevant to ~1 GeV neutrino quasielastic scattering; the model shows larger deviations from electron scattering data at lower beam energies where momentum transfer at the quasielastic peak is below ~400 MeV, indicating the impulse approximation is no longer reliable.

## Method

The spectral function is divided into a correlated part (using an analytical formula from Kulagin and Petti) and a mean-field part modeled with Gaussian level widths for each shell-model state, using momentum distributions from Bisconti, Arias, and Co. The authors test the model against electron scattering data for oxygen, calcium, and argon, then apply the argon spectral function to compute the quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross section, including final state interactions via Pauli blocking and an optical potential.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca(e,e') scattering at 45.5°, the quasielastic peak is reduced by 1.7% for beam energy 841 MeV and by 2.2% for 471 MeV due to the current conservation restoration procedure.
- Momentum transfer at the quasielastic peak for oxygen: 638 MeV (beam energy 1200 MeV), 441 MeV (730 MeV), and 325 MeV (537 MeV).
- Momentum transfer at the quasielastic peak for calcium: 602 MeV (beam energy 841 MeV), 490 MeV (681 MeV), and 342 MeV (471 MeV).
- Momentum transfer at the quasielastic peak for argon: 371 MeV (electron scattering).
- For neutrino energy 0.8 GeV, 52% of the differential cross section in scattering angle comes from angles between 20° and 56°.

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach, previously applied to light nuclei like carbon and oxygen by Benhar, Fabrocini, Fantoni, and Sick (1994) and Benhar, Farina, and Nakamura (2004), to medium-sized nuclei calcium and argon. The authors compare their results to the Fermi gas model and to calculations by Butkevich and Mikheyev (2002) for calcium. The argon spectral function enables improved predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments, where the Fermi gas model has been the standard but less accurate approach.
