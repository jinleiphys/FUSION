# Covariant baryon charge radii and magnetic moments in a chiral constituent quark model

> The charge radii and magnetic moments of all the light and strange baryons are investigated within the framework of a constituent quark model based on Goldstone-boson-exchange dynamics. Following the point-form approach to relativistic quantum mechanics, the calculations are performed in a manifestly covariant manner. Relativistic (boost) effects have a sizeable influence on the results. The direct predictions of the constituent quark model are found to fall remarkably close to the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model, when calculated in a manifestly covariant point-form approach, produces charge radii and magnetic moments for all light and strange baryons that fall "remarkably close" to experimental data, but only when both a relativistic current operator and Lorentz boosts are included; nonrelativistic calculations give accidentally good results for some octet magnetic moments but fail for charge radii and decuplet baryons.

## Method

The authors solve the mass operator eigenvalue problem for the three-quark system using the point-form version of relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics, where only the four-momentum operator carries interactions and Lorentz boosts remain kinematic. They compute elastic electromagnetic form factors in the Breit frame using a point-form spectator approximation, in which one quark couples to the virtual photon while the other two act as spectators, and extract charge radii and magnetic moments from the Sachs form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) in the limit \(Q^2 \to 0\).

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: \(r_{ch}^2 = 0.764\ \text{fm}^2\) (predicted; experimental value \(0.768 \pm 0.001\ \text{fm}^2\))
- Neutron charge radius: \(r_{ch}^2 = -0.113\ \text{fm}^2\) (predicted; experimental value \(-0.116 \pm 0.002\ \text{fm}^2\))
- Proton magnetic moment: \(\mu = 2.793\ \mu_N\) (predicted; experimental value \(2.793\ \mu_N\))
- Neutron magnetic moment: \(\mu = -1.913\ \mu_N\) (predicted; experimental value \(-1.913\ \mu_N\))
- \(\Delta^{+}\) magnetic moment: \(\mu = 2.70\ \mu_N\) (predicted; nonrelativistic impulse approximation gives \(2.08\ \mu_N\), a deviation of about 30%)
- \(\Omega^{-}\) magnetic moment: \(\mu = -2.09\ \mu_N\) (predicted; experimental value \(-2.02 \pm 0.05\ \mu_N\))

## Context

This work extends the Graz group's earlier covariant calculations of nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors (Wagenbrunn et al., 2001) and axial form factors (Göckeler et al., 2001) to all octet and decuplet baryon ground states. It demonstrates that the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model, previously successful for baryon spectroscopy (Glozman et al., 1998), can simultaneously reproduce charge radii and magnetic moments without additional phenomenological quark form factors. The results enable future covariant calculations of baryon transition form factors and electroweak properties of excited baryon states.
