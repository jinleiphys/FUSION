# Mini-jet Clustering Algorithm Using Transverse-momentum Seeds in High-energy Nuclear Collisions

> We propose an algorithm to detect mini-jet clusters in high-energy nuclear collisions, by selecting a high-transverse-momentum ($p_T$) particle as a seed and assigning a clustering radius ($R$) in the pseudorapidity and azimuthal-angle space. Our PYTHIA simulations for $p$+$p$ collisions show that a scheme with a seeding $p_T$ of around 0.5 GeV/$c$ and $R$ of approximately 0.6 satisfactorily identifies mini-jet clusters. The correlation between clusters obtained in PYTHIA calculations using the algorithm exhibits the proper behavior of hard-scattering-like processes, suggesting its usefulness in isolating mini-jet-like clusters from non-hard-scattering soft processes when applied to actual nuclear-collision data, thereby allowing a closer examination of both the mini-jet and the soft mechanisms.

## Key claim

The authors propose a clustering algorithm that uses high-transverse-momentum particles as seeds to identify mini-jet clusters in high-energy nuclear collisions, with PYTHIA simulations showing that a seeding $p_T$ of around 0.5 GeV/$c$ and clustering radius $R$ of approximately 0.6 satisfactorily identifies mini-jet clusters, though the algorithm's effectiveness in distinguishing mini-jets from soft processes depends on the chosen $p_{T0}$ threshold and becomes more prominent at higher thresholds such as 1.5 GeV/$c$.

## Method

The algorithm selects particles above a transverse-momentum threshold $p_{T0}$ as seed particles in pseudorapidity ($\eta$) and azimuthal-angle ($\phi$) space, then merges clusters whose centers are closer than a distance threshold of 0.816 (derived from the distance distribution within a uniform disk of radius $R=0.6$). After merging, all particles within a radius of 0.6 around each seed cluster center are used to refine the cluster positions using a weighted average with a power index $\gamma=2$. The authors evaluated the algorithm using PYTHIA8.1 simulations of $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, comparing same-event correlations with mixed-event correlations for both two-particle and two-cluster analyses.

## Key numbers

- Seeding $p_T$ threshold of approximately 0.5 GeV/$c$ and clustering radius $R$ of approximately 0.6 satisfactorily identify mini-jet clusters in PYTHIA simulations
- Merging threshold of 0.816 corresponds to approximately 83.4% probability that two points randomly sampled from a circle of radius $R=0.6$ belong to the same mini-jet
- Two-particle correlation ratio away-side ridge magnitude of approximately 1.04 for $p_{T0}=0.5$ GeV/$c$ and approximately 1.15 for $p_{T0}=1.5$ GeV/$c$
- Two-cluster correlation ratio away-side ridge magnitude of approximately 1.25 for $p_{T0}=0.5$ GeV/$c$ and approximately 1.4 for $p_{T0}=1.5$ GeV/$c$ with $K=2$
- Ratio $K_{\rm same}/K_{\rm mixed}$ decreases from 0.98 to 0.94 with increasing multiplicity for $p_{T0}=0.5$ GeV/$c$, and remains roughly constant around 0.86 for $p_{T0}=1.5$ GeV/$c$
- PYTHIA simulations performed for $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV within the range $-1 \leq \eta \leq 1$ and $-\pi \leq \phi \leq \pi$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous $k$-means clustering approach for identifying mini-jet-like clusters in high-energy $p$+$p$ collisions (Wong 2020), which found that multiple mini-jet-like clusters arise in high-multiplicity events but that randomly produced particles showed comparable clustering behavior. The new $p_T$-seeded algorithm addresses the need to distinguish bona fide mini-jets from random clusters by requiring each mini-jet to contain at least one high-$p_T$ seed particle. The algorithm enables closer examination of both mini-jet dynamics and soft processes in nuclear collisions, building on earlier work establishing mini-jet dominance extending to $p_T$ regions of a few tenths of a GeV/$c$ in high-energy collisions (Wong 2013, Wong 2015).
