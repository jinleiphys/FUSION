# Configuration mixing in low-lying spectra of carbon hypernuclei

> We perform a coupled-channels study of the low-lying states in $^{13,15,17,19}_{~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~Λ}$C with a covariant energy density functional based microscopic particle-core coupling model. The energy differences of $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states in $^{13}_Λ$C and $^{15}_Λ$C are predicted to be 0.25 MeV and 0.34 MeV, respectively. We find that configuration mixings in the $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states of $^{15}_Λ$C are the weakest among those of $^{13,15,17,19}_{~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~Λ}$C. It indicates that $^{15}_Λ$C provides the best candidate among the carbon hypernuclei to study the spin-orbit splitting of $p_Λ$ hyperon state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the previously measured energy difference between the \(1/2^-_1\) and \(3/2^-_1\) states in \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C likely underestimates the true spin-orbit splitting of the single-\(p_\Lambda\) state, because configuration mixing in that nucleus is non-negligible; instead, \(^{15}_\Lambda\)C, with about 97% purity in the relevant configurations, is a better candidate for extracting that splitting.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic particle-core coupling model built on a multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT). The core-nucleus states for \(^{12,14,16,18}\)C were first obtained from angular-momentum and particle-number projected relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations, and then the \(\Lambda\) hyperon was coupled to these core states via a relativistic point-coupling \(N\Lambda\) interaction. The coupled-channels equations for the \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei \(^{13,15,17,19}_\Lambda\)C were solved by expanding the \(\Lambda\) radial wave functions on a spherical harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between the \(1/2^-_1\) and \(3/2^-_1\) states in \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C is predicted to be 0.253 MeV, close to the experimental value of 0.152(90) MeV.
- The energy difference between the \(1/2^-_1\) and \(3/2^-_1\) states in \(^{15}_\Lambda\)C is predicted to be 0.344 MeV.
- The energy differences for \(^{17}_\Lambda\)C and \(^{19}_\Lambda\)C are only 0.067 MeV and 0.033 MeV, respectively.
- The calculated \(\Lambda\) binding energy for the ground state of \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C is 13.22 MeV (compared to the data 11.38(5) MeV).
- The probability of the dominant \([\Lambda p_{1/2} \otimes 0^+_1]\) configuration in the \(1/2^-_1\) state of \(^{15}_\Lambda\)C is 0.9643, and the probability of the \([\Lambda p_{3/2} \otimes 0^+_1]\) configuration in the \(3/2^-_1\) state of \(^{15}_\Lambda\)C is 0.9738.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic particle-core coupling model previously applied to other hypernuclei (Mei 2014, Mei 2015, Mei 2016) and builds on the multi-reference covariant density functional theory that successfully described carbon isotopes (Yao 2011). It directly challenges the earlier interpretation by Auerbach (1981) and Tanida (2001) that the \(1/2^-_1\) and \(3/2^-_1\) states in \(^{13}_\Lambda\)C are pure single-particle configurations, showing instead that configuration mixing from the \(2^+_1\) core state is significant. The results suggest that a new \(\gamma\)-ray spectroscopy measurement for \(^{15}_\Lambda\)C, feasible at the J-PARC facility, would provide a cleaner determination of the \(p_\Lambda\) spin-orbit splitting.
