# Nuclear Schiff Moments and CP Violation

> This paper reviews the calculation of nuclear Schiff moments, which one must know in order to interpret experiments that search for time-reversal-violating electric dipole moments in certain atoms and molecules. After briefly reviewing the connection between dipole moments and CP violation in and beyond the Standard Model of particle physics, Schiff's theorem, which concerns the screening of nuclear electric dipole moments by electrons, Schiff moments, and experiments to measure dipole moments in atoms and molecules, the paper examines attempts to compute Schiff moments in nuclei such as $^{199}$Hg and octupole-deformed isotopes such as $^{225}$Ra, which are particularly useful in experiments. It then turns to ab initio nuclear-structure theory, describing ways in which both the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group and coupled-cluster theory can be used to compute important Schiff moments more accurately than the less controlled methods that have been applied so far.

# Nuclear Schiff Moments and CP Violation

## Key claim

This review argues that nuclear Schiff moments, radially weighted nuclear electric dipole moments, are essential for interpreting experiments searching for time-reversal-violating electric dipole moments in atoms and molecules, and that *ab initio* nuclear-structure methods (In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group and coupled-cluster theory) can compute these moments more accurately than the less controlled phenomenological approaches used previously, though the uncertainty associated with nuclear-structure methods remains particularly large.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework connecting CP-violating physics beyond the Standard Model to nuclear Schiff moments through chiral effective field theory (χEFT), which parameterizes parity- and time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions in terms of unknown couplings (ḡ₀, ḡ₁, ḡ₂, C̄₁, C̄₂). It then surveys computational approaches for nuclear Schiff moments: simple single-particle estimates, the phenomenological shell model applied to ¹²⁹Xe and ¹⁹⁹Hg, random-phase approximation and density-functional theory in spherical and deformed nuclei, and *ab initio* methods including the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group and coupled-cluster theory.

## Key numbers

- The neutron EDM experimental upper limit is less than about 1.8 × 10⁻²⁶ e·cm, implying the QCD θ̄ parameter is ≲ 10⁻¹⁰
- The Standard Model CKM phase contribution to the neutron EDM is about 10⁻³² e·cm
- The most sensitive atomic EDM limit comes from ¹⁹⁹Hg: |d| < 7.4 × 10⁻³⁰ e·cm
- The ¹²⁹Xe atomic EDM limit is |d| < 1.4 × 10⁻²⁷ e·cm
- For the θ̄ source, lattice QCD gives ḡ₀ = (15.5 ± 2.6) × 10⁻³ θ̄
- For the θ̄ source, resonance saturation gives ḡ₁/ḡ₀ ≈ −0.2
- The atomic EDM induced by the nucleus is reduced by a factor of about 10⁻³ relative to the unscreened case (not the naive 10⁻⁸–10⁻⁹ from simple scaling)
- A simple estimate for the Schiff moment in odd-proton nuclei gives S^ch ≈ |e| × [1 ± (j + 1/2)]/(j + 1) × A²/³ × 10⁻² ε fm³

## Context

This review extends the line of work on Schiff moments and CP violation that began with Schiff's theorem (Schiff, 1963) and the derivation of the Schiff moment operator (Flambaum, 1984; Khriplovich, 1997). It builds on chiral effective field theory formulations of parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear forces (Maekawa, 2011; De Vries, 2020) and lattice QCD calculations of the θ̄-source coupling ḡ₀ (Borsanyi, 2015; Brantley, 2016). The review enables more reliable interpretation of ongoing and future EDM experiments in diamagnetic atoms (¹⁹⁹Hg, ¹²⁹Xe) and octupole-deformed isotopes (²²⁵Ra, ²²³Ra), as well as experiments using polar molecules, by providing a roadmap for reducing nuclear-structure uncertainties through *ab initio* methods.
