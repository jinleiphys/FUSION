# Spectral properties of the Goldstino in supersymmetric Bose-Fermi mixtures

> We evaluate the spectral properties of the Goldstino in a Bose-Fermi mixture of cold atoms and molecules whose dynamics is governed by a supersymmetric hamiltonian. Model independent results are obtained from sum rules obeyed by the spectral function. We carry out specific calculations, at zero and finite temperature, using the Random Phase Approximation, and obtain in particular analytic expressions for the dispersion relation at small momentum. These explicit calculations allow us to pin down more precisely the features of the Goldstino that can be attributed to the supersymmetry alone, together with its spontaneous breaking. The anomalous large effect of the Fermi sea at moderate and large momenta is emphasized.

## Key claim

The Goldstino excitation exists as a sharp mode at zero momentum with energy equal to the difference of fermion and boson chemical potentials, but at finite momentum its properties are strongly modified by the presence of the Fermi sea, and the simple expectation that the Goldstino exhausts the spectral sum rule at all momenta fails in generic situations with finite fermion density.

## Method

The authors evaluate the retarded propagator of the supercharge operator using the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) in a two-dimensional continuum model of a Bose-Fermi mixture with supersymmetric Hamiltonian (equal hopping parameters and equal boson-boson and boson-fermion interaction strengths). They derive model-independent sum rules from the canonical (anti)commutation relations and the continuity equation for the supercharge, then perform explicit RPA calculations at zero and finite temperature to obtain the spectral function and dispersion relation at small momentum.

## Key numbers

* The Goldstino pole at zero momentum is located at \(\omega = \Delta\mu\), where \(\Delta\mu = \mu_f - \mu_b\) is the difference of chemical potentials; the residue equals the total density \(\rho = \rho_f + \rho_b\).
* The second sum rule gives \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{d\omega}{2\pi} \omega \sigma(\omega, \mathbf{p}) = \rho \Delta\mu + t_{\rm h} p^2 (\rho_f - \rho_b)\), where \(t_{\rm h}\) is the hopping parameter and \(p = |\mathbf{p}|\).
* In the free case (\(U=0\)) with \(\rho_f = 0.5\) and \(\rho_b = 1\) (in units where \(t_{\rm h}=1\)), the continuum of boson particle-fermion hole excitations has support \(t_{\rm h}p^2 - 2t_{\rm h}k_F p \le \tilde{\omega} \le t_{\rm h}p^2 + 2t_{\rm h}k_F p\), where \(\tilde{\omega} = \omega - \Delta\mu^0\) and \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum.
* At zero temperature with interactions, the chemical potential difference becomes \(\Delta\mu = \epsilon_F - U\rho\), where \(\epsilon_F = t_{\rm h}k_F^2\) is the Fermi energy and \(U\) is the interaction strength.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of Goldstino excitations in supersymmetric Bose-Fermi mixtures, building on the experimental proposal of Shi (2009) for observing the Goldstino in cold atoms on optical lattices. The authors provide explicit RPA calculations that complement the model-independent sum-rule approach and the effective action construction of Bradlyn (2015). The results enable comparison with genuine supersymmetric relativistic quantum field theories studied by Kratzert (2003) and with analogous collective fermionic excitations (the "plasmino") in the quark-gluon plasma studied by Blaizot (2001).
