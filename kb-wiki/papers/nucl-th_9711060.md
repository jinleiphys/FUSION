# Two-Neutrino Double Beta Decay: Critical Analysis

> We have performed a critical analysis of different approximation schemes for the calculation of two-neutrino double beta decay (TNDBD) matrix elements. We have shown that within the single-particle approximation of nuclear Hamiltonian the TNDBD matrix element is equal to zero. The (renormalized) quasiboson approximation scheme imply for TNDBD transition operator to be a constant, if one requires the equivalence of initial and final (renormalized) QRPA Hamiltonians. It means that TNDBD is a higher order process in the boson expansion of the nuclear Hamiltonian. We have found that the mismatching of both Hamiltonians is getting worse with increasing strength of particle- particle interaction especially in the case of QRPA Hamiltonians. It is supposed to be one of the reasons of the extreme sensitivity of studied matrix element to the residual interaction appearing in explicit calculations involving the intermediate nucleus. Further, the Operator Expansion Method (OEM) has been reconsidered and new transition operators have been rederived in a consistent way. The validity of the OEM approximation has been discussed in respect to the other approximation schemes. The OEM combined with QRPA or RQRPA ground state wave functions reflects sensitively the instabilities incorporated in the considered ground states. Therefore, the predicting power of the OEM should be studied with help of other ground state wave functions. e.g. shell model ones.

## Key claim

Within the quasiboson approximation (QBA) and renormalized QBA (RQBA) schemes, the two-neutrino double beta decay transition operator reduces to a constant if one requires equivalence of the initial and final QRPA or RQRPA Hamiltonians, meaning that two-neutrino double beta decay is a higher-order process in the boson expansion of the nuclear Hamiltonian and its higher-order boson approximations are important.

## Method

The authors performed a critical analysis of approximation schemes for two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements using the time integral representation of the matrix element. They examined the single-particle Hamiltonian approximation, the quasiboson approximation (QBA) and renormalized QBA (RQBA) schemes underlying QRPA and RQRPA methods, and the Operator Expansion Method (OEM). They derived conditions for equivalence of initial and final QRPA and RQRPA Hamiltonians and tested these conditions numerically for the decay of 76Ge using three different model spaces.

## Context

This work extends the critical analysis of QRPA-based methods for two-neutrino double beta decay, building on earlier studies by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) and subsequent work by Toivanen and Suhonen (1995) who proposed the renormalized QRPA. The paper identifies that the suppression of the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element is connected to it being a higher-order effect in boson expansion, which explains the extreme sensitivity to the particle-particle interaction strength observed in explicit calculations. The analysis enables future work using fermion-boson mapping procedures as proposed by Sambataro, Catara and Suhonen within a schematic model, and suggests that the OEM should be studied with other ground state wave functions such as shell model ones.
