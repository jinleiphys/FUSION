# The Joint Physics Analysis Center: Recent results

> We review some of the recent achievements of the Joint Physics Analysis Center, a theoretical collaboration with ties to experimental collaborations, that aims to provide amplitudes suitable for the analysis of the current and forthcoming experimental data on hadron physics. Since its foundation in 2013, the group is focused on hadron spectroscopy in preparation for the forthcoming high statistics and high precision experimental data from BELLEII, BESIII, CLAS12, COMPASS, GlueX, LHCb and (hopefully) PANDA collaborations. So far, we have developed amplitudes for $πN$ scattering, $\bar{K}N$ scattering, pion and $J/ψ$ photoproduction, two kaon photoproduction and three-body decays of light mesons ($η$, $ω$, $ϕ$). The codes for the amplitudes are available to download from the group web page and can be straightforwardly incorporated to the analysis of the experimental data.

## Key claim
The Joint Physics Analysis Center (JPAC) provides theoretically rigorous scattering amplitudes for hadron spectroscopy, but the results are explicitly preparatory: they are designed to be incorporated into future analyses of high-statistics data from upcoming experiments (BELLEII, BESIII, CLAS12, COMPASS, GlueX, LHCb, PANDA), and the existence of exotic states such as pentaquarks remains conditional on further confirmation.

## Method
JPAC develops amplitudes using S-matrix principles (analyticity, crossing symmetry, unitarity) with K-matrix and N/D parametrizations, supplemented by chiral symmetry constraints at low energies or Regge asymptotics at high energies. For meson-baryon scattering, they constructed coupled-channel models fitted to single-energy partial waves via genetic algorithms and MINUIT, with uncertainties estimated by bootstrap. For photoproduction, they modeled background with Regge or Pomeron exchange and introduced resonances as Breit-Wigner amplitudes, then compared to existing experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For $\eta \to 3\pi$ decay, a global fit to KLOE-2 and WASA-at-COSY data determined $Q = 21.6 \pm 0.4$, where $Q$ relates to the light-quark mass difference.
- For $J/\psi$ photoproduction, data allow a pentaquark with $J/\psi p$ branching ratio below 30% for $J^P=3/2^-$ and below 17% for $J^P=5/2^+$ at 95% confidence level.
- The high-energy neutral pion photoproduction model covers the $E_\gamma > 4$ GeV region.
- The $\bar{K}N$ scattering model incorporates up to 13 channels per partial wave.

## Context
This work extends the JPAC collaboration's program (founded 2013) of providing amplitudes for hadron spectroscopy, building on prior approaches such as the Kent State University single-energy partial waves (Manley, 2013) and the low-energy $\pi N$ amplitudes from RLW (2012). The results enable future experimental analyses by BELLEII, BESIII, CLAS12, COMPASS, GlueX, LHCb, and PANDA to extract resonance properties and test for exotic hadrons, including the two $\Lambda(1405)$ states (where the higher-mass pole appears mostly three-quark and the lower-mass pole either a molecule or pentaquark) and the $P_c(4450)$ pentaquark candidate.
