# KKbar photoproduction from protons

> We study the contribution of the Drell mechanism driven by K^+ and K^- exchange to the reaction gamma N -> KKbar N. Our calculation implements the full KN and KbarN reaction amplitudes in the form of partial wave amplitudes taken from a meson-exchange model (KN) and a partial wave analysis (KbarN), respectively. Comparing our results to data of the LAMP2 collaboration we observe that the Drell mechanism alone cannot describe the large Lambda(1520) photoproduction rate observed experimentally. We argue that the discrepancy could be due to significant contributions from K*-meson exchange with subsequent excitation of the Lambda(1520) resonance. After adding such contributions to our model a good agreement of the LAMP2 experiment is achieved. When applying the same model to the recent SAPHIR data we find an excellent description of the K^+p spectrum and can determine the parameters of the Lambda(1600) P01 resonance, M_R = 1617 +/- 2 MeV and Gamma_R = 117 +/- 4 MeV, from the K^-p mass distribution.

## Key claim

The Drell mechanism alone, which incorporates full KN and KbarN scattering amplitudes, cannot describe the large Lambda(1520) photoproduction rate observed in the LAMP2 experiment; additional contributions from K*-meson exchange with subsequent excitation of the Lambda(1520) resonance are required to achieve agreement with data.

## Method

The authors calculated the Drell mechanism for gamma N -> K Kbar N using K+ and K- exchange, implementing full KN and KbarN reaction amplitudes as partial wave amplitudes from a meson-exchange model (KN) and a partial wave analysis (KbarN), respectively. They then added K*-meson exchange contributions for Lambda(1520) production and fitted the unknown parameters to the available gamma p -> K+ Lambda(1520) data. The model was applied to LAMP2 data at photon energies 2.8 to 4.8 GeV and to SAPHIR data at 1.74 to 2.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Lambda(1600) P01 resonance mass: M_R = 1617 +/- 2 MeV, extracted from the K-p mass distribution in SAPHIR data
- Lambda(1600) P01 resonance width: Gamma_R = 117 +/- 4 MeV, extracted from the same data
- Cutoff parameter t_0 = 0.33 GeV^2, corresponding to a cutoff mass of Lambda = 0.76 GeV in a standard monopole form factor, determined by fitting the total gamma p -> K+ K- p cross section
- Photon energy range for LAMP2 experiment: 2.8 < E_gamma < 4.8 GeV
- Photon energy range for SAPHIR experiment: 1.74 <= E_gamma <= 2.6 GeV
- Lambda(1520) nominal mass: 1519.5 +/- 1.0 MeV and full width 15.6 +/- 1.0 MeV (from PDG)

## Context

This work extends the Soding model (Soding, 1960) and Drell mechanism approach, previously applied to gamma p -> pi+ pi- p, to the gamma N -> K Kbar N reaction. It is complementary to studies by Roberts, Oset, Oh, Nam, Grishina, and Titov (all around 2004-2005) that considered various reaction mechanisms but did not use the full KN and KbarN amplitudes. The model enables reliable evaluation of background contributions from K exchange in analyses of experimental data, and demonstrates the potential for extracting hyperon resonance parameters from photoproduction data, as shown with the Lambda(1600) determination from SAPHIR data.
