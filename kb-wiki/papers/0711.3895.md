# Hidden Local Symmetry and Dense Half-Skyrmion Matter

> Transition from baryonic matter to color-flavor-locked quark matter is described in terms of skyrmion matter changing into half-skyrmion matter. The intermediate phase between the density $n_p$ at which a skyrmion turns into two half skyrmions and the chiral transition density $n_c^{χSR}$ at which hadronic matter changes over to quark matter corresponds to a chiral symmetry restored phase characterized by a vanishing quark condensate and a {\em non-vanishing} pion decay constant. When hidden local fields are incorporated, the vector manifestation of Harada-Yamawaki HLS theory implies that as density approaches $n_c^{χSR}$, the gauge coupling $g$ goes to zero (in the chiral limit) and the symmetry "swells" to $SU(N_f)^4$ as proposed by Georgi for the "vector limit." This enhanced symmetry, not present in QCD, can be interpreted as "emergent" in medium due to collective excitations. The fractionization of skyrmions into half-skyrmions resembles closely the magnetic Néel--to-valence bond solid (VBS) paramagnet transition where "baby" half-skyrmions enter as relevant degrees of freedom in the intermediate phase. It is suggested that the half-skyrmion phase in dense matter corresponds to the "hadronic freedom" regime that plays a singularly important role in inducing kaon condensation that leads to the collapse of massive compact stars into black holes..

## Key claim

The transition from baryonic matter to color-flavor-locked quark matter proceeds through an intermediate half-skyrmion phase where chiral symmetry is restored (vanishing quark condensate) but the pion decay constant remains non-zero, and when hidden local symmetry is incorporated, the gauge coupling goes to zero near the chiral transition density, producing an emergent $SU(N_f)^4$ symmetry not present in QCD.

## Method

The author simulates dense baryonic matter by placing skyrmions on a face-centered cubic crystal lattice and squeezing the crystal to increase density, using the Skyrme Lagrangian with a dilaton scalar field that encodes the trace anomaly of QCD. The energy density is minimized with respect to Fourier coefficients of the mean fields as variational parameters to determine the ground state configuration at each density. The analysis is then extended by incorporating hidden local symmetry with vector mesons (rho and omega) via the Harada-Yamawaki HLS Lagrangian, which is treated as a truncation of the infinite tower of vector mesons with higher-lying states integrated out.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density: $n_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- The density $n_p$ at which a skyrmion turns into two half-skyrmions occurs below the chiral transition density $n_c^{\chi SR}$
- Kaon condensation is predicted at $n_c^K \sim 3n_0$
- For high temperature, the flash temperature is $T_{flash} \simeq 125$ MeV and the chiral transition temperature is $T_c^{\chi SR} \simeq 175$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach of Klebanov (1985) and the FCC lattice calculations of the Skyrme matter collaboration, and connects to the Harada-Yamawaki (1996) vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry and Georgi's (1990) vector limit. The half-skyrmion phase is identified with the "hadronic freedom" regime previously invoked to explain the STAR $\rho^0/\pi^-$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions and to predict kaon condensation (Brown, Lee, Rho 2007). The fractionization of skyrmions into half-skyrmions is compared to the deconfined quantum critical phenomenon in condensed matter, specifically the Neel-to-valence bond solid transition described by Senthil et al. (2004). The work enables a unified description of the transition from nuclear matter to color-flavor-locked quark matter and suggests that the pseudogap phase could significantly affect the equation of state for compact stars, potentially leading to black hole collapse via kaon condensation.
