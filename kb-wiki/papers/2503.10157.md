# Exploring the multiplicity dependence of the flavor hierarchy for hadron productions in high energy pp collisions

> In this work, we perform a systematic study on the multiplicity dependence of hadron productions at mid-rapidity ($|y|<0.5$), ranging from the light to the charm sector in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV. This study utilizes a multi-phase transport model (AMPT) coupled with PYTHIA8 initial conditions. We have investigated the baryon to meson ratios as well as the strange to non-strange meson ratios varying with the charged particle density. By tuning the coalescence parameters, the AMPT model provides a reasonable description to the experimental data for inclusive productions of both light and charm hadrons, comparable to the string fragmentation model calculations with color reconnection effects. Additionally, we have analyzed the relative production of hadrons by examining self-normalized particle ratios as a function of the charged hadron density. Our findings suggest that parton evolution effects and the coalescence hadronization process in AMPT model lead to a strong flavor hierarchy in the multiplicity dependence of the baryon to meson ratio. Furthermore, our investigation on the $p_T$ differential double ratio of baryon to meson fraction between high and low multiplicity events indicates distinct modifications to the flavor associated baryon to meson ratio $p_T$ shape in high multiplicity events when comparing the coalescence hadronization model to the color reconnection model. These observations highlight the importance of understanding the hadronization process in high-energy proton-proton collisions through a comprehensive multiplicity dependent multi-flavor analysis.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with parton coalescence hadronization produces a strong flavor hierarchy in the multiplicity dependence of baryon-to-meson ratios (p/π, Λ/K_S^0, Λ_c^+/D^0), with the slope increasing from light to charm hadrons, whereas the color reconnection model in PYTHIA8 predicts a similar magnitude of multiplicity dependence for non-strange and strange baryon-to-meson ratios without a clear ordering.

## Method

The authors used the string melting AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with PYTHIA8 initial conditions to simulate pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV, applying parton cascade (ZPC) with a parton scattering cross section σ = 0.15 mb and a spatial coalescence hadronization model with flavor-dependent coalescence parameters (r_BM = 0.53 for non-strange light quarks, r_BM^s = 0.9 for strange quarks, r_BM^c = 1.4 for charm quarks). They compared results from three AMPT configurations (noFSI, pFSI with parton rescattering only, allFSI with both parton and hadron rescattering) to PYTHIA8 with beyond leading color reconnection and to ALICE experimental data for eight hadron species.

## Key numbers

- Parton-parton scattering cross section set to σ = 0.15 mb, chosen to describe elliptic flow measurements in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV
- Coalescence parameters: r_BM = 0.53 (non-strange light flavor), r_BM^s = 0.9 (strange quark involved), r_BM^c = 1.4 (charm quark involved)
- Inelastic cross section σ_inel = 77.6 mb used to convert ALICE charm hadron cross sections to yields
- Mid-rapidity range |y| < 0.5 for all particle species studied
- High multiplicity events defined as 0-5% centrality, low multiplicity as 64.5-100% centrality for double ratio analysis

## Context

This work extends the study of multiplicity-dependent hadron production from light flavors (previously measured by ALICE (2020) for pions, kaons, protons, K_S^0, and Λ) to charm hadrons (D^0, D_s^±, Λ_c^±) in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV. It compares two competing theoretical frameworks: the coalescence hadronization model (AMPT) which assumes deconfined quark matter formation, and the color reconnection model (PYTHIA8) which modifies string fragmentation in dense environments. The results enable future experimental tests to distinguish between these mechanisms by measuring the flavor hierarchy pattern in baryon-to-meson ratios across multiplicity classes, particularly for charm hadrons where the two models predict different saturation behavior at high multiplicity.
