# Phenomenology of Strangeness enhancement in heavy ion collisions

> We give an overview of the phenomenology of strangeness enhancement in heavy ion collisions, within the paradigm of the statistical model of particle production. We argue that, while strangeness enhancement data is suggestive of a phase transition, the mechanism triggering enhancement is still elusive. We study the feasibility to constrain this mechanism by determining the scaling variable of strangeness enhancement. We further argue that to test the applicability of the statistical model generally, and to confirm our interpretation of the statistical physics responsible for strangeness enhancement, the scaling of strange particle fluctuations ($K/π$ and other particles) w.r.t. yields has to be explored.

## Key claim

While strangeness enhancement data is suggestive of a phase transition, the mechanism triggering enhancement is still elusive; different statistical model scenarios (canonical suppression, chemical equilibration in a quark-gluon plasma, or oversaturation from a fast transition) predict different scaling variables for the enhancement, and these can be distinguished by studying the scaling of strange particle fluctuations with yields.

## Method

The paper reviews the statistical model of particle production, parameterizing particle abundances via temperature, fugacities, and phase space occupancy factors (gamma_q, gamma_s), and examines how strangeness enhancement E_Y scales with net strangeness (s - s_bar) versus total strangeness (s + s_bar) of the hadron. It then proposes that the observable Psi = <N_1> * sigma_dyn(N_1/N_2), where sigma_dyn is the dynamical fluctuation width, should be independent of multiplicity and centrality if the statistical model holds, and its value can be calculated from the same parameters used for yields.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement E_Y is defined as E_Y = (<N_part^{p-A}> / <N_part^{A-A}>) * (<N_Y^{A-A}> / <N_Y^{p-A}>) or equivalently using dN/dη.
- In the canonical suppression scenario, E_Y scales as (N_part^{AA} / N_part^{p-A})^{|Q_Y - Q_bar_Y|}, where Q is the net charge.
- In the chemical equilibration scenario, E_Y scales as (gamma_s^{A-A} / gamma_s^{p-A})^{s + s_bar}, with gamma_s approaching 1 as N_part → ∞.
- The dimensionless parameter alpha = [chi_s(T,mu_B) / rho_s(T,mu_B)] * [dT/dtau + dmu_B/dtau] * tau_s determines whether the system adjusts smoothly (alpha << 1) or produces the cold phase out of chemical equilibrium (alpha >> 1).
- The observable Psi^{pi-}_{K-/pi-} for top centrality RHIC matches non-equilibrium freeze-out expectations and is significantly above equilibrium freeze-out.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach to strangeness enhancement (Rafelski, 1982; Becattini, 2006; Braun-Munzinger, 2004) by reviewing competing interpretations: canonical suppression (Cleymans, 1999) versus faster equilibration in a quark-gluon plasma versus oversaturation from a fast transition (Janssen, 2008). It proposes that fluctuation scaling (Gazdzicki, 1995; Jeon, 2000) can falsify these models, and notes that a centrality scan of Psi^{pi-}_{K-/pi-} at RHIC has not yet been performed. The coming low-energy run at FAIR and the SHINE program at the SPS are identified as optimal opportunities to search for an energy where multi-strange enhancement does not occur.
