# Discovery of the $^{151}$Eu $α$ decay

> We report on the first compelling observation of $α$ decay of $^{151}$Eu to the ground state of $^{147}$Pm. The measurement was performed using a 6.15 g Li$_6$Eu(BO$_3$)$_3$ crystal operated as a scintillating bolometer. The Q-value and half-life measured are: Q = 1948.9$\pm 6.9(stat.) \pm 5.1(syst.)$ keV, and T$_{1/2}=\left( 4.62\pm0.95(stat.)\pm0.68(syst.)\right) \times 10^{18}$ y . The half-life prediction of nuclear theory using the Coulomb and proximity potential model are in good agreement with this experimental result.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first compelling observation of alpha decay of $^{151}$Eu to the ground state of $^{147}$Pm, with a statistical significance of 7.4 sigma, using a 6.15 g Li$_6$Eu(BO$_3$)$_3$ crystal operated as a scintillating bolometer. The abstract glosses over the fact that the measured half-life agrees with the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM) prediction but is incompatible with semi-empirical calculations, and that the Q-value measurement shows a 2-sigma discrepancy with the previously accepted value.

## Method

A 6.15 g Li$_6$Eu(BO$_3$)$_3$ crystal (41.1% Eu by mass) was operated as a scintillating bolometer at millikelvin temperatures in the Gran Sasso underground laboratory for 462.2 hours. The simultaneous readout of heat and scintillation light signals allowed discrimination of alpha events from beta/gamma background via the light yield parameter. An unbinned extended likelihood fit using a double crystal ball response function was applied to the alpha energy spectrum to extract the number of $^{151}$Eu decay events and the Q-value.

## Key numbers

- Q-value: 1948.9 $\pm$ 6.9 (stat.) $\pm$ 5.1 (syst.) keV
- Half-life: T$_{1/2} = (4.62 \pm 0.95 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.68 \text{ (syst.)}) \times 10^{18}$ y
- Statistical significance of the signal: 7.4 sigma (q$_0$ = 54)
- Number of $^{151}$Eu events observed: 37.6 $\pm$ 7.5 counts (after efficiency correction)
- Crystal mass: 6.15 g; number of $^{151}$Eu atoms: (4.76 $\pm$ 0.07) $\times 10^{21}$
- Energy resolution (FWHM) at the $^{151}$Eu peak: 65 $\pm$ 7 keV
- Live time: 462.2 hours
- $^{147}$Sm activity in the crystal: 454 mBq/kg

## Context

This work extends the application of scintillating bolometers to rare alpha decays, following the technique used for $^{209}$Bi (Nature, 2003) and $^{180}$W (W180, 2008). It confirms an earlier indication of $^{151}$Eu alpha decay reported by a CaF$_2$:Eu scintillator experiment (Eu_DAMA, 2012) which gave T$_{1/2}=5^{+11}_{-3}\times10^{18}$ y with large uncertainty. The result favors the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM) over semi-empirical calculations (Tav07, Poe83, Poe85) for odd-mass nuclei, and enables a refined estimate of promethium mass in the Earth's crust (about 565 g total).
