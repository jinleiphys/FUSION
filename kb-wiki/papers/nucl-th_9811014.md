# The interacting boson model with SU(3) charge symmetry and its application to even--even N $\approx$ Z nuclei

> The isospin-invariant interacting boson model IBM-3 is analyzed in situations where $SU_T(3)$ charge symmetry [or, equivalently, $U_L(6)$ $sd$ symmetry] is conserved. Analytic expressions for energies, electromagnetic transitions, two-nucleon transfer probabilities, and boson-number expectation values are obtained for the three possible dynamical symmetry limits, U(5), SU(3), and O(6). Results found in IBM-3 are related to corresponding ones in IBM-1 and IBM-2. Numerical calculations are presented for $f_{7/2}$-shell nuclei and some features that distinguish IBM-3 from its predecessors IBM-1 and IBM-2 are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic expressions for energies, electromagnetic transitions, two-nucleon transfer probabilities, and boson-number expectation values in the IBM-3 under the assumption that $SU_T(3)$ charge symmetry (equivalently $U_L(6)$ symmetry) is conserved, which is an approximation that restricts applicability but is expected to hold approximately for low-energy states in $N\approx Z$ nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyze the algebraic structure of the isospin-invariant interacting boson model IBM-3, which includes a complete isospin triplet of $T=1$ bosons ($\pi$, $\delta$, $\nu$), and consider the dynamical symmetry limits U(5), SU(3), and O(6) arising from the reduction $U(18) \supset U_L(6) \otimes SU_T(3)$. They derive analytic formulas for energies using Casimir operators, compute electromagnetic transition matrix elements (M1, E2, M3) and two-nucleon transfer probabilities for symmetric $[N]$ and non-symmetric $[N-1,1]$ states, and relate these to corresponding results in IBM-1 and IBM-2. Numerical calculations are performed for $f_{7/2}$-shell nuclei to illustrate features distinguishing IBM-3 from its predecessors.

## Key numbers

* For symmetric $[N]$ states in the SU(3) limit, the isovector E2 transition matrix element from the ground state to the first $2^+$ state is proportional to $T/N$ times the corresponding IBM-1 matrix element, where $T$ is the isospin and $N$ the total boson number.
* For symmetric $[N]$ states, the isotensor E2 transition matrix element (same $T$) is proportional to $T(2N+3)/[(2T+3)N]$ times the IBM-1 matrix element.
* For symmetric $[N]$ states, the isotensor E2 transition matrix element connecting $T$ to $T+2$ is proportional to $6\sqrt{(T+1)(N-T)(N+T+3)/[(2T+3)^2(2T+5)N^2]}$ times the IBM-1 matrix element.
* The isovector and isotensor operators belong to the same $SU(3)$ representation (1,1), and their $O_T(3)\subset SU_T(3)$ reduced matrix elements are related by a factor of $-\sqrt{3}$.
* The Majorana interaction operator is given by $\hat{M} = \frac{1}{4}(N(N+5) - \hat{C}_2[U_L(6)])$, where $\hat{C}_2[U_L(6)]$ is the quadratic Casimir operator of $U_L(6)$.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic treatment of the interacting boson model to include isospin symmetry explicitly via IBM-3, building on the earlier IBM-2 formulation by Arima et al. (1977) and Otsuka et al. (1978, 1981) and the introduction of proton-neutron $T=1$ pairs by Elliott and White (1980). The results enable analytic predictions for $N\approx Z$ nuclei where protons and neutrons occupy the same valence shell, complementing numerical IBM-3 calculations by Evans et al. (1985), Abdelaziz et al. (1988, 1989), and Elliott et al. (1996). The derived relations between IBM-3 and IBM-1/IBM-2 matrix elements allow direct comparison of transition strengths across different boson models.
