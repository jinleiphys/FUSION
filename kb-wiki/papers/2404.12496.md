# Constraints on extra dimensions theories from gravitational quantum barrier experiments

> We discuss the quantum-bouncer experiment involving ultracold neutrons in a braneworld scenario. Extra-dimensional theories typically predict the strengthening of gravitational interactions over short distances. In this paper, we specifically study the anomalous gravitational interaction between the bouncing neutron and the reflecting mirror, resulting from hidden dimensions, and its effect on the outcome of this experiment in the context of a thickbrane model. This analysis allows us to identify which physical quantity of this extra-dimensional theory this neutron experiment is capable of constraining. Based on the experimental data, we found a new and independent empirical bound on free parameters of the model: the higher-dimensional gravitational constant and a parameter related to a transverse width of the confined matter inside the thickbrane. This new bound is valid in scenarios with an arbitrary number of extra dimensions greater than two. In this manner, by considering the thickness of the brane, we have been able to extend previous studies on this topic, which were limited to models with few codimensions, due to non-computability problems of power-law corrections of the gravitational potential.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the gravitational quantum barrier (quantum bouncer) experiment with ultracold neutrons can constrain extra-dimensional theories with any number of extra dimensions greater than two, provided the brane thickness is taken into account to avoid divergence problems that previously limited analyses to scenarios with four or fewer extra dimensions.

## Method

The authors model the neutron-mirror system in a thick-brane braneworld scenario, calculating the modified gravitational potential between the neutron and the mirror using the linearized Einstein equations in the weak-field regime. They treat the mirror as a semi-infinite plate with a non-singular matter distribution in the extra dimensions, compute the exterior and interior gravitational potentials, and use first-order perturbation theory to estimate the shift in the quantum bouncer energy levels. The constraint is derived by requiring that the anomalous gravitational interaction does not produce a reduction in the height of the first bound state exceeding the experimental error of 2.5 micrometers.

## Key numbers

- The experimental height of the first quantum state is \(h_{1}^{\exp}=12.2\ \mu\mathrm{m}\pm(1.8_{\mathrm{syst}}+0.7_{\mathrm{stat}})\ \mu\mathrm{m}\), compared to the theoretical height \(h_{1}^{\mathrm{teo}}=13.7\ \mu\mathrm{m}\).
- The natural length scale of the quantum bouncer is \(z_{0}=(\hbar^{2}/2m^{2}g)^{1/3}\approx 5.8\ \mu\mathrm{m}\).
- The condition that the anomalous interaction does not contradict the data requires \(|\langle U_{A}\rangle_{1}|<0.3\ \mathrm{peV}\) for the first state.
- The neutron optical potential of the mirror is approximately \(U_{op}=100\ \mathrm{neV}\).
- The parameter \(\gamma\) relating the brane thickness to the exterior potential is given by \(\gamma=[\Gamma((\delta+1)/2)/((\delta-1)\pi^{1/2})]^{1/(\delta-2)}\) for \(\delta>2\).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of the quantum bouncer experiment that were limited to \(\delta\leq4\) extra dimensions due to divergence problems in the power-law correction of the gravitational potential (Fabien, no year given). The authors show that by incorporating brane thickness, the analysis can be extended to any number of extra dimensions, filling a gap in the literature where no experimental limits existed for power-law corrections with five or more extra dimensions from this neutron experiment. The constraints obtained are less stringent than those from collider data, but the work demonstrates that the existence of bound states for ultracold neutrons does not rule out extra-dimensional theories with five or more hidden dimensions in thick-brane scenarios.
