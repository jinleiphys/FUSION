# Azimuthal angle dependence of the charge imbalance from charge conservation effects

> The experimental search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions is based on charge dependent correlations between emitted particles. Recently, a sensitive observable comparing event-by-event distributions of the charge splitting projected on the directions along and perpendicular to the direction of the elliptic flow has been proposed. The results of a 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic model show that the preliminary experimental data of the STAR Collaboration can be explained as due to background effects, such as resonance decays and local charge conservation in the particle production. A related observable based on the third order harmonic flow is proposed to further investigate such background effects in charge dependent correlations.

## Key claim

The convex shape of the correlator $R(\Delta S)$ that has been proposed as a sensitive probe of the chiral magnetic effect can be qualitatively reproduced by standard charge correlations from resonance decays and local charge conservation alone, without any topological effects.

## Method

The author performed 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2760$ GeV, Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, and p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5020$ GeV, using a quark Glauber Monte Carlo model for initial conditions. Particles were emitted from a freeze-out hypersurface at constant temperature $T=150$ MeV, with charge correlations introduced either through resonance decays alone or through both resonance decays and local charge conservation where particles and antiparticles are emitted in pairs from the same fluid element. The event-by-event distributions of charge splitting projected along and perpendicular to the second and third order event planes were calculated and compared to reshuffled events.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and centrality $30-40\%$, the relative difference of RMS values for in-plane and out-of-plane charge imbalance distributions is $0.022$, close to the experimental value of $0.019$ from STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk 2013)
- The freeze-out temperature used in the hydrodynamic model is $T=150$ MeV
- The acceptance region for charged particles is $|\eta|<1$ and $0.15$ GeV $< p_\perp < 2$ GeV
- The efficiency used in event-plane resolution studies is $80\%$

## Context

This work extends the ongoing search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, which has been studied experimentally by STAR (Abelev 2009, Adamczyk 2013) and CMS (Khachatryan 2016). The paper directly addresses the observable proposed by Magdy (2017) and shows that background effects from resonance decays and local charge conservation, previously studied by Bzdak (2010, 2012) and Schlichting (2010), can reproduce the convex shape of $R(\Delta S)$ seen in preliminary STAR data. The author proposes a new observable based on third order harmonic flow ($\Psi_3$) that could provide an additional test of background effects, though it requires good event-plane resolution to be measurable.
