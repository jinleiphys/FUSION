# On the properties of qudits

> Motivated by the growing interest in the applications of quantum information science in astrophysical settings, especially for the neutrino transport in compact objects where three-flavors of neutrinos need to be mapped on qutrits, we review properties of one- and two-qudit systems. We contrast two-qubit and two-qudits systems by pointing out how some of the properties of two-qubit systems generalize to higher dimensions and explore emerging new properties for dimensions three or higher. One example is provided by the Werner states: when the density operator is written in the fundamental representation, we show that only two-qubit Werner states can be pure states, but not two-qudit Werner states when the qudit dimension is larger than two.

## Key claim

The paper reviews properties of one- and two-qudit systems for applications in neutrino transport in compact objects, showing that while many two-qubit properties generalize to higher dimensions, some features emerge only for dimensions three or higher; notably, only two-qubit Werner states can be pure states when the density operator is written in the fundamental representation, but two-qudit Werner states cannot be pure when the qudit dimension exceeds two.

## Method

The authors write density matrices for single and bipartite qudit systems in the generalized Gell-Mann matrix basis of SU(N). They derive conditions for pure states by imposing ρ² = ρ, express entanglement entropy in terms of SU(N) Casimir invariants, and analyze the positivity constraints via elementary symmetric polynomials of the eigenvalues. For two-qudit systems, they generalize the two-qubit formalism and apply the Werner state invariance condition (U ⊗ U)ρ(U† ⊗ U†) = ρ to determine when pure Werner states exist.

## Key numbers

- For a single qutrit, the norm squared of the polarization vector satisfies |P|² ≤ 3, and the cubic invariant Q obeys −1 ≤ √3 Q/|P|³ ≤ +1.
- For a pure single-qudit state, |P|² = N(N−1)/2, where N is the qudit dimension.
- For a pure two-qubit Werner state, the parameter α = −1 (from α² = N²/4 and α = −N(N²−2)/4, consistent only for N=2).
- For mixed two-qudit Werner states, the necessary condition from e₂ ≥ 0 gives −N/2 < α < +N/2.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of two-qubit systems (reviewed in Fano (1983)) to higher-dimensional qudits, motivated by three-flavor neutrino transport in core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers where qutrits are needed. It complements prior studies of multiqudit systems (Ciavarella (2021), Gustafson (2021), Gonzalez-Cuadra (2022), Zache (2023), Calajo (2024), Illa (2023)) and provides the formal framework for systematic study of three-flavor collective neutrino oscillations on quantum computers, building on preliminary attempts by Siwach (2022), Balantekin (2023), and Nguyen (2022).
