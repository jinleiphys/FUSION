# The Transition Temperature to the Superconducting Phase of QCD at High Baryon Density

> Recent interest in the study of color superconductivity has focused on the regime of high baryon density where perturbative QCD may be employed. Based on the dominant one-gluon-exchange interaction, both the transition temperature and zero temperature gap have been determined to leading order in the coupling, g. While the leading non-BCS behavior, $T_C\simμg^{-5}e^{-κ/g}$, is easily obtained, the pre-exponential factor has proved more difficult to evaluate. Focusing on the transition temperature, we present a perturbative derivation of this factor, exact to leading order in g. This approach is first motivated by the study of a toy model and involves working to second order in the perturbative expansion. We compare this result to the zero temperature gap. Additionally, we extend the analysis to the case of higher angular momentum for longitudinal and transverse quark pairing.

## Key claim

The paper presents a perturbative derivation of the pre-exponential factor for the transition temperature to the color superconducting phase of QCD at high baryon density, exact to leading order in the coupling \(g\), but only for the dominant one-gluon-exchange interaction and in the regime where perturbative QCD is valid.

## Method

The authors formulate the problem in the normal phase using a Schwinger-Dyson equation for the proper four-fermion vertex function, which is cast into a Fredholm integral equation and then into a general eigenvalue problem. They solve this eigenvalue problem perturbatively to second order in the coupling, working in the Coulomb gauge with hard dense loops included in the gluon propagator, and focusing on the antisymmetric color channel for longitudinal and transverse quark pairings of arbitrary angular momentum \(J\).

## Key numbers

* For \(SU(3)_c\) QCD, the pre-factor \({\mathcal C}_1\) is \(0.629\) (dimensionless), arising from radiative corrections to the quark propagator and sub-leading logarithmic infrared behavior.
* The leading-order factor in the transition temperature expression is \(\Delta_0 = 256 \pi^4 \left( \frac{2}{N_f} \right)^{\frac{5}{2}} \frac{\mu}{g^5} e^{-\sqrt{6N\over N+1}{\pi^2\over g}}\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential and \(g\) is the running coupling at \(\mu\).
* The ratio of the transition temperature to the zero-temperature gap in the \(s\)-wave channel is \(k_BT = {e^\gamma\over\pi}\Delta\) with \(\gamma=0.5772\ldots\) (the Euler constant), identical to the BCS case.
* For longitudinal pairs with \(J=0\), the angular-momentum-dependent pre-factor is \({\mathcal C}_2^{J=0} = 1\); for transverse pairs with \(J=0\), \({\mathcal C}_2^{J=0}=0\).
* Higher angular-momentum channels are suppressed: \(p\)-wave pairing is down by about three orders of magnitude relative to \(s\)-wave.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of color superconductivity at high density by Son (1998), Schafer and Wilczek (1999), and Pisarski and Rischke (1999), who first obtained the non-BCS exponential form \(\Delta_0 \sim \mu g^{-5} e^{-\kappa/g}\) using renormalization group methods. The paper provides the exact pre-exponential factor for the transition temperature, which had been difficult to evaluate, and confirms the leading-order ratio of \(T_C\) to the zero-temperature gap found by Pisarski and Rischke (1999). This enables more precise quantitative predictions for the phase diagram of dense QCD matter, relevant for neutron star cores and heavy ion collisions.
