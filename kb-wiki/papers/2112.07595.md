# QCD at high temperature and density: selected highlights

> I review some of the recent progress in QCD at high temperature and density, with a focus on the nature of the high-temperature transition; cold and dense matter; and hadron properties and transport coefficients at high temperature.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress in QCD at high temperature and density, but the author explicitly states that the overview is "necessarily selective" and omits important topics such as the QCD equation of state, QCD in nonzero magnetic fields, topology, deconfinement, and non-equilibrium dynamics.

## Method

The review synthesizes results from lattice QCD simulations (using staggered, Wilson-clover, and Wilson fermion formulations), Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), functional renormalisation group (FRG) methods, and perturbation theory. It covers determinations of the chiral transition temperature, the curvature of the transition line at nonzero chemical potential, complex Langevin simulations at large density, strong coupling expansions, and spectral function reconstructions for hadron properties and transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential: T_chi = 153-158 MeV at the physical point, with critical temperature in the chiral limit T_0 = 130-140 MeV
- Curvature parameter kappa_2 from lattice QCD: consistent across continuum-extrapolated results; kappa_4 consistent with zero within errors
- Upper limit for critical pion mass separating first-order transition from crossover: m_pi^c less than or equal to 50 MeV from highly improved staggered fermions, and less than or equal to 110 MeV from Wilson-clover fermions
- Complex Langevin simulations achieved pion masses down to approximately 500 MeV with lattice spacing a approximately 0.06 fm
- Temperature range for infrared scaling of Dirac spectral density: T > T_IR = 200-250 MeV, above T_c
- Heavy quark diffusion coefficient: results from EFT method and fermionic correlators are internally consistent but systematically different from each other
- Electrical conductivity: good agreement across multiple lattice groups using different methods, with results now including physical up, down, and strange quark masses

## Context

This review extends and updates earlier overviews by Fukushima (2013), Ding (2017), Fischer (2018), Ghiglieri (2020), and Guenther (2020). It highlights unresolved tensions between lattice groups regarding U(1)_A restoration (HotQCD 2012, Buchoff 2013, Dick 2015, Ding 2020, Kaczmarek 2021 finding broken symmetry; Cossu 2013, Brandt 2016, Tomiya 2016, Aoki 2020 finding restoration) and between different methods for determining heavy quarkonium properties and the heavy quark diffusion coefficient. The results enable further constraints on the QCD phase diagram, neutron star equations of state through combination with observational data from gravitational waves and the NICER observatory, and improved understanding of transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
