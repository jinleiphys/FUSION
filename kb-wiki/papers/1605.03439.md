# Sub-threshold charm production in nuclear collisions

> We present the first predictions for sub-threshold open charm and charmonium production in nuclear collisions. The production mechanism is driven by multi-step scatterings of nucleons and their resonance states, accumulating sufficient energy for the production of $J/Ψ$, $Λ_c + \overline{D}$ and $D+\overline{D}$. Our results are of particular importance for the CBM experiment at FAIR, as they indicate that already at the SIS100 accelerator one can expect a significant number of charmed hadrons to be produced. This opens new possibilities to explore charm dynamics and the formation of charmed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first quantitative predictions for sub-threshold charm production in nuclear collisions, showing that multi-step scatterings of nucleons and their resonance states can accumulate enough energy to produce charmed hadrons even at beam energies below the elementary p+p threshold. The authors caution that their results are conservative estimates because the associated production cross-section is smaller than earlier estimates from hadronic Lagrangian models.

## Method

The authors employ the UrQMD transport model extended with additional high-mass baryonic resonances (nucleon and Delta resonances up to 4.6 GeV) that serve as intermediate energy reservoirs. Charm production occurs through secondary interactions of these heavy baryonic states, which then decay into J/Ψ, Λc + D̅, or N + D + D̅ final states. The branching fractions for these decays are fixed using experimental data on J/Ψ production in p+p collisions at √s_pp = 6.7 GeV and statistical model ratios for relative charm hadron abundances. For charmed nuclei predictions, a coalescence model (CB model) is applied to assemble Λc particles with nucleons into light clusters.

## Key numbers

- Threshold center-of-mass energies for charm production in p+p: N+N → N+N+J/Ψ at 4.973 GeV, N+N → N+Λc+D̅ at 5.096 GeV, N+N → N+N+D+D̅ at 5.611 GeV
- Branching fraction for N* → N + J/Ψ set to Γ_N+J/Ψ/Γ_tot = 5 × 10⁻⁵, fixed from experimental J/Ψ cross section at √s_pp = 6.7 GeV
- Branching fraction for N* → Λc + D̅ set to Γ_Λc+D̅/Γ_tot = 1 × 10⁻², based on statistical model ratios
- Branching fraction for N* → N + D + D̅ set to Γ_N+D+D̅/Γ_tot = 2 × 10⁻³
- At fixed target beam energy E_lab = 6 A GeV in central Au+Au: expected yields per event of 1 × 10⁻⁷ J/Ψ, 5 × 10⁻⁵ Λc, 6 × 10⁻⁵ D̅, and 1 × 10⁻⁵ D
- At E_lab = 11 A GeV (SIS100 maximum), yields are predicted to be one order of magnitude larger than at 6 A GeV
- Total inelastic J/Ψ + p cross section from detailed balance is on the order of a few microbarn

## Context

This work extends previous studies of near-threshold charm production that relied on parametrized p+N cross sections (Cassing 2000, Linnyk 2008) or effective interaction Lagrangians (Liu 2003, Rekalo 2002), which assumed charm production occurs primarily in first binary collisions with Fermi momentum contributions. The authors had previously shown that deep below threshold, Fermi momenta become negligible (Steinheimer 2016) and that secondary baryon interactions are essential, as demonstrated for φ meson production (Steinheimer 2015). The results enable feasibility studies for the CBM experiment at FAIR's SIS100 accelerator, particularly for measuring charmed hadrons at high baryon densities and potentially discovering charmed nuclei, whose existence was first suggested by Tyapkin and Dover (1977).
