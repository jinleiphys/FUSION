# Low Energy Theorems For Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

> Low energy theorems are derived for the coefficients of the effective range expansion in s-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering valid to leading order in an expansion in which both $m_π$ and $1/a$ (where $a$ is the scattering length) are treated as small mass scales. Comparisons with phase shift data, however, reveal a pattern of gross violations of the theorems for all coefficients in both the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ channels. Analogous theorems are developed for the energy dependence $ε$ parameter which describes $^3S_1 - ^3D_1$ mixing. These theorems are also violated. These failures strongly suggest that the physical value of $m_π$ is too large for the chiral expansion to be valid in this context. Comparisons of $m_π$ with phenomenological scales known to arise in the two-nucleon problem support this conjecture.

## Key claim

Low energy theorems derived for the coefficients of the effective range expansion in s-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering, valid to leading order in an expansion treating both the pion mass and the inverse scattering length as small scales, are grossly violated when compared with phase shift data, suggesting that the physical value of the pion mass is too large for the chiral expansion to be valid in this context.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the coefficients v_i (i >= 2) in the effective range expansion for s-wave scattering and for the coefficients g_i in the low-momentum expansion of the epsilon parameter describing 3S1-3D1 mixing, using the Q counting scheme (where m_pi, 1/a, and external momentum k are all treated as order Q). They then compared these predicted coefficients with coefficients extracted from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering data, using a least squares fit at very low energies for the singlet channel and values from the literature for the triplet channel.

## Key numbers

- The predicted v_2 coefficient in the triplet channel overstates the extracted value by more than a factor of 20.
- The predicted v_i coefficients in both the 1S0 and 3S1 channels are typically a factor of approximately 5 larger than the extracted values.
- The predicted g_1 coefficient for 3S1-3D1 mixing is approximately 130 percent larger than the value extracted from data when using the physical scattering length.
- The predicted g_2 coefficient is approximately 4 times the extracted value, and the predicted g_3 coefficient is approximately 8 times the extracted value.
- The dimensionless expansion parameter eta = (m_pi g_A^2 M)/(16 pi f_pi^2) is approximately 0.47, indicating a slowly converging theory.
- The lower bound on the matching radius R from potential model analysis is 1.1 fm, corresponding to m_pi R greater than 0.78, with a square well fit giving R = 2.3 fm and m_pi R approximately 1.6.

## Context

This work extends the Q counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for nucleon-nucleon scattering, which treats both m_pi and 1/a as small scales, and tests the "including pions" component of that program. The authors find that the chiral expansion fails for these low energy theorems, consistent with earlier concerns raised by Gegelia (1998) and Steele and Furnstahl (1998) about the perturbative treatment of pion exchange. The results suggest that the short-distance scale in the nucleon-nucleon problem is comparable to m_pi, as previously argued by Scaldeferri et al. (1997) using a generalization of effective range theory, making the chiral expansion problematic for observables sensitive to both pion physics and s-wave interactions.
