# In a hot, chirally symmetric phase, $π^0$ doesn't go into $2 γ$, but $π^0 σ$ does

> In a constituent quark model at nonzero temperature, the amplitude for $π^0 \rightarrow 2 γ$ vanishes in a chirally symmetric phase, while that for $π^0 σ\rightarrow 2 γ$ does not.

## Key claim

In a constituent quark model at nonzero temperature, the amplitude for π⁰ → 2γ vanishes in a chirally symmetric phase (where the chiral condensate σ₀ = 0), while the amplitude for π⁰σ → 2γ does not vanish; this contradicts earlier expectations that the anomaly form would remain unchanged at finite temperature.

## Method

The author computes the one-loop triangle diagrams for π⁰ → 2γ in a constituent quark model with two flavors and three colors, first at zero temperature and then at nonzero temperature using the Matsubara formalism. The calculation is repeated for the π⁰σ → 2γ process, requiring evaluation of six box diagrams with the fermion loop integral performed in the limit where the constituent quark mass m is much less than the temperature T. The effective lagrangian is derived by expanding the loop integrals in powers of external momenta.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature loop integral I(0,0,m) = 1/(32π²m²), giving the effective lagrangian coefficient e²N_c/(96π²f_π) for π⁰ → 2γ
- At nonzero temperature with m ≪ T, the loop integral equals 7ζ(3)/(128π⁴T²), where ζ(3) = 1.20206...
- The nonzero-temperature effective lagrangian for π⁰ → 2γ has coefficient 7ζ(3)e²g̃²N_c/(96π⁴T²) × σ₀, which vanishes when σ₀ = 0
- The π⁰σ → 2γ amplitude receives equal contributions from all six diagrams, each giving 7ζ(3)e²g̃²N_c/(288π⁴T²) × ε^{μναβ}P₁^αP₂^β

## Context

This work extends the understanding of chiral anomalies in thermal media, showing that while the anomaly in terms of fundamental fields is unrenormalized by temperature (as established by earlier work), the manifestation in terms of effective fields changes qualitatively. The result contradicts the expectation of previous authors that the π⁰ → 2γ coupling would persist in a chirally symmetric phase. The analysis enables predictions for other anomalous processes at finite temperature, including ω → ρπ, ω → πππ, and the Wess-Zumino-Witten term governing KK → πππ, all of which acquire σ-associated partners in the symmetric phase.
