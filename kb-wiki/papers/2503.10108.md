# $γW$-exchange contributions in neutron $β$ decay in the forward-angle limit

> In this work, the contributions from $γW$-exchange in neutron $β$ decay are estimated at the amplitude level. Using a general form for the electromagnetic (EM) form factors (FFs) of the proton, the EM FFs of the neutron, and the weak FFs of the $Wnp$ interaction as inputs, we present analytical expressions for the inner part of the $γW$-exchange amplitude under the forward angle limit. The differences and relations between our method and those used in the references are discussed. To compare our numerical results with those provided in the references, we consider three types of FFs as examples. The numerical results show that when the favored EM FFs are used, our result for the contribution from the Fermi part is consistent with those reported in the references, while our results for the Gamow-Teller parts are about 7\% and 13\% larger than those reported in Ref.~\cite{Hayen-2021}.

## Key claim

The authors estimate the inner radiative corrections from γW-exchange in neutron β decay at the amplitude level under the forward-angle limit, finding that when favored electromagnetic form factors are used, their result for the Fermi part is consistent with references while their Gamow-Teller parts are about 7% and 13% larger than those reported in Hayen (2021), depending on the specific form factor parameterization.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for the γW-exchange amplitude in the forward-angle limit using a general parameterization of the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors and the weak form factors of the Wnp interaction, expressed as sums of dipole-type functions. They performed the calculation at the amplitude level rather than the cross-section level, reducing the four-dimensional amplitudes to a two-dimensional form expressed in terms of 16 Pauli-spinor operators, and used the packages FeynCalc, PackageX, and LoopTools for Dirac matrix handling and loop integration. They avoided the first-class current approximation after applying the forward-angle limit to prevent non-uniqueness issues identified in prior work.

## Key numbers

- The neutron mass used is 939.56542 MeV, the proton mass is 938.27209 MeV, and the electron mass is 0.51100 MeV.
- The axial-vector coupling constant is taken as g_A = -1.26, the vector coupling as g_V = 1, and the weak magnetism coupling as g_M = 3.706.
- The proton anomalous magnetic moment parameter is F_20^p = 1.793, and the neutron anomalous magnetic moment parameter is F_20^n = -1.913.
- For the favored type I form factors, the dipole mass parameters include Lambda_11 = 0.815 GeV, Lambda_12 = 1.230 GeV, Lambda_21 = 0.970 GeV, and Lambda_22 = 1.598 GeV for the proton, and Lambda_31 = 1.288 GeV, Lambda_32 = 1.378 GeV, Lambda_41 = 0.699 GeV, and Lambda_42 = 1.214 GeV for the neutron.
- The Gamow-Teller contributions from the authors are about 7% and 13% larger than those reported in Hayen (2021) when using the favored electromagnetic form factors.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Towner (1992) and Hayen (2021) for the Born elastic contribution C_Born to the γW-exchange inner radiative corrections in neutron β decay, but uses independent electromagnetic form factors for the proton and neutron rather than assuming isospin symmetry with a single dipole form. The authors identify a non-uniqueness issue in the first-class current approximation used by Hayen (2021) after applying the forward-angle limit and avoid it by not using that approximation. The analytical expressions provided can be used to estimate corrections to the neutron lifetime and to all polarized measurements, and can be updated when improved measurements of the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors become available.
