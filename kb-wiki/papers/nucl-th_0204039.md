# Variation after Angular Momentum Projection for the Study of Excited States Based on Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> In order to study the structure of excited states we perform a variational calculation after spin parity projection (VAP) within the framework of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD). The framework is proven to be a new powerful approach for the study of the various structures of excited states because it is free from model assumptions such as inert cores, existence of clusters, and the axial symmetry. By using finite range interactions with a density dependent term we reproduce well all the energy levels below 15 MeV in $^{12}$C. This is the first theoretical model that reproduces many $E2$ transition rates and $β$ decays to $^{12}$C successfully.

## Key claim

The paper claims that variation after angular momentum projection (VAP) within the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) framework is the first theoretical model to reproduce all energy levels below 15 MeV in \(^{12}\)C, along with many \(E2\) transition rates and \(\beta\) decays to \(^{12}\)C, but this success is conditional on using finite-range interactions with a density-dependent term and a specific choice of force parameters (Majorana parameter \(m=0.62\), G3RS strength \(u_1=-u_2=3000\) MeV).

## Method

The authors performed a variational calculation after parity and total angular momentum projection (VAP) within the AMD framework, where the basis wave functions are Slater determinants of Gaussian wave packets with complex variational parameters for the centers and intrinsic spins. Energy variation was carried out using the frictional cooling method for the lowest states of each spin-parity \(J^\pm\), while higher excited states were obtained by orthogonalizing to lower states and then diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix formed from all intrinsic states. The adopted interactions included the modified Volkov no1 central force (case 3) with a zero-range three-body repulsive term, the G3RS spin-orbit force, and the Coulomb force, with a Gaussian width parameter \(\nu = 0.18\) fm\(^{-2}\).

## Key numbers

- All energy levels below 15 MeV in \(^{12}\)C were reproduced; the level spacing between \(0_1^+\) and \(2_1^+\) was 4.4 MeV (experimental value), compared to 2.2 MeV in a previous GCM calculation.
- \(B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+)\): present result 8.8 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\), experimental value 7.81 \(\pm\) 0.44 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
- \(B(E2; 0_2^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+)\): present result 19.3 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\), experimental value 13.4 \(\pm\) 1.8 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\), compared to 3.5 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) in a GCM calculation.
- \(\beta\) decay log\(ft\) from \(^{12}\)N to \(^{12}\)C \(0_1^+\): theory 3.8, experiment 4.120 \(\pm\) 0.003.
- \(\beta\) decay log\(ft\) from \(^{12}\)N to \(^{12}\)C \(0_2^+\) (7.65 MeV): theory 4.0, experiment 4.34 \(\pm\) 0.06.
- \(\beta\) decay log\(ft\) from \(^{12}\)N to \(^{12}\)C \(0_3^+\) (10.3 MeV): theory 4.7, experiment 4.36 \(\pm\) 0.17.
- \(\beta\) decay log\(ft\) from \(^{12}\)N to \(^{12}\)C \(1_1^+\) (12.71 MeV): theory 3.8, experiment 3.52 \(\pm\) 0.14.

## Context

This work extends the AMD framework, previously applied to ground states of light unstable nuclei (Kanada-En'yo, 1995, 1998), by incorporating variation after angular momentum projection to study excited states. It addresses failures of conventional cluster models (e.g., GCM calculations by the 3\(\alpha\) cluster model) that underestimated the \(0_2^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+\) \(E2\) transition strength and could not describe \(\beta\) decays due to the forbidden nature of nucleon decay within \(\alpha\) clusters, as well as limitations of \((0+2)\hbar\omega\) shell-model calculations (e.g., by the p-shell shell model) that could not produce the \(0_3^+\) and \(2_2^+\) states. The approach enables unified description of both shell-model-like and molecule-like structures in light nuclei, and is applicable to stable and unstable nuclei for systematic studies of structural changes with excitation energy.
