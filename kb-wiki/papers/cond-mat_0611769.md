# Effective Range Corrections to Three-Body Recombination for Atoms with Large Scattering Length

> Few-body systems with large scattering length a have universal properties that do not depend on the details of their interactions at short distances. The rate constant for three-body recombination of bosonic atoms of mass m into a shallow dimer scales as \hbar a^4/m times a log-periodic function of the scattering length. We calculate the leading and subleading corrections to the rate constant which are due to the effective range of the atoms and study the correlation between the rate constant and the atom-dimer scattering length. Our results are applied to 4He atoms as a test case.

## Key claim

The three-body recombination rate constant α for bosonic atoms with large scattering length a receives leading and subleading corrections from the effective range r_e, which can be calculated up to second order in the expansion in γl (where γ = 1/a at leading order and l is the natural low-energy length scale). The correlation between α and the atom-dimer scattering length a_ad is modified by these effective range corrections, with the convergence of the expansion depending on the value of γr_e and the absolute value of a_ad.

## Method

The authors solve an integral equation for the three-body amplitude A_S(p,k;E) that includes the effective range r_e through the dimer propagator and the bound-state pole position γ. They expand the kernel of the integral equation perturbatively in r_e to avoid instabilities from a deep pole in the dimer propagator for positive effective range. The recombination constant α and atom-dimer scattering length a_ad are then extracted from the amplitude at specific kinematic points, and the correlation between them is computed at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the effective-range expansion.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order recombination constant is α = 67.12 sin²(s₀ ln(a/a_*) + 1.67) × ħa⁴/m [1 + O(l/a)], where s₀ = 1.00624...
- The discrete scaling factor is e^(π/s₀) ≈ 22.7; zeros of α occur at a ≈ (e^(π/s₀))ⁿ × 0.20 a_*, and maxima at a ≈ (e^(π/s₀))ⁿ × 0.94 a_*.
- For the TTY potential (⁴He): a = 100.01 Å, r_e = 7.329 Å, γ = 0.01040 Å⁻¹, r_eγ = 0.076; the NNLO recombination constant is α = 3.778 ± 0.014 ħ/(mγ⁴).
- For the HFD-B3-FCI1 potential (⁴He): a = 91.0 Å, r_e = 7.291 Å, γ = 0.01149 Å⁻¹, r_eγ = 0.084; the NNLO atom-dimer scattering length is a_ad = 1.424 γ⁻¹ (solution 1) or 2.024 γ⁻¹ (solution 2).

## Context

This work extends the universal leading-order results for three-body recombination in systems with large scattering length, previously formulated by Nielsen and Macek (1999), Esry, Greene, and Burke (1999), and Bedaque, Braaten, and Hammer (2000), by including effective range corrections up to second order. The calculations are applied to ⁴He atoms using the TTY potential of Tang, Toennies, and Yiu (1995) and the HFD-B3-FCI1 potential of Aziz, Janzen, and Moldover (1995), enabling predictions of previously uncalculated three-body observables. The results enable more precise theoretical descriptions of ultracold atomic gas experiments, such as those with ¹³³Cs atoms by the Innsbruck group, and can be extended to include recombination into Efimov trimers and dimer-dimer scattering resonances.
