# Two-proton overlap functions in the Jastrow correlation method and cross section of the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ reaction

> Using the relationship between the two-particle overlap functions (TOF's) and the two-body density matrix (TDM), the TOF's for the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ reaction are calculated on the basis of a TDM obtained within the Jastrow correlation method. The main contributions of the removal of $^1S_0$ and $^3P_1$ $pp$ pairs from $^{16}$O are considered in the calculation of the cross section of the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ reaction using the Jastrow TOF's which include short-range correlations (SRC). The results are compared with the cross sections calculated with different theoretical treatments of the TOF's.

## Key claim

The Jastrow correlation method (JCM) can produce two-proton overlap functions (TOF's) for the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ reaction that reproduce the main qualitative features of the cross section found in previous theoretical studies and in data, but only as a first attempt that relies on a TDM incorporating only short-range correlations (SRC) and using harmonic-oscillator single-particle wave functions.

## Method

The authors extracted two-proton overlap functions from the two-body density matrix (TDM) of $^{16}$O calculated within the low-order approximation of the Jastrow correlation method, using the asymptotic relationship between TOF's and the TDM established by Adsley et al. (1999). They then used these TOF's to compute the differential cross section of the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C$_{\rm g.s.}$ reaction within the theoretical framework of Giusti et al. (1991, 1997, 1998), including one-body and two-body (Delta-isobar) currents, with final-state interactions described by distorted waves from a phenomenological optical potential.

## Key numbers

- Jastrow correlation parameters for $^{16}$O: $\alpha_{\rm osc.} = 0.61\;{\rm fm^{-1}}$, $\beta = 1.30\;{\rm fm^{-1}}$, $c = 0.77$.
- Spectroscopic factors from the JCM: 0.958 for the $^1S_0$ overlap function and 0.957 for the $^3P_1$ overlap function; total TOF spectroscopic factor is 0.965 (uncorrelated case gives 1.0).
- Super-parallel kinematics at MAMI: incident electron energy $E_0 = 855$ MeV, energy transfer $\omega = 215$ MeV, momentum transfer $q = 316$ MeV/$c$.
- NIKHEF kinematics: $E_0 = 584$ MeV, $\omega = 212$ MeV, $q = 300$ MeV/$c$, kinetic energy of first outgoing proton $T'_1 = 137$ MeV, angle $\gamma_1 = -30^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the method of Adsley et al. (1999) for extracting TOF's from the TDM, previously applied to one-nucleon overlap functions, to the two-proton case for $^{16}$O. It compares cross sections with two prior approaches: the spectral function (SF) calculation of Giusti et al. (1998) and Antonov et al. (1996), which includes both SRC and long-range correlations (LRC) via a dressed RPA description, and a simpler shell-model-plus-correlation (SM+CORR) product approach from Giusti et al. (1997). The results show that the JCM TOF's produce lower cross sections at low recoil momenta and much larger cross sections at high momenta compared to the SF results, indicating that the procedure is sensitive to the treatment of correlations and would benefit from more realistic TDM's incorporating both SRC and LRC.
