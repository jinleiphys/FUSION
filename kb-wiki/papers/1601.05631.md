# Specific Heat of Matter Formed in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We report the excitation energy dependence of specific heat (\cv) of hadronic matter at freeze-out in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider energies by analyzing the published data on event-by-event mean transverse momentum (\meanpt) distributions. The \meanpt~distributions in finite \pt~ranges are converted to distributions of effective temperatures, and dynamical fluctuations in temperature are extracted by subtracting widths of the corresponding mixed event distributions. The heat capacity per particle at the kinetic freeze-out surface is presented as a function of collision energy, which shows a sharp rise in \cv~below \sNN~=~62.4~GeV. We employ the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to estimate \cv~at the chemical and kinetic freeze-out surfaces. The experimental results are compared to the HRG and other theoretical model calculations. HRG results show good agreement with data. Model predictions for \cv~at the Large Hadron Collider energy are presented.

## Key claim

The specific heat per particle of hadronic matter at kinetic freeze-out shows a sharp rise below center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair (√sNN) = 62.4 GeV, with the experimental results extracted from event-by-event mean transverse momentum distributions after subtracting mixed-event statistical fluctuations and accounting for transverse flow effects.

## Method

The authors converted published event-by-event mean transverse momentum (⟨pT⟩) distributions from STAR for central Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions into effective temperature (Teff) distributions using a relation derived from Boltzmann-type pT spectra. Dynamical temperature fluctuations were extracted by subtracting the widths of mixed-event Teff distributions from the real data widths, and the heat capacity per particle was obtained from the relation 1/C = (ΔTdyn_eff)²/⟨Tkin⟩², where Tkin values came from blast-wave fits to identified particle spectra. Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model calculations of specific heat were performed at both chemical and kinetic freeze-out surfaces for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 20 GeV: mean Teff = 0.2341 GeV with σ = 0.00912 GeV (data) and σ = 0.00869 GeV (mixed events)
- For Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV: mean Teff = 0.2677 GeV with σ = 0.00815 GeV (data) and σ = 0.00718 GeV (mixed events)
- For Cu+Cu collisions at √sNN = 62.4 GeV: mean Teff = 0.2550 GeV with σ = 0.0175 GeV (data) and σ = 0.0156 GeV (mixed events)
- For Cu+Cu collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV: mean Teff = 0.2684 GeV with σ = 0.0161 GeV (data) and σ = 0.0140 GeV (mixed events)
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature Tkin = 0.095 ± 0.010 GeV for Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV and Tkin = 0.09 ± 0.005 GeV for Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- Transverse flow velocity βT = 0.59 ± 0.051 for Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV and βT = 0.651 ± 0.02 for Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- Previous NA49 result gave specific heat = 60 ± 100 at T = 180 MeV for SPS energies

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to extract specific heat from pT correlations at SPS energies by Korus et al. (2004), which yielded values with large errors (60 ± 100) due to low particle multiplicities. The present analysis uses higher-multiplicity RHIC data from STAR (reported in STAR publications from 2005-2009) to obtain statistically meaningful results. The experimental specific heat values agree well with HRG model calculations at kinetic freeze-out and are compared to PACIAE transport model results from Ben-Hao et al. (2013), which miss the data point at √sNN = 20 GeV. The results enable future comparisons with LHC data and provide constraints on the equation of state near the QCD phase transition.
