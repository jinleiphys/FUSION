# Nonrelativistic model of tetraquarks and predictions for their masses from fits to charmed and bottom meson data

> We investigate a nonrelativistic model of tetraquarks, which are assumed to be compact and to consist of diquark-antidiquark pairs. We fit, for the first time, basically all currently known values for the measured masses of 45 mesons, including both charmed and bottom mesons, to the model and predict masses of tetraquarks as well as diquarks. In particular, we find masses of four axial-vector diquarks, i.e., $qc$, $cc$, $qb$, and $bb$, where $q = u,d$, and 24 ground-state tetraquarks, including both heavy-light tetraquarks ($qc\overline{qc}$ and $qb\overline{qb}$) and heavy tetraquarks ($cc\overline{cc}$ and $bb\overline{bb}$). In general, our results for the masses of $qb\overline{qb}$, $cc\overline{cc}$, and $bb\overline{bb}$ are largely comparable with other reported results, whereas our results for the masses of $qc\overline{qc}$ are slightly larger than what has been found earlier. Finally, we identify some of the obtained predictions for masses of tetraquarks with masses of experimental tetraquark candidates, and especially, we find that $ψ(4660)$, $Z_b(10610)$, and $Z_b(10650)$ could be described by the model.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a nonrelativistic diquark-antidiquark model, fitted to 45 charmed and bottom mesons, can predict tetraquark masses that are largely comparable with other reported results for heavy tetraquarks, but the predicted masses for heavy-light tetraquarks ($qc\overline{qc}$) are slightly larger than earlier findings. The model identifies specific experimental candidates, $\psi(4660)$, $Z_b(10610)$, and $Z_b(10650)$, as potentially described by this framework, though the identification is conditional on the model's assumptions about compact tetraquark structure.

## Method

The authors model tetraquarks as bound states of diquark-antidiquark pairs using a nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation with a Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear confinement) and a smeared Gaussian spin-spin interaction. They first fit the model's four free parameters (constituent quark mass, strong coupling constant, string tension, and spin-spin smearing parameter) to experimental masses of 45 mesons across five data sets (charmonium, bottomonium, D mesons, B mesons, and combined charmonium+bottomonium). Diquark masses are then computed by halving the color factor and string tension to account for the different color structure of quark-quark systems, and tetraquark masses are predicted using the diquark masses as constituents with the same potential parameters as mesons.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium fit (data set I) yields constituent charm quark mass $m = 1.459$ GeV, $\alpha_S = 0.5234$, string tension $b = 0.1480$ GeV$^2$, and $\sigma = 1.048$ GeV, with $\chi^2 = 0.01887$.
- Bottomonium fit (data set II) yields constituent bottom quark mass $m = 4.783$ GeV, $\alpha_S = 0.3841$, $b = 0.1708$ GeV$^2$, and $\sigma = 1.50$ GeV, with $\chi^2 = 0.005485$.
- D meson fit (data set III) yields $\alpha_S = 0.70$, $b = 0.08417$ GeV$^2$, $\sigma = 0.5760$ GeV, with $\chi^2 = 0.02182$.
- B meson fit (data set IV) yields $\alpha_S = 0.70$, $b = 0.09467$ GeV$^2$, $\sigma = 0.3049$ GeV, with $\chi^2 = 0.0001696$.
- Combined charmonium+bottomonium fit (data set V) yields $\alpha_S = 0.3714$, $b = 0.1445$ GeV$^2$, $\sigma = 1.50$ GeV, with $\chi^2 = 0.1694$.
- Predicted diquark masses (ground state $1^3S_1$): $qc$ diquark mass $M = 2018$ MeV (energy eigenvalue $E = 236.6$ MeV); $cc$ diquark mass $M = 3128$ MeV ($E = 210.6$ MeV); $qb$ diquark mass $M = 5339$ MeV ($E = 232.8$ MeV); $bb$ diquark mass $M = 9643$ MeV ($E = 76.40$ MeV).
- The model predicts masses for 24 ground-state tetraquark states (results tabulated in the paper).

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic diquark-antidiquark model of tetraquarks previously applied in the literature (e.g., Debastiani (2017), Maiani (2014)) by performing, for the first time, a simultaneous fit to both charmed and bottom meson data (45 mesons total) rather than fitting to each sector separately. The approach is comparable to lattice QCD and other phenomenological models (e.g., molecular states, QCD sum rules) but differs by treating tetraquarks as compact diquark-antidiquark pairs rather than meson molecules. The predictions enable future experimental searches for the 24 tetraquark states, particularly the heavy tetraquarks $cc\overline{cc}$ and $bb\overline{bb}$, and provide a framework for identifying observed $XYZ$ states such as $\psi(4660)$, $Z_b(10610)$, and $Z_b(10650)$ as tetraquark candidates.
