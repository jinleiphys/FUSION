# Low-energy hypernuclear spectra with microscopic particle-rotor model with relativistic point coupling hyperon-nucleon interaction

> We extend the microscopic particle-rotor model for hypernuclear low-lying states by including the derivative and tensor coupling terms in the point-coupling nucleon-$Λ$ particle ($NΛ$) interaction. Taking $^{13}_{~Λ}$C as an example, we show that a good overall description for excitation spectra is achieved with four sets of effective $NΛ$ interaction. We find that the $Λ$ hyperon binding energy decreases monotonically with increasing the strengths of the high-order interaction terms. In particular, the tensor coupling term decreases the energy splitting between the first $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states and increases the energy splitting between the first $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states in $^{13}_{~Λ}$C.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including higher-order derivative and tensor coupling terms in the point-coupling nucleon-Lambda (N-Lambda) interaction within the microscopic particle-rotor model can reproduce the low-lying excitation spectra of hypernuclei, but the four parameter sets tested overestimate the Lambda binding energy of 13-Lambda-C by 8% to 18% compared to the empirical value, requiring scaling to match experiment.

## Method

The authors extended the microscopic particle-rotor model (MPRM) for hypernuclear low-lying states by incorporating derivative and tensor coupling terms into the relativistic point-coupling N-Lambda interaction. They constructed hypernuclear wave functions by coupling a valence Lambda hyperon to low-lying states of the 12-C core nucleus, which were generated using a quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (GCM) with the PC-F1 nucleon-nucleon interaction. The coupled-channels equations for the Dirac spinor components of the Lambda hyperon were solved by expanding the radial wave functions on a harmonic oscillator basis with 18 shells.

## Key numbers

* The Lambda binding energy of 13-Lambda-C was calculated as 15.72 MeV (PCY-S1), 13.63 MeV (PCY-S2), 15.42 MeV (PCY-S3), and 13.22 MeV (PCY-S4), compared to the empirical value of 11.38 +/- 0.05 MeV.
* To reproduce the empirical binding energy, the coupling strengths needed scaling by 18% (PCY-S1), 9% (PCY-S2), 16% (PCY-S3), and 8% (PCY-S4).
* The tensor coupling term reduced the energy splitting between the first 1/2- and 3/2- states from 2.28 MeV to 0.31 MeV for PCY-S1, and from 1.25 MeV to 0.25 MeV for PCY-S4.
* The Lambda binding energy decreased from 21.28 MeV to 15.72 MeV when derivative coupling terms were added to the PCY-S1 interaction (changing the sum of derivative strengths from 0 to the original value).
* The tensor coupling term reduced the Lambda binding energy by 0.9 MeV for PCY-S1 and 1.99 MeV for PCY-S4 when turned on from zero strength.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic particle-rotor model calculations (Mei 2014, Mei 2015) that included only leading-order scalar and vector N-Lambda interactions, by implementing the higher-order derivative and tensor coupling terms proposed by Tanimura (2012). The study enables systematic assessment of how different components of the N-Lambda interaction affect hypernuclear fine structure, particularly the spin-orbit splitting of the p-state Lambda hyperon, and provides a framework for analyzing upcoming experimental data from facilities such as J-PARC (Tamura 2009).
