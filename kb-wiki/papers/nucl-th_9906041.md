# Chiral Symmetry and Weak Decay of Hypernuclei

> The weak decays of hyperons and hypernuclei are studied from the chiral symmetry viewpoint. The soft pion relations are useful in understanding the isospin properties of the weak hyperon decays. Recent development on the short-range part of the $\Lam N\to NN$ weak transitions shows fairly good account of the weak decays of hypernuclei, though it fails to explain the $n/p$ ratio. The $\pip$ decays of light hypernuclei are studied in the soft pion approach. They are related to the $\DI=3/2$ amplitudes of the nonmesonic decay.

# Chiral Symmetry and Weak Decay of Hypernuclei

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry, via the soft-pion theorem, provides a powerful framework for understanding weak hyperon and hypernuclear decays; in particular, the soft-pion approach shows that the $\pi^+$ decay mode of light hypernuclei is induced only by the $\Delta I = 3/2$ component of the weak Hamiltonian, while the short-range direct quark mechanism for $\Lambda N \to NN$ transitions gives a fairly good account of nonmesonic hypernuclear decays but fails to explain the observed neutron-to-proton (n/p) ratio.

## Method

The author applies the soft-pion theorem to relate parity-violating (S-wave) pionic hyperon decay amplitudes to parity-conserving baryonic matrix elements, and extends this technique to the $\Lambda p \to nn\pi^+$ process in hypernuclei. For nonmesonic decays, a direct quark (DQ) transition potential is constructed from $su \to ud$ and $sd \to dd$ valence quark transitions, combined with one-pion and one-kaon exchange potentials, and applied to calculate decay rates of $\Lambda$ in nuclear matter and light hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- The parity-violating (S-wave) amplitude for $\Sigma^+ \to n\pi^+$ is 0.13, compared to the parity-conserving (P-wave) amplitude of 42.2, confirming that the soft-pion theorem predicts this decay proceeds only through the P-wave channel.
- The $\pi^+/\pi^-$ emission ratio from $^4_{\Lambda}He$ is about 5% from old experimental data; charge-exchange and $\Sigma^+$ mixing mechanisms give only 1.2% and at most 0.2%, respectively.
- The direct quark mechanism combined with one-pion exchange yields a $\pi^+$/soft-$\pi^-$ ratio of approximately 0.34 for $^4_{\Lambda}He$ (or 0.31 if kaon exchange is omitted).

## Context

This work extends the soft-pion approach, previously applied to free hyperon decays by Donoghue (1970s) and others, to hypernuclear weak decays for the first time. The direct quark mechanism for $\Lambda N \to NN$ transitions was proposed by Ito and Oka (1990s) and by Maltman and Shmatikov (1990s) to address the short-range part of the nonmesonic decay, which meson-exchange models (e.g., the Bonn potential with hard form factors) fail to describe adequately, particularly regarding the n/p ratio problem. The results enable future application of chiral perturbation theory to hypernuclear decays, going beyond the soft-pion limit to include finite pion mass and flavor SU(3) breaking effects.
