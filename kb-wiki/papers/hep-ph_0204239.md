# Nucleon Charge and Magnetization Densities from Sachs Form Factors

> Relativistic prescriptions relating Sachs form factors to nucleon charge and magnetization densities are used to fit recent data for both the proton and the neutron. The analysis uses expansions in complete radial bases to minimize model dependence and to estimate the uncertainties in radial densities due to limitation of the range of momentum transfer. We find that the charge distribution for the proton is significantly broad than its magnetization density and that the magnetization density is slightly broader for the neutron than the proton. The neutron charge form factor is consistent with the Galster parametrization over the available range of Q^2, but relativistic inversion produces a softer radial density. Discrete ambiguities in the inversion method are analyzed in detail. The method of Mitra and Kumari ensures compatibility with pQCD and is most useful for extrapolating form factors to large Q^2.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic inversion of Sachs form factors using the Mitra-Kumari prescription (with λ=2) produces nucleon charge and magnetization densities that are consistent with perturbative QCD scaling at large Q², while fits using lower λ values require artificial constraints on the intrinsic form factor near the limiting frequency k_m=2m to achieve pQCD compatibility.

## Method

The analysis uses linear expansions in complete radial bases (Fourier-Bessel and Laguerre-Gaussian expansions) to fit Sachs form factor data for both proton and neutron, minimizing model dependence. The relativistic inversion relates Sachs form factors G_E and G_M to intrinsic densities via the relation ρ̃(k) = G(Q²)(1+τ)^λ, where k² = Q²/(1+τ) accounts for Lorentz contraction, with λ=2 chosen for consistency with pQCD. Uncertainties in radial densities due to limited momentum transfer range are estimated using pseudodata constraints based on a k⁻⁴ envelope for the unmeasured spatial frequency region.

## Key numbers

- The dipole form factor parameter Λ² = 0.71 (GeV/c)²
- The limiting spatial frequency k_m = 2m, corresponding to the nucleon Compton wavelength
- The zitterbewegung correction to the proton mean-square radius equals 0.066λ fm²
- The expansion radius R_max = 4.0 fm was chosen for Fourier-Bessel expansions
- The Gaussian scale parameter b = 0.556 fm was chosen for Laguerre-Gaussian expansions
- The tail bias parameters: Λ = 4.27 fm⁻¹, r_m = 2.0 fm, Δr = 0.2 fm, N_d = 10, w = 2
- The neutron charge form factor data extend to Q² < 1.6 (GeV/c)²
- The proton form factor ratio G_Ep/G_Mp shows an almost linear decrease for Q² > 1 (GeV/c)²
- Data for G_Mp exist for Q² > 20 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends the relativistic inversion approach of Licht and Pagnamenta (1970) and Mitra and Kumari (1977) to fit recent polarization-transfer data for both proton and neutron form factors, confirming quantitatively that the proton charge distribution is broader than its magnetization density. The analysis contradicts the earlier soliton-model choices of Ji (1991) and Holzwarth (1996) (λ_E=0, λ_M=1) by showing that λ=2 for both electric and magnetic form factors provides natural compatibility with pQCD scaling without artificial constraints. The method enables extraction of model-independent radial densities with quantified uncertainties, and the resulting densities can be used to compute up and down quark distributions in the proton via combinations of neutron and proton charge densities.
