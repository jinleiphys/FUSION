# Hadron Physics and Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> Detailed investigations of the structure of hadrons are essential for understanding how matter is constructed from the quarks and gluons of QCD, and amongst the questions posed to modern hadron physics, three stand out. What is the rigorous, quantitative mechanism responsible for confinement? What is the connection between confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking? And are these phenomena together sufficient to explain the origin of more than 98% of the mass of the observable universe? Such questions may only be answered using the full machinery of nonperturbative relativistic quantum field theory. These lecture notes provide an introduction to the application of Dyson-Schwinger equations in this context, and a perspective on progress toward answering these key questions.

## Key claim

These lecture notes argue that Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a systematic, nonperturbative framework for addressing three fundamental questions in hadron physics, the mechanism of confinement, its connection to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and the origin of more than 98% of the mass of the observable universe, but the authors explicitly note that QCD is not known to be a rigorously well-defined theory and that assumptions must be made and their consequences explored, as a rigorous definition would require proving that all renormalisation constants are nonperturbatively well-behaved.

## Method

The paper derives Dyson-Schwinger equations from the functional integral formulation of quantum field theory, using the vanishing of the functional integral of a total derivative to obtain equations of motion for Green functions. It illustrates the procedure for QED by deriving the photon vacuum polarisation and the fermion gap equation, then discusses how these methods are extended to QCD to study hadron properties. The authors introduce generating functionals for connected and one-particle-irreducible (1PI) Green functions, and show how functional derivatives of the 1PI generating functional yield inverses of propagators and proper vertices.

## Key numbers

* The free-fermion propagator is given by \(S_0(p)= (p\!\!\slash\, + m)/(p^2-m^2+i\eta^+)\), with the spin-degeneracy factor "2" in the fermion determinant, which becomes "2 \(N_c\)" when colour is included in QCD.
* The logarithm of the fermion determinant for a free Dirac field is \(\mathrm{Ln}\, N_0^\psi = \int d^4 x \int d^4 p/(2\pi)^4\; 2\,\ln \Delta_0(p^2)\), where \(\Delta_0(p^2)=1/(p^2-m^2+i\eta^+)\).
* The paper states that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and confinement together may explain the origin of "more than 98% of the mass of the observable universe."

## Context

These lecture notes provide an introduction to the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to nonperturbative QCD, extending the foundational work of Dyson (1949) and Schwinger (1951). The paper builds on the functional integral methods described in Itzykson & Zuber (1980) and Pascual & Tarrach (1984), and discusses the Faddeev-Popov determinant and ghost fields for gauge fixing. The work enables future calculations of meson and baryon properties, including nucleon and Delta masses and nucleon electromagnetic form factors, via the Faddeev equation, and addresses open questions about the rigorous definition of QCD as a quantum field theory.
