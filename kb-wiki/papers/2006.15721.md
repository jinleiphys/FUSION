# Observable signatures of initial state momentum anisotropies in nuclear collisions

> We show that the correlation between the elliptic momentum anisotropy, $v_2$, and the average transverse momentum, $[p_T]$, at fixed multiplicity in small system nuclear collisions carries information on the origin of the observed momentum anisotropy. A calculation using a hybrid IP-Glasma+\textsc{Music}+UrQMD model that includes contributions from final state response to the initial geometry as well as initial state momentum anisotropies of the Color Glass Condensate, predicts a characteristic sign change of the correlator $\hatρ(v_2^2,[p_T])$ as a function of charged particle multiplicity in p+Au and d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200\,{\rm GeV}$, and p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02\,{\rm TeV}$. This sign change is absent in calculations without initial state momentum anisotropies. The model further predicts a qualitative difference between the centrality dependence of $\hatρ(v_2^2,[p_T])$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200\,{\rm GeV}$ and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02\,{\rm TeV}$, with only the latter showing a sign change in peripheral events. Predictions for O+O collisions at different collision energy show a similar behavior. Experimental observation of these distinct qualitative features of $\hatρ(v_2^2,[p_T])$ in small and large systems would constitute strong evidence for the presence and importance of initial state momentum anisotropies predicted by the Color Glass Condensate effective theory.

## Key claim

The correlator $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ between elliptic flow and mean transverse momentum at fixed multiplicity exhibits a sign change from positive to negative with increasing multiplicity in small systems (p+Au, d+Au, p+Pb) when initial-state momentum anisotropies from the Color Glass Condensate are included; this sign change is absent in calculations that only include geometry-driven final-state effects.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid IP-Glasma+Music+UrQMD model that includes both final-state hydrodynamic response to initial geometry and initial-state momentum anisotropies from the Color Glass Condensate. They computed the correlator $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ at fixed multiplicity for p+Au, d+Au, p+Pb, O+O, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and compared it to estimators based purely on initial spatial eccentricity ($\varepsilon_2$) and initial momentum anisotropy ($\varepsilon_p$). They also performed a control calculation using only the initial energy density without initial momentum anisotropies.

## Key numbers

- In d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ changes sign from positive to negative with increasing multiplicity, approaching the geometric estimator at high multiplicity and the initial momentum anisotropy estimator at low multiplicity
- In p+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, a sign change of $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ is clearly visible as a function of $dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta$
- In O+O collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ is positive for all multiplicities, while at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV it changes sign twice as a function of centrality
- In Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ remains positive for all centralities, while in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV it shows a sign change in peripheral events
- The correlator is computed in the transverse momentum range $0.2$ GeV $< p_T < 2$ GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the correlation between initial momentum anisotropy and final elliptic flow in small systems (Schenke 2019) and the correlator $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ introduced by Bozek (2016). It contradicts purely geometry-driven models that produce negative $\hat{\rho}(v_2^2,[p_T])$ for all multiplicities in small systems, and provides an experimentally observable signature to distinguish between initial-state and final-state origins of azimuthal anisotropies. The predictions for Au+Au at RHIC versus Pb+Pb at LHC enable experimental tests using existing data from the ATLAS Collaboration (2019) and future O+O runs at both facilities.
