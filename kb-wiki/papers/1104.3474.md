# Current constraints on the EFT for the ΛN --> N N transition

> The relation between the low energy constants appearing in the effective field theory description of the ΛN --> N N transition potential and the parameters of the one-meson-exchange model previously developed are obtained. We extract the relative importance of the different exchange mechanisms included in the meson picture by means of a comparison to the corresponding operational structures appearing in the effective approach. The ability of this procedure to obtain the weak baryon-baryon-meson couplings for a possible scalar exchange is also discussed.

## Key claim

The low-energy constants (LECs) of the effective field theory (EFT) for the ΛN → NN transition can be related to the parameters of a one-meson-exchange (OME) model via a low-momentum expansion, but this mapping is model-dependent because the strong couplings and cut-offs are taken from specific strong-interaction models (Nijmegen NSC97f and Jülich B) and the weak couplings are fixed by SU(3) symmetry and the pole model, not by direct experimental data.

## Method

The authors perform a low-momentum expansion of the regularized OME potentials (excluding π and K, which are explicitly included in both approaches) up to O(q²/M²) and match the resulting contact terms to the operator basis of the EFT four-fermion potential. They then compute the LECs at leading order (LO) using the meson-exchange parameters from two strong-interaction models (Nijmegen NSC97f and Jülich B) and compare these values to LECs obtained from a direct fit to hypernuclear decay observables.

## Key numbers

- The weak coupling constants are given in units of G_F m_π² = 2.21 × 10⁻⁷; for example, the weak π-exchange constants are B_π = -7.15 and A_π = 1.05 (Nijmegen NSC97f model).
- The LO LEC C₀⁰ from the OME expansion is -0.22 ± 0.11 (Nijmegen) and -0.15 ± 0.08 (Jülich B), while the LO fit to data gives C₀⁰ = -0.33 ± 0.04.
- The LO LEC C₀¹ from the OME expansion is -0.05 ± 0.03 (Nijmegen) and -0.10 ± 0.05 (Jülich B), while the LO fit to data gives C₀¹ = -0.07 ± 0.02.
- The momentum transfer in the weak reaction is |q| ~ 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the EFT formulation of the ΛN → NN transition first developed by Jun (2000) and by Pérez-Obiol, Parreño, and Juliá-Díaz (2005) by performing a resonance saturation mapping to the OME model. It is partly motivated by the possible role of a scalar σ-meson exchange, which has been included in some OME treatments (e.g., Itonaga et al., 1995; Oset et al., 2001; Bauer and Machleidt, 2005; Chumillas et al., 2007) but is not part of the ground-state meson octet. The results enable future higher-order EFT descriptions and provide constraints on the weak baryon-baryon-sigma couplings.
