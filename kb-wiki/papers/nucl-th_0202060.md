# Field-theoretical description of the multichannel gamma-p scattering reaction in the Delta resonance region and determination of the magnetic moment of the Delta+ resonance

> The cross-sections of the $γp-γ' N'$, $γp-π' N'$ and $γp-γ'π' N'$ reactions are calculated in the framework of the field-theoretical one-particle ($π,ω,ρ$-mesons, nucleon and $Δ$-resonance) exchange model. Unlike the other relativistic approaches, our resulting amplitudes of the $γp$ multichannel reactions require one-variable covariant vertex functions as input ingredient and every diagram of these amplitudes satisfies the current conservation condition in the Coulomb gauge. The complete set of the model independent skeleton diagrams for the $γ p\toγ'π'N'$ reaction is presented. The separable model of the $πN$ interaction is generalized to construct the spin 3/2 particle propagator of the $Δ$-resonance. This procedure allows to obtain the $πN-Δ$ form factor and $Δ$ propagator directly from the $πN$ $P_{33}$ phase shifts. The numerical calculation of the differential cross section of the $γp-γ' N'$, $γp-{π^o}' N'$ and $γp-γ'{π^o}'N'$ reactions are performed with two different separable models of the $Δ$ propagator and with the propagator of Breit-Wigner shape. It is demonstrated that the numerical description of these reactions in the $Δ$-resonance region are very sensitive to the form of the $Δ$-propagator. The sensitivity of the cross-sections of the $γp\toγ'{π^o}'p'$ reaction to the magnitude of the $Δ^+$ magnetic moment is examined and the most convenient kinematical region for the determination of the magnetic moment of the $Δ^+$-resonance from the forthcoming data is indicated.

## Key claim

The cross sections of the γp → γ'N', γp → π'N', and γp → γ'π'N' reactions in the Δ resonance region are very sensitive to the form of the Δ propagator, and the γp → γ'π°p' reaction shows sufficient sensitivity to the Δ⁺ magnetic moment to allow its determination from forthcoming data, provided the calculations are performed in a field-theoretical framework that avoids off-mass-shell ambiguities by using only on-mass-shell vertex functions.

## Method

The authors constructed amplitudes for the multichannel γp reactions using old-fashioned perturbation theory (spectral decomposition over asymptotic states) with one-particle exchange of π, ω, ρ mesons, nucleons, and the Δ resonance. They generalized the separable model of πN interaction to construct a spin-3/2 Δ propagator directly from πN P₃₃ phase shifts, and performed numerical calculations of differential cross sections using two different separable models of the Δ propagator and a Breit-Wigner shape propagator. The Coulomb gauge was employed to ensure current conservation for every diagram without requiring tree-level approximations.

## Key numbers

- The Δ resonance region is centered at about 400 MeV photon energy.
- The Breit-Wigner mass of the Δ resonance is mΔ = 1232 MeV with width ΓΔ = 120 MeV; the pole mass is mΔpole = 1210 MeV with width ΓΔpole = 100 MeV.
- For photon energies kγ ≈ 350–450 MeV, the zero-component difference E_p'_N − E_p_N is approximately 40–50 MeV, demonstrating the importance of retardation effects over tree approximations.
- The form factors F₁,₂(t) in the γN vertex at these energies are approximately 0.85–0.96.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical approach of Machavariani and collaborators (previous papers M1, M2, M3, M5) to provide a unified description of Compton scattering, pion photoproduction, and the γp → γ'π°p' reaction, incorporating retardation effects that are neglected in tree-level calculations. The proposal to determine the Δ magnetic moment from reactions with γπN final states originated with Kondratyuk and collaborators, and numerical estimates of the Δ⁺ magnetic moment dependence were previously made in refs. M5, Dr1, and Dr2. The first experimental data on γp → γ'π°p' were obtained by the A2/TAPS collaboration at MAMI (Kotulla), and future experiments using the Crystal Ball detector at MAMI are planned (Beck). The approach avoids the off-mass-shell ambiguities inherent in effective spin-3/2 Lagrangian methods (Pascalutsa, Castro, Drechsel) by constructing the Δ propagator directly from πN phase shifts.
