# Relevance of glueball bound states in the Yang-Mills plasma within a many-body $T$-matrix approach

> The strongly coupled phase of Yang-Mills plasma with gauge group SU(3) is studied in a $T$-matrix approach. The existence of lowest-lying glueballs, interpreted as bound states of two transverse gluons (quasi-particles in a many-body setup), is analyzed in a non-perturbative scattering formalism with the input of lattice-QCD static potentials. The relevance of the singlet and the (colored) octet and 27 channels at finite temperature is discussed. We compute the equation of state of the system in Dashen, Ma and Bernstein's formulation of statistical mechanics and compare to quenched SU(3) lattice data. Further analysis for the general case of SU(N) is envisaged.

## Key claim
Glueball bound states in the Yang-Mills plasma survive up to temperatures of about 1.3-1.5 T_c in the singlet and octet channels, but the authors note that using the internal energy U_1 as the interaction potential provides an upper bound on dissociation temperatures, meaning the actual survival temperature may be lower.

## Method
The authors solve a 3D-reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation for the in-medium two-body T-matrix of transverse gluons, using lattice QCD static potentials (Casimir-scaled from quark-antiquark to gluon-gluon channels) as input. They compute the equation of state via Dashen, Ma and Bernstein's formulation of statistical mechanics, which expresses the grand potential in terms of bound state contributions below threshold and scattering phase shifts above threshold. The quasi-gluon mass is treated as a temperature-dependent parameter with a bare value of 0.7 GeV, and the interaction potential is taken from the internal energy U_1 of a quark-antiquark pair in quenched SU(3) lattice simulations.

## Key numbers
- Bare gluon mass parameter: m_g^0 = 0.7 GeV
- Singlet scalar glueball mass at zero temperature: 2.17 GeV (compared to lattice value 1.73 GeV)
- Singlet pseudoscalar glueball mass at zero temperature: 2.39 GeV (compared to lattice value 2.59 GeV)
- Singlet tensor glueball mass at zero temperature: 2.34 GeV (compared to lattice value 2.40 GeV)
- Singlet scalar bound state dissociates at about 1.3 T_c, with considerable strength remaining at threshold up to about 1.5 T_c
- Quasi-gluon mass set to 1.22 GeV in the fixed-mass calculation (using m_g^0 = 0.7 GeV and delta = V_sb/2)

## Context
This work extends the T-matrix approach previously applied to quark-gluon plasma by Rapp, Cabrera, Hess, and Riek to the pure gauge SU(3) sector, using lattice QCD static potentials from Kaczmarek and collaborators. The results are compared to quenched SU(3) lattice data on the equation of state from Panero and to glueball spectral function analysis by the CLQCD Collaboration. The approach enables calculation of bulk thermodynamic properties such as shear viscosity and allows extension to SU(N) gauge groups and the exceptional group G_2.
