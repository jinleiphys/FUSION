# Schwinger Mechanism for Gluon Pair Production in the Presence of Arbitrary Time Dependent Chromo-Electric Field in Arbitrary Gauge

> We study non-perturbative gluon pair production from arbitrary time dependent chromo-electric field E^a(t) with arbitrary color index a =1,2,...8 via Schwinger mechanism in arbitrary covariant background gauge α. We show that the probability of non-perturbative gluon pair production per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum \frac{dW}{d^4xd^2p_T} is independent of gauge fixing parameter α. Hence the result obtained in the Fynman-'t Hooft gauge, α=1, is the correct gauge invariant and gauge parameter αindependent result.

## Key claim

The probability of non-perturbative gluon pair production per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum from an arbitrary time-dependent chromo-electric field is independent of the gauge fixing parameter α used in the covariant background gauge, provided the shift conjecture (which is not yet established) is valid.

## Method

The author starts from the background field method of QCD, writing the gauge field as a sum of a classical chromo-field A_μ^a and a quantum gluon field Q_μ^a, and keeps terms up to quadratic in Q for gluon pair production. The vacuum-to-vacuum transition amplitude is evaluated via path integration, separating the one-loop effective action (gluon pair production) from the tadpole effective action (single gluon). The gauge parameter α dependence is isolated by writing the operator M_μν^{ab}[A] as the α=1 term plus an α-dependent term proportional to (D_μ D_ν)^{ab}, and the trace of the logarithm is expanded and summed using the shift theorem to show cancellation of α-dependent contributions between the interacting and free parts.

## Key numbers

- The gluon pair production probability per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum is given by: dW_{g(ḡ)}/(dt d³x d²p_T) = (1/(4π³)) Σ_{j=1}^3 |gΛ_j(t)| ln[1 + exp(-π p_T²/|gΛ_j(t)|)]
- The eigenvalues Λ_j are defined via: Λ₁² = C₁(t)/2 [1 - cos θ(t)]; Λ_{2,3}² = C₁(t)/2 [1 + cos(π/3 ± θ(t))]; with cos 3θ(t) = -1 + 6 C₂(t)/C₁³(t)
- The two independent time-dependent Casimir/gauge invariants in SU(3) are: C₁(t) = [E^a(t)E^a(t)] and C₂(t) = [d_{abc}E^a(t)E^b(t)E^c(t)]²
- The tadpole effective action per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum is zero for any non-vanishing transverse momentum p_T, as it contains a factor δ^(2)(p_T)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of Schwinger mechanism for gluon pair production from arbitrary time-dependent chromo-electric fields in the Feynman-'t Hooft gauge (α=1) to arbitrary covariant background gauge α. It builds on Schwinger's original 1951 proper-time method for electron-positron pair production from a constant electric field, and on the author's earlier extensions to time-dependent fields in QED and QCD for quarks and gluons. The result shows that the α=1 result is the correct gauge-invariant and gauge-parameter-independent result, and the author notes that gluon production from classical chromo-fields may be relevant to studying quark-gluon plasma production at RHIC and LHC.
