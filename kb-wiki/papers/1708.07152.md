# Determination of the $^{36}$Mg($n,ő≥$)$^{37}$Mg reaction rate from Coulomb dissociation of $^{37}$Mg

> We use the Coulomb dissociation (CD) method to calculate the rate of the $^{36}$Mg(${n,ő≥}$)$^{37}$Mg radiative capture reaction. The CD cross sections of the $^{37}$Mg nucleus on a $^{208}$Pb target at the beam energy of 244 MeV/nucleon, for which new experimental data have recently become available, were calculated within the framework of a finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory that is extended to include the projectile deformation effects. Invoking the principle of detailed balance, these cross sections are used to determine the excitation function and subsequently the rate of the $^{36}$Mg($n,ő≥$)$^{37}$Mg reaction. We compare these rates to those of the $^{36}$Mg($őĪ,n$)$^{39}$Si reaction calculated within a Hauser-Feshbach model. We find that for $T_9$ as large as up to 1.0 (in units of 10$^9$ K) the $^{36}$Mg($n,ő≥$)$^{37}$Mg reaction is much faster than the $^{36}$Mg($őĪ,n$)$^{39}$Si one. The inclusion of the effects of $^{37}$Mg projectile deformation in the breakup calculations, enhances the ($n,ő≥$) reaction rate even further. Therefore, it is highly unlikely that the $(n,ő≥)$ $ő≤$-decay $r$-process flow will be broken at the $^{36}$Mg isotope by the $őĪ$-process.

## Key claim

The authors use the Coulomb dissociation method to determine that the $^{36}$Mg($n,\gamma$)$^{37}$Mg reaction is much faster than the $^{36}$Mg($\alpha,n$)$^{39}$Si reaction for stellar temperatures $T_9$ up to 1.0 (in units of $10^9$ K), with the qualification that this conclusion depends on the adopted ground-state configuration of $^{37}$Mg (either $^{36}$Mg(0$^+$)$\otimes$2$p_{3/2}n$ or $^{36}$Mg(0$^+$)$\otimes$2$s_{1/2}n$) and on the values of the one-neutron separation energy and deformation parameter.

## Method

The Coulomb dissociation cross sections of $^{37}$Mg on a $^{208}$Pb target at 244 MeV/nucleon were calculated within a finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory extended to include projectile deformation effects. The photodissociation cross sections for $^{37}$Mg($\gamma,n$)$^{36}$Mg were extracted from these CD cross sections using the virtual photon number method, and then converted to the radiative capture cross sections $^{36}$Mg($n,\gamma$)$^{37}$Mg via the principle of detailed balance. The reaction rates were obtained by averaging these cross sections over a Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distribution, and compared to the $^{36}$Mg($\alpha,n$)$^{39}$Si rates calculated with the Hauser-Feshbach code NON-SMOKER from Rauscher (2001).

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy $S_n$ = 0.35 $\pm$ 0.06 MeV for the $^{36}$Mg(0$^+$)$\otimes$2$p_{3/2}n$ configuration, and $S_n$ = 0.50 $\pm$ 0.07 MeV for the $^{36}$Mg(0$^+$)$\otimes$2$s_{1/2}n$ configuration.
* The $^{36}$Mg($n,\gamma$)$^{37}$Mg reaction rate changes from 10 cm$^3$ mole$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$ at $T_9$ = 0.05 to about 5000 cm$^3$ mole$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$ at $T_9$ = 10, with a value around 200 cm$^3$ mole$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$ at $T_9$ = 0.6.
* The maximum contribution to the reaction rate at $T_9$ = 1 comes from center-of-mass energies between 0.2 and 0.3 MeV.
* The quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$ for the $^{36}$Mg(0$^+$)$\otimes$2$p_{3/2}n$ configuration is constrained to be below 0.32 based on comparison with experimental one-neutron removal cross sections.
* The asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) for the $p_{3/2}$ state with $S_n$ = 0.35 MeV ranges from 0.383 fm$^{-1/2}$ at $\beta_2$ = 0.0 to 0.370 fm$^{-1/2}$ at $\beta_2$ = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb dissociation analysis of $^{37}$Mg breakup data from Kobayashi (2014) to determine astrophysical reaction rates, following the earlier speculation by Terasawa (2001) that the $^{36}$Mg($\alpha,n$)$^{39}$Si reaction might break the $r$-process flow at $^{36}$Mg. The results contradict the suggestion that the $\alpha$-process dominates at low temperatures, showing instead that for $T_9$ below 2 the $(n,\gamma)$ reaction is faster, enabling the classical $r$-process path through $^{36}$Mg to proceed without interruption by $\alpha$-capture. The method demonstrates the power of Coulomb dissociation as an indirect technique for reactions involving short-lived nuclei like $^{36}$Mg (half-life approximately 5 ms) that cannot be measured directly.
