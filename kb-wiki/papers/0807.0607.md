# Weak-Interacting Holographic QCD

> We propose a simple prescription for including low-energy weak-interactions into the framework of holographic QCD, based on the standard AdS/CFT dictionary of double-trace deformations. As our proposal enables us to calculate various electro-weak observables involving strongly coupled QCD, it opens a new perspective on phenomenological applications of holographic QCD. We illustrate efficiency and usefulness of our method by performing a few exemplar calculations; neutron beta decay, charged pion weak decay, and meson-nucleon parity non-conserving (PNC) couplings. The idea is general enough to be implemented in both Sakai-Sugimoto as well as Hard/Soft Wall models.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a prescription for including low-energy weak interactions into holographic QCD models by using the AdS/CFT dictionary for double-trace deformations, which modifies the UV boundary conditions of 5D gauge fields. The authors claim this method is valid for moderately higher energies before QCD asymptotic freedom sets in, not only at zero momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors implement effective four-fermi weak interactions as double-trace deformations of the QCD Lagrangian, which modify the UV boundary conditions of the 5D bulk gauge fields in holographic models. They apply this prescription to two prototype holographic QCD models: the top-down Sakai-Sugimoto model and the bottom-up Hard/Soft Wall model, solving for the normalizable modes without weak interactions first and then computing first-order-in-G_F non-normalizable perturbations. The method is illustrated through three exemplar calculations: charged pion weak decay, neutron beta decay, and meson-nucleon parity non-conserving couplings.

## Key numbers

- Charged pion decay constant: f_pi approximately 92 MeV, predicted by the model and matching experimental value
- Axial coupling constant g_A approximately 1.3 from the Sakai-Sugimoto model, compared with experimental value g_A_exp = 1.27
- The Sakai-Sugimoto model parameter kappa = pi/4 f_pi^2 approximately 7.45 x 10^-3, with M_KK approximately 0.95 GeV set to 1
- The Hard/Soft Wall model gauge coupling g_5^2 = 12 pi^2 / N_c = 4 pi^2

## Context

This work extends previous holographic QCD calculations that only addressed pure QCD and electromagnetic couplings, with the exception of Hambye (2005) whose method differed from the present approach. The prescription is general enough to be implemented in both the Sakai-Sugimoto model and the Hard/Soft Wall model, and enables calculation of various electroweak observables involving strongly coupled QCD. The method opens a new perspective on phenomenological applications of holographic QCD, allowing estimation of hadronic weak processes that were previously inaccessible in this framework.
