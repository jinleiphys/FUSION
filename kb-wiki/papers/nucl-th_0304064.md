# Searching for multi-boson effects and space-time asymmetries in particle production

> The influence of the multi-boson effects on pion multiplicities, single-pion spectra and two-pion correlation functions is discussed in terms of an analytically solvable model. It is argued that spectacular multi-boson effects are likely to be observed only in the rare events containing sufficiently high density fluctuations. The possibilities of unlike particle correlations for a study of the space-time asymmetries in particle production, including the sequence of particle emission, are demonstrated.

# Searching for multi-boson effects and space-time asymmetries in particle production

## Key claim
Multi-boson effects on pion multiplicities, single-pion spectra, and two-pion correlation functions are likely to be observable only in rare events with sufficiently high density fluctuations, not in typical heavy-ion collision events at present or future energies. The paper demonstrates that unlike-particle correlations can measure relative space-time asymmetries in particle production, including mean relative emission time delays as small as a few fm/c.

## Method
The author develops an analytically solvable Gaussian model in which pions are instantaneously emitted according to a Gaussian emission function characterized by dispersions in momentum and ordinary space, assuming a Poissonian distribution of original boson multiplicities. For unlike-particle correlations, the formalism uses the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in the continuous spectrum of two-particle states, with the equal-time approximation, to derive correlation functions sensitive to the odd term ~sin(k*·r*) that encodes space-time asymmetries. The method is tested by simulating correlation functions R+ and R- (separated by the sign of k*·v) for various two-particle systems using the event generators VENUS 5.14 and RQMD v2.3 for Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers
- Mean pion phase-space density at present energies is typically less than ~0.2, indicating rather small multi-boson effects.
- The intrinsic phase-space parameter ξ = η/(r₀Δ + 1/2)³ has a maximal value of 1 corresponding to explosion of the multiplicity distribution; comparing model phase-space density ~0.2 with experimental estimates gives ξ ≈ 0.4–0.5.
- The mean multiplicity ⟨n⟩ becomes close to the asymptotic scaling value ξ/(1-ξ) only at ξ > 0.9.
- For the K⁺K⁻ system, the Bohr radius a = -110 fm; for πK, πp, and Kp systems, a = ±249, ±226, and ±84 fm respectively.
- For sufficiently relativistic pairs (γv > 0.5), the R+/R- ratio can be sensitive to shifts in particle emission times of the order of a few fm/c.
- From RQMD simulations of 158 A·GeV Pb+Pb central collisions: for π⁺p, ⟨Δt⟩ = -0.5 fm, ⟨Δx⟩ = -6.2 fm, ⟨Δx*⟩ = -7.9 fm; for π⁺K⁺, ⟨Δt⟩ = 4.8 fm, ⟨Δx⟩ = -2.7 fm, ⟨Δx*⟩ = -7.9 fm; for K⁺p, ⟨Δt⟩ = -5.5 fm, ⟨Δx⟩ = -3.2 fm, ⟨Δx*⟩ = -0.4 fm.
- The Coulomb suppression of the global BE weight ω₂ is about (a r₀ Δ²)⁻¹, usually less than one per mill.

## Context
This work extends the Kopylov and Podgoretsky (1972) formalism for particle interferometry and the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1981) treatment of final-state interactions to include multi-boson effects and space-time asymmetries. The multi-boson model builds on earlier work by Pratt (1993) and references therein, using cumulant methods from Ameodo and Lednicky. The unlike-particle correlation method for measuring relative time delays was introduced by Lednicky, Lyuboshitz, Erazmus, and Nouais (1995) and is here applied to simulations using VENUS (Werner, 1993) and RQMD v2.3, with experimental confirmation at AGS reported by Miskowiec (1998) and at GANIL by Ghidini (1995) and Nouais (1996). The method enables measurement of relative emission time delays at time scales as small as 10⁻²³ s and can probe transient strange quark matter states decaying on strong interaction time scales.
