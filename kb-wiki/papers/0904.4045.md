# Non-factorized calculation of the process 3He(e,e'p)pn at medium energies

> The cross section and the transverse-longitudinal asymmetry A_{TL} of the three-body-break-up (3BBU) process 3He(e,e'p)pn have been calculated by a non-factorized and parameter-free approach based upon realistic few-body wave functions corresponding to the $AV18$ interaction, treating the rescattering of the struck nucleon within a generalized eikonal approximation. The results of calculations exhibit a good agreement with recent JLab experimental data and show that the Final State Interaction (FSI) of the struck proton in the region of missing momentum 300 < p_m < 600 MeV/c and removal energy E_m > p_m^2/4m_N is dominated by single nucleon rescattering, in agreement with nucleon-nucleon correlation predictions.

## Key claim

The cross section and transverse-longitudinal asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) for the three-body-break-up process \(^3He(e,e'p)pn\) are calculated in a non-factorized and parameter-free approach, showing good agreement with JLab data; the authors find that final state interaction (FSI) of the struck proton in the region \(300 < p_m < 600\) MeV/c and removal energy \(E_m > p_m^2/4m_N\) is dominated by single nucleon rescattering, consistent with nucleon-nucleon correlation predictions.

## Method

The authors used realistic few-body wave functions from a variational solution of the Schrödinger equation with the AV18 interaction, and treated final state interactions (FSI) via a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) that extends the Glauber approach by including a parallel momentum shift \(\Delta_z\) of nuclear origin. The nuclear current was computed in momentum space from Feynman diagrams, including single and double rescattering contributions, using the CC1 form of the nucleon current operator and elastic NN amplitudes from world data. The cross section and asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) were evaluated without factorizing the nucleon current from the nuclear overlap integral, except for a comparison where factorization was imposed.

## Key numbers

- The cross section was calculated at missing momentum values \(p_m = 440\) MeV/c and compared with experimental data; the non-factorized calculation differs by 5–7% from the factorized one at this momentum.
- At \(p_m = 440\) MeV/c, the factorized and non-factorized results differ by at most 10% at \(\phi = 180^\circ\).
- The region of interest for FSI dominance by single rescattering is \(300 \lesssim p_m \lesssim 600\) MeV/c and removal energy \(E_m \gtrsim p_m^2/4m_N\).
- The correlation peak position is given by \(E_2^* = E_m - E_{min} \sim p_m^2/(4m_N)\) in the non-relativistic limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-factorized calculation of the two-body break-up channel \(^3He(e,e'p)^2H\) (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 2005) to the three-body break-up channel, using the same theoretical framework. The results confirm that FSI in the correlation region is dominated by single rescattering, supporting the two-nucleon correlation picture, and show that the factorized approximation (FA) breaks down at higher missing momenta. The calculations enable future comparisons with JLab data on \(A_{TL}\) and provide a benchmark for testing short-range correlation models in few-body systems.
