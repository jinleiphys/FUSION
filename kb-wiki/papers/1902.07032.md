# Bayesian analysis of the crust-core transition with a compressible liquid-drop model

> The crust-core phase transition of neutron stars is quantitatively studied within a unified meta-modelling of the nuclear Equation of State (EoS). The variational equations in the crust are solved within a Compressible Liquid Drop (CLD) approach, with surface parameters consistently optimized for each EoS set on experimental nuclear mass data. When EoS parameters are taken from known Skyrme or RMF functionals, the transition point of those models is nicely reproduced. A model-independent probability distribution of EoS parameters and of the transition density and pressure is determined with a Bayesian analysis, where the prior is given by an uncorrelated distribution of parameters within the present empirical uncertainties, and constraints are applied both from neutron star physics and ab-initio modelling. We show that the characteristics of the transition point are largely independent of the high density properties of the EoS, while ab-initio EoS calculations of neutron and symmetric matter are far more constraining. The most influential parameter for the determination of the transition point governs the surface properties of extremely neutron rich matter, and it is strongly unconstrained. This explains the large dispersion of existing predictions of the transition point. Only if the surface tension is fixed to a reasonable but somewhat arbitrary value, strong correlations with isovector parameters ($L_{sym},K_{sym}$ and $Q_{sym}$) are recovered. Within the present experimental and theoretical uncertainties on those parameters, we estimate the transition density as $n_t= 0.072\pm 0.011$ fm$^{-3}$ and the transition pressure as $P_t=0.339\pm0.115$ MeV fm$^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The crust-core transition density and pressure are largely independent of the high-density properties of the equation of state, while ab-initio calculations of neutron and symmetric matter at low density are far more constraining; the most influential parameter for the transition point governs the surface properties of extremely neutron-rich matter and remains strongly unconstrained, explaining the large dispersion of existing predictions.

## Method

The authors solved the variational equations for non-uniform matter within a compressible liquid-drop (CLD) approach, minimizing the energy density in a Wigner-Seitz cell with respect to cluster size, isospin asymmetry, density, and neutron gas density. They used a meta-modelling technique for the homogeneous matter equation of state (EoS) with a Taylor expansion up to order N=4 around saturation, and a parametrized surface tension from Ravenhall et al. (1983) with an extra parameter p governing the isovector behavior at extreme isospin. A Bayesian analysis was performed with an uncorrelated flat prior on EoS parameters within empirical uncertainties, applying constraints from ab-initio many-body perturbation theory calculations by Drischler et al. (2016) and from neutron star maximum mass.

## Key numbers

- Transition density: \(n_t = 0.072 \pm 0.011\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Transition pressure: \(P_t = 0.339 \pm 0.115\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)
- For the SLy4 functional with p=3, the transition density was 0.079 fm\(^{-3}\) and transition pressure 0.355 MeV fm\(^{-3}\)
- The prior range for the surface tension parameter p was [2.5, 3.5], based on reproducing transition points from 13 different Skyrme and RMF functionals
- The low-density b parameter was varied in the interval [1, 10] relative to \(\ln 2\), corresponding to the Taylor expansion breaking down between \(n_{min} = 0.1 n_{sat}\) and \(n_{min} = n_{sat}\)

## Context

This work extends the compressible liquid-drop approach of Baym, Bethe and Pethick (1971) and Douchin and Haensel (2001) by performing a Bayesian analysis to quantify model dependence, complementing earlier sensitivity studies by Ducoin et al. (2011) that identified \(L_{sym}\) as the most influential parameter. The results enable model-independent predictions of crust thickness and moment of inertia for neutron stars, as presented in a companion paper by the same authors, and are consistent with the independent Bayesian study by Holt (2018) on radii and tidal polarizabilities.
