# Weak Strangeness and Eta Production

> We have studied strange particle production off nucleons through $ΔS =0 $ and $|ΔS| = 1$ channels, and specifically single kaon/antikaon, eta, associated particle production for neutrino/antineutrino induced processes as well as antineutrino induced single hyperon production processes. We have developed a microscopical model based on the SU(3) chiral Lagrangians. The basic parameters of the model are $f_π$, the pion decay constant, Cabibbo angle, the proton and neutron magnetic moments and the axial vector coupling constants for the baryons octet. For antikaon production we have also included $Σ^*(1385)$ resonance and for eta production $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ resonances are included.

# Weak Strangeness and Eta Production

## Key claim
The authors present a microscopical model based on SU(3) chiral Lagrangians for strange particle production in neutrino/antineutrino-nucleon reactions, but the cross sections are calculated only for free nucleon targets and do not include full nuclear medium effects beyond Fermi motion and Pauli blocking, which are found to be negligible.

## Method
The authors developed a model using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory at the baryon and meson octet level for background terms, with parameters fixed by the pion decay constant, Cabibbo angle, nucleon magnetic moments, and axial vector coupling constants. For antikaon production, the Σ*(1385) resonance was included, and for eta production, the S11(1535) and S11(1650) resonances were added. The vector and axial vector form factors were determined using helicity amplitudes and a Goldberger-Treiman relation, with a global dipole form factor of mass 1 GeV applied to the hadronic current.

## Key numbers
- For antineutrino-induced π⁰ production in ¹²C averaged over the MiniBooNE flux, the contribution from hyperon excitations is almost 75% of the contribution from Δ excitation in the peak region of the Q² distribution.
- The ratio of squared couplings for the vertices NK⁰Λ to NK⁰Σ⁰ is g²_NKΛ / g²_NKΣ ≈ 14.
- The antineutrino energy region where hyperon contributions to pion production are significant is 0.6–1.0 GeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of weak strangeness production, which were limited in number and suffered from poor statistics and large systematic errors in experiments. The model builds on the chiral perturbation theory approach of Rafi Alam (2010) and the antineutrino hyperon production prescription of Singh (2006). The results are relevant for neutrino oscillation analyses and background estimation in proton decay searches at experiments such as MiniBooNE and Super-Kamiokande, where antineutrino-induced pion production from hyperon decays can be significant at low energies.
