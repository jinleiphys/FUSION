# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering in Effective Field Theory

> I outline the effective field theory (EFT) calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering which was recently carried out to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) by Noam Shoresh and myself. In this calculation only potential pion contributions are included. These are the leading contributions from pions. A toy model was also considered in which potential pions are the only pion effects. The effective range expansion (ERE), which is valid for low energies, is used to derive matching conditions on some of the EFT couplings. Renormalization group flow analysis is used to fix the rest of the couplings leaving no free parameters at NNLO.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective field theory (EFT) calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering in the \({}^{1}S_{0}\) channel to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) that includes only potential pion contributions, not radiation or soft pions; the authors argue this is sufficient for external energies close to the pion production threshold, but acknowledge that at much lower energies radiation and soft pions could contribute.

## Method

The authors use the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) power counting scheme with dimensional regularization and the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme. They compute the scattering amplitude to NNLO by summing bubble chains of contact interactions non-perturbatively at leading order and treating pion exchanges and higher-derivative contact operators perturbatively. The effective range expansion (ERE) is used to derive matching conditions on some couplings, while renormalization group (RG) flow analysis fixes the remaining couplings, leaving no free parameters at NNLO. A two-Yukawa toy model with scalar \(\pi\) and \(\rho\) exchanges is also studied to validate the approach.

## Key numbers

* Singlet channel scattering length in the toy model: \(a = -24.7\ \mathrm{fm}\), obtained by tuning \(g_\rho = 13.5\).
* Toy model parameters: \(\gamma = -7.743\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(s_0 = 1.584\ \mathrm{fm}\), used to determine the EFT couplings.
* Breakdown scale for the toy model: \(\sim 520\ \mathrm{MeV}\), slightly higher than \(m_\rho/2\).
* Renormalized couplings for the toy model at \(\mu = m_\pi\) (in \(\mathrm{fm}^2\)): \(C_0 = -3.526\), \(C_2 m_\pi^2 = 0.06912\), \(B_{0,0} = 1.412\), \(B_{0,1} = 0.5620\), \(B_{2,-1} m_\pi^2 = 0.2886\), \(C_4 m_\pi^4 = -0.001355\).
* Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\); rho meson mass: \(m_\rho = 770\ \mathrm{MeV}\); nucleon mass: \(M = 940\ \mathrm{MeV}\); pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 132\ \mathrm{MeV}\); axial coupling: \(g_A = 1.25\).

## Context

This work extends the KSW power counting scheme (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998) for nucleon-nucleon scattering in effective field theory by computing the amplitude to NNLO with only potential pion contributions. The approach enables a parameter-free prediction at NNLO when combined with RG flow analysis and matching conditions, and it provides a framework for testing the convergence of the EFT expansion. The toy model with two Yukawa potentials serves as a controlled environment to validate the power counting before applying it to real \({}^{1}S_{0}\) scattering data.
