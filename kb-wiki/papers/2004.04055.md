# Elastic and inelastic scattering of neutrinos and weakly interacting massive particles on nuclei

> The event rates for WIMP-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering processes, expected to be detected in ton-scale rare-event detectors, are investigated. We focus on nuclear isotopes that correspond to the target nuclei of current and future experiments looking for WIMP- and neutrino-nucleus events. The nuclear structure calculations, performed in the context of the deformed shell model, are based on Hartree-Fock intrinsic states with angular momentum projection and band mixing for both the elastic and the inelastic channels. Our predictions in the high-recoil-energy tail show that detectable distortions of the measured/expected signal may be interpreted through the inclusion of the non-negligible incoherent channels

## Key claim

The paper claims that for WIMP-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering in ton-scale detectors, the inclusion of incoherent (inelastic) channels can produce detectable distortions in the high-recoil-energy tail of the measured or expected signal, but this effect is conditional on the nuclear structure being calculated with the deformed shell model (DSM) and is most relevant for specific isotopes such as 127I, 133Cs, and 133Xe.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear structure calculations using the deformed shell model (DSM), which is based on Hartree-Fock deformed intrinsic states with angular momentum projection and band mixing, for the target nuclei 127I, 133Cs, and 133Xe (and also 23Na, 40Ar, and 73Ge for neutrino scattering). They computed energy spectra, magnetic moments, and spin structure functions for both elastic and inelastic scattering channels, then integrated these with standard WIMP and neutrino flux models to obtain differential event rates per unit detector mass.

## Key numbers

- For 127I, the DSM calculated magnetic moment for the ground state (5/2+) is 1.207 nm, compared to the experimental value of 2.813 nm (using bare gyromagnetic ratios); using effective gyromagnetic ratios (g_l=1.5, g_s^p=4, g_s^n=-3.0) gives 2.742 nm.
- For 133Cs, the DSM calculated magnetic moment for the 7/2+ state is 1.69 nm (experimental 2.582 nm); with effective gyromagnetic ratios it becomes 3.884 nm.
- For 133Xe, the DSM calculated magnetic moment for the 3/2+ state is 1.26 nm (experimental 0.81 nm); with effective gyromagnetic ratios it becomes 1.280 nm.
- The harmonic oscillator size parameter b used for 127I is 2.09 fm, for 133Cs and 133Xe it is 2.11 fm.
- The local WIMP density is taken as 0.3 GeV/cm^3, the Sun's velocity relative to the galactic center is 220 km/s, and the escape velocity from the galaxy is 625 km/s.

## Context

This work extends the deformed shell model (DSM) to nuclei beyond A=90 for the first time, specifically 127I, 133Cs, and 133Xe, which are used in current and future dark matter and coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS) experiments. It builds on prior DSM calculations for 73Ge by Sahu (2017) and on earlier work by Papoulias (2018) that investigated new physics contributions to CEvNS event rates. The results enable more accurate predictions of the neutrino floor for direct dark matter detection experiments and highlight the need to include inelastic channels when interpreting high-recoil-energy signals.
