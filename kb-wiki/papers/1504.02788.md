# Many-body forces, isospin asymmetry and dense hyperonic matter

> The equation of state (EoS) of asymmetric nuclear matter at high densities is a key topic for the description of matter inside neutron stars. The determination of the properties of asymmetric nuclear matter, such as the symmetry energy ($a_{sym}$) and the slope of the symmetry energy ($L_0$) at saturation density, has been exaustively studied in order to better constrain the nuclear matter EoS. However, differently from symmetric matter properties that are reasonably constrained, the symmetry energy and its slope still large uncertainties in their experimental values. Regarding this subject, some studies point towards small values of the slope of the symmetry energy, while others suggest rather higher values. Such a lack of agreement raised a certain debate in the scientific community. In this paper, we aim to analyse the role of these properties on the behavior of asymmetric hyperonic matter. Using the formalism presented in Ref. (R.O. Gomes et al 2014}, which considers many-body forces contributions in the meson-baryon coupling, we calculate the EoS of asymmetric hyperonic matter and apply it to describe hyperonic matter and hyperon stars.

## Key claim

The paper analyzes how the symmetry energy and its slope affect dense hyperonic matter and hyperon stars, finding that while these asymmetric matter properties have small effects on maximum stellar masses, they significantly impact particle populations and neutron star radii. The authors qualify that their results depend on the specific many-body forces parameterization (ζ) and the range of symmetry energy values considered (25–33 MeV for a_sym and 73–100 MeV for L_0).

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with derivative coupling for mesons to incorporate many-body forces, including σ, ω, ρ, δ, and φ mesons. They calculate the equation of state (EoS) of asymmetric hyperonic matter for the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and leptons, fitting coupling constants to reproduce saturation properties (ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, B/A = -15.75 MeV) and hyperon potential depths (U_Λ^N = -28 MeV, U_Σ^N = +30 MeV, U_Ξ^N = -18 MeV). They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for hyperon stars.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³; binding energy per baryon: B/A = -15.75 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass ratios m*_n/m_n: 0.66, 0.68, and 0.70 for ζ = 0.040, 0.049, and 0.059 respectively
- Compressibility modulus K₀: 297 MeV (ζ=0.040), 272 MeV (ζ=0.049), 253 MeV (ζ=0.059)
- Symmetry energy a_sym⁰ varied between 25–33 MeV; symmetry energy slope L₀ varied between 73–115 MeV
- Hyperon potential depths: U_Λ^N = -28 MeV, U_Σ^N = +30 MeV, U_Ξ^N = -18 MeV
- A shift of ~15 MeV in L₀ changes the radius of a 1.4 M_⊙ canonical star by roughly 0.5 km
- A shift of 6 MeV in a_sym⁰ changes the radius of a 1.4 M_⊙ canonical star by ~0.4 km
- Highest maximum mass: M_max = 2.15 M_⊙ for the ζ = 0.040 parametrization

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Gomes et al. (2014) by systematically varying the symmetry energy and its slope to study their effects on hyperonic matter and hyperon stars. It addresses the ongoing debate in the literature, where studies such as Steiner (2012), Lattimer (2013), and Li (2013) suggest small values of L₀, while Chen (2005), Tsang (2012), Cozma (2013), Wang (2014), and Sotani (2015) suggest higher values. The results enable better constraints on the nuclear matter EoS for neutron star modeling, particularly for determining radii of hyperon stars as suggested by Lopes (2014), Gomes (2014), and Oertel (2015).
