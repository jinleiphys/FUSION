# High order cumulants of the azimuthal anisotropy in the dilute-dense limit: Connected graphs

> We analytically compute higher order cumulants of the azimuthal anisotropy, $c_2\{2m\}$, and corresponding $v_2\{2m\}$ at high transverse momentum in the dilute-dense limit. The dense target is considered in the framework of the McLerran-Venugopolan model. The absolute values of the harmonics $v_2\{2m\}$ of the azimuthal anisotropy are approximately equal, $|v_2\{2m\}| \approx |v_2\{2m'\}| $, for large $m$ and $m'$. However, the harmonics with order $2m=4n$ are complex. We argue that this is a generic property of connected graphs, which remains true in the dense-dense limit.

## Key claim

Higher-order cumulants of azimuthal anisotropy computed from fully connected graphs in the Color Glass Condensate framework produce positive four-particle cumulants, meaning that the corresponding harmonic \(v_2\{4\}\) is complex (imaginary), which contradicts experimental observations at high multiplicities but may describe low-multiplicity collisions.

## Method

The author analytically computes higher-order cumulants \(c_2\{2m\}\) and harmonics \(v_2\{2m\}\) for high transverse momentum in the dilute-dense limit, modeling the dense target using the McLerran-Venugopalan model. The calculation involves a gradient expansion of the S-matrix for quarks scattering off the target's classical field, averaging over impact parameters with Gaussian and exponential correlation functions, and performing angular integrations to extract the cumulants from fully connected graphs.

## Key numbers

- For the Gaussian correlator, the four-particle cumulant is \(c_2^G\{4\} = \frac{1}{4} \left( \frac{\xi}{N_c^2-1} \right)^3\), leading to \((v_2^G\{4\})^4 = -\frac{1}{4} \left( \frac{\xi}{N_c^2-1} \right)^3 < 0\).
- For the exponential correlator, the four-particle cumulant is \(c_2^E\{4\} = \frac{1}{10} \left( \frac{\xi}{N_c^2-1} \right)^3\), leading to \((v_2^E\{4\})^4 = -\frac{1}{10} \left( \frac{\xi}{N_c^2-1} \right)^3 < 0\).
- For large order \(m\), the absolute value of the harmonics approaches \(\lim_{m\to\infty} |v_2\{m\}| = \frac{\xi}{N_c^2-1} \frac{j_{0,1}}{2}\), where \(j_{0,1}\) is the first zero of the Bessel function \(J_0(x)\).
- The coefficient relating cumulants to harmonics for order \(2m=6\) is \(1/\kappa_6 = -4\); for order \(2m=8\), \(1/\kappa_8 = 33\); for order \(2m=10\), \(1/\kappa_{10} = -456\).

## Context

This work extends the "glasma graph" approach to azimuthal anisotropy in the Color Glass Condensate framework, previously applied to two-particle correlations, to higher-order cumulants. It contrasts with hydrodynamic descriptions of p-Pb collisions at the LHC and d-Au collisions at RHIC, where higher-order harmonics are real. The results enable distinguishing between initial-state correlations from parton dynamics in the target wave function and final-state interactions (rescattering), with the complex \(v_2\{4\}\) suggesting that connected graphs dominate only at low multiplicities, while high multiplicities require other mechanisms such as those proposed by Kovner and Lublinsky (2011) or developed in subsequent CGC studies.
