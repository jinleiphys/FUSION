# Light Nuclei and Hypernuclei from Quantum Chromodynamics in the Limit of SU(3) Flavor Symmetry

> The binding energies of a range of nuclei and hypernuclei with atomic number A <= 4 and strangeness |s| <= 2, including the deuteron, di-neutron, H-dibaryon, 3He, Lambda 3He, Lambda 4He, and Lambda Lambda 4He, are calculated in the limit of flavor-SU(3) symmetry at the physical strange quark mass with quantum chromodynamics (without electromagnetic interactions). The nuclear states are extracted from Lattice QCD calculations performed with n_f=3 dynamical light quarks using an isotropic clover discretization of the quark-action in three lattice volumes of spatial extent L ~ 3.4 fm, 4.5 fm and 6.7 fm, and with a single lattice spacing b ~ 0.145 fm.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first lattice QCD calculations of hypernuclei with baryon number A > 2, specifically for s-shell nuclei and hypernuclei up to A = 5, but only in the limit of exact SU(3) flavor symmetry where the up, down, and strange quarks have equal masses at the physical strange quark mass, and without electromagnetic interactions. The calculations use a single lattice spacing and a pion mass of approximately 800 MeV, meaning the results cannot be directly compared to experiment and require future extrapolations to the physical light-quark masses and continuum limit.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using an isotropic clover fermion action with n_f = 3 dynamical light quarks on three lattice volumes with spatial extents L ~ 3.4 fm, 4.5 fm, and 6.7 fm at a single lattice spacing b ~ 0.145 fm. Multibaryon correlation functions were constructed from quark propagators using hadron-level and quark-level wavefunctions with definite quantum numbers, and binding energies were extracted from the energy shifts relative to the sum of free-baryon masses. The calculations were performed at the SU(3)-flavor symmetric point where m_pi = m_K = m_eta ~ 800 MeV, corresponding to the physical strange quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 805.9(0.6)(0.4)(8.9) MeV (statistical, fitting systematic, lattice spacing uncertainties)
- Octet baryon mass: m_B = 1.634(0)(0)(18) GeV
- Deuteron binding energy: B_d = 19.5(3.6)(3.1)(0.2) MeV
- Di-neutron binding energy: B_nn = 15.9(2.7)(2.7)(0.2) MeV
- H-dibaryon binding energy: B_H = 74.6(3.3)(3.3)(0.8) MeV
- Helium-3 (3He) binding energy: B_het = 53.9(7.1)(8.0)(0.6) MeV
- Lambda-3He (Lambda 3He) binding energy (J=1/2+): B_hetLJhalf = 69(5)(12)(0) MeV
- Lambda-3He binding energy (J=3/2+): B_hetLJthreehalf = 100(10)(10) MeV
- Helium-4 (4He) binding energy: B_hef = 107(12)(21)(1) MeV
- Lambda-4He (Lambda 4He) binding energy: B_hefL = 107(12)(21)(1) MeV (same as 4He)
- Lambda-Lambda-4He (Lambda Lambda 4He) binding energy: B_hefLL = 156(16)(21)(2) MeV
- Five-body state (Lambda Xi0 p n n) binding energy: B_hymag = 245(28)(81) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of two-baryon systems by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane et al., 2010, 2011) and others (Inoue et al., 2010, 2011) that provided evidence for the H-dibaryon bound state, and earlier quenched calculations of 3He and 4He (Yamazaki et al., 2009). It enables future studies that will perform calculations at smaller lattice spacings and lighter quark masses to extrapolate to physical values, and establishes benchmarks for hypernuclear spectroscopy that is being explored experimentally at facilities including DA Phi NE, FAIR, Jefferson Lab, J-PARC, and Mainz.
