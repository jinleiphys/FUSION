# Electromagnetic spectral function and dilepton rate in a hot magnetized QCD medium

> The dilepton production rate in hot QCD medium is studied within a effective description of the medium in the presence of magnetic field. This could be done by obtaining the one-loop self energy of photon due to the effective (quasi-) quark loop at finite temperature under an arbitrary external magnetic field while employing the real time formalism of Thermal Field Theory. The effective quarks and gluons encode hot QCD medium effective in terms of their respective effective fugacities. The magnetic field enters in the form of landau level quantization, in the matter sector (quarks, antiquarks). The full Schwinger proper time propagator including all the Landau levels is considered for the quasi quarks while calculating the photon self energy. The electromagnetic Debye screening (in terms of the self-energy) has seen to be influenced both by the hot QCD medium effects and magnetic field. Analogous results are also obtained from the semi classical transport theory. The imaginary part of the photon self energy function is obtained from the discontinuities of the self energy across the Unitary cuts which are also present at zero magnetic field and the Landau cuts which are purely due to the magnetic field. The dilepton production rate is then obtained in terms of the product of electromagnetic spectral functions due to quark loop and lepton loop. The modifications of both the quarks/antiquarks as well as leptons in presence of an arbitrary external magnetic field have been considered in the formalism. Significant enhancement of the low invariant mass dileptons due the appearance of the Landau cuts in the electromagnetic spectral function at finite external magnetic field has been observed. A substantial enhancement of dilepton rate is also found when the EOS effects are considered through the effective quarks/antiquraks.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the dilepton production rate in a hot QCD medium is significantly enhanced at low invariant mass due to the appearance of Landau cuts in the electromagnetic spectral function when an external magnetic field is present, and that this enhancement is further increased when realistic QCD equation-of-state effects are included via effective quark/antiquark fugacities, but the calculation is performed for an arbitrary magnetic field strength without making strong or weak field approximations.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop photon self-energy using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory, with the full Schwinger proper-time propagator (including all Landau levels) for quasi-quarks that encode hot QCD medium effects through temperature-dependent effective fugacities. The imaginary part of the photon self-energy was obtained from discontinuities across Unitary cuts (also present at zero magnetic field) and Landau cuts (purely due to the magnetic field). The dilepton production rate was then expressed as the product of electromagnetic spectral functions from the quark loop and the lepton loop, with both quarks/antiquarks and leptons modified by the external magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* Debye screening mass for massless quarks at zero magnetic field with ideal EOS: \(m_D^2 = N_c \sum_f (e_f^2 T^2 / 3)\)
* Debye screening mass for massless quarks at zero magnetic field with EQPM: \(m_D^2 = -(4 N_c T^2 / \pi^2) \text{Li}_2(-z_q) \sum_f e_f^2\), where \(\text{Li}_2\) is the dilogarithm function and \(z_q\) is the effective quark fugacity
* Debye screening mass in presence of external magnetic field: \(m_D^2 = (N_c B / \pi^2) \sum_f |e_f|^3 \sum_{n=0}^\infty (2 - \delta_n^0) \int_0^\infty (dk_z / T) f(\omega_{k,n}^f) [1 - f(\omega_{k,n}^f)]\), where \(\omega_{k,n}^f = \sqrt{k_z^2 + m_f^2 + 2n |e_f B|}\)
* The Landau-level-dependent quark mass: \(m_{f,l} = \sqrt{m_f^2 + 2n |e_f B|}\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production from magnetized QGP by incorporating realistic QCD equation-of-state effects through the Effective fugacity Quasi Particle Model (EQPM) developed by Chandra (2011), rather than using ideal Fermi-Dirac distributions as in earlier works by Tuchin (2012, 2013), Sadooghi (2016), and Bandyopadhyay (2016, 2017). The calculation uses the full Schwinger propagator without strong or weak field approximations, unlike most prior studies. The formalism enables future investigations of transport coefficients and other electromagnetic probes in magnetized QGP, building on the EQPM extension to magnetized media by Kurian (2017).
