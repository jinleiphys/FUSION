# Formation of exotic baryon clusters in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Recent experiments at RHIC and LHC have demonstrated that there are excellent opportunities to produce light baryonic clusters of exotic matter (strange and anti-matter) in ultra-relativistic ion collisions. Within the hybrid-transport model UrQMD we show that the coalescence mechanism can naturally explain the production of these clusters in the ALICE experiment at LHC. As a consequence of this mechanism we predict the rapidity domains where the yields of such clusters are much larger than the observed one at midrapidity. This new phenomenon can lead to unique methods for producing exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The coalescence mechanism, implemented within the hybrid UrQMD transport model, can naturally explain the production of light baryonic clusters (including strange and anti-matter) observed by ALICE at LHC, and predicts that the maximum yields of such clusters are not at midrapidity but are shifted to forward/backward rapidities around |y| ≈ 4, where yields can be orders of magnitude larger.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD hybrid model, which combines a 3+1d ideal fluid dynamical description for the dense quark-gluon plasma phase (starting at τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c) with a subsequent hadronic cascade, to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV per nucleon. After the dynamical stage, a generalized coalescence model (coalescence of baryons, CB) was applied at 40–50 fm/c to form clusters from baryons with relative velocities below a coalescence parameter v_c, with the resulting primary fragments then undergoing statistical decay.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence parameter v_c = 0.07c for deuterons (²H) and v_c = 0.1c for helium-3 (³He) provided reasonable descriptions of ALICE transverse momentum spectra in central Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV per nucleon.
- The deuteron-to-triton yield ratio is around 800 at midrapidity (y ≈ 0) and decreases to around 60 at |y| ≈ 4, for v_c = 0.1c.
- The maximum yields of composite fragments are located at center-of-mass rapidities around +4 or -4, shifted from midrapidity.
- Variation of the coalescence application time within 40–50 fm/c leads to an uncertainty in yield around 10% for a fixed coalescence parameter.

## Context

This work extends the transport-plus-coalescence approach previously applied at intermediate energies (Botvina 2015) to LHC energies, and provides a dynamical alternative to thermal models (Andronic 2011, 2013; Stachel 2014) which cannot describe energy spectra or flows. The predictions for enhanced cluster production at forward/backward rapidities enable future searches for exotic nuclei, hypernuclei, and anti-nuclei at LHCb and other experiments, building on observations of hyper-tritons by STAR and ALICE.
