# Chiral symmetry breaking, chiral partners, and the $K_1$ and $K^*$ in medium

> We clarify the concept of chiral partners. For a vector meson, the isospin-zero and hypercharge-zero state in the flavor octet mixes with the flavor singlet state. Since the flavor singlet vector meson does not have a chiral partner, the mixed $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons will not have chiral partners. This means that even when chiral symmetry is restored, these mesons will not become degenerate with their corresponding parity partners. On the other hand, the $K_1$ and $K^*$ mesons are chiral partners, and both have widths smaller than 100 MeV. Therefore, we emphasize that studying these mesons in environments where chiral symmetry is restored is particularly important for understanding the effect of chiral symmetry restoration on chiral partners and their masses.

## Key claim

The paper clarifies that the $\omega$ and $\phi$ mesons, being mixed flavor octet-singlet states, do not have chiral partners because the flavor singlet vector meson does not transform under chiral symmetry; therefore, even when chiral symmetry is restored, these mesons will not become degenerate with their parity partners. In contrast, the $K_1$ and $K^*$ mesons are genuine chiral partners, and both have vacuum widths smaller than 100 MeV, making them the only realistic candidates for observing chiral partner degeneracy in medium.

## Method

The author uses operator product expansion (OPE) to analyze the correlation functions of vector and axial-vector currents, showing that the difference between chiral partner correlation functions is proportional to the chiral order parameter (the quark condensate), while the difference between non-chiral partners (such as $\phi$ and $f_1(1420)$) receives additional contributions from disconnected diagrams that are chiral-symmetric and do not vanish even when chiral symmetry is restored. The analysis is performed within the framework of flavor SU(3) symmetry with octet-singlet mixing, and the physical widths and decay channels of the relevant mesons are compared to assess experimental feasibility.

## Key numbers

* $K^*(892)$ vacuum width: 50.3 MeV; $K_1(1270)$ vacuum width: 90 MeV (both below 100 MeV)
* $\rho$ meson vacuum width: 150 MeV; $a_1$ meson vacuum width: 250-600 MeV (too broad for medium measurements)
* $\phi$ meson vacuum width: 4.27 MeV; $\omega$ meson vacuum width: 8.49 MeV (narrow but not chiral partners)
* $K^*(892)$ mass: 892 MeV/$c^2$; $K_1(1270)$ mass: 1270 MeV/$c^2$
* Hadronization temperature in heavy-ion collisions: approximately 156 MeV (from statistical hadronization model)

## Context

This work extends the earlier discussion of vector meson mass shifts in nuclear matter by Hatsuda and Lee (1991) and addresses the experimental difficulties encountered in measuring $\rho$ meson mass shifts, as reviewed by Leupold et al. (2009) and Rapp (1999). The paper argues that the E16 experiment at JPARC and the E-88 experiment are promising for measuring $\phi$ meson mass shifts, but that only the $K_1$-$K^*$ pair can provide a clean signal of chiral symmetry restoration because they are genuine chiral partners with narrow widths. The analysis enables future experimental searches at JPARC using kaon beams and at GSI using pion beams, as well as heavy-ion collision studies at LHC where the $K_1/K^*$ production ratio in peripheral collisions could reveal chiral symmetry restoration effects.
