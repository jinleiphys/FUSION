# Non-occurrence of trapped surfaces and Black Holes in spherical gravitational collapse: An abridged version

> We have shown in that for arbitrary EOS and radiation transport properties, (even) the idealized spherical gravitational collapse DOES NOT lead to the formation of trapped surfaces: 2GM(r,t)/R <=1. Hence all singularity theorems of Hawking, Penrose and Geroch, built on the assumption of formation of trapped surfaces, get invalidated! And this inequality, demands that M->0 if indeed R->0. We have shown that the final state corresponds to a zero mass BH state and, this state would occur only after infinite proper time indicating that GR is indeed the only naturally singularity free theory for isolated bodies (as was cherished by Einstein). This M->0 state would materialize after the body radiates its entire initial mass-energy. Thus there is no event horizon at any finite R or M, and, therefore all the great theoretical confusions like whether there could be (i) White Holes, (ii) whether t (R) becomes spacelike (timelike) inside the EH (iii) Loss of information in gravitational collapse, and (iv) validity of cosmic censorship conjecture get resolved. At any finite proper time, the collapsed object would be either static (z<2) or may appear static (R almost frozen) though, in extreme cases, internally, in terms of proper radial length, it might be collapsing at a speed ~c! We call the latter as Eternally Collapsing Objects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, contrary to the standard interpretation of general relativity, spherical gravitational collapse of an isolated fluid with arbitrary equation of state and radiation transport properties cannot produce trapped surfaces because the Einstein equations impose a global constraint \(2GM(r,t)/R(r,t)c^2 \le 1\). This inequality forces the final gravitational mass to approach zero as the circumference radius approaches zero, meaning black holes with finite mass and event horizons do not form; instead, the end state is a zero-mass object reached only after infinite proper time.

## Method

The author derives the general relativistic collapse equations for a spherically symmetric perfect fluid in comoving coordinates, incorporating radiation transport via the Vaidya metric and the geometrical optics limit of the stress-energy tensor. By combining the definition of the 3-speed \(V\) from Landau and Lifshitz with the fundamental constraint \(\Gamma^2 = 1 + U^2 - 2GM/R\) (where \(\Gamma = dR/dl\) and \(U = dR/d\tau\)), the paper shows that \(\Gamma^2(1-V^2) = 1 - 2GM/R\). Since \(1-V^2 \ge 0\) for timelike or null worldlines and \(\Gamma^2 \ge 0\), the right-hand side must be non-negative, yielding \(2GM/R \le 1\). The analysis is applied to the Oppenheimer-Snyder dust collapse solution, where the author argues that Eq. (36) of that paper requires \(2GM_b/R_b c^2 \le 1\) because the argument of a logarithm cannot be negative.

## Key numbers

- The global constraint is \(2GM(r,t)/R(r,t)c^2 \le 1\), derived from the condition that the 3-speed \(V\) cannot exceed the speed of light.
- For the Oppenheimer-Snyder dust collapse, the author argues that Eq. (36) of that paper demands \(y_b = R_b/R_{gb} \ge 1\), or equivalently \(2GM_b/R_b c^2 \le 1\).
- The closure density for event horizon formation is given as \(\rho_g = 3c^6/(32\pi G^3 M_f^2) \approx 2 \times 10^{16} \text{ g cm}^{-3} (M_f/M_\odot)^{-2}\), where \(M_f\) is the final gravitational mass (not the initial mass).
- The surface redshift of a canonical neutron star is \(z_s \sim 0.1\), while an event horizon would have \(z_s = \infty\).

## Context

This work directly challenges the standard singularity theorems of Hawking, Penrose, and Geroch, which assume the formation of trapped surfaces (\(2GM/R > 1\)) as a prerequisite for singularity formation in gravitational collapse. The paper extends earlier critiques of the Oppenheimer-Snyder (1939) dust collapse solution, claiming that the original authors ignored the logarithmic constraint in their Eq. (36) and worked with an approximate form (Eq. 37) that obscured the fundamental inequality. The author also references Senovilla (1997) for the possibility of singularity-free collapse outcomes. The claimed result would resolve several theoretical puzzles including the nature of white holes, the information loss paradox, and the cosmic censorship conjecture, while suggesting that observed compact objects are actually "Eternally Collapsing Objects" (ECOs) rather than black holes with event horizons.
