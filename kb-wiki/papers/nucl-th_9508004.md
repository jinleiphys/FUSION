# Analysis of the (N,xN') reactions by quantum molecular dynamics plus statistical decay model

> We propose a model based on quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) incorporated with statistical decay model (SDM) to describe various nuclear reactions in an unified way. In this first part of the work, the basic ingredients of the model are defined and the model is applied systematically to the nucleon($N$)-induced reactions. It has been found that our model can give a remarkable agreement in the energy-angle double differential cross sections of $(N,xN')$ type reactions for incident energies from 100 MeV to 3 GeV with a fixed parameter set. An unified description of the major three reaction mechanisms of $(N,xN')$ reactions, i.e. compound, pre-equilibrium and spallation processes, is given with our model.

## Key claim

The authors propose a unified model combining quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) with a statistical decay model (SDM) that can describe compound, pre-equilibrium, and spallation processes in nucleon-induced reactions simultaneously, achieving "remarkable agreement" with experimental energy-angle double differential cross sections for (N,xN') reactions across incident energies from 100 MeV to 3 GeV using a single fixed parameter set.

## Method

The model uses QMD to simulate the dynamical phase of nuclear reactions, where each nucleon is represented by a Gaussian wave packet and propagated via Newtonian equations derived from a Skyrme-type effective interaction with relativistic corrections, including nucleon resonances (Delta and N*(1440)) and pions with isospin degrees of freedom. After the dynamical phase, the statistical decay model handles evaporation of light particles from excited fragments. The two-body collision term includes elastic scattering, resonance production and absorption, and pion absorption channels, with cross sections parametrized using modified Cugnon and VerWest-Arndt forms extended to 3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: 100 MeV to 3 GeV for nucleon-induced reactions
- Compressibility K = 237.3 MeV from Skyrme parameters A = -219.4 MeV, B = 165.3 MeV, tau = 4/3
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.168 fm^-3 with binding energy of 16 MeV per nucleon for infinite nuclear matter
- Symmetry energy parameter C_s = 25 MeV
- Gaussian width L = 2.0 fm^2
- Delta resonance mass M_0 = 1220 MeV with width Gamma_0 = 120 MeV
- N*(1440) resonance mass M_0 = 1430 MeV with width Gamma_0 = 200 MeV
- Branching ratio Gamma_{N* -> Delta+pi} / [Gamma_{N*->Delta+pi} + Gamma_{N*->N+pi}] = 0.4

## Context

This work extends microscopic heavy-ion models such as QMD (Aichelin 1991) and BUU/VUU (Cugnon 1980) to light-ion reactions, addressing a limitation of Peilert et al. (1992) who analyzed (p,xn) reactions with QMD but could not treat the lower part of neutron energy spectra because statistical decay was not included. The model enables systematic analysis of (N,xN') reactions across a wide energy range and is intended as the first step toward unified descriptions of (N,xpi), (pi,xN) reactions, and heavy-ion collisions in subsequent works.
