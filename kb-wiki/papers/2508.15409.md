# Critical Phenomena in Hadronic and DIS Processes

> We compare the critical phenomena (e.g. phase transitions, crossover) in proton-proton, proton-nucleus scattering and in lepton-proton deep inelastic scattering (DIS) systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that critical phenomena (phase transitions) in both hadronic collisions and deep inelastic scattering can be described by a van der Waals equation of state, but the mapping of the saturation region in DIS onto the spinodal region in the van der Waals EoS "requires further cautious studies and accurate numerical tests" and remains the most delicate and complicated point in the thermodynamic description.

## Method

The authors compare the van der Waals equation of state applied to two systems: hadronic scattering (proton-proton, proton-nucleus, nucleus-nucleus) using Fermi statistics for interacting nucleons, and deep inelastic scattering (lepton-proton) using a statistical model of structure functions with Bjorken x as the thermodynamic variable. For DIS, they write the gluon distribution function as a Fermi-Dirac-like distribution in x and derive a modified Stefan-Boltzmann equation of state P ~ T^(4+b). They then apply the van der Waals equation in reduced variables to describe the gas-liquid phase transition, identifying the particle number density with the structure function F2(x,Q^2).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for nuclear matter from the van der Waals equation with Fermi statistics: Tc ≈ 19.7 MeV, close to experimental estimates
- Van der Waals parameters used for the pressure-density calculation in DIS: a = 5 GeV^(-2) and b = 0.2 GeV^(-3)
- Critical temperature expressed in terms of van der Waals parameters: Tc = 8a/(27Rb)
- Critical volume: Vc = 3b; critical pressure: pc = a/(27b^2)

## Context

This work extends the van der Waals approach previously applied to nuclear matter by Jenkovszky and collaborators to the DIS regime, building on statistical models of structure functions by Bhalerao, Mac, and Cleymans. It connects the saturation phenomenon in DIS (color glass condensate) to the spinodal region of a van der Waals phase transition, and compares this with the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter that has been experimentally detected. The paper enables a unified thermodynamic description of critical phenomena across hadronic and DIS systems, though it notes that detailed numerical mapping between the saturation region and the van der Waals spinodal region remains an open problem requiring further study.
