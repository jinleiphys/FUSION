# S-pairing in neutron matter. I. Correlated Basis Function Theory

> S-wave pairing in neutron matter is studied within an extension of correlated basis function (CBF) theory to include the strong, short range spatial correlations due to realistic nuclear forces and the pairing correlations of the Bardeen, Cooper and Schrieffer (BCS) approach. The correlation operator contains central as well as tensor components. The correlated BCS scheme of Ref. [Nucl. Phys. A363 (1981) 383], developed for simple scalar correlations, is generalized to this more realistic case. The energy of the correlated pair condensed phase of neutron matter is evaluated at the two--body order of the cluster expansion, but considering the one--body density and the corresponding energy vertex corrections at the first order of the Power Series expansion. Based on these approximations, we have derived a system of Euler equations for the correlation factors and for the BCS amplitudes, resulting in correlated non linear gap equations, formally close to the standard BCS ones. These equations have been solved for the momentum independent part of several realistic potentials (Reid, Argonne v_{14} and Argonne v_{8'}) to stress the role of the tensor correlations and of the many--body effects. Simple Jastrow correlations and/or the lack of the density corrections enhance the gap with respect to uncorrelated BCS, whereas it is reduced according to the strength of the tensor interaction and following the inclusion of many--body contributions.

## Key claim

S-wave pairing in neutron matter is studied using an extension of correlated basis function (CBF) theory that includes both central and tensor components in the correlation operator, generalizing the earlier Jastrow-correlated BCS scheme of Fantoni (1981). The correlated gap equations are solved for the momentum-independent part of several realistic potentials (Reid, Argonne v14, and Argonne v8') to show that simple Jastrow correlations and/or the lack of density corrections enhance the gap relative to uncorrelated BCS, whereas the gap is reduced according to the strength of the tensor interaction and following the inclusion of many-body contributions.

## Method

The authors construct a correlated BCS wave function by applying a symmetrized product of six-component correlation operators (containing central, spin, and tensor terms) to a BCS model state for pure neutron matter. The energy expectation value is evaluated at the two-body order of the cluster expansion, with one-body density and vertex corrections computed at the first order of the Power Series expansion, yielding a system of Euler equations for the correlation factors and BCS amplitudes that results in correlated nonlinear gap equations formally similar to standard BCS.

## Key numbers

- The empirical nuclear matter saturation density is given as rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, which provides the density scale for the inner crust regime where 1S0 pairing may occur.
- For the Reid v6 potential with Jastrow correlations, a reduction of the BCS gap of about 30% was found in prior work by Chen et al. (1986), attributable to a poor choice of the Jastrow factor.
- The second-order perturbative CBF correction computed by Chen et al. (1986) on top of the Jastrow estimate led to a dramatic reduction of the gap by approximately 80%, which the authors note is inconsistent with X-ray observations by Alpar (1987).
- The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method has been used to simulate up to 114 neutrons in a periodic box to evaluate the equation of state at zero temperature in neutron matter in the normal phase.

## Context

This work extends the correlated BCS scheme of Fantoni (1981), which was developed for simple scalar (Jastrow) correlations, to the more realistic case of spin-isospin dependent correlations with both central and tensor components. The approach contrasts with earlier work by Chen et al. (1986) who studied S-pairing with the Reid v6 potential using a simple Jastrow correlation and found large gap reductions from second-order perturbative corrections; the present work aims to provide a more consistent variational treatment. The resulting BCS amplitudes are intended as a fundamental input for Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo simulations of superfluid neutron matter, enabling more accurate calculations of the gap energy for finite numbers of neutrons.
