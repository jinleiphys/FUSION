# Electromagnetic form factors of $Λ$ hyperon in the vector meson dominance model and a possible explanation of the near-threshold enhancement of the $e^+e^- \to Λ\barΛ$ reaction

> The near-threshold $e^+e^- \to Λ\barΛ$ reaction is studied with the assumption that the production mechanism is due to a near-$Λ\barΛ$-threshold bound state. The cross section of $e^+e^- \to Λ\barΛ$ reaction is parametrized in terms of the electromagnetic form factors of $Λ$ hyperon, which are obtained with the vector meson dominance model. It is shown that the contribution to the $e^+e^- \to Λ\barΛ$ reaction from a new narrow state with quantum numbers $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ is dominant for energies very close to threshold. The mass of this new state is around 2231 MeV, which is very close to the mass threshold of $Λ\barΛ$, while its width is just a few MeV. This gives a possible solution to the problem that all previous calculations seriously underestimate the near-threshold total cross section of the $e^+e^- \to Λ\barΛ$ reaction. We also note that the near-threshold enhancement can be also reproduced by including these well established vector resonances $ω(1420)$, $ω(1650)$, $ϕ(1680)$, or $ϕ(2170)$ with a Flatt${\rm\acute{e}}$ form for their total decay width, and a strong coupling to the $Λ\barΛ$ channel.

# Electromagnetic form factors of Λ hyperon in the vector meson dominance model and a possible explanation of the near-threshold enhancement of the e+e- → ΛΛ̄ reaction

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement of the e+e- → ΛΛ̄ cross section can be explained by a new narrow vector meson X(2231) with JPC = 1-- and mass around 2231 MeV, but the authors note that the same enhancement can also be reproduced by including established vector resonances ω(1420), ω(1650), ϕ(1680), or ϕ(2170) with a Flatté form for their total decay width and a strong coupling to the ΛΛ̄ channel, meaning the existence of a new state is not uniquely required.

## Method

The authors parametrize the Dirac and Pauli form factors of the Λ hyperon using a vector meson dominance (VMD) model that includes contributions from ω, ϕ, and a new narrow vector state X(2231). They perform seven-parameter χ² fits to 18 experimental data points on the effective form factor Geff and the form factor ratio R = |GE/GM| from the BaBar and BESIII collaborations, covering center-of-mass energies from 2.2324 to 3.08 GeV. The total cross section of e+e- → ΛΛ̄ is then computed under the one-photon exchange approximation using the fitted form factors.

## Key numbers

- The Born cross section at √s = 2.2324 GeV measured by BESIII is 305 ± 45^{+66}_{-36} pb
- The fitted mass of the new state X(2231) is mx = 2230.9^{+3.4}_{-3.5} MeV
- The fitted width of X(2231) is Γx = 4.7^{+2.2}_{-4.7} MeV
- The seven-parameter fit yields χ²/dof = 0.9
- The fitted intrinsic form factor parameter γ = 0.43 GeV^{-2} (with alternative fit giving γ = 0.33 ± 0.03 GeV^{-2} and C0 = 0.10 ± 0.03)
- The fitted βx parameter is 1.50 × 10^{-3}
- The fitted βω parameter is -1.13
- The fitted βϕ parameter is 1.35
- The fitted αϕ parameter is -0.40
- The phase angle ΔΦ = 37° ± 12° ± 6° at √s = 2.396 GeV from BESIII cannot be reproduced by the narrow X(2231) state

## Context

This work extends the VMD model analysis of Yang (2019) for Λ hyperon electromagnetic form factors and addresses the discrepancy where previous calculations by Haidenbauer (2016) and Yang (2019) could not reproduce the large BESIII cross section at √s = 2.2324 GeV. The narrow state X(2231) is motivated by predictions from Xiao (2019) using the Godfrey-Isgur model and from Carbonell (1993) for a ΛΛ̄ bound state, though the latter was ruled out by high-statistics p̄p → ΛΛ̄ measurements by Barnes (2000). The work enables future experimental tests at BESIII or the planned Super tau-charm Facility to confirm or rule out this narrow resonance, and notes that the same data can be fit using established resonances ϕ(2170), ω(1420), ω(1650), or ϕ(1680) with Flatté parametrizations.
