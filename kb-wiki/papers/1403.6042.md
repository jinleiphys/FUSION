# Hydrodynamic models of ultrarelativistic collisions

> We discuss the use of the hydrodynamic model for the description of the evolution of dense matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The collective flow observed in heavy-ion collisions at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and at the CERN Large Hadron Collider is consistent with the assumption that a fireball of strongly interacting matter is formed. Experimental results from p-Pb and d-Au collisions show similar phenomena, which suggests that collective expansion appears in small systems as well. We review the recent application of the hydrodynamic model to small systems and discuss limitations and possible further checks of this scenario.

## Key claim

The collective flow observed in p-Pb and d-Au collisions is consistent with hydrodynamic model predictions, suggesting that collective expansion occurs in small systems, but the applicability of second-order viscous hydrodynamics is less justified in these systems due to large velocity gradients and short evolution times.

## Method

The authors perform event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic calculations using Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions, with shear and bulk viscosity included, and the THERMINATOR code to simulate statistical hadron emission at freeze-out and subsequent resonance decays. They compare the resulting two-particle correlation functions, flow coefficients v₂ and v₃, and average transverse momenta to experimental data from the LHC and RHIC for p-Pb, d-Au, and A-A collisions.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s is estimated to be not far from the AdS/CFT estimate of η/s = 0.08
- Two-particle correlation functions in p-Pb collisions are analyzed with a cut of |Δη| > 2 to reduce short-range non-flow correlations
- Initial thermalization time varied between 0.2 and 0.6 fm/c in model comparisons
- For pions, kaons, and protons, the elliptic flow mass splitting appears for p⊥ < 1.5 GeV
- In ¹²C-²⁰⁸Pb collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, the triangularity can be as high as 0.3 for events with large number of participants
- The number of wounded nucleons in ¹²C-Au collisions ranges up to 85 at the highest RHIC energy
- Glauber model calculations use σNN = 42 mb and fraction of binary collisions a = 0.145

## Context

This review extends the hydrodynamic modeling approach from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where collective flow is well-established (Back 2004, Adams 2005, Adcox 2004), to small systems like p-Pb and d-Au collisions. The work builds on earlier hydrodynamic predictions for small systems by Bozek (2011) and addresses alternative interpretations based on color glass condensate effects (Dusling 2012, 2013). The paper enables future experimental programs using deformed projectiles such as deuteron, ³He, ⁹Be, and ¹²C to test collectivity, and identifies the need for theoretical frameworks beyond near-equilibrium hydrodynamics for small systems (Florkowski 2013, Heller 2011).
