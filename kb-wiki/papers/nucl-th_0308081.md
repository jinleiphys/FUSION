# Structure of the Isovector Dipole Resonance in Neutron-Rich $^{60}Ca$ Nucleus and Direct Decay from Pygmy Resonance

> The structure of the isovector dipole resonance in neutron-rich calcium isotope, $^{60}Ca$, has been investigated by implementing a careful treatment of the differences of neutron and proton radii in the continuum random phase approximation ($RPA$). The calculations have taken into account the current estimates of the neutron skin. The estimates of the escape widths for direct neutron decay from the pygmy dipole resonance ($PDR$) were shown rather wide, implicating a strong coupling to the continuum. The width of the giant dipole resonance ($GDR$) was evaluated, bringing on a detailed discussion about its microscopic structure.

## Key claim

The pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich \(^{60}\)Ca is composed of a small number of neutron 1p-1h configurations (at most two per peak) rather than a coherent superposition of many excitations, and its escape width is large (mean value 2.6 MeV), indicating strong coupling to the continuum; the giant dipole resonance (GDR) in the same nucleus shows a very narrow 1p-1h escape width, suggesting that more complex excitations beyond 1p-1h are needed to explain its total width.

## Method

The authors used a continuum random phase approximation (RPA) approach with a discrete particle-hole basis that incorporates single-particle resonance widths via complex energies, treating the continuum through projection operators. The mean field was described by a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters adjusted for \(^{60}\)Ca, and the residual interaction was a Landau-Migdal force with density-dependent parameters. Separate neutron and proton Fermi distributions were used to account for the neutron skin, and harmonic oscillator radial wavefunctions with different size parameters for neutrons and protons were employed.

## Key numbers

- The PDR in \(^{60}\)Ca appears as a broad peak centered at approximately 8.6 MeV, exhausting about 8% of the Energy Weighted Sum Rule (EWSR).
- The three main peaks composing the PDR have complex energies: \(8.05 - i\,1.29\) MeV, \(8.69 - i\,1.35\) MeV, and \(8.94 - i\,0.40\) MeV.
- The mean escape width of the PDR is \(\overline{\Gamma}_n^{\uparrow} = 2.6\) MeV (from the weighted average of the three peaks).
- The neutron separation energy is \(S_n \approx 3.5\) MeV and the proton separation energy is \(S_p \approx 25\) MeV for \(^{60}\)Ca.
- The GDR is centered at 15 MeV; using the relation \(\Gamma_{PDR} = \Gamma_{GDR} (E_{PDR}/E_{GDR})^2\) from Goriely (2002) yields an estimated \(\Gamma_{GDR} = 7.9\) MeV.
- The neutron root-mean-square radius is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_\nu} = 4.03\) fm, the proton root-mean-square radius is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_\pi} = 3.62\) fm, and the total root-mean-square radius is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = 3.90\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations for \(^{60}\)Ca by Catara (1998), Lanza (1999), Hamamoto (2001), and relativistic RPA calculations by Vretenar (2001), but adds a careful treatment of the neutron skin via separate neutron and proton density distributions and explicitly calculates escape widths for direct neutron decay. The results indicate that the PDR has a strong coupling to the continuum, while the GDR requires contributions beyond 1p-1h (such as 2p-2h configurations) to account for its decay width, consistent with the phonon damping model calculations of Dang (2001). The findings are relevant for understanding radiative neutron capture in the r-process nucleosynthesis, as the dipole strength and widths of the PDR and GDR are key inputs for capture cross-section calculations.
