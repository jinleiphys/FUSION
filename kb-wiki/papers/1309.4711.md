# Parity violation in proton-proton scattering from chiral effective field theory

> We present a calculation of the parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry in proton-proton scattering. The calculation is performed in the framework of chiral effective field theory which is applied systematically to both the parity-conserving and parity-violating interactions. The asymmetry is calculated up to next-to-leading order in the parity-odd nucleon-nucleon potential. At this order the asymmetry depends on two parity-violating low-energy constants: the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant $h_π$ and one four-nucleon contact coupling. By comparison with the existing data, we obtain a rather large range for $h_π= (1.1\pm 2)\cdot 10^{-6}$. This range is consistent with theoretical estimations and experimental limits, but more data are needed to pin down a better constrained value. We conclude that an additional measurement of the asymmetry around 125 MeV lab energy would be beneficial to achieve this goal.

## Key claim

The parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry in proton-proton scattering is calculated up to next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, where the asymmetry depends on two low-energy constants (the weak pion-nucleon coupling \(h_\pi\) and one four-nucleon contact coupling \(C\)), and the extracted range for \(h_\pi = (1.1 \pm 2) \times 10^{-6}\) is consistent with theoretical estimates and experimental limits but remains poorly constrained due to the limited data.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space with a combined parity-conserving nucleon-nucleon potential (up to N\(^3\)LO from chiral EFT) and a parity-violating potential at NLO (including two-pion-exchange diagrams and one contact term). The Coulomb interaction was included via a matching procedure at \(R = 12\) fm between short-range and long-range potentials, and the longitudinal analyzing power \(A_z\) was computed for proton-proton scattering at several lab energies. The parity-odd potential was treated perturbatively, and the theoretical uncertainty was quantified by varying the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) between 450 and 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The integrated asymmetry at 13.6 MeV lab energy: \(\bar A_z = (-0.93 \pm 0.21) \times 10^{-7}\) (Bonn experiment, angular range \(20^\circ\)–\(78^\circ\) lab).
- The integrated asymmetry at 45 MeV lab energy: \(\bar A_z = (-1.50 \pm 0.22) \times 10^{-7}\) (PSI experiment, angular range \(23^\circ\)–\(52^\circ\) lab).
- The integrated asymmetry at 221 MeV lab energy: \(\bar A_z = (0.84 \pm 0.34) \times 10^{-7}\) (TRIUMF transmission experiment).
- Extracted weak pion-nucleon coupling: \(h_\pi = (1.1 \pm 2) \times 10^{-6}\).
- Fitted contact coupling \(C\) for the intermediate cutoff combination (550, 600) MeV with DDH best value \(h_\pi = 4.6 \times 10^{-7}\): \(C = (-4.5, -5.1, -5.5) \times 10^{-6}\) for three cutoff combinations.
- DDH range for \(h_\pi\): \(0 \leq h_\pi \leq 1.2 \times 10^{-6}\), with best value \(h_\pi \simeq 4.6 \times 10^{-7}\).
- Lattice QCD result: \(h_\pi = (1.1 \pm 0.5 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-7}\) at \(M_\pi \simeq 389\) MeV.
- Upper limit from \(^{18}\)F: \(h_\pi < 1.3 \times 10^{-7}\).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid calculations of parity violation in proton-proton scattering (Liu 2006, Partanen 2012) by applying a consistent chiral EFT framework for both parity-conserving and parity-violating interactions, in contrast to the earlier DDH meson-exchange model (Desplanques 1979) and pionless EFT (Phillips 2008) which is limited to very low energies. The results enable future extraction of the weak pion-nucleon coupling \(h_\pi\) with improved precision if additional measurements are performed, particularly at around 125 MeV lab energy as suggested by the authors.
