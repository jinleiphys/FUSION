# $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4337)^+$ as interfering $Σ_c\bar{D}$ and $Λ_c\bar{D}^{*}$ threshold cusps

> The recent LHCb data on $B_s^0\to J/ψp\bar{p}$ revealed a new pentaquark-like $P_c(4337)^+$ structure, while finding no evidence for $P_c(4312)^+$ discovered earlier in $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp K^-$. Though puzzling, the data actually offer an important hint to understand the nature of the pentaquark candidates. We develop a model to analyze the $B_s^0\to J/ψp\bar{p}$ data. We find that a $Σ_c\bar{D}$ one-loop mechanism causes a threshold cusp that fits well the $P_c(4337)^+$ peak. Also, the $Σ_c\bar{D}$ and $Λ_c\bar{D}^{*}$ threshold cusps interfere with each other to reproduce an oscillating behavior in the proton helicity angle distribution. These results combined with our earlier analysis on $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp K^-$ indicate that $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4337)^+$ are created by different interference patterns between the $Σ_c\bar{D}$ and $Λ_c\bar{D}^{*}$ (anomalous) threshold cusps. The proposed scenario consistently explains why the $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4337)^+$ peaks appear in $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp K^-$ and $B_s^0\to J/ψp\bar{p}$, respectively, but not vice versa or both.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $P_c(4337)^+$ peak observed in $B_s^0\to J/\psi p\bar{p}$ is not a genuine exotic hadron but a threshold cusp from the $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ one-loop mechanism, and that both $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4337)^+$ arise from different interference patterns between $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ and $\Lambda_c\bar{D}^{*}$ (anomalous) threshold cusps, which explains why each peak appears in only one of the two decay processes.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for $B_s^0\to J/\psi p\bar{p}$ consisting of one-loop amplitudes for $\Lambda_c\bar{D}$, $\Lambda_c\bar{D}^{*}$, and $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ intermediate states, plus a direct decay amplitude. The one-loop amplitudes produce threshold cusps in the $J/\psi p$ invariant mass distribution, and the model was fitted to LHCb data for the $M_{J/\psi p}$, $M_{J/\psi \bar{p}}$, and proton helicity angle distributions using six complex coupling constants as fitting parameters. The $Y_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ single-channel scattering amplitudes were included with scattering lengths of $a \sim 0.5$ fm (attractive) for $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ and $a \sim -0.2$ fm (repulsive) for $\Lambda_c\bar{D}^{*}$ and $\Lambda_c\bar{D}$.

## Key numbers

* The $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ threshold cusp fits the $P_c(4337)^+$ peak structure appearing near $M_{J/\psi p} \approx 4.337$ GeV in $B_s^0\to J/\psi p\bar{p}$.
* The $P_c(4337)^+$ signal has a statistical significance of $3.1-3.7\sigma$ in the LHCb data.
* The scattering length for $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ is $a \sim 0.5$ fm (attractive), while for $\Lambda_c\bar{D}^{*}$ it is $a \sim -0.2$ fm (repulsive).
* The common cutoff value in the dipole form factors is $\Lambda = 1$ GeV, varied over $\Lambda = 0.8-2$ GeV without significant change in fit quality.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Nakamura 2020) that explained $P_c(4312)^+$ in $\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p K^-$ as an interference between a double triangle singularity and a smooth amplitude. It contradicts interpretations of $P_c(4337)^+$ as a resonance or bound state from $\Lambda_c^{+}\bar{D}^{*0}-\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar{D}$ coupled-channel dynamics (Beihang group 2020) or as a $\chi_{c0}p$ hadrocharmonium state. The proposed threshold-cusp scenario enables a consistent understanding of why $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4337)^+$ appear selectively in different decay processes, and calls for reconsideration of other data interpreted as exotic hadron signals.
