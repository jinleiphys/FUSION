# A 2-level system of cold sodium atoms with high and tunable susceptibility against the magnetic field

> A 2-level spin-system of cold sodium atoms is proposed. Both the cases that the system has arrived at thermo-equilibrium and is in the early stage of evolution have been studied. This system is inert to the magnetic field $B$ in general but very sensitive in a narrow domain around $B=B_0$, where $B_0$ can be predicted and is tunable. A characteristic constant $γ=0.278466$ dedicated to various 2-level systems is found, and leads to a upper limit for the internal energy $U$ of the whole system so that $U\leqγk_B T$. This limit is considerably lower than the energy assigned to the spatial motion of only a single particle. Under thermo-equilibrium, the populations of spin-components measured at distinct $T$ converge to a fixed value when $B=B_0$. The period and amplitude of population oscillation are found to be seriously affected by the special sensitivity against $B$. Rich messages on the dynamic parameters could be obtained via experimental measurements of these systems.

# A 2-level system of cold sodium atoms with high and tunable susceptibility against the magnetic field

## Key claim

A two-level spin system of cold sodium atoms, with total magnetic quantum number fixed at M = N-2, is inert to magnetic field B in general but becomes extremely sensitive in a narrow domain around a specific field B₀, where B₀ can be predicted and tuned by changing atom number N or trap frequency ω; the claimed high susceptibility is conditional on operating within this narrow domain, and the system's behavior differs fundamentally from that of rubidium-based two-level systems.

## Method

The authors consider N spin-1 sodium atoms trapped in an isotropic harmonic potential with all spatial degrees of freedom frozen, using a Hamiltonian that includes contact interactions (with spin-dependent coupling c₂) and a quadratic Zeeman term. They diagonalize the resulting 2×2 matrix in the basis of two total-spin states (S = N-2 and S = N, both with M = N-2) using fractional parentage coefficients, obtaining analytical expressions for eigenenergies, eigenstates, and the energy gap E_gap. They then compute the fidelity susceptibility Γ(B), the internal energy U under thermal equilibrium, and the time-dependent population oscillation Q₀(B,t) for an initially prepared Fock state.

## Key numbers

- Characteristic constant γ = 0.278466, which sets the upper limit for internal energy U ≤ γ k_B T for any two-level system regardless of N, ω, or interaction parameters.
- For N = 4, the difference B_peak − B₀ = 0.0096 Gauss; for N = 300, B_peak − B₀ = 0.0003 Gauss; for N = 3000, B_peak − B₀ = 0 Gauss.
- At B = B₀, the weighted probability of a particle in μ = 0 is exactly 1/N, independent of temperature T.
- The maximum number of μ = 0 particles in the population oscillation is N₀,MAX = 2, achieved only when B = B₀.
- The overlap ⟨N−1,0,1|ϑ_{N−2,N−2}^N⟩ = 0.9915 and ⟨N−1,0,1|ϑ_{N,N−2}^N⟩ = 0.1302, explaining the gap behavior for sodium versus rubidium.

## Context

This work extends studies of cold-atom two-level systems as candidates for quantum bits, building on prior theoretical frameworks for spinor condensates (Katsura and Bao, 2004; Bao, 2005) and experimental work on spin dynamics in cold gases (Chang et al., 2005; Black et al., 2007; Kronjäger et al., 2005, 2006; Higbie et al., 2005; Widera et al., 2005, 2006; Pechkis et al., 2013). The paper identifies a characteristic constant γ = 0.278466 that universally bounds the internal energy of any two-level system, and shows that sodium (unlike rubidium) exhibits a minimum in the energy gap at nonzero B₀ due to the relative populations of μ ≠ 0 particles in the two basis states. The results enable experimental extraction of dynamic parameters by measuring the convergence point of population probabilities at different temperatures, and suggest further study of how spatial-mode interference affects the spin dynamics when temperature exceeds the threshold T_b.
