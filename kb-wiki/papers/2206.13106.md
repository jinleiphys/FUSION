# Three-body continuum states and Efimov physics in non-integer geometry

> Continuum structures of three short-range interacting particles in a deformed external one-body field are investigated. We use the equivalent $d$-method employing non-integer dimension, $d$, in a spherical calculation with a dimension-dependent angular momentum barrier. We focus on dimensions close to the critical dimension, $d=d_E$, between two and three, defined by zero two-body energies, where the Efimov effect can occur. We design for this dimension region a schematic, long-distance realistic, square-well based, three-body spherical model, which is used to derive analytic expressions for the wave functions, scattering lengths, phase shifts, and elastic scattering cross sections. The procedure and the results are universal, valid for all short-range potentials, and for large scattering lengths. We discuss the properties and validity of the derived expressions by means of the simplest system of three identical bosons. The derived expressions are particularly useful for very small energies, where full numerical calculations are often not feasible. For energies where the numerical calculations can be performed, a good agreement with the analytic results is found. These model results may be tested by scattering experiments for three particles in an equivalent external deformed oscillator potential. The cross sections all vanish in the zero-energy limit for $d<3$ with definite $d$-dependent power of energy.

# Three-body continuum states and Efimov physics in non-integer geometry

## Key claim

The authors extend the $d$-method (non-integer dimension formalism) from bound states to three-body continuum states, deriving analytic expressions for wave functions, phase shifts, and cross sections that are valid for dimensions close to the critical dimension $d_E$ where the Efimov effect occurs. The results are universal for all short-range potentials with large scattering lengths, but the analytic expressions are specifically designed for the regime where full numerical calculations become infeasible due to extremely small two-body binding energies.

## Method

The authors employ the $d$-method, which replaces a deformed external one-body field in three dimensions with a spherical calculation in non-integer dimension $d$ using a dimension-dependent angular momentum barrier. They construct a semi-realistic square-well based three-body spherical model in hyperspherical coordinates, dividing the hyperradius $\rho$ into three intervals: a short-range region ($0 \leq \rho \leq \rho_0$), an intermediate region ($\rho_0 \leq \rho \leq |a_{\text{av}}|$) where the effective potential is determined by the universal asymptotic $\lambda$-function, and an outer region ($\rho \geq |a_{\text{av}}|$) where either a $3 \rightarrow 3$ or $1+2 \rightarrow 1+2$ scattering channel applies. Analytic solutions are obtained by matching Bessel function solutions at the interval boundaries, yielding expressions for the phase shifts $\delta_m$ and $\delta_o$ and the three-body scattering length $a_{\text{3b}}$.

## Key numbers

- The critical dimension $d_E$ lies between two and three, defined by zero two-body energies where the Efimov effect can occur.
- For three identical particles, the short-range interval boundary $\rho_0$ is estimated as either $\rho_0 = r_0/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.7 r_0$ or $\rho_0 = \sqrt{2/3}r_0 \approx 0.8 r_0$, where $r_0$ is the two-body square-well radius.
- The phase shift $\delta_o$ approaches zero in the zero-energy limit as a power of energy: the power is $2\ell_C+1 = 2d-2$ when no dimer exists, and $2\ell_C+1 = d-2$ when a bound two-body state is present.
- In the low-energy limit for $1+2 \rightarrow 1+2$ reactions with three identical particles, $\kappa |a_{\text{av}}| \approx i\sqrt{2}$ when $|E_2| \approx \hbar^2/(m a_{\text{av}}^2)$.
- The three-body scattering length $a_{\text{3b}}$ is defined by $\lim_{\kappa_C\rightarrow 0} \kappa_C^{2\ell_C+1} \cot\delta_o = -1/a_{\text{3b}}^{2\ell_C+1}$, with $a_{\text{3b}}/|a_{\text{av}}| = A_{\text{3b}}^{1/(2\ell_C+1)}$.

## Context

This work extends the $d$-method previously developed for two-body continuum states (Garrido 2022) and three-body bound states (Garrido 2019a, 2019b, 2020, 2021) to three-body continuum states, enabling analysis of scattering experiments in deformed external fields. The method builds on the demonstration that the Efimov effect can be induced by an external squeezing potential in three dimensions (Garrido 2021), corresponding to the critical dimension $d_E$ in the $d$-formalism. The analytic expressions are particularly useful for very small collision energies where full numerical calculations are not feasible, and they enable future comparison with scattering experiments for three particles in an equivalent external deformed oscillator potential.
