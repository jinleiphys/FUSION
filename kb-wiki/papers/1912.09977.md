# Helical massive fermions under rotation

> The properties of a massive fermion field undergoing rigid rotation at finite temperature and chemical potential are discussed. The polarisation imbalance is taken into account by considering a helicity chemical potential, which is dual to the helicity charge operator. The advantage of the proposed approach is that, as opposed to the axial current, the helicity charge current remains conserved at finite mass. A computation of thermal expectation values of the vector, helicity and axial charge currents, as well as of the fermion condensate and stress-energy tensor is provided. In all cases, analytic constitutive equations are derived for the non-equilibrium transport terms, as well as for the quantum corrections to the equilibrium terms (which are derived using an effective relativistic kinetic theory model for fermions with helicity imbalance) in the limit of small masses. In the context of the parameters which are relevant to relativistic heavy ion collisions, the expressions derived in the massless limit are shown to remain valid for masses up to the thermal energy, except for the axial charge conductivity in the azimuthal direction, which presents strong variations with the particle mass.

## Key claim

For massive fermions under rigid rotation, the helicity charge current remains conserved (unlike the axial current), and the massless-limit expressions for most transport coefficients remain valid for masses up to the thermal energy in heavy-ion collision conditions, except for the axial charge conductivity in the azimuthal direction which shows strong mass dependence.

## Method

The author computes exact thermal expectation values of the vector, helicity, and axial charge currents, the fermion condensate, and the stress-energy tensor for a free Dirac field in rigid rotation at finite temperature and chemical potentials, using a mode-sum approach with simultaneous eigenfunctions of the Hamiltonian, linear momentum, angular momentum, and helicity operators. Analytic constitutive equations are derived for non-equilibrium transport terms and quantum corrections to equilibrium terms in the small-mass limit, using an effective relativistic kinetic theory model for fermions with helicity imbalance as the classical reference.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 150 MeV, vector chemical potential μ_V = 30 MeV, and angular velocity Ω = 6.6 MeV (corresponding to 10^22 s^-1) are used as the heavy-ion collision (HIC) parameter regime
- The speed of light surface (SLS) is located at ρ_SLS = Ω^-1, where the Lorentz factor Γ = (1 - ρ^2 Ω^2)^(-1/2) diverges
- The helicity operator eigenvalues are λ = ±1/2, distinguishing right-handed from left-handed helicity particles
- The kinematic vorticity vector component along the rotation axis is ω^ẑ = Γ^2 Ω

## Context

This work extends the massless-fermion analysis of Ambrus (2019) (the "helican" paper) to the finite-mass regime, where the axial chemical potential cannot be consistently introduced as a thermodynamic variable because the axial current is not conserved for massive fermions (∂_μ J_A^μ = 2iM ψ̄ γ^5 ψ). The approach using helicity chemical potential offers an alternative to the axial chemical potential approach of Buzzegoli (2018) and the spin potential approach of Becattini (2019), both of which have drawbacks for massive fermions. The results enable quantitative modeling of polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, where the quark-gluon plasma undergoes rigid rotation, and can be compared with measurements of Λ hyperon polarization from STAR (2017, 2018).
