# Semi-inclusive photon-hadron production in pp and pA collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We investigate semi-inclusive photon-hadron production in the color glass condensate (CGC) framework at RHIC and the LHC energies in proton-proton (pp) and proton-nucleus (pA) collisions. We calculate the coincidence probability for azimuthal correlation of pairs of photon-hadron and show that the away-side correlations have a double-peak or a single-peak structure depending on trigger particle selection and kinematics. This novel feature is unique for semi-inclusive photon-hadron production compared to a similar measurement for double inclusive dihadron production in pA collisions. We obtain necessary conditions between kinematics variables for the appearance of a double-peak or a single peak structure for the away-side photon-hadron correlations in pp and pA collisions at forward rapidities and show that this feature is mainly controlled by the ratio p_T^hadron/p_T^photon. Decorrelation of away-side photon-hadron production by increasing the energy, rapidity and density, and appearance of double-peak structure can be understood by QCD saturation physics. We also provide predictions for the ratio of single inclusive prompt photon to hadron production, and two-dimensional nuclear modification factor for the semi-inclusive photon-hadron pair production at RHIC and the LHC at forward rapidities.

## Key claim

The away-side azimuthal correlation for semi-inclusive photon-hadron production in pp and pA collisions can exhibit either a double-peak or a single-peak structure, depending on whether the trigger particle is a prompt photon or a hadron, and this feature is primarily controlled by the ratio \(z_T = p_T^{\text{hadron}}/p_T^{\text{photon}}\); the double-peak appears only when \(z_T \le 1\) and a kinematic condition involving rapidities and energy is satisfied.

## Method

The author calculated the coincidence probability for photon-hadron azimuthal correlations using the color glass condensate (CGC) framework, with the dipole-target forward scattering amplitude obtained from the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation. The cross sections for semi-inclusive photon-hadron, single inclusive prompt photon, and single inclusive hadron production were computed at leading twist, using NLO MSTW 2008 parton distribution functions and NLO KKP fragmentation functions for neutral pions, with the factorization scale set to the transverse momentum of the produced particle. The analysis was performed for minimum-bias pp and pA collisions at RHIC (\(\sqrt{S}=0.2\) TeV) and LHC (\(\sqrt{S}=8.8\) TeV) energies at forward rapidities.

## Key numbers

- The initial saturation scale for the proton was taken as \(Q_{0p}^2 \simeq 0.168\ \text{GeV}^2\) with \(\gamma \simeq 1.119\), extracted from a global fit to proton structure functions in DIS and single inclusive hadron data.
- For a nuclear target, the initial saturation scale was varied within \(Q_{0A}^2 = 3 \div 4\ Q_{0p}^2\), corresponding to an impact-parameter averaged value.
- The number of binary collisions \(N_{coll}\) was taken as 3.6 at \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV and 7.4 at \(\sqrt{s}=8.8\) TeV for pA collisions.
- The ratio \(\gamma^{inclusive}/\pi^0\) at the LHC (\(\sqrt{S}=8.8\) TeV) was found to be smaller than one for a large range of rapidities, increasing with rapidity and transverse momentum while decreasing with energy.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based study of prompt photon production in pA collisions by Rezaeian (2012) to semi-inclusive photon-hadron correlations, showing that the asymmetric nature of photon-hadron production leads to novel correlation structures not seen in dihadron production. The double-peak away-side correlation is unique to photon-hadron production compared to dihadron measurements at RHIC, where only a single peak was observed. The results enable experimental verification at RHIC and the LHC, and provide predictions for the two-dimensional nuclear modification factor for photon-hadron pairs, which can be used to probe QCD saturation physics and cold nuclear matter effects.
