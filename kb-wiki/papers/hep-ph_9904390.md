# Dynamical Viscosity of Nucleating Bubbles

> We study the viscosity corrections to the growth rate of nucleating bubbles in a first order phase transition in scalar field theory. We obtain the non-equilibrium equation of motion of the coordinate that describes small departures from the critical bubble and extract the growth rate consistently in weak coupling and in the thin wall limit. Viscosity effects arise from the interaction of this coordinate with the stable quantum and thermal fluctuations around a critical bubble. In the case of 1+1 dimensions we provide an estimate for the growth rate that depends on the details of the free energy functional. In 3+1 dimensions we recognize robust features that are a direct consequence of the thin wall approximation and give the leading viscosity corrections.These are long-wavelength hydrodynamic fluctuations that describe surface waves, quasi-Goldstone modes which are related to ripples on interfaces in phase ordered Ising-like systems. We discuss the applicability of our results to describe the growth rate of hadron bubbles in a quark-hadron first order transition.

## Key claim

The growth rate of slightly supercritical nucleating bubbles receives viscosity corrections from the interaction of the unstable radial coordinate with stable quantum and thermal fluctuations around the critical bubble, with the dominant contribution in 3+1 dimensions coming from long-wavelength hydrodynamic surface waves (quasi-Goldstone modes) rather than from coupling to independent fields as studied in prior work.

## Method

The authors expand the scalar field around the critical bubble configuration in a complete basis of eigenfunctions of the fluctuation operator, separating the unstable mode (proportional to dφ/dR) from stable modes. They treat the unstable coordinate as a collective coordinate and integrate out the stable fluctuations in a weak-coupling expansion and thin-wall approximation (R_c >> ξ), obtaining an effective non-equilibrium equation of motion and a non-Markovian Langevin equation for the radial coordinate. The calculation is performed to lowest order in the quartic coupling λ in both 1+1 and 3+1 dimensions, with finite temperature effects included via the finite-temperature effective potential.

## Key numbers

- In 3+1 dimensions for φ⁴ theory at temperature T < T_c, the growth rate to lowest order in λ is Ω = (√2 / R_c) [1 - 0.003 λ T ξ (R_c/ξ)²], where R_c is the critical radius and ξ is the bubble wall width.
- The critical radius in 3+1 dimensions is R_c = 2σ / ΔF, where σ is the surface tension and ΔF is the free energy difference between phases.
- The second derivative of the bubble energy at the critical radius gives -ω² = -8πσ, independent of R_c and ΔF.
- The unstable eigenvalue Ω² is related to the energy curvature by ω² = Ω² / N_{-1}, where N_{-1} is the normalization factor of the unstable mode.

## Context

This work extends Langer's (1969) theory of nucleation by providing a microscopic derivation of viscosity corrections to the bubble growth rate, in contrast to the phenomenological hydrodynamic approach of Csernai and Kapusta (1986) and the coupling-to-other-fields approach of Khlebnikov (1992) and Arnold (1993). The results are applicable to first-order phase transitions in QCD hadronization and the electroweak theory, and the identification of surface-wave hydrodynamic modes as the dominant source of viscosity corrections is a robust feature independent of the specific scalar field model.
