# Pressure Corrections to the Equation of State in the Nuclear Mean Field

> We show the connection between stiffness of equation of state in a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) of Nuclear Matter (NM) and the existence of a strong violation of longitudinal Momentum Sum Rule (MSR) in RMF for a finite pressure. The increasing pressure between nucleons starts to increase the ratio of nucleon Fermi to average single particle energy and according to the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem valid for NM the MSR is broken. In order to satisfy that MSR we propose changes which modify the nucleon Parton Distribution Function (PDF) above a saturation density. The course of Equation o State in our modified RMF model is very close to semi-empirical estimation and to results obtained from extensive DBHF calculations with a Bonn A potential. Other features of the model includes a good values saturation properties including spin-orbit term. Specially the proper stiffness of EoS recently discussed in an application to compact and neutron stars is important when studying star properties (mass-radius constraint), especially the mass of "PSR J1614-2230" the most massive known neutron star, which rules out many soft equations of state including exotic matter. An admixture of additional hyperons are discussed in our approach.

# Pressure Corrections to the Equation of State in the Nuclear Mean Field

## Key claim

The paper argues that the violation of the longitudinal Momentum Sum Rule (MSR) in Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models at finite pressure, which arises from the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, can be corrected by modifying the nucleon Parton Distribution Function (PDF) above saturation density, thereby softening the equation of state. The claim is conditional: the proposed modifications apply only for positive pressure above saturation density, and the resulting equation of state is close to semi-empirical estimates and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations with a Bonn A potential.

## Method

The author modifies the nucleon PDF in the nuclear medium by introducing a scaling relation \(F_2^B(x) = a F_2^N(a (E_F^A / \varepsilon_A) x)\) where the parameter \(a\) preserves the number of valence quarks, and the condition \(\int dx F_2^B(x) = \varepsilon_A / E_F^A\) ensures the MSR is satisfied. This modification is implemented in the linear Walecka model by replacing the constant nucleon mass \(M_N\) with a density-dependent medium mass \(M_{med} = M_N \varepsilon_A / E_F^A\), converting the standard RMF equations into a differential-recursive set of equations above saturation density. The resulting equation of state is solved numerically for two parameterizations of the Walecka model and compared with DBHF calculations and experimental flow constraints from heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility \(K^{-1} = 290\) MeV obtained in the modified model for the \(S_2\) parameterization, compared to the unrealistic \(K^{-1} = 560\) MeV in the standard linear Walecka model.
- At density \(\varrho = 0.3\) fm\(^{-3}\), the pressure correction \(E_{press} = p_H / \varrho\) is approximately 150 MeV in the stiff Walecka model and only 25 MeV in the soft Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) model.
- The saturation density for the \(S_1\) parameterization is \(\varrho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) with coupling constants \(C_v^2 = 273.8\) and \(C_s^2 = 357.4\); for the \(S_2\) parameterization \(\varrho_0 = 0.19\) fm\(^{-3}\) with \(C_v^2 = 195.9\) and \(C_s^2 = 267.1\).
- The limiting Bjorken \(x\) value for single nucleon visibility in DIS is \(x_L \sim 0.25\), derived from \(x_L = 2/(M_N d) \simeq 2\varrho^{1/3}/M_N\).
- The nucleon mass in medium at saturation is approximately \(M_{med} \simeq M_N\) for zero pressure, decreasing with density according to \(M_{med} = M_N / (1 + p_H / \varrho \varepsilon_A)\).

## Context

This work extends the standard Relativistic Mean Field approach of Walecka (1974) and Serot and Walecka (1986) by addressing the violation of the longitudinal Momentum Sum Rule that occurs at finite pressure, a problem previously noted in the context of the EMC effect and nuclear PDFs. The modified equation of state is compared with DBHF calculations using the Bonn A potential from Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and with experimental flow constraints from heavy ion collisions analyzed by Danielewicz, Lacey, and Lynch (2002). The results are relevant for neutron star structure, particularly the mass-radius constraint for PSR J1614-2230, and the approach can be extended to include hyperons via a generalized Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem for multiple baryon species.
