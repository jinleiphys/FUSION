# Quadrupole Moments of N and Delta in the 1/N_c Expansion

> We calculate expressions for the quadrupole moments of nonstrange baryons in which the number of QCD color charges is N_c. Using only the assumption of single-photon exchange, we obtain 4 relations among the 6 moments, and show how all of them may be obtained from Q_{Δ^+ p} up to O(1/N_c^2) corrections. We compare to the N_c=3 case, and obtain relations between the neutron charge radius and quadrupole moments. We also discuss prospects for the measurement of these moments.

## Key claim

Using only the assumption of single-photon exchange (the photon couples to only one quark), the six quadrupole moments of nonstrange baryons can be described by just two independent operators, leading to four relations among them; all moments can be expressed in terms of the transition quadrupole moment \(Q_{\Delta^+ p}\) up to \(O(1/N_c^2)\) corrections.

## Method

The authors construct a basis of spin-flavor operators for quadrupole moments in the \(1/N_c\) expansion, restricting to operators that involve the quark charge only once (single-photon exchange approximation). They evaluate matrix elements of the two resulting operators (a 2-body operator suppressed by \(1/N_c\) and a 3-body operator suppressed by \(1/N_c^2\)) on the ground-state nonstrange baryon multiplet using angular momentum projection techniques for the two-flavor case. The unknown coefficients \(B\) and \(C\) (of order unity times a characteristic hadronic quadrupole size) are then related to the six observables: the diagonal quadrupole moments \(Q_{\Delta^{++}}\), \(Q_{\Delta^+}\), \(Q_{\Delta^0}\), \(Q_{\Delta^-}\) and the off-diagonal transition moments \(Q_{\Delta^+ p}\) and \(Q_{\Delta^0 n}\).

## Key numbers

- The transition quadrupole moment extracted from pion photoproduction data is \(Q_{N\to \Delta} = -0.108 \pm 0.009 \pm 0.034\) fm\(^2\) (from the measured \(E2/M1\) ratio of \([-3.07 \pm 0.26 \, ({\rm stat + syst}) \pm 0.24 \, ({\rm model})]\%\)).
- Using the neutron charge radius \(r_n^2 = -0.113(3)(4)\) fm\(^2\) and the relation \(Q_{\Delta^+ p} = r_n^2 / \sqrt{2}\) (valid to within 1.2% for all \(N_c\)), the predicted value is \(Q_{\Delta^+ p} = -0.0799(4)\) fm\(^2\).
- For \(N_c=3\), the diagonal matrix elements are \(4Q(3B+C)/9\); in the \(N_c\to\infty\) limit they become \(4I_3B/5 = 4(Q-1/2)B/5\).
- The \(I=3\) combination vanishes identically: \(Q_{\Delta^{++}} - 3 Q_{\Delta^+} + 3 Q_{\Delta^0} - Q_{\Delta^-} = 0\).
- The \(I=2\) combination vanishes identically: \(Q_{\Delta^{++}} - Q_{\Delta^+} - Q_{\Delta^0} + Q_{\Delta^-} = 0\).
- The off-diagonal equality \(Q_{\Delta^+ p} = Q_{\Delta^0 n}\) holds for all \(N_c\).
- The \(N_c\)-dependent relation \(\frac{Q_{\Delta^0}}{Q_{\Delta^{+}p}} = -\frac{2}{5} \frac{N_c-3}{N_c} \sqrt{\frac{2(N_c+5)}{N_c-1}}\) vanishes for \(N_c=3\).

## Context

This work extends the \(1/N_c\) expansion method previously applied to baryon masses (Jenkins and Lebed, 1995), magnetic moments (Luty et al., 1992; Dashen et al., 1994), and charge radii (Buchmann and Lebed, 2001) to the case of quadrupole moments. It generalizes the \(N_c=3\) calculation of Buchmann and Hester (2001) to arbitrary \(N_c\) and provides a rigorous operator basis for the two-flavor case. The results enable predictions of all nonstrange baryon quadrupole moments from the measurable transition \(Q_{\Delta^+ p}\) or from the neutron charge radius, and set the stage for a subsequent treatment of the three-flavor case (Buchmann, Hester, and Lebed, 2003).
