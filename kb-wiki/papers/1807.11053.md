# Generator-coordinate reference states for spectra and $0νββ$ decay in the in-medium similarity renormalization group

> We use a reference state based on symmetry-restored states from deformed mean-field or generator-coordinate-method (GCM) calculations in conjunction with the in-medium similarity-renormalization group (IMSRG) to compute spectra and matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay. Because the decay involves ground states from two nuclei, we use evolved operators from the IMSRG in one nucleus in a subsequent GCM calculation in the other. We benchmark the resulting IMSRG+GCM method against complete shell-model diagonalization for both the energies of low-lying states in $^{48}$Ca and $^{48}$Ti and the $0νββ$ matrix element for the decay of $^{48}$Ca, all in a single valence shell. Our approach produces better spectra than either the IMSRG with a spherical-mean-field reference or GCM calculations with unevolved operators. For the $0νββ$ matrix element the improvement is slight, but we expect more significant effects in full ab-initio calculations.

## Key claim

The IMSRG+GCM method produces better spectra than either the IMSRG with a spherical-mean-field reference or GCM calculations with unevolved operators, but for the $0\nu\beta\beta$ matrix element the improvement is slight; the authors expect more significant effects in full ab-initio calculations.

## Method

The authors combine generator-coordinate-method (GCM) reference states, built from angular-momentum- and particle-number-projected deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states, with the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) to compute spectra and $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay matrix elements. They first perform a GCM calculation to generate a correlated reference state, then run an IMSRG calculation based on that state to evolve the Hamiltonian and transition operators, and finally carry out a second GCM calculation using those evolved operators. For the $0\nu\beta\beta$ matrix element, they use evolved operators from the IMSRG in one nucleus in a subsequent GCM calculation in the other, employing two procedures (PI and PF) that differ in which nucleus's unitary transformation is applied first.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{48}$Ca: IMSRG+GCM gives -7.56 MeV, compared to exact shell-model -7.57 MeV, GCM alone -7.12 MeV, and spherical HFB -6.45 MeV
- Ground-state energy of $^{48}$Ti: IMSRG+GCM gives -23.81 MeV, compared to exact shell-model -23.66 MeV, GCM alone -22.18 MeV, and spherical HFB -18.76 MeV
- The IMSRG+GCM overestimates the energy of $^{48}$Ti by about 1% relative to exact diagonalization
- GCM ground-state energies before IMSRG evolution: -7.12 MeV for $^{48}$Ca and -22.18 MeV for $^{48}$Ti
- Deformation parameter range used: $\beta_2 \in \{-0.3, -0.2, \dots 0.2, 0.3\}$ for $^{48}$Ca and $\beta_2 \in \{-0.3, -0.2, \dots 0.4, 0.5\}$ for $^{48}$Ti

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG framework to use symmetry-restored GCM reference states, generalizing prior efforts that used number-projected spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov reference states (Hergert 2013, 2014) or no-core shell-model reference states (Gebrerufael 2017). The method enables application of the IMSRG to mid-shell nuclei with strong collective correlations including deformation and pairing, which are difficult to capture with spherical reference states. The approach is benchmarked against complete shell-model diagonalization within a single valence shell using the KB3G interaction (Poves 2001), and the authors report they are applying the method in ab-initio calculations of this and other decays.
