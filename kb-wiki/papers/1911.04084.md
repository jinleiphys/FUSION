# Theoretical study of $ \rm{Nb} $ isotope productions by muon capture reaction on $ {}^{100} \rm{Mo} $

> The isotope $ {}^{99} \rm{Mo} $, the generator of $ {}^{99m} \rm{Tc} $ used for diagnostic imaging, is supplied by extracting from fission fragments of highly enriched uranium in reactors. However, a reactor-free production method of $ {}^{99} \rm{Mo} $ is searched over the world from the point of view of nuclear proliferation. Recently, $ {}^{99} \rm{Mo} $ production through a muon capture reaction was proposed and it was found that about $ 50 \, \% $ of $ {}^{100} \rm{Mo} $ turned into $ {}^{99} \rm{Mo} $ through $ {}^{100} \rm{Mo} \left( μ^-, n \right) $ reaction [arXiv:1908.08166]. However, the detailed physical process of the muon capture reaction is not completely understood. We, therefore, study the muon capture reaction of $ ^{100} \rm{Mo} $ by a theoretical approach. We used the proton-neutron QRPA to calculate the muon capture rate. The muon wave function is calculated with considering the electronic distribution of the atom and the nuclear charge distribution. The particle evaporation process from the daughter nucleus is calculated by a statistical model. From the model calculation, about $ 38 \, \% $ of $ {}^{100} \rm{Mo} $ is converted to $ {}^{99} \rm{Mo} $ through the muon capture reaction, which is in a reasonable agreement with the experimental data. It is revealed that negative parity states, especially $ 1^- $ state, play an important role in $ {}^{100} \rm{Mo} \left( μ^-, n \right) {}^{99} \rm{Nb} $. The feasibility of $ {}^{99} \rm{Mo} $ production by the muon capture reaction is also discussed. Isotope production by the muon capture reaction strongly depends on the nuclear structure.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that about 38% of 100Mo is converted to 99Mo via the muon capture reaction, which is in reasonable agreement with the experimental value of about 50% reported in Hashim (2018). The discrepancy is attributed to the model's overestimation of feeding to excited states below the one-neutron separation energy, particularly for negative parity states.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) based on Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS (SHFBCS) with the SLy4 effective interaction to calculate muon capture rates on 100Mo. The muon wave function was obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation under a potential that includes both the nuclear Coulomb interaction (using the spherical-averaged charge distribution from SHFBCS) and the electron distribution from density functional theory. The particle evaporation from the daughter nucleus 100Nb was calculated using the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model implemented in the CCONE code, with transmission coefficients from Koning-Delaroche optical potentials and nuclear level densities from the Gilbert-Cameron method with Mengoni-Nakajima parameters.

## Key numbers

- Calculated muon capture rate for natural Mo: 11.3 × 10^6 s^-1, compared to experimental value of (9.614 ± 0.15) × 10^6 s^-1 from Suzuki (1987), a deviation of about 12%
- Calculated production rate of 99Nb from 100Mo(μ^-, n): 38.1%, compared to experimental value of 51% from Hashim (2018)
- Calculated production rates for other channels: 28.9% for (μ^-, 0n) to 100Nb, 23.8% for (μ^-, 2n) to 98Nb, 8.73% for (μ^-, 3n) to 97Nb, 0.28% for (μ^-, 4n) to 96Nb, 0.01% for (μ^-, 5n) to 95Nb
- Charged particle emission rate for 100Mo target: 0.06%
- Mean excitation energy for the dominant 1^- state: below the two-neutron separation energy of 11.1 MeV and one-proton separation energy of 9.5 MeV
- For 92Mo target, charged particle emission rate reaches 13.1%, with S_p = 5.8 MeV lower than S_n = 7.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the experimental studies of Hashim (2018) and Hashim (2019) on Nb isotope mass distributions from muon capture on Mo isotopes at MuSIC (J-PARC MLF) and MUSE (Osaka University), which found about 50% conversion to 99Nb. The authors improve upon the phenomenological pre-equilibrium and equilibrium model used by Ejiri (1989) by providing a microscopic description using pn-QRPA that reveals the crucial role of the 1^- state from proton pf-shell to neutron sdg-shell transitions. This enables future systematic calculations of muon capture rates across the nuclear chart and assessment of 99Mo production feasibility at muon beam facilities.
