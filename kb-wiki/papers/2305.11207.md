# Fast Neutrino Flavor Conversions can Help and Hinder Neutrino-Driven Explosions

> We present the first simulations of core-collapse supernovae in axial symmetry with feedback from fast neutrino flavor conversion (FFC). Our schematic treatment of FFCs assumes instantaneous flavor equilibration under the constraint of lepton-number conservation individually for each flavor. Systematically varying the spatial domain where FFCs are assumed to occur, we find that they facilitate SN explosions in low-mass (9-12 solar masses) progenitors that otherwise explode with longer time delays, whereas FFCs weaken the tendency to explode of higher-mass (around 20 solar masses) progenitors.

## Key claim

Fast neutrino flavor conversions (FFCs) can either facilitate or hinder neutrino-driven supernova explosions depending on the progenitor mass and the spatial region where FFCs occur, with low-mass progenitors (9-12 solar masses) exploding earlier when FFCs are included, while a 20 solar mass progenitor shows weakened explosion tendency.

## Method

The authors performed 2D (axial symmetry) neutrino-hydrodynamic simulations using the Alcar code, implementing a schematic treatment of FFCs that assumes instantaneous flavor equilibration under lepton-number conservation individually for each flavor. They systematically varied the threshold density (from 10^9 to 10^14 g/cm^3) below which FFCs were assumed to occur, and evolved three progenitor models (9, 11.2, and 20 solar masses) from 5 ms post-bounce onward on a polar grid with 640 radial and 80 lateral zones.

## Key numbers

- 9-12 solar mass progenitors: FFCs facilitate earlier explosions compared to noFC models
- 20 solar mass progenitor: FFCs cause faster and stronger shock contraction, with no explosion within 500 ms post-bounce
- Threshold densities tested: 10^9, 10^10, 10^11, 10^12, 10^13, 10^14 g/cm^3
- Model M11.2-2D-1e10 produces a neutron star with baryonic mass of 1.30 solar masses (gravitational mass ~1.19 solar masses for 12 km radius) versus ~1.36 solar masses without FFC
- PNS masses: ~1.9 solar masses for 20 solar mass progenitor versus 1.30-1.36 solar masses for lower-mass progenitors
- Shock expansion in M11.2-2D-1e13 occurs at ~70 ms post-bounce when Si/O interface arrives
- For 9.0 solar mass progenitor, mass accretion rate drops below that of 11.2 solar mass only at ~200 ms post-bounce

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 1D study (Ehring 2023) to multi-dimensional simulations, being the first to include FFC feedback in axial symmetry. The results contrast with earlier multi-D simulations (Melson 2015, Glas 2019) that found the 20 solar mass progenitor failed to explode even without FFCs, while confirming that low-mass progenitors explode more readily. The findings suggest FFCs could affect neutron star and black hole mass distributions, potentially explaining observed neutron stars below 1.2 solar masses (Stockinger 2020, Suwa 2018) and the red supergiant problem (Smartt 2009).
