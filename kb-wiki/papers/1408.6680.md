# States of Carbon-12 in the Skyrme Model

> The Skyrme model has two Skyrmion solutions of baryon number $12$, with $D_{3h}$ and $D_{4h}$ symmetries. The first has an equilateral triangular shape and the second an extended linear shape, analogous to the triangle and linear chain structures of three alpha particles. We recalculate the moments of inertia of these Skyrmions, and deduce the energies and spins of their quantized rotational excitations. There is a good match with the ground-state band of Carbon-12, and with the recently established rotational band of the Hoyle state. The ratio of the root mean square matter radii also matches the experimental value.

# States of Carbon-12 in the Skyrme Model

## Key claim

The Skyrme model provides a quantitative match to the ground-state rotational band of Carbon-12 and the recently established rotational band of the Hoyle state, but the model cannot confirm the 7.65 MeV energy difference between these states because the classical Skyrmion energies are too close relative to their numerical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors numerically compute two static Skyrmion solutions of baryon number 12, one with D3h symmetry (equilateral triangular shape) and one with D4h symmetry (linear chain shape), by allowing symmetric arrangements of three B=4 Skyrmions to relax to minimal energy configurations. They recalculate the moments of inertia tensors for both solutions and use the rigid-body quantization Hamiltonian for symmetric tops to deduce the energies and spins of rotational excitations, converting from Skyrme units to physical units using a fitted conversion factor.

## Key numbers

- Moments of inertia for the D3h triangular Skyrmion: V11 = 5039, V33 = 7689 (numerical errors ±3)
- Moments of inertia for the D4h chain Skyrmion: V11 = 12699, V33 = 2106
- Classical Skyrmion energies: 1816 (D3h) and 1812 (D4h) in Skyrme units, with numerical errors up to 0.2%
- Ratio of V11 values (D4h/D3h) = 12699/5039 = 2.52; experimental slope ratio from best linear fit is 2.45
- Ratio of root mean square matter radii: 1.22 (Skyrme model), 1.19 (experimental)
- Predicted energies for five spin-6 states: 29.7, 27.5, 20.9, 19.4, and 42.0 MeV
- Experimental energies for ground-state band: 0+ at 0 MeV, 2+ at 4.4 MeV, 4+ at 14.1 MeV
- Experimental energies for Hoyle band: 0+ at 7.65 MeV, 2+ at 9.8 MeV, 4+ at 13.3 MeV
- Fitted conversion factor C = 7130 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior Skyrme model calculations of B=12 Skyrmions by Battye, Manton, and Sutcliffe (2007) and provides the first accurate calculation of moments of inertia for the D4h chain Skyrmion, which had only been estimated previously using the parallel axis theorem by Wood. The results support the interpretation of the Hoyle state as a linear chain of three alpha particles, in contrast to lattice calculations from chiral effective field theory that find an obtuse triangular structure for the Hoyle state (Epelbaum et al., 2012). The work enables predictions for spin-6 states in both bands and for isospin excitations, and suggests that proposed 3- and 4- Hoyle state excitations will not be observed.
