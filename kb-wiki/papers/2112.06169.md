# Isovector density and isospin impurity in $^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$

> We study isoscalar (IS) and isovector (IV) densities in ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$ in comparison with theoretical densities calculated by Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) models. The charge symmetry breaking and the charge independence breaking forces are introduced to study the effect on the IV density. The effect of isospin mixing in the ground-state density is examined by using the particle-vibration coupling model taking into account the collective IV giant monopole excitation. We show a clear correlations in the IV density and isospin impurity of ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$ within the HF and the particle-vibration coupling model. We extract for the first time the experimental information of isospin impurity from the magnitude of IV density.

## Key claim

The authors show for the first time that experimental information on isospin impurity in the ground state of ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$ can be extracted from the magnitude of the isovector density, but this extraction relies on a clear correlation between the isovector density and isospin impurity that holds within both the Hartree-Fock and particle-vibration coupling models.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the SAMi-J family of energy density functionals to compute isoscalar and isovector densities for ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$, introducing charge symmetry breaking (CSB) and charge independence breaking (CIB) Skyrme-type forces to study their effect on the isovector density. They also employed a particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model that couples the ground state to the collective isovector giant monopole resonance (IVGMR) via the isospin symmetry breaking interaction, using the Werntz-Uberall form for the transition density normalized to satisfy the energy-weighted sum rule. The isospin impurity was evaluated from the mixing amplitude between the ground state and the IVGMR state, and the resulting isovector densities were compared with experimental densities extracted from proton elastic scattering at 295 MeV and electron scattering data.

## Key numbers

* Experimental root-mean-square radii for ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$: charge radius $r_{\mathrm{ch}} = 3.480$ fm, proton radius $r_p = 3.385$ fm, neutron radius $r_n = 3.375$ fm, neutron-skin thickness $\Delta r_{np} = -0.010$ fm (from Refs. Zenihiro and electron-sca).
* The isospin impurity from the hydrodynamical model is $\varepsilon^2 = 2.5 \times 10^{-6} Z^4 / A^{4/3}$, giving $\varepsilon^2 = 0.29\%$ for ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$.
* The microscopic HF+TDA model with the SIII Skyrme interaction gives a larger isospin impurity of 0.57% in ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$.
* The collective amplitude for the IVGMR in ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$ is $|\alpha^{\tau=1}|^2 = 0.0192$ using the SIII interaction.
* The IVGMR excitation energy from the two-fluid model is $\hbar \omega^{\tau=1}_{\mathrm{GMR}} = 170 / A^{1/3}$ MeV, which is 49.7 MeV for ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$; the self-consistent RPA with SIII gives a lower average excitation energy of $\overline{\hbar \omega}^{\tau=1} = 33.5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron density distributions and neutron-skin thicknesses in closed-shell nuclei by Zenihiro and collaborators, who determined these quantities from polarized proton elastic scattering at 295 MeV. The authors build on the Skyrme HF framework of Yoshida (2020) and the treatment of isospin symmetry breaking forces by Jacheck and Xavi, using the PVC model originally applied to isospin impurity by Auerbach. The results enable future extraction of isospin impurity from experimental isovector densities, and the finding that the small occupation probability of the $2s_{1/2}$ orbit in ${}^{40} \mathrm{Ca}$ is an open question to be addressed in future studies.
