# A new insight into the observation of spectroscopic strength reduction in atomic nuclei: implication for the physical meaning of spectroscopic factors

> Experimental studies of one nucleon knockout from magic nuclei suggest that their nucleon orbits are not fully occupied. This conflicts a commonly accepted view of the shell closure associated with such nuclei. The conflict can be reconciled if the overlap between initial and final nuclear states in a knockout reaction are calculated by a non-standard method. The method employs an inhomogeneous equation based on correlation-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and allows the simplest wave functions, in which all nucleons occupy only the lowest nuclear orbits, to be used. The method also reproduces the recently established relation between reduction of spectroscopic strength, observed in knockout reactions on other nuclei, and nucleon binding energies. The implication of the inhomogeneous equation method for the physical meaning of spectroscopic factors is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed reduction of spectroscopic strength in one-nucleon knockout reactions does not necessarily imply that shell-model orbits in magic nuclei are not fully occupied; instead, the reduction can be explained if the overlap functions between initial and final nuclear states are calculated using an inhomogeneous equation method that accounts for coupling to excluded model spaces, rather than by direct evaluation of the overlap in truncated model spaces.

## Method

The author calculates spectroscopic factors (SFs) by solving the inhomogeneous equation of Pinkston and Satchler (1965) for the radial overlap function \(I_{lj}(r)\), using a coordinate-space Green function and an effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction (M3YE) in the source term. The method employs the simplest \(0\hbar\omega\) shell-model wave functions (single Slater determinants) for the initial and final nuclei, with the effective interaction calibrated to reproduce asymptotic normalization coefficients from peripheral transfer experiments. Calculations are performed for nuclei with \(A < 16\) and for \(^{16}\)O, using oscillator radii chosen to reproduce experimental root-mean-square radii.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{16}\)O → \(^{15}\)N, the direct evaluation SF \(S^{DE} = 2.13\) is reduced to \(S^{IE} = 1.57\) with M3YE and to 1.29 with a renormalized interaction RM3YE, compared to the experimental \((e,e'p)\) value of \(1.27(13)\).
- For \(^{12}\)C → \(^{11}\)B, \(S^{DE} = 2.85\) is reduced to \(S^{IE} = 1.55\) (M3YE) and 1.76 (RM3YE), compared to the experimental value of \(1.72(11)\).
- For the deuteron (\(A=2\)), the method yields \(S^{IE} = 0.91\), close to the \(s\)-wave probability of 0.94 from the realistic AV18 potential.
- For \(^{4}\)He → \(^{3}\)He, \(S^{DE} = 2.0\) is reduced to \(S^{IE} = 1.29\) (M3YE) and 1.42 (RM3YE), compared to the ab-initio VMC value of 1.50.
- The \((e,e'p)\) cross sections on \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40,48}\)Ca, and \(^{208}\)Pb are 50-60% smaller than expected from the independent particle model.

## Context

This work extends the earlier inhomogeneous equation approach of Pinkston and Satchler (1965) and the source-term method of Mukhamedzhanov and Timofeyuk (1990) by applying it to the recently observed phenomenon of spectroscopic strength reduction and its dependence on nucleon separation energies (Gade et al., 2008). The method contradicts the traditional interpretation that spectroscopic factors directly measure shell-model orbit occupancies; instead, it suggests they measure the strength of effective NN interactions in a fixed model space. The approach enables the use of minimal shell-model spaces to reproduce experimental SFs, and could be incorporated into existing shell-model codes and ab-initio methods for applications in nuclear astrophysics.
