# High-spin structures of 124-131Te: Competition of proton and neutron pair breakings

> The 124-131Te nuclei have been produced as fission fragments in two fusion reactions induced by heavy-ions (12C + 238U at 90 MeV bombarding energy and 18O + 208Pb at 85 MeV) and studied with the Euroball array. Their high-spin level schemes have been extended to higher excitation energy from the triple gamma-ray coincidence data. The gamma-gamma angular correlations have been analyzed in order to assign spin and parity values to many observed states. Moreover the half-lives of isomeric states have been measured from the delayed coincidences between the fission-fragment detector SAPhIR and Euroball, as well as from the timing information of the Ge detectors. The behaviors of the yrast structures identified in the present work are first discussed in comparison with the general features known in the mass region, particularly the breakings of neutron pairs occupying the nuh11/2 orbit identified in the neighboring Sn nuclei. The experimental level schemes are then compared to shell-model calculations performed in this work. The analysis of the wave functions shows the effects of the proton-pair breaking along the yrast lines of the heavy Te isotopes.

## Key claim
The paper extends the high-spin level schemes of 124-131Te to higher excitation energies and shows that, in addition to the previously known neutron-pair breaking in the νh11/2 orbit, proton-pair breaking becomes competitive along the yrast lines of the heavier Te isotopes, as revealed by shell-model wave-function analysis.

## Method
The Te isotopes were produced as fission fragments in two heavy-ion fusion reactions (12C + 238U at 90 MeV and 18O + 208Pb at 85 MeV) and studied with the Euroball gamma-ray spectrometer. Triple gamma-ray coincidence data were used to extend level schemes, gamma-gamma angular correlations were analyzed to assign spin and parity values, and half-lives of isomeric states were measured using delayed coincidences between the SAPhIR fission-fragment detector and Euroball, as well as timing information from Ge detectors. Shell-model calculations using the SN100PN effective interaction were performed in the full valence space for 128-131Te and compared to the experimental results.

## Key numbers
- Half-life of the 10+ isomeric state in 128Te: T1/2 = 236(20) ns, measured from delayed coincidences, giving B(E2; 10+ → 8+) = 85(7) e²fm⁴ (2.2(2) W.u.)
- Half-life of the 10+ isomeric state in 130Te: previously measured as T1/2 = 1.90(8) μs (from thermal neutron induced fission), cited from prior work
- In 126Te, the 10+ state at 2972.4 keV is isomeric (half-life from Ref. [nndc])
- In 128Te, the 10+ state at 2788.9 keV decays via a 101.3(3) keV transition to the 8+ state at 2687.6 keV
- In 124Te, the 10+ state is at 3152.4 keV and the 11- state at 3987.1 keV
- In 126Te, the 12+ state is at 3685.6 keV and the 14+ state at 4535.7 keV
- In 128Te, the 12+ state is at 3506.0 keV and the 14+ state at 4428.6 keV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Te isotopes from deep-inelastic reactions (Zhang et al., 1998) which identified states up to spin (23/2) in 127-131Te and up to spin (12) or (14) in 126,128Te, i.e., up to the breaking of the first νh11/2 pair. The results are compared to shell-model calculations using the SN100PN effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential (Brown et al., 2005), which had previously been tested in nuclei near the doubly-magic 132Sn core. The new data enable testing of all parts of this effective interaction in nuclei with neutron holes in the presence of a few proton particles, and reveal the competition between proton and neutron pair breakings at high spin.
