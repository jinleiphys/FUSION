# Robust formulation of Wick's theorem for computing matrix elements between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wavefunctions

> Numerical difficulties associated with computing matrix elements of operators between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wavefunctions have plagued the development of HFB-based many-body theories for decades. The problem arises from divisions by zero in the standard formulation of the nonorthogonal Wick's theorem in the limit of vanishing HFB overlap. In this paper, we present a robust formulation of Wick's theorem that stays well-behaved regardless of whether the HFB states are orthogonal or not. This new formulation ensures cancellation between the zeros of the overlap and the poles of the Pfaffian, which appears naturally in fermionic systems. Our formula explicitly eliminates self-interaction, which otherwise causes additional numerical challenges. A computationally efficient version of our formalism enables robust symmetry-projected HFB calculations with the same computational cost as mean-field theories. Moreover, we avoid potentially diverging normalization factors by introducing a robust normalization procedure. The resulting formalism treats even and odd number of particles on equal footing and reduces to Hartree-Fock as a natural limit. As proof of concept, we present a numerically stable and accurate solution to a Jordan-Wigner-transformed Hamiltonian, whose singularities motivated the present work. Our robust formulation of Wick's theorem is a most promising development for methods using quasiparticle vacuum states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a reformulation of the nonorthogonal Wick's theorem that remains numerically well-behaved even when the overlap between two Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wavefunctions vanishes, by ensuring cancellation between the zeros of the overlap and the poles of the Pfaffian that appears in the standard formulation.

## Method

The authors derive a robust Wick's theorem by performing a canonical (antisymmetric) decomposition of the overlap matrix S, extracting its singular values s_r, and rewriting the matrix element as a sum over hyper-Pfaffians of modified contractions that explicitly cancel the s_r^{-1} factors against the overlap. A low-scaling version is also developed that partitions the contraction matrix into singular and regular parts, applying the robust treatment only to the singular subspace. A robust normalization procedure is introduced that replaces the unbounded normalization factor of HFB states with bounded matrices derived from the Bloch-Messiah decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The robust formula for the 2-RTM (two-particle reduced transition matrix) scales as O(M^5) for constructing the full matrix, but tensor contraction with two-body operator parameters scales as O(M^3) to O(M^4) after exploiting locality
- The low-scaling version reduces computational cost to O(M^2) to O(M^3) for two-body operator matrix elements, matching Hartree-Fock scaling with a larger prefactor
- The robust normalization procedure ensures all elements of the transformed overlap matrix S' are bounded by 1 in absolute value
- The number of zero elements in the singular value vector s determines the generalized Slater-Condon rules: if mu elements are strictly zero, the matrix element vanishes when mu exceeds the operator rank d

## Context

This work extends the nonorthogonal Wick's theorem formalism of Balian (1969) and Onishi (1966) to handle the previously problematic orthogonal limit where HFB overlap vanishes. It generalizes the singular value decomposition approach used for Hartree-Fock nonorthogonal configuration interaction by Koch (1993), Thom (2009), and Burton (2021) to the HFB case. The robust normalization procedure is similar to that proposed by Carlsson (2021) but treats even and odd particle numbers on equal footing. The formalism enables numerically stable symmetry-projected HFB calculations and resolves singularities encountered in Jordan-Wigner transformed spin systems studied by Henderson (2022).
