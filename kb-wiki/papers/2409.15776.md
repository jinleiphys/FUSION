# Twist-2 distribution amplitudes of $a_{0}(980)$ and $a_{0}(1450)$

> We investigate the twist-2 distribution amplitudes of the scalar mesons $a_{0}(980)$ and $a_{0}(1450)$ in the two-quark picture. The moments of these scalar mesons are obtained up to the third order with QCD sum rules method. With these moments, the first two Gegenbauer coefficients are determined and utilized to analyze the twist-2 distribution amplitudes. Our numerical results indicate that the meson $a_{0}(980)$ favors a conventional two-quark ground state. The paper concludes with an examination of the form factors for the transitions $B/D\rightarrow a_{0}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the scalar meson \(a_{0}(980)\) favors a conventional two-quark ground state, while \(a_{0}(1450)\) may not be a pure two-quark state and could involve tetraquark admixtures or be the first excited state of \(a_{0}(980)\). This conclusion is hedged by the assumption that both mesons are treated as ground-state particles in the analysis.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules to compute the first two non-zero odd moments \(\langle\xi^{1}\rangle\) and \(\langle\xi^{3}\rangle\) of the twist-2 distribution amplitudes for \(a_{0}(980)\) and \(a_{0}(1450)\) in the two-quark picture. The operator product expansion is truncated at dimension-6 operators, and Borel transformations are applied to suppress higher-state contributions. From these moments, the first two Gegenbauer coefficients \(B_{1}\) and \(B_{3}\) are determined, and the distribution amplitudes are reconstructed. Transition form factors for \(B/D \rightarrow a_{0}\) decays are then calculated using light-cone sum rules.

## Key numbers

*   For \(a_{0}(980)\) at scale \(\mu = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\): \(\langle\xi^{1}\rangle = -0.431 \pm 0.030 \pm 0.001\) and \(\langle\xi^{3}\rangle = -0.187 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.006\).
*   For \(a_{0}(1450)\) at scale \(\mu = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\): \(\langle\xi^{1}\rangle = -0.185 \pm 0.020 \pm 0.001\) and \(\langle\xi^{3}\rangle = -0.086 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.008\).
*   Gegenbauer coefficient \(B_{1} = -0.718\) for \(a_{0}(980)\) and \(B_{1} = -0.308\) for \(a_{0}(1450)\); \(B_{3} = -0.011\) for \(a_{0}(980)\) and \(B_{3} = -0.035\) for \(a_{0}(1450)\).
*   Transition form factor \(f_{+} = 0.53\) for \(B \rightarrow a_{0}(980)\) and \(f_{+} = 0.23\) for \(B \rightarrow a_{0}(1450)\).
*   Transition form factor \(f_{+} = 0.77\) for \(D \rightarrow a_{0}(980)\) and \(f_{+} = 0.32\) for \(D \rightarrow a_{0}(1450)\).
*   Predicted branching fraction for \(D^{+} \rightarrow a_{0}^{0}(980) e^{+} \nu_{e}\) is 0.79, compared to the BESIII measurement of \(1.66^{+0.81}_{-0.66}\).

## Context

This work extends QCD sum rule studies of scalar meson distribution amplitudes, following earlier approaches by Huang (2004) and Cheng (2005). It provides the first systematic determination of the twist-2 distribution amplitudes for \(a_{0}(980)\) and \(a_{0}(1450)\) using moments up to third order. The results enable improved predictions for heavy-flavor decays at LHCb and Belle II, and the discrepancies found for \(a_{0}(1450)\) motivate further investigation into its possible tetraquark or mixed configuration, as previously discussed by Jaffe (1976) and Wang (2009).
