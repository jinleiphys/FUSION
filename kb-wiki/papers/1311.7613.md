# Kinetic freeze-out, particle spectra and harmonic flow coefficients from mode-by-mode hydrodynamics

> The kinetic freeze-out for the hydrodynamical description of relativistic heavy ion collisions is discussed using a background-fluctuation splitting of the hydrodynamical fields. For a single event, the particle spectrum, or its logarithm, can be written as the sum of background part that is symmetric with respect to azimuthal rotations and longitudinal boosts and a part containing the contribution of fluctuations or deviations from the background. Using a complete orthonormal basis to characterize the initial state allows one to write the double differential harmonic flow coefficients determined by the two-particle correlation method as matrix expressions involving the initial fluid correlations. We discuss the use of these expressions for a mode-by-mode analysis of fluctuating initial conditions in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a technically complete documentation of how kinetic freeze-out of fluctuating modes is handled in mode-by-mode hydrodynamics, using a background-fluctuation splitting where the freeze-out hypersurface is defined at constant *background* temperature rather than constant total temperature, with the two descriptions being equivalent to linear order in fluctuations.

## Method

The authors perform a Cooper-Frye freeze-out on an azimuthally symmetric and Bjorken boost-invariant hypersurface defined at constant background temperature, expanding all fluid dynamic fields (temperature, velocity, shear stress tensor) into background and fluctuating parts. They then Fourier-expand the fluctuating fields in azimuthal angle and space-time rapidity, and analytically integrate the Cooper-Frye formula over the hypersurface to obtain expressions for particle spectra and harmonic flow coefficients as linear responses to specific fluctuation modes. The background part of the shear stress tensor is parameterized with two independent components, and the fluctuating shear stress is reduced to five independent components using the tracelessness and orthogonality constraints.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_0 = 120 \,\text{MeV}\) used for the freeze-out curve calculations in Fig. 1
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratios compared: \(\eta/s = 0\), \(\eta/s = 0.08\), and \(\eta/s = 0.3\) for the freeze-out curves and particle spectra
- Transverse momentum range where hydrodynamic models provide good description: up to \(p_T \simeq 2 - 3\) GeV (stated in the Introduction)
- The background contribution to the particle spectrum \(S_0(m_T,p_T)\) is given as an integral over the freeze-out surface parameter \(\alpha\) with modified Bessel functions \(K_1\), \(I_0\), \(K_0\), and \(I_1\) evaluated at arguments involving \((1+j)\) factors for bosons and fermions

## Context

This work extends the mode-by-mode hydrodynamics approach developed by the same authors (Floerchinger and Wiedemann, 2013) for a differential study of fluctuation effects in heavy ion collisions. It provides the technical freeze-out formalism that complements the earlier framework, enabling a systematic decomposition of how specific fluid dynamic fluctuation modes translate into experimental observables like particle spectra and harmonic flow coefficients. The approach contrasts with standard Cooper-Frye prescriptions where freeze-out occurs on a surface of constant total temperature, and it can be used to establish to what extent specific fluid dynamic features are washed out or survive hadronization.
