# Present status of the nonstrange and other flavor partners of the exotic Theta+ baryon

> Given the existing empirical information about the exotic Theta+ baryon, we analyze possible properties of its SU(3)F-partners, paying special attention to the nonstrange member of the antidecuplet N*. The modified piN partial-wave analysis presents two candidate masses, 1680 MeV and 1730 MeV. In both cases, the N* should be rather narrow and highly inelastic. Our results suggest several directions for experimental studies that may clarify properties of the antidecuplet baryons, and structure of their mixing with other baryons. Recent experimental evidence from the GRAAL and STAR Collaborations could be interpreted as observations of a candidate for the Theta+ nonstrange partner.

# Present status of the nonstrange and other flavor partners of the exotic Theta+ baryon

## Key claim
The state commonly known as N(1710) is not the appropriate candidate to be a member of the antidecuplet together with the Theta+(1540); instead, the modified piN partial-wave analysis suggests two candidate masses for the nonstrange antidecuplet member N* at 1680 MeV and 1730 MeV, both of which should be narrow and highly inelastic, though the authors acknowledge essential theoretical uncertainties and that the existence of the Theta+ itself is not definitively established.

## Method
The authors performed a modified partial-wave analysis (PWA) of piN scattering data, assuming the existence of a narrow resonance and comparing the quality of fit with and without such structures. This method, previously used to search for the Theta+(1540), was applied to search for a narrow nucleon-like state assumed to be a member of the antidecuplet accompanying the Theta+. The analysis used the Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula with a sigma-term from the GW SAID analysis to determine antidecuplet mass differences and mixing angles.

## Key numbers
- Theta+(1540) mass near 1540 MeV with upper bounds on width of order 10 MeV
- Xi--(3/2) mass 1862 +/- 2 MeV with width less than 18 MeV (less than experimental resolution)
- Antidecuplet mass difference of about 110 MeV (instead of originally predicted 180 MeV), based on sigma-term from latest piN data analysis
- Mass difference between neighboring isospin multiplets in antidecuplet: (M_Xi3/2 - M_Theta)/3 approximately 107 MeV
- Candidate N* masses from modified PWA: M_R = 1680 MeV and 1730 MeV
- For M_R = 1680 MeV candidate: Gamma_el less than 0.5 MeV
- For M_R = 1730 MeV candidate: Gamma_el less than 0.3 MeV
- N(1710) PDG width estimates range from approximately 90 MeV to approximately 500 MeV
- N(1710) branching ratios: 10-20% for Npi, 40-90% for Npipi, 6 +/- 1% for Neta
- With larger mixing (sin phi approximately 0.15), partial width N* -> piDelta increases to about 15 MeV, total width remains not higher than approximately 30 MeV

## Context
This work extends the search for exotic baryons following the observation of the Theta+(1540) by Nakano and collaborators, and the Xi--(3/2) by the Xi Collaboration. It contradicts the assignment of the PDG-listed N(1710) as the nonstrange antidecuplet partner, which was used as input in the original prediction by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). The analysis builds on the modified PWA method previously applied to the Theta+ by Arndt, Strakovsky, and Workman (2003) and to light nucleon resonances by Arndt et al. (2003). The results suggest experimental searches for narrow nucleon states near 1700 MeV in piN collisions, photoproduction, and final states such as piDelta, etaN, and KLambda, and enable tests of octet-antidecuplet mixing through photoexcitation amplitude comparisons and branching ratio measurements for the Xi(3/2).
