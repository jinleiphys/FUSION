# Multi-isotope degeneracy of neutrinoless double beta decay mechanisms in the quasi-particle random phase approximation

> We calculate nuclear matrix elements (NME) of neutrinoless double beta decay in four different candidate nuclei (Ge-76, Se-82, Mo-100, Te-130) within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and its uncertainties. We assume (up to) four coexisting mechanisms for neutrinoless double beta decay, mediated by light Majorana neutrino exchange, heavy Majorana neutrino exchange, R-parity breaking supersymmetry, and squark-neutrino, interfering either constructively or destructively with each other. We find that, unfortunately, current NME uncertainties appear to prevent a robust determination of the relative contribution of each mechanism to the decay amplitude, even assuming accurate measurements of decay lifetimes. The near-degeneracy of the decay mechanisms is analyzed with simple algebraic techniques, which do not involve assumptions about the statistical distribution of errors. We discuss implications of such degeneracy on prospective searches for absolute neutrino masses.

## Key claim
Current nuclear matrix element (NME) uncertainties within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) prevent a robust determination of the relative contribution of each of four coexisting neutrinoless double beta decay mechanisms, even assuming accurate measurements of decay lifetimes; the near-degeneracy of mechanisms is analyzed with simple algebraic techniques that do not involve assumptions about the statistical distribution of errors.

## Method
The authors calculated NMEs for four candidate nuclei (Ge-76, Se-82, Mo-100, Te-130) using the self-consistent renormalized QRPA (SRQRPA), considering four mechanisms: light Majorana neutrino exchange, heavy Majorana neutrino exchange, R-parity breaking supersymmetry, and squark-neutrino exchange. They introduced eight QRPA variants by varying the model space size (intermediate or large), the nucleon-nucleon potential (Argonne or CD-Bonn), and the axial coupling constant (g_A = 1.00 or 1.25), then applied singular value decomposition (SVD) to analyze the stability of the linear system relating NMEs to lepton-number-violating parameters.

## Key numbers
- Phase space factors G_i for the four nuclei: Ge-76 (7.93), Se-82 (35.2), Mo-100 (57.3), Te-130 (55.4), all in units of 10^{-15} y^{-1}.
- For QRPA variant 1 (g_A=1, Argonne potential, intermediate model space), NMEs for the light neutrino mechanism: Ge-76 (3.85), Se-82 (3.59), Mo-100 (3.62), Te-130 (3.29).
- For QRPA variant 1, NMEs for the heavy neutrino mechanism: Ge-76 (172), Se-82 (165), Mo-100 (185), Te-130 (171).
- For QRPA variant 1, NMEs for the R-parity breaking mechanism: Ge-76 (387), Se-82 (375), Mo-100 (412), Te-130 (385).
- For QRPA variant 1, NMEs for the squark-neutrino mechanism: Ge-76 (396), Se-82 (379), Mo-100 (405), Te-130 (382).
- Condition numbers kappa for the eight QRPA variants range from 0.68 x 10^3 (variant 7) to 3.05 x 10^3 (variant 6), with reciprocal condition numbers rho between 0.33 x 10^{-3} and 1.48 x 10^{-3}.
- In a test scenario with true light neutrino exchange only (m_beta_beta = 0.316 eV), the reconstructed relative contributions W_j for Ge-76 vary dramatically across QRPA variants; for variant 1, W_nu = +1.05, W_N = -0.01, W_R = +0.12, W_q = -0.16; for variant 6, W_nu = +7.90, W_N = +16.22, W_R = +1.86, W_q = -24.98.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Simkovic et al. (2010) by including full QRPA variations in NME calculations, whereas that earlier study used fixed NMEs from a single calculation. It also builds on earlier discrimination studies by Deppisch et al. (2007), Gehman et al. (2007), and Fogli et al. (2009), which used Monte Carlo simulations or likelihood analyses with inhomogeneous NME comparisons. The results imply that multi-isotope half-life data alone cannot reliably distinguish between the four mechanisms, and that future progress requires either improved nuclear structure calculations or independent constraints from absolute neutrino mass searches.
