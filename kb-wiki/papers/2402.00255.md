# The parity-odd structure function of nucleon from the Compton amplitude

> The dominant contribution to the theoretical uncertainty in the extracted weak parameters of the Standard Model comes from the hadronic uncertainties in the electroweak boxes, i.e. $γ-W^\pm/Z$ exchange diagrams. A dispersive analysis relates the box diagrams to the parity-odd structure function, $F_3$, for which the experimental data either do not exist or belong to a separate isospin channel. Therefore a first-principles calculation of $F_3$ is highly desirable. In this contribution, we report on the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration's progress in calculating the moments of the $F_3^{γZ}$ structure function from the forward Compton amplitude at the SU(3) symmetric point.

## Key claim

The authors report preliminary lattice QCD calculations of the parity-odd Compton amplitude for the nucleon, from which the lowest moment of the \(F_3^{\gamma Z}\) structure function can be extracted at \(\omega=0\) with sub-percent statistical precision, but they refrain from quoting moment results until lattice artefacts are addressed.

## Method

The QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration computes the forward Compton amplitude for a vector and an axial-vector current using a second-order Feynman-Hellmann approach on the lattice, which reduces the problem to analyzing two-point correlation functions in the presence of spatially oscillating background fields. The parity-odd structure function \(\mathcal{F}_3\) is isolated by choosing off-diagonal tensor components with kinematics \(\mu=1, \nu=3\) and setting the nucleon and current momenta such that \(p_1 = q_1 = 0\). The energy shift at mixed order \(\lambda_1\lambda_2\) is extracted from a ratio of four perturbed two-point functions, and the \(\omega\)-dependence of \(\mathcal{F}_3\) is mapped by varying the nucleon momentum at fixed photon virtuality.

## Key numbers

- Lattice ensemble: \(48^3 \times 96\) volume, coupling \(\beta=5.65\), lattice spacing \(a=0.068\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Pion mass at the SU(3) symmetric point: \(m_\pi \approx 420\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Fixed photon virtuality: \(Q^2 \sim 5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), accessed via two momentum transfers \(\mathbf{q} = (0,3,5)\,\frac{2\pi}{L}\) and \((0,5,3)\,\frac{2\pi}{L}\).
- Statistics: approximately 500 measurements for \(\mathbf{q}=(0,3,5)\,\frac{2\pi}{L}\) and 250 measurements for \(\mathbf{q}=(0,5,3)\,\frac{2\pi}{L}\).
- Coupling strengths used: \(|\lambda| = [0.0125, 0.025]\).
- The \(\omega=0\) point (lowest moment) is determined with sub-percent statistical precision.

## Context

This work extends the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration's previous Feynman-Hellmann calculations of unpolarised (PhysRevD.102.114505, QCDSFUKQCDCSSM:2022ncb) and polarised (Can:2022chd) parity-conserving nucleon structure functions to the parity-odd case. The lowest moment of \(F_3^{\gamma Z}\) is related via dispersion relations to the \(\gamma W\) and \(\gamma Z\) electroweak box diagrams, which dominate the hadronic uncertainty in determinations of CKM matrix elements (Hardy 2014) and the weak mixing angle (Kumar 2013), and is also a clean test of the Gross–Llewellyn-Smith sum rule. Once systematic uncertainties are controlled, the results will provide input for superallowed nuclear and neutron beta decays (complementary to Ma 2023) and for the neutrino oscillation program at DUNE.
