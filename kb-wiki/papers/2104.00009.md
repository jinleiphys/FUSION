# Spinning Gluons from the QCD Light-Ray OPE

> We study the transverse spin structure of the squeezed limit of the three-point energy correlator, $\langle \mathcal{E}(\vec n_1) \mathcal{E}(\vec n_2) \mathcal{E}(\vec n_3) \rangle$. To describe its all orders perturbative behavior, we develop the light-ray operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD. At leading twist the iterated OPE of $\mathcal{E}(\vec n_i)$ operators closes onto light-ray operators $\mathbb{O}^{[J]}(\vec n)$ with spin $J$, and transverse spin $j=0,2$. We compute the $\mathcal{E}(\vec n_1) \mathcal{E}(\vec n_2)$, $\mathcal{E}(\vec n_1) \mathbb{O}^{[J]}(\vec n_2) $ and $\mathbb{O}^{[J_1]}(\vec n_1) \mathbb{O}^{[J_2]}(\vec n_2) $ OPEs as analytic functions of $J$, which allows for the description of arbitrary squeezed limits of $N$-point correlators in QCD. We use these results with $J=3$ to reproduce the perturbative expansion in the squeezed limit of the three-point correlator, as well as to resum the leading twist singular structure for both quark and gluon jets, including transverse spin contributions, as required for phenomenological applications. Finally, we briefly comment on the transverse spin structure at higher twists, and show that to all orders in the twist expansion the highest transverse spin contributions are universal between quark and gluon jets, and are descendants of the leading twist transverse spin-2 operator, allowing their resummation into a simple two-dimensional Euclidean conformal block. Due to the general applicability of our results to arbitrary correlation functions of energy flow operators, we anticipate that they can be widely applied to improving our understanding of jet substructure at the LHC.

## Key claim
The paper develops the light-ray operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD to describe the transverse spin structure of the squeezed limit of the three-point energy correlator, showing that at leading twist the OPE closes onto light-ray operators with spin J and transverse spin j=0,2, and that the highest transverse spin contributions are universal between quark and gluon jets to all orders in the twist expansion.

## Method
The authors compute the OPEs of energy flow operators E(n) and light-ray operators O[J](n) as analytic functions of the spin J, using the squeezed limit of the three-point energy correlator in QCD and N=4 super Yang-Mills theory. They expand the fully integrated three-point correlator results from Chen (2019) in the squeezed limit using a symmetric parametrization (x, w) that eliminates odd power corrections, and also perform an integrand-level expansion by separating soft and collinear momentum regions to extract the twist expansion.

## Key numbers
- The three-point energy correlator at leading order is proportional to (alpha_s/4pi)^2 times a factor of 16/x_L times G_i(z), where G_i(z) depends on the jet type (quark, gluon, or N=4 SYM).
- In the squeezed limit parametrization, the expansion variable is |w| = theta_S/(2 theta_L), with theta_S the small angle between the squeezed pair and theta_L the larger angle.
- The leading twist contribution in the squeezed limit scales as |w|^0 (no power suppression), while higher twist contributions scale as |w|^2, |w|^4, etc.
- For quark jets, the leading twist term in the squeezed limit expansion contains a cos(2phi) modulation from transverse spin-2 operators, with the coefficient proportional to the spin-2 structure constant.

## Context
This work extends the light-ray OPE framework developed by Hofman and Maldacena (2008) and Kravchuk (2018) to QCD, building on the three-point energy correlator calculations of Chen (2019) and the transverse spin analysis of Chen (2020). The results enable resummation of large logarithms in the squeezed limit of energy correlators for phenomenological applications at the LHC, and provide a systematic framework for computing arbitrary N-point energy correlators in QCD using the light-ray OPE.
