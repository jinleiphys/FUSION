# Photoproduction of Pentaquark in Feynman and Regge Theories

> Photoproduction of the Theta+ pentaquark on the proton is analyzed by using an isobar and a Regge models. The difference in the calculated total cross section is found to be more than two orders of magnitude for a hadronic form factor cut-off Lambda > 1 GeV. Comparable results would be obtained for 0.6 < Lambda < 0.8 GeV. We also calculate contribution of the Theta+ photoproduction to the GDH integral. By comparing with the current phenomenological calculation, it is found that the GDH sum rule favors the result obtained from Regge approach and isobar model with small Lambda.

## Key claim

The total cross section for photoproduction of the Θ⁺ pentaquark on the proton differs by more than two orders of magnitude between an isobar model and a Regge model when the hadronic form factor cut-off Λ exceeds 1 GeV, but comparable results are obtained for Λ between 0.6 and 0.8 GeV; the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule favors the Regge model and the isobar model with small Λ.

## Method

The author calculated tree-level Feynman diagrams for γ + p → K̄⁰ + Θ⁺ using an isobar model with p, Θ⁺, K*, and K₁ intermediate states, including hadronic form factors with a Haberzettl prescription. A Regge model was also constructed by replacing the Feynman propagators for K* and K₁ exchange with Regge propagators, using the trajectories from Guidal et al. (1997). The spin-dependent total cross sections σ_T and σ_TT' were computed from both models, and their contributions to the GDH integral were evaluated and compared with the known phenomenological value.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g_{KΘN}/√(4π) = 0.39, derived from a Θ⁺ decay width of 1 MeV.
- The K*⁰ → K⁰γ decay width is 116 ± 10 keV, yielding g_{K*⁰K⁰γ} = -1.27.
- The vector coupling G^V_{K*ΘN}/(4π) = 8.72 × 10⁻².
- The vector coupling G^V_{K₁ΘN}/(4π) = -7.64 × 10⁻³.
- The GDH integral for the proton is -205 μb from the sum rule; the Θ⁺ photoproduction contribution is positive and small, with the Regge model and isobar model at Λ ≤ 0.6 GeV being favorable.
- The isobar model cross section increases by two orders of magnitude when Λ is varied from 0.6 to 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior isobar-model studies of Θ⁺ photoproduction (Y. Oh et al., 2004; W. Liu and C. M. Ko, 2004; S. I. Nam et al., 2004; Q. Zhao, 2004; Y. R. Liu et al., 2004; P. Ko, 2004) which gave cross sections spanning from several nanobarns to nearly 1 μbarn, by systematically comparing with a Regge model and constraining the hadronic form factor cut-off. The use of Regge theory follows the approach of Guidal et al. (1997) for KΛ and KΣ photoproduction, where it worked well at high energies. The GDH sum-rule comparison with the phenomenological calculation of Drechsel et al. (2003) provides an additional constraint favoring the Regge model and soft form factors, enabling future experimental tests at JLab, SPRING-8, or ELSA.
