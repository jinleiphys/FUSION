# Quark-Gluon plasma fireball evolution with one loop correction in the peshier potential

> The study of free energy evolution of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with one loop correction factor in the peshier potential is discussed. The energy evolution with the effect of the correction factor in peshier potential shows the transition temperature obtained in the range of temperature~$T=180-250~MeV$. The transition temperature is also effected with the decrease of dynamical flow parameter of quark and gluon used in the potential and it shows the observable QGP droplets of the stable size of fermi radius viz $2.5-4.5 fm$.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a one-loop correction factor in the Peshier potential modifies the free energy evolution of the quark-gluon plasma fireball, yielding a transition temperature in the range of 180–250 MeV and stable QGP droplets of radius 2.5–4.5 fm, but only when the gluon dynamical flow parameter is adjusted to values between 8 and 10 times the quark flow parameter.

## Method

The authors construct a density of states for quarks and gluons using the Thomas-Fermi model, starting from a confining Peshier potential modified by a one-loop correction factor in the strong coupling constant. They then compute the free energy of quarks, gluons, and pions, plus an interfacial energy term replacing the MIT bag energy, and numerically evaluate the total free energy as a function of droplet radius for various temperatures and gluon flow parameters.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature range: T = 180–250 MeV, obtained with gluon flow parameter γ_g = 4 γ_q to γ_g < 8 γ_q.
- Stable QGP droplet radii: 2.5–4.5 fm, observed for gluon flow parameters in the range 8 γ_q ≤ γ_g ≤ 10 γ_q.
- Quark flow parameter fixed at γ_q = 1/8 throughout the calculations.
- QCD scale parameter Λ = 0.15 GeV used in the running coupling.
- Quark masses taken as m_u = m_d = 0 MeV, m_s = 0.15 GeV; pion mass m_π = 0.14 GeV.
- Individual free energy contributions shown at T = 152 MeV for γ_g = 12 γ_q.

## Context

This work extends the earlier approach of Ramanathan et al. (2004, 2007) by adding a one-loop correction to the Peshier potential, which modifies the density of states and the free energy evolution. The results show that the one-loop correction requires smaller gluon flow parameters to reproduce stable droplet formation, bringing the model closer to lattice QCD expectations. The findings enable further study of surface tension and thermodynamic properties of QGP based on the smaller stable droplet sizes obtained.
