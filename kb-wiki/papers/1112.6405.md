# Relativistic Theory of Hydrodynamic Fluctuations with Applications to Heavy Ion Collisions

> We develop the relativistic theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations for application to high energy heavy ion collisions. In particular, we investigate their effect on the expanding boost-invariant (Bjorken) solution of the hydrodynamic equations. We discover that correlations over a long rapidity range are induced by the propagation of the sound modes. Due to the expansion, the dispersion law for these modes is non-linear and attenuated even in the limit of zero viscosity. As a result, there is a non-dissipative wake behind the sound front which is generated by any instantaneous point-like fluctuation. We evaluate the two-particle correlators using the initial conditions and hydrodynamic parameters relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. In principle these correlators can be used to obtain information about the viscosities because the magnitudes of the fluctuations are directly proportional to them.

## Key claim

The paper develops a relativistic theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations and applies it to the expanding boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow, finding that correlations over a long rapidity range are induced by sound mode propagation even in the limit of zero viscosity, with the magnitude of fluctuations directly proportional to the shear and bulk viscosities via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Method

The authors generalize the non-relativistic theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations to the relativistic domain by adding stochastic noise terms to the energy-momentum tensor and baryon current, with correlation functions fixed by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. They then apply these stochastic equations to the Bjorken boost-invariant flow in (1+1) dimensions, integrating over transverse coordinates, and solve the linearized hydrodynamic equations for fluctuations in entropy density and longitudinal flow velocity using Fourier transforms in spatial rapidity. The two-particle correlators are evaluated by integrating the Green functions over proper time from an initial thermalization time to freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature from lattice QCD is given as T_c approximately 165 MeV, citing Aoki (2006) and Borsanyi (2010).
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from RHIC data is quoted as 4 pi eta/s less than 2.5, citing Song (2010).
- The entropy density evolves as s(tau) = s(tau_0) tau_0 / tau for ideal fluid flow.
- For a constant speed of sound squared and constant nu = (4 eta/3 + zeta)/s, the temperature solution is T(tau) = [T_0 + v_s^2 gamma_s^2 nu / tau_0] (tau_0/tau)^{v_s^2} - v_s^2 gamma_s^2 nu / tau, where gamma_s = 1/sqrt(1 - v_s^2).
- The noise correlation in rapidity space is given by <f(xi_1, tau_1) f(xi_2, tau_2)> = [2 T(tau_1) / (A tau_1 w^2(tau_1))] [4 eta(tau_1)/3 + zeta(tau_1)] delta(tau_1 - tau_2) delta(xi_1 - xi_2), where A is the effective transverse area.

## Context

This work extends the textbook theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations from Landau and Lifshitz (Statistical Physics, Part 2) to the relativistic domain and applies it to the Bjorken boost-invariant flow model from Bjorken (1983). It contrasts with the extensive literature on initial state fluctuations studied by Shuryak (2009), Staig (2010-2011), and Schenke (2010-2011) by focusing instead on thermal hydrodynamic fluctuations as a distinct source of correlations. The framework enables future numerical solutions of stochastic hydrodynamic equations in three spatial dimensions to extract shear and bulk viscosities from experimental correlation data at RHIC and LHC.
