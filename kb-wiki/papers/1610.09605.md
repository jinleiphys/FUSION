# Puzzle of the $Λ_c$ spectrum

> There is a puzzle in the $Λ_c^+$ family, i.e., one member with $J^P=3/2^+$ is missing in a $L=2$ multiplet which the heavy quark effective theory predicts, and $J^P$'s of $Λ_c(2765)^+$ and $Λ_c(2940)^+$ are unknown. Using a light diquark picture to calculate baryon masses, we study possible assignments of two $Λ_c$'s with unknown $J^P$ and the missing $Λ_c^+$ with $3/2^+$ for $L=2$, and we find the most probable possibility that the peak corresponding to $Λ_c(2880)^+$ actually includes a missing member with spin $3/2^+$ for $L=2$ and that quantum numbers of $Λ_c(2765)^+$ and $Λ_c(2940)^+$ are $2S(1/2^+)$ and $2P(1/2^-)$, respectively.

## Key claim

The missing $J^P = 3/2^+$ member of the $L=2$ heavy-quark spin multiplet in the $\Lambda_c^+$ family is most probably hidden within the experimentally observed peak of $\Lambda_c(2880)^+$, whose mass and width are too narrow to resolve the two states separately; the quantum numbers of $\Lambda_c(2765)^+$ and $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ are assigned as $2S(1/2^+)$ and $2P(1/2^-)$, respectively.

## Method

The authors adopt a heavy quark-light diquark picture, first calculating diquark masses using the relativized Godfrey-Isgur (GI) quark model with a parameter set (CI) adapted from Capstick and Isgur (1986). They then treat the baryon as a heavy quark bound to a light scalar or axial-vector diquark and compute $\Lambda_c^+$ masses by applying the same relativized Hamiltonian to the heavy quark-diquark system. The resulting masses are compared with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (PDG) and with predictions from other models (Ebert et al. 2011, Chen et al. 2016, Capstick and Isgur 1986, Roberts and Pervin 2007).

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c(2286)^+$ mass: 2286.86 MeV (PDG); predicted 2267 MeV (GI) and 2177 MeV (CI).
- $\Lambda_c(2765)^+$ mass: 2766.6 MeV (PDG); predicted as $2S(1/2^+)$ at 2749 MeV (CI) and 2891 MeV (GI).
- $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ mass: 2939.3 MeV (PDG); predicted as $2P(1/2^-)$ at 3030 MeV (CI) and 3204 MeV (GI).
- $\Lambda_c(2880)^+$ mass: 2881.53 MeV (PDG); predicted $1D(5/2^+)$ at 2919 MeV (CI) and $1D(3/2^+)$ at 2930 MeV (CI), with mass difference within about 10 MeV.
- $\Lambda_c(2880)^+$ width: $5.8 \pm 1.1$ MeV (PDG).
- Diquark masses (CI): scalar $[u,d]$ = 642 MeV; axial-vector $\{u,d\}$ = 779 MeV; scalar $[u,s]$ = 838 MeV; axial-vector $\{u,s\}$ = 934 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Matsuki et al. (2016) that heavy-light mesons with the same orbital angular momentum $L$ are nearly degenerate, and applies the same idea to $\Lambda_c^+$ baryons. It resolves a puzzle in the $\Lambda_c^+$ spectrum where a $J^P = 3/2^+$ state predicted by heavy quark effective theory for $L=2$ had not been identified. The proposed solution, that the missing state is unresolved in the $\Lambda_c(2880)^+$ peak, was also suggested by Chen et al. (2016) using a semi-relativistic quark potential model. The results call for higher-resolution measurements at LHCb and Belle II to test the prediction.
