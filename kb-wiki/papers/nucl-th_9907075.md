# Dynamics and Sizes of the Fireball at Freeze-out

> Analyzing the m_t-spectrum and two-particle correlations of negative hadrons from 158 AGeV/c Pb+Pb collisions at slightly forward rapidities we find a (thermal) freeze-out temperature of about 100 MeV and transverse flow with <v_t> approximately 0.55c. The M_t-dependence of the correlation radii prefers a box-like transverse density profile over a Gaussian. From an analysis of the pion phase-space density we find pion chemical potential of approximately 60 MeV at thermal freeze-out.

## Key claim

The combined analysis of single-particle spectra and two-particle correlations from 158 AGeV/c Pb+Pb collisions resolves the ambiguity between temperature and transverse flow, yielding a thermal freeze-out temperature of about 100 MeV and strong transverse flow with average velocity approximately 0.55c, but only under the assumption that the transverse density profile is box-like rather than Gaussian.

## Method

The authors parameterized the fireball at freeze-out using an emission function assuming thermal equilibrium at temperature T, boost-invariant longitudinal flow, and a tunable transverse expansion profile with flow rapidity proportional to radius. They fitted this model simultaneously to the single-particle m_t-spectrum and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation radii of negative hadrons (h^-) from central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV/c measured by the NA49 collaboration, testing both box-like and Gaussian transverse density profiles. The fit used chi-squared minimization to extract temperature, transverse flow, and source size parameters, with resonance contributions from negative kaons and antiprotons included in the spectrum calculation.

## Key numbers

- Thermal freeze-out temperature: T ≈ 80–110 MeV from the box-model fit
- Average transverse flow velocity: ⟨v_t⟩ ≈ 0.47c–0.62c from the box-model fit
- Pion chemical potential at thermal freeze-out: μ_π ≈ 60 MeV at T ≈ 100 MeV
- Box-model transverse density radius: R_B = 12.1 fm, corresponding to r_rms = 8.6 fm
- Longitudinal density width: Δη = 1.3, fixed from dN/dy width
- Baryon chemical potential used in multiplicity calculation: μ_B ≈ 360 MeV, corresponding to entropy per baryon S/A = 38

## Context

This work extends the method of combining single-particle spectra and two-particle correlations, previously developed by Chapman et al. (1996), Csorgo et al. (1995), and Csernai et al. (1995), to resolve the temperature-flow ambiguity in heavy-ion collisions. The finding that the fireball is out of chemical equilibrium at thermal freeze-out, with pions having a chemical potential of about 60 MeV, supports the picture that chemical freeze-out occurs near the confinement phase transition at T_cr ≈ 170 MeV, as discussed by Heinz (1999) and others. The result that a box-like transverse density profile fits the M_t-dependence of correlation radii better than a Gaussian provides constraints for hydrodynamic models of fireball expansion and enables more reliable back-extrapolation to earlier stages to investigate quark-gluon plasma formation.
