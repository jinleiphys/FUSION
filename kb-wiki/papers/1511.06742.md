# Jet Hadronization via Recombination of Parton Showers in Vacuum and in Medium

> We have studied the hadronization of jet parton showers based on the quark recombination model. This is achieved by letting gluons at the end of the perturbative shower evolution undergo a non-perturbative splitting into quark and antiquark pairs, then applying a Monte-Carlo version of instantaneous quark recombination, and finally subjecting remnant quarks (those which have not found a recombination partner) to Lund string fragmentation. When applied to parton showers from the PYTHIA Monte Carlo generator, the final hadron spectra from our calculation compare quite well to PYTHIA jets that have been hadronized with the default Lund string fragmentation. Modeling the quark gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions by a blast wave model, we have further studied medium effects on the hadronization of jet shower partons by also including their recombination with the thermal partons from the quark gluon plasma. We find that the latter leads to a significant enhancement of intermediate transverse momentum pions and protons at both RHIC and LHC. Our results thus suggest that medium modification of jet fragmentation provides a plausible explanation for the enhanced production of intermediate transverse momentum hadrons observed in experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a hybrid hadronization model combining quark recombination with Lund string fragmentation can reproduce vacuum jet fragmentation from PYTHIA, while also providing a plausible explanation for the enhanced production of intermediate transverse momentum hadrons in heavy ion collisions through shower-thermal parton recombination. The hedge is that this explanation is suggested as plausible rather than proven, and the in-medium results rely on a blast wave model rather than full dynamical simulations.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte-Carlo hadronization module that first forces gluons at the end of perturbative shower evolution (virtuality Q0 around 1 GeV) to split non-perturbatively into quark-antiquark pairs. They then apply instantaneous quark recombination using harmonic oscillator Wigner functions for mesons and baryons, with width parameters fixed by measured charge radii of pions, kaons, and nucleons. Remnant quarks that do not find recombination partners are hadronized via Lund string fragmentation using PYTHIA 6 routines. For medium studies, thermal partons from a blast wave model of the quark gluon plasma are included in the recombination process alongside shower partons.

## Key numbers

* 100 GeV PYTHIA quark showers were used for vacuum tests of the hadronization module
* The distribution of quark-antiquark pairs in 100 GeV showers peaks at approximately Δr ≈ 0.5 fm and Δk ≈ 0.4 GeV in their common rest frame
* The hadronization cutoff scale for perturbative evolution is Q0 of order 1 GeV
* Intermediate momentum range for recombination effects in heavy ion collisions is approximately 2-6 GeV/c
* Enhancement of intermediate transverse momentum pions and protons was found at both RHIC and LHC energies

## Context

This work extends the instantaneous quark recombination model previously applied to thermal partons and leading jet partons by Greco (2003), Fries (2003, 2004, 2008), and others, now incorporating full parton showers from PYTHIA (Sjostrand 2006). The hybrid approach combining recombination with string fragmentation for remnant quarks is new, and the idea that quarks in jet showers could hadronize by recombination was earlier discussed by Hwa (2003). The work enables future combination with realistic in-medium jet shower Monte Carlo simulations being developed within the JET collaboration to study jet quenching and medium modification of jet fragmentation in heavy ion collisions.
