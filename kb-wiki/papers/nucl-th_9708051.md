# A unitary model for meson-nucleon scattering

> In an effective Lagrangian model employing the K-matrix approximation we extract nucleon resonance parameters. To this end we analyze simultaneously all available data for reactions involving the final states $πN$, $ππN$, $ηN$ and $K Λ$ in the energy range $m_N + m_π \le \sqrt s \le 1.9$ GeV. The background contributions are generated consistently from the relevant Feynman amplitudes, thus significantly reducing the number of free parameters.

## Key claim
The authors extract nucleon resonance parameters using a K-matrix approximation with effective Lagrangians, but they note that the extracted parameters depend strongly on the choice of πN partial wave analysis and the functional form of the form factors used, with the S11(1535) parameters varying drastically depending on these choices.

## Method
The authors constructed a unitary coupled-channel model using the K-matrix approximation, with the interaction potential V built from effective Lagrangians describing couplings between nucleons, mesons (π, η, K, ζ), and nucleon resonances with spin 1/2 and 3/2. They simultaneously fitted data for πN → πN, πN → ππN, πN → ηN, and πN → KΛ in the energy range from threshold to √s = 1.9 GeV, using three different form factor parameterizations and two different πN partial wave analyses (KA84 and SM95) to test systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Energy range covered: m_N + m_π ≤ √s ≤ 1.9 GeV
- For the S11(1535) resonance, literature values for mass range from 1.526 to 1.553 GeV, with partial widths Γ_π = 20-84 MeV and Γ_η = 54-91 MeV
- The πN scattering length and effective range were extracted from the S11 phase shift near threshold using the formula |q|(1/S_11 + i) ≈ 1/a_I + ½ r_I |q|²
- Form factor cutoffs were varied independently for nucleon (Λ_N), spin-1/2 resonances (Λ_1/2), spin-3/2 resonances (Λ_3/2), and t-channel exchanges (Λ_t)
- Partial waves were calculated up to l_max = 5 to ensure convergence of the partial wave expansion

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel approach of Manley and Saleski (1992) and the CMB group (Cutkosky et al., 1979) by generating background contributions consistently from Feynman amplitudes rather than parameterizing them independently, significantly reducing free parameters. The model is intended as a first step toward a unified description of hadronic and electromagnetic reactions using the same Lagrangians, following earlier work by Sauermann et al. (1996) and Batinic et al. (1997). The results enable future extraction of hadronic couplings for photoproduction reactions, where the product of hadronic and electromagnetic couplings must be disentangled.
