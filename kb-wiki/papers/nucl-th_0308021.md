# The Gamow-Teller Resonance in Finite Nuclei in the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation

> Gamow-Teller(GT) resonances in finite nuclei are studied in a fully consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) framework. A relativistic form of the Landau-Migdal contact interaction in the spin-isospin channel is adopted. This choice ensures that the GT excitation energy in nuclear matter is correctly reproduced in the non-relativistic limit. The GT response functions of doubly magic nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb are calculated using the parameter set NL3 and $g_0'$=0.6 . It is found that effects related to Dirac sea states account for a reduction of 6-7 % in the GT sum rule.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) framework, using a relativistic form of the Landau-Migdal contact interaction with parameter set NL3 and \(g_0' = 0.6\), can reproduce Gamow-Teller (GT) resonance energies in finite nuclei, but only about 93% of the Ikeda sum rule is exhausted up to 60 MeV; the missing strength is attributed to Dirac sea state contributions, which account for a reduction of 6–7% in the sum rule.

## Method

The authors built a relativistic RPA on a relativistic mean field (RMF) ground state, using the NL3 parameter set. They introduced a pseudo-vector pion term and a vector \(\rho\) meson term in the Lagrangian, along with a relativistic Landau-Migdal contact interaction in the spin-isospin channel with \(g' = 0.6\). The GT response functions for the doubly magic nuclei \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{90}\)Zr, and \(^{208}\)Pb were calculated by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in momentum space, including configurations from occupied Fermi states, unoccupied positive-energy states, and negative-energy Dirac sea states.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{48}\)Ca: the RPA centroid energy is 10.1 MeV, exhausting 93.8% of the Ikeda sum rule up to 60 MeV; the experimental energy is 10.5 MeV.
- For \(^{90}\)Zr: the RPA centroid energy is 15.4 MeV, exhausting 93.2% of the Ikeda sum rule up to 60 MeV; the experimental energy is 15.6 ± 0.3 MeV.
- For \(^{208}\)Pb: the RPA centroid energy is 18.9 MeV, exhausting 92.6% of the Ikeda sum rule up to 60 MeV; the experimental energy is 19.2 ± 0.2 MeV.
- Effects related to Dirac sea states account for a reduction of 6–7% in the GT sum rule.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic RPA approach, previously applied mainly to electric excitations, to spin-isospin GT resonances in finite nuclei. It builds on the relativistic description of GT resonances in nuclear matter by Kurasawa, Suzuki, and Giai (2003), which pointed out a new quenching mechanism due to relativistic effects. The results enable further study of the missing GT strength in finite nuclei, complementing earlier non-relativistic investigations of 2p-2h admixtures by Bertsch (1982) and \(\Delta\)-hole coupling by Oset (1979).
