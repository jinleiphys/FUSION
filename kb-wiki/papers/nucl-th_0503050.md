# Light Nuclei within Nuclear Matter

> We investigate the properties of 3He, 4He, 6He, 7Li and 16O nuclei in nuclear matter of finite temperature and density. A Dyson expansion of the many-body Green function leads to few-body equations that are solved using the ntegro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) and the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) methods. The use of the latter method allows us to trace the individual movement of the wave packet for each nucleon and the formation and disintegration of quasi-nuclei in a changing thermodynamical nuclear matter environment.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the Mott transition (the density at which a cluster's binding energy vanishes due to medium effects) for light nuclei up to ¹⁶O, extending previous work on deuterons and three- and four-nucleon systems. The authors note that their calculations use a simplified Volkov interaction and that the extreme adiabatic approximation (eaa) overbinds isolated ¹⁶O, so the quantitative results are indicative rather than definitive.

## Method

The authors start from a Dyson expansion of the many-body Green function to derive few-body equations for clusters embedded in nuclear matter at finite temperature (T=10 MeV). They transform the resulting energy-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction into an energy-independent one using the Marchenko inverse scattering method. They then solve the few-body equations using two configuration-space methods: the Integro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) for ³He, ⁴He, and ¹⁶O, and the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) method for ⁶He and ⁷Li, which allows tracking individual nucleon wave packets.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies in the extreme adiabatic approximation (eaa) for ³He: 8.67 MeV at ρ=0 fm⁻³, 3.84 MeV at ρ=0.003 fm⁻³, 0.75 MeV at ρ=0.007 fm⁻³, and no bound state at ρ=0.009 fm⁻³.
- Binding energies in the eaa for ⁴He: 30.7 MeV at ρ=0 fm⁻³, 18.1 MeV at ρ=0.003 fm⁻³, 8.56 MeV at ρ=0.007 fm⁻³, 5.70 MeV at ρ=0.009 fm⁻³, and no bound state at ρ=0.017 fm⁻³.
- Binding energies in the eaa for ¹⁶O: 1630 MeV at ρ=0 fm⁻³, 1280 MeV at ρ=0.003 fm⁻³, 979.6 MeV at ρ=0.007 fm⁻³, 874.8 MeV at ρ=0.009 fm⁻³, 628.0 MeV at ρ=0.017 fm⁻³, and 507.8 MeV at ρ=0.034 fm⁻³.
- The temperature is fixed at T=10 MeV throughout the calculations.
- For ⁴He at ρ=0.009 fm⁻³ (just below the Mott density), the Faddeev component maximum shifts to approximately 5 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations of the deuteron Mott transition by Kuhrts et al. (2000) and earlier studies of ³He/³H by Schadow and the α-particle by Sofianos and Beyer (2003), which were based on the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations. The combination of the Marchenko inverse scattering method with IDEA and AMD enables the study of clusters up to A=16 in a medium, which was previously limited to A≤4. The results are relevant for understanding multi-fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions and for neutron star physics, and the authors note that more realistic forces including Bartlett, Heisenberg, and LS components are needed for a complete picture.
