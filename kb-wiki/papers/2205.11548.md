# Fully-charm and -bottom pentaquarks in a Lattice-QCD inspired quark model

> The fully-charm and -bottom pentaquarks, \emph{i.e.} $cccc\bar{c}$ and $bbbb\bar{b}$, with spin-parity quantum numbers $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$, $\frac{3}{2}^-$ and $\frac{5}{2}^-$, are investigated within a Lattice-QCD inspired quark model, which has already successfully described the recently announced fully-charm tetraquark candidate $X(6900)$, and has also predicted several other fully-heavy tetraquarks. A powerful computational technique, based on the Gaussian expansion method combined with a complex-scaling range approach, is employed to predict, and distinguish, bound, resonance and scattering states of the mentioned five-body system. Both baryon-meson and diquark-diquark-antiquark configurations, along with all of their possible color channels are comprehensively considered. Narrow resonances are obtained in each spin-parity channel for the fully-charm and -bottom systems. Moreover, most of them seems to be compact multiquarks whose wave-functions are dominated by either hidden-color baryon-meson or diquark-diquark-antiquark structure, or by the coupling between them.

## Key claim

Narrow resonances are predicted in each spin-parity channel for both fully-charm and fully-bottom pentaquarks, but no bound states are found; the resonances are compact multiquarks whose wavefunctions are dominated by hidden-color baryon-meson or diquark-diquark-antiquark structures, or the coupling between them.

## Method

The authors employed a non-relativistic quark model with a two-body interaction based on a Lattice-QCD inspired potential (Cornell potential plus spin-spin term) to study the five-body systems \(cccc\bar{c}\) and \(bbbb\bar{b}\). The Gaussian expansion method combined with the complex scaling method was used to solve the Schrodinger equation, allowing simultaneous identification of bound, resonance, and scattering states. Both baryon-meson and diquark-diquark-antiquark configurations, along with all possible color channels, were comprehensively considered in coupled-channel calculations.

## Key numbers

* Fully-charm pentaquark with \(J^P = 1/2^-\): resonance pole at \((8098 + i0.16)\) MeV, with quark-quark distance \(r_{cc} = 0.53\) fm and quark-antiquark distance \(r_{c\bar{c}} = 0.56\) fm; 100% diquark-diquark-antiquark component.
* Fully-charm pentaquark with \(J^P = 1/2^-\): resonance pole at \((8634 + i0.38)\) MeV, with \(r_{cc} = 0.75\) fm and \(r_{c\bar{c}} = 0.64\) fm; 100% diquark-diquark-antiquark component.
* Fully-charm pentaquark with \(J^P = 1/2^-\): resonance pole at \((8742 + i0.86)\) MeV, with \(r_{cc} = 0.76\) fm and \(r_{c\bar{c}} = 0.71\) fm; 46% color-singlet baryon-meson, 52.7% hidden-color baryon-meson, 1.3% diquark-diquark-antiquark.
* Fully-charm pentaquark with \(J^P = 3/2^-\): lowest mass in real-range calculation is 7765 MeV, corresponding to the \(\Omega_{ccc}\eta_c\) threshold; no bound state found.
* Model parameters: charm quark mass \(M_c = 1.290\) GeV, bottom quark mass \(M_b = 4.700\) GeV; Coulomb strength \(\alpha = 0.4105\); confinement strength \(\sigma = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\); spin-spin parameters \(\gamma = 1.982\) GeV, \(\beta_{cc} = 2.06\) GeV, \(\beta_{bb} = 0.318\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of fully-heavy tetraquarks (Yang et al., 2021) using the same Lattice-QCD inspired quark model, which had successfully described the fully-charm tetraquark candidate \(X(6900)\). The study is motivated by experimental observations of fully-heavy tetraquarks by LHCb (2020) and theoretical predictions for fully-charm pentaquark masses around 7.9 GeV from quark models (Yan et al., 2021; An et al., 2020, 2022) and QCD sum rules (Wang et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2020). The results provide specific resonance energies and widths that can guide experimental searches for fully-heavy pentaquarks at facilities such as LHC.
