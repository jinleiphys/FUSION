# Residual Mean Field Model of Valence Quarks in the Nucleon

> We develop a non-perturbative model for valence parton distribution functions (PDFs) based on the mean field interactions of valence quarks in the nucleonic interior. The main motivation for the model is to obtain a mean field description of the valence quarks as a baseline to study the short range quark-quark interactions that generate the high $x$ tail of PDFs. The model is based on the separation of the valence three-quark cluster and residual system in the nucleon. Then the nucleon structure function is calculated within the effective light-front diagrammatic approach introducing nonperturbative light-front valence quark and residual wave functions. Within the model a new relation is obtained between the position, $x_p$, of the peak of $xq_V(x)$ distribution of the valence quark and the effective mass of the residual system, $m_R$, in the form: $x_{p} \approx {1\over 4} (1-{m_R\over m_N})$ at starting $Q^2$. This relation explains the difference in the peak positions for d- and u- quarks through the expected difference of residual masses for valence d- and u- quark distributions. The parameters of the model are fixed by fitting the calculated valence quark distributions to the phenomenological PDFs. This allowed us to estimate the overall mean field contribution in baryonic and momentum sum rules for valence d- and u- quarks. Finally, the evaluated parameters of the non-perturbative wave functions of valence 3q-cluster and residual system can be used in calculation of other quantities such as nucleon form factors, generalized partonic and transverse momentum distributions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a non-perturbative mean-field model for valence quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) that treats the three valence quarks as a bound cluster interacting with a residual nucleon system, and derives a new relation \(x_{p} \approx \frac{1}{4} (1 - \frac{m_R}{m_N})\) linking the peak position of the \(x q_V(x)\) distribution to the effective mass of the residual system, thereby explaining the difference in peak positions for \(u\)- and \(d\)-quarks through flavor-dependent residual masses.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon as a compact three-valence-quark cluster embedded in a residual system of sea quarks, gluons, and pions, using light-front perturbation theory with effective nonperturbative wave functions. The mean-field interaction among valence quarks is described by coupled relativistic three-dimensional harmonic oscillators, and the scattering amplitude is calculated via effective light-front diagrammatic rules. The model parameters, including the effective residual masses \(m_R(u)\) and \(m_R(d)\), are fixed by fitting the calculated valence quark distributions to phenomenological PDFs at a starting scale \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- The relation \(x_{p} \approx \frac{1}{4} (1 - \frac{m_R}{m_N})\) connects the peak position \(x_p\) of the \(x q_V(x)\) distribution to the effective residual mass \(m_R\) and nucleon mass \(m_N\).
- The average transverse momentum of valence quarks is estimated as \(\langle k_t\rangle \sim 200\) MeV/c, leading to a mean-field Bjorken \(x\) range of \(x \lesssim 0.4\).
- The transverse separation between any pair of valence quarks in the 3q cluster is assumed to be \(b_{qq} \lesssim 0.3\) Fm, corresponding to a 3q system size of up to 0.6 Fm.
- The starting scale for the mean-field calculation is set at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\), requiring the produced final mass in deep inelastic scattering to be \(\ge 2\) GeV.
- The ratio \(d_V/u_V\) at the peak position is approximately 0.5 and is largely \(Q^2\) independent across multiple PDF sets (CJ15, CT14, MMHT2014, etc.).

## Context

This work extends the multi-nucleon correlation framework from nuclear physics (Frankfurt 1988, Egiyan 2005, Arrington 2011) to the valence quark structure of the nucleon, treating the three-quark cluster analogously to bound fermion systems in nuclei and ultracold atomic Fermi gases (Tan 2008, Hen 2014). The model provides a baseline mean-field description that enables future inclusion of two- and three-quark short-range correlations to generate the high-\(x\) tail of PDFs, and the extracted wave function parameters can be used to calculate nucleon form factors, generalized parton distributions, and transverse momentum distributions.
