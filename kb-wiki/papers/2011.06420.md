# Higher order conserved charge fluctuations inside the mixed phase

> General formulas are presented for higher order cumulants of the conserved charge statistical fluctuations inside the mixed phase. As a particular example the van der Waals model in the grand canonical ensemble is used. The higher order measures of the conserved charge fluctuations up to the hyperkurtosis are calculated in a vicinity of the critical point (CP). The analysis includes both the mixed phase region and the pure phases on the phase diagram. It is shown that even-order fluctuation measures, e.g. scaled variance, kurtosis, and hyperkurtosis, have only positive values in the mixed phase, and go to infinity at the CP. For odd-order measures, such as skewness and hyperskewness, the regions of positive and negative values are found near the left and right binodals, respectively. The obtained results are discussed in a context of the event-by-event fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collisions.

# Higher order conserved charge fluctuations inside the mixed phase

## Key claim

The paper presents general formulas for higher-order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations inside the mixed phase of a first-order phase transition, showing that even-order fluctuation measures (scaled variance, kurtosis, hyperkurtosis) are only positive in the mixed phase and diverge at the critical point, while odd-order measures (skewness, hyperskewness) can take either sign depending on proximity to the left (gaseous) or right (liquid) binodal. The results are derived in the grand canonical ensemble and apply to any equation of state with a first-order phase transition, not only the van der Waals model used for illustration.

## Method

The authors derived model-independent expressions for conserved charge cumulants inside the mixed phase by partitioning the total system volume into volumes occupied by the two coexisting phases, assuming independent fluctuations of densities and volume fractions in the thermodynamic limit. They applied these general formulas to the van der Waals fluid equation of state, calculating cumulant ratios up to sixth order (hyperkurtosis) both inside and outside the mixed phase region, using iterative differentiation of the van der Waals density equation with respect to chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical point parameters for the van der Waals model: reduced temperature T_c = 8a/(27b), reduced density n_c = 1/(3b), reduced pressure p_c = a/(27b^2)
- Scaled variance omega approaches +infinity at the critical point, both inside and outside the mixed phase
- Kurtosis kappa*sigma^2 is only positive inside the mixed phase, tending to +infinity when approaching the critical point from inside the mixed phase
- Negative values of kurtosis appear only at supercritical temperatures T > T_c, not inside the mixed phase at T < T_c
- The second cumulant of volume fraction x fluctuations is given by kappa_{2,x} = (1/V^2) * (kappa_{2,1} * kappa_{2,2}) / (n_2^2 * kappa_{2,1} + n_1^2 * kappa_{2,2})
- x-fluctuation effects become notable for fifth and sixth order cumulants near the right binodal where x_0 is less than approximately 1/2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of conserved charge fluctuations in pure phases (Vovchenko 2015, Stephanov 2011) by providing a general formalism for fluctuations inside the mixed phase region, which had received less attention. The results are relevant for interpreting event-by-event fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the HADES experiment at GSI which plans to measure higher-order net-proton and net-charge fluctuations in Au+Au reactions at E_lab = 0.2A to 1.0A GeV to probe the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition region. The formalism enables future work on global charge conservation effects in the mixed phase using the subensemble acceptance method (Vovchenko 2020, Poberezhnyuk 2020) and on strongly intensive fluctuation measures designed to cancel volume fluctuation effects.
