# Matching the Quasi Parton Distribution in a Momentum Subtraction Scheme

> The quasi parton distribution is a spatial correlation of quarks or gluons along the $z$ direction in a moving nucleon which enables direct lattice calculations of parton distribution functions. It can be defined with a nonperturbative renormalization in a regularization independent momentum subtraction scheme (RI/MOM), which can then be perturbatively related to the collinear parton distribution in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme. Here we carry out a direct matching from the RI/MOM scheme for the quasi-PDF to the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ PDF, determining the non-singlet quark matching coefficient at next-to-leading order in perturbation theory. We find that the RI/MOM matching coefficient is insensitive to the ultraviolet region of convolution integral, exhibits improved perturbative convergence when converting between the quasi-PDF and PDF, and is consistent with a quasi-PDF that vanishes in the unphysical region as the proton momentum $P^z\to \infty$, unlike other schemes. This direct approach therefore has the potential to improve the accuracy for converting quasi-distribution lattice calculations to collinear distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the RI/MOM scheme matching coefficient for quasi-PDFs to $\overline{\text{MS}}$ PDFs is insensitive to the ultraviolet region of the convolution integral, exhibits improved perturbative convergence, and is consistent with a quasi-PDF that vanishes in the unphysical region as the proton momentum $P^z \to \infty$, unlike other schemes.

## Method

The authors carry out a direct perturbative matching calculation at next-to-leading order in QCD, computing the non-singlet quark matching coefficient that converts the RI/MOM scheme quasi-PDF to the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ PDF. They use dimensional regularization with off-shell quark states as an infrared regulator, evaluate the one-loop Feynman diagrams for the quasi-PDF (vertex, sail, tadpole, and wavefunction renormalization contributions), and impose the RI/MOM renormalization condition to determine the matching coefficient $C^{\text{OM}}$.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop matching coefficient $C^{\text{OM}}$ is computed at next-to-leading order in $\alpha_s$ for the non-singlet quark case.
- The RI/MOM renormalization condition is imposed at $p^2 = -\mu_R^2$ with $p^z = p_R^z$, where $\mu_R$ is the renormalization scale and $p_R^z$ is the momentum component used in the subtraction.
- The matching coefficient depends on the dimensionless ratio $r_R \equiv \mu_R^2 / p_R^{z\,2}$, with the Euclidean condition $\mu_R^2 \ge p_R^{z\,2}$ on the lattice.
- The result is independent of the choice of infrared regulator, verified by repeating the calculation with dimensional regularization as the IR regulator.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF approach proposed by Ji (2013) by providing a direct matching from the RI/MOM scheme to the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme, improving upon earlier matching calculations in a transverse momentum cutoff scheme by Xiong et al. (2013). The RI/MOM scheme has been used in lattice calculations reported by Alexandrou et al. (2017), Chen et al. (2017), Green et al. (2017), and Lin et al. (2017). This direct matching enables a single-step conversion from lattice quasi-PDF results to collinear PDFs, with the potential for improved accuracy compared to approaches that first convert to the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme perturbatively.
