# Unusual Zeeman-field effects in two-dimensional spin-orbit-coupled Fermi superfluids

> We investigate the Zeeman field effects on the bulk superfluid properties and the collective modes in two-dimensional (2D) attractive atomic Fermi gases with Rashba-type spin-orbit coupling. In the presence of a large spin-orbit coupling, the system undergoes a quantum phase transition to a topological superfluid state at a critical Zeeman field. We show that the nonanalyticities of the thermodynamic functions as well as other physical quantities at the quantum phase transition originate from the infrared singularities caused by the gapless fermionic spectrum. The same argument applies also to the BCS-BEC evolution in 2D fermionic superfluids with $p$- or d-wave pairing. The superfluid density $n_s$ and the velocity of the Goldstone sound mode $c_s$ behave oppositely in the normal and the topological superfluid phases: they are suppressed by the Zeeman field in the normal superfluid phase, but get enhanced in the topological superfluid phase. The velocity of the Goldstone sound mode also shows nonanalyticity at the quantum phase transition. For large Zeeman field, we find $n_s\rightarrow n$ and $c_s\rightarrow \upsilon_{\rm F}$, where $n$ is the total fermion density and $\upsilon_{\rm F}$ is the Fermi velocity of noninteracting system. The unusual behavior of the superfluid density and the collective modes can be understood by the fact that the spin-orbit-coupled superfluid state at large Zeeman field can be mapped to the $p_x+ip_y$ superfluid state of spinless fermions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a two-dimensional spin-orbit-coupled Fermi superfluid, the superfluid density and Goldstone sound velocity are suppressed by the Zeeman field in the normal superfluid phase but enhanced in the topological superfluid phase, with the quantum phase transition between these phases being of third order rather than first order.

## Method

The authors use a mean-field (saddle-point) approximation in the imaginary-time functional integral formalism to compute the thermodynamic potential and gap equation for a 2D attractive Fermi gas with Rashba-type spin-orbit coupling and a Zeeman field. They analyze the infrared singularities of momentum integrals to determine the order of the quantum phase transition, and expand the Gaussian fluctuation action to study the superfluid density and collective mode dispersions. The system is characterized by the dimensionless parameters ln(k_F a_2D) (attractive strength) and λ/k_F (spin-orbit coupling strength), with numerical results presented for ln(k_F a_2D)=2 and λ/k_F=0.5.

## Key numbers

- The quantum phase transition occurs at critical Zeeman field h_c = (μ² + Δ²)^(1/2) ≃ 0.51 ε_F, where ε_F = πn is the Fermi energy
- The Clogston-Chandrasekhar limit for the BCS state is h_CC = 0.707 Δ_0, where Δ_0 is the pairing gap at h=0
- The FFLO state survives between h_CC and h_FFLO = 0.754 Δ_0
- For large Zeeman field (h → ∞), the superfluid density n_s → n (total fermion density) and the sound velocity c_s → υ_F (Fermi velocity of noninteracting system)
- The lower-band Fermi wavevector at large h is k̃_F ≃ √2 k_F, giving n ≃ k̃_F²/(4π)
- The Fermi velocity at large h is υ̃_F ≃ √2 υ_F (1 - λ²/η_F) where η_F = (λ²k̃_F² + h²)^(1/2)

## Context

This work extends studies of Zeeman field effects on BCS superconductivity (Clogston 1962, Chandrasekhar 1962) and the FFLO inhomogeneous state (Fulde and Ferrell 1964, Larkin and Ovchinnikov 1965) to two-dimensional systems with Rashba spin-orbit coupling. The results connect to topological superconductivity (Sato and Fujimoto 2009, Sau and Das Sarma 2010) and show that the spin-orbit-coupled superfluid at large Zeeman field maps to a p_x+ip_y superfluid of spinless fermions. The findings enable experimental detection of the quantum phase transition through density profile analysis in trapped systems using local-density approximation, and suggest that similar nonanalyticities should appear in 2D BCS-BEC evolution with p- and d-wave pairings.
