# Thermal Flavor Production and Signatures of Deconfinement

> Using renormalization group methods we evaluate the thermal strangeness and charm chemical equilibrium relaxation times in the deconfined quark-gluon plasma. We present a reaction model and evaluate the total production rate of strangeness in fixed target Pb-Pb collisions at 10--300 A GeV. We discuss the relevance of our results to the diagnosis and understanding of the properties of the deconfined state.

## Key claim

Using renormalization group methods to incorporate running QCD coupling and running quark masses, the authors evaluate thermal strangeness and charm chemical equilibrium relaxation times in deconfined quark-gluon plasma, finding that strangeness approaches chemical equilibrium within the QGP lifetime at SPS energies while charm does not, but that charm equilibrium may become accessible at RHIC/LHC temperatures around 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute thermal flavor production rates by integrating angle-averaged two-particle cross sections for gluon-gluon and quark-antiquark fusion into strange or charm quark pairs, using running QCD coupling constant α_s(μ) and running quark masses m_s(μ), m_c(μ) obtained from numerical integration of renormalization group equations at three-loop order in the MS scheme. They then derive strangeness and charm relaxation times τ_s and τ_c as functions of temperature, and integrate a rate equation within an entropy-conserving fireball model with radial expansion to compute the phase-space occupancy γ_s and total strangeness yield per baryon N_s/B for S-W and Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies.

## Key numbers

* Strangeness relaxation time τ_s in QGP at T ≈ 250 MeV: approximately 10⁻²³ s (order of 1 fm/c), confirming near chemical equilibrium at SPS energies.
* Charm relaxation time τ_c in QGP at T ≈ 200 MeV: approximately 100 times longer than τ_s; at T ≈ 500 MeV, τ_c → 30 fm/c, within factor two of expected QGP lifetime.
* Running strange quark mass: m_s(1 GeV) ≈ 200 ± 40 MeV for input m_s(M_Z) = 90 ± 18 MeV and α_s(M_Z) = 0.118.
* Running charm quark mass: m_c(1 GeV) ≈ 1550 ± 110 MeV for input m_c(M_Z) = 700 ± 50 MeV.
* Threshold for strange pair production: 2m_s^th = 611 MeV for α_s(M_Z) = 0.118; 2m_s^th = 566 MeV for α_s(M_Z) = 0.115.
* Threshold for charm pair production: 2m_c^th ≈ 2.3 GeV (reduced from ~3.1 GeV expected from m_c(1 GeV)).
* For Pb-Pb at 158 A GeV: γ_s = 0.69 ± 0.15 (η=50% stopping) and 0.76 ± 0.16 (η=75%); N_s/B = 0.954 ± 0.20 (η=50%) and 0.950 ± 0.20 (η=75%).
* For S-W at 200 A GeV: γ_s = 0.53 ± 0.14 (η=35%) and 0.65 ± 0.15 (η=50%); N_s/B = 0.67 ± 0.16 (η=35%) and 0.70 ± 0.16 (η=50%).
* Experimental specific strangeness yield for S-Ag at 200 A GeV: N_s/B|_exp = 0.86 ± 0.14 (from Ref. GR96).
* Experimental γ_s for S-W from WA85 data: 0.75 ± 0.1.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Rafelski, Letessier, and Tounsi (1996) and the authors' own impact-paper calculations (1996) by incorporating the then-new world average α_s(M_Z) = 0.118 from ICHEP 1996, which reduces the dominant uncertainty in earlier strangeness production calculations. The results are consistent with the QGP formation hypothesis for SPS data at 160-200 A GeV, and the authors predict that at RHIC/LHC temperatures around 500 MeV, thermal charm production will become significant enough to serve as a diagnostic tool analogous to strangeness at SPS energies. The paper enables future experimental determination of the strange quark mass from measured strangeness yields in heavy-ion collisions, and provides predictions for the energy dependence of strange baryon and antibaryon yields that could signal the onset of deconfinement.
