# Disordered mesoscopic systems with interactions: induced two-body ensembles and the Hartree-Fock approach

> We introduce a generic approach to study interaction effects in diffusive or chaotic quantum dots in the Coulomb blockade regime. The randomness of the single-particle wave functions induces randomness in the two-body interaction matrix elements. We classify the possible induced two-body ensembles, both in the presence and absence of spin degrees of freedom. The ensembles depend on the underlying space-time symmetries as well as on features of the two-body interaction. Confining ourselves to spinless electrons, we then use the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation to calculate HF single-particle energies and HF wave functions for many realizations of the ensemble. We study the statistical properties of the resulting one-body HF ensemble for a fixed number of electrons. In particular, we determine the statistics of the interaction matrix elements in the HF basis, of the HF single-particle energies (including the HF gap between the last occupied and the first empty HF level), and of the HF single-particle wave functions. We also study the addition of electrons, and in particular the distribution of the distance between successive conductance peaks and of the conductance peak heights.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a generic, model-independent approach to studying interaction effects in diffusive or chaotic quantum dots in the Coulomb blockade regime, where the randomness of single-particle wave functions induces randomness in the two-body interaction matrix elements; the approach is valid beyond the infinite-Thouless-conductance limit (g → ∞) and captures finite-g corrections to the universal Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors first classify the possible induced two-body ensembles for both spinless and spinful electrons by writing the first two cumulants of the interaction matrix elements in a covariant form that ensures invariance under changes of the single-particle basis, with the classification depending on space-time symmetries (orthogonal or unitary) and on features of the two-body interaction (e.g., contact vs. finite-range). For spinless electrons, they then apply the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation to many realizations of the ensemble, calculating HF single-particle energies, HF wave functions, and the statistics of interaction matrix elements in the HF basis, as well as observables related to electron addition such as peak-spacing and peak-height distributions.

## Key numbers

* For a diffusive dot, the variance parameter u² is proportional to Δ²/g², with u² = c Δ²/g², where c depends on geometry (e.g., for a 2D circular dot, c ≈ 0.67).
* For a ballistic dot, u² = c′ (Δ²/g²) ln(c″ g), with c′ = 3/4 (geometry-independent) and c″ ≈ 0.81 for a circular dot.
* For a contact interaction (orthogonal symmetry), the variance of off-diagonal matrix elements v_{αβ;γδ} (all indices different) is σ² = 3u², while for diagonal elements v_{αα;αα} it is σ² = 72u².
* For a local finite-range interaction (orthogonal symmetry), the variance of off-diagonal elements v_{αβ;γδ} is σ² = u², of direct elements v_{αβ;αβ} is σ² = 4u², of exchange elements v_{αβ;βα} is σ² = 2u², and of diagonal elements v_{αα;αα} is σ² = 8u².

## Context

This work extends the universal Hamiltonian approach of Kurland, Aleiner, and Altshuler (2000) and Aleiner, Brouwer, and Glazman (2002) by systematically including finite-g corrections beyond the g → ∞ limit, and it generalizes earlier model-specific HF studies of interacting quantum dots by Levit and Orgad (1999), Walker, Wilhelm, and Kotliar (1999), and Cohen, Richter, and Smilansky (1999). The induced two-body ensembles connect to the two-body embedded Gaussian ensembles introduced by Mon and French (1975) in nuclear physics, and the results enable generic predictions for peak-spacing and peak-height distributions in Coulomb blockade experiments without relying on non-generic model details.
