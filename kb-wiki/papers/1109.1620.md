# Bound H-dibaryon in the Flavor SU(3) Limit from a Full QCD Simulation on the Lattice

> Existence of the H-dibaryon in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit is studied by full QCD simulations on the lattice, in the approach recently developed for the baryon-baryon (BB) interactions. Potential of the flavor-singlet BB channel is derived from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function, and a bound H-dibaryon is discovered from it, with the binding energy of 20--50 MeV for the pseudo-scalar meson mass of 469--1171 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a bound H-dibaryon exists in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit of full QCD, with binding energy of 20–50 MeV for pseudo-scalar meson masses between 469 and 1171 MeV. However, the authors explicitly caution that this binding energy is measured from the BB threshold averaged in the (S,I)=(-2,0) sector, and that in the real world with flavor SU(3) breaking the binding energy measured from the ΛΛ threshold is expected to be much smaller or even negative.

## Method

The authors employ the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function approach to define a baryon-baryon potential from lattice QCD, solving a Schrödinger-like equation in Euclidean space-time to extract a local potential V(r). They perform Nf=3 full QCD simulations on a 32^3 × 32 lattice using the Iwasaki gauge action and non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson quark action at five different quark masses, then solve the Schrödinger equation in infinite volume with the fitted potential to obtain bound state energies.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy at M_p.s. = 837 MeV: 37.8 MeV with statistical error 3.1 MeV and systematic error 4.2 MeV
- Binding energy range across all quark masses: 20–50 MeV for pseudo-scalar meson masses 469–1171 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.121(2) fm
- Lattice size: L = 3.87 fm
- Pseudo-scalar meson masses: 468.9(8) MeV, 672.3(6) MeV, 836.5(5) MeV, 1015.2(6) MeV, 1170.9(7) MeV
- Baryon masses at corresponding quark masses: 1163(2) MeV, 1484(2) MeV, 1749(1) MeV, 2031(2) MeV, 2274(2) MeV
- Number of gauge configurations: 600 at lightest quark mass, 420–480 at heavier masses

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction of the H-dibaryon by Jaffe (1977) based on the quark model, and addresses the long-standing difficulty in lattice studies of dibaryons where isolating the ground state from excited states requires prohibitively large imaginary-time evolution. The method differs from previous lattice approaches such as those reviewed by Wetzorke (2002) and the conventional Lüscher method, instead using a volume-independent potential derived from the NBS wave function. The results enable future (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations with coupled-channel analysis of ΛΛ–NΞ–ΣΣ to determine whether the H-dibaryon exists as a bound state in the real world.
