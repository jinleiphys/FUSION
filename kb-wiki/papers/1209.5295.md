# The search for the onset of Color Transparency: a status report

> Color Transparency refers to the vanishing of the hadron-nucleon interaction for hadrons produced inside a nucleus in high momentum exclusive processes. We briefly review the concept behind this unique Quantum Chromo Dynamics' phenomenon, the experimental search for its onset and the recent progress made at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

Color Transparency (CT) refers to the predicted suppression of hadron-nucleon interactions for hadrons produced inside a nucleus in high momentum exclusive processes, but the paper reports that unambiguous observation of the onset of CT at intermediate energies remains elusive, with existing data ruling out CT effects larger than 7% over the Q² range from 2.0 to 8.1 (GeV/c)² in electron scattering experiments, while recent pion and rho meson experiments at Jefferson Lab show evidence consistent with the onset of CT.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental searches for Color Transparency using three main approaches: quasi-elastic proton knockout A(p,2p) reactions at BNL measuring nuclear transparency as the ratio of quasi-elastic cross section from nuclear targets to free pp elastic cross section; quasi-elastic electron scattering A(e,e'p) at SLAC and JLab comparing measured cross sections to plane wave impulse approximation predictions; and meson production experiments including pion photoproduction and electroproduction as well as rho meson electroproduction at JLab, where nuclear transparency is extracted from ratios of cross sections on nuclear targets to those on hydrogen or deuterium.

## Key numbers

- For the BNL A(p,2p) experiment: incident proton momenta p_p of 6 - 12 GeV/c, with transparency rising between 6 - 9.5 GeV/c but decreasing between 9.5 and 12 GeV/c
- For the Fermilab J/ψ photoproduction experiment: measured α = 1.4 ± 0.06 ± 0.04 for coherently produced J/ψ, compared to vector-meson dominance expectation of α = 4/3
- For the Fermilab E791 dijet experiment: σ ∝ A^(1.61±0.08) compared to predicted σ ∝ A^(1.54), with transverse separations d ≤ 0.3 fm
- For the JLab (e,e'p) experiments: no Q² dependence in nuclear transparency above Q² = 2 (GeV/c)², with constant α = -0.24 for Q² > 2.0 (GeV/c)² up to Q² = 8.1 (GeV/c)²
- For the JLab pion electroproduction experiment (piCT): Q² range of 1 - 5 (GeV/c)², with restriction of -t ≤ 0.5 GeV²
- For the NMC rho meson experiment: Q² values close to 10 (GeV/c)², showing no Q² dependence

## Context

This status report reviews the search for Color Transparency, a phenomenon first discussed by Mueller and Brodsky (1982) in the context of perturbative QCD, and later connected to QCD factorization theorems and Generalized Parton Distributions introduced by Ji and Radyushkin. The paper extends earlier work by describing how high-energy experiments at Fermilab (Sokoloff 1986, Aitala 2000) established CT for small dipole interactions at x ~ 10⁻², while intermediate-energy experiments at BNL (Carroll 1988, Leksanov 2001, Aclander 2004) and SLAC (Makins 1994) showed conflicting results. The recent JLab experiments (Clasie 2007, Qian 2010, ElFassi 2012) provide the first extensive evidence for the onset of CT in mesons at few-GeV energies, which is a necessary precondition for the validity of factorization theorems for meson production (Collins 1996) and for accessing GPDs. Future experiments with improved precision are needed to confirm these conclusions and determine the Q² value at which the factorization regime is reached.
