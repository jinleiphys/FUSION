# Gaussian expansion approach to Coulomb breakup

> An accurate treatment of Coulomb breakup reactions is presented by using both the Gaussian expansion method and the method of continuum discretized coupled channels. As $L^2$-type basis functions for describing bound- and continuum-states of a projectile, we take complex-range Gaussian functions, which form in good approximation a complete set in a large configuration space being important for Coulomb-breakup processes. Accuracy of the method is tested quantitatively for $^{8}{\rm B}+^{58}$Ni scattering at 25.8 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the pseudo-state continuum discretized coupled channels (PS-CDCC) method, using complex-range Gaussian basis functions, can accurately describe Coulomb breakup processes, which require a much larger configuration space (up to 100 fm) than nuclear breakup. The authors show this for \(^{8}\text{B}+^{58}\text{Ni}\) scattering at 25.8 MeV, but note this is only a first step toward a four-body CDCC analysis that would be needed to determine the astrophysical factor \(S_{17}\) with the required 5% accuracy.

## Method

The authors performed three-body CDCC calculations for \(^{8}\text{B}\) breakup from \(^{58}\text{Ni}\) at 25.8 MeV, comparing two discretization methods for the breakup continuum: the standard Average (Av) method and the pseudo-state (PS) method. In the PS method, the internal Hamiltonian of the projectile was diagonalized using complex-range Gaussian basis functions of the form \(r^{\ell}\exp[-(r/a_j)^2]\cos[b(r/a_j)^2]\) and \(r^{\ell}\exp[-(r/a_j)^2]\sin[b(r/a_j)^2]\), with \(b=\pi/2\) and geometric progression of the range parameters \(a_j\). The Av method used bin averaging with constant weight functions, and its converged result was taken as the "exact" solution for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The maximum internal coordinate \(r_{\text{max}}\) required for convergence was 100 fm for the Av method and 130 fm for the PS method, reflecting the long-range nature of Coulomb coupling.
- The cutoff momentum \(k_{\text{max}}\) was 0.66 fm\(^{-1}\), with bin widths \(\Delta_{i\ell}=0.66/16\) fm\(^{-1}\) for \(\ell=1\) and \(0.66/32\) fm\(^{-1}\) for \(\ell=0\) in the Av method.
- The complex-range Gaussian basis used \(a_1=1.0\), \(a_n=35.0\), \(2n=60\) basis functions, and \(b=\pi/2\).
- The maximum total angular momentum \(J_{\text{max}}\) was 1000, and \(R_{\text{max}}\) was 500 fm.
- The \(^{8}\text{B}\) single-particle potential depth was fitted to reproduce the proton separation energy of 137 keV.

## Context

This work extends the PS-CDCC method, previously shown to work for nuclear breakup of deuteron and \(^{6}\text{Li}\) (Matsumoto, 2003), to the more challenging case of Coulomb breakup where the effective configuration space extends to \(r \lesssim 100\) fm. The authors note that previous CDCC analyses of \(^{8}\text{B}\) breakup (Yamashita, 2004; Ogata, 2003) used the Av method and found that the Notre Dame data contain an irrelevant component from the \(1/2^-\) \(^{7}\text{Be}\) excited state, making those \(S_{17}\) values upper limits. The present work enables future four-body CDCC analysis of \(^{8}\text{B}\) as a \(p+{}^{3}\text{He}+{}^{4}\text{He}\) system, which would be necessary to determine \(S_{17}\) with the 5% accuracy required for solar neutrino physics.
