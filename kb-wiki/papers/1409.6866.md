# Effects of global charge conservation on time evolution of cumulants of conserved charges in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the effect of the global charge conservation on the cumulants of conserved charges observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions in a finite rapidity window, $Δη$, with a special emphasis on the time evolution of fluctuations in the hadronic medium. It is argued that the experimental result of the net-electric charge fluctuation observed by ALICE does not receive effects from the global charge conservation, because of the finite diffusion distance of charged particles in the hadronic stage. We emphasize that the magnitude of the effect of the global charge conservation can be estimated experimentally by combining the information on the $Δη$ dependences of various cumulants of conserved charges, similarly to other dynamical properties of the hot medium.

## Key claim

The global charge conservation (GCC) effect on net-electric charge fluctuations observed by ALICE is negligible for the rapidity windows used, because the diffusion distance of charged particles in the hadronic stage is much smaller than the distance from the mid-rapidity window to the system boundaries; the GCC only affects fluctuations when the distance from the window edge to the boundary is less than the diffusion length.

## Method

The authors model the time evolution of conserved charge fluctuations in the hadronic medium using a diffusion master equation on a discrete lattice of rapidity cells with reflecting boundaries, then take the continuum limit. They solve for the first four cumulants of net charge in a finite rapidity window Δη as functions of the diffusion length d(τ), total rapidity length η_tot, and initial fluctuations constrained by global charge conservation. The initial conditions are constructed from a multinomial distribution representing an equilibrated free classical gas in a finite volume, with the total net charge fluctuation vanishing at hadronization time τ₀.

## Key numbers

- The maximum rapidity coverage of the ALICE TPC detector is Δη = 1.6, corresponding to Δη/η_tot = 0.2 with η_tot = 8.
- The total rapidity length at LHC energy √s_NN = 2.76 TeV is taken as η_tot = 8, which is considerably smaller than twice the beam rapidity 2y_beam ≈ 16.
- The condition for GCC to affect fluctuations is (η_tot − Δη)/2 ≲ d(τ), i.e., when the distance from the window edge to the boundary is less than the diffusion length.
- For L = η_tot/d(τ) = 3, the Δη dependence of the second cumulant becomes almost linear, consistent with the equilibrium formula ⟨(δQ)²⟩ = ⟨(δQ)²⟩_GC (1 − Δη/η_tot).
- The second cumulant vanishes at Δη = η_tot for all finite L values, a trivial consequence of GCC.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Shuryak (2000) and Kitazawa (2013) on conserved charge fluctuations by incorporating finite system size with reflecting boundaries, generalizing the earlier infinite-volume treatments. It contradicts the assumption in Jeon (2000) and Bleicher (2000) that the GCC suppression formula ⟨(δQ)²⟩ = ⟨(δQ)²⟩_GC (1 − Δη/η_tot) applies universally; the authors show this formula only holds when the system is equilibrated (d(τ) ≳ η_tot/2). The results enable experimental extraction of the diffusion constant of the hadronic medium and initial fluctuations at hadronization by comparing the Δη dependence of cumulants, and allow separation of GCC effects from the primordial suppression signature of deconfinement.
