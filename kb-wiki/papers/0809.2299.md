# Flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea in an unquenched quark model

> We discuss the flavor asymmetry of the nucleon in an unquenched quark model for baryons in which the effects of quark-antiquark pairs (u anti-u, d anti-d and s anti-s) are taken into account in an explicit form. It is shown that the inclusion of q anti-q pairs leads to an excess of anti-d over anti-u quarks in the proton.

## Key claim

The inclusion of quark-antiquark pairs (u anti-u, d anti-d, s anti-s) in the nucleon wave function via an unquenched quark model leads to an excess of anti-d over anti-u quarks in the proton, in qualitative agreement with the NMC result for the Gottfried integral, but the calculation is exploratory with parameters taken from the literature and no attempt was made to optimize them.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed flux-tube breaking model (based on valence-quark plus glue dominance with s anti-s pairs added perturbatively) to include u anti-u and d anti-d loops. The pair-creation mechanism uses the 3P0 quantum numbers of the vacuum, and the one-loop diagrams are calculated by summing over a complete set of intermediate baryon-meson states (including both pseudoscalar and vector mesons), rather than just a few low-lying states.

## Key numbers

- The NMC experimental value of the Gottfried integral is 0.2281 ± 0.0065 at Q² = 4 (GeV/c)² over the range 0.004 ≤ x ≤ 0.8.
- The HERMES value is 0.23 ± 0.02 at ⟨Q²⟩ = 2.3 (GeV/c)² over 0.020 < x < 0.30.
- The E866/NuSea value is 0.255 ± 0.008 at ⟨Q²⟩ = 54 (GeV/c)² over 0.015 < x < 0.35.
- The effect of the quark-antiquark pairs on the Gottfried integral is a reduction of about one third with respect to the Gottfried sum rule value of 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube breaking model of baryons (2004) to include u anti-u and d anti-d loops, addressing the violation of the Gottfried sum rule first clearly observed by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC) at CERN. The approach contrasts with the cloudy bag model of Thomas (1983), which attributes the flavor asymmetry to pion cloud coupling, and with earlier studies of q⁴ anti-q configurations in nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Riska (2002). The unquenched quark model enables future systematic applications to baryon spectroscopy, including electromagnetic and strong couplings, elastic and transition form factors, and sea quark content.
