# Description of the reactions 36S + 238U and 64Ni + 238U within the two-stage fusion-fission model

> We describe the capture, fusion, fission and evaporation residue formation cross sections of superheavy nuclei within the proposed earlier two stages dynamical model. The approaching of the projectile nucleus to the target nucleus is described in the first stage of the model. On the second stage, the evolution of the system formed after the touching of the projectile and target nuclei is considered. The evolution of the system on both stages is described by Langevin equations. The transport coefficients of these equations are calculated within the microscopic linear response theory. The mutual orientation of the colliding ions, the tunneling through the Coulomb barrier in the entrance channel and the shell effects in the potential energy on both stages of the calculations are taking into account. The obtained results are compared with the available experimental data and other theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The two-stage dynamical model, which combines Langevin equations with transport coefficients from microscopic linear response theory, can reproduce experimental capture and fission cross sections for both the more mass-asymmetric reaction 36S + 238U and the less asymmetric 64Ni + 238U, but the calculated evaporation residue cross sections for 274Hs are on average one order of magnitude smaller than experimental data from similar reactions, a discrepancy the authors consider acceptable given experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors model the reaction in two stages: first, the approach of a spherical projectile to a deformed target nucleus using four collective coordinates (distance, two deformation parameters, and target orientation), with the Coulomb barrier penetration probability computed from an ensemble of Langevin trajectories; second, the evolution of the compact mono-system formed after touching, described by three Cassini-ovaloid shape parameters (elongation, mass asymmetry, neck radius), with the same Langevin formalism. The friction and inertia tensors are calculated from microscopic linear response theory in the local harmonic approximation, and the de-excitation of the compound nucleus by neutron and gamma emission is treated with a statistical model at each integration step.

## Key numbers

- For the 36S + 238U reaction, calculations were carried out at excitation energies Ex = 57.7, 47.3, 41.6, and 35.8 MeV, corresponding to the experimental energies of Kozulin (2016).
- For the 64Ni + 238U reaction, calculations were carried out at Ex = 64.1, 45.1, 33.5, and 23.2 MeV, also matching Kozulin (2016).
- The initial distance between centers of mass in the entrance channel was set to r = 50 fm.
- For the 274Hs compound nucleus at initial Ex = 47.3 MeV, the most probable excitation energy after first neutron emission is 38.7 MeV, and after second neutron emission is 31.0 MeV.
- The number of trajectories that form an evaporation residue in the 274Hs case is 13 to 15 orders of magnitude smaller than the initial number of trajectories that reached the ground state.
- For comparison, the cold fusion reaction 58Fe + 208Pb → 266Hs at GSI Darmstadt had an excitation energy of 18 ± 2 MeV and a production cross section of 19 +18 −11 pb.
- The calculated evaporation residue cross sections for 36S + 238U → 274Hs are on average one order of magnitude smaller than experimental data from the similar reactions 34S + 238U at Ex = 38.5 MeV (Nishio, 2010) and 26Mg + 248Cm at Ex = 44 MeV and 52.1 MeV (Dvorak, 2006).

## Context

This work extends the two-stage dynamical model previously developed by the same group (Litnevsky, 2012; 2019) to describe superheavy element formation in hot fusion reactions, specifically comparing the more mass-asymmetric 36S + 238U system with the less asymmetric 64Ni + 238U system. The results are compared with experimental capture and fission data from Kozulin (2016) and with evaporation residue data from Nishio (2010) and Dvorak (2006), as well as with theoretical predictions from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock plus Langevin approach of Hagino (2019) and the model of Zagrebaev (2008). The model enables predictions for the synthesis of element Z = 120 via the 64Ni + 238U reaction, where the calculated fusion and evaporation residue cross sections are several orders of magnitude smaller than for the 274Hs case.
