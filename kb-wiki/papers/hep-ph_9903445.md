# Towards Phonon-Like Excitations of Instanton Liquid

> The phonon-like excitations of (anti)-instanton ($\bar II$) liquid due to adiabatic variations of vacuum wave functions are studied in this paper. The kinetic energy term is found and the proper effective Lagrangian for such excitations is evaluated. The properties of their spectrum, while corresponding masses are defined by $Λ_{QCD}$, are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that phonon-like excitations of the instanton liquid can arise from adiabatic dilatational deformations of instanton solutions, with the resulting mass gap fixed by Λ_QCD; however, the numerical results are presented as qualitative illustrations of the principle rather than definitive predictions, and the authors note that a "complete theory" would require going beyond the superposition ansatz.

## Method

The authors start from the standard (anti)-instanton liquid model with a superposition ansatz for the gauge fields and a self-consistent distribution function for instanton sizes. They then introduce adiabatic time-dependent variations of the instanton size ρ, compute the resulting kinetic energy term κ from corrections to the chromoelectric field and the gauge potential, and derive an effective Lagrangian for the collective coordinate ρ(x). By expanding the effective potential around its minimum, they extract a mass term for the phonon-like excitations and evaluate it using self-consistently determined parameters of the instanton liquid.

## Key numbers

- Average instanton size: \(\bar\rho\Lambda \approx 0.37\) (for \(N_c=3\))
- Beta function at average size: \(\beta \approx 17.5\) (for \(N_c=3\))
- Instanton density: \(n \Lambda^{-4} \approx 0.44\) (for \(N_c=3\))
- Phonon mass: \(m \approx 1.21 \Lambda\)
- Wavelength in the Euclidean time direction: \(\lambda_4 \Lambda \approx 0.83\)
- System volume scale: \(\bar V^{1/4} \Lambda \approx 0.81\)
- Kinetic coefficient from strength tensor calculation: \(\kappa_{s.t.} = 12\pi^2/g^2\)
- Kinetic coefficient from gauge potential calculation: \(\kappa = 32\pi^2/g^2\)

## Context

This work extends the standard instanton liquid model, as developed by Shuryak and collaborators in the 1980s-1990s, by introducing dynamical excitations beyond the static ensemble. The authors build on the self-consistent treatment of the instanton size distribution and pair interactions from earlier work (referenced as [2] in the text). The results enable a possible connection between instanton liquid dynamics and light hadron spectroscopy, suggesting that some light hadrons might be interpreted as phonon-like excitations of the QCD vacuum, though the authors caution that including quark condensate effects and pion cloud contributions would be necessary for a complete theory.
