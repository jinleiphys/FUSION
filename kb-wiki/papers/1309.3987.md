# Extending the nuclear chart by continuum: from oxygen to titanium

> Nuclear masses ranging from O to Ti isotopes are systematically investigated with relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory, which can provide a proper treatment of pairing correlations in the presence of the continuum. From O to Ti isotopes, there are 402 nuclei predicted to be bound by the density functional PC-PK1. For the 234 nuclei with mass measured, the root mean square (rms) deviation is 2.23 MeV. It is found that the proton drip-lines predicted with various mass models are roughly the same and basically agree with the observation. The neutron drip-lines predicted, however, are quite different. Due to the continuum couplings, the neutron drip-line nuclei predicted are extended further neutron-rich than other mass models. By comparison with finite-range droplet model (FRDM), the neutron drip-line nucleus predicted by RCHB theory has respectively 2(O), 10(Ne), 10(Na), 6(Mg), 8(Al), 6(Si), 8(P), 6(S), 14(K), 10(Ca), 10(Sc), and 12(Ti) more neutrons.

## Key claim

The relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory predicts neutron drip-lines that extend further neutron-rich than other mass models, specifically by 2 to 14 additional neutrons per element from oxygen to titanium compared to the finite-range droplet model (FRDM), but only under the assumption of spherical symmetry and using the PC-PK1 density functional with a specific density-dependent delta pairing force.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in coordinate representation (RCHB theory) for spherical nuclei from oxygen to titanium isotopes, using the PC-PK1 covariant density functional for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta pairing force for the particle-particle channel. The equations were solved in a box of radius 20 fm with 0.1 fm step size, with a continuum cutoff energy of approximately 100 MeV. A nucleus was considered bound only if both its one-nucleon separation energy was positive and its Fermi level was negative.

## Key numbers

- 402 nuclei from O to Ti isotopes are predicted to be bound by RCHB theory with PC-PK1.
- For the 234 nuclei with measured masses, the root mean square (rms) deviation between calculated and experimental binding energies is 2.23 MeV.
- For spherical O isotopes, the rms deviation reduces to 1.67 MeV; for spherical Ca isotopes, it reduces to 1.09 MeV.
- Compared to FRDM, the neutron drip-line nucleus predicted by RCHB theory has respectively 2 (O), 10 (Ne), 10 (Na), 6 (Mg), 8 (Al), 6 (Si), 8 (P), 6 (S), 14 (K), 10 (Ca), 10 (Sc), and 12 (Ti) more neutrons.
- The density-dependent delta pairing force used a saturation density of 0.152 fm^-3 and strength V0 = 685.0 MeV fm^3, fixed by reproducing experimental odd-even mass differences of Ca isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) mass predictions to include proper treatment of continuum couplings via the RCHB method, building on earlier CDFT mass tables that either omitted pairing (Hirata et al., 1997) or used BCS pairing (Geng et al., 2005). The results are compared with the macroscopic-microscopic FRDM (Moller et al., 1995), the WS3 model (Liu et al., 2011), and the nonrelativistic HFB-21 (Goriely et al., 2010). The extended neutron drip-lines suggest the possible appearance of giant halos in light neutron-rich nuclei, as previously predicted for Ca isotopes by Meng et al. (2002). Future work should incorporate deformation effects, which may explain the 13 experimentally bound nuclei absent from the present spherical predictions.
