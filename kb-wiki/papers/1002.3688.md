# Helicity Amplitudes of the Lambda(1670) and two Lambda(1405) as dynamically generated resonances

> We determine the helicity amplitudes A_1/2 and radiative decay widths in the transition Lambda(1670) to gamma Y (Y=Lambda or Sigma^0). The Lambda(1670) is treated as a dynamically generated resonance in meson-baryon chiral dynamics. We obtain the radiative decay widths of the Lambda(1670) to gamma Lambda as 3 \pm 2 keV and to gamma Sigma^0 as 120 \pm 50 keV. Also, the Q^2 dependence of the helicity amplitudes A_1/2 is calculated. We find that the K Xi component in the Lambda(1670) structure, mainly responsible for the dynamical generation of this resonance, is also responsible for the significant suppression of the decay ratio Gamma_{gamma Lambda}/Gamma_{gamma Sigma^0}. A measurement of the ratio would, thus, provide direct access to the nature of the Lambda(1670). To compare the result for the Lambda(1670), we calculate the helicity amplitudes A_1/2 for the two states of the Lambda(1405). Also, the analytic continuation of Feynman parameterized integrals of more complicated loop amplitudes to the complex plane is developed which allows for an internally consistent evaluation of A_1/2.

## Key claim

The radiative decay widths and helicity amplitudes of the Lambda(1670) are pure predictions of the chiral unitary approach with no free parameters, but the results depend on treating the resonance as dynamically generated from meson-baryon components; the significant suppression of the decay ratio Gamma_{gamma Lambda}/Gamma_{gamma Sigma^0} is tied to the presence of the K Xi channel, which is mainly responsible for the dynamical generation of the resonance.

## Method

The authors calculate the helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and radiative decay widths for the transitions Lambda(1670) to gamma Lambda and gamma Sigma^0 using a chiral unitary approach where the Lambda(1670) is dynamically generated from meson-baryon scattering. The photon couples to the constituent mesons and baryons in the loop diagrams (meson pole term and Kroll-Ruderman term), with coupling strengths of the Lambda(1670) to meson-baryon channels obtained from the residues of the scattering amplitude at the pole position z = 1680 - 20i MeV. The authors also develop an analytic continuation of Feynman parameterized integrals to evaluate the loop amplitudes at the complex pole position, and compare results with evaluations at the real part of the pole.

## Key numbers

* Radiative decay width of Lambda(1670) to gamma Lambda: 3 +/- 2 keV
* Radiative decay width of Lambda(1670) to gamma Sigma^0: 120 +/- 50 keV
* Pole position of the Lambda(1670) used in the calculation: z = 1680 - 20i MeV
* Meson decay constant used: f = 1.123 f_pi with f_pi = 93 MeV
* Axial vector coupling constants: D = 0.85 +/- 0.06, F = 0.52 +/- 0.04
* Monopole form factor cutoff for pion: Lambda_pi = 0.727 GeV (corresponding to <r^2> = 0.44 fm^2)
* Monopole form factor cutoff for kaon: Lambda_K = 0.828 GeV (corresponding to <r^2> = 0.34 fm^2)

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the Lambda(1405) by Jido et al. (2003) and to the N(1535) by Jido et al. (2007, 2008) and Doring et al. (2009), now calculating electromagnetic properties of the Lambda(1670) which was found to be dynamically generated in the same framework by Bennhold et al. The results enable comparison with quark model calculations by Van Cauteren et al. (2005) and provide predictions that can be tested at J-PARC experiments with intense kaon beams. The analytic continuation method developed for Feynman parameterized integrals allows for internally consistent evaluation of resonance properties at the complex pole, complementing earlier work on magnetic moments and electromagnetic radii of the Lambda(1405) by Jido et al. (2002) and Sekihara et al. (2008).
