# Electromagnetic decays of the neutral pion

> We complement studies of the neutral pion transition form factor pi^0 --> gamma^(*) gamma^(*) with calculations for the electromagnetic decay widths of the processes pi^0 --> e^+ e^-, pi^0 --> e^+ e^- gamma and pi^0 --> e^+ e^- e^+ e^-. Their common feature is that the singly- or doubly-virtual transition form factor serves as a vital input that is tested in the non-perturbative low-momentum region of QCD. We determine this form factor from a well-established and symmetry-preserving truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations. Our results for the three- and four-body decays match results of previous theoretical calculations and experimental measurements. For the rare decay we employ a numerical method to calculate the process directly by deforming integration contours, which in principle can be generalized to arbitrary integrals as long as the analytic structure of the integrands are known. Our result for the rare decay is in agreement with dispersive calculations but still leaves a 2 sigma discrepancy between theory and experiment.

# Electromagnetic decays of the neutral pion

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter calculation of the rare decay π⁰ → e⁺e⁻ yields a branching ratio that agrees with dispersive calculations but still leaves a 2σ discrepancy between theory and experiment, meaning the long-standing puzzle is not resolved by this first-principles approach.

## Method

The authors determine the π⁰ → γ(*)γ(*) transition form factor from a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations using the Maris-Tandy model for the quark-gluon interaction, with dressed quark propagators, the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and the dressed quark-photon vertex all solved consistently from their respective equations. For the three-body (Dalitz) and four-body (double Dalitz) decays, the decay widths are computed by integrating the squared matrix elements over phase space using the transition form factor as the only non-perturbative input. For the rare two-body decay, the amplitude is evaluated both via a once-subtracted dispersion relation and by a direct numerical contour-deformation method that avoids the singularities in the integration domain.

## Key numbers

- Transition form factor at zero virtuality: F(0,0) = 0.996 (consistent with the chiral anomaly value of 1)
- Dalitz decay width: Γ(π⁰ → e⁺e⁻γ) = 9.11(4) × 10⁻¹¹ GeV, compared to the PDG value of 9.06(18) × 10⁻¹¹ GeV
- Double Dalitz decay width: Γ(π⁰ → 2e⁺2e⁻) = 2.63(1) × 10⁻¹³ GeV, compared to the PDG value of 2.58(12) × 10⁻¹³ GeV
- Rare decay branching ratio from dispersion relation: B(π⁰ → e⁺e⁻) = 6.21(3) × 10⁻⁸, compared to the experimental value of 6.87(36) × 10⁻⁸ (after radiative corrections)
- Rare decay branching ratio from direct contour deformation: B(π⁰ → e⁺e⁻) = 6.22(3) × 10⁻⁸
- Unitary bound for the rare decay branching ratio: 4.75 × 10⁻⁸
- Pion mass and decay constant from the model: mπ⁰ = 135.0(2) MeV, fπ⁰ = 92.4(2) MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the π⁰ transition form factor at large spacelike momenta (Eichmann 2017) to the timelike region relevant for electromagnetic decays. The results for the Dalitz and double Dalitz decays agree with calculations using chiral perturbation theory (Terschlüsen 2013) and data-driven approaches using rational approximants (Escribano 2015). For the rare decay, the result agrees with dispersive calculations by Dorokhov (2007, 2009) and Masjuan (2015) but remains below the KTeV experimental value (Abouzaid 2006) after radiative corrections (Vasko 2011, Husek 2014), confirming the persistent 2σ discrepancy that motivates further experimental and theoretical work.
