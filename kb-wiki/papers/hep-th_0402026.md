# Quark-antiquark pair production in space-time dependent fields

> Fermion-antifermion pair-production in the presence of classical fields is described based on the retarded and advanced fermion propagators. They are obtained by solving the equation of motion for the Dirac Green's functions with the respective boundary conditions to all orders in the field. Subsequently, various approximation schemes fit for different field configurations are explained. This includes longitudinally boost-invariant forms. Those occur frequently in the description of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions in the semiclassical limit. As a next step, the gauge invariance of the expression for the expectation value of the number of produced fermion-antifermion pairs as a functional of said propagators is investigated in detail. Finally, the calculations are carried out for a longitudinally boost-invariant model-field, taking care of the last issue, especially.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general formalism for computing fermion-antifermion pair production in arbitrarily space-time dependent classical fields using retarded and advanced propagators solved to all orders in the field, extending previous work that was limited to purely time-dependent fields. The gauge invariance of the resulting expression for the expectation value of the number of produced pairs is investigated in detail, and the calculations are carried out for a longitudinally boost-invariant model field as a specific application.

## Method

The retarded and advanced Dirac propagators are obtained by solving the equation of motion for the Dirac Green's functions with the appropriate boundary conditions to all orders in the classical field, using a product ansatz that introduces a generalized translation operator. Various approximation schemes are then derived from the full expression, including weak-field expansion, strong-field expansion, gradient expansion, combined strong-field and gradient expansion, and an Abelian expansion scheme. The formalism is applied to different classes of field configurations, with particular attention to longitudinally boost-invariant fields relevant for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the gauge invariance of the pair-number expectation value is examined.

## Context

This work generalizes the approach of Field (no year given in text) from purely time-dependent classical fields to arbitrarily space-time dependent fields, providing a unified framework for pair production in QED and QCD contexts. The formalism is relevant for the physics of the early universe (Cornwall, no year given), strong laser fields (Laser, no year given), and ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the McLerran-Venugopalan model (JIMWLK, no year given) treats gluons as classical background fields. The paper enables systematic calculations of quark-antiquark pair production beyond the Schwinger mechanism (Schwinger, no year given) for constant fields, and provides approximation schemes suitable for different field configurations encountered in heavy-ion collisions.
