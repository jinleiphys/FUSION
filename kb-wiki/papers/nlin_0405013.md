# 1/f noise in the Two-Body Random Ensemble

> We show that the spectral fluctuations of the Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE) exhibit 1/f noise. This result supports a recent conjecture stating that chaotic quantum systems are characterized by 1/f noise in their energy level fluctuations. After suitable individual averaging, we also study the distribution of the exponent αin the 1/f^α noise for the individual members of the ensemble. Almost all the exponents lie inside a narrow interval around α=1 suggesting that also individual members exhibit 1/f noise, provided they are individually unfolded

## Key claim

The spectral fluctuations of the Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE) exhibit 1/f noise, supporting the conjecture that chaotic quantum systems are characterized by 1/f noise in their energy level fluctuations; however, individual members only show this behavior after suitable individual averaging of the power spectrum and after being individually unfolded.

## Method

The authors constructed TBRE Hamiltonian matrices for N=6 spinless fermions in M=11, 12, 13, and 14 single-particle states, yielding Hilbert space dimensions of 462, 924, 1716, and 3003 respectively, with two-body matrix elements taken as independent Gaussian random variables. They diagonalized 200 matrices per ensemble, unfolded each spectrum individually using a polynomial fit of grade 5 to the accumulated level density, and computed the power spectrum of the δ_q statistic (the deviation of excitation energy from its mean). For individual members, they applied binning averaging in the logarithmic frequency domain and running spectral averaging over sets of 256 consecutive levels to extract the exponent α in the 1/f^α law.

## Key numbers

- Ensemble-averaged power spectrum exponents: α = 1.09 ± 0.04 for (N,M)=(6,11); α = 1.08 ± 0.01 for (6,12); α = 1.07 ± 0.01 for (6,13); α = 1.07 ± 0.01 for (6,14)
- For an individual member of the (6,14) ensemble: raw power spectrum fit gave α = 1.10 ± 0.07; binning-averaged fit gave α = 1.10 ± 0.06; running-averaged fit over 256-level sets gave α = 1.02 ± 0.05
- Distribution of α for 200 matrices of the (6,14) ensemble: mean <α> = 1.06, width σ_α = 0.08

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior finding (Relano 2002) that classical random matrix ensembles (Gaussian and Circular ensembles) exhibit 1/f noise, to the more physically realistic Two-Body Random Ensemble (also called Embedded GOE), which models systems with effective two-body interactions like atomic nuclei and quantum dots. The TBRE is not ergodic (Bohigas 1971, French 1973, Brody 1981), unlike the GOE, so individual unfolding is required to recover GOE-like statistics; the results enable application of 1/f noise analysis to experimental data such as nuclear masses (Hirsch 2004).
