# Hartree shift and pairing gap in ultracold Fermi gases in the framework of low-momentum interactions

> In this paper we consider a two-component gas of fermions on the BCS side of the BCS-BEC crossover at zero temperature. We use a momentum dependent interaction that reproduces the s-wave scattering phase shifts of a contact interaction up to a momentum cutoff that is scaled with the Fermi momentum. Using a diagrammatic formulation of Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory, suitably augmented by self-consistency conditions, we obtain the Hartree shift and the pairing gap to third order. In the weak-coupling regime, our results are not only well-converged but also agree with the well-established Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov corrections for the gap and the Galitskii result for the Hartree shift. Near the unitary regime, our results for the Nambu-Gor'kov self-energy are less converged, but there is still reasonable agreement with experiments as well as with quantum Monte-Carlo results. Perspectives for improvements and applications of this approach to neutron matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a momentum-dependent low-momentum interaction with a cutoff scaled to the Fermi momentum, combined with Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory up to third order with improved self-consistency conditions, can reproduce the known Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov correction for the pairing gap and the Galitskii result for the Hartree shift in the weak-coupling regime, but the results are less converged near the unitary regime.

## Method

The authors constructed a separable low-momentum interaction that reproduces the s-wave scattering phase shifts of a contact interaction up to a momentum cutoff Λ scaled with the Fermi momentum kF, using a smooth regulator function. They employed a diagrammatic formulation of Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory within the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism, computing the normal self-energy (Hartree shift) and anomalous self-energy (pairing gap) to third order, with self-consistency conditions imposed at the Fermi momentum to determine the mean-field parameters U and Δ.

## Key numbers

* For the Hartree shift U at coupling strength (kF a)^(-1) = -5, a plateau builds up in the cutoff range 1.5 ≲ Λ/kF ≲ 4 when including second-order and third-order corrections.
* The pairing gap Δ in the weak-coupling regime is expected to be suppressed by a factor of approximately 0.45 relative to the BCS value due to the Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov correction.
* The Hartree shift U and pairing gap Δ are evaluated at k = kF, with U evaluated at ω = 0 and Δ evaluated at ω = E_kF (approximately Δ_HFB), in units of the Fermi energy EF = kF^2/(2m).
* Results are presented for four interaction strengths: (kF a)^(-1) = -5, -2, -1, and 0 (unitary Fermi gas).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the ground state energy of ultracold Fermi gases using low-momentum interactions (Urban 2021) by now computing the self-energy corrections. It addresses the well-known problem that the BCS mean-field pairing gap is far from satisfactory, as established by Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov (1961), who showed that particle-hole fluctuations reduce the gap by more than 50% in the weak-coupling limit. The approach enables future applications to neutron matter, where large scattering lengths are inherent, and suggests that induced three- and higher-body interactions may need to be included for improved convergence at low cutoffs.
