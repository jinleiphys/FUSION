# Rapidity Asymmetry in High-energy $d+A$ Collisions

> In contrast to the recent prediction of high $p_T$ hadron suppression within the parton saturation model, it is shown that multiple parton scattering suffered by the projectile will enhance high $p_T$ hadron spectra in $d+A$ collisions relative to a superposition of binary $p+p$ collisions at RHIC. A stronger enhancement in the forward rapidity region of the projectile is also predicted, resulting in a unique rapidity asymmetry of the hadron spectra at high $p_T$. The shape of the rapidity asymmetry should be reversed for low $p_T$ hadrons that are dominated by soft and coherent interactions which suppress hadron spectra in the projectile rapidity region. The phenomenon at the LHC energies is shown to be qualitatively different because of parton shadowing.

# Rapidity Asymmetry in High-energy $d+A$ Collisions

## Key claim

In contrast to the parton saturation model prediction of high $p_T$ hadron suppression in $d+A$ collisions, multiple parton scattering suffered by the projectile will enhance high $p_T$ hadron spectra relative to a superposition of binary $p+p$ collisions at RHIC, with a stronger enhancement in the forward rapidity region of the projectile. The shape of this rapidity asymmetry should be reversed for low $p_T$ hadrons dominated by soft and coherent interactions, and the phenomenon at LHC energies is qualitatively different because of parton shadowing.

## Method

The author uses a lowest-order pQCD-inspired parton model with Gaussian initial transverse momentum distributions, incorporating impact-parameter dependent nuclear parton distributions (using both HIJING and EKS parameterizations for nuclear modification) and transverse momentum broadening proportional to the number of scatterings the projectile suffers inside the nucleus. The model includes a $K\approx 1.5$ factor to account for higher-order QCD corrections, and the $k_T$ broadening per nucleon-nucleon collision is parameterized as $\delta^2(Q^2)=0.225\ln^2(Q/{\rm GeV})/[1+\ln(Q/{\rm GeV})]$ GeV$^2/c^2$, chosen to fit existing $p+A$ data up to $\sqrt{s}=40$ GeV. The rapidity distributions of charged hadrons are then calculated for minimum-biased $d+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and $d+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV for $d+Au$ collisions, the $k_T$ broadening enhances hadron spectra for $p_T=2-8$ GeV/$c$ in both forward and backward rapidity regions, with larger enhancement in the forward (projectile) region.
- For $p_T>8$ GeV/$c$ at RHIC, the EMC effect suppresses hadron spectra, with stronger suppression in the target rapidity region than in the projectile region.
- At $p_T=1.5$ GeV/$c$ in $d+Au$ at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, parton shadowing can overcome the enhancement from transverse momentum broadening, reversing the rapidity asymmetry.
- The initial transverse momentum distribution uses $\langle k^2_T\rangle_N(Q^2)= 1.2$ GeV$^2 + 0.2\alpha_s(Q^2) Q^2$.
- The $k_T$ broadening parameterization is $\delta^2(Q^2)=0.225\ln^2(Q/{\rm GeV})/[1+\ln(Q/{\rm GeV})]$ GeV$^2/c^2$.
- At LHC energy $\sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV for $d+Pb$ collisions, the Cronin enhancement is smaller and the nuclear modification factor has much smaller variation than at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the multiple parton scattering model previously applied to $p+A$ collisions by Wang (1998) to predict a unique rapidity asymmetry in $d+A$ collisions, in contrast to the parton saturation model of Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2003) which predicts high $p_T$ suppression in $p+A$ collisions. The rapidity dependence was also studied by Vitev (2003), but this paper emphasizes the opposite asymmetry between high and low $p_T$ hadrons. The predictions are directly testable with upcoming $d+Au$ data from RHIC, which can distinguish between the multiple scattering and parton saturation mechanisms and verify whether jet quenching from parton energy loss is the underlying mechanism for hadron suppression observed in central $A+A$ collisions.
