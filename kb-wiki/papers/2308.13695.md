# Constraining the pion distribution amplitude using Drell-Yan reactions on a proton

> Using a reaction model that incorporates pion bound state effects and continuum results for proton parton distributions and the pion distribution amplitude, $φ_π$, we deliver parameter-free predictions for the $μ^+$ angular distributions in $πN \to μ^+ μ^- X$ reactions on both unpolarised and polarised targets. The analysis indicates that such angular distributions are sensitive to the pointwise form of $φ_π$ and suggests that unpolarised targets are practically more favourable. The precision of extant data is insufficient for use in charting $φ_π$; hence, practical tests of this approach to charting $φ_π$ must await data with improved precision from new-generation experiments. The reaction model yields a nonzero single-spin azimuthal asymmetry, without reference to $T$-odd parton distribution functions (DFs). This may necessitate additional care when attempting to extract such $T$-odd DFs from data.

## Key claim

The angular distributions of muons produced in pion-nucleon Drell-Yan reactions are sensitive to the pointwise form of the pion distribution amplitude, but the precision of existing data is insufficient to discriminate between different realistic forms; practical tests must await new-generation experiments with improved precision.

## Method

The authors used a reaction model incorporating pion bound state effects via hard gluon exchange between the annihilating valence antiquark in the pion and a spectator valence quark in the proton, combined with continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) results for proton parton distributions and the pion distribution amplitude. They computed the five-fold differential cross-section for the process pi- N -> mu+ mu- X in the Gottfried-Jackson frame, evaluating the angular distribution coefficients lambda, mu, nu, bar-mu, and bar-nu for both unpolarised and longitudinally polarised proton targets at various kinematic settings.

## Key numbers

* The CSM pion distribution amplitude at scale zeta_2 = 2 GeV is given by phi_pi(x; zeta_2) = n_0 ln(1 + x(1-x)/r_phi^2) with r_phi = 0.162.
* For E615 data at rho = 0.4, chi^2/datum = 2.7 using the CSM pion DA, 2.3 using the bimodal BMS DA, and 4.8 using the asymptotic DA phi_as(x) = 6x(1-x).
* For individual panels at rho = 0.4: panel D yields chi^2/datum = 1.9 (CSM), 1.4 (BMS), 1.0 (asymptotic); panel E yields 4.8 (CSM), 3.9 (BMS), 10 (asymptotic); panel F yields 1.3 (CSM), 1.5 (BMS), 3.5 (asymptotic).
* The Lam-Tung combination 2nu - 1 + lambda is consistent with zero at the 55% level based on E615 data at rho = 0.4.
* The single-spin asymmetry magnitude is roughly rho * bar-mu / (2 + lambda).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Brandenburg (1994) and Brandenburg (1995) for using Drell-Yan angular distributions to probe the pion distribution amplitude, and corrects an error in the formula used by Bakulev (2007) for polarised targets. The authors provide parameter-free predictions using modern CSM results for both the pion DA from Chang (2013) and proton parton distributions from Lu (2022) and Cheng (2023), enabling tests of the emergent hadron mass paradigm. The analysis shows that unpolarised targets are practically more favourable than polarised ones for charting the pion DA, and that the nonzero single-spin azimuthal asymmetry arises from bound state effects without requiring T-odd parton distribution functions, which may complicate extraction of such T-odd DFs from data.
