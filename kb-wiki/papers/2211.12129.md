# Molecular state interpretation of charmed baryons in the quark model

> Stimulated by the observation of $Λ_c(2910)^+$ by the Belle Collaboration, the $S$-wave $qqq\bar{q}c~(q=u~\text{or}~d)$ pentaquark systems with $I$ = 0, $J^P$ = $\frac{1}{2}^-,~\frac{3}{2}^- and~\frac{5}{2}^-$ are investigated in the framework of quark delocalization color screening model(QDCSM). The real-scaling method is utilized to check the bound states and the genuine resonance states. The root mean square of cluster spacing is also calculated to study the structure of the states and estimate if the state is resonance state or not. The numerical results show that $Λ_{c}(2910)$ cannot be interpreted as a molecular state, and $Σ_{c}(2800)$ cannot be explained as the $ND$ molecular state with $J^P=1/2^-$. $Λ_{c}(2595)$ can be interpreted as the molecular state with $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$ and the main component is $Σ_{c}π$. $Λ_{c}(2625)$ can be interpreted as the molecular state with $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^-$ and the main component is $Σ_{c}^{*}π$. $Λ_{c}(2940)$ is likely to be interpreted as a molecular state with $J^P=3/2^-$, and the main component is $ND^{*}$. Besides, two new molecular states are predicted, one is the $J^P=3/2^-$ $Σ_{c}ρ$ resonance state with the mass around 3140 MeV, another one is the $J^P=\frac{5}{2}^-$ $Σ_{c}^*ρ$ with the mass of 3188.3 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that the newly observed $\Lambda_c(2910)^+$ cannot be interpreted as a molecular state in the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), and that $\Sigma_c(2800)$ cannot be explained as an $ND$ molecular state with $J^P=1/2^-$, while several other charmed baryons can be interpreted as molecular states with specific quantum numbers and dominant components.

## Method

The authors investigated $S$-wave $qqq\bar{q}c$ pentaquark systems with isospin $I=0$ and spin-parities $J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-$ using the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM). They employed the resonating group method (RGM) with generating coordinates for dynamical calculations, considered both $qqq-\bar{q}c$ and $qqc-\bar{q}q$ cluster structures including their coupling, and used the real-scaling method to distinguish genuine resonance states from bound states and continuum states.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c(2910)^+$ has experimentally measured mass $(2913.8 \pm 5.6 \pm 3.8)~\mathrm{MeV}/c^2$ and width $(51.8 \pm 20.0 \pm 18.8)~\mathrm{MeV}$ from Belle Collaboration
- $\Lambda_c(2595)$ can be interpreted as a molecular state with $J^P=1/2^-$ and main component $\Sigma_c\pi$, with corrected coupled-channel energy $2574.4$ MeV
- $\Lambda_c(2625)$ can be interpreted as a molecular state with $J^P=3/2^-$ and main component $\Sigma_c^*\pi$, with corrected coupled-channel energy $2627.8$ MeV
- $\Lambda_c(2940)$ is likely a molecular state with $J^P=3/2^-$ and main component $ND^*$, with corrected coupled-channel energy $2944.5$ MeV
- A predicted $J^P=3/2^-$ $\Sigma_c\rho$ resonance state with mass around $3140$ MeV
- A predicted $J^P=5/2^-$ $\Sigma_c^*\rho$ state with mass $3188.3$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmed baryons using the QDCSM framework, which has been applied to $NN$ and $YN$ interactions and deuteron properties (Wu 1996, Ping 2000), and earlier work on $ND$ systems (Zhao 2016). The results contradict molecular interpretations for $\Lambda_c(2910)$ and $\Sigma_c(2800)$ that were proposed in other approaches such as QCD sum rules (Zhang 2012) and effective field theory (Nieves 2019). The findings enable future experimental searches for the predicted $\Sigma_c\rho$ and $\Sigma_c^*\rho$ resonance states and provide constraints on the nature of observed charmed baryons for further theoretical studies.
