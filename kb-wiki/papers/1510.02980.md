# Harmonically trapped fermions in two dimensions: ground-state energy and contact of SU(2) and SU(4) systems via nonuniform lattice Monte Carlo

> We study harmonically trapped, unpolarized fermion systems with attractive interactions in two spatial dimensions with spin degeneracies Nf = 2 and 4 and N/Nf = 1, 3, 5, and 7 particles per flavor. We carry out our calculations using our recently proposed quantum Monte Carlo method on a nonuniform lattice. We report on the ground-state energy and contact for a range of couplings, as determined by the binding energy of the two-body system, and show explicitly how the physics of the Nf-body sector dominates as the coupling is increased.

## Key claim

The ground-state energy per particle of harmonically trapped 2D fermions with SU(2) and SU(4) symmetry is dominated by the Nf-body bound-state contribution at strong coupling, and no new N-body bound states appear beyond N = Nf, but this conclusion is restricted to the range of couplings and particle numbers studied (N/Nf = 1, 3, 5, 7) and to the specific lattice discretization used.

## Method

The authors used a nonuniform lattice quantum Monte Carlo method based on Gauss-Hermite quadrature points to discretize the spatial coordinates, combined with a Suzuki-Trotter decomposition of the imaginary-time evolution operator (with temporal discretization τ = 0.05 in lattice units) and a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to represent the interaction via auxiliary fields. They tuned the coupling to physical values by matching the two-body ground-state energy on the lattice to the known continuum solution relating energy to the 2D scattering length a0, then computed ground-state energies and Tan's contact for systems with Nf = 2 and 4 flavors and N/Nf = 1, 3, 5, and 7 particles per flavor using 10^4 decorrelated auxiliary-field samples.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy per particle for Nf = 2, N/Nf = 1 in the noninteracting limit: E/(ħω N) = 1
- Ground-state energy per particle for Nf = 2, N/Nf = 3 in the noninteracting limit: E/(ħω N) = 5/3
- Ground-state energy per particle for Nf = 2, N/Nf = 5 in the noninteracting limit: E/(ħω N) = 12/5
- Ground-state energy per particle for Nf = 2, N/Nf = 7 in the noninteracting limit: E/(ħω N) = 3
- Temporal discretization parameter: τ = 0.05 in lattice units
- Lattice size fixed at Nx = 10 for production calculations
- Statistical uncertainty of order 1% from 10^4 decorrelated samples

## Context

This work extends the nonuniform lattice Monte Carlo method previously introduced by the same group (GHBDA) from 1D to 2D harmonically trapped fermions, complementing earlier ab initio studies of the 2D ground-state equation of state by Bertaina (2011) and Shi, Chiesa, and Zhang (2013). The results provide predictions for ongoing experiments with ultracold fermionic atoms in quasi-2D optical traps, particularly for systems with SU(Nf) symmetry as realized in optical lattices (Cazalilla and Rey, 2014), and for few-body experiments such as those by the Heidelberg group (2014). The calculations enable future comparisons with experimental measurements of Tan's contact and ground-state energy in 2D trapped systems.
