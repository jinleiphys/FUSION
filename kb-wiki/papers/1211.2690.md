# Superfluid properties of the inner crust of neutron stars. II. Wigner-Seitz cells at finite temperature

> We investigate the superfluid properties of the inner crust of neutron stars at finite temperature for different pairing functionals. We generalize the formalism adopted in previous article to include the effect of the temperature to calculate the specific heat of each given Wigner-Seitz cell. The calculations are done for two pairing forces, Gogny D1 and $V_{\text{low-k}}$, with finite range and a density dependent contact interaction. We compare in such a way the effect of the pairing strength and of the range on the thermal properties of the inner crust.

# Superfluid properties of the inner crust of neutron stars. II. Wigner-Seitz cells at finite temperature

## Key claim

The specific heat of Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust of neutron stars shows two distinct phase transitions for low-density cells: one corresponding to the disappearance of superfluidity in the external neutron gas and another for the bound neutrons inside the nuclear cluster, a feature that depends on the range and strength of the pairing interaction used.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations at finite temperature for spherical Wigner-Seitz cells representing different density regions of the inner crust, using the Negele-Vautherin cell sequence. Three pairing interactions were compared: the finite-range Gogny D1 and Vlow-k interactions, and a density-dependent contact interaction (DDDI) fitted to reproduce the Vlow-k pairing gaps in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, all using the SLy4 Skyrme functional for the particle-hole channel.

## Key numbers

- The DDDI interaction strength is V0 = -458.0 MeV fm^3, with a cutoff on quasiparticle energy of 60 MeV and neutron-neutron scattering length ann = -18.5 fm
- For the cell 180Zr, the first critical temperature for neutrons is kB Tc^n,1 = 0.09 MeV (gas phase transition) and the second is kB Tc^n,2 = 0.32 MeV (bound neutron phase transition)
- The proton gas density at kB T = 1 MeV is approximately 10^-11 fm^-3, reaching approximately 10^-7 fm^-3 at kB T = 2 MeV
- The number of protons in the gas region is Np^out ≈ 0.008 at kB T = 1 MeV and Np^out ≈ 0.05 at kB T = 2 MeV
- The empirical BCS relation kB Tc^q ≈ 0.56 * Δ^q(kB T=0) is compatible with the findings
- The DDDI interaction reproduces the Vlow-k neutron critical temperatures within approximately 30 keV difference
- The difference between BCE and BCO boundary conditions for critical temperature is at most approximately 10 keV in the cell 500Zr

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature formalism of Pastore (2011) to finite temperature, generalizing the approach to calculate specific heats of Wigner-Seitz cells. It builds on prior work by Baldo et al. (2006) showing that pairing correlations modify the sequence of Wigner-Seitz cells compared to the original Negele-Vautherin (1973) calculations, and by Grill et al. (2011) and Sandulescu et al. (2004) who performed similar calculations with zero-range pairing interactions. The results enable more precise modeling of neutron star cooling, as the specific heat of the inner crust determines the thermal response time of the star, following the arguments of Petick and collaborators.
