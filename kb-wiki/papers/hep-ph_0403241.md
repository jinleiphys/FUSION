# Radiative neutron beta-decay in effective field theory

> We consider radiative β--decay of the neutron in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, with an extension including explicit Δdegrees of freedom. We compute the photon energy spectrum as well as the photon polarization; both observables are dominated by the electron bremsstrahlung contribution. Nucleon-structure effects not encoded in the weak coupling constants g_A and g_V are determined at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, and enter at the {\cal O}(0.5%)-level, making a sensitive test of the Dirac structure of the weak currents possible.

# Radiative neutron beta-decay in effective field theory

## Key claim

The photon energy spectrum and photon polarization in radiative neutron beta-decay are dominated by electron bremsstrahlung, with nucleon-structure effects beyond the weak coupling constants g_V and g_A entering at the 0.5% level at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. The recoil-order corrections are smaller than those in ordinary neutron beta-decay because the presence of an additional final-state particle suppresses them further.

## Method

The authors compute the matrix element for n → p e⁻ ν̄_e γ using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) and the small scale expansion (SSE) that includes explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom. They evaluate the vector-vector and vector-axial correlators to next-to-leading order (O(1/M) or O(ε²)), integrate over the four-particle phase space numerically, and compute both the differential photon energy spectrum dΓ/dω and the circular polarization P of the emitted photon as functions of photon energy and electron energy.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio for ω ∈ [0.035 MeV, 0.100 MeV] is 2.21 × 10⁻³, compatible with the experimental upper limit of Br < 6.9 × 10⁻³ (90% CL) from Beck (1993)
- The branching ratio for ω ∈ [0.005 MeV, 0.035 MeV] is 5.17 × 10⁻³
- The branching ratio for ω ∈ [0.100 MeV, ω_max = 0.782 MeV] is 1.44 × 10⁻³
- Recoil-order corrections to the photon energy spectrum are no larger than O(0.5%)
- The SSE (Delta-resonance) contribution is itself of O(0.1%)
- The photon polarization evolves from near-zero at low photon energies to nearly 100% left-handed polarization at high photon energies
- Input parameters include: λ = g_A/g_V = 1.267, κ_v = 3.706, neutron lifetime τ_n = 885.7 s, M = 939.56533 MeV, M' = 938.27200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculation of Gaponov (1960s) by including all O(1/M) recoil-order corrections systematically using effective field theory, whereas Gaponov neglected such effects. The authors find that their numerical results for the photon energy spectrum disagree with Gaponov by roughly a factor of two for ω/m_e > 0.2, though the discrepancy decreases at lower photon energies. The calculation uses the same hadronic matrix elements previously computed in Bernard, Hemmert, and Meißner (2003) for radiative muon capture, but at much smaller momentum transfers. The work enables precision tests of the Dirac structure of the weak currents through photon polarization measurements, complementing searches for non-SM effects in processes such as K⁺ → μ⁺ ν γ decay and b → s γ decay.
