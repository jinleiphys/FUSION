# Flavor isospin waves in one-dimensional axisymmetric neutrino gases

> Flavor oscillations can occur on very short spatial and temporal scales in the dense neutrino media in a core-collapse supernova (CCSN) or binary neutron star merger (BNSM) event. Although the dispersion relations (DRs) of the fast neutrino oscillations can be obtained by linearizing the equations of motion (EoM) before the emergence of any significant flavor conversion, one largely depends on numerical calculations to understand this interesting phenomenon in the nonlinear regime. In this work we demonstrate that there exist nontrivial solutions to the flavor EoM that govern the fast oscillations in one-dimensional axisymmetric neutrino gases. These solutions represent a coherent flavor isospin wave similar to the magnetic spin wave in a lattice of magnetic dipoles. We also compute the DRs of such waves in some example cases which are closely related to the DRs of the fast neutrino oscillations obtained in the linear regime. This result sheds new light on the long-term behavior of fast neutrino oscillations which can have various implications for the CCSN and BNSM events.

# Flavor isospin waves in one-dimensional axisymmetric neutrino gases

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that nontrivial flavor isispin wave solutions exist in one-dimensional axisymmetric neutrino gases, where the flavor Bloch vectors of all neutrinos with a given velocity precess uniformly in space and time. These solutions exist specifically when the neutrino electron lepton number (ELN) distribution has a crossing, and their dispersion relations are closely related to those of the trivial wave solutions obtained from linearized equations of motion.

## Method

The authors derive a set of self-consistent equations from the nonlinear equations of motion for flavor Bloch vectors in a one-dimensional axisymmetric neutrino gas, assuming a monochromatic wave ansatz where all Bloch vectors precess with common frequency Omega and wave number K. They solve these equations numerically for two toy ELN distributions (with and without a crossing) to obtain the dispersion relations and Bloch vector configurations of the flavor isospin waves. They then perform time-dependent numerical simulations using the NuGas Python package with the Lax42 algorithm to verify that these wave solutions propagate stably and to study the dynamics when trivial and nontrivial wave solutions are mixed.

## Key numbers

- For the ELN distribution G(u) = 0.5 - 0.3u (no crossing), the authors compute wave solutions with ELN density N3 = 0.8 and wave number K = 0.5, yielding frequency Omega approximately -0.144, F_perp approximately 0.169, and F3 approximately -0.121.
- For the ELN distribution G(u) = 0.5 - 0.7u (with crossing), the authors compute wave solutions with N3 = 1 and K = -1.25, yielding Omega approximately 0.392, F_perp approximately 0.064, and F3 approximately -0.435.
- The asymptotic limits of the wave frequency and wave number as |K| approaches infinity are given by Omega_0 = N3 and K_0,pm = +/- c0[(u_s,pm)^2 - 1] -/+ (2c1/3)(u_s,pm)^3, where u_s,pm are the angular spectral split locations.
- The angular spectral split locations for the linear ELN distribution G(u) = c0 - c1u are given by u_s,pm = (c0/c1) - sqrt((c0/c1)^2 + 1 -/+ N3/c1).
- Numerical simulations were performed with 1000 equally spaced z bins and 1001 equally spaced u bins in a periodic box of length L = 10pi/|K|.

## Context

This work extends the gyroscopic pendulum picture of two-flavor neutrino oscillations (Hannestad 2006, Duan 2007) to inhomogeneous, one-dimensional neutrino gases, and realizes the prediction of flavor isospin waves made by Duan (2008). The dispersion relations for fast neutrino flavor conversions obtained by Izaguirre (2016) correspond to the trivial wave solutions (P_u3 = 1) of the present framework. The results provide semi-analytic understanding of the nonlinear regime of fast neutrino oscillations, which has been explored numerically by Martin (2019, 2021), Bhattacharyya (2020), Richers (2021), and Wu (2021), and may help resolve the apparent disagreement between coherent and depolarizing flavor evolution seen in those calculations. The work enables further study of whether coherent flavor isospin waves persist against arbitrary perturbations and whether they exist in neutrino gases with deep ELN crossings.
