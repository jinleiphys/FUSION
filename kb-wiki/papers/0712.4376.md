# Study on $N\bar{N}$ $S$-wave Elastic Cross Section and Possible Bound States Within a Constituent Quark Model

> In the framework of a chiral constituent quark model, considering the contributions of $π$ annihilation and one-gluon annihilation, the proton-antiproton $S$-wave elastic scattering cross section experimental data can be reproduced by adjusting properly one-gluon annihilation coupling constant. Meanwhile, using the fixed model parameter, we do a dynamical calculation for all possible $S$-wave nucleon-antinucleon states, the results show that, there is no $S$-wave bound state as indicated by a strong enhancement at threshold of $p\bar{p}$ in $J/ψ$ and $B$ decays.

## Key claim
Within a chiral constituent quark model that includes π annihilation and one-gluon annihilation, the authors find no S-wave nucleon-antinucleon bound state, contrary to the interpretation of a threshold enhancement in J/ψ and B decays as a resonance. This conclusion depends on the assumption that the chiral constituent quark model is suitable for the N̄N system and that multi-π channel coupling and hidden color channel coupling effects can be neglected.

## Method
The authors extended a chiral constituent quark model, previously used for NN interactions and baryon spectra, to the N̄N system by adding effective potentials for π annihilation and one-gluon annihilation between quarks and antiquarks. They fixed all model parameters from baryon spectrum and NN scattering data, leaving only the one-gluon annihilation coupling constant α'_s adjustable. Using the resonating group method (RGM) with the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variational method, they calculated S-wave elastic cross sections for p̄p and solved for possible bound states in all four S-wave N̄N channels (IJ=11, 10, 01, 00).

## Key numbers
- The quark mass m_q is taken as 313 MeV and the σ meson mass m_σ as 3.421 fm⁻¹.
- The gluon exchange coupling constant α_s is 0.485, determined from the Δ-N mass difference.
- With α'_s = α_s/3 (approximately 0.162), including π annihilation, the theoretical p̄p S-wave elastic cross section agrees with experimental data to within 5 mb.
- With α'_s = α_s/0.9 (approximately 0.539), excluding π annihilation, the cross section also reproduces experimental data.
- The deuteron binding energy B_D from the model is 2.0 MeV, with root-mean-square radius √⟨r²⟩ = 1.96 fm and D-state probability P_D = 4.86%.
- No bound state or resonant state below the system mass of 3.9 GeV was found in the dynamical calculation for any S-wave N̄N channel.

## Context
This work extends the chiral constituent quark model, previously successful for baryon spectra and NN interactions (Salamanca group, 1990s), to the N̄N system by incorporating annihilation effects. It contradicts earlier traditional meson-exchange studies (Dover, 1990s) that found many bound states when annihilation was neglected, and it directly addresses the interpretation of threshold enhancements observed by BES (2003) in J/ψ → γp̄p and by Belle (2003) in B decays as possible S-wave bound states. The results enable further study of whether multi-π channel coupling or hidden color channel effects, omitted here, could produce such bound states.
