# Pygmy and core polarization dipole modes in $^{206}$Pb: connecting nuclear structure to stellar nucleosynthesis

> A high-resolution study of the electromagnetic response of $^{206}$Pb below the neutron separation energy is performed using a ($\vecγ$,$γ'$) experiment at the HI$\vecγ$S facility. Nuclear resonance fluorescence with 100% linearly polarized photon beams is used to measure spins, parities, branching ratios, and decay widths of excited states in $^{206}$Pb from $4.9$ to $8.1$MeV. The extracted $Σ$$B$(E1)$\uparrow$ and $Σ$$B$(M1)$\uparrow$ values for the total electric and magnetic dipole strength below the neutron separation energy are 0.9$\pm$0.2e$^2$fm$^2$ and 8.3$\pm$2.0$μ_{N}^2$, respectively. These measurements are found to be in very good agreement with the predictions from an energy-density functional (EDF) plus quasiparticle phonon model (QPM). Such a detailed theoretical analysis allows to separate the pygmy dipole resonance from both the tail of the giant dipole resonance and multi-phonon excitations. Combined with earlier photonuclear experiments above the neutron separation energy, one extracts a value for the electric dipole polarizability of $^{206}$Pb of $α_{D}\!=\!122\pm10$mb/MeV. When compared to predictions from both the EDF+QPM and accurately calibrated relativistic EDFs, one deduces a range for the neutron-skin thickness of $R_{\rm skin}^{206}\!=\!0.12$-$0.19$fm and a corresponding range for the slope of the symmetry energy of $L\!=\!48$-$60$MeV. This newly obtained information is also used to estimate the Maxwellian-averaged radiative cross section $^{205}$Pb(n,$γ$)$^{206}$Pb at 30keV to be $σ\!=\!130\!\pm\!25$mb. The astrophysical impact of this measurement--on both the s-process in stellar nucleosynthesis and on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter--is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by combining high-resolution measurements of the dipole response in $^{206}$Pb below the neutron separation energy with theoretical modeling, one can separate the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) from the tail of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) and multi-phonon excitations, and then use this information to extract a range for the neutron-skin thickness and the slope of the symmetry energy, but only when the low-energy data are combined with earlier photonuclear experiments above the neutron separation energy.

## Method

Nuclear resonance fluorescence (NRF) with 100% linearly polarized photon beams from the HI$\vec{\gamma}$S facility was used to measure spins, parities, branching ratios, and decay widths of excited states in $^{206}$Pb from 4.9 to 8.1 MeV. Six HPGe detectors arranged at 90 degrees and 135 degrees to the incident beam distinguished E1, M1, and E2 multipolarities. The experimental dipole strength distributions were compared to predictions from an energy-density functional (EDF) plus quasiparticle phonon model (QPM) that included coupling to one-, two-, and three-phonon states.

## Key numbers

* Total electric dipole strength below the neutron separation energy: $\Sigma B(E1)\uparrow = 0.9 \pm 0.2$ e$^2$fm$^2$
* Total magnetic dipole strength below the neutron separation energy: $\Sigma B(M1)\uparrow = 8.3 \pm 2.0$ $\mu_N^2$
* Electric dipole polarizability of $^{206}$Pb: $\alpha_D = 122 \pm 10$ mb/MeV
* Neutron-skin thickness range for $^{206}$Pb: $R_{\rm skin}^{206} = 0.12$-$0.19$ fm
* Corresponding range for the slope of the symmetry energy: $L = 48$-$60$ MeV
* Maxwellian-averaged radiative cross section $^{205}$Pb(n,$\gamma$)$^{206}$Pb at 30 keV: $\sigma = 130 \pm 25$ mb
* Sensitivity limit for a single E1 transition: $\sim 5 \times 10^{-4}$ e$^2$fm$^2$
* Sensitivity limit for a single M1 transition: $\sim 10^{-2}$ $\mu_N^2$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the pygmy dipole resonance in lead isotopes, particularly those by Enders et al. (2003) and Ryezayeva et al. (2002), by using a fully self-consistent EDF+QPM approach rather than the less elaborate QPM calculations of Enders et al. (2003). The measured electric dipole polarizability provides a complementary constraint on the neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb to that from the PREX experiment (Abrahamyan et al., 2012), and the experimentally constrained neutron capture cross section for $^{205}$Pb(n,$\gamma$)$^{206}$Pb puts the s-process nucleosynthesis modeling of the extinct radionuclide $^{205}$Pb on much more solid ground.
