# Analytical Solution and Lie Algebra of the Relativistic Boltzmann Equation

> In this work, we present a novel and more efficient approach to constructing the relativistic BKW (Bobylev, Krook, and Wu) solution. By introducing a class of ansatz functions for the distribution function, we demonstrate that within this specific ansatz space, only the equilibrium and BKW-type forms yield exact solutions to the nonlinear Boltzmann equation. Furthermore, guided by physical insight and drawing upon the framework of relativistic kinetic theory, we derive the Lie algebra of invariant transformations admitted by the relativistic Boltzmann equation. From this algebra, the corresponding symmetry group transformations can be systematically constructed.

# Analytical Solution and Lie Algebra of the Relativistic Boltzmann Equation

## Key claim

Within a specific ansatz space of distribution functions parametrized as an exponential times a polynomial in momentum, only the equilibrium form (n=0) and the BKW-type form (n=1) yield exact solutions to the nonlinear relativistic Boltzmann equation for massless particles with constant cross-section, under the assumptions of spatial homogeneity and momentum isotropy.

## Method

By analytically evaluating the collision integral in a transformed phase-space representation using momentum-transfer variables, they match powers of ¥( p^0 ¥) on both sides of the equation, obtaining a system of ordinary differential equations for the coefficients ¥( A_0(¥tau), A_1(¥tau) ¥), and ¥( ¥alpha(¥tau) ¥). The system is solved using the conserved particle number density and energy density as constraints. Separately, the authors derive the Lie algebra of invariant transformations admitted by the relativistic Boltzmann equation by analyzing symmetry generators.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic BKW solution originally discovered by Bobylev (1976) and Krook and Wu (1976) to the relativistic regime using a more efficient method than the conventional moment approach used by Bazow (2015) and Bazow (2016). The authors also build on the recent relativistic BKW solution with anisotropic scattering cross sections by Hu (2024). The Lie algebra analysis fills a gap in relativistic kinetic theory, where systematic symmetry analysis of the Boltzmann equation had been lacking. The results enable systematic construction of new exact solutions via symmetry group transformations and provide a benchmark for testing numerical Boltzmann solvers and moment methods in far-from-equilibrium regimes.
