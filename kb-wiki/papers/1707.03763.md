# Prolate over oblate dominance in deformed nuclei as a consequence of the SU(3) symmetry and the Pauli principle

> We show that the dominance of prolate over oblate shapes in even-even deformed nuclei can be derived from the SU(3) symmetry and the Pauli principle.

# Prolate over oblate dominance in deformed nuclei as a consequence of the SU(3) symmetry and the Pauli principle

## Key claim
The paper argues that the observed dominance of prolate over oblate ground-state deformations in even-even deformed nuclei can be derived from the SU(3) symmetry (approximately recovered in higher shells) combined with the Pauli principle, but only after substituting opposite-parity orbitals invading a shell from above with normal-parity orbitals that have moved to the shell below.

## Method
The author restores approximate SU(3) symmetry in higher harmonic-oscillator shells (beyond the sd shell) by replacing intruder opposite-parity orbitals with normal-parity orbitals from the shell below, keeping angular momentum projections unchanged while reducing the total oscillator quantum number N and the z-direction quantum number n_z by one. The highest-weight irreducible representation (λ, μ) of SU(3) for a given number of valence protons and neutrons is then determined using tabulations or a computational code, with prolate shapes corresponding to λ > μ and oblate shapes to λ < μ. The total highest-weight irrep for a nucleus is obtained by summing the proton and neutron irreps (λ_p + λ_n, μ_p + μ_n).

## Key numbers
- The prolate-to-oblate shape/phase transition in W, Os, and Pt isotopes occurs at N = 116, with the first oblate isotopes appearing at N = 116.
- ¹⁹⁰W, ¹⁹²Os, and ¹⁹⁴Pt are N = 116 isotones, all lying at the predicted transition point.
- For rare earths with 50–82 protons and 50–82 neutrons, the prolate-to-oblate transition occurs at N = 72 in the W, Os, and Pt isotopic chains.
- The particle-hole symmetry is valid only up to 4 particles or 4 holes in shells higher than the sd shell.

## Context
This work addresses a question raised in a review by Hamamoto and Mottelson (2017), who noted that the near-complete dominance of prolate over oblate deformations in deformed even-even nuclei was not yet adequately understood. The paper extends the SU(3) symmetry framework of Elliott (1958) from the sd shell to higher shells by using an approximate symmetry that is complementary to the pseudo-SU(3) symmetry of Arima, Harvey, and Shimizu (1969) and Ginocchio (1978). The results enable predictions of prolate-to-oblate transitions in isotopic chains and agree with self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations by Sarriguren (2015) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Robledo (2015) and Nomura (2016), as well as with experimental data on ¹⁹⁰W, ¹⁹²Os, ¹⁹⁴Os, ¹⁹⁸Os, and the chain ¹⁸⁰Hf–²⁰⁰Hg studied by Linnemann (2005).
