# Two programs for studying stellar evolution and nuclear astrophysics

> Two FORTRAN programs are presented which plot the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram and the temporal evolution of such stellar quantities as: central and photospheric isotopic abundances, central densities and temperatures, luminosities,effective temperatures and photospheric radii for a wide range of stellar masses. The programs, which are modifications and extensions of some modules of the TYCHO 6.0 stellar evolution package, are accompanied by various control input files as well as by a library of data. The data library is actually one of the output files generated by TYCHO 7.0 (a modified version of TYCHO 6.0), while the plots themselves are generated by the PGPLOT Graphics Subroutine Library which is also publicly available and well cited.

## Key claim

Two FORTRAN programs, hrtemp and hrplot, are presented as modifications and extensions of modules from the TYCHO 6.0 stellar evolution package, enabling users to plot the temporal evolution of stellar quantities and Hertzsprung-Russell diagrams from pre-generated data libraries, with the caveat that the quality of plots in strongly screened domains (e.g., white-dwarf progenitor phases) may not be satisfactory because the equation of state needs adjustment for strong screening effects.

## Method

The programs read data from hr.prefix files generated by TYCHO 7.0 (a modified version of TYCHO 6.0), which contain time-step data including central and photospheric isotopic abundances, central densities and temperatures, luminosities, effective temperatures, and photospheric radii. Hrtemp plots the temporal evolution of these quantities using the PGPLOT Graphics Subroutine Library, while hrplot generates Hertzsprung-Russell diagrams with the added capability of depicting evolutionary lifetimes. Both programs use control input files (hrtemp.in and hrplot.in) to specify display device, number of sequences, data files, and plot limits.

## Key numbers

- The hr.prefix data files are roughly 5MB to 10MB in size
- The mixing length parameter used in simulations is set to 1.8
- All hr.prefix files were generated for a composition of X=0.7, Y=0.28, Z=0.02
- Test runs were performed for 1 solar mass, 3 solar mass, and 5 solar mass stars
- After log(t)=16 (time in seconds) the 3 solar mass star begins its ascent up the Asymptotic Giant Branch
- The TYCHO 6.0 reaction network for small temperatures (T less than 10^7 K) consists of only 29 isotopes
- The hrplot.in example shows evolutionary lifetime points at coordinates: (logT=4.06, logL=2.028), (logT=4.029, logL=2.153), and (logT=3.688, logL=1.918)

## Context

This work extends the TYCHO 6.0 stellar evolution code (developed by the University of Arizona astrophysics group and Arnett) by adding temporal evolution plotting capabilities that were previously absent, as typical HR diagrams and static composition profiles lack temporal information. The programs enable users to study the evolution of stars across a wide range of masses using pre-generated data libraries, with the author independently evolving the code as TYCHO 7.0 to include screening effects in thermonuclear reaction rates (based on Mitler (1977)) and additional auxiliary programs, while a parallel effort by the University of Arizona group produced TYCHO 6.11 with different improvements.
