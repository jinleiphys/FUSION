# From first to second minimum: Parity-dependent level densities in $^{240,242}$Pu

> We calculate the parity-dependent level density ratios for $^{240,242}$Pu across a broad range of quadrupole deformations, from the spherical configuration up to the superdeformed region, explicitly including both the ground-state minimum and the second minimum (fission isomer). The parity-equilibration energy, defined as the excitation energy at which positive- and negative-parity level densities approach equilibrium, is compared between configurations. A significant reduction is observed near the second minimum, indicating a faster equilibration process in this region.

## Key claim

The parity equilibration energy (the excitation energy at which positive- and negative-parity level densities approach equilibrium) is significantly reduced near the second minimum (fission isomer) compared to the ground-state minimum in $^{240,242}$Pu, indicating faster parity equilibration at large deformation; this reduction correlates with shell correction minima rather than with the total potential-energy minima.

## Method

The authors used a superfluid model based on finite-temperature BCS theory with Nilsson-model single-particle energies to calculate parity-projected nuclear level densities for $^{240,242}$Pu over quadrupole deformations $\beta_2 \in [0, 1.25]$. Parity projection was performed using a Poisson-distribution method that separates single-particle levels into positive- and negative-parity groups, from which the ratio $R = \rho^-/\rho^+$ was computed. The equilibration energy $E_{eq}$ was defined as the excitation energy where $R(U) = 0.98$, extracted by fitting the calculated ratios to the function $R(U) = \tanh(\gamma U)$.

## Key numbers

* For $^{240,242}$Pu, the ratio $R = \rho^-/\rho^+$ was calculated over quadrupole deformations $\beta_2 \in [0, 1.25]$ at excitation energies $U = 0.5{-}20$ MeV.
* The equilibration energy $E_{eq}$ is defined at the threshold $R(U) = 0.98$, chosen to avoid temporary overshoots where $R > 1$.
* The equilibration energies for $^{240,242}$Pu are systematically smaller than those obtained for lighter Mo and Dy isotopes at their ground-state deformations, consistent with the mass-number dependence reported by Singhal (2011).
* A pronounced reduction of $E_{eq}$ is observed at the second minimum compared to the ground-state deformation for both isotopes.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parity-dependent level densities that were restricted to ground-state deformations (Rauscher 1997, Mengoni 1986, Pichon 1994) by examining actinide nuclei with multiple deformation minima. The results connect to earlier shell-model Monte Carlo calculations (Ozen 2007, Alhassid 1999) and quasi-particle methods (Mocelj 2007) that highlighted the role of single-particle structure and pairing on parity equilibration. The findings may provide input for evaluating isomer population probabilities against prompt fission probabilities in Pu isotopes, as discussed by Goerlach (1978).
