# EoS's of different phases of dense quark matter

> Compact stars with significant high densities in their interiors can give rise to quark deconfined phases that can open a window for the study of strongly interacting dense nuclear matter. Recent observations on the mass of two pulsars, PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, have posed a great restriction on their composition, since their equations of state must be hard enough to support masses of about at least two solar masses. The onset of quarks tends to soften the equation of state, but due to their strong interactions, different phases can be realized with new parameters that affect the corresponding equations of state and ultimately the mass-radius relationships. In this paper I will review how the equation of state of dense quark matter is affected by the physical characteristics of the phases that can take place at different baryonic densities with and without the presence of a magnetic field, as well as their connection with the corresponding mass-radius relationship, which can be derived by using different models.

## Key claim

This review argues that the MIT bag model's ability to produce stiff equations of state (EoS) by arbitrarily increasing the pairing gap is misleading, because the more self-consistent Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model shows that increasing the diquark coupling actually softens the EoS, making it difficult to reach the observed mass of about two solar masses for pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432.

## Method

The paper reviews EoS calculations for dense quark matter in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase using both the MIT bag model and the NJL model, comparing how the pairing gap is treated in each. It then extends the NJL analysis to include gluon degrees of freedom (which acquire Debye and Meissner masses in the CFL phase) and to incorporate a strong magnetic field, which splits the pressure into parallel and perpendicular components. The mass-radius (M-R) relation is obtained by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for isotropic cases, while the anisotropic magnetized case is discussed as an open problem.

## Key numbers

* Pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has a mass M = 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses, measured via Shapiro delay (Demorest).
* In the NJL model, changing the diquark coupling constant from G = 4.32 to 7.10 GeV⁻² yields gap parameters at μ = 500 MeV that change from Δ = 10 to 100 MeV.
* The dynamically calculated bag constant B₀ = 57.3 MeV/fm³ is used in the gauged-NJL model with gluons.
* For the magnetized CFL (MCFL) phase at a field of ~10¹⁸ G, the shift in energy density relative to zero field is ~200 MeV/fm³ for the same pressure in the parallel-pressure case.
* Gluon contributions decrease the maximum mass for each sequence by up to 20%, and sequences including gluons cannot reach 2 solar masses if G_V/G_S < 0.2.

## Context

This review extends work comparing the MIT bag model and NJL model for CFL quark matter (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2015) and incorporates gluon effects (Ferrer, de la Incera, and Paulucci, 2015) that were previously neglected in zero-temperature NJL treatments. It also addresses the anisotropy introduced by strong magnetic fields in the MCFL phase (Paulucci, Ferrer, and de la Incera, 2011), which prevents direct application of the standard TOV equations. The paper enables future work on hybrid stars and other quark phases (such as 2SC or magnetized DCDW phases) that may be realized at the moderate densities near the hadron-quark transition.
