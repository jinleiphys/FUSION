# New treatment of breakup continuum in the method of continuum discretized coupled channels

> A new method of pseudo-state discretization is proposed for the method of continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) to deal with three-body breakup processes. We propose real- and complex-range Gaussian bases for the pseudo-state wave functions, and show that they form in good approximation a complete set in the configuration space which is important for breakup processes. Continuous S-matrix elements are derived with the approximate completeness from discrete ones calculated by CDCC. Accuracy of the method is tested quantitatively for two realistic examples, d+$^{58}$Ni scattering at 80 MeV and $^{6}$Li+$^{40}$Ca scattering at 156 MeV with the satisfactory results. Possibility of application of the method to four-body breakup processes is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new pseudo-state discretization method for continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) that uses real- and complex-range Gaussian basis functions to approximate a complete set in the configuration space important for breakup processes, enabling the derivation of continuous S-matrix elements from discrete ones without assuming any a priori form for the k-distribution.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the projectile internal Hamiltonian in a space spanned by either real-range Gaussian functions (r^ℓ exp[-(r/a_j)^2]) or complex-range Gaussian functions (r^ℓ exp[-(1+ib)(r/a_j)^2]) to obtain discretized pseudo-states representing the breakup continuum. They then use an interpolation formula S^(J)_ℓL(k) ≈ Σ_i f^PS_iℓ(k) Ŝ_γ,γ₀, where f^PS_iℓ(k) is the overlap between the exact continuum state and the discretized pseudo-state, to reconstruct continuous breakup S-matrix elements from the discrete CDCC results. The method is tested by comparing with converged average-method calculations for d+⁵⁸Ni at 80 MeV and ⁶Li+⁴⁰Ca at 156 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For d+⁵⁸Ni at 80 MeV: model space with ℓ=0,2 and k_max=1.3 fm⁻¹; convergence achieved with 18 breakup channels for real-range Gaussian basis and 16 (s-wave) and 17 (d-wave) for complex-range Gaussian basis
- For d+⁵⁸Ni: real-range Gaussian basis parameters a₁=1.0 fm, a_n=30.0 fm, n=30; complex-range Gaussian basis parameters a₁=1.0 fm, a_n=20.0 fm, 2n=40, b=π/2
- For ⁶Li+⁴⁰Ca at 156 MeV: model space with k_max=2.0 fm⁻¹ and ℓ_max=2; convergence with 21 s-wave and 22 d-wave breakup channels
- For ⁶Li+⁴⁰Ca: resonance parameters ε_res=2.96 MeV and Γ=0.62 MeV for the d-wave resonance
- For ⁶Li+⁴⁰Ca: the resonance peak expressed by only 8 breakup channels in complex-range Gaussian PS method versus 30 bins in the average method

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-state discretization approach previously applied to virtual breakup processes (PS1, PS2) to real breakup reactions, and builds on the average and midpoint discretization methods established in earlier CDCC work (Yahiro 1986, Sakuragi 1986). The method enables more efficient treatment of breakup channels than the average method, requiring fewer channels (e.g., 8 vs 30 for the ⁶Li resonance case), and the authors discuss its potential extension to four-body breakup reactions where the average method would require exact three-body wave functions that are impossible to obtain.
