# Combined analysis of the $־³n \to K^0־£^0$ and $־³n \to K^+־£^-$ reactions

> The recently released data on differential cross sections for $־³n \to K^0־£^0$ from the A2 and BGOOD Collaborations are used to examine the theoretical model constructed in our previous work [Phys. Rev. D \textbf{105}, 094017 (2022)] for $־³n \to K^+־£^-$, and it is found that the model predictions are able to qualitatively reproduce the A2 data but fail to describe the BGOOD data. Then, a combined analysis of the $־³n \to K^0־£^0$ and $־³n \to K^+־£^-$ reactions is performed to revise the theoretical model. Due to the inconsistency problem, the A2 and BGOOD data are included in fits separately. In the case of including the A2 data, both the data for $־³n \to K^0־£^0$ and $־³n \to K^+־£^-$ can be fairly well described, and the contributions from the $N(1710)1/2^+$, $N(1880)1/2^+$, $N(1900)3/2^+$, and $־”(1920)3/2^+$ resonances are found to dominate the reactions in the lower energy region. While in the case of including the BGOOD data, although most of the data for the $־³n \to K^+ ־£^-$ reaction can be described with the exception of some noticeable discrepancies on beam asymmetries at lower energies, the BGOOD data for $־³n \to K^0־£^0$ can be only qualitatively described, and the contributions from the $N(1710)1/2^+$, $N(1900)3/2^+$, and $־”(1910)1/2^+$ resonances are found to dominate the reactions in the lower energy region. In both cases, the $t$-channel $K^\ast(892)$ exchange is found to play a crucial role at forward angles in the higher energy region. Further precise measurements of data for $־³n \to K^0־£^0$ are called on to disentangle the discrepancies between the data sets from the A2 and BGOOD Collaborations.

## Key claim
The theoretical model previously constructed for the $\gamma n \to K^+\Sigma^-$ reaction can qualitatively reproduce the A2 data for $\gamma n \to K^0\Sigma^0$ but fails to describe the BGOOD data for the same reaction, and due to the inconsistency between these two data sets, the A2 and BGOOD data must be included in separate fits rather than a combined fit.

## Method
The authors performed a combined analysis of the $\gamma n \to K^0\Sigma^0$ and $\gamma n \to K^+\Sigma^-$ reactions using an effective Lagrangian approach in the Bonn approximation with tree-level amplitudes. The model included $s$-channel nucleon and $\Delta$ resonance exchanges, $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast(892)$ exchanges, $u$-channel $\Sigma$ exchange, and an interaction current to ensure gauge invariance. Due to discrepancies between the A2 and BGOOD data sets, two separate fits were performed: fit A using A2 data for $\gamma n \to K^0\Sigma^0$ and all available data for $\gamma n \to K^+\Sigma^-$, and fit B using BGOOD data for $\gamma n \to K^0\Sigma^0$ and all available data for $\gamma n \to K^+\Sigma^-$.

## Key numbers
- Fit A achieved a $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom of $3.21$ with $N = 828$ data points.
- In fit A, the $N(1710)1/2^+$ resonance was fitted with mass $1692 \pm 1$ MeV and width $94 \pm 1$ MeV; the $N(1880)1/2^+$ with mass $1860 \pm 1$ MeV and width $400 \pm 2$ MeV; the $N(1900)3/2^+$ with mass $1931 \pm 1$ MeV and width $141 \pm 2$ MeV; and the $\Delta(1920)3/2^+$ with mass $1870 \pm 1$ MeV and width $360 \pm 1$ MeV.
- In fit B, the $N(1710)1/2^+$ was fitted with mass $1692 \pm 1$ MeV and width $200 \pm 1$ MeV; the $N(1900)3/2^+$ with mass $1890 \pm 1$ MeV and width $235 \pm 3$ MeV; and the $\Delta(1910)1/2^+$ with mass $1850 \pm 1$ MeV and width $200 \pm 1$ MeV.
- The A2 data for $\gamma n \to K^0\Sigma^0$ showed a maximum differential cross section of approximately $0.14$ $\mu$b/sr, while the BGOOD data remained below $0.4$ $\mu$b/sr.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of $\gamma n \to K^+\Sigma^-$ (Wei, 2022) by incorporating newly released differential cross section data for $\gamma n \to K^0\Sigma^0$ from the A2 Collaboration (2019) and BGOOD Collaboration (2021). It contradicts the findings of an independent isobar model analysis (Byd, 2021) which reached different conclusions about resonance contents and contributions for the same $\gamma n \to K^+\Sigma^-$ reaction. The combined analysis enables better determination of resonance hadronic and electromagnetic couplings in $K\Sigma$ photoproduction off neutron, but the discrepancies between A2 and BGOOD data sets call for further precise measurements to resolve the inconsistencies.
