# Solutions of the bound state Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in three dimensions by using NN and 3N potential models

> A recently developed three-dimensional approach (without partial-wave decomposition) is considered to investigate solutions of Faddeev-Yakubovsky integral equations in momentum space for three- and four-body bound states, with the inclusion of three-body forces. In the calculations of the binding energies, spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential models (named, S$_{3}$, MT-I/III, YS-type and P$_{5.5}$GL) are considered along with the scalar two-meson exchange three-body potential. Good agreement of the presently reported results with the ones obtained by other techniques are obtained, demonstrating the advantage of an approach in which the formalism is much more simplified and easy to manage for direct computation.

# Solutions of the bound state Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in three dimensions by using NN and 3N potential models

## Key claim

The three-dimensional (3D) approach to Faddeev-Yakubovsky integral equations, which avoids partial-wave decomposition, yields three- and four-nucleon binding energies in good agreement with partial-wave-based methods for several spin-dependent NN potential models (S3, MT-I/III, YS-type, P5.5GL) and with inclusion of a scalar two-meson exchange three-body force, while the formalism is "much more simplified and easy to manage for direct computation", though the authors acknowledge that the 3D approach trades algebraic simplicity for higher-dimensional integral equations and potentially larger matrices than partial-wave calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Faddeev-Yakubovsky integral equations in momentum space for three- and four-nucleon bound states using a three-dimensional (non-partial-wave) representation with vector Jacobi momenta as continuous variables. They employed spin-dependent and spin-averaged versions of four NN potential models (S3, MT-I/III, YS-type, P5.5GL) and, for the MT-I/III averaged case, added two scalar two-meson exchange three-nucleon force models (MT3-I and MT3-II). The coupled integral equations were discretized and solved numerically, with results compared to binding energies from variational (VAR), hyperspherical harmonic (HHE, HH), separable kernel finite rank (SKFR), integrodifferential equation (SIDE, IDEA), direct Faddeev-Yakubovsky (DFY), and partial-wave Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY(PW)) methods.

## Key numbers

- For the S3 potential (spin-dependent): triton binding energy = -8.20 MeV, alpha-particle binding energy = -28.8 MeV; for the spin-averaged version: triton = -6.41 MeV, alpha = -25.7 MeV.
- For the YS-I potential: triton = -11.05 MeV, alpha = -45.9 MeV; YS-II: triton = -10.70 MeV, alpha = -44.4 MeV; YS-III: triton = -10.13 MeV, alpha = -42.4 MeV; YS-IV: triton = -8.47 MeV, alpha = -37.8 MeV.
- For the MT-I/III potential (spin-dependent): triton = -8.54 MeV, alpha = -30.3 MeV; spin-averaged: triton = -7.55 MeV, alpha = -28.8 MeV.
- For the P5.5GL potential: triton = -8.04 MeV, alpha = -28.9 MeV.
- With MT-I/III averaged plus MT3-I three-body force: triton = -8.68 MeV, alpha = -35.7 MeV; with MT3-II: triton = -8.45 MeV, alpha = -34.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the 3D Faddeev-Yakubovsky formalism previously developed by Bayegan et al. (2008) to additional NN potential models (S3, YS-type, P5.5GL) not previously tested in the 3D approach, and includes three-nucleon forces for the first time in this framework. The results agree with partial-wave Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations by Kamada et al. (1992) and with other techniques such as the direct Faddeev-Yakubovsky method by Schellingerhout et al. (1993) and the 2DI method by Gibson et al. (1977). The approach enables future applications to three-nucleon scattering and photodisintegration at higher energies where partial-wave methods converge slowly, and can be extended to atomic and molecular few-body systems.
