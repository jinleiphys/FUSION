# Towards the determination of CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings

> The nucleon matrix elements (NMEs) associated with quark chromo-magnetic dipole moments (cMDMs) play a crucial role in determining the CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings induced by quark chromo-electric dipole moments. In recent years, it has been argued that the NMEs of cMDMs can be related to the third moment of the nucleon's higher-twist (specifically, twist-three) parton distribution function (PDF) $e(x)$, which can, in principle, be measured through dihadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes. By applying the spin-flavor expansion to the cMDM operators in the large-$N_c$ limit, where $N_c$ is the number of quark colors, we show that the NMEs receive contributions not only from the twist-three PDF $e(x)$ but also from an additional, previously neglected nucleon form factor. Incorporating constraints from the spin-flavor expansion, recent experimental data on $e(x)$, as well as model calculations of $e(x)$, we estimate the NMEs of the cMDM operators. Our analysis indicates that the NMEs are dominated by the nucleon form factors, and the cMDM contributions to pion-nucleon couplings can be comparable to those from the quark sigma terms.

## Key claim

The nucleon matrix elements (NMEs) of quark chromo-magnetic dipole moments (cMDMs), which are essential for determining CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings induced by quark chromo-electric dipole moments, receive dominant contributions from previously neglected nucleon form factors rather than from the twist-three parton distribution function \(e(x)\) alone, making the cMDM contributions to pion-nucleon couplings potentially comparable to those from the quark sigma terms.

## Method

The authors apply the spin-flavor expansion to the cMDM operators in the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD, where \(N_c\) is the number of quark colors, to show that the NMEs receive contributions from both the twist-three PDF \(e(x)\) and an additional nucleon form factor \(B^q\). They then incorporate constraints from the spin-flavor expansion, recent experimental data on \(e(x)\) from the CLAS Collaboration, and model calculations of \(e(x)\) to estimate the NMEs of the cMDM operators and compare their contributions to the CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings with those from the quark sigma terms.

## Key numbers

* The isoscalar sigma term \(\sigma^0\) is 60.9 MeV (for \(N_f=2+1+1\)) or 42.2 MeV (for \(N_f=2+1\)), and the isovector sigma term \(\sigma^3\) is 2.74 MeV, from the FLAG 2024 results.
* The vacuum condensate ratio \(r\) is estimated as \(0.4 \pm 0.05\) GeV\(^2\) from QCD sum rules.
* The pion decay constant \(F_\pi\) is 92.2 MeV.
* The isoscalar cMDM form factor \(B^{(u+d)}\) scales as \(O(N_c)\) and the isovector \(B^{(u-d)}\) scales as \(O(N_c^0)\), with the \(u\) and \(d\) contributions to the matrix elements equal to one another up to approximately 30% corrections.
* The third moment of the twist-3 PDF \(e^q(x)\) is dominated by the pure twist-three contribution, with the quark mass term contribution approximately \(10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Seng (2018), which related cMDM NMEs to the twist-3 PDF \(e(x)\) and neglected the form factor \(B^q\), by showing that the spin-flavor expansion in the large-\(N_c\) limit requires nonzero \(B^q\) contributions. The analysis incorporates updated experimental data from the CLAS Collaboration and model calculations of \(e(x)\), and it enables more accurate predictions for CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings needed for interpreting neutron and atomic electric dipole moment searches. The results are consistent with the scaling analysis of Samart (2016) for the parity- and time-reversal-violating two-nucleon potential.
