# Solar neutrino physics: Sensitivity to light dark matter particles

> Neutrinos are produced in several neutrino nuclear reactions of the proton-proton chain and carbon-nitrogen-oxygen cycle that take place at different radius of the Sun's core. Hence, measurements of solar neutrino fluxes provide a precise determination of the local temperature. The accumulation of non-annihilating light dark matter particles (with masses between 5 GeV and 16 GeV in the Sun produces a change in the local solar structure, namely, a decrease in the central temperature of a few percent. This variation depends on the properties of the dark matter particles, such as the mass of the particle and its spin-independent scattering cross-section on baryon-nuclei, specifically, the scattering with helium, oxygen, and nitrogen among other heavy elements. This temperature effect can be measured in almost all solar neutrino fluxes. In particular, by comparing the neutrino fluxes generated by stellar models with current observations, namely 8B neutrino fluxes, we find that non-annihilating dark matter particles with a mass smaller than 10 GeV and a spin-independent scattering cross-section with heavy baryon-nuclei larger than 3 x 10^{-37} cm^-2 produce a variation in the 8B neutrino fluxes that would be in conflict with current measurements.

## Key claim

Non-annihilating light dark matter particles with masses below 10 GeV and spin-independent scattering cross-sections on heavy baryon-nuclei larger than 3 x 10^{-37} cm^2 produce a variation in the 8B neutrino flux that would conflict with current measurements, assuming combined experimental and theoretical uncertainties of about 15%.

## Method

The authors computed solar evolution models using a modified version of the one-dimensional stellar evolution code CESAM, incorporating dark matter capture and energy transport. For each set of dark matter parameters (particle mass between 5 and 16 GeV, spin-independent cross-section between 10^{-41} and 10^{-34} cm^2, and spin-dependent cross-section fixed at 10^{-40} cm^2), a solar-calibrated model was obtained by adjusting helium abundance and mixing length until luminosity and radius matched present solar values within 10^{-5}. The resulting neutrino fluxes were compared to the solar standard model and to experimental measurements from SNO, Borexino, and other detectors.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter particle mass range considered: 5 GeV to 16 GeV
- Spin-independent scattering cross-section range considered: 10^{-41} cm^2 to 10^{-34} cm^2
- Excluded region: dark matter particles with mass less than or equal to 11 GeV and spin-independent cross-section greater than or equal to 3 x 10^{-37} cm^2, based on 8B flux variation exceeding 15%
- 8B neutrino flux dependence on central temperature: proportional to T_c^{24}
- 7Be neutrino flux dependence on central temperature: proportional to T_c^{10}
- pep neutrino flux dependence on central temperature: proportional to T_c^{-2.4}
- SNO measured 8B flux (no oscillations): 5.05^{+0.19}_{-0.20} x 10^6 cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- Borexino measured 8B flux: 5.88 ± 0.65 x 10^6 cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- Borexino measured 7Be flux: 4.87 ± 0.24 x 10^9 cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- Borexino measured pep flux: 1.6 ± 0.3 x 10^8 cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- 8B flux variation of 15% corresponds to a central temperature variation ΔT_c/T_c of 0.6%
- Excluded region corresponds to 7Be flux variation ≥ 6.25% or pep flux variation ≤ -1.5%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter effects on solar structure (Gould 1987; Spergel and Press 1985) and solar neutrino fluxes (Lopes and Silk 2010; Taoso et al. 2010; Cumberbatch et al. 2010) by systematically exploring the parameter space of light non-annihilating dark matter particles with masses between 5 and 16 GeV. The results enable future neutrino experiments such as LENA (expected 1% precision on 8B flux) to constrain or exclude dark matter candidates in the mass and cross-section range suggested by DAMA/LIBRA and CoGeNT experiments under isospin-violating interpretations.
