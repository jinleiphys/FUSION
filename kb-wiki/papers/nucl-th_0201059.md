# Hadronic physics in peripheral heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the production of hadronic resonances in very peripheral heavy ion collisions, where the ions collide with impact parameter larger than twice the nuclear radius and remain intact after the collision. We compare the resonance production through two-photon and double Pomeron exchange, showing that when we impose the condition for a peripheral interaction the $γγ$ process dominates over the Pomeron interaction, due to the short range propagation of this last one. We also discuss the observation of light resonances through the subprocess $γγ\to R \to γγ$, which is a clean signal for glueball candidates as well as one way to check the existence of a possible scalar $σ$ meson.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in very peripheral heavy ion collisions (impact parameter larger than twice the nuclear radius), two-photon fusion dominates over double Pomeron exchange for resonance production, but this dominance is conditional on using very heavy ions; for lighter ions the Pomeron contribution becomes comparable or dominant.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections for hadronic resonance production via two-photon (γγ) and double Pomeron (PP) exchange in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. They use the equivalent-photon (Weizsäcker-Williams) approximation with a realistic photon distribution from Cahn and Jackson, and a Donnachie-Landshoff model for the Pomeron. They enforce the peripheral condition through a geometrical cut (b > 2R_A) and also through a Glauber absorption factor, then compare the resulting rates for various resonances and for the continuum γγ → γγ process.

## Key numbers

- For π⁰ production at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV/nucleon, ²³⁸U ions): σ_γγ = 7.1 mbarn, σ_PP = 0.05 mbarn (geometrical cut)
- For π⁰ production at LHC (√s = 6300 GeV/nucleon, ²⁰⁶Pb ions): σ_γγ = 40 mbarn
- For η production at RHIC: σ_γγ = 1.5 mbarn, σ_PP = 0.038 mbarn
- For η_c production at RHIC: σ_γγ = 0.32 × 10⁻² mbarn, σ_PP = 0.47 × 10⁻⁴ mbarn
- For η_b production at LHC: σ_γγ = 0.37 × 10⁻³ mbarn, σ_PP = 0.11 × 10⁻¹⁰ mbarn
- Ratio of Glauber-absorption to geometrical-cut cross sections for PP processes at LHC: approximately 3.5 × 10⁻³ for all resonances listed
- For π⁰ production with Ca (A=40) at √s = 3500 GeV/nucleon: σ_γγ = 0.14 mbarn, σ_PP (geometrical) = 0.043 mbarn
- For π⁰ production with Si (A=28) at √s = 200 GeV/nucleon: σ_γγ = 0.69 × 10⁻² mbarn, σ_PP (geometrical) = 0.34 × 10⁻² mbarn
- Continuum γγ → γγ events at RHIC: 1532 events/year in a 200 MeV bin centered at 700 MeV (luminosity 2.0 × 10²⁶ cm⁻²s⁻¹)
- Events/year above background for η: 7.44 × 10⁵; for η′: 2.67 × 10⁴; for f₀(1710): 42

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of hadronic resonance production in peripheral heavy ion collisions by Bertulani (several years prior) and Klein, and builds on the two-photon physics formalism developed at e⁺e⁻ colliders by Budnev and others. The paper specifically compares γγ and double Pomeron mechanisms using the Donnachie-Landshoff model for the Pomeron, showing that for very heavy ions the electromagnetic process dominates. It enables experimental searches for glueball candidates (η(1440), f₀(1710)) and the controversial σ meson via the clean γγ → R → γγ signal at RHIC and LHC, complementing information from hadronic decays as discussed by Close.
