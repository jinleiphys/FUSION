# Hadronic physics in peripheral heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the production of hadronic resonances in very peripheral heavy ion collisions, where the ions collide with impact parameter larger than twice the nuclear radius and remain intact after the collision. We compare the resonance production through two-photon and double Pomeron exchange, showing that when we impose the condition for a peripheral interaction the $ОіОі$ process dominates over the Pomeron interaction, due to the short range propagation of this last one. We also discuss the observation of light resonances through the subprocess $ОіОі\to R \to ОіОі$, which is a clean signal for glueball candidates as well as one way to check the existence of a possible scalar $Пғ$ meson.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in very peripheral heavy ion collisions (impact parameter larger than twice the nuclear radius), two-photon fusion dominates over double Pomeron exchange for resonance production, but this dominance is conditional on using very heavy ions; for lighter ions the Pomeron contribution becomes comparable or dominant.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections for hadronic resonance production via two-photon (ОіОі) and double Pomeron (PP) exchange in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. They use the equivalent-photon (WeizsГӨcker-Williams) approximation with a realistic photon distribution from Cahn and Jackson, and a Donnachie-Landshoff model for the Pomeron. They enforce the peripheral condition through a geometrical cut (b > 2R_A) and also through a Glauber absorption factor, then compare the resulting rates for various resonances and for the continuum ОіОі вҶ’ ОіОі process.
