# Toward Thermalization in Heavy Ion Collisions at Strong Coupling

> We find the trapped surface for a collision of two sourceless shock waves in AdS$_5$ and conclude that such collisions always lead to a creation of a black hole in the bulk. Due to holographic correspondence, in the boundary gauge theory this result proves that a thermalized medium (quark-gluon plasma) is produced in heavy ion collisions at strong coupling (albeit in ${\cal N} =4$ super-Yang-Mills theory). We present new evidence supporting the analytic estimate for the time of thermalization that exists in the literature and find that thermalization time is parametrically much shorter than the time of shock wave stopping, indicating that our result may be relevant for description of heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The authors prove that a collision of two sourceless shock waves in AdS$_5$ always leads to black hole formation in the bulk, which by holographic correspondence implies thermalized quark-gluon plasma production in heavy ion collisions at strong coupling in ${\cal N}=4$ super-Yang-Mills theory, with the caveat that the thermalization time is parametrically much shorter than the shock wave stopping time, potentially relevant for experiments but generating numbers too short to describe RHIC physics.

## Method

The authors construct a trapped surface for a head-on collision of two sourceless shock waves in AdS$_5$ by first obtaining the trapped surface for shock waves with extended bulk sources (following Lin (2009) and Gubser (2008)), then taking the limit where the sources are sent to the deep infrared ($z_0 \rightarrow \infty$) while keeping the boundary energy-momentum tensor fixed. They verify the equivalence of this limit to the sourceless case by performing a perturbative solution of Einstein equations to lowest nontrivial order in the shock wave strength $\mu$, showing the post-collision metric reduces to that found by Albacete (2008) for sourceless shock waves.

## Key numbers

* The trapped surface lower boundary remains at a finite value of the 5th dimension coordinate $z$ after sending sources to $z_0 \rightarrow \infty$, with finite area giving a finite expression for produced entropy.
* The thermalization proper time is parametrically much shorter than the light-cone stopping time for shock waves found by Albacete (2008, 2009), though the approximate numerical estimates are too short to describe RHIC physics (the required thermalization time from hydrodynamic simulations is of order 1 fm/c).
* The perturbative solution for the metric function $h(x^+,x^-,z)$ in the $z_0 \rightarrow \infty$ limit yields $h = (8\pi G_5 \mu / L^3)^2 \, \theta(x^+)\theta(x^-) \, z^4 [\tau^2 + z^2/3]$, matching the sourceless result of Albacete (2008).

## Context

This work extends the trapped surface analysis of Gubser (2008) and Lin (2009), who found trapped surfaces for shock waves with point-like and extended bulk sources respectively, by removing the uncertainty of what bulk sources imply for the boundary theory. The result proves that thermalization occurs at strong coupling even without sources, supporting the analytic thermalization time estimate in the literature (Grumiller 2008) and suggesting the holographic approach may be qualitatively relevant for heavy ion collision experiments, though the numerical thermalization times are too short for RHIC.
