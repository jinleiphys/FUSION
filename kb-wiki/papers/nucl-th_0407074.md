# Form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics: Is there a favored approach? Why?

> Form factors of a simple system have been calculated in various forms of relativistic quantum mechanics, using a single-particle current. Their comparison has shown large discrepancies. The comparison is extended here to instant- and front-form calculations in unusual momentum configurations as well as to a point-form approach inspired from the Dirac's one (based on a hyperboloid surface). It is found that these new results depend on the momentum transfer, Q, through its ratio to the total mass, Q/M, (closely related to the Breit-frame velocity of the system). They evidence features similar for a part to those shown by an earlier ``point-form'' implementation (based on hyperplanes perpendicular to the velocity of the initial and final states). It thus appears that the standard instant- and front-form calculations, which generally do well compared either to experiment or to predictions of a theoretical model, rather represent exceptional cases. An argument explaining the success of these last approaches is presented and discussed. It is based on transformations of currents under Poincaré space-time translations, going beyond the energy-momentum conservation property which results from the Lagrangian invariance under them. Depending on the approach, analytic or approximate numerical methods are proposed to correct form factors for missing constraints then expected.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large discrepancies observed between form factors calculated in different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics (instant, front, and point forms) are not arbitrary but can be traced to whether the approach respects transformation properties of currents under Poincaré space-time translations beyond simple energy-momentum conservation; the standard instant- and front-form calculations that succeed in reproducing experiment or theoretical models represent exceptional cases tied to specific momentum configurations.

## Method

The author calculates form factors for the ground state of a two-body system of equal-mass scalar constituents using a single-particle current, comparing results across instant-form, front-form, and two point-form implementations (the earlier "point-form" based on hyperplanes perpendicular to the system velocity, and a new Dirac-inspired point-form based on a hyperboloid surface). The study extends earlier comparisons by examining instant- and front-form calculations in unusual momentum configurations (parallel momentum configuration with large total momentum, and front-form with \(q^+ \neq 0\)) and by deriving expressions for the Dirac-inspired point-form approach. Results are compared to exact Bethe-Salpeter calculations for two extreme interaction models: infinite-mass boson exchange (triangle diagram) and zero-mass boson exchange (Wick-Cutkosky model), which serve as the "experiment."

## Key numbers

- Constituent mass \(m = 0.3\) GeV and total system mass \(M = 0.14\) GeV (values appropriate to the pion) are used for numerical calculations.
- In the infinite-mass boson exchange model, form factors behave asymptotically as \(Q^{-2}\) for the charge form factor and \(Q^{-4}\) for the scalar form factor, up to log terms.
- In the zero-mass boson exchange (Wick-Cutkosky) model, the asymptotic behavior is given by \(F_1(Q^2)_{Q^2 \rightarrow \infty} = 2\,F_0(Q^2)_{Q^2 \rightarrow \infty} = 540\,(m^2/Q^2)^2 \left( \frac{\sqrt{4m^2+Q^2}}{2Q} \log\left[\frac{\sqrt{4m^2+Q^2}+Q}{\sqrt{4m^2+Q^2}-Q}\right] - 2 + \frac{m^2}{Q^2} \log^2\left[\frac{\sqrt{4m^2+Q^2}+Q}{\sqrt{4m^2+Q^2}-Q}\right] \right)\).
- The earlier "point-form" approach produces an extra factor in the momentum transfer dependence: \(Q^2 \rightarrow Q^2 \left(1 + Q^2/(4M^2)\right)\), giving asymptotic behavior \(Q^{-2n}\) where other approaches give \(Q^{-n}\).
- The charge form factor at \(Q^2=0\) satisfies \(F_1(0) = \int d\vec{k}/(2\pi)^3 \; \phi_0^2(k) = 1\) independently of the system velocity in all approaches considered.
- In the parallel momentum configuration with \(|\vec{P}_i+\vec{P}_f| \rightarrow \infty\), the dependence on momentum transfer appears through the Breit-frame velocity \(v = \sqrt{Q^2/(Q^2+4M^2)}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of form factors in different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics by Amghar et al. (2002) and addresses the striking discrepancies found in the "point-form" implementation of Klink (1998) and Bakamjian (1961), which produced results at odds with experiment for the pion charge form factor (Allen et al., 1998; Amghar et al., 2003; He et al., 2004). The paper shows that similar effects appear in instant-form calculations away from the Breit frame (Amghar et al., 2002) and in front-form calculations with \(q^+ \neq 0\) (Simula, 2002), establishing that the dependence on the ratio \(Q/(2M)\) is a general feature rather than a peculiarity of the point-form approach. The work enables a deeper understanding of why single-particle currents succeed in standard instant- and front-form calculations while requiring two-body currents in other approaches, and proposes analytic or approximate numerical methods to correct form factors for missing constraints related to space-time translation invariance.
