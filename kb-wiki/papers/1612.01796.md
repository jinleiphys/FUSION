# SQM2016: Theory Summary

> This is an overview of the main theoretical developments presented at SQM2016 held in Berkeley, California (USA) in June 2015.

# SQM2016: Theory Summary

## Key claim
The conference overview reports progress in understanding hot QCD matter, but many results remain preliminary or model-dependent; for example, the simultaneous description of heavy quark $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ is still challenged by uncertainties in the temperature dependence of the transport coefficient, and the chiral magnetic effect signal remains weak over a large background.

## Method
The paper summarizes theoretical plenary talks from the SQM2016 conference, covering collectivity in small systems, sub-threshold strangeness and charm production, thermal models and lattice QCD susceptibilities, heavy flavor dynamics, quarkonia production and regeneration, the chiral magnetic effect, and global polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- $\phi/K^-$ and $\Xi^-/\Lambda$ ratios at subthreshold energies: a peak in production predicted at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=1.25$ GeV using uRQMD with $N^* \rightarrow \phi, \Xi^-$ decays
- Statistical hadronization model (SHM) describes hadronic yields spanning about 9 orders of magnitude with hadronization temperature $T\simeq 155-165$ MeV from SPS to LHC energies
- Lattice QCD susceptibilities: the Hadron Resonance Gas model from PDG fails to reproduce Koch's ratio $\chi_{11}^{BS}/\chi_2^S$ for $T\geq 160$ MeV; $\chi_4^S/\chi_2^S$ is missed already at $T>140$ MeV
- Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration: Equation of State evaluated up to $\mu_B/T < 2.5$ and up to $(\mu_B/T)^6$ order
- Kurtosis $\kappa\sigma^2$ of net protons from STAR and $\chi_4/\chi_2$ from lattice QCD agree within uncertainties for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>19$ GeV ($\mu_B/T<2$)
- Heavy quark $v_2$: a weak temperature dependence of the drag coefficient (as in Quasi-Particle models or T-matrix) gives $v_2$ about a factor 2-3 larger than the $T^2$ dependence from pQCD or AdS/CFT
- Langevin vs Boltzmann dynamics for charm: differences of $10-30\%$ in intermediate $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow
- Charm quarks reach thermalization at LHC energy for $p_T < 2$ GeV
- $\Psi(2S)/J/\Psi$ ratio: dynamical regeneration predicts a peak in $R_{AA}(p_T)$ for $\Psi(2S)$ at $p_T \simeq 4$ GeV in central Pb+Pb collisions
- Chiral magnetic effect: charge separation measured at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7-62.4$ GeV at BES-RHIC
- Global polarization: predictions show increase of $\Lambda$ polarization with decreasing beam energy; experimental polarization of order a few percent is larger than predictions

## Context
This theory summary extends the ongoing discussion of collectivity in small systems, building on work by Mantysaari (2016) on proton shape fluctuations and hydrodynamics, and by Lappi (2015) on initial momentum correlations. It contrasts the statistical hadronization model (Andronic, 2008) with lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration (Ratti) and the Bielefeld group (Karsch), highlighting tensions in susceptibilities. The heavy flavor section builds on prior T-matrix approaches (vanHees, 2007) and Quasi-Particle models (Berrehrah, 2013; Das, 2015), while the quarkonia discussion compares the SHM prediction (Andronic, 2009) with dynamical regeneration models (Du). The chiral magnetic effect and global polarization sections open new directions for understanding vorticity and anomaly-driven transport in heavy-ion collisions.
