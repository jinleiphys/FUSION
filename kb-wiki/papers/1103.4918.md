# Multiple chiral doublet candidate nucleus $^{105}$Rh in a relativistic mean-field approach

> Following the reports of two pairs of chiral doublet bands observed in $^{105}$Rh, the adiabatic and configuration-fixed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations are performed to investigate their triaxial deformations with the corresponding configuration and the possible multiple chiral doublet (M$ѕЗ$D) phenomenon. The existence of M$ѕЗ$D phenomenon in $^{105}$Rh is highly expected.

## Key claim

Following reports of two pairs of chiral doublet bands observed in $^{105}$Rh, adiabatic and configuration-fixed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations find triaxial deformations suitable for chirality for two specific three-quasiparticle configurations, but the existence of the multiple chiral doublet (MѕЗD) phenomenon is only "highly expected" and the calculations are restricted to the non-rotating mean field only, with future work needed to verify whether the rotating mean field will attain chirality.

## Method

Adiabatic and configuration-fixed constrained triaxial RMF calculations were performed for $^{105}$Rh using the PK1 effective interaction, with Dirac spinors expanded in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis (12 major shells for nucleons, 10 for meson fields). The potential energy surfaces in the ќ≤-ќ≥ plane were obtained by constraining axial and triaxial mass quadrupole moments, and configuration-fixed calculations with "parallel-transport" ensured the same configuration was maintained during the constraint procedure. Calculations were first performed without time-odd fields to search for triaxial deformations and valence nucleon configurations favorable for chirality, then confirmed with time-odd fields included.

## Context

This work extends the prediction of multiple chiral doublet (MѕЗD) bands from $^{106}$Rh (Meng 2006) and other rhodium isotopes $^{104,106,108,110}$Rh (Peng 2008a) to $^{105}$Rh, where two pairs of chiral doublet bands were experimentally observed by Alcantara (2004) and Timar (2004). The calculations use the same configuration-fixed constrained triaxial RMF approach previously applied to $^{106}$Rh with time-odd fields included (Yao 2009). The results enable future tilted axis cranking (TAC) or particle-rotor model (PRM) calculations to verify whether the rotating mean field attains chirality for these configurations, and also raise the need to distinguish between chiral doublet bands and pseudospin doublet bands involving the (1gвВЗ/вВВ, 2dвВЕ/вВВ) pseudospin doublet states.
