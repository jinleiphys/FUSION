# Universality of the chiral soliton lattice and its interaction with quark matter

> In this paper, we show that the chiral soliton lattice (ChSL) is, in a precise sense, a universal feature of the low-energy limit of QCD minimally coupled to Maxwell theory. Here, we disclose that not only can the ChSL be obtained from the gauged Skyrme model in $3+1$ dimensions, including the back-reaction of the Maxwell $U(1)$ gauge field, we also demonstrate that the ChSL remains unchanged when higher-order terms arising from QCD, specifically the sub-leading corrections in the 't Hooft large $N_c$ expansion, are included. By considering a suitable ansatz adapted to describe topological solitons at finite baryon density in a constant magnetic field, the generalized Skyrme model coupled to the Maxwell theory is reduced to the effective Lagrangian of the ChSL phase, which describes a lattice of domain walls made of hadrons. One of the key points in this construction is the fact that even when the usual topological charge density vanishes, the presence of the Callan-Witten term in the topological charge density allows for a non-vanishing baryon number. In the present approach, the magnetic field can be external, as is usually assumed for the ChSL, or it can be self-consistently generated by the hadronic layers themselves. Finally, we show how our formulation allows us to study the coupling of the ChSL with quark matter. In particular, we derive the exact analytical spectrum of the Dirac equation in the high-density limit, providing a microscopic characterization of the fermionic excitations within the inhomogeneous hadronic background provided by the ChSL. The comparison of the present spectrum of the Dirac operator within the ChSL with the spectrum of the usual Dirac operator in a constant magnetic field discloses the fundamental role of both the quark-Skyrmion coupling and the hadronic profile in opening a gap and generating a shift in the spectrum itself.

## Key claim

The chiral soliton lattice (ChSL) is shown to be a universal feature of the low-energy limit of QCD minimally coupled to Maxwell theory, remaining unchanged even when higher-order sub-leading corrections from the 't Hooft large N_c expansion are included, provided the magnetic field satisfies a quantization condition derived from the requirement that the topological charge be an integer.

## Method

The authors reduce the gauged generalized Skyrme model in 3+1 dimensions to an effective sine-Gordon theory by adopting an ansatz where the Skyrme field depends only on one spatial coordinate and the third isospin angle is fixed to pi, while the Maxwell potential produces a constant magnetic field. They then couple the resulting ChSL configuration to quark matter via a Yukawa interaction and derive the exact analytical spectrum of the Dirac equation in the high-density limit by performing a chiral rotation and using ladder operators for the Landau levels.

## Key numbers

* The critical magnetic field for ChSL formation is B_ChSL = 16 pi m_pi f_pi^2 / mu_B, where m_pi is the pion mass, f_pi is the pion decay constant, and mu_B is the baryon chemical potential.
* In the chiral limit (m_pi to 0), the gradient of the pion field satisfies alpha' = mu_B B / (4 pi^2 f_pi^2).
* The Dirac spectrum in the high-density limit yields four branches: E_{s,lambda,plus minus,plus minus}(k) = plus minus sqrt(Omega_{s,lambda}^2 + (a plus minus sqrt(g^2 + k^2))^2), where Omega_{1,lambda} = sqrt(2 |omega_1| (lambda+1)) for isospin s=1 and Omega_{2,lambda} = sqrt(2 |omega_2| lambda) for s=-1, with omega_s = Q_s B and Q_1 = 2/3, Q_{-1} = -1/3.
* The topological charge is n_B = n pi B, requiring pi B to be an integer for baryon number quantization.

## Context

This work extends the description of the chiral soliton lattice from effective sine-Gordon theory (Brauner 2016, Higaki) to a first-principles derivation from the gauged Skyrme model, showing that the ChSL can exist even when the magnetic field is dynamically generated rather than external. The construction builds on exact soliton solutions at finite volume (Alvarez 2017, Canfora 2022) and enables the microscopic characterization of fermionic excitations within the inhomogeneous hadronic background, with applications to neutron star physics and heavy ion collisions (Kogut).
