# Sub-barrier capture reactions with $^{16,18}$O beams

> Various sub-barrier capture reactions with beams $^{16,18}$O are treated within the quantum diffusion approach. The role of neutron transfer in these capture reactions is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the similarity of capture cross sections for reactions with $^{16}$O and $^{18}$O beams on target nuclei differing by two neutrons arises because after two-neutron transfer the deformations of the interacting nuclei remain similar, so the Coulomb barriers are almost the same; the effect of neutron transfer is therefore an indirect effect of quadrupole deformation, not a direct enhancement.

## Method

The authors apply the quantum diffusion approach, which treats the collision of nuclei using a single collective variable (the relative distance) and incorporates fluctuation and dissipation effects to model coupling with low-lying collective modes. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated with a double-folding formalism using a Skyrme-type density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, and nuclear deformation effects are included through the dependence of the potential on deformations and mutual orientations. The two-neutron transfer with positive $Q_{2n}$-value is assumed to occur just before capture, changing the mass numbers, deformation parameters, and thus the height and shape of the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers

- Friction coefficient in momentum: $\hbar\lambda = 2$ MeV, used for all calculations.
- Quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0$ for $^{16}$O in the ground state (double magic nucleus).
- Extracted $\beta_2 = 0.1$ for the ground state of $^{18}$O from describing the reaction $^{18}$O+$^{208}$Pb.
- For $^{18}$O+$^{92}$Mo $\to$ $^{16}$O+$^{94}$Mo: $\beta_2$ changes from 0.1 and 0.05 to 0 and 0.151, respectively.
- For $^{18}$O+$^{74}$Ge $\to$ $^{16}$O+$^{76}$Ge: $\beta_2$ changes from 0.1 and 0.283 to 0 and 0.262, respectively.
- For $^{18}$O+$^{112}$Sn $\to$ $^{16}$O+$^{114}$Sn: $\beta_2$ changes from 0.1 and 0.123 to 0 and 0.121, respectively.
- For $^{18}$O+$^{118}$Sn $\to$ $^{16}$O+$^{120}$Sn: $\beta_2$ changes from 0.1 and 0.111 to 0 and 0.104, respectively.
- For $^{18}$O+$^{124}$Sn $\to$ $^{16}$O+$^{126}$Sn: $\beta_2$ changes from 0.1 and 0.095 to 0 and 0.09, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously applied to many heavy-ion capture reactions (Sargsyan et al., 2010; 2011) to a systematic set of sub-barrier reactions with $^{16,18}$O beams on Cr, Ge, Mo, and Sn isotopes. It tests the hypothesis of Broglia (1985) that sub-barrier capture depends on two-neutron transfer with positive $Q_{2n}$-value, and it provides a theoretical explanation for the experimental observations of Jia (2012) that capture cross sections for $^{16}$O+$^{76}$Ge and $^{18}$O+$^{74}$Ge are very similar. The results enable future experiments to probe the role of deformation changes in neutron-transfer-induced capture.
