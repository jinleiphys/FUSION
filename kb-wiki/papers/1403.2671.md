# Relativistic Coulomb excitation within Time Dependent Superfluid Local Density Approximation

> Within the framework of the unrestricted time-dependent density functional theory, we present for the first time an analysis of the relativistic Coulomb excitation of the heavy deformed open shell nucleus $^{238}$U. The approach is based on Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) formulated on a spatial lattice that can take into account coupling to the continuum, enabling self-consistent studies of superfluid dynamics of any nuclear shape. We have computed the energy deposited in the target nucleus as a function of the impact parameter, finding it to be significantly larger than the estimate using the Goldhaber-Teller model. The isovector giant dipole resonance, the dipole pygmy resonance and giant quadrupole modes were excited during the process. The one body dissipation of collective dipole modes is shown to lead a damping width $Γ_\downarrow \approx 0.4$ MeV and the number of pre-equilibrium neutrons emitted has been quantified.

## Key claim

The authors present the first analysis of relativistic Coulomb excitation of the heavy deformed open shell nucleus 238U using unrestricted time-dependent density functional theory, finding that the energy deposited in the target nucleus is significantly larger than the estimate from the Goldhaber-Teller model. However, this comparison is limited because the Goldhaber-Teller model is equivalent to a linear response result, and the TDSLDA framework naturally incorporates non-linearity and many more degrees of freedom beyond pure dipole oscillations.

## Method

The authors use the Time Dependent Superfluid Local Density Approximation (TDSLDA) formulated on a spatial lattice, with the Skyrme SLy4 energy functional and nuclear pairing. A 238U projectile moving at v=0.7c along the z-axis produces Lienard-Wiechert electromagnetic fields that couple to a deformed 238U target nucleus, and the time evolution is governed by the TDSLDA mean-field-like equations. The authors perform a transformation to a system where the lattice moves with the center of mass of the target, and compute the emitted electromagnetic radiation spectrum from the time-dependent proton current using a multipole expansion.

## Key numbers

- Damping width from one-body dissipation of collective dipole modes: Gamma_downward arrow approximately 0.4 MeV, corresponding to a characteristic damping time of 500 fm/c
- Total radiated energy during evolution time (about 2500 fm/c) does not exceed 1 MeV, which is about 2-3% of the deposited energy
- Pygmy dipole resonance contribution to total radiated energy: 1.7, 2.4, 1.5, and 0.8 keV for impact parameters 12.2, 14.6, 16.2, and 20.2 fm respectively (about 0.22%, 0.5%, 0.43%, and 0.45% of emitted radiation due to internal motion)
- Internal excitation energy for the perpendicular-parallel orientation: 39.29 MeV at b=12.2 fm, 19.2 MeV at b=14.6 fm, 12.87 MeV at b=16.2 fm, and 5.41 MeV at b=20.2 fm
- Goldhaber-Teller model internal excitation energy for the perpendicular-parallel orientation: 17.58 MeV at b=12.2 fm, 10.32 MeV at b=14.6 fm, 7.41 MeV at b=16.2 fm, and 3.43 MeV at b=20.2 fm
- Cross section for emission of radiation: sigma_gamma approximately 108 mb

## Context

This work extends the TDSLDA framework, previously applied to nuclear dynamics, to the problem of relativistic Coulomb excitation, which has traditionally been treated perturbatively using electromagnetic multipole matrix elements as in the textbook approach of Winther and Alder (1979) and Bertulani and Baur (1988). The results show that linear response approaches such as QRPA would severely underestimate the internal energy deposited, and the method enables self-consistent studies of superfluid dynamics of any nuclear shape including coupling to the continuum, with the next step being to extend the framework to include two-body effects via stochastic extensions to account for collisional damping.
