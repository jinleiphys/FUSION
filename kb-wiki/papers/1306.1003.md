# Two-neutron transfer probabilities and spatial-localization effects at the drip line

> We examine Cr isotopes at the drip line, where surface effects related to the existence of a weakly bound $s1/2$ state are known to be important and tightly connected with the pairing phenomenon (anti-halo effect). For these weakly-bound isotopes, we evaluate the ground state to ground state two-neutron transfer probabilities within a mean-field-based approach. An important part of the discussion is devoted to the analysis of several procedures that can be employed to constrain the parameters of a phenomenological pairing interaction. The parameters are first adjusted to reproduce the experimental gaps evaluated with the five-point formula. This choice has however some consequences on the evolution of the pairing correlations along the isotopic chains and, in particular, at shell closures. Other procedures are then followed (adjustment on a theoretical pairing gap at mid-shell and on the two-neutron separation energies). For the transfer probabilities, we discuss the effects associated to different choices of the spatial localization of the pairing interaction. We indicate that the analysis of pair-transfer reactions for such cases (where the last bound state is a low-$l$ state in a weakly bound nucleus) may improve our understanding of two aspects: the spatial distribution of pairing correlations in nuclei and the general problem of the persistence of pairing at the drip lines.

# Two-neutron transfer probabilities and spatial-localization effects at the drip line

## Key claim

For Cr isotopes at the neutron drip line where the last bound state is the weakly-bound $3s1/2$ orbital, the trend of ground-state-to-ground-state two-neutron transfer probabilities can distinguish between volume, mixed, and surface-peaked pairing interactions, but only if pairing correlations persist at the drip line; if pairing is suppressed, the transfer probability decreases monotonically regardless of the interaction's spatial localization.

## Method

Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed with the Skyrme SkM* parametrization in the mean-field channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction of the form $V_{pair}=V_0[1-\eta(\rho/\rho_0)^\alpha]\delta(\mathbf{r}_1-\mathbf{r}_2)$, with $\alpha=1$ and $\rho_0=0.16$ fm$^{-3}$. The pairing strength $V_0$ was adjusted for three spatial localizations ($\eta=0$ volume, $\eta=0.5$ mixed, $\eta=1$ surface-peaked) using three different procedures: (i) fitting to experimental five-point pairing gaps $\Delta^{(5)}$ for Ca isotopes from $^{42}$Ca to $^{46}$Ca including odd nuclei with blocking; (ii) fitting to have the same theoretical $\Delta_\nu$ at mid-shell $^{44}$Ca; (iii) fitting to reproduce two-neutron separation energies $S_{2n}$ for Ca isotopes. Addition transfer probabilities $P^{Add}(A)$ for $^A$Cr $\rightarrow$ $^{A+2}$Cr were then computed from $^{74}$Cr to $^{82}$Cr using both a simple formula $P=|\int dr \sum_{nlj} u^A_{nlj} v^A_{nlj}|^2$ and an improved formula $P^{Add}(A)=|\int dr \sum_{nlj} u^A_{nlj} v^{A+2}_{nlj}|^2$.

## Key numbers

- The Hartree-Fock drip-line Cr isotope is $^{82}$Cr ($N=58$), shifted by six neutrons from the Woods-Saxon prediction of $^{76}$Cr ($N=52$) in Hagino (2012).
- For the volume interaction ($\eta=0$) fitted to $\Delta^{(5)}$, $V_0 = -185$ MeV fm$^{-1}$, giving $\Delta_\nu = 1.52$ MeV and $\Delta^{(5)} = 1.74$ MeV for $^{44}$Ca.
- For the mixed interaction ($\eta=0.5$) fitted to $\Delta^{(5)}$, $V_0 = -376$ MeV fm$^{-1}$, giving $\Delta_\nu = 3.34$ MeV and $\Delta^{(5)} = 1.64$ MeV for $^{44}$Ca.
- For the surface-peaked interaction ($\eta=1$) fitted to $\Delta^{(5)}$, $V_0 = -295$ MeV fm$^{-1}$, giving $\Delta_\nu = 0.55$ MeV and $\Delta^{(5)} = 1.74$ MeV for $^{44}$Ca.
- When fitted to have $\Delta_\nu \sim 1.5$ MeV at $^{44}$Ca, the mixed interaction gave $V_0 = -280$ MeV fm$^{-1}$ and $\Delta^{(5)} = 0.38$ MeV; the surface-peaked interaction required $V_0 = -510$ MeV fm$^{-1}$.
- When fitted to $S_{2n}$, the volume interaction gave $V_0 = -170$ MeV fm$^{-1}$ ($\Delta_\nu = 1.26$ MeV, $\Delta^{(5)} = 1.49$ MeV); the mixed gave $V_0 = -250$ MeV fm$^{-1}$ ($\Delta_\nu = 1.23$ MeV, $\Delta^{(5)} = 0.48$ MeV); the surface-peaked gave $V_0 = -460$ MeV fm$^{-1}$ ($\Delta_\nu = 1.24$ MeV).
- For the surface-peaked interaction of Table II (fitted to $\Delta_\nu$), a change of slope in $P^{Add}(A)$ was found at $A=78$ when using the improved formula, with enhancement at $A=80$ where the $3s1/2$ orbital contributes.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pair-transfer reactions in Sn isotopes by Grasso (2012) and Matsuo (2011) to the more exotic Cr isotopic chain at the neutron drip line, where the anti-halo effect from a weakly-bound $s1/2$ state is expected to be important. It directly addresses the question raised by Scamps (2012) about whether different fitting procedures for the pairing interaction affect transfer-probability predictions, and connects to the debate between Hamamoto (2005) and Hagino (2012) on whether pairing correlations persist or disappear for weakly-bound systems at the drip line. The results suggest that future measurements of transfer probabilities in Cr isotopes near $^{82}$Cr could simultaneously resolve the spatial localization of the pairing interaction and the persistence of pairing at the drip line, though the authors note these isotopes are not producible with present facilities.
