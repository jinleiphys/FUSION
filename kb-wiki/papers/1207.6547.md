# Bott Periodicity and Realizations of Chiral Symmetry in Arbitrary Dimensions

> We compute the chiral symmetries of the Lagrangian for confining "vector-like" gauge theories with massless fermions in $d$-dimensional Minkowski space and, under a few reasonable assumptions, determine the form of the quadratic fermion condensates which arise through spontaneous breaking of these symmetries. We find that for each type (complex, real, or pseudoreal) of representation of the gauge group carried by the fermions, the chiral symmetries of the Lagrangian, as well as the residual symmetries after dynamical breaking, exactly follow the pattern of Bott periodicity as the dimension changes. The consequences of this for the topological features of the low-energy effective theory are considered.

## Key claim

The paper computes the chiral symmetries of the Lagrangian for confining vector-like gauge theories with massless fermions in arbitrary dimensions, and determines the form of quadratic fermion condensates arising from spontaneous breaking of these symmetries, under the assumptions of Lorentz and gauge invariance, mass terms, flavor democracy, parity invariance, and minimal breaking. The key result is that for each type (complex, real, or pseudoreal) of representation of the gauge group carried by the fermions, the chiral symmetries of the Lagrangian and the residual symmetries after dynamical breaking exactly follow the pattern of Bott periodicity as the dimension changes.

## Method

The authors classify the irreducible representations of the spin group Spin(1,d-1) using Shaw's classification system, which refines the real/pseudoreal/complex categorization. For each space-time dimension d (mod 8) and each type of gauge group representation (complex, real, pseudoreal), they construct all possible candidate operators (Hermitian or bilinear forms) that are invariant under Spin(1,d-1) and the gauge group, non-degenerate, and parity-invariant. They then impose the assumption of minimal breaking (maximal residual symmetry among candidate operators) to determine the pattern of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, yielding the residual symmetry group H from the original chiral symmetry group G.

## Context

This work extends the known results for chiral symmetry breaking in d = 4 dimensions by Peskin (1980) and Preskill, and in d = 3 dimensions by Pisarski (1984), Appelquist (1986, 1990, 1995), and others, to arbitrary dimensions d. The paper unifies these results by showing that the pattern of chiral symmetries and their breaking follows Bott periodicity modulo 8 in the space-time dimension. The results enable the study of the low-energy effective theory of Goldstone bosons as a nonlinear sigma model on the coset space G/H, and allow investigation of topological features such as the interpretation of baryons as topological solitons in arbitrary dimensions.
