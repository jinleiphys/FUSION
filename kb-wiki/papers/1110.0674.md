# Neutrino nucleus reactions within the GiBUU model

> The GiBUU model, which implements all reaction channels relevant at medium neutrino energy, is used to investigate the neutrino and antineutrino scattering on iron. Results for integrated cross sections are compared with NOMAD and MINOS data. It is shown, that final state interaction can noticeably change the spectra of the outgoing hadrons. Predictions for the Miner$ν$a experiment are made for pion spectra, averaged over NuMI neutrino and antineutrino fluxes.

# Neutrino nucleus reactions within the GiBUU model

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model, using default parameters without fine-tuning, reproduces the measured total cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on iron, but the agreement is better than the agreement of the different experimental datasets with each other, especially for antineutrinos below 25 GeV. The model predicts that final state interactions significantly modify the kinetic energy spectra of outgoing pions, with effects ranging from moderate depletion of dominant channels to dramatic changes in sub-dominant channels where side feeding becomes the main source of events.

## Method

The GiBUU transport model calculates the total cross section as the sum of quasielastic scattering, resonance production, background processes, and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) simulated with PYTHIA, with a smooth switching between resonance and DIS contributions in the shallow inelastic region (1.6 GeV < W < 2.0 GeV). Final state interactions are modeled by solving the semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation, which describes the dynamical evolution of phase space densities for each particle species under mean field potentials and collision terms. The calculations are performed for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on iron targets without any fine-tuning to the data, using default GiBUU parameters.

## Key numbers

- World average cross section per energy for an isoscalar target: sigma_tot/E_nu (nu) = 0.667 ± 0.014 × 10^{-38} cm^2/GeV for neutrinos and sigma_tot/E_nu (antinu) = 0.334 ± 0.008 × 10^{-38} cm^2/GeV for antineutrinos
- Different prescriptions for providing quasi-free kinematics to PYTHIA in the GiBUU simulation result in a 5-7% difference in the calculated cross sections
- For dominant pion channels, final state interactions decrease the cross section at pion kinetic energies T_pi > 0.2 GeV and increase the cross section at T_pi < 0.15 GeV
- The DIS contribution becomes noticeable at neutrino energies around 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport model, previously developed for nucleon-, nucleus-, pion-, and electron-induced reactions from hundreds of MeV to tens of GeV, to neutrino-induced reactions including DIS processes at medium energies. The calculations are compared with MINOS data (Adamson 2009) and NOMAD data, as well as older IHEP-JINR results (Anikeev 1995). The predictions for pion spectra averaged over NuMI fluxes are intended for the Miner nu a experiment, which will measure on multiple nuclear targets (Plastic, Iron, Lead, Carbon, Water, liquid Helium) to study nuclear effects. The treatment of nuclear corrections in DIS remains controversial, with different approaches (nuclear parton distributions from Eskola 1998 versus Fermi motion with nuclear potential) giving different results, and the authors note that no other event generator accounts for nuclear corrections in high-energy neutrino reactions.
