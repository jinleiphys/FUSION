# Coupling the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) and the Coupled Cluster (CC) Methods: A way towards continuum spectra of "not-so-few-body" systems

> Here we summarize how the LIT and CC methods can be coupled, in order to allow for ab initio calculations of reactions in medium mass nuclei. Results on 16O are reviewed and preliminary calculations on 40Ca are presented.

# Coupling the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) and the Coupled Cluster (CC) Methods: A way towards continuum spectra of "not-so-few-body" systems

## Key claim

The LIT and CC methods can be coupled to enable *ab initio* calculations of continuum response functions in medium-mass nuclei, but the method's reliability depends on achieving convergence in the model space size; for 40Ca, full convergence has not yet been reached, so inversion of the transform to obtain the response function is not yet warranted.

## Method

The LIT method reduces the continuum problem of computing the response function S(omega) to a bound-state problem by calculating the norm of a localized state obtained via a Lorentzian integral transform of the response. The authors reformulate the LIT within the CC framework using a similarity transformation e^T, expressing the transform as a matrix element between left and right states in the CC similarity-transformed Hamiltonian (bar-H). The resulting linear equations for the left and right LIT states are solved using an equation-of-motion (EoM) approach with source terms, expanding the operators in one-particle-one-hole (1p-1h) and two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) excitations within the CCSD approximation.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, the LIT was calculated at a Lorentzian width Gamma = 10 MeV, using model spaces up to Nmax = 18; this is the smallest Gamma value for which convergent results were obtained.
- The theoretical LIT for 16O at Gamma = 10 MeV was compared to the LIT of experimental data from Ahrens et al., showing that the experimental centroid of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) is well reproduced by calculations using only the chiral N3LO two-body potential.
- For 40Ca, preliminary LIT results at Gamma = 10 MeV are shown for model spaces Nmax = 16 and Nmax = 18, but full convergence has not yet been reached, preventing inversion of the transform.

## Context

This work extends the LIT method, previously restricted to nuclei with A < 8 using hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansions, to medium-mass nuclei by coupling with the CC method, which is powerful for bound states of many-body systems. The results on 16O demonstrate that the chiral N3LO two-body potential alone can reproduce the GDR centroid, though the inverted response shows a less pronounced peak than experiment, suggesting possible shortcomings in the potential or the neglect of three-body forces and higher clusters. The preliminary 40Ca calculations aim toward *ab initio* treatment of 48Ca, for which recent photoabsorption measurements exist (Ref15 in the paper), and indicate that larger model spaces are needed to achieve convergent LIT results before inversion can be attempted.
