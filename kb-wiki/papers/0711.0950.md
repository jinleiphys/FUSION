# The effects of topological charge change in heavy ion collisions: "Event by event P and CP violation"

> Quantum chromodynamics (QCD) contains field configurations which can be characterized by a topological invariant, the winding number Q_w. Configurations with nonzero Q_w break the charge-parity CP symmetry of QCD. We consider a novel mechanism by which these configurations can separate charge in the presence of a background magnetic field - the "Chiral Magnetic Effect". We argue that sufficiently large magnetic fields are created in heavy ion collisions so that the Chiral Magnetic Effect causes preferential emission of charged particles along the direction of angular momentum. Since separation of charge is CP-odd, any observation of the Chiral Magnetic Effect could provide a clear demonstration of the topological nature of the QCD vacuum. We give an estimate of the effect and conclude that it might be observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that topological charge-changing transitions in QCD, combined with the strong magnetic fields generated in off-central heavy ion collisions, produce a "Chiral Magnetic Effect" that separates electric charge along the direction of angular momentum; the authors hedge that this effect "might be observed experimentally" and that their estimate represents an upper limit derived under idealized conditions (chiral limit and extremely large magnetic field).

## Method

The authors derive the Chiral Magnetic Effect by analyzing how gauge field configurations with nonzero winding number Q_w induce chirality changes in quarks via the axial anomaly, then show that in the presence of a background magnetic field, the spin alignment of quarks combined with their helicity change produces a net electromagnetic current along the magnetic field direction. They compute the charge separation for both the idealized case of a very large magnetic field (all quarks in the lowest Landau level) and for moderate magnetic fields using a polarization function β_f(ω) that depends on the magnetic field strength and the size ρ of the topological configuration. The application to heavy ion collisions considers that only transitions near the surface of the hot matter produce observable charge separation, analogous to jet quenching.

## Key numbers

- The sphaleron transition rate in QCD is estimated as Γ^± ∼ 192.8 α_S^5 T^4, assuming N_c scaling from the SU(2) Yang-Mills result of 25.4 α_W^5 T^4
- For a very large homogeneous magnetic field with N_f = 2 flavors, the maximum charge separation from a single topological configuration is Q = 2 Q_w (in units of elementary charge e)
- For N_f = 3 flavors, the maximum charge separation is Q = (8/3) Q_w
- For moderate magnetic fields, the charge separation is approximately Q ≈ 4 Φ Q_w Σ_f q_f^2, where Φ = e B ρ^2 is the magnetic flux through the configuration
- The typical size of a topological configuration in hot plasma is of order the magnetic screening length α_S T, requiring magnetic fields of order α_S^2 T^2, estimated as 10^2 to 10^3 MeV^2 for RHIC initial conditions
- The typical energy of QCD sphalerons is Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Kharzeev (2006) that P- and CP-odd domains in heavy ion collisions can induce charge separation in the presence of angular momentum or magnetic fields, and provides a detailed microscopic mechanism (the Chiral Magnetic Effect) based on topological charge-changing transitions. The paper builds on earlier work by 't Hooft (1976) on instanton-induced transitions, by Manton (1983) and Klinkhamer (1984) on sphaleron configurations, and by McLerran (1990) on the QCD counterpart of electroweak baryon number violation. The effect enables experimental searches for CP violation in heavy ion collisions using the charge separation observable proposed by Voloshin (2004), with the STAR Collaboration at RHIC having already presented first results (Selyuzhenkov, 2005).
