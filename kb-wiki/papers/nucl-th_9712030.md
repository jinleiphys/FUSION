# Hadronic matter compressibility from event-by-event analysis of heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a method to measure the hadronic matter compressibility by means of the event-by-event analysis of heavy-ion collisions at high energies. The method, which utilizes the thermodynamical relation between the compressibility and the particle number fluctuations, requires a simultaneous measurement of the particle source size, temperature and particle multiplicity.

# Hadronic matter compressibility from event-by-event analysis of heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the isothermal compressibility of hadronic matter can be extracted from event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, provided the system volume, temperature, and particle multiplicity at chemical freeze-out can be simultaneously measured. The method relies on the assumption that the hadron system at freeze-out is in both thermal and chemical equilibrium, which the author notes is well satisfied for nonstrange particles based on prior analyses of AGS and SPS data.

## Method

The author derives a thermodynamical relation connecting the particle number fluctuation matrix to the isothermal compressibility for a multi-component hadron system, generalizing the single-component relation. The left-hand side of the relation involves the average particle numbers and the inverse of the moment matrix of multiplicity fluctuations, while the right-hand side contains the volume, temperature, and pressure derivative with respect to volume at fixed average particle numbers. The method requires measuring the particle multiplicity distribution, system volume (via centrality selection and interferometry), and temperature (from transverse mass spectra) on an event-by-event basis, then grouping events with similar volume and temperature to extract the compressibility.

## Key numbers

- For an ideal gas of massless pions with zero chemical potential, the right-hand side of the compressibility relation equals approximately 0.71 times the average particle number: \( (6 \zeta(3) / \pi^2) \langle N \rangle \cong 0.71 \langle N \rangle \), where \(\zeta(3) \cong 1.202\).
- For a classical multi-component van der Waals gas, the compressibility relation gives \(\sum_j \langle N_j \rangle / (1 - \sum_i \langle N_i \rangle v_i / V)^2\), which approximates \((1 + 2 \sum_i \langle N_i \rangle v_i / V) \sum_j \langle N_j \rangle\) when the van der Waals correction is small.
- The temperature is stated to be presumably measurable within 10% accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event fluctuation approach previously applied to temperature fluctuations as a measure of heat capacity by Stodolsky (1995) and Shuryak (1997), who also briefly considered particle number fluctuations in relation to the chemical potential derivative. The author had earlier shown (Gazdzicki, 1992; 1997) that multiplicity–transverse momentum correlations cause fluctuations in total transverse momentum, and that NA49 Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon showed smaller fluctuations than proton-proton interactions, demonstrating the role of secondary interactions. The method enables extraction of the equation of state of hadronic matter from heavy-ion collision data, provided the technical challenges of measuring volume and temperature at chemical freeze-out can be overcome, with reference to the chemical freeze-out analysis procedures of Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995) and Becattini et al. (1997).
