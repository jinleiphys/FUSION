# Triple-Pomeron Matrix Model for Dispersive Corrections to Nucleon-Nucleus Total Cross Section

> Dispersive corrections to the total cross section for high-energy scattering from a heavy nucleus are calculated using a matrix model, based on the triple-Pomeron behavior of diffractive scattering from a single nucleon, for the cross section operator connecting different states of the projectile nucleon . Energy-dependent effects due to the decrease in longitudinal momentum transfers and the opening of more channels with increasing energy are included. The three leading terms in an expansion in the number of inelastic transitions are evaluated and compared to exact results for the model in the uniform nuclear density approximation for the the scattering of nucleons from Pb^{208} for laboratory momenta ranging from 50 to 200 GeV/c.

# Triple-Pomeron Matrix Model for Dispersive Corrections to Nucleon-Nucleus Total Cross Section

## Key claim

Dispersive corrections to nucleon-nucleus total cross sections, calculated using a matrix model consistent with triple-Pomeron behavior, are small compared to the uncorrected Glauber cross section but the expansion in the number of inelastic transitions converges poorly at higher energies; the leading second-order term overestimates the exact correction by nearly 80% at 200 GeV/c, contrary to the assumption that this term alone is adequate for heavy nuclei.

## Method

The author constructs a finite matrix model for the cross section operator connecting different internal states of the projectile nucleon, with matrix elements constrained by the triple-Pomeron behavior of single diffraction dissociation from a single nucleon. The model is evaluated in the uniform nuclear density approximation for scattering from Pb-208, using a z-ordered expansion of the profile function to compute the second-, third-, and fourth-order terms in the number of inelastic transitions, and comparing these to the exact result obtained by exponentiating a z-independent operator. Longitudinal momentum transfers due to mass differences between excited states are included via an imaginary contribution to the diagonal matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The uncorrected Glauber cross section for nucleon-Pb-208 scattering is approximately 2580 mb.
- At 50 GeV/c laboratory momentum, the second-order dispersive reduction is 45.4 mb, the third-order is -16.4 mb, the fourth-order is 5.6 mb, and the exact result is 34.2 mb.
- At 200 GeV/c, the second-order reduction is 69.7 mb, the third-order is -68.2 mb, the fourth-order is 75.3 mb, and the exact result is 38.9 mb.
- The leading second-order term overestimates the exact correction by 33% at 50 GeV/c and by nearly 80% at 200 GeV/c.
- The triple-Pomeron vertex is taken as g_PPP = 0.363 mb^(1/2), with nucleon-nucleon total cross section sigma_T = 38.5 mb.
- The mass-squared spacing parameter m_0^2 = 1.5 GeV^2, with a^2 = 0.5 and d = 0.1.
- The matrix dimension N increases linearly with momentum as N approximately 2 p_1 m_pi / m_0^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of dispersive corrections by Jen (year not given) that included only the leading second-order term and ignored longitudinal momentum transfers, and by Van Hove (year not given) who considered a simplified example with zero longitudinal momentum transfer. The matrix model approach is motivated by the lack of a reliable microscopic model for the internal degrees of freedom of highly excited nucleons. The results show that previous approximations can lead to large errors for heavy nuclei at high energies, and the author suggests extending the calculations to single diffraction dissociation from nuclei, where higher-order terms may be relatively more important.
