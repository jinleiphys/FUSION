# Nonlinear waves in magnetized quark matter and the reduced Ostrovsky equation

> We study nonlinear waves in a nonrelativistic ideal and cold quark gluon plasma immersed in a strong uniform magnetic field. In the context of nonrelativistic hydrodynamics with an external magnetic field we derive a nonlinear wave equation for baryon density perturbations, which can be written as a reduced Ostrovsky equation. We find analytical solutions and identify the effects of the magnetic field.

# Nonlinear waves in magnetized quark matter and the reduced Ostrovsky equation

## Key claim

The authors derive that nonlinear baryon density perturbations in a cold, nonrelativistic, ideal quark-gluon plasma immersed in a strong uniform magnetic field obey a reduced Ostrovsky equation (ROE), and that the magnetic field is essential for the existence of localized solitonic solutions, without it, the equation reduces to a breaking wave equation without soliton solutions. The magnetic field reduces the wave amplitude, and this effect is dominated by the magnetic field's inclusion in the hydrodynamics equations rather than in the equation of state.

## Method

The authors apply the Reductive Perturbation Method (RPM) to the nonrelativistic Euler equation and continuity equation for each quark flavor (up, down, strange) in an external uniform magnetic field along the z-direction, using stretched coordinates and a small expansion parameter σ. They consider two equations of state: a nonrelativistic one with anisotropic sound speeds (parallel and perpendicular to the field) and the mQCD (magnetized QCD) equation of state, which includes quark Landau levels. The resulting nonlinear wave equations are transformed into the reduced Ostrovsky equation, which is solved analytically using a hyperbolic tangent function method.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: m_u = 2.2 MeV, m_d = 4.7 MeV, m_s = 96 MeV; electron charge Q_e = 0.08542 (natural units)
- Background nuclear matter density: ρ_N = 0.17 fm⁻³
- For the nonrelativistic EOS example: c_s⊥ = c_s∥ = 0.3, λ = 1 fm⁻¹, γ = 0.1, pulse propagation speed α+γ = 0.7
- For the mQCD EOS example: chemical potential ν_f = 300 MeV, B_QCD = 70 MeV/fm³, g_h = 0.05, m_G = 300 MeV, background density ρ₀ ≈ 2ρ_N to 2.1ρ_N for B = 10¹⁷ G to 10¹⁹ G, c_s⊥ ≈ 0.2, pulse propagation speed α+γ = 0.5
- For magnetic fields ~10¹⁶ G or smaller, the perturbation amplitude |δρ_B_u| exceeds 1 (violating the perturbation expansion assumption)
- The dispersion coefficient Γ = (Q_f B)² / [2 m_f² c_s⊥] determines the amplitude scaling as 1/Γ

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous linear analysis (Fogaça et al., 2017) of stable and causal perturbations in magnetized QGP to the nonlinear regime, and improves upon earlier nonlinear treatments (Fogaça et al., earlier work) by including strong magnetic field effects. It corroborates and extends the conclusion of Azam et al. (earlier work) that increasing the magnetic field reduces the amplitude of nonlinear waves, and contrasts with a more recent study (Javier et al., 2017) which found solitonic solutions of a modified nonlinear Schrödinger equation where the magnetic field increased the phase speed and reduced the width. The results could be applied to deconfined cold quark matter in compact stars and to cold QGP formed in heavy ion collisions at FAIR or NICA.
