# A quantum-kinetic treatment for internal dynamics of multilevel atomic systems moving through a target matter

## Key claim
The paper derives a set of quantum-kinetic equations for the density matrix elements describing the internal state evolution of relativistic elementary atoms (EA) at the EA rest frame, going beyond the classical probability-based kinetic equations of Tarasov (prior work) by including interference (quantum) effects. The derivation assumes a "frozen" target approximation and neglects current-related terms in the Hamiltonian because typical particle velocities within the EA are of order αc ≪ c.

## Method
The authors start from the Schrödinger equation for a pionium atom (A₂π) moving through a target, with the Hamiltonian including the Coulomb interaction between the EA and target atoms. Using a path integral representation of the Green function and averaging over frozen target atom positions following the prescriptions of Landau and Pomeranchuk (1981) and Akhiezer and Shul'ga (prior work), they derive a quantum-kinetic equation for the density matrix in the EA rest frame. The resulting equation contains a term proportional to v₀γn₀Ω(s,s′) that describes the Coulomb interaction between EA and target atoms with account of all multiphoton exchanges, represented via a generalized optical potential V_opt(s,s′) = kΩ(s,s′) with k = -iv₀γn₀.

## Key numbers
- The typical velocities of particles forming the EA are of order αc ≪ c, where α is the fine structure constant.
- The Lorentz factor γ = 1/√(1 - v₀²/c²) appears in the scalar potential transformation Φ(R) = γΦ₀√(R² + γ²(v₀R)²).
- The target atom density n₀ (number of atoms per unit volume at the target rest frame) enters the interaction term as v₀γn₀.
- The EA level width Γ_{i(k)} = 1/τ_{i(k)} appears in the final density matrix equation, where τ_{i(k)} is the EA lifetime.

## Context
This work extends the classical kinetic equations for pionium atoms proposed by Tarasov (prior work) by incorporating quantum interference effects through a density matrix formalism, which the earlier probability-based approach could not capture. The formalism is specifically motivated by the DIRAC experiment (Dirac Collaboration, 2004; Nemenov, prior work), which aims to measure the lifetime of hydrogenlike A₂π atoms consisting of π⁺ and π⁻ mesons. The derived equations enable a more accurate description of excitation, deexcitation, and ionization of pionium atoms during target passage, with numerical solutions in the first Born approximation performed in a companion paper (Santa, prior work). The structure of the resulting equation is similar to Eq. (116) in Chang (prior work) describing internal dynamics of multilevel atoms in laser fields, where the last term Γρ describes spontaneous relaxation.
