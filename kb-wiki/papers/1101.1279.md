# Equation of state and initial temperature of quark gluon plasma at RHIC

> In gold-gold collisions of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) a perfect fluid of quarks, sometimes called the strongly interacting quark gluon plasma (sQGP) is created for an extremely short time. The time evolution of this fluid can be described by hydrodynamical models. After expansion and cooling, the freeze-out happens and hadrons are created. Their distribution reveals information about the final state of the fluid. To investigate the time evolution one needs to analyze penetrating probes, such as direct photon observables. Transverse momentum distributions of low energy direct photons were mesured in 2010 by PHENIX, while azimuthal asymmetry in 2011. These measurements can be compared to hydrodynamics to determine the equation of state and the initial temperature of sQGP. In this paper we analyze an 1+3 dimensional solution of relativistic hydrodynamics. We calculate momentum distribution, azimuthal asymmetry and momentum correlations of direct photons. Based on earlier fits to hadronic spectra, we compare photon calculations to measurements to determine the equation of state and the initial temperature of sQGP. We find that the initial temperature in the center of the fireball is 507+-12 MeV, while for the sound speed we get a speed of sound of 0.36+-0.02. We also estimate a systematic error of these results. We find that the measured azimuthal asymmetry is also not incompatible with this model, and predict a photon source that is significantly larger in the out direction than in the side direction.

## Key claim

The initial temperature of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC is determined to be greater than 507 ± 12 MeV at 0.7 fm/c, and the average speed of sound is 0.36 ± 0.02, but these values carry a systematic uncertainty of ±90 MeV and ±0.04 respectively, and the result depends on the assumption that photon radiation is thermal even if the photons themselves are not thermalized.

## Method

The authors used an exact 1+3 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic solution with ellipsoidal symmetry and Hubble-type expansion to model the fireball created in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They calculated the invariant transverse momentum distribution and elliptic flow of thermal direct photons from this solution, then fitted the model to PHENIX direct photon data (2010) using the Minuit package, with the equation of state parameter κ and initial time t_i as free parameters while fixing freeze-out properties from earlier hadronic fits.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature in the center of the fireball: T_i > 507 ± 12 MeV (statistical) ± 90 MeV (systematic) at 0.7 fm/c
- Speed of sound: c_s = 0.36 ± 0.02 (statistical) ± 0.04 (systematic)
- Equation of state parameter: κ = 7.9 ± 0.7
- Freeze-out proper time: τ_0 = 7.7 fm/c (fixed from hadronic fits)
- Central freeze-out temperature: T_0 = 204 MeV (fixed from hadronic fits)
- Eccentricity parameter: ε = 0.34 (fixed from hadronic fits)
- Fit confidence level: 7.2% with χ² = 7.0 for 3 degrees of freedom
- Maximum initial time within 95% probability: 0.7 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the analytic hydrodynamic approach of Csorgo (2003) and Csanad (2009), who previously calculated hadronic observables from the same 1+3 dimensional relativistic solution. It is the first simultaneous extraction of the equation of state and initial temperature from both photon and hadron data at RHIC. The results agree with lattice QCD calculations (Borsanyi, 2010) and experimental results from hadronic data (Adare, 2006; Lacey, 2006), and the model predicts a photon source with significantly larger HBT radius in the out direction than in the side direction, which could be tested by future measurements.
