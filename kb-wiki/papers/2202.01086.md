# How an $ω_0$ condensate can spike the speed of sound in cold quarkyonic matter

> I describe a novel mechanism where the variation of an $ω_0$ condensate can generate a "spike" in the speed of sound in hadronic matter. An $ω_0$ condensate naturally increases the speed of sound; the real problem is how to get the speed of sound to decrease. I suggest this can happen through the appearance of a Quantum Pion Liquid.

## Key claim

An ω₀ condensate naturally increases the speed of sound in hadronic matter, but the real problem is how to get the speed of sound to decrease; the author suggests this can happen through the appearance of a Quantum Pion Liquid, which provides a dynamical mechanism for the ω₀ condensate to evaporate.

## Method

The author constructs an effective Lagrangian for the ω meson coupled to the O(4) chiral field for two light flavors, including a quartic coupling κ² between ωμ and the chiral field φ. The analysis considers the large-Nc limit with Nf held fixed, where the confined phase can depend on density because meson-baryon couplings are large (∼ Nc^{1/2}). The author then examines how a negative coefficient Z in the spatial derivative term of the chiral Lagrangian generates spatially inhomogeneous structures (chiral spirals), leading to a linear infrared divergence in Goldstone boson fluctuations that produces a dynamical mass and a quantum pion liquid phase.

## Key numbers

- The ω₀ condensate contributes to the free energy as ∼ Nc, with nB ∼ Nc⁰ and gω ∼ Nc^{1/2}
- The speed of sound from the leading ω₀ condensate term equals the speed of light (cs² = 1), as first demonstrated by Zeldovich (1962)
- The speed of sound in an ideal quark gas is cs² = 1/3
- The equation of state of nuclear matter must be soft until nB ∼ 1.5–1.8 n₀ (where n₀ is the saturation density of nuclear matter), then increase sharply
- In the quantum pion liquid, fluctuations are very large at large Z, with ⟨φ²⟩ ∼ Z², so the ω meson mass squared is mω² ∼ Z² and ⟨ω₀⟩ ∼ 1/Z²

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter framework developed by McLerran (2007), Kojo (2009-2011), and Pisarski (2021), where at nonzero density the free energy is that of interacting quarks and gluons but excitations near the Fermi surface remain confined. The mechanism addresses the astrophysical requirement from neutron star observations (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) and the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement for a spike in the speed of sound, going beyond standard Quantum HadroDynamics models (Serot 1997) and the matching approach of Cao and Liao (2020). The quantum pion liquid concept was introduced in Pisarski (2021) and builds on the linear sigma model analysis of Pisarski (2020).
