# The neutron halo of $^6$He in a microscopic model

> The two--neutron separation energy of $^6$He has been reproduced for the first time in a realistic parameter--free microscopic multicluster model comprising the $α+n+n$ and $t+t$ clusterizations, with $α$ cluster breathing excitations included. The contribution of the $t+t$ channel is substantial. A very thick (0.85 fm) neutron halo has been found in full agreement with the results of the latest phenomenological analysis.

## Key claim

The two-neutron separation energy of 6He was reproduced for the first time in a parameter-free microscopic multicluster model, but only after including both the alpha+n+n and t+t clusterizations; the model still required readjustment of the effective interaction when the t+t channel was added, and the final result (0.961 MeV) is within 0.014 MeV of the experimental value (0.975 MeV).

## Method

The author used a microscopic multiconfiguration three-cluster model with a trial wave function that superposes alpha+n+n and n(alpha n) partitions, including breathing excitations of the alpha cluster. The effective nucleon-nucleon interaction of Tang et al. was employed, with parameters fixed to reproduce alpha particle properties, alpha+n scattering phase shifts, and the antibound character of the 1S0 N+N state. The model space was later extended to include an explicit t+t clusterization term, requiring a readjustment of the exchange mixture parameter u and the spin-orbit strength V4 to maintain the quality of the alpha+n phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron separation energy of 6He: 0.961 MeV in the full {alpha+n+n; t+t} model, compared to the experimental value of 0.975 MeV (Ajzenberg 1988).
* Neutron halo thickness (rn - rp) in 6He: 0.855 fm in the {alpha+n+n; t+t} model, in agreement with the phenomenological analysis of Tanihata (1992) which gave 0.87 +/- 0.06 fm.
* Point nucleon rms radii in 6He from the {alpha+n+n; t+t} model: matter radius 2.397 fm, neutron radius 2.648 fm, proton radius 1.793 fm.
* Weight of the t+t clusterization in 6He: 0.547 in the full model space.
* Energy gain from including alpha breathing modes: approximately 0.09 MeV (from 0.652 MeV to 0.740 MeV two-neutron separation energy).

## Context

This work extends microscopic multicluster calculations previously applied to 6Li (Csoto 1992) to the neutron halo nucleus 6He. It directly addresses the underbinding problem in macroscopic three-body models such as those of Danilin (1991), Kukulin (1986), and Lehman (1983), which underbind 6He by 0.3-0.6 MeV. The finding that the t+t rearrangement channel has a large weight (0.547) explains this missing binding energy and resolves the discrepancy between two conflicting experimental predictions for the neutron halo thickness: the simpler Glauber analysis of Tanihata (1985) giving about 0.4 fm versus the more realistic analysis of Tanihata (1992) giving about 0.9 fm. The results enable future calculations of electric dipole strength, beta-decay branching ratios, and momentum distributions for 6He and other halo nuclei such as 11Li.
