# 20Ne + 76Ge elastic and inelastic scattering at 306 MeV

> Background: Double charge exchange (DCE) nuclear reactions have recently attracted much interest as tools to provide experimentally driven information about nuclear matrix elements of interest in the context of neutrinoless double-beta decay. In this framework, a good description of the reaction mechanism and a complete knowledge of the initial and final-state interactions are mandatory. Presently, not enough is known about the details of the optical potentials and nuclear response to isospin operators for many of the projectile-target systems proposed for future DCE studies. Among these, the 20Ne + 76Ge DCE reaction is particularly relevant due to its connection with 76Ge double-beta decay. Purpose: We intend to characterize the initial-state interaction for the 20Ne + 76Ge reactions at 306 MeV bombarding energy and determine the optical potential and the role of the couplings between elastic channel and inelastic transitions to the first low-lying excited states. Methods: We determine the experimental elastic and inelastic scattering cross-section angular distributions, compare the theoretical predictions by adopting different models of optical potentials with the experimental data, and evaluate the coupling effect through the comparison of the distorted-wave Born approximation calculations with the coupled channels ones. Results: Optical models fail to describe the elastic angular distribution above the grazing angle (9.4°). A correction in the geometry to effectively account for deformation of the involved nuclear systems improves the agreement up to about 14°. Coupled channels effects are crucial to obtain good agreement at large angles in the elastic scattering cross section.

## Key claim

Optical models fail to describe the elastic angular distribution above the grazing angle (9.4°), but a 5% increase in the radius of the nuclear density profiles to account for deformation improves agreement up to about 14°, while coupled channels effects are crucial for good agreement at larger angles.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions were measured for 20Ne + 76Ge at 306 MeV using the MAGNEX magnetic spectrometer at INFN-LNS. The experimental cross sections were compared with optical model calculations using Akyuz-Winther, double folding (DFOL), and São Paulo potentials, and with distorted-wave Born approximation and coupled channels calculations using the FRESCO code.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: 306 MeV (15.3 AMeV)
- Grazing angle: 9.4° in the center-of-mass frame
- Sommerfeld parameter: η = 12.9; grazing angular momentum: LG ≈ 129 ħ
- Target thickness: 390 ± 40 μg/cm² 76Ge (98% enriched) on 56 ± 6 μg/cm² carbon backing
- Energy resolution: δE(FWHM) ≈ 0.5 MeV; angular resolution: δθLAB(FWHM) ≈ 0.5°
- Total reaction cross sections: AW = 2807 mb, DFOL = 2819 mb, SPP = 2799 mb
- B(E2; 0⁺→2⁺) = 0.0333 e²b² for 20Ne; B(E2; 0⁺→2⁺) = 0.2735 e²b² for 76Ge
- Measured angular range: 5° ≤ θc.m. ≤ 22°
- Coupling effects become important above 0.3 fm⁻¹ transferred momentum

## Context

This work is part of the NUMEN project at INFN-LNS, which aims to extract nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay via double charge exchange reactions. It provides the first elastic and inelastic scattering data for the 20Ne + 76Ge system at 306 MeV, extending prior optical model studies of heavy-ion scattering (Akyuz and Winther, 1979; Broglia and Winther, 1991) and coupled channels analyses of systems like 12C+12C (Ohkubo, 2014) and 16O+27Al (Zagatto, 2018). The results enable improved characterization of initial-state interactions for future double charge exchange measurements on 76Ge.
