# The strongly interacting Quark Gluon Plasma, and the critical behaviour of QCD at imaginary chemical potential

> We explore the highly non-perturbative hot region of the QCD phase diagram close to Tc by use of an imaginary chemical potential mu which avoids the sign problem. The number density and the quark number susceptibility are consistent with a critical behaviour associated with the transition line in the negative mu^2 half-plane. We compare the analytic continuation of these results with various phenomenological models, none of which provides a satisfactory description of data, a failure on which we make some comments. These results complement and extend the information obtained via the analysis of the susceptibilities evaluated at zero mu, yielding a simple description of the candidate strongly interacting QGP phase. As a byproduct of our analysis we investigate the Polyakov loop and its hermitian conjugate. Our data offer a vivid evidence of the importance of the complex nature of the functional integral measure, which results in L (mu) ne \bar L(mu) for a real chemical potential.

# The strongly interacting Quark Gluon Plasma, and the critical behaviour of QCD at imaginary chemical potential

## Key claim

The non-perturbative features of the strongly interacting Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) near T_c can be described by a conventional critical behaviour associated with the chiral transition line in the negative μ² half-plane, rather than by free-field or simple hadron-resonance-gas models. The data are consistent with a power-law equation of state, but the critical exponent and critical point location depend on the fitting range and constraints, with no single fit providing a fully satisfactory description across all intervals.

## Method

Lattice QCD simulations were performed on a 16³ × 4 lattice with four flavors of degenerate staggered fermions at β = 5.1 (T ≈ T_RW, the endpoint of the Roberge-Weiss transition) and bare quark mass m̂ = 0.05, using an imaginary chemical potential μ_I to avoid the sign problem. The number density, quark number susceptibility, chiral condensate, and Polyakov loop were measured as functions of μ_I and fitted to power-law forms motivated by critical behaviour near the transition line, and the results were analytically continued to real chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The critical fit to the number density n(μ_I) = A μ_I (μ_I^c² - μ_I²)^α over the entire μ_I interval with unconstrained μ_I^c gave A = -0.94(4), μ_I^c² = 0.0804(2), α = 0.28(2), with reduced χ² = 2.4.
- The exponent α from the number density fits ranged between 0.26(3) and 0.34(8) depending on the fitting interval, with μ_I^c² between 0.078(4) and 0.091(12).
- The quark number susceptibility fit gave γ = 0.66(16) with unconstrained μ_I^c, and γ = 0.44(22) with μ_I^c constrained to (π/12)².
- The chiral condensate fit to <ψ̄ψ> = K(b - μ²)^α_c + A gave α_c = 0.47(2) over the full range, and α_c = 0.32(12) when constraining to μ_I > 0.2.
- The Polyakov loop absolute value fit gave an exponent of 0.45(2).
- The free-field parametrisation for the number density on a 16³ × 4 lattice with m_q = 0.05 was n(μ)_free = 3 sinh(μ/T) / (K + cosh(μ/T)).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the quark-gluon plasma at non-zero baryon density using the imaginary chemical potential approach, complementing earlier analyses of susceptibilities at zero chemical potential (D'Elia 2004, D'Elia 2005) and higher-temperature regimes (Fodor 2002, Ejiri 2005). The results contradict simple free-field behaviour and Hadron Resonance Gas model predictions (Toublan 2004, Karsch 2003), and instead support a critical description linked to the chiral transition line at negative μ². The analysis enables a parametrised equation of state for the sQGP region that can be analytically continued to real chemical potential, and provides a direct lattice demonstration of the complex phase of the fermion determinant through the asymmetry L(μ) ≠ L̄(μ) at real μ.
