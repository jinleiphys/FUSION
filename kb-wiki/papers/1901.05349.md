# Nuclear configurational entropy of the energy-energy correlation in $e^+e^-$ annihilation processes

> In this paper the nuclear configurational entropy of the energy--energy correlation function is scrutinized, in $e^+e^-$ collisions to hadrons in the back-to-back region, within the QCD perturbative theory. The critical points of the nuclear configurational entropy concept, in the hot nuclear system, is shown to correspond to the critical angle between the two scattered hadrons, corroborating with phenomenological data with great accuracy. The main tools employ the main coefficients of the soft-gluon resummation formula, taking into account logarithmically enhanced contributions up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy.

## Key claim

The nuclear configurational entropy (NCE) of the energy-energy correlation function in $e^+e^-$ annihilation to hadrons has a global minimum at a critical angle $\chi \approx 4.22^{\circ}$, which corresponds to the most probable nuclear system configuration and agrees with phenomenological data within 1.7% accuracy, but this result is obtained specifically within perturbative QCD using soft-gluon resummation up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy and only for the back-to-back region.

## Method

The author computed the nuclear configurational entropy of the energy-energy correlation (EEC) function in $e^+e^-$ annihilation to hadrons by first calculating the Fourier transform of the EEC cross section into momentum space, then deriving the modal fraction (the normalized power spectrum), and finally evaluating the Shannon-type entropy integral over all momentum modes. The EEC itself was computed using the Sudakov resummation formalism with coefficients $A^{[1]}$, $A^{[2]}$, $A^{[3]}$, $B^{[1]}$, $B^{[2]}$, and $H^{[1]}$ taken from the literature, incorporating logarithmically enhanced contributions up to NNLL accuracy, with the QCD scale $\Lambda^{n_F=5}_{\rm QCD}=0.1665$ GeV and $\alpha_s(M_Z)=0.130$.

## Key numbers

- The global minimum of the nuclear configurational entropy occurs at $\chi \approx 4.22^{\circ}$, with entropy value $S_\Upsigma \approx 2.42$.
- This critical angle agrees with phenomenological data from Florian (2013) within 1.7% accuracy.
- The maximum critical point of the EEC itself (not the entropy) is at $\chi \approx 4.3^{\circ}$, where the EEC equals 1.63.
- The QCD parameters used were $\Lambda^{n_F=5}_{\rm QCD}=0.1665$ GeV and $\alpha_s(M_Z)=0.130$, with renormalization scale $\mu_R=Q$.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear configurational entropy paradigm previously developed by Karapetyan (2016, 2017, 2018) for nuclear reaction cross sections to the specific observable of the energy-energy correlation in $e^+e^-$ annihilation. It corroborates the phenomenological results of Florian (2013) for the EEC in the back-to-back region, where the EEC shows a maximum at $\chi \approx 4.3^{\circ}$. The approach enables prediction of the dominant nuclear system configurations from an information-theoretic perspective and can be extended to other QCD observables and AdS/QCD setups involving dilatons and warp factors.
