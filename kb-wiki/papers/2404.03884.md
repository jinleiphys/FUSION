# Gamow-Teller strength distributions of 18O and well-deformed nuclei 24,26Mg by deformed QRPA

> We investigate the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength distributions of {strongly} deformed nuclei, $^{24,26}$Mg, as well as of $^{18}$O. The calculations are performed within a deformed quasi-particle random phase approximation (DQRPA) which explicitly includes the deformation degree of freedom in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) and RPA calculations. The residual particle-particle ($p-p$) interaction as well as the particle-hole ($p-h$) interaction are extracted from Brückner $G$-matrix calculations. The {residual interaction} dependence of the low-lying GT strength of these strongly deformed nuclei is examined by changing the strength of the residual $p-p$ and $p-h$ interactions. We have found that the low-lying GT peaks are quite similar in energy to those found in {spherical} $N=Z$ and $N=Z+2$ nuclei near magic shells, but the configurations {of $^{24,26}$Mg are largely mixed by} the pairing correlations and the deformation. Our results are compared to the experimental GT $(\pm)$ transition data by ($t$, $^3$He) and ($^{3}$He, $t$) reactions, {and found to reproduce the main features of GT strength distributions.

## Key claim

The low-lying Gamow-Teller peaks in the strongly deformed nuclei 24,26Mg are found at energies quite similar to those in spherical N=Z and N=Z+2 nuclei near magic shells, but the underlying configurations are largely mixed by pairing correlations and deformation, rather than being simple particle-hole excitations.

## Method

The authors performed deformed quasi-particle random phase approximation (DQRPA) calculations based on a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) mean field with axial deformation, using the SkP parameter set for Mg isotopes and SLy4 for 18O. The residual particle-particle and particle-hole interactions were extracted from Brückner G-matrix calculations using the CD Bonn potential, and the strength of these residual interactions was varied to examine their dependence on the low-lying GT strength.

## Key numbers

- 24Mg: deformation parameter β2 = 0.45 at ground state minimal energy; proton pairing gap Δp = 0.178 MeV, neutron pairing gap Δn = 0.184 MeV
- 26Mg: deformation parameter β2 = 0.45 at ground state minimal energy; proton pairing gap Δp = 0.149 MeV, neutron pairing gap Δn = 0.300 MeV
- 18O: deformation parameter β2 = 0.03 at ground state minimal energy; proton pairing gap Δp = 0.03 MeV, neutron pairing gap Δn = 0.13 MeV
- A quenching factor q = 0.684 was adopted for the GT operator, taken from a shell model study of nuclear β- decay half-lives in fp and fpg shell nuclei
- The Fermi energy gap between protons and neutrons is about 1.5 MeV for 24Mg and about 6 MeV for 26Mg and 16O
- The first low-lying GT(-) state of 24Mg appears at excitation energy Eex ≈ 0-1 MeV, with the dominant configuration being π 1/2+3 (d3/2) → ν 3/2+1 (d5/2) particle-hole excitation (forward amplitude X = 0.640)
- The first low-lying GT(-) state of 26Mg appears at Eex ≈ 1 MeV, with the dominant configuration being π 3/2+1 (d5/2) → ν 3/2+1 (d5/2) hole-hole excitation (forward amplitude X = 0.54)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DQRPA calculations (Ha 2015a, Ha 2015b, Ha 2016) by using a Skyrme-type mean field instead of the Woods-Saxon potential, and by employing realistic residual interactions from the Brückner G-matrix with the CD Bonn potential. The study is motivated by the need for reliable GT strength distributions for neutrino-induced reactions in supernovae and for electron capture rates in stellar burning, where prior theoretical results for 24Mg showed inconsistencies (Fuller 1982, James 2007). The results enable improved modeling of weak interaction processes in deformed nuclei relevant to supernova neutrino physics and nucleosynthesis, and provide a benchmark for comparing with experimental GT data from charge-exchange reactions at RCNP and other facilities.
