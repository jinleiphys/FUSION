# Suppression of Ground-State Magnetization in Finite-Sized Systems Due to Off-Diagonal Interaction Fluctuations

> We study a generic model of interacting fermions in a finite-sized disordered system. We show that the off-diagonal interaction matrix elements induce density of states fluctuations which generically favor a minimum spin ground state at large interaction amplitude, $U$. This effect competes with the exchange effect which favors large magnetization at large $U$, and it suppresses this exchange magnetization in a large parameter range. When off-diagonal fluctuations dominate, the model predicts a spin gap which is larger for odd-spin ground states as for even-spin, suggesting a simple experimental signature of this off-diagonal effect in Coulomb blockade transport measurements.

# Suppression of Ground-State Magnetization in Finite-Sized Systems Due to Off-Diagonal Interaction Fluctuations

## Key claim

Off-diagonal interaction matrix elements induce density-of-states fluctuations that generically favor a minimum-spin ground state at large interaction amplitude \(U\), competing with and suppressing the exchange-driven magnetization that would otherwise dominate. This suppression is strongest when the off-diagonal fluctuations dominate over the mean-field spin-spin interaction, and the effect produces a spin gap that is larger for odd-spin ground states than for even-spin ground states.

## Method

The authors study a generic model of interacting fermions in a finite-sized disordered system, expressed in the basis of Slater determinants constructed from single-particle eigenstates of the non-interacting Hamiltonian. The interaction matrix elements are taken as random, zero-centered Gaussian variables with width \(U\), and the effective Hamiltonian includes both a fluctuating off-diagonal part (the Two-Body Random Interaction Model, TBRIM) and a mean-field ferromagnetic spin-spin interaction of strength \(\lambda U\). The many-body density of states (MBDOS) is computed for each magnetization block, and the variance of each block is shown to scale with a connectivity \(K\) that decreases monotonically with increasing magnetization \(\sigma_z\). Numerical diagonalizations are performed for systems with \(n\) particles and \(m\) orbitals to extract ground-state spin probabilities and spin gaps.

## Key numbers

- The Stoner instability threshold for a clean system with Hubbard interaction is \(U_c = \Delta\), where \(\Delta\) is the single-particle level spacing.
- The variance of the many-body density of states for fixed spin block scales as \(K U^2\), where the connectivity \(K\) is a monotonously decreasing function of \(\sigma_z\).
- For \(n=5\) particles and \(m=10\) orbitals, the probability of a magnetized ground state is plotted as a function of \(U/\Delta\) for \(\lambda = 2, 4, 5,\) and \(7\) (2000 realizations), showing strong suppression by off-diagonal matrix elements.
- For \(n=5\), \(m=14\), and \(\lambda=7\), the probability of a magnetized ground state is also computed (stars in Fig. 3).
- The spin gap \(\Delta_s^U\) for \(\lambda=0\) is computed numerically for \(m=10, 12, 14, 16\) with varying particle number \(n\), and the dilute estimate uses a numerical factor \(\beta = 1.5\).
- The critical magnetic field to flip one spin is \(\langle B_c \rangle = \Delta_s / g \mu_B\).
- The slope of the Coulomb blockade peak position with magnetic field is \(|\partial V_g/\partial B| = g \mu_B/2\) for minimal magnetization difference \(\delta \sigma_z(n) = (-1)^n/2\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the Stoner instability in finite-size disordered metals, specifically building on the perturbative work of Altshuler and the more recent results of Andreev and Kamenev (1998) who found a reduction of the Stoner threshold due to spatial correlations in diffusive wavefunctions. It also relates to the work of Brouwer et al. (1999) on mesoscopic wavefunction fluctuations increasing the probability of nonzero ground-state spin, and Baranger et al. (1999) who proposed that spontaneous magnetization could explain kinks in Coulomb blockade resonances. The present paper introduces a competing off-diagonal fluctuation effect not treated in those prior works, drawing on the Two-Body Random Interaction Model from nuclear physics (French, 1967) and its use in studying thermalization and quantum chaos. The results suggest an experimental signature in Coulomb blockade transport measurements: the even-odd behavior of the spin gap leads to characteristic patterns in the magnetic-field dependence of conductance peak positions, with the scale set by \(U\) rather than \(\Delta\).
