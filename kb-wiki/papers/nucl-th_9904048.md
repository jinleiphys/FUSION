# Dynamical Interpretation of Chemical Freeze-Out Parameters

> It is shown that the condition for chemical freeze-out, average energy per hadron approximately 1 GeV, selects the softest point of the equation of state, namely the point where the pressure divided by the energy density has a minimum. The sensitivity to the equation of state used is discussed. The previously proposed mixed phase model, which is consistent with lattice QCD data naturally leads to the chemical freeze-out condition.

# Dynamical Interpretation of Chemical Freeze-Out Parameters

## Key claim
The condition that chemical freeze-out occurs when the average energy per hadron is approximately 1 GeV selects the softest point of the equation of state, defined as the minimum of the pressure-to-energy-density ratio p/ε. This correlation holds for the mixed phase model but not for purely hadronic models or two-phase bag models, and the authors argue this supports a crossover type of deconfinement phase transition rather than a first-order transition.

## Method
The authors used the mixed phase (MP) model, which assumes homogeneous coexistence of unbound quarks and gluons with hadrons, with a density-dependent mean-field potential for quarks and gluons of the form U(ρ) = A/ρ^γ. They calculated isoentropic expansion trajectories for central Au+Au collisions using a scaled hydrodynamical model with the MP equation of state, starting from initial conditions estimated from a transport model. The resulting trajectories in the T-μ_B plane were compared to chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from hadronic abundances at SPS, AGS, and SIS energies.

## Key numbers
- The softest point of the MP equation of state at zero baryon density is located at energy density ε_SP ≈ 0.45 GeV/fm³.
- The deconfinement temperature in the MP model for two light quark flavors at n_B=0 is T_dec = 153 MeV.
- The MP model parameters are fixed as γ = 0.62 and A^(1/(3γ+1)) = 250 MeV from the pure gluonic SU(3) case.
- At beam energies E_lab = 150 and 10 AGeV, the admixture of unbound quarks at the softest point amounts to about 13% and 8%, respectively.
- The softest point is gradually washed out at baryon densities n_B ≳ 0.4 n₀.
- The two-phase bag model has its softest point at ε_SP > 1 GeV/fm³, independent of n_B.

## Context
This work extends the observation by Cleymans and Redlich (1998, 1999) that chemical freeze-out parameters lie on a unique curve corresponding to fixed energy per hadron of approximately 1 GeV, by providing a dynamical interpretation in terms of the equation of state. The mixed phase model, previously proposed by Nikonov, Shanenko, and Toneev (1998) and Toneev, Nikonov, and Shanenko (1998), is consistent with lattice QCD data from Karsch and differs from both the interacting hadron gas model (which has no soft point) and the two-phase bag model of Heiselberg and Staubo (1994) (which has a first-order phase transition with a different softest point location). The results enable further hydrodynamic studies of expansion dynamics and suggest that the crossover-type deconfinement transition is favored over a first-order transition for the SU(3) system with massive quarks.
