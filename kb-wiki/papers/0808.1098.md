# Edward Teller and Nuclei: Along the Trail to the Neutrino

> I discuss two of Edward Teller's contributions to nuclear physics, the introduction of the Gamow-Teller operator in beta decay and the formulation of the Goldhaber-Teller model for electric dipole transitions, in the context of efforts to understand the weak interaction and the nature of the neutrino.

# Edward Teller and Nuclei: Along the Trail to the Neutrino

## Key claim
Two of Edward Teller's contributions, the Gamow-Teller operator in beta decay and the Goldhaber-Teller model for electric dipole transitions, are presented as foundational to understanding the weak interaction and neutrino properties, but the paper emphasizes that the Gamow-Teller paper did not explicitly discuss parity violation, and that the full resolution of the weak interaction's V-A structure required experimental discoveries 20 years later.

## Method
The paper reviews the historical development of the Gamow-Teller operator, which introduced a spin-dependent axial-vector current to explain observed beta-decay selection rules beyond Fermi's vector-current model, and the Goldhaber-Teller collective model of the giant dipole resonance, in which neutrons oscillate against protons with a linear restoring force from the nuclear symmetry energy. The author then connects these models to modern neutrino physics, including the seesaw mechanism for neutrino mass and the neutrino process for nucleosynthesis in supernovae.

## Key numbers
- The Gamow-Teller paper argued that first-forbidden beta-decay rates would be suppressed by a factor of approximately 10^{-3} relative to allowed transitions.
- Second-forbidden operators would be suppressed by an additional factor of approximately 10^{-2} due to nucleon velocities in the nucleus of order |v/c| ~ 0.1.
- The energy-weighted E1 sum rule saturated by the Goldhaber-Teller model is given by (2M/hbar^2) * sum_f (E_f - E_gs) * |<f| sum_i z(i) tau_3/2 |gs>|^2 = A/4.
- The amplitude for neutrinoless double beta decay is proportional to the handedness admixture m_nu / E_nu ~ 3 x 10^{-8} * (m_nu / 1 eV), with the exchanged neutrino energy estimated as E_nu ~ R_nucleus^{-1} ~ 6 fm^{-1}.
- Neutrino mass bounds are given as sum_i m_i < 6.6 eV (laboratory) and sum_i m_i < 0.7 eV (cosmology).
- At least one neutrino has a mass m > 0.04 eV from oscillation experiments.
- The seesaw mechanism yields m_nu^light ~ m_D * (m_D / M_R); taking m_nu ~ sqrt(delta m^2_atmos) ~ 0.05 eV and m_D ~ m_top ~ 180 GeV gives M_R ~ 0.3 x 10^{15} GeV.
- Each core-collapse supernova releases about 4 x 10^{57} muon and tauon neutrinos with average energy ~ 25 MeV; the total fluence through the Ne shell is ~ 10^{38} / cm^2.
- The neutrino-induced cross section for converting Ne to F is sigma ~ 3 x 10^{-41} cm^2, leading to approximately 1/300 of the Ne shell converted to F.
- The correct ratio of 19F/20Ne is obtained for a heavy-flavor neutrino temperature of ~ 6 MeV.

## Context
This paper extends the historical narrative of Teller's work by connecting the Gamow-Teller operator (introduced in Gamow and Teller (1936)) and the Goldhaber-Teller model (Goldhaber and Teller (1948)) to modern neutrino physics, including the V-A structure confirmed by Lee and Yang (1957), Wu et al. (1957), and Goldhaber, Grodzins, and Sunyar (1957). The discussion of neutrino mass and the seesaw mechanism builds on Pontecorvo's early work on neutrino identity and the Cl detector experiments by Davis at Savannah River, and enables calculations of the neutrino process in supernovae for nucleosynthesis of elements like fluorine.
