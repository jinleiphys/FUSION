# A Second Look at Single Photon Production in S+Au Collisions at 200 A$\cdot$GeV and Implications for Quark Hadron Phase Transition

> We reanalyze the production of single photons in S+Au collisions at CERN SPS to investigate: i) the consequences of using a much richer equation of state for hadrons than the one used in an earlier study by us; and, ii) to see if the recent estimates of photon production in quark-matter (at two loop level) by Aurenche et al. are consistent with the upper limit of the photon production measured by the WA80 experiment. We find that the measured upper limit is consistent with a quark hadron phase transition. The measured upper limit is also consistent with a scenario where no phase transition takes place, but where the hadronic matter reaches a density of several hadrons per unit volume; which is rather unphysical.

# A Second Look at Single Photon Production in S+Au Collisions at 200 A·GeV and Implications for Quark Hadron Phase Transition

## Key claim
The measured upper limit on single photon production from the WA80 experiment is consistent with a quark-hadron phase transition scenario when using two-loop photon emission rates from quark matter. However, the same upper limit is also consistent with a no-phase-transition scenario, but only if the hadronic matter reaches a density of several hadrons per cubic femtometer, which the authors consider unphysical.

## Method
The authors performed a hydrodynamic evolution of S+Au collisions at 200 A·GeV, assuming a chemically and thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma formed at τ₀ = 1 fm/c with initial temperature determined from the Bjorken condition using a particle rapidity density of 225. They used a hadronic equation of state including all hadrons and resonances with mass below 2.5 GeV, with a phase transition temperature of 160 MeV and freeze-out at 120 MeV. Photon production rates from quark matter were evaluated at both one-loop and two-loop order (the latter from Aurenche et al.), while hadronic photon rates came from a two-loop approximation of the photon self-energy including π-ρ interactions and the A₁ resonance.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Srivastava and Sinha, 1998) which argued that WA80 data were consistent with quark-gluon plasma formation but not with a pure hadronic scenario. It incorporates the two-loop photon emission rates from quark matter calculated by Aurenche et al., which include bremsstrahlung and a new quark-antiquark annihilation-with-scattering mechanism that dominates hard photon production. The results are compared to the earlier one-loop calculations and to the work of Cleymans, Redlich, and Srivastava (1999), who used a comprehensive hadronic equation of state for Pb+Pb collisions. The findings enable future studies of chemically non-equilibrated plasmas and pre-equilibrium photon contributions from parton cascade models.
