# Momentum--dependent nuclear mean fields and collective flow in heavy ion collisions

> We use the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model to simulate the dynamical evolution of heavy ion collisions and to compare the effects of two parametrizations of the momentum--dependent nuclear mean field that have identical properties in cold nuclear matter. We compare with recent data on nuclear flow, as characterized by transverse momentum distributions and flow ($F$) variables for symmetric and asymmetric systems. We find that the precise functional dependence of the nuclear mean field on the particle momentum is important. With our approach, we also confirm that the difference between symmetric and asymmetric systems can be used to pin down the density and momentum dependence of the nuclear self consistent one--body potential, independently. All the data can be reproduced very well with a momentum--dependent interaction with compressibility K = 210 MeV.

## Key claim

The precise functional dependence of the nuclear mean field on particle momentum is important for reproducing heavy ion collision data; two parametrizations (GBD and MDYI) that share identical properties in cold nuclear matter at saturation density produce significantly different collective flow in nonequilibrium dynamical situations, and all data considered can be reproduced very well with a momentum-dependent interaction having compressibility K = 210 MeV.

## Method

The Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model was used to simulate the dynamical evolution of heavy ion collisions for symmetric (Nb+Nb, Au+Au) and asymmetric (Ar+Pb) systems at beam energies from 250 to 1800 MeV/nucleon. Two parametrizations of the momentum-dependent nuclear mean field (GBD and MDYI) were compared, along with momentum-independent hard and soft potentials, after reparametrizing both to share identical saturation properties (E/A = -16 MeV, ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, m*/m = 0.67, U(ρ₀, p→∞) = 30.5 MeV, K = 210 MeV). Transverse momentum distributions and flow parameter F values were calculated and compared with experimental data from the DIOGENE collaboration, the Riverside/GSI/LBL Streamer chamber group, and the EOS TPC Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility K = 210 MeV for both reparametrized NGBD and NMDYI potentials, obtained by setting σ = 12/11, E/A = -16 MeV, ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, U(ρ₀, p→∞) = 30.5 MeV, and m*/m = 0.67
- For symmetric Nb+Nb at E = 400 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 2.1 fm, the asymptotic average transverse momenta from hard momentum-independent and soft momentum-dependent potentials differ by only 4 MeV/c
- In Vlasov mode (no hard collisions), NGBD and NMDYI potentials produce average transverse momenta differing by approximately 10 MeV/c
- For Ar+Pb at 400 MeV/nucleon, the flow parameter F as a function of impact parameter shows NMDYI reproduces DIOGENE data while NGBD gives larger values
- For Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/A, imposing exact angular momentum conservation increases the flow parameter by roughly 23% in cascade simulations and by 8% in BUU calculations

## Context

This work extends the findings of Pan and Danielewicz (1993) who showed that flow data for asymmetric systems could differentiate between hard momentum-independent and soft momentum-dependent equations of state, favoring the latter. The paper confirms that asymmetric systems can separate interactions with similar compressibility but different momentum dependence, and provides the first comparison of DIOGENE and EOS TPC data with BUU results. The study enables a consistent picture of the nuclear equation of state with compressibility around 210 MeV, and establishes that MDYI-type interactions are theoretically preferable to GBD-type because MDYI has Galilean invariance from the start and can be identified with the Fourier transform of a Yukawa potential.
