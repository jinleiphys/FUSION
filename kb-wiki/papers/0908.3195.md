# Suppression of the stellar enhancement factor and the reaction 85Rb(p,n)85Sr

> It is shown that a Coulomb suppression of the stellar enhancement factor occurs in many endothermic reactions at and far from stability. Contrary to common assumptions, reaction measurements for astrophysics with minimal impact of stellar enhancement should be preferably performed for those reactions instead of their reverses, despite of their negative reaction Q-value. As a demonstration, the cross section of the astrophysically relevant 85Rb(p,n)85Sr reaction has been measured by activation between 2.16<=E_{c.m.}<= 3.96 MeV and the astrophysical reaction rates at p-process temperatures for (p,n) as well as (n,p) are directly inferred from the data. Additionally, our results confirm a previously derived modification of a global optical proton potential. The presented arguments are also relevant for other alpha- and proton-induced reactions in the p-, rp-, and nu-p-processes.

# Suppression of the stellar enhancement factor and the reaction 85Rb(p,n)85Sr

## Key claim

Contrary to the common assumption that exothermic reactions should always be measured to minimize stellar enhancement, a Coulomb suppression effect causes the stellar enhancement factor (SEF) to be smaller for many endothermic reactions than for their exothermic reverses, provided the reaction Q-value is small compared to the Coulomb barrier and the entrance and exit channels have different Coulomb barriers. This means that for such reactions, measuring the endothermic direction can yield a laboratory cross section closer to the true stellar rate than measuring the exothermic direction.

## Method

The authors performed a large-scale theoretical survey using the NON-SMOKER statistical model code to compute stellar enhancement factors for reactions involving light projectiles (nucleons, alpha particles) on targets from neon to bismuth between the proton and neutron driplines, comparing forward (exothermic) and reverse (endothermic) SEF values at temperatures up to 4.5 GK. As an experimental demonstration, they measured the cross section of the endothermic reaction 85Rb(p,n)85Sr (Q = -1.847 MeV) using the activation method at center-of-mass energies between 2.16 and 3.96 MeV, and directly inferred astrophysical reaction rates for both the (p,n) and (n,p) directions at p-process temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The reaction 85Rb(p,n)85Sr has a Q-value of -1.847 MeV.
- Cross sections were measured for center-of-mass energies in the range 2.16 ≤ E_c.m. ≤ 3.96 MeV.
- The theoretical survey identified 1200 reactions across the nuclear chart where the reverse (endothermic) SEF is smaller than the forward (exothermic) SEF, with the additional constraints that f_rev ≤ 1.5 and f_forw/f_rev ≥ 1.1 at temperatures T ≤ 4.5 GK.
- For (p,n) reactions with f_rev < f_forw, the maximum allowed |Q| increases with target charge Z, reflecting the height of the Coulomb barrier.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of stellar enhancement factors beyond the standard assumption that exothermic reactions always have smaller SEF, which was based on the larger number of energetically possible transitions in exothermic directions. The authors provide a systematic survey of the effect across the nuclear chart, identifying specific target nuclei where the suppression occurs, including many stable or long-lived targets for (p,n) and (alpha,n) reactions listed in Tables V and VIII. The experimental measurement of 85Rb(p,n)85Sr also confirms a previously derived modification of a global optical proton potential (Kiss 2008). The results are relevant for p-process, rp-process, and nu-p-process nucleosynthesis studies, and provide guidance for experimentalists on which reaction direction to measure to obtain rates with minimal stellar enhancement.
