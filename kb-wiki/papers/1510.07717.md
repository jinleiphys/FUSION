# ${^3{\rm He}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Be}}$ and ${^3{\rm H}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Li}}$ astrophysical $S$ factors from the no-core shell model with continuum

> The ${^3{\rm He}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Be}}$ and ${^3{\rm H}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Li}}$ astrophysical $S$ factors are calculated within the no-core shell model with continuum using a renormalized chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction. The ${^3{\rm He}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Be}}$ astrophysical $S$ factors agree reasonably well with the experimental data while the ${^3{\rm H}}(α,γ){^7{\rm Li}}$ ones are overestimated. The seven-nucleon bound and resonance states and the $α+{^3{\rm He}}/{^3{\rm H}}$ elastic scattering are also studied and compared with experiment. The low-lying resonance properties are rather well reproduced by our approach. At low energies, the $s$-wave phase shift, which is non-resonant, is overestimated.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) using a renormalized chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction yields astrophysical S factors for 3He(α,γ)7Be that agree reasonably well with experimental data, while the 3H(α,γ)7Li S factors are overestimated; the low-energy s-wave phase shift, which is non-resonant, is overestimated in both systems.

## Method

The authors solved the microscopic Schrödinger equation for the seven-nucleon systems using the NCSMC approach, which combines square-integrable no-core shell model (NCSM) eigenstates of 7Be and 7Li with α+3He and α+3H cluster states built from NCSM eigenstates of 4He, 3He, and 3H via the resonating-group method (RGM). The nucleon-nucleon interaction was a chiral N3LO potential softened by the similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) method with resolution scale Λ=2.15 fm−1, and the coupled equations were solved using the microscopic R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. Electromagnetic multipole matrix elements between scattering and bound states were computed to obtain the astrophysical S factors.

## Key numbers

- 7Be ground-state energy: −1.52 MeV (NCSMC) vs. −1.587 MeV (experiment), relative to the α+3He threshold
- 7Li ground-state energy: −2.43 MeV (NCSMC) vs. −2.467 MeV (experiment), relative to the α+3H threshold
- 7Be charge radius: 2.62 fm (NCSMC) vs. 2.647(17) fm (experiment)
- 7Li charge radius: 2.42 fm (NCSMC) vs. 2.39(3) fm (experiment)
- 7Be quadrupole moment: −6.14 e fm2 (NCSMC) vs. no experimental value available
- 7Li quadrupole moment: −3.72 e fm2 (NCSMC) vs. −4.00(3) e fm2 (experiment)
- 7Be first 7/2− resonance energy: 3.61 MeV (NCSMC) vs. 2.98 MeV (experiment), width 0.33 MeV vs. 0.175 MeV
- 7Li first 7/2− resonance energy: 2.79 MeV (NCSMC) vs. 2.18 MeV (experiment), width 0.214 MeV vs. 0.069 MeV
- α+3He 1/2+ scattering length: 7.7 fm (NCSMC) vs. 41.06 fm (from experimental phase shifts)
- α+3H 1/2+ scattering length: 5.4 fm (NCSMC) vs. 13.05 fm (from experimental phase shifts)
- S3He(α,γ)7Be(0): 0.59 keV b (NCSMC) vs. 0.56(2)(2) keV b (experiment)
- S3H(α,γ)7Li(0): 0.13 keV b (NCSMC) vs. 0.1067(4)(60) keV b (experiment)

## Context

This work extends the NCSMC approach, previously applied to lighter systems such as 7Be(p,γ)8B by Navrátil et al. (2011), to the first NCSMC study of radiative capture where the lightest colliding nucleus has three nucleons. It contrasts with earlier microscopic approaches by Kajino (1986), Mertelmeier and Hofmann (1986), and Nollett (2001) that were not parameter-free, and with the external-capture model of Tombrello and Parker (1963). The results enable future inclusion of three-nucleon forces and larger model spaces to improve agreement with experimental phase shifts and S factors, particularly for the 3H(α,γ)7Li reaction.
