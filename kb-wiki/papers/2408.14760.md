# Interrelation between $\bar{p}$-Ca Atom Spectra and Nuclear Density Profiles

> This work studies $\bar{p}$-Ca atom spectra in light of the strong shifts and level widths, using the optical model with several types of parametric coefficients. The spectroscopic quantities are obtained as the eigenvalues of the Dirac equation, where the nuclear densities computed via nuclear Density Functional Theory and the effect of the anomalous magnetic moment are incorporated. The results indicate that the isovector term's contribution to the optical potential is crucial for explaining the systematical differences in the strong shifts between $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca. Furthermore, it is found that both the strong shifts and the level widths exhibit significant dependence on the nuclear density profiles. These findings provide critical insights into the nuclear structures, particularly in the context of Calcium isotopes, by offering a more comprehensive understanding of the underlying nuclear-hadron properties.

## Key claim
The isovector term in the optical potential is crucial for explaining the systematic differences in strong shifts between 40Ca and 48Ca, and both strong shifts and level widths show significant dependence on the nuclear density profiles, but this conclusion is conditional on using realistic density distributions beyond the conventional 3-parameter Fermi model.

## Method
The authors solved the Dirac equation for antiproton-Ca systems using a matrix diagonalization method in coordinate space, incorporating the optical potential within the linear density approximation with s-wave and p-wave terms. Nuclear densities were computed using Skyrme Hartree-Fock Density Functional Theory with several parameter sets, as well as the 3-parameter Fermi model for comparison. The calculations included the Coulomb potential with vacuum polarization and the anomalous magnetic moment of the antiproton (kappa = 1.79284734), focusing on the n=5 and n=6 orbits for 40Ca and 48Ca.

## Key numbers
- The conventional optical model with parameter b0 = (1.5 + 2.5i) fm predicts strong shifts around 3 eV for both 40Ca and 48Ca, while experimental data show systematic differences by a factor of several between these isotopes
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the antiproton used in the Dirac equation is kappa = 1.79284734
- The fine structure constant used is alpha = 1/137.036
- The averaged nucleon mass used is M_N = 938.918 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous global fitting studies of antiprotonic atoms (Batty 1995, Friedman 2004, 2005) which concluded that only the isoscalar term in the optical potential is needed, by showing that the isovector b1 term becomes important for Calcium isotopes. It addresses discrepancies noted by Hartmann (2001) and Klos (2007) that isotope differences in strong shifts cannot be explained by isoscalar-only potentials. The use of Skyrme Hartree-Fock densities from Density Functional Theory (Colo 2020) goes beyond the simple 2-parameter and 3-parameter Fermi models used in prior work (Garcia-Recio 1992, Schmidt 1998, Trzcinska 2001). This enables future extraction of nuclear density profiles from planned high-resolution X-ray spectroscopy experiments using quantum sensors (Fujioka 2024).
