# Fluctuations in the Canonical Ensemble

> The particle number and energy fluctuations in the system of charged particles are studied in the canonical ensemble for non-zero net values of the conserved charge. In the thermodynamic limit the fluctuations in the canonical ensemble are different from the fluctuations in the grand canonical one. The system with several species of particles is considered. We calculate the quantum statistics effects which can be taken into account for the canonical ensemble fluctuations in the infinite volume limit. The fluctuations of the particle numbers in the pion-nucleon gas are considered in the canonical ensemble as an example of the system with two conserved charges - baryonic number and electric charge.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the thermodynamic limit, particle number and energy fluctuations in the canonical ensemble (CE) remain different from those in the grand canonical ensemble (GCE), even though average quantities become equivalent; this difference depends on the net conserved charge and is not removed by taking the infinite volume limit.

## Method

The authors calculate the partition functions and scaled variances (variance divided by mean) for particle number and energy fluctuations in the CE for a multi-species Boltzmann ideal gas with non-zero net conserved charge, using modified Bessel functions to enforce exact charge conservation. They extend the formalism to include particles with charges ±1 and ±2, and then incorporate quantum statistics (Bose and Fermi) effects in the thermodynamic limit using occupation number correlators. As an application, they compute fluctuations for a pion-nucleon gas with two conserved charges (baryonic number and electric charge).

## Key numbers

- For Q=0 in the small system limit (z→0): ⟨N±⟩_CE ≃ z², while ⟨N±⟩_GCE = z.
- For Q≥1 in the small system limit: ⟨N+⟩_CE ≃ Q, ⟨N-⟩_CE ≃ z²/(Q+1).
- In the thermodynamic limit (z→∞) at fixed Q/2z=y: ω_CE^+ ≃ 1/2 − y/(2√(1+y²)), ω_CE^- ≃ 1/2 + y/(2√(1+y²)), ω_CE^ch ≃ 1/(1+y²).
- For Q=0 in the thermodynamic limit: ω_CE^± ≃ 1/2, ω_CE^ch ≃ 1.
- For the system with single and double charged particles at Q=0 in the thermodynamic limit: ω_CE^± ≃ 0.5 when z̃/z is either much smaller or much larger than 1, with a maximum of 5/9 at z=2z̃.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of CE fluctuations for one particle species with zero net charge (Begun et al., 2004) to systems with non-zero net charge and multiple species. It contradicts the common assumption that all statistical ensembles become equivalent for fluctuations in the thermodynamic limit, as previously noted for the zero-charge case. The results enable more accurate modeling of event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions, where the created system has positive baryonic number and electric charge, and can be applied to systems with multiple conserved charges such as the pion-nucleon gas.
