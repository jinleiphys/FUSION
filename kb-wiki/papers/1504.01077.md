# Electric Dipole Polarizability in $^{208}$Pb as a Probe of the Symmetry Energy and Neutron Matter around $ρ_0/3$

> It is currently a big challenge to accurately determine the symmetry energy $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ)$ and the pure neutron matter equation of state $E_{\text{PNM}}(ρ)$, even their values around saturation density $ρ_0 $. We find that the electric dipole polarizability $α_ {\text{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb can be determined uniquely by the magnitude of the $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ)$ or almost equivalently the $E_{\text{PNM}}(ρ)$ at subsaturation densities around $ρ_0/3 $, shedding a light upon the genuine correlation between the $α_ {\text{D}}$ and the $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ)$. By analyzing the experimental data of the $α_ {\text{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb from RCNP using a number of non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field models, we obtain very stringent constraints on $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ)$ and $E_{\text{PNM}}(ρ)$ around $ρ_0/3 $. The obtained constraints are found to be in good agreement with the results extracted in other analyses. In particular, our results provide for the first time the experimental constraints on $E_{\text{PNM}}(ρ)$ around $ρ_0/3 $, which are in harmony with the recent determination of $E_{\text{PNM}}(ρ)$ from microscopic theoretical studies and potentially useful in constraining the largely uncertain many-nucleon interactions in microscopic calculations of neutron matter.

# Electric Dipole Polarizability in \(^{208}\)Pb as a Probe of the Symmetry Energy and Neutron Matter around \(\rho_0/3\)

## Key claim

The electric dipole polarizability \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) in \(^{208}\)Pb is uniquely determined by the magnitude of the symmetry energy \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho)\) or equivalently the pure neutron matter equation of state \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho)\) at subsaturation densities around \(\rho_0/3\), not at saturation density \(\rho_0\) as previously assumed. This correlation is model-independent and holds across 62 non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field models, but only when the symmetry energy is evaluated at densities near \(\rho_0/3\) (approximately 0.05 fm\(^{-3}\)).

## Method

The authors analyzed 62 representative non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) interactions that all reproduce ground-state properties of finite nuclei but predict different density dependences of the symmetry energy. For each interaction, they computed \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) in \(^{208}\)Pb using random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations, then correlated the inverse polarizability \(1/\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) with the symmetry energy \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_r)\) evaluated at various reference densities \(\rho_r\). The experimental \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) value from the Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP) was then used to extract constraints on \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho)\) and \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho)\) via linear fits at the density where the Pearson correlation coefficient \(r\) is maximized.

## Key numbers

- The experimental electric dipole polarizability of \(^{208}\)Pb measured at RCNP is \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}} = 20.1 \pm 0.6\) fm\(^3\) (Tamii 2011).
- The strongest linear correlation between \(1/\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) and \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_r)\) occurs at \(\rho_r = 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\) (approximately \(\rho_0/3\)), with Pearson correlation coefficient \(r = 0.977\).
- The linear fit at \(\rho_r = 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\) gives: \(10^3/\alpha_{\mathrm{D}} = (18.94 \pm 0.85) + (1.94 \pm 0.05) E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_r)\), with \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) in fm\(^3\) and \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}\) in MeV.
- The extracted constraint on the symmetry energy at \(\rho_r = 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\) is \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_r = 0.05~\text{fm}^{-3}) = 15.91 \pm 0.99\) MeV.
- The previously existing constraint on pure neutron matter is \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho = 0.10~\text{fm}^{-3}) = 11.4 \pm 1.0\) MeV (Brown 2013).

## Context

This work resolves a controversy in the literature regarding the correlation between \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) and the symmetry energy. Earlier studies by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010) and Piekarewicz et al. (2012) found conflicting correlations at saturation density, while Roca-Maza et al. (2013) and Zhang and Chen (2014) reported correlations at \(\rho_0\) or \(2\rho_0/3\). The present paper demonstrates that the genuine correlation is with the symmetry energy at \(\rho_0/3\), explaining why previous correlations at higher densities were weaker or model-dependent. The results provide the first experimental constraints on \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho)\) around \(\rho_0/3\), which can be used to constrain many-nucleon interactions in microscopic calculations such as chiral effective field theory (Tews 2013, Wellenhofer 2015) and quantum Monte Carlo (Gandolfi 2012, Gezerlis 2013, Wlazlowski 2014).
