# An explanation of the elliptic flow difference between proton and anti-proton from the UrQMD model with hadron potentials

> The time evolution of both proton and anti-proton $v_2$ flows from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7 GeV are examined by using both pure cascade and mean-field potential versions of the UrQMD model. Due to a stronger repulsion at the early stage introduced by the repulsive potentials and hence much less annihilation probabilities, anti-protons are frozen out earlier with smaller $v_2$ values. Therefore, the experimental data of anti-proton $v_2$ as well as the flow difference between proton and anti-proton can be reasonably described with the potential version of UrQMD.

# An explanation of the elliptic flow difference between proton and anti-proton from the UrQMD model with hadron potentials

## Key claim
The elliptic flow difference between protons and anti-protons in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV can be explained by the UrQMD model with mean-field potentials, where a stronger repulsive potential at early times causes anti-protons to freeze out earlier with smaller v2 values and reduced annihilation probabilities, but only if potentials are applied to both pre-formed and formed hadrons.

## Method
The authors used both the pure cascade (UrQMD/C) and mean-field potential (UrQMD/M) versions of the UrQMD transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV. The mean-field potential version applies a density-dependent potential (with soft equation of state, incompressibility K=314 MeV) to both pre-formed hadrons from string fragmentation and formed baryons, while formed mesons receive no nuclear potential. For each reaction, more than 1 million events were simulated, and the program stopped at t_cut=50 fm/c. The elliptic flow v2 was calculated as the second Fourier coefficient of the azimuthal distribution of emitted particles.

## Key numbers
- Maximum reduced baryon density rho_{B+aB}/rho_0 reaches about 4 at approximately 3 fm/c in central zone (R_cm < 5 fm), returning to normal density at about 7-8 fm/c
- Newly produced baryons can be as many as 30% of total baryons at t=4 fm/c
- Anti-baryons are produced at approximately 1 fm/c and freeze out faster than baryons, with reduced density returning to normal by about 4 fm/c
- The proton v2 calculated with UrQMD/M is only about 2/3 of the experimental data
- At sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV, the equivalent fixed-target beam energy is approximately 29.7 GeV for Au on Au

## Context
This work extends previous investigations using the UrQMD/M model (Li 2007, Li 2008, Li 2010) that addressed HBT puzzles, elliptic flow puzzles, and strange baryon yields. It directly addresses the proton-antiproton v2 splitting observed by the STAR collaboration (Adamczyk 2013) at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. The results are consistent with the explanation from the AMPT model with NJL model (Xu 2013) that attributes the splitting to different mean-field potentials for hadrons and anti-hadrons, but differs in mechanism: here the effect arises from repulsive early pressure on pre-formed hadrons rather than partonic vector potentials. The work also contrasts with the UrQMD hybrid model (Steinheimer 2012) where the flow difference from the hydrodynamic phase is washed out by subsequent cascade transport.
