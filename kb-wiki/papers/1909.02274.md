# The key factor to determine the relation between radius and tidal deformability of neutron stars: slope of symmetry energy

> The constraints on tidal deformability $Λ$ of neutron stars are first extracted from GW170817 by LIGO and Virgo Collaborations but the relation between radius $R$ and tidal deformability $Λ$ is still nuder debate. Using an isospin-dependent parameterized equation of state (EOS), we study the relation between $R$ and $Λ$ of neutron stars and its dependence on parameters of symmetry energy $E_{\rm sym}$ and EOS of symmetric nuclear matter $E_0$ when the mass is fixed as $1.4$ $M_\odot$, $1.0$ $M_\odot$, and $1.8$ $M_\odot$, respectively. We find that, though the changes of high order parameters of $E_{\rm sym}$ and $E_0$ can shift the individual values of $R_{1.4}$ and $Λ_{1.4}$ to different values, the $R_{1.4}\simΛ_{1.4}$ relation approximately locates at the same fitted curve. The slope of symmetry energy $L$ plays the dominated role in determining the $R_{1.4}\simΛ_{1.4}$ relation. By checking the mass dependence of $R\simΛ$ relation, the well fitted $R\simΛ$ relation for 1.4 $M_\odot$ is broken for massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

The slope of symmetry energy $L$ is the dominant factor determining the $R_{1.4}\sim\Lambda_{1.4}$ relation for canonical neutron stars, even though both $L$ and the curvature $K_{\rm sym}$ play almost equally important roles in determining the individual values of $R_{1.4}$ and $\Lambda_{1.4}$; the well-fitted $R\sim\Lambda$ relation for 1.4 $M_\odot$ is broken for massive neutron stars.

## Method

Using an isospin-dependent parameterized equation of state (EOS) with Taylor-expanded forms for symmetric nuclear matter energy $E_0(\rho)$ and symmetry energy $E_{\rm sym}(\rho)$, the authors varied the parameters $L$, $K_{\rm sym}$, $J_{\rm sym}$, and $J_0$ in steps of 5, 50, 100, and 50 MeV respectively within their uncertainties. They solved the TOV equations coupled to the tidal deformability differential equation to compute radius $R$ and tidal deformability $\Lambda$ for neutron stars with fixed masses of 1.0, 1.4, and 1.8 $M_\odot$, then examined how the $R\sim\Lambda$ relation depends on each nuclear matter parameter.

## Key numbers

- The slope of symmetry energy $L$ is fixed at 58.7 MeV as the central value, with uncertainty $\approx 58.7 \pm 28.1$ MeV from terrestrial experiments.
- The refined constraint from GW170817 gives $70 < \Lambda_{1.4} < 580$ at 90% confidence level.
- From the $\Lambda_{1.4}$ constraint, the radius of canonical neutron stars is constrained as $9.11 < R_{1.4} < 13.14$ km.
- Varying $K_{\rm sym}$ and $J_{\rm sym}$ at fixed $L=30$ MeV and $J_0=0$ MeV produces less than 5% deviation from the fitted $R_{1.4}\sim\Lambda_{1.4}$ curve.
- Varying $L$ from 30 to 90 MeV increases the deviation to about 20%.
- Adding the freedom of $J_0$ (changing from 0 to 400 MeV) adds an additional 2% deviation.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Fattoyev (2013, 2014) showing that tidal deformability of low-mass neutron stars is sensitive only to $L$, and by Tsang (2019) finding a strong relation between $\Lambda_{1.4}$ and $K_{\rm sym}$ using over 200 Skyrme EOSs. The authors' previous work (Zhang 2018, 2019, 2019b) had shown individual $R_{1.4}$ and $\Lambda_{1.4}$ values in a 3D parameter space; here they systematically study the $R\sim\Lambda$ relation itself. The results enable future joint constraints from heavy-ion reactions, NICER radius measurements, and LIGO/Virgo gravitational wave events to break degeneracies among $L$, $R_{1.4}$, and $\Lambda_{1.4}$.
