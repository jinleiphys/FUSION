# Quantum fluctuations in the BCS-BEC crossover of two-dimensional Fermi gases

> We present a theoretical study of the ground state of the BCS-BEC crossover in dilute two-dimensional Fermi gases. While the mean-field theory provides a simple and analytical equation of state, the pressure is equal to that of a noninteracting Fermi gas in the entire BCS-BEC crossover, which is not consistent with the features of a weakly interacting Bose condensate in the BEC limit and a weakly interacting Fermi liquid in the BCS limit. The inadequacy of the 2D mean-field theory indicates that the quantum fluctuations are much more pronounced than those in 3D. In this work, we show that the inclusion of the Gaussian quantum fluctuations naturally recovers the above features in both the BEC and the BCS limits. In the BEC limit, the missing logarithmic dependence on the boson chemical potential is recovered by the quantum fluctuations. Near the quantum phase transition from the vacuum to the BEC phase, we compare our equation of state with the known grand canonical equation of state of 2D Bose gases and determine the ratio of the composite boson scattering length $a_{\rm B}$ to the fermion scattering length $a_{\rm 2D}$. We find $a_{\rm B}\simeq 0.56 a_{\rm 2D}$, in good agreement with the exact four-body calculation. We compare our equation of state in the BCS-BEC crossover with recent results from the quantum Monte Carlo simulations and the experimental measurements and find good agreements.

## Key claim

The mean-field theory for two-dimensional Fermi gases incorrectly predicts a constant pressure equal to that of a noninteracting Fermi gas across the entire BCS-BEC crossover; including Gaussian quantum fluctuations recovers the correct weakly interacting Bose condensate behavior in the BEC limit and the Fermi liquid corrections in the BCS limit, with the composite boson scattering length determined to be \(a_{\rm B}\simeq 0.56 a_{\rm 2D}\).

## Method

The authors used a path integral formalism with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a pairing field, then expanded the effective action to Gaussian order around the mean-field saddle point. The mean-field gap equation was retained to determine the order parameter, ensuring Goldstone's theorem and the Silver Blaze property, while the Gaussian fluctuation contribution to the grand potential was evaluated via an imaginary-frequency integration formalism. The equation of state in the BEC limit was compared to the known Bogoliubov grand canonical equation of state for weakly interacting two-dimensional Bose gases to extract the composite boson scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Composite boson scattering length ratio: \(a_{\rm B}\simeq 0.56 a_{\rm 2D}\), determined by comparing the GPF equation of state near the vacuum-BEC quantum phase transition with the Bogoliubov equation of state for 2D Bose gases.
- Mean-field critical chemical potential for the vacuum-to-matter quantum phase transition: \(\mu_c = -\varepsilon_{\rm B}/2\), where \(\varepsilon_{\rm B}\) is the two-body binding energy.
- Mean-field order parameter as a function of chemical potential: \(\Delta_{\rm MF}(\mu) = \sqrt{\varepsilon_{\rm B}(2\mu+\varepsilon_{\rm B})}\ \Theta(2\mu+\varepsilon_{\rm B})\).
- Mean-field grand potential: \(\Omega_{\rm MF}(\mu) = -\frac{m}{8\pi}(2\mu+\varepsilon_{\rm B})^2\Theta(2\mu+\varepsilon_{\rm B})\).
- Mean-field particle density: \(n_{\rm MF}(\mu) = \frac{m}{2\pi}(2\mu+\varepsilon_{\rm B})\Theta(2\mu+\varepsilon_{\rm B})\).
- Mean-field boson-boson coupling constant: \(g_{\rm B} = 4\pi/m\), which is constant and corresponds to the Born approximation for four-body scattering in 2D.

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian pair fluctuation theory previously applied to three-dimensional Fermi gases (He, 2003; He, 2004) to the two-dimensional case, where quantum fluctuations are much more pronounced. The mean-field theory for 2D Fermi gases (Randeria, 1990; Randeria, 1992) fails qualitatively because it predicts a constant boson-boson coupling and a pressure equal to that of a noninteracting Fermi gas, contradicting experimental measurements (Frohlich, 2011; Feld, 2011) and quantum Monte Carlo simulations (Bertaina, 2011; Bertaina, 2012). The result \(a_{\rm B}\simeq 0.56 a_{\rm 2D}\) agrees with the exact four-body calculation (Petrov, 2004) and with the pole approximation treatment (He, 2015). The full equation of state enables comparison with quantum Monte Carlo data and experimental measurements across the entire BCS-BEC crossover.
