# $\bar{b}\bar{c}\,q_1q_2$four-quark states from Lattice QCD

> We present the results of a lattice calculation of four-quark states with the quark contents $\bar{b}\bar{c}q_1q_2$ where $q_1,q_2 \in u,d,s$. For the spin 1 states, when the light quark ($q_1, q_2$) masses are lighter, we find at least one energy level below the possible elastic threshold energy levels. These calculations are performed on three dynamical $N_f = 2+1+1$ highly improved staggered quark ensembles at lattice spacings of about 0.12, 0.09, and 0.06 fm. We use the overlap action for light to charm quarks while a non-relativistic action with non-perturbatively improved coefficients is employed for the bottom quark.

## Key claim

The authors report preliminary lattice QCD results indicating the presence of an energy level below the elastic threshold for both \(\bar{b}\bar{c}ud\) and \(\bar{b}\bar{c}us\) four-quark systems with spin 1, contrary to two other recent lattice calculations that found no binding for these channels.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on three \(N_f = 2+1+1\) HISQ gauge ensembles at lattice spacings of approximately 0.12, 0.09, and 0.06 fm, using the overlap action for light to charm quarks and a non-relativistic NRQCD action with non-perturbatively improved coefficients for the bottom quark. They constructed correlation matrices from six interpolating operators (meson-meson type and antidiquark-diquark type) and extracted energy levels via a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP). The study varied light quark masses while keeping charm and bottom quarks at physical values, and examined basis dependence and lattice spacing dependence of the results.

## Key numbers

- For \(\bar{b}\bar{c}ud\) at the finest lattice spacing (0.0582 fm), the preliminary estimate of the energy difference below the elastic \(B^*D\) threshold is approximately 20-40 MeV at physical quark masses.
- On the coarsest lattice ensemble (0.12 fm) for \(\bar{b}\bar{c}us\) at a light quark mass corresponding to a pion mass of 340 MeV, the energy splitting from the elastic threshold is smaller and consistent with zero within statistics.
- The calculations used three lattice ensembles with lattice spacings of 0.12 fm (24^3 x 64), 0.0888 fm (32^3 x 96), and 0.0582 fm (48^3 x 144).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of doubly heavy four-quark states, particularly the ILGTI group's earlier calculations on \(\bar{b}\bar{b}ud\) and \(\bar{b}\bar{b}us\) systems (Junnarkar 2018) which found binding energies of 100-150 MeV and 70-100 MeV respectively, and their prediction of a \(T_{cc}^+\) state 23 +/- 11 MeV below the \(DD^*\) threshold. The results contradict two recent lattice calculations (PhysRevD.102.114506 and Pflaumer 2021) that found no binding for \(\bar{b}\bar{c}ud\) or \(\bar{b}\bar{c}us\), while being consistent with an earlier lattice study (PhysRevD.99.054505) that reported a binding of 15-61 MeV for \(\bar{b}\bar{c}ud\). The authors note that a detailed finite-volume analysis is required to determine whether the observed energy levels correspond to genuine bound states or scattering states.
