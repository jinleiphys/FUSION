# Circumventing the axial anomalies and the strong CP problem

> Many meson processes are related to the U_A(1) axial anomaly, present in the Feynman graphs where fermion loops connect axial vertices with vector vertices. However, the coupling of pseudoscalar mesons to quarks does not have to be formulated via axial vertices. The pseudoscalar coupling is also possible, and this approach is especially natural on the level of the quark substructure of hadrons. In this paper we point out the advantages of calculating these processes using (instead of the anomalous graphs) the graphs where axial vertices are replaced by pseudoscalar vertices. We elaborate especially the case of the processes related to the Abelian axial anomaly of QED, but we speculate that it seems possible that effects of the non-Abelian axial anomaly of QCD can be accounted for in an analogous way.

## Key claim

The authors argue that many meson processes usually attributed to the U_A(1) axial anomaly can be equivalently calculated using pseudoscalar (P) coupling of quarks to mesons instead of axial (A) vertices, and that this approach may also account for the non-Abelian gluon anomaly effects, potentially circumventing the strong CP problem without requiring axions.

## Method

The paper surveys the pseudoscalar coupling method, where quark-loop diagrams with pseudoscalar vertices (PVV triangles and VPPP boxes) replace the anomalous AVV and VAAA graphs. The authors demonstrate the equivalence using both simple constituent quark models with the Goldberger-Treiman relation and the more sophisticated Dyson-Schwinger (DS) approach with dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter bound-state vertices, showing that the chiral-limit amplitudes for processes like π⁰ → γγ and γ → π⁺π⁰π⁻ are reproduced exactly.

## Context

This work extends the Steinberger (1949) approach of calculating π⁰ → γγ via quark loops with pseudoscalar coupling, and connects it to modern Dyson-Schwinger bound-state methods developed by Roberts (1996) and Bando et al. (1994). The paper builds on the Wess-Zumino (1971) term and the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly (1969), showing that pseudoscalar coupling calculations reproduce the same results without requiring ad hoc anomaly terms. The approach enables a unified treatment of over 15 meson decay amplitudes to within 3% accuracy, and suggests a resolution of the strong CP problem without invoking axions, following arguments by Mitra.
