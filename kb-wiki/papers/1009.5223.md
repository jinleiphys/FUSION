# Charged particle's $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV Au+Au collisions: QGP vs. hadronic resonance gas

> We show that if the hadronic resonance gas (HRG), with viscosity to entropy ratio $η/s\approx$0.24, is physical at temperature $T\approx$220 MeV, charged particles $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, over a wide range of collision centrality do not distinguish between initial QGP fluid and initial hadronic resonance gas. Unambiguous identification of bulk of the matter produced in Au+Au collisions require clear demonstration that HRG is unphysical at temperature $T<$200 MeV. It calls for precise lattice simulations with realistic boundary conditions.

## Key claim

If the hadronic resonance gas (HRG) with viscosity-to-entropy ratio η/s ≈ 0.24 is physically viable at temperature T ≈ 220 MeV, then charged particle pT spectra and elliptic flow in 0-40% Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV cannot distinguish between an initial quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fluid and an initial hadronic resonance gas. Unambiguous identification of the bulk matter as QGP requires demonstrating that HRG is unphysical at temperatures below 200 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA, solving the conservation equations for energy-momentum tensor together with a relaxation equation for the shear stress tensor, assuming longitudinal boost-invariance. Two scenarios were compared: a "no phase transition" (NPT) scenario initialized with a non-interacting hadronic resonance gas (all resonances with mass ≤ 2.5 GeV) at central temperature Ti = 220 MeV and initial time τi = 1 fm, and a "phase transition" (PT) scenario initialized with QGP at τi = 0.6 fm using a lattice-based equation of state with cross-over temperature Tco = 196 MeV. The simulations were tuned to reproduce PHENIX data for charged particle pT spectra and elliptic flow in 0-40% centrality Au+Au collisions, with χ² analysis used to quantify the quality of fit for different viscosity values.

## Key numbers

- In the NPT scenario, the best fit to charged particle pT spectra in 0-40% Au+Au collisions was obtained with η/s = 0.24, yielding (χ²/N)min = 3.3; this η/s value agrees with theoretical estimates of η/s = 0.24-0.30 for a hot hadronic resonance gas.
- In the PT scenario, the best fit to pT spectra was obtained with η/s = 0.08, yielding (χ²/N)min = 5.6, with comparable fit at η/s = 0.12.
- For elliptic flow, the best fit in the PT scenario was η/s = 0.08 with (χ²/N)min = 6.7; in the NPT scenario, the best fit was η/s = 0.24 with (χ²/N)min = 11.1.
- When pT spectra and elliptic flow were analyzed together, the combined minimum χ² was approximately 6.1 in the PT scenario and approximately 7.2 in the NPT scenario.
- In the NPT scenario, the central energy density was fixed at ε0 = 5.1 GeV/fm³ (corresponding to Ti = 220 MeV) with freeze-out temperature TF = 110 MeV; in the PT scenario, central energy densities ranged from ε0 = 20.8 to 35.5 GeV/fm³ depending on viscosity, with TF = 150 MeV.
- For HRG initialized at Ti = 200 MeV with η/s = 0.5 and τi = 3 fm, the χ²/N for combined spectra and flow was approximately 22, a factor of about 3 larger than the minimally viscous QGP scenario.

## Context

This work extends the debate over whether the matter produced in RHIC Au+Au collisions is QGP or hadronic resonance gas, building on earlier hydrodynamic studies (Kolb et al., 2000) that found ideal HRG gave poorer descriptions of data than QGP. The authors incorporate viscous effects following theoretical estimates that HRG has a larger η/s than the ADS/CFT limit (Policastro et al., 2001), as computed by Gorenstein et al. (2007) and Demir et al. (2008). The results challenge the interpretation of bulk observables as unambiguous QGP signatures, and call for more precise lattice QCD simulations with realistic boundary conditions to resolve the confinement-deconfinement transition temperature, noting the current discrepancy between HotQCD (Tc ≈ 192 MeV) and Wuppertal-Budapest (Tc ≈ 157-170 MeV) collaborations.
