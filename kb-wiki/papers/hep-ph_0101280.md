# SPIN - Progress and Prospects

## Key claim

Progress in fundamental spin physics over the past several years has been striking, and the prospects for the future are excellent, though many open questions remain, particularly regarding the spin structure of the nucleon, where the quark spin contribution accounts for only about 30% of the nucleon's spin and the polarized gluon distribution remains poorly constrained.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical developments in spin physics, covering measurements of the muon anomalous magnetic moment (g-2) at Brookhaven, electric dipole moment searches, polarized deep inelastic scattering data from HERMES and SMC, and the commissioning of polarized proton beams at RHIC. The author surveys the status of Bjorken's sum rule, polarized parton distribution functions, transversity, fragmentation functions, and spin-dependent static moments, comparing experimental results with theoretical predictions from QCD.

## Context

This talk, presented at SPIN2000, reviews the state of spin physics following the SMC analysis of polarized structure functions (Adeva 1998) and the HERMES observation of azimuthal asymmetries (Airapetian 2000). It extends discussions from SPIN98 (Prescott 1998) and anticipates the polarized proton program at RHIC (Bunce 2000) and the COMPASS experiment (Bradamante 2000). The paper identifies direct measurement of the polarized gluon distribution as the highest priority for QCD spin physics, with prospects for significant advances through photon-gluon fusion at COMPASS and QCD Compton scattering at RHIC.
