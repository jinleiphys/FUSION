# Charmonium in a hot medium: melting vs absorption

> A charmonium produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC propagates through a dense co-moving matter with a rather high relative momentum, <p_T^2>=4-10GeV^2. In spite of Debye screening of the binding potential, the charmonium survives with a substantial probability, even if the c-cbar potential is completely screened in the hot environment. In addition, the color-exchange interaction with the medium is another important source of charmonium suppression. Attenuation in a hot medium caused by both effects is evaluated by means of the path integral technique, which requires ability of boosting the binding potential to a moving reference frame. This problem is solved in the approximation of small intrinsic velocities of the charmed quarks.

## Key claim

Charmonium produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC can survive with substantial probability even if the binding potential is completely screened by the hot medium, provided the charmonium has sufficiently high transverse momentum; the survival arises because the short time interval during which the potential is weakened prevents the quarks from moving apart, and at high momentum the medium becomes fully transparent due to Lorentz time dilation and color transparency.

## Method

The authors employ a path-integral technique to describe the propagation of a colorless \(\bar cc\) dipole through a hot medium, solving a 2-dimensional Schrödinger-type equation for the dipole Green function. The imaginary part of the potential accounts for color-exchange absorption (proportional to the transport coefficient \(\hat q\) times \(r_T^2\)), while the real part uses a screened Cornell potential with Debye screening from lattice parametrizations. To handle moving charmonia, they derive a boost-invariant Schrödinger equation exploiting the smallness of the asymmetry in fractional light-cone momenta of the charm quarks, interpolating between the rest frame and the light-cone frame.

## Key numbers

- The mean transverse momentum squared of charmonia at LHC is \(\langle p_\psi^2 \rangle = 7-10\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The transport coefficient at high temperature scales as \(\hat q \approx 3.6\, T^3\) for \(T \gg T_c\).
- The mean squared asymmetry in fractional light-cone momenta of the \(c\) and \(\bar c\) quarks is \(\langle \lambda^2 \rangle = 0.017\), derived from a mean quark velocity \(\langle v^2 \rangle \approx 0.2\).
- The survival probability amplitude for a completely screened potential is given analytically; the suppression factor vanishes at \(p_\psi = 0\) and approaches transparency at high \(p_\psi\) for path lengths \(L = 1,\ 3,\ 5\ \text{fm}\).
- For central lead-lead collisions at LHC, the transport coefficient is taken as \(q_0 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\), based on analysis of high-\(p_T\) light hadrons.
- The ratio of suppression factors for \(\psi'(2S)\) and \(J/\psi\) shows a bump exceeding unity at \(p_T \gtrsim 10\ \text{GeV}\) due to the node in the \(\psi'\) wave function.
- The azimuthal asymmetry parameter \(v_2\) for \(J/\psi\) is calculated for centralities \(10\%,\ 30\%,\ 60\%\) and matches CMS data for \(p_T > 7\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the path-integral treatment of dipole propagation in hot matter developed by Kopeliovich and collaborators (1991, 1998) and applies it to charmonium suppression at RHIC and LHC energies. It contrasts with the usual expectation that Debye screening alone destroys charmonium (Satz), by showing that a moving charmonium can survive screening due to finite formation time effects. The results enable extraction of the transport coefficient \(\hat q\) from charmonium data, complementing analyses of high-\(p_T\) light hadrons (Kopeliovich et al., 2013), and provide predictions for the azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2\) that can be compared with CMS measurements.
