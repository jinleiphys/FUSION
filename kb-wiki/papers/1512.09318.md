# Charm hadrons above $T_c$

> From the analysis of the lattice data on fluctuations and correlations of charm we conclude that open charm meson and baryon-like excitations exist above the QCD crossover temperature, and in fact are the dominant degrees of freedom for thermodynamics in the vicinity of the transition. Charm quarks become the dominant charm degrees of freedom only for temperatures T>200 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that open charm meson and baryon-like excitations exist above the QCD crossover temperature and are the dominant charm degrees of freedom near the transition, but only for temperatures below about 200 MeV; above that temperature, charm quarks become the dominant degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors analyzed lattice QCD data on diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities (fluctuations and correlations) involving charm and light quarks up to fourth order, for temperatures from T_c (154 MeV) to about 330 MeV. They modeled the total charm pressure as a sum of partial pressures from charm mesons, charm baryons, and charm quarks, and extracted these partial pressures from the lattice data using the Boltzmann approximation.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover temperature is T_c = (154 ± 9) MeV.
- Charm quarks become the dominant charm degrees of freedom only for temperatures T > 200 MeV.
- The off-diagonal susceptibility χ_{13}^{uc} is only about a factor of three smaller than χ_{22}^{uc} at temperatures below 200 MeV.
- Charm meson and baryon pressures become negligible only at T > 200 MeV.
- For charmonium, the screening mass approaches the free theory value at temperatures T > 300 MeV; for a correlated light-charm pair, this happens at T > 250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (Bazavov 2012, Borsanyi 2011) and specifically of charm (Bazavov 2014). It contradicts the simple picture that only quark degrees of freedom are relevant above T_c, and instead supports the proposal of bound partonic states in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (Shuryak 2003). The findings have implications for modeling heavy flavor elliptic flow and nuclear modification factors at small and moderate transverse momentum, as discussed in models using Langevin dynamics and energy loss of heavy quarks (Moore 2004) and in studies of heavy-light bound states (He 2012).
