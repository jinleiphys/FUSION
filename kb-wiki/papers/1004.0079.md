# Curvature Correction in the Strutinsky's Method

> Mass calculations carried out by Strutinsky's shell correction method are based on the notion of smooth single particle level density. The smoothing procedure is always performed using curvature correction. In the presence of curvature correction a smooth function remains unchanged if smoothing is applied. Two new curvature correction methods are introduced. The performance of the standard and new methods are investigated using harmonic oscillator and realistic potentials.

## Key claim

The paper introduces two new curvature correction methods for Strutinsky's shell correction procedure, many width curvature correction (MWCC) and derivative curvature correction (DCC), and demonstrates that MWCC produces a significantly larger plateau region (where shell correction is independent of smoothing width) than standard methods, especially for realistic potentials with continuum states, though the plateau property is not as good as in the harmonic oscillator case.

## Method

The authors define the smooth single-particle level density via convolution of the quantum level density with a kernel function, and enforce curvature correction by requiring that smoothing leaves polynomials of order up to (2m+1) unchanged. Two new methods are derived: MWCC, which constructs kernels as linear combinations of the starting kernel evaluated at multiple widths γc^k using Gaussian q-binomial coefficients, and DCC, which generates kernels via a recursion involving the derivative of the previous kernel. The methods are tested on harmonic oscillator (ħω=6 MeV, Nτ=70) and spherically symmetric Saxon-Woods potentials (with spin-orbit term, parameters from Dudek (1981)), using both Gaussian and Cosh-type starting kernels, and compared against the standard polynomial curvature correction (PCC) and Brack-Pauli curvature correction (BPCC) methods.

## Key numbers

- For the harmonic oscillator potential (ħω=6 MeV, Nτ=70, m=3), the plateau region of the MWCC method is roughly two times larger than the plateau of the PCC method.
- For the realistic Saxon-Woods potential (neutron shell correction of ¹⁴⁶Gd), the total variation of shell correction over the γ region is 0.3 MeV for MWCC and 3 MeV for PCC (Gaussian starting kernel).
- For the harmonic oscillator case, the total variation of shell correction for the MWCC method is only 0.01 MeV.
- The averaging window size used to determine the MWCC parameter c was 100 nuclei, with lower bound Nτ=50 for the realistic potential case.

## Context

This work extends the Strutinsky shell correction method (Strutinsky, 1968) by addressing the curvature correction requirement that smoothing should leave smooth functions unchanged, a condition formalized by Bunatyan (1972) and Brack and Pauli (1973). The paper responds to recent findings by Centelles et al. (2006) and Roca-Maza et al. (2007) that the average value of the fluctuating part of the energy is nonzero and contributes to the smooth part, and to the observation by Hilaire (2004) of regular correlations in nuclear mass error distributions from the macroscopic-microscopic calculations of Moller et al. (1995). The MWCC method enables more reliable global mass calculations by providing a larger plateau region and stability with respect to the order of curvature correction, particularly for realistic potentials where standard methods only satisfy a local plateau condition.
