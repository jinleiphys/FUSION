# Universal decay rule for reduced widths

> Emission processes including $α$-decay, heavy cluster decays, proton and di-proton emission are analyzed in terms of the well known factorisation between the penetrability and reduced width. By using a shifted harmonic oscilator plus Coulomb cluster-daughter interaction it is possible to derive a linear relation between the logarithm of the reduced width squared and the fragmentation potential, defined as the difference between the Coulomb barrier and Q-value. This relation is fulfilled with a good accuracy for transitions between ground states, as well as for most $α$-decays to low lying $2^+$ excited states. The well known Viola-Seaborg rule, connecting half lives with the Coulomb parameter and the product between fragment charge numbers, as well as the Blendowke scalling rule connecting the spectroscopic factor with the mass number of the emitted cluster, can be easily understood in terms of the fragmentation potential. It is shown that the recently evidenced two regions in the dependence of reduced proton half-lives versus the Coulomb parameter are directly connected with the corresponding regions of the fragmentation potential.

## Key claim

A universal linear relation exists between the logarithm of the reduced width squared and the fragmentation potential (defined as the difference between the Coulomb barrier and Q-value) for cold emission processes including alpha-decay, heavy cluster decays, proton emission, and di-proton emission, but this relation is derived from a simplified shifted harmonic oscillator plus Coulomb potential model and is fulfilled with "reasonable accuracy" rather than exact precision.

## Method

The author analyzed experimental decay data for alpha-decay, heavy cluster emission, proton emission, and two-proton emission using the standard factorization of decay width into penetrability and reduced width squared. A shifted harmonic oscillator potential surrounded by a Coulomb interaction was used to model the cluster-daughter system, from which a linear relationship between log10(gamma^2) and the fragmentation potential V_frag = V_C(r_B) - Q was derived. Experimental reduced widths were extracted from measured half-lives and compared to this linear prediction across five regions of the nuclear chart for alpha-decay, and separately for heavy cluster, proton, and two-proton emitters.

## Key numbers

- For heavy cluster emission processes (excluding alpha-decay), the linear fit is log10(gamma^2) = -0.586 (V_C - Q) + 15.399
- The slope of this fit corresponds to a harmonic oscillator energy hbar-omega ≈ 1.5 MeV for heavy cluster emission and alpha-decay
- For proton emission with Z<68: log10(gamma^2) = -0.283 (V_C - Q) + 1.329
- For proton emission with Z>68: log10(gamma^2) = -0.365 (V_C - Q) + 3.440
- For two-proton emission (di-proton approximation): log10(gamma^2) = -2.075 (V_C - Q) + 4.403, corresponding to hbar-omega ≈ 0.2 MeV
- The generalized Viola-Seaborg rule for heavy cluster emission uses parameters a1=1.517, a2=0.053, b2=-92.911, with c2=0 for even-mass and c2=1.402 for odd-mass nuclei
- The touching radius used in calculations is r_B = 1.2 (A_D^{1/3} + A_C^{1/3})

## Context

This work extends the Viola-Seaborg rule (Viola 1966) and the Blendowke scaling rule (Ble91) for spectroscopic factors by providing a theoretical interpretation in terms of the fragmentation potential. The derived linear relation between reduced width squared and fragmentation potential explains the two-region pattern observed in proton emitter half-lives versus Coulomb parameter (Son02, Del06b) and provides a basis for understanding hindrance factors in alpha-decay to excited 2+ states. The approach enables systematic predictions of reduced widths across different emission processes and nuclear regions, though the simple shifted harmonic oscillator model shows limitations for two-proton emission where the di-proton is not a bound system.
