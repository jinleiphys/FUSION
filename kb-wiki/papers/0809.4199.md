# Similarity Renormalization Group Evolution of Many-Body Forces in a One-Dimensional Model

> A one-dimensional system of bosons with short-range repulsion and mid-range attraction is used as a laboratory to explore the evolution of many-body forces by the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG). The free-space SRG is implemented for few-body systems in a symmetrized harmonic oscillator basis using a recursive construction analogous to no-core shell model implementations. This approach, which can be directly generalized to three-dimensional nuclei, is fully unitary up to induced A-body forces when applied with an A-particle basis (e.g., A-body bound-state energies are exactly preserved). The oscillator matrix elements for a given A can then be used in larger systems. Errors from omitted induced many-body forces show a hierarchy of decreasing contribution to binding energies. An analysis of individual contributions to the growth of many-body forces demonstrates such a hierarchy and provides an understanding of its origins.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of a Hamiltonian in a one-dimensional boson model induces many-body forces whose contributions to binding energies exhibit a hierarchy of decreasing magnitude, but this hierarchy is only fully preserved when the evolution is performed in an A-particle basis; errors from omitted induced many-body forces show a peak at intermediate flow parameters before decreasing.

## Method

The free-space SRG was implemented for few-body systems (A=2 through A=5) of one-dimensional bosons using a symmetrized harmonic oscillator basis with a recursive construction analogous to no-core shell model implementations. The Hamiltonian was evolved as a whole in each A-particle sector using the kinetic energy as the flow operator, with matrix elements built by recursive embedding of two-body and three-body potentials into successively larger particle spaces. The initial two-body potential was a sum of two gaussians (short-range repulsion and mid-range attraction), and a regulated contact three-body force was also included in some calculations.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy for two bosons with potential V_alpha: E_2 = -0.920 (dimensionless units), constant under SRG evolution to high accuracy.
* Ground-state energy for three bosons with V_alpha and no initial three-body force: E_3 = -2.567 (dimensionless units) at the initial (unevolved) stage.
* Ground-state energy for four bosons with V_alpha and no initial three-body force: E_4 = -4.695 (dimensionless units) at the initial stage.
* Ground-state energy for three bosons with V_beta (purely attractive) and no initial three-body force: E_3 = -1.708 (dimensionless units) at the initial stage.
* Ground-state energy for four bosons with V_beta and no initial three-body force: E_4 = -3.846 (dimensionless units) at the initial stage.
* Basis size N_max = 28 gave ground-state energies converged to one part in 10^4; computation times for A=3 with N_max = 32 were a factor of 3 longer than with N_max = 28.

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach previously applied to three-dimensional nuclear interactions by Bogner, Furnstahl, and collaborators (2006-2007) to a simplified one-dimensional boson model that mimics the short-range repulsion and mid-range attraction of realistic nuclear potentials. The recursive symmetrization formalism adapts the no-core shell model techniques developed by Navratil and collaborators (2000) for three-dimensional fermionic calculations. The results establish a road map for generalizing the method to full three-dimensional nuclear calculations with realistic interactions from chiral effective field theory, where the same hierarchy of induced many-body forces is expected to hold.
