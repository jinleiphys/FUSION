# Structure of Krypton Isotopes using the Generalised Bohr Hamiltonian Method

> We investigate the properties of the excited spectra of the even-even isotopes of krypton using a Generalised Bohr Hamiltonian with three different Skyrme functionals. In particular, we investigate the evolution of the low-lying $2^{+}_{1}$ and $4^{+}_{1}$ states and their associated electromagnetic transitions. The model reproduces quite nicely the energy trends apart from ${}^{88}$Kr, where none of the interactions used here are able to grasp a sudden change in the energy spectrum. Additionally, we explore the neutron deficient region ${}^{72-76}$Kr which is a proposed region for shape coexistence. We observe that the model can reproduce the structure of the experimental spectrum of ${}^{72}$Kr exceedingly well.

## Key claim

The Generalised Bohr Hamiltonian method using three different Skyrme functionals (UNEDF0, UNEDF1, UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$) reproduces the energy trends of low-lying states in even-even krypton isotopes quite well, except for $^{88}$Kr where none of the interactions can capture a sudden change in the experimental energy spectrum; the model reproduces the experimental spectrum of $^{72}$Kr exceedingly well, but this success is conditional on the choice of functional, with UNEDF1 giving the best agreement.

## Method

The authors solved constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations using the HFODD solver in Cartesian coordinates with a deformed harmonic oscillator basis of 16 major shells, calculating potential energy surfaces and six mass parameters at various deformation points ($\beta$, $\gamma$) for three Skyrme functionals: UNEDF0, UNEDF1, and UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$. The mass parameters were computed via the cranking approximation and uniformly rescaled by a factor of 1.3 to account for neglected time-odd fields. These microscopic inputs were then used in the 5-dimensional Generalised Bohr Hamiltonian to compute collective excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities for even-even krypton isotopes.

## Key numbers

- $^{72}$Kr $2^{+}_{1}$ energy: experimental 0.70972 MeV; UNEDF0 gives 0.73662 MeV; UNEDF1 gives 0.61159 MeV; UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$ gives 0.29790 MeV
- $^{88}$Kr $2^{+}_{1}$ energy: experimental 0.77532 MeV; UNEDF0 gives 1.16732 MeV; UNEDF1 gives 1.31064 MeV; UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$ gives 1.45241 MeV (all functionals fail to reproduce the experimental value)
- $^{72}$Kr $4^{+}_{1}$ energy: experimental 1.32140 MeV; UNEDF0 gives 1.49576 MeV; UNEDF1 gives 1.33322 MeV; UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$ gives 0.87611 MeV
- $B(E2:2^{+}_{1}\rightarrow 0^{+}_{1})$ for $^{72}$Kr: experimental $57\pm 8$ W.u.; UNEDF0 gives 31.385 W.u.; UNEDF1 gives 32.976 W.u.; UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$ gives 97.764 W.u.
- $B(E2:4^{+}_{1}\rightarrow 2^{+}_{1})$ for $^{72}$Kr: UNEDF0 gives 78.680 W.u.; UNEDF1 gives 73.619 W.u.; UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$ gives 151.363 W.u.
- Most $2^{+}_{1}$ energies from UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 fall within $\pm 200$ keV of experimental values across the isotopic chain
- UNEDF1$_{\text{SO}}$ systematically underestimates the $2^{+}_{1}$ energy, e.g., for $^{72}$Kr giving 0.29790 MeV versus experimental 0.70972 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Generalised Bohr Hamiltonian approach of Prochniak (2015) to the krypton isotopic chain using Skyrme functionals, complementing earlier studies by Girod (2009) and Fu (2013) who used similar GBH methods but with different functionals (Gogny D1S interaction in the case of Girod (2009)). The paper enables systematic comparison of three UNEDF functionals for describing shape coexistence in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes ($^{72-76}$Kr), a region previously studied by Clement (2007), Girod (2009), Rodriguez (2014), and Fu (2013). The results show that none of the functionals produce the triaxial secondary minimum in $^{72}$Kr that was observed in the Gogny D1S calculations of Girod (2009), while UNEDF1 gives the best overall reproduction of the experimental spectrum for $^{72}$Kr.
