# Nuclear dependence of the 2p2h electroweak response in the Relativistic Fermi Gas model

> We present the results of a recent study of meson-exchange two-body currents in lepton-nucleus inclusive scattering at various kinematics and for different nuclei within the Relativistic Fermi Gas model. We show that the associated nuclear response functions at their peaks scale as $A k_F^2$, for Fermi momentum $k_F$ going from 200 to 300 MeV/c and momentum transfer $q$ from $2k_F$ to 2 GeV/c. This behavior is different from what is found for the quasielastic response, which scales as $A/k_F$. This result can be valuable in the analyses of long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments, which need to implement these nuclear effects in Monte Carlo simulations for different kinematics and nuclear targets.

## Key claim

The 2p-2h meson-exchange current (MEC) response function per nucleon scales approximately as \(A k_F^2\) at its peak for Fermi momenta between 200 and 300 MeV/c and momentum transfers from \(2k_F\) to 2 GeV/c, but this scaling is accurate only around the peak at high \(q\) and breaks down as \(q\) approaches \(2k_F\) or lower.

## Method

The authors computed the 2p-2h nuclear response functions within the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, using fully relativistic meson-exchange currents derived from a Lagrangian including nucleon, pion, and \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom. They evaluated the electromagnetic transverse response \(R^T_{\rm MEC}\) exactly (without dimensional reduction approximations) for several values of Fermi momentum \(k_F\) and momentum transfer \(q\), and examined its scaling behavior by defining a reduced response per nucleon divided by single-nucleon form factors and then scaled by \(\eta_F^2 = (k_F/m_N)^2\).

## Key numbers

- The 2p-2h MEC response per nucleon scales as \(k_F^2\) for Fermi momenta \(k_F\) from 200 to 300 MeV/c and momentum transfers \(q\) from \(2k_F\) to 2 GeV/c.
- For a light nucleus (\(k_F = 200\) MeV/c), the MEC peak drops from about 30% to about 10% of the quasielastic peak height as \(q\) increases from 0.8 to 2 GeV/c.
- For a heavy nucleus (\(k_F = 300\) MeV/c), the MEC peak is almost as high as the quasielastic peak at \(q = 0.8\) GeV/c and about half of it at \(q = 2\) GeV/c.
- The 1-body quasielastic response scales as \(A/k_F\), so the relative importance of the 2p-2h contribution grows as \(k_F^3\).
- The scaling law is excellent around the MEC peak for high \(q\), starts to break down around \(q = 2k_F\), and worsens as \(q\) decreases further.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 2p-2h excitations in electron scattering (Donnelly 1978, De Pace 2004) and neutrino scattering (Martini 2009, Amaro 2010) that were mostly focused on carbon targets, to a systematic study of the nuclear density dependence across multiple nuclei. The result that the 2p-2h response scales as \(k_F^2\) (unlike the quasielastic \(1/k_F\) scaling found in superscaling analyses by Donnelly 1999) enables extrapolation of 2p-2h contributions from one nucleus to another, which is directly applicable to event generators for long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments using targets such as oxygen, argon, and calcium (T2K, NOvA, T2HK, DUNE).
