# Theoretical developments on the initial state in relativistic particle collisions

> We discuss recent progress towards developing accurate initial state descriptions for heavy ion collisions focusing on weak coupling based approaches, that enable one to constrain the high-energy structure of nuclei from deep inelastic scattering or proton-nucleus collisions. We review recent developments to determine the event-by-event fluctuating nuclear geometry, to describe gluon saturation phenomena at next-to-leading order accuracy, and to include longitudinal dynamics to the initial state descriptions.

# Theoretical developments on the initial state in relativistic particle collisions

## Key claim
Weak-coupling approaches (collinear factorization and Color Glass Condensate) can simultaneously describe heavy ion collision initial states and deep inelastic scattering data, but achieving precision-level comparisons requires promoting all ingredients, including the non-perturbative initial condition for high-energy evolution, to next-to-leading order accuracy in the strong coupling constant.

## Method
The author reviews recent progress in three areas: (1) constraining event-by-event fluctuating nuclear geometry using exclusive vector meson photoproduction in ultra-peripheral collisions and deep inelastic scattering; (2) developing next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations for gluon saturation phenomena, including NLO Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution, NLO impact factors for DIS and vector meson production, and first NLO fits to structure function data; (3) incorporating longitudinal (rapidity-dependent) dynamics into initial state models via 3D extensions of the EKRT collinear factorization model and the McDipper CGC-based approach, as well as JIMWLK evolution.

## Key numbers
- Incoherent J/psi photoproduction data from ALICE at the LHC shows a t-spectrum more steeply falling than the Fourier transform of the Woods-Saxon density profile, interpreted as a signature of saturation phenomena at small x_Pom ~ 10^{-5}.
- The NLO BK evolution equation became available in 2007 (Balitsky 2008), and the first NLO fit to proton structure function data was reported in 2020 (Beuf 2020).
- The NLO fit successfully describes both total and heavy quark production cross sections in DIS (Hanninen 2022).
- NLO impact factors are now known for exclusive vector meson production, dijet/dihadron production in DIS, and inclusive hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions.
- The 3D EKRT model, using x-dependent nuclear PDFs (EPPS21) with event-by-event fluctuations and dynamical saturation, reproduces charged hadron multiplicity pseudorapidity distributions compared to ALICE data.
- The McDipper CGC initial condition, with proton structure fitted to HERA data, underestimates flow decorrelation compared to STAR data.
- JIMWLK evolution results show initial momentum correlations are short-range in rapidity, while event geometry correlations vanish much more slowly with increasing rapidity separation.

## Context
This review extends the line of work connecting heavy ion initial state descriptions to deep inelastic scattering and proton-nucleus collisions, building on the EKRT model (Paatelainen 2012) based on collinear factorization and the IP-Glasma framework (Schenke 2012) based on the Color Glass Condensate. It contrasts these weak-coupling approaches with parametrization-based models such as T_RENTo (Soeder 2023) and event generators (Pythia/Angantyr, EPOS, HIJING). The developments reviewed enable precision studies of gluon saturation phenomena at the future Electron-Ion Collider (Abdul Khalek 2021) and allow 3+1D hydrodynamical simulations of heavy ion collisions to be compared with observables away from midrapidity. Open questions include the need for uncertainty estimates on the non-perturbative input to NLO BK evolution (currently unavailable at NLO) and the need for additional sources of fluctuations to reproduce longitudinal flow decorrelation.
