# Generalised Parton Distributions from Lattice Feynman-Hellmann Techniques

> We report on the use of Feynman-Hellmann techniques to calculate the off-forward Compton amplitude (OFCA) in lattice QCD. At leading-twist, the Euclidean OFCA is parameterised by the Mellin moments of generalised parton distributions (GPDs). Hence we extract GPD moments for two values of the soft momentum transfer, $t=-1.10, -2.20\;\text{GeV}^2$ and zero-skewness kinematics at an unphysical pion mass of $m_π\approx 470\;\text{MeV}$. This includes the first determination of the $n=4$ moments.

## Key claim

The authors report the first lattice QCD determination of the $n=4$ Mellin moments of generalised parton distributions (GPDs), but caution that the calculation is "highly exploratory" and the results are too noisy to draw strong distinctions between the $t$ dependence of different moments.

## Method

The authors extend Feynman-Hellmann techniques to compute the off-forward Compton amplitude (OFCA) in lattice QCD by calculating nucleon propagators in the presence of weakly-coupled background fields. They isolate the quadratic contribution in the coupling to extract four-point functions, then parameterise the Euclidean OFCA at leading-twist using the Mellin moments of GPDs. The moments are extracted by fitting the $\omega$-expansion of the OFCA truncated at $n=8$ using Markov chain Monte Carlo methods with monotonically decreasing moment constraints.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: $m_\pi \approx 470\;\text{MeV}$ (unphysical, at the SU(3) flavour symmetric point)
- Soft momentum transfer values: $t = -1.10\;\text{GeV}^2$ and $t = -2.20\;\text{GeV}^2$
- Hard momentum transfer: $\bar{Q}^2 \approx 6-7\;\text{GeV}^2$ (specifically $7.13\;\text{GeV}^2$ for set #1 and $6.03\;\text{GeV}^2$ for set #2)
- Lattice spacing: $a = 0.074(2)\;\text{fm}$ on a $32^3 \times 64$ lattice with 1763 configurations
- First determination of $n=4$ GPD moments (no numerical values reported for these moments)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of GPD moments, which had been limited to $n=3$ moments (GPD Lattice Collaboration, various years), and earlier Feynman-Hellmann calculations of the forward Compton amplitude (Hannaford-Gunn et al., 2021; Can et al., 2020). The method enables future separation of helicity-conserving and helicity-flipping GPDs $H$ and $E$, and will allow constraints on GPD models and application of other methods to access GPDs directly from the Euclidean OFCA.
