# Revisting the boiling of quark nuggets at nonzero chemical potential

> The boiling of possible quark nuggets during the quark-hadron phase transition of the Universe at nonzero chemical potential is revisited within the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach employed for the hadron phase, using two kinds of baryon interactions as fundamental inputs. To describe the deconfined phase of quark matter, we use a recently developed quark mass density-dependent model with a fully self-consistent thermodynamic treatment of confinement. We study the baryon number limit $A_{\rm boil}$ (above which boiling may be important) with three typical values for the confinement parameter $D$. It is firstly found that the baryon interaction with a softer equation of state for the hadron phase would only lead to a small increase of $A_{\rm boil}$. However, results depend sensitively on the confinement parameter in the quark model. Specifically, boiling might be important during the Universe cooling for a limited parameter range around $D^{1/2} = 170$ MeV, a value satisfying recent lattice QCD calculations of the vacuum chiral condensate, while for other choices of this parameter, boiling might not happen and cosmological quark nuggets of $10^2 < A < 10^{50}$ could survive.

## Key claim

The boiling of primordial quark nuggets during the quark-hadron phase transition is found to depend sensitively on the confinement parameter in the quark model, with boiling being important only for a limited parameter range around D^{1/2} = 170 MeV; for other choices of this parameter, boiling might not happen and cosmological quark nuggets of baryon number between 10^2 and 10^50 could survive.

## Method

The hadron phase was described using the finite-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with two baryon interactions (a stiff microscopic three-body force and a soft phenomenological Urbana three-body force), while the quark phase was described using a quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model with a fully self-consistent thermodynamic treatment of confinement. The pressure difference between the two phases was computed under chemical equilibrium conditions, and the surface tension of quark nuggets was calculated self-consistently from all fermion species. The baryon number limit A_boil, above which boiling becomes efficient, was then evaluated using classical nucleation theory.

## Key numbers

- The confinement parameter D^{1/2} = 170 MeV satisfies recent lattice QCD calculations of the vacuum chiral condensate, with a range of (161 MeV, 195 MeV) from lattice QCD.
- For D^{1/2} = 170 MeV, large amounts of cosmological quark nuggets between 10^2 < A < 10^50 would boil around T = 90 MeV.
- The lower bound of the confinement parameter is D^{1/2} = 156 MeV, and the upper bound is D^{1/2} = 270 MeV.
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is 0.17 fm^{-3}.
- The strange quark current mass was taken as m_{s0} = 95 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Alcock and Olinto (1989) and Madsen and Olesen (1991), who used an ideal gas and a Walecka-type model for the hadron phase respectively, and a MIT bag model for the quark phase. The present study improves upon these by employing microscopic BHF theory for the hadron phase and a self-consistent QMDD model for the quark phase, finding that the confinement parameter has a much larger effect on boiling than the hadron equation of state. The results suggest that if the confinement parameter falls in the range favored by lattice QCD, boiling would be efficient and quark nuggets would not survive, while for other parameter values they could persist as dark matter candidates.
