# Formation of correlations and energy-conservation at short time scales

> The formation of correlations due to collisions in an interacting nucleonic system is investigated. Results from one-time kinetic equations are compared with the Kadanoff and Baym two-time equation with collisions included in Born approximation. A reasonable agreement is found for a proposed approximation of the memory effects by a finite duration of collisions. This form of collision integral is in agreement with intuitive estimates from Fermi's golden rule. The formation of correlations and the build up time is calculated analytically for the high temperature and the low temperature limit. Different approximate expressions are compared with the numerical results. We present analytically the time dependent interaction energy and the formation time for Gauß- and Yukawa type of potentials.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the formation of correlations in an interacting nucleonic system can be approximated by a finite-duration collision integral that reproduces the time-dependent correlation energy from the full Kadanoff-Baym two-time equations, provided the limit of complete collisions is taken after the time evolution rather than before.

## Method

The authors compare the Levinson equation (a one-time kinetic equation with full memory effects in Born approximation) with the Kadanoff-Baym two-time Green's function formalism. They derive a "finite duration" approximation that retains the off-shell energy broadening from the cosine memory kernel while neglecting the time retardation of the distribution functions themselves. Analytical expressions for the time-dependent correlation energy are obtained in the high-temperature (Maxwell-Boltzmann) and low-temperature (degenerate Fermi) limits for both Gaussian and Yukawa-type potentials, and these are compared with numerical solutions of the full Kadanoff-Baym equations.

## Key numbers

- For two counter-flowing streams of nuclear matter at T1=10 MeV, n1=n0/60 and T2=5 MeV, n2=n0/10 with relative momentum 1.5 hbar/fm (45 MeV/nucleon collision energy), the correlation build-up time is tau_c = 3 fm/c.
- For a more degenerate system with n1=n0/60, T1=0.5 MeV and n2=n0/20, T2=0.1 MeV at relative velocity 1 hbar/fm (21 MeV/nucleon), the correlation time increases to tau_c = 9 fm/c.
- The low-temperature formation time of correlations is given analytically as tau_c = (pi hbar)/(3 epsilon_f + epsilon_c) * (1 + (pi T/(epsilon_f+epsilon_c))^2), which for nuclear matter at saturation density gives approximately 4-5 fm/c.
- For the Gaussian potential with eta=0.57 fm and V0=-453 MeV, the high-temperature correlation time in the quasi-classical limit reduces to tau = (pi^2 eta)/(8 v_th), where v_th is the thermal velocity.

## Context

This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym (1962) two-time formalism and the Generalized Kadanoff-Baym Ansatz of Lipavsky et al. (1986) by providing an analytically tractable approximation for the short-time formation of correlations. The finite-duration approximation bridges the gap between the Markovian Boltzmann equation (which conserves kinetic energy and has no correlation energy) and the full two-time quantum kinetic theory. The results enable quantitative estimates of the memory time and the validity range of the quasiparticle picture, connecting to earlier work on the Landau Fermi-liquid criterion by Pines (1955) and to numerical studies of memory times by Gering et al. (1994).
