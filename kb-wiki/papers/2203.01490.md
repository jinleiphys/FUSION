# Overview of intermittency analysis in heavy-ion collisions

> In this paper, a search for power-law fluctuations with fractality and intermittency analysis to explore the QCD phase diagram and the critical point is summarized. Experimental data on self-similar correlations and fluctuations with respect to the size of phase space volume in various high energy heavy-ion collisions are presented, with special emphasis on background subtraction and efficiency correction of the measurement. Phenomenological modelling and theoretical work on the subject are discussed. Finally, we highlight possible directions for future research.

## Key claim

This review summarizes the search for power-law fluctuations and intermittency as a signature of the QCD critical point, but emphasizes that background subtraction and efficiency correction are essential to isolate a genuine critical signal from trivial non-critical contributions.

## Method

The paper reviews the calculation of scaled factorial moments (SFMs) of multiplicity distributions in partitioned momentum space to search for power-law scaling behavior indicative of critical fluctuations. It describes two main background subtraction techniques: the mixed event method, which subtracts the SFM of uncorrelated mixed events from the data SFM, and the cumulative variable method, which transforms momentum variables to produce a uniform distribution and remove distortions from non-uniform single-particle spectra. A cell-by-cell efficiency correction method using binomial probability distributions is also presented to recover true SFMs from experimentally measured ones.

## Key numbers

- The critical intermittency index \(\phi_2\) is predicted to be \(\frac{5}{6}\) for baryon density and \(\frac{2}{3}\) for pion density near the QCD critical point.
- The NA49 experiment measured \(\phi_2 = 0.96 \pm 0.16\) in Si+Si collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, approaching the theoretical prediction.
- The scaling exponent \(\nu\) from Ginzburg-Landau theory is predicted to be 1.304 in full phase space, and 1.0 from a two-dimensional Ising model.
- The CMC model with Levy exponent \(\nu = 1/6\) and \(p_{\rm min}/p_{\rm max} = 10^{-7}\) yields \(\phi_2 = 0.834 \pm 0.001\), consistent with the \(\frac{5}{6}\) prediction.
- The STAR experiment observed a non-monotonic energy dependence of the scaling exponent \(\nu\) for charged particles in Au+Au collisions, with a dip between 20 and 30 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the experimental and theoretical work on intermittency analysis performed by the NA49 collaboration (NA49PRC, NA49EPJC), NA61/SHINE collaboration (NA61QM2019, NA61CFM), and STAR collaboration (STARIntermittency) at RHIC BES-I energies. It also discusses the Critical Monte-Carlo (CMC) model belonging to the 3D Ising universality class (AntoniouPRL, CMCPLB) and the UrQMD transport model (UrQMD1, UrQMD2). The work enables future comparisons between experimental data and phenomenological models with proper background subtraction and efficiency corrections, and suggests that measuring the relative density fluctuation \(\Delta n\) can provide an indirect way to extract the intermittency index.
