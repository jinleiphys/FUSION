# Bremsstrahlung Cross Section with Polarized Beams for Luminosity Determination at the EIC

> The bremsstrahlung cross section is calculated at leading order for polarized beams of electrons and ions, which is needed for luminosity measurements at the upcoming Electron Ion Collider (EIC). Analytic expressions, differential in the emitted photon energy and polar angle, are derived. The component of the cross section which depends on the beam polarizations is found to be highly suppressed with respect to the unpolarized Bethe-Heitler component, owing to the low $q^2$ that characterizes the bremsstrahlung process.

# Bremsstrahlung Cross Section with Polarized Beams for Luminosity Determination at the EIC

## Key claim
The polarized component of the leading-order bremsstrahlung cross section for electron-ion collisions is highly suppressed relative to the unpolarized Bethe-Heitler component, by a factor of approximately \(m_e^2/(\varepsilon \varepsilon_p)\), due to the low virtuality \(q^2 \sim m_e^2\) that characterizes the bremsstrahlung process at the EIC. This suppression means the polarization-dependent contribution is negligible for practical luminosity measurements, even though both beams at the EIC will be polarized.

## Method
The author calculated the leading-order (Born approximation) bremsstrahlung cross section for longitudinally polarized electron and proton beams using standard QED Feynman diagrams. The amplitude was squared including spin-dependent terms via the Pauli-Lubanski pseudovectors for both beams, and the resulting tensor contractions were evaluated analytically. The cross section was integrated over the scattered electron angles analytically using basis vectors and Feynman parameter techniques, then over the photon polar angle, with Mathematica used to assemble and simplify the algebraic expressions. The no-recoil approximation (\(q = (0,\mathbf{q})\)) and ultra-relativistic approximations were applied, consistent with the original Bethe-Heitler calculation.

## Key numbers
- The polarized cross section is suppressed relative to the unpolarized Bethe-Heitler component by a factor of \(m_e^2/(\varepsilon \varepsilon_p)\), where \(\varepsilon\) and \(\varepsilon_p\) are the electron and proton beam energies in the laboratory frame.
- For top EIC energies of \(\varepsilon = 18\) GeV and \(\varepsilon_p = 275\) GeV, the polarized component was calculated and shown in figures, with the product of beam polarizations set to \(\mathbb{P}_e \mathbb{P}_p = 1\).
- For heavy nuclei such as uranium, the Bethe-Maximon and Bethe-Heitler unpolarized cross sections differ by about \(2\%\), with the Coulomb correction function \(f(\alpha Z) \approx 1.2 (\alpha Z)^2\) for light nuclei where \(\alpha Z \ll 1\).
- The polarized component becomes negative very near the photon energy limit (18 GeV for the top EIC energies), a feature also observed in prior numerical calculations at larger \(q^2\) by Akushevich (1998).

## Context
This work extends the classic Bethe-Heitler calculation (Bethe and Heitler, 1934) to include beam polarization effects, which were not needed for the HERA luminosity program where beams were unpolarized. Prior calculations of the polarized bremsstrahlung cross section by Akushevich (1998) and Afanasev (2001) considered larger \(q^2\) values than those relevant for EIC luminosity measurements. The analytic expressions presented here for low \(q^2\) enable the EIC to use the bremsstrahlung process for luminosity determination with polarized beams, though the polarized component is so suppressed that it will not affect the measurement. Further work is needed to estimate the effect of the no-recoil approximation and to extend the calculation to transversely polarized protons and heavy-ion beams where the Bethe-Maximon correction (Bethe and Maximon, 1954) becomes relevant.
