# Di-Electrons from Resonances in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions

> The contribution of the low-lying nucleon resonances $P_{33}(1232)$, $P_{11}(1440)$ $D_{13}(1520)$ and $S_{11}(1535)$ to the invariant mass spectra of di-electrons stemming from the exclusive processes $pp\to pp e^+e^-$ and $pn\to pn e^+e^-$ is investigated within a fully covariant and gauge invariant diagrammatical approach. We employ, within the one-boson exchange approximation, effective nucleon-meson interactions including the exchange mesons $π$, $η$, $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ as well as excitations and radiative decays of the above low-lying nucleon resonances. The total contribution of these resonances is dominant, however, bremsstrahlung processes in $pp$ and, in particular, $pn$ collisions at beam energies of 1 - 2 GeV are still significant in certain phase space regions.

## Key claim

The contribution of low-lying nucleon resonances to di-electron production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is dominant overall, but bremsstrahlung processes remain significant in certain phase space regions at beam energies of 1–2 GeV, particularly in proton-neutron collisions.

## Method

The authors employ a fully covariant and gauge-invariant diagrammatic approach within the one-boson exchange approximation, using effective nucleon-meson interactions with exchange mesons π, η, σ, ω, and ρ. They calculate the invariant mass spectra of di-electrons from exclusive pp → pp e⁺e⁻ and pn → pn e⁺e⁻ processes, including excitations and radiative decays of the P₃₃(1232), P₁₁(1440), D₁₃(1520), and S₁₁(1535) nucleon resonances, with gauge invariance restored through meson exchange currents, seagull terms, and Kroll-Rudermann type diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The pn bremsstrahlung cross section is larger than the pp cross section by a factor of 5–6 at the considered energies.
- Meson exchange diagrams and contact terms amplify the pure nucleonic current contribution by approximately 40% at low di-electron invariant masses and up to a factor of 2–3 at higher invariant masses.
- The coupling constants for the Δ resonance are g₁ = 5.478, g₂ = 7.611, and g₃ = 7.0, with off-shell parameters z₁ = 0.05, z₂ = 1.499, and z₃ = 0.
- The Δ Nπ coupling constant is f_{ΔNπ} = 2.13 GeV, and the Δ Nρ coupling constant is f_{ΔNρ} = 7.14 GeV, with cutoff parameters Λ_{ΔNπ} = 1.4214 GeV and Λ_{ΔNρ} = 2.273 GeV.
- The effect of ρ exchange seagull type diagrams varies from 10% at low di-electron invariant masses up to 35% at the kinematical limit.
- The helicity amplitudes for the Δ resonance from the Particle Data Group are in the ranges −A_{1/2}^{PDG} ∼ 0.128–0.145 and −A_{3/2}^{PDG} ∼ 0.243–0.261, yielding uncertainties g₁ ∼ 4.5–5.5 and g₂ ∼ 4.5–8.5.

## Context

This work extends previous covariant and gauge-invariant calculations of nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung by Kaptari and Kampfer (2008) to include low-lying nucleon resonances beyond the Δ isobar. The results are relevant for interpreting di-electron data from the DLS and HADES collaborations, and the authors note that earlier transport models (e.g., Bratkovskaya et al., 2008) that used their bremsstrahlung cross sections successfully described the DLS and HADES data, resolving the so-called "DLS puzzle." The paper enables more accurate modeling of di-electron production in heavy-ion collisions by providing elementary cross sections that include resonance contributions and their interference with bremsstrahlung.
