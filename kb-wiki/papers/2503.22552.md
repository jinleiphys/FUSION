# Spin Polarization of $Λ$ hyperons from Dissipative Spin Hydrodynamics

> We present a framework for spin dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Under the approximation of small polarization, macroscopic spin degrees of freedom decouple from the background, and their evolution equations and transport coefficients have been computed using quantum kinetic theory of massive particles with nonlocal collisions. Employing this theory, we numerically solve dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics. We explore three interaction scenarios between constituent particles and apply this framework to compute both global and local spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV. Our results show that the initially vanishing spin potential relaxes toward thermal vorticity, driving global polarization. Furthermore, we demonstrate that the sign of longitudinal polarization is sensitive to the interaction type, emphasizing the need for a consistent treatment of dissipative effects in spin hydrodynamics to describe experimental data.

## Key claim

Under the approximation of small polarization, the macroscopic spin degrees of freedom decouple from the hydrodynamic background, and the initially vanishing spin potential relaxes toward thermal vorticity during the fireball's lifetime at 200 GeV; however, the sign of longitudinal polarization is sensitive to the interaction type, and the framework may be especially relevant at lower collision energies where spin may not fully thermalize.

## Method

The authors numerically solve dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics in (3+1) dimensions using a Godunov-type HLLE Riemann solver, evolving the spin potential, its electric and magnetic parts, and the spin-shear stress simultaneously with a background hydrodynamic simulation. The evolution equations and transport coefficients are derived from quantum kinetic theory of massive spin-1/2 particles with nonlocal collisions, using the inverse-Reynolds dominance (IReD) approach. Three interaction scenarios are considered: scalar, scalar+pseudoscalar (equivalent to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model), and vector interactions, with a constant particle mass of 300 MeV and zero baryon diffusion.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, impact parameter b = 8.4 fm (corresponding to 20-50% centrality)
- Lambda hyperon mass m_Lambda = 1.115 GeV used in spin polarization formula
- Switching hypersurface defined by energy density epsilon_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Particle mass assumed for transport coefficients: 300 MeV (on the order of thermal masses m_th ~ gT)
- Relaxation times tau_omega, tau_kappa, and tau_t are tabulated as functions of z = m/T in units of the shear-stress relaxation time tau_pi for the three interaction types

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium spin-hydrodynamic approach of Becattini (2015) and Karpenko (2016), which assumed vanishingly small spin-relaxation timescales and successfully described global polarization but failed to reproduce the sign of longitudinal polarization. It goes beyond the numerical solution of spin hydrodynamics presented by Singh (2024) by including dissipative effects from nonlocal collisions, which were shown by Wagner (2024) to drive relaxation of the spin potential toward thermal vorticity. The framework enables dynamical treatment of spin degrees of freedom at lower collision energies where spin may not fully thermalize, and demonstrates that the spin-shear stress plays a role analogous to the thermal shear in local-equilibrium scenarios.
