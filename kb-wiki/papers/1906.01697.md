# Quark Flavour Dependence of the Shear Viscosity in a Quasiparticle Model

> We study the temperature-dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in pure Yang-Mills theory and in QCD with light and strange quarks within kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation. As effective degrees of freedom in a deconfined phase we consider quasiparticle excitations with quark and gluon quantum numbers and dispersion relations that depend explicitly on the temperature. The quasiparticle relaxation times are obtained by computing the microscopic two-body scattering amplitudes for the elementary scatterings among the quasiparticles. For pure Yang-Mills theory we show that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio exhibits a characteristic non-monotonicity with a minimum at the first-order phase transition. In the presence of dynamical quarks the ratio smoothens while still exhibiting a minimum near confinement. Furthermore, there is a significant increase of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in QCD resulting from the quark contributions. This observation differs from previously reported estimates based on functional methods but is in line with perturbative QCD expectations at higher temperatures.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in QCD with light and strange quarks is significantly larger than in pure Yang-Mills theory, with a minimum of about 0.4 near the pseudo-critical temperature, which contrasts with functional method estimates that found only a moderate increase from quark contributions but aligns with perturbative QCD expectations at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors employed kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, modeling deconfined QCD matter as quasiparticle excitations with temperature-dependent effective masses and a residual mean field interaction. The relaxation times for gluons, light quarks, and strange quarks were computed from microscopic two-body scattering amplitudes at tree level for elementary scatterings among massive quasiparticles, using an effective coupling G(T) adjusted to reproduce lattice entropy density data. The shear viscosity was then obtained from the standard kinetic theory expression involving the relaxation times and thermal distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- In pure Yang-Mills theory, the minimum of eta/s reaches the Kovtun-Son-Starinets lower bound of 1/(4 pi) at the first-order phase transition temperature T_c.
- In N_f=2+1 QCD, the total eta/s exhibits a shallow minimum of about 0.4 around the pseudo-critical temperature T_c = 155 MeV.
- The gluon relaxation time in QCD is roughly a factor 4-5 smaller than the light and strange quark relaxation times.
- The entropy density in QCD is increased by about a factor 2-3 compared to pure Yang-Mills theory in the deconfined phase.
- The ratio eta_s/eta_l is less than 0.5, approaching 0.5 only slowly with increasing temperature, while eta_g/eta_l is less than or equal to 0.2 in the studied temperature interval.

## Context

This work extends quasiparticle model calculations of shear viscosity from pure Yang-Mills theory (Peshier 2005, Bluhm 2009) to full QCD with dynamical quarks, and contrasts with functional diagrammatic estimates by Christiansen (2014) that found only a moderate increase from quark contributions. The results enable improved fluid dynamical simulations of the quark-gluon plasma at LHC and RHIC energies by providing a temperature-dependent eta/s that accounts for quark flavour effects, and the minimal value found is comparable to that observed in ultra-cold atomic Fermi gases at unitarity.
