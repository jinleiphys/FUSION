# Constraints on the dense matter equation of state from young and cold isolated neutron stars

> Neutron stars are the dense and highly magnetic relics of supernova explosions of massive stars. The quest to constrain the Equation of State (EoS) of ultra-dense matter and thereby probe the behavior of matter inside neutron stars, is one of the core goals of modern physics and astrophysics. A promising method involves investigating the long-term cooling of neutron stars, and comparing theoretical predictions with various sources at different ages. However, limited observational data, and uncertainties in source ages and distances, have hindered this approach. In this work, re-analyzing XMM-Newton and Chandra data from dozens of thermally emitting isolated neutron stars, we have identified three sources with unexpectedly cold surface temperatures for their young ages. To investigate these anomalies, we conducted magneto-thermal simulations across diverse mass and magnetic fields, considering three different EoS. We found that the "minimal" cooling model, failed to explain the observations, regardless the mass and the magnetic field, as validated by a machine learning classification method. The existence of these young cold neutron stars suggests that any dense matter EoS must be compatible with a fast cooling process at least in certain mass ranges, eliminating a significant portion of current EoS options according to recent meta-modelling analysis.

## Key claim

The existence of three young (800-8000 yr) isolated neutron stars with surface temperatures unexpectedly cold by about an order of magnitude compared to other sources at similar ages provides evidence that any dense matter equation of state must be compatible with a fast cooling process at least in certain mass ranges, thereby eliminating a significant portion of current equation of state options according to a meta-modelling analysis, though the exact equation of state cannot yet be constrained.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed XMM-Newton and Chandra data for a sample of 70 thermally emitting isolated neutron stars with reliable ages and distances, identifying three sources (PSR J0205+6449, PSR B2334+61, and CXOU J0852-4617) with anomalously low thermal luminosities. They conducted 81 magneto-thermal simulations using three different equations of state (SLy4 with minimal cooling, BSK24 with enhanced cooling via nucleon direct Urca, and GM1A with enhanced cooling via hyperon direct Urca) across three masses (1.4, 1.6, and 1.8 solar masses) and nine initial magnetic field values from 1e12 G to 7e13 G, then applied a machine learning classification method in 3D and 4D parameter spaces to determine which models could reproduce the observed thermal luminosities, spin periods, and spin derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Three sources identified as anomalously cold: PSR J0205+6449 (age 841 yr, dipolar magnetic field 7e12 G, spin period 70 ms), PSR B2334+61 (age 7700 yr, dipolar magnetic field 2e13 G, spin period 490 ms), and CXOU J0852-4617 (age 2500-5000 yr)
- The three cold sources are colder by about an order of magnitude in thermal luminosity compared to other objects at similar young ages
- Simulations covered three masses: 1.4, 1.6, and 1.8 solar masses
- Initial magnetic field values ranged from 1e12 G to 7e13 G at the pole
- For PSR B2334+61, the best-fit parameters indicate a mass of about 1.6 solar masses and a dipolar magnetic field at birth of about 0.7-3e13 G
- For PSR J0205+6449, compatible masses of about 1.6 solar masses and initial magnetic field within about 3-20e12 G
- The SLy4 equation of state with minimal cooling gave probabilities below 5% for matching the data
- According to a simplified nucleonic meta-modelling analysis, about 75% of currently available equations of state are potentially excluded

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to constrain the neutron star equation of state through cooling studies (Page 2004, Potekhin 2015) and earlier evidence for enhanced cooling in isolated neutron stars (Ho 2009, Beznogov 2015) and transiently accreting neutron stars (Jonker 2007, Heinke 2007, Brown 2018). The study overcomes previous limitations from poorly constrained ages and distances by using three sources with well-determined ages from associated supernova remnants and historical records. The results enable future work to systematically test specific fast-cooling mechanisms (hyperons, quarks, pure nucleonic matter with large symmetry energy) and to further narrow down the allowed equations of state through additional observations and simulations.
