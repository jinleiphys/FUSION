# Tensor-polarized parton distribution functions for spin-1 hadrons

> Spin-1 hadrons contain different aspects of spin physics from the ones of the spin-1/2 nucleon because of the existence of tensor-polarized structure functions. In the charged-lepton deep inelastic scattering from a spin-1 hadron or nucleus, such as the deuteron, there are leading-twist structure functions $b_1$ and $b_2$. In addition, there exists a gluon transversity which does not exist in the spin-1/2 nucleon. In the deuteron, these observables could probe interesting dynamical aspects beyond a simple bound system of a proton and a neutron. In addition, there are recent theoretical studies on higher-twist distributions. Tensor-polarized deuteron experiments are now under preparation at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility, so that the topic of polarized deuteron is expected to become one of exciting fields in hadron physics. This paper is a brief overview on the tensor-polarized parton distribution functions, including transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions and fragmentation functions up to twist 4.

## Key claim

Spin-1 hadrons such as the deuteron contain tensor-polarized structure functions beyond those of spin-1/2 nucleons, including the leading-twist functions \(b_1\) and \(b_2\) and a gluon transversity that does not exist in the nucleon; however, the standard convolution model for the deuteron gives results for \(b_1\) that are rather different from the existing HERMES data, indicating that new hadronic physics could be necessary for interpreting \(b_1\).

## Method

The paper provides a brief overview of tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) for spin-1 hadrons, covering the definition of the four structure functions \(b_{1\text{--}4}\) in charged-lepton deep inelastic scattering, the convolution model calculation of \(b_1\) for the deuteron using the spectral function and deuteron wave functions, and a \(\chi^2\) analysis of the HERMES data to parametrize tensor-polarized PDFs. It also catalogs the possible transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) and collinear PDFs up to twist 4 by expanding the quark correlation function under Hermiticity, parity, and time-reversal invariance, and notes that corresponding fragmentation functions are obtained by changing variables and function names.

## Key numbers

- The HERMES collaboration measured \(\int_{0.02}^{0.85} dx \, b_1(x) = [0.35 \pm 0.10 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.18 \text{ (sys)}] \times 10^{-2}\) at \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\) (average \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\)).
- The standard deuteron convolution model calculations for \(xb_1(x)\) are shown at \(Q^2 = 1.0\), 2.5, and 5.0 GeV\(^2\) and are very different from the HERMES data.
- The initial scale for the tensor-polarized PDF parametrization is taken as \(Q_0^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), the average \(Q^2\) of the HERMES data.
- The tensor-polarized PDF parametrization uses the form \(\delta_{_T} w(x) = a x^b (1-x)^c (x_0-x)\) with \(c=1\) fixed; the parameters \(a\), \(b\), and \(\alpha_{\bar q}\) are determined by \(\chi^2\) analysis.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for spin-1 structure functions beyond the spin-1/2 nucleon case, building on the initial measurement of \(b_1\) by the HERMES collaboration (Airapetian 2005) and the convolution model calculations for the deuteron (Cosyn 2017). The paper notes that the Gottfried sum-rule violation led to the discovery of flavor asymmetric antiquark distributions, and similarly a violation of the \(b_1\) sum rule (Close 1990) could indicate finite tensor-polarized antiquark distributions. The upcoming tensor-polarized deuteron experiments at Jefferson Lab (Poudel 2025) and possible measurements at Fermilab, NICA, LHC-spin, and electron-ion colliders are expected to enable tests of these predictions and potentially reveal new hadronic dynamics.
