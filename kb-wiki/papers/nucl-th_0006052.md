# Strange hadronic stellar matter within the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory

> In the framework of the non-relativistic Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory, we derive a microscopic equation of state for asymmetric and $β$-stable matter containing $Σ^-$ and $Λ$ hyperons. We mainly study the effects of three-body forces (TBFs) among nucleons on the hyperon formation and the equation of state (EoS). We find that, when TBFs are included, the stellar core is almost equally populated by nucleons and hyperons. The resulting EoS, which turns out to be extremely soft, has been used in order to calculate the static structure of neutron stars. We obtain a value of the maximum mass of 1.26 solar masses (1 solar mass $M_o \simeq 1.99 \cdot 10^{33} g$). Stellar rotations increase this value by about 12%.

## Key claim

The inclusion of three-body forces among nucleons leads to a stellar core almost equally populated by nucleons and hyperons, producing an extremely soft equation of state that yields a maximum neutron star mass of only 1.26 solar masses, a value that lies below the observed mass of PSR1913+16 (1.44 solar masses) unless stellar rotation is included.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for asymmetric and β-stable nuclear matter containing Σ⁻ and Λ hyperons, using the Paris and Argonne v₁₈ potentials for nucleon-nucleon interactions and the Nijmegen soft-core model for nucleon-hyperon interactions. Three-body forces among nucleons were included via a contribution with long-range two-pion exchange attraction and intermediate-range repulsion to reproduce the empirical nuclear matter saturation point. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static and rotating neutron star configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of static neutron stars with hyperons: 1.26 solar masses (Mₒ ≈ 1.99 × 10³³ g)
- Stellar rotation at the Kepler frequency increases the maximum mass by about 12%
- Hyperon formation begins at densities ρ ≈ 2–3 times normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³)
- Maximum mass without hyperons: about 2.1 Mₒ
- Observed mass of pulsar PSR1913+16: 1.44 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body approach (Baldo, previous work) to include hyperonic degrees of freedom in neutron star matter, building on the BHF scheme with continuous choice for the single-particle potential. The results contradict the possibility of explaining the observed 1.44 Mₒ pulsar mass with purely static hyperonic stars, highlighting the need for realistic hyperon-hyperon interactions (currently absent due to lack of experimental data) and demonstrating the extreme sensitivity of neutron star properties to the details of the nuclear interaction. The outer crust equations of state used are from Feynman-Metropolis-Teller (1949), Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (1971), and Negele-Vautherin (1973).
