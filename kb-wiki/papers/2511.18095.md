# Quantum and classical analyses of intertwined phase transitions in odd-mass Nb isotopes

> Quantum phase transitions (QPTs) in odd-mass Nb isotopes are investigated in the framework of the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing. A quantum analysis reveals a Type I QPT (gradual shape-evolution within the intruder configuration) superimposed on a Type II QPT (abrupt crossing of normal and intruder states), thus demonstrating the occurrence of intertwined QPTs. A classical analysis highlights the implications for the single particle motion in the deformed field generated by the even-even Zr cores.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that odd-mass Nb isotopes exhibit intertwined quantum phase transitions (QPTs): a Type I QPT (gradual shape evolution within the intruder configuration) superimposed on a Type II QPT (abrupt crossing of normal and intruder configurations), but only when the mixing between configurations is weak enough that the individual QPTs remain distinguishable.

## Method

The authors employ the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM), coupling a single proton in a π(1g9/2) orbit to bosonic spaces representing normal (N bosons) and intruder (N+2 bosons) configurations of the even-even Zr cores. They perform a quantum analysis by diagonalizing the IBFM-CM Hamiltonian for Nb isotopes with mass numbers A=93-105 and compare calculated energies, B(E2) values, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments to experimental data. A classical analysis is carried out by constructing Bose-Fermi potential surfaces from coherent states, diagonalizing the resulting matrix potentials to obtain Landau potentials as functions of the quadrupole deformation β.

## Key numbers

- Mass range studied: A=93-105 for Nb isotopes, corresponding to neutron numbers 52-64
- The center of gravity of the quintuplet built on 2+1;A in 92Zr is 0.976 MeV, in agreement with the observed energy 0.935 MeV of 2+1 in 92Zr
- The center of gravity of the nonet built on 4+1;A in 92Zr is 1.591 MeV, close to 1.495 MeV of 4+1;A
- The center of gravity of the quintuplet built on 2+2;B in 92Zr is 1.705 MeV, compared to 1.847 MeV of 2+2;B
- Observed E2 rate 1.03(9) W.u. for 9/2+2 → 9/2+1 in 93Nb, compared to calculated value 0.85 W.u.
- Observed E2 rate 1.52(10) W.u. for 9/2+3 → 9/2+1 in 93Nb
- The ground state changes from 9/2+1 to 5/2+1 at neutron number 60, marking the Type II QPT critical point

## Context

This work extends the study of intertwined QPTs previously observed in even-even Zr isotopes by Gavrielov et al. (2019, 2020, 2022) to the adjacent odd-even Nb isotopes. The analysis shows that the same intricate scenario of a Type I QPT within the intruder configuration (evolving from spherical weak-coupling in 93Nb to deformed strong-coupling in 103Nb) superimposed on an abrupt Type II configuration crossing persists in the odd-mass system. The classical analysis using Bose-Fermi potential surfaces provides geometric interpretation of the quantum results, showing how the odd fermion modifies the QPT landscape compared to the purely bosonic cores. The work enables future studies of configuration-mixed QPTs in other odd-mass isotopic chains and demonstrates that mean-field approaches without configuration mixing, such as that of Guzman et al. (2011), fail to reproduce the ground-state spin switch from 9/2+ to 5/2+ in Nb isotopes.
