# Photoproduction of the $πN$ pair on nuclei and isobar configurations

> A model of $πN$ pair photoproduction on nuclei at high momentum transfer is presented. The $A(γ,πN)B$ reaction amplitude is obtained by means of an extended impulse approximation, according to which the nuclear wavefunction includes delta-isobar components in addition to nucleon components. A one-particle transition operator is defined in terms of the two-body $γN\toπN$ and $γΔ\toπN$ photoproduction amplitudes. Direct and exchange mechanisms of the nuclear photoproduction reaction are studied, and numerical estimates are made and presented of $^{12}{\rm C}(γ,π^- p){^{11}{\rm C}}$ and $^{12}{\rm C}(γ,π^+ p){^{11}{\rm Be}}$ differential cross sections at photon incident energies in the $Δ(1232)$ resonance region.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model of πN pair photoproduction on nuclei at high momentum transfer, where the nuclear wavefunction includes delta-isobar components in addition to nucleon components; the model is intended to test whether previously reported experimental signatures of quasibound isobar-nucleus states (Glavanakov and Tabachenko, earlier works; other authors, 2000s) might instead be explained by isobar configurations in the nuclear ground state.

## Method

The reaction amplitude for A(γ,πN)B is obtained via an extended impulse approximation, using one-body density matrices that include both nucleon and Δ-isobar degrees of freedom, together with single-particle transition operators for γN→πN and γΔ→πN processes. Direct and exchange reaction mechanisms are formulated in configuration space, with the nuclear wavefunction expanded in a shell-model basis with ls- or jj-coupling and a one-boson-exchange transition potential for the NN→ΔN vertex. Numerical estimates are performed for 12C(γ,π⁻p)11C and 12C(γ,π⁺p)11Be differential cross sections at photon energies in the Δ(1232) resonance region.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Arenhovel et al. (1970s-1980s) for isobar configurations in nuclear ground states to the photoproduction of pion-nucleon pairs, and directly addresses the interpretation of experiments at Tomsk synchrotron, Saclay, and MAMI (Mainz) that had been interpreted as evidence for quasibound Δ-nucleus states. The model enables separation of the isobar-configuration contribution from the quasibound-state mechanism, allowing future experimental tests of whether the observed cross sections in 12C(γ,π⁻p) and 12C(γ,π⁺p) reactions arise from virtual isobars in the ground state rather than from a true Δ-nucleus bound state.
