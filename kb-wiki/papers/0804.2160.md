# Direct-decay properties of Giant Resonances

> A semi-microscopic approach based on the continuum-RPA method and a phenomenological treatment of the spreading effect is developed and applied to describe direct-decay properties of a few isovector giant resonances. Capabilities of the approach to describe giant-resonance gross properties are also checked.

# Direct-decay properties of Giant Resonances

## Key claim
The paper develops a semi-microscopic approach that can satisfactorily describe direct-decay properties of several isovector giant resonances, but the approach shows only qualitative agreement for some cases (e.g., 21.4% calculated total direct-neutron-decay branching ratio for the IVGDR in ⁴⁸Ca versus the experimental value of 39(5)%) and fails to reproduce the experimental distribution of decay probabilities for the IVGSMR in ²⁰⁸Bi despite matching the total branching ratio.

## Method
The approach combines continuum-RPA (CRPA) equations, formulated consistently with Migdal's finite Fermi-system theory, with a phenomenological treatment of the spreading effect via an energy-averaging substitution ω → ω + (1/2)iI, where I is an adjustable spreading parameter. The effective single-particle probing operator determines both the strength function and the nucleon-escape amplitudes, which are computed from integrals of bound-state and continuum-state wave functions. For high-energy "over-barrier" giant resonances where doorway-state resonances overlap, the spreading parameter is taken as radial- and energy-dependent and introduced directly into the CRPA equations.

## Key numbers
- Total direct-neutron-decay branching ratio for IVGDR in ⁴⁸Ca: calculated 21.4% versus experimental 39(5)%
- IVGQR in ²⁰⁸Pb: peak energy ≈ 21.5 MeV, total width ≈ 7 MeV (using velocity parameter k₂ = 0.1)
- IVGDR2 (overtone) in ²⁰⁸Pb: peak energy ≈ 34 MeV, total width ≈ 15 MeV; total neutron branching ratio 48%, total proton branching ratio 19%
- IVGSMR in ²⁰⁸Bi: calculated total width ≈ 11 MeV versus experimental ≈ 14 MeV; calculated total proton branching ratio 63% versus experimental 52(12)%
- IVGSMR in ⁹⁰Nb: predicted total proton branching ratio 72%
- IVGSMR in ¹²⁰Sb: predicted total proton branching ratio 65%
- 1⁻ IAR in ⁹⁰Zr at E_x ≈ 16 MeV: calculated partial widths with pairing (Δ_p = 1 MeV), Γ_γ₀ = 110 eV, Γ_p₀ = 55 keV, Γ_p₁ = 1.7 keV, Γ_p₂ = 10.5 keV, Γ_p₃ = 0.1 keV; experimental values, Γ_γ₀ = 108(35) eV, Γ_p₀ = 54(18) keV, Γ_p₁ < 2 keV, Γ_p₂ = 20(5) keV, Γ_p₃ = 7(2) keV

## Context
This work extends the author's previous semi-microscopic CRPA-based descriptions of direct-decay properties for the isobaric analog resonance, Gamow-Teller resonance, isovector giant spin-dipole resonance, isoscalar giant monopole resonance, and isoscalar giant dipole resonance (Urin, various years). The approach is presented as a semi-microscopic version of the phenomenological direct-semidirect (DSD) model (Ref. [1] in the paper), with the key difference that the GR form factor is proportional to the transition density and has no imaginary part. The results enable predictions for decay properties of high-energy isovector resonances that could be observed in coincidence experiments following inelastic scattering of electrons or light ions, and identify the need for improved treatment of non-resonant reaction amplitudes and nucleon pairing effects.
