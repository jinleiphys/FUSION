# Impact of nuclear mass models on $r$-process nucleosynthesis and heavy element abundances in $r$-process enhanced metal-poor stars

> Due to the lack of experimental data on extremely neutron-rich nuclei, theoretical values derived from nuclear physics models are essential for the rapid neutron capture process ($r$-process). Metal-poor stars enriched by the $r$-process offer valuable cases for studying the impact of nuclear physics models on $r$-process nucleosynthesis. This study analyzes four widely used nuclear physics models in detail: Finite-Range Droplet Model, Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov, Duflo-Zuker, and Weizs$\ddot{\rm a}$cker-Skyrme (WS4). Theoretical values predicted by the WS4 model are found to be in good agreement with experimental data, with deviations significantly smaller than those predicted by other models. The heavy element abundances observed in $r$-process enhanced metal-poor stars can be accurately reproduced by $r$-process nucleosynthesis simulations using the WS4 model, particularly for the rare earth elements. This suggests that nuclear data provided by nuclear physics model like WS4 are both essential and crucial for $r$-process nucleosynthesis studies.

# Impact of nuclear mass models on r-process nucleosynthesis and heavy element abundances in r-process enhanced metal-poor stars

## Key claim

The Weizsäcker-Skyrme (WS4) nuclear mass model reproduces observed heavy element abundances in r-process enhanced metal-poor stars more accurately than three other widely used models (FRDM2012, HFB27, DZ31), particularly for rare earth elements from lanthanum (Z=57) to lutetium (Z=71), but this conclusion is conditional on the specific set of six astrophysical trajectories from numerical relativity simulations of neutron star mergers used in the study.

## Method

The authors used the nuclear reaction network code SkyNet, containing over 7,800 nuclide species and 140,000 nuclear reactions, to perform r-process nucleosynthesis simulations with parameterized trajectories from numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers (six sets of astrophysical parameters). They updated nuclear masses from the AME2020 database and NUBASE2020 decay data, and for nuclei without experimental data used theoretical values from four models: FRDM2012, HFB27, DZ31, and WS4; neutron capture rates were recalculated using the Hauser-Feshbach statistical code TALYS and converted to REACLIB polynomial format. Simulated abundance patterns were compared to observed abundances in eight r-process enhanced metal-poor stars that contain both thorium and uranium.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation between WS4 model nuclear masses and AME2020 experimental data: 0.295 MeV for all nuclides, 0.360 MeV for newly measured nuclei (those in AME2020 but not AME2016)
- Root-mean-square deviation for FRDM2012 nuclear masses: 0.606 MeV (all), 0.796 MeV (new); for HFB27: 0.518 MeV (all), 0.673 MeV (new); for DZ31: 0.425 MeV (all), 0.619 MeV (new)
- Root-mean-square deviation for neutron capture rates based on WS4 model: 0.318 (all), 0.331 (new), indicating overall discrepancy within a factor of 10^0.3 ≈ 2
- Root-mean-square deviation between simulated abundances and observed abundances in metal-poor stars: WS4 = 0.347, FRDM2012 = 0.456, HFB27 = 0.380, DZ31 = 0.372
- Element abundances of metal-poor stars scaled to Y(Z=63) = 5.0 × 10^-5 for comparison
- r-process simulations start when temperature drops below T = 6 × 10^9 K and end at t = 10^9 s
- Astrophysical parameters include electron fractions Ye from 0.13 to 0.17, specific entropies from 15 to 22 kB/baryon, and expansion velocities from 0.16c to 0.22c

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the impact of nuclear physics inputs on r-process nucleosynthesis, particularly following the multi-messenger observation of neutron star merger GW170817/GRB170817A/AT2017gfo which confirmed kilonova models and suggested approximately 0.05 solar masses of heavy r-process nuclei were synthesized. The study builds on prior work by Mumpower et al. (2016) reviewing nuclear data for r-process, and by various authors (e.g., Surman et al. 2001, Arcones et al. 2011, Mumpower et al. 2012) who noted significant variations in rare earth element abundances from different nuclear models. The eight metal-poor stars studied include CS 31082-001, BD+17°3248, HE 1523-0901, CS 29497-004, J2038-0023, J0954+5246, J2003-1142, and J2213-5137, all with detected thorium and uranium, whose ages from Th/U chronometry are consistent with the cosmic age of 13.8 billion years according to Wang et al. (2022). The results enable more reliable age determinations of metal-poor stars using Th/U nuclear chronometry and recommend using WS4 model values for future r-process simulations.
