# Neutron matter at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory

> Neutron matter presents a unique system for chiral effective field theory (EFT), because all many-body forces among neutrons are predicted to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). We present the first complete N3LO calculation of the neutron matter energy. This includes the subleading three-nucleon (3N) forces for the first time and all leading four-nucleon (4N) forces. We find relatively large contributions from N3LO 3N forces. Our results provide constraints for neutron-rich matter in astrophysics with controlled theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim

The first complete calculation of the neutron matter energy at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in chiral effective field theory, including subleading three-nucleon (3N) forces for the first time and all leading four-nucleon (4N) forces, finds relatively large contributions from N3LO 3N forces that reduce the theoretical uncertainty compared to N2LO but not by the factor of ~1/3 expected from power counting, reflecting large low-energy constant contributions at N4LO.

## Method

The authors evaluated the neutron matter energy per particle at the Hartree-Fock level for all N3LO 3N and 4N force contributions, using Jacobi-momenta regulators with cutoff Λ = 2-2.5 fm⁻¹. They used N2LO and N3LO two-nucleon (NN) potentials from Epelbaum, Glöckle and Meißner (EGM) with cutoffs 450/500 and 450/700 MeV, and the Λ=500 MeV N3LO NN potential from Entem and Machleidt (EM). Beyond Hartree-Fock, they included all diagrams involving NN interactions and N2LO 3N forces to second order, plus particle-particle diagrams to third order, with theoretical uncertainties estimated by varying the 3N/4N cutoff, the low-energy constants c₁ and c₃, and the single-particle spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Neutron matter energy per particle at saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³: 14.1-21.0 MeV (full N3LO band); omitting the EM 500 potential gives 14.1-18.4 MeV
- N2LO neutron matter energy per particle at n₀: 15.5-21.4 MeV
- Two-pion-exchange 3N force contribution at n₀: approximately -1.5 MeV per particle
- Two-pion–one-pion-exchange 3N force contribution at n₀: -3.6 MeV per particle
- Pion-ring 3N force contribution at n₀: +3.3 MeV per particle
- Two-pion-exchange–contact 3N force contribution at n₀: -2.8 to +1.3 MeV depending on NN potential
- Total 4N force contribution at n₀: -174 ± 10 keV per particle
- Symmetry energy S_v: 28.9-34.9 MeV
- Density dependence of symmetry energy L: 43.0-66.6 MeV
- Neutron star radius for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: 9.7-13.9 km

## Context

This work extends previous neutron matter calculations at N2LO (Hebeler et al., 2010) by including for the first time all N3LO 3N and 4N forces, which had been derived only recently. The results are compared with NLO lattice simulations at low densities, Quantum Monte Carlo simulations (Gandolfi et al., 2012), variational calculations using phenomenological potentials (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998), and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations (Gandolfi, Carlson, and Reddy, 2012). The N3LO band provides constraints for the nuclear equation of state and neutron-rich matter in astrophysics, and the large N3LO 3N contributions signal the importance of including Δ excitations explicitly in future chiral EFT calculations.
