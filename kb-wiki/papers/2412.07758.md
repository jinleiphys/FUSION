# Fastest spinning millisecond pulsars: indicators for quark matter in neutron stars?

> We study rotating hybrid stars, with a particular emphasis on the effect of a deconfinement phase transition on their properties at high spin. Our analysis is based on a hybrid equation of state (EoS) with a phase transition from hypernuclear matter to color-superconducting quark matter, where both phases are described within a relativistic density functional approach. By varying the vector meson and diquark couplings in the quark matter phase, we obtain different hybrid star sequences with varying extension of the quark matter core, ensuring consistency with astrophysical constraints from mass, radius and tidal deformability measurements. As a result, we demonstrate the impact of an increasing rotational frequency on the maximum gravitational mass, the central energy density of compact stars, the appearance of the quasi-radial oscillations and non-axisymmetric instabilities. We demonstrate that for the most favorable parameter sets with a strong vector coupling, hybrid star configurations with color superconducting quark matter core can describe the fastest spinning and heaviest galactic neutron star (NS) J0952-0607, while it is out of reach for the purely hadronic hypernuclear star configuration. We also revise the previously proposed empirical relation between the Kepler frequency, gravitational mass, and radius of non-rotating NSs, obtained based on the assumption that all NSs, up to the heaviest, are hadronic. We show how the phase transition to quark matter alters this relation and, consequently, the constraints on the dense matter EoS. Our findings reveal that incorporating the hybrid EoS has significant implications for the constraints on the properties of strongly interacting matter and NSs, placing the upper limit on $R_{1.4} \leq 14.90$ km and $R_{0.7}<11.49$ km (considering 716 Hz frequency limit from J1748+2446ad) and $R_{1.4}\leq$11.90~km (for 1000 Hz).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hybrid star configurations with a color-superconducting quark matter core can describe the fastest spinning and heaviest galactic neutron star J0952-0607, while purely hadronic hypernuclear star configurations cannot reach this object; however, this conclusion depends on selecting parameter sets with a strong vector coupling in the quark matter phase.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state using a two-phase approach with Maxwell construction, matching a hypernuclear hadronic phase (DD2npY-T model) to a color-superconducting quark matter phase described by a confining relativistic density functional approach with vector and diquark couplings as free parameters. They solve the Einstein equations for uniformly rotating axisymmetric compact stars using the publicly available RNS code, computing mass-radius relations, Kepler frequencies, and stability against quasi-radial and non-axisymmetric oscillations for sequences of rotating hybrid stars with varying onset densities of the deconfinement phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of rotating hybrid stars reaches up to 2.965 solar masses for the parameter set with vector coupling 0.452 and diquark coupling 0.780, compared to 2.414 solar masses for the corresponding static configuration
- The fastest known pulsar PSR J1748-2446ad has a spin period of 1.4 ms corresponding to 716 Hz
- Upper limit on radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is constrained to 14.90 km when considering the 716 Hz frequency limit from J1748+2446ad, and to 11.90 km for a 1000 Hz limit
- Upper limit on radius of a 0.7 solar mass neutron star is constrained to 11.49 km considering the 716 Hz frequency limit
- For the parameter set with vector coupling 0.3 and diquark coupling 0.760, the onset of deconfinement occurs at a baryon density of 0.1778 fm^-3 with an energy density jump of 151.438 MeV fm^-3
- The oblateness of a 1.5 solar mass rest mass star follows a linear relation with normalized angular velocity, with the fitting parameter 2*sqrt(a) approximately equal to 0.772

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rotating hybrid stars by Ippolito (2007), Dhiman (2010), Ayvazyan (2013), Bhattacharyya (2017), and Largani (2021) by incorporating recent observational constraints from NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0437-4715, as well as the maximum mass limits from Antoniadis (2013) and Romani (2021, 2022). The authors revise the previously proposed empirical relation between Kepler frequency, gravitational mass, and radius of non-rotating neutron stars that was obtained assuming all neutron stars are purely hadronic, showing how the phase transition to quark matter alters this relation and enables new constraints on the dense matter equation of state. The findings enable future tests of quark matter presence in neutron stars through observations of the fastest spinning millisecond pulsars.
