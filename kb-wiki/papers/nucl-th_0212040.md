# Nuclear Structure in Nonmesonic Weak Decay of Hypernuclei

> A general shell model formalism for the nonmesonic weak decay of the hypernuclei has been developed.It involves a partial wave expansion of the emitted nucleon waves,preserves naturally the antisymmetrization between the escaping particles and the residual core, and contains as a particular case the weak Lambda-core coupling formalism. The hypernuclei are grouped having in view their A-1 cores, that is in those with even-even, even-odd and odd-odd cores.It is shown that in all three cases the nuclear structure manifests itself basically through Pauli Principle, and very simple expressions are derived for the neutron and proton induced decays rates, which does not involve the spectroscopic factors.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general shell model formalism for nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei and claims that nuclear structure effects are of minor importance, manifesting themselves primarily through the Pauli principle. However, the authors hedge that while the calculated total nonmesonic decay rates \(\Gamma_{NM}\) are consistent with experimental data, the measured neutron-to-proton ratios \(\Gamma_{n/p}\) are not well accounted for by the One-Meson-Exchange Model (OMEM), and they suggest that unless additional degrees of freedom are incorporated, the OMEM parameters should be radically modified.

## Method

The authors develop a shell model formalism using a partial wave expansion of emitted nucleon waves, preserving antisymmetrization between escaping particles and the residual core. They group hypernuclei by their \(A-1\) cores (even-even, even-odd, and odd-odd) and derive simple expressions for neutron- and proton-induced decay rates \(\Gamma_n\) and \(\Gamma_p\) that do not involve spectroscopic factors. The weak \(\Lambda N \to NN\) transition potential is evaluated using the One-Meson-Exchange Model (OMEM) including the complete pseudoscalar meson octet (\(\pi, \eta, K\)) and vector meson octet (\(\rho, \omega, K^*\)), with finite nucleon size and short range correlations included simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Total nonmesonic decay rate for \({}^{5}_\Lambda He\): \(\Gamma_{NM} = 0.609\ \Gamma^0\) (PS+V model), where \(\Gamma^0 = 2.50 \times 10^{-6}\) eV; experimental value is \(0.41 \pm 0.14\ \Gamma^0\) (Sz91).
- Total nonmesonic decay rate for \({}^{12}_\Lambda C\): \(\Gamma_{NM} = 1.000\ \Gamma^0\) (PS+V model); experimental values range from \(0.89 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.03\ \Gamma^0\) (No95) to \(1.14 \pm 0.08\ \Gamma^0\) (Bh98).
- Total nonmesonic decay rate for \({}^{28}_\Lambda Si\): \(\Gamma_{NM} = 1.322\ \Gamma^0\) (PS+V model); experimental value is \(1.30 \pm 0.10\ \Gamma^0\) (Pa00).
- Neutron-to-proton ratio for \({}^{12}_\Lambda C\): \(\Gamma_{n/p} = 0.258\) (PS+V model); experimental values range from \(1.17^{+0.09+0.20}_{-0.08-0.18}\) (Ha02) to \(1.87 \pm 0.59^{+0.32}_{-1.00}\) (No95).
- Neutron-to-proton ratio for \({}^{5}_\Lambda He\): \(\Gamma_{n/p} = 0.320\) (PS+V model); experimental values are \(0.93 \pm 0.55\) (Sz91) and \(1.97 \pm 0.67\) (No95).
- Neutron-to-proton ratio for \({}^{4}_\Lambda He\): \(\Gamma_{n/p} = 0.061\) (PS+V model); experimental value is \(0.25 \pm 0.13\) (Ze98).
- Free \(\Lambda\) hyperon decay total rate: \(\Gamma^0 = 2.50 \times 10^{-6}\) eV, corresponding to lifetime \(\tau^0 = 2.63 \times 10^{-10}\) sec.
- Q-value for nonmesonic decay: \(Q_{NM} \sim 120-135\) MeV, with mean \(\varepsilon_N^\downarrow \sim 30\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the shell model formalism previously developed by Barbero et al. (2002) for hypernuclei with odd-mass cores (such as \({}^{4}_\Lambda H\), \({}^{4}_\Lambda He\), \({}^{12}_\Lambda C\), and \({}^{28}_\Lambda Si\)) to also cover hypernuclei with even-even cores (\({}^{5}_\Lambda He\), \({}^{17}_\Lambda O\)) and odd-odd cores (\({}^{3}_\Lambda H\), \({}^{11}_\Lambda B\)). The formalism contrasts with earlier approaches using coefficients of fractional parentage and spectroscopic factors (e.g., Ramos et al. 1992, Parreno et al. 1997, Itonaga et al. 1998, 2002). The results confirm that the simple one-pion-exchange (OPE) model accounts for total rates but fails for \(\Gamma_{n/p}\), and that including all pseudoscalar and vector mesons does not resolve the discrepancy. The authors suggest that future work should incorporate additional degrees of freedom such as factorizable terms, axial-vector-meson exchanges, or correlated \(2\pi\) exchange as considered by Itonaga et al. (2002).
