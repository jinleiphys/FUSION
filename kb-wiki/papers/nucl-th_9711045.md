# Approximate Treatment of Lepton Distortion in Charged-Current Neutrino Scattering from Nuclei

> The partial-wave expansion used to treat the distortion of scattered electrons by the nuclear Coulomb field is simpler and considerably less time-consuming when applied to the production of muons and electrons by low and intermediate-energy neutrinos. For angle-integrated cross sections, however, a modification of the "effective-momentum-transfer" approximation seems to work so well that for muons the full distorted-wave treatment is usually unnecessary, even at kinetic energies as low as an MeV and in nuclei as heavy as lead. The method does not work as well for electron production at low energies, but there a Fermi function usually proves adequate. Scattering of electron-neutrinos from muon decay on iodine and of atmospheric neutrinos on iron are discussed in light of these results.

## Key claim

For angle-integrated cross sections in charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering, a modified effective-momentum-transfer approximation works so well for muons that the full distorted-wave treatment is usually unnecessary, even at kinetic energies as low as 1 MeV and in nuclei as heavy as lead; the method does not work as well for electron production at low energies, but there a Fermi function usually proves adequate.

## Method

The paper compares three approaches for including Coulomb distortion of the outgoing lepton in charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering: the full distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) using a partial-wave expansion, the standard effective-momentum approximation (EMA) used in electron scattering, and a modified EMA that rescales the lepton wave function amplitude by sqrt(k_eff E_eff / k E) instead of k_eff/k. The author tests these approximations by computing total cross sections for transitions from 0+ ground states to excited states with various multipolarities, using surface-peaked transition densities proportional to delta(r-R) in nuclei from 12C to 208Pb, for both electrons and muons over a range of kinetic energies.

## Key numbers

- For muons in 208Bi, the modified EMA agrees with full DWBA to within about 5% at muon kinetic energies of 150 MeV for the 0+ multipole, and better for other multipoles.
- For electrons in 208Pb, the standard EMA works well above about 30 MeV kinetic energy.
- For muons in 12N, k_eff R is approximately 0.5 or larger, making the Fermi function inaccurate by about 10%.
- For the 0+ multipole in 208Bi with electrons at low energies, the Fermi function gives values about 50% larger than the DWBA cross section when only the vector charge is included.
- For atmospheric neutrino scattering on 54Fe at 100 MeV outgoing lepton kinetic energy, the muon-neutrino event rate is 231 with Coulomb distortion versus 165 without; the electron-neutrino rate is 134 with versus 104 without; the muon-to-electron ratio changes from 1.65 with Coulomb to 1.54 without.
- At 200 MeV in 54Fe, the muon-to-electron ratio is 1.72 with Coulomb and 1.70 without; at 300 MeV, both ratios are 1.81.

## Context

This work extends the effective-momentum approximation, previously derived for electron scattering by Yennie, Lenz, and Knoll, to charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering where the outgoing lepton can be a muon. The paper modifies the EMA to account for the muon mass, which was not needed in the original electron-scattering formulation. The results enable simpler calculations of total cross sections for neutrino experiments such as the LAMPF measurements on 12C (Kolbe, 1995), the iodine solar-neutrino detector calibration (Lande, 1993), and atmospheric neutrino scattering on iron in the SOUDAN detector, where the author shows that Coulomb effects change individual event rates significantly but leave the muon-to-electron ratio nearly unchanged.
