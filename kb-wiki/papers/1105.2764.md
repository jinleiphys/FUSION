# The Role of the Roper in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We include the Roper excitation of the nucleon in a version of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory recently developed for energies around the delta resonance. We find significant improvement in the $P_{11}$ channel.

# The Role of the Roper in Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
Including the Roper resonance explicitly in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) improves the description of the $P_{11}$ channel in elastic $\pi N$ scattering at energies around the delta resonance, but only when the Roper pole diagram is promoted over its crossed counterpart because the Roper and delta are not widely separated in energy. The improvement is significant at the first non-vanishing order, but the extraction of Roper parameters is difficult due to near-cancellation between repulsive and attractive contributions that makes the $P_{11}$ phase shifts very small over a wide kinematic window.

## Method
The authors extend the chiral Lagrangian of Ref. [us] (Long and van Kolck, 2008) by introducing a heavy-Roper field with the same quantum numbers as the nucleon, constructing interactions according to a chiral index that counts derivatives, pion masses, and the delta and Roper mass splittings. They compute the $\pi N$ scattering amplitude in the $P_{11}$ channel at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO, $\mathcal{O}(Q)$) and next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO, $\mathcal{O}(Q^2/\mqcd)$) using tree diagrams from the lowest-index Lagrangian and its first correction, then fit the Roper mass splitting $\rho$, the nucleon-Roper coupling $g'_A$, and a short-range constant $B$ to the George Washington phase-shift analysis for center-of-mass energies from 1250 to 1300 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Roper pole position: $m_R - m_N - i\Gamma_R/2 \simeq (420 - 80i)$ MeV, with $\rho \equiv m_R - m_N \simeq 4.5 f_\pi$ where $f_\pi \simeq 92$ MeV.
- Delta pole position: $m_\Delta - m_N - i\Gamma_\Delta/2 \simeq (270 - 50i)$ MeV, with $\delta \equiv m_\Delta - m_N \simeq 3 f_\pi$.
- At N$^3$LO, the best-fit values are $g'_A = 0.32^{+0.14}_{-0.18}$, $\rho = 470^{+250}_{-40}$ MeV, and $B = -2.3^{+0.7}_{-0.7}$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The N$^3$LO scattering volume is $a_{P_{11}} = -0.081 \, m_\pi^{-3}$, compared to $a_{P_{11}} = -(0.0799 \pm 0.0016) m_\pi^{-3}$ from a low-energy phase-shift analysis (Matsinos, year not given).
- The Roper width scales as $\Gamma_R \sim \Gamma_\Delta \rho^3/2\delta^3$, consistent with ChPT widths scaling as $Q^3/\mqcd^2$.

## Context
This work extends the heavy-baryon ChPT framework of Long and van Kolck (2008) for energies around the delta resonance by adding the Roper, which had previously been considered only in unitarized approaches (such as the $N/D$ method or Bethe-Salpeter equation) or in phenomenological hadronic models (K-model series, year not given). The Roper's role in the $P_{11}$ channel had been identified phenomenologically, but this is the first systematic inclusion within a manifest power-counting EFT. The results enable future extension to energies around the Roper pole itself, though the authors note that convergence may be slow due to the proximity of other resonances ($\sigma$, $N^\star(1520)$, $N^\star(1535)$) at that scale.
