# Effective Field Theory for Few-Boson Systems

> We study universal bosonic few-body systems within the framework of effective field theory at leading order (LO). We calculate binding energies of systems of up to six particles and the atom-dimer scattering length. Convergence to the limit of zero-range two- and three-body interactions is shown, indicating that no additional few-body interactions need to be introduced at LO. Generalizations of the Tjon line are constructed, showing correlations between few-body binding energies and the binding energy of the trimer, for a given dimer energy. As a specific example, we implement our theory for 4He atomic systems, and show that the results are in surprisingly good agreement with those of sophisticated 4He-4He potentials. Potential implications for the convergence of the EFT expansion are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for bosonic few-body systems with a large two-body scattering length, effective field theory at leading order (LO) requires no additional few-body interactions beyond two- and three-body contact forces for systems of up to six particles, with the caveat that this conclusion holds within the zero-range limit and for the specific regularization scheme employed.

## Method

The authors solved the N-body Schrodinger equation for N=2 to 6 bosons using the stochastic variational method (SVM) with a correlated Gaussian basis, employing a local Gaussian regulator to regularize the contact interactions. The two-body low-energy constant was fixed by reproducing the dimer binding energy, and the three-body low-energy constant was fixed by reproducing the excited trimer binding energy, with calculations performed at various cutoff values and extrapolated to the zero-range limit.

## Key numbers

- For the LM2M2 potential: dimer binding energy B2 = 1.3094 mK, excited trimer binding energy B3* = 2.2779 mK, ground trimer binding energy B3 = 126.50 mK, ground tetramer binding energy B4 = 559.22 mK
- For the PCKLJS potential: dimer binding energy B2 = 1.6154 mK, excited trimer binding energy B3* = 2.6502 mK, ground trimer binding energy B3 = 131.84 mK, ground tetramer binding energy B4 = 573.90 mK
- The asymptotic value of the dimensionless two-body low-energy constant C(0)_infinity = -2.379, with expansion coefficients alpha = 2.241 and beta = 1.456
- The induced effective range scales as r2(Lambda) = gamma/Lambda with gamma = 2.869
- Ground-state energies grow to about 16 times the trimer energy at N=6, with agreement with potential models within about 15%

## Context

This work extends previous LO effective field theory calculations for three- and four-boson systems (Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck 1999; Platter, Hammer, Meissner 2004) to systems of five and six bosons, demonstrating that no higher-body forces are needed at LO. The results generalize the Tjon line correlations between few-body binding energies and the trimer binding energy, previously constructed from phenomenological potentials (Nakaichi-Maeda, Lim, Akaishi, Tanaka 1979; Lim, Nakaichi-Maeda, Akaishi, Tanaka 1980), and provide a baseline for future higher-order calculations and experimental comparisons with 4He atomic systems.
