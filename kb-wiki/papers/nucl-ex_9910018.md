# Energy shifted level densities in the rare earth region

> The density of accessible levels at low spin in the (3-He,alpha gamma) reaction has been extracted for the 161,162-Dy and 171,172-Yb nuclei. The energy shift between the level densities of the even-odd and even-even isotopes is measured as a function of excitation energy. The results are compared with predictions from various semi-empirical models. The energy shift procedure works well for excitation energies between 3.5 and 7 MeV in the even-even nucleus, provided that a proper level density function is used. The experimental energy shift is close to the pairing gap parameter Delta.

# Energy shifted level densities in the rare earth region

## Key claim

The energy shift between the level densities of even-odd and even-even isotopes in the rare earth region is close to the pairing gap parameter Delta, but this agreement holds only for excitation energies between 3.5 and 7 MeV in the even-even nucleus, provided a proper level density function is used; below 3.5 MeV, nuclear structure effects prevent a simple energy shift procedure.

## Method

Level densities for 161,162Dy and 171,172Yb were extracted from gamma-ray spectra measured in coincidence with alpha particles from the (3He,alpha gamma) reaction at 45 MeV beam energy, using the CACTUS multidetector array at the Oslo Cyclotron Laboratory. The first-generation gamma-ray matrix was factorized according to the Brink-Axel hypothesis into a product of level density and gamma-energy dependent function, with absolute normalization achieved by matching to known low-lying levels and neutron resonance spacings at the neutron binding energy. The energy shift delta(E) between even-odd and even-even isotopes was then determined as the shift required to make the even-odd level density match the even-even level density as a function of excitation energy.

## Key numbers

* Energy shift delta between 161Dy and 162Dy: 1.13(7) MeV in the 3.5-7 MeV excitation region
* Energy shift delta between 171Yb and 172Yb: 0.84(10) MeV in the 3.5-7 MeV excitation region
* Effective pairing gap Delta_eff for 162Dy from separation energies: 1.05 MeV
* Effective pairing gap Delta_eff for 172Yb from separation energies: 0.93 MeV
* Empirical pairing gap from formula Delta = 12 A^{-1/2} MeV: 0.94 MeV for 162Dy, 0.91 MeV for 172Yb
* Level densities for odd-mass isotopes (161Dy, 171Yb) are about five times higher than for neighboring even-even isotopes (162Dy, 172Yb)
* The energy shift procedure works well between 3.5 and 7 MeV excitation energy in the even-even nucleus

## Context

This work extends the Oslo group's method for extracting level densities from gamma-ray spectra (Guttormsen et al., 1995; Schiller et al., 1999) to test the common assumption that neighboring isotopes share the same level density after an energy shift. The results contradict the two-component level density formula of Gilbert and Cameron (1965), which gives shifts deviating by 0.3-0.5 MeV from the experimental pairing gap, while the formalism of von Egidy et al. (1988) agrees within 0.2 MeV. The findings enable revision of semi-empirical level density formulae for the rare earth region and suggest that a refined two-component formula with constant energy shifts between neighboring isotopes could provide better analytical descriptions for future nuclear structure and reaction calculations.
