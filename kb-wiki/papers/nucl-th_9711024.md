# The nucleon Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule within a relativistic constituent quark model

> The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule for the nucleon is investigated within a relativistic constituent quark model formulated on the light-front. The contribution of the N - Delta(1232) transition is explicitly evaluated using different forms for the baryon wave functions and adopting a one-body relativistic current for the constituent quarks. It is shown that the N - Delta(1232) contribution to the sum rule is sharply sensitive to the introduction of anomalous magnetic moments for the constituent quarks, at variance with the findings of non-relativistic and relativized quark models. The experimental value of the isovector-isovector part of the sum rule is almost totally reproduced by the N - Delta(1232) contribution, when the values of the quark anomalous magnetic moments are fixed by fitting the experimental nucleon magnetic moments. Our results are almost independent of the adopted form of the baryon wave functions and only slightly sensitive to the violation of the angular condition caused by the use of a one-body current. The calculated average slope of the generalized sum rule around the photon point results to be only slightly negative at variance with recent predictions of relativized quark models.

## Key claim

The N-Delta(1232) transition almost totally reproduces the experimental isovector-isovector part of the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule, but only when the constituent quark anomalous magnetic moments are included and fixed by fitting the experimental nucleon magnetic moments; this sharply contrasts with non-relativistic and relativized quark models, which fail to reproduce the sum rule.

## Method

The authors evaluated the N-Delta(1232) transition contribution to the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule using a relativistic constituent quark model formulated on the light front. They adopted four different forms for the baryon wave functions (models A-D, based on the Capstick-Isgur Hamiltonian and variants) and used a one-body relativistic electromagnetic current for the constituent quarks, including both Dirac and Pauli form factors. The quark anomalous magnetic moments were fixed by fitting the experimental nucleon magnetic moments, and the helicity amplitudes for the N-Delta(1232) transition were calculated at the photon point and at finite Q^2.

## Key numbers

* The experimental nucleon magnetic moments used for fitting are mu_p^exp = 2.793 and mu_n^exp = -1.913 (in nuclear magnetons).
* The experimental anomalous magnetic moments are kappa_p^exp = 1.793 and kappa_n^exp = -1.913.
* The calculated ratio E1/M1 for the N-Delta(1232) transition in model A ranges from 0.37% to -2.06% depending on the angular prescription used.
* The relativistic dilution factor <gamma_M> from Melosh rotations ranges from 0.48 (model A) to 0.75 (model D).
* The constituent quark mass is m = 0.22 GeV for models A-C and m = 0.33 GeV for model D.
* The N-Delta(1232) contribution to the DHG integral is sharply sensitive to quark anomalous magnetic moments, changing by up to a factor of approximately 2 in model A when kappa_q is introduced.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constituent quark model approach of Cardarelli et al. (1997) to the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule, contrasting with non-relativistic quark models (Drechsel and Giannini, 1993) and relativized quark models (Li, 1995) that fail to reproduce the sum rule. The results enable future comparisons with planned experiments at ELSA, MAMI, LEGS, GRAAL, and TJNAF, and suggest that the unexplained isoscalar-isovector part of the sum rule may arise from background processes involving constituent quark inelastic channels.
