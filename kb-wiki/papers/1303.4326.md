# Unambiguous Identification of the Second 2+ State in 12C and the Structure of the Hoyle State

> The second 2+ state of 12C, predicted over fifty years ago as an excitation of the Hoyle state, has been unambiguously identified using the 12C(g,a_0)8Be reaction. The alpha particles produced by the photodisintegration of 12C were detected using an Optical Time Projection Chamber (O-TPC). Data were collected at beam energies between 9.1 and 10.7 MeV using the intense nearly mono-energetic gamma-ray beams at the HIgS facility. The measured angular distributions determine the cross section and the E1-E2 relative phases as a function of energy leading to an unambiguous identification of the second 2+ state in 12C at 10.03(11) MeV, with a total width of 800(130) keV and a ground state gamma-decay width of 60(10) meV; B(E2: 2+ ---> gs) = 0.73(13) e2fm4 [or 0.45(8) W.u.]. The Hoyle state and its rotational 2+ state that are more extended than the ground state of 12C presents a challenge and constraints for models attempting to reveal the nature of three alpha particle states in 12C. Specifically it challenges the ab-initio Lattice Effective Field Theory (L-EFT) calculations that predict similar r.m.s. radii for the ground state and the Hoyle state.

## Key claim

The second 2+ state in 12C, long sought as a rotational excitation of the Hoyle state, is unambiguously identified at 10.03(11) MeV using the 12C(gamma,alpha0)8Be reaction, with the caveat that the identification relies on the measured E1-E2 relative phase agreeing with a two-resonance model and that the resonance energy is slightly lower than the maximum of the calculated cross section due to energy-dependent widths.

## Method

The authors used nearly mono-energetic gamma-ray beams from the HIgammaS facility at energies between 9.1 and 10.7 MeV to photodisintegrate 12C via the 12C(gamma,alpha0)8Be reaction. The outgoing alpha particles were detected with an Optical Time Projection Chamber (O-TPC) operating at 100 torr with a CO2(80%) + N2(20%) gas mixture, achieving nearly 100% detection efficiency. Events were separated from background reactions (primarily 16O(gamma,alpha)12C) using line-shape analysis of time projection signals, and unbinned maximum likelihood fits of the measured angular distributions were used to extract E1 and E2 cross sections and their relative phase as a function of energy.

## Key numbers

- Second 2+ state in 12C at 10.03(11) MeV, with a total width of 800(130) keV
- Ground state gamma-decay width of 60(10) meV
- B(E2: 2+2 -> 0+1) = 0.73(13) e2fm4 [or 0.45(8) W.u.]
- The 2+ state lies 2.38(11) MeV above the Hoyle state (0+ at 7.654 MeV)
- The alpha-particle width exhausts 65(9)% of the Wigner limit
- Angular distributions measured at seven beam energies between 9.1 and 10.7 MeV, with energy spreads of 300-350 keV and on-target intensities of approximately 2 x 10^8 gamma/sec
- At 9.6 MeV: sigma(E2)/sigma = 0.97(+0.01/-0.02) with phase phi12 = 80 +/- 6 degrees
- At 10.7 MeV: sigma(E2)/sigma = 0.71(+0.04/-0.05) with phase phi12 = 132 +/- 5 degrees

## Context

This work resolves a fifty-year search for the second 2+ state in 12C, which had been observed in inelastic scattering reactions (Osaka group 2004, iThemba group 2009, Freer 2012) but not seen in beta-decay studies (Hyldegaard 2009, 2010) or 3He-induced reactions (Alcorta 2012). The measured B(E2) of 0.73(13) e2fm4 agrees with the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) model prediction of 0.46 e2fm4 but is smaller than the Lattice Effective Field Theory (L-EFT) prediction of 2(1) e2fm4. The result challenges ab-initio L-EFT calculations (Epelbaum 2012) that predict similar rms radii for the ground state and Hoyle state, and instead supports models where the Hoyle state is an extended object with an rms radius approximately sqrt(2) larger than the ground state, consistent with the U(7) model (Bijker and Iachello 2002) and inelastic scattering measurements (Danilov 2009).
