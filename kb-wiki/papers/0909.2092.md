# Couplings between dipole and quadrupole vibrations in tin isotopes

> We study the couplings between collective vibrations such as the isovector giant dipole and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances in tin isotopes in the framework of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory with a Skyrme energy density functional. These couplings are a source of anharmonicity in the multiphonon spectrum. In particular, the residual interaction is known to couple the isovector giant dipole resonance with the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance built on top of it, inducing a nonlinear evolution of the quadrupole moment after a dipole boost. This coupling also affects the dipole motion in a nucleus with a static or dynamical deformation induced by a quadrupole constraint or boost respectively. Three methods associated with these different manifestations of the coupling are proposed to extract the corresponding matrix elements of the residual interaction. Numerical applications of the different methods to 132Sn are in good agreement with each other. Finally, several tin isotopes are considered to investigate the role of isospin and mass number on this coupling. A simple 1/A dependence of the residual matrix elements is found with no noticeable contribution from the isospin. This result is interpreted within the Goldhaber-Teller model.

## Key claim

The residual interaction coupling the isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) in tin isotopes is extracted via three independent TDHF methods, yielding consistent matrix elements for 132Sn; the coupling strength follows a simple 1/A dependence with no noticeable isospin contribution, interpreted within the Goldhaber-Teller model.

## Method

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation was solved on a 3D spatial grid with the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional using the tdhf3d code. Three distinct methods were employed to extract the residual interaction matrix element v_Q coupling the GDR and GQR: (1) the quadratic response of the quadrupole moment after a dipole boost, (2) the linear response of the GDR frequency under a static quadrupole constraint, and (3) the coupling response function from a simultaneous dipole-plus-quadrupole boost. Calculations were performed for several tin isotopes to study mass and isospin dependence.

## Key numbers

- GDR energy in 132Sn: ω_D ≈ 15.2 MeV (from first minimum of dipole moment after boost)
- GDR energy in 120Sn: ω_D ≈ 15.3 MeV (compared to experimental peak 15.4 MeV)
- GQR energy in 132Sn: ω_Q ≈ 13.0 MeV (from quadrupole boost)
- GQR energy in 120Sn: ω_Q ≈ 13.3 MeV (compared to experimental value 13.24 ± 0.13 MeV)
- Dipole transition amplitude in 132Sn: q_D ≈ 6.98 fm
- Quadrupole transition amplitude in 132Sn: q_Q ≈ 61.4 fm²
- Residual interaction matrix element v_Q from quadratic response method: v_Q^(1) ≈ -0.61 MeV
- Residual interaction matrix element v_Q from static constraint method: v_Q^(2) ≈ -0.56 MeV
- Residual interaction matrix element v_Q from double-boost method: v_Q^(3) ≈ -0.68 MeV
- Static quadrupole deformation slope in 132Sn: (∂Q_Q⁰/∂λ)_(λ=0) ≈ -1260.4 fm⁴ MeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic investigations of multiphonon anharmonicity using TDHF and RPA with boson mapping techniques (Simenel 2003, Chomaz 2004, Faleiro 2003). The three complementary extraction methods provide cross-validated measurements of the GDR-GQR coupling, enabling systematic studies of how such couplings evolve across isotopic chains. The observed 1/A scaling without isospin dependence offers a simple predictive tool for modeling anharmonic effects in heavier nuclei and guides future experimental searches for multiphonon states in exotic tin isotopes near the neutron drip line (estimated around 176Sn with SLy4).
