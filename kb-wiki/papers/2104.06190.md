# Thermonuclear fusion and generalized Gamow penetrability factor in intense laser fields

> A theoretical study on thermonuclear fusion including deuterium-tritium (D-T) fusion and D-$^{3}$He fusion in intense laser fields has been shown in this article. With the laser fields expected to be available in the near future, some quantitative results for the laser-induced modifications to the cross-sections are given. It is reported that the cross-sections are more sensitive to the external laser fields at the lower energies. An explicit generalized form of the Gamow penetrability factor is given for the predictions of the laser-induced effects for some other similar nuclear processes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that intense laser fields at a peak intensity of 10^24 W/cm^2 can produce considerable modifications to thermonuclear fusion cross-sections for D-T and D-^3He reactions, but only under the condition that the quasistatic approximation applies (Keldysh parameter gamma = 0.068), meaning the laser field does not change appreciably during the tunneling time.

## Method

The author solves the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the relative motion of the two-nuclei system in a linearly polarized near-infrared laser field (800 nm), using a quasistatic approximation. The fusion cross-section in the laser field is calculated via the WKB method for the penetrability factor, with the laser interaction treated as a perturbation -q_eff * r * epsilon(t) * cos(theta) added to the Coulomb-plus-square-well nuclear potential. The astrophysical S-function from Bosch and Hale (1992) is used to separate the nuclear part of the reaction, and the relative change in cross-section Delta_sigma is computed as a function of incident energy, laser intensity, and angle theta relative to the laser polarization axis.

## Key numbers

- Laser-induced modification to D-T fusion cross-section at E = 3 keV: 79.1% (at 10^24 W/cm^2)
- Laser-induced modification to D-^3He fusion cross-section at E = 3 keV: 731.7% (at 10^24 W/cm^2)
- Laser-induced modification to D-T fusion cross-section at E = 10 keV: 3.2% (at 10^24 W/cm^2)
- Laser-induced modification to D-^3He fusion cross-section at E = 10 keV: 13.4% (at 10^24 W/cm^2)
- Keldysh parameter for both D-T and D-^3He fusion in 800 nm laser: gamma = 0.068
- Laser electric field strength at 10^24 W/cm^2 is comparable to Coulomb field strength from a nucleus at about 100 fm
- The generalized Gamow penetrability factor coefficients: B_G = pi * sqrt(2*mu) * q1 * q2 / hbar; C_G = 3*pi*sqrt(2*mu)*(q1*q2)^2*q_eff*epsilon*cos(theta)/(8*hbar); D_G = 35*pi*sqrt(2*mu)*(q1*q2)^3*(q_eff*epsilon*cos(theta))^2/(64*hbar)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of laser-induced effects on nuclear processes, including alpha decay (Keitel 2013, Misicu 2013, Delion 2017, Qi 2019, Palffy 2019, Qi 2020) and D-T fusion in the x-ray regime using Floquet scattering (Queisser and Schutzhold 2019) or Kramers-Henneberger approximation (Lv 2019). It addresses the near-infrared regime relevant to facilities like ELI and SULF, where prior methods were not applicable. The generalized Gamow penetrability factor provides an analytical tool to predict laser-induced modifications for other similar nuclear processes, enabling quantitative estimates for future experiments with intense laser facilities expected to reach 10^23-10^24 W/cm^2.
