# Neutron matter: The unitary limit and its far-reaching impact

> We explore low-density neutron matter and its behavior in proximity to the unitary limit. To that end, we construct unitary nucleon-nucleon potentials with infinite 1S0 neutron-neutron scattering lengths. We discuss the Berstch parameter in relation to results from ultra-cold atomic gases. Unitarity as a constraint for neutron matter and the symmetry energy has been discussed in the literature. We revisit some of those arguments and emphasize the relevance of keeping a firm link with low-energy nuclear physics for robust predictions of neutron-rich systems. Our predictions are obtained from realistic few-nucleon forces based on chiral effective field theory at N3LO.

# Neutron matter: The unitary limit and its far-reaching impact

## Key claim
Low-density neutron matter approaches universal behavior characteristic of a unitary Fermi gas, but only up to Fermi momenta where the $^1$S$_0$ channel dominates (approximately $k_F$ = 1.0 fm$^{-1}$); beyond this point, finite-range effects become non-negligible and the system departs from unitarity. The unitarity bound provides a lower limit on the neutron matter energy and, through the parabolic approximation, constraints on the symmetry energy and its slope $L$, though these constraints are qualitative and must be combined with other microscopic and experimental inputs.

## Method
The equation of state for neutron matter is computed via a non-perturbative summation of particle-particle ladders (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with continuous spectrum) using chiral effective field theory two-nucleon forces at N$^3$LO (with cutoff $\Lambda$ = 450 MeV) and three-nucleon forces up to N$^3$LO, expressed as density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions. To study the unitary limit, the author constructs ad hoc unitary nucleon-nucleon potentials by tuning the leading-order contact parameter in the $^1$S$_0$ channel to produce a scattering length of nearly $-10^4$ fm, and compares results from the chiral potential and the CD-Bonn potential. The superfluid singlet gap is calculated using the BCS equation solved via the Khodel method.

## Key numbers
- The neutron-neutron scattering length in the $^1$S$_0$ channel is $a_s$ = -18.9(4) fm, with effective range $r_e$ = 2.75(11) fm.
- The $^1$S$_0$ channel dominates up to approximately $k_F$ = 1.0 fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to a density of 0.074 fm$^{-3}$ (close to one-half of normal nuclear density, taken as 0.16 fm$^{-3}$).
- The difference between the original N$^3$LO potential and its unitary version in the energy per neutron is less than 1 MeV at these densities.
- The Bertsch parameter $\xi$ from ultra-cold atomic gases ranges from approximately 0.36 to 0.51; theoretical predictions for $\xi$ range from 0.3 to 0.7.
- For the symmetry energy slope $L$, using the unitarity constraint with $E_{sym,0}$ = 32 MeV yields the approximate bound $-18.7$ MeV $\le L \le 70.5$ MeV (though the negative lower limit is an artifact of approximations).
- Neutron matter displays universal behavior up to values of $k_F|a_s|$ approximately equal to 4, beyond which finite-range effects become non-negligible.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous chiral EFT results for the neutron matter equation of state and symmetry energy (Sammarruca 2021, 2022) by specifically investigating the unitary limit regime. It revisits the unitarity constraint argument proposed by Tews et al. (2017) for neutron matter and the symmetry energy, emphasizing the importance of maintaining a firm link with low-energy few-nucleon physics. The study also provides baseline BCS pairing gap results using chiral forces, building toward systematic calculations including medium effects beyond three-nucleon forces, and notes correlations between neutron matter and few-nucleon observables identified by Kievsky et al. (2017). The work enables more robust predictions for neutron-rich systems and neutron star crust physics, particularly for S-wave superfluidity relevant to pulsar glitches and neutron star cooling.
