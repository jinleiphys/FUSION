# Exposing the dressed quark's mass

> This snapshot of recent progress in hadron physics made in connection with QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations includes: a perspective on confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB); a pre'cis on the physics of in-hadron condensates; results on the hadron spectrum, including dressed-quark-core masses for the nucleon and Delta, their first radial excitations, and the parity-partners of these states; an illustration of the impact of DCSB on the electromagnetic pion form factor, thereby exemplifying how data can be used to chart the momentum-dependence of the dressed-quark mass function; and a prediction that F_1^{p,d}/F_1^{p,u} passes through zero at Q^2\approx 5m_N^2 owing to the presence of nonpointlike scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations in the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the momentum-dependent dressed-quark mass function, a signature of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB), can be mapped experimentally through hadron form factor measurements, and that this mass function is the dominant source of mass for visible matter, with the Higgs mechanism contributing less than 2% of a nucleon's mass. The authors hedge that while a contact-interaction model can describe static properties, it fails for momentum transfers above approximately 0.17 GeV², demonstrating that form factor data can distinguish between different interaction models.

## Method

The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) in a symmetry-preserving truncation scheme to compute meson and baryon spectra, using a contact-interaction DSE kernel that produces a momentum-independent dressed-quark mass. For baryons, they formulate a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation incorporating nonpointlike scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, with a static approximation for the quark exchange kernel. They also compute the electromagnetic pion form factor and flavour-separated nucleon Dirac form factors using a more sophisticated momentum-dependent mass function.

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark mass in the chiral limit: M = 0.40 GeV (from the contact-interaction model)
- Current-quark mass used: m = 8 MeV, producing a momentum-independent dressed-quark mass M = 0.41 GeV
- Pion mass from rainbow-ladder truncation: m_π = 0.141 GeV (experimental: 0.140 GeV)
- Rho meson mass: m_ρ = 0.798 GeV (experimental: 0.775 GeV)
- a_1 meson mass with spin-orbit repulsion (g_SO = 0.32): m_a1 = 1.243 GeV (experimental: 1.243 GeV)
- Dressed-quark-core mass of the nucleon: m_N = 1.05 GeV
- Dressed-quark-core mass of the Delta: m_Δ = 1.33 GeV (EBAC bare mass: 1.39 GeV)
- Dressed-quark-core mass of the first radial excitation of the nucleon: m_N* = 1.73 GeV (EBAC: 1.76 GeV)
- Dressed-quark-core mass of the Delta parity-partner (3/2-): m_Δ(3/2-) = 2.01 GeV (EBAC: 1.98 GeV)
- Diquark correlation size: r_qq ≈ 0.8 fm
- Confinement length-scale: 1/Λ_ir = 0.83 fm
- Prediction that F_1^{p,d}/F_1^{p,u} passes through zero at Q² ≈ 5 m_N²
- Threshold for perturbative QCD behaviour in meson form factors: Q² > 8 GeV²
- Threshold for perturbative QCD behaviour in baryon form factors: Q² > 18 GeV²

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of hadron structure by Bhagwat (2003, 2006) and lattice-QCD calculations by Bowman (2005), which established the momentum-dependent dressed-quark mass function. The contact-interaction model follows Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010) and is contrasted with the QCD-based DSE prediction for the pion form factor by Maris (2000), which successfully predicted the measured form factor by Volmer (2000). The nucleon form factor analysis builds on the comprehensive study by Cloet (2008) and provides a prediction testable at upgraded Jefferson Laboratory facilities, while the baryon spectrum results are compared with bare masses from the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) analysis by Suzuki (2009).
