# Nuclear Symmetry Energy and Neutron Skin Thickness of $^{208}Pb $ using a finite range effective interaction

> We use a finite range simple effective interaction to construct nuclear equations of state for the study of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. The EoSs provide good descriptions of the nuclear symmetry energy at a subsaturation density $ρ_c=0.11$ fm$^{-3}$ and at a density around two times the saturation density $ρ_0$. We obtain a correlation between the neutron skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb and the density slope parameter at the subsaturation density. A linear relation is obtained between the neutron skin thickness and the parameter $β^{\prime}=\frac{L(ρ_c)}{3E_s(ρ_0)}$, where $E_s(ρ_0)$ and $L(ρ_c)$ are respectively the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density and the density slope parameter at the subsaturation density.

## Key claim

The paper constructs nuclear equations of state using a finite-range simple effective interaction (SEI) that reproduce the nuclear symmetry energy at a subsaturation density of ρ_c = 0.11 fm⁻³ (constrained to 26.65 ± 0.20 MeV from binding energy differences of heavy isotope pairs) and at approximately twice saturation density, but the authors note that only the set with E_s(ρ_0) = 35 MeV passes through the region predicted by ASY-EOS data from heavy-ion collisions, while the other two sets cannot be ruled out based on other constraints.

## Method

The authors used a finite-range simple effective interaction (SEI) with Yukawa form factor to construct equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM). The interaction parameters were constrained by requiring the SNM EoS to have incompressibility K = 240 MeV, saturation density ρ_0 = 0.1658 fm⁻³, and binding energy e_0(ρ_0) = 923 MeV, while the PNM parameters were fixed by requiring the symmetry energy at subsaturation density ρ_c = 0.11 fm⁻³ to be E_s(ρ_c) = 26.65 ± 0.20 MeV for three different choices of E_s(ρ_0) (33, 34, and 35 MeV). The resulting EoSs were then used to calculate the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb and its correlation with the density slope parameter at subsaturation density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ_0 = 0.1658 fm⁻³; energy per nucleon in SNM at saturation e_0(ρ_0) = 923 MeV
- Subsaturation cross density ρ_c = 0.11 fm⁻³; symmetry energy at this density constrained to E_s(ρ_c) = 26.65 ± 0.20 MeV (from Zhang and Chen, 2013)
- Three parameter sets yield E_s(ρ_0) = 33, 34, and 35 MeV with corresponding slope parameters L(ρ_0) = 44.16, 55.05, and 66.0 MeV, respectively
- Symmetry energy at 2ρ_0 ranges from 38.65 MeV (Set I) to 50.90 MeV (Set III), within the constraint E_s(2ρ_0) = 46.9 ± 10.1 MeV from gravitational wave observations
- Density slope parameter at subsaturation density L(ρ_c) ranges from 46.69 MeV (Set I) to 55.63 MeV (Set III), within the extracted value L(ρ_c) = 47.3 ± 7.8 MeV from electric dipole polarisability (Zhang and Chen, 2014)
- Curvature parameter at saturation K_sym(ρ_0) ranges from -201.32 MeV (Set I) to -136.16 MeV (Set III); at subsaturation K_sym(ρ_c) ranges from -120.69 MeV to -82.80 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of using finite-range effective interactions to study nuclear symmetry energy, following earlier applications of the same SEI to nuclear mean fields (Behera, 1998; 2002; 2005) and temperature dependence of symmetry energy (Behera, 2009; 2011). The constraint on E_s(ρ_c) = 26.65 ± 0.20 MeV comes from binding energy differences of heavy isotope pairs (Zhang and Chen, 2013), while the high-density constraint E_s(2ρ_0) = 46.9 ± 10.1 MeV is derived from the tidal deformability measurement of GW170817 (Zhang, 2019; Li, 2019). The correlation between neutron skin thickness and the parameter β' = L(ρ_c)/[3E_s(ρ_0)] provides a linear relation that can be tested by future PREX-II measurements, which aim to reduce the uncertainty on the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb to 0.06 fm.
