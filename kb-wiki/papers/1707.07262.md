# Investigating different $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarizations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the chiral kinetic equations of motion, spin polarizations of various quarks, due to the magnetic field induced by spectator protons as well as the quark-antiquark vector interaction, are studied within a partonic transport approach. Although the magnetic field in QGP enhances the splitting of the spin polarizations of partons compared to the results under the magnetic field in vacuum, the spin polarizations of $s$ and $\bar s$ quarks are also sensitive to the quark-antiquark vector interaction, challenging that the different $Λ$ and $\bar Λ$ spin polarization is a good measure of the magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It is also found that there is no way to obtain the large splitting of the spin polarization between $Λ$ and $\bar Λ$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV with partonic dynamics.

## Key claim

The different spin polarizations of Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) hyperons are sensitive to both the magnetic field in the quark-gluon plasma and the quark-antiquark vector interaction, challenging the proposal that this polarization splitting is a clean measure of the magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV, the large observed splitting cannot be reproduced by partonic dynamics alone.

## Method

The authors used a partonic transport approach based on chiral kinetic equations of motion, with initial parton phase-space distributions from the AMPT/HIJING model for midcentral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=39\) and 7.7 GeV. The vector potentials included the real magnetic field from spectator protons (with a temperature-dependent electrical conductivity in the QGP) and the effective magnetic field from the flavor-singlet quark-antiquark vector interaction in a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model framework. Spin polarizations of \(u\), \(d\), \(s\) quarks and their antiquarks were computed from the chiral kinetic equations and related to \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) polarizations via a coalescence model.

## Key numbers

- The spin polarization splitting between \(s\) and \(\bar{s}\) quarks at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV, estimated from the thermal formula \(\langle\vec{P}\rangle = \hbar\vec{B}/(4T^2)\) using the initial magnetic field and \(T=0.2\) GeV, is about \(0.8\%\), much smaller than the experimentally observed splitting.
- The electrical conductivity of the QGP is taken as \(\sigma_{con}=0.0058\,(T/T_c)\) GeV, with critical temperature \(T_c\approx0.165\) GeV.
- The vector coupling constant \(G_V\) is set to 0 or 1.1 times the scalar coupling constant in the NJL model.
- The partonic phase ends at about \(t \sim 3.7\) fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=39\) GeV.
- The average impact parameter for midcentral (20-50%) Au+Au collisions is 8.87 fm.

## Context

This work extends studies of hyperon polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions that previously attributed polarization primarily to vorticity coupling (e.g., studies by Karpenko et al. (2017) and Sun et al. (2017)). It directly tests the proposal by Muller et al. (2018) that the \(\Lambda\)-\(\bar{\Lambda}\) polarization difference can serve as a magnetic field probe. The results show that the quark-antiquark vector interaction, whose strength is constrained by NJL model fits to vector meson masses and by elliptic flow data from Xu et al. (2014), introduces significant uncertainty. The inability to reproduce the large polarization splitting at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV suggests that hadronic phase effects, such as spin-orbit couplings in baryon-rich matter as proposed by Csernai et al. (2018), may be necessary to explain the experimental data.
