# Bridging a gap between continuum-QCD and ab initio predictions of hadron observables

> Within contemporary hadron physics there are two common methods for determining the momentum-dependence of the interaction between quarks: the top-down approach, which works toward an ab initio computation of the interaction via direct analysis of the gauge-sector gap equations; and the bottom-up scheme, which aims to infer the interaction by fitting data within a well-defined truncation of those equations in the matter sector that are relevant to bound-state properties. We unite these two approaches by demonstrating that the renormalisation-group-invariant running-interaction predicted by contemporary analyses of QCD's gauge sector coincides with that required in order to describe ground-state hadron observables using a nonperturbative truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations in the matter sector. This bridges a gap that had lain between nonperturbative continuum-QCD and the ab initio prediction of bound-state properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the renormalisation-group-invariant running interaction predicted by modern analyses of QCD's gauge sector (top-down approach) coincides with the interaction required to describe ground-state hadron observables using the DB truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (bottom-up approach), thereby bridging a gap between nonperturbative continuum-QCD and ab initio predictions of bound-state properties. The agreement holds specifically for the DB truncation, not for the simpler rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation, which overmagnifies the infrared interaction strength.

## Method

The authors compared the renormalisation-group-invariant (RGI) running interaction strength \(\hat d(k^2)\) obtained from combined DSE and lattice-QCD analyses of the gauge sector (gluon and ghost propagators) with the phenomenological interaction kernel \({\mathcal I}(k^2)=k^2{\cal G}(k^2)\) used in bottom-up DSE studies of hadron observables. The gauge-sector result was computed using the pinch technique and background field method to extract the universal subcomponent of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, while the bottom-up interaction was parametrized as a Gaussian plus perturbative running term with a single free parameter \(\varsigma_G\) fitted to the pion decay constant, using either the RL vertex (Eq. (RLvertex)) or the DB vertex (a more sophisticated dressed-quark-gluon vertex consistent with Ward-Green-Takahashi identities).

## Context

This work unites two previously separate approaches in continuum-QCD: the top-down analysis of gauge-sector gap equations (pioneered by Aguilar, Binosi, Papavassiliou and others around 2008-2013) and the bottom-up DSE phenomenology of hadron observables (developed by Roberts, Chang, and collaborators using the DB truncation from 2009 onward). The result enables genuinely ab initio predictions of hadron properties from continuum-QCD, and demonstrates that the simpler RL truncation (used extensively since the 1990s by Maris, Tandy, and others) produces an interaction that is too strong in the infrared because it suppresses dynamical chiral symmetry breaking effects in the vertex. The paper also connects the dynamically generated gluon mass-scale to confinement and fragmentation, arguing that Gribov copies have no measurable impact on Standard Model observables because far-infrared gluonic modes are dynamically screened.
