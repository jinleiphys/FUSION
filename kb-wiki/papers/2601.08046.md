# Insights into Meson and Baryon Structure using Continuum Schwinger Function Methods

> The bulk of visible mass is supposed to emerge from nonperturbative dynamics within quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Following years of development and refinement, continuum and lattice Schwinger function methods have recently joined in revealing the three pillars that support this emergent hadron mass (EHM); namely, a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge, and dressed-quarks with running masses that take constituent-like values at infrared momenta. One may argue that EHM and confinement are inextricably linked; and theory is now working to expose their manifold expressions in hadron observables and highlight the types of measurements that can be made in order to validate the paradigm. This contribution sketches these ideas via the unified explanation of pion and proton electromagnetic and gravitational form factors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that emergent hadron mass (EHM) accounts for approximately 94% of the proton mass, with the Higgs mechanism contributing only about 1.4%, and that this EHM paradigm is supported by three pillars: a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge, and dressed-quarks with running masses that take constituent-like values at infrared momenta.

## Method

The authors employ continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs), specifically symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, to compute electromagnetic and gravitational form factors for pions, kaons, and nucleons. For nucleons, the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation is solved using the same interaction kernel as for mesons, yielding parameter-free predictions for all elastic form factors. The analysis also uses the Schlessinger point method (SPM) for model-independent extraction of information from existing experimental data on proton form factor ratios.

## Key numbers

- Higgs boson contribution to proton mass: $\hat m_H = 2\hat m_u + \hat m_d \approx 13\,{\rm MeV} = 0.014\,m_N$
- Constructive interference (Higgs + other sources): $\hat m_\sigma = \hat \sigma_N - \hat m_H \approx (60-13)\,{\rm MeV} = 0.05\,m_N$
- EHM contribution: $\hat m_{\rm EHM} \approx 0.94\,m_N$
- Renormalization group invariant gluon mass scale: $\hat m = 0.43(1)\,$GeV
- Process-independent effective charge at infrared: $\hat\alpha(k^2=0)/\pi=0.97(4)$
- Pion charge radius predicted: $r_\pi=0.67\,$fm
- Predicted zero in proton electric form factor ratio at $Q^2 /{\rm GeV}^2= 8.86^{+1.93}_{-0.86}$
- Neutron electric form factor exceeds proton electric form factor at $Q^2/{\rm GeV}^2 = 4.66^{+0.18}_{-0.13}$
- Proton gravitational D-term: $D(0) = -3.11(1)$
- Mass radius ordering: $r_{\rm mass} = 0.81(5) r_{\rm ch} > r_{\rm mech} = 0.72(2) r_{\rm ch}$ where $r_{\rm ch}=0.887(3)\,$fm
- Gluon-to-quark contribution ratio at $\zeta=2\,$GeV: ${\mathpzc g}(Q^2)/{\mathpzc q}(Q^2) = 0.71(4)$

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger function method framework developed by Roberts and collaborators over decades, building on the foundational EHM concept from Cornwall (1981) and the pion form factor predictions of Lepage and Brodsky (1979-1980). The results unify meson and nucleon electromagnetic and gravitational form factors within a single framework, enabling direct comparison with lattice-QCD results from Ding (2023) and Hackett (2023). The predictions for proton form factor zeros and gravitational D-term are testable at Jefferson Laboratory and future electron-ion colliders, providing experimental validation pathways for the EHM paradigm.
