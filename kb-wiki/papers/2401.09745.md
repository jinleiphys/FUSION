# Impact of Limited Statistics on the Measured Hyper-Order Cumulants of Net-Proton Distributions in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Hyper-order cumulants $C_5/C_1$ and $C_6/C_2$ of net-baryon distributions are anticipated to offer crucial insights into the phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions. However, the accuracy of $C_5$ and $C_6$ is highly contingent on the fine shape of the distribution's tail, the detectable range of which could be essentially truncated by low statistics. In this paper, we use the fast Skellam-based simulations, as well as the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model, to assess the impact of limited statistics on the measurements of $C_5/C_1$ and $C_6/C_2$ of net-proton distributions at lower RHIC energies. Both ratios decrease from the unity baseline as we reduce statistics, and could even turn negative without a pertinent physics mechanism. By incorporating statistics akin to experimental data, we can replicate the net-proton $C_5/C_1$ and $C_6/C_2$ values comparable to the corresponding measurements for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 7.7, 11.5 and 14.5 GeV. Our findings underscore a caveat to the interpretation of the observed beam energy dependence of hyper-order cumulants.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the measured hyper-order cumulant ratios \(C_5/C_1\) and \(C_6/C_2\) of net-proton distributions at lower RHIC energies can be reproduced by statistical simulations with limited statistics alone, without invoking any phase-transition physics; the observed beam-energy dependence of these ratios may therefore be an artifact of insufficient event counts rather than a genuine signal of the QCD phase transition.

## Method

The authors used fast Skellam-based Monte Carlo simulations, where proton and antiproton multiplicities follow Poisson distributions with means matched to UrQMD model outputs for each RefMult3 bin, to generate subsamples of varying event counts. They also performed UrQMD (version 3.4) calculations for 0–40% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5\) GeV, applying the centrality bin width correction (CBWC) by computing cumulants within each RefMult3 bin before averaging. The net-proton kinematic acceptance was \(0.4 < p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|y| < 0.5\), and statistical errors were estimated using the Bootstrap method.

## Key numbers

*   For a Skellam distribution with \(\mu_1 = 31.217\) and \(\mu_2 = 0.96272\) (matching 0–5% central Au+Au at 11.5 GeV), the region \(\Delta N_p \ge 58\) has a cumulative probability of \(6.35 \times 10^{-6}\), corresponding to only 6–7 expected events in \(10^6\) events.
*   In UrQMD calculations for 0–40% Au+Au at 11.5 GeV, the ensemble-averaged \(\left<C_6/C_2\right>\) from subsamples of \(10^6\) events is significantly lower than that from subsamples of \(5\times10^6\) events, with both averages being negative.
*   In Skellam-based simulations for 0–40% Au+Au at 7.7, 11.5, and 14.5 GeV, \(\left<C_6/C_2\right>\) is significantly negative for subsamples with \(N_{\rm Evt} < 2\times10^7\) events, and \(\left<C_5/C_1\right>\) is negative for \(N_{\rm Evt} < 3\times10^6\) events.
*   The experimental datasets from RHIC BES-I have approximately \(3\times10^6\), \(6.6\times10^6\), and \(2\times10^7\) events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5\) GeV, respectively; Skellam-based simulations with these statistics yield \(C_5/C_1\) values between 0 and 1 and significantly negative \(C_6/C_2\) values, both consistent with STAR measurements within uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Chen et al. (2021, 2014, 2015, 2017) on the impact of limited statistics on cumulant measurements, and directly addresses the interpretation of STAR Collaboration results (2021, 2023) for net-proton \(C_5/C_1\) and \(C_6/C_2\) from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. The findings caution against attributing the observed beam-energy dependence of hyper-order cumulants to the QCD phase transition, as the same trends can arise purely from low statistics combined with the CBWC procedure. The results motivate the use of higher-statistics BES-II data and the development of realistic event generators that incorporate phase-transition physics to disentangle statistical artifacts from genuine signals.
