# Toward electrodynamics of unconventional phases of dilute nuclear matter

> The phase diagram of isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter may feature a number of unconventional phases, which include the translationally and rotationally symmetric, but isospin-asymmetrical BCS condensate, the current-carrying Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase, and the heterogeneous phase-separated phase. Because the Cooper pairs of the condensate carry a single unit of charge, these phases are charged superconductors and respond to electromagnetic gauge fields by either forming domains (type-I superconductivity) or quantum vortices (type-II superconductivity). We evaluate the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) parameter across the phase diagram and find that the unconventional phases of isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter are good type-II superconductors and should form Abrikosov vortices with twice the quantum of magnetic flux. We also find that the LOFF phase at the boundary of the transition to the type-I state, with the GL parameter being close to the critical value $1/\sqrt{2}$.

## Key claim

The unconventional phases of isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter are good type-II superconductors that should form Abrikosov vortices carrying twice the quantum of magnetic flux; however, the LOFF phase is at the boundary of the transition to the type-I state, with its Ginzburg-Landau parameter being close to the critical value \(1/\sqrt{2}\).

## Method

The authors evaluated the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) parameter \(\kappa = \lambda/\xi\) across the phase diagram of dilute nuclear matter by combining a microscopically computed coherence length \(\xi\) (obtained from the root-mean-square radius of the Cooper-pair wave function) with a newly computed London penetration depth \(\lambda\) derived from the macroscopic electrodynamics of superconductors. The penetration depth was calculated using the density of charge carriers (protons) and the nucleon effective mass, accounting for the fact that neutron-proton Cooper pairs carry a single unit of charge rather than two. The coherence length for the LOFF phase was rescaled from the weak-coupling regime value by the ratio of the BCS gap to the LOFF gap.

## Key numbers

- In the weak-coupling regime (WCR) at \(\log_{10}(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.5\), \(k_F = 0.91\) fm\(^{-1}\), and \(T = 0.5\) MeV: \(\xi_{\rm rms} = 3.17\) fm, \(\lambda = 18.5\) fm, \(\kappa_{\rm rms} = 5.8\).
- In the intermediate-coupling regime (ICR) at \(\log_{10}(\rho/\rho_0) = -1.5\), \(k_F = 0.42\) fm\(^{-1}\), and \(T = 0.5\) MeV: \(\xi_{\rm rms} = 0.94\) fm, \(\lambda = 30.5\) fm, \(\kappa_{\rm rms} = 32.4\).
- In the strong-coupling regime (SCR) at \(\log_{10}(\rho/\rho_0) = -2.5\), \(k_F = 0.20\) fm\(^{-1}\), and \(T = 0.2\) MeV: \(\xi_{\rm rms} = 0.57\) fm, \(\lambda = 50.5\) fm, \(\kappa_{\rm rms} = 88.2\).
- For the LOFF phase in the WCR at asymmetry \(\alpha = 0.49\): \(\Delta = 1.27\) MeV, \(Q = 0.4\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(\xi_{\rm rms}^{\rm LOFF} \simeq 23.4\) fm, \(\kappa_{\rm rms}^{\rm LOFF} = 0.8\).
- The quantum vortices in the neutron-proton condensate carry twice the quantum of magnetic flux: \(\phi^* = 2\phi_0 = 2\pi\hbar c/e\).

## Context

This work extends the phase diagram studies of Stein (2012, 2014, 2015) on the combined effects of BCS-BEC crossover and mismatched Fermi surfaces in nuclear and neutron matter, where the coherence length was extracted microscopically from Cooper-pair wave functions. The present contribution evaluates the electromagnetic response of these phases, complementing prior work on type-II versus type-I superconductivity of the S-wave proton condensate in neutron-star cores by Alford (2004), Armen Sedrakian (2005), and others. The results enable future microscopic studies of the response functions of neutron-proton condensates to electromagnetic fields using Green's functions.
