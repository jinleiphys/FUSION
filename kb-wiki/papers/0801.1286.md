# Hot QCD equations of state and response functions for quark-gluon plasma

> We study the response functions (chromo-electric susceptibilities) of quark-gluon plasma as a function of temperature in the presence of interactions. We consider two equations of state for hot QCD. The first one is fully perturbative, of $O(g^5)$ EOS and, and the second one which is $O[g^6\ln(1/g)+δ]$, incorporates some non-perturbative effects. Following a recent work (Physical Review {\bf C 76}, 054909(2007)), the interaction effects contained in the EOS are encapsulated in terms of effective chemical potentials($\tildeμ$) in the equilibrium distribution functions for the partons.By using them in another recent formulation of the response functions({\tt arXiv:0707.3697}), we determine explicitly the chromo-electric susceptibilities for QCD plasma. We find that it shows large deviations from the ideal behavior. We further study the modification in the heavy quark potential due to the medium effects. In particular, we determine the temperature dependence of the screening lengths by fixing the effective coupling constant $Q$ which appears in the transport equation by comparing the screening in the present formalism with exact lattice QCD results. Finally, we study the dissociation phenomena of heavy quarkonium states such as $c\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$, and determine the dissociation temperatures. Our results are in good agreement with recent lattice results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that interaction effects in hot QCD, encoded via effective fugacities in the equilibrium distribution functions, produce large deviations in chromo-electric susceptibilities from ideal behavior, and that the resulting heavy quark potential remains long-ranged (not short-ranged like in Maxwellian plasmas) even at high temperature, which may signal either the failure of these equations of state or a crossover rather than a phase transition.

## Method

The authors map interaction effects from two hot QCD equations of state (a fully perturbative O(g⁵) EOS and a non-perturbative O[g⁶ln(1/g)+δ] EOS) into effective chemical potentials (fugacities) for quasi-free quarks and gluons, following Chandra et al. (2007). They then insert these modified distribution functions into a semi-classical transport-theory formalism for chromo-electric response functions developed by Ranjan and Ravishankar (2007), obtaining the Abelian and non-Abelian permittivities. Finally, they use the static (ω=0) permittivity to compute the Debye-screened heavy quark potential and determine dissociation temperatures for charmonium and bottomonium by matching binding energy to thermal energy.

## Key numbers

* The Debye mass is given by m_D² = 8π Q² T² [N_f f₂(z_q) + g₂(z_g)], where f₂ and g₂ are fugacity-dependent integrals.
* For pure gauge theory, the dissociation temperature condition is (m_q Λ²)/(384π² Q⁴ T_c⁵) = (T_D/T_c)⁵ g₂²(z_g).
* For full QCD, the dissociation condition is (m_q Λ²)/(384π² Q⁴ T_c⁵) = (1/4)(T_D/T_c)⁵ [N_f f₂(z_q) + g₂(z_g)]².
* The parameter δ in EOSδ is chosen in the range 0.8 ≤ δ ≤ 1.2 to match lattice results of Karsch (2003) for T > 2T_c.
* The effective coupling Q is fixed by comparing the screening length from the formalism with exact lattice QCD results.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical transport approach of Ranjan and Ravishankar (2007) from ideal quarks and gluons to interacting QGP by incorporating the hot QCD equations of state of Arnold and Zhai (1994) and Kajantie et al. (2003). It also builds on the effective-fugacity method of Chandra et al. (2007) for adapting those EOS to quasi-particle distributions. The results enable future comparison with lattice QCD results for screening lengths and dissociation temperatures, and the authors note that a similar analysis with the lattice equation of state (Karsch, 2003) will be taken up separately.
