# Thermoelectric effects of an interacting hadron gas in the presence of an external magnetic field

> The hot and dense hadronic medium formed during the heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and Large Hadron Collider energies can show thermoelectric effects in the presence of temperature gradients and nonzero baryon chemical potential. In this article, we study the thermoelectric coefficients of an interacting hot and dense hadron gas using the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation. We discuss the thermoelectric properties within different frameworks of hardon resonance gas models. In the presence of an external magnetic field, the thermoelectric coefficients become anisotropic, which leads to Hall-like thermoelectric coefficients, namely Nernst coefficients, along with the magneto-Seebeck coefficients. For the first time, we also estimate the Thomson coefficient of the medium, which comes into the picture due to the temperature dependence of the Seebeck coefficient of the medium. In the context of studying the thermoelectric generator performance, we calculate the values of the thermoelectric figure of merit of the medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermoelectric coefficients of a hot and dense hadron gas become anisotropic in the presence of an external magnetic field, producing Hall-like Nernst coefficients and magneto-Seebeck coefficients, and that the Thomson coefficient can be estimated for the first time in such a medium; however, these results depend on the specific hadron resonance gas model used (ideal, excluded volume, van der Waals, or repulsive mean-field) and the relaxation time approximation.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation to compute thermoelectric coefficients for an interacting hadron gas. They employ four different hadron resonance gas frameworks: the ideal hadron resonance gas (IHRG) model, the excluded volume hadron resonance gas (EVHRG) model, the van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model, and the repulsive mean-field hadron resonance gas (RMFHRG) model, including all hadrons and resonances up to a mass cutoff of 2.6 GeV from the Particle Data Group. In the presence of an external magnetic field, the formalism is extended to include anisotropic transport, yielding the magneto-Seebeck and Nernst coefficients, and the Thomson coefficient is derived from the temperature dependence of the Seebeck coefficient.

## Key numbers

- The external magnetic field can reach up to \(m_\pi^2 \sim 10^{18}\) G in Au-Au collisions at RHIC and \(15 m_\pi^2\) in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.
- The IHRG model explains lattice QCD thermodynamic estimates up to \(T \approx 150\) MeV.
- The repulsive mean-field parameter for baryons is taken as \(K_B = 0.450\) GeV fm\(^3\) and for mesons as \(K_M = 0.050\) GeV fm\(^3\).
- The mass cutoff for hadrons and resonances included in the calculations is 2.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermoelectric transport in hadronic matter using the hadron resonance gas model, such as those by Das (2020, 2021) and Bhatt (2021), which explored Seebeck and Nernst coefficients in isotropic and anisotropic settings. It also builds on investigations of magneto-thermoelectric effects on conserved charge diffusion by Zhang (2024). The inclusion of the Thomson coefficient and the thermoelectric figure of merit enables future comparisons with lattice QCD results and provides a framework for understanding how thermoelectric properties influence the evolution dynamics of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
