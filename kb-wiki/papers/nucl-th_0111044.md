# Time dependence of critical behavior in multifragmentation

> We study signatures of critical behavior in microscopic simulations of small, highly excited Lennard-Jones drops. We focus our attention on the behavior of the system at the time of fragment formation (which takes place in phase space) and compare the results with the corresponding ones obtained at asymptotic times (experimentally accessible). The four critical exponents ($τ$,$β$, $σ$ and $γ$) found at fragmentation time have shown to be stable against time evolution, indicating that the asymptotic stage reflects accurately the physics at fragmentation time. Even though evidence of critical behavior arises from the calculations, we can not affirm that the system is performing a second order like phase transition.

## Key claim

The four critical exponents (tau, beta, sigma, gamma) extracted from microscopic simulations of Lennard-Jones drops at the time of fragment formation are stable against time evolution, meaning the experimentally accessible asymptotic stage accurately reflects the physics at fragmentation time. However, despite evidence of critical behavior, the authors cannot affirm that the system is performing a second-order-like phase transition.

## Method

The authors simulated 147-particle Lennard-Jones drops with a 6-12 potential and cut-off radius of 3 sigma, using the Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm (ECRA) to identify fragments in phase space at the time of fragment formation (tau_ff) and the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm to identify asymptotic fragments at tau_a = 600 t_0. They calculated the critical exponents tau, beta, sigma, and gamma from fragment mass distributions at both times, using scaling model fits and analyzing the second moment M_2, normalized variance of the maximum fragment (NVM), and chi-squared minimization to locate the critical multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Time of fragment formation ranged from tau_ff ~ 75 t_0 for E = -0.5 epsilon up to tau_ff ~ 10 t_0 for E = 3.7 epsilon.
- At tau_ff, NVM peaked at multiplicity m = 60 +/- 1 and M_2 peaked at m = 65 +/- 4; at tau_a, NVM peaked at m = 73 +/- 1 and M_2 peaked at m = 77 +/- 3.
- Critical multiplicity from chi-squared minimization: m_c = 59 +/- 2 for tau_ff and m_c = 76 +/- 2 for tau_a.
- Critical exponent tau = 2.18 +/- 0.03 at both tau_ff and tau_a.
- Critical exponent sigma = 0.51 +/- 0.15 at tau_ff and 0.64 +/- 0.18 at tau_a.
- Critical exponent beta = 0.29 +/- 0.08 at tau_ff and 0.28 +/- 0.13 at tau_a.
- Critical exponent gamma = 0.77 +/- 0.25 at tau_ff and 0.72 +/- 0.33 at tau_a.

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of fragment formation in Lennard-Jones drops by Strachan and Dorso (1997, 1999) and the ECRA algorithm of Dorso and Randrup (1993), and connects to experimental multifragmentation analyses by Elliott et al. (2000) and Bauer et al. (2002). The results show that asymptotic fragment distributions faithfully preserve the critical exponents from fragmentation time, enabling experimental extraction of these exponents from measured mass spectra, though the gamma exponent being lower than the 3D-Ising value suggests non-equilibrium effects may play a role at fragmentation time.
