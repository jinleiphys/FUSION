# Cross sections for inelastic meson-meson scattering

> We study two kinds of inelastic meson-meson scattering. The first kind is inelastic 2-to-2 meson-meson scattering that is governed by quark interchange as well as quark-antiquark annihilation and creation. Cross-section formulas are provided to get unpolarized cross sections for $πK \to ρK^\ast$ for $I=1/2$, $πK^\ast \to ρK$ for $I=1/2$, $πK^\ast \to ρK^\ast$ for $I=1/2$, and $ρK \to ρK^\ast$ for $I=1/2$. Near threshold, quark interchange dominates the reactions near the critical temperature. The second kind is 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering with the process that a quark in an initial meson and an antiquark in another initial meson annihilate into a gluon and subsequently the gluon is absorbed by the other antiquark or quark. The transition potential for the process is derived. Four Feynman diagrams at tree level contribute to the 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering. Starting from the $S$-matrix element, the isospin-averaged unpolarized cross section with transition amplitudes is derived. The cross sections for $ππ\to ρ$ and $πK \to K^*$ decrease with increasing temperature.

## Key claim

The paper provides cross-section formulas for two types of inelastic meson-meson scattering (2-to-2 and 2-to-1) within a relativistic constituent quark potential model, but the numerical results are specifically for isospin channels that have not been previously included in models for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the dominance of quark interchange over annihilation/creation mechanisms is claimed only near threshold and near the critical temperature.

## Method

For 2-to-2 reactions (πK → ρK*, πK* → ρK, πK* → ρK*, ρK → ρK* for I=1/2), the authors compute unpolarized cross sections by summing transition amplitudes from Born-order quark interchange (in both prior and post forms) and from quark-antiquark annihilation and creation, using mesonic quark-antiquark wave functions obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation with a temperature-dependent quark potential. For 2-to-1 reactions (ππ → ρ and πK → K*), they derive transition potentials for the process where a quark and an antiquark annihilate into a gluon which is subsequently absorbed by another quark or antiquark, using four tree-level Feynman diagrams and approximating the delta function for energy conservation with a Gaussian broadening.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used in the model: up quark 0.32 GeV, down quark 0.32 GeV, strange quark 0.5 GeV, charm quark 1.51 GeV (these reproduce experimental masses of π, ρ, K, K*, J/ψ, ψ', χc, D, D*, Ds, D*s mesons at zero temperature)
- The cross sections for ππ → ρ and πK → K* decrease with increasing temperature (no specific numerical cross section values or temperatures are given in the truncated text)
- The isospin-averaged unpolarized cross section for 2-to-1 reactions is given by σ^un = (2I_H+1)/[(2I_A+1)(2I_B+1)] σ^unpol, where I_A, I_B, I_H are the isospins of the initial and final mesons

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elastic meson-meson scattering via quark interchange by Barnes et al. (1997) and inelastic scattering via quark-antiquark annihilation and creation by Xu et al. (2015) to new isospin channels (I=1/2 for πK → ρK*, πK* → ρK, πK* → ρK*, ρK → ρK*) that were not previously included in models for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The 2-to-1 reactions (ππ → ρ and πK → K*) are compared to experimental data on ππ → ρ from Froggatt et al. (1977) and an estimate by Ko (1985) for πK → K* in vacuum, and the temperature dependence of these cross sections is studied for application in hadronic matter.
