# Jet Charge: A Flavor Prism for Spin Asymmetries at the EIC

> We propose the jet charge observable as a novel probe of flavor structure in the nucleon spin program at the Electron Ion Collider (EIC). We show that jet charge measurements can substantially enhance the sensitivity of spin asymmetries to different partonic flavors in the nucleon. This sensitivity can be further improved by constructing the jet charge using only a subset of hadron species (pions or kaons) in the jet. As an example, we use the Sivers asymmetry in back-to-back electron-jet production at the EIC to show that the jet charge can be a unique tool in constraining the Sivers function for different partonic flavors.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the jet charge observable in back-to-back electron-jet production at the EIC can substantially enhance flavor sensitivity for both unpolarized TMDs and the Sivers function, but this enhancement is conditional on selecting appropriate jet charge bins and, optionally, restricting the hadron species used to construct the jet charge.

## Method

The authors develop a factorization framework for the process \(e + p \to e + \text{jet} + X\) in the back-to-back region where the electron-jet transverse momentum imbalance \(q_T\) is small. They replace the standard jet function \(\mathcal{J}_i\) with a jet-charge-dependent function \(\mathcal{G}_i(Q_\kappa, p_T R, \mu)\) and define the zero-th moment \(r_{i,\text{bin}}\) as the fractional contribution of parton flavor \(i\) in a given jet charge bin. Using Pythia8 simulations to determine the bin fractions \(r_{i,\text{bin}}\) for different quark flavors, they then compute the unpolarized cross section and the Sivers asymmetry \(A_{UT}\) in three jet charge bins (negative, neutral, positive) for EIC kinematics at \(\sqrt{s}=105\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* The jet charge parameter \(\kappa = 0.3\) is used as the default value, based on prior findings that this value gives better separation of peaks for different quark flavors.
* The EIC center-of-mass energy is \(\sqrt{s} = 105\) GeV, with event selection cuts: \(Q^2 \ge 10\) GeV\(^2\), \(0.1 \le y \le 0.9\), \(15\) GeV \(\le p_T^e \le 20\) GeV, \(q_T \le 2.5\) GeV, and \(10\) GeV \(\le p_T \le 25\) GeV.
* The jet radius is \(R = 1\) and the jet charge bins are defined as: negative bin \(Q_\kappa \le -0.25\), neutral bin \(|Q_\kappa| < 0.25\), and positive bin \(Q_\kappa \ge 0.25\).
* For the \(u\)-quark, the bin fractions from Pythia8 are \(r_{u,+} = 0.52\), \(r_{u,-} = 0.15\), \(r_{u,0} = 0.33\); for the \(d\)-quark, \(r_{d,+} = 0.15\), \(r_{d,-} = 0.52\), \(r_{d,0} = 0.33\).
* A jet charge based only on charged kaons can increase the negative charge bin fractions of the \(s\)-quarks by a factor of \(\mathcal{O}(5)\) relative to the \(u\) or \(d\) quark fractions.

## Context

This work extends the use of the jet charge observable, previously studied at the LHC (Aad 2015, Sirunyan 2017) and explored at RHIC (Aschenauer 2015), to the spin program at the EIC. It builds on recent factorization frameworks for jet production at the EIC (Liu 2018, Arratia 2020, Liu 2020) and addresses the challenge of flavor separation in jet-based spin asymmetries, which had not been tackled in the literature. The proposed method complements conventional flavor tagging via semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS) and enables future global analyses that combine jet charge measurements from \(ep\), \(pp\), and \(e^+e^-\) collisions to constrain flavor-dependent TMDs and Sivers functions.
