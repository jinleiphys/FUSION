# Implementing PCAC in Nonperturbative Models of Pion Production

> Traditional few-body descriptions of pion production use integral equations to sum the strong interactions nonperturbatively. Although much physics is thereby included, there has not been a practical way of incorporating the constraints of chiral symmetry into such approaches. Thus the traditional few-body descriptions fail to reflect the underlying theory of strong interactions, QCD, which is largely chirally symmetric. In addition, the lack of chiral symmetry in the few-body approaches means that their predictions of pion production are in principle not consistent with the partial conservation of axial current (PCAC), a fact that has especially large consequences at low energies. We discuss how the recent introduction of the ``gauging of equations method'' can be used to include PCAC into traditional few-body descriptions and thereby solve this long standing problem

## Key claim

The authors show that the "gauging of equations method," previously developed for electromagnetic currents, can be applied to incorporate partial conservation of axial current (PCAC) into traditional nonperturbative few-body models of pion production, thereby solving the long-standing problem that such models were inconsistent with chiral symmetry and QCD.

## Method

The authors start from a traditional relativistic four-dimensional integral-equation description of the πNN system (with amplitudes for NN→πNN, NN→NN, and πNN→πNN) that lacks chiral symmetry. They replace the three-component pion field with a four-component field (pion plus sigma) to achieve linear chiral transformations, then couple an external axial vector field to every term in the integral equations using the gauging of equations method. The resulting axial transition current for NN→NN is constructed from the gauged integral equations plus external-leg contributions, and its residue at the pion pole yields the pion production amplitude that obeys exact PCAC.

## Key numbers

* The axial Ward-Takahashi identity for the nucleon vertex function includes the pion decay constant fπ and the pion mass mπ: qμ Γi^{aμ}(k,p) = i[di^{-1}(k)γ5 + γ5 di^{-1}(p)]t^a - i fπ mπ^2 fi^a(k,p) dπ(q).
* The PCAC relation for the two-nucleon axial current j^{aμ}_{NN} involves the pion production amplitude T_{N0}^a multiplied by fπ mπ^2 and the pion propagator dπ(q): qμ j^{aμ}_{NN} = ... - i fπ mπ^2 T_{N0}^a(k1k2,p1p2) dπ(q).

## Context

This work extends the "gauging of equations method" introduced by the same authors (Blankleider and Kvinikhidze, 1999) for electromagnetic interactions to the axial-vector case, solving a problem that traditional few-body approaches (e.g., the relativistic πNN model of Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1999) could not address. The method enables the construction of nonperturbative few-body models that respect chiral symmetry and PCAC, which is essential for low-energy pion production where chiral low-energy theorems apply, and provides a practical closed-form expression (T^μ = (1+T G_t) V^μ (1+G_t T) + T G_t^μ T) that can be applied to any kernel, including a simple one-pion-exchange potential.
