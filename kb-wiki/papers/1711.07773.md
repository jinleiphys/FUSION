# Principal component analysis of the nonlinear coupling of harmonic modes in heavy-ion collisions

> The principal component analysis of flow correlations in heavy-ion collisions is studied. The correlation matrix of harmonic flow is generalized to correlations involving several different flow vectors. The method can be applied to study the nonlinear coupling between different harmonic modes in a double differential way in transverse momentum or pseudorapidity. The procedure is illustrated with results from the hydrodynamic model applied to Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2760$GeV. Three examples of generalized correlations matrices in transverse momentum are constructed corresponding to the coupling of $v_2^2$ and $v_4$, of $v_2v_3$ and $v_5$, or of $v_2^3$, $v_3^3$, and $v_6$. The principal component decomposition is applied to the correlation matrices and the dominant modes are calculated.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that principal component analysis (PCA) can be extended to generalized correlation matrices that couple different harmonic flow modes at two different transverse momenta, providing a unified framework to study both mode mixing and factorization breaking. The method is illustrated with hydrodynamic model results, but the author notes that extensive high-statistics simulations for different initial conditions and viscosities are beyond the scope of this paper.

## Method

The author constructs generalized correlation matrices that combine different harmonic flow vectors (e.g., \(v_2^2\) with \(v_4\), \(v_2v_3\) with \(v_5\), or \(v_2^3\), \(v_3^2\), and \(v_6\)) at two different transverse momentum bins, using the cumulative probability distribution in transverse momentum to ensure uniform statistical uncertainties. Principal component decomposition is then applied to these matrices, yielding eigenvectors that have components in each harmonic sector and show momentum dependence. The calculations use a 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from a Glauber Monte Carlo model with quark degrees of freedom, with particles emitted statistically from the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

* Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2760\) GeV are simulated for centralities \(0-5\%\) and \(30-40\%\).
* Transverse momentum range used is \(0.3\) GeV \(< p < 3\) GeV, divided into 6 bins (or 4 bins for the \(v_2^3\), \(v_3^2\), \(v_6\) correlation), with the last bin approximately \([1.2, 3.0]\) GeV having an average value of \(1.66\) GeV for the centralities studied.
* The correlation matrix \(c_{2;3|3:2|6}\) has dimensions \(12\times 12\), with three sectors \(v_2^3\), \(v_3^2\), and \(v_6\) and 4 momentum bins.

## Context

This work extends the principal component analysis of flow correlations introduced by Bhalerao (2014) from single harmonic modes to coupled harmonic modes, building on studies of nonlinear coupling between flow harmonics by Teaney (2012), Bhalerao (2011), Yan (2015), and others. The method enables a double differential study in transverse momentum or pseudorapidity that could identify sources of mode mixing beyond collective flow, and provides a framework for experimental measurements to constrain both the initial state and the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma.
