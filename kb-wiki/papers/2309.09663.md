# Probe nuclear structure using the anisotropic flow at the Large Hadron Collider

> Recent studies have shown that the shape and radial profile of the colliding nuclei have strong influences on the initial condition of the heavy ion collisions and the subsequent development of the anisotropic flow. Using A Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) model, we investigated the impact of nuclear quadrupole deformation $β_2$ and nuclear diffuseness $a_0$ of $^{129}$Xe on various of flow observables in Xe--Xe collisions at $\sqrtnn =$ 5.44 TeV. We found that $β_2$ has a strong influence on central collisions while $a_0$ mostly influences the mid-central collisions. The relative change of flow observables induced by a change in $β_2$ and $a_0$ are also found to be insensitive to the values of parameters controlling the strength of the interaction among final state particles. Our study demonstrates the potential for constraining the initial condition of heavy ion collisions using future system scans at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ of ¹²⁹Xe strongly influences anisotropic flow observables in central Xe–Xe collisions at √sNN = 5.44 TeV, while the nuclear diffuseness a₀ primarily affects mid-central collisions; however, the study is limited by large statistical uncertainties that prevent firm conclusions on sensitivity to the triaxial parameter γ or to higher-order cumulants v₂{6} and v₂{8}.

## Method

The authors used the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model with string melting to simulate Xe–Xe collisions at √sNN = 5.44 TeV, parameterizing the ¹²⁹Xe nucleon density with a Woods-Saxon distribution. They varied β₂ (0, 0.18), γ (0°, 27°, 60°), a₀ (0.492 fm, 0.57 fm), and the parton-scattering cross-section σ (3.0 mb, 6.0 mb) across six parameter sets, then computed flow coefficients vₙ{2}, vₙ{4}, vₙ{6}, vₙ{8}, nonlinear flow modes v₄,₂₂, v₅,₃₂, χ₄,₂₂, χ₅,₃₂, ρ₄,₂₂, ρ₅,₃₂, and normalized symmetric cumulants NSC(3,2) and NSC(4,2) using multi-particle correlation methods.

## Key numbers

- v₂{2} increases significantly in ultra-central collisions (0–0.2% centrality) when β₂ changes from 0 to 0.18, while v₃{2} shows negligible differences with β₂ variation
- v₂{4} exhibits a slight increase when β₂ changes from 0 to 0.18, but with sizable statistical uncertainties
- v₄,₂₂ shows approximately 20% reductions with β₂ = 0 in the ultra-central collision region
- ρ₄,₂₂ shows a distinct drop with β₂ = 0 in ultra-central collisions, while χ₄,₂₂ stays unchanged with different nuclear structure configurations
- NSC(3,2) shows a tiny and insignificant increase when β₂ decreases from 0.18 to 0
- Calculations with larger a₀ (0.57 fm) lead to smaller v₂ results in semi-central collisions compared to a₀ = 0.492 fm
- The ratios v₂{6}/v₂{4} and v₂{8}/v₂{4} are close to unity within uncertainties and unchanged when varying β₂ and γ

## Context

This work extends previous studies at RHIC energies (Jia 2022, Magdy 2022) that demonstrated sensitivity of flow observables to nuclear deformation in ⁹⁶Ru, ⁹⁶Zr, ²³⁸U, and ¹⁹⁷Au collisions, now applying similar methodology to ¹²⁹Xe at LHC energies. The study complements earlier ALICE measurements (2018, 2021) of vₙ{2} and ρ(vₙ², [pT]) in Xe–Xe collisions by systematically investigating more intricate flow observables based on multi-particle correlations. The results enable future experimental system scans at the LHC to constrain both nuclear quadrupole deformation and nuclear diffuseness, though the triaxial parameter γ and higher-order cumulants require higher-statistics studies.
