# Three-body resonances of $ααM$ clusters ($M=ϕ$, $J/ψ$, $η_c$) in $^{8}_{M}{\mathrm{Be}}$ nuclei

> Motivated by the recently obtained HAL QCD potentials for the $N$-$ϕ$, $N$-$J/ψ$, and $N$-$η_c$ interactions, we investigate the structure of the exotic nuclei $^{8}_ϕ{\text{Be}}$, $^{8}_{J/ψ}{\text{Be}}$, and $^{8}_{η_c}{\text{Be}}$ as $α+α+M$ three-body systems ($M$ denotes the meson). The bound and resonant states are calculated consistently using the Gaussian expansion method, with resonances identified via the complex scaling method. For the $αϕ$ and $α$-charmonium interactions, a folding potential is constructed based on the HAL QCD potentials and fitted to a Woods-Saxon form. We find that the $ϕ$ meson exhibits a strong ``glue-like" effect, binding the $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$, and $4^+_1$ resonant states of $^8$Be into stable states and significantly reducing the $α$-$α$ distance. In contrast, the interactions of $J/ψ$ and $η_c$ with the nucleus are weaker, forming only shallow bound states with the $0^+_1$ state of $^8$Be and even increasing the $α$-$α$ separation. Notably, our analysis predicts weakly bound $α$-$J/ψ$ states in the $^4S_{3/2}$ and $^2S_{1/2}$ channels, a result not reported in prior studies, which suggests that $^{8}_{J/ψ}{\text{Be}}$ may not be a Borromean nucleus. The sensitivity of the $^{8}_{M}{\mathrm{Be}}(4^+_1)$ state-transitioning from bound to resonant depending on the $α$-particle radius-highlights the subtle dynamics at play. These results provide a systematic theoretical comparison of how different vector mesons modify nuclear clustering, offering critical predictions for future experimental searches of such exotic hadron-nucleus systems.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the \(\phi\) meson can bind the \(0^+_1\), \(2^+_1\), and \(4^+_1\) resonant states of \(^8\)Be into stable states (a "glue-like" effect), while the \(J/\psi\) and \(\eta_c\) mesons form only shallow bound states with the \(0^+_1\) state; however, the \(^8_{\phi}{\mathrm{Be}}(4^+_1)\) state can transition from bound to resonant depending on the assumed \(\alpha\)-particle root-mean-square matter radius, indicating a sensitivity that the abstract does not detail.

## Method

The authors treated \(^8_M{\mathrm{Be}}\) (\(M=\phi, J/\psi, \eta_c\)) as an \(\alpha+\alpha+M\) three-body system and solved the Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM). Resonant states were identified via the complex scaling method (CSM). The \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) interaction was described by a phenomenological double-Gaussian Ali-Bodmer potential, while the \(\alpha\)-\(M\) potentials were constructed by folding the HAL QCD \(N\)-\(M\) potentials (for \(N\)-\(\phi\), \(N\)-\(J/\psi\), and \(N\)-\(\eta_c\)) into a Gaussian nucleon density distribution of the \(\alpha\) particle; for the \(\alpha\)-\(\phi\) interaction in the \(^2S_{1/2}\) channel, the folded potential was fitted to a Woods-Saxon form.

## Key numbers

*   The \(\phi\) meson binds the \(0^+_1\), \(2^+_1\), and \(4^+_1\) states of \(^8\)Be into stable states; for the \(^8_{\phi}{\mathrm{Be}}\) system using the spin-averaged potential with \(R_\alpha=1.70\) fm, the calculated energies are \(-14.1\) MeV (\(0^+_1\)), \(-9.92\) MeV (\(2^+_1\)), and \(-6.12\) MeV (\(4^+_1\)).
*   The \(^8_{J/\psi}{\mathrm{Be}}\) and \(^8_{\eta_c}{\mathrm{Be}}\) systems form only shallow bound states with the \(0^+_1\) state of \(^8\)Be, and the \(J/\psi\) and \(\eta_c\) mesons increase the \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) separation, in contrast to the \(\phi\) meson which reduces it.
*   The \(\alpha\)-\(J/\psi\) interaction predicts weakly bound states in the \(^4S_{3/2}\) and \(^2S_{1/2}\) channels, a result not reported in prior studies, suggesting that \(^8_{J/\psi}{\mathrm{Be}}\) may not be a Borromean nucleus.
*   The \(^8_{\phi}{\mathrm{Be}}(4^+_1)\) state transitions from bound to resonant depending on the \(\alpha\)-particle root-mean-square matter radius \(R_\alpha\), which was varied among 1.84, 1.70, and 1.56 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of meson-nucleus systems, specifically the calculations of bound states in \(^8_{\phi}{\mathrm{Be}}\), \(^8_{J/\psi}{\mathrm{Be}}\), and \(^8_{\eta_c}{\mathrm{Be}}\) by Filikhin (2024) and Etminan (2025), by also investigating the resonant states of these systems and the dynamic effects imparted by the mesons. The authors use the HAL QCD potentials for \(N\)-\(\phi\), \(N\)-\(J/\psi\), and \(N\)-\(\eta_c\) interactions from Lyu (2022, 2024), and compare the "glue-like" role of the \(\phi\), \(J/\psi\), and \(\eta_c\) particles with that of the \(\Lambda\) hyperon studied in earlier hypernuclear physics. The results provide systematic theoretical predictions for future experimental searches of such exotic hadron-nucleus systems.
