# Investigation of Nonlinear Collective Dynamics in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions Using A Multi-Phase Transport Model

> The nonlinear response coefficient, $χ_{4,22}$, is a crucial observable for probing the dynamical properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). While traditionally understood as a signature of medium response, recent studies suggest that $χ_{4,22}$ also encapsulates critical information regarding the intrinsic initial-state configuration of the colliding nuclei. In this study, we utilize A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to investigate the microscopic origin and stage-by-stage development of $χ_{4,22}$ in $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV. By tracking the flow observables through the partonic cascade, quark coalescence, and hadronic rescattering phases, we map the translation of initial geometric eccentricities into final-state momentum anisotropies. Our results demonstrate that the absolute magnitude of $χ_{4,22}$ increases continuously during the collective expansion, confirming its nature as a dynamically generated medium response. However, the comparative ratio of this coefficient between the U+U and Au+Au systems is stable across all evolutionary stages within statistical uncertainties. This indicates that the ratio approximately cancels complex evolutionary dynamics to isolate intrinsic geometric correlations present at the initial state. These findings provide compelling theoretical support and crucial insights for recent experimental efforts aiming to extract high-order nuclear structure, such as hexadecapole deformation, using nonlinear flow observables.

## Key claim

The nonlinear response coefficient χ₄,₂₂ is confirmed as a dynamically generated medium response whose absolute magnitude increases continuously during collective expansion; however, the ratio of this coefficient between U+U and Au+Au systems remains stable across all evolutionary stages within statistical uncertainties, indicating that the ratio approximately cancels complex evolutionary dynamics to isolate intrinsic geometric correlations present at the initial state.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to simulate ²³⁸U+²³⁸U and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, tracking flow observables through three distinct stages: after the partonic cascade (ZPC), after hadronization via quark coalescence, and after full evolution including hadronic rescattering (ART). The initial nuclear density profiles were sampled with deformed Woods-Saxon distributions using β₂ = 0.286 and β₄ = 0.100 for uranium and β₂ = -0.131 and β₄ = 0.031 for gold. A total of 1×10⁷ minimum-bias events were generated for each collision system.

## Key numbers

- Uranium deformation parameters: β₂ = 0.286, β₄ = 0.100 (from Raman (2001) and Ryssens (2023))
- Gold deformation parameters: β₂ = -0.131, β₄ = 0.031
- Center-of-mass energy: √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Partonic scattering cross section: σ = 1.5 mb (with α_s = 0.33 and Debye screening mass μ_D = 3.2032 fm⁻¹)
- Maximum evolution time for hadronic cascade: t_max = 30 fm/c
- Maximum evolution time for isolating hadronization: t_max = 0.6 fm/c
- Lund fragmentation parameters: a = 0.55, b = 0.15 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends recent studies by Xu (2024) and Ryssens (2023) that suggested χ₄,₂₂ is sensitive to hexadecapole deformation (β₄) of colliding nuclei, and provides theoretical support for preliminary STAR collaboration results pointing to a sizable β₄ deformation. The comparative ratio method between U+U and Au+Au systems builds on prior isobar collision approaches by Xu (2017) and Li (2019) for reducing theoretical uncertainties from medium evolution. The findings enable experimental extraction of high-order nuclear structure, such as hexadecapole deformation, using nonlinear flow observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
