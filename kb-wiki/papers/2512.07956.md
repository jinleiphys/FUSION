# Theory of inverse beta decay for reactor antineutrinos

> Inverse beta decay (IBD), $\overlineν_e p \to e^+ n \left( γ\right)$, is the main detection channel for reactor antineutrinos in water- and hydrocarbon-based detectors. As reactor antineutrino experiments now target sub-percent-level sensitivity to oscillation parameters, a precise theoretical description of IBD, including recoil, weak magnetism, nucleon structure, and radiative corrections, becomes essential. In this work, we give a detailed and precise calculation of the total and differential cross sections for radiative IBD, $\overlineν_e p \to e^+ n γ$. We use a heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework, systematically incorporating electroweak, electromagnetic, and strong-interaction corrections. We derive new analytic cross-section expressions, clarify the collinear structure of radiative corrections, and provide a systematic uncertainty analysis. We also discuss phenomenological applications for reactor antineutrino experiments, e.g., JUNO, and neutron decay. Our results enable sub-permille theoretical precision, supporting current and future experiments.

## Key claim
This work provides a sub-permille-level theoretical description of inverse beta decay (IBD) cross sections, including radiative corrections, by using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to systematically incorporate electroweak, electromagnetic, and strong-interaction corrections, but the claimed sub-permille precision is conditional on the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) and the CKM matrix element \(V_{ud}\) being the dominant remaining uncertainties, and the results are derived within the HBChPT framework with exact three-body phase-space integrals that violate rigorous HBChPT power counting near kinematic endpoints.

## Method
The authors compute total and differential cross sections for radiative IBD (\(\overline{\nu}_e p \to e^+ n \gamma\)) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, incorporating recoil corrections, weak magnetism from nucleon magnetic moments, nucleon structure effects via form factors, and virtual QED corrections. They derive new analytic expressions for cross sections with exact three-body phase-space integrals, evaluate both positron and electromagnetic energy spectra with exact phase-space boundaries, and provide a systematic uncertainty budget for total and differential cross sections at the sub-percent level.

## Key numbers
- IBD threshold energy: \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e}^\mathrm{thr} \approx 1.806066~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Leading-order total cross section expression: \(\sigma_{\rm LO} = \frac{{G}_{\rm F}^2 |V_{ud}|^2}{\pi} \left( E_{\overline{\nu}_e} - E_0 \right) \frac{\sqrt{\Sigma \left( s, m^2_n, m^2_e \right)}}{2m_n} \left( g_V^2 + 3 g_A^2 \right)\), with \(E_0 \approx 1.292581~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Recoil correction to total unpolarized IBD cross section: \(1.9\%\) at \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e} = 5~\mathrm{MeV}\) and \(4.0\%\) at \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e} = 10~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Recoil correction to differential unpolarized IBD cross section: up to \(1.5\%\) at \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e} = 5~\mathrm{MeV}\) and \(3.1\%\) at \(E_{\overline{\nu}_e} = 10~\mathrm{MeV}\), reaching \(3.2\%\) near threshold
- Vector coupling constant at electron-mass renormalization scale: \(g_V = 1.02499(13)\)
- Axial-vector-to-vector coupling constant ratio (PDG): \(\lambda^{\mathrm{I}} = 1.2754(13)\)
- CKM matrix element: \(V_{ud} = 0.97348(31)\)
- Relative correction to neutron lifetime from Coulomb enhancement and other effects: \(\Delta_{\rm TOT} - \left( g^2_V - 1\right) = 2.700(7) \%\)
- JUNO expects about 83 IBD events per day with a 20-kiloton liquid scintillator detector, yielding approximately 180,000 events over six years

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of IBD cross sections first comprehensively treated by Vogel (1983) for QED radiative corrections, and later refined by Fayans (1985) and Strumia (2003), who provided a compact parameterization with estimated uncertainties of 0.4%. Recent analyses by Ankowski (2016) and Ricciardi (2022) improved uncertainties to the 0.05-0.1% range. The present paper addresses unresolved issues including the distinction between positron and electromagnetic energy spectra, exact phase-space boundaries for radiative processes, and the inclusion of hard photon emission effects. The results enable sub-permille theoretical precision for current and future reactor antineutrino experiments such as JUNO, and provide analytic expressions and numerical tools (a Mathematica notebook and Python library) for fast evaluation of IBD cross sections.
