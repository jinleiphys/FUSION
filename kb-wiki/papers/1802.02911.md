# Event-by-Event Efficiency Fluctuations and Efficiency Correction for Cumulants of Superposed Multiplicity Distributions in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collision Experiments

> We performed systematic studies on the effects of event-by-event efficiency fluctuations on efficiency correction for cumulant analysis in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. Experimentally, particle efficiencies of events measured under different experimental conditions should be different. For fluctuation measurements, the final event-by-event multiplicity distributions should be the superposed distributions of various type of events measured under different conditions. We demonstrate efficiency fluctuation effects using numerical simulation, in which we construct an event ensemble consisting of events with two different efficiencies. By using the mean particle efficiencies, we find that the efficiency corrected cumulants show large deviations from the original inputs when the discrepancy between the two efficiencies is large. We further studied the effects of efficiency fluctuations for the cumulants of net-proton distributions by implementing the UrQMD events of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV in a realistic STAR detector acceptance. We consider the unequal efficiency in two sides of the Time Projection Chamber (TPC), multiplicity dependent efficiency, and the event-by-event variations of the collision vertex position along the longitudinal direction ($V_\mathrm{z}$). When the efficiencies fluctuate dramatically within the studied event sample, the effects of efficiency fluctuations have significant impacts on the efficiency corrections of cumulants with the mean efficiency. We find that this effect can be effectively suppressed by binning the entire event ensemble into various sub-event samples, in which the efficiency variations are relatively small. The final efficiency corrected cumulants can be calculated from the weighted average of the corrected factorial moments of the sub-event samples with the mean efficiency.

## Key claim

Using the mean efficiency to correct higher-order cumulants of superposed multiplicity distributions fails when event-by-event efficiency fluctuations are large, because the cumulant of a superposition of different distributions is not the weighted average of the individual cumulants; the effect can be suppressed by binning events into sub-samples with small efficiency variations and then averaging the corrected factorial moments.

## Method

The authors constructed event ensembles mixing two types of events with different fixed efficiencies (e.g., \(\epsilon_1\) varying from 0.4 to 0.8, \(\epsilon_2 = 0.8\)) and input proton numbers following a Poisson distribution with \(\lambda = 100\). They then implemented a more realistic simulation using UrQMD events of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV with a fast detector simulation of the STAR TPC, incorporating unequal west/east TPC efficiencies, multiplicity-dependent efficiency, and event-by-event variations of the collision vertex position \(V_z\). Efficiency-corrected cumulants were computed both with the mean efficiency of the whole ensemble and by binning events into sub-samples with smaller efficiency variations, then averaging the corrected factorial moments.

## Key numbers

* For a Poisson input with \(\lambda = 100\), using the mean efficiency to correct \(C_4\) (fourth-order cumulant) showed large deviations from the input value when the efficiency difference \(\Delta\epsilon\) between two event types was as small as 0.1 (e.g., \(\epsilon_1 = 0.7\), \(\epsilon_2 = 0.8\)).
* In the UrQMD simulation at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, the collision vertex position \(V_z\) was set to a flat distribution within the range \((-30, 30)\) cm.
* With 5 efficiency bins (larger efficiency variations per bin), the factorial-moments-average method gave a \(C_4\) closer to the original input than the cumulants-average method, but both methods failed to reproduce the input higher-order cumulants.
* With 100 efficiency bins (finer binning), both methods reproduced the original input cumulants, and the additional uncertainties from the cumulants-average method became much smaller.

## Context

This work extends the standard binomial efficiency correction framework for cumulants (Bzdak, 2013; Luo, 2014) by addressing the previously overlooked effect of event-by-event efficiency fluctuations in real experimental conditions. It contradicts the implicit assumption that using the mean efficiency is sufficient for higher-order cumulant corrections, and it enables more reliable extraction of net-proton cumulants in STAR Beam Energy Scan data by recommending sub-event binning based on efficiency-related quantities such as collision vertex position and TPC side asymmetry.
