# D mesons in a magnetic field

> We investigate the mass spectra of open heavy flavor mesons in an external constant magnetic field within QCD sum rules. Spectral ansätze on the phenomenological side are proposed in order to properly take into account mixing effects between the pseudoscalar and vector channels, and the Landau levels of charged mesons. The operator product expansion is implemented up to dimension-5 operators. As a result, we find for neutral D mesons a significant positive mass shift that goes beyond simple mixing effects. In contrast, charged D mesons are further subject to Landau level effects, which together with the mixing effects almost completely saturate the mass shifts obtained in our sum rule analysis.

## Key claim

The paper finds that neutral D mesons experience a significant positive mass shift in a constant magnetic field that goes beyond simple mixing effects, while charged D mesons are further subject to Landau level effects which together with mixing effects almost completely saturate the mass shifts obtained in the QCD sum rule analysis.

## Method

The authors apply QCD sum rules to compute the mass spectra of open heavy flavor mesons (D mesons) in an external constant magnetic field, implementing the operator product expansion up to dimension-5 operators. On the phenomenological side, they construct spectral ansätze that properly account for mixing effects between pseudoscalar and vector channels induced by the magnetic field, as well as Landau level quantization for charged mesons. The Borel transform is used to extract the lowest-lying pole masses from the dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- Neutral D meson mass shift: a significant positive mass shift is found, going beyond simple mixing effects (no explicit numerical value given in the truncated text)
- The operator product expansion is implemented up to dimension-5 operators
- The expansion is valid when the magnitude of the magnetic field is small compared to the Borel mass, which serves as the separation scale
- Operators up to dimension 4 and second order in eB are computed for terms depending on the magnetic field

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule framework previously applied to charmonium spectroscopy in magnetic fields by the same authors (Cho et al., 2014; Cho et al., 2015) to open heavy flavor mesons. It differs from a preceding QCD sum rule study of B mesons in magnetic fields by Machado et al. (2014) in two key respects: first, the present work includes mixing effects between pseudoscalar and vector mesons that were neglected in that earlier study; second, it properly treats the emergence and exact cancellation of infrared divergences on the OPE side, which were not handled correctly in the prior work. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD simulations and effective model calculations of heavy meson properties in strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions and magnetars.
