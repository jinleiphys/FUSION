# Constraining the chiral magnetic effect using spectator and participant planes across Au+Au and isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 200$ GeV

> We investigate the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions through an improved two-plane method analysis of the $Δγ$ observable, probing $\mathcal{CP}$-symmetry breaking in strong interactions and topological properties of the QCD vacuum. Using a multiphase transport model with tunable CME strengths, we systematically compare Au+Au and isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 200$ GeV. We observe a reduced difference in the CME signal-to-background ratio between the spectator and participant planes for Au+Au collisions compared to isobar collisions. A comprehensive chi-square analysis across all three collision systems reveals stronger CME signatures in Au+Au collisions relative to isobar collisions, particularly when measured with respect to the spectator plane. Our findings demonstrate an enhanced experimental reliability of the two-plane method for the CME detection in Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the ratio of the CME signal measured with respect to the participant plane relative to the spectator plane (parameter \(b\)) differs from the corresponding ratio of elliptic flow (parameter \(a\)), specifically \(b/a = 0.88 \pm 0.08\) in Au+Au collisions, which is larger than the value of \(0.65 \pm 0.18\) previously found for isobar collisions. This means the assumption that \(b = a\) (used in the standard two-plane method) leads to an overestimation of the CME signal fraction, and the correction is system-dependent.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with a string melting mechanism and a tunable CME-like charge separation implemented in the initial partonic stage by setting a percentage \(p\) of quarks to undergo directed charge separation along the magnetic field. They reconstructed the spectator plane (SP) from initial-state spectator neutrons and the participant plane (PP) from initial-state participating partons, then calculated the charge-dependent azimuthal correlator \(\Delta\gamma\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) with respect to both planes. By comparing \(\Delta\gamma\) values with and without the CME signal, they extracted the ratio \(b\) of the CME signal between the two planes and used it to compute a corrected CME fraction \(f_{\mathrm{CME}}\{b\}\) via a modified formula.

## Key numbers

*   For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 200\) GeV in the 20-50% centrality range, the ratio \(b/a = 0.88 \pm 0.08\).
*   For isobar (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) collisions at the same energy, the previously reported ratio \(b/a = 0.65 \pm 0.18\).
*   The CME signal percentage \(p = 5\%\) or \(7.5\%\) in the AMPT model produces \(\Delta\gamma\) values that align most closely with STAR experimental data for Au+Au collisions.
*   The ratio \(f_{\mathrm{CME}}\{b\}\) to \(f_{\mathrm{CME}}\) (the uncorrected fraction) is less than unity for the 20-50% centrality bins, indicating that assuming \(b=a\) overestimates the CME signal fraction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMPT-based study of the two-plane method in isobar collisions (Chen, 2023) to Au+Au collisions, providing a simultaneous constraint on CME strengths across three collision systems. The results directly address a critical assumption in the two-plane method used by the STAR collaboration (STAR, 2021) for CME detection, showing that the ratio \(b\) of CME signals between planes does not equal the ratio \(a\) of elliptic flow, and that this discrepancy is system-dependent. The findings enable more reliable extraction of the CME fraction from experimental data and suggest that after correcting for \(b\), Au+Au collisions exhibit stronger CME signatures than isobar collisions, contrary to the trend seen without the correction.
