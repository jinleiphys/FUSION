# Meson-Meson interaction in a non-perturbative chiral approach

> A non-perturbative method which combines constraints from chiral symmetry breaking and coupled channel unitarity is used to describe the meson-meson interaction up to about 1.2 GeV. The approach uses the O(p^2) and O(p^4) chiral Lagrangians. The seven free parameters of the O(p^4) Lagrangian are fitted to the data. The results are in good agreement with a vast amount of experimental analyses. The amplitudes develop poles in the complex plane corresponding to the f0, a0, rho, K*, phi, sigma and kappa resonances; the latter two, very broad. The total and partial decay widths of the resonances are also well reproduced. Further extensions and applications of this chiral non-perturbative scheme are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative chiral approach that combines constraints from chiral symmetry breaking with coupled-channel unitarity to describe meson-meson interactions up to about 1.2 GeV, but the method is limited by the omission of multipion states and the ηη channel, and it loses precision at low energies compared to full O(p⁴) chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The approach uses the O(p²) and O(p⁴) chiral Lagrangians, with the seven free parameters of the O(p⁴) Lagrangian fitted to experimental data. The scattering amplitude T is constructed via a coupled-channel generalization of the Inverse Amplitude Method, where T = T₂ · [T₂ − T₄^P − T₂·G·T₂]⁻¹ · T₂, with T₂ from the O(p²) Lagrangian, T₄^P the polynomial tree-level O(p⁴) contribution, and G the loop integral regularized with a cutoff q_max = 1.02 GeV. The method is applied to J = 0 and J = 1 partial waves with coupled meson-meson states (ππ, K\bar{K}, πη, Kπ, Kη) for various isospin channels.

## Key numbers

- The σ resonance pole appears at √s ≈ 442 − i 225 MeV.
- The f₀ resonance pole appears around 980 − i 14 MeV, corresponding to an apparent mass of about 980 MeV and a width of 30 MeV.
- The κ resonance pole appears around 770 − i 250 MeV.
- The ρ resonance is well described, with phase shift data reproduced up to 1.2 GeV; the K*(892) resonance is also reproduced with good agreement up to 1.2 GeV.
- The φ resonance appears as a pole around 990 MeV, about 30 MeV below its physical mass of 1020 MeV (a relative deviation of 3%).
- The cutoff used to regularize loop integrals is q_max = 1.02 GeV.
- The approach reproduces phase shifts and inelasticities up to about 1.2 GeV, with small discrepancies starting around 1.2 GeV due to omitted channels.

## Context

This work extends the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) of Truong (1988) and Dobado et al. (1990) by incorporating coupled channels, which allows the f₀(980) and a₀(980) resonances to appear as poles (absent in the single-channel IAM). It also generalizes the earlier coupled-channel approach of Oller and Oset (1997), which used only the O(p²) Lagrangian and a single cutoff parameter, by including O(p⁴) contributions to correctly describe the J = 1 sector (ρ, K*, φ). The method enables future extensions to include additional channels or higher-order terms, and it establishes a link between unitarized chiral perturbation theory and vector meson dominance.
