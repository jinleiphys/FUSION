# Geometry-induced azimuthal anisotropy in coherent $J/ψ$ photoproduction

> Azimuthal anisotropies in heavy-ion collisions are conventionally interpreted as signatures of hydrodynamic flow. We demonstrate that in peripheral collisions, a significant $\cos 2ϕ$ asymmetry in the decay leptons of coherently photoproduced $J/ψ$ mesons arises purely from the initial-state geometry of the nuclear electromagnetic field. This modulation originates from the linear polarization of coherent photons, which is radially aligned in impact parameter space and transferred to the vector meson. By employing light-cone perturbation theory within the dipole formalism, we calculate the centrality dependence of this asymmetry for collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. Our predictions quantitatively reproduce STAR data. This observable thus provides a rigorous benchmark for distinguishing electromagnetic initial-state effects from collective medium dynamics.

# Geometry-induced azimuthal anisotropy in coherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction

## Key claim
The observed \(\cos 2\phi\) azimuthal asymmetry in decay leptons from coherently photoproduced \(J/\psi\) mesons in peripheral heavy-ion collisions arises purely from the initial-state geometry of the nuclear electromagnetic field, specifically from the linear polarization of coherent photons that is radially aligned in impact parameter space, and does not require hydrodynamic flow to explain the STAR data.

## Method
The authors employ light-cone perturbation theory within the dipole formalism, where the incoming photon splits into a quark-antiquark pair that elastically scatters off the color glass condensate gluons in the target nucleus before recombining into a \(J/\psi\) meson. The production amplitude is computed using the IP-Sat saturation model for the nuclear gluon distribution, with the impact parameter mapped to collision centrality via an optical Glauber model using Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions. The \(\cos 2\phi\) asymmetry is then extracted from the lepton-pair cross section by isolating the polarization-dependent terms in the decay amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The \(\cos 2\phi\) asymmetry reaches nearly \(40\%\) in the centrality category \([50\%-80\%]\) for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV at RHIC.
- The theoretical calculation reproduces STAR data within uncertainties for the centrality dependence of the asymmetry at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.36\) TeV at the LHC, the asymmetry shows a similar centrality dependence, rising gradually and saturating at higher centralities.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used are \(42 \pm 3\) mb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and \(68 \pm 1.2\) mb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.36\) TeV.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters are \(R_{WS} = 6.38\) fm and \(a = 0.535\) fm for Au nuclei, and \(R_{WS} = 6.62\) fm and \(a = 0.546\) fm for Pb nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the study of photon-induced processes from ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs), where hadronic interactions are suppressed, into peripheral and semi-central collisions where hadronic overlap is present. The \(\cos 2\phi\) modulation is conceptually similar to the \(\cos 4\phi\) asymmetry in \(\gamma\gamma \to \ell^+\ell^-\) studied by Xiao et al. (2020), but the intermediate spin-1 vector meson dictates a \(\cos 2\phi\) signature. The results provide a rigorous benchmark for distinguishing electromagnetic initial-state effects from collective medium dynamics, enabling future measurements at the LHC by CMS to test these predictions and refine the separation of initial-state geometry from final-state hydrodynamic flow.
