# Photo Reactions on the Deuteron in the Delta-Resonance

> Photo disintegration of the deuteron and photo pionproduction on the deuteron are described in the region of the $Δ$-resonance. The two reactions are unitarily coupled. A novel definition of electromagnetic exchange currents in the presence of pionproduction is given. Numerical predictions are done for spin-independent and spin-dependent observables of both reactions. The sensitivity of the results with respect to the nucleon-$Δ$ transition current and with respect to the irreducible nucleon-$Δ$ potential is studied.

## Key claim

The paper develops a consistent theoretical framework for describing both photo disintegration of the deuteron and photo pionproduction on the deuteron in the Delta-resonance region, with the two reactions unitarily coupled; the authors note that their description is unlikely to add much new understanding of the processes themselves, but provides a thorough discussion of consistent treatment of pionic and electromagnetic reactions from the deuteron.

## Method

The authors employ a nonrelativistic quantum mechanical force model with a Hilbert space containing purely nucleonic states, nucleon-Delta states, and pion-nucleon states, where pion production and absorption proceed exclusively through Delta-isobar excitation and deexcitation. They define a novel electromagnetic exchange current in the presence of pion production, ensuring current conservation, and compute the multi-channel transition matrix for photon reactions in the two-nucleon system. The hadronic interaction parameters are taken from Poepping et al. (1995), while the single-baryon current parameters are fitted to single-nucleon photo pionproduction data up to 1.4 GeV center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic dipole strength for the nucleon-Delta transition current: G_{M1}^{Delta N} = 3.65 (dimensionless, fitted to single-nucleon data)
* Electric quadrupole strength for the nucleon-Delta transition current: G_{E2}^{Delta N} = 0.10 (dimensionless, fitted to single-nucleon data)
* Cut-off parameter for the Born current form factor in the magnetic dipole channel: Lambda_B^{M1} = 245.5 MeV
* Cut-off parameter for the Born current form factor in the electric quadrupole channel: Lambda_B^{E2} = 379.0 MeV
* Bare mass of the Delta-isobar: m_Delta^0 = 1315.8 MeV
* The force model is valid for center-of-mass energies below 500 MeV, limited by the inclusion of inelastic channels with at most one pion
* The P33 pion-nucleon resonance mass is 1232 MeV at resonance in the pion-nucleon center-of-mass system

## Context

This work extends the hadronic force model of Poepping et al. (1995) to include electromagnetic reactions, completing work started by Oelfke and Sauer (1995). It builds on the pioneering studies of deuteron disintegration by Arenhoevel and Leidemann (1990s) which demonstrated the importance of meson-exchange currents and isobar excitation, and extends the simultaneous description of photo pionproduction on the deuteron by Wilhelm et al. (1990s). The framework enables consistent calculation of spin-independent and spin-dependent observables for both photo disintegration and photo pionproduction, and allows study of sensitivity to the nucleon-Delta transition current and the irreducible nucleon-Delta potential.
