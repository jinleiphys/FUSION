# Analysis of Peierls-Yoccoz rotational energy of nuclei with semi-realistic interaction

> The Peierls-Yoccoz (PY) rotational energy of nuclei has been analyzed by the angular-momentum projection (AMP) on the axial Hartree-Fock solutions, by using the semi-realistic effective Hamiltonian M3Y-P6. The rotational energy is decomposed into contributions of the individual terms of the Hamiltonian, and their ratios to the total PY rotational energy are calculated. Except for light or weakly-deformed nuclei, the ratios of the individual terms of the Hamiltonian are insensitive to nuclides and deformation. The contributions of kinetic energies are large and close to the rigid-rotor values, although those of central forces are sizable. For light or weakly-deformed nuclei, the ratios significantly depend on nuclei and deformation. The contributions of noncentral forces are not negligible. Regardless of nuclides, the attractive forces decrease the moment-of-inertia, and the repulsive forces increase it. A general formula for the PY rotational energy is derived, which suggests that higher-order terms of the cumulant expansion play roles in the rotational energy and the moment-of-inertia for light or weakly-deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The Peierls-Yoccoz (PY) rotational energy, obtained by angular-momentum projection (AMP) on fixed axial Hartree-Fock (HF) solutions using the semi-realistic M3Y-P6 interaction, decomposes into contributions from individual Hamiltonian terms whose ratios to the total PY rotational energy are insensitive to nuclides and deformation except for light or weakly-deformed nuclei, where the ratios depend significantly on nuclei and deformation.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum projection (AMP) on axial Hartree-Fock (HF) solutions for deformed Mg, Zr, Nd, and Sm isotopes using the semi-realistic effective Hamiltonian M3Y-P6. They decomposed the PY rotational energy into contributions from kinetic energy, density-independent central forces, density-dependent central forces, LS force, tensor force, and the central part of the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP), calculating the ratios of each term's contribution to the total PY rotational energy. A general formula for the PY rotational energy was derived using cumulant expansion, extending previous formulations by Peierls and Yoccoz (1957) and Kamlah (1968).

## Key numbers

- For medium-heavy deformed nuclei (Zr, Nd, Sm), the contributions of kinetic energy (K), central force (C), and density-dependent central force (Cρ) to the 2+ excitation energy are approximately +75%, +75%, and -50% of the total E_x(2+), respectively.
- The ratios E_x(4+)/E_x(2+) obtained from the AMP calculations are close to the experimental values and to the rigid-rotor value of 10/3 for deformed Zr, Nd, and Sm nuclei up to J ≈ 12.
- For Mg isotopes, the ratios E_x(4+)/E_x(2+) are close to 10/3 except for 40Mg, where the spin and parity of excited states have not been confirmed experimentally.
- The contributions of the OPEP term (V^(OPEP)) to the rotational energy are at most about 10% of the total E_x(2+).
- The LS force and tensor force contributions are small for medium-heavy nuclei but become relatively larger for light Mg isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the Peierls-Yoccoz (1957) and Kamlah (1968) formulations of rotational energy by providing a more general cumulant expansion that includes higher-order terms important for light or weakly-deformed nuclei. It applies the M3Y-type semi-realistic interactions (Nakada 2013, 2020) to AMP calculations for the first time, complementing earlier cranking model approaches (Inglis 1954-55, Belyaev 1959) and Thouless-Valatin formula (1962). The results enable future studies incorporating variation-after-projection (VAP) schemes and pair correlations, as well as applications to nuclei where the J(J+1) rule may break down.
