# Seniority and $(\frac{9}{2})^n$ configurations in neutron-rich Nickel isotopes

> Excited states in low-energy spectra in $^{70-76}$Ni are considered. To this end, pairing forces in form of surface delta interaction are employed to account for formation of the ground state multiplet with seniority $ν= 2$ states. The multiplet splitting is described with mass relations of masses of neighbouring nuclei. Subsequently, seniority model is used to reproduce or predict the states $ν= 3$ in odd-even isotopes and $ν= 4$ in even-even isotopes. Correct account of $2_1^+$ state should allow for description of reversed order of states $J = 4$ with $ν= 2$ and $ν= 4$ observed in experiment. The results obtained are compared with the structure of similar multiplets in $N=50$ isotones.

# Seniority and $(9/2)^n$ configurations in neutron-rich Nickel isotopes

## Key claim

The seniority model combined with a surface delta interaction approximation for pairing can reproduce or predict the low-energy spectra of neutron-rich nickel isotopes $^{70-76}$Ni, provided the energy of the $2^+$ state in the $\nu=2$ ground state multiplet is taken from experiment rather than calculated, because the delta-force approximation systematically overestimates this energy by approximately 1 MeV.

## Method

The authors treat neutron-rich nickel isotopes $^{70-76}$Ni within a model space restricted to the $g_{9/2}$ subshell only, taking $^{68}$Ni as a doubly magic core. The $\nu=2$ ground state multiplet splitting is calculated using the surface delta interaction approximation, with the pairing energy $\Delta_{nn}^{(4)}$ extracted from a four-point mass relation using binding energies from AME2020. States with higher seniority ($\nu=3$ in odd-even isotopes, $\nu=4$ in even-even isotopes) are then constructed using fractional parentage coefficients from Bayman (1966), with the $\nu=2$ energies either taken from the delta-force calculation or fixed to experimental values.

## Key numbers

- The $6^+$ and $8^+$ states in $^{70}$Ni and $^{76}$Ni are reproduced within 0.1 MeV using the delta-force approximation.
- The $4^+$ states in $^{70}$Ni and $^{76}$Ni are reproduced with an error of no more than 0.5 MeV.
- The $2^+$ energy is overestimated by approximately 1 MeV in the delta-force approximation.
- For $^{70}$Ni, the ratio $E^*(2^+)/\Delta_{nn}^{(4)} = 0.44$; for $^{76}$Ni, this ratio is 0.39.
- For $^{92}$Mo, $E^*(2^+)/\Delta_{pp}^{(4)} = 0.53$; for $^{98}$Cd, this ratio is 0.56.
- The total splitting of the $\nu=3$ GSM in $^{71}$Ni equals $4/5\Delta_{nn}$, approximately 2.27 MeV.
- The $12^+$ state in $^{72}$Ni and $^{74}$Ni is estimated at 5–6 MeV.
- The occupation of $\nu g_{9/2}$ in $^{68}$Ni is affected on a scale of around 4.7% compared to $N=40$ isotones, based on shell model calculations by Coraggio and Covello (2014).

## Context

This work extends the seniority-based description of $g_{9/2}$ configurations previously applied to nuclei near $^{208}$Pb by Stepantsov and Isakov (2018) and to $N=50$ isotones by various authors. It directly addresses the observed reversal of $4^+$ states with different seniority in $^{72}$Ni and $^{74}$Ni reported by Muecher et al. (2016) and Morales et al. (2018), and compares the nickel chain to the $N=50$ isotone chain ($^{94}$Ru, $^{96}$Pd) where the $4^+$ inversion does not occur. The results enable predictions for unmeasured high-spin states in neutron-rich nickel isotopes and clarify which experimental states correspond to $\nu=2$ versus $\nu=4$ seniority, informing future spectroscopic studies at facilities such as RIBF, FRIB, and FAIR.
