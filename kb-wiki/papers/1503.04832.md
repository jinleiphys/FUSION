# Quark matter in high-mass neutron stars?

> The recent measurements of the masses of the pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 provide independent proof for the existence of neutron stars with masses in range of 2 $M_\odot$. This fact has significant implications for the physics of high density matter and it challenges the hypothesis that the cores of NS can be composed of deconfined quark matter. In this contribution we study a description of quark matter based on the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio effective model and construct the equation of state for matter in beta equilibrium. This equation of state together with the hadronic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state is used here to describe neutron star and hybrid star configurations. We show that compact stars masses of 2 $M_\odot$ are compatible with the possible existence of deconfined quark matter in their core.

## Key claim

The existence of neutron stars with masses around 2 solar masses does not exclude the possibility of deconfined quark matter in their cores, provided the diquark and vector coupling constants of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model are tuned to a narrow allowed region.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state (EoS) for quark matter using a three-flavor NJL model with scalar, vector, and diquark interaction channels, solved at mean-field level for matter in beta equilibrium. This quark matter EoS was matched via a Maxwell construction to a hadronic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) EoS to describe hybrid star configurations. A systematic scan of the vector and diquark coupling parameters was performed, with the additional constraint from heavy-ion collision flow data.

## Key numbers

- Pulsar PSR J1614-2230 mass: \(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\)
- Pulsar PSR J0348-0432 mass: \(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\)
- The most promising parameter region for successful neutron star phenomenology is diquark couplings around 1.0 and vector couplings above 0.6 (in units relative to the scalar coupling)
- An example promising parameter set (\(\eta_D = 1.01\), \(\eta_V = 0.5\)) gives an onset of quark matter in stars with mass \(\sim 1.5 M_\odot\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Klahn (2013) showing that 2-solar-mass neutron stars can be compatible with quark matter cores, directly contradicting the suggestion by Lattimer (2012) that such high masses rule out deconfined quark matter. The results enable further studies of NJL model extensions to identify additional interaction channels that could quantitatively change the picture, and motivate Bayesian analyses of mass and radius data as pursued by Lattimer (2010) and Ayriyan (2014).
