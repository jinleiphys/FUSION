# Parton distribution functions from scalar light-front parton gas model

> We propose an application of a microcanonical ensemble with light-front kinematics to model the phase-space distribution of relativistic constituents of a bound state. These constituents denoted by partons are treated as classical spin-zero particles confined inside the bound state with inter-parton collisions as their only interaction. The microcanonical molecular dynamics ensemble is applied to obtain the phase-space distribution of such a thermodynamic system. We sample this phase-space distribution using Monte Carlo algorithms to obtain the parton distribution functions (PDFs) in scenarios with $3$, $4$, and $5$ identical partons. In addition PDFs when a selected number of massless partons are mixed with $3$ massive partons are also presented.

# Parton distribution functions from scalar light-front parton gas model

## Key claim

The authors propose that parton distribution functions (PDFs) can be modeled using a microcanonical molecular dynamics ensemble of classical spin-zero particles with light-front kinematics, where the only interaction is inter-parton elastic collisions. The model is restricted to fixed particle number (no creation or annihilation) and ignores quantum field theory effects, spin, and coordinate-space interactions, making it a purely classical thermodynamic description of parton phase-space distributions.

## Method

The authors treat partons as classical spin-zero particles confined inside a bound state, with dynamics governed by the light-front kinetic energy Hamiltonian. The phase-space distribution is given by the microcanonical molecular dynamics ensemble (EVNP ensemble) with fixed energy, volume, particle number, and total light-front 3-momentum. They analytically marginalize the transverse momentum integrals to obtain the joint longitudinal momentum-fraction distribution, then compute single-parton longitudinal momentum-fraction distributions (the PDFs) through analytical integration for N=2 and N=3 identical massive partons, and through quadrature and Gibbs sampling (Markov Chain Monte Carlo) for N=3, 4, and 5 identical partons, as well as for mixtures of massless and massive partons.

## Key numbers

- For N=2 identical massive partons in the limit u >> 4m^2, the single-parton distribution reduces to ω_x(x) = 6x(1-x)
- For N=2 identical massive partons at the threshold u → 4m^2, the distribution becomes ω_x(x) = δ(x-1/2)
- For N massless partons, the single-parton distribution is ω_x(x) = (2N-2)(2N-1) x (1-x)^(2N-3) θ(P^+E - P^⊥²)
- For N=3 massless partons, the distribution is ω_x(x) = 20 x (1-x)^3
- For N=3 identical massive partons with mass m=1, the distribution is given by Eq. (55) with x_± = [u-3 ± √((u-9)(u-1))]/(2u) and requires u > 9m²
- Numerical quadrature for N=3 used 99 quadrature points per transverse momentum component for massive partons and 199 for massless partons to achieve accuracy within 1%
- Gibbs sampling used sample size of 1×10^6 after discarding 1×10^5 initial samples, with instrumental variance a=0.1, and 16 independent samples per parameter combination

## Context

This work extends statistical descriptions of hadron PDFs, such as those by Mangano (1995), Bhalerao (1996), and Bourrely (2001, 2015), by introducing a microcanonical ensemble approach based on light-front kinematics rather than canonical or grand-canonical ensembles. The model connects to basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approaches by Vary (2009) and provides a classical thermodynamic complement to quantum field calculations. The large-x behavior of the massless parton distributions agrees with quark counting rules from Ball (2016) and deTeramond (2018). This approach enables future studies of phase-space distributions as functions of light-front kinematic variables in terms of thermodynamic properties, and can be extended to include spin, quantum statistics, and coordinate-space interactions.
