# Isospin Transport at Fermi Energies

> In this paper we investigate isospin transport mechanisms in semi-peripheral collisions at Fermi energies. The effects of the formation of a low density region (neck) between the two reaction partners and of pre-equilibrium emission on the dynamics of isospin equilibration are carefully analyzed. We clearly identify two main contributions to the isospin transport: isospin diffusion due to the $N/Z$ ratio and isospin drift due to the density gradients. Both effects are sensitive to the symmetry part of the nuclear Equation of State (EOS), in particular to the value and slope around saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper identifies two distinct isospin transport mechanisms in semi-peripheral collisions at Fermi energies: isospin diffusion driven by the N/Z ratio and isospin drift driven by density gradients; the interplay between these two processes and pre-equilibrium emission leads to a stronger overall equilibration for a soft symmetry energy, even though a stiff symmetry energy produces a larger isospin transfer through the neck region.

## Method

The authors performed stochastic Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport model simulations for the mixed system 124Sn+112Sn at 50 AMeV bombarding energy, for impact parameters b = 6, 8, 9, and 10 fm. They compared two representative parameterizations of the symmetry energy: a soft (SKM*) form that saturates above normal density and a "superstiff" form roughly proportional to ρ². Only binary events were selected for analysis, and the isospin transport ratio was computed from the projectile-like and target-like fragments at separation time.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: 50 AMeV for the 124Sn+112Sn system
- Impact parameters studied: b = 6, 8, 9, 10 fm
- Interaction times: approximately 140, 120, 100, and 80 fm/c for b = 6, 8, 9, and 10 fm, respectively
- At b = 8 fm, about 25% of events are ternary; at b = 6 fm, one or two intermediate-mass fragments are found in more than 70% of events
- Initial isospin asymmetry of projectile (124Sn): I_P^0 = 0.192; initial isospin asymmetry of target (112Sn): I_T^0 = 0.107
- For b = 8 fm (t_c = 120 fm/c), the transferred isospin satisfies: I_PT^(asysuperstiff) > I_PT^(asysoft) > I_P^0 = 0.192 and I_TP^(asysuperstiff) > I_T^0 = 0.107 > I_TP^(asysoft)

## Context

This work extends the experimental and theoretical analysis of isospin transport in Sn-isotope collisions at MSU (Tsang et al., 2004) by explicitly including the effects of pre-equilibrium emission and the formation of a low-density neck region, which were not analyzed in those earlier studies. The results show that the isospin transport ratio depends not only on the symmetry energy stiffness but also on pre-equilibrium emission, which reduces the absolute value of the transport ratios; this contrasts with the assumptions in Tsang et al. (2004) and Chen et al. (2004). The findings enable further study of the interplay between isoscalar and isovector parts of the equation of state on isospin transport observables, with implications for neutron skin, isovector collective response in finite nuclei, and neutron star crust structures.
