# Chemical Equilibration in Hadronic Collisions

> We study chemical equilibration in out-of-equilibrium Quark-Gluon Plasma using the first principles method of QCD effective kinetic theory, accurate at weak coupling. In longitudinally expanding systems--relevant for relativistic nuclear collisions--we find that for realistic couplings chemical equilibration takes place after hydrodynamization, but well before local thermalization. We estimate that hadronic collisions with final state multiplicities ${dN_\text{ch}}/{dη}\gtrsim 10^2$ live long enough to reach approximate chemical equilibrium, which is consistent with the saturation of strangeness enhancement observed in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim
Using QCD effective kinetic theory, the authors find that for realistic couplings, chemical equilibration in longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma occurs after hydrodynamization but well before local thermalization, and they estimate that hadronic collisions with final state multiplicities above approximately 100 charged particles per unit rapidity live long enough to reach approximate chemical equilibrium.

## Method
The authors numerically solve the Boltzmann equation for homogeneous boost-invariant quark and gluon distribution functions using the QCD effective kinetic theory of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002), which includes 2-to-2 scattering processes, collinear splitting and merging, and Hard Thermal Loop in-medium screening effects. They use Color-Glass-Condensate motivated initial conditions for gluons with zero initial fermion density, and map the coupling constant to the specific shear viscosity to fix the speed of microscopic dynamics. Simulations are performed for multiple coupling values and the results are converted to physical units using universality of the scaled time variable and typical values from hydrodynamic simulations.

## Key numbers
- Chemical equilibration time: 90% of fermion equilibrium energy fraction is reached at τ ≈ 1.2 τ_R, where τ_R = 4πη/(sT) is the kinetic relaxation time.
- Hydrodynamization time: total energy density is within 10% of viscous hydrodynamic estimate at τ_hydro ≈ 0.46 τ_R.
- Thermalization time: total energy density is within 10% of ideal hydrodynamic estimate at τ_therm ≈ 2 τ_R (in units of τ_R).
- For physical conversion with η/s = 0.16, chemical equilibration occurs at τ ≈ 1.5 fm and local thermalization at τ ≈ 3.3 fm.
- Minimum multiplicity for chemical equilibration: dN_ch/dη ≳ 110 × (τ_chem/1.2τ_R)^3 × (η/s/0.16)^3 × (τ_chem/R)^(-2).

## Context
This work extends previous far-from-equilibrium studies in pure Yang-Mills theory (Kurkela 2015, Keegan 2016) and N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory to full QCD with quarks, addressing chemical equilibration for the first time in an ab initio framework. The results are consistent with the saturation of strangeness enhancement observed by ALICE (2017) across proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions at multiplicity dN_ch/dη ∼ 10^2, and enable future high-luminosity LHC studies to test whether strangeness saturation occurs at the same multiplicity across different collision systems.
