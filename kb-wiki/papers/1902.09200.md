# Longitudinal conductivity of hot magnetized collisional QCD medium in the inhomogeneous electric field

> The longitudinal current density induced by the inhomogeneous electric field in the hot magnetized quark-gluon plasma has been investigated and utilized in obtaining the conductivity of the medium. The analysis has been done in the regime where inhomogeneity of the field is small so that the collision effect could be significant. The modeling of the QCD medium is based on a quasiparticle description where the medium effects have been encoded in the effective quarks, antiquarks and gluons. The temperature dependence of the linear longitudinal current density (in terms of the electric field) and the additional components of current density due to the inhomogeneity of electric field (in terms of its derivatives) have been obtained by solving the $(1+1)-$dimensional effective covariant kinetic theory with a proper collision term. The conductivity has been obtained from the current density in the presence of the inhomogeneous field. The collisional aspects of the medium have been captured by including both thermal relaxation approximation and the Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook collision kernels in the analysis. Further, the hot QCD medium effects and higher Landau level contributions to the current density and the conductivity have been investigated. It has been seen that the effects of inhomogeneity of the field and the mean field corrections to the current density and the conductivity are more visible in the temperature regions which are not far from the transition temperature.

## Key claim

The longitudinal conductivity of a hot magnetized quark-gluon plasma is modified by the inhomogeneity of the electric field, but these effects are significant only in temperature regions not far from the transition temperature and when the inhomogeneity is small enough that collision effects cannot be neglected.

## Method

The authors solved a (1+1)-dimensional effective covariant kinetic theory with collision terms, using both the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) and the Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision kernel, to compute the longitudinal current density induced by an inhomogeneous electric field in a magnetized QCD medium. The medium was modeled using the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM), which encodes hot QCD medium effects into temperature-dependent effective fugacities for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons. The analysis included contributions from higher Landau levels (HLLs) beyond the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation, and the momentum-dependent thermal relaxation time was computed for the dominant 1→2 processes in the strong magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The LLL longitudinal conductivity in the RTA is given by σ_eff^L = (|q_f B|/(2π)) (q_f^2 N_c)/(π C_2 α_eff) (T/ln(T/m)) ((z_q+1)/(2z_q)) × (1/4) { (z_q+z_g - (z_g-z_q) ln(z_q))/(z_q m^2) }, where m_u = 3 MeV, m_d = 5 MeV, m_s = 100 MeV.
- The LLL linear current density component is J_z_(l) = (q_f^2 N_c/π) (|q_f B|/(2π)) (T/(C_2 m^2 α_eff)) (h_(l)/ln(T/m)) E minus a mean-field correction term proportional to δω.
- The LLL nonlinear current density component (due to time inhomogeneity) is J_z_(nl) = -(q_f^2 N_c/π) (|q_f B|/(2π)) (T^2/(C_2^2 m^4 α_eff^2)) (h_(nl)/(48 (ln(T/m))^2)) \dot{E} plus a mean-field correction term.
- The background electric field used for numerical calculations has spatial width σ = 4.0 fm, duration τ_E = 1.0 fm, impact parameter b = 7.2 fm, and nuclear radius R = 6.38 fm, with z = 4 fm chosen for the analysis.
- The ratio R_zz = J_z_(l) / (J_z_(l) + J_z_(nl)) was computed at |q_f B| = 10 m_π^2 considering up to 20 Landau levels.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of longitudinal conductivity in magnetized QGP by Hattori et al. (2016) and Kurian and Chandra (2017, 2018), which were limited to the LLL approximation in the regime T^2 ≪ |q_f B|. The present analysis incorporates HLL contributions and the effects of electric field inhomogeneity, which are relevant for the more realistic regime gT ≪ √(|q_f B|) encountered in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The results enable more accurate modeling of electromagnetic responses of the QGP and the late-time behavior of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collision experiments, where inhomogeneous electromagnetic fields are expected to be generated as discussed by Deng, Tuchin (2013), and Hongo et al. (2013).
