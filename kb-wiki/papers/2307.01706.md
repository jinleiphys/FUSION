# Cluster decay dynamics of Actinides yielding non-Pb-daughter

> The cluster dynamics of radioactive nuclei decaying to neighbouring daughter nuclei of the double magic $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb is investigated using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with NL3$^*$ parameter set within the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM). The novel feature of the present study is the application of the newly derived preformation formula, laying the groundwork for accessing the break-up of the Q-value: preformation energy, cluster emission energy and the recoil energy of the daughters formed. The energy associated with cluster preformation is theoretically quantified for the first time. This treatment underscores the shell effect, pairing correlation as well as the blocking of particular orbitals by unpaired nucleons. To ascertain the applicability of the new formula, the PCM based calculations are carried out with nuclear potential obtained using the phenomenological M3Y and microscopic RMF-based R3Y nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials along with corresponding densities. We found a marginal variation that can be attributed to the difference in their barrier properties, however, the predictions for the case of both M3Y and R3Y potentials are found to agree well with the experimental half-lives. Although none of the considered reaction systems yields a double magic daughter nucleus, we found that the kinematics of their cluster emissions is governed by their proximity to the shell closure. The deduced systematic of the recoil energy in cluster decays can provide valuable insight for the synthesis of elements in superheavy mass region in the future.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have theoretically quantified the energy associated with cluster preformation for the first time, using a newly derived preformation formula that breaks down the Q-value into preformation energy, cluster emission energy, and recoil energy of the daughter nuclei; however, this quantification applies specifically to systems yielding non-Pb daughters, and the authors note that none of the considered reaction systems yields a double magic daughter nucleus.

## Method

The study employs the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM) within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach using the NL3* parameter set to investigate cluster decay dynamics of actinides. Nuclear matter densities of decay fragments were obtained from RMF calculations, and the nuclear interaction potential was calculated using the double-folding technique with both the phenomenological M3Y and microscopic RMF-based R3Y nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials. The newly derived preformation formula (Eq. P0) was applied to compute preformation probabilities, and the WKB approximation was used to calculate penetration probabilities, with the neck-length parameter Delta-R set to 0.5 fm for M3Y and 1.0 fm for R3Y interactions.

## Context

This work extends the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM) originally developed by Gupta and Greiner, applying it to actinide decays that produce daughters near the double magic nuclei 132Sn and 208Pb rather than the double magic daughters themselves. The study builds on previous work by Majekodunmi et al. (2022, 2023) that established the preformation formula, and it contrasts with fission-like models by Poenaru et al. (1985, 1986, 2002, 2010) where preformation probability is taken as unity. The deduced systematic of recoil energy in cluster decays is presented as potentially valuable for future synthesis of elements in the superheavy mass region, since experimental measurement of recoil energy of daughter nuclei is beyond present-day experimental technique.
