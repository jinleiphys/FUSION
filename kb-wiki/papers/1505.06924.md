# Form factors and radii of light nuclei

> We discuss the determination of electromagnetic form factors from the {\em world} data on electron-nucleus scattering for nuclei $Z \leq 3$, with particular emphasis on the derivation of the moments required for comparison with measurements from electronic/muonic atoms and isotope shifts.

## Key claim

The paper argues that precise electromagnetic form factors and radii for light nuclei (Z ≤ 3) can be extracted from world electron scattering data, but only if the large-radius behavior of the charge density is constrained by a Whittaker function governed by the separation energy of the lightest charged constituent; without this constraint, extrapolations to q=0 are unreliable and can yield unphysical moments.

## Method

The author reanalyzes the world data on elastic electron-nucleus scattering for deuteron, ³He, ⁴He, and ⁶Li using the Sum-Of-Gaussians (SOG) parameterization, which has an analytical Fourier transform allowing simultaneous fitting in q-space and r-space. The large-r tail of the charge density is constrained to a Whittaker function determined by the separation energy of the lightest charged constituent, with the asymptotic norm for ⁴He fixed by forward dispersion relation analysis of proton-⁴He scattering. Charge and magnetic form factors are separated using forward- and backward-angle data, with tensor polarization observables (T₂₀) used for the deuteron quadrupole separation.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron charge rms-radius: 2.130 ± 0.010 fm from (e,e), compared to preliminary muonic deuterium value of 2.1289 ± 0.0012 fm
- ³He charge rms-radius: 1.973 ± 0.016 fm; magnetic rms-radius: 1.976 ± 0.047 fm
- ⁴He charge rms-radius: 1.681 ± 0.004 fm (most precise radius for any nucleus from electron scattering)
- ³He first Zemach moment ⟨r⟩₂: 2.628 ± 0.016 fm; third Zemach moment ⟨r³⟩₂: 28.15 ± 0.70 fm³
- ⁴He third Zemach moment ⟨r³⟩₂: 16.73 ± 0.10 fm³
- ⁶Li charge rms-radius: 2.589 ± 0.039 fm
- ³He-⁴He charge isotope shift δ⟨r²⟩: 1.066 ± 0.060 fm²
- ⁶He-⁴He charge isotope shift δ⟨r²⟩: 1.047 ± 0.034 fm²; matter radius shift δR_matter: 0.81 ± 0.08 fm
- ⁸He-⁴He charge isotope shift δ⟨r²⟩: 0.911 ± 0.095 fm²; matter radius shift δR_matter: 0.96 ± 0.08 fm

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analyses of electron scattering form factors (Sick 2001, 2012) and provides the reference radii needed to convert isotope shifts from laser spectroscopy (Mueller 2007, Wang 2004, Noertershaeuser 2011) and muonic atom measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2011) into absolute radii. The results enable direct comparison with ab initio Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations (Pieper 2001, Carlson 2014) and test the consistency of electron and muon interactions: the ⁴He radius agrees with muonic helium to better than 0.004 fm, in contrast to the 0.04 fm proton radius discrepancy between electron scattering and muonic hydrogen (Pohl 2010, Arrington 2015).
