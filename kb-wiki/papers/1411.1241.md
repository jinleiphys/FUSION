# In-medium antikaon interactions and bound states

## Key claim
Correct treatment of subthreshold $\bar K N$ dynamics is mandatory for kaonic-atom and $\bar K$-nuclear bound-state calculations, and in-medium chirally-based models must be supplemented by appreciable many-nucleon contributions to reproduce kaonic-atom data; $\bar K$-nuclear widths larger than 50 MeV are anticipated.

## Method
The authors use in-medium chirally-based models of $\bar K N$ interactions (specifically the Cieplý-Smejkal NLO30 and Ikeda-Hyodo-Weise NLO models) to construct subthreshold scattering amplitudes, converting their energy dependence into density dependence via self-consistent iteration of the downward energy shift $\delta\sqrt{s}$ in the nuclear medium. These in-medium amplitudes are then used as input to the Klein-Gordon equation for kaonic atoms and to few-body and many-body bound-state calculations, with many-nucleon absorptive and dispersive contributions added phenomenologically to fit global kaonic-atom data.

## Key numbers
- For $K^-$ quasibound states in Ca using NLO30 in-medium $s$-wave interactions alone: $1s_K$ binding energy $B_K = 70.5$ MeV, width $\Gamma_K = 14.9$ MeV; $1p_K$: $B_K = 50.6$ MeV, $\Gamma_K = 18.0$ MeV; $1d_K$: $B_K = 28.8$ MeV, $\Gamma_K = 30.3$ MeV; $2s_K$: $B_K = 23.9$ MeV, $\Gamma_K = 33.8$ MeV.
- Adding two-nucleon absorption estimated from kaonic-atom fits increases widths substantially: $1s_K$ $\Gamma_K = 58.9$ MeV, $1p_K$ $\Gamma_K = 53.6$ MeV, $1d_K$ $\Gamma_K = 59.7$ MeV, $2s_K$ $\Gamma_K = 67.1$ MeV, with binding energies decreased by $\lesssim 2$ MeV.
- The $I=1/2$ $\bar KNN$ ground state (`$K^-pp$') is bound by 4.3 MeV below the $N+(\bar KN)_{I=0}$ threshold at $-11.4$ MeV.
- The difference between upper-pole positions in the IHW and Révai-Shevchenko chiral models for $(\bar KN)_{I=0}$ is $\Delta E = 7$ MeV, leading to an estimated $\Delta E(K^-pp) \approx 10.5$ MeV.
- Average nucleon kinetic energy $T_N = 23.0$ MeV and average nucleon binding energy $B_N \approx 8.5$ MeV are used in the downward energy shift formula.

## Context
This work extends the program of the Prague-Jerusalem Collaboration (Cieplý, Friedman, Gal, Barnea, Mareš, Gazda) on in-medium $\bar K N$ interactions, following earlier studies by Friedman and Gal (2012, 2013) and Barnea, Gal and Liverts (2012). It builds on chiral-model amplitudes from Ikeda, Hyodo and Weise (2011) and Cieplý and Smejkal (2012), and addresses the subthreshold energy dependence that was overlooked by many previous calculations assuming $\vec p_K = 0$ in finite nuclei. The results enable more realistic predictions for $\bar K$-nuclear bound states, particularly $K^-pp$ searches as reviewed by Nagae (2014), and demonstrate that large widths (50-70 MeV) from many-nucleon absorption make distinct quasibound states unlikely except perhaps in very light $K^-$ nuclei.
