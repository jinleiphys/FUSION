# Charge-exchange dipole excitations in deformed nuclei

> Background: The electric giant-dipole resonance (GDR) is the most established collective vibrational mode of excitation. A charge-exchange analog, however, has been poorly studied in comparison with the spin (magnetic) dipole resonance (SDR). Purpose: I investigate the role of deformation on the charge-exchange dipole excitations and explore the generic features as an isovector mode of excitation. Methods: The nuclear energy-density functional method is employed for calculating the response functions based on the Skyrme--Kohn--Sham--Bogoliubov method and the proton-neuton quasiparticle-random-phase approximation. Results: The deformation splitting into $K=0$ and $K=\pm 1$ components occurs in the charge-changing channels and is proportional to the magnitude of deformation as is well known for the GDR. For the SDR, however, a simple assertion based on geometry of a nucleus cannot be applied for explaining the vibrational frequencies of each $K$-component. A qualitative argument on the strength distributions for each component is given based on the non-energy-weighted sum rules taking nuclear deformation into account. The concentration of the electric dipole strengths in low energy and below the giant resonance is found in neutron-rich unstable nuclei. Conclusions: The deformation splitting occurs generically for the charge-exchange dipole excitions as in the neutral channel. The analog pygmy dipole resonance can emerge in deformed neutron-rich nuclei as well as in spherical systems.

# Charge-exchange dipole excitations in deformed nuclei

## Key claim

Deformation splitting into $K=0$ and $K=\pm 1$ components occurs generically for charge-exchange dipole excitations as in the neutral channel, but for the spin-dipole resonance a simple geometrical argument based on nuclear shape cannot explain the vibrational frequencies of each $K$-component; the analog pygmy dipole resonance can emerge in deformed neutron-rich nuclei as well as in spherical systems.

## Method

The nuclear energy-density functional method was employed, using the Skyrme-Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov (KSB) method to describe the ground state of mother nuclei and the proton-neutron quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) to describe excited states of daughter nuclei. The KSB equation was solved in coordinate space using cylindrical coordinates with a mesh size of 0.6 fm and a box boundary condition at (14.7, 14.4) fm, assuming axial and reflection symmetry. The SkM* functional was primarily used for the normal part of the EDF, with a density-dependent pairing functional.

## Key numbers

- The $K$-splitting energy for the $\Delta T_z=-1$ giant resonance in $^{238}$U was 2.7 MeV, evaluated in the energy interval 15 MeV to 45 MeV, with mean excitation energies of 26.6 MeV ($K=0$) and 29.3 MeV ($K=1$)
- The $K$-splitting energy for the $\Delta T_z=0$ giant resonance in $^{238}$U was 2.6 MeV, with mean excitation energies of 11.3 MeV ($K=0$) and 13.9 MeV ($K=1$), evaluated in the region 5 MeV to 35 MeV
- In $^{40}$Mg, the $K$-splitting energy for the $\Delta T_z=-1$ giant resonance was 3.5 MeV, compatible with 3.8 MeV for the $\Delta T_z=0$ giant resonance; mean energies were 13.2 MeV ($K=0$) and 16.7 MeV ($K=1$) for the $\Delta T_z=-1$ mode
- The mean excitation energies for spin-dipole components in $^{154}$Sm ($^{144}$Sm) were 30.4 (33.3) MeV for $\lambda=0$, 28.8 (30.0) MeV for $\lambda=1$, and 21.4 (24.7) MeV for $\lambda=2$, evaluated in the interval 0 MeV to 50 MeV
- The Coulomb-energy shift in $^{24}$Mg was 4.61 MeV, represented by the difference in chemical potentials $\lambda_\pi - \lambda_\nu$
- The root-mean-square radius of protons and neutrons in $^{24}$Mg was 3.03 fm and 2.99 fm, respectively

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study on spherical nuclei (Yoshida, 2017) to deformed systems, investigating the role of deformation on charge-exchange dipole excitations across light to heavy nuclei. The study builds on established knowledge of the electric giant-dipole resonance (GDR) splitting in deformed nuclei, as described in standard textbooks (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975), and extends it to the charge-exchange channels. The results enable predictions for the analog pygmy dipole resonance in deformed neutron-rich nuclei and provide a framework for understanding charge-exchange responses relevant to weak processes in stellar environments and neutrino-nucleus reactions.
