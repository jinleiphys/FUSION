# Statistical Ensembles with Volume Fluctuations

> The volume fluctuations in statistical mechanics are discussed. First, the volume fluctuations in ensembles with a fixed external pressure, the so called pressure ensembles, are considered. Second, a generalization of the pressure ensembles is suggested. Namely, the statistical ensembles with the volume fluctuating according to externally given distributions are considered. Several examples and possible applications in statistical models of hadron production are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard thermodynamic equivalence of ensembles holds for mean values in the thermodynamic limit but does not extend to fluctuations, specifically the scaled variance of multiplicity distributions, and that introducing volume fluctuations through pressure ensembles or externally given volume distributions provides a framework for modeling particle number fluctuations in hadron production.

## Method

The author constructs four pressure ensembles (pressure micro-canonical, pressure canonical, pressure grand micro-canonical, and pressure grand canonical) by replacing fixed volume with fixed external pressure, then generalizes to ensembles where volume fluctuates according to externally given distributions. Analytical calculations are performed for non-relativistic Boltzmann gases and ultra-relativistic ideal gases, with the van der Waals equation of state used to examine behavior at first-order phase transitions and critical points.

## Key numbers

- For the non-relativistic ideal gas in the pressure canonical ensemble: average volume \(\overline{V} = NT/p_0\) and scaled variance of volume fluctuations \(\omega_V = T/p_0\)
- For the van der Waals equation at the critical point: \(T_{cr} = (8/27)(a/b)\), \(V_{cr} = 3Nb\), \(p_{cr} = (1/27)(a/b^2)\), and \(\omega_V \cong 2.703\,b\sqrt{N}\)
- For the ultra-relativistic gas in the grand canonical ensemble: scaled variance of particle number \(\omega_{gce} = 1\)
- For the ultra-relativistic gas in the grand micro-canonical ensemble: scaled variance of particle number \(\omega_{gmc} = 1/4\)
- For the ultra-relativistic gas in the pressure grand canonical ensemble: average volume \(\overline{V} = T/(p_0 - p(T))\) with limiting temperature \(T^* = (\pi^2 p_0)^{1/4}\)

## Context

This work extends the concept of pressure ensembles known from textbooks (Landau and Lifshitz, Tolpygo) and generalizes the alpha-ensemble framework introduced in earlier work (Ref. alpha) to volume fluctuations. The author connects to recent findings (Refs. fluc, mce1, mce2, res, exp, clt, acc) that ensemble equivalence fails for fluctuations even in the thermodynamic limit, and to the micro-canonical ensemble with volume fluctuations (Ref. MCEsVF) used to reproduce KNO-scaling in hadron collisions. The results enable systematic treatment of volume fluctuations in statistical models of hadron production, particularly for understanding multiplicity distributions in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions.
