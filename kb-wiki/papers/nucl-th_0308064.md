# Effects of Dirac sea polarization on hadronic properties - A chiral SU(3) approach

> The effect of vacuum fluctuations on the in-medium hadronic properties is investigated using a chiral SU(3) model in the nonlinear realization. The effect of the baryon Dirac sea is seen to modify hadronic properties and in contrast to a calculation in mean field approximation it is seen to give rise to a significant drop of the vector meson masses in hot and dense matter. This effect is taken into account through the summation of baryonic tadpole diagrams in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA), where the baryon self energy is modified due to interactions with both the non-strange $(σ)$ and the strange $(ζ)$ scalar fields.

## Key claim

The inclusion of baryon Dirac sea polarization via the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) in a chiral SU(3) model leads to a significant drop of vector meson masses in hot and dense matter, in contrast to mean field calculations which show only insignificant modifications; however, this effect depends on the specific treatment of the baryon self-energy through coupling to both the non-strange (sigma) and strange (zeta) scalar fields.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) model in the nonlinear realization to describe baryon octet and meson degrees of freedom, solving the field equations in both the mean field approximation (MFA) and the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). The RHA incorporates vacuum fluctuations by summing baryonic tadpole diagrams, where the baryon self-energy is modified due to interactions with both the non-strange (sigma) and strange (zeta) scalar fields, with divergences handled via dimensional regularization and counterterms. Vector meson masses were then computed from the dispersion relation using the renormalized vector meson self-energy, which separates into a Feynman (vacuum polarization) part and a density-dependent (Fermi sea) part.

- Compressibility fitted to lie between 200-300 MeV in mean field approximation

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson masses in nuclear matter using a chiral SU(3) model (Paper3, 1997; Springer, 1999) by incorporating Dirac sea polarization effects previously applied only to nuclear matter (Hartree, 2002). It contrasts with mean field calculations in the Quantum Hadrodynamics framework (Hatsuda, 1992; Hatsuda, 1993; Jeans, 1994; Caillon, 1999) which found insignificant vector meson mass modifications from Fermi sea contributions alone. The results are relevant for interpreting enhanced dilepton production in the low invariant mass regime observed in relativistic heavy ion collision experiments (HELIOS, CERES, DLS collaborations), and enable more realistic modeling of hot hyperonic matter with strange baryons.
