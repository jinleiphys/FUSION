# Unified equations of state for cold non-accreting neutron stars with Brussels-Montreal functionals. II. Pasta phases in semi-classical approximation

> We generalize our earlier work on neutron stars (arXiv:1903.04981), which assumed spherical Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust, to admit the possibility of pasta phases, i.e., non-spherical cell shapes. Full fourth-order extended Thomas-Fermi calculations using the density functional BSk24 are performed for cylindrical and plate-like cells. Unlike in our spherical-cell calculations, we do not include shell and pairing corrections, but there are grounds for expecting these corrections for pasta to be significantly smaller. It is therefore meaningful to compare the ETF pasta results with the full spherical-cell results, i.e., with shell and pairing corrections included. However, in view of the many previous studies in which shell and pairing corrections were omitted entirely, it is of interest to compare our pasta results with the ETF part of the corresponding spherical calculations. Making this latter comparison we find that as the density increases the cell shapes pass through the usual sequence sphere $\to$ cylinder $\to$ plate before the transition to the homogeneous core. The filling fractions found at the phase transitions are in close agreement with expectations based on the liquid-drop model. On the other hand, when we compare with the full spherical-cell results, we find the sequence to be sphere $\to$ cylinder $\to$ sphere $\to$ cylinder $\to$ plate. In neither case do any "inverted", i.e., bubble-like, configurations appear. We provide accurate fitting formulas to all our essential numerical results for each of the three phases, designed especially for the density range where the nonspherical shapes are expected, which enable one to capture not only the general behavior of the fitted functions, but also the differences between them in different phases.

## Key claim

The paper extends the authors' earlier spherical-cell neutron star inner crust calculations (Pearson et al., 2019) to include non-spherical "pasta" phases (cylinders and plates) using the BSk24 density functional, but omits shell and pairing corrections for the pasta phases. The sequence of shapes with increasing density depends on whether one compares pasta results to spherical results with or without those corrections: the simple sphere→cylinder→plate sequence appears only when comparing to the ETF (no shell/pairing) spherical results, while comparing to the full ETFSI+BCS spherical results yields sphere→cylinder→sphere→cylinder→plate.

## Method

Full fourth-order extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) calculations were performed for cylindrical and plate-like Wigner-Seitz cells using the BSk24 Skyrme-type energy-density functional, with nucleon density distributions parametrized by a background term plus a cluster term with a modified Fermi function. The total energy per nucleon was minimized with respect to six geometric parameters and the nucleon number A for fixed proton number Z at each mean baryon density, and the optimal Z was then selected by inspection; no shell or pairing corrections were included for the non-spherical cells.

## Key numbers

- The sphere→cylinder transition occurs at mean baryon density \(\bar{n} \approx (0.050-0.051)\) fm\(^{-3}\) when comparing pasta ETF results to spherical ETF results, and at \(\bar{n} = 0.051\) fm\(^{-3}\) when comparing to spherical ETFSI+BCS results.
- The back-transition cylinder→sphere (only seen when comparing to spherical ETFSI+BCS results) occurs at \(\bar{n} \approx 0.067\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The cylinder→plate transition occurs at \(\bar{n} \approx 0.073\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The crust-core transition density for BSk24 is \(\bar{n} = 0.0807555\) fm\(^{-3}\) from a separate Thomas-Fermi analysis (Ducoin et al., 2007), but the authors could not obtain reliable solutions above \(\bar{n} = 0.0777\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- In the pasta phases, the energy per nucleon differs at most by 0.05% from the spherical-cell values, and the pressure differs at most by 0.6%.
- The proton drip for spherical cells occurs at \(\bar{n} \approx 0.073\) fm\(^{-3}\); for cylinders it starts around \(\bar{n} = 0.077\) fm\(^{-3}\), a density where plates are energetically favored.

## Context

This work extends the unified equation-of-state calculations of Pearson et al. (2019) for the BSk24 functional by relaxing the assumption of spherical Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust, following the pasta-phase literature initiated by Ravenhall et al. (1983) and Hashimoto et al. (1984). The results disagree with the second-order ETF calculations of Martin and Urban (2015) using the same BSk24 functional, who found the sphere→cylinder transition at \(\bar{n} \simeq 0.057\) fm\(^{-3}\) and the cylinder→plate transition at \(\bar{n} \simeq 0.069\) fm\(^{-3}\). The accurate fitting formulas provided for each phase enable future studies to capture energy differences between shapes, which is essential for determining the equilibrium pasta structure in neutron star crust models.
