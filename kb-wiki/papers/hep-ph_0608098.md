# Phenomenology of the Lambda/Sigma^0 production ratio in pp collisions

> We show that the recently measured asymmetry in helicity-angle spectra of the Lambda-hyperons produced in the reaction pp -> K^+Lambda p reaction and the energy dependence of the total pp -> K^+Lambda p cross section can be explained consistently by the same Lambda p final-state interaction. Assuming that there is no final-state interaction in the Sigma^0 p channel, as suggested by the available data, we can also reproduce the energy dependence of the Lambda/Sigma^0 production ratio and, in particular, the rather large ratio observed near the reaction thresholds. The nominal ratio of the Lambda and Sigma^0 production amplitudes squared, i.e. when disregarding the final-state interaction, turns out to be about 3, which is in line with hyperon production data from proton and nuclear targets available at high energies.

## Key claim

The large near-threshold enhancement of the Λ/Σ⁰ production ratio in pp collisions can be explained primarily by the Λp final-state interaction (FSI), assuming no FSI in the Σ⁰p channel. The nominal ratio of the Λ and Σ⁰ production amplitudes squared, when disregarding FSI, is about 3, consistent with high-energy data.

## Method

The authors employed a Watson-Migdal approach, factorizing the reaction amplitude into a constant production amplitude and a Jost function representing the Λp FSI. They used Λp effective-range parameters (a = -1.8 fm, r = 2.8 fm) corresponding to α = -72.3 MeV/c and β = 212.7 MeV/c. For the Σ⁰p channel, the FSI term was omitted entirely. The production amplitudes were parametrized with an exponential energy dependence, with |ℳ₀|² = 1.89 exp(-1.3ε) μb for the Λ channel and |ℳ₀|² = 0.61 exp(-1.3ε) μb for the Σ⁰ channel.

## Key numbers

- The Λ/Σ⁰ cross section ratio is as large as 28 near threshold, as measured by Sewerin et al. at COSY.
- The ratio of the squared production amplitudes alone (disregarding FSI) is 3.1.
- At excess energy ε = 171 MeV, the calculated pp → K⁺Λp cross section is 18.5 μb, compared to the experimental value of 16.5 ± 0.4 μb from the COSY-TOF Collaboration.
- The Λp FSI produces an enhancement factor of 8 in the cross section over the COSY energy range, while the production amplitude variation is only about 20%.
- The Λp effective-range parameters used are a = -1.8 fm and r = 2.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Gasparian, Sibirtsev, Shyam, and others who attempted to describe both pp → K⁺Λp and pp → K⁺Σ⁰p reactions, but none could reproduce the energy dependence of the Λ/Σ⁰ ratio convincingly. The paper directly contradicts the interpretation of Liu et al., who attributed the near-threshold enhancement primarily to the N*(1535) resonance while neglecting Λp FSI. The results enable future tests using helicity-angle spectra for Σ⁰ production, which the COSY-TOF Collaboration has already measured at excess energies 60 < ε < 210 MeV and is currently analyzing.
