# Description of electromagnetic and favored $α$-transitions in heavy odd-mass nuclei

> We describe electromagnetic and favored α-transitions to rotational bands in odd-mass nuclei built upon a single particle state with angular momentum projection $Ω=\frac{1}{2}$ in the region $88 \le Z \le 98$. We use the particle coupled to an even-even core approach described by the Coherent State Model (CSM) and the coupled channels method to estimate partial $α$-decay widths. We reproduce the energy levels of the rotational band where favored $α$-transitions occur for 26 nuclei and predict B (E2) values for electromagnetic transitions to the bandhead using a deformation parameter and a Hamiltonian strength parameter for each nucleus, together with an effective collective charge depending linearly on the deformation parameter. Where experimental data is available, the contribution of the single particle effective charge to the total B (E2) value is calculated. The Hamiltonian describing the $α$- nucleus interaction contains two terms, a spherically symmetric potential given by the double-folding of the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction plus a repulsive core simulating the Pauli principle and a quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interaction. The $α$-decaying state is identified as a narrow outgoing resonance in this potential. The intensity of the transition to the first excited state is reproduced by the QQ coupling strength. It depends linearly both on the nuclear deformation and the square of the reduced width for the decay to the bandhead, respectively. Predicted intensities for transitions to higher excited states are in a reasonable agreement with experimental data. This formalism offers a unified description of energy levels, electromagnetic and favored $α$-transitions for known heavy odd-mass $α$-emitters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified description of energy levels, electromagnetic transitions, and favored alpha-decay fine structure in heavy odd-mass nuclei (Z=88-98) can be achieved by coupling an odd nucleon to a coherent-state-model core and using a coupled-channels approach with a quadrupole-quadrupole alpha-nucleus interaction, but only for rotational bands built upon single-particle states with angular momentum projection Omega not equal to 1/2; the special case Omega=1/2 requires a separate formalism deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors couple an odd nucleon to an even-even core described by the Coherent State Model (CSM) of quadrupole bosons, projecting the product state to good total angular momentum. They compute energy levels using a two-term Hamiltonian (a boson term and a particle-core quadrupole-quadrupole term) and predict B(E2) values from collective and single-particle contributions. For alpha decay, they solve coupled-channels equations with a double-folded M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction plus a repulsive core for the monopole potential, and a quadrupole-quadrupole coupling term; the decaying state is identified as a narrow outgoing resonance, and partial widths are extracted from the asymptotic scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* Energy levels were reproduced for 26 odd-mass alpha-emitters in the region 88 ≤ Z ≤ 98, using a deformation parameter d and Hamiltonian strength A1 fitted to available levels (about 4 levels required for reliable deformation determination).
* For the transition 9/2+ → 5/2+ in Th-229, the experimental B(E2) value is 170 ± 30 W.u.; the model predicts 117.8 W.u. using collective effective charge systematics, requiring a single-particle effective charge q0^sp = 7.004 (W.u.)^(1/2) to match experiment.
* The effective alpha-nucleus coupling strength C was fitted for 23 out of 26 decay processes to reproduce the experimental intensity Υ1; the linear fit parameters are C0 = -0.088, a_α = 0.971, σ = 0.023.
* The coupling strength C shows a linear correlation with the reduced width γ^2_Ω0: C = 10.096 γ^2_Ω + 0.037, σ = 0.021.
* A universal linear correlation between decay intensities Υ_i and excitation energies E_i was found: Υ_i = 0.017 E_i + 0.169, σ = 0.316.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels approach previously applied to even-even nuclei by Delion (2006, 2015) to odd-mass emitters, using a particle-plus-core formalism from Delion (1993). It contrasts with the multichannel cluster model of Ni (2012) for favored transitions in heavy emitters (93<Z<102) and the microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method of Ward (2015) for alpha-decay in the 84<Z<88 region. The formalism enables predictions of alpha-decay fine structure intensities and B(E2) values for odd-mass nuclei where experimental data are scarce, and demonstrates a universal decay law linking intensities to excitation energies.
