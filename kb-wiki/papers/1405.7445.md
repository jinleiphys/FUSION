# Evidence for Triangular D_3h Symmetry in 12C

> We report a measurement of a new high spin Jp = 5- state at 22.4(0.2) MeV in 12C which fits very well to the predicted (ground state) rotational band of an oblate equilateral triangular spinning top with a D_3h symmetry characterized by the sequence 0+, 2+, 3-, 4+/-, 5- with almost degenerate 4+ and 4- (parity doublet) states. Such a D_3h symmetry was observed in triatomic molecules and it is observed here for the first time in nuclear physics. We discuss a classification of other rotation-vibration bands in 12C such as the (0+) Hoyle band and the (1-) bending mode band and suggest measurements in search of the predicted ("missing") states that may shed new light on clustering in 12C and light nuclei. In particular the observation (or non-observation) of the predicted ("missing") states in the Hoyle band will allow us to conclude the geometrical arrangement of the three alpha-particle composing the Hoyle state at 7.654 MeV in 12C.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of a nuclear state with spin-parity 5- at 22.4(0.2) MeV in 12C, which completes the ground-state rotational band predicted by the U(7) algebraic cluster model for an oblate equilateral triangular configuration with D3h symmetry; the authors hedge that the identification of the D3h symmetry is conditional on the observed sequence of states (0+, 2+, 3-, 4+/-, 5-) fitting the predicted rotational trajectory, and that the classification of other bands (Hoyle band, bending mode) remains tentative pending observation of predicted "missing" states.

## Method

The experiment used the 12C(4He,3α)4He reaction with a 40 MeV 4He beam incident on a 100 μg/cm2 carbon target at the Birmingham MC40 cyclotron. Four silicon strip detectors (each 5×5 cm2, 500 μm thick, with 16×16 strips) placed at distances 11.0–13.0 cm from the target and covering θlab = 20° to 75° detected three of the four final-state α-particles; the fourth α-particle was reconstructed via energy and momentum conservation. Events where two detected α-particles originated from 8Begs decay were selected via a Dalitz plot, and the spin of the 22.4 MeV state was determined model-independently by comparing the angular correlation of the decay products to Legendre polynomials.

## Key numbers

- New state at 22.4(0.2) MeV in 12C with Jπ = 5- (spin determined by angular correlation matching |P5[cos(ψ)]|2)
- Ground-state rotational band sequence: 0+, 2+, 3-, 4+/-, 5- (parity doublet 4+ and 4- nearly degenerate)
- Hoyle state at 7.654 MeV (0+); other observed states: 9.641 MeV (3-), 10.844 MeV (1-), 14.083 MeV (4+)
- B(E2;2+ → 0+) = 4.9 ± 0.3 W.U. and B(E3;3- → 0+) = 12.9 ± 1.7 W.U. for ground-state band transitions
- Hoyle rotational band moment of inertia larger than ground-state band by a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the algebraic cluster model of Bijker and Iachello (2000, 2002), which applied the U(7) spectrum-generating algebra to three-α systems, predicting D3h symmetry in 12C analogous to that observed in triatomic molecules like H3+. The observed mixed-parity rotational structure resembles the diatomic U(4) mixed-parity structure seen in 18O (Gai et al., 1983) and the tetrahedral Td symmetry recently observed in 16O (Bijker and Iachello, 2014). The results enable future searches for predicted states in the Hoyle band (particularly 3- and 4- states) to distinguish between equilateral triangular and bent-arm configurations of the three α-particles in the Hoyle state, a question dating to the discovery of the Hoyle state itself.
