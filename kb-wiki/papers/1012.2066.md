# Identified Particle Spectra for Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV from STAR, PHENIX and BRAHMS in Comparison to Core-Corona Model Predictions

> The core-corona model describes quite successfully the centrality dependence of multiplicity and $<p_t>$ of identified particles observed in heavy ion reaction at beam energies between $\sqrt{s}$ = 17 GeV and 200 GeV. Also the centrality dependence of the elliptic flow, $v_2$, for all charged and identified particles could be explained in this model. Here we extend this analysis and study the centrality dependence of single particle spectra of identified particles. We concentrate here on protons, antiprotons, kaons and pions which have all been measured by the PHENIX, STAR and BRAHMS collaborations. We find that an analysis of the spectra in the core-corona model suffers from differences in the data published by the different experimental groups, notably for the pp collisions. For each experience the data agree well with the prediction of the core-corona model but the value of the two necessary parameters depends onthe experiments.

# Identified Particle Spectra for Au+Au Collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV from STAR, PHENIX and BRAHMS in Comparison to Core-Corona Model Predictions

## Key claim
The core-corona model can describe the centrality dependence of single particle spectra for protons, antiprotons, kaons, and pions measured by STAR, PHENIX, and BRAHMS, but only if separate parameter sets are used for each experiment because the published data from different experimental groups disagree significantly, especially for pp collisions and peripheral events.

## Method
The authors applied the core-corona model, which separates nucleons into core particles (those undergoing multiple collisions and reaching thermal equilibrium) and corona particles (those scattering only once and producing particles like in pp collisions). The corona contribution was taken as half the measured pp spectra, and the core contribution was extracted from the most central Au+Au collision data after subtracting the corona component. The model predictions for intermediate centralities were then compared to experimental spectra from STAR, PHENIX, and BRAHMS for pions, kaons, and (anti)protons at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Core fraction f_core ranges from 0.269 for the 80%-92% centrality bin to 0.911 for the 0%-5% centrality bin, based on EPOS Glauber calculations.
- The number of participants N_part for the most central bin (0%-5%) is 351.3 (EPOS), 345.8 (STAR), and 351.4 (PHENIX).
- For pions, STAR observes a 10% increase in multiplicity per participant toward the most central bin that is not seen by PHENIX.
- For pp and very peripheral AA collisions (N_part = 6.9), the pion multiplicities differ by 30% between experiments.
- The proton and antiproton multiplicity in central collisions differs by more than a factor of 2 between STAR and PHENIX, and by close to a factor of 3 for peripheral reactions.
- The inverse slope parameter T for protons varies by a factor of 2 between central and peripheral reactions.
- The PHENIX collaboration reports that 30% to 40% of observed protons and antiprotons are due to weak decay feed-down.

## Context
This work extends previous core-corona model studies by Aichelin et al. (2008, 2010) that successfully described the centrality dependence of multiplicity, mean transverse momentum, and elliptic flow v_2 for identified particles in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions. The present analysis tests whether the model can also describe single particle p_t spectra, which had been interpreted as evidence for collective radial flow. The authors find that the observed centrality dependence of average p_t for different particles is merely a consequence of the superposition of core and corona spectra, not necessarily requiring collective flow. The results enable future studies to subtract the corona contribution and isolate the core part, which may carry signals of the quark-gluon plasma. The authors note that predictions for Cu+Cu collisions follow directly from the Au+Au parameters without additional input.
