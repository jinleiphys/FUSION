# Kinetic temperature and radial flow velocity estimation using identified hadrons and light (anti-)nuclei produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We report the investigation of the kinetic freeze-out properties of identified hadrons ($π^\pm$, $K^\pm$ and $p(\bar p)$) along with light (anti-)nuclei $d (\bar d)$, $t (\bar t)$ and ${}^{3}He$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. A simultaneous fit is performed with the Blast-Wave (BW) model to the transverse momentum ({\ppt}) spectra of identified hadrons together with light (anti-)nuclei produced in $Au+Au$ collisions at {\sqrtsNN} = 7.7 -- 200 GeV at the RHIC and in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{\mathrm{s}_{_{\mathrm{NN}}}}$ = 2.76 TeV at the LHC. The energy and centrality dependence of freeze-out parameters, i.e., kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_{kin}$) and collective flow velocity $\langle β\rangle$ has been studied. It is observed that light (anti-)nuclei also participate in the collective expansion of the medium created in the collision when included in a common fit with the light hadrons. We observe a marginal rise in $T_{kin}$ and a slight decrease in $\langle β\rangle$ when compared to the values obtained from the fit to light hadrons. A similar $\langle β\rangle$ and significantly larger $T_{kin}$ is observed when a fit is performed to only protons and light (anti-)nuclei. Both, $T_{kin}$ and $\langle β\rangle$ show a weak energy dependence at most collision energies.

# Kinetic temperature and radial flow velocity estimation using identified hadrons and light (anti-)nuclei produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

When light (anti-)nuclei (deuterons, tritons, helium-3) are included in a simultaneous Blast-Wave fit with identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons), the extracted kinetic freeze-out temperature shows a marginal rise and the average radial flow velocity shows a slight decrease compared to fits using only light hadrons; however, the authors note that the Blast-Wave model is only a simplified hydrodynamic approach with certain limitations, and caution that strong conclusions should be avoided.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous Blast-Wave (BW) model fits to the transverse momentum (p_T) spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and their antiparticles together with light (anti-)nuclei (d, anti-d, t, anti-t, and He-3) in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7-200 GeV from RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV from LHC. The velocity profile exponent n was fixed to 1 for both collision systems to test universality, leaving only temperature T_kin, average radial flow velocity <beta>, and normalization as free parameters. The fits used the MINUIT package within the ROOT framework, with p_T fit ranges of 0.5-1.3 GeV/c for pions, 0.25-1.4 GeV/c for kaons, and 0.4-1.3 GeV/c for protons, while light (anti-)nuclei were fitted up to their maximum available p_T range.

## Key numbers

* In central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV, the simultaneous fit to pions, kaons, protons, deuterons, and tritons (Set A) yielded T_kin = 116 +/- 3 MeV and <beta> = 0.465 +/- 0.003
* In central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV, the fit to only protons and light (anti-)nuclei (Set B) gave T_kin = 129 +/- 9 MeV and <beta> = 0.464 +/- 0.017
* In central (0-10%) Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the simultaneous fit to pions, kaons, protons, deuterons, and He-3 (Set A) yielded T_kin = 115 +/- 3 MeV and <beta> = 0.673 +/- 0.003
* In central (0-10%) Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the fit to only protons and light (anti-)nuclei (Set B) gave T_kin = 149 +/- 7 MeV and <beta> = 0.633 +/- 0.08
* In central (0-10%) Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, when fixing the velocity profile to match Set A (Set B'), T_kin was reduced to 132 +/- 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Blast-Wave analyses of identified hadrons at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2014) and LHC (ALICE Collaboration) by including light (anti-)nuclei in simultaneous fits, building on earlier studies that fitted only hadrons or only light nuclei separately. The results enable comparison between the coalescence model, where nuclei form at or after kinetic freeze-out, and the statistical hadronization model, where nuclei form near the phase boundary. The finding that including only protons with light (anti-)nuclei yields a surprisingly high T_kin (129 +/- 9 MeV at RHIC, 149 +/- 7 MeV at LHC) requires further investigation and may relate to pre-hadronic multiquark states as discussed in previous work (Andronic et al., 2018; ALICE Collaboration, 2022).
