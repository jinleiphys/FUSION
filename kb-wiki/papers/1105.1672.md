## Context
This work extends the analysis of anomalous Œ•(1S,2S)œÄ+œÄ‚àí production first reported by Belle (Chen et al., 2008) and addresses defects in the tetraquark interpretation proposed by Ali et al. (2009, 2010), which the authors show fails to simultaneously fit the dipion invariant mass and cosŒ∏ distributions for the Œ•(2S) channel. The final-state interaction approach via B(*)BÃÖ(*) loops was previously explored by Meng et al. (2007, 2008) and Simonov (2008), but those works did not provide explicit fits to the line shapes. The present work introduces interference between direct dipion emission (using the QCD multipole expansion framework of Yan and Kuang, 1981) and rescattering contributions, enabling a description of both the anomalous rates and the differential distributions. The remaining discrepancy in the Œ•(2S) cosŒ∏ distribution motivates further theoretical work and more precise measurements from Belle-II and SuperB.
