# Dipion invariant mass distribution of the anomalous $Υ(1S) π^{+} π^{-}$ and $Υ(2S) π^{+} π^{-}$ production near the peak of $Υ(10860)$

> Considering the defects of the previous work for estimating the anomalous production rates of $e^+e^-\to Υ(1S)π^+π^-,\,Υ(2S)π^+π^-$ near the peak of the $Υ(5S)$ resonance at $\sqrt s=10.87$ GeV [K.F. Chen {\it et al}. (Belle Collaboration), Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 100}, 112001 (2008)], we suggest a new scenario where the contributions from the direct dipion transition and the final state interactions interfere to result in not only the anomalously large production rates, but also the lineshapes of the differential widths consistent with the experimental measurement when assuming the reactions are due to the dipion emission of $Υ(5S)$. At the end, we raise a new puzzle that the predicted differential width $dΓ(Υ(5S)\toΥ(2S)π^+π^-)/d\cosθ$ has a discrepant trend from the data while other predictions are well in accord with the data. It should be further clarified by more accurate measurements carried by future experiments.

# Dipion invariant mass distribution of the anomalous Υ(1S)π+π− and Υ(2S)π+π− production near the peak of Υ(10860)

## Key claim
The anomalously large production rates of e+e− → Υ(1S)π+π− and Υ(2S)π+π− near the Υ(5S) resonance at √s = 10.87 GeV can be explained by the interference between direct dipion emission and final-state interactions via B(*)B̅(*) meson loops, rather than by a tetraquark interpretation alone. However, the model fails to reproduce the cosθ angular distribution for the Υ(2S)π+π− channel, leaving a new puzzle for future experiments.

## Method
The authors construct a decay amplitude for Υ(5S) → Υ(nS)π+π− (n=1,2) that includes a direct dipion transition term (parameterized via the Novikov-Shifman formalism with S-wave and D-wave contributions) and a sum over rescattering amplitudes mediated by intermediate scalar resonances σ(600), f0(980), and tensor f2(1270). The rescattering amplitudes are computed using effective Lagrangians for ΥB(*)B̅(*) and SB(*)B̅(*) couplings, with loop integrals evaluated via a monopole form factor (Λ = mE + αΛQCD, α=2). The free parameters (F, κ, coupling constants, and phase angles φR) are fitted to Belle data on partial widths and differential distributions using the MINUIT package.

## Key numbers
- Γ(Υ(5S) → Υ(1S)π+π−) = 0.59 ± 0.04(stat) ± 0.09(syst) MeV (Belle data)
- Γ(Υ(5S) → Υ(2S)π+π−) = 0.85 ± 0.07(stat) ± 0.16(syst) MeV (Belle data)
- Fitted integrated width for Υ(5S) → Υ(1S)π+π−: 0.54 MeV (from model)
- Fitted integrated width for Υ(5S) → Υ(2S)π+π−: 0.845 MeV (from model)
- Direct transition parameter F = 0.186 ± 0.061 (Υ(1S) channel) and F = 2.315 ± 1.904 (Υ(2S) channel)
- κ parameter = 0.459 ± 0.084 (Υ(1S) channel) and 0.572 ± 0.283 (Υ(2S) channel)
- gσππ = 16.2 GeV−1, gf0ππ = 2.40 GeV−1 (couplings to dipion)
- Masses: mΥ(5S) = 10.870 GeV, mΥ(1S) = 9.460 GeV, mΥ(2S) = 10.024 GeV, mσ = 0.526 GeV, mf0(980) = 0.980 GeV, mf2 = 1.275 GeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of anomalous Υ(1S,2S)π+π− production first reported by Belle (Chen et al., 2008) and addresses defects in the tetraquark interpretation proposed by Ali et al. (2009, 2010), which the authors show fails to simultaneously fit the dipion invariant mass and cosθ distributions for the Υ(2S) channel. The final-state interaction approach via B(*)B̅(*) loops was previously explored by Meng et al. (2007, 2008) and Simonov (2008), but those works did not provide explicit fits to the line shapes. The present work introduces interference between direct dipion emission (using the QCD multipole expansion framework of Yan and Kuang, 1981) and rescattering contributions, enabling a description of both the anomalous rates and the differential distributions. The remaining discrepancy in the Υ(2S) cosθ distribution motivates further theoretical work and more precise measurements from Belle-II and SuperB.
