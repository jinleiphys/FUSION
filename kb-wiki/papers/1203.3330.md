# Analysis of the Reaction: $np \rightarrow np π^+ π^-$ from the Point of View of Oper-Model

> The reaction $np \rightarrow np π^+ π^-$ was studied at the various momenta of incident neutrons. It was shown that the characteristics of the reaction at the momenta above 3 GeV/c could be described by the model of reggeized $π$ exchange (OPER). At the momenta below 3 GeV/c, it was necessary to use additionally the mechanism of one baryon exchange (OBE).

## Key claim

The reaction \(np \rightarrow np \pi^+ \pi^-\) can be described by the reggeized pion exchange (OPER) model at incident neutron momenta above 3 GeV/c, but at momenta below 3 GeV/c the one baryon exchange (OBE) mechanism must be added; the OPER model alone is insufficient for the full kinematic region even at higher momenta, requiring additional diagrams for inelastic \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi \pi N\) scattering and pomeron exchange.

## Method

The reaction was studied using data from a 1 m hydrogen bubble chamber with quasimonochromatic neutron beams (\(\delta P < 2.5\%\)) at eight incident momenta between 1.25 and 5.20 GeV/c. The OPER model was applied with three free parameters (Regge slope \(\alpha_\pi'\), form-factor parameter \(R_2^2\), and parameter \(c\)), using amplitudes of elastic \(\pi N\)-scattering from partial wave analysis and inelastic \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi \pi N\) amplitudes. At momenta below 3 GeV/c, OBE diagrams with baryon exchange and nonlinear trajectories were added, with vertex functions calculated from elastic \(np\) scattering and \(\Delta N\) scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Incident neutron momenta studied: \(P_0 = 1.25, 1.43, 1.73, 2.23, 3.10, 3.83, 4.10, 5.20\) GeV/c
- Neutron beam momentum spread: \(\delta P < 2.5\%\)
- Regge slope of pion trajectory: \(\alpha_\pi' = 0.7\) GeV\(^{-2}\)
- Form-factor parameter: \(R_2^2 = 3.3\) GeV\(^{-2}\)
- Parameter \(c\) in \(\kappa^2\) for 2\(\times\)2 diagrams: \(c = 2m_\pi/(\sqrt{s} - 2m_N)\)
- Coupling constant for \(N\pi N\) vertex: \(G^2/4\pi = 14.6\)
- Energy scale factor: \(s_0 = 1\) GeV\(^2\)
- The OPER model describes energies in the range 2 to 200 GeV
- Interference between main diagrams is small, not exceeding a few percent at \(P_0 < 10\) GeV/c
- Contribution of "hanged" diagrams to cross-sections is negligible at \(P_0 < 10\) GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the reggeized pion exchange (OPER) model developed at ITEP (Ponomarev, year not given) to the reaction \(np \rightarrow np \pi^+ \pi^-\), which had previously been applied to other inelastic \(NN\), \(N\bar N\), and \(\pi N\) interactions. The study uses data from the LHEP JINR bubble chamber program and shows that at lower momenta (below 3 GeV/c) the OBE model (suggested by an author cited as OBE, year not given) must supplement the OPER framework. The results enable future application of the OPER model to other reactions such as \(np \rightarrow pp \pi^-\), \(np \rightarrow pp \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^-\), and \(np \rightarrow np e^+ e^-\) for the HADES experiment, as well as reactions with polarized particles.
