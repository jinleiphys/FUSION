# Sideward Peak of Intermediate Mass Fragments in High Energy Proton Induced Reactions

> Intermediate mass fragment (IMF) formation in the 12 GeV proton induced reaction on Au target is analyzed by using a combined framework of a transport model (JAM/MF) and a newly developed non-equilibrium percolation (NEP) model. In this model, we can well reproduce the mass distribution of fragments. In addition, the sideward peaked angular distribution would emerge under the condition that the fragment formation time is very short, around 20 fm/c. Within this short time period, the un-heated part of the residual nucleus is kept to have doughnut shape, then the Coulomb repulsion from this shape strengthens the sideward peak of IMF.

## Key claim

The sideward peaked angular distribution of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) in 12 GeV proton induced reactions on Au emerges only under the condition that the fragment formation time is very short, around 20 fm/c, where the un-heated part of the residual nucleus retains a doughnut shape and Coulomb repulsion from this shape strengthens the sideward peak.

## Method

The authors combined a transport model (JAM/MF, which incorporates hadron-string cascade processes from JAM and a mean field verified in Quantum Molecular Dynamics) with a newly developed Non-Equilibrium Percolation (NEP) model. The NEP model takes nucleon phase space information from the transport model as initial conditions, using position-dependent bond breaking probabilities based on local excitation energy rather than assuming uniform nucleon distribution. Fragments are identified as bond-connected nucleons after breaking bonds according to a probability function that depends on local excitation energy at the nucleon pair center-of-mass position.

## Key numbers

- Fragment formation time scale of approximately 20 fm/c is required for the sideward peak to emerge
- Bond breaking probability parameterized as p_b(i,j) = 1 - exp[-nu * E*(R_ij)] with nu = 600 (1/MeV)
- Cutoff distance R_cut = 2.1 fm for bond breaking when excitation energy E* >= E_0(A_res) where E_0 = 8 * c_1 * A_res MeV with c_1 = 0.2
- Gaussian smoothing parameter a = 2.0 fm for local excitation energy calculation
- Kinetic energy threshold e = 45.0 MeV below which nucleons contribute zero local excitation
- Switching times t_sw = 15, 20, and 30 fm/c were tested for the transition from transport model to NEP
- Impact parameter range b <= 7.5 fm used in transport simulations

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of sideward peaked IMF angular distributions observed in KEK experiments with 12 GeV protons on Au and in BNL-AGS experiments with protons above 10 GeV, which contradict the forward-peaked expectation from statistical fragmentation of an equilibrated spherical residual nucleus. The authors extend previous percolation models (Perc1, Perc2, Perc3, Perc4, Perc5) that successfully described mass distributions but assumed isotropic emission, and they contrast their dynamical approach with proposed mechanisms including shock wave propagation, strange shaped nucleus formation (Bubble1, Bubble2), and recoil effects (AGS). The combined JAM/MF+NEP framework enables future studies of non-equilibrium fragmentation in light-ion induced reactions where collective flow effects are smaller than in heavy-ion collisions.
