# Quark Flavor Separation in $Λ$-Baryon Fragmentation

> It is shown that neutrino and antineutrino deep inelastic scattering of unpolarized and polarized $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ productions, can provide a clean separation of unpolarized and polarized fragmentation functions of a quark into a $Λ$, for both light-flavor quarks and antiquarks and also for strange quarks. Combining with $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ productions in polarized electron deep inelastic scattering, one can systematically measure or check the various flavor and spin dependent fragmentation functions. Such measurements can provide crucial tests of different predictions concerning the spin structure of hadrons and the quark-antiquark asymmetry of the nucleon sea.

## Key claim

Neutrino and antineutrino deep inelastic scattering (DIS) with unpolarized and polarized Λ and anti-Λ production can provide a clean separation of unpolarized and polarized fragmentation functions for light-flavor quarks, antiquarks, and strange quarks, but only if one accounts for Cabibbo-suppressed processes and the fact that the antiquark contribution of the target cannot be safely neglected at large y for certain processes.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the longitudinal polarization of Λ and anti-Λ produced in the current fragmentation region of neutrino and antineutrino charged-current DIS on an unpolarized nucleon target. By measuring these polarizations and the corresponding unpolarized cross sections at y ≈ 1, and by applying u-d flavor symmetry and matter-antimatter symmetry for the fragmentation functions, one can extract the four independent light-flavor fragmentation functions (D_q^Λ, D_anti-q^Λ, ΔD_q^Λ, ΔD_anti-q^Λ). The strange fragmentation functions D_s^Λ and ΔD_s^Λ can then be determined from the remaining processes, or by combining with polarized electron DIS data to obtain six independent equations.

## Key numbers

* The Λ → p π- decay mode has a branching ratio of 64%, enabling self-analyzing polarization measurements.
* For process ν N → μ- Λ X at y ≈ 1, the predicted Λ polarization is 0.14 ± 0.04 for case I and 0.09 ± 0.04 for case II in Jaffe (1996), versus approximately 0.02 in the baryon-meson fluctuation model of Brodsky and Ma (1996).
* For process ν N → μ- anti-Λ X at y ≈ 1, the predicted anti-Λ polarization is 0.06 ± 0.02 for case I and 0.12 ± 0.04 for case II in Jaffe (1996), versus 0 in the baryon-meson fluctuation model of Brodsky and Ma (1996).

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals by Jaffe (1996) and Kotzinian (1997) for measuring Λ fragmentation functions, which relied on u-quark dominance assumptions that the authors argue are unsafe at large z. It provides a systematic alternative to the e+e- annihilation method on the Z0 resonance proposed by Burkardt and Jaffe (1993). The results enable tests of predictions for the spin structure of hadrons from Brodsky and collaborators (1988, 1996) and the quark-antiquark asymmetry of the nucleon sea from Brodsky and Ma (1996, 1997), and can help measure the strange and antistrange polarizations of the proton.
