# Strangeness Production via Parton Cascade

> We study pre-equilibrium strangeness production at RHIC energies in a new parton cascade. Starting with the turbulent glue HIJING initial conditions we investigate the interplay between mini-jet and soft beam jet gluons for strangeness production prior to hadronization, and show the importance of soft beam jet gluons in the strangeness production.

# Strangeness Production via Parton Cascade

## Key claim
Pre-equilibrium strangeness production at RHIC energies is studied using a new parton cascade code that does not assume local thermal equilibrium; the authors show that chemical equilibrium is not achieved, contrary to simple rate-equation estimates, and that soft beam jet gluons contribute significantly to strangeness production, producing a strangeness-to-gluon ratio about four times larger than minijet gluons alone.

## Method
The authors developed a parton cascade code using on-shell particles with real parton scattering cross sections, regulated by a medium-generated screening mass rather than a lower momentum cutoff. Initial conditions were taken from HIJING output, with 1000 soft gluons per unit rapidity bin at a temperature parameter around 250 MeV to account for beam energy loss at RHIC. The time evolution of strangeness and gluon content was monitored in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, comparing cases with only minijet gluons versus minijet plus soft beam jet gluons.

## Key numbers
- Screening mass for minijet gluons only: μ ≈ 4.5 fm⁻¹, using α = 0.4 and minijet rapidity density N_G/(2Y) = 300
- Screening mass for minijet plus soft gluons: μ ≈ 7 fm⁻¹, using α = 0.4 and rapidity density N_G/(2Y) = 1300
- Chemical equilibrium ratio n_s/n_g = 9/16 = 0.5625 (quantum statistics) or 3/4 = 0.75 (classical)
- Soft gluons plus minijet gluons produce a strangeness-to-gluon ratio approximately 4 times larger than minijet gluons alone
- Strangeness content increases rapidly before 4 fm, freezes out after 6 fm
- Production rate exceeds annihilation rate before 5 fm; after 5 fm the system cannot be considered thermal

## Context
This work extends earlier investigations of strangeness production from HIJING minijet initial conditions using rate equations in a 1-dimensional expanding system (prior work by the same group), which also found that chemical equilibrium was not reached. It contrasts with the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) of Geiger, which used virtual particles and a lower momentum cutoff rather than a screening mass. The results indicate that pre-equilibrium strangeness production at RHIC is insufficient to reach chemical equilibrium, and that future work must include inelastic scatterings and hadronization effects to fully address the strangeness signal.
