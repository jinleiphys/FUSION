# Nucleon strangeness form factors from N_f=2+1 clover fermion lattice QCD

> We present the N_f=2+1 clover fermion lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon strangeness form factors. We evaluate disconnected insertions using the Z(4) stochastic method, along with unbiased subtractions from the hopping parameter expansion. We find that increasing the number of nucleon sources for each configuration improves the signal significantly. We obtain G_M^s(0) = -0.017(25)(07), where the first error is statistical, and the second is the uncertainties in Q^2 and chiral extrapolations. This is consistent with experimental values, and has an order of magnitude smaller error.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first direct full QCD lattice calculation of nucleon strangeness form factors using disconnected insertions, finding that the strangeness magnetic moment is negative and consistent with experimental values but with an order of magnitude smaller error, though the result carries significant statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed N_f=2+1 clover fermion lattice QCD simulations on 16^3 x 32 lattices with lattice spacing a^-1 = 1.625 GeV, using CP-PACS/JLQCD configurations at three values of the light quark hopping parameter corresponding to pion masses of 0.60, 0.70, and 0.84 GeV. Disconnected insertions were evaluated using the Z(4) stochastic method with 600-800 noise vectors per configuration, combined with unbiased subtractions from hopping parameter expansion up to order (kappa D)^4 and multiple nucleon sources (64-82 per configuration) to improve the signal-to-noise ratio.

## Key numbers

* G_M^s(0) = -0.017(25)(07), where the first error is statistical and the second is systematic from Q^2 and chiral extrapolation uncertainties
* At Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2: G_M^s = -0.015(23) and G_E^s = 0.0022(19)
* For the heaviest quark mass (kappa_ud = 0.13760, m_pi = 0.84 GeV): G_M^s = -0.0096(29) at n=1 momentum (|q| = 0.64 GeV)
* For the lightest quark mass (kappa_ud = 0.13825, m_pi = 0.60 GeV): G_M^s = -0.0109(41) at n=1 momentum
* Electric Sachs radius: <r_s^2>_E = -2.4(15) x 10^{-3} fm^2
* Magnetic Sachs radius: <r_s^2>_M = -7.4(71) x 10^{-3} fm^2

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice QCD calculations of strangeness form factors, which gave G_M^s(0) = -0.36(20) from the first quenched calculation by the Kentucky group and G_M^s(0.1 GeV^2) = 0.05(6) from another quenched study, by providing the first direct disconnected insertion calculation in full QCD with dynamical fermions. The results are consistent with global experimental analyses from parity-violating electron scattering experiments (SAMPLE, A4, HAPPEX, G0) but with significantly smaller uncertainties, and enable tighter theoretical constraints on related quantities such as the axial form factor G_A^s and electroweak radiative corrections including the nucleon anapole moment.
