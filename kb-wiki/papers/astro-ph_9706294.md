# Nuclear Level Density and the Determination of Thermonuclear Rates for Astrophysics

> The prediction of cross sections for nuclei far off stability is crucial in the field of nuclear astrophysics. We discuss the model mostly employed for such calculations: the statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach). Special emphasis is put on the uncertainties arising from nuclear level density descriptions and an improved global description is presented. Furthermore, criteria for the applicability of the statistical model are investigated and a "map" for the applicability of the model to reactions of stable and unstable nuclei with neutral and charged particles is given.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved global parametrization of nuclear level densities within the back-shifted Fermi-gas formalism that reduces the average cross section uncertainty to about 40% from previous factors of 2-3, but this improvement depends on using microscopic corrections from the FRDM mass formula and the energy-dependent level density parameter approach of Ignatyuk (1975, 1978).

## Method

The authors employed the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model with a back-shifted Fermi-gas description of nuclear level densities, using an energy-dependent level density parameter a(U,Z,N) based on microscopic corrections from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) mass formula by Moller et al. (1995). They determined the backshift delta from pairing gaps calculated via binding energy differences of neighboring nuclei, and fitted the three free parameters alpha, beta, and gamma to experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacings of 272 nuclei at the neutron separation energy. The level density description was combined with a constant temperature formula at low excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- Averaged ratio g = 1.48 between calculated and experimental level densities for 272 nuclei in the mass range 20 ≤ A ≤ 245, using FRDM microscopic corrections with parameters α=0.1337, β=-0.06571, γ=0.04884
- Using the Hilf et al. (1976) mass formula instead gave g = 2.08 with parameters α=0.0987, β=0.09659, γ=0.05368
- Using the ETFSI mass formula gave g = 1.61 with parameters α=0.12682, β=-0.03652, γ=0.045
- The new level density description reduced average cross section uncertainty to a factor of about 1.4 (40% average deviation from experiments), compared to previous uncertainties of a factor 2-3
- Pairing gaps were calculated as Δ_even-even = 12/√A MeV, Δ_odd = 0, Δ_odd-odd = -12/√A MeV

## Context

This work extends previous statistical model calculations by Thielemann et al. (1987) and the compilations by Holmes et al. (1976) and Woosley et al. (1978), which had level density uncertainties of factors 3-4 or even exceeding 10. The improved level density description enables more reliable predictions of thermonuclear reaction rates for nuclei far from stability, particularly for r-process and p-process nucleosynthesis calculations. The authors also provide a "map" of the nuclear chart indicating where the statistical model is applicable for neutron-, proton-, and alpha-induced reactions, which was not systematically available before.
