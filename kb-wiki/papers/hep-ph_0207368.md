# Neutron charge form factor and quadrupole deformation of the nucleon

> A quark model relation between the neutron charge form factor and the N->Delta charge quadrupole form factor is used to predict the C2/M1 ratio in the N->Delta transition from the elastic neutron form factor data. Excellent agreement with the electro-pionproduction data is found, indicating the validity of the suggested relation. The implication of the negative C2/M1 ratio for the intrinsic deformation of the nucleon is discussed.

# Neutron charge form factor and quadrupole deformation of the nucleon

## Key claim
A quark model relation connecting the neutron charge form factor to the N→Δ charge quadrupole form factor predicts the C2/M1 ratio from elastic neutron form factor data, and this prediction agrees with electro-pionproduction data, but the relation relies on the assumption that two-body exchange currents dominate over one- and three-body operators for both observables, and that SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry holds for the N and Δ wave functions.

## Method
The author uses a constituent quark model with two-body exchange currents to derive a relation between the neutron charge form factor G_{C0}^n(q^2) and the N→Δ charge quadrupole form factor G_{C2}^{p→Δ+}(q^2). Together with the SU(6) relation for the magnetic dipole form factor, this yields the C2/M1 ratio for the N→Δ transition. The low-q expansion of this relation further expresses the N→Δ quadrupole moment and transition radius in terms of the neutron charge radius r_n^2 and its fourth moment r_n^4.

## Key numbers
- Neutron charge radius: r_n^2 = -0.113(3) fm^2 (experimental, from Gra01)
- Neutron charge fourth moment: r_n^4 = -0.32(8) fm^4 (experimental, from Gra01)
- Predicted N→Δ quadrupole moment: Q_{p→Δ+} = -0.080(2) fm^2
- Predicted N→Δ quadrupole transition radius squared: r^2_{p→Δ+} = -0.84(21) fm^2

## Context
This work extends earlier quark model studies by Buchmann (1997, 2000) and Henley et al. (2001) that established a connection between the neutron charge form factor and nucleon deformation. The predicted C2/M1 ratio agrees with electro-pionproduction data from Gra01 and Got01. The results enable a determination of the intrinsic quadrupole deformation of the nucleon from elastic form factor measurements, showing a prolate proton and oblate Δ+ in both the quark model and the pion cloud model of Hen62, with the same qualitative results also obtained in the Bohr-Mottelson collective model by Hen01.
