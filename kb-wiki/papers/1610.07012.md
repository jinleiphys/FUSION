# Probing the Source of Proton Mass by"Unbreaking" Scale-Chiral Symmetry

> I describe a possible scenario for the origin of proton mass in terms of Cheshire Cat, half-skyrmions, topology change and interplay between hidden chiral-scale symmetry and induced local symmetry. This differs from the standard constituent-quark scenario. As the baryonic matter density is increased toward the vector manifestation (VM) fixed-point at which the $ρ$ mass is to vanish, the effective in-medium mass ratio $m^*_ρ/m^*_N$ is to tend to zero proportionally to $g^*_ρ$ where $g^*_ρ$ is the in-medium hidden gauge coupling constant. I develop the thesis that the intricacy involved in the mass generation could be decoded from experiments at RIB accelerators and massive compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the proton mass may originate from a combination of topology change (skyrmion to half-skyrmion transition) and hidden scale-chiral symmetry, rather than from the standard constituent-quark (Nambu) mechanism. The author hedges that this scenario is a "possible" one and that the intricacy involved "could be decoded" from experiments, not that it is established.

## Method

The author develops a theoretical framework combining the Cheshire Cat phenomenon (where baryon charge is partitioned between a chiral bag interior and an exterior skyrmion), hidden local symmetry for vector mesons, and hidden scale symmetry for a dilaton scalar. Skyrmions are placed on a crystal lattice and squeezed to simulate increasing baryonic density, revealing a transition to half-skyrmions at approximately twice nuclear matter density. The effective Lagrangian used is a scale-invariant hidden local symmetry (sHLS) model, later extended to include baryons (bsHLS), with parity-doubled nucleon fields.

## Key numbers

- The proton mass is measured as 938.272046 ± 0.000021 MeV, with more than 99.9% of visible mass accounted for by nucleon number and less than 1% from binding energy.
- Lattice QCD with no mass terms (QCDLite) predicts approximately 95% of the proton mass.
- The transition density from skyrmions to half-skyrmions is estimated at approximately 2n₀, where n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density.
- In the half-skyrmion phase, the quark condensate averaged over the crystal lattice (Σ) vanishes, while the nucleon mass follows m_N = m₀ + Δm(Σ) with Δm → 0 as Σ → 0 at n → n₁/₂, where m₀ can be as large as (0.7–0.9) m_N.
- The ρ meson mass in medium scales as m_ρ^* ∝ g_ρ^* → 0 at the vector manifestation fixed point, with the ratio m_ρ^*/m_N^* tending to zero proportionally to g_ρ^*.

## Context

This work extends the Cheshire Cat picture of Rho, Goldhaber, and Brown (1983) and the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) to the problem of proton mass generation. It contrasts with the standard constituent-quark scenario of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961), where the nucleon mass arises from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the ratio m_ρ/m_N ≈ 2/3 is expected. The paper suggests that experiments at rare isotope beam (RIB) accelerators and observations of massive compact stars could probe whether the topology-change scenario with parity doubling and vector manifestation is realized in nature.
