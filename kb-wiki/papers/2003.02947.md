# Lepton Pair Production Through Two Photon Process in Heavy Ion Collisions

> This paper investigates the electromagnetic production of lepton pairs with low transverse momentum in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We estimate the initial photons' transverse momentum contributions by employing models where the average transverse momentum squared of the incoming photon can be calculated in the equivalent photon approximation. We further derive an all order QED resummation for the soft photon radiation, which gives an excellent description of the ATLAS data in ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC. For peripheral and central collisions, additional $p_T$-broadening effects from multiple interaction with the medium and the magnetic field contributions from the quark-gluon plasma are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the transverse momentum broadening observed in lepton pair production in heavy ion collisions can be explained by a combination of the intrinsic transverse momentum of the incoming photons (which depends only mildly on impact parameter) and QED Sudakov resummation, with additional medium-induced broadening from the quark-gluon plasma needed to describe peripheral and central collisions.

## Method

The authors employ the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) to model the photon flux from relativistic nuclei, extending it to include impact-parameter-dependent photon distributions and a generalized EPA using photon Wigner distributions that contain both transverse momentum and impact parameter information. They derive an all-order QED Sudakov resummation for soft photon radiation and apply the Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS) formalism to estimate medium-induced pT-broadening of the produced leptons, comparing their calculations to ATLAS data from ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) and discussing implications for peripheral and central collisions.

## Key numbers

- For lead nuclei at the LHC, the nuclear radius is taken as R_A = 7 fm with a form factor parameter a = 0.7 fm
- The average transverse momentum squared for incoming photons scales as ⟨k_T²⟩ ∝ 1/b_⊥², with the relation breaking down when xm_p becomes comparable to 1/b_⊥ at x = 10⁻²
- The two-photon interaction rate peaks around impact parameter b_⊥ ∼ 3R_A before decreasing at larger impact parameters
- The ATLAS UPC data shows a tail containing about 1% of events with relatively high acoplanarity, which the Sudakov resummation describes well

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dijet azimuthal correlations in heavy ion collisions (Mueller 2016, Chen 2016-2018) to dilepton angular correlations, noting that the QED case is simpler due to the smaller coupling constant. It builds on the STARLIGHT simulation framework (Klein 2016, 2018) and the BDMPS formalism for medium-induced broadening (Baier 1996-1998). The results enable future experimental studies at both the LHC and RHIC to quantitatively probe the electromagnetic properties of the quark-gluon plasma, complementing QCD jet pT-broadening measurements.
