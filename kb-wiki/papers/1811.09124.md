# Exclusive $ρ$ and $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral $pA$ collisions: Predictions of the gluon saturation models for the momentum transfer distributions

> In this letter we complement previous studies on exclusive vector meson photoproduction in hadronic collisions presenting a comprehensive analysis of the $t$ - spectrum measured in exclusive $ρ$ and $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in $pA$ collisions at the LHC. We compute the differential cross sections considering two phenomenological models for the gluon saturation effects and present predictions for $pPb$ and $pCa$ collisions. Moreover, we compare our predictions with the recent preliminary CMS data for the exclusive $ρ$ photoproduction. We demonstrate that the gluon saturation models are able to describe the CMS data at small - $t$. On the other hand, the models underestimate the few data point at large -- $t$. Our results indicate that future measurements of the large -- $t$ region can be useful to probe the presence or absence of a dip in the $t$ -- spectrum and discriminate between the different approaches to the gluon saturation effects.

# Exclusive ρ and J/Ψ photoproduction in ultraperipheral pA collisions: Predictions of the gluon saturation models for the momentum transfer distributions

## Key claim
The bCGC and IP-Sat gluon saturation models describe the preliminary CMS data for exclusive ρ photoproduction in pPb collisions at small |t| (below ~0.4 GeV²), but both models underestimate the few data points at large |t|, suggesting that the spatial distribution of gluons in the proton may need improvement.

## Method
The authors computed differential cross sections for exclusive ρ and J/Ψ photoproduction in pA collisions using the color dipole formalism, where the amplitude factorizes into a photon fluctuation into a qqbar dipole, dipole-proton scattering, and recombination into the vector meson. They employed two phenomenological models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude: the impact parameter Color Glass Condensate (bCGC) model and the IP-Sat model, both fitted to HERA data. The cross sections were calculated by convolving the nuclear photon flux (using a point-like form factor) with the γp → V p differential cross section, and predictions were made for pPb and pCa collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- For pPb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV, the bCGC and IP-Sat predictions for the t-spectra are similar at small |t| but differ at larger values, with the dip position model-dependent.
- The bCGC model predicts the dip at smaller values of |t| compared to IP-Sat, independent of the vector meson produced.
- For ρ production, the dip region of interest is in the range 0.75 ≤ |t| ≤ 1.5 GeV²; for J/Ψ production, the relevant range is 2.0 ≤ |t| ≤ 3.0 GeV².
- The bCGC model parameters used: γ_s = 0.6599, κ = 9.9, B_CGC = 5.5 GeV⁻², N_0 = 0.3358, x_0 = 0.00105, λ = 0.2063.
- The CMS preliminary data for exclusive ρ photoproduction in pPb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV are well described by both models for |t| ≤ 0.4 GeV², but underestimated at larger |t|.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Goncalves, Navarra, Spiering, 2018) on t-spectra in pp and PbPb collisions to pA collisions, and provides the first comparison of gluon saturation model predictions with preliminary CMS data on exclusive ρ photoproduction in pPb collisions. The results complement earlier global analyses (e.g., run2 study) that indicated rapidity distributions of multiple final states are needed to discriminate between theoretical approaches. The findings suggest that future measurements of the large-t region can probe the presence or absence of a dip in the t-spectrum and discriminate between the bCGC and IP-Sat approaches to gluon saturation effects, potentially motivating improved models of the spatial distribution of gluons in the proton.
