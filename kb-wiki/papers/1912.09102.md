# Quark/hadronic matter and dualities of QCD thermodynamics

> In this letter the dualities of thermodynamics of dense quark/hadronic matter are discussed. It is shown that chiral symmetry breaking (CSB) phenomenon is dual to charged pion condensation (PC) phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a (3+1)-dimensional Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the chiral symmetry breaking (CSB) phenomenon is dual to the charged pion condensation (PC) phenomenon, meaning the thermodynamic potential is invariant under the simultaneous exchange of the chiral condensate with the charged pion condensate and the isospin chemical potential with the chiral isospin chemical potential.

## Method

The authors study the phase structure of two-flavored dense quark matter using a (3+1)-dimensional NJL model with four-fermion interactions, including terms for baryon, isospin, chiral, and chiral isospin chemical potentials. They derive the thermodynamic potential (TDP) in the leading order of the large-\(N_c\) expansion using a semi-bosonized version of the Lagrangian with composite bosonic fields for the sigma meson and pions. By analyzing the TDP, they identify exact symmetries (dualities) under transformations that exchange the order parameters (chiral condensate \(M\) and charged pion condensate \(\Delta\)) and the corresponding chemical potentials (\(\nu\) and \(\nu_5\)).

## Key numbers

- The main duality transformation is: \(M \longleftrightarrow \Delta\), \(\nu \longleftrightarrow \nu_5\) (Eq. 16), where \(M\) is the chiral condensate, \(\Delta\) is the charged pion condensate, \(\nu = \mu_I/2\) is the isospin chemical potential, and \(\nu_5 = \mu_{I5}/2\) is the chiral isospin chemical potential.
- A constrained duality \({\cal D}_M\) (Eq. 19) holds when \(\Delta = 0\): \(\mu_5 \longleftrightarrow \nu_5\), where \(\mu_5\) is the chiral chemical potential.
- A constrained duality \({\cal D}_\Delta\) (Eq. 21) holds when \(M = 0\): \(\mu_5 \longleftrightarrow \nu\).

## Context

This work extends duality studies in QCD thermodynamics using effective models, building on prior investigations of four-fermion models in low dimensions as toy models for QCD (Schon and Thies, 2000; Winstel and Wagner, 2019) and in connection with condensed matter systems (Yoshii and Takada, 2019; Caldas, 2019). The dualities simplify the exploration of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon density, where lattice QCD faces difficulties, by showing that CSB in a system with isospin imbalance is equivalent to charged PC in a system with chiral imbalance, and vice versa. This enables mapping between different regions of the phase diagram, potentially guiding future studies of dense quark matter in heavy-ion collisions or neutron stars.
