# Nuclear interactions with modern three-body forces lead to the instability of neutron matter and neutron stars

> It is shown that the neutron matter interacting through Argonne V18 pair-potential plus modern variants of Urbana or Illinois three-body forces is unstable. For the energy of $N$ neutrons $E(N)$, which interact through these forces, we prove mathematically that $E(N) = -cN^3 + \mathcal{O}(N^{8/3})$, where $c>0$ is a constant. This means that: (i) the energy per particle and neutron density diverge rapidly for large neutron numbers; (ii) bound states of $N$ neutrons exist for $N$ large enough. The neutron matter collapse is possible due to the form of the repulsive core in three-body forces, which vanishes when three nucleons occupy the same site in space. The old variant of the forces Urbana VI, where the phenomenological repulsive core does not vanish at the origin, resolves this problem. We prove that to prevent the collapse one should add a repulsive term to the Urbana IX potential, which should be larger than 50 MeV when 3 nucleons occupy the same spatial position.

# Nuclear interactions with modern three-body forces lead to the instability of neutron matter and neutron stars

## Key claim

The authors prove mathematically that neutron matter described by the Argonne V18 pair potential plus modern Urbana IX or Illinois 7 three-body forces is unstable, with the energy scaling as E(N) = -cN³ + O(N^(8/3)), meaning the energy per particle diverges for large neutron numbers. The collapse occurs because the repulsive core in these three-body forces vanishes when three nucleons occupy the same point in space, and the authors show that a repulsive term larger than 50 MeV must be added to the Urbana IX potential at zero separation to prevent this instability.

## Method

The authors construct a fully antisymmetrized trial wave function for 2N neutrons placed into two disjoint cubes, each containing N neutrons in excited Fermi gas states, and apply the variational principle to obtain an upper bound on the ground state energy. They evaluate the contributions from the kinetic energy, the Argonne V18 two-body potential, and the three-body Urbana IX interaction, showing that the three-body term dominates with a -cN³ scaling while the kinetic and two-body terms contribute at most O(N^(8/3)). The stability condition is then derived by requiring that the sum of integrals over the three-body interaction in the two-cube configuration be non-negative, which yields a minimum repulsive strength needed to prevent collapse.

## Key numbers

- The three-body interaction vanishes at zero separation: T(0) = Y(0) = 0, where T(r) and Y(r) are the form factors in the Urbana IX potential.
- For Urbana IX, the constants in the three-body interaction are A₂π = -0.0293 MeV and U₀ = 0.0048 MeV.
- The function W(0,0,D) at D = 0.9 fm is approximately -13.7 MeV, as shown in Figure 2.
- The minimal repulsive constant U_C required to prevent collapse must be larger than 51 MeV when added as a Saxon-Woods term with R = 0.5 fm and c = 0.2 fm.
- Preliminary estimates with the AV6' potential instead of AV18 show a bound multineutron with negative energy contains fewer than 4500 neutrons.
- The energy per particle diverges at least as fast as N² for large N, since E(2N)/(2N) ~ -cN².

## Context

This work extends the mathematical stability analysis of Dyson and Lennard (1967) and Lieb and Thirring (1975) from Coulomb systems to nuclear systems, applying the method of Zhislin (2000) to prove that the condition for instability (negative integrated interaction over disjoint cubes) is satisfied by modern three-body forces. The paper contradicts the assumption that Urbana IX and Illinois 7 interactions, which successfully describe light nuclei as shown by Pieper (2002) and others, produce stable neutron matter; instead, the authors demonstrate that these forces lead to unphysical collapse at large neutron numbers. The result affects all calculations using Urbana or Illinois three-body forces in nuclear systems, including neutron star equations of state as computed by Akmal (1998), and suggests that the repulsive core must be corrected, with the older Urbana VI interaction (which does not vanish at the origin) avoiding this problem.
