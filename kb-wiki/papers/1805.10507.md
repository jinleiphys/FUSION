# Nucleon charges with dynamical overlap fermions

> We calculate the scalar and tensor charges of the nucleon in 2+1-flavor lattice QCD, for which the systematics of the renormalization of the disconnected diagram is well controlled. Numerical simulations are performed at a single lattice spacing a = 0.11 fm. We simulate four pion masses, which cover a range of $m_π\sim$ 290 - 540 MeV, and a single strange quark mass close to its physical value. The statistical accuracy is improved by employing the so-called low-mode averaging technique and the truncated solver method. We study up, down, and strange quark contributions to the nucleon charges by calculating disconnected diagrams using the all-to-all quark propagator. Chiral symmetry is exactly preserved by using the overlap quark action to avoid operator mixing among different flavors, which complicates the renormalization of scalar and tensor matrix elements and leads to possibly large contamination to the small strange quark contributions. We also study the nucleon axial charge with contribution from the disconnected diagram. Our results are in reasonable agreement with experiments and previous lattice studies.

## Key claim

The authors calculate scalar, tensor, and axial charges of the nucleon using 2+1-flavor lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry preserved by the overlap fermion action, which avoids operator mixing among different flavors and thereby controls the systematics of renormalization for disconnected diagrams, a condition that is not guaranteed in calculations using Wilson-type fermions.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations at a single lattice spacing a = 0.11 fm using the overlap quark action and Iwasaki gauge action. They compute nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions for up, down, and strange quark contributions to scalar, axial, and tensor charges, including disconnected diagrams via the all-to-all quark propagator. Statistical accuracy is improved using low-mode averaging (LMA) and the truncated solver method (TSM), with results extracted from the asymptotic behavior of the ratio R_Γ(Δt, Δt') in the limit of large temporal separations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.112(1) fm, fixed from the Ω baryon mass
- Pion masses covered: m_π ~ 290, 379, 453, and 540 MeV (four values)
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 0.080, very close to physical value m_s,phys = 0.081
- Isovector axial charge g_A at m_π = 290 MeV: 1.134(27)
- Isovector axial charge g_A at m_π = 540 MeV: 1.176(27)
- Strange axial charge Δs at m_π = 290 MeV: -0.060(25)
- Strange axial charge Δs at m_π = 540 MeV: -0.035(20)
- Singlet axial charge ΔΣ at m_π = 290 MeV: 0.400(81)
- Singlet axial charge ΔΣ at m_π = 540 MeV: 0.551(70)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the JLQCD Collaboration (Ohki et al., Takeda et al.) that used overlap fermions to compute the nucleon sigma term and strange scalar content, now providing separate up, down, and strange quark contributions to scalar and tensor charges. The use of exact chiral symmetry avoids the operator mixing that complicates renormalization in Wilson-type fermion calculations (e.g., QCDSF-UKQCD, ETM, RQCD), enabling a cleaner extraction of the small strange quark contributions. The results enable improved inputs for dark matter direct detection (scalar charge) and searches for new sources of CP violation (tensor charge), and provide a benchmark comparison with the experimentally well-known isovector axial charge g_A = 1.2783 ± 0.0022.
