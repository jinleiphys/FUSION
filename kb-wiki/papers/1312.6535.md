# Borromean ground state of fermions in two dimensions

> The study of quantum mechanical bound states is as old as quantum theory itself. Yet, it took many years to realize that three-body borromean systems that are bound when any two-body subsystem is unbound are abundant in nature. Here we demonstrate the existence of borromean systems of spin-polarized (spinless) identical fermions in two spatial dimensions. The ground state with zero orbital (planar) angular momentum exists in a borromean window between critical two- and three-body strengths. The doubly degenerate first excited states of angular momentum one appears only very close to the two-body threshold. They are the lowest in a possible sequence of so-called super-Efimov states. While the observation of the super-Efimov scaling could be very difficult, the borromean ground state should be observable in cold atomic gases and could be the basis for producing a quantum gas of three-body states in two dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three spin-polarized (spinless) identical fermions in two spatial dimensions can form a borromean ground state with zero orbital angular momentum, existing in a window between the critical two-body and three-body interaction strengths. The authors hedge that while the doubly degenerate first excited states of angular momentum one appear very close to the two-body threshold and may be the lowest in a possible sequence of super-Efimov states, observation of the super-Efimov scaling could be very difficult.

## Method

The authors employed a coordinate-space adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method in two dimensions, solving the hyperangular part of the Hamiltonian to obtain effective three-body potentials. They used the stochastic variational method to numerically calculate ground state energies for three qualitatively different two-body potentials (purely attractive, attractive with an external repulsive barrier, and with a repulsive core). The Faddeev decomposition and expansion on angular eigenfunctions were used to find the lowest adiabatic potentials for angular momentum M=0 and M=1 channels.

## Key numbers

- For potential V1, the critical two-body strength g2^cr = 6.72, and the ratio g3^cr/g2^cr = 0.72
- For potential V2, g2^cr = 28.98, and g3^cr/g2^cr = 0.68
- For potential V3, g2^cr = 8.63, and g3^cr/g2^cr = 0.72
- At the two-body threshold, the three-body ground state energy for V1 is E3 = -1.50 in units of hbar^2/(m b^2)
- The first excited state energy at the two-body threshold for V1 is E3* = -0.18 in the same units
- The mean square hyperradius for the ground state at threshold for V1 is ⟨ρ^2⟩_gr = 1.65 in units of b^2
- The mean square hyperradius for the excited state at threshold for V1 is ⟨ρ^2⟩_exc = 6.0 in units of b^2
- The lower bound for the ratio g3^cr/g2^cr is 2/3, derived from the inequality E3(m,g) ≥ 2E2(m,3g/2)

## Context

This work extends the study of borromean systems, previously known for three-body systems in three dimensions for both bosons and fermions (Nielsen 2001, Jensen 2004), to spin-polarized fermions in two dimensions. It contrasts with bosonic systems in two dimensions, where borromean states require potentials with positive net volume and substantial repulsion (Nielsen 1997, 1999). The paper addresses the recent prediction of super-Efimov states with double-exponential scaling (SuperEfimov 2013) and discusses a discrepancy with Gao (2014) regarding a non-universal term in the effective three-body potential. The findings enable experimental searches for borromean three-fermion clusters in cold atomic gases using tunable interactions, potentially leading to a quantum gas of three-body states in two dimensions.
