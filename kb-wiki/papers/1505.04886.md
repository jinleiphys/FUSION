# From holography towards real-world nuclear matter

> Quantum chromodynamics is notoriously difficult to solve at nonzero baryon density, and most models or effective theories of dense quark or nuclear matter are restricted to a particular density regime and/or a particular form of matter. Here we study dense (and mostly cold) matter within the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model, aiming at a strong-coupling framework in the wide density range between nuclear saturation density and ultra-high quark matter densities. The model contains only three parameters, and we ask whether it fulfills two basic requirements of real-world cold and dense matter, a first-order onset of nuclear matter and a chiral phase transition at high density to quark matter. Such a model would be extremely useful for astrophysical applications because it would provide a single equation of state for all densities relevant in a compact star. Our calculations are based on two approximations for baryonic matter, firstly an instanton gas and secondly a homogeneous ansatz for the non-abelian gauge fields on the flavor branes of the model. While the instanton gas shows chiral restoration at high densities but an unrealistic second-order baryon onset, the homogeneous ansatz behaves exactly the other way around. Our study thus provides all ingredients that are necessary for a more realistic model and allows for systematic improvements of the applied approximations.

## Key claim

The holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model, despite containing only three parameters, cannot simultaneously reproduce two basic requirements of real-world cold dense matter (a first-order onset of nuclear matter and a chiral phase transition to quark matter at high density) using either the instanton gas or homogeneous ansatz approximations; each approximation captures only one of the two required features, and the study provides the ingredients needed for systematic improvements rather than a fully realistic model.

## Method

The authors study dense cold matter within the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model using two distinct approximations for baryonic matter: an instanton gas (where baryons are treated as finite-size BPST instantons with a Gaussian-like density profile in the holographic coordinate) and a homogeneous ansatz for the non-abelian gauge fields on the flavor D8-branes. They work in the deconfined geometry with non-antipodal asymptotic separation of the flavor branes, solving the equations of motion for the abelian gauge field and the brane embedding while minimizing the free energy with respect to the instanton width and density (for the instanton gas) or the homogeneous gauge field parameters (for the homogeneous ansatz).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}\) (given as the known real-world value for context, not computed in the model)
- The model contains exactly three free parameters: the Kaluza-Klein mass \(M_{\rm KK}\), the asymptotic separation of flavor branes \(L\), and the 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\)
- The critical temperature for deconfinement in the antipodal version is \(T_c = M_{\rm KK}/(2\pi)\)
- The instanton gas shows a second-order baryon onset (unrealistic) but does exhibit chiral restoration at high densities
- The homogeneous ansatz shows a first-order baryon onset (realistic) but does not exhibit chiral restoration at high densities

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of dense matter in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, particularly the pointlike instanton approximation of Bergman (2007) which showed a second-order baryon onset and no chiral restoration, and the finite-size instanton approach of Ghoroku (2012, 2013) which claimed a first-order onset. The authors find that a fully dynamical calculation of finite-size instantons does not produce a first-order onset, contradicting those earlier claims. The study also builds on the homogeneous ansatz introduced by Rozali (2007) for the confined geometry. The results enable systematic improvements of the approximations needed to construct a single equation of state valid from nuclear saturation to quark matter densities for compact star applications, as previously attempted by Berges (1998) and Benic (2015) using patched-together models.
