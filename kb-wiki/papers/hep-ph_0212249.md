# Intrinsic parton transverse momentum in next-to-leading-order pion production

> We study pion production in proton-proton collisions within a pQCD-improved parton model in next-to-leading order augmented by intrinsic transverse momentum ($k_\perp$) of the partons. We find the introduction of $k_\perp$ necessary to reproduce the experimental data in the CERN SPS to RHIC energy range. We study the influence of intrinsic $k_\perp$ on the so-called $K$ factor, the ratio of the NLO cross section to the Born term. A strong $p_T$ dependence is seen, especially in the 3-6 GeV transverse momentum region of the outgoing pion, where nuclear effects (e.g. the Cronin effect) play an important role.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that even at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD, an intrinsic parton transverse momentum ($k_\perp$) distribution with a fitted width is necessary to reproduce experimental pion production data in proton-proton collisions across the CERN SPS to RHIC energy range, and that the resulting $K$ factor (ratio of NLO to Born cross section) shows a strong $p_T$ dependence in the 3–6 GeV region where nuclear effects like the Cronin effect are important.

## Method

The authors extend a standard NLO pQCD parton model by introducing a Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distribution for initial-state partons, with width $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle$, and implement this in the Aversa group NLO code. They compute inclusive pion production cross sections in $pp$ collisions using MRST-cg parton distribution functions and KKP fragmentation functions, with factorization, renormalization, and fragmentation scales set to $\kappa p_T/z_c$ and $\kappa p_T$ respectively (with $\kappa=2/3$ at NLO). The best-fit values of $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle$ are extracted by minimizing $\chi^2$ in the $3-6$ GeV transverse momentum window for experimental data from $\sqrt{s}=19.5$ to $200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

* At $\sqrt{s}=27.4$ GeV (FNAL 400 GeV experiment), NLO calculations without intrinsic $k_\perp$ underpredict the data by a factor of 2 using scales $Q=Q_r=Q_f=p_T/2$; adding a Gaussian width $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle = 1$ GeV$^2$ brings the calculation into agreement with the data.
* The best-fit width of the intrinsic transverse momentum distribution is $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle \approx 2$ GeV$^2$ at energies $\sqrt{s}\sim 30$ GeV, as required to reproduce the Cronin peak in $pA$ collisions.
* For $\sqrt{s}\gtrsim 60$ GeV, the pionic $K$ factor can be taken approximately energy-independent as a first approximation.
* At $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle = 2$ GeV$^2$, the ratio $R = K(\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle)/K(0)$ shows a maximal enhancement of 20% at $p_\perp \approx 2-3$ GeV; for $p_T \gtrsim 4$ GeV the $k_\perp$-driven correction vanishes.

## Context

This work extends earlier leading-order studies by the same group (Papp et al., 2002) and others (Wang, 1990s; Eskola, 1990s) that introduced intrinsic $k_\perp$ or effective $K$ factors to reconcile pQCD with data. It provides the first NLO calculation with intrinsic transverse momentum for pion production, showing that the fitted width $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle \approx 2$ GeV$^2$ is consistent across both $pp$ and $pA$ (Cronin effect) data. The results enable more reliable LO calculations for nucleus-nucleus collisions by providing a well-founded, $p_T$-dependent $K$ factor, and establish a baseline for identifying collective nuclear effects in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and beyond.
