# An effective model of QCD thermodynamics

> A combined effective model reproducing the equation of state of hadronic matter as obtained in recent lattice QCD simulations is presented. The model reproduces basic physical characteristics encountered in dense hadronic matter in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase and the lower temperature hadron resonance gas phase. The hadronic phase is described by means of an extended Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas while the QGP phase is described by the extended PNJL model. The dissociation of hadrons is obtained by including the state dependent hadron resonance width.

# An effective model of QCD thermodynamics

## Key claim

The paper presents a combined effective model that reproduces the lattice QCD equation of state across both the hadron resonance gas and quark-gluon plasma phases, but the high-temperature agreement in earlier versions relied on an accidental coincidence: the number of low-lying mesonic degrees of freedom with masses below 1 GeV approximately equals the number of thermodynamic degrees of freedom of a quark-gluon gas, which artificially mimics the Stefan-Boltzmann limit without physically describing deconfinement.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described by an extended Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas with a state-dependent hadron resonance width given by the inverse collision time scale, derived from a binary collision approximation using the geometrical Povh-Hufner law for in-medium hadron-hadron cross sections. The quark-gluon plasma phase is described by the Polyakov-loop improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, further refined by adding perturbative corrections to O(alpha_s) for the high-momentum region above the three-momentum cutoff. The total pressure is the sum of the MHRG pressure, the PNJL mean-field pressure, and the perturbative correction pressure.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn temperature is set to T_H = 165 MeV.
- The mass threshold for light hadrons treated as stable is m_0 = 1 GeV.
- The number of low-lying mesonic degrees of freedom with masses below 1 GeV is 32, which accidentally coincides with the quark and gluon degrees of freedom (31.5) for N_c = N_f = 3.
- The lattice QCD data are reproduced up to a temperature of approximately 170 MeV by the hadron resonance gas, corresponding to the pseudocritical temperature of the chiral phase transition.
- The hadronic resonance gas contribution vanishes at T approximately 250 MeV.
- The critical temperature region is T_c approximately 150-170 MeV from lattice QCD.

## Context

This work extends the Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas model of Blaschke (2003) and Turko (2011) by introducing a state-dependent hadron resonance width based on the collision time scale approach of Blaschke (2011) for chemical freeze-out. It combines this with the PNJL model of Fukushima (2003) and Ratti (2005) to describe the quark-gluon plasma phase, and compares results to lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2009). The model enables a unified treatment of hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom across the QCD transition region, with future work needed to incorporate continuum correlations in hadronic scattering channels following the Levinson theorem as discussed by Dashen (1969) and recently applied to pion dissociation within the PNJL model by Wergieluk (2012).
