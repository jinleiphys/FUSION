# Generalized Pseudo-SU(3) Model and Pairing

> The pseudo-SU(3) model is extended to explicitly include the spin and proton-neutron degrees of freedom. A general formalism for evaluating matrix elements of one-body and two-body tensor operators within this framework is presented. The pairing interaction, which couples different irreducible representations of SU(3), is expressed in terms of pseudo-space tensors and a general result is given for calculating its matrix elements. The importance of pairing correlations in pseudo-SU(3) model calculations is demonstrated by examining the dependence of wavefunctions, low-energy collective excitation spectra, and moments of inertia on the strength of the pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The pseudo-SU(3) model is extended to explicitly include spin and proton-neutron degrees of freedom, and the pairing interaction, which couples different irreducible representations of SU(3), is incorporated into the formalism for the first time, enabled by a recently developed general code for calculating matrix elements between different SU(3) irreps.

## Method

The authors construct basis states organized by group chains including U(2Ω_σ) ⊃ U(Ω_σ) ⊗ U(2)^{(σ)} ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SO(3) for protons and neutrons separately, then couple proton and neutron SU(3) irreps to total (λ,μ) and couple orbital and spin angular momenta to total J. They define one-body and two-body SU(3) tensor operators in second quantization, including creation, annihilation, and coupled proton-neutron operators, and give general expressions for their reduced matrix elements using SU(3) coupling coefficients and 9-(λ,μ) recoupling coefficients. The pairing Hamiltonian is transformed from normal-space to pseudo-space quantum numbers and expressed as a sum of (2,2) SU(3) tensor operators, with matrix elements evaluated using the code of Bahri and Draayer (1994).

## Key numbers

- The model space is restricted to the most symmetric irreps of U(Ω_π) and U(Ω_ν), justified because spin-flip modes lie "significantly higher in energy than the low-energy collective excitations"
- The number of normal parity levels in the η_σ shell is given by Ω_σ = (η_σ+1)(η_σ+2)/2
- The pairing strength G is taken to be "somewhat different for protons and neutrons"
- The Hamiltonian studied includes terms: (χ/2) Q^a·Q^a + G_π H^π_P + G_ν H^ν_P + a K_J² + b J²

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) model of Ratcliff (1973) and the earlier formulation of Castaños and Draayer (1987) by incorporating spin degrees of freedom and a full proton-neutron treatment, enabling description of even-odd, odd-even, and odd-odd nuclei in addition to even-even ones. The inclusion of pairing, which was previously impossible due to the lack of a general code for SU(3) inter-irrep matrix elements (now provided by Bahri and Draayer, 1994), addresses a known limitation of the model compared to other shell-model theories (Rowe and Spring, 1980; Bohr and Mottelson, 1969). The formalism enables future applications to collective excitation spectra, scissors mode studies, and double-beta decay half-lives (Civitarese and Hirsch, 1994) with a more realistic Hamiltonian.
