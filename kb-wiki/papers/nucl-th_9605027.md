# Ground-state Spectrum of Light-quark Mesons

> A confining, Goldstone theorem preserving, separable Ansatz for the ladder kernel of the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation is constructed from phenomenologically efficacious $u$, $d$ and $s$ dressed-quark propagators. The simplicity of the approach is its merit. It provides a good description of the ground-state isovector-pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector meson spectrum; facilitates an exploration of the relative importance of various components of the two-body Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, showing that sub-leading Dirac components are quantitatively important in the isovector-pseudoscalar meson channels; and allows a scrutiny of the domain of applicability of ladder truncation studies. A colour-antitriplet diquark spectrum is obtained. Shortcomings of separable Ansätze and the ladder kernel are highlighted.

# Ground-state Spectrum of Light-quark Mesons

## Key claim
The paper constructs a confining, Goldstone theorem preserving separable Ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel that provides a good description of ground-state isovector-pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector meson spectra, but the approach is limited by the separable approximation and ladder truncation, which produce unphysical diquark bound states and cannot reproduce certain meson masses without additional contributions such as kaon-antikaon admixtures.

## Method
The authors construct a rank-1 separable Ansatz for the ladder kernel of the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation in Feynman-like gauge, using phenomenologically constrained dressed-quark propagators for u, d, and s quarks that are entire functions in the complex p^2 plane (ensuring confinement). The kernel is completely determined by the quark propagators via the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow truncation, with ultraviolet regularization parameters introduced to make the kernel finite. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in the meson rest frame as an eigenvalue problem, yielding bound state masses and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for various J^PC channels.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass m_{0-+} = 0 in the chiral limit (m_{u/d} = 0), preserving Goldstone's theorem
- Ratio of current-quark masses: 2 m_s / (m_u + m_d) = 24.4, corresponding to m_{u/d} = 4.6 MeV and m_s = 112 MeV with D = 0.160 GeV^2
- Euclidean constituent-quark masses: M_E^u = 315 MeV, M_E^s = 397 MeV
- Sub-leading axial-vector component of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude contributes 17% of the mass and 39% of the decay constant
- Sub-leading axial-vector component of the kaon Bethe-Salpeter amplitude contributes 17% of the mass and 33% of the decay constant
- The 0^{--} state mass is approximately 10 times the 0^{-+} mass; the 0^{+-} state mass is approximately 2 times the 0^{++} mass
- No true 0^+ eigenstate found below 2 GeV for the u-s system; the closest approach to an eigenstate occurred at 1.18 GeV

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to meson spectroscopy using the algebraic parametrization of confining dressed-quark propagators previously employed in studies of pion and kaon observables by Roberts (1994) and Burden, Roberts, and Tandy (1996). It provides a simpler alternative to the numerical rainbow-ladder studies of Jain and Munczek (1993), which required derivative expansions to handle off-axis propagator evaluations. The separable Ansatz enables systematic exploration of sub-leading Dirac components in Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, which had been neglected in most prior studies, and allows scrutiny of the domain of applicability of ladder truncation. The unphysical diquark bound states obtained are a known defect of ladder truncation that is eliminated by repulsive terms at higher order, as shown by Bender, Roberts, and von Smekal (1996).
