# BCS-BCS crossover between atomic and molecular superfluids in a Bose-Fermi mixture

> We theoretically examine a continuity between atomic and molecular Fermi superfluids in a Bose-Fermi mixture near the Feshbach resonance. Considering a two-channel model describing the Feshbach resonance between Fermi and Bose atoms, we have constructed the mean-field framework based on the perturbative expansion of the Feshbach atom-dimer coupling. The resulting effective Hamiltonian exhibits not only the continuity between atom-atom to molecule-molecule Cooper pairings but also becomes equivalent to the two-band-superconductor model with Suhl-Matthias-Walker type pair-exchange coupling. We demonstrate how these atomic and molecular Fermi superfluids coexist within the two-band-like superfluid theory. The pair-exchange coupling and resulting superfluid gaps are found to be strongly enhanced near the Feshbach resonance due to the interplay between the infrared singularity of Bogoliubov phonons and their Landau damping arising from the coupling with fermions. The pair-exchange coupling can be probed via the observation of the intrinsic Josephson effect between atomic and molecular superfluids.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates theoretically that atomic and molecular Fermi superfluids in a Bose-Fermi mixture near a Feshbach resonance can exhibit a continuous crossover (BCS-BCS crossover) rather than a sharp phase transition, but this result depends on the perturbative expansion of the Feshbach coupling being valid (narrow resonance regime) and on the pair-exchange interaction mediated by Bogoliubov phonons being the dominant mechanism.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-channel mean-field model for a spinless Bose-Fermi mixture (fermionic atoms f, bosonic atoms b, and closed-channel molecular fermions F) near a narrow Feshbach resonance. They performed a perturbative expansion to second order in the Feshbach coupling g, deriving effective interactions including a Suhl-Matthias-Walker-type pair-exchange coupling (ff ↔ FF) mediated by anomalous Bogoliubov phonon propagators, with Landau damping from fermion-phonon coupling regularizing infrared singularities. The resulting effective Hamiltonian was solved within a two-band-like mean-field theory to obtain superfluid gaps and ground-state energies for the atomic and molecular pairing channels.

- Mass ratio used: m_F/m_f = (40+23)/40 = 1.575, corresponding to the ²³Na-⁴⁰K mixture

## Context

This work extends previous studies of BCS-BEC crossover in Fermi-Fermi mixtures (Ketterle, 2008) and single-channel models of Bose-Fermi mixtures (Viverit, 2000; Viverit, 2002; Efremov, 2002; Fratini, 2010; Guidini, 2015) by including new scattering processes (ff ↔ FF and fF ↔ Ff) that arise at second order in the Feshbach coupling. The pair-exchange mechanism is analogous to the Suhl-Matthias-Walker mechanism in multiband superconductors (Suhl, 1959; Kondo, 1963) and to the quark-hadron continuity scenario in high-density QCD (Schäfer, 1999; Hatsuda, 2006). The results enable experimental probes of the pair-exchange coupling through the intrinsic Josephson effect between atomic and molecular superfluids, and suggest an alternative route to realize two-band Fermi superfluidity with incipient flat bands in mass-imbalanced Bose-Fermi mixtures.
