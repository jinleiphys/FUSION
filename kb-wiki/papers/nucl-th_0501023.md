# Parity-Violation With Electrons: Theoretical Perspectives

> I review recent progress and developments in parity-violating electron scattering as it bears on three topics: strange quarks and hadron structure, electroweak radiative corrections, and physics beyond the Standard Model. I also discuss related developments in parity-conserving scattering with transversely polarized electrons as a probe of two-photon processes. I conclude with a perspective on the future of the field.

## Key claim

Parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) has matured from a specialized subfield into a mainstream research area, but the interpretation of results for strange quark form factors and electroweak radiative corrections remains complicated by non-perturbative QCD effects that cannot be computed from first principles without additional experimental input.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical developments in PVES across three topics: strange quark contributions to nucleon form factors, electroweak and QED radiative corrections, and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model. For strange quarks, the author describes dispersion theory analyses of kaon cloud contributions and chiral perturbation theory calculations, noting that the leading-order low-energy constants contain an SU(3)-singlet component that cannot be determined from existing experiments. For radiative corrections, the author discusses anapole moment contributions to the axial vector form factor and two-photon exchange amplitudes, including an effective field theory calculation of the transverse-spin asymmetry for elastic electron-proton scattering.

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic radius from chiral perturbation theory: ⟨r_s²⟩_M = -[0.04 + 0.3 b_s^r] fm², where the first term is the loop contribution at renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV and b_s^r is a next-to-leading-order counterterm of order unity
- Predicted strangeness magnetic moment from quenched lattice QCD with charge symmetry assumption: μ_s = -0.051 ± 0.021 (Leinweber, 2004)
- The Z-γ box graph contribution to the vector channel is suppressed by 1 - 4 sin²θ_W ≈ 0.1
- Theoretical radiative correction uncertainty in the axial vector transition form factor G_A^{Δ,e} at Q² = 0 is of order 10-20%
- The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation predicts G_A^Δ(0) = √(2/3) [g_{πNΔ} F_π / m_N] (1 - Δ_π), where g_{πNΔ} is known with 5% accuracy

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier contribution to the Mainz PAVI proceedings (Ramsey-Musolf, 2003) and summarizes developments since the early 1990s. The work builds on the theoretical framework of Kaplan and Manohar (1988) for flavor decomposition using weak neutral currents, Jaffe's dispersion theory analysis (1989) predicting large strange quark form factors, and the experimental program initiated by McKeown (1989). The paper contrasts the successful dispersion theory treatment of nucleon isovector form factors by Federbush, Goldberger, and Treiman (late 1950s) with the more complicated situation for strange quarks, where contributions from higher-mass intermediate states cancel against the kaon cloud but cannot be computed reliably. The results enable future precision electroweak tests including the E158 Møller experiment at SLAC and the Q-Weak experiment at JLab, and may help resolve the NuTeV anomaly through studies of isospin violation in parton distribution functions.
