# Gapless Color-Flavor-Locked Quark Matter

> In neutral cold quark matter that is sufficiently dense that the strange quark mass M_s is unimportant, all nine quarks (three colors; three flavors) pair in a color-flavor locked (CFL) pattern, and all fermionic quasiparticles have a gap. We argue that as a function of decreasing quark chemical potential mu or increasing M_s, there is a quantum phase transition from the CFL phase to a new ``gapless CFL phase'' in which only seven quasiparticles have a gap. The transition occurs where M_s^2/mu is approximately equal to 2*Delta, with Delta the gap parameter. Gapless CFL, like CFL, leaves unbroken a linear combination Qtilde of electric and color charges, but it is a Qtilde-conductor with a nonzero electron density. These electrons and the gapless quark quasiparticles make the low energy effective theory of the gapless CFL phase and, consequently, its astrophysical properties are qualitatively different from those of the CFL phase, even though its U(1) symmetries are the same. Both gapless quasiparticles have quadratic dispersion relations at the quantum critical point. For values of M_s^2/mu above the quantum critical point, one branch has conventional linear dispersion relations while the other branch remains quadratic, up to tiny corrections.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in neutral cold quark matter, as the strange quark mass increases or the quark chemical potential decreases, the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase undergoes a continuous quantum phase transition to a new "gapless CFL phase" (gCFL) before any transition to the two-flavor (2SC) phase occurs, with the transition occurring where M_s^2/mu is approximately equal to 2*Delta rather than the previously assumed 4*Delta.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a pointlike four-quark interaction having the quantum numbers of single-gluon exchange, imposing electrical and color neutrality conditions via chemical potentials mu_e, mu_3, and mu_8. They solve six coupled integral equations: three neutrality conditions and three gap equations for the gap parameters Delta_1, Delta_2, and Delta_3 describing down-strange, up-strange, and up-down Cooper pairs respectively, working to leading nontrivial order in all small parameters relative to the quark chemical potential mu.

## Key numbers

- The critical condition for the CFL to gapless CFL transition is M_s^2/mu approximately equal to 2*Delta_CFL, where Delta_CFL is the CFL gap parameter.
- For mu=500 MeV and Delta_0=25 MeV, the critical strange quark mass M_s^c lies between 153 MeV and 154 MeV, corresponding to (M_s^2/mu)_c = 46.8 MeV.
- At M_s=153 MeV in the CFL phase, all three gap parameters equal 23.5 MeV.
- A first-order gCFL to unpaired quark matter transition occurs at M_s^2/mu approximately 129 MeV, with the gCFL phase ceasing to exist as a solution at M_s^2/mu approximately 144 MeV.
- At M_s^2/mu=80 MeV, the electron chemical potential mu_e=14.6 MeV at the lower boundary of the allowed range, and the bu blocking region width (p^{bu}_2 - p^{bu}_1) is 0.0046 MeV.
- The maximum quasiparticle energy between p^{bu}_1 and p^{bu}_2 at M_s^2/mu=80 MeV is approximately 0.13 eV, negligible at compact star temperatures.

## Context

This work extends previous model-independent calculations by Alford (2002) that compared the free energy of the CFL phase with the 2SC and unpaired phases, which had suggested a transition at M_s^2/mu < 4*Delta_CFL. The paper shows that the CFL phase becomes unstable at the lower value M_s^2/mu = 2*Delta_CFL, and that the resulting gapless CFL phase is analogous to the unstable Sarma phase but rendered stable by the neutrality constraint, as first noted in the two-flavor case by Shovkovy (2003) and Gubankova (2003). The discovery enables future calculations of compact star cooling with a gCFL core, where neutrino emission will involve conversion between quasiparticles with linear and quadratic dispersion relations.
