# Can tetraneutron be a narrow resonance?

> The search for a resonant four-neutron system has been revived thanks to the recent experimental hints reported in Phys. Rev. Lett. \textbf{116}, 052501 (2016). The existence of such a system would deeply impact our understanding of nuclear matter and requires a critical investigation. In this work, we study the existence of a four-neutron resonance in the quasi-stationary formalism using \textit{ab initio} techniques with various two-body chiral interactions. We employ the No-Core Gamow Shell Model and the Density Matrix Renormalization Group method, both supplemented by the use of natural orbitals and a new identification technique for broad resonances. We demonstrate that while the energy of the four-neutron system may be compatible with the experimental value, its width must be larger than the reported upper limit, supporting the interpretation of the experimental observation as a reaction process too short to form a nucleus.

## Key claim

While the energy of a four-neutron resonance may be compatible with the experimental value of 0.83 MeV above threshold, its width must be larger than the reported upper limit of 2.6 MeV, supporting the interpretation that the experimental observation is a reaction process too short to form a nucleus.

## Method

The authors employed the No-Core Gamow Shell Model (NCGSM) and the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) method, both using the Berggren basis to include couplings to the continuum. They supplemented these approaches with natural orbitals and a new identification technique for broad resonances, where the interaction is progressively scaled by a factor f > 1 to bind the system and then gradually reduced to f = 1 while tracking the state. Calculations were performed with various two-body chiral interactions (N3LO, N2LOopt, N2LOsat) and the JISP16 interaction, with different renormalization cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- For the N3LO interaction with two neutrons in the continuum, the four-neutron system has energy 7.28 MeV and width 3.67 MeV (for cutoff λ = 1.9 fm⁻¹).
- For the N2LOopt interaction with two neutrons in the continuum, the system has energy 7.33 MeV and width 3.78 MeV (for cutoff λ = 1.9 fm⁻¹).
- For the JISP16 interaction with two neutrons in the continuum, the system has energy 7.00 MeV and width 3.72 MeV.
- The experimental hint from Kisamori et al. (2016) gives an energy of 0.83 ± 0.65 (stat) ± 1.25 (syst) MeV above threshold and a maximal width of 2.6 MeV.
- The DMRG results without truncations are about 1 MeV lower in energy than the NCGSM-3p3h (n.o.) results at a scaling factor of f = 2.0.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of the four-neutron system following experimental hints by Kisamori et al. (2016) and earlier work by Marques et al. (2002). It contradicts the possibility of a narrow resonance suggested by Shirokov et al. (2016), who obtained E = 0.8 MeV and Γ = 1.4 MeV from extrapolation of phase-shifts, and is consistent with Hiyama et al. (2016) who found that unrealistic modifications to the nuclear interaction would be needed to obtain a four-neutron system at the experimental value. The study enables future investigations of other unbound systems such as neutron-rich hydrogen isotopes.
