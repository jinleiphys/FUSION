# $η'$ and $η$ mesons at high T when the U_A(1) and chiral symmetry breaking are tied

> The approach to the eta'-eta complex employing chirally well-behaved quark-antiquark bound states and incorporating the non-Abelian axial anomaly of QCD through the generalization of the Witten-Veneziano relation, is extended to finite temperatures. Employing the chiral condensate has led to a sharp chiral and U_A(1) symmetry restoration, but with the condensates of quarks with realistic explicit chiral symmetry breaking, which exhibit a smooth, crossover chiral symmetry restoration in qualitative agreement with lattice QCD results, we get a crossover U_A(1) transition, with smooth and gradual melting of anomalous mass contributions. This way we obtain a substantial drop of the eta' mass around the chiral transition temperature, but no eta mass drop. This is consistent with the present empirical evidence.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when realistic quark masses are used (rather than the chiral limit), the restoration of $U_A(1)$ symmetry at high temperature becomes a smooth crossover rather than a sharp transition, producing a substantial drop in the $\eta'$ mass near the chiral transition temperature while the $\eta$ mass does not drop significantly.

## Method

The authors extend their Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to the $\eta$-$\eta'$ complex to finite temperature, using a separable interaction model in the rainbow-ladder approximation. They incorporate the $U_A(1)$ anomaly through Shore's generalization of the Witten-Veneziano relation, which expresses the topological charge parameter $A$ in terms of the full QCD topological susceptibility and the quark-antiquark condensates of massive $u$, $d$, and $s$ quarks, rather than using the chiral-limit condensate or the pure-gauge Yang-Mills topological susceptibility.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum $\eta'$ mass is $M_{\eta'}=957.8$ MeV, and the model predicts it drops by at least 200 MeV inside the fireball in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
* The model's chiral transition temperature is $T_{\rm Ch}=128$ MeV, approximately 17% below the accepted central value of $154\pm 9$ MeV from lattice QCD.
* At $T=0$, using $A = \chi_{\rm YM} = (0.191\ {\rm GeV})^4$ from lattice, the model yields $M_{\eta'}=997$ MeV and $M_{\eta}=554$ MeV.
* The Witten-Veneziano relation gives $M_{\eta'}^2 + M_\eta^2 - 2 M_K^2 = M_{U_A(1)}^2$, with the right-hand side roughly 0.8 to 0.9 GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier approach of Benic et al. (2011) and Benic et al. (2014), which tied $U_A(1)$ symmetry restoration to chiral symmetry restoration through the chiral-limit condensate. The previous approach predicted a drop in the $\eta$ mass that has not been seen in experimental data from PHENIX and STAR collaborations or in newer results from Adare et al. (2017) and Aidala et al. (2018). By using condensates of quarks with realistic explicit chiral symmetry breaking, the present work eliminates the problematic $\eta$ mass drop while preserving the $\eta'$ mass drop consistent with the empirical findings of Csorgo et al. The results enable further study of $U_A(1)$ restoration signatures in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, and NICA.
