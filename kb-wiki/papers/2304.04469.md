# Variation of the quadrupole hyperfine structure and nuclear radius due to an interaction with scalar and axion dark matter

> Atomic spectroscopy is used to search for the space-time variation of fundamental constants which may be due to an interaction with scalar and pseudo-scalar (axion) dark matter. In this letter, we study the effects which are produced by the variation of the nuclear radius and electric quadrupole moment. The sensitivity of the electric quadrupole hyperfine structure to both the variation of the quark mass and the effects of dark matter exceeds that of the magnetic hyperfine structure by 1-2 orders of magnitude. Therefore, the measurement of the variation of the ratio of the electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole hyperfine constants is proposed. The sensitivity of the optical clock transitions in the Yb$^+$ ion to the variation of the nuclear radius allows us to extract, from experimental data, limits on the variation of the hadron and quark masses, the QCD parameter $θ$ and the interaction with axion and scalar dark matter.

# Variation of the quadrupole hyperfine structure and nuclear radius due to an interaction with scalar and axion dark matter

## Key claim
The sensitivity of the electric quadrupole hyperfine structure constant B to quark mass variation and dark matter interactions exceeds that of the magnetic hyperfine constant A by 1–2 orders of magnitude, but this enhancement relies on the assumption that the dependence of the magnetic hyperfine constant on hadron parameters can be neglected in a zeroth-order approximation. The authors also claim that their calculated coefficient for the dependence of nuclear radius on pion mass is 6 times larger and opposite in sign compared to the result in Banerjee (2023), implying 6 times stronger limits on axion interactions.

## Method
The authors calculate the dependence of nuclear radius on hadron masses using sensitivity coefficients from Variational Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of nuclear energies and radii in light nuclei (Wiringa 2007, Wiringa 2009), then relate hadron mass variations to quark mass variations using results from Roberts (2008). They extract limits on the drift of nuclear radius, quark mass, pion mass, and the QCD parameter θ² from existing measurements of the ratio of electric octupole (E3) and electric quadrupole (E2) transition frequencies in Yb⁺ clock transitions (Filzinger 2023). They then derive the sensitivity of the electric quadrupole hyperfine constant B and the ratio B/A to these variations, and relate the results to scalar and axion dark matter fields through the identification of the axion field with a dynamical QCD θ parameter.

## Key numbers
- Sensitivity coefficient of nuclear radius r₀ to pion mass: K_π = 1.8
- Sensitivity coefficient of nuclear radius r₀ to nucleon mass: K_N = −4.8
- Average sensitivity coefficient of nuclear radius r₀ to quark mass: δr₀/r₀ = 0.3 δm_q/m_q
- Drift of the mean-square nuclear radius from Yb⁺ clock data: δ⟨r_n²⟩/⟨r_n²⟩ = −0.50(0.75) × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹
- Drift of the quark mass: δm_q/m_q = −0.83(1.25) × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹
- Drift of the pion mass: δm_π/m_π = −0.21(0.31) × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹
- Drift of θ²: dθ²/dt = 4(6) × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹
- Sensitivity of the ratio of E3 and E2 Yb⁺ transition frequencies to nuclear radius variation: δ(ν_E3/ν_E2)/(ν_E3/ν_E2) = 2.4 × 10⁻³ δ⟨r_n²⟩/⟨r_n²⟩
- Measured drift of the ratio of E3 and E2 Yb⁺ transition frequencies: −1.2(1.8) × 10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹
- Sensitivity of quadrupole constant B to quark mass: δB/B = 0.6 δm_q/m_q
- Sensitivity of B/A to quark mass: δ(B/A)/(B/A) ≈ 0.6 δm_q/m_q
- Sensitivity of B/A to pion mass: δ(B/A)/(B/A) ≈ 2.4 δm_π/m_π
- Sensitivity of B/A to θ²: δ(B/A)/(B/A) ≈ −0.12 θ²

## Context
This work extends the approach of Banerjee (2023), who proposed using Yb⁺ clock transition sensitivity to nuclear radius for dark matter searches, but corrects the sign and magnitude of the nuclear radius sensitivity to pion mass (coefficient β = 1.2 versus β = −0.2 in that work). The authors also build on calculations of nuclear radius dependence on fundamental constants from Wiringa (2007) and Wiringa (2009), and on prior limits on quark mass variation from measurements of Cs/Rb hyperfine transition frequency ratios (Guena). The proposed measurement of the ratio of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole hyperfine constants B/A in the same atom offers a new route to improved sensitivity to quark mass variation, the QCD θ parameter, and scalar or axion dark matter interactions, potentially surpassing the sensitivity of magnetic hyperfine structure measurements previously considered in Tedesco (2006) and Perez (2022).
