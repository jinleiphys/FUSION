# Master Integrals for two-loop QCD corrections to Quasi PDFs

> We compute the master integrals for two-loop QCD corrections to quasi parton distribution functions (PDFs) in large momentum effective theory. Analytical results of the master integrals are derived using the method of differential equations, along with a proper choice of canonical basis. The results of master integrals are expressed in terms of Goncharov polylogarithms. These integrals allow to extract the two-loop short-distance matching coefficients between quasi and light cone PDFs in large momentum effective theory, and are helpful to extract the nucleon PDFs from first principles.

## Key claim

The paper computes the master integrals needed for two-loop QCD corrections to quasi parton distribution functions (PDFs) in large momentum effective theory (LaMET), providing analytic results expressed in terms of Goncharov polylogarithms; these integrals enable the extraction of two-loop short-distance matching coefficients between quasi and light-cone PDFs, but the actual matching coefficients themselves are not derived in this work.

## Method

The authors used integration-by-parts (IBP) reduction with the FIRE package to reduce all two-loop Feynman integrals appearing in quasi-PDF amplitudes into three families of master integrals (36, 32, and 28 independent integrals, respectively). They applied the method of differential equations with a canonical basis choice following Henn (2013) to obtain analytic results, determining boundary conditions from regularity conditions at special kinematic points. The results are expressed in terms of Goncharov polylogarithms (GPLs) and validated against numerical evaluations using the FIESTA package.

## Key numbers

* Family 1 contains 36 linearly independent master integrals; Family 2 contains 32; Family 3 contains 28.
* For off-shell quarks, the range of x is divided into 4 regions for Family 1 (x < -1, -1 < x < 0, 0 < x < 1, x > 1) and 3 regions for Families 2 and 3 (x < 0, 0 < x < 1, x > 1).
* For on-shell quarks (p1^2 = 0), there are 14 independent integrals for Family 1, 13 for Family 2, and 12 for Family 3.
* Validation of integral I^1_{1,1,0,0,2,1,0} at (p1^2 = -1/2, x = 1/3, p_z = 1): analytic result 1/epsilon * (-2.492900960) + 0.4498613241 + epsilon * (-21.287203876) agrees with FIESTA numerical result 1/epsilon * (-2.49290 +/- 0.0000652) + 0.449836 +/- 0.000847 + epsilon * (-21.2872 +/- 0.004169).
* Validation of same integral at on-shell point (p1^2 = 0, x = 1/3, p_z = 1): analytic result 0.5/epsilon^2 + (-0.4315231087)/epsilon + 4.9871880743 + epsilon * (-15.840344856) agrees with FIESTA result 0.5 +/- 0.000017/epsilon^2 + (-0.431509 +/- 0.000130)/epsilon + 4.98750 +/- 0.001077 + epsilon * (-15.8377 +/- 0.007512).

## Context

This work extends the authors' own previous NNLO calculation for flavor non-diagonal quark contributions to quasi PDFs (Chen, Wang, Zhu 2020) by providing the full set of master integrals needed for all two-loop QCD corrections. The results are essential for computing two-loop matching coefficients between quasi and light-cone PDFs in LaMET, which was originally proposed by Ji (2013, 2014). Current lattice extractions of PDFs mostly rely on one-loop perturbative kernels, so these two-loop master integrals will help reduce theoretical uncertainties in future lattice QCD determinations of nucleon PDFs from first principles.
