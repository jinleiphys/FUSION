# Non-locality of the nucleon-nucleon potential from Lattice QCD

> The Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function for two nucleons on the lattice has been shown to yield a non-local and energy-independent nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, U(r,r'). In practice, the derivative expansion of U(r,r') is currently employed to determine the potential at low energies. In this report, we study the magnitude of non-locality to check the convergence of such a derivative expansion. With quenched lattice QCD at m_π= 530MeV, we compare the NN potentials at the center of mass energy E ~ 0 MeV and at E ~ 45 MeV. We also investigate the angular momentum dependence of the spin singlet potential, by comparing the potentials in 1S0 and 1D2 channels. We find that the non-locality and the angular momentum dependence in the above energy range are negligible within statistical errors.

## Key claim
The non-locality and angular momentum dependence of the nucleon-nucleon potential extracted from lattice QCD are negligible within statistical errors for center-of-mass energies up to approximately 45 MeV and angular momenta up to L=2, supporting the validity of using only the leading-order local term in the derivative expansion of the non-local potential.

## Method
The authors computed the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function for two nucleons in quenched lattice QCD on a 32^3x48 lattice at beta=5.7 with pion mass 530 MeV. They defined a "truncated local potential" from the NBS wave function and compared it at two different center-of-mass energies (approximately 0 MeV and 45 MeV) in the ^1S_0 channel, and between the ^1S_0 and ^1D_2 channels at 45 MeV, using periodic and anti-periodic boundary conditions with wall and momentum-wall sources respectively.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: m_pi = 530 MeV; nucleon mass: m_N = 1.33 GeV; lattice spacing: a^{-1} = 1.44(2) GeV (a = 0.137 fm); spatial extension: L = 32a = 4.4 fm
- Center-of-mass energies compared: E = 0 MeV (periodic boundary condition) and E = 45 MeV (anti-periodic boundary condition)
- 4000 quenched gauge configurations accumulated; potentials measured with configurations separated by 200 sweeps
- Ground state saturation for potentials confirmed at time separation t-t_0 >= 9

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration method (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2009) for extracting nucleon-nucleon potentials from lattice QCD via the NBS wave function. It directly tests the convergence of the derivative expansion previously proposed by Aoki (2009) for the non-local potential U(r,r'). The results justify using leading-order local potentials obtained at zero energy for applications up to 45 MeV and L=2, and enable future extraction of next-to-leading order terms such as the spin-orbit potential from finite angular momentum NBS wave functions.
