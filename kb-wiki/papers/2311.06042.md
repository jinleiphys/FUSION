# Coherent interactions of a fast proton with a short-range $NN$ correlation in the nucleus

> Nuclear structure at short $NN$-distances is still poorly understood. In particular, the full quantum structure of the nucleus with a correlated $NN$-pair is a challenge to theory. So far, model descriptions have been limited to the average mean-field picture of the remaining nuclear system after removing the $NN$-pair. In the recent experiment of the BM@N Collaboration at JINR \cite{Patsyuk:2021fju}, the reactions $^{12}\mbox{C}(p,2pn_s)^{10}\mbox{B}$ and $^{12}\mbox{C}(p,2pp_s)^{10}\mbox{Be}$ induced by the hard elastic $pp$ scattering were studied. Here, $n_s$ or $p_s$ denote the undetected slow nucleon in the rest frame of $^{12}\mbox{C}$. In contrast to the previous experiments, the residual bound nucleus was also detected which requires a new level of theoretical understanding. In the present work, we apply the technique of fractional parentage coefficients of the translationally-invariant shell model (TISM) to calculate the spectroscopic amplitude of the system $NN-B$ where $B$ is the remaining nuclear system. The spectroscopic amplitude enters the full amplitude of a nuclear reaction. The relative $NN-B$ wave function is no longer a free parameter of the model but is uniquely related to the internal state of $B$. The interaction of the target proton with the $NN$-pair is considered in the impulse approximation. We also include the initial- and final state interactions of absorptive type as well as the single charge exchange processes. Our calculations are in a reasonable agreement with the BM@N data.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the translationally-invariant shell model (TISM) with fractional parentage coefficients can describe the coherent production of specific low-lying states of the residual nucleus in reactions induced by hard elastic proton-proton scattering, going beyond the average mean-field picture of previous models.

## Method

The authors apply the technique of fractional parentage coefficients of the TISM to calculate the spectroscopic amplitude for separating a correlated NN-pair from the 12C nucleus, with the relative NN-B wave function uniquely determined by the internal state of the residual nucleus rather than being a free parameter. The hard elastic pp scattering is treated in the impulse approximation, while initial- and final-state interactions of absorptive type and single charge exchange processes are included via the eikonal approximation. The internal wave functions of the NN-pairs are taken from phenomenology: the deuteron wave function for (S,T)=(1,0) pairs and the 1S0 wave function of free pp-scattering at zero energy for (S,T)=(0,1) pairs.

## Key numbers

- About 20-25% of nucleons in medium-to-heavy nuclei are in the state of short-range correlations, populating the momentum distribution above the Fermi momentum of ~250 MeV/c
- The missing momentum range p_miss = 400-800 MeV/c indicates the transition from dominating tensor- to dominating repulsive scalar NN interaction
- The oscillator rule fixes the relative NN-B motion quantum number n = N_A - N_X - N_B, giving n=2 for transitions from 12C ground state to residual nucleus in the s^4p^6 configuration
- The absorption factors vary on a length scale of 2/(ρ_0 σ_NN) ≃ 3 fm, where ρ_0 ≃ 0.16 fm^-3 and σ_NN ≃ 40 mb
- The harmonic oscillator parameter r_0 ≃ 1.6 fm characterizes the length scale of the relative X-B motion wave function

## Context

This work extends the TISM spectroscopic approach previously used to analyze quasi-elastic knock-out of fast deuterons from light nuclei by protons at 670 MeV (Albrecht 1979, Ero 1981, Zhusupov 1986, Zhusupov 1987). It directly addresses the BM@N Collaboration experiment at JINR (Patsyuk 2021) on reactions 12C(p,2pn_s)10B and 12C(p,2pp_s)10Be, where the residual bound nucleus was detected for the first time. The approach contrasts with the generalized contact formalism (Cruz-Torres 2019) which does not include information about the internal wave function of the residual nucleus and implicitly gives the total production rate for all possible internal states. The calculations enable future fully exclusive measurements where individual low-lying states of the residual nucleus can be resolved, providing cleaner information on short-range correlation dynamics.
