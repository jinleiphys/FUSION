# Violation of mass ordering for multi-strange hadrons at RHIC and LHC

> We study effects of the hadronic rescattering on final observables especially for multi-strange hadrons such as $ϕ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$ in high-energy heavy-ion collisions within an integrated dynamical approach. In this approach, (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics is combined with a microscopic transport model, JAM. We simulate the collisions with or without hadronic rescatterings and compare observables between these two options so that we quantify the effects of the hadronic rescattering. We find that the mean transverse momentum and the elliptic flow parameter of multi-strange hadrons are less affected by hadronic rescattering and, as a result, the mass ordering of the $p_T$-differential elliptic flow parameter $v_2(p_T)$ is violated: At the RHIC and the LHC energies the $v_2(p_T)$ for $ϕ$-mesons is larger than that for protons in the low-$p_T$ regions.

## Key claim

The mass ordering of the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2(p_T)\) is violated for multi-strange hadrons at both RHIC and LHC energies: the \(v_2(p_T)\) for \(\phi\)-mesons becomes larger than that for protons in the low-\(p_T\) region, because multi-strange hadrons are less affected by hadronic rescattering than non-strange hadrons.

## Method

The authors used an integrated dynamical approach combining (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with the microscopic hadron cascade model JAM. They simulated Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV on an event-by-event basis, using a Monte Carlo Glauber model for initial conditions and the s95p-v1.1 equation of state. They compared observables from simulations with hadronic rescatterings (default JAM) to simulations without hadronic rescatterings (only resonance decays) to quantify the effects of hadronic rescattering on multi-strange hadrons.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV), proton \(v_2(p_T)\) crosses \(\phi\)-meson \(v_2(p_T)\) at approximately 1.5 GeV, violating the conventional mass ordering.
- At LHC energy (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV), the crossing point between proton and \(\phi\)-meson \(v_2(p_T)\) shifts to a higher \(p_T\) region compared to RHIC, with the violation occurring below about 2 GeV.
- Pion \(v_2\) increases by about 20% during the hadronic stage, while the \(v_2\) of all other hadrons shows a much smaller increase of 0-5%.
- The switching temperature from hydrodynamics to JAM was set to 155 MeV to reproduce experimentally observed pion-to-kaon and pion-to-proton ratios at RHIC.
- The lifetime of \(\phi\)-mesons is about 47 fm/\(c\), larger than the typical system lifetime of about 10 fm/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends earlier hydro + cascade predictions of mass ordering violation for \(\phi\)-mesons, which were recently observed by the STAR collaboration (Nasim, 2012). The study systematically quantifies the effect for \(\Xi\)- and \(\Omega\)-baryons as well as \(\phi\)-mesons, using a more sophisticated integrated dynamical model than previous approaches. The results demonstrate that multi-strange hadrons freeze out soon after the fluid stage and can serve as "penetrating" probes of the quark gluon plasma, similar to photons and dileptons but providing information specifically about the hadronization stage.
