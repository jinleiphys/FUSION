# Final state interactions in semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering: Application to T2K and MINER$ν$A experiments

> We present a complete comparison of semi-inclusive $ν_μ$-$^{12}$C cross-section measurements by T2K and MINER$ν$A collaborations with the predictions from the SuSAv2-MEC model implemented in the neutrino-nucleus event generator GENIE and an unfactorized approach based on the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA). Results, that include cross sections as function of the final muon and proton kinematics and correlations between both, show that the agreement with data obtained by the RDWIA approach, that accounts for final-state interactions, matches or improves GENIE-SuSAv2 predictions for very forward angles where scaling violations are relevant.

## Key claim
The RDWIA approach, which accounts for final-state interactions (FSI) using a relativistic optical potential or a modified relativistic mean-field potential, matches or improves upon the GENIE-SuSAv2 predictions for semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections, particularly at very forward angles where scaling violations are relevant. The improvement is conditional on the specific FSI model used and the kinematic region considered.

## Method
The authors compare semi-inclusive νμ-12C cross-section measurements from T2K and MINERνA with two theoretical approaches: (1) an unfactorized RDWIA model that describes the ejected nucleon as a scattering solution of the Dirac equation with either a relativistic optical potential (ROP) fitted to elastic proton-nucleus scattering data or an energy-dependent relativistic mean-field (ED-RMF) potential; and (2) the SuSAv2-MEC inclusive model implemented in the GENIE event generator via a factorization approximation, where hadron kinematics are generated by sampling from a local Fermi gas and applying a cascade FSI model. For both approaches, SuSAv2-2p2h MEC and pion absorption contributions from GENIE are added to the quasielastic results for comparison with the CC0πNp cross-section data.

## Key numbers
- T2K phase-space restrictions: muon momentum k' > 0.25 GeV, cosθ_l > -0.6, proton momentum p_N = 0.45-1.0 GeV, cosθ_N^L > 0.4
- MINERνA phase-space restrictions: muon momentum k' = 1.5-10 GeV, cosθ_l > 0.939, proton momentum p_N = 0.45-1.2 GeV, cosθ_N^L > 0.342
- Missing energy density parameters for 12C: 1s1/2 shell mean μ_κ = 37.0 MeV, standard deviation σ_κ = 10.0 MeV, occupation number n_κ = 1.9; 1p3/2 shell μ_κ = 17.8 MeV, σ_κ = 2.0 MeV, n_κ = 3.3; background parameter a = 0.031127 MeV^-1, b = 0.011237 MeV^-1, c = 40 MeV, w = 5 MeV
- SuSAv2 hadron tensor tables in GENIE use a binning of 5 MeV in transferred momentum and energy

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of T2K and MINERνA semi-inclusive CC0π cross-section measurements in the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) by Franco-Patino et al. (2021) to include FSI within a fully relativistic, quantum mechanical, and unfactorized approach. It tests the validity of the factorization approximations used by neutrino event generators like GENIE to obtain hadron kinematics from inclusive models such as SuSAv2, which was implemented in GENIE by Dolan et al. (2021). The RDWIA-ROP approach has been extensively applied to describe exclusive electron scattering (e,e'p) cross-section measurements by Udias et al. (1993, 1995) and Amaro (2021). The results enable benchmarking of generator-level approximations against microscopic calculations and support improved modeling of nuclear effects for oscillation experiments like T2K, MINERνA, DUNE, and Hyper-K.
