# Upper Limit on the molecular resonance strengths in the ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C fusion reaction

> Carbon burning is a crucial process for a number of important astrophysical scenarios. The lowest measured energy is around E$_{\rm c.m.}$=2.1 MeV, only partially overlapping with the energy range of astrophysical interest. The currently adopted reaction rates are based on an extrapolation which is highly uncertain because of potential resonances existing in the unmeasured energy range and the complication of the effective nuclear potential. By comparing the cross sections of the three carbon isotope fusion reactions, ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C, ${}^{12}$C+${}^{13}$C and ${}^{13}$C+${}^{13}$C, we have established an upper limit on the molecular resonance strengths in ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C fusion reaction. The preliminary results are presented and the impact on nuclear astrophysics is discussed.

## Key claim

By comparing the cross sections of the three carbon isotope fusion reactions, the authors establish an upper limit on the molecular resonance strengths in the ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C fusion reaction, but they cannot rule out the possibility of a strong resonance formed by a different mechanism in the unmeasured energy range.

## Method

The authors compared the experimental S* factors (modified astrophysical S factors) for the ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C, ${}^{12}$C+${}^{13}$C, and ${}^{13}$C+${}^{13}$C fusion reactions, finding that the ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C peak cross sections match the smooth cross sections of the other two systems. They then fitted the Equivalent Square Well (ESW) model to the major ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C resonance peak cross sections above 3 MeV and extrapolated to lower energies to establish an upper limit. A separate upper limit was estimated using coupled-channels calculations with the Akyuz-Winther potential.

## Key numbers

* The lowest measured energy for ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C fusion is E$_{\rm c.m.}$ = 2.1 MeV, where the cross section is below 1 nb.
* The strong resonance at E$_{\rm c.m.}$ = 2.14 MeV has a width less than 12 keV, much less than the common 100 keV observed at higher energies.
* The resonant cross section at 2.14 MeV is about a factor of 40 higher than the ESW extrapolation.
* The two resonances at 1.5 MeV and 2.14 MeV are about a factor of 10 higher than the Akyuz-Winther coupled-channels calculation.
* The new ${}^{12}$C+${}^{13}$C measurement reached a lowest cross section of about 20 nb, a factor of 50 smaller than achieved by Dayras (1975).
* The coupled-channels calculation with the Akyuz-Winther potential over-predicts the ${}^{12}$C+${}^{13}$C fusion cross section at 2.6 MeV by a factor of 3.4.
* The upper limit established with the ESW fit is only 20% higher than the currently adopted rate based on an averaged S* factor of 3$\times$10$^{16}$ MeV*b.
* The real ${}^{12}$C+${}^{12}$C fusion rate should not be more than a factor of 6 stronger than the currently adopted rate in the energy range of 1 to 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of molecular resonances in carbon fusion, first discovered by Almqvist, Kuehner and Bromley (1960) at energies E$_{\rm c.m.}$ = 5.68, 6.00, and 6.32 MeV, and later observed down to 2.14 MeV by Spillane (2007). The paper contradicts the hypothetical resonance proposed by Cooper (2009) that would enhance the fusion rate by over two orders of magnitude at T$_9$ = 0.5 K to explain superburst ignition. The established upper limit provides an additional constraint for superburst models, suggesting that unknown physics processes (such as additional heating in the neutron star crust) must be considered instead.
