# Effect of momentum anisotropy on quark matter in the quark-meson model

> We investigate the chiral phase structure of quark matter with spheroidal momentum-space anisotropy specified by one anisotropy parameter $ξ$ in the 2+1 flavor quark-meson model. We find that the chiral phase diagram and the location of the critical endpoint (CEP) are affected significantly by the value of $ξ$. With the increase of $ξ$, the CEP is shifted to smaller temperatures and larger quark chemical potentials. And the temperature of the CEP is more sensitive to the anisotropy parameter than the corresponding quark chemical potential, which is opposite to the study for finite system volume effect. Furthermore, the effects of momentum anisotropy on the thermodynamic properties and scalar (pseudoscalar) meson masses are also studied at vanishing quark chemical potential. The numerical results show that an increase of $ξ$ can hinder the restoration of chiral symmetry. We also find that shear viscosity and electrical conductivity decrease as $ξ$ grows. However, bulk viscosity exhibits a significant non-trivial behavior with $ξ$ in the entire temperature domain of interest.

## Key claim

The chiral phase diagram and the location of the critical endpoint (CEP) are significantly affected by the momentum-space anisotropy parameter ξ, with the CEP shifting to smaller temperatures and larger quark chemical potentials as ξ increases; the temperature of the CEP is more sensitive to ξ than the quark chemical potential coordinate, which is opposite to the trend observed for finite system volume effects.

## Method

The authors adopt the 2+1 flavor quark-meson model under the mean-field approximation and introduce momentum-space anisotropy by replacing the isotropic equilibrium distribution function in the fermionic thermodynamic potential with a spheroidally deformed distribution characterized by a single anisotropy parameter ξ, following the Romatschke-Strickland prescription. For small ξ (|ξ| ≪ 1), the anisotropic distribution is expanded to linear order in ξ, and the ξ-dependent thermodynamic potential is minimized to obtain the chiral condensates as functions of temperature T and quark chemical potential μ. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity, electrical conductivity, bulk viscosity) are computed using the relativistic Boltzmann equation under the relaxation time approximation with the linearized anisotropic distribution.

## Key numbers

- For ξ = 0 (isotropic case), the chiral critical temperature at μ = 0 is T_c^χ = 146 MeV for the non-strange sector and T_s^χ = 248 MeV for the strange sector.
- For ξ = 0.4, T_c^χ increases to 159 MeV and T_s^χ increases to 270 MeV; for ξ = -0.4, T_c^χ decreases to 137 MeV and T_s^χ decreases to 233 MeV.
- The CEP location shifts from (T_CEP, μ_CEP) = (91, 222) MeV at ξ = 0 to (79, 270) MeV at ξ = 0.4 and to (100, 174) MeV at ξ = -0.4.
- The value of μ_CEP increases by about 50% from ξ = -0.4 to ξ = 0.4, while T_CEP changes by about 20%.
- At high temperature and μ = 0, the scaled pressure P/T^4 for ξ = -0.4 stabilizes at approximately 4.0, compared to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of about 5.2.
- The vacuum condensate values at T = 0 are σ_x^0 ≈ 92.4 MeV and σ_y^0 ≈ 94.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral phase transitions in QCD effective models by incorporating momentum-space anisotropy, which has been previously studied in the context of parton self-energies (Romatschke and Strickland, 2003), transport coefficients (Rath et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2020), and heavy-quark potential (Dumitru et al., 2007), but not in the framework of chiral effective models. The results contrast with finite-volume effects studied in the quark-meson model (Tripolt et al., 2013), where the temperature coordinate of the CEP is more strongly affected than the chemical potential coordinate, and with non-extensive effects in the linear sigma model (Shen et al., 2017). The findings enable future studies of momentum anisotropy in more realistic descriptions of quark matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
