# Odd-even staggering and shell effects of charge radii for nuclei with even $Z$ from $36$ to $38$ and from $52$ to $62$

> A unified theoretical model reproducing charge radii of known atomic nuclei plays an essential role in making extrapolations for unknown nuclei. Recently developed new ansatz which phenomenologically takes into account the neutron-proton short-range correlations ($np$-SRCs) can describe the discontinuity properties and odd-even staggering (OES) effect of charge radii along isotopic chains remarkably well. In this work, we further review the modified root-mean-square (rms) charge radii formula in the framework of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The charge radii are calculated along various isotopic chains that include the nuclei featuring the $N=50$ and $82$ magic shells. Our results suggest that RMF with and without considering a correction term give an almost similar trend of nuclear size for some isotopic chains with open proton shell, especially the abrupt increases across the strong neutron closed shells and the OES behaviors. This reflects that the $np$-SRCs have almost no influence for some nuclei due to the strong coupling between different levels around Fermi surface. The weakening OES behavior of nuclear charge radii is observed generally at completely filled neutron shells and this may be proposed as a signature of magic indicator.

# Odd-even staggering and shell effects of charge radii for nuclei with even Z from 36 to 38 and from 52 to 62

## Key claim

The neutron-proton short-range correlations (np-SRCs) correction term in the modified charge radius formula has almost no influence on calculated charge radii for nuclei with open proton shells, because the Cooper pair condensation components for neutrons and protons are comparable due to strong coupling between levels around the Fermi surface. The weakening of odd-even staggering (OES) behavior at completely filled neutron shells (N=50 and N=82) is proposed as a signature of magicity.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set, solving the Dirac equation and Klein-Gordon equations using an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis (12 shells for fermion fields, 20 shells for meson fields). Ground state properties were obtained by constraining quadrupole deformation beta_20 from -0.60 to 0.60 in steps of 0.01, with pairing correlations treated via the BCS method using a delta force interaction (pairing strength V_0 = 322.8 MeV fm^3, pairing window 12 MeV above and below the Fermi surface). Charge radii were calculated both with the standard RMF formula and with a modified formula that adds a term proportional to the difference between neutron and proton Cooper pair condensation functions (D_n - D_p), using normalization constants a_0 = 0.834 for Z < 50 and a_0 = 0.22 for Z >= 50.

## Key numbers

- Normalization constant a_0 = 0.834 for nuclei with Z < 50, and a_0 = 0.22 for nuclei with Z >= 50, used in the modified charge radius formula.
- Pairing strength V_0 = 322.8 MeV fm^3 used for all calculations across isotopic chains.
- For 90Sr, D_n = 1.9021 while D_p = 0, giving a large difference that overestimates OES.
- For 75Kr, the calculated quadrupole deformation beta_20 is approximately 0.48, while experimental data suggest beta_20 approximately 0.27.
- For 98-100Sr, the calculated quadrupole deformation parameters beta_20 are almost 0.45.
- Self-consistent iteration convergence criterion: binding energy lower than 10^-6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the modified charge radius formula previously developed by An et al. (2020) for Cu and In isotopic chains, applying it to even-Z isotopic chains with Z = 36, 38, 52, 54, 56, 58, 60, and 62 that feature the N=50 and N=82 magic shells. The study tests the claim by Miller (2018) that np-SRCs have no contribution to charge radii for some open shell nuclei. The results are consistent with experimental data compiled by Angeli (2013) and with earlier RMF calculations by Lalazissis (1995) for kinks at N=50. The work enables future inclusion of np-SRCs self-consistently in density functional theory and suggests that the weakening OES at magic numbers can serve as a magicity indicator across the nuclear chart.
