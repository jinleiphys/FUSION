# T-matrix analysis of pion-proton femtoscopy

> The observed shift of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance peak in $π$-$p$ femtoscopic correlations challenges the conventional Breit-Wigner description of resonances in femtoscopy. We revisit the Koonin-Pratt framework by formulating femtoscopy in the momentum-space representation and employing the T-matrix approach to disentangle on-shell and off-shell contributions. By employing a Friedrichs-Lee model constrained by low-energy scattering data, we demonstrate that the finite spatial extent of the emission source induces sensitivity to off-shell dynamics, which leads to a peak shift accompanied by a dip on the high-momentum side of the peak. The resulting correlation strength, however, does not fully reproduce the measured amplitude, and a high-momentum side dip is not observed in experiments. The remaining discrepancies may be attributed to the structural complexity of the emission source beyond a simple spherical Gaussian approximation.

## Key claim

The observed shift of the Δ(1232) resonance peak in π-p femtoscopic correlations arises from off-shell scattering dynamics induced by the finite spatial extent of the emission source, rather than from an explicit shift of the resonance pole position; however, the calculated correlation strength does not fully reproduce the measured amplitude and a predicted high-momentum side dip is not observed in experiments.

## Method

The authors reformulated the Koonin-Pratt femtoscopy framework in momentum-space representation and employed a T-matrix approach to separate on-shell and off-shell contributions to the correlation function. They constructed a Friedrichs-Lee model constrained by fitting the background-subtracted cross section of π⁺-p → Δ⁺⁺ from Carter (1971), with parameters E_Δ0 = 1327.69 MeV, g_0 = 0.026 MeV^{-1/2}, and Λ = 458.80 MeV, and applied a spectral decomposition to isolate interference and scattering components.

## Key numbers

- The fitted resonance pole lies at √s_pole = 1211.65 − i 98.88/2 MeV, and the resonance peak position at E_peak = 1231.19 MeV, consistent with vacuum Δ(1232) properties from the Particle Data Group.
- The model parameters are E_Δ0 = 1327.69 MeV, g_0 = 0.026 MeV^{-1/2}, and Λ = 458.80 MeV, extracted from fitting the background-subtracted π⁺-p → Δ⁺⁺ cross section.
- Gaussian source radii from R ≃ 0.8 to 2.5 fm were used, consistent with radii extracted from experimental fits in different m_T regions in the ALICE (2025) measurement.

## Context

This work extends the Koonin-Pratt framework (Koonin 1977, Pratt 1986) by incorporating a T-matrix formulation to study resonance femtoscopy, addressing the peak shift observed by ALICE (2025) in π-p correlations at √s = 13 TeV pp collisions. The approach contrasts with previous explanations invoking in-medium mass shifts (Zhang 2025) or re-scattering effects (Reichert 2019), and establishes a scattering-based baseline that isolates off-shell dynamics as the origin of the peak shift. The remaining discrepancies with experimental data point to the need for more sophisticated source models beyond a simple spherical Gaussian approximation.
