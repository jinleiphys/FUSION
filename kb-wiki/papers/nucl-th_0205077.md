# Extended 9Li+n+n three-body model of 11Li with the pairing correlation in 9Li

> We discuss the binding mechanism of 11Li based on an extended three-body model of Li+n+n. In the model, we take into account the pairing correlation of p-shell neutrons in 9Li, in addition to that of valence neutrons outside the 9Li nucleus, and solve the coupled-channel two- and three-body problems of 10Li and 11Li, respectively. The results show that degrees of freedom of the pairing correlation in 9Li play an important role in the structure of 10Li and 11Li. In 10Li, the pairing correlation in 9Li produces a so-called pairing-blocking effect due to the presence of valence neutron, which degenerates s- and p-wave neutron orbits energetically. In 11Li, on the other hand, the pairing-blocking effect is surpassed by the core-n interaction due to two degrees of freedom of two valence neutrons surrounding 9Li, and as a result, the ground state is dominated by the p-shell closed configuration and does not show a spatial extension with a large r.m.s. radius. These results indicate that the pairing correlation is realized differently in odd- and even-neutron systems of 10Li and 11Li. We further improve the tail part of the 9Li-n interaction, which works well to reproduce the observed large r.m.s. radius in 11Li.

## Key claim

The pairing correlation of p-shell neutrons in the 9Li core, treated via configuration mixing between the (0p3/2)^4 and (0p3/2)^2(0p1/2)^2 configurations, produces a pairing-blocking effect in 10Li that degenerates s- and p-wave neutron orbits, but in 11Li this blocking is surpassed by the core-n interaction from two valence neutrons, leading to a p-shell closed ground state without a large spatial extension unless the tail of the 9Li-n interaction is improved.

## Method

The authors solved a coupled-channel three-body model of 9Li+n+n for 11Li and a two-body model of 9Li+n for 10Li, using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) with a folding-type 9Li-n potential derived from the MHN interaction and the Minnesota interaction for the neutron-neutron force. The 9Li core was described by a linear combination of pairing configurations (C0: (0p3/2)^4 and C1: (0p3/2)^2(0p1/2)^2), and the three-body wave function was expanded using the hybrid-TV model (cluster orbital shell model plus extended cluster model) with Gaussian basis functions, solved variationally. The complex scaling method was employed to treat resonances in 10Li.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of 11Li: 0.31 MeV (from Audi (1993), cited as experimental value)
- Matter radius of 9Li: 2.32 ± 0.02 fm (from Tanihata (1988), used to set the harmonic oscillator length parameter b = 1.69 fm)
- 1+ resonance energy in 10Li: 0.42 MeV (from Bohlen et al. (1993), used to fit the δ parameter of the 9Li-n interaction)
- Pairing matrix element for 0p3/2-0p3/2 in 9Li: -3.95 MeV (MHN interaction), -3.93 MeV (HN-1), -3.59 MeV (GPT central only), -2.81 MeV (GPT with central+LS+tensor)
- Pairing matrix element for 0p3/2-0p1/2 in 9Li: -2.76 MeV (MHN), -2.78 MeV (HN-1), -2.57 MeV (GPT central only), -3.67 MeV (GPT with central+LS+tensor)
- Mixing probability of the C1 configuration in 9Li: 15.5% (MHN, labeled PC-W), 25.0% (KYI, labeled PC-S)
- Energy gain from configuration mixing in 9Li: -1.18 MeV (MHN), -3.25 MeV (KYI)
- LS splitting in p-shell: 1.62 MeV (HN-1), 1.65 MeV (MHN), 0.92 MeV (GPT with central+LS+tensor)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body model of 11Li (Myo et al. (1998)), which used a frozen 9Li core with a single (0p3/2)^4 configuration and underbound the ground state by about 1 MeV relative to experiment. It also builds on their study of 10Li (Kato et al. (1999)), where the pairing-blocking effect from the 9Li core was shown to explain the low-energy s-wave states. The approach contrasts with earlier three-body models by Thompson and Zhukov (1994), who used state-dependent 9Li-n interactions without a microscopic origin for the state dependence. The present work enables a unified description of 10Li and 11Li where the pairing correlation in the core is treated explicitly, and it provides a foundation for future studies of the soft dipole resonance and excited states in 11Li.
