# Spectral properties of $ρ$ meson in a magnetic field

> We calculate the rho meson mass in a weak magnetic field using effective $ρππ$ interaction. It is seen that both $ρ^0$ and $ρ^\pm$ masses decrease with the magnetic field in vacuum. $ρ$ meson dispersion relation has been calculated and shown to be different for $ρ^0$ and $ρ^\pm$. We also calculate the $ρππ$ decay width and spectral functions of $ρ^0$ and $ρ^\pm$. The width is seen to decrease with $eB$ and the spectral functions become narrower.

## Key claim

Using an effective $\rho\pi\pi$ interaction in a weak magnetic field (where the QCD scale dominates over $eB$), both neutral and charged $\rho$ meson masses decrease with increasing field strength, but the authors cannot predict the exact critical field for condensation because their weak-field expansion is only valid up to $\mathcal{O}((eB)^2)$.

## Method

The authors calculated the one-loop self-energy of the $\rho$ meson in a constant weak magnetic field using the Schwinger proper-time formalism for charged scalar propagators, expanded to leading order $\mathcal{O}((eB)^2)$. They employed a phenomenological $\rho\pi\pi$ interaction Lagrangian, performed dimensional regularization and renormalization with the condition ${\rm Re}\Pi_{\rm vac}(k^2=m_\rho^{\ast2})=0$, and solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation to obtain the effective mass, dispersion relations, decay width, and spectral functions for both $\rho^0$ and $\rho^\pm$.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant $g_{\rho\pi\pi}=6.03$, obtained from the vacuum decay width of $\rho\rightarrow\pi\pi$ as 150 MeV.
- Pion mass taken as $m_\pi=0.14$ GeV; vacuum $\rho$ mass taken as $m_\rho=770$ MeV.
- For both $\rho^0$ and $\rho^\pm$, effective mass decreases with $eB$; at $eB=0.2$ GeV$^2$, the effective mass of $\rho^\pm$ remains non-zero.
- The decay width $\Gamma_\rho$ decreases with $eB$ but remains finite even at the maximum studied field $eB=0.2$ GeV$^2$.
- The critical field for $\rho$ meson condensation is predicted to be higher than $eB_c\sim 0.2$ GeV$^2$ reported by Liu et al. (2015) in an NJL model.

## Context

This work extends the methodology of Ghosh et al. (2016) on pion mass and dispersion relations in a magnetic field to the $\rho$ meson system, using the same effective Lagrangian approach with bosonic propagators. It contrasts with the NJL model calculation of Liu et al. (2015), which found charged $\rho$ masses vanishing at $eB_c\sim 0.2$ GeV$^2$ using fermionic propagators with $\mathcal{O}(eB)$ corrections; the present work finds no $\mathcal{O}(eB)$ corrections because bosonic propagators have leading $\mathcal{O}((eB)^2)$ corrections. The results partially agree with lattice QCD for $\rho^0$ with $S_z=0$, $\rho^+$ with $S_z=+1$, and $\rho^-$ with $S_z=-1$, where mass decreases with field, but disagree for other spin states. The spectral function narrowing with increasing field qualitatively supports the vacuum superconductivity scenario proposed by Chernodub (2011).
