# Neutrino interactions in neutron matter

> Neutrino flow is the dominant mechanism of energy transfer in the latest stages of supernovae explosions and in compact stars. The Standard Model of particle physics and accelerator data, provide a satisfactory description of neutrino physics in vacuum up to TeV scale. Nevertheless modeling the dynamics of neutrino interaction in the nuclear environment involves severe difficulties. This thesis in mainly aimed at obtaining the weak response of infinite matter, using both the Correlated Basis Function theory and Landau Theory of Fermi liquid to take into account properly nucleon-nucleon hard core potential and long range correlation (quasi-particle, collective modes, ecc.)

## Key claim

The thesis develops a correlated basis function (CBF) approach to compute the weak response of infinite neutron matter, showing that an effective two-nucleon interaction derived from the Argonne v6 potential, when combined with a density-dependent three-body force model, reproduces the ground state energy of pure neutron matter in agreement with state-of-the-art variational and Monte Carlo calculations, but the approach involves truncation at the two-body cluster level and neglects explicit momentum-dependent contributions from the correlation gradient term.

## Method

The author constructs correlated basis states by applying a symmetrized product of two-body correlation operators (with the same operatorial structure as the Argonne v6 potential) to non-interacting Fermi gas wavefunctions, then derives an effective two-nucleon interaction w12 from a cluster expansion of the Hamiltonian expectation value truncated at the two-body level. The effective interaction is obtained as w12 = f12(-(1/m)∇² + v12)f12, where f12 are correlation functions determined by solving Euler-Lagrange equations from functional minimization of the two-body cluster energy, with boundary conditions enforcing healing at distances d and dt for central and tensor channels respectively. The ground state energy is then computed using this effective interaction in a Hartree-Fock-like expression, with three-body force effects incorporated through the density-dependent modification of Lagaris and Pandharipande (1981).

## Key numbers

- Compressibility modulus of symmetric nuclear matter: K = 230 MeV
- Density at which effective interaction components are compared to bare Argonne v6: ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Neutron star radius after cooling: approximately 10-15 km
- Timescale for neutrino mean-free-path to exceed star radius: approximately 50 s

## Context

This work extends the Correlated Basis Function approach of Feenberg (1969) and Clark (1979) to neutrino interactions in dense matter, building on the FHNC/SOC variational calculations of Akmal et al. (1998) and the effective interaction method of Pandharipande and Benhar. The results are compared to the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of Fantoni et al. using the Argonne v8 interaction and to the variational FHNC-SOC results of Akmal et al. using Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX. The approach enables calculation of neutrino response functions in neutron matter for supernova and neutron star applications, where the effective interaction provides a screened potential suitable for perturbative treatment.
