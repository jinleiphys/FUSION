# Thermal rates for baryon and anti-baryon production

> We use a form of the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to derive formulas giving the rate of production of spin-1/2 baryons in terms of the fluctuations of either meson or quark fields. The most general formulas do not assume thermal or chemical equilibrium. When evaluated in a thermal ensemble we find equilibration times on the order of 10 fm/c near the critical temperature in QCD.

## Key claim
The paper derives baryon/anti-baryon production rates using a fluctuation-dissipation theorem that does not assume thermal or chemical equilibrium, but the numerical equilibration times of about 10 fm/c near the QCD critical temperature are evaluated specifically for a thermal ensemble with a Boltzmann weighting of states.

## Method
The authors use linear response theory and a version of the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to express the production rate of spin-1/2 baryons in terms of the imaginary part of retarded correlation functions of vector and axial-vector currents or fields. These currents are expressed either in terms of pion fields using the nonlinear sigma model or in terms of quark fields using vector meson dominance (VMD). The rates are then evaluated in thermal equilibrium, and the spectral densities for the currents are taken from perturbative QCD, with form factors extracted from experimental antiproton-proton annihilation cross sections parameterized by Cugnon and Vandermeulen.

## Key numbers
- Equilibration times on the order of 10 fm/c near the critical temperature in QCD (abstract).
- The pion decay constant is \( f_{\pi} \approx 93 \) MeV.
- The axial coupling constant is \( g_A \approx 1.26 \).
- The QCD coupling at the tau mass scale is \( \alpha_s(m_{\tau}^2) = 0.35 \pm 0.03 \), used because \( m_{\tau} = 1777 \) MeV is close to \( 2m_N \).
- The form factor for nucleon/anti-nucleon production is fit with a monopole function \( F_{\rm ANN}(s) = 1 / [2.21 + (s-4m_N^2)/\Lambda^2] \) where \( \Lambda = 1.63 \) GeV.
- The total rate for nucleon/anti-nucleon production in the nonrelativistic limit is \( R(\bar{N}N) = \frac{9}{2(2\pi)^4} \left( 1+\frac{\alpha_{s}(4m_N^2)}{\pi} + \cdots \right) \frac{m_N^5 T^3}{f_{\pi}^{4}} \exp\left(-2m_N/T\right) \).
- For a system at fixed temperature, the equilibration times for baryons (proton, lambda, sigma, cascade) are plotted as functions of temperature, with intersections at 10 fm/c suggesting freezeout temperatures in the range 168 to 180 MeV (from Fig. 3, described in text).

## Context
This work extends the fluctuation-dissipation approach previously used for dilepton production to baryon/anti-baryon production, providing a more general description than either kinetic theory (reviewed by Koch, Muller, and Rafelski) or the disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) approach (used by Kapusta and Wong for Omega production). The results enable calculation of chemical equilibration times for baryons in hot QCD matter, which can be compared to data from RHIC and SPS heavy ion collisions, and can be used in rate equations for expanding and cooling systems.
