# Higher-order transverse momentum fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations are sensitive to overlap area and energy density fluctuations in initial state. We present a framework to calculate $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ fluctuations up to $4^{\mathrm{th}}$-order using standard and subevent methods, which is validated using the HIJING model. We observe a power-law dependence for cumulants of all orders as a function of charged particle multiplicity $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$, consistent with a simple independent source picture. The fluctuation in $pp$ collisions is observed to be larger than for $p+$Pb, Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions at the same $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ due to bias in number of contributing sources. The short-range correlations are greatly suppressed in subevent method in comparison to calculations based on the standard method. This study provides a baseline for transverse momentum fluctuations without the presence of final state effects.

## Key claim
The paper presents a framework for calculating transverse momentum fluctuations up to 4th order using standard and subevent methods, validated with the HIJING model, but the results are explicitly a baseline without final-state effects; the observed power-law scaling consistent with independent sources is a property of the HIJING model, not a claim about experimental data.

## Method
The authors derive algebraic expressions for 2nd-, 3rd-, and 4th-order pT cumulants using both a standard method (all particles in |η| < 2.5) and a two-subevent method (particles from two rapidity-separated windows: -2.5 < η < -0.75 and 0.75 < η < 2.5). They validate the framework by comparing with direct loop calculations and apply it to HIJING simulations of pp, p+Pb, Pb+Pb, and Xe+Xe collisions at center-of-mass energies around 5 TeV, calculating cumulants as functions of charged particle multiplicity Nch.

## Key numbers
- Power-law index b from fits of the form a/(Nch)^b for pT cumulants in Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions is "very close to (n-1)" expected for nth-order cumulant (exact numerical values not given in text).
- The scaled variance k2 from the two-subevent method is suppressed by a factor of 3 compared to the standard method in Pb+Pb collisions for particles with 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV.
- Simulations use pp, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV.
- Particles are selected from |η| < 2.5 with two pT ranges: 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV and 0.2 < pT < 5 GeV.
- Nch is defined using charged hadrons with |η| < 2.5 and 0.5 < pT < 5 GeV, consistent with ATLAS experiment choices.

## Context
This work extends previous measurements of pT variance in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (ALICE 2014, ATLAS 2017, STAR 2019), which found a power-law index of about 0.81 instead of the expected 1.0, indicating final-state effects. The paper provides a baseline from the HIJING model (Wang 1991) without final-state effects, enabling future experimental measurements of pT skewness and kurtosis to be compared against this independent-source picture, as recently predicted by Giacalone (2020) that skewness could probe nuclear deformation.
