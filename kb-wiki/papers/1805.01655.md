# Parametrization of cross sections for elementary hadronic collisions involving strange particles

> The production of strange particles (kaons, hyperons) and hypernuclei in light charged particle induced reactions in the energy range of a few GeV (2-15 GeV) has become a topic of active research in several facilities (e.g., HypHI and PANDA at GSI and/or FAIR (Germany), JLab (USA), and JPARC (Japan)). This energy range represents the low-energy limit of the string models (degree of freedom: quark and gluon) or the high-energy limit of the so-called spallation models (degree of freedom: hadrons). A well known spallation model is INCL, the Liège intranuclear cascade model (combined with a de-excitation model to complete the reaction). INCL, known to give good results up to 2-3 GeV, was recently upgraded by the implementation of multiple pion emission to extend the energy range of applicability up to roughly 15 GeV. The next step, to account also for strange particle production, both for refining the high energy domain and making it usable when strangeness appears, requires the following main ingredients: i) the relevant elementary cross sections (production, scattering, and absorption) and ii) the characteristics of the associated final states. Some of those ingredients are already known and, sometimes, are already used in models of the same type (e.g., Bertini, GiBUU), but this paper aims at reviewing the situation by compiling, updating, and comparing the necessary elementary information which are independent of the model used.

## Key claim

This paper compiles, updates, and compares elementary cross sections for strange particle production (kaons, antikaons, Lambda, Sigma) in hadronic collisions, providing parametrizations that are independent of any specific intranuclear cascade model and can be used by other model builders, but the authors note that the hadron exchange model procedure produced unreliable results for the πN → ΣK reaction and that the Bystricky procedure combined with experimental data was sufficient for that case.

## Method

The authors first surveyed available experimental data for reactions involving strangeness production in NN, πN, ΛN, ΣN, KN, and K̅N collisions, then applied two isospin-based methods to extend the database: the Bystricky procedure (using isospin decomposition of initial and final states) and a hadron exchange model (HEM) based on leading-order Feynman diagrams with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. For the 65% of channels still missing after the Bystricky procedure, the HEM method was applied using ratios of cross sections derived from isospin symmetry at the vertex level, with additional hypotheses about diagram contributions and exchange particle types.

## Key numbers

- The energy range considered is 2-15 GeV for light charged particle induced reactions
- INCL model was previously validated up to 2-3 GeV and extended to roughly 15 GeV via multiple pion emission implementation
- Strange particle production contributes a few percent of the nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section for energies from 2 GeV to 15 GeV
- Using only experimental data, reaction cross sections were determined for about 17% of the 488 isospin channels listed (excluding the inclusive reactions NN → K+X and πN → K+X)
- The Bystricky procedure increased the knowledge of reaction cross sections by approximately a factor of 2, bringing parametrized channels to 35%
- For the πN → ΣK reaction, 3 out of 10 channels were reasonably well measured
- Gini coefficients for data distribution across isospin channels range from 0.50 (ΛN → NΛ) to 0.89 (KN → NKππ)
- The K̅N → NK̅ reaction has 687 experimental data points across 6 channels, while NN → NΛKππ has only 14 data points across 16 channels

## Context

This work extends the INCL (Liège Intranuclear Cascade) model, which was recognized as one of the best spallation codes up to 2-3 GeV according to the 2010 IAEA benchmark, to include strange particle production for energies up to 15 GeV. The paper reviews and updates elementary cross sections that are already used in models such as Bertini, GiBUU, JAM, LAQGSM, and INCL2.0, providing a systematic and coherent set of parametrizations based on symmetry and simple hadronic models. The parametrizations enable future implementation of strange particle production in intranuclear cascade codes, which will allow simulation of experiments involving kaon emission and hypernuclei studies at facilities like HypHI, PANDA, JLab, and J-PARC.
