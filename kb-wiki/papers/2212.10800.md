# The time-like electromagnetic kaon form factor

> We compute the electromagnetic charged kaon form factor in the time-like region by employing a Poincaré covariant formalism of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to study quark-antiquark bound states in conjunction with the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator. Following a recent kindred calculation of the time-like electromagnetic pion form factor, we include the most relevant intermediate composite particles permitted by their quantum numbers in the interaction kernel to allow for a decay mechanism for the resonances involved. This term augments the usual gluon mediated interaction between quarks. For a sufficiently low energy time-like probing photon, the electromagnetic form factor is saturated by the $ρ(770)$ and $ϕ(1020)$ resonances. We assume $SU(2)$ isospin symmetry throughout. Our results for the absolute value squared of the electromagnetic form factor agree qualitatively rather well and quantitatively moderately so with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors compute the time-like electromagnetic charged kaon form factor using a Poincaré covariant Bethe-Salpeter/Schwinger-Dyson formalism, but the results are only in "qualitatively rather well and quantitatively moderately" agreement with experimental data, and the calculation is limited to the isospin symmetric limit (SU(2) symmetry) and includes only the impulse approximation diagrams, neglecting further couplings.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled formalism of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark bound states, using the Maris-Tandy model for the effective gluon interaction. They go beyond the standard rainbow-ladder truncation by including explicit pion and kaon exchange in the interaction kernel (t-channel, s-channel, and u-channel meson exchange) to allow for decay mechanisms of the rho and phi resonances. The electromagnetic form factor is computed from the quark-photon vertex, which is obtained by solving an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, using only the impulse approximation diagrams where the photon couples directly to the valence quarks.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson mass extracted from the form factor is 0.752 GeV (BRL-I) and 0.728 GeV (BRL-II), compared to the experimental value of 0.775 GeV.
- The phi meson mass extracted from the form factor is 1.077 GeV (BRL-I) and 1.030 GeV (BRL-II), compared to the experimental value of 1.019 GeV.
- The rho meson decay width extracted from the form factor is 0.122 GeV (BRL-I) and 0.123 GeV (BRL-II), compared to the experimental value of 0.145 GeV.
- The phi meson decay width extracted from the form factor is 0.006 GeV (BRL-I) and 0.005 GeV (BRL-II), compared to the experimental value of 0.004 GeV.
- The kaon charge radii obtained are 0.592 fm (BRL-I) and 0.590 fm (BRL-II).
- The calculation uses current quark masses m_u/d = 6.8 MeV and m_s = 85.0 MeV at the renormalization scale mu = 19 GeV.

## Context

This work extends a recent calculation of the time-like electromagnetic pion form factor by Miramontes et al. (2021) to the charged kaon case, using the same beyond-rainbow-ladder truncation scheme. The results are compared with experimental data from Kozyrev et al. (2017) and other measurements near the phi resonance. The approach enables future studies of isospin symmetry breaking effects (building on Miramontes et al. 2022) and can be applied to other form factors relevant for hadronic light-by-light contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
