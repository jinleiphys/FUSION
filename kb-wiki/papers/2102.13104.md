# Angular Distributions, Polarization Observables, Spin Density Matrices and Statistical Tensors in Photoproduction of Two Pseudoscalar Mesons off a Nucleon

> In two meson photoproduction off a nucleon, for the case in which two of the three final state hadrons are products of the decay of an intermediate resonance, general expressions for its decay distribution and for polarization observables are derived in a model independent way. These are functions of either the spin density matrix elements (SDME's) or statistical tensors of the resonance, and the angles of its decay products. The expressions are general enough that it also describes cases where more than one resonance of arbitrary quantum numbers contributes, including interference effects. They can therefore be used to extract the SDME's or statistical tensors of the resonances that contribute to the reaction.

## Key claim
The paper derives model-independent general expressions for the decay distribution and polarization observables in two-meson photoproduction off a nucleon, specifically for cases where two of the three final-state hadrons originate from the decay of an intermediate resonance. The formalism is sufficiently general to handle contributions from multiple resonances of arbitrary quantum numbers, including interference effects, but the actual extraction of spin density matrix elements or statistical tensors from experimental data is left as an application rather than demonstrated.

## Method
The author derives general angular distribution formulas and polarization observables for the reaction γN → M₁M₂N, where two of the three final hadrons come from the decay of an intermediate resonance R (e.g., ρ → ππ or Δ → πN). The expressions are written in terms of either the spin density matrix elements (SDMEs) or the statistical tensors of the decaying resonance, and the angular variables of its decay products. The derivation is performed in a model-independent way using the helicity formalism and angular momentum algebra, allowing for arbitrary spins and parities of the intermediate resonance and including interference between multiple resonances.

- No specific numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal theoretical derivation with no experimental data or computed numerical values.

## Context
This work extends the formalism for polarization observables in photoproduction reactions, building on prior approaches by Pichowsky (1996) and Roberts (2005) for single-meson photoproduction, and by Ballam (1972) for quasi-two-body photoproduction channels such as γN → ρN and γN → πΔ. The derived expressions enable complete experiments at facilities like CEBAF, ELSA, and MAMI to extract spin density matrix elements of intermediate resonances from angular distributions of their decay products, which is relevant for identifying "missing" baryon resonances that couple weakly to πN channels but strongly to πΔ and ρN channels.
