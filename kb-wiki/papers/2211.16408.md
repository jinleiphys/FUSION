# Probing initial baryon stopping and equation of state with rapidity-dependent directed flow of identified particles

> Using a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid framework with parametric initial conditions, we study the rapidity-dependent directed flow $v_1(y)$ of identified particles, including pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas in heavy-ion collisions. Cases involving Au+Au collisions are considered, performed at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ ranging from 7.7 to 200 GeV. The dynamics in the beam direction is constrained using the measured pseudo-rapidity distribution of charged particles and the net proton rapidity distribution. Within this framework, the directed flow of mesons is driven by the sideward pressure gradient from the tilted source, and that of baryons mainly due to the initial asymmetric baryon distribution with respect to the beam axis driven by the transverse expansion. Our approach successfully reproduces the rapidity- and beam energy-dependence of $v_1$ for both mesons and baryons. We find that the $v_1(y)$ of baryons has strong constraining power on the initial baryon stopping, and together with that of mesons, the directed flow probes the equation of state of the dense nuclear matter at finite chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The observed sign change in the net protons' directed flow slope around 10-20 GeV collision energy is not evidence of a first-order phase transition of QCD matter, but rather a consequence of the initial baryon stopping, because the study uses a smooth cross-over equation of state and still reproduces the data.

## Method

The authors used a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid framework with parametric initial conditions for entropy density and baryon density, where the longitudinal profiles are constrained by measured pseudorapidity distributions of charged particles and net proton rapidity distributions. They introduced a rapidity-independent plateau component in the initial baryon density profile, along with a transverse-plane-dependent longitudinal shift of the baryon peaks, to simultaneously reproduce the directed flow of mesons and baryons and the net proton yields. The hydrodynamic evolution used the NEOS-B equation of state (with vanishing strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials) for Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7, 19.6, 62.4, 200\) GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- Kinematic cuts for pions \(\pi^+\) and kaons \(K^+\): \(p_T > 0.2\) GeV and \(p < 1.6\) GeV; for protons: \(0.4 < p_T < 2\) GeV; for lambdas \(\Lambda\): \(0.2 < p_T < 5\) GeV.
- Without the plateau component at 19.6 GeV, the maximum directed flow of baryons reached \(|v_1|_{\rm max} \approx 0.07\) around \(|y|=1\), about half of that in a prior study by Shen (2020).
- At 7.7 GeV, the baryon \(v_1(y)\) starts decreasing at \(|y| \gtrsim 0.7\) while the measured value keeps increasing.

## Context

This work extends prior hybrid model studies of directed flow by Shen (2020) and others, which could not simultaneously reproduce the baryon \(v_1(y)\) and net proton yields. It contradicts the interpretation that a non-monotonic energy dependence of the directed flow slope is a signature of a first-order phase transition, as argued by Stoecker (2004) and Csernai (1999). The plateau component in the baryon profile is linked to the string junction stopping conjecture by Kharzeev (1996) and Sjostrand-Skands (2002), and the results enable future Bayesian model selection with upcoming BES-II measurements to quantify support for different baryon stopping mechanisms.
