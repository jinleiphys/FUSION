# Global optical potential for nucleus-nucleus systems from 50 MeV/u to 400 MeV/u

> We present a new global optical potential (GOP) for nucleus-nucleus systems, including neutron-rich and proton-rich isotopes, in the energy range of $50 \sim 400$ MeV/u. The GOP is derived from the microscopic folding model with the complex $G$-matrix interaction CEG07 and the global density presented by S{\~ a}o Paulo group. The folding model well accounts for realistic complex optical potentials of nucleus-nucleus systems and reproduces the existing elastic scattering data for stable heavy-ion projectiles at incident energies above 50 MeV/u. We then calculate the folding-model potentials (FMPs) for projectiles of even-even isotopes, $^{8-22}$C, $^{12-24}$O, $^{16-38}$Ne, $^{20-40}$Mg, $^{22-48}$Si, $^{26-52}$S, $^{30-62}$Ar, and $^{34-70}$Ca, scattered by stable target nuclei of $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si, $^{40}$Ca $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{120}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb at the incident energy of 50, 60, 70, 80, 100, 120, 140, 160, 180, 200, 250, 300, 350, and 400 MeV/u. The calculated FMP is represented, with a sufficient accuracy, by a linear combination of 10-range Gaussian functions. The expansion coefficients depend on the incident energy, the projectile and target mass numbers and the projectile atomic number, while the range parameters are taken to depend only on the projectile and target mass numbers. The adequate mass region of the present GOP by the global density is inspected in comparison with FMP by realistic density. The full set of the range parameters and the coefficients for all the projectile-target combinations at each incident energy are provided on a permanent open-access website together with a Fortran program for calculating the microscopic-basis GOP (MGOP) for a desired projectile nucleus by the spline interpolation over the incident energy and the target mass number.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new global optical potential (GOP) for nucleus-nucleus systems, derived from the microscopic double-folding model with the complex G-matrix interaction CEG07b and the global density of the Sao Paulo group, which reproduces existing elastic scattering data for stable heavy-ion projectiles at incident energies above 50 MeV/u with essentially no renormalization of the real part, though a slight adjustment of the imaginary part normalization factor (N_W) may be needed for optimal fits.

## Method

The authors calculated double-folding model potentials (FMPs) using the CEG07b complex G-matrix interaction and the Sao Paulo global density for projectiles of even-even isotopes (C, O, Ne, Mg, Si, S, Ar, Ca) scattered by stable target nuclei (C, O, Si, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, Pb) at 14 incident energies from 50 to 400 MeV/u. The calculated FMPs were then parameterized as a linear combination of 10-range Gaussian functions, with expansion coefficients depending on incident energy, projectile and target mass numbers, and projectile atomic number, while range parameters depend only on projectile and target mass numbers. The full set of parameters and a Fortran program (MGOP) for calculating the microscopic-basis GOP via spline interpolation over incident energy and target mass number are provided on an open-access website.

## Key numbers

* Incident energy range: 50 to 400 MeV/u.
* Projectile isotopes covered: C-8 to C-22, O-12 to O-24, Ne-16 to Ne-38, Mg-20 to Mg-40, Si-22 to Si-48, S-26 to S-52, Ar-30 to Ar-62, Ca-34 to Ca-70.
* Target nuclei: C-12, O-16, Si-28, Ca-40, Ni-58, Zr-90, Sn-120, Pb-208.
* Incident energies used: 50, 60, 70, 80, 100, 120, 140, 160, 180, 200, 250, 300, 350, and 400 MeV/u.
* The Gaussian range parameters are given by gamma_n = 0.45 * ((n+8)/18) * (A_p^(1/3) + A_t^(1/3) + 1) fm, where n = 1 to 10.
* The total number of parameters provided is about 280,000.
* The real part of the volume integral per nucleon pair smoothly shifts from attractive to repulsive around E = 200 to 300 MeV/u.
* For the O-16 + Si-28 system at 93.9 MeV/u, a stronger absorption with N_W = 1.6 was needed to reproduce data at backward angles.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic folding model approach using the CEG07 complex G-matrix interaction (Furumoto et al., 2009) to construct a global optical potential for nucleus-nucleus systems, covering a wider range of projectiles including neutron-rich and proton-rich isotopes than previous GOPs for heavy ions (such as those by Chamon et al., 2002, or Kobayashi et al., 1984). The GOP enables systematic analyses of elastic scattering for unstable nuclei near the driplines, where experimental data are difficult to obtain, and provides a practical alternative to the computationally intensive double-folding model calculations for analyzing direct nuclear reactions such as inelastic scattering and transfer reactions.
