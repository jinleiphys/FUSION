# Parton Saturation Approach in Heavy Quark Production at High Energies

> The high parton density regime of the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), where the physics of parton saturation is expected to be dominant, is briefly discussed. Some phenomenological aspects of saturation are described, mainly focusing on possible signatures of the non-linear QCD dynamics in the heavy quark production in electron-proton/nucleus collisions. Implications of these effects in the heavy quark production in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that heavy quark production in high-energy collisions can serve as a probe of parton saturation effects, but the authors qualify that in the HERA kinematic regime the saturation scale remains below the charm mass scale, meaning charm production probes mostly the color transparency regime and saturation corrections are not very important there.

## Method

The authors use the color dipole approach, where the virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair (color dipole) that interacts with the target via a dipole cross section. They employ the phenomenological saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1999), which parameterizes the dipole cross section with an exponential saturation form, and extend it to nuclear targets using a Glauber-like multiple scattering formalism. For heavy quark photoproduction, they also compute cross sections using k-perpendicular factorization with unintegrated gluon distributions derived from the saturation model, and compare with collinear factorization using the EKS and AG nuclear gluon parameterizations.

## Key numbers

* Saturation scale estimates: Q_sat ~ 1 GeV at HERA/RHIC and Q_sat ~ 2-3 GeV at LHC
* Charm mass used in calculations: m_c = 1.5 GeV, giving mu_c^2 = 4 m_c^2 ~ 10 GeV^2
* Bottom mass used in calculations: m_b = 4.5 GeV
* Predicted total cross section for charm photoproduction on lead at W_gamma_A = 10^3 GeV: approximately 2 x 10^3 microbarns
* Predicted total cross section for bottom photoproduction on lead at W_gamma_A = 10^3 GeV: approximately 40 microbarns
* Maximum center-of-mass energy for photon-nucleus collisions at LHC: W_gamma_A ~ 950 GeV, with Lorentz factor gamma_L = 2930 for lead

## Context

This work extends the saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1999) from proton targets to nuclear targets, building on the nuclear extension proposed by Armesto (2002). The paper demonstrates geometric scaling in inclusive charm production at HERA, consistent with the scaling property predicted by Iancu, Itakura, and McLerran (2002). The results enable future tests of saturation physics in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, where the saturation scale may exceed the charm mass, and motivate comparisons with the Color Glass Condensate framework of Kharzeev and Tuchin (2004) for open charm in heavy-ion collisions.
