# Derivation of breakup probabilities from experimental elastic backscattering data

> We suggest simple and useful method to extract breakup probabilities from the experimental elastic backscattering probabilities in the reactions with toughly and weakly bound nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors derive a simple formula to extract breakup probabilities from experimental elastic backscattering data by comparing reactions with weakly bound projectiles to reference reactions with toughly bound projectiles that form the same or nearly the same compound nucleus, under the assumption that the Coulomb barriers of the two very asymmetric systems are approximately equal.

## Method

The method uses the conservation of total reaction flux at a given center-of-mass energy and angular momentum to relate elastic scattering probability, capture probability, and breakup probability. From this flux conservation, the authors derive an expression for breakup probability in terms of the elastic scattering probability with and without breakup. The practical extraction requires comparing experimental elastic backscattering probabilities from a reaction with a weakly bound projectile (e.g., ⁶He) to those from a reaction with a toughly bound projectile (e.g., ⁴He) on targets that produce the same or almost the same compound nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The method is applicable to reactions with light weakly bound projectiles including ⁶,⁸He, ⁸Li, ⁷,⁹,¹¹Be, ⁸,⁹B, ¹⁵C, and ¹⁷F at energies near and above the Coulomb barrier.
- Specific reaction pairs listed for analysis include ⁶He+⁶⁸Zn and ⁴He+⁶⁸,⁷⁰Zn, ⁶He+¹²²Sn and ⁴He+¹²²,¹²⁴Sn, and ⁶He+²³⁶U and ⁴He+²³⁶,²³⁸U, among others.
- The formula for extracting breakup probability at angular momentum J=0 is given as P_BU = 1 - P_el,qe[⁶He+ᴬ⁻²X] / P_el,qe^noBU[⁴He+ᴬX].

## Context

This work extends the quasi-elastic backscattering technique previously suggested (EPJSub4) for investigating breakup probability, providing a simpler method using only elastic backscattering data rather than quasi-elastic data. The approach addresses the long-standing question of whether fusion is enhanced or suppressed with weakly bound radioactive ion beams, where previous studies (Gomes, EPJSub4, PRSGomes5) have yielded contradictory results and no clear systematic behavior of breakup probability as a function of target charge. The method enables systematic analysis of breakup channels across many projectile-target combinations using the same or nearly the same compound nucleus, which could help resolve the ambiguity in fusion dynamics with exotic nuclei.
