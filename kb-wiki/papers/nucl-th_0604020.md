# General study of superscaling in quasielastic $(e,e')$ and $(ν,μ)$ reactions using the relativistic impulse approximation

> The phenomenon of superscaling for quasielastic lepton induced reactions at energies of a few GeV is investigated within the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation. A global analysis of quasielastic inclusive electron and charged-current neutrino scattering reactions on nuclei is presented. Scaling and superscaling properties are shown to emerge from both types of processes. The crucial role played by final state interactions is evaluated by using different approaches. The asymmetric shape presented by the experimental scaling function, with a long tail in the region of positive values of the scaling variable, is reproduced when the interaction in the final state between the knockout nucleon and the residual nucleus is described within the relativistic mean field approach. The impact of gauge ambiguities and off-shell effects in the scaling function is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA), when including strong relativistic potentials for final state interactions (FSI), reproduces the asymmetric shape of the experimental superscaling function for quasielastic lepton-nucleus reactions, which previous non-relativistic or semirelativistic approaches failed to achieve.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive quasielastic electron-nucleus and charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections within the relativistic impulse approximation, using Dirac-Hartree bound nucleon states from a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach and describing the outgoing nucleon with either plane waves (RPWIA), real relativistic optical potentials (rROP), or the same RMF potential used for bound states. They then divided the cross sections by appropriate single-nucleon cross sections weighted by proton and neutron numbers to extract scaling functions, which were plotted against the dimensionless scaling variable ψ' and analyzed for dependence on momentum transfer (first-kind scaling) and nuclear species (second-kind scaling).

## Key numbers

- Incident electron beam energy: ε = 1 GeV, scattering angle θ_e = 45°, used for the differential cross section calculations on ¹²C.
- Axial-vector form factor parameters: g_A = 1.26, M_A = 1032 MeV, used in the weak current for neutrino scattering.
- The RFG superscaling function is given by f_RFG(ψ') = (3/4)(1 - ψ'²)θ(1 - ψ'²), which is symmetric and limited to |ψ'| ≤ 1 with maximum value 3/4.

## Context

This work extends the scaling and superscaling analysis of quasielastic electron scattering data (Day 1990, Donnelly and Sick 1999) to neutrino reactions within the RIA framework, building on earlier semirelativistic mean field calculations (neutrino2) that produced symmetric scaling functions inconsistent with experiment. The results enable predictions for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering using the universal asymmetric scaling function, and provide a foundation for testing whether various RIA-FSI models superscale for those processes as well.
