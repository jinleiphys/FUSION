# Influence of clustering and hadron potentials on the rapidity distribution of protons from the UrQMD model

> The Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model supplemented by potentials for both pre-formed hadrons and confined baryons (called UrQMD/M) are used to describe rapidity distributions of both the E895 proton data at AGS energies and the NA49 net proton data at SPS energies. With the help of a coalescence afterburner using only one parameter set of ($R_0$, $P_0$)=(3.8 fm, 0.3 GeV$/$c), both sets of experimental data can be described fairly well except for a small discrepancy seen for the net protons at mid-rapidity from heavy ion collisions (HICs) at high SPS energies. Furthermore, in contrast to the logarithmic dependence with beam energy at SIS energies there are still about 10$\%$ of protons in clusters from central HICs at the beam energy of $80$ GeV$/$nucleon.

## Key claim
The UrQMD model with mean-field potentials (UrQMD/M) plus a coalescence afterburner can describe both E895 proton data at AGS energies and NA49 net proton data at SPS energies fairly well, but a small discrepancy remains for net protons at mid-rapidity at high SPS energies, and the clustered proton fraction at 80 GeV/nucleon is still about 10%, contradicting a simple logarithmic extrapolation from lower energies.

## Method
The UrQMD model was run in a version with mean-field potentials for pre-formed hadrons and confined baryons (UrQMD/M), and a coalescence afterburner was applied after a cutoff time of 50 fm/c to cluster nucleons with relative momentum below 0.3 GeV/c and relative distance below 3.8 fm. The authors simulated central Au+Au collisions at AGS energies (2, 4, 6, 8 GeV/nucleon) and central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (20, 30, 40, 80 GeV/nucleon), then compared the resulting free (net) proton rapidity distributions to E895 and NA49 data.

## Key numbers
- Coalescence parameters used: R0 = 3.8 fm, P0 = 0.3 GeV/c (single parameter set for all energies).
- At beam energy 80 GeV/nucleon, about 10% of protons remain in clusters from central heavy ion collisions.
- At SIS energies (0.2 to 1.5 GeV/nucleon), the clustered proton fraction shows a linear dependence on log10(Eb), but extrapolation to 6.5 GeV/nucleon would predict zero clusters, which is not supported by the UrQMD/M calculations.
- At 20-30 GeV/nucleon, the free proton fraction is about 85%, matching the percentage of wounded nucleons from the Glauber approach.

## Context
This work extends the study of baryon stopping and clustering from SIS energies (where the FOPI collaboration, Reisdorf (2010), found a logarithmic excitation function for clustered protons) up to AGS and SPS energies. It addresses a known overestimation of free proton yields in the cascade-mode UrQMD model (UrQMD/C) as noted by Yuan (2010). The results enable more systematic studies of the nuclear equation of state, effective string tension, and cross-section modifications at energies where a hadron gas to quark-gluon plasma transition may occur.
