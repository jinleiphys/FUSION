# The sigma and f_0(980) from K_e4+pi-pi, gamma-gamma scatterings, J/psi, phi to gamma sigma_B and D_s to l nu sigma_B

> We extract the pole positions, hadronic and gamma-gamma widths of sigma and f_0(980, from pi-pi and gamma-gamma scattering data using an improved analytic K-matrix model. Our results favour a large gluon component for the sigma and a \bar ss or/and gluon component for the f_0(980) but neither a large four-quark nor a molecule component. Gluonium sigma_B production from J/psi, phi radiative and D_s semi-leptonic decays are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors extract pole positions and hadronic and gamma-gamma widths of the sigma and f_0(980) mesons using an improved analytic K-matrix model, and their results favor a large gluon component for the sigma and a strange-antistrange or gluon component for the f_0(980), but neither a large four-quark nor a molecule component for either state.

## Method

The authors use an improved analytic K-matrix model that incorporates a real analytic form factor (shape function) to account for the Adler zero and left-hand singularities, first in a single-channel analysis of pi-pi scattering and then generalized to coupled pi-pi and K-anti-K channels with two bare resonances. They fit the model parameters to pi-pi phase shift and inelasticity data from multiple experimental sets, then extend the analysis to gamma-gamma to pi-pi scattering by including Born, unitarized, and direct resonance coupling amplitudes to extract the gamma-gamma widths.

## Key numbers

* Sigma meson pole position: M_sigma = 452(12) MeV, Gamma_sigma/2 = 260(15) MeV (average of single and coupled channel results)
* Sigma hadronic coupling to pi+ pi-: |g_sigma pi+ pi-| = 2.65(10) GeV
* Ratio of sigma couplings to K+ K- versus pi+ pi-: r_sigma pi K = 0.37(6)
* f_0(980) mass: M_f = 981(34) MeV
* f_0(980) half-width: Gamma_f/2 = 18(11) MeV
* f_0(980) hadronic coupling to pi+ pi-: |g_f pi+ pi-| = 1.17(26) GeV
* Ratio of f_0(980) couplings to K+ K- versus pi+ pi-: r_f pi K = 2.59(1.34)
* Total gamma-gamma width of sigma: Gamma_sigma_tot = 3.11 keV (Set 2) and 3.10 keV (Set 3), measured at sqrt(s) = 1.09 GeV
* Total gamma-gamma width of f_0(980): Gamma_f0_tot = 0.17 keV (Set 2) and 0.15 keV (Set 3), measured at sqrt(s) = 1.09 GeV
* Predicted branching ratio for J/psi to sigma_B gamma: B(J/psi to sigma_B gamma) x B(sigma_B to all) = (0.4 to 1.0) x 10^(-3)
* Predicted branching ratio for phi to sigma_B gamma: B(phi to sigma_B gamma) = 0.12 x 10^(-3)

## Context

This work extends the K-matrix approach of Mennessier (1983) by incorporating a shape function from Ochs (2008) and generalizing it to coupled channels as done in earlier work by the authors (KMN, MNW, MNW1). The results contradict pure four-quark or molecule interpretations of the sigma and f_0(980) and instead support a significant gluonium component, which is further tested through predictions for J/psi, phi radiative decays and D_s semi-leptonic decays that agree with existing data. The analysis enables future experimental tests of the gluonium nature of light scalar mesons through these decay channels.
