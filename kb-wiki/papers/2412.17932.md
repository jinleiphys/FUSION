# Improving $ππ$ dispersive analyses and resonance determination with Forward Dispersion Relations

> We present preliminary results of an improved pion-pion scattering dispersive analysis that includes: a refined treatment of inelasticities, the introduction of G-waves, the extension of Forward Dispersion Relations as constraints up to 1.6 GeV, and data description up to roughly 1.8 GeV. Additionally, we impose Roy-like dispersion relations. As a result, we obtain three reliable solutions corresponding to three different datasets. From the Forward Dispersion Relation output, we extract resonance pole parameters in a parameterization-independent way using continued fractions.

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary results showing that an improved dispersive analysis of pion-pion scattering, which includes refined inelasticities, G-waves, and Forward Dispersion Relations extended to 1.6 GeV, yields three reliable solutions corresponding to different datasets, with the caveat that these are preliminary and only Solution I is shown in detail.

## Method

The authors constructed global parameterizations for partial waves up to angular momentum ℓ=3 (including G0 and G2 waves) describing ππ scattering data up to roughly 1.8 GeV, with improved treatment of inelasticities and matching to the Regge regime at higher energies. They imposed Forward Dispersion Relations up to 1.6 GeV (or 1.4 GeV for one amplitude to avoid artifacts) and Roy-like dispersion relations up to 1.1 GeV, performing constrained fits to data with penalty functions ensuring the dispersion relations were satisfied within uncertainties. From the Forward Dispersion Relation output, they extracted resonance pole parameters using continued fractions (Padé approximants) as a model-independent analytic continuation method.

## Context

This work extends the previous dispersive analyses of GarciaMartin (2011) and Pelaez (2019) by including G-waves, improving inelasticity treatments (e.g., opening P-wave inelasticity at the πω threshold instead of KK threshold), and extending Forward Dispersion Relation constraints to higher energies. The improved precision above 0.9 GeV enables model-independent extraction of resonance pole parameters for states including the ρ(1450), which was not accessible in earlier analyses, and the authors report no evidence for the ρ(1250) claimed by Hammoud (2020) from the same data.
