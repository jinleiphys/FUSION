# Color-flavor dependence of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and QCD phase diagram

> We study the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking/restoration for the various numbers of light quarks flavors $N_f$ and colors $N_c$, using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of quarks, dressed with a color-flavor dependence of effective coupling. Initially, we set $N_f = 2$, and varying the number of colors $N_c$, we find that the dynamical chiral symmetry is broken when $N_c$ exceeds to its critical value $N^{c}_{c}\approx2.2$. Secondly, we take $N_c = 3$, and varying $N_f$, we observed that the dynamical chiral symmetry is restored when $N_f$ reaches to its critical value $N^{c}_{f}\approx8$. The strong interplay observed between $N_c$ and $N_f$, i.e., $N_c$ anti-screens the strong interactions by strengthening the dynamical mass and quark-antiquark condensate, while $N_f$ screens the strong interaction by suppressing both the parameters. We further sketch the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) phase diagram at finite temperature $T$ and quark chemical potential $μ$ for various $N_c$ and $N_f$. At finite $T$ and $μ$, we observed that the critical number of colors $N^{c}_c$ enhances while the critical number of flavors $N^{c}_f$ suppresses as $T$ and $μ$ increases. Of course, the parameters $T$ and $μ$ produce the screening effect. Consequently, the critical temperature $T_c$, $μ_c$ and co-ordinates of the critical endpoint $(T^{E}_c,μ^{E}_c)$ in the QCD phase diagram enhances as $N_c$ increases while suppresses when $N_f$ increases. Our findings agree with the Lattice QCD and Schwinger-Dyson equations predictions.

## Key claim

Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a color-flavor dependent effective coupling, the authors find that dynamical chiral symmetry breaks when the number of colors exceeds a critical value \(N_c^c \approx 2.2\) (for \(N_f=2\)), and restores when the number of flavors reaches \(N_f^c \approx 8\) (for \(N_c=3\)); these critical values shift with temperature and chemical potential, with the results agreeing qualitatively with lattice QCD and Schwinger-Dyson equation predictions.

## Method

The authors solve the NJL model gap equation in the Hartree-Fock mean-field approximation, incorporating color and flavor dependence into the effective coupling \(\mathcal{G}^{N_c}(N_f)\) by modifying the flavor sector \(G(N_f)\) with a factor proportional to \(\sqrt{1 - (N_f-2)/\mathcal{N}_f^c}\). The three-dimensional momentum integral is regularized with a hard cutoff \(\Lambda = 587.9\) MeV, and the gap equation is extended to finite temperature \(T\) and quark chemical potential \(\mu\) using the Matsubara formalism. The critical numbers of colors and flavors are extracted from the inflection points of the color-gradient and flavor-gradient of the quark-antiquark condensate.

## Key numbers

*   Dynamical quark mass \(M = 399\) MeV for \(N_f = 2\) and \(N_c = 3\).
*   Quark-antiquark condensate \(-\langle \bar{q} q \rangle^{1/3} = 250\) MeV for \(N_f = 2\) and \(N_c = 3\).
*   Critical number of colors for chiral symmetry breaking: \(N_c^c \approx 2.2\) (at \(N_f=2\), \(T=0\), \(\mu=0\)).
*   Critical number of flavors for chiral symmetry restoration: \(N_f^c \approx 8\) (at \(N_c=3\), \(T=0\), \(\mu=0\)).
*   For \(N_f=2\) and \(N_c=3\): critical temperature at \(\mu=0\) is \(T_c \approx 235\) MeV; critical endpoint coordinates are \((\mu_c^E \approx 330\) MeV, \(T_c^E \approx 81\) MeV).
*   For \(N_f=2\) and \(N_c=6\): critical temperature at \(\mu=0\) is \(T_c \approx 623\) MeV; critical endpoint coordinates are \((\mu_c^E \approx 604\) MeV, \(T_c^E \approx 349\) MeV).
*   At finite \(\mu\) for \(N_f=2\): critical endpoint for color variation at \((N_c^c \approx 2.5, \mu_c^c \approx 290\) MeV).
*   At finite \(\mu\) for \(N_c=3\): critical endpoint for flavor variation at \((N_f^c \approx 5, \mu_c^f \approx 290\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of color-flavor dependence of chiral symmetry breaking from Schwinger-Dyson equation approaches (Ahmad, 2020) to the NJL model framework, and generalizes the QCD phase diagram to variable numbers of colors and flavors. The results agree with lattice QCD simulations (LSD Collaboration, 2014) and continuum methods (Bashir, 2013) that predict chiral symmetry restoration in the conformal window \(8 \lesssim N_f^c < 12\) for \(N_c=3\). The findings enable mapping of how the critical endpoint and phase transition order in the \(T-\mu\) plane depend on the gauge group parameters \(N_c\) and \(N_f\), which is relevant for physics beyond the Standard Model and for understanding the phase structure of strongly interacting matter.
