# Charmonium Productions: Nucleon-Nucleon versus Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Charmonium productions in $p-p$ and $A-B$ collisions have been estimated within the ambit of colour evaporation model (CEM). The model parameters have been fixed by fitting the theoretical results with CDF data. The method is then applied to RHIC and LHC energies to obtain the transverse momentum distributions of $J/ψ$, $ψ^{\prime}$ and $χ_c$. Suppression due to Debye screening in a quark gluon plasma (QGP) is estimated at various centrality cuts. The final $p_T$ distributions of various resonances are then predicted convoluting with the survival probability.

# Charmonium Productions: Nucleon-Nucleon versus Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The colour evaporation model (CEM) at leading order with gluon splitting can reproduce CDF data for charmonium production in p-p collisions, and when combined with a Debye screening survival probability, predicts that J/ψ mesons with transverse momentum above 15 GeV will escape suppression in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, but only if the dissociation temperature is taken as 1.1 times the critical temperature; for a higher dissociation temperature of 1.5 T_c, the suppression threshold drops to approximately 6 GeV.

## Method

The authors compute charmonium production cross sections using the colour evaporation model at leading order with gluon splitting, fitting the non-perturbative normalization factors F[nJ^PC] to CDF data at √s = 1800 GeV. They then apply the Glauber model to obtain production rates in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and estimate the survival probability against Debye screening in a quark-gluon plasma using the prescription of Chu and Matsui (1989), with initial temperatures derived from a two-component model of hadron multiplicity and Bjorken scaling.

## Key numbers

- F[J/ψ] = 0.045 reproduces CDF data for prompt J/ψ production at √s = 1800 GeV with m_c = 1.3 GeV and CTEQ(LO) parton distribution functions
- F[ψ′] = 0.0126 and Σ_J B(χ_cJ → J/ψ X) F[χ_cJ] = 0.005 (no feed-down included)
- For J/ψ with T_d = 1.1 T_c and hard fraction x = 0.3 at LHC (√s = 5500 GeV), suppression occurs for p_T < 15 GeV in central collisions (0 ≤ b ≤ 3 fm) and p_T < 10 GeV in mid-peripheral collisions (6 ≤ b ≤ 9 fm)
- For J/ψ with T_d = 1.5 T_c at LHC, suppression occurs below p_T ∼ 6 GeV
- For ψ′ with T_d = 0.15 T_c at LHC, suppression persists even at p_T ∼ 30 GeV
- At RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) with τ_i = 0.6 fm/c and x = 0.1, J/ψ is not suppressed even for most central collisions
- Initial temperatures for Pb-Pb at LHC range from ⟨T_i⟩ = 0.438 GeV (9-12 fm centrality) to 0.617 GeV (0-3 fm centrality)
- Dissociation temperatures from lattice QCD: J/ψ ⟨T_d⟩/T_c = 1.5, χ_c ⟨T_d⟩/T_c = 0.9, ψ′ ⟨T_d⟩/T_c = 0.625 (in pure gluonic plasma with T_c ∼ 270 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the colour evaporation model approach of Schuler (1994), Kramer (1995), and Mangano (1996) to nucleus-nucleus collisions, building on the Matsui and Satz (1986) proposal that J/ψ suppression signals quark-gluon plasma formation. The survival probability calculation follows Chu and Matsui (1989), while the Glauber model treatment and nuclear absorption parametrization follow Kharzeev (1996) and Wong (1994). The results provide predictions for the ALICE dimuon spectrometer at LHC and for RHIC experiments, showing that the observation of J/ψ suppression depends critically on the dissociation temperature and the hard fraction parameter x, which remains unknown at LHC energies.
