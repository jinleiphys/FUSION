# Electromagnetic Gauge Invariance of Chiral Hybrid Quark Models

> In this work, we investigate the question, whether the conventional analysis of the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon, evaluated in the framework of the cloudy bag model (CBM) or other chirally invariant hybrid quark models utilizing the same philosophy, is gauge invariant. In order to address that point, we first formulate the CBM in a style that resembles the technique of loop integrals. Evaluating the self energy and the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in that manner and comparing with the standard analysis where nonrelativistic perturbation theory is used, allows us to show that our approach is appropriate and to point out, what approximations are made in the standard derivation of the model. {}From the form of those loop integrals, we then show that additional diagrams are needed to preserve electromagnetic gauge invariance and we assess the corresponding corrections.

## Key claim

The conventional analysis of electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in the cloudy bag model (CBM) and other chirally invariant hybrid quark models violates electromagnetic gauge invariance because the momentum-dependent meson-nucleon vertex function requires additional "seagull" diagrams to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identities, though the underlying quark-level Lagrangian is gauge invariant.

## Method

The authors reformulated the cloudy bag model using loop integral techniques to evaluate the nucleon self energy and electromagnetic Sachs form factors, comparing their results with the standard nonrelativistic perturbation theory approach. They then demonstrated that the momentum-dependent vertex function u(|k|R) = 3j₁(|k|R)/(|k|R) spoils gauge invariance, and derived the necessary "seagull" correction terms following either the prescription of Gross and Riska (1987) or the minimal substitution method of Ohta (1989), verifying that the modified vertex functions obey the Ward-Takahashi identities.

## Key numbers

- The vertex function for the CBM is u(|k|R) = 3j₁(|k|R)/(|k|R), with the bag eigenfrequency ω = 2.04
- The pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling constant is g = ω/(6fπ(ω-1)) = 3√(4π)fπAB/(5mπ), with the microscopically determined value fπNN = 0.23 typically replaced by the experimental value fπNN = 0.28
- For a typical bag radius of R = 1 fm, the gauge invariance correction to the magnetic moment from the π⁺ cloud is about 100% for μ_M ≡ G_M^{mes}(|q|=0)
- In the limit of very small R, the gauge invariance correction is almost 50% of the uncorrected value, and both diverge like 1/R
- The strange magnetic moment prediction changes from F₂^s(0) = -0.0260 to F₂^s(0) = -0.0917 after including gauge invariance corrections

## Context

This work extends the cloudy bag model formalism of Theberge, Thomas and Miller (1980) and Thomas, Theberge and Miller (1981) by addressing the gauge invariance issue raised by Musolf and Burkardt (1993). The paper corrects a previous investigation by Koepf, Henley and Pollock (1992) of the strangeness content of the nucleon, where seagull diagrams were not considered, and brings those results into agreement with the kaon loop calculations of Musolf and Burkardt (1993). The findings also apply to other chirally invariant hybrid quark models such as those introduced by Williams and Dodd (1988) for the Nielsen-Patkos color dielectric model and the Friedberg-Lee soliton model.
