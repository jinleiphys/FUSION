# Comparison of renormalized interactions using one-dimensional few-body systems as a testbed

> Even though the one-dimensional contact interaction requires no regularization, renormalization methods have been shown to improve the convergence of numerical ab initio calculations considerably. In this work, we compare and contrast these methods: `the running coupling constant' where the two-body ground state energy is used as a renormalization condition, and two effective interaction approaches that include information about the ground as well as excited states. In particular, we calculate the energies and densities of few-fermion systems in a harmonic oscillator with the configuration interaction method, and compare the results based upon renormalized and bare interactions. We find that the use of the running coupling constant instead of the bare interaction improves convergence significantly. A comparison with an effective interaction, which is designed to reproduce the relative part of the energy spectrum of two particles, showed a similar improvement. The effective interaction provides an additional improvement if the center-of-mass excitations are included in the construction. Finally, we discuss the transformation of observables alongside the renormalization of the potential, and demonstrate that this might be an essential ingredient for accurate numerical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while one-dimensional contact interactions require no regularization, renormalization methods significantly improve convergence of configuration interaction calculations for harmonically trapped few-fermion systems; the improvement is most pronounced for an effective interaction that reproduces the full low-energy two-body spectrum including center-of-mass excitations, which has not been previously explored in cold-atom few-body models.

## Method

The authors performed configuration interaction (CI) calculations for harmonically trapped two-component fermions with contact interactions, comparing four interaction schemes: bare contact interaction, a running coupling constant tuned to reproduce the two-body ground state energy in the truncated Hilbert space, an effective interaction constructed from the relative-coordinate two-body spectrum, and a new effective interaction built from the complete two-body spectrum including center-of-mass excitations. The effective interactions were constructed using a unitary transformation approach analogous to the Lee-Suzuki method from nuclear physics, and the authors also transformed one-body and two-body observables alongside the potential renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Running coupling constant for trapped systems shows less than 1% disagreement with the unconfined running coupling constant for single-body cutoffs n ≥ 21
- The two-body ground state energy was fixed to E_{1+1} = -1 (in units where ℏ = m = ω = 1), corresponding to g(n→∞) = -2.5067
- The momentum-space cutoff λ is related to the single-body cutoff n via λ = 2√n for the harmonic oscillator
- The Hilbert space dimension grows as dim ℋ = C(n, N↑) × C(n, N↓), where C(n,k) is the binomial coefficient

## Context

This work extends the application of renormalization methods from nuclear physics (Lee-Suzuki transformations, renormalization group methods by Bogner (2007) and Bogner (2010)) to one-dimensional cold-atom systems, building on prior studies of effective interactions in cold atoms by Rotureau (2013), Lindgren (2014), and Rammelmüller (2023). The paper fills a gap by directly comparing different renormalization schemes that were previously only benchmarked against bare interactions, and introduces the full-spectrum effective interaction (including center-of-mass excitations) that has not been explored in this context. The results enable more accurate numerical calculations for strongly interacting few-body systems with larger particle numbers.
