# Decay behavior of hidden-strange hadronic molecular state $N(2270)$

> In this article, we systematically discuss the decay patterns of the hidden-strange hadronic molecular state $N(2270)$ which is assumed as an S-wave $K^\astΣ^\ast$ shallow bound state with its possible quantum numbers $J^P$ which are ${1/2}^-$, ${3/2}^-$ and ${5/2}^-$. By using the effective Lagrangian approach and considering pseudo-scalar meson and vector meson exchanges, we have thoroughly calculated and discussed the decay behavior of the $N(2270)$ molecular state, including hadronic and radiative decay and the cutoff dependence, for different $J^P$ values. For all three cases, $K^\ast Λ$, $K^\ast Σ$, and $ρN$ final states are always included as the main decay channels. However, the $K Λ$, $πN$ and $πΔ$ final states exhibit notable differences. These different decay properties will provide valuable guidance for future experimental searches and aid in distinguishing different $J^P$ assumptions and understanding their internal structures.

## Key claim

The paper systematically calculates the decay patterns of the hidden-strange hadronic molecular state N(2270), assumed as an S-wave K*Σ* shallow bound state with binding energy 8.19 MeV, for three possible quantum numbers JP = 1/2−, 3/2−, and 5/2−; the abstract glosses over the fact that the total width and some branching ratios show significant dependence on the cutoff parameters, so the predictions are given as ranges rather than precise values.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach with pseudo-scalar and vector meson exchanges in triangle diagrams to compute hadronic and radiative decay widths of the N(2270) molecular state. The coupling constants for the molecular vertex are determined using the Weinberg compositeness criterion with a binding energy of 8.19 MeV, while other couplings are obtained from SU(3) relations and PDG data. Two cutoff parameters (Λ and Λ0) are introduced via Gaussian and multi-pole regulators, both set to 900 MeV for central results, with variations from 800 to 1000 MeV to study model dependence.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy EB = 8.19 MeV for the K*Σ* system (mK* = 893.61 MeV, mΣ* = 1384.58 MeV)
- Effective coupling constants g0: 1.340 for JP = 1/2−, 1.468 for JP = 3/2−, 1.895 for JP = 5/2−
- Total width at (Λ, Λ0) = (900, 900) MeV: 247.61 MeV for JP = 1/2−, 365.52 MeV for JP = 3/2−, 78.89 MeV for JP = 5/2−
- Radiative decay branching ratios: 0.116% for JP = 1/2−, 0.016% for JP = 3/2−, 0.023% for JP = 5/2−
- For JP = 1/2−: KΛ branching ratio varies in range 25% to 48%; KΣ BR 10% to 15%; K*Λ, ρN, φN BRs around 10%
- For JP = 3/2−: K*Λ BR ∼ 53%; KΣ* and πΔ BRs around 12%
- For JP = 5/2−: K*Λ BR ∼ 25%; KΣ* BR ∼ 17%; πΔ and KΛ BRs in range 14% to 20%; φN BR around 10%

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular interpretation of nucleon resonances in the hidden-strange sector, following earlier studies by Lin et al. (2018) on N(1875)3/2− and N(2080)3/2− as KΣ* and K*Σ molecular states, and by Ben and Wu (2023) on N(2270) in photoproduction reactions. The calculated decay patterns provide distinctive signatures for different JP assignments that can guide experimental searches at facilities such as JLab and future electron-ion colliders, and help distinguish between molecular and compact pentaquark interpretations of the N(2270) state.
