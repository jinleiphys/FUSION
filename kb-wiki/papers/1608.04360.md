# High-precision quadrupole moment reveals significant intruder component in 33Al20 ground state

> The electric quadrupole moment of the 33Al20 ground state, located at the border of the island of inversion, was obtained using continuous-beam beta-detected nuclear quadrupole resonance (beta-NQR). From the measured quadrupole coupling constant Q = 2.31(4) MHz in an alpha-Al2O3 crystal, a precise value for the electric quadrupole moment is extracted: Qs= 141(3) mb. A comparison with large-scale shell model calculations shows that 33Al has at least 50% intruder configurations in the ground state wave function, favoring the excitation of two neutrons across the N = 20 shell gap. 33Al therefore clearly marks the gradual transition north of the deformed Na and Mg nuclei towards the normal Z>14 isotopes.

## Key claim

The electric quadrupole moment of the 33Al ground state, measured with 2% precision, is inconsistent with a pure sd-shell configuration and requires at least 50% intruder configurations (neutron excitations across the N=20 shell gap) in the ground state wave function, placing 33Al as a transitional isotope at the border of the island of inversion rather than outside it.

## Method

The electric quadrupole moment was measured using continuous-beam beta-detected nuclear quadrupole resonance (beta-NQR) at the LISE-GANIL facility. A 77 MeV/u 36S primary beam fragmented on a 9Be target produced spin-polarized 33Al nuclei, which were implanted into an alpha-Al2O3 single crystal in a static external magnetic field of 2002(7) gauss. The quadrupole coupling constant was extracted from the resonance condition, and the quadrupole moment was determined relative to the known value for 27Al.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole coupling constant for 33Al: nu_Q = 2.31(4) MHz in alpha-Al2O3
- Electric quadrupole moment of 33Al: |Q_s| = 141(3) mb (2% precision, improved from 12% in prior work by Shimada (2012))
- 33Al ground state contains at least 50% intruder configurations (neutron 2p-2h excitations across N=20)
- With the SDPF-M interaction, 62% intruder configurations (55% 2p-2h and 7% 4p-4h) reproduce the experimental quadrupole moment
- The SDPF-U-MIX interaction predicts only 17% intruder configurations and cannot reproduce the experimental value

## Context

This measurement resolves conflicting evidence about the nature of 33Al: mass measurements by Kwiatkowski (2015) and beta-decay studies by Morton (2002) placed 33Al outside the island of inversion, while g-factor measurements by Himpe (2006) suggested intruder components. The high-precision quadrupole moment confirms and strengthens the conclusion of the g-factor work, showing that 33Al marks the gradual transition north of the deformed Na and Mg nuclei towards the normal Z>=14 isotopes. The result provides a sensitive test for state-of-the-art shell model interactions (SDPF-M and SDPF-U-MIX) and motivates further investigation to reconcile the quadrupole moment with mass and beta-decay data.
