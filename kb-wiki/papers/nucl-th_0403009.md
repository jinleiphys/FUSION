# Parity Nonconservation in the Photodisintegration of the Deuteron at Low Energy

> The parity-nonconserving asymmetry in the deuteron photodisintegration, $\vecγ+d\to n+p$, is considered with the photon energy ranged up to 10 MeV above the threshold. The aim is to improve upon a schematic estimate assuming the absence of tensor as well as spin-orbit forces in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The major contributions are due to the vector-meson exchanges, and the strong suppression of the pion-exchange contribution is confirmed. A simple argument, going beyond the observation of an algebraic cancellation, is presented. Contributions of meson-exchange currents are also considered, but found to be less significant.

## Key claim

The parity-nonconserving asymmetry in deuteron photodisintegration is strongly suppressed for the pion-exchange contribution, contrary to earlier claims of enhancement by Oka (1983), and the suppression persists even when realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions with tensor and spin-orbit forces are used, though the cancellation is not exact as in the schematic model of Khriplovich and Korkin (2000).

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation with the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential for both scattering and deuteron states, including the 1S0, 3P0, 3P1, 3P2-3F2 channels and their parity-admixed partners consistently. The parity-nonconserving asymmetry was calculated using the DDH weak interaction potential, with the E1 operator treated via the Siegert theorem and the M1 operator evaluated with explicit one-body currents, while meson-exchange currents were included for both parity-conserving and parity-nonconserving contributions.

## Key numbers

- At the threshold photon energy of 2.235 MeV, the asymmetry is Aγ(th) ≈ [-8.44 hρ0 - 17.65 hρ2 + 3.63 hω0 + O(c1,c3,c6)] × 10-3, yielding Aγ(th) ≈ 2.53 × 10-8 using DDH "best" values.
- The circular polarization in radiative thermal neutron capture by proton, calculated with the same Argonne v18 potential, is Pγ(th) ≈ [-8.75 hρ0 - 17.47 hρ2 + 3.39 hω0 + O(c1,c3,c6)] × 10-3.
- At 10 MeV above threshold, the total pion-exchange PNC response from the five dominant exit channels is -0.371 × 10-5 × hπ1, compared to -4.037 × 10-5 × hπ1 when using the formulas from Oka (1983), a factor of approximately 30 difference.
- The zero-energy transition amplitudes for the Argonne v18 model are: mN λt = -0.043 hρ0 - 0.022 hω0, mN λs = -0.125 hρ0 - 0.109 hω0 + 0.102 hρ2, and mN C = 1.023 hπ1 + 0.007 hρ1 - 0.021 hω1.

## Context

This work extends the schematic calculation of Khriplovich and Korkin (2000), which assumed no tensor or spin-orbit forces and found complete cancellation of the pion-exchange contribution, by using the realistic Argonne v18 potential that includes these forces. It contradicts the earlier calculation by Oka (1983), which predicted a large enhancement of the pion-exchange contribution at photon energies above 5 MeV; the discrepancy arises from Oka's omission of parity-admixed components in the 3P1 scattering state and incorrect factors for the scalar magnetic moment. The results enable future experiments at JLab, IASA, LEGS, TUNL, and SPring-8 to interpret measured asymmetries in terms of the weak meson-nucleon coupling constants, particularly the isoscalar and isotensor couplings hρ0, hρ2, and hω0, rather than the pion coupling hπ1.
