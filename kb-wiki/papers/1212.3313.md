# Testing scaling laws for the elastic scattering of protons

> Theoretical proposals of scaling laws for the differential elastic scattering cross sections of protons are confronted with experimental data over a wide energy range. Different combinations of the transferred momentum and energy resulting from the solution of the definite partial differential equation are attempted as scaling variables. Reasonable scaling of the differential cross sections in the diffraction cone has been shown for one of these variables. The violation of the geometrical scaling is ascribed to the increase of the proton blackness with energy. The origin of high-t region violations of scaling laws is discussed.

## Key claim

The simple geometrical scaling law \(t^2 d\sigma/dt = \phi^2(t\sigma_t)\) is violated even in the diffraction cone at the highest energies (7 TeV), but a modified scaling law with an adjustable exponent \(a = 1.2\) can restore approximate scaling in the diffraction cone across all available energies. The violation is attributed to the increase of proton blackness with energy, not to changes in proton radius.

## Method

The authors derived a partial differential equation for the imaginary part of the elastic scattering amplitude by equating two expressions for the ratio of real to imaginary parts \(\rho(s,t)\) from local dispersion relations and a linear \(t\)-approximation. They then tested the resulting scaling law \(t^{2a} d\sigma/dt = \omega(t^a \sigma_t)\) against experimental data for proton-proton elastic scattering from \(\sqrt{s} = 4.4\) GeV to 7 TeV, varying the parameter \(a\) to find the best fit in the diffraction cone.

## Key numbers

- The best scaling in the diffraction cone was found for \(a = 1.2\), using data from \(\sqrt{s} = 4.4\) GeV to 7 TeV.
- At 7 TeV, the TOTEM data show clear violation of geometrical scaling (\(a = 1\)) in the diffraction cone, which is cured by the modified law with \(a = 1.2\).
- The maximum of the function \(t^2 d\sigma/dt\) in the diffraction cone is positioned at \(t_m \sigma_t = 2\sigma_t / B = 16\pi (1 - \exp(-\Omega(s)))\), depending only on the proton opacity \(\Omega(s)\).
- In the Orear region at 7 TeV, the fit required \(\rho \approx -2.1\), indicating strongly non-linear behavior not captured by the linear approximation.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical scaling proposals of Akhiezer (1971) and others, derived from a partial differential equation by Dremin (2012). It contradicts the simple geometrical scaling law that would hold if opacity saturated, showing instead that the energy dependence of \(\sigma_t\) (a volume effect) and the diffraction cone slope \(B\) (a surface effect) differ. The modified scaling with \(a = 1.2\) enables a unified description of the diffraction cone across energies, but the Orear region and high-\(t\) tails remain unexplained, requiring further non-linear extensions of the relation for \(\rho(s,t)\).
