# Productions of $η$, $ρ^0$ and $ϕ$ at large transverse momentum in Heavy ion Collisions

> The suppression of the productions of the $η$ meson in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and its ratio of $η/π^0$ are computed theoretically in the framework of the perturbative QCD(pQCD) to confront the experimental data which matches well. We explore how the hadron production ratios as$η/π^0$ would further disclose the informations of the production suppressions due to the energy loss of the energetic jet that propagating though the QGP medium. Also, we present our further studies on vector mesons such as $ρ^0$ and $ϕ$ within the same framework. The theoretical predictions based on pQCD are thus firstly given which give a decent description on the experimental measurements. It paved the way to the uniformly understanding of the strong suppression of single hadron productions at large transverse momentum which is a convincing evidence of the jet quenching effect.

## Key claim
The authors show that the perturbative QCD framework with higher-twist parton energy loss can simultaneously describe the suppression of η, ρ⁰, and φ mesons at large transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions, but the agreement for φ meson is less satisfactory than for the other mesons, indicating that the strange-quark fragmentation function needs better experimental constraints.

## Method
The authors compute single-inclusive hadron spectra in p+p collisions using NLO pQCD with parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions, then incorporate medium-modified fragmentation functions via the generalized QCD factorization of twist-4 processes to model parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. For ρ⁰ and φ mesons, the fragmentation functions are evolved using LO DGLAP evolution equations from a starting scale of Q₀² = 1.5 GeV², and the same jet transport parameter q̂₀ extracted from π⁰ production is applied to all mesons.

## Key numbers
- η meson nuclear modification factor R_AA in 5% most central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV is computed with q̂₀τ₀ = 0.48–1.02 GeV², and the calculation agrees with PHENIX data in the range p_T = 2–20 GeV
- η meson R_AA prediction for central Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV uses q̂₀τ₀ = 0.84–1.8 GeV²
- ρ⁰ nuclear modification factor in 0–10% most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC 200 GeV uses q̂₀τ₀ = 0.72 GeV² and explains data at large p_T
- φ meson R_AA in 0–10% Au+Au at RHIC 200 GeV is approximately 0.2, similar to π⁰ and η values
- η/π⁰ ratio in both p+p and A+A collisions approaches a universal value of approximately 0.5 at very high p_T

## Context
This work extends the higher-twist approach previously applied to π⁰ suppression by Chen et al. (2010, 2011) to η, ρ⁰, and φ mesons, using the same jet transport parameter q̂₀ extracted from π⁰ data. The study enables a unified understanding of identified hadron suppression patterns and shows that the η/π⁰ ratio is insensitive to flavor-dependent energy loss at high p_T, contrary to naive expectations. The predictions for ρ⁰ and φ at LHC energies provide benchmarks for upcoming ALICE measurements, while the φ results highlight the need for improved strange-quark fragmentation function parameterizations.
