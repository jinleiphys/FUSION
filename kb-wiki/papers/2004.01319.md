# Landscape of pear-shaped even-even nuclei

> The phenomenon of reflection-asymmetric nuclear shapes is relevant to nuclear stability, nuclear spectroscopy, nuclear decays and fission, and the search for new physics beyond the standard model. Global surveys of ground-state octupole deformation, performed with a limited number of models, suggest that the number of pear-shaped isotopes is fairly limited across the nuclear landscape. We carry out global analysis of ground-state octupole deformations for particle-bound even-even nuclei with $Z \leq 110$ and $N \leq 210$ using nuclear density functional theory (DFT) with several non-relativistic and covariant energy density functionals. In this way, we can identify the best candidates for reflection-asymmetric shapes. The calculations are performed in the frameworks of axial reflection-asymmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory using DFT solvers employing harmonic oscillator basis expansion. We consider five Skyrme and four covariant energy density functionals. We predict several regions of ground-state octupole deformation. In addition to the "traditional" regions of neutron-deficient actinide nuclei around $^{224}$Ra and neutron-rich lanthanides around $^{146}$Ba, we identified vast regions of reflecion-asymmetric shapes in very neutron-rich nuclei around $^{200}$Gd and $^{288}$Pu, as well as in several nuclei around $^{112}$Ba. Our analysis suggests several promising candidates with stable ground-state octupole deformation, primarily in the neutron-deficient actinide region, that can be reached experimentally. Detailed comparison between Skyrme and covariant models is performed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts several regions of ground-state octupole (pear-shaped) deformation in even-even nuclei across the nuclear landscape, but the authors note that many predicted octupole-deformed nuclei have small octupole deformation energies (under 500 keV), indicating soft potential energy surfaces where the octupole deformation may be transitional rather than static.

## Method

The authors performed global calculations using axial reflection-asymmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with five Skyrme energy density functionals (UNEDF0, UNEDF1, UNEDF2, SLy4, SV-min) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory with four covariant energy density functionals (DD-ME2, NL3*, PC-PK1, DD-PC1). The calculations covered particle-bound even-even nuclei with Z up to 110 and N up to 210, using harmonic oscillator basis expansion in the DFT solvers HFBTHO and a parallel RHB code. The authors defined model multiplicity m(Z,N) as the number of functionals (out of nine) predicting nonzero octupole deformation for a given nucleus.

## Key numbers

- 12 even-even nuclei predicted by all nine energy density functionals to be octupole-deformed: $^{146}$Ba, $^{224,226}$Ra, $^{226,228}$Th, $^{228}$Pu, $^{288,290}$Pu, $^{288,290}$Cm, and $^{288,290}$Cf
- Root-mean-square error of binding energy for Skyrme EDFs ranges from 1.7 MeV (UNEDF0) to 5.3 MeV (SLy4) compared to AME2016 dataset
- Root-mean-square error of binding energy for covariant EDFs ranges from 2.15 MeV (DD-PC1) to 3.0 MeV (NL3*) compared to AME2012 dataset
- Largest octupole deformation energy exceeding 2 MeV calculated for $^{224}$U in Skyrme calculations
- Octupole deformation energy for $^{220}$Rn averages around -0.5 MeV
- $^{228}$Pu has a half-life of 1.1 s
- Systematic shift of 2-4 neutrons between Skyrme and covariant predictions for octupole-unstable regions

## Context

This work extends previous global surveys of octupole deformation by Moller (2008), Robledo (2011), Agbemava (2016), Xia (2017), and Ebata (2017), but provides the first comprehensive inter-model comparison between five Skyrme and four covariant energy density functionals. The results identify robust candidates for atomic electric dipole moment measurements (particularly in the neutron-deficient actinide region around $^{224}$Ra) and provide a starting point for systematic searches of parity doublets in odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, which are relevant for searches of physics beyond the standard model. The study also identifies two exotic regions of octupole instability in very neutron-rich nuclei around $^{200}$Gd and $^{288}$Pu that may be relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis modeling.
