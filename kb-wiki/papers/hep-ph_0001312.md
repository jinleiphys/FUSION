# QCD forces and heavy quark bound states

> The present knowledge of QCD confining forces between static test charges is summarised, with an emphasis on lattice results. Recent developments in relating QCD potentials to quarkonium properties by use of effective field theory methods are presented. The validity of non-relativistic QCD and the adiabatic approximation with respect to heavy quark bound states is explored. Besides the static potential and relativistic correction terms, the spectra of glueballs and gluinoballs, hybrid excitations of the QCD flux tube between fundamental colour sources, potentials between charges in various representations of the SU(3) gauge group, and multi-particle interactions are discussed. Some implications for quarkonia systems and quark-gluon hybrid mesons are drawn.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the static QCD potential, combined with relativistic corrections derived from non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and lattice simulations, can be used to derive a semi-relativistic Hamiltonian for heavy quarkonium bound states, but the adiabatic approximation and the non-relativistic expansion are valid only for sufficiently heavy quarks where the characteristic time scale of quark motion is much larger than that of gluonic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results for the static potential between colour sources, including potentials in higher representations of SU(3), hybrid excitations of the QCD flux tube, and multi-body forces. Relativistic correction terms to the static potential are computed by separating gluonic degrees of freedom from heavy quark dynamics using NRQCD and integrating out the gluonic fields via lattice simulations. The resulting potentials are then applied to the Schrödinger equation for quarkonium systems, with spin-dependent terms treated via Pauli-Thomas-like extensions.

## Key numbers

* String tension from the ρ, a2, … Regge trajectory: √σ = (429 ± 2) MeV, extracted from linear fits to light meson masses in the m²–J plane.
* String tensions from other light meson Regge trajectories range from √σ = 424(5) MeV (K*, K*2, …) to √σ = 480(4) MeV (K, K1, …).
* The 2³S₁ – 1³S₁ splitting for charmonium is 589 MeV and for bottomonium is 563 MeV, agreeing within 5 %.
* The spin-averaged 1P – 1S splitting is 429 MeV for charmonium and 440 MeV for bottomonium.
* Ratio of fine structure splittings between bottomonium and charmonium: (mχb2 – mχb0) / (mχc2 – mχc0) = 0.38(1) ≈ 53 MeV / 141 MeV.
* Ratio of mχ2 – mχ1 splittings between bottomonium and charmonium: 0.47(2).
* Cornell potential parameters from fits to spin-averaged quarkonia spectra: e ≈ 0.25, √σ ≈ 455 MeV (before Υ(2S) discovery); later fits gave e ≈ 0.51, √σ ≈ 412 MeV and e ≈ 0.52, √σ ≈ 427 MeV.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on potential models for quarkonium, such as the Cornell potential of Eichten (1975) and the phenomenological models of Quigg (1979) and Buchmüller (1981), by connecting them directly to lattice QCD results and effective field theory methods (NRQCD) developed by Caswell (1986) and Lepage (1992). The paper enables future lattice calculations to test models like the stochastic vacuum model of Dosch (1987) and dual QCD of Baker (1983) against non-perturbative QCD predictions, and provides a framework for predicting properties of heavy quark-gluon hybrid mesons and Bc mesons.
