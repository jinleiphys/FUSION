# Spin Asymmetry and Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule for the Deuteron

> An explicit evaluation of the spin asymmetry of the deuteron and the associated GDH sum rule is presented which includes photodisintegration, single and double pion and eta production as well. Photodisintegration is treated with a realistic retarded potential and a corresponding meson exchange current. For single pion and eta production the elementary operator from MAID is employed whereas for double pion production an effective Lagrangean approach is used. A large cancellation between the disintegration and the meson production channels yields for the explicit GDH integral a value of 27.31 $μ$b to be compared to the sum rule value 0.65 $μ$b.

# Spin Asymmetry and Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule for the Deuteron

## Key claim

The explicit evaluation of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) integral for the deuteron yields 27.31 microbarns, which overshoots the sum rule value of 0.65 microbarns, despite a large cancellation between photodisintegration and meson production channels; the discrepancy indicates remaining theoretical uncertainties, particularly from two-pion production and incomplete convergence of some channels.

## Method

The authors computed the spin asymmetry and GDH integral for the deuteron by combining four reaction channels: photodisintegration (treated with a retarded potential, retarded pion-exchange currents, and coupled-channel NDelta dynamics), single pion and eta production (using the MAID model for the elementary operator), and double pion production (using an effective Lagrangian approach). Final-state interactions were included for the NN and piN subsystems where relevant, and the integrals were evaluated up to 0.8 GeV for photodisintegration, 1.5 GeV for single pion and eta production, and 2.2 GeV for double pion production.

## Key numbers

- GDH integral for the deuteron from explicit evaluation: 27.31 microbarns, compared to the sum rule value of 0.65 microbarns
- Photodisintegration contribution to the deuteron GDH integral: -381.52 microbarns (integrated up to 0.8 GeV)
- Single pion production contribution on deuteron: 263.44 microbarns (up to 1.5 GeV)
- Double pion production contribution on deuteron: 159.34 microbarns (up to 2.2 GeV)
- Eta production contribution on deuteron: -13.95 microbarns (up to 1.5 GeV)
- Deuteron anomalous magnetic moment: -0.143 nuclear magnetons, giving the sum rule value of 0.65 microbarns
- Previous evaluation (Arenhövel and Kress, 1997) gave -187 microbarns up to 550 MeV, with only single pion production and no final-state interactions
- Neutron GDH integral from explicit evaluation: 215.20 microbarns (sum rule value 233.16 microbarns)
- Proton GDH integral from explicit evaluation: 261.54 microbarns (sum rule value 204.78 microbarns)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Arenhövel and Kress (1997) and Darwish and Arenhövel (2003), which suffered from simplified production operators, neglect of final-state interactions, and limited energy range. The present calculation improves upon those by including retarded potentials, coupled-channel NDelta dynamics, the realistic MAID model for single pion production, and double pion and eta production for the first time. The results demonstrate that the deuteron GDH sum rule provides a stringent test of strong interaction dynamics across very different energy regimes, and that polarization data on the deuteron cannot be simply reduced to neutron spin asymmetries by subtracting proton contributions, requiring instead a reliable theoretical model for interpretation.
