# Chiral effects on radiation and energy loss in quark-gluon plasma

> The emergent axion excitations in the quark-gluon plasma are generated by the sphaleron transitions. Their interactions with the photon and gluon fields are governed by the axion electro- and chromodynamics respectively. We discuss the effect of the emergent axion on the photon production and energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. In particular, we review the Chiral Cherenkov effect, and the impact of the chiral anomaly on bremsstrahlung.

## Key claim

The emergent axion field in quark-gluon plasma, generated by sphaleron transitions, modifies photon production and energy loss through the chiral anomaly; however, the effect is significant only when the chiral magnetic conductivity is positive and the photon helicity matches the sign of the anomaly, and the classical energy loss scaling as \(E^2\) is reduced to a linear dependence on energy in the full quantum treatment.

## Method

The authors derive the electromagnetic fields of a point charge moving through chiral matter by solving the Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations with a constant chiral magnetic conductivity \(\sigma_\chi\), using both classical (Fermi model) and quantum field theory approaches. In the quantum treatment, they compute the photon radiation rate from a charged fermion using the high-energy approximation to the Dirac equation and the modified photon dispersion relation \(k^2 = -\lambda \sigma_\chi |\mathbf{k}|\), considering both infinite homogeneous chiral matter and two semi-infinite domains separated by a domain wall.

## Key numbers

- The classical energy loss per unit length for an ultrarelativistic particle is \(-\frac{dE}{dz} = \frac{q^2}{4\pi v^2} \left( \omega_p^2 \ln \frac{v}{a\omega_p} + \frac{1}{4} \gamma^2 \sigma_\chi^2 \right)\), where the second term dominates at high energies and scales as \(\gamma^2\).
- The quantum energy loss per unit length for a fermion in infinite chiral matter is \(\frac{dE}{dz} = \frac{1}{3} \alpha q^2 \sigma_\chi E\), scaling linearly with energy \(E\) rather than quadratically.
- The classical Chiral Cherenkov radiated power is \(P = \frac{q^2}{4\pi} \frac{\sigma_\chi^2 \gamma^2}{4}\), with the spectrum valid for \(\omega < \sigma_\chi \gamma^2\).
- The photon radiation rate in infinite chiral matter for right-polarized photons is \(\frac{dW_+}{dx} = \frac{\alpha q^2}{2 x} \left\{ \sigma_\chi \left(\frac{x^2}{2} - x + 1\right) - m^2 x \right\} \theta(x_0 - x)\), where \(x_0 = 1/(1 + m^2/(\sigma_\chi E))\).
- The magnetic field in the diffusion approximation acquires oscillatory components: \(B_\phi = \frac{eb}{8\pi x_-^2} e^{-b^2\sigma/(4x_-)} \left[ \sigma \cos\left(\frac{b^2\sigma_\chi}{4x_-}\right) + \sigma_\chi \sin\left(\frac{b^2\sigma_\chi}{4x_-}\right) \right]\), with \(\sigma = 5.8\) MeV used for numerical estimates.

## Context

This review extends the classical work of Fermi (1940) on collisional energy loss to chiral matter, and builds on prior studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) and Fukushima (2008). The quantum treatment of Chiral Cherenkov radiation generalizes the transition radiation formalism of Baier (1998) and Schildknecht (2005) to include the anomalous axion-photon coupling. The results enable phenomenological studies of photon production and jet energy loss in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly for understanding how topological \(CP\)-odd domains affect electromagnetic probes of the quark-gluon plasma.
