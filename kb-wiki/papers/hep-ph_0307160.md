# Longitudinal structure function of the proton at low momentum transfer and extended constituents

> Recent data on the (inelastic) Nachtmann moments of the unpolarized proton structure function F2p, obtained at low momentum transfer with the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab, have been interpreted in terms of the dominance of the elastic coupling of the virtual photon with extended substructures inside the proton. Adopting the same constituent form factors and the same light-front wave function describing the motion of the constituents in the proton, the (inelastic) Nachtmann moments of the longitudinal proton structure function FLp are calculated (without further parameters) for values of the squared four-momentum transfer 0.2 < Q**2 (GeV/c)**2 < 2. The different role played by the Pauli form factor of the constituents in the transverse and longitudinal channels is illustrated. Our predictions, including an estimate of the theoretical uncertainties, may be checked against the forthcoming results of the experiment E94110 at Jefferson Lab. A positive comparison with the new data may provide compelling evidence that constituent quarks are intermediate substructures between the hadrons and the current quarks and gluons of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the (inelastic) Nachtmann moments of the longitudinal proton structure function \(F_L^p\) at low momentum transfer (\(0.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 2\) (GeV/c)²) are sharply sensitive to the Pauli form factor of constituent quarks for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 0.5\) (GeV/c)², unlike the transverse channel where the Pauli term has little effect. The predictions are made without introducing new parameters beyond those already fixed in the generalized two-stage model of Simula, Petronzio, and Ricco (2003).

## Method

The author applies the generalized two-stage model, in which the inelastic coupling of the virtual photon to constituent quarks (CQs) is replaced by elastic coupling at low \(Q^2\), to compute the dual longitudinal structure function \(F_L^{(dual)}(\xi, Q^2)\) using Eq. (13). This function includes a term proportional to the longitudinal-to-transverse ratio at the CQ level, \(R_Q\), which vanishes when the CQ Pauli form factor \(f_2^j\) is zero. The dual longitudinal Nachtmann moments \(M_n^{(L, dual)}(Q^2)\) for orders \(n = 4, 6, 8, 10\) are then evaluated via Eq. (15) using the same gaussian light-front wave function (with \(\beta = 0.3\) GeV and \(m = 0.25\) GeV) and the same CQ form factors (monopole for Dirac, dipole for Pauli) as in the prior transverse analysis of Simula, Petronzio, and Ricco (2003). Theoretical uncertainties are estimated by varying the CQ size parameters \(r_{1Q}\) and \(r_{2Q}\) independently between 0.2 and 0.4 fm.

## Key numbers

- The calculation covers the squared four-momentum transfer range \(0.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 2\) (GeV/c)².
- The CQ Dirac form factor is a monopole: \(f_1^j(Q^2) = e_j / (1 + r_Q^2 Q^2 / 6)\) with \(r_Q = 0.21\) fm for the comparison of Pauli effects (Fig. 1).
- The CQ Pauli form factor is a dipole: \(f_2^j(Q^2) = \kappa_j / (1 + r_Q^2 Q^2 / 12)^2\) with \(\kappa_U = -0.064\) and \(\kappa_D = 0.017\).
- For the final predictions (Fig. 2), the CQ size is set to \(r_Q = 0.33\) fm, which gives the best reproduction of nucleon elastic data.
- At \(Q^2 = 2\) (GeV/c)², the contribution of the resonance region (\(W \leq 2\) GeV) to the longitudinal moment integral is approximately 70%, 88%, 96%, and 99% for \(n = 4, 6, 8,\) and 10, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the generalized two-stage model of Simula, Petronzio, and Ricco (2003), which successfully interpreted CLAS data on the transverse Nachtmann moments of \(F_2^p\) and extracted a CQ size of \(\approx 0.2\)–\(0.3\) fm. The present calculation provides predictions for the longitudinal channel that can be tested against the forthcoming results of Jefferson Lab experiment E94110. A positive comparison would provide compelling evidence that constituent quarks are intermediate substructures between hadrons and the current quarks and gluons of QCD.
