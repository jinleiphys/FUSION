# Natural orbitals representation and Fermi sea depletion in finite nuclei and nuclear matter

> The natural orbitals and natural occupation numbers of various N = Z, sp and sd shell nuclei are calculated by applying a correlated one-body density matrix. The correlated density matrix has been evaluated by considering central correlations of Jastrow type and an approximation named factor cluster expansion. The correlation effects on the natural orbitals, natural occupation numbers and the Fermi sea depletion are discussed and analysed. In addition, an approximate expression for the correlated one-body density matrix of the nuclear matter has been used for the evaluation of the relative momentum distribution and the Fermi sea depletion. We found that the value of the Fermi sea depletion is higher in closed shell nuclei compared to open shell ones and it is lower compared to the case of nuclear matter. This statement could be confirmed by relevant experimental studies.

# Natural orbitals representation and Fermi sea depletion in finite nuclei and nuclear matter

## Key claim
The Fermi sea depletion is higher in closed shell nuclei than in open shell ones, but lower in both cases compared to nuclear matter, even for the same strength of short-range correlations. This conclusion depends on the use of a Jastrow-type correlation function with a Gaussian form and a factor cluster expansion approximation, and the authors note that definitive confirmation requires future experimental studies.

## Method
The authors calculated the correlated one-body density matrix for N=Z sp and sd shell nuclei using central Jastrow-type correlations with a Gaussian correlation function f(r)=1-exp(-beta r^2) and a factor cluster expansion. They diagonalized this density matrix to obtain natural orbitals and natural occupation numbers, with the harmonic oscillator size parameter b and correlation parameter beta determined by least squares fits to experimental form factors from prior work. For nuclear matter, they employed a low order approximation expression for the correlated one-body density matrix to evaluate the relative momentum distribution and Fermi sea depletion, using a nuclear matter density of 0.182 fm^-3 corresponding to k_F=1.3915 fm^-1.

## Key numbers
- Fermi sea depletion values for finite nuclei: 5.16% for 4He, 3.41% for 12C, 4.00% for 16O, 1.59% for 24Mg, 2.85% for 28Si, 2.52% for 32S, 5.38% for 36Ar, and 5.91% for 40Ca.
- For comparison, Stoitsov et al. found Fermi sea depletions of 5.7% for 4He, 3.9% for 16O, and 6.3% for 40Ca using a different method.
- Experimental analysis of (e,e'p) and (d,3He) reactions on 40Ca by Kramer (1990) gave a depletion value of 9.4%.
- The correlation parameter beta range considered was 1.13 fm^-2 to 7 fm^-2, corresponding to wound parameter k_dir values from 0.3 to 0.02.
- The nuclear matter density used was rho_0=0.182 fm^-3 with Fermi wave number k_F=1.3915 fm^-1.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of short-range correlations in finite nuclei using the correlated one-body density matrix approach developed by Moustakidis (2000) and the factor cluster expansion method of Clark and co-workers. The results are compared with the theoretical calculations of Stoitsov et al. (1993) for closed shell nuclei and with experimental data from Kramer (1990) for 40Ca. The study enables future experimental investigations of Fermi sea depletion that include both closed and open shell nuclei, and provides a comparison between finite nuclei and nuclear matter under similar correlation conditions.
