# Comment on $\frac{K}π$ Fluctuations at Relativistic Energies

> We discuss the $\frac{K}π$ fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions reported by the STAR collaboration, PRL${\bf 103}$,092301(2009), also discussed by the NA49 collaboration at SPS-CERN. We show that these fluctuations could be explained simply by imposing strangeness and charge conservations.

## Key claim

The dynamical fluctuations of the K/π ratio reported by STAR and NA49 can be explained simply by imposing strangeness and charge conservation on Poissonian distributions for each particle type, without invoking a phase transition to Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Method

The authors generated events from Poissonian distributions for each particle type (kaons and pions) using average particle numbers taken from experimental data at each beam energy. They imposed that the number of K⁺ and K⁻ differs by at most 1-3 units to enforce strangeness and charge conservation, then calculated the dynamical fluctuation quantities ν_dynKπ and σ_dyn from these simulated events.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 200 GeV, the ratio of K⁻/K⁺ is approximately 0.9, indicating that strict equality of K⁺ and K⁻ numbers is not achieved due to other strange particles
- The best agreement with experimental data is obtained when the charged kaon difference |N_K⁺ - N_K⁻| is at most 2
- Calculations with |N_K⁺ - N_K⁻| ≤ 1 (triangles), ≤ 2 (circles), and ≤ 3 (diamonds) are shown, with stronger constraints producing larger fluctuations

## Context

This comment addresses the dynamical fluctuation measurements reported by the STAR collaboration (2009) and NA49 collaboration at SPS-CERN, which had been proposed as possible signatures of a phase transition from nuclear matter to Quark-Gluon Plasma. The authors argue that before attributing the observed fluctuations to a phase transition, trivial effects such as strangeness and charge conservation must be ruled out. Their simple Poissonian model with conservation constraints reproduces the data, suggesting that either there is no phase transition in the energy region investigated, or that the expansion during collision is too fast for critical fluctuations to develop, pointing toward the need for other observables sensitive to phase transitions in finite dynamical systems.
