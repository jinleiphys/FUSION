# Pion form factor in the Kroll-Lee-Zumino model

> The renormalizable Abelian quantum field theory model of Kroll, Lee, and Zumino is used to compute the one-loop vertex corrections to the tree-level, Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) pion form factor. These corrections, together with the known one-loop vacuum polarization contribution, lead to a substantial improvement over VMD. The resulting pion form factor in the space-like region is in excellent agreement with data in the whole range of accessible momentum transfers. The time-like form factor, known to reproduce the Gounaris-Sakurai formula at and near the rho-meson peak, is unaffected by the vertex correction at order $\cal{O}$$(g_\rpp^2)$.

# Pion form factor in the Kroll-Lee-Zumino model

## Key claim
The one-loop vertex correction to the tree-level Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) pion form factor, computed in the renormalizable Kroll-Lee-Zumino (KLZ) Abelian quantum field theory, yields a parameter-free prediction that agrees with experimental data in the space-like region with a chi-squared per degree of freedom close to unity, while the time-like form factor near the rho-meson peak remains unaffected by the vertex correction at order g_ρππ^2 and reproduces the Gounaris-Sakurai formula.

## Method
The authors computed the one-loop vertex correction to the ρππ coupling in the KLZ model using dimensional regularization in d dimensions, renormalized at the off-shell point q^2 = 0 to exploit the known normalization F_π(0) = 1. They combined this vertex correction with the known one-loop vacuum polarization contribution from Gale and Kapusta (1998) to obtain the complete pion form factor at order g_ρππ^2, performing numerical integration of the resulting Feynman parameter integrals. The seagull-type corrections at the same order were shown to cancel after renormalization.

## Key numbers
- The ρππ coupling constant used is g_ρππ ≃ 5, with the loop suppression factor 1/(4π)^2 ensuring one-loop contributions remain reasonable corrections.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the KLZ prediction in the space-like region is χ_F^2 = 1.1, compared to χ_F^2 = 5.0 for tree-level VMD.
- The mean-square radius of the pion from the KLZ model is ⟨r_π^2⟩ = 0.40 fm^2, compared to the experimental value ⟨r_π^2⟩ = 0.439 ± 0.008 fm^2 and the tree-level VMD value ⟨r_π^2⟩ = 6/M_ρ^2 = 0.39 fm^2.
- The ρ meson width follows from the vacuum polarization as Γ_ρ = (g_ρππ^2 / 48π) (1/M_ρ^2) (M_ρ^2 - 4 m_π^2)^(3/2), with f_ρ = 4.97 ± 0.07 from the Particle Data Group.

## Context
This work extends the one-loop vacuum polarization calculation of Gale and Kapusta (1998) in the KLZ model by adding the one-loop vertex correction, completing the next-to-leading order calculation. The resulting form factor improves significantly over naive tree-level VMD and achieves agreement comparable to Dual Large N_c QCD (QCD_∞) from Dominguez (2000s), which requires one free parameter in the space-like region. The KLZ model uniquely reproduces the Gounaris-Sakurai formula (1968) in the time-like region without free parameters, and the authors argue for its viability as a tool for analyzing ππ dynamics, noting that charged rho-meson dynamics would remain excluded if renormalizability is insisted upon.
