# Near-threshold $ϕ$ meson production in proton-nucleus reactions and $ϕ$ width in finite nuclei

> In the framework of the nuclear spectral function approach for incoherent primary proton-nucleon and secondary pion-nucleon production processes we study the inclusive $ϕ$ meson production in the interaction of 2.83 GeV protons with nuclei. In particular, the A- and momentum-dependences of the absolute and relative phi meson yields are investigated within the different scenarios for its in-medium width. Our model calculations take into account the acceptance window of the ANKE facility used in a recent experiment performed at COSY. They show that the pion-nucleon production channel contributes distinctly to the "low-momentum" $ϕ$ creation in heavy nuclei in the chosen kinematics and, hence, has to be taken into consideration on close examination of the dependences of the phi meson yields on the target mass number with the aim to get information on its width in the medium.

# Near-threshold φ meson production in proton-nucleus reactions and φ width in finite nuclei

## Key claim

The A-dependence of the relative φ meson production cross section and the momentum-dependence of the absolute φ meson yield in 2.83 GeV proton-nucleus reactions are appreciably sensitive to the φ in-medium width, but only if the secondary pion-nucleon production channel is included in the analysis, particularly for low-momentum φ mesons in heavy nuclei.

## Method

The author employed the nuclear spectral function approach for incoherent primary proton-nucleon (pN → pNφ and pn → dφ) and secondary pion-nucleon (πN → φN) production processes, incorporating the struck target nucleon momentum and removal energy distributions. The model calculations included four different scenarios for the total φ in-medium width (ranging from the free vacuum width of 4.45 MeV up to approximately 60 MeV at saturation density) and imposed the ANKE spectrometer kinematical cuts on laboratory φ momenta (0.6 to 1.8 GeV/c) and production angles (0° to 8°). The nuclear density distributions used were harmonic oscillator for 12C and Woods-Saxon for heavier targets (27Al, 63Cu, 108Ag, 197Au, 238U).

## Key numbers

- The free φ meson vacuum width is Γφ = 4.45 MeV.
- The incident proton beam energy was 2.83 GeV, with a free production threshold for φ mesons of approximately 2.59 GeV.
- The ANKE acceptance window was 0.6 GeV/c ≤ pφ ≤ 1.8 GeV/c and 0° ≤ θφ ≤ 8°.
- For the in-medium width scenario ii, the total (decay + collisional) φ width reaches around 30 MeV at saturation density ρ0.
- For scenario iii, the φ collisional width at ρ0 is 25.4 MeV, giving a total φ width of about 45 MeV at ρ0.
- For scenario iv, the φ collisional width at ρ0 is 40.4 MeV, giving a total φ width of about 60 MeV at ρ0.
- The calculated double ratio R(AX)/R(12C) for heavy nuclei in the full ANKE acceptance window ranges from approximately 0.30 to 0.52 depending on the φ width scenario and production mechanism considered.
- The φ meson branching ratio to K+K- is approximately 50%.

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by previous authors (2004) of using the A-dependence of φ production cross sections in proton- and photon-induced reactions as a measure for in-medium φ broadening. The calculations are designed for direct comparison with the ANKE-at-COSY experiment (Hartmann et al., 2006; Kiselev et al., 2007). The paper enables extraction of the φ meson in-medium width from future data by showing that both the A-dependence of relative yields and the momentum-dependence of absolute yields are sensitive to φ absorption, and that the secondary pion-induced channel must be included for low-momentum φ production in heavy nuclei.
