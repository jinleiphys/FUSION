# Jet quenching

> We present a comprehensive review of the physics of hadron and jet production at large transverse momentum in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. Emphasis is put on experimental and theoretical "jet quenching" observables that provide direct information on the (thermo)dynamical properties of hot and dense QCD matter.

## Key claim

Jet quenching observables provide direct information on the (thermo)dynamical properties of hot and dense QCD matter, but the quantitative extraction of medium properties such as the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ depends on higher-order corrections that could result in much larger values than leading-order estimates.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for parton energy loss in a Quark-Gluon Plasma, deriving collisional and radiative energy loss formulas for both QED and QCD cases. It presents the parametric dependences of energy loss on parton energy $E$, medium temperature $T$, thickness $L$, and Debye mass $m_D$, distinguishing between Bethe-Heitler and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal regimes for radiative losses, and between light and heavy quark cases for collisional losses.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density for QGP formation: $\varepsilon_{crit} \approx 1$ GeV/fm$^3$ from lattice QCD
- For a gluon plasma at $T=0.4$ GeV with $\alpha_s \approx 0.5$: gluon density $\rho_g \approx 15$ fm$^{-3}$, energy density $\epsilon_g \approx 17$ GeV/fm$^3$, Debye mass $m_D \approx 1$ GeV, gluon-gluon cross section $\sigma_{gg} \approx 1.5$ mb, gluon mean free path $\lambda_g \approx 0.45$ fm, quark mean free path $\lambda_q \approx 1$ fm, transport coefficient $\hat{q} \approx 2.2$ GeV$^2$/fm
- QCD scale parameter: $\Lambda_{QCD} \approx 0.2$ GeV
- Colour factors: $C_A = 3$ for gluons, $C_F = 4/3$ for quarks, ratio $C_A/C_F = 9/4$

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on jet quenching proposed by Bjorken (1982) and the collisional energy loss calculations by Braaten and Thoma (1991), incorporating later improvements including running coupling effects from Peshier (2006) and Peigne (2008). It provides the theoretical basis for interpreting RHIC measurements of high-$p_T$ hadron suppression and sets the stage for jet quenching studies at the LHC, where the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ and the initial gluon density $dN^g/dy$ can be extracted from experimental data.
