# Low-lying spectroscopy of a few even-even silicon isotopes investigated by means of the multiparticle-multihole Gogny energy density functional

> A multiconfiguration microscopic method has been applied with the Gogny effective interaction to the calculation of low-lying positive-parity states in even-even $^{26-28}$Si isotopes. The aim of the study is to compare the results of this approach with those of a standard method of GCM type and to get insight into the predictive power of multiconfiguration methods employed with effective nucleon-nucleon force taylored to mean-field calculations. It is found that the multiconfiguration approach leads to an excellent description of the low-lying spectroscopy of $^{26}$Si, $^{28}$Si and $^{32}$Si, but gives a systematic energy shift in $^{30}$Si. A careful analysis of this phenomenon shows that this discrepancy originates from too large matrix elements in the proton-neutron residual interaction supplied by the Gogny interaction. Finally, a statistical analysis of highly excited configurations in $^{28}$Si is performed, revealing exponential convergence in agreement with previous work in the context of the shell model approach. This latter result provides strong arguments towards an implicit treatment of highly excited configurations.

## Key claim

The multiparticle-multihole (mp-mh) configuration mixing method with the Gogny D1S interaction provides an excellent description of low-lying positive-parity states in even-even 26Si, 28Si, and 32Si, but produces a systematic upward energy shift of approximately 2.5 MeV for all calculated states in 30Si, which the authors trace to overly large proton-neutron matrix elements in the residual interaction.

## Method

The authors applied a variational multiparticle-multihole configuration mixing approach using the finite-range density-dependent D1S Gogny interaction, solving only the configuration interaction part of the equations (not the full self-consistent problem) within a spherical harmonic oscillator basis of N0=11 major shells. For each silicon isotope, they constructed trial wave functions as linear combinations of direct products of proton and neutron Slater determinants containing up to 6p-6h excitations on the proton side and up to 6p-6h on the neutron side, yielding total configuration spaces up to 12p-12h. They compared their results to experimental data and to a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) approach based on the generator coordinate method.

## Key numbers

- For 26Si: mp-mh gives 2+1 at 1.502 MeV (experiment: 1.795 MeV) and 2+2 at 2.567 MeV (experiment: 2.783 MeV); 5DCH gives 2+1 at 2.426 MeV
- For 28Si: mp-mh gives 2+1 at 1.993 MeV (experiment: 1.779 MeV) and 4+1 at 5.372 MeV (experiment: 4.618 MeV); 5DCH gives 2+1 at 2.469 MeV
- For 30Si: mp-mh gives 2+1 at 4.609 MeV (experiment: 2.235 MeV), a systematic overestimate of approximately 2.5 MeV; after subtracting 2.5 MeV, the average discrepancy reduces to about 0.69 MeV
- For 32Si: mp-mh gives 2+1 at 1.959 MeV (experiment: 1.941 MeV) and 2+2 at 4.871 MeV (experiment: 4.230 MeV); 5DCH gives 2+1 at 2.215 MeV
- The neutron gap between nu-2s1/2 and nu-1d3/2 orbitals in 30Si reaches a maximum value of approximately 4.56 MeV
- Convergence of low-lying state energies in the mp-mh approach was achieved within an accuracy better than 0.1 keV
- The largest differences (approximately 500 keV) from including 1f7/2 and 2p3/2 orbitals were encountered in 30Si and 32Si

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the mp-mh configuration mixing method (Pillet, 2008), which was initially applied only to pairing correlations in tin isotopes, to the description of low-lying spectroscopy in silicon isotopes. The results are compared with the 5DCH approach of Delaroche (2010) and Libert, which uses the same Gogny D1S interaction but exploits quadrupole deformation properties rather than explicit residual interaction matrix elements. The statistical analysis of highly excited configurations in 28Si, revealing exponential convergence, agrees with previous shell model work (Horoi, 1999; Zelevinsky, 1996) and provides support for implicit treatment of highly excited configurations in future calculations.
