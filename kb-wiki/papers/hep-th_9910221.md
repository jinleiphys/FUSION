# A Model for Topological Fermions

> We introduce a model designed to describe charged particles as stable topological solitons of a field with values on the internal space S^3. These solitons behave like particles with relativistic properties like Lorentz contraction and velocity dependence of mass. This mass is defined by the energy of the soliton. In this sense this model is a generalisation of the sine-Gordon model from 1+1 dimensions to 3+1 dimensions, from S^1 to S^3. (We do not chase the aim to give a four-dimensional generalisation of Coleman's isomorphism between the Sine-Gordon model and the Thirring model which was shown in 2-dimensional space-time.) For large distances from the center of solitons this model tends to a dual U(1)-theory with freely propagating electromagnetic waves. Already at the classical level it describes important effects, which usually have to be explained by quantum field theory, like particle-antiparticle annihilation and the running of the coupling.

## Key claim

This paper proposes a classical field-theoretic model in 3+1 dimensions where charged particles such as electrons are described as stable topological solitons of an SU(2)-valued field on S^3, generalizing the sine-Gordon model; the model claims to reproduce relativistic properties and effects usually requiring quantum field theory (such as particle-antiparticle annihilation and running coupling) already at the classical level, but only for odd winding numbers of the soliton field.

## Method

The author constructs a Lagrangian for an SU(2)-valued field Q(x) on S^3, using a hedgehog ansatz Q = cos α(r) + i (r̂·σ) sin α(r). The electric field strength is defined via the curvature (Riemann-Christoffel tensor) of the internal-space geometry, related to the dual gauge field C. The static energy functional combines an electric-field term (analogous to the Skyrme term) with a stabilizing potential term proportional to cos^(2m) α, introducing a fundamental length scale r₀. Minimization yields a nonlinear second-order differential equation for the profile α(r), which is solved analytically for m=3 and numerically for other cases.

## Key numbers

* For the m=3 case, the analytical solution for the lowest-energy (n_w=1) soliton is α(r) = atan(r/r₀), with sin α = ρ/√(1+ρ²) and cos α = 1/√(1+ρ²), where ρ = r/r₀.
* The radial energy densities for this solution are: h_rad = (α_f ħc / r₀) · ρ² / [2(1+ρ²)²], h_tan = (α_f ħc / r₀) · ρ² / (1+ρ²)³, h_p = (α_f ħc / r₀) · ρ² / (1+ρ²)³.
* The electric flux through the sphere at infinity is ∮ dS·E = –(e₀/ε₀) T_r, matching Gauss's law for the electron charge.
* The static energy functional in scaled coordinates ρ = r/r₀ is H = (α_f ħc / r₀) ∫₀^∞ dρ [ sin⁴α/(2ρ²) + (∂_ρ cos α)² + ρ² cos^(2m) α ].

## Context

This work extends the 1+1-dimensional sine-Gordon model (which has topological kink solutions with relativistic properties) to 3+1 dimensions by replacing the target space S¹ with S³. It contrasts with the Skyrme model (Manton, 1993), which uses even winding numbers and a different field-strength definition that suppresses long-range Coulomb fields; the present model instead defines field strength via curvature to retain a Coulomb-like tail. The stabilization mechanism parallels the 't Hooft-Polyakov monopole (1974) in the Georgi-Glashow model but uses a simpler potential without requiring renormalizability. The model aims to provide an intuitive, classical foundation for phenomena usually treated by quantum field theory, such as the running coupling and the Pauli principle.
