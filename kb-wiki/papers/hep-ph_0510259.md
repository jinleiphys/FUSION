# Lepton-nucleus scattering in the impulse approximation regime

> We discuss theoretical calculations of electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering, carried out using realistic nuclear spectral functions and including the effect of final state interactions. Comparison between electron scattering data and the calculated inclusive cross sections off oxygen shows that the Fermi gas model fails to provide a satisfactory description of the measured cross sections, and inclusion of nuclear dynamics is needed. The role of Pauli blocking in charged-current neutrino induced reactions at low $Q^2$ is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a realistic nuclear spectral function combined with a treatment of final state interactions (FSI) can describe inclusive electron-oxygen scattering data far better than the Fermi gas model, but only in the quasi-elastic peak region; the calculation significantly underestimates the data in the Delta-production region, likely due to deficiencies in the elementary electron-nucleon cross section at low momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive electron- and neutrino-nucleus cross sections within the impulse approximation (IA) using a nuclear spectral function obtained from the Local Density Approximation. They incorporate final state interactions through a folding function derived from the eikonal approximation, and treat Pauli blocking by modifying the spectral function with a step function that imposes a threshold on the struck nucleon momentum relative to the average nuclear Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- The calculated \(^{16}\text{O}(e,e^\prime)\) cross sections at beam energies between 700 MeV and 1200 MeV and scattering angle 32° agree with data to better than 10% in the quasi-elastic peak region.
- The Fermi gas model with Fermi momentum \(p_F = 225\) MeV and nucleon removal energy \(\epsilon = 25\) MeV largely overestimates the measured cross section at beam energy 880 MeV and scattering angle 32°.
- Pauli blocking produces a large effect on the \(Q^2\) distributions for \(Q^2 < 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) in neutrino-induced reactions at \(E_\nu = 1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the many-body theory of electron-nucleus scattering developed by Benhar and collaborators (2001) to charged-current neutrino-induced reactions on oxygen, the main target nucleus in the Super-Kamiokande and K2K experiments. The results contradict the simpler Fermi gas model predictions and show that both dynamical FSI and Pauli blocking must be included to reproduce data. The approach enables more reliable neutrino energy reconstruction in oscillation experiments, though the failure in the Delta region points to the need for improved nucleon structure functions at low \(Q^2\), as discussed in Benhar et al. (2005).
