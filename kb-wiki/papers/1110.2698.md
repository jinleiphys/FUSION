# The O(2) model in polar coordinates at nonzero temperature

> We study the restoration of spontaneously broken symmetry at nonzero temperature in the framework of the O(2) model using polar coordinates. We apply the CJT formalism to calculate the masses and the condensate in the double-bubble approximation, both with and without a term that explicitly breaks the O(2) symmetry. We find that, in the case with explicitly broken symmetry, the mass of the angular degree of freedom becomes tachyonic above a temperature of about 300 MeV. Taking the term that explicitly breaks the symmetry to be infinitesimally small, we find that the Goldstone theorem is respected below the critical temperature. However, this limit cannot be performed for temperatures above the phase transition. We find that, no matter whether we break the symmetry explicitly or not, there is no region of temperature in which the radial and the angular degree of freedom become degenerate in mass. These results hold also when the mass of the radial mode is sent to infinity.

## Key claim

The authors find that in the O(2) model using polar coordinates within the CJT formalism in the double-bubble approximation, the mass of the angular degree of freedom becomes tachyonic above approximately 300 MeV when explicit symmetry breaking is present, and the chiral partners never become degenerate in mass regardless of temperature or whether symmetry is explicitly broken.

## Method

The authors apply the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism to the O(2) model in polar coordinates at nonzero temperature, working in the double-bubble (Hartree) approximation. They derive and solve self-consistent equations for the condensate, the dressed masses of the radial (sigma) and angular (pion) degrees of freedom, and a wave-function renormalization factor for the pion, both with and without an explicit symmetry-breaking term H. They also study the case with a shifted potential (parameter v) to circumvent singularities at the origin, and examine the limit where the radial mode mass is sent to infinity.

## Key numbers

* The zero-temperature tree-level masses are set to m_sigma = 600 MeV and m_pi = 139.5 MeV, with the chiral condensate f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
* For H ≠ 0 and v = 0, the pion mass becomes tachyonic above T_max ≈ 300.5 MeV, signaling a breakdown of the model.
* For H ≠ 0 and v = 0, a first-order phase transition occurs at T* ≈ 279.6 MeV, where the effective potential has equal values at two minima.
* For H = 0 and v = 0, the pion mass remains exactly zero at all temperatures below the chiral phase transition, satisfying the Goldstone theorem.

## Context

This work extends studies of symmetry restoration in the O(N) model, which has been investigated in Cartesian coordinates by Pisarski (1983), Wilczek (1992), Lenaghan (1999), and others. The authors show that using polar coordinates within the CJT double-bubble approximation yields results substantially different from the standard Cartesian treatment, particularly regarding the behavior of the Goldstone mode and the absence of mass degeneracy between chiral partners. The findings highlight limitations of angular variables at nonzero temperature and suggest that the polar coordinate representation becomes ill-defined in the high-temperature region due to singular terms proportional to inverse powers of the condensate.
