# Photo-nuclear reaction rates of $^{157,159}$Ho and $^{163,165}$Tm and their impact in the $Îł$--process

> Reliable photo-nuclear reaction rates at the stellar conditions are essential to understand the origin of the heavy stable neutron-deficient isotopes between $^{74}$Se and $^{196}$Hg-p-nuclei, however, many reaction rates of relevance still have to rely on the Hauser-Feshbach model due to rare experimental progress. One such case is in the mass range of 160 for Dy, Er, Ho and Tm isotopes. In this work we attempt to constrain the Hauser-Feshbach model in the TALYS package by reproducing the available experimental data of $^{160}$Dy($p,Îł$)$^{161}$Ho and $^{162}$Er($p,Îł$)$^{163}$Tm in the $A\sim 160$ mass region, and examine the effects of level density, gamma strength function and the optical model potential. The constrained model then allows us to calculate the reaction rates of $^{157, 159}$Ho($Îł$, $p$) and $^{163,165}$Tm($Îł$, $p$) for the $Îł$-process nucleosynthesis in carbon-deflagration SNe Ia model. Our recommended rates differ from the JINA REACLIB by more than 1 order of magnitude in the temperature range of 2-3 GK. This results in the changes of final abundance of $p$-nuclei in the $A\sim 160$ mass range by -5.5-3\% from those with JINA, which means that the ($Îł$, $p$) reactions uncertainty is not predominant for the synthesis of these nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors constrain Hauser-Feshbach model parameters in the TALYS package by reproducing experimental data for proton capture on $^{160}$Dy and $^{162}$Er, then calculate photo-proton reaction rates for $^{157,159}$Ho and $^{163,165}$Tm that differ from JINA REACLIB by more than one order of magnitude at 2-3 GK; however, these rate differences change final $p$-nuclei abundances in the $A\sim160$ mass range by only -5.5% to 3%, indicating that ($\gamma$, $p$) reaction uncertainty is not predominant for synthesizing these nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using TALYS 2.0, varying 52 key physical inputs including nuclear level density (NLD), gamma strength function (GSF), and optical model potential (OMP) to generate 10,000 random model combinations. They constrained the model by minimizing the reduced chi-square against experimental cross sections of $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$)$^{161}$Ho and $^{162}$Er($p,\gamma$)$^{163}$Tm at proton energies of 3-9 MeV, then used the optimized model to calculate stellar reaction rates for $^{157,159}$Ho($\gamma$, $p$) and $^{163,165}$Tm($\gamma$, $p$). These rates were applied to a carbon-deflagration SNe Ia nucleosynthesis model with peak temperatures $T_{9\mathrm{p}} = [2, 2.9]$ and peak density $\rho_{\mathrm{p}} = 10^6$ g cm$^{-3}$.

## Key numbers

- The reduced chi-square minimum for the optimized model fit to both experimental reactions is $R^2 = 9.3$.
- Varying the OMP, NLD, and GSF changes chi-square values by factors of up to 4, 17, and 11, respectively.
- For $^{157}$Ho($\gamma$, $p$)$^{156}$Dy at $T=2-3$ GK, the ratio of the present rates to JINA rates is 0.3-2.0.
- For $^{159}$Ho($\gamma$, $p$)$^{158}$Dy at $T=2-3$ GK, the ratio of the present rates to JINA rates is 0.1-0.4.
- For $^{163}$Tm($\gamma$, $p$)$^{162}$Er at $T_9=2-3$, the present rates are larger than JINA by factors of 0.5-20.8.
- For $^{165}$Tm($\gamma$, $p$)$^{164}$Er at $T_9=2-3$, the present rates are larger than JINA by factors of 0.6-25.7.
- The changes in final abundance of $p$-nuclei in the $A\sim160$ mass range are -5.5% to 3% compared to JINA rates.
- The constrained asymptotic level-density parameter $\widetilde{a}$ values are 17.14 for $^{160}$Dy, 17.14 for $^{160}$Ho, and 17.23 for $^{161}$Ho, with damping parameter $\gamma = 0.072$ for all three.

## Context

This work extends previous $\gamma$-process nucleosynthesis studies in the $A\sim160$ mass region, where solar abundances of $p$-nuclei such as $^{152}$Gd, $^{156,158}$Dy, and $^{162,164}$Er are severely underproduced in both core-collapse supernovae and thermonuclear supernovae models (Rauscher 2013). The authors build on experimental proton capture data for $^{160}$Dy (Cheng 2021) and $^{162}$Er (2017) to constrain the Hauser-Feshbach model, enabling improved reaction rates for isotopes where experimental data are unavailable due to low cross sections (0.3 nb to 0.4 mb) and low isotopic fractions (0.06% to 1.60%). The results enable more reliable $\gamma$-process network calculations for SNe Ia, particularly for the low-temperature layer not included in the previous study by Kusakabe (2010).
