# Anomalous current from covariant Wigner functions

> We consider accelerated and rotating media of weakly interacting fermions in local thermodynamic equilibrium. Kinetic properties of this media are described by covariant Wigner function calculated on the basis of relativistic distribution function of particles with spin. We obtain the formulae for axial current beyond the approximation of smallness of thermal vorticity tensor and chemical potential and calculate its divergence. In the massless limit higher order terms in vorticity and chemical potential compensate each other with only the low-order contributions surviving. It is shown, that axial current gets a topological component along the 4-acceleration vector. The similarity between different approaches to baryon polarisation is established.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytic formulae for the axial current in accelerated and rotating fermionic media, showing that beyond the usual small-vorticity approximation, higher-order terms in vorticity and chemical potential compensate each other in the massless limit, leaving only low-order contributions. The axial current acquires a topological component along the 4-acceleration vector, which contributes to its divergence.

## Method

The authors use covariant Wigner functions constructed from relativistic distribution functions for particles with spin in local thermodynamic equilibrium, following the approach of Becattini (2013). They compute the axial current by evaluating traces of the distribution functions with Dirac matrices and integrating over momentum space, without expanding in powers of the thermal vorticity tensor. For the massless limit, the integrals are evaluated analytically using polylogarithmic functions and their identities.

## Key numbers

- Axial current in massless limit (global thermodynamic equilibrium): \(\langle :j_{\mu}^{5}:\rangle = \left(\frac{1}{6}\left[T^2+\frac{a^{2} -\omega^2}{4\pi^2}\right]+\frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2}\right)\omega_{\mu}+\frac{1}{12\pi^2}(\omega\cdot a)\,a_{\mu}\)
- Divergence of axial current in global thermodynamic equilibrium: \(\partial^{\mu}\langle :j_{\mu}^{5}:\rangle=\frac{1}{6\pi^2}(\omega\cdot a)(a^2 +\omega^2)\)
- Topological component of axial current: \(\langle :j_{\mu}^{5}:\rangle_{\mathrm{top}} = \frac{1}{12\pi^2}(\omega\cdot a)\,a_{\mu}\)
- Vortical term independent of temperature and chemical potential: \(\langle :j_{\mu}^{5}:\rangle_{\mathrm{vort}}= \frac{a^{2} -\omega^2}{24\pi^2}\omega_{\mu}\)
- Thermal vortical current: \(\langle :j_{\mu}^{5}:\rangle_{\mathrm{Tvort}} =\left(\frac{T^2}{6}+\frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2}\right)\omega_{\mu}\)

## Context

This work extends the kinetic approach of Becattini (2013) and Buzzegoli (2017) by providing exact analytic expressions for the axial current beyond the linear vorticity approximation, and establishes the equivalence between the kinetic approach and effective field theory calculations of the chiral vortical effect by Sadofyev (2010) and Rogachevsky (2010). The results enable a direct comparison between different approaches to baryon polarization in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for experiments at NICA, and show that the numerical similarity found by Baznat (2017) between thermodynamic and anomalous-current approaches is not accidental.
