# Spectra and elliptic flow of thermal photons from full overlap U+U collisions at RHIC

> We calculate $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow for tip-tip and body-body configurations of full overlap uranium-uranium (U+U) collisions using a hydrodynamic model with smooth initial density distribution and compare the results with those obtained from Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Production of thermal photons is seen to be significantly larger for tip-tip collisions compared to body-body collisions of uranium nuclei in the region $p_T >$ 1 GeV. The thermal photon spectrum from body-body collisions is found to be close to the spectrum from most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The elliptic flow parameter calculated for body-body collisions is found to be large and comparable to the $v_2(p_T)$ for mid-central collisions of Au nuclei. On the other hand, as expected, the $v_2(p_T)$ is close to zero for tip-tip collisions. The qualitative nature of the photon spectra and elliptic flow for the two different orientations of uranium nuclei is found to be independent of the initial parameters of the model calculation. We show that the photon results from fully overlapping U+U collisions are complementary to the results from Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

Thermal photon production from full overlap U+U collisions depends strongly on the orientation of the deformed uranium nuclei, with tip-tip collisions producing significantly more high-\(p_T\) photons than body-body collisions; however, the results depend on the initial energy deposition values taken from prior work (Uli et al., 2015), which are yet to be constrained precisely from hadronic measurements.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1 dimensional longitudinally boost invariant hydrodynamic model (AZHYDRO) with smooth initial density distributions to simulate the evolution of the system produced in full overlap U+U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=193\) GeV at RHIC. Initial entropy densities at the center of the fireball were taken as 167 fm\(^{-3}\) for tip-tip and 110 fm\(^{-3}\) for body-body collisions, with an initial formation time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm; a lattice-based equation of state and a freeze-out temperature of 140 MeV were used. Thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow were calculated using next-to-leading order QGP rates from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) and hadronic phase rates from Turbet, Rapp, and Gale (2000).

## Key numbers

- Initial entropy density at the fireball center: 167 fm\(^{-3}\) for tip-tip and 110 fm\(^{-3}\) for body-body collisions, a difference of about 34%.
- Number of binary collisions: \(\sim 1870\) for tip-tip and \(\sim 1430\) for body-body collisions; number of participants is the same for both.
- Average temperature at initial time \(\tau_0\): \(\sim 350\) MeV for tip-tip collisions, about 6% larger than for body-body collisions.
- Thermal photon production from tip-tip collisions is about a factor of 2–5 times larger than from body-body collisions in the region \(2 < p_T < 4\) GeV.
- Initial spatial anisotropy \(\epsilon_{\rm in}\) for body-body collisions is about 0.26; for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter \(b = 5.4\) fm, \(\epsilon_{\rm in}\) is about 0.19.
- Prompt photon contribution is about 30% larger for tip-tip than for body-body collisions, consistent with the 30% larger \(N_{\rm coll}\).
- Direct photon spectrum for tip-tip is about a factor of 2 larger than for body-body in the range \(p_T < 5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic studies of U+U collisions at RHIC (Uli et al., 2015) by calculating thermal photon observables for the two limiting orientations of deformed uranium nuclei, complementing earlier photon studies in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (PHENIX, 2010; ALICE, 2013). The results show that photon spectra and elliptic flow from U+U collisions can provide additional constraints on the initial state and help address the photon \(v_2\) puzzle, where theory under-predicts experimental data. The authors note that a complete calculation using viscous hydrodynamics with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions would be valuable for a future study.
