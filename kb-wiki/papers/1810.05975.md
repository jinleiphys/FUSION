# Fusion Cross Section of $\rm T(d,n)^{4}He$ and $\rm ^{3}He(d,p)^{4}He$ Reactions by Four Parameters Formula

> Fusion cross sections of light nuclei are calculated by a complex potential and taking into account of conservation of angular momentum and parity. The nuclear potential is assumed to be as simple as a spherical complex square well with a rigid core. Then, the nuclear phase shift is extracted from continuity condition of inverse of the logarithmic derivative of the wave functions as a complex quantity. The quantum tunneling probability and cross section are obtained via real and complex components of nuclear phase shift. The obtained results for the two most important light nuclei reactions, $\rm T(d,n)^{4}He$, $\rm ^{3}He(d,p)^{4}He$ are compared with other theoretical formulas and experimental data. Despite that the theory is simplified as much as possible and the complexities and details of nuclear interactions has been ignored, excellent agreements with experimental data are achieved.

# Fusion Cross Section of T(d,n)⁴He and ³He(d,p)⁴He Reactions by Four Parameters Formula

## Key claim

The authors claim that a simplified four-parameter formula based on a spherical complex square well potential with a rigid core, incorporating conservation of angular momentum and parity, achieves excellent agreement with experimental fusion cross section data for T(d,n)⁴He and ³He(d,p)⁴He across the full energy ranges studied, despite ignoring the complexities and details of nuclear interactions. The hedge is that the four parameters (Vr, Vi, a, Rrc) must be refitted for different energy ranges because higher energies access more compound nucleus levels, affecting the absorption mechanism.

## Method

The authors model the nuclear potential as a spherical complex square well with a rigid core (infinite repulsive core at small radius), a constant complex potential in the nuclear interior, and a pure Coulomb potential outside. They solve the radial Schrödinger equation for each partial wave, match the inverse logarithmic derivatives of the nuclear and Coulomb wave functions at the nuclear surface to extract a complex nuclear phase shift, and compute the transmission probability and fusion cross section from the imaginary part of the phase shift. The cross section is summed over partial waves allowed by conservation of total angular momentum and parity for each accessible compound nucleus state, with statistical spin factors included.

## Key numbers

- For T(d,n)⁴He: the model is applied over the center-of-mass energy interval 0.12 ≤ ECM (keV) ≤ 170, with two energy regimes: 0.12–137 keV (ground state and first excited state of ⁵He accessible) and 137–170 keV (second excited state also accessible).
- For ³He(d,p)⁴He: the model is applied over the center-of-mass energy interval 0.54 ≤ ECM (keV) ≤ 850, with two energy regimes: 0.54–484 keV and 484–850 keV.
- The fitted real potential depth ranges from 38.5 < Vr (MeV) < 39.2, and the imaginary potential depth ranges from 83 < Vi (keV) < 187.2.
- The matching radius a ranges from 15.2 < a (fm) < 27.2, and the rigid core radius Rrc ranges from 1.2 < Rrc (fm) < 1.9.
- The nuclear radius is defined as RN = 1.24 (Ap^(1/3) + At^(1/3)), and the condition Rrc < RN < a is satisfied.
- For T(d,n)⁴He, the statistical factor for the ground state and second excited state (I^π = 3/2^- and 3/2^+) is g = 2/3, and for the first excited state (I^π = 1/2^-) is g = 1/3.
- For ³He(d,p)⁴He, the same statistical factors apply due to analogous spin-parity structure of ⁵Li.

## Context

This work extends the 3-parameter resonant tunneling formula previously developed by the same research group (Koohrokhi et al., earlier papers) by adding a rigid core and including higher partial waves with angular momentum and parity conservation. It is compared against the 5-parameter Duane formula (Duane, 1972) based on Breit-Wigner resonance theory and the 9-parameter Bosch-Hale formula (Bosch and Hale, 1992) based on R-matrix theory, which are widely used in plasma fusion research. The present 4-parameter formula aims to improve upon the 3-parameter formula's poor extrapolation to higher energies while maintaining good low-energy performance. The authors note that the parameter variation with energy suggests coupled-channels mechanisms (reference to a 2015 paper) may resolve this, and future work should include spin-orbit coupling and energy-dependent nuclear potentials.
