# Pion polarizabilities: No conflict between dispersion theory and ChPT

## Key claim

The apparent disagreement between chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions for pion polarizabilities and values obtained from dispersion relations by Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005) arises not from a genuine physical conflict but from the use of non-analytic amplitudes in the dispersion-relation approach; when the full analytic structure including unphysical cuts is properly accounted for, the dispersion-relation results become consistent with ChPT.

## Method

The authors examine the analytic properties of the specific forms used by Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005) for the absorptive part of Compton scattering amplitudes on pions, focusing on the vector-meson (rho and omega) contributions. They evaluate the dispersion integrals for the polarizabilities by separating contributions from the physical right-hand cut (two-pion production threshold at s=4m^2) and the unphysical left-hand cut arising from the 1/sqrt(s) factor introduced for convergence, and compare these to the direct evaluation of the real parts of the model amplitudes at the Compton threshold.

## Key numbers

- ChPT prediction at O(p^4): alpha_pi+ = 2.64 +/- 0.09 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- ChPT two-loop prediction: alpha_pi+ + beta_pi+ = 0.16 +/- 0.1 (10^-4 fm^3)
- ChPT two-loop prediction: alpha_pi+ - beta_pi+ = 5.7 +/- 1.0 (10^-4 fm^3)
- Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005) dispersion-relation result: alpha_pi+ - beta_pi+ = 13.60 +/- 2.15 (10^-4 fm^3)
- For the rho meson s-channel contribution, the left-cut contribution to alpha+beta is 0.17 and the right-cut contribution is 0.03, summing to +0.20 (10^-4 fm^3)
- For the rho meson s-channel contribution, the left-cut contribution to alpha-beta is 0.98 and the right-cut contribution is -1.18, summing to -0.20 (10^-4 fm^3)
- The ratio R = (alpha-beta)/(alpha+beta) from the full calculation is -1, consistent with a magnetic dipole transition, while the right-hand cut alone gives R approximately -40

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Pasquini 2008) of the analytic structure of dispersion-relation models for pion Compton scattering. It directly addresses the conflict between ChPT predictions (Gasser 2006) and the dispersion-relation results of Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2005), showing that the latter's neglect of unphysical cuts from 1/sqrt(s) factors leads to grossly overestimated vector-meson contributions and a spurious large negative electric polarizability. The resolution enables future reliable extractions of pion polarizabilities from experimental data using properly analytic dispersion relations, and reaffirms ChPT as the correct framework for these fundamental structure constants.
