# Sensitivity study of a sapphire detector using Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering process

> The Indian Coherent Neutrino-nucleus Scattering Experiment(ICNSE) has been proposed at Bhabha Atomic Research Centre in India to measure the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering process using electron antineutrinos produced from reactors. Phenomenological studies are performed to find out the sensitivity of a sapphire detector for various fundamental physics parameters at an exposure of one year. Reactors of different core compositions, sizes, and thermal powers have been considered as sources of electron antineutrinos. The potential of the ICNSE to measure the weak mixing angle at a low energy regime has been extracted. Furthermore, the detector's capability has been investigated for examining the electromagnetic properties of neutrinos, including their magnetic moment. Additionally, an exploration has been conducted on the detector's sensitivity in restricting new interactions between neutrinos and electrons or nuclei, thereby constraining the parameter space related to light mediators. It is found that the ICNSE detector can put a stronger constraints on the scalar and vector mediators masses.

# Sensitivity study of a sapphire detector using Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering process

## Key claim

The proposed ICNSE sapphire detector, assuming one year of exposure and a 100 eV energy threshold, can place stronger constraints on scalar and vector mediator masses than current limits, but only if the detector achieves the assumed background levels and systematic uncertainties (5% normalization uncertainty). The sensitivity to the weak mixing angle, neutrino magnetic moment, and new mediators is derived from a chi-squared analysis that assumes the measured event distribution matches the Standard Model prediction exactly, with any deviation treated as a signature of new physics.

## Method

The study uses a Monte-Carlo simulation to generate neutrino production points inside four different reactor cores (Apsara-U, Dhruva, PFBR, VVER) and interaction points in a 10 kg sapphire (Al2O3) cryogenic detector with a baseline recoil energy resolution of 18 eV and a threshold of 100 eV. The expected CEνNS event rate is calculated using the Huber-Muller model for antineutrino spectra above 2.0 MeV and data from TEXONO (2006) for lower energies, then a chi-squared statistic with pull parameters is minimized to extract sensitivity to the weak mixing angle, neutrino magnetic moment, and scalar/vector mediator masses and couplings.

## Key numbers

* Apsara-U reactor (3.0 MWth, 4 m distance) yields 0.42 counts/day/kg in a sapphire detector; Dhruva (100 MWth, 10 m) yields 3.25 counts/day/kg; PFBR (1250 MWth, 25 m) yields 5.26 counts/day/kg; VVER (3000 MWth, 30 m) yields 10.85 counts/day/kg; all assuming 100 eV threshold, 80% efficiency, 90% fiducial volume, 70% duty cycle, 1 day exposure.
* A baseline recoil energy resolution of 18 eV corresponding to a recoil energy threshold of 54 eV has been achieved with a 100 g sapphire detector (Verma 2022).
* The best current limit on the neutrino magnetic moment from reactor antineutrinos is 2.9 x 10^-11 μB at 90% C.L. (GEMMA experiment, Beda 2013); the BOREXINO solar neutrino limit is 2.8 x 10^-11 μB at 90% C.L. (Borexino 2017).
* The weak mixing angle sin^2θW is taken as 0.23867 ± 0.00016 (Erler 2004).
* Systematic uncertainties considered: total neutrino flux, number of target atoms, and detector efficiency (5%); energy calibration (1%); nonlinear energy response (1%).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological study of Behera (2023) by evaluating the ICNSE detector sensitivity across multiple reactor types and physics channels, building on the COHERENT collaboration's first CEνNS measurements on CsI (COHERENT 2017, 6.7σ) and germanium (COHERENT 2024, 3.9σ). The study enables future experimental comparisons between reactor-based CEνNS constraints on the weak mixing angle at low momentum transfer (complementing high-energy measurements from CHARM-II, 1994) and on neutrino magnetic moment (improving upon limits from GEMMA, Beda 2013, and BOREXINO, 2017). The analysis also provides a framework for constraining light scalar and vector mediator models (Farzan 2018, Cerdeno 2016) that could be tested by the planned ICNSE setup at the Apsara-U reactor.
