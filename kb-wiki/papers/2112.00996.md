# Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb with KIDS density functional

> Neutron skin thickness ($R_{np}$) of the atomic nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb are considered in the KIDS (Korea:IBS-Daegu-Sungkyunkwan) density functional formalism. Model parameters most relevant to $R_{np}$ are constrained from standard nuclear data and neutron star properties determined from modern observations. Mean values of $R_{np}$ are obtained as 0.161, 0.220 and 0.160 fm for $^{48}$Ca, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb, respectively, and the corresponding standard deviations are 0.011, 0.020 and 0.021 fm. Correlations between $R_{np}$ of the three nuclei are considered, and we find that $R_{np}$'s are correlated very strongly. We obtain correlation coefficient 0.978 between $R_{np}$ of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, and 0.996 between $R_{np}$ of $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb.

# Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb with KIDS density functional

## Key claim

The KIDS density functional formalism, constrained by both standard nuclear data and neutron star radius observations, predicts mean neutron skin thickness values of 0.161 fm for $^{48}$Ca, 0.220 fm for $^{132}$Sn, and 0.160 fm for $^{208}$Pb, with the surprising result that applying the neutron star radius constraint does not significantly change the mean values or standard deviations compared to the nuclear-data-only constraint, despite the known role of the symmetry energy slope $L$ in determining both quantities.

## Method

The author constructed 4410 initial energy density functionals (EDFs) by surveying the parameter space of nuclear matter EoS parameters ($K_0$, $J$, $L$, $K_\tau$) over ranges from the literature. Each EDF was transformed to a Skyrme-type force, with the two additional constants (coefficients of $\nabla^2$ and spin-orbit terms) fit to 13 nuclear data points (energies and radii of $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{132}$Sn, $^{208}$Pb, and the energy of $^{218}$U). Models with average deviation per datum (ADPD) less than 0.3% were retained (358 models, called ADPD03 set), and then further filtered by requiring the radius of a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star to lie between 11.80 and 12.50 km (158 models, called R14 set). Neutron skin thicknesses were calculated for all models in both sets.

## Key numbers

- Mean neutron skin thickness $R_{np}$ for $^{48}$Ca: 0.161 fm (R14 set), standard deviation 0.011 fm.
- Mean neutron skin thickness $R_{np}$ for $^{132}$Sn: 0.220 fm (R14 set), standard deviation 0.020 fm.
- Mean neutron skin thickness $R_{np}$ for $^{208}$Pb: 0.160 fm (R14 set), standard deviation 0.021 fm.
- Correlation coefficient between $R_{np}$ of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb: 0.978 (R14 set).
- Correlation coefficient between $R_{np}$ of $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb: 0.996 (R14 set).
- Mean symmetry energy slope $L$: 49.8 MeV (R14 set), standard deviation 5.2 MeV, reduced from 54.7 +/- 9.7 MeV in the ADPD03 set.
- Mean symmetry energy curvature $K_{\rm sym}$: -82.4 MeV (R14 set), standard deviation 33.7 MeV, reduced from -52.4 +/- 72.0 MeV in the ADPD03 set.
- Correlation coefficient of $L$ with $R_{1.4}$: 0.79 (eR14 set).
- Correlation coefficient of $K_{\rm sym}$ with $R_{1.4}$: 0.90 (eR14 set).
- Correlation coefficient of $R^{208}_{np}$ with $R_{1.4}$: 0.09 (eR14 set), indicating negligible influence of the neutron star radius constraint on the neutron skin prediction.

## Context

This work extends the KIDS density functional formalism previously developed by the author and collaborators, and directly builds on the parameter-space survey of Hyun (2020) which defined the ADPD03 and R14 model sets. The results are compared to experimental measurements: the PREX-II result for $^{208}$Pb (0.212-0.354 fm) does not overlap with the KIDS-R14 mean of 0.160 fm, while the electric dipole polarizability measurement at RCNP (0.135-0.181 fm) is consistent. For $^{48}$Ca, the results agree with the RIKEN scattering measurement (0.146 +/- 0.048 fm) and the RCNP polarizability result (0.14-0.20 fm). The strong correlations between neutron skin thicknesses of different nuclei confirm earlier findings by Roca-Maza et al. (2012) and Tagami et al. (2020), and the linear approximations provided enable predictions for $^{48}$Ca and $^{132}$Sn based on future $^{208}$Pb measurements. The upcoming CREX result for $^{48}$Ca and R3B measurement for $^{132}$Sn will provide critical tests of the KIDS predictions.
