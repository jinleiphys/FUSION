# A dynamical $α$-cluster model of $^{16}$O

> We calculate the low-lying spectrum of the $^{16}$O nucleus using an $α$-cluster model which includes the important tetrahedral and square configurations. Our approach is motivated by the dynamics of $α$-particle scattering in the Skyrme model. We are able to replicate the large energy splitting that is observed between states of identical spin but opposite parities, as well as introduce states that were previously not found in other cluster models, such as a $0^-$ state. We also provide a novel interpretation of the first excited state of $^{16}$O and make predictions for the energies of $6^-$ states that have yet to be observed experimentally.

# A dynamical α-cluster model of ¹⁶O

## Key claim

The paper claims that a global α-cluster model including both tetrahedral and square configurations, with dynamics motivated by the Skyrme model, can replicate the large energy splitting between states of identical spin but opposite parities and introduce a 0⁻ state not previously found in other cluster models; however, the calculated 0⁻ state energy (16.35 MeV) is significantly higher than the experimentally observed 0⁻ state (11.0 MeV), and the authors note this discrepancy may be reduced by using a more realistic potential that flattens at large separation.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-dimensional vibrational space of α-particle configurations with D₂ symmetry, connecting tetrahedral and square arrangements via scattering modes derived from the Skyrme model. They quantised the system by solving a Schrödinger equation on a 6-punctured sphere with constant negative curvature, using a separable potential with two free parameters (ω and μ), and combined vibrational wavefunctions with rigid-body rotational states subject to S₃ symmetry constraints. The energy spectrum was computed using first-order perturbation theory to include the dependence of moments of inertia on vibrational coordinates, with the ground state fixed at 0 MeV and the excited 0⁺ and lowest 4⁺ states used to scale vibrational and rotational energy units respectively.

## Key numbers

- The energy gap between the lowest 2⁺ and 2⁻ states is predicted to be 1.8 MeV, close to the experimentally observed gap of 1.96 MeV.
- The 0⁻ state is calculated at 16.35 MeV, compared to the lowest experimentally observed 0⁻ state at 11.0 MeV.
- A 6⁺ state is found at 21.7 MeV, agreeing with an experimentally observed state at 21.6 MeV.
- Two 6⁻ states are predicted: one at 22.2 MeV from the sign representation and one at 27.1 MeV from the standard representation.
- The calculated spectrum is presented up to 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the local harmonic approximation of the tetrahedral E vibration studied by the authors of TetVib (2015), which produced a degeneracy between states of equal spin but opposite parity not seen experimentally. It also goes beyond the rigid-body analysis of α-cluster models by the authors of alpha (2007). The Skyrme model dynamics, previously shown to reproduce α-particle clustering by the authors of B4build (2013) and Hoyle (2014), provide the foundation for the vibrational space. The results enable predictions for unobserved 6⁻ states and suggest a path toward including additional vibrational modes (F vibration and breather mode) and electromagnetic transitions, extending the work of EMtrans (2016).
