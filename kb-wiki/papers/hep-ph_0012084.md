# Hypernuclear potentials and the pseudoscalar meson exchange contribution

> The pieces of the hypernuclear strangeness violating potential due to the pseudoscalar meson exchanges are derived using methods which were successfully applied to hyperon nonleptonic decays. The estimates are tested by comparison with measured hyperon nonleptonic decay amplitudes. All isospin changes $ΔI=1/2$ and $ΔI=3/2$ are included in the derived potential. All calculational methods used are reviewed and described in detail.

## Key claim

The paper derives the strangeness-violating hypernuclear potential arising from pseudoscalar meson (pi, K, eta) exchange using methods previously applied to hyperon nonleptonic decays, but the derived potential is tested only by comparison with measured hyperon nonleptonic decay amplitudes rather than directly against hypernuclear decay data, and the full calculation of hypernuclear decay widths is deferred to a subsequent paper.

## Method

The authors employed a one-pseudoscalar-meson-exchange mechanism to construct an effective baryon-baryon (hyperon) potential for the weak Lambda N to N N transition. They applied the same calculational techniques used in hyperon nonleptonic decay studies, including both Delta I = 1/2 and Delta I = 3/2 isospin changes. The potential pieces were derived for pion, kaon, and eta exchanges, and the estimates were validated by comparing with measured hyperon nonleptonic decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The Lambda mass excess of 176 MeV is converted into kinetic energy of the final state nucleon in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay channels.
* Hypernuclei heavier than 5_Lambda He decay mainly through nonmesonic channels.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonmesonic hypernuclear decays, where the Lambda mass excess of 176 MeV is converted into nucleon kinetic energy, suggesting that heavier meson exchanges could play a significant role. The paper focuses on pseudoscalar meson exchanges (pi, K, eta) as a first step, with vector and axial-vector meson exchanges to be treated in a following paper (Barbero et al., in preparation). The calculation of hypernuclear decay widths Gamma, which requires detailed nuclear state descriptions, is also deferred to a subsequent work (Barbero et al., in preparation). The experimental hypernuclear programs at BNL, KEK, CEBAF, and DUBNA are cited as providing data for future exhaustive hypernuclear studies.
