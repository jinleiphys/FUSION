# Polarized light-antiquark distributions in a meson-cloud model

> Flavor asymmetry is investigated in polarized light-antiquark distributions by a meson-cloud model. In particular, rho meson contributions to Delta u-bar - Delta d-bar are calculated. We point out that the g_2 part of rho contributes to the structure function g_1 of the proton in addition to the ordinary longitudinally polarized distributions in rho. This kind of contribution becomes important at medium x (>0.2) with small Q^2 (~1 GeV^2). Including N->rho N and N->rho Delta splitting processes, we obtain the polarized rho effects on the light-antiquark flavor asymmetry in the proton. The results show Delta d-bar excess over Delta u-bar, which is very different from some theoretical predictions. Our model could be tested by experiments in the near future.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the polarized light-antiquark flavor asymmetry Δū − Δd̅ in the proton receives contributions not only from the ordinary longitudinally polarized ρ meson distributions but also from the g₂ part of the ρ meson, and this g₂ contribution becomes important at medium x (>0.2) with small Q² (~1 GeV²), leading to a Δd̅ excess over Δū that differs from some other theoretical predictions.

## Method

The authors use a meson-cloud model in which the proton fluctuates into a virtual ρ meson plus a baryon (either nucleon or Δ), and the polarized structure functions of the nucleon are expressed as convolutions of meson momentum distributions with the ρ meson's polarized parton distributions. The meson momentum distributions are calculated using time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) with the VNN and VNΔ interaction vertices, including both longitudinal and transverse polarization contributions as well as the g₂ part of the ρ meson approximated by the Wandzura-Wilczek relation.

## Key numbers

- The g₂ contribution becomes important at medium x > 0.2 with small Q² ~ 1 GeV²
- The momentum distributions are calculated at Q² = 1 GeV² and Q² = 2 GeV² with x = 0.2
- Coupling constants used: g_V²/(4π) = 0.84, f_V = 6.1 g_V, and f_VNΔ²/(4π) = 20.45
- The ρNN process yields positive Δf₁L(y) and Δf₂L(y) distributions, while the ρNΔ process yields mainly negative distributions

## Context

This work extends the meson-cloud model studies of polarized antiquark flavor asymmetry, specifically building on the ρ-meson contributions calculated by Fries and Schäfer (2000) by including the previously neglected g₂ part of the ρ meson. The authors note that a meson-cloud model and a chiral-soliton model predict totally different contributions to Δū − Δd̅, although their results are similar for the unpolarized distribution ū − d̅. The predictions could be tested by the RHIC and COMPASS experiments, which were expected to clarify the details of polarized antiquark distributions.
