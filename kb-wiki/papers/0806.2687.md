# Forward Production of Protons and Pions in Heavy-ion Collisions

> The problem of forward production of hadrons in heavy-ion collision at RHIC is revisited with modification of the theoretical treatment on the one hand and with the use of new data on the other. The basic formalism for hadronization remains the same as before, namely, recombination, but the details of momentum degradation and quark regeneration are improved. Recent data on the p/pi and pbar/p ratios are used to constrain the value of the degradation parameter. The p_T spectrum of the average charged particles is well reproduced. A prediction on the p_T dependence of the pbar/p ratio at eta=3.2 is made.

# Forward Production of Protons and Pions in Heavy-ion Collisions

## Key claim

The recombination model, with improved treatment of momentum degradation and quark regeneration, can reproduce the large proton-to-pion ratio (~4 at p_T = 1.1 GeV/c) observed in forward production at RHIC, provided the degradation parameter κ is constrained by both the p/π and anti-p/p ratios simultaneously. The authors claim that the large p/π ratio would not emerge if recombination were not the hadronization mechanism, but the result depends on the specific choice of κ ≈ 0.67 determined from fitting these two particle ratios.

## Method

The authors use the recombination model for hadronization, where protons form from three valence quarks (uud) and pions from a quark-antiquark pair, with the parton distributions derived from a valon model that includes momentum degradation (parameterized by a multiplicative factor κ per collision) and soft-parton regeneration from momentum loss. The quark distributions after ν collisions are computed by summing over all possible distributions of collisions among the three valons in a nucleon, with regenerated sea quarks described by a simple (1-x)^n form normalized to the total momentum loss. The hadron x-distributions are then combined with exponential transverse momentum distributions having mass-dependent slopes to compare with BRAHMS and PHOBOS data at η = 3.2.

## Key numbers

- p/π ratio = 4.08 ± 0.2 at η = 3.2 ± 0.2, 0.9 < p_T < 1.3 GeV/c, 0-10% centrality in Au-Au collisions at √s = 62.4 GeV
- anti-p/p ratio = 0.0231 ± 0.0012 at y = 3.0 ± 0.1, 0.5 < p_T < 1.4 GeV/c
- Degradation parameter κ ≈ 0.67 (from grid-line analysis of particle ratios)
- Transverse slope parameter T_0 = 0.2 GeV/c (from fitting charged particle p_T spectrum)
- Transverse flow velocity squared ⟨v_t⟩² = 0.028 (⟨v_t⟩ ≈ 0.17)
- The x-distributions are calculated for κ = 0.7 and b = 3.3 fm for 0-10% centrality

## Context

This work extends the recombination model of Hwa and collaborators (Hwa 1980, Hwa and Yang 2003, Hwa and Yang 2004) for forward hadron production, which previously predicted a p/π ratio about 3 times lower than the new preliminary BRAHMS data (Staszel 2008). The authors improve upon their earlier treatment (Hwa and Yang 2004, Hwa and Yang 2005) by modifying how momentum degradation is distributed among valons and by using a simpler, sum-rule-based form for regenerated sea quarks rather than a convolution approach. The new data on both p/π and anti-p/p ratios (Arsene 2008) provide simultaneous constraints that were not available previously. The work enables predictions for the p_T dependence of the anti-p/p ratio at η = 3.2, which can be tested by future BRAHMS data, and suggests connections to intermediate-p_T hadronization at LHC.
