# Neutron star mass limit at $2M_\odot$ supports the existence of a CEP

> We point out that the very existence of a "horizontal branch" in the mass-radius characteristics for neutron stars indicates a strong first-order phase transition and thus supports the existence of a critical endpoint (CEP) of first order phase transitions in the QCD phase diagram. This branch would sample a sequence of hybrid stars with quark matter core, leading to the endpoint of stable compact star configurations with the highest possible baryon densities. Since we know of the existence of compact stars with $2M_\odot$, this hypothetical branch has to lie in the vicinity of this mass value, if it exists. We report here a correlation between the maximal radius of the horizontal branch and the pressure at the onset of hadron-to-quark matter phase transition which is likely to be a universal quantity of utmost relevance to the upcoming experiments with heavy-ion collisions at NICA and FAIR.

# Neutron star mass limit at $2M_\odot$ supports the existence of a CEP

## Key claim

The existence of a "horizontal branch" in the mass-radius diagram for neutron stars, if confirmed observationally, would indicate a strong first-order phase transition in cold dense matter and thereby support the existence of a critical endpoint (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram. This conclusion depends on the condition that the horizontal branch lies near the observed $2M_\odot$ mass limit and that the phase transition satisfies $\Delta\varepsilon/\varepsilon_{\rm trans} > 0.6$, where $\varepsilon_{\rm trans}$ is the critical energy density and $\Delta\varepsilon$ the latent heat.

## Method

The authors review and compare several hybrid equation-of-state (EoS) models that produce high-mass twin star solutions: the ZHAHP constant-speed-of-sound scheme, a bag model with color-flavor-locked (CFL) superconductivity, an NJL model with multiquark interactions, a nonlocal PNJL model, and a density-functional string-flip model. For each model, the hadronic phase (typically the DD2 model with excluded volume corrections) is combined with a quark matter phase via a Maxwell construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius sequences. The authors then correlate the transition pressure $p_{\rm trans}$ at the onset of deconfinement with the maximal radius of the horizontal branch, and compare these values to the universal transition pressure extracted from heavy-ion collision data.

## Key numbers

- The condition for a disconnected (third family) branch is $\Delta\varepsilon/\varepsilon_{\rm trans} > 0.6$, where $\Delta\varepsilon$ is the latent heat and $\varepsilon_{\rm trans}$ the critical energy density at the phase transition.
- For the CFL bag model, a $2M_\odot$ neutron star is realized with parameters $\Delta = 50$ MeV and $B^{1/4} = 218$ MeV.
- For the NJL model with multiquark interactions, high-mass twins are found for $\eta_2 = 0.08$, $\eta_4 = 5,6,7,8,9$, and excluded volume parameters $v = 6-8$ fm$^3$.
- The universal transition pressure from heavy-ion collision statistical model analysis is $p_{\rm trans} = 82 \pm 8$ MeV/fm$^3$ (Petran and Rafelski, 2013-2014).
- A high-mass twin model with $p_{\rm trans} = 80$ MeV/fm$^3$ corresponds to a neutron star radius of 13.8 km.
- For the DD2-MEV vs. hNJL hybrid EoS, the relative difference in transition pressure between symmetric matter and beta-equilibrated neutron star matter is at the level of 15% (ranging from 13.49% to 15.68% for the parameter sets shown).
- The fit formula relating radius at $2M_\odot$ to transition pressure is $R/(10\ {\rm km}) = 1.495 - 0.0021(p_{\rm trans}/({\rm MeV/fm}^3) - 22) + (p_{\rm trans}/({\rm MeV/fm}^3) - 31)^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on hybrid neutron stars and the QCD phase diagram initiated by Alford et al. (2013) on the classification of stable hybrid star branches, and by Alvarez-Castillo et al. (2013, 2015) on the connection between mass twins and the critical endpoint. It contrasts with approaches predicting a continuous quark-hadron transition, such as the three-window approach of Kojo et al. (2015) and the quark-hadron continuity arguments of Schafer and Wilczek (1998) and Hatsuda et al. (2006). The paper enables future experimental searches for the CEP at NICA and FAIR by providing a quantitative correlation between neutron star observables (maximal radius of the horizontal branch) and the transition pressure that can be measured in heavy-ion collisions.
