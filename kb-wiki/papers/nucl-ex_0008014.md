# Threshold Reaction Rates Inside and on the Surface of Thick W-Na Target Irradiated with 0.8 GeV Protons

> The preliminary results are presented of the experimental determination of threshold reaction rates in experimental samples made of Al, Co, Bi, In, Au, to name but a few, placed both inside and on the surface of extended thick W-Na target irradiated with 0.8 GeV protons. The target consists of 26 alternating discs each 150 mm in diameter: 6 tungsten discs are 20 mm thick, 7 tungsten discs are 40 mm thick, and 13 sodium discs are 40 mm thick. The relative position of discs is matched with the aim of flattening the neutron field along the target surface. The comparison is made of the measured rates with results of their simulation using the LAHET and KASKAD-S codes, and the ENDF/B6, MENDL2, MENDL2P, SADKO-2, and ABBN-93 databases. The results are of interest both in terms of integral data collection and to test the up-to-the-date predictive power of the codes applied in designing of hybrid Accelerator Driven System (ADS) systems that use tungsten targets cooled with sodium.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary experimental measurements of threshold reaction rates in a thick W-Na target irradiated with 0.8 GeV protons, but only results for two reactions (24Na and 27Mg production from Al) are reported; the full set of reaction rates listed in the experimental plan is still being processed and will be determined after completion of gamma-spectra analysis.

## Method

A micromodel of an ADS target was constructed from 26 alternating tungsten and sodium discs (150 mm diameter, total thickness 900 mm) with disc sequence chosen to flatten the neutron field. Experimental foil samples (Al, Co, In, Au, Bi, Cu, Nb, Zn, F, C, Ta, Tm) were placed both inside the target and on its surface and irradiated with 0.8 GeV protons at the ITEP synchrotron for 10 hours at an average intensity of 4.8×10^10 p/cm^2 per pulse. After irradiation, gamma spectra were measured and reaction rates were determined using the PCNUDAT decay database and GENIE2000 code, then compared with simulations using the LAHET and KASKAD-S codes with multiple nuclear data libraries.

## Key numbers

- Target total thickness: 900 mm (380 mm tungsten, 520 mm sodium)
- Tungsten disc composition: 97.5% W, 1.75% Ni, 0.75% Fe, with average density 18.6 g/cm^3
- Proton beam energy: 0.8 GeV; irradiation duration: 10 hours; average intensity: 4.8×10^10 p/cm^2 per pulse; pulse repetition rate: 15 pulses per minute
- For 27Al(n,p)27Mg reaction on the target surface, LAHET predictions are systematically overestimated by a factor of 1.6 on average compared to measured values
- For 27Al(n,p)27Mg reaction on the target surface, KASKAD-S predictions are systematically underestimated by a factor of 1.6 on average compared to measured values

## Context

This work extends the experimental database for neutron and proton reaction rates in thick targets relevant to Accelerator Driven System (ADS) design, specifically for tungsten targets cooled with sodium as considered in several projects. The results provide integral data for testing the predictive power of the LAHET and KASKAD-S codes and the MENDL2, MENDL2P, ENDF/B6, SADKO-2, and ABBN-93 nuclear data libraries. The complete set of measured reaction rates, once processed, will enable systematic evaluation of code accuracy for designing the target region of ADS facilities.
