# Low-energy theorems of QCD and bulk viscosity at finite temperature and baryon density in a magnetic field

> The nonperturbative QCD vacuum at finite temperature and a finite baryon density in an external magnetic field is studied. Equations relating nonperturbative condensates to the thermodynamic pressure for $T\neq 0$, $μ_q \neq 0$ and $H\neq 0$ are obtained, and low-energy theorems are derived. A bulk viscosity $ζ(T, μ, H)$ is expressed in terms of basic thermodynamical quantities describing the quark-gluon matter at $T\neq 0$, $μ_q \neq 0$, and $H\neq 0$. Various limiting cases are also considered.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general expression for the bulk viscosity of quark-gluon matter at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and external magnetic field, linking it to thermodynamic quantities such as entropy density, sound velocity, and magnetic susceptibility. The derivation relies on low-energy theorems of QCD and the one-loop approximation for the beta function, with the caveat that electromagnetic corrections proportional to e^4 are neglected by choosing an ultraviolet cutoff scale much larger than the magnetic field strength.

## Method

The author starts from the QCD partition function in Euclidean formulation with an external Abelian magnetic field, then uses renormalization group invariance to relate the gluon condensate to derivatives of the thermodynamic pressure with respect to temperature, chemical potential, magnetic field, and quark masses. Low-energy theorems (Ward identities) are derived by applying the operator (T ∂/∂T + Σ μ_q ∂/∂μ_q + 2H ∂/∂H - d) to condensates, yielding relations between n-point correlation functions of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. The bulk viscosity is then expressed via a Kubo formula as the static limit of a bilocal correlator of the energy-momentum tensor trace, which is evaluated using the low-energy theorems and expressed in terms of thermodynamic quantities.

## Key numbers

- The normal nuclear density is n_0 ≈ 0.17 fm^{-3}, used as a reference for high baryon density conditions.
- The QCD scale for high temperature is T ∼ 200 MeV, above which quark-gluon plasma formation is expected.
- Magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions can reach eH ∼ 10 to 10^4 MeV^2, as cited from prior work.
- The vacuum energy density is given by ε_vac = -(b/(128π^2)) ⟨G^2⟩_0 + (1/4) Σ_q m_q ⟨q̄q⟩_0, where b = (11N_c - 2N_f)/3 in the one-loop approximation.
- The vacuum contribution to the bilocal correlator Π_0 is -16 ε_vac - 2 Σ_q m_q ⟨q̄q⟩_0, which for T=μ=H=0 becomes 16|ε^g_vac| + 6(F_π^2 M_π^2 + F_K^2 M_K^2) using the PCAC relation.
- The bulk viscosity ζ(T, μ, H) satisfies 9ζ ω_0 = Π, where ω_0 is a scale parameter determining the onset of perturbative behavior.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorems of QCD, originally developed for the vacuum by Novikov et al. (1980s) and later generalized to finite temperature and chemical potential by Ellis (1998) and Shushpanov (1998), to include an external magnetic field. It builds directly on the approach of Kharzeev (2007) and Karsch (2007), who used Kubo formulas and low-energy theorems to evaluate bulk viscosity in hot QCD. The derived formula for ζ(T, μ, H) enables future studies of the quark-hadron phase transition in heavy-ion collisions and the early Universe, where strong magnetic fields are present, and predicts a jump in bulk viscosity at the transition due to the sign change of magnetic susceptibility between diamagnetic hadron matter and paramagnetic quark-gluon plasma.
