# Insights into the equation of state of neutron-rich matter since GW170817

> The historical detection of gravitational waves emitted from the binary neutron star merger GW170817 has opened the new era of multi-messenger astronomy. Since then, many other significant discoveries -- both on heaven and earth -- are providing new clues into the behavior of neutron-rich matter. It is the goal of this article to illustrate how the remarkable progress made during the last few years is spearheading the field into the golden age of neutron-star physics.

# Insights into the equation of state of neutron-rich matter since GW170817

## Key claim
The combined constraints from gravitational-wave detections, NICER mass-radius measurements, and the PREX neutron skin thickness of 208Pb suggest a stiff equation of state near nuclear saturation density that must soften at intermediate densities to accommodate the revised tidal deformability limit from GW170817, yet stiffen again at the highest densities to support ~2 solar mass neutron stars. The author notes that if the revised lower limit on tidal deformability (Λ1.4 ≲ 580) is confirmed, it would create "serious tension" with the PREX result and may suggest the onset of a phase transition.

## Method
The paper reviews and synthesizes multiple observational and experimental constraints on the neutron-star equation of state: Shapiro delay measurements of neutron star masses from pulsar timing, pulse profile modeling from NICER to determine simultaneous mass and radius, parity-violating elastic electron scattering (PREX) to extract the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb, and gravitational-wave analysis of the tidal deformability from GW170817. These constraints are compared against predictions from a collection of accurately calibrated covariant energy density functionals that predict both finite-nuclei properties and neutron-star structure within a single theoretical framework.

## Key numbers
- Mass of pulsar J1614-2230: M = 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ from Shapiro delay (Demorest 2010)
- Mass of millisecond pulsar J0740+6620: M = 2.1+0.10−0.09 M⊙, later refined to M = 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙ (Cromartie 2019, Fonseca 2021)
- NICER mass-radius for PSR J0030+0451: M = 1.34+0.15−0.16 M⊙ and R = 12.71+1.14−1.19 km (Riley 2019); M = 1.44+0.15−0.14 M⊙ and R = 13.02+1.24−1.06 km (Miller 2019)
- NICER mass-radius for PSR J0740+6620: M = 2.072+0.067−0.066 M⊙ and R = 12.39+1.30−0.98 km (Riley 2021)
- Combined NICER+LIGO radius for 1.40 M⊙ neutron star: R = 12.45 ± 0.65 km (Miller 2021)
- Combined NICER+LIGO radius for 2.08 M⊙ neutron star: R = 12.35 ± 0.75 km (Miller 2021)
- PREX neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: Rskin = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm (Adhikari 2021)
- Combined constraints yield: 0.21 ≲ Rskin(fm) ≲ 0.31; 13.25 ≲ R⋆1.4(km) ≲ 14.26; 642 ≲ Λ⋆1.4 ≲ 955 (Reed 2021)
- GW170817 discovery paper limit: Λ⋆1.4 ≲ 800 (Abbott 2017); revised limit: Λ1.4 ≲ 580 (Abbott 2018)
- Bauswein (2017) lower limit on radius of 1.6 M⊙ neutron star combined with upper limits yields 10.6-13.3 km interval

## Context
This review synthesizes progress since the GW170817 detection (Abbott 2017) and builds on the "EOS ladder" concept analogous to the cosmic distance ladder in cosmology. It extends earlier work on neutron-star structure by connecting laboratory measurements of neutron skins (PREX) to astrophysical constraints from NICER and LIGO, following the framework of energy density functionals that simultaneously describe finite nuclei and neutron stars (Yang 2019). The paper highlights that before GW170817, only two mass measurements existed for ~2 M⊙ neutron stars (Demorest 2010 for J1614, Antoniadis 2013 for J0348), whereas now simultaneous mass-radius determinations and tidal deformability constraints are available. The tension between the PREX result (stiff EOS near saturation) and the revised GW170817 tidal deformability limit (softer EOS at intermediate densities) suggests possible phase transitions and motivates further theoretical and experimental work to resolve the discrepancy.
