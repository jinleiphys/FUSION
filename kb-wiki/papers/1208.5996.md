# Scaling of elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions

> The common interpretation of $v_2$ in heavy ion collisions is that it is produced by hydrodynamic flow at low transverse momentum and by parton energy loss at high transverse momentum. In this talk we discuss this interpretation in view of the dependence of $v_2$ with energy, rapidity and system size, and show that it might not be trivial to reconcile these models with the relatively simple scaling found in experiment

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed scaling of elliptic flow ($v_2$) across different energies, system sizes, and rapidities may not be trivially reconcilable with the standard two-component interpretation (hydrodynamic flow at low transverse momentum and parton energy loss at high transverse momentum), and that this scaling imposes strong constraints that could force a revision of current theoretical models.

## Method

The authors examine the scaling properties of $v_2$ as a function of transverse momentum $p_T$, rapidity $y$, system size, and centrality, using experimental data from RHIC and LHC. They analyze the implications of these scalings for hydrodynamic models at low $p_T$ and for tomographic (energy loss) models at high $p_T$, using the ABC model parametrization of energy loss ($-dE/d\tau = \kappa p^a T^b \tau^c$) to test whether different energy loss mechanisms (collisional, LPM radiative, AdS/CFT) preserve or break the observed scaling. The authors perform numerical simulations with a longitudinally expanding ellipsoidal background fitted to global multiplicity and system size, scanning over different initial time assumptions ($\tau_0 = 1$ fm, $\tau_0 \sim 1/\sqrt{s}$, $\tau_0 \sim 1/T_0$).

## Key numbers

* The average transverse momentum of the system is $\langle p_T \rangle \sim \mathcal{O}(0.5-1)$ GeV, with the tomographic regime appearing at $\mathcal{O}(3-4) \times \langle p_T \rangle$.
* The experimental parametrization $\langle v_2 \rangle / \epsilon = 4 \times 10^{-3} \times (1/S)(dN/dy)$ is used for the momentum-integrated elliptic flow.
* The critical transverse momentum separating hydrodynamic and tomographic regimes scales as $p_{T1}^{tomo}/p_{T2}^{tomo} \sim (S_2/S_1 \times dN_1/dy / dN_2/dy)^\omega$, where $\omega = 1/2$ for collisional-dominated equilibration and could increase to $3/2$ if radiative processes become important.
* Experimental data shows scaling holds across different centralities up to $p_T \simeq 20$ GeV at LHC, with possible breakdown only at $p_T \simeq 40$ GeV.
* At RHIC, scaling is seen up to $p_T \simeq 3.5$ GeV across Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of elliptic flow scaling first predicted by Heiselberg (1999) under weakly coupled assumptions, and addresses tensions with hydrodynamic interpretations previously noted by Heinz and collaborators. The paper contrasts the scaling behavior with expectations from both weakly coupled (LPM regime) and strongly coupled (AdS/CFT falling string) energy loss models, and suggests that if scaling persists at high $p_T$, non-tomographic effects from initial state dynamics (Color Glass Condensate) or final state fragmentation may play a larger role than currently expected. The authors call for further experimental scaling studies at high $p_T$ to test whether $v_2$ at high $p_T$ is generated tomographically.
