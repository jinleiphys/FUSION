# On Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering at next-to-leading order

> Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering in the near forward kinematic region is the golden access to Generalized Parton Distributions. We studied the O(alphaS) corrections to the scattering amplitude for both spacelike and timelike kinematics relevant respectively to the leptoproduction of a real photon and to the photoproduction of a lepton pair. It turns out that these corrections are phenomenologically important and that the gluonic contributions are by no means negligible, even in the moderate energy range of JLab12 and of the COMPASS-II experiment at CERN.

## Key claim

NLO corrections to the DVCS and TCS amplitudes are phenomenologically important and gluonic contributions are by no means negligible, even at the moderate energy ranges of JLab12 and COMPASS-II, but the size of these corrections depends on the choice of factorization scale \(\mu_F\) and further studies are needed to justify a particular scale choice.

## Method

The authors computed the real and imaginary parts of the Compton Form Factor \(\mathcal{H}\) at LO and NLO for both spacelike (DVCS) and timelike (TCS) kinematics, using two GPD models based on the Radyushkin Double Distribution factorized Ansatz: the Goloskokov-Kroll model built from CTEQ6m PDFs, and an MSTW08-based model. Two independent numerical codes were systematically compared and validated to an accuracy of 0.1% over the kinematic range studied, with skewness varying between \(10^{-3}\) and 1.

## Key numbers

- NLO corrections to the CFF \(\mathcal{H}\) were computed at \(t = -0.1~\GeV^2\), \(Q^2 = \mu_F^2 = 4.~\GeV^2\) for both spacelike and timelike kinematics.
- For CLAS12 predictions: DVCS cross sections computed at \(E_e = 11.~\GeV\), \(x_B = 0.36\), \(t = -0.2~\GeV^2\), \(Q^2 = \mu_F^2 = 4.~\GeV^2\).
- For COMPASS-II predictions: combined charge-spin DVCS cross sections computed at \(E_\mu = 160.~\GeV\), \(\xi = 0.05\), \(t = -0.2~\GeV^2\), \(Q^2 = \mu_F^2 = 4.~\GeV^2\).
- For TCS at CLAS12: cross sections computed at \(E_\gamma = 10.~\GeV\) (\(\eta \simeq 0.11\)), \(t = -0.1~\GeV^2\), \(Q^2 = \mu_F^2 = 4.~\GeV^2\), integrated over \(\theta \in [\pi/4, 3\pi/4]\).
- Typical kinematics for existing experiments: COMPASS at \(x_B = 0.05\), \(Q^2 = 2.00~\GeV^2\), \(t = -0.20~\GeV^2\); HERMES at \(x_B = 0.09\), \(Q^2 = 2.50~\GeV^2\), \(t = -0.12~\GeV^2\); CLAS at \(x_B = 0.19\), \(Q^2 = 1.25~\GeV^2\), \(t = -0.19~\GeV^2\).

## Context

This work extends the NLO coefficient function calculations for DVCS from Belitsky (1997) and for TCS from Pire (2011), and builds on the relation between DVCS and TCS coefficient functions established by Muller (2012). The GK model used was previously developed for Deeply Virtual Meson Electroproduction by Goloskokov and Kroll (2005-2007) and tested against DVCS data by Kroll (2012). The results demonstrate that NLO corrections are sizable enough that global fits of DVCS and TCS data will be needed to separate quark and gluon contributions, and that the COMPASS-II and JLab12 experiments may constrain gluon GPDs, enabling 3D tomography of the gluonic structure of the nucleon.
