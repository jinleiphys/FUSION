# Incoherent single pion electroproduction on the deuteron with polarization effects

> Incoherent pion electroproduction on the deuteron is studied from threshold up to the second resonance region with special emphasis on the influence of final state interaction, in particular on polarization observables. The elementary $γN\toπN$ amplitude is taken from the MAID-2003 model. Final state interaction is included by considering complete rescattering in the final $NN$ and $πN$ subsystems. Their influence on the structure functions governing the semi-exclusive differential cross section, where besides the scattered electron only the produced pion is detected, is investigated in detail. For charged pion-production the effect of $NN$-rescattering is moderate whereas $πN$-rescattering is almost negligible, except very close to threshold. $NN$-rescattering appears much stronger in neutral pion production for which the primary mechanism is the elimination of a significant spurious coherent contribution in the impulse approximation. Sizeable effects are also found in some of the polarization structure functions for beam and/or target polarizations.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates the influence of final state interactions (FSI) on incoherent single pion electroproduction on the deuteron, finding that for charged pion production NN-rescattering effects are moderate while πN-rescattering is almost negligible except very close to threshold, but for neutral pion production NN-rescattering appears much stronger primarily because it eliminates a significant spurious coherent contribution present in the impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors compute the T-matrix for the reaction γ* + d → π + N₁ + N₂ using the MAID-2003 model for the elementary γN → πN amplitude, then add final state interactions by including complete rescattering in the NN and πN subsystems via separable representations of the Paris potential (for NN) and a realistic πN interaction (for πN). The semi-exclusive differential cross section and polarization structure functions are evaluated by integrating over the unobserved nucleon angles, with results presented for various kinematic conditions from threshold through the Δ-resonance up to the second resonance region.

## Key numbers

- The invariant total mass W is defined as W = q₀ + √(M_d² + q²) = √((2q₀^{lab} + M_d)M_d − Q²)
- The maximum pion momentum in the c.m. system is given by p_{π,max} = (1/(2W)) √(((W − m_π)² − 4M²)((W + m_π)² − 4M²))
- The quasi-free lab energy of the final πN system is E_{πN}^{qf,lab} = M_d − M + q₀^{lab}
- For the NN-rescattering calculation, all partial waves up to ³D₃ are included
- For the πN-rescattering calculation, all partial waves up to l = 2 are taken into account

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous photoproduction studies (Arenhövel and Fix, 2005; Fix and Arenhövel, 2005) to the electroproduction case, allowing independent variation of energy and momentum transfer. It builds on earlier experimental work by Brown et al. (1971) on isoscalar-isovector interference, Gilman et al. (1990) on forward-angle charged pion production, and Gaskell et al. (2001) on longitudinal pion production, as well as theoretical studies by Loucks et al. (1994), Hafidi and Lee (2001), and Levchuck et al. (2004). The results enable future extraction of elementary production amplitudes on the neutron using the deuteron as an effective neutron target, and allow investigation of medium effects from the spectator nucleon.
