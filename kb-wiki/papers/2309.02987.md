# Production of doubly charmed hadron $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ and $T_{cc}^+$ in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Heavy ion collisions provide a unique opportunity for studying the properties of exotic hadrons with two charm quarks. The production of $T_{cc}^+$ is significantly enhanced in nuclear collisions compared to proton-proton collisions due to the creation of multiple charm pairs. In this study, we employ the Langevin equation in combination with the Instantaneous Coalescence Model (LICM) to investigate the production of $T_{cc}^+$ and $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ which consists of two charm quarks. We consider $T_{cc}^+$ as molecular states composed of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons. The Langevin equation is used to calculate the energy loss of charm quarks and $D$ mesons in the hot medium. The hadronization process, where charm quarks transform into each $D$ state as constituents of $T_{cc}^+$ production, is described using the coalescence model. The coalescence probability between $D$ and $D^*$ is determined by the Wigner function, which encodes the information of the $T_{cc}^+$ wave function. Our results show that the $T_{cc}^+$ production varies by approximately one order of magnitude when different widths in the Wigner function, representing distinct binding energies of $T_{cc}^+$, are considered. This variation offers valuable insights into the nature of $T_{cc}^+$ through the analysis of its wave function. The $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ is treated as a hadronic state produced at the hadronization of the deconfined matter. Its production is also calculated as a comparison with the molecular state $T_{cc}^+$.

## Key claim
The production yield of the molecular state \(T_{cc}^+\) in Pb-Pb collisions varies by approximately one order of magnitude depending on the assumed width of its Wigner function (which encodes different binding energies), meaning that the measured yield in heavy-ion collisions can be used to discriminate between different internal structures of the state.

## Method
The authors coupled a Langevin equation (for energy loss of charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma and of \(D\) mesons in the hadronic medium) with an Instantaneous Coalescence Model (LICM). The coalescence probability for \(T_{cc}^+\) (treated as a \(D D^*\) molecule) was computed using a Gaussian Wigner function whose width is related to the root-mean-square radius of the state; different radii were obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with a \(DD^*\) potential from chiral effective field theory. For \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) (a compact hadronic state), a three-body coalescence formula was used with a fixed root-mean-square radius of 0.5 fm. The hot medium evolution was simulated with the MUSIC hydrodynamic package using an equation of state from the HotQCD Collaboration.

## Key numbers
- The rapidity-differential cross-section for charm pairs in pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV is \(d\sigma_{pp}/dy = 1.165\) mb.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient in the QGP was taken as \(\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T)=5\); in the hadronic medium a midpoint value of \(\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T)=8\) was used.
- For the \(T_{cc}^+\) molecule, solving the Schrödinger equation with a cut-off parameter \(\Lambda\) from 0.66 to 1.0 GeV gave binding energies from 0.03 keV to 38.4 keV and root-mean-square radii from 5.04 fm down to 0.62 fm.
- The production of \(T_{cc}^+\) increases by approximately 3.5 times when the root-mean-square radius is reduced from 5.0 fm to 3.0 fm.
- The coalescence probability \(P_{D+D^*\rightarrow T_{cc}}\) increases by around 40% when the coalescence temperature changes from 0.12 GeV to 0.16 GeV (for \(\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle}=3.0\) fm).
- The hadronic state \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) has a production yield about 10 times larger than the molecular \(T_{cc}^+\).
- The charm quark mass was set to \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV; the light quark mass was set to \(m = 0.3\) GeV.
- The root-mean-square radius of the \(D\) meson was taken as \(\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle}_D = 0.43\) fm.
- The root-mean-square radius of \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) was taken as \(\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle}_{\Xi_{cc}^{++}} = 0.5\) fm.
- The critical temperature for the QGP-to-hadron transition was set to \(T_c = 170\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the application of coalescence models (previously used for light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions by Gosset (1976) and the STAR Collaboration (2001, 2010)) to the production of doubly charmed exotic hadrons. It builds on earlier studies of charmonium and charmed hadron production in heavy-ion collisions by Chen (2017, 2021) and Zhao (2016, 2017). The results enable experimental extraction of the \(T_{cc}^+\) wave function properties from its measured yield in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, and provide a direct comparison between a molecular interpretation and a compact hadronic state (\(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\)).
