# First-principles results for electromagnetic properties of $sd$ shell nuclei

> In this work we present $ab~initio$ shell-model calculations for electric quadrupole moments and magnetic dipole moments of $sd$ shell nuclei using valence-space Hamiltonians derived with two $ab~initio$ approaches: the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) and the coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI). Results are in a reasonable agreement with the available experimental data as well as with the results from the phenomenological USDB effective interaction. This work will add more information to the available $ab~initio$ results for the spectroscopy of $sd$ shell nuclei.

## Key claim

First-principles shell-model calculations using IM-SRG and CCEI valence-space Hamiltonians yield electromagnetic moments for sd shell nuclei in reasonable agreement with experiment and with the phenomenological USDB interaction, but the agreement degrades for heavier-Z nuclei and for isotopes near the island of inversion where the sd model space alone is insufficient.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model diagonalizations using NUSHELLX with two ab initio valence-space Hamiltonians: one derived from the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) using chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions with ℏΩ=24 MeV, and one from the coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI) using N2LO NN and N3LO 3N chiral interactions with ℏΩ=20 MeV and a 13-oscillator-shell Hartree-Fock basis. They computed magnetic dipole moments using free-nucleon g factors and electric quadrupole moments using effective charges e_p=1.5e and e_n=0.5e, with a harmonic-oscillator parameter ℏΩ=45A^{-1/3}-25A^{-2/3} MeV, and compared results to experimental data and to calculations with the phenomenological USDB interaction.

## Context

This work extends the ab initio studies of Stroberg et al. (2012) and Jansen et al. (2014, 2016) that focused on spectroscopic properties (spins, parities, level energies) of sd shell nuclei, by adding electromagnetic moments as a more sensitive test of the wavefunctions. The results enable systematic comparison of IM-SRG and CCEI approaches for observables beyond excitation spectra, and highlight where the sd model space is insufficient, particularly for isotopes in the island of inversion region (N≈20) such as ³⁰Na, ³¹Mg, and ³³Al, which require extended sdpf model spaces as shown by Utsuno et al. (2001) and others.
