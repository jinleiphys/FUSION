# Dynamical Properties of Flux Tubes in the Friedberg-Lee Model

> A dynamical model of confinement based on a microscopic transport description of the Friedberg-Lee model is extended to explicit color degrees of freedom. The string tension is reproduced by an adiabatic string formation from the nucleon ground state. As a particular application, we address the question of how a charmonium state might be dissociated by the strong color electric fields when moving through a color electric flux tube and speculate on the importance of such an effect with respect to the issue of $J/ψ$--suppression observed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Furthermore, we show the dynamical breakup of flux tubes via $q-\bar{q}$-particle production and the disintegration into mesons. There we encounter some problems within the Vlasov-type realization of describing the motion of the quarks which can be resolved by a molecular dynamical approach.

# Dynamical Properties of Flux Tubes in the Friedberg-Lee Model

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a charmonium (J/ψ) state moving through a color electric flux tube will be dissociated by field ionization on a timescale of less than approximately 1 fm/c, potentially providing an alternative or additional mechanism for J/ψ suppression observed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. However, this conclusion remains speculative and requires implementation within a full microscopic transport algorithm for verification.

## Method

The authors extend a semiclassical transport description of the Friedberg-Lee nontopological soliton model to include explicit color degrees of freedom, solving Vlasov-type transport equations for quark and antiquark phase-space distributions coupled to classical mean fields for the scalar σ-field and the color electric field. Static soliton solutions for mesons and baryons are constructed using a Thomas-Fermi approximation and a shooting method, while dynamical simulations of flux tube formation, J/ψ dissociation, and string fragmentation via q-qbar pair production are performed using test-particle and molecular dynamical approaches.

## Key numbers

- String tension τ ≈ 1 GeV/fm is reproduced with α_s ≈ 2
- Volume-averaged electric field ⟨E_z⟩ = 1.6 fm⁻², with maximum E_z,max = 2.2 fm⁻² along the z-axis
- String radius approximately 0.8 fm
- Meson mass from static soliton solution: 798 MeV (experimental mean of pion and rho-meson: 465 MeV)
- Baryon mass from static soliton solution: 1099 MeV (experimental mean of nucleon and delta: 1087 MeV)
- RMS radius of meson: 0.653 fm (experimental: 0.66 fm); RMS radius of baryon: 0.693 fm (experimental: 0.83 fm)
- Charmonium (c-cbar) mass: ≈ 3 GeV (approximately 60 MeV above experimental value)
- D-meson (c-qbar) mass: ≈ 1.7 GeV
- Charm quark mass taken as ≈ 1.25 GeV
- Dissociation timescale for J/ψ in flux tube: ≤ 1 fm/c
- σ-field relaxation time after screening: approximately 5 fm/c
- Glueball mass from model parameters: 1.045 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Friedberg-Lee model (Friedberg and Lee, 1977) from static hadron properties to dynamical simulations of confinement and flux tube behavior. The J/ψ dissociation mechanism proposed here offers an alternative to the original quark-gluon plasma screening explanation of Matsui and Satz (1986) and to the hadronic absorption models of Gerland (1992), which attribute suppression to J/ψ absorption on nucleons or comoving mesons. The string fragmentation results show correspondence with Lund model phenomenology (Andersson et al., 1983) but reveal limitations of the Vlasov approach that the authors propose to resolve with molecular dynamics. This work enables future implementation of these dissociation and fragmentation mechanisms within microscopic transport algorithms such as HSD for more realistic heavy-ion collision simulations.
