# Neutron star matter with strange interactions in a relativistic quark model

> The effect of strange interactions in neutron star matter and the role of the strange meson-hyperon couplings are studied in a relativistic quark model where the confining interaction for quarks inside a baryon is represented by a phenomenological average potential in an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form. The hadron-hadron interaction in nuclear matter is then realized by introducing additional quark couplings to $σ$, $ω$, $ρ$, $σ^*$ and $ϕ$ mesons through mean-field approximations. The meson-baryon couplings are fixed through the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry and the SU(3) flavor symmetry to determine the hadronic equation of state (EoS). We find that the SU(3) coupling set gives the potential depth between $Λ$s around $-5$ MeV and favours a stiffer EoS.The radius for the canonical neutron star lies within a range of $12.7$ to $13.1$ km.

## Key claim

The SU(3) flavor symmetry coupling set for hyperon-meson interactions produces a stiffer equation of state than the SU(6) spin-flavor sets, enabling a maximum neutron star mass of 1.90 solar masses that can reach the pulsar PSR J1614-2230 limit, whereas the SU(6) sets fail to achieve this limit.

## Method

The authors use the modified quark meson coupling (MQMC) model, where quarks inside baryons are confined by an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic potential rather than a bag. The hadron-hadron interaction in nuclear matter is realized by introducing quark couplings to sigma, omega, rho, sigma-star and phi mesons through mean-field approximations, with hyperon couplings fixed via three schemes: SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry (Set I), hyperon-nucleon potential fitting (Set II), and SU(3) flavor symmetry with mixing angle from the Nijmegen extended-soft-core model (Set III). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then integrated to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The potential depth between two Lambda hyperons in the SU(3) coupling set is -5.28 MeV, consistent with the Nagara event constraint of about -5 MeV.
- The radius for the canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star lies between 12.7 km (Sets I and II) and 13.1 km (Set III).
- The maximum mass achieved with the SU(3) coupling Set III is 1.90 solar masses with a corresponding radius of 11.2 km.
- The nuclear matter incompressibility K at saturation density is 242.41 MeV for quark mass 200 MeV.
- The nucleon effective mass ratio M_N*/M_N at saturation density is 0.83.
- The hyperon-nucleon interaction potentials used to fix Set II couplings are U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = 30 MeV, and U_Xi = -18 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the MQMC model previously applied to hyperon stars without hyperon-hyperon interactions (Sahoo et al., earlier work) by incorporating hidden strange mesons sigma-star and phi that couple only to strange quarks. The results are compared with QMC, FSUGold, and GM3 parametrizations from Miyatsu et al. The SU(3) coupling scheme, following the Nijmegen extended-soft-core model, produces stiffer equations of state than SU(6) schemes and can satisfy the GW170817 constraint on the 1.4 solar mass neutron star radius between 9.9 and 13.6 km, while enabling future studies of neutron star mergers and maximum mass constraints from pulsar observations.
