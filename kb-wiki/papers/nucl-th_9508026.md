# Chirally Constraining the $ππ$ Interaction in Nuclear Matter

> A general prescription for the construction of $ππ$ interaction potentials which preserve scattering length constraints from chiral symmetry when iterated in scattering equations is derived. The prescription involves only minor modifications of typical meson-exchange models, so that coupling constants and cut-off masses in the models are not greatly affected. Calculations of $s$-wave $ππ$ scattering amplitudes in nuclear matter for two models are compared with those for similar models which violate the chiral constraint. While the prescription tends to suppress the accumulation of the near sub-threshold strength of the $ππ$ interaction, an earlier conjecture that amplitudes which satisfy chiral constraints will not exhibit an instability towards $ππ~s-$wave pair condensation appears to be incorrect. At the same time, however, conventional $ππ$ interaction models which fit scattering data well can readily be adjusted to avoid the instability in nuclear matter without recourse to exotic mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general prescription for constructing ππ interaction potentials that preserve chiral symmetry constraints on scattering lengths when iterated in scattering equations, but finds that contrary to an earlier conjecture, amplitudes satisfying these constraints do not automatically guarantee stability against ππ s-wave pair condensation in nuclear matter at low-to-moderate density.

## Method

The authors develop a prescription for off-shell continuation of Mandelstam variables in meson-exchange models that ensures the scattering amplitude is proportional to the center-of-mass energy squared (s) in the chiral limit, thereby enforcing the minimal chiral constraint (MCC) on scattering lengths. They apply this prescription to two models, the linear σ-model and the Jülich meson-exchange model, and solve the Blankenbecler-Sugar scattering equation to compute s-wave ππ scattering amplitudes in nuclear matter, comparing results with models that violate the chiral constraint.

## Key numbers

- For the linear σ-model with MCC, the amplitude shows no tendency towards pair condensation even at densities as high as 2ρ₀ (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, normal nuclear matter density).
- For the chirally improved Jülich model with MCC, the critical density for ππ pair condensation is pushed up to approximately 1.4ρ₀.
- The critical density can be increased to approximately 2ρ₀ with a relatively small increase in the cutoff mass for the contact terms while retaining a good fit to the phase shift.
- For the Jülich model using the BbS (on-energy-shell) off-shell continuation (violating MCC), the condensation density is about 1.5ρ₀.
- Typical cutoff parameters for the contact terms in the Jülich model are Λc = 700–1000 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study by Aouissat et al. (1995, referred to as I), which demonstrated ππ pair condensation at densities of 1.2–1.3ρ₀ for two phenomenological models (Lee and Loh) and suggested that chiral constraints might suppress the instability. The present paper shows that the earlier conjecture is incorrect, chiral constraints delay but do not eliminate the instability, and that conventional meson-exchange models (such as the Jülich model) can be adjusted to avoid condensation without exotic mechanisms. The results enable further investigation into how chiral constraints affect the range of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in nuclear matter.
