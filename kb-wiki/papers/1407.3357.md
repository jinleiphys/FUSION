# Spectroscopic factors of magic and semimagic nuclei within the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems

> A scheme is presented to find single-particle spectroscopic factors (SF) of magic and semimagic nuclei within the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS). In addition to the energy dependence of the mass operator $Σ$ induced by the surface-phonon coupling effects which are commonly considered in this problem, the in-volume energy dependence of the operator $Σ$ inherent in the self-consistent TFFS is also taken into account. This dependence arises due to the effect of high-lying particle-hole excitations and persists in nuclear matter. The self-consistent basis of the energy density functional method by Fayans {\it et al.} is used. Both the surface and in-volume contributions to the SFs turned out to be of comparable magnitude. The results for magic $^{40,48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb nuclei and semimagic lead isotopes are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a scheme for calculating single-particle spectroscopic factors (SFs) in magic and semimagic nuclei that includes both the usual surface-phonon coupling contribution and an additional in-volume energy dependence of the mass operator; both contributions are found to be of comparable magnitude, but the in-volume term depends weakly on the nucleus and state while the phonon-coupling term fluctuates strongly.

## Method

The authors use the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) with the Fayans energy density functional (parameter set DF3-a) to generate the quasiparticle basis. The total Z-factor (spectroscopic factor) is computed as the product of an in-volume contribution Z0, derived from the energy derivative of the quasiparticle mass operator, and a phonon-coupling (PC) contribution ZPC, obtained from the pole diagram of the mass operator in the gL^2 approximation using low-lying surface phonons. For semimagic nuclei where near-degeneracies occur between single-particle states and phonon energies, a two-level mixing treatment is applied.

## Key numbers

- In 208Pb, the total Z-factor Ztot for neutron states ranges from 0.503 (2f7/2^-n) to 0.799 (1i11/2^n), with experimental SFs ranging from 0.53 to 0.9; for proton states Ztot ranges from 0.387 (1g7/2^-p) to 0.807 (1h9/2^p), with experimental SFs from 0.52 to 1.18.
- In 40Ca, Ztot for neutron states ranges from 0.821 (2s1/2^-n) to 0.866 (1f5/2^n), with experimental SFs from 0.7 to 0.94; for proton states Ztot ranges from 0.825 (2s1/2^-p) to 0.891 (2p1/2^p), with experimental SFs from 0.33 to 1.12.
- In 48Ca, Ztot for neutron states ranges from 0.699 (2p1/2^n) to 0.844 (1f7/2^-n), with experimental SFs from 0.47 to 1.03; for proton states Ztot ranges from 0.558 (2p3/2^p) to 0.796 (1d3/2^-p), with experimental SFs from 0.54 to 1.22.
- In 204Pb, Ztot for proton states ranges from 0.513 (2d3/2^-p) to 0.743 (3s3/2^-p), with experimental SFs from 0.46 to 0.90; in 206Pb, Ztot ranges from 0.306 (2d3/2^-p) to 0.786 (3s3/2^-p), with experimental SFs from 0.49 to 0.90.
- The in-volume Z0 factor in nuclear matter is 0.8, corresponding to the dimensionless parameter lambda02 = -0.25.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach previously applied to single-particle energies (Levels, 2014), magnetic moments (EPL1, 2013), and quadrupole moments (QEPJ, 2013) by now calculating spectroscopic factors. The phonon-coupling treatment follows the gL^2 approximation used in earlier TFFS work (KhS, 2005; AB1, 1989), while the in-volume contribution is derived from the quasiparticle Lagrangian formalism of Khodel and Saperstein (KhS, 2005). The results enable comparison with experimental SFs extracted from (d,p) and (d,n) reactions, though the authors caution that such extractions have large uncertainties as criticized by DWBA1 (2006) and DWBA2 (2008). The calculations provide predictions for the chain of lead isotopes 188-212Pb, where experimental data are scarce.
