# Initial and Final State Interaction Effects in Small-x Quark Distributions

> We study the initial and final state interaction effects in the transverse momentum dependent parton distributions in the small-$x$ saturation region. In particular, we discuss the quark distributions in the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan lepton pair production and dijet-correlation processes in $pA$ collisions. We calculate the quark distributions in the scalar-QED model and then extend to the color glass condensate formalism in QCD. The quark distributions are found universal between the DIS and Drell-Yan processes. On the other hand, the quark distribution from the $qq'\to qq'$ channel contribution to the dijet-correlation process is not universal. However, we find that it can be related to the quark distribution in DIS process by a convolution with the normalized unintegrated gluon distribution in the color glass condensate formalism in the large $N_c$ limit.

# Initial and Final State Interaction Effects in Small-x Quark Distributions

## Key claim

The transverse momentum dependent (TMD) quark distributions at small-x are not universal across different processes: while universality holds between semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan processes, the quark distribution extracted from the dijet-correlation process (specifically the $qq'\to qq'$ channel) differs from those in SIDIS and Drell-Yan, although it can be related to the DIS quark distribution via a convolution with the normalized unintegrated gluon distribution in the color glass condensate formalism in the large $N_c$ limit.

## Method

The authors calculate TMD quark distributions in a scalar-QED model with heavy and light charged scalars interacting via massive U(1) gauge fields, resumming all-order initial and final state interactions with the nucleus target using eikonal approximations. They then extend the analysis to QCD using the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the color glass condensate formalism, computing amplitudes for DIS, Drell-Yan, and dijet-correlation processes up to three-gluon exchange order and performing Fourier transforms to obtain all-order resummed expressions.

## Key numbers

- The scalar-QED model uses heavy scalar mass $M$, light scalar mass $m$, and gauge boson mass $\lambda$; the eikonal propagator for final state interactions yields the factor $\frac{g_{1}P_{A}^{+}}{-k_{g}^{+}+i\epsilon}$.
- The Fourier-transformed amplitudes involve the Bessel function $K_{0}(M_{0}r_{\perp})$ with $M_{0}^{2}=2xP^{+}p_{2}^{-}+m^{2}$ and the function $W(r_{\perp},R_{\perp})=\frac{1}{2\pi}\log\left(\frac{|\vec{R}_{\perp}+\vec{r}_{\perp}|}{R_{\perp}}\right)$.
- The all-order resummed amplitude for the dijet-correlation process takes the form $A^{(tot)}(R,r)=iV(r_{\perp})\left\{1-e^{igg_{1}[G(R_{\perp}+r_{\perp})-G(R_{\perp})]}\right\}e^{-igg_{2}G(R_{\perp})}$, where $G(R_{\perp})=K_{0}(\lambda R_{\perp})/2\pi$ and $V(r_{\perp})=K_{0}(Mr_{\perp})/2\pi$.
- At the two-gluon exchange level, the amplitude contains terms proportional to $g_{1}^{2}$ and $g_{1}g_{2}$; at three-gluon exchange, terms proportional to $g_{1}^{3}$, $g_{1}^{2}g_{2}$, and $g_{1}g_{2}^{2}$ appear.

## Context

This work extends the universality discussions of TMD parton distributions from the large-x regime (where non-universality was established for transverse spin dependent TMDs by Brodsky, Hwang, and Schmidt (2002), Collins (2002), and Belitsky, Ji, and Yuan (2002)) to the small-x saturation domain. The authors build on the scalar-QED model of Brodsky et al. (2002) and the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1993) for high-density small-x partons in large nuclei. The results enable effective $k_t$ factorization formulas for $pA$ collisions with modified nuclear parton distributions, relevant for interpreting di-hadron correlation measurements at RHIC where strong back-to-back de-correlation was observed in forward deuteron-gold collisions.
