# The effect of single-particle space-momentum angle distribution on two-pion HBT correlation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by using a multiphase transport model

> With the string melting version of a multiphase transport(AMPT) model, we analyze the transverse momentum dependence of HBT radius $R_{\rm s}$ and the single-pion transverse angle distribution in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}=19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200$ GeV. And base on a series of functions, a numerical connection between these two phenomena has been built. We can estimate the single-pion transverse angle distribution from the HBT analysis.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a numerical connection between the transverse momentum dependence of the HBT radius \(R_{\rm s}\) and the single-pion space-momentum angle distribution on the transverse plane, using the string melting AMPT model. The authors claim that from a measured series of \(R_{\rm s}\) values at different transverse momenta, one can estimate the \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution, but this connection relies on several empirically chosen constants (0.0005, -4.5, -3.5) and a presumed minimum value of parameter \(b\) (-0.5) that are not derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model to generate central Au+Au collisions at five beam energy scan energies. They calculated two-pion HBT correlation functions using the CRAB code, fitting them with a three-dimensional Gaussian to extract the sideward HBT radius \(R_{\rm s}\) in bins of pair transverse momentum \(K_{\rm T}\). Separately, they computed the normalized \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution (where \(\Delta\theta\) is the angle between the transverse position and transverse momentum vectors of single pions at freeze-out) and parameterized it with a double-exponential function. Finally, they fitted the \(K_{\rm T}\)-dependence of \(R_{\rm s}\) with a power law \(R = a K_{\rm T}^{b}\) and correlated the exponent \(b\) with the parameters of the \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution.

## Key numbers

* Central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200\) GeV with impact parameter 0 fm were simulated.
* The HBT radius \(R_{\rm s}\) was fitted with \(R = a K_{\rm T}^{b}\); the exponent \(b\) becomes more negative (stronger \(K_{\rm T}\) dependence) with increasing collision energy.
* The normalized \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution was fitted with \(f = 0.0005 \exp\{ c_1 \exp[ c_2 \cos(\Delta\theta) ] \}\), where the constant 0.0005 was settled by the authors.
* The parameters \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) were further fitted as functions of \(K_{\rm T}\) using \(c_1 = k_1 \exp[-4.5 \times (K_{\rm T}/1000)^2] + j_1\) and \(c_2 = k_2 \exp[-3.5 \times (K_{\rm T}/1000)^2] + j_2\), with the constants -4.5 and -3.5 settled by the authors.
* The numerical connection between \(b\) and the fit parameters is given by equations such as \(b(k_1) = \mu_{11} |k_1|^{\mu_{12}} - 0.5\), with \(\mu_{11} = 60 \pm 20\) and \(\mu_{12} = -3.9 \pm 0.2\) for the \(c_1\) branch, and \(\mu_{21} = 0.219 \pm 0.005\) and \(\mu_{22} = -3.0 \pm 0.2\) for the \(c_2\) branch.

## Context

This work extends the study of space-momentum correlations in heavy-ion collisions, previously quantified by the single-particle space-momentum angle distribution in Yang (2020), by explicitly linking it to the transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii. The HBT method, originally developed by Hanbury Brown and Twiss (1956) and extended to particle physics by Goldhaber et al. (1960), is a standard tool for extracting source sizes; the transverse momentum dependence of \(R_{\rm s}\) is attributed to collective flow as discussed by Pratt (1996) and others. The numerical connection built here enables future experimental analyses to infer the \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution from measured HBT radii, potentially providing information about the freeze-out stage and collective flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC beam energy scan energies.
