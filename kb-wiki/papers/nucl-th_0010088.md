# Transition to resonance-rich matter in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies

> The equilibration of hot and dense nuclear matter produced in the central region in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV is studied within the microscopic transport model UrQMD. The pressure here becomes isotropic at $t \approx 5$ fm/c. Within the next 15 fm/c the expansion of the matter proceeds almost isentropically with the entropy per baryon ratio $S/A \approx 150$. During this period the equation of state in the $(P,ε)$-plane has a very simple form, $P=0.15 ε$. Comparison with the statistical model (SM) of an ideal hadron gas reveals that the time of $\approx 20$ fm/$c$ may be too short to attain the fully equilibrated state. Particularly, the fractions of resonances are overpopulated in contrast to the SM values. The creation of such a long-lived resonance-rich state slows down the relaxation to chemical equilibrium and can be detected experimentally.

## Key claim

The hot and dense nuclear matter produced in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies reaches kinetic equilibrium by about 5 fm/c, but the subsequent 20 fm/c may be too short to achieve full chemical equilibrium; instead, a long-lived resonance-rich state forms that slows relaxation and could be detected experimentally.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic transport model UrQMD to simulate central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV and analyzed the central cell (volume 125 fm³) of the reaction. They compared the time evolution of hadron yields, energy spectra, pressure, and entropy per baryon in the cell to predictions from a statistical model (SM) of an ideal hadron gas, using the energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density from UrQMD as input to the SM to determine temperature and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Pressure isotropy (kinetic equilibrium) is reached at t ≈ 5 fm/c in the central cell at RHIC energy.
- The entropy per baryon ratio during the nearly isentropic expansion (from 5 to 20 fm/c) is S/A ≈ 150, with a predicted range of 150 ≤ s/ρ_B ≤ 170 for RHIC.
- The equation of state in the (P, ε)-plane takes the form P = 0.15 ε at both SPS and RHIC energies.
- At t = 10 fm/c, the partial baryon density in UrQMD is R_B^mic = 0.05 fm⁻³ versus R_B^SM = 0.08 fm⁻³ from the statistical model; the antibaryon density is R_¯B^mic = 0.02 fm⁻³ versus R_¯B^SM = 0.05 fm⁻³.
- The baryon chemical potential at RHIC energies is μ_B ≈ 50 MeV.
- The final time of calculations, defined by freeze-out conditions ε ≈ 0.1 GeV/fm³ or ρ_tot ≈ 0.5 ρ₀, corresponds to t ≈ 21 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group at AGS (10.7 AGeV) and SPS (160 AGeV) energies (Bravina et al., 1998, 1999), where similar central-cell analysis was performed. The finding that the resonance-rich state persists until thermal freeze-out contradicts the assumption of full chemical equilibrium used in statistical model fits to experimental data (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1996; Becattini et al., 1996; Cleymans and Redlich, 1999). The predicted entropy per baryon ratio of 150-170 at RHIC can be tested against upcoming experimental data, and the large fraction of reconstructible resonances (over 80% of baryon non-strange resonances are Δ(1232)) offers an experimental signature for the resonance-abundant state.
