# Exploring the limits of nucleonic metamodelling using different relativistic density functionals

> In this work, we explore two classes of density dependent relativistic mean-field models, their predictions of proton fractions at high densities and neutron star structure. We have used a metamodelling approach to these relativistic density functionals. We have generated a large ensemble of models with these classes and then applied constraints from theoretical and experimental nuclear physics and astrophysical observations. We find that both models produce similar equations of state and neutron star mass-radius sequences. But, their underlying compositions, denoted by the proton fraction in this case, are vastly different. This reinstates previous findings that information on composition gets masqueraded in $β$-equilibrium. Additional observations of non-equilibrium phenomena are necessary to pin it down.

# Exploring the limits of nucleonic metamodelling using different relativistic density functionals

## Key claim
While two classes of density-dependent relativistic mean-field models (TW and GDFM) produce similar equations of state and neutron star mass-radius sequences after applying nuclear and astrophysical constraints, their predicted proton fractions at high densities are vastly different, confirming that composition information is masqueraded in beta-equilibrium and cannot be extracted from equilibrium observations alone.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using nested sampling for two density-dependent relativistic mean-field models (TW and GDFM), generating large ensembles of model parameters and applying constraints from nuclear matter parameters at saturation, chiral effective field theory calculations of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, the AME2016 nuclear mass table, and astrophysical constraints from pulsar mass measurements and the GW170817 tidal deformability estimate. Unified equations of state were constructed by matching a non-relativistic metamodel crust at subsaturation densities with the relativistic core at saturation density, and the TOV equations were solved to obtain neutron star properties.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density prior range: 0.14 to 0.17 fm^-3
- Saturation energy prior range: -17 to -14 MeV
- Incompressibility prior range: 150 to 350 MeV
- Symmetry energy prior range: 20 to 45 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope parameter prior range: 20 to 180 MeV
- GDFM proton fraction reaches up to approximately 0.45 in the prior and more than approximately 0.3 in the posterior at high densities
- TW proton fraction saturates between 0.12 and 0.2 in the prior and becomes very narrow in the posterior at high densities
- Central densities for maximum mass stars shift to higher values from prior to posterior for both models

## Context
This work extends the metamodelling approach developed by Char (2023) for relativistic density functionals, comparing the TW functional proposed by Typel (1999) with the GDFM functional proposed by Gogelein (2007). The results reinforce earlier findings by Mondal (2021) and Xie (2020) that neutron star composition cannot be probed through observations of beta-equilibrium properties alone. The study also challenges the conclusions of Carvalho (2023), who used a non-linear RMF model with a narrow proton fraction range to train machine learning algorithms for composition extraction, suggesting such approaches may be biased by the limited compositional freedom of the training models. The work enables future investigations of non-equilibrium phenomena such as neutron star cooling and transport properties, which are necessary to distinguish between these model classes.
