# Perturbative QCD Results on Pion Production in pp, pA and AA Collisions

> We summarize new pQCD results on pion production in proton-proton (pp), proton-nucleus (pA) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions. Our calculation introduces intrinsic parton transverse momentum (k_T) and is performed effectively at next-to-leading order (NLO), applying a K factor extracted for jet events. Two different factorization scales, Q=p_{T,jet}/2 and p_{T,jet} are used. Experimental data in pA collisions imply a preference for the latter choice at NLO level. We display our results at CERN SPS for AA collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents pQCD calculations for pion production that introduce intrinsic parton transverse momentum (k_T) and use a K factor extracted for jet events to approximate NLO corrections; the choice of factorization scale Q = p_T,jet (rather than p_T,jet/2) is preferred based on experimental data in pA collisions, but this preference is conditional on the NLO level of calculation and the specific parameterization of the Cronin effect.

## Method

The authors calculate pion production in pp, pA, and AA collisions using a pQCD-improved parton model with intrinsic parton transverse momentum distribution described by a Gaussian with width ⟨k_T²⟩. They extract an energy- and transverse-momentum-dependent K factor from full NLO jet calculations by the Ellis-Kunszt-Soper (EKS) group using MRST PDFs, then apply this K factor to Born-level partonic cross sections. For pA and AA collisions, they incorporate nuclear effects including shadowing via a scale-independent parameterization from Wang (1991) and additional k_T broadening from multiple semihard collisions parameterized by a constant C and maximum collision number ν_max.

## Key numbers

- The extracted K factor at scale Q = p_T,jet/2 is parameterized as K_jet(s, p_T,jet) = 1.6 + 20/√s - 24/(√s - 10)² · p_T,jet + 6/(√s - 10)² · p_T,jet², valid for 20 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 200 GeV
- The extracted K factor at scale Q = p_T,jet is parameterized as K_jet(s, p_T,jet) = 1.79 + 20/√s - 45/(√s - 7)² · p_T,jet + 7/(√s - 9)² · p_T,jet²
- At RHIC energy (√s = 200 AGeV), the K factor is approximately 1.7 at Q = p_T,jet/2 and approximately 1.9 at Q = p_T,jet
- The Cronin enhancement parameter C = 0.35 GeV² and ν_max = 3
- For CERN SPS energies, ⟨k_T²⟩_pp = 1.6 GeV² at E_beam = 158 AGeV and 1.7 GeV² at E_beam = 200 AGeV
- In Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS, the pQCD calculation overestimates data by up to 40%
- The Cronin peak appears in the transverse momentum window 2 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 6 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier LO calculations by Barnafoldi et al. (2002) and Levai et al. (2000) by introducing a proper NLO treatment via a jet-level K factor rather than a constant K=2 approximation. The paper builds on the discovery of the Cronin effect by Cronin (1975) and Antreasyan (1979), and uses the EKS NLO jet code with MRST PDFs. The results enable quantitative comparison with CERN SPS data from WA80 and WA98 experiments, and the observed 30-40% overestimate in central Pb+Pb collisions points to the need for including jet quenching effects, which became prominent at RHIC energies in PHENIX and STAR data.
