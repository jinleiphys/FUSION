# Structure of even-even Cadmium isotopes from the beyond-mean-field interacting boson model

> The structure of even-even $^{108-116}$Cd isotopes is investigated based on the self-consistent mean-field approach. By mapping the quadrupole-$(β,γ)$ deformation energy surface, obtained from the constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations with a choice of the Skyrme force and pairing property, onto the Hamiltonian of the interacting boson model with configuration mixing, the strength parameters of the Hamiltonian are determined. The low-lying excitation spectra and electric quadrupole transition rates for the considered Cd nuclei are computed by the resultant Hamiltonian, and are compared in detail with the experimental data. Our semi-microscopic prediction identifies several intruder states as suggested empirically, and overall, provides a reasonable qualitative description of the experimental energy levels and transition rates.

## Key claim

The semi-microscopic approach, which maps self-consistent mean-field deformation energy surfaces onto an interacting boson model Hamiltonian without adjusting parameters to experimental data, provides a reasonable qualitative description of low-lying states and transitions in even-even Cadmium isotopes, but systematically overestimates the energy of intruder states and predicts normal states that are too deformed compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations using the Skyrme SLy6 parametrization and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force (strength V0=1000 MeV fm³) to obtain quadrupole deformation energy surfaces in the (β,γ) plane for ¹⁰⁸–¹¹⁶Cd. These energy surfaces were then mapped onto the expectation value of a configuration-mixing IBM-2 Hamiltonian in the boson coherent state, determining all Hamiltonian strength parameters without fitting to experimental data. The resulting Hamiltonian, which includes both normal (0p-0h) and intruder (2p-2h) proton configurations, was diagonalized to compute excitation spectra, E2 transition rates, and E0 transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The global minimum for all considered Cd nuclei is prolate with axial deformation β ≈ 0.15
- The pairing strength used in the main calculation is V0 = 1000 MeV fm³; a comparison calculation for ¹¹²Cd uses V0 = 1250 MeV fm³
- The mixing strength ω in the Hamiltonian is fixed at ω = 0.15 MeV
- The energy offset Δ between normal and intruder configurations ranges from 1.804 MeV (¹¹²Cd) to 2.811 MeV (¹¹⁶Cd)
- The boson effective charges used are e^ν₁ = e^π₁ = 0.084 eb for normal states and e^ν₃ = e^π₃ = 0.113 eb for intruder states
- For ¹¹²Cd, the experimental B(E2; 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) = 51(14) W.u. is underpredicted by theory as 4.5 W.u.
- For ¹¹⁴Cd, the experimental ρ²(E0; 0⁺₂ → 0⁺₁) = 19(2)×10⁻³ compares with a theoretical value of 12×10⁻³
- The intruder fraction in the 0⁺₃ state ranges from 5% (¹⁰⁸Cd) to 82% (¹¹⁶Cd), with a maximum mixing of 55% in ¹¹²Cd and 52% in ¹¹⁴Cd

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent mean-field to IBM mapping approach of Nomura (2008, 2012) to the even-even Cd isotopes, which have been extensively studied experimentally (Jolie et al., various years) and phenomenologically with IBM-2 (various authors, 1990s–2010s). The calculations aim to provide a microscopic foundation for the intruder states first identified in transfer experiments (Cohen, 1961; Andersen, 1977) and systematically mapped by Heyde (1987). The results enable future improvements by showing that the current Skyrme force and pairing prescription produce too-deformed normal states and too-high intruder energies, pointing to the need for better microscopic input or inclusion of additional degrees of freedom.
