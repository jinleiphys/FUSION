# Deformation of Jets Induced by Ambient Medium Flow

> The evolution of jets showers in high energy nuclear collisions is influenced in various ways by the presence of a surrounding medium. The interaction of jet constituents with the medium can happen during the partonic stage of the jet, during hadronization, and even during its hadronic stage. We demonstrate how flow of the ambient medium in a direction transverse to the jet can introduce both dipole and quadrupole defomations. We propose to analyze the $n=1$ and $n=2$ harmonic deformations of soft and semi-hard hadrons or subjets in a jet with respect to the jet core using the method of $q$-vectors. We discuss simulations which show how the transverse shapes and their preferred angles evolve when the ambient environment of jets changes from the vacuum to a parton medium without flow and finally to a medium with various rates of transverse flow. Our study includes the effects of both flow during the development of the parton shower and hadronization. The existence of dipole deformations, and the correlation of the angles of dipole and quadrupole deformations could constitute promising experimental signals for the presence and size of ambient transverse flow.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that transverse flow of the ambient medium in high-energy nuclear collisions can induce both dipole (n=1) and quadrupole (n=2) deformations in the transverse shape of jets, but caution that the flow velocities used in their simulations (up to v=0.8) are likely larger than those expected in realistic scenarios, meaning observable deformations may be subtle.

## Method

The authors used the JETSCAPE 3.0 Monte Carlo framework with MATTER for parton showering and LBT for parton propagation through quark-gluon plasma, implementing ambient flow through Lorentz boosts to and from the local fluid rest frame. Hadronization was performed using Hybrid Hadronization, which allows recombination of shower partons with sampled medium partons. They simulated jets with initial momentum 100 GeV in a static QGP "brick" at temperature T=300 MeV and length 4 fm, with transverse flow velocities of v=0, 0.4, and 0.8, and analyzed the angular distributions of soft subjets (momentum between 2 and 10 GeV, reclustered with radius R=0.1) using q-vector multipole analysis.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the static QGP brick: T = 300 MeV
- Brick size: 4 fm
- Initial jet momentum: 100 GeV
- Soft subjet momentum range: 2 to 10 GeV
- Subjet reclustering radius: R = 0.1
- Transverse flow velocities simulated: v = 0 (no flow), v = 0.4 (intermediate flow), and v = 0.8 (large flow)

## Context

This work extends recent efforts to incorporate flow and gradient effects into jet quenching theories, following earlier speculation by Armesto (2004) and more recent work by He (2020), Sadofyev (2021), Antiporda (2021), and Barata (2023). The authors note that Ref. JETSCAPE (2025) showed medium flow effects can be transferred to jets through in-medium hadronization. The proposed q-vector analysis method for jet-by-jet multipole deformations could enable a new type of tomography of the QGP fireball, though the authors emphasize that full Monte Carlo studies with realistic 3-D simulations of the soft background medium are needed next.
