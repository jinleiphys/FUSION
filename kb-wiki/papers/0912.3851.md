# Non-leptonic weak processes in spin-one color superconducting quark matter

> The non-leptonic weak processes $s+u\to u+d$ and $u+d \to s+u$ are known to dominate the dissipation mechanism responsible for the viscosity of strange quark matter in its normal phase. The rates of such processes remain unknown for many color superconducting phases of quark matter. In this paper, we partially fill up the gap by calculating the difference of the rates of the two non-leptonic weak processes in four transverse spin-one color superconducting phases of quark matter (slightly) out of $β$-equilibrium. The four phases studied are the color-spin locked phase, the polar phase, the planar phase and the {\em A}-phase. In the limit of vanishing color superconducting gap, we reproduce the known results in the normal phase. In the general case, the rates are suppressed relative to the normal phase. The degree of the suppression is determined by the structure of the gap function in momentum space, which in turn is determined by the pairing pattern of quarks. At low temperatures, the rate is dominated by the ungapped modes. In this limit, the strongest suppression of the rate occurs in the color-spin-locked phase, and the weakest is in the polar phase and the {\em A}-phase.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the net rate difference of the two non-leptonic weak processes (s+u → u+d and u+d → s+u) in four transverse spin-one color superconducting phases of quark matter slightly out of β-equilibrium, finding that the rates are suppressed relative to the normal phase with the degree of suppression determined by the momentum-space structure of the gap function.

## Method

The authors used the Kadanoff-Baym formalism to derive a kinetic equation for the d-quark Green function, from which they obtained the net rate of d-quark production. They calculated the W-boson polarization tensor using the explicit quark propagators in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism for each of the four spin-one color superconducting phases: color-spin locked (CSL), polar, planar, and A-phase. The net rate was then evaluated numerically using Monte-Carlo integration methods, with the results expressed as a function of the dimensionless ratio φ/T.

## Key numbers

- The energy gap in spin-one color superconductors is estimated to be in the range from 0.01 MeV to 1 MeV
- In the CSL phase at low temperatures (φ/T → ∞), the asymptotic rate is λ^(CSL) ≈ (928/27027) λ₀ ≈ 0.034 λ₀, where λ₀ = (64 G_F² V_ud² V_us²)/(5π³) μ⁵ T² is the rate in normal phase quark matter
- The subleading correction to the CSL asymptotic rate is suppressed by an exponentially small factor exp(-√2 φ/T)
- The normal phase rate λ₀ contains the factor μ⁵ T², where μ is the quark chemical potential and T is the temperature

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of weak process rates in color superconducting quark matter, which had been performed for the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase by Jaikumar (2002), Reddy (2002), Alford (2007), and others, and for the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase by Alford (2006) and Jaikumar (2005). The Urca process rates in spin-one color superconducting phases had been calculated by Schmitt (2005) and Sa'd (2006), but the non-leptonic rates remained unknown until this work. The results enable calculations of bulk viscosity in spin-one color superconducting quark matter, which affects the damping of rotational r-mode instabilities in neutron stars.
