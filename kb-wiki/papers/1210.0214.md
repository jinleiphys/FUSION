# Density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction from UIX three-nucleon force

> A density-dependent two-nucleon potential has been derived in the formalism of correlated basis function. The effects of 3-particle interactions has been included by integrating out the degrees of freedom of the third nucleon. The potential can be easily employed in nuclear matter calculations. It yields results in agreement with those obtained from the underlying three-body potential. The use of the density dependent potential allowed us to study the effects of three-nucleon interactions in symmetric nuclear matter within the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) computational scheme.

# Density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction from UIX three-nucleon force

## Key claim
A density-dependent two-nucleon potential derived from the UIX three-nucleon force, using correlated basis function theory and cluster expansion techniques, reproduces the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter obtained with the full three-body potential, but only when both dynamical and statistical nucleon-nucleon correlations are properly included; the reduction is not a simple averaging but requires accounting for correlation diagrams up to next-to-leading order.

## Method
The authors derived an effective density-dependent two-nucleon potential by integrating out the degrees of freedom of the third nucleon from the UIX three-nucleon force, using the correlated basis function (CBF) formalism and Fantoni-Rosati cluster expansion. The expectation values of the three-body potential were equated to those of a two-body density-dependent potential, with the two-body distribution functions computed in the FHNC/SOC (Fermi-Hyper-Netted-Chain/Single Operator Chain) approximation. The resulting potential was then implemented in Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) with 28 nucleons and pure neutron matter (PNM) with 66 nucleons in periodic boxes.

## Key numbers
- SNM saturation density from FHNC/SOC with $v_6^\prime + v(\rho)$: $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$; binding energy $E_0 = -11.2$ MeV; compressibility $K = 192$ MeV
- SNM saturation density from FHNC/SOC with $v_8^\prime + v(\rho)$: $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$; $E_0 = -10.3$ MeV; $K = 198$ MeV
- SNM saturation density from AFDMC with $v_6^\prime + v(\rho)$: $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$; $E_0 = -10.9$ MeV; $K = 201$ MeV
- Finite-size errors in PNM AFDMC calculations do not exceed 2% of the asymptotic energy value
- Finite-size errors in SNM AFDMC calculations estimated at approximately 7% (from comparison of 14-neutron PNM with twist-averaged boundary conditions)
- A gap between UIX and density-dependent potential results appears in SNM above $\rho = 0.32$ fm$^{-3}$

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological TNI (three-nucleon interaction) approach of Lagaris and Pandharipande (1981) and Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), which fitted density-dependent parameters to saturation properties, by instead deriving the density-dependent potential from the microscopic UIX three-body force. The method improves on earlier AFDMC results (Schmidt and Fantoni, 1999) that did not reproduce the binding energy from FHNC calculations. The derived potential enables AFDMC simulations of SNM with three-nucleon forces for the first time, and can be applied to other many-body methods such as G-matrix and self-consistent Green function theories (Baldo, Polls, Dickhoff). The authors note that the UIX potential itself may be deficient, as it does not reproduce both the nd scattering length and vector polarization observables (Kievsky, 2012).
