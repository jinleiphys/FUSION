# Lorentz Contracted Proton

> The proton charge and magnetization density distributions can be related to the well known Sachs electromagnetic form factors $G_{E,M}({\bm q}^{2})$ through Fourier transforms, only in the Breit frame. The Breit frame however moves with relativistic velocities in the Lab and a Lorentz boost must be applied to the form factors before extracting the static properties of the proton from the corresponding densities. Apart from this, the Fourier transform relating the densities and form factors is inherently a non-relativistic expression. We show that the relativistic corrections to it can be obtained by extending the standard Breit equation to higher orders in its $1/c^2$ expansion. We find that the inclusion of the above corrections reduces the size of the proton determined from electron proton scattering data. Indeed the central value of the latest proton radius of $r_p = 0.879$ fm as determined from e-p scattering changes to $r_p = 0.8404$ fm after applying corrections.

## Key claim

The authors show that applying relativistic corrections (via a higher-order Breit equation) and a Lorentz boost to the proton charge density extracted from electron-proton scattering reduces the extracted proton radius from 0.879 fm to 0.8404 fm, bringing it close to the muonic hydrogen spectroscopy value of 0.84087(39) fm and thus partly resolving the "proton radius puzzle." The relativistic corrections alone increase the radius, while the Lorentz boost reduces it, and the combination of both effects produces the net reduction.

## Method

The authors extended the standard Breit equation (which involves a 1/c² expansion of the electron-proton scattering amplitude) to higher orders in 1/c², up to 1/c⁶, using Foldy-Wouthuysen transformed Dirac spinors with coefficients up to order p⁶/m⁶c⁶. From this higher-order Breit equation, they extracted the relativistically corrected proton electric potential in momentum space, then used the Poisson equation to obtain the modified charge density ρ_C(q²) and magnetization density ρ_M(q²), which depend on both the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M. They then applied a Lorentz boost factor [1 + (q²/4m_p²c²)]^λ to transform the densities from the Breit frame to the proton rest frame, with λ_E and λ_M as model-dependent parameters.

## Key numbers

- The central value of the proton radius from e-p scattering, r_p = 0.879 fm (Bernauer et al.), changes to r_p^L = 0.8404 fm after applying only the Lorentz boost with λ_E = 1
- The muonic hydrogen Lamb shift value is r_p = 0.84087(39) fm (Antognini et al.)
- The CODATA value is r_p = 0.8768(69) fm
- For the dipole parametrization (Bosted), the uncorrected radius is 0.811 fm; with relativistic corrections up to 1/c⁴ it becomes 0.831 fm; with both corrections and λ_E = 1 it becomes 0.790 fm
- For Alber Fit I, the uncorrected radius is 0.884 fm; with both corrections and λ_E = 2 it becomes 0.826 fm
- For Alber Fit II, the uncorrected radius is 0.866 fm; with both corrections and λ_E = 1 it becomes 0.847 fm
- For the Walcher parametrization, the uncorrected radius is 0.858 fm; with both corrections and λ_E = 1 it becomes 0.839 fm
- The proton magnetic radius r_M = 0.87 fm changes to r_M = 0.865 fm after relativistic and Lorentz boost corrections

## Context

This work addresses the "proton radius puzzle" arising from the discrepancy between the CODATA value of 0.8768(69) fm (based largely on e-p scattering and hydrogen spectroscopy) and the muonic hydrogen spectroscopy value of 0.84087(39) fm from Antognini et al. (2013). The authors extend earlier work by Rosenfelder (2000) and Friar (1979) on relativistic corrections to charge densities, and incorporate the Lorentz boost approach of Kelly (2002) who proposed using G_{E,M}^L(q²) = G_{E,M}(q²)[1 + (q²/4m_p²)]^λ with λ as a model-dependent parameter. The results enable a consistent framework for extracting proton moments from scattering data that brings e-p results into agreement with muonic hydrogen measurements.
