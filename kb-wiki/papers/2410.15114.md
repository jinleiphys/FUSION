# Beauty hadron production in high energy proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions in the EPOS4HQ framework

> Charmed hadron observables from the RHIC to LHC energies have been very successfully described with the recently advanced EPOS4HQ event generator. Here we extend this investigation to the production of beauty hadrons in proton-proton (pp) and heavy ion (HI) collisions

# Beauty hadron production in high energy proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions in the EPOS4HQ framework

## Key claim

The EPOS4HQ event generator, previously successful for charmed hadrons, can describe beauty hadron production in both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions, but the energy loss of bottom quarks in pp collisions is tiny due to their large mass, whereas in heavy-ion collisions the energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma is not negligible and leads to a clear modification of the transverse momentum spectra.

## Method

The EPOS4HQ framework generates heavy quark pairs via gluon splitting, flavor creation, and flavor excitation processes, with higher-order contributions from full space-time cascades. In spatial regions where the energy density of light partons exceeds 0.57 GeV/fm³, a quark-gluon plasma is formed and heavy quarks interact with it through elastic and inelastic processes. Bottom quarks inside the QGP hadronize via a mixture of coalescence and fragmentation using a Gaussian Wigner density whose width is adjusted to the root-mean-square radius of the bottom hadron wave function from a Schrödinger equation, while quarks outside the QGP hadronize by pure fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Energy density threshold for QGP formation: 0.57 GeV/fm³, applied in both pp and heavy-ion collisions
- Bottom hadron spectra calculated at √s = 5.02 TeV for pp collisions, with |y| < 2.4
- Branching ratios for B⁰ → D⁰X: ~51%; B⁻ → D⁰X: ~62%; total branching ratio for all decay channels to D⁰: 82%
- Non-prompt D⁰ elliptic flow v₂ in PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV is approximately half that of prompt D⁰
- Heavy-ion collisions studied at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for PbPb, with centralities 0-10% and 30-50%

## Context

This work extends the EPOS4HQ framework, previously applied to charmed hadron observables from RHIC to LHC energies by Zhao (2023, 2024), to beauty hadron production. The bottom quark results are compared with FONLL pQCD calculations by Cacciari (1998) and experimental data from CMS (2017, 2018) and ALICE (2021, 2022, 2023). The simultaneous description of prompt and non-prompt D⁰ mesons and their elliptic flow enables further constraints on heavy quark energy loss and hadronization mechanisms in high-energy nuclear collisions.
