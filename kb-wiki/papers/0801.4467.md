# Isospin effects in the thermodynamics of finite nuclei

> It has been proposed that multifragmentation can be related to the liquid-gas phase transition of nuclear matter. We study the statistical properties of finite nuclear matter near the phase transition with the help of a Lattice Gas Model (LGM). The original version of LGM with only one type of charge-neutral particles is well known to feature the properties of the liquid-gas phase transition. In this contribution, we address the effect of Coulomb and isospin dependence interaction for the finite nuclei transition, and study the symmetry energy properties of finite temperature systems.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the symmetry coefficient \(c_{sym}\) extracted from a Lattice Gas Model (LGM) with isospin and Coulomb interactions shows a strong variation with temperature, including a bump near the liquid-gas transition temperature, which contrasts with mean-field nuclear equation-of-state calculations; however, for isospin-symmetric systems without Coulomb, the entire temperature dependence of the volume and surface coefficients can be explained by the change in average density alone, even in the highly clusterized phase transition region.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of a cubic lattice gas model (linear site \(L=20\)) with four degrees of freedom per cell: a discrete isospin variable (\(\sigma_p=1, \sigma_n=-1, \sigma_0=0\)) and three continuous momentum variables. The Hamiltonian included nearest-neighbor isospin-dependent couplings (\(\epsilon_{-1}=5.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), Coulomb repulsion between all protons (\(I_c=1.44\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}\)), and a kinetic term (nucleon mass \(m=939\ \mathrm{MeV}\)). Calculations were done in the isobar canonical ensemble at subcritical pressure \(P=5\times10^{-5}\ \mathrm{MeV\cdot fm^{-3}}\), and a liquid-drop parametrization with four macroscopic coefficients (volume \(a_v\), surface \(a_s\), symmetry \(c_{sym}\), Coulomb \(\alpha_c\)) was fitted to the interaction energy of systems with \(A=150\) nucleons at various temperatures and isospin asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature for the \((75,75)\) scalar system (no isospin or Coulomb): \(T_t = 3.17\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(P=5\times10^{-5}\ \mathrm{MeV\cdot fm^{-3}}\).
- Transition temperature for the \((75,75)\) system with realistic isospin and Coulomb: \(T_t = 2.27\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at the same pressure.
- Transition temperatures for asymmetric systems at the same pressure: \((85,65)\): \(T_t = 2.285\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \((88,62)\): \(T_t = 2.290\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \((91,59)\): \(T_t = 2.295\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The isospin dependence of the transition temperature is weak, less than a few percent.
- The Coulomb interaction constant \(\alpha_c\) is temperature-independent; \(a_v\) and \(a_s\) decrease with increasing temperature; \(c_{sym}\) decreases with increasing temperature with a bump near \(T_t \approx 2.25\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the Lattice Gas Model (LGM) of Campi and collaborators, which originally used only one type of charge-neutral particle, by adding isospin-dependent short-range interactions and long-range Coulomb repulsion. The study aims to clarify the connection between the symmetry energy coefficient \(c_{sym}\) appearing in mean-field equations of state (parameterized as \(c_{sym}(\rho) = c_{sym}^0 (\rho/\rho_0)^\gamma\) by Chen and others) and the isoscaling parameter \(\alpha\) measured in heavy-ion collision experiments (Tsang, Shetty, Lefevre, Botvina). The finding that density dependence alone may explain the temperature behavior of macroscopic coefficients in symmetric systems suggests that multifragmentation experiments could access the density dependence of \(c_{sym}\), as actively searched for in recent analyses by Shetty, Lefevre, and Botvina. The connection between isoscaling observables and the symmetry energy within this model is reported as work in progress.
