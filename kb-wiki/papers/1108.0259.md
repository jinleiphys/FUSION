# Study of the strong $ОЈ_b\to О›_b\, ПЂ$ and $ОЈ_b^{*}\to О›_b\, ПЂ$ in a non-relativistic quark model

> We present results for the strong widths corresponding to the $ОЈ_b\to О›_b\, ПЂ$ and $ОЈ_b^{*}\to О›_b\, ПЂ$ decays. We apply our model in Ref. Phys. Rev. D 72, 094022 (2005) where we previously studied the corresponding transitions in the charmed sector. Our non-relativistic constituent quark model uses wave functions that take advantage of the constraints imposed by heavy quark symmetry. Partial conservation of axial current hypothesis allows us to determine the strong vertices from an analysis of the axial current matrix elements.

## Key claim
The authors compute strong decay widths for ОЈb в†’ О›b ПЂ and ОЈb* в†’ О›b ПЂ using a non-relativistic constituent quark model with heavy quark symmetry (HQS) constrained wave functions and the partial conservation of axial current (PCAC) hypothesis, finding that the dimensionless coupling constants for bottom and charm sectors agree at the 1вЂ“2% level, consistent with HQS expectations.

## Method
The authors extend their earlier model from the charm sector (Phys. Rev. D 72, 094022, 2005) to bottom baryons. They solve the three-body problem variationally using HQS-constrained wave functions from a prior study (albertus04) and determine the strong pion-emission amplitude via PCAC, relating it to the divergence of the axial current matrix element. The decay widths are then computed from the extracted coupling constants using experimental baryon masses where available, with the ОЈb0 mass taken from an HQET prediction (Hwang 2008).

## Context
This work extends the authors' non-relativistic quark model from the charm sector (Phys. Rev. D 72, 094022, 2005) to bottom baryons, providing predictions for ОЈb and ОЈb* strong decays into О›b ПЂ. The results are compared with earlier calculations using the MIT bag model combined with chiral dynamics (hwang07), Bethe-Salpeter formalism (guo08), non-relativistic 3P0 quark models (Chen 2007, Faessler 2010, Limphirat 2010), light cone QCD sum rules (azizi09, azizi11, azizi11-2), and an HQET-based estimate by the CDF Collaboration (2007). The authors' widths are systematically 17вЂ“38% larger than those of hwang07, lie in the lower part of the range from guo08, and are 10вЂ“20% larger than those of Faessler (2010) and Limphirat (2010). The results enable tests of HQS scaling predictions and can be compared with future LHC measurements of bottom baryon decays.
