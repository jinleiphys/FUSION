# Strange Baryonic Matter from Chiral Effective Lagrangians

> We investigate the existence of bound states of baryons in a kaon condensate using chiral mean field theory. The interactions are described by an effective SU(3)_L X SU(3)_R chiral lagrangian where terms of higher order in density, baryon momentum, and kaon mass are suppressed by powers of the symmetry breaking scale, Lambda. We take up to next to leading order terms (n = 2,3,4). We search for infinite baryon number solutions, namely ``strange baryonic matter'', using a Thomas-Fermi approximation for a slowly varying condensate and a lowest order Hartree approximation to describe the many body interactions. For simplicity we study a pure K^0 condensate and only neutrons, the lightest baryons in that condensate. We find solutions with neutron number densities, rho_n > 3.5 rho_0, where rho_0 is the infinite nuclear matter density. This is consistent with the estimate of the onset of a K-condensate at rho_n = 2-4 rho_0. We show that the binding energies, E_b, grow with rho_n and for rho_n < 7 rho_0 (at rho_n > 7 rho_0 perturbative expansion is lost) we find E_b < 150 MeV (E_b < 70 MeV for rho_n < 5 rho_0) even in the most favorable cases. These binding energies may be too low for this type of matter to appear and persist in the early universe.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether bound states of baryons in a kaon condensate can exist as "strange baryonic matter" with infinite baryon number, but find that the binding energies may be too low for this type of matter to appear and persist in the early universe, even in the most favorable cases.

## Method

The authors use a chiral SU(3)L x SU(3)R effective Lagrangian with terms up to next-to-leading order (n = 2, 3, 4), applying a Thomas-Fermi approximation for a slowly varying K0 condensate and a lowest order Hartree approximation for many-body interactions. They search for infinite baryon number solutions with only neutrons in a pure K0 condensate, imposing conditions of zero effective potential at both the interior condensate value and the true vacuum to find solutions with constant density over large volumes.

## Key numbers

- Neutron number densities found are rho_n > 3.5 rho_0, where rho_0 is infinite nuclear matter density (1.28 x 10^6 MeV^3)
- For rho_n < 7 rho_0, binding energies E_b < 150 MeV; for rho_n < 5 rho_0, E_b < 70 MeV
- The onset of K-condensation is estimated at rho_n = 2-4 rho_0
- The expansion parameter rho / f_pi^2 Lambda is approximately rho / 7 rho_0, with f_pi = 93 MeV and Lambda approximately 1 GeV
- The parameter a_3 = 1.3 +/- 0.2, with a higher value of a_3 = 1.5 giving solutions at lower densities
- The neutron effective mass parameter a_t = 2a_3 + a_2 + a_1 = 2.32 +/- 0.4

## Context

This work extends the study of kaon condensation in dense baryonic matter, previously investigated by Lynn, Nelson, and Tetradis (LNT) who used a phenomenological combination of a chiral Lagrangian with the Walecka model. The authors differ from LNT by relying on a perturbative expansion dictated by broken chiral symmetry, including four-baryon terms and higher-order terms with six baryon fields, rather than introducing separate Walecka fields. The results are consistent with the estimated onset density for K-condensation but suggest that strange baryonic matter may not have sufficient binding to persist in the early universe, motivating further study of solutions with more baryonic degrees of freedom.
