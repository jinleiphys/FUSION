# Chiral symmetry breaking and the generation of light hadron masses

> A quantitative study of the effects of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking on the mass generation of the low-lying hadrons in a dynamical lattice QCD simulation is presented. The evolution of light hadron masses upon increasing the number of low-lying Dirac operator eigenmodes in the quark propagators is confronted with the hadron masses obtained upon removal of such low-lying eigenmodes from standard full quark propagators. The low-lying chiral symmetry breaking modes provide roughly two third of the nucleon and $ρ$ masses, while the $a_1$ mass is affected to a much smaller degree.

## Key claim

The low-lying chiral symmetry breaking modes of the Dirac operator provide roughly two thirds of the nucleon and rho meson masses, but the a1 meson mass is affected to a much smaller degree; this conclusion is based on comparing hadron masses constructed from only the low-lying eigenmodes versus masses from propagators with those modes removed.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical lattice QCD simulation with n_f=2 chirally improved fermions on a 16^3 x 32 lattice. They decomposed the quark propagator into eigenmodes of the Hermitian Dirac operator and constructed both "low mode" propagators (using only the k lowest-lying eigenmodes) and "reduced" propagators (full propagator minus the k lowest-lying eigenmodes) at truncation levels k = 32, 64, and 128. Hadron masses were extracted from correlation functions using a variational method with multiple interpolators for the rho, a1, and nucleon channels.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.144(1) fm
- Pion mass in the ensemble: M_pi = 322(5) MeV
- Truncation energies for k = 32, 64, and 128: 42, 71, and 117 MeV respectively
- The 32 lowest-lying modes provide roughly two thirds of the rho and nucleon masses (no explicit numerical mass values given for these hadrons)
- The remaining one third of the rho and nucleon masses comes from higher-lying eigenmodes not related to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking

## Context

This work extends previous qualitative lattice studies by Neff (2001), DeGrand (2003), and Bali (2010) that investigated the role of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in hadron masses. It follows the authors' own prior work (Lang 2011, Glozman 2012) where chiral symmetry was artificially restored by removing low-lying modes from valence quark propagators. The new contribution is the construction and analysis of hadrons from the low-mode part exclusively, enabling a quantitative comparison of the contribution of chiral symmetry breaking modes to different hadrons. This enables future studies to separate the contributions of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking from confinement in hadron mass generation.
