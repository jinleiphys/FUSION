# Hadron formation in the deconfined matter at RHIC and LHC

> We have studied the probability of two succinct particle production mechanisms, likely to occur in the transition from the deconfined medium produced in RHIC and LHC heavy ion collisions back to the hadron gas, which is abundant at lower temperatures. Evidence has been found for in-medium fragmentation of non-equilibrated partons in the deconfined medium as well as bound state formation in the quark-gluon phase itself, as described by lattice QCD . Through the comparison to PNJL model calculations we attempt to quantify an extended phase of mixed degrees of freedom in a temperature range just above the QCD transition temperature. The extracted hadron formation probabilities exhibit flavor and baryon number dependencies, which are experimentally verifiable. We confront the calculations of the non-equilibrium and equilibrium particle production in heavy ion collisions with measurements from RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the transition from deconfined matter to hadrons is not a single-temperature process but involves an extended mixed phase (1–2 \(T_c\)) where hadron formation probabilities depend on flavor and baryon number, with strange and baryonic bound states surviving to higher temperatures than light mesonic states.

## Method
The study compares lattice QCD results for quark number susceptibilities (light and strange) with Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model calculations that include fluctuations of the Polyakov loop, chiral condensates, and mesonic condensates. The difference between lattice and PNJL results is used to quantify the contribution of hadronic bound states above the QCD transition temperature. The lattice data are also compared to hadron resonance gas (HRG) model predictions to separate mesonic and baryonic contributions.

## Key numbers
- The QCD transition temperature range is quoted as 150–200 MeV from earlier lattice calculations.
- The transition region extends up to 2 \(T_c\) before reaching the fully deconfined and chirally symmetric state.
- The physical quark mass ratio used in the latest lattice calculations is \(m_s/m_{u,d} = 28.15\), compared to a ratio of 10 in earlier results.
- Non-diagonal \(u-s\) susceptibilities exhibit a pronounced peak near \(T_c\) and remain finite for temperatures up to 1–2 \(T_c\).
- The PNJL model at mean-field level yields a flat \(u-s\) correlator equal to zero over the full temperature range; including fluctuations brings the curve closer to lattice data.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of the QCD phase transition (Aoki 2006; Borsanyi 2010) and PNJL model calculations (Skokov 2010) by focusing on flavor-dependent hadron formation in the cross-over region. It contradicts the assumption of a single hadronization temperature and instead supports the “semi-QGP” concept (Hidaka 2008). The findings enable experimental tests via particle-identified measurements at RHIC and LHC, such as the ALICE observations of flavor-dependent nuclear suppression factors and particle yield ratios that cannot be described by a single-temperature statistical hadronization model (Andronic).
