# From wave-function to fireball geometry: the role of a restored broken symmetry in ultra-relativistic collisions of deformed nuclei

> Traditional Monte Carlo (MC) Glauber models treat the shapes of deformed nuclei classically; that is, in a collision event, each nucleus is randomly assigned a configuration with fixed deformation parameters and orientation (collective coordinates). Quantum mechanically, however, a valid nuclear ground state must be a superposition of these configurations to preserve rotational symmetry, creating entanglement in the nucleon wave function. We show that for collisions with a moderate number of participants, this quantum superposition -- particularly in the orientation of deformed nuclei -- has non-negligible impacts on the initial geometry of the quark-gluon plasma fireball. We propose a minimal extension to the MC Glauber model to approximately incorporate these superposition effects and find a $6$-$8\%$ reduction in the second-order eccentricity in central Ne-Ne and Ne-Pb collisions relative to results from classical treatments of nuclear orientation. Though these effects are expected to be small for large collision systems such as U-U, this study demonstrates the importance of properly accounting for quantum superposition and the associated off-diagonal information when studying nuclear deformation in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for collisions with a moderate number of participants (light nuclei such as Ne and Si), the quantum superposition of deformed nuclear orientations, which is required to restore rotational symmetry in the nuclear ground state, has non-negligible impacts on the initial geometry of the quark-gluon plasma fireball, producing a 6-8% reduction in second-order eccentricity relative to classical treatments that assign fixed orientations event-by-event.

## Method

The author proposes a minimal extension to the Monte Carlo Glauber model that incorporates off-diagonal information from the nuclear density matrix by introducing a decorrelation function over collective coordinates and a generalized thickness function built from the product of square-root density profiles at two different orientations. The modified model samples two sets of orientations for each nucleus according to their quantum overlap, then computes the inelastic collision probability and resulting fireball eccentricities using the TRENTo parameterization for energy deposition.

## Key numbers

- 6-8% reduction in second-order eccentricity \(\sqrt{\langle|\epsilon_2|^2\rangle}\) in central Ne-Pb collisions when comparing the quantum-superposition-improved MC Glauber model to the classical MC Glauber model
- Approximately 5% reduction in \(\sqrt{\langle|\epsilon_2|^2\rangle}\) for oblate deformed \({}^{28}\)Si in central Si+Pb collisions
- About 6% reduction in \(\sqrt{\langle|\epsilon_2|^2\rangle}\) for central Ne+Ne and Si+Si collisions
- About 2% reduction in \(\sqrt{\langle|\epsilon_3|^2\rangle}\) for central Ne+Ne and Si+Si collisions
- 20-40% alteration in the symmetric cumulant \(\mathrm{NS}_{\epsilon,23}\) (correlation between \(n=2\) and \(n=3\) eccentricities) in central Ne+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the standard MC Glauber model (Miller 2007, Shou 2014, Loizides 2025) by addressing a fundamental quantum-mechanical question: how fireball geometry emerges from nuclear wave functions that must preserve rotational symmetry. It builds on the growing connection between ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and nuclear structure studies, including deformation measurements in isobaric Zr/Ru collisions (Zhang 2021), \({}^{238}\)U shape determination (Ryssens 2023, STAR 2024), and proposals to study light nuclei such as O and Ne at RHIC and LHC (Giacalone 2024, ALICE 2025, ATLAS 2025). The results enable more precise extraction of nuclear deformation parameters from flow observables, particularly for light nuclei where the 6-8% effect may alter interpretation of measured elliptic and triangular flow ratios.
