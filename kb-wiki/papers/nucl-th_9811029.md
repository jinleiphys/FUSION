# n + p -> d + gamma in Effective Field Theory

> The radiative capture process n+p-> d+gamma provides clear evidence for meson exchange currents in nuclear physics. We compute this process at low energies using a recently developed power counting for the effective field theory that describes nucleon-nucleon interactions. The leading order contribution to this process comes from the photon coupling to the nucleon magnetic moments. At subleading order there are other contributions. Among these are graphs where the photon couples directly to pions, i.e. meson exchange currents. These diagrams are divergent and require the presence of a local four-nucleon-one photon counterterm. The coefficient of this operator is determined by the measured cross section, 334.2 +- 0.5 mb, for incident neutrons with speed |v|=2200 m/s.

## Key claim

The leading-order effective field theory calculation of the n + p → d + γ cross section recovers the effective range theory result (297.2 mb), which is about 10% smaller than the experimental value; at next-to-leading order, the meson exchange current contribution is divergent and requires a local four-nucleon-one-photon counterterm whose coefficient is determined by fitting to the measured cross section, making the meson exchange current contribution scheme-dependent and not uniquely defined.

## Method

The authors compute the radiative capture cross section using the power counting of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for nucleon-nucleon effective field theory, where the leading-order amplitude comes from the isovector magnetic moment interaction in the 1S0 channel. At next-to-leading order, they include perturbative insertions of two-derivative four-nucleon operators (C2), quark-mass-dependent operators (D2), potential pion exchange with the photon coupling both to nucleon magnetic moments and minimally to pions (meson exchange currents), and a local L1 counterterm. The calculation is performed in the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme, and the unknown L1 coefficient is fixed by requiring the NLO cross section to match the experimental value.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order cross section: σ^LO = 297.2 mb, about 10% below the experimental value
- Experimental cross section: σ^expt = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb for incident neutrons with speed |v| = 2200 m/s
- 1S0 scattering length: a^(1S0) = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm
- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.2255 MeV, corresponding to γ = sqrt(MB)
- Determined counterterm coefficient: L1(m_π) = 1.63 fm^4 (in PDS scheme with δ given by equation in text)
- Alternative scheme (overline MS with δ = -1/2) gives L1(m_π) = 3.03 fm^4
- NLO contributions at μ = m_π: Y^(C2) = 0.38, Y^(π,B) = -0.33, Y^(π,E) = 0.60

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for nucleon-nucleon interactions to electromagnetic processes, specifically the classic radiative capture reaction where meson exchange currents were first identified by Brown and Riska (1972). The calculation supersedes earlier effective field theory work by Park, Min, and Rho (1995) which used Weinberg's power counting with a momentum cutoff and resonance saturation, finding σ = 334 ± 2 mb. Having determined the L1 counterterm, the authors note that other electromagnetic processes such as deuteron breakup (e + d → e' + n + p) can now be computed at next-to-leading order.
