# Two and Three Nucleons in a Trap and the Continuum Limit

> We describe systems of two and three nucleons trapped in a harmonic-oscillator potential with interactions from the pionless effective field theory up to next-to-leading order (NLO). We construct the two-nucleon interaction using two-nucleon scattering information. We calculate the trapped levels in the three-nucleon system with isospin $T=1/2$ and determine the three-nucleon force needed for stability of the triton. We extract neutron-deuteron phase shifts, and show that the quartet scattering length is in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the pionless effective field theory (EFT) up to next-to-leading order (NLO) can be used to describe two- and three-nucleon systems confined in a harmonic-oscillator trap, with the trap serving as a calculational artifact that must be extrapolated to the continuum limit to extract physical observables such as scattering phase shifts.

## Method

The two-nucleon interaction was constructed from pionless EFT up to NLO, with the low-energy constants \(C_0\) and \(C_2\) fitted to reproduce the lowest two energy levels of the trapped two-nucleon system, which are related to the scattering length and effective range via the transcendental equation \(\Gamma(3/4-E_{2;n}/2\omega)/\Gamma(1/4-E_{2;n}/2\omega) = -\sqrt{E_{2;n}/2\omega} \cot\delta_2(\sqrt{m_N E_{2;n}})\). For the three-nucleon system with isospin \(T=1/2\), the Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a harmonic-oscillator basis using Jacobi coordinates, with separate cutoffs \(N_2^{max}\) for the two-body subsystem and \(N_3^{max}\) for the three-body system. Neutron-deuteron phase shifts were extracted from the trapped three-nucleon energy levels above the deuteron ground state using an analogous relation involving the reduced mass \(\mu_{nd}\).

## Key numbers

* Triplet two-nucleon scattering length \(a_{2t} = 5.425\) fm and effective range \(r_{2t} = 1.749\) fm, used as input for the two-body interaction in the \(^3S_1\) channel.
* Singlet two-nucleon scattering length \(a_{2s} = -18.7\) fm and effective range \(r_{2s} = 2.75\) fm, used as input for the \(^1S_0\) channel.
* Deuteron energy in free space at NLO: \(E^{free}_d = -2.213\) MeV; in the trap at \(\omega = 1\) MeV, the NLO deuteron energy is \(E_d = -2.123\) MeV, and at \(\omega = 0.1\) MeV it is \(E_d = -2.212\) MeV.
* For the \(J^\pi = 3/2^+\) channel at \(\omega = 3\) MeV, the extrapolated LO ground-state energy is \(E^{LO}_3(\infty) = 6.241\) MeV and the NLO value is \(E^{NLO}_3(\infty) = 6.417\) MeV.
* Experimental neutron-deuteron quartet scattering length: \(a_{3q} = 6.35 \pm 0.02\) fm; doublet scattering length: \(a_{3d} = 0.65 \pm 0.04\) fm.
* Triton ground-state energy: \(E_t = -8.482\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the trapped-system method previously applied to two-state fermions (Rotureau et al., 2010) and bosons (Hansen et al., 2011) to the nuclear three-nucleon system using pionless EFT. It builds on the leading-order (LO) calculation of Rotureau et al. (2009) that demonstrated the approach for the no-core shell model, and on the formal connection between trapped levels and scattering established by Stetcu et al. (2007) and Luu et al. (2010). The results enable future applications of the same method with pionful (chiral) EFT, where the breakdown scale \(M_{hi}\) is higher and denser nuclear systems can be handled.
