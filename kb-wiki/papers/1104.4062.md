# The Klein first integrals in an equilibrium system with electromagnetic, weak, strong and gravitational interactions

> The isothermal Tolman condition and the constancy of the Klein potentials originally expressed for the sole gravitational interaction in a single fluid are here generalized to the case of a three quantum fermion fluid duly taking into account the strong, electromagnetic, weak and gravitational interactions. The set of constitutive equations including the Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi equations as well as the ones corresponding to the strong interaction description are here presented in the most general relativistic isothermal case. This treatment represents an essential step to correctly formulate a self-consistent relativistic field theoretical approach of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the isothermal Tolman condition and the constancy of the Klein potentials from a single fluid with only gravitational interactions to a three-fluid system of neutrons, protons, and electrons that includes electromagnetic, weak, strong, and gravitational interactions, but only within the mean-field approximation and assuming the system is in thermodynamic equilibrium with a finite temperature.

## Method

The authors start from a total Lagrangian density that includes the gravitational field, free fermions (neutrons, protons, electrons), and meson fields (sigma, omega, rho) mediating the strong interaction, plus the electromagnetic field. They apply the mean-field approximation to replace fermion field operators by their expectation values, write the occupation numbers as Fermi-Dirac distributions, and derive the full set of Einstein-Maxwell equations coupled to the meson field equations and the Dirac equations for the fermions. From the energy-momentum conservation law and the generalized Gibbs-Duhem relation, they derive the constancy of the generalized chemical potentials (Klein potentials) for each particle species and the constancy of the gravitationally red-shifted temperature throughout the configuration.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear density: rho_nuc ~ 2.7 x 10^14 g/cm^3, given as the density exceeded in neutron star cores.
* Maximum density at the verge of gravitational collapse to a black hole: ~ 10^17 g/cm^3.
* Mass number of stellar-sized nuclear matter cores: A ~ (m_Planck / m_n)^3 ~ 10^57, corresponding to M_core ~ M_sun.
* Critical electric field value: E_c = m_e^2 c^3 / (e hbar), which is exceeded for electron Fermi energy E_e^F = 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous treatment of compressed atoms in the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation (Rueda, Ruffini, Xue, 2011) and their earlier generalization of the Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi system for neutrons, protons, and electrons in beta-equilibrium (Rueda, Ruffini, Xue, 2011). It also builds on the pioneering work of O. Klein (1949) on the constancy of general relativistic Fermi energies and the Tolman isothermal condition (Tolman, 1930). The paper enables a self-consistent relativistic field theoretical approach to neutron stars that goes beyond the traditional Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the assumption of local charge neutrality, incorporating strong interactions via the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974; Duerr, 1956) and its extended version with sigma, omega, and rho meson fields.
