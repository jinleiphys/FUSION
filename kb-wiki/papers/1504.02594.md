# Proton and neutron correlations in $^{10}$B

> We investigate positive-parity states of $^{10}$B with the calculation of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics focusing on $pn$ pair correlations. We discuss effects of the spin-orbit interaction on energy spectra and $pn$ correlations of the $J^πT=1^+_10$, $=3^+_10$, and $0^+_11$ states. The $1^+_10$ state has almost no energy gain of the spin-orbit interaction, whereas the $3^+_10$ state gains the spin-orbit interaction energy largely to come down to the ground state. We interpret a part of the two-body spin-orbit interaction in the adopted effective interactions as a contribution of the genuine $NNN$ force, and find it to be essential for the level ordering of the $3^+_10$ and $1^+_10$ states in $^{10}$B. We also apply a $2α+pn$ model to discuss effects of the spin-orbit interaction on $T=0$ and $T=1$ $pn$ pairs around the 2$α$ core. In the spin-aligned $J^πT=3^+0$ state, the spin-orbit interaction affects the $(ST)=(10)$ pair attractively and keeps the pair close to the core, whereas, in the $1^+0$ state, it gives a minor effect to the $(ST)=(10)$ pair. In the $0^+1$ state, the $(ST)=(01)$ pair is somewhat dissociated by the spin-orbit interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a part of the effective two-body spin-orbit interaction in the adopted phenomenological interactions can be interpreted as a contribution from the genuine three-nucleon (NNN) force, and that this contribution is essential for reproducing the correct level ordering of the \(3^+_10\) ground state and \(1^+_10\) first excited state in \(^{10}\)B, a result that depends on the strength of the spin-orbit interaction being sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations with variation after parity and total-angular-momentum projection (AMD+VAP) for positive-parity states of \(^{10}\)B, using the Volkov No.2 central interaction and the G3RS spin-orbit interaction. They also applied a simplified \(2\alpha+pn\) model to isolate the effects of the spin-orbit interaction on \(T=0\) and \(T=1\) proton-neutron pairs around the two-alpha core. The dependence of the energy spectra on the spin-orbit strength was studied by comparing results with \(u_{ls}=1300\) MeV and \(u_{ls}=1000\) MeV, and the change was associated with the NNN force contribution based on Kohno's G-matrix evaluation.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of \(^{10}\)B is \(3^+_10\) and the first excited state is \(1^+_10\) at \(E_x=0.72\) MeV experimentally.
* The default spin-orbit strength used is \(u_{ls}=1300\) MeV, corresponding to a Scheerbaum factor \(B_S=103\) MeV; the weaker strength is \(u_{ls}=1000\) MeV, corresponding to \(B_S=79\) MeV.
* The change in spin-orbit strength \(\Delta u_{ls}\sim 300\) MeV corresponds to a change in the Scheerbaum factor \(\Delta B_S = 24\) MeV, which is associated with the NNN force contribution.
* For the Volkov central interaction, the ratio of the \(^3\)E to \(^1\)E interaction is \(f=1.67\) for the default parametrization (\(b=h=0.125\)) and \(f=1.27\) for the modified one (\(b=h=0.06\)).
* The calculated binding energy of the \(3^+_10\) state is 58.7 MeV with interaction (A) and 57.7 MeV with interaction (B), compared to the experimental value of 64.751 MeV.
* The calculated proton radius of the \(3^+_10\) state is 2.31 fm (A) and 2.33 fm (B), compared to the experimental value of 2.28(5) fm.
* The calculated quadrupole moment of the \(3^+_10\) state is 7.95 e fm\(^2\) (A) and 8.2 e fm\(^2\) (B), compared to the experimental value of 8.47(6) e fm\(^2\).
* The calculated magnetic moment of the \(3^+_10\) state is 1.84 \(\mu_N\) (A) and 1.85 \(\mu_N\) (B), compared to the experimental value of 1.8006 \(\mu_N\).
* The calculated \(B(E2;1^+_10\rightarrow 3^+_10)\) is 4.2 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (A) and 3.6 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (B), compared to the experimental value of 4.14(2) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of \(^{18}\)F using an \(^{16}\)O+\(pn\) model (Kanada-En'yo and Kobayashi, 2014), which found that the spin-orbit interaction reduces the \(T=1\) pair energy in the \(0^+1\) state and lowers the spin-aligned \(T=0\) \(3^+0\) state. The present results for \(^{10}\)B are compared with no-core shell model calculations by Navratil et al. (2007) using chiral \(NN\)+\(NNN\) forces, which showed that the NNN force is essential for the level ordering of the \(3^+_10\) and \(1^+_10\) states. The paper also connects to the work of Kohno (2012), who evaluated that the NNN force contributes an attractive \(\Delta B_S = 20-30\) MeV to the effective two-body spin-orbit interaction in nuclear matter. This analysis enables a phenomenological interpretation of the effective spin-orbit strength in terms of NNN force contributions and clarifies the role of the spin-orbit interaction in determining the ground-state spin of \(Z=N=\)odd nuclei.
