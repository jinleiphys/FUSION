# Local Current Operators for Arbitrary Spin Particles

> Free current operators are constructed for massive particles with arbitrary spin $j$. Such current operators are related to representations of the U(N,N) type groups and are covariant under the (extended) Poincaré group and charge conjugation, where the charge conjugation operation is defined as an automorphism on U(N,N) elements. The currents are also required to satisfy current conservation, hermiticity, and locality. The condition that the currents be local is shown to be equivalent to certain integral constraints on form factors. These constraints are satisfied by writing the currents in terms of free local spin $j$ fields. It is shown that there are $(2j+1)$ different local currents for a spin $j$ particle, each with an arbitrary form factor, generalizing the Dirac and Pauli currents for spin 1/2 particles. Static properties of the various currents are also given.

# Local Current Operators for Arbitrary Spin Particles

## Key claim
Free current operators for massive particles of arbitrary spin $j$ can be constructed from representations of $U(N,N)$ type groups, satisfying Poincaré covariance, current conservation, hermiticity, locality, and charge conjugation invariance. The paper claims there are exactly $(2j+1)$ different local currents for a spin $j$ particle, each with an arbitrary form factor, but this result depends on constructing the currents from free local spin $j$ fields using generalized gamma matrices and appropriate numbers of derivatives.

## Method
The author constructs current operators as bilinears in creation and annihilation operators that form the Lie algebra of $U(N,N)$ groups, then imposes locality by requiring that the commutator of the current with itself vanishes for spacelike separation. Two classes of currents are built from $2(2j+1)$-component free fields using Weinberg's generalized gamma matrices: one class generalizes the spin-0 current (producing only electric form factors) by differentiating fields $2j$ times to contract with sigma tensor indices, and the other class generalizes the $\bar{\Psi}\gamma_\mu\Psi$ construction for spin-1/2 (producing both electric and magnetic form factors) by leaving one index uncontracted. The number of independent currents in each class depends on whether the spin is integer or half-integer.

## Key numbers
- There are $(2j+1)$ different local currents for a spin $j$ particle, each with an arbitrary form factor.
- For integer spin, there are $j+1$ electric-type currents (from the first construction) and $j$ magnetic-type currents (from the second construction); for half-integer spin, there are $j+1/2$ of each type.
- For spin 1/2, the electric form factor at $Q^2$ is $F^a_{\mu=0}(Q^2) = 4\cosh\alpha \, I$ and the magnetic form factor is $F^a_{\mu=i}(Q^2) = 2i(\hat{z}\times\vec{\sigma})_i \sinh\alpha$, where $Q^2 = 4m^2\sinh^2\alpha$.
- For spin 1 with $k=2$, the electric form factor matrix is $4\,\text{diag}(\cosh 2\alpha, 1, \cosh 2\alpha)$; with $k=1$, it is $2\,\text{diag}(1, \cosh 2\alpha, 1)$.
- For spin 3/2 with $k=3$, the electric form factor matrix is $4\,\text{diag}(\cosh 3\alpha, \cosh\alpha, \cosh\alpha, \cosh 3\alpha)$; with $k=2$, it is $4\cosh\alpha\,\text{diag}(1, 1+\frac{2}{3}\sinh^2\alpha, 1+\frac{2}{3}\sinh^2\alpha, 1)$.
- The momentum transfer squared is given by $Q^2/(4m^2) = \sinh^2\alpha$.

## Context
This work extends the construction of local current operators from free fields to arbitrary spin, generalizing the Dirac and Pauli currents for spin-1/2 particles. It builds on Weinberg's (1964) work on local fields for arbitrary spin particles and the generalized gamma matrices defined therein. The paper is the first in a series investigating many-body relativistic theories in the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics, where interactions are specified by four-momentum operators obtained by integrating vertices over the forward hyperboloid. The results enable the construction of electromagnetic vertices for arbitrary spin particles with arbitrary form factors, and allow investigation of acausal behavior of high-spin particles in external electromagnetic fields.
