# Isospin symmetry breaking of Cooper-Quartets in nuclei

> We present a new procedure of isospin symmetry breaking of cooper-quartets in nuclear matter using Ginzburg-Landau theory, namely we couple the cooper-quartets to the pion-meson field and $SU(2)$ gluons. The paper also describes phenomena resulting from this approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Ginzburg-Landau approach to isospin symmetry breaking of Cooper-quartets in nuclear matter by coupling the quartet field to the pion-meson field and SU(2) gluons, but the model achieves only approximately 80 percent accuracy for the rho meson mass (622.9 MeV versus the experimental 770 MeV), indicating the theory is not fully equipped to describe vector meson masses.

## Method

The author constructs a Lagrangian coupling a complex scalar quartet field, a pion-meson field (treated as a linear sigma model), and an SU(2) gluon field, with covariant derivatives containing both gluon and pion coupling terms. The theory is quantized to obtain Feynman rules, and one-loop renormalization is performed to derive beta functions for the coupling constants and mass parameters. The macroscopic model is connected to a microscopic Hamiltonian via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, and quasiparticle dispersion relations are obtained using mean-field theory and Bogoliubov transformations.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson mass predicted from the model is \( m_{\rho} \approx 622.9 \) MeV, compared to the experimental value \( m_{\rho}^{exp} = 770 \) MeV, using the pion decay constant \( f_{\pi} = 93 \) MeV and coupling constant \( g_{ANN} = 4.75 \)
- The beta function for the SU(2) gluon coupling constant is \( \mu \frac{d}{d\mu} g_{ANN} = \left( \frac{11}{3} C(g) - n_{\phi} \frac{1}{3} C(r) \right) \frac{-g_{ANN}^3}{16\pi^2} \) with \( n_{\phi} = 2 \) scalar fields (quartet and pion)
- The beta function for the pion-nucleon coupling is \( \mu \frac{d}{d\mu} g_{\pi NN} = \frac{1}{3} \frac{g_{\pi NN}^3}{16\pi^2} \)
- The quasiparticle dispersion relation is \( E_k = \sqrt{\epsilon^2 + g_I^2 V_I^2 |\Delta|^2} \)

## Context

This work extends the microscopic theory of Cooper-quartets developed in earlier studies (references 1-4 in the paper) by adding pion and gluon degrees of freedom through a Ginzburg-Landau effective field theory, drawing an analogy to the Ginzburg-Landau theory of superconductivity (reference 5). The approach connects to chiral perturbation theory and the linear sigma model (reference 20), and the microscopic Hamiltonian is analogous to d-wave superconductivity models in metals (reference 31) but with additional isospin and spin SU(2) symmetry. The model enables calculation of modified cross sections for pion-pion scattering inside nuclear matter and provides a framework for studying isospin symmetry breaking effects on nuclear stability, though the rho meson mass prediction requires improvement.
