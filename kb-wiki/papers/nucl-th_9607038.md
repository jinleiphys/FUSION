# Microcanonical Thermodynamics, ``Fragmentation Phase Transition'',and the Topology of the N-body Phase Space

> Microcanonical thermodynamics (MT) is analysed for phase transitions of first and second order in finite systems. The transiton temperature, the latent heat and the surface tension of first order transitions can easily be determined by MT from the caloric equation of state T(E). The outstandig problem of the physical meaning of its backbending is clarified for the Potts-model. The fragmentation phase transition for nuclear multifragmentation is examined in detail. Further experimental signals of the fragmentation transition in hot nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

Microcanonical Thermodynamics (MT) can unambiguously identify first- and second-order phase transitions in finite systems via the shape of the caloric equation of state T(E/N), and the fragmentation phase transition in hot nuclei is distinct from the liquid-gas transition in infinite nuclear matter, contrary to common belief.

## Method

The author analyzes the topology of the N-body phase space by computing the volume of accessible phase space (number of quantum states) as a function of conserved quantities (energy, angular momentum, mass, charge, linear momentum, and spatial volume). The entropy is defined as the logarithm of this phase-space volume, and the thermodynamic temperature is obtained from its derivative with respect to energy. The method is applied to the 2-dimensional 10-state Potts model using Microcanonical Thermodynamics and to nuclear fragmentation using the microcanonical statistical multifragmentation model (MMMC) with standard parameters, comparing results to experimental evaporation spectra from incomplete fusion reactions.

## Key numbers

- For the 2-dimensional 10-state Potts model on a 200x200 lattice, the transition temperature, specific latent heat, and specific interphase surface entropy were determined and found to be closer to asymptotic values than in the canonical ensemble for surprisingly small systems.
- For nuclear fragmentation of 131Xe, the caloric equation of state shows a pronounced anomaly with backbending at specific energy epsilon ~ 2.5 MeV and temperature T ~ 4.5 MeV, with a width (latent heat) q_lat ~ 1 MeV/nucleon.
- A second, less pronounced anomaly without backbending occurs at epsilon ~ 5 MeV and T ~ 6 MeV, with q_lat ~ 1.5 MeV/nucleon, possibly a second-order transition.
- The freeze-out radius for nuclear multifragmentation was taken as 2.2 A^(1/3) fm, corresponding to densities of about 1/6 normal nuclear density.
- The angular momentum of the source in the 701 MeV 28Si + 100Mo experiment ranged from L = 18.2 to 48.8 hbar.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier predictions of nuclear multifragmentation (Gross 1984, Bondorf 1981) and the review article proposing the caloric equation of state for 131Xe (Gross 1995). It contrasts with conventional canonical thermodynamics, which cannot access the coexistence region where the microcanonical specific heat becomes negative. The analysis of experimental data from Chbihi (1991) and the Texas A&M group (Nebbia 1986, Wada 1989) provides evidence for the fragmentation phase transition, while the Aladin collaboration (Pochodzalla 1995) and Schwarz (1996) found signals of the higher-temperature transition. The work enables future studies of phase transitions in finite systems with long-range forces and rotating systems, and clarifies that phase separation is not a useful signal of first-order transitions in small systems.
