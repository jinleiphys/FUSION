# Rapidity profiles from 3+1D Glasma simulations with finite longitudinal thickness

> We present our progress on simulating the Glasma in the early stages of heavy ion collisions in a non-boost-invariant setting. Our approach allows us to describe colliding nuclei with finite longitudinal width by extending the McLerran-Venugopalan model to include a parameter for the Lorentz-contracted but finite extent of the nucleus in the beam direction. We determine the rapidity profile of the Glasma energy density, which shows strong deviations from the boost invariant result. Both broad and narrow profiles can be produced by varying the initial conditions. We find reasonable agreement when we compare the results to rapidity profiles of measured pion multiplicities from RHIC.

# Rapidity profiles from 3+1D Glasma simulations with finite longitudinal thickness

## Key claim

By extending the McLerran-Venugopalan model to include a parameter for the Lorentz-contracted but finite longitudinal extent of nuclei, the authors obtain non-flat rapidity profiles of the Glasma energy density that show reasonable agreement with measured pion multiplicities from RHIC, but the width of these profiles depends strongly on the infrared regulator in a way that is not well understood.

## Method

The authors solve the full 3+1D classical Yang-Mills equations in the laboratory frame with color currents that have finite longitudinal support, using a Gaussian profile for the longitudinal shape of each nucleus. The transverse color charge density follows the McLerran-Venugopalan model, and the longitudinal width is related to the Lorentz-contracted diameter of the nucleus (2R/γ) at RHIC-like collision energies of 200 GeV and 130 GeV for central Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal velocity v_z(t,z) matches the free-streaming case v_z = z/t at times t_1 = 0.5 fm/c, t_2 = 1.5 fm/c, and t_3 = 2.5 fm/c
- The local rest frame energy density ε_loc(τ_0, η_s) is evaluated at τ_0 = 1 fm/c for √s_NN = 200 GeV
- The width of rapidity profiles is rather independent of evaluation time τ_0 for τ_0 ≳ 0.3 fm/c
- Three values of the infrared regulator m are studied: m = 0.2 GeV, m = 0.4 GeV, and m = 0.8 GeV, with large effects on the profile width
- The classical evolution holds only up to roughly τ ≲ 1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the standard boost-invariant Glasma picture derived from infinitely thin shock waves (Kovner 1995, Lappi 2006) by incorporating finite longitudinal thickness of nuclei, motivated by the JIMWLK evolution picture where nuclei may be thicker than the classical Lorentz-contracted extent (Iancu 2011). It builds on the authors' previous numerical work (Gelfand 2016, Ipp 2017) and enables more realistic comparisons to RHIC pion multiplicity data (Bearden 2004) at finite collision energies, while leaving open questions about the role of the infrared regulator and the need for further in-depth studies.
