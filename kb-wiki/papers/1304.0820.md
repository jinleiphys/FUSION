# Effects of ground-state correlations on collective excitations of $^{16}$O

> The effects of the correlations in the ground state of $^{16}$O on the octupole and dipole excitations are studied using the extended random phase approximation (ERPA) derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory. It is found that the ground-state correlation effects are significant especially in the octupole excitation. It is shown that the first $3^-$ state calculated in the random phase approximation (RPA) is shifted upward when the self-energy contributions are included in particle - hole pairs. The coupling to the two particle - two hole states plays a role in shifting the first $3^-$ state down to the right position. It is also found that the dipole strength is fragmented due to the partial occupation of the single-particle states and that the peak position of the giant dipole resonance calculated in ERPA is little changed from that in RPA due to the above-mentioned competing effects: the increase in particle - hole energy and the coupling to two particle - two hole configurations.

## Key claim

Ground-state correlations in \(^{16}\)O significantly affect the octupole excitation, shifting the first \(3^-\) state upward via self-energy contributions, but the coupling to two particle–two hole (2p-2h) configurations shifts it back downward to near the experimental value; for the isovector dipole excitation, the competing effects of increased particle–hole energy and coupling to 2p-2h configurations leave the giant dipole resonance peak position little changed from the random phase approximation (RPA) result.

## Method

The author uses the extended random phase approximation (ERPA) derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), which includes ground-state correlations via the occupation matrix \(n_{\alpha\alpha'}\) and the correlated part of the two-body density matrix \(C_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'}\). The ground state is obtained as a stationary solution of TDDM using the gradient method with the Skyrme III force, and the ERPA equations are solved for the one-body and two-body amplitudes. A simplified residual interaction (only \(t_0\) and \(t_3\) terms of Skyrme III) is used, and the continuum is discretized in a 15 fm sphere with single-particle states up to 50 MeV and angular momentum \(j \le 11/2\hbar\).

## Key numbers

- Occupation probabilities in TDDM: proton \(1p_{3/2}\) = 0.894, \(1p_{1/2}\) = 0.868, \(1d_{5/2}\) = 0.108, \(2s_{1/2}\) = 0.019; neutron \(1p_{3/2}\) = 0.893, \(1p_{1/2}\) = 0.865, \(1d_{5/2}\) = 0.109, \(2s_{1/2}\) = 0.021.
- Correlation energy \(E_c\) = \(-23.7\) MeV; total energy gain from HF to TDDM = 6.4 MeV.
- First \(3^-\) state excitation energy: RPA = 3.54 MeV, mRPA = 9.1 MeV, ERPA = 6.56 MeV; experimental value = 6.13 MeV.
- \(B(E3)\) for the \(3^-\) state at 6.56 MeV in ERPA = 130 \(e^2\)fm\(^6\); experimental value = \(204 \pm 6\) \(e^2\)fm\(^6\).
- In RPA, the summed energy-weighted octupole strength exhausts 97% of the sum rule; the dipole strength exhausts 84% of the dipole sum rule including a 26% enhancement term.
- Giant dipole resonance peak: RPA at 19.5 MeV, mRPA at 21.5 MeV, ERPA shifted downward by about 2 MeV relative to mRPA.

## Context

This work extends RPA-based studies of collective excitations in doubly closed-shell nuclei by incorporating ground-state correlations, motivated by recent shell-model calculations (Utsuno and Chiba, 2013) showing that the ground state of \(^{16}\)O is highly correlated. The ERPA approach builds on the time-dependent density-matrix theory (Tohyama, 2001) and the self-consistent RPA (Janssen, 1977), and it contrasts with standard RPA and second RPA (SRPA) which start from the Hartree-Fock ground state. The results demonstrate that both ground-state correlations and coupling to 2p-2h configurations are essential for describing the octupole excitation, and they enable future studies of these effects in other nuclei.
