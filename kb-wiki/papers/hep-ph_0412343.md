# Elliptic flow at RHIC: where and when does it formed?

> Evolution of the elliptic flow of hadrons in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies is studied within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. The elliptic flow is shown to have a multi-component structure caused by (i) rescattering and (ii) absorption processes in spatially asymmetric medium. Together with different freeze-out dynamics of mesons and baryons, these processes lead to the following trend in the flow formation: the later the mesons are frozen, the weaker their elliptic flow, whereas baryon fraction develops stronger elliptic flow during the late stages of the fireball evolution. Comparison with the PHOBOS data demonstrates the model ability to reproduce the v2(eta) signal in different centrality bins.

# Elliptic flow at RHIC: where and when does it formed?

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of hadrons in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies has a multi-component structure arising from both rescattering and absorption processes in a spatially asymmetric medium, and the time evolution of the flow is opposite for mesons and baryons: the later mesons freeze out, the weaker their elliptic flow, whereas baryons develop stronger elliptic flow during the late stages of the fireball evolution. The authors qualify that the apparent elliptic flow at a given time does not correspond one-to-one with the contribution from particles that have already decoupled from the system.

## Method

The authors used the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), a microscopic Monte Carlo model based on Gribov-Regge theory, to simulate gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV and 200 AGeV with impact parameter b = 8 fm. They tracked the time evolution of the elliptic flow v2(y) for pions, nucleons, kaons, and lambdas by taking snapshots at fixed times (2, 4, 6, 8, 10 fm/c and at thermal freeze-out) and by decomposing the final flow according to the freeze-out time of each particle. The model results were compared to PHOBOS data on v2(eta) for charged particles in different centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- Maximum elliptic flow of pions at midrapidity reaches v2^pi(y=0) approximately 5% at t = 6 fm/c for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV with b = 8 fm.
- Pion freeze-out distribution peaks at t_max^pi = 5-6 fm/c, while baryon freeze-out distributions peak at t_max^B = 10-12 fm/c at RHIC energies.
- At AGS and SPS energies, the corresponding peak times were t_max^pi approximately 10 fm/c and t_max^B approximately 18 fm/c, showing a shift to earlier times at RHIC.
- For semiperipheral collisions at midrapidity, the PHOBOS data are approximately 15% above the model results for v2(eta) at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV.
- The centrality bins used for comparison were 0-15% (impact parameter 0 fm to 2.3 fm), 15-25% (2.3 fm to 6.5 fm), and 25-50% (6.5 fm to 9.2 fm).

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic model studies of elliptic flow at RHIC energies (ell_fl01, BlSt02, St04, LPX99, Sn00, CGG04, LK02, LK04) which often showed quantitative disagreement with data, particularly overestimating v2(eta) or v2(pt >= 1.5 GeV/c). The authors build on previous findings of continuous freeze-out in microscopic models from AGS to RHIC energies (FO_ags, FO_sps, FO_sor, FO_urq), showing that the freeze-out order (pions first, then kaons, lambdas, nucleons) persists at RHIC but with earlier peak times. The results enable more detailed experimental analyses of v2(y) in different pT intervals to disentangle the hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic contributions to elliptic flow.
