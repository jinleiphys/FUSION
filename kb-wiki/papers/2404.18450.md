# Detailed derivation of the $\mathbf{^3P_0}$ strong decay model applied to baryons

> We provide an in-detail derivation through the $^3P_0$ pair creation model of the transition matrix for a baryon decaying into a meson-baryon system. The meson's analysis was conducted in Ref. [1] and we extend the same formalism to the baryon sector, focusing on the $Δ(1232)\to πN$ strong decay width because all hadrons involved in the reaction are very well established, the two hadrons in the final state are stable, avoiding further analysis, all quarks are light and so equivalent, and the decay width of the process is relatively well measured. Taking advantage of a Gaussian expansion method for the hadron's radial wave functions, the expression of the invariant matrix element can be related with the mean-square radii of hadrons involved in the decay. We use their experimental measures in such a way that only the strength of the quark-antiquark pair creation from the vacuum is a free parameter. This is then taken from our previous study of strong decay widths in the meson sector, obtaining a quite compatible result with experiment for the calculated $Δ(1232)\to πN$ decay width.

## Key claim

The authors extend the $^3P_0$ pair creation model formalism from the meson sector to the baryon sector, deriving an analytical expression for the transition matrix of a baryon decaying into a meson-baryon system. They claim that by using experimental mean-square radii of the hadrons and fixing the quark-antiquark pair creation strength from their previous meson study, the calculated $\Delta(1232)\to \pi N$ decay width is "quite compatible" with experiment, though the paper does not state the exact experimental value for direct comparison.

## Method

The authors derive the transition matrix for baryon strong decays using the $^3P_0$ model, where a quark-antiquark pair with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ quantum numbers is created from the vacuum. They employ Gaussian expansion methods for the hadron radial wave functions, express the invariant matrix element in terms of hadron mean-square radii, and separate the calculation into color, flavor, and spin-space contributions. The spin-space contribution is further decomposed into angular momentum coupling terms and linear momentum integrals, with the latter evaluated using Jacobi coordinates and Gaussian basis functions.

## Key numbers

* The $\Delta(1232)\to \pi N$ strong decay width is calculated using the $^3P_0$ model with the pair creation strength $\gamma$ taken from the authors' previous meson sector study (Segovia 2012).
* The transition operator contains a factor of $3\gamma'$ (replacing $\sqrt{3}\gamma'$ from the meson case) to account for color contributions in baryon decays.
* The decay width formula is $\Gamma_{A \rightarrow BC} = 2\pi \frac{E_B(k_0)E_C(k_0)}{m_Ak_0}\sum_{J_{BC},l}|\mathcal{M}_{A\rightarrow BC}|^2$, where $k_0$ is the relative momentum of final products.

## Context

This work extends the $^3P_0$ model formalism previously applied to meson strong decays by Segovia (2012) into the baryon sector, following the original formulation by Micu (1968) and later developments by Le Yaouanc (1972) and Hayne (1982). The authors use the $\Delta(1232)\to \pi N$ decay as a test case because all hadrons are well-established, the final state hadrons are stable, and all quarks are light and equivalent. This derivation enables future calculations of baryon strong decay widths within the same constituent quark model framework that has been applied to meson spectra, decays, and hadronic transitions by the group.
