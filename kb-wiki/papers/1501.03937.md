# Using electron scattering to constrain the axial-vector form factor

> We present an analysis of elastic and quasielastic parity-violating (PV) electron scattering processes. These reactions can help to constrain the weak neutral current form factors of the nucleon that play an essential role in the description of neutrino cross sections at intermediate energies. We show that combining information from the analysis of elastic and quasielastic reactions the current knowledge on the strange and axial-vector form factors can be significantly improved.

## Key claim
Combining parity-violating elastic and quasielastic electron scattering data can significantly improve constraints on the strange and axial-vector form factors of the nucleon, but the analysis reveals that radiative corrections in the axial sector may be larger than current estimates, and the strong correlations between parameters mean that definitive conclusions about the strange form factors require better understanding of these corrections.

## Method
The authors performed a statistical chi-squared fit of all available parity-violating elastic electron-proton and electron-helium-4 asymmetry data, using the strange electric (rho_s) and magnetic (mu_s) parameters, the weak neutral current effective vector couplings (xi_V^p, xi_V^n), and the axial-vector form factor at zero momentum transfer (G_A^ep) as free parameters. They then extended the analysis to inclusive parity-violating quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering using relativistic Fermi gas, relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation, and relativistic mean field with final-state interactions nuclear models to study sensitivity to nucleonic form factors.

## Key numbers
- The fit yields G_A^ep = -0.62 ± 0.41 for the axial-vector form factor at zero momentum transfer
- The reduced chi-squared value for the elastic fit is 1.30
- The correlation coefficient between rho_s and G_A^ep is 0.711, and between mu_s and G_A^ep is -0.749
- Radiative corrections may modify the tree-level value of G_A^ep(Q^2) by more than 12%, introducing an additional uncertainty of order 50%
- The analysis covers momentum transfers in the range 0.02 < |Q^2| < 1 (GeV/c)^2
- The vector dipole mass is M_V = 0.84 GeV/c^2 and the axial dipole mass is M_A = 1.03 GeV/c^2
- At the quasielastic peak (omega ≈ 500 MeV), the three nuclear models deviate by less than about 7%

## Context
This work extends the statistical analysis of parity-violating elastic electron scattering data from Gonzalez-Jimenez et al. (2014) and earlier studies by Musolf et al. (1994). The results are directly relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE, SciBooNE, Minerva, NOMAD, K2K, and T2K, which require accurate weak neutral current form factors for neutrino-nucleus cross section calculations at intermediate energies. The analysis shows that backward-angle quasielastic measurements could help constrain the isovector radiative corrections in the axial-vector form factor, complementing the elastic data that are dominated by forward-angle measurements from HAPPEX-III and G0.
