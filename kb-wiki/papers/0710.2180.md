# Critical endpoint in the Polyakov-loop extended NJL model

> The critical endpoint (CEP) and the phase structure are studied in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in which the scalar type eight-quark (σ^4) interaction and the vector type four-quark interaction are newly added. The σ^4 interaction largely shifts the CEP toward higher temperature and lower chemical potential, while the vector type interaction does oppositely. At zero chemical potential, the σ^4 interaction moves the pseudo-critical temperature of the chiral phase transition to the vicinity of that of the deconfinement phase transition.

## Key claim

The critical endpoint (CEP) in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model is shifted toward higher temperature and lower chemical potential by the scalar-type eight-quark (σ⁴) interaction, while the vector-type four-quark interaction shifts it in the opposite direction; the PNJL model with the σ⁴ interaction yields a CEP closest to empirical estimates and satisfies the lattice constraint μₑ/Tₑ ≲ 2.5, but all model results remain far from the empirical value.

## Method

The authors studied the two-flavor PNJL model under the mean-field approximation, adding a scalar-type eight-quark (σ⁴) interaction and a vector-type four-quark interaction to the standard NJL sector. They solved the stationary conditions for the chiral condensate σ, the vector density ρᵥ, the Polyakov loop Φ, and its conjugate Φ̄, using a Polyakov-loop potential fitted to pure-gauge lattice QCD data. The pseudo-critical temperatures were determined from peaks in the chiral and Polyakov-loop susceptibilities, and the CEP location was identified where both susceptibilities diverge.

## Key numbers

- In the original PNJL model at μ=0, the pseudo-critical temperatures are T_c = 273 MeV (chiral) and 242 MeV (deconfinement); in the PNJL+σ⁴ model, they are 247 MeV and 236 MeV, respectively, reducing the difference from 31 MeV to 11 MeV.
- The CEP in the PNJL+σ⁴ model (T₀=270 MeV) is located at Tₑ = 0.149 GeV (149 MeV) and μₑ = 0.261 GeV (261 MeV), giving μₑ/Tₑ = 1.8.
- With rescaled T₀=170 MeV, the PNJL+σ⁴ model yields Tₑ = 0.118 GeV (118 MeV), μₑ = 0.250 GeV (250 MeV), and μₑ/Tₑ = 2.1.
- The PNJL+σ⁴ model gives Tₑ/T_c ≈ 0.6–0.7, consistent with the lattice analysis prediction of Tₑ/T_c ∼ 0.8.
- For comparison, the original NJL model CEP is at Tₑ = 0.047 GeV and μₑ = 0.330 GeV (μₑ/Tₑ = 7.0); the PNJL model CEP is at Tₑ = 0.111 GeV and μₑ = 0.325 GeV (μₑ/Tₑ = 2.9).

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model studies of Ratti et al. (2006) and Hansen et al. (2007) by incorporating the σ⁴ interaction previously studied in the NJL model by Kashiwa et al. (2007) and the vector interaction motivated by neutron star constraints from Blaschke et al. (2007). The results contradict the earlier NJL-only predictions of Asakawa and Yazaki (1989) and Scavenius et al. (2001), which placed the CEP at much lower T and higher μ. The PNJL+σ⁴ model brings the CEP closer to the empirical estimate of Lacey (2007) (Tₑ ∼ 165 MeV, μₑ ∼ 50–60 MeV) and satisfies the lattice constraint μₑ/Tₑ ≲ 2.5 from Ejiri et al. (2007), enabling future studies of thermodynamic quantities like pressure and quark number density with this interaction included.
