# Two-Pion-Exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interactions in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> Employing the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, we calculate the leading and next-to-leading order two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions to $NN$ interaction up to order $O(p^3)$. We compare the so-obtained $NN$ phase shifts with $2\leq L\leq 6$ and mixing angles with $2\leq J\leq6$ with those obtained in the nonrelativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory, which allows us to check the relativistic corrections to the medium-range part of $NN$ interactions. We show that the contributions of relativistic TPE are more moderate than those of the nonrelativistic TPE. The relativistic corrections play an important role in F-waves especially the $^3\text{F}_2$ partial wave. Moreover, the relativistic results seem to converge faster than the nonrelativistic results in almost all the partial waves studied in the present work, consistent with the studies performed in the one-baryon sector.

## Key claim

The relativistic (covariant) two-pion-exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction are more moderate than the nonrelativistic ones, and the relativistic results converge faster in almost all partial waves studied, but the paper only examines the medium-range (two-pion-exchange) part without including short-range contact terms, so the phase shifts shown are not complete predictions for the full interaction.

## Method

The authors used covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to calculate the leading-order (O(p²)) and next-to-leading-order (O(p³)) two-pion-exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon T-matrix. They then performed partial-wave projections to obtain phase shifts for partial waves with L from 2 to 6 and mixing angles for J from 2 to 6, and compared these with the nonrelativistic results of Kaiser (1997) and the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: fπ = 92.4 MeV; axial coupling constant: gA = 1.29; nucleon mass: mn = 939 MeV; pion mass: mπ = 139 MeV
- Low-energy constants (in GeV⁻¹): c₁ = -1.39, c₂ = 4.01, c₃ = -6.61, c₄ = 3.92, taken from Chen (2012) for the EOMS scheme
- Renormalization scale μ set to 1 GeV, varied from 0.5 GeV to 1.5 GeV to generate uncertainty bands
- For the ³F₂ partial wave, the relativistic results show fair agreement with data for Tlab ≤ 210 MeV due to cancellation between c₃ and c₄ contributions
- For the ¹F₃ partial wave, relativistic results are almost identical to Nijmegen data up to Tlab = 200 MeV
- For G-wave partial waves (¹G₄, ³G₄, ³G₃) and mixing angle ε₄, relativistic results are almost identical to data up to Tlab = 280 MeV
- For the ³G₅ partial wave, relativistic phase shift agrees with data up to Tlab = 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions proposed by Ren (2016) by calculating the two-pion-exchange contributions that were previously only treated in the nonrelativistic heavy-baryon framework by Kaiser (1997). The paper shows that relativistic corrections are particularly important for F-waves, especially the ³F₂ partial wave where Kaiser (1997) had noted a non-negligible discrepancy with Nijmegen data. This work enables future construction of a complete relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon potential by adding short-range contact terms to the medium-range two-pion-exchange contributions calculated here.
