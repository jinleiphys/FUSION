# Fate of the doubly heavy spin$-3/2$ baryons in a dense medium

> We investigate the behavior of the doubly heavy spin$-3/2$ baryons in cold nuclear matter. In particular, we study the variations of the spectroscopic parameters of the ground state $Ξ^*_{QQ'}$ and $Ω^*_{QQ'}$ particles, with $ Q $ and $ Q' $ being $b $ or $ c $ quark, with respect to the changes in the density of the nuclear medium. We find the shifts on the parameters under question at saturation medium density compared to their vacuum values. It is observed that the parameters of the $Ξ^*_{QQ'}$ states containing two heavy quarks and one up or down quark are affected by the medium, considerably. The parameters of the $Ω^*_{QQ'}$ states containing two heavy quarks and one strange quark, however, do not show any sensitivity to the density of the cold nuclear medium. We also discuss the variations of the vector self-energy at each channel with respect to the changes in the density. The negative shifts in the mass of $Ξ^*_{QQ'}$ states due to nucleons in the medium can be used to study the doubly heavy baryons' interactions with the nucleons. The results obtained can also be used in analyses of the results of the future in-medium experiments.

## Key claim

The spectroscopic parameters (mass and residue) of the doubly heavy spin-3/2 baryons \(\Xi^*_{QQ'}\) (containing a light up or down quark) show significant sensitivity to cold nuclear matter density, while the parameters of the \(\Omega^*_{QQ'}\) states (containing a strange quark) exhibit no sensitivity to the density of the medium.

## Method

The authors employed in-medium QCD sum rules, extending the vacuum formalism to finite nuclear matter density by modifying quark, gluon, and mixed condensates as functions of density and including extra operators that appear at finite density. They calculated the two-point correlation function for the interpolating currents of the \(\Xi^*_{QQ'}\) and \(\Omega^*_{QQ'}\) baryons, matched the phenomenological and QCD sides after Borel transformation and continuum subtraction, and solved the coupled sum rules for the in-medium mass, residue, and vector self-energy. The density was varied in the interval \([0, 1.5] \times \rho_N^{\text{sat}}\), where \(\rho_N^{\text{sat}} = (0.11)^3\) GeV\(^3\).

## Key numbers

* Vacuum mass of \(\Xi^*_{cc}\): \(3.73 \pm 0.07\) GeV (present work at \(\rho_N \rightarrow 0\)).
* Vacuum mass of \(\Omega^*_{cc}\): \(3.76 \pm 0.05\) GeV (present work at \(\rho_N \rightarrow 0\)).
* Vacuum mass of \(\Xi^*_{bc}\): \(6.98 \pm 0.09\) GeV (present work at \(\rho_N \rightarrow 0\)).
* Vacuum mass of \(\Omega^*_{bc}\): \(7.05 \pm 0.07\) GeV (present work at \(\rho_N \rightarrow 0\)).
* Vacuum mass of \(\Xi^*_{bb}\): \(10.06 \pm 0.12\) GeV (present work at \(\rho_N \rightarrow 0\)).
* Vacuum mass of \(\Omega^*_{bb}\): \(10.11 \pm 0.10\) GeV (present work at \(\rho_N \rightarrow 0\)).
* Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(\rho_N^{\text{sat}} = (0.11)^3\) GeV\(^3\).
* Borel mass working intervals: \([3,5]\) GeV\(^2\) for \(\Xi^*_{cc}\) and \(\Omega^*_{cc}\); \([6,8]\) GeV\(^2\) for \(\Xi^*_{bc}\) and \(\Omega^*_{bc}\); \([8,12]\) GeV\(^2\) for \(\Xi^*_{bb}\) and \(\Omega^*_{bb}\).
* In-medium continuum threshold chosen as \(s_0^* = (m_{DH} + [0.5-0.7])^2\) GeV\(^2\).
* Average pole contribution for the \(\Xi^*_{cc}\) channel at saturation density: PC = 0.71.

## Context

This work extends the study of doubly heavy baryons from vacuum to cold nuclear matter, building on prior vacuum predictions for these states using methods such as the Extended Chromomagnetic Model (PhysRevD.97.054008), Bethe-Salpeter Equation Approach (Yu 2018), and Lattice QCD (PhysRevD.90.094507). It also follows earlier in-medium studies of light baryons (Azizi 2014) and single-heavy baryons (Azizi 2017, Azizi 2018), as well as the in-medium investigation of the exotic X(3872) state (Azizi 2018). The results enable future experimental analyses of doubly heavy baryon interactions with nucleons in dense medium and can inform studies of heavy-ion collisions and neutron star structure.
