# Phenomenological level density model with hybrid parameterization of deformed and spherical state densities

> A phenomenological level density model that has different level density parameter sets for the state densities of the deformed and the spherical states, and the optimization of the parameters using experimental data of the average s-wave neutron resonance spacing are presented. The transition to the spherical state from the deformed one is described using the parameters derived from a microscopic nuclear structure calculation. The nuclear reaction calculation has been performed by the statistical model using the present level density. Resulting cross sections for various reactions with the spherical, deformed and transitional target nuclei show a fair agreement with the experimental data, which indicates the effectiveness of the present model. The role of the rotational collective enhancement in the calculations of those cross sections is also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological level density model that uses separate parameter sets for deformed and spherical state densities, connected by a damping function whose parameters are derived from microscopic finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FTHFB) calculations; the authors note that the explicit treatment of rotational collective enhancement does not significantly improve the reproduction of s-wave neutron resonance spacings compared to effective models, but its role becomes apparent in nuclear reaction cross-section calculations.

## Method
The authors constructed a hybrid level density model where the total level density is a weighted sum of spherical and deformed Fermi-gas state densities, connected by a damping function with parameters (E_ts, E_def) derived from FTHFB calculations using the SkM* energy density functional. The asymptotic level density parameters for both spherical and deformed states were optimized separately by fitting experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacings (D_0) for 300 nuclei from the RIPL-3 database, using a chi-squared minimization procedure. Nuclear reaction cross sections were calculated using the CCONE statistical model code with the hybrid, effective (no collective enhancement), and collective (with rotational enhancement) level density models, and compared with experimental data for spherical, deformed, and transitional target nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The hybrid model achieves f_rms^{D_0} = 1.66 for the reproduction of experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacings, compared to 1.74 for the effective model and 1.66 for the collective model
- The asymptotic level density parameter for spherical states is a_s(*) = 0.07110 * A * (1 + 3.608 * A^{-1/3}) MeV^{-1}
- The asymptotic level density parameter for deformed states is a_d(*) = 0.01291 * A * (1 + 30.54 * A^{-1/3}) MeV^{-1}
- The damping function width parameter C is set to 0.35, chosen for better agreement with (n,2n) reaction cross sections for Se isotopes
- Most deformed nuclei with A < 150 have E_ts values of 10-20 MeV, indicating disappearance of deformation affects reactions with incident beam energy below 20 MeV
- The pairing energy systematics uses Delta = 11/sqrt(A) MeV

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological level density approach of Gilbert and Cameron (1965) and the global parameter systematics of Mengoni and Nakajima (1991) and Koning et al. (2008), by introducing separate parameter sets for deformed and spherical states with a microscopically-informed transition function. The hybrid model enables more reliable nuclear reaction calculations for deformed and transitional nuclei, particularly for applications in nuclear data evaluation such as JENDL, and addresses the open question of how rotational collective enhancement fades with excitation energy, which previous phenomenological models treated only empirically.
