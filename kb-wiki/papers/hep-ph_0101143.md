# Small, Dense Quark Stars from Perturbative QCD

> As a model for nonideal behavior in the equation of state of QCD at high density, we consider cold quark matter in perturbation theory. To second order in the strong coupling constant, $α_s$, the results depend sensitively on the choice of the renormalization mass scale. Certain choices of this scale correspond to a strongly first order chiral transition, and generate quark stars with maximum masses and radii approximately half that of ordinary neutron stars. At the center of these stars, quarks are essentially massless.

## Key claim
Using perturbative QCD to second order in the strong coupling constant as a model for the equation of state of cold quark matter, the choice of renormalization scale determines whether quark stars have maximum masses and radii similar to ordinary neutron stars (weakly first order chiral transition) or approximately half those values (strongly first order chiral transition). The authors stress they do not suggest the perturbative equation of state is a good approximation for the densities of interest in quark stars, but use it merely as a model.

## Method
The authors computed the thermodynamic potential of cold, dense QCD to second order in the strong coupling constant using the momentum-space subtraction (MOM) scheme, converted to the modified minimal subtraction (MS-bar) scheme. They used modern determinations of the running QCD coupling constant and considered three flavors of massless quarks with equal chemical potentials. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations were solved numerically to determine the mass-radius relationship of quark stars for different choices of the renormalization scale ratio.

## Key numbers
- For the choice of renormalization scale ratio \(\bar\Lambda = 2\mu\), the pressure vanishes at a critical chemical potential \(\mu_c = 425\) MeV, corresponding to a quark density of approximately \(4.35 \rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is the density of quarks in nuclear matter, \(\sim 3 \times 0.16\)/fm³).
- For \(\bar\Lambda = 2\mu\), the maximum mass is \(M_{\rm max} \approx 1.05 M_\odot\) with a radius \(R_{\rm max} \approx 5.81\) km; the central chemical potential is \(\mu \approx 649\) MeV, corresponding to a quark density of \(\rho_{\rm max} \approx 14 \rho_0\).
- For \(\bar\Lambda = 3\mu\), the maximum mass is \(M_{\rm max} \approx 2.14 M_\odot\) with a radius \(R_{\rm max} \approx 12\) km; the central chemical potential is \(\mu \approx 456\) MeV, corresponding to a quark density of \(\rho_{\rm max} \approx 4.87 \rho_0\).
- For \(\bar\Lambda = 3\mu\), the pressure agrees with an effective bag model with \(B_{\rm eff}^{1/4} = 157\) MeV and \(a_{\rm eff} = 0.626\) to within 2% for \(\mu: 300 \rightarrow 470\) MeV.
- For \(\bar\Lambda = 2\mu\), the pressure agrees with an effective bag model with \(B_{\rm eff}^{1/4} = 223\) MeV and \(a_{\rm eff} = 0.628\) to within 4% for \(\mu: 425 \rightarrow 650\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD equation of state calculations of Freedman and McLerran (1977) and Baluni (1978) by using modern determinations of the running coupling constant from the Particle Data Group (2000). The results contrast with the standard MIT bag model, which typically produces quark stars with maximum masses around \(2 M_\odot\) and radii around 10 km. The paper enables a classification of compact stars based on the order of the chiral phase transition: a weakly first order (or no) transition produces one type of compact object, while a strongly first order transition produces two distinct branches, potentially explaining MACHO microlensing events with masses 0.15–0.9 \(M_\odot\) as small, dense quark stars.
