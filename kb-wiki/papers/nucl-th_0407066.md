# Hydrodynamic aspects of relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC

> The current status of the application of hydrodynamics to ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is reviewed. We elaborate on the arguments for strong transverse flow and rapid thermalization and discuss future applications and trends in hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic calculations of relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC provide a surprisingly accurate description of experimental data, indicating that the created fireball expands hydrodynamically for the dominant part of its lifetime, but this success depends on the assumption of rapid thermalization (equilibration time less than 1 fm/c) and a hard equation of state (p ~ e/3), with the caveat that hydrodynamics overestimates anisotropies at large transverse momenta and in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of ideal hydrodynamics to Au+Au collisions at RHIC, solving the conservation equations for the energy-momentum tensor with an equation of state relating energy density and pressure, using initial conditions from geometric considerations or saturation models. The evolution of thermodynamic fields is computed numerically, and the resulting transverse momentum spectra and momentum anisotropies (v_n coefficients) are compared to experimental data from the four RHIC experiments, with parameters such as equilibration time (tau_equ = 0.6 fm/c) and central fireball temperature (360 MeV) adjusted to reproduce spectra.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time of tau_equ = 0.6 fm/c and central fireball temperature of 360 MeV used to reproduce transverse momentum spectra at RHIC 200 GeV center-of-mass energy per nucleon.
- Average transverse flow velocity greater than c/2 achieved on a time scale of about 10 fm/c.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature of T_chem ~ 175 MeV from hadronic abundances, while spectral slopes require further transverse acceleration down to temperatures of about 100 MeV.
- Momentum anisotropies generated during the first 5 fm/c of the reaction, with hydrodynamics describing anisotropies up to transverse momenta of 2 GeV for pions and 4 GeV for baryons.
- Antibaryon to baryon ratio at midrapidity of about 0.75.

## Context

This review extends the hydrodynamic predictions made by Eskola (2001), Kolb et al. (2000), and Teaney et al. (2001) that were confirmed by early RHIC data from the STAR experiment (2001), winning the RHIC prediction competition. The work enables future studies of viscous hydrodynamics (Teaney, 2004; Muronga, 2004) to estimate QGP viscosity, three-dimensional calculations (Morita et al., 2002) to explore finite rapidity regions and the tricritical point (Nonaka, 2003), and investigations of higher-order flow anisotropies to test partonic collectivity across all quark flavors.
