# Majorana flipping of quarkonium spin states in transient magnetic field

> We demonstrate that spin flipping transitions occur between various quarkonium spin states due to transient magnetic field produced in non central heavy ion collisions (HICs). The inhomogeneous nature of the magnetic field results in \textit{non adiabatic evolution} of (spin)states of quarkonia moving inside the transient magnetic environment. Our calculations explicitly show that the consideration of azimuthal inhomogeneity gives rise to dynamical mixing between different spin states owing to Majorana spin flipping. Notably, this effect of non-adiabaticity is novel and distinct from previously predicted mixing of the singlet and one of the triplet states of quarkonia in the presence of a static and homogeneous magnetic field.

# Majorana flipping of quarkonium spin states in transient magnetic field

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that azimuthal inhomogeneity of the transient magnetic field produced in non-central heavy ion collisions causes non-adiabatic evolution of quarkonium spin states, leading to dynamical mixing among all four spin states (not just between the singlet and one triplet state as previously predicted for static homogeneous fields). This effect is conditional on the magnetic field being sufficiently weak and rapidly decaying, such that the Larmor frequency becomes negligibly small and adiabaticity breaks down.

## Method
The authors model the spin-field interaction Hamiltonian for a heavy quark-antiquark pair moving through an inhomogeneous magnetic field that varies both spatially and temporally, with azimuthal inhomogeneity considered up to first order in a Taylor expansion of the azimuthal angle. They solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the four coupled spin states (two triplet states |11⟩ and |1-1⟩, the mixed triplet-singlet states |ψ₃⟩ and |ψ₄⟩) and compute transition probabilities between them as functions of time for different values of the polar angle θ.

## Key numbers
- The adiabaticity condition is given by the ratio |⟨ψ_m|ψ̇_n⟩/(E_m-E_n)| = |φ̇ sinθ / (2ω)|, where ω is the Larmor frequency and φ̇ represents the degree of azimuthal inhomogeneity.
- For states |ψ₁⟩ and |ψ₂⟩ (the |11⟩ and |1-1⟩ states), the ratio is 0/0 because their eigenvalues E₁ and E₂ are equal.
- For states |ψ₃⟩ and |ψ₄⟩, the ratio is exactly 0.
- The coupled differential equations for the coefficients C₁, C₂, C₃, C₄ are derived in the weak-field regime where the magnetic field intensity takes a negligibly small value.
- Transition probabilities are plotted for four values of θ: 0.5, 0.9, 1.6, and 2.5 radians as functions of time.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of quarkonium spin mixing in magnetic fields, specifically the work of Michael (2013) and Yang (2011) and Filip (2013), which considered homogeneous and constant (or time-dependent) fields and found mixing only between the |10⟩ triplet state and the |00⟩ singlet state. The present paper introduces the novel effect of azimuthal inhomogeneity, which enables Majorana spin flipping among all spin states. The results provide a potential observable to discriminate between scenarios where the magnetic field decays rapidly (producing non-adiabatic mixing) versus scenarios where the quark-gluon plasma medium sustains the field for longer times (preserving adiabatic evolution), as discussed by Ajit (2017) and Tuchin (2013).
