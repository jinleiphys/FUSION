# Towards a first principles light-front Hamiltonian for the nucleon

> We solve the nucleon's wave functions from the eigenstates of the light-front quantum chromodynamics Hamiltonian for the first time, using a fully relativistic and nonperturbative approach based on light-front quantization, without an explicit confining potential. These eigenstates are determined for the three-quark, three-quark-gluon, and three-quark-quark-antiquark Fock representations, making them suitable for low-resolution probes. From this, we calculate the nucleon's quark and gluon matter densities, helicity, and transversity distributions, which show qualitative consistency with experimental extractions. We also compute the contributions of quark and gluon helicity to the proton spin and the tensor charges. The obtained light-front wave functions represent a significant advancement towards a unified description of various hadron distribution functions in both longitudinal and transverse momentum space.

## Key claim

The authors solve the nucleon's light-front wave functions from the eigenstates of the light-front QCD Hamiltonian without an explicit confining potential, obtaining qualitative (not quantitative) agreement with experimental parton distributions and form factors, with the caveat that the valence quark PDFs are narrower than global fits in the x > 0.1 region due to the absence of longitudinal confinement.

## Method

The authors use Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) to diagonalize the light-front QCD Hamiltonian in a truncated Fock space including |qqq>, |qqqg>, and |qqqqqbar> sectors, employing a 2D harmonic oscillator basis in transverse momentum space and discretized longitudinal momentum modes. They fit model parameters (quark masses, coupling constant, and oscillator scale) to the proton mass and electromagnetic properties, then compute electromagnetic form factors, unpolarized PDFs, helicity PDFs, and transversity PDFs from the resulting light-front wave functions. The PDFs are evolved from the model scale (mu0^2 = 0.22 GeV^2) to higher scales using the DGLAP equations via the HOPPET toolkit for comparison with global analyses.

- Model scale determined by matching: mu0^2 = 0.22 +/- 0.02 GeV^2
- Fock sector probabilities at model scale: |qqq> = 53.10%, |qqqg> = 26.53%, |qqquubar> = 8.52%, |qqqddbar> = 8.56%, |qqqssbar> = 3.29%

- Gluon helicity contribution: Delta G = 0.29 +/- 0.03 for x_g in [0.05, 0.2] at 10 GeV^2
- Orbital angular momentum contributions: L_z^u = 0.036 +/- 0.002, L_z^d = 0.058 +/- 0.001, L_z^g = -0.037 +/- 0.004

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to mesons (Lan 2021) and nucleons (Xu 2023) by removing the phenomenological confining potential and using physical gluon mass, demonstrating that confinement can arise from fundamental QCD interactions within the truncated Fock space. The results enable a unified framework for computing generalized parton distributions (GPDs), transverse momentum distributions (TMDs), Wigner distributions, and double parton correlations, with systematic improvements possible by including higher Fock sectors with multi-gluon configurations.
