# Green's function method for single-particle resonant states in relativistic mean field theory

> Relativistic mean field theory is formulated with the Green's function method in coordinate space to investigate the single-particle bound states and resonant states on the same footing. Taking the density of states for free particle as a reference, the energies and widths of single-particle resonant states are extracted from the density of states without any ambiguity. As an example, the energies and widths for single-neutron resonant states in $^{120}$Sn are compared with those obtained by the scattering phase-shift method, the analytic continuation in the coupling constant approach, the real stabilization method and the complex scaling method. Excellent agreements are found for the energies and widths of single-neutron resonant states.

## Key claim

The Green's function method formulated within relativistic mean field theory can extract both energies and widths of single-particle resonant states from the density of states without ambiguity, using the free-particle density of states as a reference to distinguish resonant from non-resonant continuum contributions.

## Method

The relativistic mean field theory is solved self-consistently in coordinate space using the Dirac Green's function, constructed from two independent Dirac spinors with correct asymptotic behaviors at the origin and at infinity. The density of states for each angular-momentum block is obtained by integrating the imaginary part of the Green's function over coordinate space, and resonant states are identified as peaks in the density of states that remain stable against changes in the box size. The energies and widths of resonant states are extracted from the full-width at half-maximum of these peaks after subtracting the free-particle density of states background.

## Key numbers

- For $^{120}$Sn with PK1 interaction, the $j_{15/2}$ resonant state appears at 12.956 MeV with width 1.375 MeV (RMF-GF), compared to 12.865 MeV with width 1.027 MeV from RMF-RSM.
- For $^{120}$Sn with NL3 interaction, the $i_{13/2}$ resonant state appears at 3.264 MeV with width 0.003 MeV (RMF-GF), compared to 3.262 MeV with width 0.004 MeV from RMF-ACCC.
- For $^{120}$Sn with NL3 interaction, the $f_{5/2}$ resonant state appears at 0.685 MeV with width 0.042 MeV (RMF-GF), compared to 0.685 MeV with width 0.023 MeV from RMF-ACCC.
- Bound state energies from RMF-GF agree with the shooting method to within 0.0000 MeV for deeply bound levels, with differences up to 0.0551 MeV for the very weakly bound $3p_{3/2}$ state.
- The contour path width $\gamma$ is taken as 0.1 MeV, energy step on the contour as 0.005 MeV, and the imaginary part parameter $\epsilon$ as $1\times10^{-6}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Green's function method, previously applied in non-relativistic Skyrme-HFB theory by Zhang et al. (2011) and in relativistic continuum random-phase-approximation by Daoutidis and Ring (2009), to the relativistic mean field framework for single-particle resonant states. The results for $^{120}$Sn are compared with those from the scattering phase-shift method (Cao et al., 2002), the analytic continuation in the coupling constant approach (Zhang et al., 2004), the real stabilization method (Zhang et al., 2008), and the complex scaling method (Guo et al., 2010), showing excellent agreement. The method enables simultaneous treatment of bound and resonant states with correct asymptotic behaviors, and can be extended to self-consistent calculations of exotic nuclei where continuum coupling is important.
