# Properties of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and the $T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-$ states in nuclear matter

> Since its first detection, the interesting properties of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ have made it to be one of the most prominent tetraquark-like states up to date. In this work we present a joint analysis of the $T_{cc}^+$ and its charge-conjugated partner in a dense nuclear medium. We start by considering both states as purely isoscalar $D^{\ast} D$ and $\overline{D}{}^{\ast} \overline{D}$ $S$-wave bound states, respectively. We use previous results for the in-medium $D$, $\overline{D}$, $D^\ast$ and $\overline{D}{}^\ast$ spectral functions to determine the modified two-meson amplitudes. We find important changes in the in-medium mass and width of both tetraquark-like resonances, which become more visible when increasing the nuclear density and molecular probabilities. The experimental confirmation of the found distinctive patterns will support the existence of molecular components in the $T_{cc}^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}^-$ wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and its charge-conjugate $T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-$ states, when treated as purely isoscalar $D^* D$ and $\overline{D}{}^* \overline{D}$ $S$-wave bound states, develop distinct in-medium mass shifts and width increases that become more visible at higher nuclear density and larger molecular probabilities; these medium effects are a potential experimental signature of a molecular component in their wave functions.

## Method

The authors start from a single-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for $D^* D$ scattering in the $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$ channel in the exact isospin limit, using two families of energy-dependent contact potentials ($V_A(s)=a+bs$ and $V_B(s)=1/(c+ds)$). They introduce nuclear-medium effects only through the in-medium meson loop function, computed using previously developed spectral functions for $D$, $\overline{D}$, $D^*$, and $\overline{D}{}^*$ mesons (from Tolos (2009), Garcia-Recio (2010), Garcia-Recio (2011)), with a sharp cutoff of $\Lambda=700$ MeV. The vacuum parameters are fixed by imposing the $T_{cc}$ mass at $\delta m = -800$ keV below the $D^* D$ threshold, while the molecular probability $P_0$ is treated as a free parameter (values 0.2 and 0.8 are shown).

## Key numbers

- The $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ vacuum mass is taken as $m_{\rm thr} + \delta m$ with $m_{\rm thr} = 3875.09$ MeV and $\delta m = -800$ keV (exact isospin limit, not the experimental $-360 \pm 40^{+4}_{-0}$ keV).
- The vacuum width of the $T_{cc}^+$ is $\Gamma = 48 \pm 2^{+0}_{-14}$ keV (from LHCb (2021)).
- Nuclear densities are considered up to normal nuclear density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The cutoff used to regularize the in-medium loop integral is $\Lambda = 700$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to the $X(3872)$ in nuclear matter by Albaladejo (2021) to the doubly-charmed tetraquark candidates $T_{cc}^+$ and $T_{\bar c \bar c}^-$. It builds on femtoscopic correlation studies of $D^0 D^{*+}$ and $D^+ D^{*0}$ channels in heavy-ion collisions by Kamiya (2022) and Vidana (2023). The results suggest that measuring the density-dependent mass and width patterns of these states in experiments such as PANDA (FAIR) with fixed nuclear targets or CBM (FAIR) in heavy-ion collisions could discriminate between molecular and compact tetraquark interpretations.
