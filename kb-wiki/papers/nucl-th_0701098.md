# Charged current neutrino induced coherent pion production

> We analyze the neutrino induced charged current coherent pion production at the energies of interest for recent experiments like K2K and MiniBooNE. Medium effects in the production mechanism and the distortion of the pion wave function, obtained solving the Klein Gordon equation with a microscopic optical potential, are included in the calculation. We find a strong reduction of the cross section due to these effects and also substantial modifications of the energy distributions of the final lepton and pion.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear medium effects (Δ resonance self-energy and pion distortion via a Klein-Gordon optical potential) produce a strong reduction of the charged-current coherent pion production cross section, but their flux-averaged cross section for K2K (10.0 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²) still exceeds the experimental upper limit (7.7 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²), suggesting that the discrepancy may arise from uncertainties in the axial N-Δ form factors, the optical potential, or the treatment of nuclear correlations in the Monte Carlo simulations used to separate coherent from incoherent events.

## Method

The authors model the elementary ν + N → μ⁻ + N + π⁺ process as dominated by Δ(1232) excitation and decay, using vector and axial transition form factors fitted to neutrino scattering data. The Δ resonance properties are modified in the nuclear medium via a density-dependent self-energy (including Pauli blocking, absorption processes, and a repulsive Landau-Migdal interaction), and the final-state pion distortion is treated by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a Δ-hole model optical potential. The cross section for coherent pion production on nuclei (¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb) is then computed in the distorted-wave impulse approximation, with the amplitude summed coherently over all nucleons weighted by isospin factors.

## Key numbers

- Reduction of the cross section due to Δ self-energy in the propagator: approximately 35% at E_ν = 1 GeV on ¹²C.
- Flux-averaged integrated cross section for CC coherent π⁺ production on ¹²C over the K2K spectrum: σ^{CC}_{coh} = 10.0 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² (without experimental threshold: 12.0 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²).
- Experimental upper limit from K2K for the same process: 7.7 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm².
- Average neutrino energy for K2K: 1.3 GeV; for MiniBooNE: 0.75 GeV.
- Normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- Axial mass used for form factors: M_A = 1.28 GeV.
- Vector form factor parameter: C₃^V = 2.05 / (1 − q²/0.54 GeV²)².
- Axial form factor values at q² = 0: C₃^A(0) = 0, C₄^A(0) = −0.3, C₅^A(0) = 1.2.
- Δ Nπ coupling constant: f* = 2.13.
- Form factor cutoff for Δ Nπ coupling: Λ = 1 GeV.
- Real part of Δ selfenergy: Re Σ_Δ(ρ) ≈ 40 MeV × (ρ/ρ₀).
- Landau-Migdal parameter: g' = 0.63.

## Context

This work extends the model of Kelkar et al. (1996) by using a fully relativistic elementary amplitude (including all transverse parts) while retaining a realistic treatment of pion distortion via the Klein-Gordon equation, improving upon the eikonal approximation used by Singh et al. (2006). The calculation addresses the discrepancy between the Rein and Sehgal (1983) model predictions and the K2K upper limit (Hasegawa, 2005), and provides predictions for MiniBooNE data (Wascko, 2006). The results enable more accurate separation of coherent pion production from incoherent processes in neutrino oscillation experiments, and highlight the need for improved data on axial N-Δ form factors and for better treatment of nuclear correlations in Monte Carlo simulations.
