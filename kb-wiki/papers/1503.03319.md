# Baryonium, a common ground for atomic and high energy physics

> Indications of the existence of quasi-bound states in the N-Nbar system are presented. Measurements by BES discovered a broad enhancement close to the p-pbar threshold in the S wave, isospin 0 state formed in radiative decays of J/psi. Another enhancement located about 50 MeV below the threshold was found in mesonic decays of J/psi. In terms of the Paris potential model it was shown that these are likely to represent the same state. Antiprotonic atomic data provide some support for this interpretation and indicate the existence of another fairly narrow quasi-bound state in a P wave.

## Key claim

Indications exist for quasi-bound states in the N-Nbar system, but the evidence is indirect and model-dependent: the Paris potential model suggests that a broad enhancement near the p-pbar threshold and a second enhancement about 50 MeV below threshold likely represent the same S-wave, isospin 0 state, while antiprotonic atomic data hint at an additional narrow P-wave state.

## Method

The authors analyzed BES Collaboration data on J/psi radiative decays to gamma p-pbar and mesonic decays to gamma pi+ pi- eta', using the Paris N-Nbar potential to calculate final-state interactions via half-off-shell T-matrices. They also extracted antiproton capture ratios from antiprotonic atomic level widths and radiochemical experiments, comparing these to optical potential predictions to infer subthreshold N-Nbar amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The Paris potential generates a quasi-bound state in the 11S0 wave at -4.8 MeV binding energy with width 52 MeV (Gamma/2 = 26 MeV)
- The Paris potential predicts a quasi-bound state in the 33P1 wave at -4.5 MeV binding energy with width 18 MeV (Gamma/2 = 9.0 MeV)
- The Gaussian source radius for gamma p-pbar creation is R = 0.28 fm at maximal photon momentum k ~ 1.2 GeV, changing to R = 0.39 fm at k = 0
- Branching ratio for J/psi to gamma p-pbar is (3.8 +/- 1.0) x 10^-4
- Branching ratio for J/psi to omega p-pbar is (1.1 +/- 0.15) x 10^-3
- Branching ratio for J/psi to pi0 p-pbar is (1.19 +/- 0.08) x 10^-3
- Branching ratio for J/psi to p-pbar is (2.12 +/- 0.1) x 10^-3

## Context

This work extends earlier LEAR-era searches for baryonium states (Adiels 1986, Bertini 1982) that found no narrow signals, and builds on BES observations of threshold enhancements in J/psi decays (Bai 2003) and the X(1835) resonance (Ablikim 2005). The Paris potential calculations (El-Bennich 2009) unify these two BES findings as the same quasi-bound state, while antiprotonic atom data from light nuclei (Augsburger 1999, Schneider 1991) provide complementary subthreshold constraints. The results motivate future searches with antiproton beams at J-PARC and FAIR, particularly for narrow gamma signals from p-pbar annihilation and fine-structure measurements in antiprotonic atoms.
