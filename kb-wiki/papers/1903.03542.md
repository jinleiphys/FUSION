# Neutrino pion-production on a nucleon

> Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT), where the $Δ$ resonance is included, is used in order to examine the axial charged-current component of the weak interaction process at low neutrino energies. At leading chiral order the Adler theorems, derived using PCAC, are satisfied. At next-to-leading chiral order this effective field theory goes beyond these theorems. I will show that $χ$PT generates deviation from the PCAC predictions, which means that some neutrino-nucleon models that are used in evaluating neutrino nucleus scattering amplitudes, might need modifications.

## Key claim

Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT) with the Δ resonance included shows that at next-to-leading chiral order the axial current contribution to neutrino pion-production on a nucleon gives corrections to the PCAC predictions, meaning that some neutrino-nucleon models used in evaluating neutrino-nucleus scattering amplitudes might need modifications.

## Method

The author uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT), an effective field theory that respects QCD symmetries, to evaluate the axial charged-current component of neutrino pion-production on a nucleon at low energies. The nucleon and Δ propagators are treated with an expansion in orders of the nucleon mass m_N, and the Δ resonance is included following the approach of Hemmert et al., where the nucleon-Δ mass difference δ_{NΔ} is taken to be of order the pion mass m_π. The leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) amplitudes are computed from the chiral Lagrangian terms L^{(1)}_{πN} and L^{(2)}_{πN}, and the resulting matrix elements are examined for their deviation from the PCAC structure predicted by the Adler theorems.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion parameter is p/Λ_χ ≪ 1, where Λ_χ ≈ m_N ≈ 1 GeV and p is of order the pion mass m_π.
- The nucleon-Δ mass difference δ_{NΔ} = m_Δ - m_N is considered to be of order m_π.
- At neutrino energies of about 400 MeV, for final neutron π⁺ or proton π⁰ states, the covariant ChPT evaluation of Yao et al. (2018) showed that nucleon degrees of freedom are very important and the Δ is not as prominent as assumed in many model calculations.

## Context

This work extends the use of heavy baryon χPT for neutrino pion-production, following earlier work by Bernard et al. (1994) who used χPT to go beyond Adler's soft-pion theorems for s-wave pion production at threshold. The results contradict the common assumption in neutrino-nucleus reaction models (e.g., those discussed in Sato (2018)) that PCAC is valid, and show that NLO corrections to PCAC exist. The paper also references the recent covariant ChPT evaluation by Yao et al. (2018) which included NNLO contributions and found them to be larger than expected and important for reproducing measured low-energy neutrino cross sections. This work enables improved neutrino-nucleon interaction models for use in reconstructing neutrino energy spectra from accelerator-based neutrino experiments.
