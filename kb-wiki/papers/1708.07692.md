# $η$-nucleus interaction from the $d+d$ reaction around the $η$ production threshold

> The $η$ mesic nucleus is considered to be one of the interesting exotic many body systems and has been studied since 1980's theoretically and experimentally. Recently, the formation of the $η$ mesic nucleus in the fusion reactions of the light nuclei such as $d + d \rightarrow (η+ α) \rightarrow X$ has been proposed and the experiments have been performed by WASA-at-COSY. We develop a theoretical model to evaluate the formation rate of the $η$ mesic nucleus in the fusion reactions and show the calculated results. We find that the $η$ bound states could be observed in the reactions in cases with the strong attractive and small absorptive $η$-nucleus interactions. We compare our results with existing data of the $d + d \rightarrow η+ α$ and the $d + d \rightarrow {^3 \rm He} + N + π$ reactions. We find that the analyses by our theoretical model with the existing data can provide new information on the $η$-nucleus interaction.

## Key claim

The authors develop a theoretical model to evaluate the formation rate of η mesic nuclei in d+d fusion reactions and find that η bound states could be observed in cases with strong attractive and small absorptive η-nucleus interactions, but the observation is conditional on the specific values of the real and imaginary parts of the η-α optical potential.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological model for the d+d→(η+α)→X reaction using a Green's function technique to sum over all η-α final states, with the transition amplitude parameterized by a form factor F(q) and an interaction strength c adjusted to reproduce observed cross sections. The η-α optical potential is taken as a Gaussian-shaped potential with strength parameters V0 (real) and W0 (imaginary), and the total cross section is separated into a conversion part (η absorption) and an escape part (η emission). The model parameters are fixed by comparing the calculated escape cross section with existing data from the d+d→η+α reaction above threshold.

## Key numbers

- The η production threshold in the d+d reaction corresponds to a deuteron beam momentum of p=2.3 GeV/c
- The α particle density is described by a Gaussian with range parameter a=1.373 fm, reproducing an RMS radius of 1.681 fm
- The central density of the α particle is ρ_α(0)=0.28 fm^{-3} ≈ 1.6 ρ_0, where ρ_0=0.17 fm^{-3} is the normal nuclear density
- For the potential parameters (V0,W0)=-(100,10) MeV with p0=500 MeV/c, a clear peak appears at E_η-m_η ≈ -7 MeV corresponding to η-α bound state formation
- The upper limit of the η-nucleus formation cross section from WASA-at-COSY is reported as around 3-6 nb for the d+d→³He+n+π⁰ reaction
- The distortion effects from initial deuterons are estimated to be almost constant within approximately 15% accuracy in the energy region considered

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of η mesic nuclei first predicted by Haider and Liu in the 1980s, and addresses recent experimental efforts at WASA-at-COSY (Krzemien 2015, Adlarson 2016) searching for η bound states in light nuclei like He. The model enables extraction of η-nucleus interaction parameters from existing d+d→η+α data (Frascaria 1994, Willis 1997, Wronska 2005) and provides a framework to interpret subthreshold spectra from ongoing experiments, potentially resolving the long-standing controversy over the existence of η bound states in light nuclei that began with the (π⁺,p) experiments of Chrien (1988).
