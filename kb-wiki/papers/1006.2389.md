# The physics of $Z^0/γ^*$-tagged jets at the LHC

> Electroweak bosons produced in conjunction with jets in high-energy collider experiments is one of the principle final-state channels that can be used to test the accuracy of perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics calculations and to assess the potential to uncover new physics through comparison between data and theory. In this paper we present results for the $Z^0/γ^*$+jet production cross sections at the LHC at leading and next-to-leading orders. In proton-proton reactions we elucidate up to ${\cal O}(G_Fα_s^2)$ the constraints that jet tagging via the $Z^0/γ^*$ decay dileptons provides on the momentum distribution of jets. In nucleus-nucleus reactions we demonstrate that tagged jets can probe important aspects of the dynamics of quark and gluon propagation in hot and dense nuclear matter and characterize the properties of the medium-induced parton showers in ways not possible with more inclusive measurements. Finally, we present specific predictions for the anticipated suppression of the $Z^0/γ^*$+jet production cross section in the quark-gluon plasma that is expected to be created in central lead-lead collisions at the LHC relative to the naive superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon scatterings.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD, the transverse momentum of a jet tagged by a $Z^0/\gamma^*$ boson is not tightly constrained by the boson's momentum, with the standard deviation of the jet $p_T$ distribution reaching approximately 25% of the mean value, contrary to the naive leading-order expectation of exact momentum balance.

## Method

The authors calculate $Z^0/\gamma^*$+jet production cross sections in proton-proton collisions at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) using the Monte Carlo for FeMtobarn processes (MCFM) framework, including the $Z^0/\gamma^* \rightarrow \mu^+\mu^-$ decay channel with invariant mass cuts around the $Z^0$ peak. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, they incorporate medium-induced parton energy loss using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism for non-Abelian bremsstrahlung in an expanding quark-gluon plasma, evaluating the suppressed cross section via a probabilistic superposition of vacuum and medium-modified parton showers.

## Key numbers

- The $Z^0$ boson mass is taken as $m_Z = 91.2$ GeV with width $\Gamma_z = 2.5$ GeV; dilepton pairs are selected in the invariant mass range $m_Z \pm 3\Gamma_z$.
- For a $Z^0/\gamma^*$ tag with $92.5\;{\rm GeV} < p_T < 112.5$ GeV, the mean jet $p_T$ at NLO ranges from 93.9 GeV (cone radius $R=0.2$) to 100.1 GeV ($R=0.8$), with standard deviations of 25.2 GeV, 24.9 GeV, and 24.2 GeV for $R=0.2, 0.4, 0.8$ respectively.
- For a low-$p_T$ tag with $7\;{\rm GeV} < p_T < 13$ GeV, the mean jet $p_T$ at NLO ranges from 8.7 GeV ($R=0.2$) to 9.8 GeV ($R=0.8$), with standard deviations of 4.5 GeV, 4.7 GeV, and 4.6 GeV.
- The LO calculation for $Z^0/\gamma^*$+jet production at the Tevatron ($\sqrt{s}=1.96$ TeV) underpredicts the measured cross section by approximately 30%.
- The charged particle rapidity density used for the QGP medium is $dN^{ch+neut.}/dy = 2800$ in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4$ TeV.

## Context

This work extends perturbative QCD calculations of electroweak boson+jet production, previously studied at the Tevatron by D0 (Abazov, 2008), to LHC energies and to heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the authors' prior formalism for inclusive jet cross sections in nucleus-nucleus reactions (Vitev, 2008; Vitev, 2009) and applies it to tagged jets, demonstrating that $Z^0/\gamma^*$+jet measurements can probe medium-induced parton shower properties inaccessible via leading particle correlations or inclusive jet observables. The results enable experimental tests of parton energy loss mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma at LHC energies, particularly distinguishing between different theoretical approaches to non-Abelian energy loss such as the GLV formalism used here.
