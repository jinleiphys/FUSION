# Acoustic horizons in nuclear fluids

> We consider a hydrodynamic description of the spherically symmetric outward flow of nuclear matter, accommodating dispersion in it as a very weak effect. About the resulting stationary conditions in the flow, we apply an Eulerian scheme to derive a fully nonlinear equation of a time-dependent radial perturbation. In its linearized limit, with no dispersion, this equation implies the static acoustic horizon of an analogue gravity model. We, however, show that time-dependent nonlinear effects destabilize the static horizon. We also model the perturbation as a high-frequency travelling wave, and perform a {\it WKB} analysis, in which the effect of weak dispersion is studied iteratively. We show that even arbitrarily small values of dispersion make the horizon fully opaque to any acoustic disturbance propagating against the bulk flow, with the amplitude and the energy flux of the radial perturbation undergoing a discontinuity at the horizon, and decaying exponentially just outside it.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an acoustic horizon (analogous to a white hole) exists in a spherically symmetric outflow of nuclear matter, but that even arbitrarily weak dispersive effects from baryon-vector meson interactions make this horizon fully opaque to acoustic disturbances, while nonlinear effects destabilize the static position of the horizon.

## Method

The authors adopt a hydrodynamic description of nuclear matter with a weakly dispersive term derived from a non-local baryon-vector meson coupling at the mean-field level. They apply an Eulerian perturbation scheme about stationary flow solutions to derive a fully nonlinear wave equation for radial perturbations, then linearize it and perform a WKB analysis for high-frequency travelling waves, treating dispersion iteratively as a small parameter.

## Key numbers

- The acoustic horizon occurs when the flow velocity equals the acoustic propagation speed: \(v_0^2 = a_0^2\), with \(a_0^2 = 3 c_{\mathrm s}^2 (n_0/n_{\mathrm e})(n_0/n_{\mathrm e} - 2/3)\)
- No acoustic propagation is possible when the scaled density \(x = n_0/n_{\mathrm e} \leq 2/3\)
- At the horizon, the scaled density satisfies \(x_{\mathrm c} = [1 + \sqrt{1 + (2B/3)}]/2\), where \(B\) is the scaled Bernoulli constant, giving \(x_{\mathrm c} > 1\)
- The horizon radius in scaled units is \(R_{\mathrm c}^4 = 1/[x_{\mathrm c}^3 (3x_{\mathrm c} - 2)]\)
- The scaled velocity at the horizon is \(y_{\mathrm c} \simeq 1.5\) (for \(B=2\))
- The dispersion parameter is scaled as \(\zeta_{\mathrm s} = c_{\mathrm s}^2 r_{\mathrm c}^2 / n_{\mathrm e}\), with the dimensionless tuning parameter \(\eta = \zeta / \zeta_{\mathrm s}\) required to satisfy \(\eta \ll 1\)

## Context

This work extends the analogue gravity concept of acoustic horizons, previously studied in astrophysical accretion flows and shallow-water systems (Unruh 1981, Visser 1998), to nuclear hydrodynamics. It builds on hydrodynamic models of nuclear matter that include dispersive effects for soliton studies (Fonck et al. 2006, 2007), and on prior work showing that strong perturbations shift acoustic horizons in spherical accretion (Mach and Malec 2008). The results enable future investigation of whether strongly attractive nuclear forces on small scales could allow stationary flow solutions to penetrate the acoustic horizon.
