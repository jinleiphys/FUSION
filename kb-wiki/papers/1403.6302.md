# Microscopic description of $^7$Li in the $^{7}\text{Li}+^{12}\text{C}$ and $^7\text{Li}+^{28}\text{Si}$ elastic scattering at high energies

> We employ a microscopic continuum-discretized coupled-channels reaction framework (MCDCC) to study the elastic angular distribution of the $^7$Li$=α+t$ nucleus colliding with $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si targets at $E_{\text{Lab}}$=350 MeV. In this framework, the $^7$Li projectile is described in a microscopic cluster model and impinges on non-composite targets. The diagonal and coupling potentials are constructed from nucleon-target interactions and $^7$Li microscopic wave functions. We obtain a fair description of the experimental data, in the whole angular range studied, when continuum channels are included. The inelastic and breakup angular distributions on the lightest target are also investigated. In addition, we compute $^{7}$Li$+^{12}$C MCDCC elastic cross sections at energies much higher than the Coulomb barrier and we use them as reference calculations to test the validity of multichannel eikonal cross sections.

## Key claim

The microscopic continuum-discretized coupled-channels (MCDCC) framework provides a fair description of elastic scattering data for the weakly-bound projectile \(^7\)Li on light targets at 350 MeV, but only when continuum (breakup) channels are included; the agreement is limited to angles up to about \(15^\circ\) for \(^{12}\)C and \(10^\circ\) for \(^{28}\)Si, beyond which the predictions overestimate the data by roughly a factor of two.

## Method

The authors describe the \(^7\)Li projectile as an \(\alpha+t\) cluster using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction, obtaining bound and pseudostate wave functions. Diagonal and coupling potentials are constructed via a folding technique that combines Fourier transforms of the projectile transition densities with central nucleon-target optical potentials from Weppner et al. (2009) for \(^{12}\)C and Koning and Delaroche (2003) for \(^{28}\)Si. The coupled-channel equations are solved with the R-matrix method on a Lagrange-Legendre mesh, including continuum states up to a cutoff excitation energy of 15 MeV and projectile spins up to \(J_{\text{Pmax}}=7/2\).

## Key numbers

- \(E_{\text{Lab}}=350\) MeV: the incident laboratory energy for the \(^7\)Li+\(^{12}\)C and \(^7\)Li+\(^{28}\)Si elastic scattering measurements.
- \(J_{\text{Pmax}}=7/2\): the maximum projectile spin at which the CDCC calculations converge for both targets.
- \(E_{\text{max}}=15\) MeV: the cutoff excitation energy (from the \(\alpha+t\) threshold) above which the CDCC elastic cross sections converge.
- \(J_{\text{max}}=200\): the maximum total angular momentum of the projectile-target system used to compute the elastic cross sections at 350 MeV.
- Channel radius \(a=18\) fm and Lagrange-Legendre mesh of \(N=130\) basis functions used in the R-matrix method.
- At \(E_{\text{Lab}}=1050\) MeV, the difference between eikonal-CDCC and CDCC elastic cross sections is reduced to less than 0.15 (in ratio to Rutherford).
- For \(^{12}\)C, inclusion of breakup channels improves the description at large angles by about a factor of 2 relative to single-channel results; for \(^{28}\)Si, the improvement is about a factor of 4.

## Context

This work extends the MCDCC method of Descouvemont and Hansen (2013), previously applied to \(^7\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb near the Coulomb barrier, to lighter targets (\(^{12}\)C and \(^{28}\)Si) at the higher energy of 350 MeV. It provides a parameter-free test of the microscopic cluster description for weakly-bound projectiles and uses the CDCC results as reference to benchmark the multichannel eikonal method of Ogata and Yahiro (2003). The study shows that the eikonal-CDCC approximation becomes fairly valid only at incident energies of at least about 1000 MeV, where breakup effects are already very small, and it opens the way to applying the same framework to other exotic nuclei such as Borromean systems.
