# Upper bounds for fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We obtain upper limits for the contributions of the incomplete fusion and sequential complete fusion processes to the total fusion cross section. Through those upper bounds we find that these processes are negligible in reactions induced by projectiles such as $^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li, which break up into neutrons and one fragment containing the full projectile charge.

## Key claim

The authors derive upper bounds for incomplete fusion (IF) and sequential complete fusion (SCF) cross sections in collisions of weakly bound projectiles, and show that for projectiles like \(^{6}\)He and \(^{11}\)Li which break up into neutrons and a single charged fragment, these processes are negligible compared to complete fusion, so the measured total fusion cross section can be approximated by the complete fusion cross section.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical approximation based on the Alder-Winther (AW) method, which treats the relative motion classically and the intrinsic dynamics quantum mechanically via time-dependent coupled-channels equations. They obtain upper bounds for IF and SCF by setting the population probability of the breakup channel at the point of closest approach to unity (\(\bar{P}_{l}^{(1)} = 1\)) and evaluating the tunneling probability for the appropriate fragment (e.g., \(^{4}\)He from \(^{6}\)He) through its own potential barrier with the target, using the Akyuz-Winther parametrization for nuclear potentials and the ingoing wave boundary condition.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^{6}\)He + \(^{238}\)U system at near-barrier energies, the upper bound for the incomplete fusion cross section is very small, making the total fusion cross section close to the complete fusion cross section (figure 1).
* For the \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi system, the upper bound for incomplete fusion induced by the \(^{3}\)H fragment exceeds the single-barrier penetration model (BPM) cross section at low energies above the barrier; the experimental IF cross section is about 30% of the total fusion cross section (Dasgupta 2002).
* For the \(^{6}\)He + \(^{209}\)Bi system, the upper bounds for both incomplete fusion and sequential complete fusion are much smaller than the BPM estimate for complete fusion (figure 3).
* The breakup threshold energy for \(^{6}\)He is \(B = 0.975\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Alder-Winther method, previously applied to fusion in a simplified two-channel problem by Hussein et al. (2004), to derive upper bounds for incomplete and sequential complete fusion. It addresses the experimental difficulty in separating complete and incomplete fusion events, particularly for projectiles that break into uncharged fragments, as noted in experiments by Dasgupta (2002) and others. The method enables a simple theoretical estimate showing that for neutron-halo projectiles like \(^{6}\)He and \(^{11}\)Li, the total fusion cross section can be reliably approximated by the complete fusion cross section, simplifying comparison between theory and experiment.
