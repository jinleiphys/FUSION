# Light-Cone QCD Plasma

> We deduce the maximum-entropy state of partons created in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions. This state is characterized by a distribution function n(x,pT) depending on the light-cone fraction x and the transverse momentum pT of forward or backward particles, respectively. The mean transverse momentum <pT> determines the single parameter of the maximum entropy distribution which is constrained by the sum of all light-cone momentum fractions being unity. The total multiplicity is related to the transverse area of the colliding Lorentz-contracted hadrons. Assuming parton-hadron duality we can compare the model to data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the maximum-entropy state of partons produced in high-energy collisions, characterized by a light-cone distribution function with a single parameter (mean transverse momentum), can describe inclusive particle spectra in both pp and AA collisions, but only under the hedge that a phenomenological K-factor (reducing effective center-of-mass energy to account for leading particles) and parton-hadron duality are assumed.

## Method

The authors derive a light-cone plasma distribution function n(x,p_T) = 1/(exp(p_T/lambda + x w) - 1) by maximizing entropy subject to constraints of light-cone momentum conservation (sum of x fractions equals unity) and fixed total transverse energy. They convert the gluon distribution to a pion distribution via parton-hadron duality with degeneracy factor g_pi=3, and fit the three parameters (transverse area L_bot^2, effective temperature lambda, and softness w) to experimental data on dN_ch/deta at eta=0 and mean transverse momentum at eta=0 from RHIC and LHC, using the light-cone momentum sum rule as a third constraint.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV with K=0.5: L_bot=1.34 fm, lambda=0.183 GeV, w=3.44, dN_ch/deta|_eta=0=2.20, <p_T>|_eta=0=0.39 GeV
- For pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7.0 TeV with K=0.5: L_bot=1.20 fm, lambda=0.288 GeV, w=8.21, dN_ch/deta|_eta=0=5.65, <p_T>|_eta=0=0.56 GeV
- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (0-3% centrality) with K=1: N_part=361, <p_T>=0.516 GeV, lambda=0.263 GeV, w=5.650, (1/(N_part/2)) dN_ch^AA/deta=3.81
- Entropy comparison for central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: light-cone distribution S^lc=142.4, boosted thermal distribution S^bt=141.1

## Context

This work extends the maximum-entropy approach of Jaynes (1957) to light-cone phase space, contrasting with the traditional thermal fireball model used by Schnedermann (1993), Braun-Munzinger (1994), and Becattini (2007) which assumes equilibrated longitudinal and transverse momenta. The light-cone plasma distribution emphasizes the non-equilibrium nature of the collision process with asymmetric transverse and longitudinal momenta, and the authors plan to extend the model to include separate distributions for quarks and gluons and to study fluctuations.
