# Bayesian analysis of muon capture on deuteron in chiral effective field theory

> We compute the muon capture on deuteron in the doublet hyperfine state for a variety of nuclear interactions and consistent nuclear currents. Our analysis includes a detailed examination of the theoretical uncertainties coming from different sources: the single-nucleon axial form factor, the truncation of the interaction and current chiral expansion, and the model dependence. Moreover, we study the impact of the use of different power counting scheme for the electroweak currents on the truncation error. To estimate the truncation error of the chiral expansion of interactions and currents we use the most modern techniques based on Bayesian analysis. This method enables us to give a clear statistical interpretation of the computed theoretical uncertainties. Finally, we provide the differential capture rate as function of the kinetic energy of the outgoing neutron which may be measured in future experiments. Our recommended theoretical value for the total doublet capture rate is $Γ_{\rm th}=395\pm 10$ s$^{-1}$ ($68\%$ confidence level). We calculated also the capture rate in the quartet hyperfine state, which turns out to be in the range $[13.3-13.8]$ s$^{-1}$ depending on the adopted nuclear interaction.

## Key claim

The paper provides a recommended theoretical value for the total doublet muon capture rate on deuteron of 395 ± 10 s⁻¹ at 68% confidence level, but this result depends on the specific treatment of theoretical uncertainties from multiple sources including the single-nucleon axial form factor, truncation of the chiral expansion, and model dependence, with the analysis using Bayesian methods to give a statistical interpretation of these uncertainties.

## Method

The authors computed the muon capture on deuteron in both doublet and quartet hyperfine states using chiral effective field theory (χEFT) interactions and consistent nuclear currents, employing two families of nuclear interactions: the Norfolk (NV) local interactions up to N3LO including Δ-isobars, and the Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk (EMN) non-local interactions up to N3LO with three different cutoffs. The differential capture rate as a function of the outgoing neutron kinetic energy was calculated using partial wave expansions up to J=4, and the truncation errors of the chiral expansion for both interactions and currents were estimated using Bayesian analysis with Gaussian processes following the formalism of Melendez et al. (2019).

## Key numbers

- Total doublet capture rate: Γ_th = 395 ± 10 s⁻¹ (68% confidence level)
- Quartet hyperfine state capture rate: range [13.3–13.8] s⁻¹ depending on the adopted nuclear interaction
- For NV interactions at N3LO: Γ^(1/2) values range from 392.5 s⁻¹ (NVIIa) to 393.7 s⁻¹ (NVIb), with current truncation error σ^C_k=3 of 5.1 s⁻¹ (Bochum power counting) or 1.1 s⁻¹ (JLab-Pisa power counting)
- For EMN interactions at N3LO: Γ^(1/2) values range from 396.0 s⁻¹ (EMN450) to 397.3 s⁻¹ (EMN500), with current truncation error σ^C_k=3 of 6.0–6.4 s⁻¹ (Bochum) or 2.1–2.2 s⁻¹ (JLab-Pisa)
- LEC uncertainty σ_LECs = 3.9 s⁻¹ for all interactions, with derivatives ∂Γ/∂r_A² = −24 s⁻¹ fm⁻² and ∂Γ/∂d_R = 0.66 s⁻¹
- Partial waves with J>2 contribute approximately 0.75% to the total capture rate
- Vector coupling constant G_V' = 1.149 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation that considered only the ¹S₀ neutron-neutron channel (Ceccarelli et al., 2023) by adding missing partial wave channels and providing a comprehensive theoretical uncertainty analysis. It builds upon earlier fully consistent χEFT calculations by Marcucci et al. (2012) and Adam et al. (2011), which were later corrected for an error in the relation between the three-nucleon interaction LEC and the axial current LEC (Marcucci et al., 2019), and parallels recent work by Bonilla et al. (2023) that performed a complete partial wave expansion with uncertainty analysis. The results are directly relevant to the ongoing MuSun experiment at the Paul Scherrer Institute, which aims to measure the capture rate with approximately 1.5% precision, enabling more stringent tests of χEFT predictions.
