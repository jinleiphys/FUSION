# Coulomb screening correction to the $Q$ value of the triple alpha process in thermal plasmas

> The triple alpha reaction is a key to $^{12}$C production and is expected to occur in weakly-coupled, thermal plasmas as encountered in normal stars. We investigate how Coulomb screening affects the structure of a system of three alpha particles in such a plasma environment by precise three-body calculations within the Debye-Hückel approximation. A three-alpha model that has the Coulomb interaction modified in the Yukawa form is employed. Precise three-body wave functions are obtained by a superposition of correlated Gaussian bases with the aid of the stochastic variational method. The energy shifts of the Hoyle state due to the Coulomb screening are obtained as a function of the Debye screening length. The results, which automatically incorporate the finite size effect of the Hoyle state, are consistent with the conventional result based on the Coulomb correction to the chemical potentials of ions that are regarded as point charges in a weakly-coupled, thermal plasma. We have given a theoretical basis to the conventional point-charge approach to the Coulomb screening problem relevant for nuclear reactions in normal stars by providing the first evaluation of the Coulomb corrections to the $Q$ value of the triple alpha process that produces a finite size Hoyle state.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first evaluation of Coulomb corrections to the Q value of the triple alpha process that accounts for the finite spatial extent of the Hoyle state, finding that the conventional point-charge approximation gives a very good estimate of the screening-induced Q value shift in normal stars, with finite-size corrections being only of order 10^{-7}.

## Method

The authors performed precise three-body variational calculations of the three-alpha system using a superposition of correlated Gaussian basis functions optimized by the stochastic variational method. The Coulomb interaction between alpha particles was modified to a Yukawa form to incorporate Debye-Huckel screening in a thermal plasma, and the energy of the Hoyle state was calculated as a function of the screening factor C (inverse Debye length). Two different three-alpha potential models (Set 1 and Set 2) were employed to check model dependence.

## Key numbers

- The modified Ali-Bodmer potential gives the S-wave 8Be (0_1+) resonance position at 88.84 keV, close to the empirical value of 91.8 keV
- With Set 1 Hamiltonian at C=0, the 12C ground state energy is -9.40 MeV and the Hoyle state energy is 0.349 MeV relative to the three-alpha threshold
- With Set 2 Hamiltonian at C=0, the 12C ground state energy is -8.99 MeV and the Hoyle state energy is 0.475 MeV
- At C=0.0162 fm^{-1}, the 8Be ground state energy becomes approximately 10^{-5} MeV, while the Hoyle state remains a resonance at 0.082 MeV (Set 1) or 0.210 MeV (Set 2)
- The rms pair distance of the 8Be ground state at C=0 is 6.3 fm
- The rms radius of the Hoyle state from the center of mass is 3.43 fm (Set 1) and 3.71 fm (Set 2) at C=0, and 2.74 fm (Set 1) and 2.84 fm (Set 2) at C=infinity
- The Debye screening length in typical stellar conditions is of order 10^3 to 10^4 fm
- The conventional point-charge prediction for the Q value shift is 12e^2/lambda_D

## Context

This work extends the conventional point-charge approach to Coulomb screening in nuclear reactions, originally formulated by Salpeter (1954), by providing the first three-body calculation that accounts for the finite size of the Hoyle state. The results contradict the conjecture made by Jensen (1995) and Higa (2008) that the Hoyle state could emerge as an Efimov state. The paper enables more accurate estimates of carbon production rates in normal stars undergoing stable helium burning and suggests future work on more realistic effective Coulomb potentials and applications to X-ray bursting neutron star environments.
