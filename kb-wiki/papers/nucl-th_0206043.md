# Medium effects to N(1535) resonance and eta mesic nuclei

> The structure of eta-nucleus bound systems (eta mesic nuclei) is investigated as one of the tools to study in-medium properties of the N(1535) (N^*) resonance by using the chiral doublet model to incorporate the medium effects of the N^* resonance in a chiral symmetric way. We find that the shape and the depth of the eta-nucleus optical potential are strongly affected by the in-medium properties of the N^* and the nucleon. Especially, as a general feature of the potential, the existence of a repulsive core of the eta-nucleus potential at the nuclear center with an attractive part at the nuclear surface is concluded. We calculate the level structure of bound states in this 'central-repulsive and surface-attractive' optical potential and find that the level structure is sensitive to the in-medium properties of the N^*. The (d,3He) spectra are also evaluated for the formation of these bound states to investigate the experimental feasibility. We also make comments on the possible existence of halo-like eta states in beta-unstable halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the in-medium reduction of the mass difference between the nucleon and the N(1535) resonance, driven by partial chiral symmetry restoration, can cause the eta-nucleus optical potential to become repulsive at the nuclear center while remaining attractive at the surface, but only if the chiral restoration parameter C exceeds about 0.08; for the expected realistic value C ~ 0.2, no eta bound states are actually formed in stable or halo nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct an eta-nucleus optical potential using the N(1535) dominance model, where the eta self-energy is expressed in terms of the in-medium N and N* masses and widths. The in-medium masses and the pi-N-N* coupling are calculated within the chiral doublet model with mirror assignment, using a linear parameterization of the sigma condensate as a function of density (Phi(rho)=1-C rho/rho_0 with C=0.0-0.3). The in-medium N* width includes the pi-N decay channel (with medium-modified masses and coupling) and the two-nucleon absorption channel N*N -> pi NN, while the eta-N decay is Pauli-blocked. Bound-state energies and widths are obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation in momentum space with an energy-dependent optical potential, and the (d,3He) reaction spectra are evaluated using the Green function method.

## Key numbers

- The N(1535) mass in vacuum is 1535 MeV, the nucleon mass is 940 MeV, and the eta mass is approximately 547 MeV; the N* lies about 50 MeV above the eta-N threshold.
- The eta-N-N* coupling constant g_eta is approximately 2.0, chosen to reproduce the partial width Gamma(N* -> eta N) ~ 75 MeV at tree level.
- For C=0.0 (no medium effects), the 1s binding energy in 132Xe is 38.4 MeV with a width of 39.6 MeV; for C=0.02, the binding energy increases to 41.2 MeV and the width to 49.0 MeV; for C=0.05, binding energy 45.1 MeV and width 69.3 MeV; for C=0.08, binding energy 46.1 MeV and width 106.3 MeV; for C=0.1 and above, no bound states are found.
- The critical density where the real part of the eta self-energy changes sign occurs for C > 0.08, meaning the repulsive core appears at densities below saturation for such C values.
- The two-nucleon absorption width N*N -> pi NN is estimated to be about 15 MeV at saturation density, depending on the C parameter and eta energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of eta-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu (1986) and Chiang, Oset and Liu (1991), who predicted attractive eta-nucleus potentials and bound states based on the eta-nucleon scattering length. It also builds on the chiral doublet model developed by DeTar and Kunihiro (1989) and later by Jido, Hatsuda and Kunihiro (2000), which predicts a reduction of the N-N* mass difference in medium. The paper contradicts the simple Woods-Saxon type potential picture by showing that medium effects can produce a repulsive core, and it enables experimental searches for eta-mesic nuclei via the (d,3He) reaction, following the successful method used for deeply bound pionic atoms by Yamazaki et al. (1996). The results are compared with a contemporaneous chiral unitary approach by Inoue et al. (2002), which also finds a strongly energy-dependent eta optical potential.
