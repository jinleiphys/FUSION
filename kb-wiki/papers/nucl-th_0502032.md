# Hyperfine anomaly in Be isotopes in the cluster model and the neutron spatial distribution

> The study of the hyperfine anomaly of neutron rich nuclei, in particular, neutron halo nuclei, can give a very specific and unique way to measure their neutron distribution and confirm a halo structure. The hyperfine structure anomaly in Be+ ions is calculated with a realistic electronic wave function, obtained as a solution of the Dirac equation. In the calculations, the Coulomb potential modified by the charge distribution of the clustered nucleus and three electrons in the 1s^2 2s configuration is used. The nuclear wave function is obtained in the core+nucleon model of 9,11Be. The aim of this study is to test whether the hyperfine structure anomaly reflects a halo structure in 11Be.

## Key claim

The hyperfine structure anomaly in Be isotopes is sensitive to the spatial distribution of the valence neutron and the weights of mixed states in the ground state wave function, but the calculated anomaly for 11Be is smaller than previously reported by Fujita et al. (1999) when using more realistic electronic wave functions obtained from the Dirac equation with screened Coulomb potential and extended nuclear charge distributions.

## Method

The authors calculated the hyperfine structure anomaly for Be+ ions using relativistic electronic wave functions obtained as numerical solutions of the Dirac equation, incorporating the Coulomb potential modified by the cluster-model charge distribution of the nucleus and screening by the 1s^2 closed shell. The nuclear wave functions for 9,11Be were obtained in a core+nucleon cluster model, with 11Be described as a 10Be core plus valence neutron in an admixture of 2s1/2 and 1d5/2 states, and 9Be described as an 8Be core plus p3/2 neutron. The hyperfine anomaly was computed by folding the electronic and nuclear wave functions using the Bohr-Weisskopf formalism with the Breit-Rosenthal-Crawford-Shawlow correction.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be+ with weights ws=0.72 and wd=0.28, the total hyperfine anomaly including all effects is epsilon = -0.0556%
- For 11Be+ with weights ws=0.5 and wd=0.5, the total hyperfine anomaly is epsilon = -0.0430%
- The 2s1/2 state in 11Be contributes epsilon = -0.0719% with neutron separation energy Bn = 0.504 MeV and mean neutron distance rn = 6.056 fm
- The 1d5/2 state in 11Be contributes epsilon = -0.0112% with Bn = 3.872 MeV and rn = 2.634 fm
- The Breit-Rosenthal-Crawford-Shawlow correction for 11Be is delta = 0.0476%
- The experimental magnetic moment of 11Be is mu_I = -1.6816(8) mu_N (Gei99)
- The experimental magnetic moment of 9Be is mu_I = -1.1447 mu_N
- For 9Be+ with weights w0+ = 0.579 and w2+ = 0.421, the total hyperfine anomaly is epsilon = -0.0249% (consistent with Fujita 1999 and Yamada 2000)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of hyperfine anomalies in Be isotopes by Fujita et al. (1999), who used a simplified electronic wave function and a homogeneous spherical charge distribution, finding a larger anomaly for 11Be that was interpreted as evidence for a halo structure. The present calculations use more realistic electronic wave functions from the Dirac equation with screened Coulomb potential and cluster-model charge distributions, showing that the previous approach overestimated the anomaly for weakly bound nuclei. The results demonstrate that precise measurements of the hyperfine anomaly could determine spectroscopic factors of the s- and d-states in 11Be and distinguish between the two-cluster model for 11Be and the three-cluster (alpha+alpha+n) structure suggested for 9Be by Descouvemont (2002).
