# The Narrowing of Charge Balance Function and Hadronization Time in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The widths of charge balance function in high energy hadron-hadron and relativistic heavy ion collisions are studied using the Monte Carlo generators PYTHIA and AMPT, respectively. The narrowing of balance function as the increase of multiplicity is found in both cases. The mean parton-freeze-out time of a heavy-ion-collision event is used as the characteristic hadronization time of the event. It turns out that for a fixed multiplicity interval the width of balance function is consistent with being independent of hadronization time.

## Key claim
The narrowing of the charge balance function with increasing multiplicity occurs even in hadron-hadron collisions where hadronization is instantaneous, and for a fixed multiplicity interval the width is consistent with being independent of hadronization time, casting doubt on using balance-function narrowing as a signal of quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method
The authors used the PYTHIA5.720 Monte Carlo generator to simulate proton-proton collisions at four center-of-mass energies (22, 64, 130, and 200 GeV) and the AMPT1.11 multi-phase transport model to simulate Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon pair. They computed the width of the charge balance function (mean relative rapidity of oppositely charged particle pairs) in full phase space and in a fixed rapidity window [-3,3], binning events by charged multiplicity and, for AMPT, by the event-mean parton freeze-out time as a proxy for hadronization time.

## Key numbers
- In PYTHIA p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=22, 64, 130, and 200 GeV, the width of the full-phase-space balance function decreases with increasing charged multiplicity (Fig. 1).
- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the two-particle correlation function R^{+-}(0,y) of oppositely charged particles shows a width consistent with being independent of multiplicity (Fig. 2).
- In Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, central collisions (impact parameter b <= 7 fm) have event-mean parton freeze-out times larger than 5 fm, while peripheral collisions (b > 7 fm) have freeze-out times concentrated below 5 fm (Fig. 5, Fig. 6).
- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV in the rapidity window [-3,3], the width of the balance function decreases with increasing multiplicity, but for a fixed multiplicity interval the width is consistent with being independent of the mean parton freeze-out time (Fig. 7).

## Context
This work extends the study of charge balance functions from hadron-hadron collisions (NA22 collaboration, 1990s) and heavy-ion collisions (STAR and NA49 experiments, early 2000s) to examine whether the observed narrowing with centrality is due to late hadronization or simply a multiplicity effect. The authors use PYTHIA (Sjostrand, 1994) and AMPT (Lin et al., 2005) to show that the narrowing is primarily a multiplicity-driven phenomenon, contradicting the proposal by Bass, Danielewicz, and Pratt (2000) that balance-function narrowing signals a long-lived quark-parton phase. The results suggest that further investigation with models including equilibrium phase transitions is needed.
