# Properties of quarks and mesons in the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach

> In this thesis, the Dyson-Schwinger - Bethe-Salpeter formalism is investigated and used to study the meson spectrum at zero temperature, as well as the chiral phase transition in finite-temperature QCD. First, the application of sophisticated matrix algorithms to the numerical solution of both the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) and the inhomogeneous vertex BSE is discussed, and the advantages of these methods are described in detail. Turning to the finite temperature formalism, the rainbow-truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is used to investigate the impact of different forms of the effective interaction on the chiral transition temperature. A strong model dependence and no overall correlation of the value of the transition temperature to the strength of the interaction is found. Within one model, however, such a correlation exists and follows an expected pattern. In the context of the BSE at zero temperature, a representation of the inhomogeneous vertex BSE and the quark-antiquark propagator in terms of eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the homogeneous BSE is given. Using the rainbow-ladder truncation, this allows to establish a connection between the bound-state poles in the quark-antiquark propagator and the behavior of eigenvalues of the homogeneous BSE, leading to a new extrapolation technique for meson masses. This is used to study the ground- and excited-state meson spectrum for all quark masses from light to bottom, for pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, axialvector and tensor mesons. Good agreement with experiment is found, e.g., for all ground states in the bottomonium system. In addition, new applications of the inhomogeneous vertex BSE, such as the possibility to calculate on-shell quantities like decay constants, are investigated. Finally, we study the influence of the infrared behavior of the effective interaction on properties of pi and rho mesons.

## Key claim

The chiral transition temperature in finite-temperature QCD is strongly model-dependent within the rainbow-truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation, and there is no overall correlation between the transition temperature and the strength of the effective interaction; however, within a single interaction model such a correlation does exist and follows the expected pattern.

## Method

The author solves the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) and the inhomogeneous vertex BSE using sophisticated matrix algorithms, and applies the rainbow-ladder truncation to the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation. At zero temperature, a representation of the inhomogeneous vertex BSE and the quark-antiquark propagator in terms of eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the homogeneous BSE is used to establish a connection between bound-state poles and eigenvalue behavior, leading to a new extrapolation technique for meson masses. At finite temperature, the rainbow-truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is used with different forms of the effective interaction (MT, AWW, MR, and MN models) to study the chiral phase transition.

## Key numbers

- For the MT model with parameter sets MT1, MT2, and MT3, the pion masses are 0.230 GeV, 0.230 GeV, and 0.232 GeV, respectively, and the rho meson masses are 0.780 GeV, 0.774 GeV, and 0.762 GeV, respectively.
- The chiral condensate for the MT model ranges from (0.239 GeV)^3 to (0.243 GeV)^3 across the three parameter sets.
- For the AWW model, the pion masses are 0.135 GeV, 0.135 GeV, and 0.137 GeV for parameter sets AWW1, AWW2, and AWW3, respectively.
- The MN model yields a pion mass of 0.14 GeV and a rho meson mass of 0.77 GeV, with a chiral condensate of (0.115 GeV)^3.
- The current quark masses used with the MT model at renormalization scale mu^2 = 19 GeV^2 are: m_u/d = 0.01 GeV, m_s = 0.0685 GeV, m_c = 0.828 GeV, and m_b = 3.71 GeV.
- The maximal bound state mass accessible is limited by the appearance of complex conjugate poles in the propagator dressing functions; the parabola cutoff parameter lambda_2 is set to 10 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter formalism for meson spectroscopy, building on the Maris-Tandy model (Maris 1999) and earlier studies by Alkofer, Watson, and Weigel (2002), Maris and Roberts (1997), and Munczek and Nemirovsky (1983). The new extrapolation technique for meson masses enables the study of ground- and excited-state meson spectra for all quark masses from light to bottom, including pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, axialvector, and tensor mesons. The finite-temperature analysis opens the door to systematic investigations of how different effective interactions affect the chiral phase transition, while the zero-temperature results provide a benchmark for future calculations of decay constants and other on-shell quantities.
