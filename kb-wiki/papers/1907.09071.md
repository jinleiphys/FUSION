# The n-th order Moment of the Nuclear Charge Density and Contribution from the Neutrons

> The relativistic expression for the $n$-th order moment of the nuclear charge density is presented. For the mean square radius(msr) of the nuclear charge density, the non-relativistic expression, which is equivalent to the relativistic one, is also derived consistently up to $1/M^2$ with use of the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation. The difference between the relativistic and non-relativistic expressions for the msr of the point proton density is also discussed. The $n(\ge 4)$-th order moment of the nuclear charge density depends on the point neutron density. The 4-th order moment yields a useful information on the msr of the point neutron density, and is expected to play an important role in electron scattering off neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The relativistic expression for the n-th order moment of the nuclear charge density is presented, and for the mean square radius (msr) the non-relativistic expression equivalent up to 1/M^2 is derived consistently using the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation; the 4th order moment depends on the point neutron density and is expected to play an important role in electron scattering off neutron-rich nuclei, but the experimental values of the single-proton and single-neutron sizes are still under discussion.

## Method

The authors derived the relativistic expression for the n-th order moment of the nuclear charge density from the Fourier transform of the charge density operator, using Sachs and Pauli form factors for the nucleon. For the msr, they performed a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation of the Dirac equation to obtain a non-relativistic expression consistent up to order 1/M^2, including a new term not discussed previously. They then applied these expressions to calculate the msr and 4th order moment for 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb using the relativistic mean field models NL3 and NL-SH, and the non-relativistic model SLy4.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the experimental root msr is reported as 5.5012(0.0013) fm.
- The Darwin-Foldy correction term 3/4M^2 equals 0.0331 fm^2; in relativistic models with effective mass M* ≈ 0.6M, this is enhanced to 3/4M*^2 ≈ 0.0920 fm^2.
- For 208Pb using NL3, the calculated charge msr is 30.242 fm^2 compared to the experimental value 30.28 fm^2; for 48Ca using NL3, the calculated charge msr is 11.774 fm^2 compared to the experimental value 11.91 fm^2.
- For 208Pb using NL3, the calculated 4th order moment is 1156.81 fm^4 compared to the experimental value 1171.58 fm^4; for 48Ca using NL3, the calculated 4th order moment is 191.664 fm^4 compared to the experimental value 194.714 fm^4.
- The parity-violating asymmetry measurement for 208Pb provides the form factor at a single momentum transfer q = 0.475 fm^-1 with an error of about 10%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear charge densities by providing a consistent relativistic framework for moments beyond the mean square radius, addressing ambiguities in how nuclear models like NL3 (Lalazissis, 1997), NL-SH (Sharma, 1993), and SLy4 (Chabanat, 1998) treat corrections from spin-orbit densities and the Darwin-Foldy term. The results enable more precise comparisons between model calculations and experimental electron scattering data, particularly for exploring neutron distributions in neutron-rich nuclei through the 4th order moment, complementing parity-violating electron scattering experiments (Abrahamyan, 2012) and hadron scattering approaches.
