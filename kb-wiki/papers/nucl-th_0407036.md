# Adjustment studies in self-consistent relativistic mean-field models

> We investigate the influence of the adjustment procedure and the set of measured observables on the properties and predictive power of relativistic self-consistent mean-field models for the nuclear ground state. These studies are performed with the point-coupling variant of the relativistic mean-field model. We recommend optimal adjustment algorithms for the general two-part problem and we identify various trends and dependencies as well as deficiencies of current models. Consequences for model improvements are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that current relativistic mean-field (RMF) models cannot simultaneously describe nuclear masses and geometry (form factors) with desired accuracy, because the surface thickness is systematically underestimated and this underestimation is linked to an overly large symmetry energy (a4 ≈ 38 MeV). The authors show that enforcing a more realistic surface thickness drives a4 even higher, while constraining a4 to a lower value worsens isotope binding-energy trends.

## Method

The authors performed least-squares adjustments of the point-coupling variant of the relativistic mean-field model (RMF-PC) using the Bevington curved-step algorithm (Levenberg-Marquardt plus trial step). They varied the set of fitted observables (binding energies, diffraction radii, surface thicknesses, charge rms radii, pairing gaps) and their weights, as well as the mass range of nuclei (from 16O to 214Pb), and compared the resulting forces on isotope and isotone chains, form factors, neutron skins, and nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

- The PC-F1 force yields a symmetry energy a4 = 37.8 MeV, compressibility K = 270 MeV, saturation density ρ0 = 0.151 fm⁻³, and effective mass m*/m = 0.61.
- Adjusting only to binding energies (PC-masses) gives a4 = 33.9 MeV and K = 237 MeV.
- Constraining a4 to 34 MeV (PC-asym) yields a4 = 34.0 MeV and K = 268 MeV.
- Emphasizing surface thickness (PC-surf) drives a4 to 40.6 MeV and K to 238 MeV, with saturation density 0.147 fm⁻³.
- A 50% reduction in the weight for binding energy (from 0.2% to 0.1%) increases total χ² by a factor of 2.9; similar reductions for diffraction radius, surface thickness, and rms radius increase χ² by factors of 1.4, 1.2, and 1.5 respectively.
- The χ²/dof of PC-F1 is 2.75.
- Correlated errors of coupling constants range from 5% to 70%, sometimes exceeding the parameter value itself.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the PC-F1 force (Buer02) and compares with the RMF finite-range force NL-Z2 (NLZ2) and with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approaches. It identifies a competition between binding energy and form-factor observables in the fit, and a correlation between surface thickness and symmetry energy that was noted earlier by Furnstahl (FurnstahlN). The results motivate systematic exploration of extended RMF-PC functionals (EXT) and suggest that additional terms (e.g., density-dependent couplings, gradient terms, Wigner-energy terms) are needed to resolve the trade-off between light and heavy systems and to disentangle isoscalar and isovector channels.
