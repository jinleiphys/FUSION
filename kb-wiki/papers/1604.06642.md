# Pairing effects on neutrino transport in low-density stellar matter

> We investigate the impact of pairing correlations on neutrino transport in stellar matter. Our analysis is extended to nuclear matter conditions where large density fluctuations develop, associated with the onset of the liquid-vapor phase transition, and clustering phenomena occur. Within a thermodynamical treatment, we show that at moderate temperatures, where pairing effects are still active, the scattering of neutrinos in the nuclear medium is significantly affected by pairing correlations, which increase the neutrino trapping, thus modifying the cooling mechanism, by neutrino emission, of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations significantly increase neutrino scattering cross sections in low-density stellar matter near the spinodal border, but this effect is conditional on the temperature being below the critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal transition and on the system being close to the spinodal instability region where the isoscalar-like eigenvalue of the curvature matrix becomes small.

## Method

The authors computed the neutral-current neutrino differential cross section from the static structure factor, which they obtained via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem from the curvature matrix of the free energy density. They employed a Skyrme energy functional (SAMi-J35 parametrization) for the nuclear mean field and a BCS pairing interaction fitted to reproduce the 1S0 pairing gap of pure neutron matter from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, then evaluated the curvature matrix elements including Coulomb and surface terms for stellar matter at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- At density ρ = ρ₀/100 and T = 0.5 MeV with proton fraction y_p = 0.045, pairing effects produce a pronounced bump in the neutrino differential cross section at intermediate neutrino energies, with the ratio R between full and no-pairing calculations reaching large values as the spinodal border is approached.
- At density ρ = ρ₀/4 and T = 1.4 MeV with smaller proton fractions, pairing effects are still significant but reduced compared to the lower-density case because the derivative of the pairing gap with respect to density is less steep.
- The critical temperature for neutron pairing at the densities studied is approximately T_c ≈ 0.65 MeV for y_p = 0.1 and T_c ≈ 0.55 MeV for y_p = 0.3 at ρ = ρ₀/100.
- A jump in the cross section occurs at T = T_c, where the cross section suddenly reaches the value of normal nuclear matter; a smaller jump also occurs at a lower temperature (approximately 0.05-0.1 MeV) due to the disappearance of proton pairing.
- The spinodal border was mapped in the (ρ, y_p) plane at T = 0.5 MeV and momentum transfer q = 30 MeV, covering densities from very low up to ρ ≈ 0.4 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the saturation density) and proton fractions from low values up to y_p ≈ 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutrino transport in the presence of density fluctuations associated with the liquid-gas phase transition, previously studied by Margueron et al. (2004), by adding the effect of pairing correlations. The authors build on their own earlier work (Burrello et al., 2014) on pairing effects near spinodal instabilities and on the BCS treatment of pairing in neutron star crusts by Chamel et al. The results indicate that pairing correlations increase neutrino trapping, which would modify the cooling mechanism of neutron stars by neutrino emission, and suggest that future work should go beyond the second curvature of the free energy density to accurately evaluate density fluctuations inside the spinodal region.
