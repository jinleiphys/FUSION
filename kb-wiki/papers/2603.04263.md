# Quantum Kinetic Theory for Quantum Chromodynamics

> We develop a quantum kinetic theory for QCD, which incorporates all leading order collision terms. At lowest order in gradient expansion, it reproduces the spin-averaged Boltzmann equation with both elastic and inelastic collisions. At next order in gradient expansion, the solution to the quantum kinetic equations give spin polarization of on-shell quarks and gluons in quark-gluon plasma when the gradients are of hydrodynamic ones. A power counting in the coupling shows the spin polarization behaves differently in vortical and non-vortical gradients: the former is free of collisional contribution to leading order, while the latter contains a collisional contribution at parametrically the same order as the free theory counterpart. We also find the inelastic collision in a spin basis provides a possible mechanism for conversion between spin and orbital angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper develops a full quantum kinetic theory for QCD that incorporates all leading-order collision terms, but the spin polarization results are derived only at next-to-leading order in gradient expansion and specifically for hydrodynamic gradients, with the caveat that vortical gradients receive no collisional contribution to leading order while non-vortical gradients do.

## Method

The author derives Kadanoff-Baym equations for quark and gluon Wigner functions in Coulomb gauge, then performs a gradient expansion organized by the dimensionless parameter epsilon ~ hbar * partial_X / Lambda, where Lambda ~ T is the typical momentum scale. Self-energies are computed at one-loop level for thermal mass and width, while two-loop and multi-loop diagrams provide elastic and inelastic collision terms; the resulting equations are solved order by order in gradient to obtain spin polarization of on-shell quarks and gluons.

## Key numbers

* Thermal mass and width scale as g*Lambda and g^2*Lambda respectively, with Lambda ~ T for finite-temperature QGP
* The upper bound of hydrodynamic scale is set by the mean free path: partial_X less than or similar to g^4 * Lambda
* Screening lengths are (g*Lambda)^{-1} for quarks and longitudinal gluons, and (g^2*Lambda)^{-1} for transverse gluons
* The dimensionless gradient expansion parameter is epsilon ~ hbar * partial_X / Lambda
* Color factors: d_F = N_c for quarks, d_A = N_c^2 - 1 for gluons; Casimir C_F for quark self-energy, C_A for gluon self-energy

## Context

This work extends the QED-based quantum kinetic theory developed by Lin (2021) to full QCD, incorporating non-abelian group structure and gluon self-interactions that introduce additional self-energy diagrams. The formalism reproduces the spin-averaged Boltzmann equation for QCD from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002) at lowest gradient order, and enables systematic treatment of collisional contributions to spin polarization that were previously only possible with phenomenological interactions. The inelastic collision mechanism for spin-orbital angular momentum conversion provides a new framework for understanding spin alignment measurements of vector mesons from STAR (2017, 2019, 2022) and ALICE (2019, 2022, 2025).
