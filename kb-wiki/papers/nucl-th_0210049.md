# Shape of Dipole Radiative Strength Function for Asymmetric Nuclei

> The semiclassical method for description of the radiative strength function is used for asymmetric nuclei with $N \ne Z$. The theory is based on the linearized Vlasov-Landau equations in two-component finite Fermi liquid. The dependence of the shape $E1$ strength on the coupling constant between proton and neutron subsystems was studied.

## Key claim

The semiclassical Fermi-liquid model for the dipole radiative strength function is extended to asymmetric nuclei (N ≠ Z), and the calculations show that the shape of the strength function does not change strongly for nuclei with asymmetry coefficient |I| ≤ 0.25, allowing one-component models with modified width to be used in that range.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Landau-Vlasov kinetic equations for a two-component Fermi-liquid system (neutrons and protons) with a separable interaction potential and a collision integral in the relaxation-time approximation including retardation effects. The self-consistent mean field was treated with an infinite wall potential, and the strength function was derived from the response function of the system to an external dipole electric field. The coupling constants between nucleon subsystems (k^np) and within each subsystem (k^nn, k^pp) were varied to study their effect on the shape of the E1 strength function.

## Key numbers

- For ^208Pb, the coupling constants were set to k^nn = k^pp = 113/A^(5/3) (with A=208), and the neutron-proton coupling constant k^np was varied to study its effect on the resonance peak.
- The asymmetry coefficient is defined as I = (N-Z)/A; the shape of the radiative strength function does not change strongly for nuclei with |I| ≤ 0.25.
- The width of the radiative strength function is proportional to I^2.
- The coefficients for the dipole electric external field are C^(p)_1 = 2N/A and C^(n)_1 = 2Z/A.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Fermi-liquid model of Landau (Land1) and Torro (torro) to asymmetric nuclei, going beyond the two-component model of China (china) which did not include the interaction between nucleon subsystems or the difference in Fermi energies of neutrons and protons. The results enable the use of simplified one-component models of the radiative strength function for nuclei with moderate asymmetry, which is relevant for astrophysical calculations and applied problems involving nuclei far from the beta-stability valley.
