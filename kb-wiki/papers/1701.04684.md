# Diagonalization scheme for the many-body Schroedinger equation

> A new convenient method to diagonalize the non-relativistic many-body Schroedinger equation with two-body central potentials is derived. It combines kinematic rotations (democracy transformations) and exact calculation of overlap integrals between bases with different sets of mass-scaled Jacobi coordinates, thereby allowing for a great simplification of this formidable problem. We validate our method by obtaining a perfect correspondence with the exactly solvable three-body ($N=3$) Calogero model in 1D.

# Diagonalization scheme for the many-body Schroedinger equation

## Key claim
The authors present a new method to diagonalize the non-relativistic many-body Schroedinger equation with two-body central potentials that combines kinematic rotations (democracy transformations) with analytic calculation of overlap integrals between bases using different mass-scaled Jacobi coordinates. The method is validated only for the exactly solvable three-body (N=3) Calogero model in 1D with harmonic pairwise interactions, and the authors acknowledge that numerical approximations enter in the one-dimensional integrals and matrix diagonalization.

## Method
The method uses mass-scaled Jacobi coordinates to separate center-of-mass motion, then expands the wavefunction in a totally decoupled tensor-product basis of known orthonormal functions (one for each internal coordinate). For matrix elements of two-body potentials V_ij other than V_12, the authors perform a kinematic rotation (democracy transformation) to a new set of Jacobi coordinates where the pair ij becomes the "first" coordinate, then analytically calculate the overlap integrals (sigma coefficients) between the original and transformed basis states using umbral identities for Hermite polynomials and Gamma functions. The matrix elements are then reduced to one-dimensional radial integrals in a single coordinate.

## Key numbers
- The Calogero model spectrum reproduced is E_Cal = sqrt(3) * hbar * omega * (2n + l + 1) with n, l non-negative integers.
- The basis truncation used for validation was N_q = 15 quanta.
- The calculation runs in "a few seconds on a table-top machine" for the three-body 1D case.
- The sigma coefficient formula involves sums over k from 0 to n_1 and j from 0 to n_2, with factorial denominators 2^{-(n_1+n_2+n_u1+n_u2)/2} / (pi * sqrt(n_1! n_2! n_u1! n_u2!)).

## Context
This work extends the use of democracy transformations (previously applied by LoGa, BaNo, Fano) and mass-scaled Jacobi coordinates (De-Aq) to a general diagonalization scheme for N-body problems with two-body central potentials. The method is validated against the exactly solvable Calogero model (Cal1, Cal2) in 1D for three bosons, and the authors state it opens the way to studies of few-body systems in 3D (atoms, molecules, nuclei, BEC), Efimov states, condensation, and phase transitions. The approach contrasts with brute-force multidimensional integration by pushing analytical treatment as far as possible before numerical diagonalization.
