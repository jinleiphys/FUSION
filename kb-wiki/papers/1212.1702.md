# Global parton distributions with nuclear and finite-Q^2 corrections

> We present three new sets of next-to-leading order parton distribution functions (PDFs) determined by global fits to a wide variety of data for hard scattering processes. The analysis includes target mass and higher twist corrections needed for the description of deep inelastic scattering data at large x and low Q^2, and nuclear corrections for deuterium targets. The PDF sets correspond to three different models for the nuclear effects, and provide a more realistic uncertainty range for the d quark PDF, in particular, compared with previous fits. We describe the PDF error sets for each choice of the nuclear corrections, and provide a user interface for utilizing the distributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents three new sets of next-to-leading order parton distribution functions (CJ12min, CJ12mid, CJ12max) that differ only in the size of the nuclear corrections applied to deuterium target deep inelastic scattering data, providing a more realistic uncertainty range for the d quark PDF at large x than previous fits, but the choice of nuclear model is not uniquely determined by the data.

## Method

The authors performed global fits to 3958 data points from deep inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan, W and Z boson production, jet production, and photon plus jet production, using NLO QCD calculations with target mass corrections and a phenomenological higher twist term parametrized as a multiplicative 1/Q^2 factor. For deuterium DIS data, nuclear corrections were applied using the weak binding approximation with three different deuteron wave functions (WJC-1, AV18, CD-Bonn) combined with varying nucleon swelling effects (0.3%, 1.2%, 2.1%) to produce the mild, intermediate, and strong nuclear correction sets. The PDFs were parametrized at an input scale Q_0^2 = (1.3 GeV)^2 using a standard functional form with an additional admixture of u_v in the d_v parametrization to allow a finite d_v/u_v ratio as x approaches 1.

## Key numbers

- Total data points used in global fits: 3958, with kinematic cuts Q^2 > (1.3 GeV)^2 and W^2 > 3 GeV^2, allowing approximately 1300 more DIS data points than fits using standard cuts of Q^2 > 4 GeV^2 and W^2 > 14 GeV^2
- Total chi^2 values: 4059 (CJ12min), 4055 (CJ12mid), 4096 (CJ12max) for 3958 data points
- Strong coupling constant fixed at alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1180, corresponding to Lambda^(5) = 0.2268 GeV for 5 active flavors
- Charm and bottom quark mass thresholds set at m_c = 1.3 GeV and m_b = 4.5 GeV
- PDF error sets produced using Hessian method with tolerance T = 10, yielding 38 error PDFs for each of the three nuclear correction choices
- Strange quark PDF fixed as (s+bar s) = 0.4 (bar u + bar d)

## Context

This work extends the earlier CJ10 and CJ11 studies by Accardi et al. (2010, 2011) that systematically relaxed kinematic cuts on DIS data to access larger x values, synthesizing those lessons into three complete PDF sets. The CJ12 PDFs differ from other global fits such as CT10, MSTW08, and ABKM09 by using lower Q^2 and W^2 cuts and explicitly including nuclear corrections for deuterium targets, enabling constraints on PDFs at x values approaching 0.9. The results enable future studies of the d/u ratio at large x and provide a framework for evaluating theoretical uncertainties from nuclear corrections in observables sensitive to the d quark PDF, while the fixed strange quark treatment and omission of neutrino-iron dimuon data leave open questions about strangeness determination that will require future analysis including nuclear corrections for heavy nuclei.
