# Spectra and flow of light nuclei in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC

> Within the framework of the coalescence model based on the phase-space distributions of protons and neutrons generated from the {\tt iEBE-VISHNU} hybrid model with {\tt AMPT} initial conditions, we study the spectra and elliptic flow of deuterons and helium-3 in relativistic heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Larger Hadron Collider (LHC). Results from our model calculations for Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC are compared with available experimental data. Good agreements are generally seen between theoretical results and experimental data, except that the calculated yield of helium-3 in Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV underestimates the data by about a factor of two. Possible reasons for these discrepancies are discussed. We also make predictions on the spectra and elliptic flow of deuterons and helium-3 in Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV that are being studied at LHC.

## Key claim

The coalescence model using phase-space distributions of protons and neutrons from the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with AMPT initial conditions can generally describe the spectra and elliptic flow of deuterons and helium-3 in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, but the calculated yield of helium-3 in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV underestimates the experimental data by about a factor of two, even when including both the p+p+n and p+d coalescence channels.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model (combining 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics VISH2+1 with the UrQMD hadron cascade) with AMPT initial conditions to generate the phase-space distributions of protons and neutrons at kinetic freeze-out. These distributions were then input into a coalescence model that uses Wigner functions of harmonic oscillator wave functions to compute the production of deuterons and helium-3, including both the p+p+n and p+d coalescence channels for helium-3. The hydrodynamic parameters were fixed by previous fits to charged hadron yields, spectra, and flow harmonics.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: the model reproduces deuteron spectra from PHENIX and roughly describes helium-3 spectra from STAR for 0-80% centrality
- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: the calculated helium-3 yield underestimates the ALICE data by about a factor of two for 0-20% centrality
- The switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadron cascade is set to T_sw = 148 MeV
- The specific shear viscosity in QGP is set to eta/s = 0.08 with zero bulk viscosity
- The hydrodynamic starting time is tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- The deuteron width parameter sigma_rho = 2.473 fm, with charge radius R = 2.1421 fm
- The helium-3 width parameters for p+p+n channel: sigma_rho = 1.390 fm, sigma_lambda = 1.390 fm, with charge radius R = 1.9661 fm

## Context

This work extends previous coalescence model studies using modified blast-wave models (Yin 2017, Zhu 2017) by instead using the dynamically generated nucleon phase-space distributions from the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which naturally includes space-momentum correlations without additional free parameters. The iEBE-VISHNU model had previously been shown to describe spectra and flow of pions, kaons, and protons (Zhao 2017, Xu 2016). The results support the picture that light nuclei form through coalescence of nucleons at kinetic freeze-out, but the helium-3 underprediction at LHC energies suggests that additional production mechanisms or earlier formation times may need to be considered.
