# Pairing core swelling effect in Pb isotopes at $N>126$

> We revisit a sudden increase of the isotope shift of the charge radius of Pb isotope at $N>126$ based on a Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. New parametrizations of the pairing interaction optimized for selected four Skyrme interactions greatly improve a description of this phenomenon. The density-dependent spin-orbit interaction is also investigated and further increases the charge radius. The pairing correlations significantly change the properties of the neutron orbits near the Fermi level and play a vital role in pulling out the well-bound protons in the Pb isotopes at $N>126$. Regarding $^{208}$Pb as a "core" nucleus, a novel pairing core swelling effect is proposed: The pairing interaction reduces the radius of "valence" neutron orbits by the shrinkage of diffused $1g_{9/2}$ orbit and the mixing of sharp $0i_{11/2}$ orbit. Simultaneously, the core nucleus swells, leading to the sudden enhancement of the charge radius at $N > 126$. This characteristic behavior appears in the density profile near the nuclear surface and its measurement is highly desirable.

## Key claim

The sudden increase (kink) in the charge radius of Pb isotopes at neutron numbers N>126 is reproduced by a Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory only when the pairing interaction is optimized to reproduce the pairing-rotational energy around 208Pb; the density-dependent spin-orbit (DDLS) interaction further increases the charge radius but its effect is smaller than that of the pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Skyrme-HFB calculations using the HFBRAD solver for Pb isotopes with four standard Skyrme functionals (SkM*, SLy4, SV-min, UNEDF1) in the particle-hole channel and a mixed-type pairing interaction in the particle-particle channel. The pairing strength V0 for each functional was adjusted to reproduce the experimental pairing-rotational energy around 208Pb, and the parameters of a density-dependent spin-orbit (DDLS) interaction were tuned to preserve the experimental neutron single-particle level ordering while improving the charge radius isotope shift.

## Key numbers

- For 214Pb with SkM*: the occupation number of the 1g9/2 orbit changes from 6 (HF) to 3.68 (HFB) to 3.47 (HFB+DDLS); the occupation of the 0i11/2 orbit becomes 0.76 (HFB) and 1.30 (HFB+DDLS)
- The rms single-particle radius of the 1g9/2 orbit in 214Pb with SkM* is 6.57 fm (HF), 6.47 fm (HFB), and 6.48 fm (HFB+DDLS)
- The rms single-particle radius of the 0i11/2 orbit in 214Pb with SkM* is 6.31 fm (HFB) and 6.36 fm (HFB+DDLS)
- The charge radius r_ch of 214Pb with SkM* is 5.52 fm (HF), 5.54 fm (HFB), and 5.55 fm (HFB+DDLS)
- The pairing strength V0 for SkM* is -269 MeV for both HFB and HFB+DDLS; for SLy4 it is -321 MeV (HFB) and -323 MeV (HFB+DDLS)
- The DDLS parameters for SkM* are f_ls=0.75 and g_ls=0.15; for SLy4 they are f_ls=0.80 and g_ls=0.20

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the charge radius kink in Pb isotopes at N>126, previously studied by Reinhard (1994) and Nakada (2015) using Skyrme functionals with isospin-dependent or density-dependent spin-orbit interactions. The authors adopt the pairing-rotational energy method proposed by Hinohara (2016) to determine pairing strengths, improving upon conventional parametrizations. The proposed "pairing core swelling effect" parallels the core swelling mechanism recently discussed for Ca isotopes at N>28 by Ruiz (2016) and Tanaka (2020), and formalized by Horiuchi (2020) for spherical nuclei. The results enable future measurements of nuclear surface diffuseness to probe the degree of configuration mixing in Pb isotopes beyond N=126.
