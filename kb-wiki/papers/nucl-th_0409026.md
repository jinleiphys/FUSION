# The Delta(1232) at RHIC

> We investigate properties of the Delta(1232) and nucleon spectral functions at finite temperature and baryon density within a hadronic model. The medium modifications of the Delta consist of a renormalization of its pion-nucleon cloud and resonant pi-Delta scattering. Underlying coupling constants and form factors are determined by the elastic π-N scattering phase shift in the isobar channel, as well as empirical partial decay widths of excited baryon resonances. For cold nuclear matter the model provides reasonable agreement with photoabsorption data on nuclei in the Delta-resonance region. In hot hadronic matter typical for late stages of central Au-Au collisions at RHIC we find the Delta-spectral function to be broadened by $\sim$65 MeV together with a slight upward mass shift of 5-10 MeV, in qualitative agreement with preliminary data from the STAR collaboration.

## Key claim
The Delta(1232) spectral function in hot hadronic matter typical for late stages of central Au-Au collisions at RHIC is broadened by ~65 MeV with a slight upward mass shift of 5-10 MeV, in qualitative agreement with preliminary STAR data; the authors note that for more conclusive comparison a detailed treatment of freeze-out dynamics is mandatory.

## Method
The authors constructed a hadronic model using relativistic kinematics for baryons and fully relativistic pions, with interaction Lagrangians of nonrelativistic form involving spin-1/2 Pauli matrices and spin-3/2 transition operators. Coupling constants and form factors were determined by fitting the elastic π-N scattering phase shift in the isobar channel and empirical partial decay widths of excited baryon resonances (N(1440), N(1520), N(1535), Δ(1600), Δ(1620), Δ(1700)). In-medium self-energies were computed within the Matsubara finite-temperature field theory formalism, including renormalization of the π-N cloud and resonant π-Δ scattering, with pion and nucleon propagators modified by thermal pion interactions and p-wave nucleon- and Δ-hole excitations supplemented by short-range correlations via Migdal parameters.

## Key numbers
- Δ spectral function broadening of ~65 MeV in hot hadronic matter at RHIC conditions
- Upward mass shift of 5-10 MeV for the Δ at RHIC conditions
- At thermal freeze-out (T=100 MeV, ρ_N=0.12ρ_0): Δ peak position at M≈1.226 GeV and width Γ≈177 MeV
- Vacuum values: Δ peak position at M≈1.219 GeV and width Γ≈110 MeV
- Bare mass of Δ: m_Δ^(0)=1302 MeV
- πNΔ coupling constant: f_πNΔ=3.2
- πNΔ form-factor cutoff: Λ_πNΔ=290 MeV
- γNΔ coupling constant: f_γNΔ=0.653 with form-factor cutoff Λ_γNΔ=400 MeV
- Hadronic form factor cutoff (except πNN and πNΔ): Λ_πB1B2=500 MeV
- Nonresonant background estimate for photoabsorption: 80 μb
- Average nuclear density used for photoabsorption on nuclei: 0.8ρ_0
- Migdal parameters: g'_NN=0.8, g'_NΔ=g'_ΔΔ=0.33

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical studies of in-medium Δ properties in cold nuclear matter (Horiuchi 1980, Oset 1987, Ehehalt 1988, Migdal 1990, Xia 1994, Lutz 2003) and the few existing hot-matter treatments (Ko 1989, Korpa 1995). The model provides fair agreement with photoabsorption data on nuclei (Ahrens 1984, 1985; Frois 1992, 1994; Bianchi 1993, 1996) and enables future improvements including u-channel exchange diagrams, spin-3/2-Δ* excitations, and implementation of in-medium baryon propagators into chiral descriptions of axial/vector mesons to study the chiral phase transition and equation of state of hadronic matter.
