# Heavy Flavor in Medium Momentum Evolution: Langevin vs Boltzmann

> The propagation of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) has been often treated within the framework of the Langevin equation (LV), i.e. assuming the momentum transfer is small or the scatterings are sufficiently forward peaked, small screening mass $m_D$. We address a direct comparison between the Langevin dynamics and the Boltzmann collisional integral (BM) when a bulk medium is in equilibrium at fixed temperature. We show that unless the cross section is quite forward peaked ($m_D\cong T $) or the mass to temperature ratio is quite large ($M_{HQ}/T \gtrsim 8-10$) there are significant differences in the evolution of the $p-$spectra and consequently on nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(p_T)$. However for charm quark we find that very similar $R_{AA}(p_T)$ between the LV and BM can be obtained, but with a modified diffusion coefficient by about $\sim 15-50\%$ depending on the angular dependence of the cross section which regulates the momentum transfer. Studying also the momentum spread suffered by a single heavy quarks we see that at temperatures $T\gtrsim \, 250\,\rm MeV$ the dynamics of the scatterings is far from being of Brownian type for charm quarks. In the case of bottom quarks we essentially find no differences in the time evolution of the momentum spectra between the LV and the BM dynamics independently of the angular dependence of the cross section, at least in the range of temperature relevant for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Finally, we have shown the possible impact of this study on $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and $v_2(p_T)$ for a realistic simulation of relativistic HIC. For larger $m_D$ the elliptic flow can be about $50\%$ larger for the Boltzmann dynamics with respect to the Langevin. This is helpful for a simultaneous reproduction of $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and $v_2(p_T)$.

## Key claim

The Langevin equation is a good approximation to the full Boltzmann collision integral for heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma only when the cross section is quite forward peaked (screening mass \(m_D \cong T\)) or the heavy-quark mass-to-temperature ratio is quite large (\(M_{HQ}/T \gtrsim 8-10\)); otherwise, significant differences appear in the momentum spectra and nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\), though for charm quarks similar \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) can be obtained by adjusting the diffusion coefficient by about 15-50%.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation numerically using a stochastic implementation of the collision probability in a box with a bulk medium of gluons at fixed temperature, employing the pQCD Combridge scattering matrix for \(g+HQ \rightarrow g+HQ\) with a Debye screening mass \(m_D\) to regulate the t-channel divergence. They compared this to the Langevin equation (Fokker-Planck) using drag and diffusion coefficients derived from the same scattering matrix, for three values of \(m_D\) (0.4, 0.83, and 1.6 GeV) that produce angular distributions ranging from forward-peaked to nearly isotropic, at a temperature of \(T = 400\) MeV for both charm (\(M_c = 1.3\) GeV) and bottom (\(M_b = 4.2\) GeV) quarks.

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks at \(T = 400\) MeV with \(m_D = 0.83\) GeV, the Langevin-to-Boltzmann ratio of spectra deviates by about 40% at \(t = 4\) fm/c and about 50% at \(t = 6\) fm/c at \(p = 5\) GeV.
- For charm quarks with \(m_D = 0.4\) GeV (forward-peaked), the deviation is about 15% at \(t = 6\) fm/c and \(p \approx 5\) GeV.
- For charm quarks with \(m_D = 1.6\) GeV (nearly isotropic), the deviation can be as large as a factor of 70% at \(t = 4\) fm/c.
- For bottom quarks with \(m_D = 0.83\) GeV, the Langevin-to-Boltzmann ratio stays practically around one for all times, with at most a 10% difference for \(m_D = 1.6\) GeV.
- For charm quarks, similar \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) between Langevin and Boltzmann can be obtained by modifying the diffusion coefficient by about 15-50%.
- For larger \(m_D\), the elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) can be about 50% larger for the Boltzmann dynamics compared to the Langevin.

## Context

This work directly compares the Langevin (Fokker-Planck) and Boltzmann transport approaches for heavy quarks in a QGP, extending prior studies that often assumed the Fokker-Planck approximation was valid (e.g., Moore and Teaney (2005), Rapp and van Hees (2010), Gossiaux and Aichelin (2008)). The results show that for charm quarks at typical QGP temperatures, the dynamics are not of Brownian type, contradicting the common assumption of small momentum transfer, while for bottom quarks the Langevin approximation holds well. This enables more accurate modeling of heavy-flavor observables like \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) and \(v_2(p_T)\) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and suggests that future studies of \(D\bar{D}\) or \(B\bar{B}\) correlations and charmonium suppression may be sensitive to the choice of transport framework.
