# Higher order flow coefficients -- A Messenger of QCD medium formed in heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Anisotropic flow and fluctuations are sensitive observables of the initial state effects in heavy ion collisions and are characterized by the medium properties and final state interactions. Using event-shape observables, one can constrain the probability distributions of anisotropic flow coefficients, thus reducing the linear and nonlinear contributions in the measured higher-order harmonics. In this paper, we use transverse spherocity as an event shape observable to study the flow coefficients and elliptic flow fluctuations. Transverse spherocity is found to have a strong correlation with elliptic flow and its fluctuations. We exploit this feature of transverse spherocity to remove the contribution to elliptic flow from higher-order harmonics. The study is performed in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV using a multi-phase transport model. The multi-particle Q-cumulant method estimates the anisotropic flow coefficients, which reduces the non-flow contributions. We observe a stronger system response to the flow coefficients for the events with smaller values of elliptic flow.

## Key claim

Using transverse spherocity as an event-shape observable in AMPT simulations of Pb--Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the authors demonstrate that events with smaller elliptic flow values exhibit a stronger system response to higher-order flow coefficients, and that spherocity-based selection can more effectively separate events by elliptic flow magnitude than traditional reduced flow vector ($q_2$) selection, particularly in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The study uses the string-melting version (2.26t9b) of the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to simulate Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV. Transverse spherocity ($S_0$) is calculated for each event using charged hadrons with $|\eta|<0.8$ and $p_{\rm T}>0.15$ GeV/$c$, and events are classified into the lowest 20% (low-$S_0$, jetty) and highest 20% (high-$S_0$, isotropic) of the spherocity distribution. Anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ are estimated using the multi-particle Q-cumulant method with two- and four-particle correlations, applying a pseudorapidity gap $|\Delta\eta|>1.0$ in the two-subevent method to suppress non-flow contributions.

## Key numbers

- $v_2\{2\}$ and $v_2\{4\}$ are compared across centrality classes; the difference between them becomes negligibly small for low-$S_0$ events, while remaining significant for $S_0$-integrated events.
- The ratio of maximum to minimum $v_2\{2\}$ with $q_2$-based selection reduces from a factor of 3.8 in (0-10)% centrality to 3.6 in (60-70)% centrality, while $S_0$-based selection provides broader coverage of $v_2$ values across all centralities.
- $\sigma_{v_2}$ (elliptic flow fluctuation) increases from central to peripheral collisions for all spherocity classes; it is largest for $S_0$-integrated events and smallest for low-$S_0$ events.
- Relative eccentricity fluctuation $F(\epsilon_2)$ decreases from most central to mid-central collisions; for low-$S_0$ events, $F(\epsilon_2)$ begins to rise towards peripheral collisions, while for $S_0$-integrated and high-$S_0$ events it remains nearly flat from mid-central to peripheral.
- Relative elliptic flow fluctuation $F(v_2)$ is minimum for mid-central collisions and larger in most-central and peripheral collisions, consistent with ATLAS (2013) and CMS (2013) results at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using transverse spherocity in heavy-ion collisions by Mallick (2021), Prasad (2021, 2022), and Tripathy (2025), which established correlations between spherocity and elliptic/triangular flow, number-of-constituent-quark scaling, and symmetry plane correlations. The paper builds on the framework of nonlinear contributions to higher-order flow harmonics from lower-order harmonics, as formulated by Teaney (2013) and Gardim (2011), and on the multi-particle cumulant method of Bilandzic (2010, 2013). By demonstrating that spherocity-based event selection can reduce elliptic flow fluctuations and disentangle $v_2$ contributions from $v_4$ and $v_5$, this work enables more precise extraction of medium transport properties (such as specific shear viscosity) from higher-order flow coefficients in future experimental analyses at the LHC.
