# Multiple Critical Points in the QCD Phase Diagram

> We use the linear sigma model with two flavors of quarks to study the phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential as a function of the vacuum pion mass. Our calculations include thermal fluctuations of both the bosonic and fermionic fields. An interesting phase structure occurs that results in two, one, or no critical points depending on the value of the pion mass.

# Multiple Critical Points in the QCD Phase Diagram

## Key claim
Using the linear sigma model with two flavors of quarks, the phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential can exhibit two, one, or no critical points depending on the value of the vacuum pion mass, with the unconventional result of two critical points appearing for sufficiently small pion masses (e.g., 35 MeV).

## Method
The authors study the linear sigma model coupled to two flavors of identical mass quarks, integrating out the quark degrees of freedom to obtain an effective mesonic Lagrangian. They decompose the scalar field into a condensate plus a fluctuation, compute the thermodynamic potential self-consistently including thermal fluctuations of both bosonic and fermionic fields, and solve the resulting equations numerically to determine the phase boundaries. The vacuum pion mass is scanned from zero to over 300 MeV while fixing the pion decay constant at 92.4 MeV, the sigma mass at 700 MeV, and the quark mass at 313 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant fixed at f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Sigma mass fixed at m_sigma = 700 MeV
- Quark mass set to m_q = 313 MeV (one-third of the nucleon mass)
- Vacuum pion mass varied from 0 to m_sigma/2 = 350 MeV
- For m_pi,vac = 321 MeV, the critical point sits on the T = 0 axis; for m_pi,vac > 321 MeV, there is no phase transition at all
- For a vacuum pion mass of 35 MeV: first critical point at mu_c1 approximately 725 MeV and T_c1 approximately 92 MeV; second critical point at mu_c2 approximately 240 MeV and T_c2 approximately 137 MeV
- In the vicinity of mu = 575 MeV and T = 110 MeV, the latent heat gets pinched to zero for some critical value of the vacuum pion mass between 0 and 35 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies using the linear sigma model (Scavenius 2001, Fraser, Carter 1997, Carter 2000, Mocsy 2004) by including thermal fluctuations of both meson and fermion fields and scanning a wide range of vacuum pion masses. The results challenge the conventional wisdom that for two flavors of quarks there is a single curve of first-order phase transition terminating in one critical point, as found in models such as the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model (Asakawa 1989, Berges 1998, Scavenius 2001), random matrix models (Halasz 1998), and lattice QCD (Fodor 2002, Ejiri 2003, de Forcrand 2003, Gavai 2005). The finding of two critical points for small pion masses is novel and suggests that the phase diagram may be more complex than previously assumed, with potential implications for heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR and low-energy runs at RHIC.
