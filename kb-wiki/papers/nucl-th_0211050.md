# Collision damping in the pi 3He -> d'N reaction near the threshold

> We present a simple quantum mechanical model exploiting the optical potential approach for the description of collision damping in the reaction pi 3He -> d'N near the threshold, which recently has been measured at TRIUMF. The influence of the open d'N -> NNN channel is taken into account. It leads to a suppression factor of about ten in the d' survival probability. Applications of the method to other reactions are outlined.

## Key claim

The collision damping effect from the open d'N → NNN channel reduces the d' survival probability by a factor of about ten in the π³He → d'N reaction near threshold, bringing theoretical predictions into reasonable agreement with experimental data from TRIUMF, though the authors note that the pion exchange mechanism contributes only 30-40% of the observed spreading width, requiring additional mechanisms such as σ-exchange.

## Method

The authors developed a simple quantum mechanical model using an optical potential approach with a step-like imaginary potential to describe the propagation of the hypothetical d' resonance in the nuclear medium. They calculated the spreading width from virtual pion exchange using the d'NNπ vertex coupling constant f ≈ 14 (derived from Γ ≈ 0.5 MeV) and the pseudoscalar NNπ coupling g²/4π ≈ 14.3, then integrated over the three-particle phase space including final state interactions between nucleons in the relative s-wave. The survival probability was computed as a function of excess energy above threshold using the relation w_surv = exp(-ηRΓ_s / (ρ_A V v)).

## Key numbers

- The d' decay width Γ ≈ 0.5 MeV (deduced from DCX reactions to discrete levels)
- The d'NNπ coupling constant f ≈ 14
- The pseudoscalar NNπ coupling g²/4π ≈ 14.3
- The inelastic cross section σ_in = 4.5/v mb (in the limit v → 0)
- The spreading width Γ_s ≈ 10-20 MeV (with pion exchange contributing only 30-40% of this)
- The survival probability w_surv ≈ 0.1 (only about 10% of created resonances survive) for excess energy of 3 MeV above threshold
- The ratio of elastic to inelastic cross section σ_el/σ_in ≈ 1/10 (for velocity v ∼ 1/10)
- The Pauli blocking factor η ≈ 2.2
- The nuclear matter density ρ_A = (1/2)m_π³
- The NN s-wave scattering length parameter R ≈ 0.8 fm
- The enhancement factor η_0 ≈ 4-5 for NN FSI in d' decay

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the d' resonance hypothesis, which was suggested to explain the peculiar resonance-like behavior of pionic double charge exchange (DCX) on nuclei from ⁷Li to ⁹³Nb at T_π ≈ 50 MeV (observed by GKD and modeled by Nuseirat et al. and Gibbs and Wu). The model specifically addresses the suppression of d' production cross sections observed in ⁴He(π⁺,π⁻) and ³He(π⁻,π⁺) reactions (JG4 and JG3), where the contribution was almost an order of magnitude smaller than expected. The authors note that a similar FSI mechanism explains the surprisingly small Σ production cross section in pp → KNY reactions near threshold (sigma), where Σ⁰ is transferred into Λ via strong FSI.
