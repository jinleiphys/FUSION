# Scalar tetraquarks with open bottom

> The relativistic four-quark equations in the framework of the dispersion relation technique are derived. The four-quark amplitudes of the scalar tetraquarks with open bottom, including u, d, s and bottom quarks, are constructed. The poles of these amplitudes determine the masses and widths of scalar tetraquarks.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that scalar tetraquarks with open bottom have relatively small widths (approximately 50-100 MeV), making them potentially observable exotic states, but these predictions depend on a model with fixed cutoff and coupling parameters determined from hidden-bottom tetraquark states.

## Method

The authors derive relativistic four-quark equations using the dispersion relation technique with a coupled-channel formalism that includes dynamical mixing between meson-meson states and four-quark states. They apply the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach to construct the four-quark amplitude for scalar tetraquarks with open bottom, using the dispersion N/D method with a four-fermion interaction input. The system of integral equations is reduced to algebraic equations by extracting leading singularities and expanding smooth functions around a chosen point in the physical region.

## Key numbers

- Tetraquark (anti-b u anti-u u) mass M = 5914 MeV with width Gamma = 104 MeV
- X_b(6020) mass M = 6017 MeV with width Gamma = 69 MeV (decay channels B_s^0 eta and B^+ K^-)
- Tetraquark (anti-b s u anti-s) mass M = 6122 MeV with width Gamma = 48 MeV
- Quark masses used: m_u,d = 385 MeV, m_s = 510 MeV, m_b = 4787 MeV
- Cutoff parameter Lambda = 7.63 and gluon coupling constant g = 1.53, fixed from hidden-bottom tetraquark masses

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of tetraquarks, including their earlier calculation of the X(3700) tetraquark with width 140 MeV (Gerasyuta and Kochkin, 2008). The approach builds on the tetraquark explanation for the X(3872) proposed by Maiani et al. (2005, 2007) and contrasts with calculations by Ebert et al. (2006) who found light scalar tetraquarks above the open charm threshold. The results enable experimental searches for open-bottom tetraquark states in the 5.9-6.1 GeV mass range, particularly through B meson decay channels.
