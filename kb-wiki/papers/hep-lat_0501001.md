# Monte Carlo simulations of the NJL model near the nonzero temperature phase transition

> We present results from numerical simulations of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with an SU(2)xSU(2) chiral symmetry and N_c=4,8, and 16 quark colors at nonzero temperature. We performed the simulations by utilizing the hybrid Monte Carlo and hybrid Molecular Dynamics algorithms. We show that the model undergoes a second order phase transition. The critical exponents measured are consistent with the classical 3d O(4) universality class and hence in accordance with the dimensional reduction scenario. We also show that the Ginzburg region is suppressed by a factor of 1/N_c in accordance with previous analytical predictions.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with SU(2)xSU(2) chiral symmetry undergoes a second order phase transition at nonzero temperature, with critical exponents consistent with the 3d O(4) universality class, but only when sufficiently large lattice volumes are used to reach the asymptotic finite-size scaling regime; smaller lattices produce significantly different exponent values.

## Method

The authors performed hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) simulations for N_c=8 and 16 quark colors, and hybrid Molecular Dynamics (HMD) simulations for N_c=4, on asymmetric lattices with fixed temporal extent L_t=4 and spatial sizes ranging from L_s=16 to L_s=54. They employed finite-size scaling analysis of the susceptibility, logarithmic derivatives of the order parameter, and the effective order parameter itself, using histogram reweighting to interpolate between simulation couplings. In the broken phase, they fitted the order parameter data to both mean-field and 3d O(4) scaling forms to determine the crossover between scaling regimes.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling for N_c=8: β_c = 0.52589(9) from susceptibility fits on lattices L_s=16 to 54
- Correlation length exponent: ν = 0.747(20) from susceptibility, consistent with 3d O(4) value 0.7479(90)
- γ/ν = 1.94(5) from susceptibility, consistent with 3d O(4) value 1.97(1)
- β_mag/ν = 0.566(8) from order parameter fits, deviating from 3d O(4) value 0.5129(11)
- For N_c=4, crossover from 3d O(4) scaling to mean-field occurs at β_cross ≈ 0.465 with ⟨Φ⟩_cross = 0.34
- For N_c=8, crossover occurs at β_cross ≈ 0.5175 with ⟨Φ⟩_cross = 0.190
- N_c=16 data fits mean-field scaling for all β values studied
- Inverse lattice spacing at critical coupling for N_c=8 is approximately 1 GeV when fixing F_π=93 MeV and constituent quark mass=400 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous analytical predictions by Stephanov (1994) that the Ginzburg critical region in four-fermion models is suppressed by 1/N_c, and provides numerical confirmation using lattice simulations. The results support the dimensional reduction scenario proposed by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984), where the finite-temperature chiral transition in QCD with two massless quarks should be described by the 3d O(4) universality class. The study contrasts with earlier large-N_c mean-field predictions by Rosenstein and Kocic (1993) and with results by Oertel (2004) who found a first-order transition when including mesonic fluctuations. The findings enable future comparisons with strong-coupling QCD results where the critical region width does not depend on N_c.
