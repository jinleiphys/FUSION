# The Chrial Magnetic Wave and Strong Field Effects in Heavy Ion Collisions

> A number of recent progresses in the study of strong field effects in heavy ion collisions are discussed here: 1) the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) and its experimental manifestation via splitting of positive/negative pions' elliptic flow; 2) the event-by-event azimuthal fluctuations of strong EM fields and its correlations with matter geometry; 3) a new mechanism for generating axial current in external electric field, the Chiral Electric Separation Effect (CESE).

# The Chiral Magnetic Wave and Strong Field Effects in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW), arising from the interplay between the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and the Chiral Separation Effect (CSE), induces an electric charge quadrupole moment in the quark-gluon plasma that leads to a splitting of positive and negative pion elliptic flow linearly dependent on net charge asymmetry; however, other mechanisms may also contribute to the observed v2 splitting, and final conclusions require improved modeling and additional measurements.

## Method
The author reviews theoretical developments in strong field effects in heavy ion collisions, focusing on three topics: (1) the CMW as a collective excitation derived from combining CME and CSE wave equations with diffusion terms, solved numerically with initial conditions and QGP properties to predict charge density distributions; (2) event-by-event Monte Carlo calculations of electromagnetic field azimuthal orientations relative to participant planes using a nuclear collision model; (3) derivation of the Chiral Electric Separation Effect (CESE) from chiral kinetic theory in thermal QED plasma, yielding a conductivity expression.

## Key numbers
- CMW wave velocity: v = (N_c e B alpha)/(2 pi^2), where N_c is the number of colors, e is the elementary charge, B is the magnetic field strength, and alpha is the susceptibility connecting charge density and chemical potential.
- CMW calculations assumed magnetic field strength eB = m_pi^2 (where m_pi is the pion mass), magnetic field lifetime tau = 4 fm/c, temperature T = 165 MeV, and impact parameter b = 3 fm for the charge density plots.
- The slope parameter r_e (in percent) from CMW calculations was compared with STAR data for magnetic field lifetimes tau = 3, 4, 5, and 6 fm/c across centrality classes.
- CESE conductivity for thermal QED plasma: sigma_e = chi_e mu_V mu_A approximately 20.499 (mu_V mu_A / T^2) (T / (e^3 ln(1/e))).
- The optimal centrality class for searching strong field effects corresponds to impact parameter range b ~ 8-10 fm, based on azimuthal correlation studies.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of chiral effects in heavy ion collisions, building on the original CME prediction by Kharzeev (2004) and CSE by Son and Zhitnitsky (2004) and Metlitski and Zhitnitsky (2005). The CMW was first proposed by Kharzeev and Yee (2010) and its experimental signature via pion elliptic flow splitting was predicted by Burnier, Kharzeev, Liao, and Yee (2011). The author's event-by-event field fluctuation study (Bloczynski, Liao, and collaborators, 2012) extends earlier field calculations by Bzdak and Skokov (2011) and Deng and Huang (2012). The CESE (Huang and Liao, 2013) fills a gap in the known set of chiral transport effects. These developments enable quantitative comparison with STAR measurements of charge-dependent elliptic flow and suggest new measurements in asymmetric Cu-Au collisions at RHIC.
