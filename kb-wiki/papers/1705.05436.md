# Pairing and deformation effects in nuclear excitation spectra

> We investigate effects of pairing and of quadrupole deformation on two sorts of nuclear excitations,$γ$-vibrational $K^π=2^+$ states and dipole resonances (isovector dipole, pygmy, compression, toroidal). The analysis is performed within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) based on the Skyrme energy functional using the Skyrme parametrization SLy6. Particular attention is paid to i) the role of the particle-particle (pp) channel in the residual interaction of QRPA, ii) comparison of volume pairing (VP) and surface pairing (SP), iii) peculiarities of deformation splitting in the various resonances. We find that the impact of the pp-channel on the considered excitations is negligible. This conclusion applies also to any other excitation except for the $K^π=0^+$ states. Furthermore, the difference between VP and SP is found small (with exception of peak height in the toroidal mode). In the low-energy isovector dipole (pygmy) and isoscalar toroidal modes, the branch $K^π=1^-$ is shown to dominate over $K^π=0^-$ one in the range of excitation energy $E <$ 8--10 MeV. The effect becomes impressive for the toroidal resonance whose low-energy part is concentrated in a high peak of almost pure $K^π=1^-$ nature. This peculiarity may be used as a fingerprint of the toroidal mode in future experiments. The interplay between pygmy, toroidal and compression resonances is discussed, the interpretation of the observed isoscalar giant dipole resonance is partly revised.

## Key claim

The particle-particle (pp) channel in the QRPA residual interaction has a negligible impact on nuclear excitations with \(K^{\pi} \ne 0^+\), including \(\gamma\)-vibrational \(K^{\pi}=2^+\) states and all dipole resonances, though this conclusion is qualified by the observation that the pp-channel remains important for \(K^{\pi}=0^+\) states where it generates pairing vibrations.

## Method

The analysis was performed using the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) based on the Skyrme energy functional with the SLy6 parametrization, implemented in a two-dimensional code with cylindrical coordinates. The calculations used a HF+BCS scheme for pairing, employing a monopole contact \(\delta\)-force interaction with both volume pairing (VP, \(\eta=0\)) and surface pairing (SP, \(\eta=1\)) as limiting cases. The study examined the lowest \(\gamma\)-vibrational \(K^{\pi}=2^+\) states and four types of dipole resonances (isovector GDR, pygmy PDR, compression CDR, and toroidal TDR) in the well-deformed, axially symmetric nuclei \(^{152,154,156}\)Sm.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters \(\beta\) = 0.311, 0.339, and 0.361 for \(^{152,154,156}\)Sm, respectively, obtained from minimization of total nuclear energy; these are close to experimental values \(\beta_{\rm exp}\) = 0.308 for \(^{152}\)Sm and \(\beta_{\rm exp}\) = 0.339 for \(^{154}\)Sm.
- For the Skyrme force SLy6, the ratio of ph-channel to pp-channel strengths is \(R_n=1.50\) and \(R_p=1.45\) for VP, and \(R_n=0.50\) and \(R_p=0.41\) for SP, with the ph-channel dominated by the proton-neutron interaction having amplitude ratio \(b_0/(b_0-b_0')=16.1\).
- The isoscalar quadrupole energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for \(\gamma\)-vibrational modes in \(^{154}\)Sm is exhausted by approximately 97%, and the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule for dipole excitations is exhausted in the interval 2–45 MeV by approximately 94%.
- The toroidal TDR peak at 7–8 MeV is almost fully built from \(K^{\pi}=1^-\) strength, and SP makes this peak essentially higher than VP, representing the only significant difference between VP and SP observed in the study.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent Skyrme QRPA studies of \(\gamma\)-vibrational states that were performed both with the pp-channel (Te11g) and without it (Ne16g), and also builds on earlier investigations of deformation splitting in dipole resonances (Kv13Sm, Kv14Yb, Ne17). The finding that the pp-channel can be ignored for all excitations with \(K^{\pi} \ne 0^+\) considerably simplifies future QRPA calculations, while the observed dominance of the \(K^{\pi}=1^-\) branch in the low-energy toroidal resonance may serve as a fingerprint for experimental identification of the toroidal mode. The results also support the interpretation that the pygmy dipole resonance is a local boundary manifestation of the toroidal nuclear flow, as previously suggested by Re13.
