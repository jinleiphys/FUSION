# In-Medium Quarkonia at SPS, RHIC and LHC

> A kinetic-rate equation approach in a thermally expanding medium is employed to calculate the evolution of charmonium and bottomonium distributions in heavy-ion collisions. The equilibrium properties of the quarkonia are taken from in-medium spectral functions which are schematically constrained by euclidean correlators from lattice QCD. The initial conditions for the rate equation (heavy-flavor cross sections, nuclear absorption) and the thermal evolution are constrained by data as available. After fixing two free parameters to describe charmonium data at SPS and RHIC, the predictions for LHC are discussed in light of recent data.

# In-Medium Quarkonia at SPS, RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

A kinetic-rate equation approach, with two free parameters fixed to charmonium data at SPS and RHIC, provides a unified description of both charmonium and bottomonium suppression and regeneration across collision energies from SPS through RHIC to LHC, with the strong-binding scenario (rather than weak-binding) favored by data.

## Method

The authors solve a kinetic rate equation for quarkonium number evolution in a thermally expanding fireball, using in-medium spectral functions from a T-matrix approach constrained by lattice QCD Euclidean correlators. Quasifree dissociation is used for charmonia (binding energy less than temperature) and gluo-dissociation for bottomonia (binding energy greater than temperature), with the coupling constant as one fit parameter; the charm-quark relaxation time is the second fit parameter. Initial conditions for heavy-flavor cross sections and nuclear absorption are constrained by available experimental data, and feeddown from excited states is included.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark relaxation time: tau_c^eq ~ 5 fm/c (strong-binding scenario)
- Coupling constant: alpha_s ~ 0.3 for quasifree dissociation rate (strong-binding scenario)
- Regeneration component reaches ca. 30% of total J/psi yield in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 AGeV)
- Charm cross section per unit rapidity used for LHC predictions: 0.5 mb and 0.33 mb (two scenarios)
- SPS energy: sqrt(s)=17.3 AGeV for Pb-Pb collisions
- RHIC energy: sqrt(s)=200 AGeV for Au-Au collisions
- LHC energy: sqrt(s)=2.76 ATeV for Pb-Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the kinetic-rate equation approach of Grandchamp (2003) and Zhao (2010) to simultaneously describe charmonium and bottomonium observables across multiple collision energies. The results are consistent with the transport approach of Liu (2009) and the statistical hadronization model of Andronic (2006), though the latter uses smaller charm cross sections. The findings enable discrimination between suppression and regeneration mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions, with bottomonium suppression providing a more direct probe of the in-medium QCD force and suggesting a strong binding potential persists into the quark-gluon plasma.
