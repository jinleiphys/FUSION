# Unusual temperature behavior of entropy of antiferromagnetic spin state in nuclear matter with effective finite range interaction

> The unusual temperature behavior of the entropy of the antiferromagnetic (AFM) spin state in symmetric nuclear matter with the Gogny D1S interaction, being larger at low temperatures than the entropy of nonpolarized matter, is related to the dependence of the entropy on the effective masses of nucleons in a spin polarized state. The corresponding conditions for comparing the entropies of the AFM and nonpolarized states in terms of the effective masses are formulated, including low and high temperature limits. It is shown that the unexpected temperature behavior of the entropy of the AFM spin state at low temperatures is caused by the violation of the corresponding low temperature criterium.

## Key claim
The entropy of the antiferromagnetic (AFM) spin state in symmetric nuclear matter with the Gogny D1S interaction is larger than that of nonpolarized matter at low temperatures, contrary to intuitive expectation. This unusual behavior is caused by the violation of a low-temperature criterion expressed in terms of the effective masses of nucleons, and does not lead to thermodynamic instability of the AFM state.

## Method
The author uses the finite-range Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction within a Fermi liquid approach, solving self-consistent equations for the distribution functions and quasiparticle spectra in symmetric nuclear matter. The entropy and free energy of the AFM spin state are computed and compared to those of the nonpolarized state, using both exact single-particle energies and a quadratic approximation that introduces effective masses. Low-temperature and high-temperature expansions of the entropy are derived in terms of these effective masses, yielding analytic criteria for the sign of the entropy difference.

## Key numbers
- Critical density for AFM spin ordering at zero temperature: \(\varrho_c \approx 3.8\varrho_0\), where \(\varrho_0 = 0.16\,\text{fm}^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density.
- At density \(\varrho = 0.64\,\text{fm}^{-3}\), the low-temperature expansion for the entropy difference is valid up to approximately \(T = 10\,\text{MeV}\).
- The effective mass approximation reproduces the exact entropy density to within \(7\%\) even for totally polarized matter over the entire temperature domain considered.
- Critical densities \(\varrho_S\) above which the entropy difference becomes positive at low temperatures: \(\varrho_S(\Pi=0.3) \approx 0.07\,\text{fm}^{-3}\), \(\varrho_S(\Pi=0.6) \approx 0.08\,\text{fm}^{-3}\), \(\varrho_S(\Pi=1) \approx 0.17\,\text{fm}^{-3}\).
- For comparison, totally polarized neutron matter with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction gives \(\varrho_S \approx 0.15\,\text{fm}^{-3}\) (from RPV reference).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Isayev, 2006) which first identified AFM spin ordering in symmetric nuclear matter with the Gogny D1S interaction. It contrasts with results for Skyrme effective interactions (RPV reference), where the entropy of spin-polarized neutron matter remains larger than that of nonpolarized matter at all temperatures above a critical density, leading to a decrease of the critical density with temperature. For the Gogny interaction, the entropy difference changes sign from positive at low temperatures to negative at high temperatures, so the critical density for AFM ordering increases with temperature, consistent with intuitive expectations. The findings enable further study of spin-polarized states in nuclear matter relevant to supernova explosions and neutron star cooling.
