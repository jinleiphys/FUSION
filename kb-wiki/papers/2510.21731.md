# QCD in strong magnetic fields: fluctuations of conserved charges and EoS

> Strong magnetic fields can profoundly affect the equilibrium properties, characterized by the equation of state and bulk thermodynamics of strongly interacting matter. Although such fields are expected in off-central heavy-ion collisions, directly measuring their experimental imprints remains extremely challenging. To address this, we propose the baryon-electric charge correlations $χ^{\rm BQ}_{11}$ and the chemical potential ratio $μ_{\rm Q}/μ_{\rm B}$ as magnetic-field-sensitive probes, based on (2+1)-flavor QCD lattice simulations at physical pion masses. Along the transition line, $χ^{\rm BQ}_{11}$ and $(μ_{\rm Q}/μ_{\rm B})_{\rm LO}$ in Pb-Pb collisions increase by factors of 2.1 and 2.4 at $eB \simeq 8M_π^2$, respectively. To bridge theoretical predictions and experimental observations, we construct HRG-based proxies and apply systematic kinematic cuts to emulate STAR and ALICE detector acceptances. Furthermore, we extend this investigation to the QCD equation of state, and examine the leading-order thermodynamic coefficients for strangeness-neutral scenarios up to $eB \simeq 0.8 {\rm GeV}^2 \sim 45 m_π^2$, revealing intriguing non-monotonic structures.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the baryon-electric charge correlation χBQ₁₁ and the chemical potential ratio μQ/μB serve as magnetic-field-sensitive probes in heavy-ion collisions, but the abstract's claim of "factors of 2.1 and 2.4" applies specifically along the transition line at eB ≃ 8Mπ², and the results are based on lattice QCD simulations that require HRG-based proxies with kinematic cuts to bridge to experimental observables.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations with the HISQ action at physical pion masses on 32³×8 and 48³×12 lattices, computing second-order fluctuations and correlations of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in a constant U(1) magnetic background. They constructed HRG-based proxies for net-conserved charges (using protons, pions, and kaons) and applied systematic kinematic cuts on transverse momentum and pseudo-rapidity to emulate STAR and ALICE detector acceptances. The leading-order thermodynamic coefficients for strangeness-neutral scenarios with isospin asymmetry r = nQ/nB = 0.4 were extracted via Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure.

## Key numbers

- χBQ₁₁ and (μQ/μB)LO in Pb-Pb collisions increase by factors of 2.1 and 2.4 respectively at eB ≃ 8Mπ² along the transition line
- Lattice results extend up to eB ≃ 0.8 GeV² ∼ 45 mπ² for the equation of state
- For the Ru isobar collision system, R((μQ/μB)LO) reaches approximately 4 at eB ≃ 8Mπ², about 1.5 times stronger magnetic sensitivity than Pb/Au/Zr
- The Rcp-like ratio for χBQ₁₁/χQ₂ shows enhancements of ∼2 at eB ≃ 8Mπ²
- The Rcp-like ratio for χBQ₁₁/χQS₁₁ shows enhancements of ∼2.25 at eB ≃ 8Mπ²
- HRG-based proxies with kinematic cuts retain at least ∼80% of the magnetic lattice QCD sensitivity for χBQ₁₁/χQ₂
- With kinematic cuts, proxies yield increases up to 25% for χBQ₁₁/χQ₂ and up to 60% for χBQ₁₁/χQS₁₁ at eB ≃ 8Mπ²
- In the magnetized ideal gas high-temperature limit, -q₁ saturates to 0.2308 and s₁ saturates to 0.4103
- Non-monotonic structures in the leading-order pressure coefficient P₂ emerge for eB ≳ 0.6 GeV², with a peak structure at eB = 0.79 GeV²

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies that used heavier-than-physical pion masses (Mπ ≃ 220 MeV) at a single lattice spacing (Ding 2021) to physical pion masses with continuum estimates, building on recent results (Ding 2023, Ding 2025). The proposed χBQ₁₁-based observables provide QCD benchmarks for interpreting heavy-ion collision data, with the ALICE collaboration already reporting centrality-dependent enhancements in χBQ₁₁/χQ₂ (ALICE 2025) that qualitatively agree with these theoretical predictions. The results enable experimental tests of magnetic field effects in relativistic nuclear collisions and deepen understanding of the QCD equation of state under extreme magnetic conditions, with implications for the quark-gluon plasma and magnetar physics.
