# Deconfinement Phase Transition with External Magnetic Field in Friedberg-Lee Model

> The deconfinement phase transition with external magnetic field is investigated in the Friedberg-Lee model. In the frame of functional renormalization group, we extend the often used potential expansion method for continuous phase transitions to the first-order phase transition in the model. By solving the flow equations we find that, the magnetic field displays a catalysis effect and it becomes more difficult to break through the confinement in hot and dense medium.

## Key claim

Using the functional renormalization group (FRG) method extended to first-order phase transitions in the Friedberg-Lee model, the author finds that an external magnetic field displays a catalysis effect on the deconfinement phase transition, making it more difficult to break through confinement in hot and dense medium, in contrast to lattice simulations that show inverse magnetic catalysis.

## Method

The author applies the functional renormalization group (FRG) method to the Friedberg-Lee model, a nontopological soliton model where confinement is simulated via a nonlinear scalar field. The potential expansion method, usually used for continuous phase transitions, is generalized to first-order phase transitions by expanding the effective potential separately around the two local minima and comparing them to determine the true ground state. The flow equations are solved numerically from an ultraviolet scale of Λ = 2000 MeV down to k = 0, including the effects of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and external magnetic field via Landau level summation for the quark sector.

## Key numbers

- In vacuum at zero temperature and density, the first-order phase transition occurs at a critical scale k_c = 268 MeV, with the physical vacuum located at σ = 44 MeV and the confined quark mass m_q = gσ = 547 MeV.
- With increasing magnetic field at T = μ = 0, the quark mass rises continuously from 547 MeV in vacuum to 606 MeV at eB = 16 m_π².
- The critical temperature T_c at μ = 0 and the critical baryon chemical potential μ_c at T = 0 both increase monotonically with magnetic field strength, indicating magnetic catalysis.
- The initial parameters for the FRG calculation are taken at the ultraviolet scale Λ = 2000 MeV, with the first parameter set using a = 1749.51 fm⁻², b = -6400.35 fm⁻¹, c = 16549.2, and g = 12.416.

## Context

This work extends the Friedberg-Lee model (first proposed by Friedberg and Lee) to include an external magnetic field, using the FRG method previously applied to chiral symmetry and deconfinement in hadron and quark matter studies. The result of magnetic catalysis (increasing critical temperature and density with field strength) contradicts lattice simulations that show inverse magnetic catalysis for the Polyakov loop, and also differs from the MIT bag model and Polyakov quark meson model which find inverse catalysis, while being consistent with the Polyakov NJL model without a magnetic-field-dependent coupling. The work enables further study of deconfinement phase transitions in strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy ion collisions and compact stars.
