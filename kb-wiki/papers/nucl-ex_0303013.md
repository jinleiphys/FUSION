# Deuteron Photodissociation in Ultraperipheral Relativistic Heavy-Ion on Deuteron Collisions

> In ultraperipheral relativistic deuteron on heavy-ion collisions, a photon emitted from the heavy nucleus may dissociate the deuterium ion. We find deuterium breakup cross sections of 1.38 barns for deuterium-gold collisions at a center of mass energy of 200 GeV per nucleon, as studied at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, and 2.49 barns for deuterium-lead collisions at a center of mass energy of 6.2 TeV, as proposed for the Large Hadron Collider. This cross section includes an energy-independent 140 mb contribution from hadronic diffractive dissociation. At the LHC, the cross section is as large as that of hadronic interactions. The estimated error is 5%. Deuteron dissociation could be used as a luminosity monitor and a `tag' for moderate impact parameter collisions.

# Deuteron Photodissociation in Ultraperipheral Relativistic Heavy-Ion on Deuteron Collisions

## Key claim

The total deuteron dissociation cross section in ultraperipheral dA collisions is 1.38 barns for dAu at RHIC and 2.49 barns for dPb at the LHC, with an estimated 5% uncertainty; however, these values include an energy-independent 140 mb contribution from hadronic diffractive dissociation, and the photodissociation component alone is 1.24 barns and 2.35 barns respectively.

## Method

The photodissociation cross section is calculated by integrating the Weizsacker-Williams photon flux from the heavy ion over the photon-deuteron breakup cross section, using impact parameters larger than the sum of the nuclear radii (R_min = 9.08 fm for dAu, 9.21 fm for dPb). The photon-deuteron breakup cross section is constructed from experimental measurements at photon energies from 2.754 MeV up to 75 MeV, combined with theoretical calculations below 4 MeV and fits to data from 20 to 440 MeV, with an extrapolation to 2 GeV. The diffractive dissociation contribution is calculated separately using a Glauber-type absorptive model for a completely black target nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Total dissociation cross section for dAu at 200 GeV/nucleon center-of-mass energy: 1.38 barns (includes 136 mb diffractive contribution)
- Total dissociation cross section for dPb at 6.2 TeV/nucleon center-of-mass energy: 2.49 barns (includes 139 mb diffractive contribution)
- Photodissociation cross section alone for dAu at RHIC: 1.24 barns
- Photodissociation cross section alone for dPb at LHC: 2.35 barns
- Estimated overall uncertainty on total cross section: 5%
- Hadronic cross section for dAu at RHIC: 2.26 +/- 0.10 barns (Kharzeev, Levin and Nardi, 2002) and 2.37 +0.13/-0.12 barns (Miller, private communication)
- Deuteron breakup probability at impact parameter 10 fm for dAu at RHIC: 1.8%, dropping to 0.1% at 45 fm
- Event rate for dAu at RHIC at luminosity 4 x 10^28 cm^-2 s^-1: 50,000 interactions per second
- Event rate for dPb at LHC at luminosity 2.7 x 10^29 cm^-2 s^-1: 650,000 interactions per second
- Increasing R_min by 2 fm reduces the cross section by 1.8% for gold at RHIC and 1.1% for lead at LHC

## Context

This work extends the study of ultraperipheral collisions from AA systems to dA collisions, where the photon source is unambiguous because the photon almost always comes from the heavy ion. The photodissociation cross section is comparable to the hadronic dA cross sections calculated by Kharzeev, Levin and Nardi (2002) and Miller (private communication). The reaction provides a clean experimental signature (a proton and neutron with roughly beam momenta and no other visible products) that can serve as a luminosity monitor and as an impact parameter tag for other ultraperipheral collisions, analogous to the use of mutual Coulomb dissociation in heavy ion collisions (Chiu et al., 2002; STAR Collaboration, 2002; Baltz, Klein and Nystrand, 2002).
