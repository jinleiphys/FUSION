# Towards a parameter-free determination of critical exponents and chiral phase transition temperature in QCD

> In order to quantify the universal properties of the chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD, we make use of an improved, renormalized order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking which is obtained as a suitable difference of the $2$-flavor light quark chiral condensate and its corresponding light quark susceptibility. Having no additive ultraviolet as well as multiplicative logarithmic divergences, we use ratios of this order parameter constructed from its values for two different light quark masses. We show that this facilitates determining in a parameter-independent manner, the chiral phase transition temperature $T_c$ and the associated critical exponent $δ$ which, for sufficiently small values of the light quark masses, controls the quark mass dependence of the order parameter at $T_c$. We present first results of these calculations from our numerical analysis performed with staggered fermions on $N_τ=8$ lattices.

# Towards a parameter-free determination of critical exponents and chiral phase transition temperature in QCD

## Key claim

The authors show that ratios of a renormalized chiral order parameter, constructed from the light quark condensate and its susceptibility, can determine the chiral phase transition temperature \(T_c\) and critical exponent \(\delta\) without assuming a specific universality class *a priori*, provided the light quark masses are sufficiently small that sub-leading corrections are negligible. The method works only when the light-to-strange quark mass ratio \(H = m_\ell/m_s\) is smaller than about 1/40; for larger ratios (e.g., \(H = 1/27\), corresponding to physical quark masses), the unique intersection point is lost due to regular contributions.

## Method

The authors use (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with the HISQ action and \({\cal O}(a^2)\) improved Symanzik gauge action on \(N_\tau = 8\) lattices. They construct a renormalized order parameter \(M(T,H) = M_\ell(T,H) - H\,\chi_\ell(T,H)\), where \(H = m_\ell/m_s\), which eliminates additive ultraviolet divergences and multiplicative logarithmic divergences. They compute ratios \(R(T,H,c) = M(T,cH)/M(T,H)\) and their logarithmic form \(B(T,H,c) = \ln R / \ln c\) for different light quark masses, and locate the unique intersection point of these ratios as a function of temperature to extract \(T_c\) and \(\delta\).

## Key numbers

- The chiral phase transition temperature on \(N_\tau = 8\) lattices is \(T_c^{N_\tau=8} = 143.7(2)\ \text{MeV}\), determined previously from scaling fits (Ding 2024).
- The non-universal scale parameter \(h_0^{-1/\delta} = 39.2(4)\) was obtained from fits to the rescaled order parameter in the temperature interval \(T \in [140\ \text{MeV} : 148\ \text{MeV}]\) (Ding 2024).
- Light-to-strange quark mass ratios studied range from \(H = 1/160\) to \(1/20\), corresponding to pion masses \(55\ \text{MeV} \le m_\pi \le 160\ \text{MeV}\).
- New data were added at \(T = 144.05\ \text{MeV}\) for \(H = 1/32.2\) and \(1/16.1\), with statistics of about 20,000 gauge configurations.
- The critical exponent for the 3-dimensional \(O(2)\) universality class is \(\delta = 4.7798\), used for rescaling the order parameter.

## Context

This work extends previous scaling analyses of the chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD using staggered fermions (Ejiri 2009, Bazavov 2011, Ding 2024). It builds on the renormalized order parameter introduced by Unger (2010) and used in subsequent studies (Kotov 2021, Dini 2021, Ding 2024). The method aims to distinguish between the 3-dimensional \(O(4)\) and \(U(2) \times U(2)\) universality classes (Pelissetto 2013), which predict different critical exponents for the chiral transition depending on whether the axial \(U(1)_A\) anomaly is effectively restored. The results enable future parameter-free determinations of critical exponents and transition temperature, provided more data are collected near the chiral limit and finite-volume and cut-off effects are analyzed.
