# The relativistic impulse approximation for the exclusive electrodisintegration of the deuteron

> The electrodisintegration of the deuteron in the frame of the Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable kernel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is considered. This conception keeps the covariance of a description of the process. A comparison of relativistic and nonrelativistic calculations is presented. The factorization of the cross section of the reaction in the impulse approximation is obtained by analytical calculations. It is shown that the photon-neutron interaction plays an important role.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the exclusive electrodisintegration cross section of the deuteron can be factorized into a one-body photon-nucleon interaction part and a deuteron structure part within the relativistic impulse approximation, but only under the conditions of plane-wave approximation for the final np-pair, spectator neutron assumption, and inclusion of only positive-energy deuteron states (neglecting P-waves).

## Method

The authors employ the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) approach with a separable Graz II kernel for the nucleon-nucleon interaction to describe the deuteron. The hadron electromagnetic current is constructed using the Mandelstam technique, and the cross section is calculated in the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) with plane waves for the final np-pair. Analytical calculations of the trace are performed using the REDUCE system, and results are compared with nonrelativistic calculations using the same Graz II kernel.

## Key numbers

- The spectator neutron contribution to the cross section reaches 50% in the Saclay_III kinematic range.
- The cross section is calculated for eight experimental data sets: Saclay_I, Saclay_II, Saclay_III, Bonn_I, Bonn_II, Bonn_III, Bonn_IV, Bonn_V.
- Relativistic effects are shown to be important even for small momentum transfers Q^2 (specific Q^2 values are not given in the truncated text).
- The ratio δ = (σ_{p+n} - σ_p)/σ_{p+n} is unchanged when varying the D-state magnitude, indicating the difference is mainly due to the spectator neutron contribution.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach for the deuteron, previously developed by Burov and collaborators, to the exclusive electrodisintegration reaction. It contrasts with nonrelativistic approaches such as those using the Paris potential by Shebeko, and with other relativistic approaches including the one-boson-exchange model by Gross and collaborators, and the covariant models by Adam. The paper enables future inclusion of final state interactions, negative-energy states, and P-wave contributions, as well as calculation of asymmetries in the (ed → enp) process.
