# The 3-$α$ and 4-$α$ particle systems within short-range Effective Field Theory

> ${}^{12}{\rm C}$ and ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ nuclei represent essential elements for life on Earth. The study of their formation plays a key role in understanding heavy element nucleosynthesis and stellar evolution. In this paper we present the study of ${}^{12}{\rm C}$ and ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ nuclei as systems composed of $α$-particle clusters using the short-range effective field theory approach. The fundamental and excited states of the studied nuclei are calculated within an ab-initio approach, using the Hyperspherical Harmonics method. Thanks to the two-body potential and fine-tuning of the three-body force, we have found the ${}^{12}{\rm C}$ system nicely reproduced by theory. However, for the ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ case, it is necessary to include a 4-body force in order to achieve agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The short-range Effective Field Theory (EFT) for alpha clusters, when combined with a two-body potential and a fine-tuned three-body force, can reproduce the 12C ground and excited states; however, for 16O, a four-body force must be added to achieve agreement with experimental data, indicating that the three-body force alone is insufficient for the four-alpha system.

## Method

The authors treat 12C and 16O as systems of three and four alpha particles, respectively, using a short-range EFT approach where the alpha-alpha interaction is derived from a contact theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). The low-energy constants (LECs) of the two-body potential are fitted to S-wave and D-wave alpha-alpha scattering data below 5 MeV center-of-mass energy using the Kohn variational principle. The bound-state calculations are performed ab initio by solving the Schrodinger equation via the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle with wave functions expanded on a Hyperspherical Harmonics (HH) basis, including a three-body force for 12C and an additional four-body force for 16O.

## Key numbers

- The experimental ground-state energy of 12C (three-alpha system) is -7.275 MeV; using only two-body interactions, the calculated ground-state energy ranges from -1.803 MeV (Lambda=120 MeV) to -5.605 MeV (Lambda=160 MeV)
- With the three-body force included, the 12C ground state, 2+ excited state, and Hoyle state (0+2) are reproduced; the three-body force parameters V03 range from -19.44 MeV to -18.58 MeV for cutoffs Lambda=120-150 MeV
- The experimental binding energy of 16O (four-alpha system) is -14.437 MeV; a four-body force with parameters V04 and V24 fitted to the ground state and 2+ excited state is required to match experiment
- The two-body potential fit to 28 experimental data points (14 S-wave, 14 D-wave) yields chi-squared per datum around 0.74 for Lambda=140 MeV (chi-squared=20.84)
- The alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts are reproduced up to approximately 15 MeV laboratory energy, and the G-wave (L=4) is predicted without being included in the fit

## Context

This work extends the cluster EFT approach previously applied to halo and cluster nuclei (Bert2002, Higa2008, Chenhalo, Bed2003) and pionless EFT (van Kolck 1998, Kaplan 1998) to the specific case of 12C and 16O as multi-alpha systems. Prior phenomenological studies characterized these nuclei as 3-alpha and 4-alpha systems using empirical potentials (1974PThPh, Ronen 2005), but this work derives interactions from Cluster EFT for a more theoretically grounded framework. The results enable future studies of astrophysical reactions involving carbon and oxygen formation, particularly the triple-alpha process and subsequent alpha capture to form 16O.
