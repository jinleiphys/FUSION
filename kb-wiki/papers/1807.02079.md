# Finite resonance widths influence the thermal-model description of hadron yields

> Different scenarios for modeling resonances in a thermal model description of hadron yields measured in heavy-ion collisions are explored: the zero-width approximation, the energy independent Breit-Wigner scheme, and the energy dependent Breit-Wigner (eBW) scheme. Application of the eBW scheme leads to a notable suppression in the proton yields, stemming mainly from a reduced feeddown from $Δ$ resonances because of the threshold effects. A significantly improved agreement of thermal model with hadron yields measured in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}} = 2.76$ TeV by the ALICE collaboration is obtained in the eBW scheme at $T \simeq 155$ MeV, indicating a possible resolution of the so-called 'proton anomaly'. The results obtained show that there are significant systematic uncertainties in the thermal model due to the modeling of broad resonances.

## Key claim

The energy-dependent Breit-Wigner (eBW) scheme for modeling finite resonance widths significantly improves the thermal model fit to hadron yields in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, resolving the "proton anomaly" at least partially, but the improvement is conditional on the specific treatment of broad resonances and does not eliminate all tensions (e.g., the Lambda yield becomes slightly worse).

## Method

The authors performed thermal model fits within the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) framework using three different treatments of resonance widths: the zero-width approximation, the energy-independent Breit-Wigner (BW) scheme, and the energy-dependent Breit-Wigner (eBW) scheme. The eBW scheme implements mass-dependent partial widths for two-body decays following the prescription used in the SHARE package, with angular momentum factors and threshold effects. Fits were performed to midrapidity hadron yields measured by the ALICE collaboration in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV across five centrality classes, using the open-source Thermal-FIST package.

## Key numbers

- The eBW scheme reduces the final proton yield by a factor of about 0.86 relative to the zero-width approximation at T = 155 MeV and mu_B = 0
- The proton-to-pion ratio crosses the experimental value (0.046 +/- 0.005) at T = 147 +/- 4 MeV in the eBW scheme, compared to T = 141 +/- 4 MeV in the zero-width and BW schemes
- For 0-10% central collisions, the chi^2/dof improves from 15.4/6 (zero-width) and 19.3/6 (BW) to 9.1/6 (eBW) when light nuclei are excluded from the fit
- The extracted chemical freeze-out temperature is T = 157.5 +/- 2.8 MeV in the eBW scheme for 0-10% centrality, similar to the zero-width value of 155.3 +/- 2.8 MeV
- The feeddown from Delta resonances to final protons is reduced by about 25% in the eBW scheme compared to the zero-width approximation

## Context

This work extends the standard thermal model approach used by Stachel (2013), Floris (2014), and others to describe ALICE data, where the "proton anomaly" (overestimation of proton yields) had been noted by Andronic (2012) and Stachel (2013). Previous explanations included hadronic phase annihilation (Steinheimer 2012, Becattini 2012), incomplete hadron spectrum (Stachel 2013, Noronha-Hostler 2014), chemical non-equilibrium (Petran 2013, Begun 2013), flavor-dependent freeze-out (Bellwied 2013, Chatterjee 2016), and excluded volume effects (Alba 2016). The present work shows that the eBW scheme, previously implemented in the SHARE package (Torrieri 2004) but not systematically compared to other schemes at LHC energies, provides a simpler resolution within the chemical equilibrium framework. The results enable precision tests with upcoming 5.02 TeV data from ALICE and motivate further studies combining eBW treatment with other effects such as non-resonant correlated pairs and excluded volume interactions.
