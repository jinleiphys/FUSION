# Particle Production at CBM in a Thermal Model Approach

> The Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment planned at Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) will provide a major scientific effort for exploring the properties of strongly interacting matter in the high baryon density regime. One of the important goal behind such experiment is to precisely determine the equation of state (EOS) for the strongly interacting matter at extreme baryon density. In this paper, we have used a thermal model EOS incorporating excluded volume description for the hot and dense hadron gas (HG). We then predict different particle ratios and the total multiplicity of various hadrons in the CBM energy range i.e. from $10$ A GeV to $40$ A GeV lab energies, which corresponds to $4.43$ A GeV and $8.71$ A GeV center-of-mass energies. Our main emphasis is to estimate the strange particles enhancement as well as increase in the net baryon density in CBM experiment. We have also compared our results with the results obtained from various other theoretical approaches existing in the literature such as hadron string dynamics (HSD) model and ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) etc.

## Key claim
The excluded-volume thermal model predicts that the CBM experiment will probe the maximum achievable net baryon density at freezeout in heavy-ion collisions, but the model's agreement with experimental data is limited to specific energy ranges and does not reproduce the "horn" structure in the \(K^{+}/\pi^{+}\) ratio.

## Method
The authors used a thermodynamically consistent excluded-volume hadron gas model, incorporating a hard-core radius of 0.8 fm for all baryons while treating mesons as pointlike particles. They extracted freezeout temperature and baryon chemical potential by fitting experimental particle ratios from SIS to RHIC energies, then parametrized these thermal parameters as functions of center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\). Using a constant fireball volume of 5000 fm³, they calculated total multiplicities and particle ratios for \(\pi^{+}\), \(K^{+}\), \(K^{-}\), and antiparticle-to-particle ratios in the CBM energy range (4.43 to 8.71 A GeV center-of-mass energy), imposing strangeness conservation to determine the strange chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The CBM energy range spans 10 A GeV to 40 A GeV lab energies, corresponding to 4.43 A GeV to 8.71 A GeV center-of-mass energies.
- The maximum freezeout net baryon density occurs near \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8-9\) GeV and is approximately half of normal nuclear density \(\rho_{0} = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- A constant fireball volume of 5000 fm\(^{3}\) was used to calculate total multiplicities of all hadrons.
- The excluded-volume model uses a hard-core radius of 0.8 fm for each baryon species.
- The model includes all baryons and mesons with masses up to 2 GeV/\(c^{2}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous excluded-volume hadron gas model (Prakash et al., 2013) to predict particle production specifically for the CBM experiment at FAIR. It compares results with transport models such as hadron string dynamics (HSD) and ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD), as well as with experimental data from AGS and SPS experiments. The paper highlights that the CBM experiment will provide high-luminosity measurements to resolve the "horn" structure in the \(K^{+}/\pi^{+}\) ratio observed by NA49, which remains unexplained by current models.
