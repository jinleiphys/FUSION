# Searching for phase transitions in neutron stars with modified Gaussian processes

> Gaussian processes provide a promising framework by which to extrapolate the equation of state (EoS) of cold, catalyzed matter beyond $1-2$ times nuclear saturation density. Here we discuss how to extend Gaussian processes to include nontrivial features in the speed of sound, such as bumps, kinks, and plateaus, which are predicted by nuclear models with exotic degrees of freedom. Using a fully Bayesian analysis incorporating measurements from X-ray sources, gravitational wave observations, and perturbative QCD results, we show that these features are compatible with current constraints and report on how the features affect the EoS posteriors.

## Key claim

Current astrophysical constraints are consistent with both smooth equations of state and those containing sharp features like bumps, kinks, and plateaus in the speed of sound, but the data are not yet informative enough to favor or disfavor such nontrivial features; the Bayes factor comparing modified Gaussian process EoS to standard Gaussian process EoS is 1.126, a statistically insignificant deviation from unity.

## Method

The authors generate synthetic equations of state using a modified Gaussian process (mGP) framework, where a smooth baseline EoS sampled from a GP is modified over some pressure range to produce bumps, dips, plateaus, or double plateaus in the speed of sound. They produce 900,000 candidate EoS (25% unmodified GP, 75% mGP), prune them using conservative cuts from NICER measurements, GW170817, and a maximum mass requirement of at least 1.8 solar masses, leaving 104,594 EoS for a fully Bayesian analysis incorporating mass measurements of three high-mass pulsars, tidal deformability posteriors from GW170817 and GW190425, NICER mass-radius posteriors, and perturbative QCD constraints.

## Key numbers

- Bayes factor comparing GP to mGP evidence: K = 1.126, indicating statistically indistinguishable support for smooth versus sharp-featured EoS
- Out of 900,000 candidate EoS, only 104,594 (approximately 11.6%) survived prior pruning based on observational constraints
- At twice nuclear saturation density, GP posterior gives c_s^2 = 0.29^{+0.27}_{-0.11}, while mGP posterior gives c_s^2 = 0.29^{+0.34}_{-0.14}
- At four times nuclear saturation density, GP posterior gives c_s^2 = 0.63^{+0.27}_{-0.23}, while mGP posterior gives c_s^2 = 0.59^{+0.31}_{-0.34}
- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat = 0.15 ± 0.01 fm^{-3}
- Prior pruning criteria: M_max ≥ 1.8 M_sun, 10 ≤ Λ_1.4 ≤ 2000, 9.0 ≤ R_1.4 ≤ 18.0 km

## Context

This work extends Gaussian process approaches to neutron star EoS inference (Landry 2018, Essick 2019, Legred 2021) by introducing a method to explicitly generate sharp features in the speed of sound that are predicted by nuclear models with exotic degrees of freedom (Clevinger 2022). The modified GP framework enables systematic study of phase transitions and nontrivial EoS structure at low computational cost, and the results show that current constraints from NICER (Miller 2019, Miller 2021; Riley 2019, Riley 2021) and gravitational wave detections are consistent with such features. Future work can use this framework to investigate whether upcoming measurements, such as binary Love relation slopes (Tan 2021), can provide unambiguous signatures of phase transitions in neutron star cores.
