# A possible evidence of the hadron-quark-gluon mixed phase formation in nuclear collisions

> The performed systematic meta-analysis of the quality of data description (QDD) of existing event generators of nucleus-nucleus collisions allows us to extract a very important physical information. Our meta-analysis is dealing with the results of 10 event generators which describe data measured in the range of center of mass collision energies from 3.1 GeV to 17.3 GeV. It considers the mean deviation squared per number of experimental points obtained by these event generators, i.e. the QDD, as the results of independent meta-measurements. These generators and their QDDs are divided in two groups. The first group includes the generators which account for the quark-gluon plasma formation during nuclear collisions (QGP models), while the second group includes the generators which do not assume the QGP formation in such collisions (hadron gas models). Comparing the QDD of more than a hundred of different data sets of strange hadrons by two groups of models, we found two regions of the equal quality description of data which are located at the center of mass collision energies 4.4-4.87 GeV and 10.8-12 GeV. At the collision energies below 4.4 GeV the hadron gas models describe data much better than the QGP one and, hence, we associate this region with hadron phase. At the collision energies between 5 GeV and 10.8 GeV and above 12 GeV we found that QGP models describe data essentially better than the hadron gas ones and, hence, these regions we associate with the quark-gluon phase. As a result, the collision energy regions 4.4-4.87 GeV and 10.8-12 GeV we interpret as the energies of the hadron-quark-gluon mixed phase formation. Based on these findings we argue that the most probable energy range of the QCD phase diagram (tri)critical endpoint is 12-14 GeV.

## Key claim

The authors claim that systematic comparison of the quality of data description (QDD) between event generators that include quark-gluon plasma formation and those that do not reveals two collision energy regions where both model classes describe experimental data equally well, which they interpret as evidence for hadron-quark-gluon mixed phase formation. The hedge is that this is a "possible evidence" based on meta-analysis of existing model calculations rather than direct experimental observation, and the interpretation depends on the assumption that equal QDD indicates mixed phase formation.

## Method

The authors performed a meta-analysis of the mean deviation squared per number of experimental points (QDD) for 10 event generators of nucleus-nucleus collisions, divided into two groups: those that account for quark-gluon plasma formation (QGP models: Quark Combination model, 3-fluid dynamics model, PHSD model, Core-Corona model) and those that do not (hadron gas models: ARC, RQMD2.1/2.3, HSD, UrQMD1.3/2.0/2.1/2.3, statistical hadronization model, AGSHIJET_N*). They calculated QDD values for strange hadron data (charged kaons, K0_s, phi mesons, Lambda, anti-Lambda, Xi, Omega hyperons) across 10 collision energies from 3.1 GeV to 17.3 GeV, using both arithmetic and weighted averaging procedures over models, observables (transverse mass distributions, rapidity distributions, yields), and hadron species.

## Key numbers

- The two collision energy regions of equal QDD between HG and QGP models are located at center-of-mass energies 4.4-4.87 GeV and 10.8-12 GeV
- Below 4.4 GeV, hadron gas models describe data much better than QGP models, associated with hadron phase
- Between 5 GeV and 10.8 GeV and above 12 GeV, QGP models describe data essentially better than hadron gas models, associated with quark-gluon phase
- The most probable energy range for the QCD phase diagram (tri)critical endpoint is argued to be 12-14 GeV
- The analysis covers collision energies from 3.1 GeV to 17.3 GeV, with specific energies analyzed: 3.1, 3.6, 4.2, 4.87, 5.4, 6.3, 7.6, 8.8, 12.3, and 17.3 GeV
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 4.87 GeV, the arithmetic average QDD for K+/- meson transverse mass spectra from HG models was 1.26 +/- 0.34

## Context

This work extends the search for the onset of deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions, addressing conflicting claims in the literature: the Strangeness Horn signal at 7.6 GeV (Gazdzicki et al., 2010), the onset at 6.3 GeV (Rafelski and Letessier), and the mixed phase formation at 4.2-4.9 GeV (Bugaev et al., 2014a, 2014b). The authors argue that previous approaches relied on specific data sets or particular models, whereas their meta-analysis systematically compares multiple event generators across many data sets. The results provide practical suggestions for the collision energies of the future RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and aim to help locate the critical endpoint of the QCD phase diagram.
