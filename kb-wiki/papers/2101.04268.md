# Quarkonium propagation in the quark gluon plasma

> In relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, a quark gluon plasma (QGP) is created for a short duration of about 10fm/c. Quarkonia (bound states of $c\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$) are sensitive probes of this phase on length scales comparable to the size of the bound states which are less than 1fm. Observations of quarkonia in these collisions provide us with a lot of information about how the presence of a QGP affects various quarkonium states. This has motivated the development of the theory of heavy quarks and their bound states in a thermal medium, and its application to the phenomenology of quarkonia in heavy ion collisions. We review some of these developments here.

# Quarkonium propagation in the quark gluon plasma

## Key claim
The paper reviews theoretical developments in understanding how quarkonium states (bound states of heavy quark-antiquark pairs) are affected by the quark gluon plasma, emphasizing that the potential between the heavy quark and antiquark in a thermal medium is complex (not just screened), with the imaginary part arising from scattering with medium constituents. The review notes that while weak coupling analyses provide key insight, they are not expected to be quantitatively accurate at temperatures relevant for LHC and RHIC phenomenology.

## Method
The review examines the theoretical framework for quarkonium propagation in the QGP using effective field theories, particularly potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) at finite temperature, and analyzes the complex potential derived from finite-temperature perturbation theory. It discusses how the correlator of point-split currents for infinitely massive quarks reduces to the expectation value of a Wilson loop in a thermal medium, from which the complex potential is extracted, and how the imaginary part relates to the gluonic spectral function at zero frequency via Landau damping.

## Key numbers
- QGP lifetime in heavy ion collisions: approximately 10 fm/c
- Charm quark mass: approximately 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass: approximately 4.9 GeV
- QCD scale: approximately 0.2 GeV
- Temperature range in QGP phase: approximately 0.2-0.5 GeV
- Inverse size for J/psi: 0.35 GeV (r = 0.56 fm); for Upsilon(1S): 0.67 GeV (r = 0.29 fm)
- Binding energy for both J/psi and Upsilon(1S): approximately 0.4-0.5 GeV
- Debye screening mass at leading order in perturbation theory: m_D ~ gT
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio needed to match elliptic flow: eta/s ~ 1/(4pi)
- Relative velocity v ~ 0.5 for charm quarks and v ~ 0.3 for bottom quarks

## Context
This review extends the early work on quarkonium suppression in the QGP proposed by Matsui (1986) and the subsequent development of screening and dissociation mechanisms by Karsch (1987, 1991) and Digal (2001). It builds on the key perturbative result by Laine (2006) showing that the Q-Qbar potential develops an imaginary part at finite temperature, and on the development of pNRQCD by Brambilla (1999, 2004). The review enables phenomenological applications connecting microscopic calculations of decay rates to observed quarkonium suppression in heavy ion collisions, while noting that the open quantum system approach (Akamatsu 2011, Kajimoto 2017, Brambilla 2016) provides a more rigorous framework than the rate equation approach.
