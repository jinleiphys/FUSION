# Nuclear electric dipole moment as a good probe of CP violation

> The electric dipole moment (EDM) is an excellent probe of new physics beyond the standard model of particle physics. The EDM of light nuclei is particularly interesting due to the high sensitivity to the hadron level CP violation. In this proceedings contribution, we investigate the mechanism of the generation of the EDM for several light nuclei and the prospect for the discovery of new physics.

# Nuclear Electric Dipole Moment as a Good Probe of CP Violation

## Key claim
The electric dipole moment (EDM) of light nuclei is a sensitive probe of hadron-level CP violation beyond the Standard Model, and the non-alignment of EDM coefficients across different nuclei allows constraining multiple new-physics parameters simultaneously, provided the experimental sensitivity reaches O(10⁻²⁹) e·cm.

## Method
The nuclear EDM induced by CP-odd nuclear forces is parametrized as a linear combination of isoscalar, isovector, and isotensor one-pion exchange couplings (Ḡπ⁽⁰⁾, Ḡπ⁽¹⁾, Ḡπ⁽²⁾). The coefficients aπ⁽ⁱ⁾ for each nucleus are calculated using ab initio methods for the deuteron, ³He, and ³H, and using cluster models for p-shell nuclei (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, ¹¹B, ¹³C). The results are compared with the heavier nucleus ¹²⁹Xe.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron (²H): aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.45 × 10⁻² e·fm (isovector coefficient only)
- ³He: aπ⁽⁰⁾ = 0.59, aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.08, aπ⁽²⁾ = 1.68 (all × 10⁻² e·fm)
- ³H: aπ⁽⁰⁾ = −0.59, aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.08, aπ⁽²⁾ = −1.70 (all × 10⁻² e·fm)
- ⁶Li: aπ⁽¹⁾ = 2.2 × 10⁻² e·fm (isovector only)
- ⁷Li: aπ⁽⁰⁾ = −0.6, aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.6, aπ⁽²⁾ = −1.7 (all × 10⁻² e·fm)
- ⁹Be: aπ⁽¹⁾ = 1.4 × 10⁻² e·fm (isovector only)
- ¹¹B: aπ⁽⁰⁾ = −0.5, aπ⁽¹⁾ = 2, aπ⁽²⁾ = −1 (all × 10⁻² e·fm)
- ¹³C: aπ⁽¹⁾ = −0.20 × 10⁻² e·fm (isovector only)
- ¹²⁹Xe: aπ⁽⁰⁾ = 7.0 × 10⁻³, aπ⁽¹⁾ = 7.4 × 10⁻³, aπ⁽²⁾ = 3.7 × 10⁻² (all × 10⁻² e·fm)
- Neutron (for comparison): aπ⁽⁰⁾ = 1, aπ⁽²⁾ = −1 (× 10⁻² e·fm)
- Prospective experimental sensitivity: O(10⁻²⁹) e·cm, which can probe supersymmetry breaking at O(TeV) and right-handed W boson mass up to PeV
- Standard Model contribution to quark EDM: dq ∼ 10⁻³⁵ e·cm (three-loop level), with hadronic long-distance contributions three to four orders of magnitude larger

## Context
This proceedings contribution extends the theoretical framework for nuclear EDM calculations previously developed for the neutron by Crewther (1979) and for light nuclei by Liu (2004), Bsaisou (2015), and Yamanaka (2017). The work provides a systematic survey of EDM coefficients for light nuclei from deuteron through ¹³C, including new cluster-model results for ⁷Li and ¹¹B from Yamanaka (2018). The results enable multi-parameter constraints on new physics models such as supersymmetric SM, extended Higgs models, and left-right symmetric models, where the nuclear EDM sensitivity at O(10⁻²⁹) e·cm can probe mass scales from TeV to PeV. The paper notes that heavy nuclei like ¹²⁹Xe suffer from configuration mixing that suppresses EDM sensitivity, making light nuclei particularly attractive for storage-ring experiments.
