# Charged pion production from Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$ GeV in the Relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model

> Using the isospin-dependent relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) model, we study charged pion ($π^\pm$) production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 2.4 GeV. By fitting the density dependence of the $Δ$ resonance production cross section in nuclear medium to reproduce the experimental $π^\pm$ multiplicities measured by the HADES Collaboration, we obtain a good description of the rapidity distributions and transverse momentum spectra of $π^\pm$ in collisions at various centralities. Some shortcomings in the description of $π^{\pm}$ production may indicate the need for including the strong potential on $π^\pm$ in RVUU, which is at present absent. We also calculate the proton rapidity distribution in the most central collisions and compare with the coalescence invariant proton rapidity distribution extracted from preliminary HADES data.

## Key claim

By introducing isospin-dependent in-medium suppression factors for the Δ resonance production cross section (α⁺=0.39 for Δ⁺ and Δ⁺⁺ channels, α⁻=0.70 for Δ⁰ and Δ⁻ channels), the RVUU model can reproduce the HADES charged pion multiplicities, but the absence of a pion mean-field potential likely causes remaining discrepancies in the transverse momentum spectra.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN=2.4 GeV. They introduced a density-dependent exponential suppression factor e^{-α(ρ/ρ₀)^{3/2}} to the NN→NΔ inelastic cross section, with separate α parameters for Δ⁺/Δ⁺⁺ and Δ⁰/Δ⁻ channels, and fitted these parameters to reproduce the total charged pion multiplicities measured by HADES. The model then calculated rapidity distributions and transverse momentum spectra for π⁻ and π⁺ across multiple centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- Fitted in-medium suppression parameters: α⁺ = 0.39 for Δ⁺ and Δ⁺⁺ channels, α⁻ = 0.70 for Δ⁰ and Δ⁻ channels
- Without suppression (α⁺=α⁻=0), RVUU overpredicts π⁻ multiplicity (17.2 vs. 11.1±0.6±0.6 from HADES) and π⁺ multiplicity (8.7 vs. 6.0±0.3±0.3) for 0%-40% centrality
- With isospin-dependent suppression (α⁺=0.39, α⁻=0.70), RVUU gives π⁻ multiplicity 11.3 and π⁺ multiplicity 6.2, close to HADES values
- Proton dN/dy at mid-rapidity from RVUU is 126, while the coalescence-invariant HADES value is 102, requiring a scaling factor of 0.81 for comparison
- The suppression splitting between α⁺ and α⁻ is considerably larger than predicted by theoretical calculations in Cui (2021) and Li (2017)

## Context

This work addresses the failure of five other transport models (Hartnack 1997, Cassing 1999, Aichelin 2019, Buss 2011, Petersen 2018) to describe HADES pion data, which overpredict multiplicities by factors of 1.2 to 2.1. The study extends the RVUU model previously used by Song (2015) and Zhang (2017) by introducing isospin-dependent in-medium suppression of Δ production. The results enable future inclusion of pion mean-field potentials and provide benchmarked in-medium cross sections for the transport model evaluation project (TMEP) and for comparison with the SπRIT Collaboration data.
