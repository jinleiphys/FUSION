# Kinematic biases on centrality selection of jet events in pPb collisions at the LHC

> Centrality selection has been observed to have a large effect on jet observables in pPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, stronger than that predicted by the nuclear modification of parton densities. We study to which extent simple considerations of energy-momentum conservation between the hard process and the underlying event affect jets observables in such collisions. We develop a simplistic approach that considers first the production of jets in a pp collision as described by PYTHIA. From each pp collision, the value of the energy of the parton from the proton participating in the hard scattering is extracted. Then, the underlying event is generated simulating a pPb collision through HIJING, but with the energy of the proton decreased according to the value extracted in the previous step, and both collisions are superimposed. This model is able to capture the bulk of the centrality effect for central to semicentral collisions, for the two available sets of data: dijets from the CMS Collaboration and single jets from the ATLAS Collaboration. As expected, the model fails for peripheral collisions where very few nucleons from Pb participate.

## Key claim

Simple energy-momentum conservation between the hard jet process and the underlying event can explain the bulk of the observed centrality dependence of jet observables in pPb collisions at the LHC, without invoking nuclear modification of parton densities; the model fails for peripheral collisions where constraints on the Pb nucleus become important.

## Method

The authors generate hard pp events using PYTHIA and extract the momentum fraction \(x_p\) of the parton from the proton participating in the hard scattering. They then generate minimum-bias pPb underlying events using HIJING, but with the proton beam energy reduced to \((1-x_p)E_p\) to account for energy taken by the hard process. The hard and underlying events are superimposed in the LHC laboratory frame, and the HIJING event is reweighted by impact parameter to match the \(N_{coll}\) scaling expected for hard events. The model does not modify the parton momentum fraction from the Pb nucleus.

## Key numbers

* Dijet events: leading jet \(p_{T,1} > 120\) GeV/c, subleading jet \(p_{T,2} > 30\) GeV/c, azimuthal separation \(\Delta \phi_{1,2} > 2\pi/3\), reconstructed with anti-\(k_T\) algorithm with distance parameter 0.3.
* Single-jet events: jets reconstructed with anti-\(k_T\) algorithm with distance parameter 0.4, in region \(|\eta_{\rm jet}-\eta_{\rm CM}|<3\).
* Event samples: \(10^5\) events for dijets, \(2\times 10^6\) events for single-jet spectra.
* Centrality classification for CMS dijets uses transverse energy in \(4 < |\eta| < 5\); for ATLAS single jets uses transverse energy in Pb-going direction \(-4.9 < \eta < -3.2\).
* The model reproduces CMS dijet pseudorapidity distributions and ATLAS nuclear modification factor \(R_{p\rm Pb}\) for central (0-10%) and semicentral (20-30%) collisions, but fails for peripheral (60-90%) collisions.

## Context

This work addresses the unexpectedly strong centrality dependence of jet observables in pPb collisions at the LHC, first reported by CMS (Chatrchyan 2014) and ATLAS (ATLAS 2014), which cannot be explained by the impact-parameter dependence of nuclear parton densities (Armesto 2003, Frankfurt 2011, Helenius 2012). The authors provide a minimal kinematic explanation based on energy-momentum conservation, complementing more dynamical approaches (Martinez-Garcia 2014, Bzdak 2014, Alvioli 2014, Perepelitsa 2014). The results imply that centrality biases from energy conservation must be accounted for before extracting other nuclear effects from pPb data.
