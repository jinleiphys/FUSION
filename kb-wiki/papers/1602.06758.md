# The decay of quadrupole-octupole $1^-$ states in $^{40}$Ca and $^{140}$Ce

> Background: Two-phonon excitations originating from the coupling of two collective one-phonon states are of great interest in nuclear structure physics. One possibility to generate low-lying $E1$ excitations is the coupling of quadrupole and octupole phonons. Purpose: In this work, the $帠$-decay behavior of candidates for the $(2_1^+\otimes 3_1^-)_{1^-}$ state in the doubly-magic nucleus $^{40}$Ca and in the heavier and semi-magic nucleus $^{140}$Ce is investigated. Methods: $(\vec帠,帠')$ experiments have been carried out at the High Intensity $帠$-ray Source (HI$帠$S) facility in combination with the high-efficiency $帠$-ray spectroscopy setup $帠^3$ consisting of HPGe and LaBr$_3$ detectors. The setup enables the acquisition of $帠$-$帠$ coincidence data and, hence, the detection of direct decay paths. Results: In addition to the known ground-state decays, for $^{40}$Ca the decay into the $3^-_1$ state was observed, while for $^{140}$Ce the direct decays into the $2^+_1$ and the $0^+_2$ state were detected. The experimentally deduced transition strengths and excitation energies are compared to theoretical calculations in the framework of EDF theory plus QPM approach and systematically analyzed for $N=82$ isotones. In addition, negative parities for two $J=1$ states in $^{44}$Ca were deduced simultaneously. Conclusions: The experimental findings together with the theoretical calculations support the two-phonon character of the $1^-_1$ excitation in the light-to-medium-mass nucleus $^{40}$Ca as well as in the stable even-even $N=82$ nuclei.

## Key claim

The experimental observation of direct decays from the $1^-_1$ state to the $2^+_1$ and $0^+_2$ states in $^{140}$Ce, together with the decay to the $3^-_1$ state in $^{40}$Ca, supports the two-phonon $(2^+_1 \otimes 3^-_1)_{1^-}$ character of these excitations, but only when interpreted through theoretical calculations that include contributions from higher-lying one-phonon pygmy dipole resonance states and three-phonon configurations beyond the simple harmonic picture.

## Method

$(\vec{\gamma},\gamma')$ experiments were performed at the High Intensity $\gamma$-ray Source (HI$\gamma$S) facility using quasi-monochromatic, linearly polarized photon beams at energies of 3.6 MeV for $^{140}$Ce and 5.9 MeV for $^{40}$Ca. The $\gamma^3$ setup, consisting of four HPGe detectors at $\theta=135^\circ$ and four LaBr$_3$ scintillation detectors at $\theta=90^\circ$, enabled $\gamma$-$\gamma$ coincidence measurements to detect direct decay paths from the $1^-$ states to excited states. Branching ratios relative to the ground-state decay were extracted from both singles and coincidence spectra, and transition strengths were deduced using known $B(E1)$ values from previous nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments. Theoretical calculations were performed within the framework of energy-density functional theory combined with the quasiparticle-phonon model (EDF+QPM) including up to three-phonon configurations.

## Key numbers

- $^{140}$Ce: $B(E1,1^-_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 0.54(3)$ mW.u. and $B(E1,1^-_1 \rightarrow 0^+_2) = 0.75(6)$ mW.u., measured for the first time in this work
- $^{140}$Ce: upper limit $B(E2,1^-_1 \rightarrow 3^-_1) < 28$ W.u. deduced from background analysis
- $^{140}$Ce: $B(E1,1^-_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 4.1(6)$ mW.u. adopted from Volz (2006)
- $^{40}$Ca: decay into the $3^-_1$ state was observed (no numerical transition strength given in the truncated text)
- QPM calculations for $N=82$ isotones: the $1^-_1$ state has a major two-phonon $(2^+_1 \otimes 3^-_1)_{1^-}$ content of more than 93% in all nuclei studied ($^{138}$Ba, $^{140}$Ce, $^{142}$Nd, $^{144}$Sm)
- $^{140}$Ce: $B(E2,2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 13.7(3)$ W.u. adopted from Raman (1987); $B(E3,3^-_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 26(3)$ W.u. adopted from Pitt (1970)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-phonon quadrupole-octupole $1^-$ states in $N=82$ nuclei, where the decay behavior had been established only for $^{142}$Nd and $^{144}$Sm via inelastic proton-scattering experiments by Wilhelm (1996, 1998) and Robinson (1994). The new $\gamma$-$\gamma$ coincidence measurements at HI$\gamma$S provide the first observation of the $1^-_1 \rightarrow 0^+_2$ decay in $^{140}$Ce, which cannot be explained in the simple harmonic phonon coupling picture but requires the more advanced EDF+QPM approach including three-phonon configurations. The systematic analysis across $N=82$ isotones enables testing the evolution of two-phonon properties with proton number and supports the existence of this collective excitation mode in the lighter nucleus $^{40}$Ca, extending the mass range of such studies.
