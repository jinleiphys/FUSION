# The Centrality Dependence of the Parton Bubble Model for high energy heavy ion collisions and fireball surface substructure at RHIC

> In an earlier paper we developed a QCD inspired theoretical parton bubble model (PBM) for RHIC/LHC. The PBM quantitatively agreed with the strong charged particle pair correlations observed by the STAR collaboration at RHIC in the highest energy Au + Au central collisions, and also agreed with the Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) observed small final state source size approximately 2f radii in the transverse momentum range above 0.8 GeV/c. The model assumed a substructure of a ring of localized adjoining 2f radius bubbles(gluonic hot spots) perpendicular to the collider beam direction, centered on the beam, at mid-rapidity and located on the expanding fireball surface of the Au + Au collisions. In this paper we extend the model (PBME) to include the changing development of bubbles with centrality from the most central region where bubbles are very important to the most peripheral where the bubbles are gone. Energy density is found to be related to bubble formation and as centrality decreases the maximum energy density and bubbles shift from symmetry around the beam axis to the reaction plane region causing a strong correlation of bubble formation with elliptic flow. We obtained reasonably quantitative agreement (within a few percent of the total correlations) with a new precision RHIC experiment which extended the centrality region investigated to the range 0-80% (most central to most peripheral). The characteristics and behavior of the bubbles imply they represent a significant substructure formed on the surface of the fireball at kinetic freezeout

## Key claim

The Parton Bubble Model Extended (PBME) achieves "reasonably quantitative agreement (within a few percent of the total correlations)" with STAR collaboration data on charged particle pair correlations across the full 0-80% centrality range in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, but the authors acknowledge the model "does not completely include important non-perturbative QCD effects contained in the data."

## Method

The authors extended their earlier Parton Bubble Model (PBM) for central collisions to cover 0-80% centrality by varying the number of bubbles per event and partons per bubble as a function of centrality, using a blast wave model to determine where energy density is highest and thus where bubbles form. They generated 500,000 simulated events per centrality bin using a modified HIJING event generator where Pythia jets were replaced with bubble rings, with jet quenching set to maximum for 0-30% centralities and non-quenched HIJING used for 30-80% centralities. The model was compared to STAR data by projecting two-dimensional Delta-phi vs Delta-eta correlation functions into five Delta-eta bins and applying multiplicity scaling.

## Key numbers

* For 0-5% centrality: 12 bubbles per event with 3-4 partons per bubble, impact parameter range 0.0-2.9 fm
* For 5-10% centrality: 10 bubbles per event with 3 partons per bubble, impact parameter range 2.9-4.1 fm
* For 10-20% centrality: 7 bubbles per event with 3 partons per bubble, impact parameter range 4.1-5.8 fm
* For 20-30% centrality: 5 bubbles per event with 3 partons per bubble, impact parameter range 5.8-7.1 fm
* For 30-40% centrality: 1.5 bubbles per event with 2 partons per bubble, impact parameter range 7.1-8.2 fm
* For 40-50% centrality: 0.6 bubbles per event with 2 partons per bubble, impact parameter range 8.2-9.2 fm
* For 50-60% centrality: 0.3 bubbles per event with 1 parton per bubble, impact parameter range 9.2-10.1 fm
* The bubble radius is approximately 2 fm, and the bubble ring radius is 8 fm for central collisions
* The fraction of final state particles from bubbles is approximately 1/2
* The transverse momentum range used for comparison is 0.8 GeV/c to 4.0 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Parton Bubble Model (PBM) from 2003, which was developed to explain strong charged particle pair correlations observed by STAR in central Au+Au collisions, and builds on the earlier bubble model proposed by van Hove (1980s) which predicted localized rapidity bumps that were never experimentally observed. The PBME successfully describes the centrality evolution of both Charge Independent (CI) and Charge Dependent (CD) correlations across nine centrality bins from 0-80%, and the results imply that bubble substructure on the fireball surface at kinetic freezeout is a significant feature of heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies. The model enables future investigations of whether similar bubble production occurs at LHC energies and whether the bubble substructure can be observed in other experimental observables.
