# Chiral symmetry restoration in linear sigma models with different numbers of quark flavors

> Chiral symmetry restoration at nonzero temperature is studied in the framework of the O(4) linear sigma model and the U(N_f)_r x U(N_f)_l linear sigma model with N_f=2,3, and 4 quark flavors. We investigate the temperature dependence of the masses of the scalar and pseudoscalar mesons, and the non-strange, strange, and charm condensates within the Hartree approximation as derived from the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism. We find that the masses of the non-strange and strange mesons at nonzero temperature depend sensitively on the particular symmetry of the model and the number of light quark flavors N_f. On the other hand, due to the large charm quark mass, neither do charmed mesons significantly affect the properties of the other mesons, nor do their masses change appreciably in the temperature range around the chiral symmetry restoration temperature. In the chiral limit, the transition temperatures for chiral symmetry restoration are surprisingly close to those found in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry restoration temperatures in the chiral limit, computed within the Hartree approximation for O(4) and U(N_f) linear sigma models, are surprisingly close to lattice QCD results, but the Hartree approximation slightly violates the predicted second-order transition for N_f=2 with the U(1)_A anomaly by producing a weak first-order transition.

## Method

The authors study chiral symmetry restoration at nonzero temperature using the O(4) linear sigma model and U(N_f)_r × U(N_f)_ℓ linear sigma models for N_f = 2, 3, and 4 quark flavors. They compute the temperature dependence of scalar and pseudoscalar meson masses and the non-strange, strange, and charm condensates within the Hartree approximation, derived from the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism, which yields gap equations for the meson masses as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperatures for chiral symmetry restoration in the chiral limit are found to be close to lattice QCD values around ~150 MeV (at zero net-baryon number density).
- For the O(4) linear sigma model, the Hartree approximation yields a first-order transition, while the correct order is second order.
- For N_f = 2 with the U(1)_A anomaly, the Hartree approximation produces a weak first-order transition, whereas the correct transition is second order.
- For N_f = 3 massless flavors, the transition is always first order, and the Hartree approximation correctly reproduces this first-order transition.
- For N_f = 4, the transition remains first order in both the presence and absence of the U(1)_A anomaly.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the O(4) linear sigma model by Petropoulos (1998) and Lenaghan (1999), and the U(3)_r × U(3)_ℓ model by Lenaghan (2000), by systematically comparing results across N_f = 2, 3, and 4 flavors and including the charm degree of freedom. The findings enable future investigations beyond the Hartree approximation, such as including energy-momentum dependent self-energies as explored by van Hees (2000, 2001, 2002), and provide a framework for comparing effective model predictions with lattice QCD calculations by Karsch (2001) regarding the order and temperature of the chiral phase transition.
