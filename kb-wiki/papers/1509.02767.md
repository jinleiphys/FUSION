# Pinning down QCD-matter shear viscosity in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions via EbyE fluctuations using pQCD + saturation + hydrodynamics

> We introduce an event-by-event pQCD + saturation + hydro ("EKRT") framework for high-energy heavy-ion collisions, where we compute the produced fluctuating QCD-matter energy densities from next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD (pQCD) using saturation to control soft particle production, and describe the space-time evolution of the QCD matter with viscous hydrodynamics, event by event (EbyE). We compare the computed centrality dependence of hadronic multiplicities, p_T spectra and flow coefficients v_n against LHC and RHIC data. We compare also the computed EbyE probability distributions of relative fluctuations of v_n, as well as correlations of 2 and 3 event-plane angles, with LHC data. Our systematic multi-energy and -observable analysis not only tests the initial state calculation and applicability of hydrodynamics, but also makes it possible to constrain the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio, eta/s(T), of QCD matter in its different phases. Remarkably, we can describe all these different flow observables and correlations consistently with eta/s(T) that is independent of the collision energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent description of a wide range of flow observables and correlations at both LHC and RHIC energies can be achieved with a shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio, eta/s(T), that is independent of collision energy, but the actual hedge is that this description works only for specific temperature-dependent parametrizations of eta/s(T) that are between a constant value of 0.2 and a modestly decreasing (increasing) function in the hadron gas (QGP) phase.

## Method

The authors developed an event-by-event (EbyE) framework ("EKRT") that combines next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD (pQCD) minijet transverse energy production with a gluon saturation criterion to compute fluctuating initial energy densities for viscous hydrodynamics. They sampled nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon density, computed nuclear thickness functions, solved for the saturation scale p_sat locally using the criterion dE_T/d^2r = (K_sat/pi) p_0^3 Delta y, and extracted formation times and energy densities. They then evolved the system with 2+1 D second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics, testing several temperature-dependent parametrizations of eta/s(T) against LHC and RHIC data for charged hadron multiplicities, p_T spectra, flow coefficients v_n, probability distributions of relative fluctuations of v_n, and event-plane angle correlations.

## Key numbers

* The width parameter for the gluon transverse density around each nucleon was set to sigma = 0.43 fm, obtained from exclusive J/psi electroproduction data at HERA/ZEUS.
* The minimum saturation scale p_sat^min was set to 1 GeV, corresponding to a minimum formation time tau_0 = 1/p_sat^min = 0.2 fm.
* The parameter beta in the measurement functions was fixed to beta = 0.8.
* The chemical decoupling temperature in the equation of state was set to 175 MeV, with kinetic freeze-out at 100 MeV.
* The framework successfully described data up to 40-50% centrality classes, where the delta f corrections remained conveniently small.

## Context

This work extends the original EKRT saturation model (Eskola 1999) to NLO pQCD and event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics, building on earlier NLO extensions (Paatelainen 2012) and prior hydrodynamics studies (Niemi 2011, 2012). It enables a controlled extraction of the temperature dependence of eta/s(T) by providing orthogonal constraints from flow coefficients, event-by-event fluctuations, and event-plane correlations. The results are consistent with constant eta/s values obtained by other groups (Song 2010, Gale 2012), and the framework is intended for future global analyses including bulk viscosity effects and predictions for 5 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
