# Hadronic modes in the quark plasma with an internal symmetry

> We show that requiring the quark partition function to be color singlet of SU(3) color gauge group leads to reordering the thermodynamic potential in terms of the colorless multi-quark modes ($q{\bar q}, qqq, {\bar q}{\bar q}{\bar q}, ...$) at any given temperature. In accord with the "preconfinement" property of QCD, under a suitable confining mechanism, these could evolve into color singlet hadrons/baryons at low temperatures. At fairly high temperatures these multi-quark color singlet structures propagate in the plasma like hadronic modes, just as in the more familiar low temperature phase. This suggests that there exists a strong correlation in the plasma at all temperatures which is in conformity with lattice results.

# Hadronic Modes in the Quark Plasma with an Internal Symmetry

## Key claim
Requiring the quark partition function to be a color singlet of the SU(3) color gauge group reorders the thermodynamic potential in terms of colorless multi-quark modes (such as q-qbar, qqq, qbar-qbar-qbar) at any temperature, but these color singlet structures are not bound states in a real sense; they are combinations of constituent quarks only. The authors suggest that if there is a phase transition in light-quark QCD, it may be of chiral character rather than a deconfinement character.

## Method
The authors start from the partition function for a quantum gas of quarks and antiquarks within a finite volume, inserting a projection operator onto color singlet states of SU(3) using the Haar measure and group integration. They evaluate the traces for fermions, perform the group integrations for N_C=3 and N_f=2, and obtain a thermodynamic potential expressed as a sum over Boltzmann factors for colorless multi-quark modes (mesonic and baryonic/antibaryonic). In the infinite volume limit, the sum over states is replaced by an integration over momentum.

## Key numbers
- The mesonic modes appear with coefficients such as 16 e^{-2 beta epsilon_alpha}, 136 e^{-4 beta epsilon_alpha}, 816 e^{-6 beta epsilon_alpha}, up to e^{-24 beta epsilon_alpha}, corresponding to quark content q^n qbar^n for n=1,...,12.
- The baryonic modes include a term 20 e^{-3 beta (epsilon_alpha - mu)} for low-lying baryons (m=0, B=1, N_C=3), representing 20 baryonic and 20 antibaryonic states consistent with SU(2) flavor and SU(2) spin symmetry (nucleons and deltas degenerate, total degeneracy 40).
- The baryonic modes include terms up to e^{-12 beta (epsilon_alpha - mu)} for baryon number B=4.
- The first term in the mesonic sector (n=1) has a factor of 16, corresponding to 16 low-lying degenerate mesonic states.

## Context
This work extends the approach of color projection in statistical thermodynamics previously used by Redlich and Turko (1980), Elze and Greiner (1986), Gorenstein et al. (1983), Auberson et al. (1986), and Mustafa (1994), but claims to be the first to explicitly demonstrate the reordering into multi-quark modes. It is motivated by lattice calculations by DeTar (1985, 1987) and DeTar and Kogut (1987) showing that the long-distance behavior of the high-temperature phase is characterized by color singlet multi-quark structures, and by the "preconfinement" property of QCD noted by Amati and Veneziano (1979). The results suggest that strong color correlations exist in the quark-gluon plasma at all temperatures, and the authors speculate that the three-flavor case could yield a 6-quark structure (quark-alpha) relevant to strangelet searches in heavy ion collisions.
