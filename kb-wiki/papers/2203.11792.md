# Machine learning light hypernuclei

> We employ a feed-forward artificial neural network to extrapolate at large model spaces the results of {\it ab-initio} hypernuclear No-Core Shell Model calculations for the $Λ$ separation energy $B_Λ$ of the lightest hypernuclei, $^3_Λ$H, $^4_Λ$H and $^4_Λ$He, obtained in computationally accessible harmonic oscillator basis spaces using chiral nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions. The overfitting problem is avoided by enlarging the size of the input dataset and by introducing a Gaussian noise during the training process of the neural network. We find that a network with a single hidden layer of eight neurons is sufficient to extrapolate correctly the value of the $Λ$ separation energy to model spaces of size $N_{max}=100$. The results obtained are in agreement with the experimental data in the case of $^3_Λ$H and the $0^+$ state of $^4_Λ$He, although they are off of the experiment by about $0.3$ MeV for both the $0^+$ and $1^+$states of $^4_Λ$H and the $1^+$ state of $^4_Λ$He. We find that our results are in excellent agreement with those obtained using other extrapolation schemes of the No-Core Shell Model calculations, showing this that an ANN is a reliable method to extrapolate the results of hypernuclear No-Core Shell Model calculations to large model spaces.

## Key claim

A feed-forward artificial neural network with a single hidden layer of eight neurons can reliably extrapolate the Lambda separation energy of light hypernuclei from No-Core Shell Model calculations performed in computationally accessible harmonic oscillator basis spaces to model spaces of size N_max=100, with results in excellent agreement with other extrapolation schemes, although the agreement with experimental data is limited by the Hamiltonian and symmetries used in the underlying NCSM calculations rather than by the ANN performance.

## Method

The author trained a feed-forward artificial neural network with two input features (harmonic oscillator spacing hbar-omega and maximum basis size N_max), one hidden layer of eight neurons using a sigmoid activation function, and one output neuron for the Lambda separation energy B_Lambda. The network was trained on hypernuclear No-Core Shell Model results from Refs. Wirth (2018), Htun (2021), and Gazda (2022) for the hypernuclei 3_Lambda-H, 4_Lambda-H, and 4_Lambda-He, using chiral nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon-nucleon, and hyperon-nucleon interactions. To avoid overfitting with the limited dataset (128 data points for 3_Lambda-H and fewer for the others), the author enlarged the input dataset via cubic spline interpolation in hbar-omega at each N_max and introduced Gaussian noise during training, using 80% of the data for training and 20% for testing with the Adam optimization algorithm.

## Key numbers

- For 3_Lambda-H ground state at N_max=100 and hbar-omega=10 MeV: ANN predicts B_Lambda = 0.16 +/- 0.02 MeV, compared to experimental value 0.13 +/- 0.05 MeV (Davis 2005, Gal 2016)
- For 4_Lambda-H 0+ state at N_max=100 and hbar-omega=30 MeV: ANN predicts B_Lambda = 2.49 +/- 0.05 MeV, compared to experimental value 2.157 +/- 0.077 MeV (Schulz 2016)
- For 4_Lambda-H 1+ state at N_max=100 and hbar-omega=32 MeV: ANN predicts B_Lambda = 1.35 +/- 0.16 MeV, compared to experimental value 1.067 +/- 0.08 MeV (Yamamoto 2015)
- For 4_Lambda-He 0+ state at N_max=100 and hbar-omega=30 MeV: ANN predicts B_Lambda = 2.43 +/- 0.06 MeV, compared to experimental value 2.39 +/- 0.05 MeV (Davis 2005)
- For 4_Lambda-He 1+ state at N_max=100 and hbar-omega=32 MeV: ANN predicts B_Lambda = 1.33 +/- 0.11 MeV, compared to experimental value 0.984 +/- 0.05 MeV (Yamamoto 2015)
- The ANN predictions for 4_Lambda-H (both states) and 4_Lambda-He 1+ state are off from experiment by about 0.3 MeV
- The ANN has 33 fitting parameters (24 weights and 9 biases)
- The input dataset for 3_Lambda-H contained 128 data points with hbar-omega ranging from 10-40 MeV and N_max from 14-70

## Context

This work extends the ANN extrapolation method previously applied to nuclear NCSM calculations by Negoita (2018, 2019) for 6-Li and by Jiang (2019) for 4-He, 6-Li, and 16-O, now applying it to hypernuclear systems. The results are in excellent agreement with the extrapolation schemes used by Wirth (2018) and Htun (2021) for the same hypernuclear NCSM calculations, demonstrating that ANNs provide a reliable alternative to traditional extrapolation methods. The author suggests future comparison with infrared extrapolation methods (Forssen 2018, Gazda 2022) and application to heavier p-shell hypernuclei such as 7_Lambda-Li, 9_Lambda-Be, and 13_Lambda-C from Wirth (2014).
