# Equivalence of light-front and conventional thermal field theory

> It is shown that light front thermal field theory is equivalent to conventional thermal field theory. The proof is based on the use of spectral representations, and applies to all Lagrangians for which such equivalence has been proven at zero temperature. It is also pointed out that conventional spectral functions can be used to express light-front finite temperature free propagators. As an application of our approach, we derive the light-front finite temperature spin 1/2 fermion propagator in full Dirac space.

## Key claim

The paper proves that light-front thermal field theory is equivalent to conventional thermal field theory for all Lagrangians where such equivalence has already been established at zero temperature, using spectral representations as the central tool rather than direct perturbative comparison.

## Method

The authors relate the light-front imaginary-time propagator to the light-front real-time Green function via Lehmann spectral representations, then show that the analytic continuations of both light-front and conventional imaginary-time propagators are identical when the spectral functions are equal. For scalar particles, the free propagators and perturbation theories are shown to be directly equal; for non-scalar particles (e.g., spin-1/2 fermions), extra instantaneous terms in the light-front Hamiltonian are absorbed into the propagator to recover the conventional form, following the same strategy used at zero temperature by Chang (1973), Rohrlich (1971), and Yan (1973).

## Key numbers

* Free scalar spectral function: \(\rho^{Lf}_0(p) = \rho^f_0(p) = 2\pi\epsilon(p_0)\delta(p^2-m^2)\), identical in light-front and conventional approaches.
* Free spinor spectral function: \(\rho^{Lf}_{1/2}(p) = \rho^f_{1/2}(p) = 2\pi\epsilon(p_0)(p\!\!\!/+m)\delta(p^2-m^2)\), identical in both approaches.
* Light-front imaginary-time scalar propagator: \(\Delta^{Lf}_0(i\omega_n,\up) = -1/(2p^-_n p^+ - m^2 - p_\perp^2)\) with \(p^-_n = \sqrt{2} i \pi 2nT - p^+\).
* Light-front imaginary-time fermion propagator (before adding instantaneous term): \(\Delta^{Lf}_{1/2}(i\omega_n,\up) = -(\bar p\!\!\!/+m)/(2p^-_n p^+ - m^2 - p_\perp^2)\).
* After adding instantaneous term \(\gamma^+/(2p^+)\), the light-front fermion propagator becomes \((p\!\!\!/_n+m)/(p_n^2 - m^2)\), matching the conventional form with \(p_0 = i(2n+1)\pi T\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature equivalence proofs of Chang (1973), Rohrlich (1971), Yan (1973), and Tomboulis (1973) to finite temperature, and resolves apparent differences found in earlier light-front thermal calculations by Alves, Das, and Perez (1999) and Weldon (2000). The result enables the use of conventional spectral functions to construct light-front finite-temperature propagators for any spin, and provides the foundation for applying light-front methods to thermal field theory calculations without needing to re-derive the formalism from scratch.
