# A meson exchange model for the YN interaction

> We present a new model for the hyperon-nucleon (Lambda N, Sigma N) interaction, derived within the meson exchange framework. The model incorporates the standard one boson exchange contributions of the lowest pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with coupling constants fixed by SU(6) symmetry relations. In addition - as the main feature of the new model - the exchange of two correlated pions or kaons, both in the scalar-isoscalar (sigma) and vector-isovector (rho) channels, is included.

# A meson exchange model for the YN interaction

## Key claim
The paper presents a new hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction model where the scalar-isoscalar (sigma) and vector-isovector (rho) exchanges are replaced by explicit correlated two-pion and two-kaon exchange, eliminating the need for free coupling constants at strange vertices. The model achieves excellent agreement with YN scattering data while removing a spurious bound-state structure in the Sigma N channel that appeared in the earlier Julich model A.

## Method
The authors construct a meson exchange model for the Lambda N and Sigma N interactions using standard one-boson exchange for the lowest pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with SU(6) symmetry relations for coupling constants. The central new feature is the inclusion of correlated pi pi and K Kbar exchange in both the scalar-isoscalar (sigma) and vector-isovector (rho) channels, derived from a microscopic model of B Bbar -> pi pi, K Kbar amplitudes using dispersion theory and unitarity, with parameters fitted to quasiempirical N Nbar -> pi pi amplitudes. Additional contributions from a0(980) and kappa meson exchange are included as effective parameterizations of strong meson-meson correlations.

## Key numbers
- The scalar-isoscalar part of correlated exchanges in the hyperon-nucleon systems (Lambda N, Sigma N) is about a factor of 2 weaker than in the NN channel
- The sigma meson mass is approximately 550 MeV
- The kappa meson mass is approximately 1000 MeV
- The correlated pi pi and K Kbar model describes empirical pi pi phase shifts from threshold up to 1.3 GeV
- The earlier Julich model A predicted a broad shoulder in Lambda p -> Lambda p at p_lab approximately 350 MeV/c due to a bound state in the 1S0 partial wave of the Sigma N channel; this structure is absent in the new model

## Context
This work extends the Bonn NN potential approach of Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster (1987) and the earlier Julich YN model of Holzenkamp, Holinde, and Speth (1989) by replacing phenomenological sigma and rho exchanges with microscopically derived correlated pi pi and K Kbar exchange, following the method of Reuber et al. (1999) for baryon-baryon channels with strangeness. The model resolves the unsatisfactory feature of free sigma-coupling parameters at strange vertices present in earlier Julich YN models, and enables more reliable predictions for hypernuclear physics and strangeness production reactions. The authors note that effective field theory approaches to NN scattering remain problematic for quantitative description, particularly in the strangeness sector where the expansion parameter m_K/m_N may not be small enough.
