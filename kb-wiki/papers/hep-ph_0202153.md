# The large-N(c) nuclear potential puzzle

> An analysis of the baryon-baryon potential from the point of view of large-N(c) QCD is performed. A comparison is made between the N(c)-scaling behavior directly obtained from an analysis at the quark-gluon level to the N(c)-scaling of the potential for a generic hadronic field theory in which it arises via meson exchanges and for which the parameters of the theory are given by their canonical large-N(c) scaling behavior. The purpose of this comparison is to use large-N(c) consistency to test the widespread view that the interaction between nuclei arises from QCD through the exchange of mesons. Although at the one- and two-meson exchange level the scaling rules for the potential derived from the hadronic theory matches the quark-gluon level prediction, at the three- and higher-meson exchange level a generic hadronic theory yields a potential which scales with N(c) faster than that of the quark-gluon theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while one- and two-meson exchange potentials derived from a generic hadronic field theory match the large-\(N_c\) scaling rules obtained directly from quark-gluon QCD, at three- and higher-meson exchange levels the hadronic theory yields a potential that scales with \(N_c\) faster than the quark-gluon prediction, creating a "large-\(N_c\) nuclear potential puzzle" that challenges the view that nuclear forces arise from QCD via meson exchange.

## Method

The authors compare the \(N_c\)-scaling behavior of the baryon-baryon potential derived from quark-gluon level analysis (using Witten's Hartree picture and the contracted \(SU(4)\) symmetry, following the approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Manohar, collectively referred to as KSM) to the scaling obtained from a generic hadronic field theory where the potential arises via meson exchanges with canonical large-\(N_c\) scaling parameters. They analyze multi-meson exchange diagrams (including seagull, ladder, and crossed-ladder topologies) using a non-abelian eikonal formula to sum amplitudes, and extract irreducible contributions to the potential by removing iterates of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

* The KSM analysis predicts that the strength of spin-isospin structures of the nucleon-nucleon potential scale as \(\mathcal{V}_{I=J} \sim N_c\) and \(\mathcal{V}_{I \neq J} \sim N_c^{-1}\).
* The three-meson exchange contribution to the potential from seagull-type diagrams scales as \(N_c^2\), which is incompatible with the KSM rules (which allow at most \(N_c\) for \(\mathcal{V}_{I=J}\)).
* The deuteron binding energy \(\varepsilon_B\) is of order \(N_c\) while the delta-nucleon mass splitting \(\delta M\) is of order \(1/N_c\) in the large-\(N_c\) world.
* The single meson-baryon coupling \(g_{1m}\) scales as \(\sqrt{N_c}\), while the seagull coupling \(g_{2m}\) scales as \(N_c^0\).
* The coupling of \(N\) mesons to a baryon scales at most as \(g_{Nm} \sim N_c^{1 - N/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kaplan and Savage (1995) and Kaplan and Manohar (1996) (collectively KSM), who derived large-\(N_c\) scaling rules for the nucleon-nucleon potential from quark-gluon level considerations, and the two-meson exchange calculation of Banerjee, Cohen, and Gelman (2001), which showed cancellations between box and crossed-box diagrams that made the two-meson exchange consistent with KSM rules. The present paper demonstrates that such cancellations do not occur for three- and higher-meson exchanges in a generic hadronic theory, raising questions about whether the traditional meson-exchange picture of nuclear forces is justified by QCD. The puzzle suggests that either the hadronic theory requires additional constraints beyond canonical large-\(N_c\) scaling, or that the mapping between quark-gluon and hadronic descriptions is more subtle than previously assumed.
