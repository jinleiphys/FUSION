# Isovector and isoscalar pairing in odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei within a quartet approach

> The quartet condensation model (QCM) is extended for the treatment of isovector and isoscalar pairing in odd-odd N=Z nuclei. In the extended QCM approach the lowest states of isospin T=1 and T=0 in odd-odd nuclei are described variationally by trial functions composed by a proton-neutron pair appended to a condensate of 4-body operators. The latter are taken as a linear superposition of an isovector quartet, built by two isovector pairs coupled to the total isospin T=0, and two collective isoscalar pairs. In all pairs the nucleons are distributed in time-reversed single-particle states of axial symmetry. The accuracy of the trial functions is tested for realistic pairing Hamiltonians and odd-odd N=Z nuclei with the valence nucleons moving above the cores $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca and $^{100}$Sn. It is shown that the extended QCM approach is able to predict with high accuracy the energies of the lowest T=0 and T=1 states. The present calculations indicate that in these states the isovector and the isoscalar pairing correlations coexist together, with the former playing a dominant role.

## Key claim

The extended quartet condensation model (QCM) can predict the energies of the lowest T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei with high accuracy (errors under 1%), but the calculations indicate that isovector pairing correlations play a dominant role over isoscalar pairing in both T=0 and T=1 states, with the two coexisting together.

## Method

The authors extended the quartet condensation model (QCM) from even-even to odd-odd N=Z nuclei by constructing variational trial states composed of a proton-neutron pair appended to a condensate of 4-body operators (quartets). The quartets are taken as a linear superposition of an isovector quartet (built from two isovector pairs coupled to total isospin T=0) and two collective isoscalar pairs, with all nucleons distributed in time-reversed single-particle states of axial symmetry. The accuracy of the trial functions was tested against exact diagonalization of realistic pairing Hamiltonians for nuclei with valence nucleons above the cores 16O, 40Ca, and 100Sn, using Skyrme-HF single-particle states and zero-range delta pairing forces.

## Key numbers

- For all nuclei studied, the QCM correlation energies for the lowest T=0 and T=1 states show errors under 1% relative to exact results (e.g., 22Na T=0: 13.87 MeV, error 0.00%; 54Co T=1: 16.12 MeV, error 0.14%; 114La T=0: 8.93 MeV, error 0.28%).
- The best approximation neglecting isoscalar pairs in the even-even core (states 10,11) yields errors still under 1% for all states (e.g., 30P T=0: 12.55 MeV, error 0.86%; 106I T=1: 5.13 MeV, error 0.23%).
- Neglecting the isovector quartet (states 12,13) produces much larger errors, up to 12.14% for 110Cs T=1 (7.16 MeV vs exact 8.03 MeV).
- Pure condensates of isoscalar or isovector pn pairs (states 14,15) give errors up to 6.94% for 30P T=1 (10.96 MeV vs exact 11.72 MeV) and up to 6.77% for 110Cs T=0 (6.65 MeV vs exact 7.09 MeV).
- Schmidt numbers for the odd T=0 pair range from 1.09 (50Mn) to 1.65 (30P), indicating significant collectivity and not a pure two-quasiparticle structure.

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation model previously applied to even-even N=Z nuclei (Negrea, Sandulescu, Gambacurta, 2016) to odd-odd systems, providing a microscopic framework that conserves particle number and isospin exactly. It contrasts with simpler three-body models (Sagawa, 2013) that treat the odd pn pair on top of an inert core, which work well for 18F and 42Sc but fail when core degrees of freedom are important. The results enable future studies of angular momentum restoration and applications to Gamow-Teller transitions in odd-odd N=Z nuclei, building on shell model calculations (e.g., Fujita, 2011) that show sensitivity to T=0 pn interactions.
