# Evolution of fluctuations near QCD critical point

> We propose to describe the time evolution of quasi-stationary fluctuations near QCD critical point by a system of stochastic Boltzmann-Langevin-Vlasov-type equations. We derive the equations and study the system analytically in the linearized regime. Known results for equilibrium stationary fluctuations as well as the critical scaling of diffusion coefficient are reproduced. We apply the approach to the long-standing question of the fate of the critical point fluctuations during the hadronic rescattering stage of the heavy-ion collision after chemical freezeout. We find that if conserved particle number fluctuations survive the rescattering, so do, under a certain additional condition, the fluctuations of non-conserved quantities, such as mean transverse momentum. We derive a simple analytical formula for the magnitude of this "memory" effect.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that fluctuations of non-conserved quantities, such as mean transverse momentum, can survive the hadronic rescattering stage after chemical freezeout if conserved particle number fluctuations also survive, provided a certain additional condition is met; this "memory" effect is derived analytically in an idealized regime, rather than being guaranteed for all conditions.

## Method

The author derives a system of stochastic Boltzmann-Langevin-Vlasov-type equations to describe the time evolution of quasi-stationary fluctuations near the QCD critical point, coupling a scalar field σ to classical particles with variable mass. The equations are linearized around equilibrium, and noise correlators are determined using the fluctuation-dissipation relation. The time evolution of fluctuation correlators is then studied analytically by solving the resulting Fokker-Planck equation for the probability distribution, focusing on the slowest (hydrodynamic) mode corresponding to particle number conservation.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium two-particle correlator in momentum space is given by \( V^{-1}\langle\delta\nu_{\bm p}\delta\nu_{\bm p'}\rangle = f_0\delta_{\bm p \bm p'} + \frac{g^2}{m^2 T}\frac{f_0}{\gamma}\frac{f_0'}{\gamma'} \), where \( m \) is the in-medium mass of the σ field, \( g = dM/d\sigma \), and \( f_0 \) is the equilibrium distribution.
- The diffusion coefficient \( D \) is defined by the ratio \( \lambda_0 / \bm q^2 = D \) for the hydrodynamic mode at small wavevector \( \bm q \), where \( \lambda_0 \) is the eigenvalue of the slowest mode.
- The zero eigenvalue mode \( \lambda_0 = 0 \) at \( \bm q = 0 \) satisfies \( g n_0 \mathcal{M}[1/\gamma] \bar h = -m^2 \sigma \), where \( n_0 = \int_{\bm p} f_0 \) is the equilibrium particle density and \( \mathcal{M} \) denotes the average over the equilibrium distribution.

## Context

This work extends the study of critical point fluctuations beyond the equilibrium assumption of Stephanov (1999) by incorporating dynamical evolution during the hadronic stage of heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the stochastic approach of Fox and Uhlenbeck for linearized Boltzmann equations and the hydrodynamic fluctuation treatment of Son (2004), but introduces kinetic Boltzmann-Vlasov equations to directly address experimental observables like mean transverse momentum fluctuations. The results enable quantitative predictions for the survival of critical fluctuations after chemical freezeout, which is relevant for the search for the QCD critical point at RHIC, SPS, FAIR, and NICA.
