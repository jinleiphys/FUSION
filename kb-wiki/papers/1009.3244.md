# Elliptic and triangular flow in event-by-event (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamics

> We present results for the elliptic and triangular flow coefficients in Au+Au collisions at root-s=200 AGeV using event-by-event (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic simulations. We study the effect of initial state fluctuations and finite viscosities on the flow coefficients v_2 and v_3 as functions of transverse momentum and pseudo-rapidity. Fluctuations are essential to reproduce the measured centrality dependence of elliptic flow. We argue that simultaneous measurements of v_2 and v_3 can determine eta/s more precisely.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations in the initial state are essential to reproduce the measured centrality dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at √s=200 AGeV, and simultaneous measurements of v₂ and v₃ can determine η/s more precisely than v₂ alone.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the music code with a variant of the Israel-Stewart formalism for shear viscosity. Initial energy density distributions were generated from a Glauber Monte-Carlo model with Gaussian smearing (width σ₀=0.4 fm) of wounded nucleon contributions, and the s95p-v1 equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas was employed. Freeze-out was performed using the Cooper-Frye formalism with viscous corrections to the distribution function, including resonance decays up to the φ-meson.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron elliptic flow v₂ at midrapidity is well described for a wide range of centralities when using η/s=0.08, the conjectured lower bound from AdS/CFT
- Triangular flow v₃(p_T) is reduced by approximately 20-30% by the inclusion of resonance decays
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section at √s=200 AGeV is σ_NN=42 mb
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is τ₀=0.4 fm/c
- Impact parameter ranges: 0-5% centrality uses b_min=0 fm, b_max=3.37 fm, ⟨b⟩=2.24 fm; 30-40% centrality uses b_min=8.23 fm, b_max=9.5 fm, ⟨b⟩=8.87 fm
- Energy density distributions are shown for one event with b=2.4 fm at initial time and after τ=6 fm/c for η/s=0 and η/s=0.16

## Context

This work extends the (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic simulations of Schenke (2010) by including shear viscosity and event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, building on earlier studies of fluctuations by Alver (2010), Petersen (2010), and others. The results enable more precise determination of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s through combined analysis of v₂ and v₃, which is not possible with averaged initial conditions where odd flow coefficients vanish by definition. The finding that η/s=0.08 describes elliptic flow data agrees with the parton cascade model of Xu (2007) based on perturbative QCD.
