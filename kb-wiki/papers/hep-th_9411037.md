# A Simple Confinement Mechanism for Light-Front Quantum Chromodynamics

> Light-front field theory offers a scenario in which a constituent picture of hadrons may arise, but only if cutoffs that violate explicit covariance and gauge invariance are used. The perturbative renormalization group can be used to approximate the cutoff QCD hamiltonian, and even at lowest orders the resultant hamiltonian displays interesting phenomenological features. A general scheme for computing and using these hamiltonians is discussed and it is explicitly shown that a confining interaction appears when the hamiltonian is computed to second order.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a confining interaction between quarks and gluons appears naturally in a light-front QCD hamiltonian computed to second order in the coupling constant using a similarity renormalization group and coupling coherence, but only if one accepts cutoffs that violate explicit covariance and gauge invariance and assumes that the infrared divergences cancel in color-singlet states rather than in individual parton energies.

## Method

The author computes the light-front QCD hamiltonian to second order in the coupling constant using a similarity renormalization group transformation with a cutoff on free-energy differences at vertices, combined with coupling coherence to determine counterterms. The resulting hamiltonian is then analyzed by isolating the most singular part of the quark-antiquark interaction arising from the exchange of wee gluons with very small longitudinal momentum, and adding it to the instantaneous gluon exchange interaction to obtain the net confining potential.

## Key numbers

* The quark self-energy to second order for zero current mass is given by \(\Sigma_{\Lambda}(p) = \frac{g_\Lambda^2 C_F \Lambda^2}{4 \pi^2 {\cal P}^+} \left\{ \ln\left(\frac{p^+}{\epsilon {\cal P}^+}\right) - \frac{3}{4} \right\} + {\cal O}(\epsilon)\), where \(\Lambda\) is the mass-dimension cutoff parameter, \({\cal P}^+\) is an arbitrary longitudinal momentum scale, and \(\epsilon\) is an infrared cutoff on longitudinal momenta.
* The most singular part of the quark-antiquark interaction after adding the induced and instantaneous terms is \(V_{singular} = -4 g_\Lambda^2 C_F \sqrt{p_1^+ p_2^+ k_1^+ k_2^+} \left(\frac{1}{q^+}\right)^2 \theta(\Lambda^2/{\cal P}^+ - |q^-|) \theta(|q^+| - \epsilon {\cal P}^+)\), where \(q^+\) is the exchanged longitudinal momentum and \(q^- = q_\perp^2/q^+\).
* The gluon self-energy to second order has a dominant term proportional to \(\frac{g_\Lambda^2 \Lambda^2}{{\cal P}^+} \ln\left(\frac{p^+}{\epsilon {\cal P}^+}\right)\), which is infrared divergent.

## Context

This work extends the program of Wilson and collaborators (1990s) to derive a constituent picture of hadrons from light-front QCD using renormalization group methods, and specifically builds on the similarity renormalization group developed by Glazek and Wilson (1993-1994). The paper explicitly shows that the confining interaction first reported in Perry (1994) arises from the cancellation of the instantaneous gluon exchange with the induced second-order interaction, leaving a singular potential that grows as \(1/(q^+)^2\). This mechanism is contrasted with QED, where the same second-order analysis would produce confinement that is canceled by long-range photon exchange; in QCD, the gluon self-energy is also infrared divergent, making it self-consistent to assume that confinement turns off the long-range gluon exchanges that would otherwise cancel it. The work enables future non-perturbative studies of the cutoff hamiltonian using bound state perturbation theory, and raises the open question of whether the confining interactions survive at higher orders in the coupling expansion.
