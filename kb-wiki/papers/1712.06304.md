# Holographic three flavor baryon in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with the D0-D4 background

> With the construction of the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model in the D0-D4 background, we systematically investigate the holographic baryon spectrum in the case of three flavors. The background geometry in this model is holographically dual to $U\left(N_{c}\right)$ Yang-Mills theory in large $N_{c}$ limit involving an excited state with a nonzero $θ$ angle or glue condensate $\left\langle \mathrm{Tr}\mathcal{F}\wedge\mathcal{F}\right\rangle =8π^{2}N_{c}\tildeκ$, which is proportional to the charge density of the smeared D0-branes through a parameter $b$ or $\tildeκ$. The classical solution of baryon in this model can be modified by embedding the Belavin-Polyakov-Schwarz-Tyupkin (BPST) instanton and we carry out the quantization of the collective modes with this solution. Then we extend the analysis to include the heavy flavor and find that the heavy meson is always bound in the form of the zero mode of the flavor instanton in strong coupling limit. The mass spectrum of heavy-light baryons in the situation with single- and double-heavy baryon is derived by solving the eigen equation of the quantized collective Hamiltonian. Afterwards we obtain that the constraint of stable baryon states has to be $1<b<3$ and the difference in the baryon spectrum becomes smaller as the D0 charge increases. It indicates that quarks or mesons can not form stable baryons if the $θ$ angle or glue condensate is sufficiently large. Our work is an extension of the previous study of this model and also agrees with those conclusions.

## Key claim

The holographic baryon spectrum in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with a D0-D4 background is extended to three flavors, but stable baryon states exist only when the D0 charge parameter satisfies \(1 < b < 3\); if the \(\theta\) angle or glue condensate becomes sufficiently large, quarks or mesons cannot form stable baryons.

## Method

The authors embed a BPST instanton solution into the \(SU(3)\) gauge field on the flavor D8/\(\overline{\text{D8}}\)-branes in the D0-D4 background, which introduces a nonzero \(\theta\) angle or glue condensate via the parameter \(b\) (related to D0-brane charge density). They quantize the collective modes of the soliton (position, size, and \(SU(3)\) orientation) and derive the mass spectrum by solving the eigen equation of the quantized collective Hamiltonian. Heavy flavor is included by embedding an additional pair of flavor branes separated from the light flavor branes, with heavy-light strings approximated as bi-fundamental vector fields.

## Key numbers

* The classical soliton mass minimum is \(M_{\text{min}} = 8 a b^{3/2} \pi^2 N_c + \sqrt{\frac{3-b}{15}} N_c\), where \(b\) is the D0 charge parameter and \(N_c\) is the number of colors.
* The minimized instanton size is \(\rho_{\text{min}}^2 = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{20} \frac{1}{\sqrt{3-b}} \frac{1}{a b^{3/2} \pi^2}\), with \(Z_{\text{min}} = 0\).
* The constraint for stable baryon states is \(1 < b < 3\); outside this range, stable baryons do not form.
* The glue condensate is \(\langle \text{Tr} \mathcal{F} \wedge \mathcal{F} \rangle = 8\pi^2 N_c \tilde{\kappa}\), where \(\tilde{\kappa}\) is proportional to the D0-brane charge density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-flavor (\(N_f=2\)) analysis of the WSS model in the D0-D4 background (Cai and Li, 2017) to the three-flavor case (\(N_f=3\)). It also generalizes the holographic baryon treatment of Sakai and Sugimoto (2005) and the instanton-based baryon description of Hata et al. (2007) to include \(\theta\)-angle dependence via D0-branes. The results enable future studies of heavy-light baryon spectra in holographic QCD with nonzero \(\theta\) angle or glue condensate, and can be compared with experimental data on heavy-light multiquark states.
