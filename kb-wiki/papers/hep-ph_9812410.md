# Gluon Condensate and Parton Propagation in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> A calculation of the thermal quark propagator is presented taking the gluon condensate above the critical temperature into account. The quark dispersion relation following from this propagator is derived.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the gluon condensate (which remains nonzero above the critical temperature) in the thermal quark propagator yields quark dispersion relations with two massive branches, similar to the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, but with an effective quark mass proportional to temperature rather than to the coupling constant times temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the quark self-energy to lowest order in the gluon condensate using the imaginary time formalism, expanding the loop integral for small loop momenta. They relate the moments of the longitudinal and transverse gluon propagators to the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates, which are extracted from lattice measurements of space- and timelike plaquette expectation values. The effective quark propagator is then decomposed into helicity eigenstates, and the dispersion relations are obtained numerically from the poles of this propagator.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature gluon condensate is ⟨G²⟩₀ = (2.5 ± 1.0) T_c⁴.
- The thermal gluon condensate ⟨G²⟩_T above T_c increases like T^α with α between 3 and 4.
- In the temperature range 1.1 T_c < T < 4 T_c, the effective quark mass is approximately m_eff = 1.15 T.
- The quark dispersion relations are shown numerically for T = 1.1 T_c, T = 2 T_c, and T = 4 T_c.

## Context

This work extends zero-temperature calculations of quark and gluon propagators containing condensates (Ref. [ref5] in the paper) to finite temperature, using lattice results for plaquette expectation values (Ref. [ref4]). The resulting dispersion relations are qualitatively similar to those found perturbatively in the HTL approximation (Ref. [ref8]), but with a different effective mass: m_eff = gT/√6 in HTL versus m_eff ∝ T here. The authors propose using this effective quark propagator to compute photon and dilepton production rates from the quark-gluon plasma, and discuss the possibility of constructing a thermal gluon propagator containing the gluon condensate to address the absence of static magnetic screening in HTL-resummed propagators.
