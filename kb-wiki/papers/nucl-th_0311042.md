# Finite-width QCD sum rules for rho and omega mesons

## Key claim

The paper presents a combined Borel QCD sum rule analysis of the in-medium behavior of rho and omega mesons that explicitly accounts for finite widths, finding that the rho and omega peak positions are strongly sensitive to the density dependence of the 4-quark condensate (parameterized by kappa_N) and that the strikingly different subtraction constants for the two mesons cause them to behave differently in medium, contrary to universal scaling hypotheses.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Borel-transformed QCD sum rule for the vector current-current correlation function at rest in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, truncating the operator product expansion at mass dimension 6 and twist 2. The hadronic spectral function is modeled using a Breit-Wigner-type resonance shape with density-dependent mass parameter m_V(n) and width factor gamma_V(n), incorporating the off-shell forward meson-nucleon scattering amplitude T_VN from Lutz (2002) via the Bethe-Salpeter approach. The sum rule is solved by requiring maximum flatness of the extracted mass as a function of the Borel parameter M^2 within a chosen Borel window, with the continuum threshold s_V adjusted accordingly.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum masses adjusted to m_rho,omega(n=0) = 777 MeV using kappa_0 = 3
- Vacuum widths: rho = 150.7 MeV, omega = 8.43 MeV
- Normal nuclear density n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3 (standard value, used throughout)
- Borel window: M_min^2 ~ 0.6 GeV^2 (10% truncation criterion), M_max^2 = 1.5 GeV^2 for omega, 2.4 GeV^2 for rho (50% continuum rule)
- Chiral condensate: <qbar q>_0 = -(0.24 GeV)^3, m_q = 5.5 MeV, sigma_N = 45 MeV
- Gluon condensate: <(alpha_s/pi) G^2>_0 = (0.33 GeV)^4, alpha_s = 0.38, M_N^0 = 770 MeV
- At n_0 and kappa_N = 3, the 4-quark condensate drops to 60% of its vacuum value
- Reasonable numerical limits for kappa_N: 0 <= kappa_N <= 4 for n <= n_0

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Hatsuda (1993) and Leupold (1998) by treating rho and omega mesons on a common footing with explicit finite-width spectral functions, motivated by transport model studies showing that relative peak shifts and broadenings must be known for HADES experiments. The analysis uses the meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes from Lutz (2002) and contrasts with the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (1991) by finding no universal in-medium behavior. The results enable experimental constraints from the omega decay measurement by Metag (2003) to determine the 4-quark condensate density dependence, which in turn fixes the in-medium rho mass shift.
