# Renyi statistics in equilibrium statistical mechanics

> The Renyi statistics in the canonical and microcanonical ensembles is examined in the general case and in particular for the ideal gas. In the microcanonical ensemble the Renyi statistics is equivalent with the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics. By the exact analytical results for the ideal gas, it is shown that in the canonical ensemble in the thermodynamic limit the Renyi statistics is also equivalent with the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics. Furthermore it satisfies the requirements of the equilibrium thermodynamics, i.e. the thermodynamical potential of the statistical ensemble is a homogeneous function of degree 1 of its extensive variables of state. We conclude that the Renyi statistics duplicates the thermodynamical relations stemming from the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics in the thermodynamical limit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the thermodynamic limit (infinite system size), the Rényi statistics in both the canonical and microcanonical ensembles becomes equivalent to the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, and therefore satisfies all requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics. However, this equivalence holds only in the thermodynamic limit; for finite systems, the Rényi statistics violates the zeroth law of thermodynamics, the principle of additivity, and the Euler theorem.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the phase space distribution function, partition function, free energy, entropy, pressure, chemical potential, and heat capacity for the Rényi statistics in the canonical ensemble, both in general form and specifically for the classical nonrelativistic ideal gas of N identical particles. They then examined the thermodynamic limit by taking N→∞ and V→∞ with constant specific volume v=V/N, considering two cases for the parameter z=1/(q-1): z intensive (z=constant) and z extensive (z/N=constant). The thermodynamic consistency was tested by checking the additivity of entropy, the zeroth law of thermodynamics, the Euler theorem, and the Gibbs-Duhem relation.

## Key numbers

- For the ideal gas in the canonical ensemble, the mean energy is ⟨H⟩ = (3/2)kTN, identical to the Boltzmann-Gibbs result.
- The heat capacity for the Rényi statistics ideal gas is C_{VzN} = (3/2)kN, the same as for Gibbs statistics.
- In the thermodynamic limit with z intensive, the single-particle distribution function reduces to the Maxwell-Boltzmann form f(p) = 1/(2πmkT)^(3/2) exp(-p²/(2mkT)).
- The pressure in the thermodynamic limit with z intensive is p = kT/v, identical to the Gibbs result.
- In the Gibbs limit z→±∞ with N=constant, the Rényi partition function reduces to the Gibbs partition function Z_R|_(z→±∞) = Z_G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Parvan and Biro, 2008) on the general relations for Rényi statistics in the canonical ensemble. It directly addresses the thermodynamic self-consistency of Rényi statistics, which had been questioned in the literature. The paper contradicts claims by Oikonomou (2007) and Bagci (2007) that Rényi statistics yields exponential rather than power-law distribution functions. The results enable a clear comparison with Tsallis statistics, which the authors note has been more extensively studied but faces similar issues with the zeroth law of thermodynamics, as previously analyzed by Parvan (2006, 2007) and Botet et al. (2001, 2002).
