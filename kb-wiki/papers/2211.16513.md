# Freezing-In Gravitational Waves

> The thermal plasma in the early universe produced a stochastic gravitational wave (GW) background, which peaks today in the microwave regime and was dubbed the cosmic gravitational microwave background (CGMB). In previous works only single graviton production processes that contribute to the CGMB have been considered. Here we also investigate graviton pair production processes and show that these can lead to a significant contribution if the ratio between the maximum temperature and the Planck mass, $T_{\rm max}/m_{\rm p}$, divided by the internal coupling in the heat bath is large enough. As the dark matter freeze-in production mechanism is conceptually very similar to the GW production mechanism from the primordial thermal plasma, we refer to the latter as ``GW freeze-in production''. We show that quantum gravity effects appear in single graviton production and are smaller by a factor $(T_{\rm max}/m_{\rm p})^2$ than the leading order contribution. In our work we explicitly compute the CGMB spectrum within a scalar model with quartic interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that graviton pair production processes can contribute significantly to the cosmic gravitational microwave background (CGMB) if the ratio of the maximum temperature to the Planck mass, divided by the internal coupling in the heat bath, is large enough; this contribution is proportional to \((T_{\rm max}/m_{\rm p})^3\) and can dominate over the previously considered single graviton production which is proportional to \(g^2 \times (T_{\rm max}/m_{\rm p})\).

## Method

The authors compute the CGMB spectrum within a massless complex scalar field model with quartic interaction \(\lambda\), coupled to gravity via linearized general relativity. They expand the scalar and graviton distribution functions perturbatively in the quartic coupling \(\lambda\) and the gravitational coupling \(\kappa \propto 1/m_{\rm p}\), solving the Boltzmann-like evolution equations for the graviton distribution function up to fourth order in these couplings. The single graviton production rate at order \(\mathcal{O}(\lambda^2 \kappa^2)\) and the graviton pair production rate at order \(\mathcal{O}(\kappa^4)\) are calculated from the relevant Feynman diagrams, and the resulting GW energy density is obtained by integrating over the cosmological expansion history from a maximum temperature \(T_{\rm max}\) down to the present day.

## Key numbers

* The Planck mass is \(m_{\rm p} \approx 1.2 \times 10^{19}\) GeV.
* In slow roll inflationary cosmology, the maximum temperature cannot be much higher than \(10^{-3}\, m_{\rm p}\).
* The single graviton production contribution to the GW energy density is proportional to \(g^2 \times (T_{\rm max}/m_{\rm p})\).
* The graviton pair production contribution to the GW energy density is proportional to \((T_{\rm max}/m_{\rm p})^3\).
* Quantum gravity effects in single graviton production are smaller by a factor \((T_{\rm max}/m_{\rm p})^2\) than the leading order contribution.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the CGMB spectrum that considered only single graviton production processes in the Standard Model (Ghiglieri 2015, Ghiglieri 2020) and in beyond Standard Model theories (Ringwald 2020, Castells-Tiestos 2022, Klose 2022). By including graviton pair production processes for the first time, the paper identifies a regime where these can dominate the CGMB spectrum, and it explicitly identifies the order at which quantum gravity and back-reaction effects would appear, providing a roadmap for probing the quantization of gravity through future GW observations.
