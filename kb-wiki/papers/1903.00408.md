# The energy-momentum tensor of spin-1 hadrons: formalism

> We provide the complete decomposition of the local gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor for spin-1 hadrons, including non-conserved terms for the individual parton flavors and antisymmetric contributions originating from intrinsic spin. We state sum rules for the gravitational form factors appearing in this decomposition and provide relations for the mass decomposition, work balance, total and orbital angular momentum, mass radius, and inertia tensor. Generalizing earlier work, we derive relations between the total and orbital angular momentum and the Mellin moments of twist-2 and 3 generalized parton distributions, accessible in hard exclusive processes with spin-1 targets. Throughout the work, we comment on the unique features in these relations originating from the spin-1 nature of the hadron, being absent in the lower spin cases.

## Key claim

The paper provides the complete decomposition of the local gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor for spin-1 hadrons, including non-conserved terms for individual parton flavors and antisymmetric contributions from intrinsic spin, but the authors note that the antisymmetric part of the gluon EMT vanishes because no local gauge-invariant definition of gluon spin exists, and the antisymmetric quark contribution is conserved due to the QCD equations of motion.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general parametrization of the energy-momentum tensor matrix elements for an on-shell spin-1 hadron in terms of gravitational form factors (GFFs), separating the symmetric and antisymmetric components and distinguishing between conserved total EMT and non-conserved partial EMT for quarks and gluons. They derived sum rules from energy-momentum conservation, computed multipole moments including mass decomposition, angular momentum, mass radius, and inertia tensor in the Breit frame, and established relations between GFFs and Mellin moments of twist-2 and twist-3 generalized parton distributions (GPDs).

## Key numbers

* The total EMT forward matrix element sum rule gives ∑_a 𝒢₁^a(0) = 1, where the sum runs over quarks and gluons.
* The angular momentum sum rule gives ∑_a 𝒢₅^a(0) = 2, first derived by Abidin (2008).
* The quark spin contribution is S_q^i = (s^i/2) \tilde{G}_1(0) = -(s^i/2) 𝒢^q_{10}(0), relating the antisymmetric GFF 𝒢^q_{10} to the axial-vector form factor at t=0.
* The partial orbital angular momentum for parton flavor a is L_a^i = (s^i/2)[𝒢₅^a(0) + (1/2)𝒢₇^a(0) + 𝒢^a_{10}(0)], with the 𝒢₇ term being a new feature absent in spin-0 and spin-1/2 targets.
* The squared mass radius is ⟨r²⟩ = (1/M²) ∑_a [3𝒜_a(0) + ℬ_a(0)], where 𝒜_a and ℬ_a are combinations of GFFs defined in the text.

## Context

This work extends earlier decompositions of the spin-1 EMT by Holstein (2006), Abidin (2008), and Taneja (2011) by including all non-conserved terms (adding 𝒢₇, 𝒢₈, 𝒢₉ for the symmetric part and 𝒢₁₁ for the antisymmetric part) and providing complete sum rules and physical interpretations. It generalizes the spin-1/2 and spin-0 results from Ji (1996), Leader (2013), and Lorcé (2017) to spin-1 systems, and derives a spin-1 analogue of the Penttinen-Polyakov-Shuvaev-Strikman (PPSS) sum rule relating twist-3 GPD moments to orbital angular momentum. The results enable experimental extraction of spin-1 gravitational form factors from deeply virtual Compton scattering and meson production at Jefferson Lab and a future Electron-Ion Collider.
