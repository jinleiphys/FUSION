# New Description of the Doublet Bands in Doubly Odd Nuclei

> The experimentally observed $ΔI = 1$ doublet bands in some odd-odd nuclei are analyzed within the orthosymplectic extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM). A new, purely collective interpretation of these bands is given on the basis of the obtained boson-fermion dynamical symmetry of the model. It is illustrated by its application to three odd-odd nuclei from the $A\sim 130$ region, namely $^{126}Pr$, $^{134}Pr$ and $^{132}La$. The theoretical predictions for the energy levels of the doublet bands as well as $E2$ and $M1$ transition probabilities between the states of the yrast band in the last two nuclei are compared with experiment and the results of other theoretical approaches. The obtained results reveal the applicability of the orthosymplectic extension of the IVBM.

## Key claim

The doublet bands in odd-odd nuclei from the A~130 region can be described within a purely collective framework using the orthosymplectic extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), without requiring a rigid triaxial core or chiral geometry. The authors hedge that while the model reproduces energy levels and transition probabilities, the interpretation does not rely on the chiral mechanism that other models assume.

## Method

The authors extend the symplectic IVBM to the orthosymplectic algebra OSp(2Ω/12,R), coupling boson collective degrees of freedom (described by Sp(12,R)) with fermion degrees of freedom for one or two particles occupying the h11/2 orbital. They first fit the five parameters of the even-even core Hamiltonian to the ground and γ bands of 124Ce, 134Ce, and 132Ba using a χ² procedure, then add three additional parameters (γ, ζ, η) to describe the odd-mass neighbors (125Ce, 135Ce, 133La) and finally the odd-odd nuclei (126Pr, 134Pr, 132La). The yrast and side bands are mapped onto sequences of SU(3) basis states with ΔN=2, where the yrast band corresponds to (0,μ) multiplets and the side band to (λ≠0,μ) multiplets.

## Key numbers

- For 126Pr, 134Pr, and 132La, the ground state of the yrast band is assigned KπJ = 8+, arising from vector coupling of proton I_p = 11/2 and neutron I_n = 11/2.
- The energy levels of the ground state band of 124Ce are compared with experiment up to high angular momenta (figure shows states up to approximately L=12).
- The energy levels of the ground and γ bands of 134Ce are compared with experiment, with IVBM and IBM results described as "almost identical" for the ground state band.
- The energy levels of the ground and γ bands of 132Ba are compared with experiment, with IVBM predictions reproducing band structures better than CPHCM and IBM.
- For odd-mass neighbors, the ground state band of 125Ce (Kπ = 5/2+) is described with states J = 5/2, 7/2, 9/2, ... corresponding to N = 2(J-I) = 0, 2, 4, ...

## Context

This work extends the orthosymplectic IVBM previously applied to odd-mass nuclei (Ganev et al., 2008) to describe odd-odd nuclei, offering an alternative to chiral interpretations based on the tilted axis cranking model (Frauendorf and Meng, 1997), particle-rotor model, and core-quasiparticle coupling model. The authors note that the Interacting Boson Fermion-Fermion Model (IBFFM) applied to 134Pr (Petrache et al., 2000; Tonev et al., 2002) found that doublet bands arise from weak dynamic chirality combined with an intrinsic symmetry, while the present work provides a purely collective interpretation without requiring triaxial rigidity. The approach enables systematic description of even-even, odd-mass, and odd-odd nuclei within a single algebraic framework, starting from the even-even core and building up through successive particle couplings.
