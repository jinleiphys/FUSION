# Implications of parity-violating electron scattering experiments on $^{48}$Ca (CREX) and $^{208}$Pb (PREX-II) for nuclear energy density functionals

> Recent precise parity-violating electron scattering experiments on $^{48}$Ca (CREX) and $^{208}$Pb (PREX-II) provide a new insight on the formation of neutron skin in nuclei. Within the energy density functional (EDF) framework, we investigate the implications of CREX and PREX-II data on nuclear matter symmetry energy and isovector properties of finite nuclei: neutron skin thickness and dipole polarizability. The weak-charge form factors from the CREX and PREX-II experiments are employed directly in constraining the relativistic density-dependent point coupling EDFs. The EDF established with the CREX data acquires considerably smaller values of the symmetry energy parameters, neutron skin thickness and dipole polarizability both for $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, in comparison to the EDF obtained using the PREX-II data, and previously established EDFs. Presented analysis shows that CREX and PREX-II experiments could not provide consistent constraints for the isovector sector of the EDFs, and further theoretical and experimental studies are required.

## Key claim

The CREX and PREX-II parity-violating electron scattering experiments cannot provide consistent constraints for the isovector sector of nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs), as the DDPC-CREX functional yields considerably smaller symmetry energy parameters, neutron skin thicknesses, and dipole polarizabilities than the DDPC-PREX functional, with previously established EDFs falling between them.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with density-dependent point-coupling interactions to constrain 12 model parameters by minimizing a chi-squared objective function. They used nuclear ground-state properties (binding energies for 34 nuclei, charge radii for 26 nuclei, and mean pairing gaps for 15 nuclei) together with the weak-charge form factors from CREX (for ⁴⁸Ca) and PREX-II (for ²⁰⁸Pb) to establish three new functionals: DDPC-CREX, DDPC-PREX, and DDPC-REX (the latter using both nuclei's data with larger adopted errors). Statistical uncertainties were estimated via covariance analysis.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation for DDPC-CREX: J = 27.01 ± 0.23 MeV; for DDPC-PREX: J = 36.18 ± 0.80 MeV; for DDPC-REX: J = 28.86 ± 0.33 MeV.
- Slope of symmetry energy at saturation for DDPC-CREX: L = 19.60 ± 1.01 MeV; for DDPC-PREX: L = 101.78 ± 9.34 MeV; for DDPC-REX: L = 30.03 ± 2.06 MeV.
- Neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca: DDPC-CREX = 0.110 ± 0.004 fm; DDPC-PREX = 0.218 ± 0.005 fm; DDPC-REX = 0.142 ± 0.005 fm.
- Neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb: DDPC-CREX = 0.051 ± 0.006 fm; DDPC-PREX = 0.250 ± 0.010 fm; DDPC-REX = 0.105 ± 0.008 fm.
- CREX experimental weak-charge form factor for ⁴⁸Ca at q = 0.8733 fm⁻¹: F_W = 0.1304 ± 0.0052 (stat) ± 0.0020 (syst).
- PREX-II experimental weak-charge form factor for ²⁰⁸Pb at q = 0.3978 fm⁻¹: F_W = 0.368 ± 0.013 (exp).
- Experimental dipole polarizability: α_D(⁴⁸Ca) = 2.07 ± 0.22 fm³; α_D(²⁰⁸Pb) = 19.6 ± 0.6 fm³.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of parity-violating electron scattering data from the CREX (2022) and PREX-II (2021) experiments, following earlier studies that found difficulties in simultaneously describing the parity-violating asymmetry A_PV in both ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb using EDFs (PhysRevLett.127.232501, 2021; arXiv:2206.03134). The results are compared with previously established relativistic functionals DD-PC1 (PhysRevC.78.034318) and DD-PCX (PhysRevC.99.034318), as well as with ab initio predictions for neutron skin thicknesses (Hagen 2016; Simonis 2019). The findings highlight the need for further theoretical and experimental studies to resolve the inconsistencies between CREX and PREX-II constraints on the isovector sector of nuclear EDFs.
