# On $m_T$ dependence of femtoscopy scales for meson and baryon pairs

> The $m_T$-dependencies of the femto-scales, the so-called interferometry and source radii, are investigated within the hydrokinetic model for different types of particle pairs - pion-pion, kaon-kaon, proton-proton and proton-lambda, - produced in Pb+Pb and $p+p$ collisions at the LHC. In particular, such property of the femto-scales momentum behavior as $m_T$-scaling is studied for the systems with (w) and without (w/o) intensive transverse flow, and also w and w/o re-scattering at the final afterburner stage of the matter evolution. The detail spatiotemporal description obtained within hydrokinetic model is compared with the simple analytical results for the spectra and longitudinal interferometry radii depending on the effective temperature on the hypersurface of maximal emission, proper time of such emission, and intensity of transverse flow. The derivation of the corresponding analytical formulas and discussion about a possibility for their utilization by the experimentalists for the simple femtoscopy data analysis is the main aim of this theoretical investigation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the commonly expected $m_T$-scaling of femtoscopy radii (where radii scale as $1/\sqrt{m_T}$) is violated for pion-kaon pairs in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies due to strong transverse flows, re-scattering in the afterburner stage, and non-Gaussian correlation function shapes; however, the same analytical formula can still extract the maximal emission time for each hadron pair, and surprisingly, the angle-averaged source radii for meson and baryon pairs (including proton-proton and proton-lambda) do exhibit $m_T$-scaling in the pair rest frame.

## Method

The authors used the hydrokinetic model (HKM) to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV and $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV at the LHC, with initial conditions from the MC Glauber generator GLISSANDO, hydrodynamic evolution, and UrQMD cascade for the afterburner stage. They derived an analytical formula for the longitudinal interferometry radius $R_l$ that accounts for transverse flow via a parameter $\alpha$ and the maximal emission time $\tau$, and fitted this formula to HKM results for pion-pion, kaon-kaon, proton-proton, and proton-lambda pairs. The fits used parameters constrained by combined fits to pion and kaon momentum spectra, and the source radii $r_0$ were extracted from Gaussian fits to the angle-averaged source functions.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, without re-scattering stage: $\tau_{\pi}=7.41$ fm/$c$ for pions, $\tau_{K}=7.56$ fm/$c$ for kaons, with $\alpha=2.8$ and $T=165$ MeV (Fig. 2 fit).
- For full HKM (with re-scattering) in Pb+Pb collisions, fitting radii extracted from narrow $q$ range ($0-0.04$ GeV/$c$): $\tau_{\pi}=9.44 \pm 0.02$ fm/$c$, $\tau_K=12.40 \pm 0.04$ fm/$c$, with $T_\pi=147$ MeV, $T_K=141$ MeV, $\alpha_\pi=8.5$, $\alpha_K=1.5$ (Fig. 4 fit).
- For source radii $r_0$ from angle-averaged source functions in Pb+Pb collisions: $\tau_{\pi\pi}=11.47 \pm 0.03$ fm/$c$, $\tau_{KK}=11.26 \pm 0.04$ fm/$c$, $\tau_{pp}=11.30 \pm 0.13$ fm/$c$, $\tau_{p\Lambda}=12.44 \pm 0.29$ fm/$c$, with $T=141$ MeV and $\alpha \rightarrow \infty$ for all pairs (Fig. 6 fit).
- For $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, mean charged multiplicity $\langle dN_{ch}/d\eta \rangle = 9.2$: $\tau=1.60 \pm 0.04$ fm/$c$, $\alpha=9.52 \pm 0.44$, $T=165$ MeV (Fig. 7 fit).
- For $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, mean charged multiplicity $\langle dN_{ch}/d\eta \rangle = 17.9$: $\tau=1.83 \pm 0.02$ fm/$c$, $\alpha=13.9 \pm 0.22$, $T=165$ MeV (Fig. 8 fit).

## Context

This work extends the hydrokinetic model studies of femtoscopy by Sinyukov and collaborators (e.g., HKM1, 2012; Shapoval et al., 2014) that predicted strong violation of $m_T$-scaling between pions and kaons at LHC energies, contradicting the earlier 1D hydrodynamic result of Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988) which predicted $R_l \propto \sqrt{T/m_T}$. The paper provides an analytical fitting formula that enables experimentalists to extract the maximal emission time for different hadron pairs from femtoscopy data, and it enables future comparisons of pion, kaon, proton, and lambda femtoscopy at the LHC to test the surprising $m_T$-scaling found for source radii in the pair rest frame.
