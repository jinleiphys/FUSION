# The hyperon neutron star mean field model

> In this paper the impact of the strength of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon on the maximum neutron star mass is shown. The strong hyperon-hyperon coupling constant together with the additional nonlinear meson interaction terms lead to the essential softening of the equation of state and to the existence of another more compact branch of stable neutron star configurations. The analysis of the mass-radius relations leads to the conclusion that the additional stable configurations of hyperon-rich neutron stars are characterized by the reduced value of radii.

# The hyperon neutron star mean field model

## Key claim

The inclusion of strong hyperon-hyperon interactions together with additional nonlinear meson coupling terms (beyond standard relativistic mean field theory) can produce a second, more compact branch of stable neutron star configurations, not just a single family of solutions. This additional branch exists specifically for the TM1* parameter set with strong Y-Y coupling, not for the standard TM1 set or for weak Y-Y interactions.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean field model based on the chiral effective Lagrangian of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (FST), extended with nonlinear scalar-vector and vector-vector self-interactions. They used two parameter sets (G2 and TM1*) and supplemented them with hyperon-meson couplings determined from SU(6) symmetry for vector couplings and from hyperon potential depths for scalar couplings, considering both weak (U_ΛΛ ≈ 5 MeV) and strong (U_ΛΛ ≈ 20 MeV) hyperon-hyperon interactions. The equation of state was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov hydrostatic equilibrium equations to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron star configurations.

## Key numbers

- The reduction in maximum mass when going from weak to strong hyperon-hyperon interaction is of the order of 0.1-0.2 solar masses
- The additional stable branch of configurations shows a reduction in radius of approximately 2.5 km compared to ordinary neutron stars
- The critical bag parameter for quark matter is B^{1/4}_{crit} = 171 MeV; for B > B_{crit} no quark phase appears in the neutron star interior
- For B^{1/4} = 150 MeV (below B_{crit}), the maximum hybrid star mass is about 1.52 solar masses, greater than the maximum mass of the additional compact branch
- The scalar meson mass parameters used are m_σ = 520.25 MeV (G2) and 511.20 MeV (TM1*)
- The hyperon potential depths considered for Λ in saturated nuclear matter are -27 MeV, -28 MeV, and -30 MeV, corresponding to g_σΛ values of 9.203, 9.158, and 9.069 respectively

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (1997) by applying their effective field theory Lagrangian to hyperon-rich neutron star matter, building on earlier studies of hyperon stars by Weber, Glendenning, and Schaffner. The authors compare their results with the standard TM1 parameter set of Sugahara and Toki (1994) and the extended TM1* set of Del Estal et al. (2001). The finding of a second stable branch of compact hyperon-rich configurations (termed "hyperstars") suggests a possible connection to short gamma-ray bursts via phase transitions, as proposed by Dar (1998). The analysis also establishes conditions under which quark matter may appear, enabling future studies of hybrid star configurations.
