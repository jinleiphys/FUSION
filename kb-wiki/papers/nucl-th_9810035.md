# Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock simulation of the expansion of abraded nuclei

> A recent interpretation of the caloric curve based on the expansion of the abraded spectator nucleus is re-analysed in the framework of the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) evolution. It is shown that the TDHF dynamics is more complex than a single monopolar collective motion at moderate energy. The inclusion of other important collective degrees of freedom may lead to the dynamical creation of hollow structure. Then, low density regions could be locally reached after a long time by the creation of these exotic density profiles. In particular the systematic of the minimum density reached during the expansion (the so-called turning points) appears to be different.

## Key claim

The TDHF dynamics of abraded nuclei is more complex than the single monopolar breathing mode assumed by Papp and Nörenberg (1998) to explain the caloric curve; at moderate excitation energies (T < 5 MeV), the inclusion of other collective degrees of freedom leads to the dynamical creation of hollow structures, making the systematic of minimum density reached during expansion (turning points) different from the collective model predictions.

## Method

The authors initialized hot, diluted spherical nuclei with masses A-ΔA_i (ΔA_i from 8 to 108 nucleons) at initial density ρ_i = 0.8 ρ_0 and excitation energies E_i* = γ ΔA_i with γ = 13.3 MeV, using a scaling assumption on radial wave functions. They then evolved these systems using the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations with spherically symmetric mean-field potentials corresponding to either a soft (SkM*) or stiff (SIII) equation of state, conserving occupation numbers and total energy while allowing particle evaporation into a 15 fm sphere.

## Key numbers

- Initial excitation energies: E_i* = γ ΔA_i with γ = 13.3 MeV, for ΔA_i ranging from 8 to 108 nucleons
- Initial density: ρ_i = 0.8 ρ_0 for all systems
- Time step: Δt = 0.75 fm/c; spatial discretization: Δr = 0.2 fm; total r-space: 300 fm
- For A=191 at low excitation energy, the TDHF simulation reached lower central densities than the initial dilution by creating a hole at the center, in contrast to the schematic model prediction
- For A=150 and A=111, turning points from TDHF showed little difference from the schematic construction; for A=191 and A=89, differences were significant
- At high initial temperature (T > 5 MeV) and masses below 150, the collective model predicted larger expansion amplitude than TDHF
- Fourier analysis of density evolution over 1500 fm/c revealed collective modes at frequencies 17, 20, 25, and approximately 40 MeV for η = 0.8

## Context

This work re-analyzes the interpretation of the caloric curve proposed by Papp and Nörenberg (1998), who used a macroscopic breathing mode model to explain the ALADIN experimental results on gold projectile fragmentation. The authors extend prior TDHF studies of expanding nuclear systems (Vautherin 1980, Strack 1987) and BUU simulations (bubble dynamics) by demonstrating that the breathing mode assumption fails at moderate energies, and that hollow structures can form through beating of intrinsic collective modes as previously suggested in their own work (Lacroix and Chomaz 1998). This finding implies that the determination of an effective temperature during expansion is not well-defined without additional equilibration processes beyond mean-field, and that future models must consider an enlarged phase space of collective degrees of freedom.
