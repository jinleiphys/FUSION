# Non-extensive statistics in Au-Au collisions

> Particle production yields measured in central Au-Au collision at RHIC are obtained with free Fermi and Bose gases and also with a replacement of these statistics by non-extensive statistics. For the latter calculation, a set of different parameters was used with values of the Tsallis parameter $q$ chosen between 1.01 and 1.25, with 1.16 generating the best agreement with experimental data, an indication that non-extensive statistics may be one of the underlying features in heavy ion-collisions.

## Key claim

Non-extensive statistics with a Tsallis parameter of q = 1.16 provides a better description of particle production yields in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC than standard Fermi and Bose statistics, but the authors note that the range corresponding to χ²/dof ~ 1 is 1.11 < q < 1.22, indicating that the optimal value is not uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors computed particle production yields for central Au-Au collisions at √s = 130 GeV using free Fermi and Bose gas models, then replaced the standard statistics with non-extensive Tsallis statistics. They performed a χ² minimization over the Tsallis parameter q in the range 1.01 to 1.25, fitting theoretical particle ratios to experimental data from the STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and BRAHMS collaborations, with temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Optimal Tsallis parameter: q = 1.16, obtained from χ² minimization
- At q = 1.16: T = 58.2 MeV, μB = 51.5 MeV, χ²/dof = 0.75926 (with dof = 14)
- At q = 1.0 (standard statistics): T = 148.8 MeV, μB = 32.5 MeV, χ²/dof = 2.97387
- The acceptable range for q (χ²/dof ~ 1) is 1.11 < q < 1.22
- The q parameter was varied between 1.01 and 1.25
- The antiproton-to-proton ratio at q = 1.16 is 0.656767, compared to experimental values of 0.65 ± 0.07 (STAR), 0.64 ± 0.07 (PHENIX), 0.60 ± 0.07 (PHOBOS), and 0.64 ± 0.07 (BRAHMS)
- The K⁻/K⁺ ratio at q = 1.16 is 0.829491, compared to experimental values of 0.88 ± 0.05 (STAR), 0.78 ± 0.13 (PHENIX), 0.91 ± 0.09 (PHOBOS), and 0.89 ± 0.07 (BRAHMS)

## Context

This work extends previous applications of non-extensive statistics to heavy-ion collisions by Menezes (2015), Megias (2015), Deppman (2012), and Chiapparini (2019), using a broader set of experimental data from multiple RHIC collaborations. The results suggest that non-extensive statistics can describe particle production in Au-Au collisions more accurately than standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, and the approach enables future studies of additional factors influencing particle distributions in complex collision systems.
