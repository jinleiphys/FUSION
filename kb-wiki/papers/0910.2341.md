# Detailed analysis of quantum phase transitions within the $u(2)$ algebra

> We analyze in detail the quantum phase transitions that arise in models based on the $u(2)$ algebraic description for bosonic systems with two types of scalar bosons. First we discuss the quantum phase transition that occurs in hamiltonians that admix the two dynamical symmetry chains $u(2)\supset u(1)$ and $u(2)\supset so(2)$ by diagonalizing the problem exactly in the $u(1)$ basis. Then we apply the coherent state formalism to determine the energy functional. Finally we show that a quantum phase transition of a different nature, but displaying similar characteristics, may arise also within a single chain just by including higher order terms in the hamiltonian.

# Detailed analysis of quantum phase transitions within the $u(2)$ algebra

## Key claim
Quantum phase transitions in the $u(2)$ algebra can arise not only from mixing two different dynamical symmetry chains ($u(1)$ and $so(2)$), but also within a single chain ($u(2) \supset u(1)$) by including higher-order Casimir operators, contradicting the common association of a phase with a single dynamical symmetry chain.

## Method
The authors diagonalize a transitional Hamiltonian that linearly interpolates between the $u(1)$ and $so(2)$ dynamical symmetry limits in the $u(1)$ basis for boson numbers up to N=20. They then apply the vector coherent state formalism with a non-standard choice of phases (taking $z_1=1$ and $z_2=iy$ purely imaginary) to derive the energy functional and locate the critical point. Finally, they analyze a Hamiltonian containing only linear and quadratic Casimir operators of $u(1)$ (with no $so(2)$ term) to demonstrate a phase transition within a single symmetry chain.

## Key numbers
- Critical point for the $u(1)$-$so(2)$ transition with $\alpha=0$: $\xi_c = (N-1)/(2N-1)$, which approaches $1/2$ in the large $N$ limit
- For N=7 bosons, the critical point sits at $\xi_c = 6/13$
- For the single-chain $u(1)$ Hamiltonian $\hat H_I = \epsilon \hat n_t/N + \alpha \hat n_t^2/N^2$, the second-order critical point occurs when $\epsilon = -\alpha$, for all N
- The ground state eigenvector retains the $n_t=0$ component as its largest component down to approximately $\xi \sim 0.35$ for N=7, even though the critical point is at $\xi_c = 6/13 \approx 0.462$

## Context
This work extends the general treatment of $u(n-1)$-$o(n)$ quantum phase transitions by Cejnar and Iachello (2007) to the simplest specific case of $u(2)$, which is the algebra underlying the schematic Lipkin model. The authors note that their results are very similar to the corresponding U(3) diagrams in that prior work. The paper also connects to the pedagogical discussion of $u(2)$ dynamical symmetries in Frank and van Isacker (2005) and to the thesis of van Roosmalen (1982). The demonstration that a phase transition can occur within a single symmetry chain by including higher-order Casimir operators suggests that similar effects may exist in more complicated algebras used in molecular and nuclear physics, such as the Interacting Boson Model ($u(6)$) and the Vibron model ($u(4)$), potentially leading to a reconsideration of several algebraic models.
