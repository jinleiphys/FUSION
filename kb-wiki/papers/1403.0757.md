# Faddeev calculations of the $\bar{K}NN$ system with chirally-motivated $\bar{K}N$ interaction. II. The $K^- pp$ quasi-bound state

> New calculations of the quasi-bound state in the $K^- pp$ system using Faddeev-type equations in AGS form with coupled $\bar{K}NN$ and $πΣN$ channels were performed. A chiral $\bar{K}N$ potential together with phenomenological models of $\bar{K}N$ interaction with one- and two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance were used. All three potentials reproduce experimental data on low-energy $K^- p$ scattering and kaonic hydrogen with the same level of accuracy. New method of calculating the subthreshold resonance position and width in a three-body system was proposed and used together with the direct search of the resonance pole. We obtained binding energy of the $K^-pp$ quasi-bound state $\sim 32$ MeV with the chirally motivated and $47 - 54$ MeV with the phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potentials. The width is about $50$ MeV for the two-pole models of the interaction, while the one-pole potential gives $\sim 65$ MeV width. The question of using an energy dependent potential in few-body calculations is discussed in detail. It is shown that ``self-consistent'' variational calculations using such a potential are unable to produce a reasonable approximation to the exact result.

## Key claim

The binding energy of the $K^- pp$ quasi-bound state depends strongly on the model of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction, with the chirally motivated potential yielding a binding energy of about 32 MeV, while phenomenological potentials give 47–54 MeV; the width is about 50 MeV for two-pole models and about 65 MeV for the one-pole potential.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev-type equations in Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form for the three-body system with coupled $\bar{K}NN$ and $\pi \Sigma N$ channels, using separable $s$-wave isospin-dependent two-body potentials. Two methods were used to locate the complex pole of the quasi-bound state: a direct search with contour rotation into the complex momentum plane, and a new method fitting a Breit-Wigner curve to the function $1/|{\rm Det}(z)|^2$ calculated on the real energy axis. Three $\bar{K}N$ interaction models were employed: a chirally motivated three-channel potential and two phenomenological coupled-channel potentials with one-pole and two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy $B_{K^- pp} = 32.2$ MeV with the chirally motivated $\bar{K}N$ potential (pole position $-32.2 - i\,24.3$ MeV from $\bar{K}NN$ threshold).
- Binding energy $B_{K^- pp} = 47.4$ MeV with the two-pole phenomenological potential (pole $-47.4 - i\,24.9$ MeV).
- Binding energy $B_{K^- pp} = 53.3$ MeV with the one-pole phenomenological potential (pole $-53.3 - i\,32.4$ MeV).
- Width $\Gamma_{K^- pp} = 48.6$ MeV for the chirally motivated potential.
- Width $\Gamma_{K^- pp} = 49.8$ MeV for the two-pole phenomenological potential.
- Width $\Gamma_{K^- pp} = 64.8$ MeV for the one-pole phenomenological potential.
- The "self-consistent" real $\bar{K}N$ energies used in variational calculations were ${\rm Re}\,z_{\bar{K}N}^{DHW} = -39$ MeV and ${\rm Re}\,z_{\bar{K}N}^{BGL} = -43$ MeV; the exact pole requires ${\rm Re}\,z_{\bar{K}N} \simeq -58$ MeV with ${\rm Im}\,z_{\bar{K}N} \simeq -7$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev calculations of the $\bar{K}NN$ system (Shevchenko et al., 2007) by using improved $\bar{K}N$ potentials that reproduce low-energy $K^- p$ scattering and kaonic hydrogen data from the SIDDHARTA collaboration. The results contradict the variational calculations of Dote, Hyodo, and Weise (2009) and Barnea, Gal, and Liverts (2012), which used a "self-consistent" method to handle the energy-dependent chiral $\bar{K}N$ potential; the present exact Faddeev treatment shows that this method cannot produce a reasonable approximation to the exact result. The work also differs from the Faddeev calculations of Ikeda and Sato (2009) by using a more accurate chiral potential and finding no second pole in the $K^- pp$ system. The new $1/|{\rm Det}(z)|^2$ method for locating subthreshold resonances is introduced and validated.
