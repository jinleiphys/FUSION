# Importance of multicranked configuration mixing for angular-momentum-projection calculations: Study of superdeformed rotational bands in $^{152}$Dy and $^{194}$Hg

> Recently we have investigated an effective method of multicranked configuration-mixing for angular-momentum-projection calculation, where several cranked mean-field states are coupled after projection: The basic idea was originally proposed by Peierls and Thouless more than fifty years ago. With this method a good description of the rotational band has been achieved in a fully microscopic manner. In the present work, we apply the method to the high-spin superdeformed band, for which long rotational sequence is observed, and study how the good description is obtained for the rotational spectrum as well as the $\Jonem$ and $\Jtwom$ moments of inertia as functions of angular momentum. The Gogny D1S force is employed as an effective interaction, and the yrast superdeformed bands in $^{152}$Dy and $^{194}$Hg are taken as typical examples in the $A\approx 150$ and $A\approx 190$ regions, respectively. The effect of pairing correlations is examined by the variation after particle-number projection approach to understand the different behaviors of $\Jtwom$ moments of inertia observed in these two nuclei. The particle-number projection on top of the angular-momentum projection has been performed for the first time with the multicranked configuration-mixing.

## Key claim

The multicranked configuration-mixing method, where several selfconsistently cranked mean-field states are coupled after angular-momentum projection, is crucial for obtaining correct magnitudes of both dynamic and kinematic moments of inertia for superdeformed rotational bands; projection from a single cranked mean-field state gives Yoccoz inertia values that are about 20-25% smaller than the final configuration-mixing result, which instead agrees with the Thouless-Valatin inertia from the cranked mean-field approximation.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum-projected multicranked configuration-mixing calculations using the Gogny D1S effective interaction with no adjustable parameters, generating four cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) or cranked variation-after-number-projection (VANP) mean-field states at rotational frequencies of 0.30, 0.45, 0.60, and 0.75 MeV for each nucleus. The Hill-Wheeler equation was solved to obtain the configuration-mixing amplitudes, and for the VANP calculations, particle-number projection was performed on top of the angular-momentum projection for the first time in this type of multicranked configuration-mixing. The yrast superdeformed bands in 152Dy and 194Hg were studied as representative examples in the A≈150 and A≈190 mass regions.

## Key numbers

- For 152Dy: deformation parameter β2 = 0.715 at zero rotational frequency from Gogny HFB calculation; β2 = 0.713 at ħωrot = 0.30 MeV and β2 = 0.696 at ħωrot = 0.75 MeV
- For 152Dy: maximum angular momentum Imax = 62 and Kmax = 22 used in projection calculations
- For 194Hg: maximum angular momentum Imax = 52 and Kmax = 22 used in projection calculations
- Number of integration-mesh points for Euler angles: Nβ = 130, Nα = Nγ = 60
- Number of integration-mesh points for gauge angle in particle-number projection: Nφ = 7
- Number of cranked mean-field states used in configuration-mixing: nmax = 4
- For 152Dy: the calculated kinematic moment of inertia from configuration-mixing overestimates the experimental value by about 10%
- For 152Dy: the absolute energy of the projected configuration-mixing spectrum using VANP states is about 3.7 MeV smaller at I≈0 compared to the HFB-based calculation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous angular-momentum-projected multicranked configuration-mixing method (Ushitani, Tagami, and Shimizu, 2015, 2016), which was applied to ground-state rotational bands in rare earth nuclei, to the high-spin superdeformed bands in 152Dy and 194Hg. The method implements the original idea of Peierls and Thouless (1962) for superposing wave functions with different velocities to obtain proper mass parameters. The results enable fully microscopic description of superdeformed rotational bands without adjustable parameters, and the comparison between the HFB and VANP treatments clarifies the role of pairing fluctuations in the different behaviors of dynamic moments of inertia observed in the A≈150 and A≈190 mass regions.
