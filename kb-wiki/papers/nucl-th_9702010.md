# Spectral Distribution Studies of fp shell nuclei with modified Kuo--Brown Interaction

> The structure of nuclei in the lower half of fp shell is investigated by the spectral distribution method using the modified Kuo-Brown interaction. This interaction recently showed success in reproducing observed properties through detailed shell model studies. Spectral distribution studies avoid explicit diagonalization and hold promise for applications to astrophysics.

# Spectral Distribution Studies of fp shell nuclei with modified Kuo–Brown Interaction

## Key claim

Spectral distribution theory using the modified Kuo–Brown (KB3) interaction, with corrections for non-zero skewness and excess (third and fourth moments of the Hamiltonian), yields binding energies for lower fp shell nuclei that agree substantially better with experiment than earlier spectral distribution methods, achieving an average deviation of 0.15 MeV and an RMS deviation of 1.49 MeV.

## Method

The authors applied spectral distribution theory to nuclei in the lower half of the fp shell (valence particles in the $f_{7/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, and $p_{1/2}$ orbits) using the KB3 interaction. They computed smoothed densities of states in configuration-isospin spaces, incorporating corrections for skewness ($\gamma_1$) and excess ($\gamma_2$) via the Cornish–Fisher expansion, which had not been done previously for fp shell nuclei. Ground state energies were obtained by the Ratcliff inversion procedure, and orbit occupation probabilities were calculated from the energy-dependent configuration-isospin densities.

## Key numbers

- Average deviation of corrected binding energies from experimental values: 0.15 MeV; RMS deviation: 1.49 MeV (for nuclei in the lower half of the fp shell).
- RMS deviations from earlier spectral distribution methods using other interactions: 5.59 MeV (KB), 2.19 MeV (MHW2), 5.79 MeV (KB10), 8.39 MeV (bare), and 3.60 MeV (MWH) as reported by Kota and Potbhare.
- Centroids of KB3 and MHW2 interactions differ by up to 6 MeV for particle numbers ranging from 6 to 16.
- The width of MHW2 is consistently smaller than KB3 by a few percent.
- The phenomenological correction term for $\gamma_2$ in (m,T) space was parameterized as $\gamma_2(n,T) = 0.04n - 0.04 T^2$.

## Context

This work extends spectral distribution studies from the sd shell (where similar methods succeeded with the universal-sd interaction) to the fp shell, using the KB3 interaction that had recently shown success in detailed shell model calculations for A=48 nuclei (Poves and Zuker, 1981) and other heavier nuclei. Earlier spectral distribution studies in the fp shell by Haq and Parikh (using the MHW2 interaction with excited state corrections) and by Kota and Potbhare (using various interactions with excited state corrections and phenomenological terms) gave substantially larger RMS deviations. The incorporation of skewness and excess corrections, previously shown to be important in the sd shell, enables improved predictions for binding energies and excitation spectra, and the calculated orbit occupation probabilities are relevant for estimating Gamow–Teller sum rule strengths needed for astrophysical applications such as pre-supernova stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis.
