# Asymmetric pairing of realistic mass quarks and color neutrality in the Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model of QCD

> We investigate the effects of realistic quark masses and local color neutrality on quark pairing in the three-flavor Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. While prior studies have indicated the presence of light flavor quark (2SC) or symmetric color-flavor-locked (CFL) pairing at low temperatures, we find that in the absence of a local color neutrality constraint the inclusion of the Polyakov loop gives rise to phases in which all quark colors and flavors pair, but with unequal magnitudes. We study this asymmetric color-flavor-locked (ACFL) phase, which can exist even for equal mass quarks, identifying its location in the phase diagram, the order of the associated phase transitions, and its symmetry breaking pattern, which proves to be the intersection of the symmetry groups of the 2SC and CFL phases. We also investigate the effects of the strange quark mass on this new phase and the QCD phase diagram generally. Finally, we analyze the effect of a local color neutrality constraint on these phases of asymmetric pairing. We observe that for massless quarks the neutrality constraint renders the 2SC phase energetically unfavorable, eliminating it at low temperatures, and giving rise to the previously proposed low temperature critical point, with associated continuity between the hadronic and ACFL phases. For realistic strange quark masses, however, the neutrality constraint shrinks the 2SC region of the phase diagram, but does not eliminate it, at T=0.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in the three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, the inclusion of the Polyakov loop (confinement) gives rise to an asymmetric color-flavor-locked (ACFL) phase with unequal pairing amplitudes for different quark colors and flavors, even for equal mass quarks, but only in the absence of a local color neutrality constraint; when color neutrality is imposed, the 2SC phase is eliminated at low temperatures for massless quarks but only shrunk for realistic strange quark masses.

## Method

The authors employ the three-flavor PNJL model with a Polyakov loop potential, including four-quark and six-quark interactions that incorporate the QCD axial anomaly. They solve the mean-field thermodynamic potential by minimizing with respect to scalar chiral condensates, diquark condensates (allowing distinct ud, us, and ds pairing amplitudes), and the Polyakov loop field, then construct phase diagrams for both massless and massive quarks with various strange quark masses and axial anomaly coupling strengths. To impose local color neutrality, they introduce a color chemical potential μ₈ chosen to enforce zero color density n₈ = 0.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement transition at μ = 0 is matched to lattice QCD value T_QGP_c = 176 MeV, yielding T₀ values ranging from 204 to 210 MeV across parameter sets.
- For massless quarks in the PNJL model, the chiral transition temperature increases from 151 MeV (NJL) to 193 MeV (PNJL) due to the Polyakov loop.
- The ACFL-2SC phase transition is first-order when T_c ≲ 50 MeV and second-order when T_c ≳ 50 MeV.
- For massless quarks with K′ = 4.2K, the CFL phase at μ = 500 MeV persists roughly constant for T ≲ 30 MeV, then falls as d ∼ √(T_c − T) for 30 MeV ≲ T < 71 MeV, vanishing as d ∼ (T_c − T) for 71 MeV < T < 73 MeV.
- For realistic strange quark masses (m_s = 80 MeV) with K′ = 4.2K, the BEC-BCS critical point drops below the T axis, yielding a smooth BEC-BCS crossover.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quark pairing in the PNJL model by Powell and Basler (2012) and Basler and Buballa (2010), which considered only CFL and 2SC phases, by allowing distinct ud, us, and ds pairing amplitudes and investigating the resulting ACFL phase. The paper also builds on Abuki et al. (2010) by incorporating the Polyakov loop and studying the effects of local color neutrality, which was previously imposed only in NJL models by Iida (2005), Steiner (2002), and Buballa (2005). The results enable future work on the QCD phase diagram at intermediate chemical potential and the possibility of quark-hadron continuity in neutron star matter.
