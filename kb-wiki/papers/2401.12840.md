# Understanding Gravitational Form Factors with the Weizsäcker-Williams Method

> Understanding the internal structure of nucleons and nuclei has been a topic of enduring interest in high-energy physics. Gravitational form factors (GFFs) provide an important portal for us to probe the energy-momentum/mass distribution of nucleons and nuclei. This letter presents the study of the photon and gluon momentum GFFs, also known as the A-GFFs, of relativistic hadrons using the Weizsäcker-Williams method. To begin, we express the photon A-GFFs in terms of charge form factors and discuss the corresponding photon radius. Furthermore, an integral relation between the gluon A-GFF and the Laplacian of dipole scattering amplitude is derived in the small-$x$ framework, and it allows us to unravel the gluon energy momentum distribution inside hadrons through measurements at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider. In addition, we generalize the analysis to study the A-GFF of nuclei and propose employing the nuclear gluon mean square radius, together with the charge distribution, to constrain the neutron distribution for large nuclei. This work provides an interesting perspective into the fundamental structure of high-energy hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors derive an integral relation between the gluon A-GFF and the Laplacian of the dipole scattering amplitude within the small-x framework, but this relation is obtained under the assumption that the dipole-type and WW-type gluon GTMDs reduce to the same gluon generalized parton distribution after integrating out transverse momentum, and the result is leading-order in the coupling and valid only at zero skewness.

## Method

The authors apply the Weizsäcker-Williams (equivalent photon) method to compute the photon A-GFF by expressing it in terms of charge form factors through QED perturbation theory at leading order in alpha, using the infinite momentum frame and the small-x limit where photon virtualities approach zero. For the gluon case, they relate the gluon A-GFF to the unpolarized gluon generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) in the color-glass-condensate formalism, and derive an expression connecting the gluon A-GFF to the Laplacian of the color-dipole scattering amplitude with respect to dipole size at zero dipole separation.

## Key numbers

- Photon A-GFF for a pointlike charge yields an asymptotic small-t behavior: A_gamma(t) = (alpha/pi)[U.V. + (t/m^2)((3/16)(m pi^2/sqrt(-t)) - 1/3) + ...], where the non-analytic term proportional to sqrt(-t) in the denominator causes an infinite photon momentum radius.
- For a charge-neutral dipole with separation R_perp, the long-range contribution to the photon radius gives 4 dA_gamma(t)/dt|_{t=0} approx = (alpha R_perp^2)/8, which is finite.
- In the IP-Sat model with Gaussian dipole scattering amplitude S_x(b_perp, r_perp) = exp[-(r_perp^2/4) Q_s^2(x,b_perp)], the gluon A-GFF becomes A_g(t) = A_g(0) integral d^2b_perp e^{-i Delta_perp dot b_perp} T(b_perp) with A_g(0) = integral_0^1 dx x g(x, mu^2), and the asymptotic gluon momentum fraction is (4C_F)/(4C_F + n_f) for quark flavor number n_f.

## Context

This work extends the Weizsäcker-Williams method, previously successful in describing soft photon radiation in ultraperipheral collisions (Fermi 1924, von Weizsäcker 1934, Williams 1935) and small-x gluon distributions in the color-glass-condensate formalism (McLerran 1993, Kovchegov 1996), to the study of gravitational form factors. The results connect gluon GFFs to the dipole scattering amplitude that governs small-x evolution (Balitsky 1995, Jalilian-Marian 1997, Kovchegov 1999), and enable extraction of gluon energy-momentum distributions and the nuclear gluon mean square radius from measurements at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider, as well as constraining neutron distributions in large nuclei when combined with charge distribution data.
