# Large quadrupole moment in $^{20}$Ne:Localization and tensor-force effects

> Using the Beyond-Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with various Skyrme-type $NN$ effective interactions, we explore the large spectroscopic quadrupole moment $Q_s$ of the first excited $2_{1}^{+}$ state in $^{20}$Ne. Our calculated $Q_s(2_{1}^{+}) \approx -0.20~e\textrm{b}$ aligns closely with the experimental value of $-0.22(2)~e\textrm{b}$. Consistent with other theoretical methods, our results confirm the presence of an $α$ cluster structure in this state. We also investigate the robustness of the $α$ cluster against tensor-force, demonstrating that incorporating tensor components significantly enhances $Q_s(2_{1}^{+})$ and strengthens the cluster structure along the symmetrical axis.

## Key claim

The Beyond-Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach reproduces the large spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the first excited 2₁⁺ state in ²⁰Ne, yielding Q_s ≈ -0.20 e·b compared to the experimental -0.22(2) e·b, but only when tensor-force components are included does the calculation fully capture the enhanced α-clustering structure along the symmetry axis.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum-projected generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations using axially symmetric intrinsic wave functions with the quadrupole deformation parameter β as the generator coordinate, spanning -1.5 to 3.0 with 76 basis functions. They employed multiple Skyrme-type energy density functionals (SLy4, SIII, SKM′, SLy5, UDF0, SAMi, SGII) and systematically compared results from three categories of tensor-force treatments: functionals without explicit tensor terms, those with perturbative tensor inclusion (SLy5+T, SIII+T), and fully fitted tensor interactions (TIJ family with 36 parameter sets, Skxta, Skxtb, SAMi-T). Localization functions were extended from the mean-field framework to beyond-mean-field states to quantify α-clustering.

## Key numbers

- Calculated Q_s(2₁⁺) ≈ -0.20 e·b, compared to experimental value of -0.22(2) e·b
- SAMi interaction reproduces the experimental value exactly: Q_s(2₁⁺) = -0.22 e·b
- TIJ parameter sets yield Q_s(2₁⁺) values ranging from -0.202 e·b (T11) to -0.221 e·b (T66)
- SAMi+T gives Q_s(2₁⁺) = -0.223 e·b, marginally larger than SAMi at -0.220 e·b
- Localization functions for both 0₁⁺ and 2₁⁺ states reach a maximum value of approximately 0.95, indicating pronounced α clustering
- Pairing force strength set to V₀ = -410 MeV·fm³ for both protons and neutrons

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic beyond-mean-field approach to study the long-standing challenge of reproducing the large quadrupole moment in ²⁰Ne, which previous models including the shell model (Spear 1981), the rotational model of Bohr and Mottelson, relativistic MR-EDF (Zhou 2016), and ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations (Hergert 2016, Stroberg 2019) have systematically underestimated. The results confirm the α-cluster structure found in the relativistic MR-EDF model (Mehl 2024) and demonstrate that tensor-force effects, previously shown to influence nuclear deformation (Sagawa 2014), are essential for enhancing both the quadrupole moment and the directional clustering along the z axis. This enables future systematic studies of tensor-force effects on clustering in octupole-deformed nuclei.
