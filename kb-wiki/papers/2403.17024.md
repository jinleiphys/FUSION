# Dark matter effects on the properties of neutron stars: compactness and tidal deformability

> We systematically study the observable properties of dark-matter admixed neutron stars, employing a realistic nuclear EOS in combination with self-interacting fermionic dark matter respecting constraints on the self-interaction cross section. Deviations from universal relations valid for nucleonic neutron stars are analyzed over the whole parameter space of the model and unequivocal signals for the presence of dark matter in neutron stars are identified.

## Key claim

Deviations from universal relations between neutron star compactness and tidal deformability can serve as unequivocal signals for the presence of dark matter in neutron stars, but only if the dark matter fraction is not tiny; the effect depends strongly on the dark matter particle mass and fraction, with different signatures for DM-core versus DM-halo configurations.

## Method

The authors solve two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for a system combining a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock nuclear equation of state (Argonne V18 potential with three-body forces) and a self-interacting fermionic dark matter equation of state with a repulsive Yukawa potential. The dark matter self-interaction cross section is fixed to sigma/mu = 1 cm^2/g, leaving the dark matter particle mass mu as the free parameter, and the dark matter fraction f = M_D/M is varied from 0 to 1. The tidal deformability and Love numbers are computed in the two-fluid formalism.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass of pure neutron star (V18 EOS): 2.36 solar masses
* Pure neutron star radius at 1.4 solar masses: 12.28 km
* Pure neutron star tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses: 430
* Pure neutron star Love number k_2 at 1.4 solar masses: 0.086
* For mu = 1 GeV, maximum mass of pure dark star: 3.11 solar masses
* For mu < 1.11 GeV, maximum DNS mass can exceed the pure NS maximum mass of 2.36 solar masses
* GW170817 constraint on tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses: 70-580
* Dark matter self-interaction cross section constraint: sigma/mu = 1 cm^2/g = 4560/GeV^3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Liu 2023) of dark-matter admixed neutron star optical radii and tidal deformability over the full parameter space of a self-interacting fermionic dark matter model. It builds on prior work establishing universal I-Love-Q relations for nucleonic neutron stars (Yagi 2013, 2017; Godzieba 2021) and on studies of dark matter effects on tidal deformability (Maselli 2017; Routaray 2023; Thakur 2023). The results enable identification of dark matter signatures in future gravitational wave events by detecting violations of the universal relation between compactness and tidal deformability.
