# Hard collinear gluon radiation and multiple scattering in a medium

> The energy loss of hard jets produced in the Deep-Inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus is considered in the collinear limit. In particular, the single gluon emission cross section due to multiple scattering in the medium is calculated. Calculations are carried out in the higher-twist scheme, which is extended to include contributions from multiple transverse scatterings on both the produced quark and the radiated gluon. The leading length enhanced parts of these power suppressed contributions are resummed. Various interferences between such diagrams lead to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. We resum the corrections from an arbitrary number of scatterings and isolate the leading contributions which are suppressed by one extra power of the hard scale $Q^{2}$. All powers of the emitted gluon forward momentum fraction $y$ are retained. We compare our results with the previous calculation of single scattering per emission in the higher-twist scheme as well as with multiple scattering resummations in other schemes. It is found that the leading ($1/Q^2$) contribution to the double differential gluon production cross section, in this approach, is equivalent to that obtained from the single scattering calculation once the transverse momentum of the final quark is integrated out. We comment on the generalization of this formalism to Monte-Carlo routines.

## Key claim

The paper extends the higher-twist (HT) scheme for jet energy loss in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) to include multiple coherent scatterings per emitted gluon, not just the single scattering per emission assumed in prior HT calculations. The leading \(1/Q^2\) contribution to the double differential gluon production cross section from this multiple-scattering resummation is shown to be equivalent to the single-scattering result once the transverse momentum of the final quark is integrated out.

## Method

The author calculates the single gluon emission cross section from a hard quark produced in DIS off a large nucleus, working in the collinear limit within the higher-twist framework. The calculation extends the HT scheme by including contributions from multiple transverse scatterings on both the produced quark and the radiated gluon, resumming the leading length-enhanced power-suppressed contributions from an arbitrary number of scatterings. The analysis uses light-cone gauge (\(A^- = 0\)), retains all powers of the emitted gluon forward momentum fraction \(y\), and isolates the leading contributions suppressed by one extra power of the hard scale \(Q^2\).

## Key numbers

- The formation time of a radiated gluon is estimated as \(\tau_f \sim \nu / [2(\Delta Q)^2] \sim 1\ \text{fm}\) for a typical HERMES event with \(Q^2 \sim 10\ \text{GeV}^2\), forward energy \(\nu \sim 30\ \text{GeV}\), and a momentum drop \(\Delta Q \sim 2\ \text{GeV}\) with momentum fraction \(1/2\).
- The Bjorken variable \(x_B\) is defined as \(x_B = Q^2 / (2 p^+ q^-)\), and the new momentum fraction \(x_L = l_\perp^2 / [2 p^+ q^- y (1-y)]\) scales as \(\sim \lambda^2\) in the collinear regime where \(l_\perp \sim \lambda Q\) and \(y, (1-y) \lesssim 1\).
- The leading-twist differential hadronic tensor for single gluon emission contains the splitting function \(P_{q \to q g}(y) = (2 - 2y + y^2)/y\) and a factor \(1/l_\perp^2\).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist approach of Wang (2002) and Majumder (2007) by including multiple coherent scatterings per gluon emission, going beyond the single-scattering-per-emission assumption of the original HT scheme. It complements the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV), Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY), and Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) formalisms, which already incorporate multiple scatterings, and provides a path toward unifying these approaches. The results enable future inclusion of multiple emissions via iteration of the single-gluon emission kernel and eventual numerical comparison with experimental data from HERMES and RHIC.
