# Nearly perfect fluid in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> In the Israel-Stewart's theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, we have analysed the STAR data on $ϕ$ meson production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV. From a simultaneous fit to $ϕ$ mesons multiplicity, mean $p_T$ and integrated $v_2$, we obtain a phenomenological estimate of QGP viscosity, $η/s =0.07 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.14$, the first error is due to the experimental uncertainty in STAR measurements, the second reflects the uncertainties in initial and final conditions of the fluid.

## Key claim

From a simultaneous fit to STAR data on φ meson multiplicity, mean pT, and integrated v2 in Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV using Israel-Stewart dissipative hydrodynamics, the shear viscosity to entropy ratio of the quark-gluon plasma is estimated as η/s = 0.07 ± 0.03 ± 0.14, where the first error is from experimental uncertainty and the second reflects uncertainties in initial and final conditions of the fluid; the author notes this estimate should be considered an upper bound because bulk viscosity was neglected.

## Method

The space-time evolution of the QGP fluid was obtained by solving Israel-Stewart's second-order dissipative hydrodynamics (conservation equations plus relaxation equation for the shear stress tensor) assuming boost-invariance, using the code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA. Initial conditions assumed thermalization at τi=0.6 fm with zero initial fluid velocity, a Glauber-model energy density profile (75% participants, 25% binary collisions), and freeze-out at TF=150 MeV; the equation of state combined lattice QCD simulations with a hadronic resonance gas. The STAR data on φ meson multiplicity, mean pT, and integrated v2 across centrality bins were fitted simultaneously by varying the initial energy density and η/s, with χ2 minimization used to determine the best-fit viscosity.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit shear viscosity to entropy ratio: η/s = 0.07 ± 0.03 (experimental uncertainty) ± 0.14 (systematic uncertainty from initial/final conditions)
- For η/s=0.08, the fitted initial central energy density was εi = 29.1 ± 3.6 GeV/fm3 and initial temperature Ti = 359.1 ± 11.5 MeV in b=0 fm Au+Au collisions
- For η/s=0.0 (ideal fluid), the fitted initial central energy density was εi = 35.5 ± 5.0 GeV/fm3 and Ti = 377.0 ± 13.7 MeV
- The combined χ2/N for the three data sets (dN/dy + pT + v2) was 7.76 for η/s=0.08, compared to 9.77 for ideal hydrodynamics
- Systematic uncertainty from initial time (τi=0.6 ± 0.4 fm) was estimated as ~90% in η/s; from freeze-out temperature (TF=150 ± 10 MeV) as ~100%; from hard scattering fraction (0-95%) as ~55%; from initial transverse velocity as ~100%

## Context

This work extends prior estimates of QGP viscosity from experimental data, including Gavin and Abdel-Aziz (2006) who estimated η/s=0.08-0.30 from transverse momentum fluctuations, Drescher et al. (2007) who found η/s=0.11-0.19 from elliptic flow scaling, and Lacey et al. (2006) who obtained η/s ~0.09 ± 0.015 from kinetic energy scaling. The analysis uses φ mesons as a probe because they are hidden strange particles unaffected by resonance decays, with mass and width not modified in medium, making them ideal for investigating medium properties. The result enables more precise constraints on QGP transport coefficients and can be compared to the ADS/CFT lower bound of η/s ≥ 1/4π and lattice QCD estimates such as Meyer (2007) who found η/s=0.134(33) at T=1.165Tc.
