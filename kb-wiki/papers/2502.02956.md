# Baryon-Electric Charge Correlations and Chemical Potentials as Probes of Magnetized QCD

> We present the first lattice QCD results of quadratic fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges in (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD in the presence of a background magnetic field. The simulations were performed using the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks with physical pion mass $m_π$ = 135 MeV on $N_τ=8$ and 12 lattices. We find that the correlation between net baryon number and electric charge, denoted as $χ^{\rm BQ}_{11} $, can serve as a magnetometer of QCD. At pseudocritical temperatures ($T_{pc}$) the $χ^{\rm BQ}_{11}$ starts to increase rapidly with magnetic field strength $eB \gtrsim 2M^2_π$ and by a factor 2 at $eB\simeq 8 M^2_π$. By comparing with the hadron resonance gas model, we find that the $eB$ dependence of $χ^{\rm BQ}_{11}$ is mainly due to the doubly charged $Δ$(1232) baryon. Although the doubly charged $Δ$(1232) could not be detected experimentally, its decay products, protons and pions, retain the $eB$ dependence of $Δ$(1232)'s contribution to $χ^{\rm BQ}_{11}$. Furthermore, the ratio of electric charge chemical potential to baryon chemical potential, $μ_{\rm Q}/μ_{\rm B}$, shows significant dependence on the magnetic field strength and varies with the ratio of electric charge to baryon number in the colliding nuclei in heavy ion collisions. These results provide baselines for effective theory and model studies, and both $χ^{\rm BQ}_{11}$ and $μ_{\rm Q}/μ_{\rm B}$ could be useful probes for the detection of magnetic fields in relativistic heavy ion collision experiments as compared with corresponding results from the hadron resonance gas model.

## Key claim

The correlation between net baryon number and electric charge, χBQ₁₁, can serve as a magnetometer of QCD, but this claim is conditional: the enhancement only becomes rapid at magnetic field strengths eB ≳ 2M²π, and the effect is primarily due to the doubly charged Δ(1232) baryon, which cannot be detected directly but whose decay products (protons and pions) retain the magnetic field dependence.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks with physical pion mass mπ = 135 MeV on Nτ = 8 and 12 lattices, with additional checks on Nτ = 16. A constant background magnetic field was introduced along the z-direction via U(1) gauge links in the Landau gauge. Continuum estimates were obtained by linear extrapolation in 1/Nτ², and results were compared with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to identify which hadronic states drive the magnetic field dependence.

## Key numbers

- χBQ₁₁ increases by a factor of 2 at eB ≃ 8 M²π relative to its value at vanishing magnetic field, at pseudocritical temperatures
- χBQ₁₁ reaches 2.4 times its zero-field value at eB ≃ 8 M²π at T = 145 MeV
- The magnetic field strengths studied range up to eB = 8 M²π, with six distinct values implemented
- The pseudocritical temperature Tpc is approximately 156 MeV
- For the Ruthenium collision system (⁹⁶₄₄Ru), which has only 10% larger charge-to-mass ratio than Zirconium (⁹⁶₄₀Zr), the ratio of q₁ exceeds that for Zirconium by 50% at eB ∼ 8 M²π
- The ratio q₃/q₁ remains within approximately 5% on Nτ = 8 lattices at Tpc for nQ/nB = 0.4

## Context

This work provides the first lattice QCD results for quadratic fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges in a background magnetic field with physical pion mass, extending prior lattice studies by Ding (2021) that used unphysical pion mass (Mπ ≃ 220 MeV) and by Borsanyi (2023) that used Nf = 2+1+1 QCD on Nτ = 8 lattices. The results establish model-independent benchmarks for effective theories such as the hadron resonance gas model and the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and enable experimental searches for magnetic field effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions through proxies constructed from stable hadrons (protons, pions, kaons) that retain the magnetic field dependence of χBQ₁₁.
