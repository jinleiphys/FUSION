# Boson Realization of the su(3)-Algebra. III

> Following the basic idea developed in (I), the su(3)-model presented by Elliott is investigated. A method for constructing the orthogonal set, in which the angular momenta are good quantum numbers, is discussed without using the angular momentum projection.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for constructing an orthogonal set of eigenstates for the Elliott su(3)-model in which the angular momenta are good quantum numbers, without using the angular momentum projection method. The authors acknowledge that the excited state generating operators \( \hat{C}_1^* \) and \( \hat{C}_2^* \) do not form spherical tensors with respect to the angular momentum operators, requiring a special device to obtain the orthogonal set.

## Method

The authors use a Schwinger boson representation of the su(3)-algebra in terms of six kinds of boson operators \((\hat{a}_{\pm,0}, \hat{b}_{\pm,0})\) and their conjugates, along with an associated su(1,1)-algebra. They construct an intrinsic state \(\ket{T,R}\) that satisfies minimum weight conditions for both the su(3) and su(1,1) algebras. Excited states are built by applying operators \(\mib{C}_1^*\) and \(\mib{C}_2^*\) to the intrinsic state, and an orthogonal set is obtained by diagonalizing the operator \(\mib{Q}_0\) in the space spanned by states projected with the operator \(\wtilde{P}\) that enforces the su(1,1) minimum weight condition.

## Key numbers

* The intrinsic state \(\ket{T,R}\) has eigenvalues \(L^0 = 2(T-3/2)\) for \(\hat{L}_0\) and \(Q^0 = 2R\) for \(\hat{Q}_0\), where \(T = 3/2, 2, 5/2, 3, \dots\) and \(-(T-3/2) \leq R \leq (T-3/2)\).
* The eigenvalue of the su(3) Casimir operator \(\hat{\Gamma}_{su(3)}\) for the intrinsic state is \(2[(T-3/2)(T-3/2+2) + (1/3)R^2]\).
* The eigenvalue of the su(1,1) Casimir operator \(\hat{\Gamma}_{su(1,1)}\) for the intrinsic state is \(2T(T-1)\).
* The angular momentum quantum number \(L\) runs in the range \(|Q^0| \leq L \leq L^0\), where \(L^0 = l_a + l_b\) and \(Q^0 = l_a - l_b\) with \(l_a = T-3/2+R\) and \(l_b = T-3/2-R\).

## Context

This work extends the boson realization of the su(3)-algebra developed in Parts I and II of the series, which were based on the Schwinger and Holstein-Primakoff representations. It specifically addresses the Elliott su(3)-model from 1958, which is fundamental in nuclear structure theory for describing rotational spectra and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. The method enables the construction of eigenstates with good angular momentum quantum numbers without the detour of angular momentum projection, providing a more direct algebraic approach for studying collective motion in nuclei.
