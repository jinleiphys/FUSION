# Refined determination of the weak mixing angle at low energy

> The weak mixing angle is a fundamental parameter of the electroweak theory of the standard model whose measurement in the low-energy regime is still not precisely determined. Different probes are sensitive to its value, among which atomic parity violation, coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering and parity-violating electron scattering on different nuclei. In this work, we attempt for the first time to combine all these various determinations by performing a global fit that also keeps into account the unavoidable dependence on the experimentally poorly known neutron distribution radius of the nuclei employed, for which a new measurement using proton-cesium elastic scattering became available. By using all present direct determinations of the neutron distribution radius of cesium we find $\sin^2\!\vartheta_{W} =0.2396^{+0.0020}_{-0.0019}$, which should supersede the previous value determined from atomic parity violation on cesium. When including electroweak only, but also indirect, determinations of the neutron distribution radius of cesium the uncertainty reduces to 0.0017 maintaining the same central value, showing an excellent agreement independently of the method used.

## Key claim

By performing a global fit that combines atomic parity violation, coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, and parity-violating electron scattering data while accounting for the neutron distribution radius of the target nuclei, the authors obtain a refined value of the weak mixing angle at low energy that supersedes the previous determination from atomic parity violation on cesium alone, but the result depends on which theoretical parity-nonconserving amplitude is used and on whether strong-probe or electroweak-only determinations of the neutron radius are employed.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit of the weak mixing angle and the neutron distribution radius of cesium and lead nuclei using data from atomic parity violation on cesium (APV(Cs)), coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering on cesium iodide from the COHERENT experiment (COH), parity-violating electron scattering on lead from PREX-II (PVES), atomic parity violation on lead (APV(Pb)), and a new direct measurement of the cesium neutron skin from proton-cesium elastic scattering at the CSRe facility. They also performed an electroweak-only combination that excludes strong-probe neutron radius measurements, using a model-independent linear correlation between the lead and cesium neutron skins to convert between nuclei.

## Key numbers

*   Weak mixing angle from APV(Cs) + COH + CSRe fit: sin^2 theta_W = 0.2396^{+0.0020}_{-0.0019}
*   Weak mixing angle from electroweak-only combined fit (APV(Cs) + COH + PREX-II + APV(Pb)): sin^2 theta_W = 0.2396 +/- 0.0017
*   Weak mixing angle from global fit including all data and non-EW neutron radius measurements on lead: sin^2 theta_W = 0.2387 +/- 0.0016
*   Cesium neutron radius from APV(Cs) + COH + CSRe fit: R_n(^133Cs) = 5.04 +/- 0.19 fm
*   Cesium neutron radius from electroweak-only combined fit: R_n(^133Cs) = 5.04 +/- 0.06 fm
*   Direct measurement of cesium neutron skin from CSRe: 0.12 +/- 0.21 fm
*   Average of non-electroweak measurements of the lead neutron skin: Delta R_np(Pb) = 0.16 +/- 0.01 fm
*   Electroweak determination of lead neutron skin from APV(Pb)+PREX-II: Delta R_np(Pb) = 0.262 +/- 0.136 fm
*   Standard Model prediction at zero momentum transfer: sin^2 theta_W^SM (Q=0) = 0.23863 +/- 0.00005

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to determine the weak mixing angle at low energy, which had been limited by the imprecise knowledge of neutron distribution radii in target nuclei. The previous most precise low-energy determination came from atomic parity violation on cesium (Wood 1997, Dzuba 2012) giving sin^2 theta_W = 0.2367 +/- 0.0018, which is slightly below the Standard Model prediction. The authors build on the method developed by Cadeddu (2018) for combining APV and CE nu NS data, and incorporate a new direct measurement of the cesium neutron skin from proton-cesium scattering at CSRe (Huang 2024). The results enable a more reliable test of the Standard Model running of the weak mixing angle at low energies and provide a baseline for searches for new physics such as light Z' bosons.
