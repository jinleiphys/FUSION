# Hadron-quark phase transition in a hadronic and Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio models perspective

> In this work we study the hadron-quark phase transition matching relativistic hadrodynamical mean-field models (in the hadronic phase) with the more updated versions of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (on the quark side). Systematic comparisons are performed showing that the predicted hadronic phases of the matching named as RMF-PNJL, are larger than the confined phase obtained exclusively by the Polyakov quark models. This important result is due to the effect of the nuclear force that causes more resistance of hadronic matter to isothermal compressions. For sake of comparison, we also obtain the matchings of the hadronic models with the MIT bag model, named as RMF-MIT, showing that it presents always larger hadron regions, while shows smaller mixed phases than that obtained from the RMF-PNJL ones. Thus, studies of the confinement transition in nuclear matter, done only with quark models, still need nuclear degrees of freedom to be more reliable in the whole $T\timesμ$ phase diagram.

## Key claim
The hadronic phase predicted by matching relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models (RMF-PNJL) is larger than the confined phase obtained exclusively by the PNJL quark models, because the nuclear force causes hadronic matter to resist isothermal compression more strongly. This indicates that studies of the confinement transition done only with quark models still need nuclear degrees of freedom to be more reliable across the full temperature-chemical potential phase diagram.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic matching of ten different relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic models (including WAL, NL3, and NLZ2) with four different parametrizations of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) quark model (RTW05, RRW06, FUKU08, and DS10) using the Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium. For comparison, they also matched the same RMF models with the MIT bag model (RMF-MIT). The hadron-quark phase transition boundaries were then computed and compared across the different model combinations.

## Key numbers
- The RMF models used cover a very large range of nuclear matter incompressibility, from K = 172 MeV (NLZ2, a very soft EOS) to K = 554.4 MeV (WAL, a very hard EOS).
- The DS10 Polyakov potential parametrization predicts a critical end point (CEP) at a baryon chemical potential of mu_B = 354 MeV and a temperature of T = 167 MeV.
- The transition temperature at vanishing chemical potential, T_c(mu=0), was used to rescale the Polyakov potential parameter T_0 from 270 MeV to 190 MeV to better match lattice QCD results.
- The FUKU08 potential parameter b was changed from b = 0.03 Lambda^3 to b = 0.007 Lambda^3 to reproduce lattice results for T_c(mu=0).

## Context
This work extends the study of Gyshao (2010), which investigated the hadron-quark phase transition using only one parametrization for the PNJL and RMF models, by performing a systematic comparison across multiple parametrizations of both models. The authors show that the RMF models affect the phase transition quite small despite the large range of incompressibilities (172 < K < 554 MeV). This work enables more reliable predictions for the QCD phase diagram at high density and moderate temperatures, which can be tested by future experiments at facilities like FAIR and NICA.
