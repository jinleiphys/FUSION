# Direct mechanism in solar nuclear reactions

> A short overview of the direct reaction mechanism and the models used for the analysis of such processes is given. Nuclear reactions proceeding through the direct mechanism and involved in solar hydrogen burning are discussed. The significance of these nuclear reactions with respect to the solar neutrino problem is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the solar neutrino problem is not resolved by improved nuclear reaction rates, because the authors' potential-model calculations favor a higher astrophysical S-factor for the reaction 7Be(p,gamma)8B, which actually increases the predicted high-energy neutrino flux and therefore worsens the discrepancy with observations; even if one adopts the much lower S-factor from the RIKEN Coulomb dissociation experiment, the 37Cl experiment remains inconsistent with the Standard Solar Model.

## Method

The authors review the direct reaction (DI) mechanism and compare two theoretical approaches for calculating astrophysical S-factors for key pp-chain reactions: microscopic methods (Resonating Group Method and Generator Coordinate Method) and potential models (Optical Model, Distorted Wave Born Approximation, and Direct Capture). For the potential-model calculations, the authors use folding potentials adjusted to reproduce elastic scattering differential cross sections for 3He-3He and 3He-4He in the 1-3 MeV range, and then compute S-factors for the reactions 3He(3He,2p)4He, 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be, and 7Be(p,gamma)8B, comparing these to experimental data and to the currently accepted values from Bahcall and Pinsonneault (1992).

## Key numbers

- For 3He(3He,2p)4He: potential-model S0 = 4.854 x 10^3 keV b, dS/dE = -1.328 b; accepted value from Bahcall (1992) is S0 = 5.0(1 +/- 0.18) x 10^3 keV b.
- For 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be: potential-model S0 = 0.516 keV b, dS/dE = -3.67 x 10^-4 b; accepted value from Bahcall (1992) is S0 = 0.533(1 +/- 0.096) keV b.
- For 7Be(p,gamma)8B: potential-model S0 = 0.0249 keV b, dS/dE = -3.2 x 10^-5 b; accepted value from Bahcall (1992) is S0 = 0.0224(1 +/- 0.28) keV b.
- The potential-model calculation for 7Be(p,gamma)8B is about 11% higher than the accepted value.
- Using the potential-model rates, the predicted 8B neutrino flux increases by 11% relative to the accepted values, giving a 37Cl rate of 8.7 SNU (instead of 8.0 SNU).
- Using the RIKEN Coulomb dissociation S-factor (55% of the accepted value), the 37Cl rate becomes 5.2 SNU, still outside experimental errors.
- Observed-to-predicted ratios: Davis experiment 0.275 +/- 0.025; Kamiokande 0.47 +/- 0.05 +/- 0.06; Gallex 0.63 +/- 0.20; Sage 0.44 (+0.24, -0.29).

## Context

This review extends the line of work by Bahcall (1989) and Bahcall and Pinsonneault (1992) on the solar neutrino problem, comparing microscopic calculations (e.g., Typel et al. 1991; Kajino and Arima 1986) with the authors' own potential-model approach. The paper shows that improved nuclear reaction rates alone cannot resolve the solar neutrino problem, and it highlights the unresolved discrepancy between laboratory screening effects and theoretical predictions (where screening energies are too low by up to a factor of about 2), which raises doubts about extrapolations of S-factors to thermonuclear energies. The work enables future studies to focus on non-nuclear solutions (e.g., neutrino oscillations) and on resolving the screening discrepancy.
