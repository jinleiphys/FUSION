# Parity-Violating Interaction Effects in the np System

> We investigate parity-violating observables in the np system, including the longitudinal asymmetry and neutron-spin rotation in np elastic scattering, the photon asymmetry in np radiative capture, and the asymmetries in deuteron photo-disintegration d(gamma,n)p in the threshold region and electro-disintegration d(e,e`)np in quasi-elastic kinematics. To have an estimate of the model dependence for the various predictions, a number of different, latest-generation strong-interaction potentials--Argonne v18, Bonn 2000, and Nijmegen I--are used in combination with a weak-interaction potential consisting of pi-, rho-, and omega-meson exchanges--the model known as DDH. The complete bound and scattering problems in the presence of parity-conserving, including electromagnetic, and parity-violating potentials is solved in both configuration and momentum space. The issue of electromagnetic current conservation is examined carefully. We find large cancellations between the asymmetries induced by the parity-violating interactions and those arising from the associated pion-exchange currents. In the np capture, the model dependence is nevertheless quite small, because of constraints arising through the Siegert evaluation of the relevant E1 matrix elements. In quasi-elastic electron scattering these processes are found to be insignificant compared to the asymmetry produced by gamma-Z interference on individual nucleons.

## Key claim

Parity-violating observables in the np system exhibit large cancellations between asymmetries induced by weak interactions and those from associated pion-exchange currents, but the model dependence in np radiative capture is small due to constraints from Siegert evaluation of E1 matrix elements; in quasi-elastic electron scattering, the parity-violating effects are insignificant compared to the asymmetry from gamma-Z interference on individual nucleons.

## Method

The authors solved the complete bound and scattering problems for the np system in both configuration and momentum space, using three different strong-interaction potentials (Argonne v18, Bonn 2000, and Nijmegen I) combined with the DDH weak-interaction potential based on pi-, rho-, and omega-meson exchanges. They calculated parity-violating observables including the longitudinal asymmetry and neutron-spin rotation in np elastic scattering, the photon asymmetry in np radiative capture, and asymmetries in deuteron photo-disintegration and electro-disintegration, while carefully examining electromagnetic current conservation and including parity-violating two-body currents from pion exchange.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal asymmetry in np elastic scattering: calculated for lab energies 0–350 MeV using three strong potentials (AV18, Bonn 2000, Nijmegen I) with DDH weak couplings
- Neutron spin rotation at zero energy: calculated with significant sensitivity to both weak vector-meson couplings and the input strong-interaction potential
- Photon asymmetry in np radiative capture at thermal neutron energies: model dependence found to be quite small due to Siegert evaluation constraints
- Deuteron photo-disintegration asymmetry d(gamma,n)p: calculated from threshold up to 20 MeV, exhibiting significant sensitivity to weak vector-meson couplings
- Deuteron electro-disintegration asymmetry d(e,e')np at quasi-elastic kinematics: found to be insignificant compared to asymmetry from gamma-Z interference on individual nucleons
- Four-momentum transfers squared of 0.04 and 0.09 (GeV/c)^2 for the SAMPLE experiment kinematics

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of parity-violating effects in two-nucleon systems, following their calculation of the longitudinal asymmetry in pp elastic scattering (Carlson 2002) and a preliminary account of np capture and deuteron electro-disintegration asymmetries (Schiavilla 2003a). The paper provides a thorough analysis using the DDH model (Desplanques 1980) with updated strong-interaction potentials, and demonstrates that neutron spin rotation and deuteron photo-disintegration asymmetries are sensitive probes of short-ranged meson couplings, reinforcing that parity-violating mixings rather than meson couplings are the physically relevant parameters.
