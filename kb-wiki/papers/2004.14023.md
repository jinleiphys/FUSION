# Internal conversion of the low energy $^{229m}$Th isomer in the Thorium anion

> A process of the decay of the anomalously low lying nuclear isomer $^{229m}$Th$(3/2^+,8.28 \pm 0.17$ eV) in the Thorium anion (Th$^-$) via the internal conversion (IC) channel is studied. We show that the half life of the nuclear isomer in the $6d_{3/2}^37s_{1/2}^2$ ground state and in the $6d_{3/2}^2 7s_{1/2}^2 7p_{1/2}^1$ excited state of Th$^-$ is $\approx1.5$ and $\approx1.1$ times bigger than in the $6d_{3/2}^2 7s_{1/2}^2$ ground state of the Th atom. The IC probabilities in the anion decreases despite the decay via the additional $6d_{3/2}$ or $7p_{1/2}$ electrons. This counterintuitive result is a consequence: a) of a decrease in the amplitudes of the $6d_{3/2}$ and $7s_{1/2}$ wave functions near the nucleus due to an increase in their diffuseness of upon the addition of extra electron, b) of mutual compensation in the IC probability due to a kinematic factor, which depends on the energy of the conversion electron in the continuum as $E_c^{-1/2}$, and the $E_c^{1/4}$ growth of the amplitudes of the electron wave functions.

## Key claim

The half-life of the $^{229m}$Th isomer in the Thorium anion (Th$^-$) is approximately 1.5 times longer in the ground state and about 1.1 times longer in the excited state than in the neutral Thorium atom, a counterintuitive result because the IC probability decreases despite the presence of an additional valence electron available for the decay.

## Method

The internal conversion coefficients (ICC) for the $^{229m}$Th isomer decay were calculated using Dirac-Hartree-Fock wave functions for the bound states of Th, Th$^-$, and Th$^{-^*}$ from Tang (2019), combined with numerical solutions of the Dirac equation for continuum electron wave functions. The electron shell potential was obtained via density functional theory (DFT) with self-consistent exchange and correlation effects, and the ICC were computed using the standard formulas for electric and magnetic multipole transitions at the isomer energy of 8.28 eV.

## Key numbers

- Isomer energy: $E_{\text{is}} = 8.28 \pm 0.17$ eV
- For the Th atom ground state ($6d_{3/2}^2 7s_{1/2}^2$): $\alpha_{M1}(7s_{1/2}) = 7.93 \times 10^8$, $\alpha_{E2}(7s_{1/2}) = 1.06 \times 10^{15}$
- For the Th$^-$ ground state ($6d_{3/2}^3 7s_{1/2}^2$): $\alpha_{M1}(7s_{1/2}) = 5.40 \times 10^8$, $\alpha_{E2}(7s_{1/2}) = 6.59 \times 10^{14}$
- For the Th$^{-^*}$ excited state ($6d_{3/2}^2 7s_{1/2}^2 7p_{1/2}^1$): $\alpha_{M1}(7p_{1/2}) = 3.37 \times 10^7$, $\alpha_{E2}(7p_{1/2}) = 5.44 \times 10^{16}$
- Isomer half-life in Th atom (Set 1): $T_{1/2} = 7.86 \times 10^{-7}$ s
- Isomer half-life in Th$^-$ ground state (Set 1): $T_{1/2} = 1.15 \times 10^{-6}$ s (ratio 1.47 relative to Th)
- Isomer half-life in Th$^{-^*}$ excited state (Set 1): $T_{1/2} = 8.98 \times 10^{-7}$ s (ratio 1.14 relative to Th)
- Binding energies: $7s_{1/2}$ in Th = -6.49 eV, in Th$^-$ = -1.60 eV; $6d_{3/2}$ in Th = -7.25 eV, in Th$^-$ = -0.63 eV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $^{229m}$Th isomer decay channels, particularly internal conversion in neutral Th atoms (Strizhov 1991, Tkalya 2015) and in Rydberg states (Tkalya 2019), to the case of the Thorium anion. The results are relevant for experiments using laser ablation and ion traps to study the isomer, as demonstrated by Tang (2019) who produced Th$^-$ anions and characterized their electronic structure. The findings enable more accurate predictions for isomer lifetime measurements in trapped anion systems, which are being developed for nuclear clock applications.
