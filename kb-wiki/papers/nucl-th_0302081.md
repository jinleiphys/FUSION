# Renormalization Group Methods for the Nuclear Many-Body Problem

> The application of renormalization group methods to microscopic nuclear many-body calculations is discussed. We present the solution of the renormalization group equations in the particle-hole channels for neutron matter and the application to S-wave pairing. Furthermore, we point out that the inclusion of tensor and spin-orbit forces leads to spin non-conserving effective interactions in nuclear matter.

# Renormalization Group Methods for the Nuclear Many-Body Problem

## Key claim

The renormalization group approach to the nuclear many-body problem, when applied to neutron matter, yields a low-momentum interaction that is model-independent and phase-shift equivalent to realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, but the inclusion of tensor and spin-orbit forces leads to spin non-conserving effective interactions that are not captured by conventional many-body calculations.

## Method

The authors solve renormalization group equations in the particle-hole channels for infinite neutron matter, starting from a free-space low-momentum potential \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. The RG decimation integrates out high-momentum modes relative to the Fermi surface, yielding a running effective interaction that is successively renormalized as the cutoff is lowered, with the resulting quasiparticle scattering amplitude used to compute Landau parameters and the S-wave superfluid pairing gap.

## Key numbers

- The low-momentum potential \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) is constructed with a cutoff \(\Lambda \sim 2.1 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\), the scale up to which potential models are fitted to empirical phase shifts.
- The density-dependent cutoff for \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) is set to \(\Lambda_{V_{\text{low}\,k}} = \sqrt{2}\,k_{\text{F}}\).
- The S-wave superfluid gap is suppressed from \(3.3 \, \text{MeV}\) to \(0.8 \, \text{MeV}\) at maximum due to particle-hole screening in the many-body medium.
- The cutoff range for which \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) is almost independent in the \(T=1\) channel is \(0.7 \, \text{fm}^{-1} \lesssim \Lambda \lesssim 2.1 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach of Shankar (1994) to nuclear matter, building on prior developments of the low-momentum potential \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) by Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators. The results for the S-wave pairing gap agree with the polarization potential model of Wambach et al. (1993), and the RG method provides a systematic framework for computing effective interactions that includes induced interactions beyond the independent pair approximation. The identification of spin non-conserving effective interactions from tensor and spin-orbit forces opens the door to improved calculations of P-wave pairing and other spin-dependent phenomena in neutron matter.
