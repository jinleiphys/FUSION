# Initial state in heavy ion collisions

> We briefly review advances in understanding the initial stages of a heavy ion collision. In particular the focus is on moving from parametrizing the initial state to calculating its properties from QCD, consistently with the description of hard probes and dilute-dense scattering experiments. Modeling the event-by-event fluctuating nuclear geometry in initial state calculations has significantly improved in recent years. We also discuss prospects of directly seeing effects of particle correlations created in the initial state in the experimental observables.

# Initial state in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that the weak coupling, parton saturation (Color Glass Condensate) picture of the initial state in heavy ion collisions is progressing from qualitative to quantitative descriptions, but emphasizes that even the most sophisticated dynamical models still rely on Monte Carlo Glauber nuclear geometry as an input, and that no current calculation is fully free of adjustable parameters or uncertainties in normalization.

## Method

The paper reviews the weak coupling picture of gluon production via a cascade of splittings from valence quarks, leading to saturation at scale Qs where gluon merging balances splitting. It compares dilute-dense control measurements (deep inelastic scattering, proton-nucleus collisions) to constrain the unintegrated gluon distributions, and contrasts Monte Carlo Glauber parametrizations with dynamical models (MCKLN, MCrcBK, IPglasma, EKRT) that combine nuclear geometry with QCD-based particle production mechanisms. The review also discusses calculations of long-range rapidity correlations from initial-state color field domains versus final-state collective flow.

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity growth in pp collisions scales as ~ sqrt(s)^0.2 and in AA collisions as ~ sqrt(s)^0.3, consistent with a weak coupling estimate dN_g/dy ~ sqrt(s)^(alpha_s)
- A recent weak coupling kinetic theory calculation extrapolated to realistic alpha_s values gives matching times to viscous hydrodynamics as short as 1 fm
- CGC calculations of R_pA require a "K-factor" of order ~2 to fit spectra, but this normalization uncertainty cancels in the ratio
- R_pA predictions from different calculations differ by a factor of ~2 between Albacete (2010) and Lappi (2013), attributed to treatment of nuclear geometry
- Color field domain correlations are suppressed by ~1/(N_c^2 Q_s^2 S_perp), where S_perp is the interaction area, making them enhanced in small collision systems

## Context

This review extends the line of work on initial state modeling from parametrizations (Monte Carlo Glauber, Miller 2007) toward QCD-based calculations constrained by HERA DIS data and proton-nucleus measurements. It contrasts the weak coupling approach with strong coupling pictures that would predict complete baryon stopping. The paper enables future work on disentangling initial-state correlations from final-state collective flow in small collision systems, and emphasizes the need for better constrained gluon fragmentation functions as noted by d'Enterria (2013) and de Florian (2014).
