# Global Data-Driven Determination of Baryon Transition Form Factors

> Hadronic resonances emerge from strong interactions encoding the dynamics of quarks and gluons. The structure of these resonances can be probed by virtual photons parameterized in transition form factors. In this study, twelve $N^*$ and $Δ$ transition form factors at the pole are extracted from data with the center-of-mass energy from $πN$ threshold to $1.8\,{\rm GeV}$, and the photon virtuality $0\leq Q^2/{\rm GeV}^2\leq 8$. For the first time, these results are determined from a simultaneous analysis of more than one state, i.e., $\sim 10^5$ $πN$, $ηN$, and $KΛ$ electroproduction data. In addition, about $ 5\cdot 10^4$ data in the hadronic sector as well as photoproduction serve as boundary conditions. For the $Δ(1232)$ and $N(1440)$ states our results are in qualitative agreement with previous studies, while the transition form factors at the poles of some higher excited states are estimated for the first time. Realistic uncertainties are determined by further exploring the parameter space.

## Key claim

For the first time, baryon transition form factors for twelve \(N^*\) and \(\Delta\) resonances are extracted at the pole from a simultaneous analysis of multiple final states (\(\pi N\), \(\eta N\), \(K\Lambda\) electroproduction), yielding reaction-independent results; however, the uncertainties are larger than in previous single-channel studies because the parameter space is explored more extensively to reflect the sparsity and incompleteness of electroproduction data.

## Method

The Jülich-Bonn-Washington (JBW) dynamical coupled-channel framework is used, where a potential derived from a chiral Lagrangian is iterated in a Lippmann-Schwinger-like equation to obtain scattering amplitudes. Electroproduction multipoles are extracted from a simultaneous fit to approximately \(10^5\) \(\pi N\), \(\eta N\), and \(K\Lambda\) electroproduction data plus about \(5 \times 10^4\) hadronic and photoproduction data, for center-of-mass energies from \(\pi N\) threshold to \(1.8\) GeV and photon virtualities \(0 \leq Q^2 \leq 8\) GeV\(^2\). Transition form factors at the pole are then obtained via a Laurent expansion of the helicity amplitudes and the elastic \(\pi N\) partial waves, using the pole residues from the hadronic JüBo2017 analysis.

## Key numbers

- Twelve \(N^*\) and \(\Delta\) transition form factors extracted at the pole, for states with pole mass \(\text{Re}(z_p) \leq 1.8\) GeV and orbital angular momentum \(l \leq 3\): \(N(1535)\), \(N(1650)\), \(N(1440)\), \(N(1710)\), \(N(1720)\), \(N(1520)\), \(N(1675)\), \(N(1680)\), \(\Delta(1620)\), \(\Delta(1232)\), \(\Delta(1600)\), \(\Delta(1700)\).
- Data set includes \(\sim 10^5\) electroproduction data and about \(5 \times 10^4\) hadronic and photoproduction data.
- Energy range: from \(\pi N\) threshold to \(1.8\) GeV; photon virtuality range: \(0 \leq Q^2/\text{GeV}^2 \leq 8\).
- For the \(N(1440)\), the zero crossing in \(\text{Re}\,A_{1/2}\) occurs at \(Q^2 = (0.1 \pm 0.04)\) GeV\(^2\), smaller than the ANL-Osaka pole determination (zero at \(Q^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn-Washington coupled-channel analyses of hadronic and photoproduction reactions (Ronchen 2018) to electroproduction, providing the first simultaneous extraction of transition form factors at the pole for multiple resonances. It complements and is compared with the ANL-Osaka model (Kamano 2018) and the MAID model (Tiator 2016), which typically report Breit-Wigner or single-channel results; for the \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N(1440)\) the results are in qualitative agreement with those prior studies, while for higher excited states the pole TFFs are estimated for the first time. The results enable future extensions to higher photon virtualities at JLab and can guide experiments to reduce uncertainties in kinematic regions most relevant for probing resonance structure, including possible hybrid baryons.
