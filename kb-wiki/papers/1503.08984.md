# Gluon Propagation in Curved Space

> In quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the vacuum structures are taken to be formed by nonperturbative interactions between gluons. The contributions of gluonic interactions can be parametrized by gluon condensates and especially the dimension-2 condensate $\langle A_μ^{2} \rangle$ can be used to generalize the $θ$ vacuum. In the generalized picture, each gauge fixed slice forms a curved surface around a hadron and other strongly interacting objects, and the propagation of a gluon has to be considered on this curved surface. The gluon propagator turns out to be massive due to the curvature of the gauge fixed surface implementing the possibility that the massless gauge bosons might provide the majority of the hadronic mass and even behave as dark matter in cosmological scales.

# Gluon Propagation in Curved Space

## Key claim

The authors argue that the gluon propagator acquires a mass term naturally from the curvature of gauge-fixed slices in a generalized θ-vacuum picture, rather than from perturbative loop corrections, and suggest that these massive gluons could constitute dark matter on cosmological scales, but the result depends on the assumption that the gauge-fixed surfaces are maximally symmetric spaces where the Ricci tensor takes the form R^{μν} = m²g^{μν}.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized θ-vacuum by parametrizing coordinate-dependent θ values through the dimension-2 gluon condensate ⟨A_μ²⟩, where each fixed value of the condensate defines a curved gauge-fixed slice around hadronic matter. They derive the functional form of the condensate variation by comparing nonlocal quark condensates with gluon condensates, using a measure M(Q) = -k ln(Q/Q₀) that leads to an exponential distance dependence. The gluon propagator is then obtained by quantizing the gluon field on these curved slices, replacing ordinary derivatives with covariant derivatives and using the maximally symmetric space condition R^{μν} = m²g^{μν} to extract an effective mass term.

## Key numbers

- The dynamical gluon mass from constant dimension-2 condensate is phenomenologically determined to be about 500–600 MeV, but the authors note this value is too large to represent a single gluon within a hadron.
- The neutron electric dipole moment measurement gives the limit θ < 3 × 10⁻¹⁰ for the CP-violating parameter.
- The functional form of the nonlocal quark condensate Q(r) for equal weight F(ν)=1 and α=2 is Q = Q₀ exp{-(1/k)(r² - r)/ln r}, with r = |x - y|/ℓ where ℓ is a scale parameter.
- The perturbative local correction gives Q = (Q₀/r^β) exp{-(1/k)(r² - r)/ln r}.

## Context

This work extends the generalized θ-vacuum picture previously developed by the same group (Lee, Kim, Choi, earlier works) and the concept of in-hadron condensates (R4, R7 in the paper). It builds on lattice studies that confirmed O(1/k²) corrections to the gluon propagator indicating the existence of dimension-2 condensate ⟨A_μ²⟩ (R17), and on instanton shape recognition procedures (R18). The approach contrasts with standard dynamical mass generation through loop expansions and renormalization, which the authors argue produces masses too large and uncertain for nonperturbative gluonic interactions. The work enables a new dark matter candidate from nonperturbative QCD effects, connecting to experimental observations of P-odd charge separation at RHIC and LHC (R11).
