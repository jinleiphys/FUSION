# Soft pions at high energy and the flavor asymmetry of the light sea quarks in the nucleon

> The modified Gottfried sum rule makes clear importance of the high energy region not only in the theoretical meaning but also in the numerical analysis. In this talk, it is shown that the soft pion theorem in the inclusive reaction at high energy can explain the magnitude about $0.02 \sim 0.04$ in the NMC deficit. The main contribution comes from the small $x$ region. We also estimate the soft pion contribution to the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule and show it to be negligible. However we find that the contribution to $g_1^{ep}$ becomes positive below $x = 0.002$.

# Soft pions at high energy and the flavor asymmetry of the light sea quarks in the nucleon

## Key claim

The soft pion theorem applied to inclusive reactions at high energy can explain a magnitude of about 0.02 to 0.04 in the NMC deficit of the Gottfried sum, with the main contribution coming from the small x region; however, this result depends heavily on the spin-dependent structure function (g1^ep - g1^en) and on the assumption that Feynman scaling holds.

## Method

The author applies the soft pion theorem to semi-inclusive current-nucleon reactions using light-cone variables, taking the soft pion limit with k^+ = 0, transverse momentum = 0, and then k^- = 0. The hadronic tensor is obtained by squaring the amplitude, and the relation to structure functions in total inclusive reactions is derived using light-cone current algebra at an initial Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, with nucleon and antinucleon multiplicities parameterized as a function of s. The phase space for the soft pion is restricted by requiring transverse momentum squared less than or equal to m_pi^2 and a Feynman scaling variable cut at x_F = 0.1.

## Key numbers

- The soft pion contribution to the Gottfried sum is estimated to be about -0.04 to -0.02.
- The theoretical value for the charge asymmetry (F^+ - F^-) is roughly 0.15 to 0.18, compared to the experimental value of 0.28 ± 0.05 in the region 0 < x_F < 0.1.
- The parameter a in the multiplicity formula <n> = a log_e s + 1 is fixed at 0.2, based on e^+e^- annihilation data from DELPHI.
- The initial Q^2 for the valence quark distribution functions is set at Q_0^2 = 4 GeV^2.
- The soft pion contribution to g1^ep becomes positive below x = 0.002, but the contribution above x = 0.0001 to the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule is negligible.

## Context

This work extends the modified Gottfried sum rule approach, which had previously explained the NMC deficit almost model-independently as a reflection of hadronic vacuum from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The calculation addresses a shortcoming of mesonic models (reviewed in Mesonic Models (1998)), which account for only about half of the NMC deficit and cannot reliably estimate contributions from the small x region. The soft pion mechanism provides a background contribution that may compensate for this flaw, and the numerical prediction based on the modified Gottfried sum rule exactly agrees with the experimental value from the E866/NuSea collaboration (1998).
