# Correlated gluonic hot spots meet symmetric cumulants data at LHC energies

> We present a systematic study on the influence of spatial correlations between the proton constituents, in our case gluonic hot spots, their size and their number on the symmetric cumulant SC(2,3), at the eccentricity level, within a Monte Carlo Glauber framework [1]. When modeling the proton as composed by 3 gluonic hot spots, the most common assumption in the literature, we find that the inclusion of spatial correlations is indispensable to reproduce the negative sign of SC(2,3) in the highest centrality bins as dictated by data. Further, the subtle interplay between the different scales of the problem is discussed. To conclude, the possibility of feeding a 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulation with our entropy profiles is exposed.

## Key claim
Spatial correlations between gluonic hot spots in the proton, implemented via a short-range repulsive core, are indispensable for reproducing the negative sign of the normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(2,3) in the highest centrality bins of p+p collisions at LHC energies, but the sign also depends on the interplay of hot spot size, number, and repulsive core distance.

## Method
The authors use a Monte Carlo Glauber framework where the proton is modeled as a collection of gluonic hot spots, each characterized by a radius \(R_{hs}\) and number \(N_{hs}\) (default 3). A novel short-range repulsive correlation between hot spots, controlled by a repulsive core distance \(r_c\), is introduced to modify their spatial distribution in the transverse plane. The normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(2,3) is computed at the eccentricity level from the entropy deposited by wounded hot spots, and the results are compared between correlated and uncorrelated scenarios.

## Key numbers
- NSC(2,3) becomes negative only in the correlated scenario for the ultra-central bins [0–0.1%] and [0.1–1%], matching the sign observed in CMS data.
- For \(N_{hs}=4\), NSC(2,3) is compatible with negative values in the [0–0.1%] bin even in the uncorrelated case, within statistical uncertainty.
- The value of NSC(2,3) is identical across all scenarios (correlated/uncorrelated, \(N_{hs}=2\) to 4) for a fixed ratio \(N_w/N_{\rm coll}\).
- The hydrodynamic simulation is initialized at proper time \(\tau = 0.2\) fm with decoupling temperature \(T_{\rm dec} = 100\) MeV and a minimum of \(\eta/s\) at \(T = 150\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Monte Carlo Glauber model of Albacete (2016) by adding short-range repulsive correlations between proton constituents, addressing the lack of a successful theoretical description for the sign change of SC(2,3) in p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV measured by the CMS Collaboration (2017). The results suggest that the ratio \(N_w/N_{\rm coll}\) is a critical parameter controlling the sign of NSC(2,3), and the framework enables future hydrodynamic simulations to test whether elliptic flow \(v_2\) is similarly sensitive to proton substructure correlations.
