# Effects of the nuclear equation of state on the r-mode instability and evolution of neutron stars

> I study the effect of nuclear equation of state on the r-mode instability of a rotating neutron star. I consider the case where the crust of the neutron star is perfectly rigid and I employ the related theory introduced by Lindblom {\it et al.} \cite{Lidblom-2000}. The gravitational and the viscous time scales, the critical angular velocity and the critical temperature are evaluated by employing a phenomenological nuclear model for the neutron star matter. The predicted equations of state for the $β$-stable nuclear matter are parameterized by varying the slope $L$ of the symmetry energy at saturation density on the interval $72.5 \ {\rm MeV} \leq L \leq 110 \ {\rm MeV}$. The effects of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy on r-mode instability properties and the time evolution of the angular velocity are presented and analyzed. A comparison of theoretical predictions with observed neutron stars in low-mass x-ray binaries (LMXBs) and millisecond radio pulsars (MSRPs) is also performed and analyzed. I estimate that it may be possible to impose constraints on the nuclear equation of state, by a suitable treatment of observations and theoretical predictions of the rotational frequency and spindown rate evolution of known neutron stars.

## Key claim

The study finds that the slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density, varied in the range \(72.5\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq L \leq 110\ \mathrm{MeV}\), systematically affects the r-mode instability window and the spin evolution of neutron stars with a perfectly rigid crust, but the author notes that the analysis neglects bulk and shear viscosity contributions that could increase damping for larger \(L\) and that the results depend on the assumption of a perfectly rigid crust.

## Method

The author employed a phenomenological momentum-dependent interaction model (MDIM) for the energy per baryon of asymmetric nuclear matter, parameterizing the density dependence of the symmetry energy by varying the slope parameter \(L\) between 65 and 110 MeV while fixing the symmetry energy at saturation to 30 MeV. The core-crust transition density was determined using a thermodynamic stability method, and the crust equation of state was taken from Feynman, Metropolis, and Teller (1949) and Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain neutron star structure, and the r-mode instability was analyzed using the formalism of Lindblom et al. (2000) for a perfectly rigid crust, focusing on the \(m=2\) r-mode and shear dissipation in the viscous boundary layer at the crust-core interface.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter \(L\) was varied over the interval \(72.5\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq L \leq 110\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the main r-mode analysis (with a broader range \(65\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq L \leq 110\ \mathrm{MeV}\) used in the nuclear model).
- The transition density \(n_t\) was found to follow a linear relation: \(n_t = 0.1253 - 0.0007\ L\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}\), where \(L\) is in MeV.
- For a \(1.4\ M_\odot\) neutron star, the integral \(I(R_c) = \int_0^{R_c} (\epsilon(r)/\mathrm{MeV\ fm^{-3}}) (r/\mathrm{km})^6 d(r/\mathrm{km})\) was fitted as \(I(R_c) = (0.7 + 0.084\ L) \times 10^8\).
- For a \(1.8\ M_\odot\) neutron star, the same integral was fitted as \(I(R_c) = (1.24 + 0.0845\ L) \times 10^8\).
- The critical temperature below which the r-mode instability is completely suppressed was given by \(T_c / 10^8\ \mathrm{K} = (3/2)^{11/2} (-\tilde{\tau}_{GR} / \tilde{\tau}_v)\).
- The critical angular velocity was expressed as \(\Omega_c / \Omega_0 = (2/3) (T_c / T)^{2/11}\).
- The shear viscosity from electron-electron scattering was given by \(\eta_{ee} = 6.0 \times 10^6\ \rho^2 T^{-2}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-1}\ s^{-1}}\) and from neutron-neutron scattering by \(\eta_{nn} = 347\ \rho^{9/4} T^{-2}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-1}\ s^{-1}}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Wen et al. (2012) and Vidana (2012) on the sensitivity of the r-mode instability window to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, using a phenomenological model that covers a range of \(L\) values. It also builds on the formalism of Lindblom et al. (2000) and Bildsten and Ushomirsky (2000) for boundary-layer damping in neutron stars with a rigid crust. The results enable constraints on the nuclear equation of state by comparing theoretical predictions of rotational frequency and spindown evolution with observations of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries and millisecond radio pulsars.
