# Pion form factor from an AdS deformed background

> We consider a bottom-up AdS/QCD model with a conformal exponential deformation $e^{k\,z^2}$ on a Lorentz invariant AdS background. In this model, we assume the conformal dimension associated with the operator that creates pions at the boundary as $Δ=3$. Regarding the infrared scale related to photon field $k_γ$, we analyze two cases: constant and depending on the transferred momentum $q$. In these two cases, we computed the electromagnetic pion form factor as well as the pion radius. We compare our results with experimental data as well as other theoretical (holographic and non-holographic) models. In particular, for the momentum-dependent infrared scale, we find good agreement with the available experimental data as well as non-holographic models.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the correct large-\(q^2\) scaling behavior of the pion form factor can be recovered without reinterpreting the conformal dimension as twist (as done in light-front holography) by instead promoting the infrared scale associated with the virtual photon, \(k_\gamma\), to be a function of the transferred momentum \(q\). This modification is motivated by the fact that the photon and pion are described by different deformed geometries, so the photon scale is not tied to confinement.

## Method

The authors use a bottom-up AdS/QCD model with a conformal exponential deformation \(e^{k_I z^2}\) in the metric, where \(k_\pi\) is fixed to reproduce the pion mass and \(k_\gamma\) is the scale for the virtual photon. They solve the scalar field equation of motion for the pion with conformal dimension \(\Delta = 3\) and the massless gauge boson equation for the photon, then compute the electromagnetic pion form factor via the holographic three-point function from the minimal coupling action. The form factor integral is evaluated numerically for two cases: constant \(k_\gamma\) and momentum-dependent \(k_\gamma(q) = q\,k_\gamma\).

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 0.139\) GeV, obtained with \(k_\pi = -0.0425^2\) GeV\(^2\).
* Pion radius with constant \(k_\gamma = -3.8\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\Delta=3\): \(r_\pi = 0.458\) fm, with an error around 30% compared to the experimental value.
* Pion radius with momentum-dependent \(k_\gamma(q) = q\,k_\gamma\) and \(k_\gamma = -2.8\) GeV\(^2\): \(r_\pi = 0.671\) fm, with a relative error of 2.0% compared to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the deformed AdS background approach of Andreev (2006) and FolcoCapossoli (2019) to the electromagnetic pion form factor. It directly addresses the issue raised by Grigoryan (2008) that using \(\Delta=3\) gives incorrect scaling, and contrasts with the solution of Brodsky (2007) who reinterpreted the conformal dimension as twist. The momentum-dependent \(k_\gamma\) proposal enables agreement with experimental data from CERN, DESY, JLab, and Cornell, as well as with non-holographic models such as Dyson-Schwinger (Maris 2000) and dispersion relations (Geshkenbein 1998), while preserving the standard AdS/CFT dictionary for conformal dimensions.
