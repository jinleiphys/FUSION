# Implementing the three-particle quantization condition for $π^+π^+K^+$ and related systems

> Recently, the formalism needed to relate the finite-volume spectrum of systems of nondegenerate spinless particles has been derived. In this work we discuss a range of issues that arise when implementing this formalism in practice, provide further theoretical results that can be used to check the implementation, and make available codes for implementing the three-particle quantization condition. Specifically, we discuss the need to modify the upper limit of the cutoff function due to the fact that the left-hand cut in the scattering amplitudes for two nondegenerate particles moves closer to threshold; we describe the decomposition of the three-particle amplitude $\mathcal{K}_\text{df,3}$ into the matrix basis used in the quantization condition, including both $s$ and $p$ waves, with the latter arising in the amplitude for two nondegenerate particles; we derive the threshold expansion for the lightest three-particle state in the rest frame up to $\mathcal{O}(1/L^5)$; and we calculate the leading-order predictions in chiral perturbation theory for $\mathcal{K}_\text{df,3}$ in the $π^+π^+K^+$ and $π^+K^+K^+$ systems. We focus mainly on systems with two identical particles plus a third that is different ("2+1" systems). We describe the formalism in full detail, and present numerical explorations in toy models, in particular checking that the results agree with the threshold expansion, and making a prediction for the spectrum of $π^+π^+K^+$ levels using the two- and three-particle interactions predicted by chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper provides a practical implementation of the recently derived three-particle quantization condition for systems with two identical particles and a third distinct particle (2+1 systems), including explicit code and checks, but the formalism is limited to spinless particles and the numerical results rely on toy models or leading-order chiral perturbation theory rather than actual lattice QCD data.

## Method

The authors implement the finite-volume quantization condition for 2+1 systems of spinless particles by constructing the matrices \(F_3\) and \(\mathcal{K}_{\text{df},3}\) in a basis of spectator momenta and partial waves (s and p waves), using real spherical harmonics and UV regularization following the method of Hansen and Sharpe (2014). They derive the threshold expansion for the lightest three-particle state up to \(\mathcal{O}(1/L^5)\) and compute the leading-order chiral perturbation theory predictions for \(\mathcal{K}_{\text{df},3}\) in the \(\pi^+\pi^+K^+\) and \(\pi^+K^+K^+\) systems. The implementation is provided in Python scripts that evaluate the quantization condition matrix and solve for finite-volume energy levels.

## Key numbers

- For a toy model with masses \(M_1 = 100\) MeV, \(M_2 = 50\) MeV, box size \(L = 5/M_1\), and scattering parameters \(a_{1s}=0.15\), \(a_{1p}=0.2\), \(a_{2s}=0.1\) (all in units of \(1/M_1\)), the quantization condition matrix has dimension 11 and eigenvalues ranging from approximately 60310 to 270697 (dimensionless units).
- The noninteracting three-particle energies in the rest frame for this system are \(E_{\text{cm}}^* = 2.500000\) (in units of \(M_1\)) with degeneracy 1 in irrep \(A_{1u}\), and \(E_{\text{cm}}^* = 3.711938\) with degeneracy 3 in irreps \(A_{1u}\) and \(E_u\).
- With repulsive interactions, the interacting energy levels shift upward: the \(A_{1u}\) ground state shifts from 2.500000 to 2.556465 (shift 0.056465), the \(A_{1u}\) excited state shifts from 3.711938 to 3.767897 (shift 0.055959), and the \(E_u\) state shifts to 3.765428 (shift 0.053489), all in units of \(M_1\).

## Context

This work extends the three-particle quantization condition formalism from identical particles (Hansen and Sharpe, 2014) and fully nondegenerate systems (Blanton, Romero-Lopez, and Sharpe, 2019) to the practically important 2+1 case, providing the first explicit implementation with public code. The threshold expansion results agree with the nonrelativistic quantum mechanics approach of Smigielski (2008) for \(\pi^+\pi^+K^+\) and \(\pi^+K^+K^+\) systems. The paper enables future lattice QCD calculations of three-particle systems containing two identical mesons and a third distinct meson, such as \(\pi^+\pi^+K^+\) and \(\pi^+K^+K^+\), by providing the necessary formalism and numerical tools.
