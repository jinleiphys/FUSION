# Thermodynamics of QCD at vanishing density

> We study the phase structure of QCD at finite temperature within a Polyakov-loop enhanced quark-meson model. Such a model describes the chiral as well as the confinement-deconfinement dynamics. In the present investigation, based on the approach and results put forward in [1-4], both, matter as well as glue fluctuations are included. We present results for the order parameters as well as some thermodynamic observables and find very good agreement with recent results from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The authors find that their functional renormalisation group (FRG) treatment of a Polyakov-loop enhanced quark-meson model, including both matter and glue fluctuations, yields very good agreement with lattice QCD results for the shape of thermodynamic observables when expressed in terms of reduced temperature, although the absolute scale of the chiral transition temperature differs from lattice results by about 10%.

## Method

The authors solve the FRG flow equation for a (2+1)-flavour Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model at leading order in a derivative expansion, including quantum and thermal fluctuations of quarks and mesons. The glue potential is constructed from a Yang-Mills parametrisation rescaled using a linear relation t_YM ≈ 0.57 t_glue derived from FRG results for QCD with two massless flavours, with the critical temperature of the glue sector treated as an open parameter fixed to 210 MeV by comparison to lattice pressure data.

## Key numbers

- Chiral crossover temperature T_χ = 172 MeV, defined by the peak of dΔ_{l,s}/dT
- Polyakov-loop transition temperature T_d = 163 MeV, defined by the inflection point of dΦ/dT
- Glue sector critical temperature T_cr^{glue} = 210 MeV, fixed by lattice pressure comparison
- Yukawa coupling h = 6.5, sigma meson mass m_σ = 400 MeV for the FRG calculation
- Cutoff scale Λ = 1 GeV for the low-energy effective description
- Linear rescaling relation between Yang-Mills and QCD glue potentials: t_YM ≈ 0.57 t_glue
- FRG estimate for two-flavour QCD glue critical temperature: T_cr^{glue}(N_f=2) = 203 MeV from Braun (2009)

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the PQM model by Herbst et al. (2013), Haas et al. (2013), and Mitter et al. (2013) by including both quantum and thermal fluctuations beyond mean-field approximations, and by incorporating unquenching effects on the glue potential from first-principles QCD results. The results enable quantitative comparison with lattice QCD thermodynamics from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2010) and the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2012), and provide predictions for two-flavour QCD thermodynamics where lattice results are not available.
