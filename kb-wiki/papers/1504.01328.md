# More on Heisenberg's model for high energy nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We revisit Heisenberg's model for nucleon-nucleon scattering which admits a saturation of the Froissart bound. We examine its uniqueness, and find that up to certain natural generalizations, it is the only action that saturates the bound. We find that we can extract also sub-leading behaviour for $σ_{\rm tot}(s)$ from it, though that requires a knowledge of the wavefunction solution that is hard to obtain, and a black-disk model allows the calculation of $σ_{elastic}(s)$ as well. The wavefunction solution is analyzed perturbatively, and its source is interpreted. Generalizations to several mesons, addition of vector mesons, and curved space regimes are also found. We discuss the relations between Heisenberg's model and holographic models that are dual to QCD-like theories.

## Key claim

The paper revisits Heisenberg's 1952 model for nucleon-nucleon scattering and finds that the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action is essentially the only action that can saturate the Froissart bound, up to certain natural generalizations; however, the saturation is only approximate because the average energy per pion depends logarithmically on energy rather than being exactly constant.

## Method

The authors analyze Heisenberg's DBI action for the pion field, which depends on the boost-invariant variable \(s = t^2 - x^2\), and derive the classical field equations near the shockwave at \(s=0\). They examine the uniqueness of the DBI action by testing whether finite-order polynomial derivative interactions can produce the required \(\phi \propto \sqrt{s}\) behavior near the shock, and they generalize the model to include potentials, multiple scalar fields (sigma models), vector mesons, and curved space regimes. The total cross section is extracted from the average energy per pion using the relation \(\sigma_{\rm tot} = \pi b_{\rm max}^2\) with \(b_{\rm max} = (1/m) \ln(\sqrt{\tilde s}/\langle k_0 \rangle)\).

## Key numbers

- The Froissart bound is \(\sigma_{\rm tot}(s) \leq C \ln^2(s/s_0)\) with \(C \leq \pi/m^2\), where \(m\) is the lightest exchange particle mass (the pion mass in QCD).
- For the DBI action, the average energy per pion is \(\langle k_0 \rangle \simeq m \ln \gamma\), where \(\gamma\) is the Lorentz factor, giving \(\sigma_{\rm tot} = (\pi/m^2) \ln^2(\sqrt{\tilde s}/\langle k_0 \rangle)\).
- For a canonical kinetic term with polynomial potential, the average energy per pion scales as \(\langle k_0 \rangle \propto \sqrt{\tilde s} / \ln \sqrt{\tilde s}\), yielding a constant total cross section.
- The solution near \(s=0\) for the massive DBI case is \(\phi = \sqrt{s}/l^2 (1 + a s m^2 + \dots)\) for \(0 \leq s \ll 1/m^2\).
- The large-\(s\) solution for the massive case is \(\phi \simeq \gamma s^{-1/4} m^{-1/2} \cos(m\sqrt{s} + \delta)\) for \(s \gg 1/m^2\).
- Experimental fits to proton-proton and proton-antiproton total cross sections use an effective mass \(m \simeq 1\) GeV rather than the pion mass.

## Context

This work extends Heisenberg's original 1952 model, which proposed a DBI action for the pion field to describe maximal high-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering. The authors connect this model to the Polchinski-Strassler holographic model (2001) for QCD scattering, where saturation of the Froissart bound arises from gravitational shockwave collisions with black hole formation on an IR brane, and to the Sakai-Sugimoto model (2004) for flavor branes in confining backgrounds. The analysis enables further study of sub-leading corrections to the total cross section and the ratio of elastic to total cross section via a black-disk model, as well as generalizations to curved space regimes relevant to holographic duals of QCD-like theories.
