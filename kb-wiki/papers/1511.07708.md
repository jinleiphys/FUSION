# Recent developments in the theory of electromagnetic probes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The theoretical developments in the study of electromagnetic radiation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are reviewed. The recent progress in the rates for photon and lepton pair production is discussed. Together with the improvements in the hydrodynamic descriptions of the bulk medium, the combined effort is discussed to resolve the "direct photon flow puzzle" in the RHIC and the LHC experiments. Further prediction of the direct photon production in high multiplicity proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC energy can serve as a signature of the quark gluon plasma formation in these small systems. Phenomenological study of dilepton production at finite net baryon density is highlighted at the collision energies available for the RHIC beam energy scan program.

## Key claim

The "direct photon flow puzzle", where theoretical calculations underestimate both the yield and the large momentum anisotropy of low-transverse-momentum direct photons measured at RHIC and LHC, may be resolved by combining improved photon emission rates (including next-to-leading-order QGP rates, baryonic hadron-gas channels, and out-of-equilibrium corrections) with more realistic hydrodynamic descriptions that incorporate bulk viscosity and event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical progress in computing thermal photon emission rates from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to next-to-leading order in the strong coupling, and from a hot hadron gas including baryon resonances and bremsstrahlung processes. These rates are coupled to event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations (with shear and bulk viscosities) and to transport approaches (PHSD) to compute direct photon spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients. The effects of non-perturbative corrections near the phase transition (semi-QGP model, chiral perturbation theory) and of non-thermal sources (hadronization photons, jet-medium interactions, chemically undersaturated QGP) are also examined.

## Key numbers

- The next-to-leading order correction to the QGP photon emission rate represents a **20% increase** for α_s = 0.3, with a functional form similar to the leading-order result.
- Baryon channels contribute about **60%** to the net hadronic thermal photon spectrum for photon energy below **3 GeV**.
- Including bulk viscosity increases the space-time volume of the fireball by about **50%** compared to evolution with only shear viscosity, increasing thermal photon production by **50%** while reducing radial flow by **10%**.
- The semi-QGP model produces a **30% suppression** in the direct photon spectrum compared to standard calculations.
- The leading-order hadronic photon emission rate from chiral perturbation theory is **40% to 100% larger** than the standard rate for T = 100–180 MeV.
- In 0–1% central p+Pb collisions at LHC, thermal QGP photon production is **twice** the prompt photon yield for p_T < 2.5 GeV.
- In minimum bias p+Pb collisions, thermal photons enhance the direct photon R_pA by about **50%** for p_T < 3 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the ongoing effort to resolve the "direct photon flow puzzle" first identified in measurements by the PHENIX (Adare 2014) and ALICE (Wilde 2012, Lohner 2012) collaborations, where low-p_T direct photons carry elliptic flow comparable to hadrons. The work builds on the leading-order QGP photon rate of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) and the hadronic rates of Turbide, Rapp, and Gale (2003), and incorporates recent NLO rate calculations by Ghiglieri et al. (2013). The results enable quantitative tests of QGP formation in small collision systems (p+Pb, d+Au, 3He+Au) and provide a framework for extracting transport properties of baryon-rich QCD matter from dilepton measurements in the RHIC beam energy scan program.
