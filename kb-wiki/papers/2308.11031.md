# Exploring Rapidity-Even Dipolar Flow in Isobaric Collisions at RHIC

> Employing the AMPT transport model, we investigate the response of the rapidity-even dipolar flow ($\mathrm{v^{even}_{1}}$) and its associated Global Momentum Conservation (GMC) parameter $\mathrm{K}$ to structural disparities within $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr nuclei. We analyze $\rm Ru+Ru$ and $\rm Zr+Zr$ collisions at a center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{\textit{s}_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. Our analysis demonstrates that the eccentricity $\varepsilon_1$, $\mathrm{v^{even}_{1}}$ and $\mathrm{K}$ exhibit subtle yet discernible sensitivity to the input nuclear structure distinctions between $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr isobars. This observation suggests that measuring $v^{\rm even}_{1}$ and the GMC parameter in these isobaric collisions could serve as a means to fine-tune the comprehension of their nuclear structure disparities and offer insights to enhance the initial condition assumptions of theoretical models.

# Exploring Rapidity-Even Dipolar Flow in Isobaric Collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

Using the AMPT transport model, the rapidity-even dipolar flow (v1^even) and the Global Momentum Conservation parameter K show subtle but discernible sensitivity to nuclear structure differences between 96Ru and 96Zr isobars in collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, suggesting that measuring these observables could help refine theoretical model inputs for nuclear structure, but the differences are modest, with maximum deviations of 2% for v1^even and 4% for K between the two systems.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string-melting option to simulate 96Ru+96Ru and 96Zr+96Zr collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, employing a deformed Woods-Saxon distribution with quadrupole (beta2) and octupole (beta3) deformation parameters. They extracted the initial-state eccentricity epsilon1 from the energy density distribution, and measured v1^even and the GMC parameter K via two-particle correlation functions with a |Delta-eta| > 0.7 cut to suppress short-range non-flow, fitting the v11 matrix as a function of transverse momentum for charged hadrons with 0.2 < pT < 3.0 GeV/c and |eta| < 1.0.

## Key numbers

- Maximum deviation of 2% between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr for v1^even integrated over 0.2 < pT < 0.9 GeV/c in central collisions
- Maximum deviation of 4% +/- 1.4% between Case-1 (Ru) and Case-2 (Zr) for the slope of K vs. inverse average multiplicity
- Epsilon1 ratios show ~4.0% variation between Case-1/Case-2 in central collisions, diminishing with increasing centrality
- Epsilon1 shows ~3.0% difference between Case-1/Case-3 and Case-4/Case-3 in central collisions due to beta3 variations
- Epsilon1 differences of ~1-3% from Case-3/Case-2 ratios due to variations in a0 and R0
- Parton-scattering cross-section fixed at sigma_pp = 2.8 mb
- Woods-Saxon parameters: Ru (Case-1) R0=5.09 fm, a0=0.46 fm, beta2=0.162, beta3=0.00; Zr (Case-2) R0=5.02 fm, a0=0.52 fm, beta2=0.06, beta3=0.20

## Context

This work extends the program of using isobaric collisions to constrain nuclear structure, following prior studies by STAR (2021) and others (Li 2018, Li 2019, Xu 2021, Jia 2022) that focused on elliptic flow (v2) and triangular flow (v3) ratios for the same isobar pair. The authors build on the understanding that v1^even arises from initial-state fluctuations and hydrodynamic expansion (Luzum 2010, Teaney 2010, Gardim 2011) and is less sensitive to viscous effects than higher harmonics (Retinskaya 2012, STAR 2018). The results enable future experimental measurements by the STAR experiment at RHIC, which collected 1.2 billion minimum-bias events per system in 2018, to distinguish nuclear structure differences at the sub-percent level in anisotropic flow observables.
