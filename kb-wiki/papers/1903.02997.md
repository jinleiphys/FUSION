# Effect of in-medium mass-shift on transverse-momentum spectrum and elliptic anisotropy of $ϕ$ meson

> We study the effect of in-medium mass-shift on transverse-momentum spectrum and elliptic anisotropy of $ϕ$ meson. It is found that the mass-shift enhances the $ϕ$ yields and suppresses the elliptic flow $v_2$ in large momentum region, and the effects increase with the increasing mass-shift. The effects are various for different sources and decrease with the increasing expanding velocity. We further study the effects for parts of all $ϕ$ meson with mass-shift, and the effects decrease with the decreasing probability of $ϕ$ meson with mass-shift. Since the different mass-shift lead to different transverse-momentum spectrum and $v_2$, our study may provide a way to restrict the ranges of in-medium mass-shift of $ϕ$ meson in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Effect of in-medium mass-shift on transverse-momentum spectrum and elliptic anisotropy of φ meson

## Key claim

The in-medium mass-shift of the φ meson enhances its yield and suppresses its elliptic flow v₂ at large transverse momentum, with the magnitude of these effects increasing for larger mass-shifts and decreasing for higher source expansion velocities; the study suggests that comparing simulated transverse-momentum spectra and v₂ to experimental data can constrain the allowed range of in-medium mass-shift, but only if the probability of φ mesons experiencing a mass-shift is also taken into account.

## Method

The authors used ideal (2+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics with Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion to model the transverse expansion of the φ meson emission source at zero net baryon density. The single-particle momentum distribution for bosons with in-medium mass-shift was computed using a Bogoliubov transformation relating free-particle and in-medium creation/annihilation operators, integrated over the freeze-out hypersurface with the s95p-PCE equation of state and a Gaussian initial energy density distribution. The freeze-out temperature was fixed at 140 MeV, and the initial source parameters were set to Rx = 3 fm, Ry = 4 fm with two initial energy densities (ε₀ = 20 and 40 GeV/fm³).

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature of φ meson: 140 MeV
- Initial source radii: Rx = 3 fm, Ry = 4 fm
- Initial energy densities studied: ε₀ = 20 GeV/fm³ and ε₀ = 40 GeV/fm³
- For ε₀ = 20 GeV/fm³, the effect of mass-shift on the transverse-momentum spectrum is larger than for ε₀ = 40 GeV/fm³ at the same mass-shift value
- The v₂ suppression effect for ε₀ = 20 GeV/fm³ is "a little stronger" than for ε₀ = 40 GeV/fm³ at fixed mass-shift
- The ratio of the average coefficient F₂ to the average occupation number n'ₖ' increases with increasing transverse momentum, driving the mass-shift effect at high pT

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of φ meson production in heavy-ion collisions (Shor 1985; STAR 2007; PHENIX 2007; ALICE 2015) by incorporating an in-medium mass-shift that had been predicted by several theoretical calculations (Hatsuda; Song; Smith 1998; Lin 2002; Cabrera 2003; Metag). The study uses the Cooper-Frye formalism with Bogoliubov-transformed operators (Asakawa and Csörgő 1996; Padula 2006) and the s95p-PCE equation of state (Shen 2010). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and LHC to constrain the magnitude of the φ meson mass-shift in the hadronic medium, addressing the open question raised by ALICE (2015) about whether the φ meson has a larger hadronic cross section than currently estimated.
