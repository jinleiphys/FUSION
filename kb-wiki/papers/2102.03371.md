# Determining the leading-order contact term in neutrinoless double $\boldsymbolβ$ decay

> We present a method to determine the leading-order (LO) contact term contributing to the $nn \to pp e^-e^-$ amplitude through the exchange of light Majorana neutrinos. Our approach is based on the representation of the amplitude as the momentum integral of a known kernel (proportional to the neutrino propagator) times the generalized forward Compton scattering amplitude $n(p_1) n(p_2) W^+ (k) \to p(p_1^\prime) p(p_2^\prime) W^- (k)$, in analogy to the Cottingham formula for the electromagnetic contribution to hadron masses. We construct model-independent representations of the integrand in the low- and high-momentum regions, through chiral EFT and the operator product expansion, respectively. We then construct a model for the full amplitude by interpolating between these two regions, using appropriate nucleon factors for the weak currents and information on nucleon-nucleon ($N\! N$) scattering in the $^1S_0$ channel away from threshold. By matching the amplitude obtained in this way to the LO chiral EFT amplitude we obtain the relevant LO contact term and discuss various sources of uncertainty. We validate the approach by computing the analog $I = 2$ $N\! N$ contact term and by reproducing, within uncertainties, the charge-independence-breaking contribution to the $^1S_0$ $N\! N$ scattering lengths. While our analysis is performed in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, we express our final result in terms of the scheme-independent renormalized amplitude ${\cal A}_ν(|{\bf p}|,|{\bf p}^\prime|)$ at a set of kinematic points near threshold. We illustrate for two cutoff schemes how, using our synthetic data for ${\cal A}_ν$, one can determine the contact-term contribution in any regularization scheme, in particular the ones employed in nuclear-structure calculations for isotopes of experimental interest.

## Key claim

The leading-order contact term in the $nn \to pp e^-e^-$ amplitude from light Majorana neutrino exchange can be determined by matching a "full" amplitude constructed via a Cottingham-like integral representation to the chiral EFT amplitude, with the caveat that the method relies on interpolating between model-independent low- and high-momentum regions using phenomenological input, and is validated at the 30% accuracy level by reproducing charge-independence-breaking in $NN$ scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors represent the $nn \to pp$ amplitude as an integral over neutrino three-momentum of a kernel proportional to the neutrino propagator times the generalized forward Compton scattering amplitude $n(p_1) n(p_2) W^+(k) \to p(p_1^\prime) p(p_2^\prime) W^-(k)$, analogous to the Cottingham formula for electromagnetic mass shifts. They construct model-independent integrands for low momentum (using chiral EFT) and high momentum (using the operator product expansion), then interpolate between these regions using nucleon weak form factors and $NN$ scattering information in the $^1S_0$ channel. By matching this full amplitude to the LO chiral EFT amplitude, they extract the LO contact term, and validate the approach by computing the analogous $I=2$ $NN$ contact term and comparing to charge-independence-breaking in $^1S_0$ scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- The elastic (pole) contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is $m_{p-n}^\text{el} = 0.75(2)\ \text{MeV}$, while the inelastic contribution from lattice QCD is $m_{p-n}^\text{inel} = 0.28(11)\ \text{MeV}$ and from nucleon structure functions is $m_{p-n}^\text{inel} = -0.17(16)\ \text{MeV}$, indicating elastic estimates are accurate at the 30% level.
- The separation scale $\Lambda$ between low/intermediate and high-momentum regions is taken as $\Lambda \gtrsim 1.5\ \text{GeV}$, with the chiral EFT breakdown scale $\Lambda_\chi \lesssim 1\ \text{GeV}$ and the Fermi momentum $k_F \sim 100\ \text{MeV}$.
- The asymptotic behavior of the integrand at large $|\mathbf{k}|$ scales as $d^3\mathbf{k} / |\mathbf{k}|^5$, ensuring the amplitude is finite.

## Context

This work extends the Cottingham formula approach, previously applied to pion and nucleon electromagnetic mass differences (Ecker 1988, Gasser 1974, Gasser 2015), to the two-nucleon system for neutrinoless double beta decay. It addresses the need for the leading-order contact term in the $^1S_0$ channel identified in chiral EFT (Cirigliano 2018, Cirigliano 2019), which is required for renormalization and is amplified in nuclear matrix elements due to cancellations between $^1S_0$ and higher partial waves. The method enables determination of this contact term in any regularization scheme, including those used in nuclear-structure calculations for experimentally relevant isotopes, and is validated against charge-independence-breaking in $NN$ scattering lengths.
