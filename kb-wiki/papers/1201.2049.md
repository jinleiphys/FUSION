# Local-Duality QCD Sum Rules for Pion Elastic and (pi^0, eta, eta') to gamma gamma* Transition Form Factors Revisited

> The local-duality formulation of QCD sum rules allows for the prediction of hadronic form factors without knowledge of the subtle details of their structure. With the aid of this formalism, we take a fresh look at the behaviours of the charged-pion elastic form factor and of the form factors entering in the transitions of the ground-state neutral unflavoured pseudoscalar mesons pi^0, eta, eta' to one real and one virtual photon within a broad range of momentum transfers Q^2. The uncertainties induced by the approximations inherent to this local-duality approach are estimated by studying, in parallel to QCD, quantum-mechanical potential models, where the exact form factors, obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation, may be compared with the corresponding local-duality sum-rule results. For Q^2 larger than 5-6 GeV^2, we judge the predictions of the simplest local-duality model to be reliable and expect their accuracy to improve very fast with increasing Q^2. The large-Q^2 prediction for the pion elastic form factor should be approached already at moderate momentum transfer squared of approximately 4-8 GeV^2; large deviations from its local-duality behaviour for Q^2 = 20-50 GeV^2, predicted by some hadron-structure models, seem rather unlikely. The (eta, eta') to gamma + gamma* form factors deduced from the simplest local-duality approach exhibit excellent agreement with experiment. In startling contrast, BaBar measurements of the pi^0 to gamma + gamma* form factor imply local-duality violations which even rise with Q^2.

## Key claim

The simplest local-duality (LD) model, which uses constant effective thresholds fixed by asymptotic large-\(Q^2\) behaviour, yields reliable predictions for hadronic form factors for \(Q^2 \ge 5\text{–}6\ \text{GeV}^2\), with accuracy improving rapidly as \(Q^2\) increases; however, the BaBar data for the \(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma^*\) transition form factor show a violation of local duality that grows with \(Q^2\), in stark contrast to the successful LD description of the \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) transition form factors.

## Method

The authors employ local-duality QCD sum rules for three-point correlators: a double spectral density for the pion elastic form factor \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) and a single spectral density for the transition form factors \(F_{P\gamma}(Q^2)\) (\(P = \pi^0, \eta, \eta'\)). The effective thresholds \(s_{\text{eff}}(Q^2)\) and \(\bar s_{\text{eff}}(Q^2)\) are taken as constants equal to their asymptotic values \(4\pi^2 f_\pi^2 \approx 0.671\ \text{GeV}^2\). To estimate the uncertainties of this approximation, the authors also study quantum-mechanical potential models with confining and Coulomb interactions, comparing exact Schrödinger-equation form factors with the corresponding LD sum-rule results for several power-law potentials.

## Key numbers

- Charged-pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 130\ \text{MeV}\).
- Asymptotic effective threshold for both elastic and transition form factors: \(4\pi^2 f_\pi^2 \approx 0.671\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For the pion elastic form factor, the LD model is expected to be applicable for \(Q^2 \ge 4\text{–}8\ \text{GeV}^2\), with high precision reached for \(Q^2 \ge 20\text{–}30\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For the \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) transition form factors, the non-strange and strange decay constants used are \(f_n \approx 1.07 f_\pi\) and \(f_s \approx 1.36 f_\pi\); the strange quark mass is taken as \(m_s = 100\ \text{MeV}\).
- The BaBar data for \(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma^*\) show a linear rise of the equivalent effective threshold with \(Q^2\) up to at least \(40\ \text{GeV}^2\), with no sign of approaching the LD limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of the pion elastic form factor (Balakireva, Lucha, Melikhov, 2011) and the \(P \to \gamma\gamma^*\) transition form factors (Lucha, Melikhov, 2011) by providing a unified retrospective and a quantum-mechanical validation of the LD approach. The results contradict predictions from some hadron-structure models that claim large deviations from LD behaviour at \(Q^2 = 20\text{–}50\ \text{GeV}^2\). The puzzling BaBar data for \(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma^*\) are consistent with the findings of Roberts (2009) and Mikhailov (2009), and the authors note that future precise measurements from JLab after the 12 GeV CEBAF upgrade may help resolve the controversy.
