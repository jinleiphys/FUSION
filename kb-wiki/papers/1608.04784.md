# Revealing the source of the radial flow patterns in proton--proton collisions using hard probes

> In this work, we propose a tool to reveal the origin of the collective-like phenomena observed in proton--proton collisions. We exploit the fundamental difference between the underlying mechanisms, color reconnection (CR) and hydrodynamics, which produce radial flow patterns in \textsc{Pythia}~8 and \textsc{Epos}~3, respectively. Specifically, we proceed by examining the strength of the coupling between the soft and hard components which, by construction, is larger in \textsc{Pythia}~8 than in \textsc{Epos}~3. We study the transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) distributions of charged pions, kaons and (anti)protons in inelastic pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 7\,TeV produced at mid-rapidity. Specific selections are made on an event-by-event basis as a function of the charged particle multiplicity and the transverse momentum of the leading jet ($p_{\rm T}^{\rm jet}$) reconstructed using the \textsc{FastJet} algorithm at mid-pseudorapidity ($|η|<1$). From our studies, quantitative and qualitative differences between \textsc{Pythia}~8 and \textsc{Epos}~3 are found in the $p_{\rm T}$ spectra when (for a given multiplicity class) the leading jet $p_{\rm T}$ is increased. In addition, we show that for low-multiplicity events the presence of jets can produce radial flow-like behavior. Motivated by our findings, we propose to perform a similar analysis using experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that by examining the coupling strength between soft and hard components in proton-proton collisions, which is larger in Pythia 8 (color reconnection) than in Epos 3 (hydrodynamics), one can distinguish whether radial flow patterns originate from color reconnection or hydrodynamic evolution, though neither model fully describes the experimental data quantitatively.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 100 million inelastic proton-proton collision events at sqrt(s)=7 TeV using Pythia 8.212 (with and without color reconnection) and Epos 3.117 (with and without hydrodynamics). They classified events by charged particle multiplicity at mid-pseudorapidity and by the transverse momentum of the leading jet reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm in FastJet 3.1.3 with cone radius 0.4 and minimum jet pT of 5 GeV/c within |eta|<1. They then compared the proton-to-pion ratios and performed simultaneous blast-wave model fits to the pT spectra of charged pions, kaons, and (anti)protons across different multiplicity and jet pT classes.

## Key numbers

- Average mid-rapidity particle density for inelastic pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: <dNch/deta> = 5.505
- For Pythia 8, going from <dNch/deta>=2.12 to 46.1, the average leading jet pT ranges from 7.09 GeV/c up to 19.7 GeV/c
- For the highest multiplicity class (5<z<6), more than 90% of events contain jets with pTjet>5 GeV/c
- For low-multiplicity events (0<z<1) in Pythia 8 without jets: chi2/n.d.f = 3.28 (with CR) and 4.65 (without CR); with jets (20<pTjet<25 GeV/c): chi2/n.d.f = 1.51 (with CR) and 1.09 (without CR)
- For high-multiplicity events (5<z<6) in Pythia 8 without jets: chi2/n.d.f = 1.01 (with CR) and 2.36 (without CR); with jets (20<pTjet<25 GeV/c): chi2/n.d.f = 0.19 (with CR) and 1.31 (without CR)
- The core region in Epos 3 originates around 30% of central particle production for average pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, reaching approximately 75% for <dNch/deta>~20.8

## Context

This work extends studies of collective-like phenomena in small collision systems at the LHC, where radial flow signals (Adam 2016), long-range angular correlations (Khachatryan 2016), and strangeness enhancement (Adam 2016) have been observed. It builds on earlier findings that color reconnection in Pythia produces radial flow patterns via boosted color strings (Ortiz 2013) and that hydrodynamic calculations reproduce many observations qualitatively (Bozek 2013). The proposed differential analysis using both multiplicity and jet pT selection provides a more powerful tool than multiplicity-only studies for distinguishing between color reconnection and hydrodynamic mechanisms, and the authors suggest applying this analysis to experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
