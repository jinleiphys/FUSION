# Modifying the pion mass in the loosely bound Skyrme model

> We study the loosely bound Skyrme model with the addition of two different pion mass terms; this is the most general potential of polynomial form up to second order in the trace of the Skyrme field. The two pion mass terms are called the standard pion mass term and the modified pion mass term. We find that the binding energies are not reduced by the introduction of the modified pion mass, but it is analogous to the standard pion mass term with a decrease in the value of the mass parameter of the loosely bound potential (for large values of the latter parameter). We find by increasing the overall pion mass that we can reduce the classical binding energy of the 4-Skyrmion to the 2.7% level and the total binding energy including the contribution from spin/isospin quantization is reduced to the 5.8% level.

## Key claim

By adding the most general polynomial potential up to second order in the trace of the Skyrme field, the authors find that the modified pion mass term does not reduce binding energies compared to the standard pion mass term; instead, it merely acts like a decrease in the mass parameter of the loosely bound potential, and the overall pion mass must be increased to achieve lower binding energies while preserving the cubic symmetry of the 4-Skyrmion.

## Method

The authors performed numerical relaxation of the Skyrme field equations on a 101^3 grid using fourth-order finite differences, solving the hedgehog ODE for the 1-Skyrmion and a full 3D PDE for the 4-Skyrmion. They scanned the parameter space of the potential V = m_pi^2 (1 - sigma) + (1/2) m_2^2 (1 - sigma)^2, where the effective mass parameter m_2^2 incorporates both the loosely bound term and the modified pion mass term, and calibrated each solution to the mass and size of ^4He.

## Key numbers

* Classical binding energy of the 4-Skyrmion reduced to 2.7% for m_pi = 0.5 at the largest m_2^2 preserving cubic symmetry
* Total binding energy including spin/isospin quantization reduced to 5.8% for the same parameter point
* Pion mass parameter values studied: m_pi = 0.125, 0.25, 0.375, 0.5 (dimensionless)
* The value m_pi = 0.25 corresponds to the choice m = 1 in Battye (2006) and Manko (2007)
* Numerical precision of baryon charge for 4-Skyrmion solutions: about 1.7 x 10^{-3} or better
* Energy accuracy for the 1-Skyrmion: about 2.7 x 10^{-4} or better

## Context

This work extends the loosely bound Skyrme model introduced by Gudnason (2016) by adding the most general polynomial potential up to second order in sigma, which includes both the standard linear pion mass term and the modified quadratic pion mass term from Piette (1997) and Kudryavtsev (1999). The study enables systematic comparison of binding energies, pion decay constants, and mass spectra across the full parameter space, showing that increasing the overall pion mass allows larger values of the loosely bound potential parameter and thus lower binding energies while preserving the cubic symmetry of the 4-Skyrmion that is essential for nuclear clustering models.
