# Frame dependence of form factors in light-front dynamics

> In light-front dynamics, form factors are traditionally computed with the "good current" $J^+$ within the Drell-Yan frame $q^+=0$. Due to truncations imposed in practical calculations, the from factor may acquire frame dependence, which is often neglected. In this work, we explore the form factors in more general frames, preserving the boost covariance. We find the frame dependence of the elastic form factors for mesons is small in basis light-front holography and related models with two-body Fock space truncation. We suggest to use the difference between form factor results from Drell-Yan frame and the "longitudinal frame" as a metric for the violation of the Lorentz symmetry due to Fock space truncation.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the frame dependence of elastic form factors for mesons is small in basis light-front holography and related models with two-body Fock space truncation, but this conclusion is explicitly qualified to heavy quarkonia (charmonium and bottomonium) and does not extend to light mesons like the pion, where frame dependence is especially large.

## Method

The authors compute elastic electromagnetic form factors for (pseudo-)scalar mesons using light-front wave functions (LFWFs) obtained from basis light-front holography (BLFQ), which combines light-front holographic QCD with one-gluon exchange. They evaluate form factors in a general frame parametrized by two boost invariants (z and Δ⊥) rather than only the traditional Drell-Yan frame (q⁺=0), and compare results across different frames including the "longitudinal frame" (Δ⊥=0). The calculations are performed with basis truncations Nmax = Lmax = 8, 16, and 32 for heavy quarkonia, and with leading basis functions for light mesons.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium ηc, the form factor shows moderate frame dependence with basis truncation Nmax = Lmax = 32; the difference between Drell-Yan and longitudinal frames characterizes the spread.
- For bottomonium ηb, the frame dependence is smaller than for charmonium, consistent with bottomonia being less relativistic.
- For the pion, the mean-square radius in the Drell-Yan frame scales as r²π ∝ κ⁻² ln(M²π/κ²) + O(M²π/κ²), while in the longitudinal frame r²π ∝ κ²/M⁴π + O(M²π/κ²), where κ = 0.54 GeV is the confining scale and Mπ is the pion mass.
- Light meson calculations use quark masses mq = 46 MeV and ms = 356 MeV with confining scale κ = 0.54 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of frame dependence in light-front dynamics, which was previously studied by Isgur (1988) and Sawicki (1992) for specific models, and by Karmanov and collaborators in covariant light-front dynamics. The authors propose using the difference between Drell-Yan frame and longitudinal frame results as a metric for Lorentz symmetry violation due to Fock space truncation. The paper enables future work on implementing dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in light-front models, which is identified as essential for a self-consistent description of the pion.
