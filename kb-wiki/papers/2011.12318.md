# Non-dissipative second-order transport, spin, and pseudo-gauge transformations in hydrodynamics

> We derive a set of nontrivial relations between second-order transport coefficients which follow from the second law of thermodynamics upon considering a regime close to uniform rotation of the fluid. We demonstrate that extension of hydrodynamics by spin variable is equivalent to modifying conventional hydrodynamics by a set of second-order terms satisfying the relations we derived. We point out that a novel contribution to the heat current orthogonal to vorticity and temperature gradient reminiscent of the thermal Hall effect is constrained by the second law.

# Non-dissipative second-order transport, spin, and pseudo-gauge transformations in hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The authors derive five constraints relating seven second-order transport coefficients from the second law of thermodynamics, reducing the number of independent coefficients to two free thermodynamic functions; this equivalence holds only under a specific power-counting hierarchy where vorticity and temperature gradients are larger than dissipative gradients, and the constraints apply to parity-even plasmas.

## Method

The authors introduce a power-counting scheme where vorticity and temperature gradients are treated as larger than dissipative gradients (shear, expansion, and gradients of chemical potential over temperature), allowing them to isolate vorticity-related second-order terms as the leading contributions to entropy production. They write general parity-even constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor, charge current, and entropy current with seven second-order transport coefficients, then compute the entropy production rate up to order ωβε (vorticity times temperature gradient times dissipative gradient). The second law requires each of five independent terms in the entropy production to vanish, yielding five constraints. Separately, they show that ideal spin hydrodynamics, when transformed via pseudo-gauge transformations to conventional hydrodynamics, produces the same set of constrained second-order transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Five constraints (C^(1) through C^(5)) are derived from the second law, reducing seven second-order transport coefficients {a_i, b_i, c_i} to two free functions (a_0 and a_1).
- The spin susceptibility χ appears as a thermodynamic function; in the conformal case, the relation a_1 = 3a_0 holds, and the spin density is S^{ij} = ω^{ij}/(24ħβ²) + O(ħ⁰) from chiral kinetic theory.
- The vorticity-driven heat current in the no-drag frame is q^{i} = ω^{ij}∂_jβ/(48ħβ³) + O(ħ⁰), derived from chiral kinetic theory at order ħ².
- The spin density in local equilibrium is S^{μν} = χω^{μν}, with χ identified as the spin susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends prior constraints on second-order transport coefficients found for chargeless fluids (Bhattacharyya 2012, Jensen 2012, Banerjee 2012) and charged fluids (Bhattacharyya 2014), but finds more stringent constraints (two free parameters versus four). The results reconcile spin hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2017, Hattori 2019, Gallegos 2020) with conventional second-order hydrodynamics via pseudo-gauge transformations (Becattini 2012, 2018; Florkowski 2018; Speranza 2020). The work enables systematic classification of non-dissipative second-order transport and clarifies the meaning of spin densities in hydrodynamic descriptions, with implications for heavy-ion collision phenomenology (STAR 2017) and condensed matter systems.
