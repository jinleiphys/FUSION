# The role of chiral symmetry in two-pion exchange nuclear potential

> We evaluate the two pion exchange contribution to the nucleon-nucleon potential in configuration space using a "minimal chiral model" containing only pions and nucleons. We argue that this model has nowadays a rather firm conceptual basis, which entitles it to become a standard ingredient of any modern potential. The main features of this model is that the scalar-isoscalar component of the interaction is relatively small, due to cancellations between large terms, and fails to reproduce the intermediate range attraction in the central channel. We show that chiral symmetry is the responsible for these large cancellations in the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction, which are similar to those occuring in free pion-nucleon scattering. Another feature of the model is that these results do not depend on how chiral symmetry is implemented.

## Key claim

The authors argue that chiral symmetry, implemented in a "minimal chiral model" containing only pions and nucleons, is responsible for large cancellations in the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential that prevent the scalar-isoscalar component from reproducing the intermediate range attraction in the central channel; these cancellations are similar to those occurring in free pion-nucleon scattering and do not depend on how chiral symmetry is implemented.

## Method

The authors evaluated the two-pion exchange contribution to the nucleon-nucleon potential in configuration space using a minimal chiral model with only pions and nucleons, based on a Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. They calculated contributions from five loop diagrams (box, crossed box, triangle, and bubble) and parametrized the resulting central, spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor potentials as functions of the dimensionless variable x = μr, where μ is the pion mass. They then computed phase shifts for singlet waves (^1D_2, ^1G_4, ^1F_3, ^1H_5) using the variable phase method and decomposed the contributions into long-range OPEP, iterated OPEP, pseudoscalar (box and crossed box), and chiral (triangle and bubble) components.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass μ = 137.29 MeV and pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV were adopted for the parametrization
- Axial πN coupling constant g_A = 1.33 was used in the phase shift calculations
- The parametrized expressions reproduce the complete theoretical calculations to within 0.25% except for a few cases and regions
- For the ^1F_3 and ^1H_5 waves, the net result is given just by the long-OPEP contribution, with the relationship δ_ℓ)πI + δ_ℓ)PS = -δ_ℓ)S holding, indicating complete cancellation of medium range contributions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of the two-pion exchange potential (da Rocha and Robilotta, 1994; 1995) and builds on the framework of chiral perturbation theory developed by Ordonez and van Kolck (1992) and Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994). The paper shows that the minimal chiral model fails to reproduce experimental phase shifts for lower partial waves like ^1D_2, but works reasonably for higher waves like ^1G_4, ^1F_3, and ^1H_5. The authors note that including the Δ resonance (as done by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck, 1994) or incorporating empirical πN subthreshold coefficients from Hohler, Jacob, and Strauss (1972) can improve the model's predictive power.
