# Neutron Dark Decay in Neutron Stars: The Role of the Symmetry Energy

> We conduct a systematic investigation of the influence of the nuclear symmetry energy on the proposed neutron decay into dark matter particles within the cores of neutron stars. Unlike the majority of previous studies that considered only pure neutron matter, the present analysis is extended to encompass $β$-stable nuclear matter. Furthermore, in relation to previous studies, the interactions between dark matter and baryons are incorporated and systematically studied regarding their effect on the structure of neutron stars. Our findings indicate that the nuclear symmetry energy plays a critical role in shaping the total equation of state (EoS) for dense neutron star matter containing dark sector components. The strength of interactions among dark matter particles, as well as between dark matter and baryons, is shown to be pivotal in determining both the composition and the macroscopic properties of neutron stars. The concurrent tuning of interaction strengths alongside the symmetry energy parameters may facilitate a more accurate reproduction of recent observational data relevant to neutron star properties. In any case, the extent to which the proposed dark decay of the neutron is affected by the extreme conditions prevailing in the interior of neutron stars remains an open problem.

## Key claim
The nuclear symmetry energy plays a critical role in shaping the equation of state for neutron star matter containing dark sector components from neutron dark decay, but the extent to which the proposed dark decay of the neutron is affected by the extreme conditions in neutron star interiors remains an open problem.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state for beta-stable nuclear matter including neutrons, protons, electrons, and dark matter fermions from the decay channel n -> chi + phi, using a parametrized nuclear symmetry energy with the combined parameter eta = (K0 L^2)^(1/3). They solved the chemical equilibrium conditions and TOV equations for various combinations of dark matter self-interaction strength (parameter z_chi) and baryon-dark matter interaction strength (parameters z_chi n and z_chi p), with the dark matter modeled as a repulsively interacting Yukawa-type fermion gas.

## Key numbers
- The dark matter particle mass was fixed at m_chi = 938 MeV with m_phi = 0, satisfying the nuclear stability constraint 937.993 MeV < m_chi + m_phi < 939.565 MeV
- The parameter eta ranged from 70.6 MeV to 147.5 MeV, corresponding to K0 values from 220 to 256 MeV and L values from 40 to 112 MeV
- Dark matter self-interaction parameter z_chi values studied: 10 MeV (very strong), 25 MeV, 50 MeV, and 100 MeV
- Baryon-dark matter interaction parameters z_chi n = z_chi p values studied: 50 MeV, 70 MeV, 235 MeV, and 250 MeV
- The saturation density was taken as n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, with E0 = -16 MeV and J = 30 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutron dark decay in neutron stars (Fornal and Grinstein 2018, Baym 2018, Grinstein 2019, McKeen 2018) by systematically incorporating the nuclear symmetry energy and considering beta-stable matter rather than pure neutron matter. It also includes baryon-dark matter interactions following the approach of Gil (2024). The results enable constraints on dark sector interaction strengths from neutron star observations and open questions about how extreme neutron star conditions (pressure, density, temperature) affect the neutron dark decay process itself.
