# Entropy flow of a perfect fluid in (1+1) hydrodynamics

> Using the formalism of the Khalatnikov potential, we derive exact general formulae for the entropy flow dS/dy, where y is the rapidity, as a function of temperature for the (1+1) relativistic hydrodynamics of a perfect fluid. We study in particular flows dominated by a sufficiently long hydrodynamic evolution, and provide an explicit analytical solution for dS/dy. We discuss the theoretical implications of our general formulae and some phenomenological applications for heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors derive exact general formulae for the entropy flow dS/dy as a function of temperature for (1+1) relativistic hydrodynamics of a perfect fluid, with the condition that the Khalatnikov potential exists; the explicit analytical solution for dS/dy is provided specifically for flows dominated by a sufficiently long hydrodynamic evolution where the effect of initial flow is negligible compared to the initial pressure gradient.

## Method

The authors use the Khalatnikov potential formalism to convert the nonlinear (1+1) hydrodynamic equations into a linear second-order hyperbolic partial differential equation. They solve the Khalatnikov equation for a constant speed of sound using a Green's function approach, then derive the entropy flow through fixed-temperature hypersurfaces by expressing the proper time and space-time rapidity in terms of the Khalatnikov potential and its derivatives. For evolution-dominated flows, they consider sources initially at rest and use Laplace transform techniques to obtain the entropy flow formula.

## Key numbers

- The general entropy flow formula is given by dS/dy(y) = (s_F)/(2 T_F) [∂²_y χ(θ,y) - ∂_θ χ(θ,y)] evaluated at θ = θ_F, where T_F is the fixed temperature and s_F is the entropy density at that temperature
- For the Bjorken flow (boost-invariant case), the entropy flow is constant: dS/dy = s_F τ_F = s_0 τ_0
- For the harmonic flows, the entropy flow is given by dS/dy(y) = (√2 λ T_0 s_F)/(g T_F) exp[(g-1)/2 (θ + √(θ² - y²/g))] × |y|/√(θ² - y²/g) × (-θ - √(θ² - y²/g))^{-1/2}
- The Khalatnikov equation for constant sound speed c_s² = 1/g takes the form c_s² ∂²_θ χ + (1 - c_s²) ∂_θ χ - ∂²_y χ = 0

## Context

This work extends the seminal (1+1) hydrodynamic approaches of Landau (1953) and Bjorken (1983) by providing general analytical expressions for entropy flow that go beyond specific solutions. The Khalatnikov potential method, originally developed by Khalatnikov (1954) and used in early work by Belenkij and Landau (1956), is revived and applied to obtain exact formulae that can be used for phenomenological applications in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable systematic calculation of entropy distributions for arbitrary initial conditions in (1+1) hydrodynamics, which can be related to particle multiplicity distributions in heavy-ion experiments.
