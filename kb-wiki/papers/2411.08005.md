# Berry Curvature and Spin-One Color Superconductivity

> We explore the interplay between Berry curvature and topological properties in single-flavor color superconductors, where quarks form spin-one Cooper pairs. By deriving a new relation, we connect the topological nodal structure of the gap function in momentum space to the (nonabelian) Berry flux associated with paired quarks. This generalizes the early work by Li and Haldane [Phys. Rev. Lett. 120, 067003 (2018)] to systems with additional internal quantum numbers, such as color. In the ultrarelativistic limit, we uncover rich topological structures driven by the interplay of spin, chirality, and color. Specifically, we identify chirality-induced topological nodes in the transverse (opposite chirality pairing) polar and A phases. In contrast, the color-spin-locking phase lacks these nodes due to a nontrivial color Berry flux, which in turn induces gapless excitations with total Berry monopole charges of $\pm 3/2-$differing from conventional Weyl fermions. Our findings can be potentially extended to other fermionic systems carrying additional internal degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Li-Haldane (2018) relation between pairing monopole charge and topological nodes to systems where paired fermions carry additional internal quantum numbers like color, showing that the color contribution to Berry flux can cancel the chirality contribution and thereby eliminate topological nodes that would otherwise be expected.

## Method

The authors derive a generalized relation connecting the sum of topological numbers around gap nodes (g) to the difference in Berry monopole charges of the paired fermions (Δq = q − q′), incorporating contributions from both chirality and color degrees of freedom. They apply this relation to analyze single-flavor spin-one color superconductors in the ultrarelativistic limit, examining the polar, A, and color-spin-locking (CSL) phases for both transverse (opposite chirality) and longitudinal (same chirality) pairing channels, computing the Berry fluxes and nodal structures explicitly.

## Key numbers

- For the transverse polar phase: Δq_ch = 1, Δq_co = 0, g = 2, with nodes at the north and south poles (θ = 0 and θ = π)
- For the transverse A phase: Δq_ch = 1, Δq_co = 0, g = 2, with a single node at θ = π
- For the transverse CSL phase: Δq_ch = 1, Δq_co = −1, g = 0 (fully gapped), with gapless excitations carrying total Berry monopole charges of ±3/2
- For the transverse CSL phase: Δq_0,co = 2, where q_0,co is the color monopole charge of the gapless mode
- For the longitudinal CSL phase: gapless excitations have Berry monopole charge ±1/2 instead of ±3/2

## Context

This work extends the earlier relation by Li and Haldane (2018) for doped Weyl metals to systems with additional internal quantum numbers such as color, resolving a puzzle about why the transverse CSL phase remains fully gapped despite nonzero chirality monopole charge. The analysis reveals that transverse and longitudinal color superconductive phases with the same order parameter can be distinguished by their topological node structure and quasiparticle Berry monopole charges, and the findings may extend to ultracold alkaline-earth Fermi gases with SU(N) symmetry (Cazalilla, 2014), twisted bilayer graphene, and multi-Weyl semimetals.
