# Strong decays of nucleon and delta resonances

> We study the strong couplings of the nucleon and delta resonances in a collective model. In the ensuing algebraic treatment we derive closed expressions for decay widths which are used to analyze the experimental data for strong decays into the pion and eta channels.

# Strong decays of nucleon and delta resonances

## Key claim
The strong decay widths of nucleon and delta resonances into pion and eta channels can be described by a collective model with only two parameters (g and h) in the transition operator, plus a strong interaction radius a. However, the model fails to reproduce the large eta decay width of the N(1535)S11 resonance, suggesting this resonance may not be a conventional three-quark state but rather a quasi-bound meson-baryon S-wave resonance.

## Method
The authors used an algebraic U(7) collective model in which baryon resonances are interpreted as vibrations and rotations of an oblate symmetric top with D3h point group symmetry. They derived closed-form expressions for the spatial matrix elements of the transition operator in the large-N limit using spherical Bessel functions, folded with a distribution function that reproduces the observed dipole form of the proton electric form factor. The spin-flavor matrix elements were combined with these spatial contributions to obtain helicity amplitudes and decay widths, with the parameters g, h, and a determined by a least-squares fit to the Nπ partial widths of 3- and 4-star resonances (excluding S11 resonances).

## Key numbers
- Strong interaction radius a = 0.232 fm (same as the electromagnetic value from previous work)
- Parameter g = 1.164 GeV⁻¹
- Parameter h = -0.094 GeV⁻¹
- The distribution function g(β) = β² e^{-β/a} / 2a³ reproduces the observed dipole form for the proton electric form factor G^p_E = 1/(1 + k²a²)²
- For the collective model, the S-wave elementary partial wave amplitude W₀(k) ≈ 3h m₃ k₀ a for small ka, which suppresses eta decays due to the small value of h

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous algebraic U(7) approach (Bijker, Leviatan, 1994) that had been used to analyze mass spectra and electromagnetic couplings of nonstrange baryon resonances. It provides an alternative to nonrelativistic quark model calculations (Koniuk, Isgur, 1980) and relativized quark model calculations (Capstick, Roberts, 1993) which emphasize single-particle aspects of quark dynamics. The collective model results improve somewhat over harmonic oscillator calculations when analyzing the decay of a Delta Regge trajectory into Nπ, as compared with the harmonic oscillator model of Dalitz (1965) and the calculations of Le Yaouanc et al. (1973). The failure to reproduce the large eta width of N(1535)S11 suggests the presence of a quasi-bound KΣ-KΛ S-wave resonance, as proposed by Kaiser et al. (1995), which lies outside the conventional three-quark model space.
