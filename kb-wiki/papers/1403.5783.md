# Heavy quark master equations in the Lindblad form at high temperatures

> We derive the quantum master equations for heavy quark systems in a high-temperature quark- gluon plasma in the Lindblad form. The master equations are derived in the influence functional formalism for open quantum systems in perturbation theory. These master equations have a wide range of applications, such as decoherence of a heavy quarkonium and Langevin dynamics of a heavy quark in the quark-gluon plasma. We also show the equivalence between the quarkonium master equations in the recoilless limit and the Schroedinger equations with stochastic potential.

## Key claim

The paper derives quantum master equations for heavy quark systems in a high-temperature quark-gluon plasma that are explicitly in the Lindblad form, which guarantees complete positivity of the reduced density matrix under Markovian evolution. The derivation requires inclusion of a new term \(S_{\rm L}\) proportional to \((\dot\rho_1-\dot\rho_2)^2\) in the influence functional, which is essential for obtaining the Lindblad form and was absent in prior work by the same author (Akamatsu 2012).

## Method

The author applies the Feynman-Vernon influence functional formalism to finite-temperature QCD, taking the nonrelativistic limit for heavy quarks (keeping only color electric interactions), performing a perturbative expansion to leading order in the coupling constant \(g\), and then coarse-graining in time under the quantum Brownian motion regime (\(\tau_{\rm E} \ll \tau_{\rm S}\)). The influence functional is expanded in velocity up to order \(v^2\), yielding four terms: a potential term \(S_{\rm pot}\), a fluctuation term \(S_{\rm fluct}\), a dissipation term \(S_{\rm diss}\), and a new acceleration-squared term \(S_{\rm L}\). From this influence functional, the author derives explicit Lindblad-form master equations for a single heavy quark and for a heavy quark-antiquark system (quarkonium), identifying the Hermitian Hamiltonian \(\hat H\), Lindblad operators \(\hat L_i\), and positive coefficients \(\gamma_i\).

## Key numbers

- The condition for the quantum Brownian motion regime to apply to quarkonium bound states is \(M\alpha^2 \ll gT\); using phenomenological values \(\alpha \sim 1/4\) and \(g \sim 2\), this condition is satisfied for charmonium but is not obvious for bottomonium at lower temperature.
- The decoherence time scale for a bound state of size \(l_{\rm coh}\) is estimated as \(t_{\rm D}(l_{\rm coh},T) \sim \frac{1}{g^2T}\left(a + \frac{b}{g^2\ln(1/g)T^2 l_{\rm coh}^2}\right)\), with \(a\) and \(b\) of order \(\mathcal O(g^0)\).
- The gluon correlation time is \(\sim 1/gT\) or shorter (\(\sim 1/T\)), while the relaxation time for decoherence and color diffusion is \(\sim 1/g^2T \gg 1/gT\); the kinetic relaxation time is much longer, \(\sim M/g^4T^2\).
- The counting in perturbative and velocity expansion is: \(S_{\rm pot}, S_{\rm fluct} \sim g^2 v^0\), \(S_{\rm diss} \sim g^2 v\), and \(S_{\rm L} \sim g^2 v^2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior derivation of quantum master equations for nonrelativistic heavy quark systems (Akamatsu 2012) by adding the \(S_{\rm L}\) term to obtain the Lindblad form, which enables numerical simulation techniques such as quantum state diffusion and quantum jump methods. The paper provides a theoretical basis for the stochastic potential concept introduced earlier (Akamatsu 2011) and recently simulated (Rothkopf 2013), showing that the stochastic Schroedinger equation derived from \(S_{\rm pot}\) and \(S_{\rm fluct}\) is equivalent to the quarkonium master equations in the recoilless limit. The Lindblad-form master equations can be applied to study decoherence of heavy quarkonium and Langevin dynamics of a single heavy quark in the quark-gluon plasma, and they clarify that the complex potential alone (as calculated in lattice QCD by Rothkopf 2011) only captures one source of decoherence (between wave functions at the same point \(X\)), while the full decoherence at different points \(X\) and \(Y\) requires additional information.
