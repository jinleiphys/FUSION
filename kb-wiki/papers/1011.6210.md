# Measuring the thermalization time

> A new method of measuring the pressure anisotropy in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions is discussed. It is shown that the transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow and interferometry radii are not sensitive to the early anisotropy. We propose the directed flow as a measure of the early transverse and longitudinal pressures. Calculations indicate that the transverse and longitudinal pressures equilibrate in less than 0.25 fm/c.

# Measuring the thermalization time

## Key claim

The transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and interferometry radii are not sensitive to early-stage pressure anisotropy in heavy-ion collisions; instead, directed flow can serve as a measure of early transverse and longitudinal pressures, and calculations indicate that these pressures equilibrate in less than 0.25 fm/c.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, using a parameterization of the equation of state based on lattice QCD results. They introduced a simple exponential time-dependence for the pressure anisotropy, with the longitudinal pressure initially set to zero and decaying with a relaxation time τ_iso, and compared the resulting observables to experimental data from the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The transverse and longitudinal pressures equilibrate in less than 0.25 fm/c, with the allowed range for the isotropization time given as 0 ≤ τ_iso ≤ 0.25 fm/c.
- Simulations were performed for relaxation times τ_iso = 0 (perfect fluid), 0.25 fm/c, and 0.5 fm/c.
- The initial formation time τ_0 = 0.1–0.25 fm/c is preferred for describing femtoscopy results, based on prior work by Broniowski (2008) and Pratt (2008).
- The directed flow calculations were compared to STAR Collaboration data for Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV with centrality 5–40%.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies by Broniowski (2008) and Vredevoogd (2008) which showed that transverse observables are insensitive to longitudinal pressure variations. It contradicts the expectation that transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, or interferometry radii could reveal early-stage thermalization, and instead proposes directed flow as a sensitive probe. The results enable experimental constraints on microscopic models of initial equilibration, such as those by Rebhan (2008), Bjoraker (2000), Xu (2004), Chesler (2009), and Dusling (2010), and provide a quantitative upper limit on the thermalization time in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
