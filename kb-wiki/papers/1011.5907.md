# Gluon shadowing and hadron production in heavy-ion collisions at LHC

> The recently published first measurement of charged hadron multiplicity density at mid-rapidity $dN_{ch}/dη=1584 \pm 4 ({\rm stat.}) \pm 76 ({\rm sys.})$ in central $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 2.76$ TeV by the ALICE Experiment at LHC is in good agreement with the HIJING2.0 prediction within the experimental errors and theoretical uncertainties. The new data point is used to carry out a combined fit together with the RHIC data to reduce the uncertainty in the gluon shadowing parameter $s_{g}$ which controls the overall magnitude of gluon shadowing at small fractional momentum $x$ in HIJING2.0 model. Predictions on the centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity density at mid-rapidity with reduced uncertainty are given for $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ and 5.5 TeV. The centrality dependence is surprisingly independent of the colliding energy as compared to RHIC energy for most of centralities starting at $N_{\rm part}=50$ (100) at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ (7) TeV. However, it becomes stronger in peripheral collisions at higher colliding energies.

## Key claim

The ALICE measurement of charged hadron multiplicity density in central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV agrees with the HIJING2.0 prediction within experimental errors and theoretical uncertainties, and a combined fit with RHIC data reduces the allowed range of the gluon shadowing parameter s_g from 0.17–0.22 to 0.20–0.23, enabling predictions with reduced uncertainty.

## Method

The authors performed a combined chi-squared fit of the ALICE data point for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV together with RHIC data on the centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity density in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, using the HIJING2.0 model which employs a two-component (soft + perturbative QCD mini-jet) approach with an impact-parameter-dependent gluon shadowing parameterization. The gluon shadowing parameter s_g controls the overall magnitude of gluon shadowing at small fractional momentum x in the HIJING parameterization, and the fit narrowed its allowed range. Predictions for the centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity density at mid-rapidity were then calculated for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 and 5.5 TeV using the reduced s_g range.

## Key numbers

- ALICE measured dN_ch/dη = 1584 ± 4 (stat.) ± 76 (sys.) for the top 5% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV.
- The gluon shadowing parameter s_g was constrained to the range 0.20–0.23 by the combined fit, reduced from the previous range 0.17–0.22 allowed by RHIC data alone.
- The theoretical uncertainty in charged hadron multiplicity from gluon shadowing in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV was about 15% before the ALICE constraint.
- The centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity density becomes independent of colliding energy starting at N_part = 50 for sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV and at N_part = 100 for sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV.
- The HIJING2.0 parameterization violates the momentum sum rule by about 16%.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING2.0 model (Deng 2010) which was previously constrained only by RHIC data (Adler 2004) and uses the first LHC heavy-ion data from ALICE (Aamodt 2010) to reduce uncertainties in gluon shadowing. The resulting gluon shadowing is much stronger than that in the EPS09 (Eskola 1998) and HKN07 (Hirai 2007) parameterizations, but comparable to the parameterization by Strikman et al. The predictions enable more precise initial conditions for hydrodynamical studies of collective flow and jet quenching at LHC energies, and the nearly energy-independent centrality dependence has implications for initial eccentricity in elliptic flow studies.
