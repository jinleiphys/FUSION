# Zero range potential for particles interacting via Coulomb potential: application to electron positron annihilation

> The zero range potential is constructed for a system of two particles interacting via the Coulomb potential. The singular part of the asymptote of the wave function at the origin which is caused by the common effect of the zero range potential singularity and of the Coulomb potential is explicitly calculated by using the Lippmann-Schwinger type integral equation. The singular pseudo potential is constructed from the requirement that it enforces the solution to the Coulomb Schrödinger equation to possess the calculated asymptotic behavior at the origin. This pseudo potential is then used for constructing a model of the imaginary absorbing potential which allows to treat the annihilation process in positron electron collisions on the basis of the non relativistic Schrödinger equation. The functional form of the pseudo potential constructed in this paper is analogous to the well known Fermi-Breit-Huang pseudo potential. The generalization of the optical theorem on the case of the imaginary absorbing potential in presence of the Coulomb force is given in terms of the partial wave series.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a mathematically rigorous zero-range pseudo potential for two particles interacting via the Coulomb potential, showing that the resulting singular boundary condition at the origin includes a logarithmic term proportional to the Coulomb strength n, unlike the pure 1/r singularity of the short-range case. The authors claim this pseudo potential, when used as an imaginary absorbing potential, correctly describes electron-positron annihilation within a non-relativistic Schrödinger equation, but only if the zero-range potential formalism is used rather than a naive delta function.

## Method

The authors start from the Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for the Coulomb Hamiltonian and derive the asymptotic expansion of the wave function at the origin by explicitly computing the singular part of the Coulomb Green's function G_C(r,0,k^2+i0) using the Whittaker function representation and the irregular Kummer function expansion. From this asymptotic form, they construct the pseudo potential W(r) = δ(r) (1+n r log r)^2/(1-n r) d/dr [r/(1+n r log r)] that enforces the required boundary condition. They then apply this formalism to electron-positron annihilation by setting the coupling constant λ = ig, solve the bound state problem to find the positronium spectrum with first-order energy corrections, and derive the scattering amplitude and annihilation cross section using a generalized optical theorem expressed in terms of partial wave series.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g for the annihilation potential is given by g = -k_1 σ_0, where σ_0 = π r_0^2 (c/v), with r_0 the classical electron radius, c the speed of light, and v the incident positron velocity.
- The first-order correction to positronium energy levels due to the annihilation potential is ig (-n)^3 / (8π N^3) for principal quantum number N = 1,2,...
- The Coulomb energy levels (zero-order approximation) are z_N = -n^2/(4N^2) for N = 1,2,...
- The additional scattering amplitude from annihilation is f'(θ) = -ig/(4π) Γ^2(1+iη) e^{-πη} / [1+ig C(k)], which is angular independent.
- The parameter n = ε (2μ e^2 Z_1 Z_2)/ħ^2 with ε = ±1 for repulsive/attractive cases; for positronium ε = -1 and n < 0.

## Context

This work extends the zero-range potential concept, originally developed for short-range interactions by Fermi, Breit, and Huang (Fermi, 1936; Breit, 1947; Huang, 1957), to systems with Coulomb interaction, addressing a limitation noted by Kuperin (1988) that the standard pseudo potential form is invalid for long-range potentials. The construction enables a mathematically sound treatment of electron-positron annihilation in positron-hydrogen scattering, replacing the ad hoc Gaussian-smoothed delta function used by Ivanov (2000), Igarashi (2002, 2003), and Yamashita (2003) with a rigorous zero-range potential. The results enable future calculations of annihilation cross sections in positron-atom collisions that go beyond the perturbative Born approximation and remain valid above the positronium formation threshold.
