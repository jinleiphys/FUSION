# In-Medium Effective Axial-Vector Coupling Constant

> The axial-vector coupling constant in nuclear medium is systematically studied using chiral perturbation theory. For normal nuclear matter, the first non-trivial corrections are estimated based on the independent particle approximation taking into account nuclear correlations in nuclear medium. The one-pion-exchange (OPE) two-body interactions turn out to enhance the coupling constant by $δg_A/g_A\simeq (0.09 \sim 0.15)$. The role of the four-nucleon contact interaction, whose low-energy constants are yet to be determined, is analyzed in connection with the empirical quenching factor of $δg_A/g_A\simeq -0.2$ in the presence of the nuclear short-range correlation. Conventional approaches are discussed in the light of our result.

## Key claim

The one-pion-exchange two-body interactions enhance the axial-vector coupling constant in normal nuclear matter by δg_A/g_A ≃ (0.09 ∼ 0.15), but the empirical quenching of δg_A/g_A ≃ −0.2 is reproduced only when the four-nucleon contact interaction (whose low-energy constants are not yet determined from experiment) is included and the short-range correlation is taken into account.

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory generalized to many-nucleon systems, taking nucleons and pions as the relevant degrees of freedom and encoding Δ-isobar contributions in low-energy constants. They compute the leading non-trivial medium corrections to the Gamow-Teller operator from tree-level two-body currents at order ν = 6 − 3A, considering one-pion-exchange graphs and four-nucleon contact graphs. Matrix elements are evaluated in the independent particle approximation for symmetric nuclear matter at normal density, averaging over the direction of probed nucleons on the Fermi surface, and short-range correlations are incorporated by cutting off the Fock contribution at a radius r_c.

## Key numbers

- The one-pion-exchange contribution to δg_A/g_A from resonance exchanges (Δ, ρ, scalar meson, Roper) plus kinetic term sums to −0.521 at normal nuclear density without short-range correlation.
- With short-range correlation included, the OPE contribution to δg_A/g_A is (0.09 ∼ 0.15) depending on the cutoff radius r_c (0.7 fm to 0^+).
- The fitted low-energy constant d̂_1 ranges from 0.94 to 1.11, corresponding to a Landau-Migdal parameter g_{NΔ}' = (0.37 ∼ 0.43).
- Normal nuclear matter density is ρ ≃ 0.17 fm⁻³ with Fermi momentum k_F ≃ 1.36 fm⁻¹.
- The low-energy constants from πN scattering are c_3 = −5.29 ± 0.25 GeV⁻¹ and c_4 = 3.63 ± 0.10 GeV⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the systematic chiral perturbation theory approach to the in-medium axial-vector coupling constant, building on the framework of Weinberg (1990) for many-nucleon systems and the chiral filter phenomena studied by Park, Min, and Rho (1993) and others. The results contradict the naive expectation from conventional Δ-hole screening models (e.g., Rho, 1974) by showing that the one-pion-exchange contribution actually enhances g_A, while the quenching arises primarily from four-nucleon contact interactions encoding short-range dynamics. This enables future studies of density-dependent modifications of nucleon properties and their relation to Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991), as well as more realistic calculations incorporating surface effects and improved nuclear wavefunctions from chiral perturbation theory.
