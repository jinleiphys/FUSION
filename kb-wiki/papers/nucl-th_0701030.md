# Skyrme-HFB Calculations in Coordinate Space for the Krypton Isotopes Up to the Two-Neutron Dripline

> For axially symmetric even-even nuclei, we solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a 2-D grid in cylindrical coordinates. The Skyrme SLy4 interaction is used for the mean field and a zero-range interaction for the pairing field. After decoupling the HFB lattice equations, we obtain quasiparticle states with equivalent single-particle energies up to 100 MeV or more. We present results for the Krypton isotope chain up to the two-neutron dripline, including two-neutron separation energies, pairing gaps, and quadrupole deformations for the ground states and isomeric minima.

## Key claim

The paper claims that solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a 2-D coordinate-space grid can reliably predict ground-state properties of neutron-rich Krypton isotopes up to the two-neutron dripline, but the results are conditional on the use of the Skyrme SLy4 interaction for the mean field and a zero-range pairing force adjusted to reproduce the measured average neutron pairing gap in \(^{120}\)Sn.

## Method

The authors solve the HFB equations for axially symmetric even-even nuclei on a 2-D grid in cylindrical coordinates, using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction for the mean field and a zero-range interaction for the pairing field. The HFB lattice equations are decoupled via a unitary transformation to reduce the diagonalization problem, and quasiparticle states with equivalent single-particle energies up to 100 MeV are obtained. Production runs are carried out on parallel supercomputers using OPENMP/MPI message passing.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron dripline for Krypton isotopes: \(^{116}\)Kr is predicted to be the last stable nucleus against two-neutron emission.
* Quadrupole deformations: \(\beta_2 = 0.38\) for \(^{104}\)Kr (prolate), \(\beta_2 = -0.31\) for \(^{108}\)Kr (oblate), and \(\beta_2 = 0.1\) for \(^{114,116}\)Kr (prolate).
* Shape coexistence energy differences: for \(^{104}\)Kr and \(^{106}\)Kr, the energy difference between prolate and oblate minima is of order 10-40 keV.
* Average neutron pairing gaps for Krypton isotopes in the mass region \(A=104-118\) vary between 0.1 and 0.9 MeV, with a maximum of 0.9 MeV at neutron number \(N=76\).
* The pairing strength was adjusted to reproduce the measured average neutron pairing gap of 1.245 MeV in \(^{120}\)Sn.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB-2D-LATTICE calculations on neutron-rich Zirconium isotopes (Oberacker et al., 2005) to the Krypton isotope chain, and compares favorably with Finite Range Droplet Model calculations (Moller and Nix, 1995) and Relativistic Mean-Field calculations (Lalazissis et al., 1999) for shape transitions, though those models use BCS pairing which is unreliable near the dripline. The results enable future QRPA calculations of collective giant multipole resonances by providing high-energy continuum states up to 100 MeV.
