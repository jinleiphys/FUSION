# Two-level systems coupled to an oscillator: Excitation transfer and energy exchange

> We consider models in which two sets of matched two-level systems are coupled to a common oscillator in the case where the oscillator energy is small relative to the two-level transition energies. Since the two sets of two-level systems are coupled indirectly through the oscillator, excitation transfer from one set of two-level systems to the other is possible. In addition, the excitation energy from the two-level systems may be exchanged with the oscillator coherently, even though the oscillator energy may be orders of magnitude smaller than the two-level system transition energy. In the lossless case, we demonstrate these effects numerically, and also use an approximate diagonalization to show that these effects are expected from the model Hamiltonian. We augment the model to include loss effects, and show that loss enhances the excitation transfer effect by breaking the severe cancelation between different paths that occurs in the lossless case. We describe a simple approximate model wavefunction appropriate when the loss increases rapidly with energy. Within this model approximation, we present numerical and analytical results for excitation transfer and energy transfer rates, showing that they are greatly increased. Our study of these models is motivated in part by claims of excess heat production in electrochemical experiments in heavy water. We examine the question of whether the rates associated with this kind of model are sufficiently large to be relevant to the experimental claims. We find that consistency is possible given recent experimental results showing strong screening effects in low energy deuteron-deuteron fusion experiments in metals.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that excitation transfer and coherent energy exchange between two-level systems and an oscillator can occur even when the oscillator energy is orders of magnitude smaller than the two-level transition energies, but only under precise resonance conditions in the lossless case; the inclusion of loss dramatically increases these rates by breaking destructive interference between different quantum pathways.

## Method

The authors model two sets of matched two-level systems coupled to a common harmonic oscillator using a pseudospin Hamiltonian, with one set weakly coupled (including a Gamow factor for tunneling) and the other strongly coupled. They perform numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation for the lossless case, apply an approximate unitary rotation to diagonalize the Hamiltonian and identify second-order coupling terms, and then augment the model with a non-Hermitian loss operator derived from a Feshbach-type Brillouin-Wigner elimination of decay channels. Analytical perturbation theory and numerical calculations are used to evaluate excitation transfer and energy exchange rates in both lossless and lossy regimes.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator energy is set to \(\hbar \omega_0 = 0.03 \Delta E\) in the excitation transfer numerical demonstration (Figure 1).
- In the energy exchange demonstration, oscillator energy is \(\hbar \omega_0 = \Delta E_2 / 11\) and coupling strength is \(V_2 \sqrt{n_0} / \Delta E_2 = 0.33744\), with total oscillator quanta of \(10^8\).
- The excitation transfer numerical calculation uses 14 two-level systems in each set, with \(V_2 \sqrt{n_0} / \Delta E = 0.1\) and \(V_1 e^{-G} \sqrt{n_0} / \Delta E = 0.1\).
- The energy exchange calculation uses 8 receiver-side two-level systems, where approximately 15 oscillator quanta match one dressed two-level system transition energy.

## Context

This work extends the spin-Boson problem literature (Cibils, 1991) and Wagner's rotation method to a model with two sets of two-level systems coupled to a common oscillator, motivated by claims of excess heat production in heavy-water electrochemical experiments (2004 DoE review). The indirect excitation transfer mechanism differs from Förster-type Coulombic transfer and is analogous to indirect coupling schemes previously considered for optical excitation transfer via a microwave mode (XRL, 1994). The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental excess heat claims, suggesting consistency when strong screening effects from low-energy deuteron-deuteron fusion experiments in metals are included.
