# Space-like Electromagnetic Form Factors of Lambda- and Sigma-Baryons from Quark-Diquark Faddeev Equations

> An important goal of ongoing and future experiments is to explore spectra and transition form factors of baryons with non-zero strangeness. Of particular interest is the transition form factor$γ^{(*)} Σ^0 \rightarrow Λ$ in the time-like momentum region that can be extracted from Dalitz decays. On the road towards a theoretical description of these form factors we extend a covariant dynamical quark-diquark model for the baryon Faddeev equation to the strange-quark sector. Based on an excellent description of the mass spectrum of selected baryon octet and decuplet states and reasonable results for the nucleon form factors we determine the elastic electromagnetic form factors of $Λ$ and $Σ^+, Σ^0, Σ^-$ hyperons in the space-like region as well as the ones for the octet transition $γ^{(*)} Σ^0 \rightarrow Λ$. We discuss qualitative and quantitative features of the diquark-quark picture and compare systematically with previous results from a three-body Faddeev approach and lattice data where available.

## Key claim

The authors extend a covariant dynamical quark-diquark model for baryon Faddeev equations to the strange-quark sector, obtaining elastic electromagnetic form factors of Lambda and Sigma hyperons in the space-like region that are in reasonable agreement with lattice QCD and three-body Faddeev results, though the abstract's claim of "excellent description" of the mass spectrum is qualified by noting that five diquark mass parameters were fitted to five selected baryon states, and the resulting model uncertainty is estimated by varying those diquark masses by 5%.

## Method

The authors solve the quark-diquark Faddeev equation using parametrized quark propagators (with u/d parameters from prior nucleon studies and s-quark parameters fitted to Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions) and parametrized scalar and axial-vector diquark propagators and amplitudes. The five diquark mass parameters are fitted to the experimental masses of N(940), Delta(1232), Lambda(1116), Sigma(1193), and Omega(1672), after which the electromagnetic form factors are computed by "gauging" the Faddeev equation to ensure gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Diquark masses fitted to baryon data: M_{[ud]} = 0.667 GeV, M_{[uu],[ud],[dd]} = 0.868 GeV, M_{[ds],[su]} = 0.950 GeV, M_{[ds],[su]} = 1.066 GeV, M_{[ss]} = 1.254 GeV
- Calculated baryon masses (with 5% diquark mass variation uncertainty): N(940) = 0.939(74) GeV, Lambda(1116) = 1.116(90) GeV, Sigma(1193) = 1.193(90) GeV, Sigma(1385) = 1.377(87) GeV, Xi(1315) = 1.301(107) GeV, Omega(1672) = 1.672(111) GeV
- Constituent quark masses at zero momentum: approximately 400 MeV for up/down quarks and 700 MeV for strange quarks
- Diquark coupling constants: g_{[ud]} = 21.52, g_{[uu],[ud],[dd]} = 15.03, g_{[ds],[su]} = 17.64, g_{[ds],[su]} = 15.97, g_{[ss]} = 15.62

## Context

This work extends the quark-diquark Faddeev equation approach, previously applied to nucleons and Delta baryons by Cloet (2009), Segovia (2014), and others, to hyperons with non-zero strangeness for the first time. The results are compared with three-body Faddeev calculations by Sanchis-Alepuz (2016, 2018), lattice QCD results from the CSSM and QCDSF/UKQCD collaborations (2014), and vector-meson dominance models by Li (2021). The authors note that unlike genuine three-body Faddeev treatments, the diquark-quark picture naturally produces the mass splitting between Lambda and Sigma baryons, and they plan future work extending to the time-like momentum region for comparison with HADES FAIR-phase-0 experiments.
