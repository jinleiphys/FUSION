# Strange dibaryon resonance in the anti-KNN--piYN system

> Three-body resonances in the \bar{K}NN system have been studied within a framework of the \bar{K}NN-πYN coupled hannel Faddeev equation. By solving the three-body equation the energy dependence of the resonant \bar{K}N amplitude is fully taken into account. The S-matrix pole has been investigated from the eigenvalue of the kernel with the analytic continuation of the scattering amplitude on the unphysical Riemann sheet. The \barKN interaction is constructed from the leading order term of the chiral Lagrangian using relativistic kinematics. The Λ(1405) resonance is dynamically generated in this model, where the \bar{K}N interaction parameters are fitted to the data of scattering length. As a result we find a three-body resonance of the strange dibaryon system with binding energy, B~79 MeV, and width, Γ~74 MeV. The energy of the three-body resonance is found to be sensitive to the model of the I=0 \barKN interaction.

## Key claim

The authors find a three-body resonance in the $\bar{K}NN$ system with binding energy $B \sim 79$ MeV and width $\Gamma \sim 74$ MeV, but note that the energy of this resonance is sensitive to the model of the $I=0$ $\bar{K}N$ interaction, meaning the result is not a fixed prediction but depends on the chosen two-body input parameters.

## Method

The authors solved the $\bar{K}NN-\pi\Sigma N-\pi\Lambda N$ coupled-channel Faddeev equation (AGS form) for the $J^\pi=0^-$, $I=1/2$ strange dibaryon system, using separable two-body interactions. The $\bar{K}N$ interaction was constructed from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian with relativistic kinematics, fitting parameters to the $\bar{K}N$ scattering length and dynamically generating the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance. The S-matrix pole was located by analytically continuing the scattering amplitude onto the unphysical Riemann sheet via deformation of the momentum integration contour, then finding the energy where the kernel eigenvalue equals unity.

## Key numbers

- Three-body resonance binding energy: $B \sim 79$ MeV, width: $\Gamma \sim 74$ MeV for the $\bar{K}NN$ system with $J^\pi=0^-$, $S=-1$, $I=1/2$
- Without $\pi Y N$ Fock space and without $NN$ interaction, binding energy is about 18 MeV for the standard model (aA)
- Adding the $NN$ interaction increases binding to 25.1 MeV (relativistic model) or 22.8 MeV (nonrelativistic model)
- The $I=0$ $\bar{K}N$ scattering length used for the standard model is $a^{I=0}_{\bar{K}N} = -1.70 + i0.68$ fm, fitted to data from Mart (year not given in text)
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ pole in the standard model is at $1420 - i30$ MeV (relativistic) or $1420.1 - i30.1$ MeV (nonrelativistic)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of kaonic nuclear states by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002, 2003), who used $\bar{K}$ optical potentials and found deeply bound states like $^3_{\bar{K}}$H with $B \sim 100$ MeV, and by Dote (year not given) using antisymmetrised molecular dynamics. The present calculation improves on those approaches by fully solving the three-body Faddeev equation with explicit $\bar{K}NN-\pi YN$ channel coupling and energy-dependent $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes, rather than using a $g$ matrix or optical potential. The results can be compared with the experimental signal from the FINUDA Collaboration (2005) reporting $(B,\Gamma) = (115,67)$ MeV, and with the Faddeev calculation of Shevchenko et al. (year not given) using phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ interactions. The work enables future studies of the role of the $\Lambda(1405)$ structure in few-body kaonic nuclei and the sensitivity of three-body resonances to the $I=0$ $\bar{K}N$ interaction model.
