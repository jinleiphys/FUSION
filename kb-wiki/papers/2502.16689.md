# Mechanical properties of proton using flavor-decomposed gravitational form factors

> We investigate the mechanical properties of the proton by extracting its flavor-decomposed gravitational form factors (GFFs) using Light-Cone QCD sum rules (LCSR). These form factors encode critical information about the internal dynamics and spatial distribution of energy, momentum, and internal forces within the proton. The flavor decomposition of the quark sector indicates the role of each flavor in the proton's pressure and shear force distributions. Our results show that the up quark contributes more significantly compared to the down quark in the three conserved proton GFFs, as well as in the energy and shear force distributions. Additionally, we define the non-conserved form factor $\bar{c}^q (t)$, which takes part in the distributions of energy and pressure; the latter is essential for maintaining proton stability. Furthermore, we determine the proton's mass and mechanical radii, providing valuable insight into its internal structure and dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper extracts flavor-decomposed gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the proton's valence quarks using Light-Cone QCD sum rules, finding that the up quark contributes more significantly than the down quark to the conserved GFFs and to energy and shear force distributions, but the results are subject to uncertainties from the auxiliary parameters (Borel mass, continuum threshold, and mixing parameter) and are performed in the chiral limit with gluon contributions excluded.

## Method

The authors compute the quark-sector GFFs (A, J, D, and the non-conserved form factor \(\bar{c}\)) for up and down valence quarks separately, using Light-Cone QCD sum rules with nucleon distribution amplitudes from Braun et al. (2006) at scale \(\mu = 1\) GeV. They evaluate a two-point correlation function involving the nucleon interpolating current and the quark energy-momentum tensor, match the hadronic and QCD sides after Borel transformation and continuum subtraction, and extract the form factors for singlet (\(u+d\)) and triplet (\(u\), \(d\), \(u-d\)) flavor combinations.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass set to \(m_N = 0.94\) GeV (chiral limit, \(m_u = m_d = 0\)).
- Quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q\rangle = (-0.24 \pm 0.01)^3\) GeV\(^3\) and quark-gluon condensate parameter \(m_0^2 = 0.8 \pm 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) used for nucleon residue calculation.
- Borel parameter range: \(1~\text{GeV}^2 \leqslant M^2 \leqslant 1.5~\text{GeV}^2\).
- Continuum threshold range: \(2.25~\text{GeV}^2 \leqslant s_0 \leqslant 2.40~\text{GeV}^2\).
- Nucleon distribution amplitude parameters include \(f_N = (5.0 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-3}~\text{GeV}^2\), \(\lambda_1 = -(2.7 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-2}~\text{GeV}^2\), \(\lambda_2 = (5.4 \pm 1.9) \times 10^{-2}~\text{GeV}^2\), and several dimensionless parameters such as \(f^{d}_1 = 0.40 \pm 0.05\), \(f^{d}_2 = 0.22 \pm 0.05\), \(f^{u}_1 = 0.07 \pm 0.05\), \(A^{u}_1 = 0.38 \pm 0.15\), and \(V^{d}_1 = 0.23 \pm 0.03\).

## Context

This work extends previous LCSR studies of nucleon GFFs (Anikin 2019, Azizi 2019) by performing flavor decomposition into up and down valence quarks, complementing lattice QCD studies such as Hackett (2023) that include sea quarks and gluons. The results enable comparison with other flavor-decomposed approaches like basis light-front quantization (Nair 2024) and provide a foundation for understanding the distinct mechanical roles of individual quark flavors in the proton's pressure, shear force, and energy distributions, though the gluon contribution remains unaddressed due to the lack of mixed quark-gluon distribution amplitudes.
