# $Λ(1405)$ production in the $π^-p\to K^0πΣ$ reaction

> We discuss the mechanisms that lead to $Λ(1405)$ production in the $π^-p\to K^0πΣ$ reaction. The problem has gained renewed interest after different works converge to the conclusion that there are two resonances around the region of 1400 MeV, rather than one, and that they couple differently to the $πΣ$ and $\bar{K}N$ channels. We look at the dynamics of that reaction and find two mechanisms which eventually filter each one of the resonances, leading to very different shapes of the $πΣ$ invariant mass distributions. The combination of the two mechanisms leads to a shape of this distribution compatible with the experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed shape of the $\pi\Sigma$ invariant mass distribution in the $\pi^-p\to K^0\pi\Sigma$ reaction is not an intrinsic property of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance but depends on the reaction dynamics, specifically on which of the two poles around 1400 MeV (a lower-energy pole coupling strongly to $\pi\Sigma$ and a higher-energy pole coupling strongly to $\bar{K}N$) is preferentially excited by the production mechanism.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral unitary model for the $\pi^-p\to K^0\pi\Sigma$ reaction near threshold ($\sqrt{s}\sim 2$ GeV, $p_\pi\sim 1.7$ GeV/c). They separate the process into a tree-level production amplitude $\pi^-p\to K^0 M_i B_i$ (using meson pole and contact terms from the chiral Lagrangian at order $f^{-3}$) and a final-state interaction $M_i B_i\to \pi\Sigma$ described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels. They also include an $s$-channel $N^*(1710)$ resonance contribution, with couplings derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction and partial decay widths.

## Key numbers

* The chiral mechanism alone produces a $\pi\Sigma$ mass distribution peaking around 1420 MeV, narrower than experiment, because the $\bar{K}N$ channels dominate the tree-level amplitude (with coefficients involving $3F-D$ and $D+F$, where $F=0.51$, $D=0.75$) and couple strongly to the higher-energy pole at $z_R\sim (1426+i16)$ MeV.
* The lower-energy pole appears at $z_R=1390+i66$ MeV and couples strongly to $\pi\Sigma$; it is accessed primarily via the $N^*(1710)$ resonant mechanism, where the $K^0\pi\Sigma$ channel is favored because the energy difference $\omega_{K^0}-\omega_\pi$ is about 300 MeV.
* With parameter Set I ($M_R=1710$ MeV, $\Gamma=100$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\pi N}=15$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\pi\pi N}=65$ MeV), the resonant mechanism is weaker than the chiral terms; with Set II ($M_R\sim 1740$ MeV, $\Gamma=200$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\pi N}=40$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\pi\pi N}=100$ MeV), the coherent sum of both mechanisms yields acceptable agreement with the experimental data from Thomas (1973).
* The calculation is performed at $\sqrt{s}=2020$ MeV ($p_\pi=1690$ MeV/c in the lab frame), matching the experiment.

## Context

This work extends the finding of Jido (2003) that two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles exist with different couplings to $\pi\Sigma$ and $\bar{K}N$, and that the observed $\pi\Sigma$ mass distribution depends on the reaction. It directly calculates the production coefficients $C_i$ in the general formula $d\sigma/dM_I = |\sum_i C_i t_{i\to\pi\Sigma}|^2 p_{CM}$ (previously fitted to data by Oller (2000)) for the $\pi^-p\to K^0\pi\Sigma$ reaction, the main source of experimental $\Lambda(1405)$ data. The results enable future studies of $\Lambda(1405)$ production in other reactions (e.g., photoproduction) where the relative weight of the two poles can be tuned.
