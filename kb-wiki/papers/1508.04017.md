# Interferometry for rotating sources

> The two particle interferometry method to determine the size of the emitting source after a heavy ion collision is extended. Following the extension of the method to spherical expansion dynamics, here we extend the method to rotating systems. It is shown that rotation of a cylindrically symmetric system leads to modifications, which can be perceived as spatial asymmetry by the "azimuthal HBT" method. We study an exact rotating and expanding solution of the fluid dynamical model of heavy ion reactions. We consider a source that is azimuthally symmetric in space around the axis of rotation, and discuss the features of the resulting two particle correlation function. This shows the azimuthal asymmetry arising from the rotation. We show that this asymmetry leads to results similar to those given by spatially asymmetric sources.

## Key claim

The paper extends two-particle interferometry (HBT) to rotating, cylindrically symmetric sources, showing that rotation of an azimuthally symmetric system produces an apparent spatial asymmetry in the "azimuthal HBT" method, yielding results similar to those from genuinely spatially asymmetric sources.

## Method

The authors study an exact, rotating and expanding solution of the fluid dynamical model for heavy ion reactions, using a source with Gaussian density profiles, constant temperature T = 200 MeV, and a velocity field that includes both expansion and rotation. They calculate the two-pion correlation function for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV/nucl with impact parameter b = 0.7 b_max, then fit the correlation functions using the azimuthal HBT parametrization to extract apparent radius parameters in different detector directions.

## Key numbers

- Initial parameters at t = 0 fm/c: transverse radius R = 2.50 fm, longitudinal length Y = 4.00 fm, expansion speeds R_dot = 0.25 c and Y_dot = 0.30 c, angular velocity omega = 0.150 c/fm
- At t = 3 fm/c: R = 3.970 fm, Y = 5.258 fm, omega = 0.059 c/fm
- At t = 8 fm/c: R = 7.629 fm, Y = 8.049 fm, omega = 0.016 c/fm
- The approximate radius decrease from omega = 0 to 0.15 c/fm is 3-4%, and from omega = 0 to 0.30 c/fm is 15%
- Calculations use k = 5 fm^{-1} and detector directions k = (1,0,0)k, k^+ = (0.924,0,0.383)k, and k^- = (0.924,0,-0.383)k

## Context

This work extends the Differential Hanbury Brown and Twiss (DHBT) method previously introduced by Csernai et al. (2011) and applied to Particle in Cell Relativistic fluid dynamical model results. It builds on exact hydrodynamic solutions for rotating and expanding fireballs developed by Csorgo et al. (2014) and collaborators. The results enable experimental detection of rotation in heavy ion collisions through azimuthal HBT analysis, complementing recent experimental verification of Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization in Au+Au reactions by the STAR collaboration (2015).
