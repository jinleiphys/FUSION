# Lambda(1405) as a K-bar N Feshbach resonance in the Skyrme model

> We describe the Lambda(1405) hyperon as a Feshbach resonance of a bar-KN quasi-bound state coupled by a decaying channel of pi Sigma in the Skyrme model. A weakly bound bar-KN state is generated in the laboratory frame, while the Sigma hyperon as a strongly bound state of bar-KN in the intrinsic frame. We obtain a coupling of bar-KN and pi Sigma channels by computing a baryon matrix element of the axial current. This coupling enables the decay of the bar-KN bound state to pi-Sigma . It is shown that the Skyrme model supports the Lambda(1405) as a narrow Feshbach resonance.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model supports the Λ(1405) as a narrow Feshbach resonance, where a weakly bound \(\bar K N\) state couples to the \(\pi \Sigma\) decay channel; however, the predicted decay widths (2.3–7.4 MeV) are significantly smaller than the experimental average (50.5 MeV), though they are comparable to the expected width (~20 MeV) for the \(\bar K N\)-dominant pole.

## Method

The authors construct the \(\bar K N\) and \(\pi \Sigma\) channels using the SU(3) Skyrme model with a hedgehog soliton for the nucleon and a kaon field expanded to second order. The \(\bar K N\) bound state for Λ(1405) is treated in the laboratory frame (weak coupling scheme), while the Σ hyperon is treated as a strongly bound \(\bar K N\) state in the intrinsic frame (Callan–Klebanov approach). The coupling between channels is computed from the baryon matrix element of the axial current, derived from the Skyrme action including the Wess–Zumino–Witten term, and the decay amplitude is evaluated via an overlap integral of the kaon wavefunctions and the soliton profile.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the \(\bar K N\) state: 20.6 MeV (Set A), 32.2 MeV (Set B), 81.3 MeV (Set C), for \(F_\pi\) = 205, 186, 129 MeV respectively.
* Decay width \(\Gamma(\Lambda(1405) \to \pi \Sigma)\): 2.3 MeV (Set A), 3.3 MeV (Set B), 7.4 MeV (Set C).
* Coupling constant \(g_{\Lambda(1405) \pi \Sigma}\): 0.187 (Set A), 0.223 (Set B), 0.333 (Set C), compared to the experimental value 0.87 (or ~0.55 for the \(\bar K N\)-dominant pole).
* Kinematic inputs: \(m_{\Lambda(1405)} = 1420\) MeV, \(m_\Sigma = 1193\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(m_K = 49\) MeV, pion momentum \(|\bm{q}| = 166\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Skyrme-model studies of the \(\bar K N\) interaction (Ezoe 2016, Ezoe 2017), which generated a weakly bound \(\bar K N\) state with a binding energy of order ten MeV, identified with Λ(1405). It contrasts with the phenomenological potential approach of Yamazaki (2002) and Akaishi (2002), which predicted deeply bound \(\bar K N\) states, and with chiral models (Oset 1997, Jido 2003, Hyodo 2007) that produce a two-pole structure for Λ(1405). The result enables further study of the \(\bar K N\) system as a Feshbach resonance and its implications for kaonic nuclei and high-density matter.
