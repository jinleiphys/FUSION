# Nonlinear causality and stability of perfect spin hydrodynamics and its nonperturbative character

> Four formulations of perfect spin hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles, distinguished by their treatment of spin (classical vs. quantum) and by the underlying particle statistics (Boltzmann vs. Fermi-Dirac), are analyzed and shown to satisfy the requirements of a divergence-type theory. Moreover, for all the formulations, we define the generating functions associated with the relevant thermodynamic currents and demonstrate that the constructed hydrodynamic theory is nonlinearly causal and stable. The latter is achieved by employing the exact expressions for the distribution functions, indicating a nonperturbative character of our approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that four formulations of perfect spin hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles satisfy nonlinear causality and stability criteria, but this holds only when exact (nonperturbative) expressions for the distribution functions are used; expansions in the spin polarization tensor may violate these properties and lead to instabilities.

## Method

The authors analyze four formulations of perfect spin hydrodynamics distinguished by their treatment of spin (classical vs. quantum) and particle statistics (Boltzmann vs. Fermi-Dirac). For each case, they construct a scalar generating function from which the thermodynamic currents are derived, then compute the four-vector M^lambda = M^{lambda AB} Z_A Z_B and verify that it is future-oriented and timelike, which implies the hydrodynamic equations can be written in symmetric hyperbolic form and are therefore nonlinearly causal and stable.

## Key numbers

- The spin Casimir operator eigenvalue for spin-1/2 particles is spin^2 = 3/4
- The spin integration measure yields a degeneracy of 2: int dS = 2
- The generating function for Fermi-Dirac statistics includes a constant pi^2/6 chosen so that dF_eq(y) vanishes exponentially as y approaches infinity
- The convergence of generating function integrals imposes limits on components of the spin polarization tensor omega for given particle mass, temperature, and hydrodynamic flow, with the same applicability range for both Boltzmann and Fermi-Dirac statistics

## Context

This work generalizes the results of Abboud (2025) who demonstrated that perfect spin hydrodynamics with classical spin and Boltzmann statistics is a divergence-type theory with nonlinear causality and stability. The paper extends this analysis to quantum spin descriptions and Fermi-Dirac statistics, building on prior work by Drogosz (2024) and Bhadury (2025) on spin hydrodynamics formulations. The nonperturbative character explains why numerical simulations by Drogosz (2024) diverge when initial spin polarization tensor values are large and expansions in omega are used, and the framework is suitable for numerical simulations of spin dynamics in quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
