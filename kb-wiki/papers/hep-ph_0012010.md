# Color degrees of freedom in nuclear collisions and charmonium suppression

> In high energy nuclear collisions, the Glauber model is commonly used to evaluate $J/ψ$ suppression due to inelastic interaction with colorless bound nucleons. This requires an effective value for the $J/ψ$-nucleon absorption cross section which is larger than theoretically expected. On the other hand, multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions mediated by color exchange interactions, excite their color degrees of freedom. We investigate the importance of this effect and conclude that the related corrections are important to explain the effective value extrapolated from experiment.

## Key claim

The authors argue that color exchange processes in multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions, which excite nucleons into color octet and decuplet states, provide an important correction to the standard Glauber model treatment of J/ψ suppression; the data suggest but do not conclusively prove that charmonium is absorbed by colored nucleons in pA collisions, due to large experimental errors.

## Method

The authors use the two-gluon exchange model of Low and Nussinov to calculate inelastic cross sections for a charmonium state Ψ (a mixture of J/ψ, ψ', and χ) scattering on nucleons in different color representations (singlet, octet, decuplet). They then compute the probability distribution of nucleon color states after multiple scatterings in a nucleus by solving an evolution equation for the color density matrix of the three-quark system, and incorporate these position-dependent effective cross sections into a modified Glauber model for pA and AB collisions.

## Key numbers

- Absorption cross section for Ψ on a color singlet nucleon: 5.8 mb (calculated with α_s = 0.6, μ = 140 MeV, and a Gaussian wave function with root mean square transverse separation ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} = 0.25 fm)
- Enhancement factor δ_n for color octet nucleon: 1.35 (35% increase over singlet), giving σ^{abs}_{ΨN_8} = 7.8 mb
- Enhancement factor δ_n for color decuplet nucleon: 1.7 (70% increase over singlet), giving σ^{abs}_{ΨN_{10}} = 9.9 mb
- Effective absorption cross section on singlet nucleons including formation time effects: σ^{abs}_{ΨN_1} = 3.8 mb (for A = 208, p_Ψ = 50 GeV)
- Effective absorption cross section on color octet nucleons including formation time effects: σ^{abs}_{ΨN_8} = 5.1 mb
- Fitted effective absorption cross section from pA data (average of NA3, NA38/51, E866) without gluon corrections: 3.5 ± 0.6 mb
- Fitted effective absorption cross section from pA data including gluon anti-shadowing corrections: 5.7 ± 0.6 mb
- Charmonium formation time τ_f = (m_{ψ'} - m_{J/ψ})^{-1} = 0.3 fm
- In the statistical limit after many scatterings, approximately 16/27 ≈ 2/3 of nucleons are in octet state and 10/27 ≈ 1/3 in decuplet state

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model treatment of J/ψ suppression in nuclear collisions by incorporating color degrees of freedom excited in multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions, building on earlier work by Hüfner and Kopeliovich (1998) and Hüfner and Kopeliovich (2000) on gluon radiation contributions. The authors use the two-gluon exchange model of Low (1975) and Nussinov (1975) and compare their results with data from the NA3, NA38/51, and E866 fixed target experiments. The improved treatment provides a better baseline for future calculations of anomalous suppression in Pb+Pb collisions, enabling more precise separation of conventional nuclear effects from possible quark-gluon plasma signatures.
