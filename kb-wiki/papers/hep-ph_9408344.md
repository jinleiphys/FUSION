# The LPM effect in QCD and Radiative energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma

> The non-abelian analog of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect is investigated in perturbative QCD. Extending our previous studies, the suppression of induced soft bremsstrahlung due to multiple scatterings of quarks in the spinor representation is considered. The effective formation time of gluon radiation due to the color interference is shown to depend on the color representation of the emitting parton, and an improved formula for the radiative energy loss is derived that interpolates between the factorization and Bethe-Heitler limits.

# The LPM effect in QCD and Radiative energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The non-abelian Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect suppresses induced soft gluon radiation from multiple scatterings, with the effective formation time depending on the color representation of the emitting parton; the derived radiative energy loss formula interpolates between the factorization limit (logarithmic energy dependence) and the Bethe-Heitler limit (linear energy dependence) as a function of the dimensionless ratio ξ = r₂E/(μ²λ), where the abstract's claim of an "improved formula" glosses over the condition that the mean free path must be much larger than the interaction range (λ ≫ 1/μ) for the derivation to hold.

## Method
The authors extended their previous perturbative QCD analysis of multiple parton scatterings by considering induced gluon radiation from a spin-1/2 quark undergoing two elastic scatterings, using a static Debye-screened potential model regularized by a color screening mass μ. They computed the radiation amplitude in the A⁺ = 0 gauge, showing that target parton radiation is negligible for the energy loss in the dominant kinematic range, and derived a radiation formation factor C_m(k) that depends on the color Casimir operators C_F (quarks) and C_A (gluons). The radiative energy loss dE/dz was then obtained by integrating the gluon spectrum over the phase space restricted by the effective formation time τ_QCD = (C_A/2C₂) · (2 cosh y)/k_⊥.

## Key numbers
- The radiation formation factor for quarks in the factorization limit approaches 1/r₂ = 8/9, meaning the induced radiation from many scatterings is slightly less than from a single scattering.
- The radiation formation factor for gluons in the factorization limit approaches 1/r₂ = 2, meaning the induced radiation approaches 2 times that from a single scattering.
- The gluon intensity radiated by a gluon jet is 9/4 times higher than that by a quark jet due to the color interference in multiple scatterings.
- The mean free path for a quark in a 3-flavor plasma is λ_q⁻¹ ≈ (2πα_s²/μ²) × 4 × 7ζ(3) T³/π², where ζ(3) ≈ 1.2.
- The mean free path for a gluon in a 3-flavor plasma is λ_g⁻¹ ≈ (2πα_s²/μ²) × 9 × 7ζ(3) T³/π².
- The dimensionless parameter ξ = r₂E/(λμ²) ≈ (9/2π³) · (E/T) for both quarks and gluons.
- The average transverse momentum transfer is ⟨q_⊥²⟩ = μ² ln(3ET/2μ²).
- The color screening mass is μ² = 4πα_s T².

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Wang and Gyulassy, 1992) on the non-abelian LPM effect in QCD, clarifying the controversy in the literature on the magnitude and energy dependence of parton energy loss (Ryskin, 1992; Gavin, 1992; Soren, 1992; Brodsky, 1992; Ralston, 1992). The derived formula improves upon the authors' earlier estimate by including the full k_⊥ integration, yielding a logarithmic energy dependence absent in their previous work where only the very soft k_⊥ < μ region was considered. The results enable quantitative predictions for jet quenching as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC energies, building on the proposal by Gyulassy and Wang (1992) that high-p_T parton jets could serve as a unique probe of deconfined matter.
