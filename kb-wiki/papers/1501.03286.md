# A hybrid model approach for strange and multi-strange hadrons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> Using the VISHNU hybrid model, we calculate the multiplicity, spectra, and elliptic flow of $Λ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$ in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions. Comparisons between our calculations and the ALICE measurements show that the model generally describes the soft hadron data of these strange and multi-strange hadrons at several centrality bins. Mass ordering of elliptic flow among $π$, K, p, $Λ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$ has also been studied and discussed. With a nice description of the particle yields, we explore chemical and thermal freeze-out of various hadrons species at the LHC within the framework of the VISHNU hybrid model.

## Key claim

The VISHNU hybrid model, with MC-KLN initial conditions and a QGP specific shear viscosity of 0.16, generally describes the multiplicity, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow of strange and multi-strange hadrons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions, but the model fails to correctly describe the mass ordering of elliptic flow among protons, Lambda, and Xi, and deviations increase for peripheral collisions (60-80% centrality).

## Method

The authors used the VISHNU hybrid model, which couples (2+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) for the QGP expansion with the UrQMD hadronic transport model for the hadron resonance gas evolution, with a switching temperature of 165 MeV. They employed MC-KLN initial conditions with a tuned lambda parameter of 0.138, started hydrodynamic simulations at tau_0 = 0.9 fm/c, and used single-shot simulations with smooth initial entropy density profiles averaged over many events within specific centrality bins. The QGP specific shear viscosity was set to 0.16, and the normalization factor for initial entropy density was fixed by the charged hadron multiplicity density in most central collisions.

## Key numbers

*   Multiplicity density per participant pair for Lambda, Xi, and Omega in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions: deviations between theory and experiment are within 20% from most central to semi-peripheral collisions, increasing to 40% for Xi and 100% for Omega at the 60-80% centrality bin.
*   Baryon-antibaryon annihilations in UrQMD reduce yields by approximately 30% for Lambda, 20% for Xi, and 20% for Omega in the most central Pb+Pb collisions.
*   The switching temperature connecting hydrodynamics to the hadron cascade is set to 165 MeV, which is close to the QCD phase transition temperature.
*   The lambda parameter in the MC-KLN model was tuned to 0.138 for a better fit of the centrality dependent multiplicity density for all charged hadrons.
*   The QGP specific shear viscosity (eta/s)_QGP is set to 0.16 for MC-KLN initial conditions.

## Context

This work extends earlier VISHNU studies (Song 2013) that described pions, kaons, and protons at the LHC, now applying the same framework to strange and multi-strange hadrons (Lambda, Xi, Omega) measured by the ALICE Collaboration (Abelev 2013, Abelev 2014). The study addresses the "proton puzzle" from statistical models, where a chemical freeze-out temperature of 165 MeV over-predicts proton data at the LHC, by showing that baryon-antibaryon annihilations in the hadronic afterburner improve the description of baryon yields. The results enable future investigations of initial flow effects and improved hadronic cross sections in UrQMD to resolve the remaining issues with mass ordering of elliptic flow among baryons.
