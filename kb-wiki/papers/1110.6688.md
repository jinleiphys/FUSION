# Alpha decay chains study for the recently observed superheavy element Z=117 within the Isospin Cluster Model

> The recently observed $α$-decay chains $^{293-294}117$ were produced by the fusion reactions with target $^{249}Bk$ and projectile $^{48}Ca$ at Dubna in Russia. The reported cross-sections for the mentioned reaction are $σ=0.5(+1.1,-0.4)$pb and $σ$=1.3(+1.5,-0.6)$pb$ at $E^{*}=35MeV$ and $E^{*}=39MeV$, respectively. The Q-values of $α$-decay and the half-lives $Log_{10}T^α_{1/2}$(s) are calculated for the $α$-decay chains of $^{293-294}117$ nuclei, within the framework of Isospin Cluster Model (ICM). In the ICM model the proximity energy is improved by using the isospin dependent radius of parent, daughter and alpha particle. The binding energy $B(A_{i}, Z_{i})$ (i=1,2) of any nucleus of mass number A and atomic number Z was obtained from a phenomenological and more genaralized BW formula given by \cite{samanta02}. The calculated results in ICM are compared with the experimental results and other theoretical Macro-Microscopic(M-M), RMF(with NL3 and SFU Gold forces parameter) model calculations. The estimated values of $α$-decay half-lives are in good agreement with the recent data. The ICM calculation is in favor of the persence of magic number at N=172.

## Key claim

The Isospin Cluster Model (ICM), which incorporates isospin-dependent nuclear radii, yields alpha-decay half-lives for the recently observed superheavy element Z=117 isotopes that are in good agreement with experimental data, and the calculation supports the presence of a magic neutron number at N=172, as indicated by a maximum half-life observed at the nucleus \(^{282}111\) (Z=111, N=171).

## Method

The author calculated Q-values and logarithmic half-lives (\(\log_{10} T^{\alpha}_{1/2}\) in seconds) for the alpha-decay chains of \(^{293}117\) and \(^{294}117\) using the Isospin Cluster Model (ICM). In this model, the proximity potential is modified by employing isospin-dependent radii for the parent, daughter, and alpha particle, and the binding energies are obtained from a generalized Bethe-Weizsäcker (BW) mass formula. The calculated results were compared with experimental data and with predictions from Macro-Microscopic (M-M) and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models using NL3 and FSU Gold force parameters.

## Key numbers

- The fusion reactions producing \(^{293-294}117\) used a \(^{249}Bk\) target and a \(^{48}Ca\) projectile; cross-sections were \(\sigma = 0.5(+1.1,-0.4)\) pb at excitation energy \(E^{*}=35\) MeV and \(\sigma = 1.3(+1.5,-0.6)\) pb at \(E^{*}=39\) MeV.
- A maximum half-life was observed in the ICM(\(Q^{Expt.}\)) calculation for the nucleus \(^{282}111\) (Z=111, N=171), indicating shell effects near the proposed magic number N=172.

## Context

This work extends earlier alpha-decay studies of superheavy elements using the preformed cluster model (PCM) by incorporating isospin physics, which improved the agreement with experimental data. The calculations are compared with experimental results from Oganessian (2010) and with theoretical predictions from the Macro-Microscopic model (Sobiczewski, 2010) and RMF models (Patra, 2011; Roy, 2011). The results support the existence of a neutron magic number at N=172, a key question in the search for the next doubly magic nucleus beyond \(^{208}Pb\).
