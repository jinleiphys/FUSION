# Possible bound states of Heavy Baryonium and Heavy Dibaryon systems

> In this work, we systematically study the heavy baryonium and heavy dibaryon systems using the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder and instantaneous approximations for the kernel. Our results indicate that all the heavy baryonium systems, specifically $Λ_Q\barΛ_Q$, $Ξ_Q\barΞ_Q$, $Σ_Q\barΣ_Q$, $Ξ'_Q\barΞ'_Q$, and $Ω_Q\barΩ_Q$ ($Q=c, b$), can form bound states. Among the heavy dibaryon systems, only the $Ξ_QΞ_Q$ system with $I=0$ and the $Σ_QΣ_Q$ systems with $I=0$ and $I=1$ can exist as bound states. Additionally, the $Σ_Q\barΣ_Q$ system with $I=2$ and the $Σ_QΣ_Q$ system with $I=1$ are not deeply bound.

# Possible bound states of Heavy Baryonium and Heavy Dibaryon systems

## Key claim

All heavy baryonium systems (Λ_Q\bar{Λ}_Q, Ξ_Q\bar{Ξ}_Q, Σ_Q\bar{Σ}_Q, Ξ'_Q\bar{Ξ}'_Q, and Ω_Q\bar{Ω}_Q for Q=c,b) can form bound states, but among heavy dibaryon systems only the Ξ_QΞ_Q system with I=0 and the Σ_QΣ_Q systems with I=0 and I=1 can exist as bound states, with the Σ_Q\bar{Σ}_Q system with I=2 and the Σ_QΣ_Q system with I=1 being not deeply bound. These results depend on a free cutoff parameter α (varied from 0.3 to 8) that governs the interaction range and is of order unity.

## Method

The authors used the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation in the ladder and instantaneous approximations for the kernel, with effective chiral Lagrangians for heavy baryons (antitriplet and sextet representations) and light mesons (pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar). The interaction kernels were derived from one-meson exchange diagrams (π, η, ρ, ω, φ, σ) with monopole form factors at vertices, where the cutoff parameter was reparameterized as Λ = m + αΛ_QCD with Λ_QCD = 220 MeV. The three-dimensional BS equation was reduced to a one-dimensional integral equation via azimuthal integration and solved as a matrix eigenvalue equation using Gaussian quadrature.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 132 MeV
- The QCD scale parameter is Λ_QCD = 220 MeV
- The parameter α was varied over the range 0.3–8 to search for bound state solutions
- For the Ξ_c\bar{Ξ}_c system with I=0, bound states with binding energy 0–50 MeV exist when α ranges from 1.12 to 2.88
- For the Ξ_c\bar{Ξ}_c system with I=1, bound states with binding energy 0–50 MeV exist when α ranges from 1.32 to 3.97
- The Λ_c mass is 2286.46 MeV, the Λ_b mass is 5619.60 MeV
- The Σ_c^{++} mass is 2453.97 MeV, the Σ_b^+ mass is 5810.56 MeV
- The Ω_c^0 mass is 2695.2 MeV, the Ω_b^- mass is 6045.8 MeV
- The σ meson mass is taken as 500 MeV, the ρ meson mass is 775.26 MeV, the ω meson mass is 782.66 MeV

## Context

This work extends studies of heavy hadron molecules from tetraquark and pentaquark systems to hexaquark systems (baryonium and dibaryon), motivated by the discovery of the Y(4630) near the Λ_c\bar{Λ}_c threshold by Belle (2008) and the observation of a p\bar{p} bound state by BESIII (2023). The results are compared with prior calculations using the one-boson-exchange model (Lee 2011), chiral effective field theory (Chen 2017, Lu 2017), the quasipotential BS equation (Song 2022, Song 2023), and lattice QCD (Junnarkar 2022). The finding that Λ_cΛ_c cannot form a bound state disagrees with some models (Song 2022, Yu 2021, Lu 2017) but agrees with others (Huang 2013, Carames 2015, Lee 2011). The results enable experimental searches for these predicted heavy baryonium and dibaryon bound states at facilities such as LHCb, Belle II, and BESIII.
