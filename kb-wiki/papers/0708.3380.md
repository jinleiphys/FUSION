# Constraining relativistic models through heavy ion collisions

> Relativistic models can be successfully applied to the description of compact star properties in nuclear astrophysics as well as to nuclear matter and finite nuclei properties, these studies taking place at low and moderate temperatures. Nevertheless, all results are model dependent and so far it is unclear whether some of them should be discarded. Moreover, in the regime of hot hadronic matter very few calculations exist using these relativistic models, in particular when applied to particle yields in heavy ion collisions. In the present work we comment on the known constraints that can help the selection of adequate models in this regime and investigate the main differences that arise when the particle production during a Au+Au collision at RHIC is calculated with different models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to expectations, the particle yields from Au+Au collisions at RHIC cannot be used to discriminate between different relativistic hadronic models because all models tested produce similarly good fits to the data, due to the very low baryonic chemical potential at freeze-out.

## Method

The authors used seven different relativistic hadronic models (NL3, TM1, GM1, GM3, NLωρ, TW, and DDME1) within a mean-field approximation to compute particle yields and ratios for Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They performed a χ² fit to experimental particle ratios (including antiproton/proton, π⁻/π⁺, K⁻/K⁺, K⁰*/h⁻, and anti-K⁰*/h⁻) to extract the freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential for each model, also comparing with free Fermi/Bose gas approximations.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature for all interacting models: approximately 146.5–152.5 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential for NLWM parametrizations (NL3, TM1, GM1, GM3): ~47–48 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential for density-dependent models: 59 MeV (DDME1) and 62.5 MeV (TW)
- Free octet and octet+decuplet models gave chemical potentials 15–20 MeV smaller than interacting models
- For the NL3 parametrization with renormalized meson masses (pion and kaon mass 1.1 times vacuum mass, K* mass 0.9 times vacuum mass): χ² = 5.3, T = 149.5 MeV, μ_B = 48.75 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at freeze-out: at most 15% smaller than vacuum values; hyperon effective masses show 5–6% reduction

## Context

This work extends the statistical model analyses of Braun-Munzinger et al. (2001, 2004) for Pb+Pb at SPS and Au+Au at RHIC, and the chiral model study of Schaffner-Bielich et al. (2002), by applying a wider set of relativistic mean-field models. The authors find that RHIC data cannot discriminate between models, in contrast to the SPS regime where higher chemical potentials may provide better constraints. The paper suggests that future constraints may come from neutron star observations, neutron skin thickness measurements, or heavy ion collisions at SPS energies, and identifies needed improvements such as inclusion of strange meson mediators and the δ (a0(980)) field.
