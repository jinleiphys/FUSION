# Validity of the Generalized Density Matrix Method for Microscopic Calculation of Collective/Bosonic Hamiltonian

> Recently a procedure by generalized density matrix (GDM) is proposed for calculating a collective/bosonic Hamiltonian microscopically from the shell-model Hamiltonian. In this work we examine the validity of the method by comparing the GDM results with that of the exact shell-model diagonalization in a number of models. It is shown that the GDM method reproduces the low-lying collective states quite well, both for energies and transition rates, across the whole region going from vibrational to gamma-unstable and deformed nuclei.

# Validity of the Generalized Density Matrix Method for Microscopic Calculation of Collective/Bosonic Hamiltonian

## Key claim

The Generalized Density Matrix (GDM) method reproduces low-lying collective states of the exact shell model quite well for energies and transition rates across vibrational, gamma-unstable, and deformed regimes, but the method's accuracy is limited by the RPA frequency ω² being calculated only to leading order in 1/Ω, introducing a systematic error that shifts all GDM curves relative to exact results.

## Method

The authors map a fermionic shell-model Hamiltonian with a Q·Q residual interaction onto a bosonic Hamiltonian in collective coordinate α and momentum π, using the GDM expansion of the density matrix R₁₂ = a₂†a₁ in powers of α and π. They compare the GDM bosonic Hamiltonian results against exact shell-model diagonalization (via Lanczos method) for four schematic models with two degenerate single-particle levels, varying the selection rules of the quadrupole operator q and the asymmetry between upper and lower single-particle groups. The bosonic Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a finite physical bosonic space of up to 12 phonon states.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle levels have energies e = ±1/2, with the Fermi surface between them; the first model uses 12 particles in 24 single-particle levels (m = ±1/2, ±3/2, ..., ±11/2).
- The GDM bosonic space is truncated to {|0 ≤ n ≤ 12⟩} where |n⟩ is the n-phonon state.
- The coefficient u for the phonon creation operator is fixed by u⁴ = (Σ_{2<F<1} |r^{(10)}_{12}|²) / (Σ_{2<F<1} |r^{(01)}_{12}|²), where indices 1 and 2 run over unoccupied and occupied single-particle levels respectively.
- The third model has 10 lower single-particle levels (m = ±3/2, ..., ±11/2) and 14 upper levels (m = ±1/2, ..., ±13/2); the fourth model has 12 lower levels (m = ±1/2, ..., ±11/2) and 10 upper levels (m = ±1/2, ..., ±9/2).

## Context

This work extends the GDM procedure proposed by Jia and Zelevinsky (2012) for deriving collective bosonic Hamiltonians from shell-model Hamiltonians, which itself builds on the original GDM formulation by Kerman (1963), Belyaev and Zelevinsky, and Shtokman (1975). The method reproduces Hartree-Fock and RPA at lowest orders and fixes anharmonic terms at higher orders. The results enable microscopic calculation of collective spectra without symmetry breaking and restoration, making the approach suitable for shape fluctuation and shape coexistence problems where traditional deformed mean-field methods with subsequent angular momentum projection may fail. The authors note that generalizing the GDM code to include angular-momentum vector coupling is necessary for realistic calculations, and work on incorporating pairing correlations beyond mean field is in progress.
