# Ghostbusters: Unitarity and Causality of Non-equilibrium Effective Field Theories

> For a non-equilibrium physical system defined along a closed time path (CTP), a key constraint is the so-called largest time equation, which is a consequence of unitarity and implies causality. In this paper, we present a simple proof that if the propagators of a non-equilibrium effective action have the proper pole structure, the largest time equation is obeyed to all loop orders. Ghost fields and BRST symmetry are not needed. In particular, the arguments for the proof can also be used to show that if ghost fields are introduced, their contributions vanish.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for non-equilibrium effective field theories defined on a closed time path, the largest time equation (a consequence of unitarity that implies causality) is obeyed to all loop orders provided the propagators have the proper retarded pole structure, without requiring ghost fields or BRST symmetry.

## Method

The authors expand a local equilibrium effective field theory around a physical background (with sources set to zero) and write the perturbative Lagrangian density in terms of free quadratic terms and interaction terms, where the free Lagrangian contains retarded r-a propagators and a-r propagators. They then prove that the largest time equation holds to all loop orders by exploiting the retarded nature of these propagators, and separately show that if ghost fields are introduced via BRST symmetry, their contributions vanish identically due to the same retarded structure.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of unitarity constraints in non-equilibrium effective field theories previously studied by the same authors (Gao, Glorioso, Liu, 2017-2018) for local equilibrium processes and fluctuating hydrodynamics. The proof that ghost fields are unnecessary generalizes results known for the Langevin equation (Arnold, Gonzalez) to non-equilibrium EFTs defined on the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path, and clarifies that the BRST symmetry approach introduced in earlier work (CGL, 2017) is not required when the retarded nature of propagators is properly accounted for.
