# Impact of tensor force on β-decay of magic and semi-magic nuclei

> Effect of the tensor force on $β$?-decay is studied in the framework of the proton-neutron random-phase-approximation (RPA) with the Skyrme force. The investigation is performed for even-even semi-magic and magic nuclei, $^{34}$Si, $^{68}$, $^{78}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn. The tensor correlation induces strong impact on low-lying Gamow-Teller state. In particular, it improves the ?$β$-decay half-lives. $Q$ and $ft$ values are also investigated and compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The tensor force, when fully included in both the Hartree-Fock mean field and the random-phase-approximation residual interaction, systematically reduces the energy of low-lying Gamow-Teller 1⁺ states and improves the agreement of calculated β-decay half-lives with experimental data for magic and semi-magic nuclei where isoscalar proton-neutron pairing plays a minor role.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus random-phase-approximation (HF+RPA) calculations in spherical symmetry using Skyrme effective interactions, adding tensor force terms perturbatively to the SkO and Skx parameter sets (creating SKO_T' and using Skxta, respectively). They discretized the continuum in a box of 16.0 fm with a step of 0.1 fm, included single-particle states up to 30 MeV, and truncated the RPA model space at a particle-hole energy of 70 MeV. The β-decay half-lives, Q values, and log ft values were computed using the ratio g_A/g_V = 1.26.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³²Sn, the experimental β-decay half-life is 39.7 seconds; calculations without tensor force yield a stable or extremely long-lived nucleus, while including the tensor force brings the half-life into agreement with experiment.
- The tensor coupling constants used for SKO_T' are T = 553.7 fm⁵MeV and U = -325.7 fm⁵MeV; for Skxta they are T = 384.0 fm⁵MeV and U = 144.0 fm⁵MeV.
- The spin-orbit strength of SkO_T' was reduced by 15% when the tensor force was added.
- The s-wave time-odd Landau-Migdal parameter g₀' is 0.98 for SkO, 0.79 for SkO', and 0.51 for Skx.
- The calculated log ft values for ⁶⁸Ni with tensor force settle around 3.5; for ⁷⁸Ni and ³⁴Si they settle around 3.5–4.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent QRPA studies of β-decay (Engel 1999, Niksic 2005, Tomislov 2007) which found that low-lying GT states in closed- and sub-closed-shell nuclei remained at too high energy, leading to half-lives longer than experiment. The authors show that including the tensor force, which was absent in those earlier calculations, provides a mechanism to attract GT strength to lower energy and improve half-life predictions. The study builds on earlier demonstrations that tensor force affects magnetic dipole excitations (Cao 2009) and GT transitions in stable nuclei (Bai 2011, Bai 2012), and it enables future extension to open-shell and deformed nuclei for more reliable predictions relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis and decay-heat evaluation.
