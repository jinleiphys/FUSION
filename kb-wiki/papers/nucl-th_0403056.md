# Charged polytropic compact stars

> In this work, we analyze the effect of charge in compact stars considering the limit of the maximum amount of charge they can hold. We find that the global balance of the forces allows a huge charge (~ 10^{20} Coulomb) to be present in a neutron star producing a very high electric field (~ 10^{21} V/m). We have studied the particular case of a polytropic equation of state and assumed that the charge distribution is proportional to the mass density. The charged stars have large mass and radius as we should expect due to the effect of the repulsive Coulomb force with the M/R ratio increasing with charge. In the limit of the maximum charge the mass goes up to ~ 10 M_sun which is much higher than the maximum mass allowed for a neutral compact star. However, the local effect of the forces experienced by a single charged particle, makes it to discharge quickly. This creates a global force imbalance and the system collapses to a charged black hole.

# Charged polytropic compact stars

## Key claim

The global balance of forces in a compact star allows a huge net charge (up to ~10^20 Coulomb) to be present, producing electric fields of ~10^21 V/m, but the local forces on individual charged particles cause them to discharge quickly, leading to a global force imbalance and collapse to a charged black hole. The authors emphasize that while the global equilibrium equations permit such extreme charges, the stars are necessarily short-lived because the enormous radial repulsive force on individual charged particles causes them to leave the star rapidly.

## Method

The authors solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations that include the Maxwell-Einstein stress tensor for a static, spherically symmetric star, assuming a polytropic equation of state with index n=1.5 (adiabatic exponent Gamma=5/3) and a charge distribution proportional to the mass density (rho_ch = f * epsilon). They integrate the coupled equations for the mass, metric coefficient, and pressure gradient to obtain mass-radius relations and charge-mass relations for different values of the charge fraction f.

## Key numbers

- Maximum charge at the surface of a neutron star: ~10^20 Coulomb, corresponding to Q = sqrt(G) * M_sun * (M/M_sun)
- Maximum electric field inside the star: ~10^21 V/m
- For the maximum allowed charge fraction f=0.0011, the maximum mass stable star has mass 9.7 M_sun and radius 68 km
- For charge fraction f=0.001, a star with mass 4.3 M_sun has radius 35 km
- The charge fraction f=0.0011 corresponds to rho_ch = 0.95616 * rho in geometrical units
- The charge-to-mass ratio Z/A is approximately 10^-18, justifying the use of an equation of state calculated for neutral matter
- The critical charge fraction below which no departure from the chargeless case is seen: f=0.0001
- For the maximum charge fraction, the mass contribution from electric energy density is 10% of that from mass density
- The conversion factor used: 1 Coulomb ~ 0.75 x 10^19 [MeV fm]^(1/2)

## Context

This work extends the general relativistic treatment of charged fluid spheres, building on the foundational studies by Majumdar (1947) and Papapetrou (1947) for charged dust, and the stability analyses by Bekenstein (1971), Zhang et al. (1982), de Felice and Yu (1995), and Anninos and Rothman (2001). The authors use a polytropic equation of state with parameters that reproduce realistic neutron star equations of state, following Taurines et al. (2001). The results show that charged compact stars are necessarily transient objects, serving as an intermediate state between supernova collapse and the formation of a charged black hole, with a possible charge generation mechanism suggested by Mosquera Cuesta et al. (2003).
