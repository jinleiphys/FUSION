# Interpreting scattering wave functions in the presence of energy-dependent interactions

> In scattering theory, the squared relative wave function $|ϕ({\bf q},{\bf r})|^2$ is often interpreted as a weight, due to final-state interactions, describing the probability enhancement for emission with asymptotic relative momentum $q$. An equivalence relation also links the integral of the squared wave function over all coordinate space to the density of states. This relation, which plays an important role in understanding two-particle correlation phenomenology, is altered for the case where the potential is energy dependent, as is assumed in various forms of reaction theory. Here, the modification to the equivalence relation is derived, and it is shown that the squared wave function should be augmented by a additional factor if it is to represent the emission enhancement for final-state interactions. Examples with relativistic vector interactions, e.g., the Coulomb interaction, are presented.

# Interpreting scattering wave functions in the presence of energy-dependent interactions

## Key claim

The standard equivalence relation linking the integral of the squared scattering wave function over coordinate space to the density of states (via the derivative of the phase shift) is invalid when the interaction potential depends on the asymptotic momentum. The squared wave function must be augmented by a multiplicative factor [1 − (1/2q)∂_qU(q,r)] to correctly represent the emission enhancement for final-state interactions when the potential is energy-dependent.

## Method

The author derives a generalized form of the equivalence relation between the radial integral of the squared partial wave and the derivative of the phase shift by starting from the Schrödinger equation with an energy-dependent potential U(q,r) and considering solutions for two neighboring values of asymptotic momentum q and q'. Integration by parts and subtraction of the zero-potential case yields the modified relation containing the correction factor. The result is then applied to the Klein-Gordon equation for a vector potential, to relativistic two-body systems with Coulomb interactions, and to the classical limit via phase-space focusing arguments.

## Key numbers

- The correction factor for the squared wave function is [1 − (1/2q)∂_qU(q,r)], derived in Eq. (generalresult)
- For the Klein-Gordon equation with vector potential V(r), the squared wave function must be multiplied by (1 − V(r)/E) to maintain consistency with the level density
- For relativistic two-body Coulomb scattering, the effective mass is μ = E/4 − (m₁² − m₂²)²/(4E³)
- In the large-q limit for equal-mass relativistic Coulomb scattering, the phase-space focusing factor becomes d³q₀/d³q ≈ 1 − μe²/(q²r) − e²/(μr), where the last term is four times larger than the second term in the relativistic limit

## Context

This work extends the standard Koonin equation (Koonin, 1977) and Beth-Uhlenbeck formula (Beth and Uhlenbeck, 1937) for two-particle correlations to the case of energy-dependent interactions, which arise in relativistic motion and in optical models such as those of Myers and Swiatecki (1996) and Bandyopadhyay (2002). The derivation corrects a previously assumed equivalence that was used to test numerical solutions of scattered wave functions and to justify the Koonin formula. The results enable proper treatment of final-state interactions in relativistic regimes, such as ππ correlations near the ρ meson resonance (Rapp, 2000; Bauer, 2000) and charge balance function analyses in high-energy heavy-ion collisions (Bass, 2000; Cheng, 2000), where the energy dependence of the effective mass becomes significant.
