# Non-perturbative Extraction of the Effective Mass in Neutron Matter

> We carry out non-perturbative calculations of the single-particle excitation spectrum in strongly interacting neutron matter. These are microscopic quantum Monte Carlo computations of many-neutron energies at different densities as well as several distinct excited states. As input, we employ both phenomenological and chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. We use the single-particle spectrum to extract the effective mass in neutron matter. With a view to systematizing the error involved in this extraction, we carefully assess the impact of finite-size effects on the quasiparticle dispersion relation. We find an effective-mass ratio that drops from 1 as the density is increased. We conclude by connecting our results with the physics of ultracold gases as well as with energy-density functional theories of nuclei and neutron-star matter.

## Key claim

The effective-mass ratio in neutron matter drops from 1 as density increases, reaching a minimum of 0.8 at the highest density studied (0.20 fm⁻³), but the detailed values differ between phenomenological and chiral nuclear potentials at higher densities, indicating a model-dependent extraction.

## Method

The authors performed Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations for 66 and 67 neutrons at densities from 0.02 to 0.20 fm⁻³, using both phenomenological (Argonne v8' plus Urbana IX) and local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. They computed quasiparticle energies by taking energy differences between N-particle and (N+1)-particle systems at constant density, placing the extra particle at several discrete momentum states near the Fermi surface. A novel finite-size correction prescription was applied to extrapolate results to the thermodynamic limit, and the effective mass was extracted by fitting the quasiparticle dispersion relation to a straight line.

## Key numbers

- Effective-mass ratio m*/m = 0.920 ± 0.040 at density 0.05 fm⁻³ (using AFDMC with AV8'+UIX)
- Effective-mass ratio reaches a minimum value of 0.8 at the highest density studied (0.20 fm⁻³)
- Densities studied range from 0.02 to 0.20 fm⁻³
- Simulations used 66 particles (closed-shell configuration) as the optimal choice for finite-size control
- The effective-mass ratio for the unitary Fermi gas is m*/m = 0.92, comparable to the low-density neutron matter result

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio calculations of neutron matter properties (Gezerlis 2008, 2010, 2013; Gandolfi 2015) and provides the first controlled non-perturbative extraction of the effective mass in neutron matter using quantum Monte Carlo methods. The results differ from many-body approaches that show m*/m first rising above 1 then dropping (Wambach 1993, Schwenk 2003, Drischler 2014), and are closest to variational calculations (Friedman 1981). The findings can be incorporated into Skyrme or other nuclear energy-density functionals to improve descriptions of neutron-rich nuclei and neutron-star physics.
