# Polarization effects in double open-charm production at LHCb

> Double open-charm production is one of the most promising channels to disentangle single from double parton scattering (DPS) and study different properties of DPS. Several studies of the DPS contributions have been made. A missing ingredient so far has been the study of polarization effects, arising from spin correlations between the two partons inside an unpolarized proton. We investigate the impact polarization has on the double open-charm cross section. We show that the longitudinally polarized gluons can give significant contributions to the cross section, but for most of the considered kinematic region only have a moderate effect on the shape. We compare our findings to the LHCb data in the D0D0 final state, identify observables where polarization does have an impact on the distribution of the final state particles, and suggest measurements which could lead to first experimental indications of, or limits on, polarization in DPS.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that longitudinally polarized gluons can give significant contributions to the double open-charm cross section in double parton scattering, but for most of the considered kinematic region they only have a moderate effect on the shape of distributions; the quantitative impact depends strongly on the choice of initial scale Q0 and the hard scale mu, with the polarized contribution ranging from roughly 2% to 30% of the unpolarized cross section depending on these choices.

## Method

The authors calculate the double parton scattering cross section for double c-cbar production at leading order, including contributions from unpolarized, longitudinally polarized, and linearly polarized gluon double parton distributions (DPDs). They use the homogeneous double DGLAP evolution equations to evolve the DPDs from an initial scale Q0 (chosen between 1 and 2 GeV) to the hard scale, saturating positivity bounds at the initial scale to obtain maximal polarization effects. The numerical results are compared to LHCb D0D0 data at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, with the charm quark kinematics (3 <= pT <= 12 GeV, 2 <= y <= 4) taken as proxies for the D0 mesons.

## Key numbers

- The polarized contribution relative to the unpolarized cross section ranges from approximately 4% (Q0=1 GeV, mu=2m) to 30% (Q0=2 GeV, mu=2m) for the rapidity difference distribution
- With the scale choice mu=mT (average transverse mass), the polarized contribution decreases to about 2% (Q0=1 GeV) and 15% (Q0=2 GeV)
- The suppression factor m^2/m_Ti^2 for longitudinally polarized gluons relative to unpolarized is 0.16 at p_Ti=3 GeV
- The phase space requires charm quark transverse momenta in the range 3 <= p_Ti <= 12 GeV and rapidities 2 <= y_i <= 4 at sqrt(s)=7 TeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of double open-charm production in double parton scattering (such as van Hameren (2014) and Maciula (2013)) by including for the first time the quantitative impact of spin correlations between the two partons inside an unpolarized proton. The authors build on the theoretical framework for polarized DPDs developed by Diehl (2013) and Kasemets (2014), and use the positivity bounds derived in those works to set upper limits on the polarized distributions. The results enable future experimental searches for polarization effects in DPS at LHCb, potentially leading to first measurements of or limits on spin correlations in double parton scattering.
