# Gamow shell model and realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions

> We present a new and efficient method to obtain a Gamow shell-model basis and matrix elements generated by realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. We derive a self-consistent Hartree-Fock potential from the renormalized N3LO interaction model. The corresponding Gamow one-body eigenstates are generated in a plane wave basis in order to build a Gamow shell-model set of basis states for the closed shell nuclei 4He and 16O. We address also the problem of representing a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction in a two-particle Berggren basis in the laboratory frame. To achieve this, an expansion of matrix elements of the residual nucleon-nucleon interaction in a finite set of harmonic oscillator wave functions is used. We show that all loosely bound and narrow resonant states converge surprisingly fast. Even broad resonances in these two-particle valence systems converge within a reasonable number of harmonic oscillator functions. Examples of 6He and 18O Gamow shell-model calculations using 4He and 16}O as closed shell cores are presented. This procedure allows Gamow shell-model calculations to be performed with all realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and with either momentum or position space representations for the Gamow basis. The possibility to remove the center of mass spuriosity of Gamow shell-model nuclear states with this method is also discussed. Perspectives for nuclear structure calculations of dripline nuclei are outlined.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to construct a Gamow shell-model basis and matrix elements using a renormalized realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction (the low-momentum N3LO interaction, \(V_{\mathrm{low-k}}\)), but the approach is limited to the Hartree-Fock level and the results show a strong dependence on the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\), meaning the single-particle energies are not uniquely predicted without including additional many-body forces.

## Method

The authors first derive a self-consistent Hartree-Fock potential from the renormalized N3LO interaction using a plane-wave basis, then analytically continue this potential to the complex momentum plane via Fourier-Bessel transformations to obtain a Gamow (Berggren) basis of bound, resonant, and scattering states. To represent the realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction in a two-particle Berggren basis in the laboratory frame, they expand the interaction matrix elements in a finite set of harmonic oscillator wave functions, enabling efficient calculation through the Talmi transformation. The method is applied to generate single-particle bases for the closed-shell nuclei \(^{4}\)He and \(^{16}\)O, and Gamow shell-model calculations are performed for the two-valence-nucleon systems \(^{6}\)He and \(^{18}\)O.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{16}\)O with \(\Lambda = 1.9\) fm\(^{-1}\), the neutron \(s_{1/2}\) hole state energy is Re[E] = -73.977 MeV (Im[E] = 0.000 MeV); experimental value is -44.000 MeV.
- For \(^{16}\)O with \(\Lambda = 1.9\) fm\(^{-1}\), the neutron \(p_{3/2}\) hole state energy is Re[E] = -37.082 MeV (Im[E] = 0.000 MeV); experimental value is -21.840 MeV.
- For \(^{16}\)O with \(\Lambda = 1.9\) fm\(^{-1}\), the neutron \(p_{1/2}\) hole state energy is Re[E] = -23.981 MeV (Im[E] = 0.000 MeV); experimental value is -15.660 MeV.
- For \(^{16}\)O with \(\Lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), the neutron \(s_{1/2}\) hole state energy is Re[E] = -68.496 MeV; for \(\Lambda = 2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), it is Re[E] = -63.078 MeV.
- The cutoff \(\Lambda\) values used for \(^{16}\)O are 1.9, 2.0, and 2.1 fm\(^{-1}\); for \(^{4}\)He they are 1.8, 1.9, and 2.0 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the Gamow shell model (Michel et al., 2002; Hagen et al., 2005) by replacing phenomenological Woods-Saxon or Gaussian potentials with a self-consistent Hartree-Fock potential derived from a realistic renormalized nucleon-nucleon interaction (the low-momentum N3LO interaction of Bogner et al., 2003). The method enables future fully microscopic Gamow shell-model calculations for dripline nuclei, but the strong cutoff dependence observed indicates that a three-body force (as used by Nogga et al., 2004 for \(^{4}\)He and \(^{3}\)H) will be necessary to achieve predictive power for heavier systems.
