# Thouless-Valatin Rotational Moment of Inertia from the Linear Response Theory

> Spontaneous breaking of continuous symmetries of a nuclear many-body system results in appearance of zero-energy restoration modes. Such modes introduce a non-physical contributions to the physical excitations called spurious Nambu-Goldstone modes. Since they represent a special case of collective motion, they are sources of important information about the Thouless-Valatin inertia. The main purpose of this work is to study the Thouless-Valatin rotational moment of inertia as extracted from the Nambu-Goldstone restoration mode that results from the zero-frequency response to the total angular momentum operator. We examine the role and effects of the pairing correlations on the rotational characteristics of heavy deformed nuclei in order to extend our understanding of superfluidity in general. We use the finite amplitude method of the quasiparticle random phase approximation on top of the Skyrme energy density functional framework with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. We have successfully extended this formalism and established a practical method for extracting the Thouless-Valatin rotational moment of inertia from the strength function calculated in the symmetry restoration regime. Our results reveal the relation between the pairing correlations and the moment of inertia of axially deformed nuclei of rare-earth and actinide regions of the nuclear chart. We have also demonstrated the feasibility of the method for obtaining the moment of inertia for collective Hamiltonian models. We conclude that from the numerical and theoretical perspective, the finite amplitude method can be widely used to effectively study rotational properties of deformed nuclei within modern density functional approaches.

## Key claim

The Thouless-Valatin rotational moment of inertia can be extracted from the zero-frequency strength function of the total angular momentum operator using the finite amplitude method of the quasiparticle random phase approximation, with the method's validity demonstrated specifically for axially deformed nuclei under the assumption of simplex-y symmetry at the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov level.

## Method

The authors used the finite amplitude method of the quasiparticle random phase approximation (FAM-QRPA) on top of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with the Skyrme SkM* energy density functional and a volume pairing interaction. The HFB ground states were obtained using the HFBTHO code with 20 major harmonic oscillator shells, and the FAM-QRPA equations were solved iteratively using the modified Broyden method. The Thouless-Valatin rotational moment of inertia was obtained by evaluating the strength function of the y-component of the total angular momentum operator at zero frequency, with the FAM-QRPA calculations performed under simplex-y symmetry and using the full time-odd part of the energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- For erbium isotopes, pairing strengths were adjusted to $^{166}$Er: $V_0^p = -211.20$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V_0^n = -178.83$ MeV fm$^3$, reproducing proton and neutron pairing gaps of 1.20 MeV and 1.02 MeV respectively
- For ytterbium isotopes, pairing strengths were adjusted to $^{168}$Yb: $V_0^p = -212.40$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V_0^n = -180.60$ MeV fm$^3$, reproducing proton and neutron pairing gaps of 1.26 MeV and 1.09 MeV respectively
- For uranium isotopes, pairing strengths were adjusted to $^{238}$U: $V_0^p = -216.18$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V_0^n = -170.95$ MeV fm$^3$, reproducing proton and neutron pairing gaps of 1.11 MeV and 0.67 MeV respectively
- For plutonium isotopes, pairing strengths were adjusted to $^{240}$Pu: $V_0^p = -213.20$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V_0^n = -170.80$ MeV fm$^3$, reproducing proton and neutron pairing gaps of 0.99 MeV and 0.64 MeV respectively
- For $^{166}$Er with 16 oscillator shells, the FAM-QRPA Thouless-Valatin inertia yielded 34.511 MeV$^{-1}$, while the cranking HFB calculation gave 34.533 MeV$^{-1}$
- The quasiparticle cut-off energy was set to 60 MeV in all calculations
- Gauss-Hermite quadrature used 80 mesh points and Gauss-Laguerre quadrature used 40 mesh points

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method approach previously applied to translational and particle number gauge symmetry restoring Nambu-Goldstone modes (Hinohara 2015, Hinohara 2016) to the rotational Nambu-Goldstone mode, providing a computationally efficient alternative to standard matrix QRPA and cranking HFB calculations. The results demonstrate the crucial role of pairing correlations in reducing the moment of inertia from values several times higher (without pairing) to values in good agreement with experimental moments of inertia extracted from $2^+$ state energies of ground-state rotational bands, and the method enables future extraction of collective mass parameters for microscopic collective Hamiltonian models.
