# Shape transitions in neutron-rich Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopes within a Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS approach

> Self-consistent axially symmetric Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations are performed to study the evolution of shapes with the number of nucleons in various chains of Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopes from neutron number N=110 up to N=122. Potential energy curves are analyzed in a search for signatures of oblate-prolate phase shape transitions and results from various Skyrme and pairing forces are considered. Comparisons with results obtained with the Gogny interaction as well as with relativistic mean field calculations are presented. The role of the $γ$ degree of freedom is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shape transitions from prolate to oblate ground-state deformations occur in neutron-rich Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopes as neutron number increases, but the exact nuclide at which the transition takes place depends sensitively on the effective Skyrme force and pairing treatment used, with energy differences between competing minima often being only a few hundred keV.

## Method

Self-consistent axially symmetric Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations were performed for five isotopic chains (Yb, Hf, W, Os, Pt) from neutron number N=110 to N=122, using the Skyrme parametrizations Sk3, SLy4, and SLy6 in the particle-hole channel with various pairing treatments including a zero-range density-dependent pairing force, constant-force, and constant-gap approaches. Potential energy curves were constructed as functions of the mass quadrupole moment, and for a subset of nuclei (N=114, 116, 118) triaxial calculations were performed using a three-dimensional cartesian mesh to explore the role of the gamma degree of freedom. Results were compared with Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations and relativistic mean-field calculations using the NL3 parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The prolate-to-oblate shape transition occurs at N=116 in Yb and Hf isotopes, at N=116 and N=118 in W and Os isotopes, and is very soft in Pt isotopes
- For the nucleus 190W, the triaxial ground state lies at (Q, gamma) = (14 barns, 25 degrees), less than 0.5 MeV below the prolate minimum and less than 1.3 MeV below the oblate minimum
- Energy barriers at zero deformation between oblate and prolate minima can change by a few MeV depending on the force, with Sk3 giving lower barriers and the delta pairing force also lowering barriers
- The ratio E4+/E2+ for 182Hf is 3.29, for 184W is 3.27, and for 186Os is 3.16, with experimental moments of inertia extracted from first 2+ energies ranging from 19.4 MeV^-1 (188Os) to 30.7 MeV^-1 (182Hf)
- Cranking moments of inertia from SLy4 calculations range from 29.2 MeV^-1 (186Os) to 33.6 MeV^-1 (184Hf), while Gogny calculations give values from 23.3 MeV^-1 (188W) to 35.0 MeV^-1 (184Hf)
- The pairing strength used is g=1000 MeV fm^3 for both neutrons and protons, with a smooth cut-off of 5 MeV around the Fermi level and a critical density rho_c=0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape transitions in the A=170-200 mass region, including shell correction calculations by Nazarewicz (1990) that found the prolate-oblate change in Os isotopes between N=114 and N=116, and total routhian surface calculations by Wheldon (2000) suggesting the transition at N=118. The paper provides the first systematic Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS study of these shape transitions, comparing with relativistic mean-field calculations using NL3 by Fossion (2002) and with Gogny-D1S calculations, finding qualitative agreement on the location of minima but differences in energy barriers. The results enable future studies incorporating dynamical correlations beyond the static mean field, such as symmetry restoration and configuration mixing, to better describe ground-state properties in these transitional nuclei where shape coexistence is prominent.
