# Probing "long-range" neutrino-mediated forces with atomic and nuclear spectroscopy

> The exchange of a pair of low-mass neutrinos between electrons, protons and neutrons produces a "long-range" $1/r^5$ potential, which can be sought for in phenomena originating on the atomic and sub-atomic length scales. We calculate the effects of neutrino-pair exchange on transition and binding energies in atoms and nuclei. In the case of atomic s-wave states, there is a large enhancement of the induced energy shifts due to the lack of a centrifugal barrier and the highly singular nature of the neutrino-mediated potential. We derive limits on neutrino-mediated forces from measurements of the deuteron binding energy and transition energies in positronium, muonium, hydrogen and deuterium, as well as isotope-shift measurements in calcium ions. Our limits improve on existing constraints on neutrino-mediated forces from experiments that search for new macroscopic forces by 18 orders of magnitude. Future spectroscopy experiments have the potential to probe long-range forces mediated by the exchange of pairs of standard-model neutrinos and other weakly-charged particles.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutrino-pair exchange produces a long-range $1/r^5$ potential whose effects on atomic and nuclear transition and binding energies are dramatically enhanced in $s$-wave states due to the lack of a centrifugal barrier, but the derived constraints are on an effective coupling $G_\mathrm{eff}^2$ that is still many orders of magnitude larger than the standard-model Fermi constant $G_\mathrm{F}^2$, meaning the forces have not yet been detected at the standard-model level.

## Method

The author calculated the expectation value of the $1/r^5$ neutrino-pair-exchange potential for $s$-wave states in several systems: the deuteron (using a spherical hard-core nuclear potential model), positronium and muonium (using non-relativistic hydrogen-like wavefunctions with a cutoff at the Z-boson Compton wavelength), hydrogen and deuterium (using nuclear-radius cutoff), and heavy atoms such as Ca$^+$ (using relativistic atomic wavefunctions). For each system, the predicted energy shift from the neutrino-mediated potential was compared to the difference between measured and theoretically predicted transition or binding energies to set upper limits on the effective coupling strength $G_\mathrm{eff}^2$.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy: measured $E_B^\mathrm{exp} = 2.2245663(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}$, theoretical $E_B^\mathrm{theor} = 2.22457(1)\ \mathrm{MeV}$; constraint $G_\mathrm{eff}^2 \lesssim 7.9 \times 10^8\ G_\mathrm{F}^2$.
* Positronium $1^3S_1 - 2^3S_1$ transition: measured $\nu_{1S-2S}^\mathrm{exp} = 1\,233\,607\,216.4(3.2)\ \mathrm{MHz}$, theoretical $\nu_{1S-2S}^\mathrm{theor} = 1\,233\,607\,222.18(58)\ \mathrm{MHz}$; constraint $G_\mathrm{eff}^2 \lesssim 2.6 \times 10^{8}\ G_\mathrm{F}^2$.
* Positronium ground-state hyperfine splitting: measured $\nu_\mathrm{hfs}^\mathrm{exp} = 203\,389.10(74)\ \mathrm{MHz}$, theoretical $\nu_\mathrm{hfs}^\mathrm{theor} = 203\,392.01(46)\ \mathrm{MHz}$; constraint $G_\mathrm{eff}^2 \lesssim 1.5 \times 10^{7}\ G_\mathrm{F}^2$.
* Muonium ground-state hyperfine splitting: measured $\nu_\mathrm{hfs}^\mathrm{exp} = 4\,463\,302\,776(51)\ \mathrm{Hz}$, theoretical $\nu_\mathrm{hfs}^\mathrm{theor} = 4\,463\,302\,868(271)\ \mathrm{Hz}$; constraint $G_\mathrm{eff}^2 \lesssim 1.9 \times 10^{2}\ G_\mathrm{F}^2$; the standard-model shift is at the level $\approx 2\ \mathrm{Hz}$.
* Hydrogen-deuterium $1s-2s$ isotope shift: measured difference $670\,994\,334\,606(15)\ \mathrm{Hz}$, theoretical difference $670\,994\,348.9(4.9)\ \mathrm{kHz}$; constraint $G_\mathrm{eff}^2 \lesssim 1.6 \times 10^{11}\ G_\mathrm{F}^2$.
* Calcium-ion isotope-shift non-linearity: experimental constraint $\lesssim 25\ \mathrm{MHz}\cdot\mathrm{GeV}$ over $40 \le A \le 48$; constraint $G_\mathrm{eff}^2 \lesssim 4.0 \times 10^{11}\ G_\mathrm{F}^2$.
* The derived limits improve on existing constraints from macroscopic-force experiments by 18 orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of neutrino-pair-exchange forces originally developed by Feinberg (1968) and Sikivie (1994), who calculated the $1/r^5$ potential but only considered macroscopic-scale searches. The author applies the potential to atomic and nuclear length scales for the first time, exploiting the huge enhancement in $s$-wave states. The technique of using King-plot non-linearities in isotope-shift measurements, previously applied to hypothetical Higgs-like particles by Delaunay (2016) and Berengut (2017), is here adapted to neutrino-mediated forces. The most promising future system is muonium, where the standard-model shift is $\approx 2\ \mathrm{Hz}$, but probing it requires more precise calculations of fourth-order and fifth-order QED contributions and one-loop electroweak contributions not involving neutrinos.
