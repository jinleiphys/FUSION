# Shape transition and fluctuation in neutron-rich Cr isotopes around N = 40

> The spherical-to-prolate shape transition in neutron-rich Cr isotopes from N = 34 to 42 is studied by solving the collective Schr\"odinger equation for the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian. The collective potential and inertial functions are microscopically derived with use of the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus local quasiparticle random-phase approximation method. Nature of the quadrupole collectivity of low-lying states is discussed by evaluating excitation spectra and electric quadrupole moments and transition strengths. The result of calculation indicates that Cr isotopes around 64Cr are prolately deformed but still possess transitional character; large-amplitude shape fluctuations dominate in their low-lying states.

## Key claim

The calculation indicates that Cr isotopes around $^{64}$Cr are prolately deformed but still possess transitional character, with large-amplitude shape fluctuations dominating in their low-lying states, rather than exhibiting stable rigid-rotor behavior.

## Method

The authors solved the collective Schrodinger equation for the five-dimensional (5D) quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, with the collective potential and inertial functions microscopically derived using the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (CHFB+LQRPA) method. The pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) model including quadrupole pairing interaction was employed, with single-particle energies determined from constrained Skyrme-HFB calculations using the SkM* functional. The collective Schrodinger equation was solved on a 60 x 60 mesh grid in the (beta, gamma) deformation plane.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R_{4/2} = E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+) at N=40 (^{64}Cr) is 2.68, considerably smaller than the rigid-rotor value of 3.33
- The collective potential was calculated for neutron-rich Cr isotopes from N=34 to 42 (^{58-66}Cr)
- The CHFB+LQRPA equations were solved at 60 x 60 mesh points in the (beta, gamma) plane with beta_i = (i - 0.5) x 0.01 for i = 1 to 60 and gamma_j = (j - 0.5) x 1 degree for j = 1 to 60
- Effective charges used for E2 transition calculations were (e_eff^{(n)}, e_eff^{(p)}) = (0.5, 1.5)
- The monopole pairing strengths followed the mass number dependence G_0^tau ~ A^{-1} and chi' ~ A^{-5/3}

## Context

This work extends the CHFB+LQRPA method previously applied to shape coexistence in Se and Kr isotopes (Hinohara 2010, Sato 2011), triaxial deformation in Mo (Watanabe 2011), and shape fluctuations in neutron-rich Mg isotopes (Hinohara 2011). The study addresses the spherical-to-prolate shape transition in Cr isotopes near N=40, where prior mean-field calculations by Oba (2008) using Skyrme HFB showed quadrupole instability but with extremely shallow deformed minima. The approach enables distinction between equilibrium shape and shape fluctuations, which was not sufficiently clear in spherical shell model calculations by Lenzi (2010) and Kaneko (2008). The results enable comparison with the shape transition near N=20 in Mg isotopes and with the neighboring N=40 nucleus ^{66}Fe.
