# Analysis of experimental series of plutonium nitrate in aqueous solution and their correlation coefficients

> In this work we performed a detailed analysis on the calculation of 43 critical experiments from 6 experimental series all describing plutonium nitrate in aqueous solution contained in metal spheres. The underlying experimental data is taken from the handbook of the International Criticality Safety Benchmark Evaluation Project (ICSBEP) Working Group. We present our modeling assumptions which were derived from the interpretation of the experimental data and discuss the resulting sensitivity analysis. Although the experiments share some components, the derived correlation coefficients are for many cases statistically not significant. Comparing our findings for the correlation coefficients with available data from the DICE Database we find an agreement for the correlation coefficients due to nuclear data. We also compare our results for the correlation coefficients due to experimental uncertainty. Our findings indicate that for the reliable Determination of correlation coefficients a detailed study of the underlying experimental data, the modeling approach and assumptions, and the resulting sensitivity analysis seems to be inevitable.

## Key claim

The authors find that for plutonium nitrate solution spheres, shared experimental components do not necessarily produce statistically significant correlation coefficients between experiments, contrary to findings for fuel rod lattices where such correlations can be large. The correlation coefficients due to shared system parameters are mostly in the range [-0.1, 0.3] and only reach values around 0.7 for a few specific experiments.

## Method

The authors modeled 43 critical experiments from 6 ICSBEP PU-SOL-THERM series using the CSAS5 sequence of SCALE 6.1.2 with the ENDF/B-VII continuous energy cross-section library. They performed a full Monte Carlo analysis with 250 samples per experiment using the GRS tool SUnCISTT to vary uncertain system parameters (such as plutonium density, weight percent of 240Pu, sphere volume, and wall thickness) according to normal distributions, with shared parameters receiving identical random seeds to induce correlations. Correlation coefficients due to nuclear data uncertainties were calculated using TSUNAMI-3D-5K and TSUNAMI-IP.

## Key numbers

* 43 critical experiments from 6 experimental series (PST-03 to -06, -20, -21) were analyzed, all describing plutonium nitrate in aqueous solution contained in metal spheres
* Correlation coefficients due to shared system parameters range from slightly negative values to 0.7, with most in the range [-0.1, 0.3]; the statistical error on correlation coefficients is about 0.1 for values near zero with 250 samples
* For series PST-03, 04, 05, and 20, the calculated standard deviation from system parameter variations is 30-50% smaller than the experimental uncertainty of 0.0047 (assumed worst case)
* For series PST-06 and PST-21, the calculated standard deviations agree excellently with experimental uncertainties of 0.0035 and 0.0025-0.0044 respectively
* The c_k correlation coefficients due to nuclear data uncertainties are above 0.98 for all experiments except PST-21-08 (down to 0.96), in very good agreement with DICE database values
* The two experiments with cadmium coating (PST-20-14 and -15) deviate from experimental keff values by approximately -0.0112 and -0.0080 respectively; reducing cadmium thickness from 0.03 inch to 0.01 inch does not significantly increase keff

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous contributions to the OECD-NEA EGUACSA Benchmark on "Role of Integral Experiment Covariance Data for Criticality Safety Validation" (Hoefer, 2014), which previously examined water moderated arrays of fuel rods. The present study examines water reflected spheres of low concentrated plutonium nitrate solution with thermal neutron spectrum, finding that correlation coefficients are much smaller than those found for fuel rod lattices (Peters, 2015; Peters, 2016). The results support the DICE database (OECD-NEA) indications of possible correlations between PST-03 through PST-06 series, but show that the actual correlation magnitudes are small and likely negligible for validation procedures, while also identifying experiments (PST-20, PST-21, and several individual experiments) that have no statistically significant correlations with other series.
