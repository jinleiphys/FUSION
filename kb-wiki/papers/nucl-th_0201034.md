# 3D Real-Space Calculation of the Continuum Response

> We present that a linear response theory in the continuum can be easily formulated with Absorbing Boundary Condition (ABC). The theory is capable of describing continuum spectra and dynamical correlations. Application of the ABC does not require the spherical symmetry and the method is suitable for mesh representation in the real coordinate space. Isovector giant dipole resonances in beryllium isotopes are studied with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock with the Skyrme force in a three-dimensional mesh space with the ABC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an Absorbing Boundary Condition (ABC) can replace the Outgoing Boundary Condition (OBC) in linear response calculations, enabling continuum descriptions in three-dimensional real-space without requiring spherical symmetry, though the ABC must satisfy specific conditions on absorber strength and width to minimize reflection.

## Method

The authors implement a real-time Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculation on a three-dimensional uniform mesh, using a complex absorbing potential of linear radial dependence placed outside the physical region (R < r < R+Δr) to enforce outgoing wave behavior. The initial state is prepared by boosting the Hartree-Fock ground state with an instantaneous external field, and the time evolution is computed up to total period T = 4 MeV⁻¹, followed by Fourier transform of the expectation values to obtain excitation strength functions.

## Key numbers

- Absorbing potential parameters: η₀ = 14 MeV, Δr = 10 fm, with physical box radius R = 8 fm and mesh spacing Δx = Δy = Δz = 1 fm
- Time step Δt = 0.001 MeV⁻¹ and total evolution period T = 4 MeV⁻¹, corresponding to energy resolution of approximately 1.6 MeV
- For the BKN interaction test in ¹⁶O, the ABC requires only Δr = 10 fm plus R = 10 fm to match OBC results, while BBC requires R > 100 fm to achieve comparable accuracy
- Large deformation splitting over 10 MeV is obtained in ⁸,¹⁰,¹⁴Be isotopes for E1 strengths parallel versus perpendicular to the symmetry axis

## Context

This work extends the Continuum Random-Phase Approximation (CRPA) method of Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) and the spherical Skyrme Hartree-Fock linear response calculations of Giai and Sagawa (1981) to deformed nuclei without spherical symmetry. The ABC approach enables treatment of continuum effects in three-dimensional real-space calculations that were previously limited to one-dimensional radial coordinates. The method enables future studies of breakup processes in halo nuclei and investigations of unstable nuclear systems, as noted by Yabana (2002).
