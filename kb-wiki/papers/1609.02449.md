# Unveiling the nucleon tensor charge at Jefferson Lab: A study of the SoLID case

> Future experiments at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade, in particular, the Solenoidal Large Intensity Device (SoLID), aim at a very precise data set in the region where the partonic structure of the nucleon is dominated by the valence quarks. One of the main goals is to constrain the quark transversity distributions. We apply recent theoretical advances of the global QCD extraction of the transversity distributions to study the impact of future experimental data from the SoLID experiments. Especially, we develop a simple strategy based on the Hessian matrix analysis that allows one to estimate the uncertainties of the transversity quark distributions and their tensor charges extracted from SoLID data simulation. We find that the SoLID measurements with the proton and the effective neutron targets can improve the precision of the u- and d-quark transversity distributions up to one order of magnitude in the range 0.05 < x < 0.6.

## Key claim

The SoLID experiments at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV, using both proton and effective neutron targets, can improve the precision of the u- and d-quark transversity distributions by up to one order of magnitude in the range 0.05 < x < 0.6, provided that the anti-quark transversities are assumed negligible and the Hessian matrix analysis method is applied to simulated data based on the KPSY15 global QCD extraction.

## Method

The authors used the KPSY15 global QCD analysis of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data and e+e- annihilation into hadron pairs, which includes TMD evolution, to simulate pseudodata for the SoLID experiment. They developed a Bayesian-inspired Hessian error analysis method that updates the covariance matrix from the prior KPSY15 fit by adding the Hessian of the chi-squared function computed from the simulated SoLID data, allowing estimation of uncertainty improvements without a combined re-fit. The simulated data covered four-dimensional bins in x, z, Q2, and PT for both proton (NH3) and effective neutron (3He) targets, with statistical and systematic uncertainties added in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- The SoLID experiment covers the kinematic range 0.05 < x < 0.6, with Q2 from 1.0 to 8.0 GeV2, z from 0.3 to 0.7, and PT from 0.0 to 1.6 GeV
- The average statistical precision per bin for SoLID p(e,e')pi+ is about 1% across more than 600 bins, compared to 37.1% relative precision for HERMES with 7 bins in x
- For the combined proton and neutron targets, errors on both u- and d-quark transversity are reduced by approximately 90% (one order of magnitude) in the region 0.05 < x < 0.6
- The truncated isovector tensor charge g_T at Q2 = 2.4 GeV2 is predicted as +0.55 +/- 0.018 (90% C.L.) for the SoLID-covered region 0.05 < x < 0.6
- The full isovector tensor charge g_T at Q2 = 2.4 GeV2 is predicted as +0.64 +/- 0.021 (90% C.L.) for the region 0 < x < 1
- The uncertainty on delta u in the SoLID region (0.05 < x < 0.6) improves from 0.122 to 0.015 (12% of original) at Q2 = 2.4 GeV2
- The uncertainty on delta d in the SoLID region (0.05 < x < 0.6) improves from 0.073 to 0.006 (approximately 9% of original) at Q2 = 2.4 GeV2

## Context

This work extends the global QCD extraction of transversity distributions by Kang et al (2015) (KPSY15) by projecting the impact of future SoLID measurements at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV. It provides a methodology for estimating uncertainty improvements from planned experiments without requiring full re-analysis, and demonstrates that the combination of proton and neutron targets is essential for balanced extraction of both u- and d-quark transversity. The predicted precision on the isovector tensor charge g_T will be comparable to lattice QCD calculations from Bali et al (2014), Gupta et al (2015), and others, enabling constraints on new physics beyond the Standard Model, while the low-x region not covered by SoLID will be explored by the future Electron Ion Collider.
