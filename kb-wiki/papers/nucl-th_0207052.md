# Photoproduction of the Eta Meson from the Deuteron Near Threshold

> Very recent data for the reaction gamma{d} --> eta{np} are analyzed within a model that includes contributions from the impulse approximation and next order corrections due to the np and eta{N} interactions in the final state. Comparison between the calculations and data indicate sizable contributions from the np and eta{N} final state interactions close to threshold.

## Key claim
The data for $\gamma d \to np\eta$ near threshold require sizable contributions from both the $np$ and $\eta N$ final state interactions, but the $\eta N$ effect is only significant for excess energies below about 40 MeV and interferes constructively with the $np$ FSI.

## Method
The authors model the reaction using an impulse approximation amplitude dominated by the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance, then add final state interactions via half-shell $t$-matrices. The $np$ FSI is computed from the CD-Bonn potential in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ partial waves, while the $\eta N$ FSI is taken from the Jülich meson-baryon coupled-channel model. The total cross section and differential spectra are compared to data from Hejny (2002) and Krusche (1995) for photon energies from threshold to about 700 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance parameters used: $M_R = 1544$ MeV, $\Gamma = 203$ MeV, $\Gamma_\eta/\Gamma = 0.45$, $\Gamma_\pi/\Gamma = 0.45$, $\Gamma_{\pi\pi}/\Gamma = 0.1$.
- The $\eta N$ scattering length from the Jülich model is $a_{\eta N} = 0.42 + i0.32$ fm.
- The range of $\eta N$ scattering lengths considered from other models: $0.25 + i0.16$ fm to $1.05 + i0.27$ fm.
- The full calculation (IA + $np$ FSI + $\eta N$ FSI) describes data near threshold but underpredicts the cross section by about 10% between 660 and 680 MeV photon energy.
- The $\eta N$ FSI effect is negligible for photon energies above 670 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Arenhövel (1999) that treated the $\eta N$ interaction with a separable ansatz, and by the same authors (Elster et al., 2002) using the Jülich model. The results are consistent with a recent calculation of $np \to d\eta$ near threshold by Pena (2002), which also favors moderate values of the $\eta N$ scattering length. The analysis enables future extraction of the $\eta N$ scattering length from photoproduction data and suggests that the $\eta N$ FSI may also be visible in $pp \to pp\eta$ data from COSY and CELSIUS.
