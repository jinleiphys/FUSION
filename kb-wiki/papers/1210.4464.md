# A Method to Extract Charged Hadron Properties from Lattice QCD in Magnetic Fields

> By analyzing the external field dependence of correlation functions, the magnetic properties of hadrons can be determined using lattice QCD in magnetic fields. To compute the magnetic moments and polarizabilities of charged hadrons, for example, one requires sufficiently weak magnetic fields. Such field strengths, however, lead to closely spaced Landau levels that are not straightforwardly resolved using standard lattice spectroscopy. Focusing on charged spinless hadrons, we introduce a simple projection technique that can be used to isolate the lowest Landau level. As the technique requires the explicit coordinate-space wave-function, we investigate the extent to which the continuum, infinite volume wave-function can be employed. We find that, in practice, the effects of discretization can be handled using a perturbative expansion about the continuum. Finite volume corrections are taken into account by using the discrete magnetic translational invariance of the torus. We show that quantized magnetic fields can lead to pernicious volume effects which depend on the magnetic flux quantum, rather than on the lattice volume.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a projection technique to isolate the lowest Landau level for charged spinless hadrons in lattice QCD with magnetic fields, but note that the method requires the explicit coordinate-space wavefunction and that discretization effects can be handled via perturbative expansion about the continuum, while finite volume corrections depend on the magnetic flux quantum rather than lattice volume.

## Method

The authors construct a two-point correlation function for charged scalar particles that projects out the lowest Landau level by integrating the coordinate-space correlator against the ground-state harmonic oscillator wavefunction, rather than using a standard Fourier transform. They analyze discretization effects by expanding the lattice Landau level Hamiltonian in powers of the dimensionless magnetic field parameter b = |e a^2 B|, treating the leading corrections via Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory. Finite volume effects are studied using the discrete magnetic translational invariance of the torus, accounting for the quantization condition |q_d| B = 2π n_Φ / L^2.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless magnetic field parameter b = |e a^2 B| is limited to b ≲ 1/10 in practice, requiring at least N_L = 32 spatial sites with lattice spacing a = 0.1 fm and (n_Φ)_max = 4
- The leading discretization correction to the lowest Landau level energy eigenvalue is a^2 λ_0^(1) = -b^2/8
- The relative difference between the lattice and continuum wavefunctions for the lowest Landau level is less than 1% for magnetic fields b = 0.1
- For a physical mass pion with lattice spacing a = 0.1 fm, the polarizability parameter β = a M (β_M)/(α a^3) is expected to be β ∼ 1, and ignoring discretization corrections would affect the extracted polarizability by ∼10-20%

## Context

This work extends the background field method for lattice QCD, which was used in early calculations of nucleon magnetic moments (Martinelli 1982, Bernard 1982) and later revisited for magnetic moments and polarizabilities (Lee 2005). The projection technique addresses the problem that weak magnetic fields produce closely spaced Landau levels that standard lattice spectroscopy cannot resolve, building on the proper-time formalism pioneered by Schwinger (1951). The method enables extraction of magnetic polarizabilities of charged hadrons from lattice QCD, with the next step being application to actual lattice data using the perturbative treatment of discretization and finite volume effects developed here.
