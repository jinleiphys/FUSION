# Relativistic quark-diquark model of baryons. Non strange spectrum and nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> We briefly describe our relativistic quark-diquark model, developed within the framework of point form dynamics, which is the relativistic extension of the interacting quark-diquark model. In order to do that we have to show the main properties and quantum numbers of the effective degree of freedom of constituent diquark. Our results for the nonstrange baryon spectrum and for the nucleon electromagnetic form factors are discussed.

## Key claim
The relativistic quark-diquark model, developed within point form dynamics, reproduces the nonstrange baryon spectrum up to 2 GeV without predicting missing states, and yields nucleon electromagnetic form factors in reasonable agreement with experimental data up to 8 GeV², including a zero crossing of the ratio μₚGₑᵖ/Gₘᵖ at Q² ≈ 8 GeV².

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic mass operator for a quark-diquark system in the baryon rest frame, including a direct interaction (Coulomb-like with cutoff plus linear confinement), an exchange interaction, and a contact interaction. The mass operator is diagonalized numerically using a variational basis of 256 harmonic oscillator shells without perturbative approximations. Nucleon electromagnetic form factors are computed in the point form spectator impulse approximation, using the resulting wave functions and introducing phenomenological form factors for the constituent quark and diquark.

## Key numbers
- Quark mass m_q = 200 MeV; scalar diquark mass m_S = 600 MeV; axial-vector diquark mass m_AV = 950 MeV (mass difference 350 MeV)
- Model parameters: τ = 1.25, β = 2.15 fm⁻², μ = 75.0 fm⁻¹, σ = 1.71 fm⁻¹, A_S = 375 MeV, A_I = 260 MeV, A_SI = 375 MeV, E_0 = 154 MeV, D = 4.66 fm², η = 10.0 fm⁻¹, ε = 0.200
- Diquark form factor parameters: A_D = 1.27 GeV, B_D = 2.01
- Quark form factor parameters: A_u = 1.68 GeV, B_u = 0.66; A_d = 1.50 GeV, B_d = 1.40; C_u = 0.72 GeV, D_u = 1.34, κ_u = 0.38; C_d = 1.20 GeV, D_d = 1.95, κ_d = -0.33
- The model predicts a zero crossing of the ratio μₚGₑᵖ/Gₘᵖ at Q² ≈ 8 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the nonrelativistic interacting quark-diquark model of Santopinto (2004) to a fully relativistic framework using point form dynamics, following the formalism of Klink (1998) and Ferretti (2011). The results are comparable to those of other relativistic constituent quark models such as Capstick (1986) and Loring (2001), but the model does not predict missing states below 2 GeV, addressing a known problem in three-quark models. The calculation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors builds on earlier point form calculations for three-quark systems by De Sanctis (2004, 2007) and Santopinto (2010), and reproduces the unexpected decrease of μₚGₑᵖ/Gₘᵖ with Q² observed in JLab experiments (Jones 1999, Punjabi 2005).
