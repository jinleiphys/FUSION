# Spin observables in the $NN\to Yﾎ録+$ reaction at the threshold and quantum numbers of the $ﾎ録+$ pentaquark

> General formulae for the spin-spin correlation parameters $C_{i,j}$ and spin-transfer coefficients $K_i^j$ are derived for the reaction $N N\to Y ﾎ録+$ at the threshold for an arbitrary spin of the pentaquark $ﾎ録+$. It is shown that a measurement of the sign of $C_{y,y}$ or an observation of the non-zero polarization transfer from the nucleon to the hyperon $Y$ allow one to determine the P-parity of the $ﾎ録+$ unambiguously and independently on the spin of the $ﾎ録+$. Measurement of these spin observables in the both $pp$- and $pn$- channels of this reaction determines also the isospin of the $ﾎ録+$.

## Key claim

Measurement of the sign of the spin-spin correlation parameter \(C_{y,y}\) or observation of non-zero polarization transfer from the nucleon to the hyperon \(Y\) in the reaction \(NN\to Y\Theta^+\) at threshold determines the P-parity of the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark unambiguously and independently of its spin, which may be \(1/2\) or \(3/2\) and is not yet known.

## Method

General formulae for the spin-spin correlation parameters \(C_{i,j}\) and spin-transfer coefficients \(K_i^j\) were derived for the reaction \(NN\to Y\Theta^+\) at threshold using the standard method of spin-tensor operators and assuming S-wave dominance in the final state. The analysis was performed for an arbitrary spin of the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark, with the total angular momentum \(J\) and parity conservation restricting the orbital momentum \(L\) and the spin \(S\) of the initial nucleon pair. The spin structure was expressed in terms of complex amplitudes \(a_J^{LS}\), and the polarized cross section was computed for both spin-singlet (\(S=0\)) and spin-triplet (\(S=1\)) initial states.

## Key numbers

- For the spin-singlet initial state (\(S=0\)): \(C_{x,x}=C_{y,y}=C_{z,z}=-1\) and all spin-transfer coefficients \(K_i^j=0\).
- For the spin-triplet initial state (\(S=1\)): \(C_{x,x}=C_{y,y}\) is non-negative, and \(C_{z,z}=1-2C_{y,y}\).
- For the reaction with all spins \(1/2\), total isospin \(T=0\) and parity \(\pi=+1\): \(K_x^x=K_y^y = \frac{|\sqrt{2}\,a_1^0+a_1^2|^2-3\,\text{Re}\,(\sqrt{2}\,a_1^0+a_1^2){a_1^2}^*}{3\,(|a_1^0|^2+|a_1^2|^2)}\) and \(K_z^z = \frac{|\sqrt{2}\,a_1^0+a_1^2|^2}{3\,(|a_1^0|^2+|a_1^2|^2)}\).
- For the reaction with all spins \(1/2\), total isospin \(T=1\) and parity \(\pi=-1\): \(K_x^x=K_y^y = \frac{\sqrt{6}\,\text{Re}\,a_0^1\,{a_1^1}^*}{|a_0^1|^2+3|a_1^1|^2}\) and \(K_z^z = \frac{3|a_1^1|^2}{|a_0^1|^2+3|a_1^1|^2}\).

## Context

This work generalizes previous results by Thomas (2004), Hanhart (2004), Uzikov (2004), and Rekalo (2004), which assumed the spin of the \(\Theta^+\) was \(1/2\), to the case of arbitrary spin. The derived formulae enable model-independent determination of the P-parity and isospin of the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark through combined measurements of spin observables in both \(pp\to\Sigma^+\Theta^+\) and \(pn\to\Lambda^0\Theta^+\) reactions, which can be performed at facilities such as COSY or JLab.
