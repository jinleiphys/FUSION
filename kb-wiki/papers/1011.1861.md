# The RMS Charge Radius of the Proton and Zemach Moments

> On the basis of recent precise measurements of the electric form factor of the proton, the Zemach moments, needed as input parameters for the determination of the proton rms radius from the measurement of the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen, are calculated. It turns out that the new moments give an uncertainty as large as the presently stated error of the recent Lamb shift measurement of Pohl et al.. De Rujula's idea of a large Zemach moment in order to reconcile the five standard deviation discrepancy between the muonic Lamb shift determination and the result of electronic experiments is shown to be in clear contradiction with experiment. Alternative explanations are touched upon.

## Key claim

The paper shows that De Rújula's proposed large third Zemach moment (⟨r³⟩(₂) = 36.6 fm³) intended to reconcile the 5σ discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift determination of the proton radius and electronic determinations is in clear contradiction with experiment, but the new precise electron scattering data from Bernauer et al. (2010) yield a third Zemach moment that only changes the muonic radius by +0.00025 fm, a minor correction within the quoted theoretical error.

## Method

The authors assembled the electric form factor G_E,p of the proton from the recent precise Bernauer et al. (2010) Mainz Microtron MAMI data for momentum transfer Q < 0.7 GeV/c and the Arrington et al. (2007) fits for Q ≥ 0.7 GeV/c. They numerically evaluated the integrals for the first and third Zemach moments (⟨r¹⟩(₂) and ⟨r³⟩(₂)) in momentum space using Eqs. (3) and (4), and also computed the folded charge distribution ρ(₂)(r) via Fourier transform. The full spread of form factor models from Bernauer et al. was used to determine uncertainties, including readjustment of Arrington et al. normalizations by up to 2%.

## Key numbers

- The muonic hydrogen measurement gives r_p = 0.84184(67) fm (Pohl et al., 2010)
- The CODATA value from electronic hydrogen gives r_p = 0.8768(69) fm (Mohr et al., 2008)
- The new Mainz electron scattering result gives r_p = 0.879(8) fm (Bernauer et al., 2010)
- The deviation between muonic and electronic determinations is ≈ 0.04 fm with 5 standard deviations significance
- The Bernauer-Arrington fit assembly yields ⟨r²⟩ = 0.774(8) fm², ⟨r¹⟩(₂) = 1.085(3) fm, ⟨r³⟩(₂) = 2.85(8) fm³, and f = 4.18(13)
- The standard dipole gives ⟨r³⟩(₂) = 2.023 fm³ and f = 3.789
- De Rújula's toy model gives ⟨r³⟩(₂) = 36.2 fm³ and f = 53.5, with rms radius 0.771 fm²
- Using the new f = 4.18 would increase the muonic r_p by +0.00025 fm to 0.84245(67) fm
- The Lamb shift formula is L^(5th) = (209.9779(49) - 5.2262⟨r²⟩/fm² + 0.00913⟨r³⟩(₂)/fm³) meV
- The difference of 0.038 fm between the two radius determinations corresponds to a Lamb shift of -0.341 meV
- A particle-antiparticle pair producing such a shift would have mass 23 MeV

## Context

This work extends the precise form factor measurements of Bernauer et al. (2010) at the Mainz Microtron MAMI and the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement of Pohl et al. (2010). It directly contradicts the proposal of De Rújula (2010) that a large third Zemach moment could resolve the 5σ proton radius puzzle, confirming the earlier contradiction shown by Cloët and Miller (2010) using older data. The paper enables future work on hadronic corrections to the Lamb shift, suggesting that if QED calculations are exact, the precise electronic radius and the new Zemach moments could be used to determine vacuum polarization by quark loops at very low Q² scales.
