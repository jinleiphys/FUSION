# Transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at next-to-leading order

> Within the framework of higher-twist collinear factorization, transverse momentum broadening for the final hadrons in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic $e+A$ collisions is studied at the next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD. Through explicit calculations of real and virtual corrections at twist-4, the transverse-momentum-weighted differential cross section due to double scattering is shown to factorize at NLO and can be expressed as a convolution of twist-4 nuclear parton correlation functions, the usual twist-2 fragmentation functions and hard parts which are finite and free of any divergences. A QCD evolution equation is also derived for the renormalized twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function which can be applied to future phenomenological studies of transverse momentum broadening and jet quenching at NLO.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off nuclear targets can be factorized at next-to-leading order within the higher-twist collinear factorization framework, provided one considers only the double-scattering contribution and absorbs collinear divergences into renormalized twist-4 quark-gluon correlation functions and standard fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors performed explicit one-loop calculations of real and virtual corrections at twist-4 for the transverse-momentum-weighted differential cross section in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering. They classified secondary scatterings as soft-soft, hard-hard, and their interferences, carried out collinear expansion of hard parts in transverse momentum, and isolated divergences using dimensional regularization in the MSbar scheme. The remaining collinear divergences were absorbed into the renormalization of the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function and the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi evolution of fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order transverse momentum broadening is given by \(\Delta\langle\ell_{hT}^2\rangle = \left(\frac{4\pi^2\alpha_s z_h^2}{N_c}\right)\frac{\sum_q e_q^2 T_{qg}(x_B,0,0) D_{h/q}(z_h)}{\sum_q e_q^2 f_{q/A}(x_B) D_{h/q}(z_h)}\), where \(N_c=3\), \(C_F=(N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)\), and \(T_R=1/2\).
- The LO double-scattering contribution to the weighted cross section is \(\frac{d\langle\ell_{hT}^2\sigma^D\rangle^{\rm (LO)}}{d{\cal PS}} = \sigma_h \sum_q e_q^2 \int \frac{dx}{x} T_{qg}(x,0,0) \int \frac{dz}{z} D_{h/q}(z) \delta(1-\hat x)\delta(1-\hat z)\), with \(\sigma_h = (4\pi^2\alpha_s z_h^2/N_c)\sigma_0\) and \(\sigma_0 = \frac{2\pi\alpha_{em}^2}{Q^2}\frac{1+(1-y)^2}{y}(1-\epsilon)\).
- The soft-soft double scattering contribution contains a double-pole divergence \(1/\epsilon^2\) and single-pole divergences \(1/\epsilon\) in dimensional regularization with \(n=4-2\epsilon\), with the divergent piece proportional to \(C_F \int dx/x\, T_{qg}(x,0,0) [\frac{2}{\epsilon^2}\delta(1-\hat x)\delta(1-\hat z) - \frac{1}{\epsilon}\delta(1-\hat x)\frac{1+\hat z^2}{\hat z^2(1-\hat z)_+} - \frac{1}{\epsilon}\delta(1-\hat z)\frac{1+\hat x^2}{(1-\hat x)_+}]\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order higher-twist factorization formalism for transverse momentum broadening developed by Guo and Wang (2000) and earlier by Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1992-1994) to next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD. It provides the first complete NLO calculation of the twist-4 double-scattering contribution to transverse-momentum-weighted SIDIS cross sections, including a QCD evolution equation for the renormalized twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function. This enables future phenomenological studies of jet quenching and transverse momentum broadening at NLO accuracy, which is essential for more precise extraction of medium properties from experimental data at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, the Large Hadron Collider, and the proposed Electron Ion Collider.
