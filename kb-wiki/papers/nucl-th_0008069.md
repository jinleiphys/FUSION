# Partial Dynamical Symmetry in a Fermionic Many-Body System

# Partial Dynamical Symmetry in a Fermionic Many-Body System

## Key claim
The paper introduces partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) for an interacting fermion system, showing that the associated Hamiltonians are closely related to the realistic nuclear quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. The PDS Hamiltonians are not SU(3) invariant, yet they possess a subset of "special" states that respect the symmetry, including all 0ℏω states and some excited configurations.

## Method
The authors construct PDS Hamiltonians within the symplectic shell model (SSM) framework, using the symplectic generators that create and annihilate 2ℏω excitations. They analyze the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction expressed in terms of SU(3) and Sp(6,R) Casimir invariants, and derive a family of Hamiltonians H(β₀,β₂) that exhibit partial SU(3) symmetry. The method is applied to ¹²C by comparing energy spectra and wave function decompositions between the PDS Hamiltonian and the exact quadrupole-quadrupole interaction.

## Key numbers
- The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction connects harmonic oscillator states differing in energy by 0ℏω, ±2ℏω, and ±4ℏω.
- For β₀=12, β₂=18, the Hamiltonian H(β₀,β₂) reproduces the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction up to constant terms and shell-coupling terms: Q₂·Q₂ = H(β₀=12,β₂=18) + const(N) − 3L̂² + terms coupling different shells.
- The PDS calculation for ¹²C is performed with an 8ℏω basis for the Q₂·Q₂ calculation, and the PDS eigenstates belong entirely to one level of excitation (0ℏω, 2ℏω, 4ℏω, or 6ℏω) without inter-shell mixing.

## Context
This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry, previously developed in bosonic systems, to fermionic many-body systems within the symplectic shell model of nuclei (SymplM). The PDS Hamiltonians are closely related to the nuclear quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, which is a key ingredient in models of quadrupole collective properties. The approach enables analytical evaluation of energies and E2 transition rates for the solvable rotational band states, as further detailed in Escher (2000b). The application to ¹²C demonstrates that PDS eigenstates approximately reproduce the structure of exact quadrupole-quadrupole eigenstates for both ground and resonance bands, though structural differences arise in sensitive interband transition rates.
