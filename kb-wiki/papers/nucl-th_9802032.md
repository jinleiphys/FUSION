# Center-of-mass corrections revisited: a many-body expansion approach

> A many-body expansion for the computation of the charge form factor in the center-of-mass system is proposed. For convergence testing purposes, we apply our formalism to the case of the harmonic oscillator shell model, where an exact solution exists. We also work out the details of the calculation involving realistic nuclear wave functions. Results obtained for the Argonne $v$18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions are reported. No corrections due to the meson-exchange charge density are taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a many-body expansion method to compute the charge form factor in the center-of-mass system, and claims that truncating the expansion at third order provides a good approximation for the independent-particle model wave function, with the caveat that no corrections due to meson-exchange charge density are included.

## Method

The authors expand the translation-invariant form factor operator as a series of one-body, two-body, and three-body terms using second quantization formalism, with the operator expressed in terms of spherical Bessel functions and spherical harmonics. They test convergence by applying the expansion to harmonic oscillator shell-model wave functions where an exact analytic solution exists, then apply the same expansion to realistic nuclear wave functions for 16O obtained from the coupled cluster (exp(S)) method using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- For 4He and 16O harmonic oscillator test cases, the many-body expansion shows excellent agreement with the exact form factor for momentum transfer q less than 3 fm^-1, and remains reasonably good for q up to 4 fm^-1
- In the 16O realistic calculation, correlations shift the first diffraction minimum by 5 percent to the right
- The three-body term contribution changes the rms radius by less than 1 percent in the 16O case
- For 4He, omitting the three-body term produces false minima at momentum transfer q as low as 3.6 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the long-standing problem of center-of-mass corrections in nuclear shell-model calculations, which was previously solved exactly only for harmonic oscillator wave functions by Elliott and Skyrme (1954). The method enables calculation of translation-invariant form factors for general Hartree-Fock wave functions with correlations, and the results for 16O show a second diffraction minimum consistent with experimental observations by Sick and McCarthy (1970). The approach can be applied to heavier nuclei where higher-order terms in the expansion become less important due to the 1/A dependence.
