# Two-nucleon scattering in multiple partial waves

> We determine scattering phase shifts for S,P,D, and F partial wave channels in two-nucleon systems using lattice QCD methods. We use a generalization of Luscher's finite volume method to determine infinite volume phase shifts from a set of finite volume ground- and excited-state energy levels on two volumes, V=(3.4 fm)^3 and V=(4.5 fm)^3. The calculations are performed in the SU(3)-flavor limit, corresponding to a pion mass of approximately 800 MeV. From the energy dependence of the phase shifts we are able to extract scattering parameters corresponding to an effective range expansion.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon scattering phase shifts for S-, P-, D-, and F-wave channels, but the results are obtained in the SU(3)-flavor limit corresponding to an unphysical pion mass of approximately 800 MeV, and the analysis neglects all partial wave mixing as a first approximation.

## Method

The authors use a generalization of Luscher's finite volume method to relate finite-volume energy levels to infinite-volume scattering phase shifts, employing displaced nucleon-nucleon operators at the source and momentum-space operators at the sink corresponding to exact non-interacting eigenstates. Calculations are performed on two lattice volumes, (3.4 fm)^3 and (4.5 fm)^3, using isotropic clover configurations with lattice spacing b ~ 0.145 fm. The phase shifts are extracted from the energy dependence of the finite-volume spectra and fit to an effective range expansion (ERE) to determine scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: b ~ 0.145 fm
- Two volumes: V = (3.4 fm)^3 (24^3 x 48 lattice) and V = (4.5 fm)^3 (32^3 x 48 lattice)
- Pion mass: approximately 800 MeV (SU(3)-flavor limit)
- Kaon mass: approximately 800 MeV
- The t-channel cut occurs at momentum q = m_pi/2, above which the ERE is expected to break down
- Deeply bound states identified in both the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 channels, with a possible second bound state near threshold in the ^3S_1 channel

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane 2013) to include P-, D-, and F-wave channels for the first time using the Luscher finite volume method. The results enable future calculations of multi-nucleon matrix elements, including the parity-violating neutral weak current and the Delta I=2 hadronic parity violating matrix element involving the ^3P_0 to ^1S_0 transition (Kurth 2015). The method is particularly successful for the ^3P_2 channel, showing consistent behavior across multiple cubic irreps and volumes, and represents a step toward studying few- and many-body nuclear systems from first principles.
