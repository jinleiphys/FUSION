# Comparative study of nuclear effects in polarized electron scattering from 3He

> We present a detailed analysis of nuclear effects in inclusive electron scattering from polarized 3He nuclei for polarization asymmetries, structure functions and their moments, both in the nucleon resonance and deep-inelastic regions. We compare the results of calculations within the weak binding approximation at finite Q^2 with the effective polarization ansatz often used in experimental data analyses, and explore the impact of Δ components in the nuclear wave function and nucleon off-shell corrections on extractions of the free neutron structure. Using the same framework we also make predictions for the Q^2 dependence of quasielastic scattering from polarized 3He, data on which can be used to constrain the spin-dependent nuclear smearing functions in 3He.

## Key claim

Nuclear corrections in polarized electron scattering from ³He are computed within the weak binding approximation at finite Q², and the effective polarization ansatz commonly used in experimental analyses is shown to be an approximation that becomes exact only in the limit of zero nuclear binding and γ → 1, with deviations that depend on kinematics and the nuclear wave function model.

## Method

The authors compute spin-dependent structure functions g₁ and g₂ for polarized ³He using convolution formulas with nucleon light-cone momentum distributions (smearing functions) derived in the weak binding approximation to order p²/M², using realistic spectral functions from the KPSV and SS models. They compare full finite-Q² results with the effective polarization approximation, and also incorporate contributions from Δ(1232) isobar admixtures in the nuclear wave function and nucleon off-shell corrections expanded about the on-shell limit.

## Key numbers

- Average neutron polarization ⟨σ_z⟩ⁿ = 0.856 (KPSV) and 0.888 (SS) from the spectral function integrals
- Average proton polarization ⟨σ_z⟩ᵖ = −0.029 (KPSV) and −0.022 (SS)
- Effective polarizations for the neutron: P₁ⁿ = 0.851 (KPSV), 0.884 (SS); P₂ⁿ = 0.844 (KPSV), 0.878 (SS)
- Effective polarizations for the proton: P₁ᵖ = −0.028 (KPSV), −0.021 (SS); P₂ᵖ = −0.028 (KPSV), −0.021 (SS)
- Sum of Δ transition effective polarizations P₁^{nΔ⁰} + P₁^{pΔ⁺} = −0.0125 using DSSV PDFs at Q² = 5 GeV²
- Ratio of axial charges g_A^{³H}/g_A = 0.956 ± 0.004 from tritium β decay

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of nuclear corrections in polarized ³He by Kulagin et al. (2004, 2005) within the weak binding approximation, and provides a systematic comparison with the effective polarization ansatz used in experimental analyses by groups such as Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1992–1997) and Bissey et al. (2001). The results enable more reliable extraction of free neutron spin structure functions from upcoming Jefferson Lab 12 GeV experiments, particularly for the d₂ moment measured in experiment E06-014 and for the neutron asymmetry A₁ⁿ.
