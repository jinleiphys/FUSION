# Coherent polychotomous waves from an attractive well

> A novel effect of a wave packet scattering off an attractive one- dimensional well is found numerically and analytically. For a wave packet narrower than the width of the well, the scattering proceeds through a quasi-bound state of almost zero energy. The wave reflected from the well is a polychotomous (multiple peak) monochromatic and coherent train. The transmitted wave is a spreading smooth wave packet. The effect is strong for low average speeds of the packet, and it disappears for wide packets.

# Coherent polychotomous waves from an attractive well

## Key claim

A wave packet narrower than the width of an attractive one-dimensional well can produce a reflected wave that is a coherent, monochromatic, multi-peak (polychotomous) train, provided the packet's initial width is smaller than the well width; the effect is strong for low average packet speeds and disappears for wide packets, with the reflected wave's speed being independent of the initial packet energy.

## Method

The author solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation numerically in coordinate space using the method of Goldberg et al., which conserves wave normalization with an error of less than 0.01% even after hundreds of thousands of time steps. A minimal-uncertainty Gaussian wave packet with initial width δ=0.5, mass m=20, and varying average momenta q was scattered off an attractive exponential well of depth A=1 and width w=1. The results were verified analytically using a square packet scattering off a square well, solved by contour integration of the Fourier amplitude in the complex momentum plane that avoids bound-state poles.

## Key numbers

- Initial wave packet width δ=0.5, well width w=1, mass m=20, well depth A=1 (dimensionless units throughout)
- Reflected wave center-of-mass speed approximately v=0.03, independent of the initial packet speed
- For the parameters used, the bound state condition gives k' ≈ 2π (wavenumber inside the well)
- The condition for the reflected wave wavenumber at formation is 4kw = π
- For a nucleon of 20 MeV energy scattering on a 30 MeV deep nuclear well, the well must exceed 3 Fermi in radius to satisfy the resonance condition
- The numerical method conserves normalization with error less than 0.01%

## Context

This work extends the textbook treatment of wave packet scattering off an attractive well (Merzbacher) by revealing a previously unnoticed resonant phenomenon. The polychotomous reflected wave is analogous to effects seen when a superintense laser field focuses on an atom (Grobe), where ionization is hindered and the wave function localizes. The effect may be tested in back-angle nuclear reactions, for example using O-16 as a target with 20 MeV nucleons, and could serve as a method to determine nuclear well depths or radii by measuring the dead time between bunches in the reflected wave beam.
