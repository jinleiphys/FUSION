# Neutral Current Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering. Theory

> The treatment of nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus interactions is one of the main sources of systematic uncertainty for the analysis and interpretation of data of neutrino oscillation experiments. Neutrinos interact with nuclei via charged or neutral currents and both cases must be studied to obtain a complete information. We give an overview of the theoretical work that has been done to describe nuclear effects in neutral-current neutrin onucleus scattering in the kinematic region ranging between beam energies of a few hundreds MeV to a few GeV, which is typical of most ongoing and future accelerator-based neutrino experiments, and where quasielastic scattering is the main interaction mechanism. We review the current status and challenges of the theoretical models, the role and relevance of the contributions of different nuclear effects, and the present status of the comparison between the numerical predictions of the models as well as the available experimental data. We discuss also the sensitivity to the strange form factors of the nucleon and the methods and observables that can allow one to obtain evidence for a possible strange quark contribution from measurements of neutrino and antineutrino-nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical models for neutral-current quasielastic (NCQE) neutrino-nucleus scattering, emphasizing that while many models were originally developed for electron-nucleus scattering and extended to neutrino scattering, the extension to the semi-inclusive NCQE case is less appropriate than for inclusive charged-current scattering, and that no single model can simultaneously describe all available data without including explicit nuclear effects beyond the impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical approaches to NCQE neutrino-nucleus scattering in the beam energy range from a few hundred MeV to a few GeV, where quasielastic scattering dominates. They compare models based on the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) and those incorporating additional nuclear effects such as final-state interactions (FSI), multinucleon processes, two-body meson-exchange currents (MEC), and np-nh excitations. The review examines the general formalism for the hadronic and leptonic tensors, the single-nucleon weak current, and the decomposition of cross sections into structure functions for inclusive and semi-inclusive processes.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi constant is given as G_F approximately 1.16639 x 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}

- The oversimplified relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model can describe MiniBooNE CCQE and NCE data with M_A approximately 1.35 GeV
- The kinematic region considered spans beam energies from a few hundred MeV to a few GeV
- Future neutrino experiments require models covering from 200 MeV to 10 GeV or more

## Context

This review extends the theoretical work on electron-nucleus scattering (Boffi et al., 1993; Donnelly and Sick, 2007) to neutral-current neutrino-nucleus interactions, complementing earlier reviews on neutrino-nucleus scattering (Gallagher et al., 2011; Alvarez-Ruso et al., 2014; Katori and Martini, 2016). The paper identifies that models validated against electron scattering data should be extended to neutrino scattering, but notes that the semi-inclusive nature of NCQE scattering (where only the ejected nucleon is detected) makes direct extension less straightforward than for inclusive charged-current scattering. The work enables future high-precision neutrino oscillation experiments (DUNE, T2HK) by clarifying the open challenges in modeling nuclear effects, particularly the need for consistent treatment of multinucleon processes, two-body currents, and strange form factor contributions.
