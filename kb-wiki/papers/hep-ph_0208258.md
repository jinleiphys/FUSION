# Quark-hadron duality in electron-pion scattering

> We explore the relationship between exclusive and inclusive electromagnetic scattering from the pion, focusing on the transition region at intermediate Q^2. Combining Drell-Yan data on the leading twist quark distribution in the pion with a model for the resonance region at large x, we calculate QCD moments of the pion structure function over a range of Q^2, and quantify the role of higher twist corrections. Using a parameterization of the pion elastic form factor and phenomenological models for the pi -> rho transition form factor, we further test the extent to which local duality may be valid for the pion.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether quark-hadron duality, well-established for the proton, also manifests for the pion, finding that higher twist effects in the pion structure function are larger than in the nucleon (by about 50%) due to the pion's smaller confinement radius, which makes the onset of duality less clean than for the proton.

## Method

The author combines Drell-Yan data on the leading twist quark distribution in the pion with a model for the resonance region at large x, including elastic and pi-to-rho transition contributions, to calculate QCD moments of the pion structure function over a range of Q^2. A parameterization of the pion elastic form factor is constructed from existing data, and several phenomenological models for the pi-to-rho transition form factor are used to quantify uncertainties. The higher twist contributions are extracted by subtracting the leading twist and target mass corrections from the total moments.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f_pi = 132 MeV, used in the asymptotic pQCD prediction for the elastic form factor.
- The monopole fit to the low Q^2 pion form factor data gives a cut-off mass of approximately 0.74 GeV.
- At Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, the low W region (W < 1 GeV) contributes as much as 50% to the total n=2 moment, decreasing to less than 1% for Q^2 > 10 GeV^2.
- The n=10 moment is almost completely saturated by the resonance region at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, and still receives about 40% of its strength from W < 1 GeV at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2.
- At Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, the higher twist contribution to the n=2 moment is as large as the leading twist, decreasing to about 1/3 at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, and vanishes rapidly for Q^2 > 5 GeV^2.
- The E615 Collaboration finds the value <k_T^2> = 0.8 +/- 0.3 GeV^2 for the intrinsic transverse momentum of quarks in the pion.
- The best fit exponent for the x to 1 behavior of the valence quark distribution from E615 data is b approximately 1.21 to 1.30.

## Context

This work extends the Bloom-Gilman duality analysis, previously applied to the proton by De Rujula et al. (1975) and more recent Jefferson Lab experiments, to the pion as the simplest q-qbar system in QCD. The study is timely given planned Jefferson Lab experiments on pion elastic and transition form factors (Mack, White; Kossov, Burkert) and new inclusive pion structure function measurements at HERA and planned at Jefferson Lab (Krishni). The analysis provides estimates of higher twist effects that can guide future lattice QCD calculations and help interpret upcoming experimental data on the interplay between soft and hard scattering from the pion.
