# Towards higher-order collinear splittings with massive partons

> The singularities associated with QCD factorization in the collinear limit are key ingredients for high-precision theoretical predictions in particle physics. They govern the collinear behaviour of scattering amplitudes, as well as the perturbative energy evolution of parton densities (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs). In this talk, we present the computation of multiple collinear and higher-order QCD splittings with massive partons. Our results are highly-relevant for the consistent introduction of mass effects in the subtraction formalism and PDF/FF evolution.

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of triple-collinear splitting amplitudes and (un)polarized splitting kernels with massive partons at tree level in QCD, showing that the factorization structure formally matches the massless case but with implicit and explicit mass dependence that regulates collinear singularities while producing large logarithmic corrections of the form \(\ln^p(Q^2/m^2)\).

## Method

The authors adopt a Sudakov parametrization generalized to massive partons, defining the quasi-collinear limit by uniformly rescaling masses and transverse momenta as \(\lambda \to 0\). They compute the full set of tree-level triple-collinear splitting amplitudes and unpolarized splitting kernels for QCD processes involving massive quarks, including the processes \(Q \to Q'_1 \bar{Q}'_2 Q_3\) and \(g \to g_1 Q_2 \bar{Q}_3\), and verify the massless limit reproduces known results from Catani (1999).

## Key numbers

- For the quark-initiated process \(\bar{Q}_1^\prime Q_2^\prime Q_3\), the unpolarized splitting kernel contains terms proportional to \(m_{Q^\prime}^2 / s_{12}^2\) and \(m_Q^2 \tilde{s}_{12} / s_{12}^2\), as well as terms of order \(m_{Q^\prime}^4 / s_{12}^2\) and \(m_Q^2 m_{Q^\prime}^2 / s_{12}^2\).
- For the gluon-initiated process \(g_1 Q_2 \bar{Q}_3\), the abelian part of the unpolarized splitting kernel includes terms proportional to \(m_Q^2 / \tilde{s}_{12}\) and \(m_Q^4 / \tilde{s}_{13}\).
- For the gluon-initiated process \(g_1 Q_2 \bar{Q}_3\), the non-abelian part of the unpolarized splitting kernel includes terms proportional to \(m_Q^2 / s_{23}^2\) and \(m_Q^4 / (\tilde{s}_{12} \tilde{s}_{13})\).

## Context

This work extends the massless triple-collinear splitting calculations of Catani (1999) to include massive partons, building on earlier double-parton quasi-collinear results by Keller (1998) and Catani (2000). The results enable the consistent inclusion of mass effects in subtraction frameworks for NNLO calculations and in parton shower generators, and could allow calculation of higher-order corrections to Altarelli-Parisi kernels for PDF/FF evolution with heavy quark masses. An independent calculation by Craft (2023) confirmed the quark-initiated massive triple-collinear splitting kernels.
