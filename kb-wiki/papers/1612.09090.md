# Triton and Neutron-Deuteron Scattering up to Next-to-Leading Order in Chiral EFT

> Determination of the proper power-counting scheme is an important issue for the systematic application of Chiral Effective Field Theory in nuclear physics. We analyze the cutoff dependence of three-nucleon observables (the neutron-deuteron scattering lengths and the triton binding energy) at the leading and next-to-leading orders of a power counting that ensures order-by-order renormalization in the two-nucleon system. Our results imply that three-body forces are not needed for renormalization of the three-nucleon system up to next-to-leading order, as usually assumed in the literature. (Erratum to the original article is included)

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to common assumptions in chiral effective field theory, three-body forces are not required for renormalization of the three-nucleon system up to next-to-leading order (NLO), though there is indirect evidence that such a three-body force might be numerically significant at higher orders.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the two-nucleon system at leading order (LO) using a chiral potential with one-pion exchange and contact interactions in all partial waves where the tensor force is attractive and non-perturbative, using exponential regulators with exponents n=2, 4, and 6. They then solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the three-nucleon system to compute the triton binding energy and neutron-deuteron scattering lengths, removing spurious deep two-body bound states via an orthogonalizing pseudo-potential technique. NLO corrections were treated perturbatively using distorted-wave Born approximation for the two-body system and first-order perturbation theory for three-body observables.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy: approached a constant for cutoff values up to 4 GeV with a separable momentum regulator of the form exp[-p^4/Λ^4], consistent with Nogga et al. (2005)
- Two-nucleon counterterms fitted to PWA93 phase-shift data at laboratory energy T_L = 5 MeV or 10 MeV (except ^3D_3 channel fitted globally up to T_L = 200 MeV)
- Singlet scattering length a_s = -23.75 fm and triplet scattering length a_t = 5.42 fm used for alternative S-wave fitting
- Effective range r_s = 2.77 fm used for NLO counterterm fitting
- Cutoff range extended to 10 GeV for two-nucleon renormalization analysis
- Deepest spurious bound state (least-bound ^3P_0 state) at approximately 170 MeV, giving an estimated breakdown scale M_hi ~ 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the LO cutoff dependence analysis of Nogga et al. (2005) by examining cutoff values up to 10 GeV and testing different regulator functions (exponential with n=2, 4, and 6). It contradicts the argument by Kievsky et al. (2016) that a three-nucleon force should be included at LO in chiral EFT, finding no renormalization rationale for such promotion. The analysis confirms the finding of Long and Yang (2012) that an NLO correction is needed in the ^1S_0 channel, and enables future work to determine at which order three-body forces actually become necessary for renormalization.
