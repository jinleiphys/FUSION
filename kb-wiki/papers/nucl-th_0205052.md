# Effective Lagrangian approach to the omega meson photoproduction near threshold

> We apply the effective Lagrangian approach to investigate the role of the nucleon resonances in $ω$ meson photoproduction at energies near the threshold. The resonant amplitudes are calculated from effective Lagrangians with the gamma NN* and omega NN* coupling constants fixed by the empirical helicity amplitudes and the vector meson dominance model. The contributions from the nucleon resonances are found to be significant in changing the differential cross sections in a wide interval of t and various spin observables.

# Effective Lagrangian approach to the omega meson photoproduction near threshold

## Key claim

The nucleon resonances contribute significantly to omega meson photoproduction near threshold (E_gamma ~ 1.1-1.25 GeV), but the quantitative predictions depend strongly on the choice of coupling constants within the uncertainties of empirical helicity amplitudes; only by allowing these coupling constants to vary within their PDG uncertainties (model II) can a good description of existing differential cross section and beam asymmetry data be achieved.

## Method

The authors apply an effective Lagrangian approach combining non-resonant amplitudes (pseudoscalar pi/eta exchange and nucleon Born terms, with parameters from OTL01) with resonant amplitudes from 12 nucleon resonances. The gamma N N* coupling constants are fixed using empirical PDG helicity amplitudes, while the omega N N* coupling constants are derived via the vector meson dominance model with gamma_omega = 8.53. The resonant amplitudes are calculated using fully covariant effective Lagrangians for spin-1/2, 3/2, 5/2, and 7/2 resonances, with finite decay widths and form factors (cutoff Lambda_N* = 0.7-0.75 GeV/c).

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: E_gamma ~ 1.1-1.25 GeV, near the omega production threshold
- Pseudoscalar exchange coupling constants: g_piNN/4pi = 14, g_etaNN/4pi = 0.99
- omega-gamma-pi coupling: g_omegagammapi = 1.823
- omega-gamma-eta coupling: g_omegagammaeta = 0.416
- omega NN coupling: g_omegaNN = 10.35, kappa_omega = 0
- Vector meson dominance coupling strengths: gamma_omega = 8.53, gamma_rho = 2.52
- Form factor cutoffs: Lambda_pi = Lambda^pi_omegagammapi = 0.6 GeV/c, Lambda_eta = 1 GeV/c, Lambda_omegagammaeta = 0.9 GeV/c, Lambda_B = Lambda_N = 0.5 GeV
- Best-fit cutoff for resonant amplitudes: Lambda_N* = 0.7 GeV/c (model I), 0.75 GeV/c (model II)
- 12 nucleon resonances included: P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535), S11(1650), D15(1675), F15(1680), D13(1700), P11(1710), P13(1720), F17(1990), D13(2080), G17(2190)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of omega photoproduction at higher energies (Zhao et al. 1998-1999 using SU(6)xO(3) constituent quark model; OTL01 using constituent quark model with configuration mixing) down to the near-threshold region where well-established low-lying nucleon resonances become important. It differs from the approach of Riska and Brown (2000) by using empirical helicity amplitudes rather than quark model predictions for gamma N N* couplings, and from Post and Mosel (2000) by using a fully covariant formulation. The results enable future experimental tests at facilities like Jefferson Lab, ELSA-SAPHIR, GRAAL, and LEPS, particularly through measurements of single and double spin asymmetries which the authors predict will discriminate between their two models.
