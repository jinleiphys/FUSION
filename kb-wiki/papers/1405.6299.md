# Vacuum polarization of graphene with a supercritical Coulomb impurity: Low-energy universality and discrete scale invariance

> We study massless Dirac fermions in a supercritical Coulomb potential with the emphasis on that its low-energy physics is universal and parametrized by a single quantity per supercritical angular momentum channel. This low-energy parameter with the dimension of length is defined only up to multiplicative factors and thus each supercritical channel exhibits the discrete scale invariance. In particular, we show that the induced vacuum polarization has a power-law tail whose coefficient is a sum of log-periodic functions with respect to the distance from the potential center. This coefficient can also be expressed in terms of the energy and width of so-called atomic collapse resonances. Our universal predictions on the vacuum polarization and its relationship to atomic collapse resonances shed new light on the longstanding fundamental problem of quantum electrodynamics and can in principle be tested by graphene experiments with charged impurities.

# Vacuum polarization of graphene with a supercritical Coulomb impurity: Low-energy universality and discrete scale invariance

## Key claim

The low-energy physics of massless Dirac fermions in a supercritical Coulomb potential is universal and parametrized by a single quantity per supercritical angular momentum channel, but this low-energy parameter is defined only up to multiplicative factors of e^{π/√(g²−j²)} and thus each supercritical channel exhibits discrete scale invariance rather than a simple constant coefficient for the power-law tail of the induced vacuum polarization.

## Method

The author solves the two-dimensional Dirac equation for massless Dirac fermions in a supercritical Coulomb potential using a Green's function method with partial-wave expansion. The radial Green's function is constructed from solutions to the radial Dirac equation, with the short-distance boundary condition in supercritical angular momentum channels (|j| < |g|) parametrized by a single length scale r_j^*. The vacuum polarization electron density is then computed from the trace of the Green's function integrated over imaginary energy, with renormalization performed by requiring the total induced electron number to vanish.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for the supercritical regime is |g| > 1/2, where g = Ze²/(4πε₀ħv_F) is the dimensionless coupling constant and v_F ≈ 10⁶ m/s is the Fermi velocity of graphene.
- The corresponding "fine structure constant" in graphene is e²/(4πε₀ħv_F) ≈ 2.
- The log-periodic function F_j(r/r_j^*) satisfies F_j(r/r_j^*) = F_j(e^{nπ/γ} r/r_j^*) with γ = √(g²−j²) and n an arbitrary integer; for the example shown (j=1/2, g=4/3), one period spans 0 ≤ ln(ρ) ≤ π/γ ≈ 2.55.
- The mean value of the coefficient of the power-law tail is F̄_j(r/r_j^*) = (γ/2π²) sgn(g).
- For the tight-binding lattice calculation with g=4/3 and 124² lattice sites, the fit to the predicted form r²n(r) ≈ 2γ/π² − A sin[2γ ln(r/a) + φ] yields A ≈ 0.11 and φ ≈ 1.85, with the mean value 2γ/π² ≈ 0.25.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vacuum polarization in graphene under supercritical Coulomb potentials by Pereira (2007), Shytov (2007a), and others, which had considered the coefficient of the power-law tail to be a constant. The author shows that the coefficient is actually a log-periodic function due to discrete scale invariance, a phenomenon also known from nonrelativistic one-body problems (Case, 1950), two-body problems (Nishida, 2012), and the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970). The results enable experimental tests using scanning probe microscopy on graphene with charged impurities (Wang, 2013) and provide a relationship between vacuum polarization and atomic collapse resonances that can be used to predict one from the other.
