# On the role of isospin violation in the pion-nucleon $σ$-term

> In recent years, a persistent tension between phenomenological and lattice QCD determinations of the pion-nucleon $σ$-term $σ_{πN}$ has developed. In particular, lattice-QCD calculations have matured to the point that isospin-violating effects need to be included. Here, we point out that the standard conventions adopted in both fields are incompatible, with the data-driven extraction based on the charged-pion mass, but lattice-QCD conventions relying on the mass of the neutral pion to define the isospin limit. The corresponding correction amounts to $Δσ_{πN}=3.1(5)\,\text{MeV}$ when evaluated in chiral perturbation theory with low-energy constants determined from a Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering as well as $σ_{πN}$ itself. It reduces the tension with lattice QCD, and should be included in the comparison to phenomenological determinations. We also update the extraction from pionic atoms accounting for the latest measurement of the width of pionic hydrogen, $σ_{πN}=59.0(3.5)\,\text{MeV}$, and provide the corresponding set of scalar couplings of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The persistent tension between phenomenological and lattice QCD determinations of the pion-nucleon σ-term arises partly because the two fields use incompatible definitions of the isospin limit: phenomenology defines the σ-term using the charged-pion mass, while lattice QCD uses the neutral-pion mass. The correction to convert between conventions is ΔσπN = 3.1(5) MeV, which reduces the tension but does not fully resolve it.

## Method

The authors computed the isospin-violating correction ΔσπN using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at orders p², p³, and p⁴, with low-energy constants (LECs) determined from a Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering and the σ-term itself. They also updated the extraction of πN scattering lengths from pionic atoms by including the final PSI measurement of the pionic hydrogen width Γ1sπH = 0.856(27) eV, then used the master formula σπN = 59.1(3.1) MeV + Σ cIs(aIs - aIs0) to obtain the updated σ-term.

## Key numbers

- ΔσπN = 3.1(5) MeV: the correction to convert between charged-pion and neutral-pion isospin-limit conventions, evaluated in ChPT at O(p⁴)
- σπN = 59.0(3.5) MeV: updated phenomenological value from pionic atoms including the new pionic hydrogen width measurement
- Γ1sπH = 0.856(27) eV: final PSI result for the width of ground-state pionic hydrogen
- aπ-pcex = -124.3(2.0) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹: charge-exchange scattering length from the pionic hydrogen width
- a⁻ = 86.6(1.0) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹: isovector scattering length from the global fit of pionic atom data
- a⁺ = 7.8(2.6) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹: isoscalar scattering length from the global fit
- fup = 19.7(1.4) × 10⁻³, fdp = 38.3(2.7) × 10⁻³: scalar couplings of the proton
- fun = 17.8(1.3) × 10⁻³, fdn = 42.3(2.6) × 10⁻³: scalar couplings of the neutron

## Context

This work extends the Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering by Hoferichter et al. (2015) and the pionic-atom constraints from Baru et al. (2010, 2011). It addresses the tension between phenomenological σπN values around 59 MeV and lower lattice QCD results from multiple groups including the FLAG collaboration (2021). The correction enables more meaningful comparisons between data-driven extractions and lattice calculations, and the updated scalar couplings are relevant for dark matter direct detection, neutrino scattering, and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
