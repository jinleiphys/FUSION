# Hadrons in Medium -- Theory confronts experiment

> In this talk we briefly summarize our theoretical understanding of in-medium selfenergies of hadrons. With the special case of the $ω$ meson we demonstrate that earlier calculations that predicted a significant lowering of the mass in medium are based on an incorrect treatment of the model Lagrangian; more consistent calculations lead to a significant broadening, but hardly any mass shift. We stress that the experimental reconstruction of hadron spectral functions from measured decay products always requires knowledge of the decay branching ratios which may also be strongly mass-dependent. It also requires a quantitatively reliable treatment of final state interactions which has to be part of any reliable theory.

## Key claim

Earlier calculations that predicted a significant lowering of the omega meson mass in nuclear medium are based on an incorrect treatment of the model Lagrangian; more consistent calculations that avoid the heavy-baryon approximation lead to a significant broadening (about 60 MeV at saturation density) but hardly any mass shift, with the caveat that the low-density approximation used may not be reliable up to nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors recalculated the in-medium selfenergy of the omega meson using the same effective Lagrangian as Klingl et al. (1997, 1999) but without invoking the heavy-baryon approximation, evaluating the omega-nucleon forward-scattering amplitude on tree-level. They also performed a separate large-scale K-matrix analysis of all available gamma N and pi N data that respects unitarity and constrains the 2-pi channel by inelasticities in the 1-pi channel, using the low-density theorem to relate the selfenergy to the forward-scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell width of the omega meson in nuclear medium reaches about 50 MeV at saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}) for an omega at rest.
- At omega momentum of about 500 MeV (the region where CBELSA/TAPS measures), the transverse width increases to about 125 MeV and the polarization-averaged width amounts to 100 MeV.
- The in-medium spectral function peak from the tree-level calculation without heavy-baryon approximation is shifted to 544 MeV, compared to the Klingl et al. result of about 620 MeV.
- The CBELSA/TAPS data indicate a lowering of the omega mass by about 60 MeV in photon-produced experiments on nuclei.
- The measured cross section dependence on mass number A is reproduced if the inelastic omega-N cross section is increased by 25% over the usually used parametrization.

## Context

This work extends and corrects earlier calculations by Klingl et al. (1997, 1999) who predicted a dropping omega mass using a heavy-baryon approximation that the authors show is unjustified. It also builds on coupled-channel approaches by Lutz et al. (2002) who solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation and found a complex spectral function with a second peak, and on K-matrix analyses by Penner and Mosel (2002) and Shklyar et al. (2006). The results enable more reliable comparisons with CBELSA/TAPS experimental data and highlight the need for quantitatively treating final-state interactions and mass-dependent branching ratios when reconstructing spectral functions from decay products.
