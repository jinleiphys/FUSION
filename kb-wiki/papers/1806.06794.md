# A new and finite family of solutions of hydrodynamics. Part I: Fits to pseudorapidity distributions

> We highlight some of the interesting properties of a new and finite, exact family of solutions of 1 + 1 dimensional perfect fluid relativistic hydrodynamics. After reviewing the main properties of this family of solutions, we present the formulas that connect it to the measured rapidity and pseudo-rapidity densities and illustrate the results with fits to p+p collisions at 8 TeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02 $ TeV.

# A new and finite family of solutions of hydrodynamics. Part I: Fits to pseudorapidity distributions

## Key claim

The CKCJ family of exact 1+1 dimensional perfect fluid hydrodynamics solutions describes pseudorapidity distributions in both p+p collisions at 8 TeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but only when fits are restricted to mid-rapidity (|η_x| < 2.5) and the speed of sound is fixed to a constant value of c_s² = 0.1, with the acceleration parameter λ and midrapidity density as the primary shape-controlling parameters.

## Method

The authors embed a 1+1 dimensional exact solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics (the CKCJ solution, expressed parametrically in terms of the fluid rapidity Ω and proper time τ) into 1+3 dimensions, assume a freeze-out hypersurface pseudo-orthogonal to the four-velocity, and apply saddle-point integration to derive analytic formulas for rapidity and pseudorapidity density distributions. The pseudorapidity density is obtained as a parametric curve in momentum-space rapidity y, incorporating a new explicit relation for the rapidity-dependent average transverse momentum p̅_T(y) that depends on the equation-of-state parameter κ, particle mass m, and effective temperature T_eff but is independent of the acceleration parameter λ.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound fixed to c_s² = 1/κ = 0.1, based on the PHENIX measurement of c_s = 0.35 ± 0.05 in √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions (Adare, 2006)
- Acceleration parameter λ = 1.14 used for illustrative solutions with c_s² = 0.1
- Fits to CMS p+p data at √s = 8 TeV use fixed T_eff = 145 MeV
- Fits to ALICE Pb+Pb data at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (40-50% centrality) use fixed T_eff = 270 MeV
- Fit range uniformly limited to |η_x| < 2.5 to satisfy validity conditions
- Validity condition for mid-rapidity approximation: |y| < 1/(λ - 1)

## Context

This work extends the Csörgő-Nagy-Csanád (CNC) family of solutions (Csörgő, 2006; Nagy, 2007) to a more general, accelerating, and longitudinally finite family that includes the Hwa-Bjorken boost-invariant solution (Hwa, 1974; Bjorken, 1982) and the Csörgő-Grassi-Hama-Kodama (CGHK) family (Csörgő, 2003) as limiting cases. The results confirm similar findings by Ze-Fang (2017) based on the CNC solutions, and the rapidity-dependent average transverse momentum formula reproduces the Lorentzian shape previously obtained in the Buda-Lund hydro model (Csörgő, 1995). The solution differs from the Bialas-Janik-Peschanski (BJP) solution (Bialas, 2007) in that the fluid rapidity as a function of space-time rapidity is independent of proper time. This work enables extraction of the acceleration parameter λ from pseudorapidity distributions without requiring particle identification, and suggests that longitudinal expansion dynamics in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies are very similar.
