# Particle-hole state densities with non-equidistant single-particle levels

> The correct use of energy-dependent single-particle level (s.p.l.) densities within particle-hole state densities based on the equidistant spacing model (ESM) is analysed. First, an analytical expression is obtained following the convolution of energy-dependent excited-particle and hole densities. Next, a comparison is made with results of the ESM formula using average s.p.l. densities for the excited particles and holes, respectively. The Fermi-gas model (FGM) s.p.l. densities calculated at the corresponding average excitation energies are used in both cases. The analysis concerns also the density of particle-hole bound states. The pairing correlations are taken into account while the comparison of various effects includes the exact correction for the Pauli exclusion principle. Quantum-mechanical s.p.l. densities and the continuum effect can also match a corresponding FGM formula, suitable for use within the average energy-dependent partial state density in multistep reaction models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using average energy-dependent single-particle level densities within the standard equidistant spacing model (ESM) formula yields particle-hole state densities that are "rather close" to the exact convolution of energy-dependent single-particle level densities, but only for medium excitation energies; the agreement degrades near the maximum energy for a given particle-hole configuration.

## Method

The authors derived an analytical recursive formula for particle-hole state densities by convolving energy-dependent single-particle level densities for excited particles and holes, using the Fermi-gas model (FGM) single-particle level density with a quadratic expansion around the Fermi energy. They then compared this exact expression with an approximate ESM formula that uses average single-particle level densities evaluated at the mean excitation energies of particles and holes separately, following the approach of Kalbach (1985). The comparison included corrections for the finite depth of the nuclear potential well, the Pauli exclusion principle (both approximate and exact treatments), and pairing correlations using the Kalbach (1987-89) formulation.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi energy \(F\) is taken as 38 MeV, the single-particle level density at the Fermi level \(g_0 = 14\) MeV\(^{-1}\), and the nucleon binding energy \(B = 10\) MeV for the global calculations.
- The ground-state pairing gap \(\Delta_0 = 1\) MeV is used in the pairing correction calculations.
- The ratio of the exact recursive formula to the approximate average energy-dependent ESM formula shows agreement within about 1% for the exact Pauli correction calculation just above the threshold for each particle-hole configuration.
- The critical number of excitons for pairing phase transition is given by \(n_c = 0.792 g_0 \Delta_0\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Gadioli et al. (1973, 1977), Kalbach (1981, 1985), and Herman et al. (1989) to incorporate energy-dependent single-particle level densities into preequilibrium reaction formalisms. It provides a systematic comparison between the exact convolution method and the simpler average-energy approach, validating the latter for practical use in multistep reaction models. The analysis enables improved calculations of partial state densities for preequilibrium emission, particularly by quantifying the relative importance of the single-particle level density energy dependence versus exact Pauli correction calculations, and by providing a framework to include the continuum effect from quantum-mechanical calculations of Shlomo (1992, 1997).
