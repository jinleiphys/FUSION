# Pion observables calculated in Minkowski and Euclidean spaces with Ansätze for quark propagators

> We study two quark--propagator meromorphic Ansätze that admit clear connection between calculations in Euclidean space and Minkowski spacetime. The connection is established through a modified Wick rotation in momentum space, where the integration contour along the imaginary axis is adequately deformed. The Ansätze were previously proposed in the literature and fitted to Euclidean lattice QCD data. The generalized impulse approximation is used to calculate the pion transition form factor and electromagnetic form factor, correcting an earlier result. The pion decay constant and distribution amplitude are also calculated. The latter is used to deduce the asymptotic behavior of the form factors. Such an asymptotic behavior is compared with those obtained directly from the generalized impulse approximation and the causes of differences are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two meromorphic Ansätze for quark propagators (MMF and ADFM), which were previously fitted to Euclidean lattice QCD data, can be consistently used to calculate pion observables in both Euclidean and Minkowski spaces through a modified Wick rotation, but their inability to reproduce perturbative QCD asymptotic behavior impairs the calculation of high-Q² form factor behavior.

## Method

The authors used the generalized impulse approximation with the Ball-Chiu vertex for quark-photon coupling and the Goldberger-Treiman relation for the pion Bethe-Salpeter vertex to calculate the pion decay constant, electromagnetic form factor, transition form factor, and distribution amplitude. For the MMF Ansatz, calculations were performed using three independent methods: analytic evaluation with FeynCalc and PACKAGE-X packages, numerical integration in Euclidean space, and Minkowski space integration using light-cone variables and Cauchy's residue theorem. For the ADFM Ansatz, a modified Wick rotation with a deformed integration contour passing between poles in the complex q⁰ plane was employed.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant fπ = 87.5599 MeV for the MMF Ansatz (compared to 90 MeV in Mello et al. (2017))
- Pion decay constant fπ = 71.5611 MeV for the ADFM Ansatz
- Pion charge radius rπ = 0.632 fm for MMF Ansatz and rπ = 0.699 fm for ADFM Ansatz (experimental value: 0.659 ± 0.004 fm)
- Fπ(0) = 0.950 for MMF Ansatz and Fπ(0) = 1.32 for ADFM Ansatz
- MMF Ansatz parameters: m₀ = 0.014 GeV, m = 0.574 GeV, λ = 0.846 GeV
- ADFM Ansatz parameters: a₁ = 0.341 GeV, a₂ = -1.31 GeV, a₃ = -1.35919 GeV, r₁ = 0.365, r₂ = 1.2, r₃ = -1.065, Z₂ = 0.982731

## Context

This work extends the approach of Mello, de Melo, and Frederico (2017) and Alkofer, Detmold, Fischer, and Maris (2003), who proposed meromorphic Ansätze for quark propagators fitted to lattice QCD data. The authors correct an earlier result for the pion electromagnetic form factor from Mello et al. (2017) and demonstrate that while these simple meromorphic Ansätze work well for low-energy observables, their failure to reproduce the logarithmic corrections of perturbative QCD asymptotics limits their applicability at high momentum transfers. The work enables future comparisons between Euclidean and Minkowski space calculations of hadron observables using analytically continued propagators.
