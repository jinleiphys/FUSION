# Mott mechanism and anomalous charmonium suppression

> We investigate a scenario where conditions for Mott dissociation of charmonium are fulfilled locally in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at the short time scales before hadronization and study possible consequences to be drawn from observation of ``anomalous'' thresholds in the J/ψsuppression pattern. We suggest that for a compilation of experiments with different kinematics, variables presently in use to display the observed suppression pattern should be rescaled by the $γ$ factor of the $c\bar c$ state relative to the participant center of mass. This systematics can be tested by inverse kinematics experiments as, e.g., Pb-S collisions and by sampling data sets in p_T and y of the dimuon pairs.

# Mott mechanism and anomalous charmonium suppression

## Key claim
The authors propose that the "anomalous" threshold in J/ψ suppression observed by NA50 in Pb-Pb collisions can be explained by Mott dissociation of charmonium states *in statu nascendi*, provided that the participant density variable is rescaled by the Lorentz factor γ of the c\(\bar c\) pair relative to the participant center of mass. The universality of this threshold behaviour across different collision systems depends on this rescaling, and the authors suggest that quark-gluon plasma may be present in S-U collisions even though the J/ψ suppression there is not "anomalous" when measured without the γ rescaling.

## Method
The authors implement a Glauber model for J/ψ production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, modified by a step-function cutoff in the transverse plane that removes regions where the participant density exceeds a critical Mott density. The critical Mott density for a c\(\bar c\) pair *in statu nascendi* is taken to scale as γ^β (with β=2) relative to the rest-frame value, where γ depends on the rapidity shift Δy and transverse momentum p_T of the dimuon pair. The total J/ψ survival probability includes a 30% feeding from χ_c decays, and the model is applied to S-U and Pb-Pb collisions at CERN-SpS.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear absorption cross section σ_abs = 7.3 mb, compatible with pA data, gives an excellent description of J/ψ production in AB collisions as performed by the NA38 experiment.
- For S-U collisions, the mean γ² value is <γ²> = 2.6, and the authors state that at this value the J/ψ production as measured by NA38 is not "anomalous".
- The time scale for the participant density to reach its maximum is t₀ = τ₀ γ ~ 1 fm/c.
- The two thresholds in the suppression pattern correspond to Mott dissociation of the χ_c (1p state) and the J/ψ (1s state), respectively.

## Context
This work extends the Glauber model approach to J/ψ suppression previously applied by Kharzeev et al. (1996) and others, which successfully described pA and light-ion data but failed for the Pb-Pb data from the NA50 experiment (Gonin, 1996). The authors build on the suggestion by Matsui and Satz (1986) that charmonium dissociation in a quark-gluon plasma would signal deconfinement, and on earlier work by Blaschke et al. (1991) and Röpke et al. (1993) on Mott transitions in dense matter. The key new element is the γ-dependent rescaling of the critical density, which predicts that the threshold position depends on the kinematics of the c\(\bar c\) pair. This can be tested by inverse kinematics experiments (e.g., Pb-S collisions) and by binning NA50 data in p_T and rapidity. A more detailed kinetic treatment of the hadronization process is stated to be in progress.
