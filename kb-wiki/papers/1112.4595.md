# Extension of the Schiff theorem to ions and molecules

> According to the Schiff theorem the nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM) is screened in neutral atoms. In ions this screening is incomplete. We extend a derivation of the Schiff theorem to ions and molecules. The finite nuclear size effects are considered including Z^2 alpha^2 corrections to the nuclear Schiff moment which are significant in all atoms and molecules of experimental interest. We show that in majority of ionized atoms the nuclear EDM contribution to the atomic EDM dominates while in molecules the contribution of the Schiff moment dominates. We also consider the screening of electron EDM in ions.

# Extension of the Schiff theorem to ions and molecules

## Key claim

The authors extend the Schiff theorem, which states that the nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM) is completely screened in neutral atoms, to ions and molecules, showing that in ions the screening is incomplete and the nuclear EDM contribution to the atomic EDM typically dominates over the Schiff moment contribution, except in molecules where the Schiff moment contribution is enhanced by up to five orders of magnitude due to rotational state mixing.

## Method

The authors derive the effective Hamiltonian for atomic and molecular ions in an external electric field, starting from the full many-body Hamiltonian with finite nuclear size and P,T-odd nuclear charge distributions. They use the commutator method of Schiff together with the Ehrenfest theorem to calculate the average nuclear acceleration and the resulting screened nuclear EDM interaction, and they separately treat the relativistic corrections to the Schiff moment potential using an expansion of the Dirac electron wavefunctions inside the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear EDM estimate: d ~ 10^{-21} η e·cm, where η is the strength constant of the nuclear P,T-odd interaction in units of the weak Fermi constant G.
- Schiff-moment-induced atomic EDM estimate: D_A ~ (Z/100)^2 · 10^{-24} η e·cm.
- For spherical nuclei in singly ionized atoms, the nuclear EDM screening factor is 1/Z, giving an ionic EDM contribution ~ 1/Z · 10^{-21} η |e| cm, which exceeds the Schiff moment contribution by at least one order of magnitude.
- For the molecule PbF⁺, the Schiff moment contribution D_S ~ 10^{-20} η e·cm, which is three orders of magnitude larger than the screened nuclear EDM contribution D_N ~ 10^{-23} η e·cm.
- For the molecule TlF, the rotational constant B = 1.025 × 10^{-6} a.u., the dipole moment D_M = 1.65 a.u., and the constant X ≈ 8000 a.u., yielding K_m = 5 × 10^{10} a.u.
- The Schiff moment for an odd isotope of Pb is estimated as S = 10^{-8} η e·fm³.
- The relativistic correction to the Schiff moment involves terms of order Z²α², with the ratio b₃/b₁ ≈ -0.5 Z²α²/R_N² for the s-p matrix elements.

## Context

This work extends the original Schiff theorem (Schiff, 1963) to ions and molecules, building on earlier treatments of nuclear EDM screening in ions by Dzuba (1986), Itoi (2010), and Dzuba (2010). The finite nuclear size corrections follow the approach of Flambaum (2002), who showed that the atomic EDM is produced by a nuclear local dipole moment differing from the Schiff moment by corrections ∼ Z²α². The results enable quantitative comparison of nuclear EDM versus Schiff moment contributions in proposed experiments with molecular ions such as those initiated by Leanhardt (2011) and in storage-ring experiments with atomic ions. The molecular enhancement mechanism relies on the mixing of close rotational states, as previously described by Sushkov (1978, 1984) and Sandars (1967).
