# Pseudospin, Spin, and Coulomb Dirac-Symmetries: Doublet Structure and Supersymmetric Patterns

> Relativistic symmetries of the Dirac Hamiltonian with a mixture of spherically symmetric Lorentz scalar and vector potentials, are examined from the point of view of supersymmetric quantum mechanics. The cases considered include the Coulomb, pseudospin and spin limits relevant, respectively, to atoms, nuclei and hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the degeneracy patterns and wave-function relations observed in the Coulomb, pseudospin, and spin limits of the Dirac Hamiltonian are formally equivalent to the patterns found in supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM), with the caveat that the transformation operators connecting partner states are matricial Darboux transformations rather than the first-order scalar operators typical of one-dimensional SUSYQM.

## Method

The author imposes an intertwining relation between two radial Dirac Hamiltonians \(H_{\kappa_1}\) and \(H_{\kappa_2}\) that share the same scalar and vector potentials, using a \(2\times 2\) matricial Darboux transformation operator \(L\). Solving the intertwining relation for the unknown operator and potentials yields three physically relevant solutions corresponding to \(\kappa_1+\kappa_2 = 0\) (Coulomb limit), \(\kappa_1+\kappa_2 = 1\) (pseudospin limit), and \(\kappa_1+\kappa_2 = -1\) (spin limit). For each limit, the explicit form of \(L\) is derived, the doublet wave-function relations are obtained, and the resulting spectral patterns are shown to match the SUSYQM isospectral structure.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb limit: quantization condition \(\gamma + \eta_1 = -n_r\) with \(n_r=0,1,2,\ldots\), where \(\gamma = \sqrt{\kappa^2 + \alpha_S^2 - \alpha_V^2}\) and \(\eta_1 = (\alpha_S M + \alpha_V E)/\lambda\) with \(\lambda = \sqrt{M^2 - E^2}\); eigenvalues are two-fold degenerate for \(n_r \geq 1\) with respect to the sign of \(\kappa\).
- Pseudospin limit: doublet states have quantum numbers \((n,\ell,j=\ell+1/2)\) for \(\kappa_1<0\) and \((n-1,\ell+2,j'=\ell+3/2)\) for \(\kappa_2>0\), with \(\kappa_1+\kappa_2=1\).
- Spin limit: doublet states have quantum numbers \((n,\ell,j=\ell+1/2)\) for \(\kappa_1<0\) and \((n,\ell,j'=\ell-1/2)\) for \(\kappa_2>0\), with \(\kappa_1+\kappa_2=-1\).

## Context

This work extends the known supersymmetric structure of the Dirac-Coulomb problem (Sukumar 1985) to the pseudospin and spin symmetry limits relevant to nuclear and hadronic physics. The pseudospin doublets, originally observed empirically in nuclei (Arima 1969) and later explained via a relativistic SU(2) pseudospin symmetry (Ginocchio 1997), are here shown to form a SUSYQM pattern with an additional non-degenerate intruder state at the bottom of the \(\kappa_1<0\) tower. The spin doublets, relevant to heavy-light quark mesons (Page 2001), exhibit a broken SUSY pattern with no non-degenerate states. The paper enables future work to treat groups of several doublets and intruder levels as larger supersymmetric multiplets in nuclear and hadronic spectra.
