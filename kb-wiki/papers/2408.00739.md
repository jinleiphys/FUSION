# Investigating the role of nuclear parameters on oscillation modes in hot Neutron Stars

> Recent studies have revealed that certain nuclear parameters are more dominant than others in governing global neutron star properties, such as its structure or oscillation mode characteristics. Although neutron stars can in general assumed to be cold, in astrophysical scenarios such as newly born neutron stars or remnants of binary neutron star mergers, finite temperature effects play a non-negligible role. In this work, we perform a consistent and systematic investigation of the role of nuclear parameters and thermal effects on neutron star properties and fluid oscillation modes within a full general relativistic scheme. We impose constraints on the parameter space of the relativistic mean field model using state-of-the-art information from terrestrial experiments and multi-messenger astrophysical data. We find effective nucleon mass to be the most important nuclear parameter controlling astrophysical observables of hot neutron stars, similar to the cold beta equilibrated matter. However, we conclude that the interplay among saturation properties and astrophysical observables depends not only on the thermal configurations considered but also on the constraints imposed. We also investigated the role of nuclear saturation parameters on some universal relations for hot NSs which are important in gravitational wave asteroseismology. Our investigation confirmed that these relations are mostly insensitive to nuclear saturation properties and mainly affected by variation of charge fraction in the star.

## Key claim

The paper finds that effective nucleon mass is the most important nuclear parameter controlling astrophysical observables of hot neutron stars, similar to cold beta equilibrated matter, but the interplay among saturation properties and astrophysical observables depends not only on the thermal configurations considered but also on the constraints imposed.

## Method

The authors extended a non-linear relativistic mean field (NL-RMF) formalism previously developed for cold neutron star matter to incorporate finite temperature effects, imposing constraints from chiral effective field theory at low densities, heavy-ion collision data at intermediate densities, and multi-messenger astrophysical data at high densities. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for hot neutron star configurations and computed fluid oscillation modes within a full general relativistic treatment, studying correlations between nuclear saturation parameters and astrophysical observables for different thermal configurations defined by entropy per baryon and charge fraction.

## Key numbers

- Maximum observed neutron star mass of 2.08 solar masses (from Miller 2021) or 2.078 solar masses (from Riley 2021) used as astrophysical constraint
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star constrained to 13.4 plus or minus 0.2 km (from Tong and Zhao 2020)
- Effective tidal deformability for the GW170817 binary system estimated as 300 with upper uncertainty plus 420 and lower uncertainty minus 230 (from LIGO Scientific Collaboration 2019)
- Temperature range for hot homogeneous matter spans 0 to 100 MeV, charge fraction range 0.01 to 0.60, and density range 0.5 to 6.0 times nuclear saturation densities in binary neutron star mergers and supernovae
- f-mode frequencies lie in the 1 to 2 kHz range for gravitational wave detection prospects

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Ghosh and Chatterjee (2022), Ghosh and Pradhan (2022), and Shirke and Ghosh (2023) from cold neutron star matter to finite temperature scenarios such as proto-neutron stars and binary neutron star merger remnants. It builds upon studies by Raduta and Beznogov (2024) on finite temperature EoS within covariant density functional approaches and by Thapa and Beznogov (2023) on thermal effects in density-dependent RMF models. The results enable systematic investigation of how nuclear saturation parameters affect gravitational wave asteroseismology for hot neutron stars, particularly through universal relations that the authors confirm are mostly insensitive to nuclear saturation properties and mainly affected by variation of charge fraction.
