# Does Sub-Barrier Bremsstrahlung in alpha-decay of 210Po Exist?

> A quantum mechanical model for the description of the alpha-decay of heavy nuclei with accompanying photons emission is presented. The model is based on a quantum mechanical one-particle model of alpha-decay through a decay barrier. The bremsstrahlung spectrum calculation employs multipole expansion of the vector potential of the Coulomb field of the daughter nucleus and takes into account the dependence on the angle between the directions of the alpha-particle propagation and the photon emission. Spectra of 210Po are obtained for the angles 25 and 90, and the best agreement with the experimental data in the 90 case in a comparison with other existing models is achieved. From the angular analysis, the model gives monotonic behavior of the bremsstrahlung spectrum for any value of the angle. We find that sub-barrier photon emission (i. e. bremsstrahlung photon emission during alpha-particle tunneling through decay barrier) exists but gives a small contribution to the total bremsstrahlung spectrum.

## Key claim

Sub-barrier bremsstrahlung (photon emission during alpha-particle tunneling through the decay barrier) exists in the alpha-decay of 210Po, but it gives only a small contribution to the total bremsstrahlung spectrum; the model achieves the best agreement with experimental data at 90 degrees compared to other existing models, but cannot explain the "hole" observed in experimental data at 25 degrees.

## Method

The authors developed a quantum mechanical one-particle model of alpha-decay through a decay barrier, using multipole expansion of the vector potential of the Coulomb field of the daughter nucleus (including E1, M1, E2, and M2 multipoles) without employing the transformation used in the Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) and Tkalya (1999) models. The bremsstrahlung spectrum calculation employed wave packets for the initial and final states and took into account the angular dependence between the alpha-particle propagation direction and photon emission direction. Spectra for 210Po were calculated at angles of 25 degrees and 90 degrees, both with and without including sub-barrier photon emission.

## Key numbers

- The bremsstrahlung spectrum for 210Po was calculated at angles of 25 degrees and 90 degrees, with the model showing monotonic behavior for any angle value in the region 5 degrees to 90 degrees
- The model's result for 90 degrees (line 3) lies nearer to the experimental data of Eremin (2000) than both the Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) model and the Tkalya (1999) quantum mechanical model (line 5)
- The spectrum calculated without sub-barrier emission (line 4) lies "somewhat farther" from the experimental data than the total spectrum including sub-barrier emission (line 3)
- The authors suggest obtaining more accurate experimental data for photon energies w_fi greater than or equal to 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Maydanyuk and Olkhovsky, 2001, 2002) for calculating bremsstrahlung spectra in alpha-decay, now incorporating angular dependence. It directly compares with the quantum mechanical models of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) and Tkalya (1999), which used a transformation that the authors argue reduces accuracy. The model achieves better agreement with the 90-degree experimental data of Eremin (2000) than these prior approaches, but like them, cannot explain the "hole" in the 25-degree experimental data of Kasagi (1997). The work enables future investigation of whether sub-barrier bremsstrahlung exists in other nuclei such as 214Po, 226Ra, and 244Cm, and suggests the need for more detailed experimental data at different angles.
