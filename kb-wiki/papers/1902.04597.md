# Gravitational Waves from Compact Dark Objects in Neutron Stars

> Dark matter could be composed of compact dark objects (CDOs). We find that the oscillation of CDOs inside neutron stars can be a detectable source of gravitational waves (GWs). The GW strain amplitude depends on the mass of the CDO, and its frequency is typically in the range 3-5 kHz as determined by the central density of the star. In the best cases, LIGO may be sensitive to CDO masses greater than or of order $10^{-8}$ solar masses.

# Gravitational Waves from Compact Dark Objects in Neutron Stars

## Key claim
The oscillation of compact dark objects (CDOs) inside neutron stars can produce detectable gravitational waves, but only if CDO masses are greater than or of order 10⁻⁸ solar masses and the neutron star is within approximately 1 kpc of Earth; the detectability is conditional on LIGO reaching its target sensitivity at 3-5 kHz and on the existence of a population of CDOs that are captured by neutron stars.

## Method
The authors calculate the gravitational wave strain and lifetime for a CDO in circular orbit inside a neutron star using the quadrupole formula for gravitational radiation, assuming the CDO mass is much less than the neutron star mass and the orbital velocity is non-relativistic. They model the neutron star density profile as ρ(r) = ρ_c (1 - (r/R_NS)²)^(1/2) and compute the evolution of orbital radius, GW frequency, and strain amplitude as the CDO inspirals from outside the star to inside. Detectability is assessed using the sensitivity depth D = √S_n(f_GW)/h_0, comparing to a threshold of D < 50 Hz^(-1/2) for detection.

## Key numbers
- Gravitational wave frequency from CDO oscillation inside a neutron star: f_GW ≈ 5.3 kHz for central density ρ_c ≈ 10¹⁵ g/cm³
- Characteristic strain amplitude for a CDO at the neutron star surface: h_0^max = 3.3 × 10⁻¹⁷ × (m_D/M_⊙) × (R_NS/12 km)² × (f_GW/3.3 kHz)² × (1 kpc/d)
- Characteristic lifetime at the neutron star surface: T* = 1.7 ms × (M_⊙/m_D) × (12 km/R_NS)² × (3.3 kHz/f_GW)⁴
- LIGO sensitivity depth at 3.3 kHz: D(3.3 kHz) = 9.1 × 10⁻⁷ Hz^(-1/2) × (M_⊙/m_D) × (d/kpc)
- Minimum detectable CDO mass: m_D > 1.8 × 10⁻⁸ M_⊙ at 1 kpc distance, or m_D > 1.8 × 10⁻⁷ M_⊙ at 10 kpc
- Orbital frequency at neutron star center: ν_c = 841 Hz × √(ρ_c/10¹⁴ g/cm³)
- GW orbital lifetime for a CDO inside a neutron star: T ≈ 68 h × (M_⊙ m² / m_D r²) × (3.3 kHz/f_GW)⁴
- Maximum strain for a 10⁻³ M_⊙ CDO at 1 kpc: h_0^max = 3.3 × 10⁻¹⁷ (from Eq. h0max with m_D = 10⁻³ M_⊙)

## Context
This work extends the search for gravitational waves from sub-solar mass compact objects, following LIGO-Virgo searches for binaries with masses 0.2-1 M_⊙ (Abbott 2018) and studies of dark boson star mergers (Bezares 2018). It differs from earlier proposals by Ellis (2017) that required 0.05 M_⊙ or more of dark matter in neutron stars to modify merger signals. The paper enables a new observational channel to detect compact dark objects, including primordial black holes and self-bound dark matter objects, through their gravitational wave emission while orbiting inside neutron stars, and simultaneously provides a method to probe neutron star interior density profiles and the equation of state. The authors note that microlensing constraints from MACHO (Alcock 1995, 2000) and EROS (Tisserand 2006) rule out CDOs making up most of the galactic halo dark matter in the mass range 10⁻⁷-15 M_⊙, but allow a small fraction (~10%) of the mass in such objects.
