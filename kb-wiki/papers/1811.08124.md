# Probing vorticity in heavy ion collision with dilepton production

> We study the effect of vorticity present in heavy ion collisions (HICs) on the temperature evolution of hot quark-gluon plasma in the presence of spin-vorticity coupling. The initial global rotation entails a nontrivial dependence of the longitudinal flow velocity on the transverse coordinates and also develops a transverse velocity component that depends upon the longitudinal coordinate. Both of these velocities lead to a 2+1 dimensional expansion of the fireball. It is observed that with finite vorticity and spin-polarization the fireball cools faster as compared to the case without vorticity. Furthermore, we discuss the consequence of this on the production of thermal dileptons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin-vorticity coupling in the thermodynamic relation (adding a term \(\Omega w\) to \(\epsilon+P=Ts+\mu n\)) causes the quark-gluon plasma fireball to cool faster than in the standard case without vorticity, but this effect only becomes significant when the spin-vorticity coupling term is included in the equation of state; vorticity alone (without spin-polarization) does not significantly alter the temperature evolution.

## Method

The authors model the fireball as an ideal relativistic fluid with a 2+1 dimensional expansion, using a non-relativistic approximation for the flow velocity. They solve the temperature evolution equation derived from energy-momentum conservation, incorporating a modified thermodynamic relation that includes a spin chemical potential \(\Omega\) proportional to the thermal vorticity. The vorticity evolution is taken from an analytic solution of the Euler equation in the non-relativistic limit, and the thermal dilepton production rate is computed using the Born approximation for \(q\bar{q}\rightarrow l\bar{l}\) with the temperature profile obtained from the evolution.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_0 = 300\) MeV and critical temperature \(T_c = 150\) MeV are used for the temperature evolution.
- For zero initial vorticity (\(\omega_0 = 0\) fm\(^{-1}\)), the critical time (time to reach \(T_c\)) is \(\tau_c = 4.05\) fm; for \(\omega_0 = 0.9\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\tau_c = 2.75\) fm (Table 1).
- For initial vorticity \(\omega_0 = 0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\), the maximum suppression of dilepton production is about 15% at invariant mass around 1 GeV; for \(\omega_0 = 0.7\) fm\(^{-1}\), the suppression is about 28% at the same mass.
- The transverse momentum dependence shows maximum suppression around \(p_T = 1-1.5\) GeV, with 15% for \(\omega_0 = 0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) and 28% for \(\omega_0 = 0.7\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The initial thermalization time is taken as \(\tau_0 = 0.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions, such as those by Deng (2016) who derived the vorticity evolution equation used here, and by Florkowski (2017, 2018) who developed the spin-vorticity coupling in the thermodynamic relation. The paper also builds on the experimental observation of \(\Lambda\) hyperon polarization by STAR (2017), which measured vorticity \(\omega \sim (9 \pm 1) \times 10^{21}\) s\(^{-1}\). The results enable future experimental tests of the spin-vorticity coupling term in the equation of state via measurements of thermal dilepton yields, particularly the predicted suppression pattern as a function of invariant mass and transverse momentum.
