# Electron-deuteron scattering in a relativistic theory of hadrons

> We review a three-dimensional formalism that provides a systematic way to include relativistic effects including relativistic kinematics, the effects of negative-energy states, and the boosts of the two-body system in calculations of two-body bound-states. We then explain how to construct a conserved current within this relativistic three-dimensional approach. This general theoretical framework is specifically applied to electron-deuteron scattering both in impulse approximation and when the $ρπγ$ meson-exchange current is included. The experimentally-measured quantities $A$, $B$, and $T_{20}$ are calculated over the kinematic range that is probed in Jefferson Lab experiments. The role of both negative-energy states and meson retardation appears to be small in the region of interest.

# Electron-deuteron scattering in a relativistic theory of hadrons

## Key claim

The role of both negative-energy states and meson retardation appears to be small in the kinematic region probed by Jefferson Lab experiments, but the inclusion of negative-energy states shifts the minimum in the B form factor to somewhat larger Q², contrary to the spectator formalism of van Orden et al. (1995) where such inclusion brought impulse approximation into agreement with data.

## Method

A three-dimensional (3D) reduction of the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation was performed using the equal-time (ET) formalism of Mandelzweig and Wallace (1987), which incorporates relativistic kinematics, negative-energy states, and boost effects while maintaining the correct one-body limit. A conserved electromagnetic current was constructed within this 3D formalism by expanding the current matrix element to the same order in the coupling constant as the kernel used to define the vertex function. The formalism was applied to elastic electron-deuteron scattering using one-boson exchange interactions (π, σ, η, ρ, ω, δ mesons) with parameters from the Bonn-B fit, calculating observables A, B, and T₂₀ in both impulse approximation and with the ρπγ meson-exchange current included.

## Key numbers

- The σ coupling constant g_σ²/4π required to reproduce the correct deuteron binding energy varies from 8.08 (instant interaction, positive-energy states only) to 8.55 (instant interaction, all states included), with retarded interactions giving 8.39 (positive-energy only) and 8.44 (all states)
- The ρπγ coupling was set to g_{ρπγ} = 0.56, with a vector meson dominance form factor F_{ρπγ}(q) = 1/(q² - m_ω²) at the ρπγ vertex
- The tensor polarization T₂₀ shows insensitivity to dynamical details up to Q² = 4 GeV²
- At Q² of order 2 GeV², the ρπγ meson-exchange current makes a significant contribution to all three observables (A, B, and T₂₀)

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional relativistic treatment of the deuteron pioneered by Hummel and Tjon (1989, 1990, 1994) and Devine and Wallace (1994), but improves upon those analyses by using a systematic 3D formalism that incorporates retardation effects in the interaction and constructs a deuteron electromagnetic current that exactly satisfies Ward-Takahashi identities. The results differ from the spectator formalism calculations of van Orden et al. (1995), where negative-energy states brought impulse approximation into agreement with the minimum in B, whereas in this equal-time formalism they do not. The calculations enable future work to include retardation effects in the current operator and to refit the NN interaction parameters using the scattering equations of this formalism.
