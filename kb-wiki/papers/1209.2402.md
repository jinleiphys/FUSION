# Evidence of new nucleon resonances from electromagnetic meson production

> The study of nucleon resonances in electromagnetic meson production with the CLAS detector is discussed. The electromagnetic interaction is complementary to pion scattering in the exploration of the nucleon excitation spectrum. Higher mass states often decouple from the $Nπ$ channel and are not seen in $πN \to πN$. Photoproduction of mesons, such as $KΛ$, $ωp$ and $η^\prime p$ may be more sensitive to many of these states. The CLAS detector, combined with the use of energy-tagged polarized photons and polarized electrons, as well as polarized targets and the measurement of recoil polarization, are the tools needed for a comprehensive nucleon resonance program. Some of the recently published high statistics data sets had significant impact on further clarifying the nucleon excitation spectrum.

# Evidence of new nucleon resonances from electromagnetic meson production

## Key claim
High-precision photoproduction data from CLAS, particularly for $K^+\Lambda$ and $K^+\Sigma^\circ$ final states, provide evidence for several new nucleon resonances in the mass range 1900-2150 MeV that were not previously seen in pion scattering, though the identification depends on coupled-channel analyses (BnGa) that incorporate these data alongside hadronic scattering channels.

## Method
The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab measured differential cross sections and polarization observables for photoproduction reactions including $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$, $\gamma p \to K^+\Sigma^\circ$, $\gamma p \to p\omega$, and $\gamma p \to p\eta^\prime$ using energy-tagged polarized photons and polarized electrons. The Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa) group performed coupled-channel partial wave analyses incorporating these photoproduction data together with hadronic scattering data and other photoproduction channels to extract resonance parameters. Single-channel energy-independent analyses were also applied to the $\gamma p \to p\omega$ data.

## Key numbers
- The Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$ pole mass was found to shift to 1365 MeV downward from its bare core mass of 1736 MeV in coupled-channel analyses by the EBAC group at JLab.
- The CLAS $K^+\Lambda$ and $K^+\Sigma^\circ$ data span the invariant mass range from threshold to 2.9 GeV, covering the full nucleon resonance domain.
- Resonance-like structures appear near 1.7 GeV and 1.9 GeV in the integrated $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ cross section.
- The $\gamma p \to p\omega$ data cover the mass range from threshold to 2400 MeV in a single-channel energy-independent event-based analysis.
- Five new candidate nucleon states were identified in the BnGa analysis, with masses in the range 1900-2150 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the search for nucleon resonances beyond traditional $\pi N \to \pi N$ scattering, which misses higher-mass states that decouple from the $N\pi$ channel. The constituent quark model predictions (Capstick and others) and Lattice QCD calculations (with pion masses of 396 MeV) provide theoretical context, while the EBAC group (Kamano and others) demonstrated that coupled-channel effects cause significant mass shifts. The BnGa analysis (Bonn-Gatchina group, 2012) incorporated the CLAS photoproduction data alongside hadronic data, leading to new entries and upgraded ratings in the 2012 Review of Particle Properties. The results enable future "complete experiments" where all four complex production amplitudes for a single channel can be determined model-independently, and ultimately fully coupled-channel analyses with energy-independent partial wave extraction.
