# Fluctuations near the liquid-gas and chiral phase transitions in hadronic matter

> We investigate the fluctuations of the net-baryon number density in dense hadronic matter. Chiral dynamics is modeled via the parity doublet Lagrangian, and the mean-field approximation is employed to account for chiral criticality. We focus on the qualitative properties and systematics of the second-order susceptibility of the net-baryon number density for individual positive- and negative-parity nucleons whose masses become degenerate at the chiral restoration. It is shown that the second-order susceptibility of the positive-parity state can become negative when the chiral symmetry is restored, as a natural consequence of the unique relationship of the mass to the order parameter. Moreover, we find that such negative fluctuations are indicative of approaching the critical point on the chiral phase boundary. Our results may have consequences for the interpretation of the experimental data on net-proton fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The second-order susceptibility of the net-baryon number density for positive-parity nucleons can become negative near chiral symmetry restoration, and this negative sign is a qualitative signal of approaching the critical point on the chiral phase boundary. This result is obtained under mean-field approximation and depends on the mass of the positive-parity state having a minimum at a finite value of the chiral condensate.

## Method

The authors model chiral dynamics using the parity doublet Lagrangian with mirror assignment for nucleons, coupling hadronic degrees of freedom to scalar (sigma, pion) and vector (omega) meson fields. They employ the mean-field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential and solve the gap equations for the mean fields, then calculate the second-order susceptibility of the net-baryon number density separately for positive- and negative-parity nucleon states. The model parameters are fixed by vacuum nucleon masses, pion decay constant, and nuclear ground-state properties at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature of chiral crossover at vanishing chemical potential: T_c = 209 MeV
- Liquid-gas critical point: T_lg = 16 MeV, mu_B = 909 MeV, n_B = 0.053 fm^{-3} = 0.33 n_0
- Chiral critical point: T_ch = 7 MeV, mu_B = 1526 MeV, n_B = 1.25 fm^{-3} = 7.82 n_0
- Chirally invariant mass parameter: m_0 = 750 MeV
- Vacuum masses: m_+ = 939 MeV (positive-parity nucleon), m_- = 1500 MeV (negative-parity nucleon)
- Nuclear saturation density: n_B = 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy E/A - m_+ = -16 MeV, compressibility K = 240 MeV
- Minimum of positive-parity mass occurs at order parameter delta_sigma = m_- - m_+ = 133 MeV for m_0 = 750 MeV
- Negative chi_2^+ fluctuations appear at T = 45 MeV and below

## Context

This work extends parity doublet model studies of hot and dense hadronic matter (Detar 1988, Jido 1999, Sasaki 2011, Marczenko 2017-2022) by analyzing fluctuations of conserved charges separately for chiral partners. It contrasts with the hadron resonance gas model (Braun-Munzinger 2003, Andronic 2017) which does not capture chiral in-medium effects, and with lattice QCD results (Aarts 2015, 2017, 2018) showing parity doubling structure near chiral crossover. The results suggest that net-proton fluctuations may not be a straightforward proxy for net-baryon fluctuations near the chiral critical point, which has implications for interpreting beam energy scan data from RHIC (STAR 2010) and SPS (Mackowiak-Pawlowska 2020).
