# The optimal detection angles for producing N=126 neutron-rich isotones in the multinucleon transfer reactions

> The challenge of isotopic identification over a wide angular distribution has limited the measurement of neutron-rich nuclei produced via the multinucleon transfer (MNT) process. To investigate the optimal detection angles for the N=126 isotones, we propose a method to construct the reasonable scattering angles of the MNT products in the dinuclear system (DNS-sysu) model. The reactions $^{136,144}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb are investigated. The calculated results are in rather good agreement with the available experimental data in the reaction $^{136}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb. The entrance channel effects on the scattering angle are investigated. It is found that the scattering angular distribution strongly depends on the isospin and the impact parameter of the collision system. The optimal angle ranges for detecting $N=126$ neutron-rich nuclides $^{204}$Pt, $^{203}$Ir, $^{202}$Os, and $^{201}$Re in the $^{136}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb reaction at the incident energy $E_{c.m.} = 526MeV$ are predicted. Our results suggest that the angle range $45^{\circ} \leqslant θ_{\mathrm{lab}} \leqslant 50^{\circ}$ is most favorable for detecting unknown N=126 isotones. Given the current difficulties in separating and identifying experimental MNT fragments, the results of this work could provide significant contributions to future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the optimal laboratory detection angle range for producing unknown N=126 neutron-rich isotones (specifically $^{204}$Pt, $^{203}$Ir, $^{202}$Os, and $^{201}$Re) via multinucleon transfer in the $^{136}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb reaction at $E_{c.m.} = 526$ MeV is $45^{\circ} \leqslant \theta_{\mathrm{lab}} \leqslant 50^{\circ}$, but this prediction is conditional on the validity of the dinuclear system (DNS-sysu) model's newly proposed method for constructing scattering angles, which shows only "rather good agreement" with available experimental data and slightly underestimates the angular distribution width.

## Method

The authors extended the DNS-sysu model by constructing the scattering angle of multinucleon transfer products as the sum of an ingoing Coulomb deflection angle, a dissipation angle from the rotating dinuclear system during the sticking time, and an outgoing Coulomb deflection angle. They solved the master equation for fragment distribution probability including charge, neutron number, and quadrupole deformation degrees of freedom, then computed differential angular distributions by integrating over the entrance angular momentum with a transmission coefficient. The method was validated against experimental data for $^{136}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb at two incident energies ($E_{c.m.} = 526$ and 617 MeV) before predicting optimal angles for unknown isotones.

## Key numbers

- The optimal detection angle range for N=126 isotones $^{204}$Pt, $^{203}$Ir, $^{202}$Os, and $^{201}$Re in the $^{136}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb reaction at $E_{c.m.} = 526$ MeV is $45^{\circ} \leqslant \theta_{\mathrm{lab}} \leqslant 50^{\circ}$.
- For $^{204}$Pt, the most probable scattering angle is $49^{\circ}$ in the laboratory system, with 44.9% of production yields detectable within the angle range $47^{\circ} \leqslant \theta_{\mathrm{lab}} \leqslant 51^{\circ}$.
- The cross section proportions within 5 degrees around the optimal detection angle decrease from 44.9% for $^{204}$Pt to 42.1% for $^{203}$Ir, 39.5% for $^{202}$Os, and 37.2% for $^{201}$Re.
- The ingoing Coulomb deflection angle $\theta_{\mathrm{in}}$ decreases from $28^{\circ}$ to $21^{\circ}$ to $17^{\circ}$ for neutron stripping channels $\Delta N = -1, -3, -5$ respectively in $^{136}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb.
- The dissipation angle $\theta_{\mathrm{dissipation}}$ in $^{144}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb is smaller than in $^{136}$Xe + $^{208}$Pb, e.g., $28^{\circ} < 42^{\circ}$ for $\Delta N = -1$, $36^{\circ} < 52^{\circ}$ for $\Delta N = -3$, and $45^{\circ} < 56^{\circ}$ for $\Delta N = -5$.

## Context

This work extends the DNS-sysu model, previously applied to production cross sections of neutron-rich heavy nuclei (Zhu 2021, Zhu 2022, Liao 2023), by adding the capability to predict scattering angular distributions for multinucleon transfer products. It addresses a gap noted by Heinz (2022) and Valverde (2020) regarding the need for theoretical guidance on emission angles to improve detection efficiency, complementing prior work using the multidimensional Langevin model (Karpov 2017) which noted similar behavior of decreasing cross section proportions for more neutron-rich isotones. The predicted optimal angle range provides direct guidance for future experiments aiming to synthesize unknown neutron-rich nuclei around N=126 using large acceptance magnetic spectrometers.
