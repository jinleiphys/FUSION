# Resolution-dependent quark masses from meson correlators

> We explore the impact of a resolution-dependent constituent quark mass, as recently applied to diffractive meson production, in QCD correlation functions of several spin-0 and spin-1 meson channels. We compare the resulting correlators with experimental and lattice data, analyze the virtues and limitations of the approach, and discuss the channel dependence of the obtained effective quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether a universal, channel-independent resolution-dependent constituent quark mass (RDQM) can describe QCD correlation functions across multiple meson channels, finding that the approach works reasonably for vector and axial-vector channels but fails qualitatively for the pseudoscalar channel due to the Goldstone nature of the pion, and cannot reproduce the scalar-isoscalar versus scalar-isovector difference.

## Method

The authors generalize a previous procedure (Dosch 1997) by equating the second derivative with respect to \(Q^{2}\) of the free quark-loop correlator (with a resolution-dependent mass replacing the constant mass) to the same derivative of a phenomenological or lattice-based parametrization of the interacting correlator. The interacting correlator is represented by a zero-width ground-state pole plus a free-quark continuum above a threshold \(s_{0,i}\), with parameters \(m_i\), \(\lambda_i\), and \(s_{0,i}\) taken from experimental data, lattice simulations (Chu 1993, Hands 1994), and the instanton liquid model (Shuryak 1992). The equation is solved numerically for \(m_{\text{eff}}^{(i)}(Q^2)\) independently in each channel.

## Key numbers

* Vector channel (phenomenological input): \(m_{\text{eff}}(0) = 227 \pm 15\) MeV; critical scale \(Q_c^2 = 1320^{+528}_{-318}\) MeV\(^2\).
* Vector channel (lattice I input): \(m_{\text{eff}}(0) = 193 \pm 44\) MeV; \(Q_c^2 = 464^{+1957}_{-259}\) MeV\(^2\).
* Vector channel (lattice II input): \(m_{\text{eff}}(0) = 191 \pm 159\) MeV; \(Q_c^2 = 531^{+\infty}_{-475}\) MeV\(^2\).
* Axial-vector transverse channel (phenomenological input): \(m_{\text{eff}}(0) = 381 \pm 42\) MeV; no finite \(Q_c\) (mass does not vanish).
* Scalar channel (lattice I input): \(m_{\text{eff}}(0) = 338 \pm 40\) MeV; no finite \(Q_c\).
* Pseudoscalar channel (phenomenological input): \(m_{\text{eff}}(0) = 0.9 \pm 0.1\) MeV; \(Q_c^2 = 2.5 \pm 0.1\) MeV\(^2\).
* Pseudoscalar channel (lattice I input): \(m_{\text{eff}}(0) = 1.6 \pm 0.1\) MeV; \(Q_c^2 = 7.3 \pm 1.0\) MeV\(^2\).
* The linear parametrization from Dosch (1997) for the vector channel gives \(m(Q^2) \simeq (0.22\text{ GeV})(1 - Q^2/Q_c^2)\) with \(Q_c^2 = 1.05\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the RDQM approach of Dosch (1997), which was applied only to the vector channel for diffractive meson production, to scalar, pseudoscalar, and axial-vector channels. It compares results against phenomenological estimates by Shuryak (1993), lattice data from Chu (1993) and Hands (1994), and the instanton liquid model of Shuryak (1992). The analysis reveals that the RDQM method cannot capture the strong channel dependence of meson correlators, particularly the exceptional behavior of the pion channel and the sign difference between scalar-isoscalar and scalar-isovector correlators, indicating that additional dynamical mechanisms beyond a universal running mass are required.
