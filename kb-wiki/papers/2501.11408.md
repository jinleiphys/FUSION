# Description of femtoscopic correlations with realistic pion-kaon interactions: the $κ/K^*_0(700)$ case

> In this work, we show how $π^+ K_S$ femtoscopic correlations, recently reported by ALICE collaboration in $ pp $ collisions, can be well described taking into account relativistic corrections and using realistic $ πK $ interactions. These are obtained from a dispersive analysis of scattering data, which provides an accurate and model-independent description of the $ κ/K^*_0(700) $ resonance pole. The chiral symmetry suppression of the $ πK $ interactions at low energies and the non-ordinary features of the $ κ/K^*_0(700) $ seem to suggest that the $ π^+ K_S $ source radius might be surprisingly smaller than for other hadronic processes when using the standard and simple Lednicky-Lyuboshits factorization approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that π⁺K_S femtoscopic correlations from ALICE can be well described using realistic πK interactions from a dispersive analysis and relativistic corrections, but the resulting source radius is surprisingly smaller than typical values for other hadronic processes when using the standard Lednicky-Lyuboshits factorization approximation.

## Method

The authors implement three improvements to the standard Lednicky-Lyuboshits formalism used by ALICE: relativistic corrections via the Bethe-Salpeter equation, replacement of the elastic Breit-Wigner parametrization with a dispersive Constrained Fit to Data (CFD) description of πK scattering that includes both I=1/2 and I=3/2 isospin components, and inclusion of coupled channels (π⁺K_S couples to π⁺K_L and π⁰K⁺). They fit the three ALICE datasets with only three free parameters (normalization factor κ, source radius R, and correlation strength λ) instead of the six parameters used by ALICE.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic corrections produce a large effect of approximately 20% at threshold due to virtual momentum integration in the relativistic versions of F₁ and F₂
- The maximum momentum considered in the data is k* ≈ 0.76 GeV, significantly larger than the pion mass M_π ≈ 0.14 GeV, motivating relativistic treatment
- The source radius values obtained from the fits are smaller than typical values around 1 fm found in the literature for other hadronic processes
- The normalization factor κ differs from 1 by less than 1% across all three datasets

## Context

This work extends the ALICE collaboration's femtoscopic analysis of π⁺K_S correlations (ALICE, 2023) which used a non-relativistic Lednicky-Lyuboshits model with a Breit-Wigner parametrization of the κ/K*₀(700) resonance. The authors show that the Breit-Wigner parametrization overestimates the interaction near threshold and fails to reproduce πK scattering data from Aston (1987) and Estabrooks (1977). By using the dispersive CFD analysis of Peláez (2020) and including relativistic corrections, the paper enables more reliable extraction of source parameters and raises questions about the validity of on-shell factorization models for relativistic light mesons.
