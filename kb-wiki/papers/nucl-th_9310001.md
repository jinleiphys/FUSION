# Manifestation of Infrared Instabilities in High-Energy Processes in Nonabelian Gauge Theories

> We show that a high frequency standing wave in SU(2) gauge theory is unstable against decay into long wavelength modes. This provides a non-perturbative mechanism for energy transfer from initial high momentum modes to final states with low momentum excitations. The abelian case does not manifest such instability. Our analysis is supported by lattice simulations.

# Manifestation of Infrared Instabilities in High-Energy Processes in Nonabelian Gauge Theories

## Key claim
A high-frequency standing wave in SU(2) gauge theory is unstable against decay into long-wavelength modes for arbitrarily small amplitude of the driving wave, providing a non-perturbative mechanism for energy transfer from high-momentum to low-momentum modes. This instability exists only for nonabelian perturbations transverse in color space, while abelian perturbations (restricted to the same color direction as the background field) remain stable.

## Method
The authors analytically studied small perturbations around an abelian standing wave solution of the SU(2) Yang-Mills equations in temporal gauge, deriving the linearized field equations and computing the selfenergy of the fluctuating field to second order in the background field amplitude after spacetime averaging. They then performed classical lattice simulations on a 16^3 lattice initialized with an abelian standing wave plus either an abelian or nonabelian perturbation, tracking the evolution of the field energy and Fourier spectrum.

## Key numbers
- The exponential growth rate (imaginary part of frequency) for unstable modes with wave vector k_x much larger than the amplitude A is Im[omega(k)] = A/(2*sqrt(2)), independent of k_x.
- The largest Lyapunov exponent lambda_0 scales as lambda_0 * a = k * a * f(alpha) with alpha = gAa/(ka), where for small alpha the function f(alpha) is approximately 0.5*alpha, giving lambda_0 approximately gA/2.
- The lattice simulation used a 16^3 lattice with initial standing wave amplitude corresponding to less than 5% of the maximal magnetic energy density, and the instability became visible around time t=20 (in lattice units).
- The numerical results for the scaling function f(alpha) were obtained by varying amplitude A with fixed wave number ka=0.49 on a 256x4^2 lattice.

## Context
This work extends the study of energy transfer mechanisms in classical field theories relevant to electroweak baryon-number changing processes, which had previously been investigated in the (1+1)-dimensional abelian Higgs model by Rajagopal and Turok (1992) and in phi^4-theory by Goldberg, Nash, and Vaughn (1992), where no efficient energy transfer was found. The authors attribute the absence of such a mechanism in those models to their integrable nature, contrasting with the nonintegrable character of nonabelian gauge theories established by Matinyan, Savvidi, and Ter-Arutyunyan-Savvidi (1981) and Chirikov and Shepelyanskii (1981). The instability is related to the magnetic moment interaction of a spin-1 particle and resembles the instability of a uniform chromomagnetic background field studied by Nielsen and Olesen (1978). This work enables further investigation of non-perturbative mechanisms for multi-particle production in high-energy collisions and suggests that transverse dynamics in nonabelian gauge theories cannot be ignored in the high-energy limit.
