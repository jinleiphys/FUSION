# Quarkonium Polarization in Medium from Open Quantum Systems and Chromomagnetic Correlators

> We study the spin-dependent in-medium dynamics of quarkonia by using the potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) and the open quantum system framework. We consider the pNRQCD Lagrangian valid up to the order $\frac{r}{M^0}=r$ and $\frac{r^0}{M}=\frac{1}{M}$ in the double power counting. By considering the Markovian condition and applying the Wigner transformation upon the diagonal spin components of the quarkonium density matrix with the semiclassical expansion, we systematically derive the Boltzmann transport equation for quarkonia with polarization dependence in the quantum optical limit. Unlike the spin-independent collision terms governed by certain chromoelectric field correlators, new gauge invariant correlators of chromomagnetic fields determine the recombination and dissociation terms with polarization dependence at the order we are working. We also derive a Lindblad equation describing the in-medium transitions between spin-singlet and spin-triplet heavy quark-antiquark pairs in the quantum Brownian motion limit. The Lindblad equation is governed by new transport coefficients defined in terms of the chromomagnetic field correlators. Our formalism is generic and valid for both weakly coupled and strongly coupled quark gluon plasmas. It can be further applied to study spin alignment of vector quarkonia in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives spin-dependent Boltzmann transport equations and Lindblad equations for quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma by incorporating chromomagnetic field correlators at order \(r\) and \(1/M\) in the pNRQCD power counting, extending previous spin-independent treatments. The formalism is valid for both weakly and strongly coupled plasmas, but the actual derivation relies on the Markovian condition and either the quantum optical limit with semiclassical expansion or the quantum Brownian motion limit with a high-temperature expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the pNRQCD Lagrangian including the spin-dependent chromomagnetic interaction term \(c_4 (g\mathbf{B}\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma})/(2M)\) for heavy quarks and antiquarks. They decompose the heavy quark-antiquark composite field into color-singlet and color-octet components, perform a multipole expansion in the relative coordinate \(\mathbf{r}\), and then apply the open quantum system framework. In the quantum optical limit, they apply a Wigner transformation to the diagonal spin components of the quarkonium density matrix and perform a semiclassical expansion to derive a Boltzmann transport equation with polarization-dependent collision kernels. In the quantum Brownian motion limit, they expand the system operators in powers of \(H_S t_1\) and derive a Lindblad equation for transitions between spin-singlet and spin-triplet states, with transport coefficients defined by chromomagnetic field correlators.

## Key numbers

* The pNRQCD Lagrangian is valid up to order \(\frac{r}{M^0}=r\) and \(\frac{r^0}{M}=\frac{1}{M}\) in the double power counting.
* The chromomagnetic interaction term in NRQCD is \(c_4 \frac{g\mathbf{B}\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}}{2M}\) with Wilson coefficient \(c_4=1\) at leading order in \(\alpha_s\).
* The spin alignment observable \(\rho_{00}\) deviates from \(\frac{1}{3}\) when the polarized distribution functions \(f_0\), \(f_+\), and \(f_-\) are not equal.

## Context

This work generalizes the spin-independent Boltzmann transport equation for quarkonia derived in Yao (2018) and Yao (2020) using pNRQCD and open quantum systems, by including spin-dependent chromomagnetic interactions. It extends the formalism to describe polarization dynamics, which is needed to interpret the relatively large \(J/\psi\) spin alignment measured by the ALICE Collaboration (2022) in Pb-Pb collisions, in contrast to earlier measurements in proton-proton collisions. The derived equations enable future phenomenological studies of vector quarkonium spin alignment in heavy ion collisions, complementing previous work on light vector meson spin alignment from glasma fields by Muller (2021), Yang (2021), and Kumar (2023).
