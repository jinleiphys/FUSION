# Inclusive jet production as a probe of polarized PDFs at a future EIC

> We present a detailed phenomenological study of polarized inclusive jet production in electron-proton collisions at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Our analysis is performed at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD using the numerical code DISTRESS and includes all relevant partonic channels and resolved photon contributions. We elucidate the role of different kinematic regions in probing different aspects of proton and photon structure and study the impact of the EIC collision energy on the measurement of polarized parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

Inclusive jet production at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) is highly sensitive to the polarized gluon distribution, particularly in the high transverse momentum and forward rapidity region, but this sensitivity arises specifically from the \(g\gamma\) channel which only contributes when no cut on the photon virtuality \(Q^2\) is imposed and the final-state lepton is not tagged.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculation of polarized inclusive jet production in electron-proton collisions using the numerical code DISTRESS, which implements the N-jettiness subtraction scheme to regularize infrared divergences. They included all relevant partonic channels (quark-lepton, gluon-lepton, quark-photon, gluon-photon, and resolved photon contributions) and studied the unpolarized cross section and double-longitudinal spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}\) as functions of jet transverse momentum and pseudorapidity for three different EIC center-of-mass energies. The analysis used NNPDF 3.1 unpolarized PDFs and NNPDF 1.1 polarized PDFs, with resolved photon contributions modeled using GRV distributions and the minimal and maximal models of polarized photon distributions from Gluck (1992).

## Key numbers

- For \(\sqrt{s} = 141.4\) GeV, the spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}\) reaches nearly 20% at \(p_T^j = 35\) GeV.
- The unpolarized cross section PDF uncertainties are at or below the 1% level over most of the accessible kinematics for \(\sqrt{s} = 141.4\) GeV.
- The resolved photon contribution dominates the unpolarized cross section at low \(p_T^j\) and falls off rapidly as \(p_T^j\) increases, with the \(ql\) channel dominating at high \(p_T^j\) (above approximately 15-20 GeV).
- The result without the polarized gluon contribution differs by more than the estimated errors throughout the region \(15 \, \text{GeV} \leq p_T^j \leq 35 \, \text{GeV}\) for \(\sqrt{s} = 141.4\) GeV.
- Three EIC collision energies were studied: \(\sqrt{s} = 141.4\) GeV (jet \(p_T\) range 5-35 GeV, \(\eta\) range -3 to 3), \(\sqrt{s} = 63.2\) GeV (jet \(p_T\) range 5-30 GeV, \(\eta\) range -2.5 to 2.5), and \(\sqrt{s} = 44.7\) GeV (jet \(p_T\) range 5-20 GeV, \(\eta\) range -2 to 2), all assuming 10 fb\(^{-1}\) of integrated luminosity.

## Context

This work extends the NLO computation of the double longitudinal spin asymmetry for inclusive jet production in polarized DIS performed by Hinderer (2017) and Uebler (2017), which used the narrow cone approximation, by providing a fully differential NLO Monte Carlo computation that allows any kinematic cut. It also builds on previous studies of inclusive jet photoproduction in polarized collisions by de Florian (1999) and Jager (2008). The analysis is complementary to studies by Aschenauer (2015), Chu (2017), and Aschenauer (2017) that demonstrated sensitivity of EIC jet production to other aspects of proton structure. The results enable future global QCD analyses to use EIC jet data to reduce the uncertainty in the polarized gluon distribution, particularly at low Bjorken-x, and to determine the polarized photon distribution functions.
