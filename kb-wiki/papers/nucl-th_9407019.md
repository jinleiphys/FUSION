# Democratic Decay

> Results of some recent investigations of democratic three-body decays of nuclear systems are discussed. In particular, we consider experimental studies of three-body ($α+N+N$)-decay of $A=6$ nuclei and $J$-matrix calculations of monopole excitations in $^{12}$C and $E1$ transitions in $^{11}$Li in the three-body-continuum cluster models $α+ α+ α$ and $^{9}$Li + n + n, respectively.

## Key claim

The democratic decay approximation, which accounts only for three-body decay channels and ignores two-body channels, is a valid and useful framework for analyzing nuclear systems where all two-body decay channels are closed; however, its applicability depends critically on the absence of bound or sharp resonant states in the two-body subsystems, as otherwise the hyperspherical harmonics expansion of the decay amplitude converges slowly or not at all.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental studies of three-body (α+N+N) decay of A=6 nuclei, where energy spectra of emitted particles were analyzed using an expansion of the 3→3 scattering amplitude in hyperspherical harmonics. For ¹²C, J-matrix calculations of monopole excitations were performed in the α+α+α cluster model, using an Ali-Bodmer αα potential with a truncation boundary of Ñ=18 and allowing hyperspherical harmonics up to K≤18 in the interaction region. For ¹¹Li, J-matrix calculations in the ⁹Li+n+n cluster model used shallow Gaussian n-⁹Li and n-n potentials, with the ground state energy determined by locating the S-matrix pole.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶Be(0⁺) → α+p+p decay: relative weights of (6±5)% for K=0 component; (44±12)% for K=2, S=0 component; and (50±17)% for K=2, S=1 component.
- In ¹²C, the calculated binding energy was EB=7.27 MeV (matching the experimental value); the first excited 0⁺ state appeared at 6.11 MeV excitation energy (experimental value 7.66 MeV with width Γ=9 eV).
- In ¹²C, quasistationary levels at 6.11, 10.39, 13.60 and 22.30 MeV exhausted 31%, 29%, 13% and 13% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), respectively; in the 12-26 MeV region, 33% of EWSR was exhausted, with 16% in the 18-22 MeV interval.
- For ¹¹Li ground state with Ñ=24: J-matrix energy E0=-0.240 MeV (experimental -0.247±0.080 MeV); rms radius <r²>¹/²₁₁=3.32 fm (experimental 3.16±0.11 fm).
- The soft dipole mode in ¹¹Li exhausts about 90% of the cluster EWSR, but this corresponds to only about 8.3% of the total EWSR (σ∞^clust/σ∞^tot = 1/12).
- For electromagnetic dissociation of 0.8 GeV/nucleon ¹¹Li beams: minimal impact parameter bmin=9.0 fm for Pb target and 6.8 fm for Cu target.

## Context

This work extends the democratic decay approach pioneered by Jibuti and collaborators in the 1970s, applying it to both experimental data analysis and theoretical calculations. The ¹²C monopole calculations resolve a long-standing discrepancy between microscopic predictions of ~90% EWSR exhaustion and experimental observations of only ~5%, by showing that continuum effects from democratic three-body decay fragment and broaden the strength. The ¹¹Li calculations demonstrate that the J-matrix method can handle the extended neutron halo wave functions that conventional variational calculations cannot, and the results are compared with calculations by other groups (refs. 21 and 22 in the paper) that used different approaches. The work enables more accurate predictions for electromagnetic dissociation cross sections of halo nuclei and provides a framework for studying other Borromean nuclei such as ⁶He, ⁸He, and ¹⁴Be.
