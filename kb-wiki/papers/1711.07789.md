## Key claim

The paper finds that the scaled variances of particle number distributions in central A+A collisions are smaller than in inelastic p+p collisions at the same SPS energy, contradicting the wounded nucleon model; however, this conclusion is qualified by the observation that the numerical values of the scaled variances differ significantly between the EPOS, PHSD, and UrQMD transport models, and no model shows a non-monotonic behavior indicative of a phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of p+p, Ar+Sc, and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.1-17.3 GeV) using three relativistic transport models: EPOS, PHSD, and UrQMD. They calculated the scaled variances omega[X] for negative, positive, and all charged hadrons, and the strongly intensive quantities Delta[K+,pi+] and Sigma[K+,pi+] for kaon and pion yields, applying three different acceptance criteria (full 4pi, central rapidity |y|<1, and the NA61/SHINE experimental acceptance). The model results were compared with data from the NA61/SHINE and NA49 collaborations, and the validity of the model of independent sources and the role of experimental acceptance and centrality selection were examined.

## Key numbers

* Scaled variance omega[N_-] in p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=17.28 GeV ranges from approximately 1.2 to 1.8 depending on the transport model and acceptance (full 4pi acceptance).
* Scaled variance omega[N_-] in central (b=0 fm) Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=16.84 GeV is smaller than in p+p, with values below 1.0 for most models with full 4pi acceptance.
* In 0.2% most central Ar+Sc collisions with NA61/SHINE acceptance, all transport models produce omega[N_-] values smaller than unity, in agreement with data.
* The strongly intensive measures Delta[K+,pi+] and Sigma[K+,pi+] in p+p collisions are tightly located near a value of 1 for all models.
* The collision energies studied are sqrt(s_NN)=6.27, 7.75, 8.77, 12.33, 17.28 GeV for p+p and sqrt(s_NN)=6.12, 7.62, 8.77, 11.94, 16.84 GeV for Ar+Sc and Pb+Pb.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly addressing the surprising NA61/SHINE finding (Seryakov 2017) that fluctuations are larger in p+p than in central A+A collisions, which contradicts the wounded nucleon model of Bialas (1976). The results challenge the commonly used formula for acceptance effects (Begun 2004) and the model of independent sources, showing that transport models reproduce the experimental trend. The study enables future investigations of the transition region between small and large systems and motivates applying transport models to light nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies.
