# Strangelets at finite temperature

> Within the MIT bag model picture of QCD, we have studied the stability of finite size strangelets at finite temperature with baryon number $A \leq 100$. The light quarks $u$ and $d$ are considered massless while mass of the $s$ quark is taken as $150$ MeV. Using discrete eigen energies of the non-interacting quarks in the bag, grand canonical partition function is constructed and then the free energy is minimized with respect to the bag radius. In the $T\rightarrow 0$ limit clear shell structures are found which persist upto about $T=10$ MeV. Infact for $A=6$ the shell structure disappears only at $T > 20$ MeV.

## Key claim
Within the MIT bag model, shell structures in strangelets (with baryon number A ≤ 100) persist up to about T = 10 MeV, and for A = 6 the shell structure disappears only at T > 20 MeV, contrary to the expectation that shell effects would wash out at lower temperatures.

## Method
The authors constructed a grand canonical partition function using discrete single-particle eigenenergies of non-interacting quarks (massless u and d, massive s with m_s = 150 MeV) confined in a spherical MIT bag. For each baryon number A and temperature T, the free energy was minimized with respect to the bag radius R, imposing charge neutrality and flavor equilibrium via weak interactions. Electrons were treated in the continuum limit, while gluons and anti-quarks were neglected as negligible below T = 40 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Baryon number range studied: A ≤ 100
- s-quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV
- Bag constant: B^(1/4) = 145 MeV, giving bulk energy per baryon ε_b = 850 MeV
- Shell closures observed at A = 6, 18, 24, 42, 54, 84, 102, 150 (with 102 and 150 not shown in the figure)
- For A ≥ 20, strangelets remain stable against nucleon decay (E/A < 940 MeV) up to T = 30 MeV
- At T > 35 MeV, E/A exceeds 940 MeV and strangelets become unstable compared to nucleons
- For A = 6, shell structure persists up to T = 20 MeV
- For A ≥ 10, strangelets are stable against neutron decay (binding energy relative to neutron mass m_n ≈ 940 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the zero-temperature shell model study of strangelets by Gilson and Jaffe (1993) to finite temperatures, using a similar MIT bag model approach but incorporating charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium. It also builds on earlier finite-temperature studies of strange quark matter by Reinhardt and Dang (1988), Alcock and Olinto (1989), and Chakrabarty (1991, 1993). The results are relevant for understanding strangelet formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions where quark-gluon plasma cools and hadronizes, and they enable future studies using a color-singlet partition function to incorporate QCD interactions more fully.
