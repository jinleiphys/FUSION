# On spinodal points and Lee-Yang edge singularities

> We address a number of outstanding questions associated with the analytic properties of the universal equation of state of the $ϕ^4$ theory, which describes the critical behavior of the Ising model and ubiquitous critical points of the liquid-gas type. We focus on the relation between spinodal points that limit the domain of metastability for temperatures below the critical temperature, i.e., $T < T_{\rm c}$, and Lee-Yang edge singularities that restrict the domain of analyticity around the point of zero magnetic field $H$ for $T > T_{\rm c}$. The extended analyticity conjecture (due to Fonseca and Zamolodchikov) posits that, for $T < T_{\rm c}$, the Lee-Yang edge singularities are the closest singularities to the real $H$ axis. This has interesting implications, in particular, that the spinodal singularities must lie off the real $H$ axis for $d < 4$, in contrast to the commonly known result of the mean-field approximation. We find that the parametric representation of the Ising equation of state obtained in the $\varepsilon = 4-d$ expansion, as well as the equation of state of the ${\rm O}(N)$-symmetric $ϕ^4$ theory at large $N$, are both nontrivially consistent with the conjecture. We analyze the reason for the difficulty of addressing this issue using the $\varepsilon$ expansion. It is related to the long-standing paradox associated with the fact that the vicinity of the Lee-Yang edge singularity is described by Fisher's $ϕ^3$ theory, which remains nonperturbative even for $d\to 4$, where the equation of state of the $ϕ^4$ theory is expected to approach the mean-field result. We resolve this paradox by deriving the Ginzburg criterion that determines the size of the region around the Lee-Yang edge singularity where mean-field theory no longer applies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for temperatures below the critical temperature (T < T_c) and spatial dimensions d < 4, the spinodal singularities that limit metastability are displaced off the real magnetic field axis into the complex plane, contrary to the mean-field prediction that they lie on the real axis. This is consistent with the extended analyticity conjecture of Fonseca and Zamolodchikov, which posits that the Lee-Yang edge singularities are the closest singularities to the real H axis even for T < T_c.

## Method

The authors analyze the universal equation of state of the phi^4 theory (Ising model) using two complementary approaches: the epsilon = 4-d expansion of the parametric representation of the equation of state, and the large-N limit of the O(N)-symmetric phi^4 theory. They derive a Ginzburg criterion that quantifies the breakdown of mean-field theory near the Lee-Yang edge singularities by comparing the cubic coupling to one-loop contributions, determining the size of the region where the nonperturbative phi^3 theory fixed point governs the scaling.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field Lee-Yang edge singularities are located at H_LY = +/- (2/(3*sqrt(3))) i t^(3/2) for t > 0, with corresponding magnetization M_LY = +/- (1/sqrt(3)) i t^(1/2).
- The mean-field spinodal points are located at real H for t < 0, but for d < 4 the spinodal points acquire a phase shift Delta_phi = pi(beta*delta - 3/2), where beta*delta = 3/2 + (1/12) epsilon^2 + O(epsilon^3) in the epsilon expansion.
- The Ginzburg criterion near the Lee-Yang edge singularity requires |w - w_LY| >> epsilon^2 for 0 < epsilon << 1, where w is the scaling-invariant variable w ~ H t^(-beta*delta) and w_LY = +/- 2i/(3*sqrt(3)).
- For 4 < d < 6, the Ginzburg criterion becomes |w - w_LY| >> (u_t)^(4/(6-d)), where u_t = u t^(-epsilon/2) and u is the quartic coupling.
- The critical exponent sigma characterizing the singular behavior near the Lee-Yang point is approximately 0.26 in d = 4, compared to the mean-field value sigma = 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fonseca and Zamolodchikov (2001) on the extended analyticity conjecture for the Ising equation of state, and addresses a paradox raised by Fisher (1978) regarding the nonperturbative nature of the phi^3 theory description of Lee-Yang edge singularities even as d approaches 4. The results enable better parametrizations of the Ising equation of state that incorporate correct analytic properties, and are relevant for determining the position of the QCD critical point using lattice Taylor expansion methods as discussed by Stephanov (2006).
