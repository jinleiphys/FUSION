# Chiral-partner D mesons in a heat bath within QCD sum rules

> Utilizing QCD sum rules, we extract the temperature dependences of the spectral properties of the pseudo-scalar and scalar D mesons regarded as chiral partners. Besides the masses also decay constants are analyzed as the D meson yields in heavy-ion collisions may be sensitive to their altered decay properties in an ambient strongly interacting medium. Our findings are ($i$) a decreasing scalar D meson mass for growing temperatures while its pseudo-scalar partner meson seems hardly affected, which is in qualitative agreement with hadronic model calculations; ($ii$) inferring an equally weak temperature dependence of the pseudo-scalar D meson decay properties the decreasing residua and decay constants of the scalar particle point towards partial chiral restoration. As a bonus of our analysis in the pseudo-scalar sector we determine the pseudo-scalar decay constant at vanishing temperature. Due to the connection to particular leptonic branching fractions this decay constant is of great interest allowing for the determination of the off-diagonal CKM matrix element $|V_{cd}|$ at zero temperature.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the scalar D meson mass decreases with increasing temperature while the pseudo-scalar D meson mass remains nearly unchanged, supporting a chiral restoration pattern where the scalar mass approaches the pseudo-scalar mass; however, the authors note that the conventional QCD sum rule analysis for the pseudo-scalar channel is hampered by peculiarities that prevent pinning down reliable mass and residue values within that approach.

## Method

The authors employ finite-temperature QCD sum rules using the Borel-transformed operator product expansion (OPE) for pseudo-scalar and scalar D meson currents, evaluated in the light chiral limit at zero net-baryon density. They use a pole-plus-continuum ansatz for the spectral density and extract masses and residues from the ratio sum rule and its derivative, scanning over the Borel mass and continuum threshold parameter within the Borel window. The temperature dependence of condensates is estimated for an ambient non-interacting pion gas.

## Key numbers

* Pseudo-scalar D meson vacuum mass: 1.865 GeV (experimental value cited from Olive 2016)
* Scalar D meson vacuum mass: 2.318 GeV (experimental value cited from Olive 2016)
* Scalar D meson mass drops by about 150 MeV when temperature increases from 0 to 150 MeV (from contour analysis)
* Scalar D meson residue drops by about 0.2 GeV^6 when temperature increases from 0 to 150 MeV
* Pseudo-scalar D meson mass grows by at most 50 MeV when temperature increases from 0 to 150 MeV
* Ioffe-type ratio QSR estimate: pseudo-scalar D meson mass mildly grows from 1.945 GeV in vacuum to 1.997 GeV at T=150 MeV; scalar D meson mass drops from 2.477 GeV to 2.339 GeV at T=150 MeV
* Pseudo-scalar decay constant at zero temperature is determined as a bonus result, connected to the off-diagonal CKM matrix element |V_cd|

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of open-charm mesons at finite density by Hilger (2008, 2011) to finite temperature, and contrasts its findings with hadronic model calculations by Sasaki (2014) which also favor a decreasing scalar D meson mass approaching the pseudo-scalar mass. The results enable future extraction of the off-diagonal CKM matrix element |V_cd| from the pseudo-scalar vacuum decay constant, and contribute to understanding chiral restoration patterns in heavy-light meson systems beyond mass degeneracy, including decay properties.
