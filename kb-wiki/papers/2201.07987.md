# The $^6$Li$(p, γ)^7$Be reaction rate in the light of the new LUNA data

> We present new calculations of the astrophysical $S-$factor and reaction rate for the $^{6}$Li$(p,γ)^{7}$Be reaction at energies of 10 keV to 5 MeV in the framework of a modified potential cluster model with forbidden states, including low lying resonances. The astrophysical $S(E)-$factor is compared with the available experimental data and calculations done within different models. The results for the $S-$factor are in good agreement with the data set (for $E<0.3$ MeV) and calculations (for $E<0.6$ MeV) of LUNA collaboration (Phys. Rev. C 102$,$ 052802, 2020). The recommended extrapolated zero value $S(0)$ turned out to be 101 eV $\cdot $ b. Using the theoretical total cross-sections$,$ the $^{6}$Li$(p,γ)^{7} $Be capture reaction rate is calculated at temperatures ranging from 0.01 to 10 $T_{9}$ and compared with NACRE and NACRE II. Analytical expressions for the $S-$factor and reaction rate are given, and the effect of low-lying resonances on the reaction rate is estimated. We suggest to update the NACRE and NACRE II databases in light of the new LUNA data and present calculations.

## Key claim

The modified potential cluster model (MPCM) calculations of the astrophysical S-factor for the 6Li(p,γ)7Be reaction agree with the LUNA experimental data only for energies below 0.3 MeV and with LUNA calculations only below 0.6 MeV, while the recommended extrapolated zero-energy S-factor is 101 eV·b.

## Method

The authors employed a modified potential cluster model (MPCM) with Gaussian-type intercluster interaction potentials, classifying bound and scattering states according to Young's orbital diagrams to implement the Pauli principle via forbidden states. They constructed bound-state potentials for the ground state (2P3/2) and first excited state (2P1/2) of 7Be using asymptotic constants from the literature, and scattering potentials based on the resonance spectrum of 7Be including resonances at excitation energies of 7.2, 9.29, and 9.9 MeV. Total cross-sections for radiative capture were calculated using standard formulas for electric and magnetic multipole transitions, and the reaction rate was computed by integrating the cross-sections over a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for temperatures from 0.01 to 10 T9.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated zero-energy astrophysical S-factor S(0) = 101 eV·b for the 6Li(p,γ)7Be reaction.
- Ground state binding energy of 7Be in the p6Li channel: 5.60580 MeV.
- First excited state (0.4291 MeV excitation) binding energy: 5.17670 MeV.
- Resonance at excitation energy 7.2(1) MeV with c.m. energy 1.59(10) MeV and width 0.40(5) MeV (E2 transition, 2F5/2 state).
- Resonance at excitation energy 9.29(31) MeV with c.m. energy 3.68(31) MeV and width 1.93(96) MeV (E2 transition, 2F7/2 state).
- Resonance at excitation energy 9.9 MeV with c.m. energy 4.3 MeV and width 1.8 MeV (M1 transition, 2P3/2 state).
- Temperature range for reaction rate calculation: 0.01 to 10 T9 (where T9 is temperature in 109 K).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous MPCM calculations from 2010-2011, which were limited to energies below 1 MeV and did not include resonances or reaction rate calculations. The new calculations incorporate the 2020 LUNA experimental data (Piatti 2020, Phys. Rev. C 102, 052802) and include low-lying resonances up to 5 MeV, enabling comparison with the NACRE and NACRE II reaction rate databases. The results suggest updating these databases in light of the new LUNA data and the present calculations, and provide analytical expressions for the S-factor and reaction rate for use in nuclear astrophysics applications.
