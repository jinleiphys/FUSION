# Big Bang Nucleosynthesis with Stable $^8$Be and the Primordial Lithium Problem

> A change in the fundamental constants of nature or plasma effects in the early universe could stabilize $^8$Be against decay into two $^4$He nuclei. Coc et al. examined this effect on big bang nucleosynthesis as a function of $B_8$, the mass difference between two $^4$He nuclei and a single $^8$Be nucleus, and found no effects for $B_8 \le 100$ keV. Here we examine stable $^8$Be with larger $B_8$ and also allow for a variation in the rate for $^4$He + $^4$He $\longrightarrow$ $^8$Be to determine the threshold for interesting effects. We find no change to standard big bang nucleosynthesis for $B_8 < 1$ MeV. For $B_8 \gtrsim 1$ MeV and a sufficiently large reaction rate, a significant fraction of $^4$He is burned into $^8$Be, which fissions back into $^4$He when $B_8$ assumes its present-day value, leaving the primordial $^4$He abundance unchanged. However, this sequestration of $^4$He results in a decrease in the primordial $^7$Li abundance. Primordial abundances of $^7$Li consistent with observationally-inferred values can be obtained for reaction rates similar to those calculated for the present-day (unbound $^8$Be) case. Even for the largest binding energies and largest reaction rates examined here, only a small fraction of $^8$Be is burned into heavier elements, consistent with earlier studies. There is no change in the predicted deuterium abundance for any model we examined.

## Key claim

For $B_8 \gtrsim 1$ MeV and a sufficiently large $^4$He + $^4$He reaction rate, stable $^8$Be during BBN sequesters $^4$He and reduces the primordial $^7$Li abundance to values consistent with observations, while leaving the final $^4$He and deuterium abundances unchanged.

## Method

The authors used the AlterBBN code, replacing the triple-$\alpha$ reaction with $^4$He + $^4$He $\longrightarrow$ $^8$Be + $\gamma$ and $^8$Be + $^4$He $\longrightarrow$ $^{12}$C + $\gamma$, treating $B_8$ (the mass difference between two $^4$He nuclei and $^8$Be) as a free parameter up to 3 MeV. The $^4$He + $^4$He rate was parametrized by a multiplicative constant $F_0$ in a simplified charged-particle reaction formula, varied from $10^9$ to $10^{12}$, while the $^8$Be + $^4$He rate was taken from Nomoto et al. Additional reactions linking $^7$Li, $^7$Be, and $^8$Be were tested and found negligible.

## Key numbers

- No change to standard BBN for $B_8 < 1$ MeV; significant changes occur only for $B_8 > 1$ MeV.
- For $F_0 = 1.0 \times 10^{11}$ and $B_8$ increasing from 1 to 3 MeV, the $^4$He mass fraction drops sharply while $^8$Be mass fraction rises; the sum of the two remains constant.
- $^7$Li/H reduction begins at $F_0 = 1.0 \times 10^{10}$ and $B_8 \ge 2$ MeV; values consistent with the observationally-inferred $^7$Li/H = $(1.6 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-10}$ are achieved for $F_0$ between $1.0 \times 10^{10}$ and $1.0 \times 10^{11}$.
- The prefactor corresponding to $F_0$ in the Nomoto et al. nonresonant rate lies between $4 \times 10^{11}$ and $2 \times 10^{10}$ for $T_9 \sim 1 - 0.1$.
- A value of $B_8 \sim 1$ MeV requires a Debye mass $m_D \sim 3$ MeV in the plasma model of Yao et al., or a change in the strong coupling constant of $\sim 15\%$.
- No significant primordial production of CNO elements (CNO/H $\sim 10^{-15} - 10^{-14}$) even at the largest $B_8$ and $F_0$ examined.

## Context

This work extends the earlier BBN study by Coc et al. (2012), who examined stable $^8$Be only up to $B_8 \le 100$ keV and found no effects. It also builds on the stellar evolution analysis of Adams and Grohs (2017) and the plasma stabilization proposal of Yao et al. (2017). The key new mechanism is the sequestration of $^4$He into $^8$Be during BBN, which later fissions back, reducing $^7$Li without altering $^4$He or deuterium. The results enable future work to incorporate simultaneous changes to other binding energies (especially deuterium) in specific models of varying fundamental constants, and to test whether plasma effects can produce MeV-scale $B_8$ values.
