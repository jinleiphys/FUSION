# Nuclear Modification Factor Using Tsallis Non-extensive Statistics

> The nuclear modification factor is derived using Tsallis non-extensive statistics in relaxation time approximation. The variation of nuclear modification factor with transverse momentum for different values of non-extensive parameter, $q$, is also observed. The experimental data from RHIC and LHC are analysed in the framework of Tsallis non-extensive statistics in a relaxation time approximation. It is shown that the proposed approach explains the $R_{AA}$ of all particles over a wide range of transverse momenta but doesn't seem to describe the rise in $R_{AA}$ at very high transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The paper derives an expression for the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) using Tsallis non-extensive statistics within the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann transport equation, and shows that this approach explains R_AA for heavy flavor particles across all transverse momenta but fails to describe the rise in R_AA at very high transverse momenta for light flavor hadrons, particularly kaons.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, using a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution as the initial distribution for energetic particles and a Boltzmann equilibrium distribution as the final state. The resulting expression for R_AA depends on the Tsallis non-extensive parameter q, the Tsallis temperature T, the equilibrium temperature T_eq, and the ratio of freeze-out time to relaxation time t_f/tau. The model is fitted to experimental R_AA data for various particle species (pions, kaons, protons, Lambdas, D0 mesons, and J/psi) from RHIC Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and LHC Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV using chi-squared minimization.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts using the Fokker-Planck equation to study R_AA (Alam 2006, Svetitsky 1987, vanHees 2005, Das 2009) by instead employing the Boltzmann transport equation with relaxation time approximation and Tsallis statistics. The approach successfully describes heavy flavor R_AA across all p_T ranges where the Fokker-Planck approach has been applied, but fails for light flavor hadrons at very high p_T where experimental data show a rise in R_AA. The model enables a systematic extraction of the relaxation time ratio t_f/tau for different particle species, showing that heavier particles have larger relaxation times, and provides a framework for analyzing R_AA data from both RHIC and LHC energies within a single non-extensive statistical formalism.
