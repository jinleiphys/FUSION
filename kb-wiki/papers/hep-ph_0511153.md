# X(1835): A Natural Candidate of $η^\prime$'s Second Radial Excitation

> Recently BES collaboration observed one interesting resonance X(1835). We point out that its mass, total width, production rate and decay pattern favor its assignment as the second radial excitation of $η^\prime$ meson very naturally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the X(1835) resonance observed by BES is "very naturally" explained as the second radial excitation of the η′ meson, but this assignment depends on the assumption that the p̅p threshold enhancement and X(1835) are not the same state, a point the authors explicitly note lacks strong experimental evidence.

## Method

The authors compared the mass, total width, production rate, and decay pattern of X(1835) with the known properties of the first and second radial excitations of the pseudoscalar nonet (π, K, η, η′). They constructed an effective SU(3) flavor Lagrangian to analyze the S-wave decay X(1835) → η′ππ and evaluated which coupling terms dominate, concluding that the g₁ term (involving traces of P M and M²) best explains the observed decay pattern while suppressing the ηππ mode.

## Key numbers

- Mass of X(1835): m_X = (1833.7 ± 6.2 ± 2.7) MeV
- Width of X(1835): Γ(X(1835)) = (67.7 ± 20.3 ± 7.7) MeV
- Product branching fraction measured by BES: B(J/ψ → γ η′(1835)) × B(η′(1835) → π⁺π⁻η′) = (2.2 ± 0.4 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴
- Estimated B(J/ψ → γ η′(1835)) from naive suppression factor: ~0.8 × 10⁻³
- Estimated B(J/ψ → γ η′(1835)) from product branching fraction assuming 40% decay to π⁺π⁻η′: ~0.6 × 10⁻³
- Known masses of second radial excitations for comparison: π(1800), K(1830), η(1760)
- Known widths for comparison: Γ(η(1295)) = (55 ± 5) MeV, Γ(η′(1475)) = (50–90) MeV, Γ(η(1760)) = (60 ± 16) MeV
- Mixing angle used: θ ≈ −π/9

## Context

This work extends the classification of radial excitations of light pseudoscalar mesons, noting that the first radial excitations (π(1300), K(1460), η(1295), η′(1475)) form a nearly degenerate nonet and that the second radial excitations (π(1800), K(1830), η(1760)) are similarly close in mass, with X(1835) filling the missing η′ slot. The paper contrasts with earlier proposals that X(1835) is a p̅p baryonium (Zhu and Gao, 2005) or a pseudoscalar glueball (Koch, 2005; other authors), arguing that the glueball assignment faces a serious mass problem since lattice QCD predicts pure pseudoscalar glueballs around 2.6 GeV. The authors suggest that future BES searches for X(1835) in ηππ, η′4π, and η′(1475) in η′ππ will test their assignment.
