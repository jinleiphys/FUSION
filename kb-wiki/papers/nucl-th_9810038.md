# Problems with Extraction of the Nucleon to Delta(1232) Photonic Amplitudes

> We investigate the model dependence and the importance of choice of database in extracting the {\it physical} nucleon-Delta(1232) electromagnetic transition amplitudes, of interest to QCD and baryon structure, from the pion photoproduction observables. The model dependence is found to be much smaller than the range of values obtained when different datasets are fitted. In addition, some inconsistencies in the current database are discovered, and their affect on the extracted transition amplitudes is discussed.

## Key claim

The model dependence in extracting the physical nucleon-to-Delta(1232) electromagnetic transition amplitudes from pion photoproduction data is much smaller than the variation caused by choosing different experimental datasets; an inconsistency exists between the Bonn neutral-pion differential cross sections at forward and backward angles and the Mainz photon asymmetry data.

## Method

The authors constructed test datasets of pion photoproduction observables in the energy region from threshold to the two-pion threshold, then applied two distinct theoretical approaches to extract the resonant M1 and E2 amplitudes: the RPI effective Lagrangian approach (with pseudovector nucleon Born terms, s/u-channel Delta exchange, and t-channel rho/omega exchanges, containing five parameters) and the VPI energy-dependent multipole analysis (with parameterized s, p, and d-wave multipoles and K-matrix unitarity). Fits were performed on different datasets, including one that rejected all pre-1980 cross section data and another that added two sets of Bonn pi0 p differential cross sections, to isolate the effects of database choice.

## Key numbers

- The RPI analysis gave E2/M1 = -2.1% at the K-matrix pole, while the VPI analysis gave E2/M1 = -1.9% when pre-1980 cross sections were removed (Fit 1).
- The extracted M1 amplitude was stable at about 290 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, roughly 30% larger than most quark model predictions.
- The E2/M1 ratio ranged from -(1.5 ± 0.5)% to -(3.2 ± 0.2)% across different analyses in the literature.
- Using the Mainz photon asymmetry data at 340 MeV as a benchmark, the authors found E2/M1 = -(2.8 ± 0.4)%.
- When Bonn data were truncated to the same angular range as Mainz data (75° to 125°), the E2/M1 ratio became -1.4% in the RPI approach.
- The BNL cross sections implied an M1 amplitude of about 310 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, leading to a conservative estimate of M1 = 300 ± 20 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to extract nucleon-to-Delta(1232) electromagnetic transition amplitudes as tests of QCD and baryon structure, following earlier analyses by the RPI group (Davidson, Mukhopadhyay, and collaborators, 1986) and the VPI group (Arndt, Strakovsky, Workman, and collaborators). The paper directly addresses the disagreement between recent Mainz (Beck, 1997) and BNL LEGS measurements and earlier Bonn data (Genzel and collaborators, 1974), showing that the spread in E2/M1 ratios arises primarily from database choice rather than theoretical model dependence. The results enable future work by identifying the need for a benchmark dataset and wider-angle differential cross section measurements from Mainz to resolve the inconsistency.
