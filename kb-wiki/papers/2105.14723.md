# Search for doubly-heavy dibaryons in the quark-delocalization color-screening model

> We perform a systemical investigation of the low-lying doubly-heavy dibaryon systems with strange $S=0$, isospin $I=0$, $1$, $2$ and the angular momentum $J=0$, $1$, $2$, $3$ in the quark delocalization color screening model. We find the effect of channel-coupling cannot be neglected in the study of the multi-quark systems. Due to the heavy flavor symmetry, the results of the doubly-charm and doubly-bottom dibaryon systems are similar with each other. Both of them have three bound states, the quantum numbers of which are $IJ=00$, $IJ=02$ and $IJ=13$, respectively. The energies are $4554$ MeV, $4741$ MeV, and $4969$ MeV respectively for the doubly-charm systems and $11219$ MeV, $11416$ MeV, and $11633$ MeV respectively for the doubly-bottom dibaryon systems. Besides, six resonance states are obtained, which are $IJ=00$ $NΞ_{cc}$ and $NΞ_{bb}$ with resonance mass of $4716$ MeV and $11411$ MeV respectively, $IJ=11$ $NΞ^*_{cc}$ and $NΞ^*_{bb}$ with resonance mass of $4757$ MeV and $11432$ MeV respectively, and $IJ=12$ $Σ_{c}Σ^*_{c}$ and $Σ_{b}Σ^*_{b}$ with resonance mass of $4949$ MeV and $11626$ MeV respectively. All these heavy dibaryons are worth searching for on experiments, although it will be a challenging work.

## Key claim

The quark-delocalization color-screening model (QDCSM) predicts three bound doubly-heavy dibaryon states and three resonance states for both the doubly-charm and doubly-bottom systems, but only when channel-coupling effects are included; single-channel calculations alone do not yield these bound states.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic investigation of low-lying doubly-heavy dibaryon systems with strangeness S=0, isospin I=0,1,2, and angular momentum J=0,1,2,3 using the resonating group method (RGM) within the QDCSM framework. They first calculated effective potentials between baryon pairs for each channel, then solved the RGM equation by expanding the relative motion wave function in Gaussians, performing both single-channel and channel-coupling calculations to obtain system energies. To search for resonance states, they varied the distance between the two baryon clusters from 5.0 fm to 8.0 fm and looked for stable energy values.

## Key numbers

- Doubly-charm bound state with IJ=00: energy 4554 MeV, binding energy 19 MeV below the Lambda_c Lambda_c threshold
- Doubly-charm bound state with IJ=02: energy 4741 MeV, binding energy 21 MeV below the N Xi*_cc threshold
- Doubly-charm bound state with IJ=13: energy 4969 MeV, binding energy 14 MeV below the Sigma*_c Sigma*_c threshold
- Doubly-charm resonance N Xi_cc with IJ=00: resonance mass 4716 MeV, about 17 MeV below the N Xi_cc theoretical threshold
- Doubly-charm resonance N Xi*_cc with IJ=11: resonance mass 4757 MeV
- Doubly-charm resonance Sigma_c Sigma*_c with IJ=12: resonance mass 4949 MeV
- Doubly-bottom bound state with IJ=00: energy 11219 MeV, binding energy 20 MeV below the Lambda_b Lambda_b threshold
- Doubly-bottom bound state with IJ=02: energy 11416 MeV, binding energy 17 MeV below the N Xi*_bb threshold
- Doubly-bottom bound state with IJ=13: energy 11633 MeV, binding energy 3 MeV below the Sigma*_b Sigma*_b threshold
- Doubly-bottom resonance N Xi_bb with IJ=00: resonance mass 11411 MeV
- Doubly-bottom resonance N Xi*_bb with IJ=11: resonance mass 11432 MeV
- Doubly-bottom resonance Sigma_b Sigma*_b with IJ=12: resonance mass 11626 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous QDCSM studies of heavy dibaryons, including N Lambda_c and N Lambda_b systems (Huang et al.), H-like dibaryon states Lambda_c Lambda_c and Lambda_b Lambda_b (Huang et al.), N Omega-like dibaryons N Omega_ccc and N Omega_bbb (Huang et al.), and fully heavy dibaryons (Huang et al.). The results are consistent with hadron-level calculations for the IJ=00 doubly-charm system (Liu et al.) and with the conclusion of Fromel et al. regarding the N Xi_cc resonance. The predicted bound and resonance states provide specific candidates for experimental searches at facilities such as RHIC, LHC, and future experiments, though the authors note that detection will be challenging.
