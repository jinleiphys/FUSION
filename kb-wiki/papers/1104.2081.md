# gamma^* gamma^* -> pi^0 Form Factor from AdS/QCD

> The recently measured $γγ^*\rightarrow π^0$ anomalous form factor is analyzed using the $D4/D8\bar{D8}$ holographic approach to QCD. The half-on-shell transition form factor is vector meson dominated and is shown to exactly tie to the pion form factor. The holographic result compares well with the data for the lowest vector resonance.

## Key claim
The half-on-shell gamma* gamma* -> pi^0 transition form factor, when analyzed using the D4/D8 anti-D8 holographic approach to QCD, is vector meson dominated and exactly ties to the charged pion form factor; however, the agreement with BaBar data up to Q^2 = 40 GeV^2 only holds when the lowest (rho meson) resonance is retained, as the full infinite tower of vector resonances yields a vanishing asymptotic result.

## Method
The authors used the top-down Sakai-Sugimoto D4/D8 anti-D8 holographic construction, which supports vector meson dominance, to compute the anomalous gamma* gamma* -> pi^0 form factor. The form factor is expressed as a sum over vector meson resonances with couplings a_n (vector couplings to external EM current) and c_mn (anomalous pi^0 coupling to vectors), all fixed by holographic wave functions. For the half-on-shell case (one photon real), the transitional form factor K(0,Q^2) is evaluated both with only the n=1 (rho) resonance and with the first eight resonances, and compared to data from Cello, Cleo, and BaBar.

## Key numbers
- The on-shell intercept at the photon point is fixed by the Abelian anomaly: F_{gamma gamma pi^0}(0,0) = N_c / (12 pi^2 f_pi), with f_pi = 0.0924 GeV and N_c = 3.
- For n=1 (rho meson only), the large Q^2 asymptotic is: lim_{Q^2 -> infinity} Q^2 F_{gamma gamma* pi^0}(0,Q^2) |_{n=1} ~= a_1 g_{1 pi pi} m_1^2 / (4 pi^2 f_pi) = 1.31 m_1^2 / (4 pi^2 f_pi), with m_1 = m_rho and a_1 g_{1 pi pi} ~= 1.31.
- The pQCD asymptotic (Brodsky-Lepage) is 30% lower than the holographic asymptotic with n=1.
- The small-Q^2 expansion gives K(0,Q^2) ~= 1 - Q^2/m_1^2 ~= 1 - a_pi Q^2/m_pi^2, where a_pi ~= 0.039, compared to the measured value a_pi = 0.026 +/- 0.024 +/- 0.0048.
- The BaBar data extend up to Q^2 ~= 40 GeV^2; the holographic model with only the rho resonance agrees with data in the range 10 < Q^2 < 35 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the gamma* gamma* -> pi^0 form factor using the top-down holographic QCD approach of Sakai and Sugimoto (2004, 2005), in contrast to bottom-up hard-wall constructions by Grigoryan (2008), Cappiello (2010), and Brodsky (2010). It addresses the discrepancy between BaBar measurements (Aubert, 2009) and pQCD factorization predictions (Lepage, 1979, 1980; Brodsky, 1981), which had prompted modifications to the pion distribution amplitude (by Radici, Polyakov, Dorokhov, 2010). The holographic result ties the transitional form factor to the charged pion form factor, a Ward-like identity that the authors find is well obeyed by existing data up to Q^2 ~= 10 GeV^2.
