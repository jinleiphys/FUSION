# Realistic description of the rotational bands in rare earth nuclei by angular-momentum-projected multi-cranked configuration-mixing method

> Recently we have proposed a reliable method to describe the rotational band in a fully microscopic manner. The method has recourse to the configuration-mixing of several cranked mean-field wave functions after the angular-momentum-projection. By applying the method with the Gogny D1S force as an effective interaction, we investigate the moments of inertia of the ground state rotational bands in a number of selected nuclei in the rare earth region. As another application we try to describe, for the first time, the two-neutron aligned band in $^{164}$Er, which crosses the ground state band and becomes the yrast states at higher spins. Fairly good overall agreements with the experimental data are achieved; for nuclei, where the pairing correlations are properly described, the agreements are excellent. This confirms that the previously proposed method is really useful for study of the nuclear rotational motion.

## Key claim

The angular-momentum-projected multi-cranked configuration-mixing method, using the Gogny D1S force with no adjustable parameters, provides a reliable microscopic description of ground-state rotational bands in rare earth nuclei, with excellent agreement when the self-consistently calculated pairing gaps match experimental even-odd mass differences; for the two-neutron aligned (s-) band in ¹⁶⁴Er, the method successfully reproduces the band crossing for the first time, although the crossing occurs at a slightly higher spin (I ≈ 18) than observed (I = 14–16) and the s-band moment of inertia is overestimated at high spin due to vanishing neutron pairing in the cranked HFB states.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum-projected configuration-mixing (projected generator coordinate method) using the cranking frequency as the generator coordinate, with cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states generated self-consistently using the Gogny D1S effective interaction. For ground-state bands, five cranked HFB states with frequencies ℏω_rot = 0.01, 0.05, 0.10, 0.15, 0.20 MeV (or 0.01, 0.075, 0.150, 0.225, 0.300 MeV for N=98 isotopes) were mixed after angular-momentum projection; for the s-band in ¹⁶⁴Er, four cranked HFB states with frequencies ℏω_rot = 0.25, 0.30, 0.35, 0.40 MeV were used. The harmonic oscillator basis expansion with N_osc^max = 10 (or 12 for ¹⁶⁴Er) was employed, and the Hill-Wheeler equation was solved with norm cut-off values in the range 10⁻¹⁰–10⁻¹².

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶⁰Gd, ¹⁶²Dy, ¹⁶⁴Dy, and ¹⁶⁴Yb, where calculated average pairing gaps agreed with even-odd mass differences to within 14%, the calculated moments of inertia showed excellent agreement with experimental data across the entire spin range shown.
- For ¹⁵⁶Gd, the calculated neutron average pairing gap (0.649 MeV) was only 65% of the experimental even-odd mass difference (1.004 MeV), leading to considerably overestimated moments of inertia at low spins.
- For ¹⁷⁰Yb, the calculated average pairing gaps for both neutrons (0.615 MeV) and protons (0.682 MeV) were about 27% smaller than the even-odd mass differences (0.840 and 0.945 MeV, respectively), resulting in poor agreement.
- The energy gain from configuration-mixing at I = 20 ℏ was about 0.7–1.5 MeV compared to simple projection from one infinitesimally cranked HFB state.
- For the s-band in ¹⁶⁴Er, the calculated excitation energy of the 12⁺ state was 2.69 MeV, about 170 keV higher than the experimental value.
- The aligned angular momentum of the two quasineutrons in ¹⁶⁴Er was estimated to be about 10 ℏ.
- The expectation values of spin for the cranked HFB states used for the s-band were I ≈ 19.3, 23.6, 29.1, 35.1 at ℏω_rot = 0.25, 0.30, 0.35, 0.40 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed angular-momentum-projected multi-cranked configuration-mixing method (Shimizu, Tagami, and Shimizu, 2015) to a systematic study of ground-state rotational bands across 12 rare earth nuclei (Gd, Dy, Er, Yb isotopes with N=92–100) and to the first microscopic description of the two-neutron aligned (s-) band in ¹⁶⁴Er. The method builds on the Peierls-Thouless generator coordinate approach (Peierls and Thouless, 1962) and the infinitesimal cranking technique (Tagami and Shimizu, 2016), and differs from the Projected Shell Model (Hara and Sun, 1995) by mixing a small number of cranked mean-field configurations rather than many quasiparticle configurations. The results enable future studies of band crossing phenomena and high-spin rotational motion in deformed nuclei, though the s-band description shows sensitivity to the choice of cranking frequency set and suffers from overestimated moments of inertia at high spin due to the collapse of neutron pairing in the cranked HFB states.
