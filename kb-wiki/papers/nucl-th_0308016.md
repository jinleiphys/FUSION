# Three-body Coulomb breakup of 11Li in the complex scaling method

> Coulomb breakup strengths of 11Li into a three-body 9Li+n+n system are studied in the complex scaling method. We decompose the transition strengths into the contributions from three-body resonances, two-body ``10Li+n'' and three-body ``9Li+n+n'' continuum states. In the calculated results, we cannot find the dipole resonances with a sharp decay width in 11Li. There is a low energy enhancement in the breakup strength, which is produced by both the two- and three-body continuum states. The enhancement given by the three-body continuum states is found to have a strong connection to the halo structure of 11Li. The calculated breakup strength distribution is compared with the experimental data from MSU, RIKEN and GSI.

## Key claim

The authors find no dipole resonances with a sharp decay width in 11Li; the low-energy enhancement observed in the Coulomb breakup strength arises from continuum states (both two-body 10Li+n and three-body 9Li+n+n) rather than from a distinct resonant state.

## Method

The authors model 11Li as a 9Li+n+n three-body system using an extended cluster model with a hybrid-TV basis and solve for unbound states using the complex scaling method (CSM). They decompose the dipole transition strength into contributions from three-body resonances, two-body continuum states (10Li(1+,2+)+n), and three-body continuum states (9Li+n+n) by expanding the complex-scaled Green's function over the eigenstates of the complex-scaled Hamiltonian. Three types of ground-state wave functions (P-1, P-2, P-3) are prepared, characterized by different (1s1/2)2 probabilities (21.0%, 29.4%, 38.8%), and the calculated breakup strengths are compared with experimental data from MSU, RIKEN, and GSI.

## Key numbers

- The 11Li ground state binding energy is 0.31 MeV (reproduced by adjusting the (0p)2-(1s)2 pairing coupling).
- The (1s1/2)2 probabilities in the three ground-state wave functions are 21.0% (P-1), 29.4% (P-2), and 38.8% (P-3).
- The matter radii Rm for the three wave functions are 3.33 fm (P-1), 3.58 fm (P-2), and 3.85 fm (P-3); experimental values are 3.12±0.16 fm and 3.53±0.06 fm.
- The 10Li 1+ resonance energy is 0.42 MeV (used to constrain the 9Li-n potential).
- The integrated dipole strengths up to 5 MeV are 1.401 e2fm2 (P-1), 1.901 e2fm2 (P-2), and 2.439 e2fm2 (P-3); the non-energy weighted sum rule values (NEW-SRV) are 2.215, 2.856, and 3.593 e2fm2, respectively.
- The ratios of three-body continuum (9Li+n+n) contribution to total integrated strength are 0.392 (P-1), 0.522 (P-2), and 0.658 (P-3).
- The 10Li s-wave scattering lengths for the 2- state are -12.7 fm (P-1), -17.0 fm (P-2), and -21.7 fm (P-3); for the 1- state they are -6.5 fm (P-1), -8.6 fm (P-2), and -10.7 fm (P-3).

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method (CSM) previously applied to 6He (Myo, 2001) to the Borromean halo nucleus 11Li, where the 9Li+n and n+n subsystems have no bound states. The results contradict predictions by Garrido et al. (2002) of at least three sharp dipole resonances in 11Li, instead finding that the low-energy enhancement is a continuum threshold effect. The decomposition into two-body and three-body continuum components enables comparison with experimental data from MSU (Ieki, 1993), RIKEN (Shimoura, 1995), and GSI (Zinser, 1997), and shows that the breakup mechanism of 11Li differs from that of 6He due to the larger (1s1/2)2 probability in the 11Li ground state.
