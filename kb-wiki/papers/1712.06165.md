# Relativistic corrections to exclusive $χ_{cJ} + γ$ production from $e^+ e^-$ annihilation

> We calculate in the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization framework all leading relativistic corrections to the exclusive production of $χ_{cJ}+γ$ in $e^+ e^-$ annihilation. In particular, we compute for the first time contributions induced by octet operators with a chromoelectric field. The matching coefficients multiplying production long distance matrix elements (LDMEs) are determined through perturbative matching between QCD and NRQCD at the amplitude level. Technical challenges encountered in the non-relativistic expansion of the QCD amplitudes are discussed in detail. The main source of uncertainty comes from the not so well known LDMEs. Accounting for it, we provide the following estimates for the production cross sections at $\sqrt{s} = 10.6\textrm{ GeV}$: $σ(e^+ e^- \to χ_{ c0} + γ) = (1.3 \pm 0.4) \textrm{ fb}$, $σ(e^+ e^- \to χ_{ c1} + γ) = (15.4 \pm 6.7) \textrm{ fb}$, and $σ(e^+ e^- \to χ_{ c2} + γ) = (4.7 \pm 2.6) \textrm{ fb}$.

## Key claim

The paper computes for the first time the contributions from octet operators containing a chromoelectric field to the exclusive production of $\chi_{cJ}+\gamma$ in $e^+e^-$ annihilation at $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^0 v^2)$ in the NRQCD factorization framework, providing the last missing piece for complete $\mathcal{O}(v^2)$ relativistic corrections; the main source of uncertainty remains the poorly known long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs).

## Method

The authors perform perturbative matching between QCD and NRQCD at the amplitude level to determine the short-distance coefficients multiplying production LDMEs, including those from color octet operators with chromoelectric fields that project onto the subleading $Q\overline{Q}g$ Fock state. The matching coefficients $T_8(^3P_J)$ for the three $\chi_{cJ}$ states ($J=0,1,2$) are computed at tree level, and the NRQCD-factorized cross sections are constructed including the leading $\mathcal{O}(v^0)$ term, the $\mathcal{O}(v^2)$ relativistic correction from the dominant Fock state, and the new $\mathcal{O}(v^2)$ octet contributions. The nonrelativistic expansion of QCD amplitudes is carried out using the threshold expansion method of Braaten and Chen.

## Key numbers

- $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \chi_{c0} + \gamma) = (1.3 \pm 0.4)\ \text{fb}$ at $\sqrt{s} = 10.6\ \text{GeV}$
- $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \chi_{c1} + \gamma) = (15.4 \pm 6.7)\ \text{fb}$ at $\sqrt{s} = 10.6\ \text{GeV}$
- $\sigma(e^+e^- \to \chi_{c2} + \gamma) = (4.7 \pm 2.6)\ \text{fb}$ at $\sqrt{s} = 10.6\ \text{GeV}$

## Context

This work extends previous NRQCD calculations of $e^+e^- \to \chi_{cJ} + \gamma$ that considered only operators contributing through the dominant $Q\overline{Q}$ Fock state, such as those by Chung et al. (2008) at $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^0 v^0)$, Sang et al. (2009) and Li et al. (2009) at $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s v^0)$, and Li et al. (2013) and Chao et al. (2013) at partial $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^0 v^2)$. The inclusion of octet operators with chromoelectric fields, previously incorporated in studies of the decay $\chi_{cJ} \to \gamma\gamma$ by Ma (2002) and Brambilla et al. (2006), completes the $\mathcal{O}(v^2)$ relativistic corrections for this production process. The results enable more precise predictions for comparison with future measurements at Belle II and BES-III, and are relevant for understanding the $X(3872)$ state measured in the same $e^+e^- \to X(3872) + \gamma$ channel.
