# Anisotropic jet broadening and jet shape

> In this paper, we explore the use of jet substructure as a way of probing phenomena which break the isotropic behavior of jets, such as jet propagation through an anisotropically flowing quark-gluon plasma or spin correlations. We introduce two novel observables for this purpose: the azimuthal-dependent jet broadening and the azimuthal-dependent jet shape, which generalize the traditional isotropic substructure studies. Using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory, we explicitly calculate the jet functions associated with these observables with a standard jet axis and with a Winner-Take-All jet axis in both the resummed and fixed order limits. While our analysis first and foremost establishes the formalism for the azimuthal-dependent jet substructure, it also brings to light new results for jet substructure in the azimuthally integrated case, such as the semi-inclusive jet function and the exclusive jet shape for the Winner-Take-All axis, and the jet broadening in the fixed order region. As an illustrative example for the new formalism we demonstrate that the azimuthal-dependent jet broadening can be used as a direct probe of the transversity parton distribution function in deep inelastic scattering.

## Key claim
The paper introduces azimuthal-dependent generalizations of jet broadening and jet shape observables to probe anisotropic phenomena such as jet propagation through a flowing quark-gluon plasma or spin correlations, but the analysis primarily establishes the formalism rather than providing phenomenological predictions; the authors explicitly state that the work "first and foremost establishes the formalism for the azimuthal-dependent jet substructure."

## Method
Using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), the authors calculate jet functions for azimuthal-dependent jet broadening and jet shape with both a Standard Jet Axis (SJA) and a Winner-Take-All (WTA) jet axis, in both resummed and fixed order limits. The jet broadening observable is defined by slicing the jet into azimuthal wedges, while the jet shape uses an iterative wedge definition. The factorization involves hyper-collinear and soft-collinear functions, with the WTA axis simplifying the soft-collinear function to a delta function in transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- Tree-level exclusive jet broadening function: \(\hat{G}^{{\rm axis}\, (0)}_{i\, {\rm alg}}\left(\varphi, \tau, R, \omega_J, \mu, \zeta\right) = \frac{\varphi}{2\pi}\delta\left(\tau\right)\), where \(\varphi\) is the azimuthal wedge angle and \(\tau\) is the jet broadening variable.
- Tree-level semi-inclusive jet broadening function: \(\hat{G}^{{\rm axis}\, (0)}_{i\, {\rm alg}}\left(z, \varphi, \tau, R, \omega_J, \mu, \zeta\right) = \frac{\varphi}{2\pi}\delta\left(\tau\right)\delta\left(1-z\right)\), where \(z\) is the energy fraction of the jet relative to the parent parton.
- The power counting parameter for the resummed region is \(\lambda \sim \tau\), with hyper-collinear modes scaling as \(p_{c}^\mu \sim \omega_J\left(\lambda^2, 1, \lambda\right)\) and soft-collinear modes scaling as \(p_{s}^\mu \sim \omega_J\frac{\lambda}{R}\left(R^2, 1, R\right)\), where \(\omega_J\) is the large light-cone momentum of the jet and \(R\) is the jet radius.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of traditional isotropic jet substructure observables such as jet angularities (Berger 2003, Almeida 2008) and jet shapes (Ellis 1992) to include azimuthal dependence, enabling sensitivity to phenomena like the Collins effect (Collins 1992) and anisotropic quark-gluon plasma flow (Sadofyev 2021). The authors also derive new results for the azimuthally integrated case, including the semi-inclusive jet function for the Winner-Take-All axis and the exclusive jet shape with the WTA axis. The formalism enables future studies of transversity parton distribution functions in deep inelastic scattering and jet-medium interactions in heavy ion collisions.
