# Kinematic Azimuthal Asymmetries and Lam-Tung Relation

> The origin of Lam-Tung relation for the angular asymmetry of Drell-Yan dileptons is analyzed. The asymmetry constrained by this relation is shown to belongto the class of kinematic azimuthal asymmetries, emerging due to the deviation of the reference axis from the natural physical one. The validity and violation of Lam-Tung relation due to radiative and power QCD corrections is discussed. The relation is generalized to the case when the virtual photon is not the transverse oneand the possible applications are suggested.

# Kinematic Azimuthal Asymmetries and Lam-Tung Relation

## Key claim
The Lam-Tung relation, which survives leading-order QCD corrections, describes an azimuthal asymmetry that is purely kinematical in origin: the asymmetry is absent in the natural reference frame defined by the collision kinematics and emerges only as a kinematical effect when transforming to the actual experimental reference frame. The relation is generalized to the case where the virtual photon is not transverse, reducing to the standard Lam-Tung relation only when the transverse polarization parameter λ₀ equals 1.

## Method
The author starts from an angular distribution expressed with respect to a varying axis m, where the distribution depends only on cos²θₙₘ, and derives expressions for the angular coefficients λ and ν as functions of the angle θ₀ between the physical axis and the coordinate axis. By eliminating θ₀, a new relation between λ and ν is obtained that reduces to the standard Lam-Tung relation (λ + 2ν = 1) when the virtual photon is transverse (λ₀ = 1). The author then applies a theorem for averages to justify summing over directions of the physical axis in inclusive Drell-Yan processes.

## Key numbers
- The standard Lam-Tung relation is given by λ + 2ν = 1 (Equation labeled LT).
- The new relation derived is λ₀ = (λ + 3ν/2) / (1 − ν/2), which reduces to the Lam-Tung relation when λ₀ = 1.
- The angular coefficients λ and ν are expressed in terms of λ₀ and θ₀ as: λ = λ₀ (2 − 3 sin²θ₀) / (2 + λ₀ sin²θ₀) and ν = λ₀ (2 sin²θ₀) / (2 + λ₀ sin²θ₀).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the Lam-Tung relation originally established by Lam and Tung (1978) for Drell-Yan dilepton angular distributions. The author connects the relation to the broader class of kinematic azimuthal asymmetries, contrasting with dynamical mechanisms that violate the relation at next-to-leading order QCD and in higher-twist contributions as discussed by Boer, Brodsky, and Mulders (2001) and by Berger, Brodsky, and collaborators. The results enable applications to dileptons produced in quark-gluon plasma and hadronic media, where the polar angular asymmetry may differ and serve as a sensitive probe of the production mechanism.
