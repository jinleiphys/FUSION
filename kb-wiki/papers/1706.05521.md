# Photo- and electroproduction of $Λ(1405)$ via $γ^{(*)}p\to K^+π^+Σ^-$

> In this work, we perform a \textit{toy-model} analysis for the unpolarized photo- and electroproduction of $Λ(1405)\equivΛ^*$ via $γ^{(*)}p\to K^+π^+Σ^-$ by employing the effective Lagrangian approach at the tree level only. We consider that $Λ^*$ consists of the high-mass ($H$) and low-mass ($L$) poles as suggested by the chiral unitary model (ChUM). We determine all the model parameters including coupling constants and cutoff parameters for the phenomenological strong form factors by using the theoretical information from ChUM and available experimental data. The electromagnetic (EM) form factors for the two poles are parameterized appropriately being similar to that for the neutron by employing the ChUM estimates for the EM root-mean-squared (rms) radii for the two poles. We observe from the numerical calculations that, for the photoproduction of $Λ^*$, the interference between the two poles turns out to be destructive, resulting in the single-peak line at $M_{π^+Σ^-}\approx1405$ MeV in the invariant-mass distribution. On the contrary, there appear two peaks in the distribution for the electroproduction as observed in the CLAS/Jlab electroproduction data, due to the constructive interference, which is mainly caused by the Dirac form factors for the two poles. From this observation, we conclude that, in order to explain the photo- and electroproduction data simultaneously, i.e., \textit{single} and \textit{double} peaks, respectively, in the invariant-mass distribution, the interference patterns for the two poles should follow those suggested by ChUM. In turn, it is a strong theoretical and experimental supports for the two-pole structure scenario for $Λ^*$.

# Photo- and electroproduction of Λ(1405) via γ^{(*)}p → K⁺π⁺Σ⁻

## Key claim

The single-peak line shape observed in photoproduction and the double-peak line shape observed in electroproduction of Λ(1405) can be simultaneously explained only if the interference patterns between the high-mass and low-mass poles follow those suggested by the chiral unitary model (ChUM), providing support for the two-pole structure scenario. This conclusion is drawn from a toy-model analysis at tree level only, with several simplifying assumptions including the exclusion of Σ*(1385), K* exchange, nucleon resonances, and far-off-shell diagrams.

## Method

The author employed an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level to compute the invariant-mass distribution for the Dalitz process γ^{(*)}p → K⁺π⁺Σ⁻, considering only three Feynman diagrams where the incident photon couples to the proton, the kaon, and the Λ* baryon. The Λ* was treated as a composite of two poles (high-mass H and low-mass L) with coupling constants and pole positions taken from ChUM and adjusted to reproduce CLAS/Jlab photoproduction and electroproduction data. Electromagnetic form factors for the two poles were parameterized similarly to those for the neutron, using ChUM estimates for the electromagnetic root-mean-squared radii, with a free parameter α_{H,L}=4 introduced for the Dirac form factors.

## Key numbers

- Low-mass pole L: mass 1368 MeV, width 100 MeV, with coupling constants g_{KNL}=1.2+1.7i and g_{πΣL}=-2.5-1.5i, anomalous magnetic moment κ_L=0.30
- High-mass pole H: mass 1423 MeV, width 50 MeV, with coupling constants g_{KNH}=-2.5+0.94i and g_{πΣH}=0.42-1.4i, anomalous magnetic moment κ_H=0.41
- The strong cutoff parameter Λ for the phenomenological form factors was determined to be 900 MeV, similar to that from previous two-body reaction work
- Calculations were performed at center-of-mass energy W=2.4 GeV to avoid nucleon-resonance contributions
- For electroproduction, the photon virtuality Q²=2 GeV² was used with transverse polarization parameter ε=0.5
- The combined strong coupling constants for photoproduction: g_{KNL}g_{πΣL}=-0.450-6.050i and g_{KNH}g_{πΣH}=0.266+3.895i
- The EM-modified couplings in the u-channel at Q²=2 GeV²: g_{KNL}g_{πΣL}F¹ˡ=0.660-0.549i and g_{KNH}g_{πΣH}F¹ʰ=-0.583-2.363i
- Electric rms radii from ChUM: ⟨r²⟩ᴱ_ᴴ=-3.365+7.783i, ⟨r²⟩ᴱ_ᴸ=0.462-0.051i, ⟨r²⟩ᴱ_ₙ=-2.877±0.077 (in 1/GeV²)

## Context

This work extends the line-shape analysis of Λ(1405) production, following the chiral unitary model calculations by Jido et al. (2003) and Nam et al. (2003) that predicted a two-pole structure. It directly addresses the discrepancy between photoproduction experiments (CLAS/Jlab, Moriya et al. 2013) showing a single peak and electroproduction experiments (CLAS/Jlab, Lu et al. 2013) showing a double peak. The analysis enables future studies of the internal structure of Λ(1405) through electromagnetic probes, and the author notes that further theoretical and experimental studies for the Dirac form factors of the two poles will help determine the value of the free parameter α_{H,L}.
