# Point Form Quantum Field Theory on Velocity Grids I: Bosonic Contractions

> In constrast to discretized space-time approximations to continuum quantum field theories, discretized velocity space approximations to continuum quantum field theories are investigated. A four-momentum operator is given in terms of bare fermion-antifermion-boson creation and annihilation operators with discrete indices. In continuum quantum field theories the fermion-antifermion creation and annihilation operators appear as bilinears in the four-momentum operator and generate a unitary algebra. When the number of modes range over only a finite number of values, the algebra is that associated with the Lie algebra of U(2N). By keeping N finite (but arbitrary) problems due to an infinite Lorentz volume and to the creation of infinite numbers of bare fermion-antifermion pairs are avoided. But even with a finite number of modes, it is still possible to create an infinite number of bare bosons. We show how the full boson algebra arises as the contraction limit of another unitary algebra that restricts the number of bare bosons in any mode to be finite. Generic properties of finite mode Hamiltonians are investigated, as are several simple models to see the rate of convergence of the boson contraction; the possibility of fine tuning the bare strong coupling constant is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Discretizing velocity space (rather than space-time) and keeping the number of fermion-antifermion modes finite (N) avoids problems from infinite Lorentz volume and infinite bare fermion-antifermion pairs, but even with finite N it remains possible to create an infinite number of bare bosons; the paper shows that the full boson algebra emerges as the contraction limit of another unitary algebra that restricts the number of bare bosons in any mode to be finite.

## Method

The four-momentum operator is written in point form quantum field theory as a sum of free and interacting terms, with interactions introduced via vertices integrated over the forward hyperboloid. Fermion-antifermion creation and annihilation operators appear only as bilinears, generating the Lie algebra of U(2N) when the number of modes is finite. The bosonic algebra (creation, annihilation, and number operators) is obtained as a contraction limit of a U(N+1) Lie algebra, where a contraction parameter ρ is taken to zero while the representation label M goes to infinity such that Mρ² = 1, ensuring that before the limit the number of bare bosons in any mode is bounded by M.

## Key numbers

- The fermion-antifermion symmetry for finite modes is generated by the Lie algebra of U(2N), with N the number of fermion modes.
- The bosonic contraction parameter ρ satisfies Mρ² = 1 in the limit M → ∞, ρ → 0, where M controls the maximum number of bare bosons per mode.
- For the quantum anharmonic oscillator (one-mode field theory), the true eigenvalue is approached for M about 100.
- In the one-mode fermion-boson model with trilinear coupling (no boson self-coupling), the lowest eigenvalue decreases linearly with the bare coupling constant α for sufficiently large α.
- In the isospin model with M=1, the Hamiltonian matrix is 4×4 and the lowest eigenvalue is λ_min(α) = −√(4 + 6α²), which for α ≫ 1 goes as a negative constant times α.
- In the isospin model with a quartic boson self-coupling term added, the Hamiltonian matrix becomes 4×4 with a 3α² term on the diagonal; this self-coupling is not sufficient to produce a zero eigenvalue for the lowest eigenvalue.

## Context

This work extends the point form approach to quantum field theory (Klink, prior work) by introducing discretized velocity grids as an alternative to space-time lattice methods (lattice QCD) and instant/front form discretized quantum field theory. It builds on the algebraic structure of U(2N) for fermion bilinears and uses contraction limits of unitary algebras (Inonu and Wigner, contraction of Lie algebras) to handle bosonic degrees of freedom. The paper enables future study of inductive limits as N → ∞ and the construction of finite-dimensional matrix approximations to Hamiltonian QCD eigenvalue problems, including the possibility of fine-tuning the bare strong coupling constant.
