# Muon capture on nuclei with N > Z, random phase approximation, and in-medium renormalization of the axial-vector coupling constant

> We use the random phase approximation to describe the muon capture rate on ${}^{44}$Ca,${}^{48}$Ca, ${}^{56}$Fe, ${}^{90}$Zr, and ${}^{208}$Pb. With ${}^{40}$Ca as a test case, we show that the Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) and the standard RPA give essentially equivalent descriptions of the muon capture process. Using the standard RPA with the free nucleon weak form factors we reproduce the experimental total capture rates on these nuclei quite well. Confirming our previous CRPA result for the $N = Z$ nuclei, we find that the calculated rates would be significantly lower than the data if the in-medium quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant were employed.

## Key claim

Using the standard random phase approximation (RPA) with free nucleon weak form factors, the authors reproduce experimental total muon capture rates on nuclei with N > Z quite well, and find that applying the in-medium quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant would make the calculated rates significantly lower than the data.

## Method

The authors used the standard random phase approximation (RPA) with a phenomenological Landau-Migdal residual interaction to calculate total muon capture rates on ⁴⁴Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁶Fe, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb. They first demonstrated that the Continuum RPA (CRPA) and standard RPA give equivalent descriptions of muon capture using ⁴⁰Ca as a test case, then applied the standard RPA to the heavier nuclei. All single-particle states below the Fermi level and two oscillator shells above it were included, and the free nucleon weak form factors with the unquenched axial-vector coupling constant g_A(0) = 1.26 were used.

## Key numbers

- ⁴⁰Ca: calculated SRPA rate 2547 × 10³/s with g_A = 1.26 versus experimental 2544 ± 7 × 10³/s; with quenched g_A = 1.0 the rate drops to 1846 × 10³/s
- ⁴⁴Ca: calculated SRPA rate 1722 × 10³/s with g_A = 1.26 versus experimental 1793 ± 40 × 10³/s; with g_A = 1.0 the rate is 1238 × 10³/s
- ⁴⁸Ca: calculated SRPA rate 1301 × 10³/s with g_A = 1.26 versus experimental 1164 × 10³/s (extrapolated); with g_A = 1.0 the rate is 930 × 10³/s
- ⁵⁶Fe: calculated SRPA rate 4460 × 10³/s with g_A = 1.26 versus experimental 4400 ± 100 × 10³/s; with g_A = 1.0 the rate is 3430 × 10³/s
- ⁹⁰Zr: calculated SRPA rate 10288 × 10³/s with g_A = 1.26 versus experimental 9350 ± 100 × 10³/s; with g_A = 1.0 the rate is 7400 × 10³/s
- ²⁰⁸Pb: calculated SRPA rate 16057 × 10³/s with g_A = 1.26 versus experimental 13450 ± 180 × 10³/s; with g_A = 1.0 the rate is 11436 × 10³/s
- For ⁵⁶Fe, the 1⁺ part of the capture rate was quenched by an empirical factor of 2.54 based on comparison of experimental and calculated Gamow-Teller strength

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CRPA study of muon capture on N = Z nuclei (Kolbe, Langanke, and Vogel, 1994) to heavier nuclei with neutron excess, using the computationally simpler standard RPA. The results contradict the expectation from analyses of beta decay in the (sd) shell (Wildenthal, 1984) and (pf) shell (Caurier, Poves, and Zuker, 1995), as well as the "missing Gamow-Teller strength" problem from charge-exchange reactions, which all indicate quenching of g_A to approximately 1.0. The findings suggest that in-medium quenching of the axial current matrix elements is restricted to the 0ħω Gamow-Teller operator and does not apply to the parity-changing operators that dominate muon capture in these nuclei. The analysis also supports the independent work of Warburton and Towner (1994) on first forbidden beta decays in the lead region, which found a quenching factor of 0.98 ± 0.05, compatible with no quenching.
