# Studying the process $γd \to π^0ηd$

> In these proceedings we present our recent results on the study of the process $γd \to π^0 ηd$, where the existence of a dibaryon in the $ηd$ invariant mass distribution has been recently claimed. As we will show, many of the relevant aspects observed in the experiment, as the shift of the $ηd$ and $πd$ invariant mass distributions with respect to phase space can be described with our model, where no dibaryon is formed. Instead, we consider the interaction of the $γ$ with the nucleons forming the deuteron to proceed through $γN \to Δ(1700)\to ηΔ(1232) \to ηπ^0 N$, followed by the rescattering of the $π$ and $η$ with the other nucleon of the deuteron. Theoretical uncertainties related to different parameterizations of the deuteron wave function are investigated

## Key claim
The observed shifts in the $\eta d$ and $\pi d$ invariant mass distributions for the $\gamma d \to \pi^0 \eta d$ reaction can be explained by a model involving $\Delta(1700)$ and $\Delta(1232)$ production followed by meson rescattering, without requiring the existence of a dibaryon resonance. The model reproduces the shape of the distributions but underestimates the magnitude at forward angles and overestimates it at backward angles.

## Method
The authors model the reaction as proceeding through $\gamma N \to \Delta(1700) \to \eta \Delta(1232) \to \eta \pi^0 N$, followed by rescattering of the $\pi$ and $\eta$ with the other nucleon in the deuteron. They compute the amplitude in the impulse approximation using known couplings for the vertices ($g_{\gamma p\Delta^*} = 0.188$, $g_{\eta \Delta\Delta^*} = 1.7 - i 1.4$, $f^* = 2.13$) and then add contributions from $\pi$ rescattering in s-wave and p-wave (via $\Delta(1232)$ exchange) and $\eta$ rescattering in s-wave (via $N^*(1535)$). The deuteron wave function is implemented using several parameterizations (Bonn, Paris, Reid hard-core and soft-core, Hulthén) to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Coupling $g_{\gamma p\Delta^*} = 0.188$ used for the $\gamma N \to \Delta(1700)$ vertex, reproducing the radiative decay width of $\Delta(1700)$ from Particle Data Group (2020).
- Coupling $g_{\eta \Delta\Delta^*} = 1.7 - i 1.4$ for the $\Delta(1700) \to \Delta(1232) \eta$ vertex, from Sarkar (2004).
- Coupling $f^* = 2.13$ for the $\Delta \to \pi N$ vertex.
- Deuteron coupling constant $g_d = (2\pi)^{3/2} \times 2.68 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1/2}$.
- The rescattering contributions increase the magnitude of the differential cross sections by about 50% relative to the impulse approximation.
- The deuteron wave function is most frequently probed in the momentum range 300-400 MeV for the photon energies considered (950-1150 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of $\gamma d \to \pi^0 \eta d$ previously investigated experimentally by Ishikawa (2021, 2022), who claimed evidence for a dibaryon $D_{12}(2150)$ and a pole near $\eta d$ threshold. The authors contrast their approach with earlier theoretical models by Egorov (2013, 2020) that implemented $\eta NN$ interactions with different $\eta N$ scattering lengths. The mechanism using $\Delta(1700)$ and $\Delta(1232)$ follows the approach of Debastiani (2017) for $\gamma N \to \pi^0 \eta N$. The work enables future studies of whether dibaryon interpretations are necessary for this reaction, while noting that the angular distribution discrepancy remains unresolved.
