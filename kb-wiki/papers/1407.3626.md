# Doorway States in the Random-Phase Approximation

> By coupling a doorway state to a see of random background states, we develop the theory of doorway states in the framework of the random-phase approximation (RPA). Because of the symmetry of the RPA equations, that theory is radically different from the standard description of doorway states in the shell model. We derive the Pastur equation in the limit of large matrix dimension and show that the results agree with those of matrix diagonalization in large spaces. The complexity of the Pastur equation does not allow for an analytical approach that would approximately describe the doorway state. Our numerical results display unexpected features: The coupling of the doorway state with states of opposite energy leads to strong mutual attraction.

## Key claim

The paper develops a theory of doorway states within the random-phase approximation (RPA) framework, which is radically different from the standard shell-model description due to the symmetry of the RPA equations; the authors show that the Pastur equation can be derived in the large-matrix limit but its complexity prevents analytical approximations for the doorway state, and numerical results reveal unexpected attraction between states of opposite energy.

## Method

The authors constructed a variable-coupling model where a collective doorway state (from a separable RPA interaction) is embedded in a sea of random background states described by a random-matrix RPA ensemble with Gaussian-distributed matrix elements for the residual interaction matrices A and C. They derived the Pastur equation for the average Green function in the limit N → ∞ for the unitary case, obtaining a pair of coupled nonlinear equations for the self-energy functions σ₁(E) and σ₂(E). Numerical solutions of the Pastur equation were compared with results from direct matrix diagonalization of the RPA equations for N = 50 background states.

## Key numbers

- The unperturbed background states are located at r = 2, with the random matrix A having half-width λ = 1/2, so the spectrum of A extends from E = 1 to E = 3 and from E = -3 to E = -1 when C = 0.
- For the variable-coupling model with r = 2 and a = 0.5, coalescence of two non-zero real eigenvalues occurs for coupling parameters v₁ = 0.111803 and v₂ = 0.125, with corresponding w values w₁ = 2.12426 and w₂ = 2.125.
- The doorway strength is of order 1/N relative to the background, so for N = 50 the height decreases as 1/N and becomes progressively less visible for larger N.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of doorway states in the shell-model framework (De Pace 2007, De Pace 2011) and the random-matrix approach to RPA equations (Baran 2009) to combine a collective RPA doorway state with random background states. The paper shows that the RPA symmetry introduces two distinct coupling parameters (v coupling same-sign states and w coupling opposite-sign states) that produce level repulsion and level attraction respectively, a phenomenon absent in the standard shell-model treatment. This enables future studies of doorway phenomena in collective nuclear excitations where RPA is mandatory, such as giant resonances, though the authors note the complexity prevents simple analytical expressions for the doorway location and width.
