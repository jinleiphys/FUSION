# Neutral pion photoproduction on the nucleon in a chiral quark model

> A chiral quark-model approach is adopted to study the $γp\rightarrow π^0p$ and $γn\rightarrow π^0 n$ reactions. Good descriptions of the total and differential cross sections and single-polarization observables are obtained from the pion production threshold up to the second resonance region. It is found that (i) the $n=0$ shell resonance $Δ(1232)P_{33}$, the $n=1$ shell resonances $N(1535)S_{11}$ and $N(1520)D_{13}$, and the $n=2$ shell resonance $N(1720)P_{13}$ play crucial roles in these two processes. They are responsible for the first, second and third bump structures in the cross sections, respectively. (ii) Furthermore, obvious evidences of $N(1650)S_{11}$ and $Δ(1620)S_{31}$ are also found in the reactions. They notably affect the cross sections and the polarization observables from the second resonance region to the third resonance region. (iii) The $u$-channel background plays a crucial role in the reactions. It has strong interferences with the $s$-channel resonances. (iv) The $t$-channel background seems to be needed in the reactions. Including the $t$-channel vector-meson exchange contribution, the descriptions in the energy region $E_γ= 600\sim 900$ MeV are improved significantly. The helicity amplitudes of the main resonances, $Δ(1232)P_{33}$, $N(1535)S_{11}$, $N(1520)D_{13}$, $N(1720)P_{13}$, $N(1650)S_{11}$ and $Δ(1620)S_{31}$, are extracted and compared with the results from other groups.

## Key claim
A chiral quark model with SU(6) x O(3) symmetry breaking via configuration mixing and empirical coupling strength parameters can simultaneously describe neutral pion photoproduction on both proton and neutron targets from threshold through the second resonance region, but the extracted resonance couplings show significant model dependence and require different strength parameters for the two reactions.

## Method
The authors calculated s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel (vector-meson exchange) transition amplitudes using a non-relativistic constituent quark model with a chiral Lagrangian for quark-pion coupling. They introduced empirical coupling strength parameters C_R for each resonance to account for SU(6) x O(3) symmetry breaking, and included explicit configuration mixing between N(1535)S11/N(1650)S11 (mixing angle theta_S = 26 degrees) and N(1520)D13/N(1700)D13 (mixing angle theta_D = 10 degrees). The model was fitted to 450 differential cross section data points and 53 total cross section data points for gamma p -> pi0 p, plus 36 total cross section data points for gamma n -> pi0 n from MAMI.

## Key numbers
- Coupling strength for Delta(1232)P33: C_R = 1.83 (both reactions), indicating the constituent quark model underestimates its photocoupling
- Coupling strength for Delta(1620)S31: C_R = 1.80 (both reactions)
- Coupling strength for N(1520)D13: C_R = 1.41 (proton) and 1.32 (neutron)
- Coupling strength for N(1720)P13: C_R = 1.00 (proton) and 3.20 (neutron), showing a large enhancement needed for the neutron reaction
- Extracted piNN coupling constant: g_piNN = 13.2 (alpha_pi = 13.8)
- Extracted omega qq coupling: g_omega qq = 4.28 (from g_omega pi gamma = 0.32)
- Constituent quark mass: m_q = 330 MeV; harmonic oscillator strength: alpha^2 = 0.16 GeV^2
- Chi-squared per data point for gamma p -> pi0 p fit: chi^2/N_data = 4.3

## Context
This work extends the chiral quark model approach previously applied to eta photoproduction by Zhong (2011) to neutral pion photoproduction, addressing the puzzle of the narrow structure near W = 1.68 GeV observed in eta production off neutrons. The results are compared with partial-wave analyses from the BnGa group (Anisovich, 2015), SAID (Workman, 2012), MAID (Drechsel, 2007), and Juelich (Ronchen, 2014), showing that extracted gamma N couplings for resonances like N(1535)S11 and N(1650)S11 differ notably between groups. The study enables systematic comparison of resonance properties extracted from pi0 N and eta N channels, and provides predictions for the gamma n -> pi0 n reaction that can be tested against new MAMI data from Dieterle (2014).
