# Nucleon and $Δ$ isobar in a strong magnetic field

> We investigate the static properties of the nucleon in the presence of strong magnetic fields and discuss the consequent changes of the nucleon structure, based on the Skyrme model. The results show that at large values of the magnetic field ($\sim10^{17}$ to $10^{18} \mathrm{G}$), which is supposed to appear in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC energies, the soliton starts to deviate from the spherically symmetric form and its size starts to change. At extremely large values of the magnetic field ($\sim 10^{19}$ G), which may be found at the LHC experiments, the soliton becomes more compact than in free space. The results also show that in the presence of the external magnetic field, the mass of the nucleon tends to increase in general and the mass degeneracy of the $Δ$ isobars from isospin symmetry will be lifted. We also discuss the changes in the mass difference between the $Δ$ and the nucleon, $Δm_{Δ\mathrm{N}}$, due to the influence of the external magnetic field. We find that $Δm_{Δ\mathrm{N}}$ increases as the strength of the magnetic field grows.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the Skyrme model, the nucleon's structure remains nearly unchanged for magnetic fields up to about \(10^{17}\) G, but at higher fields (around \(10^{18}\) to \(10^{19}\) G) the soliton deforms from spherical symmetry and becomes more compact, with the nucleon mass increasing and the \(\Delta\) isobar mass degeneracy partially lifted; however, these results are derived from a variational approach with trial functions and apply specifically to a homogeneous magnetic field oriented along the quantization axis.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme model, an effective chiral Lagrangian with parameters \(F_\pi = 108.783\) MeV, \(e = 4.854\), and \(m_\pi = 134.977\) MeV, and introduced a U(1) gauge field to couple a static homogeneous magnetic field \(\mathbf{B}_M\) along the \(z\)-axis. They employed an axially symmetric ansatz for the soliton profile functions \(P(r,\theta)\) and \(\Theta(r,\theta)\), parameterized with variational parameters, and minimized the classical soliton mass functional to find the deformed solutions. The spheroidal soliton was then quantized via collective coordinates for independent rotations in coordinate and isospin spaces to obtain the masses of nucleon and \(\Delta\) isobar states.

## Key numbers

- The classical soliton mass increases by approximately 150 MeV when the magnetic field reaches \(10^{19}\) G (compared to zero field).
- At \(B_M = 10^{17}\) G, the baryon charge integrated within 1 fm is \(B_{\text{(1 fm)}} = 0.9024\), compared to \(0.9014\) at \(B_M = 0\).
- At \(B_M = 10^{19}\) G, the baryon charge integrated within 1 fm is \(B_{\text{(1 fm)}} = 0.9665\).
- The pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 108.783\) MeV, the Skyrme parameter is \(e = 4.854\), and the pion mass is \(m_\pi = 134.977\) MeV.
- The magnetic field strengths considered range from \(10^{15}\) G (magnetar scale) to \(10^{19}\) G (LHC scale), with \(1\) G corresponding to \(2 \times 10^{-20}\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior Skyrme model studies of nucleons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear environments (Yakhshiev 2013, Meissner 2008, Meissner 2009) to the case of an external magnetic field, and it contrasts with earlier works that focused on neutron modifications in neutron stars (Andreichikov 2013, Bigdeli 2017). The results enable future studies of nucleon structure in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and compact astrophysical objects, and the authors note that more general studies including both magnetic field and nuclear medium effects are left for future work.
