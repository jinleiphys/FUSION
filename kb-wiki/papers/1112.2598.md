# Running Coupling Corrections to Nonlinear Evolution for Diffractive Dissociation

> We determine running coupling corrections to the kernel of the non-linear evolution equation for the cross section of single diffractive dissociation in high energy DIS. The running coupling kernel for diffractive evolution is found to be exactly the same as the kernel of the rcBK evolution equation.

## Key claim

The running coupling corrections to the kernel of the non-linear evolution equation for the cross section of single diffractive dissociation in high energy deep inelastic scattering (DIS) are exactly the same as the kernel of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation, with the derivation performed in the large-Nc limit and using the Brodsky-Lepage-Mackenzie (BLM) prescription to dress gluon propagators and multi-gluon vertices with quark bubble corrections.

## Method

The author re-derives the fixed-coupling non-linear evolution equation for single diffractive dissociation in DIS using a method based on the S-matrix-like quantity S^D, which contains the no-interaction term, interactions to the left or right of the cut, and the diffractive cross section N^D. Running coupling corrections are then incorporated by applying the BLM prescription to dress all gluon propagators and multi-gluon vertices with quark bubble corrections, replacing the number of quark flavors N_f by -6 pi beta_2, and showing that final-state cancellations of gluon emissions and absorptions at positive light-cone times still hold when quark loop corrections are included.

## Key numbers

- The running coupling kernel in the Balitsky prescription is given by K_rc^Bal (x0, x1, x2) = (N_c alpha_s(x10^2))/(2 pi^2) [ x10^2/(x20^2 x21^2) + (1/x20^2)(alpha_s(x20^2)/alpha_s(x21^2) - 1) + (1/x21^2)(alpha_s(x21^2)/alpha_s(x20^2) - 1) ], with alpha_s(x_perp^2) = alpha_s(4 e^{-5/3 - 2 gamma_E} / x_perp^2) in the MS-bar scheme.
- The running coupling kernel in the Kovchegov-Weigert prescription is given by K_rc^KW (x0, x1, x2) = (N_c/(2 pi^2)) [ alpha_s(x20^2)/x20^2 - 2 (alpha_s(x20^2) alpha_s(x21^2))/(alpha_s(R^2)) (x20 dot x21)/(x20^2 x21^2) + alpha_s(x21^2)/x21^2 ], with R^2 defined as a function of x20 and x21.
- The one-loop QCD beta-function is beta_2 = (11 N_c - 2 N_f)/(12 pi).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of running coupling corrections previously performed for the linear Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) evolution equation by Kovchegov (2006) and for the non-linear BK and Jalilian-Marian-Iancu-McLerran-Weigert-Leonidov-Kovner (JIMWLK) evolution equations by Kovchegov (2006), Balitsky (2006), Gardi (2006), and Albacete (2007) to the diffractive dissociation evolution equation originally derived by Kovchegov (1999) at fixed coupling and leading-log 1/x and large-Nc approximations. The result enables phenomenological analysis of single diffractive dissociation in DIS using the same running coupling framework that has been successfully applied to total DIS cross sections and structure functions by Albacete (2009, 2010), and may become relevant for future DIS machines where small-x evolution corrections to the q-qbar-plus-one-gluon approximation become necessary.
