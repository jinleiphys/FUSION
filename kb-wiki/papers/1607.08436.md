# Single-particle and collective motion in unbound deformed $ ^{39}\text{Mg} $

> Background: Deformed neutron-rich magnesium isotopes constitute a fascinating territory where the interplay between collective rotation and single-particle motion is strongly affected by the neutron continuum. The unbound $fp$-shell nucleus $ ^{39}\text{Mg} $ is an ideal candidate to study this interplay. Purpose: In this work, we predict the properties of low-lying resonant states of $ ^{39}\text{Mg} $, using a suite of realistic theoretical approaches rooted in the open quantum system framework. Method: To describe the spectrum and decay modes of $ ^{39}\text{Mg} $ we use the conventional Shell Model, Gamow Shell Model, Resonating Group Method, Density Matrix Renormalization Group method, and the non-adiabatic Particle-Plus-Rotor model formulated in the Berggren basis. Results: The unbound ground state of $ ^{39}\text{Mg} $ is predicted to be either a $ J^π = {7/2}^- $ state or a $ {3/2}^- $ state. A narrow $ J^π = {7/2}^- $ ground-state candidate exhibits a resonant structure reminiscent of that of its one-neutron halo neighbor $ ^{37}\text{Mg} $, which is dominated by the $ f_{7/2} $ partial wave at short distances and a $ p_{3/2} $ component at large distances. A $ J^π={3/2}^- $ ground-state candidate is favored by the large deformation of the system. It can be associated with the $ {1/2}^- [321] $ Nilsson orbital dominated by the $\ell=1$ wave; hence its predicted width is large. The excited $J^π = {1/2}^-$ and $5/2^-$ states are expected to be broad resonances, while the $ J^π = {9/2}^- $ and $ {11/2}^- $ members of the ground-state rotational band are predicted to have very small neutron decay widths. Conclusion: We demonstrate that the subtle interplay between deformation, shell structure, and continuum coupling can result in a variety of excitations in an unbound nucleus just outside the neutron drip line.

## Key claim

The unbound ground state of \(^{39}\text{Mg}\) is predicted to be either a \(J^\pi = 7/2^-\) state or a \(3/2^-\) state, with the assignment depending on the theoretical model used; the \(7/2^-\) candidate is narrow and dominated by the \(f_{7/2}\) partial wave at short distances, while the \(3/2^-\) candidate is favored by large deformation and has a large width due to its \(\ell=1\) (p-wave) character.

## Method

The authors employed five complementary theoretical approaches rooted in the open quantum system framework: the conventional Shell Model (SM), the Gamow Shell Model (GSM), the Resonating Group Method (RGM), the Density Matrix Renormalization Group method (DMRG), and the non-adiabatic Particle-Plus-Rotor model (PRM) formulated in the Berggren basis. The GSM interaction was optimized by fitting to experimental data for \(^{34-40}\text{Mg}\) isotopes, using a \(^{28}\text{O}\) core with valence protons in the \(0d_{5/2}\), \(1s_{1/2}\), and \(0d_{3/2}\) shells and valence neutrons in the \(0f_{7/2}\), \(1p_{3/2}\), \(0f_{5/2}\), and \(1p_{1/2}\) shells, with continuum coupling included via the Berggren expansion. The PRM used a deformed Woods-Saxon potential for the core, with parameters optimized independently for \(^{37}\text{Mg}\) and \(^{39}\text{Mg}\).

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole deformation of the even-even magnesium isotopes \(^{36,38,40}\text{Mg}\) is predicted to be about \(\beta_2 = 0.3\).
- In the PRM optimization, the Woods-Saxon parameters for \(^{39}\text{Mg}\) are: diffuseness \(a = 0.728\) fm, radius \(R_0 = 4.206\) fm, depth \(V_0 = -35.00\) MeV, spin-orbit strength \(V_{\text{so}} = 8.000\) MeV, and quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.300\).
- The rotational energies of the \(^{38}\text{Mg}\) core used in the PRM are \(E(2^+_1) = 0.656\) MeV and \(E(4^+_1) = 2.016\) MeV.
- The PRM predicts the \(J^\pi = 9/2^-\) and \(11/2^-\) members of the ground-state rotational band to have very small neutron decay widths, with the \(9/2^-\) state having 62% \(f_{7/2}\) and 30% \(p_{3/2}\) partial wave contributions, and the \(11/2^-\) state having 80% \(f_{7/2}\) and 19% \(p_{3/2}\) contributions.
- The DMRG convergence criterion was set to \(\varepsilon = 10^{-8}\).

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of deformed neutron-rich magnesium isotopes, particularly the one-neutron halo nucleus \(^{37}\text{Mg}\) studied by Kobayashi (2014) and Takechi (2014), and the deformed \(N=28\) isotope \(^{40}\text{Mg}\) studied by Crawford (2014). The predictions for \(^{39}\text{Mg}\) are relevant to understanding how deformed halo structures evolve as the neutron chemical potential moves into the particle continuum, and the results enable future experimental searches for narrow rotational resonances in unbound nuclei, analogous to those predicted in \(^{11}\text{Be}\) by Fossez (2016). The work also connects to earlier studies of deformed halo systems such as \(^{17}\text{C}\), \(^{31}\text{Ne}\), and \(^{37}\text{Mg}\) by Misu (1997) and Zhou (2010).
