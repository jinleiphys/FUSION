# Improved phenomenology of $πN$ transition distribution amplitudes

> To study cross sections and polarization asymmetries for the processes $e p \to e n π^+$ and $e p \to e p π^0$ in the backward region, we develop a flexible phenomenological model for nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes ($πN$ TDAs), which are used in the QCD collinear factorization description of the scattering amplitudes. Our model is based on the two-component factorized Ansatz for the corresponding spectral densities, quadruple distribution. It takes into account the constraints for $πN$ TDAs arising from the threshold pion production theorem and also includes a forward limit contribution that can be fitted to experimental data. We examine the sensitivity of observable predictions to various modelling assumptions.

## Key claim

The authors develop a flexible phenomenological model for nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes (πN TDAs) that incorporates constraints from the threshold pion production theorem and a forward limit contribution, but emphasize that only the normalization component has been fitted to CLAS data while other features remain subject to assumptions, making the predictions outcomes of a data-driven model rather than a fully robust fit.

## Method

The model is constructed using a spectral representation of πN TDAs in terms of quadruple distributions, generalized from the double distribution approach used in GPD phenomenology. A factorized Ansatz separates the forward limit function (expressed as an expansion in orthogonal polynomials on a hexagon domain) from a profile function that controls skewness dependence, with a two-component model implementing the ξ=1 limit fixed by the threshold pion production theorem. The free parameters are constrained by fitting to CLAS data for the unseparated cross section of e p → e' n π+ in near-backward kinematics, assuming σ_T >> σ_L.

## Key numbers

- The forward limit function uses orthogonal polynomials up to total power n=1, with coefficients c_0, c_1, c_2 set in ratio 1:1:1, and c_0 determined by fitting to CLAS data
- The u-dependence is described by a modified dipole formula G(u) = (1 - (u - m_N^2)/m_D^2)^{-2}
- Cross sections and asymmetries are shown for Q^2 = 2.44 GeV^2, W = 2.2 GeV, -u = 0.5 GeV^2, and electron beam energy E_e = 10.6 GeV
- The profile function parameter b controls the strength of skewness dependence, with the weight function parameter d varied as d=1,2,3 to test sensitivity

## Context

This work extends the TDA framework reviewed in Pire (2021) and builds on the spectral representation approach of Pire (2010) and the two-component model for double distributions proposed by Teryaev (2001). It enables quantitative predictions for upcoming JLab experiments, including a dedicated backward exclusive π0 production experiment at Hall C (Li, 2020) and the CLAS measurements of beam spin asymmetry for π+ production (CLAS, 2020), and provides a Monte Carlo implementation in the EpIC generator for experimental acceptance studies.
