# The dijet mass distribution in heavy ion collisions

> In these proceedings, we review the production of both light and heavy flavor dijets in heavy ion collisions and highlight a promising observable to expose their distinct signatures. We propose the modification of dijet invariant mass distributions in heavy ion collisions as a novel observable that exhibits striking sensitivity to the quark-gluon plasma transport properties and heavy quark mass effects on in-medium parton showers. This observable has the advantage of amplifying the effects of jet quenching in contrast to conventional observables, such as the dijet momentum imbalance shift, which involve cancellations of such effects and, hence, result in less pronounced signals. Predictions are presented for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV to guide the future sPHENIX program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The dijet invariant mass distribution in heavy ion collisions is proposed as a novel observable that amplifies jet quenching effects compared to conventional observables like dijet momentum imbalance, because the mass depends on the product of jet transverse momenta rather than their ratio, thereby combining rather than canceling medium-induced modifications. The paper presents predictions for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV to guide the future sPHENIX program, but these predictions depend on model parameters such as the medium coupling \(g_{\rm med}\) and the assumed mass of the propagating heavy quark system.

## Method

The dijet production cross section in heavy ion collisions was calculated by first generating the p+p baseline using Pythia 8 with sPHENIX kinematic cuts, then incorporating nuclear geometry via the optical Glauber model thickness functions and medium-induced energy loss from both radiative and collisional processes. The energy loss probability distributions and the fraction of energy lost outside each jet cone were used to map the p+p cross section to Au+Au collisions. From this cross section, differential in leading and subleading jet transverse momenta, the dijet imbalance \(z_J = p_{2T}/p_{1T}\) and dijet invariant mass \(m_{12}\) distributions were constructed, and the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) was computed for both inclusive and b-tagged dijets.

## Key numbers

- For the dijet imbalance shift, \(\Delta \langle z_J\rangle _{bb} = 0.065 \pm 0.012\) for heavy flavor dijets and \(\Delta \langle z_J\rangle _{jj} = 0.100 \pm 0.005\) for light flavor dijets in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for inclusive dijets shows a factor of \(\sim 10\) or more suppression up to a dijet invariant mass of about 100 GeV.
- In the low invariant mass range around 20 GeV, the suppressions experienced by light and heavy flavor dijets differ by almost an order of magnitude, as shown by the ratio of b-tagged \(R_{AA}\) to inclusive \(R_{AA}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Kang (2018) for dijet production in heavy ion collisions and provides predictions specifically for the sPHENIX experiment at RHIC. The proposed dijet mass observable contrasts with traditional dijet imbalance measurements performed by CMS (Chatrchyan 2013) and ALICE (Acharya 2017), which exhibit cancellation of medium effects due to the ratio of transverse momenta. The results enable experimental discrimination between light and heavy flavor energy loss mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma, addressing an open question about the mass dependence of parton energy loss.
