# Universal trend of charge radii of even-even Ca-Zn nuclei

> Radii of nuclear charge distributions carry information about the strong and electromagnetic forces acting inside the atomic nucleus. While the global behavior of nuclear charge radii is governed by the bulk properties of nuclear matter, their local trends are affected by quantum motion of proton and neutron nuclear constituents. The measured differential charge radii $δ\langle r^2_c\rangle$ between neutron numbers $N=28$ and $N=40$ exhibit a universal pattern as a function of $n=N-28$ that is independent of the atomic number. Here we analyze this remarkable behavior in even-even nuclei from calcium to zinc using two state-of-the-art theories based on quantified nuclear interactions: the ab-initio coupled cluster theory and nuclear density functional theory. Both theories reproduce the smooth rise of differential charge radii and their weak dependence on the atomic number. By considering a large set of isotopic chains, we show that this trend can be captured by just two parameters: the slope and curvature of ${δ\langle r^2_c\rangle(n)}$. We demonstrate that these parameters show appreciable model dependence, and the statistical analysis indicates that they are not correlated with any single model property, i.e., they are impacted by both bulk nuclear properties as well as shell structure.

## Key claim

The measured differential charge radii of even-even nuclei from calcium to zinc beyond neutron number N=28 exhibit a universal pattern as a function of n = N-28 that is independent of the atomic number, and this trend can be captured by just two parameters (slope and curvature) that show appreciable model dependence and are not correlated with any single model property.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary theoretical approaches: ab-initio coupled cluster (CC) theory using the ΔNNLO_GO chiral interaction with a 394 MeV cutoff, and nuclear density functional theory (DFT) using the Skyrme parametrization SV-min and the Fayans functional Fy(Δr, HFB). CC calculations were performed in the singles and doubles approximation (CCSD) using a natural-orbital basis from axially symmetric Hartree-Fock states with 15 harmonic oscillator shells, while DFT calculations used the SkyAx and HFBTHO solvers allowing for deformed solutions. The differential charge radii for isotopic chains with atomic numbers Z=20 to Z=30 and neutron numbers up to N=40 were computed and fitted to a parabolic function δ⟨r²_c⟩ = an + bn².

## Key numbers

- The CC calculations used a model space of 15 harmonic oscillator shells (N_max=14) and natural orbital truncation at N_max^nat=12, with oscillator spacing ħω=16 MeV
- For ⁶⁴Zn, the CCSD ground-state energy converged to -524.384 MeV and charge radius to 3.886 fm at N_max^nat=12 with ħω=16 MeV
- The nucleonic charge radii used were ⟨r_p²⟩=0.709 fm² and ⟨r_n²⟩=-0.106 fm², with Darwin-Foldy term ⟨r_DF²⟩=0.033 fm²
- Ab-initio CC results for charge radii carry ±2% uncertainty due to model-space and cluster-operator truncations
- For the Fayans functional Fy, the average curvature coefficient b is very close to zero; for SV-min, b is positive (concave up pattern)
- Analysis of Green's function results from Soma (2021) yields a=0.084±0.006 fm² and b=0.003±0.001 fm² for the slope and curvature parameters

## Context

This work extends the experimental observation by Koszorus (2021) that measured differential charge radii exhibit an element-independent steep increase beyond neutron number N=28 from potassium (Z=19) to iron (Z=26). The study uses the generalized seniority model framework of Zamick (1971) and Talmi (1984), which predicts parabolic patterns of differential radii in even-N isotopes. The results enable future investigations into how the slope and curvature parameters of charge radii trends relate to properties of nuclear forces, and motivate experimental extension of charge radii data for neutron-rich Ti, Cr, and Fe isotopes.
