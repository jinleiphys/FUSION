# Model Study of Three-Body Forces in the Three-Body Bound State

> The Faddeev equations for the three-body bound state with two- and three-body forces are solved directly as three-dimensional integral equation. The numerical feasibility and stability of the algorithm, which does not employ partial wave decomposition is demonstrated. The three-body binding energy and the full wave function are calculated with Malfliet-Tjon-type two-body potentials and scalar Fujita-Miyazawa type three-body forces. The influence of the strength and range of the three-body force on the wave function, single particle momentum distributions and the two-body correlation functions are studied in detail. The extreme case of pure three-body forces is investigated as well.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that solving the Faddeev equation for the three-body bound state directly in three-dimensional momentum space (without partial wave decomposition) is numerically feasible and stable even when three-body forces are included, but the authors explicitly state that since they omit spin and isospin degrees of freedom, their numerical examples are not meant to address physical questions about the three-nucleon system directly, only to shed light on wave function properties.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equation for three identical bosons interacting via Malfliet-Tjon-type two-body potentials and scalar Fujita-Miyazawa-type three-body forces, working directly with vector Jacobi momenta as variables rather than performing a partial wave decomposition. The three-body force is decomposed into three pieces symmetric under exchange of two particles, and the resulting integral equation is solved iteratively using a Lanczos-type algorithm on a three-dimensional grid of momentum magnitudes and angles. The matrix elements of the three-body force are evaluated through a sequence of one-dimensional integrations and three-dimensional interpolations using cubic splines.

## Key numbers

- Two-body binding energy of the MT2-II potential: 0.284 MeV
- Three-body binding energy with two-body forces alone (MT2-II): 7.6986 MeV
- Three-body binding energy with attractive three-body force MT3-I added: 8.8732 MeV
- Three-body binding energy with mixed attractive-repulsive three-body force MT3-II added: 8.6478 MeV
- Experimental triton binding energy cited for comparison: 8.48 MeV
- Grid sizes of 97 × 97 × 42 points in (p, q, x) yield binding energy accuracy of 5 significant figures
- Momentum grid ranges: p up to 60 fm⁻¹, q up to 40 fm⁻¹
- Angle grid: 42 points for the x integration
- Difference between expectation value ⟨H⟩ and eigenvalue E is about 10 keV for the purely attractive three-body force, and about 5 keV for the mixed three-body force
- Deviation from ideal equilateral triangle geometry (δ) is 3% or less for all cases studied

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-dimensional approach to the three-boson bound state (Elster 1998) by including three-body forces, and contrasts with the traditional partial-wave decomposition method used in most Faddeev calculations since Malfliet and Tjon (1969) and Osborn (1970). The approach is analogous to Greens Function Monte Carlo methods in configuration space but works in momentum space. The paper enables future inclusion of spin and isospin degrees of freedom to address the physical three-nucleon system, and provides benchmark results for testing other methods. The authors note that the single-particle momentum distributions and two-body correlation functions they obtain are qualitatively similar to those found with realistic nuclear forces by Nogga (PhD thesis).
