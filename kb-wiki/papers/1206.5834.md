# Thermal signatures of pairing correlations in nuclei and nano-scale metallic grains

> Atomic nuclei and nano-scale metallic grains are in the crossover regime of pairing correlations between the bulk limit, where the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory of superconductivity is valid, and the fluctuation-dominated regime, where BCS theory breaks down. In this fluctuation-dominated regime, the pairing gap is comparable to or smaller than the single-particle mean level spacing. We discuss thermal signatures of pairing correlations in nuclei and ultra-small metallic grains that survive despite the large fluctuations of the pairing field.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations survive in the fluctuation-dominated regime (where the pairing gap is comparable to or smaller than the single-particle mean level spacing) and can be identified through thermal signatures such as odd-even effects in thermodynamic observables, even though BCS mean-field theory breaks down.

## Method

The author reviews finite-temperature methods that go beyond BCS mean-field theory, including the static path approximation (SPA) and SPA+RPA, which include thermal and small-amplitude quantal fluctuations of the pairing field. For full inclusion of all fluctuations, the auxiliary-field Monte Carlo (AFMC) method is applied to both nuclei (using the configuration-interaction shell model with monopole pairing and multipole-multipole interactions) and nano-scale metallic grains (using the universal Hamiltonian with pairing and exchange interactions). For metallic grains, a number-parity and spin-projected SPA+RPA method is used to efficiently study mesoscopic fluctuations across many random-matrix-theory realizations of the single-particle spectrum.

## Key numbers

- In even-even nuclei \(^{56}\)Fe and \(^{62}\)Fe, a shoulder (S-shape) in the heat capacity versus temperature is observed in AFMC calculations, surviving despite large fluctuations; this shoulder is suppressed in the odd-even nucleus \(^{57}\)Fe.
- The spin-cutoff parameter \(\sigma^2\) extracted from AFMC spin distributions for \(^{55}\)Fe, \(^{56}\)Fe, and \(^{60}\)Co is compared with the rigid-body formula \(\sigma^2 = I T / \hbar^2\); for the even-even nucleus \(^{56}\)Fe, \(\sigma^2\) is suppressed at low excitation energies, correlated with the onset of large \(J=0\) pair correlations \(\langle \Delta^\dagger \Delta \rangle\).
- For a metallic grain with \(\Delta/\delta = 3\) and equally spaced single-particle spectrum, the AFMC heat capacity shows an odd-even effect; the spin susceptibility of the odd grain exhibits a re-entrance effect (decreases as temperature is lowered before diverging as \(T \to 0\)).
- The number-parity projected SPA+RPA method reproduces exact Richardson-solution results for heat capacity (grain with \(\Delta/\delta = 3\), \(J_s/\delta = 0.5\)) and spin susceptibility (grain with \(\Delta/\delta = 0.5\), \(J_s/\delta = 0.5\)) above a critical temperature, but breaks down below that temperature.

## Context

This review extends the work of Anderson (1959) on the crossover from BCS superconductivity to the fluctuation-dominated regime in finite systems, and builds on experimental studies of ultra-small metallic grains (e.g., single-electron tunneling spectroscopy) and nuclear heat capacity measurements using the Oslo method (Schiller, 2001). The methods discussed enable the identification of pairing signatures, such as odd-even effects in heat capacity, spin distributions, and moment of inertia, in systems where BCS theory fails, and open the door to studying mesoscopic fluctuations of thermodynamic observables in chaotic metallic grains using efficient finite-temperature techniques.
