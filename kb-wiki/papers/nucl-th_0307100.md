# A simple model for the quenching of pairing correlations effects in rigidly deformed rotational bands

> Using Chandrasekhar's S-type coupling between rotational and intrinsic vortical modes one may simply reproduce the HFB dynamical properties of rotating nuclei within Routhian HF calculations free of pairing correlations yet constrained on the relevant so-called Kelvin circulation operator. From the analogy between magnetic and rotating systems, one derives a model for the quenching of pairing correlations with rotation, introducing a critical angular velocity -- analogous to the critical field in supraconductors -- above which pairing vanishes. Taking stock of this usual model, it is then shown that the characteristic behavior of the vortical mode angular velocity as a function of the global rotation angular velocity can be modelised by a simple two parameter formula, both parameters being completely determined from properties of the band-head (zero-spin) HFB solution. From calculation in five nuclei, the validity of this modelised Routhian approach is assessed. It is clearly shown to be very good in cases where the evolution of rotational properties is only governed by the coupling between the global rotation and the pairing-induced intrinsic vortical currents. It therefore provides a sound ground base for evaluating the importance of coupling of rotation with other modes (shape distortions, quasiparticle degrees of freedom).

## Key claim

Using a simple two-parameter formula derived from the analogy between rotating nuclei and magnetic systems, the characteristic behavior of the intrinsic vortical mode angular velocity as a function of global rotation can be modeled, with both parameters completely determined from properties of the zero-spin Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) solution, but the model is only valid when rotational evolution is governed solely by the coupling between global rotation and pairing-induced intrinsic vortical currents, and it fails when other degrees of freedom (quasiparticle excitations, shape distortions) become important.

## Method

The authors model the quenching of pairing correlations with rotation using a magnetic analog inspired by Mottelson and Valatin (1960), introducing a critical angular velocity analogous to the critical field in superconductors. They propose an ansatz where the Kelvin circulation velocity omega is proportional to the global rotation angular velocity Omega times a power of the pairing correlation energy, leading to a three-parameter expression that is reduced to two parameters (k and Omega_c) by setting the power gamma to 1. The critical angular velocity is determined from the zero-spin pair condensation energy and a semiclassical moment of inertia, while the scale parameter k is obtained from the low-angular-velocity dynamical moment of inertia using Inglis cranking formulas with a Thouless-Valatin correction factor. The model is tested by comparing Routhian HF+V calculations using the model omega(Omega) values against full HFB calculations for five nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Quentin et al., 2003, referred to as I) which showed that dynamical effects of pairing correlations at finite spins could be represented by an intrinsic counter-rotating flow using the Kelvin circulation operator. The model builds on the Coriolis anti-pairing effect proposed by Mottelson and Valatin (1960) and uses Chandrasekhar's S-type coupling between rotational and intrinsic vortical modes. The approach enables simple Routhian HF calculations free of pairing correlations to reproduce HFB dynamical properties, providing a foundation for evaluating the importance of coupling rotation with other modes such as shape distortions and quasiparticle degrees of freedom, which are shown to cause deviations in 154Sm and 178Hf where up-bending patterns appear due to quasiparticle intrusions rather than pairing-rotation coupling.
