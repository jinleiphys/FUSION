# Interaction of the pseudoscalar glueball with (pseudo)scalar mesons and nucleons

> We study the interactions of the pseudoscalar glueball with scalar and pseudoscalar quark-antiquark meson fields and with the nucleon and its chiral partner. In both cases we introduce the corresponding chiral Lagrangian and discuss its properties. We calculate the mesonic and baryonic decays of a pseudoscalar glueball with mass of about 2.6 GeV as predicted by Lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper presents chiral Lagrangians for the interaction of a pseudoscalar glueball with mass of about 2.6 GeV (as predicted by lattice QCD) with mesons and baryons, but the branching ratios for mesonic decays are independent of the unknown coupling constant and the baryonic decay ratio is a prediction that can be experimentally checked in the future at PANDA.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian coupling the pseudoscalar glueball field to scalar and pseudoscalar mesons via a determinant term, and separately to nucleon fields and their chiral partner using the mirror assignment scheme. They calculate two-body decays into one scalar and one pseudoscalar meson, three-body decays into three pseudoscalar mesons, and baryonic decays into nucleon-antinucleon and nucleon-chiral partner pairs, accounting for interference effects between direct three-body decays and sequential decays through intermediate scalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar glueball mass: about 2.6 GeV, as predicted by lattice simulations (Morningstar)
- Branching ratio for three-body decay $\tilde{G}\rightarrow KK\pi$: 0.46 of total width
- Branching ratio for three-body decay $\tilde{G}\rightarrow \eta\pi\pi$: 0.16 of total width
- Branching ratio for two-body decay $\tilde{G}\rightarrow a_{0}\pi$: 0.083 of total width
- Branching ratio for two-body decay $\tilde{G}\rightarrow KK_{S}$: 0.059 of total width
- Relative interference error for $\tilde{G}\rightarrow K^{0}\bar{K}^{0}\pi^{0}$: 7.3% (for $g>0$) or 2.2% (for $g<0$)
- Ratio $\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\overline{N}N}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\overline{N}^{\ast}N+h.c.}=1.94$
- Coupling constant $g=2.73$ GeV for $K_{S}K\pi$ interaction, obtained from experimental total decay width $\Gamma_{K_{0}^{\ast}}=270$ MeV
- Mass parameter $m_{0}=460\pm136$ MeV from fit to vacuum properties (gallas)

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach to pseudoscalar glueballs previously developed by the authors (Eshraim et al., 2012) and builds on lattice QCD glueball spectrum calculations (Morningstar). The mesonic sector follows the chiral Lagrangian framework of Schechter and the multiplet structure of Dick. The baryonic interaction is new and enables predictions for the PANDA experiment at FAIR, where antiproton-proton collisions can produce pseudoscalar glueballs in the center-of-mass energy range above 2.5 GeV. The interference analysis addresses a subtle issue only mentioned in the authors' prior work, showing that sequential decays through scalar mesons do not spoil the direct three-body decay predictions at the current level of precision.
