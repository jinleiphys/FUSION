# BCS approximation to the effective vector vertex of superfluid fermions

> We examine the effective interaction of nonrelativistic fermions with an external vector field in superfluid systems. In contrast to the complicated vertex equation, usually used in this case, we apply the approach which does not employ an explicit form of the pairing interaction. This allows to obtain a simple analytic expression for the vertex function only in terms of the order parameter and other macroscopic parameters of the system. We use this effective vertex to analyze the linear response function of the superfluid medium at finite temperatures. At the time-like momentum transfer, the imaginary part of the response function is found to be proportional to the fourth power of small Fermi velocity, i.e. the energy losses through vector currents are strongly suppressed. As an application, we calculate the neutrino energy losses through neutral weak currents caused by the pair recombination in the superfluid neutron matter at temperatures lower than the critical one for S-wave pairing. This approach confirms a strong suppression of the neutrino energy losses as predicted in Ref.[4].

## Key claim

The paper derives a simple analytic expression for the effective vector vertex of nonrelativistic superfluid fermions that satisfies current conservation without requiring an explicit form of the pairing interaction, valid under the conditions \(\omega \ll \mu\) and \(|\mathbf{q}| \ll \mathsf{p}_F\) (where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential and \(\mathsf{p}_F\) the Fermi momentum), and shows that at time-like momentum transfer the imaginary part of the response function is proportional to the fourth power of the small Fermi velocity, strongly suppressing energy losses through vector currents.

## Method

The author uses the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism to describe quasiparticles in a superfluid, starting from the BCS inverse propagator with an energy gap \(\Delta\). Instead of solving the full vertex equation with a specific pairing interaction, the linear correction to the gap in an external vector field is expressed via an unknown kernel \(Q_\mu(q)\), which is then determined by imposing current conservation on the polarization tensor \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}(q)\). The resulting effective vertex is obtained in terms of \(\Delta\) and macroscopic parameters, and the polarization tensor is evaluated analytically in the quasiparticle approximation, neglecting recoil and expanding to order \(V_F^2\).

## Key numbers

* The imaginary part of the response function at time-like momentum transfer is proportional to \(V_F^4\), where \(V_F\) is the Fermi velocity (small in nonrelativistic systems, e.g., \(V_F \sim 0.1\) for typical astrophysical applications).
* The sound velocity in the Fermi gas is given by \(c_s \equiv V_F / \sqrt{3}\).
* The longitudinal polarization at zero temperature is \(\Pi_l(q; T=0) = -\frac{p_F M^*}{\pi^2} \frac{\mathsf{q}^2 c_s^2}{(\omega + i0)^2 - \mathsf{q}^2 c_s^2}\), where \(p_F\) is the Fermi momentum and \(M^*\) the effective mass.
* At temperatures above the critical temperature \(T_c\), the longitudinal polarization reduces to the normal Fermi gas result: \(\Pi_l(q; T > T_c) = -\frac{p_F M^*}{\pi^2} \left(1 - \frac{1}{2} \frac{\omega}{\mathsf{q} V_F} \ln \frac{\omega + q V_F}{\omega - q V_F}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the BCS theory of gauge invariance, originally developed by Bogoliubov (1958), Anderson (1958), Nambu (1960), and Ambegaokar (1964), to finite temperatures and time-like momentum transfers, where previous solutions were limited to \(\omega, |\mathbf{q}| < \Delta\) at zero temperature. It corrects earlier calculations of neutrino energy losses from pair recombination in superfluid neutron matter by Flower (1976), which overestimated the rate, and by Voskresensky (2001) and Sedrakian (2005), which violated current conservation or the zero-gap limit. The derived effective vertex enables accurate calculation of the linear response of superfluid neutron matter to weak vector currents, with direct application to neutrino emission from neutron star crusts via neutral weak currents.
