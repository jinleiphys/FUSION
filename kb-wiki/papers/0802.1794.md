# Metastability of hadronic compact stars

> Pure hadronic compact stars, above a threshold value of their gravitational mass (central pressure), are metastable to the conversion to quark stars (hybrid or strange stars). In this paper, we present a systematic study of the metastability of pure hadronic compact stars using different relativistic models for the equation of state (EoS). In particular, we compare results for the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model with those for the Glendenning--Moszkowski parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM). For QMC model, we find large values ($M_{cr} = 1.6$ -- $1.9 M_\odot$) for the critical mass of the hadronic star sequence and we find that the formation of a quark star is only possible with a soft quark matter EoS. For the Glendenning--Moszkowski parametrization of the NLWM, we explore the effect of different hyperon couplings on the critical mass and on the stellar conversion energy. We find that increasing the value of the hyperon coupling constants shifts the bulk transition point for quark deconfinement to higher densities, increases the stellar metastability threshold mass and the value of the critical mass, and thus makes the formation of quark stars less likely. For the largest values of the hyperon couplings we find a critical mass which may be as high as 1.9 - 2.1 $M_\odot$. These stellar configurations, which contain a large central hyperon fraction ($f_{Y,cr} \sim 30 %$), would be able to describe highly-massive compact stars, such as the one associated to the millisecond pulsars PSR B1516+02B with a mass $M = 1.94^{+ 0.17}_{- 0.19} M_{\odot}$.

# Metastability of hadronic compact stars

## Key claim

Pure hadronic compact stars with gravitational mass above a threshold value (the "critical mass" $M_{cr}$) are metastable to conversion into quark stars (hybrid or strange stars), but the actual observable lifetime of such stars depends on the nucleation time of the first quark matter drop; stars with mass above $M_{cr}$ (defined as the mass for which nucleation time equals one year) are very unlikely to be observed, while those below $M_{cr}$ are safe from sudden transition.

## Method

The authors computed the quantum nucleation time of a non-$\beta$-stable quark matter drop in the center of a hadronic star using a semi-classical WKB tunneling approach, with the droplet effective mass and potential barrier derived from the Gibbs free energy difference between hadronic and quark phases. They used two families of relativistic mean-field models for the hadronic equation of state: the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model and the Glendenning-Moszkowski (GM1 and GM3) parametrizations of the non-linear Walecka model, with varying hyperon-meson coupling constants ($x_\sigma = 0.6, 0.7, 0.8$). For the quark phase they adopted the MIT bag model with bag constants $B = 75, 85, 100, 150$ MeV/fm$^3$, strange quark mass $m_s = 150$ MeV, and $\alpha_s = 0$, and considered surface tensions $\sigma = 10$ and $30$ MeV/fm$^2$.

## Key numbers

- Critical mass for QMC model: $M_{cr} = 1.6$ -- $1.9 M_\odot$ (range depending on bag constant and surface tension)
- Critical mass for GM1/GM3 with largest hyperon couplings ($x_\sigma = 0.8$): up to $1.9$ -- $2.1 M_\odot$
- Central hyperon fraction at critical mass for largest hyperon couplings: $f_{Y,cr} \sim 30\%$
- Observed mass of PSR B1516+02B: $M = 1.94^{+0.17}_{-0.19} M_\odot$ (1 $\sigma$)
- Observed mass of PSR J1748-2021B: $M = 2.74^{+0.41}_{-0.51} M_\odot$ (2 $\sigma$)
- Increase in critical mass when $x_\sigma$ changes from 0.6 to 0.8: $0.3$ -- $0.4 M_\odot$
- Surface tension values considered: $\sigma = 10$ and $30$ MeV/fm$^2$
- Bag constants considered: $B = 75, 85, 100, 150$ MeV/fm$^3$
- Strange quark matter is absolutely stable only for $B = 75$ MeV/fm$^3$ with the adopted parameter set

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark matter nucleation in neutron stars by Bombaci et al. (2004) and others (Iida & Sato 1997, 1998; Berezhiani et al. 2003; Drago et al. 2004; Lugones et al. 2005) by systematically comparing the QMC model with the Glendenning-Moszkowski parametrizations and by exploring the effect of varying hyperon-meson couplings beyond the standard "low" value ($x_\sigma = 0.6$) used in prior work. The results enable interpretation of recent high-mass neutron star measurements (PSR B1516+02B at $1.94 M_\odot$ and PSR J1748-2021B at $2.74 M_\odot$) in terms of metastable hadronic stars that could avoid conversion to quark stars if their mass lies below the critical mass, or alternatively could explain the existence of highly-massive compact stars if hyperon couplings are large enough to raise the critical mass to $1.9$ -- $2.1 M_\odot$.
