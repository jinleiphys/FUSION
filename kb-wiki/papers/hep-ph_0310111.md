# Enhancement of charm quark production due to nonlinear corrections to the DGLAP equations

> We have studied how parton distributions based on the inclusion of nonlinear scale evolution and constraints from HERA data affect charm production in $pp$ collisions at center-of-mass energies of 5.5, 8.8 and 14 TeV. We find that, while the resulting enhancement can be substantial, it is very sensitive to the charm quark mass and the scale entering the parton densities and the strong coupling constant.

## Key claim

Nonlinear (GLRMQ) corrections to the DGLAP evolution equations, when constrained by HERA data, can produce a substantial enhancement of charm quark production in pp collisions at LHC energies, but the magnitude of this enhancement is very sensitive to the assumed charm quark mass and the choice of factorization/renormalization scale.

## Method

The authors calculated leading-order (LO) charm quark production cross sections in pp collisions using the EHKQS parton distribution functions (PDFs), which incorporate nonlinear GLRMQ (Gribov-Levin-Ryskin-Mueller-Qiu) recombination terms in the DGLAP evolution and are constrained by HERA F2 data. They compared these results to calculations using the standard LO DGLAP-evolved CTEQ61L PDFs, computing ratios of differential cross sections as functions of charm quark transverse momentum (pT), rapidity (y), and c-cbar pair invariant mass (M) at center-of-mass energies of 5.5, 8.8, and 14 TeV.

## Key numbers

* For m_c = 1.2 GeV and Q^2 = 4m_T^2, the unintegrated ratio R(p_T,y,y_2) at central rapidities (y=y_2=0) and p_T ≈ 0 is approximately 1.4–1.5 at √S = 14 TeV.
* For m_c = 1.3 GeV and Q^2 = m_T^2, the same ratio R(p_T,y,y_2) at central rapidities and p_T ≈ 0 is approximately 3.7–5.5 at √S = 14 TeV.
* For m_c = 1.8 GeV and Q^2 = 4m_T^2, R(p_T,y,y_2) at central rapidities and p_T ≈ 0 is 1.1–1.2 at √S = 14 TeV; for Q^2 = m_T^2 it is 1.7–2.1.
* The integrated rapidity enhancement R(y) at midrapidity ranges from approximately 1.07 to 1.8 across the energies studied.
* The integrated p_T enhancement R(p_T=0) ranges from approximately 1.05–1.25 with Q^2 = 4m_T^2 and approximately 1.6–4.5 with Q^2 = m_T^2 over the range of m_c investigated.
* The unconstrained x-region (x < 10^{-5}) contributes to charm production at |y| ≥ 2.2, 2.6, and 3.1 for √S = 14, 8.8, and 5.5 TeV respectively (for m_c = 1.2 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier EHKQS analysis (Eskola, Kolhinen, and Salgado, 2003) which showed that adding GLRMQ nonlinear terms to DGLAP evolution can improve LO fits to HERA DIS data. The study directly compares the EHKQS nonlinear PDFs to standard DGLAP-evolved PDFs from CTEQ (specifically CTEQ61L) and MRST, which both successfully describe high-(Q^2,x) data but face challenges at low (Q^2,x). The results demonstrate that charm production at the LHC could serve as a more sensitive probe of nonlinear QCD evolution than structure function measurements alone, though the strong sensitivity to m_c and scale means that further theoretical input (such as high-energy resummation) and experimental data are needed to distinguish linear from nonlinear evolution.
