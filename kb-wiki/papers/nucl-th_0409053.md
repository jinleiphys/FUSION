# Critical densities for the Skyrme type effective interactions

> We use the stability conditions of the Landau parameters for the symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter to calculate the critical densities for the Skyrme type effective nucleon-nucleon interactions. We find that the critical density can be maximized by adjusting appropriately the values of the enhancement factor $κ$ associated with isovector giant dipole resonance, the quantity $L$ which is directly related to the slope of the symmetry energy and the Landau parameter $G_0^\prime$. However, restricting $κ$, $L$ and $G_0^\prime$ to vary within acceptable limits reduces the maximum value for the critical density $\tildeρ_{cr}$ by $\sim 25%$. We also show that among the various quantities characterizing the symmetric nuclear matter, $\tildeρ_{cr}$ depends strongly on the isoscalar effective mass $m^*/m$ and surface energy coefficient $E_s$. For realistic values of $m^*/m$ and $E_s$ we get $\tildeρ_{cr} = 2ρ_0$ to $ 3ρ_0$ ($ρ_0 = 0.16$fm$^{-3}$).

# Critical densities for the Skyrme type effective interactions

## Key claim
The maximum critical density for Skyrme interactions is reduced by about 25% when the enhancement factor κ, the symmetry-energy slope L, and the Landau parameter G₀′ are restricted to physically acceptable ranges, compared to calculations that vary the Skyrme parameter combinations tᵢxᵢ without such restrictions.

## Method
The authors used the stability conditions of Landau parameters (Fₗ, Fₗ′, Gₗ, Gₗ′ for symmetric nuclear matter and Fₗ⁽ⁿ⁾, Gₗ⁽ⁿ⁾ for pure neutron matter) to determine the maximum density up to which all inequality conditions are satisfied. They expressed the Skyrme parameters tᵢ, xᵢ, and α in terms of nuclear matter quantities (ρₙₘ, B/A, m*/m, Kₙₘ, Eₛ, J) and then varied κ, L, and G₀′ within constrained ranges (0.25 ≤ κ ≤ 0.5, 0 ≤ L ≤ 100 MeV, 0 ≤ G₀′ ≤ 0.5) while requiring L > 0 at 3ρ₀.

## Key numbers
- For standard nuclear matter input (ρₙₘ = 0.16 fm⁻³, B/A = 16 MeV, Kₙₘ = 230 MeV, m*/m = 0.7, Eₛ = 18 MeV, J = 32 MeV), the maximum critical density with unrestricted parameters is 3.5ρ₀ (κ = 1.0, L = 36 MeV, G₀′ = 0.20).
- With restrictions (0.25 ≤ κ ≤ 0.5, 0 ≤ L ≤ 100 MeV, 0 ≤ G₀′ ≤ 0.5), the maximum critical density is reduced by up to 25% compared to unrestricted calculations.
- For m*/m = 0.6 (other quantities at standard values), the restricted calculation gives ρ̃_cr = 4.5ρ₀ versus 6ρ₀ in the unrestricted case.
- For m*/m = 0.7 (other quantities at standard values), the restricted calculation gives ρ̃_cr = 2.8ρ₀ versus 3.5ρ₀ in the unrestricted case.
- For realistic values of m*/m and Eₛ, the critical density lies in the range 2ρ₀ to 3ρ₀ (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³).
- For Eₛ = 18 ± 1 MeV, ρ̃_cr = 2ρ₀ corresponds to m*/m = 0.72 – 0.85, and ρ̃_cr = 3ρ₀ corresponds to m*/m = 0.63 – 0.73.
- The value of L at ρ₀ for standard nuclear matter input is 47 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Margueron (2002), who calculated critical densities by varying the Skyrme parameter combinations tᵢxᵢ without restrictions, by instead constraining the physically meaningful quantities κ, L, and G₀′. The study complements the systematic survey by Stone (2003), which found that only 27 out of 87 Skyrme parameterizations have positive symmetry-energy slope up to 3ρ₀ and are suitable for neutron star models. The results enable the construction of Skyrme interactions that simultaneously satisfy stability conditions up to high densities, reproduce finite-nuclei properties, and remain applicable to neutron star studies, with the caveat that pairing correlations were not included and may alter the critical density values.
