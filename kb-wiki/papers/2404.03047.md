# Glue in hadrons at medium resolution and the QCD instanton vacuum

> We discuss a general framework for the evaluation of the gluonic form factors in light hadrons at low momentum transfer, in the QCD instanton vacuum. At medium resolution of the order of the inverse mean instanton size, the glue is mostly localized in single or pair of pseudoparticles, and globally constrained by the fluctuations of their topological charges. These pseudoparticles trap light quarks, giving rise to emerging multiflavor 't Hooft interactions. We explicitly evaluate the gluonic scalar, pseudoscalar, energy-momentum tensor (EMT), and the leading C-odd and C-even three gluons hadronic form factors, at next to leading order (NLO) in the instanton density, including molecular clusters of like and unlike instantons. We use the results for the EMT to address the contribution of the gluons in Ji$^\prime$s mass and spin sum rules, at low resolution. When evolved, our results for the mass and spin composition of the nucleon, are shown to be in good agreement with the recently reported lattice results at higher resolution.

## Key claim

The QCD instanton vacuum provides a well-defined framework for evaluating gluonic form factors in light hadrons at medium resolution (momentum transfers of order the inverse instanton size, ~0.6 GeV), with results for the nucleon mass and spin composition that, when evolved to higher resolution, agree with recent lattice QCD results, provided that contributions from both isolated instantons and instanton-anti-instanton molecular clusters are included at next-to-leading order in the instanton density.

## Method

The authors evaluate gauge-invariant matrix elements of gluonic operators (scalar G², pseudoscalar G\tilde{G}, energy-momentum tensor, and C-odd and C-even three-gluon operators) in light hadrons using the QCD instanton vacuum, where the gluon fields are treated as semiclassical pseudoparticles (instantons and anti-instantons) with their bosonic and fermionic moduli. The calculations are carried out to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the instanton density (packing fraction κ ≈ 0.1), including contributions from both like and unlike instanton pairs (molecular clusters), and the results are expressed in terms of induced non-local form factors that connect gluonic matrix elements to known forward light-quark operators.

## Key numbers

- Mean instanton size ρ ≈ 1/3 fm and instanton density n_{I+A} ≈ 1 fm⁻⁴, giving packing fraction κ = n_{I+A}π²ρ⁴ ≈ 0.1
- Intermediate scale μ² ~ 1/ρ² ≈ (0.6 GeV)², reaching up to μ² ~ 1 GeV²
- Scalar glueball mass m_{0⁺⁺} = 1.25 GeV (with band 1.25–1.75 GeV from lattice), coupling λ_{0⁺⁺} = 15.6 GeV³
- Sigma meson mass m_σ = 683.1 MeV, coupling λ_σ = 2.6 GeV³
- Perturbative threshold s₀ = 2.4 GeV, chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ = 1.1 GeV
- η' meson mass m_{η'} ≈ 681.6 MeV (for two flavors)

## Context

This work extends earlier instanton vacuum studies of gluonic operators in vacuum correlators (Schäfer, 1994; Kacir, 1996; Schäfer, 2004) and forward hadronic matrix elements (Diakonov, 1995) to non-forward matrix elements of a large class of gluonic operators, including contributions from instanton molecular clusters. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD calculations of nucleon gluonic form factors (Wang, 2024; Hackett, 2023) and provide a framework for interpreting semi-inclusive processes such as heavy meson production at threshold (Duran, 2022; Meziani, 2024), where gluonic matrix elements are notoriously difficult to measure experimentally due to the confining nature of QCD in the infrared.
