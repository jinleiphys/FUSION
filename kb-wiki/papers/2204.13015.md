# Refined nuclear magnetic dipole moment of rhenium: $^{185}$Re and $^{187}$Re

> The refined values of the magnetic dipole moments of $^{185}$Re and $^{187}$Re nuclei are obtained. For this, we perform a combined relativistic coupled cluster and density functional theory calculation of the shielding constant for the ReO$_4^-$ anion. In this calculation, we explicitly include the effect of the finite nuclear magnetization distribution in the single-particle nuclear model using the Woods-Saxon potential for the valence nucleon. By combining the obtained value of the shielding constant $σ=4069(389)$~ppm with the available experimental nuclear magnetic resonance data we obtain the values: $μ(^{185}{\rm Re})=3.1567(3)(12) μ_N, μ(^{187}{\rm Re})=3.1891(3)(12) μ_N$, where the first uncertainty is the experimental one and the second is due to theory. The refined values of magnetic moments are in disagreement with the tabulated values, $μ(^{185}{\rm Re})=3.1871(3) μ_N, μ(^{187}{\rm Re})=3.2197(3) μ_N$, which were obtained using the shielding constant value calculated for the atomic cation Re$^{7+}$ rather than the molecular anion. The updated values of the nuclear magnetic moments resolve the disagreement between theoretical predictions of the hyperfine structure of H-like rhenium ions which were based on the tabulated magnetic moment values and available experimental measurements. Using these experimental data we also extract the value of the parameter of nuclear magnetization distribution introduced in [J. Chem. Phys. \textbf{153}, 114114 (2020)], which is required to predict hyperfine structure constants for rhenium compounds.

## Key claim
The refined magnetic dipole moments of 185Re and 187Re, obtained by combining a new relativistic calculation of the shielding constant for the ReO4− anion with existing NMR data, disagree with the tabulated values that were based on a shielding constant calculated for the atomic cation Re7+ rather than the molecular anion.

## Method
The authors performed four-component relativistic coupled cluster (CCSD(T)) and density functional theory (PBE0) calculations of the NMR shielding constant for the ReO4− anion, explicitly including the finite nuclear magnetization distribution effect using the single-particle Woods-Saxon nuclear model. The shielding constant was combined with experimental NMR resonance frequencies from Alder (1951) to derive the intrinsic nuclear magnetic moments via the relation μ = μuncorr. / (1 − σ). The uncertainty budget accounted for electron correlation, basis set incompleteness, Gaunt interaction, solvent effects, finite magnetization distribution, geometry, and quantum electrodynamics contributions.

## Key numbers
- Shielding constant for ReO4−: σ = 4069(389) ppm.
- Refined magnetic moment of 185Re: μ = 3.1567(3)(12) μN (first uncertainty experimental, second theoretical).
- Refined magnetic moment of 187Re: μ = 3.1891(3)(12) μN (first uncertainty experimental, second theoretical).
- Tabulated (previous) values: μ(185Re) = 3.1871(3) μN, μ(187Re) = 3.2197(3) μN.
- Uncorrected NMR values: μuncorr.(185Re) = 3.1439(3) μN, μuncorr.(187Re) = 3.1761(3) μN.
- Finite nuclear magnetization distribution contribution to σ: −73 ppm (Woods-Saxon model) vs. −96 ppm (uniformly magnetized ball model).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Antusek (2020), who used nonrelativistic coupled cluster theory combined with relativistic DFT corrections, by employing a fully relativistic coupled cluster treatment that avoids the systematic error of non-additivity between correlation and relativistic effects. The refined moments resolve a disagreement between theoretical predictions of the hyperfine structure of H-like rhenium ions (Crespo Lopez-Urrutia, 1998) and experimental measurements, which were based on the older tabulated values. The results also enable extraction of the nuclear magnetization distribution parameter introduced in Prosnyak (2020) for predicting hyperfine structure constants in rhenium compounds.
