# Deep exclusive charged $ŽĆ$ electroproduction above the resonance region

> A description of exclusive charged pion electroproduction $(e,e'ŽĆ^{\pm})$ off nucleons at high energies is proposed. The model combines a Regge pole approach with residual effect of nucleon resonances. The exchanges of $ŽĆ$(140), vector $Žü(770)$ and axial-vector $a_1(1260)$ and $b_1(1235)$ Regge trajectories are considered. The contribution of nucleon resonances is described using a dual connection between the exclusive hadronic form factors and inclusive deep inelastic structure functions. The model describes the measured longitudinal, transverse and interference cross sections at JLAB and DESY. The scaling behavior of the cross sections is in agreement with JLAB and deeply virtual HERMES data. The results for a polarized beam-spin azimuthal asymmetry in $(\vec{e},e'ŽĆ^{\pm})$ are presented. Model predictions for JLAB at 12 GeV are given.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a model for exclusive charged pion electroproduction that combines Regge pole exchanges with a resonance/partonic background modeled via a Bloom-Gilman duality connection, claiming that this combined approach can describe the measured transverse, longitudinal, and interference cross sections across a wide kinematic range from JLAB to HERMES, though the transverse cross section remains the primary challenge that the resonance/partonic contribution is designed to address.

## Method

The model combines reggeized pion (ŽĆ), vector Žü(770), and axial-vector aŌéü(1260) and bŌéü(1235) exchanges in the t-channel with a gauge-invariant nucleon-pole term, where the nucleon transition form factors are modified by integrating over an infinite tower of nucleon resonances using a local Bloom-Gilman duality connection between exclusive form factors and inclusive deep inelastic structure functions. The resonance contributions are treated as a continuous integral over resonance masses with a power-law suppression factor s_i^{-╬▓} and a dipole form factor that hardens with increasing resonance mass, effectively replacing the discrete resonance sum by an effective form factor that depends on Q┬▓, s, and u.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Kaskulov and Mosel, 2008, 2009) where the transverse cross section was modeled directly via partonic string fragmentation, by now implementing the resonance/partonic contribution at the amplitude level to allow for interference effects with the meson-exchange amplitudes. The model addresses a long-standing discrepancy where meson-exchange and Regge pole models fail to reproduce the measured transverse and interference cross sections at JLAB (Horn et al., 2006, 2007; Blok et al., 2008), and provides a framework that can be used to minimize systematic uncertainties in the extraction of the pion charge form factor from longitudinal cross section data.
