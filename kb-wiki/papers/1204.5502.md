# $N\bar D $ system: A challenge for $\bar {\rm P}$ANDA

> We study the $N \bar D$ system by means of a chiral constituent quark model. This model, tuned in the description of the baryon and meson spectra as well as the $NN$ interaction, provides parameter-free predictions for the charm -1 two-hadron systems. The presence of a heavy antiquark makes the interaction rather simple. We have found sharp quark-Pauli effects in some particular channels, generating repulsion, due to lacking degrees of freedom to accommodate the light quarks. Our results point to the existence of an attractive state, the $Δ\bar D^*$ with $(T,S)=(1,5/2)$, presenting a resonance close to threshold. It would show up in the scattering of $\bar D$ mesons on nucleons as a D wave state with quantum numbers $(T)J^P=(1)5/2^-$. This resonance resembles our findings in the $ΔΔ$ system, that offered a plausible explanation to the cross section of double-pionic fusion reactions through the so-called {\it ABC} effect. The study of the interaction of $D$ mesons with nucleons is a goal of the $\bar {\rm P}$ANDA Collaboration at the European facility FAIR and, thus, the present theoretical study is a challenge to be tested at the future experiments.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model predicts a bound state in the $\Delta \bar D^*$ channel with quantum numbers $(T,J)=(1,5/2)$ and a binding energy of 3.87 MeV, which would appear as a $D$ wave resonance in $\bar D$-nucleon scattering; however, this prediction depends on the inclusion of quark-exchange effects, and purely hadronic models that neglect such effects would instead predict a bound state in the $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ channel.

## Method

The authors used a chiral constituent quark model previously tuned to describe the baryon and meson spectra and the $NN$ interaction, providing parameter-free predictions for the charm $-1$ two-hadron systems. They derived local baryon-meson interactions from the basic quark-quark interaction using a Born-Oppenheimer approximation, including one-gluon-exchange, one-sigma-exchange, one-pion-exchange, and confinement potentials. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for negative energies using the Fredholm determinant method, considering coupled channels in the isospin-spin $(T,J)$ basis with $S$ and $D$ wave couplings.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 3.87 MeV for the $\Delta \bar D^*$ state with $(T,J)=(1,5/2)$
- The $\Delta \bar D^*$ state with $(T,S)=(1,5/2)$ is predicted as a resonance close to threshold with quantum numbers $(T)J^P=(1)5/2^-$
- The $C(S,T)$ coefficient for the Pauli blocked $\Delta \bar D^*$ channel with $(T,S)=(2,5/2)$ equals exactly 1, generating strong repulsion
- The $C(S,T)$ coefficient for the Pauli suppressed $N \bar D^*$ channel with $(T,J)=(0,1/2)$ equals 2/3, generating repulsion at short distances
- The strong coupling constant $\alpha_s$ is approximately 0.54 for the light-quark sector and approximately 0.43 for $uc$ pairs
- Quark model parameters include: light quark mass $m_{u,d}=313$ MeV, charm quark mass $m_c=1752$ MeV, light quark gaussian parameter $b=0.518$ fm, charm quark gaussian parameter $b_c=0.6$ fm

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach previously applied to the $\Delta\Delta$ system by Valcarce et al. (2001), which predicted an $S$ wave resonance with maximum spin that was later used to explain the cross section of double-pionic fusion reactions through the ABC effect. The present prediction of a $\Delta \bar D^*$ resonance resembles those findings. The study contrasts with hadronic model calculations by Yasui and Yamaguchi (2009, 2011) using Lagrangians satisfying heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry, which found the $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ channel to be the most attractive with a bound state of around 1.4 MeV; the authors show that neglecting quark-exchange effects reproduces this result, emphasizing the importance of quark dynamics. The work provides predictions testable by the $\bar {\rm P}$ANDA Collaboration at the FAIR facility, which aims to study the interaction of $D$ mesons with nucleons.
