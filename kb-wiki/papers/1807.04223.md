# Viscous hydrodynamics for nonconformal anisotropic fluids

> A new formulation of (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics is presented that accounts nonperturbatively for the large longitudinal-transverse pressure anisotropy and bulk viscous pressure in heavy-ion collisions. The initialization of the anisotropic hydrodynamic stage is discussed, and a comparison to standard viscous hydrodynamics for (0+1)- dimensional Bjorken expansion is presented.

# Viscous hydrodynamics for nonconformal anisotropic fluids

## Key claim

The paper presents a new (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics formulation that treats the longitudinal and transverse pressures at leading order nonperturbatively, with only the smaller residual shear stresses treated perturbatively; this avoids the assumption that shear stress and bulk viscous pressure are small compared to thermal pressure, which is questionable at early times and near hadronization in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors decompose the energy-momentum tensor into leading-order anisotropic contributions (energy density, longitudinal pressure, transverse pressure) and smaller residual dissipative terms (longitudinal-momentum diffusion current and transverse shear stress tensor). They derive relaxation equations from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation for a system of weakly interacting quasiparticles with a medium-dependent mass, using a 14-moment approximation for the residual corrections and generalized Landau matching conditions to eliminate microscopic anisotropy parameters in favor of macroscopic hydrodynamic variables. The transport coefficients are calculated within the quasiparticle kinetic model as functions of the microscopic parameters obtained from energy density, longitudinal pressure, and transverse pressure.

## Key numbers

- The bulk relaxation time in the quasiparticle model differs by an order of magnitude around the critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 154\) MeV compared to the small mass limit
- Initial conditions for the Bjorken evolution comparison: initial temperature \(T_0 = 0.5\) GeV at \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/\(c\)
- For the initialization study, the pressure ratio \(\PL / \Pperp\) approaches 0.08 before the root-finding algorithm breaks down because longitudinal kinetic pressure becomes negative
- The pressure anisotropy converges onto a hydrodynamic attractor after about 1 fm/\(c\) of evolution
- For the comparison to standard viscous hydrodynamics, initial pressure ratio \((\PL/\Pperp)_0 = 0.01\) at \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/\(c\) with \(T_0 = 0.5\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework of Molnar (2016) and Tinti (2016) by providing a fully macroscopic formulation with transport coefficients computed from a quasiparticle kinetic model tuned to the lattice QCD equation of state. The approach contrasts with standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics codes such as iEBE-VISHNU (Shen, 2014) and MUSIC (Denicol, 2018), which require regulation of large shear stresses for numerical stability; the new formulation avoids this by evolving the pressure anisotropy nonperturbatively at leading order. The results enable future Bayesian statistical constraints on the temperature dependence of the specific bulk viscosity \((\zeta/s)(T)\), potentially requiring promotion of the bulk relaxation time to a phenomenological function of temperature.
