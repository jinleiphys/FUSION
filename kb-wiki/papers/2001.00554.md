# Electromagnetic character of the competitive $γγ/γ$-decay from $^{137\mathrm{m}}$Ba

> Second-order processes in physics is a research topic focusing attention from several fields worldwide including, for example, non-linear quantum electrodynamics with high-power lasers, neutrinoless double-$β$ decay, and stimulated atomic two-photon transitions. For the electromagnetic nuclear interaction, the observation of the competitive double-$γ$ decay from $^{137\mathrm{m}}$Ba has opened up the nuclear structure field for detailed investigation of second-order processes through the manifestation of off-diagonal nuclear polarizability. Here we confirm this observation with an $8.7σ$ significance, and an improved value on the double-photon versus single-photon branching ratio as $2.62\times10^{-6}(30)$. Our results, however, contradict the conclusions from the original experiment, where the decay was interpreted to be dominated by a quadrupole-quadrupole component. Here, we find a substantial enhancement in the energy distribution consistent with a dominating octupole-dipole character and a rather small quadrupole-quadrupole element in the decay, hindered due to an evolution of the internal nuclear structure. The implied strongly hindered double-photon branching in $^{137\mathrm{m}}$Ba opens up the possibility of the double-photon branching as a feasible tool for nuclear-structure studies on off-diagonal polarizability in nuclei where this hindrance is not present.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the existence of competitive double-gamma decay from $^{137\mathrm{m}}$Ba with an $8.7\sigma$ significance, but contradicts the original experiment by Walz et al. (Nature, 2016) by finding that the decay is dominated by an octupole-dipole (E3M1) character rather than the previously claimed quadrupole-quadrupole (M2E2) component.

## Method

The experiment used eleven $3\times3$ inch CeBr$_3$ detectors from the ELIGANT system at ELI-NP, arranged in a circular configuration with 40 cm distance to the source and lead shielding between detectors. A $^{137}$Cs source with 336 kBq activity was measured for 49.5 days of active data taking, and coincident gamma-ray events were selected using a time difference condition of $\Delta t_{1,2} \leq 655$ ps. The angular distribution and energy-sharing distributions of the two emitted photons were fitted to extract the off-diagonal polarizability coefficients $\alpha_{\mathrm{M2E2}}$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{E3M1}}$, with theoretical calculations performed using the EDF+QPM and Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) approaches.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio of double-photon versus single-photon decay: $2.62\times10^{-6}(30)$
- Statistical significance of the double-gamma decay observation: $8.7\sigma$
- Extracted off-diagonal polarizability $\alpha_{\mathrm{E3M1}} = \pm36.4(20)$ $e^2$fm$^4$/MeV (dominating component)
- Extracted off-diagonal polarizability $\alpha_{\mathrm{M2E2}} = \pm8.8(50)$ $e^2$fm$^4$/MeV (small component)
- Energy integration limits for branching ratio extraction: $E_1=180$ keV and $E_2=331$ keV
- Time condition for prompt coincidences: $\Delta t_{1,2} \leq 655$ ps
- Source activity: 336 kBq $^{137}$Cs
- Data collection period: 49.5 days

## Context

This work independently confirms the competitive double-gamma decay first reported by Walz et al. (Nature, 2016) but contradicts their conclusion that the decay is dominated by a quadrupole-quadrupole (M2E2) component. The finding of a dominant octupole-dipole (E3M1) character, supported by both EDF+QPM and MCSM calculations, suggests that the quadrupole-quadrupole component is strongly hindered due to the evolution of internal nuclear structure in $^{137}$Ba. This opens the possibility that double-photon branching could be significantly larger in other nuclei without this hindrance, making it a feasible tool for nuclear-structure studies of off-diagonal polarizability, potentially enabling experiments with more exotic sources or in-beam measurements.
