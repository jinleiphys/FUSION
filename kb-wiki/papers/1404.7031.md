# Nucleon effective masses within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory: Impact on stellar neutrino emission

> We calculate the effective masses of neutrons and protons in dense nuclear matter within the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory and study the impact on the neutrino emissivity processes of neutron stars. We compare results based on different nucleon-nucleon potentials and nuclear three-body forces. Useful parametrizations of the numerical results are given. We find substantial in-medium suppression of the emissivities, strongly dependent on the interactions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nucleon effective masses in dense nuclear matter using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach and finds that the resulting in-medium suppression of neutrino emissivities is substantial but strongly dependent on the choice of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and three-body forces (TBF), reflecting the current theoretical uncertainty regarding TBF at high density.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the reaction matrix G within the BHF approximation using the continuous choice for the single-particle potential, employing four combinations of NN potentials (Argonne V18, CD-Bonn) and TBF (microscopic, phenomenological Urbana model). They computed neutron and proton effective masses at the Fermi surface for asymmetric nuclear matter at various densities and proton fractions, then used these masses to evaluate the reduction factors M_ij for the direct Urca, modified Urca, and NN bremsstrahlung neutrino emission processes in beta-stable neutron star matter.

- Modified Urca proton branch emissivity: Q^(Mp) ≈ 8.1 × 10^21 M_13 T_9^8 α_p β_p (1 - k_Fe/4k_Fp) Θ_Mp erg cm^-3 s^-1, with α_p = 1.13 and β_p = 0.68.
- NN bremsstrahlung emissivities: Q^(Bnn) ≈ 2.3 × 10^20 M_40 T_9^8 α_nn β_nn (ρ_n/ρ_p)^(1/3) erg cm^-3 s^-1, with α_nn = 0.59 and β_nn = 0.56; Q^(Bnp) ≈ 4.5 × 10^20 M_22 T_9^8 α_np β_np erg cm^-3 s^-1, with α_np = 1.06 and β_np = 0.66; Q^(Bpp) ≈ 2.3 × 10^20 M_04 T_9^8 α_pp β_pp erg cm^-3 s^-1, with α_pp = 0.11 and β_pp = 0.70.

## Context

This work extends previous BHF calculations of nuclear matter effective masses and neutrino emissivities by systematically comparing results from different NN potentials (Argonne V18, CD-Bonn) and TBF models (microscopic, Urbana). The authors note that without TBF the effective masses decrease with increasing density, while TBF inclusion causes an increase above 0.3-0.4 fm^-3 due to repulsive TBF effects. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutron star cooling by providing parametrized effective masses and in-medium correction factors, though the strong interaction dependence highlights the need for consistent calculations with given sets of two-body and three-body interactions, as emphasized in prior work by Song (1998), Satu (2001), and Polls (2001).
