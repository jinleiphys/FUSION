# Core swelling in spherical nuclei: An indication of the saturation of nuclear density

> Background: Nuclear radius is one of the most important and basic properties of atomic nuclei and its evolution is closely related to the saturation of the nuclear density in the internal region but the systematics of the nuclear radii for the neutron-rich unstable nuclei is not well known. Purpose: Motivated by the recent interaction cross section measurement which indicates the 48Ca core swelling in the neutron-rich Ca isotopes, we explore the mechanism of the enhancement of the neutron and proton radii for spherical nuclei. Methods: Microscopic Hartree-Fock calculations with three sets of Skyrme-type effective interactions are performed for the neutron-rich Ca, Ni and Sn isotopes. The total reaction cross sections for the Ca isotopes are evaluated with the Glauber model to compare them with the recent cross section data. Results: We obtain good agreement with the measured cross sections and charge radii. The neutron and proton radii of the various "core" configurations are extracted from the full Hartree-Fock calculation and discuss the core swelling mechanism. Conclusions: The core swelling phenomena occur depending on the properties of the occupying valence single-neutron states to minimize the energy loss that comes from the saturation of the densities in the internal region, which appears to be prominent in light nuclei such as Ca isotopes.

## Key claim

Core swelling in spherical nuclei occurs when valence neutrons occupy nodal or j-lower orbits, causing a rearrangement of the mean field to reduce central density and minimize energy loss from saturation, and this effect is prominent in light-medium mass nuclei such as Ca isotopes.

## Method

Microscopic Hartree-Fock calculations with three Skyrme-type effective interactions (SkM*, SLy4, and SkI3) were performed for neutron-rich Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes. Total reaction cross sections for Ca isotopes on a 12C target at 280 MeV/nucleon were evaluated using the Glauber model with the nucleon-target formalism. The neutron and proton radii of various "core" configurations were extracted from the full Hartree-Fock calculations by isolating single-particle orbits corresponding to closed shell or subshell neutron configurations.

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross sections of 42-51Ca on a 12C target at 280 MeV/nucleon were calculated and compared with experimental interaction cross section data from Tanaka (2020)
- The rms point-proton radius of 12C used for the target density was 2.33 fm, extracted from charge radius data from Angeli (2013)
- Central densities of the 48Ca or 56Ni core had to be reduced to keep the saturation density around 0.16-0.17 fm^-3 when adding neutrons up to N=40
- In some isotopes (e.g., N=58 for Ni, N=70 and N=84 for Sn), central densities of the core became approximately 0.15 fm^-3
- The heaviest Ca isotope found experimentally is 60Ca (Tarasov 2018), with theoretical predictions placing the neutron dripline near 60Ca (Hagen 2012), possibly at 70Ca (Newfcourt 2019), or near 64Ca (Tagami 2020)

## Context

This work extends the recent experimental finding of 48Ca core swelling in neutron-rich Ca isotopes from Tanaka (2020) by providing a theoretical mechanism based on Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations. The core swelling phenomenon was previously discussed for O isotopes using 16O plus few-neutron models by Masui (2012). The results help formulate "core" plus few-nucleon models for describing light-medium mass dripline nuclei such as 22C and 29,31F, and suggest that systematic measurements of total reaction cross sections on a proton target would be the most promising way to investigate core swelling in short-lived neutron-rich nuclei.
