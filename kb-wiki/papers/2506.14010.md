# Enhancement of axial anomaly effects in hot two-color QCD: FRG approach in the linear sigma model

> We investigate the thermal properties of hadrons in two-color quantum chromodynamics (QC$_2$D) using the functional renormalization group (FRG) method, with particular focus on modifications of the $U(1)$ axial anomaly effects. The hadrons are described by a linear sigma model (LSM) based on the Pauli-GĂĽrsey $SU(4)$ symmetry, which incorporates both low-lying $0^\pm$ mesons and diquark baryons. We find that all quartic couplings are comparably suppressed when physical values of the pion mass and decay constant are taken as inputs, for which a reasonably smooth chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature is reproduced. Consequently, mass differences among chiral partners remain small. Despite these tiny mass differences, mass degeneracies of chiral partners in the hot medium are clearly demonstrated, consistent with chiral symmetry restoration. Moreover, we find that the couplings responsible for the $U(1)$ axial anomaly are enhanced upon entering the finite temperature regime. Baryonic fluctuations also provide sizable contributions to these enhancements. Finally, the fate of the topological susceptibility in the hot QC$_2$D medium is examined.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the couplings responsible for the $U(1)_A$ axial anomaly are enhanced at finite temperature in two-color QCD, contrary to the expectation that anomaly effects disappear at high temperature due to Debye screening; this enhancement occurs despite the fact that all quartic couplings are comparably suppressed and mass differences among chiral partners remain small.

## Method

The authors apply the functional renormalization group (FRG) method within the local potential approximation to a linear sigma model of two-color QCD with two flavors, based on the Pauli-Gursey SU(4) symmetry. The model incorporates both low-lying mesons (sigma, pions, eta, a0) and diquark baryons (B, Bbar, B', Bbar') in a unified 12-hadron framework, and the flow equations for the scale-dependent couplings are derived by expanding the Wetterich equation up to quartic order in the fields. The initial conditions at the UV scale Lambda_UV = 1 GeV are fixed by fitting the vacuum pion mass to 0.14 GeV and the pion decay constant to 0.093 GeV, while the eta mass is treated as a free parameter taking values 0.3, 0.5, and 0.95 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass in vacuum: 0.14 GeV (fixed as input)
- Pion decay constant in vacuum: 0.093 GeV (fixed as input, equal to the vacuum expectation value sigma_0)
- UV cutoff scale: Lambda_UV = 1 GeV
- Three scenarios for vacuum eta mass: 0.3, 0.5, and 0.95 GeV
- Quartic coupling lambda_2 at UV scale: 5 (chosen to produce reasonable chiral condensate reduction)
- Quartic coupling lambda_1 at UV scale: 1.25 (set by large N_c ansatz lambda_2 = 4 lambda_1)
- Quadratic coupling m^2 at UV scale: -0.0426 GeV^2 (for M_eta = 0.3 GeV), 0.0465 GeV^2 (for M_eta = 0.5 GeV), 0.385 GeV^2 (for M_eta = 0.95 GeV)
- Axial anomaly coupling a at UV scale: -0.032 GeV (for M_eta = 0.3 GeV), -0.108 GeV (for M_eta = 0.5 GeV), -0.428 GeV (for M_eta = 0.95 GeV)
- Anomalous quartic couplings c_1 and c_2 at UV scale: 0 (large N_c ansatz)

## Context

This work extends the FRG-based analysis of U(1)_A anomaly enhancement previously found in three-color QCD with N_f=2+1 flavors by Fejos (2016, 2021) to the two-color case, in order to test whether the anomaly enhancement is universal to the number of colors. The study establishes a framework for describing thermodynamics of two-color QCD with both mesonic and baryonic degrees of freedom using FRG, which had not been attempted before. The results provide predictions that can be tested by future lattice simulations of two-color QCD, which are currently being performed by groups such as Boz (2019), Buividovich (2020), Astrakhantsev (2020), Iida (2024), and Braguta (2023), and the authors note that current lattice simulations use a heavy pion mass around 0.7 GeV, making their lighter-pion predictions a benchmark for future computations.
