# Equation of state and transition temperatures in the quark-hadron hybrid model

> We analyze the equation of state of 2+1 flavor lattice QCD at zero baryon density by constructing the simple quark-hadron hybrid model that has both quark and hadron components simultaneously. We calculate hadron and quark contribution separately and parameterizing those to match with LQCD data. Lattice data on the equation of state are decomposed into hadron and quark components by using the model. The transition temperature is defined by the temperature at which the hadron component is equal to the quark one in the equation of state. The transition temperature thus obtained is about 215 MeV and somewhat higher than the chiral and the deconfinement pseudocritical temperatures defined by the temperature at which the susceptibility or the absolute value of the derivative of the order parameter with respect to temperature becomes maximum.

## Key claim

The transition temperature defined by the temperature at which the hadron component equals the quark component in the equation of state is about 215 MeV, which is somewhat higher than both the chiral and the deconfinement (Z3) pseudocritical temperatures defined by the maximum of susceptibility or derivative of the order parameter.

## Method

The authors constructed a quark-hadron hybrid model that simultaneously includes both quark and hadron components, using the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model for the hadronic phase and a Polyakov-loop quark model for the quark phase. They determined the hadron volume fraction function f_H by fitting to 2+1 flavor lattice QCD data on entropy density, baryon number susceptibility, and isospin number susceptibility at zero baryon density. The transition temperature was then defined as the temperature where the hadron and quark contributions to the entropy density are equal.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature T_c^(s) = 215 MeV, defined as the crosspoint of hadron and quark entropy density contributions at zero baryon and isospin chemical potential
- Transition temperature defined by equal pressures (P_Q = P_H) yields T_c^(P) = 249 MeV, larger than T_c^(s)
- The phenomenological cutoff parameter Lambda_T = 1.95 GeV, determined to reproduce the entropy density at T = 300 MeV from lattice QCD
- Parameters of the hadron volume fraction function: a = 27.0326 MeV, b = 205.458 MeV, c = 174.154 MeV, d = 17
- Parameters for the derivative of f_H with respect to nu_B: a_B = -3.74513 MeV^-2, b_B = 192 MeV, c_B = 30.6622 MeV
- Parameters for the derivative of f_H with respect to nu_I: a_I = -5.2969 MeV^-2, b_I = 181.929 MeV, c_I = 36.3811 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron hybrid model approach of Hatsuda (1987) to analyze 2+1 flavor lattice QCD data from Borsanyi et al. (2010, 2012). The authors note that previous PNJL-type models (such as the entanglement PNJL model by Sakai et al. and nonlocal PNJL model by Kashiwa et al.) successfully reproduce two-flavor lattice data where chiral and Z3 transitions occur nearly simultaneously, but struggle with 2+1 flavor data where the chiral transition temperature is considerably lower than the Z3 transition temperature. The hybrid model enables decomposition of the equation of state into hadron and quark components, providing a new definition of the quark-hadron transition temperature distinct from the chiral and Z3 pseudocritical temperatures.
