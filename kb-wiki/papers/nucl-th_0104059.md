# One and Two-photon Physics with Relativistic Heavy Ions

## Key claim
Peripheral collisions of relativistic heavy ions (PCRHI) provide a clean probe of one- and two-photon processes, but the extraction of electromagnetic matrix elements from data is complicated by non-perturbative effects (e.g., post-acceleration in Coulomb breakup) and by the need to separate nuclear-induced contributions, which are model-dependent.

## Method
The paper reviews the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the cross section for one-photon processes is given by an integral over the equivalent photon number n(ω) times the photon-induced cross section, and for two-photon processes by a double integral over n(ω₁) and n(ω₂) times the two-photon cross section. Specific calculations are presented for Bremsstrahlung, Delbrück scattering, atomic ionization, free and bound-free electron-positron pair production, relativistic Coulomb excitation and fragmentation, and meson/hadron production, with comparisons to experimental data from GSI/Darmstadt and RHIC/BNL.

## Key numbers
- For Bremsstrahlung at a collider: differential cross section dσ_γ/dω = 16Z⁶α³(3ωA²m_N²)⁻¹ ln(γ/ωR), with γ_c ~ 100 for RHIC/BNL and R ~ 2.4 × A^(1/3) fm.
- Delbrück scattering cross section at LHC: about 50 b, with total cross section σ = 2.54 Z⁴ α⁴ r_e² ln³(γ/m_e R) for ω ≫ m_e.
- Atomic ionization cross section for naked projectiles on fixed target: σ_I = Z_P² r_e² (Z_T α)⁻² [1.8π + 9.8 ln(2γ/Z_T α)], with numerical factors corrected by Baltz (2001) to 1.74–1.83 and 7.21.
- Pair production with electron capture cross section: σ = 3.3π Z⁸ α⁶ r_e² [exp(2πZα) – 1]⁻¹ [ln(0.681γ/2) – 5/3].
- Para-positronium production cross section at RHIC: about 18 mb.
- Coulomb excitation cross section for 1-phonon GDR: σ₁ ~ 2πS ln[2γ A_T^(1/3)(A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3))⁻¹], with S = 5.45 × 10⁻⁴ Z_P² Z_T N_T A_T⁻²/³ mb; experimental cross sections for DGDR are about 30% larger than theoretical predictions.
- Mutual Coulomb excitation cross section at RHIC: about 3 b, measured by Sebastian White and collaborators using the Zero Degree Calorimeter.
- Higgs boson production via γγ fusion at LHC: cross section of order 1 nanobarn.

## Context
This review extends the foundational work on the equivalent photon approximation by Fermi (1924) and Bertulani and Baur (1985, 1988, 1994), and summarizes experimental results from GSI/Darmstadt (Iwasa et al., 1999; Krämer et al., 1998) and RHIC/BNL (White et al., 2001). It enables future studies of nuclear structure via relativistic Coulomb excitation of exotic nuclei, beam monitoring in colliders, and searches for rare processes such as Delbrück scattering and Higgs boson production via two-photon fusion. The paper also highlights open questions, including the proper treatment of Coulomb distortion in pair production, the role of screening in atomic ionization and capture, and the need for non-perturbative calculations in strongly interacting systems.
