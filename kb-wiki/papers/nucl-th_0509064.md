# 3D Jet Tomography of the Twisted Color Glass Condensate

> Jet Tomography is proposed as a new test of Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial conditions in non-central $A+A$ collisions. The $k_{T}$ factorized CGC formalism is used to calculate the rapidity twist in the reaction plane of both the bulk low $p_T< 2$ GeV matter as well as the rare high $p_T> 6$ GeV partons. Unlike conventional perturbative QCD, the initial high $p_{T}$ CGC gluons are shown to be twisted even further away from the beam axis than the the low $p_T$ bulk at high rapidities $|η|>2$. Differential directed flow $v_{1}(p_{T}>6,|η|>2)$ is proposed to test this novel high $p_T$ rapidity twist predicted by the CGC model.

## Key claim

Jet tomography of non-central A+A collisions can distinguish between Color Glass Condensate (CGC) and conventional perturbative QCD initial conditions because the CGC model predicts that high-pT (above 6 GeV) gluons exhibit a greater rapidity twist away from the beam axis than the low-pT bulk matter at high rapidities (|η|>2), opposite to the expectation from collinear factorized pQCD where high-pT jets remain untwisted.

## Method

The authors used the kT-factorized Gribov-Levin-Ryskin (GLR) formalism with a Lorentzian parameterization of unintegrated gluon distributions to compute the transverse coordinate dependence of produced gluons as a function of pT and rapidity η for non-central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=9 fm. They calculated the average transverse spatial coordinate ⟨x⟩(pT,η) to quantify the rapidity twist, then computed the nuclear modification factor RAA(pT,η,φ) using a geometric model of jet attenuation through the bulk medium, extracting the directed flow v1 as the first azimuthal Fourier moment.

## Key numbers

- Bulk matter defined as pT < 2 GeV; high-pT partons defined as pT > 6 GeV
- Saturation scale ⟨Qs²(x=0.01)⟩ ≈ 2 GeV² for central collisions at midrapidity
- Parameter κ ≈ 0.5 set to reproduce dNg/dη ≈ 1000 at midrapidity in central collisions
- Parameter μ = 0.04 set to give RAA(η=0,b=0) ≈ 0.25
- Impact parameter b = 9 fm used for all non-central calculations
- Momentum scale Λ = 0.2 GeV used as regulator for high rapidity y > 4.5
- QCD coupling αs regulated with maximum value αmax = 0.5
- λ = 0.2 and n = 4 used in nucleonic gluon distribution xG_nuc(x,Q²)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of rapidity twist in non-central collisions from Adil and Gyulassy (2005) to the KLN/CGC implementation of initial conditions, building on the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) model (Kharzeev, 2002) and the Hirano and Nara (2004) observation that CGC sharpens the bulk surface geometry. The predicted sign change of v1 at high pT provides a test to distinguish CGC initial conditions from conventional collinear factorized pQCD and from hydrodynamic models, and enables experimental searches for long-range rapidity anticorrelations of directed flow at RHIC.
