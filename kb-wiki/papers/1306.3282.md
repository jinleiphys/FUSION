# A Pseudo su(1,1)-Algebraic Deformation of the Cooper-Pair in the su(2)-Algebraic Many-Fermion Model

> A pseudo su(1,1)-algebra is formulated as a possible deformation of the Cooper-pair in the su(2)-algebraic many-fermion system. With the aid of this algebra, it is possible to describe behavior of individual fermions which are generated as the result of interaction with the external environment. The form presented in this paper is a generalization of a certain simple case developed recently by the present authors. Basic idea follows the su(1,1)-algebra in the Schwinger boson representation for treating energy transfer between the harmonic oscillator and the external environment. Hamiltonian is given under the idea of the phase space doubling in the thermo-field dynamics formalism and the time-dependent variational method is applied to this Hamiltonian. Its trial state is constructed in the frame deformed from the BCS-Bogoliubov approach to the superconductivity. Several numerical results are shown.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalization of a previously developed pseudo su(1,1)-algebraic deformation of the Cooper-pair, extending it from the zero-seniority case (t=1/2) to nonzero seniority (t>1/2), enabling the description of individual fermion behavior arising from interaction with an external environment within the su(2)-algebraic many-fermion framework.

## Method

The authors construct a pseudo su(1,1)-algebra by deforming the su(2)-algebra generators of the many-fermion model, introducing operators that act as deformed Cooper-pair creation and annihilation operators. They apply the time-dependent variational method to a Hamiltonian formulated under the phase-space doubling idea of thermo-field dynamics, using a trial state constructed as an exponential of the deformed Cooper-pair creation operator acting on the minimum weight state. The normalization constant and expectation values are computed as polynomials in |z|^2, and approximate expressions are derived for two separate regions of the parameter x = |z|^2.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle states are confined in 4Ω₀ states, where Ω₀ denotes integer or half-integer.
- The su(2)-algebra quantum numbers obey s = 0, 1/2, 1, ..., Ω₀ and s₀ = -s, -s+1, ..., s-1, s.
- The su(1,1)-algebra quantum numbers obey t = 1/2, 1, 3/2, ..., ∞ and t₀ = t, t+1, t+2, ..., ∞.
- The relation between the two algebras is given by t = t' = Ω₀ + 1/2 - s, with t₀ = Ω₀ + 1/2 + s₀ and t_m = Ω₀ + 1/2 + s.
- The normalization constant Γ_t(x) = Σ_{n=0}^{2Ω₀-(2t-1)} x^n C(2t-1+n, 2t-1) = 1 + 2tx + ... for 0 ≤ x < ∞.
- The degree of the polynomial Γ_t(x) is 2Ω₀ - (2t-1), and the number of states in the maximum-weight subspace is 2s+1 = t_m - t + 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development (Tsue et al., 2013, referred to as (A)) which treated the pseudo su(1,1)-algebra only for the case t=1/2 (s=Ω₀, zero seniority). The formalism generalizes the BCS-Bogoliubov approach to superconductivity by introducing a deformed Cooper-pair operator, and follows the su(1,1)-algebra in the Schwinger boson representation (Schwinger, 1965) used for describing energy transfer between a harmonic oscillator and an external environment. The approach enables future investigation of time-evolution in many-fermion systems where the Pauli principle produces cyclic behavior, in contrast to the infinite boson-number su(1,1) model which shows no cyclic behavior.
