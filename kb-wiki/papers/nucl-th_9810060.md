# Scaling properties of the longitudinal and transversal asymmetries of the $\vec{n}\vec{d}$ total cross section

> The longitudinal and transversal asymmetries of the total $\vec{n}\vec{d}$ cross section are calculated. Four modern nucleon-nucleon interactions: AV18, CD Bonn, NijmI and NijmII, give different predictions for these observables. When the three-nucleon Hamiltonian is supplemented by the $2π$-exchange Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force (3NF), individually adjusted with each particular NN potential to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy, all predictions practically coincide. We propose to check this scaling behavior experimentally in order to get a clear signal for 3NF effects in the low energy three-nucleon continuum. Connected to that is the proposal to measure the energy at which the longitudinal asymmetry goes through zero. This energy is shifted by about 400 keV when 3NF's are acting.

# Scaling properties of the longitudinal and transversal asymmetries of the $\vec{n}\vec{d}$ total cross section

## Key claim

When the three-nucleon Hamiltonian is supplemented by the $2\pi$-exchange Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force (3NF), individually adjusted with each particular NN potential to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy, the predictions from four modern nucleon-nucleon interactions (AV18, CD Bonn, NijmI, NijmII) for the longitudinal and transversal asymmetries of the total $\vec{n}\vec{d}$ cross section practically coincide, whereas without the 3NF they scatter within 5-10%. The energy at which the longitudinal asymmetry crosses zero is shifted by about 400 keV when 3NFs are included.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev-type integral equations for the elastic neutron-deuteron scattering amplitude, including the nucleon exchange term, the direct action of the TM 3NF, and multiple scattering contributions through a $\tilde T$ operator. Calculations were performed at neutron energies from 0.6 MeV to 65 MeV using four modern NN potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, NijmI, NijmII) alone and then supplemented by the TM 3NF with the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ adjusted individually for each NN potential to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy. All partial wave states with total two-nucleon angular momenta up to $j_{max}=3$ were included.

## Key numbers

- The predictions for $\Delta\sigma_L$ and $\Delta\sigma_T$ obtained with NN interactions only scatter within a range of approximately 5-10% depending on energy.
- Including the 3NF reduces the spread of $\Delta\sigma_L$ and $\Delta\sigma_T$ values to within a range of approximately 1-2% at incoming neutron energies smaller than approximately 20 MeV.
- The scaled values of $\Delta\sigma_L$ and $\Delta\sigma_T$ with 3NF differ by about 5% from the averaged predictions of NN potentials only.
- At 12 MeV, the pure 2N force predictions for $\Delta\sigma_L$ relative to the averaged 2N+3NF prediction are -0.84 (AV18), -0.48 (CD Bonn), -0.87 (NijmI), and -0.84 (NijmII).
- The pure 2N force predictions give the zero-crossing energy $E_n^{cross}(2N) \approx 11.8$ MeV for $\Delta\sigma_L$.
- With the 3NF included, the zero-crossing energy shifts to $E_n^{cross}(2N+3NF) \approx 12.2$ MeV, a shift of about 400 keV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-nucleon forces in the continuum, following the finding that modern NN interactions underbind $^3H$ by about 500-800 keV (Nogga, 1997) and that large discrepancies exist in elastic Nd scattering cross section minima above approximately 60 MeV (Witała, 1998). The authors propose the longitudinal and transversal asymmetries as new observables for detecting 3NF effects in the low-energy three-nucleon continuum, complementing the vector analyzing power $A_y$ in elastic Nd scattering where 3NF models have shown insignificant effects (Witała, 1998; Tornow, 1998). The results enable experimental tests of present-day 3NF models through measurements of these asymmetries and the zero-crossing energy shift.
