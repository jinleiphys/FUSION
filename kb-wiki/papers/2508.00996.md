# Re-optimization of a deep neural network model for electron-carbon scattering using new experimental data

> We present an updated deep neural network model for inclusive electron-carbon scattering. Using the bootstrap model [Phys.Rev.C 110 (2024) 2, 025501] as a prior, we incorporate recent experimental data, as well as older measurements in the deep inelastic scattering region, to derive a re-optimized posterior model. We examine the impact of these new inputs on model predictions and associated uncertainties. Finally, we evaluate the resulting cross-section predictions in the kinematic range relevant to the Hyper-Kamiokande and DUNE experiments.

## Key claim

The re-optimized deep neural network model for inclusive electron-carbon scattering, using the bootstrap model of Kowal (2024) as a prior and incorporating new data from Mihovilovic (2024) and Gomez (1993), achieves improved agreement with these measurements but still shows a comparatively larger chi-squared per point (1.8) for the Mihovilovic training dataset, indicating limited flexibility in that narrow kinematic region; the model does not overfit, as the test chi-squared per point is lower (0.4).

## Method

The authors re-optimized an ensemble of 50 deep neural networks, each with 10 blocks of 300 hidden units and batch normalization, using the bootstrap approach. They incorporated two new datasets, Mihovilovic (2024) at Q² ≈ 0.8 GeV² and Gomez (1993) in the deep inelastic scattering region, alongside 11 older datasets, splitting the new data into training and test sets (9:1 ratio for Mihovilovic, all points for Gomez in training). Each network was re-optimized from prior weights for fewer than 1000 epochs using the AdamW algorithm, minimizing a loss function that includes chi-squared terms and normalization parameters for each dataset.

## Key numbers

- The Mihovilovic (2024) dataset contributes 54 data points with a 2% normalization uncertainty; the posterior model yields a training chi-squared per point of 1.799 and a test chi-squared per point of 0.399.
- The Gomez (1993) dataset contributes 7 data points with a 0.6% normalization uncertainty; the posterior model yields a training chi-squared per point of 9.364.
- For the prior model, the Mihovilovic training chi-squared per point was 17.711 and the test chi-squared per point was 7.956; for the Gomez dataset, the prior training chi-squared per point was 393.384.
- At electron energy E = 0.6 GeV (Hyper-Kamiokande peak), the lowest predictive uncertainty within the 68% event contour is about 7.8% at θ = 35°; uncertainties exceed 100% at scattering angles above 100°.
- At electron energy E = 2.5 GeV (DUNE peak), the lowest predictive uncertainty within the 68% event contour is about 6.5% at θ = 35°; uncertainties are of order 10% for energy transfers between 0.5 and 1.5 GeV and scattering angles between 15° and 30°.

## Context

This work extends the data-driven deep neural network model for electron-carbon scattering from Kowal (2023) and Graczyk (2024) by incorporating new experimental data from Mihovilovic (2024) and Gomez (1993), improving constraints in the quasi-elastic and deep inelastic scattering regions. The model is entirely data-driven, in contrast to approaches that extract longitudinal and transverse components using theoretical constraints, such as those by Christy (2007) and Bosted (2007), or the superscaling approach of Donnelly (1999). The updated model enables quantitative uncertainty maps for electron-carbon cross sections in kinematic ranges relevant to Hyper-Kamiokande and DUNE, highlighting that current electron-scattering data constrain nuclear models at the 10–20% level, while neutrino experiments require few-percent precision.
