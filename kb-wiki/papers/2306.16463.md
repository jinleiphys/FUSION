# Floquet insulators and lattice fermions

> Floquet insulators are periodically driven quantum systems that can host novel topological phases as a function of the drive parameters. These new phases exhibit features reminiscent of fermion doubling in discrete-time lattice fermion theories. We make this suggestion concrete by mapping the spectrum of a noninteracting (1+1)D Floquet insulator for certain drive parameters onto that of a discrete-time lattice fermion theory with a time-independent Hamiltonian. The resulting Hamiltonian is distinct from the Floquet Hamiltonian that generates stroboscopic dynamics. It can take the form of a discrete-time Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model with half the number of spatial sites of the original model, or of a (1+1)D Wilson-Dirac theory with one quarter of the spatial sites.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quasienergy spectrum of a specific (1+1)D noninteracting Floquet insulator model can be mapped directly onto the spectrum of a discrete-time lattice fermion theory with a time-independent Hamiltonian, but only along the line where the drive parameter satisfies t0/T = pi/4, which ensures a pi-pairing condition in the single-particle spectrum.

## Method

The authors consider a continuous-time (1+1)D Floquet model of spinless complex fermions on a spatial lattice of 2N sites, with a two-step driving protocol using Hamiltonians H0 and H1 that are the trivial and topological parts of the static Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) model. They analytically compute the quasienergy spectrum of the Floquet operator UF = exp(-iH1t1)exp(-iH0t0) and show that along the line t0/T = pi/4, the spectrum exhibits pi-pairing (every quasienergy epsilon has a partner at pi/T - epsilon). They then partition the quasienergy spectrum and identify a time-independent Hamiltonian whose energy spectrum E satisfies E = (1/T) sin(T epsilon_tilde), matching the pole structure of a discrete-time lattice fermion theory with temporal lattice spacing tau = T.

## Key numbers

* The Floquet model is defined on a spatial lattice of 2N sites, with driving period T = t0 + t1.
* The quasienergy spectrum along the line t0/T = pi/4 is given by epsilon(k) = +/- cos^{-1}[-cos(2eta)cos(2k)], where eta = t1/T - pi/4 measures distance from the gap closure at t1/T = pi/4.
* The SSH mapping yields parameters u = [1 +/- sin(2eta)]/(2T) and v = 1/T - u, with the SSH model defined on N sites (half the original lattice size).
* The Wilson-Dirac mapping yields parameters m = +/- sin(2eta)/T and R = 1/(2T) - m/2, with the Wilson-Dirac model defined on N/2 sites (one quarter of the original lattice size).
* Finite-size scaling shows the maximum difference between discrete-time spectra and quasienergy spectra scales as approximately 1/N for system sizes N = 100 to 900.

## Context

This work extends the connection between Floquet systems and lattice fermion doubling previously explored by PhysRevLett.121.196401 and demarco2018single, but focuses on establishing a direct spectral correspondence rather than using Floquet systems merely as an analogy. The mapping enables the interpretation of pi modes in Floquet insulators as doubler modes of a discrete-time lattice fermion theory, and opens the possibility of extending the equivalence to higher dimensions (where anomalous Floquet topological insulators emerge), to bulk topological invariants, and to interacting Floquet systems where prethermalization or localization physics becomes relevant.
