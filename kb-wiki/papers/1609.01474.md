# Goldstone-Type Pseudoscalar Mesons: Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Models

## Key claim
A nontrivial instantaneous reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, which retains the momentum dependence of quark propagator functions by discarding only their dependence on the time component of relative momentum, allows massless pseudoscalar mesons to be discussed from a configuration-space-potential point of view, provided the bound-state mass is set to the Goldstone point.

## Method
The author applies an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation that approximates the quark propagator functions M(p^2) and Z(p^2) by setting their dependence on the time component p_0 to zero (a "p_0^2=0 approximation"), while keeping their full three-momentum dependence. For pseudoscalar mesons at the Goldstone point (bound-state mass zero), the coupled Salpeter amplitude equations decouple, allowing the interquark potential V(r) to be extracted by inversion from the Fourier transform of the effective kinetic term. The required input for the Salpeter amplitude is obtained from Dyson-Schwinger solutions for the quark propagator, exploiting a Ward-Takahashi identity that connects the full quark propagator to the flavour-nonsinglet pseudoscalar-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers
- For constituent quark mass m = 0.35 GeV, 0.5 GeV, 1 GeV, and 1.69 GeV, the configuration-space potential V(r) derived from a Fierz-symmetric kernel is shown to rise confiningly from a finite negative value at r=0 to infinity, for m below some crucial value.
- For the analytic ansatz with mass parameter mu > 0 and mixing parameter eta in R, the potential V(r) exhibits a logarithmically softened Coulomb singularity at the origin, shown for m=0 and m=mu with eta values of 0, 0.5, 1, 2, -0.5, and -1.

## Context
This work extends the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter approach previously developed by Lucha (2005) and compares with the free-propagator approximation of Lucha (2000). The inversion method builds on Lucha (2013) for extracting potentials from bound-state equations. The Dyson-Schwinger input for quark propagator functions M(k^2) and Z(k^2) comes from the models of Maris and Roberts (1997, 1999, 2000). The analytic ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude incorporates the asymptotic behavior M(k^2) ~ 1/k^2 from Dyson-Schwinger studies (Maris and Roberts, 1997) and the confinement condition requiring an inflection point in M(k^2) at positive k^2 (Chang, Chen, and Roberts, 2008). The results enable further analytic studies of interquark potentials for light pseudoscalar mesons as Goldstone bosons of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in quantum chromodynamics.
