# Onset of color decoherence for soft gluon radiation in medium

> We report on recent studies of the phenomenon of color decoherence in jets in QCD media. The effect is most clearly observed in the radiation pattern of a quark-antiquark antenna, created in the same quantum state, traversing a dense color deconfined plasma. Multiple scattering with the medium color charges gradually destroys the coherence of the antenna. In the limit of opaque media this ultimately leads to independent radiation off the antenna constituents. Accordingly, radiation off the total charge vanishes implying a memory loss effect induced by the medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that color coherence in a quark-antiquark antenna is gradually destroyed by multiple scattering with a dense QCD medium, with the decoherence parameter Delta_med controlling the transition from vacuum-like to fully decoherent radiation. In the opaque limit, the antenna loses memory of its initial color state and radiates as two independent emitters, independent of whether the pair was originally in a color-singlet or color-octet configuration.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in light-cone gauge for a quark-antiquark antenna traversing a medium of length L, modeling the medium as a classical background color field. The soft gluon radiation amplitude is derived from the covariantly conserved current of the energetic quarks, which are color-rotated by Wilson lines along their trajectories. The resulting gluon radiation spectrum is computed to leading logarithmic accuracy, with all medium effects encapsulated in a single decoherence parameter Delta_med defined by the trace of the product of the quark and antiquark Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

* The decoherence parameter is defined as Delta_med = 1 - (1/(N_c^2 - 1)) * Tr[U_p(L,0) U_pbar^dagger(L,0)], where N_c = 3 for QCD.
* In the dilute limit, Delta_med approaches 0 and the vacuum spectrum is recovered.
* In the opaque limit, unitarity bounds Delta_med such that Delta_med approaches 1, corresponding to total decoherence.
* The soft gluon radiation spectrum contains terms proportional to the color factors C_F = 4/3 and C_A = 3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of medium-induced modifications of jet fragmentation (Mehtar-Tani, Salgado, Tywoniuk 2010, 2011) and builds on the foundational understanding of color coherence in vacuum jets established by Bassetto (1983), Dokshitzer (1978, 1991), Mueller (1981), and Ermolaev (1981). The results provide a starting point for reformulating Monte Carlo codes for high-p_T physics in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that angular ordering in the parton shower should be partially or completely destroyed in opaque media, in qualitative agreement with LHC data.
