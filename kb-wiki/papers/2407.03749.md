# Examination of the evidence for a proton halo in $^{22}$Al

> More and more halo nuclei or candidates have been identified or suggested in experiments in recent years. It was declared that the halo structure of $^{22}$Al is revealed by the large isospin asymmetry in $^{22}$Si/$^{22}$O mirror Gamow-Teller transitions [Phys. Rev. Lett. 125, 192503 (2020)]. We highlight that a significant mirror asymmetry already exists between wave functions of the likely unbound nucleus $^{22}$Si and the doubly-magic nucleus $^{22}$O, which largely explains the observed asymmetry in the transitions. Furthermore, these transitions involve only the $1^+$ excited states of the daughter nuclei $^{22}$Al and $^{22}$F. The $1^+$ state of $^{22}$Al cannot be considered a halo state due to its proton-unbound nature. An analysis of the spin-parity suggests that a weakly bound $2s_{1/2}$ valence proton in the ground state of $^{22}$Al is improbable. To investigate the shell structure for the ground state of $^{22}$Al, we employ the state-of-the-art deformed and triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theories in continuum. We find that a small $s$-wave component of $5\%$ appears for the weakly bound valence proton in $^{22}$Al only when triaxial deformation is considered. While the examination of densities and rms radii indicates that this small $s$-wave component is insufficient to form a discernible proton halo in $^{22}$Al, slightly larger $2s_{1/2}$ occupations have been reported in other recent theoretical results. The question of how many low-$\ell$ components are sufficient to form a proton halo in the presence of the Coulomb barrier remains open. Thus, future measurements of reaction or interaction cross sections and momentum distributions of breakup fragments are highly desirable to verify whether $^{22}$Al is a halo nucleus.

## Key claim

The large mirror asymmetry observed in the $^{22}$Si/$^{22}$O Gamow-Teller transitions, previously claimed to reveal a proton halo in $^{22}$Al, is largely explained by pre-existing wave-function differences between the likely unbound $^{22}$Si and the doubly-magic $^{22}$O, and the transitions involve only the $1^+$ excited state of $^{22}$Al which is proton-unbound and cannot be a halo state; the ground state of $^{22}$Al acquires only a small $5\%$ $2s_{1/2}$ component when triaxial deformation is considered, which is insufficient to form a discernible proton halo.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) and its triaxial extension (TRHBc) with the density functionals PC-PK1 and PC-F1 to calculate the ground-state properties of $^{22}$Al, including binding energies, separation energies, deformation parameters, single-particle orbital compositions, and angle-averaged proton densities. They compared these results with experimental data and with calculations for the $N=9$ isotone chain from $^{17}$O to $^{22}$Al, and analyzed the spin-parity constraints on the valence proton configuration using the known level scheme of $^{21}$Mg.

## Key numbers

- The one-proton separation energy of $^{22}$Al is measured as $100.4(8)$ keV (Campbell 2024).
- The TRHBc calculation with PC-F1 yields a binding energy of $149544.8$ keV for $^{22}$Al, reproducing the experimental value $149313.1(3)$ keV within $0.01\%$ discrepancy.
- The TRHBc calculation gives a one-proton separation energy of $21.7$ keV for $^{22}$Al, about one-fifth of the experimental $100.4(8)$ keV.
- The ground-state deformation parameters from TRHBc are $\beta = 0.30$ and $\gamma = 7.93^\circ$ for $^{22}$Al.
- The valence proton orbital in the TRHBc calculation contains about $93\%$ $1d_{5/2}$ and $5\%$ $2s_{1/2}$ components.
- Increasing the pairing strength from $V_0 = -342.5$ MeV fm$^3$ to $-350$ MeV fm$^3$ increases the $2s_{1/2}$ component to $8\%$ but makes the one-proton separation energy negative.
- The two-proton separation energy of $^{22}$Si is deduced as $-108(125)$ keV from experiment (Xu 2017) and extrapolated to $-1584(500)$ keV in AME2020.
- The one-neutron separation energy of $^{22}$F is $5230(13)$ keV (AME2020).
- The two-neutron separation energy of $^{22}$O is $10656(57)$ keV (AME2020).

## Context

This work directly challenges the claim by Lee (2020) that the large mirror asymmetry of $\delta=209(96)\%$ in $^{22}$Si/$^{22}$O Gamow-Teller transitions reveals a proton halo in $^{22}$Al. The authors argue that the asymmetry is largely due to the wave-function difference between the unbound $^{22}$Si and the doubly-magic $^{22}$O, and that the transitions involve only the $1^+$ excited state of $^{22}$Al, not its ground state. The study extends the application of DRHBc and TRHBc theories, previously successful for neutron halo nuclei such as $^{17,19}$B (Yang 2021), $^{15,19,22}$C (Sun 2018, 2020), $^{31}$Ne (Zhong 2022, Pan 2024), and $^{37}$Mg (Zhang 2023, An 2024), to examine a proton halo candidate. The results leave open the question of how much low-$\ell$ component is needed to form a proton halo in the presence of the Coulomb barrier, and call for future measurements of reaction cross sections and momentum distributions of breakup fragments for $^{22}$Al.
