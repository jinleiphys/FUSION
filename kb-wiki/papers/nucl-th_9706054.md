# Meson-induced correlations of nucleons in nuclear Compton scattering

> The non-resonant (seagull) contribution to the nuclear Compton amplitude at low energies is strongly influenced by nucleon correlations arising from meson exchange. We study this problem in a modified Fermi gas model, where nuclear correlation functions are obtained with the help of perturbation theory. The dependence of the mesonic seagull amplitude on the nuclear radius is investigated and the influence of a realistic nuclear density on this amplitude is dicussed. We found that different form factors appear for the static part (proportional to the enhancement constant $κ$) of the mesonic seagull amplitude and for the parts, which contain the contribution from electromagnetic polarizabilities.

# Meson-induced correlations of nucleons in nuclear Compton scattering

## Key claim

The mesonic seagull amplitude in low-energy nuclear Compton scattering requires three distinct form factors, one for the static part proportional to the enhancement constant κ and another for the energy-dependent parts containing the electromagnetic polarizabilities δα and δβ, rather than a single two-nucleon form factor F₂, and these form factors differ significantly from both the nuclear charge form factor F₁ and the phenomenological approximation F₁²(Δ/2).

## Method

The authors employed a modified Fermi gas model in which nucleon correlation functions were obtained perturbatively by evaluating three-nucleon and additional two-nucleon diagrams, including both π-meson and ρ-meson exchange contributions. The mesonic seagull amplitude was expanded to order ω², and the resulting form factors were computed for both homogeneous nuclear density and realistic nuclear densities using a local-density approximation with a three-parameter Fermi parametrization. The Δ-isobar contribution to the magnetic polarizability was also included.

## Key numbers

- For nuclear matter (infinite nuclear radius) in the pure Fermi gas model, κ^π = κ^ρ = 0.2 for both pion and rho-meson contributions to the enhancement constant.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is f²/4π = 0.08.
- The ρ-meson coupling constants used are f²_ρ/4π = 4.86 and \tilde f²_ρ = 0.4 f²_ρ.
- The Δ-isobar coupling constants are f_Δ = 2f and f_{γNΔ} = 0.35.
- For carbon (A/2 = 6) with realistic density, the form factor radii are r₁ = 1.9 fm (for the κ term) and r₂ = 1.4 fm (for the polarizability terms).
- For calcium (A/2 = 20) with realistic density, r₁ = 3.0 fm and r₂ = 2.5 fm.
- For lead (A/2 = 104) with realistic density, r₁ = 5.0 fm and r₂ = 4.7 fm.
- The absence of vanishing correlation functions at zero distance affects the values of κ, δα, and δβ by less than eight percent.

## Context

This work extends the modified Fermi gas model of Huett and Milstein (1995) by incorporating finite nuclear size effects and realistic nuclear densities into the calculation of the mesonic seagull amplitude. It addresses contradictions in experimental data at large scattering angles reported by Nathan et al. (1993), the Lund group (1993), and others, where the angular dependence of the Compton amplitude is sensitive to the form factor choice. The results enable improved extraction of bound-nucleon polarizabilities from nuclear Compton scattering data by providing a more accurate subtraction of the meson-exchange current contribution, which had previously been approximated using the phenomenological form factor F₂(Δ) = F₁²(Δ/2) suggested by Riska (1985) and Nathan et al. (1991). The calculation also relates to the work of Christillin et al. (1985) on alternative form factors and to the Fermi liquid theory treatment of the enhancement constant by Brown (1971).
