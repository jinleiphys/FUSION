# The Neutrino Opacity of Neutron Star Inner Crust

> The study of neutron rich matter, present in neutron star, proto-neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae, can lead to further understanding of the behavior of nuclear matter in highly asymmetric nuclei. Heterogeneous structures are expected to exist in these systems, often referred to as nuclear pasta. We have carried out a systematic study of neutrino opacity for different thermodynamic conditions in order to assess the impact that the structure has on it. We studied the dynamics of the neutrino opacity of the heterogeneous matter at different thermodynamic conditions with semiclassical molecular dynamics model already used to study nuclear multifragmentation. For different densities, proton fractions and temperature, we calculate the very long range opacity and the cluster distribution. The neutrino opacity is of crucial importance for the evolution of the core-collapse supernovae and the neutrino scattering.

## Key claim

The neutrino opacity of neutron star inner crust matter is strongly affected by the presence of heterogeneous "nuclear pasta" structures, but the quantitative results depend on the specific model chosen to describe neutron-rich matter; the authors state their results are "qualitatively correct, but quantitatively dependent on the model."

## Method

The authors used a semiclassical molecular dynamics model (CMD) with Yukawa-type two-body potentials and a Thomas-Fermi screened Coulomb interaction to simulate neutron-rich matter at sub-saturation densities. They simulated approximately 1000 different configurations spanning four proton fractions (x = 0.2 to 0.5), ten densities (0.001 to 0.08 fm^-3), and thirty temperatures, then calculated the neutron-neutron static structure factor S(q) and its Fourier transform from the pair distribution function to determine the neutrino opacity via the relation σ_total = (G_F^2 E_ν^2 / 6π) × S(q). Cluster identification was performed using the Minimum Spanning Tree Energy (MSTE) algorithm to distinguish bound pasta structures from the surrounding neutron gas.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density of the model: ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, with binding energy E(ρ_0) = 16 MeV/nucleon and compressibility of 250 MeV
- For the gnocchi phase at x = 0.4, ρ = 0.01 fm^-3, T = 0.5 MeV: the pasta peak in the structure factor is located at q_peak = 0.37 fm^-1, corresponding to neutrino energy E_ν ≈ 70 MeV, with full width at half maximum Δq_FWHM = 0.08 fm^-1 (ΔE_FWHM ≈ 15 MeV)
- At high densities, the maximum opacity occurs for neutrinos with E_ν ≈ 80 MeV
- The opacity peak height reaches its maximum at ρ = 0.01 fm^-3 for the gnocchi phase
- For temperatures above T = 0.8 MeV, the opacity peak falls to about 1/4 of its value at T = 0.5 MeV
- The screening length for the Coulomb interaction was set to λ = 20 fm
- The cutoff radius for the nuclear potential is r_c = 5.4 fm
- Simulations used N = 5500 particles under periodic boundary conditions

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of neutrino opacity in nuclear pasta by Horowitz et al. (2004), who studied a single temperature and proton fraction and found an opacity peak in the Urca neutrino energy region for the gnocchi phase. The authors build on their own previous work (Alcain et al., 2014) which showed that new pasta phases beyond the traditional lasagna, spaghetti, and gnocchi structures (termed "Generalized Nuclear Pasta") exhibit opacity peaks at characteristic wavelengths. The results enable future combination with hydrodynamic models of supernovae and proto-neutron stars (such as those by Ruffert, Mezzacappa, and Liebendorfer) to test different nuclear models and constrain the nuclear equation of state.
