# Role of the symmetry energy slope in neutron stars: exploring the model-dependency

> Using six different parametrizations of the quantum hadrodynamics (one of which is original), I study how different values of the symmetry energy slope ($L)$ affect some microscopic and macroscopic properties of neutron stars, such as the proton fraction, the maximum mass, the radius of the canonical 1.4$M_\odot$ star and its dimensionless tidal parameter $Λ$. I show that while most quantities present the same qualitative results, the tidal parameter can increase or decrease with the slope, depending on the model. Moreover, special attention is given to the minimum mass that enables the direct URCA process to occur in neutron stars' interiors ($M_{DU})$. Assuming the weak constraint $M_{DU}~>~1.35M_\odot$, we see that the maximum value of $L$ that satisfies it lies between 79 and 86 MeV. A range of only 7 MeV. Therefore, $M_{DU}$ is an easy way to impose upper bounds to the slope.

## Key claim

Using six different parametrizations of quantum hadrodynamics (one original), the author shows that while most neutron star properties (proton fraction, maximum mass, radius of the canonical 1.4 solar mass star) show the same qualitative trend with the symmetry energy slope, the dimensionless tidal parameter can either increase or decrease with the slope depending on the model; the minimum mass enabling the direct URCA process provides a model-dependent upper bound on the slope that varies by only 7 MeV across parametrizations.

## Method

The author fixes five nuclear matter properties at saturation density (saturation density, nucleon effective mass, binding energy per baryon, incompressibility, symmetry energy) for six quantum hadrodynamics parametrizations and varies only the symmetry energy slope from 44 to 92 MeV by adjusting the rho-meson coupling and the nonlinear omega-rho coupling. The equation of state is solved in mean field approximation, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are integrated to obtain mass-radius relations, with the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland and Baym-Bethe-Pethick equations of state used for the crust. The minimum mass enabling the direct URCA process is computed from the proton fraction threshold, and the dimensionless tidal deformability is calculated from the Love number and compactness.

## Key numbers

- The maximum slope value satisfying the weak direct URCA constraint (minimum mass for direct URCA greater than 1.35 solar masses) lies between 79 and 86 MeV across the six parametrizations, a range of only 7 MeV.
- For the original L1omega4 parametrization with slope = 44 MeV, the radius of the canonical 1.4 solar mass star is 12.30 km, the maximum mass is 2.29 solar masses, and the dimensionless tidal parameter is 446.
- For the NLrho parametrization with slope = 79 MeV, the minimum mass for direct URCA is exactly 1.35 solar masses, the radius of the canonical star is 12.47 km, and the dimensionless tidal parameter is 397.
- For the BigApple parametrization with slope = 86 MeV, the minimum mass for direct URCA is 1.35 solar masses, the radius of the canonical star is 13.62 km, and the dimensionless tidal parameter is 695.
- The number density where the proton fraction reaches the direct URCA threshold ranges from 0.28 fm^-3 (FSU2H, slope = 92 MeV) to 1.48 fm^-3 (NLrho, slope = 44 MeV).

## Context

This work extends studies of the symmetry energy slope's role in neutron stars (Paar 2014, Steiner 2014, Lattimer 2013) by systematically varying the slope while fixing other saturation properties across multiple quantum hadrodynamics parametrizations, including the IUFSU model's nonlinear omega-rho coupling. The results enable tighter constraints on the slope from the direct URCA process and neutron star observations, complementing constraints from hadron-quark phase transitions (Lopes 2024) and the PREX II and CREX experimental results (Tagami 2022). The original L1omega4 parametrization is introduced to simultaneously satisfy the strong radius constraint from PSR J0437-4715 (Dexheimer 2024) and the mass constraint from PSR J0952-0607 (Romani 2022).
