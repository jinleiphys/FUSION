# On the modification of the Efimov spectrum in a finite cubic box

> Three particles with large scattering length display a universal spectrum of three-body bound states called "Efimov trimers''. We calculate the modification of the Efimov trimers of three identical bosons in a finite cubic box and compute the dependence of their energies on the box size using effective field theory. Previous calculations for positive scattering length that were perturbative in the finite volume energy shift are extended to arbitrarily large shifts and negative scattering lengths. The renormalization of the effective field theory in the finite volume is explicitly verified. Moreover, we investigate the effects of partial wave mixing and study the behavior of shallow trimers near the dimer energy. Finally, we provide numerical evidence for universal scaling of the finite volume corrections.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous perturbative calculations of Efimov trimer energies in a finite cubic box to arbitrarily large finite-volume shifts and to negative scattering lengths, while explicitly verifying that the effective field theory remains properly renormalized in the finite volume.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory for three identical bosons with short-range interactions, where a boson field and an auxiliary diboson field describe the system. The finite cubic box with side length L and periodic boundary conditions quantizes momenta, replacing loop integrals with sums. The bound-state equation is derived from the pole of the boson-diboson scattering amplitude, and the angular dependence is expanded in kubic harmonics to handle the broken spherical symmetry. The resulting coupled integral equations are solved numerically for the trimer energies as functions of L.

## Key numbers

- For state II (infinite-volume energy E3∞ = -5.04626/(ma²), Λ*a = 1.66): at L = 2a, the energy deviates 10% from the infinite-volume value; the dimensionless ratio r = -mE3∞ L10%² ≈ 20.
- For state III (E3∞ = -11.1322/(ma²), Λ*a = 3.66): at L = 1.3a, the energy deviates 10% from the infinite-volume value; r ≈ 19.
- For state Ia (E3∞ = -1.18907/(ma²), Λ*a = 5.66): the trimer energy becomes positive near L = 0.67a, crossing the diboson energy near L ≈ 1.2a.
- For state Ia at L = a with Λ = 300a⁻¹: E3(L) ma² = -4.60298 including ℓ=4 partial wave, compared to -4.60872 for s-wave only (about 0.1% difference).
- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is eπ/s0 ≈ 22.7, with s0 ≈ 1.00624.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative finite-volume calculation of Kreuzer (2008) for positive scattering length to arbitrarily large shifts and to negative scattering lengths. It builds on the two-body finite-volume formalism of Beane (2003) and the three-body studies of Tan (2008) for repulsive interactions. The results enable interpretation of lattice QCD simulations of three-nucleon systems, where the triton may display Efimov physics if quark masses are tuned appropriately, as conjectured by Braaten (2003) and studied further by Epelbaum (2006) and Hammer (2007).
