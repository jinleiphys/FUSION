# Dynamical Fluctuations in Baryon--Meson Ratios

> The event-by-event dynamical fluctuations in kaon-to-proton and proton-to-pion ratios have been studied in dependence on center--of--mass energies of nucleon--nucleon collisions $\sqrt{s}$. Based on changing phase space volume which apparently is the consequence of phase transition from hadrons to quark--gluon plasma at large $\sqrt{s}$, the single--particle distribution function $f$ is assumed to be rather modified. Varying $f$ and phase space volume are implemented in the grand--canonical partition function, especially at $\sqrt{s}>17$ GeV, so that hadron resonance gas model, when taking into account the experimental acceptance ${\cal A}$ and quark phase space occupation factor $γ$, turns to be able to reproduce the dynamical fluctuations in $(K^++K^-)/(p+\overline{p})$ and $(p+\overline{p})/(π^++π^-)$ ratios over the entire range of $\sqrt{s}$.

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, when modified with a phase space factor Q to account for the transition from hadronic to partonic degrees of freedom at center-of-mass energies above 17 GeV, can reproduce the experimentally measured dynamical fluctuations in kaon-to-proton and proton-to-pion ratios over the entire energy range, but only if the quark phase space occupation factor gamma and experimental acceptance A are also adjusted separately for SPS and RHIC data sets.

## Method

The author modifies the grand-canonical partition function of the HRG model by introducing a phase space factor Q(x,k) that reflects the change in phase space volume when hadronic degrees of freedom are replaced by partonic ones at high energy densities. The dynamical fluctuations in (K++K-)/(p+anti-p) and (p+anti-p)/(pi++pi-) ratios are calculated from the particle number susceptibilities, including resonance decay contributions and subtracting Poisson statistical fluctuations. The model parameters gamma (quark phase space occupation factor), A (experimental acceptance), and Q are adjusted separately for SPS data (sqrt(s) below 17 GeV) and RHIC data (sqrt(s) above 17 GeV), with no statistical fitting performed.

## Key numbers

- For (K++K-)/(p+anti-p) ratio at SPS energies: gamma = 0.35, A = 0.1, Q not used
- For (K++K-)/(p+anti-p) ratio at RHIC energies: gamma = 0.35, A = 0.1, Q = 2.285
- For (p+anti-p)/(pi++pi-) ratio at SPS energies: gamma = 0.55, A = 0.55, Q not used
- For (p+anti-p)/(pi++pi-) ratio at RHIC energies: gamma = 0.55, A = 0.55, Q = 0.636
- The transition energy where the phase space modification becomes necessary is sqrt(s) > 17 GeV
- The chemical freeze-out condition uses the relation sqrt(s) = (1/0.614) * (3.286/mu - 2.53) where mu is chemical potential in GeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous HRG analysis of kaon-to-pion ratio fluctuations (Tawfik, 2009) to the remaining measured particle ratios: kaon-to-proton and proton-to-pion. The model builds on Fermi's concept of phase space dominance and the observation that the equilibrium single-particle distribution function fails at high energy densities where the hadronic system undergoes a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma. The approach enables comparison with transport models such as RQMD and HSD, and opens the possibility of describing other collective properties like net charge fluctuations and transport coefficients (bulk and shear viscosity) within the same framework.
