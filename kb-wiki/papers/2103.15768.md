# Proton Mass Decomposition and Hadron Cosmological Constant

> Lattice results on sigma terms and global analysis of parton momentum fractions are used to give the quark and glue fractions of the proton mass and rest energy. The mass decomposition in terms of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor is renormalization group invariant. The decomposition of the rest energy from the Hamiltonian and the gravitational form factors are scheme and scale dependent. The separation of the energy-momentum tensor into the traceless part which is composed of the quark and glue parton momentum fractions and the trace part has the minimum scheme dependence. We identify the glue part of the trace anomaly $\langle H_β\rangle $ as the vacuum energy from the glue condensate in the vacuum. From the metric term of the gravitational form factors, which is the stress part of the stress-energy-momentum tensor, we find that the trace part of the rest energy, dominated by $\langle H_β\rangle$, gives a {\it constant} restoring pressure which balances that from the traceless part of the Hamiltonian to confine the hadron, much like the cosmological constant Einstein introduced for a static universe. From a lattice calculation of $\langle H_β\rangle$ in the charmonium, we deduce the associated string tension which turns out to be in good agreement with that from a Cornell potential which fits the charmonium spectrum.

## Key claim

The trace part of the proton's rest energy, dominated by the glue part of the trace anomaly ⟨H_β⟩, provides a constant restoring pressure that balances the pressure from the traceless part of the Hamiltonian to confine the hadron, analogous to the cosmological constant Einstein introduced for a static universe. This identification depends on interpreting the metric term of the gravitational form factors as the stress part of the stress-energy-momentum tensor.

## Method

The author uses lattice QCD results for nucleon sigma terms (from overlap fermion calculations with exact chiral symmetry) and global analysis of parton momentum fractions (CT18 PDFs) to decompose the proton mass and rest energy into quark and glue components. The decomposition is performed via the trace of the energy-momentum tensor (renormalization group invariant) and separately via the Hamiltonian and gravitational form factors (scheme and scale dependent). A lattice calculation of the glue trace anomaly ⟨H_β⟩ in charmonium is used to deduce the associated string tension.

## Key numbers

- For the (2+1)-flavor case at hadronic scale: f_{πN}^N = 4.9(8)% (light quark sigma term fraction), f_s^N = 4.3(1.3)% (strange sigma term fraction), f_a^N = 90.8(1.5)% (trace anomaly fraction) of the proton mass
- For the (2+1+1+1+1)-flavor case including heavy quarks: f_c^N = f_b^N = f_t^N = 9.4(3.1)% each, f_{q total}^N = 37.4(5.6)%, f_a^N = 62.6(5.6)%
- At μ = 2 GeV from CT18: ⟨x⟩_u = 35.0(7)%, ⟨x⟩_d = 19.4(7)%, ⟨x⟩_s = 3.3(1.5)%, ⟨x⟩_c = 1.1(4)%, ⟨x⟩_g = 41.3(1.4)% of total proton momentum
- At μ = 250 GeV from CT18: ⟨x⟩_u = 24.5(4)%, ⟨x⟩_d = 15.2(4)%, ⟨x⟩_s = 5.6(9)%, ⟨x⟩_c = 4.3(9)%, ⟨x⟩_b = 2.9(4)%, ⟨x⟩_g = 47.5(5)%
- At μ = 2 GeV: \bar{C}_q(0) = -0.124(6) (without mixing corrections) or -0.161(10) (with one-loop mixing)
- At μ = 250 GeV: \bar{C}_q(0) = -0.038(14) (without mixing corrections) or -0.122(47) (with one-loop mixing)

## Context

This work extends the decomposition of hadron mass pioneered by Ji (1995) and subsequent lattice calculations by Yang et al. (2014, 2018) and Gao et al. (2015). It addresses the scheme dependence issues raised by Hatta, Rajan and Tanaka (2018), Metz, Pasquini and Rodini (2020), and Rodini (2020). The interpretation of the trace anomaly as a hadron cosmological constant builds on the thermodynamic interpretation of gravitational form factors by Lorce (2017, 2018) and Polyakov and Schweitzer (2018). The string tension deduced from the charmonium lattice calculation agrees with that from the Cornell potential used in charmonium spectroscopy, supporting the confinement mechanism proposed here.
