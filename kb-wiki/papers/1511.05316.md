# Hadron Spectroscopy in the Unquenched Quark Model

> We present the last applications of the unquenched quark model (UQM) to the description of flavour asymmetry and strangeness in the proton when baryon-meson components are included. In the meson sector, the UQM is used to calculate the charmonium and bottomonium spectra with self-energy corrections due to the coupling to the meson-meson continuum.

## Key claim
The unquenched quark model (UQM) provides a systematic way to include quark-antiquark sea-pair effects in the constituent quark model, yielding flavor asymmetry and strangeness observables in the proton that agree with experimental data, while self-energy corrections to charmonium and bottomonium spectra are found to be small (2–6% and 1%, respectively) but significant for states near open-flavor thresholds.

## Method
The hadron wave function is constructed as a zeroth-order valence quark configuration (qqq for baryons, qq̄ for mesons) plus a sum over higher Fock components generated by a ³P₀ quark-antiquark pair-creation operator. For baryons, the flavor asymmetry and strangeness content of the proton are computed from the expectation values of operators on the dressed proton state. For mesons, the physical mass is obtained as the sum of a bare energy from a potential model and a self-energy correction computed from the coupling to meson-meson continuum channels.

## Key numbers
- Gottfried sum rule from NMC: 0.2281 ± 0.0065 at Q² = 4 (GeV/c)² over x range 0.004–0.8
- Strange magnetic moment of the proton in UQM: 0.0006 μ_N
- Strange radius of the proton in UQM: −0.004 fm²
- Self-energy corrections for charmonium states: 2–6% of the bare mass
- Self-energy corrections for bottomonium states: approximately 1% of the bare mass
- X(3872) probability in its cc̄ core component: approximately 45%
- X(3872) probability in continuum components: approximately 55%

## Context
This work extends the unquenched quark model approach previously developed by Törnqvist and collaborators (1980s–1990s) using unitarized quark models, and by Van Beveren and Rupp using a t-matrix approach. It also builds on earlier studies of scalar mesons by Boglione and Pennington (2002) and of baryon-meson loop effects by Capstick and Morel. The results enable further investigation of hadron properties near open-flavor decay thresholds, such as the X(3872) and χb(3P) mesons, and provide a framework for calculating observables that depend explicitly on sea-quark components.
