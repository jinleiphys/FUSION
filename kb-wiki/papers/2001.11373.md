# Angular distribution of fragments in neutron-induced nuclear fission at energies 1-200 MeV: data, theoretical models and relevant problems

> In recent years, investigations of angular distributions of fragments in neutron-induced nuclear fission have been extended to intermediate energies, up to 200 MeV, as well as to a wide range of target isotopes. Using as an example the latest data obtained by our group for the reaction 237-Np(n,f), we discuss the specific features of fission fragment angular distribution and present a method for their simulation based on the code TALYS. It is shown that a simplified model reasonably describes energy dependence of the angular distribution in the whole range 1-200 MeV. The ways to improve the model are discussed along with the possibilities to use it for obtaining new information on fission and pre-equilibrium processes in neutron-nucleus interaction. We consider also the relevant problems of describing fission fragment angular distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simplified model, implemented as an addition to the TALYS code, reasonably reproduces the gross structure of the energy dependence of fission fragment angular anisotropy for neutron-induced fission of 237-Np across the full range 1–200 MeV, but only if one assumes that direct and pre-equilibrium processes contribute an isotropic component and that the spin orientation of residual nuclei from statistical decay dominates the anisotropy at high energies.

## Method

The authors extended the TALYS nuclear reaction code to compute differential fission cross sections by calculating spin-tensors of orientation and anisotropy parameters for all fissioning nuclei formed in the reaction. They assumed that direct and pre-equilibrium processes produce only isotropic fission contributions, while the compound-nucleus decay chain carries spin alignment via statistical particle emission. The distribution over the projection K of the nuclear spin on the deformation axis was taken as a Gaussian above the fission barrier and as a Gaussian centered at a fixed K1 below the barrier, with a single effective moment of inertia Jeff applied to all fissioning isotopes.

## Key numbers

* For the reaction 237-Np(n,f), the angular anisotropy W(0°)/W(90°) was measured for neutron energies from 1 to 200 MeV; data above 20 MeV were obtained for the first time.
* The calculated fission cross section with corrected barrier parameters agrees with experimental data in the range 0.1–100 MeV; discrepancies in the 2–16 MeV region do not exceed 25–30%.
* Two values of the effective moment of inertia parameter were used: ℏ²/Jeff = 0.012 MeV and 0.022 MeV, chosen to bracket almost all experimental anisotropy points above ~1 MeV.
* For sub-barrier fission of 238-Np, the dominant transition state projection K1 was varied from 0 to 4; none of the curves described the experimental data satisfactorily, with the most appropriate being the extreme values K1 = 0 and K1 = 4.

## Context

This work extends the experimental and theoretical study of fission fragment angular distributions from the low-energy region (below 20 MeV) to intermediate energies up to 200 MeV, following earlier measurements by Ryzhov (2005) for 232-Th and 238-U up to 100 MeV. The authors’ group had previously measured angular distributions for 232-Th, 235-U, 238-U, 209-Bi, 233-U, nat-Pb, 239-Pu, and 237-Np, while similar investigations were performed by the n_TOF and NEFFTE collaborations. The simplified model enables future extraction of information about fission barriers, transition states, and pre-equilibrium processes, and can be extended to reactions initiated by charged particles such as (p,f) and (p,γ).
