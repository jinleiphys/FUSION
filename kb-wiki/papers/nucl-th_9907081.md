# Quasi-Deuteron Configurations in odd-odd N=Z nuclei

> The isovector M1 transitions between low-lying T=1 and T=0 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei are analyzed. Simple analytical expressions for M1 transition strengths are derived within a single-j-shell approximation for both j=l+1/2 and j=l-1/2 cases. The large B(M1) values for the j=l+1/2 case are attributed to quasi-deuteron configurations. The B(M1) values for the j=l-1/2 case are found to be small due to partly cancellation of spin and orbital parts of the M1 matrix element.

# Quasi-Deuteron Configurations in odd-odd N=Z nuclei

## Key claim

Isovector M1 transitions between low-lying T=1 and T=0 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei can be understood within a simple single-j-shell approximation, where large B(M1) values arise specifically for the j=l+1/2 case due to constructive interference of spin and orbital contributions (quasi-deuteron configurations), while the j=l-1/2 case yields small B(M1) values due to partial cancellation. The paper claims that this simple model captures the essential physics, but acknowledges that configuration mixing and quenching of spin g-factors are needed for quantitative agreement with data.

## Method

The authors applied a core-plus-two-nucleon single-j-shell (2NSj) approximation, treating an odd-odd N=Z nucleus as an inert even-even N=Z core with one valence proton and one valence neutron in the same shell-model orbital (nlj). Analytical expressions for the reduced M1 transition matrix elements between J=0 and J=1 states were derived separately for the j=l+1/2 and j=l-1/2 cases, using the isovector part of the M1 operator. The resulting B(M1) values were then related to Schmidt magnetic moments of neighboring odd-mass nuclei, and compared with experimental data using both free and quenched (alpha_q=0.7) spin g-factors.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron (modeled bound state, j=l+1/2, 1s_{1/2}): B(M1;0+ -> 1+) = 15.86 mu_N^2 (free g-factors)
* ^6Li (pi1p_{3/2} nu1p_{3/2}): experimental B(M1) = 15.4(4) mu_N^2; free theory = 12.96 mu_N^2
* ^18F (pi1d_{5/2} nu1d_{5/2}): experimental B(M1) = 20(4) mu_N^2; free theory = 15.18 mu_N^2
* ^34Cl (pi1d_{3/2} nu1d_{3/2}, j=l-1/2): experimental B(M1) = 0.23(2) mu_N^2; free theory = 0.42 mu_N^2
* ^38K (pi1d_{3/2}^{-1} nu1d_{3/2}^{-1}, j=l-1/2): experimental B(M1) = 0.47(4) mu_N^2; free theory = 0.42 mu_N^2
* ^42Sc (pi1f_{7/2} nu1f_{7/2}): experimental B(M1) = 11(4) mu_N^2; free theory = 18.23 mu_N^2; effective theory (alpha_q=0.7) = 12.16 mu_N^2
* ^14N (j=l-1/2, p_{1/2} shell): experimental B(M1;0+_1 -> 1+_1) = 0.05(2) mu_N^2; free theory = 0.58 mu_N^2
* ^14N (j=l+1/2, p_{3/2} shell, 1+_2 state at 3.948 MeV): experimental B(M1) = 5(+3/-2) mu_N^2
* ^42Sc B(M1) estimated from experimental magnetic moments of ^41Sc (5.535 mu_N) and ^41Ca (-1.595 mu_N) via Eq. (bmu): 15.62 mu_N^2; experimental = 10(4) mu_N^2
* Predicted B(M1) for ^82Nb from experimental moments: 15.52 mu_N^2

## Context

This work extends the analysis of M1 properties in N=Z nuclei beyond the earlier shell-model study by Ronen and Shlomo (1998) and complements the discussion of spin-orbit interference in even-even s-d shell nuclei by Fayache, Zamick and collaborators (1999). The paper provides simple analytical benchmarks analogous to Schmidt values for magnetic moments, enabling predictions for heavier odd-odd N=Z nuclei such as ^46V, ^50Mn, ^54Co, and ^82Nb where experimental data were then emerging (Frießner et al., 1999; Schneider et al., 1999). The relation between B(M1) values in odd-odd N=Z nuclei and magnetic moments of neighboring odd-A nuclei offers a practical tool for interpreting the structure of 0+ and 1+ states without requiring full shell-model calculations.
