# Ab initio limits of atomic nuclei

> We predict the limits of existence of atomic nuclei, the proton and neutron drip lines, from the light through medium-mass regions. Starting from a chiral two- and three-nucleon interaction with good saturation properties, we use the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group to calculate ground-state and separation energies from helium to iron, nearly 700 isotopes in total. We use the available experimental data to quantify the theoretical uncertainties for our ab initio calculations towards the drip lines. Where the drip lines are known experimentally, our predictions are consistent within the estimated uncertainty. For the neutron-rich sodium to chromium isotopes, we provide predictions to be tested at rare-isotope beam facilities.

## Key claim

Using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) starting from a chiral two- and three-nucleon interaction fit only to few-body data, the authors predict the proton and neutron drip lines from helium to iron with quantified theoretical uncertainties; the predictions are consistent with known drip lines within the estimated uncertainty, but the authors caution that an error of a few tens of keV can make the difference between bound and unbound, and that their uncertainty model neglects possible emergent phenomena in the neutron-rich region.

## Method

The authors start from the 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interaction, which was fit only to the triton binding energy and the helium-4 charge radius. They use the VS-IMSRG in the IMSRG(2) approximation to decouple a valence-space Hamiltonian for each nucleus, then diagonalize it with the NuShellX@MSU or K-shell shell-model codes. The calculation covers nearly 700 isotopes from helium (Z=2) to iron (Z=26), using a harmonic-oscillator basis with 15 major shells and a 3N cutoff of E3Max=16, with extrapolations in emax for infrared convergence.

## Key numbers

- Overall root-mean-square (rms) deviation from experimental absolute ground-state energies: 3.3 MeV
- Rms deviation from experimental separation energies: 0.7 to 1.4 MeV
- Slope of the systematic error model for separation energy residuals: approximately 0.08 (dimensionless), consistent with the expectation from the observed 4% systematic underprediction of nuclear radii
- For comparison, state-of-the-art energy-density functionals achieve rms deviations of 0.6 to 2.0 MeV for energies and 0.4 to 1.25 MeV for separation energies (over a much larger mass range)

## Context

This work extends ab initio nuclear theory from calculations of individual closed-shell nuclei or selected isotopic chains to a global survey of nearly all nuclei from helium to iron, providing the first ab initio predictions of the neutron drip line above neon (Z=10). The results are consistent with earlier ab initio calculations by Hagen (2013) and Holt (2014a) for calcium, which found Ca-62 as the heaviest bound isotope, but also show a flat binding-energy trend that leaves the drip line ambiguous, similar to the statistical analysis of Neufcourt (2019). The predictions for neutron-rich sodium to chromium isotopes are intended to guide experiments at rare-isotope beam facilities such as FRIB and FAIR, and the approach points toward future ab initio input for r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
