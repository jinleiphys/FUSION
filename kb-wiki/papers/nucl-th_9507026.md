# Rho-Omega Mixing via QCD Sum Rules with Finite Mesonic Widths

> Based on the analysis of both Borel and Finite-Energy QCD sum rules, the inclusion of finite mesonic widths leads to a dramatic effect on the predictions for the momentum dependence of the rho-omega mixing matrix element. It is shown that the rho-omega mixing matrix element traditionally discussed in the literature, has the same sign and similar magnitude in the space-like region as its on-shell value. This contrasts the zero-width result where the mixing matrix element is typically of opposite sign in the space-like region.

## Key claim

Including the finite widths of the rho and omega mesons in QCD sum rule analyses reverses the sign of the mixing parameter lambda compared to the zero-width approximation, causing the rho-omega mixing matrix element in the space-like region (q^2 between -1.0 and 0 GeV^2) to have the same sign and similar magnitude as its on-shell value, rather than the opposite sign predicted by zero-width calculations.

## Method

The authors applied both Borel QCD sum rules (BSR) and Finite-Energy QCD sum rules (FESR) to the rho-omega mixed propagator, replacing the zero-width delta-function spectral densities with normalized Breit-Wigner distributions for the rho and omega mesons using their physical widths (Gamma_rho = 151.5 MeV, Gamma_omega = 8.4 MeV). The mixing parameter lambda was extracted from fits to the sum rules using Monte Carlo based uncertainty analysis over QCD condensate parameters, with the continuum threshold s_0 and resonance strengths as fit parameters, both with and without the on-shell constraint relating f_rho and f_omega.

## Key numbers

- Gamma_rho = 151.5 MeV and Gamma_omega = 8.4 MeV (physical half-widths used for the rho and omega mesons)
- lambda = -0.64 (+0.38/-0.75) from BSR analysis with on-shell constraint for finite physical widths (compared to lambda = +1.63 (+0.34/-0.55) for zero-width case)
- lambda = -0.62 (+0.48/-0.93) from BSR analysis without the on-shell constraint for finite physical widths
- lambda = -0.67 from FESR analysis with finite physical widths (compared to lambda = +1.53 for zero-width FESR)
- On-shell mixing matrix element Theta(m^2) = -0.0051 GeV^2 (physical value used as input)
- Sum rule prediction for on-shell value: Theta(m^2) = -0.0046 (+0.0022/-0.0040) GeV^2 from unconstrained fit
- Valid Borel mass regime: 1.15 <= M <= 2.45 GeV
- Quark mass ratio m_u/m_d = 0.50 +/- 0.25 used in uncertainty analysis; adopting m_u/m_d = 0.57 +/- 0.04 gives lambda = -0.52 +/- 0.32

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule analysis of rho-omega mixing by Hatsuda et al. (1994), who used zero-width approximations and found positive lambda with opposite sign in the space-like region. The results contradict earlier arguments by Goldman et al. (1992), Krein et al. (1993), and Piekarewicz and Williams (1993) that the off-shell mixing matrix element should change significantly, which would reduce its contribution to the charge symmetry breaking nucleon-nucleon potential. The finding that lambda becomes negative with finite widths enables a reevaluation of the charge symmetry breaking potential and corresponding physical observables, with a comprehensive analysis planned for a future publication.
