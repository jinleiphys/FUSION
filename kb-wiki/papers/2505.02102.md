# Gauge invariance and color charge fluctuations

> The nature of confinement is connected with color charge. Unfortunately, the color charge densities in QCD, the Noether charge densities associated with the global color invariance, are not invariant under local color rotations. This implies that the expectation values of the net color charge in any region of any physical state in QCD, states that satisfy the color Gauss law, are automatically zero for all components of color. In this paper it is shown that the expectation value of the square of the net color charge in a region, a measure of the color charge fluctuations, is necessarily nonzero when evaluated in physical states and the result, while depending on the scheme and scale by which the theory is regulated, is gauge invariant. This holds despite the formal lack of gauge invariance of the operator. Moreover, there is a particular combination of the color charge fluctuations for the vacuum and for a system describable by a non-trivial density matrix that is independent that has a well-defined continuum limit

## Key claim

The expectation value of the square of the net color charge in a spatial region, a measure of color charge fluctuations, is necessarily nonzero in physical QCD states and is gauge invariant despite the formal lack of gauge invariance of the operator, though the result depends on the scheme and scale by which the theory is regulated. A particular combination of the color charge fluctuations for the vacuum and for a system described by a nontrivial density matrix has a well-defined continuum limit.

## Method

The analysis uses a Hamiltonian formulation of QCD with lattice regularization, working in the temporal gauge (A0^a = 0) with residual spatial gauge freedom. Physical states are defined as those annihilated by the color Gauss law operators, and the projection operator onto the physical subspace is used to extract the gauge-invariant part of the color charge fluctuation operator. The expectation value of the squared color charge in a region R is re-expressed as a surface integral of the squared color electric field normal to the boundary, with the ultraviolet regulator introducing a factor of a_l^2/g(a_l)^2.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum expectation value of the squared color charge in a region R scales as ⟨Q²_R⟩_vac = (4π const² A_R) / (a_l² α_s(a_l)), where A_R is the area of the region and a_l is the lattice spacing.
- For four cubic regions of volumes V_A:V_B:V_C:V_D = 1:2:2:7, the vacuum charge fluctuations scale as ⟨Q²_RA⟩:⟨Q²_RB⟩:⟨Q²_RC⟩:⟨Q²_RD⟩ = 1:2:5/3:13/3.
- For a thermal density matrix at temperature T, the ratio of the well-defined combination F_R;T for the same four regions is F_RA;T:F_RB;T:F_RC;T:F_RD;T = 1:2:5/3:13/3.
- In the high-temperature limit, the ratio of F_R;T at two temperatures satisfies F_R;T1 / F_R;T2 → T1²/T2² as T1,T2 → ∞.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of color confinement by connecting it to color charge fluctuations, building on the foundational lattice QCD formulation of Kogut and Susskind (1974) and the Wilson loop approach of Wilson (1974). The paper shows that despite the trivial vanishing of net color charge expectation values in any region for physical states, the fluctuations of color charge are nontrivial and gauge invariant, with a peculiar area-law scaling rather than the volume scaling one might naively expect. This opens the possibility of using color charge fluctuations as a diagnostic for confinement, though the author notes that the interpretation of these quantities and their utility for understanding confinement remains an open question.
