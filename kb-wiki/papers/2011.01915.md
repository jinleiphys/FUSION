# The $P_{cs}(4459)$ pentaquark from a combined effective field theory and phenomenological perspective

> The observation of the $P_{cs}(4459)$ by the LHCb collaboration adds a new member to the set of known hidden-charm pentaquarks, which includes the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$. The $P_{cs}(4459)$ is expected to have the light-quark content of a $Λ$ baryon ($I=0$, $S=-1$), but its spin is unknown. Its closeness to the $\bar{D}^* Ξ_c$ threshold -- $4478\,{\rm MeV}$ in the isospin-symmetric limit -- suggests the molecular hypothesis as a plausible explanation for the $P_{cs}(4459)$. While in the absence of coupled-channel dynamics heavy-quark spin symmetry predicts the two spin-states of the $\bar{D}^* Ξ_c$ to be degenerate, power counting arguments indicate that the coupling with the nearby $\bar{D} Ξ_c'$ and $\bar{D} Ξ_c^*$ channels might be a leading order effect. This generates a hyperfine splitting in which the $J=\tfrac{3}{2}$ $\bar{D}^* Ξ_c$ pentaquark will be lighter than the $J=\tfrac{1}{2}$ configuration, which we estimate to be of the order of $5-15\,{\rm MeV}$. We also point out an accidental symmetry between the $P_{cs}(4459)$ and $P_c(4440/4457)$ potentials. Finally, we argue that the spectroscopy and the $J/ψΛ$ decays of the $P_{cs}(4459)$ might suggest a marginal preference for $J = \tfrac{3}{2}$ over $J = \tfrac{1}{2}$.

## Key claim

The $P_{cs}(4459)$ pentaquark can be explained as a $\bar{D}^* \Xi_c$ hadronic molecule, but its spin is unknown; the authors argue that coupled-channel dynamics with the nearby $\bar{D} \Xi_c'$ and $\bar{D} \Xi_c^*$ channels generate a hyperfine splitting in which the $J = 3/2$ configuration is lighter than the $J = 1/2$ configuration, with a marginal preference for $J = 3/2$ suggested by spectroscopy and $J/\psi \Lambda$ decays.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic contact-range effective field theory (EFT) with Gaussian regulator and cutoff $\Lambda = 0.5-1.0$ GeV, incorporating heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and SU(3)-flavor symmetry constraints. They determine the spin-independent coupling $C_a$ from the $P_c(4312)$ pentaquark, then consider two scenarios (scenario A: observed $P_{cs}$ is $J=1/2$; scenario B: observed $P_{cs}$ is $J=3/2$) to determine the diagonal coupling $D_a$ and transition coupling $E_b$ from the $P_{cs}$ mass. The hyperfine splitting is computed by solving coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the $\bar{D} \Xi_c'$-$\bar{D}^* \Xi_c$ ($J=1/2$) and $\bar{D}^* \Xi_c$-$\bar{D} \Xi_c^*$ ($J=3/2$) bases, and the $E_b/D_a$ ratio is estimated via power counting and meson-exchange saturation.

## Key numbers

* $P_{cs}(4459)$ mass: $M_{P_{cs}} = 4458.8 \pm 2.9 {}^{+4.7}_{-1.1}$ MeV; width: $\Gamma_{P_{cs}} = 17.3 \pm 6.5 {}^{+8.0}_{-5.7}$ MeV (statistical significance $3.1\sigma$)
* $\bar{D}^* \Xi_c$ threshold: $4478.0$ MeV in the isospin-symmetric limit; $\bar{D} \Xi_c'$ threshold at $4446.0$ MeV; $\bar{D} \Xi_c^*$ threshold at $4513.2$ MeV
* Predicted masses for $\bar{D} \Xi_c'$ and $\bar{D} \Xi_c^*$ molecules from $P_c(4312)$ input: $M(P_{cs}') = 4436.7$ (4436.1) MeV and $M(P_{cs}^*) = 4503.6$ (4502.7) MeV for $\Lambda = 0.5$ (1.0) GeV
* Estimated hyperfine splitting from power counting: $\Delta M^A \sim 27-40$ MeV (scenario A) and $\Delta M^B \sim 12-13$ MeV (scenario B) after renormalization factor
* Two-peak solution from LHCb: $M(P_{cs1}) = 4454.9 \pm 2.7$ MeV, $M(P_{cs2}) = 4467.8 \pm 3.7$ MeV, giving $\Delta M = 12.9 \pm 4.6$ MeV; corresponding $E_b/D_a = 0.37-0.22$ for $\Lambda = 0.5-1.0$ GeV
* Power counting estimate: $Q/M \sim 0.27$ from $\gamma = \sqrt{2\mu B_2} \sim 206$ MeV and $m_\rho \sim 770$ MeV; coupled-channel size ratios: $B_2/\Delta_{CC} \sim 0.60$ ($\bar{D} \Xi_c'$), $0.54$ ($\bar{D} \Xi_c^*$), $0.24$ ($\bar{D}_s^* \Lambda_c$), $0.14$ ($\bar{D} \Xi_c$), $0.11$ ($\bar{D}^* \Xi_c^*$)

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $P_c(4312/4440/4457)$ pentaquarks observed by LHCb (Aaij 2019) to the newly observed $P_{cs}(4459)$ (Aaij 2020), building on SU(3)-flavor and HQSS constraints previously applied by Peng (2019) and the contact-range EFT formalism developed for pentaquarks by Liu (2018). The authors contrast their approach with that of Xiao (2019) and Xiao (2019), who set spin-dependent couplings to zero and thus predict no hyperfine splitting. The results enable future experimental determination of the $P_{cs}$ spin and tests of the molecular hypothesis through precise measurements of the $J/\psi \Lambda$ decay channel and possible two-peak structure.
