# Microscopic Model of the Time Like Electromagnetic Form Factor of the Nucleon

> A microscopic model of the electromagnetic form factor of the nucleon is developed in a hadronic framework, including pions, nucleons and the Delta-resonance explicitly. The space like on-shell form factors are reproduced and predictions for the half off-shell dependence are made. The impact of this off-shell dependence in the time like sector (q^2 < 1 GeV^2, thus including the region of vector meson dominance) is of main interest in this investigation.

# Microscopic Model of the Time Like Electromagnetic Form Factor of the Nucleon

## Key claim

The paper develops a hadronic model of the nucleon electromagnetic form factor that includes pions, nucleons, and the Delta-resonance explicitly, and finds that the off-shell dependence of the form factor in the time-like region (q² < 1 GeV²) may significantly alter the vector meson dominance (VMD) predictions, but the model is restricted to nucleon invariant masses below 2 GeV and photon invariant masses below approximately 1 GeV to avoid unphysical decay channels from the regularization cutoff.

## Method

The authors construct the electromagnetic vertex from a pion-loop expansion of the nucleon propagator, coupling external photons to each charged particle in the loop, using pseudovector coupling for pions and nucleons and including the Delta-resonance via the Rarita-Schwinger formalism. A covariant monopole cutoff is applied to the NNπ vertex and a dipole cutoff to the NΔπ vertex, with the cutoff function absorbed into a "reduced" propagator formalism that allows a proof of gauge invariance via the Ward-Takahashi identity. The irreducible vertex is calculated from the self-energy diagrams, and the full half off-shell form factor is obtained by dividing by the off-shell charge form factor at q²=0.

## Key numbers

- The model is restricted to nucleon off-shell energies W_max = 2 GeV, requiring the cutoff mass Λ to be somewhat larger than 1 GeV.
- The time-like region studied is q² < 1 GeV², which includes the vector meson mass region around 750-800 MeV for ρ and ω mesons.
- The space-like data cover momentum transfers from q² = 0 up to about q² = 35 GeV² and are parametrized by a dipole fit.
- The cutoff function has the form f_c(q²) = (m_π² - Λ²)/(q² - Λ²), where m_π is the pion mass.
- The photon invariant mass M = √(q²_γ) is restricted to M < m_π + Λ ≈ 1 GeV to avoid pion-cop production.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic model calculations of Naus and Koch (1990) and Tiemeijer and Tjon (1990), who studied the space-like regime with off-shell nucleons below the pion production threshold (W < m + m_π), by including the Delta-resonance and allowing decay channels for time-like momentum transfers. The paper is motivated by the failure of conventional pole fits (Dubnicka, 1990) to describe LEAR data from p̅p annihilation (Bardin, 1994), and aims to provide guidance for dilepton production experiments by the DLS collaboration (Roche, 1990) and for simulations of NN → NN e⁺e⁻ processes by the Giessen group (Schaefer, 1992; Feuster, 1993). The model enables quantitative investigation of whether VMD is still visible when off-shell dependence is properly taken into account.
