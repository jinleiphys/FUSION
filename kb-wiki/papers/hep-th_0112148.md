# Exact One-Loop Thermal Free Energies of Solitons

> I show how to compute the exact one-loop thermal correction to the free energy of a soliton. The method uses the effective potential as an auxiliary step to ensure that the soliton is quantized around the appropriate vacuum. The exact result is then computed using scattering theory techniques, and includes all orders in the derivative expansion. It can be efficiently combined with a calculation of the exact quantum correction to yield the full free energy to one loop. I demonstrate this technique with explicit computations in $ϕ^4$ models, obtaining the free energy for a kink in 1+1 dimensions and a domain wall in 2+1 dimensions.

# Exact One-Loop Thermal Free Energies of Solitons

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that exact one-loop thermal corrections to soliton free energies can be computed using scattering theory techniques that include all orders in the derivative expansion, provided one first uses the effective potential to shift the soliton to the thermally-corrected vacuum. The method works for temperatures below the symmetry-restoration point, where the soliton still exists.

## Method

The author first computes the thermal effective potential to find the temperature-dependent vacuum expectation value φ₀(T), then constructs a modified classical potential with artificial finite counterterms that force its minimum to coincide with φ₀(T). The exact one-loop quantum and thermal corrections are then computed using the phase shift formalism, where the density of states is expressed in terms of scattering phase shifts and bound state energies. The artificial counterterms are subtracted by removing the first Born approximation to the phase shift, ensuring the free energy is minimized at the correct vacuum. The method is applied to the φ⁴ kink in 1+1 dimensions and the domain wall in 2+1 dimensions, where the small oscillations potential is of the exactly solvable Pöschl-Teller reflectionless form.

## Key numbers

- For the 1+1 dimensional kink, the quantum correction to the modified potential is \(\tilde m \left(\frac{1}{4\sqrt{3}} - \frac{3}{2\pi}\right)\)
- For the 1+1 dimensional kink, the classical contribution is \(\tilde m^3/(3\lambda)\)
- For the 2+1 dimensional domain wall, the quantum correction per unit transverse length is \(\frac{3m^2}{16\pi}\left({\rm arccoth}(2) - 2\right)\)
- The 1+1 dimensional effective potential is plotted at \(T = 0.7 m\) with \(\lambda = 0.2 m^2\)
- The 2+1 dimensional effective potential is plotted at \(T = 2.4 m\) with \(\lambda = 0.2 m\)

## Context

This work extends the exact one-loop quantum correction methods developed in Graham (prior work) and earlier ideas from the same group, which implemented scattering theory techniques for soliton energies at zero temperature. It addresses a gap in the prior calculation by Carvalho (year not specified), which computed thermal corrections in the same φ⁴ model but did not account for the thermal shift of the vacuum. The method enables continuous tracking of soliton free energies from zero temperature up to the melting point, and can be extended to models with conserved charges, phase transitions, and percolation temperatures in statistical systems.
