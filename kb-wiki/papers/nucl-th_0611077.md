# Nuclear Physics with Electroweak Probes

> The research activitities carried out in Italy during the last two years in the field of theoretical nuclear physics with electroweak probes are reviewed. Different models for electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering are compared. The results obtained for electromagnetic reactions on few-nucleon systems and on complex nuclei are discussed. The recent developments in the study of electron- and photon-induced reactions with one and two-nucleon emission are presented.

## Key claim

This paper reviews theoretical nuclear physics with electroweak probes conducted in Italy over two years, but it is a review of existing models and results rather than presenting a single new claim. The abstract's implication of comprehensive comparison is hedged by the text's repeated emphasis that different models (RPA, RDWIA, GFA, scaling) have different strengths and weaknesses, and that no single approach is fully satisfactory for all kinematics or nuclei.

## Method

The paper surveys and compares several theoretical approaches for electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering: Random Phase Approximation (RPA) for collective excitations; Relativistic Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA) and Green's Function Approach (GFA) for quasielastic scattering with final-state interactions; and a superscaling approach that extracts empirical scaling functions from electron scattering data to predict neutrino cross sections. For few-nucleon systems, the Hyperspherical Harmonics method with realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions (Argonne v18, Urbana IX) is used, with conserved two- and three-body electromagnetic currents constructed via meson-exchange or minimal substitution schemes.

## Key numbers

* RPA calculations for neutrinos of 20-40 MeV have large cross-section uncertainties; above 50 MeV, results become rather independent of the interaction, and many-particle many-hole excitations reduce RPA cross sections by a factor of approximately 10-15%.
* In the Green's Function Approach for (e,e') reactions, short-range correlations produce effects within about 5% and tensor correlations add effects within 10%.
* For the 16O(νμ,μ-) reaction at Eν = 500 and 1000 MeV at θμ = 30°, the GFA and rROP results show very small differences, meaning flux conservation is the most important condition.
* The superscaling approach reproduces (e,e') cross sections with typical deviations of 10% or less.
* For ν- and ν̄-deuteron disintegration at solar neutrino energies, the uncertainty of standard nuclear physics calculations has been reduced from 5-10% to about 3% by including two-body weak axial exchange currents.
* For A=3 systems at energies 0-20 MeV, exactly conserved currents resolve discrepancies in tensor polarizations T20 and T21 for pd radiative capture at Ec.m. = 2 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the work of Co' (2004) who previously pointed out the need for a more complete experimental program with electromagnetic probes. It compares Italian groups' models including those of Meucci, Co', Benhar, and others, and discusses the scaling approach developed by Caballero and collaborators. The paper enables future work by showing that the superscaling method can be applied to neutral-current reactions as well as charged-current ones, and that consistent treatment of final-state interactions via the Green's Function Approach is essential for inclusive reactions. Open questions include the need for relativistic models at energies around 1 GeV and the challenge of reproducing the asymmetric shape of the experimental scaling function, which only the Relativistic Mean Field model achieves.
