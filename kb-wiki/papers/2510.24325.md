# Emergent Bell-Triplet State in Proton-Proton Scattering

> Entanglement is a key resource in quantum information science, yet its properties and applications in nuclear systems remain largely unexplored. Here, using proton-proton scattering as a quantum laboratory, we report the emergence of a near-pure Bell-triplet state at a laboratory energy of 151 MeV and a center-of-mass scattering angle of 90 degrees. In this unique kinematic regime, the scattering amplitude functions as a transition operator connecting distinct Bell states. Building upon this emergent resource, we propose a quantum teleportation protocol for proton spins, exploiting the intrinsic Hamiltonian of the strong interaction to perform the requisite Bell measurement. These findings effectively bridge few-body nuclear physics and quantum technology, establishing proton-proton scattering as both a source of high-fidelity entanglement and a natural processor for quantum information.

## Key claim

The paper reports the emergence of a near-pure Bell-triplet state in proton-proton scattering at a specific kinematic point, but this result is conditional on using realistic nuclear interactions beyond leading order (LO) in chiral effective field theory; the LO interaction largely misses the effect, indicating the phenomenon depends on higher-order contributions to the nuclear force.

## Method

The authors computed spin entanglement measures (entanglement power and concurrence) for proton-proton scattering using the Nijmegen PWA93 partial-wave analysis database and chiral effective field theory interactions from LO to N3LO, with Coulomb corrections included. They analyzed the final-state spin density matrix for unpolarized initial protons as a function of laboratory kinetic energy and center-of-mass scattering angle, and decomposed the scattering amplitude in the Bell basis to identify the dominant transition operator. The tensor force contribution was isolated by scaling the tensor component of the N3LO chiral potential and recalculating the entanglement measures.

## Key numbers

- Concurrence reaches 0.977 at laboratory energy 151 MeV and center-of-mass scattering angle 90 degrees for a completely unpolarized initial state
- The final-state density matrix at this kinematic point has coefficients a1 = 0.988 for the |Psi+> Bell state, a2 = 0.009 for |Phi->, and a3 = 0.003 for |Psi->
- The dominant Bell-basis matrix element of the scattering amplitude at (151 MeV, 90 degrees) is (-3.845 - i0.058) |Psi+><Phi-|, with all other components smaller by more than one order of magnitude
- Modern interactions reproduce pp differential cross sections up to approximately 350 MeV with chi-squared per degree of freedom approximately 1

## Context

This work extends recent theoretical investigations of spin entanglement in nucleon-nucleon scattering using the full S-matrix formalism by Bai (2023), Miller (2023), Cavallin (2025), and Witala (2025), but focuses on the experimentally most accessible proton-proton channel which had remained incomplete. The low-energy entanglement regime (below 10 MeV) was previously exploited for nuclear Bell tests by Lamehi-Rachti (1976) and Sakai (2006) using the spin-singlet state from 1S0 partial-wave scattering. The discovery of a Bell-triplet window enables a proposed quantum teleportation protocol for proton spins and provides a new benchmark for constraining nucleon-nucleon interactions, particularly the tensor force component.
