# QED-QCD Interference Effect on the Charge Dependent N-N Interaction

## Key claim

By including the QED-QCD interference effect alongside the quark mass difference and electromagnetic interaction in a quark cluster model, the charge symmetry breaking and charge independence breaking N-N scattering length differences can be simultaneously reproduced, but only with model parameters derived from a perturbative QCD calculation, not with the phenomenological GMS parameter set, which gives a charge independence breaking value of the wrong sign.

## Method

The authors used a resonating group method with a quark cluster model to calculate the N-N ^1S_0 scattering length differences Δa_CSB and Δa_CIB. The Hamiltonian included five charge-dependent terms: quark mass difference (δm_i), kinetic energy correction (δK_i), gluon exchange correction (δV^G_ij), photon exchange (V^γ_ij), and the QED-QCD interference interaction (V^I_ij). The charge-independent part of the N-N interaction was taken from the Reid soft core phenomenological potential, and the scattering lengths were obtained by solving a resonating group equation with quark exchange operators.

## Key numbers

- Charge independent scattering length from Reid soft core potential: a_NN = -17.13 fm
- Recommended Δa_CSB from literature: 1.5 fm; recommended Δa_CIB from literature: 5.7 fm
- GMS phenomenological model (most favorable parameter set): Δa_CSB ~ 2-3 fm (too large), Δa_CIB = -1.5 fm (wrong sign)
- Perturbative QCD model MQ II: Δa_CSB = 2.16 fm, Δa_CIB = 7.33 fm
- Perturbative QCD model MQP (constrained by n-p and Δ++-Δ0 mass differences): Δa_CSB = 1.50 fm, Δa_CIB = 5.73 fm (nearly identical to recommended values)
- Quark mass difference range from GMS fit: -4.96 MeV ≤ Δm ≤ 6.28 MeV; most favorable Δm = 0.66 MeV (compared to ~3 MeV used in other quark model calculations)

## Context

This work extends direct quark model approaches to charge-dependent N-N interactions (Refs. 12, 13 in the paper), which previously could not simultaneously fit charge symmetry and charge independence breaking because the quark mass difference does not affect n-p scattering and the electromagnetic effect alone was insufficient. The authors incorporate the QED-QCD interference effect proposed by Goldman, Maltman and Stephenson (1994), also called "electromagnetic penguins", which had not been included in prior quark model calculations. The results show that a perturbative QCD model can reproduce the recommended scattering length differences, while the phenomenological GMS parameter set cannot, indicating that further quantitative work is needed to resolve the discrepancy.
