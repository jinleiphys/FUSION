# Charmonium production in a thermalizing heat bath

> Using the Remler formalism for the creation of composed particles, we study charmonium production both in thermalized and thermalizing boxes, which contain charm and anticharm quarks. The thermalizing box studies include the lowering of the box temperature, the spatial diffusion of charm and anticharm quarks, which are initially confined in the central region, as well as the combination of both, what imitates heavy-ion collisions. Comparing numerical and analytical results we demonstrate that the rate of the original Remler formalism has to be supplemented by two rates to obtain, for $t\to \infty$, results, which are consistent with the statistical model predictions: i) a rate, which takes into account the temperature dependence of the Wigner density of the quarkonium during the expansion and, in the case that a heavy quark potential is not implemented in the Monte Carlo approach, ii) a rate which comes from the change of the relative distance between the heavy quark and antiquark. These results provide the basis for future applications of the Remler formalism to heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The original Remler formalism for quarkonium production must be supplemented by two additional rates to obtain asymptotic results consistent with statistical model predictions: one accounting for the temperature dependence of the Wigner density during expansion, and another accounting for the change in relative distance between heavy quark and antiquark when no heavy quark potential is implemented in the Monte Carlo approach.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo box simulations of charm and anticharm quarks using the Remler formalism, studying four scenarios: a static thermal box at fixed temperature, a cooling box where initial temperature drops from 400 MeV to 200 MeV, an expanding box where quarks initially confined to a smaller volume diffuse spatially, and a combined cooling-and-expanding scenario mimicking heavy-ion collisions. They compared the time-integrated rate approach against direct Wigner projection and statistical model predictions, identifying where the original formalism fails and testing corrections.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass taken as 1.5 GeV and charmonium radius as 0.5 fm throughout the simulations
- Box size of 100^3 fm^3 used for all simulations; initial confined volume for expansion scenarios was 50^3 fm^3
- Initial temperature of 400 MeV cooling to 200 MeV in the cooling scenario; interaction rate fixed to 1.0 c/fm
- Charmonium abundance at T=200 MeV from the coalescence approach is about 77% of the statistical model abundance for m_Q=1.5 GeV
- The charmonium radius modeled as sqrt(<r^2>) = 0.5 * (T_eff / 0.2 GeV)^2 fm, giving 0.5 fm at T=200 MeV
- Ensemble average of 200 events used for results

## Context

This work extends the Remler formalism, originally applied to deuteron production in heavy-ion collisions by Remler (1975) and Gyulassy (1982), to charmonium production in thermalizing systems. It builds on recent attempts by Villar (2022) and Song (2017) to apply the formalism to quarkonium production, and uses lattice QCD results from Lafferty (2019) showing temperature-dependent J/psi radii. The results provide the basis for future applications of the Remler formalism to heavy-ion collisions, particularly for studying charmonium regeneration in quark-gluon plasma.
