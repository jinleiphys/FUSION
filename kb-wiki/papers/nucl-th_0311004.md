# Nuclear effects in positive pion electroproduction on the deuteron near threshold

> Positive pion electroproduction from the deuteron near threshold has been considered within an approach based on the unitary transformation method. The gauge independence of the treatment is provided by using an explicitly gauge independent expression for the reaction amplitude. The results of calculations for kinematics of the experiments on forward-angle $π^+$ meson electroproduction accomplished at Saclay and Jefferson Laboratory are discussed and compared with those given by the impulse approximation. It is shown that the observed behaviour of the cross sections is in accordance with the calculations based on the pion-nucleon dynamics. In particular, the pion production rate suppression in the $^2H(e,e'π^+)nn$ reaction compared to that for the $^1H(e,e'π^+)n$ one can be due to such ``nuclear medium'' effects as nucleon motion and binding along with Pauli blocking in the final $nn$ state.

# Nuclear effects in positive pion electroproduction on the deuteron near threshold

## Key claim

The observed suppression of positive pion production in the deuteron reaction compared to the proton reaction can be explained by nuclear medium effects (nucleon Fermi motion, binding, and Pauli blocking in the final nn state) rather than primarily by final-state interactions between the outgoing neutrons, contrary to earlier claims by Loucks (1994). The authors show that their unitary transformation method, combined with an extension of the Siegert theorem for gauge independence, yields a ratio R = 0.80 that agrees with the Saclay measurement of 0.80 ± 0.05, while the final-state interaction actually increases the production rate slightly rather than suppressing it.

## Method

The authors apply the unitary transformation method of Okubo (1954) to construct an effective operator for pion electroproduction on the deuteron, separating the amplitude into one-body and two-body contributions. Gauge independence is enforced not by the standard subtraction procedure (8) but by an explicitly gauge-independent expression derived from an extension of the Siegert theorem, expressing the amplitude through generalized electric and magnetic dipole moments. Calculations are performed for the kinematics of the Saclay experiment (Gilman, 1990) and Jefferson Lab experiment E91003 (Gaskell, 2003) using a generalized Born approximation with pseudovector πNN coupling, Paris potential wave functions for the deuteron, and including nn final-state interactions in the ¹S₀ state via the matrix inversion method.

## Key numbers

- Ratio R of forward-angle π⁺ electroproduction cross section on deuteron to that on proton at W = 1160 MeV: measured as 0.80 ± 0.05 (Saclay experiment), calculated as 0.80 by the authors' approach
- Plane-wave impulse approximation gives R = 0.74; impulse approximation with ¹S₀ nn final-state interaction gives R = 0.79
- Calculated d³σₚ/dΩ'dE'dΩ_π for ¹H(e,e'π⁺)n at W = 1160 MeV: 65.3 pb/MeV·sr² (compared to measured 46 ± 3 pb/MeV·sr²)
- Saclay kinematics: incident beam energy E = 645 MeV, electron scattering angle θ = 36°, pion detection angle = 4°, ω = 290 MeV, |q| = 414 MeV/c
- Jefferson Lab E91003 kinematics: q² = -0.4 (GeV/c)², W = 1160 MeV, E = 845 MeV, ω = 450 MeV
- For the Jefferson Lab kinematics, the authors observe up to 20% discrepancy in the ¹S₀ nn FSI peak region between their approach and impulse approximation
- Nuclear medium effects reduce the overall pion production rate by more than 20%

## Context

This work extends the unitary transformation formalism developed by Levchuk and Shebeko (1995, 1999) for photomeson processes on nuclei, providing an alternative to the standard impulse approximation of Goldberger and Watson (1964) which suffers from gauge invariance violations and overdetermined kinematics. The calculations contradict the earlier conclusion of Loucks (1994) that final-state interactions between the outgoing neutrons are the primary cause of pion production suppression; instead, the authors find that Pauli blocking in the final nn state reduces the ratio by approximately the same amount that nn FSI increases it. The approach enables more reliable extraction of neutron pion production amplitudes from deuteron data and can be extended to include Δ₃₃ resonance contributions and three-body dynamics for future Jefferson Lab experiments.
