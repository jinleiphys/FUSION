# Nuclear medium effects on the sigma mass and width

> The meson meson interaction in the scalar isoscalar channel at finite baryonic density is studied in the framework of a chiral unitary approach which generates the f0 and sigma resonances and reproduces well the meson meson phase shifts in vacuum. We investigate the sigma spectral function, its mass and its width as a function of the baryon density and discuss possible experimental signatures in the (pi,2pi) and the (gamma,2pi) reactions.

## Key claim

The sigma meson's mass and width both decrease as baryonic density increases, reaching a mass around 200 MeV and a similar width at 1.5 times normal nuclear density, but the calculation is more reliable at low densities because some contributions of order ρ² or higher are missing.

## Method

The authors solve a Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson-meson scattering in the scalar isoscalar channel, using lowest-order chiral Lagrangians as the potential and regularizing the loop integral with a cutoff of 1.03 GeV. In the nuclear medium, the pion propagator is modified by incorporating p-wave selfenergy from particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations, and the two-pion loop function is evaluated using the Lehmann representation. The sigma pole position is then extracted from the scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane for densities up to 1.5 ρ₀.

## Key numbers

- In vacuum, the sigma pole position occurs at a mass around 470 MeV and a width around 400 MeV.
- At 1.5 times normal nuclear density, the sigma mass reaches around 200 MeV and the half width is similar (around 200 MeV).
- The cutoff used to regularize the two-meson loop integral is Λ = 1.03 GeV.
- The calculation covers densities from ρ₀/8 up to 1.5 ρ₀, with results shown for ρ = ρ₀/8, ρ₀/4, ρ₀/2, ρ₀, and 1.5 ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ππ scattering in nuclear matter by Schuck (1988), Rapp (1996), and Aouissat (1995), who found enhancement of the ππ amplitude near threshold, and by Chiang (1998) using a chiral unitary approach. The results are compared to those of Hatsuda and Kunihiro (2001), who attribute medium modifications to a reduction of fπ, whereas the present authors find the mass decrease is driven by the p-wave interaction of pions with baryons. The paper discusses how these medium effects could be tested in (π,2π) and (γ,2π) reactions, noting that (γ,2π) is more promising because it avoids the strong initial pion distortion that plagues pion-induced reactions.
