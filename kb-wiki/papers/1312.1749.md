# Studies of exotic hadrons by high-energy exclusive reactions

> We investigate the possibility of clarifying internal structure of exotic hadrons by high-energy exclusive reactions. In particular, the constituent-counting rule could be used for determining the internal configuration in large-angle exclusive scattering. As an example, we show the cross section pi^- + p -> K^0 + Lambda(1405) in comparison with the one for the ground-state Lambda production pi^- + p -> K^0 + Lambda. The counting rule indicates that the cross section scales as s^8 dsigma /dt=constant if Lambda(1405) is an ordinary three-quark baryon, whereas it is s^{10} dsigma /dt=constant if Lambda(1405) is a five-quark baryon. Here, s and t are Mandelstam variables. Such experiments could be possible at J-PARC, LEP, JLab, CERN-COMPASS, and other high-energy facilities.

## Key claim

The constituent-counting rule can distinguish whether the Lambda(1405) is an ordinary three-quark baryon or a five-quark exotic state by measuring the asymptotic scaling behavior of the exclusive cross section pi- + p -> K0 + Lambda(1405) at large angles, provided that experiments reach sufficiently high energies where the scaling regime dominates over resonance contributions.

## Method

The authors apply the constituent-counting rule from perturbative QCD, which predicts that the differential cross section for large-angle exclusive scattering scales as dsigma/dt proportional to 1/s^(n-2), where n is the total number of constituent quarks in the initial and final hadrons. They extract existing experimental data for pi- + p -> K0 + Lambda at theta_cm = 90 degrees and fit the scaling exponent to verify the rule for ground-state Lambda production. They then use the single available experimental data point for pi- + p -> K0 + Lambda(1405) together with the counting rule to predict the high-energy scaling behavior for two scenarios: a three-quark Lambda(1405) and a five-quark Lambda(1405).

## Key numbers

- For the ground-state Lambda production pi- + p -> K0 + Lambda, fitting the data yields a scaling exponent n = 10.1 +/- 0.6, consistent with the total constituent count of 10 (2 for pion, 3 for proton, 2 for kaon, 3 for Lambda).
- If Lambda(1405) is an ordinary three-quark baryon, the cross section scales as s^8 dsigma/dt = constant (total constituents n = 10).
- If Lambda(1405) is a five-quark baryon, the cross section scales as s^10 dsigma/dt = constant (total constituents n = 12).
- The transition from hadron degrees of freedom to quark degrees of freedom in gamma + p -> pi+ + n appears at sqrt(s) = 2.5 GeV.
- The Lambda(1405) has mass 1405.1 MeV and width 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the constituent-counting rule, previously tested experimentally at BNL and JLab for processes like gamma + p -> pi+ + n, to the study of exotic hadron candidates. It specifically addresses the long-standing puzzle of Lambda(1405), which the naive quark model cannot explain because its mass is much lighter than the lightest nucleon resonance with (1/2)-, N(1535), leading to proposals that it may be a pentaquark or a Kbar N molecule (exotics, 2013). The approach enables future experiments at J-PARC, LEPS, JLab, and CERN-COMPASS to determine the internal quark configuration of Lambda(1405) and potentially other exotic hadron candidates through high-energy exclusive scattering measurements.
