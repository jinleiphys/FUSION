# Light-quark baryon spectroscopy within ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels approach

> Recent results on the study of light-quark baryons with the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) approach are presented, which contain the N* and Delta* spectroscopy via the analysis of pi N and gamma N reactions and the Lambda* and Sigma* spectroscopy via the analysis of K- p reactions. A recent application of our DCC approach to neutrino-nucleon reactions in the resonance region is also presented.

# Light-quark baryon spectroscopy within ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels approach

## Key claim
The ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) approach can extract resonance parameters for N*, Delta*, Lambda*, and Sigma* baryons from comprehensive multichannel analyses of meson-production reactions, but the existing data for K- p reactions are still insufficient to constrain the extracted partial-wave amplitudes and resonance parameters unambiguously, as demonstrated by the existence of two distinct parameter sets (Model A and Model B) that yield nearly identical chi-squared values.

## Method
The DCC model solves coupled-channels integral equations for partial-wave amplitudes with total angular momentum J, parity P, and total isospin I, incorporating eight channels for the N*/Delta* sector (gamma(*)N, pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, pi Delta, rho N, sigma N) and seven channels for the S=-1 sector (Kbar N, pi Sigma, pi Lambda, eta Lambda, K Xi, pi Sigma*, Kbar* N). The transition potential includes nonresonant terms from effective Lagrangians via unitary transformation, Z-diagram potentials from projection operator methods, and bare N* propagators. Model parameters are determined by fitting to approximately 23,000 data points for pi N and gamma N reactions up to W = 2.1 GeV, and more than 17,000 data points for K- p reactions from threshold to W = 2.1 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Approximately 23,000 data points were fitted for the N* and Delta* sector from pi p -> pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma and gamma p -> pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma up to W = 2.1 GeV (up to W = 2.3 GeV for pi p -> pi N)
- Approximately 3,200 data points were fitted for gamma n -> pi N up to W less than or approximately 2 GeV
- More than 17,000 data points were fitted for K- p -> Kbar N, pi Sigma, pi Lambda, eta Lambda, K Xi from threshold to W = 2.1 GeV
- 18 resonances (10 Lambda* and 8 Sigma*) were found in Model A, while 20 resonances (10 Lambda* and 10 Sigma*) were found in Model B, searched in the complex W region with m_Kbar + m_N <= Re(W) <= 2.1 GeV and 0 <= -Im(W) <= 0.2 GeV
- A new narrow J^P = 3/2+ Lambda* resonance was found only in Model B with pole mass M_R = (1671+2-8, 5+11-2) MeV (real and imaginary parts)
- 9 resonances (6 Lambda* and 3 Sigma*) showed pole masses agreeing between both models within uncertainties, corresponding to PDG resonances including Lambda(1670)1/2-, Lambda(1600)1/2+, Lambda(1520)3/2-, Lambda(1690)3/2-, Lambda(1820)5/2+, Lambda(1830)5/2-, Sigma(1670)3/2-, Sigma(1775)5/2-, and Sigma(2030)7/2+

## Context
This work extends the ANL-Osaka DCC approach previously applied to N* and Delta* spectroscopy (Kamano et al., 2013; Kamano, 2013) into the strangeness S=-1 sector for Lambda* and Sigma* resonances, following the framework developed by Matsuyama, Sato, and Lee (2007). The analysis reveals that four-star PDG resonances rated using Breit-Wigner parameters, such as Lambda(1890)3/2+ and Sigma(1915)5/2+, are not confirmed when resonance parameters are extracted at pole positions via multichannel analyses. The DCC approach has also been newly applied to neutrino-nucleon reactions in the resonance region, providing the first fully coupled-channels calculation of nu N reactions beyond the Delta(1232)3/2+ region, which enables improved neutrino-nucleus reaction models needed for next-generation oscillation experiments at J-PARC and other facilities.
