# Dynamical Structure of Baryons

> Compton scattering offers a unique opportunity to study the dynamical structure of hadrons over a wide kinematic range, with polarizabilities characterizing the hadron active internal degrees of freedom. We present calculations and detailed analysis of electric and magnetic and the spin-dependent dynamical polarizabilities for the lowest in mass SU(3) octet of baryons. These extensive calculations are made possible by the recent implementation of semi-automatized calculations in chiral perturbation theory which allows evaluating polarizabilities from Compton scattering up to next-to-the-leading order. The dependencies for the range of photon energies covering the majority of the meson photoproduction channels are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper presents calculations of electric, magnetic, and spin-dependent dynamical polarizabilities for the entire SU(3) octet of baryons using chiral perturbation theory, but the results are limited to one-loop order without including baryon resonances in the loop calculations, making them only "somewhat consistent" with experimental values.

## Method

The authors used a computational hadronic model (CHM) based on relativistic chiral perturbation theory to calculate Compton scattering structure functions up to one-loop order, then extracted energy-dependent polarizabilities via multipole expansion. The structure-dependent pole contribution from the nucleon Delta-resonance excitation was added separately, and resonance loop contributions were borrowed from the small scale expansion (SSE) approach for comparison with experimental values.

## Key numbers

- Proton static electric polarizability: alpha_p = 10.63 x 10^(-4) fm^3 (from pi-loop 7.38, Delta-pole -0.95, SSE 4.2)
- Proton static magnetic polarizability: beta_p = 1.49 x 10^(-4) fm^3 (from pi-loop -2.20, Delta-pole 3.0, SSE 0.7)
- Proton forward spin polarizability gamma_0(p) = 1.1 x 10^(-4) fm^4 (this work) versus experimental value (-0.90 +/- 0.08 +/- 0.11) x 10^(-4) fm^4
- Proton backward spin polarizability gamma_pi(p) = 6.1 x 10^(-4) fm^4 (this work) versus experimental value (8.0 +/- 1.8) x 10^(-4) fm^4
- PDG averaged experimental values: alpha_p = (12.0 +/- 0.6) x 10^(-4) fm^3, beta_p = (1.9 +/- 0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3, alpha_n = (11.6 +/- 1.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3, beta_n = (3.7 +/- 2.0) x 10^(-4) fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of hyperon polarizabilities by Meissner (using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory) and Kumar (spin-dependent static polarizabilities), and provides the first results for dynamical spin-dependent polarizabilities of hyperons. The dynamical electric and magnetic polarizabilities for hyperons were first calculated in a prior work by the same authors (Aleksejevs and Barkanova). The calculations enable future comparison with experimental hyperon polarizabilities, which remain unmeasured, and the authors identify the inclusion of baryon resonances in loop calculations as the next necessary step.
