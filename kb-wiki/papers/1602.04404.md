# Deuteron-induced nucleon transfer reactions within an ab initio framework: First application to p-shell nuclei

> Background: Low-energy transfer reactions in which a proton is stripped from a deuteron projectile and dropped into a target play a crucial role in the formation of nuclei in both primordial and stellar nucleosynthesis, as well as in the study of exotic nuclei using radioactive beam facilities and inverse kinematics. Ab initio approaches have been successfully applied to describe the $^3$H$(d,n)^4$He and $^3$He$(d,p)^4$He fusion processes. Purpose: An ab initio treatment of transfer reactions would also be desirable for heavier targets. In this work, we extend the ab initio description of $(d,p)$ reactions to processes with light $p$-shell nuclei. As a first application, we study the elastic scattering of deuterium on $^7$Li and the ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li transfer reaction based on a two-body Hamiltonian. Methods: We use the no-core shell model to compute the wave functions of the nuclei involved in the reaction, and describe the dynamics between targets and projectiles with the help of microscopic-cluster states in the spirit of the resonating group method. Results: The shape of the excitation functions for deuteron impinging on ${}^{7}$Li are qualitatively reproduced up to the deuteron breakup energy. The interplay between $d$-$^7$Li and $p$-$^8$Li particle-decay channels determines some features of the ${}^{9}$Be spectrum above the $d$+${}^{7}$Li threshold. Our prediction for the parity of the 17.298 MeV resonance is at odds with the experimental assignment Conclusions: Deuteron stripping reactions with $p$-shell targets can now be computed ab initio, but calculations are very demanding. A quantitative description of the ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li reaction will require further work to include the effect of three-nucleon forces and additional decay channels, and improve the convergence rate of our calculations.

## Key claim

The authors extend the ab initio NCSM/RGM description of (d,p) transfer reactions from s-shell to p-shell targets, specifically to the 7Li(d,p)8Li reaction, but only achieve a qualitative reproduction of the excitation function shape up to the deuteron breakup energy, and their parity assignment for the 17.298 MeV resonance in 9Be disagrees with experiment.

## Method

The authors use the no-core shell model (NCSM) to compute the wave functions of the 7Li target, deuteron projectile, 8Li residual nucleus, and proton, then describe the dynamics between targets and projectiles using microscopic-cluster states in the spirit of the resonating group method (RGM). The calculation is based on a two-body Hamiltonian only, and the authors overcome computational limitations for p-shell targets by generalizing optimized algorithms previously applied to nucleon-nucleus scattering.

## Key numbers

- The main resonant peak in the 9Be spectrum lies at approximately 0.60 MeV above the d+7Li threshold, corresponding to deuterons of approximately 0.78 MeV kinetic energy.
- The calculation reproduces the shape of excitation functions for deuteron impinging on 7Li up to the deuteron breakup energy (approximately 2.2 MeV).
- The 17.298 MeV resonance in 9Be is predicted with a parity at odds with the experimental assignment.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM description of (d,N) transfer reactions introduced by Navratil et al. (2012) for s-shell targets (3H(d,n)4He and 3He(d,p)4He) to p-shell nuclei, enabled by optimized algorithms from Roth (2014) and Langhammer (2015). The 7Li(d,p)8Li reaction is relevant to the cosmic lithium depletion problem and to calibrating 7Be target densities for proton-capture measurements (Adelberger 1998). A quantitative description will require including three-nucleon forces and additional decay channels, and improving convergence.
