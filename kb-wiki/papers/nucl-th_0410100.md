# Inclusive scattering data on light nuclei as a precision tool for the extraction of G_M^n

> We demonstrate that refinements in the analysis of inclusive scattering data on light nuclei enable the extraction of, generally accurate, values of the neutron magnetic form factor G_M^n(Q^2). In particular, a recent parametrization of ep inclusive resonance excitation enables a reliable calculation of the inelastic background, and as a consequence a separation of quasi-elastic and inelastic contributions. A far larger number of data points than previously considered is now available for analysis and enables a more reliable extraction of G_M^n from cross section and R_T data on D and He. The achieved accuracy appears mainly limited by the present uncertainties in the knowledge of proton form factors and by the accuracy of the data.

# Inclusive scattering data on light nuclei as a precision tool for the extraction of \( G_M^n \)

## Key claim

Refinements in the analysis of inclusive electron scattering data on deuterium and helium-4 enable extraction of accurate values of the neutron magnetic form factor \( G_M^n(Q^2) \), but the achieved accuracy is mainly limited by present uncertainties in the knowledge of proton form factors and by the accuracy of the data, not by the method itself.

## Method

The authors separate quasi-elastic (nucleon-elastic) and inelastic (nucleon-inelastic) contributions to inclusive cross sections on D and \( ^4 \)He using a recent parametrization of ep inclusive resonance excitation to compute the inelastic background reliably. They extract the neutron magnetic form factor from the difference between experimental data and the computed inelastic background in the region around the quasi-elastic peak, using a convolution formalism that relates nuclear structure functions to averaged nucleon structure functions via a point-nucleon spectral function \( f^{PN,A} \). The analysis incorporates data from multiple experiments (NE3, NE9, NE11, NE18, and Rock data sets) and tests two different choices for proton form factors (polarization-transfer based and Rosenbluth-separated based) to bracket the systematic uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- For D at \( \bar{Q}^2 = 2.774 \) GeV\(^2\) (E=4.045 GeV, \( \theta=30^\circ \)): extracted \( \alpha_n \) values with error bars from cross section data, with sensitivity to \( \pm 5\% \) variation in \( \alpha_n \) visible only in the immediate neighborhood of the quasi-elastic peak.
- For \( ^4 \)He at \( \bar{Q}^2 = 0.973 \) GeV\(^2\) (E=3.6 GeV, \( \theta=15^\circ \)): computed NI background falls short of data by approximately \( 30\% \) at the first pseudo-resonance peak for the lowest \( Q^2 \) set, reducing to approximately \( 10\% \) for larger \( Q^2 \).
- For D at \( \bar{Q}^2 = 0.972 \) GeV\(^2\) (E=4.045 GeV, \( \theta=15^\circ \)): pseudo-resonance structure clearly visible; for \( \theta \ge 30^\circ \) the pseudo-resonance structure gradually disappears.
- For D at \( \bar{Q}^2 = 10.0 \) GeV\(^2\) (E=20.999 GeV, \( \theta=10^\circ \)): extracted \( \alpha_n \) from Rock data shows relatively large value for E=18.476 GeV subset, not in line with other points in the data set.
- The uncertainty in \( G_E^n \) affects extracted \( \alpha_n \) by less than \( 1\% \) for \( Q^2 \gtrsim 0.5 \) GeV\(^2\).
- The analysis uses a threshold \( Q_0^2 \approx 2.5 \) GeV\(^2\) for validity of the convolution relation, but applications are made down to \( Q^2 = 0.5-1.0 \) GeV\(^2\) with expected less good fits.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Rinat, Taragin, Viviani, earlier report) which neglected inelastic background and severely restricted data points around the quasi-elastic peak. It incorporates new low-\( Q^2 \) quasi-elastic data on \( ^4 \)He from the PhD thesis of J-P. Chen (NE9 data) and recent high-quality inclusive resonance excitation data on the proton from Christy (2004) and Stuart (1998), superseding older SLAC parameterizations. The analysis is complicated by the ongoing discrepancy between proton form factor ratios from Rosenbluth-separated elastic ep cross sections and from polarization transfer measurements (Jones, 2000; Punjabi, 2005), with two-photon exchange contributions (Blunden, 2003) believed to resolve the ratio but not yet applied to correct the cross section data. The method enables extraction of \( G_M^n \) from a far larger number of data points than previously possible, but further improvement requires better proton form factor knowledge, higher quality data, and reduced uncertainty in the inelastic nucleon structure functions.
