# On the dynamical kernels of fermionic equations of motion in strongly-correlated media

> Two-point fermionic propagators in strongly-correlated media are considered with an emphasis on the dynamical interaction kernels of their equations of motion (EOM). With the many-body Hamiltonian confined by a two-body interaction, the EOMs for the two-point fermionic propagators acquire the Dyson form and, before taking any approximation, the interaction kernels decompose into the static and dynamical (time-dependent) contributions. The latter translate to the energy-dependent and the former map to the energy-independent terms in the energy domain. We dwell particularly on the energy-dependent terms, which generate long-range correlations while making feedback on their short-range static counterparts. The origin, forms, and various approximations for the dynamical kernels of one-fermion and two-fermion propagators, most relevant in the intermediate-coupling regime, are discussed. Applications to the electromagnetic dipole response of $^{68,70}$Ni and low-energy quadrupole response of $^{114,116,124}$Sn are presented.

# On the dynamical kernels of fermionic equations of motion in strongly-correlated media

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dynamical (energy-dependent) kernels of fermionic propagator equations of motion, which generate long-range correlations while feeding back on short-range static counterparts, can be truncated at the two-body level via quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC) approximations to achieve a reasonable compromise between accuracy and feasibility for intermediate-coupling regimes. The claim is conditional on using modern effective interactions and on the assumption that three-body forces are adequately captured by the non-perturbative many-body dynamics described by the in-medium fermionic propagators.

## Method

The author derives exact forms of the static and dynamical kernels for one-fermion and two-fermion propagators from the many-body Hamiltonian with two-body interaction, obtaining Dyson equations with self-energies decomposed into energy-independent (Hartree-Fock-like) and energy-dependent (retardation) parts. The dynamical kernel of the one-fermion propagator is expressed via cluster decomposition of the three-fermion Green function, which is then truncated to retain only one-fermion and two-fermion propagators, leading to quasiparticle-vibration coupling vertices for both particle-hole and particle-particle (pairing) phonons. The formalism is applied to compute the electromagnetic dipole response of 68,70Ni and low-energy quadrupole response of 114,116,124Sn using self-consistent relativistic qPVC implementations.

## Context

This work extends the Green function and equation-of-motion methods developed by Martin (1959), Schuck and Ethofer (1973), and Adachi and Schuck (1989), particularly the symmetric forms of dynamical kernels elaborated by Peter Schuck and coauthors. It builds on the ab-initio formulation for superfluid systems by Litvinova (2021) and the response theory for superfluid fermionic systems by Litvinova (2022), which unified normal and pairing phonon modes. The paper enables systematic improvement of beyond-mean-field approaches such as the nuclear field theory of Bohr and Mottelson (1969, 1975) and the quasiparticle-phonon model of Soloviev (1992), by providing a framework to derive qPVC vertices from bare or effective interactions rather than introducing phonons phenomenologically.
