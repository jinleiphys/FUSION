# Scale setting the Möbius Domain Wall Fermion on gradient-flowed HISQ action using the omega baryon mass and the gradient-flow scales $t_0$ and $w_0$

> We report on a sub-percent scale determination using the omega baryon mass and gradient-flow methods. The calculations are performed on 22 ensembles of $N_f=2+1+1$ highly improved, rooted staggered sea-quark configurations generated by the MILC and CalLat Collaborations. The valence quark action used is Möbius Domain-Wall fermions solved on these configurations after a gradient-flow smearing is applied with a flowtime of $t_{\rm gf}=1$ in lattice units. The ensembles span four lattice spacings in the range $0.06 \lesssim a \lesssim 0.15$ fm, six pion masses in the range $130 \lesssim m_π\lesssim 400$ MeV and multiple lattice volumes. On each ensemble, the gradient-flow scales $t_0/a^2$ and $w_0/a$ and the omega baryon mass $a m_Ω$ are computed. The dimensionless product of these quantities is then extrapolated to the continuum and infinite volume limits and interpolated to the physical light, strange and charm quark mass point in the isospin limit, resulting in the determination of $\sqrt{t_0}=0.1422(14)$ fm and $w_0 = 0.1709(11)$ fm with all sources of statistical and systematic uncertainty accounted for. The dominant uncertainty in this result is the stochastic uncertainty, providing a clear path for a few-per-mille uncertainty, as recently obtained by the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper reports a sub-percent determination of the gradient-flow scales sqrt(t0) and w0 using the omega baryon mass, with the dominant uncertainty being stochastic rather than systematic, but notes that for sqrt(t0) there are comparable continuum extrapolation uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on 22 ensembles of Nf=2+1+1 highly improved rooted staggered sea-quark configurations (HISQ) generated by the MILC and CalLat Collaborations, using Mobius domain wall fermions for the valence sector after applying gradient-flow smearing with a fixed flowtime of tgf=1 in lattice units. On each ensemble they computed the gradient-flow scales t0/a^2 and w0/a along with the omega baryon mass a m_Omega, then extrapolated the dimensionless products sqrt(t0) m_Omega and m_Omega w0 to the continuum and infinite volume limits while interpolating to the physical light, strange and charm quark mass point in the isospin limit.

## Key numbers

* sqrt(t0) = 0.1422(14) fm, with statistical uncertainty 0.0009 fm, chiral 0.0002 fm, continuum 0.0005 fm, volume 0.0000 fm, physical-point 0.0002 fm, and model selection 0.0007 fm
* w0 = 0.1709(11) fm, with statistical uncertainty 0.0010 fm, chiral 0.0002 fm, continuum 0.0005 fm, volume 0.0000 fm, physical-point 0.0003 fm, and model selection 0.0001 fm
* The dimensionless product sqrt(t0) m_Omega = 1.205(12) and w0 m_Omega = 1.4483(97)
* Calculations spanned four lattice spacings in the range 0.06 less than or similar to a less than or similar to 0.15 fm and six pion masses in the range 130 less than or similar to m_pi less than or similar to 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the mixed-action approach previously used by the same collaboration for calculations of the nucleon axial coupling g_A (Bouchard 2016, Berkowitz 2017, Chang 2018) and FK/Fpi (Miller 2020) to provide a precise scale setting for future calculations of nucleon form factors and other quantities. The results are consistent with determinations from the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal Collaboration (Borsanyi 2020) and provide a path toward few-per-mille uncertainty through improved stochastic precision, while the use of both gradient-flow scales and the omega baryon mass follows the approach of earlier work by the MILC Collaboration (Bazavov 2015) and others.
