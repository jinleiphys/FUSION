# Robustness of the Baryon-Stopping Signal for the Onset of Deconfinement in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The impact of the experimental acceptance, i.e. transverse-momentum ($p_T$) cut-off and limited rapidity region, on the earlier predicted irregularity in the excitation function of the baryon stopping is studied. This irregularity is a consequence of the onset of deconfinement occurring in the compression stage of a nuclear collision and manifests itself as a wiggle in the excitation function of the reduced curvature ($C_y$) of the net-proton rapidity distribution at midrapidity. It is demonstrated that the wiggle is a very robust signal of a first-order phase transition that survives even under conditions of a very limited acceptance. At the same time the $C_y$ for pure hadronic and crossover transition scenarios become hardly distinguishable, if the acceptance cuts off too much of the low-$p_T$ proton spectrum and/or puts too narrow rapidity window around midrapidity. It is found that the shape of the net-proton rapidity distribution near midrapidity depends on the $p_T$ cut-off. This implies that the measurements should be taken at the same acceptance for all collision energies in order to reliably conclude on the presence or absence of the irregularity.

## Key claim

The wiggle in the excitation function of the reduced curvature (C_y) of the net-proton rapidity distribution at midrapidity is a robust signal of a first-order deconfinement phase transition that survives even under very limited experimental acceptance, but under the same restricted acceptance conditions the C_y for pure hadronic and crossover transition scenarios become hardly distinguishable.

## Method

The authors performed three-fluid dynamics (3FD) simulations of central Au+Au collisions (b=2 fm) using three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, a 2-phase EoS with a first-order phase transition, and a crossover EoS. They applied four different acceptance windows for transverse momentum (p_T) and rapidity (y) to the simulated net-proton rapidity distributions, including the expected NICA MPD acceptance (0.4<p_T<1 GeV/c, |y|<0.5) and STAR acceptance (0.4<p_T<3 GeV/c, |y|<0.5), and compared the resulting reduced curvature C_y excitation functions with experimental data from AGS and SPS experiments.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 60% of protons produced at midrapidity are covered by the MPD acceptance window (0.4<p_T<1 GeV/c, |y|<0.5), while the STAR acceptance (0.4<p_T<3 GeV/c, |y|<0.5) covers approximately 80%.
- The wiggle in the C_y excitation function for the first-order phase transition scenario survives even under the MPD acceptance conditions (0.4<p_T<1 GeV/c, |y|<0.5) and STAR acceptance conditions (0.4<p_T<3 GeV/c, |y|<0.5).
- The hadronic-EoS C_y excitation functions under MPD and STAR acceptance conditions exhibit a local weak wiggle in the region 8 < sqrt(s_NN) < 12 GeV, qualitatively similar to that for the crossover transition.
- The C_y curvature for the hadronic EoS becomes negative at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV under the restricted acceptance conditions (except for the high-p_T range 1<p_T<2 GeV/c).

## Context

This work extends the earlier predictions of Ivanov (2010, 2011, 2012, 2013) that baryon stopping can probe the onset of deconfinement, which were made assuming full acceptance for net-protons. The study addresses the practical constraints of the NICA MPD experiment (Merz, 2012) and the STAR beam energy scan (Nu Xu, 2015), showing that the wiggle signal survives restricted acceptance for first-order transitions but that hadronic and crossover scenarios become ambiguous. The authors note that hybrid fluid models, unlike the 3FD model, cannot produce this irregularity because they use the same hadronic kinetic model for the initial state regardless of the EoS, similar to the situation with directed flow discussed in Ivanov (2014).
