# Recent Results on the Tetraneutron

> We describe the recent experiments which claimed an observation of a tetra-neutron signal. Production reactions like transfer, knockout, fragmentation or photodisintegration have been used at very different experiments and facilities to form systems just made of neutrons. As a possible explanation of the partly contradicting results we suggest that some observed the bound ground state and some an unbound but still correlated state of the four neutrons at different exitation energy. We also refer to some of the theoretical works.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the partly contradictory experimental results on the tetraneutron can be explained if some experiments observed a bound ground state and others an unbound but still correlated excited state of the four-neutron system at different excitation energies, a view they acknowledge is in conflict with nearly all published theoretical results.

## Method

The paper reviews recent experiments that used transfer, knockout, fragmentation, and photodisintegration reactions at facilities including GANIL (France), RIKEN (Japan), the Technical University of Munich (Germany), the Alikanyan National Science Laboratory (Armenia), and JINR (Russia) to produce systems made of four neutrons. The authors compare the reported energies and widths of the observed tetraneutron signals and examine whether the different results can be reconciled by assuming the population of different states. They also discuss theoretical approaches including Green's function Monte-Carlo calculations, no-core shell model calculations, chiral effective field theory, continuum calculations, and a new energy density functional approach from Lenske.

## Key numbers

- Kisamori et al. (2016): resonance at 0.83 ± 0.65(stat) ± 1.25(syst) MeV above the four-neutron threshold, with an upper limit on width Γ ≤ 2.6 MeV (FWHM), from the ⁴He(⁸He,⁸Be)⁴n reaction at 186 MeV/nucleon
- Faestermann et al. (2022): a bound tetraneutron with binding energy E_B = 0.42 ± 0.16 MeV and width less than 0.24 MeV (FWHM), from the ⁷Li(⁷Li,¹⁰C)⁴n reaction with a 46 MeV ⁷Li beam
- Duer et al. (2022): resonance at E_4n = 2.37 ± 0.38(stat) ± 0.44(syst) MeV with width Γ = 1.75 ± 0.22(stat) ± 0.30(syst) MeV, from the ⁸He(p,pα) reaction at 156 MeV/nucleon
- Muzalevskii et al. (2025): humps in the E_4n spectrum at 3.5 ± 0.7 MeV and 3.2 ± 0.8 MeV from the ²H(⁸He,⁶Li)⁴n and ²H(⁸He,³He)⁷H reactions at 26 MeV/nucleon
- Kotanjyan et al. (2023): weighted cross section σ_w = 0.442(0.048) μb for the ²⁰⁹Bi(γ,4n)²⁰⁵Bi reaction at 30 MeV electron energy, with an upper limit of σ ≤ 0.2 μb at 28 MeV
- The weighted mean of the Duer et al. and Muzalevskii et al. energies is 2.92 ± 0.39 MeV
- The average excitation energy of the 2⁺ state for the four even-even N=4 neighbours (⁶He, ⁸Be, ¹⁰C, ¹²O) is E* = 2.5 ± 0.7 MeV (RMS)

## Context

This review extends the earlier review by Marques and Carbonell (2021) and attempts to reconcile the conflicting experimental results from Kisamori et al. (2016), Faestermann et al. (2022), Duer et al. (2022), and Muzalevskii et al. (2025). The authors suggest that the bound ground state seen by Faestermann et al. and the unbound resonance seen by Duer et al. and Muzalevskii et al. could represent the 0⁺ ground state and 2⁺ first excited state of the tetraneutron, respectively. This interpretation is in conflict with nearly all theoretical results, including those from Pieper (2003), Shirokov et al. (2016), Gandolfi et al. (2017), and Lazauskas et al. (2023), who interpret the low-energy bump as originating from final-state interaction of two dineutrons. The paper enables future experiments to verify or falsify the existence of a bound tetraneutron state, with additional experiments already performed at RIBF awaiting full analysis.
