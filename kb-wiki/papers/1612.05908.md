# Time Dependent $\hat{q}$ from AdS/CFT

> We present the first ever AdS/CFT calculation of $\hat{q}$ for a light quark jet as a function of position or, equivalently, time. Our result does not suffer from the gamma factor blow up of the usual time-independent AdS/CFT heavy quark setup and is qualitatively similar to, but differs by $\sim\mathcal{O}(1)$ factor from, the light flavor result of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann. Our findings can be immediately implemented into any $\hat{q}$-based energy loss model. Our $\hat{q}$ derivation relies on our calculation of the average distance squared, $s^2(t)$, travelled by the endpoint of a string falling in an AdS$_3$-Schwarzschild spacetime. The early time behavior is ballistic, $s^2(t)\sim t^2$, but the late time behavior is the usual diffusive Brownian motion, $s^2(t)\sim t$. These late time dynamics are universal and depend only on the near-horizon physics, which allows us to generalize our results to arbitrary dimensions and thus make contact with the physics explored by RHIC and LHC. Additionally, we find that AdS/CFT predicts angular ordering for radiation in medium, just as in vacuum, and in contradistinction to weak-coupling, with its anti-angular ordering prediction. Finally, our results also imply, sensibly, that AdS/CFT predicts a smooth interpolation between the angular correlations of open heavy flavor and light flavor observables.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first AdS/CFT calculation of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) for a light quark as a function of time (or position), which avoids the gamma factor divergence of previous time-independent heavy quark setups; the result is qualitatively similar to, but differs by an \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) factor from, the light flavor result of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann.

## Method

The author derives the average distance squared \(s^2(t)\) traveled by the free endpoint of a string falling in an AdS\(_3\)-Schwarzschild spacetime, then generalizes to arbitrary dimensions using the universality of late-time near-horizon dynamics. The classical solution for a straight, vertically stretched string with one endpoint fixed near the black hole horizon and the other freely falling is obtained; perpendicular fluctuations are quantized and populated semiclassically via Bose statistics. The diffusion coefficient \(D\) is extracted from the late-time diffusive behavior \(s^2(t) \sim 2Dt\), and \(\hat{q}\) is related to \(D\) via \(\hat{q} = 4T^2/(vD)\), where \(v\) is the quark speed and \(a\) is a parameter controlling the fraction of the local speed of light at which the endpoint falls (\(a=0\) for heavy quark, \(a=1\) for light quark).

## Key numbers

* For small virtuality at early times (\(t \ll \beta\)), the ballistic motion gives \(s^2(t) \sim t^2/(6\sqrt{\lambda})\).
* For small virtuality at late times (\(\beta \ll t\)), the diffusive motion gives \(s^2(t) \sim (\beta t)/(\pi \sqrt{\lambda})(1 - a/2)\).
* The diffusion coefficient in \(d\) dimensions is \(D(a,d) = (d-1)^2 \beta/(8\pi\sqrt{\lambda})(1 - a/2)\).
* For heavy quarks (\(a=0\)), \(\hat{q}_{\text{MH}}^{\text{Heavy Quarks}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\lambda} T^3 / v\), which remains finite as \(v \to 1\).
* For highly energetic light quarks, \(\hat{q}_{\text{MH}}^{\text{Light Quarks}} \simeq 2\pi\sqrt{\lambda} T^3 \approx 6.2\sqrt{\lambda} T^3\), compared to the Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann result of \(\pi^{3/2}\Gamma(3/4)/\Gamma(5/4) \approx 7.5\sqrt{\lambda} T^3\).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering Brownian motion calculations for static heavy quarks in AdS/CFT by de Boer et al. (2008) and Atmaja (2010) to light quarks, and provides a time-dependent \(\hat{q}\) that can be implemented into any \(\hat{q}\)-based energy loss model. The result contradicts the anti-angular ordering prediction of weak-coupling in-medium radiation (Mehtar-Tani, 2010) and instead predicts angular ordering for radiation in medium, as in vacuum. The findings enable smooth interpolation between heavy flavor and light flavor angular correlations and can be generalized to arbitrary dimensions to connect with RHIC and LHC physics.
