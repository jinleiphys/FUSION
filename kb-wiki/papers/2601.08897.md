# Comment on "Spin-trap isomers in deformed, odd-odd nuclei in the light rare-earth region near N = 98"

> Mass spectrometry studies of odd-odd light rare-earth nuclei by Orford et al. [Phys. Rev. C 102, 011303(R) (2020)] suggested the existence of new isomers in the neutron rich isotopes $^{162}$Tb and $^{164}$Tb. More recently, Stryjczyk et al. [Phys. Rev. C 111, 049801 (2025)] commented on the former, citing inconsistencies between the available experimental data and the proposed presence of an isomer in $^{162}$Tb. To further examine the possibility of isomeric states in $^{162,164}$Tb, we employed the well-tested empirical Two Quasiparticle Rotor Model to construct their low-lying level structure. The resulting level schemes support the potential existence of low-lying isomeric states in both isotopes and we propose their corresponding spin-parities, orbital configurations, and excitation energies.

## Key claim

The Two Quasiparticle Rotor Model calculations support the existence of low-lying spin-trap isomers in both \(^{162}\)Tb and \(^{164}\)Tb, but for \(^{164}\)Tb the predicted isomer is a \(1^{-}\) state at approximately 66 keV rather than the \(2^{+}\) state at 170 keV proposed by Orford et al. (2020).

## Method

The authors employed the semi-empirical Two Quasiparticle Rotor Model (TQRM) to construct the low-lying level structures of \(^{162}\)Tb and \(^{164}\)Tb. First, they mapped the systematics of single-particle proton and neutron orbital energies from neighboring isotopes and isotones, then determined the spin-parities of two-quasiparticle bandheads (Gallagher-Moszkowski doublets) formed by coupling these orbitals. The energies of the two-quasiparticle states were calculated using the TQRM expression incorporating experimental single-particle excitation energies, a rotational energy correction term, and the proton-neutron residual interaction defined through Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting and Newby odd-even shift.

## Key numbers

- In \(^{162}\)Tb, the model places the \(J^{\pi}=4^{-}\) singlet state of the ground-state Gallagher-Moszkowski doublet at an excitation energy of approximately 194 keV.
- In \(^{164}\)Tb, the model places the \(J^{\pi}=1^{-}\) state at approximately 66 keV, which is proposed as the spin-trap isomer.
- For \(^{164}\)Tb, the model admits eight two-quasiparticle bandheads below \(E_x = 400\) keV.
- The adopted Q(\(\beta^{-}\)) value for \(^{162}\)Tb from AME20 is 2301(22) keV, while Kawade et al. (1977) reported Q(\(\beta^{-}\)) = 2580(22) keV from \(\beta\)-\(\gamma\) coincidence studies.

## Context

This work extends the mass spectrometry studies of Orford et al. (2020), who predicted isomers in \(^{162}\)Tb and \(^{164}\)Tb, and responds to the comment by Stryjczyk et al. (2025) questioning the existence of the \(^{162}\)Tb isomer based on inconsistencies with S\(_{2n}\) curves and \(\beta\)-decay reports. The calculated level schemes resolve discrepancies in the evaluated data sheets for \(^{162}\)Tb, which list the \(J^{\pi}=4^{-}\) level at two different energies (216 keV from Burke et al. (2007) and 284 keV from Orford et al. (2020)), and provide the first theoretical predictions for the unmeasured \(^{164}\)Tb level structure. The results enable future dedicated \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) spectroscopy studies with internal conversion measurements, for which the authors' assignments can serve as location guides.
