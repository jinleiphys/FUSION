# Inclusive Cross-Sections of (p, xp) and (p, x$α$) Reactions on $^{56}$Fe Nucleus at E$_{p}$=29.9 MeV

> In this paper, we present new experimental data measured at E$_{p}$=29.9 MeV for the inclusive reactions (p,xp) and (p,x$α$) on nucleus $^{56}$Fe. We investigate the adequacy of the theoretical models in explaining the measured experimental data and we determine the contributions of multi-step direct and multi-step compound processes in the formation of the cross-sections. We show that the traditional frameworks are valid for the description of the new experimental data and our measurements are in agreement with the previous measurements for the (p,xp) and (p,x$α$) reactions on the $^{54}$Fe nucleus. The only exception is within the energy region of E$_{p}$=15 and 25 MeV for both reactions, where the cross-section for the $^{56}$Fe nucleus is smaller than the cross-section for the $^{54}$Fe nucleus.

## Key claim

The new experimental data for (p,xp) and (p,xα) reactions on ⁵⁶Fe at 29.9 MeV are generally consistent with previous measurements on ⁵⁴Fe, except in the energy region between 15 and 25 MeV where the cross-section for ⁵⁶Fe is smaller than that for ⁵⁴Fe, indicating a limited isotope dependence rather than a complete absence.

## Method

Double-differential cross-sections for (p,xp) and (p,xα) reactions on a self-supporting ⁵⁶Fe target were measured using a proton beam at 29.9 MeV from the U-150M isochronous cyclotron, over laboratory angles from 30° to 135° in 15° steps. Reaction products were identified via the ΔE-E method using silicon surface-barrier detectors and a CsI(Tl) crystal, with total energy resolution of 400 keV. The experimental spectra were analyzed within the Griffin exciton model using the code PRECO-D2 and the Hauser-Feshbach theory using the code EMPIRE-II, separating multi-step direct (MSD) and multi-step compound (MSC) contributions.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 29.9 MeV, measured with 1.2% uncertainty.
- Angular range: 30° to 135° in 15° intervals in the laboratory system.
- Total energy resolution: 400 keV.
- Systematic uncertainties: target thickness (~7%), current integrator calibration (~1%), solid angle (~1.3%), registration angle (<0.5%); total systematic error <10%.
- Statistical uncertainties: <10% for protons and <20% for α particles in the high-energy region of the spectra.
- Solid angles of telescopes: 2.72 × 10⁻⁵ sr for the (p,xα) reaction and 2.59 × 10⁻⁵ sr for the (p,xp) reaction.
- Single-particle level density parameter: g = A/13, where A = 56.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements on ⁵⁴Fe at 29.0 and 39.0 MeV by the same group (Duisebayev et al., reference [106]) and complements studies by a Japanese group (reference [ref3]) on ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Fe at 26.0 MeV using the FKK-GNASH code. The results provide new experimental data for ⁵⁶Fe at 29.9 MeV, a region not previously studied in detail, and validate the use of traditional pre-equilibrium models (exciton model, Hauser-Feshbach theory) for describing inclusive proton- and alpha-induced reactions on iron isotopes, enabling improved modeling for accelerator-driven systems and nuclear transmutation applications.
