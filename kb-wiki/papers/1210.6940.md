# Single photon production $ν_l N\rightarrow ν_l N γ$ in neutrino-nucleon scattering

> The quasielastic charged current (CCQE) $ν_e n \rightarrow e^- p$ scattering is the dominant mechanism to detect appearance of a $ν_e$ in an almost $ν_μ$ flux at the 1 GeV scale. Actual experiments show a precision below 1% and between less known background contributions, but necessary to constraint the event excess, we have the radiative corrections. A consistent model recently developed for the simultaneous description of elastic and radiative $πN$ scattering, pion-photoproduction and single pion production processes, both for charged and neutral current neutrino-nucleon scattering, is extended for the evaluation of the radiative $ν_l N\rightarrow ν_l N γ$ cross section. Our results are similar to a previous (but inconsistent) theoretical evaluation in the low energy region, and show an increment in the upper region where the $Δ$ resonance becomes relevant.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously developed consistent model for elastic and radiative πN scattering to evaluate the ν_l N → ν_l N γ cross section, finding results similar to a previous inconsistent theoretical evaluation at low energies but showing an increment in the upper energy region where the Δ resonance becomes relevant.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutral current single photon production cross section by considering only the resonant amplitude contribution from the Δ(1232) resonance, using a set of reduced A-independent Feynman rules to avoid inconsistencies in the Δ propagator and vertex. They employ a consistent Δ propagator (with parameter A = -1/3) and a Δ → γ N decay vertex extracted through the CVC hypothesis, computing the total cross section via a five-dimensional phase space integral. The isospin factor for both ν_μ p → ν_μ p γ and ν_μ n → ν_μ n γ processes is 2/3.

## Key numbers

- The NC1γ cross section is roughly one hundred times smaller than the NC1π⁰ cross section, since g_{ΔNγ} = √(4π/137) << f_{ΔNπ} = √(4π × 0.317).
- The calculation covers the neutrino energy range 0 ≤ E_ν ≤ 1.5 GeV, relevant for MiniBooNE and SciBooNE experiments.
- The Δ resonance mass is 1232 MeV.

## Context

This work extends a consistent model previously developed by the same authors for elastic and radiative πN scattering, pion-photoproduction, and single pion production in neutrino-nucleon scattering (Barbero and Mariano, 2008). It addresses inconsistencies in an earlier calculation by Hill (2009), which used different values of the contact transformation parameter A for the Δ vertex and propagator. The results enable a more reliable reanalysis of whether radiative corrections provide enough photons to explain the low-energy excess of electron-like events observed by MiniBooNE.
