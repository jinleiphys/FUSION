# Particle-vibration coupling for giant resonances beyond the diagonal approximation

> A self-consistent particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model without diagonal approximation is presented. The diagonal approximation, that neglects completely the interaction between the doorway states, has been removed by taking into account the interaction between the two particle-holes inside the doorway states. As applications, isoscalar giant monopole, dipole, and quadrupole resonances in 16O are investigated based on the use of Skyrme functionals. The diagonal approximation is found to clearly impact the strength distribution of the giant quadrupole resonance, and the description of the experimental data is improved without this approximation. The impact of the diagonal approximation is analyzed in detail, especially its effect on the eigenenergies and the induced coupling between neutron and proton particle-hole configurations. The latter is a direct and physically sound effect of the improvement on our formalism. The importance of using self-consistently the full effective interaction in the PVC vertex, and the effect of its renormalization via the subtraction method, are also discussed. For completeness, we also analyze the dependence of our results on the Skyrme parametrization.

## Key claim

The diagonal approximation in particle-vibration coupling (PVC) models, which neglects interactions between doorway states, significantly impacts the strength distribution of giant resonances, especially the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), and removing this approximation improves agreement with experimental data, though the effect is less pronounced for the isoscalar giant monopole and dipole resonances.

## Method

The authors developed a self-consistent PVC model that removes the diagonal approximation by including the interaction between the two particle-holes inside the doorway states (1p-1h ⊗ phonon configurations), using the full Skyrme effective interaction for both the mean field and the PVC vertex. They applied the model to calculate the strength distributions of isoscalar giant monopole, dipole, and quadrupole resonances in 16O using the SAMi Skyrme functional, comparing results with and without the diagonal approximation and with experimental data. The subtraction method was employed to renormalize the effective interaction and avoid double-counting of many-body contributions.

## Key numbers

- The energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for ISGMR, ISGDR, and ISGQR in 16O is 100% fulfilled at the RPA level with a single-particle energy cut-off of 80 MeV.
- The centroid shift from RPA to PVC is a maximum of about 1.5 MeV for the ISGQR case.
- Experimental data for 16O shows (48 ± 10)% of EWSR for ISGMR, (32 ± 7)% for ISGDR, and (53 ± 10)% for ISGQR in the excitation energy region from 11 to 40 MeV.
- The smearing parameter ε in the PVC calculation was chosen as 0.25 MeV.
- Phonons up to multipolarity 5⁻ with energy cut-off 30 MeV and strength fraction cut-off 2% were included in the doorway states.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent PVC studies (Colò 1994, Roca-Maza 2017) that employed the diagonal approximation, and is analogous to tests of the diagonal approximation in second RPA (Gambacurta 2010) and relativistic time-blocking approximation (Litvinova 2010, Litvinova 2013). The removal of the diagonal approximation enables a more realistic description of giant resonance widths and strength distributions, particularly for the ISGQR, and provides a framework for systematic studies of higher-order correlations beyond the 2p-2h level.
