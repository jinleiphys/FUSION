# Causal UV completions of relativistic hydrodynamics

> Relativistic hydrodynamics successfully provides an effective field theory description for the low energy regime of many out-of-equilibrium systems. On the other hand, in this paper we proof that any stand-alone hydrodynamic EFT is inherently acausal and therefore requires the addition of transient UV modes in order to restore causality. This is made possible by the exponential decay of dissipative hydrodynamics in a majority of the lightcone, allowing the possibility of a causal description that still reduces to the hydrodynamic one at late timescales. We then investigate the emergence and possible restrictions of the non-hydrodynamic modes in these causal UV completions.

# Causal UV completions of relativistic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Any stand-alone hydrodynamic effective field theory is inherently acausal and requires the addition of transient UV modes to restore causality; however, because dissipative hydrodynamics exhibits exponential decay in most of the lightcone, a causal description that still reduces to the hydrodynamic one at late timescales is always possible for dispersion relations with subluminal sound speed and finite radius of convergence.

## Method

The paper proves three mathematical theorems about retarded correlation functions and their singularities in complex frequency space (the modes of the system). First, it demonstrates that any dissipative hydrodynamic dispersion relation (with ω(0)=0 and Im ω(k)<0 for real nonzero k) produces a correlation function with support outside the lightcone, using analyticity arguments and theorems from Heller (2022) and Heller (2023). Second, it derives the exponential decay rate Γ(v) of hydrodynamic correlation functions along radial lines x=vt using saddle-point methods, showing Γ(v)>0 for all v≠c_s where c_s=ω'(0) is the sound speed. Third, it constructs a causal UV completion by cutting the hydrodynamic mode to a finite interval in k-space, multiplying by θ(t²−x²) to enforce causality, and showing the removed tails decay exponentially and can be re-added as UV modes.

## Key numbers

- For pure diffusion with dispersion relation ω(k)=−iDk², the correlation function along a radial line x=vt decays as G(t,vt) ∝ exp(−v²t/(4D)), giving decay rate Γ(v)=v²/(4D).
- For pure diffusion, the maximal imaginary gap of the non-hydrodynamic sector is Im ω = −1/(4D) at real k, setting a lower bound on the thermalization timescale τ_R ≥ 4D.
- The sound speed c_s must satisfy |c_s|<1 for a causal UV completion to exist; dissipative modes with |c_s|≥1 violate the causality condition Im ω(k) ≤ |Im k| in any complex neighborhood of k=0.
- The causal completion for diffusion produces branch cuts at ω ∈ ±k − i[1/(4D), ∞).

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining relativistic hydrodynamics using causality, following the hydrohedron framework of Heller (2023) and the acausality proofs of Hiscock (1983, 1985) and Gavassino (2021). It contrasts with approaches like MIS theory (Muller 1967, Israel 1976, Israel 1979) and the BDNK framework (Bemfica 2017, Kovtun 2019, Bemfica 2019), which modify the hydrodynamic dispersion relation itself with higher-gradient corrections. Instead, this paper constructs UV completions that leave the hydrodynamic mode as an exact mode below a cutoff, similar in spirit to the bootstrap approach of Gavassino (2026). The results imply that causality does not directly restrict the hydrodynamic dispersion relation itself, but rather restricts the regime of applicability of hydrodynamics (the interval K in k-space where the hydrodynamic mode remains the slowest decaying mode).
