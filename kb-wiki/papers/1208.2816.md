# Spinodal Instabilities in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter Based on Realistic $NN$ Interactions

> A density dependent relativistic mean-field model is determined to reproduce the components of the nucleon self-energy at low densities. This model is used to investigate spinodal instabilities in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures. The inhomogeneous density distributions in the spinodal region are investigated through calculations in a cubic Wigner-Seitz cell. Compared to results obtained in phenomenological calculations the spinodal region is large, i.e. the spinodal region at zero temperature can reach densities above 0.12 fm$^{-3}$. The predicted spinodal region is concentrated around isospin symmetric nuclear matter and the critical temperature is considerably lower than in the previous microscopic based investigation within a non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach.

## Key claim

The authors construct a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) model that reproduces Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) self-energies at low densities, and find that the spinodal instability region in asymmetric nuclear matter is larger than in phenomenological models but has a lower critical temperature than previous microscopic calculations. The hedge is that the spinodal region at zero temperature can reach densities above 0.12 fm$^{-3}$, but this result depends on the specific DBHF parameterization using the Bonn A potential and is concentrated around isospin-symmetric matter.

## Method

The authors fit density-dependent coupling functions for sigma, omega, rho, and delta mesons to reproduce the nucleon self-energy components from DBHF calculations (Bonn A potential) at five densities between 0.017 and 0.467 fm$^{-3}$ for a proton fraction of 0.4. They then compute the free energy curvature matrix in the neutron-proton density plane to identify spinodal instability regions at temperatures from 0 to 10 MeV, and perform variational calculations in a cubic Wigner-Seitz cell of side 20 fm to study inhomogeneous density distributions in isospin-symmetric matter.

## Key numbers

- Maximum spinodal density at zero temperature: 0.127 fm$^{-3}$, larger than the phenomenological limit of below 0.1 fm$^{-3}$
- Saturation density of the DDRH model: 0.1759 fm$^{-3}$, compared to 0.181 fm$^{-3}$ in the original DBHF calculation
- Binding energy at saturation: -14.39 MeV in the DDRH model, compared to -16.15 MeV in DBHF
- Compressibility modulus: 193 MeV in DDRH, compared to 230 MeV in DBHF
- Symmetry energy: 35.03 MeV in DDRH, compared to 34.16 MeV in DBHF
- Critical temperature: below 11 MeV, compared to 17.5 MeV in the non-relativistic BHF calculation of Vidana and Polls (2008)
- At density 0.125 fm$^{-3}$ and zero temperature, the density distribution in the Wigner-Seitz cell resembles homogeneous nuclear matter

## Context

This work extends microscopic studies of spinodal instabilities by Margueron et al. (2007) using DBHF and by Vidana and Polls (2008) using non-relativistic BHF with the Argonne V18 potential, but improves on the earlier DBHF-based functional by using density-dependent couplings rather than a density expansion. The results confirm that spinodal instabilities are dominated by isoscalar density fluctuations (mechanical instabilities) as found in phenomenological studies by Margueron et al. (2003) and Chomaz et al. (2004), but predict a larger spinodal region than those phenomenological models. The lower critical temperature and higher spinodal densities near the critical point enable more precise modeling of the liquid-gas phase transition in supernova collapse and neutron star crust formation.
