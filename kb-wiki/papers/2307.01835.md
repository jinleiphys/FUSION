# Reaction dynamics in clustered nuclear systems

> This work is concerned with the theoretical study of nuclear reactions between light charged ions at incident energies around and below the reactants Coulomb barrier, with a focus on the energy range of astrophysical interest for Big Bang and quiescent stellar processes. The main goal of the analysis is to investigate the sensitivity of nuclear reaction dynamics, and more specifically the cross-section predictions, to the description of the structure of each reactant, and in particular to clustering phenomena. The case of the $^6$Li + p $\to$ $^3$He + $^4$He reaction was investigated explicitly, treating the process as a direct deuteron transfer in first- or second-order distorted-wave Born approximation and through a coupled-reaction-channels approach. The penetrability of the Coulomb barrier in the initial state was also analysed phenomenologically from available experimental data, and studied theoretically taking into account the $^6$Li ground-state deformation.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the description of nuclear clustering in light reactants affects cross-section predictions for sub-Coulomb reactions, specifically testing whether the observed anomalous electron screening enhancement in the \(^6\)Li + p → \(^3\)He + \(^4\)He reaction might be connected to clustering phenomena, but the analysis is limited to theoretical sensitivity studies without claiming to resolve the screening anomaly.

## Method

The \(^6\)Li + p → \(^3\)He + \(^4\)He reaction was treated as a direct deuteron transfer using first- and second-order distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) and a coupled-reaction-channels approach, with the cross-section evaluated over the sub-Coulomb energy range from a few keV to about 1.5 MeV centre-of-mass energy. The Coulomb barrier penetrability in the initial state was also analysed phenomenologically from experimental data and studied theoretically by incorporating \(^6\)Li ground-state deformation within a di-cluster model, using a semi-classical approach improved from Spitaleri (2016).

## Key numbers

- The cross-section was evaluated for centre-of-mass incident energies between a few keV and about 1.5 MeV, covering the sub-Coulomb range up to the resonance corresponding to the second \(\frac{5}{2}^-\) state of \(^7\)Be.
- The effective nuclear radius was defined using the scaling \(R_n = r_0 \cdot (A_1^{1/3} + A_2^{1/3})\), with the reduced radius \(r_0\) taken as a phenomenological constant of the order of 1 femtometre.
- The transmission coefficient for sharp-edge nuclear potentials was derived in the limit of strong nuclear potential (\(\tilde k \to +\infty\)), where the term involving the derivative of the Coulomb function may be neglected.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of clustering effects on low-energy reaction dynamics, such as the Faddeev calculation by Vasilevsky (2009) for the \(^6\)Li + p → \(^3\)He + \(^4\)He reaction including dynamic excitations, and addresses the atomic electron screening problem noted by Spitaleri (2016), where experimental cross-section enhancements exceed atomic theory predictions. The results enable future comparisons between fully quantum cluster-model calculations and experimental data for light-ion reactions of astrophysical interest, particularly for nucleosynthesis and stellar evolution modelling, and provide analytical expressions for overlap functions and cluster-model observables that were previously unavailable for systems of three or more clusters.
