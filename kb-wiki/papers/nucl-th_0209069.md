# Non-Linear Vibrations in Nuclei

> We have perfomed Time Dependant Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on the non linear response of nuclei. We have shown that quadrupole (and dipole) motion produces monopole (and quadrupole) oscillations in all atomic nuclei. We have shown that these findings can be interpreted as a large coupling between one and two phonon states leading to large anharmonicities.

## Key claim

Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations show that quadrupole and dipole motion induces monopole and quadrupole oscillations in all atomic nuclei, which can be interpreted as a large coupling between one- and two-phonon states leading to strong anharmonicities; however, the TDHF approach omits two-body dissipation and correlations, and the extracted coupling coefficients are about 50% larger than those reported in the boson-mapping approach of Fallot et al. (2001).

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations using the SGII and SLy4d Skyrme interactions, applying collective boosts (monopole, quadrupole, and isovector dipole) to spherical nuclei and propagating the time evolution of the one-body density matrix. They extracted the linear and quadratic responses by monitoring the time-dependent expectation values of monopole, quadrupole, and dipole moments, and derived the residual interaction matrix elements coupling one-phonon to two-phonon states from the amplitude of the induced oscillations.

## Key numbers

*   In $^{40}$Ca, the GMR energy $\omega_0 = 22.9$ MeV, transition probability $q_0 = 11.6$ fm$^2$, and energy-weighted sum-rule $S_1 = 3090$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^4$.
*   In $^{40}$Ca, the GQR energy $\omega_2 = 18.6$ MeV, $q_2 = 21.4$ fm$^2$, $S_1 = 9020$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^4$, and the coupling $<\nu|V|\nu 0> = -4.28$ MeV.
*   In $^{40}$Ca, the GDR energy $\omega_D = 17.2$ MeV, $q_D = 3.47$ fm, $S_1 = 199$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^2$, and couplings $<\nu|V|\nu 0> = -4.58$ MeV and $<\nu|V|\nu 2> = -3.92$ MeV.
*   In $^{208}$Pb, the GMR energy $\omega_0 = 15.7$ MeV, $q_0 = 57.1$ fm$^2$, $S_1 = 51200$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^4$.
*   In $^{208}$Pb, the GQR energy $\omega_2 = 11.1$ MeV, $q_2 = 99.0$ fm$^2$, $S_1 = 128000$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^4$, and coupling $<\nu|V|\nu 0> = -2.17$ MeV.
*   In $^{208}$Pb, the GDR energy $\omega_D = 13.0$ MeV, $q_D = 8.94$ fm, $S_1 = 877$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^2$, and couplings $<\nu|V|\nu 0> = -2.40$ MeV and $<\nu|V|\nu 2> = -0.70$ MeV.
*   For $^{40}$Ca with the SLy4d force, the coupling exciting the GMR on top of the GDR is $-4.01$ MeV, and on top of the GQR is $-4.36$ MeV; the quadrupole response during dipole oscillation gives a residual interaction of $-3.98$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier TDHF study of Simenel et al. (2001) on dipole coupling in fusion reactions to the ground-state response, and provides an independent confirmation of the large one- to two-phonon couplings predicted by the boson-mapping approach of Fallot et al. (2001), which had been questioned due to possible Pauli principle violations. The results demonstrate that TDHF captures the 3-particle-1-hole and 3-hole-1-particle residual interaction terms responsible for anharmonicities, and the extracted coupling coefficients are systematically about 50% larger than those from the boson-mapping method, which the authors attribute to the weighted sum over fragmented states in TDHF. This work enables future studies using extended TDHF with collision terms or time-dependent density matrix approaches to incorporate additional correlations and dissipation.
