# Projection method for boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric dissipative hydrodynamics

> Tensors describing boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric expansion of a relativistic dissipative fluid are decomposed in a suitable chosen basis of projection operators. This leads to a simple set of scalar equations which determine the fluid behavior. As special examples, we discuss the case of the Israel-Stewart theory and the model of highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics ADHYDRO. We also introduce the matching conditions between the ADHYDRO description suitable for the very early stages of heavy-ion collisions and the Israel-Stewart theory applicable for later stages when the system is close to equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper presents a projection method that decomposes tensors in boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric dissipative hydrodynamics into scalar equations, and introduces matching conditions between the ADHYDRO model (for highly-anisotropic early stages) and the Israel-Stewart theory (for near-equilibrium later stages), with the caveat that the matching is valid only when the anisotropy parameter \(x\) is close to unity and the transverse expansion is included.

## Method

The authors construct a basis of four orthogonal four-vectors (\(U^\mu\), \(X^\mu\), \(Y^\mu\), \(Z^\mu\)) adapted to boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric flow, and use them to decompose the expansion tensor, shear tensor, and stress tensor into scalar components. They then apply this decomposition to the Israel-Stewart relaxation equations and the ADHYDRO model, deriving scalar evolution equations for the energy density, transverse rapidity, shear stress components, and anisotropy parameter. Finally, they impose continuity of energy density, transverse flow, pressure components, and entropy density at a transition proper time \(\tau_{\rm tr}\) to match the ADHYDRO description to the Israel-Stewart description.

## Key numbers

- The shear tensor components for the case without radial flow are \(\sigma_X = \sigma_Y = 1/(3 \tau)\) and \(\sigma_Z = -2/(3 \tau)\).
- The entropy source in ADHYDRO is \(\Sigma(\sigma,x) = \frac{(1-\sqrt{x})^2}{\sqrt{x}} \frac{\sigma}{\tau_{\rm eq}}\), where \(\tau_{\rm eq}\) is a timescale parameter.
- The matching condition for the shear stress component is \(\pi_X = \varepsilon_{\rm eq}(\sigma) x r'(x) \approx \varepsilon_{\rm eq}(\sigma_{\rm eq}) \frac{4(x-1)}{45}\) near equilibrium.
- The entropy density in ADHYDRO near equilibrium is \(\sigma = \sigma_{\rm eq} \left(1 - \frac{(x-1)^2}{30} \right)\).
- The relaxation time relation used is \(\tau_\pi = \frac{5\eta}{T \sigma_{\rm eq}}\), derived in Martinez (2009).

## Context

This work extends the projection operator approach to dissipative hydrodynamics for systems with both boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry, generalizing earlier treatments that considered only longitudinal expansion (Martinez, 2009). It provides a formal framework to connect the ADHYDRO model (Florkowski, 2010; Ryblewski, 2010, 2011), which describes highly-anisotropic early stages of heavy-ion collisions, with the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel, 1979; Muronga, 2003) applicable near equilibrium. The matching conditions enable hybrid simulations that combine anisotropic early evolution with viscous hydrodynamics at later times, applicable to central collisions at RHIC and LHC.
