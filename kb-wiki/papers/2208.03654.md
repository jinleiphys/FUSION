# Stability against three-body clustering in one-dimensional spinless p-wave fermions

> We theoretically investigate in-medium two- and three-body correlations in one-dimensional spinless fermions with attractive two-body p-wave interaction. By investigating the variational problem of two- and three-body states above the Fermi sea, we elucidate the fate of the in-medium two- and three-body cluster states. The one-dimensional system with the strong p-wave interaction is found to be stable against the formation of three-body clusters even in the presence of the Fermi sea, in contrast to higher-dimensional systems that suffer the strong three-body loss associated with the trimer formation.

## Key claim

The one-dimensional spinless p-wave fermion system with attractive two-body interaction is stable against three-body clustering even in the presence of the Fermi sea, in contrast to higher-dimensional systems; however, this stability relies on the absence of any additional three-body interaction, and the weak-coupling side is more sensitive to such residual three-body forces than the strong-coupling side.

## Method

The authors solved the generalized Cooper problem for two-body and three-body states above the Fermi sea using a variational wavefunction approach for one-dimensional spinless fermions with a short-range attractive p-wave two-body interaction. They derived the in-medium two-body bound-state equation and the in-medium Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosov (STM) equation for the three-body sector, then solved these equations numerically with a finite momentum cutoff to determine the existence of cluster states and the fermion-dimer T-matrix.

## Key numbers

* The two-body ground-state energy in the weak-coupling limit scales as \(|E_2| \simeq 8E_{\rm F} e^{\pi/(k_{\rm F}a)}\), analogous to the three-dimensional s-wave case.
* In the strong-coupling limit, the two-body energy approaches \(|E_2| \simeq 2E_{\rm F} + 1/(ma^2)\) in the limit \(\Lambda/k_{\rm F} \rightarrow \infty\).
* The difference between the three-body solution energy \(E_{\rm sol.}\) and the two-body state energy \(E'_2\) is approximately \(E_{\rm sol.} - E'_2 = \frac{3}{2}E_{\rm F}\).
* The calculation of \(E_{\rm sol.}\) is stopped at \(1/(k_{\rm F}a) \simeq 0.65\), where \(E_{\rm sol.}\) becomes zero.
* The critical value for the topological phase transition predicted by mean-field theory is \(1/(k_{\rm F}a) \simeq 1.27\).

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium cluster states from higher-dimensional p-wave systems, where three-body loss is severe (Levinsen 2007), to one-dimensional geometry, where suppression of three-body loss was predicted (Zhou 2017) and observed experimentally (Chang 2020, Marcum 2020). The authors apply the generalized Cooper problem approach previously used for Cooper triples (Niemann 2012, Kirk 2017, Tajima 2021) and Cooper quartets (Guo 2022) to the one-dimensional p-wave case, and connect to the Bose-Fermi duality framework (Sekino 2021). The results enable further investigation of unconventional superconductors and superfluids in one dimension, and open the question of how finite temperature or additional three-body forces might induce in-medium three-body bound states.
