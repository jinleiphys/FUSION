# Light new physics and the $τ$ lepton dipole moments

> Testing New-Physics (NP) scenarios that couple predominantly to the third generation is notoriously difficult experimentally, as exemplified by comparing limits for the $τ$ lepton dipole moments to those of electrons and muons. In this case, extracting limits from processes such as $e^+e^-\toτ^+τ^-$ often relies on effective-field-theory (EFT) arguments, which allow for model-independent statements, but only apply if the NP scale is sufficiently large compared to the center-of-mass energy. In this work we offer a comprehensive analysis of light NP contributions to the $τ$ dipole moments, providing a detailed account of the interpretation of asymmetry measurements in $e^+e^-\toτ^+τ^-$ that are tailored towards the extraction of dipole moments, for the test cases of new light spin-$0$ and spin-$1$ bosons. Moreover, we study the decoupling to the EFT limit in these scenarios and discuss the complementarity to constraints from other related processes, such as production in $e^+e^-$ reactions. While covering a wide range of light NP scenarios, as specific case study we present a detailed discussion of a tauphilic gauge vector boson at Belle II.

## Key claim
The paper argues that extracting limits on the tau lepton dipole moments from asymmetry measurements in \(e^+e^- \to \tau^+\tau^-\) requires careful subtraction of momentum-dependent form factor contributions when new physics is light (mass comparable to or below the center-of-mass energy), not just the effective-field-theory decoupling limit assumed in prior work.

## Method
The authors computed one-loop contributions to the electromagnetic form factors \(F_2(q^2)\) and \(F_3(q^2)\) for the \(\tau\) lepton induced by light spin-0 (scalar, pseudoscalar, axionlike) and spin-1 (vector boson) particles, providing analytic expressions in various kinematic limits. They then studied how these form factors map onto the anomalous magnetic moment \(a_\tau\) and electric dipole moment \(d_\tau\) at \(q^2 = 0\), and compared the indirect constraints from form-factor measurements at Belle II with direct constraints from tree-level production of the same light particles.

## Key numbers
- The experimental limit for the muon electric dipole moment is \(d_\mu^\text{exp} < 2 \times 10^{-19} \, e\,\text{cm}\).
- The experimental limit for the electron electric dipole moment is \(d_e^\text{exp} < 4.1 \times 10^{-30} \, e\,\text{cm}\).
- The measured anomalous magnetic moment of the muon is \(a_\mu^{\text{exp}} = 116\,592\,071.5(14.5) \times 10^{-11}\).
- The measured anomalous magnetic moment of the electron is \(a_e^{\text{exp}} = 115\,965\,218\,059(13) \times 10^{-14}\).
- For a light scalar with only Yukawa couplings, in the limit \(m_\tau \gg M_\phi\), the contribution to \(a_\tau\) is \(\frac{3}{16\pi^2} (c_S^\tau m_\tau/\Lambda)^2 - \frac{1}{16\pi^2} (c_P^\tau m_\tau/\Lambda)^2\).
- For a light scalar with only Yukawa couplings, in the limit \(m_\tau \gg M_\phi\), the contribution to \(d_\tau\) is \(\frac{1}{8\pi^2} \frac{e}{m_\tau} \frac{m_\tau^2 c_P^\tau c_S^\tau}{\Lambda^2}\).
- The Belle II operational center-of-mass energy is \(\sqrt{s_B} = 10.58\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of tau dipole moment constraints from \(e^+e^- \to \tau^+\tau^-\) asymmetries, previously studied in an EFT framework by Crivellin (2021) and the Belle II collaboration (2022), to the case of light new physics where the EFT decoupling assumption fails. It provides a detailed comparison of indirect constraints from form-factor measurements with direct production constraints, using a tauphilic gauge vector boson at Belle II as a specific case study motivated by the \(B \to K^{(*)} + E_{\text{miss}}\) anomaly discussed by Di Luzio (2025). The results enable future experimental searches at B factories to properly interpret asymmetry measurements in the presence of light new states.
