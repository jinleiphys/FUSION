# 3D calculation of Tucson-Melbourne 3NF effect in triton binding energy

> As an application of the new realistic three-dimensional (3D) formalism reported recently for three-nucleon (3N) bound states, an attempt is made to study the effect of three-nucleon forces (3NFs) in triton binding energy in a non partial wave (PW) approach. The spin-isospin dependent 3N Faddeev integral equations with the inclusion of 3NFs, which are formulated as function of vector Jacobi momenta, specifically the magnitudes of the momenta and the angle between them, are solved with Bonn-B and Tucson-Melbourne NN and 3N forces in operator forms which can be incorporated in our 3D formalism. The comparison with numerical results in both, novel 3D and standard PW schemes, shows that non PW calculations avoid the very involved angular momentum algebra occurring for the permutations and transformations and it is more efficient and less cumbersome for considering the 3NF.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a three-dimensional (3D) non-partial-wave formalism for solving Faddeev integral equations with three-nucleon forces (3NFs) can reproduce triton binding energies comparable to standard partial-wave (PW) calculations, while avoiding the cumbersome angular momentum algebra required in the PW approach. The claim is qualified as a first attempt toward planned investigations with more modern forces, and the 3D result for Bonn-B plus Tucson-Melbourne (TM) 3NF converges to -9.75 MeV versus -9.80 MeV in the PW scheme at the lowest angular momentum truncation.

## Method

The spin-isospin dependent three-nucleon Faddeev integral equations, including the Tucson-Melbourne two-pion exchange 3NF, were formulated in a 3D basis using vector Jacobi momenta (magnitudes and the angle between them) rather than partial-wave quantum numbers. The equations were solved numerically for the triton bound state using the Bonn-B nucleon-nucleon potential and the TM 3NF in operator form, with the TM cutoff mass set to 5.828 pion masses. The 3D approach required solving eight coupled integral equations for the spin-isospin states, and convergence was studied as a function of grid points in Jacobi momenta and spherical angles.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with Bonn-B alone in 3D approach: -8.152 MeV (converged), compared to -8.14 MeV in PW approach at j12max=4.
- Triton binding energy with Bonn-B plus TM 3NF in 3D approach: -9.75 MeV (converged with 40 Jacobi momentum grid points and 24 or more spherical angle grid points).
- Corresponding PW result with Bonn-B plus TM 3NF at j12max=1: -9.80 MeV.
- TM 3NF cutoff mass used: Lambda_pi = 5.828 m_pi.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously reported 3D formalism for three-nucleon bound states (Hadizadeh 2009, Bayegan 2008) by incorporating the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force. It provides an alternative to the standard partial-wave approach used by Gloeckle (1993) and others, which requires complex angular momentum recoupling. The results enable future calculations with more modern forces such as AV18 plus modified TM' 3NF and chiral three-nucleon forces, as well as the planned inclusion of relativistic effects in the same 3D framework.
