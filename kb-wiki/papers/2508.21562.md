# Nuclear suppression in diffractive vector meson production within the color glass condensate framework

> We perform a global Bayesian analysis of diffractive $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production in $γ+p$ and $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions within a Color Glass Condensate based framework. Using data from HERA and the LHC, we find that a simultaneous description of $γ+p$ and $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ observables is challenging. Introducing a global $K$-factor to account for theoretical uncertainties improves the agreement with data and enhances the framework's predictive power. We present predictions for integrated $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ cross sections at different photon-nucleus energies and study their $A$-dependence relative to a no-saturation baseline, quantifying nuclear suppression and providing insights into the onset of saturation effects.

## Key claim

A simultaneous description of diffractive J/ψ production in both γ+p and γ+Pb collisions within a Color Glass Condensate framework is challenging, and introducing a global K-factor of approximately 0.33 to absorb theoretical uncertainties is required to improve agreement with data, implying a strong rescaling of the cross-section normalization that is compensated by a larger color charge density.

## Method

The authors performed a global Bayesian analysis of coherent diffractive J/ψ production using the CGC framework with JIMWLK energy evolution, computing the cross section from the dipole-target amplitude obtained from Wilson lines in the McLerran-Venguopalan model. They confronted both γ+p data from HERA and γ+Pb data from the LHC, using 25 parameter samples from the posterior distribution generated in a companion study (Mäntysaari 2025) to propagate parameter uncertainties to predictions for the A-dependence of the cross section at two photon-nucleus center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- The preferred value of the global K-factor was found to be approximately 0.33, implying a strong rescaling of the cross-section normalization.
- For the heaviest nuclei, the ratio of the integrated coherent cross section to the γ+p case is reduced to approximately 0.3 at W=31.5 GeV (corresponding to x=0.01).
- For the heaviest nuclei, the ratio is reduced to approximately 0.15 at W=813 GeV (corresponding to x=1.5×10^{-5}).
- The suppression shows about a factor of two difference between the two W values for large A, while the two curves approach each other at small A.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based framework previously applied to coherent and incoherent diffractive J/ψ production at HERA (Mäntysaari 2016, 2018) to nuclear targets in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions, where experiments such as ALICE (2023) and CMS (2023) have measured W-dependent coherent J/ψ cross sections for γ+Pb that show stronger nuclear suppression than expected. The results enable future quantitative assessment of saturation effects at the Electron-Ion Collider, where measuring the A-dependence of exclusive vector meson production as a function of photon virtuality Q² can provide a clean probe of the onset of gluon saturation.
