# On the physical meaning of Sachs form factors and on the violation of the dipole dependence of G_E and G_M on Q^2

> We discuss questions related to the interpretation of unexpected results of measurements of the proton form factors ratio G_E/G_M in the polarization experiments done in JLab in the region of 0.5 < Q^2 < 8.5 GeV^2. For this purpose, in the case of the hard scattering mechanism we calculated (in the leading approximation) the matrix elements of the proton current J^{\pm δ,δ}_p for the full set of spin combinations corresponding to the number of the spin-flipped quarks, which contribute to the proton transition without spin-flip (J^{δ,δ}_p) and with the spin-flip (J^{-δ,δ}_p). This set is: (0,1), (0,3), (2,1), (2,3), where the first number in parentheses is the number of the spin-flipped quarks, which contribute to the J^{δ,δ}_p, and the second one is the number of the spin-flipped quarks which contribute to the J^{-δ,δ}_p. For the sets of (0,1) and (2,3), we found that the ratio G_E/G_M ~ 1, and the form factors G_E and G_M behave for the set of (0,1) as G_E, G_M ~ 1/Q^6, and for the set of (2,3) as G_E, G_M ~ 1/Q^4. At the same time the set of (0,1) is realized for τ<< 1, and the set (2,3) for τ>> 1 (τ=Q^2/4m^2). This allows us to suppose that: 1) at the lower boundary of the experimental measurements of the ratio G_E/G_M not dipole dependence appears but the law of G_E, G_M ~ 1/Q^6; 2) the conditions for the observation of the dipole dependence in the experiments has not yet been achieved; 3) since for quarks J^{δ,δ}_q ~ 1 and J^{-δ,δ}_q ~ \sqrtτ, then the dipole dependence is realized when τ>> 1 in the case when the quark transitions with spin-flip are dominate.

## Key claim

The dipole dependence of the proton form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) on \(Q^2\) (where \(G_E = G_M/\mu = (1+Q^2/0.71)^{-2}\)) has not yet been achieved in the JLab polarization experiments in the region \(0.5 < Q^2 < 8.5\) GeV\(^2\); instead, near the lower boundary of this region (\(Q^2 \approx 1\) GeV\(^2\)), the form factors behave as \(G_E, G_M \sim 1/Q^6\) with a ratio \(G_E/G_M \sim 1\), and the dipole dependence (\(G_E, G_M \sim 1/Q^4\)) would only be realized at much higher \(Q^2\) when quark spin-flip transitions dominate.

## Method

The authors calculated, in the leading approximation of the hard scattering mechanism, the matrix elements of the proton current \(J_p^{\pm\delta,\delta}\) for all four spin combinations of the three constituent quarks that contribute to proton non-spin-flip (\(J_p^{\delta,\delta}\)) and spin-flip (\(J_p^{-\delta,\delta}\)) transitions: (0,1), (0,3), (2,1), and (2,3), where the first number is the count of spin-flipped quarks contributing to \(J_p^{\delta,\delta}\) and the second to \(J_p^{-\delta,\delta}\). Using the diagonal spin basis (DSB) formalism, they expressed the proton current matrix elements in terms of the Sachs form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) and then applied a perturbative QCD counting rule where the proton current amplitude is the product of three pointlike quark current amplitudes divided by \(Q^6\) from two gluon and two quark propagators.

## Key numbers

* For the (0,1) spin set (realized when \(\tau = Q^2/4m^2 \ll 1\)): \(G_E \sim 1/Q^6\) and \(G_M \sim 1/Q^6\), with \(G_E/G_M \sim 1\).
* For the (2,3) spin set (realized when \(\tau \gg 1\)): \(G_E \sim 1/Q^4\) and \(G_M \sim 1/Q^4\), with \(G_E/G_M \sim 1\) (the dipole dependence).
* For the (0,3) set: \(G_E \sim 1/Q^6\), \(G_M \sim \tau/Q^6\), giving \(G_E/G_M \sim 4m^2/Q^2\).
* For the (2,1) set: \(G_E \sim \tau/Q^6 \sim 1/Q^4\), \(G_M \sim 1/Q^6\), giving \(G_E/G_M \sim Q^2/4m^2\).
* The linear fit to the JLab polarization data for the ratio \(R = \mu G_E/G_M\) in the range \(0.5 < Q^2 < 5.6\) GeV\(^2\) is \(R = 1 - 0.13\,(Q^2 - 0.04)\) (Gayou 2002).

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the Rosenbluth method (which found \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) following the dipole form up to \(Q^2 \approx 10\) GeV\(^2\)) and the polarization transfer experiments at JLab (Jones 2000, Gayou 2002, Puckett 2010, Meziane 2011, Puckett 2012) which showed a linear decrease of \(\mu G_E/G_M\) with \(Q^2\). The authors argue that the one-photon exchange mechanism is sufficient to explain the JLab results without invoking two-photon exchange corrections (as proposed by Brodsky 2005, Perdrisat 2007, and reviewed by Arrington 2011). The derived spin parametrization formula \(G_E/G_M = (\alpha_0 + \alpha_2 \tau)/(\beta_1 + \beta_3 \tau)\) provides a framework for fitting future experimental data to determine the number of quarks undergoing spin-flip transitions, and the analysis suggests that the dipole dependence (the (2,3) set) requires conditions not yet reached in existing experiments.
