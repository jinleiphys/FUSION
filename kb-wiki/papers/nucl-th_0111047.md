# Deuteron Compton Scattering in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Compton scattering on the deuteron is studied in the framework of baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order in small momenta, for photon energies of order the pion mass. The scattering amplitude is a sum of one- and two-nucleon mechanisms with no undetermined parameters. Our results are in good agreement with the intermediate energy experimental data, and a comparison is made with the recent higher-energy data obtained at SAL.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free chiral perturbation theory (χPT) calculation of deuteron Compton scattering at third order in small momenta (O(Q³)) for photon energies around the pion mass, finding good agreement with intermediate-energy data but noting that the 95 MeV prediction carries considerable uncertainties that likely require a next-order calculation for a more stringent test.

## Method

The scattering amplitude was constructed as a sum of one-nucleon and two-nucleon mechanisms in baryon chiral perturbation theory, with all diagrams evaluated up to O(Q³) in the small-momentum expansion. The one-nucleon contributions were boosted from the γN center-of-mass frame to the γNN center-of-mass frame, while two-nucleon contributions were included explicitly. The photon-deuteron T-matrix was calculated using the energy-independent Bonn OBEPQ wave function, and the laboratory differential cross section was evaluated directly from it.

## Key numbers

- At 49 MeV photon laboratory energy, the O(Q³) calculation shows reasonable agreement with the Illinois data (Lucas et al.), with O(Q³) corrections not large compared to the leading O(Q²) result.
- At 69 MeV photon laboratory energy, the O(Q³) calculation shows good agreement with the Illinois data (Lucas et al.), with the convergence appearing to be good.
- At 95 MeV photon laboratory energy, the O(Q³) prediction is compared with recent SAL data (Saskatoon), but the cross section comes out somewhat smaller than at lower energies, particularly in backward directions.

## Context

This work extends the application of baryon chiral perturbation theory from single-nucleon Compton scattering to the deuteron, following the framework of Weinberg (1990) for nuclear forces. The calculation enables extraction of neutron electromagnetic polarizabilities from deuteron Compton scattering data, which is motivated by the fact that the sum α_p + α_n may be accessible via interference with the dominant proton Thomson term. The results at 49 and 69 MeV agree with the Illinois data (Lucas et al.), while the 95 MeV comparison with SAL data (Saskatoon) shows the need for a full O(Q⁴) calculation to test the theory at higher energies.
