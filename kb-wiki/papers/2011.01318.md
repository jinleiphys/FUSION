# Dark matter admixed neutron star properties in the light of gravitational wave observations: a two fluid approach

> We consider the effect of density dependent dark matter on the neutron star mass, radius, and tidal deformability. Nuclear matter (normal matter) as well as the fermionic dark matter sector is considered in a mean field model. We adopt the two fluid formalism to investigate the effect of dark matter on the neutron star properties. In the two fluid picture, there is no direct interaction between the dark matter and the nuclear matter. Rather these two sectors interact only through gravitational interaction. The nuclear matter sector is described by the $σ-ω-ρ$ meson interaction in the "FSU2R" parameterization. In the dark matter sector, we use the Bayesian parameter optimization technique to fix the unknown parameters in the dark matter equation of state. In the two fluid picture, we solve the coupled Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the mass and radius of dark matter admixed neutron stars (DANSs). We also estimate the effect of the density dependent dark matter sector on the tidal deformability of dark matter admixed neutron stars (DANSs).

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating a density-dependent fermionic dark matter component, interacting with nuclear matter only through gravity in a two-fluid formalism, can significantly alter neutron star mass, radius, and tidal deformability, and that Bayesian optimization of the unknown dark matter sector parameters yields a distribution of viable dark matter admixed neutron star configurations that can satisfy observational constraints from gravitational wave events such as GW170817.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter using a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach with the FSU2R parameterization, including sigma, omega, and rho meson interactions. The dark matter sector is modeled analogously with a single fermionic component and self-interactions mediated by dark scalar and dark vector bosons in a mean field approximation. The two fluids (nuclear matter and dark matter) interact only gravitationally, and the coupled Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability. Unknown dark matter parameters (scalar coupling-to-mass ratio, vector coupling-to-mass ratio, dark matter bare mass, and central dark matter energy density fraction) are fixed using Bayesian parameter optimization with uniform priors, using neutron star maximum mass, radius at 1.4 solar masses, and tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses as input data.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density is given as n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The FSU2R model parameters include: nucleon mass M_n = 939 MeV, sigma meson mass m_sigma = 497.479 MeV, omega meson mass m_omega = 782.500 MeV, rho meson mass m_rho = 763.000 MeV
- The tidal deformability k2 for neutron stars is typically in the range 0.05-0.15
- The Universe is composed of approximately 6% visible matter, 26% dark matter, and 68% dark energy
- Neutron star mass range is given as 1.4-3.0 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the two-fluid formalism developed by Sandin et al. (2014) and Xiang et al. (2014) for studying dark matter effects on neutron star structure, and the approach of Leung et al. (2011) using general relativistic two-fluid master function formalism. It contrasts with single-fluid approaches that include nongravitational interactions between normal and dark matter, such as those by Panotopoulos and Lopes (2017) and earlier work by the same authors using NL3 parameterization. The Bayesian optimization technique, previously applied in compact star studies by Biswas et al. (2020), Steiner et al. (2010), and the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2018), enables systematic parameter estimation for the dark matter sector. This work enables future studies to constrain dark matter properties using multimessenger observations of neutron star mergers, particularly tidal deformability measurements from gravitational wave events.
