# Heavy-quark diffusion in 2+1D and Glasma-like plasmas: evidence of a transport peak

> We examine how plasma excitations affect the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient in 2+1 dimensional and Glasma-like plasmas, akin to the pre-equilibrium matter describing relativistic heavy-ion collisions at early times. We find that the transport coefficient $2κ$ transverse to and $κ_z$ along the beam direction display a qualitatively different evolution. We attribute this to the underlying excitation spectra of these systems, thus providing evidence for the existence of non-perturbative properties in the spectrum. This is accomplished by first reconstructing the diffusion coefficients using gauge-fixed correlation functions, which accurately reproduces the time evolution of $2κ$ and $κ_z$. We then modify these excitation spectra to study the impact of their non-perturbative features on the transport coefficients. In particular, we find evidence for a novel transport peak in the low-frequency spectrum that is crucial for heavy-quark diffusion. We also demonstrate that gluonic excitations are broad while scalar excitations associated with $κ_z$ are narrow and strongly enhanced at low momenta. Our findings indicate that the large values and dynamical properties of transport coefficients in the Glasma could originate from genuinely non-perturbative features in the spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence that the qualitatively different time evolution of the heavy-quark diffusion coefficients transverse to and along the beam direction in 2+1D and Glasma-like plasmas originates from non-perturbative features in the excitation spectrum, specifically a novel transport peak at zero frequency that is crucial for heavy-quark diffusion, though the authors note that these systems are simplified models that drop the longitudinal Bjorken expansion of the metric compared to the actual Glasma.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in 2+1 dimensions and a Glasma-like theory with an additional adjoint scalar field, both initialized with Gaussian occupation numbers. They computed the heavy-quark diffusion coefficients directly from chromo-electric field correlation functions and reconstructed them using frequency-dependent gauge-fixed correlation functions in Coulomb gauge. The excitation spectra were then systematically modified to study the impact of their non-perturbative features (broad gluonic peaks, narrow scalar excitations, and a zero-frequency transport peak) on the transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The heavy-quark diffusion coefficient $2\kappa$ for the gluonic sector and $\kappa_z$ for the scalar sector were computed at $Qt = 500$ on $1024^2$ lattices (2D theory) and $512^2$ lattices (Glasma-like theory) with lattice spacing $Qa_s = 1/8$ and $Qa_s = 1/4$, respectively
- The perturbative scaling estimate for the gluonic diffusion coefficient gives $2\kappa_\infty^{\mathrm{pert}}(t) \sim Q^3 (Qt)^{-3/5}$ using the self-similar scaling exponent $\beta = -1/5$
- The perturbative estimate for the scalar sector yields $\kappa_{z,\infty}^{\mathrm{pert}} = 0$, in contrast to the non-zero numerical results
- The linear growth regime for $2\kappa$ at early times occurs for $Q\Delta t \lesssim 1$, following $2\kappa(t,\Delta t) \approx (g^2/N_c)\langle EE\rangle(t,0)\,\Delta t$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy-quark diffusion in 3+1 dimensional highly occupied plasmas (Boguslavski 2020) and in the Glasma (Carrington 2020, Avramescu 2023) by focusing on dimensionally reduced 2+1D systems where hard thermal loop perturbation theory breaks down (Boguslavski 2019). The findings enable a physical explanation for the large anisotropic values of the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient observed in Glasma simulations and connect the non-perturbative excitation spectra identified in earlier work (Boguslavski 2021) to transport properties relevant for heavy-quark flow and quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions.
