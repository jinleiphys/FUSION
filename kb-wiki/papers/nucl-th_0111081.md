# A Possibility to Observe Short-Range NN Properties in the Deuteron Breakup $pd\to ppn$

> Quasi-binary reaction of the deuteron breakup $p+d\to (pp)+n$ with the final proton-proton pair $(pp)$ in the $^1S_0$ state is analyzed at initial energies $0.5 - 2$ GeV in the kinematics of backward elastic pd-scattering $pd\to dp$. On the basis of the main mechanisms of the $pd\to dp$ process, including initial and final state interaction, we show that unpolarized cross section and spin observables of this reaction exhibit important properties of the half-off-shell $pp(^1S_0)$-scattering amplitude, which are relevant to the nucleon-nucleon interaction at short distances.

## Key claim

The unpolarized cross section and spin observables of the quasi-binary deuteron breakup reaction \(p+d\to (pp)+n\) with the final proton-proton pair in the \(^1S_0\) state can reveal the node of the half-off-shell \(pp(^1S_0)\)-scattering amplitude at an off-shell momentum \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, provided the relative energy of the final pp-pair is kept below 3 MeV to suppress contributions from higher orbital momenta.

## Method

The reaction amplitude was constructed by analogy with backward elastic \(pd\to dp\) scattering, using a model that includes one-nucleon exchange (ONE), single pN scattering (SS), and double pN scattering with intermediate \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation. The final deuteron state in the \(pd\to dp\) formalism was replaced by a scattering state of the NN-system in the \(^1S_0\) state, and initial and final state interactions were treated in the eikonal distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) for the ONE mechanism, while SS and \(\Delta\) mechanisms were treated in the plane-wave approximation. Numerical calculations were performed with the Reid soft-core (RSC) potential, and results with the Paris potential were noted to be very similar.

## Key numbers

- The node of the half-off-shell \(pp(^1S_0)\) amplitude \(t_s(q,k)\) occurs at an off-shell momentum \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, corresponding to an initial proton kinetic energy \(T_p\sim 0.7\) GeV at a neutron scattering angle \(\theta_{cm}=180^\circ\).
- The ONE mechanism dominates at \(T_p<0.5\) GeV and above 1 GeV; the \(\Delta\) and SS contributions have their maxima at \(T_p\sim 0.6\) GeV, close to the dip of the ONE cross section.
- The maximal cross section of the reaction is expected at a final pp-pair relative energy \(E_{pp}=0.3-0.7\) MeV; at \(E_{pp}<0.3\) MeV the cross section decreases rapidly due to Coulomb repulsion.
- The node of the deuteron S-wave function \(u(q)\) in momentum space also lies at \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, and the node of the deuteron charge form factor \(G_C(Q)\) occurs at a transferred momentum \(Q\sim 4.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) (citing Nikhef and Abbott).
- The coherent sum ONE+SS+\(\Delta\) predicts a well-pronounced dip in the cross section near \(T_p\sim 0.7\) GeV and a plateau at \(T_p>1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of backward elastic \(pd\to dp\) scattering (Lak, Kolybas, Uzikov 1998, 2000) to the breakup channel \(pd\to ppn\) in order to isolate short-range NN properties that are masked in the elastic channel by the deuteron D-state and by isobar excitation mechanisms. The proposed reaction suppresses \(\Delta\)-isobar contributions by an isospin factor of 1/3 compared to elastic \(pd\) scattering (Imambekov and Uzikov 1990), and the dominance of the S-wave in the final pp-pair at low relative energy makes the node of the half-off-shell NN amplitude directly observable. The reaction has not yet been measured exclusively; the only exclusive measurement of \(pd\to pnp\) at 585 MeV (Witten) showed a very small singlet fraction, and direct measurement of the singlet channel is planned at COSY.
