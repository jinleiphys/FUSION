# The Bulk Viscosity of High-Temperature QCD

> We compute the bulk viscosity zeta of high-temperature QCD to leading order in powers of the running coupling alpha_s(T). We find that it is negligible compared to shear viscosity eta for any alpha_s that might reasonably be considered small. The physics of bulk viscosity in QCD is a bit different than in scalar phi^4 theory. In particular, unlike in scalar theory, we find that an old, crude estimate of zeta as 15 ((1/3)-v_s^2)^2 eta gives the correct order of magnitude, where v_s is the speed of sound. We also find that leading-log expansions of our result for zeta are not accurate except at very small coupling.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of high-temperature QCD is negligible compared to shear viscosity for any coupling that might reasonably be considered small, and an old crude estimate of ζ ≈ 15((1/3) − v_s²)² η gives the correct order of magnitude, unlike in scalar φ⁴ theory where such an estimate fails.

## Method

The authors compute the bulk viscosity ζ of high-temperature QCD to leading order in the running coupling α_s(T) using kinetic theory, linearizing the Boltzmann equation around local equilibrium for isotropic compression or expansion. They determine the source term q^a(p) from the left-hand side of the Boltzmann equation, which involves both the spatial gradient and the time derivative of the distribution function, and then solve the resulting linearized collision integral using a variational method. The calculation accounts for conformal symmetry breaking both from the running coupling (the beta function) and from nonzero quark masses.

## Key numbers

- For massless QCD with n_f = 3, the ratio ζ/α_s⁴ η approaches the limit 0.973 as α_s → 0 (where leading-log approximations become applicable).
- The parametric behavior is ζ ∼ α_s² T³ / log[1/α_s] when all quark masses m₀ ≪ α_s T, and ζ ∼ m₀⁴ / (T α_s² log[1/α_s]) when α_s T ≪ m₀ ≪ T.
- The bulk viscosity ζ is shown to be smaller than shear viscosity η by roughly a factor of 1000 for n_f = 3 QCD at couplings up to α_s ≈ 0.3 (see Fig. 3, where ζ/s is rescaled by a factor of 1000 for comparison with η/s).

## Context

This work fills a gap in the literature where no previous paper correctly stated even the power of α_s to which the bulk viscosity is proportional, despite extensive prior calculations of shear viscosity in QCD by Hosoya and Kajantie (1980s), Baym (1990), and the leading-order results of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000s). The calculation extends the kinetic theory techniques developed for shear viscosity to the bulk case, revealing that number-changing processes in QCD are much more efficient than in scalar φ⁴ theory (studied by Jeon and Yaffe), so that the bottleneck for bulk viscosity is the same 2↔2 scattering rate that determines shear viscosity. The results enable more reliable inclusion of bulk viscosity in non-ideal hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions and provide a benchmark for testing the conjecture that the quark-gluon plasma is nearly ideal.
