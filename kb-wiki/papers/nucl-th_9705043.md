# Neutron star properties in the Thomas-Fermi model

> The modern nucleon-nucleon interaction of Myers and Swiatecki, adjusted to the properties of finite nuclei, the parameters of the mass formula, and the behavior of the optical potential is used to calculate the properties of $β$--equilibrated neutron star matter, and to study the impact of this equation of state on the properties of (rapidly rotating) neutron stars and their cooling behavior. The results are in excellent agreement with the outcome of calculations performed for a broad collection of sophisticated nonrelativistic as well as relativistic models for the equation of state.

## Key claim

The Thomas-Fermi model of Myers and Swiatecki, despite its simplicity (only seven free parameters), produces neutron star equations of state, structural properties, and cooling behavior that agree well with both sophisticated nonrelativistic variational calculations and, over certain density ranges, with relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results. However, the agreement is conditional on the specific parameter set used (TF96 being the latest), and the composition of neutron star matter (proton fraction) differs qualitatively from relativistic models because the symmetry energy in the Thomas-Fermi model saturates or decreases at high density rather than increasing monotonically.

## Method

The authors used the Thomas-Fermi model of Myers and Swiatecki, which employs a density- and momentum-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction with a Yukawa radial dependence, to compute the equation of state of beta-equilibrated neutron star matter (neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) under charge neutrality and generalized beta-equilibrium constraints. They solved Einstein's field equations for both static and rapidly rotating neutron stars using the computed equation of state, and calculated cooling curves by solving the thermal evolution equations including modified and direct Urca processes, with and without superfluid effects.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter for the TF96 parameter set: energy per baryon E/A = -16.24 MeV, saturation density rho_00 = 0.161 fm^-3, incompressibility K = 234 MeV, symmetry energy J = 33 MeV (Table I).
- For the TF96 model, the proton fraction x in beta-stable matter peaks at x = 0.0803 at rho = 0.40 fm^-3, then decreases to x = 0.0274 at rho = 1.25 fm^-3 (Table III).
- The sound velocity in the Thomas-Fermi model does not exceed 0.96 c for densities up to rho = 1.246 fm^-3, and reaches the causal limit at rho = 1.36 fm^-3.
- The mass increase of the heaviest neutron stars due to rapid rotation at the Kepler frequency is about 0.3 solar masses, and radial changes due to rotation amount to about 2 km.
- The limiting rotational period for a neutron star of mass M = 1.4 solar masses is about 1 ms.
- For the TF96 model, the direct Urca process is not permitted because the proton fraction remains below the critical threshold of approximately 0.13.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Thomas-Fermi model of Myers and Swiatecki (1990) from finite nuclei and symmetric nuclear matter to neutron star structure and cooling, comparing results with nonrelativistic variational calculations by Wiringa et al. (1988) using the UV14+UVII and UV14+TNI potentials, and with relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Huber et al. (1994, 1996). The findings enable the use of this simple seven-parameter model as a computationally efficient alternative to more elaborate microscopic schemes for further studies of neutron star properties, including investigations of rapidly rotating pulsars and thermal evolution.
