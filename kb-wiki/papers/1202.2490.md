# $ν(\barν)$-$^{208}$Pb deep inelastic scattering

> Nuclear-medium effects in the weak structure functions $F_2(x,Q^2)$ and $F_3(x,Q^2)$ in the charged current neutrino and antineutrino induced deep inelastic reactions in $^{208}$Pb have been studied. The calculations have been performed in a theoretical model using relativistic nuclear spectral functions which incorporate Fermi motion, binding and nucleon correlations. We also consider the pion and rho meson cloud contributions calculated from a microscopic model for meson-nucleus self-energies. Using these structure functions, the results for the differential cross section have been obtained and compared with the CERN Hybrid Oscillation Research apparatUS (CHORUS) data. The results for the ratios $\frac{2F_{i}^{Pb}}{208F_i^D}$, $\frac{4F_{i}^{Pb}}{208F_i^{He}}$, $\frac{12F_{i}^{Pb}}{208F_i^C}$, $\frac{16F_{i}^{Pb}}{208F_i^O}$, and $\frac{56F_{i}^{Pb}}{208F_i^{Fe}}$ (i=2,3) have also been obtained and a few have been compared with some of the phenomenological fits.

# ν(ν̄)-²⁰⁸Pb deep inelastic scattering

## Key claim
Nuclear-medium effects in the weak structure functions F₂(x,Q²) and F₃(x,Q²) for charged-current neutrino and antineutrino deep inelastic scattering on ²⁰⁸Pb are calculated using a relativistic nuclear spectral function approach that includes Fermi motion, binding, nucleon correlations, and meson cloud contributions; the model reproduces CHORUS differential cross section data, but the agreement improves noticeably only when next-to-leading-order (NLO) parton distribution functions and mesonic degrees of freedom are both included, and the nonisoscalarity correction for lead is found to be 4–5% at x=0.175 rising to 10–12% at x=0.65.

## Method
The nuclear hadronic tensor is constructed via a convolution of the nucleonic tensor with relativistic hole spectral functions for protons and neutrons separately, using a local-density approximation with two-parameter Fermi densities for lead. The calculation includes target mass corrections, pion and rho meson cloud contributions from a microscopic meson-nucleus self-energy model, and shadowing corrections following Kulagin and Petti (2006); structure functions are evaluated with CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO).

## Key numbers
- Nonisoscalarity correction to the differential cross section in ²⁰⁸Pb: 4–5% at x=0.175, increasing to 10–12% at x=0.65 (for E_ν = 25 GeV, LO CTEQ PDFs).
- Shadowing effect on F₂^A in lead: 5–7% at x=0.1, Q²=1–5 GeV²; 1–2% at x=0.2, Q²=1–5 GeV²; dies out with increasing x and Q².
- Ratios presented at Q²=5 GeV² and Q²=50 GeV² for 2F_i^Pb/(208 F_i^D), 4F_i^Pb/(208 F_i^He), 12F_i^Pb/(208 F_i^C), 16F_i^Pb/(208 F_i^O), and 56F_i^Pb/(208 F_i^Fe) (i=2,3) at NLO.
- Differential cross sections (1/E) d²σ/dxdy computed for ν_μ and ν̄_μ on ²⁰⁸Pb at beam energies of 25 GeV and 70 GeV, compared with CHORUS data.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous spectral-function-based calculations for electromagnetic structure functions (Haider et al., 2011) and for weak structure functions in carbon and iron (Haider et al., 2011) to the nonisoscalar target ²⁰⁸Pb. It contrasts with phenomenological fits by Hirai et al. (2007) and Eskola et al. (2009), which treat nuclear modifications via parameterized parton distributions; the present approach finds that the ratios F₂ and F₃ are not identical as a function of x, unlike those phenomenological studies. The results are intended for use in the analysis of the MINERνA experiment, which uses deuterium, helium, carbon, iron, and lead targets.
