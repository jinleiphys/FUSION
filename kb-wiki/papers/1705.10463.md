# Three-nucleon forces and superfluidity in neutron matter

> The existence of superfluidity of the neutron component in the core of a neutron star, associated specifically with triplet $P-$wave pairing, is currently an open question that is central to interpretation of the observed cooling curves and other neutron-star observables. Ab initio theoretical calculations aimed at resolving this issue face unique challenges in the relevant high-density domain, which reaches beyond the saturation density of symmetrical nuclear matter. These issues include uncertainties in the three-nucleon (3N) interaction and in the effects of strong short-range correlations -- and more generally of in-medium modification of nucleonic self-energies and interactions. A survey of existing solutions to the gap equations in the triplet channel shows that the separate or combined impacts of 3N forces, coupled channels, and mass renormalization range from moderate to strong to devastating, thus motivating a detailed analysis of the competing effects. In the present work we track the effects of the 3N force and in-medium modifications in the representative case of the $^3P_2$ channel, based on the Argonne V18 two-nucleon (2N) interaction supplemented by 3N interactions of the Urbana IX family. Sensitivity of the results to the input interaction is clearly demonstrated, while consistency issues arise with respect to the simultaneous treatment of 3N forces and in-medium effects. We consider this pilot study as the first step towards a systematic and comprehensive exploration of coupled-channel $^3P F_2$ pairing using a broad range of 2N and 3N interactions from the current generation of refined semi-phenomenological models and models derived from chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The combined effect of three-nucleon (3N) forces and in-medium modifications on the triplet \(^3P_2\) pairing gap in neutron matter ranges from moderate to strong to devastating depending on the precise inputs, and the effects of the 3N force and correlations can be both large and opposite, raising consistency issues for many-body theories.

## Method

The authors solved the angle-averaged BCS gap equation for the uncoupled \(^3P_2\) channel in pure neutron matter, using the Argonne V18 two-nucleon (2N) interaction supplemented by Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon interactions. Unlike previous treatments, they avoided averaging the 3N interaction over the third particle to produce a density-dependent effective 2N interaction; instead, they introduced the explicit 3N interaction when constructing the two-body pairing-interaction matrix elements in momentum space. They also introduced a defect function (a Gaussian Jastrow correlation factor) to suppress the repulsive component of the 3N potential when the third unpaired nucleon comes close to its paired neighbors.

## Key numbers

- The original UIX parameters are \(A_{2\pi} = -0.0293\) MeV and \(U_0 = 0.0048\) MeV; the modified UIX parameters are \(A_{2\pi} = -0.0333\) MeV and \(U_0 = 0.00038\) MeV (Table 1).
- With the AV18 2N interaction alone and bare nucleon mass, the coupled \(^3PF_2\) channel gap at peak (\(k_F \approx 2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\)) is more than tripled compared to the uncoupled \(^3P_2\) channel.
- Using the modified UIX 3N interaction with a free single-particle spectrum, Zuo et al. obtained a maximal gap of about 1.2 MeV at \(k_F \approx 2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\); with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock spectrum, the gap was reduced to about 0.5 MeV.
- Dong et al. found a maximal gap of about 0.6 MeV at \(k_F \approx 2.3\) fm\(^{-1}\) with Bonn B plus a meson-exchange 3N potential; including the Z-factor reduced the gap to about 50 keV at \(k_F \approx 1.8\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- Maurizio et al. found a maximal gap of 1.15 MeV with chiral potentials; inclusion of effective-mass in-medium effects reduced the gap to about 0.35 MeV at \(k_F \approx 2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- With the original UIX force fully turned on, there is no positive solution for the gap (\(\Delta_F(k) \equiv 0\)).
- For \(k_F = 3.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), the gap vanishes already for a very weak 3N force.
- The effective mass values used were \(m^*/m = 0.82\) and 0.86 from Zuo et al. (2008), determined with and without a 3N interaction respectively.
- The defect function range parameters considered were \(b = 0.6\) and 0.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of triplet pairing in neutron matter by Zuo et al. (2008), Dong et al. (2013), and Maurizio et al. (2014), which showed widely varying effects of 3N forces and in-medium modifications on the pairing gap. The authors demonstrate extreme sensitivity of the \(^3P_2\) gap to the choice of 3N interaction parameters, particularly the repulsive \(U_0\) term, and show that the original UIX parameterization kills the gap entirely while the modified UIX has very weak influence. This pilot study is intended as a first step toward a systematic exploration of coupled-channel \(^3PF_2\) pairing using a broad range of 2N and 3N interactions from semi-phenomenological models and chiral effective field theory.
