# Method for measuring the charge radii of charged hyperons from the time-like region

> We propose a novel method for measuring the charge radii of charged stable hadrons, with which the first measurement of the charge radii of the $Σ^+$ and the $Ξ^-$ is foreseen. The method explores the facts that the Dalitz decay $ψ(2S) \to Y\bar{Y}e^+e^-$ contains the hyperon form factors and the lowest measurable four-momentum transfer squared can be as low as $\sim 4m_e^2= 1.05\times10^{-6}\,{\rm GeV^2}$ in the time-like region. We identify a kinematic region where the hyperon form factors are essential and propose a method for subtracting the background from the data. It is estimated that the hyperon charge radii can be measured to a precision of about {0.2~fm} with the BES\Rom{3} experiment and one order of magnitude better at the future Super $τ$-Charm Facility. Moreover, the same method can be used to measure the charge radius of the proton, which provides an independent cross-check on the extraction of proton radius from elastic $ep$ scattering or leptonic hydrogen spectroscopy.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the charge radii of the charged hyperons Σ⁺ and Ξ⁻ can be measured for the first time using the Dalitz decay ψ(2S) → YȲe⁺e⁻ in the time-like region, with the lowest accessible four-momentum transfer squared reaching ∼4mₑ² = 1.05×10⁻⁶ GeV². The method relies on identifying a kinematic region where hyperon elastic form factors dominate and subtracting background using the corresponding neutral-hyperon decays, but the precision estimates assume a dipole form factor model and synthetic data generation.

## Method

The method uses the four-body Dalitz decay ψ(2S) → YȲe⁺e⁻, where the virtual photon converting to e⁺e⁻ is emitted from the hyperon or antihyperon via final-state radiation (type-A and type-B diagrams), encoding the hyperon electromagnetic form factors. The type-X contribution (where the YȲ pair is produced via two gluons) is subtracted by measuring the corresponding decays into neutral hyperons ψ(2S) → Σ⁰Σ̄⁰e⁺e⁻ and ψ(2S) → Ξ⁰Ξ̄⁰e⁺e⁻, exploiting isospin symmetry. The optimal kinematic regions for extraction are identified as m_{Σ⁺Σ̄⁻} > 2.8 GeV and m_{Ξ⁻Ξ̄⁺} > 2.9 GeV, where the hyperon pole dominates over contributions from excited resonances such as Σ(1385)⁺ and Ξ(1530)⁻.

## Key numbers

- The lowest measurable four-momentum transfer squared is ∼4mₑ² = 1.05×10⁻⁶ GeV² in the time-like region.
- For m_{Σ⁺Σ̄⁻} > 2.8 GeV, the Σ⁺ pole contributes at least 85% to the type-A and type-B mechanisms; for m_{Ξ⁻Ξ̄⁺} > 2.9 GeV, the Ξ⁻ pole contributes at least 74%.
- At BESIII, with 2.7×10⁹ ψ(2S) events, the expected event yield is ∼10² events for both Σ⁺ and Ξ⁻; at the future Super τ-Charm Facility (STCF) with 6.4×10¹¹ events, the yield is about 300 times larger.
- From Monte Carlo simulation with 200 events (BESIII scale), the extracted charge radii are 1.02(23) fm for Σ⁺ and 1.13(23) fm for Ξ⁻; with 2×10⁴ events (STCF scale), the extracted radii are 0.78(4) fm for Σ⁺ and 0.80(3) fm for Ξ⁻.
- The precision at BESIII is estimated to be about 0.2 fm; at STCF, the precision is one order of magnitude better.

## Context

This work extends the methodology of measuring hadron charge radii from the time-like region, previously applied to the proton via J/ψ → pṗe⁺e⁻ as studied by Kappert et al. (2022), to the charged hyperons Σ⁺ and Ξ⁻, for which only the Σ⁻ charge radius (0.78(10) fm) has been measured by the SELEX collaboration (2001) using a Σ⁻ beam. The proposed method enables the first measurements of Σ⁺ and Ξ⁻ charge radii, which will test flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking in hadron structure and provide input for understanding baryon-baryon interactions relevant to neutron star physics. The same approach can also provide an independent cross-check on the proton charge radius extraction from elastic ep scattering (e.g., PRad experiment) and muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (CREMA experiment), though the precision may not be competitive with those dedicated measurements.
