# Bulk matter evolution and extraction of jet transport parameter in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC

> Within the picture of jet quenching induced by multiple parton scattering and gluon bremsstrahlung, medium modification of parton fragmentation functions and therefore the suppression of large transverse momentum hadron spectra are controlled by both the value and the space-time profile of the jet transport parameter along the jet propagation path. Experimental data on single hadron suppression in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC energy are analyzed within the higher-twist (HT) approach to the medium modified fragmentation functions and the next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD (pQCD) parton model. Assuming that the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ is proportional to the particle number density in both QGP and hadronic phase, experimental data on jet quenching in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclear targets can provide guidance on $\hat q_{h}$ in the hot hadronic matter. One can then study the dependence of extracted initial value of jet quenching parameter $\hat q_{0}$ at initial time $τ_{0}$ on the bulk medium evolution. Effects of transverse expansion, radial flow, phase transition and non-equilibrium evolution are examined. The extracted values are found to vary from $\hat q_{0}τ_{0}= 0.54$ GeV$^{2}$ in the (1+3)d ideal hydrodynamic model to 0.96 GeV$^{2}$ in a cascade model, with the main differences coming from the initial non-equilibrium evolution and the later hadronic evolution. The overall contribution to jet quenching from the hadronic phase, about 22-44%, is found to be significant. Therefore, realistic description of the early non-equilibrium parton evolution and later hadronic interaction will be critical for accurate extraction of the jet transport parameter in the strongly interacting QGP phase in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The extracted initial jet transport parameter $\hat q_0$ at initial time $\tau_0$ depends strongly on the assumed model for bulk medium evolution, varying from $\hat q_0\tau_0 = 0.54$ GeV$^2$ in a (1+3)d ideal hydrodynamic model to 0.96 GeV$^2$ in a cascade model, with the main differences arising from the early non-equilibrium evolution and later hadronic evolution rather than from the QGP phase alone.

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental data on single hadron suppression in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC using the higher-twist (HT) approach for medium-modified fragmentation functions combined with a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD parton model for initial jet production. They assumed the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ is proportional to particle number density in both QGP and hadronic phases, using guidance from DIS off nuclear targets for the hadronic value, and then computed the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ within three different bulk evolution models: (1+1)d Bjorken expansion, (1+3)d ideal hydrodynamics, and a parton cascade model (BAMPS).

## Key numbers
- Extracted $\hat q_0\tau_0 = 0.54$ GeV$^2$ in the (1+3)d ideal hydrodynamic model.
- Extracted $\hat q_0\tau_0 = 0.96$ GeV$^2$ in the parton cascade model.
- The hadronic phase contributes 22-44% of the total jet quenching effect.
- The jet transport parameter in cold nuclear matter from DIS is $\hat q_N \approx 0.02$ GeV$^2$/fm at the center of a large nucleus.
- The nucleon density in the center of a large nucleus is $\rho_N = n_0 \approx 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The critical temperature for the QGP-to-hadron phase transition is $T_c = 170$ MeV, with a Bag constant $B = (247\ \text{MeV})^4$.
- The initial temperature at the center of the most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is $T_0 = 369$ MeV for the extracted $\hat q_0 = 0.9^{+0.05}_{-0.04}$ GeV$^2$/fm.

## Context
This work extends prior phenomenological studies of jet quenching at RHIC (Vitev 2002, Wang 2003, Eskola 2004, Renk 2006) by systematically comparing the extracted jet transport parameter across different bulk evolution models, including the effects of transverse expansion, radial flow, phase transition, and non-equilibrium evolution. It builds on the HT approach for medium-modified fragmentation functions (Guo and Wang 2000, Majumder 2007) and uses the jet transport parameter in cold nuclei from DIS (Deng 2009) to constrain the hadronic phase contribution. The results enable more accurate extraction of the QGP jet transport parameter by highlighting the need for realistic descriptions of early non-equilibrium parton evolution and hadronic interactions.
