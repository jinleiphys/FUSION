# Medium-induced gluon radiation with full resummation of multiple scatterings for realistic parton-medium interactions

> The new precision era of jet quenching observables at both RHIC and the LHC calls for an improved and more precise description of in-medium gluon emissions. The development of new theoretical tools and analytical calculations to tackle this challenge has been hampered by the inability to include the effects of multiple scatterings with the medium using a realistic model for the parton-medium interactions. In this paper, we show how the analytical expressions for the full in-medium spectrum, including the resummation of all multiple scatterings, can be written in a form where the numerical evaluation can be easily performed without the need of the usually employed harmonic or single hard approximations. We present the transverse momentum and energy-dependent medium-induced gluon emission distributions for known realistic interaction models to illustrate how our framework can be applied beyond the limited kinematic regions of previous calculations.

## Key claim

The authors show that the full medium-induced gluon emission spectrum, including the resummation of all multiple scatterings, can be numerically evaluated for realistic parton-medium interaction models without resorting to the harmonic (Gaussian) or single-hard (opacity) approximations, but the derivation assumes the emitted gluon is soft with \(\omega/E \ll 1\), a condition that will be relaxed in a subsequent publication.

## Method

The authors reorganize the BDMPS-Z expression for the medium-induced gluon spectrum by analytically integrating over the emission time in the amplitude, which removes the need to separate in-medium and vacuum-interference contributions. They then recast the remaining path integral and momentum-broadening factor as propagators that satisfy first-order linear differential equations in momentum space, which are solved numerically using conventional methods. The numerical evaluation is performed for two realistic collision rate models: a Yukawa-type interaction and a hard thermal loop (HTL) interaction, in a static thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling is fixed to \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) and the parent parton is taken as a quark with Casimir \(C_R = C_F = 4/9\).
- For the Yukawa interaction, the collision rate is \(V(\vec{q}) = 8\pi \mu^2 / (\vec{q}^2 + \mu^2)^2\), where \(\mu\) is the screening mass (related to the Debye mass).
- The rescaled gluon energy is defined as \(x = 2\omega / (\mu^2 L)\) and the rescaled transverse momentum as \(\kappa^2 = k^2 / \mu^2\).
- Results are shown for two medium opacities: \(n_0 L = 1\) and \(n_0 L = 5\).
- At large energy and transverse momentum, the full spectrum and the GLV first opacity approximation have the same magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) by providing a numerical framework that avoids the harmonic oscillator approximation (which suppresses high-\(k_\perp\) tails) and the opacity expansion (which misses LPM interferences). It follows the direction of Caron-Huot (2010) and contrasts with the Monte Carlo approach of Feal (2018). The method enables quantitative comparisons with first-opacity (GLV) results and qualitative comparisons with the harmonic oscillator, and it is intended to resolve conflicting extractions of medium properties from RHIC and LHC data (Andres 2016, Burke 2013). Future work will extend the framework to expanding media and relax the soft-gluon approximation.
