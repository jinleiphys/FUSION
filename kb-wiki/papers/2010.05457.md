# Bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow using Heavy Quarkonium Quantum Dynamics

> We introduce a framework called Heavy Quarkonium Quantum Dynamics (HQQD) which can be used to compute the dynamical suppression of heavy quarkonia propagating in the quark-gluon plasma using real-time in-medium quantum evolution. Using HQQD we compute large sets of real-time solutions to the Schrödinger equation using a realistic in-medium complex-valued potential. We sample 2 million quarkonia wave packet trajectories and evolve them through the QGP using HQQD to obtain their survival probabilities. The computation is performed using three different HQQD model parameter sets in order to estimate our systematic uncertainty. After taking into account final state feed down we compare our results to existing experimental data for the suppression and elliptic flow of bottomonium states and find that HQQD predictions are good agreement with available data for $R_{AA}$ as a function of $N_{\rm part}$ and $p_T$ collected at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 5.02 TeV. In the case of $v_2$ for the various states, we find that the path-length dependence of $Υ(1s)$ suppression results in quite small $v_2$ for $Υ(1s)$. Our prediction for the integrated elliptic flow for $Υ(1s)$ in the $10{-}90$\% centrality class, which now includes an estimate of the systematic error, is $v_2[Υ(1s)]$ = 0.003 $\pm$ 0.0007 $\pm\,^{0.0006}_{0.0013}$. We also find that, due to their increased suppression, excited bottomonium states have a larger elliptic flow. Based on this observation we make predictions for $v_2[Υ(2s)]$ and $v_2[Υ(3s)]$ as a function of centrality and transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The Heavy Quarkonium Quantum Dynamics (HQQD) framework, which solves the time-dependent Schrödinger equation with a complex in-medium potential for 2 million sampled trajectories, yields predictions for bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow that agree with available experimental data at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, but only after including final-state feed down and with systematic uncertainties estimated by varying the medium initialization time and Debye mass prefactor.

## Method

The authors solved the 3+1D time-dependent Schrödinger equation for bottomonium wave packets using a split-step pseudo-spectral method with discrete sine transforms, employing a complex-valued in-medium potential consisting of an internal-energy-based real part and a leading-order perturbative QCD imaginary part. They sampled 2 million Monte-Carlo-generated quarkonium wave-packet trajectories through a 3+1D anisotropic hydrodynamics background tuned to soft hadronic observables at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, with initial central temperature T0 = 630 MeV at τ0 = 0.25 fm/c and constant specific shear viscosity 4πη/s = 2. Survival probabilities were computed from quantum-mechanical overlaps with vacuum eigenstates, and a 9 × 9 feed-down matrix using vacuum branching ratios from the Particle Data Group was applied to obtain final-state abundances for comparison with experimental RAA and v2 data.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature of the hydrodynamics background: T0 = 630 MeV at τ0 = 0.25 fm/c
- Specific shear viscosity: 4πη/s = 2
- Number of sampled wave-packet trajectories: 2 million
- Lattice parameters: N = 4096 points in r, rmax = 19.7 fm, lattice spacing a ≈ 0.0048 fm
- Vacuum masses obtained for bottomonium states: {9.46, 10.0, 9.88, 10.36, 10.25, 10.13} GeV for Υ(1s), Υ(2s), χb(1p), Υ(3s), and χb(2p) respectively
- Predicted integrated elliptic flow for Υ(1s) in the 10-90% centrality class: v2[Υ(1s)] = 0.003 ± 0.0007 ±0.0013+0.0006
- Experimental cross sections used for Υ states at √s = 5.02 TeV: ⟨dσ[Υ(1s), Υ(2s), Υ(3s)]/dy⟩y = {57.6, 19, 6.8} nb
- Primordial cross sections for χb states: ⟨dσ[χb0(1p), χb1(1p), χb2(1p)]/dy⟩y = {3.72, 13.69, 16.1} nb; ⟨dσ[χb0(2p), χb1(2p), χb2(2p)]/dy⟩y = {3.27, 12.0, 14.15} nb
- QGP transition temperature: TQGP = 155 MeV
- Medium initialization time varied: τmed ∈ {0.25, 0.4, 0.6} fm/c
- Debye mass prefactor varied: λ ∈ {0.8, 1, 1.2}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Islam and Strickland, 2020) by providing more numerical details, a 9 × 9 feed-down matrix, and systematic uncertainty estimates from varying model parameters. It goes beyond earlier phenomenological approaches that used the adiabatic approximation (Margotta et al., 2011; Strickland and collaborators, 2011-2017; Krouppa and Strickland, 2015-2017; Jaiswal and Strickland, 2017; Bhaduri et al., 2018-2020) by solving the full time-dependent Schrödinger equation with a complex potential, building on the stochastic potential model framework of Akamatsu (2011-2014) and Kajimoto et al. (2017). The results enable future work incorporating in-medium thermal noise, initial octet state production, and singlet-octet transitions to improve quantitative agreement with experimental data.
