# Isobaric analog states in the f_{7/2} and g_{9/2} shells

> Calculations are performed for energies of isobaric analog states with isospins T=2 and T=3 in regions where they have been found experimentally e.g. f-p shell, and regions where they have not yet been found e.g. g_{9/2} near Z=50,N=50. We consider two approaches--one using binding energy formulas and Coulomb energies contained therein and the other using shell model calculations. It is noted that some (but not all) calculations yield very low excitation energies for the J=0^{+} T=2 isobaric analog state in ^{96} Ag.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the excitation energy of the J=0+ T=2 isobaric analog state in 96Ag to be just slightly above 1 MeV (1.121 MeV) using binding energy differences and Coulomb energy estimates, but notes that some shell-model calculations yield significantly lower values (0.842-0.900 MeV) while others yield higher values (1.640-1.917 MeV), indicating substantial model dependence.

## Method

The authors compute excitation energies of isobaric analog states using the formula E* = BE(Z+1,N-1) - BE(Z,N) + V_C, where binding energies come from the atomic mass evaluation and Coulomb energies are estimated from several semi-empirical formulas including the Bethe-Weizsäcker formula, a ten-term mass formula, the Duflo-Zuker approach, and the Anderson et al. fit to mirror nuclei and Coulomb displacement energies. They compare these predictions with shell-model calculations in both single-j and large model spaces using various effective interactions (GXPF1, jj44b, JUN45, INTd, CCGI) for nuclei in the f7/2 shell (A=44-60) and g9/2 shell (A=94-96).

## Key numbers

- For 44Sc: predicted excitation energy 2.873 MeV, experiment 2.779 MeV (agreement within 94 keV)
- For 46Sc: predicted excitation energy 5.024 MeV, experiment 5.022 MeV (agreement within 2 keV)
- For 52Mn: predicted excitation energy 2.905 MeV, experiment 2.926 MeV (agreement within 21 keV)
- For 60Cu: predicted excitation energy 2.520 MeV, experiment 2.536 MeV (agreement within 16 keV)
- For 94Rh: predicted excitation energy 2.585 MeV (no experimental value available)
- For 96Ag: predicted excitation energy 1.121 MeV (no experimental value available)
- Single-j shell-model results for 96Ag: 0.900 MeV (INTd interaction) and 0.842 MeV (CCGI interaction)
- Large-space shell-model results for 96Ag: 1.917 MeV (jj44b interaction) and 1.640 MeV (JUN45 interaction)
- Duflo-Zuker mass formula gives 0.530 MeV for 96Ag; ten-term formula gives -0.057 MeV (negative, implying ground state inversion)

## Context

This work extends the study of isobaric analog states from the well-studied f7/2 shell region (A=44-60) into the g9/2 shell near Z=50, N=50 (A=94-96), where experimental data are lacking. The authors compare their binding-energy-based approach with shell-model calculations using interactions such as GXPF1 (Honma et al., 2004), jj44b (Brown and Lisetskiy, unpublished), JUN45 (Honma et al., 2009), and single-j calculations (Escuderos, Zamick, Bayman, 2005; Zamick and Escuderos, 2012). The work aims to stimulate experimental searches for the J=0+ T=2 analog states in 94Rh and 96Ag, and to encourage development of unified approaches that simultaneously treat nuclear spectra and bulk properties such as binding energies.
