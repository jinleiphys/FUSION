# Production correlation of light (hyper-)nuclei in Au-Au collisions from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan

> Based on nucleons ($p$, $n$) and hyperons ($Λ$, $Ω^-$) formed at kinetic freeze-out from a quark combination model, we systematically study the production of light nuclei and hyper-nuclei in the hadronic coalescence picture. We present the analytical formula of the nucleus momentum distribution of two-body coalescence and that of three-body coalescence. We explain the experimental data of the transverse momentum spectra of deuteron ($d$), triton ($t$), helium-3 ($^3$He) and hypertriton ($^3_Λ$H) measured in Au-Au collisions from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan I and II, and also provide the corresponding predictions of different $Ω-$hypernuclei $H(pΩ^-)$, $H(nΩ^-)$ and $H(pnΩ^-)$. We further study the production correlations of different species of light (hyper-)nuclei and discuss their interesting behaviors as a function of collision energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a coalescence model, using nucleons and hyperons from a quark combination model as input, can simultaneously explain the transverse momentum spectra of deuteron, triton, helium-3, and hypertriton measured in Au-Au collisions across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energy range, but the model's success depends on the specific internal structure assumed for the hypertriton and on correcting the primordial hadron inputs for isospin asymmetry and weak/electromagnetic decay feed-down.

## Method

The authors extended an analytical coalescence model, previously developed for LHC energies, to RHIC energies by incorporating isospin asymmetry from gold nuclei and using a Quark Combination Model (QCM) to obtain the primordial momentum distributions of protons, neutrons, Λ, and Ω⁻ at kinetic freeze-out. They derived analytical formulas for two-body coalescence (for deuteron and Ω-hypernuclei) and three-body coalescence (for triton, helium-3, and hypertriton), integrating over Gaussian relative coordinate distributions with an effective source radius R_f, and applied these formulas at midrapidity for central Au-Au collisions at eleven collision energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron, triton, helium-3, and hypertriton p_T spectra were explained at eleven collision energies: √s_NN = 7.7, 9.2, 11.5, 14.5, 17.3, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4, 62.4, and 200 GeV in central Au-Au collisions.
* Predictions were provided for Ω-hypernuclei H(pΩ⁻), H(nΩ⁻), and H(pnΩ⁻) at the same eleven collision energies.
* The root-mean-square radius R_Hj of light (hyper-)nuclei is stated to be always about or larger than 2 fm, leading to a Gaussian width in the momentum-dependent kernel function of about or smaller than 0.1 GeV/c.
* The yield ratios and average transverse momentum ratios of different light (hyper-)nuclei were studied as a function of collision energy from √s_NN = 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coalescence model study for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (Wang 2024) to Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, using the Quark Combination Model (Feng 2022) to provide primordial hadron inputs. It directly addresses recent experimental data from the STAR collaboration's Beam Energy Scan I and II, including new measurements of ³He and ³_ΛH from BES II (Liu 2025, Li 2025) and earlier d and t data from BES I (STAR 2019). The study enables future comparisons with measurements from the China HyperNuclear Spectrometer at HIAF and the Compressed Baryonic Matter detector at FAIR, and tests whether a universal coalescence mechanism holds across a wide range of collision energies.
