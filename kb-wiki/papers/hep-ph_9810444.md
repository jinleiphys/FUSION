# Transversity distributions and spin asymmetries

> We discuss transversity distributions and transverse spin asymmetries. First, $Q^2$ evolution results are shown for transversity and longitudinally polarized distributions. Second, the antiquark flavor asymmetry $Δ_{_T} \bar u/Δ_{_T} \bar d$ is discussed in two different descriptions, a meson-cloud model and an exclusion model. Both calculations produce a significant $Δ_T \bar d$ excess over $Δ_{_T} \bar u$. Third, we explain general formalism of the polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes. This study is partly intended to find the asymmetry $Δ_{_T} \bar u/Δ_{_T} \bar d$ by the p-d asymmetry. However, the existence of tensor structure in the deuteron produces a number of new structure functions. There exist 108 structure functions and 22 ones even after integrating the cross section over the virtual-photon transverse momentum $\vec Q_T$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the transversity antiquark flavor asymmetry \(\Delta_T \bar u / \Delta_T \bar d\) can be extracted from combined polarized proton-proton and polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan measurements, but cautions that the tensor structure of the deuteron introduces many new structure functions that must be accounted for, making the extraction nontrivial.

## Method

The authors compute the next-to-leading-order \(Q^2\) evolution of transversity and longitudinally polarized parton distributions using their own evolution program. They then calculate the light antiquark flavor asymmetry \(\Delta_T \bar u / \Delta_T \bar d\) in two models: a meson-cloud model (where \(\rho\)-meson clouds contribute polarized antiquarks) and an exclusion model (based on Pauli blocking in the SU(6) quark model). Finally, they derive the general formalism for polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes, enumerating all structure functions allowed by Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance.

## Key numbers

- The singlet \(Q^2\) evolution of transversity distributions is shown to be significantly different from that of longitudinally polarized distributions, even when the initial distributions are identical (no explicit numerical values given for the evolution, but the difference is highlighted).
- In the polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan formalism, there exist **108 structure functions** in the general case, and **22 structure functions** after integrating over the virtual-photon transverse momentum \(\vec Q_T\) or taking the limit \(Q_T \rightarrow 0\).
- Among the 22 structure functions after integration, **11 are new** structure functions specific to spin-one hadrons, in addition to the 11 present in spin-1/2 hadron Drell-Yan processes.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on longitudinally polarized parton distributions (based on \(g_1\) data) to the unmeasured transversity sector, and builds on prior calculations of the \(Q^2\) evolution of transversity distributions by Hino et al. (1998) and on models of antiquark flavor asymmetry by Kumano and Miyama (1997) and by Kumano (1998). The formalism for polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes, completed by the same group (1998), enables future experiments at RHIC-Spin to extract \(\Delta_T \bar u / \Delta_T \bar d\) and to study new tensor structure functions of the deuteron.
