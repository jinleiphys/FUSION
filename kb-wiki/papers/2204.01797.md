# Pseudo-gauges and relativistic spin hydrodynamics for interacting Dirac and Proca fields

> We present the explicit expressions of different pseudo-gauge transformations for Dirac and Proca fields considering a general interaction term. The particular case of the interaction of Dirac and Proca fields with a background electromagnetic field is also studied. Starting from the quantum kinetic theory with collisions derived from the Wigner-function formalism for massive spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles, we establish a connection between different pseudo-gauges and relativistic spin hydrodynamics. The physical implications of the various decompositions of orbital and spin angular momentum are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit pseudo-gauge transformations for both Dirac (spin-1/2) and Proca (spin-1) fields with general interaction terms, showing that the Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen spin tensor is conserved only for free fields but not in the presence of nonlocal collisions, and that the canonical spin tensor lacks a clear physical interpretation as a spin density even for free fields.

## Method

The authors start from the quantum kinetic theory based on the Wigner-function formalism for massive spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles, including nonlocal collision terms derived from interacting Lagrangians. They compute the canonical energy-momentum and spin tensors via Noether's theorem, then apply pseudo-gauge transformations to obtain the Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen, de-Groot-van-Leeuwen-van-Weert, and alternative Klein-Gordon currents, expressing all tensors in terms of Wigner function components and distribution functions. For Proca fields, they present the pseudo-gauge transformations for both free and interacting cases, and for both field types they also study the case of coupling to a background electromagnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The canonical spin tensor for Dirac fields is given by \(S_{D,C}^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = -\frac{\hbar}{2} \epsilon^{\lambda\mu\nu\alpha} \int d^4p\, \mathcal{A}_\alpha\), where \(\mathcal{A}_\alpha\) is the axial-vector component of the Wigner function
- The Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen energy-momentum tensor for Dirac fields is \(T_{D,HW}^{\mu\nu} = \int d\Gamma\, p^\mu p^\nu f(x,p,\mathfrak{s}) + \mathcal{O}(\hbar^2)\), with \(d\Gamma \equiv d^4p\, \delta(p^2-m^2)\, dS\)
- The Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen spin tensor for Dirac fields is \(S_{D,HW}^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = \int d\Gamma\, p^\lambda \left(\frac{1}{2} \Sigma_\mathfrak{s}^{\mu\nu} - \frac{\hbar}{4m^2} p^{[\mu}\partial^{\nu]}\right) f(x,p,\mathfrak{s}) + \mathcal{O}(\hbar^2)\), where \(\Sigma_\mathfrak{s}^{\mu\nu} \equiv -\frac{1}{m} \epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} p_\alpha \mathfrak{s}_\beta\)
- The spin tensor for Proca fields in the Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen-like pseudo-gauge is \(S_{P,HW}^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{2m} \int d^4p\, p^\lambda \mathcal{S}^{\mu\nu}\), analogous to the Dirac case

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pseudo-gauge transformations for free spin-1/2 particles (Speranza, 2020) to include general interaction terms and to cover spin-1 Proca fields for the first time. It builds on the relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics derived from quantum kinetic theory for massive spin-1/2 particles with nonlocal collisions (Weickgenannt, 2020; Weickgenannt, 2021; Weickgenannt, 2022), providing the explicit pseudo-gauge transformations that underpin those results. The paper enables further development of relativistic spin hydrodynamics for both spin-1/2 and spin-1 systems, particularly relevant for understanding the "polarization sign problem" in heavy-ion collisions and the unexpectedly large spin alignment observed for vector mesons such as \(\phi\) and \(K^{\star 0}\) (ALICE, 2019; Mohanty, 2021).
