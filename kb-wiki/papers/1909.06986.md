# Effects of $P_{\mathrm{tot}}$ gates and velocity gates on light-particle momentum correlation in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Momentum correlation functions at small relative momenta are calculated for light particles $\left(n, p, d, t\right)$ emitted from $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at different impact parameters and beam energies within the framework of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model complemented by the $Lednick\acute{y}$ and $Lyuboshitz$ analytical method. We first make sure our model is able to reproduce the FOPI data of proton-proton momentum correlation in a wide energy range from 0.4$A$ GeV to 1.5$A$ GeV. Then we explore more physics insights through the emission times and momentum correlations among different light particles. The specific emphasize is the effects of total pair momentum among different light particles, impact parameters and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section. Both two-deuteron and two-triton correlation functions are anti-correlation due to the final state interaction, and they are affected by in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section for the higher total momentum of the particle pairs, but not for the lower ones. In addition, impact parameter and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section dependences of the emission source radii are extracted by fitting the momentum correlation functions. The results indicate that momentum correlation functions gating with total pair momentum is stronger for the smaller in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section factor $\left(η\right)$ or impact parameter $\left(b\right)$. Non-identical particle correlations ($np, pd, pt, $ and $dt$) are also investigated by the velocity-gated correlation functions which can give information of the particles' emission sequence, and the result indicates that heavier ones $\left(deuteron/triton\right)$ are, one the average, emitted earlier than protons, in the small relative momentum region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that momentum correlation functions for light particles (n, p, d, t) in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions are sensitive to total pair momentum gates and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, but this sensitivity is conditional: it is stronger for higher total pair momenta and weaker for lower total pair momenta, and the effect diminishes with increasing particle mass.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) transport model to generate phase-space distributions of emitted particles from 197Au + 197Au collisions, then applied the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz analytical formalism to construct momentum correlation functions at small relative momenta. They validated their approach by reproducing FOPI experimental data for proton-proton correlation functions across beam energies from 0.4A GeV to 1.5A GeV. Source radii were extracted by fitting correlation functions with a Gaussian source assumption.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range for model validation: 0.4A GeV to 1.5A GeV for Au + Au collisions
- Impact parameters used: b = 0.0 fm (central) and b = 6.0 fm (semi-peripheral)
- In-medium NNCS factors: η = 0.2 and η = 0.5
- Total momentum gates for neutron and proton pairs: low 0-0.4 GeV/c, high 0.8-1.2 GeV/c
- Total momentum gates for deuteron pairs: low 0-0.8 GeV/c, high 1.6-2.4 GeV/c
- Total momentum gates for triton pairs: low 0-1 GeV/c, high 2-3 GeV/c
- Proton and neutron emission time peaks at about 50 fm/c; deuteron and triton emission time peaks at about 60 fm/c
- Coalescence parameters: distance less than 3.5 fm, relative momentum smaller than 0.3 GeV/c
- Density threshold for emission: 0.02/fm^3
- Two-proton correlation function peak at q ≈ 20 MeV/c
- p-d correlation function peak at q ≈ 55 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-proton correlation functions in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, particularly the work of Ma (2006) and Wang (2018) which used the IQMD model with the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz method. It also builds on earlier experimental and theoretical investigations of light-particle correlations by Kotte (2005), Ghetti (2003), and Gong (1991). The results enable future studies of emission time sequences among different particle species and provide a method to probe the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section through momentum-gated correlation functions, especially for higher total pair momenta.
