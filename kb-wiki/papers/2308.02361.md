# Probing the magnetic field strength dependence of the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> The article presents a study aimed at probing the dependence of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) on the magnetic field strength using the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model in Pb--Pb at LHC energies. The results demonstrate the quadratic dependence of the correlators used for the study of the CME in heavy ion collisions on the number of spectators, a proxy of the magnitude of the magnetic field. The article also presents the extension of this approach to a two dimensional space, formed by both the aforementioned proxy of the magnetic field strength but also a proxy of the final state ellipticity, a key ingredient of the background in these measurements, for each centrality interval. This provides an exciting possibility to experiments to isolate the background contributions from the potential CME signal.

## Key claim

The quadratic dependence of the CME-sensitive correlator Δγ on the number of spectator nucleons (a proxy for magnetic field strength) is demonstrated using the AVFD model, and this dependence can be combined with event shape engineering on elliptic flow to create a two-dimensional grid that may allow experiments to isolate the CME signal from background contributions.

## Method

The study used the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework to generate Pb–Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, with initial conditions from a Glauber model, 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) starting at 0.6 fm/c, and a hadron cascade (UrQMD) after decoupling. Events in the 10–70% centrality range were analyzed, with the 50–60% centrality interval receiving 1M events for the two-dimensional study. The analysis selected events based on percentiles of the number of spectator nucleons (a proxy for magnetic field strength) and percentiles of the reduced flow vector q2 (a proxy for final-state ellipticity), then measured the charge-dependent correlator Δγ = γ_os − γ_ss.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers Pb–Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, with primary charged particles in |η| < 0.8 and 0.2 < pT < 5 GeV/c.
- The magnetic field lifetime τB was set to 0.2 fm/c, and the initial hydrodynamics time was 0.6 fm/c.
- Shear viscosity over entropy density η/s = 0.08 and bulk viscosity ζ/s = 0 were used.
- The decoupling energy density was ε = 0.18 fm/c^3.
- Around 100K events were produced for the 10–70% centrality range in 10% steps, plus 1M events for the 50–60% centrality interval.
- Each centrality interval was split into four subsamples corresponding to 0–25%, 25–50%, 50–75%, and 75–100% percentiles of spectator number and of q2.
- The quadratic dependence of Δγ on spectator number was confirmed by fitting with a second-order polynomial yielding a significantly non-zero second-order coefficient.

## Context

This work extends the AVFD-based study of Christakoglou (2021) by explicitly probing the magnetic field dependence of the CME correlators, rather than only tuning the model to describe experimental data. It builds on the event shape engineering technique introduced by Schukraft (2012) and the isobar comparison approach of STAR (2021), both of which yielded only upper limits on the CME. The proposed two-dimensional selection on spectator number and q2 provides a new experimental strategy to disentangle the CME signal from the dominant background of local charge conservation combined with elliptic flow, which has been the main obstacle since the early measurements by STAR (2009).
