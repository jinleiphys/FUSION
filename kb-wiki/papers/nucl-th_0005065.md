# Effect of changes in meson properties in a nuclear medium - $J/Ψ$ dissociation in nuclear matter, and meson-nucleus bound states

> We discuss the effect of changes in meson properties in a nuclear medium on physical observables, notably, $J/Ψ$ dissociation on pion and $ρ$ meson comovers in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and the prediction of the $ω$-, $η$- and $η'$-nuclear bound states.

## Key claim
Changes in the in-medium properties of charmed mesons (D, D*, and ρ) can substantially enhance the J/Ψ dissociation cross section on pion and ρ meson comovers in a density- and energy-dependent way, which the authors argue is necessary to explain the "anomalous" J/Ψ suppression observed in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method
The authors use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, a mean-field approach where quarks and antiquarks inside hadron bags interact with scalar (σ) and vector (ω, ρ) mean fields in nuclear matter. They calculate the in-medium masses of D, D*, ρ, and J/Ψ mesons by solving the Dirac equation for confined quarks and then use these modified masses to compute the π+J/Ψ → D*+D̄ and ρ+J/Ψ → D+D̄ dissociation cross sections as functions of nuclear density. For the ω-, η-, and η'-nuclear bound states, they solve the Klein-Gordon equation with the calculated mean-field potentials to obtain bound-state energies and widths.

## Key numbers
- The thermally averaged J/Ψ absorption cross section on pions at ρ_B = 3ρ_0 is about a factor of 7 larger than that at ρ_B = 0, for p_J/Ψ = 0 and a pion gas temperature of 120 MeV.
- The thermally averaged ρ+J/Ψ dissociation cross section at ρ_B = 3ρ_0 becomes larger than 1 mb.
- The nuclear absorption cross section for J/Ψ on nucleons is taken as 4.5 mb.
- For the ω-nuclear bound state in ²⁰⁸Pb, the 1s state energy is -118 MeV with a width of 33.1 MeV (QMC model, γ_ω = 0.2).
- For the η-nuclear bound state in ⁴⁰Ca, the 1s state energy is -46.0 MeV with a width of 31.7 MeV (γ_η = 0.5).
- For the η'-nuclear bound state in ⁴⁰Ca, the 1s state energy is -51.8 MeV (assuming Γ*_η' = 0).

## Context
This work extends the QMC model previously applied to nuclear matter and kaon systems (Guichon, 1988; Tsushima et al., 1998) to charmed mesons and J/Ψ dissociation. It directly addresses the debate between hadronic dissociation mechanisms (Brodsky, 1988; Capella, 1996) and QGP formation (Matsui and Satz, 1986) as explanations for J/Ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions. The authors show that their density-dependent, in-medium cross sections can reproduce the NA50 Pb+Pb data (1997) as well as phenomenological constant cross section models, but with a theoretically motivated basis. The predicted ω-, η-, and η'-nuclear bound states extend earlier work on meson-nucleus bound states (Hayano, 1999; Friman, 1999) and suggest experimental searches for these states are feasible.
