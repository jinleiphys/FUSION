# Novel Scalings of Neutron Star Properties from Analyzing Dimensionless Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff Equations

> The TOV equations govern the radial evolution of pressure and energy density in static neutron stars (NSs) in hydrodynamical equilibrium. Using the reduced pressure and energy density with respect to the NS central energy density, the original TOV equations can be recast into dimensionless forms. While the traditionally used integral approach for solving the original TOV equations require an input nuclear Equation of State (EOS), the dimensionless TOV equations can be anatomized by using the reduced pressure and energy density as polynomials of the reduced radial coordinate without using any input nuclear EOS. Interesting and novel perspectives about NS core EOS can be extracted directly from NS observables using this new approach based on Intrinsic and Perturbative Analyses of the Dimensionless (IPAD) TOV equations (IPAD-TOV). In this review, we first discuss the length and energy density scales of NSs as well as the dimensionless TOV equations for scaled variables and their perturbative solutions near NS cores. We then review several new insights into NS physics gained from using the IPAD-TOV. We also demonstrate that the strong-field gravity plays a fundamental role in extruding a peak in the density/radius profile of the speed of sound squared (SSS) in massive NS cores independent of the nuclear EOS. Finally, some future perspectives of NS research using the IPAD-TOV are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by recasting the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations into dimensionless forms using reduced pressure and energy density scaled to the central energy density, one can extract neutron star core equation of state (EOS) properties directly from observational data without requiring any input nuclear EOS model, though the authors acknowledge that to evaluate accuracy and determine truncation orders they do use existing EOS models and randomly generated EOSs, making the scalings "largely instead of absolutely independent of nuclear EOS models."

## Method

The authors reformulate the original TOV equations into dimensionless forms by defining reduced pressure and energy density with respect to the neutron star central energy density, then expand these reduced quantities as polynomials of the reduced radial coordinate near the neutron star center. This Intrinsic and Perturbative Analysis of the Dimensionless (IPAD-TOV) approach allows perturbative solutions near neutron star cores without specifying any nuclear EOS model, in contrast to the traditional bottom-up approach where an EOS is first constructed and then inserted into the TOV equations to obtain mass-radius relations. The authors verify their scaling relations using a large set of microscopic and phenomenological nuclear EOSs from the literature as well as 10^5 randomly generated nuclear EOSs in a meta-model.

## Key numbers

- The energy density at nuclear saturation density is approximately 150 MeV/fm^3 (denoted as epsilon_0).
- Causality in neutron stars sets an upper bound of about 0.374 for the ratio of pressure over energy density (phi = P/epsilon).
- The constant relating solar mass to kilometers is approximately 1.477 (Sigma = M_sun/km).
- The nuclear saturation density rho_0 is approximately 0.16 fm^(-3).

## Context

This work extends the authors' own recent series of papers (Cai, Li, and collaborators, 2023-2024) developing the IPAD-TOV approach, and contrasts with the traditional bottom-up method where nuclear EOS models are constructed from low to high densities using various inputs and constraints before being inserted into the TOV equations. The approach enables direct extraction of core EOS properties from neutron star observables without relying on uncertain nuclear EOS models, and the authors demonstrate that a linear EOS of the form P = epsilon/3 for an ultra-relativistic Fermi gas in neutron star cores is excluded by analyzing the scaled TOV equations alone. The work also addresses questions about the speed of sound squared profiles, the trace anomaly, and causality boundaries that have been investigated by many others in the neutron star physics community.
