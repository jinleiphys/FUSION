# Nuclear matter symmetry energy and the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei

> Correlations between the thickness of the neutron skin in finite nuclei and the nuclear matter symmetry energy are studied in the Skyrme Hartree-Fock model. From the most recent analysis of the isospin diffusion data in heavy-ion collisions based on an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, a value of $L=88\pm 25$ MeV for the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density is extracted, and this imposes stringent constraints on both the parameters in the Skyrme effective interactions and the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei. Predicted thickness of the neutron skin is $0.22\pm 0.04$ fm for $% ^{208}$Pb, $0.29\pm 0.04$ fm for $^{132}$Sn, and $0.22\pm 0.04$ fm for $% ^{124}$Sn.

## Key claim

The slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density is extracted as \(L=88\pm 25\) MeV from isospin diffusion data in heavy-ion collisions, and this value imposes stringent constraints on both Skyrme interaction parameters and the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei; the predicted neutron skin thicknesses are \(0.22\pm 0.04\) fm for \({}^{208}\)Pb, \(0.29\pm 0.04\) fm for \({}^{132}\)Sn, and \(0.22\pm 0.04\) fm for \({}^{124}\)Sn, but these values depend on the specific linear correlation derived from 21 Skyrme interactions and the schematic determination of \(L\) from only four MDI interaction parameter values.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme Hartree-Fock model to compute the neutron skin thickness \(S\) (difference between root-mean-square radii of neutrons and protons) for \({}^{208}\)Pb, \({}^{132}\)Sn, \({}^{124}\)Sn, and \({}^{48}\)Ca using 21 sets of Skyrme interaction parameters. They then extracted the slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density by comparing the isospin diffusion observable \(1-R_i\) from the IBUU04 transport model (with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections) to experimental data from NSCL/MSU for \({}^{124}\)Sn + \({}^{112}\)Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter about 6 fm. Linear fits between \(S\) and \(L\) for each heavy nucleus were used to convert the extracted \(L\) value into predicted neutron skin thicknesses.

## Key numbers

- Extracted slope of nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: \(L = 88 \pm 25\) MeV, from isospin diffusion data in \({}^{124}\)Sn + \({}^{112}\)Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon.
- Predicted neutron skin thickness for \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(0.22 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- Predicted neutron skin thickness for \({}^{132}\)Sn: \(0.29 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- Predicted neutron skin thickness for \({}^{124}\)Sn: \(0.22 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- Linear correlation coefficient between \(S\) and \(L\) for \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(99.25\%\); for \({}^{132}\)Sn: \(98.76\%\); for \({}^{124}\)Sn: \(98.75\%\); for \({}^{48}\)Ca: \(93.66\%\).
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density from the 21 Skyrme interactions: range \(26\) to \(35\) MeV.
- Values of \(L\) from the 21 Skyrme interactions: range \(-50\) to \(100\) MeV.
- Values of \(K_{\text{sym}}\) from the 21 Skyrme interactions: range \(-700\) to \(50\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Brown (2000), Horowitz (2001), Typel (2001), Furnstahl (2002), and others that established a correlation between neutron skin thickness and the slope \(L\) of the symmetry energy. It uses the isospin diffusion data from Tsang (2004) and the IBUU04 transport model analysis by Chen (2005) and Li (2005) to provide the first quantitative constraint on \(L\) from heavy-ion collisions, which then yields predictions for neutron skin thickness that are consistent with but more precise than existing experimental data (which range from 0.1 to 0.28 fm for \({}^{208}\)Pb). The results enable future parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Laboratory to test the predicted neutron skin thickness of \({}^{208}\)Pb within 0.05 fm, and they agree surprisingly well with predictions from a relativistic mean-field parametrization by Piekarewicz (2005).
