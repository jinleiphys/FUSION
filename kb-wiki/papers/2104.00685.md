# Magnetic monopole in a chiral plasma: chiral dyon

> The placement of a magnetic monopole into an electrically-neutral chiral plasma with a non-zero axial density results in an electric polarization of the matter. The electric current produced by the chiral magnetic effect is balanced by charge diffusion and Ohmic dissipation, which generates a non-trivial charge distribution. In turn, the latter induces a separation of chiralities along the magnetic field of the monopole due to the chiral separation effect. We find the stationary states of such a system, with vanishing total electric current and stationary axial current balanced by the chiral anomaly. In this solution, the monopole becomes "dressed" with an electric charge that is proportional to the averaged chiral density of the matter -- forming a chiral dyon. The interplay between the chiral effects on the one hand, and presence of magnetic field of the monopole on the other, may affect the evolution of the monopole density in the early Universe, contribute to the process of baryogenesis, and can also be instrumental for detection of relic monopoles using chiral materials.

## Key claim

A magnetic monopole placed into an electrically neutral chiral plasma with non-zero axial density becomes "dressed" with an electric charge proportional to the averaged chiral density, forming a chiral dyon. This result holds under the assumptions of a finite monopole radius, uniform hot matter with temperature as the largest energy scale, and linearized constitutive relations for the currents.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled system of vector current conservation and anomalous axial current non-conservation in the stationary limit, using linearized hydrodynamic constitutive relations that include the chiral magnetic effect, chiral separation effect, charge diffusion, and Ohmic dissipation. They impose boundary conditions of vanishing electric current, no axial current flow at the monopole surface, and total electric neutrality, then find analytic solutions for the radial profiles of electric and axial densities in the infinite volume limit.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless parameter controlling the solution is beta = (1/(2 pi)^3) * (e g)/(a sigma), where e is elementary charge, g is monopole charge, a is monopole radius, and sigma is conductivity.
- For free Dirac fermions at high temperature with e g = 2 pi, the total electric charge of the chiral dyon is q/e = -(1/(pi * bar_sigma)) * (bar_mu_5 / T), where bar_sigma = e^2 sigma / T and bar_mu_5 is the average axial chemical potential.
- For the early Universe plasma at T ~ 10-100 GeV, bar_sigma ~ 140, giving q/e < 1 for bar_mu_5 up to T.
- For QGP at T ~ 160 MeV, bar_sigma ~ 10^-2 to 10^-1, allowing q ~ e when bar_mu_5 is an order of magnitude below T.
- The Debye mass m = e sqrt(kappa) ~ e T, with kappa = T^2/3 for non-interacting Dirac fermions.
- The electric charge cloud has thickness delta r = 1/m and total charge q = -(1/(2 pi^2)) * (e g)/(sigma m^2) * bar_n_5 * e.

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous transport phenomena in chiral matter, specifically the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect (CSE), which have been investigated in contexts ranging from quark-gluon plasma to Weyl semimetals. The formation of a chiral dyon is related to the chiral magnetic wave (Kharzeev 2010) and chiral magnetic instability (Joyce 1997, Akamatsu 2013), but represents a novel stationary configuration. The results may affect models of monopole evolution in the early Universe and baryogenesis, and could enable detection of relic monopoles using chiral materials such as Weyl semimetals.
