# Dispersive $πK\to πK$ and $ππ\to K \bar{K}$ amplitudes from scattering data, threshold parameters and the lightest strange resonance $κ$ or $K^*_0(700)$

> We discuss the simultaneous dispersive analyses of $πK\to πK$ and $ππ\to K \bar{K}$ scattering data and the $κ/K^*_0(700)$ resonance. The unprecedented statistics of present and future Hadron Experiments, modern Lattice QCD calculations, and the wealth of new states and decays, require such precise and model-independent analyses to describe final state interactions. We review the existing and often conflicting data and explain in detail the derivation of the relevant dispersion relations, maximizing their applicability range. Next, we review and extend the caveats on some data, showing their inconsistency with dispersion relations. Our main result is the derivation and compilation of precise amplitude parameterizations constrained by several $πK\to πK$ and $ππ\to K \bar{K}$ dispersion relations. These constrained parameterizations are easily implementable and provide the rigor and accuracy needed for modern experimental and phenomenological Hadron Physics. As applications, after reviewing their status and interest, we will provide new precise threshold and subthreshold parameters and review our dispersive determination of the controversial $κ/K^*_0(700)$ resonance and other light-strange resonances.

## Key claim

The authors derive precise, dispersion-relation-constrained parameterizations of the piK to piK and pipi to K anti-K scattering amplitudes that are model-independent and easily implementable, but they note that some existing scattering data are inconsistent with dispersion relations and must be excluded or reweighted to achieve consistency.

## Method

The authors simultaneously analyze piK to piK and pipi to K anti-K scattering data using forward dispersion relations, Roy-Steiner equations, and hyperbolic dispersion relations to maximize the applicability range. They review existing experimental data, identify inconsistencies with dispersion relations, and then perform constrained fits (UFD and CFD) to produce amplitude parameterizations that satisfy multiple dispersion relation constraints simultaneously.

## Key numbers

* The constrained fit (CFD) yields a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.7 for the Re f0-(s) dispersion relation, compared to 14.1 for the unconstrained fit (UFD), demonstrating dramatically improved consistency with dispersion relations.
* The paper presents differential cross sections for pipi to K anti-K at t=0 as a function of sqrt(t) in GeV, with values ranging from 0 to approximately 50 mb for the I=0 channel and 0 to approximately 30 mb for the I=1 channel.
* The delta 3/2_2(s) phase shift is shown as a function of sqrt(s) from approximately 0.8 to 1.6 GeV, with values ranging from 0 to 180 degrees.
* The modulus sqrt(5)*|hat(f_D)(s)| is shown as a function of sqrt(s) from approximately 0.8 to 1.6 GeV, with values ranging from 0 to approximately 1.2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersive analyses of pion-pion and pion-kaon scattering by simultaneously constraining both the piK to piK and pipi to K anti-K channels. It provides the rigorous, model-independent amplitude parameterizations needed for modern hadron experiments, lattice QCD calculations (such as those by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration), and phenomenological studies of final-state interactions. The results enable precise determination of threshold and subthreshold parameters and a dispersive determination of the controversial kappa/K*_0(700) resonance, as well as other light-strange resonances.
