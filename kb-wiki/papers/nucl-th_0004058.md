# Properties of $^{12}$C in the {\it ab initio} nuclear shell-model

> We obtain properties of $^{12}$C in the {\it ab initio} no-core nuclear shell-model. The effective Hamiltonians are derived microscopically from the realistic CD-Bonn and the Argonne V8' nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials as a function of the finite harmonic oscillator basis space. Binding energies, excitation spectra and electromagnetic properties are presented for model spaces up to $5\hbarΩ$. The favorable comparison with available data is a consequence of the underlying NN interaction rather than a phenomenological fit.

## Key claim

The no-core shell-model, using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials without phenomenological fitting, produces a reasonable description of the 0 and 1-ℏΩ-dominated states of ¹²C, but the binding energy (about 88 MeV in the 4ℏΩ space) is estimated to be within 10% of the exact two-body solution and likely requires a true three-body NN interaction to reach the experimental value.

## Method

The authors start from the two-body Hamiltonian for the A-nucleon system with realistic CD-Bonn and Argonne V8' NN potentials, add a harmonic-oscillator center-of-mass term, and derive a model-space-dependent effective Hamiltonian using a unitary transformation (Da Providencia-Shakin approach) with the Lee-Suzuki decoupling condition. They solve the many-body problem in finite harmonic-oscillator model spaces up to 5ℏΩ using the m-scheme Many-Fermion Dynamics code, with matrix dimensions reaching 6,488,004 at Nmax=5, and neglect higher than two-body clusters in the unitary transformed Hamiltonian expansion.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy: 88.518 MeV (CD-Bonn, 4ℏΩ space) and 87.675 MeV (AV8', 4ℏΩ space), compared to experimental 92.162 MeV
- Ground-state point-proton rms radius: 2.199 fm (CD-Bonn, 4ℏΩ space) and 2.202 fm (AV8', 4ℏΩ space), compared to experimental 2.35(2) fm
- Excitation energy of 2⁺ 0 state: 3.697 MeV (CD-Bonn, 4ℏΩ space) and 3.584 MeV (AV8', 4ℏΩ space), compared to experimental 4.439 MeV
- Excitation energy of 0⁺ T=2 state: between 27 and 29 MeV depending on potential and model space, compared to experimental 27.595 MeV
- Ground-state wave function in 4ℏΩ calculation: about 61% 0ℏΩ component; occupancy of 0p3/2 level about 5.74 nucleons, 0p1/2 level about 1.90 nucleons
- Renormalization correction to point-proton rms radius: +0.06 fm (Nmax=0), +0.02 fm (Nmax=2), +0.01 fm (Nmax=4)
- Lowest 2ℏΩ 0⁺ state: about 40 MeV excitation in 2ℏΩ space, dropping to about 35 MeV in 4ℏΩ space (experimental GQR strength observed at 18-28 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell-model approach, previously applied successfully to three- and four-nucleon systems (Navratil and Barrett, 1998; Navratil, Kamuntavicius and Barrett, 2000), to the vastly more complex ¹²C nucleus. It contrasts with earlier multi-ℏΩ shell-model studies of ¹²C (Karataglidis, 1995; Suzuki and Sagawa, 1998; Hayes and Towner, 1999) that used phenomenological effective interactions rather than realistic NN potentials. The results enable future predictions of cross sections for neutrino and muon reactions with ¹²C, relevant for neutrino studies using liquid scintillator detectors and for parity-violating electron scattering measurements of nucleon strangeness content.
