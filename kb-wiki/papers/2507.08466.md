# Spectroscopic and femtoscopic insights into vector-baryon interactions in the strangeness $-1$ sector

> We revisit the strangeness $-1$ sector of the interaction between vector mesons of the $ρ-$nonet and $1/2^+$ ground state baryons ($VB$), within the unitary coupled-channel hidden gauge formalism. We adopt a renormalization scheme that induces a reasonable short-distance behavior of the two-hadron wave functions, which is the major limitation of femtoscopy techniques at this time. We perform an exhaustive spectroscopy study, implementing different improvements and considerations, and find compatibilities between the poles extracted from the present approach, and some of the states listed in the Review of Particle Physics. Finally, we predict several correlation functions (CFs) associated with various meson-baryon pairs in this sector, paying special attention to the distinctive and clear signatures produced by the dynamically generated states. To obtain realistic estimates for the CFs, we have calculated the production weights using the Thermal-FIST package. Such studies will shed light into the odd-parity $Λ^*$ and $Σ^*$ hadron spectra, up to 2 GeV, and will open the strategy to improve effective field theories by using low-energy constants fitted to femtoscopy data.

## Key claim

The authors revisit the strangeness $-1$ sector of vector-baryon interactions using the hidden gauge formalism with a renormalization scheme that induces a reasonable short-distance behavior of the two-hadron wave functions, which is the major limitation of femtoscopy techniques at this time. The predicted correlation functions are intended as qualitative reliable theoretical predictions for future comparison with experimental data, rather than definitive quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors employ the unitary coupled-channel hidden gauge formalism to compute vector-baryon scattering amplitudes in the strangeness $-1$ sector, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels with a scale-free dimensional regularization scheme using a single UV cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 830$ MeV. They include convolution of the loop functions with the mass distributions of unstable $\rho$ and $K^*$ mesons to account for their finite widths. Correlation functions are calculated using the Koonin-Pratt formalism with production weights obtained from the Thermal-FIST package using the $\gamma_S$CSM model, and source radii are taken from ALICE measurements for analogous channels.

## Key numbers

- UV cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 830$ MeV, fixed to optimally reproduce the experimental position of the $I=1$ $\Sigma^*$ resonances
- Source radius $R_{\bar{K}^*N} = 1.08$ fm, taken from ALICE measurements of $\bar{K}N$ and $\phi N$ pairs
- Source radii $R_{V\Lambda} = R_{V\Sigma} = 1.28$ fm, approximately equivalent to the double Gaussian parametrization used by ALICE for $\bar{K}\Lambda$ pairs
- Source radius $R_{K^*\Xi} = 1.0$ fm for heavier $K^*\Xi$ channels
- A 10% error is associated to all source radii
- Production weights for the $\bar{K}^{*0}p$ CF: $\bar{K}^{*0}p$ weight = 1 (reference), $\rho^+\Lambda$ weight = $1.49 \pm 0.11$, $\rho^0\Sigma^+$ weight = $2.15 \pm 0.17$
- Production weights for the $\phi\Lambda$ CF: $\bar{K}^{*0}n$ weight = $5.91 \pm 0.53$, $K^{*-}p$ weight = $5.55 \pm 0.51$, $\rho^0\Lambda$ weight = $4.49 \pm 0.42$

## Context

This work extends the hidden gauge formalism study of Oset and Ramos (2010) for vector-baryon interactions in the strangeness $-1$ sector, employing an improved renormalization scheme and benefiting from updated experimental knowledge of resonances. It builds on recent femtoscopy studies including the ALICE measurement of the $\phi p$ correlation function and the analysis of Feijoo et al. (2024) that used $\phi p$ CF data to constrain the theoretical interaction model. The predictions enable future comparison with upcoming ALICE measurements of vector-baryon correlation functions in the $S=0$ and $S=-1$ sectors, and may help resolve the odd-parity $\Lambda^*$ and $\Sigma^*$ hadron spectra up to 2 GeV.
