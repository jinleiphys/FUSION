# Meson Mixing and Dilepton Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the possibility of $ρ-a_0$ mixing via N-N excitations in dense nuclear matter. This mixing is found to induce a peak in the dilepton spectra at an invariant mass equal to that of the $a_0$. We calculate the cross section for dilepton production through mixing and we compare its size with that of $π-π$ annihilation. In-medium masses and mixing angles are also calculated. Some preliminary results of the mixing effect on the dilepton production rates at finite temperature are also presented.

# Meson Mixing and Dilepton Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper proposes that ρ–a₀ mixing via nucleon-nucleon excitations in dense nuclear matter opens a new dilepton production channel (π + η → e⁺ + e⁻) that produces an additional peak at the a₀ mass in the dilepton spectrum; however, the authors caution that this may appear not as a distinct peak but as a shoulder in the φ spectrum, and the effect is exclusively density- and temperature-dependent.

## Method
The authors calculate the in-medium ρ–a₀ mixing amplitude using a nucleon-nucleon loop polarization tensor derived from an interaction Lagrangian with σ, a₀, ω, and ρ meson couplings to nucleons. They compute the mixing angle by diagonalizing the mass matrix and evaluate the dilepton production cross section for π + η → e⁺ + e⁻ through the mixing channel, comparing it with the standard π–π annihilation channel. Preliminary finite-temperature dilepton production rates are obtained using the independent particle approximation of kinetic theory assuming a thermal gas of mesons at T = 50 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Mixing angle becomes appreciable for momenta beyond |q| ≈ 0.2 GeV/c at a density of 1.5 times normal nuclear matter density.
- At density ρ/ρ₀ = 1.5 and T = 50 MeV, the mixing contribution to the dilepton production rate exceeds the π–π annihilation rate in the vicinity of M = 1.0 GeV.
- The dispersion curves and mixing angle are shown at density ρ = 2.5 ρ₀.
- The contribution at the a₀ mass from the mixing channel is comparable to that of π + π → e⁺ + e⁻ near the ρ peak for densities higher than ρ₀.

## Context
This work extends studies of in-medium vector meson properties, particularly the suggestion by Brown (1991) that vector meson masses drop in nuclear matter as a precursor to chiral symmetry restoration. It builds on prior calculations of ρ meson propagation in matter by Abhee et al. (1997) and σ–ω mixing by Saito et al. (1998). The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton measurements from experiments such as DLS at LBL, HELIOS and CERES at CERN, and future measurements by PHENIX at RHIC and HADES at GSI. The paper enables experimental searches for density-dependent signatures in the dilepton spectrum near the φ mass region.
