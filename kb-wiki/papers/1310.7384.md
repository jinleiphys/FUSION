# Nucleon pair approximation description of the low-lying structure of $^{108,109}$Te and $^{109}$I

> The low-lying level schemes and electromagnetic transitions of $^{109}$Te, $^{109}$I, and the neighboring even-even nucleus $^{108}$Te, are calculated within the framework of the $SD$-pair approximation of the nuclear shell model. Good agreement is obtained between the calculated results and experimental data. The favored components of low-lying bands are discussed in the collective nucleon-pair subspace. The weak-coupling picture shown in these nuclei and its relationship with residual quadrupole-quadrupole interaction between valence protons and neutrons are analyzed.

## Key claim

The nucleon pair approximation (NPA) with only SD pairs reproduces the low-lying level schemes and electromagnetic transitions of $^{108,109}$Te and $^{109}$I, with the caveat that the quadrupole-quadrupole proton-neutron interaction strength must be weaker than values predicted by empirical formulas to achieve agreement with all available experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed calculations within the SD-pair approximation of the nuclear shell model, using a Hamiltonian with monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions for valence protons and neutrons in the $^{100}$Sn core. The nucleon pair subspace was constructed by coupling collective S and D pairs for even-even $^{108}$Te, and by coupling these pairs to an unpaired nucleon in a single-j orbit for odd-mass $^{109}$Te and $^{109}$I. The seven two-body interaction parameters were fitted to excitation energies and B(E2) values in the three nuclei, with the proton-neutron quadrupole-quadrupole strength varied to study sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- B(E2, $2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+$) in $^{108}$Te: NPA gives 733 $e^2$fm$^4$, experiment gives 780($^{+100}_{-80}$) $e^2$fm$^4$
- B(E2, $11/2_1^+ \rightarrow 7/2_1^+$) in $^{109}$I: NPA gives 762 $e^2$fm$^4$, experiment gives 710(90) $e^2$fm$^4$
- B(E2, $9/2_2^+ \rightarrow 5/2_1^+$) in $^{109}$Te: NPA gives 595 $e^2$fm$^4$, experiment gives 608(392) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Overlap of $^{109}$Te $|5/2_1^+\rangle$ with $|(\nu d_{5/2})\otimes ^{108}\text{Te}(0_1^+)\rangle$: 0.94
- Overlap of $^{109}$I $|7/2_1^+\rangle$ with $|(\pi g_{7/2})\otimes ^{108}\text{Te}(0_1^+)\rangle$: 0.82
- Overlap of $^{109}$Te $|11/2_1^-\rangle$ with $|(\nu h_{11/2})\otimes ^{108}\text{Te}(0_1^+)\rangle$: 0.95
- Overlap of $^{109}$Te $|15/2_1^-\rangle$ with $|(\nu h_{11/2})\otimes ^{108}\text{Te}(2_1^+)\rangle$: 0.98
- Overlap of $^{109}$Te $|19/2_1^-\rangle$ with $|(\nu h_{11/2})\otimes ^{108}\text{Te}(4_1^+)\rangle$: 0.99
- Overlap of $^{109}$Te $|23/2_1^-\rangle$ with $|(\nu h_{11/2})\otimes ^{108}\text{Te}(6_1^+)\rangle$: 0.99
- Neutron effective charge: $e_\nu = 1.28 e$; proton effective charge: $e_\pi = 1.79 e$
- Optimal proton-neutron quadrupole-quadrupole strength: $\kappa_{\pi\nu} \sim -0.05$ MeV/$r_0^4$

## Context

This work extends the nucleon pair approximation (NPA) to neutron-deficient tellurium and iodine isotopes near $N=Z=50$, following previous NPA applications to nuclei with A~80, 100, 130, and 210. The results support the weak-coupling picture suggested by recent lifetime measurements on $^{108}$Te (Te108, 2013), $^{109}$Te (Te109, 2013), and $^{109}$I (I109-2, 2013), where the additional unpaired nucleon couples weakly to the even-even $^{108}$Te core. The analysis shows that the quadrupole-quadrupole correlation in realistic shell-model interactions may be too strong for this region, as shell-model calculations (Te109, I109-2) reproduced experimental B(E2) values only for $^{108}$Te but not for the odd-mass nuclei. The NPA enables systematic investigation of the sensitivity of B(E2) values to the proton-neutron interaction strength, which would be computationally prohibitive in the full shell model.
