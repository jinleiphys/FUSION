# The effects of colored quark entropy on the bag pressure

> We study the effects of the ground state entropy of colored quarks upon the bag pressure at low temperatures. The vacuum expectation values of the quark and gluon fields are used to express the interactions in QCD ground state in the limit of low temperatures and chemical potentials. Apparently, the inclusion of this entropy in the equation of state provides the hadron constituents with an additional heat which causes a decrease in the effective latent heat inside the hadronic bag and consequently decreases the non-perturbative bag pressure. We have considered two types of baryonic bags, $Δ$ and $Ω^-$. In both cases we have found that the bag pressure decreases with the temperature. On the other hand, when the colored quark ground state entropy is not considered, the bag pressure as conventionally believed remains constant for finite temperature.

# The effects of colored quark entropy on the bag pressure

## Key claim
Including the ground state (quantum) entropy of colored quarks in the equation of state for baryonic bags causes the bag pressure to decrease with temperature, whereas without this entropy the bag pressure remains constant at finite temperatures. The effect is demonstrated for two specific baryonic bags (Δ and Ω⁻) at low temperatures, with the caveat that the results apply in the limit of low temperatures and zero quark chemical potential.

## Method
The authors use the phenomenological bag model of QCD, expressing interactions in the QCD ground state via vacuum expectation values of quark and gluon fields (quark and gluon condensates) in the limit of low temperatures and chemical potentials. They incorporate the ground state entropy of colored quarks from SU(3)ₐ color symmetry into the trace anomaly equation of state, then numerically solve for the bag pressure B as a function of temperature for two baryonic systems: the Δ (three light quarks) and the Ω⁻ (three strange quarks). The gluon contribution is approximated as an ideal gas with spin degeneracy, and the quark condensates are taken as temperature-independent at low T.

## Key numbers
- The gluon condensate value used is 1.95 GeV/fm³ (extracted from charmonium spectrum), with a specific adopted value of 1.91 GeV/fm³ for the Δ bag calculations.
- For the Ω⁻ bag, the vacuum expectation value used is 1.68 GeV/fm³.
- Light quark masses are taken as m_q = 6 MeV (u and d degenerate); strange quark mass is m_s = 150 MeV.
- Light quark condensate: −42 MeV/fm³; strange quark condensate: −273 MeV/fm³.
- Without quantum entropy, the bag pressure for the Δ bag increases by only 0.04 MeV/fm³ over 30 MeV temperature range, remaining essentially constant below 20 MeV.
- With quantum entropy included, the bag pressure shows an almost linear decrease with temperature for both baryonic bags.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of colored quark ground state entropy (Miller, 2003; Miller and Tawfik, 2004) by applying it to the bag model equation of state. It contrasts with the conventional bag model assumption of a constant bag pressure, and relates to finite-density QCD sum rule calculations (Furnstahl, Jin, and Lee) that predict a reduction in B. The results are relevant for understanding confinement, the thermodynamics of hybrid stars, and color superconductivity, and connect to lattice QCD results for the entropy of static quark-antiquark pairs (Kaczmarek, Karsch, et al., 2000) which give a value of 2 ln 3 at vanishing temperature.
