# The Interest in Kaonic-Deuterium Atoms

> K meson-deuteron scattering lengths, atomic level shifts and widths are calculated. These quantities depend on controversial kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes extrapolated by few MeV below the $KN$ threshold. Experimental consequences of several $KN$ interaction models are studied.

## Key claim

The kaonic-deuterium atomic level shifts and widths are sensitive to the controversial kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes extrapolated a few MeV below the KN threshold, and the widths of the 2P and 3D states in particular should distinguish between models that reproduce scattering data and those that reproduce the kaonic-hydrogen 1S level shift.

## Method

The kaon-deuteron scattering length was calculated by summing the multiple scattering series derived from three-body Faddeev equations, expressing the result in terms of deuteron radius, nucleon-nucleon interactions, and subthreshold kaon-nucleon S-wave scattering amplitudes averaged over the recoil energy distribution of the spectator nucleon. The atomic level shifts and widths for the 1S, 2P, and 3D states were then obtained from the scattering lengths using the Trueman formula for S-waves and the Lambert formula for higher partial waves, with input effective KN amplitudes taken from seven different theoretical models and phenomenological analyses.

## Key numbers

* The 1S level shift for the Martin model is 0.76 keV with a half-width of 0.64 keV; for the Schnick model it is 0.58 keV with a half-width of 2.87 keV.
* The 2P half-width for the Martin model is 12 meV; for the Schnick model it is 3.7 meV.
* The 3D half-width for the Martin model is 6.9 x 10^-2 micro-eV; for the Kumar model it is 2.3 x 10^-2 micro-eV.
* The Bohr radius for the kaonic-deuterium 1S state is 69.2 fm.
* The impulse approximation KD scattering length for the test case (a_Kn = -0.1 + i0.5 fm, a_Kp = -1.0 + i1.5 fm) is -1.39 + i2.42 fm; the fully converged third-order result is -1.24 + i0.95 fm.
* The recoil energy distributions for the KN subsystems peak at -12 MeV, -7 MeV, and -5 MeV for L = 0, 1, and 2 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the multiple-scattering formalism previously applied to eta-deuteron scattering by Green, Niskanen, and Wycech (1996) to the kaon-deuteron system. The results are motivated by the unresolved discrepancy between the Kp scattering length from scattering experiments (Ciborowski et al., 1982) and that extracted from kaonic-hydrogen 1S level shifts (Davies et al., 1979; Izycki et al., 1980; Bird et al., 1983; Iwasaki et al., 1995), as well as by the anomalously large 2P width in kaonic-helium atoms (Wiegand and Pehl, 1971; Baird et al., 1983; Batty et al., 1983). The paper enables the DEAR experiment at DAΦNE to use kaonic-deuterium X-ray measurements to discriminate between competing models of the KN interaction and to test for a possible near-threshold KD quasibound state.
