# Three-point functions from a Schwinger-Keldysh effective action, resummed in derivatives

> The search for the conjectured QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions requires to account for far-from equilibrium effects as well as fluctuations, and in particular non-Gaussian fluctuations, in the modeling of the dynamics of the hot and dense matter created in such collisions. In order to study far-from equilibrium effects as well as fluctuations, in this work we construct a Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory (EFT) for the diffusion of the density to all orders in derivatives. The dissipation in the free part of our EFT follows the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA). The interaction part of the EFT is constructed based on the self-interaction of the density field. We analytically find the quadratic and cubic parts of the KMS-invariant EFT in closed form, resummed in derivatives. We then explicitly compute the symmetrized three-point function at tree level, and investigate its analytical structure in detail. We also analytically calculate the branch-point singularity that appears in the structure of the two-point response function due to loop effects. Our results are important for future studies of the real-time dynamics of the correlation functions and the possible relation to thermalization when the system is far from equilibrium.

## Key claim

The authors construct a Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory for charge diffusion that is resummed to all orders in derivatives, and they compute the symmetrized three-point function at tree level; the analytic structure of this three-point function is entirely determined by the analytic structure of the external legs, provided the coupling is small enough that loop effects from cubic interactions can be neglected.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for massless particles and solve it exactly to obtain a resummed diffusion equation for the charge density. They then construct the quadratic and cubic parts of a KMS-invariant Schwinger-Keldysh effective action in closed form, resummed in derivatives, and compute the symmetrized three-point function at tree level in momentum space. They also calculate the branch-point singularity that appears in the two-point response function due to a one-loop diagram from the cubic interaction.

## Key numbers

* The diffusion constant expansion is \( \mathcal{D}(k^2) = \frac{\tau}{3} \left[1 + \frac{1}{15}(\tau k)^2 + \frac{2}{315}(\tau k)^4 + \frac{1}{1575}(\tau k)^6 + \frac{2}{31185}(\tau k)^8 + \ldots \right] \), where \(\tau\) is the relaxation time and \(k = |\mathbf{k}|\).
* The series for the diffusion mode converges for \(k < k_c\) with \(k_c \approx 3.14/\tau\).
* At leading order in derivatives, the branch-point singularity from loop effects is located at \(\omega_{\text{b.p.}} = -\frac{i}{2} D k^2\), where \(D = \tau/3\) is the leading-order diffusion constant.
* For momenta \(k < k^*\), the two-point function has a simple pole and a branch cut between \(-i/\tau - k < \omega < -i/\tau + k\) in the lower half of the complex-frequency plane; for \(k > k^*\) the pole disappears.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Keldysh EFT approach for diffusive systems, previously applied to simple diffusive systems by Delacretaz (2023) and to holographic systems by Pantelidou (2022), to a kinetic-theory system of massless particles in RTA. It provides a systematic framework for computing non-Gaussian correlation functions beyond the Gaussian (two-point) results known from Romatschke (2015) for the same kinetic system. The results enable future studies of real-time dynamics of correlation functions and thermalization in far-from-equilibrium systems relevant to the search for the conjectured QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions.
