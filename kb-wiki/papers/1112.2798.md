# Mass distributions for induced fission of different Hg isotopes

> With the improved scission-point model the mass distributions are calculated for induced fission of different Hg isotopes with the masses 180-196. The drastic change in the shape of the mass distribution from asymmetric to symmetric is revealed with increasing mass number of the fissioning Hg isotope, and the reactions are proposed to verify this prediction experimentally. The asymmetric mass distribution of fission fragments observed in the recent experiment on the fission of 180Hg is explained. The calculated mass distribution and mean total kinetic energy of fission fragments are in a good agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The improved scission-point model predicts a drastic change in the mass distribution shape from asymmetric to symmetric as the mass number of the fissioning Hg isotope increases from 180 to 196, but this prediction is conditional on the specific excitation energies and reactions proposed; the asymmetric distribution for \(^{180}\)Hg is explained by strong shell effects from the magic neutron number \(N=50\) in the symmetric split \(^{90}\)Zr+\(^{90}\)Zr, which are absent in heavier Hg isotopes.

## Method

The fissioning nucleus at scission is modeled as two nearly touching coaxial spheroids (a dinuclear system) with masses \(A_L\), \(A_H\) and charges \(Z_L\), \(Z_H\). The potential energy includes liquid-drop energies, Strutinsky shell corrections from a two-center shell model, Coulomb and nuclear interaction energies, and rotational energy; thermodynamical equilibrium is assumed at scission, and the yield of each scission configuration is proportional to a Boltzmann factor \(\exp(-U/T)\) with temperature \(T=\sqrt{E^*/a}\). Mass distributions are obtained by integrating over fragment charge numbers and deformation parameters \(\beta_L,\beta_H\) for each mass split, restricting calculations to even-even fragments.

## Key numbers

- For \(\beta\)-delayed fission of \(^{180}\)Tl (fissioning nucleus \(^{180}\)Hg), the excitation energy \(E^*_{CN}\) does not exceed 10.44 MeV; the calculated mass distribution is sharply asymmetric with maximum yield for \(^{76}\)Se+\(^{104}\)Pd, while the experiment gives maximum for \(^{80}\)Kr+\(^{100}\)Ru.
- For induced fission of \(^{180}\)Hg at impact energy 10 MeV above the Coulomb barrier (\(V_b=126.2\) MeV), the compound nucleus excitation energy \(E^*_{CN}=44.2\) MeV; the mass distribution remains asymmetric even at \(E^*_{CN}=64.2\) MeV.
- For \(^{184}\)Hg (reaction \(^{40}\)Ar+\(^{144}\)Sm, \(V_b=124.55\) MeV), \(E^*_{CN}=43.9\) MeV at 10 MeV above barrier; the mass distribution is flat.
- For \(^{188}\)Hg (reaction \(^{40}\)Ar+\(^{148}\)Sm, \(V_b=123.9\) MeV), \(E^*_{CN}=49.7\) MeV at 10 MeV above barrier; the mass distribution is symmetric but very wide.
- For \(^{192}\)Hg (reaction \(^{32}\)S+\(^{160}\)Gd, \(V_b=114.4\) MeV), \(E^*_{CN}=62.4\) MeV at 10 MeV above barrier; the mass distribution is sharply symmetric.
- For \(^{196}\)Hg (reaction \(^{36}\)S+\(^{160}\)Gd, \(V_b=112.8\) MeV), \(E^*_{CN}=56.0\) MeV at 10 MeV above barrier; the mass distribution is sharply symmetric.
- For \(\beta\)-delayed fission of \(^{180}\)Tl, the calculated average total kinetic energy (TKE) is 136 MeV, in good agreement with experiment.

## Context

This work extends the improved scission-point model, previously applied to actinide fission (Andreev et al., earlier work), to the lighter Hg isotopes, explaining the unexpected asymmetric mass distribution observed in \(\beta\)-delayed fission of \(^{180}\)Tl by Andreyev et al. (2010). The model contradicts the earlier expectation from studies of Coulomb-excited fission of radioactive beams (Schmidt et al.) and proton/\(^{3,4}\)He-induced fission of stable targets (Itkis et al.) that asymmetric fission gives way to symmetric fission with decreasing mass number. The predictions for induced fission of \(^{180-196}\)Hg enable experimental verification using the proposed reactions (e.g., \(^{36}\)Ar+\(^{144}\)Sm, \(^{40}\)Ar+\(^{144}\)Sm, \(^{40}\)Ar+\(^{148}\)Sm, \(^{32}\)S+\(^{160}\)Gd, \(^{36}\)S+\(^{160}\)Gd) and provide a test of shell effects at scission in the neutron-deficient region.
