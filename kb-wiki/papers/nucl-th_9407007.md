# Relativistic Deuteron Structure Function

> We calculate the unpolarised deep inelastic structure function of a relativistic deuteron within a covariant framework. An exact treatment of nucleon off-shell effects is shown to give corrections to the widely-used convolution model, even in impulse approximation. Neglecting off-shell effects in the extraction of the neutron structure function from deuterium data introduces errors of order $1-2\%$.

## Key claim

Neglecting nucleon off-shell effects in the extraction of the neutron structure function from deuterium data introduces errors of order 1–2% for Bjorken x_N < 0.9, but the off-shell corrections to the convolution formula are numerically small and partially cancel between the vertex and nucleon-structure contributions.

## Method

The authors calculate the unpolarised deep inelastic structure function of a relativistic deuteron within a covariant framework, starting from the most general Dirac structure of the off-shell nucleon tensor and a covariant DNN vertex function. They separate the full impulse-approximation result into a convolution term (involving on-shell nucleon structure functions and a relativistic momentum distribution) plus two correction terms: one from the off-shell and P-state components of the relativistic DNN vertex, and one from the off-shell part of the truncated nucleon structure function. The nucleon structure function is modelled using diquark spectator vertex functions with parameters fitted to on-shell proton data at a low scale Q0 ≈ 400 MeV, then evolved to Q² = 5 GeV².

## Key numbers

- Neglecting off-shell effects in extracting the neutron structure function from deuterium data introduces errors of order 1–2% for x_N < 0.9.
- The combined nuclear effect in deuterium (binding, Fermi motion, and nucleon off-shellness) is predicted to be about 5% at x_N ≈ 0.6–0.7.
- The diquark spectator model uses cut-off parameters Λ₀ = 750 MeV and Λ₁ = 450 MeV for scalar and vector diquarks, with exponents n₀ = 1.3 and n₁ = 2.4.
- The input gluon distribution is parameterised as x g(x) ~ x² (1–x)⁴ at Q₀² = (0.39 GeV)².
- The second moments of (u+d)_val and q_sea are approximately 83% and 3%, respectively, at the input scale.

## Context

This work extends the covariant formalism for off-shell nucleon structure developed by Melnitchouk et al. (1994) and applies it to the deuteron, correcting the widely-used convolution model of West (1970), Bodek (1979), and others. The results enable more precise extraction of the neutron structure function from deuterium data, which is essential for testing parton model sum rules such as the Gottfried sum rule and the Bjorken sum rule. The authors note that reliable predictions below x_N ~ 0.2 would require inclusion of additional mechanisms beyond impulse approximation, such as nuclear shadowing and meson-exchange effects.
