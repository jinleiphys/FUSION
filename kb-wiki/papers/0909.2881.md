# The Missing Three-Nucleon Forces: Where Are They?

> In recent years, there has been substantial progress in the derivation of nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory. Accurate two-nucleon forces (2NF) have been constructed up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) of chiral perturbation theory and applied in microscopic nuclear structure calculations with a good degree of success. However, chiral three-nucleon forces (3NF) have been used only at N2LO, improving some miscroscopic predictions, but leaving also several issues, like the "Ay puzzle'" of nucleon-deuteron scattering, unresolved. Thus, the 3NF at N3LO is needed for essentially two reasons: For consistency with the 2NF, and to (hopefully) improve some critical predictions of nuclear structure and reactions. However, there are indications that the 3NF at N3LO (in the so-called Delta-less version of the theory) is rather weak and may not solve any of the outstanding problems. If this suspicion is confirmed, we have to go beyond, which may be similar to opening Pandora's Box. In this talk, I will discuss the various possible scenarios and how to deal with them.

# The Missing Three-Nucleon Forces: Where Are They?

## Key claim

The chiral three-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO) in the Delta-less version of chiral effective field theory is anticipated to be weak and unlikely to resolve outstanding problems such as the Ay puzzle of nucleon-deuteron scattering; sizable contributions that may constitute the missing three-nucleon force are instead expected from one-loop diagrams at N⁴LO of the Delta-less theory (or equivalently N³LO of the Delta-full theory).

## Method

The author applies chiral perturbation theory power counting to classify three-nucleon force (3NF) contributions order by order, using the formula ν = -2 + 2A - 2C + 2L + ΣΔi to determine the chiral order of each diagram. The analysis examines 3NF topologies at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), N³LO, and N⁴LO in the Delta-less theory, and compares them with corresponding diagrams in the Delta-full theory that explicitly includes the Δ(1232) isobar. The discussion draws on prior calculations of specific diagram classes by Ishikawa and Robilotta (2007) and the Bonn-Jülich group (2008), as well as Kaiser's evaluations of three-pion exchange contributions to the two-nucleon force.

## Key numbers

- The leading four-nucleon force at N³LO contributes approximately 0.1 MeV to the ⁴He binding energy, compared to the total ⁴He binding energy of 28.3 MeV.
- The chiral expansion parameter is (Q/Λχ)ν+1, where Λχ ≈ 1 GeV is the chiral symmetry breaking scale.
- The two-nucleon force at N³LO provides a quantitative description of nucleon-nucleon interaction up to about 300 MeV laboratory energy.
- Three-nucleon forces at NNLO appear at order (Q/Λχ)³, while two-nucleon forces start at order (Q/Λχ)⁰, explaining the empirical hierarchy 2NF ≫ 3NF ≫ 4NF.

## Context

This talk extends the chiral effective field theory program for nuclear forces, following the construction of accurate two-nucleon potentials at N³LO by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and their successful application in nuclear structure calculations. The work addresses the unresolved Ay puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering and similar analyzing-power problems in p-³He scattering reported by Fisher et al. (2006) and Deltuva and Fonseca (2007), which the NNLO three-nucleon force of van Kolck (1994) and Epelbaum et al. (2002) fails to resolve. The analysis points toward the need for explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) isobar, as in the Delta-full chiral EFT, to efficiently generate sizable higher-order three-nucleon force contributions, and notes that the Illinois three-nucleon force of Pieper et al. (2001) already includes one-loop diagrams with one and two Δ-isobars for similar reasons.
