# Quark-gluon vertex model and lattice-QCD data

> A model for the dressed quark-gluon vertex, at zero gluon momentum, is formed from a nonperturbative extension of the two Feynman diagrams that contribute at 1-loop in perturbation theory. The required input is an existing ladder-rainbow model Bethe-Salpeter kernel from an approach based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations; no new parameters are introduced. The model includes an Ansatz for the triple-gluon vertex. Two of the three vertex amplitudes from the model provide a point-wise description of the recent quenched lattice-QCD data. An estimate of the effects of quenching is made.

## Key claim

A model for the dressed quark-gluon vertex at zero gluon momentum, built from nonperturbative extensions of the two 1-loop Feynman diagrams and using an existing ladder-rainbow kernel without new parameters, reproduces two of the three vertex amplitudes from quenched lattice-QCD data; the third amplitude (lambda_2) is seriously underestimated by the model.

## Method

The authors construct the dressed quark-gluon vertex at zero gluon momentum by taking the two diagrams that contribute at 1-loop in perturbation theory (an Abelian-like term and a non-Abelian term involving the triple-gluon vertex) and replacing the bare propagators and couplings with dressed quantities from an existing ladder-rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) kernel. Two DSE models are used: DSE-Lat, which maps quenched lattice gluon propagator data into a continuum kernel, and DSE-MT, which fits light quark meson physics and represents unquenched dynamics. The nonperturbative extension of the non-Abelian diagram at zero gluon momentum uses an Ansatz for the triple-gluon vertex that follows from the Slavnov-Taylor identity.

## Key numbers

- At p=0, the ratio of non-Abelian to Abelian contributions to the vertex amplitudes is: lambda_1^NA/lambda_1^A = -60, lambda_2^NA/lambda_2^A = -14, lambda_3^NA/lambda_3^A = -12.
- The lattice data and model calculations are performed at renormalization scale mu = 2 GeV with quark current mass m(mu) = 60 MeV.
- The non-Abelian term dominates to a greater extent than the ratio of color factors (-9) would suggest.

## Context

This work extends prior DSE-based modeling of hadron physics using the ladder-rainbow truncation (Maris 2003, Alkofer 2000) by moving beyond the bare quark-gluon vertex. It provides a direct comparison to the first lattice-QCD data on the dressed quark-gluon vertex (Skullerud 2003), which was previously unavailable. The model enables future studies of the vertex's role in hadron observables and provides an estimate of quenching effects by comparing the DSE-Lat (quenched) and DSE-MT (unquenched) models, while also indicating that contributions from the four-gluon vertex and further iterations may be needed to describe the lambda_2 amplitude.
