# Nonequilibrium thermodynamics of accreted neutron-star crust

> We show that, in order to determine the equation of state of the inner crust of an accreting neutron star, one should minimize not the Gibbs free energy, as it is generally assumed in the literature, but a different thermodynamic potential $Ψ$, which tends to the minimum at fixed pressure and neutron chemical potential. Once this potential is specified, one can calculate the heat-release distribution in the stellar crust due to nonequilibrium nuclear reactions induced by accretion of matter onto the neutron-star surface. The results are important for adequate modeling of the accreted crust and interpretation of the observations of accreting neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the inner crust of an accreting neutron star, the correct thermodynamic potential to minimize is not the Gibbs free energy (as assumed in prior literature) but a different potential Psi, which tends to a minimum at fixed pressure and neutron chemical potential; this correction is necessary to respect the neutron hydrostatic/diffusion equilibrium condition that unbound neutrons can move independently of nuclei.

## Method

The authors perform a thought experiment with a cylindrical vessel containing crustal matter, first with walls impenetrable to particles (reproducing the traditional Gibbs minimization) and then with walls permeable to unbound neutrons, allowing neutron exchange with the outside medium. They derive the thermodynamic potential Psi = E + PV - mu_n N_b - TS from the first and second laws of thermodynamics for the permeable-wall case, and show that irreversible nuclear reactions at fixed pressure, temperature, and neutron chemical potential lead to minimization of Psi. They then apply this potential to calculate heat release distributions in the fully accreted crust using the condition that the redshifted potential Psi^infinity is constant in regions without nuclear reactions.

## Key numbers

- The neutron hydrostatic/diffusion equilibrium condition requires mu_n^infinity = constant throughout the inner crust, where the redshift factor is e^{nu(r)/2}.
- In the fully accreted crust, the number of atomic nuclei N_N remains constant as a volume element sinks toward the crust-core boundary.
- The heat release per accreted baryon in the fully accreted crust is given by q^infinity = mbar_{b,ash} e^{nu_s/2} - mu_{b,core}^infinity, where mbar_{b,ash} is the average mass of ashes per baryon and e^{nu_s/2} is the redshift factor at the stellar surface.
- The pressure at the outer-inner crust interface, P_oi, is not necessarily equal to the neutron drip pressure P_nd; in particular, P_oi can be less than P_nd due to neutron redistribution.
- The narrow layer near the outer-inner crust interface where neutrons can be nonsuperfluid is less than or approximately 5 meters thick.

## Context

This work extends the neutron hydrostatic/diffusion equilibrium approach introduced by Gusakov and Chugunov (2019) and corrects the traditional approach to accreted crust modeling used by Haensel and Zdunik (1990, 1990b, 2003, 2008), Steiner (2012), Chamel et al. (2015), Fantina et al. (2018), and others, which minimized the Gibbs free energy and ignored the independent motion of unbound neutrons. The results provide a theoretical basis for calculating heat release distributions in both fully and partially accreted crusts, enabling more adequate modeling of accreting neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries and interpretation of observational data.
