# Covariant four-dimensional scattering equations for the $NN-πNN$ system

> We derive a set of coupled four-dimensional integral equations for the $NN-πNN$ system using our modified version of the Taylor method of classification-of-diagrams. These equations are covariant, obey two and three-body unitarity and contain subtraction terms which eliminate the double-counting present in some previous four-dimensional $NN-πNN$ equations. The equations are then recast into a from convenient for computation by grouping the subtraction terms together and obtaining a set of two-fragment scattering equations for the amplitudes of interest.

## Key claim

The authors derive a set of coupled four-dimensional integral equations for the $NN-\pi NN$ system that are covariant, obey two- and three-body unitarity, and contain subtraction terms to eliminate double-counting present in previous four-dimensional $NN-\pi NN$ equations, but the derivation assumes fully dressed propagators and does not specify the precise form of the interaction Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors apply a modified version of Taylor's classification-of-diagrams method to the covariant Feynman diagrammatic expansion of the field theory of nucleons and pions. They first define Green's functions and amplitudes via LSZ reduction, then classify diagrams by their $s$-channel cut structure into five classes, and use the last internal cut lemma to sum each class. The double-counting problem that arises when applying Taylor's method directly is corrected by identifying overlapping sub-classes and subtracting their intersections using a set-theoretic inclusion-exclusion formula.

## Key numbers

- The free single-nucleon propagator is given by $d_N(p) = i/(\not{p} - m_N)$ and the free single-pion propagator by $d_\pi(k) = i/(k^2 - m_\pi^2)$, where $m_N$ and $m_\pi$ are the dressed nucleon and pion masses.
- The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $NN$ amplitude is $T_{NN}^{(1)} = T_{NN}^{(2)} + T_{NN}^{(2)} d_1 d_2 T_{NN}^{(1)}$, where $d_1$ and $d_2$ are fully-dressed free propagators for nucleons 1 and 2.
- The equation for the two-particle irreducible $NN$ amplitude $T_{NN}^{(2)}$ involves subtraction terms $D_3$, $D_4$, and $D_5$ to remove double-counting from the sums of classes $C_3$, $C_4$, and $C_5$ respectively.

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivations of $NN-\pi NN$ equations by Avishai and Mizutani (1983) and Afnan and Blankleider (1985), which contained double-counting when viewed in a four-dimensional framework, as recognized by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1994). The authors' modified Taylor method provides a rigorous, Lagrangian-independent derivation that eliminates this double-counting, enabling future numerical solution of the four-dimensional equations to test whether the standard three-dimensional approximations are adequate before invoking mechanisms beyond the one-pion sector.
