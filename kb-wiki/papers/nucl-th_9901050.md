# Generalized routhian calculations within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approximation

> We consider here variational solutions in the Hartree-Fock approximation upon breaking time reversal and axial symmetries. When decomposed on axial harmonic oscillator functions, the corresponding single particle triaxial eigenstates as functions of the usual cylindrical coordinates (r, $θ$, z) are evaluated on a mesh in r and z to be integrated within Gauss-Hermite and Gauss-Laguerre approaches and as Fourier decompositions in the angular variable $θ$. Using an effective interaction of the Skyrme type, the Hartree-Fock hamiltonian is also obtained as a Fourier series allowing a two dimensional calculation of its matrix elements. This particular choice is shown to lead in most cases to shorter computation times compared to the usual decomposition on triaxial harmonic oscillator states. We apply this method to the case of the semi-quantal approach of large amplitude collective motion corresponding to a generalized routhian formalism and present results in the A=150 superdeformed region for the coupling of global rotation and intrinsic vortical modes in what is known after Chandrasekhar as the S-ellipsoid coupling case.

# Generalized routhian calculations within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approximation

## Key claim

The authors present an alternative method for describing triaxial nuclear shapes using an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis, where triaxial wave functions are obtained by mixing states with different eigenvalues of the angular momentum projection, and claim this approach leads to shorter computation times compared to the usual decomposition on triaxial harmonic oscillator states *provided that the number of axially symmetric basis wavefunctions necessary for an accurate description of the triaxial solution is not too large*.

## Method

Single-particle triaxial eigenstates are decomposed on axial harmonic oscillator functions in cylindrical coordinates, evaluated on a mesh in r and z using Gauss-Hermite and Gauss-Laguerre quadrature, with Fourier decomposition in the angular variable theta. Using the Skyrme effective interaction (SkM* parametrisation), the Hartree-Fock hamiltonian is obtained as a Fourier series, allowing calculation of its matrix elements through two-dimensional integrals rather than three-dimensional ones. The method is applied to the generalized routhian formalism for large amplitude collective motion, specifically the S-ellipsoid coupling case of Chandrasekhar, combining global rotation and intrinsic vortical modes.

## Key numbers

- For the I=20 hbar solution of Sr-80 with N0=14 major shells: routhian expectation value = -690.61 MeV, quadrupole moment Q0 = 6.10 b, non-axial quadrupole moment Q22 = 0.43 b, angular velocity Omega = 0.832 MeV, dynamical moment of inertia Im(2) = 23.91 hbar^2.MeV^{-1}
- Computation time for one Hartree-Fock iteration in the triaxial case (after first iteration) on a HP9000/780 workstation: 1.49 s for N0=6, 4.69 s for N0=8, 15.2 s for N0=10, 52.1 s for N0=12, 150 s for N0=14
- For N0=14, the formalism is expected to be 4 times faster than a fully triaxial formalism; for N0=6, the expected speedup factor is 9
- For Gd-150 with N0=10 basis size, computation time after first iteration is less than 20 seconds, compared to 9 seconds on a CRAY C90 (vectorised) for the triaxial harmonic oscillator basis code of Dobaczewski and Dudek
- The routhian matrix element calculation involves 10-point Gauss-Hermite and Gauss-Laguerre integrations on a 10 by 10 grid
- Coulomb potential calculation uses a 48-point Gauss-Legendre quadrature formula for the angular variable

## Context

This work extends the axially symmetric Skyrme-Hartree-Fock formalism of Vautherin (1973) to triaxial shapes with time-reversal symmetry breaking, and generalizes the cranking (routhian) approximation to include intrinsic vortical modes beyond global rotation. The method enables description of the S-ellipsoid coupling case from Chandrasekhar's fluid dynamics work, applied to the A=150 superdeformed region. The approach is compared with triaxial harmonic oscillator basis calculations by Egido, Girod, Dudek, and the code of Dobaczewski and Dudek, and is expected to enable faster computation of triaxial nuclear states within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework.
