# First 4D lattice calculation of transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ for pure gluon plasma

> The transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ plays a pivotal role in describing the phenomenon of jet quenching in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. It is challenging to compute this coefficient from first principles due to its non-perturbative nature. In this article, we present an $ab$-$initio$ formulation of $\hat{q}$ based on the standard techniques of perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) and lattice gauge theory. We construct $\hat{q}$ by considering a leading order (LO) process where a hard parton produced from the hard scattering undergoes transverse broadening due to scatterings with the thermal medium. We do an analytic continuation to the Euclidean region and use the dispersion relation to express $\hat{q}$ in terms of series of local Field-Strength-Field-Strength (FF) operators. Each term in the series is suppressed by the hard scale $q^{-}$. Finally, we compute the local operators on the quenched SU(3) lattice and present our estimates for $\hat{q}$.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first 4D lattice calculation of the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) for a pure gluon plasma, but the result is limited to a leading-order process with only the first two terms in an operator product expansion, and the calculation is performed on a quenched (not full QCD) lattice.

## Method
The authors construct \(\hat{q}\) from a leading-order process where a hard quark undergoes transverse broadening via gluon exchange with the thermal medium. They analytically continue to Euclidean space and use a dispersion relation to express \(\hat{q}\) as a series of local Field-Strength-Field-Strength (FF) operators, each suppressed by the hard scale \(q^{-}\). They compute the first two terms of this series on a quenched SU(3) lattice using the Wilson gauge action, with statistical averages over 5000 gauge configurations generated via the heat-bath algorithm, for lattice sizes \(n_{\tau}=4,6,8\) with \(n_s=4n_{\tau}\).

## Key numbers
- \(\hat{q}/T^3 \sim 0.5-1\) at high temperature for a pure gluon plasma, extracted from the quenched SU(3) lattice calculation.
- The hard quark energy used is \(q^{-}=20\) GeV, and the QCD coupling constant is \(\alpha_s=0.2\) at \(\mu^2 \simeq 8\) GeV\(^2\).
- The critical temperature for pure SU(3) gauge theory is taken as \(T_c \sim 265\) MeV.
- The transition region for the FF correlators and \(\hat{q}/T^3\) is observed around \(T \in (250,350)\) MeV.
- Lattice sizes used: \(n_{\tau}=4\) with \(n_s=16\), \(n_{\tau}=6\) with \(n_s=24\), and \(n_{\tau}=8\) with \(n_s=32\).

## Context
This work extends a previous lattice gauge theory approach developed by the authors for a quenched SU(2) plasma (Kumar, Majumder, Nonaka, 2018) to a quenched SU(3) plasma. It provides a first-principles non-perturbative calculation of \(\hat{q}\) that complements hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory predictions (which scale as \(T^3 \log(E/T)\)) and phenomenological extractions by the JET collaboration (Burke et al., 2014). The results enable future unquenched calculations including dynamical quarks and corrections from medium-induced radiative splitting, as well as extensions to improved lattice actions.
