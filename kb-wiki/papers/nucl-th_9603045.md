# Realistic effective interactions for halo nuclei

> We study halo nuclei using a two-frequency shell-model approach employing wave functions of two different oscillator constants $\hbarω_{in}$ and $\hbarω_{out}$, the former for the inner orbits and the latter for the halo (outer) orbits. An initial application has been made for the halo nuclei $^6$He and $^6$Li, with $0s_{1/2}$ taken as the inner and ( $0p_{3/2}$, $0p_{1/2}$) as the halo orbits. Starting from the Paris NN interaction, we have derived a G-matrix folded-diagram effective interaction for this two-frequency model space, using an essentially exact treatment for the Pauli exclusion operator for the G-matrix. While keeping $\hbarω_{in}$ fixed, we have performed calculations with different choices for $\hbarω_{out}$, treating it as a variation parameter. For $\hbarω_{in}=19.7 MeV$ and $\hbarω_{out}=8.2 MeV$, our calculated valence energies for $^6$He and $^6$Li are -2.77 and -3.55 Mev, respectively, both in good agreement with experiments. The importance of certain three-body-force diagrams is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a two-frequency shell-model approach, using different oscillator constants for core and halo orbits, can reproduce the valence energies of the halo nuclei $^6$He and $^6$Li when starting from the realistic Paris NN interaction, but only for a specific choice of the outer oscillator parameter ($\hbar\omega_{out}=8.2$ MeV, corresponding to $b_{out}=2.25$ fm) while keeping the inner parameter fixed at $\hbar\omega_{in}=19.7$ MeV ($b_{in}=1.45$ fm).

## Method

The authors derived a G-matrix folded-diagram effective interaction from the Paris NN potential for a two-frequency model space, using an exact treatment of the Pauli exclusion operator with a large cutoff ($n_3=21$) to incorporate three-body-force effects into the G-matrix. They applied this to $^6$He and $^6$Li with the $0s_{1/2}$ orbit as the inner (core) space using $\hbar\omega_{in}=19.7$ MeV and the $0p_{3/2}$ and $0p_{1/2}$ orbits as the halo space, treating $\hbar\omega_{out}$ as a variation parameter. The effective interaction matrix elements were calculated via the $\hat{Q}$-box folded-diagram method including first- and second-order diagrams in G, and valence energies were obtained by diagonalizing the two-particle matrix within the p-shell.

## Key numbers

- For $\hbar\omega_{in}=19.7$ MeV and $\hbar\omega_{out}=8.2$ MeV (corresponding to $b_{in}=1.45$ fm and $b_{out}=2.25$ fm), the calculated valence energy for $^6$He is -2.77 MeV, in good agreement with the empirical value of -2.76 MeV.
- For the same parameters, the calculated valence energy for $^6$Li is -3.55 MeV, compared to the empirical value of -3.83 MeV.
- The empirical rms radius for $^6$He is about 2.5 fm; using the two-frequency wave function with $b_{in}=1.45$ fm and $b_{out}=2.25$ fm yields a calculated rms radius of 2.37 fm.
- The empirical $\hbar\omega$ for $^6$He from the formula $\hbar\omega=45A^{-1/3}-25A^{-2/3}$ is approximately 17.2 MeV, corresponding to $b=1.55$ fm.
- The two-neutron separation energy for $^6$He is only about 1 MeV, and the single-nucleon separation energies for $^5$He and $^5$Li are both negative (unbound).

## Context

This work extends the G-matrix folded-diagram approach, pioneered by Brown and Kuo (1967) and Kuo and Brown (1966), to halo nuclei by introducing a two-frequency shell-model space, addressing a limitation of the conventional shell model where all wave functions share a common $\hbar\omega$ value. It contrasts with the hyperspherical harmonics method used by Zhukov, Danilin, Fedorov, Bang, Thompson, and Vaagan (1993), which cannot easily incorporate realistic NN interactions, and with the variational shell-model approach of Otsuka and Fukunishi (1996) using Skyrme interactions. The results enable future microscopic calculations of total binding energies for halo nuclei using realistic NN potentials, though the authors note that their preliminary total binding energies for $^4$He and $^6$He are too small compared to experiment.
