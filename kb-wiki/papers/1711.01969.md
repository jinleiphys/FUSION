# Spin Dependence of Small-Angle Proton-Nucleus Scattering

> We study the single-spin asymmetry, $A_N(t)$, arising from Coulomb-nuclear interference (CNI) at small 4-momentum transfer squared, $-t=q^2$, aiming at explanation of the recent data from the PHENIX experiment at RHIC on polarized proton-nucleus scattering, exposing a nontrivial $t$-dependence of $A_N$. We found that the failure of previous theoretical attempts to explain these data, was due to lack of absorptive corrections in the Coulomb amplitude of $pA$ elastic scattering. Our prominent observation is that the main contribution to $A_N(t)$ comes from interference of the amplitudes of ultra-peripheral and central collisions.

# Spin Dependence of Small-Angle Proton-Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim
The failure of previous theoretical attempts to explain PHENIX data on single-spin asymmetry in polarized proton-nucleus scattering was due to the use of an incorrect electromagnetic form factor that lacked absorptive corrections. The main contribution to the asymmetry comes from interference between amplitudes of ultra-peripheral and central collisions, not from the spin-flip ratio of hadronic amplitudes alone.

## Method
The authors calculated the single-spin asymmetry $A_N(t)$ for proton-nucleus elastic scattering using the Glauber eikonal model, introducing a new electromagnetic form factor that includes absorptive corrections via an exponential factor $e^{-\frac{1}{2}\sigma_{tot}^{pp}T_A(b)}$ in the impact parameter integration. They compared their results with PHENIX data for proton-gold, proton-carbon, and proton-aluminum scattering, fitting the ratio of spin-flip to non-flip hadronic amplitude, $r_5$, for each nuclear target.

## Key numbers
- For proton-gold scattering at RHIC energies, the new electromagnetic form factor (red solid line, zero $r_5$) reproduces the shape of the $t$-dependence of $A_N(t)$ observed in PHENIX data, in contrast to the previous form factor (black dashed curve, zero $r_5$) which showed a nearly inverse trend.
- The electromagnetic form factor normalization $N^{em}$ keeps $F_A^{em}(0)=1$, with the absorptive correction factor $e^{-\frac{1}{2}\sigma_{tot}^{pp}T_A(b)}$ where $\sigma_{tot}^{pp}$ is the total nucleon-nucleon cross section.
- The hadronic amplitude $F_A^{h}(t)$ is normalized by the total $p+A$ cross section $\sigma^{pA}_{tot}$ and is non-zero only at small impact parameters $b<R_A$.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Kopeliovich (Kopeliovich-b, Kopeliovich-a) that successfully described $A_N$ in proton-carbon scattering but failed for heavier targets like gold measured by the PHENIX experiment (Poblaguev). The corrected electromagnetic form factor resolves the discrepancy for gold, but the best-fit values of $r_5$ differ significantly among carbon, aluminum, and gold targets, contradicting the assumption of a universal $r_5$. The authors note that their reliance on the Glauber eikonal model is subject to Gribov corrections (Gribov, kopeliovich-gribov), which may make nuclei more transparent and affect results; further work incorporating these corrections is ongoing.
