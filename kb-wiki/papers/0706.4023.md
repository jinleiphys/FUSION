# Role of the Delta(1232) in pion-deuteron scattering at threshold within chiral effective field theory

> We investigate the role of the delta isobar in the reaction $πd\to πd$ at threshold in chiral effective field theory. We discuss the corresponding power counting and argue that this calculation completes the evaluation of diagrams up to the order (m_pi/M_N)^(3/2), with m_pi (M_N) for the pion (nucleon) mass. The net effect of all delta contributions at this order to the pion-deuteron scattering length is (2.4 +/- 0.4)x 10^(-3) m_pi^{-1}.

## Key claim

The delta isobar contributes to the pion-deuteron scattering length at order χ^(3/2) (where χ = m_π/M_N), and the net effect of all delta contributions at this order is (2.4 ± 0.4) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), but this result depends on using a dynamical (rather than static) delta propagator to avoid large model dependence from the deuteron wave function.

## Method

The authors calculated the leading delta-isobar contributions to πd → πd at threshold within chiral effective field theory, using time-ordered perturbation theory with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom. They evaluated one-body and two-body diagrams involving the πNΔ vertex (with coupling h_A = 2.77) and the nucleon-delta propagator, keeping the kinetic energy of the delta in the propagator. The calculations were performed for seven different NN potentials: four phenomenological models (Paris, AV18, CD-Bonn, CCF) and three variants of N^2LO chiral NN wave functions (EGM1, EGM2, EGM3), with all integrals evaluated up to 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Net delta contribution to the πd scattering length: δa^Δ_πd = (2.38 ± 0.40) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), averaged over seven NN potentials with h_A = 2.77
- With the lower coupling h_A = 2.1, the result rescales to δa^Δ_πd = (1.4 ± 0.2) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
- Theoretical uncertainty estimate from omitted χ^2 terms: Δa^(theor) = 0.6 × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
- Total contribution at order χ^(3/2) combining delta and dispersive corrections: (-0.6 ± 1.5) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
- Residual boost correction with c_2 = -0.25 GeV^(-1): -(5.7 to 6.6) × 10^(-4) m_π^(-1)
- Dispersive corrections evaluated with four phenomenological NN potentials: δa^(disp)_πd = (-2.9 ± 1.4) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)

## Context

This work completes the evaluation of diagrams at order χ^(3/2) for πd scattering, alongside the dispersive corrections calculated by the same group in a prior study (Baru et al., 2006). The explicit inclusion of the dynamical delta resolves a strong model dependence seen in earlier phenomenological treatments: previous works (Boudard and Koltun, 1980; Döring et al., 2004) using static delta propagators found results varying by factors of 1.6 to over 4 across different NN potentials, whereas the present dynamical treatment yields nearly model-independent results. The calculation enables a more precise extraction of the isoscalar πN scattering length a^+ from πd scattering, though isospin-violating corrections (including virtual photon effects) remain to be calculated at next-to-leading order to match the accuracy achieved here for the strong, isospin-symmetric contributions.
