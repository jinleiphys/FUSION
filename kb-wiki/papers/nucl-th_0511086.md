# Effects of Time-Odd Components in Mean Field on Large Amplitude Collective Dynamics

> We apply the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method to the multi-O(4) model and study collective mass (inertia function) of the many-body tunneling motion. Comparing results with those of the exact diagonalization, we show that the ASCC method succeeds in describing gradual change of excitation spectra from an anharmonic vibration about the spherical shape to a doublet pattern associated with a deformed double-well potential possessing the oblate-prolate symmetry. The collective mass is significantly increased by the quadrupole-pairing contribution to time-odd components of the moving mean field. In contrast, the cranking (Inglis-Belyaev) mass based on the constrained mean field, which ignores the time-odd components, is smaller than the ASCC mass and fails to reproduce the exact spectra.

## Key claim

The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, when applied to the multi-O(4) model extended to include quadrupole-type pairing, succeeds in describing the gradual change of excitation spectra from an anharmonic vibration about the spherical shape to a doublet pattern associated with a deformed double-well potential, but only if the time-odd components of the moving mean field, specifically the quadrupole-pairing contribution, are included; without these, the cranking (Inglis-Belyaev) mass is too small and fails to reproduce the exact spectra.

## Method

The authors applied the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method to a multi-O(4) model Hamiltonian that includes monopole-pairing, quadrupole-type pairing, and quadrupole particle-hole interactions. They solved the moving-frame Hartree-Bogoliubov equation and the local harmonic equations (moving-frame quasiparticle RPA) iteratively to determine the collective path, the collective potential V(q), and the collective mass B(q). The collective mass was compared with the Inglis-Belyaev cranking mass derived from constrained mean-field calculations that neglect time-odd components, and both were benchmarked against exact diagonalization of the model.

## Key numbers

* The collective mass (inertia function) from the ASCC method is significantly increased by the quadrupole-pairing contribution to time-odd components of the moving mean field, in contrast to the cranking (Inglis-Belyaev) mass which is smaller and fails to reproduce the exact spectra.
* The quadrupole-pairing gap Δ₂ and monopole-pairing gap Δ₀ are defined as Δ₀(q) = G₀ Σᵢ uᵢ(q)vᵢ(q) and Δ₂(q) = G₂ Σᵢ dᵢσᵢ uᵢ(q)vᵢ(q), with the strength G₂ varied within a range consistent with experimental data for the ratio Δ₂/Δ₀.
* The collective frequency ω(q) is determined by the condition det S(ω²(q)) = 0, and the smallest ω²(q) solution (including negative values) is chosen as the collective mode; with the choice B(q)=1, ω²(q) equals the curvature ∂²V(q)/∂q².

## Context

This work extends the multi-O(4) model studies of Kobayasi et al. (2003) and earlier applications of the SCC method to include quadrupole-type pairing interactions, which are known to be important for low-frequency collective excitations (see review by Shimizu et al., 2001). The results demonstrate that the time-odd components of the moving mean field, neglected in the widely used Inglis-Belyaev cranking mass (Rowe, 1980), are essential for reproducing exact spectra in large-amplitude collective dynamics, particularly for tunneling motion in deformed double-well potentials. This enables future microscopic calculations of collective inertia for phenomena such as nuclear fission and shape coexistence in realistic nuclei, building on the work of Kobayasi et al. (2004) on oblate-prolate shape coexistence in ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr.
