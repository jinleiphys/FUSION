# Even Parity $Θ$-Pentaquark and Stable Strange Nuclear Matter

> The newly discovered exotic $Θ$ baryon of mass 1540 MeV (and very small width) truly has a very low mass, if it is a pentaquark system of even parity. A schematic model in which the coherent interaction of $u\bar s$ and $d\bar s$ pairs leads to a very large residual (non-confining) attractive interaction is introduced. This collective vibrational model accounts for the mass and small decay width to the $KN$ channel, but yields a significant coupling to the virtual $K^*N$ channel. The schematic model predicts an attractive $Θ$-nucleon interaction strong enough to bind a $Θ$ particle to a nucleus in a state that is stable against decay via strong interactions. The discovery of $Θ$-nuclei could be a definitive proof that the $Θ$ parity is even.

# Even Parity Θ-Pentaquark and Stable Strange Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The paper argues that if the Θ⁺ pentaquark (mass 1540 MeV) has even parity, its low mass can be explained by a collective vibrational model in which coherent interactions of u\bar s and d\bar s pairs produce a very large residual attraction; this model then predicts that the Θ-nucleon interaction is strong enough to bind Θ to nuclei with binding energy considerably greater than 100 MeV, producing states stable against strong decay. The hedge is that this entire prediction depends on the Θ parity being even, if the parity is odd, the argument for strong binding collapses.

## Method

The author constructs a schematic quark-model Hamiltonian in which color-singlet pseudoscalar u\bar s and d\bar s excitations move in a p-wave relative to a nucleon core, with a residual attractive interaction λ between these excitations and the nucleon. The Schrödinger equation is solved for the Θ wave function as a coherent superposition of 18 or more such states, using techniques analogous to the nuclear giant dipole resonance model (Brown and Bolsterli, 1959). The resulting Θ-nucleon potential is then derived from the same interaction and convolved with nuclear density to estimate binding in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Θ mass: 1540 MeV (experimental value, cited as the basis for the model)
- Naive quark model estimate for Θ-N mass difference: approximately 1400 MeV (contrasted with the actual ~600 MeV difference)
- Number of possible pseudoscalar excitation states: 18 (9 from d\bar s plus 9 from u\bar s, each with three color and three L_z values)
- Estimated range of the parameter μ (effective mass of the excitation): 250 to 800 MeV
- Central nuclear potential for Θ in infinite nuclear matter: approximately 490 MeV (using μ = 250 MeV, α = 1/4, R₀ = 1 fm, nuclear density 1/6 fm⁻³)
- Threshold energy for strong decay of Θ-nucleus: 105 MeV
- Estimated mass of doubly strange pentaquark Ξ(3/2): approximately 1920 MeV (compared to experimental 1862 MeV)
- For 3 GeV photon beam on ⁴⁰Ca target with 200 MeV Θ binding: (p_γ − p_Σ)² = 0.004 GeV² for forward production
- For 3 GeV kaon beam: (p_K − p_π)² = 0.026 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton prediction of Diakonov et al. (1997) that an extremely narrow positive-strangeness pentaquark exists at 1.54 GeV with Jᴾ = 1/2⁺, following experimental confirmations by Nakano et al. (2003) and others. The model contrasts with naive quark model expectations (which would place the state ~600 MeV higher) and with lattice calculations by Csikor et al. (2003) that find odd parity as the lowest S=+1 state. The collective mechanism is adapted from the nuclear giant dipole resonance treatment of Brown and Bolsterli (1959). The paper predicts that Θ-nuclei could be produced in photon and kaon beam experiments, and that analogous charmed and bottom pentaquarks (following Jaffe and Wilczek, 2003) should also be stable against strong decay.
