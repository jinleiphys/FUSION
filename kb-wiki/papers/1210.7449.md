# Velocity Dependence of Charmonium Dissociation Temperature in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> In high-energy nuclear collisions, heavy quark potential at finite temperature controls the quarkonium production. Including the relaxation of the medium induced by the relative velocity between quarkonia and the deconfined expanding matter, the Debye screening is reduced and the quarkonium dissociation takes place at a higher temperature. As a consequence of the velocity dependent dissociation temperature, the quarkonium suppression at high transverse momentum is significantly weakened in high energy nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The dissociation temperature of charmonia increases with the quarkonium's velocity relative to the deconfined medium, because the finite relaxation time of the medium reduces Debye screening for a moving source; this velocity dependence weakens the suppression of high-transverse-momentum \(J/\psi\) in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC, but the effect is conditional on the still-uncertain stationary dissociation temperature \(T_d(0)\).

## Method

The authors model the non-equilibrium screening charge density around a moving heavy-quark source using a transport equation in the relaxation-time approximation, with the relaxation length \(L = \upsilon\tau\) estimated from the QCD conductivity. The resulting velocity-dependent heavy-quark potential is obtained analytically from the stationary lattice-QCD screened potential via an integral transform. The dissociation temperature for a moving quarkonium is then fixed by requiring that the screening radius equals the constant value determined from the stationary dissociation condition, and the effect on \(J/\psi\) suppression is computed by coupling this temperature to a hydrodynamic + transport model of the fireball evolution.

## Key numbers

* The stationary dissociation temperature \(T_d(0)\) is taken as an adjustable parameter; fitting the RHIC \(R_{AA}(N_{\text{part}})\) data yields \(T_d(0) = 1.6\,T_c\).
* For a stationary source, the screening radius \(r_d(T_d(0),0)\) runs from \(0.44\ \text{fm}\) to \(0.21\ \text{fm}\) as \(T_d(0)\) varies from \(T_c\) to \(2.5\,T_c\).
* At \(\upsilon = 0.8c\), the dissociation temperature rises to \(T_d \sim 1.2\text{--}2.7\,T_c\) (compared to \(T_d \sim 1\text{--}2\,T_c\) at \(\upsilon=0\)).
* The relaxation length is estimated as \(L = 15\upsilon/(2\pi^2 T)\).
* At RHIC, the \(J/\psi\) nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_t)\) rises to \(\approx 0.6\) at high \(p_t\); at LHC, \(R_{AA} \approx 0.3\) at high \(p_t\) (CMS data, \(|y|<2.4\)).
* The average transverse momentum of initially produced \(J/\psi\)s is about \(2\ \text{GeV}\) at RHIC and \(3\ \text{GeV}\) at LHC, corresponding to an average relative velocity above \(0.5c\).

## Context

This work extends the standard picture of quarkonium dissociation via Debye screening in a static medium (Satz 2005, Wong 2004) by incorporating the finite relaxation time of the QGP, following earlier linear-response studies of moving sources (Mustafa 2004, Chakraborty 2006). It provides a dynamical explanation for the observed rise of \(R_{AA}(p_t)\) at high transverse momentum at both RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) and LHC (CMS), which constant-\(T_d\) models cannot reproduce without invoking a strong Cronin effect disfavored by d+Au data (Adare 2012). The results enable future high-statistics measurements to test the velocity-dependent dissociation ansatz and refine the extraction of the in-medium heavy-quark potential.
