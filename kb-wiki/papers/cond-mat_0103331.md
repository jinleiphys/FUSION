# Three-body recombination into deep bound states in a Bose gas with large scattering length

> An effective field theory for the three-body system with large scattering length a is applied to three-body recombination into deep bound states in a Bose gas. The recombination constant αis calculated to first order in the short-distance interactions that allow the recombination. For a < 0, the dimensionless combination m α/(\hbar a^4) is a periodic function of \ln |a| that exhibits resonances at values of a that differ by multiplicative factors of 22.7. This dramatic behavior should be observable near a Feshbach resonance when a becomes large and negative.

# Three-body recombination into deep bound states in a Bose gas with large scattering length

## Key claim

For negative scattering length a, the dimensionless recombination constant mα/(ħ a⁴) is a periodic function of ln|a| that exhibits resonances at values of a differing by multiplicative factors of 22.7; these divergences are artifacts of first-order perturbation theory in the short-distance parameter h₁ and would become finite resonances when the width of Efimov states at threshold is accounted for nonperturbatively.

## Method

The authors apply an effective field theory (EFT) for three-body systems with large scattering length a, using a local Lagrangian with a boson field ψ and an auxiliary dimer field d. The recombination constant α_D for deep bound states is calculated to first order in the imaginary part h' of the three-body contact interaction coefficient, using the optical theorem to relate the imaginary part of the 3→3 scattering amplitude to the recombination rate; the integral equation for the transition amplitude t(p) is solved numerically with a momentum cutoff Λ_n that depends on the three-body parameter Λ_*.

## Key numbers

- For a > 0, the deep-state recombination constant is α_D = 0.041 h₁ ħ a⁴ / m, where h₁ is a dimensionless free parameter.
- For a < 0, the deep-state recombination constant follows α_D = (5.61 h₁ / sin²[s₀ ln(Λ_* |a|) + 1.77]) ħ a⁴ / m, with s₀ ≈ 1.0064.
- The multiplicative factor between successive resonance positions is exp(π/s₀) ≈ 22.7.
- For a > 0, the shallow-state recombination constant is α_S ≈ 67.9 cos²[s₀ ln(a Λ_*) + 1.74] ħ a⁴ / m.
- The Feshbach resonance in ⁸⁵Rb has width Δ = 11.5 G at B₀ = 155.2 G; the free parameter h₁ = 24.7 reproduces the overall magnitude of the loss rate below the resonance.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to three-body systems with large scattering length developed by Braaten, Hammer, and collaborators (2000), which previously calculated recombination into the shallow bound state for a > 0. The results for a < 0 confirm a general scaling law predicted by Efimov (1971) and explain discrepancies with numerical calculations by Esry et al. (1999), who found ratios |a_div|/a_max ranging from 1.6 to 2 for various two-body potentials; the EFT predicts this ratio should be 1 when short-distance parameters are constant, as expected near a Feshbach resonance. The predictions enable experimental observation of Efimov resonances in ultracold gases such as ⁸⁵Rb by tuning the scattering length through a Feshbach resonance.
