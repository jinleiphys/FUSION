# Gluon Transport Equation with Effective Mass and Dynamical Onset of Bose-Einstein Condensation

> We study the transport equation describing a dense system of gluons, in the small scattering angle approximation, taking into account medium-generated effective masses of the gluons. We focus on the case of overpopulated systems that are driven to Bose-Einstein condensation on their way to thermalization. The presence of a mass modifies the dispersion relation of the gluon, as compared to the massless case, but it is shown that this does not change qualitatively the scaling behavior in the vicinity of the onset.

## Key claim

The presence of a finite gluon mass modifies the dispersion relation compared to the massless case, but does not change qualitatively the scaling behavior in the vicinity of the onset of Bose-Einstein condensation, with the critical exponent for the evolution of the chemical potential remaining approximately one.

## Method

The authors derived a Fokker-Planck equation from the Boltzmann transport equation for gluons under the small scattering angle approximation, incorporating medium-generated effective masses for both the colliding gluons and the exchange gluons. They solved this transport equation numerically for isotropic, uniform systems with Glasma-type initial conditions, studying both underpopulated and overpopulated cases for various values of the gluon mass m and the Debye screening mass m_D.

## Key numbers

- Critical occupation factor f_0^c = 0.508 for m = 0.3 and m_D = 0.3 (in units of Q_s)
- Onset time tau_c = 0.101 for m = 0.3, m_D = 0.3, and f_0 = 1.0
- Onset time tau_c = 0.029 for m = 0.1, m_D = 0.1, and f_0 = 1.0
- Onset time tau_c = 0.663 for m = 0.7, m_D = 0.7, and f_0 = 1.0
- Onset time tau_c = 0.242 for m = 0.7, m_D = 0.1, and f_0 = 1.0
- Onset time tau_c = 0.095 for m = 0.1, m_D = 0.7, and f_0 = 1.0
- Critical exponent eta approximately 1 for the power-law fit mu = m - lambda (tau_c - tau)^eta in all cases studied

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of gluon condensation in overpopulated systems (Blaizot 2011, Blaizot 2013) by incorporating finite gluon masses into the transport equation. It confirms that the dynamical onset of Bose-Einstein condensation occurs on short time scales, consistent with earlier transport calculations using the small angle approximation (Blaizot 2013) and complete solutions of the Boltzmann equation (Scardina 2014, Xu 2014). The results enable further study of the evolution beyond the onset, including the formation and dynamics of the condensate, which is addressed in a companion paper.
