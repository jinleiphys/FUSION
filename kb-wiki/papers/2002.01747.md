# $p_T$-dependent multiplicity fluctuations from PCA and initial conditions

> We present a Principal Component Analysis for a hydrodynamic simulation and compare with CMS experimental data. While the results are reasonable for anisotropic flow, for multiplicity fluctuations they are qualitatively different. We argue that this is due to too large transverse momentum ($p_T$) fluctuations and $N-p_T$ covariance in the simulation than seen experimentally. In turn this is related to too large initial size fluctuations.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the qualitative disagreement between hydrodynamic simulation and CMS data for multiplicity fluctuations (n=0 principal component) arises because the simulation produces too large transverse momentum ($p_T$) fluctuations and $N-p_T$ covariance, which in turn originate from too large initial size fluctuations in the model.

## Method

The authors performed a Principal Component Analysis (PCA) on event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations using the NeXSPheRIO 3+1D perfect ideal fluid model, diagonalizing the covariance matrix of the single-particle distribution in transverse momentum bins. They compared the resulting leading and subleading principal components for multiplicity fluctuations (n=0) with CMS experimental data at LHC energies and with an analytical toy model that expresses the eigenvectors in terms of multiplicity fluctuations, $p_T$ fluctuations, and their covariance.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (2.76 TeV), the leading component $v_0^{(1)}(p_T)$ from CMS data is approximately flat in $p_T$, while the hydrodynamic simulation shows an increase with $p_T$; the increase is less strong at RHIC (200 GeV)
- The subleading component $v_0^{(2)}(p_T)$ crosses the horizontal axis at a point almost independent of centrality, consistent with CMS data
- The analytical model gives $v_0^{(2)}(p_T) \propto \sigma_{p_T}/\langle \bar{p}_T \rangle$, where $\sigma_{p_T}/\langle \bar{p}_T \rangle$ is the relative $p_T$ fluctuation
- The leading component is approximated as $v_0^{(1)}(p_T) \simeq \sigma_N/\langle N \rangle + [\ldots] \cdot p_T/\langle \bar{p}_T \rangle$, where the slope depends on $p_T$ fluctuations and $N-p_T$ covariance

## Context

This work extends the PCA framework proposed by Bhalerao et al. (2014) for studying event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions, and compares with first experimental PCA results from the CMS collaboration (Sirunyan et al., 2017). While previous work (Gardim et al., 2019) showed that hydrodynamic simulations agree with CMS data for anisotropic flow (n=2,3), this paper identifies a specific discrepancy for multiplicity fluctuations (n=0) and traces it to excessive initial size fluctuations. The results suggest that n=0 PCA provides a stringent test for models of initial conditions, complementing earlier studies of $p_T$ fluctuations by Bozek (2012) and Broniowski (2009).
