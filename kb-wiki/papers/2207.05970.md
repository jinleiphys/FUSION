# Impacts of anomaly on nuclear and neutron star equation of state based on a parity doublet model

> We examine the role of the $U(1)_A$ anomaly in a parity doublet model of nucleons which include the chiral variant and invariant masses. Our model expresses the $U(1)_A$ anomaly by the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t\,Hooft (KMT) interaction in the mesonic sector. After examining the roles of the KMT term in vacuum, we discuss its impacts on nuclear equations of state (EOS). The $U(1)_A$ anomaly increases the masses of the $η'$ and $σ$ mesons and enhances the chiral symmetry breaking. The $U(1)_A$ anomaly enlarges the energy difference between chiral symmetric and symmetry broken vacuum; in turn, the chiral restoration at high density adds a larger energy density (often referred as a bag constant) to EOSs than in the case without the anomaly, leading to softer EOSs. Including these $U(1)_A$ effects, we update the previously constructed unified equations of state that interpolate the nucleonic EOS at $n_B \le 2n_0$ ($n_{0} = 0.16\, \rm{fm^{-3}}$: nuclear saturation density) and quark EOS at $n_B \ge 5n_0$. The unified EOS is confronted with the observational constraints on the masses and radii of neutron stars. The softening of EOSs associated with the $U(1)$ anomaly reduces the overall radii, relaxing the previous constraint on the chiral invariant mass $m_0$. Including the attractive nonlinear $ρ$-$ω$ coupling to improved estimates for the slope parameter in the symmetry energy, our new estimate is $400\,{\rm MeV} \leq m_0 \leq 700\,{\rm MeV}$, with $m_0$ smaller than our previous estimate by $\sim 200$ MeV.

## Key claim

The $U(1)_A$ anomaly, implemented via the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) interaction, increases the energy difference between the chiral symmetry broken and restored phases, leading to a larger bag constant and softer nuclear equations of state; this softening reduces neutron star radii and relaxes the previous constraint on the chiral invariant mass $m_0$ to $400\,{\rm MeV} \leq m_0 \leq 700\,{\rm MeV}$, which is about $200$ MeV smaller than earlier estimates.

## Method

The authors constructed a parity doublet model (PDM) for nucleons including both chiral variant and invariant masses, coupled to a three-flavor mesonic Lagrangian with scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector mesons. The $U(1)_A$ anomaly was incorporated via the KMT interaction in the mesonic sector, with parameters tuned to reproduce vacuum properties (masses of $\eta$, $\eta'$, $\pi$, $K$, $\omega$, $\rho$, and nucleons) and nuclear saturation properties (density $n_0=0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}$, binding energy $16$ MeV, incompressibility $240$ MeV, symmetry energy $31$ MeV, slope parameter $57.7$ MeV). Unified equations of state were constructed by interpolating the hadronic PDM EOS at $n_B \le 2n_0$ with a three-flavor NJL quark model EOS at $n_B \ge 5n_0$, including $\beta$-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and then confronted with neutron star observational constraints.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $n_0 = 0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}$
- Binding energy at saturation: $16$ MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: $K_0 = 240$ MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: $S_0 = 31$ MeV
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: $L_0 = 57.7$ MeV
- New constraint on chiral invariant mass: $400\,{\rm MeV} \leq m_0 \leq 700\,{\rm MeV}$, smaller than previous estimate by $\sim 200$ MeV
- For $B=600$ MeV, calculated meson masses in vacuum: $m_{\eta}^{\rm PDM} = 542 \pm 15\,{\rm MeV}$, $m_{\eta'}^{\rm PDM} = 962 \pm 20\,{\rm MeV}$ (experimental: $m_{\eta}^{\rm exp} \simeq 547.9\,{\rm MeV}$, $m_{\eta'}^{\rm exp} \simeq 957.8\,{\rm MeV}$)
- Hadronic EOS used up to $n_B \le 2n_0$; quark EOS used at $n_B \ge 5n_0$
- Neutron star radius constraint from PSR J0740+6620 ($2.08\pm 0.07M_\odot$): $R_{2.08} = 12.35 \pm 0.75$ km (Miller 2021) and $12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98}$ km (Riley 2021)

## Context

This work extends previous parity doublet model studies of nuclear and neutron star equations of state (Motohiro 2015, PhysRevC.103.045205) by incorporating the $U(1)_A$ anomaly via the KMT interaction, which was previously omitted. The inclusion of the anomaly, together with attractive nonlinear $\rho$-$\omega$ coupling terms, softens the EOS at low densities ($\simeq 1$-$2n_0$), leading to smaller neutron star radii and relaxing the earlier constraint on $m_0$ from $600\,{\rm MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 900\,{\rm MeV}$ to $400\,{\rm MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 700\,{\rm MeV}$. The unified EOS framework, interpolating between hadronic and quark matter, enables confrontation with multi-messenger neutron star observations including the $2M_\odot$ mass constraint (NANOGrav 2019) and gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 (LIGO Scientific 2017, 2018).
