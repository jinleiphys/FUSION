# Estimation of Stopped Protons at Energies Relevant for a Beam Energy Scan at the BNL Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider

> The recent net-proton fluctuation results of the STAR (Solenoidal Tracker At RHIC) experiment from beam energy scan (BES) program at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) have drawn much attention to exploring the QCD critical point and the nature of deconfinement phase transition. There has been much speculation that the non-monotonic behavior of $κσ^{2}$ of the produced protons around $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 19.6 GeV in the STAR results may be due to the existence of QCD critical point. However, the experimentally measured proton distributions contain protons from heavy resonance decays, from baryon stopping and from direct production processes. These proton distributions are used to estimate the net-proton number fluctuation. Because it is difficult to disentangle the protons from the above-mentioned sources, it is better to devise a method which will account for the directly produced baryons (protons) to study the dynamical fluctuation at different center-of-mass energies. This is because, it is assumed that any associated criticality in the system could affect the particle production mechanism and hence the dynamical fluctuation in various conserved numbers. In the present work, we demonstrate a method to estimate the number of stopped protons at RHIC BES energies for central $0-5\%$ \auau collisions within STAR acceptance and discuss its implications on the net-proton fluctuation results.

## Key claim

The non-monotonic behavior of the net-proton fluctuation observable $\kappa\sigma^{2}$ observed by STAR around $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 19.6 GeV may be influenced by protons from baryon stopping, not only by the QCD critical point; the authors propose a method to estimate the stopped-proton contribution so that the dynamical fluctuations of directly produced protons can be isolated.

## Method

The authors fit net-proton rapidity distributions for central Au+Au collisions at AGS (2, 4, 8 AGeV) and RHIC (62.4, 200 GeV) with a two-source thermal function. From these fits, they extract the fraction of stopped protons in $|y| < 0.5$ and parametrize its energy dependence with an exponential function to interpolate values at RHIC BES energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200 GeV). They then fit proton $p_T$ spectra at available BES energies with Levy-Tsallis or Tsallis functions to determine the fraction of protons in the STAR $p_T$ acceptance (0.4–0.8 GeV/c), and combine both fractions with the total number of participating protons (158) to estimate the absolute number of stopped protons in the STAR acceptance.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7 GeV, the percentage of stopped protons in $|y| < 0.5$ is 27.27% ± 1.30%.
- At $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 19.6 GeV, the percentage of stopped protons in $|y| < 0.5$ is 13.90% ± 1.25%.
- At $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200 GeV, the percentage of stopped protons in $|y| < 0.5$ is 2.60% ± 0.51%.
- The number of stopped protons in STAR acceptance ($|y| < 0.5$, 0.4 < $p_T$ < 0.8 GeV/c) is 17.21 ± 0.86 at 7.7 GeV, 8.76 ± 0.80 at 19.6 GeV, and 1.6 ± 0.33 at 200 GeV.
- After subtracting stopped protons from the STAR mean proton number, the remaining produced protons are consistent with the measured antiproton mean number at 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV (e.g., at 200 GeV: difference = 4.12 ± 0.33 vs. antiproton mean = 4.11).

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon stopping as a probe of the QCD phase transition, following the three-fluid dynamics calculations of Ivanov (2010) which showed that a first-order phase transition qualitatively reproduces the non-monotonic behavior of mid-rapidity curvature. It directly addresses the STAR net-proton fluctuation results (Adamczyk, 2014) by quantifying the contribution of stopped protons that contaminate the fluctuation observable $\kappa\sigma^{2}$. The method enables future studies to add systematic uncertainties from stopping in Monte Carlo simulations and to search for the QCD critical point using only directly produced protons, particularly at lower BES energies where stopping is dominant.
