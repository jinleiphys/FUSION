# Finite-Q^2 corrections to parity-violating DIS

> Parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS) has been proposed as an important new tool to extract the flavor and isospin dependence of parton distributions in the nucleon. We discuss finite-Q^2 effects in PVDIS asymmetries arising from subleading kinematical corrections and longitudinal contributions to the photon-Z interference. For the proton, these need to be accounted for in order to accurately extract the d/u ratio at large x; for the deuteron they are important to consider when searching for evidence of charge symmetry violation in parton distributions or signals for physics beyond the standard model. We further explore the dependence of PVDIS asymmetries for polarized targets on the u and d helicity distributions at large x.

## Key claim

Finite-\(Q^2\) corrections to parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS) asymmetries, arising from subleading kinematical effects and longitudinal contributions to the \(\gamma Z\) interference, must be accounted for in order to accurately extract the \(d/u\) ratio at large \(x\) from a proton target and to search for charge symmetry violation or beyond-standard-model signals from a deuteron target; the magnitude of these corrections depends on the currently unknown ratio of longitudinal to transverse cross sections for the \(\gamma Z\) interference, \(R^{\gamma Z}\).

## Method

The authors derived the full expression for the PVDIS left-right asymmetry \(A^{\rm PV}\) in terms of electromagnetic and interference structure functions, including the ratios \(R^\gamma\) and \(R^{\gamma Z}\) of longitudinal to transverse virtual-photon cross sections and the kinematical factor \(r^2 = 1 + Q^2/\nu^2\). They then evaluated the asymmetry numerically for proton and deuteron targets at fixed beam energy \(E = 10\) GeV and \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), using the CTEQ6 parton distribution functions and the \(R_{1990}\) parametrization for \(R^\gamma\), while varying \(R^{\gamma Z}\) by \(\pm 10\%\) and \(\pm 20\%\) relative to \(R^\gamma\) to estimate its impact. For the deuteron, they also incorporated charge symmetry violation (CSV) distributions from the MRSTQED fit and a parametrized form to quantify the effect on the asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The functions \(Y_1\) and \(Y_3\) that encode finite-\(Q^2\) corrections: at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x = 0.7\), varying \(R^{\gamma Z}\) by \(\pm 20\%\) relative to \(R^\gamma\) shifts \(Y_1\) by \(\approx 4\%\) at \(Q^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\), decreasing to \(< 1\%\) at \(Q^2 \sim 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- The difference between the full \(Y_3\) and the Bjorken-limit result is of order \(40\%\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\sim 20\%\) at \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the proton asymmetry \(A^{\rm PV}_p\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), the purely kinematical \(r^2\) correction in the \(Y_3\) term (with \(R^\gamma=0\)) gives a \(2-4\%\) effect over \(0.5 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.9\); including \(R^\gamma\) reduces this to \(\approx 1-3\%\) over the same range, with an uncertainty of \(\approx \pm 0.5\%\) for \(x \lesssim 0.8\) and \(\approx 1\%\) at larger \(x\).
- A \(20\%\) difference between \(R^{\gamma Z}\) and \(R^\gamma\) shifts \(A^{\rm PV}_p\) by \(\approx 2\%\) relative to the \(R^{\gamma Z}\)-independent asymmetry.
- For the deuteron asymmetry \(A^{\rm PV}_d\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), the \(r^2\) correction (with \(R^\gamma=0\)) increases the asymmetry by \(\approx 1\%\) over \(0.5 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.9\); including \(R^\gamma\) reduces the correction to \(\lesssim 0-0.5\%\) for \(x \lesssim 0.6\) and \(\lesssim 0.5-1\%\) for \(x > 0.6\).
- A \(20\%\) modification of \(R^{\gamma Z}\) relative to \(R^\gamma\) shifts \(A^{\rm PV}_d\) by an additional \(\approx 1\%\) for \(x > 0.5\).
- The ratio of the axial to vector terms in the proton asymmetry, \(a_3^p/a_1^p\), ranges from \(0.21\) to \(0.24\) for \(0.4 < x < 0.9\) using CTEQ6 PDFs.
- The relative change in \(A^{\rm PV}_p\) from a modified \(d/u\) ratio (finite \(x\to1\) limit of 0.2) compared to the standard CTEQ6 prediction is \(\approx 2\%\) at \(x \sim 0.5\) and rises to \(\sim 10\%\) at \(x \sim 0.9\).

## Context

This work extends the parton-model treatment of PVDIS asymmetries by including finite-\(Q^2\) corrections that had not been previously considered, building on the formalism of Anselmino et al. (1995) and the earlier experimental confirmation of the standard model by Prescott et al. (1978). The analysis is motivated by proposals to use PVDIS on a proton to determine the \(d/u\) ratio at large \(x\) (Souder, 2004) and on a deuteron to search for charge symmetry violation in PDFs (reviewed by Miller et al., 2006). The results enable future experiments at Jefferson Lab (12 GeV upgrade) to quantify the systematic uncertainties from finite-\(Q^2\) effects when extracting PDFs or testing the standard model, and they highlight the need for empirical or theoretical determination of the interference ratio \(R^{\gamma Z}\).
