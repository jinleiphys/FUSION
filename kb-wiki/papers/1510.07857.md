# The influence of the Coulomb exchange term on nuclear single-proton resonances

> Nuclear single-proton resonances are sensitive to the Coulomb field, while the exchange term of Coulomb field is usually neglected due to its nonlocality. By combining the complex scaling method with the relativistic mean-field model, the influence of the Coulomb exchange term on the single-proton resonances is investigated by taking Sn isotopes and $N=82$ isotones as examples. It is found that the Coulomb exchange term reduces the single-proton resonance energy within the range of $0.4-0.6$ MeV, and lead to similar isotopic and isotonic trends of the resonance energy as those without the Coulomb exchange term. Moreover, the single-proton resonance width is also reduced by the Coulomb exchange term, whose influence generally decreases with the increasing neutron number and increases with the increasing proton number. However, the influence of the Coulomb exchange term cannot change the trend of the resonance width with respect to the neutron number and proton number. Furthermore, the influence of the Coulomb exchange term on the resonance width is investigated for the doubly magic nuclei $^{40}$Ca, $^{56,78}$Ni, $^{100,132}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb. It is found that the Coulomb exchange term reduces the proton resonance width within $0.2$ MeV, whose magnitude depends on the specific nucleus and the quantum numbers of resonant states.

## Key claim
The Coulomb exchange term reduces single-proton resonance energy by 0.4–0.6 MeV and reduces resonance width by up to 0.2 MeV, but these reductions are not large enough to change the isotopic or isotonic trends of either the resonance energy or the resonance width. The magnitude of the width reduction depends on the specific nucleus and the quantum numbers of the resonant state.

## Method
The authors combined the complex scaling method (CSM) with the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using the NL3 parameter set. The Coulomb exchange term was included phenomenologically via an effective charge factor η(Z,A) that multiplies the coupling strength of the Coulomb field, with parameters fitted to reproduce exact Coulomb exchange energies. The complex-scaled Dirac equation was solved by expansion in a harmonic oscillator basis, and resonance parameters (energy and width) were extracted from the θ-trajectory at the densest point.

## Key numbers
- The Coulomb exchange term reduces single-proton resonance energy within the range of 0.4–0.6 MeV for Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones.
- For the 1h9/2 resonant state in 120Sn, the resonance energy is 7.135 MeV and the width is 0.0037 MeV (at θ = 6.0°).
- The resonance energy spans from 4.643 MeV (2f7/2 in 124Sn) to 9.187 MeV (2f5/2 in 114Sn).
- The Coulomb exchange term reduces the proton resonance width within 0.2 MeV for doubly magic nuclei 40Ca, 56,78Ni, 100,132Sn, and 208Pb.
- For the broad resonant states 3p1/2 and 3p3/2 of 136Xe, the change in width reaches about 0.07 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the RMF-CSM approach previously developed by Guo (2010) for neutron resonances and by Zhu (2014) for proton resonances in 120Sn, where the Coulomb exchange term was neglected. The study systematically investigates isotopic chains (Sn isotopes) and isotonic chains (N=82 isotones) for the first time, and also examines doubly magic nuclei across a wide mass range. The phenomenological formula for the Coulomb exchange term was developed by Niu (2013) and shown to reproduce exact Coulomb exchange energies with relative deviations less than 1% for Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes. The results enable future studies of proton resonances in exotic nuclei where the Coulomb exchange term may have a more significant impact.
