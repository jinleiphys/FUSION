# Strangeness production time and the K+/pi+ horn

> We construct a hadronic kinetic model which describes production of strange particles in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions in the energy domain of SPS. We test this model on description of the sharp peak in the excitation function of multiplicity ratio K+/pi+ and demonstrate that hadronic model reproduces these data rather well. The model thus must be tested on other types of data in order to verify the hypothesis that deconfinement sets in at lowest SPS energies.

## Key claim
A hadronic kinetic model without deconfinement can reproduce the sharp peak in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) excitation function (the "horn") at SPS energies, but the model must be tested on other data before the hypothesis that deconfinement sets in at lowest SPS energies can be verified or falsified.

## Method
The authors constructed a non-equilibrium kinetic model for strange-particle production in which the fireball expansion is parametrized (rather than derived from hydrodynamics) using two periods: an initial accelerating phase and a later scaling expansion with power-law density evolution. Densities of \(K^+\), \(K^0\), \(K^{*+}\), and \(K^{*0}\) were evolved via a master equation including production and annihilation terms from hadronic reactions (e.g., \(\pi N \leftrightarrow KY\), \(\pi\pi\) reactions, \(K^*\) decay), while non-strange species were kept in chemical equilibrium and strange-antistrange species in relative chemical equilibrium. The model was run for Au+Au at 11.6 \(A\)GeV and Pb+Pb at 30, 40, 80, and 158 \(A\)GeV, with final-state densities matched to chemical freeze-out fits from Becattini (2004).

## Key numbers
- Initial energy densities used: 1 GeV/fm\(^3\) at 11.6 \(A\)GeV, 1.5 GeV/fm\(^3\) at 30 \(A\)GeV, 2 GeV/fm\(^3\) at 40 \(A\)GeV, 2.25 GeV/fm\(^3\) at 80 \(A\)GeV, 2.75 GeV/fm\(^3\) at 158 \(A\)GeV.
- Total fireball lifetimes (\(\tau_T\)) used: 25 fm/\(c\) at 11.6 and 30 \(A\)GeV, 20 fm/\(c\) at 40 \(A\)GeV, 15 fm/\(c\) at 80 and 158 \(A\)GeV.
- Final-state temperatures from the model (\(T_f\)) vs. chemical freeze-out temperatures (\(T\)) from Becattini (2004): at 11.6 \(A\)GeV, \(T_f = 114.7\) MeV vs. \(T = 118.1\) MeV; at 30 \(A\)GeV, \(T_f = 134.1\) MeV vs. \(T = 139.0\) MeV; at 40 \(A\)GeV, \(T_f = 143.3\) MeV vs. \(T = 147.6\) MeV; at 80 \(A\)GeV, \(T_f = 149.3\) MeV vs. \(T = 153.7\) MeV; at 158 \(A\)GeV, \(T_f = 153.6\) MeV vs. \(T = 157.8\) MeV.
- The model reproduces the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio peak at 30 \(A\)GeV with a lifetime of 25 fm/\(c\) and initial energy density 1.5 GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Context
This work extends the program of testing whether purely hadronic descriptions can explain the excitation functions observed in the SPS energy scan, specifically the "horn" in \(K^+/\pi^+\), the "kink" in pion multiplicity per participant, and the "step" in kaon mean \(p_t\) reported by the NA49 collaboration (2002). Prior transport codes generally failed to reproduce these multiplicities (Bass et al., 1999), while statistical hadronisation models (Braun-Munzinger et al., 2001) gave a broader peak than observed. The authors' hadronic kinetic model succeeds for the horn but requires further testing against transverse momentum spectra, correlation radii, and dilepton spectra to either verify or falsify the hadronic description and thereby clarify whether deconfinement onset occurs at lowest SPS energies.
