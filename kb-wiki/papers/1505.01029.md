# Can a many-nucleon structure be visible in bremsstrahlung emission during $α$ decay?

> We analyze if the nucleon structure of the $α$ decaying nucleus can be visible in the experimental bremsstrahlung spectra of the emitted photons which accompany such a decay. We develop a new formalism of the bremsstrahlung model taking into account distribution of nucleons in the $α$ decaying nuclear system. We conclude the following: (1) After inclusion of the nucleon structure into the model the calculated bremsstrahlung spectrum is changed very slowly for a majority of the $α$ decaying nuclei. However, we have observed that visible changes really exist for the $^{106}{\rm Te}$ nucleus ($Q_α=4.29$ MeV, $T_{1/2}$=70 mks) even for the energy of the emitted photons up to 1 MeV. This nucleus is a good candidate for future experimental study of this task. (2) Inclusion of the nucleon structure into the model increases the bremsstrahlung probability of the emitted photons. (3) We find the following tendencies for obtaining the nuclei, which have bremsstrahlung spectra more sensitive to the nucleon structure: (a) direction to nuclei with smaller $Z$, (b) direction to nuclei with larger $Q_α$-values.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the many-nucleon structure of an alpha-decaying nucleus can produce visible changes in the bremsstrahlung photon spectrum, but only for specific nuclei; for the majority of alpha emitters the effect is very small. The key condition is that the nucleus must have a low atomic number Z and a high Q_alpha value, with the specific candidate nucleus 106Te (Q_alpha = 4.29 MeV, half-life 70 microseconds) showing visible changes for photon energies up to 1 MeV.

## Method

The authors develop a new formalism for bremsstrahlung emission during alpha decay that includes the distribution of nucleons in both the alpha particle and the daughter nucleus. They construct the photon emission operator in the center-of-mass frame, separate the total wave function into relative motion and internal many-nucleon components using Slater determinants, and compute the matrix element in the dipole approximation for the effective charge while retaining multipole corrections elsewhere. The internal nucleon structure is included via electromagnetic form factors for the alpha particle and daughter nucleus, while the relative motion wave function is calculated using alpha-nucleus potentials extracted from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- 106Te nucleus: Q_alpha = 4.29 MeV, half-life T_1/2 = 70 microseconds (microseconds, denoted "mks" in the text)
- Visible changes in the bremsstrahlung spectrum for 106Te are observed for emitted photon energies up to 1 MeV
- The formalism applies to alpha decay observed for more than 420 nuclei with A > 105 and Z > 52

## Context

This work extends previous bremsstrahlung models for alpha decay (Kasagi 1997, Maydanyuk 2008, Giardina 2008) that achieved close agreement with experimental data without including many-nucleon structure, and builds on microscopic approaches to alpha decay (Thomas 1954, Delion 1992, Xu 2006). The paper also draws on the formalism developed for proton-nucleus scattering by Maydanyuk and Zhang (2015). The results identify 106Te as a candidate for future experimental study and establish criteria (low Z, high Q_alpha) for finding other nuclei where nucleon structure effects might be detectable.
