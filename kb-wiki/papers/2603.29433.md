# Nuclear shapes of Nb isotopes

> The study of the structure of odd-mass nuclei in regions characterized by the interplay of multiple particle-hole configurations represents a major challenge in nuclear structure physics. The odd-mass niobium isotopes ($Z = 41$), located near the $N = 60$ region, are of particular interest due to shape coexistence and quantum phase transitions. This work investigates the structure of the $^{93-103}$Nb isotopes using the intrinsic-frame formalism of the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM), aiming to determine nuclear shapes and explore shape coexistence, configuration crossing, and quantum phase transitions. We employ the intrinsic formalism of the IBFM-CM, including both 0p-0h (regular) and 2p-2h (intruder) configurations interacting with the unpaired nucleon, providing a self-consistent framework to study energy surfaces, shape coexistence, and intruder bands for both positive- and negative-parity states. A realistic Hamiltonian for niobium, determined in previous studies, is adopted. The formalism is applied to the $^{93-103}$Nb isotopes for both positive- and negative-parity bands. A detailed analysis of the mean-field energy surfaces has been performed, including axial energy curves, triaxial energy surfaces in the $β-γ$ plane, and the corresponding equilibrium deformation parameters. The results reveal clear evidence of configuration coexistence and crossing along the isotopic chain. The existence of crossing configurations is demonstrated around $N = 60$, corresponding to a quantum phase transition previously identified in the Sr and Zr isotopic chains. Furthermore, the presence of an unpaired nucleon in Nb influences the abruptness of the quantum phase transition, underscoring the sensitivity of the structural evolution to single-particle degrees of freedom.

# Nuclear shapes of Nb isotopes

## Key claim

The intrinsic-frame formalism of the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM) reveals that the odd-mass niobium isotopes exhibit configuration crossing around N=60, corresponding to a quantum phase transition previously identified in Sr and Zr, but the presence of the unpaired proton in Nb influences the abruptness of this transition, making it more sensitive to single-particle degrees of freedom than in the even-even cores.

## Method

The authors applied the intrinsic-state formalism of the IBFM-CM, which includes both 0p-0h (regular) and 2p-2h (intruder) boson configurations coupled to an unpaired proton occupying the 1g9/2 orbit for positive-parity states and the 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2 orbits for negative-parity states. A realistic Hamiltonian for niobium, previously determined through least-squares fits to experimental data in the laboratory frame by Gavr22b and Gavr2023, was adopted. The formalism was used to calculate mean-field energy surfaces including axial energy curves, triaxial energy surfaces in the β-γ plane, and equilibrium deformation parameters for the 93-103Nb isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- The isotopic chain studied spans 93Nb to 103Nb (Z=41, N=52 to N=62)
- For positive-parity states, the Hamiltonian matrix diagonalized has dimension 10 (from including both regular and intruder configurations)
- For negative-parity states, the Hamiltonian matrix diagonalized has dimension 12
- Configuration crossing between regular and intruder states occurs between A=99 and A=101 for positive-parity states
- For positive-parity states in the heaviest isotopes (101-103Nb), triaxial minima are located near γ ≈ 20°
- For negative-parity states in 93-99Nb, the first state has 1.0 probability for the p1/2- regular level, while for 101-103Nb the first state has 100% probability for the f5/2- intruder level
- The regular content of the ground state for negative parity is around 1 for 93-99Nb and 0 for 101-103Nb in the present work

## Context

This work extends previous laboratory-frame IBFM-CM studies of Nb isotopes by Gavr22b and Gavr2023 into the intrinsic-frame formalism, providing a geometric visualization of shape coexistence and quantum phase transitions. The results confirm the existence of an intertwined quantum phase transition in Nb, similar to that previously documented in even-even Zr isotopes by Gavrielov et al. (2019) and in Sr and Mo isotopes. The formalism enables comparison with the boson-core results from IBM-CM, showing that the unpaired proton modifies the transition sharpness compared to the Zr core. This work enables future studies of odd-mass nuclei in the A≈100 region using the intrinsic-frame approach to explore how single-particle degrees of freedom affect collective shape transitions.
