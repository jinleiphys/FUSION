# Elliptic and Triangular flow in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions

> We present a study of the elliptic (v2) and triangular (v3) flow and their corresponding eccentricity fluctuations for asymmetric (Au+Ag, Au+Cu and Au+Si) collisions at \sqrt_NN = 200 GeV. These are compared to the corresponding results from symmetric (Au+Au and Cu+Cu) collisions at the same energy. The study which is carried out using a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model shows that triangularity (ε_3), fluctuations in triangularity and v3 do not show much variation for the different colliding ion sizes studied. However the eccentricity (ε_2), fluctuations in eccentricity and v2 shows a strong dependence on colliding ion size for a given number of participating nucleons. Our study thus indicates that asymmetric heavy-ion collisions could be used to constrain models dealing with flow fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper uses the AMPT model to show that elliptic flow (v2) and its associated eccentricity fluctuations depend strongly on colliding ion size for a given number of participating nucleons, while triangular flow (v3) and triangularity show little variation across different asymmetric collision systems. The authors hedge that this sensitivity is primarily useful for constraining models of flow fluctuations related to the second harmonic, not the third.

## Method
The study uses the default AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with HIJING initial conditions and a Glauber model for participant nucleon distributions. The authors simulate Au+Au, Au+Ag, Au+Cu, Cu+Cu, and Au+Si collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, computing participant eccentricity (epsilon_2), triangularity (epsilon_3), their fluctuations, and the corresponding flow coefficients v2 and v3 as functions of number of participating nucleons, centrality fraction, and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- All collisions are at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- For mid-central collisions (60 < N_part < 250), fluctuations in epsilon_2 increase as system size decreases, then saturate for systems of size comparable to or smaller than Cu+Cu
- For 80 < N_part < 120 at midrapidity, v2 and v3 are proportional to epsilon_2 and epsilon_3 respectively for all collision systems studied
- v2 versus pT shows a clear dependence on colliding system size, increasing with larger ion size, while v3 versus pT shows much smaller dependence

## Context
This work extends studies of flow fluctuations from symmetric systems (Au+Au, Cu+Cu) to asymmetric systems (Au+Ag, Au+Cu, Au+Si) using the AMPT model, motivated by the proposed asymmetric heavy-ion collision program at RHIC. The results are compared to the approach of Alver and Roland (2010) on participant eccentricity and to the cumulant method of Bhalerao et al. (2011) for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV. The study indicates that asymmetric collisions can help disentangle path length and energy density effects in flow fluctuations, particularly for v2-related observables.
