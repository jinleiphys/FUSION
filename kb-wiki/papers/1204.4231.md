# Neutrino Capture on $^{13}$C

> We present neutrino cross sections on $^{13}$C. The charged-current cross sections leading to various states in the daughter $^{13}N$ and the neutral-current cross sections leading to various states in the daughter $^{13}$C are given. We also provide simple polynomial fits to those cross sections for quick estimates of the reaction rates. We briefly discuss possible implications for the current and future scintillator-based experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents updated neutrino cross sections on 13C using a new shell-model Hamiltonian (SFO) that improves the description of magnetic properties and Gamow-Teller transitions compared to earlier calculations, but the authors note that the cross sections are accurate only to within a few percent and that reactor neutrino flux uncertainties can cause a factor of two variation in predicted count rates.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the SFO Hamiltonian, which includes up to 2-3 hbar-omega excitations in the p-sd shell configurations, to evaluate charged-current and neutral-current neutrino cross sections on 13C. The cross sections were calculated using multipole expansion of the weak hadronic currents, with final-state interactions accounted for by the Fermi function for charge-exchange reactions. The results were fitted to a third-order polynomial in (E_nu - Q) to provide simple analytical forms for quick rate estimates.

## Key numbers

- For the charged-current reaction 13C(nu_e, e-)13N leading to the ground state (1/2-), the cross section at E_nu = 2.3 MeV is 2.00 x 10^-45 cm^2 and at E_nu = 16.5 MeV is 5.73 x 10^-42 cm^2
- For the charged-current reaction leading to the 3/2- state at 3.502 MeV excitation in 13N, the cross section at E_nu = 5.8 MeV is 1.22 x 10^-44 cm^2 and at E_nu = 17.0 MeV is 8.60 x 10^-42 cm^2
- The B(GT) value for the transition to 13N ground state (1/2-) is 0.284 with SFO Hamiltonian, compared to the experimental value of 0.411 +/- 0.004
- The B(GT) value for the transition to 13N (3/2-, 3.50 MeV) is 2.103 with SFO, compared to the experimental value of 1.64 +/- 0.10
- The B(M1) value for 13C (3/2-, 3.68 MeV) to 13C (1/2- g.s.) is 0.878 mu_N^2 with SFO, compared to the experimental value of 0.698 +/- 0.072
- The polynomial fit coefficients for the charged-current ground-state transition are a_1 = 0.16 MeV^-1, a_2 = 2.86 MeV^-2, a_3 = 0.00 MeV^-3, with Q = 2.2 MeV
- The energy-integrated count rate for electron neutrino appearance in reactor antineutrino flux varies between 5.20 and 13.02 in arbitrary units depending on the reactor flux model used

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Fukugita (1989) and others who used Cohen-Kurath wave functions with a larger quenching factor (g_A^eff/g_A = 0.69), while the SFO Hamiltonian achieves better agreement with experimental B(GT) and B(M1) values using a smaller quenching factor (0.95). The results are directly applicable to current scintillator-based reactor experiments (Daya Bay, Double Chooz, RENO) and future detectors like LENA, enabling searches for electron neutrino appearance in reactor antineutrino flux and helping to sort out neutrino flavors from supernova bursts.
