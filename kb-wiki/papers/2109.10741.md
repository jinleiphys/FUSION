# Combining Halo-EFT descriptions of nuclei and precise models of nuclear reactions

> The clear separation of scales observed in halo nuclei between the extended halo and the compact core makes these exotic nuclei a perfect subject for Effective Field Theory (EFT). Such description leads to a systematic expansion of the core-halo Hamiltonian, which naturally orders the nuclear-structure observables. In this short review, I show the advantage there is to include Halo-EFT descriptions within precise models of reactions. It helps identifying the nuclear-structure observables that matter in the description of the reactions, and enables us to easily bridge predictions of nuclear-structure calculations to reaction observables. I illustrate this on breakup, transfer and knockout reactions with 11Be, the archetypical one-neutron halo nucleus.

## Key claim

Including Halo-EFT descriptions within precise reaction models helps identify which nuclear-structure observables are actually probed in reactions and enables bridging predictions from nuclear-structure calculations to reaction observables, but the agreement with data depends on the reaction type and the degrees of freedom included (e.g., core excitation is needed for nuclear breakup on light targets).

## Method

The projectile (11Be) is described as a two-body core-halo system using Halo-EFT, where the core-neutron interaction is expanded in contact terms regularized by a Gaussian cutoff r0, with Low-Energy Constants (LECs) fitted to reproduce the neutron separation energy and, at next-to-leading order (NLO), also the Asymptotic Normalization Constant (ANC) from ab initio NCSMC calculations. Breakup reactions are modeled using the Dynamical Eikonal Approximation (DEA) at intermediate energies and the Coulomb-Corrected Eikonal model (CCE) at relativistic energies, while transfer reactions use the Adiabatic Distorted Wave Approximation (ADWA) in its finite-range implementation.

## Key numbers

- 11Be ground state binding energy: 503 keV (1s1/2 neutron bound to 10Be core)
- 11Be first excited bound state binding energy: 184 keV (0p1/2 neutron bound to 10Be)
- 5/2+ state at E = 1.274 MeV described as a d5/2 single-particle resonance
- 3/2+ state at E = 2.90 MeV tentatively described as a d3/2 resonance
- Breakup measured at RIKEN at 69A MeV (Pb target) and 67A MeV (C target)
- Breakup measured at GSI at 520A MeV (Pb and C targets)
- Transfer measured at deuteron energies Ed = 21.4, 18, 15, and 12 MeV
- Cutoff values r0 = 1.2 fm, 1.5 fm, and 2.0 fm tested for Halo-EFT interaction

## Context

This work extends the Halo-EFT framework (Bertulani, Hammer, van Kolck 2002; Hammer, Ji, Phillips 2017) by combining it with precise reaction models that go beyond perturbative approaches (Hammer, Phillips 2011; Acharya, Phillips 2013; Sanchez Sanchez et al. 2019). It builds on earlier studies using precise reaction models (Capel, Nunes 2006, 2007) and ab initio NCSMC calculations of 11Be (Calci et al. 2016). The results enable identification of which structure observables matter for each reaction type and validate ab initio predictions against experimental data, while also showing that core excitation (Moro, Lay 2012) is needed to describe nuclear breakup on light targets.
