# Collective motion in triaxial nuclei within minimal length concept

> The concept of minimal length, inspired by Heisenberg algebra, is applied to the geometrical collective Bohr- Mottelson model (BMM) of nuclei. With the deformed canonical commutation relation and the Pauli-Podolsky prescription, we have derived the quantized Hamiltonian operator for triaxial nuclei as we have previously done for axial prolate $γ$-rigid ones (M. Chabab et al., Phys. Lett. B 758 (2016) 212-218). By considering an infinite square well like potential in $β$ collective shape variable, the eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian are obtained in terms of zeros of Bessel functions of irrational order with an explicit dependence on the minimal length parameter. Moreover, the associated symmetry with the model that we have constructed here can be considered as a new quasi-dynamical critical point symmetries (CPSs) in nuclear structure. The theoretical results indicate a dramatic contribution (Low effect) of the minimal length to energy levels for lower values of the angular momentum and regular (significant effect) for higher values. In fact, these features show that the minimal length scenario which is useful in recognizing the properties of real deformed nuclei having high spins or strong rotations. Finally, numerical calculations are performed for some nuclei such as ${}^{124,128,130}$Xe and ${}^{114}$Pd revealing a qualitative agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing a minimal length parameter into the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian for triaxial gamma-rigid nuclei (the Z(4) model) produces a new quasi-dynamical critical point symmetry (Z(4)-ML) whose energy levels show a "low effect" of the minimal length for low angular momentum and a "significant effect" for high angular momentum, but the agreement with experimental data for four nuclei is only qualitative.

## Method

The authors start from the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian for triaxial gamma-rigid nuclei and incorporate a deformed canonical commutation relation with a minimal length parameter alpha, acting only on the beta collective coordinate. They solve the resulting differential equation analytically by assuming an infinite square well potential in beta, obtaining energy eigenvalues expressed in terms of zeros of Bessel functions of irrational order. The free parameters (alpha and the well width beta_omega) are then fitted to experimental energy spectra of four nuclei using a least-squares method.

## Key numbers

- For 114Pd: minimal length parameter alpha = 0.00006302, well width beta_omega = 0.64712
- For 124Xe: minimal length parameter alpha = 0.00010959, well width beta_omega = 0.66447
- For 128Xe: minimal length parameter alpha = 0.00000012, well width beta_omega = 0.89820
- For 130Xe: minimal length parameter alpha = 0.00000091, well width beta_omega = 0.89474
- The order of the Bessel function is given by v = (1/2) * sqrt[ L(L+4) + 3 n_omega (2L - n_omega) + 4 ], where L is angular momentum and n_omega is the wobbling quantum number
- The energy formula to first order in alpha is E ~ (hbar^2 / 2B_m) * (x_{s,v} / beta_omega)^2 * [1 + 2 hbar^2 alpha (x_{s,v} / beta_omega)^2], where x_{s,v} are zeros of Bessel functions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the minimal length concept to axial prolate gamma-rigid nuclei (Chabab et al., Phys. Lett. B 758 (2016) 212-218) into the triaxial regime, specifically for gamma = 30 degrees. It builds on the Z(4) model (a gamma-rigid version of the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian) and on earlier critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello 2000) and X(5) (Iachello 2001), as well as the X(3) model (Bonatsos et al. 2006). The Z(4)-ML model provides a framework for describing triaxial gamma-rigid nuclei with high spins or strong rotations, though the authors note that very few experimental candidates for triaxial shapes have been found.
