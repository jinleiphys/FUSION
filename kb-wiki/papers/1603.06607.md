# Consequences of high-$x$ proton size fluctuations in small collision systems at RHIC

> Recent measurements of jet production rates at large transverse momentum ($p_T$) in the collisions of small projectiles with large nuclei at RHIC and the LHC indicate that they have an unexpected relationship with estimates of the collision centrality. One compelling interpretation of the data is that it captures an $x_p$-dependent decrease in the average interaction strength of the nucleon in the projectile undergoing a hard scattering. A weakly interacting or "shrinking" nucleon in the projectile strikes fewer nucleons in the nucleus, resulting in a particular pattern of centrality-dependent modifications to high-$p_T$ processes. We describe a simple one-parameter geometric implementation of this picture within a modified Monte Carlo Glauber model tuned to $d$$+$Au jet data, and explore two of its major consequences. First, the model predicts a particular projectile-species dependence to the centrality dependence at high-$x_p$, opposite to that expected from an energy loss effect. Second, we find that some of the large centrality dependence observed for forward di-hadron production in $d$$+$Au collisions at RHIC may arise from the physics of the "shrinking" projectile nucleon, in addition to impact parameter-dependent shadowing or saturation effects at low nuclear-$x$. We conclude that analogous measurements in recently collected $p$$+$Au and $^3$He$+$Au collision data at RHIC can provide a unique test of these predictions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed centrality-dependent modifications to high-transverse-momentum jet production in small collision systems at RHIC can be explained by an $x_p$-dependent decrease in the interaction cross-section of the projectile nucleon undergoing a hard scattering, rather than by energy loss effects. The model predicts a specific projectile-species ordering of the modifications that is opposite to what would be expected from energy loss, providing a testable distinction between the two mechanisms.

## Method

The authors implement a one-parameter geometric model within a modified Monte Carlo Glauber framework, where the nucleon-nucleon interaction radius for the hard-scattered projectile nucleon decreases exponentially with the momentum fraction $x_p$ as $r(x_p) = \exp(-\beta x_p) r_0$, with $\beta$ as the single free parameter. They tune $\beta$ to the PHENIX centrality-dependent jet $R_{CP}$ data in $d$+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, then apply the same model to predict the nuclear modification factors $R_{pA}$ and $R_{CP}$ for $p$+Au and $^3$He+Au collisions at the same energy, using the PHOBOS Glauber Monte Carlo code and the PHENIX centrality framework based on beam-beam counter charge distributions.

## Key numbers

* The single free parameter $\beta$ was determined to be $1.38^{+0.09}_{-0.07}$ by fitting the calculated $d$+Au $R_{CP}$ in the 0-20%/60-88% centrality selection to PHENIX jet data.
* For projectile nucleon configurations with $x_p \sim 0.2$, the modified nucleon-nucleon cross-section $\sigma(x_p)$ is reduced by approximately 25% relative to the nominal $\sigma_{NN} = 42$ mb at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV.
* For projectile nucleon configurations with $x_p \sim 0.6$, $\sigma(x_p)$ is reduced by approximately 50% relative to the nominal $\sigma_{NN}$.
* At $x_p = 0.3$, the calculated 0-20%/60-88% $R_{CP}$ values are 0.38, 0.57, and 0.69 for $p$+Au, $d$+Au, and $^3$He+Au collisions respectively.

## Context

This work extends the proton color fluctuation picture first proposed by Alvioli et al. (2014) to explain the centrality-dependent jet modifications observed by PHENIX in $d$+Au collisions (Adare et al., 2015) and by ATLAS in $p$+Pb collisions (ATLAS Collaboration, 2014). The model provides an alternative to energy loss interpretations (Kang et al., 2015; Zhang et al., 2013) and to explanations based on impact-parameter-dependent nuclear parton densities (Helenius et al., 2012). The key prediction, that the modifications should be strongest in $p$+Au and weakest in $^3$He+Au collisions, can be tested with recently collected RHIC data on $p$+Au and $^3$He+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, enabling discrimination between the shrinking nucleon picture and energy loss mechanisms.
