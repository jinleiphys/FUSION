# Pseudo SU(3) shell model: Normal parity bands in odd-mass nuclei

> A pseudo shell SU(3) model description of normal parity bands in 159-Tb is presented. The Hamiltonian includes spherical Nilsson single-particle energies, the quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions, as well as three rotor terms. A systematic parametrization is introduced, accompained by a detailed discussion of the effect each term in the Hamiltonian has on the energy spectrum. Yrast and excited band wavefunctions are analyzed together with their B(E2) values.

## Key claim

The pseudo SU(3) shell model can quantitatively describe normal parity bands in odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei using a Hamiltonian whose primary interaction strengths (Nilsson single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole, and pairing) are fixed by systematics rather than fitted, with only two rotor parameters (a and b) adjusted to fine-tune the spectra.

## Method

The authors constructed a many-body basis for 159-Tb using pseudo SU(3) irreps, selecting the 15 proton-neutron coupled irreps with the largest expectation values of the second-order Casimir operator C2. They diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing spherical Nilsson single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions, plus three rotor-like terms (K_J^2, J^2, and an asymmetry term), with strengths taken from nuclear systematics rather than fitted to the data. Wavefunctions and B(E2) transition strengths were then analyzed for the four lowest-lying rotational bands.

## Key numbers

- 159-Tb has 15 valence protons (9 in normal parity orbitals, 6 in the 1h11/2 intruder orbital) and 12 valence neutrons (8 in normal parity orbitals, 4 in the 1i13/2 intruder orbital)
- The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength chi = 0.00753, derived from chi = 35 / A^(5/3)
- Pairing strengths: G_pi = 21/A = 0.132, G_nu = 17/A = 0.106
- Rotor parameters for 159-Tb: a = 0.0198 (K_J^2 term), b = -0.0031 (J^2 term), A_asym = 0.0008 (asymmetry term)
- Effective charges for B(E2) calculations: e_pi = 2.3, e_nu = 1.3
- For the yrast, 5/2_1^+, and 3/2_2^+ bands, the difference between experimental and predicted levels is less than 50 keV
- The 1/2_1^+ band head is predicted at an energy 150 keV larger than the measured value
- The K_J^2 term shifts the 5/2_1 band head from 277 keV (a=0) to 356 keV (with a=0.0198), close to the experimental value of 348 keV
- The J^2 term introduces a change of about 15% in the rotational spectra
- The interband transition 1/2_1 -> 5/2_1 is predicted at 2.96 e^2 b^2 x 10^(-2)
- Intraband B(E2) transition strengths are on the order of hundreds of e^2 b^2 x 10^(-2), while interband transitions are on the order of e^2 b^2 x 10^(-2) or fractions thereof

## Context

This work extends the pseudo SU(3) model previously applied to even-even nuclei (Beuscher et al., 1998; Beuscher et al., 1999) and odd-mass nuclei (Vargas et al., 1999) to a detailed analysis of 159-Tb, examining the role of each Hamiltonian term. The approach builds on the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) and the pseudo-spin concept (Hecht and Adler, 1969; Arima et al., 1969; Ratcliff, 1973), using a truncation scheme validated by large shell-model calculations (Zuker et al., 1995). The results enable future microscopic studies of other nuclear properties in heavy deformed nuclei, such as g-factors, M1 transitions, and beta decays, and open the possibility of treating intruder orbitals on the same footing using SU(3) irreps.
