# Constrained gamma-Z interference corrections to parity-violating electron scattering

> We present a comprehensive analysis of gamma-Z interference corrections to the weak charge of the proton measured in parity-violating electron scattering, including a survey of existing models and a critical analysis of their uncertainties. Constraints from parton distributions in the deep-inelastic region, together with new data on parity-violating electron scattering in the resonance region, result in significantly smaller uncertainties on the corrections compared to previous estimates. At the kinematics of the Qweak experiment, we determine the gamma-Z box correction to be ReBox^V_{gamma-Z} = (5.57 +- 0.36) x 10^{-3}. The new constraints also allow precise predictions to be made for parity-violating deep-inelastic asymmetries on the deuteron.

## Key claim

The gamma-Z box correction to the proton's weak charge at Qweak kinematics is determined to be ReBox^V_{gamma-Z} = (5.57 +/- 0.36) x 10^{-3}, with significantly smaller uncertainties than previous estimates because constraints from parton distributions in the deep-inelastic region and new parity-violating electron scattering data in the resonance region limit the model dependence that had caused disagreement among earlier analyses.

## Method

The authors compute the gamma-Z interference correction using a dispersion relation formalism that relates the real part of the box diagram to an integral over the imaginary part, which is expressed in terms of gamma-Z interference structure functions. They construct the Adelaide-Jefferson Lab-Manitoba (AJM) model by separating the kinematic range into three regions: a low-W, low-Q^2 resonance region described by the Christy-Bosted parametrization of electromagnetic structure functions transformed to the gamma-Z case; a high-W, low-Q^2 region described by a vector meson dominance plus Regge model; and a high-W, high-Q^2 region described by parton distribution functions from global fits. The uncertainties are constrained using data on parton distributions near the resonance-DIS transition and new inclusive parity-violating electron scattering asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- ReBox^V_{gamma-Z} = (5.57 +/- 0.36) x 10^{-3} at the kinematics of the Qweak experiment (approximately 1 GeV beam energy, t = -0.03 GeV^2)
- Previous unconstrained estimates: GHRM gave (5.4 +/- 2.0) x 10^{-3}, SBMT gave (4.7_{-0.4}^{+1.1}) x 10^{-3}, and CR gave (5.7 +/- 0.9) x 10^{-3}
- The CB parametrization of electromagnetic structure functions agrees with resonance region electron-proton scattering data to within 5%
- The vector meson contributions to the real photon-nucleon cross section have relative strengths r_V = {0.67, 0.062, 0.059} for V = rho, omega, and phi, respectively

## Context

This work resolves disagreements among previous analyses of the gamma-Z box correction by Gorchtein et al. (2011), Sibirtsev et al. (2010), and Carlson and Rislow (2012), whose central values agreed but whose uncertainties differed by factors of two. The new constraints from parton distribution functions and parity-violating electron scattering data reduce the dominant uncertainty from the continuum contribution in the VMD model, which had previously been assigned a 100% uncertainty. The results enable precise extraction of the proton's weak charge from the Qweak experiment and allow predictions for parity-violating deep-inelastic asymmetries on the deuteron.
