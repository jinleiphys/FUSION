# Light WIMPs And Equivalent Neutrinos

> Very light WIMPs (chi), thermal relics that annihilate late in the early Universe, change the energy and entropy densities at BBN and at recombination. BBN, in combination with the CMB, can remove some of the degeneracies among light WIMPs and equivalent neutrinos, constraining the existence and properties of each. Depending on the nature of the light WIMP (Majorana or Dirac fermion, real or complex scalar) the joint BBN + CMB analyses set lower bounds to m_chi in the range 0.5 - 5 MeV (m_chi/m_e > 1 - 10), and they identify best fit values for m_chi in the range 5 - 10 MeV. The joint BBN + CMB analysis finds a best fit value for the number of equivalent neutrinos, Delta N_nu ~ 0.65, nearly independent of the nature of the WIMP. In the absence of a light WIMP (m_chi > 20 MeV), N_eff = 3.05(1 + Delta N_nu /3). In this case, there is excellent agreement between BBN and the CMB, but the joint fit reveals Delta N_nu = 0.40+-0.17, disfavoring standard big bang nucleosynthesis (SBBN) (Delta N_nu = 0) at ~ 2.4 sigma, as well as a sterile neutrino (Delta N_nu = 1) at ~ 3.5 sigma. The best BBN + CMB joint fit disfavors the absence of dark radiation (Delta N_nu = 0 at ~ 95% confidence), while allowing for the presence of a sterile neutrino (Delta N_nu = 1 at ~ 1 sigma). For all cases considered here, the lithium problem persists. These results, presented at the TAUP 2013 Conference, are based on Nollett & Steigman (2013).

## Key claim

Joint BBN + CMB analyses can break degeneracies among light WIMP mass and equivalent neutrino number, but the results depend on the WIMP's nature (Majorana or Dirac fermion, real or complex scalar) and the presence of dark radiation; the lithium problem persists in all cases considered.

## Method

The authors performed BBN calculations for electromagnetically coupled light WIMPs (χ) with masses below ~20 MeV, allowing for the presence of equivalent neutrinos (ΔN_ν). They compared BBN-predicted primordial abundances of deuterium and ⁴He (using observational values y_DP = 2.60 ± 0.12 and Y_P = 0.254 ± 0.003) with Planck CMB constraints on Ω_b h² and N_eff, then performed joint likelihood analyses to constrain m_χ, ΔN_ν, and Ω_b h² simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Lower bounds on m_χ from joint BBN + CMB analysis range from 0.5 to 5 MeV (m_χ/m_e > 1 to 10), depending on WIMP nature.
- Best-fit WIMP masses from joint analysis lie in the range 5 to 10 MeV, depending on WIMP nature.
- Joint BBN + CMB best-fit value for equivalent neutrinos: ΔN_ν ≈ 0.65, nearly independent of WIMP nature.
- Without a light WIMP (m_χ > 20 MeV), N_eff = 3.05(1 + ΔN_ν/3); joint fit gives ΔN_ν = 0.40 ± 0.17.
- Without a light WIMP, SBBN (ΔN_ν = 0) is disfavored at ~2.4σ; a sterile neutrino (ΔN_ν = 1) is disfavored at ~3.5σ.
- For a Majorana fermion WIMP, lower bound m_χ ≥ 1.7 MeV and best fit m_χ = 7.9 MeV.
- Joint BBN + CMB fit without a light WIMP gives Ω_b h² = 0.0224 ± 0.0003 and N_eff = 3.46 ± 0.17.
- BBN-predicted lithium abundance for all cases: A(Li) = 2.72 ± 0.04, a factor of ~3 above the observed Spite Plateau value of A(Li) = 2.20 ± 0.06.

## Context

This work extends the earlier BBN calculations of Kohri, Takayama, and Yamaguchi (2005), Serpico and Raffelt (2005), and Boehm, Dolgov, and collaborators (2013) by allowing for simultaneous presence of both light WIMPs and dark radiation (ΔN_ν ≠ 0). The results are based on the more detailed analysis of Nollett and Steigman (2013). The joint BBN + CMB approach enables breaking degeneracies between WIMP mass and equivalent neutrino number that the CMB alone cannot resolve, and it identifies best-fit parameter ranges that can guide future experimental searches for light WIMPs and sterile neutrinos.
