# Systematic study of longitudinal and transverse helicity amplitudes in the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) reproduces the overall Q² behavior of nucleon helicity amplitudes for many resonances, especially the 1/2 amplitudes, but systematically underestimates the strength at low Q² due to missing quark-antiquark pair or meson production mechanisms.

## Method

The authors use the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model Hamiltonian with a Coulomb-like plus linear confining potential depending on the hyperradius, plus a hyperfine interaction. After fixing the quark mass to 1/3 of the nucleon mass and fitting the remaining parameters (τ = 4.59, α = 1.61 fm⁻²) to the baryon spectrum, they compute the transverse (A₁/₂, A₃/₂) and longitudinal (S₁/₂) helicity amplitudes as matrix elements of the non-relativistic quark electromagnetic current between nucleon and resonance states up to Q² = 5 (GeV/c)².

## Key numbers

- Quark mass fixed to 1/3 of the nucleon mass; hyperfine interaction strength set to reproduce the Δ–Nucleon mass difference.
- Potential parameters: τ = 4.59 and α = 1.61 fm⁻², fitted to the baryon spectrum.
- Neutron photocouplings (units 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²): N(1440) Bonn data 43 ± 12 vs. hCQM 58; N(1520) Bonn −49 ± 8 vs. hCQM −1; N(1535) Bonn −93 ± 11 vs. hCQM −82; N(1675) Bonn −60 ± 7 vs. hCQM −37; N(1680) Bonn 34 ± 6 vs. hCQM 38; N(1650) Bonn 25 ± 20 vs. hCQM −21; N(1710) Bonn −40 ± 20 vs. hCQM −22; N(1720) Bonn −80 ± 50 vs. hCQM −48.
- Helicity amplitudes calculated up to Q² = 5 (GeV/c)² for both proton and neutron.

## Context

This work extends the hCQM predictions previously published for photocouplings (Giannini et al., earlier work) and transverse excitation of negative parity resonances (Giannini et al., earlier work) to a systematic calculation of both transverse and longitudinal helicity amplitudes for 14 resonances. The results are compared with recent Jefferson Lab data and with the DMT model (DMT, earlier work) which includes meson cloud effects relevant at low Q². The paper discusses future inclusion of relativistic effects using the Point form of Dirac relativistic dynamics, which has already improved elastic form factor predictions (Giannini et al., 2007, 2010), and the unquenching of the quark model to incorporate quark-antiquark pair creation, following recent developments in the baryon sector (Santopinto et al., recent work).
