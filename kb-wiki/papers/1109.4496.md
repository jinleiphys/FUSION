# What happens to the Upsilon and eta_b in the quark-gluon plasma? Bottomonium spectral functions from lattice QCD

> We study bottomonium spectral functions in the quark-gluon plasma in the Upsilon and eta_b channels, using lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of light quark on highly anisotropic lattices. The bottom quark is treated with nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD). In the temperature range we consider, 0.42 < T/T_c < 2.09, we find that the ground states survive, whereas the excited states are suppressed as the temperature is increased. The position and width of the ground states are compared to analytical effective field theory (EFT) predictions. Systematic uncertainties of the maximum entropy method (MEM), used to construct the spectral functions, are discussed in some detail.

## Key claim

The ground states of the Upsilon and eta_b survive in the quark-gluon plasma up to at least T/T_c = 2.09, while the excited states become suppressed and are no longer discernible as separate peaks between 1.4 and 1.68 T/T_c, with the caveat that results at the two highest temperatures (T/T_c = 1.86 and 2.09) carry additional uncertainties from limited statistics and euclidean time range.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of light quark on highly anisotropic lattices (a_s/a_tau = 6), treating the bottom quark with nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) at O(v^4). They computed euclidean correlation functions in the vector (Upsilon) and pseudoscalar (eta_b) channels at seven temperatures between 0.42 and 2.09 T_c, then constructed spectral functions using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) with point sources.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 0.42 less than or equal to T/T_c less than or equal to 2.09, corresponding to T = 90 MeV to 458 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a_s approximately 0.162 fm, a_tau^{-1} approximately 7.35 GeV
- Ground state masses at zero temperature: Upsilon(1S) set to 9460.30 MeV (experimental value used to set scale), eta_b(1S) = 9438(8) MeV
- First excited state masses: Upsilon(2S) = 10026(15) MeV, eta_b(2S) = 10019(15) MeV
- Temperature dependence of correlators: monotonic effect below 3% across all temperatures
- Statistical relative errors on correlators: of order 10^{-4}
- Width estimate: Gamma/T approximately 1, corresponding to alpha_s approximately 0.4 when compared to weak-coupling prediction
- Thermal mass shift prediction used for comparison: delta E_thermal/M = 5.93 alpha_s (T_c/M)^2 (T/T_c)^2 approximately 0.0046 (T/T_c)^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Aarts et al., 2010) which found strong temperature dependence in P wave bottomonium correlators (chi_b channels) indicating melting, but much milder effects in S wave correlators. The results are consistent with experimental findings from CMS (Chatrchyan et al., 2011) showing survival of Upsilon(1S) but suppression of Upsilon(2S+3S) in heavy ion collisions. The temperature dependence of ground state peak positions and widths is compared to analytical effective field theory predictions from Brambilla et al. (2010), and the authors note their results are not inconsistent with the predicted quadratic temperature dependence of the mass shift, though systematic uncertainties prevent confirmation.
