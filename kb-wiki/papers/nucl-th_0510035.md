# Effects of In-Medium NN Cross Sections on Particle Production

> The in-medium modification of nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross sections is investigated by means of particle production in heavy ion collisions (HIC) at intermediate energies. In particular, the density dependence of the {\it inelastic} cross sections considerably affects the pion and kaon yields and their rapidity distributions. However, the $(π^{-}/π^{+})$- and $(K^{+}/K^{0})$-ratios depend only moderately on the in-medium behavior of the inelastic cross sections. It turns out that particle ratios seem to be robust observables in determining the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and, particularly, its isovector sector.

# Effects of In-Medium NN Cross Sections on Particle Production

## Key claim
The density dependence of inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections strongly affects pion and kaon yields and rapidity distributions in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies, but the (π⁻/π⁺) and (K⁺/K⁰) ratios depend only moderately on these in-medium modifications, making particle ratios robust observables for determining the nuclear equation of state, particularly its isovector sector.

## Method
The authors employed a Relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport equation to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 1.48 AGeV incident energy. They introduced a phenomenological parametrization of the inelastic NN cross section that scales with baryon density via a quenching function κ(ρ,β), where β is a free parameter, and compared results using free versus in-medium cross sections for pion and kaon production.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear matter saturation density is ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- At ρ = 1.5 ρ₀ with β = -1, the effective inelastic NN cross section is reduced by a factor of 2 relative to the free case
- For central Au+Au collisions at 1.48 AGeV, the (π⁻/π⁺) ratio is 1.63 (±0.11) with free σ_inel and 1.92 (±0.14) with in-medium σ_inel (β = -1)
- For the same system, the (K⁺/K⁰) ratio is 0.77 (±0.05) with free σ_inel and 0.74 (±0.05) with in-medium σ_inel (β = -1)
- The NL2 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model uses a compression modulus of 200 MeV and an effective Dirac mass of m* = 0.82 M (where M is the bare nucleon mass)

## Context
This work extends prior studies of in-medium elastic NN cross sections by Gaitanos et al. and Fuchs et al., which showed strong suppression of elastic cross sections at finite baryon density and their influence on hadronic reaction dynamics. The authors note that microscopic T-matrix calculations for inelastic processes are still rare, with only preliminary work by Larionov et al. The finding that particle ratios, particularly (K⁺/K⁰), are robust against in-medium modifications of inelastic cross sections supports their use as clean probes of the nuclear equation of state, especially the symmetry energy, as previously proposed by Ferini et al., Stoecker et al., and Bao et al.
