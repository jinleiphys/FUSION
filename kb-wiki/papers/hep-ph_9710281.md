# Masses, decays and mixings of gluonia in QCD

> This is a review of the estimate of the gluonia masses, decay and mixings from QCD spectral sum rules and low-energy theorems. Some phenomenological maximal gluonium-quarkonium mixing shemes in the scalar sector are presented. This talk is a compact version of the work in Ref.[1] (hep-ph/9612457) having the same title.

# Masses, Decays and Mixings of Gluonia in QCD

## Key claim

This review argues that QCD spectral sum rules and low-energy theorems predict a scalar gluonium state (G) with a mass around 1.5 GeV that is "almost pure," but that the observed scalar mesons below 2 GeV require a maximal mixing scheme between gluonia and quarkonia, with the caveat that the predictions depend on stability criteria for the sum rule parameters and on the specific choice of continuum threshold.

## Method

The analysis uses QCD spectral sum rules (Laplace/Borel sum rules) applied to two-point correlators built from gauge-invariant gluonic currents (two-gluon and three-gluon operators) to extract masses and decay constants of unmixed gluonia. Low-energy theorems (Veneziano-Novikov sum rules) are then employed to compute decay widths of scalar gluonia into ππ, ηη, ηη', 4π, and γγ, and a phenomenological 3×3 mixing matrix is constructed to explain the observed scalar meson spectra below 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Gluonic condensate: ⟨α_s G²⟩ ≈ (0.07 ± 0.01) GeV⁴
- QCD scale for three flavours: Λ = (375 ± 125) MeV
- Scalar gluonium G mass: 1.5 ± 0.2 GeV (upper bound: 2.16 ± 0.22 GeV)
- Tensor gluonium T mass: 2.0 ± 0.1 GeV (upper bound: 2.7 ± 0.4 GeV)
- Pseudoscalar gluonium P mass: 2.05 ± 0.19 GeV (upper bound: 2.34 ± 0.42 GeV)
- Scalar gluonium decay constant f_G: 390 ± 145 MeV
- Tensor gluonium decay constant f_T: 80 ± 14 MeV
- Pseudoscalar gluonium decay constant f_P: 8–17 MeV
- G(1.5) width into ηη': 5–10 MeV
- G(1.5) width into ηη: 1.1–2.2 MeV
- G(1.5) width into (4π)_S: 60–138 MeV
- G(1.5) width into γγ: 0.2–1.8 keV
- σ_B width into ππ: 0.2–0.5 GeV (for mass 0.75–1.0 GeV)
- σ'_B width into ππ: 0.5–1.3 GeV (for mass 1.37 GeV)
- S_3 (¯ss) mass: 1474 ± 44 MeV
- f_0(0.98) γγ width: ≈ 0.3 keV (experimental)
- σ(0.75–1.0) γγ width: 0.2–0.5 keV
- f_0(1.37) width into ππ: 22–48 MeV
- f_0(1.5) width into ππ: 25 MeV (experimental)
- f_J(1.71) width into K⁺K⁻: 112 ± 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the QCD spectral sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and the low-energy theorem framework of Veneziano (1979) to provide a comprehensive analysis of gluonium properties. It updates earlier predictions by Narison (1996) and compares with lattice calculations by Teper (1990s) and QCD inequalities by West (1990s). The mixing scheme is contrasted with the scenario of Close (1990s), where the S_2 state plays a more central role. The results enable experimental searches for gluonia at facilities like GAMS, Crystal Barrel, and Obelix, and suggest that the ζ(2.2) is a good tensor gluonium candidate while the E/ι(1.44) is too low to be the pseudoscalar glueball.
