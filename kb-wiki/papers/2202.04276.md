# $Ξ$ hypernuclei $^{15}_Ξ$C, $^{12}_Ξ$Be and $ΞN$ two-body interaction

> We study the energy spectra of $Ξ$ hypernuclei $^{15}_Ξ$C and $^{12}_Ξ$Be with a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with meson exchange $ΞN$ interactions. The RMF parameters are optimized to reproduce the average energy of KINKA and IRRAWADDY events for the ground state and also the average energy of KISO and IBUKI events for the excited state in $^{15}_Ξ$C. The potential depth of average $ΞN$ mean field potential is found to be about $-12$ MeV in the nuclear matter limit. We further introduce the two-body $s$- and $p$-wave interactions between valence nucleons and $Ξ$ particle. We found that the $s$-wave interaction deduced from the HAL LQCD results is rather weak to obtain the energy difference between IRRAWADDY and KINKA events. The $p$-wave interaction is added and fitted to reproduce the energy difference. The resulting interaction together with the $s$-wave one gives a reasonable energy simultanously for the IBUKI event as an excited $Ξ_p$ state. The model is further applied to predict the energy spectrum of $^{12}_Ξ$Be.

# Ξ hypernuclei ¹⁵ΞC, ¹²ΞBe and ΞN two-body interaction

## Key claim

The authors reproduce the observed binding energies of four experimental events (KINKA, IRRAWADDY, KISO, IBUKI) in ¹⁵ΞC using a relativistic mean field (RMF) model combined with two-body s-wave and p-wave ΞN interactions, but only by adding a fitted p-wave spin-spin term because the s-wave interaction extracted from HAL Lattice QCD is too weak to account for the energy splitting between the IRRAWADDY and KINKA events.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with meson-exchange ΞN interactions, using the PK1 parameter set for nucleon-meson couplings and fitting the Ξ-meson coupling constants g_σΞ, g_ωΞ, and f_ωΞ to reproduce the average binding energies of the KINKA/IRRAWADDY ground-state doublet and the KISO/IBUKI excited-state doublet in ¹⁵ΞC. They then introduce zero-range two-body s-wave and p-wave spin-isospin dependent residual interactions between valence nucleons and the Ξ particle, with s-wave strengths derived from the HAL LQCD potential and p-wave strength v_σ^p treated as a free parameter fitted to the IRRAWADDY-KINKA energy difference. The effect of the residual interactions is evaluated by first-order perturbation theory using RMF single-particle wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The fitted RMF coupling ratios are g_σΞ/g_σN = 0.497, g_ωΞ/g_ωN = 0.5377, and f_ωΞ/g_ωΞ = -0.8.
- The RMF calculation yields B_Ξ(1s) = 5.69 MeV for the average ground-state binding energy in ¹⁵ΞC, and B_Ξ(1p3/2) = 0.86 MeV and B_Ξ(1p1/2) = 0.72 MeV for the spin-orbit split p-states.
- The s-wave interaction strengths derived from the HAL LQCD potential are v_σ = 4.87 MeV fm³, v_τ = 26.83 MeV fm³, and v_στ = -53.56 MeV fm³.
- The p-wave spin-spin coupling strength that reproduces the IRRAWADDY-KINKA splitting is either v_σ^p = -150 MeV fm⁵ (attractive) or v_σ^p = 100 MeV fm⁵ (repulsive).
- The average ΞN mean-field potential depth converges to about -12 MeV in the nuclear matter limit for heavy systems (A ≥ 40).
- For ¹²ΞBe, the RMF calculation predicts B_Ξ ≈ 3 MeV for the Ξ⁻ 1s state (with Coulomb interaction included).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock study of ¹⁵ΞC (Sun 2016), which interpreted the KISO event as ¹⁴N(g.s.) + Ξ(1p). The present analysis incorporates the newly reported KINKA and IRRAWADDY events from J-PARC-E07 (2021) as a ground-state spin doublet, alongside the previously known KISO and IBUKI events as an excited-state doublet. The s-wave interaction is taken from the HAL LQCD ΞN potential (2020), while the p-wave interaction is introduced phenomenologically. The model is then applied to predict the energy spectrum of ¹²ΞBe, which is planned for study in the J-PARC-E70 experiment via the ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺) reaction. The resulting mean-field potential depth of -12 MeV in nuclear matter is about one-fourth of the nuclear mean field and half of the NΛ mean field, which may have implications for neutron star structure.
