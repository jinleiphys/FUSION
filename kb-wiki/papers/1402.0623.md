# Exotic hadron production in hard exclusive reactions

> We consider hard exclusive production of exotic hadrons to study their internal structure. Revisiting the constituent-counting rule for the large-angle exclusive scattering, we discuss general features expected for the production cross section of exotic hadrons whose leading Fock states are given by multi-quark states other than the ordinary baryon ($qqq$) or meson ($q\bar{q}$) states.We take the production of $Λ(1405)$ as an example and propose to study its partonic configuration from the asymptotic scaling of the cross section, which is measurable at J-PARC. We also discuss the production of a pair of the light-hadrons such as $f_0(980)$s and $a_0(980)$s in $γ^*γ$ collisions in the framework of QCD factorization, in which the cross section is expressed as a convolution of the perturbative coefficients and the generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). We demonstrate how the internal structure of $f_0(980)$ or $a_0(980)$ can be explored by measuring the GDAs at $e^+e^-$ experiments such as the B-factories.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the internal partonic configuration of exotic hadrons, specifically whether they are ordinary three-quark or five-quark states for \(\Lambda(1405)\), or ordinary \(q\bar{q}\) versus tetraquark \(qq\bar{q}\bar{q}\) states for \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\), can be distinguished by measuring the asymptotic scaling of exclusive production cross sections, provided experimental data at sufficiently high momentum transfer become available.

## Method

The authors apply the constituent-counting rule to large-angle exclusive scattering, where the differential cross section scales as \(d\sigma/dt \propto 1/s^{n-2}\) with \(n\) the total number of valence constituents in the initial and final hadrons. For \(\Lambda(1405)\) production in \(\pi + p \rightarrow K + \Lambda(1405)\), they compare the scaling expected for a three-quark versus a five-quark leading Fock state. For the pair production \(\gamma^* + \gamma \rightarrow f_0(980) + \bar{f}_0(980)\) (and similarly for \(a_0(980)\)), they use QCD factorization with generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) and model the GDAs with a simple parameterization that depends on the number of constituents, then compute the cross section as a function of \(Q^2\) and \(W^2\).

## Key numbers

- For \(\pi^- + p \rightarrow K + \Lambda\) (ground-state Lambda), the measured scaling exponent is \(n = 10.1 \pm 0.6\), consistent with the constituent-counting rule prediction for ordinary hadrons.
- For \(\Lambda(1405)\) as a five-quark state, the total constituent count is \(n = 2+3+2+5 = 12\), giving \(d\sigma/dt \propto 1/s^{10}\); for a three-quark \(\Lambda(1405)\), \(n = 2+3+2+3 = 10\), giving \(d\sigma/dt \propto 1/s^{8}\).
- The transition from the resonance region to the scaling region occurs at \(\sqrt{s} = 2\)–\(3\) GeV for the processes considered.
- For the GDA analysis of \(e + \gamma \rightarrow e + h + \bar{h}\), cross sections are shown at fixed \(W = 2.0\) GeV as a function of \(Q^2\), and at fixed \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) as a function of \(W^2\), with the GDA model parameters \((\alpha,\beta) = (1,1), (2,2), (3,3)\) and cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.0\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the constituent-counting rule, previously confirmed for ordinary hadrons at BNL and JLab (BNL and JLab experiments), to the production of exotic hadron candidates. It also applies the GDA formalism, originally developed for meson pair production in \(\gamma^*\gamma\) collisions by Muller (1994) and Diehl (2000), to the scalar mesons \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\), which are long-standing tetraquark candidates. The proposed measurements are enabled by the high-momentum beam at J-PARC for \(\Lambda(1405)\) production and by \(e^+e^-\) experiments such as the B-factories for the GDA-based studies.
