# Topological tensor invariants and the current algebra approach: Analysis of 196 nonleptonic two-body decays of single and double charm baryons -- a review

> We shed new light on the standard current algebra approach to the nonleptonic two-body decays of single and double heavy charm baryons. By making use of the completeness relation for the flavor wave functions of the ground state baryon {\bf 20'} representation we are able to rewrite the results of the current algebra approach in terms of the seven topological tensor invariants describing the decays. The representation of the current algebra results in terms of topological tensor invariants depends only on the initial and final state of the process. The summation over intermediate states inherent to the current algebra result is automatically taken care of in the tensor representation. In this way one arrives at a new, quick and very compact assessment of the results of the current algebra/pole model calculation. For example, one can quickly identify the decays in which the p.v.\ $S$-wave amplitude is predicted to vanish implying zero polarization asymmetries in these decays. We provide tables of the values of the seven topological tensor invariants for all Cabibbo favored and singly and doubly Cabibbo suppressed nonleptonic single and double charm baryon decays. In total we treat 196 charm baryon decays. We also discuss the charm preserving $ΔC=0$ decays of single charm baryons and the usual hyperon decays.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard current algebra/pole model results for nonleptonic two-body decays of single and double charm baryons can be exactly rewritten in terms of seven topological tensor invariants, with the summation over intermediate states inherent to the current algebra approach automatically handled by the completeness relation for the ground state baryon flavor wave functions, provided one works within the SU(3) limit and assumes mass degeneracy for flavor-degenerate intermediate states.

## Method

The authors construct third-rank flavor tensors for the 20 ground-state baryons in the SU(4) **20'** representation and use their orthonormality and completeness relations to express the current algebra s-channel and u-channel pole contributions as linear combinations of seven topological tensor invariants. These invariants are built from contractions of the baryon flavor tensors, the pseudoscalar meson tensor, and the fourth-rank flavor-changing tensor representing the antisymmetric part of the effective weak Hamiltonian. The values of the seven topological tensor invariants are tabulated for 196 charm baryon decays, covering Cabibbo-favored, singly Cabibbo-suppressed, and doubly Cabibbo-suppressed transitions for both single and double charm baryons, as well as charm-preserving decays and hyperon decays.

## Key numbers

* The ground state baryon **20'** representation of SU(4) has dimension 20, calculated as \(d = N(N^2-1)/3\) for SU(N), giving \(d=8\) for SU(3) and \(d=20\) for SU(4).
* The completeness relation for the third-rank baryon flavor tensors is given by \(\sum_\ell B^\ell_{k[mn]}B_\ell^{b[cd]} = \frac{2}{6}(\delta_k^b \delta_m^c \delta_n^d - \delta_k^b \delta_m^d \delta_n^c) - \frac{1}{6}(\delta_m^b \delta_n^c \delta_k^d - \delta_m^b \delta_n^d \delta_k^c) - \frac{1}{6}(\delta_n^b \delta_k^c \delta_m^d - \delta_n^b \delta_k^d \delta_m^c)\).
* The measured mass of \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) is \(3621.2 \pm 0.7\) MeV; the predicted mass of \(\Omega_{cc}^+\) is 3710 MeV based on the one-gluon exchange model.
* The measured asymmetry parameter for \(\Xi_{c}^0 \to \Xi^-\pi^+\) is \(\alpha = -0.60 \pm 0.04\) from the Belle collaboration.
* The BESIII collaboration plans to increase the rate of \(\Lambda_c^+\) pairs by a factor of 16, from about \(10^5\) to \(1.6 \times 10^6\) pairs, with an energy upgrade to 4.9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the topological diagram approach to nonleptonic charm baryon decays, which was previously applied by Kohara (1991) and He et al. (2018) but with an overcounting of tensor invariants. The paper directly relates to the current algebra plus pole model calculations of Cheng and collaborators (2020, 2019, 2020) and provides a compact tensor representation that automatically accounts for the intermediate state summation. The results enable quick identification of decays where the parity-violating S-wave amplitude vanishes, implying zero polarization asymmetries, and provide a framework for testing against upcoming experimental data from BESIII, Belle, and LHCb.
