# Quantum-Information Content of Fractional Occupation Probabilities in Nuclei

> Three measures of the information content of a probability distribution are briefly reviewed. They are applied to fractional occupation probabilities in light nuclei, taking into account short-range correlations. The effect of short-range correlations is to increase the information entropy (or disorder) of nuclei, comparing with the independent particle model. It is also indicated that the information entropy can serve as a sensitive index of order and short-range correlations in nuclei. It is concluded that increasing $Z$, the information entropy increases i.e. the disorder of the nucleus increases for all measures of information considered in the present work.

# Quantum-Information Content of Fractional Occupation Probabilities in Nuclei

## Key claim

Short-range correlations increase the information entropy (disorder) of nuclei compared to the independent particle model, and the information entropy increases with atomic number Z for all three information measures considered (Jaynes, Onicescu, and Stotland). However, the depletion of the Fermi sea due to short-range correlations is approximately constant at about 32% for the region Z between 4 and 40, meaning the entropy increase with Z is not driven by a varying depletion fraction.

## Method

The authors employed fractional occupation probabilities for protons in light nuclei (A between 4 and 40) obtained from a Jastrow-type short-range correlation model fitted to charge form factor data, using the natural orbital representation to diagonalize the one-body density matrix. They calculated three discrete information measures, Jaynes entropy S_J, Onicescu information energy S_E (taken as 1/E), and Stotland entropy S_F, as functions of atomic number Z, comparing cases with short-range correlations (cases A and B) against the independent particle model (case C).

## Key numbers

- For 4He (case A): S_J = 0.693, S_E = 1.998, S_F = 0.693
- For 16O (case A): S_J = 1.086, S_E = 2.642, S_F = 1.333; for 16O (case C, IPM): S_J = 0.562, S_E = 1.600, S_F = 0.650
- For 40Ca (case A): S_J = 1.541, S_E = 4.047, S_F = 1.756; for 40Ca (case C, IPM): S_J = 1.168, S_E = 2.778, S_F diverges (not reported)
- The depletion of the Fermi sea due to short-range correlations is about 32%, approximately constant for Z between 4 and 40
- For 12C (case A): S_J = 1.018, S_E = 2.561, S_F = 1.077; for 12C (case B, state-dependent): S_J = 0.956, S_E = 2.304, S_F = 1.061

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of Shannon entropy S_S for continuous density distributions in nuclei (Massen, 2001) to discrete Jaynes entropy S_J using fractional occupation probabilities, and introduces Onicescu and Stotland measures for nuclear systems. The authors compare their results to analogous atomic calculations by Sagar (1999) and find that both discrete and continuous entropies increase with Z, though the discrete Jaynes entropy shows a different functional form. The study enables using information entropy as a sensitive index of short-range correlations and nuclear disorder, and suggests future work on similar relationships in nuclear physics to those found in chemistry, such as connections between entropy and correlation energy.
