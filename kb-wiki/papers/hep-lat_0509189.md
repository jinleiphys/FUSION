# Low-energy J/psi-Hadron Interactions from Quenched Lattice QCD

> The J/psi-hadron interaction is a key ingredient in analyzing the J/psi suppression in hot hadronic matter as well as the propagation of J/psi in nuclei. As a first step to clarify the J/psi-hadron interactions at low energies, we have calculated J/psi-pi, J/psi-rho and J/psi-nucleon scattering lengths by the quenched lattice QCD simulations with Wilson fermions for beta=6.2 on 24^3*48 and 32^3*48 lattices. Using the Luscher's method to extract the scattering length from the simulations in a finite box, we find an attractive interaction in the S-wave channel for all three systems: Among others, the J/psi-nucleon interaction is most attractive. Possibility of the J/psi-nucleon bound state is also discussed.

## Key claim
Using quenched lattice QCD simulations with Luscher's method, the authors find attractive S-wave interactions for J/psi-pi, J/psi-rho, and J/psi-nucleon systems, with the J/psi-nucleon interaction being the most attractive; however, the results are preliminary due to large statistical errors and limited lattice volumes, and the possibility of a J/psi-nucleon bound state remains an open question requiring further study.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using unimproved Wilson gauge action and Wilson fermions at beta=6.2 on 24^3x48 and 32^3x48 lattices, with lattice spacing approximately 0.067 fm. They extracted energy shifts from the ratio of J/psi-hadron four-point functions to hadron two-point functions, then used Luscher's formula directly (without large-L expansion) to obtain S-wave scattering lengths for J/psi-pi, J/psi-rho, and J/psi-nucleon systems.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a ~ 0.067 fm, with lattice sizes L ~ 1.6 fm (24^3) and L ~ 2.1 fm (32^3)
- Charm quark kappa = 0.1360, light quark kappa values = 0.1520, 0.1506, 0.1489
- J/psi mass ~ 3.0 GeV, pion masses in the range 0.6-1.2 GeV
- Number of gauge configurations: 161 for the 24^3 lattice, 169 for the 32^3 lattice
- The J/psi-pi scattering length is negative and small compared to the I=2 pi-pi scattering length (shown as a reference cross in Figure 3)
- The J/psi-nucleon scattering length could be order of magnitude larger than J/psi-pi, though with large error bars

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of J/psi-hadron interactions (referenced as AH, WE, TO, SV, SL in the paper) and provides the first lattice QCD calculation of J/psi scattering lengths with light hadrons. The results are relevant for understanding J/psi suppression in hot hadronic matter and J/psi propagation in nuclei, as observed in CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC experiments. The authors note that future work requires better statistics, larger lattice volumes, and checks of the Luscher formula applicability, as well as analysis of inelastic contributions such as D-Dbar in the J/psi-rho channel.
