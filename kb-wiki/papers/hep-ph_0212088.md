# Diffractive heavy pseudoscalar-meson productions by weak neutral currents

> A first theoretical study for neutrino-induced diffractive productions of heavy pseudoscalar-mesons, η_c and η_b, off a nucleon is performed based on factorization formalism in QCD. We evaluate the forward diffractive production cross section in perturbative QCD in terms of the light-cone wave functions of Z boson and η_{c,b} mesons, and the gluon distribution of the nucleon. The diffractive production of η_c is governed by the axial vector coupling of the longitudinally polarized Z boson to Q\bar{Q} pair, and the resulting η_c production cross section is larger than the J/ψone by one order of magnitude. The bottomonium η_b production, which shows up for higher beam energy, is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors present the first theoretical study of neutrino-induced diffractive production of heavy pseudoscalar mesons (η_c and η_b) off a nucleon, claiming that the η_c production cross section is larger than that for J/ψ by about one order of magnitude, but this result depends on the assumption that the diffractive slope B_η_Q equals that of the corresponding vector meson and uses a specific non-relativistic Cornell potential model for the meson wave functions.

## Method

The authors apply a factorization formalism in perturbative QCD at leading logarithmic order, generalizing the approach previously used for vector meson electroproduction (BFGMS, FKS, RRML). They compute the forward diffractive production cross section by combining light-cone wave functions for the Z boson and η_c/η_b mesons with the unintegrated gluon distribution of the nucleon, using the color-dipole picture. The η_Q meson wave functions are derived from the vector-meson wave functions via spin symmetry in the heavy-quark limit, and the nonperturbative scalar function φ(α,k) is taken from a non-relativistic Cornell potential model (SHIAH) with quark masses m_c=1.5 GeV and m_b=4.9 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The η_c production rate exceeds the J/ψ production rate by a factor of approximately 20, primarily due to the ratio of weak couplings (c_A/c_V)^2 ≈ 7 and additional differences in the overlap integrals.
- The elastic η_c production cross section σ(νN → ν'N' η_c) increases monotonically with neutrino beam energy E_ν, with the calculation using M_η_c = 2.98 GeV, M_N = 0.94 GeV, and M_Z = 91.2 GeV.
- The diffractive slope assumed for η_c is B_η_c = 4.5 GeV^{-2} and for η_b is B_η_b = 3.9 GeV^{-2}, taken from the corresponding vector meson values (RRML).
- For η_b, the mass is estimated as M_η_b = 9.45 GeV (HK), and the production rate is much smaller than for η_c but increases more rapidly with E_ν, potentially becoming comparable at higher beam energies.

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization formalism for diffractive vector meson electroproduction (BFGMS, FKS, RRML) to the weak neutral current sector, enabling the study of pseudoscalar mesons via neutrino beams. It contrasts with earlier theoretical calculations for J/ψ production using vector meson dominance models (BKP) and for D_s^- production with generalized parton densities (LS). The results suggest that neutrino-induced diffractive production could provide a new experimental method to observe η_b, which has not yet been detected, using high-intensity neutrino beams at ongoing or future neutrino factories.
