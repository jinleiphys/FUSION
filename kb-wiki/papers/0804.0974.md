# Soft-hadronic observables for relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> The relativistic hydrodynamics together with the single-freeze-out statistical hadronization model is used to describe the midrapidity hadron production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. At the highest RHIC energy our approach gives a quite satisfactory global description of soft hadronic observables including the HBT radii. With the increased initial energy, from RHIC to LHC, we expect the saturation of the pion elliptic flow and the moderate increase of the correlation radii.

# Soft-hadronic observables for relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
The authors claim that a combined relativistic hydrodynamics plus single-freeze-out statistical hadronization model gives a "quite satisfactory global description" of soft hadronic observables at the highest RHIC energy, including HBT radii, at the level of 10-15% for pion and kaon observables; for LHC they predict saturation of pion elliptic flow and a moderate increase of correlation radii, but note that the proton elliptic flow discrepancy at RHIC (model overpredicts data) is likely due to neglected final-state elastic rescattering.

## Method
The authors use a boost-invariant hydrodynamic code with zero baryon chemical potential and a realistic equation of state that interpolates between lattice results and a hadron gas model, starting at proper time τ₀ = 1 fm with no initial transverse flow. The freeze-out hypersurface and flow profile from hydrodynamics are fed into the THERMINATOR Monte-Carlo statistical hadronization model, which includes full hadronic resonance decays and uses a two-particle method with Coulomb effects to compute correlation functions. Initial entropy density profiles are constructed from a linear combination of wounded-nucleon and binary-collision densities via the optical Glauber model, with parameters κ = 0.14 (RHIC) and κ = 0.20 (LHC).

## Key numbers
- Initial central temperature Tᵢ = 320 MeV for RHIC (Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV) and Tᵢ = 450 MeV for LHC (Pb+Pb)
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 150 MeV for both RHIC and LHC
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section σ = 42 mb for RHIC, σ = 63 mb for LHC
- Impact parameter b = 7.16 fm for RHIC and b = 7.60 fm for LHC at centrality class c = 20-30%
- Chemical potentials at freeze-out for RHIC: μ_B = 28.5 MeV, μ_S = 9 MeV, μ_{I₃} = 0.9 MeV; for LHC: μ_B = 0.8 MeV, μ_S = 0, μ_{I₃} = 0
- Pion HBT ratio R_out/R_side ≈ 1.2-1.25 at RHIC (above experimental ratio but better than many other hydrodynamic approaches)
- Entropy conservation satisfied at relative level of 10⁻⁴ or better in the numerical code

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic approach of Chojnacki et al. (2004, 2006, 2007) and the THERMINATOR model of Kisiel et al. (2005) to provide a global description of soft hadron production at RHIC and predictions for LHC. It builds on earlier hydrodynamic models reviewed by Kolb and Heinz (2003), Huovinen and Ruuskanen (2003), and Shuryak (2004), and uses the equation of state from lattice results of Aoki et al. (2005). The approach differs from others by using a single-freeze-out scenario with a relatively high freeze-out temperature (150 MeV) and full resonance treatment, which helps describe HBT radii. The authors note that Gaussian initial conditions from Broniowski et al. (2008) yield even better HBT results, and that similar LHC predictions from other hydrodynamic models were summarized by Abreu et al. (2007). The main limitation is the neglect of elastic rescattering after chemical freeze-out, which is small for pions but more important for protons.
