# Chiral geometry of higher excited bands in triaxial nuclei with particle-hole configuration

> The lowest six rotational bands have been studied in the particle-rotor model with the particle-hole configuration $πh^1_{11/2}\otimesνh^{-1}_{11/2}$ and different triaxiality parameter $γ$. Both constant and spin-dependent variable moments of inertial (CMI and VMI) are introduced. The energy spectra, electromagnetic transition probabilities, angular momentum components and $K$-distribution have been examined. It is shown that, besides the band 1 and band 2, the predicted band 3 and band 4 in the calculations of both CMI and VMI for atomic nuclei with $γ=30^\circ$ could be interpreted as chiral doublet bands.

## Key claim
The particle-rotor model predicts that, for the particle-hole configuration πh¹₁₁/₂⊗νh⁻¹₁₁/₂ with triaxiality parameter γ=30°, not only the yrast and yrare bands (bands 1 and 2) but also the next two higher excited bands (bands 3 and 4) can form chiral doublet bands, but only in the spin region I≥20ħ and only for γ very close to 30°.

## Method
The particle-rotor model (PRM) was solved for the lowest six rotational bands of an odd-odd nucleus with the symmetric πh₁₁/₂⊗νh⁻¹₁₁/₂ configuration, using both constant moment of inertia (CMI) and spin-dependent variable moment of inertia (VMI) with parameters J₀=30 MeV⁻¹ħ² and b=0.01. The triaxial deformation parameter was set to γ=30° for the main results, with additional calculations for 15°≤γ≤30°. Energy spectra, intraband B(M1) and B(E2) transition probabilities, angular momentum component decompositions, and K-distributions were computed and compared between bands.

## Key numbers
- Energy difference between band 3 and band 4 for I≥16ħ is within 310 keV in CMI calculations and within 30 keV in VMI calculations.
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment Q₀ was taken as 3.5 eb; gyromagnetic ratios were g_R=0.44, g_p=1.21, g_n=-0.21.
- The VMI moment of inertia follows J=J₀√(1+bI(I+1)) with J₀=30 MeV⁻¹ħ² and b=0.01; at high spin (I≫10ħ), J≃3I MeV⁻¹ħ².
- Static chirality between band 3 and band 4 is indicated for spin I≥20ħ.
- Deviation of γ from 30° causes static chirality to be violated quickly in bands 3 and 4, more sensitively than for bands 1 and 2.

## Context
This work extends the earlier PRM study by Droste (2009) which suggested that higher excited bands (bands 3 and 4) might be chiral partners, by providing detailed angular momentum component and K-distribution analyses. It also connects to the multi-chiral doublet band (MχD) concept proposed by Meng (2006) for ¹⁰⁶Rh, but here the multiple chiral bands arise from the same particle-hole configuration rather than different configurations. The results enable future experimental searches for multiple chiral doublet bands in a single odd-odd nucleus in the A~130 mass region, particularly in nuclei with γ close to 30°.
