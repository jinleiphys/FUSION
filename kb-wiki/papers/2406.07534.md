# On the potential of probing the neutron star composition in accreting X-ray binaries

> Transiently accreting Low Mass X-Ray Binaries have the potential to probe the core composition of their neutron stars via deep crustal heating caused by nuclear reactions. We statistically assess this deep crustal heating scenario, taking into account the various microphysical and astrophysical uncertainties. We find that despite the sizable uncertainties there is the chance to discriminate different compositional scenarios. Several observed sources statistically challenge a minimal hadronic matter composition, where cooling proceeds exclusively via slow modified Urca reactions. Considering here two exemplary extended uniform compositions, namely ultra-dense hadronic matter with direct Urca emission and ungapped quark matter, we find that they are even within uncertainties distinguishable. We show that although exotic forms of matter are generally only expected in an inner core, which could in principle have any size, sufficiently large astrophysical data sets nonetheless have the potential to statistically discriminate compositional scenarios, in particular when further mass measurements become available.

# On the potential of probing the neutron star composition in accreting X-ray binaries

## Key claim

Despite sizable microphysical and astrophysical uncertainties, transiently accreting Low Mass X-Ray Binaries can statistically discriminate between different neutron star core compositions (minimal hadronic matter, ultra-dense hadronic matter with direct Urca emission, and ungapped quark matter), provided sufficiently large astrophysical data sets become available, particularly when further mass measurements are obtained. Several observed sources statistically challenge a minimal hadronic matter composition where cooling proceeds exclusively via slow modified Urca reactions.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic steady-state relation between the observable quiescence thermal luminosity and the average accretion rate for a given neutron star composition, based on energy conservation balancing deep crustal heating against neutrino and photon cooling. They estimate conservative maximum intervals for all microphysical and astrophysical parameters (mass, radius, deep crustal heating energy per baryon, envelope composition, neutrino emissivity pre-factors, and gravitational correction factors) and perform a statistical analysis to compute probabilities for how compatible each compositional scenario is with observed astrophysical data for individual sources.

## Key numbers

- Deep crustal heating energy per baryon: conservative maximum interval 1.2 MeV ≤ Q_B ≤ 2 MeV, with a narrower density-functional-theory range 1.5 MeV ≤ Q_B ≤ 1.7 MeV (Fantina 2018)
- Neutron star mass lower bound: M_min = 1.2 M_⊙, based on the smallest observed neutron star mass (1.174 ± 0.004 M_⊙ for pulsar J0453+1559, Martinez 2015)
- Neutron star mass upper bound: 2.5 M_⊙, based on TOV limits (M_TOV ≲ 2.16 ± 0.17 M_⊙, Rezzolla 2017) and rotational corrections (M_rot ≲ 1.20 ± 0.05 M_TOV)
- Radius range: 10 km to 13.7 km, based on NICER results (12.45 ± 0.65 km for 1.4 M_⊙ star J0030+0451, Miller 2019; 12.35 ± 0.75 km for ≈2.1 M_⊙ star J0740+6620, Miller 2021)
- Modified Urca neutrino emissivity: power-law exponent θ_mU = 8 (slow process)
- Direct Urca (hadronic and quark) neutrino emissivity: power-law exponent θ_dU = θ_qU = 6 (fast process)
- Critical proton fraction for direct Urca opening: between 11% and 15%

## Context

This work extends the deep crustal heating scenario (Brown 1998) and the long-term thermal steady-state framework (Yakovlev 2004, Potekhin 2019) by performing a systematic statistical assessment that accounts for all major uncertainties simultaneously. It builds on prior studies of neutron star cooling (Yakovlev 2000) and envelope composition effects (Gudmundsson 1982, Potekhin 1997). The analysis enables future observational programs, particularly with additional mass measurements from NICER and other instruments, to statistically rule out or confirm exotic compositions such as direct Urca hadronic matter or ungapped quark matter in the cores of accreting neutron stars.
