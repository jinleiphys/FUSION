# Neutron matter from low-momentum interactions

> We present a perturbative calculation of the neutron matter equation of state based on low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions. Our results are compared to the model-independent virial equation of state and to variational calculations, and we provide theoretical error estimates by varying the cutoff used to regulate nuclear interactions. In addition, we study the dependence of the BCS $^1$S$_0$ superfluid pairing gap on nuclear interactions and on the cutoff. The resulting gaps are well constrained by the nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, and the cutoff dependence is very weak for sharp or sufficiently narrow smooth regulators with cutoffs $\lm > 1.6 \fmi$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a perturbative calculation of the neutron matter equation of state and the BCS superfluid pairing gap using low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions, finding that the results are well constrained by nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and that the cutoff dependence is very weak for sharp or sufficiently narrow smooth regulators with cutoffs above 1.6 fm^{-1}.

## Method

The authors performed a perturbative expansion of the free energy around the Hartree-Fock energy using the Kohn-Luttinger-Ward theorem, including first-order two-nucleon and three-nucleon contributions as well as normal and anomalous second-order two-nucleon diagrams. They solved the BCS gap equation in the 1S0 channel using low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from the N3LO chiral potential, and studied the dependence of the gap on the cutoff by employing different smooth exponential regulators and a sharp cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The maximal neutron-neutron BCS gap is approximately 2.9-3.0 MeV for Fermi momenta k_F around 0.8-0.9 fm^{-1}
- The cutoff dependence is very weak for sharp or sufficiently narrow smooth regulators with cutoffs Lambda > 1.6 fm^{-1}
- At lower densities, the cutoff can be lowered further to Lambda > 1.2 k_F
- The energy per particle E/N was calculated for cutoffs Lambda = 1.9 fm^{-1} (lower line) and Lambda = 2.5 fm^{-1} (upper line) at T = 6 MeV
- The inclusion of second-order correlations lowers the energy below variational results for densities rho less than approximately 0.05 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of neutron matter using low-momentum interactions (V_low_k) developed by the authors and collaborators, building on renormalization group methods and chiral effective field theory. The results are compared to the model-independent virial equation of state and to variational calculations by Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), showing good agreement in the low-density high-temperature regime. The finding that the BCS gap is practically independent of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and well constrained by scattering phase shifts enables reliable predictions for neutron star cooling and supernova physics, with theoretical error estimates provided by varying the cutoff.
