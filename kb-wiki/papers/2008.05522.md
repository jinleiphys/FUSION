# Emergent Sp(3,R) dynamical symmetry in the nuclear many-body system from an ab initio description

> Ab initio nuclear theory provides not only a microscopic framework for quantitative description of the nuclear many-body system, but also a foundation for deeper understanding of emergent collective correlations. A symplectic Sp(3,R)$\supset$U(3) dynamical symmetry is identified in ab initio predictions, from a no-core configuration interaction approach, and found to provide a qualitative understanding of the spectrum of 7Be. Low-lying states form an Elliott SU(3) spectrum, while an Sp(3,R) excitation gives rise to an excited rotational band with strong quadrupole connections to the ground state band.

## Key claim

The paper identifies an approximate Sp(3,R)⊃U(3) dynamical symmetry in *ab initio* no-core configuration interaction calculations of ⁷Be, but the symmetry is not exact: the purity of U(3) symmetry degrades away from the yrast line, and the Sp(3,R) symmetry is significantly better preserved than the U(3) symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed symplectic no-core configuration interaction (SpNCCI) calculations for ⁷Be using the Daejeon16 internucleon interaction, in a basis incorporating all Sp(3,R) irreps with lowest-grade irreps up to 6 quanta (Nₛₑₓ ≤ 6) and carrying each up to 6 quanta (Nₓₑₓ ≤ 6), relative to the lowest Pauli-allowed configuration. The resulting space is the center-of-mass free subspace of the Nmax=6 no-core shell model space. Wave function decompositions by U(3) and Sp(3,R) contributions were extracted from the symmetry-adapted basis to identify the dynamical symmetry content of each state.

## Key numbers

- The ground state band members have about 60–70% of their probability (norm) from the ωS = 0(3,0)1/2 U(3) irrep, as illustrated for the ground state.
- For the ground state, the σS = 0(3,0)1/2 Sp(3,R) irrep accounts for over 80% of the probability.
- For the excited band 7/2⁻ member, the largest U(3) contribution comes from ωS = 2(5,0)1/2 at approximately 40% probability.
- The excited band 7/2⁻ member lies approximately 70% within the same σS = 0(3,0)1/2 Sp(3,R) irrep as the ground state band.
- The 9/2⁻ and 11/2⁻ states with strong transitions to both bands have ωS = 2(5,0)1/2 U(3) content at approximately 50–60% probability.
- The calculation used oscillator basis parameter ℏω = 15 MeV with Nmax = 6.

## Context

This work extends previous identifications of dominant U(3) and Sp(3,R) symmetry components in individual *ab initio* wave functions (Dytrych 2007, 2008, 2013; Launey 2016) to show that the symmetry organizes the entire low-lying spectrum as a dynamical symmetry pattern. It builds on the Elliott SU(3) dynamical symmetry framework (Elliott 1958) and the symplectic Sp(3,R) model proposed by Rosensteel and Rowe (1977, 1980), and it provides a microscopic foundation for understanding rotational bands and their quadrupole connections in light nuclei, linking to collective rotation-vibration interpretations in heavier systems.
