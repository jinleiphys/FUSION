# Interfering Doorway States and Giant Resonances. II: Transition Strengths

> The mixing of the doorway components of a giant resonance (GR) due to the interaction via common decay channels influences significantly the distribution of the multipole strength and the energy spectrum of the decay products of the GR. The concept of the partial widths of a GR becomes ambiguous when the mixing is strong. In this case, the partial widths determined in terms of the $K$- and $S$-matrices must be distinguished. The photoemission turns out to be most sensitive to the overlapping of the doorway states. At high excitation energies, the interference between the doorway states leads to a restructuring towards lower energies and apparent quenching of the dipole strength.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when doorway components of a giant resonance (GR) overlap strongly due to interaction via common decay channels, the standard concept of partial widths becomes ambiguous; one must distinguish between partial widths defined from the \(K\)-matrix and those from the \(T\)-matrix, and the photoemission channel is the most sensitive probe of this overlapping.

## Method

The authors extend the doorway-state formalism from Part I to calculate transition strengths into specific decay channels, using a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian that includes both internal (Hermitian) and external (continuum-mediated) interactions. They analytically derive expressions for the \(K\)-matrix and \(T\)-matrix residues, the collective part of the transition strengths, and the photoemission cross section, and they examine the role of the Bell-Steinberger nonorthogonality matrix for overlapping resonances. Numerical calculations in a model without internal damping confirm the analytical predictions.

## Key numbers

- The sum of the half-widths of the two collective doorway states equals \(\langle\gamma\rangle\) (the mean width), independent of the overlapping parameter \(\lambda\) and the mixing angle \(\Theta\): \(\Gamma_{0;1/2}+\Gamma_{1;1/2} = \langle\gamma\rangle\).
- For \(\lambda = 2\), the Bell-Steinberger nonorthogonality factor \(U_{0,1}\) becomes \(1/\sqrt{1-\tan^2\Theta}\) for \(0<\Theta<\pi/4\) and \(1/\sqrt{1-\cot^2\Theta}\) for \(\pi/4<\Theta<\pi/2\), diverging at \(\Theta=\pi/4\).
- The sum rule \(\frac{\pi}{2}\sum_c\sigma^c(E_{dw}) = \cos^2\delta_{dw'}(E_{dw}) < 1\) holds for overlapping resonances, replacing the isolated-resonance value of 1.
- The collective transition strengths \(\sigma_{coll}^c(E)\) vanish at the energy \(E_v = \varepsilon_0 + \sin^2\Theta\,{\bf D}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical and numerical investigation of dipole giant resonances as open quantum systems begun in Part I (Sokolov et al., 1997), where the formation of doorway states with internal and external collectivity was described. It contradicts the naive interpretation of partial widths extracted from Breit-Wigner fits when resonances overlap, and it provides a framework for extracting \(K\)-matrix parameters from experimental transition strengths. The results enable future studies of the apparent quenching of dipole strength at high excitation energies and the role of internal damping from compound states.
