# Nuclear modification of full jets and jet structure in relativistic nuclear collisions

> With our coupled jet-fluid model, we study the nuclear modifications of full jets and jet structures for single inclusive jet and $γ$-jet events in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The evolution of full jet showers is studied via a set of coupled transport equations including the effects of collisional energy loss, transverse momentum broadening and medium-induced splitting. The dynamics of the jet energy and momentum deposited into the medium is described by hydrodynamic equations with source terms. Our detailed analysis indicates that collisional absorption (energy loss) tends to narrow the jet shape function while transverse momentum kicks and medium-induced radiations broaden the jet transverse profile. Also, jet-induced flow plays a significant contribution to jet shape function and dominates at large angles away from the jet axis. The final nuclear modification pattern for the jet shape function is a combined effect from various jet-medium interaction mechanisms. Our detailed studies for single inclusive jets and $γ$-jets for various kinematics indicate that the nuclear modification of jet shape has strong dependence on jet energy and collision energy, and weak dependence on jet flavor (quark or gluon).

## Key claim

The nuclear modification of the jet shape function results from a combined effect of collisional energy loss (which narrows the jet shape), transverse momentum kicks and medium-induced radiations (which broaden the jet transverse profile), and jet-induced flow (which dominates at large angles); the modification pattern has strong dependence on jet energy and collision energy but weak dependence on jet flavor (quark or gluon).

## Method

The authors solve a set of coupled transport differential equations for the three-dimensional momentum distributions of quarks and gluons inside jets, incorporating collisional energy loss, transverse momentum broadening, and medium-induced splitting kernels from the higher-twist formalism. The energy and momentum deposited into the quark-gluon plasma medium are treated via hydrodynamic equations with source terms, and the resulting jet-induced flow is included in the reconstruction of full jets. Initial parton distributions are generated with Pythia, tuned to reproduce the jet shape function in proton-proton collisions, and the transport parameter \(\hat{q}\) is scaled with the local temperature and flow velocity of the medium.

## Key numbers

* Jet \(R_{AA}\) in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV increases when medium response is included, because medium response feeds energy back into the jet cone.
* The distribution of \(X_{J\gamma} \equiv p_T^{\text{jet}} / p_T^{\gamma}\) shifts toward larger values when medium response is included, for \(\gamma\)-jet events at 5.02 TeV.
* For jets with \(p_T > 100\) GeV at 2.76 TeV, the ratio \(\rho(r)^{\text{PbPb}} / \rho(r)^{\text{pp}}\) is a non-monotonic function of \(r\); for jets with \(p_T > 30\) GeV, the ratio increases monotonically with \(r\).
* The nuclear modification of the jet shape function is similar in \(\gamma\)-jet and inclusive jet events at both 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Chang 2016, Tachibana 2017, Chang 2019) on full jet energy loss and jet structure modification in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. It contrasts with earlier jet quenching studies that focused on high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression from leading partons (Qin 2007), which primarily probe total energy loss and cannot discriminate between different jet-medium interaction mechanisms. The results enable future precise measurements to test the role of medium response and to distinguish between competing jet quenching mechanisms.
