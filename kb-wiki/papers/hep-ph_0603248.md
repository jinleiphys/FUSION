# Implications of Holographic QCD in ChPT with Hidden Local Symmetry

> Based on the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with the hidden local symmetry, we propose a methodology to calculate the large $N_c$ corrections in the holographic QCD (HQCD). As an example, we apply the method to an HQCD model recently proposed by Sakai and Sugimoto. We show that the $ρ$-$π$-$π$ coupling becomes in good agreement with the experiment due to the $1/N_c$-subleading corrections.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to calculate a part of the 1/N_c corrections to holographic QCD (HQCD) predictions through meson loops, using chiral perturbation theory with hidden local symmetry (HLS ChPT). The authors show that the 1/N_c-subleading corrections improve the HQCD prediction for the ratio m_rho^2/(g_{rho pi pi}^2 F_pi^2) from about 3.0 to approximately 2.0, in good agreement with the experimental value of 1.96, but only when the matching scale and input parameters are chosen appropriately.

## Method

The authors start from the 5-dimensional gauge action of the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model and expand the 5-dimensional gauge field in terms of HLS gauge fields, integrating out heavier vector and axialvector meson modes to retain only the lightest vector meson (rho) and pions. They then treat the resulting HLS Lagrangian as a bare theory defined at a matching scale Lambda, and incorporate 1/N_c corrections by running the parameters (F_pi, a, g, z_4) via renormalization group equations (RGEs) that include quadratic divergences in the Wilsonian sense, from the matching scale down to the rho mass and then to zero momentum.

## Key numbers

* The bare HQCD prediction for the KSRF II ratio is m_rho^2/(g_{rho pi pi}^2 F_pi^2) ≈ 3.0, which is roughly 50% larger than the KSRF II relation value of 2.
* The experimental value of the ratio is m_rho^2/(g_{rho pi pi}^2 F_pi^2)|_exp = 1.96, using F_pi = 92.4 MeV, m_rho = 775.8 MeV, and g_{rho pi pi} = 5.99.
* In the chiral limit, the ratio is m_rho^2/(g_{rho pi pi}^2 F_pi^2(0))|_chi = 2.24, using F_pi(0) = 86.4 MeV.
* After including 1/N_c corrections with matching scale Lambda = 1.1 GeV, the predicted ratio becomes m_rho^2/(g_{rho pi pi}^2 F_pi^2(0)) ≈ 2.0, with a(0) ≈ 2.0 and (1/2) g^2(m_rho) z_4(m_rho) ≈ -8.0 × 10^{-3}.
* For Lambda = 1.0 GeV, the predicted ratio is 1.98 ± 1.01; for Lambda = 1.1 GeV, 2.01 ± 1.02; for Lambda = 1.2 GeV, 2.04 ± 1.04 (errors from F_pi(0) input).
* The predicted g_{rho pi pi} values are 6.38 ± 1.46 (Lambda = 1.0 GeV), 6.34 ± 1.45 (Lambda = 1.1 GeV), and 6.28 ± 1.44 (Lambda = 1.2 GeV), compared to the experimental value 5.99 ± 0.03.

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD model of Sakai and Sugimoto (2005) by incorporating subleading 1/N_c corrections that were absent in the original large N_c limit treatment. The method builds on the HLS chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Harada, Tanabashi, and Yamawaki (1990s), which systematically includes vector meson loops. The results enable future calculations of other observables such as the rho-gamma mixing strength (KSRF I relation) and the pion electromagnetic form factor, which the authors note will be addressed in a forthcoming paper.
