# The Relative Abundance of Correlated Spin-zero Nucleon Pairs

> We utilize the generalized contact formalism in conjunction with the Woods-Saxon mean-field description of the long-range part of the nuclear wave function to assess the relative prevalence of short-range correlation pairs within atomic nuclei. We validate our approach by fitting experimental charge density results and electron scattering experiments to a very good agreement. Applying our model, we calculate the spin-zero short-range correlations contact ratios. Interestingly, for nuclei with $A>50$, we observe a notable dependence on the neutron-to-proton ratio $N/Z$. Specifically, the probability per nucleon to find neutron-neutron pairs increases, while that of proton-proton pairs decreases, whereas the probability of finding neutron-proton pairs remains relatively constant. To interpret this isospin symmetry breaking effect, we employ a simple model based on generalized Levinger constants, linking it to differences in nuclear proton and neutron radii.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for nuclei with A > 50, the probability per nucleon to find spin-zero short-range correlated neutron-neutron pairs increases with neutron-to-proton ratio N/Z, while that of proton-proton pairs decreases, and neutron-proton pairs remain relatively constant; this isospin symmetry breaking is attributed to differences in proton and neutron radii rather than the Coulomb force.

## Method

The authors employ the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) with a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential to compute nuclear contact coefficients for spin-zero s-wave nucleon pairs (pp, nn, np). They validate their mean-field approach by fitting to experimental charge density data and inclusive electron scattering cross-section ratios (a2 ratios). The contact ratios are then calculated across a range of nuclei and interpreted using a generalized Levinger model that relates contacts to nuclear densities and radii.

## Key numbers

- Normalization factor η = 0.44 used to fit Woods-Saxon contact calculations to charge density data.
- Linear fit parameter β ≈ 0.63 for the relations C⁰_nn/C⁰_np ≈ [1 + β(N/Z - 1)] and C⁰_pp/C⁰_np ≈ [1 + β(Z/N - 1)] for nuclei with A > 50.
- The isospin symmetry breaking becomes notable for nuclei with A > 50, while for A < 50 the three spin-zero contacts are equal for N=Z nuclei.
- Comparison of ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca (both Z=20) shows that neutron excess in the Woods-Saxon potential, not the Coulomb force, breaks isospin symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the Generalized Contact Formalism previously applied by Weiss et al. (2015, 2018, 2019) and Torres et al. (2021) to heavier nuclei using a mean-field approximation. It builds on experimental findings by Subedi et al. (2008) and Hen et al. (2017) that np SRC pairs are nearly 20 times more prevalent than pp or nn pairs, and by Duer et al. (2019) showing linear scaling with minority population. The results enable theoretical predictions for spin-zero SRC abundances that can be tested by future experiments, and provide a simple Levinger-type model connecting contact ratios to nuclear radii differences.
