# Reply to the comment on $Δ$I=4 bifurcation in ground rotational bands of even-even nuclei and the interacting boson model

> This is the reply to the comment of Kuyucak and Stuchbery on our paper published in Phys. Rev. Lett. 79 (1997) 2006.

# Reply to the Comment on ΔI=4 Bifurcation in Ground Rotational Bands of Even-Even Nuclei and the Interacting Boson Model

## Key claim
The authors defend their earlier claim that the ΔI=4 bifurcation (staggering) pattern in ground rotational bands can arise within the simplest version of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-1), but they acknowledge that definitive experimental evidence for the phenomenon requires more precise measurements.

## Method
The authors performed full diagonalization calculations using the PHINT computer code for the IBM-1 Hamiltonian, varying the boson number N from 4 to 14. They computed the energies of the ground, beta, and gamma bands, and compared the resulting Rβ and Rγ ratios to experimental systematics. They also examined the dependence of the staggering amplitude on the parameter χ in the E2 transition operator.

## Key numbers
- Rβ values range from 0.76 to 1.53 for boson number N changed from 4 to 14.
- Rγ values range from 1.12 to 1.98 for boson number N changed from 4 to 14.
- The SU(3) limit gives Rβ and Rγ approximately 2.5, which is much worse than the authors' results.
- The amount of staggering from exact diagonalization is 20 keV, compared to 0.5 keV from experimental data when using χ ~ 3.2.
- To obtain a small staggering value, χ should be approximately 1.8.

## Context
This reply addresses the comment by Kuyucak and Stuchbery (1999) on the authors' earlier work (Toki and Wu, 1997). The authors argue that their Hamiltonian, unlike the quadrupole Hamiltonian used by Kuyucak and Stuchbery, produces good agreement with experimental systematics for both ground and excited bands. The work extends the discussion of ΔI=4 bifurcation in nuclear rotational bands and highlights the importance of exact diagonalization over 1/N expansion approximations, as used by Kuyucak and Stuchbery. The authors note that large β values (β > √2) have been used previously by Ginzburg, Warner, and Iachello.
