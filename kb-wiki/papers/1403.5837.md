# Description of collective and quasiparticle excitations in deformed actinide nuclei: The first application of the Heavy Shell Model

> The Heavy Shell Model (HSM) (Y. Sun and C.-L. Wu, Phys. Rev. C 68, 024315 (2003)) was proposed to take the advantages of two existing models, the projected shell model (PSM) and the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (FDSM). To construct HSM, one extends the PSM by adding collective D-pairs into the intrinsic basis. The HSM is expected to describe simultaneously low-lying collective and quasi-particle excitations in deformed nuclei, and still keeps the model space tractable even for the heaviest systems. As the first numerical realization of the HSM, we study systematically the band structures for some deformed actinide nuclei, with a model space including up to 4-quasiparticle and 1-D-pair configurations. The calculated energy levels for the ground- state bands, the collective bands such as β - and γ -bands, and some quasiparticle bands agree well with known experimental data. Some low-lying quasiparticle bands are predicted, awaiting experimental confirmation.

## Key claim

The Heavy Shell Model (HSM), which extends the Projected Shell Model by adding collective D-pairs into the intrinsic basis, can simultaneously describe low-lying collective and quasiparticle excitations in deformed actinide nuclei, but the calculated inter-band B(E2) transition probabilities are systematically smaller than experimental data, suggesting that realistic nuclei have stiffer potentials in both the beta and gamma directions than those assumed in the HSM.

## Method

The HSM constructs D0- and D2-pairs as linear combinations of 2-quasiparticle states with Kπ=0+ and Kπ=2+ respectively, with structure amplitudes obtained from diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the 2-qp basis. The model space includes up to 4-quasiparticle and 1-D-pair configurations, using angular-momentum projection on the intrinsic basis. The effective Hamiltonian contains spherical single-particle terms, quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing interactions, with deformation parameters fixed for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters ε2 range from 0.212 (²³⁰Th) to 0.260 (²⁴⁰Pu); hexadecapole parameters ε4 range from 0.012 (²³²U) to 0.040 (²⁴⁰Pu)
- Monopole-pairing strengths: Gⁿ_M = (19.3 - 0.08(N-Z))/A for neutrons, Gᵖ_M = (13.3 + 0.217(N-Z))/A for protons, multiplied by 0.87 in the calculation
- Quadrupole-pairing strength ratio G_Q/G_M: 0.14 for ²³⁰,²³²Th, 0.13 for ²³²,²³⁴,²³⁶U, and 0.12 for ²⁴⁰Pu
- For ²³⁰Th, the calculated B(E2) from 2⁺_g to 0⁺_g is 202.0 W.u. (experimental: 196(6) W.u.); from 2⁺_β to 0⁺_g is 0.37 W.u. (experimental: 2.7(9) W.u.)
- For ²³⁴U, the calculated β-band is at 740 keV (experimental: 810 keV) and γ-band at 1012 keV (experimental: 927 keV)
- For ²⁴⁰Pu, the calculated Kπ=3⁺ band at 1094 keV (experimental: 1031 keV) and Kπ=0⁺ band at 1029 keV (experimental: 1089 keV)

## Context

This work extends the Projected Shell Model (PSM) of Hara and Sun (1995) and the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (FDSM) of Wu et al. (1994) by combining their advantages into a unified framework. The HSM was proposed by Sun and Wu (2003) and preliminary tests for β-bands in ²³⁰,²³²Th and ²³²,²³⁴U were reported by Cui and Zhou. This first systematic application to six actinide nuclei (²³⁰,²³²Th, ²³²,²³⁴,²³⁶U, ²⁴⁰Pu) enables future studies of 2-phonon excitations (ββ, γβ, γγ bands) and predictions of low-lying quasiparticle bands awaiting experimental confirmation.
