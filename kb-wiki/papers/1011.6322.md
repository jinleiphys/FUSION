# Meson Assisted Strange Dibaryons

> The state of the art in dibaryons with strangeness is reviewed, including the K^{-}pp dibaryon which signals the onset of antikaon-nuclear binding. A new type of strange dibaryons is highlighted, where the primary binding mechanism is provided by strong p-wave pion interactions, as demonstrated by a quasibound (I=3/2,JP=2+) pion-hyperon-nucleon dibaryon calculation.

# Meson Assisted Strange Dibaryons

## Key claim

The paper reviews the state of the art in strange dibaryons and highlights a new class of meson-assisted dibaryons where the primary binding mechanism is provided by strong p-wave pion interactions, as demonstrated by a quasibound (I=3/2, JP=2+) πΛN dibaryon calculation; however, the existence of such a state depends sensitively on the poorly constrained πY form factor, and the calculation leaves room for a quasibound state but does not definitively establish one.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical and experimental work on dibaryons with strangeness, covering six-quark configurations from quark models, deuteron-like baryon-baryon candidates from meson-exchange and chiral effective field theory, and meson-assisted dibaryons. For the πΛN system, three-body Faddeev equations were solved with coupled-channel YN interactions from a chiral quark model and separable p-wave πB interactions fitted to the Δ(1232) and Σ(1385) resonances, with the πY form factor parameters varied to assess sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- For the πΛN quasibound state calculation, binding energies range from 6 to 104 MeV depending on the πY form factor and YN interaction model, with the πY r.m.s. momentum varying from 3.48 to 3.91 fm^-1
- The πN form factor for Δ(1232) has parameters A=0.2 fm^2, β=1.31 fm^-1, α=3.21 fm^-1, yielding an r.m.s. momentum of 5.55 fm^-1 (1095 MeV/c) and an r.m.s. radius of 0.875 fm
- For the K^-pp system, calculated binding energies range from 9-16 MeV (two-pole chiral models) to 48-80 MeV (single-pole models), with mesonic widths of 34-110 MeV and nonmesonic widths of 4-20 MeV
- A resonance structure at M_R ≈ 2.37 GeV with Γ_R ≈ 70 MeV from np → dππ reactions at CELSIUS-WASA is noted as a possible ΔΔ dibaryon candidate
- The H dibaryon (S=-2, I=0, JP=0+) has a predicted CM interaction gain of -2 M_0 relative to the ΛΛ threshold, with M_0 ∼ 75 MeV from the Δ-N mass difference

## Context

This review extends the long-standing search for dibaryons following Jaffe's (1977) prediction of the H dibaryon and Goldman et al. (1987) predictions for S=-3 dibaryons, which have not been confirmed experimentally. The work contrasts with quark cluster calculations by Oka et al. (1983) and Oka (1988) that found no stable six-quark dibaryons, and with meson-exchange predictions from the Nijmegen NSC97 model (Rijken, 1999) and chiral EFT (Haidenbauer, 2010) that find deuteron-like bound states for S=-3,-4. The new pion-assisted dibaryon mechanism enables searches for strange dibaryons beyond the traditional six-quark and baryon-baryon approaches, with experimental prospects at J-PARC and GSI for K^-pp and possible reactions such as K^- + d → D^- + π^+ for the πΛN system.
