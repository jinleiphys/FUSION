# Debye screening mass of hot Yang-Mills theory to three-loop order

> Building upon our earlier work, we compute a Debye mass of finite-temperature Yang-Mills theory to three-loop order. As an application, we determine a $g^7$ contribution to the thermodynamic pressure of hot QCD.

## Key claim

The authors compute the Debye screening mass of hot Yang-Mills theory to three-loop order (NNLO), but the result is restricted to the pure gauge case ($N_f=0$) and depends on two independent renormalization scales, $\bar\mu$ and $\mu_3$, whose optimal values must be chosen via a minimal-sensitivity procedure.

## Method

The calculation proceeds by matching the full four-dimensional QCD to the three-dimensional effective theory Electrostatic QCD (EQCD), requiring that the pole of the static $A_0$ propagator coincide in both theories. The authors evaluate approximately 500 three-loop Feynman diagrams for the gluon self-energy, reduce the resulting $\approx 10^7$ sum-integrals via integration-by-parts (IBP) relations to a small set of master sum-integrals, and perform a basis transformation to eliminate singular prefactors in the dimensional regularization parameter $\epsilon$ (with $d=3-2\epsilon$). The three non-trivial three-loop master sum-integrals $\intV$, $\intVb$, and $\intMcb$ are evaluated using the Arnold-Zhai subtraction technique and Tarasov's dimensional method.

## Key numbers

* The renormalized Debye mass squared to three-loop order (pure gauge, $N_c$ colors) is:
  $$\frac{m_{\mathrm{ER}}^2}{(4\pi T)^2} = \frac{N_c}{3} g_{\mathrm{R}}^2/(4\pi)^2 + \left(\frac{N_c}{3} g_{\mathrm{R}}^2/(4\pi)^2\right)^2 (22L + 5) + \left(\frac{N_c}{3} g_{\mathrm{R}}^2/(4\pi)^2\right)^3 \left(484L^2 + 424L - 180L_3 + \frac{731}{2} - \frac{56\zeta(3)}{5} - 3\times\frac{34\zeta(3)}{15}\right) + \mathcal{O}(g_{\mathrm{R}}^8)$$
  where $L = \ln(\bar\mu e^{\gamma_{\mathrm{E}}}/4\pi T)$ and $L_3 = \ln(\mu_3^2 e^{Z_1}/4\pi T^2)$ with $Z_1 = \zeta'(-1)/\zeta(-1)$.

* The optimal 4d scale is $\bar\mu_{\mathrm{opt}}/T \approx 2\pi$; the optimal 3d scale is $\mu_{3,\mathrm{opt}}/T \approx 2.85$.

* The $g^7$ contribution to the QCD pressure from the Debye mass is:
  $$p_{\mathrm{M}}(T)|_{m_{\mathrm{E}}^3,g^7} = 8\pi^2 T^4 (N_c^2-1) \left(\frac{N_c}{3} g_{\mathrm{R}}^2/(4\pi)^2\right)^{7/2} \left(605L^2 + 479L - 180L_3 + \frac{1487}{4} - 18\zeta(3)\right)$$

## Context

This work extends the two-loop Debye mass calculation of Laine (2005) and the earlier three-loop matching effort of Moeller and Schroder (2012) by completing the evaluation of all three-loop master sum-integrals. The result provides a new matching coefficient for Electrostatic QCD (EQCD) and enables the determination of a $g^7$ contribution to the thermodynamic pressure of hot QCD, representing the next-to-leading correction beyond the physical leading order (all terms up to $g^6$). The calculation demonstrates that a three-loop determination of the effective gauge coupling $g_{\mathrm{E}}^2$ is within reach, which would allow precise comparison with lattice determinations of the spatial string tension.
