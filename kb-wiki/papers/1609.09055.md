# Skyrme model study of proton and neutron properties in a strong magnetic field

> The proton and neutron properties in a uniform magnetic field are investigated. The Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula is shown to be satisfied for baryon states. It is found that with increasing magnetic field strength, the proton mass first decreases and then increases, while the neutron mass always increases. The ratio between magnetic moment of proton and neutron increases with the increase of the magnetic field strength. With increasing magnetic field strength, the size of proton first increases and then decreases, while the size of neutron always decreases. The present analyse implies that in the core part of the magnetar, the equation of state depend on the magnetic field, which modifies the mass limit of the magnetar.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in the Skyrme model, the proton mass first decreases and then increases with magnetic field strength, while the neutron mass always increases; these results depend on the semi-classical quantization at order O(N_C^{-1}), which separates the nucleon states and introduces different magnetic responses for proton and neutron.

## Method
The Skyrme model action with pion mass term and Wess-Zumino-Witten term is used, with covariant derivatives coupling to external magnetic fields in the symmetric gauge. Semi-classical quantization is performed by introducing time-dependent isospin and spatial rotations, and the Hamiltonian is computed up to O(N_C^{-1}) order. The equations of motion for proton and neutron are obtained from expectation values of the action with nucleon wave functions, and the parameters c_rho and c_z are fixed to minimize the mass for each magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers
- Proton mass decreases by about 23 MeV at |eB| ~ 2.4 m_pi^2 compared to vacuum
- At |eB| ~ 0, proton magnetic moment is 1.94 [mu_N] and neutron magnetic moment is -1.21 [mu_N]
- Ratio mu_p/mu_n is about -1.60 at |eB| ~ 0 and 0.61 at |eB| ~ 28 m_pi^2
- At |eB| ~ 0, proton RMS electric charge radius is about 0.865 [fm] (experimental: 0.84-0.87 fm)
- At |eB| ~ 0, neutron MS electric charge radius is about -0.278 [fm^2] (experimental: -0.116 fm^2)
- At |eB| ~ 10^{-2} [GeV^2] (magnetar core), proton density decreases about 3.4% and neutron density increases about 15.3% compared to vacuum
- Proton magnetic moment flips sign at |eB| ~ 0.062 [GeV^2]

## Context
This work extends the leading-order (O(N_C)) Skyrmion study in a magnetic field by He (2015) to include O(N_C^{-1}) effects via semi-classical quantization following Adkins (1983). The results are relevant for understanding nucleon properties in magnetar cores, where the magnetic field modifies the equation of state and mass limit. The paper notes that the vacuum magnetic moments are about 30% (proton) and 36% (neutron) smaller than experimental results, and the neutron MS charge radius is about 1.39 times larger than experiment, suggesting that inclusion of vector and scalar mesons (Meissner 1986, He 2015) could improve agreement.
