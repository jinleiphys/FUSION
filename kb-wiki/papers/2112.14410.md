# Evolution of $Λ$ polarization in the hadronic phase of heavy-ion collisions

> Using the AMPT + MUSIC+UrQMD hybrid model, we study the global and local spin polarizations of $Λ$ hyperons as functions of the freeze-out temperature of the spin degree of freedom in the hadronic phase of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6$ GeV. Including contributions from both the thermal vorticity and thermal shear of the hadronic matter, we find that, with the spin freeze-out temperature dropping from the hadronization temperature of 160 MeV to 110 MeV at the kinetic freeze-out, both the global and local spin polarizations of $Λ$ hyperons due to the thermal vorticity decrease by a factor of two, while those due to the thermal shear decrease quickly and become negligibly small at 140 MeV. Our results suggest the importance of understanding the dynamical evolution of the spin degree of freedom in the hadronic stage in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# Evolution of Λ polarization in the hadronic phase of heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The global and local spin polarizations of Λ hyperons due to thermal vorticity decrease by a factor of two as the spin freeze-out temperature drops from 160 MeV to 110 MeV, while those due to thermal shear become negligibly small already at 140 MeV, but this result assumes the spin degree of freedom remains in thermal equilibrium throughout the hadronic phase, which may not be physically realized.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid model combining AMPT initial conditions, the MUSIC hydrodynamic model for the quark-gluon plasma phase, and the UrQMD hadronic transport model for the hadronic phase of Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 19.6 GeV. They calculated the Λ spin polarization from both thermal vorticity and thermal shear contributions using a coarse-grained method to extract local temperature and flow fields from the UrQMD output, then evaluated the polarization as a function of the assumed spin freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers
- Global Λ polarization from thermal vorticity at T_s = 160 MeV: 5.3 × 10⁻³
- Global Λ polarization from thermal vorticity at T_s = 110 MeV (kinetic freeze-out): 2.5 × 10⁻³
- Global Λ polarization from thermal shear at T_s = 160 MeV: 2.9 × 10⁻³
- Thermal shear contribution becomes negligible at T_s = 140 MeV
- Hadronization temperature assumed: 160 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature assumed: 110 MeV
- Collision system: Au+Au at √sNN = 19.6 GeV, 20-60% centrality
- Λ hyperon selection: |η| < 1, pT > 0.15 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous hydrodynamic and transport studies of Λ polarization (STAR Collaboration, 2017; Becattini et al., 2015; Karpenko et al., 2017) by explicitly modeling the hadronic phase evolution of spin polarization, which most prior studies neglected by calculating polarization only at hadronization. The results highlight the need for a hadronic transport model that includes spin degrees of freedom, as called for in works by Xu et al. (2012) and Xia et al. (2014) on nucleon spin transport at lower energies. The study also connects to the "strange memory" scenario of Ref. PhysRevLett.127.142301 and the "isothermal local equilibrium" scenario of Becattini et al. (2021), which address the discrepancy between calculated and measured local spin polarization patterns.
