# Transverse Momentum Spectra and Nuclear Modification Factor using Boltzmann Transport Equation with Flow in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> In the continuation of our previous work, the transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra and nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) are derived using relaxation time approximation of Boltzmann Transport Equation (BTE). The initial $p_T$-distribution used to describe $p+p$ collisions has been studied with the pQCD inspired power-law distribution, the Hagedorn's empirical formula and with the Tsallis non-extensive statistical distribution. The non-extensive Tsallis distribution is observed to describe the complete range of the transverse momentum spectra. The Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) distribution is used as the equilibrium distribution in the present formalism, to describe the $p_T$-distribution and nuclear modification factor in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The experimental data for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV at the Large Hadron Collider at CERN have been analyzed for pions, kaons, protons, $K^{*0}$ and $ϕ$. It is observed that the present formalism while explaining the transverse momentum spectra upto 5 GeV/c, explains the nuclear modification factor very well upto 8 GeV/c in $p_T$ for all these particles except for protons. $R_{AA}$ is found to be independent of the degree of non-extensivity, $q_{pp}$ after $p_T \sim$ 8 GeV/c.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Boltzmann Transport Equation solved in the Relaxation Time Approximation, using a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution as the initial condition and a Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) distribution as the equilibrium distribution, can simultaneously describe both the transverse momentum spectra (up to 5 GeV/c) and the nuclear modification factor (up to 8 GeV/c) for pions, kaons, $K^{*0}$, and $\phi$ mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions, but the formalism fails for protons above 5 GeV/c due to an enhancement in the proton yield after $p_T \sim$ 3 GeV/c that is not seen for other particles.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann Transport Equation in the Relaxation Time Approximation, assuming spatial homogeneity and no external forces, to obtain the final particle distribution $f_{fin} = f_{eq} + (f_{in} - f_{eq}) e^{-t_f/\tau}$. The initial distribution $f_{in}$ is taken as the thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution (characterized by parameters $q_{pp}$ and $T_{pp}$ extracted from $p+p$ collisions), and the equilibrium distribution $f_{eq}$ is the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave function which incorporates radial collective flow. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA} = f_{fin}/f_{in}$ is then fitted to experimental data for pions, kaons, protons, $K^{*0}$, and $\phi$ in most central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV using $\chi^2$ minimization with the TMinuit class in ROOT, with the equilibrium temperature fixed to 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Transverse momentum spectra are described up to $p_T$ = 5 GeV/c for all particles studied, except for protons and pions where the fit quality degrades after $p_T$ = 3 GeV/c.
- Nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ is described up to $p_T$ = 8 GeV/c for pions, kaons, $K^{*0}$, and $\phi$, but only up to $p_T$ = 5 GeV/c for protons.
- $R_{AA}$ becomes independent of the non-extensivity parameter $q_{pp}$ after $p_T \sim$ 8 GeV/c.
- For the pion $R_{AA}$ variation study, fixed parameters include $m$ = 0.139 GeV/$c^{2}$, $T$ = 0.16 GeV, $T_{pp}$ = 0.108 GeV, $t_f/\tau$ = 2.06, and $<\beta_r>$ = 0.501.
- The equilibrium temperature $T$ is fixed to 160 MeV throughout the analysis.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism (Tripathy et al., 2016) which used the Boltzmann-Gibbs distribution as the equilibrium distribution; the present work replaces that with the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave distribution to incorporate radial collective flow, which improves the description at low $p_T$. The approach contrasts with pQCD-inspired power-law and Hagedorn distributions for the initial state, finding that the Tsallis non-extensive distribution provides the best description across the full $p_T$ range. The extracted radial flow shows mass dependence consistent with hydrodynamic behavior, though $K^{*0}$ and protons deviate from this trend. The results enable further studies of the interplay between radial flow, relaxation time, and non-extensivity in heavy-ion collisions, and suggest that a microscopic understanding of the parameter correlations is needed.
