# Global $Λ$ polarization in heavy-ion collisions from a transport model

> The polarizations of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons are important quantities in extracting the fluid vorticity of the strongly coupled quark gluon plasma and the magnitude of the magnetic field created in off-central heavy-ion collisions, through the spin-vorticity and spin-magnetic coupling. We computed the energy dependence of the global $Λ$ polarization in off-central Au+Au collisions in the energy range $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7-200$ GeV using a multiphase transport model. The observed polarizations with two different impact parameters agree quantitatively with recent STAR measurements. The energy dependence of the global $Λ$ polarization is decomposed as energy dependence of the $Λ$ distribution at hadronization and the space-time distribution of the fluid-vorticity field. The visualization of both the $Λ$ distribution and the fluid-vorticity field show a smaller tilt at higher collisional energies, which indicates that the smaller global polarization at higher collisional energies is caused by a smaller angular momentum deposition at midrapidity.

# Global $\Lambda$ polarization in heavy-ion collisions from a transport model

## Key claim
The global $\Lambda$ polarization in off-central Au+Au collisions computed using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model agrees quantitatively with STAR measurements, but this agreement requires including a 17% suppression from feed-down $\Lambda$ hyperons from resonance decays; the energy dependence is explained by the joint effect of the $\Lambda$ space-time distribution at hadronization and the thermal vorticity field, not simply by the total angular momentum of the system.

## Method
The string-melting version of the AMPT model was used to generate events for Au+Au collisions at eight beam energy scan (BES) energies from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ to 200 GeV, with fixed impact parameters of 7 and 9 fm. The parton stage was coarse-grained onto a space-time grid (30 time steps of 1 fm/c, 41x41 cells in the transverse plane with 0.5 fm spacing, 21 cells in rapidity with 0.5 unit spacing) to compute the energy-momentum tensor, from which the four-velocity and temperature fields were extracted; the thermal vorticity tensor was then calculated using finite differences. The spin vector of each $\Lambda$ hyperon produced at hadronization was computed from the local thermal vorticity via the spin-vorticity coupling formula, and the global polarization was obtained by averaging over approximately $2\times10^6$ $\Lambda$ hyperons per energy and impact parameter, projected onto the direction of the global orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- Global $\Lambda$ polarization increases from about 0.2% at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV to about 2.1% at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV (with feed-down correction), agreeing with STAR measurements within error bars.
- The global polarization at $b=9$ fm is larger than at $b=7$ fm for a given energy.
- The suppression factor from feed-down $\Lambda$ hyperons (from resonance decays such as $\Sigma(1385)\rightarrow\Lambda+\pi$ and $\Sigma^0\rightarrow\Lambda+\gamma$) is estimated to be 17%, compared to 15% in Karpenko (2016) and 20% in Becattini (2016).
- The thermal vorticity $\varpi_{zx}$ has the same magnitude at 7.7 and 200 GeV, and its decay rate with time is not sensitive to collisional energy.
- The orbital angular momenta in off-central collisions are of order $10^3-10^5\hbar$.

## Context
This work extends previous hydrodynamic calculations of global $\Lambda$ polarization by Becattini (2013), Karpenko (2016), and Xie (2016, 2017) by using a transport model (AMPT) rather than a hydrodynamic approach, allowing direct computation of the vorticity field from particle distributions. It also builds on earlier transport studies of fluid vorticity by Jiang (2016) and Deng (2016). The results enable a detailed understanding of why the global polarization decreases with increasing collision energy despite the total angular momentum increasing: the angular momentum deposited at midrapidity is smaller at higher energies due to a more symmetric $\Lambda$ distribution and vorticity field in that region. The work also provides a framework for extracting the fluid vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma and the magnetic field created in off-central collisions from experimental polarization measurements.
