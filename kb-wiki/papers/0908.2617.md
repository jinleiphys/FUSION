# Viscosity from elliptic flow: the path to precision

> Using viscous relativistic hydrodynamics we show that systematic studies of the impact parameter dependence of the eccentricity scaled elliptic flow can distinguish between different models for the calculation of the initial source eccentricity. This removes the largest present uncertainty in the extraction of the specific viscosity of the matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from precise elliptic flow measurements.

## Key claim

By exploiting the impact parameter dependence of the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow \(v_2/\varepsilon\), one can experimentally distinguish between the Glauber and fKLN models for the initial fireball eccentricity, thereby removing the largest present uncertainty in the extraction of the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) from elliptic flow measurements. The authors show that the swapped ratios \(v_2^\mathrm{fKLN}/\varepsilon_\mathrm{G}\) and \(v_2^\mathrm{G}/\varepsilon_\mathrm{fKLN}\) have qualitatively different shapes as a function of impact parameter, which allows for a binary decision between the two initialization models.

## Method

The authors performed viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of 200 \(A\) GeV Au+Au collisions using two different equations of state (EOS I, a massless parton gas with \(e=3p\), and SM-EOS Q, which includes a first-order quark-hadron phase transition) and two different initial state models (Glauber and fKLN). They computed the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow \(v_2/\varepsilon\) as a function of impact parameter \(b\) for various constant values of \(\eta/s\), and also computed the "swapped ratios" where the elliptic flow from one initial state model is divided by the eccentricity from the other model.

## Key numbers

- The minimum bias eccentricities for the fKLN and Glauber models are \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{mb}^\mathrm{fKLN} = 0.197\) and \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{mb}^\mathrm{G} = 0.174\), a difference of 12%.
- The fKLN eccentricities are 20-30% larger than the Glauber ones overall; at small impact parameter \(b\) the fKLN eccentricity is more than 60% larger, while at \(b > 8\) fm the excess drops to below 20%.
- The uncertainty in \(\eta/s\) from the 12% uncertainty in \(v_2/\varepsilon\) is a factor of about 2 when \(\eta/s\) is of order \(1/4\pi \approx 0.08\).
- The KSS bound for the specific shear viscosity is \((\eta/s)_\mathrm{KSS} = 1/4\pi \approx 0.08\).
- Previous work (Luzum and Romatschke, 2008) gave an upper limit of \(\eta/s < 0.5\) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200 A\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Luzum and Romatschke (2008), who found that the uncertainty in the initial fireball eccentricity led to variations by more than a factor of 2 in the extracted value of \(\eta/s\). The authors show that by studying the centrality dependence of \(v_2/\varepsilon\), one can distinguish between the Glauber model (Hirano, 2005) and the fKLN model (Drescher, 2006) for the initial eccentricity, thereby enabling a precise extraction of \(\eta/s\). The paper also notes that a temperature-dependent \(\eta/s\) that increases dramatically with impact parameter could mimic the experimental observation that \(v_2/\varepsilon\) falls monotonically from central to peripheral collisions, and calls for explicit calculations with realistic temperature-dependent \(\eta/s\) and bulk viscosity to rule out this possibility.
