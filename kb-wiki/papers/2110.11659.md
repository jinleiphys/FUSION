# Static quark anti-quark interactions at non-zero temperature from lattice QCD

> We study the interactions of a static quark antiquark pair at non-zero temperature using realistic 2+1 flavor lattice QCD calculations. The study consists of two parts: the first investigates the properties of Wilson line correlators in Coulomb gauge and compares to predictions of hard-thermal loop perturbation theory. As a second step we extract the spectral functions underlying the correlators using four conceptually different methods: spectral function fits, a HTL inspired fit for the correlation function, Padé rational approximation and the Bayesian BR spectral reconstruction. We find that our high statistics Euclidean lattice data are amenable to different hypotheses for the shapes of the spectral function and we compare the implications of each analysis method for the existence and properties of a well defined ground state spectral peak.

# Static quark anti-quark interactions at non-zero temperature from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The study finds that high-statistics Euclidean lattice data for static quark-antiquark correlators at non-zero temperature are amenable to different hypotheses for the shapes of the spectral function, meaning that the existence and properties of a well-defined ground state spectral peak depend on which of four conceptually different analysis methods is used.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 flavor lattice QCD calculations with realistic pion masses using the HISQ action, computing Wilson line correlators in Coulomb gauge at non-zero temperature. They first investigated the lowest three cumulants of the correlation function and compared them to predictions from hard-thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory. Subsequently, they extracted the underlying spectral functions using four distinct methods: spectral function fits with model functions, an HTL-inspired fit for the correlation function, Pade rational approximation, and the Bayesian BR spectral reconstruction method.

## Key numbers

- The lattice calculations use 2+1 flavors of quarks with realistic pion masses (the paper does not specify the exact pion mass value in the provided text)
- The most recent lattice analysis of the static Q-Qbar free energy shows interactions are screened beyond distances larger than 0.4/T (Bazavov 2018)
- The study compares correlation functions to predictions from hard-thermal loop perturbation theory at leading order

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the static in-medium potential that used the Bayesian BR method in quenched QCD (Rothkopf 2011, Burnier 2016) and in full QCD with the legacy asqtad action (Burnier 2014, Burnier 2015). It also builds on HTL-motivated decomposition of APE smeared Wilson loops in the quenched approximation (Bala 2019) and on potential extraction via modified HTL spectral function fits (Bazavov 2014) and skewed Lorentzian fits (Petreczky 2017). The study enables future non-perturbative determination of whether a potential picture can be established for static quark-antiquark pairs at temperatures just above the QCD crossover transition, which is relevant for connecting effective field theory and open quantum systems approaches to heavy quarkonium in heavy-ion collisions.
