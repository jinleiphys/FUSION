# Holography of the Conformal Window

> Inspired by the model of Jarvinen and Kiritsis, we present a simple holographic model for the on set of chiral symmetry breaking at the edge of the conformal window in QCD in the Veneziano limit. Our most naive model enforces the QCD two loop running coupling on a D3/D7 holographic brane system. The mass of the holographic field, describing the chiral condensate in the model, is driven below the BF bound when the running is sufficiently strong, triggering chiral symmetry breaking for N_f/N_c<2.9. This model though contains too great a remnant of supersymmetry and does not correctly encode the perturbative anomalous dimensions of QCD. In a second model we impose the QCD anomalous dimension result and find chiral symmetry breaking sets in for N_f/N_c=4 at a BKT-type phase transition. In this case the transition is triggered when the anomalous dimension of the mass operator γ_m=1.

## Key claim

The paper presents two holographic models for chiral symmetry breaking at the edge of the conformal window in QCD in the Veneziano limit; the first naive model gives a critical value of N_f/N_c < 2.9 with a second-order transition, but the authors argue this is incorrect due to residual supersymmetry, while a second model imposing the QCD anomalous dimension relation yields a BKT-type phase transition at N_f/N_c = 4 when the anomalous dimension of the mass operator reaches gamma_m = 1.

## Method

The authors place a probe D7 brane in an AdS_5 x S^5 background and impose a dilaton profile corresponding to the two-loop QCD running coupling, then solve the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) equation for the brane embedding to determine when chiral symmetry breaking occurs. In a second model, they instead impose the QCD one-loop anomalous dimension relation for the quark mass operator directly on the DBI action, using the two-loop running coupling to fix the IR fixed point value, and analyze the mass squared of the holographic slipping mode to identify the Breitenlohner-Freedman (BF) bound violation that triggers the transition.

## Key numbers

* In the naive model imposing two-loop QCD running, chiral symmetry breaking is triggered for N_f/N_c < 2.9, with a second-order (mean field) transition at x_c = 2.949.
* In the second model imposing the QCD anomalous dimension, chiral symmetry breaking sets in at N_f/N_c = 4, with a BKT-type phase transition.
* The transition in the second model occurs when the anomalous dimension of the mass operator gamma_m = 1, corresponding to the slipping mode mass squared reaching m^2 = -4 (the BF bound in AdS_5).
* The two-loop QCD beta function fixed point is given by lambda_* = (11 - 2x)/(13x - 34) * (4 pi)^2.

## Context

This work extends the holographic model of Jarvinen and Kiritsis (2011) for the conformal window in QCD, using the simpler D3/D7 brane system instead of 5D supergravity. The authors find that the naive D3/D7 model with running coupling alone underestimates the critical flavor ratio (x_c = 2.9) compared to Schwinger-Dyson estimates (Appelquist, 1996; Appelquist, 1998) which suggest 3.5 < x_c < 4, because the model retains too much supersymmetry in the IR. The corrected model with imposed QCD anomalous dimension gives x_c = 4, matching the one-loop perturbative field theory computation, and enables further holographic study of BKT transitions and the conformal window's phase structure.
