# Influence of nucleonic motion in Relativistic Fermi Gas inclusive responses

> Impulsive hadronic descriptions of electroweak processes in nuclei involve two distinctly different elements: one stems from the nuclear many-body physics --- the medium --- which is rather similar for the various inclusive response functions, and the other embodies the responses of the hadrons themselves to the electroweak probe and varies with the channel selected. In this letter we investigate within the context of the relativistic Fermi gas in both the quasi-elastic and $N\toΔ$ regimes the interplay between these two elements. Specifically, we focus on expansions in the one small parameter in the problem, namely, the momentum of a nucleon in the initial wave function compared with the hadronic scale, the nucleon mass. Both parity-conserving and -violating inclusive responses are studied and the interplay between longitudinal ($L$) and transverse ($T$ and $T'$) contributions is highlighted.

## Key claim

The interplay between single-baryon and nuclear many-body content in inclusive electroweak responses can be organized via expansions in the small parameter \(\eta_F = p_F/m_N\); when the factor \(\kappa^2/\tau\) is used judiciously, the leading terms of the single-baryon cross sections contain no additional \(\eta_F\)-dependence beyond that implicit in that factor, and next-to-leading corrections are of order \(\eta_F^2\) with no linear terms, except for the longitudinal response where linear terms do appear.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive parity-conserving (longitudinal \(L\) and transverse \(T\)) and parity-violating (\(L\), \(T\), and axial \(T'\)) response functions for electron-nucleus scattering within the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, covering both the quasi-elastic (QE) and \(N\to\Delta\) resonance regimes. They expand the single-nucleon tensor \(f_{\mu\nu}\) in the dimensionless nucleon momentum \(\eta\) up to linear order, and derive analytic expressions for the response functions that factorize into a universal medium factor \(R_0\) and channel-dependent functions \(U^{L,T,T'}\). The finite width of the \(\Delta\) resonance is included via a standard convolution with a P-wave width.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{12}\)C, the dimensionless Fermi energy is \(\xi_F = 0.028\).
* At the QE peak, the functions \({\cal D}^{L(T)}_P = \xi_F + \frac{1}{3(1+\tau\rho^2)}\xi_F^2\) are practically constant and independent of \(\tau\) and \(\rho\) because \(\xi_F\) is small.
* At the QE peak, \({\cal D}^{T'}_P = \frac{\xi_F}{2(1+\tau\rho^2)}\) vanishes at large \(\tau\) and is smaller at the \(\Delta\) peak than at the QE peak.
* The difference between the relativistic and non-relativistic universal factor, \(R_0 - R_{0,nr}\), does not vanish at leading order in \(\eta_F\) and can be large for high \(\tau\); in the limit \(\mu\to 1\) it gives \(\frac{3{\cal N}}{8 m_N\kappa\eta_F^3}\left[\frac{\tau^3}{1+\tau}+{\cal O}(\eta_F)\right]\).
* The electromagnetic \(N\to\Delta\) transition form factors are parametrized as \(G_{E,\Delta}(\tau) = -0.03\, f(\tau)\), \(G_{M,\Delta}(\tau) = 2.97\, f(\tau)\), and \(G_{C,\Delta}(\tau) = 0\), with \(f(\tau) = G_{E,p}(\tau) (1+\lambda_\Delta \tau)^{-1/2}\) and \(\lambda_\Delta = 1.0\).
* The isovector coupling constant for parity-violating \(N\to\Delta\) transitions is \(\beta_V^{T=1} = 1 - 2\sin^2{\theta_W} = 0.55\).

## Context

This work extends previous RFG studies of inclusive responses by Donnelly et al. (1992), Amaro et al. (1999), and Amore et al. (2000), which obtained factorized expressions for the response functions. It also builds on expansions of spinor matrix elements for electromagnetic currents by Jeschonnek et al. (1998) and Amaro et al. (1998, 1999). The paper provides a systematic expansion in the small parameter \(\eta_F = p_F/m_N\) that clarifies which response channels receive linear corrections (longitudinal) versus only quadratic corrections (transverse and axial). The derived relations between parity-violating axial and parity-conserving transverse responses, such as \(\tilde{R}^{T'}/R^T \simeq \sqrt{(\tau+1)/\tau}\, G_A^{(1)}/G_M^{(1)}\) for QE scattering, are shown to be valid to remarkable accuracy in the RFG model, enabling simplified descriptions of inclusive electroweak processes.
