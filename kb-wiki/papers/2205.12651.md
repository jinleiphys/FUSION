# Surface energy coefficient of a N2LO Skyrme energy functional : a semiclassical Extended Thomas-Fermi approach

> We generalize to N2LO Skyrme functionals the semi-classical approach of Grammaticos and Voros in order to calculate the Extended Thomas Fermi expressions of the new densities and currents appearing at the N2LO level. Within a one dimensional symmetric semi infinite nuclear matter model and using a simple Fermi-like density profile, we obtain an easy-to-use formula for the surface energy including the contributions of the central, density-dependent and spin-orbit terms up to $\hbar^2$. Such a formula can be easily used as a first attempt to constrain the surface properties of new N2LO Skyrme parametrizations. The N2LO parametrization tested in this paper is shown to exhibit a shift (compared to a full Hartree-Fock calculation) which is quantitatively similar to the one obtained with the traditional Skyrme parametrizations.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the semiclassical Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method of Grammaticos and Voros (1978, 1979) to N2LO Skyrme functionals, but only under the perturbative approximation that N2LO terms are kept in the energy functional while the one-body Hamiltonian used in the Wigner-Kirkwood transform retains only NLO potentials, justified by the small numerical contributions of 4-gradient terms.

## Method

The Wigner-Kirkwood transform of the one-body Hamiltonian is performed for a N2LO Skyrme energy density functional, keeping only NLO potentials in the Hamiltonian but including N2LO terms in the functional itself. The ETF expansion up to order hbar^2 is applied to a one-dimensional symmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter model with a simple Fermi-like density profile, yielding expressions for the new densities and currents (Q, tau^(s)_(mu nu), C_(mu nu)) that appear at the N2LO level. The surface energy formula is then derived including contributions from central, density-dependent, and spin-orbit terms.

## Key numbers

- The N2LO parametrization tested exhibits a shift compared to a full Hartree-Fock calculation that is "quantitatively similar" to the shift obtained with traditional Skyrme parametrizations (no specific numerical value given for the shift).
- The surface energy formula includes contributions up to order hbar^2.
- The calculation uses a one-dimensional symmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter model (no specific numerical surface energy coefficient value is reported in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical ETF approach of Grammaticos and Voros (1978, 1979) and the surface energy formula of Krivine and Treiner (1979, 1986) to N2LO Skyrme functionals, which include 4th-order gradient terms expected to improve nuclear properties as studied by Becker (2017). The resulting formula can be used as a computationally inexpensive constraint in fitting procedures for new N2LO Skyrme parametrizations, similar to how Jodon et al. (2016) used a simple ETF formula to produce the SLy5s parametrizations.
