# Suppression of the elastic scattering cross section for 17Ne + 208Pb system

> We investigated the elastic scattering, inelastic scattering, breakup reaction, and total fusion reactions of 17Ne + 208Pb system using the optical model (OM) and a coupled channel (CC) approaches. The aim of this study is to elucidate the suppress of the elastic cross-section that is invisible in proton-rich nuclei such as 8B and 17F projectiles but appears in neutron-rich nuclei such as 11Li and 11Be projectiles. The results revealed that this suppression was caused mainly by the nuclear interaction between the projectile and target nucleus rather than the strong Coulomb interaction observed in neutron-rich nuclei and the contributions of Coulomb excitation interaction due to two low-lying E2 resonance states are relatively small. From the simultaneous chi-square analysis of the 17Ne + 208Pb system, we can infer a strong suppression effect in the elastic scattering cross-section due to the nuclear interaction between the projectile and target nucleus, rather than the Coulomb interaction as observed in neutron-rich nuclei. Also, the contribution of the direct reaction, comprising the inelastic scattering and breakup reaction cross-sections, accounted for almost half of the total reaction. Finally, we perform the CC calculation using the parameters obtained from our OM calculation but our CC calculations could not explain the 15O production cross section.

## Key claim

The suppression of the elastic scattering cross section for the proton-rich projectile 17Ne on 208Pb is caused primarily by the nuclear interaction between projectile and target, not by the strong Coulomb interaction that drives suppression in neutron-rich projectiles like 11Li and 11Be; the contributions from Coulomb excitation via two low-lying E2 resonance states are relatively small.

## Method

The authors used the optical model (OM) with a dynamic polarization potential (DPP) decomposed into inelastic scattering, breakup, and fusion components, and performed simultaneous chi-square fitting to elastic scattering data, experimental breakup (15O production) cross sections, and a theoretical total fusion cross section from the barrier penetration model. They also performed coupled channel (CC) calculations including the 3/2- state at Ex = 1.288 MeV, the 5/2- state at Ex = 1.764 MeV, and the 3/2- state at Ex = 2.692 MeV, using B(E2) values from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The total reaction cross section from the OM calculation is 1859.9 mb (set A) and 1889.3 mb (set B) at Elab = 136 MeV.
- The breakup cross section is 602.7 mb (set A: 320.0 mb nuclear + 282.7 mb Coulomb) and 594.5 mb (set B: 316.4 mb nuclear + 278.1 mb Coulomb).
- The total fusion cross section from the barrier penetration model using the bare potential is approximately 994.3 mb; the OM fit yields 1126.8 mb (set A) and 915.6 mb (set B).
- The inelastic scattering cross section (set B) is 379.2 mb (250.9 mb nuclear + 128.3 mb Coulomb).
- The chi-square values for the fits are 2.6 (set A) and 2.1 (set B).

## Context

This work extends the study of elastic scattering suppression from neutron-rich projectiles (11Li, 11Be) to the proton-rich Borromean nucleus 17Ne, where previous work on 8B and 17F projectiles showed no such suppression due to a shielding effect. The analysis differs from Ovejas et al. (2023) by simultaneously fitting elastic scattering and breakup cross sections using a decomposed DPP approach, and by including the 3/2- state at Ex = 2.692 MeV in the CC calculations. The results enable future studies of the interplay between nuclear and Coulomb breakup mechanisms in weakly bound proton-rich systems near the Coulomb barrier.
