# Universal few-body physics in a harmonic trap

> Few-body systems with resonant short-range interactions display universal properties that do not depend on the details of their structure or their interactions at short distances. In the three-body system, these properties include the existence of a geometric spectrum of three-body Efimov states and a discrete scaling symmetry. Similar universal properties appear in 4-body and possibly higher-body systems as well. We set up an effective theory for few-body systems in a harmonic trap and study the modification of universal physics for 3- and 4-particle systems in external confinement. In particular, we focus on systems where the Efimov effect can occur and investigate the dependence of the 4-body spectrum on the experimental tuning parameters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal few-body physics (including Efimov states) can be systematically studied in a harmonic trap using an effective theory with a finite model-space cutoff, and that the trapped 4-body spectrum depends on the 2- and 3-body energies used as input, with both Efimov-like and universal states appearing.

## Method

The authors formulate an effective theory for short-range interactions in a harmonic oscillator trap, identifying the ultraviolet cutoff with the model-space cutoff N on harmonic oscillator basis functions. They diagonalize the Hamiltonian numerically for 2-, 3-, and 4-particle systems in a finite basis, renormalizing the 2-body coupling constant to reproduce a given 2-body energy and the 3-body coupling constant to reproduce a given 3-body energy. Results are extrapolated to N → ∞ using linear or quadratic fits in 1/√N.

## Key numbers

- For identical bosons in free space, the discrete scaling factor is e^(π/s0) ≈ 22.7, with s0 ≈ 1.00624.
- For two particles in a harmonic trap in the unitary limit, the ground state energy approaches ℏω/2.
- For the 3-body system of ⁶Li atoms (|1⟩⊗|1⟩⊗|2⟩) with oscillator length β ≈ 65 a_B, the ground state energy is approximately 3.2 ℏω and the first excited state approximately 5.2 ℏω.
- For the 3-body system in the unitary limit with a 3-body interaction adjusted so that E0^(3) = −ℏω, typical extrapolation errors for Efimov-like states are 2–3% and less than 1% for other states.
- For the 4-body system of two identical fermions with two distinguishable particles in the unitary limit with ε^(3) = −0.5, cutoff values N ≈ 20–30 were reached.

## Context

This work extends the effective theory approach of Stetcu et al. (2006) for short-range forces in a no-core shell model framework to 3- and 4-particle systems in a harmonic trap, focusing on systems where the Efimov effect can occur. It builds on the exact 2-body solution of Busch et al. and the analytical 3-body solution of Werner and Castin, and provides a systematic study of how the bound state spectrum depends on the 2- and 3-body energies. The results enable future experimental investigations of universal few-body physics in trapped ultracold gases, particularly for systems where trap effects cannot be neglected.
