# Working group summary: Isospin violation

## Key claim
Isospin symmetry is broken by the up-down quark mass difference and electromagnetic interactions, but a truly quantitative understanding of isospin violation has not yet emerged from experiment; the only machinery to consistently separate strong and electromagnetic isospin violation is based on effective field theory, and systematic investigations of all pertinent low-energy reactions are still lacking.

## Method
The working group reviewed isospin violation across multiple sectors: the pion sector (elastic ππ scattering and pion form factors), the pion-nucleon sector (πN scattering, neutral pion photoproduction, and the strange vector form factor of the nucleon), the nucleon-nucleon sector (charge independence breaking and charge symmetry breaking in scattering lengths, and pion production in pp and np collisions), and light nuclei. The analysis employed chiral perturbation theory, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, and the Kaplan-Savage-Wise non-perturbative effective field theory scheme for the two-nucleon system, with the electric charge counted as a small parameter on the same footing as external momenta and meson masses.

## Key numbers
- Isospin violation from dimensional analysis: (m_d - m_u)/M_ρ ≈ O(1%), with possible enhancement to (m_d - m_u)/(m_Δ - m_N) ≈ O(2%) in the presence of the Δ(1232) resonance.
- Two analyses of low-energy πN scattering indicate isospin violation of order 6-7%, much larger than the dimensional arguments would suggest.
- In the nucleon-nucleon sector, the S-wave contribution α_0 in pp → π⁺d and np → π⁰d shows isospin violation of order 10% in the threshold region, with no effect observed in P-wave terms.
- Leading charge independence breaking effects in the KSW scheme scale as α Q⁻², where Q ≈ 1/3 is the expansion parameter of the scheme; two-pion exchange and πγ graphs are suppressed by factors of 1/3 and (1/3)² respectively.

## Context
This working group summary from the Eighth International Symposium on Meson-Nucleon Physics (Zuoz, 1999) reviews the status of isospin violation following the classic review by Gasser and Leutwyler (1982). It extends earlier work on hadron mass splittings (Weinberg, 1977; Gasser and Leutwyler, 1982) and the chiral perturbation theory analysis of ππ scattering to two loops (Melnikov and Shevchenko, 1999; Knecht and Urech, 1998). The summary identifies open questions including the size of violation of Dashen's theorem, the possibility of a vanishing up quark mass, and the need for a systematic effective field theory treatment of isospin violation in πN scattering, pion photoproduction, and nucleon form factors. It enables future calculations of the πN σ-term, neutral pion scattering off nucleons, and reanalysis of strange vector form factor measurements from BATES and JLAB.
