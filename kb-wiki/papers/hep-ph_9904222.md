# QCD Rescattering and High Energy Two-Body Photodisintegration of the Deuteron

> Photon absorption by a quark in one nucleon followed by its high momentum transfer interaction with a quark in the other may produce two final-state nucleons with high relative momentum. We sum the relevant quark rescattering diagrams, to show that the scattering amplitude depends on a convolution between the large angle $pn$ scattering amplitude, the hard photon-quark interaction vertex and the low-momentum deuteron wave function. The computed absolute values of the cross section are in reasonable agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The authors propose that high-energy two-body photodisintegration of the deuteron proceeds via a quark rescattering mechanism, where the photon is absorbed by a quark in one nucleon followed by a hard gluon exchange with a quark in the other nucleon. The computed cross sections agree with data only when using the approximation that the scaling factor \(f(l^2/s)=1\) and when the hard scattering amplitude is replaced by experimental \(pn\) scattering data, with the agreement improving at larger center-of-mass angles.

## Method

The authors sum quark rescattering Feynman diagrams in which a photon is absorbed by a quark in one nucleon, the struck quark propagates as an on-shell intermediate state, and then exchanges a hard gluon with a quark from the second nucleon via the quark interchange mechanism. They integrate over the minus-momenta to obtain an amplitude expressed as a convolution of the large-angle \(pn\) scattering amplitude, the photon-quark vertex, and the low-momentum deuteron wave function, keeping only the imaginary part of the struck quark propagator to select the soft component of the deuteron wave function. The differential cross section is then computed by replacing the quark-interchange \(pn\) amplitude with experimental \(pn\) scattering data and using the Paris potential model for the non-relativistic deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic condition for the validity of the partonic picture requires the intermediate state mass \(m_{int} \ge 2.2\) GeV, leading to \(E_{\gamma} \gtrsim 2.5\) GeV.
- The regeneration distance for the struck quark is \(l_r \approx E_\gamma \cdot R^2\) with \(R \approx 0.3\) fm; for \(E_\gamma \ge 2.5\) GeV, \(l_r \gtrsim 1.1\) fm.
- The hard rescattering condition requires transverse momentum of final nucleons \(p_t \ge 1\) GeV, corresponding to \(-t,-u \gtrsim 2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The cross section scaling predicted is \(d\sigma/dt \sim s^{-11}\) at fixed angle, consistent with quark counting rules.
- The calculation uses experimental \(pn \rightarrow pn\) data at \(t_N\) values extrapolated between \(t_N^{min}\) and \(t_N^{max}\) to produce a band of predictions.

## Context

This work extends the quark interchange mechanism previously applied to baryon-baryon scattering (e.g., GBB) to the photodisintegration reaction \(\gamma d \rightarrow pn\), addressing the failure of conventional mesonic models to describe experimental data from E89012, NE8, and NE17. The approach is related to the reduced nuclear amplitude method of Brodsky and Hiller (BH) but expresses the nuclear amplitude directly in terms of the measured \(pn\) hard scattering amplitude, enabling absolute cross section calculations. The results suggest a possible new type of quark-hadron duality and open the door to using \(\gamma d \rightarrow pn\) as a probe of hard \(NN\) interactions, with further angular dependence measurements needed to verify the scaling pattern.
