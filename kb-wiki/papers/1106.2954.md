# Periodic Box FHNC calculations of neutron star crustal matter. (I)

> Neutron star crustal matter, whose properties are relevant in many models aimed at explaining observed astrophysical phenomena, has so far always been studied using a mean field approach. In order to check the results obtained in this way, a sensible next step is to make use of a realistic nuclear potential. The present paper extends the periodic-box Fermi HyperNetted Chain method to include longitudinal-isospin dependence of the correlations, making feasible a study of asymmetric crustal matter. Results are presented for the symmetry energy, the low-density neutron star equation of state and the single particle neutron and proton energies.

## Key claim

The paper extends the periodic-box Fermi HyperNetted Chain (PB-FHNC) method to handle asymmetric nuclear matter by including a longitudinal isospin dependence (τ_z(i)τ_z(j)) in the correlation operator, rather than the full isospin dependence (τ(i)·τ(j)), and demonstrates that this longitudinal approximation accounts for more than 80% of the full isospin dependence for the v_4-type interaction models considered, irrespective of density and symmetry parameter.

## Method

The authors implement a correlated basis function (CBF) approach with a Jastrow-like correlation operator that distinguishes isospin-parallel from isospin-antiparallel correlations using projection operators P_NN, P_PP, P_NP, and P_PN, with scalar functions f_∥ and f_⊥ obtained from solving second-order differential equations. They apply the Fantoni-Rosati cluster expansion and FHNC integral-equation methods to sum the series of cluster diagrams for diagonal matrix elements of the Hamiltonian on the ground state and one particle–one hole states, using v_4-type potentials (AT4' and (AV8')_4) that omit tensor and spin-orbit components.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal isospin dependence accounts for more than 80% of the full isospin dependence for the interaction models considered, irrespective of the values of the density ρ and the symmetry parameter α.
- For a system of 2060 nucleons at the experimental equilibrium density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³, the variational energy from PB-FHNC calculations with the AT4' potential is −15.150 MeV, compared to the FHNC/SOC result of −16.184 MeV.
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is a_nn ∼ −20 fm, and the resulting values of k_F a_nn range from ∼ −6 to ∼ −30 for neutron soup densities from ∼ 0.001 fm⁻³ to ∼ 0.1 fm⁻³.
- Typical lattice lengths in neutron star crustal matter are ∼ 20–60 fm.

## Context

This work extends the periodic-box FHNC method of Fantoni (2001) from Jastrow-correlated symmetric matter to asymmetric matter with isospin-dependent correlations, enabling ab initio checks on mean-field calculations of neutron star crust properties that have previously relied on effective interactions (e.g., Pastore 2008, Than 2010). The results enable future comparisons with Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone calculations (Engvik 1994) and provide a variational starting point for more resource-intensive Quantum Monte Carlo simulations of crustal matter, including the lattice of neutron-rich nuclei surrounded by superfluid neutrons.
