# Spin observables for pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the $Δ$(1232)-resonance region

> Spin observables for the three charge states of the pion for the pion photoproduction reaction on the deuteron, $γd\toπNN$, with polarized photon beam and/or oriented deuteron target are predicted. For the beam-target double-spin asymmetries, it is found that only the longitudinal asymmetries $T_{20}^{\ell}$ and $T_{2\pm 2}^{\ell}$ do not vanish, whereas all the circular and the other longitudinal asymmetries do vanish. The sensitivity of spin observables to the model deuteron wave function is investigated. It has been found that only $T_{21}$ and $T_{22}$ are sensitive to the model deuteron wave function, in particular in the case of $π^0$-production above the $Δ$-region, and that other asymmetries are not.

## Key claim

For the beam-target double-spin asymmetries in pion photoproduction on the deuteron, only the longitudinal asymmetries \(T_{20}^{\ell}\) and \(T_{2\pm 2}^{\ell}\) do not vanish, whereas all circular asymmetries and the other longitudinal asymmetries vanish; this result holds within the impulse approximation model that neglects final-state interactions and two-body effects.

## Method

The author calculates spin observables for the reaction \(\gamma d \to \pi NN\) using time-ordered perturbation theory within the impulse approximation, where the elementary pion photoproduction amplitude on a free nucleon is taken from an effective Lagrangian model (Schmidt et al., 1996). The deuteron wave function is taken from the Paris potential model, with comparisons also made using the Bonn r-space potential. The transition matrix elements are evaluated in the laboratory frame, and the five-fold differential cross section is integrated over pion momentum and the solid angle of the final two-nucleon system.

## Key numbers

* Photon lab-energies considered: 250, 290, 310, 340, 360, 390, 420, 470, and 500 MeV.
* The linear photon asymmetry \(\Sigma\) is found to vanish at \(\theta_{\pi}=0^{\circ}\) but not at \(180^{\circ}\).
* The vector target asymmetry \(T_{11}\) vanishes at \(\theta_{\pi}=0^{\circ}\) and \(180^{\circ}\).
* The tensor target asymmetry \(T_{21}\) vanishes at \(\theta_{\pi}=0^{\circ}\) and \(180^{\circ}\).
* The tensor target asymmetry \(T_{22}\) vanishes at \(\theta_{\pi}=0^{\circ}\) and \(180^{\circ}\).
* The longitudinal double-spin asymmetry \(T_{2+2}^{\ell}\) is found to be identical in value to \(T_{2-2}^{\ell}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations of cross sections and single-spin asymmetries for incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron (Darwish, 2003; Darwish, 2004) by providing complete predictions for double-polarization observables. It builds on earlier diagrammatic studies of \(\pi^-\)-photoproduction on the deuteron (Logothetidou, 2000) and is motivated by experimental programs at LEGS, MAMI, ELSA, and JLab that now enable measurements of double-spin asymmetries. The results enable future experimental tests of the reaction mechanism and can be extended to include final-state interactions and two-body effects, as well as to pion electroproduction.
