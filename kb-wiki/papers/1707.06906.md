# New insights into hadron production mechanism from $p_{T}$ spectra in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV

> We show that the experimental data of mid-rapidity $p_{T}$ spectra for proton, $Λ$, $Ξ$ , $Ω^{-}$, $\text{K(892)}^{*0}$ and $Ξ(1530)^{*0}$ in minimum-bias $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV can be systemically explained by the quark combination mechanism of hadronization. The averaged transverse momentum $\langle p_{T}\rangle$ and spectra ratios such as $Ξ/Λ$ and $Ω/ϕ$ calculated from quark combination reproduce the data much better than those from traditional string and/or cluster fragmentation. The available data of hadronic $p_{T}$ spectra released by ALICE collaboration in the first three high-multiplicity classes of $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV are also well explained. We make predictions for other hadrons, and propose two scaling behaviors among decuplet baryons and vector mesons as the effective probe of hadron production mechanism at such high collision energy.

# New insights into hadron production mechanism from $p_{T}$ spectra in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV

## Key claim

The quark combination mechanism of hadronization can systematically explain the mid-rapidity $p_{T}$ spectra for proton, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, $\Omega^{-}$, K(892)$^{*0}$ and $\Xi(1530)^{*0}$ in minimum-bias $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, but only after accounting for event-by-event fluctuations of quark numbers via a Poisson distribution with a suppression parameter $\gamma_s=0.6$ for strange quarks to handle threshold effects for multi-strangeness hyperons.

## Method

The authors apply a quark combination model (QCM) where hadrons are formed by combining constituent quarks and antiquarks neighboring in phase space, using an equal transverse velocity approximation with quark masses $m_s=500$ MeV and $m_u=m_d=330$ MeV. The single quark $p_T$ distributions are parameterized with a Levy-Tsallis-like function, and the event-averaged hadron yields are computed by summing over quark number fluctuations using a modified Poisson distribution with a suppression factor $\gamma_s=0.6$ for strange quarks. The model parameters $R_{V/P}=0.45$ (vector-to-pseudoscalar meson ratio) and $R_{O/D}=2.5$ (octet-to-decuplet baryon ratio) are fixed, and the baryon-to-meson fraction is set to $N_B/N_M\approx0.085$.

## Key numbers

- Average quark numbers in $|y|<0.5$ for minimum-bias events: $\langle N_u\rangle=\langle N_d\rangle=2.5$, $\langle N_s\rangle=0.8$
- Quark masses used: $m_s=500$ MeV, $m_u=m_d=330$ MeV
- Yield density $dN/dy$ for $\Omega^{-}$ in minimum-bias: QCM gives $0.075\times10^{-2}$, data is $0.0675\pm0.003_{-0.006}^{+0.008}\times10^{-2}$
- Yield density $dN/dy$ for $\Xi^{*0}$ in minimum-bias: QCM gives $0.28\times10^{-2}$, data is $0.256\pm0.007_{-0.037}^{+0.040}\times10^{-2}$
- Average transverse momentum $\langle p_T\rangle$ for $\Omega^{-}$ in minimum-bias: QCM gives 1.38 GeV, data is $1.455\pm0.03\pm0.08$ GeV
- Average transverse momentum $\langle p_T\rangle$ for K$^{*0}$ in minimum-bias: QCM gives 1.00 GeV, data is $1.01\pm0.003\pm0.02$ GeV
- Charged-particle multiplicity densities in high-multiplicity classes: class I (0-0.95%) $\langle dN_{ch}/d\eta\rangle=21.3\pm0.6$, class II (0.95-4.7%) $16.5\pm0.5$, class III (4.7-9.5%) $13.5\pm0.4$
- Quark numbers in class I: $\langle N_u\rangle=10.9$, $\langle N_s\rangle=3.7$
- Quark numbers in class III: $\langle N_u\rangle=7.2$, $\langle N_s\rangle=2.4$
- $\langle p_T\rangle$ for $\Omega$ in average of classes (I)+(II): QCM gives 1.57 GeV, data is $1.62\pm0.05$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark combination mechanism previously applied to $p$-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV by Song (2017) to $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, where the authors had found striking quark number scaling in hadron $p_T$ spectra. The results contradict traditional string fragmentation models such as PYTHIA6 Perugia2011 (Skands, 2010) and cluster fragmentation in SHERPA (Webber, 1983), which predict $p_T$ spectra for multi-strangeness hadrons that are 15-20% softer than data. The work enables further tests of whether a deconfined partonic system forms in small systems at LHC energies, and proposes two scaling behaviors among decuplet baryons and vector mesons as probes of the hadron production mechanism, though these scalings are found to be broken in minimum-bias $pp$ data due to event mixing effects.
