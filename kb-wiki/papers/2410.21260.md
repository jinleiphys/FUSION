# Elastic Dijet Production in Electron Scattering on a Longitudinally Polarized Proton at Small $x$: A Portal to Orbital Angular Momentum Distributions

> We calculate the elastic production of dijets from electron collisions with a longitudinally polarized proton target at small values of the Bjorken $x$ variable. Building on the pioneering proposals of \cite{Hatta:2016aoc,Bhattacharya:2022vvo, Bhattacharya:2023hbq, Bhattacharya:2024sck} for measuring the quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions, our focus is on both the longitudinal double spin asymmetry (DSA) and longitudinal single spin asymmetry (SSA). We compute the numerators of these asymmetries in the small-$x$ formalism of the light-cone operator treatment. Utilizing the small-$x$ expressions for the OAM distributions derived in our earlier paper, we demonstrate that the DSA provides a robust probe for both the quark and gluon OAM distributions within the proton. In contrast, we find that while the SSA is also sensitive to the OAM distributions, extraction of the latter from the SSA would require new developments in small-$x$ theory and phenomenology, and is probably not feasible at this point in time. These findings highlight the potential of DSA measurements in elastic dijet production at the future Electron-Ion Collider to provide the first-ever direct access to the quark and gluon OAM distributions at small $x$, paving the way for new insights into the proton spin puzzle.

## Key claim

The longitudinal double spin asymmetry (DSA) in elastic dijet production at small Bjorken x provides a robust probe for both quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions within the proton, while the longitudinal single spin asymmetry (SSA) also couples to OAM distributions but extracting them from the SSA would require new developments in small-x theory and phenomenology and is probably not feasible at this point in time.

## Method

The authors calculated the elastic production of dijets from electron collisions with a longitudinally polarized proton target at small x using the light-cone operator treatment (LCOT) formalism, which extends the standard shock-wave approach to include subeikonal contributions. They computed the numerators of the longitudinal double spin asymmetry and longitudinal single spin asymmetry by constructing the elastic dijet production cross section in polarized electron-proton scattering, expanding the DSA numerator to first nontrivial order in transverse momentum transfer Delta_perp. The small-x expressions for the OAM distributions derived in their earlier work (Kovchegov and Manley, 2023) were utilized to relate the asymmetries to the polarized dipole amplitudes and moment dipole amplitudes.

## Context

This work builds on the pioneering proposals by Hatta (2016) and Bhattacharya and collaborators (2022, 2023, 2024) for measuring quark and gluon OAM distributions through elastic dijet and pion production in longitudinally polarized lepton-proton scattering. It extends the authors' earlier studies of OAM distributions at small x (Kovchegov and Manley, 2023; Kovchegov, 2019; Manley, 2024) and the helicity evolution framework developed by Kovchegov, Pitonyak, and Sievert (KPS) and corrected by Cougoulic, Kovchegov, Tarasov, and Tawabutr (KPS-CTT). The results highlight the potential of DSA measurements in elastic dijet production at the future Electron-Ion Collider to provide the first-ever direct access to quark and gluon OAM distributions at small x, enabling new insights into the proton spin puzzle.
