# Empirical neutron star mass formula based on experimental observables

> We derive the empirical formulae expressing the mass and gravitational redshift of a neutron star, whose central density is less than threefold the nuclear saturation density, as a function of the neutron-skin thickness or the dipole polarizability of $ {}^{208} \mathrm{Pb} $ or $ {}^{132} \mathrm{Sn} $, especially focusing on the 8 Skyrme-type effective interactions. The neutron star mass and its gravitational redshift can be estimated within $ \approx 10 \, \% $ errors with our formulae, while the neutron star radius is also expected within a few $\%$ errors by combining the derived formulae. Owing to the resultant empirical formulae, we find that the neutron star mass and radius are more sensitive to the neutron-skin thickness of $ {}^{208} \mathrm{Pb} $ than the dipole polarizability of $ {}^{208} \mathrm{Pb} $ or $ {}^{132} \mathrm{Sn} $.

## Key claim

The authors derive empirical formulae that express neutron star mass and gravitational redshift directly as functions of the neutron-skin thickness or dipole polarizability of 208Pb or 132Sn, rather than via nuclear saturation parameters. However, these formulae are only applicable to low-mass neutron stars with central density less than three times nuclear saturation density, corresponding to masses at most about 1 solar mass, and are derived using only 8 Skyrme-type effective interactions with a relatively narrow range of the symmetry energy slope parameter L (40 to 80 MeV).

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star models by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using 8 Skyrme-type energy density functionals (LNS5, SKa, SkMp, SLy2, SLy4, SLy5, SLy9, SLy230a). For each interaction, they calculated the neutron-skin thickness and dipole polarizability for 208Pb and 132Sn using spherical Hartree-Fock and random phase approximation calculations with the skyrme_rpa code. They then performed linear fits of neutron star mass and gravitational redshift as functions of these experimental observables at fixed central densities, with the fitting coefficients expressed as fourth-order polynomials in the normalized central density.

## Key numbers

- The empirical formulae estimate neutron star mass and gravitational redshift within approximately 10% errors, and radius within a few percent errors, for central densities up to three times nuclear saturation density.
- The applicable range of the formulae is central density ratio uc = 0.8 to 3.0, with neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb between 0.153 fm and 0.211 fm, or of 132Sn between 0.213 fm and 0.274 fm.
- The fitting relations between neutron-skin thickness and the symmetry energy slope L are: Delta Rn(Pb) = 0.0757 + 0.176 L/100 (fm) and Delta Rn(Sn) = 0.134 + 0.182 L/100 (fm).
- The fitting relations between dipole polarizability times symmetry energy S0 and L are: alpha_D(Pb) * S0 = 449 + 382 L/100 (MeV fm^3) and alpha_D(Sn) * S0 = 232 + 177 L/100 (MeV fm^3).

## Context

This work extends previous efforts by Sotani et al. (2014, 2022) and Sotani and Ota (2022) that expressed neutron star properties as functions of nuclear saturation parameters, by instead using directly measurable experimental observables. The approach avoids the model-dependent step of inferring saturation parameters from experimental data, which has been shown to introduce significant uncertainties as discussed by Reinhard et al. (2022) and Naito et al. (2022). The resulting formulae enable estimation of neutron star mass and radius from terrestrial nuclear experiments, complementing astronomical constraints from gravitational wave observations like GW170817 and NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620.
