# Frame Dependence of the Pair Contribution to the Pion Electromagnetic Form Factor in a Light-Front Approach

> The frame dependence of the pair-term contribution to the electromagnetic form factor of the pion is studied within the Light Front approach. A symmetric ansatz for the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with a pseudo scalar coupling of the constituent to the pion field is used. In this model, the pair term vanishes for the Drell-Yan condition, while it is dominant for momentum transfer along the light-front direction.

## Key claim

Within a symmetric Bethe-Salpeter amplitude model with pseudoscalar coupling, the pair-term contribution to the pion electromagnetic form factor vanishes exactly under the Drell-Yan condition ($q^+ = 0$), but becomes dominant for momentum transfer along the light-front direction ($\alpha = 90^\circ$), especially at high momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors calculate the pion electromagnetic form factor in impulse approximation using a symmetric ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function with a regulator mass parameter. They evaluate the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame for different directions of the momentum transfer in the $z-x$ plane, parameterized by an angle $\alpha$ where $\alpha = 0^\circ$ corresponds to the Drell-Yan condition and $\alpha = 90^\circ$ corresponds to momentum transfer along the light-front direction. The form factor is decomposed into a valence contribution $F_\pi^{(I)}$ and a pair-term contribution $F_\pi^{(II)}$, with the latter arising from the non-valence part of the light-front time-ordered diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass $m = 0.220$ GeV and regulator mass $m_R = 0.6$ GeV, chosen to fit the experimental pion decay constant $f_\pi^{\text{exp}} = 92.4$ MeV
- Pion mass used: $m_\pi = 0.140$ GeV
- Charge radius from the model: $\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.74$ fm, about 10% larger than the experimental value $r_{\text{exp}} = 0.67 \pm 0.02$ fm
- Probability of the valence $q\bar{q}$ Fock-state component: $\eta = 0.77$
- For $\alpha = 90^\circ$ and $-q^2 = 1$ (GeV/c)$^2$, the pair-term contribution dominates the form factor, with the instantaneous part of the Dirac propagator in $F_\pi^{(II)}$ providing the dominant contribution at higher momentum transfers

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pair-term contribution in light-front dynamics for spin-zero composite systems (Sawicki, 1991; de Melo, Naus and Frederico, 1999; de Melo, Frederico, Pace and Salme, 2002) by using a symmetric vertex function that avoids the conceptual difficulties of nonsymmetric regulators (Bakker, Choi and Ji, 2001). The model successfully reproduces both the experimental pion decay constant and the new Jefferson Lab form factor data (Volmer et al., 2001), which was not achieved with the nonsymmetric regulator approach. The results enable further studies of nonvalence contributions in light-front calculations of electromagnetic form factors for relativistic bound systems, particularly for understanding the role of instantaneous terms in the Dirac propagator.
