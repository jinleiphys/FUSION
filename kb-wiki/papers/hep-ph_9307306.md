# High--Energy Multiparticle Distributions and a Generalized Lattice Gas Model

> A simple lattice gas model in one dimension is constructed in which each site can be occupied by at most one particle of any one of $D$ species. Particles interact with a randomly drawn nearest neighbor interaction. This model is capable of reproducing the factorial moments observed in high--energy scattering. In the limit $D \rightarrow \infty$, the factorial moments of the negative binomial distribution are obtained naturally.

# High-Energy Multiparticle Distributions and a Generalized Lattice Gas Model

## Key claim
A one-dimensional lattice gas model with many particle species and randomly drawn nearest-neighbor interactions can exactly reproduce the factorial moments of the negative binomial distribution observed in high-energy scattering, but only in the limit where the number of species D approaches infinity. For finite D, the model approximates the negative binomial results with an accuracy that improves as 1/D, and as few as D=16 species suffice to match existing experimental data within uncertainties.

## Method
The authors construct a one-dimensional lattice gas model where each site can be empty or occupied by at most one particle of any of D species, with chemical potentials and nearest-neighbor interactions between species. They solve the model analytically in the thermodynamic limit by diagonalizing a (D+1)-dimensional matrix related to the partition function, obtaining expressions for the q-body correlation functions and factorial moments. They then impose a specific prescription: set all off-diagonal matrix elements to zero, draw D random numbers from [0,1] for the diagonal elements, and adjust a global parameter L to fix the dispersion to its empirical value, then take the limit D to infinity.

## Key numbers
- For p-pbar scattering at 200 GeV, the model uses average multiplicity <n> = 20 and dispersion <Delta n^2> = 110, giving c <n> = 4.5.
- For D=1 (ordinary Ising model), the ratio r_2 = 0.75, r_3 = 0.5, r_4 = 0.3125, r_5 = 0.1875, r_6 = 0.109375 (deviating strongly from the negative binomial value of 1).
- For D=16, the ensemble average <r_2> = 0.981 +/- 0.076, <r_3> = 0.951 +/- 0.173, <r_4> = 0.916 +/- 0.270, <r_5> = 0.879 +/- 0.360, <r_6> = 0.842 +/- 0.441.
- For D=512, the ensemble average <r_2> = 1.000 +/- 0.017, <r_3> = 1.002 +/- 0.042, <r_4> = 1.004 +/- 0.072, <r_5> = 1.007 +/- 0.106, <r_6> = 1.012 +/- 0.142.
- The ensemble averages approach 1 like 1/D, and the dispersions vanish like 1/sqrt(D) as D increases.

## Context
This work extends the earlier lattice gas (Ising) model of Chau and Huang (1992, 1993), which could only reproduce the negative binomial factorial moments through linear order in M. The authors show that adding multiple particle species with random interactions allows exact replication of all factorial moments, not just the leading terms. The model provides an alternative to the interpretation of intermittency (growth of factorial moments with bin number) as evidence for critical phenomena, as argued by Bialas and Peschanski (1986). The results suggest that a simple heterogeneous equilibrium system can produce intermittency, and that critical phenomena are not the unique or even most plausible explanation for the observed factorial moments in high-energy scattering.
