# Collins-Soper Kernel in the QCD Instanton Vacuum

> We outline a general framework for evaluating the non-perturbative soft functions in the QCD instanton vacuum. In particular, from the soft function we derive the Collins-Soper (CS) kernel, which drives the rapidity evolution of the transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions. The resulting CS kernel, when supplemented with the perturbative contribution, agrees well with recent lattice results and some phenomenological parameterizations. Moreover, our CS kernel depends logarithmically on the large quark transverse separation, providing a key constraint on its phenomenological parametrization. Finally, a lattice calculation can be directly compared to our generic results in Euclidean signature, thus providing a new approach for evaluating the soft function and extracting the CS kernel by analytical continuation.

## Key claim

The Collins-Soper (CS) kernel derived from the QCD instanton vacuum, when supplemented with perturbative contributions, agrees well with recent lattice results and phenomenological parameterizations, but the agreement is contingent on the kernel's logarithmic dependence on large quark transverse separation, which provides a key constraint on its phenomenological parametrization.

## Method

The authors evaluate non-perturbative soft functions in the QCD instanton liquid model (ILM) by computing Wilson loops with cusp angles in Euclidean signature, using an ensemble of instantons and anti-instantons with typical size 1/3 fm and density about 1/fm^4. The soft function is constructed from half-infinite Wilson lines along directions with Euclidean angle θ, and the CS kernel is extracted from the cusp-angle dependence after subtracting the zero-angle contribution. Both weak-field (analytical) and strong-field (numerical) evaluations are performed, with the angular dependence factorizing as K(θ) ≈ K_CS × h(θ) where h(θ) = θ cot θ − 1.

## Key numbers

* Instanton size: 1/3 fm; instanton tunneling rate: about 1/fm^4
* Coulomb self-energy at large separation: about 80 MeV, in agreement with earlier estimates by Shuryak (2021)
* CS kernel values range from K_CS = 0.000 at b⊥ = 0.000 GeV⁻¹ to K_CS = −2.273 at b⊥ = 10.257 GeV⁻¹ (tabulated in 17 steps)
* In the large b⊥ limit, the weak-field CS kernel asymptotes to K_CS^(1) → −(4 n_{I+A} π² ρ⁴ / N_c) [ln(b⊥/ρ) + 1]
* The bremsstrahlung function K_B is related to the CS kernel as K_B ≈ −(1/3) K_CS in the ILM

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model approach previously applied to Wilson loops and spin forces between heavy quarks by Shuryak (2021) and to the QCD vacuum structure by Shuryak (1997), Schäfer (1996), and Diakonov (2009). The calculation provides a non-perturbative framework for the CS kernel that can be directly compared to lattice QCD extractions using quasi-TMDs as proposed by Ji (2013, 2014) and implemented by Shanahan (2020, 2021) and the Lattice Parton Collaboration (2020, 2022, 2023). The factorization of angular dependence in Euclidean signature enables a new approach: lattice calculations of the Euclidean soft function at large b⊥ with real angle θ can extract the CS kernel by analytically continuing θ → iχ, avoiding the need for the light-cone limit directly.
