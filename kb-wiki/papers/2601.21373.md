# Scaling Properties of Two-Particle-Two-Hole Responses in Asymmetric Nuclei for Neutrino Scattering within the Relativistic Mean-Field Framework

> We perform a systematic analysis of the nuclear dependence of two-particle-two-hole meson-exchange current contributions to inclusive lepton-nucleus scattering within the relativistic mean-field framework. We present microscopic calculations of nuclear responses for a set of 17 nuclei, ranging from helium to uranium, using a model with different Fermi momenta for protons and neutrons. We propose a novel scaling prescription based on the two-particle phase space and key nuclear parameters. The resulting description is accurate over a wide range of nuclear targets, with typical deviations below 10\%, and allows for a separate treatment of the different emission channels. In addition, a consistent benchmark against electron-scattering data is provided. The parametrization presented provides a practical framework for extending the responses to different nuclear targets in neutrino event generators.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the 2p2h-MEC nuclear response for an arbitrary nucleus can be predicted from that of 12C using a factorized scaling prescription based on the two-particle phase space and nuclear parameters, with typical deviations below 10%, but this accuracy is qualified by the fact that the scaling is extracted at a fixed momentum transfer of 500 MeV/c and at the peak of the transverse response, and the residual spread increases for light nuclei and for the nn→np channel.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of 2p2h-MEC nuclear responses for 17 nuclei (from 4He to 238U) within a relativistic mean-field framework, using independent Fermi momenta for protons and neutrons determined from a fit to over 20000 inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data points. They extracted scaling ratios R by taking the ratio of the maximum of the transverse response for each nucleus to that of 12C at fixed momentum transfer q=500 MeV/c, then developed a factorized parametrization that separates the response into a volume factor, a reduced phase-space factor (proportional to products of Fermi momenta cubed), and a reduced-response scaling function rho(X) modeled as a linear function of deviations in proton Fermi momentum, neutron Fermi momentum, and effective nucleon mass from their 12C values.

## Key numbers

- Scaling ratios R for neutrino-induced np→pp channel range from 0.185 for 4He to 17.90 for 238U, normalized to 1.000 for 12C
- Scaling ratios R for neutrino-induced nn→np channel range from 0.129 for 4He to 36.79 for 238U
- Scaling ratios R for electron-induced nn channel range from 0.117 for 4He to 35.95 for 238U
- The effective nucleon mass m_N*/m_N varies from 0.90 for 4He to 0.65 for 208Pb and 238U
- Proton Fermi momenta range from 160 MeV/c for 4He to 252 MeV/c for 238U; neutron Fermi momenta range from 160 MeV/c for 4He to 293 MeV/c for 238U
- The coefficient of determination R^2 for the factorized fit is 0.99 for both the e+np and nu_mu+np→mu+pp channels, and 0.95 for the e+nn channel
- The fit parameters b_3 (effective-mass coefficient) range from 1.34 (nu_mu+nn→mu+np) to 2.03 (nu_mu+np→mu+pp)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on nuclei with A=40 (Martinez-Consentino 2025) to a comprehensive set of 17 nuclei, addressing the limitation that neglecting nuclear isospin asymmetry and using equal Fermi momenta for protons and neutrons leads to non-negligible deviations in predicted nuclear responses. The scaling prescription enables controlled extrapolation from symmetric nuclei (like 12C) to neutron-rich targets such as 40Ar, 208Pb, and 238U, providing a practical framework for neutrino event generators like those used in DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, and JUNO experiments. The parametrization is benchmarked against electron-scattering data from the Quasielastic Electron Nucleus Scattering Archive and validated against recent JLab and MAMI measurements.
