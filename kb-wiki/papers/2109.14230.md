# Theoretical estimate of the half-life for the radioactive $^{134}$Cs and $^{135}$Cs in astrophysical scenarios

> We analyze the $^{134}_{55}$Cs$\rightarrow^{134}_{56}$Ba and $^{135}_{55}$Cs$\rightarrow^{135}_{56}$Ba $β^-$ decays, which are crucial production channels for Ba isotopes in Asymptotic Giant Branch (AGB) stars. We reckon, from relativistic quantum mechanis, the effects of multichannel scattering onto weak decays, including nuclear and electronic excited states (ES) populated above $\simeq$ 10 keV, for both parent and daughter nuclei. We find increases in the half-lives for $T>10^8$ K (by more than a factor 3 for $^{134}$Cs) as compared to previous works based on systematics. We also discuss our method in view of these previous calculations. An important impact on half-lives comes from nuclear ES decays, while including electronic temperatures yields further increases of about 20\% at energies 10-30 keV, typical of AGB stars of moderate mass ($M \lesssim 8~M_{\odot}$). Despite properly considering these effects, the new rates remain sensitively lower than the TY values, implying longer half-lives at least above 8-9 keV. Our rate predictions are in substantial accord with recent results based on the shell model, and strongly modify branching ratios along the $s$-process path previously adopted. With our new rate, nucleosynthesis models well account for the isotopic admixtures of Ba in presolar SiC grains and in the Sun.

## Key claim

The authors find that including both nuclear excited states and electronic temperature effects increases the half-lives of Cs-134 and Cs-135 at temperatures above 10^8 K compared to previous systematic-based estimates, but the new rates remain "sensitively lower" than the Takahashi and Yokoi (TY) values, implying longer half-lives at least above 8-9 keV.

## Method

The authors performed fully relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Fock calculations of the beta-decay transition operator, factorizing the weak Hamiltonian into leptonic and hadronic currents within a single-particle mean-field approximation. They included multichannel scattering effects from both nuclear excited states (populated above approximately 10 keV) and electronic excited states for both parent and daughter nuclei, computing the temperature and proton-density dependence of the half-lives for Cs-134 and Cs-135 in astrophysical plasmas.

## Key numbers

* Cs-134 half-life increases by more than a factor of 3 at temperatures above 10^8 K compared to previous systematic-based estimates.
* Including electronic temperature effects yields further increases of about 20% at energies of 10-30 keV, typical of AGB stars with masses less than about 8 solar masses.
* Cs-134 laboratory half-life is 2.0652 years, with a Q-value of 2058.7 keV.
* Cs-135 laboratory half-life is 2.3 x 10^6 years, with a ground-state to ground-state Q-value of 268.7 keV.
* Calculations were performed for a proton density of n_p = 10^26 cm^-3.

## Context

This work extends first-principles relativistic quantum mechanical calculations of beta-decay rates to the s-process branching point isotopes Cs-134 and Cs-135, which are crucial for barium nucleosynthesis in Asymptotic Giant Branch (AGB) stars. It improves upon the phenomenological Takahashi and Yokoi (1983, 1987) systematics and recent shell-model calculations by Li (2021), which neglected electronic degrees of freedom. The new rates strongly modify branching ratios along the s-process path and enable nucleosynthesis models to better account for barium isotopic admixtures observed in presolar silicon carbide grains and in the Sun.
