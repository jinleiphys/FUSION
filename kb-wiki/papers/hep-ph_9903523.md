# Coulomb Effects in Low Energy Proton-Proton Scattering

> Using a recently developed effective field theory for the interactions of nucleons at non-relativistic energies, we calculate non-perturbatively Coulomb corrections to proton-proton scattering. Including the dimension-eight derivative interaction in the PDS regularization scheme, we recover a modified form of the Blatt-Jackson relation between the scattering lengths. The effective range receives no corrections from the Coulomb interactions to this order. Also the case of scattering in channels where the Coulomb force is attractive, is considered. This is of importance for hadronic atoms.

## Key claim

Using an effective field theory with PDS regularization, the authors recover a field-theoretic derivation of the Jackson-Blatt relation between the Coulomb-modified and strong scattering lengths for proton-proton scattering, while showing that the effective range receives no Coulomb corrections to the order considered.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for non-relativistic nucleons below the pion mass, including a contact interaction \(C_0\) and a dimension-eight derivative interaction. They calculate the proton-proton scattering amplitude non-perturbatively by summing an infinite series of bubble diagrams where the Coulomb interaction is included via the full Coulomb Green's function and wavefunctions. The divergent integrals are regularized using dimensional regularization with the Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS) scheme, which subtracts poles in \(d=2\) as well as the usual ultraviolet poles in \(d=3\).

## Key numbers

- The proton-neutron spin-singlet scattering length is \(a_{pn} = -23.7\,\text{fm}\), and the neutron-neutron scattering length is \(a_{nn} = -18.4\,\text{fm}\).
- The experimentally measured Coulomb-modified proton-proton scattering length is \(a_C = -7.82\,\text{fm}\) with effective range \(r_0 = 2.83\,\text{fm}\).
- Using the Jackson-Blatt relation with the above effective range, the strong scattering length is found to be \(a_S = -17.0\,\text{fm}\), close to the \(nn\) scattering length.
- The coupling constant for proton-neutron scattering at \(\mu = m_\pi\) is \(C_0 = -3.54\,\text{fm}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) to include Coulomb interactions non-perturbatively, providing a field-theoretic derivation of the phenomenological analysis by Jackson and Blatt (1950) and the generalized effective range expansion of Bethe (1949). The results enable systematic calculations of Coulomb effects in other low-energy hadronic processes, such as \(\pi^+p\) or \(\pi^+\pi^+\) scattering, and are important for understanding energy level shifts in hadronic atoms like pionic atoms and pionium.
