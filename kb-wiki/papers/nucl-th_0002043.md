# A hybrid version of the tilted axis cranking model and its application to ^{128}Ba

> A hybrid version the deformed nuclear potential is suggested, which combines a spherical Woods Saxon potential with a deformed Nilsson potential. It removes the problems of the conventional Nilsson potential in the mass 130 region. Based on the hybrid potential, tilted axis cranking calculations are carried out for the magnetic dipole band in ^{128}Ba.

## Key claim

The hybrid potential, which replaces the spherical part of the Nilsson Hamiltonian with spherical Woods-Saxon single-particle energies while retaining the deformed Nilsson oscillator, resolves the discrepancies between previous Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) calculations and experimental data for the magnetic dipole band in ^(128)Ba, specifically correcting the predicted parity and band termination that the conventional Nilsson potential got wrong in this mass region.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid single-particle potential by taking the spherical single-particle energies from a Woods-Saxon potential (using universal parameters) and combining them with the deformed part of the Nilsson potential (an anisotropic harmonic oscillator). They implemented this hybrid potential into the Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) model, which uses a two-dimensional cranking term with a tilt angle ϑ relative to the intrinsic 3-axis, and applied the Strutinsky renormalization procedure to calculate the total Routhian. For the ^(128)Ba magnetic dipole band, they minimized the total Routhian with respect to deformation parameters (ε₂, γ, ε₄) and tilt angle ϑ at fixed rotational frequency ω, using both paired (80% of experimental odd-even mass difference) and unpaired calculations.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium deformation for the ^(128)Ba magnetic dipole band: ε = 0.205, γ = 0° (prolate), compared to the previous Nilsson-based TAC result of ε = 0.26, γ = 60° (oblate)
- Equilibrium tilt angle at ω = 0.2 MeV: ϑ₀ = 52.5°
- Pairing gaps used: Δ_ν = 0.88 MeV and Δ_π = 1.04 MeV (80% of experimental odd-even mass difference)
- The band could be followed up to spin 26ħ in the hybrid TAC calculation, whereas the previous Nilsson-based calculation predicted premature termination
- The 3-component of the spin vector stays at approximately ⟨J₃⟩ ≈ 9ħ across the band
- The band extends down to ω = 0.1 MeV and J = 11ħ in the calculations
- A nearby 13⁺ state lies at ΔE = 0.045 MeV from the band state

## Context

This work extends the TAC model of Frauendorf (1997, 1993) by addressing its failure for the ^(128)Ba magnetic dipole band, where earlier calculations by Dönau, Frauendorf, von Brentano et al. (1995) predicted wrong parity and too early band termination. The hybrid potential adapts the approach used for liquid sodium clusters by Reimann, Frauendorf, and Brack (1995) to nuclear structure, and the Woods-Saxon parameters follow Cwiok, Dudek, Nazarewicz et al. (1987). The results enable microscopic study of the transition between collective rotation and magnetic rotation (shears mechanism) in the A=130 region, and provide a starting point for investigating chiral rotation in triaxial nuclei as proposed by Frauendorf and Meng (1997).
