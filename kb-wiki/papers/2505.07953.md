# Dynamical Up-quark Mass Generation in QCD-like theories

> We calculate the dynamically generated up quark mass in some QCD-like theories with $F=3$ light flavors, obtained from supersymmetric QCD perturbed via anomaly mediated supersymmetry breaking. We match the low-energy effective theory to the traditional chiral Lagrangian of QCD and determine the coefficients to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, while also varying the number of colors $N$. We find that the dynamically generated up quark mass vanishes in the large $N$ limit, and is small for $F<N$, however for $F=N$ there is a sizeable $O(1)$ contribution. While our results are reliable only for small supersymmetry breaking, we observe that extrapolating the $F=N$ result to large supersymmetry breaking would lead to a dynamical up quark mass that is large enough to account for its entire physical mass.

# Dynamical Up-quark Mass Generation in QCD-like theories

## Key claim

The dynamically generated up quark mass in QCD-like theories with three light flavors is small for F < N and vanishes in the large N limit, but for F = N there is a sizeable O(1) contribution that, when extrapolated to large supersymmetry breaking beyond the regime of validity, could account for the entire physical up quark mass. The results are reliable only for small supersymmetry breaking, and the extrapolation to large supersymmetry breaking is not under perturbative control.

## Method

The authors start from supersymmetric QCD with F = 3 light flavors, perturbed by anomaly mediated supersymmetry breaking (AMSB), and derive the scalar potential for the meson superfields using the conformal compensator formalism. They match the low-energy effective theory to the traditional chiral Lagrangian of QCD by integrating out the heavy radial modes in the scalar sector at tree level, parameterizing the meson matrix as M = S U with S containing the scalar singlet and adjoint fields. The coefficients of the chiral Lagrangian are determined to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory (order p^4) for both the F < N and F = N cases, with the number of colors N varied.

## Key numbers

- For F = 3, N = 4 with extrapolation m ~ Lambda_c and mu_s/m = 0.5: mu_u/mu_d ~ 0.01
- For F = N = 3 with alpha = 2 and mu_s/m = 0.5: mu_u/mu_d ~ 0.4
- The leading-order pion decay constant for F = N = 3 is F_0 = 2 Lambda / sqrt(alpha)
- The leading-order constant B_0 for F = N = 3 is B_0 = 3 alpha m / 2
- The coefficient L_6 for F = N = 3 is L_6 = Lambda^2 / (216 alpha m^2) + 5 / (486 alpha^2 beta)
- The coefficient L_4 for F = N = 3 is L_4 = - Lambda^2 / (18 alpha m^2) + 16 / (81 alpha^2 beta)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Davies (2022) who determined low-energy constants to leading order in the large-N expansion and found small dynamical up-quark mass contributions in that limit. The paper provides an alternative first-principles calculation using supersymmetric QCD with AMSB, building on the framework of Murayama (2021) and Csaki (2022) who showed this theory has a QCD-like vacuum with chiral symmetry breaking. The results enable a potential resolution of the strong CP problem if the dynamical up quark mass from the F = N case can account for the entire physical up quark mass, making the underlying up Yukawa coupling unnecessary and the QCD theta angle unphysical.
