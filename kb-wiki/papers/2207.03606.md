# One-Loop Corrections to Dihadron Production in DIS at Small $x$

> We calculate the one-loop corrections to dihadron production in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at small x using the Color Glass Condensate formalism. We show that all UV and soft singularities cancel while the collinear divergences are absorbed into quark and anti quark-hadron fragmentation functions. Rapidity divergences lead to JIMWLK evolution of dipoles and quadrupoles describing multiple-scatterings of the quark anti-quark dipole on the target proton/nucleus. The resulting cross section is finite and can be used for phenomenological studies of dihadron angular correlations at small x in a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Key claim

The one-loop corrections to dihadron production in Deep Inelastic Scattering at small $x$ are calculated within the Color Glass Condensate formalism, showing that all ultraviolet and soft singularities cancel while collinear divergences are absorbed into quark and anti-quark hadron fragmentation functions, and rapidity divergences lead to JIMWLK evolution of dipoles and quadrupoles; the resulting cross section is finite and applicable for phenomenological studies, but the calculation is performed only for longitudinally polarized photons.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop (next-to-leading order) corrections to the leading-order dihadron production cross section in DIS at small $x$ using the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. Real corrections involve gluon radiation from the quark or anti-quark, while virtual corrections involve gluon loops; spinor helicity methods are used to evaluate the Dirac algebra. The cross section is expressed in coordinate space in terms of dipole and quadrupole Wilson line correlators, with the radiated gluon's momentum fraction integrated over.

## Key numbers

- The leading order cross section for longitudinally polarized photons contains Bessel functions $K_0(|\bx_{12}|Q_1)$ and $K_1(|\bx_{12}|Q_1)$ where $Q_i = Q\sqrt{z_i(1-z_i)}$, with $Q$ being the virtuality of the photon and $z_1, z_2$ the longitudinal momentum fractions of the quark and anti-quark.
- The real correction $\sigma_{1\times 1}$ contains a factor $z_2^3 (1-z_2)^2(z_1^2+(1-z_2)^2)$ in the numerator, integrated over the gluon momentum fraction $z$ with measure $\dd z/z$.
- The virtual correction $\sigma_9^L$ contains an integral $\int_0^{z_1} \frac{\dd z}{z}\frac{ [z_1^2+(z_1-z)^2]}{z_1^2} \int \dtwo{\bk_2} \frac{1}{\left(\bk_2-\frac{z}{z_1}\bp\right)^2}$.
- The phase space for real corrections includes a delta function $\delta(1-z_1-z_2-z)$ while virtual corrections have $\delta(1-z_1-z_2)$.

## Context

This work extends previous leading-order calculations of dihadron angular correlations in the Color Glass Condensate formalism (Kovner 2001, Jalilian-Marian 2004, Marquet 2007) and is consistent with recent one-loop studies of dijet production in DIS (Caucal 2021, Taels 2022), but provides a new treatment of collinear divergences through absorption into hadron fragmentation functions. The finite cross section enables future phenomenological studies of gluon saturation dynamics and dihadron angular correlations at a future Electron-Ion Collider.
