# Impact of dark energy on the structure of neutron stars: The vacuum case

> The potential role of a cosmic vacuum dark component in the properties of neutron stars is investigated. It is assumed that the static, spherically symmetric distribution of matter within neutron stars is supported by two distinct components: ordinary matter and a vacuum fluid. For normal matter we use a set of state-of-the-art nuclear matter equations of state, each grounded in nuclear physics experiments. The vacuum energy component is inhomogeneously distributed within the star and obeys the standard equation of state ($p_v = -ε_v$). This is characterized by an energy density fraction $y = ε_m/ (ε_m + ε_v)$, which we model as either a constant or radius-dependent. Our findings reveal that the inclusion of vacuum energy significantly affects the mass-radius relationships in neutron stars, influencing both the maximum achievable masses and the qualitative form of these relationships. Some constraints from current multimessenger observational data limiting the amount of vacuum energy within neutron stars are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a cosmic vacuum dark component (with equation of state \(p_v = -\epsilon_v\)) can coexist with ordinary nuclear matter inside neutron stars, finding that the inclusion of vacuum energy significantly alters mass-radius relationships and maximum masses, but the effect is conditional on the fraction and radial distribution of vacuum energy; the vacuum energy fraction must satisfy \(y > 1/(c_s^2+1)\) to maintain a negative pressure gradient, with an absolute lower limit of \(y > 0.5\) from causality.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for a static, spherically symmetric star containing two gravitationally coupled perfect fluids: ordinary nuclear matter and a vacuum fluid with \(p_v = -\epsilon_v\). They close the system by defining the fraction \(y = \epsilon_m/(\epsilon_m + \epsilon_v)\) either as a constant free parameter or as a density-dependent function \(y(\epsilon_m) = 1 + (\beta-1)/[1 + (\epsilon_\star/\epsilon_m)^4]\), where \(\beta\) sets the maximum vacuum fraction and \(\epsilon_\star\) controls the density threshold. For ordinary matter, they use a set of state-of-the-art nuclear equations of state (APR, MPA1, and several Skyrme-family models) that satisfy chiral effective field theory constraints up to \(1.1\,n_0\).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(\epsilon_0 = 2.7 \times 10^{14}~\mathrm{g/cm^3}\), used as the reference scale for the parameter \(\epsilon_\star\) (values of \(2\epsilon_0\), \(4\epsilon_0\), and \(8\epsilon_0\) are tested)
- Causality imposes an absolute lower limit of \(y > 0.5\) for the constant-\(y\) model; for realistic nuclear EOSs with sound speed below the conformal limit \(1/\sqrt{3}\), the constraint becomes \(y > 0.75\), allowing at most 25% vacuum energy at each layer
- For the APR EOS with \(\epsilon_\star = 4\epsilon_0\), results for \(\beta \lesssim 0.96\) become incompatible with the \(2M_\odot\) pulsar constraint; for \(\epsilon_\star = 8\epsilon_0\), a broader range \(\beta \lesssim 0.87\) remains consistent with the \(2M_\odot\) constraint
- The chiral EFT constraint on pressure is known to roughly \(\pm 20\%\) accuracy at nuclear saturation density \(n_0\)

## Context

This work extends the "admixed" star framework previously applied to dark matter (e.g., Li (2012), Baym (2018), Ellis (2018)) to the case of dark energy, proposing that vacuum energy and ordinary matter can coexist throughout the star rather than being confined to a pure dark energy core as in earlier "dark energy star" models by Chapline (2004) or gravastar models by Mazur and Mottola (2001). The results enable observational constraints on vacuum energy in neutron stars using multimessenger data from pulsars like PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2020, Fonseca 2021), NICER observations (Miller 2021, Riley 2021), and the GW170817 merger event (LIGO/Virgo 2017).
