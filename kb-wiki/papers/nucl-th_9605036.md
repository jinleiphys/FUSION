# Probing hadron structure by real and virtual photons

> The importance of subnuclear degrees of freedom in photon absorption and electron scattering by nuclei is discussed. After a short introduction into the basic properties of one-photon processes and a very brief survey on the nuclear response in the various regions of energy and momentum transfers, the particular role of subnuclear degrees of freedom in terms of meson and isobar degrees of freedom is considered. Their importance is illustrated by several reactions on the deuteron which are currently under study at c.w.\ electron machines like MAMI in Mainz.

## Key claim
Subnuclear degrees of freedom (meson exchange currents and isobar configurations) are essential for describing photon absorption and electron scattering on nuclei, but their extraction from data is complicated by a unitary ambiguity that makes the separation of resonant and background contributions representation-dependent.

## Method
The paper reviews one-photon processes (real and virtual) in nuclei, focusing on deuteron breakup reactions. It compares nonrelativistic calculations with those including meson exchange currents (MEC), isobar configurations (IC), and relativistic corrections (RC) for inclusive and exclusive electron scattering and photodisintegration. For the Δ-resonance region, a coupled-channel NN-NΔ approach with explicit pion degrees of freedom is used, and the unitary ambiguity in extracting resonance multipoles from pion photoproduction is demonstrated via a model with a unitary transformation parameter.

## Key numbers
- MEC and IC give about a 10 percent enhancement in thermal n-p radiative capture (M1 transition).
- In deuteron electrodisintegration near threshold at backward angles, the transverse form factor shows a large discrepancy resolved only by adding MEC and IC (Fig. 1, from SiB79).
- For deuteron photodisintegration total cross section, the full coupled-channel calculation shifts the maximum position towards lower energies compared to the impulse approximation (IA), which peaks near 320 MeV; the full calculation with modified γNΔ-coupling achieves good agreement with experiment over the whole energy range (200–450 MeV).
- The longitudinal structure function fL in the Δ region (Enp=240 MeV, q²=5 fm⁻²) shows a sizeable enhancement from the double ΔΔ component at higher momentum transfer (Fig. 6).

## Context
This work extends the long-standing program of using electromagnetic probes to study nuclear structure, from Rutherford scattering to deep inelastic scattering (Friedman, Kendall, Taylor, 1991). It specifically builds on prior studies of meson exchange currents and isobar configurations in the deuteron (ArS 91, RiB72, Are81b) and addresses the need for consistent relativistic treatments (ATA89) and coupled-channel dynamics (LeA 87, TaO 89). The results enable more precise tests of subnuclear degrees of freedom at continuous-wave electron machines like MAMI in Mainz, while the unitary ambiguity (WiW96) highlights the need for model-independent extraction of resonance properties.
