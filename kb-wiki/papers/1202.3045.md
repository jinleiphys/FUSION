# Reviving old, almost lost knowledge on T and K matrix poles and a link to the contemporary QCD spectrum

> The old knowledge about interrelation among T-matrix, K-matrix and bare poles is summarized and put into modern perspective.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when comparing lattice QCD results with experimental baryon spectra, the comparison should be made with T-matrix pole parameters rather than Breit-Wigner parameters, because Breit-Wigner parameters are inherently model dependent while T-matrix poles offer minimal model dependence; however, the exact identification between QCD resolvent singularities and scattering theory S-matrix singularities is plausible but lacks a rigorous proof.

## Method

The author reviews and revives the formal interrelation among T-matrix, K-matrix, and bare poles within scattering theory, using the multi-resonance Breit-Wigner formalism with a Hermitian width matrix and no background as a starting point. The analysis then generalizes to cases with constant background, energy-dependent background represented as a sum of unphysical poles, and fully energy-dependent background, showing how the one-to-one correspondence between T and K matrix poles is affected. The author also distinguishes between genuine resonances (with a corresponding nearby K-matrix pole) and dynamic resonances (generated by distant poles or background effects without a related bound-state singularity).

## Key numbers

- The lattice QCD calculation by Aoki et al. (2008) approached the physical pion mass limit with m_pi = 156 MeV, but the comparison with experimental nucleon mass spectrum is still done using Breit-Wigner parameters.
- The paper discusses the characteristic equation for obtaining K-matrix poles from T-matrix poles: det[(E - epsilon) delta_{mu mu'} + (1/2) i sum_k v_{mu k} v_{mu k'}] = 0, and the converse transformation for obtaining T-matrix poles from K-matrix poles: det[(E - epsilon) delta_{mu mu'} + (1/2) i sum_k v_{mu k} v_{mu k'}] = 0.

## Context

This work extends the old analyses by Aitchison (1972), Rosenfeld (1970), and McVoy (1969) on the interrelation of T and K matrix poles, and responds to the contemporary lattice QCD calculations by Aoki et al. (2008) that approach the physical pion mass. The paper aims to clarify the confusion introduced by Anisovich et al. (mid-1990s) who defined bare states as K-matrix poles with a specific mass-shift definition that differs from the standard field theory definition, and it advocates for reorganizing the PDG format to explicitly accentuate the model dependence of Breit-Wigner parameters. The work enables a more rigorous comparison between lattice QCD resonant states and experimental scattering data by identifying T-matrix poles as the optimal choice for such comparisons.
