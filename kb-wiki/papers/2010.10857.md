# Properties of dibaryons in nuclear medium

> Properties of six-quark dibaryons in nuclear medium are considered by example of $A=6$ nuclei within the three-cluster $α+2N$ model. Dibaryon production in nuclei leads to the appearance of a three-body force between the dibaryon and nuclear core. This non-conventional scalar force is shown to provide an additional attractive contribution to the three-body binding energy. This three-body contribution improves noticeably agreement between theoretical results and experimental data for the majority of observables. The most serious difference between the traditional $NN$-force models and the dibaryon-induced model is found for the nucleon momentum distribution, the latter model providing a strong enrichment of the high-momentum components both for $^6$Li and $^6$He cases.

## Key claim

The dibaryon-induced model for the \(NN\) interaction, when applied to \(A=6\) nuclei within the three-cluster \(\alpha+2N\) model, produces a three-body force that provides additional attraction and improves agreement with experimental data for most observables; the most significant difference from traditional \(NN\)-force models appears in the nucleon momentum distribution, where the dibaryon model enriches high-momentum components for both \(^6\)Li and \(^6\)He.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for the \(\alpha+2N\) system using a variational method on a non-orthogonal multiscale Gaussian basis, with an iterative procedure to handle the energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian. They employed two alternative \(NN\) potentials: the traditional Reid Soft Core (RSC) potential and the dibaryon-induced potential, which includes an effective energy-dependent \(NN\) interaction derived from coupling to an intermediate six-quark dibaryon state dressed by a \(\sigma\)-meson field, plus a three-body force from dibaryon–\(\alpha\) interaction. The \(N\alpha\) interaction was taken as the quasilocal MS potential with a forbidden \(0S\) state.

## Key numbers

- The dibaryon model parameters fitted to \(NN\) scattering phase shifts up to \(T_{\rm lab}=1\) GeV include: for the \(^3S_1-{}^3D_1\) channel, \(\lambda_{00} = -328.55\) MeV, \(\lambda_{22} = -15.65\) MeV, \(\lambda_{02} = 44.06\) MeV, \(E_0 = 693\) MeV; for the \(^1S_0\) channel, \(\lambda_{00} = -328.9\) MeV, \(E_0 = 693\) MeV.
- The weight of the internal dibaryon component in the deuteron was found to be \(P_{\rm in} = 3.66\%\).
- The RSC potential fits empirical \(NN\) phase shifts only until \(T_{\rm lab} = 300\) MeV, while the dibaryon-induced model describes them up to 1 GeV.
- The three-body force provides an additional attractive contribution to the three-body binding energy, improving agreement with experimental data for \(^6\)He and \(^6\)Li, where previous \(\alpha+2N\) model calculations had disagreements of about 0.5–0.8 MeV for binding energies and 5% error for rms charge radii.

## Context

This work extends the dibaryon-induced model, previously applied to the deuteron and three-nucleon systems (\(^3\)H, \(^3\)He) by Kukulin et al. (2001, 2019), to \(A=6\) nuclei within the three-cluster \(\alpha+2N\) model developed by Voronchev et al. (1982–1995). It contrasts with traditional meson-exchange potentials (e.g., RSC) and modern EFT-based approaches, which have failed to resolve several long-standing puzzles in few-nucleon systems (e.g., the \(A_y\) puzzle, Sagara puzzle). The results enable further study of dibaryon properties in nuclear matter, the nature of Cooper pairs in nuclei, and the relation between short-range correlations and high-momentum components in nuclei.
