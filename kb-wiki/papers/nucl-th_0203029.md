# Extended RPA within a solvable 3 level model

> Working within an exactly solvable 3 level model, we discuss am extension of the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) based on a boson formalism. A boson Hamiltonian is defined via a mapping procedure and its expansion truncated at four-boson terms. RPA-type equations are then constructed and solved iteratively. The new solutions gain in stability with respect to the RPA ones. We perform diagonalizations of the boson Hamiltonian in spaces containing up to four-phonon components. Approximate spectra exhibit an improved quality with increasing the size of these multiphonon spaces. Special attention is addressed to the problem of the anharmonicity of the spectrum.

# Extended RPA within a solvable 3 level model

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that an extended Random Phase Approximation (ERPA) based on a boson formalism truncated at four-boson terms yields solutions that remain stable near the RPA collapse point, but the quality of the approximate spectra depends strongly on which specific multiphonon state is examined, with some states showing marked anharmonicity while others remain nearly harmonic.

## Method
The authors work within an exactly solvable three-level SU(3) model with 2Ω = 10 particles, where a fermion Hamiltonian is mapped onto a boson Hamiltonian via a Marumori-type mapping procedure truncated at four-boson terms. RPA-type equations are constructed using the equations of motion method with double commutators evaluated in the ground state, yielding nonlinear equations that are solved iteratively (ERPA). The boson Hamiltonian is then diagonalized in multiphonon spaces containing up to two-, three-, and four-phonon configurations, and the resulting spectra are compared with exact fermion solutions.

## Key numbers
- Single-particle energies are set to ε(1) = ε and ε(2) = 2.5ε, with interaction strength parameter τ = (2Ωχ)/ε where 2Ω = 10 particles.
- RPA undergoes a collapse around τ ≈ 0.28, while ERPA solutions remain stable and close to reference values in this region.
- For the state |11⟩ (double excitation of the lowest phonon), the anharmonicity ratio R grows rapidly with increasing τ; for the state |22⟩, R remains almost exactly zero; for the mixed state |12⟩, only moderate anharmonicity is observed.
- Ground state energies from multiphonon diagonalizations converge toward exact values as the space is enlarged from two-phonon to four-phonon spaces.

## Context
This work extends the boson formalism approach previously applied to atomic nuclei by Catara et al. (2000) and Lanza et al. (2000), where anharmonicities in 208Pb were found to be small (a few hundred keV). It also follows a similar analysis by Volpe et al. (2000) using a two-level model. The results agree with findings by two-electron jellium calculations showing that some vibrational bands are nearly harmonic while others are not, and the paper enables systematic testing of truncation schemes in boson mapping approaches for nuclear structure and metal cluster physics.
