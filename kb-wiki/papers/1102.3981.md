# Selected topics on Hadrons in Nuclei

> In this talk we report on selected topics on hadrons in nuclei. The first topic is the renormalization of the width of the $Λ(1520)$ in a nuclear medium. This is followed by a short update of the situation of the $ω$ in the medium. The investigation of the properties of $\bar{K}$ in the nuclear medium from the study of the $(K_{flight},p)$ reaction is also addressed, as well as properties of X,Y,Z charmed and hidden charm resonances in a nuclear medium. Finally we address the novel issue of multimeson states.

# Selected topics on Hadrons in Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper surveys several hadron-in-medium phenomena, arguing that many-body effects can dramatically modify resonance properties (e.g., the width of the Λ(1520) increases from 15 MeV in vacuum to about 70 MeV at normal nuclear density), but also that some experimental claims of large in-medium mass shifts (e.g., for the ω meson) are not confirmed when background treatments and experimental coincidences are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors present a series of theoretical studies using chiral unitary approaches and coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations to describe dynamically generated resonances in nuclear matter. For the Λ(1520), they compute the in-medium width by allowing the πΣ(1385) component to decay via particle-hole excitations. For the ω meson, they reanalyze the TAPS/ELSA data and show that the invariant mass spectrum is insensitive to mass shifts within ±80 MeV. For the (K⁻,N) reaction on ¹²C, they simulate the experimental coincidence requirement and find that it drastically changes the cross section shape, invalidating earlier claims of a deep K⁻-nucleus potential. For charmed mesons, they compute the D and D̄ self-energies in matter and use them to obtain the in-medium widths of the D_{s0}(2317) and X(3700) resonances. For multi-ρ states, they use the fixed-center approximation to Faddeev equations to generate a sequence of resonances from 2 to 6 ρ mesons.

## Key numbers

- Λ(1520) width: 15 MeV in vacuum, increasing to about 70 MeV at ρ = ρ₀ (normal nuclear matter density)
- ω meson width in medium: about 100 to 150 MeV at nuclear matter density, compared to 8.4 MeV in vacuum
- For the (K⁻,N) reaction, the minimal coincidence requirement reduces the calculated cross section by about a factor of three relative to the calculation without coincidence
- D_{s0}(2317) develops a width of about 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (from zero width in vacuum)
- X(3700) develops a width of about 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (from small width in vacuum)
- The multi-ρ model reproduces the masses of f₂(1270), ρ₃(1690), f₄(2050), ρ₅(2350), and f₆(2510) with no free parameters

## Context

This conference summary extends the program of dynamically generated hadron resonances from chiral unitary theory (Oller 2000, Oset 2009) to nuclear medium effects. It contradicts the earlier claim of a 200 MeV attractive mass shift for the ω meson by Trnka (2005) and the claim of a 200 MeV deep K⁻-nucleus potential by Kishimoto (2007), showing that both conclusions relied on insufficiently controlled experimental analyses. The work enables future studies of charmed mesons in nuclei at FAIR and motivates searches for multi-meson states at facilities like BELLE, COMPASS, and WASA/COSY. The multi-ρ description of high-spin mesons (Roca 2010) provides an alternative to the conventional qq̄ interpretation of these states.
