# $Ω$ baryon spectrum and their decays in a constituent quark model

> Combining the recent developments of the observations of $Ω$ sates we calculate the $Ω$ spectrum up to the $N=2$ shell within a nonrelativistic constituent quark potential model. Furthermore, the strong and radiative decay properties for the $Ω$ resonances within the $N=2$ shell are evaluated by using the masses and wave functions obtained from the potential model. It is found that the newly observed $Ω(2012)$ resonance is most likely to be the spin-parity $J^P=3/2^-$ $1P$-wave state $Ω(1^{2}P_{3/2^{-}})$, it also has a large potential to be observed in the $Ω(1672)γ$ channel. Our calculation shows that the 1$P$-, 1$D$-, and 2$S$-wave $Ω$ baryons have a relatively narrow decay width of less than 50 MeV. Based on the obtained decay properties and mass spectrum, we further suggest optimum channels and mass regions to find the missing $Ω$ resonances via the strong and/or radiative decay processes.

## Key claim

The newly observed $\Omega(2012)$ resonance is most likely the spin-parity $J^P=3/2^-$ $1P$-wave state $\Omega(1^{2}P_{3/2^{-}})$, based on consistency of both mass and decay properties with observations, though the abstract does not mention that the assignment depends on fitting the model parameters to four specific $\Omega$ resonances including $\Omega(2012)$ itself.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger equation for a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a Coulomb-plus-linear confining potential and spin-dependent interactions (spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit terms) using a variational method with a trial spatial wave function expanded in harmonic oscillator basis functions. The six model parameters were determined by fitting the masses of four $\Omega$ resonances: the ground state $\Omega(1672)$, the newly observed $\Omega(2012)$, the predicted $\Omega(1^{2}P_{1/2^{-}})$ around 1950 MeV, and the $\Omega(2250)$ assigned as $\Omega(1^{4}D_{5/2^{+}})$. Strong and radiative decay widths were then calculated using the resulting masses and wave functions.

## Key numbers

* Ground state $\Omega(1672)$ mass: 1672 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental 1672.45 MeV.
* $\Omega(2012)$ mass: 2012 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental 2012.4 $\pm$ 0.7 (stat.) $\pm$ 0.6 (syst.) MeV with width 6.4$^{+2.5}_{-2.0}$ (stat.) $\pm$ 0.6 (syst.) MeV.
* $\Omega(1^{2}P_{1/2^{-}})$ mass: 1957 MeV (calculated).
* $\Omega(1^{4}D_{5/2^{+}})$ mass: 2252 MeV (calculated), assigned to the experimental $\Omega(2250)$.
* All 1$P$-, 1$D$-, and 2$S$-wave $\Omega$ baryons have decay widths less than 50 MeV.
* Mass splitting between the two 2$S$-wave states $\Omega(2^{2}S_{1/2^{+}})$ at 2232 MeV and $\Omega(2^{4}S_{3/2^{+}})$ at 2159 MeV is $\Delta m \simeq 70$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the $\Omega$ baryon spectrum using nonrelativistic quark models (Chao 1980, Pervin 2007, Chen 2009) and relativized quark models (Capstick 1986, Faustov 2015), and complements studies using simple harmonic oscillator wave functions in the chiral quark model (Xiao 2018) and quark pair creation model (Wang 2018). The results enable experimental searches for missing $\Omega$ resonances by suggesting optimum strong and radiative decay channels and mass regions, particularly for the 1$P$-, 1$D$-, and 2$S$-wave states.
