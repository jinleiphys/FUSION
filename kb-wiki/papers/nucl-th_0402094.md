# Quasiparticle random phase approximation based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model II: Nuclear spin and isospin excitations

> The proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) is formulated in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, for an effective Lagrangian characterized by density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings. The model includes both the T=1 and T=0 pairing channels. Pair configurations formed from the fully or partially occupied states of positive energy in the Fermi sea, and the empty negative-energy states from the Dirac sea, are included in PN-RQRPA configuration space. The model is applied to the analysis of charge-exchange modes: isobaric analog resonances and Gamow-Teller resonances.

## Key claim

The proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) formulated in the canonical basis of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, using the density-dependent effective interaction DD-ME1 and including both T=1 and T=0 pairing channels, reproduces experimental excitation energies of isobaric analog resonances (IAR) and Gamow-Teller resonances (GTR) in doubly closed-shell and open-shell nuclei, provided that the configuration space includes pair configurations formed from occupied positive-energy states and empty negative-energy states from the Dirac sea, and that the Landau-Migdal parameter is adjusted to g' = 0.55 for GTR calculations.

## Method

The PN-RQRPA matrix equations were formulated in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the RHB model for spherical nuclei, using the density-dependent meson-nucleon effective interaction DD-ME1 in the particle-hole channel and the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the T=1 particle-particle channel, with a Gaussian interaction for the T=0 proton-neutron pairing channel. The residual particle-hole interaction included ρ-meson exchange with density-dependent coupling and π-meson exchange with pseudovector coupling, plus a zero-range Landau-Migdal term with parameter g' = 0.55 for GTR calculations. The configuration space included two-quasiparticle states from bound levels, continuum states, and pair configurations formed from occupied positive-energy states and empty negative-energy states from the Dirac sea.

## Key numbers

- For IAR in ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb: calculated excitation energies agree with experimental data from (p,n) scattering (And. 1985, Bai. 1980, Wak. 1997, Aki. 1995)
- For Sn isotopes A=112-124: largest difference between theoretical and experimental IAR excitation energies is approximately 200 keV
- For the pion-nucleon coupling: m_π = 138.0 MeV and f_π²/4π = 0.08
- For the Landau-Migdal parameter: g' = 0.55 adjusted to reproduce the GTR excitation energy in ²⁰⁸Pb
- The Lorentzian folding width used for strength distributions: Γ = 1 MeV
- The Fermi non-energy weighted sum rule: S_β⁻(F) = N - Z

## Context

This work extends the relativistic QRPA formalism of Paar (2003) from the isoscalar/isovector multipole response to charge-exchange spin and isospin excitations, using density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (DD-ME1) instead of nonlinear meson self-interactions. It builds on the non-relativistic proton-neutron QRPA of Engel (1999) for β-decay rates of r-process nuclei, and on the relativistic RPA calculations of IAR and GTR in closed-shell nuclei by Con (1998, 2000) and Ma (2003), which used the NL3 interaction with π- and ρ-meson exchange. The inclusion of negative-energy Dirac sea states addresses the finding of Kur (2003) that such configurations affect the Gamow-Teller sum rule. The model enables systematic studies of charge-exchange modes in open-shell nuclei far from stability, relevant for nuclear astrophysics and the determination of neutron skin thickness from the energy spacing between GTR and IAR.
