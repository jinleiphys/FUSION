# Parity violation in atoms: neutrino-mediated long range forces and finite nuclear size

> We consider neutral-current parity-violating interactions in an atom mediated by the exchange of a neutrino-antineutrino pair. We explicitly account for the nuclear finite size encoded in the nuclear form factor. Based on its general properties, we derive an effective neutrino-mediated potential and determine its properties at short and long distances. We demonstrate that, once the form factor properties are correctly accounted for, the range of such an effective potential corresponds to the nuclear radius, removing any sensitivity to shorter-distance contributions. This potential changes sign over the atom's volume, so that the correction to the effective nuclear weak charge induced by this interaction is tiny and does not alter the interpretation of atomic parity violation experiments.

# Parity violation in atoms: neutrino-mediated long range forces and finite nuclear size

## Key claim

The authors argue that once nuclear finite size is correctly accounted for via general form factor properties (analyticity, normalization, and superconvergence relations), the effective range of the neutrino-pair-mediated parity-violating potential is set by the nuclear radius rather than by the Z-boson mass, reducing the correction to the nuclear weak charge to a tiny relative effect of order 10^{-14}, far below the 0.3% experimental precision in cesium and contradicting earlier claims of a 0.8% modification.

## Method

The authors derive the effective potential for neutrino-antineutrino exchange in atomic parity-violating interactions using a dispersion relation formalism, incorporating the nuclear form factor through its spectral representation. They compute the cumulative strength parameter κ (integral of the potential over all space) and the finite-volume integral over the atomic volume, applying superconvergence relations that follow from the asymptotic behavior of hadronic form factors. The analysis is performed in the 4-Fermi effective theory limit, with the form factor's analytic structure and pQCD scaling rules constraining the short-distance behavior.

## Key numbers

- The claimed 0.8% modification of the nuclear weak charge from neutrino-pair exchange (Ghosh et al., 2024-2025; Flambaum and Samsonov, 2026) is reduced to a relative correction of ~10^{-14} × (Z/55)^2 when nuclear finite size is included.
- For cesium-133 (Z=55, R≈4.8 fm), the product Zα m_e R ≈ 0.005, justifying the approximation F(t)=1 over the atomic volume.
- The cumulative strength of the finite-size corrected neutrino-pair potential integrates to zero over all space: κ^{FS}_{νν̄} = 0.
- The finite-volume integral yields δQ_W / Q_W = G_F (Zα m)^2 / (3√2 π²) ~ 10^{-14} for Z=55.
- The Z-boson mass is M_Z ≈ 91 GeV, corresponding to a range M_Z^{-1} ≈ 0.002 fm.
- The experimental measurement of parity violation in cesium (Wood et al., 1997) has 0.3% precision.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the recent claims by Ghosh et al. (2024, 2025) and Flambaam and Samsonov (2026) that neutrino-pair exchange could modify the apparent nuclear weak charge at the 0.1-0.8% level, potentially affecting the interpretation of atomic parity violation experiments. The authors revive and extend arguments from Tamm (1934) and Iwanenko (1936) about the impossibility of sensitivity to short-distance processes in atomic systems, and apply the dispersion formalism of Feinberg and Sucher (1968, 1989) to incorporate nuclear finite size rigorously. The result enables continued interpretation of atomic parity violation measurements as clean probes of electroweak physics without concern for this particular higher-order correction, though the authors note that purely leptonic systems like positronium and muonium (where leptons have no intrinsic size) may still be affected as argued by Ghosh et al.
