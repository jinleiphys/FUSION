# Photoproduction of lepton pair in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Dilepton production provides a unique probe of the strong electromagnetic field produced in heavy-ion collisions. To map out the behavior of its transverse momentum broadening, we present a theoretical model based on the equivalent photon approximation, and then we update it to make direct comparisons with the recent experimental measurements. We find that the model calculations can describe well, not only the average transverse momentum squared of $e^{+}e^{-}$ pairs in Au--Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV, but also the acoplanarity of $μ^{+}μ^{-}$ pairs in Pb--Pb collisions at$\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV. Furthermore, the model predictions are also able to reproduce the measured dependencies of the pair mass and the transverse momentum squared.

## Key claim

The authors present a Monte-Carlo-based hybrid model that updates the traditional equivalent photon approximation (EPA) to describe lepton pair photoproduction in hadronic heavy-ion collisions with nuclear overlap, finding that the model can describe the measured transverse momentum broadening and invariant mass spectra without requiring additional higher-order QED corrections or hot medium effects.

## Method

The model calculates the differential cross section for the Breit-Wheeler process (gamma+gamma -> l+l-) using the traditional EPA with scalar and pseudoscalar two-photon distribution functions derived from the electromagnetic fields of colliding nuclei. The single photon transverse momentum is sampled according to the differential form of the one-photon distribution function obtained by integrating over the full transverse plane perpendicular to the moving nucleus. A Monte-Carlo-based setup then samples gamma-gamma pairs according to the differential cross section at given invariant mass, rapidity, and impact parameter, performs the two-body decay kinematics for lepton pair production, and boosts results from the center-of-mass frame to the laboratory frame for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- In Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the scalar contribution to the impact-parameter-dependent cross section shows a dip structure at b approximately 9 fm, while the pseudoscalar contribution has a maximum at b approximately 12 fm
- The differential yield of e+e- pairs in 60-80% Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV peaks at M^ee approximately 0.5 GeV/c^2 and decreases exponentially toward larger M^ee
- In 60-80% Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the model predicts sqrt(<p_T^2>) values that increase with M^ee, with the maximum value reaching 1.5 times larger than in ultra-peripheral collisions at b approximately 13 fm
- In 70-90% Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, the model predicts sqrt(<p_T^2>) = 39 MeV/c for 0.4 <= M^ee <= 0.7 GeV/c^2, 43 MeV/c for 0.7 <= M^ee <= 1.1 GeV/c^2, and 45 MeV/c for 1.1 <= M^ee <= 2.7 GeV/c^2

## Context

This work extends the traditional EPA approach (Vidovic 1992, Klein 2017) by implementing a Monte-Carlo-based strategy that allows impact-parameter-dependent calculations for events with nuclear overlap, addressing the difficulty noted by Brandenburg (2022) that traditional approaches have trouble computing single-lepton kinematic distributions. The model provides an alternative to other approaches such as the Wigner formalism (Klusek-Gawenda 2021), STARLight (Klein 2017), SuperChic3, and Gamma-UPC (Shao 2022), and its ability to describe data without additional higher-order QED corrections (Li 2023) or hot medium effects suggests these effects may be smaller than previously thought, though the authors note that future high-precision measurements, particularly in more central collisions, are needed to quantify the spread of dilepton p_T distributions.
