# De Haas - van Alphen Effect under Rotation

> We explored the interplay between magnetic field and rotation in the de Hass - van Alphen oscillation. The effect is found to be reduced because of the re-weighting of different states within the same Landau level by rotation energy. The implications of our results on high energy physics and condensed matter physics are speculated.

## Key claim

Rotation reduces the de Haas-van Alphen oscillation amplitude because the rotation energy re-weights different angular momentum states within the same Landau level, lifting the degeneracy; the suppression depends on the product of angular velocity and system radius, and the effect is completely suppressed for typical neutron star parameters but may remain observable for a cold dense QGP droplet.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for massless fermions and the Schrodinger equation for non-relativistic electrons in cylindrical coordinates with a constant magnetic field parallel to the cylinder axis, using the circular gauge. They computed the thermodynamic pressure in the grand canonical ensemble with a chemical potential and an angular velocity, applied the Poisson summation formula to extract the oscillatory (dHvA) term, and introduced a sharp cutoff M_c = [eB R^2/2] on the angular momentum summation to account for finite-size effects in the thermodynamic approximation.

## Key numbers

- For a neutron star quark core: P_dHvA / P_dHvA(omega=0) ~ 2/(mu R v) ~ 3.86e-16 / [mu(MeV) R(km) v], leading to suppression by 17 orders of magnitude for typical parameters (mu ~ few hundred MeV, R ~ 1 km, v ~ 0.015)
- For a non-relativistic electron gas: the critical condition for significant suppression is m_c omega / omega_B ~ 0.43 R v (with R in cm, v in cm/s), independent of magnetic field
- For a cold dense QGP droplet with R = 10 fm, the dHvA oscillation remains present and its suppression with temperature becomes milder when omega is nonzero compared to the omega=0 case
- For non-relativistic electrons: omega / omega_B ~ 5.57e-12 * omega(Hz) / B(Tesla) for typical laboratory parameters

## Context

This work extends the standard theory of the de Haas-van Alphen effect (Onuki, 2004; Jiang, 2006) to rotating systems, motivated by rotating magnetized matter in heavy-ion collisions (Becattini, 2021; Chen, 2015; Fukushima, 2020) and neutron stars (Felipe, 2007; Watanabe, 2022). The results enable estimates of magnetic oscillation suppression in quark matter cores and dense QGP droplets, and suggest testable predictions for rotating metallic samples in condensed matter experiments.
