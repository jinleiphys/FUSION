# Bottomonium production in pp and heavy-ion collisions

> We study bottomonium $b\bar b$ production in pp collisions as well as in heavy-ion collisions, using a quantal density matrix approach. The initial bottom (anti)quarks are provided by the PYTHIA event generator. We solve the Schrödinger equation for the $b\bar b$ pair, identifying the potential with the free energy, calculated with lattice QCD, to obtain the temperature dependent $b\bar b$ density matrix as well as the dissociation temperature. The formation of bottomonium is given by projection of the bottomonium density matrix onto the density matrix of the system. With this approach we describe the rapidity and transverse momentum distribution of the $Υ$(nS) in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV extending a similar calculation for the charmonium states \cite{Song:2017phm}. We employ the Remler formalism to study the $b\bar b$ production in heavy ion collisions in which the heavy quarks scatter elastically with partons from the quark gluon plasma (QGP). The elastic scattering of heavy (anti)quark in QGP is realized by the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) and the expanding QGP is modeled by PHSD. We find that a reduction to 10 \% of the scattering cross section for a (anti)bottom quark with a QGP parton reproduces the experimental data. This suggests that due to color neutrality the scattering cross section of the small $b\bar b$ system with a parton is considerably smaller than twice the bottom-parton scattering cross section.

## Key claim

The authors claim that bottomonium production in both pp and heavy-ion collisions can be described using a quantal density matrix approach with no free parameters, but only after reducing the scattering cross section for a bottom (anti)quark with a QGP parton to 10% of its nominal value to reproduce experimental data in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for bottomonium states using a temperature-dependent heavy quark potential identified with the free energy from lattice QCD, obtaining temperature-dependent wave functions and dissociation temperatures. For pp collisions, initial bottom quarks are generated by PYTHIA and the bottomonium formation probability is computed by projecting the Wigner-transformed density matrix of the quarkonium eigenstates onto the classical phase space distribution of the heavy quark pair. For heavy-ion collisions, the Remler formalism is employed to track how the Wigner projection onto each bottomonium state changes whenever a heavy (anti)quark scatters elastically with partons from the quark-gluon plasma, with the QGP evolution modeled by PHSD and the elastic scattering by the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM).

## Key numbers

- The dissociation temperature of Υ(1S) is about 3.3 T_c, while that of excited bottomonium states is less than 1.2 T_c.
- The radii of Υ(1S), χ_b(1P), Υ(2S), χ_b(2P) and Υ(3S) used in pp collisions are 0.22 fm, 0.22 fm, 0.3 fm, 0.24 fm and 0.38 fm, respectively.
- In pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the contributions to inclusive Υ(1S) from direct production, χ_b(1P) feed-down, Υ(2S) feed-down, χ_b(2P) feed-down and Υ(3S) feed-down are 62%, 23%, 7%, 7% and 1%, respectively.
- A reduction to 10% of the scattering cross section for a (anti)bottom quark with a QGP parton reproduces the experimental data in heavy-ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends the quantal density matrix approach previously applied to charmonium production by Song (2017) to bottomonium states, demonstrating that the same formalism describes both c-cbar and b-bbar quarkonium production. The approach contrasts with the Color Octet Model (Braaten, 1994), Color Evaporation Model (Amundson, 1996), and Color Singlet Model (Chang, 1979) by containing no free parameters for the non-perturbative hadronization step. The heavy-ion results enable future studies of the relative importance of primordial production versus regeneration in bottomonium production, and the finding that the effective scattering cross section is much smaller than twice the single bottom-parton cross section suggests that color neutrality of the small b-bbar system strongly reduces its interaction with the medium.
