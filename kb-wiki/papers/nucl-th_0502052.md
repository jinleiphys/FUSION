# Isospin-violating nucleon-nucleon forces using the method of unitary transformation

> Recently, we have derived the leading and subleading isospin-breaking three-nucleon forces using the method of unitary transformation. In the present work we extend this analysis and consider the corresponding two-nucleon forces using the same approach. Certain contributions to the isospin-violating one- and two-pion-exchange potential have already been discussed by various groups within the effective field theory framework. Our findings agree with the previously obtained results. In addition, we present the expressions for the subleading charge-symmetry-breaking two-pion exchange potential which were not considered before. These corrections turn out to be numerically important. Together with the three-nucleon force results presented in our previous work, the results of the present study specify completely isospin-violating nuclear forces up to the order $q^5/Λ^5$, where $q$ ($Λ$) denotes the soft (hard) scale.

## Key claim

The authors derive the complete set of isospin-violating two-nucleon forces up to order \(q^5/\Lambda^5\) using the method of unitary transformation, confirming previously known results for one- and two-pion-exchange contributions while presenting for the first time the subleading charge-symmetry-breaking two-pion-exchange potential, which they find to be numerically important.

## Method

The authors employ the method of unitary transformation to derive energy-independent, Hermitian nucleon-nucleon potentials from the chiral effective Lagrangian, using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit isospin-breaking terms from quark mass differences and electromagnetic interactions. They count isospin-violating effects according to the power counting rules \(\epsilon \sim e \sim q/\Lambda\) and \(e^2/(4\pi)^2 \sim q^4/\Lambda^4\), and evaluate contributions to the one- and two-pion-exchange potentials as well as contact interactions up to order \(\nu = 5\) in the low-momentum expansion. The calculation incorporates renormalized pion fields and masses, nucleon mass shifts, and the relevant low-energy constants fixed from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally known pion mass difference \(M_{\pi^\pm} - M_{\pi^0} = 4.6\) MeV fixes the low-energy constant \(C = 5.9 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^4\).
- The strong contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is \((m_p - m_n)^{\text{str}} = -2.05 \pm 0.3\) MeV, and the electromagnetic contribution is \((m_p - m_n)^{\text{em}} = 0.76 \pm 0.3\) MeV.
- The low-energy constants extracted from these mass shifts are \(c_5 = -0.09 \pm 0.01\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(f_2 = -0.45 \pm 0.19\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The natural scale for the LEC \(C\) is estimated as \(F_\pi^2 \Lambda^2 / (4\pi)^2 \sim 3 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^4\) with \(\Lambda \sim M_\rho\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of leading and subleading isospin-breaking three-nucleon forces (Epelbaum and Meissner, 2004) to the corresponding two-nucleon forces at the same order in the chiral expansion. It agrees with earlier effective field theory results for isospin-violating one- and two-pion-exchange potentials obtained by van Kolck (1993, 1996, 1997), Epelbaum (1999), Walzl (2000), and Friar (1999, 2003, 2004), while providing the first complete derivation of the subleading charge-symmetry-breaking two-pion-exchange potential. Together with the three-nucleon force results, this work specifies completely isospin-violating nuclear forces up to order \(q^5/\Lambda^5\), enabling systematic studies of isospin-breaking effects in few-nucleon systems within chiral effective field theory.
