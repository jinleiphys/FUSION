# Higher-order isospin-symmetry breaking corrections to nuclear matrix elements of superallowed $0^+\to 0^+$ Fermi $β$ decay of $T=1$ nuclei

> We study the shell-model formalism to include the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction ($δ_{C}$) to nuclear matrix element of superallowed $0^+\to 0^+$ Fermi $β$ decays of $T=1$ nuclei. Based on a perturbation expansion in a small quantity, such as the deviation of the overlap integral between proton and neutron radial wave functions from unity or of the transition density from its isospin-symmetry value, we derive that $δ_C$ can be obtained as a sum of six terms, including two leading order (LO) terms, two next-to-leading order (NLO) terms, one next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) term and one next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (NNNLO) term. The first two terms have been considered in a series of shell-model calculations of Towner and Hardy as well as in the recent calculation of the present authors, while the remaining four terms are usually neglected. A numerical calculation has been carried out for 13 transitions in the $p$, $sd$ and the lower part of $pf$ shells. Our results indicate that the magnitude of the sum of all higher order terms is of the order of $10^{-3}$ %. This number is well below typical theoretical errors quantified within the shell model with Woods-Saxon radial wave functions.

# Higher-order isospin-symmetry breaking corrections to nuclear matrix elements of superallowed \(0^+\to 0^+\) Fermi \(\beta\) decay of \(T=1\) nuclei

## Key claim

The sum of all higher-order (NLO, NNLO, NNNLO) isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to the Fermi matrix element is of order \(10^{-3}\)%, well below typical theoretical errors in shell-model calculations with Woods-Saxon radial wave functions, but these terms may become significant when the leading-order terms are extremely large, such as in \(T=2\) decays or Gamow-Teller transitions.

## Method

The authors derive a perturbation expansion for the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction \(\delta_C\) based on two small parameters: the deviation of the proton-neutron radial wave function overlap integral from unity (\(\Lambda\)) and the deviation of the one-body transition density from its isospin-symmetry value (\(D\)). Using the shell model with Woods-Saxon radial wave functions, they compute \(\delta_C\) as a sum of six terms (two LO, two NLO, one NNLO, one NNNLO) for 13 superallowed Fermi decays in the \(p\), \(sd\), and lower \(pf\) shells, employing the Cohen-Kurath, USD, and GXPF1A effective interactions with their isospin-nonconserving counterparts.

## Key numbers

- Sum of all higher-order terms (NLO+NNLO+NNNLO) is of order \(10^{-3}\)% for all 13 transitions studied
- For \(^{10}\)C: \(\delta_{C3}+\delta_{C4}+\delta_{C5}+\delta_{C6} = 0.00036654\)%
- For \(^{14}\)O: \(\delta_{C3}+\delta_{C4}+\delta_{C5}+\delta_{C6} = -0.00255168\)%
- For \(^{30}\)S: \(\delta_{C3}+\delta_{C4}+\delta_{C5}+\delta_{C6} = 0.00374776\)%
- For \(^{34}\)Cl: \(\delta_{C3}+\delta_{C4}+\delta_{C5}+\delta_{C6} = -0.00219103\)%
- \(\delta_{C2}\) (radial mismatch LO term) ranges from 0.18931000% (\(^{10}\)C) to 0.70812600% (\(^{34}\)Ar)
- \(\delta_{C1}\) (isospin-mixing LO term) ranges from 0.00577545% (\(^{42}\)Ti) to 0.05890865% (\(^{30}\)S)
- \(\delta_{C3}\) (NLO term) ranges from -0.00234000% (\(^{14}\)O) to +0.00514100% (\(^{30}\)S)
- \(\delta_{C4}\) (NLO term) is always negative, ranging from -0.00011391% (\(^{18}\)Ne) to -0.00137411% (\(^{30}\)S)
- \(\delta_{C5}\) (NNLO term) ranges from -0.00001906% (\(^{30}\)S) to +0.00000368% (\(^{34}\)Cl)
- \(\delta_{C6}\) (NNNLO term) is at most -0.00000007% (\(^{30}\)S)

## Context

This work extends the shell-model formalism of Towner and Hardy (2002, 2008) and Hardy and Towner (2015, 2020), who previously considered only the two leading-order terms (\(\delta_{C1}\) and \(\delta_{C2}\)) in their calculations of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections for superallowed Fermi decays. The authors had previously developed this approach in Xayavong and Smirnova (2018). The present paper derives and numerically evaluates the four previously neglected higher-order terms, demonstrating that they are negligible for standard \(T=1\) superallowed transitions but providing a formalism that can be applied to cases with larger isospin breaking, such as \(T=2\) decays studied by Bhattacharya and collaborators, or Gamow-Teller decays where the formalism can be generalized.
