# The Study of Pole Trajectory within a bare state in the coupled channel model

> We investigate two-particle scattering and two-particle scattering with a bare basis state using Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT). We analyze the distribution of two-body scattering poles in the momentum and energy planes under relativistic conditions. Compared to the non-relativistic case, there are significant differences in the distribution of bound state poles and resonance poles in the relativistic case, primarily due to the square root term in the relativistic formula. By considering pure two-particle scattering, we examine the relationship between the form factor and the number of poles. Additionally, we clearly elucidate the effects of attractive and repulsive interactions on the bound state poles and resonance poles. More importantly, we extend our model by including a bare state and explore the poles originating from the bare state or coupled channels through the trajectories of pole positions, as well as the compositeness of bound states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in relativistic two-body scattering within Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT), the inclusion of a bare state coupled to a single channel produces pole trajectories that systematically reveal how physical bound states, resonances, and virtual states originate either from the bare state itself or from the two-body interaction, with the relativistic energy-momentum relation introducing resonance-like poles below threshold that do not appear in the non-relativistic case.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) model with separable potentials for two-body interactions and a bare state coupled to a single two-particle channel. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the T-matrix in both the pure two-body (c-c) mode and the bare-plus-two-body (b-c) mode, using S-wave and P-wave form factors with a regulator parameter Lambda = 0.8 GeV, and track pole positions in the complex energy plane as functions of the coupling constants v (two-body interaction strength) and g (bare-to-two-body coupling strength).

## Key numbers

* For the S-wave c-c model with m_M = 0.2 GeV and m_N = 0.8 GeV, the bound state disappears and a virtual state appears when the potential coefficient v becomes greater than -0.0225.
* In the form factor study with n=3, three poles were obtained at 1.98433 GeV, 1.14228 - 2.36899i GeV, and 2.22393 - 1.26388i GeV for masses m_1 = m_2 = 1 GeV and Lambda = 1 GeV.
* When fitting the n=3 phase shift with n=2 form factor, the fitted parameters were f(k) = 1.99432/(1.0328 + 1.6988 q^2)^2 and v = -1.1683, yielding poles at 1.98336 GeV and 1.79308 - 1.39633i GeV.
* When fitting the n=3 phase shift with n=1 form factor, the fitted parameters were f(k) = 2.008/(0.40655 + 1.8901 q^2)^1 and v = -0.18356, yielding a single pole at 1.97886 GeV.
* The bare state masses used in the b-c model were m_B0 = 1.3 GeV (above threshold) and m_B0 = 0.7 GeV (below threshold).

## Context

This work extends the HEFT framework previously applied to systems such as Delta-piN scattering (Hall 2013), N*(1440)-piN scattering (Wu 2017), and Lambda(1405)-KbarN scattering (Hall 2014), where the role of bare states in generating physical poles had not been systematically studied. The paper builds on the two-bare-state analysis of Abell (2023) by focusing on the simpler one-bare-state, one-channel case to clarify pole trajectories. The finding of resonance poles below threshold in the relativistic P-wave case connects to nuclear-level discussions by Lazauskas (2005) on the ACCC method for nn resonances, and to the "crazy resonance" phenomenon reported by Wang (2017) in BChPT. The results enable future work to distinguish model-dependent poles from physically meaningful ones when fitting experimental phase shifts.
