# Realistic in-medium heavy-quark potential from high statistics lattice QCD simulations

> We present our first results on a direct computation of the complex in-medium heavy quark potential from realistic lattice QCD simulations. Ensembles with $N_τ=12$ from the HotQCD and TUMQCD collaboration offer unprecedented high statistics, those with $N_τ=16$ unprecedented time resolution, making possible a robust extraction of the real part from the spectral functions of Wilson line correlators. To this end we deploy a combination of a Bayesian reconstruction (BR method), as well as a Padé-like approximation. We corroborate findings made on less realistic lattices that ${\rm Re}[V]$ smoothly transitions from a confining to a screened behavior at high temperatures and its values lie close to the color singlet free energies. A finite value of the ${\rm Im}[V]$ is observed in the quark-gluon-plasma phase.

## Key claim

The authors present first results for the complex in-medium heavy-quark potential extracted from realistic lattice QCD simulations with dynamical u, d, and s quarks, finding that the real part of the potential smoothly transitions from a confining to a screened behavior at high temperatures and lies close to the color singlet free energies, while a finite imaginary part is observed in the quark-gluon-plasma phase. The extraction is qualified by the use of two complementary reconstruction methods (Bayesian BR and Pade-like approximation) and the acknowledgment that the Pade method underestimates the imaginary part for the available temporal lattice extent.

## Method

The authors compute Wilson line correlators in Coulomb gauge on high-statistics lattice ensembles from the HotQCD and TUMQCD collaborations with N_tau=12 (unprecedented statistics) and N_tau=16 (unprecedented time resolution), spanning temperatures from 150 to 1248 MeV. They extract the spectral function rho(r,omega) of the correlator using two independent approaches: a Bayesian reconstruction (BR method) that imposes positivity and smoothness, and a Pade-like rational interpolation that exploits analyticity to analytically continue from imaginary to real frequencies. The position and width of the lowest-lying spectral peak then yield the real and imaginary parts of the potential, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range covered: T in [150, 1248] MeV
- Lattice ensembles use inverse couplings beta = 6.740 to 9.49
- Pion mass is almost physical at m_pi = 161 MeV, except at beta = 8.0 and 8.4 where m_pi approx 320 MeV
- N_tau=12 ensembles provide 2000-9000 realizations of Wilson correlators
- At T=0, N_conf = 800-1200 configurations per lattice spacing with lattice extent N_tau = 32-64
- The color singlet free energies F^(1)(r) deviate less than 2% from their T=0 form up to r < 0.3/T
- For N_tau=48 in mock tests, Im[V] can be robustly extracted up to r approx 0.4 fm
- At T=0, the potential exhibits the characteristic Cornell-type form

## Context

This work extends previous studies on less realistic lattices (Burnier et al., 2014) that first extracted the in-medium potential from spectral functions, corroborating the qualitative picture of a smooth transition from confining to screened behavior. It contrasts with earlier model-potential approaches based on color singlet free energies or internal energies, and with non-Bayesian extractions (Bazavov et al., 2014; Petreczky et al., 2017) that assumed specific spectral shapes from HTL perturbation theory. The results enable first-principles input for phenomenological models of heavy quarkonium evolution in heavy-ion collisions (Brambilla et al., 2017; Krouppa et al., 2017) and clarify the relationship between the EFT potential and the T-matrix approach (Liu et al., 2017).
