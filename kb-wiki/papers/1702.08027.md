# Phenomenological extraction of two-photon exchange amplitudes from elastic electron-proton scattering cross section data

> In this work, I improve on and extend to low and high $Q^2$ values the extractions of the $\varepsilon$ dependence of the real parts of the two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitudes relative to the magnetic form factor, as well as the ratio $P_l/P_l^{Born} (\varepsilon,Q^2)$ by using world data on $σ_R(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ with an emphasis on precise new data covering the low-momentum region which is sensitive to the large-scale structure of the nucleon. I provide simple parametrizations of the TPE amplitudes, along with an estimate of the fit uncertainties. The extracted TPE amplitudes are compared with previous phenomenological extractions and TPE calculations. The $P_l/P_l^{Born}$ ratio is extracted by using the new parametrizations of the TPE amplitudes and compared to previous extractions, TPE calculations, and direct measurements at $Q^2$ = 2.50 (GeV/c)$^2$. The extracted TPE amplitudes are on the few-percentage-points level and behave roughly linearly with increasing $Q^2$ where they become nonlinear at high $Q^2$. Contrary to $Y_M$, which is influenced mainly by elastic contributions, I find $Y_E$ to be influenced by inelastic contributions at large $Q^2$ values. While $Y_E$ and $Y_3$ differ in magnitude, they have opposite sign and tend to partially cancel each other. This suggests that the TPE correction to $σ_R(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ is driven mainly by $Y_M$ and to a lesser extent by $Y_3$ in agreement with previous phenomenological extractions and hadronic TPE calculations.

## Key claim

The paper improves on and extends to low and high $Q^2$ values the phenomenological extraction of the epsilon dependence of the real parts of the two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitudes relative to the magnetic form factor, as well as the ratio $P_l/P_l^{Born}(\varepsilon,Q^2)$, using world data on $\sigma_R(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ with an emphasis on precise new data covering the low-momentum region sensitive to the large-scale structure of the nucleon.

## Method

The author combines cross section and polarization measurements of elastic electron-proton scattering to extract the TPE amplitudes. Because recoil polarization data were confirmed experimentally to be essentially independent of $\varepsilon$, the ratio $P_t/P_l(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ is constrained to its Born value by setting TPE contributions to zero, allowing the amplitude $Y_M(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ and $\sigma_R(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ to be expressed in terms of the remaining two amplitudes $Y_E(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ and $Y_3(\varepsilon,Q^2)$. These two amplitudes are parametrized as second-order polynomials in $\varepsilon$ and $Q^2$ to preserve the linearity of $\sigma_R(\varepsilon,Q^2)$ while accounting for possible nonlinearities in the TPE amplitudes, and the Regge limit is imposed to ensure vanishing of TPE contributions as $\varepsilon \rightarrow 1$.

## Key numbers

- The extracted TPE amplitudes are on the few-percentage-points level, behaving roughly linearly with increasing $Q^2$ and becoming nonlinear at high $Q^2$.
- At $Q^2 = 2.50$ (GeV/c)$^2$, the ratio $\mu_p R = \mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p = 0.693 \pm 0.006_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.010_{\text{sys}}$ from polarization data.
- At $Q^2 = 2.64$ (GeV/c)$^2$, a fit of $\sigma_R/(\mu_p G_D)^2$ to $\varepsilon$ using the form $(a + b\varepsilon)$ yields $a = 1.106 \pm 0.006$ and $b = 0.160 \pm 0.009$.
- The ratio $(G_M^p/\mu_p G_D)^2$ at $Q^2 = 2.50$ (GeV/c)$^2$ is found to be $1.168 \pm 0.010$.
- For the ratio $P_l/P_l^{\text{Born}}$ at $Q^2 = 2.50$ (GeV/c)$^2$, Fit I gives $a_l = 0.11 \pm 0.03_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.06_{\text{sys}}$ and Fit II gives $a_l = -0.032 \pm 0.008_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.020_{\text{sys}}$.
- The $Y_{2\gamma}$ amplitude is parametrized as $Y_{2\gamma} = 0.035[1 - \exp(-Q^2/1.45)]$ in the range $0.6 \le Q^2 \le 6$ (GeV/c)$^2$.
- The true ratio $\mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p$ corrected for all TPE amplitudes is parametrized as $(1 - 0.158 Q^2)$.
- The $\delta G_M/G_M$ amplitude from pQCD calculations grows logarithmically with $Q^2$, reaching 3.5% of the Born amplitude at $Q^2 = 30$ (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the previous phenomenological extraction of TPE amplitudes by Guttmann et al. (2011) at $Q^2 = 2.50$ (GeV/c)$^2$ to both low and high $Q^2$ values, providing simple parametrizations of the TPE amplitudes and comparing them to earlier phenomenological extractions and hadronic TPE calculations. The results are compared with theoretical calculations including hadronic models (Blunden et al., 2003; Kondratyuk et al., 2005; Borisyuk and Kobushkin, 2006-2015; Lorenz et al., 2015), partonic models (Chen et al., 2004; Afanasev et al., 2005), dispersion relations (Borisyuk and Kobushkin, 2008; Tomalak et al., 2015), and pQCD (Kivel et al., 2009-2013; Braun et al., 2006; Borisyuk and Kobushkin, 2009). The extracted TPE amplitudes enable improved corrections to form factor extractions from Rosenbluth and polarization measurements, and the parametrizations allow calculation of the $P_l/P_l^{\text{Born}}$ ratio for comparison with direct measurements at $Q^2 = 2.50$ (GeV/c)$^2$.
