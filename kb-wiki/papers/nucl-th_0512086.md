# Density-Dependent Relativistic Hartree-Fock Approach

> A new relativistic Hartree-Fock approach with density-dependent $σ$, $ω$, $ρ$ and $π$ meson-nucleon couplings for finite nuclei and nuclear matter is presented. Good description for finite nuclei and nuclear matter is achieved with a number of adjustable parameters comparable to that of the relativistic mean field approach. With the Fock terms, the contribution of the $π$-meson is included and the description for the nucleon effective mass and its isospin and energy dependence is improved.

## Key claim

A new density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) approach with density-dependent σ, ω, ρ and π meson-nucleon couplings can achieve a quantitative description of finite nuclei and nuclear matter comparable to or better than the relativistic mean field (RMF) approach, using a comparable number of adjustable parameters (8 free parameters), while including the previously omitted Fock exchange terms and the π-meson contribution.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian with nucleon, σ, ω, ρ, π meson, and photon fields, where the meson-nucleon couplings (g_σ, g_ω, g_ρ, f_π) are taken as functions of baryonic density ρ_b. For σ and ω, a rational function form f_i(ξ) = a_i[1+b_i(ξ+d_i)^2]/[1+c_i(ξ+d_i)^2] with ξ=ρ_b/ρ_0 was used with five constraint conditions; for ρ and π, an exponential density-dependence was adopted. The 8-parameter set PKO1 was obtained by fitting the masses of 12 selected nuclei and nuclear matter saturation properties. Self-consistent Dirac equations were solved in coordinate space, with non-local Fock potentials treated exactly as in Bouyssy (1987), and pairing correlations treated by BCS with a density-dependent delta force.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density ρ_0 = 0.1520 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per particle at saturation E/A = -15.996 MeV
- Compression modulus K = 250.24 MeV
- Symmetry energy J = 34.37 MeV
- RMS deviation for binding energies of selected nuclei: Δ_Eb = 1.6177 MeV (PKO1), compared to 1.8825 MeV (PK1), 2.3620 MeV (PKDD), 2.2506 MeV (NL3), 2.7561 MeV (DD-ME1)
- RMS deviation for two-neutron separation energies of Pb isotopes: Δ_S2n = 0.6831 MeV (PKO1)
- RMS deviation for charge radii of selected nuclei: Δ_rc = 0.0269 fm (PKO1)
- PKO1 parameters: m_σ = 525.7691 MeV, g_σ(ρ_0) = 8.8332, g_ω(ρ_0) = 10.7299, g_ρ(0) = 2.6290, f_π(0) = 1.0000, a_ρ = 0.0768, a_π = 1.2320
- Ratio g_ρ(ρ_0)/g_ρ(0) = 0.93, f_π(ρ_0)/f_π(0) = 0.36

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) approach of Brockmann (1992), Lenske (1995), Fuchs (1995), Typel (1999), Niksic (2002), and Long (2004) by including Fock exchange terms and π-meson couplings, which were omitted in standard RMF. Earlier relativistic Hartree-Fock attempts by Bouyssy (1987) led to underbound nuclei due to missing meson self-interactions; later improvements by Bernardos (1993) and Marcos (2004) were still not quantitatively comparable to RMF. This DDRHF approach achieves comparable or better description of finite nuclei and nuclear matter, and predicts isospin and energy dependence of nucleon effective masses consistent with non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations by Zuo (2005) and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations by Ma (2004) and Dalen (2005). It enables future development of relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and relativistic RPA approaches.
