# Configuration interaction relativistic Hartree-Fock model

> The configuration interaction relativistic Hartree-Fock (CI-RHF) model is developed in this work. Compared to the conventional configuration interaction shell model (CISM), the CI-RHF model can be applied to study the structural properties of a wide range of nuclei without readjusting any parameters, as the effective Hamiltonian for different model space can be deduced consistently from a universal density-dependent Lagrangian based on the Hartree-Fock single-particle basis. The convergence of intermediate-state excitations has been examined in evaluating the effective interactions, and the core-polarization effects are illustrated, by using $^{18}$O as an example. Employing the CI-RHF model, both the bulk properties and low-lying spectra of even-even nuclei $^{18\sim 28}$Ne have been well reproduced with the model space restricted to the $sd$ shell. Studies of the isotopic evolution concerning charge radii and low-lying spectra highlight the shell closure at $N=14$ for neon isotopes. Furthermore, the cross-shell calculations extending from the $sd$ to $pf$ shell successfully reproduced the low-lying spectra of $^{30}$Ne and $^{32}$Ne. Notably, remarkably low excitation energies $E(2^{+}_{1})$ of $^{30}$Ne suggest the disappearance of the conventional magicity $N=20$.

## Key claim
The CI-RHF model can describe nuclear structure properties across a wide range of nuclei without readjusting parameters, because the effective Hamiltonian for different model spaces is derived consistently from a universal density-dependent Lagrangian using the Hartree-Fock single-particle basis; however, the current demonstration is limited to the sd shell for neon isotopes and to the specific Lagrangians PKA1 and PKO2.

## Method
The authors start from a relativistic Lagrangian with meson-nucleon couplings and perform spherical relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations to obtain a self-consistent single-particle basis. They then construct a model space on top of a frozen 16O core, derive effective two-body interactions using the folded-diagram approach (Krenciglowa-Kuo method) with Q-box corrections up to second order, and diagonalize the effective Hamiltonian using the BIGSTICK code to obtain ground-state and low-lying excitation properties.

## Key numbers
- For 18O, effective two-body matrix elements converge when particle-state energy truncation is set to epsilon ≤ 80 MeV, with most core-polarization effects captured at epsilon ≤ 20 MeV.
- The energy ratio E(4+1)/E(2+1) for 20Ne is about 2.65 both experimentally and theoretically, close to the rotational limit of 3.33.
- For 24Ne and 26Ne, the ratio E(4+1)/E(2+1) is close to 2.0, indicating vibrational collectivity.
- The charge radius of 24Ne is significantly smaller than neighboring isotopes, indicating a subshell closure at N=14.
- Effective charges of 1.29 e for protons and 0.49 e for neutrons are used for B(E2) calculations, averaged over orbits and masses in the sd shell.

## Context
This work extends the configuration interaction shell model (CISM) approach by replacing empirical effective Hamiltonians (such as the USD family by Wildenthal (1984) and Brown (2006)) with a Hamiltonian derived from a universal relativistic density-dependent Lagrangian. It builds on the relativistic Hartree-Fock model of Long (2006, 2007, 2008) and the folded-diagram method of Kuo (1974, 1990). The model enables consistent treatment of both bulk properties and low-lying spectra across isotopic chains without parameter readjustment, and can be extended to cross-shell calculations for nuclei in the "island of inversion" region.
