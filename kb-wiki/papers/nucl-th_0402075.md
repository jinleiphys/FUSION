# Charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock study with the tensor force of light nuclei

> We propose a mean field framework in which the charge and the parity symmetries of a single-particle state are broken. We break these symmetries to incorporate the correlation induced by the tensor force into a nuclear mean field model. We perform the charge- and the parity projections before variation and obtain a Hartree-Fock-like equation (charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock equation), which is solved self-consistently. We apply the Hartree-Fock-like equation to the alpha particle and find that, by breaking the parity and the charge symmetries, the correlation induced by the tensor force is obtained.

# Charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock study with the tensor force of light nuclei

## Key claim
By breaking both parity and charge symmetries in single-particle states and performing projections before variation, the tensor force correlation can be incorporated into a mean-field framework; the charge projection is essential because it activates all isospin channels of the tensor force, yielding roughly three times the tensor energy contribution compared to parity projection alone.

## Method
The authors construct single-particle states that mix positive- and negative-parity components (parity mixing) and proton and neutron components (charge mixing), then apply parity and charge projection operators before variation to obtain a projected Hartree-Fock-like equation. This charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock (CPPHF) equation is solved self-consistently using the gradient method for the alpha particle, employing the Volkov No. 1 central force and the G3RS tensor force with adjustable strength parameters.

## Key numbers
- In the CPPHF calculation with x_T=1.5 and x_TE=0.81, the tensor force expectation value is -30.59 MeV, compared to 0.00 MeV in simple HF and -10.91 MeV in parity-projected HF (PPHF)
- The total energy of the alpha particle ground state is reproduced at -28.19 MeV (CPPHF with x_T=1.5, x_TE=0.81), compared to -27.30 MeV in simple HF with original forces
- The p-state probability P(-) reaches 0.21 for x_T=2.0 in CPPHF, up from 0.08 in PPHF and 0.00 in simple HF
- The root-mean-square matter radius ranges from 1.31 fm (x_T=2.0) to 1.48 fm (HF with x_T=1.0)
- The 0^- state total energies with CPPHF range from -2.70 to -2.83 MeV across the parameter sets that reproduce the ground state binding
- In the wave function for x_T=1.5, the proton component probability is 17%

## Context
This work extends the parity-mixing mean field approach previously applied to relativistic mean field models by Toki (2002) to include charge mixing, motivated by the isovector character of the pion that mediates the tensor force. The tensor force has been shown to contribute significantly to binding in light nuclei by Akaishi (1972, 1986), Pieper (2001), and Suzuki (1998), and to single-particle spin-orbit splittings by Terasawa (1960). The CPPHF framework enables future studies of heavier nuclei and deformed systems, potentially revealing connections between clusterization and the tensor force, though short-range correlation effects remain to be incorporated.
