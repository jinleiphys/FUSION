# Partial-wave decomposition of isospin-projected subleading three-nucleon contact interactions

> We analyze the subleading three-nucleon contact interaction terms at N4LO in chiral effective field theory. We perform the isospin projection into $T=1/2$ and $T=3/2$ states, and find that only eleven of the thirteen operators contribute in the $T=1/2$ channel. By projection on the partial waves of the asymptotic configuration of $N-d$ scattering states in momentum space, we find contributions from ten combinations of LECs. These results provide a useful basis for including N4LO three-body forces in few-body calculations and for constraining them through numerical fits to data.

## Key claim

The paper performs the isospin projection and partial-wave decomposition of the subleading three-nucleon contact interactions at N4LO in chiral effective field theory, finding that only 11 of the 13 operators contribute in the T=1/2 channel and that 10 combinations of low-energy constants (LECs) appear in the partial-wave matrix elements for N-d scattering, though the authors note that the missing 11th combination arises because not all momentum operator structures were included due to the deuteron quantum numbers imposed on the scattering wave function.

## Method

The authors started from the 13 independent operators defining the subleading three-nucleon contact potential at N4LO, projected them onto the T=1/2 and T=3/2 isospin channels using isospin projectors, and then performed a partial-wave decomposition of the potential matrix elements in the N-d elastic scattering channels for J^pi = 1/2^+, 1/2^-, 3/2^-, and 5/2^-. The matrix elements were evaluated using antisymmetrized N-d scattering states with the deuteron wave function in S and D states, and the results were expressed in terms of channel constants that are linear combinations of the original LECs E_1 through E_13.

## Key numbers

- 13 independent operators define the subleading three-nucleon contact interaction at N4LO; 11 of these contribute in the T=1/2 channel, while 2 contribute in the T=3/2 channel.
- 10 channel constants (combinations of LECs) were identified in the partial-wave decomposition: 3 for J^pi = 1/2^+, 3 for J^pi = 1/2^-, 3 for J^pi = 3/2^-, and 1 for J^pi = 5/2^-.
- For the J^pi = 1/2^+ channel, the matrix element for the ^2S_{1/2} to ^2S_{1/2} transition contains terms proportional to D_0 D'_0 and D_0^2 (q'^2 + q^2), where D_0 = (1/2pi^2) integral dk k^2 phi_0(k) and D'_0 = (1/2pi^2) integral dk k^4 phi_0(k).
- For the J^pi = 1/2^- channel, the ^2P_{1/2} to ^2P_{1/2} matrix element is proportional to (1/2)(E_1 - E_2 - E_3 + 3E_4 + 1/2 E_7 - 1/2 E_8 + 7/6 E_9 - 5/2 E_{10} - 5/6 E_{11} - 1/2 E_{12} + 3/2 E_{13}) D_0^2 q'q.
- For the J^pi = 5/2^- channel, the ^4P_{5/2} to ^4P_{5/2} matrix element is proportional to (E_1 + E_3 - E_5 - 3/4 E_7 + 9/4 E_8) D_0^2 q'q.

## Context

This work extends the classification of subleading three-nucleon contact interactions by Girlanda (2011) and the subsequent isospin analysis by Witala (2022, PhysRevC.105.054004), confirming that 11 linearly independent LEC combinations contribute to N-d observables. The partial-wave decomposition provides a basis for including N4LO three-body forces in few-body calculations and for constraining them through fits to experimental data, particularly addressing the nucleon analyzing power (A_y) puzzle in N-d scattering where spin-orbit type operators O_7 and O_8 have been proposed as promising candidates by Kievsky (1999). The results enable future phase-shift analyses and fits to experimental observables using existing phase-shift analyses by Knutson (1993), Kievsky (1996), and Wood (2001).
