# Nuclear shape coexistence from the perspective of an algebraic many-nucleon version of the Bohr-Mottelson unified model

> A fully quantal algebraic version of the Bohr-Mottelson unified model is presented with the important property that its quantisation is defined by its irreducible unitary representations which span the many-nucleon Hilbert space of every nucleus. The model is uniquely defined by the requirement that its Lie algebra of observables includes the nuclear quadrupole moments and kinetic energy. It then follows that there can be no non-zero isoscalar E2 transitions between any states belonging to its different irreducible representations and, as a result, the states of the model are uniquely defined with the property that observed transitions between rotational states of nuclei are to be expressed in terms of mixtures of the model irreps. The algebraic version of the unified model parallels the Bohr-Mottelson model in most respects, including the possibility of including the effects of Coriolis and centrifugal forces as subsequent perturbations. However, it corrects its treatment of angular momentum quantisation and no longer uses an over-complete set of coordinates. These changes have significant implications for the dynamics of nuclear rotations which are hidden when its moments of inertia are considered as inertial masses in the standard expression of rotational kinetic energies. Thus, the developments put a new perspective on the phenomenon of shape coexistence.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully quantal algebraic version of the Bohr-Mottelson unified model, defined by the requirement that its Lie algebra of observables includes the nuclear quadrupole moments and kinetic energy, which corrects the treatment of angular momentum quantisation and no longer uses an over-complete set of coordinates; the key hedge is that observed transitions between rotational states of nuclei are to be expressed in terms of mixtures of the model's irreducible representations, because there can be no non-zero isoscalar E2 transitions between states belonging to different irreps.

## Method

The author constructs an algebraic model with an Sp(3,R) x SU(2)_S x SU(2)_T dynamical group whose Lie algebra is spanned by the many-nucleon quadrupole moment operators, kinetic energy operators, angular momentum operators, and shape deformation generators, all expressed in terms of harmonic-oscillator raising and lowering operators. The irreducible unitary representations of this algebra are defined on subspaces of many-nucleon harmonic-oscillator shell models, and the model is shown to be equivalent to the already-known symplectic model. The Bohr-Mottelson unified model then emerges as a mean-field approximation to this algebraic model.

## Key numbers

* The symplectic model's Lie algebra contains the operators Q_ij = sum_{n=1}^A x_ni x_nj, P_ij = sum_{n=1}^A (x_ni p_nj + p_ni x_nj), hbar L_ij = sum_{n=1}^A (x_ni p_nj - x_nj p_ni), and K_ij = sum_{n=1}^A p_ni p_nj for an A-nucleon nucleus.
* Fits to both energy levels and E2 transitions were obtained for 20Ne and four rare-earth nuclei without the use of effective charges (Rosensteel and Rowe 1977; Park, Carvalho, Vassanji, and Rowe 1984).
* Similar results were later obtained for 166Er by Bahri and Rowe (2000).

## Context

This work extends the Bohr-Mottelson unified model (Bohr and Mottelson 1953) by providing a fully quantal algebraic formulation that corrects its incomplete treatment of angular momentum quantisation. The model builds on earlier symplectic model calculations (Rosensteel and Rowe 1977, 1980; Draayer, Weeks, and Rowe 1984; Park, Carvalho, Vassanji, and Rowe 1984) and on complete decompositions of the nuclear kinetic energy operator (Zickendraht 1971; Filippov, Ovcharenko, and Smirnov 1972; Gulshani and Rowe 1976; Weaver, Cusson, and Biedenharn 1976). The development enables a new perspective on nuclear shape coexistence by showing that rotational energies can be mixtures of kinetic and potential energies of comparable magnitude, rather than purely kinetic as assumed in the standard unified model.
