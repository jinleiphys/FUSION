# Confinement through a relativistic generalization of the linear interaction

> Generalizing a covariant framework previously developed, it is shown that confinement insures that meson $\to q+\bar{q}$ decay amplitudes vanish when both quarks are on-shell. Regularization of singularities in a covariant linear potential associated with nonzero energy transfers (i.e. $q^2=0, q^μ\neq0$) is improved.

# Confinement through a relativistic generalization of the linear interaction

## Key claim
The authors show that confinement in a relativistic Gross equation framework ensures that meson-to-quark-antiquark decay amplitudes vanish when both quarks are on-shell, provided the vertex function vanishes at that kinematic point. This mechanism works even when quark propagators possess timelike mass poles, contrary to the assumption in Euclidean-based studies that propagators must be pole-free.

## Method
The authors generalize the nonrelativistic linear confining potential to a covariant form using the Gross equation (a relativistic three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) rather than the full Bethe-Salpeter equation. They define a modified kernel \( V_A(q) = -8\pi\sigma / [q^4 + (P\cdot q)^4/P^4] \) that restricts singularities to \( q^\mu = 0 \) and eliminates the need for ultraviolet regularization, then solve the resulting bound-state equation for the vertex function.

## Key numbers
- The excited pion state has mass \( \mu = 1.2 \) GeV, composed of two quarks with masses \( m = 0.34 \) GeV.
- The large component of the vertex function for the excited pion exhibits a confinement node at \( p^2 = 0.244 \) GeV\(^2\), the exact kinematic point where both quarks are on-shell.
- The nonrelativistic linear potential is \( {\cal V}(r) = -C + \sigma r \), with the momentum-space form \( V_A(\vec{q}) = -8\pi\sigma / (\vec{q}^2 + \epsilon^2)^2 \).

## Context
This work extends the earlier covariant framework of Gross and Milana (1991-1994), which used a linear potential but did not demonstrate that the vertex function vanishes at the on-shell point. It contrasts with Euclidean-based studies such as Savkli and Tabakin (1998), which assume quark propagators lack timelike mass poles. The result enables consistent relativistic calculations of meson spectra and decay properties where confinement is enforced through vertex function nodes rather than propagator structure.
