# The continuum limit of quark number susceptibilities

> We report the continuum limit of quark number susceptibilities in quenched QCD. Deviations from ideal gas behaviour at temperature T increase as the lattice spacing is decreased from T/4 to T/6, but a further decrease seems to have very little effect. The measured susceptibilities are 20% lower than the ideal gas values, and also 10% below the hard thermal loop (HTL) results. The off-diagonal susceptibility is several orders of magnitude smaller than the HTL results. We verify a strong correlation between the lowest screening mass and the susceptibility. We also show that the quark number susceptibilities give a reasonable account of the Wroblewski parameter, which measures the strangeness yield in a heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

The continuum limit of quark number susceptibilities in quenched QCD is reached already on lattices with temporal extent N_t=6, and the measured values are 20% lower than the ideal gas values and 10% below hard thermal loop (HTL) results, with the off-diagonal susceptibility several orders of magnitude smaller than HTL predictions.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations in the quenched approximation using staggered quarks on lattices with temporal extents N_t=4, 6, and 8, corresponding to lattice spacings a=T/4, T/6, and T/8. They computed diagonal and off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities at zero chemical potential for temperatures ranging from 1.1T_c to 4.7T_c and quark masses m/T_c from 0.03 to 1, using a stochastic trace evaluation technique with 80-100 random vectors per configuration.

## Key numbers

- At T=2T_c, the ratio chi_3/chi^3_FFT reaches its continuum limit at N_t=6, with an 8% decrease from the N_t=4 value
- Continuum limit results at T=2T_c: chi_3/chi^3_FFT = 0.761(3), chi_s/chi^s_FFT = 0.656(3), chi_0/chi^0_FFT = 0.740(3), chi_q/chi^q_FFT = 0.751(3)
- Continuum limit results at T=3.532T_c: chi_3/chi^3_FFT = 0.807(2), chi_s/chi^s_FFT = 0.789(2), chi_0/chi^0_FFT = 0.803(2), chi_q/chi^q_FFT = 0.805(2)
- Off-diagonal susceptibility chi_ud is consistent with zero within errors of 10^{-7} T^2 at all temperatures
- At T=3T_c, HTL predicts chi_3/chi^3_FFT = 0.90-0.94, while the lattice measurement gives 0.80
- The Wroblewski parameter lambda_s extracted from lattice data agrees with experimental measurements from SPS and RHIC

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice QCD results at fixed cutoff (a=T/4) by Gavai and Gupta (2001) to the continuum limit, and compares with resummed perturbative computations by Arnold and Zhai (2000) and HTL calculations by Mustafa (2001). The continuum extrapolated results show a genuine discrepancy with perturbative methods, supporting the presence of strong non-perturbative contributions. The measured susceptibilities enable a lattice-based prediction of the Wroblewski parameter for strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions, which agrees with experimental data from SPS and RHIC experiments.
