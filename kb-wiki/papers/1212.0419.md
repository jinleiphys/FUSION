# Electron shell and the alpha-decay

> The ratio of the alpha-decay widths of a bare nucleus and the related atom is calculated. Both the change of the form and thus the penetrability of the potential barrier and the effect of reflection in the classically-allowed region appearing due to the electron shell are taken into account in the calculations of this ratio. The contribution of each of these two effects is of one and the same order of magnitude. For long-lived radioactive samples the values of the total effect turn out to be somewhat below 1 percent.

## Key claim

The ratio of alpha-decay widths of a bare nucleus and the corresponding neutral atom is calculated to be slightly above 1.0 (suppression of the atomic decay), with the total effect for long-lived samples being somewhat below 1 percent, contrary to earlier qualitative arguments that the effect might be negligible or zero.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrodinger equation for the alpha-particle in the combined potential of the daughter nucleus and the electron cloud, using the Thomas-Fermi model for the electron distribution. They compute the ratio of decay widths by matching the resonant wave function in the region between the nuclear surface and the atomic radius, where the electron potential is approximately constant, to the asymptotic Coulomb wave functions at large distances. The calculation accounts for both the change in barrier penetrability due to the electron potential and the reflection of the alpha-particle wave in the classically-allowed region, using the coefficient A from the matching procedure to obtain the width ratio.

## Key numbers

- For $^{226}$Ra (Q = 4.7806 MeV): $\Gamma_{bn}/\Gamma_{at} = 1.0021$ (exact calculation), compared to $P_{bn}/P_{at} = 1.006$ (quasiclassical calculation)
- For $^{232}$Th (Q = 4.0288 MeV): $\Gamma_{bn}/\Gamma_{at} = 1.0052$, compared to $P_{bn}/P_{at} = 1.010$
- For $^{144}$Nd (Q = 1.9251 MeV): $\Gamma_{bn}/\Gamma_{at} = 1.0079$, compared to $P_{bn}/P_{at} = 1.014$
- For $^{148}$Sm (Q = 1.9858 MeV): $\Gamma_{bn}/\Gamma_{at} = 1.0085$, compared to $P_{bn}/P_{at} = 1.015$
- The energy correction from the electron shell alone (ignoring barrier shape change) gives a factor between 1.22 (for $^{212}$Po) and 2.60 (for $^{147}$Sm) as previously reported by Erma

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical efforts by Zinn (who estimated an upper limit of 1.02 for the half-life ratio in dense media, with ambiguous results of 1.013 and 1.000 for $^{226}$Ra) by providing a rigorous quantum-mechanical calculation that resolves the ambiguity. The results are qualitatively consistent with the experimental finding by Mikh that the half-life of $^{212}$Po differs by about 0.2 percent between Pb and Ni matrices, though the settings differ (solid-state environment versus bare nucleus vs. atom). The formalism enables future calculations for other isotopes and could be extended to study ionization effects or different screening models.
