# Computing Light-Front Wave Functions Without Light-Front Quantization: A Large-Momentum Effective Theory Approach

> Light-front wave functions play a fundamental role in the light-front quantization approach to QCD and hadron structure. However, a naive implementation of the light-front quantization suffers from various subtleties including the well-known zero-mode problem, the associated rapidity divergences which mixes ultra-violet divergences with infrared physics, as well as breaking of spatial rotational symmetry. We advocate that the light-front quantization should be viewed as an effective theory in which small $k^+$ modes have been effectively ``integrated out'', with an infinite number of renormalization constants. Instead of solving light-front quantized field theories directly, we make the large momentum expansion of the equal-time Euclidean correlation functions in instant quantization as an effective way to systematically calculate light-front correlations, including the light-front wave function amplitudes. This large-momentum effective theory accomplishes an effective light-front quantization through lattice QCD calculations. We demonstrate our approach using an example of a pseudo-scalar meson wave function.

## Key claim

The authors argue that light-front quantization should be viewed as an effective theory where small $k^+$ modes are integrated out, and that light-front wave functions can be systematically computed through a large-momentum expansion of equal-time Euclidean correlation functions (LaMET) rather than by solving light-front quantized field theories directly, provided one properly handles rapidity divergences through renormalization.

## Method

The authors formulate light-front wave function amplitudes as gauge-invariant correlation functions between hadron states and the QCD vacuum, using light-like gauge links that introduce rapidity divergences. They introduce generalized soft functions composed of $N+1$ Wilson-line cusps to remove the regulator dependence from naive light-front amplitudes. The large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach is then applied: quasi-light-front wave function amplitudes are defined at large but finite hadron momentum $P^z$, which serves as a physical off-light-cone regulator, and these quasi-amplitudes are matched to physical light-front wave function amplitudes through factorization formulas involving reduced soft functions.

## Key numbers

- The light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation is $\hat P^-|\Psi_n\rangle = \frac{M^2_n}{2P^+}|\Psi_n\rangle$, where $P^+$ is the light-front momentum and $M_n$ is the hadron mass.
- For a pseudo-scalar meson example, the leading light-front wave function amplitude is demonstrated using the large momentum expansion formalism.
- The one-loop Collins-Soper evolution kernel for TMDPDF appears as the coefficient of the divergent piece in the onium wave function calculation: $\psi_{\bar q q}(x,x_{\rm min},b_\perp,\mu)|_{\rm div} \approx -\frac{\alpha_sC_F}{\pi}\ln \mu^2b_\perp^2 \int^{1-x}_{x_{\rm min}} \frac{dx_g}{x_g}\psi_0(x,b_T)$.

## Context

This work extends the LaMET approach previously developed by Ji (2013, 2014) for parton distribution functions to the computation of light-front wave function amplitudes, addressing the long-standing zero-mode problem and rapidity divergences in light-front quantization identified by Nakanishi (1976) and later by Wilson (1994). The approach enables lattice QCD calculations of light-front wave functions without solving the light-front Hamiltonian directly, and connects to the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) framework of Bauer (2000, 2001) and to transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution function methodology developed by Collins (2011) and others.
