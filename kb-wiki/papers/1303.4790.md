# Neutrino Oscillations: Hierarchy Question

> The only experimentally observed phenomenon that lies outside the standard model of the electroweak interaction is neutrino oscillations. A way to try to unify the extensive neutrino oscillation data is to add a phenomenological mass term to the Lagrangian that is not diagonal in the flavor basis. The goal is then to understand the world's data in terms of the parameters of the mixing matrix and the differences between the squares of the masses of the neutrinos. An outstanding question is what is the correct ordering of the masses, the hierarchy question. We point out a broken symmetry relevant to this question, the symmetry of the simultaneous interchange of hierarchy and the sign of $θ_{13}$. We first present the results of an analysis of data that well determine the phenomenological parameters but are not sensitive to the hierarchy. We find $θ_{13} = 0.152\pm 0.014$, $θ_{23} = 0.25^{+0.03}_{-0.05} π$ and $Δ_{32} = 2.45\pm 0.14 \times 10^{-3}$ eV$^2$, results consistent with others. We then include data that are sensitive to the hierarchy and the sign of $θ_{13}$. We find, unlike others, four isolated minimum in the $χ^2$-space as predicted by the symmetry. Now that Daya Bay and RENO have determined $θ_{13}$ to be surprisingly large, the Super-K atmospheric data produce meaningful symmetry breaking such that the inverse hierarchy is preferred at the 97.2 % level.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a broken symmetry between the neutrino mass hierarchy and the sign of the mixing angle θ₁₃, and finds that when including Super-K atmospheric data with the now-known large value of θ₁₃, the inverse hierarchy is preferred at the 97.2% level, a stronger preference than reported by other groups.

## Method

The authors performed a global three-neutrino oscillation analysis, fixing the solar parameters θ₁₂ and Δ₂₁ to values from prior work, then fitting the remaining parameters (θ₁₃, θ₂₃, Δ₃₂) to data from MINOS neutrino and anti-neutrino disappearance, T2K muon disappearance, Daya Bay and RENO reactor experiments, and Super-K atmospheric constraints. They then added MINOS and T2K ν_μ→ν_e appearance data and Super-K atmospheric data that are sensitive to the hierarchy and the sign of θ₁₃, marginalizing over non-displayed parameters and using a Bayesian method to compute probabilities for each of the four hierarchy/sign combinations.

## Key numbers

- θ₁₃ = 0.152 ± 0.014 (from the global analysis of hierarchy-insensitive data, dominated by Daya Bay)
- θ₂₃ = 0.251^{+0.03}_{-0.05} π (from the global analysis, dominated by Super-K solar results)
- Δ₃₂ = 2.45 ± 0.14 × 10⁻³ eV² (from the global analysis, dominated by MINOS neutrino disappearance)
- For the inverse hierarchy with negative θ₁₃: θ₁₃ = 0.153 ± 0.014, with 90.0% probability
- For the inverse hierarchy with positive θ₁₃: θ₁₃ = 0.152 ± 0.013, with 7.2% probability
- For the normal hierarchy with negative θ₁₃: θ₁₃ = 0.151 ± 0.013, with 2.5% probability
- For the normal hierarchy with positive θ₁₃: θ₁₃ = 0.148 ± 0.015, with 0.3% probability
- Inverse hierarchy preferred at 97.2% level; negative θ₁₃ preferred at 92.5% level

## Context

This work extends prior global analyses by Fogli et al. (2012) and Forero et al. (2012), with which the authors disagree on the number of distinct minima in χ² space and the strength of hierarchy preference. The authors attribute the disagreement to possible use of the mass-squared dominance approximation, which they argue does not converge correctly in the interference region between the two mass-squared differences. The results enable future work with updated MINOS data, improved atmospheric analysis, and inclusion of CP violation, and highlight the potential for atmospheric data alone to resolve the hierarchy given the now-known large θ₁₃.
