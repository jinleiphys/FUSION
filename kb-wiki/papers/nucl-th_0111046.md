# The Static Baryon Potential

> Using state of the art lattice techniques we investigate the static baryon potential. We employ the multi-hit procedure for the time links and a variational approach to determine the ground state with sufficient accuracy that, for distances up to $\sim 1.2$ fm, we can distinguish the $Y$- and $Δ$- Ansätze for the baryonic Wilson area law. Our analysis shows that the $Δ$-Ansatz is favoured. This result is also supported by the gauge-invariant nucleon wave function which we measure for the first time.

## Key claim
The static baryon potential in SU(3) and SU(4) lattice gauge theory is consistent with the Δ-Ansatz (sum of two-body potentials) rather than the Y-Ansatz (flux tubes meeting at a Steiner point), but only up to interquark distances of about 1.2 fm; beyond that distance, statistical and systematic errors become appreciable and a small many-body enhancement cannot be ruled out.

## Method
The authors used lattice QCD with the multi-hit procedure for time links and a variational approach with APE smearing to construct a correlation matrix of baryonic Wilson loops, extracting the ground-state potential via generalized eigenvalue methods. They compared the measured three-quark and four-quark potentials directly to the sum of static quark-antiquark potentials measured on the same gauge configurations, without allowing the string tension to vary. For SU(3) they used 220 configurations at β=5.8 and 200 at β=6.0 on a 16³×32 lattice; for SU(4) they generated 100 configurations at β=10.9.

## Key numbers
- The maximal difference between the Y- and Δ-Ansätze for SU(3) is 15%, obtained when the three quarks form an equilateral triangle.
- For SU(4) with a regular tetrahedron configuration, the relative difference between the two Ansätze reaches 21.96%.
- The authors probed distances up to approximately 1.2 fm.
- For SU(4), the double string between two Steiner points has tension 1.357(29) times greater than the single strings.
- The SU(3) results at β=5.8 and β=6.0 show reasonable scaling and yield V₃q ≈ 3/2 V_q\bar{q}, in agreement with Bali (prior work).

## Context
This work extends the lattice study of the static baryon potential to distinguish between the Y-Ansatz (proposed by the CKP group) and the Δ-Ansatz (proposed by Cornwall), following two recent studies by Bali and by Takahashi that reached opposite conclusions. The authors also perform the first measurement of the gauge-invariant nucleon wave function, which further supports the Δ-Ansatz. The results enable more precise mass relations between baryons and mesons, and the SU(4) extension provides a cleaner test due to the larger (20%) difference between Ansätze.
