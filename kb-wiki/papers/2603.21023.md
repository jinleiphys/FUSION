# Elastic proton-proton and pion-proton scattering in holographic QCD

> The elastic proton-proton and pion-proton scattering processes are investigated in the framework of holographic QCD. Considering the Pomeron and Reggeon exchange in the Regge regime, the total and differential cross sections are calculated. In the model setup, the Pomeron and Reggeon exchange are described by the Reggeized spin-2 glueball and vector meson propagator, respectively. For the differential cross sections, contributions of the Coulomb interaction are also taken into account. Adjustable parameters involved in the model are determined with the experimental data, and it is presented that the resulting cross sections are consistent with the data in a wide kinematic region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that elastic proton-proton and pion-proton scattering cross sections calculated in holographic QCD, using Reggeized spin-2 glueball and vector meson propagators for Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, are consistent with experimental data across a wide kinematic region, but only after adjustable parameters in the model are determined by fitting to total cross section data.

## Method

The scattering amplitudes for pp and πp processes are constructed from Pomeron exchange (described by a Reggeized spin-2 glueball propagator) and Reggeon exchange (described by a Reggeized vector meson propagator). The Pomeron-hadron couplings use gravitational form factors of the proton and pion derived from bottom-up AdS/QCD models. The propagators are Reggeized to include higher spin states, and the total and differential cross sections are obtained from the invariant amplitudes, with Coulomb interaction contributions added for differential cross sections in the very forward region.

## Key numbers

- Total pp cross section: calculated as a function of √s and compared with COMPETE Collaboration results and experimental data from ParticleDataGroup (2020)
- Total πp cross section: calculated as a function of √s for π⁻p and π⁺p, compared with experimental data from ParticleDataGroup (2022)
- Differential cross sections for p p̅, π⁺p, pp, and π⁻p scattering: shown as functions of |t| with experimental data depicted by stars with error bars
- The Coulomb amplitude includes the fine structure constant α and an effective electromagnetic form factor G_eff²(t)
- The Coulomb phase uses the diffractive slope B at t→0, the Euler constant γ, and a parameter Λ²

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach to elastic hadron scattering previously developed by Liu (2022) for pp scattering and Liu (2023) for πp scattering, with further refinements by Zhang (2023, 2024) including Coulomb interaction contributions. The model uses gravitational form factors from bottom-up AdS/QCD models by Abidin (2009) for the proton and Abidin (2008) for the pion. The approach enables calculations of both total and differential cross sections without additional parameters beyond those fixed by total cross section data, and may be applied to other high-energy scattering processes for future experimental tests.
