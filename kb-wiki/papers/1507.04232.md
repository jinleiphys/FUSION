# Shear Viscosities of Photons in Strongly Coupled Plasmas

> We investigate the shear viscosity of thermalized photons in the quark gluon plasma (QGP) at weak coupling and $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills plasma (SYMP) at both strong and weak couplings. We find that the shear viscosity due to the photon-parton scattering up to the leading order of electromagnetic coupling is suppressed when the coupling of the QGP/SYMP is increased, which stems from the blue-shift of the thermal-photon spectrum at strong coupling. In addition, the shear viscosity rapidly increases near the deconfinement transition in a phenomenological model analogous to the QGP.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of thermalized photons in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) or N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma (SYMP) is suppressed when the coupling of the medium is increased, due to a blue-shift of the thermal-photon spectrum at strong coupling; additionally, the shear viscosity rapidly increases near the deconfinement transition in a phenomenological holographic model of QCD.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Boltzmann equation to describe the dynamics of thermalized photons, treating photons as probes of the equilibrium medium. The collisional terms were obtained from the retarded electromagnetic-current correlator of the medium, computed via perturbative QCD for weak coupling and via holographic (AdS/CFT) methods for strong coupling, including finite-t'Hooft-coupling corrections in N=4 SYM and a phenomenological holographic QCD model with a bulk scalar field to break conformal symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity of photons in strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma at infinite t'Hooft coupling: \(\eta^{SYM}_{\gamma}(\lambda=\infty) = 1.46 \, T^3 / (e^2 N_c^2)\)
- Ratio of photon shear viscosity to photon entropy density in strongly coupled N=4 SYM at infinite coupling (with \(N_c=3\), \(e^2=4\pi/137\)): \(\eta^{SYM}_{\gamma}(\lambda=\infty) / s_{\gamma} \approx 25/(4\pi)\)
- Shear viscosity of photons in N=4 SYM at weak coupling \(\lambda \approx 2.5\) relative to the infinite-coupling value: \(\eta^{SYM}_{\gamma}(2.5) \approx 1.4 \, \eta^{SYM}_{\gamma}(\infty)\)
- Photon relaxation time approximated from DC conductivity: \(\tau_{\gamma} \approx 5-50\) fm at \(T=200\) MeV for \(\sigma_c/T \approx 0.01-0.1\)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Müller (2015) on lepton shear viscosity in the QGP to the case of photons, using the same Boltzmann-equation framework. It contrasts the photon shear viscosity with the well-known KSS bound (Policastro 2001, Kovtun 2004) for the strongly coupled plasma itself. The results enable future studies of photon transport in heavy-ion collisions and cosmology, and suggest that incorporating the viscous effect of thermal photons into full hydrodynamic simulations could help address the puzzle of large direct-photon elliptic flow observed in experiments (Adare 2011, Lohner 2012).
