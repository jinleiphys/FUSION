# Couplings between pion and charmed mesons

> We compute the couplings $DD^*π$,$D_1D^*π$,$D^*D^*π$, $D_1 D_1π$ using QCD sum rules. These couplings are important inputs in the meson exchange model calculations used to estimate the amount of $J/ψ$ absorption due to pions and rho mesons in heavy ion collisions. Our sum rules are constructed at the first order in the pion momentum $p_μ$, which give the couplings that are not trivially related to the soft-pion theorem. Our calculated couplings, which somewhat depend upon the values of the heavy meson decay constants, are $g_{DD^*π}=8.2 \pm 0.1$, $g_{D_1 D^* π} = 15.8 \pm 2 {\rm GeV}$, $g_{D^* D^* π}= 0.3 \pm 0.03$ and $g_{D_1 D_1 π}= 0.17 \pm 0.04$.

## Key claim
The authors compute four pion–charmed-meson couplings using conventional QCD sum rules at first order in the pion momentum, obtaining values that depend on the choice of heavy-meson decay constants and that are not trivially related to the soft-pion theorem.

## Method
The authors construct operator product expansions (OPE) for correlation functions involving a pion and two charmed-meson currents, keeping terms up to twist-5 at first order in the pion momentum. They match the OPE side to the phenomenological side (low-lying pole contributions) under a Borel transformation, then linearly fit the resulting Borel curves within a chosen Borel window to extract the product of the coupling and the relevant decay constants. The couplings are then obtained by dividing by the decay constants taken from the literature.

## Key numbers
*   \(g_{DD^*\pi} = 8.2 \pm 0.1\) (dimensionless), obtained from a sum rule using the structure function \(F_1\).
*   \(g_{D_1 D^* \pi} = 15.8 \pm 2\ \text{GeV}\) (dimensionful), obtained from a sum rule using the structure proportional to \(q_\mu p_\nu + q_\nu p_\mu\).
*   \(g_{D^* D^* \pi} = 0.3 \pm 0.03\) (dimensionless), obtained from a sum rule for the \(D^* D^* \pi\) coupling.
*   \(g_{D_1 D_1 \pi} = 0.17 \pm 0.04\) (dimensionless), obtained from a sum rule for the \(D_1 D_1 \pi\) coupling.
*   The product \(f_D f_{D^*} g_{DD^*\pi}\) is found to be substantially smaller than the value 0.51 GeV\(^2\) from the light-cone QCD sum rule analysis of Belyaev (1995).

## Context
This work extends conventional QCD sum rule calculations of the \(DD^*\pi\) and \(D^*D^*\pi\) couplings by Colangelo (1994, 1995) and Di Bartolomeo (1995), applying QCD duality as suggested by Kim (2000) and using a different structure function for the \(DD^*\pi\) sum rule. It also provides new sum rules for the \(D_1 D_1 \pi\) and \(D_1 D^* \pi\) couplings, which are needed to include the axial-vector \(D_1\) meson in meson-exchange model calculations of \(J/\psi\) absorption by pions in heavy-ion collisions. The authors argue that including the \(D_1\) meson will partly cancel the direct four-point coupling in the \(J/\psi\) dissociation process, analogous to the role of the \(a_1\) meson in \(\rho\)-\(\pi\) scattering (Lee, 1995).
