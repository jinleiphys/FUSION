# Gross features of the spectrum of the 36Ar nucleus

> Samples of the spectrum of the 36Ar nucleus are known in different energy windows. In addition to the ground state region (GS), the superdeformed (SD) state is observed, too, and there is a good candidate for the hyperdeformed (HD) one, as well. They are populated in different reactions. We intend to describe the gross features of the spectra of different energies, deformations and reactions in a unified way. We apply the multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (MUSY). The SU(3) quantum numbers of the shape isomers from previous studies pave the way for this description. The MUSY reproduces the gross features of the spectra to a reasonable approximation. The energy spectrum of the three valleys (GS, SD, HD) indicates that the multiconfigurational symmetry is valid to a good approximation, and different cluster configurations coexist in the shape isomers.

## Key claim

The multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (MUSY) reproduces the gross features of the spectra of the ground-state, superdeformed, and hyperdeformed valleys of 36Ar to a reasonable approximation, but the authors note that unlike the case of 28Si, a parameter-free extrapolation from low-lying to high-lying cluster spectra does not work for 36Ar because much less information is available on the low-lying spectrum.

## Method

The authors applied the multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (MUSY), which provides a unified classification scheme for shell, collective, and cluster states via the U(3) ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SO(3) algebra chain. They used a simple Hamiltonian with an analytical solution, consisting of a harmonic oscillator term, second- and third-order Casimir operators of SU(3), and a rotational L² term, with parameters a, b, and d fitted to experimental data (GS and SD bands with unit weight, other bands with weight 0.01). The U(3) quantum numbers for the GS, SD, and HD states were taken from a previous symmetry stability and self-consistency calculation by Cseh.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator strength was set to ℏω = 11.335 MeV, obtained from the systematics ℏω = 45A⁻¹/³ − 25A⁻²/³ MeV.
- Fitted Hamiltonian parameters: a = −0.11 MeV, b = 0.00047 MeV, d = 1.03.
- The moment of inertia of the hyperdeformed candidate band is 11.36 ℏ²/MeV.
- The B(E2) parameter α² = 0.466 W.u. was fitted to the experimental 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁ transition of 8.2 W.u.
- The 6⁺ state at 9.182 MeV was considered a member of the ground-state band.

## Context

This work extends previous symmetry-based studies of 36Ar by Cseh (which determined the U(3) quantum numbers of shape isomers from stability and self-consistency arguments but did not provide their energies) and by Riczu and Cseh (which investigated possible binary cluster configurations of the deformed states). It parallels earlier successful MUSY applications to 28Si by Cseh and collaborators, where the 12C+16O resonance spectrum was obtained as a parameter-free prediction from the quartet-model Hamiltonian of the ground-state region. The present study completes the symmetry-governed investigation of 36Ar by providing energies for the ground bands of the three minima (GS, SD, HD) and for excited bands, and it enables future work connecting these theoretical predictions to experimental reaction channels via the SU(3) selection rule.
