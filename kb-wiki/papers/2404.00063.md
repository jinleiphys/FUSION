# Uncertainty Quantification of Collective Nuclear Observables From the Chiral Potential Parametrization

> We perform an uncertainty estimate of quadrupole moments and B(E2) transition rates that inform nuclear collectivity. In particular, we study the low-lying states of 6Li and 12C using the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model. For a narrow standard deviation of approximately 1% on the low-energy constants which parametrize high-precision chiral potentials, we find output standard deviations in the collective observables ranging from approximately 3-6%. The results mark the first step towards a rigorous uncertainty quantification of collectivity in nuclei that aims to account for all sources of uncertainty in ab initio descriptions of challenging collective and clustering observables.

## Key claim
For a narrow 1% standard deviation on the low-energy constants (LECs) that parametrize high-precision chiral potentials, the output standard deviations in collective observables (quadrupole moments and B(E2) transition rates) range from approximately 3-6%, but this result is conditional on using uncorrelated LEC samples drawn from Gaussian distributions centered around the NNLO$_{\textrm{opt}}$ parametrization and neglecting other sources of uncertainty such as chiral expansion truncation error.

## Method
The authors used the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with Sp(3,R)-adapted basis states to solve the A-body Schrodinger equation for low-lying states of 6Li and 12C. They constructed 300 sets of LECs by drawing from Gaussian distributions centered on the NNLO$_{\textrm{opt}}$ values with 1% standard deviations, then computed the nuclear states, quadrupole moments, and B(E2) transition rates for each LEC set using model spaces up to Nmax=8 for 6Li and Nmax=6 for 12C with an oscillator energy of 15 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Input LEC standard deviation: approximately 1% of the NNLO$_{\textrm{opt}}$ central values
- Output standard deviations in collective observables: approximately 3-6% of the output mean values
- 6Li 1+ ground state total Q2 moment: mean of -0.254 e fm^2 with standard deviation 0.032 e fm^2 (12.6% of mean)
- 6Li 3+ first excited state total Q2 moment: mean of -4.13 e fm^2 with standard deviation 0.140 e fm^2 (3.39% of mean)
- 12C 2+ first excited state total Q2 moment: mean of 5.62 e fm^2 with standard deviation 0.228 e fm^2 (4.05% of mean)
- Dominant shape probability in 6Li 1+ state (0(2 0) S=1): mean 85.75% with standard deviation 0.19%
- Dominant shape probability in 12C 0+ ground state (0(0 4) S=0): mean 84.83% with standard deviation 0.86%

## Context
This work extends the uncertainty quantification efforts in ab initio nuclear theory, building on prior studies of chiral EFT uncertainties by Furnstahl (2015) and Svensson (2023), and on the NNLO$_{\textrm{opt}}$ parametrization developed by Ekstrom (2013). The results represent a first step toward rigorous uncertainty quantification of nuclear collectivity that aims to account for all sources of uncertainty in ab initio descriptions of collective and clustering observables, enabling future work that includes correlated LEC uncertainties, chiral expansion truncation errors, and many-body method uncertainties.
