# Estimates for parameters and characteristics of the confining SU(3)-gluonic field in neutral kaons and chiral limit for pseudoscalar nonet

> First part of the paper is devoted to applying the confinement mechanism proposed earlier by the author to estimate the possible parameters of the confining SU(3)-gluonic field in neutral kaons. The estimates obtained are consistent with the widths of the electromagnetic decays $K^0,\bar{K}^0\to2γ$ too. The corresponding estimates of the gluon concentrations, electric and magnetic colour field strengths are also adduced for the mentioned field at the scales of the mesons under consideration. The second part of the paper takes into account the results obtained previously by the author to estimate the purely gluonic contribution to the masses of all the mesons of pseudoscalar nonet and also to consider a possible relation with a phenomenological string-like picture of confinement. Finally, the problem of masses in particle physics is shortly discussed within the framework of approach to the chiral symmetry breaking in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) proposed recently by the author.

## Key claim
The confining SU(3)-gluonic field parameters extracted for neutral kaons are consistent with both the measured mass and radius of the kaons and with the widths of their two-photon electromagnetic decays, provided a small proportionality constant A is introduced to relate the electric form factor to the decay constant.

## Method
The author solves the system of equations (10) and (12) for the ground state (n_j=0, l_j=0) using the experimental mass μ=497.648 MeV and root-mean-square radius ⟨r⟩≈0.560 fm of neutral kaons, with current quark masses m_d=5.0 MeV and m_s=107.5 MeV, and a gauge coupling constant g≈5.29045 derived from a running α_s with Λ=0.234 GeV and n_f=3. The resulting parameters a_j, b_j, B_j of the confining SU(3)-gluonic field are then used to compute the electric form factor at Q^2=μ^2, which is compared to the two-photon decay widths via a proportionality constant A.

## Key numbers
- Neutral kaon mass: theoretical μ = 497.648 MeV, matching the experimental value 497.648 MeV.
- Root-mean-square radius: theoretical ⟨r⟩ = 0.550510 fm, compared to experimental ⟨r⟩ ≈ 0.560 fm.
- Gauge coupling constant: g ≈ 5.290449 at the mass scale of neutral kaons.
- Reduced mass: μ_0 = 4.77778 MeV (from m_d=5.0 MeV and m_s=107.5 MeV).
- Field parameters: a_1 = 0.102484, a_2 = -0.198658; b_1 = 0.385250 GeV, b_2 = -0.130208 GeV; B_1 = -0.360, B_2 = -0.170.
- Two-photon decay widths used for consistency check: Γ_7 ≈ 0.209×10^{-10} eV (K^0_S mode) and Γ_17 ≈ 0.696×10^{-11} eV (K^0_L mode), yielding proportionality constants A ≈ 0.3263×10^{-7} and A ≈ 0.1884×10^{-7} respectively.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier confinement mechanism applied to pions, η and η' mesons (Goncharov 2006, 2007a, 2007b, 2008, 2008b) and to heavy quarkonia (Goncharov 2003, 2008a) by completing the parameter estimates for neutral kaons in the pseudoscalar nonet. The results enable further analysis of the chiral limit for the entire nonet, where meson masses arise purely from the confining gluonic field without requiring Goldstone bosons, and allow comparison with a phenomenological string-like picture of confinement.
