# Derivation of capture cross section from quasielastic excitation function

> The relationship between the quasielastic excitation function and the capture cross section is derived. The quasielastic data is shown to be a useful tool to extract the capture cross sections and the angular momenta of the captured systems for the reactions $^{16}$O+$^{144,154}$Sm,$^{208}$Pb, $^{20}$Ne+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr at near and above the Coulomb barrier energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives a direct relationship between the quasielastic excitation function (measured at 180 degrees) and the capture cross section, showing that quasielastic data can be used to extract capture cross sections and angular momenta for reactions near and above the Coulomb barrier. The derivation relies on the conservation of reaction flux and an energy-shifting approximation for the J-dependence of capture probability, with the caveat that the method shows deviations from direct measurements at sub-barrier energies for several systems.

## Method

The authors use the conservation of reaction flux to relate the quasielastic probability (reflection) and capture probability (transmission) for each partial wave J, with the J=0 quasielastic probability given by the ratio of quasielastic to Rutherford differential cross sections at 180 degrees. The J-dependence of capture probability is approximated by shifting the center-of-mass energy by the rotational energy \(\hbar^2 J(J+1)/(2\mu R_b^2)\), and the capture cross section is obtained by summing over partial waves up to a critical angular momentum \(J_{cr}\) (where the potential pocket vanishes). The nucleus-nucleus interaction potential is calculated using a double-folding formalism with Skyrme-type density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the reactions \(^{16}\)O + \(^{120}\)Sn, \(^{18}\)O + \(^{124}\)Sn, \(^{16}\)O + \(^{144}\)Sm, and \(^{16}\)O + \(^{208}\)Pb, the critical angular momenta used are \(J_{cr}=54\), 56, 57, and 62, respectively.
- For the reactions \(^{16}\)O+\(^{154}\)Sm, \(^{32}\)S+\(^{90}\)Zr, \(^{32}\)S+\(^{96}\)Zr, and \(^{20}\)Ne+\(^{208}\)Pb, the critical angular momenta are \(J_{cr}=58\), 68, 74, and 76, respectively.
- The difference between the results of the full formula (Eq. 8) and the approximate formula (Eq. 10) is less than 5% at the highest energies for the light systems studied.
- For the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{154}\)Sm reaction, the extracted capture cross section is shifted in energy by 1.7 MeV relative to direct measurements; for \(^{32}\)S+\(^{96}\)Zr, the shift is 1.9 MeV.
- For the \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb reaction, the extracted mean breakup probability \(<P_{BU}>\) has a maximum of 0.26 at \(E_{\rm c.m.}-V_b\approx 4\) MeV.
- For the \(^{70}\)Zn + \(^{208}\)Pb system, the maximal angular momentum \(J_{max}\) decreases by a factor of about 2.4 at the highest energy considered (about 17 MeV above the Coulomb barrier).

## Context

This work extends the relationship between quasielastic scattering and capture previously explored by Gomes et al. (various years) and Bala et al. (various years), providing a systematic derivation and validation across multiple reaction systems. The method enables extraction of capture cross sections and angular momentum moments from simpler quasielastic measurements, which is particularly useful for predicting capture in cold fusion reactions leading to superheavy nuclei (e.g., \(^{48}\)Ti, \(^{54}\)Cr, \(^{56}\)Fe, \(^{64}\)Ni, \(^{70}\)Zn + \(^{208}\)Pb) and for studying breakup effects in reactions with weakly bound nuclei like \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb. The approach can be extended to hot fusion reactions and provides a tool to check discrepancies in diffuseness parameters extracted from quasielastic versus fusion data at sub-barrier energies.
