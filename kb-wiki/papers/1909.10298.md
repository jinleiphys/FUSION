# The Hellmann-Feynman theorem at finite temperature

> We present a simple derivation of the Hellmann-Feynman theorem at finite temperature. We illustrate its validity by considering three relevant examples which can be used in quantum mechanics lectures: the one-dimensional harmonic oscillator, the one-dimensional Ising model and the Lipkin model. We show that the Hellmann-Feynman theorem allows one to calculate expectation values of operators that appear in the Hamiltonian. This is particularly useful when the total free-energy is available, but there is not direct access to the thermal average of the operators themselves.

## Key claim

The paper derives a finite-temperature generalization of the Hellmann-Feynman theorem, showing that the derivative of the free energy with respect to a coupling parameter equals the thermal average of the corresponding operator, provided the Hamiltonian depends linearly on that parameter. The authors note that this result holds specifically for the canonical ensemble and requires the free energy (not the internal energy) to be the relevant thermodynamic potential.

## Method

The authors start from a λ-dependent Hamiltonian H^λ = H_0 + λH_1 and define the canonical density matrix ρ^λ = e^{-βH^λ}. They compute the derivative of the free energy F^λ = -(1/β) ln Z^λ with respect to λ, using the fact that eigenvalue derivatives dE_n^λ/dλ are given by the zero-temperature Hellmann-Feynman theorem. The derivation yields ∂F^λ/∂λ = ⟨∂H^λ/∂λ⟩_T, which at λ=1 gives ⟨H_1⟩_T. The theorem is then illustrated with three analytical or numerical examples: the one-dimensional harmonic oscillator, the one-dimensional Ising model, and the Lipkin model.

## Key numbers

- For the harmonic oscillator at finite temperature: ⟨x²/2⟩_T = 1/4 + (1/2)[e^{-β}/(1 - e^{-β})], where β = 1/T
- For the harmonic oscillator: the classical limit gives ⟨x²/2⟩_T → T/2 as T → ∞
- For the harmonic oscillator: the derivative ∂S^λ/∂λ|_(λ=1) = -(β²/2)[e^{-β}/(1-e^{-β})²]
- For the Ising model: calculations performed with J=2, h=1, and N=10 particles
- For the Ising model at T→0: ⟨H_J⟩_T/N ≈ -J and ⟨H_h⟩_T/N → -h
- For the Ising model at high temperature: ⟨H⟩_T/N = -(J²+h²)/T
- For the Lipkin model: calculations performed with N=10 particles and V=3 (in units of ε)

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature Hellmann-Feynman theorem, originally formulated by Hellmann (1933) and Feynman (1939), to finite temperature. Previous finite-temperature derivations were given by Cabrera (1989), Fan (1995), Popov (1998), and Rai (2007), but the present paper provides a simpler pedagogical derivation with illustrative examples suitable for quantum mechanics courses. The theorem enables calculation of thermal expectation values of operators appearing in the Hamiltonian when only the free energy is accessible, and the authors show it also provides a connection between entropy and temperature derivatives of thermal averages via ∂S^λ/∂λ = -∂⟨H_1⟩_T/∂T.
