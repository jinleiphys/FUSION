# Neutrino induced pion production at MiniBooNE and K2K energies

> We investigate charged and neutral current neutrino-induced incoherent pion production off nuclei within the GiBUU model at energies relevant for the MiniBooNE and K2K experiments. Special attention is paid to the entanglement between measured CCQE and CC1pi+ cross sections. We further give predictions and compare to recent data measured at MiniBooNE.

# Neutrino induced pion production at MiniBooNE and K2K energies

## Key claim

The GiBUU model can reproduce MiniBooNE's FSI-corrected CC1π⁺/QE ratio data up to 1.5 GeV neutrino energy, but it systematically underestimates the uncorrected (less model-dependent) data, indicating a significant difference between the model's pion absorption and the actual nuclear absorption in the detector.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU coupled-channel transport model, treating neutrino-nucleus scattering as a two-step process: first, neutrinos interact with bound nucleons in a local Thomas-Fermi approximation with in-medium kinematics, including quasielastic scattering, 12 N* and Δ resonances below 2 GeV invariant mass, and non-resonant single-pion background; second, outgoing particles are propagated through the nucleus using the BUU equation with medium-modified spectral functions, accounting for final-state interactions such as pion absorption, rescattering, and charge exchange. The axial form factor C₅^A of the Δ resonance was fitted to ANL and BNL bubble chamber data using two different parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- CCQE-like/true CCQE ratio for tracking detectors at Eν=1 GeV on carbon: efficiency reduced to approximately 10% at a proton momentum threshold of 0.5 GeV; even at zero proton threshold, efficiency does not exceed 80% due to charge-exchange processes and secondary proton knockout.
- MiniBooNE's preliminary absolutely normalized CCQE total cross section shows an excess of about 35% compared to the total cross section measured at NOMAD, ANL and BNL.
- For the CC1π⁺/QE ratio on CH₂, the GiBUU calculation with form factor [C₅^A(Q²)]_I (M_A^I=0.95 GeV, C₅^A(0)=1.17) agrees perfectly with MiniBooNE's FSI-corrected data for energies up to 1.5 GeV, and remains within error bars for higher Eν.
- The MiniBooNE flux peaks at about 0.7 GeV neutrino energy; the K2K flux peaks at about 1.2 GeV neutrino energy.
- For NC single-π⁰ production on carbon averaged over the MiniBooNE flux, pion absorption via the Δ resonance causes a reduction in the region around Tπ=0.1-0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model framework previously developed by the same group (Leitner 2008) to neutrino-induced pion production at MiniBooNE and K2K energies. It directly addresses the entanglement between CCQE and CC1π⁺ cross sections that complicates neutrino energy reconstruction in oscillation experiments, a problem also studied by Athar (2009) who obtained a similar result for the CC1π⁺/QE ratio. The comparison with MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo 2009) reveals that the discrepancy between model and uncorrected data points to the need for improved pion absorption models. The work enables more reliable background subtraction for ν_e appearance experiments like MiniBooNE and provides predictions for NC π⁰ production that can be tested against future data.
