# The roles of deformation and orientation in heavy-ion collisions induced by light deformed nuclei at intermediate energy

> The reaction dynamics of axisymmetric deformed $^{24}$Mg + $^{24}$Mg collisions have been investigated systematically by an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model. It is found that different deformations and orientations result in apparently different properties of reaction dynamics. We revealed that some observables such as nuclear stopping power ($R$), multiplicity of fragments, and elliptic flow are very sensitive to the initial deformations and orientations. There exists an eccentricity scaling of elliptic flow in central body-body collisions with different deformations. In addition, the tip-tip and body-body configurations turn out to be two extreme cases in central reaction dynamical process.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in deformed $^{24}$Mg + $^{24}$Mg collisions at intermediate energies, the nuclear stopping power ($R$) shows an inversion between tip-tip and body-body configurations as a function of incident energy, which is first observed here; this inversion is not present in the abstract's general statement about sensitivity of observables to deformation and orientation.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model to simulate axisymmetric deformed $^{24}$Mg + $^{24}$Mg collisions. They treated $^{24}$Mg as a sharp-cutoff ellipsoid with quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_{2} = 0.416$, and performed systematic calculations for tip-tip and body-body configurations with $\beta_{2} = 0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2$ (all with the same root-mean-square radius) at different incident energies and impact parameters. Soft and hard nuclear equations of state with incompressibility $K = 200$ and $380$ MeV were used for comparison, with symmetry potential strength $C_{sym} = 32$ MeV and an energy- and isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter of $^{24}$Mg: $\beta_{2} = 0.416$ (from LGA-ADNDT-71).
- Incompressibility values used for soft and hard EOS: $K = 200$ MeV and $K = 380$ MeV, respectively.
- Symmetry potential strength: $C_{sym} = 32$ MeV.
- Central body-body collisions lead to larger $R$ than central tip-tip collisions below 50 MeV/nucleon; the situation reverses when incident energies exceed 75 MeV/nucleon.
- Transition energy for mid-central sphere-sphere collisions (where $v_{2}$ changes sign) is near 100 MeV/nucleon.
- For central body-body collisions with $b = 0$ fm, $v_{2}$ is negative at higher energies (consistent with deformed U + U collisions by ART model) and positive at lower energies, with the sign alteration first observed here.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of deformed $^{165}$Ho target effects on neutron reaction cross sections (MH-PRL-20) and $\alpha$ scattering (PDR-PRL-29), as well as deformed U + U collisions at relativistic energies (DGS-PRC-62, LBA-PRC-61, SEV-PRC-61, HU-PRL-94, KA-PRC-72, NC-PRC-76, LXF-PRC-76, MH-PLB-679). It uses the IDQMD model (MYG-PRC-73), developed from the QMD model (AJ-PR-202), to investigate deformation and orientation effects at intermediate energy where such studies were previously poor. The results enable future work on extracting the nuclear equation of state from central body-body collisions, studying liquid-gas phase transitions using tip-tip collisions, and exploring halo and cluster structure in exotic nuclei.
