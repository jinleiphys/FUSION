# From bound states to resonances: analytic continuation of the wave function

> Single-particle resonance parameters and wave functions in spherical and deformed nuclei are determined through analytic continuation in the potential strength. In this method, the analyticity of the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the Schroedinger equation with respect to the coupling strength is exploited to analytically continue the bound-state solutions into the positive-energy region by means of Pade' approximants of the second kind. The method is here applied to single-particle wave functions of the $^{154}Sm$ and $^{131}Eu$ nuclei. A comparison of the results with the direct solution of the Schroedinger equation shows that the method can be confidently applied also in coupled-channel situations requiring high numerical accuracy.

# From bound states to resonances: analytic continuation of the wave function

## Key claim
The analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) method, using Pade approximants of the second kind to extrapolate bound-state solutions into the positive-energy region, can be confidently applied to single-particle resonances in both spherical and deformed nuclei, including coupled-channel situations requiring high numerical accuracy. The method works particularly well for proton decay where resonance widths are smaller than 10^-16 MeV and are best estimated from the wave-function behavior rather than from the energy eigenvalue directly.

## Method
The authors endow the nuclear potential with a strength parameter lambda and solve the bound-state Schrodinger equation for N+M+1 different values of lambda in the bound region. They then construct a Pade approximant of the second kind for the wave number k as a function of x = sqrt(lambda - lambda_0), where lambda_0 is the threshold coupling strength. The approximant is used to estimate the resonance wave number and wave function at the physical value lambda = 1, and the results are compared against direct numerical solution of the Schrodinger equation with outgoing-wave boundary conditions for both spherical and deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For the f5/2 neutron state in 154Sm, the neutron becomes unbound at lambda_0 approximately 0.84, with the bound region extending from lambda = 1 down to this threshold.
- A (7,7) Pade approximant was used for the neutron case, and a (4,4) approximant also compared well with exact results.
- Wave-function values were evaluated at r = 7 fm (inner region, where the neutron feels strongest nuclear potential) and r = 15 fm (tail region) for the neutron in 154Sm.
- For the proton h9/2 state in 154Sm, wave functions were evaluated at r = 5 fm and r = 15 fm.
- For the 3/2+ state in 131Eu, a (3,3) Pade approximant was used, with wave-function components evaluated at r = 15 fm.
- The paper discusses proton decay widths smaller than 10^-16 MeV, corresponding to lifetimes tau >= 1 microsecond.

## Context
This work extends the ACCC method, previously applied by Tanaka, Suzuki and Varga (1997, 1999) to unbound states in 5He and 5Li as well as three-cluster nuclei, to the more challenging case of deformed nuclei where coupled-channel calculations are required. The method builds on the analyticity properties of the Jost function established by Taylor (1972) and Kukulin, Krasnopolsky and Miselkhi (1979). The approach enables the calculation of resonance parameters and wave functions for proton emitters such as 131Eu, where recent experimental data by Sonzogni et al. (1999) identified fine structure splitting in the radioactive decay. The wave-function extrapolation is particularly valuable for estimating extremely narrow resonance widths that are difficult to obtain directly from energy eigenvalues, as noted by Maglione, Ferreira and Liotta (1998, 1999).
