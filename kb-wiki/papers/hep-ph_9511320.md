# Chiral Lagrangians for Baryons coupled to massive Spin-1 Fields

> We analyze the effective low--energy field theory of Goldstone bosons and baryons chirally coupled to massive spin--1 fields. We use the electromagnetic baryon form factors to demonstrate the formal equivalence between the vector and the tensor field formulation for the spin--1 fields. We also discuss the origin of the so--called Weinberg term in pion--nucleon scattering and the role of $ρ$--meson exchange. Chirally coupled vector mesons do not give rise to this two--pion nucleon seagull interaction but rather to higher order corrections. Some problems of the formal equivalence arising in higher orders and related to loops are touched upon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that massive spin-1 fields (such as the rho meson) chirally coupled to baryons and Goldstone bosons do not contribute to the leading-order Weinberg term in pion-nucleon scattering, contrary to what naive vector-meson exchange models might suggest; the Weinberg term arises entirely from the chiral covariant derivative acting on nucleon fields, and the formal equivalence between vector and tensor field formulations for spin-1 fields requires adding specific local contact terms in the vector formulation to match the tensor formulation results up to order q^3.

## Method

The authors construct effective chiral Lagrangians for Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta) and octet baryons (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) coupled to massive spin-1 fields, using both an antisymmetric tensor field formulation and a conventional vector field formulation. They calculate the vector meson contributions to the electromagnetic form factors of the baryon octet and to the isovector pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in forward direction, comparing results from both formulations to establish equivalence conditions for the low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- F_V = 154 MeV and G_V = 69 MeV, inferred from Gamma(rho^0 -> e+ e-) and Gamma(rho -> pi pi), respectively
- F_pi = 93 MeV (pion decay constant)
- The KSFR relation M_rho^2 = 2 g^2 F_pi^2 is fulfilled within a few percent in nature
- The low-energy theorem for the isovector pion-nucleon scattering amplitude T^- in forward direction gives T^-(nu)/nu -> 1/F_pi^2 as nu -> 0

## Context

This work extends the resonance saturation program of Ecker et al. (1989) from the meson sector to the baryon sector, where the concept is more complicated due to both s-channel baryon excitations (like the Delta(1232)) and t-channel meson excitations. The paper addresses a problem raised by Gasser et al. (1988) and further elaborated by Meissner (1988) regarding how to couple spin-1 fields to baryons, and resolves an apparent double-counting issue where both the chiral covariant derivative and rho-meson exchange seemed to produce the Weinberg term in pion-nucleon scattering. The results enable consistent inclusion of vector meson exchange in chiral perturbation theory calculations of baryon properties without double-counting.
