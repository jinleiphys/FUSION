# New Results for Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss diphoton semi(exclusive) production in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions at energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5.5 TeV (LHC). The nuclear calculations are based on equivalent photon approximation in the impact parameter space. The cross sections for elementary $γγ\to γγ$ subprocess are calculated including three different mechanisms: box diagrams with leptons and quarks in the loops, a VDM-Regge contribution with virtual intermediate hadronic excitations of the photons and the two-gluon exchange contribution (formally three-loops). We got relatively high cross sections in $PbPb$ collisions. This opens a possibility to study the $γγ\to γγ$ (quasi)elastic scattering at the LHC. We find that the cross section for elastic $γγ$ scattering could be measured in the lead-lead collisions for the diphoton invariant mass up to $W_{γγ} \approx 15-20$ GeV. We identify region(s) of phase space where the two-gluon exchange contribution becomes important ingredient compared to box and nonperturbative VDM-Regge mechanisms. We discuss also first results concerning production of two $e^+ e^-$ pairs in UPCs of heavy ions. We considered only double scattering mechanism.

## Key claim

The cross section for elastic photon-photon scattering in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at the LHC could be measured for diphoton invariant masses up to approximately 15-20 GeV, and the two-gluon exchange contribution becomes important in specific regions of phase space (large rapidity difference between photons and intermediate transverse momenta of 1-5 GeV) relative to the box and VDM-Regge mechanisms.

## Method

The nuclear cross sections for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV were calculated using the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space, with photon fluxes expressed through nuclear charge form factors (realistic and monopole types). The elementary gamma-gamma to gamma-gamma cross section was computed including three mechanisms: one-loop box diagrams with leptons and quarks (using FormCalc and LoopTools), a VDM-Regge contribution with virtual vector mesons (rho, omega, phi), and a two-gluon exchange contribution (formally three-loop) with massive quarks and all helicity amplitudes. For double dielectron pair production, only the double-scattering mechanism was considered, with the cross section expressed as an integral over impact parameter of the product of two single-pair production probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Elementary cross section: in the 4 GeV < W < 50 GeV region, the lepton contribution to the box cross section is a factor of 5 larger than the quark contribution when neglecting interference effects
- Nuclear differential cross section for diphoton production at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV: expected number of counts in 1 GeV bins for integrated luminosity L_int=1 nb^-1, with cuts on W_gamma-gamma > 5.5 GeV and eta_gamma < 2.5, shows measurable distribution up to M_gamma-gamma approximately 15 GeV
- For PbPb to PbPb e+e-e+e- at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV: cross section with p_t_e > 0.2 GeV and |y_e| < 2.5 is 10.636 microbarn (10,636 events for L=1 nb^-1); with p_t_e > 0.5 GeV and |y_e| < 2.5 is 0.235 microbarn (235 events for L=1 nb^-1); with p_t_e > 1 GeV and |y_e| < 4.9 is 22.6 nb (23 events for L=1 nb^-1)
- The ATLAS Collaboration measured a fiducial cross section of 70 +/- 20 (stat.) +/- 17 (syst.) nb, compatible with the authors' predicted value of 49 +/- 10 nb for ATLAS cuts at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- VDM-Regge contribution maxima occur at |y_gamma1|,|y_gamma2| approximately 5, below the Zero Degree Calorimeter coverage (|eta| > 8.3 for ATLAS, 8.5 for CMS)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral collisions by d'Enterria and collaborators (2013) and the authors' own previous work (Klusek-Gawenda, Lebiedowicz, Schafer, Szczurek 2016), adding the two-gluon exchange contribution beyond the early treatment by Ginzburg and collaborators (1987) by including finite fermion masses and full momentum structure. The calculations for single e+e- pair production are validated against ALICE data at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, showing good agreement. The results enable experimental searches for elastic photon-photon scattering at the LHC and demonstrate that double dielectron pair production via double scattering may be observable with standard LHC luminosities for UPCs.
