# Effect of in-medium $π$ and $η$ propagators to charge symmetry breaking interaction

> We revisit the charge symmetry breaking $(CSB)$ in nucleon-nucleon $(NN)$ interaction caused by $π$-$η$ mixing in nuclear matter $(NM)$ employing one boson exchange $(OBE)$ model. The medium effect on $CSB$ is incorporated through the in-medium $π$ and $η$ propagators. In addition, the medium modification to the nucleon mass is also taken into account to construct the $CSB$ class $III$ potential considering off-shell $π$-$η$ mixing amplitude and large contribution of in-medium meson propagators to $CSB$ potential is found for the pseudoscalar $(PS)$ interaction compared to that of pseudovector $(PV)$ interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including in-medium \(\pi\) and \(\eta\) meson propagators (rather than only in-medium mixing amplitudes) yields a large contribution to the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) class III nucleon-nucleon potential in nuclear matter, and that this contribution is significantly larger for the pseudoscalar (PS) interaction than for the pseudovector (PV) interaction, under the condition that the calculation is restricted to low-momentum collective excitations and one-loop order.

## Method

The author calculates the \(\pi\) and \(\eta\) meson self-energies in nuclear matter at one-loop order using in-medium nucleon propagators that include both a vacuum part and a density-dependent (Fermi sea) part, for both pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) meson-nucleon interactions. These self-energies are then used to solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation to obtain the in-medium meson propagators, and the \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing amplitude is computed from the difference of proton-loop and neutron-loop contributions. Finally, a CSB class III potential is constructed in the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model using the in-medium propagators, the in-medium mixing amplitude, and medium-modified nucleon masses in the external nucleon spinors.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum part of the meson self-energy for the PS interaction is approximated as \(\Pi^v_{aa}(q^2) \approx A_{0a} + A_{1a} q^2\), where \(A_{1a} = 3 (g_a/2\pi)^2 (M_n^2 + M_p^2)/m_a^2\).
* The medium part of the meson self-energy for the PS interaction is approximated as \(\Pi_{aa,m}(q^2) \approx B_{0a} + B_{1a}(q_0^2 - 2\mathbf{q}^2)\), with \(B_{0a}\) and \(B_{1a}\) given by integrals over Fermi momenta \(k_p\) and \(k_n\).
* The vacuum part of the \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing amplitude for the PS interaction is \(\Pi^{PS}_{\pi\eta,v}(q^2) \approx A_{1\pi\eta} q^2\), where \(A_{1\pi\eta} = (g_\pi/2\pi)(g_\eta/2\pi) \ln(\widetilde{M}_p/\widetilde{M}_n)\).
* The vacuum part of the \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing amplitude for the PV interaction is \(\Pi^{\prime PV}_{\pi\eta,v}(q^2) \approx A^\prime_{1\pi\eta} q^2\), with \(A^\prime_{1\pi\eta} = \frac{1}{12}(g_a/2\pi)^2 (m_\pi^2/M_p^2 - m_\pi^2/M_n^2)\) (or with \(m_\eta^2\)).
* The in-medium meson propagator for the PS interaction in the spacelike region is \(\widetilde{D}_a(\mathbf{q}^2) = -1 / [(1 - A_{1a} - 2B_{1a})(\mathbf{q}^2 + M_a^2)]\), where \(M_a^2 = (m_a^2 + A_{0a} + B_{0a})/(1 - A_{1a} - 2B_{1a})\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of matter-induced meson mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter, such as those by Dutt-Mazumder, Dutt-Roy, and Kundu (1997) and by Mori and Saito (2003), which constructed CSB potentials using in-medium mixing amplitudes but did not include the effect of in-medium meson propagators or medium-modified nucleon masses in the external legs. It also builds on earlier vacuum CSB calculations using \(\pi\)-\(\eta\) mixing (Coon et al., 1986; Maltman, 1993) and on the broader context of the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly. The results enable a more consistent treatment of CSB interactions in nuclear matter, particularly for applications to asymmetric nuclear systems and mirror nuclei binding energy differences.
