# Near-integrability and confinement for high-energy hadron-hadron collisions

> We investigate an effective Hamiltonian for QCD at large s, in which longitudinal gauge degrees of freedom are suppressed, but not eliminated. In an axial gauge the effective field theory is a set of coupled (1+1)-dimensional principal-chiral models, which are completely integrable. The confinement problem is solvable in this context, and we find the longitudinal and transverse string tensions with techniques already used for a similar Hamiltonian in (2+1)-dimensions. We find some a posteriori justification for the effective Hamiltonian as an eikonal approximation. Hadrons in this approximation consist of partons, which are quarks and soliton-like excitations of the sigma models. Diffractive hadron-hadron scattering appears primarily due to exchange of longitudinal flux between partons.

## Key claim

The paper investigates an effective Hamiltonian for QCD at large center-of-mass energy squared \(s\), in which longitudinal gauge degrees of freedom are suppressed but not eliminated; the author states that it is not certain this rescaled theory definitely describes QCD, but suggests it may be a useful phenomenological approach to small-\(x\), large-\(s\) scattering.

## Method

The author regularizes the effective Hamiltonian on a spatial lattice with temporal gauge \(A_0=0\), then imposes an axial gauge condition \(U_3=1\) and takes the continuum limit in the \(x^3\)-direction while keeping transverse coordinates discrete. The resulting effective field theory becomes a set of coupled \((1+1)\)-dimensional principal-chiral SU(\(N\)) \(\times\) SU(\(N\)) sigma models, which are completely integrable. Confinement is analyzed by studying the mass gap of these sigma models and the residual gauge invariance condition, with string tensions computed using techniques previously developed for a similar anisotropic Hamiltonian in \((2+1)\)-dimensions.

## Key numbers

* The mass spectrum of the principal-chiral sigma model particles is \(m_r = m_1 \frac{\sin(r\pi/N)}{\sin(\pi/N)}\) for \(r=1,\dots,N-1\), with \(m_1 = K\Lambda(g_0^2 N)^{-1/2} e^{-4\pi/(g_0^2 N)} + \dots\), where \(K\) is a non-universal constant and \(\Lambda\) is the ultraviolet cut-off.
* The transverse string tension is \(\sigma_{\perp} = m_1 / a\), where \(a\) is the lattice spacing.
* The condition for the perturbative treatment is \((g_0')^2 = g_0^4 / (g_0'')^2 \ll (1/g_0) e^{-4\pi/(g_0^2 N)}\), ensuring the mass scale \(m_1\) is much greater than the other two mass scales \((g_0')^2/a\) and \(1/[(g_0'')^2 a]\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of anisotropic gauge theories in \((2+1)\)-dimensions (Orland, 2005; 2006; 2007) to the \((3+1)\)-dimensional case for high-energy hadron-hadron collisions. The effective Hamiltonian builds on the eikonal approximation and longitudinal rescaling proposed by Verlinde (2004), and the integrable nature of large-\(s\) scattering is also indicated by the form of Reggeized amplitudes studied by Lipatov and Korchemsky. The results enable a framework for understanding diffractive hadron-hadron scattering, the soft Pomeron, and the elastic part of the forward amplitude through the exchange of longitudinal flux between partons.
