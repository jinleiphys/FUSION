# Probing the resonance of Dirac particle by the application of complex momentum representation

> Resonance plays critical roles in the formation of many physical phenomena, and several methods have been developed for the exploration of resonance. In this work, we propose a new scheme for resonance by solving the Dirac equation in complex momentum representation, in which the resonant states are exposed clearly in complex momentum plane and the resonance parameters can be determined precisely without imposing unphysical parameters. Combining with the relativistic mean-field theory, this method is applied to probe the resonances in $^{120}$Sn with the energies, widths, and wavefunctions being obtained. Comparing with other methods, this method is not only very effective for narrow resonances, but also can be reliably applied to broad resonances.

## Key claim

The authors propose solving the Dirac equation directly in complex momentum representation (CMR) to obtain resonant states, claiming the method can determine resonance parameters precisely without imposing unphysical parameters and is reliably applicable to both narrow and broad resonances, including broad resonances that the complex scaling method (CSM) cannot handle due to divergence at large rotation angles.

## Method

The Dirac equation is transformed into momentum space and discretized using Gauss-Legendre quadrature along a triangular contour in the complex momentum plane, yielding a symmetric matrix eigenvalue problem. The method is combined with relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory to generate the scalar and vector potentials, and the contour is chosen to enclose the resonant poles in the fourth quadrant of the complex momentum plane. Resonant states are identified as solutions that do not lie on the integration contour, and their energies and widths are extracted from the complex wave vectors using the relativistic dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- For the $1h_{9/2}$ resonant state in $^{120}$Sn: energy $E_r = 0.239$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 5.52 \times 10^{-8}$ MeV (from $E_i = -2.76 \times 10^{-8}$ MeV)
- For the $2f_{5/2}$ resonant state in $^{120}$Sn: energy $E_r = 0.678$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 0.031$ MeV
- For the $1i_{13/2}$ resonant state in $^{120}$Sn: energy $E_r = 3.267$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 0.004$ MeV
- For the $2g_{9/2}$ resonant state in $^{120}$Sn: energy $E_r = 5.232$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 3.068$ MeV (from $E_i = -1.534$ MeV)
- For the $1i_{11/2}$ resonant state in $^{120}$Sn: energy $E_r = 9.607$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 1.219$ MeV
- For the $1j_{15/2}$ resonant state in $^{120}$Sn: energy $E_r = 12.584$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 0.993$ MeV
- The momentum integration contour was truncated at $k_{\text{max}} = 3.5$ fm$^{-1}$ with grid number $N = 120$ for convergence

## Context

This work extends the complex momentum representation approach, previously used in nonrelativistic contexts (Berggren 1968, Hagen 2006), to the relativistic Dirac equation within the RMF framework. It directly addresses limitations of existing bound-state-like methods: the real stabilization method (RSM) is imprecise (LZhang 2008), the analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) approach depends on the range of continuation and polynomial order (Zhang 2004), and the complex scaling method (CSM) fails for broad resonances due to divergence of the rotated potential (Guo 2010). The new RMF-CMR method enables systematic study of broad single-particle resonances in nuclei, which were previously inaccessible, and can be applied to other fields where Dirac particle resonances are relevant.
