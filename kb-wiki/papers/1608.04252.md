# Sound Attenuation in Quark Matter Due to Pairing Fluctuations

> The sound wave in dense quark matter is subject to strong absorption due to diquark field fluctuations above T critical. The result is another facet of Mandelshtam-Leontovich slow relaxation time theory.

## Key claim

Sound waves in dense quark matter experience strong absorption above the critical temperature due to fluctuations of the diquark pairing field, with the effect arising specifically from the energy-dependent density of states near the Fermi surface rather than from the constant-density term.

## Method

The author calculates the sound attenuation coefficient from the imaginary part of the retarded polarization operator using the Aslamazov-Larkin (AL) diagram, which includes two fluctuation propagators (FP) of the slow pair collective mode singular at \(T_c\). The FP is evaluated from the time-dependent Landau-Ginzburg equation with stochastic Langevin forces, and the calculation uses the second term in the expansion of the density of states near the Fermi surface, which gives the nonzero contribution to sound absorption.

## Key numbers

- The 2SC color superconducting gap is \(\Delta \sim 0.1\,\text{GeV}\), with chemical potential \(\mu \sim 0.4\,\text{GeV}\) and ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda \sim 0.8\,\text{GeV}\), giving the hierarchy \(\Delta:\Lambda:\mu \simeq 1:8:4\).
- The Ginzburg-Levanyuk number for color superconductors is \(Gi \simeq \delta T / T_c \simeq (T_c/\mu)^4 \simeq 10^{-4}\), compared to BCS values of \(10^{-12}-10^{-14}\).
- The sound attenuation coefficient scales as \(\Im \Pi \propto \omega \, g^2 \, (m^2 / p_0^4 \varkappa^3) \, (v_0^2 + 1)^2 \, \ln^2(\Lambda / 2\pi T_c) \, [T_c/(T-T_c)]^{3/2}\), where \(m\) is quark mass, \(p_0\) and \(v_0\) are Fermi momentum and velocity, and \(\varkappa^2 = \pi D / 8 T_c\) with \(D\) the diffusion coefficient.

## Context

This work extends the Mandelshtam-Leontovich slow relaxation time theory (Mandelshtam and Leontovich, 1930s) to sound absorption in quark matter, showing that the effect is another facet of that general framework. The calculation builds on the Aslamazov-Larkin diagram approach (Aslamazov and Larkin, 1968) for fluctuation contributions to transport coefficients, previously applied to BCS superconductors, and on the evaluation of the fluctuation propagator for color superconductors using Dyson equation and Matsubara formalism (Kerbikov, 2015) and time-dependent Landau-Ginzburg theory (Kerbikov, 2016). The result enables predictions for sound attenuation in dense quark matter that could be tested at future FAIR and NICA experiments.
