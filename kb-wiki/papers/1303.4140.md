# A Study of P-wave Heavy Meson Interactions in A Chiral Quark Model

> The analytical forms of the interaction potentials between one S-wave and one P-wave heavy mesons as well as the potentials between two P-wave heavy mesons are deduced based on a chiral quark model. Our results explicitly show the attractive property between two heavy mesons. Consequently, a series of possible molecular states are obtained. It is expected that our study might shed some light on the popular discussions of the newly observed XYZ states.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical effective interaction potentials between one S-wave and one P-wave heavy meson, and between two P-wave heavy mesons, using a chiral quark model; the authors claim these potentials show attractive properties that yield a series of possible molecular states, but the results are model-dependent and only four of the eighteen predicted bound states match observed XYZ resonances.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) quark model (and its extended version including vector meson exchanges) to derive analytical effective potentials between heavy mesons by integrating out internal meson coordinates. They then solved the Schrödinger equation with these potentials to find bound states for systems involving P-wave D mesons (D₀*, D₁, D₂*) and their bottom partners (B₁, B₂*), considering isospin, C-parity, and total spin quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- For the D̄D₁ system with I=0, C=+: binding energy 0.4–32.4 MeV (depending on model variant), giving a bound state mass of 4253–4285 MeV
- For the D̄D₁ system with I=0, C=−: binding energy 0.3–21.0 MeV, giving a bound state mass of 4264–4285 MeV (matching X(4260))
- For the D̄D₀* system with I=0, C=+: binding energy 1.7–29.3 MeV, giving a bound state mass of 4154–4181 MeV (matching X(4160))
- For the D̄D₂* system with I=0, C=+: binding energy 1.8–29.7 MeV, giving a bound state mass of 4297–4325 MeV (matching X(4350))
- For the D*̄D₀* system with I=0, C=−, S=2: binding energy 5.9–12.6 MeV, giving a bound state mass of 4315–4322 MeV (matching X(4360))
- For the B̄B₁ system with I=0, C=+: binding energy 20.0–69.0 MeV, giving a bound state mass of 10932–10980 MeV
- For the B̄B₁ system with I=0, C=−: binding energy 7.7–46.1 MeV, giving a bound state mass of 10955–10993 MeV
- Model parameters: up/down quark mass m_q = 313 MeV; harmonic-oscillator frequency ω = 2.522 fm⁻¹ (chiral SU(3) model) or 3.113 fm⁻¹ (extended model); σ meson mass m_σ = 595 MeV (chiral SU(3) model), 535–547 MeV (extended model); cutoff mass Λ = 1100 MeV; charm quark mass m_c = 1430 MeV; bottom quark mass m_b = 4720 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of S-wave heavy meson interactions (Li et al., 2012) to include P-wave heavy mesons, aiming to explain newly observed XYZ states such as X(3872), X(3940), Z_b(10610)±, and Z_b(10650)±. The results contradict the molecular explanations of X(4430)± as a D*̄D₁ molecule proposed by Close (2010) and Ding (2009), agreeing instead with Liu (2010) who disfavored that interpretation using the resonating group method. The work enables systematic predictions for possible heavy meson molecules that could be tested against future experimental observations of XYZ states and their bottomonium counterparts.
