# Proton-deuteron asymmetry in Drell-Yan processes and polarized light-antiquark distributions

> We discuss the relation between the ratio of the proton-deuteron (pd) Drell-Yan cross section to the proton-proton (pp) one Δ_{(T)} σ_{pd}/2 Δ_{(T)} σ_{pp} and the flavor asymmetry in polarized light-antiquark distributions. Using a recent formalism of the polarized pd Drell-Yan process, we show that the difference between the pp and pd cross sections is valuable for finding not only the flavor asymmetry in longitudinally polarized antiquark distributions but also the one in transversity distributions. It is especially important that we point out the possibility of measuring the flavor asymmetry in the transversity distributions because it cannot be found in W production processes and inclusive lepton scattering due to the chiral-odd property.

## Key claim

The ratio of the polarized proton-deuteron (pd) Drell-Yan cross section to the polarized proton-proton (pp) one, \(R_{pd} = \Delta_{(T)} \sigma_{pd} / 2 \Delta_{(T)} \sigma_{pp}\), is directly sensitive to the flavor asymmetry \(\Delta_{(T)} \bar u - \Delta_{(T)} \bar d\) in polarized light-antiquark distributions, especially in the large-\(x_F\) region; this method is particularly valuable for transversity distributions, which cannot be probed via \(W\) production or inclusive lepton scattering due to their chiral-odd nature.

## Method

The authors derive the parton-model expressions for the longitudinally-polarized and transversely-polarized pd Drell-Yan cross sections, integrating over the virtual-photon transverse momentum \(\vec Q_T\) and neglecting nuclear corrections in the deuteron (assuming isospin symmetry). They then compute the ratio \(R_{pd}\) numerically using the LSS99 (Leader-Sidorov-Stamenov) parametrization for polarized parton distributions at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), evolving the distributions from \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) to \(Q^2 = M_{\mu\mu}^2 = 25\) GeV\(^2\). The flavor asymmetry ratio \(r_{\bar q} \equiv \Delta_{(T)} \bar u / \Delta_{(T)} \bar d\) is varied among values 0.7, 1.0, and 1.3 at the initial scale to study its effect on \(R_{pd}\) as a function of \(x_F\).

## Key numbers

* The Drell-Yan cross-section ratio \(R_{pd}\) is calculated at \(\sqrt{s} = 50\) GeV and \(M_{\mu\mu} = 5\) GeV for both longitudinally- and transversely-polarized cases.
* The flavor asymmetry ratio is taken as \(r_{\bar q} \equiv \Delta_{(T)} \bar u / \Delta_{(T)} \bar d = 0.7,\ 1.0,\ \text{or}\ 1.3\) at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
* In the limit \(x_F \rightarrow -1\) with flavor-symmetric antiquarks, the ratio \(R_{pd}\) is predicted to be \(5/8 = 0.625\).
* The NLO evolution from \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) to \(Q^2 = 25\) GeV\(^2\) produces ratios nearly identical to the LO results, with slight differences in the large-\(x_F\) region.
* The ratio \(R_{pd}\) is also shown at RHIC energies \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) and 500 GeV, where the intermediate-\(x_F\) region becomes steeper and more sensitive to the parton distributions.

## Context

This work extends the formalism for polarized pd Drell-Yan processes developed by Hino and Kumano (1999) to numerical predictions, following the unpolarized pd Drell-Yan method used by the CERN-NA51 (1994) and Fermilab-E866 (1998) collaborations to measure the \(\bar u/\bar d\) asymmetry. The paper shows that the same technique can probe flavor asymmetries in longitudinally-polarized and transversity distributions, the latter being inaccessible via \(W\) production (as noted by Jaffe and Ji, 1992) or inclusive lepton scattering. The results enable future experimental feasibility studies at facilities such as FNAL, HERA, and RHIC.
