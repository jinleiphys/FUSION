# Proof of Factorization of $J/Ψ$ Production in Non-Equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC

> $J/ψ$ suppression/production is one of the main signature of quark-gluon plasma detection at RHIC and LHC. In order to study $j/ψ$ suppression/production in high energy heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, one needs to prove the factorization theorem of $j/ψ$ production in non-equilibrium QCD medium, otherwise one will predict infinite cross section of $j/ψ$. In this paper we prove factorization theorem of $j/ψ$ production in non-equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC at all order in coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper proves that infrared divergences arising from soft gluon exchange between a charm quark and a nearby light-like quark or gluon in a non-equilibrium QCD medium exactly cancel with the corresponding infrared divergences from soft gluon exchange between the anticharm quark and the same light-like quark or gluon, at all orders in the coupling constant, for \(J/\psi\) production from a color-singlet \(c\bar{c}\) pair. This cancellation is the factorization theorem, without which the predicted cross section would be infinite.

## Method

The proof uses the closed-time path (CTP) integral formalism of non-equilibrium QCD to define the generating functional and the non-perturbative matrix element for \(J/\psi\) production from a color-singlet, spin-triplet \(c\bar{c}\) pair. The infrared behavior is isolated by decomposing the gauge field into a physical (transverse) part and a pure-gauge (longitudinal) part, showing that only the pure-gauge component contributes to soft divergences. The eikonal current of a light-like Wilson line is shown to generate a pure gauge field in quantum field theory, and the cancellation of infrared divergences between charm and anticharm interactions with the same light-like parton is demonstrated at all orders in the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

* RHIC collides two gold nuclei at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV per nucleon, with total energy \(\sim 40\) TeV.
* LHC (first run) collides two lead nuclei at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV per nucleon, with total energy \(\sim 574\) TeV.
* LHC (second run) will collide two lead nuclei at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\) TeV per nucleon, with total energy \(\sim 1150\) TeV.
* The temperature of the quark-gluon plasma is \(\gtrsim 200\) MeV (\(\gtrsim 3.2 \times 10^{12}\) K), about a million times larger than the temperature of the sun (\(\sim 1.56 \times 10^{7}\) K).
* The typical time scale for hadronization in QCD is \(\sim 10^{-24}\) seconds.

## Context

This work extends the proof of factorization for \(J/\psi\) production in vacuum (e.g., in p-p collisions, as studied by Collins, Stermann, Bodwin, and others) to the non-equilibrium QCD medium relevant at RHIC and LHC. It directly addresses the need to incorporate non-equilibrium effects into the \(J/\psi\) suppression signature for quark-gluon plasma detection, which was originally proposed using equilibrium lattice QCD by Satz and others. The proof enables meaningful comparison of theory with experimental data on \(J/\psi\) production from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the quark-gluon plasma may be in non-equilibrium.
