# Investigation of Nuclear Phase Transition by Solvababe supersymmetric algebraic model and its application in Ru-Rh and Zn-Cu Isotopes

> Solvable supersymmetric algebraic model for descriptions of the spherical to gama unstable shape- phase transition in even and odd mass nuclei is proposed. This model is based on dual algebraic structure and Richardson - Gaudin method, where the duality relations between the unitary and quasispin algebraic structures for the boson and fermion systems are extended to mixed boson- fermion system. The structure of two type of nuclear supersymmetry schemes, based on the U(6/2) and U(6/4) supergroups, is discussed. We investigate the change in level structure induced by the phase transition by doing a quantal analysis. By using the generalized quasispin algebra, it is shown that the nuclear supersymmetry concept can be also used for transitional regions in addition to dynamical symmetry limits. Experimental evidence for the U(5)-O(6) transition in Ru-Rh and Zn- Cu supermultiplets is presented. The low-states energy spectra and B(E2)values for these nuclei have been calculated and compared with the experimental data.

# Investigation of Nuclear Phase Transition by Solvable Supersymmetric Algebraic Model and its Application in Ru-Rh and Zn-Cu Isotopes

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a solvable supersymmetric algebraic model based on dual algebraic structures and the Richardson-Gaudin method can describe the spherical to gamma-unstable shape-phase transition in both even-even and odd-mass nuclei, provided that the control parameters C_s and C_f are varied between 0 and 1 while C_d is held constant. The claim is hedged by the condition that the model applies specifically to U(6/2) and U(6/4) supergroups, corresponding to fermions occupying j=1/2 or j=3/2 shells respectively.

## Method

The authors construct a transitional Hamiltonian using generators of an affine generalized quasispin algebra (GQA) and Casimir operators of the relevant subalgebras, then solve for eigenvalues using the Bethe-Ansatz equations derived from the Richardson-Gaudin method. For the U(6/2) supersymmetry scheme (j=1/2 fermions), the Hamiltonian includes terms for O^B(5), O^B(3), and Spin^BF(3) Casimir operators; for the U(6/4) scheme (j=3/2 fermions), it uses Spin^BF(5) and Spin^BF(3) Casimir operators. The parameters are obtained by least-square fitting to experimental energy levels of both even-even and odd-A nuclei within each supermultiplet, and B(E2) transition probabilities are calculated using bosonic and fermionic quadrupole operators with effective charges extracted from empirical data.

## Key numbers

- For Ru-Rh supermultiplets (U(6/2) scheme), the Hamiltonian fit parameters are: alpha = 1000 keV, beta = 3.14 keV, delta = -5.26 keV, gamma = 0.0439 keV (used for Fig. 3 energy surfaces)
- For Zn-Cu supermultiplets (U(6/4) scheme), the Hamiltonian fit parameters are: alpha = 1000 keV, beta' = -1.29 keV, gamma = 6.05 keV (used for Fig. 4 energy surfaces)
- The expectation value of the d-boson number operator remains approximately constant for C_s < 0.45 and changes rapidly for C_s > 0.45, with rapid change occurring over the range 0.65 <= C_s <= 1
- The quality of energy fits is quantified by sigma (in keV) and phi (percentage), where phi is defined as the average absolute deviation divided by the average excitation energy
- For the U(6/2) scheme, the quantum number k is related to the total boson+fermion number N by N = 2k + nu_s + nu_d + nu_f

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear supersymmetry from dynamical symmetry limits into transitional regions, building on the earlier introduction of critical supersymmetry by Iachello (2001) and the study of quantum phase transitions in odd-even nuclei by Frank et al. (2004) using U(6/12) supersymmetry for Ru and Rh isotopes. The model also generalizes the duality relations between unitary and quasispin algebras previously developed for bosonic and fermionic systems separately (Refs. 12-14 in the paper) to mixed boson-fermion systems. The application to Ru-Rh supermultiplets follows the earlier work of Stachel et al. on Ru isotopes showing U(5)-O(6) transition features, and the Zn-Cu application extends the Spin(5) symmetry studies of Bijker and Kota (1984) for Cu isotopes. This enables systematic description of shape phase transitions across supermultiplets containing both even-even and odd-A nuclei with a single Hamiltonian and set of transition operators.
