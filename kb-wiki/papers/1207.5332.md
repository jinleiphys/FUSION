# Twist analysis of the nucleon spin in QCD

> The decomposition of the nucleon spin into that of quarks and gluons is related to twist-two GPDs according to Ji sum rule. Further decomposition into the helicity and the orbital angular momentum inevitably requires twist-three GPDs. In this paper we derive exact relations between twist-three GPDs and the canonical orbital angular momentum density of quarks and gluons, and check their consistency with the spin sum rule. Our work demonstrates that the complete decomposition of the nucleon spin fits well with the framework of perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact relations between twist-three generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the canonical orbital angular momentum (OAM) density of quarks and gluons, demonstrating that the complete decomposition of the nucleon spin into helicity and OAM fits within perturbative QCD, but only when using a gauge-invariant extension based on light-cone gauge.

## Method

The authors define F-type and D-type twist-three quark-gluon and three-gluon correlators, then use operator identities and equations of motion to relate these to the canonical OAM densities. They parameterize nonforward proton matrix elements in terms of twist-two and twist-three GPDs, derive identities connecting these distributions, and verify consistency with the longitudinal spin sum rule by taking x-moments of the resulting expressions.

## Key numbers

- Quark canonical OAM density relation: \( L_{can}^q(x) = x \int_x^{\epsilon(x)} \frac{dx'}{x'} (H_q(x')+E_q(x')) - x \int_x^{\epsilon(x)} \frac{dx'}{x'^2} \tilde{H}_q(x') - x \int_x^{\epsilon(x)} dx_1 \int_{-1}^1 dx_2 \Phi_F(x_1,x_2) \mathcal{P} \frac{3x_1-x_2}{x_1^2(x_1-x_2)^2} - x \int_x^{\epsilon(x)} dx_1 \int_{-1}^1 dx_2 \tilde{\Phi}_F(x_1,x_2) \mathcal{P} \frac{1}{x_1^2(x_1-x_2)} \)
- Lowest moment of quark canonical OAM: \( L_{can}^q = J_q - \frac{1}{2}\Delta q - L_{pot} \), reproducing the spin decomposition formula \( J^q = \frac{1}{2}\Delta \Sigma + L_{can}^q + L_{pot} \)
- Relation between F-type and D-type gluon distributions: \( \frac{M_D(x_1,x_2)}{x_1} = \frac{M_F(x_1,x_2)}{x_1(x_1-x_2)} - \delta(x_1-x_2)L_{can}^g(x_1) \)

## Context

This work extends the Ji sum rule (Ji, 1997) relating twist-two GPDs to total quark and gluon spin contributions, and resolves the controversy over whether a complete gauge-invariant decomposition of the nucleon spin into helicity and OAM is possible in QCD. The results build on earlier parton model analyses (Penttinen et al., 2000; Hagler et al., 2003) and the gauge-invariant extension framework (Hatta, 2011; Ji et al., 2012), enabling first-principles calculations of canonical OAM densities through perturbative QCD methods.
