# Astrophysical S-factor of the $^7$Be($p,γ)^8$B reaction from Coulomb dissociation of $^8$B

> The Coulomb dissociation method to obtain the astrophysical S-factor, $S_{17}(0)$, for the $^7$Be($p,γ)^8$B reaction at solar energies is investigated by analysing the recently measured data on the breakup reaction $^{208}$Pb$(^8$B,$^7$Be$~p)^{208}$Pb at 46.5 MeV/A beam energy. Breakup cross sections corresponding to E1, $E2$ and $M1$ transitions are calculated with a theory of Coulomb excitation that includes the effects of the Coulomb recoil as well as relativistic retardation. The interplay of nuclear and Coulomb contributions to the breakup process is studied by performing a full quantum mechanical calculation within the framework of the distorted-wave Born Approximation. In the kinematical regime of the present experiment, both nuclear as well as Coulomb-nuclear interference processes affect the pure Coulomb breakup cross sections very marginally. The $E2$ cross sections are strongly dependent on the model used to describe the structure of $^8$B. The value of $S_{17}(0)$ is deduced with and without $E2$ and $M1$ contributions added to the $E1$ cross sections and the results are discussed.

## Key claim

The Coulomb dissociation method can extract a reliable astrophysical S-factor for the $^7$Be($p,\gamma)^8$B reaction from breakup data at 46.5 MeV/A, but the extracted $S_{17}(0)$ value depends strongly on whether and how $E2$ and $M1$ contributions are included, with the $E2$ contribution being particularly model-dependent.

## Method

The authors analyzed the $^{208}$Pb($^8$B,$^7$Be$~p)^{208}$Pb breakup data at 46.5 MeV/A beam energy using first-order Coulomb excitation theory that includes Coulomb recoil and relativistic retardation effects. They calculated double differential cross sections for $E1$, $E2$, and $M1$ transitions, and performed full quantum mechanical distorted-wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations to assess nuclear and Coulomb-nuclear interference contributions. The calculated cross sections were folded with detector efficiency matrices and compared to experimental angular distributions at three relative energies (0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 MeV) to extract $S_{17}$ values via $\chi^2$ minimization.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit $E1$-only $S_{17}$ factors at individual energies: $(17.58 \pm 2.26)$ eV barn at $E_{cm}=0.6$ MeV, $(14.07 \pm 2.67)$ eV barn at $E_{cm}=0.8$ MeV, and $(15.59 \pm 3.49)$ eV barn at $E_{cm}=1.0$ MeV
- Extrapolated $S_{17}(0)$ from $E1$-only analysis: $(15.5 \pm 2.80)$ eV barn using the TB capture model
- Corrected $S_{17}(0)$ including $E2$ and $M1$ contributions: $(11.20 \pm 2.02)$ eV barn for the KPK model and $(14.0 \pm 2.45)$ eV barn for the TB model
- Correction factors $\xi(E_{cm})$ for KPK model: $0.70 \pm 0.17$ at 0.6 MeV, $0.73 \pm 0.10$ at 0.8 MeV, and $0.75 \pm 0.15$ at 1.0 MeV
- Correction factors $\xi(E_{cm})$ for TB model: $0.85 \pm 0.12$ at 0.6 MeV, $0.88 \pm 0.16$ at 0.8 MeV, and $0.91 \pm 0.20$ at 1.0 MeV
- Ratio of $(E1+E2)/E1$ differential cross sections at 0.6 MeV: approximately 3.0 at 46.5 MeV/A beam energy versus approximately 1.2 at 200 MeV/A beam energy (using KPK model)

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb dissociation method previously applied to reactions such as $\alpha + d \rightarrow ^6{\rm Li} + \gamma$ and $\alpha + t \rightarrow ^7{\rm Li} + \gamma$ (reviewed in Ref. [6]), and directly analyzes the first experimental data on $^8$B Coulomb dissociation by Motobayashi et al. (1994). The extracted $S_{17}(0)$ value of $15.5 \pm 2.80$ eV barn (E1-only) is significantly lower than the $22.4 \pm 2.0$ eV barn used by Bahcall and Pinsonneault in their standard solar model calculations, and the analysis highlights the need for reliable $E2$ capture cross section models (critiquing the KPK model as criticized by Barker and noting the TB model neglects $f$-wave capture). The results enable future experiments at higher beam energies (around 200 MeV/A) where $E2$ contributions are expected to be weaker, allowing less model-dependent extraction of $S_{17}(0)$.
