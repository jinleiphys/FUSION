# From Kadanoff-Baym dynamics to off-shell parton transport

> This review provides a written version of the lectures presented at the Schladming Winter School 2008, Austria, on 'Nonequilibrium Aspects of Quantum Field Theory'. In particular, it shows the way from quantum-field theory - in two-particle irreducible approximation - to the Kadanoff-Baym (KB) equations and various approximations schemes of the KB equations in phase space. This ultimately leads to the formulation of an off-shell transport theory that well incorporates the underlying quantum physics. Remarkably, these transport equations may be solved within a testparticle representation that allows to study non-equilibrium quantum systems in the weak and strong coupling regime. Actual applications to dilepton production in heavy-ion reactions are presented in comparison with available data. The approach, furthermore, allows to address the hadronization process from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

This review demonstrates that far off-shell 1↔3 processes, which are absent in the standard on-shell Boltzmann limit, are responsible for chemical equilibration in quantum field theories, and that a first-order gradient expansion of the Kadanoff-Baym equations yields an off-shell transport theory whose solutions reproduce the full quantum dynamics, including spectral evolution and chemical equilibration, contrary to the quasiparticle Boltzmann approximation.

## Method

The author derives the Kadanoff-Baym (KB) equations from two-particle irreducible (2PI) approximations to quantum field theory, then performs a first-order gradient expansion in phase space to obtain generalized off-shell transport equations. Numerical solutions of the full KB equations for Φ⁴-field theory in 2+1 space-time dimensions are compared with solutions of the gradient-expanded transport equations in both the Kadanoff-Baym (KB) and Botermans-Malfliet (BM) forms, as well as with the standard on-shell Boltzmann equation. The off-shell transport in the BM scheme is further solved via an extended dynamical quasiparticle Ansatz, leading to the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) used to extract partonic spectral functions from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) extracts partonic spectral functions from lattice QCD in the temperature range 0.8 ≤ T/T_c ≤ 10.
- Applications to e⁺e⁻ production from nucleus-nucleus collisions are presented from SIS to RHIC energies.
- The proper equilibrium off-shell distribution is reached for large times contrary to the quasiparticle Boltzmann limit (no specific numerical timescale given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the foundational Kadanoff-Baym formalism (Kadanoff and Baym, 1962) and the gradient expansion approaches of Botermans and Malfliet (1990) and earlier work by Danielewicz (1984). It builds on prior numerical solutions of KB equations by Danielewicz (1984), Köhler (1995, 1999), and the 2PI effective action calculations by Berges and Cox (2001) and Aarts and Berges (2001). The off-shell transport approach enables the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach for heavy-ion collisions and provides a framework for describing hadronization without the entropy reduction problem encountered in conventional coalescence models.
