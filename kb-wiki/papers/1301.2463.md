# Nuclear Drell-Yan effect in a covariant model

> We investigate effects of nuclear medium on antiquark distribution in nuclei applying the results of a recently developed relativistically-covariant self-consistent model for the pion and the isobar. We take into account Fermi motion including Pauli blocking and binding effects on the nucleons and medium effects on the isobar and pion leading to modest enhancement of the pion light-cone-momentum distribution in large nuclei. As a consequence the Drell-Yan cross-section ratio with respect to the deuteron exceeds one only for small values of the light-cone momentum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear Drell-Yan cross-section ratio with respect to the deuteron exceeds one only for small values of the light-cone momentum, based on a modest enhancement of the pion light-cone-momentum distribution in large nuclei that results from medium effects on the pion and isobar, but this enhancement is partially offset by Pauli blocking and binding effects on nucleons.

## Method

The authors use a relativistically-covariant self-consistent model for the pion and the isobar (delta baryon) in nuclear matter, building on the formalism of Korpa, Lutz, and Ramalho (2009). They compute the in-medium pion light-cone momentum distribution from both the nucleon final state (via the Sullivan process with dressed pion propagator and vertex corrections from nucleon-hole and delta-hole loops summed using Migdal parameters) and the delta final state (using a dressed delta propagator with two dominant Lorentz components). These distributions are then convolved with nucleon Fermi motion and binding effects (using a light-cone distribution from Birse (1993)) and with parton distribution functions to obtain the Drell-Yan cross-section ratio for isoscalar nuclear targets relative to deuterium.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used for nuclear matter: \(p_F = 250\) MeV
- Binding parameter: \(\eta = 0.97\)
- Bare-nucleon probability in free proton: \(Z = 0.69\)
- Form-factor cutoffs: \(\Lambda^{(\pi)}_{\pi N} = 0.95\) GeV for the \(\pi NN\) vertex; \(\Lambda^{(\pi)}_{\pi\Delta} = 0.75\) GeV for the \(\pi N\Delta\) vertex
- Delta form-factor cutoff: \(\Lambda = 0.97\) GeV with coupling \(g_{\pi N\Delta} = 20.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\)
- Effective nucleon mass values used: \(M_* = 0.89\) GeV and \(0.85\) GeV, with corresponding energy shifts \(\Sigma_N^v = 0\) and \(0.04\) GeV
- Delta mean-field scalar shifts: \(\Sigma_\Delta^s = -0.05\) GeV and \(-0.1\) GeV, with zero energy shift

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the nuclear Drell-Yan effect by Dieperink and Korpa (1997) and Korpa and Dieperink (1999) to larger \(x\) values, using an improved covariant treatment of the in-medium delta propagator and pion dressing. It is motivated by the planned SeaQuest experiment (E-906) at Fermilab (Reimer, 2011). The model reproduces the \(\bar d - \bar u\) asymmetry in the free proton similarly to Melnitchouk and Thomas (1998), and the nuclear \(F_2\) ratio shows the classical EMC effect slope for \(0.1 < x < 0.5\) as in Arnold et al. (1984). The results enable more reliable predictions for upcoming nuclear Drell-Yan experiments and help distinguish between binding/Fermi-motion effects and genuine medium modifications of the pion cloud.
