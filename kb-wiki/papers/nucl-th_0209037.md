# Theoretical Evaluations of the Fission Cross Section of the 77 eV Isomer of 235-U

> We have developed models of the fission barrier (barrier heights and transition state spectra) that reproduce reasonably well the measured fission cross section of $^{235}$U from neutron energy of 1 keV to 2 MeV. From these models we have calculated the fission cross section of the 77 eV isomer of $^{235}$U over the same energy range. We find that the ratio of the isomer cross section to that of the ground state lies between about 0.45 and 0.55 at low neutron energies. The cross sections become approximately equal above 1 MeV. The ratio of the neutron capture cross section to the fission cross section for the isomer is predicted to be about a factor of 3 larger for the isomer than for the ground state of $^{235}$U at keV neutron energies. We have also calculated the cross section for the population of the isomer by inelastic neutron scattering form the $^{235}$U ground state. We find that the isomer is strongly populated, and for $E_n = 1 MeV$ the $(n,n'γ)$ cross section leading to the population of the isomer is of the order of 0.5 barn. Thus, neutron reaction network calculations involving the uranium isotopes in a high neutron fluence are likely to be affected by the 77 eV isomer of $^{235}$U. With these same models the fission cross sections of $^{233}$U and $^{237}$U can be reproduced approximately using only minor adjustments to the barrier heights. With the significant lowering of the outer barrier that is expected for the outer barrier the general behavior of the fission cross section of $^{239}$Pu can also be reproduced.

# Theoretical Evaluations of the Fission Cross Section of the 77 eV Isomer of 235-U

## Key claim
The fission cross section of the 77 eV isomer of 235-U is calculated to be substantially lower than that of the ground state by about 45-55% at low neutron energies, contrary to earlier speculation that the isomer cross section might be higher. The ratio of the isomer cross section to the ground state cross section lies between about 0.45 and 0.55 at low neutron energies, with the cross sections becoming approximately equal above 1 MeV.

## Method
The authors developed double-humped fission barrier models for the compound nucleus 236-U, fitting barrier heights and transition state spectra to reproduce measured fission cross sections of 235-U from 1 keV to 2 MeV. They used Hauser-Feshbach theory with fluctuation averaging factors, incorporating class-II intermediate structure effects and Porter-Thomas fluctuations via Monte Carlo methods, then applied these models to calculate the fission cross section of the 77 eV isomer of 235-U over the same energy range. Four different barrier models were considered: axially symmetric inner barrier, axially asymmetric inner barrier with gamma = 11 degrees, axially asymmetric inner barrier with gamma = 30 degrees, and axially soft inner barrier.

## Key numbers
- Ratio of isomer fission cross section to ground state fission cross section: between 0.45 and 0.55 at low neutron energies
- For En = 1 MeV, the (n,n'gamma) cross section leading to population of the isomer is of the order of 0.5 barn
- Ratio of neutron capture cross section to fission cross section for the isomer is predicted to be about a factor of 3 larger than for the ground state of 235-U at keV neutron energies
- Inner barrier height for 236-U in Model 1: VA = 5.2 MeV with hbar-omega-A = 1.05 MeV
- Outer barrier height for 236-U in Model 1: VB = 5.7 MeV with hbar-omega-B = 0.6 MeV
- Neutron separation energy of 236-U: Sn = 6.53 MeV
- Energy gap above inner barrier in Model 1: 1.65 MeV; above outer barrier: 1.03 MeV
- Temperature parameters for barrier continuum states in Model 1: theta-A = theta-B = 0.42 MeV

## Context
This work extends the fission barrier systematics established by Bjornholm and Lynn in their review of actinide fission barriers, and addresses the question of whether the 77 eV isomer of 235-U (half-life 26 minutes, spin/parity 1/2+) would enhance or diminish fission yields in high neutron fluence environments such as nuclear explosions. The thermal neutron fission cross section of this isomer had been measured and found to be about twice the ground state value, leading to speculation about fast neutron behavior. The calculations show that the isomer cross section is actually lower at low neutron energies due to the high energy of the 1+ transition state at the inner barrier, which is the most important transition state for s-wave neutron induced fission. The same models were applied to 233-U, 237-U, and 239-Pu, with the 239-Pu calculation reproducing measured cross sections using a significantly lower outer barrier (VB = 5.13 MeV) consistent with systematic trends.
