# Prediction for the transverse momentum distribution of Drell-Yan dileptons at GSI-PANDA

> We predict the triple differential cross section of the Drell-Yan process (pbar p -> l^+l^-X) in the kinematical regimes relevant for the upcoming PANDA experiment, using a model that accounts for quark virtuality as well as primordial transverse momentum. We find a cross section magnitude of up to 10 nb in the low mass region. A measurement with 10% accuracy is desirable in order to constrain the partonic transverse momentum dispersion and the spectral function width within +-50 MeV and to study their evolution with M and sqrt(s).

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the triple differential cross section of the Drell-Yan process at PANDA can reach up to 10 nb in the low mass region, but this prediction depends on a model that assumes generalized factorization and uses parameters (transverse momentum dispersion D and spectral function width Gamma) extrapolated from fits to higher-energy Fermilab data, with the caveat that the evolution of these parameters with M and sqrt(s) is unknown.

## Method

The authors calculate the triple differential cross section d^3 sigma / dM dx_F dp_T for pbar p -> l+ l- X at sqrt(s) = 5.5 GeV using a model that incorporates both intrinsic transverse momentum (via a Gaussian distribution with dispersion D) and quark virtuality (via a Breit-Wigner spectral function of width Gamma) into the parton distribution functions and the leading-order partonic cross section. The model parameters D and Gamma were previously determined by fitting data from the E866 and E772 experiments at Fermilab, and are here extrapolated to the PANDA kinematics. The results are compared with the intrinsic-k_T approach (Gamma = 0) and with the PYTHIA event generator.

## Key numbers

- Cross section magnitude up to 10 nb in the low mass region (M around 1 GeV) at sqrt(s) = 5.5 GeV.
- The intrinsic transverse momentum dispersion parameter D is estimated as D = 0.6 +/- 0.18 GeV from fits to E866 and E772 data.
- The spectral function width Gamma is varied in the range 100-250 MeV, determined from fitting E866 data in the earlier work (paper2).
- At PANDA kinematics, perturbative corrections from higher orders constitute only 0.1% of the p_T-spectrum of Drell-Yan pairs with p_T not equal to zero.
- The cross section at M = 2 GeV is in the pb range (figure caption indicates scale changes from nb to pb between M = 1 GeV and M = 2 GeV).
- The peak of the p_T-distribution for M = 1 GeV is not at zero, a qualitative effect that appears at M less than about 2D.

## Context

This work extends the model developed in Linnyk et al. (2005, paper2) which successfully described Drell-Yan data from the E866 and E772 experiments at Fermilab by including quark virtuality and intrinsic transverse momentum. The paper provides the first prediction for the PANDA experiment at the future GSI facility, which will probe the Drell-Yan process at the low center-of-mass energy of 5.5 GeV, a regime on the edge of non-perturbative QCD where effects beyond leading order and leading twist are expected to be large. The results enable future PANDA measurements to constrain the partonic transverse momentum dispersion and spectral function width to within +/- 50 MeV, and to study their evolution with M and sqrt(s), provided an experimental accuracy of at least 10% is achieved.
