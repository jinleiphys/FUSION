# Nuclear Dependence in Weak Structure Functions and the Determination of Weak Mixing Angle

> We have studied nuclear medium effects in the weak structure functions $F^A_2(x)$ and $F^A_3(x)$ and in the extraction of weak mixing angle using Paschos Wolfenstein(PW) relation. We have modified the PW relation for nonisoscalar nuclear target. We have incorporated the medium effects like Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, nuclear binding energy, nucleon correlations, pion $\&$ rho cloud contributions, and shadowing and antishadowing effects.

# Nuclear Dependence in Weak Structure Functions and the Determination of Weak Mixing Angle

## Key claim
Nuclear medium effects and nonisoscalarity corrections in iron and lead targets produce deviations in the extracted weak mixing angle from the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation that can reach approximately 7-9% at low y and high x, partially accounting for the NuTeV anomaly, though the exact quantitative resolution depends on the kinematic region.

## Method
The authors computed nuclear structure functions \(F_2^A(x,Q^2)\) and \(F_3^A(x,Q^2)\) for iron-56 and lead-208 targets using CTEQ parton distribution functions at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), incorporating Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, nuclear binding energy, nucleon correlations, pion and rho meson cloud contributions, and shadowing/antishadowing effects. They then used these structure functions to evaluate charged-current and neutral-current differential cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering, and extracted the weak mixing angle \(\sin^2\theta_W\) from a modified Paschos-Wolfenstein relation that includes a nonisoscalarity correction term \(\delta R^-\).

## Key numbers
- Shadowing effect is 3-5% at \(x=0.1\) for \(Q^2=1-5\) GeV\(^2\) and 1-2% at \(x=0.2\) for \(Q^2=1-5\) GeV\(^2\), dying out with increasing \(x\) and \(Q^2\).
- Pion cloud contribution is approximately 14-16% at \(x=0.1\), reducing to 4-6% at mid values of \(x\).
- The difference in extracted \(\sin^2\theta_W\) due to medium effects is approximately 7% when evaluated at low y for \(x=0.2\), decreasing to 1% at high y; at \(x=0.6\) the difference is approximately 9% at low y, reducing to 2% at high y.
- The NuTeV Collaboration extracted \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2277 \pm 0.0004\) from iron target data, which is 3\(\sigma\) above the global fit value of \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2227 \pm 0.0004\).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of nuclear medium effects in weak structure functions previously developed by the same authors (Athar et al., 2013) and applies it to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, which was originally derived for isoscalar targets by Paschos and Wolfenstein (1973). The study directly addresses the NuTeV anomaly reported by Zeller et al. (2002), where the weak mixing angle extracted from iron target data showed a 3\(\sigma\) deviation from global electroweak fits. The results demonstrate that both nonisoscalarity corrections and nuclear medium effects must be accounted for when extracting \(\sin^2\theta_W\) from nuclear targets, and the calculations are compared with experimental data from CDHSW (Berge et al.), NuTeV (Tzanov et al.), and CHORUS (chorus1) collaborations. This work enables more precise extraction of the weak mixing angle from future neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments by providing a framework to correct for nuclear effects.
