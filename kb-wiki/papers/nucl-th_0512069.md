# Helicity amplitudes and electromagnetic decays of strange baryon resonances

> We present results for the helicity amplitudes of the lowest-lying hyperon resonances Y*, computed within the framework of the Bonn constituent-quark model, which is based on the Bethe-Salpeter approach. The seven parameters entering the model are fitted against the best known baryon masses. Accordingly, the results for the helicity amplitudes are genuine predictions. Some hyperon resonances are seen to couple more strongly to a virtual photon with finite Q^2 than to a real photon. Other Y*'s, such as the S_{01}(1670) Lambda resonance or the S_{11}(1620) Sigma resonance, have large electromagnetic decay widths and couple very strongly to real photons. The negatively-charged and neutral members of a Sigma* triplet may couple only moderately to the Sigma(1193), while the positively-charged member of the same Sigma* triplet displays a relatively large coupling to the Sigma^+(1193) state. This illustrates the necessity of investigating all isospin channels in order to obtain a complete picture of the hyperon spectrum.

## Key claim

The Bonn constituent-quark model, with its seven parameters fitted only to baryon masses, provides genuine predictions for the helicity amplitudes of the lowest-lying hyperon resonances; some resonances couple more strongly to virtual photons at finite \(Q^2\) than to real photons, while others such as the \(S_{01}(1670)\) \(\Lambda\) resonance and the \(S_{11}(1620)\) \(\Sigma\) resonance have large electromagnetic decay widths and couple very strongly to real photons.

## Method

The authors computed electromagnetic helicity amplitudes for hyperon resonances using the Bonn constituent-quark model, which is based on the Lorentz-covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach. The current matrix element for the electromagnetic transition was evaluated in the rest frame of the incoming baryon resonance, with the photon coupling to a pointlike constituent quark carrying charge \(\hat{q}\). The helicity amplitudes \(A_{1/2}\), \(A_{3/2}\), and \(C_{1/2}\) were then obtained from the current matrix elements with appropriate spin projections for the incoming and outgoing hyperon.

## Key numbers

- The \(S_{01}(1670)\) \(\Lambda\) resonance has an electromagnetic decay width of \(0.159 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV to \(\Lambda(1116)\) and \(3.827\) MeV to \(\Sigma^0(1193)\).
- The \(D_{03}(1690)\) \(\Lambda\) resonance has an electromagnetic decay width of \(0.0815\) MeV to \(\Lambda(1116)\) and \(1.049\) MeV to \(\Sigma^0(1193)\).
- The \(P_{11}(1660)\) \(\Sigma\) resonance has electromagnetic decay widths of \(0.451\) MeV to \(\Lambda(1116)\), \(0.0578\) MeV to \(\Sigma^0(1193)\), \(0.733\) MeV to \(\Sigma^+(1193)\), and \(0.141\) MeV to \(\Sigma^-(1116)\).
- The \(S_{11}(1620)\) \(\Sigma\) resonance has electromagnetic decay widths of \(1.551\) MeV to \(\Lambda(1116)\), \(0.688\) MeV to \(\Sigma^0(1193)\), \(5.955\) MeV to \(\Sigma^+(1193)\), and \(0.613\) MeV to \(\Sigma^-(1116)\).
- The \(D_{13}(1670)\) \(\Sigma\) resonance has electromagnetic decay widths of \(1.457\) MeV to \(\Lambda(1116)\), \(0.0214\) MeV to \(\Sigma^0(1193)\), \(0.440\) MeV to \(\Sigma^+(1193)\), and \(0.184\) MeV to \(\Sigma^-(1116)\).

## Context

This work extends the Bonn constituent-quark model, previously applied to non-strange baryons by Merten et al. and Van Cauteren et al., to hyperon resonances and their electromagnetic decays. The predictions are relevant for isobar models of kaon electroproduction, photo- and electroproduction of the \(\Theta(1540)\) resonance, and radiative kaon capture reactions, where the \(\gamma^{(*)} Y Y^*\) coupling is needed. The results also connect to the experimental program of the Crystal Ball Collaboration at Brookhaven investigating \(\Kbar^- p \to \gamma Y^0\) processes in the energy region around 1660 MeV.
