# The entropy puzzle and the quark combination model

> We use two available methods, the Duhem-Gibbs relation and the entropy formula in terms of particle phase space distributions, to calculate the entropy in a quark combination model. The entropy of the system extracted from the Duhem-Gibbs relation is found to increase in hadronization if the average temperature of the hadronic phase is lower than that of the quark phase. The increase of the entropy can also be confirmed from the entropy formula if the volume of the hadronic phase is larger than 2.5-3.0 times that of the quark phase. So whether the entropy increases or decreases during combination depends on the temperature before and after combination and on how much expansion the system undergoes during combination. The current study provides an example to shed light on the entropy issue in the quark combination model.

## Key claim

Whether entropy increases or decreases during hadronization in the quark combination model depends on the temperature before and after combination and on how much expansion the system undergoes during combination; the entropy increase requires the hadronic phase volume to be larger than 2.5-3.0 times that of the quark phase, or the average temperature of the hadronic phase to be lower than that of the quark phase.

## Method

The authors used two methods to calculate entropy in the Shandong quark combination model: the Duhem-Gibbs relation S = (E + PV - ΣμᵢNᵢ)/T, and the entropy formula in terms of particle phase-space distributions. For the Duhem-Gibbs method, they computed E, PV, and μN per unit rapidity from the momentum distributions of quarks and hadrons at four collision energies (17.3, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV). For the phase-space method, they approximated the spatial distribution as uniform within effective volumes V_q and V_h, then computed the entropy ratio R = S_h/S_q as a function of the volume ratio x = V_h/V_q.

## Key numbers

- At √s_NN = 200 GeV, TS per unit rapidity for the quark phase is 1187.6 GeV and for direct hadrons is 1167.2 GeV, showing approximate entropy conservation under sudden hadronization
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV, the hadronic phase volume V_h = 2200 fm³ (from thermal model freezeout values)
- The entropy of the hadronic phase exceeds that of the quark phase when the volume ratio x = V_h/V_q > 2.5-3.0, corresponding to a fireball radius ratio of about 1.35-1.44
- For an ideal Fermi-Dirac case, the hadronic phase entropy exceeds the quark phase entropy only when x ≳ 16
- The average radial flow velocity ⟨β_r⟩ for u,d quarks ranges from 0.38 (at 17.3 GeV) to 0.562 (at 200 GeV)
- The local temperature T_f for thermal quarks is set to 165 MeV
- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 330 MeV, m_s = 550 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing "entropy puzzle" in quark combination models (also called recombination or coalescence models), which had been questioned because 2-to-1 and 3-to-1 processes appear to reduce particle number and thus entropy. Previous attempts or arguments about entropy in QCM-like models were made by Greco (2003), Nonaka (2005), Hwa (2004), and Hwa (2009), but no systematic investigation had been performed. The study enables future work to incorporate the entropy constraint into hydrodynamic simulations of the fireball evolution and to treat hadronization as a dynamic process with finite time and volume expansion.
