# SU(2) non-Abelian gauge field theory in one dimension on digital quantum computers

> An improved mapping of one-dimensional SU(2) non-Abelian gauge theory onto qubit degrees of freedom is presented. This new mapping allows for a reduced unphysical Hilbert space. Insensitivity to interactions within this unphysical space is exploited to design more efficient quantum circuits. Local gauge symmetry is used to analytically incorporate the angular momentum alignment, leading to qubit registers encoding the total angular momentum on each link. The results of a multi-plaquette calculation on IBM's quantum hardware are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved mapping of one-dimensional SU(2) non-Abelian gauge theory onto qubit degrees of freedom that reduces the unphysical Hilbert space, but the actual demonstration on IBM quantum hardware is limited to a truncation of angular momentum to \(\Lambda_j = 1/2\) and only a single Trotter step yields reliable results after error mitigation.

## Method

The authors use the angular momentum basis for SU(2) lattice gauge theory in one dimension, where each link is characterized by its total angular momentum \(j\) rather than the full set of magnetic quantum numbers. Local gauge symmetry is exploited to analytically remove angular momentum alignment variables, leaving qubit registers that encode only the total angular momentum on each link. The plaquette operator is implemented as a quantum circuit with controlled operations that enforce Gauss's law through neighboring qubit registers, and the time evolution of a two-plaquette system with periodic boundary conditions is executed on IBM's Tokyo quantum device using Trotterization and error mitigation techniques including measurement error correction, CNOT extrapolation, and post-selection on the gauge-invariant subspace.

## Key numbers

- The qubit representation requires \(\lceil \log_2 (2 \Lambda_j + 1) \rceil\) qubits per link, where \(\Lambda_j\) is the angular momentum truncation per link.
- For \(\Lambda_j = 1/2\), the plaquette operator reduces to a five-qubit operator (after identifying top and bottom \(j\) values due to flux conservation).
- Non-zero matrix elements of the \(\Lambda_j = 1/2\) plaquette operator take values of 1, 1/2, and 1/4 in the physical Hilbert space.
- Before CNOT extrapolation, the \((N_{\text{Trot}}, r) = (1,1)\) measurements remained in the gauge-invariant space with a survival population of approximately 45%; after linear extrapolation this increased to approximately 65%.
- For \(N_{\text{Trot}} = 2\), the survival population was approximately 25%, consistent with loss of quantum coherence of a four-dimensional physical space embedded onto four qubits.
- The electric energy calculation used coupling \(g^2 = 0.2\) for the two-plaquette lattice.

## Context

This work extends the program of simulating lattice gauge theories on quantum devices, following earlier implementations of gauge theories on quantum hardware by Martinez (2016), Klco (2018), and others, which used various procedures to remove unphysical Hilbert space that do not scale efficiently. The improved mapping reduces the Hilbert space by solving Gauss's law through analytic incorporation of angular momentum alignment, building on the angular momentum basis approach of Kogut and Susskind (1975) and subsequent work by Burgio (1999). The results demonstrate that near-term quantum hardware can maintain gauge invariance with approximately 65% survival probability after error mitigation, enabling future extensions to higher angular momentum truncations and more complex lattice geometries.
