# Density dependence of symmetry free energy of hot nuclei

> The density and excitation energy dependence of symmetry energy and symmetry free energy for finite nuclei are calculated microscopically in a microcanonical framework taking into account thermal and expansion effects. A finite-range momentum and density dependent two-body effective interaction is employed for this purpose. The role of mass, isospin and equation of state (EoS) on these quantities is also investigated; our calculated results are in consonance with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The symmetry free energy coefficient, rather than the symmetry energy coefficient, is the quantity that should be compared with experimental data from nuclear multifragmentation, and the calculated values follow the experimental trend well when the hot nucleus is treated in a microcanonical framework that includes expansion to maximal entropy, but the agreement is less satisfactory in a grand canonical approach without expansion.

## Method

The authors employ a finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi (FTTF) formulation with a modified Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) finite-range, momentum- and density-dependent effective interaction to calculate density profiles of hot nuclei. The hot system is treated microcanonically, with expansion simulated through self-similar scaling of the density profile to find the configuration of maximal entropy at fixed excitation energy. Symmetry energy and symmetry free energy coefficients are computed in the local density approximation (LDA) and improved by adding second-order gradient corrections in the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) framework.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy coefficient of infinite nuclear matter at saturation density: \(C_E(\rho_0) = 34.0\) MeV, with density dependence exponent \(\gamma = 0.65\) for \(\rho/\rho_0\) between 0.1 and 1.
- For infinite nuclear matter at equilibrium, the density falls as \(\rho = \rho_0(1 - 0.04 E^*/A)\) with \(E^*\) in MeV, and the symmetry coefficients follow \(C_E(E^*/A) \simeq C_E(0)(1 - 0.024 E^*/A)\) and \(C_F(E^*/A) \simeq C_F(0)(1 - 0.028 E^*/A)\).
- For finite nuclei, the excitation energy dependence of the symmetry free energy coefficient is \(C_F(E^*/A) = C_F(0)(1 - \alpha_F E^*/A)\) with \(\alpha_F \simeq 0.054\) MeV\(^{-1}\), and for the symmetry energy coefficient \(\alpha_E \simeq 0.064\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- A least-squares fit of the density-dependent symmetry free energy coefficient for finite nuclei yields surface constant \(\kappa_s = 1.46\), surface density exponent \(\gamma_2 = 0.17\), and asymmetry correction \(\kappa_{sym} = 1.55\), with root mean square deviation \(\simeq 6\%\).
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter for the soft EoS is \(K_\infty = 238\) MeV (density exponent \(n = 1/6\)), and for the stiff EoS \(K_\infty = 380\) MeV (\(n = 4/3\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the symmetry energy coefficient (Samaddar et al., 2007) by computing the symmetry free energy coefficient and improving the local density approximation with second-order gradient corrections. It addresses the ongoing discussion (Baldwin et al., 2005; Kowalski et al., 2007; Raduta et al., 2007) about whether experimental symmetry coefficients from multifragmentation correspond to symmetry energy or symmetry free energy, taking the latter view. The results enable more reliable extraction of symmetry coefficients from experimental data on isoscaling, isotopic distributions, and isospin diffusion, and provide a framework for testing the equation of state through comparison with data from sources such as Shetty et al. (2005) and Fevre et al. (2005).
