# Role of pair-vibrational correlations in forming the odd-even mass difference

> In the random-phase-approximation-amended (RPA-amended) Nilsson-Strutinskij method of calculating nuclear binding energies, the conventional shell correction terms derived from the independent-nucleon model and the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer pairing theory are supplemented by a term which accounts for the pair-vibrational correlation energy. This term is derived by means of the RPA from a pairing Hamiltonian which includes a neutron-proton pairing interaction. The method was used previously in studies of the pattern of binding energies of nuclei with approximately equal numbers $N$ and $Z$ of neutrons and protons and even mass number $A = N + Z$. Here it is applied to odd-$A$ nuclei. Three sets of such nuclei are considered: (i) The sequence of nuclei with $Z = N - 1$ and $25 \le A \le 99$. (ii) The odd-$A$ isotopes of In, Sn, and Sb with $46 \le N \le 92$. (iii) The odd-$A$ isotopes of Sr, Y, Zr, Nb, and Mo with $60 \le N \le 64$. The RPA correction is found to contribute significantly to the calculated odd-even mass differences, particularly in the light nuclei. In the upper $sd$ shell this correction accounts for almost the entire odd-even mass difference for odd $Z$ and about half of it for odd $N$. The size and sign of the RPA contribution varies, which is explained qualitatively in terms of a closed expression for a smooth RPA counter term.

## Key claim

The RPA-amended Nilsson-Strutinskij method, previously applied only to even-A nuclei, is extended to odd-A nuclei and shows that pair-vibrational correlations contribute significantly to odd-even mass differences, with the size and sign of the contribution varying across nuclear regions; in the upper sd shell the RPA correction accounts for almost the entire odd-even mass difference for odd Z and about half of it for odd N, but this result depends on the specific region and the proximity of the pairing coupling constant to the BCS threshold.

## Method

The binding energy is calculated as a liquid drop energy plus shell corrections from independent nucleons, BCS pairing theory, and an RPA term derived from a pairing Hamiltonian that includes neutron-proton pairing interactions. For odd-A nuclei, the unpaired nucleon blocks the Fermi level in the BCS calculation, and the RPA calculation omits the blocked single-nucleon level from the set of pair excitations. Three sets of odd-A nuclei are studied: (i) nuclei with Z = N - 1 and 25 ≤ A ≤ 99, (ii) odd-A isotopes of In, Sn, and Sb with 46 ≤ N ≤ 92, and (iii) odd-A isotopes of Sr, Y, Zr, Nb, and Mo with 60 ≤ N ≤ 64.

## Key numbers

- For the N ≈ Z region, the pairing coupling constant is fit as G = 7.196 A^(-0.7461) MeV with an rms deviation of 0.789 MeV from the fit data
- The liquid drop contribution to the odd-even mass difference for Z = N - 1 nuclei is negative except for N = 43, with an average about -0.4 MeV
- The calculated χττ' values decrease from about 3.8 for A = 24 to about 2.6 for A = 100, which determines the sign and magnitude of the RPA contribution via the function f'(χ)
- For the Sn region, the rms deviation of the liquid drop fit is 0.515 MeV; for the ¹⁰²Zr region, the rms deviation is 0.043 MeV from only 9 doubly even nuclei

## Context

This work extends the RPA-amended Nilsson-Strutinskij method previously developed by Bentley, Frauendorf, and Neergård (2014) and refined by Neergård (2016, 2017) for even-A nuclei with N ≈ Z, now applying it to odd-A nuclei. The approach contrasts with Vaquero, Egido, and Rodríguez (2013), who used generator coordinate methods with Gogny interactions to study pairing fluctuations. The results enable systematic calculation of odd-even mass differences across broader regions of the nuclear chart and support an interpretation of the lowest T ≈ 0 states of doubly odd nuclei as essentially two-quasinucleon states.
