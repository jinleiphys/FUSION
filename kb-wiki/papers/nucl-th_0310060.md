# Low-Mass Proton-Antiproton Enhancement: Belle and BES Results, Premises of LEAR and Expectations from CLAS

> We present a simple explanation for the recently observed near-threshold proton-antiproton enhancement. It is described by a set of low-energy parameters deduced from the analysis of NantiN experiments at LEAR. We predict a related effect in photoproduction reaction under study by CLAS collaboration.

# Low-Mass Proton-Antiproton Enhancement: Belle and BES Results, Premises of LEAR and Expectations from CLAS

## Key claim
The near-threshold proton-antiproton enhancement observed by Belle and BES can be explained by low-energy effective range parameters extracted from LEAR experiments, without invoking exotic states. The explanation specifically accounts for the BES observation of a strong effect in J/psi to gamma p pbar and the absence of a similar structure in J/psi to pi0 p pbar, which follows from the dominance of the isospin I=0 S-wave over the I=1 channel.

## Method
The authors apply the Migdal-Watson final-state interaction formalism to describe the low-energy p pbar interaction, using an enhancement factor D(q) that incorporates Coulomb attraction via the Sakharov factor, isospin mixing, annihilation (complex scattering lengths), and the opening of the n nbar channel. The enhancement factor is constructed from S-wave scattering lengths a0 and a1 extracted from earlier effective range analyses of LEAR data (Kerbikov et al. 1991, 1992, 1993), and is applied to predict the invariant mass distribution in the photoproduction reaction gamma p to p p pbar.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length a0 = (-1.2 + i0.9) fm from one LEAR analysis (Kerbikov 1991) and a0 = (-1.1 + i0.4) fm from another (Kerbikov 1992), showing dominance of the I=0 channel over I=1.
- Scattering length a1 = (-0.1 + i0.4) fm from one analysis and a1 = (0.3 + i0.8) fm from the other.
- Bohr radius of the p pbar atom a_B = 57.6 fm, derived from a_B = 2/(alpha m).
- The n nbar threshold occurs at q = (m delta)^(1/2) approximately 49 MeV/c, where delta = 2(m_n - m).
- The effective range approximation is valid up to q approximately 150 MeV/c, corresponding to Q = m_23 - 2m approximately 20 MeV.
- Schwinger correction Delta approximately -0.08 fm^(-1).

## Context
This work extends the effective range analysis of low-energy N Nbar interactions developed by Kerbikov et al. (1991, 1992, 1993) from LEAR data to explain the near-threshold p pbar enhancements recently observed by Belle in B+ to K+ p pbar and B0bar to D0 p pbar, and by BES in J/psi to gamma p pbar. It contrasts with earlier theoretical explanations that invoked gluonic states (Datta and O'Donnell 2003), color-spin interactions (Brodsky 2003), or one-pion-exchange potentials (Suzuki 2003). The prediction for the photoproduction reaction gamma p to p p pbar, which can be tested by the CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Lab, provides a new experimental avenue distinct from B and J/psi decays.
