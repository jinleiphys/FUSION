# J/psi suppression in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> Using a multiphase transport model, we study the relative importance of J/psi suppression mechanisms due to plasma screening, gluon scattering, and hadron absorption in heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. We find that for collisions between heavy nuclei such as Au+Au, both plasma screening and gluon scattering are important. As a result, the effect due to absorption by hadrons becomes relatively minor. The final J/psi survival probability in these collisions is only a few percent. In the case of collisions between light nuclei such as S+S, the effect of plasma screening is, however, negligible in spite of the initial high parton density. The final J/psi survival probability thus remains appreciable after comparable absorption effects due to gluons and hadrons.

## Key claim

Using a multiphase transport model (AMPT), the authors find that in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, both plasma screening and gluon scattering contribute comparably to J/psi suppression, while hadron absorption is minor; the final J/psi survival probability is only about 6%. However, this result depends on the assumed cross sections and the inclusion of finite-size effects, and the survival probability can range from about 6% to about 26% depending on which suppression mechanisms are included.

## Method

The authors extended the AMPT model, which uses HIJING for initial conditions, Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for parton evolution, Lund string fragmentation for hadronization, and a relativistic transport model (ART) for hadronic dynamics, to include J/psi production via the perturbative reaction gg → J/psi g from PYTHIA. They then simulated the time evolution of J/psi suppression from three mechanisms: plasma screening (using a critical parton density of ~5 fm^-3), gluon scattering (with cross sections of 3 mb or 1 mb), and hadron absorption (with cross sections of 6 mb by baryons and 3 mb by mesons), tracking the survival probability in central Au+Au and S+S collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- In central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, the initial parton density is about 30 fm^-3, well above the critical density for J/psi dissociation of about 5 fm^-3.
- For Au+Au with plasma screening, gluon scattering (3 mb), and hadron absorption: gluon absorption probability = 43.9%, plasma dissociation = 47.1%, hadron absorption = 2.9%, survival probability = 6.1%.
- For Au+Au with plasma screening, gluon scattering (1 mb), and hadron absorption: gluon absorption = 20.6%, plasma dissociation = 62.4%, hadron absorption = 6.5%, survival probability = 10.5%.
- For Au+Au with only hadron absorption: survival probability = 43.3%.
- For S+S collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV with plasma screening, gluon scattering (3 mb), and hadron absorption: gluon absorption = 39.3%, plasma dissociation = 0%, hadron absorption = 10.6%, survival probability = 50.1%.
- For S+S with only hadron absorption: survival probability = 80.4%.
- The critical radius for plasma dissociation in Au+Au is about 6 fm at the start of the parton cascade and vanishes after about 1.2 fm/c; in S+S it is about 2 fm initially and vanishes after about 0.5 fm/c.
- The J/psi formation time is taken as 0.5 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of J/psi suppression using dynamical models, such as the parton cascade model of Satz and Srivastava (2000), by explicitly comparing the relative importance of plasma screening, gluon scattering, and hadron absorption within a single transport framework. The results show that finite-size effects are important even in central Au+Au collisions, and that for light systems like S+S, plasma screening is negligible despite high initial parton density, enabling future experimental study of J/psi-gluon interactions. The authors note that their model does not include J/psi from charm quark recombination or from decays of psi' and chi_c, which would likely reduce the survival probability further.
