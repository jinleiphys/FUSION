# Rapidity spectra in high-energy collisions and longitudinal nuclear suppression from nonadditive statistics

> We investigate the longitudinal nuclear suppression factor defined by a scaled ratio of rapidity distributions. To study this experimental observable, we describe three approaches involving numerical and analytical calculations. We first approach this problem by conducting model studies using EPOS, FTFP$_{BERT}$, and HIJING, and notice that while EPOS shows a decreasing trend of this ratio at forward/backward rapidities, the latter two model calculations display an increment of the ratio. The analytical approaches involve, first, the quasi-exponential distribution obtained from the Tsallis statistics, and second, the nonadditive Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation. We notice that our analytical results satisfactorily describe NA61 experimental data (for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=6.3, 7.6, 8.8, 12.3, and 17.3 GeV) for the negatively charged pions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the longitudinal nuclear suppression factor \(R_{dN/dy}\) can be described by analytical models based on nonadditive (Tsallis) statistics, but only for physically acceptable parameter values; the claim is hedged by the observation that the EPOS event generator fails to reproduce the experimental trend, while FTFP\(_{\text{BERT}}\) and HIJING do reproduce it.

## Method

The authors first performed Monte Carlo simulations using EPOS, FTFP\(_{\text{BERT}}\), and HIJING for Pb-Pb and p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.6\) and 17.3 GeV, computing the ratio of rapidity distributions scaled by the average number of binary collisions. They then derived two analytical models: (1) a closed-form expression for \(R_{dN/dy}\) by integrating a Tsallis-type single-particle distribution over transverse momentum using Mellin-Barnes contour integration, yielding a hypergeometric function; (2) a solution of the nonadditive Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, using a perturbative expansion to obtain a zeroth-order distribution that evolves from an initial Tsallis form to a final power-law stationary state. Both analytical results were compared to NA61/SHINE experimental data for negatively charged pions at five beam energies.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear suppression from the transverse plane (the usual \(R_{\text{AA}}\)) to the longitudinal plane via \(R_{dN/dy}\), a ratio of rapidity distributions. It builds on prior use of Tsallis statistics to describe transverse momentum spectra (Cleymans, 2012; Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000) and on the nonadditive Boltzmann transport equation formalism developed by Bhattacharyya (2020). The paper identifies a discrepancy among event generators (EPOS versus FTFP\(_{\text{BERT}}\) and HIJING) that warrants future investigation. The analytical results enable extraction of parameters such as the entropic index \(q\), temperature, and the freeze-out-to-relaxation time ratio from experimental data, and suggest that pions undergo Levy walks (anomalous diffusion) in the medium, consistent with the findings of Csanad (2023).
