# Large-scale calculations of supernova neutrino-induced reactions in Z=8-82 target nuclei

> Background: In the environment of high neutrino-fluxes provided in core-collapse supernovae or neutron star mergers, neutrino-induced reactions with nuclei contribute to the nucleosynthesis processes. A number of terrestrial neutrino detectors are based on inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering and modeling of the respective cross sections allow predictions of the expected detector reaction rates. Purpose: To provide a self-consistent microscopic description of neutrino-nucleus cross sections involving a large pool of Z = 8 - 82 nuclei for the implementation in models of nucleosynthesis and neutrino detector simulations. Methods: Self-consistent theory framework based on relativistic nuclear energy density functional is employed to determine the nuclear structure of the initial state and relevant transitions to excited states induced by neutrinos. The weak neutrino-nucleus interaction is employed in the current-current form and a complete set of transition operators is taken into account. Results: We perform large-scale calculations of charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections, including those averaged over supernova neutrino fluxes, for the set of even-even target nuclei from oxygen toward lead (Z = 8 - 82), spanning N = 8 - 182 (OPb pool). The model calculations include allowed and forbidden transitions up to J = 5 multipoles. Conclusions: The present analysis shows that the self-consistent calculations result in considerable differences in comparison to previously reported cross sections, and for a large number of target nuclei the cross sections are enhanced. Revision in modeling r-process nucleosynthesis based on a self-consistent description of neutrino-induced reactions would allow an updated insight into the origin of elements in the Universe and it would provide the estimate of uncertainties in the calculated element abundance patterns.

## Key claim

The self-consistent relativistic nuclear energy density functional calculations produce charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections that are systematically larger than previously reported values for most target nuclei, with enhancements up to a factor of 7, but the authors note that the cross sections are sensitive to the choice of neutrino spectrum and that supernova simulation spectra yield significantly lower flux-averaged cross sections than Fermi-Dirac spectra.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic nuclear energy density functional (RNEDF) framework, using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model for ground states and the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) for excited states, with the DD-ME2 parameterization and Gogny D1S pairing. They calculated charged-current electron neutrino-nucleus cross sections for even-even target nuclei from oxygen to lead (Z=8-82, N=8-182), including all multipole transitions up to J=5, and averaged these over Michel spectra, Fermi-Dirac distributions, and supernova simulation spectra from three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections for neutron-rich nuclei are larger by a factor of approximately 2-3 compared to those in the valley of stability
- For 56Fe, the theoretical uncertainty from different microscopic models gives <σ>_th = (258 ± 57) × 10^{-42} cm^2 for the Michel spectrum
- For target nuclei with A ≤ 50, forbidden transitions contribute less than 10% of the total flux-averaged cross sections; for heavier nuclei, forbidden transitions can provide up to 50% of the total cross section
- The present large-scale calculations yield cross sections systematically larger than the ETFSI + CQRPA model, for most nuclei up to a factor of 4, and for a smaller number of nuclei up to a factor of 7
- Supernova simulation spectra at post-bounce time t_pb = 1 s give mean neutrino energy ⟨E⟩ = 9.32 MeV and mean-square energy ⟨E^2⟩ = 108.57 MeV^2, compared to Fermi-Dirac (T=4 MeV) values of ⟨E⟩ = 12.60 MeV and ⟨E^2⟩ = 207.03 MeV^2
- At t_pb = 20 s, the supernova spectrum mean energy drops to ⟨E⟩ = 6.73 MeV with ⟨E^2⟩ = 58.32 MeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous large-scale calculations of neutrino-induced reactions by Borzov and Goriely (2000) using ETFSI + CQRPA and by Langanke and Kolbe (2001) using RPA with Landau-Migdal force and Wood-Saxon potential, but provides the first systematic calculations using a fully self-consistent energy density functional framework. The results enable improved modeling of r-process nucleosynthesis in supernova and neutron star merger environments, and provide cross section tables for neutrino detector simulations involving a wide range of target materials.
