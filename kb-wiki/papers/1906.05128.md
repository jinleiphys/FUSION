# Transport anomalies near the QCD phase transition at finite density

> We show that at finite density pressure-pressure and current-current correlators exhibit divergences at $T_c$ owing to the fluctuations of the diquark field. Specifically, this leads to a significant excess of the soft photon production rate near $T_c$.

# Transport anomalies near the QCD phase transition at finite density

## Key claim
At finite density, pressure-pressure and current-current correlators exhibit divergences at the critical temperature due to fluctuations of the diquark field, leading to a significant excess of soft photon production near T_c. The claim is conditional on the system being in the 2SC color superconducting phase with quark chemical potential around 200-300 MeV and critical temperature around 40 MeV, with both numbers being "educated guesses" based on model calculations.

## Method
The author evaluates the Aslamazov-Larkin (AL) diagram containing two singular fluctuation propagators of the diquark field and three quark Matsubara propagators, using a relativistic fluctuation propagator derived from either the Dyson equation or the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation with stochastic Langevin forces. The retarded response functions for electromagnetic and sound modes are computed by summing over internal Matsubara frequencies and integrating over internal momenta, keeping only terms linear in the external frequency. The electrical conductivity and sound attenuation coefficient are extracted from the imaginary parts of these response functions.

## Key numbers
- Quark chemical potential: μ ≃ 200-300 MeV
- Critical temperature: T_c ≃ 40 MeV
- Ginzburg-Levanyuk number: Gi ≃ δT/T_c ≃ (T_c/μ)^4 ≲ 10^{-3}
- For BCS superconductor comparison: Gi ∼ 10^{-12} to 10^{-14}
- Diffusion coefficient: D = 0.17 fm (assuming v=1, τ=0.5 fm)
- Electrical conductivity at ε = 10^{-3}: σ ≃ 0.18 fm^{-1}
- Soft photon production rate at ε = 10^{-3}: lim_{ω→0} ω dR/d^3p ≃ 0.73 × 10^{-2} fm^{-4} GeV^{-2}
- Electrical conductivity divergence: σ ∼ (T-T_c)/T_c)^{-1/2}
- Sound attenuation divergence: γ ∼ (T-T_c)/T_c)^{-3/2}
- Bulk viscosity divergence: ζ(T) ∼ ε^{-3/2}

## Context
This work extends the study of fluctuation effects in color superconductors, noting that unlike BCS superconductors with an almost sharp phase transition, the transition in color superconductors is significantly smeared (Kerbikov, prior work). The fluctuation contribution is characterized by a Ginzburg-Levanyuk number Gi ∼ 10^{-3}, much larger than the BCS value of 10^{-12} to 10^{-14}. The temperature dependence of bulk viscosity is found to be close to the scaling law ζ ∼ ξ^{z - α/ν} with dynamical critical exponent z ≃ 3, correlation length critical exponent ν ≃ 0.6, and heat capacity critical exponent α ≃ 0.11. The enhanced soft photon production near T_c provides a tentative suggestion for experimental investigation at FAIR and NICA facilities.
