# Total kinetic energy release in the fast neutron induced fission of 232Th and 235U

> We have measured the total kinetic energy release (TKE), its variance and associated fission product distributions for the neutron induced fission of 232Th and 235U for En = 2 - 90 MeV. The neutron energies were determined on an event by event basis by time of flight measurements with the white spectrum neutron beam from LANSCE. The TKE decreases non-linearly with increasing neutron energy for both systems, while the TKE variances are sensitive indicators of nth chance fission. The associated fission product distributions show the decrease in TKE with increasing beam energy that is due to the increasing probability of symmetric fission, which has a lower associated TKE, and the decreasing TKE associated with asymmetric fission, presumably due to the decreasing importance of the A = 132 shell structures.

## Key claim

The total kinetic energy release (TKE) in fast neutron induced fission of ²³²Th and ²³⁵U decreases non-linearly with increasing neutron energy from 2 to 90 MeV, but this decrease is not solely due to the increased probability of symmetric fission; the TKE associated with asymmetric fission also decreases with increasing neutron energy, presumably due to the diminishing importance of the A ≈ 132 shell structures.

## Method

The experiment used the white-spectrum neutron beam from the LANSCE facility, with neutron energies determined event-by-event via time-of-flight measurements. Fission fragments from ²³²Th and ²³⁵U targets were detected over a 5-day period in December 2014, and the pre-neutron emission TKE, its variance, and fission product mass distributions were extracted for neutron energy bins from 2 to 90 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For thermal neutron induced fission of ²³⁵U, the measured post-fission TKE was 169.8 ± 0.4 MeV and the pre-fission TKE was 170.7 ± 0.4 MeV.
- The TKE decreased by 6.0 MeV for ²³²Th and by 7.4 MeV for ²³⁵U as the neutron energy increased from approximately 0 to 90 MeV.
- The Viola systematics predict TKE values of 170.1 MeV for ²³⁵U(n,f) and 163.8 MeV for ²³²Th(n,f).
- For the spontaneous fission of ²⁵²Cf, the average post-fission light fragment TKE was 103.2 ± 1.1 MeV and the average heavy fragment TKE was 78.6 ± 1.5 MeV.
- The ²³⁵U data contained about 75,000 events while the ²³²Th data contained about 20,000 events.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of TKE in neutron-induced fission at lower energies, such as those by Madland (year not given), Duke (year not given), Trochon (year not given), Sergachev (year not given), and Kiesewetter (year not given), and is consistent with the non-linear decrease in TKE observed by Ferguson et al. (year not given) in ²³⁵U(p,f) reactions decades earlier. The results enable testing of semi-empirical fission models such as GEF and the model by Lestone (year not given), and challenge the earlier conclusion by Brosa et al. (year not given) that the reduced TKE with increasing neutron energy could be attributed simply to an increase in symmetric fission.
