# On the effect of the Delta(1232) in hypernuclear non-mesonic weak decay: a microscopic approach

> The non-mesonic weak decay of $Λ$-hypernuclei is studied within a microscopic diagrammatic approach which includes, for the first time, the effect or the $Δ$-baryon resonance. We adopt a nuclear matter formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, a one-meson exchange weak transition potential, a Bonn nucleon-nucleon strong potential and a $ΔN\to NN$ strong potential based on the Landau-Migdal theory. Ground state correlations and final state interactions (FSI), at second order in the baryon-baryon strong interaction, are introduced on the same footing for all the isospin channels of one- and two-nucleon induced decays. Weak decay rates and single and double-coincidence nucleon spectra are predicted for $^{12}_Λ$C and compared with recent KEK and FINUDA data. The $Δ(1232)$ introduces new FSI-induced decay mechanisms which lead to an improvement when comparing the obtained nucleon spectra with data, while it turns out to have a negligible effect on the decay rates. Discrepancies with experiment remain only for emission spectra involving protons, but are mostly restricted to double-nucleon correlations in the non-back-to-back kinematics.

## Key claim

The Delta(1232) resonance introduces new final-state-interaction-induced decay mechanisms that improve the agreement between calculated nucleon emission spectra and experimental data for hypernuclear non-mesonic weak decay, but it has a negligible effect on the total decay rates themselves.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic diagrammatic approach using nuclear matter formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, applied to the hypernucleus 12-Lambda-C. They constructed Goldstone diagrams for the Lambda self-energy that included, for the first time, the Delta(1232) baryon resonance, using a one-meson exchange weak transition potential, a Bonn nucleon-nucleon strong potential, and a Delta-N to NN strong potential based on Landau-Migdal theory, with ground state correlations and final state interactions treated at second order in the baryon-baryon strong interaction.

## Key numbers

- The Q-value of non-mesonic decay is approximately 177 MeV (mass difference m_Lambda minus m_n), which is insufficient to produce a Delta in the final state.
- The contribution of diagram (a) to the two-nucleon decay rate Gamma_2 in 12-Lambda-C is 0.002 in units of the free Lambda decay rate Gamma_Lambda_free, while the total Gamma_2 equals 0.36 Gamma_Lambda_free.
- The N_nn/N_np ratio for the back-to-back region (cos theta_NN' less than -0.7) with a 30 MeV nucleon kinetic energy threshold is 0.37 with the Delta included, compared to the KEK-E508 experimental value of 0.60 plus or minus 0.12.
- The N_nn/N_np ratio for the non-back-to-back region (cos theta_NN' greater than -0.7) with a 30 MeV threshold is 0.42 with the Delta included, compared to the KEK-E508 experimental value of 1.38 plus or minus 0.53.
- The hadronic form factor cutoffs used are: Lambda_pi_NN = Lambda_pi_Delta_N = 1300 MeV/c, Lambda_rho_NN = 1400 MeV/c, and Lambda_rho_Delta_N = 1700 MeV/c.
- The Landau-Migdal constant g'_Delta_N is 0.4, and the coupling constants include f^2_pi_NN/4pi = 0.081, f_pi_Delta_N = 2 f_pi_NN, and C_rho = 2.18.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic diagrammatic approach developed by Bauer and Garbarino (2011) and earlier papers by Bauer (2007, 2010) to include the Delta(1232) resonance for the first time in hypernuclear non-mesonic weak decay calculations. The approach improves upon intranuclear cascade models by providing a fully quantum-mechanical treatment that includes ground state correlations and final state interactions on the same footing, and it enables future systematic investigation of rare non-mesonic weak decays of Lambda-Lambda-hypernuclei, such as Lambda-Lambda to Lambda n, Lambda-Lambda to Sigma- p, and Lambda-Lambda to n n.
