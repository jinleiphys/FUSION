# $A=2,3$ nuclear contact coefficients in the Generalized Contact Formalism

> This work focuses on extracting nuclear contact coefficients for \( A = 2 \), \( A = 3 \) and \( A = 4 \) nuclei within the Generalized Contact Formalism framework. We investigate the universality of these coefficients across different nuclear systems and interaction models, using both local (in \( r \)-space) and non-local (in \( k \)-space) chiral potentials. The Hyperspherical Harmonics method is employed to calculate the nuclear wave functions from which we obtain the two-body momentum distributions and the two-body density functions, which are essential for extracting the contact coefficients. The adopted method is a rigorous ab-initio approach that can be applied to virtually any potential. We present ratios of contact coefficients across various spin and isospin channels, highlighting their independence from the used nuclear potential. This study extends previous work where only local interaction models were employed. Furthermore, we verify whether the contact coefficient ratio between different nuclei remains consistent even when non-local potentials are considered. Future work will extend this analysis to heavier nuclei, such as \( A = 4 \) and \( A = 6 \) nuclei.

## Key claim
The ratios of nuclear contact coefficients for the dominant neutron-proton spin-1 channel in A=3 nuclei relative to the deuteron are largely independent of the specific nuclear interaction employed, including both local and non-local chiral potentials, confirming the universality previously established only for local interactions.

## Method
The authors used the Hyperspherical Harmonics (HH) ab-initio method to compute nuclear wave functions for A=2 and A=3 nuclei, from which they extracted two-body momentum distributions (2BMDs) in k-space and two-body density functions (2BDFs) in r-space. Contact coefficients were obtained by taking the ratio of these distributions to the universal function (zero-energy scattering states for S=0, deuteron ground state for S=1) in the asymptotic regions of large relative momentum or small relative distance, both with and without the assumption of zero relative angular momentum (ℓ=0). Calculations were performed using the local AV18/UIX potential, local Norfolk chiral potentials (NV2+3/Ia*, NV2+3/IIa*, NV2+3/Ib*, NV2+3/IIb*), and non-local chiral NN interactions at orders N2LO, N3LO, and N4LO with cutoffs of 450, 500, and 550 MeV, supplemented with local three-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers
- For local potentials in k-space, the plateau region (where contact coefficients are extracted) occurs beyond 3.5–4 fm⁻¹ relative momentum.
- For non-local potentials, the plateau region in k-space occurs at slightly smaller values of k than for local potentials.
- The non-local chiral potentials used include cutoffs of Λ = 450, 500, and 550 MeV at chiral orders N2LO, N3LO, and N4LO.
- The contact coefficient ratios for the np S=1 channel in A=3 nuclei (³H and ³He) relative to the deuteron are shown for all interaction models and agree with results from Ref. (2021).

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear contact coefficients within the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) by Cruz-Torres et al. (2021), which previously used only local interaction models, to include non-local chiral potentials. The results confirm the model-independence of contact coefficient ratios across different nuclei, supporting the use of "soft" potentials for heavier nuclei where the contact coefficient for a "hard" potential can be inferred by multiplying the ratio from lighter nuclei. Future work will extend this analysis to A=4 and A=6 nuclei using the HH method.
