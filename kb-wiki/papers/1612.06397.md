# Pseudomagnetic helicons

> The existence of pseudomagnetic helicons is predicted for strained Dirac and Weyl materials. The corresponding collective modes are reminiscent of the usual helicons in metals in strong magnetic fields but can exist even without a magnetic field due to a strain-induced background pseudomagnetic field. The properties of both pseudomagnetic and magnetic helicons are investigated in Weyl matter using the formalism of the consistent chiral kinetic theory. It is argued that the helicon dispersion relations are affected by the electric and chiral chemical potentials, the chiral shift, and the energy separation between the Weyl nodes. The effects of multiple pairs of Weyl nodes are also discussed. A simple setup for experimental detection of pseudomagnetic helicons is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of "pseudomagnetic helicons", gapless collective modes in strained Dirac and Weyl materials that exist without an external magnetic field, relying instead on a strain-induced pseudomagnetic field. The authors hedge that in equilibrium (where the chiral chemical potential equals \(-eb_0\)), the dispersion is quadratic in wave vector, while a linear dispersion requires an out-of-equilibrium state with \(eb_0 + \mu_5 \neq 0\).

## Method

Using the consistent chiral kinetic theory, the authors derived the polarization vector and dielectric tensor for Weyl matter with one pair of Weyl nodes in static background magnetic and pseudomagnetic fields, including the effects of the chiral shift parameter \(\mathbf{b}\), energy separation between Weyl nodes \(b_0\), and temperature. They solved the characteristic equation for low-energy collective excitations in the long-wavelength limit (\(v_F k \ll \Omega_c\)), retaining only the lowest three Fourier harmonics (\(n = 0, \pm 1\)) of the perturbed distribution function, to obtain the helicon dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- Strain-induced pseudomagnetic fields in Cd\(_3\)As\(_2\) can reach \(B_5 \approx 0.3\) T when a static torsion is applied to a nanowire and \(B_5 \approx 15\) T when a thin film is bent (Pikulin 2016, Liu-Pikulin 2016).
- The background refractive index of Cd\(_3\)As\(_2\) is \(n_0 \approx 6\) (Freyland).
- The Fermi velocity used for numerical calculations is \(v_F \approx 1.5 \times 10^8\) cm/s (Neupane).
- The reference chiral shift scale is defined as \(eb^{*} = 0.3 \pi \hbar v_F / c_3\) with lattice spacing \(c_3 \approx 25.480\) Å.
- Numerical examples show helicon frequencies on the order of \(10^7\) to \(10^8\) s\(^{-1}\) for wave vectors around \(10^4\) to \(10^5\) m\(^{-1}\) at \(B_{0,5} = 10^{-2}\) T, \(\mu_5 = 5\) meV, and temperatures from 0 to 100 K.

## Context

This work extends the study of helicons in Weyl materials initiated by Pellegrino (2015), who showed that the dispersion encodes information on the chiral shift parameter \(\mathbf{b}\). The authors previously analyzed chiral magnetic plasmons in magnetic and pseudomagnetic fields (Gorbar 2016). The prediction of pseudomagnetic helicons enables experimental detection using strained Weyl or Dirac materials without external magnetic fields, and the authors propose a simple setup for such detection.
