# Nucleation of Quark--Gluon Plasma Droplets

> The energy densities achieved during central collisions of large nuclei at the AGS may be high enough to allow the formation of quark--gluon plasma. We propose that most collisions at AGS energies produce superheated hadronic matter, but in rare events a droplet of quark--gluon plasma is nucleated. We estimate the probability of this to occur based on homogeneous and inhomogeneous nucleation theory and discuss possible experimental signals.

# Nucleation of Quark–Gluon Plasma Droplets

## Key claim

Most central Au+Au collisions at AGS energies (11.6 GeV/A) produce superheated hadronic matter rather than quark–gluon plasma; only in rare events (roughly 1 in 100 to 1 in 1000 central collisions) is a droplet of quark–gluon plasma nucleated, based on both homogeneous and inhomogeneous nucleation theory. The probability estimates carry large uncertainties due to the assumptions and parameters entering the theoretical models.

## Method

The author applies homogeneous nucleation theory to estimate the rate of plasma droplet formation from thermal fluctuations in a superheated hadronic gas, using a first-order phase transition equation of state with a bag constant B = (220 MeV)^4 for the quark–gluon plasma phase and a hadronic gas with a repulsive mean field of strength K = 1500 MeV·fm^3. The dynamical evolution of the collision is modeled as two Lorentz-contracted disks that interpenetrate and then expand hydrodynamically, with the nucleation rate integrated over the superheated trajectory in the T–μ_B plane. Inhomogeneous nucleation theory is also applied, where the probability is estimated that a hard nucleon–nucleon collision between a projectile and target nucleon that have suffered little energy loss produces a local fluctuation of sufficient mass (≈4 GeV) to seed plasma formation, using kinetic theory with a Poisson distribution for collisions and a binomial distribution for pion multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant: B = (220 MeV)^4
- Repulsive mean field strength: K = 1500 MeV·fm^3
- Average baryon density in a nucleus: n_0 ≈ 0.145 nucleons/fm^3
- Mean free path of nucleons in hot dense hadronic matter: λ ≈ 0.4 fm
- Time to reach coexistence curve: t_f = 7 fm
- Maximum droplet density at t_f: n_drop = 2 × 10^{-5} fm^{-3}
- Volume of expanding system: ≈ 2 × 10^3 fm^3
- Average number of droplets nucleated per central Au+Au collision: ≈ 1/100
- Volume fraction q of quark–gluon plasma: ≤ 10^{-2}
- Average droplet radius at t_f: ≤ 5.0 fm
- Critical mass for a plasma droplet: ≈ 4 GeV
- Mean negatively charged particle multiplicity for purely hadronic events: N_had = 145
- Upper limit for mean negatively charged particle multiplicity in rare plasma events: N_qg = 193
- Probability of at least one plasma seed via inhomogeneous nucleation: 1% to 10% per central Au+Au collision at highest AGS energy

## Context

This work extends earlier nucleation theory calculations by Kapusta, Vischer, and Venugopalan (1995) and Csernai and Kapusta (1992, 1984) to the specific case of AGS-energy Au+Au collisions, proposing that the apparent absence of quark–gluon plasma in most events can be explained by the rarity of nucleation rather than the absence of sufficient energy density. The paper enables experimental searches for a distinct class of rare events with enhanced entropy production, visible as a shoulder or second maximum in the charged particle multiplicity distribution, and suggests that hadron interferometry and deuteron-to-proton ratios could provide additional signals. The results contradict the expectation that plasma should be produced in every collision reaching energy densities near 2 GeV/fm^3, as suggested by hadronic cascade simulators like RQMD, ARC, and ART.
