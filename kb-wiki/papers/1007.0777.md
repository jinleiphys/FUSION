# Study of the gp-->etap reaction with the Crystal Ball detector at the Mainz Microtron(MAMI-C)

> The gp-->etap reaction has been measured with the Crystal Ball and TAPS multiphoton spectrometers in the energy range from the production threshold of 707 MeV to 1.4 GeV (1.49 =< W >= 1.87 GeV). Bremsstrahlung photons produced by the 1.5-GeV electron beam of the Mainz Microtron MAMI-C and momentum analyzed by the Glasgow Tagging Spectrometer were used for the eta-meson production. Our accumulation of 3.8 x 10^6 gp-->etap-->3pi0p-->6gp events allows a detailed study of the reaction dynamics. The gp-->etap differential cross sections were determined for 120 energy bins and the full range of the production angles. Our data show a dip near W = 1680 MeV in the total cross section caused by a substantial dip in eta production at forward angles. The data are compared to predictions of previous SAID and MAID partial-wave analyses and to thelatest SAID and MAID fits that have included our data.

## Key claim

The paper reports a high-statistics measurement of the differential cross section for the reaction γp→ηp from threshold to 1.4 GeV, and the data reveal a dip near W=1680 MeV in the total cross section caused by a substantial dip in η production at forward angles, rather than the narrow resonant bump that had been suggested for a possible N*(1680) state in quasi-free η photoproduction on neutrons.

## Method

The reaction γp→ηp was measured using the Crystal Ball and TAPS multiphoton spectrometers at the Mainz Microtron MAMI-C, with bremsstrahlung photons from a 1.5-GeV electron beam momentum-analyzed by the Glasgow Tagging Spectrometer. The η meson was detected via its decay η→3π0→6γ, and events were selected using kinematic fitting at a 2% confidence level, with subtraction of random-coincidence, empty-target, and direct 3π0 backgrounds. Differential cross sections were determined for 120 energy bins and 20 bins in cosθ of the η production angle in the center-of-mass frame, using Monte Carlo simulations for acceptance correction.

## Key numbers

* Total of 3.8×10^6 γp→ηp→3π0p→6γp events were accumulated.
* The measurement covers incident photon energies from the production threshold of 707 MeV to 1.4 GeV (center-of-mass energy W from 1.49 to 1.87 GeV).
* The detector acceptance for γp→ηp→3π0p events varies from about 45% at the η threshold to about 25% at Eγ=1.4 GeV.
* The general systematic uncertainty in the cross sections was estimated to be 4%, with additional statistical uncertainties in tagging efficiencies of 1.4% at threshold to 2.5% at the largest energies.
* The fraction of empty-target background varied from 1% at threshold to 2.7% at 1.4 GeV; the fraction of direct 3π0 background varied from 0.3% at threshold to 4.4% at 1.4 GeV.
* The data show a dip near W=1680 MeV in the total cross section.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of γp→ηp from experiments at MAMI-B (Krusche et al., 1995), CLAS (Dugger et al., 2002; Williams et al., 2009), GRAAL (Renard et al., 2002), LNS (Nakabayashi et al., 2006), CB-ELSA (Crede et al., 2005), and CB-ELSA/TAPS (Crede et al., 2009), providing much finer energy binning and smaller statistical uncertainties. The data enable improved partial-wave analyses using the SAID and MAID frameworks, with the new SAID fit GE09 and the Regge-MAID model incorporating these results to better determine the properties of six overlapping N* states in the energy range up to W≈1800 MeV, including N(1650)1/2−, N(1675)5/2−, N(1680)5/2+, N(1700)3/2−, N(1710)1/2+, and N(1720)3/2+.
