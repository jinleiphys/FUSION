# Symmetry conserving configuration mixing method with cranked states

> We present the first calculations of a symmetry conserving configuration mixing method (SCCM) using time-reversal symmetry breaking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states with the Gogny D1S interaction. The method includes particle number and tridimensional angular momentum symmetry restorations as well as configuration mixing within the generator coordinate method (GCM) framework. The nucleus $^{32}$Mg is chosen to show the performance and reliability of the calculations. Additionally, $0^{+}_{1}$, $2^{+}_{1}$ and $4^{+}_{1}$ states are computed for the magnesium isotopic chain, where a noticeable compression of the spectrum is obtained by including cranked states, leading to a very good agreement with the known experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors present the first implementation of a symmetry conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) method that includes time-reversal symmetry breaking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states obtained via cranking, which compresses the calculated nuclear spectra and brings them into much better agreement with experimental data than previous SCCM calculations that only used time-reversal symmetric states.

## Method

The method constructs a set of intrinsic HFB states with different quadrupole deformations (β₂, γ) and cranking angular momenta Jc by minimizing the particle-number-projected HFB energy with constraints on Jx and quadrupole moments. These states are then projected onto good particle number and three-dimensional angular momentum, and finally mixed via the generator coordinate method (GCM) by solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equations. The Gogny D1S interaction is used throughout, and the method is applied to ³²Mg and the magnesium isotopic chain ²⁴–³⁴Mg.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ³²Mg: −253.498 MeV for the largest set (S₄) with 113 axial+triaxial states and Jc = 0, 2, 4.
- Ground state energies for smaller sets: −253.056 MeV (S₁, 17 axial states, Jc=0), −253.477 MeV (S₂, 49 axial+triaxial states, Jc=0), −253.486 MeV (S₃, 81 states, Jc=0,2).
- The minimum of the Jc=0 PN-VAP potential energy surface for ³²Mg is at the spherical point; a second minimum at β₂=0.45 lies about 1 MeV higher.
- For Jc=2 and Jc=4, the minima appear at prolate configurations with β₂=0.45, shifted to ≈2 MeV and ≈3 MeV higher than the Jc=0 minimum, respectively.
- The probability distribution W^J for a state with (β₂=0.5, γ=10°, Jc=4) peaks at J=4 for even J and J=5 for odd J, with practically zero probability for even (odd) J larger than 16 (13).
- The 2₁⁺ and 4₁⁺ excitation energies for ³²Mg from the S₄ calculation show remarkable agreement with experimental data; the 0₂⁺ state is not reproduced.
- For the Mg isotopic chain ²⁴–³⁴Mg, adding Jc=2 states compresses the spectrum and yields outstanding agreement with experimental 2₁⁺ and 4₁⁺ energies, except for ²⁴Mg where theoretical values tend to be lower than experimental.

## Context

This work extends the SCCM framework of Rodriguez and Egido (2010) by adding time-reversal symmetry breaking intrinsic states through cranking, which had been missing in previous implementations that only used axial or triaxial time-reversal symmetric states. Earlier axial SCCM calculations with Skyrme functionals (2007) and Gogny functionals (2015) systematically overestimated 2₁⁺ excitation energies by a factor of about 1.2–1.4; the present results show this discrepancy can be corrected by including triaxial and cranked states. The method is a generalization of the double projection method of Peierls and Thouless (1962) and is expected to provide moments of inertia close to experimental values, which is confirmed by the results. Future work will include calculations of electromagnetic properties.
