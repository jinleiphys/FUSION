# Possible $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ molecular states in the extended constituent quark models

> The possible neutral $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ molecular states are studied in the framework of the constituent quark models, which is extended by including the $s$-channel one gluon exchange. Using different types of quark-quark potentials, we solve the four-body Schrödinger equation by means of the Gaussian expansion method. The bound states of $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ with $J^{PC}=1^{++},2^{++}$ and $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ with $J^{PC}=0^{++},1^{+-},1^{++},2^{++}$ are obtained. The molecular states $D^{*}\bar{D}$ with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ and $B^{*}\bar{B}$ with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ are good candidates for the $X(3872)$ and $Z^0_b(10610)$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including an $s$-channel one-gluon exchange interaction with a massive gluon propagator (effective gluon mass $m_g$ in the range $0.9-1.0$ GeV) can bind $D^{*}\bar{D}$ and $B^{*}\bar{B}$ systems into molecular states, but the binding is sensitive to the chosen effective gluon mass; without a massive gluon propagator the interaction is repulsive and no bound states are found.

## Method

The authors solve the four-body Schrödinger equation for neutral $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ systems using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with two different constituent quark models: the Bhaduri-Cohler-Nogami (BCN) model and the chiral constituent quark model (ChQM). Both models are extended by adding an $s$-channel one-gluon annihilation interaction between light $u\bar{u}$ or $d\bar{d}$ quark pairs, using a massive gluon propagator with effective gluon mass $m_g$ as a free parameter. The calculations are performed for S-wave states with orbital angular momentum $L_T=0$, and binding energies are determined relative to the two-meson thresholds.

## Key numbers

- $D^{*}\bar{D}$ with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$: binding energy of 16.2 MeV (BCN model, $m_g=0.9$ GeV) and 2.5 MeV (ChQM, $m_g=0.9$ GeV); with $m_g=1.0$ GeV in BCN, binding energy is 2.6 MeV, consistent with the $X(3872)$ mass of $3871.69 \pm 0.17$ MeV being about 1 MeV below the $D^{*}\bar{D}$ threshold.
- $D^{*}\bar{D}^{*}$ with $J^{PC}=2^{++}$: binding energy of 14.1 MeV (BCN, $m_g=0.9$ GeV) and 1.4 MeV (ChQM, $m_g=0.9$ GeV).
- $B^{*}\bar{B}$ with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$: binding energy of 10.1 MeV (BCN, $m_g=0.9$ GeV) and 0.8 MeV (ChQM, $m_g=0.9$ GeV), proposed as a candidate for $Z_b^0(10610)$.
- $B\bar{B}$ with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$: binding energy of 34.7 MeV (BCN, $m_g=0.9$ GeV) and 18.5 MeV (ChQM, $m_g=0.9$ GeV).
- $B^{*}\bar{B}^{*}$ with $J^{PC}=2^{++}$: binding energy of 42.5 MeV (BCN, $m_g=0.9$ GeV) and 24.2 MeV (ChQM, $m_g=0.9$ GeV).
- Without annihilation interaction or with $m_g=0$ GeV, no bound states are found for $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ in either model; for $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ without annihilation, ChQM gives three bound states ($0^{++}$, $1^{++}$, $2^{++}$) with binding energies up to 4.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous constituent quark model studies of $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ systems by Yang and Ping (2014), who found no neutral bound states using GEM without the $s$-channel annihilation interaction, and by Vijande et al. (2007), who found no bound states in the $c\bar{c}n\bar{n}$ system using the hyperspherical harmonic formalism. The inclusion of $s$-channel one-gluon exchange with a massive gluon propagator follows the approach of Wang et al. (2010), who argued this interaction is important for binding $D^{*}\bar{D}^{*}$ as a candidate for $X(3872)$. The results enable predictions for $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ molecular states that could be searched for by LHCb, BaBar, and Belle, and suggest that the effective gluon mass parameter needs better experimental constraints.
