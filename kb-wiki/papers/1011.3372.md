# Heavy-Quark Mass and Heavy-Meson Decay Constants from QCD Sum Rules

> We present a sum-rule extraction of heavy-meson decay constants from the two-point correlator of heavy-light pseudoscalar currents. Our main concern is to control the uncertainties of the decay constants, induced by both input QCD parameters and limited accuracy of the sum-rule method. Gaining this control is possible by applying our novel procedure for the extraction of hadron observables utilizing Borel-parameter-depending dual thresholds. For the charmed mesons, we obtain f_D = 206.2 \pm 7.3 (OPE) \pm 5.1 (syst) MeV and f_{D_s} = 245.3 \pm 15.7 (OPE) \pm 4.5 (syst) MeV. For the beauty mesons, the decay constants prove to be extremely sensitive to the exact value of the b-quark MS-bar mass m_b(m_b). Matching our sum-rule prediction for f_B to the lattice findings, the very accurate b-mass value m_b(m_b) = 4.245 \pm 0.025 GeV is found, which yields f_B = 193.4 \pm 12.3 (OPE) \pm 4.3 (syst) MeV and f_{B_s} = 232.5 \pm 18.6 (OPE) \pm 2.4 (syst) MeV.

# Heavy-Quark Mass and Heavy-Meson Decay Constants from QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim
The authors extract heavy-meson decay constants using a novel sum-rule procedure with Borel-parameter-dependent dual thresholds, which they argue drastically improves accuracy compared to the standard assumption of a constant threshold. For beauty mesons, the extracted decay constants are extremely sensitive to the b-quark MS-bar mass, and matching to lattice results yields a precise b-mass value rather than a direct sum-rule prediction.

## Method
The authors compute the two-point correlator of heavy-light pseudoscalar currents using the operator product expansion (OPE) to three-loop accuracy, expressed in terms of the heavy-quark MS-bar mass. They introduce a Borel-parameter-dependent effective continuum threshold, determined variationally by requiring the dual mass to match the known ground-state mass within the Borel window. The decay constant is then extracted from the dual correlator using linear, quadratic, and cubic Ansatze for the threshold, with the spread of results providing the systematic uncertainty.

## Key numbers
- f_D = 206.2 ± 7.3 (OPE) ± 5.1 (syst) MeV
- f_Ds = 245.3 ± 15.7 (OPE) ± 4.5 (syst) MeV
- b-quark MS-bar mass m_b(m_b) = 4.245 ± 0.025 GeV, obtained by matching sum-rule f_B to lattice averages
- f_B = 193.4 ± 12.3 (OPE) ± 4.3 (syst) MeV, using the matched b-mass value
- f_Bs = 232.5 ± 18.6 (OPE) ± 2.4 (syst) MeV, using the matched b-mass value

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous development of Borel-parameter-dependent dual thresholds for QCD sum rules (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2009-2010), which was tested in potential models before application to QCD. The results for charmed mesons agree with lattice QCD calculations, while for beauty mesons the extracted b-quark mass differs from a recent determination by the author cited as "mb" (2010), which gave m_b(m_b) = 4.163 ± 0.016 GeV and produced decay constants barely compatible with lattice. The method enables systematic uncertainty estimates at the few-percent level for sum-rule predictions, bringing them into agreement with lattice and experiment.
