# Symmetric-asymmetric collision comparison: disentangling nuclear structure and subnucleonic structure effects for small system flow

> Previous flow measurements in small collision systems were mostly based on highly asymmetric collisions ($p$+Pb, $p$+Au, $d$+Au, $^{3}$He+Au), where both nuclear structure and subnucleonic fluctuations are important. Comparing these asymmetric systems with the newly available symmetric $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at RHIC and LHC provides a unique opportunity to disentangle these two contributions. Using Glauber models incorporating both nucleon and quark-level substructure, we analyze multiplicity distributions and initial-state estimators: eccentricities $\varepsilon_n$ for anisotropic flow $v_n$ and inverse transverse size $d_{\perp}$ for radial flow. We find that subnucleonic fluctuations impact O+O collisions differently from asymmetric systems, creating specific patterns in flow observables that enable disentangling the competing contributions. Such experimental comparisons will reduce uncertainties in the initial conditions and improve our understanding of the properties of the QGP-like medium produced in small systems.

## Key claim

Comparing symmetric ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions with asymmetric p/d/³He+Au systems can disentangle nuclear structure effects from subnucleonic fluctuations in small-system flow, provided that the observed flow arises from geometry-driven hydrodynamic response rather than alternative mechanisms such as initial-state momentum anisotropy.

## Method

The authors used a standard Glauber model framework with three variants (nucleon Glauber, three-quark Glauber, and seven-quark Glauber) to calculate initial geometry quantities, eccentricities ε₂ and ε₃ for anisotropic flow, inverse transverse size d⊥ for radial flow, and charged-particle multiplicity distributions p(Nch), for p+Au, d+Au, ³He+Au, and ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. Nucleon configurations for deuteron and ³He used Hulthen wavefunction and Green's function Monte Carlo with AV18+UIX interactions, while ¹⁶O configurations came from nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT); subnucleonic fluctuations were modeled by distributing quarks inside nucleons according to the "mod" configuration with a proton form factor ρ(r) = e^{-r/r₀} and r₀ = 0.234 fm.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section set to 42 mb at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Nucleon hard-core radius assumed to be 0.4 fm
- Quark-quark inelastic cross section set to 8.1 mb for three-quark Glauber model and 1.9 mb for seven-quark Glauber model
- Proton form factor parameter r₀ = 0.234 fm
- Average number of participating nucleons in minimum bias events: 5.94 (p+Au), 8.73 (d+Au), 11.23 (³He+Au), 9.72 (O+O)
- Effective fractional mass of participating nucleons F₃ (three-quark model): 0.521 (p+Au), 0.548 (d+Au), 0.580 (³He+Au), 0.627 (O+O)
- Effective fractional mass F₇ (seven-quark model): 0.318 (p+Au), 0.342 (d+Au), 0.369 (³He+Au), 0.398 (O+O)
- Negative binomial parameters for three-quark Glauber model: n̄ = 0.65346, m = 0.75148, obtained by fitting Ru+Ru data at pT > 0.2 GeV and |η| < 0.5

## Context

This work extends previous flow measurements in highly asymmetric small collision systems (p+Pb, p+Au, d+Au, ³He+Au) at RHIC and LHC, where both nuclear structure and subnucleonic fluctuations contribute to initial geometry. The authors build on prior studies showing that subnucleonic fluctuations are necessary to explain large vn in pp and p+Pb collisions (Mantysaari 2016, Loizides 2016), and that models considering only nucleon degrees of freedom predict different v₃ hierarchies than those including subnucleonic structure (Zhao 2022, Wu 2023). The comparison with symmetric O+O collisions, recently collected at both RHIC (Huang 2023) and LHC, enables disentangling these contributions and could reduce uncertainties in initial-condition modeling, potentially allowing more precise extraction of transport properties like shear viscosity in small systems.
