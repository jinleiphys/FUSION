# Nonpointlike Particles in Harmonic Oscillators

> Quantum mechanics ordinarily describes particles as being pointlike, in the sense that the uncertainty $Δx$ can, in principle, be made arbitrarily small. It has been shown that suitable correction terms to the canonical commutation relations induce a finite lower bound to spatial localisation. Here, we perturbatively calculate the corrections to the energy levels of an in this sense nonpointlike particle in isotropic harmonic oscillators. Apart from a special case the degeneracy of the energy levels is removed.

# Nonpointlike Particles in Harmonic Oscillators

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing a finite minimal uncertainty in position (Δx₀ > 0) through corrections to the canonical commutation relations produces a characteristic splitting of the ordinarily degenerate energy levels of the d-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator, except in the special case where the correction parameters satisfy β′ = β (i.e., k′² = k²), where no splitting occurs.

## Method

The author modifies the canonical commutation relations in d dimensions by adding small correction terms proportional to parameters β and β′ to the position-momentum commutator, which induces a finite lower bound Δx₀ on spatial localization. The Hamiltonian for an isotropic harmonic oscillator is expressed in momentum space and then transformed into a Fock representation, where first-order perturbation theory is applied to the degenerate energy levels of the d-dimensional oscillator. The eigenvalues of the perturbing operator ∑ᵢⱼ aᵢ² aⱼ†² are computed within each degenerate eigenspace ℋₙ to determine the splitting pattern.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier one-dimensional oscillator solution (Kempf, 1995) to the d-dimensional isotropic case, building on the algebraic framework for minimal uncertainties in position that originated in quantum groups and noncommutative geometry (Kempf, 1994). The approach provides an effective low-energy description of nonpointlike particles (such as nucleons, quasiparticles, or collective excitations) without requiring first-principles modeling of their internal structure. The results enable experimental tests of the framework by fitting the single free parameter Δx₀ to observed energy-level splittings, and the author suggests applying similar perturbative methods to the Hydrogen atom to obtain an upper bound on a possible fundamental nonpointlikeness of the electron.
