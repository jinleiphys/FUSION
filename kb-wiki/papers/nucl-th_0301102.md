# Light-front time picture of the Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We show the light-front representation of the field theoretical Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in the ladder approximation using the quasi potential reduction. We discuss the equivalence of the covariant ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation with an infinite set of coupled equations for the Green's functions of the different light-front Fock-states.

## Key claim
The covariant ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation is equivalent to an infinite hierarchy of coupled equations for light-front Fock-state Green's functions, where the effective two-body interaction emerges from coupling to higher Fock sectors; the equivalence holds specifically within the quasi-potential reduction scheme that eliminates relative light-front time.

## Method
The authors apply a quasi-potential reduction to the Bethe-Salpeter equation in light-front coordinates, using an auxiliary Green's function that isolates global light-front time propagation. They expand the effective interaction in powers of the coupling constant for a scalar boson-exchange model (Lagrangian with coupling \(g_S\)), and derive explicit matrix elements for the second-order (\(w^{(2)}\)) and fourth-order (\(w^{(4)}\)) terms. The resulting hierarchy equations couple the two-body light-front Green's function \(g^{(2)}(K)\) to successively higher Fock-state Green's functions \(g^{(3)}(K), g^{(4)}(K), \dots\), with truncation possible by replacing \(g^{(N)}(K)\) with the free propagator \(g^{(N)}_0(K)\).

## Key numbers
- The second-order effective interaction matrix element \(\langle k^{\prime +}_1 \vec k^{\prime}_{1\perp} | w^{(2)}(K) | k^+_1 \vec k_{1\perp} \rangle\) contains a factor \((ig_S)^2\) and a denominator \(K^--K^{(3)-}_0+io\) with \(K^{(3)-}_0\) defined as the sum of three on-shell light-front energies.
- The fourth-order term \(\langle k^{\prime +}_1 \vec k^{\prime}_{1\perp} | w^{(4)}(K) | k^+_1 \vec k_{1\perp} \rangle\) includes a factor \(\frac{(ig_S)^4}{2(2\pi)^3}\) and an integral over intermediate momentum \(p^+_1, d^2p_{1\perp}\) with three propagator denominators.
- The free two-body light-front Green's function is \(g_0(K) = \frac{ig_0^{(2)}(K)}{\widehat{k}^+_{1}(K^+-\widehat{k}^+_{1})}\), where \(g_0^{(2)}(K)\) contains theta functions \(\theta(\widehat k_j^{+})\theta(K^{+}-\widehat k_j^{+})\) and the denominator \((K^{-}-\widehat K_0^{(2)-}+io)^{-1}\).

## Context
This work extends the quasi-potential reduction method previously proposed by the same authors (Frederico et al., 2002) for two-particle bound states on the light-front, and builds on Dirac's (1949) light-front dynamics concept. It provides a systematic framework to connect the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation to light-front Fock-state expansions, enabling nonperturbative truncation schemes for bound-state calculations. The hierarchy equations resemble the iterated resolvent method of Brodsky (1998) and can be applied to study electromagnetic probes of bound states, as noted by Miller (2002).
