# Study of fragmentation and momentum correlations in heavy-ion collisions

> The role of momentum correlations is studied in the production of light and medium mass fragments by imposing momentum cut in clusterization the phase space. Our detailed investigation shows that momentum cut has major role to play in the emission of fragments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that imposing a momentum cut (relative momentum ≤ 150 MeV/c) during clusterization in the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model significantly alters fragment production across all colliding geometries, but the effect is system-size dependent and behaves differently for the lightest system (¹²C+¹²C) compared to heavier systems.

## Method

The authors used the QMD model with a soft equation of state and standard energy-dependent Cugnon cross section to simulate collisions of ¹²C+¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at 100 and 400 MeV/nucleon for impact parameters b̂ = 0.0, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, and 0.8. They compared two clusterization algorithms: the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) method, which uses a spatial cut of 4 fm, and the MST with momentum cut (MSTP) method, which adds a relative momentum cut of 150 MeV/c. The time evolution of the heaviest fragment (A^max), free nucleons, light charged particles (LCPs, 2≤A≤4), and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) was analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Momentum cut value used in MSTP method: p_min = 150 MeV/c
- Spatial cut value used in both MST and MSTP methods: r_min = 4 fm
- Incident energies studied: 100 and 400 MeV/nucleon
- Systems simulated: ¹²C+¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au
- Average percentage differences between MST and MSTP across all systems and impact parameters: 22% for A^max, 69% for free nucleons, -45% for LCPs, and -16% for IMFs
- Yukawa interaction parameters: t₃ = -6.66 MeV, μ = 1.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the MSTP method previously applied by Puri et al. (Kumar et al.) to heavier systems (⁹³Nb+⁹³Nb and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) by systematically studying lighter colliding systems and the role of colliding geometry. The results show that momentum correlations are significant for all systems but behave differently for the lightest system (¹²C+¹²C), where the effect increases with impact parameter due to the lack of spectator matter. This enables future studies to refine clusterization algorithms for multifragmentation simulations across a wider range of system sizes and collision geometries.
