# Adaptive Time Stepping for the Two-Time Integro-Differential Kadanoff-Baym Equations

> The non-equilibrium Green's function gives access to one-body observables for quantum systems. Of particular interest are quantities such as density, currents, and absorption spectra which are important for interpreting experimental results in quantum transport and spectroscopy. We present an integration scheme for the Green's function's equations of motion, the Kadanoff-Baym equations (KBE), which is both adaptive in the time integrator step size and method order as well as the history integration order. We analyze the importance of solving the KBE self-consistently and show that adapting the order of history integral evaluation is important for obtaining accurate results. To examine the efficiency of our method, we compare runtimes to a state of the art fixed time step integrator for several test systems and show an order of magnitude speedup at similar levels of accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper presents an adaptive time-stepping scheme for the Kadanoff-Baym equations that simultaneously adapts the integrator step size, method order, and the order of history integral evaluation, achieving an order of magnitude speedup over fixed-step integrators at similar accuracy levels, but only when the equations are solved self-consistently and the history integration order is also adapted.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Kadanoff-Baym equations as a system of ordinary differential equations in a single time variable by stacking the lesser, greater, and mixed Green's function components into a combined state vector. They apply variable-step, variable-order Adams methods (orders 1 to 12) from the CVODE package to advance this system, and compute the history integrals using polynomial interpolation with an adaptively chosen order that minimizes the difference between successive interpolation orders for a test integral. The implementation extends the NEDyson package and includes the thermal (Matsubara) branch using the Discrete Lehmann Representation.

## Key numbers

- The adaptive integrator achieves an "order of magnitude speedup" compared to a state-of-the-art fixed time step integrator at similar levels of accuracy for several test systems.
- The ramp applied to the quadratic Hamiltonian uses four terms with amplitudes a1 = a2 = -a3 = -a4 = 1 Ha and ramp times t1 = 1, t2 = 3, t3 = 10, and t4 = 12 (in inverse Hartrees).
- Each individual ramp has approximately a 10 attosecond width, which is "shorter than, but on the order of" current state-of-the-art pulse widths.
- The adaptive order selection procedure for history integrals costs approximately 1% of the total computation time.

## Context

This work builds upon the adaptive integration approach for the Kadanoff-Baym equations introduced by Meirinhos et al. (2022), extending it by including the Matsubara (thermal) branch, studying the effects of self-consistency, and implementing adaptive orders in the evaluation of history integrals. The method enables benchmarking of approximate approaches such as the Generalized Kadanoff Baym Ansatz (GKBA), which has been shown to neglect terms around two orders of magnitude smaller than those kept by Reeves et al. (2023), and bridges the gap between short-time transient dynamics and long-time steady-state physics.
