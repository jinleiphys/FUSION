# Revealing the strength of three-nucleon interactions with the Einstein Telescope

> Three-nucleon forces are crucial for the accurate description of nuclear systems, including dense matter probed in neutron stars. We explore nuclear Hamiltonians that reproduce two-nucleon scattering data and properties of light nuclei, but differ in the three-nucleon interactions among neutrons. While no significantly improved constraints can be obtained from current astrophysical data, we show that observations of neutron star mergers by next-generation detectors like the proposed Einstein Telescope could provide strong evidence to distinguish between these Hamiltonians.

## Key claim
The paper shows that while current gravitational-wave data cannot distinguish between nuclear Hamiltonians with different three-nucleon interactions among neutrons, observations of neutron-star mergers by the proposed third-generation Einstein Telescope could provide strong evidence to differentiate these Hamiltonians, though the strength of model preference depends on which Hamiltonian is the true one and on systematic biases in parameter estimation.

## Method
The authors constructed two equation-of-state sets from chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians that differ only in their three-nucleon interactions in pure neutron matter: one with only two-pion-exchange (TPE) and one additionally containing a repulsive three-nucleon contact interaction (V_{E,1}). They performed Bayesian parameter estimation on 20 synthetic binary neutron-star merger signals injected into an Einstein Telescope simulation, computing Bayes factors to quantify model preference between the two Hamiltonians across 80 inference runs in the frequency range 30 Hz to 2048 Hz.

## Key numbers
- Bayes factor for TPE injection: ln B = -53.5 ± 1.3 after 20 mergers, with the injected EOS having only 0.04% posterior weight
- Bayes factor for V_{E,1} injection: ln B = 3.8 ± 1.3 after 20 mergers
- Joint posterior tidal deformability for TPE injection: Λ_{1.4,TPE} = 204^{+4}_{-10} (90% CI), compared to injection value Λ_{1.4,inj} = 212
- Joint posterior radius for TPE injection: R_{1.4,TPE} = 10.80^{+0.12}_{-0.10} km, compared to injection value R_{1.4,inj} = 11.0 km
- Joint posterior tidal deformability for V_{E,1} injection: Λ_{1.4,VE} = 299^{+10}_{-7}, in agreement with injection value Λ_{1.4,inj} = 300
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ_sat ≈ 2.7 × 10^14 g/cm^3

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Maselli (2020) and Sabatucci (2022) who first investigated the impact of gravitational-wave measurements on three-nucleon forces, but crucially includes both truncation uncertainties from chiral effective field theory and the possibility of exotic matter at high densities via a speed-of-sound extrapolation scheme from Tews (2018). The results enable future observational programs to directly constrain microscopic nuclear interactions from neutron-star merger data, and the approach is not limited to three-nucleon interactions but can in principle constrain other parts of the nuclear Hamiltonian as well.
