## Key claim

The authors show that the $\Lambda(1600)$ resonance with spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^+$ is necessary to reproduce the experimental total cross section data of the $K^- p \to \Lambda \pi^0 \pi^0$ reaction, but they cannot describe the enhancement at low energy, which may be explained by the tail of the $\Lambda(1520)$ contribution or a possible $\Sigma^*(1380) \to \pi \Lambda$ in $s$ wave.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach and resonance model to compute tree-level Feynman diagrams for the $K^- p \to \Lambda \pi^0 \pi^0$ reaction, including $s$-channel $\Lambda(1600)$ and $\Lambda(1670)$ resonances decaying into $\pi^0 \Sigma^{*0}(1385)$ (with $\Sigma^{*0}(1385)$ decaying into $\pi^0 \Lambda$) and a $u$-channel nucleon pole as background. They determine coupling constants from experimental partial decay widths where available, and vary the mass and width of the $\Lambda(1600)$ resonance to reproduce the Crystal Ball Collaboration data at eight $K^-$ momenta between 514 and 750 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda(1600)$ resonance mass is taken as $M_{\Lambda^*_1} = 1580$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{\Lambda^*_1} = 150$ MeV to reproduce the data.
- The $\Lambda(1670)$ resonance mass is taken as $M_{\Lambda^*_2} = 1670$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{\Lambda^*_2} = 35$ MeV.
- The $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance has mass $1385$ MeV, width $37$ MeV, and branching ratio $87\%$ to $\pi \Lambda$.
- The $\Lambda(1600)$ branching ratio to $\bar{K} N$ is $22.5\%$ and to $\pi \Sigma^*(1385)$ is $6.5\%$ in the fit.
- The $\Lambda(1670)$ branching ratio to $\bar{K} N$ is $25\%$ and to $\pi \Sigma^*(1385)$ is $22.4\%$.
- The cut-off parameter for form factors is $\Lambda_s = \Lambda_u = \Lambda_{\Sigma^*} = 600$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $K^- p \to \Lambda \pi^0 \pi^0$ reaction measured by the Crystal Ball Collaboration (Prakhov, 2004), which previously showed dominance of the $\pi^0 \Sigma^{*0}(1385)$ intermediate state and that the $\Lambda(1520)$ contribution could reproduce the cross section via chiral unitary approaches (Sarkar, 2005; Roca, 2006). The present study demonstrates that the $\Lambda(1600)$ resonance is crucial for describing the data around $p_{K^-} = 630$ MeV, and the results can be used to determine properties of the $\Lambda(1600)$ resonance, which has large uncertainties in its mass (1560-1700 MeV) and width (50-250 MeV) according to the Particle Data Group (2018).
