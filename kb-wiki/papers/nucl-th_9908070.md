# Extraordinary Baryon Fluctuations and the QCD Tricritical Point

> The dynamic separation into phases of high and low baryon density in a heavy ion collision can enhance fluctuations of the net rapidity density of baryons compared to model expectations. We show how these fluctuations arise and how they can survive through freezeout.

## Key claim

Baryon number fluctuations can be enhanced beyond the Poissonian baseline if the expanding system from a heavy ion collision is quenched into the phase coexistence region below the QCD tricritical point, and these fluctuations can plausibly survive through freezeout despite diffusion.

## Method

The author develops a spinodal decomposition model to describe how a rapidly supercooled system can produce large baryon density fluctuations, then uses a diffusion equation with scaling flow to estimate whether these fluctuations dissipate before freezeout. The variance of net baryon number is computed from a bimodal distribution representing high and low density phases, and the effect of baryon diffusion is studied by solving the linearized diffusion equation for a Gaussian perturbation in spatial rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The spinodal growth time scale is estimated as τ_sp ∼ 1 fm or smaller, using a correlation length m_σ^{-1} ∼ 1 fm and a diffusion coefficient ∼ 1-3 fm from perturbative plasma estimates.
- The diffusion coefficient for nucleons in a hadron gas is D ∼ 6 fm at T = 150 MeV, with τ_diff ∼ 35 fm; for a perturbative quark-gluon plasma D ∼ 1-3 fm.
- For a quench time τ_Q = 5 fm, σ = 0.88, f_0 = 1/2, and τ_F → ∞, the variance at η ≈ 0 decreases to approximately 76% of its initial value due to diffusion.
- Simulated results use rapidity densities of baryons = 60, antibaryons = 15, and charged hadrons = 300 at impact parameter b = 0, with rapidity density contrasts δN = N_q - N_h = 20 and 40, corresponding to ρ_q - ρ_h ∼ 0.10 and 0.2 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Berges and Rajagopal (1999) and Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998) on the QCD tricritical point and critical fluctuations, proposing baryon number fluctuations as a complementary observable to pion and kaon interferometry, intermittency, and wavelet analyses (Pratt and others). The paper enables experimental searches for the first-order phase coexistence region and tricritical point by measuring the normalized variance ω_B = V_B/(N + N̅) as a function of centrality at varying beam energies, and calls for further work on the phase diagram away from the tricritical point and transport processes in the mixed phase.
