# Coupling of Surface and Volume Dipole Oscillations in C-60 Molecules

> We first give a short review of the ``local-current approximation'' (LCA), derived from a general variation principle, which serves as a semiclassical description of strongly collective excitations in finite fermion systems starting from their quantum-mechanical mean-field ground state. We illustrate it for the example of coupled translational and compressional dipole excitations in metal clusters. We then discuss collective electronic dipole excitations in C$_{60}$ molecules (Buckminster fullerenes). We show that the coupling of the pure translational mode (``surface plasmon'') with compressional volume modes in the semiclasscial LCA yields semi-quantitative agreement with microscopic time-dependent density functional (TDLDA) calculations, while both theories yield qualitative agreement with the recent experimental observation of a ``volume plasmon''.

## Key claim

The coupling of the pure translational dipole mode (surface plasmon) with compressional volume modes in the semiclassical local-current approximation (LCA) yields semi-quantitative agreement with microscopic time-dependent density functional (TDLDA) calculations for C60 molecules, while both theories yield only qualitative agreement with the experimental observation of a volume plasmon.

## Method

The authors applied the local-current approximation (LCA), a semiclassical variational approach derived from the same footing as the random phase approximation (RPA), to compute the collective electronic dipole response of C60 molecules. They used a spherical jellium model with a shell-like ionic distribution (radius 0.354 nm, thickness 0.153 nm) and expanded the local excitation operators in a finite basis of operators Q_p(r) = e r^p Y_{10}(theta) with p = 1,2,...,M, solving the resulting secular equations for M up to 9 coupled modes. The Kohn-Sham ground-state density from a prior TDLDA calculation was used, with kinetic energy treated via the extended Thomas-Fermi functional and exchange-correlation energy in the local density approximation.

## Key numbers

* Surface plasmon energy from the classical jellium model with the dipole operator Q_d = ez: 21.4 eV
* Volume plasmon energy from the classical jellium model with the monopole operator Q_0 = er^2: 37 eV
* Surface plasmon energy from coupled dipole modes in the classical jellium model (leading order in Delta/R): approximately 19.8 eV
* Volume plasmon energy from coupled dipole modes in the classical jellium model (leading order in Delta/R): approximately 31.3 eV
* Experimental surface plasmon position from two-Lorentzian fit to photoionization cross section: 22 +/- 0.1 eV
* Experimental volume plasmon position from two-Lorentzian fit: 38 +/- 2 eV
* Converged LCA volume peak position after 5.5 eV blue shift: 38 eV
* Number of valence electrons in C60: N = 240

## Context

This work extends the LCA approach, previously applied successfully to collective excitations in nuclei and metal clusters (Brack 1989, LCA papers), to the optical response of C60 molecules. It directly addresses a debate between Kossmann (2006) and Scully et al. (2005) about whether the high-energy shoulder in the C60 photoionization cross section represents a compressional volume plasmon. The LCA results confirm the interpretation of the volume peak as due to compressional components of the collective electronic motion, consistent with the earlier TDLDA calculations of Scully et al. (2005). The approach enables future inclusion of ionic structure effects, which the authors note would be too time-consuming with purely microscopic methods.
