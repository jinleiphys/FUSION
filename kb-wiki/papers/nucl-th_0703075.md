# Underlying symmetries of realistic interactions and the nuclear many-body problem

> The present study brings forward important information, within the framework of spectral distribution theory, about the types of forces that dominate three realistic interactions, CD-Bonn, CDBonn+ 3terms and GXPF1, in nuclei and their ability to account for many-particle effects such as the formation of correlated nucleon pairs and enhanced quadrupole collective modes. Like-particle and proton-neutron isovector pairing correlations are described microscopically by a model interaction with Sp(4) dynamical symmetry, which is extended to include an additional quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. The analysis of the results for the 1f7/2 level shows that both CD-Bonn+3terms and GXPF1 exhibit a well-developed pairing character compared to CD-Bonn, while the latter appears to build up more (less) rotational isovector T = 1 (isoscalar T = 0) collective features. Furthermore, the three realistic interactions are in general found to correlate strongly with the pairing+quadrupole model interaction, especially for the highest possible isospin group of states where the model interaction can be used to provide a reasonable description of the corresponding energy spectra.

## Key claim

The three realistic interactions (CD-Bonn, CD-Bonn+3terms, and GXPF1) contain 40-60% of the Sp(4) dynamical symmetry for isovector pairing in the 1f7/2 level, but this correlation is strongly isospin-dependent: the T=1 channel shows much stronger pairing character than the T=0 channel, and the CD-Bonn+3terms interaction exhibits the closest similarity to the pure isovector pairing model.

## Method

The authors used spectral distribution theory to compute correlation coefficients between the two-body matrix elements of three realistic interactions (CD-Bonn, CD-Bonn+3terms, GXPF1) and a model Hamiltonian with Sp(4) dynamical symmetry describing isovector pairing, extended with a quadrupole-quadrupole term. The analysis was performed for the single 1f7/2 level, separately in the scalar (isospin-averaged) and isospin-scalar (T=0 and T=1) distributions, using propagator functions that relate two-body matrix elements to many-particle averages without large-scale diagonalization.

## Key numbers

* Correlation coefficient between Sp(4) model and CD-Bonn in scalar distribution: 0.66 (43% shared variance)
* Correlation coefficient between Sp(4) model and CD-Bonn+3terms in scalar distribution: 0.64 (41% shared variance)
* Correlation coefficient between Sp(4) model and GXPF1 in scalar distribution: 0.76 (58% shared variance)
* T=1 isospin-scalar correlation between Sp(4) model and CD-Bonn+3terms: 0.85 (72% shared variance), the highest among the three realistic interactions
* T=1 isospin-scalar correlation between Sp(4) model and GXPF1: 0.71 (50% shared variance)
* T=1 isospin-scalar correlation between Sp(4) model and CD-Bonn: 0.61 (37% shared variance)
* Two-body (J=0,T=1) matrix element for Sp(4) model: -1.85 MeV; for CD-Bonn+3terms: -2.06 MeV; for GXPF1: -2.44 MeV
* Correlation between CD-Bonn and CD-Bonn+3terms in scalar distribution: 0.95; in T=1 isospin-scalar: 0.94
* Isospin T+2 admixture from isotensor Sp(4) interaction in 1f7/2 level: less than 0.0001% on average

## Context

This work extends the spectral distribution method of French and collaborators (French 1971, Chang 1971) and the Sp(4) pairing model of Hecht (1965) and Engel et al. (1996) to quantify how much realistic interactions contain the dynamical symmetries of pairing and quadrupole collectivity. The Sp(4) model had previously been shown to reproduce the energies of isobaric analog 0+ states in 149 nuclei across the 1f7/2 and fpg shells (Sviratcheva et al. 2004). The present correlation analysis enables identification of which realistic interaction best approximates the simpler algebraic model, allowing the Sp(4) Hamiltonian to be used as a computationally tractable approximation for larger model spaces where full diagonalization is prohibitive.
