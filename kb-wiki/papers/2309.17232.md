# Zemach and Friar radii of the proton and neutron from lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice-QCD result for the Zemach and Friar radii of the proton and neutron. Our calculation includes both quark-connected and -disconnected diagrams and assesses all sources of systematic uncertainties arising from excited-state contributions, finite-volume effects and the continuum extrapolation. At the physical point, we obtain for the proton $r_Z^p = ( 1.013 \pm 0.010\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.012\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}$ and $r_F^p = ( 1.301 \pm 0.012\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.014\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}$. These numbers suggest small values of the Zemach and Friar radii of the proton, but are compatible with most of the experimental studies.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice-QCD calculation of the Zemach and Friar radii of the proton and neutron, finding values that are small but compatible with most experimental studies within two standard deviations; the authors note that their results are highly correlated with their previously published electromagnetic radii, so the small values are not unexpected from the lattice perspective.

## Method

The calculation uses \(N_f = 2 + 1\) dynamical flavors of non-perturbatively \(\mathcal{O}(a)\)-improved Wilson fermions on CLS ensembles covering four lattice spacings \(a \in [0.050, 0.086]~\mathrm{fm}\) and pion masses down to 130 MeV. Both quark-connected and -disconnected diagrams are included, and the form factors are fitted to covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (B\(\chi\)PT) expressions with cuts in pion mass and momentum transfer, then extrapolated to large \(Q^2\) using a \(z\)-expansion with sum rules. Systematic uncertainties from excited-state contributions, finite-volume effects, and continuum extrapolation are assessed via a model average weighted by the Akaike Information Criterion.

## Key numbers

* Proton Zemach radius: \(r_Z^p = ( 1.013 \pm 0.010\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.012\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}\)
* Proton Friar radius: \(r_F^p = ( 1.301 \pm 0.012\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.014\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}\)
* Proton third Zemach moment: \(\langle r_E^3 \rangle_{(2)}^p = ( 2.200 \pm 0.060\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.071\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}^3\)
* Neutron Zemach radius: \(r_Z^n = ( -0.0411 \pm 0.0056\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.0040\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}\)
* Neutron third Zemach moment: \(\langle r_E^3 \rangle_{(2)}^n = ( 0.0078 \pm 0.0020\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.0012\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}^3\)
* Neutron Friar radius: \(r_F^n = ( 0.198 \pm 0.017\ (\mathrm{stat}) \pm 0.010\ (\mathrm{syst}) )~\mathrm{fm}\)
* The form factor contribution at \(Q^2 > 0.6~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) contributes less than 0.9% to the proton Zemach radius and less than 0.3% to the third Zemach moment.
* A \(2.6\,\sigma\) tension is observed with the dispersive analysis of Lin (2022) for the proton Zemach radius, and a \(5.3\,\sigma\) tension with the A1 \(ep\)-scattering analysis of Distler (2011) for the third Zemach moment.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice-QCD determinations of the proton and neutron electromagnetic radii (Djukanovic 2023, Djukanovic 2023a) to the Zemach and Friar radii, which are needed for interpreting hyperfine splitting measurements in muonic hydrogen. The results are compared to data-driven evaluations from muonic hydrogen HFS (Antognini 2013), electronic hydrogen HFS (Volotka 2005), \(ep\) scattering analyses using \(z\)-expansions (Borah 2020) and dispersion theory (Lin 2022), and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (Peset 2014). The calculation enables first-principles predictions to guide upcoming experiments measuring the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen with ppm precision (Sato 2014, Pizzolotto 2020, Amaro 2022).
