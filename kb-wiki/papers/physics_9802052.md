# Medium modification of two-particle scattering in nonideal Bose systems

> Medium modification of scattering properties in interacting Bose systems are considered by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation. An equation of state for the normal phase (generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula) is given using the in-medium phase shifts to include two-particle correlations. Conclusions are drawn for systems of bosonic atoms with repulsive interaction such as sodium $^{23}$Na and rubidium $^{87}$Rb. It is shown that the in-medium scattering length and the absolute value of the in-medium scattering phase shift for low scattering energies increase with density.

# Medium modification of two-particle scattering in nonideal Bose systems

## Key claim

The in-medium scattering length and the absolute value of the in-medium scattering phase shift for low scattering energies increase with density in interacting Bose systems, but this effect only becomes significant as the density approaches the critical density for Bose-Einstein condensation; the free scattering length alone is insufficient to describe scattering processes in dense media.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation using thermodynamic Green functions, incorporating intermediate Bose enhancement through the uncorrelated two-boson Green function. They employ an extended quasiparticle approximation to derive a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for the equation of state in the normal phase, and perform model calculations using a rank-one separable potential (including the zero-range contact interaction limit) for sodium-23 and rubidium-87.

## Key numbers

- For sodium-23 at T=5 microK, the in-medium scattering length is doubled at a density of 1x10^14 cm^-3 (critical density n_BEC = 6x10^14 cm^-3)
- For sodium-23 at T=50 microK, the in-medium scattering length is doubled at a density of 3.7x10^15 cm^-3 (critical density n_BEC = 1.9x10^16 cm^-3)
- For rubidium-87 at T=5 microK, the in-medium scattering length is doubled at 8x10^14 cm^-3 (critical density n_BEC = 4.5x10^15 cm^-3)
- The free ground state scattering length for sodium-23 is +/- (92 +/- 25) a_B (Bohr radius), with spectroscopic data giving 64 < a_0/a_B < 152
- For rubidium-87, the ground state triplet scattering length a_0 is approximately 5 nm, with spectroscopic values 85 < a_0/a_B < 140
- The critical density of the ideal Bose-Einstein condensation is n_BEC = 2.612 lambda^-3

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical treatment of in-medium scattering previously developed for Fermi systems by Ziesche and collaborators (1985) and Schmidt, Ropke, and Schulz (1990) to the case of bosons. It generalizes the classical Beth-Uhlenbeck formula (1936) for the second virial coefficient to dense quantum Bose gases. The results suggest that medium modifications should be observable in current Bose-Einstein condensation experiments with sodium and rubidium, and that experimentally extracted scattering lengths from cross section measurements may need to account for density-dependent effects. The approach enables improved equations of state beyond the simple hard-sphere model of Huang (1963) and provides a foundation for more advanced kinetic theories including strong correlations.
