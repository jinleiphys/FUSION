# Application of Principal Component Analysis to establish proper basis for flow studies in heavy-ion collisions

> It is shown that Principal Component Analysis (PCA) applied to event-by-event single-particle distributions in A-A collisions allows establishing the most optimal basis for anisotropic flow studies from data itself, in contrast to manual selection of the basis functions. PCA coefficients for azimuthal particle distributions are identical to Fourier coefficients from a conventional analysis techniques. PCA applied in longitudinal dimension reveals optimal basis that is similar to Legendre polynomial series. Analysis in both dimensions simultaneously allows studying the coupling of the longitudinal structure of events with the azimuthal anisotropy of particle emission.

## Key claim

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) applied to event-by-event single-particle distributions in A-A collisions can establish the most optimal basis for anisotropic flow studies from data itself, rather than requiring manual selection of basis functions; however, the optimal basis in the longitudinal (pseudorapidity) dimension is not a "standard" one and depends on the given data (collision system, energy, acceptance), unlike the azimuthal dimension where PCA confirms the Fourier basis.

## Method

PCA was applied to single-particle distributions in azimuthal angle (φ), pseudorapidity (η), and two-dimensional η-φ distributions from simulated Pb-Pb events at √sNN = 5 TeV using the AMPT event generator. For each event, the particle distribution in M bins was normalized by the number of particles, the event-averaged value was subtracted in each bin, and PCA was performed on the resulting N×M matrix via singular value decomposition to obtain orthonormal eigenvectors and decomposition coefficients. The PCA coefficients for azimuthal distributions were related to conventional flow coefficients v_n, and a correction procedure was applied to subtract statistical fluctuations by comparing with events having randomized φ-angles.

## Key numbers

- PCA was applied to 1.5 million Pb-Pb events at √sNN = 5 TeV simulated in AMPT, with particles within |η| < 0.8 and transverse momentum range 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c
- Azimuthal distributions used M = 48 φ-bins; the phase shift between sine and cosine pairs of eigenvectors equaled π/2 with 0.01% precision
- For the toy model with flow, corrected v_n values stabilized at true values at n_φ ≳ 30 for v_2, v_3, and v_4, while v_1 stabilized earlier
- The two-dimensional η-φ PCA used M = η × φ = 10 × 48 = 480 bins for AMPT semi-central collisions (centrality 20-30%)

## Context

This work extends the application of PCA to heavy-ion collisions, which was first suggested for two-particle azimuthal correlations by Ollitrault (2015), by applying PCA directly to single-particle distributions. The approach provides a data-driven justification for using Fourier harmonics in the azimuthal dimension and Legendre or Chebyshev polynomials in the longitudinal dimension, as previously suggested by Bzdak (2012) and Jia (2015). The two-dimensional PCA decomposition into azimuthal and longitudinal components enables studies of longitudinal decorrelation of harmonic flow as an alternative to methods like Jia (2014) and Bozek (2018), and could be applied to study the rapidity dependence of directed flow.
