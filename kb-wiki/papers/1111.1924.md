# Quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter with chiral three-nucleon forces

> We derive the effective interaction between two quasiparticles in symmetric nuclear matter resulting from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force. We restrict our study to the L=0,1 Landau parameters of the central quasiparticle interaction computed to first order. We find that the three-nucleon force provides substantial repulsion in the isotropic spin- and isospin-independent component F_0 of the interaction. This repulsion acts to stabilize nuclear matter against isoscalar density oscillations, a feature which is absent in calculations employing low-momentum two-nucleon interactions only. We find a rather large uncertainty for the nuclear compression modulus due to a sensitive dependence on the low-energy constant c_3. The effective nucleon mass on the Fermi surface, as well as the nuclear symmetry energy, receive only small corrections from the leading-order chiral three-body force. Both the anomalous orbital g-factor and the Landau-Migdal parameter g'_{NN} (characterizing the spin-isospin response of nuclear matter) decrease with the addition of three-nucleon correlations. In fact, the anomalous orbital g-factor remains significantly smaller than its value extracted from experimental data, whereas g'_{NN} still compares well with empirical values. The inclusion of the three-nucleon force results in relatively small p-wave (L=1) components of the central quasiparticle interaction, thus suggesting an effective interaction of short range.

## Key claim

The leading-order chiral three-nucleon force provides substantial repulsion in the isotropic spin- and isospin-independent component \(F_0\) of the quasiparticle interaction, which acts to stabilize nuclear matter against isoscalar density oscillations, but the nuclear compression modulus has a rather large uncertainty due to a sensitive dependence on the low-energy constant \(c_3\).

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the first-order (Hartree-Fock) contribution to the \(L=0,1\) central Landau parameters of the quasiparticle interaction in symmetric nuclear matter, starting from the N\(^2\)LO chiral three-nucleon force. They evaluated six topologically distinct one-loop diagrams (pion self-energy correction, one-pion exchange vertex correction, Pauli-blocked two-pion exchange, and contributions from one-pion exchange and contact three-nucleon forces) and combined these with second-order contributions from chiral two-nucleon interactions previously computed in Holt et al. (2011). Two different sets of low-energy constants (\(c_1, c_3, c_4, c_D, c_E\)) were employed, one fitted to \(A=3\) binding energies and the triton half-life with the Idaho N\(^3\)LO potential, and another fitted to \(A=3,4\) binding energies with a low-momentum NN interaction at resolution scale \(\Lambda = 2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- The isotropic spin- and isospin-independent Landau parameter \(f_0\) at nuclear matter saturation density (\(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(k_F = 1.33\) fm\(^{-1}\)) is \(1.218\) fm\(^2\) for the first set of low-energy constants and \(2.488\) fm\(^2\) for the second set.
- The spin-isospin Landau parameter \(g_0^\prime\) at saturation density is \(-0.295\) fm\(^2\) for the first set and \(-0.359\) fm\(^2\) for the second set.
- The \(L=1\) parameter \(f_1\) at saturation density is \(-0.073\) fm\(^2\) for the first set and \(-0.203\) fm\(^2\) for the second set.
- The parameter \(g_1 = f_1^\prime\) at saturation density is \(-0.232\) fm\(^2\) for the first set and \(-0.293\) fm\(^2\) for the second set.
- Using the first set of constants except with \(c_3 = -4.78\) GeV\(^{-1}\) yields \(f_0 = 2.237\) fm\(^2\) at saturation density, close to the value from the second set.
- In the chiral limit (\(m_\pi \rightarrow 0\)), the isotropic parameter \(f_0\) is \(1.287\) fm\(^2\) and \(g_0^\prime\) is \(-0.424\) fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter by Holt et al. (2011), which employed only chiral two-nucleon interactions and found a negative compression modulus (instability against density fluctuations) and an anomalous orbital \(g\)-factor significantly below the empirical range \(0.20-0.26\). Earlier studies by Sjoberg (1973), Dickhoff (1983), Backman (1985), and Holt (2007) suggested that medium-induced (Babu-Brown) interactions could provide repulsion, but were insufficient with two-body forces alone. The present inclusion of the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force provides the needed repulsion in \(F_0\) to stabilize nuclear matter, while the effective mass and symmetry energy receive only small corrections. The results enable future work incorporating the subleading N\(^3\)LO chiral three-body force for full consistency with the second-order two-body calculation.
