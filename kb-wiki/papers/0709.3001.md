# Description of superdeformed bands in light N=Z nuclei using the cranked HFB method

> Superdeformed states in light $N=Z$ nuclei are studied by means of the self-consistent cranking calculation (i.e., the P + QQ model based on the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method). Analyses are given for two typical cases of superdeformed bands in the $A \simeq 40$ mass region, that is, bands where backbending is absent ($^{40}$Ca) and present ($^{36}$Ar). Investigations are carried out, particularly for the following points: cross-shell excitations in the sd and pf shells; the role of the g$_{9/2}$ and d$_{5/2}$ orbitals; the effect of the nuclear pairing; and the interplay between triaxiality and band termination.

## Key claim

The cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with the P+QQ interaction can describe superdeformed bands in light N=Z nuclei, but the agreement with experiment depends on including pairing correlations and, for backbending at high spin, the g9/2 orbital; without the g9/2 orbital the calculation shows no backbending even up to J=25ℏ, whereas including it produces backbending at J=20ℏ as predicted by shell-model calculations.

## Method

The author used the self-consistent cranking HFB method with a P+QQ two-body interaction, solving the variational equation under constraints on total angular momentum and particle number via the gradient method. The model space included the full sd and pf shells (N=2,3) for protons and neutrons, with the g9/2 orbital (N=4) added when its role was examined. The pairing strength and quadrupole coupling constant were determined by comparing to the axially deformed Nilsson model with initial deformation β⁰=0.6 and initial pairing gaps (Δ⁰_p, Δ⁰_n) = (1.360, 1.513) MeV for the standard case.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca at J=0, the total occupation in the d5/2 orbital is 10.93, meaning about 1.07 holes (out of 12) are excited to upper orbitals; the (s1/2,d3/2) subspace holds about 5.14 particles and the pf shell holds about 7.93 particles.
- For ⁴⁰Ca at J=18ℏ without the g9/2 orbital, the d5/2 hole occupation number is 1.78 (total 10.22), the (s1/2,d3/2) subspace holds about 5.75 particles, and the pf shell holds about 7.99 particles.
- For ⁴⁰Ca at J=18ℏ with the g9/2 orbital included, the g9/2 orbital is occupied by 1.09 particles (total), the d5/2 hole occupation is 1.90 (total 10.10), and the pf shell holds about 8.01 particles.
- For ⁴⁰Ca at J=26ℏ with the g9/2 orbital, the g9/2 occupation rises to 2.12 particles, the d5/2 hole occupation becomes 2.89 (total 9.11), and the pf shell holds about 8.00 particles.
- The pairing-gap energies for ⁴⁰Ca disappear at J ≳ 8ℏ for both protons and neutrons with the standard initial pairing gaps.
- The rigid-rotor reference energy used for ⁴⁰Ca is E_R = 0.06909 × J(J+1) MeV.
- The experimental band-head energy for ⁴⁰Ca used for normalization is E(J=0) = 5.218 MeV.

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of superdeformed bands in the A≈40 region, following experimental discoveries in ³⁶Ar (Svensson et al., 2000) and ⁴⁰Ca (Ideguchi et al., 2001). It addresses limitations of prior approaches: the cranked Nilsson model and cranked relativistic mean-field calculations ignored pairing, failing to reproduce low-spin spectra; the projected shell model by Long and Sun (2001) treated pairing but assumed fixed axial deformation; and the generator coordinate method by Bender, Flocard, and Heenen (2003) included pairing but only for low-spin states (J ≤ 6ℏ) without cranking. The present cranked HFB method enables simultaneous treatment of pairing, Coriolis force, and triaxial shape evolution. The results enable future studies of band termination and backbending mechanisms in light nuclei, showing that the g9/2 orbital plays a role analogous to the i13/2 orbital in rare-earth nuclei.
