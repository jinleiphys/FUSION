# Parton-Based Gribov-Regge Theory

> We present a new parton model approach for hadron-hadron interactions and, in particular, for the initial stage of nuclear collisions at very high energies (RHIC, LHC and beyond). The most important aspect of our approach is a self-consistent treatment, using the same formalism for calculating cross sections and particle production, based on an effective, QCD-inspired field theory, where many of the inconsistencies of presently used models will be avoided. In addition, we provide a unified treatment of soft and hard scattering, such that there is no fundamental cutoff parameter any more defining an artificial border between soft and hard scattering. Our approach cures some of the main deficiencies of two of the standard procedures currently used: the Gribov-Regge theory and the eikonalized parton model. There, cross section calculations and particle production cannot be treated in a consistent way using a common formalism. In particular, energy conservation is taken care of in case of particle production, but not concerning cross section calculations. In addition, hard contributions depend crucially on some cutoff, being divergent for the cutoff being zero. Finally, in case of several elementary scatterings, they are not treated on the same level: the first collision is always treated differently than the subsequent ones. All these problems are solved in our new approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new "Parton-based Gribov-Regge Theory" that provides a self-consistent treatment of both cross section calculations and particle production using the same formalism, with energy conservation properly enforced in both cases, unlike standard Gribov-Regge theory and eikonalized parton models where energy conservation is only considered for particle production but not for cross sections.

## Method

The authors construct an effective QCD-inspired field theory where hadronic and nuclear profile functions are expressed in terms of partonic ones, allowing a clean formulation of multiple scattering theory. They distinguish three types of elementary parton-parton scatterings (soft, hard, and semi-hard), where the soft contribution is parameterized via phenomenological soft Pomeron exchange, the hard contribution uses perturbative QCD with DGLAP evolution, and the semi-hard contribution combines a hard parton ladder with soft Pomeron "ends" to handle sea quarks and gluons. The formalism is implemented as the Monte Carlo code neXus version 2.00.

## Key numbers

- The soft Pomeron amplitude is parameterized as \( T_{\mathrm{soft}}(\hat{s},t) = 8\pi s_0 \eta(t) \gamma_{\mathrm{part}}^2 (\hat{s}/s_0)^{\alpha_{\mathrm{soft}}(0)} \exp(\lambda^{(2)}_{\mathrm{soft}}(\hat{s}/s_0) t) \), with \( s_0 \simeq 1 \) GeV\(^2\) as the characteristic hadronic mass scale
- The soft contribution to the total parton interaction cross section is \( \sigma_{\mathrm{soft}}(\hat{s}) = 8\pi \gamma_{\mathrm{part}}^2 (\hat{s}/s_0)^{\alpha_{\mathrm{soft}}(0)-1} \)
- The cutoff for perturbative QCD applicability is \( Q_0^2 \sim 1 \) GeV\(^2\)
- The hard scattering slope \( R_{\mathrm{hard}}^2 \) is negligible compared to the soft Pomeron slope
- A factor \( K \simeq 1.5 \) takes effectively into account higher order QCD corrections in the hard cross section

## Context

This work extends Gribov-Regge theory (Gribov, 1968) and the eikonalized parton model by solving their main deficiencies: inconsistent treatment of cross sections versus particle production, lack of energy conservation in cross section calculations, and unequal treatment of multiple elementary scatterings. The approach enables a unified description of soft and hard scattering without an artificial cutoff parameter, and provides a transparent extrapolation from elementary interactions to nucleus-nucleus collisions for RHIC and LHC energies. The authors note that unitarity problems at very high energies remain to be addressed through triple-Pomeron interactions, which is identified as the next project.
