# In-medium properties of nuclear fragments at the liquid-gas phase coexistence

> Reactions of nuclear multifragmentation of excited finite nuclei can be interpreted as manifestation of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. During this process the matter at subnuclear density clusterizes into hot primary fragments, which are located in the vicinity of other nuclear species. In recent experiments there were found evidences that the symmetry and surface energies of primary fragments change considerably as compared to isolated cold or low-excited nuclei. The new modified properties of primary fragments should be taken into account during their secondary de-excitation.

# In-medium properties of nuclear fragments at the liquid-gas phase coexistence

## Key claim
The symmetry energy coefficient and surface energy of hot primary nuclear fragments formed during multifragmentation are significantly reduced compared to isolated cold nuclei, but this modification applies only under the specific conditions of the liquid-gas phase coexistence region (subnuclear densities and temperatures around 3–8 MeV) and must be taken into account during secondary de-excitation for a self-consistent description of the whole process.

## Method
The author analyzes experimental multifragmentation data using the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM), which treats fragments with A > 4 as heated nuclear liquid drops with free energies parameterized as a sum of bulk, surface, Coulomb, and symmetry energy terms. The symmetry energy coefficient γ is extracted from isoscaling analyses of isotope distributions in INDRA/ALADIN and TAMU experiments, while surface energy modifications are studied from ALADIN data across different event classes corresponding to varying excitation energies. The modified fragment properties are then incorporated into a specially designed evaporation code for secondary de-excitation.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy coefficient γ drops from about 25 MeV for peripheral collisions (cold isolated nuclei) to approximately 15 MeV for central collisions leading to multifragmentation, as extracted from INDRA/ALADIN experiments using tin isotopes induced by 300 and 600 MeV per nucleon ¹²C beams.
- Multifragmentation probes nuclear matter at temperatures T ≈ 3–8 MeV and densities ρ ≈ 0.1–0.3 ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear density.
- The standard SMM parameters include: bulk binding energy W₀ = 16 MeV, surface coefficient B₀ = 18 MeV, critical temperature T_c = 18 MeV, and symmetry energy coefficient γ = 25 MeV for isolated fragments.
- The Coulomb parameter uses r₀ = 1.17 fm in the Wigner-Seitz approximation.
- The characteristic time for multifragmentation is around 100 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) framework, which has been successful in describing charge and thermodynamical characteristics of fragments, by incorporating in-medium modifications of nuclear properties. The findings are consistent with predictions from dynamical models (e.g., the AMD model by Ono (2004)) that hot fragments in dilute nuclear matter have reduced density and symmetry energy. The results contradict the assumption that standard Bethe-Weizsäcker parameters for isolated nuclei apply to primary fragments in the freeze-out volume, and they enable future experiments to determine fragment properties in hot nuclear systems at subnuclear densities, with implications for understanding stellar matter during supernova collapse and explosion as discussed by Botvina (2004, 2005).
