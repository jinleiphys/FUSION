# Thermalized Non-Equilibrated Matter: Compound Processes and Beyond

> A characteristic feature of thermalized non-equilibrated matter is that, in spite of energy relaxation (thermalization), a phase memory of the way the strongly interacting many-body system was excited remains. In this contribution we analyze a low energy evaporating proton data in nucleon induced reactions at $\simeq$62 MeV incident energy with $^{197}$Au, $^{208}$Pb, $^{209}$Bi and $^{nat}$U. Our analysis demonstrates that the thermalized non-equilibrated matter survives a cascade of several evaporating particles. Thus the experiments show that the effect of the anomalously slow phase relaxation, with upper limits of the phase relaxation widths in the range 1-10$^{-4}$ eV, is stable with respect to the multi-step evaporating cascade from the thermalized compound nuclei. We also briefly mention manifestations and implications of the thermalized non-equilibrated matter for some other fields.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that thermalized non-equilibrated matter, characterized by anomalously slow phase relaxation with upper limits of phase relaxation widths in the range 1-10$^{-4}$ eV, survives a cascade of several evaporating particles from compound nuclei, not just a single evaporation step as in prior work.

## Method

The authors analyzed low-energy evaporating proton spectra from nucleon-induced reactions on $^{197}$Au, $^{208}$Pb, $^{209}$Bi and $^{nat}$U at approximately 62 MeV incident energy. They scaled the spectra by outgoing proton energy and inverse-process cross sections, then examined the angular distributions of evaporating protons for outgoing energies below 10 MeV, where multi-step direct and multi-step compound contributions are negligible and only evaporation cascade from thermalized compound nuclei contributes.

## Key numbers

- Upper limits of phase relaxation widths range from approximately 1 eV (for $^{209}$Bi(p,p') data from BP1973) down to approximately 10$^{-4}$ eV (for $^{nat}$U(p,p') data from G05arXiv)
- Typical nuclear energy relaxation (spreading) widths are evaluated as $\Gamma_{spr} \geq$ 1 MeV, making the phase relaxation widths 6 to 10 orders of magnitude smaller
- Forward thermal emission intensities exceed backward ones by factors of 2 to 6 across all data sets
- Excitation energy of compound nuclei for last-chance proton evaporation is $\geq$ 10 MeV, yielding average level-spacing $D \leq 10^{-16}$ MeV and effective Hilbert space dimensions $\Gamma_{spr}/D \geq 10^{16}$
- For the lowest upper limit $\beta \simeq 10^{-4}$ eV, the ratio $\beta/D \simeq 10^{6}$

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Kun and collaborators (2005, 2006, 2007) that dealt with lower excitation energies (approximately 15-20 MeV) where second-chance proton evaporation was forbidden or negligible. The findings contradict the standard assumption of very fast phase relaxation in the modern theory of highly excited quantum many-body systems described by Grimes, Massey, and collaborators (1998). The results have implications for many-qubit quantum computation, chemical reactions (where slow phase relaxation appears in H+D$_2$, F+HD, and He+H$_2^+$ calculations), and heavy-ion collisions (including $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg, $^{24}$Mg+$^{24}$Mg, and $^{28}$Si+$^{28}$Si scattering).
