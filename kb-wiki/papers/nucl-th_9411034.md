# The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov Sum Rule

> The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov (DHG) sum rule relates the helicity structure of the photoabsorption cross section to the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon. It is based on Lorentz and gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, causality and unitarity. A generalized DHG sum rule my be derived for virtual photons. At low momentum transfer this generalized sum rule is saturated by the resonance region, at high momentum transfer it may be expressed by the parton spin distributions measured in deep inelastic scattering. The longitudinal-transverse interference determines the Cottingham sum rule, which is related to the electric and magnetic form factors over the whole range of momentum transfer.

# The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov Sum Rule

## Key claim
The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov (DHG) sum rule connects the helicity structure of the photoabsorption cross section to the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon, but has never been measured directly; existing analyses of pion photoproduction show good agreement for the isovector contribution but a large discrepancy for the isoscalar-isovector interference term, suggesting the latter integral might need a subtraction.

## Method
The paper derives the DHG sum rule for real photons from dispersion relations for the spin-flip Compton scattering amplitude, using low energy theorems to relate the leading term to the square of the anomalous magnetic moment. It then generalizes the sum rule to virtual photons in electron scattering, expressing the helicity-dependent cross sections in terms of CGLN multipoles and relating them to the Bjorken structure functions G1 and G2. The generalized sum rule is evaluated using phenomenological models, vector meson dominance, constituent quark models, chiral perturbation theory, and perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers
- The DHG sum rule at the real photon point: I(Q^2=0) = -kappa^2/4, where kappa is the anomalous magnetic moment
- For the isovector channel (I_VV), experiment gives -0.86, in agreement with the DHG prediction of -0.86
- For the isoscalar-isovector interference (I_SV), experiment gives -0.15, while the DHG prediction is +0.06
- The Delta(1232) resonance contributes I_VV = -0.93 to the isovector channel
- The asymptotic behavior at large Q^2 follows I(Q^2) proportional to 2m^2/Q^2 times Gamma_1, with Gamma_1^p - Gamma_1^n approximately 0.19 from current algebra
- The Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule at Q^2=0 gives J_2(0) = (1/4)*kappa*mu, where mu is the total magnetic moment

## Context
This review extends the original work of Drell and Hearn (1966) and Gerasimov (1966) on the real-photon sum rule to virtual photons, connecting low-energy resonance physics with deep inelastic scattering. It discusses the failure of the constituent quark model (Isgur and Karl, 1978 and 1979) to describe the DHG sum rule despite successfully describing the excitation spectrum, and shows that two-body currents from Poincare invariance are required. The paper enables future experimental tests at CEBAF and connects to chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard et al. (1993) and QCD sum rule predictions by Balitsky et al. (1990).
