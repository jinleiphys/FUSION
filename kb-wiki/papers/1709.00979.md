# Entropy production and isotropization in Yang-Mills theory with use of quantum distribution function

> We investigate thermalization process in relativistic heavy ion collisions in terms of the Husimi-Wehrl (HW) entropy defined with the Husimi function, a quantum distribution function in a phase space. We calculate the semiclassical time evolution of the HW entropy in Yang-Mills field theory with the phenomenological initial field configuration known as the McLerran-Venugopalan model in a non-expanding geometry, which has instabilty triggered by initial field fluctuations. HW-entropy production implies the thermalization of the system and it reflects the underlying dynamics such as chaoticity and instability. By comparing the production rate with the Kolmogorov-Sinaï rate, we find that the HW entropy production rate is significantly larger than that expected from chaoticity. We also show that the HW entropy is finally saturated when the system reaches a quasi-stationary state. The saturation time of the HW entropy is comparable with that of pressure isotropization, which is around $1$ fm/c in the present calculation in the non-expanding geometry.

## Key claim

The Husimi-Wehrl (HW) entropy production rate in Yang-Mills theory starting from the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model initial condition is significantly larger than the Kolmogorov-Sinaï (KS) rate expected from intrinsic chaoticity, suggesting that instabilities in the initial glasma-like field configuration trigger the entropy production rather than chaoticity alone.

## Method

The authors performed semiclassical time evolution of the Husimi-Wehrl entropy in SU(2) Yang-Mills lattice field theory on $32^3$ and $64^3$ lattices using the test particle (TP) and parallel test particle (pTP) methods with a product ansatz. The initial Wigner function was constructed from the MV model glasma-like field configuration with Gaussian quantum fluctuations, and the Husimi function was obtained by smearing the Wigner function with a minimal Gaussian packet of width parameter $\Delta/\omega=1$.

## Key numbers

- The HW entropy per degree of freedom starts from the minimum value $S_{\rm HW}/N_D = 1$ and increases rapidly until $g^2 \mu t = 3$ at a common rate on both $32^3$ and $64^3$ lattices.
- On the $64^3$ lattice, the total energy is $\langle H \rangle = 6.5 \times 10^5$ with energy density $\varepsilon = 2.48$, and the slope from the local (intermediate) KS rate is $0.14$ ($0.074$).
- The early-time HW entropy growth rate is approximately $dS_{\rm HW}/d(g^2 \mu t)/N_D \simeq 0.14$, close to the local KS rate and significantly larger than the intermediate KS rate.
- The saturation time of the HW entropy is comparable with that of pressure isotropization, around $1$ fm/c in the non-expanding geometry.
- The produced entropy on the $64^3$ lattice is $\Delta S/N_D \simeq 0.4$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of HW entropy in Yang-Mills theory (Tsukiji 2015, Tsukiji 2016) from random initial conditions to the phenomenologically relevant MV model initial condition used in color glass condensate descriptions of heavy-ion collisions. The finding that entropy production exceeds the KS rate from chaoticity alone contrasts with earlier work (Kunihiro, Iida 2013) where the HW entropy growth rate agreed with the KS rate for random initial conditions. The results enable quantitative comparison between entropy production and pressure isotropization timescales, addressing the early thermalization problem in heavy-ion collisions where hydrodynamical models suggest thermalization at $\tau_{\rm th} = 0.6-1.0$ fm/c.
