# K_{l 3} and π_{e 3} transition form factors

> $K_{\ell 3}$ and $π_{e 3}$ transition form factors are calculated as an application of Dyson-Schwinger equations. The role of nonanalytic contributions to the quark--W-boson vertex is elucidated. A one-parameter model for this vertex provides a uniformly good description of these transitions, including the value of the scalar form factor of the kaon at the Callan-Treiman point. The $K_{\ell 3}$ form factors, $f_\pm^K$, are approximately linear on $t\in [m_e^2,m_μ^2]$ and have approximately the same slope. $f_-^K(0)$ is a measure of the Euclidean constituent-quark mass ratio: $M^E_s/M^E_u$. In the isospin symmetric limit: $-f_+^π(0)= F_π(t)$, the electromagnetic pion form factor, and $f_-^π(t)\equiv 0$.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation framework, using a one-parameter model for the dressed quark-W-boson vertex that includes nonanalytic (resonance) contributions, provides a uniformly good description of K_{l3} and π_{e3} transition form factors; without these nonanalytic contributions, the scalar form factor at the Callan-Treiman point cannot reproduce the current-algebra prediction f_0(Δ) = -f_K/f_π.

## Method

The transition form factors were calculated in impulse approximation using dressed-quark propagators (from algebraic approximations to Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions) and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the pion and kaon, with a Ball-Chiu Ansatz for the quark-W-boson vertex. A heuristic modification was introduced to incorporate nonanalytic K-π loop contributions into the vertex, parametrized by a single constant c_l determined by requiring f_0(Δ) = -f_K/f_π. The calculations were performed in Euclidean space in the isospin symmetric limit (m_u = m_d).

## Key numbers

- Euclidean constituent-quark masses: M^E_{u,d} = 330 MeV, M^E_s = 490 MeV
- f_+(0) = -0.98 (consistent with Ademollo-Gatto theorem, f_+^2(0) ≈ 1)
- f_-(0) = 0.24 (using the analytic vertex Ansatz)
- f_-(t_m) = 0.27 (using the analytic vertex Ansatz), where t_m = (m_K - m_π)^2
- λ_+^e = 0.018, λ_+^μ = 0.018 (using the analytic vertex Ansatz)
- λ_-^e = 0.012, λ_-^μ = 0.012 (using the analytic vertex Ansatz)
- f_0(Δ) = 0.95 (analytic vertex) versus 1.22 (with nonanalytic contributions, c_l = 0.17), where Δ = m_K^2 - m_π^2
- r_{πK} = 0.47 fm (analytic vertex) versus 0.60 fm (with nonanalytic contributions)
- D = 0.160 GeV^2 (the mass scale parameter)
- f_π ≈ 92 MeV, f_K ≈ 113 MeV (calculated normalisation constants)

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of electromagnetic form factors (Roberts 1996; Bicudo, Ribeiro, and Tandy 1996) to weak semileptonic transitions, and resolves a systematic discrepancy between quark-based analyses (such as S. I. and A. B. 1992; A. B. 1996) and meson-based analyses (such as L. R. 1984; G. L. 1985) regarding the scalar form factor at the Callan-Treiman point. The inclusion of nonanalytic K-π loop contributions enables correct description of f_0^K in the timelike region, and the framework allows study of the t-dependence over the entire domain -∞ < t < (m_K - m_π)^2, enabling future applications to baryon weak-interaction processes.
