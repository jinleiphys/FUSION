# Topology in the SU(N_f) chiral symmetry restored phase of unquenched QCD and axion cosmology II

> We investigate the physical consequences of the survival of the effects of the U(1)_A anomaly in the chiral symmetric phase of QCD, and show that the free energy density is a singular function of the quark mass m, in the chiral limit, and that the $σ$ and $\barπ$ susceptibilities diverge in this limit at any $T\ge T_c$. We also show that the difference between the $\barπ$ and $\barδ$ susceptibilities diverges in the chiral limit at any $T\ge T_c$, a result which seems to be excluded by recent results of Tomiya et al. from numerical simulations of two-flavor QCD. We also discuss on the generalization of these results to the $N_f\ge 3$ model.

## Key claim

If the topological effects of the U(1)_A anomaly survive in the high-temperature chiral symmetric phase of QCD, then the free energy density is a singular function of the quark mass m in the chiral limit at any T > T_c, and the sigma and pion susceptibilities diverge in this limit. This scenario appears to be excluded by recent numerical simulations of two-flavor QCD by Tomiya et al., but previous results by Dick et al. using overlap fermions in the valence sector seem to predict the divergent behavior.

## Method

The author uses analytical methods based on the continuum Euclidean Lagrangian of N_f flavor QCD with a theta term, assuming a lattice regularization with Ginsparg-Wilson fermions. The analysis examines the Q=0 topological sector and exploits the relation between observables in this sector and those at theta=0, using anomalous U(1)_A transformations and the properties of the free energy density. The argument proceeds by assuming the correlation length and sigma susceptibility are finite in the chiral limit, deriving a contradiction with the survival of U(1)_A anomaly effects, and concluding that the free energy density must be singular.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent delta for the three-dimensional O(4) vector universality class is delta = 4.789(6) at T = T_c.
- The sigma susceptibility diverges in the chiral limit as chi_sigma(m) approximately proportional to m^{(1-delta)/delta}.
- The difference between the pion and delta susceptibilities diverges as chi_pi(m) - chi_delta(m) approximately proportional to m^{(1-delta)/delta}.
- Tomiya et al. find a value of chi_pi(m) - chi_delta(m) in the chiral limit at temperatures T ~ 190-220 MeV that is compatible with zero, not divergent.
- Dick et al. on larger lattices with overlap fermions in the valence sector predict a divergent chi_pi(m) - chi_delta(m) in the chiral limit.

## Context

This paper extends the author's previous work (Azcoiti, 2017) which assumed a nonvanishing convergence radius for the perturbative expansion of the free energy density and concluded that all topological effects of the axial anomaly should disappear in the high-temperature phase. The current work takes the opposite hypothesis and derives singular behavior. The results contradict numerical simulations by Tomiya et al. of two-flavor QCD, but agree with earlier results by Dick et al. The analysis for N_f >= 3 is argued to be excluded by universality and renormalization-group arguments based on Landau-Ginzburg-Wilson Phi^4 theory (Pisarski and Wilczek, 1984; Hasenbusch, 2001).
