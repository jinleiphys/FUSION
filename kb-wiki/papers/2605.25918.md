# Predicted Exotic Doubly Heavy-Strange Pentaquarks

> We predict exotic doubly heavy--strange pentaquarks with minimal quark content $u\bar d sQQ'$ ($QQ'=cc,bc,bb$) within a coupled-channel unitary framework where the interaction is derived from an extension of the local hidden gauge approach to the heavy-quark sector. We obtain a robust spectrum of manifestly exotic states; two states appear in the $u\bar d scc$ sector, three in $u\bar d scb$, and four in $u\bar d sbb$. These emerge either as bound states or resonances, along with five additional virtual states manifested as threshold cusps. The binding mechanism is dominated by off-diagonal transitions among heavy-baryon--light-meson channels, while diagonal interactions are strongly suppressed. These results extend exotic hadron spectroscopy into the doubly heavy--strange sector and provide concrete targets for future experimental searches.

# Predicted Exotic Doubly Heavy-Strange Pentaquarks

## Key claim

The authors predict the existence of manifestly exotic doubly heavy-strange pentaquarks with minimal quark content u d-bar s Q Q' (Q Q' = cc, bc, bb) within a coupled-channel unitary framework, but the states emerge only through off-diagonal transitions among heavy-baryon-light-meson channels because diagonal interactions are strongly suppressed or vanish identically.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channel unitary framework based on an extension of the local hidden gauge approach to the heavy-quark sector, where the interaction is generated through vector-meson exchange between mesons and baryons. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in coupled channels for pseudoscalar-meson-baryon and vector-meson-baryon configurations, with the loop functions regularized using a cutoff method with q_max approximately 700 MeV. The spin-independent interaction kernel leads to degenerate spectra among different J^P channels within a given meson-baryon configuration.

## Key numbers

- Two states appear in the u d-bar s cc sector: one at 4483.33 - i0.39 MeV (VB(1/2^+) sector, J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-) and one at 4541.02 MeV (VB(3/2^+) sector, J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-)
- Four states appear in the u d-bar s bb sector: at 10827.87 - i159.98 MeV (PB(1/2^+), J^P = 1/2^-), 10856.83 - i160.58 MeV (PB(3/2^+), J^P = 3/2^-), 11048.40 - i124.64 MeV (VB(1/2^+), J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-), and 11077.41 - i125.62 MeV (VB(3/2^+), J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-)
- Three states appear in the u d-bar s cb sector: at 7773.55 MeV (VB(1/2^+), J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-), 7811.46 MeV (VB'(1/2^+), J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-), and 7832.31 MeV (VB(3/2^+), J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-)
- Cutoff variation of q_max = 675-725 MeV yields maximal mass variations of about 2 MeV for u d-bar s cc, 4 MeV for u d-bar s cb, and approximately 12 MeV in mass and 10 MeV in width for u d-bar s bb sectors
- The coupling constant g = m_V/(2 f_pi) with m_V = 800 MeV and f_pi = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of exotic hadron spectroscopy into the doubly heavy-strange sector, building on previous predictions of hidden-charm pentaquarks discovered by LHCb (2015, 2020, 2021, 2022) and theoretical frameworks using unitarized extended hidden-gauge approaches by Wu et al. (2010, 2011) and Hofmann and Lutz (2005). The formalism closely follows the approach developed by Wang et al. (2022) for Omega_cc, Omega_bb, and Omega_cb states, and the results provide concrete targets for future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb, enabling the exploration of manifestly exotic configurations that cannot be realized in conventional three-quark baryons.
