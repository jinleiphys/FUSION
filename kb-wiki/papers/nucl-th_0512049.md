# Viscous relativistic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Ideal fluid dynamics successfully describes collective flow in central Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy near midrapidity, but a consistent causal dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics framework is needed to quantify deviations in peripheral collisions, at forward rapidity, or lower energies, and to extract transport coefficients from data. The paper presents a pedagogical review of the Israel-Stewart second-order formalism and shows preliminary numerical results that depend sensitively on the initial viscous pressure, shear viscosity coefficient, and relaxation time.

## Method

The paper derives the relativistic fluid equations from conservation laws for baryon number and energy-momentum, then introduces dissipative corrections via the Israel-Stewart second-order formalism to ensure causality. For baryon-free systems with longitudinal boost invariance and azimuthal symmetry, the author presents the (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic equations in \((\tau,r,\phi,\eta)\) coordinates, coupled with relaxation equations for the bulk viscous pressure \(\Pi\) and shear pressure tensor components \(\pi^{\eta\eta}\) and \(\pi^{\phi\phi}\). Numerical simulations use a massless ideal gas equation of state \(p=e/3\) with Woods-Saxon initial energy density profiles, treating shear viscosity \(\eta\) and relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) as phenomenological parameters guided by perturbative QCD and AdS/CFT estimates.

## Key numbers

- Perturbative QCD estimate for shear viscosity at \(\alpha_s\approx0.5\): \(\eta/s = 0.135\) (Arnold et al., 2000)
- AdS/CFT lower bound for shear viscosity in strongly coupled \(N=4\) SUSY YM: \(\eta/s \geq 1/(4\pi) = 0.08\) (Policastro et al., 2001)
- Initial conditions: temperature \(T_i = 0.3\) GeV, initial time \(\tau_i = 0.5\) fm/\(c\), Woods-Saxon parameters \(R = 6.4\) fm, \(a = 0.54\) fm
- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_f = 0.16\) GeV
- Kinetic theory relaxation time estimate: \(\tau_\pi = 2\eta \beta_2 = 2\eta \cdot 3/(4p)\) (Israel and Stewart, 1979)
- Initial viscous pressure components for Bjorken scaling: \(\pi^{rr}_{\rm ini} = r^2 \pi_{\rm ini}^{\phi\phi} = -\frac{\tau_i^2}{2} \pi_{\rm ini}^{\eta\eta} = \frac{2\eta}{3\tau_i}\)
- Fireball lifetime extended by 20% when initial viscous pressure increased from zero to \(4\eta/(3\tau_i)\) at fixed \(\eta/s = 0.135\)
- Freeze-out time in fireball center increases by 25% when relaxation time increased by factor 4 (from \(0.5\tau_{\rm kin}\) to \(2\tau_{\rm kin}\))

## Context

This work extends the ideal fluid dynamics framework reviewed by Heinz (2004) and the earlier causal dissipative formulation of Israel and Stewart (1979). It builds on recent numerical implementations by Muronga (2001), Muronga and Rischke (2004), and Chaudhuri and Heinz (2005) that are (1+1)-dimensional with boost-invariant longitudinal dynamics. The paper enables future extraction of transport coefficients from heavy-ion collision data and establishes that viscous hydrodynamics has a minimum applicability time \(\tau_i\) below which matching to a preceding non-equilibrium kinetic evolution is required.
