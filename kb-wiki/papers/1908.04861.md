# The Faddeev-Yakubovsky symphony

> We briefly summarize the main steps leading to the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space for N=3, 4 and 5 interacting particles.

## Key claim

The Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations provide a rigorous mathematical formalism for solving the non-relativistic N-body quantum mechanical problem, but in practice the number of interacting particles for which they have been properly solved remains severely limited to N ≤ 5, even more than 50 years after Faddeev's seminal work.

## Method

The authors summarize the derivation of Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space for N=3, 4, and 5 interacting particles, starting from the shortcomings of the Schrödinger equation for describing three-body scattering. They present the Faddeev decomposition of the wave function into components associated with each interacting pair, then generalize to the Yakubovsky scheme for N=4 and N=5 by introducing additional decompositions corresponding to topologically distinct ways of breaking the N-body cluster into subsystems. The equations are reduced to systems of coupled integro-differential equations via partial wave expansion in Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

- For N=3, the Faddeev equations reduce to a system of 3 coupled equations for the Faddeev components Φ_{ij}
- For N=4, the Faddeev-Yakubovsky decomposition yields 18 coupled FY components: 12 K-like components and 6 H-like components
- For N=5, the decomposition yields 180 FY equations: 60 K-type amplitudes and 30 each of H, T, S, and F types
- For N=3 identical bosons with total angular momentum L=0 in S-wave approximation, the numerical coefficient c = 2 with n_a = 1
- For N=3 identical fermions with total spin J=3/2 in S-wave approximation, the numerical coefficient c = -1 with n_a = 1
- For N=3 identical fermions with total spin J=1/2 in S-wave approximation, n_a = 2 with c_{11}=c_{22}=1/2 and c_{12}=c_{21}=3/2

## Context

This work extends the original Faddeev equations (Faddeev, 1960) and Yakubovsky's generalization (Yakubovsky, 1967) by presenting a unified pedagogical treatment of the configuration-space formulation for up to N=5 particles. The authors reference their own recent solutions of the 5-body problem (Lazauskas, 2018) and note that the Merkuriev approach for handling Coulomb forces (Merkuriev, 1980, 1981) has been successfully applied to problems like p-d reactions and atomic systems. The paper enables further work on exact solutions of few-body problems with realistic interactions, though the authors note that even for the three-body case many unresolved problems remain, particularly those involving an infinite number of open asymptotic channels.
