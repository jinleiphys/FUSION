# A new spectroscopic probe to search for magic numbers at high-excitation energies

> Empirical drops in ground-state nuclear polarizabilities indicate deviations from the effect of giant dipole resonances and may reveal the presence of shell effects in semi-magic nuclei with neutron magic numbers $N=50$, 82 and 126. Similar drops of polarizability in the quasi-continuum of nuclei with, or close to, magic numbers $N=28$, 50 and 82, could reflect the continuing influence of shell closures up to the nucleon separation energy. These findings open a new avenue to investigating magic numbers at high-excitation energies and strongly support recent large-scale shell-model calculations in the quasi-continuum region, which describe the origin of the low-energy enhancement of the photon strength function as induced paramagnetism. The nuclear-structure dependence of the photon-strength function asserts the generalized Brink-Axel hypothesis as more universal than originally expected.

## Key claim

Empirical drops in ground-state nuclear polarizabilities may reveal shell effects in semi-magic nuclei with neutron magic numbers N=50, 82 and 126, and similar drops in the quasi-continuum of nuclei with or close to magic numbers N=28, 50 and 82 could reflect the continuing influence of shell closures up to the nucleon separation energy, provided that the low-energy enhancement of the photon strength function and giant dipole resonance contributions to the (-2) moment of the total photo-absorption cross section are properly combined.

## Method

The authors combine cross-section contributions from the low-energy enhancement (LEE) region and the giant dipole resonance (GDR) region using the relation f(E_gamma) = (1/(g_J pi^2 (hbar c)^2)) * sigma_total(E_gamma)/E_gamma, where g_J is the statistical factor depending on spins of initial and final states. They calculate the (-2) moment of the total photo-absorption cross section, sigma_{-2}, by integrating sigma_total(E_gamma)/E_gamma^2 over the energy range from the LEE region up through the GDR region, using experimental photo-absorption data from EXFOR and ENSDF databases. A cubic spline interpolation is employed to connect the LEE and GDR data regions, as linear interpolation underestimates total photo-nuclear cross-section values and higher-order polynomials predict un-anticipated structures.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear polarizability from ground states to the quasi-continuum region, building on the Migdal (1944) hydrodynamic model of the electric dipole polarizability and the Brink-Axel hypothesis for the photon strength function. The findings support recent large-scale shell-model calculations by Schwengner, Frauendorf and Larsen (2013) and by Brown and Larsen (2014) describing the low-energy enhancement as induced paramagnetism from high-j orbits, and by Sieja (2017) for the A~50 region. The results open a new avenue for identifying magic numbers at high-excitation energies by using the (-2) moment of the total photo-absorption cross section as a spectroscopic probe, and assert the generalized Brink-Axel hypothesis as more universal than originally expected.
