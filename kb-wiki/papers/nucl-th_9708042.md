# f_K/f_pi ratio from QCD sum rules

> Using the correlation function of the axial vector mesons, we present a QCD sum rule calculation for the decay constants f_pi and f_K. Our calculations are only weakly dependent on the SU(3) breaking-parameter for the QCD vacuum and give the ratio f_K/f_pi = 1.11 $\pm$ 0.02.

## Key claim

Using the correlation function of axial vector mesons (rather than pseudoscalar currents, which suffer from direct instanton contamination), the authors obtain a ratio f_K/f_pi = 1.11 ± 0.02 that is only weakly dependent on the SU(3) breaking parameter for the QCD vacuum, though the individual value of f_K is smaller than the experimental value.

## Method

The authors perform a QCD sum rule analysis of the two-point correlation function of axial vector currents, which couples to both the pseudoscalar mesons (pi, K) and the axial vector mesons (a_1, K_a). They extract the decay constants f_pi and f_K by fitting the Borel-transformed sum rule to a phenomenological spectral density that includes pole contributions from the pseudoscalar and axial vector mesons plus a continuum, using the experimentally known a_1 mass (1.230 ± 0.040 GeV) and an estimated K_a mass (~1.340 GeV). The analysis is performed for four cases that systematically vary the treatment of the strange quark condensate, the inclusion of a mixed condensate, and the running of the strong coupling constant alpha_s(M).

## Key numbers

*   f_K/f_pi = 1.11 ± 0.02 (Case IV, with quark condensate <qq> = -(0.230 GeV)^3, including mixed condensate and running alpha_s(M)).
*   f_pi = 0.130 ± 0.002 GeV (Case IV, <qq> = -(0.230 GeV)^3, with uncertainty from the a_1 mass).
*   f_K = 0.144 GeV (Case IV, <qq> = -(0.230 GeV)^3).
*   f_pi = 0.132 ± 0.003 GeV (Case IV, <qq> = -(0.250 GeV)^3).
*   f_K = 0.144 GeV (Case IV, <qq> = -(0.250 GeV)^3).
*   For the a_1 meson coupling: 4pi/g_{a_1}^2 = 0.42 ± 0.02 (Case IV, <qq> = -(0.230 GeV)^3).
*   For the K_a meson coupling: 4pi/g_{K_a}^2 = 0.38 (Case IV, <qq> = -(0.230 GeV)^3).

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule calculations of meson properties by Reinders, Rubinstein, and Yazaki (1985) and Govaerts et al. (1987), specifically avoiding the direct instanton contamination that plagues pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar correlation functions as noted by Shuryak (1983). The result (f_K/f_pi = 1.11) lies at the lower end of predictions from many other models (e.g., chiral perturbation theory, potential models, and other QCD sum rules) and is close to some lattice gauge theory results (e.g., Hamber (1989) with 1.10), but is smaller than the experimental value of 1.22 ± 0.02. The authors note that a better determination of the strange quark mass m_s would improve the accuracy of f_K.
