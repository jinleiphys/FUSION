# Abundance of Ground States with Positive Parity

> We investigate analytically and numerically a random-matrix model for m fermions occupying l1 single-particle states with positive parity and l2 single-particle states with negative parity and interacting through random two-body forces that conserve parity. The single-particle states are completely degenerate and carry no further quantum numbers. We compare spectra of many-body states with positive and with negative parity. We show that in the dilute limit, ground states with positive and with negative parity occur with equal probability. Differences in the ground-state probabilities are, thus, a finite-size effect and are mainly due to different dimensions of the Hilbert spaces of either parity.

# Abundance of Ground States with Positive Parity

## Key claim

In the dilute limit (where the number of fermions and single-particle states go to infinity with their ratio going to zero), ground states with positive and negative parity occur with equal probability; any observed preponderance of positive-parity ground states is therefore a finite-size effect arising primarily from differences in the dimensions of the Hilbert spaces of each parity, not from any fundamental asymmetry in the random-matrix ensemble.

## Method

The authors constructed a random-matrix model (a modified version of the embedded two-body ensemble, EGOE(2)) for m spinless fermions occupying l1 positive-parity and l2 negative-parity degenerate single-particle states, interacting through random two-body forces that conserve parity. They analytically computed normalized traces of the Hamiltonian and its square for both parity sectors, then used these moments together with a scale factor r to estimate ground-state energies via the relation E_ground = nTrace(H) - r*sigma. Numerical diagonalizations of up to 24310-dimensional Hamiltonian matrices were performed for various combinations of m, l1, and l2, with 100 random realizations per parameter set, to determine the scale factors and the probability of positive-parity ground states.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of parity preponderance in the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) of the nuclear shell model, following the observation by Johnson (1998) that ground states with spin zero occur much more frequently than their statistical weight would suggest, and the finding by Zhao (2004) of a similar preponderance for positive-parity states. The authors use an analytical trace-based method developed by Papenbrock (2004) and improved by Yoshida (2006) for estimating ground-state energies, and they apply it to parity rather than spin because parity is analytically more tractable. The results enable a clear separation of finite-size effects from asymptotic behavior in random-matrix models of nuclear structure, and they suggest that similar analyses for spin quantum numbers would require accounting for the dependence of random-matrix forms on total spin.
