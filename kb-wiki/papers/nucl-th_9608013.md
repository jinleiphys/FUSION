# Self-Screened Parton Cascades

> The high density of scattered partons predicted in nuclear collisions at very high energy makes color screening effects significant. We explain how these screening mechanisms may suppress nonperturbative, soft QCD processes, permitting a consistent calculation of quark-gluon plasma formation within the framework of perturbative QCD. We present results of a model calculation of these effects including predictions for the initial thermalized state for heavy nuclei colliding at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

Color screening from the dense medium of already-scattered partons can dynamically regulate infrared divergences in perturbative QCD calculations of quark-gluon plasma formation, eliminating the need for artificial momentum and virtuality cut-offs, but only under the condition that the parton density is high enough, specifically, this framework applies at RHIC and LHC energies for heavy nuclei but not at SPS/AGS energies or in proton-proton collisions.

## Method

The authors model nuclear collisions using a parton cascade framework based on a relativistic transport equation with perturbative QCD 2→2 scattering amplitudes. They compute a static color screening mass μ_D(p_T) from the number density of produced minijets with transverse momenta above a given p_T, then feed this screening mass back into the minijet cross section by replacing the Mandelstam variables (t̂, û) with (t̂ − μ_D², û − μ_D²). This self-consistent set of equations is solved numerically by starting at large p_T with no screening and integrating downward to smaller p_T.

## Key numbers

- For A=200 collisions at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV per nucleon pair), the screening mass saturates at slightly below 1 GeV.
- For A=200 collisions at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV per nucleon pair), the screening mass saturates at 1.5 GeV.
- Initial conditions for hydrodynamic expansion at τ_i = 0.25 fm/c: at RHIC, energy density ε_i = 61.4 GeV/fm³ and temperature T_i = 0.668 GeV; at LHC, ε_i = 425 GeV/fm³ and T_i = 1.02 GeV.
- Initial gluon mean free path at RHIC: λ_g^(i) = 0.34 fm; at LHC: λ_g^(i) = 0.43 fm.
- Pure plasma lifetime: about 4 fm/c at RHIC and 18 fm/c at LHC.
- The thermal hydrodynamic description applies after τ_i ≈ 0.3 fm/c for initial temperatures T ≥ 0.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade model of Geiger and Müller (1995) and earlier studies by Eskola and Wang (1994) by replacing arbitrary infrared cut-off parameters (p_T^min ≈ 1.5–2 GeV/c and μ_0² ≈ 0.5–1 GeV²/c²) with dynamically calculated medium-induced cut-offs based on color screening. The concept is analogous to the interaction among ladders in the traditional picture of soft hadronic interactions by Mueller (1976) and is akin to the random classical color fields proposed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) for the small-x gluon structure of large nuclei. The results enable predictions for the initial thermalized state of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, and suggest that the two approaches (self-screened cascades and classical color fields) may be connected.
