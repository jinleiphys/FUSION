# Meson Mixing in Pion Superfluid

> We investigate meson mixing and meson coupling constants in pion superfluid in the framework of two flavor NJL model at finite isospin density. The mixing strength develops fast with increasing isospin chemical potential, and the coupling constants in normal phase and in the pion superfluid phase behave very differently.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the pion superfluid phase, the conventional $\sigma$ and $\pi$ mesons are no longer eigenmodes; the new eigenmodes are linear combinations of them, with mixing that develops rapidly above the critical isospin chemical potential $\mu_I^c = m_\pi$, and the coupling constants in the normal phase and in the superfluid phase behave very differently.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation for quarks and the random phase approximation (RPA) for mesons at finite isospin chemical potential $\mu_I$, zero baryon chemical potential $\mu_B$, and zero temperature (with one case at $T=0.1$ GeV and $\mu_B=0.4$ GeV). They compute the quark propagator including off-diagonal elements due to the pion condensate, then construct the $3\times3$ meson polarization matrix for $\sigma$, $\pi_+$, and $\pi_-$ to determine the masses and coupling constants of the mixed eigenmodes $\overline\sigma$, $\overline\pi_+$, and $\overline\pi_-$, introducing mixing angles $\alpha$, $\beta$, and $\gamma$ to quantify the mixing strengths.

## Key numbers

- Critical isospin chemical potential at $T=\mu_B=0$: $\mu_I^c = m_\pi = 134$ MeV.
- At $T=0.1$ GeV and $\mu_B=0.4$ GeV, the critical isospin chemical potential increases to $\mu_I^c = 153$ MeV.
- The $\pi_+ - \sigma$ mixing angle $\alpha$ reaches $\pi/4$ at $\mu_I > 200$ MeV, indicating the $\pi_+$ component in $\overline\sigma$ exceeds the $\sigma$ component.
- The $\pi_- - \sigma$ mixing angle $\beta$ exceeds $\alpha$ for all $\mu_I > \mu_I^c$, with $\pi_-$ dominating $\overline\sigma$ for $\mu_I > 150$ MeV.
- The baryon chemical potential threshold below which the phase diagram is unchanged at $T=0$ is $\mu_B = 3(M_q - m_\pi/2) \sim 730$ MeV.
- Model parameters: current quark mass $m_0 = 5$ MeV, coupling constant $G = 4.93$ GeV$^{-2}$, cutoff $\Lambda = 653$ MeV, yielding vacuum pion mass $m_\pi = 134$ MeV, pion decay constant $f_\pi = 93$ MeV, and effective quark mass $M_q = 310$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model calculations of meson masses in the pion superfluid by He et al. (2005) to include meson mixing and coupling constants, which are needed for dynamic processes such as $\pi\pi$ scattering and the $\pi_+/\pi_-$ ratio in isospin-asymmetric matter. The results are consistent with the Goldstone theorem: the $\overline\pi_+$ mode is massless ($M_{\overline\pi_+}=0$) and contains only $\pi_+$ and $\pi_-$ components in equal fractions. The findings enable further study of signatures of the pion superfluid in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions, and complement lattice QCD results by Kogut et al. (2002) and chiral perturbation theory by Son and Stephanov (2001).
