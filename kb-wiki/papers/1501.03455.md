# $D_{s0}^{\ast\pm}(2317)$ and $KD$ scattering from $B^0_s$ decay

> We study the $\bar{B}_s^0\to D_s^-(DK)^+$ weak decay, and look at the $DK$ invariant mass distribution with the aim of obtaining relevant information on the nature of the $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ resonance. We make a simulation of the experiment using the actual mass of the $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ resonance and recent lattice QCD relevant parameters of the $KD$ scattering amplitude. We then solve the inverse problem of obtaining the $KD$ amplitude from these synthetic data, to which we have added a 5\% or 10\% error. We prove that one can obtain from these "data" the existence of a bound $KD$ state, the $KD$ scattering length and effective range, and most importantly, the $KD$ probability in the wave function of the bound state obtained, which was found to be largely dominant from the lattice QCD results. This means that one can obtain information on the nature of the $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ resonance from the implementation of this experiment, in the line of finding the structure of resonances, which is one of the main aims in hadron spectroscopy.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate through a simulation that the $\bar{B}_s^0\to D_s^-(DK)^+$ weak decay can be used to extract the $KD$ scattering amplitude and, crucially, the probability $P_{DK}$ of the $KD$ molecular component in the $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ wave function, provided the experimental error on the $DK$ invariant mass distribution is at the 5% or 10% level.

## Method

The authors generate synthetic data for the $DK$ invariant mass distribution of the $\bar{B}_s^0\to D_s^-(DK)^+$ decay using a theoretical model that incorporates the $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ as a $KD$ bound state, with parameters taken from lattice QCD (binding energy, scattering length, effective range, and $P_{DK}=0.75$). They then perform a Monte Carlo simulation, adding Gaussian errors of 5% or 10% to the synthetic data, and fit the resulting "data" to extract the $KD$ scattering amplitude, the bound-state mass, the scattering length and effective range, and the $KD$ probability $P_{DK}$.

## Key numbers

*   $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ mass: $2317\ \text{MeV}$ (nominal value used as input).
*   Binding energy from lattice QCD reanalysis: $B = 31 \pm 17\ \text{MeV}$ (Sasa et al., 2014).
*   Lattice QCD scattering length: $a_0 = -1.4 \pm 0.6\ \text{fm}$ (Sasa et al., 2014).
*   Lattice QCD effective range: $r_0 = -0.1 \pm 0.2\ \text{fm}$ (Sasa et al., 2014).
*   Lattice QCD $KD$ probability: $P_{DK} = 0.72 \pm 0.12$ (Sasa et al., 2014).
*   Input $KD$ probability for synthetic data: $P_{DK} = 0.75$.
*   Fitted $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ mass with 5% error: $2317^{+14}_{-24}\ \text{MeV}$.
*   Fitted $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ mass with 10% error: $2317^{+21}_{-73}\ \text{MeV}$.
*   Fitted $KD$ probability $P_{DK}$ with 5% error: $0.75^{+0.07}_{-0.06}$.
*   Fitted $KD$ probability $P_{DK}$ with 10% error: $0.75^{+0.16}_{-0.11}$.
*   Fitted scattering length $a_0$ (central value): $-1.0\ \text{fm}$.
*   Fitted effective range $r_0$ (central value): $-0.14\ \text{fm}$.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of the $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ by Sasa et al. (2014) and Liu et al. (2012), which found a dominant $KD$ molecular component (about 70% probability) using the Weinberg compositeness condition. The authors propose a specific experimental reaction, $\bar{B}_s^0\to D_s^-(DK)^+$, to test this molecular interpretation, building on similar approaches used for the $f_0(980)$ and $f_0(500)$ resonances in $B$ decays by Liang et al. (2014). The work enables a direct experimental determination of the $KD$ scattering length, effective range, and the $KD$ probability in the $D_{s0}^{\ast+}(2317)$ wave function, which is a key goal in hadron spectroscopy.
