# Phi-meson photoproduction from nuclei

> We study coherent and incoherent phi-meson photoproduction from nuclei. The available data are analyzed in terms of single and coupled channel photoproduction. It is found that the data on coherent photoproduction can be well reproduced within a single channel optical model and show only little room for omega-phi mixing. These data indicate a normal distortion of the phi-meson in nuclei, which is compatible with the results obtained through the vector meson dominance model. The data on incoherent phi-meson photoproduction show an anomalous A-dependence resulting in a very strong phi-meson distortion. These data can be explained by a coupled channel effect through the dominant contribution from the omega -> phi or pi -> phi transition or, more speculative, through the excitation of a cryptoexotic B_phi-baryon.

## Key claim

The data on coherent phi-meson photoproduction can be well reproduced within a single channel optical model and show only little room for omega-phi mixing, while the data on incoherent phi-meson photoproduction show an anomalous A-dependence that can be explained by a coupled channel effect through the dominant contribution from the omega-to-phi or pi-to-phi transition, or more speculatively through the excitation of a cryptoexotic B_phi-baryon.

## Method

The authors analyzed coherent phi-meson photoproduction data from Cornell (at photon energies 6.4 and 8.3 GeV) using a single channel optical model within the t-rho approximation, and analyzed incoherent phi-meson photoproduction data from SPRING-8 (at photon energies 1.5 to 2.4 GeV) using both single channel and coupled channel approaches. The coupled channel analysis included omega-phi mixing and pion-mediated transitions, with the elementary gamma-N-to-phi-N and gamma-N-to-omega-N amplitudes evaluated within the Vector Meson Dominance Model.

## Key numbers

- The slope alpha of the A^alpha dependence for coherent forward phi-meson photoproduction was 1.37 +/- 0.08 at photon energy 6.4 GeV and 1.53 +/- 0.05 at 8.3 GeV, from Cornell data
- The total phi-N cross section extracted from coherent photoproduction was about 10 mb, with the elementary gamma-N-to-phi-N forward differential cross section around 2.2 to 2.6 microbarns/GeV^2
- The slope alpha of the A^alpha dependence for incoherent phi-meson photoproduction from SPRING-8 was 0.72 +/- 0.07 at photon energies 1.5 to 2.4 GeV
- The total phi-N cross section extracted from incoherent photoproduction was 35 (+17, -11) mb, favoring a range of 23 to 63 mb
- The photon couplings to vector mesons were gamma_rho : gamma_omega : gamma_phi = 2.48 : 8.53 : 6.69

## Context

This work extends the analysis of phi-meson photoproduction from nuclei, building on earlier studies of vector meson production at Cornell (McClellan, 1971) and SPRING-8 (Ishikawa, 2005), and on the theoretical framework of the Vector Meson Dominance Model as reviewed by Bauer (1978). The results contradict the expectation from single channel optical models that the phi-N cross section extracted from coherent and incoherent production should be consistent, and instead suggest that coupled channel effects involving omega-phi mixing or pion exchange are necessary to explain the anomalous A-dependence observed in incoherent production. This enables future experimental and theoretical work to investigate the role of omega-phi mixing in nuclear matter and the possible excitation of cryptoexotic baryons with hidden strangeness.
