# Particle-Hole Mirror Symmetries around the Half-Filled Shell: The Quantum Numbers and Algebraic Structure of Composite Fermions

> Composite fermions (CFs) of the fractional quantum Hall effect are described as spherical products of electron and vortex spinors, built from underlying L=1/2 ladder operators aligned so that the spinor angular momenta Le and Lv are maximal. We identify the CF's quantum numbers as the angular momentum L in (L_e L_v)L, its magnetic projection m_L, the electron number N, with L_v={N-1)/2, and magnetic ν-spin, m_ν=L_e-L_v. Translationally invariant FQHE states are formed by filling p subshells with their respective CFs, in order of ascending L for fixed L_e and L_v, beginning with the lowest allowed value, L=|m_ν|. We show that this wave function has an exactly equivalent hierarchical form. FQHE states can be grouped into ν-spin multiplets mirror symmetric around m_ν=0, with N held constant. Electron particle-hole conjugation with respect to this vacuum is identified as the mirror symmetry relating FQHE states of the same N but distinct fillings ν= p/(2p+1} and p/( 2p-1). Alternatively, mirror symmetric ν-spin multiplets can be constructed in which the magnetic field strength is held fixed: the valence states are electron particle-vortex hole excitations. Particle-hole symmetry -- relating the N-particle FQHE state of filling ν=p/(2p+1} to the $\bar{N}$-particle state of filling {p+1)/(2p+1} -- is shown to be equivalent to electron-vortex exchange. In this construction $\bar{N}$-N CFs of the higher density state occupy an extra zero-mode subshell. We link this structure, familiar from supersymmetric quantum mechanics, to the CF Pauli Hamiltonian, which we show is isospectral, quadratic in the ν-spin raising and lowering operators, and four-fold degenerate. On linearization, it takes a Dirac form similar to that found in the integer quantum Hall effect (IQHE).

## Key claim

The paper claims that composite fermions (CFs) in the fractional quantum Hall effect carry a second magnetic quantum number called nu-spin (m_nu = L_e - L_v), which organizes FQHE states into multiplets exhibiting mirror symmetry around the half-filled shell (m_nu = 0), with the caveat that this symmetry holds when the electron number N is held constant for one class of mirror symmetries, or when the magnetic field strength is held fixed for another.

## Method

The authors construct CFs as spherical products of electron and vortex spinors built from L=1/2 ladder operators, where the electron spinor has angular momentum L_e and the vortex spinor has L_v = (N-1)/2. Translationally invariant FQHE states are formed by filling p subshells with CFs in order of ascending L for fixed L_e and L_v, beginning with the lowest allowed value L = |m_nu|. The authors identify the algebraic structure of CFs by writing them as valence operators acting on a scalar half-filled intrinsic state, and show that particle-hole symmetry is equivalent to electron-vortex exchange (b-dagger <-> v-dagger and b-tilde <-> v-tilde). The CF Pauli Hamiltonian is shown to be isospectral, quadratic in the nu-spin raising and lowering operators, and four-fold degenerate.

## Key numbers

- FQHE states have fillings nu = p/(2p+1) or nu = p/(2p-1) for p = 1, 2, 3, ...
- The vortex spinor angular momentum is L_v = (N-1)/2, where N is the electron number
- The CF angular momentum L takes values L = |m_nu|, |m_nu|+1, |m_nu|+2, ... with the lowest allowed value L = |m_nu|
- The CF subshells are indexed by I, 1 <= I <= p, with L(I) = |m_nu| + I - 1
- The Pauli Hamiltonian is H_0^eff = hbar omega_Coul [epsilon_1 + (3/2)(epsilon_{1/3} - epsilon_1)(1/S) S^nu_+ S^nu_-], where epsilon_1 = -sqrt(pi/8) and epsilon_{1/3} are average single-electron energies at nu=1 and nu=1/3 respectively
- The zero mode mass is hbar omega_Coul epsilon_1

## Context

This work extends the Ginocchio and Haxton (GH) construction (2012) of analytic FLL wave functions for FQHE states, which itself extended Laughlin's (1983) variational argument for the nu=1/3 state. The GH^2 construction (2017) provided a single-determinant CF form of FQHE wave functions by applying GH scalar operators to the half-filled shell. The present paper identifies the nu-spin quantum number and mirror symmetries, linking the algebraic structure to the SU(2) algebra of the IQHE described by Haldane (1983) and Greiter (2011), and showing that the CF Pauli Hamiltonian takes a Dirac form similar to that found in the integer quantum Hall effect. This enables a unified algebraic description of FQHE states across different fillings and provides a connection to supersymmetric quantum mechanics through the zero-mode subshell structure.
