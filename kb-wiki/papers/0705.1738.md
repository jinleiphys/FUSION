# Equation of state of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter in relativistic mean-field models with chiral limits

> Using in-medium hadron properties according to the Brown-Rho scaling due to the chiral symmetry restoration at high densities and considering naturalness of the coupling constants, we have newly constructed several relativistic mean-field Lagrangians with chiral limits. The model parameters are adjusted such that the symmetric part of the resulting equation of state at supra-normal densities is consistent with that required by the collective flow data from high energy heavy-ion reactions, while the resulting density dependence of the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities agrees with that extracted from the recent isospin diffusion data from intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions. The resulting equations of state have the special feature of being soft at intermediate densities but stiff at high densities naturally. With these constrained equations of state, it is found that the radius of a 1.4$M_\odot$ canonical neutron star is in the range of 11.9 km$\leq$R$\leq$13.1 km, and the maximum neutron star mass is around 2.0$M_\odot$ close to the recent observations.

## Key claim
The paper constructs relativistic mean-field models with chiral limits that produce equations of state which are soft at intermediate densities but stiff at high densities, and finds that the radius of a 1.4 solar mass canonical neutron star lies between 11.9 km and 13.1 km, with a maximum neutron star mass around 2.0 solar masses. The hedge is that these results depend on the specific choice of scaling functions for hadron masses and coupling constants, and the symmetry energy at high densities is not directly constrained by data but is instead predicted based on the chiral symmetry restoration assumption.

## Method
The authors constructed several relativistic mean-field Lagrangians incorporating in-medium hadron properties via the Brown-Rho scaling, using scaling functions for hadron masses and meson coupling constants with coefficients y and x. Model parameters were adjusted to reproduce saturation properties of nuclear matter (binding energy -16 MeV, zero pressure, incompressibility around 230 MeV) and to simultaneously satisfy constraints from collective flow data at densities 2-4.6 times saturation density and isospin diffusion data at sub-saturation densities. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16 MeV
- Incompressibility for parameter sets SL1 and SL1*: 230 MeV; for SL2: 219.5 MeV; for SL3: 250 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.6 MeV for all models
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass canonical neutron star: 11.9 km ≤ R ≤ 13.1 km
- Maximum neutron star mass for SL1: 2.04 solar masses with radius 9.89 km
- Maximum neutron star mass for SL2: 2.17 solar masses with radius 10.13 km
- Maximum neutron star mass for SL1*: 1.7 solar masses
- Critical density for chiral symmetry restoration (SL1, SL1*, SL3): 7.94 times saturation density; for SL2: 9.09 times saturation density
- Scaling parameter y for vector meson mass: 0.126 for SL1, SL1*, SL3; 0.11 for SL2
- Scaling parameter x for scalar meson coupling: 0.234 for SL1; 0.238 for SL1*; 0.381 for SL2

## Context
This work extends earlier relativistic mean-field models with Brown-Rho scaling by Song (2001) and Liu et al. (2004), which failed to reproduce the pressure constrained by collective flow data from heavy-ion collisions. The authors improve upon these by using different scaling functions for scalar and vector meson coupling constants, allowing the pressure to match experimental constraints. The resulting equations of state are compared to the FSUGold parameter set and the MDI interaction models, and enable predictions for neutron star properties that can be tested against observations of pulsars such as PSR J0751+1807 and EXO 07482-676.
