# Analytical attractor and the divergence of the slow-roll expansion in relativistic hydrodynamics

> We find the general analytical solution of the viscous relativistic hydrodynamic equations (in the absence of bulk viscosity and chemical potential) for a Bjorken expanding fluid with a constant shear viscosity relaxation time. We analytically determine the hydrodynamic attractor of this fluid and discuss its properties. We show for the first time that the slow-roll expansion, a commonly used approach to characterize the attractor, diverges. This is shown to hold also in a conformal plasma. The gradient expansion is found to converge in an example where causality and stability are violated.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first analytical solution for viscous relativistic hydrodynamics in Bjorken flow with a constant shear relaxation time, and demonstrates that the slow-roll expansion used to characterize the hydrodynamic attractor diverges, contrary to the apparent convergence suggested by low-order truncations in prior numerical studies.

## Method

The authors solved the Israel-Stewart-type hydrodynamic equations for a Bjorken-expanding fluid with an ideal gas equation of state (ε = 3P), zero bulk viscosity, zero chemical potential, and constant shear relaxation time τ_R. They reduced the coupled differential equations to a Riccati equation for the normalized shear stress χ = π/(ε+P), which was transformed into a linear second-order ODE and solved analytically in terms of Whittaker functions (or Bessel functions for the λ=0 case). The attractor solution was identified as the α=0 branch of the general solution, and the large-order behavior of both the gradient expansion and the slow-roll expansion was analyzed numerically up to approximately 100 terms.

## Key numbers

* For the RTA case with a = 16/45 and λ = 0, the attractor at early times (τ̂ → 0) approaches χ = 1/√5 ≈ 0.447.
* The gradient series coefficients satisfy c₀ = 0, c₁ = 1, and c_{n+1} = n c_n − a Σ_{m=0}^{n} c_{n−m} c_m; for a ≤ 8/9 (causal regime) the series diverges with factorial growth.
* For a = 1 (acausal regime), all coefficients c_{n≥1} = 1, giving a convergent series χ = (3/4) Σ_{n=1}^{∞} 1/τ̂ⁿ with radius of convergence |τ̂| > 1.
* The slow-roll expansion was computed up to n ≈ 100; the quantity J[n] = |χ_n|^{1/n} shows no sign of convergence for τ̂ = 0.5, 1, 2, and 5 with a = 16/45.
* The optimal truncation order for the gradient series is n = 2, and for the slow-roll expansion is n = 3, based on the relative integrated difference R[n] over τ̂ ∈ [1, 50].

## Context

This work extends the numerical studies of hydrodynamic attractors in Bjorken flow by Heller et al. (2015) and the gradient expansion analysis by Heller et al. (2013) and Denicol et al. (2016), providing the first closed-form analytical attractor for a viscous relativistic fluid. The analytical solution enables direct comparison of the slow-roll expansion (adapted from the cosmological slow-roll formalism of Liddle et al. (1994)) with the exact attractor, revealing its divergence in both the constant-τ_R model and in conformal hydrodynamics. The results enable future studies of resurgence theory in hydrodynamics and provide a benchmark for testing approximation schemes in far-from-equilibrium relativistic fluids.
