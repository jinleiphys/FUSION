# Jet-like Correlations with Direct-Photon and Neutral-Pion Triggers at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}} = 200$ GeV

> Azimuthal correlations of charged hadrons with direct-photon ($γ_{dir}$) and neutral-pion ($π^{0}$) trigger particles are analyzed in central Au+Au and minimum-bias $p+p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}} = 200$ GeV in the STAR experiment. The charged-hadron per-trigger yields at mid-rapidity from central Au+Au collisions are compared with $p+p$ collisions to quantify the suppression in Au+Au collisions. The suppression of the away-side associated-particle yields per $γ_{dir}$ trigger is independent of the transverse momentum of the trigger particle ($p_{T}^{\mathrm{trig}}$), whereas the suppression is smaller at low transverse momentum of the associated charged hadrons ($p_{T}^{\mathrm{assoc}}$). Within uncertainty, similar levels of suppression are observed for $γ_{dir}$ and $π^{0}$ triggers as a function of $z_{T}$ ($\equiv p_T^{\mathrm{assoc}}/p_T^{\mathrm{trig}}$). The results are compared with energy-loss-inspired theoretical model predictions. Our studies support previous conclusions that the lost energy reappears predominantly at low transverse momentum, regardless of the trigger energy.

## Key claim

The suppression of away-side associated-particle yields per direct-photon trigger in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is independent of the trigger particle transverse momentum, but is smaller at low associated charged-hadron transverse momentum; within uncertainties, similar levels of suppression are observed for direct-photon and neutral-pion triggers as a function of the ratio zT, supporting the conclusion that lost energy reappears predominantly at low transverse momentum regardless of trigger energy.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured azimuthal correlations of charged hadrons with direct-photon and neutral-pion trigger particles in central Au+Au and minimum-bias p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. Per-trigger yields of charged hadrons at mid-rapidity were extracted from the azimuthal correlation functions and compared between Au+Au and p+p collisions to quantify the suppression via the ratio I_AA of integrated yields. The suppression was studied as a function of trigger transverse momentum (p_T^trig), associated hadron transverse momentum (p_T^assoc), and the ratio zT = p_T^assoc / p_T^trig.

## Key numbers

- The suppression of away-side associated-particle yields per direct-photon trigger is independent of p_T^trig over the measured range at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The suppression is smaller at low p_T^assoc (below approximately 2 GeV/c) compared to higher p_T^assoc values.
- Similar levels of suppression are observed for direct-photon and neutral-pion triggers as a function of zT within uncertainties at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The per-trigger yields from central Au+Au collisions are compared with p+p collisions at the same center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous jet quenching studies at RHIC that established suppression of high-pT hadrons (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, BRAHMS collaborations) and earlier direct-photon triggered correlation measurements (PHENIX, STAR). The use of direct-photon triggers, which are produced in early hard scattering processes such as quark-gluon Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation, provides a cleaner probe of parton energy loss because the photon energy approximates the initial recoil parton transverse momentum. The results enable quantitative tests of energy-loss-inspired theoretical models and support the interpretation that medium-induced energy loss redistributes energy to low-momentum hadrons.
