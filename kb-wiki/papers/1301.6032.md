# On the origin and movement of the poles in the coupled channels model for $\bar{K}N$ interactions

> The origin of the poles generated by chiral coupled-channel dynamics and applied to $\bar{K}N$ interactions is related to the nonzero diagonal couplings in the $πΣ$, $\bar{K}N$ and $KΞ$ channels. An evolution of the poles from the zero coupling limit and from a limit of restored SU(3) symmetry is discussed.

## Key claim

The poles generated by chiral coupled-channel dynamics for $\bar{K}N$ interactions originate specifically from the nonzero diagonal couplings in the $\pi\Sigma$, $\bar{K}N$, and $K\Xi$ channels; the paper demonstrates that only these three channels can produce poles in the zero coupling limit, and that at least three isoscalar and three isovector poles arise from these diagonal interactions.

## Method

The authors employed a chirally motivated separable model (TW1) of meson-baryon interactions, fitted to $K^{-}p$ reaction and scattering data including SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurements, with a coupled channels space comprising $\pi\Lambda$, $\pi\Sigma$, $\bar{K}N$, $\eta\Lambda$, $\eta\Sigma$, and $K\Xi$ states. They tracked pole movements on the complex energy manifold by scaling the non-diagonal inter-channel couplings from zero (zero coupling limit, ZCL) to unity (physical limit), and also studied pole evolution from a limit of restored SU(3) symmetry (LRSU3) by scaling meson and baryon masses to channel-independent values.

## Key numbers

- In the zero coupling limit for $I=0$: $\pi\Sigma$ pole at (1366, -91) MeV on a resonance sheet; $\bar{K}N$ pole at (1434, 0) MeV on a bound sheet; $K\Xi$ pole at (1809, 0) MeV on a bound sheet.
- In the zero coupling limit for $I=1$: $\pi\Sigma$ pole at (1387, -220) MeV on a resonance sheet; $\bar{K}N$ pole at (1158, -18) MeV on a resonance sheet; $K\Xi$ pole at (1658, 0) MeV on a virtual sheet.
- In the SU(3) restoration limit, the singlet state (eigenvalue 6) gives poles at Re $z$ = 1456 MeV (bound) and 1188 MeV (virtual); the octet state (eigenvalue 3) gives poles at Re $z$ = 1518 MeV (virtual) and 1235 MeV (virtual); the 27-plet state (eigenvalue -2) gives poles at Re $z$ = 1056 MeV (virtual) and 784 MeV (bound).
- The SU(3) restoration limit uses channel-independent meson mass $m_0 = 370$ MeV and baryon mass $M_0 = 1150$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jido et al. (2003) and Hyodo and Weise (2008) on dynamically generated resonances in the $\bar{K}N$ system, specifically the two poles assigned to the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance. The authors clarify that the $\Lambda(1405)$ corresponds to the two isoscalar poles evolving from the $\pi\Sigma$ resonance and $\bar{K}N$ bound state in the ZCL, while the third isoscalar pole relates to the $\Lambda(1670)$ resonance. The methodology enables tracking all poles including those at lower energies that were missing in earlier work by Jido et al. (2003), and the results show a richer pole content from the SU(3) restoration limit than previously anticipated.
