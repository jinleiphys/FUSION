# Initial energy density of p+p collisions at the LHC

> Accelerating, exact, explicit and simple solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics allow for a simple description of highly relativistic p+p collisions. These solutions yield a finite rapidity distribution, thus they lead to an advanced estimate of the initial energy density of high energy collisions. We show that such an advanced estimate yields an initial energy density in $\sqrt{s}=7$ and 8 TeV p+p collisions at LHC around or above the critical energy density from lattice QCD, and a corresponding initial temperature above the critical temperature from QCD and the Hagedorn temperature. We also show, that several times the critical energy density may have been reached in high multiplicity events, hinting at a non-hadronic medium created in high multiplicity $\sqrt{s}=7$ and 8 TeV p+p collisions.

# Initial energy density of p+p collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
Using an accelerating hydrodynamic solution with finite rapidity distributions, the initial energy density in average 7 and 8 TeV p+p collisions at LHC is estimated to be 0.64 GeV/fm³, which is below the critical energy density of approximately 1 GeV/fm³ from lattice QCD; however, in high-multiplicity events the energy density may reach several times the critical value, satisfying a necessary condition for non-hadronic medium formation.

## Method
The authors apply an exact, accelerating solution of relativistic hydrodynamics (with acceleration parameter λ) to fit TOTEM pseudorapidity distributions dN/dη in 7 and 8 TeV p+p collisions. They then modify Bjorken's original energy density formula (Bjorken 1983) by including correction factors that account for acceleration effects and the equation of state (speed of sound c_s), using the fitted λ and estimated freeze-out to thermalization time ratio τ_f/τ_0.

## Key numbers
- Bjorken estimate for average 7 TeV p+p: ε_Bj(7 TeV) = 0.507 GeV/fm³
- Corrected estimate for average 7 TeV p+p: ε_corr(7 TeV) = 0.640 GeV/fm³, with systematic uncertainty +0.14 / -0.10 GeV/fm³
- Corrected estimate for average 8 TeV p+p: ε_corr(8 TeV) = 0.644 GeV/fm³
- Critical energy density from lattice QCD: ε_crit ≈ 1 GeV/fm³ (based on T_crit = 170 MeV)
- Acceleration parameter from TOTEM fits: λ = 1.073 ± 0.001 (stat) ± 0.003 (syst) at 7 TeV; λ = 1.067 ± 0.001 at 8 TeV
- Average transverse momentum at 7 TeV: ⟨p_t⟩ = 0.545 ± 0.005 (stat) ± 0.015 (syst) GeV/c, corresponding to ⟨E⟩ = 0.562 GeV/c²
- Transverse radius from TOTEM cross-sections: R = 1.76 ± 0.02 fm (from σ_tot = 98.0 ± 2.5 mb, σ_el = 25.1 ± 1.1 mb)
- Charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity: 6.01 ± 0.01 (stat) +0.20/-0.12 (syst) (ALICE) and 5.78 ± 0.01 (stat) ± 0.23 (syst) (CMS); average used: 5.895
- In high-multiplicity events, dN/dη can reach 25-30, yielding supercritical energy densities
- Initial temperature of 300 MeV can be reached at multiplicity 45; supercritical 200 MeV reached at multiplicity 10

## Context
This work extends Bjorken's original energy density estimate (Bjorken 1983) by incorporating acceleration effects from exact hydrodynamic solutions developed by Csorgo, Csanad, and Nagy (2006-2008). It builds on earlier applications of hydrodynamics to p+p collisions by Landau (1953) and the E735 experiment at Tevatron (Alexopoulos 2002), which found ε ≈ 1.1 ± 0.2 GeV/fm³ at 1.8 TeV. The results enable experimental searches for non-hadronic medium signatures in high-multiplicity p+p events, including radial flow, elliptic flow, HBT radius scaling, direct photon enhancement, and low-mass dilepton enhancement, and motivate comparisons with e+p and e+A collisions to distinguish hydrodynamic from non-hydrodynamic origins of collective effects such as the ridge.
