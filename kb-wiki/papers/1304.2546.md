# Non-Fermi liquid correction to the neutrino mean free path and emissivity in neutron star beyond the leading order

> In this work we have derived the expressions of the mean free path (MFP) and emissivity of the neutrinos by incorporating non-Fermi liquid (NFL) corrections upto next to leading order (NLO). We have shown how such corrections affect the cooling of the neutron star composed of quark matter core.

# Non-Fermi liquid correction to the neutrino mean free path and emissivity in neutron star beyond the leading order

## Key claim
The non-Fermi liquid (NFL) corrections to the neutrino mean free path and emissivity in a neutron star with a quark matter core, computed up to next-to-leading order (NLO), introduce fractional powers of (T/μ) in the expressions and produce a modest but significant decrease in the mean free path and increase in emissivity compared to leading-order (LO) NFL and Fermi liquid (FL) results, though the overall alteration in the cooling behavior is marginal.

## Method
The authors derived analytic expressions for the neutrino mean free path (MFP) and emissivity by incorporating the modified dispersion relation of quarks due to non-Fermi liquid effects, using the real part of the quark self-energy computed to NLO in the hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation scheme. They considered both degenerate and non-degenerate neutrinos, treating absorption and scattering processes separately via the beta decay reactions d + ν_e → u + e^- and its inverse, and integrated over phase space with the NFL-modified density of states. The results were then applied to numerically study the cooling curve of a neutron star with a quark matter core.

## Key numbers
- Quark chemical potential μ = 500 MeV, electron chemical potential μ_e = 15 MeV, and α_s = 0.1 are assumed for numerical results.
- For non-degenerate neutrino absorption at NLO: 1/l_mean^(abs,ND)|_NLO contains terms with coefficients h_1 = 0.03, h_2 = -0.149, h_3 = -0.073 multiplying powers of (T/μ)^(2/3), (T/μ)^(4/3), and (T/μ)^2 respectively.
- For non-degenerate neutrino scattering at NLO: coefficients l_1 = 0.002, l_2 = -0.009, l_3 = -0.005 appear in the expression.
- For degenerate neutrino absorption at NLO: coefficients r_1 = 0.015, r_2 = -0.075, r_3 = -0.036 appear in the expression.
- For degenerate neutrino scattering at NLO: the same coefficients r_1, r_2, r_3 appear.
- The FL emissivity result is ε_0 ≈ (457/630) G_F^2 cos^2 θ_c α_s μ_e T^6 μ^2.
- The NLO emissivity contains terms with coefficients n_1 = -0.035, n_2 = 0.015, n_3 = 0.075, n_4 = -0.109.

## Context
This work extends the Fermi liquid treatment of neutrino emission from quark matter cores in neutron stars, originally formulated by Iwamoto (1982), by incorporating non-Fermi liquid corrections beyond the leading logarithmic approximation previously studied by Schafer and Schwenzer (2004) and Pal et al. (2011). The appearance of fractional powers of (T/μ) in the MFP and emissivity is a qualitative signature of NFL behavior, and the results enable more accurate modeling of neutron star cooling, showing that while NFL corrections are significant for the MFP and emissivity individually, their net effect on the cooling curve is modest compared to the difference between quark matter and purely neutron matter cores.
