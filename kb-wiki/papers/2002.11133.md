# Finite-Temperature Quarkyonic Matter with an Excluded Volume Model

> We introduce a theoretical framework to analyze the thermal properties of a recently proposed "excluded volume" model of quarkyonic matter. This entails proposing finite temperature distribution functions and entropy functionals constrained by internal theoretical consistency. We use the formalism developed here to analyze the effect of temperature on the quark onset as a function of baryon density. We find that the baryon density at which the quarkyonic phase emerges decreases only slightly with temperature, an effect produced by large $N_c$. We furthermore find that a finite temperature increases the density of quarks produced. The framework presented here can be used to compute the equation of state of hot and dense quarkyonic matter which is of relevance to neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a finite-temperature extension of the excluded volume model of quarkyonic matter and finds that the baryon density at which the quarkyonic phase emerges decreases only slightly with temperature (approximately 2% over the range 0–50 MeV), an effect produced by large \(N_c\) factors that protect the inner quark Fermi sphere from thermal smearing.

## Method

The authors extend the zero-temperature excluded volume model of Jeong (2019) by proposing finite-temperature distribution functions for quarks and nucleons that impose a momentum-space shell structure (inner quark Fermi sphere surrounded by a nucleon shell) with a common Fermi-Dirac envelope at the outermost Fermi surface. They define entropy functionals for quarks and nucleons based on free-fermion entropy with the shell structure imposed, then minimize the Helmholtz free energy density at fixed total baryon density to determine equilibrium configurations. The analysis employs non-relativistic dispersion relations for both quarks and nucleons, with quark mass set to \(m = M/N_c\), and uses a low-temperature expansion to build analytic intuition before performing exact numerical calculations.

## Key numbers

* The quark onset density is well approximated by \(n_{\text{onset}} = 0.863 n_0\) (where \(n_0 = 2.5 n_{\text{sat}}\) is the hard-core density and \(n_{\text{sat}} = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density), correct to 5% for temperatures between 0 MeV and 50 MeV.
* At \(T = 50\) MeV and \(n = 2.5 n_{\text{sat}}\), the relative free energy shift for an all-nucleon configuration is approximately 3%, consistent with the observed \(\sim 2\%\) shift in onset density.
* The ratio of free energy changes between all-quark and all-nucleon configurations at finite temperature is \((1 - n_{\text{tot}}/n_0)^{-2/3}\), showing quarks are favored at higher temperature.
* The onset density changes by only \(\sim 2\%\) over the temperature range 0–50 MeV examined for neutron star merger conditions.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature excluded volume model of quarkyonic matter developed by McLerran (2018) and Jeong (2019), which reproduced the pronounced peak in the speed of sound expected from neutron star observations. The finite-temperature framework enables computation of the equation of state for hot and dense quarkyonic matter relevant to neutron star mergers, where temperatures reach approximately 50 MeV (Perego, 2019). The model deliberately excludes detailed nuclear interactions beyond hard-core repulsion and cannot capture chiral symmetry restoration or di-quark condensation predicted in Ginzburg-Landau calculations of high-density QCD (Hatsuda, 2006).
