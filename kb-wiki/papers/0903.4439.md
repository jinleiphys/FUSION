# The chiral structure of the neutron as revealed in electron and photon scattering

> It is a consequence of QCD's spontaneously broken chiral symmetry that the response of the neutron to long wavelength probes will be driven by excitation of its pion cloud. In this article I discuss manifestations of this in electromagnetic form factors and Compton scattering. Particular attention is given to the interplay between this physics and shorter-distance components of the neutron's electromagnetic structure.

## Key claim

The neutron's response to long-wavelength electromagnetic probes is dominated by its pion cloud due to QCD's spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, but this pion-cloud physics must be disentangled from shorter-distance components (such as the Delta(1232) resonance) that enter through low-energy constants in chiral perturbation theory; the interplay between these two regimes can be mapped out by varying photon energy or momentum transfer.

## Method

The paper uses heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to compute one-loop diagrams for neutron electromagnetic form factors and Compton scattering, with vertices from the leading-order πN and ππ Lagrangians and insertions from the second-order πN Lagrangian. For multi-nucleon systems, the chiral effective theory (χET) framework is employed, where the NN potential and external-current operators are computed to a fixed chiral order and then used in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a cutoff below about 800 MeV. The divergent loop integrals are renormalized using counterterms from higher-order Lagrangians, whose finite parts encode the effects of hadronic states with excitation energies above the pion mass.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion parameter is \(P \equiv (p, m_\pi)/\Lambda_\chi\) with \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 4\pi f_\pi \approx 1200\) MeV (where \(f_\pi \approx 93\) MeV is the pion-decay constant).
- The Delta-nucleon mass difference \(M_\Delta - M_N \approx 290\) MeV sets the breakdown scale for χPT without explicit Delta degrees of freedom.
- One-loop graphs with vertices from \({\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(1)}\) contribute at chiral order \(D=3\); graphs with one insertion from \({\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}\) contribute at \(D=4\); two-loop graphs first contribute at \(D=5\).
- The isovector electric form factor \(G_E^v\) at \(O(P^3)\) is given by an expression containing the low-energy constant \(B_{10}^{(r)}(\mu)\) that renormalizes the divergent loop integrals.
- In NN scattering, the χET potential computed to \(O(P^3)\) describes partial waves with \(l \geq 2\) successfully, with cutoffs varied between 500 and 800 MeV yielding stable predictions.

## Context

This work extends the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1992, 1998) for nucleon form factors, and the chiral effective theory approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and developed by Ordóñez, Ray, and van Kolck (1996) and Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (1999, 2003, 2005). It enables systematic, quantifiable predictions for electron and photon scattering on few-nucleon systems, where neutron structure must be extracted from nuclear targets using the same χPT framework for both the single-nucleon response and the multi-nucleon interactions.
