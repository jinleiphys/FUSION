# Suppression of Hadroproduction in Nuclei

> We argue that nuclei are not transparent for fast projectile partons. Color transparency is effective for final state interactions in heavy particle production, though nuclear filtering of initial partons can reduce the cross sections. We show that short-ranged initial state interactions, which have been neglected so far, can be important in hadroproduction on nuclei. With the present scenario of hadroproduction a qualitative description of data can be obtained.

## Key claim

The paper argues that short-ranged initial state interactions of projectile partons, which have been neglected in standard color transparency models, can suppress hadroproduction cross sections in nuclei; the author claims that color transparency is effective for final state interactions but not for initial state interactions, and that a macroscopic factor C describing the probability to escape hard initial state interactions can qualitatively describe the data.

## Method

The author derives a formalism showing that hard initial state interactions of projectile partons with target nucleons, where the exchanged momentum l satisfies l ≫ L⁻¹ (L being target length), lead to a suppression factor C that multiplies the hadroproduction amplitude. This factor is then modeled phenomenologically using a parton-nucleon absorption cross section σ_abs in an eikonal form, and applied to Drell-Yan production by pions, J/ψ production, and Υ production on nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- σ_abs^q = 2 mb gives a reasonable fit for Drell-Yan data at E_π = 140 GeV; σ_abs^q ≈ 1.5 mb would better fit the combined data
- σ_abs^g(J/ψ) ≈ 4 mb and σ_abs^g(Υ) ≈ 2 mb provide the best fit for quarkonium production data
- σ_abs = 1.5–4 mb provides an overall description of hadroproduction data
- The factorization condition for validity is Q² ≫ 0.25 A^(2/3) GeV², which is fulfilled for Υ-production and partially for Drell-Yan reactions but not for J/ψ-production
- The Sudakov formfactor at Q² = 10 GeV² is |S(Q²)|² ∼ 1/3 for J/ψ-production

## Context

This work extends the standard color transparency model of Gun (1980s) and challenges the conclusions of Mueller (1990) and Bodwin (1991) that initial state interactions cancel in hadroproduction on nuclei at large Q². The author argues that the factorization theorems of Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1985) and Bodwin (1991) for nuclear targets do not exclude the suppression factor C, which changes the normalization of the amplitude. The paper enables a reinterpretation of nuclear suppression in J/ψ and Υ production, suggesting that claims of quark-gluon plasma observation in heavy ion collisions require accounting for this initial state suppression mechanism.
