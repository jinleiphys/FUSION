# The method of unitary clothing transformations in the theory of nucleon-nucleon scattering

> The clothing procedure, put forward in quantum field theory by Greenberg and Schweber, is applied for the description of nucleon-nucleon (N-N) scattering. We consider pseudoscalar, vector and scalar meson fields interacting with 1/2 spin fermion ones via the Yukawa-type couplings to introduce trial interactions between "bare" particles. The subsequent unitary clothing transformations are found to express the total Hamiltonian through new interaction operators that refer to particles with physical (observable) properties, the so-called clothed particles. In this work, we are focused upon the Hermitian and energy-independent operators for the clothed nucleons, being built up in the second order in the coupling constants. The corresponding analytic expressions in momentum space are compared with the separate meson contributions to the one-boson-exchange potentials in the meson theory of nuclear forces. In order to evaluate the T-matrix of the N-N scattering we have used an equivalence theorem that enables us to operate in the clothed particle representation (CPR) instead of the bare particle representation with its huge amount of virtual processes. We have derived the Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation for the CPR elements of the T-matrix for a given collision energy in the two-nucleon sector of the Hilbert space of hadronic states and elaborated a code for its numerical solution in momentum space.

## Key claim

The unitary clothing transformation (UCT) method, applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering with pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchanges, yields Hermitian and energy-independent two-nucleon quasipotentials in second order in the coupling constants that can reproduce one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) phase shifts after fitting cutoff parameters, but the bare UCT results without parameter adjustment show significant deviations from the OBEP reference.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian with Yukawa-type couplings between bare nucleons, antinucleons, and mesons (π, η, ρ, ω, δ, σ). They apply a unitary clothing transformation W = exp[R] to eliminate "bad terms" that prevent bare vacuum and one-particle states from being Hamiltonian eigenstates, obtaining a clothed-particle representation (CPR) where the interaction K_I contains only physical processes. The second-order N-N quasipotentials are derived analytically in momentum space, and the T-matrix for elastic N-N scattering is obtained by solving a Lippmann-Schwinger-type integral equation in the center-of-mass system using the matrix inversion method with relativistic dispersion and form-factor regularization.

## Context

This work extends the clothing procedure of Greenberg and Schweber (1958) to N-N scattering, building on prior UCT developments by Shebeko and Shirokov (2001) and Korda, Canton, and Shebeko (2007). The derived quasipotentials are compared with the one-boson-exchange potentials of the Bonn group (Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster, 1987; Machleidt, 1989), showing that after fitting cutoff parameters the UCT approach can reproduce OBEP phase shifts across a range of laboratory energies (25-300 MeV). The method enables a field-theoretic treatment of N-N scattering that avoids the large number of virtual processes present in the bare particle representation, and the cancellation of non-invariant contact terms from vector meson contributions is identified as a new feature of the CPR approach.
