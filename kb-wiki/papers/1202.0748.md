# Strangeness production in antiproton-nucleus collisions

> Antiproton annihilations on nuclei provide a very interesting way to study the behaviour of strange particles in the nuclear medium. In low energy $\bar p$ annihilations, the hyperons are produced mostly by strangeness exchange mechanisms. Thus, hyperon production in $\bar p A$ interactions is very sensitive to the properties of the antikaon-nucleon interaction in nuclear medium. Within the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model (GiBUU), we analyse the experimental data on $Λ$ and $K^0_S$ production in $\bar p A$ collisions at $p_{\rm lab}=0.2-4$ GeV/c. A satisfactory overall agreement is reached, except for the $K^0_S$ production in $\bar p+^{20}$Ne collisions at $p_{\rm lab}=608$ MeV/c, where we obtain substantially larger $K^0_S$ production rate. We also study the $Ξ$ hyperon production, important in view of the forthcoming experiments at FAIR and J-PARC.

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model achieves satisfactory overall agreement with experimental data on Λ and K⁰_S production in antiproton-nucleus collisions at p_lab = 0.2–4 GeV/c, except for K⁰_S production in p̄+²⁰Ne collisions at p_lab = 608 MeV/c, where the model yields a substantially larger K⁰_S production rate than observed.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled system of relativistic kinetic equations (the GiBUU transport model) for hadron distribution functions in six-dimensional phase space, including mean-field potentials from the NL3 Walecka model and a collision integral that covers antibaryon-baryon annihilation, elastic and inelastic hadron-hadron scattering, and resonance decays. Elementary cross sections are taken from phenomenological fits to experimental data or theoretical calculations, with inelastic production at high invariant energies simulated using FRITIOF (for antibaryon-baryon) and PYTHIA (for baryon-baryon and meson-baryon) models. The calculations are performed for p̄ on various nuclear targets at beam momenta from 0.2 to 4 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Λ/K⁰_S yield ratio for p̄+²⁰Ne at 608 MeV/c: GiBUU gives 1.0, INC gives 1.4, experiment gives 2.3 ± 0.7
- Probability of bound Λ production on lead target at p̄ momentum 225 MeV/c: approximately 3%
- Probability of bound ΛΛ production on lead target at p̄ momentum 225 MeV/c: approximately 0.01%
- Approximately 80% of Y(Y*) production rate in p̄+²⁰Ne at 607 MeV/c is due to strangeness exchange reactions of type K̄N → Yπ
- Approximately 70–80% of Y(Y*) production rate in p̄+¹⁸¹Ta at 4 GeV/c is due to strangeness exchange processes K̄(K̄*)B → YX, K̄B → Y*, and K̄B → Y*π
- Direct N̄N → ΞΞ channel contributes only about 5% of total Ξ yield in p̄+¹⁹⁷Au at 3 GeV/c
- Threshold for double strangeness exchange process K̄N → ΞK: p_lab^thr = 1.05 GeV/c (K̄ beam momentum), y_cm^thr = 0.55 (c.m. rapidity)

## Context

This work extends earlier intranuclear cascade (INC) calculations by Cugnon et al. (1990) on strangeness production in p̄-nucleus interactions, and addresses the exotic QGP fireball scenario proposed by Rafelski (1988) to explain the large Λ/K⁰_S ratio and rapidity spectra. The GiBUU results show that the different peak positions of (Λ+Σ⁰) and Ξ rapidity spectra can be explained by pure hadronic transport without invoking a quark-gluon plasma, providing predictions testable at FAIR and J-PARC. The calculations also enable predictions for Λ- and ΛΛ-hypernuclei production on primary targets at low p̄ beam momenta.
