# Transport coefficients for the hot quark-gluon plasma at finite chemical potential $μ_B$

> We calculate transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) within the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) by explicitly computing the parton interaction rates as a function of temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ on the basis of the DQPM couplings and partonic propagators. The latter are extracted from lattice QCD by matching the equation of state, entropy density and energy density at $μ_B$= 0. For baryon chemical potentials $0 \leq μ_B \leq 500 MeV$ we employ a scaling Ansatz for the effective coupling which was shown before to lead to thermodynamic consistent results in this range. We compute the ratio of the shear and bulk viscosities to the entropy density, i.e. $η/s$ and $ζ/s$, the electric conductivity $σ_0/T$ as well as the baryon diffusion coefficient $κ_B$ and compare to related approaches from the literature. We find that the ratios $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ as well as $σ_0/T$ are in accord with the results from lattice QCD at $μ_B$=0 and only weakly depend on the ratio $T/T_c(μ_B)$ where $T_c(μ_B)$ denotes the critical temperature at finite baryon chemical potential.

# Transport coefficients for the hot quark-gluon plasma at finite chemical potential μ_B

## Key claim

The ratios η/s, ζ/s, and σ₀/T calculated within the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) are in accord with lattice QCD results at μ_B = 0 and depend only weakly on the ratio T/T_c(μ_B), where T_c(μ_B) is the critical temperature at finite baryon chemical potential, for baryon chemical potentials up to 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma within the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) by explicitly calculating parton interaction rates as functions of temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B using DQPM couplings and partonic propagators extracted from lattice QCD. The effective coupling at finite μ_B is obtained via a scaling Ansatz using the effective temperature T* = √(T² + μ_q²/π²) and a μ_B-dependent critical temperature T_c(μ_B). Transport coefficients are evaluated within the relaxation time approximation (RTA) using either the microscopically computed parton interaction rates Γ_i(p,T,μ_B) or the parton spectral widths 2γ_i(T,μ_B) for the relaxation times.

## Key numbers

- The gluon relaxation time is about 0.3-0.4 fm/c in the region 1.5 T_c ≤ T ≤ 3 T_c
- The quark relaxation time is about 1.0-1.5 fm/c in the region 1.5 T_c ≤ T ≤ 3 T_c
- Light quarks and anti-quarks contribute approximately 60% to the total η/s ratio, strange quarks and anti-quarks contribute approximately 30%, and gluons contribute approximately 10%
- The critical temperature at vanishing chemical potential T_c is approximately 0.158 GeV
- The scaling parameter α = 0.974 GeV⁻² for the μ_B-dependent critical temperature T_c(μ_B) = T_c √(1 - α μ_B²)
- The DQPM bulk viscosity peak at T_c has ζ/s ≃ 0.075, close to the Bayesian analysis maximum
- The strange quark mass offset ΔM = 30 MeV has been used

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study in Soloveva et al. (2019) by computing the full set of transport coefficients (shear and bulk viscosities, electric conductivity, and baryon diffusion coefficient) for the QGP at finite baryon chemical potential. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations for pure SU(3) gauge theory from Astrakhantsev et al. (2017, 2018) and Meyer, to predictions from a Bayesian analysis of experimental heavy-ion data from Xu et al. (2017), and to estimates based on the Chapman-Enskog method. The DQPM approach enables the exploration of transport coefficients at finite μ_B where lattice QCD cannot provide direct results due to the fermionic sign problem, and these coefficients are essential input for hydrodynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions at Beam Energy Scan energies at RHIC and future experiments at FAIR and NICA.
