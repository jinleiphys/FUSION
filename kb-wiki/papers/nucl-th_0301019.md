# Isovector nuclear spin-orbit interaction from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

> Using the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory, we calculate the momentum and density dependent isovector nuclear spin-orbit strength $V_{ls}(p,k_f)$. This quantity is derived from the spin-dependent part of the interaction energy $Σ_{spin} = {i\over 2} \vec σ\cdot (\vec q \times\vec p)[U_{ls}(p,k_f)- V_{ls}(p,k_f)τ_3 δ] $ of a nucleon scattering off weakly inhomogeneous isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. We find that iterated $1π$-exchange generates at saturation density, $k_{f0}=272.7 $MeV, an isovector nuclear spin-orbit strength at $p=0$ of $V_{ls}(0,k_{f0}) \simeq 50$ MeVfm$^2$. This value is about 1.4 times the analogous isoscalar nuclear spin-orbit strength $U_{ls}(0,k_{f0})\simeq 35$ MeVfm$^2$ generated by the same two-pion exchange diagrams. We also calculate several relativistic 1/M-corrections to the isoscalar nuclear spin-orbit strength. In particular, we evaluate the contributions from irreducible two-pion exchange to $U_{ls}(p,k_f)$. The effects of the three-body diagrams constructed from the Weinberg-Tomozawa $ππNN$-contact vertex on the isoscalar nuclear spin-orbit strength are computed. We find that such relativistic 1/M-corrections are less than 20% of the isoscalar nuclear spin-orbit strength generated by iterated one-pion-exchange, in accordance with the expectation from chiral power counting.

## Key claim

Iterated one-pion exchange generates an isovector nuclear spin-orbit strength at saturation density and zero nucleon momentum of approximately 50 MeVfm², which is about 1.4 times the isoscalar spin-orbit strength from the same diagrams; this result is obtained within the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory and is neither of relativistic nor short-range origin, but rather scales linearly with the nucleon mass and has a range set by the pion Compton wavelength.

## Method

The author calculates the momentum- and density-dependent isovector nuclear spin-orbit strength \(V_{ls}(p,k_f)\) from the spin-dependent part of the interaction energy of a nucleon scattering off weakly inhomogeneous isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, working to linear order in small spatial gradients and in the isospin asymmetry parameter \(\delta\). The calculation uses the two-loop approximation of in-medium chiral perturbation theory, evaluating iterated one-pion-exchange Hartree and Fock diagrams (labeled (a) through (g)) with medium insertions representing the Fermi spheres of protons and neutrons, and expanding the resulting integrals to linear order in \(\delta\). Additionally, relativistic \(1/M\)-corrections to the isoscalar spin-orbit strength are computed from irreducible two-pion exchange and from three-body diagrams involving the Weinberg-Tomozawa \(\pi\pi NN\)-contact vertex.

## Key numbers

* The isovector nuclear spin-orbit strength at zero nucleon momentum and saturation density (\(k_{f0}=272.7\) MeV) is \(V_{ls}(0,k_{f0}) \simeq 50\) MeVfm², about 1.4 times the isoscalar strength \(U_{ls}(0,k_{f0}) \simeq 35\) MeVfm² from the same two-pion exchange diagrams.
* The contribution of the four Hartree diagrams (a)-(d) to \(V_{ls}(0,k_{f0})\) sums to 55.7 MeVfm², while the four Fock diagrams (e)-(g) contribute negatively, with individual values ranging from \(-42.35\) to \(+41.94\) MeVfm² (see Table 1).
* The relativistic \(1/M\)-corrections to the isoscalar spin-orbit strength from irreducible two-pion exchange at saturation density and \(p=0\) are \(U^{(2\pi)}_{ls}(0,k_{f0}) = 6.93\) MeVfm², less than 20% of the iterated one-pion-exchange contribution.
* The \(1\pi\)-exchange Fock diagram contributes only \(-0.14\) MeVfm² to \(V_{ls}(0,k_{f0})\), a negligible relativistic \(1/M^2\)-correction.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation of the isoscalar nuclear spin-orbit strength from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics (Kaiser, 2002) to the isovector case, revealing the underlying isospin dependence of the spin-orbit interaction generated by two-pion exchange. The results contradict the common assumption that the ratio of isovector to isoscalar spin-orbit strengths equals that of the spin-independent potentials (about \(-0.6\)), finding instead a positive ratio of about \(+1.4\) at saturation density. The calculation enables nuclear structure studies that use these momentum- and density-dependent spin-orbit strengths as input, potentially clarifying the role of pion-exchange dynamics in finite nuclei beyond the simple shell-model parameterization.
