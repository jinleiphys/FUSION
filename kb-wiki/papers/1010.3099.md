# Isospin mixing in nuclei around N=Z and the superallowed beta-decay

> Theoretical approaches that use one-body densities as dynamical variables, such as Hartree-Fock or the density functional theory (DFT), break isospin symmetry both explicitly, by virtue of charge-dependent interactions, and spontaneously. To restore the spontaneously broken isospin symmetry, we implemented the isospin-projection scheme on top of the Skyrme-DFT approach. This development allows for consistent treatment of isospin mixing in both ground and exited nuclear states. In this study, we apply this method to evaluate the isospin impurities in ground states of even-even and odd-odd N~Z nuclei. By including simultaneous isospin and angular-momentum projection, we compute the isospin-breaking corrections to the 0+ --> 0+ superallowed beta-decay.

## Key claim

The authors implement a parameter-free isospin- and angular-momentum-projected density functional theory (DFT) approach to calculate isospin mixing and isospin-breaking corrections to superallowed beta decay, but the results for the correction delta_C are preliminary and rely on the SV Skyrme parametrization, which performs poorly for basic nuclear observables.

## Method

The method uses self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with Skyrme energy density functionals to produce fully symmetry-unrestricted Slater determinants, then applies one-dimensional isospin projection after variation to decompose the determinant into good isospin states. For odd-odd N=Z nuclei, antialigned configurations are constructed by placing the odd neutron and odd proton in time-reversed single-particle orbits, followed by simultaneous isospin and angular-momentum projection and rediagonalization of the total Hamiltonian including the Coulomb term. The calculations are performed using the HFODD solver, and for Skyrme functionals only the SV parametrization is used because it corresponds to a genuine two-body interaction free of singularities in the energy kernels.

## Key numbers

- Isospin impurity in the ground state of 80Zr before Coulomb rediagonalization: approximately 2.9%; after rediagonalization: approximately 4.4%
- Average isospin impurity for the T approximately 1 state in 42Sc (with isospin and I=0 angular-momentum projection, N=10 HO shells): 0.586(2)%; for N=12 shells: 0.620(2)%
- For the superallowed transition 14O to 14N, the isospin-breaking correction delta_C is computed as a function of basis size; a conservative 10% error is assigned to the last two points
- Mean value of the structure-independent statistical-rate function for 12 out of 13 high-precision transitions: bar-Ft = 3069.4(10)
- Resulting CKM matrix element V_ud = 0.97463(24)

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative and hydrodynamical treatments of isospin mixing from the 1960s (Slichter 1965, Bohr 1967) and the review by Auerbach (1983), which provided qualitative features but unreliable quantitative predictions. It also addresses limitations of large-scale shell-model approaches (Ormand 1995) that struggle to account for long-range Coulomb polarization effects. The method enables systematic calculation of isospin-breaking corrections needed for the superallowed beta-decay unitarity test of the CKM matrix, complementing the compilations of Towner and Hardy (2008) and the work of Miller and Schwenk (2008, 2009).
