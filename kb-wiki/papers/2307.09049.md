# Drag force near the QCD critical point

> We discuss how heavy quark dynamics is affected by the critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point at finite temperature and density. We find that the heavy quark momentum diffusion constant scales as $κ\proptoξ^{z-3-η}$. In the model H scenario, which is widely accepted for the critical dynamics, the exponents are known as $z\simeq 3, η\sim 0.04$ and the critical singularity of $κ$ is not significant if any. In the model B scenario, $z\simeq 4$ and $κ\propto ξ$ is singular near the critical point.

## Key claim

The heavy quark momentum diffusion constant scales as $\kappa \propto \xi^{z-3-\eta}$ near the QCD critical point, but under the widely accepted model H scenario (with $z \simeq 3$, $\eta \sim 0.04$) the critical singularity is not significant, whereas under the less likely model B scenario ($z \simeq 4$) the scaling $\kappa \propto \xi$ would be singular and potentially observable.

## Method

The authors construct an effective interaction Lagrangian coupling heavy quarks to the critical mode $\delta\sigma$ (the soft mode of the chiral order parameter) based on O(4) symmetry and its explicit breaking by finite quark mass. They then compute the heavy quark momentum diffusion constant $\kappa$ from the force-force correlator in the static limit, using classical field approximations for the critical fluctuations and scaling analysis of the susceptibility $\chi(q) \sim q^{-2+\eta}$ and relaxation time $\tau(q) \sim q^{-z}$ in the scaling regime.

## Key numbers

- The heavy quark momentum diffusion constant scales as $\kappa \propto \xi^{z-3-\eta}$, where $\xi$ is the correlation length, $z$ is the dynamical critical exponent, and $\eta \sim 0.04$ is the static critical exponent.
- In the model H scenario: $z \simeq 3$, $\eta \sim 0.04$, giving $z-3-\eta \approx -0.04$, so the critical singularity is not significant.
- In the model B scenario: $z \simeq 4$, giving $\kappa \propto \xi$, which is singular near the critical point.
- The typical amplitude of $\delta\sigma$ with wavelength $\xi$ scales as $\sqrt{T/\xi}$, and the energy density $(\nabla \delta\sigma)^2 \sim T/\xi^3$.

## Context

This work extends the study of critical dynamics near the QCD critical point, which belongs to the model H dynamical universality class in the classification by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) and Son (2004), to the behavior of heavy quarks as impurities. The authors propose that the elliptic flow $v_2$ of heavy quarks could show a peak structure at the collision energy where the system passes near the critical point, though they find this effect is unlikely to be observable under model H. The analysis could be extended to the O(4) chiral critical point at zero quark mass, which belongs to model G as studied by Rajagopal (1992) and Florio (2021, 2023).
