# Nuclear matrix elements for exclusive neutrino-nucleus reactions

> The formalism describing (anti)neutrino-nucleus reaction cross-sections in neutral- and charged-current processes is improved. Compact formulae for the single-particle transition matrix elements, based on the multipole expansion treatment of the relevant hadronic currents and the use of harmonic oscillator basis, are presented. As an application, the nucleus $^{127}I$, a promising target for detection of solar- and supernova-neutrinos as well as of cold dark matter candidates, is considered. Our matrix elements refer to exclusive processes of the charge-changing $^{127}I(ν_l,l^-)^{127}Xe^{\star}$ reaction and especially to the transition ${5/2}^+ \to {3/2}^+$ leading from the ground state of $^{127}$I to the lowest excitation of $^{127}$Xe nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper improves the formalism for neutrino-nucleus reaction cross-sections by deriving closed analytic formulas for single-particle reduced matrix elements of the seven basic multipole operators, using a harmonic oscillator basis, with the explicit condition that the initial and final nuclear states have well-defined spin, isospin, and parity.

## Method

The authors perform a multipole expansion of the weak hadronic current (both polar-vector and axial-vector parts) and express the seven basic one-body tensor operators (M, Σ, Σ′, Σ″, Δ, Δ′, Ω) in terms of spherical Bessel functions and vector spherical harmonics. They then derive compact polynomial expressions for the reduced matrix elements of these operators between harmonic oscillator single-particle states, separating geometrical coefficients from momentum-transfer-dependent radial integrals. As an application, they compute the coefficients needed for the specific transition \( \frac{5}{2}^+ \to \frac{3}{2}^+ \) in the charged-current reaction \( ^{127}\mathrm{I}(\nu_l, l^-)^{127}\mathrm{Xe}^\star \), using the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) for the many-body nuclear wave functions.

## Key numbers

* The lowest excitation of \( ^{127}\mathrm{Xe} \) in the \( \frac{5}{2}^+ \to \frac{3}{2}^+ \) transition lies at 124.6 keV.
* The model space for \( ^{127}\mathrm{I} \) and \( ^{127}\mathrm{Xe} \) consists of oscillator major shells with \( N = 3-5 \) plus the intruder \( 0i_{13/2} \) from the \( N = 6 \) major shell.
* The radial integrals are expressed in terms of \( y = (qb/2)^2 \), where \( q \) is the three-momentum transfer and \( b \) the harmonic oscillator length parameter.
* The polynomial expansion for the reduced matrix elements has the form \( e^{-y} y^{\beta/2} \sum_{\mu=0}^{n_{\mathrm{max}}} \mathcal{P}_\mu^J y^\mu \), with \( n_{\mathrm{max}} = (N_1 + N_2 - \beta)/2 \).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of HaKos (2002) for electromagnetic and weak processes to provide explicit analytic expressions for the reduced matrix elements needed in neutrino-nucleus cross-sections. It enables the calculation of exclusive transitions for nuclei such as \( ^{127}\mathrm{I} \), which is a promising target for solar and supernova neutrino detection (as in radiochemical experiments similar to the \( ^{37}\mathrm{Cl} \) method) and for cold dark matter detection (Kosmas and Vergados, 1997). The formalism is designed to be used with the quasiparticle-phonon model (Soloviev, 1992) and allows future fits to nuclear transition strengths for semi-leptonic processes.
