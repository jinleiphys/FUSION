# Slow proton production in deep-inelastic neutrino scattering on deuterium

> The cross section for semi-inclusive deep inelastic charged current neutrino scattering on hydrogen and deuterium in which a slow proton is observed in coincidence with the muon is computed as a function of Bjorken x and light-cone momentum of the detected proton. In the Impulse Approximation contributions from hadronization and (in case of the deuteron) the emission of a spectator nucleon are considered. In addition the probability for rescattering is computed. The results are compared to a recent analysis of the data from the WA25 (BEBC) experiment at CERN. (KVI-1024)

## Key claim

The cross section for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic charged-current neutrino scattering on deuterium with a coincident slow proton can be computed in the Impulse Approximation by summing direct (debris fragmentation) and spectator contributions, but the observed rescattering probability saturates at large energy transfer and shows little x-dependence, contradicting simple incoherent rescattering models and suggesting that only one of the produced colorless fragments effectively interacts due to finite formation time.

## Method

The authors compute the double-differential cross section dσ/dx dz for slow proton production in neutrino and antineutrino scattering on hydrogen and deuterium, using a factorized model where the semi-inclusive cross section is expressed as a convolution of quark momentum distributions and fragmentation functions for diquark (valence) and four-quark (sea) clusters. For the deuteron, the direct process is computed by folding free-nucleon semi-inclusive structure functions with the deuteron momentum distribution (Bonn potential), while the spectator process uses the light-cone momentum distribution of the nucleons in the deuteron; rescattering is estimated in the eikonal approximation with a formation-time-dependent model using the VENUS hadronization code. Results are compared to data from the WA25 (BEBC) experiment at CERN.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum distribution uses ⟨p⊥²⟩ ≈ 300 (MeV/c)².
- The experimental momentum cutoff for detected protons is 150 MeV/c.
- The inclusive rescattering probability using N_eff = 1 and a fragment-nucleon cross section σ = 45 mb gives P_resc ≈ 0.12, agreeing with the average experimental value.
- The multiplicity parametrization used is N(x,Q²) = 1.1 + 1.35 ln W²(x,Q²).
- The maximum backward momentum for spectator protons at x=0 is p_max = (3/4)m.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on free protons by Bosveld et al. (1991, 1992) to the deuteron target, and provides a theoretical framework for interpreting the WA25 (BEBC) bubble chamber data. The spectator process directly relates to the tagged EMC effect originally proposed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981), and the rescattering analysis addresses the role of formation time in hadronization, which is essential for interpreting heavy-ion reactions. The results enable decomposition of experimental events into direct, spectator, and rescattering contributions, and test the validity of the convolution approach versus non-nucleonic degrees of freedom for the EMC effect.
