# Four-body continuum effects in 11Be+d scattering

> We present a new reaction model, which permits the description of reactions where both colliding nuclei present a low threshold to breakup. The method corresponds to a four-body extension of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) model. We first discuss the theoretical formalism, and then apply the method to 11Be+d scattering at Ecm = 45.5 MeV. The 11Be nucleus and the deuteron are described by 10Be+n and p + n structures, respectively. The model involves very large bases, but we show that an accurate description of elastic-scattering data may be achieved only when continuum states of 11Be and of the deuteron are introduced simultaneously. We also discuss breakup calculations, and show that the cross section is larger for 11Be than for the deuteron. The present theory provides reliable wave functions that may be used in the analysis of (d,p) or (d,n) experiments involving radioactive beams.

## Key claim

The paper presents a four-body extension of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method that includes breakup of both colliding nuclei simultaneously, and shows that for 11Be+d scattering at Ecm = 45.5 MeV, accurate description of elastic-scattering data is achieved only when continuum states of both 11Be and the deuteron are introduced together.

## Method

The authors extend the standard three-body CDCC method to a four-body framework where both projectiles are described by two-body cluster structures: 11Be as 10Be+n and the deuteron as p+n. Internal wave functions for each nucleus are expanded over a Lagrange basis to obtain pseudostates that approximate continuum states, and the total four-body wave function is expanded over channel functions coupling the internal states with relative orbital angular momentum. The coupled-channel equations are solved using R-matrix theory with a channel radius of 25 fm and angular momenta up to Jmax = 71/2, using optical potentials from the Koning-Delaroche global potential for the nucleon-nucleus interactions and the Minnesota potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering data at Ecm = 45.5 MeV (Elab = 26.9A MeV) for the 11Be+d system
- 11Be continuum states included up to Emax = 10 MeV with angular momenta I1 = 1/2±, 3/2±, 5/2+ (orbital angular momenta 0, 1, 2)
- Deuteron continuum states included up to Emax = 15 MeV with I2 = 0+, 2+
- 25 Gauss-Laguerre functions with scaling parameter h = 0.4 fm used for the two-body systems
- Relative motion angular momenta up to Jmax = 71/2 with channel radius a = 25 fm and 50 Gauss-Legendre basis functions
- Without breakup effects, the elastic cross section is overestimated by up to a factor of two near the minimum around 22 degrees
- At large angles, the full calculation is slightly lower than data by about 15 percent
- Integrated breakup cross sections calculated up to Ecm = 40 MeV show 11Be single breakup as the dominant process

## Context

This work extends the standard CDCC method (Rawitscher 1974, Kamimura et al. 1986, Austern et al. 1987) to four-body systems where both colliding nuclei have low breakup thresholds, going beyond previous three-body CDCC calculations for 11Be+d scattering by Chen et al. (2016) that treated the deuteron as point-like. The model enables calculation of wave functions needed for (d,p) and (d,n) stripping reactions with radioactive beams, and could be extended to other systems such as 8Li+d or 13C+13C, as well as to systems involving three-body nuclei.
