# The measurement of the pp->K+ n Sigma+ reaction near threshold

> It is shown that a recent extraction of the total cross section for pp->K+ n Sigma+ from inclusive K+ production data is in conflict with experimental data on the exclusive pp->K+ p Lambda reaction. The result may be interpreted as an upper bound which is not inconsistent with the much lower values that already exist in the literature.

## Key claim

The total cross section for pp→K⁺nΣ⁺ extracted from inclusive K⁺ production data by the COSY-HIRES collaboration is in conflict with exclusive COSY-TOF data on pp→K⁺pΛ; the HIRES result should be interpreted only as an upper bound, not a definitive measurement, and is not inconsistent with the much lower values already in the literature.

## Method

The authors compare inclusive forward K⁺ production data from COSY-HIRES at a beam momentum of 2.870 GeV/c with exclusive pp→K⁺pΛ data from COSY-TOF at 2.95, 3.20, and 3.30 GeV/c. They examine the missing-mass spectra and the rise in cross section around the ΣN threshold, showing that the COSY-TOF exclusive data contradict the HIRES assumption that Λ production is essentially the same on both sides of the ΣN threshold. The authors then demonstrate that if the Λ production rise seen in COSY-TOF data is accounted for, the HIRES-derived Σ⁺ cross section reduces to a value consistent with the COSY-ANKE result.

## Key numbers

- The COSY-HIRES collaboration reported R(Σ⁺/Σ⁰) ≈ 5 ± 1 from inclusive K⁺ data at a beam momentum of 2.870 GeV/c, corresponding to an excess energy ε = 102.6 MeV for Σ⁺ production.
- The COSY-ANKE collaboration found R(Σ⁺/Σ⁰) ≈ 0.7 ± 0.1 using three different experimental techniques at several energies, with data at ε = 129 MeV and at four energies closer to threshold.
- The increase in forward cross section from left to right of the ΣN threshold is ≈ 60% for HIRES inclusive data at 2.87 GeV/c, while for COSY-TOF exclusive data at 2.95 GeV/c the change is ≈ 40%.
- If the excess to the right of the ΣN threshold were reduced from 60% to 20% to account for the Λ production jump found in COSY-TOF data, the total cross sections for pp→K⁺nΣ⁺ and pp→K⁺pΣ⁰ would be broadly similar.

## Context

This work extends the debate between the COSY-ANKE measurements (Valdau 2007, Valdau 2010) and the COSY-HIRES measurement (Budzanowski 2010) of the pp→K⁺nΣ⁺ cross section near threshold. It also contradicts the earlier COSY-11 result (Rozek 2006) by a large factor. The analysis uses exclusive COSY-TOF data (Abdel-Bary 2010, Abdel-Bary 2006) to show that coupled-channel effects between ΣN and Λp channels, analogous to cusp effects seen in K⁻d→π⁻Λp (Tan 1969) and γp→π⁺n (Althoff 1983), undermine the assumptions of the HIRES analysis. The work enables future model-independent extractions of Σ production cross sections only if reliable exclusive pp→K⁺pΛ data become available at the HIRES beam momentum.
