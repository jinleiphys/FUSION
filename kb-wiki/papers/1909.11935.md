## Key claim

The ANL-Osaka collaboration presents a comprehensive review of their Dynamical Coupled-Channel (DCC) model for determining partial-wave amplitudes of оқN and нЁN reactions up to invariant mass W = 2 GeV, with the predicted amplitudes made publicly available on a dedicated web page. ## Method

The ANL-Osaka DCC model is based on an energy-independent effective Hamiltonian that includes meson-baryon interactions, bare N* states, and оқоқN channels, with coupled-channel scattering equations solved for 8 channels: нЁN, оқN, н·N, Kн›, Kнё, and the оқоқN resonant components оқн”, оҒN, оғN. The partial-wave amplitudes are separated into a direct reaction amplitude defined by coupled-channel equations and an N*-excitation amplitude involving dressed N* decay vertices and propagators, with parameters determined by о‡б²-fits to world data. For electromagnetic reactions, the model uses parametrized helicity amplitudes for bare нЁN Б†’ N* vertices, and resonance poles are extracted by analytically continuing the amplitudes to the complex energy plane using the method detailed in earlier works.

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee (SL) model (1996) which originally focused on the н”(1232) resonance, and builds upon the Dynamical Coupled-Channel analyses performed at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at JLab during 2006-2012 (12 publications). The ANL-Osaka collaboration continued this analysis during 2013-2016 to extract 22 nucleon resonances, and the model was also extended to investigate electron and neutrino-induced meson production reactions on the nucleon. The publicly available partial-wave amplitudes enable other researchers to calculate cross sections for meson-baryon scattering, meson photoproduction, pion electroproduction, and inclusive N(e,e')X reactions without needing to reproduce the full coupled-channel calculations.
