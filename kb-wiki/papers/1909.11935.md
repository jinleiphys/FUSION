# The ANL-Osaka Partial-Wave Amplitudes of $πN$ and $γN$ Reactions

> The determination of the Argonne National Laboratory-Osaka University (ANL-Osaka) Partial-Wave Amplitudes (PWA) of $πN$ and $γN$ Reactions is reviewed. The predicted PWA are presented on a web page (https://www.phy.anl.gov/theory/research/anl-osaka-pwa). The formulas are given for using the predicted PWA to calculate the cross sections of (1) meson-baryon ($MB$) scattering $MB \rightarrow M'B'$ with $MB, M'B'= πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ$, (2) two-pion production $πN \rightarrow πΔ, ρN, σN \rightarrow ππN$, (3) Meson photoproduction $γN \rightarrow πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ$, (4) Pion electroproduction $N(e,e'π)N$, (5) inclusive $N(e,e')X$. We also present sample results from our fits to the data.

## Key claim

The ANL-Osaka collaboration presents a comprehensive review of their Dynamical Coupled-Channel (DCC) model for determining partial-wave amplitudes of πN and γN reactions up to invariant mass W = 2 GeV, with the predicted amplitudes made publicly available on a dedicated web page. The model parameters were determined by performing χ²-fits to world data of γN, πN → πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ (about 30,000 data points), but the authors note that the DCC model, like all hadron reaction models, has parameters that can only be determined phenomenologically from fitting the data in the absence of theoretical inputs.

## Method

The ANL-Osaka DCC model is based on an energy-independent effective Hamiltonian that includes meson-baryon interactions, bare N* states, and ππN channels, with coupled-channel scattering equations solved for 8 channels: γN, πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, and the ππN resonant components πΔ, σN, ρN. The partial-wave amplitudes are separated into a direct reaction amplitude defined by coupled-channel equations and an N*-excitation amplitude involving dressed N* decay vertices and propagators, with parameters determined by χ²-fits to world data. For electromagnetic reactions, the model uses parametrized helicity amplitudes for bare γN → N* vertices, and resonance poles are extracted by analytically continuing the amplitudes to the complex energy plane using the method detailed in earlier works.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers reactions up to invariant mass W = 2 GeV
- The model includes about 20 bare N* states and 8 reaction channels: γN, πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, πΔ, ρN, σN
- The fits used approximately 30,000 data points from world data
- The Δ mass used in the πΔ self-energy is mΔ = 1280 MeV; the ρ mass is mρ = 812 MeV; the σ mass is mσ = 700.0 MeV
- The Δ → πN decay form factor cutoff is 358 MeV; the ρ → ππ decay form factor cutoff is 461 MeV
- The σ model parameters include g0 = 1.638, h0 = 0.556, c = 1.02 fm, and d = 0.514 fm
- The multipole amplitudes for pion electroproduction are presented for W = 1080-2000 MeV and Q² = 0-3 (GeV/c)²
- Computational resources used: about 500,000 hours/year from DOE's NERSC and about 300,000 hours/year from Argonne's LCRC

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee (SL) model (1996) which originally focused on the Δ(1232) resonance, and builds upon the Dynamical Coupled-Channel analyses performed at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at JLab during 2006-2012 (12 publications). The ANL-Osaka collaboration continued this analysis during 2013-2016 to extract 22 nucleon resonances, and the model was also extended to investigate electron and neutrino-induced meson production reactions on the nucleon. The publicly available partial-wave amplitudes enable other researchers to calculate cross sections for meson-baryon scattering, meson photoproduction, pion electroproduction, and inclusive N(e,e')X reactions without needing to reproduce the full coupled-channel calculations.
