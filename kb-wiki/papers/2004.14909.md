# Transport coefficients of nucleon neutron star cores for various nuclear interactions within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> We consider the thermal conductivity, shear viscosity, and momentum relaxation rates in the nucleon cores of the neutron stars. We study how the choice of the nuclear interaction and the model for three-body forces may affect these transport coefficients calculated within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body nuclear theory. We find that at relatively large densities the model dependence of the results is substantial. In addition we provide the analytical approximations which allow to incorporate our results in practical simulations.

## Key claim
The transport coefficients (thermal conductivity, shear viscosity, and momentum relaxation rate) in neutron star cores depend substantially on the choice of nuclear interaction and three-body force model at high baryon densities, with differences up to an order of magnitude, while at low densities near nuclear saturation density all calculations agree.

## Method
The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) framework in a partial-wave basis up to total angular momentum J=12, for baryon densities from 0.05 to 0.6 fm^-3 and proton fractions from 0 to 0.5. They used five different nuclear interactions: Argonne v18 and CD-Bonn two-body potentials, each combined with Urbana IX three-body forces, plus Argonne v18 combined with a microscopic three-body force (TBFmic). The on-shell G-matrices at the Fermi surface were then used to compute transport cross-sections and effective mean free paths for thermal conductivity, shear viscosity, and momentum relaxation.

## Key numbers
- Baryon density range studied: 0.05 to 0.6 fm^-3
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3
- At densities nB ≲ 0.2 fm^-3, inverse mean free paths for different interactions are close; at higher densities results can diverge by an order of magnitude
- For the Av18+TBFmic interaction, the primed transport coefficients change sign at nB ∼ 0.45 fm^-3
- Proton fraction in beta-stable matter: xp ≲ 0.2-0.3
- Temperature scaling: nucleon mean free paths scale as T^-2; electromagnetic mean free paths for protons scale as T^-1 for thermal conductivity and T^-5/3 for shear viscosity

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Shternin 2013) which calculated nucleon thermal conductivity and shear viscosity for only the Argonne v18+Urbana IX interaction, by now exploring five different nuclear interactions following the analysis of effective masses in Baldo (2014). The results enable practical simulations of neutron star cooling, magnetic field evolution, and other non-equilibrium processes by providing analytical approximations for transport coefficients. The study complements lepton transport calculations by Shternin and Yakovlev (2007, 2008) and the review by Schmitt (2018).
