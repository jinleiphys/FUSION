# Effects of Critical Point on Quark Gluon Plasma

> We have used the relativistic second-order causal viscous hydrodynamics equipped with an equation of state which includes the QCD critical point to study the propagation of linear and nonlinear perturbations in quark gluon plasma created in a relativistic heavy-ion collision. We have also studied the behaviour of dynamic structure factor, which is calculated by taking the correlation of dynamical density fluctuation. It is found that the critical point has significant effects on the propagation of linear as well as on nonlinear perturbations. It is also found that the dynamic structure factors are substantially modified with the presence of the QCD critical point.

## Key claim
The paper shows that near the QCD critical point, the threshold wavelength for sound wave propagation diverges, meaning all finite-wavelength perturbations are suppressed regardless of transport coefficient values, but this conclusion depends on using an equation of state that includes the critical point while keeping transport coefficients fixed at constant values rather than scaling them near the critical point.

## Method
The author used relativistic second-order causal (Israel-Stewart) viscous hydrodynamics with an equation of state incorporating the QCD critical point via universality-class critical exponents. Linear perturbations were imposed on the fluid, and dispersion relations were derived from the conservation equations for energy-momentum tensor and net baryon number current, keeping terms up to second order in dissipative fluxes. The threshold wavevector was determined by setting the ratio of imaginary to real parts of the frequency equal to unity.

## Key numbers
- Critical point location used: (T_c, μ_c) = (154 MeV, 367 MeV)
- Current quark mass for up and down flavors taken as 10 MeV
- Threshold wavevector in the Navier-Stokes limit: k_th = (3/2) * c_s * (h/s) * (1/(η/s)), where h is enthalpy density and s is entropy density
- Transport coefficients fixed at η/s = κT/s = 1/(4π) for the critical-point calculations
- Correlation length near critical point limited to 2-3 fm at most due to critical slowing down (citing Berdnikov and Rajagopal, 1999)

## Context
This work extends the author's prior study (Hasanujjaman, 2021) on linear perturbation suppression near the critical point by now including nonlinear perturbations and calculating the dynamic structure factor. It contrasts with Minami (2015) which estimated the dynamic spectral structure without incorporating a critical-point-containing equation of state. The approach uses the universality hypothesis for the equation of state near the critical point, following the framework of Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998, 1999, 2008, 2011). The results enable future experimental searches at BES-RHIC, GSI-FAIR, and NICA by predicting which hydrodynamic modes survive near the critical point.
