# Alpha-decay properties of superheavy elements $Z=113-125$ in the relativistic mean-field theory with vector self-coupling of $ω$ meson

> We have investigated properties of $α$-decay chains of recently produced superheavy elements Z=115 and Z=113 using the new Lagrangian model NL-SV1 with inclusion of the vector self-coupling of $ω$ meson in the framework of the relativistic mean-field theory. It is shown that the experimentally observed alpha-decay energies and half-lives are reproduced well by this Lagrangian model. Further calculations for the heavier elements with Z=117-125 show that these nuclei are superdeformed with a prolate shape in the ground state. A superdeformed shell-closure at Z=118 lends an additional binding and an extra stability to nuclei in this region. Consequently, it is predicted that the corresponding $Q_α$ values provide $α$-decay half-lives for heavier superheavy nuclei within the experimentally feasible conditions. The results are compared with those of macroscopic-microscopic approaches. A perspective of the difference in shell effects amongst various approaches is presented and its consequences on superheavy nuclei are discussed.

# Alpha-decay properties of superheavy elements Z=113-125 in the relativistic mean-field theory with vector self-coupling of ω meson

## Key claim
The relativistic mean-field model NL-SV1, which includes vector self-coupling of the ω meson, reproduces experimentally observed alpha-decay energies and half-lives for the decay chains of Z=115 and Z=113 within about 0.5-0.8 MeV for most nuclei, but with a notable exception: the calculated Q_α for the parent nucleus 288115 deviates from experiment by about 0.8 MeV, and the model predicts a superdeformed ground state (β₂ ~ 0.6) for all nuclei with Z ≥ 115, which is not supported by macroscopic-microscopic approaches.

## Method
The authors performed axially symmetric deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL-SV1 Lagrangian parameter set, which incorporates nonlinear vector self-coupling of the ω meson. The Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations were solved by expanding wavefunctions in a harmonic-oscillator basis of 20 shells, with pairing included via the BCS scheme using constant pairing gaps Δ_n(p) = 4.8 N^{-1/3} (Z^{-1/3}). Constrained calculations with a quadratic constraint were carried out to map potential energy landscapes over a range of quadrupole deformations and identify ground-state minima.

## Context
This work extends the RMF approach with the NL-SV1 force, previously shown to reproduce shell effects in Ni and Sn isotopes near stability (Sharma 2000) and in the waiting-point nucleus 80Zn (Sharma 2002), to the superheavy region. The results contrast with macroscopic-microscopic approaches such as the Finite-Range Droplet Model (FRDM) (Moeller 1997) and the Yukawa-plus-exponential model calculations of Muntian et al. (2003), which do not predict superdeformation in this region and give Q_α values typically 1-2 MeV higher. The predicted superdeformed shell closure at Z=118 and the resulting half-lives in the range of milliseconds to seconds suggest that heavier superheavy nuclei up to Z=125 may be experimentally accessible, enabling future synthesis experiments to test the predicted island of stability.
