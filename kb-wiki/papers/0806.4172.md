# Prediction of a Photon Peak in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We show that if a flavour-less vector meson remains bound after deconfinement, and if its limiting velocity in the quark-gluon plasma is subluminal, then this meson produces a distinct peak in the spectrum of thermal photons emitted by the plasma. We also demonstrate that this effect is a universal property of all strongly coupled, large-Nc plasmas with a gravity dual. For the J/psi the corresponding peak lies between 3 and 5 GeV and could be observed at LHC.

## Key claim

If a flavor-less vector meson remains bound after deconfinement and its limiting velocity in the quark-gluon plasma is subluminal, then the meson dispersion relation must cross the light-cone, producing a distinct peak in the thermal photon spectrum; this effect is a universal property of all strongly coupled, large-Nc plasmas with a gravity dual, but the authors note that the two assumptions about meson behavior have not been rigorously established in QCD.

## Method

The authors use the gauge/gravity duality to compute the dispersion relation of heavy vector mesons in a strongly coupled, large-Nc plasma, showing that a subluminal limiting velocity forces the dispersion curve to cross the light-cone at a finite energy. They then model the fireball as an expanding cylinder with a temperature evolution derived from volume expansion, introduce a fugacity factor to enforce chemical non-equilibrium of charm quarks, and numerically integrate the thermal photon emission from J/psi decays over the plasma lifetime, comparing it to the background from light quarks.

## Key numbers

- The predicted photon peak for the J/psi lies between 3 and 5 GeV.
- The dissociation temperature used for the J/psi is Tf = 1.25 Tc, where Tc ≈ 170 MeV.
- The charm-quark mass is taken as Mc = 1.7 GeV.
- The width of the J/psi spectral function is modeled as Γ = 100 MeV.
- The J/psi rest mass is set to M = 3 GeV.
- The limiting velocity at Tf is parameterized with v0 = 0.2.
- The number of primordial ccbar pairs at LHC is Ncc ≈ 60, based on dσ_ccbar^pp/dy = 1850 μb.
- For the Upsilon meson, the peak lies at ωpeak ≳ 10 GeV, where thermal photons are exponentially suppressed relative to pQCD photons.

## Context

This work extends the argument of Matsui and Satz (1986) that heavy mesons may remain bound above the deconfinement temperature, and the proposal by Chu and Matsui (1989) that mesons in a plasma have a subluminal limiting velocity. It builds on calculations of the meson dispersion relation in strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma using the gravity dual (Mateos and Patino, 2007) and on the universal D-brane probe description of flavor in holographic plasmas (Karch and Randall, 2002). The paper enables experimental searches for a photon excess in the 3-5 GeV range at LHC, which could serve as a signature of heavy vector meson survival in the quark-gluon plasma.
