# Spectral functions of strange vector mesons in asymmetric hyperonic matter

> We study the medium modifications of the spectral functions as well as production cross-sections of the strange vector mesons ($ϕ$, $K^*$ and $\bar {K^*}$) in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter. These are obtained from the in-medium masses of the open strange mesons and the decay widths $ϕ\rightarrow K\bar K$, $K^* \rightarrow Kπ$ and $\bar {K^*} \rightarrow {\bar K}π$ in the hadronic medium. The decay widths are computed using a field theoretic model of composite hadrons with quark/antiquark constituents, from the matrix element of the light quark-antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian between the initial and final states. The matrix element is multiplied with a coupling strength parameter for the light quark-antiquark pair creation, which is fitted to the observed vacuum decay width of the decay process. There are observed to be substantial modifications of the spectral functions as well as production cross-sections of these vector mesons due to isospin asymmetry as well as strangeness of the hadronic medum at high densities. These studies should have observable consequences, e.g. in the yield of the hidden and open strange mesons arising from the isospin asymmetric high energy heavy ion collisions at the Compressed baryonic matter (CBM) experiments at the future facility at GSI.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the spectral functions and production cross-sections of strange vector mesons (phi, K*, and anti-K*) undergo substantial modifications in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter at high densities, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the mass shifts of the vector mesons K* and anti-K* are identical to those of the pseudoscalar mesons K and anti-K, respectively, since a direct calculation of K* and anti-K* mass modifications using the chiral SU(3) model is beyond the scope of the work.

## Method

The in-medium masses of kaons and antikaons were calculated using a chiral SU(3) model with mean-field approximation, incorporating the Weinberg-Tomozawa vectorial interaction, scalar exchange terms, and range terms. The decay widths for phi to K anti-K, K* to K pi, and anti-K* to anti-K pi were computed using a field-theoretic model of composite hadrons with quark/antiquark constituents, where the matrix element of the light quark-antiquark pair creation term from the free Dirac Hamiltonian was multiplied by a coupling strength parameter fitted to the observed vacuum decay width. The mass shifts of K* and anti-K* were assumed equal to those of K and anti-K, and the relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral functions and production cross-sections were then obtained from the in-medium masses and decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The parameters d1 and d2 of the range terms were determined to be 2.56/m_K and 0.73/m_K, fitted to empirical KN scattering lengths of a_KN(I=0) approximately -0.09 fm and a_KN(I=1) approximately -0.31 fm.
- The vacuum expectation value of the scalar-isovector field delta is zero (delta_0 = 0), since a nonzero value would break the isospin symmetry of the vacuum.
- The baryon effective mass is given by m_i* = -(g_sigma_i sigma + g_zeta_i zeta + g_delta_i delta), where sigma, zeta, and delta are the expectation values of the non-strange scalar isoscalar, strange scalar isoscalar, and non-strange scalar isovector fields respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon and antikaon in-medium properties using a chiral SU(3) model (Mishra et al., earlier works) and the Quark Meson Coupling model (Krein et al., earlier works) to the vector meson sector, specifically phi, K*, and anti-K*. The calculations are motivated by the need to understand observables in isospin-asymmetric heavy ion collisions at the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at GSI, and build on prior coupled-channel approaches (Ramos and Oset, 2000) and Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics transport model studies (Elena et al., 2016, 2017) that examined K* and anti-K* dynamics in heavy ion collisions.
