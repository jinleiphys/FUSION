# Production of neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 162 in multinucleon transfer reactions

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system model, the production mechanism of neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 162 has been investigated systematically. The isotopic yields in the multinucleon transfer reaction of $^{238}$U + $^{248}$Cm was analyzed and compared the available experimental data. Systematics on the production of superheavy nuclei via $^{238}$U on $^{252,254}$Cf, $^{254}$Es and $^{257}$Fm is investigated. It is found that the shell effect is of importance in the formation of neutron-rich nuclei around N=162 owing to the enhancement of fission barrier. The fragments in the multinucleon transfer reactions manifest the broad isotopic distribution and are dependent on the beam energy. The polar angles of the fragments tend to the forward emission with increasing the beam energy. The production cross sections of new isotopes are estimated and heavier targets are available for the neutron-rich superheavy nucleus formation. The optimal system and beam energy are proposed for the future experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The dinuclear system model predicts that the shell effect around N=162 enhances the production of neutron-rich heavy nuclei in multinucleon transfer reactions by increasing the fission barrier, but the yields of secondary fragments are reduced by several orders of magnitude due to the deexcitation process, particularly in the superheavy region.

## Method

The authors employed the dinuclear system (DNS) model, solving microscopically derived master equations for proton and neutron transfer probabilities, coupled with a Fokker-Planck equation for dissipation of relative motion and angular momentum. The survival probability of primary fragments after particle evaporation (neutrons, protons, alpha particles) in competition with binary fission was evaluated using a statistical approach based on Weisskopf evaporation theory. Angular distributions were calculated using a deflection function method combining Coulomb and nuclear interactions.

## Key numbers

- In the reaction $^{238}$U + $^{248}$Cm at E$_{lab}$ = 7.0 MeV/nucleon, the isotopic yields from the DNS model were compared with available experimental data from GSI (Kratz, 2013).
- For the reaction $^{238}$U + $^{252}$Cf, the maximal yields of isotones with N=162 occur around the new isotope $^{263}$Md.
- The shell effect increases the MNT yields above two orders of magnitude compared to calculations without shell effects.
- The emission polar angles for superheavy elements Rf, Db, Sg, and Bh at 7 MeV/nucleon range from 80° to 95°; at 8 MeV/nucleon the maximal cross section shifts to 50°.
- Excitation functions were calculated for $^{266}$Rf, $^{267}$Db, and $^{268}$Sg produced in $^{238}$U + $^{252}$Cf, showing an optimal incident energy around the Coulomb barrier.

## Context

This work extends the application of the DNS model, previously used for MNT reactions around N=126 (Feng, 2017; Chen, 2020), to the neutron-rich region around N=162. It builds on experimental MNT studies of actinide collisions at GSI (Hildenbrand, 1977; Glässel, 1979) and recent measurements of transcurium isotopes (Kratz, 2013). The results enable predictions for future experiments at facilities such as RIBF, SPIRAL2, FRIB, and HIAF, and suggest optimal target-projectile combinations (e.g., $^{238}$U + $^{257}$Fm) and beam energies for producing new neutron-rich superheavy nuclei.
