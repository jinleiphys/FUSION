# Effects of the $U_A(1)$ Anomaly on $η\to 2 γ$ Decay

> We study the $η\to 2 γ$ decay using an extended three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that includes the 't~Hooft instanton induced interaction. The $η$ meson mass and the $η\to 2 γ$ decay width are reproduced simultaneously with a rather strong instanton induced interaction. The calculated $η$ decay constant is $f_η= 2.23 f_π$ and it suggests that the $η$ meson is no longer the Goldstone boson.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\eta$ meson loses its Goldstone boson nature significantly due to the $U_A(1)$ anomaly, as evidenced by the calculated $\eta$ decay constant $f_\eta = 2.23 f_\pi$ and the failure of the Goldberger-Treiman relation at the quark level, but this conclusion depends on using a rather strong instanton-induced interaction (with $G_D \langle \bar s s \rangle/G_s = 1.58$) to simultaneously reproduce the observed $\eta$ mass and $\eta \to 2\gamma$ decay width.

## Method

The authors use an extended three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a chiral $U_L(3) \times U_R(3)$ symmetric four-quark interaction and a 't Hooft determinant six-quark interaction to incorporate the $U_A(1)$ anomaly. Quark condensates and constituent quark masses are determined self-consistently via gap equations with a covariant cutoff, and the $\eta$ meson mass is obtained from the pole position of the pseudoscalar channel quark-antiquark scattering amplitude in the ladder approximation. The $\eta \to 2\gamma$ decay amplitude is calculated from pseudoscalar-vector-vector type quark triangle diagrams, with the loop integrals performed without a cutoff to preserve the model-independent prediction for $\pi^0 \to 2\gamma$.

## Key numbers

- Calculated $\eta$ decay constant: $f_\eta = 206\ \mathrm{MeV} = 2.23 f_\pi$ at the value of $G_D$ that reproduces the observed $\eta$ mass.
- $\eta \to 2\gamma$ decay amplitude: $\widetilde{\cal T}_{\eta \to 2 \gamma} = 2.73 \times 10^{-11}\ \mathrm{eV}^{-1}$, about 10% larger than the experimental value of $(2.5 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-11}\ \mathrm{eV}^{-1}$.
- $\pi^0 \to 2\gamma$ decay amplitude: $\widetilde{\cal T}_{\pi^0 \to 2 \gamma} = 2.50 \times 10^{-11}\ \mathrm{eV}^{-1}$, agreeing with the experimental value $(2.5 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-11}\ \mathrm{eV}^{-1}$.
- Constituent u,d-quark mass: $M_{u,d} = 324.9\ \mathrm{MeV}$, independent of $G_D$.
- Constituent s-quark mass at $G_D = G_D^\eta$: $M_s = 503.3\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (decreasing from 556.3 MeV at $G_D = 0$).
- Current s-quark mass: $m_s = 192.95\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at $G_D = G_D^\eta$, with ratio $m_s/m_u = 24.1$.
- Kaon decay constant: $f_K = 96.6\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at $G_D = G_D^\eta$, about 15% smaller than the observed value.
- Mixing angle at $G_D = G_D^\eta$: $\theta = 15.1^\circ$, compared to the usual analysis value $\theta \simeq -20^\circ$ from Donoghue et al. (1985).
- Strength of instanton-induced interaction: $G_D \langle \bar s s \rangle/G_s = 1.58$ at $G_D = G_D^\eta$, meaning the six-quark contribution to u,d-quark dynamical mass is about 60% larger than the four-quark contribution.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\eta$ and $\eta'$ mesons in the extended NJL model by Klevansky (1990), Bernard et al. (1988), and others, where the instanton-induced interaction strength was typically determined from the $\eta'$ mass (giving a much weaker interaction, about 1/10 to 1/5 of the value needed here). The authors contrast their approach with the chiral perturbation theory analysis of Donoghue et al. (1985), which used the Wess-Zumino-Witten lagrangian with $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle $\theta \simeq -20^\circ$ and did not fully account for $U_A(1)$ anomaly effects on the singlet channel. The results enable future studies of other $\eta$ decay processes such as $\eta \to \pi^0 2\gamma$ and $\eta \to 3\pi$ within the same framework, and motivate constructing low-energy effective models of QCD that can also describe the $\eta'$ meson.
