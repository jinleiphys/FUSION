# Quantum Condensates in Nuclear Matter: Problems

> In connection with the contribution "Quantum Condensates in Nuclear Matter" some problems are given to become more familiar with the techniques of many-particle physics.

## Key claim

This paper presents a set of pedagogical problems and solutions designed to familiarize readers with many-particle physics techniques used in the study of quantum condensates in nuclear matter, rather than reporting new research results. The abstract's claim that the problems "become more familiar with the techniques" is qualified by the fact that the paper consists entirely of textbook-style exercises with worked solutions, not original findings.

## Method

The paper works through three problems: (1) diagonalizing the entropy operator for a superfluid state using the Bogoliubov transformation, (2) solving the two-particle Schrödinger equation with Pauli blocking for the Yamaguchi interaction to study the BCS-BEC transition, and (3) constructing Gaussian wave functions for 2, 3, and 4 nucleon bound states and calculating the Pauli blocking energy shift via first-order perturbation theory. The solutions employ standard many-body techniques including canonical transformations, separable potentials, and variational methods.

## Key numbers

- Yamaguchi potential parameters: effective range \(\gamma = 285.8484\) MeV, effective coupling constant \(\lambda_{S=1,T=0} = 0.4144\) fm\(^{-3}\) for the spin triplet, isospin singlet channel
- Critical coupling for bound state dissolution: \(\lambda_c = \gamma^3/\pi^2\), corresponding to \(y_c = 1\) in dimensionless variables
- Variational parameters for \(\alpha\)-particle model: \(\lambda_t = 1317.8\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), \(\lambda_s = 667\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), \(\alpha = 8.288 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-2}\)
- Binding energy of the \(\alpha\) particle from the Gaussian variational model: \(-28.2\) MeV

## Context

This paper is a companion problems-and-solutions set to the contribution "Quantum Condensates in Nuclear Matter" and serves as a tutorial for students. It extends the standard treatment of BCS-BEC crossover by including Pauli blocking effects in the two-body problem, following the Yamaguchi potential approach. The work enables readers to understand the Mott effect for bound states in nuclear matter and provides a framework for calculating in-medium binding energy shifts for clusters up to \(\alpha\)-particles, referencing prior work by Yamaguchi and by Schuck and Röpke for numerical solutions including Pauli blocking.
