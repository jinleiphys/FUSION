# Mechanisms of proton-proton inelastic cross-section growth in multi-peripheral model within the framework of perturbation theory. Part 2

> We demonstrate a new technique for calculating proton-proton inelastic cross-section, which allows one by application of the Laplace' method replace the integrand in the integral for the scattering amplitude in the vicinity of the maximum point by expression of Gaussian type. This in turn, allows one to overcome the computational difficulties for the calculation of the integrals expressing the cross section to sufficiently large numbers of particles. We have managed to overcome these problems in calculating the proton-proton inelastic cross-section for production (n \le 8) number of secondary particles in within the framework of ϕ^3 model. As the result the obtained dependence of inelastic cross-section and total scattering cross-section on the energy \sqrt{s} are qualitative agrees with the experimental data. Such description of total cross-section behavior differs considerably from existing now description, where reggeons exchange with the intercept greater than unity is considered.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that by applying Laplace's method to replace the integrand of the scattering amplitude with a Gaussian expression near its maximum point, they can overcome computational difficulties in calculating proton-proton inelastic cross-sections for up to eight secondary particles within the phi^3 model, achieving qualitative agreement with experimental data without invoking reggeon exchange with intercept greater than unity.

## Method

The authors compute inelastic proton-proton cross-sections within the multi-peripheral "comb" diagram framework of the phi^3 model, using the Laplace method for multidimensional integrals to approximate the scattering amplitude near its constrained maximum point. They account for all n! interference contributions from different permutations of external line attachments to the comb diagram, which are typically neglected in approaches assuming strong ordering in rapidity or Sudakov parameters. The calculation is performed for production of n ≤ 8 secondary pions, with the amplitude expressed as a Gaussian expansion around the maximum point and integrated over phase space.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed for production of n ≤ 8 secondary particles (pions of mass m)
- Energy range considered: sqrt(s) = 3 to 95 GeV
- Results shown for n = 0 through n = 8 particle production cross-sections sigma'_n(sqrt(s)) as functions of energy
- The Gaussian approximation of the amplitude is verified numerically at sqrt(s) = 5 GeV and sqrt(s) = 100 GeV for n = 10 (shown in figures)
- Proton mass M and energy sqrt(s) are nondimensionalized with the pion mass m

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Sharf et al., Part 1) which showed that the main contribution to the inelastic scattering integral does not come from the multi-Regge domain, contrary to standard approaches. The method differs considerably from existing descriptions that rely on reggeon exchange with intercept greater than unity (as in the work of Lipatov (2004, 2008) and the BFKL approach (1976)). The results enable calculation of inelastic cross-sections without the approximations of strong rapidity ordering (Holliday, Nuovo Cimento) or strong ordering in Sudakov parameters (BFKL, 1976), and suggest that interference contributions, typically underestimated, are significant for describing the experimentally observed energy dependence of cross-sections.
