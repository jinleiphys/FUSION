# Azimuthal anisotropy of direct photons

> The electromagnetic bremsstrahlung produced by a quark interacting with nucleons or nuclei is azimuthally asymmetric. In the light-cone dipole approach this effect is related to the orientation dependent dipole cross section. Such a radiation anisotropy is expected to contribute to the azimuthal asymmetry of direct photons in pA and AA collisions, as well as in DIS and in the production of dileptons.

## Key claim

The azimuthal asymmetry of direct photons arises from the orientation-dependent interaction of a color dipole with the target, and this effect contributes to the measured $v_2$ of direct photons in pA and AA collisions; however, the asymmetry for quark-nucleus collisions is suppressed by an order of magnitude compared to quark-nucleon collisions due to cancellation from interactions with nucleons at different azimuthal angles.

## Method

The authors extended the light-cone dipole approach for electromagnetic bremsstrahlung (Kopeliovich et al., 1998; Kopeliovich et al., 1999) to calculate the azimuthal angle distribution of direct photons. They modeled the partial dipole-nucleon amplitude using an off-diagonal unintegrated gluon density fitted to HERA data on the proton structure function, and for nuclear targets they used an eikonal approximation with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density parametrization. The azimuthal anisotropy parameter $v_2$ was computed by averaging over the azimuthal angle of the photon transverse momentum at fixed impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass used as an infrared cutoff is $m_q \approx 0.2\ \text{GeV}$.
- The dipole cross section saturation parameters are $\sigma_0 = 23.03\ \text{mb}$, $R_0(x) = 0.4\ \text{fm} \times (x/x_0)^{0.144}$ with $x_0 = 3.04 \times 10^{-4}$ (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998).
- The forward slope for small-dipole-proton elastic scattering is $B^{(\bar qq)N}_{el}(r) \approx 5\ \text{GeV}^{-2}$, consistent with $B^{pp}_{el}/2$.
- The mean value of $s^2$ (the squared impact parameter of the dipole) is $\langle s^2 \rangle \approx 0.4\ \text{fm}^2$.
- The central nuclear density is $\rho_0 \approx 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$.
- The anisotropy $v_2^{qA}$ for quark-lead collisions is an order of magnitude smaller than $v_2^{qN}$ for quark-nucleon collisions at the same impact parameter and energy.

## Context

This work extends the dipole description of electromagnetic radiation (Kopeliovich et al., 1998; Kopeliovich et al., 1999) to include azimuthal angle distributions, which requires more detailed modeling of the dipole amplitude's dependence on impact parameter and dipole orientation. The results contradict the assumption that the dipole cross section is independent of the light-cone momentum sharing $\beta$, and the developed formalism can be applied to direct photon and Drell-Yan production in proton-nucleus and heavy ion collisions, as well as to azimuthal asymmetry in deep inelastic scattering. The approach relies on the saturated dipole cross section fitted to HERA data (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998) and generalizes it to an off-diagonal unintegrated gluon density.
