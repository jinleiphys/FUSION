# Elliptic Flow from Non-equilibrium Initial Condition with a Saturation Scale

> A current goal of relativistic heavy ion collisions experiments is the search for a Color Glass Condensate as the limiting state of QCD matter at very high density. In viscous hydrodynamics simulations, a standard Glauber initial condition leads to estimate $4πη/s \sim 1$, while a Color Glass Condensate modeling leads to at least a factor of 2 larger $η/s$. Within a kinetic theory approach based on a relativistic Boltzmann-like transport simulation, we point out that the out-of-equilibrium initial distribution proper of a Color Glass Condensate reduces the efficiency in building-up the elliptic flow. Our main result at RHIC energy is that the available data on $v_2$ are in agreement with a $4πη/s \sim 1$ also for Color Glass Condensate initial conditions, opening the possibility to describe self-consistently also higher order flow, otherwise significantly underestimated, and to pursue further the search for signatures of the Color Glass Condensate.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the initial non-equilibrium momentum-space distribution characteristic of a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) reduces the efficiency of converting spatial eccentricity into elliptic flow, so that at RHIC energy the available $v_2$ data are consistent with $4\pi\eta/s \sim 1$ even for CGC initial conditions, contrary to previous viscous hydrodynamics studies that required $4\pi\eta/s \sim 2$ for such initial conditions.

## Method
The authors employ a 3+1D relativistic Boltzmann transport simulation with a fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($\eta/s$), where the total cross section is determined via the Chapman-Enskog approximation to achieve the desired $\eta/s$. Three initial conditions are compared: a thermalized Glauber model (Th-Glauber), a thermalized factorized-KLN (Th-fKLN) distribution in coordinate space only, and a full fKLN distribution with the saturated momentum-space distribution below the saturation scale $Q_s$, initialized at $\tau_0 = 0.15$ fm/c.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV and impact parameter $b = 7.5$ fm, the initial eccentricity for the fKLN model is $\epsilon_x = 0.357$, while for the Glauber model it is $\epsilon_x = 0.284$.
- The average saturation scale is $\langle Q_s \rangle \approx 1.4$ GeV.
- The initial time for the full fKLN condition is $\tau_0 = 0.15$ fm/c, while for the thermalized cases it is $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c.
- The multiplicity $dN/d\eta$ at midrapidity is fixed to 400 for all cases, corresponding to $N_{part} \approx 150$.
- The system becomes isotropic within approximately 1 fm/c for all initial conditions, as shown by the ratio $P_L/P_T$.

## Context
This work extends the debate on the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, where viscous hydrodynamics with Glauber initial conditions yields $4\pi\eta/s \sim 1$ while CGC-inspired initial conditions (fKLN or MC-KLN) require $4\pi\eta/s \sim 2$ to match $v_2$ data (Luzum 2008, Song 2011, Adare 2011). The authors point out that previous hydrodynamics studies only implemented the different spatial distribution of CGC models, discarding the saturated momentum-space distribution below $Q_s$. This work enables a self-consistent treatment of CGC initial conditions that can also reproduce higher-order flow harmonics like $v_3$, which were otherwise underestimated with the larger $\eta/s$ required by thermalized CGC initial conditions.
