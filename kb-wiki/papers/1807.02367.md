# Neutrino-nucleus reactions on $^{16}$O based on new shell-model Hamiltonians

> Neutrino-induced reactions on $^{16}$O are investigated by shell-model calculaions with new shell-model Hamiltonians, which can describe well the structure of $p$-shell and $p$-$sd$ shell nuclei. Distribution of the spin-dipole strengths in $^{16}$O, which give major contributions to the $ν$-$^{16}$O reaction cross sections, is studied with the new Hamiltonians. Muon-capture reaction rates on $^{16}$O are also studied to discuss the quenching of the axial-vector coupling in nuclear medium. Charged-current and neutral-current reaction cross sections are evaluated in various particle and $γ$ emission channels as well as the total ones at neutrino energies up to $E_ν\approx$ 100 MeV. Branching ratios for the various channels are obtained by the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations, and partial cross sections for single- and multi-particle emission channels are evaluated. The cross sections updated are compared with previous continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) calculations. Effects of multi-particle emission channels on nucleosynthesis in core-collapse supernova (SN) explosions are investigated. Inclusion of $α$p emission channels is found to lead to an enhancement of production yields of $^{11}$B and $^{11}$C through $^{16}$O ($ν$, $ν$' $α$p) $^{11}$B and $^{16}$O ($ν$, e$^{-}$ $α$p) $^{11}$C reactions, respectively.

## Key claim

The new shell-model Hamiltonians (SFO-tls and SFO) provide improved descriptions of spin-dipole strengths in 16O compared to previous continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) calculations, but the cross section enhancement is conditional on neutrino energy: the SFO-tls cross section is more than 50% larger than CRPA at neutrino energies below 30 MeV, yet becomes smaller than CRPA above 90 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using two Hamiltonians (SFO and a modified version SFO-tls with updated tensor and spin-orbit components in the p-sd cross shell matrix elements) to compute spin-dipole transition strengths in 16O. They evaluated charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections for 16O at neutrino energies up to approximately 100 MeV, then used the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model to calculate branching ratios for single- and multi-particle emission channels including neutron, proton, deuteron, alpha, 3He, 3H, and gamma emission.

## Key numbers

- Muon capture rate on 16O: 10.21 x 10^4 s^-1 (SFO) and 11.20 x 10^4 s^-1 (SFO-tls) with quenching factor f = 0.95, compared to experimental value 10.26 x 10^4 s^-1
- Ratio of SFO-tls to CRPA cross sections for 16O (nu_e, e-) 16F: 1.36 at temperature T = 4 MeV, 1.12 at T = 8 MeV
- Spin-dipole sum rule exhaustion below Ex = 40 MeV for SFO-tls: 99% for 0-, 77% for 1-, 82% for 2-
- Energy-weighted sum values for SFO-tls: 73.0 MeV fm^2 (0-), 173.2 MeV fm^2 (1-), 246.5 MeV fm^2 (2-)
- Centroid energies for SFO-tls: 24.5 MeV (0-), 25.1 MeV (1-), 20.1 MeV (2-)
- Enhancement of alpha emission channel cross sections compared to CRPA: 1.7-2.2 times in (nu, e-) reactions, 2.0-2.8 times in (anti-nu, e+) reactions
- Ratio of 16O (nu, nu' alpha p) 11B to 12C (nu, nu' p) 11B cross sections: approximately 10% at T = 4 and 8 MeV
- Production yields of 11B and 11C enhanced by about 10% when multi-particle channels are included

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of neutrino-induced reactions on 12C using the SFO Hamiltonian (Suzuki et al., 2006) and on 13C and 40Ar to the case of 16O, which is important for water-based supernova neutrino detectors. The results are compared with continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) calculations by Kolbe et al. (2000). The inclusion of multi-particle emission channels, particularly the alpha p channel, enables improved predictions for nucleosynthesis of 11B and 11C in core-collapse supernova explosions, building on earlier nucleosynthesis work by Woosley et al. (1990) and Yoshida et al. (2008).
