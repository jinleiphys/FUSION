# eta d scattering in the region of the S11 resonance

> We have studied the reaction eta d -> eta d close to threshold within a nonrelativistic three-body formalism. We considered several eta N and NN models, in particular potentials with separable form, fitted to the low-energy eta N and NN data to represent the two-body interactions. We found that with realistic two-body interactions a quasibound state does not exist in this system, although there is an enhancement of the cross section by one order of magnitude, in the region near threshold, which is a genuine three-body effect not predicted within the impulse approximation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the intermediate density region (where the interparticle spacing is longer than the range of the interaction but shorter than the scattering length), the energy per particle and superfluid gaps scale with the Fermi energy and become independent of the scattering length and other details of the interaction, but this result is conditional on the system having a sufficiently small number of spin-isospin or hyperspin states (ν < 2.5) to avoid mechanical instability.

## Method

The authors employed a nonrelativistic many-body formalism using Galitskii's integral equations for the effective two-particle scattering amplitude in the medium, resumming ladder diagrams to account for multiple interactions. They calculated ground state energies from the on-shell effective scattering amplitude and superfluid gaps from the Cooper pairing pole in the in-medium scattering amplitude, considering systems with varying numbers of spin-isospin or hyperspin states (ν = 2, 3, 4, 7, 10). A finite-temperature phase diagram was constructed by adding thermal pressure in the high-temperature classical approximation.

## Key numbers

- For ν = 2 systems (neutron matter, atomic gases with two hyperspin states), the energy per particle at intermediate densities is positive: E/N = E_F [3/5 - (ν-1)c_1] with c_1 ≈ 0.40, giving E/N = E_F (0.6 - 0.4) = 0.2 E_F
- The critical number of degrees of freedom for stability is ν_c ≈ 2.5, above which the energy per particle becomes negative
- For the dilute limit (k_F|a| ≪ 1), the BCS gap is Δ = E_F (8/e²) exp(π/2k_Fa), where e ≈ 2.718
- For ν spins, the gap including spin fluctuations is Δ = E_F (8/e²) (4e)^(ν/3-1) exp(π/2ak_F)
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is a = -18.8 fm
- The triplet ⁶Li scattering length is a = -2160 Bohr radii
- The sound speed squared at intermediate densities is s² = (5/9)c_1(ν_c - ν)v_F²

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) theory for nuclear matter and ³He to the novel intermediate density regime where the scattering length is much larger than the range of the interaction. The results contradict earlier conjectures based on extrapolating the dilute limit expansion (Lenz, 1954; Huang and Yang, 1957) to higher densities, which overestimated spinodal instability regions. The paper enables experimental study of these effects in magnetically trapped cold Fermi gases (JILA, 1999) and predicts that superfluidity should be attainable in the intermediate density region for systems with few spin states, contrary to the situation in Bose gases where spinodal instabilities prevent pairing transitions (Baym, 1999).
