# Electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory

> The electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are calculated at order $q^4$ in the relativistic formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory. In order to obtain a consistent power counting for the renormalized diagrams we make use of the extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme which we have discussed in a recent paper. We analyze the Dirac and Pauli form factors, $F_1$ and $F_2$, as well as the electric and magnetic Sachs form factors, $G_E$ and $G_M$. Our results are compared with those obtained in the heavy-baryon approach and in the (relativistic) infrared regularization. In addition, the Sachs form factors are compared with experimental data. We confirm previous findings that a one-loop calculation of the electromagnetic form factors does not generate sufficient curvature for the Sachs form factors $G^p_E$, $G^p_M$, and $G^n_M$. Moreover, the electric form factor of the neutron is very sensitive to higher-order contributions.

## Key claim

At one-loop order (𝒪(q⁴)) in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory using the extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme, the calculated Sachs form factors Gᴾ_E, Gᴾ_M, and Gⁿ_M do not generate sufficient curvature to match experimental data beyond Q² ∼ 0.1 GeV², and the electric form factor of the neutron is very sensitive to higher-order contributions.

## Method

The authors calculated the Dirac (F₁) and Pauli (F₂) form factors of the nucleon at one-loop order (𝒪(q⁴)) using relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme. Loop diagrams with internal nucleon lines that violate standard power counting were identified and subtracted by expanding the modified minimal subtraction (MS-tilde) results in small quantities, with the subtraction terms absorbed into finite low-energy constants of the chiral Lagrangian. The low-energy constants were fitted to the anomalous magnetic moments and to the charge and magnetic mean square radii from the dispersion-theoretical analysis of Mergell (1995).

## Key numbers

- The dipole form factor Gᴰ(Q²) = [1 + Q²/(0.71 GeV²)]⁻² is used as a reference for comparison; the calculated form factors deviate from this dipole form beyond Q² ∼ 0.1 GeV².
- The charge and magnetic radii used for fitting are: rᴾ_E = 0.847 fm, rⁿ_E = -0.113 fm, rᴾ_M = 0.836 fm, rⁿ_M = 0.889 fm, taken from the dispersion-theoretical analysis of Mergell (1995).
- The anomalous magnetic moments at Q²=0 are: κ_p = 1.793 and κ_n = -1.913.
- Low-energy constants in the EOMS scheme at 𝒪(q⁴) are: c₂ = 2.66 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 2.45 GeV⁻¹, c̃₆ = 1.26 GeV⁻¹, c̃₇ = -0.13 GeV⁻¹, d₆ = -0.69 GeV⁻², d₇ = -0.50 GeV⁻², e₅₄ = 0.19 GeV⁻³, e₇₄ = 1.59 GeV⁻³.
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is F_π = 92.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors using the EOMS renormalization scheme introduced by Fuchs (2003), and compares results with the heavy-baryon approach of Bernard (1992) and Fearing (1997) and the infrared regularization method of Kubis (2000). The one-loop calculation confirms previous findings that nucleon-pion degrees of freedom alone are insufficient to describe form factors for Q² ≥ 0.1 GeV², and that higher-order contributions, either from two-loop calculations or from explicit inclusion of vector mesons as suggested by Kubis (2000), are necessary for a quantitative description.
