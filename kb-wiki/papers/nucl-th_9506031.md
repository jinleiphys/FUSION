# Electromagnetic couplings in a collective model of the nucleon

> We study the electromagnetic properties of the nucleon and its excitations in a collective model. In the ensuing algebraic treatment all results for helicity amplitudes and form factors can be derived in closed form in the limit of a large model space. We discuss nucleon form factors and transverse electromagnetic couplings in photo- and electroproduction, including transition form factors that can be measured at new electron facilities.

# Electromagnetic couplings in a collective model of the nucleon

## Key claim
The paper claims that electromagnetic form factors and helicity amplitudes for nucleon resonances can be derived in closed form using an algebraic collective model, but only in the limit of a large model space (N → ∞) and with a specific probability distribution for charge and magnetization along the string configuration.

## Method
The authors model the nucleon as a Y-shaped string configuration of three quarks, described algebraically using a U(7) spectrum generating algebra that combines geometric degrees of freedom with spin-flavor SU(6) and color SU(3). Electromagnetic transition operators are expressed in terms of U(7) generators, and matrix elements are evaluated in the large-N limit, then folded with an exponential probability distribution g(β) = (β²/2a³) exp(-β/a) to obtain distributed-string form factors. The scale parameter a is fixed by the proton charge radius, and for flavor-dependent calculations separate parameters a_u and a_d are fitted to proton and neutron charge radii.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius: a = ⟨r²⟩^{1/2}_p = 0.249 fm (used as scale parameter for the probability distribution)
- For flavor-dependent form factors: a_u = 0.258 fm and a_d = 0.285 fm (determined by fitting proton and neutron charge radii simultaneously)
- Elastic proton electric form factor: G_E^p = 1/(1 + k²a²)² (dipole form by construction)
- Large-k behavior: elastic form factor F(k) drops as k⁻⁴; transition form factors for rotational excitations drop as k⁻³
- Ratio of neutron to proton magnetic moments in photoproduction limit: μ_n/μ_p = -0.685, compared to calculated value -2/3 from the model
- Ratio of magnetic form factors at large Q² with flavor dependence: -0.54, compared to perturbative QCD expectation of -1/2 + O(ln Q²)

## Context
This work extends the algebraic collective model of baryons previously developed by the same authors (Bijker and Leviatan, 1995) to electromagnetic couplings, contrasting with the nonrelativistic quark potential model (NRQM) and relativized quark model (RQM) that emphasize single-particle quark dynamics. The collective approach is motivated by flux-tube string models and regularities in baryon spectra such as linear Regge trajectories and parity doubling. The results enable predictions for transition form factors measurable at new electron facilities with polarized photons and CW electron accelerators, and the closed-form expressions allow direct comparison with experimental data for resonances such as N(1520)D₁₃ and N(1680)F₁₅.
