# Enhanced adiabatic index for hot neutron-rich matter from microscopic nuclear forces

> We investigate the adiabatic index $Γ_{\mathrm{th}}$ of hot and dense nuclear matter from chiral effective field theory and find that the results are systematically larger than from typical mean field models. We start by constructing the finite-temperature equation of state from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces, which we then use to fit a class of extended Skyrme energy density functionals. This allows for modeling the thermal index across the full range of densities and temperatures that may be probed in simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, including the low-density inhomogeneous mixed phase. For uniform matter we compare the results to analytical expressions for $Γ_{\mathrm{th}}$ based on Fermi liquid theory. The correlation between the thermal index and the effective masses at nuclear saturation density is studied systematically through Bayesian modeling of the nuclear equation of state. We then study the behavior of $Γ_{\mathrm{th}}$ in both relativistic and non-relativistic mean field models used in the astrophysical simulation community to complement those based on chiral effective field theory constraints from our own study. We derive compact parameterization formulas for $Γ_{\mathrm{th}}$ across the range of densities and temperatures encountered in core collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, which we suggest may be useful for the numerical simulation community.

## Key claim

The adiabatic index of hot dense nuclear matter, computed from chiral effective field theory, is systematically larger than predictions from typical mean field models used in astrophysical simulations, with the enhancement correlated to the nucleon effective mass at saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed finite-temperature equations of state from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces using many-body perturbation theory in the Matsubara imaginary time formalism, then fitted these results to an extended Skyrme energy density functional with ten parameters to model uniform matter up to twice nuclear saturation density and temperatures below 30 MeV. They employed six different chiral nuclear potentials (N2LO450, N2LO500, N3LO414, N3LO450, N3LO500) and used the liquid drop model to describe the low-density inhomogeneous mixed phase. The thermal index was extracted from the ratio of thermal pressure to thermal energy density and compared to analytical expressions from Fermi liquid theory.

## Key numbers

- The adiabatic index from chiral EFT is systematically larger than from mean field models across the full range of densities and temperatures relevant for core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers
- The nucleon effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density lies in the range 0.74 < M*/M < 0.88 from the fitted Skyrme interactions
- The neutron effective mass in pure neutron matter at saturation density lies in the range 0.86 < M*/M < 1.19
- All constructed equations of state produce neutron stars with maximum mass M_max >= 1.97 solar masses, satisfying the constraint from Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013)
- The critical temperature for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is approximately T_c ~ 20 MeV, above which all nuclei dissociate
- The neutron drip density is n_drip = 2.4 x 10^(-4) fm^(-3)
- In the Fermi liquid theory limit with constant effective mass, the upper limit of the adiabatic index at infinite density becomes Gamma_th = 8/3, while for M*/M = 1 the value becomes Gamma_th = 5/3

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature equation of state calculations from chiral effective field theory by Wellenhofer (2014, 2015) and provides a parameterized form of the thermal index for use in astrophysical simulations. It contrasts with previous studies by Baiotti (2008) and Bauswein (2010, 2012) that employed a constant value of the thermal index (typically 1.5 or 2.0), and complements recent microscopic studies of the thermal index by Carbone (2019) and Lu (2019). The parameterization formulas and covariance matrices enable the numerical simulation community to incorporate realistic density- and temperature-dependent thermal effects in core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations, where thermal effects have been shown to modify the post-merger gravitational wave signal and remnant collapse time.
