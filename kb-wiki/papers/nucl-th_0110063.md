# Finite formation time effects in inclusive and semi-inclusive electro-disintegration of few-body nuclei

> Finite Formation Time (FFT) effects in the exclusive reaction ^4He(e,e'p)^3H at high values of Q^2 are introduced and discussed. It is shown that the minimum in the momentum distributions predicted by the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA), which is filled by the Glauber-type Final State Interaction (FSI), is completely recovered when FFT effects are taken into account. The semi-inclusive process ^4 He(e,e'p)X is also investigated.

# Finite formation time effects in inclusive and semi-inclusive electro-disintegration of few-body nuclei

## Key claim

The minimum in the momentum distribution predicted by the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) for the exclusive reaction ⁴He(e,e'p)³H, which is filled by Glauber-type Final State Interaction (FSI), is completely recovered when Finite Formation Time (FFT) effects are taken into account at sufficiently high Q². The FFT approach predicts that FSI effects vanish at moderately large Q², in contrast to the colour transparency model of Benhar et al. (2000), which does not lead to vanishing FSI at Q² = 20 (GeV/c)².

## Method

The authors extended the Glauber approach to FSI by incorporating a finite formation time factor J(z) = θ(z)(1 - exp(z x m M² / Q²)) into the Glauber operator, where M represents the average virtuality of the struck nucleon. They calculated distorted momentum distributions n_D(k_m) for both the exclusive reaction ⁴He(e,e'p)³H and the semi-inclusive reaction ⁴He(e,e'p)X using ⁴He and ³H wave functions from the Reid Soft Core V₈ interaction. Calculations were performed at parallel kinematics for Q² values of 2, 5, 10, and 20 (GeV/c)², with the average excitation mass m*_Av taken as 1.8 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The PWIA minimum in the exclusive momentum distribution occurs at missing momentum k ~ 2.2 fm⁻¹.
- At Q² = 20 (GeV/c)², FFT effects completely kill the Glauber-type FSI, fully recovering the PWIA minimum.
- Calculations were performed at Q² values of 2, 5, 10, and 20 (GeV/c)².
- The average excitation mass m*_Av was taken to be 1.8 GeV/c.
- The total proton-nucleon cross section σ_tot and the ratio α of real to imaginary parts of forward elastic pN scattering amplitude were taken at proper values of invariant mass from the Particle Data Group.

## Context

This work extends the FFT approach of Braun (2000) from inclusive quasi-elastic A(e,e'p)X processes to the exclusive ⁴He(e,e'p)³H and semi-inclusive ⁴He(e,e'p)X reactions. The results contradict the colour transparency model of Benhar et al. (2000), which analyzed the same ⁴He(e,e'p)³H reaction but did not find vanishing FSI at Q² = 20 (GeV/c)². The authors conclude that exclusive electron scattering off ⁴He could discriminate between different models of hadronic final state rescattering, and experimental observation of a dip in the cross section at k_m ~ 2.2 fm⁻¹ would validate the FFT prediction.
