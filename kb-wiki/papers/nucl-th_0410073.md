# Strength of the $Σ$ Single-Particle Potential in Nuclei From Semiclassical Distorted Wave Model Analysis of $(π^-,K^+)$ Inclusive Spectrum

> Semiclassical distorted wave model is developed to describe $(π^-,K^+)$ inclusive spectra related to $Σ^-$-formation measured at KEK with $p_π=1.2$ GeV/c. The shape and magnitude of the spectrum on $^{28}$Si target are satisfactorily reproduced by a repulsive $Σ$-nucleus potential, the strength of which is of the order of 30$\sim$50 MeV. This strength is not so strong as more than 100 MeV suggested by the estimation presented in the report of the experiment.

## Key claim

The repulsive Σ-nucleus potential needed to reproduce the KEK (π⁻,K⁺) inclusive spectrum on ²⁸Si is of the order of 30–50 MeV, which is not as strong as the more than 100 MeV suggested by the original experimental analysis. The authors note that this conclusion is preliminary before accounting for multi-step processes and medium modifications of the elementary amplitude.

## Method

The authors developed a semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) model for (π,K) inclusive reactions, in which the double differential cross section is expressed as an integral over the collision point R, with local classical momenta for the pion, kaon, and hyperon obtained from distorted wave functions. The bound nucleon in the target nucleus is described by a local Fermi gas model using the Thomas-Fermi approximation, and the elementary πN→KY transition strength is taken from parameterizations of experimental data. The hyperon is treated with a real Woods-Saxon potential, and the calculated spectrum is convoluted with a Lorentzian distribution (half-width 20 MeV for Σ) to account for the imaginary part of the hyperon optical potential.

## Key numbers

- The Σ single-particle potential strength that best reproduces the (π⁻,K⁺) spectrum on ²⁸Si at θ_K = 6° ± 2° with p_π = 1.2 GeV/c is 30–50 MeV (repulsive).
- The attractive Σ potential of -10 MeV fails by predicting much larger cross sections; stronger repulsive potentials (e.g., 90 MeV) tend to underestimate the cross section.
- The elementary π⁻p→K⁺Σ⁻ amplitude strength was normalized by a factor of 0.82 to match the KEK experimental data.
- The absorptive potential for the incident 1.2 GeV/c pion uses b₀ = 0.58 fm³; for the outgoing kaon, b₀ = (1/(2p_K)) (2.1(log p_K – 2) + 0.84) fm³ with p_K in fm⁻¹.
- The Lorentzian convolution half-width is taken as 5 MeV for Λ and 20 MeV for Σ, based on the imaginary parts of the single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter calculated with the quark-model potential FSS.

## Context

This work extends the SCDW model, previously applied to (p,p') and (p,n) inclusive spectra, to (π,K) hyperon production reactions. It contradicts the earlier DWIA analysis of the same KEK experiment (reported in the experimental presentation) which deduced a Σ potential as large as 100 MeV repulsive, and instead supports the repulsive magnitude of about 20 MeV suggested by Dabrowski (1990s) from BNL data and by Batty, Friedman and Gal (1990s) from Σ⁻ atomic data. The result is consistent with G-matrix calculations using the Kyoto-Niigata SU₆ quark model (Fujiwara et al., 1996–2001), which predicted a repulsive Σ potential of about 20 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter. The framework enables future analysis of (π⁻,K⁺) data on heavier nuclei to investigate isospin dependence of the Σ-nucleus potential, and can be extended to other inclusive reactions such as (K,π), (π,η), and (γ,K).
