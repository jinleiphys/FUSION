# Dynamics of Phase Transitions in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We present several possibilities offered by the reaction dynamics of dissipative heavy ion collisions to study in detail the symmetry term of the nuclear equation of state, $EOS$. In particular we discuss isospin effects on the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, {\it Isospin Distillation}, and on collective flows. We stress the importance of a microscopic relativistic structure of the effective interaction in the isovector channel. The possibility of an {\it early} transition to deconfined matter in high isospin density regions is also suggested. We finally select {\it Eleven} observables, in different beam energy regions, that appear rather sensitive to the isovector part of the nuclear $EOS$, in particular in more exclusive experiments.

# Dynamics of Phase Transitions in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The symmetry term of the nuclear equation of state can be probed through dissipative heavy ion collisions, with eleven specific observables identified as sensitive to the isovector channel; however, the actual sensitivity depends on the microscopic covariant structure of the effective interaction (scalar versus vector contributions) rather than merely on the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic transport simulations using the Relativistic Landau Vlasov method for the mean-field evolution and a Monte-Carlo procedure for the collision term, including inelastic processes for nucleon resonance production and absorption. They compared three relativistic mean-field parametrizations (RMF-ρ, RMF-(ρ+δ), and RMF-Dρ) that share the same saturation properties for symmetric nuclear matter but differ in the Lorentz structure of the isovector channel. The simulations were applied to reactions such as ¹³²Sn+¹³²Sn and Au+Au at beam energies from 300 AMeV to 1.5 AGeV.

## Key numbers
- ¹³²Sn asymmetry (N−Z)/A = 0.2 used for mean-field and chemical potential calculations.
- ¹³²Sn+¹³²Sn reaction at 1.5 AGeV with impact parameter b = 6 fm for differential flow and elliptic flow calculations.
- Proton fraction Z/A = 0.38 for ¹³²Sn, used in deconfinement transition density studies.
- Transition density ρ_trans as low as 2 ρ₀ for proton fractions between 0.3 and 0.4, depending on the hadronic EOS parametrization.
- Bag constant B¹/⁴ = 150 MeV and α_s = 0 used for the quark phase in deconfinement calculations.
- Au+Au central collisions at 0.6 AGeV and 1.0 AGeV for π⁻/π⁺ ratio and Δ resonance production.
- Rapidity range −0.3 ≤ y/y_proj ≤ 0.3 for elliptic flow analysis.
- Transverse momentum threshold p_t ≥ 0.5 GeV/c for high-density sensitivity in elliptic flow.

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions on asymmetric nuclear matter phases by Baran et al. (1998, 2001) and Colonna et al. (1998), and builds on non-relativistic flow studies by the same group (1998, 2000). The key advance is the demonstration that the Lorentz structure of the isovector interaction, specifically the balance between scalar (δ-like) and vector (ρ) contributions, produces distinct flow signatures that cannot be reproduced by tuning the density dependence alone, as shown by comparing RMF-(ρ+δ) with RMF-Dρ. The results enable experimental searches for deconfinement precursors in collisions of radioactive beams at intermediate energies (few hundred MeV per nucleon) and motivate more exclusive measurements of the eleven proposed observables.
