# The chiral phase transition and the axial anomaly

> To date numerical simulations of lattice QCD have not found a chiral phase transition of first order which is expected to occur for sufficiently light pions. We show how the restoration of an exact global chiral symmetry can strongly decrease the breaking of the approximate, anomalous $U_A(1)$ symmetry. This is testable on the lattice through simulations for one through four flavors. In QCD a small breaking of the $U_A(1)$ symmetry in the chirally symmetric phase generates novel experimental signals.

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that in vacuum, the effective coupling of the lowest-dimension $U_A(1)$-violating operator (the determinant term $\sim \det\Phi$) is small, while higher-order anomalous terms (such as $\sim (\det\Phi)^2$) dominate due to enhancement by powers of the chiral condensate; at the chiral phase transition, where the condensate vanishes, only the lowest-dimension term remains, and if it is small, the critical pion mass for a first-order transition is much smaller than mean-field predictions.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for chiral symmetry breaking in QCD-like theories, including $U_A(1)$-invariant terms up to sixth order in the chiral field $\Phi$ and $U_A(1)$-violating terms generated by topologically nontrivial fluctuations (instantons). They analyze how the effective anomalous couplings depend on temperature and the chiral condensate, and examine the consequences for the order of the chiral phase transition for $N_f = 1, 2, 3, 4$ flavors, comparing with lattice QCD results and functional renormalization group studies.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover temperature in QCD: $T_\chi \sim 156$ MeV (Kotov 2019, Jahn 2021, Borsanyi 2021, Chen 2022, Aarts 2023)
- The temperature where topological susceptibility transitions from quantum to semiclassical instanton behavior: $T_{\rm qu} \sim 300$ MeV
- Mean-field critical pion mass for three flavors (quark meson model, no vacuum fluctuations): $m_\pi^{\rm crit} \approx 150$ MeV (Schaefer 2008)
- Mean-field critical pion mass for three flavors (with vacuum fluctuations): $m_\pi^{\rm crit} \approx 86$ MeV (Resch 2017)
- Mean-field critical pion mass (chiral matrix model): $m_\pi^{\rm crit} \approx 110$ MeV (Skokov 2023)
- Functional renormalization group critical pion mass for three flavors: $m_\pi^{\rm crit} \approx 17$ MeV (Resch 2017)
- Lattice upper bounds on critical pion mass: $m_\pi^{\rm crit} < 50$ MeV (Bazavov 2017); $m_\pi^{\rm crit} < 100$ MeV (Kuramashi 2020); $m_\pi^{\rm crit} < 90$ MeV (Dini 2021)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing analysis of the chiral phase transition and its relation to the axial anomaly, originally framed by Pisarski (1983) and based on the 't Hooft instanton mechanism (1976). The authors challenge the standard assumption that the lowest-dimension $U_A(1)$-violating operator ($\det\Phi$) dominates near the transition, proposing instead that higher-order anomalous terms (e.g., $(\det\Phi)^2$) dominate in vacuum. This conjecture reconciles the absence of a first-order transition in lattice QCD (Bazavov 2017, Kuramashi 2020, Dini 2021, Cuteri 2021) with the small critical pion masses found in functional renormalization group studies (Resch 2017). The work enables tests on the lattice for one through four flavors, and predicts novel experimental signals in heavy-ion collisions, including a light $\eta'$ meson in the chirally symmetric phase and possible $CP$ violation via $\eta'$ condensation.
