# Nuclear Matter Spectral Functions by Transport Theory

> Quantum transport theory is used to calculate the nucleon spectral function in infinite nuclear matter. A self-consistent description is obtained by utilizing the relations between collision rates and correlation functions. Static and dynamical self-energies are taken into account in the single particle propagators. The real parts of the non-static self-energy contributions are calculated by dispersion theory thus conserving the analyticity of momentum distributions. The transport theoretical spectral functions, momentum distributions, occupation probabilities and responce functions are in close agreement with results of variational and other many-body theoretical calculations. The results indicate that the nucleon spectral functions are determined only by the average short-range correlation strength.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nucleon spectral functions in infinite nuclear matter are determined only by the average short-range correlation strength, and that a universal, momentum-independent matrix element can describe these correlations globally, provided a form factor is included to guarantee analyticity.

## Method

The authors used quantum transport theory to calculate the nucleon spectral function in infinite nuclear matter at saturation density. They solved the self-consistent relations between collision rates and correlation functions iteratively, including both static and dynamical (dispersive) self-energies in the single-particle propagators. The real parts of the non-static self-energy contributions were calculated via a subtracted dispersion relation to enforce analyticity, and an energy- and momentum-dependent form factor with cutoff \(\Lambda = m_N\) was introduced to multiply the average matrix element.

## Key numbers

* The average off-shell NN scattering amplitude was adjusted to \(\left(\overline{\vert{\cal{M}}_0\vert^2}\right)^{1/2} = 207\) MeV fm\(^3\), which compares well with the Landau-Migdal in-medium scattering amplitude of \(+221.0\) MeV fm\(^3\) from the Urbana interaction model.
* The constant matrix element corresponds to a total NN cross section of about 20 mb, accounting for roughly 2/3 of the commonly used value.
* The form factor cutoff was set to \(\Lambda = m_N\) (approximately 939 MeV), suppressing collisions at total energies and momenta beyond that scale.
* The spectroscopic factor \(Z(\omega,p)\) at the on-shell energy was found to be about 7% higher at the Fermi surface compared to the many-body calculations of Benhar et al.
* The static mean-field shift relative to earlier work (Lehr 2000) was about 52.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quantum transport approach to spectral functions presented in Lehr et al. (2000) by including dispersive self-energies and a form factor, which together restore analyticity and proper normalization of the spectral function. The results are in close agreement with many-body calculations by Benhar et al. (correlated basis function approach) and Ciofi degli Atti et al., and also compare well with Dirac-Brueckner calculations by de Jong and Lenske (1997) that included hole-hole propagation. The approach enables a non-perturbative summation of the series of np-mh intermediate states and supports the universality of short-range correlations in nuclear systems, with implications for off-shell transport theory, in-medium cross sections, and nuclear response functions.
