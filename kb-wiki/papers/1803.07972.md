# Photonuclear reaction as a probe for $α$-clustering nuclei in the quasi-deuteron region

> Photon-nuclear reaction in a transport model frame, namely an Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics (EQMD) model, has been realised at the photon energy of 70-140 MeV in the quasi-deuteron (QD) regime. For an important application, we pay a special focus on photonuclear reactions of $^{12}$C($γ$,np)$^{10}$B where $^{12}$C is considered as different configurations including $α$-clustering. Obvious differences for some observables have been observed among different configurations, which can be attributed to spatial-momentum correlation of a neutron-proton pair inside nucleus, and therefore it gives us a sensitive probe to distinguish the different configurations including $α$ clustering with the help of the photonuclear reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper claims that photonuclear reactions in the quasi-deuteron energy region (70–140 MeV) can serve as a sensitive probe to distinguish different nuclear configurations including α-clustering, but the authors acknowledge that their model cannot reproduce the fine structure of experimental missing energy spectra and does not exclude a configuration with largely overlapping 3-α clusters that would show properties similar to the spherical case.

## Method

The authors implemented photonuclear reactions within the Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics (EQMD) transport model for photon energies of 70–140 MeV in the quasi-deuteron regime. They prepared three different configurations of ¹²C (chain 3-α, triangle 3-α, and spherical) using the EQMD cooling process, then simulated photon absorption by a neutron-proton pair within one α-cluster using the ²H(γ,np) cross section parameterization of Rossi et al. The excited nucleus then evolved through the transport model to produce final-state observables for the ¹²C(γ,np)¹⁰B channel.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: 70–140 MeV (quasi-deuteron regime)
- RMS radii of ¹²C configurations: chain 2.71 fm, triangle 2.35 fm, sphere 2.23 fm (experimental ground state: 2.47 fm)
- Binding energies of ¹²C configurations: chain 7.17 MeV/nucleon, triangle 7.12 MeV/nucleon, sphere 7.60 MeV/nucleon (experimental ground state: 7.68 MeV/nucleon)
- The ²H(γ,np) differential cross section parameterization covers photon energies from 20 to 440 MeV in the center-of-mass frame

## Context

This work extends prior studies of photonuclear reactions in the giant dipole resonance region (15–40 MeV) into the quasi-deuteron regime, where the wavelength is comparable to the deuteron size. It builds on the quasi-deuteron mechanism introduced by Levinger and on previous experimental measurements of ¹²C(γ,2N) by McGeorge et al. and (γ,⁴He) by Doran et al. The study enables future experimental tests using monochromatic photon beams from tagged photon facilities or Compton backscattering gamma sources to distinguish α-clustering configurations in light nuclei.
