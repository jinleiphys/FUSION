# Connection between the Largest Lyapunov Exponent, Density Fluctuation and Multifragmentation in Excited Nuclear Systems

> Within a quantum molecular dynamics model we calculate the largest Lyapunov exponent (LLE), density fluctuation and mass distribution of fragments for a series of nuclear systems at different initial temperatures. It is found that the $LLE$ peaks at the temperature ("critical temperature") where the density fluctuation reaches a maximal value and the mass distribution of fragments is best fitted by the Fisher's power law from which the critical exponents for mass and charge distribution are obtained. The time-dependent behavior of the LLE and density fluctuation is studied. We find that the time scale of the density fluctuation is much longer than the inverse LLE, which indicates that the chaotic motion can be well developed during the process of fragment formation. The finite-size effect on "critical temperature" for nuclear systems ranging from Calcium to superheavy nuclei is also studied.

## Key claim

Within a quantum molecular dynamics model, the largest Lyapunov exponent (LLE) peaks at a "critical temperature" where density fluctuation is maximal and fragment mass distributions obey Fisher's power law, but the numerical identification of this temperature carries uncertainty because the saddle-point must be extracted from a fluctuating signal, especially for lighter nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model with a Skyrme effective interaction and Coulomb force to simulate excited nuclear systems from ⁴⁰Ca to ²⁹⁸114. For each system at a given initial temperature, they computed the local-in-time largest Lyapunov exponent over an ensemble of trajectories, the density fluctuation defined as σ²_ρ = (⟨ρ²⟩ − ⟨ρ⟩²)/⟨ρ⟩², and the mass and charge distributions of fragments identified by a coalescence algorithm with R₀ = 3.5 fm and P₀ = 300 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb at T = 11 MeV, the time scale for density fluctuation growth is approximately 150 fm/c, while the inverse largest Lyapunov exponent is approximately 40 fm/c.
- For systems heavier than ¹⁹⁷Au, the "critical temperature" approaches a constant value of about T_c = 11 MeV.
- The critical exponent for mass distribution τ_m ranges from 2.642 (²²⁶Ra) to 2.700 (²³⁸U), with χ² values between 0.510 and 1.353.
- The critical exponent for charge distribution τ_z ranges from 2.406 (²²⁶Ra) to 2.514 (¹²⁴Sn), with χ² values between 0.146 and 1.606.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at T_c = 11 MeV, the best fit to the mass spectrum yields χ² = 0.678 and to the charge spectrum yields χ² = 1.140.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Lyapunov exponents in Lennard-Jones clusters by Nayak and Ramaswamy (1995) and in lattice gas and Vlasov models by Gulminelli et al. (2000) and Baldo et al. (1995) to realistic nuclear systems with Skyrme and Coulomb forces. The finding that the LLE, density fluctuation, and power-law mass spectra all peak at the same temperature for systems from ⁴⁰Ca to superheavy nuclei enables a unified dynamical signature of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition and suggests that deterministic chaos underlies multifragmentation.
