# Hyperfine splitting in ordinary and muonic hydrogen

> We provide an accurate evaluation of the two-photon exchange correction to the hyperfine splitting of S energy levels in muonic hydrogen exploiting the corresponding measurements in electronic hydrogen. The proton structure uncertainty in the calculation of $α^5$ contribution is sizably reduced.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the hyperfine splitting (HFS) of S energy levels in muonic hydrogen can be evaluated with sizably reduced proton structure uncertainty by exploiting the corresponding precise measurements in electronic hydrogen, using a weighted difference that cancels the dominant Zemach contribution.

## Method

The author expresses the TPE correction in muonic hydrogen as the sum of the electronic hydrogen experimental TPE value (scaled by the reduced mass ratio) plus a residual difference of the theoretical TPE corrections for the two systems evaluated as a single integral over photon virtuality. The polarizability contribution is determined from the first moment of the proton spin structure function g1, expanded with a low-energy constant up to Q^2 = 0.0625 GeV^2, while the recoil contribution uses elastic form factor parametrizations from Bernauer (2010, 2013) and spin structure function parametrizations from Prok (2008), Kuhn (2008), and others.

## Key numbers

- The polarizability contribution to the weighted difference: 9.4 ± 19.1 ppm
- The recoil contribution to the weighted difference: -143.1 ± 3.2 ppm
- The total proton structure correction (recoil plus polarizability): -133.6 ± 20.0 ppm
- The extracted TPE correction in electronic hydrogen from experiment: -32.629 ± 0.033 ppm
- The predicted TPE correction in muonic hydrogen (conservative uncertainty): -6201 ± 49 ppm
- The predicted 1S hyperfine splitting energy in muonic hydrogen: 182.601 ± 0.013 meV
- The corresponding 1S hyperfine splitting frequency in muonic hydrogen: 44152.8 ± 3.2 GHz
- The predicted 2S hyperfine splitting energy in muonic hydrogen: 22.8102 ± 0.0016 meV
- The corresponding 2S hyperfine splitting frequency in muonic hydrogen: 5515.49 ± 0.40 GHz

## Context

This work extends the dispersive evaluation of the TPE correction to HFS in muonic hydrogen, previously performed by Carlson (2008, 2011) with 213 ppm uncertainty and by Tomalak (2017) with 105 ppm uncertainty, by using electronic hydrogen HFS measurements to reduce the proton structure uncertainty. The approach differs from Peset (2016) by not expanding in lepton mass and by weighting the difference with the reduced mass ratio rather than the lepton mass itself. The result enables improved predictions for the upcoming 1S HFS measurements in muonic hydrogen by the CREMA and FAMU collaborations and at J-PARC, and further uncertainty reduction is expected from proton spin structure studies at Jefferson Lab.
