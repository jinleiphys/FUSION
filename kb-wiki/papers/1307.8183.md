# Goldstone modes in the neutron star core

> We formulate a theory of Goldstone bosons and their interactions in the superfluid and superconducting phase of dense nucleonic matter at densities of relevance to the neutron star core. For typical neutron star temperatures in the range T = 10^6 to 10^9 K, the Goldstone mode associated with rotational symmetry, called angulons, couple weakly to each other and to electrons. Consequently, these modes have anomalously large mean free paths and can contribute to both diffusive and ballistic transport of heat and momentum. In contrast, the two Goldstone bosons associated with density oscillations of the neutron and electron + proton fluids, called superfluid phonons, mix and couple strongly to electrons. They have shorter mean free paths, and their contribution to transport is negligible. Long-wavelength superfluid phonons and angulons can play a role in neutron star seismology, and lead to interesting phenomenology as angulons couple to magnetic fields and have anisotropic dispersion relations.

## Key claim

In the superfluid and superconducting phase of dense nucleonic matter in neutron star cores, the Goldstone mode associated with rotational symmetry (angulons) couples weakly to electrons and to each other, leading to anomalously large mean free paths that can contribute to both diffusive and ballistic transport of heat and momentum, while the two superfluid phonon modes mix and couple strongly to electrons, resulting in negligible transport contributions.

## Method

The authors formulated a low-energy effective field theory of Goldstone bosons in the neutron star core, where neutrons form a \(^3P_2\) superfluid and protons form a \(^1S_0\) superconductor. They derived the Lagrangian for angulons (associated with rotational symmetry breaking) and for superfluid phonons (associated with density oscillations of neutron and electron+proton fluids), including their couplings to electrons and magnetic fields. Using this effective theory, they calculated dispersion relations, damping rates, and mean free paths for all Goldstone modes, and estimated their contributions to thermal conductivity and shear viscosity by solving the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

* Angulon mean free path: \(\lambda_{\rm ang}(\omega) \approx 1.7 \gamma^4 v_F^3 [k_{\rm Fe}/(100\ {\rm MeV})]^2 [10\ {\rm keV}/\omega]^3\ {\rm cm}\), where \(\gamma = v_\beta/v_F\) and \(v_\beta\) is the mean angulon velocity.
* Electron-proton phonon mean free path: \(\lambda_{\rm ep}(\omega = v_p q) \simeq 1.4\times 10^{-9}\ [10\ {\rm keV}/\omega]\ [0.3/v_p]\ {\rm cm}\).
* Thermally averaged mean free path for the electron-proton mode: \(\langle \lambda_{\rm ep}(T) \rangle \approx 10^{-9}\ (0.3/v_p)\ T_8^{-1}\ {\rm cm}\), where \(T_8\) is temperature in units of \(10^8\) K.
* Mean free path of the predominantly neutron superfluid phonon mode: \(\lambda_{\phi} \approx 10^{-5}\ (0.3/v)\ T_8^{-1}\ {\rm cm}\) to \(\lambda_{\phi} \approx 10^{-8}\ (0.3/v)\ T_8^{-1}\ {\rm cm}\).
* Angulon thermal conductivity: \(\kappa_{\rm ang} \approx 1.6 \times 10^{19}\ {\rm ergs/(cm\ s\ K)}\) for typical values \(\gamma \simeq 0.5-0.7\), \(v_F\simeq 1/3\), and \(\bar{x}\simeq 10^{-3}\).
* Phonon/angulon thermal conductivity formula: \(\kappa_{\rm phn/ang} = 1.7\times 10^{21}\ T_8^3\ (0.3/v)^3\ (\langle \lambda \rangle/{\rm cm})\ {\rm erg/(cm\ s\ K)}\).
* Angulon-angulon scattering mean free path: \(\lambda_{\rm ang-ang} \approx v_\beta^3 f_\beta^4/T^5\), which for \(T\lesssim 10^9\) K is much larger than the neutron star radius \(R \simeq 10\) km.
* Eigenmode velocities in units of speed of light for the equation of state from Gandolfi (2009): at neutron density \(n_n = 0.08\ {\rm fm}^{-3}\), \(v_1 = 0.12\) and \(v_2 = 0.17\); at \(n_n = 0.32\ {\rm fm}^{-3}\), \(v_1 = 0.28\) and \(v_2 = 0.71\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of angulons by Bedaque (2003) and Bedaque (2012), which first identified these Goldstone modes associated with rotational symmetry breaking in the \(^3P_2\) neutron superfluid. The authors build on the effective field theory approach for superfluid phonons developed by Son (2005), Pethick (2010), and Cirigliano (2011), and apply it to the neutron star core environment. The results enable systematic calculations of transport properties in neutron stars, including thermal conductivity and shear viscosity, and suggest that long-wavelength angulons and superfluid phonons could play a role in neutron star seismology, while also motivating further study of ballistic transport by long-wavelength modes.
