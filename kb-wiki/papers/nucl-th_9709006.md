# Study of odd-mass N=82 isotones with realistic effective interactions

> The microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model, MQPM, is used to study the energy spectra of the odd $Z=53 - 63$, N=82 isotones. The results are compared with experimental data, with the extreme quasiparticle-phonon limit and with the results of an unrestricted $2s1d0g_{7/2}0h_{11/2}$ shell model (SM) calculation. The interaction used in these calculations is a realistic two-body G-matrix interaction derived from modern meson-exchange potential models for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. For the shell model all the two-body matrix elements are renormalized by the $\hat{Q}$-box method whereas for the MQPM the effective interaction is defined by the G-matrix.

## Key claim

The microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (MQPM), using a realistic G-matrix interaction without phenomenological fitting, can reproduce the energy spectra of odd-mass N=82 isotones comparably to an unrestricted shell model calculation, provided the equations-of-motion (EOM) form of the coupling hamiltonian is used (which improves agreement with experimental data over the earlier MQPM version).

## Method

The MQPM was applied to the odd Z=53-63, N=82 isotones by constructing BCS quasiparticles and QRPA phonons from a realistic G-matrix interaction derived from the Bonn-A meson-exchange potential, using a larger single-particle space (including neutron degrees of freedom and proton core excitations across Z=50) than the shell model. The quasiparticle-phonon basis was diagonalized using the EOM method for the H31 coupling term, and results were compared to an unrestricted shell model calculation in the 2s1d0g7/20h11/2 proton model space (with the same G-matrix renormalized by the Q-box method) and to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 135I: MQPM gives the first 5/2+ state at 0.649 MeV (experiment: 0.604 MeV; SM: 0.508 MeV); the 11/2+ state at 1.543 MeV (experiment: 1.134 MeV; SM: 1.322 MeV).
- For 137Cs: MQPM gives the 5/2+ state at 0.404 MeV (experiment: 0.456 MeV; SM: 0.208 MeV); the 1/2+ state at 1.986 MeV (experiment: 2.150 MeV; SM: 2.093 MeV).
- For 139La: MQPM gives the 5/2+ state at 0.154 MeV (experiment: 0.166 MeV; SM: 0.061 MeV); the 11/2- state at 1.660 MeV (experiment: 1.420 MeV; SM: 1.770 MeV).
- For 141Pr: MQPM gives the 5/2+ state at 0.111 MeV (experiment: 0.145 MeV; SM: 0.093 MeV); the 11/2- state at 1.432 MeV (experiment: 1.297 MeV; SM: 1.521 MeV).
- For 143Pm: MQPM gives the 5/2+ state at 0.272 MeV (experiment: 0.272 MeV; SM: 0.131 MeV); the 11/2- state at 1.399 MeV (experiment: 1.392 MeV; SM: 1.399 MeV).
- For 145Eu: MQPM gives the 5/2+ state at 0.330 MeV (experiment: 0.330 MeV; SM: 0.170 MeV); the 11/2- state at 1.384 MeV (experiment: 1.384 MeV; SM: 1.384 MeV).
- The pairing gap used in the extreme quasiparticle-phonon picture was Delta = 12 A^{-1/2} MeV.

## Context

This work extends the comparative analysis of Holt (1997) on even N=82 isotones to the odd-mass case, using the same G-matrix interaction and model space. It contrasts the MQPM approach (which includes neutron degrees of freedom and a larger proton space) with the unrestricted shell model (which accounts for those degrees of freedom via perturbative renormalization), following earlier quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff calculations by Heyde and Waroquier (1970, 1971) using phenomenological interactions. The results enable systematic testing of how well microscopic effective interactions describe odd-mass nuclei and can guide future studies of collective excitations and transition probabilities in this mass region.
