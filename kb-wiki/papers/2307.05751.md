# Exponential Resummation of QCD at finite chemical potential

> A comprehensive study of the QCD phase diagram is one of the challenging and open problems in high energy physics. Having significant astrophysical implications, this is also important in constructing the chronological evolution of the universe. With this aim, this thesis describes the behaviour of thermodynamic observables like pressure and number density with changing chemical potential $μ$, through the method of an unbiased exponential resummation of lower order Taylor series of these observables at a finite $μ$. We address the problem of biased estimates, which manifest uncontrollably in exponential resummation and which become severe in the domain of large values, higher orders of $μ$ and also in observables which are higher $μ$ derivatives of the thermodynamic potential. We show that our new formalism of unbiased exponential resummation can eliminate these biased estimates exactly upto a given order of $μ$, and can capture important contributions of higher order Taylor series for all our working temperatures starting from hadronic phase to the plasma phase, including the crossover region. We also demonstrate that this new formalism is highly efficient in saving appreciable computational time and storage space for computations.

## Key claim

The thesis claims that a new formalism of "unbiased exponential resummation" can eliminate biased estimates that arise in standard exponential resummation of Taylor series for thermodynamic observables at finite chemical potential, exactly up to a given order of mu, and can capture important contributions of higher order Taylor series across all working temperatures from the hadronic phase to the plasma phase including the crossover region.

## Method

The author develops an unbiased exponential resummation method for the Taylor expansion of the QCD partition function at finite chemical potential, where the standard exponential resummation suffers from biased estimates that become severe at large mu values and higher orders. The formalism is constructed by redefining the argument of the exponential using cumulant-like combinations of the Taylor coefficients D_n, and the proof demonstrates that extracting the real part of the exponentiated series reproduces the exact Taylor coefficients for excess pressure up to O(mu^4). The method is applied to lattice QCD with 2+1 flavors using the HISQ discretization scheme, with the line of constant physics set by tuning bare quark masses to reproduce physical meson masses.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to finite density by addressing the sign problem through Taylor expansion methods, specifically improving upon standard exponential resummation techniques that introduce biased estimates. The formalism builds on the HotQCD collaboration's lattice setup (Bazavov 2014, Bollweg 2021) for the line of constant physics and enables more reliable computation of thermodynamic observables like pressure and number density at finite chemical potential across the QCD phase diagram, with implications for astrophysics and early universe cosmology. The method is demonstrated to reproduce exact Taylor coefficients up to fourth order, and the author notes that for orders n >= 5, the linear mu formalism is recommended as it saves computational time while preserving the underlying physics.
