# Pion condensation at rotation in magnetic field, electric and scalar potential wells

> Instability of the charged pion vacuum resulting in the pion condensation is studied within the $λ|ϕ|^4$ model at the rapid rotation of the system in presence of external fields. Case of the static external scalar and electric square potential wells and the uniform magnetic field is studied in detail. The Meissner and Aharonov-Bohm effects and the London moment are taken into account. Analogies and differences with the behavior of metallic superconductors under the action of the rotation and the external magnetic field are analysed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that charged pion condensation from the vacuum can occur under rapid rotation combined with external scalar, electric, and magnetic fields, but only when the Meissner effect, Aharonov-Bohm effect, and London moment are properly accounted for, conditions that previous treatments (Zahed, Guo) overlooked by treating the magnetic field as uniform and ignoring the self-consistent screening of the field by the condensate.

## Method
The author studies the instability of the charged pion vacuum within the λ|φ|⁴ model in a rigidly rotating cylindrical frame, solving the Klein-Gordon-Fock equation for the pion field in the presence of static external scalar and electric square potential wells and a uniform magnetic field. The analysis incorporates the Meissner effect (screening of magnetic field by the condensate), the Aharonov-Bohm effect, and the London moment (generation of a uniform magnetic field in a rotating superconductor), and compares the results to the behavior of metallic superconductors under rotation and external magnetic fields.

## Key numbers
- The London magnetic field for charged pions is estimated as h_L ≈ 3×10⁻¹⁷ G for Ω ≈ 10²² Hz, using the formula h_L = −(2m/q)Ω with m = m_π ≈ 140 MeV.
- The critical magnetic field scale for pions is H_π = m_π²/|e| ≈ 3.5×10¹⁸ G.
- The maximum magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions is estimated as h_max ∼ h_VA Z|e|, where h_VA = Z|e|/R² ∼ H_π (Ze⁶)^{1/3}.
- The rotation frequency observed by STAR in noncentral Au-Au collisions is Ω ≈ (9±1)×10²¹ Hz ≈ 0.05 m_π.
- The causality condition requires Ωr < 1, so the system radius R must satisfy R < 1/Ω.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of pion condensation in rotating systems by Zahed, Guo, and Voskresensky (2023, 2024), which considered the vacuum instability in the rotation frame but ignored the Meissner effect and London moment. The paper also draws analogies with the theory of metallic superconductors (Abrikosov, 1988; Landau and Lifshitz, 1981) and with the behavior of rotating superfluid ⁴He and cold atomic gases. The results are relevant for understanding pion condensation in neutron star interiors (Migdal, 1978; Migdal, 1990) and in heavy-ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, where rapid rotation and strong magnetic fields may occur.
