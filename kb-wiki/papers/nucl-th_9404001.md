# Nijmegen Soft-Core Potential Including Two-Meson Exchange

> We report on the progress of the construction of the extended soft-core (ESC) Nijmegen potential. Next to the standard one-boson-exchange parts, the model includes the pion-meson-exchange potentials due to the parallel and crossed-box diagrams, as well as the one-pair and two-pair diagrams, vertices for which can be identified with similar interactions appearing in chiral-symmetric Lagrangians. Although the ESC potential is still under construction, it already gives an excellent description of all $N\!N$ scattering data below 350 MeV with $χ^{2}/{\rm datum}=1.3$.

## Key claim

The extended soft-core (ESC) Nijmegen potential, which includes two-meson-exchange contributions beyond the standard one-boson-exchange (OBE) model, already gives an excellent description of all nucleon-nucleon scattering data below 350 MeV with a chi-squared per datum of 1.3, although the potential is still under construction and the fit is constantly being improved.

## Method

The authors constructed the ESC potential by starting from the Nijmegen OBE potential and adding two-meson-exchange potentials from Brueckner-Watson parallel and crossed-box diagrams, including pion-pion, pion-epsilon, pion-eta, pion-rho, and pion-omega exchanges, as well as one-pair and two-pair contributions. They attached Gaussian form factors to each vertex, with cutoff masses depending on the type of meson exchanged, and refitted the coupling constants and cutoffs to the chi-squared hypersurface of the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis of 1787 pp data and 2514 np data below 350 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The ESC potential achieves chi-squared per datum = 1.3 for all NN scattering data below 350 MeV.
- The database used for fitting contains 1787 pp data and 2514 np data.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant at the pion pole is f^2 = 0.072.
- The (f/g)_rho ratio is 4.0, close to the vector-meson dominance value of 3.7.
- The g_rho coupling constant is approximately 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen group's earlier OBE potentials, such as the Nijm78 potential (Nagels, Rijken, and de Swart, 1978) and the Nijm93 potential (Stoks, Klomp, Terheggen, and de Swart, 1994), which could not achieve chi-squared per datum lower than about 1.9 even after refitting. The ESC approach goes beyond the OBE model to include two-meson-exchange contributions, motivated by the fact that OBE potentials apparently lack some important physics; the authors intend to use SU(3) symmetry to extend this potential to a general baryon-baryon potential with only a minimal set of new free parameters.
