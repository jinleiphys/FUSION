# Corrections to deuterium hyperfine structure due to deuteron excitations

> We consider the corrections to deuterium hyperfine structure originating from the two-photon exchange between electron and deuteron, with the deuteron excitations in the intermediate states. In particular, the motion of the two intermediate nucleons as a whole is taken into account. The problem is solved in the zero-range approximation. The result is in good agreement with the experimental value of the deuterium hyperfine splitting.

## Key claim

The paper calculates corrections to the deuterium hyperfine splitting from two-photon exchange with deuteron excitations, finding that accounting for the motion of the intermediate proton-neutron system as a whole yields a total nuclear-structure correction of 50 kHz, which agrees with the 45 kHz discrepancy between pure QED calculations and the experimental value, though the authors note the zero-range approximation (zra) used is a crude nuclear model.

## Method

The authors compute the electron-deuteron spin-dependent scattering amplitude from two-photon exchange in the gauge \(A_0 = 0\), using the zero-range approximation (zra) for the deuteron wave function. They evaluate nuclear matrix elements for the deuteron Compton amplitude, including contributions from convection and spin currents, and integrate over intermediate states that are plane waves (for all \(l \neq 0\) states) as well as a corrected \(^3S_1\) wave function that accounts for deviation from the free form. The calculation explicitly includes the kinetic energy of the proton-neutron system as a whole in the intermediate states, which modifies the energy denominator from \((p^2+\varkappa^2)/m_p\) to \((p^2+k^2/4+\varkappa^2)/m_p\).

## Key numbers

* Experimental deuterium hyperfine splitting: \(\nu_{\rm exp} = 327\,384.352\,522\,2(17)\) kHz (atomic deuterium maser)
* Pure QED theoretical value: \(\nu_{\rm QED} = 327\,339.27(7)\) kHz
* Discrepancy: \(\nu_{\rm exp} - \nu_{\rm QED} = 45\) kHz
* Inverse deuteron size: \(\varkappa = 45.7\) MeV
* Deuteron magnetic moment: \(\mu_d = 0.857\)
* Proton magnetic moment: \(\mu_p = 2.79\)
* Neutron magnetic moment: \(\mu_n = -1.91\)
* Hadronic scale: \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV
* Total nuclear-structure correction to hyperfine splitting: \(\Delta \nu = 50\) kHz

## Context

This work extends the systematic treatment of nuclear-structure corrections to deuterium hyperfine splitting from Khriplovich and Milstein (previous paper, cited as Ref. [mkp]), which derived the effective Hamiltonian from elastic forward Compton scattering and included corrections from deuteron virtual excitations due to spin currents. The present paper corrects and extends that analysis by accounting for the motion of the intermediate proton-neutron system as a whole, yielding a new term proportional to \(\mu_p+\mu_n\) that was absent in the earlier work. The result agrees with the earlier estimate of Low (cited as Ref. [low]) for the leading term, though the authors note their arguments differ. The calculation enables a more sensitive test of detailed deuteron structure models, as the authors note that a recent paper by Mart (cited as Ref. [mart]) using phenomenological form factors obtained a smaller elastic contribution by 13 kHz.
