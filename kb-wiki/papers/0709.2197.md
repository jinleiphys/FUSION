# Scalar and vector meson propagation in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The propagation of the scalar (σand δ) and vector (ωand ρ) mesons in an iso-asymmetric nuclear matter is studied in detail, using the Walecka model. We calculate the invariant masses and spectral functions of the mesons, including the effect of meson mixing. At finite density, the mixing effect is quite important in the propagation of the scalar and (longitudinal) vector mesons. In the σchannel, we find a three-peak structure in the spectral function, caused by the mixing effect.

## Key claim

Using the Walecka model (quantum hadrodynamics), the authors show that meson mixing effects are crucial for the propagation of scalar and longitudinal vector mesons in iso-asymmetric nuclear matter, and that in the sigma channel a three-peak structure appears in the spectral function at finite three-momentum transfer, which the abstract notes but does not qualify as occurring only at finite momentum and depending on the proton fraction.

## Method

The authors solve the nuclear ground state within the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) and compute the full meson propagators by summing ring diagrams of one-loop polarization insertions, including mixing between all four meson channels (sigma, omega, delta, rho). They regularize divergent Feynman parts using dimensional regularization and renormalization conditions, and include pion-loop contributions for the sigma and rho mesons and an eta-pi loop for the delta meson. The coupling constants are fixed to reproduce saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy -16 MeV at rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3), the symmetry energy (30 MeV), and vacuum decay widths.

## Key numbers

* Effective nucleon mass at saturation density: M*/M = 0.72 at rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3
* Incompressibility: K = 465 MeV
* Coupling constants determined: g_sigma = 8.119, g_omega = 9.506, g_rho = 4.445, g_delta = 3.732, g_sigma_pi = 23.65, g_delta_pi_eta = 0.4385, g_rho_pi = 6.014
* Free meson masses used: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 769 MeV, m_delta = 983 MeV, m_eta = 548 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV
* Vacuum decay widths used: Gamma_sigma^0 = 500 MeV, Gamma_rho^0 = 150 MeV, Gamma_delta^0 = 59 MeV, Gamma_omega^0 = 9.8 MeV
* Vacuum rho-omega mixing amplitude: <rho|H_mix|omega> at q_mu^2 = m_omega^2 = -4520 MeV^2
* At rho_B/rho_0 = 2 and proton fraction f_p = 0.3, the sigma spectral function shows three peaks: two near m_inv* ~ m_sigma (550 MeV) and a third around m_inv* ~ 0.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Saito et al. (same group, year not given) on sigma and omega meson propagation in symmetric nuclear matter to the case of iso-asymmetric matter (Z not equal to N), including the isovector delta and rho mesons. It also builds on the treatment of charge symmetry breaking by Will (year not given) and Mori (year not given). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted three-peak structure in the sigma channel spectral function, which could be probed via dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions.
