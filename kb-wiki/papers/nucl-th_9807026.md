# Nonperturbative QCD with Modern Tools

> I introduce and explore a range of topics of contemporary interest in hadronic physics: from what drives the formation of a nonzero quark condensate to the effect that mechanism has on light and heavy meson form factors, and the properties of the quark-gluon plasma. The trail leads naturally through a discussion of confinement, dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and bound state structure: phenomena that require nonperturbative methods for their explanation. In all of this, the necessary momentum-dependent modification of the quark and gluon propagators plays a significant role.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) and confinement in QCD can be understood through the momentum-dependent modification of quark and gluon propagators, which are accessible via Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) even though perturbation theory alone cannot generate a nonzero quark condensate in the chiral limit.

## Method

The author solves the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (the QCD "gap equation") for the dressed-quark self-energy by iteration, using a dressed-gluon propagator and a dressed-quark-gluon vertex as input. The gluon propagator is taken in Landau gauge with a form that enhances the interaction near zero momentum, representing the dominance of gluon self-interactions over ghost-loop contributions. The quark DSE is solved in the chiral limit (current-quark mass set to zero) to test whether a nontrivial solution for the scalar function B(p²) emerges, which would signal DCSB.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: M_u ≈ M_d ∼ 320 MeV, M_s ∼ 400 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 140 MeV; rho meson mass: m_ρ ≈ 770 MeV
- Mass ratios from the paper: m_ρ²/m_π² = 30, m_N²/m_π² = 45, m_N²/m_ρ² = 1.5
- Neutron charge radius: r²_n = -0.113 ± 0.003 fm²
- Kaon charge radius: r²_{K⁰} = -0.054 ± 0.026 fm²
- QCD scale: Λ_QCD ∼ 200 MeV
- Running coupling at Q = 34 GeV: α_S = 0.142 ± 0.03
- DCSB threshold: the quark DSE admits a B(p²) ≠ 0 solution when the effective coupling at zero momentum satisfies G(Q=0) ≥ 1

## Context

This lecture-style paper reviews the nonperturbative Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD, extending earlier work on the gluon propagator by Brown and Pennington (1989) and on quark propagators by Maris and Roberts (1997). It contrasts the DSE method with perturbative QCD, which fails to describe confinement, DCSB, and bound state structure. The approach enables calculation of hadron form factors and properties of the quark-gluon plasma, and is complementary to lattice QCD simulations. The paper surveys experimental facilities (HERA, TJNAF, CESR, RHIC, CERN SpS, LHC) that will test these nonperturbative predictions.
