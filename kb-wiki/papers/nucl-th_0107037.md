# Space-time evolution and HBT analysis of relativistic heavy ion collisions in a chiral SU(3) x SU(3) model

> The space-time dynamics and pion-HBT radii in central heavy ion-collisions at CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC are investigated within a hydrodynamic simulation. The dependence of the dynamics and the HBT-parameters on the EoS is studied with different parametrisations of a chiral SU(3) sigma-omega model. The selfconsistent collective expansion includes the effects of effective hadron masses, generated by the nonstrange and strange scalar condensates. Different chiral EoS show different types of phase transitions and even a crossover. The influence of the order of the phase transition and of the difference in the latent heat on the space-time dynamics and pion-HBT radii is studied. A small latent heat, i.e. a weak first-order chiral phase transition, or even a smooth crossover leads to distinctly different HBT predictions than a strong first order phase transition. A quantitative description of the data, both at SPS energies as well as at RHIC energies, appears difficult to achieve within the ideal hydrodynamical approach using the SU(3) chiral EoS. A strong first-order quasi-adiabatic chiral phase transition seems to be disfavored by the pion-HBT data from CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC.

## Key claim

A quantitative description of pion-HBT data at both CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC energies appears difficult to achieve within ideal hydrodynamics using the chiral SU(3) equation of state, but a strong first-order quasi-adiabatic chiral phase transition is specifically disfavored by the data, while a weak first-order transition or smooth crossover provides a better description.

## Method

The authors performed ideal relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of central heavy-ion collisions with longitudinal scaling flow and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion, using three different parametrizations of a chiral SU(3) x SU(3) sigma-omega model as the equation of state. The equations of state were derived in mean-field approximation from a Lagrangian including baryon octet and decuplet, pseudoscalar and vector meson nonets, with effective hadron masses generated by nonstrange and strange scalar condensates. Pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlation functions were computed on freeze-out hypersurfaces of fixed temperature T_f, and the resulting radii R_out and R_side were extracted from Gaussian fits to the correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Latent heat for parameter set CII (first-order transition): approximately 600 MeV/fm^3 at zero chemical potential
- Latent heat for parameter set CIII (two first-order transitions): approximately 850 + 920 = 1770 MeV/fm^3 at zero chemical potential
- Critical temperature T_c for CII: 156.3 MeV; for CIII first transition: 153.4 MeV; for CIII second transition: 155.5 MeV (at mu_q = mu_s = 0)
- Initial energy density at SPS: 49.2 epsilon_0 (CI), 40.2 epsilon_0 (CII), 37.3 epsilon_0 (CIII), where epsilon_0 = 138.45 MeV/fm^3
- Initial energy density at RHIC: 127.9 epsilon_0 (CI), 100.4 epsilon_0 (CII), 93.7 epsilon_0 (CIII)
- Initial temperature at SPS: 256.0 MeV (CI), 197.0 MeV (CII), 180.6 MeV (CIII)
- Initial temperature at RHIC: 313.3 MeV (CI), 242.0 MeV (CII), 230.0 MeV (CIII)
- Initial net baryon density at SPS: 4.5 rho_0; at RHIC: 2.3 rho_0
- Specific entropy s/rho_B = 45 at SPS, s/rho_B = 200 at RHIC
- Net baryon rapidity density dN_B/dy = 80 at SPS, dN_B/dy = 25 at RHIC
- Initial proper time tau_i = 1 fm/c at SPS, tau_i = 0.6 fm/c at RHIC
- Transverse radius R_T = 6 fm for initial wounded nucleon distribution

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic HBT studies that used bag model equations of state with large latent heat (Schlei et al., RiGy) by employing a chiral SU(3) model that can produce phase transitions with varying latent heat or smooth crossover. The results contradict the expectation from bag-model-based calculations that a first-order phase transition leads to large HBT radii; instead, a strong first-order transition is disfavored by NA49 data at SPS and STAR data at RHIC. The approach enables future studies incorporating dissipative effects, resonance decays, and coupling of longitudinal and transverse flows, as well as investigations of strangeness distillation and kaon correlations in the mixed phase.
