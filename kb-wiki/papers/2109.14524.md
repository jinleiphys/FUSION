# Search for a bound di-neutron by comparing $^3$He(e,e'p)d and $^3$H(e,e'p)X measurements

> We report on a search for a bound di-neutron by comparing electron-induced proton-knockout $(e,e'p)$ measurements from Helium-3 ($^3$He) and Tritium ($^3$H). The measurements were performed at Jefferson Lab Hall A with a 4.326 GeV electron beam, and kinematics of large momentum transfer $Q^2 \approx 1.9$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$ and $x_B>1$, to minimize contributions from non quasi-elastic (QE) reaction mechanisms. Analyzing the measured $^3$He missing mass ($M_{miss}$) and missing energy ($E_{miss}$) distributions, we can distinguish the two-body break-up reaction, in which the residual proton-neutron system remains bound as a deuteron. In the $^3$H mirror case, under the exact same kinematic conditions, we do not identify a signature for a bound di-neutron with similar binding energy to that of the deuteron. We calculate exclusion limits as a function of the di-neutron binding energy and find that, for binding equivalent to the deuteron, the two-body break-up cross section on $^3$H is less than 0.9% of that on $^3$He in the measured kinematics at the 95% confidence level.

## Key claim

The paper reports no evidence for a bound di-neutron system in tritium under kinematic conditions where a bound deuteron is clearly observed in helium-3, but the result is conditional: the exclusion limits depend on the assumed binding energy of the hypothetical di-neutron, and for binding energies equivalent to the deuteron (2.2 MeV), the two-body break-up cross section on tritium is constrained to be less than 0.9% of that on helium-3 at the 95% confidence level.

## Method

The experiment used a 4.326 GeV electron beam at Jefferson Lab Hall A to measure quasi-elastic (e,e'p) knockout reactions on tritium and helium-3 targets at large momentum transfer (Q^2 approximately 1.9 (GeV/c)^2) and x_B > 1. The missing energy and missing mass distributions were analyzed to identify two-body break-up events, where the residual A-1 system remains bound; for helium-3 this yields a deuteron, while for tritium it would yield a di-neutron. A likelihood analysis with nuisance parameters for normalization, helium contamination in the tritium target, and detection efficiency as a function of di-neutron binding energy was used to set exclusion limits on the ratio of di-neutron to deuteron production cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Electron beam energy: 4.326 GeV
- Central four-momentum transfer: Q^2 = 2.0 (GeV/c)^2
- Bjorken x_B = 1.4
- Missing energy signal region for di-neutron search: E_miss < 7 MeV
- Helium contamination in tritium target: 2.78% plus or minus 0.18%
- Relative normalization factor between tritium and helium-3 data sets: n = 1.632 plus or minus 0.041 (2.5% uncertainty)
- For binding energy equal to the deuteron (2.2 MeV), the two-body break-up cross section on tritium is less than 0.9% of that on helium-3 at the 95% confidence level
- This limit implies the di-neutron content of the tritium spectral function is less than 1.5%

## Context

This work extends the long history of searches for multi-neutron bound states, including early studies by Kundu (1948) and Cohen (1953), and more recent experimental searches such as those by Spyrou (2012) and Kisamori (2016). The technique of hard proton knockout from tritium provides a new approach that avoids the need for direct neutron-neutron scattering or charge-exchange reactions in deuteron targets as used by Witala (2012). The results enable tighter constraints on theoretical predictions from lattice QCD calculations by Beane (2011) and pion-less Effective Field Theory by Hammer (2014), and have implications for nucleon-nucleon interactions and Big Bang nucleosynthesis models.
