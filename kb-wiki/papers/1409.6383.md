# Spin Polarization versus Color-Flavor-Locking in High Density Quark Matter

> It is shown that spin polarization with respect to each flavor in three-flavor quark matter occurs instead of the color-flavor locking at high baryon density by using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with four-point tensor-type interaction. Also, it is indicated that the order of phase transition between the color-flavor locked phase and the spin polarized phase is the first order by means of the second order perturbation theory.

## Key claim

Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a four-point tensor-type interaction, spin polarization for each flavor in three-flavor quark matter is energetically favored over color-flavor locking at high baryon density, and the phase transition between the color-flavor locked phase and the spin polarized phase is first order according to second-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors start from an NJL-type Lagrangian density that includes a tensor-type four-point interaction (with coupling constant G) and a quark-pairing interaction (with coupling constant G_c) for color superconductivity. They apply the mean-field approximation to derive the effective Hamiltonian, then compute the thermodynamic potential separately for the color-flavor locked phase (with spin polarization order parameters F_3 = F_8 = 0) and for the spin polarized phase (with color superconducting gap Δ = 0). The gap equation for Δ in the CFL phase is solved, and the thermodynamic potential for the SP phase is obtained as a function of the spin polarization order parameters F_3 and F_8. The order of the phase transition between the two phases is analyzed using second-order perturbation theory, treating the tensor-type interaction as a perturbation on the CFL ground state.

## Key numbers

- The gap equation for the CFL phase is given by \(1 = \frac{2G_c}{3\pi^2} \int_0^\Lambda dp\, p^2 \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{(p-\mu)^2+4\Delta^2}} + \frac{2}{\sqrt{(p-\mu)^2+\Delta^2}} \right)\), where \(\Lambda\) is a three-momentum cutoff and \(\mu\) is the quark chemical potential.
- The thermodynamic potential for the CFL phase (with Δ ≠ 0) is \(\Phi_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_0^\Lambda dp\, p^2 \left( 9(p-\mu) - \sqrt{(p-\mu)^2+4\Delta^2} - 8\sqrt{(p-\mu)^2+\Delta^2} \right) + \frac{3\Delta^2}{2G_c}\).
- The thermodynamic potential for the SP phase (with Δ = 0) is \(\Phi_F = \frac{1}{2G}(F_3^2+F_8^2) - \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \sum_{\tau} \left[ \frac{\sqrt{\mu^2-{\cal F}_\tau^2}}{4}(3{\cal F}_\tau^2\mu+2\mu^3) + {\cal F}_\tau\mu^3 \arctan\left(\frac{{\cal F}_\tau}{\sqrt{\mu^2-{\cal F}_\tau^2}}\right) - \frac{{\cal F}_\tau^4}{8} \ln\left(\frac{(\mu+\sqrt{\mu^2-{\cal F}_\tau^2})^2}{{\cal F}_\tau^2}\right) \right]\), where \({\cal F}_u = F_3 + F_8/\sqrt{3}\), \({\cal F}_d = -F_3 + F_8/\sqrt{3}\), and \({\cal F}_s = -2F_8/\sqrt{3}\).
- For the CFL phase with Δ = 0, the thermodynamic potential is \(\Phi_0(\Delta=0) = -\frac{3\mu^4}{8\pi^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of spin polarization in two-flavor quark matter (Tsue et al., 2013) to the three-flavor case, where the color-flavor locked phase is the expected ground state at high baryon density. It also builds on earlier work by Tatsumi (2000) suggesting spin polarization at high density due to pseudovector interactions, and by Maedan (2007) showing that spin polarization disappears in the zero quark mass limit. The results indicate that the spin polarized phase can compete with the CFL phase when tensor-type interactions are included, and the first-order nature of the transition between them is established via perturbation theory, enabling future studies of the phase diagram of dense quark matter.
