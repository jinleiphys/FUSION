# Coherent elastic scattering of low energy photons by neutrons

> The Compton process with the initial states of photons and neutrons described by the density matrices of a general form is studied for low energies of photons. The coherent contribution to the inclusive probability to record a photon is investigated in detail. This contribution gives the hologram of the neutron one-particle density matrix. The evolution of the Stokes parameters of scattered photons is described. The susceptibility tensor of a neutron gas and a wave packet of a single neutron is obtained. The explicit expression for the photon polarization operator in the presence of free neutrons is derived. It turns out that this polarization operator possesses pole singularities in the short wavelength approximation. These singularities corresponding to the additional degrees of freedom are identified with plasmons and the respective plasmon-polaritons are described. There are eight independent plasmon-polariton modes in a neutron gas and on a single neutron wave packet. Some plasmon-polariton modes prove to be tachyonic and unstable manifesting a spontaneous generation of the magnetic field. The estimates of the parameters of the neutron gas when it becomes ferromagnetic are found. In the infrared limit, the neutron wave packet behaves in coherent Compton scattering as a point particle with dynamical magnetic moment, the additional degrees of freedom being reduced to the dynamical part of the magnetic moment.

## Key claim

The coherent contribution to the inclusive probability to record a photon in elastic Compton scattering off neutrons gives the hologram of the neutron one-particle density matrix; this contribution is of lower order in the coupling constant than the standard incoherent contribution entering the differential cross section, and it is substantially different from zero only for small transferred momenta of order the standard deviation of momenta in the photon one-particle density matrix.

## Method

The authors use the in-in perturbation theory to compute the inclusive probability to record a photon after coherent elastic Compton scattering by neutrons, with initial states of both photons and neutrons described by general density matrices. They derive the one-loop photon polarization operator out of the photon mass-shell for a neutron gas and a single-neutron wave packet, and analyze its pole singularities in the short-wavelength approximation to identify plasmon and plasmon-polariton modes. The effective Maxwell equations are then examined to study the dispersion and stability of these modes.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous magnetic moment of the neutron is given as \(\mu_p = -1.91 |e|/(2M)\).
- For a nondegenerate gas of ultracold neutrons at a temperature of \(1.06\) neV, the ferromagnetic transition occurs for concentrations of order \(0.5\) mol/cm\(^3\) and larger.
- This concentration is stated to be "by fifteen orders smaller than the nuclear concentration."
- There are eight independent plasmon-polariton modes in a neutron gas and on a single neutron wave packet.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on coherent scattering holograms for electrons and photons (Kazinski and Sokolov, 2022, 2023; AKS, 2025) to the case of neutrons, where the dominant electromagnetic interaction is via the anomalous magnetic moment rather than charge. The analysis reveals that, unlike the degenerate free neutron gas at nuclear densities where ferromagnetic instability is absent (Delsante, 1979; Anand, 1981), a nondegenerate ultracold neutron gas can become ferromagnetic at sufficiently low temperatures and high densities. The results enable experimental probing of neutron quantum states via coherent Compton scattering holography and predict new collective excitations (plasmon-polaritons) even on a single-neutron wave packet.
