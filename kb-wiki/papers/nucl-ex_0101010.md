# S17(0) Determined from the Coulomb Breakup of 83 MeV/nucleon 8B

> A kinematically complete measurement was made of the Coulomb dissociation of 8B nuclei on a Pb target at 83 MeV/nucleon. The cross section was measured at low relative energies in order to infer the astrophysical S factor for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction. A first-order perturbation theory analysis of the reaction dynamics including E1, E2, and M1 transitions was employed to extract the E1 strength relevant to neutrino-producing reactions in the solar interior. By fitting the measured cross section from Erel = 130 keV to 400 keV, we find S17(0) = 17.8 (+1.4, -1.2) eV b.

## Key claim

The astrophysical S factor for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction at zero energy, S17(0), is determined to be 17.8 (+1.4, -1.2) eV b from Coulomb breakup data, but this result depends on restricting the analysis to relative energies between 130 keV and 400 keV and on properly accounting for E2 transitions, which were neglected in prior Coulomb breakup analyses.

## Method

A kinematically complete measurement of the Coulomb dissociation of 83 MeV/nucleon 8B projectiles on a Pb target was performed using a dipole magnet and multiwire drift chambers to reconstruct the 4-momenta of breakup fragments. Only events with 8B scattering angles of 1.8 degrees or less were analyzed to minimize nuclear contributions and E2 effects. A first-order perturbation theory analysis including E1, E2, and M1 transitions was employed, with E2 matrix elements quenched by a factor of 0.49 relative to the theoretical model of Esbensen and Bertsch (1995-1996), and the M1 contribution calculated from the measured M1 S factor of Filippone et al. (1983).

## Key numbers

- S17(0) = 17.8 (+1.4, -1.2) eV b, extracted from fitting the Coulomb breakup cross section from Erel = 130 keV to 400 keV
- S17(0) = 19.1 (+1.5, -1.8) eV b, extracted from fitting data from Erel = 130 keV to 2 MeV
- Best-fit normalization factor for the 130-400 keV range was 0.93 (+0.05, -0.04)
- Best-fit normalization factor for the 130 keV to 2 MeV range was 1.00 (+0.02, -0.06)
- The ratio of E2 to E1 matrix element scaling factors was 0.49 (corrected from the earlier reported value of 0.7)
- The ratio of E2 to E1 S factors at Erel = 0.6 MeV was 4.7 (+2.0, -1.3) x 10^(-4)
- The 1-sigma relative energy resolution ranged from 100 keV at Erel = 300 keV to 250 keV at Erel = 1.5 MeV
- The 1-sigma resolution in reconstructed 8B scattering angle was 4.5 mrad
- The 1-sigma proton momentum resolution was 4 MeV/c
- Total systematic uncertainty was 7.5%, including beam intensity (1%), target thickness (2.6%), momentum calibration (4.2%), and theoretical uncertainty (5.6%)

## Context

This work resolves longstanding discrepancies among direct measurements of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B S factor, which had been the most uncertain input to calculated solar neutrino fluxes since 1964 (Bahcall, Basu, and Pinsonneault, 1998). The result agrees with three direct capture measurements (Hammache et al., 1998; Filippone et al., 1983; Vaughn et al., 1970), the RIKEN Coulomb breakup measurement (18.9 +/- 1.8 eV b, Kikuchi et al., 1998), and asymptotic normalization coefficient determinations (17.3 +/- 1.8 eV b, Azhari et al., 1999). The 15% lower E1 strength compared to the GSI Coulomb breakup measurement (Iwasa et al., 1999) is attributed to the neglect of E2 transitions in that analysis, which had a sigma_E2/sigma_E1 ratio about 4 times larger than in the present measurement. This work enables consistent determination of the 8B solar neutrino flux and constrains fundamental neutrino properties.
