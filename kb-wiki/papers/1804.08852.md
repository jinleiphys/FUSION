# Intertwined effects of pairing and deformation on neutron halos in magnesium isotopes

> Matter radii of the $^{34-40}$Mg nuclei are investigated by self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations assuming the axial symmetry. With the semi-realistic M3Y-P6 interaction, the $N$-dependence of the matter radii observed in the experiments is reproduced excellently. Both the pairing and the deformation play significant roles in an intertwined manner. The $^{35}$Mg nucleus has a smaller radius than the neighboring even-$N$ nuclei, which is attributed to its smaller deformation. In contrast, a neutron halo is obtained in $^{37}$Mg. We point out that, in contrast to the pairing anti-halo effect that may operate on the even-$N$ nuclei, the pair correlation enhances halos in odd-$N$ nuclei, owing to the new mechanism which we call \textit{unpaired-particle haloing}. The halo in $^{37}$Mg is predicted to have peanut shape in its intrinsic state, reflecting $p$-wave contribution, as in $^{40}$Mg. The $N$-dependence of the deformation is significant again, by which the single-particle level dominated by the $p$-wave component comes down.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, contrary to the pairing anti-halo effect which suppresses halos in even-N nuclei, pair correlation enhances halos in odd-N nuclei through a mechanism called "unpaired-particle haloing," but this mechanism depends on the deformation being sufficiently large to allow the last neutron to occupy a specific p-wave dominated level.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent axial Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations for 34-40Mg isotopes using the semi-realistic M3Y-P6 interaction, with single-particle wave functions expressed as superpositions of Gaussian basis functions truncated at orbital angular momentum ℓ_max=7. For odd-N nuclei, the blocking due to the unpaired quasiparticle was handled by the interchange (U,V) ↔ (V*,U*) under the equal-filling approximation. The deformation parameter β and spherical-limit radius r̄₀ were extracted from the calculated mean-square matter radius and intrinsic mass quadrupole moment using a relation that separates deformation effects from other contributions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron chemical potential in 37Mg: -2.25 MeV, not small enough to account for the halo if pair correlation were fully ignored
- Minimum quasiparticle energy ε^min in 37Mg: 2.02 MeV
- |λ| + ε^min ≈ 4.3 MeV (would prevent halo under even-N asymptotics)
- |λ| - ε^min ≈ 0.2 MeV (accounts for broad density distribution under odd-N asymptotics)
- Neutron pair energy in 37Mg: ~3 MeV (indicating pair correlation is present)
- 39Mg predicted to be unbound in these calculations

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological studies by UHS17 that used deformed Woods-Saxon potentials to explain the staggering of reaction cross sections in 36-38Mg via the pairing anti-halo effect in 38Mg and quenched pairing in 37Mg. The present self-consistent calculations show that pairing is not quenched in 37Mg and instead enhances the halo through the new unpaired-particle haloing mechanism. The results also contrast with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations using the Gogny-D1S interaction (Mg37exp, Wat14), which failed to reproduce the N-dependence of matter radii in 34-38Mg. The work enables further investigation of even-odd effects in nuclear radii near the drip line across a wide range of nuclear masses, and raises questions about the influence of the predicted peanut-shaped halos on observables.
