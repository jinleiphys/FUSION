# Low Energy Compton Scattering and Nucleon Structure

> The low energy virtual Compton scattering process $eN\to e'Nγ$ offers a new and potentially high resolution window on nucleon sturcture via measurement of so-called generalized polarizabilities (GPs). We present calculations of GPs within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and discuss present experimental efforts.

## Key claim

Low-energy virtual Compton scattering (VCS) offers a new window on nucleon structure through generalized polarizabilities (GPs), but the paper's calculations within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) are only at leading order (O(p³)) and the experimental extraction of GPs is challenging because the Bethe-Heitler bremsstrahlung process generally dominates the cross section unless kinematics are carefully chosen.

## Method

The author calculates the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to O(p³) in the nucleon sector, employing the standard chiral Lagrangian with terms up to third order. The GPs are defined as multipole expansions around zero outgoing photon energy (ω' = 0), retaining only terms linear in ω', which yields ten GPs (later reduced to six independent ones via crossing symmetry and charge conjugation). Closed-form expressions are derived for each GP, with explicit formulas given for the generalized electric polarizability ᾱ_E(q̄) and magnetic polarizability β̄_M(q̄) as functions of the virtual photon three-momentum q̄.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability from real Compton scattering: α_E^p = (12.1 ± 0.8 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (photons in energy range 50–100 MeV)
- Proton magnetic polarizability from real Compton scattering: β_M^p = (2.1 ∓ 0.8 ∓ 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Spin-polarizability combination from dispersion relations: γ₀^p ≡ γ₁^p − γ₂^p − 2γ₄^p ≈ −1.34 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Backward spin-polarizability from global analysis of unpolarized Compton data: γ_π = γ₁ + γ₂ + 2γ₄ = (27.7 ± 2.3 ± 2.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- At the real photon point (q̄ = 0), the chiral calculation yields α_E(0) = 10 β_M(0) = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- The generalized magnetic polarizability β̄_M(q̄) is predicted to rise with q̄, reaching a maximum at q̄ ≈ 100 MeV before falling
- In the MAMI experiment, GP effects are calculated to be about 10% in the backward direction
- At CEBAF, with ε ≈ 0.95, calculations reveal possible 20–30% effects from GPs

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier HBChPT calculations of real Compton scattering polarizabilities (which agree with experiment at O(p³)) to the virtual case, providing predictions for the q̄-dependence of generalized polarizabilities. The calculations contrast with predictions from the non-relativistic constituent quark model (Guichon, 1995) and the linear sigma model (Metz, 1997), and the magnetic polarizability's predicted rise distinguishes chiral models from constituent quark predictions. The paper discusses ongoing experimental efforts at MAMI (data already taken), BATES (OOPS collaboration, approved), and CEBAF (approved), which aim to extract GPs and test these predictions, enabling future studies of nucleon resonance signatures and the nucleon's spin and angular momentum structure at higher energies.
