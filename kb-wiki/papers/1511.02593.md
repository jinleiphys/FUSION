# Observables and initial conditions for rotating and expanding fireballs with spheroidal symmetry

> Utilizing a recently found class of exact, analytic rotating solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics, we calculate analytically the single-particle spectra, the elliptic flows and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions for rotating and expanding fireballs with spheroidal symmetry. We demonstrate, that rotation generates final state momentum anisotropies even for a spatially symmetric, spherical initial geometry of the fireball. The mass dependence of the effective temperatures, as well as the HBT radius parameters and the elliptic flow are shown to be sensitive not only to radial flow effects but also to the magnitude of the initial angular momentum.

## Key claim

Rotation generates final-state momentum anisotropies even for a spatially symmetric, spherical initial geometry of the fireball, and the mass dependence of effective temperatures, HBT radius parameters, and elliptic flow are sensitive not only to radial flow effects but also to the magnitude of the initial angular momentum, but the analysis is limited to non-relativistic hydrodynamics with spheroidal symmetry (X = Z = R) and a sudden freeze-out at a constant temperature.

## Method

The authors use a recently found exact, analytic solution of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics with spheroidal symmetry (X = Z = R) and a Gaussian density profile, which includes a time-dependent angular velocity ω(t) that decreases as R⁻² due to angular momentum conservation. They derive analytic expressions for the single-particle spectrum, elliptic flow, and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions by integrating a multi-variate Gaussian emission function at a fixed freeze-out temperature T_f, assuming a sudden freeze-out and an ideal-gas equation of state with κ = 3/2. The time evolution of the scale parameters R(t) and Y(t) is obtained by solving the Hamiltonian equations of motion derived from the hydrodynamical equations, and the observables are evaluated at the freeze-out time when T(t_f) = T_f.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions for numerical examples: R₀ = 5 fm, Y₀ = 5 fm, T₀ = 350 MeV, with zero initial radial flows (Ẋ₀ = Ẏ₀ = Ż₀ = 0).
- Three values of initial angular velocity ω₀ are compared: from nearly zero up to 0.1 fm/c.
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV, reached after approximately 8–10 fm/c depending on ω₀.
- Particle masses used for observable evaluation: m_π = 140 MeV, m_K = 494 MeV, m_p = 938 MeV.
- The effective temperatures are defined as T_x = T_z = T_f + m(Ṙ_f² + ω_f² R_f²) and T_y = T_f + m Ẏ_f².

## Context

This work extends the non-rotating, spheroidally symmetric exact solution of fireball hydrodynamics found by Csizmadia and Csörgő (1998) and later generalized by Csörgő et al. (2001) to include rotation, building on the rotating solution family discovered by Csörgő, Nagy, and Barna (2013). It provides analytic formulas for observables that previously had only been studied numerically, e.g., by Csernai et al. (2011) for directed flow and by Csernai et al. (2013) for differential HBT correlations. The results enable experimental tests of angular momentum effects in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC by disentangling rotation from radial flow in spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii.
