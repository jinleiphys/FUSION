# Coexistence of cluster structure and superdeformation in $^{44}$Ti studied with deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> The nucleus $^{44}$Ti has low-lying levels of various kinds of mutually very different nuclear structure displaying the richness of the nuclear many-body dynamics. It is shown that the deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics by the use of the Gogny D1S force reproduces successfully and unifiedly two types of coexistence phenomena in $^{44}$Ti. Namely, on one hand, the coexistence of the mean field structure and the cluster structure is confirmed by verifying the normally deformed structure of the $K^蟺$=$3^-_1$ band with a 1-particle-jump intrinsic configuration and the $伪+ ^{40}$Ca cluster structure of the $K^蟺$=$0^-_2$ band. The mixed character of the mean-field-like structure and the $伪+ ^{40}$Ca cluster structure of the ground band is also shown. On the other hand, the coexistence of the normal deformed mean field and the superdeformed mean field is confirmed by verifying the triaxial superdeformation of the $K^蟺$=$0_2^+$ band and the $K^蟺$=$2^+_1$ band which has a 4-particle-jump intrinsic configuration. Good reproduction of the experimental data is shown for many kinds of quantities including the energy spectra, electric transition rates, alpha spectroscopic factors. Preliminary discussions are given on the existence of hyperdeformed excited states, the relation between superdeformation and clustering and so on.

## Key claim

The deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculation using the Gogny D1S force reproduces and confirms two types of coexistence phenomena in $^{44}$Ti: the coexistence of mean-field structure and cluster structure, and the coexistence of normal deformed and superdeformed mean fields, though the description of the $2\hbar\omega$ structure in the superdeformed band is not sufficient and the $12^+_2$ state is absent in the calculation.

## Method

The authors employed deformed-basis AMD with triaxially deformed Gaussian single-particle wave packets, using the Gogny D1S effective nuclear force and a Coulomb force approximated by seven Gaussians. Energy variation was performed under a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$, followed by parity projection and total angular momentum projection, with final wave functions obtained by superposing states with different $\beta$ and $K$ values via the generator coordinate method (GCM). Single-particle structure was analyzed by constructing the Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian from the optimized intrinsic wave functions to estimate particle-hole configurations.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: -372.4 MeV, underestimating the observed value by 3 MeV
- Excitation energy of the $0^+_2$ state (band head of the superdeformed $K^\pi=0^+_2$ band): 1.75 MeV in calculation, compared to 1.905 MeV experimentally
- Superdeformed $4\hbar\omega$ configuration has deformation parameter $\beta \sim 0.5$ and $\gamma \sim 25^\circ$
- The $0\hbar\omega$ to $4\hbar\omega$ energy difference for the $0^+$ state is only 1.7 MeV after angular momentum projection, which lowers the energy by about 6 MeV
- Intra-band $E2$ transition in ground band: $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 142$ $e^2$fm$^4$ (calculated) vs $120\pm30$ $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental)
- Intra-band $E2$ transition in superdeformed band: $B(E2; 2^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_2) = 320$ $e^2$fm$^4$ (calculated) vs $220\pm50$ $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental)
- The $\alpha+^{40}$Ca component in the ground state is about 40%
- Hyperdeformed state around $\beta=0.7$ is higher by about 19 MeV than the $0\hbar\omega$ state for the $0^+$ case

## Context

This work extends the deformed-basis AMD framework previously applied to $^{32}$S by Kimura and Horiuchi (2004) to study $^{44}$Ti, which is the lightest $N=Z$ even-even nucleus in the $pf$ shell region and is analogous to $^{20}$Ne. The study confirms the $\alpha+^{40}$Ca cluster structure in the ground band predicted by the unique $\alpha-^{40}$Ca optical potential analysis of Ohkubo (1993) and the experimentally observed $K^\pi=0^-$ parity-doublet band found by Yamaya (1997) using $\alpha$-transfer reactions. The results also confirm the triaxial superdeformation reported in Hartree-Fock calculations by Inakura (2004) and provide a unified description of both cluster and mean-field coexistence phenomena, enabling future studies of the relation between superdeformation and clustering in the $pf$-shell region.
