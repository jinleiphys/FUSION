# Proton elastic scattering on calcium isotopes from chiral nuclear optical potentials

> We formulate microscopic optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus scattering from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. The real and imaginary central terms of the optical potentials are obtained from the nucleon self energy in infinite nuclear matter at a given density and isospin asymmetry, calculated self-consistently to second order in many-body perturbation theory. The real spin-orbit term is extracted from the same chiral potential using an improved density matrix expansion. The density-dependent optical potential is then folded with the nuclear density distributions of 40Ca, 42Ca, 44Ca, and 48Ca from which we study proton-nucleus elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections using the reaction code TALYS. We compare the results of the microscopic calculations to those of phenomenological models and experimental data up to projectile energies of E = 180 MeV. While overall satisfactory agreement with the available experimental data is obtained, we find that the elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections can be significantly improved with a weaker imaginary optical potential, particularly for larger projectile energies.

## Key claim

## Method
The real and imaginary central terms of the optical potential were obtained from the nucleon self-energy in infinite nuclear matter, calculated self-consistently to second order in many-body perturbation theory using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. The real spin-orbit term was extracted from the same chiral potential using an improved density matrix expansion. The density-dependent optical potential was then folded with nuclear density distributions of 40Ca, 42Ca, 44Ca, and 48Ca from mean field theory, and the reaction code TALYS was used to study proton-nucleus elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Projectile energies studied: up to E = 180 MeV for total reaction cross sections, and up to E = 160 MeV for differential elastic scattering cross sections.
- For proton-40Ca at E = 2.35 MeV: real central volume integral = 524 MeV fm^3 (chiral) vs 480 MeV fm^3 (Koning-Delaroche); imaginary central volume integral = 64 MeV fm^3 (chiral) vs 80 MeV fm^3 (KD).
- For proton-40Ca at E = 35 MeV: real central volume integral = 413 MeV fm^3 (chiral) vs 374 MeV fm^3 (KD); imaginary central volume integral = 120 MeV fm^3 (chiral) vs 113 MeV fm^3 (KD).
- For proton-40Ca at E = 100 MeV: real central volume integral = 236 MeV fm^3 (chiral) vs 220 MeV fm^3 (KD); imaginary central volume integral = 163 MeV fm^3 (chiral) vs 109 MeV fm^3 (KD).
- ILDA range parameter for central part varied between t_C = 1.15 fm and 1.25 fm; for spin-orbit part t_SO = 1.0 fm and 1.1 fm.
- Momentum-space cutoff of chiral potential: Lambda = 450 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier microscopic optical potential calculations from chiral effective field theory by Holt et al. (2013, 2015) and others, incorporating both two- and three-nucleon forces and a microscopic spin-orbit term derived from the improved density matrix expansion. The approach contrasts with phenomenological global optical potentials such as Koning and Delaroche (2003) and semi-microscopic potentials from Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux (1976, 1977) that often require empirical energy-dependent strength factors. The results enable future development of predictive optical potentials for exotic isotopes and for applications such as r-process nucleosynthesis calculations, where neutron-capture rates on neutron-rich nuclei are needed.
