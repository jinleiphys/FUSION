# Towards accurate nuclear mass tables in covariant density functional theory

> The current investigation focuses on detailed analysis of the anchor based optimization approach (ABOA), its comparison with alternative global fitting protocols and on the global analysis of the truncation of basis effects in the calculation of binding energies. It is shown that ABOA provides a solution which is close to that obtained in alternative approaches but at small portion of their computational time. The application of softer correction function after few initial iterations of ABOA stabilizes and speeds up its convergence. For the first time, the numerical errors in the calculation of binding energies related to the truncation of bosonic and fermionic bases have been globally investigated with respect of asymptotic values corresponding to the infinite basis in the framework of covariant density functional theory (CDFT). These errors typically grow up with the increase of the mass and deformation of the nuclei. To reduce such errors in bosonic sector below 10 keV for almost all nuclei with proton number $Z<120$ one should truncate the bosonic basis at $N_B=28$ instead of presently used $N_B=20$. The reduction of the errors in binding energies due to the truncation of the fermionic basis in CDFT is significantly more numerically costly. For the first time it is shown that the pattern and the speed of the convergence of binding energies as a function of the size of fermionic basis given by $N_F$ depend on the type of covariant energy density functional. The use of explicit density dependence of the meson-nucleon coupling constants or point couplings slows down substantially the speed of convergence of binding energies as a function of $N_F$. A new procedure for finding the asymptotic values of binding energies is suggested in the present paper: it allows better control of numerical errors.

# Towards accurate nuclear mass tables in covariant density functional theory

## Key claim

The anchor based optimization approach (ABOA) provides a solution for fitting covariant energy density functionals that is close to that obtained in alternative global fitting protocols but at a small fraction of their computational time; however, the numerical errors in binding energies due to truncation of bosonic and fermionic bases have been globally investigated for the first time in CDFT and are shown to grow with mass and deformation, with the pattern and speed of convergence depending on the type of functional.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using both spherical and axially deformed computer codes based on harmonic oscillator basis set expansion, testing three major classes of covariant energy density functionals: meson exchange with non-linear couplings (NLME), meson exchange with density-dependent couplings (DDME), and point coupling (PC) models. The ABOA was applied using twelve spherical anchor nuclei (including 16O, 40,48Ca, 90Zr, 116,124,132Sn, and 204,208,214Pb) with a correction function of the form E_corr(Z,N) = alpha_i(N-Z) + beta_i(N+Z) + gamma_i, and the convergence of binding energies was systematically studied as a function of fermionic basis size N_F (up to 38 in spherical code, up to 30 in axial code) and bosonic basis size N_B. A new procedure for finding asymptotic values of binding energies corresponding to infinite basis was introduced.

## Key numbers

- To reduce bosonic truncation errors below 10 keV for almost all nuclei with proton number Z < 120, the bosonic basis must be truncated at N_B = 28 instead of the presently used N_B = 20.
- For 208Pb with the DD-ME2 functional, binding energies calculated with N_F = 20 fermionic shells deviate from the asymptotic value by approximately 250 keV.
- In Skyrme DFT calculations with the SLy4 force, total binding energies of 120Sn and 102,110Zr obtained with N = 20 and N = 25 harmonic oscillator shells differ by 110-150 keV.
- Computational time per nucleus in axially deformed RHB code ranges from 0.15 CPU-hours (DD-PC1, N_F = 16) to 15.94 CPU-hours (DD-MEX, N_F = 30).
- The ratio of computational time in deformed versus spherical RHB calculations ranges from approximately 590 (DD-MEX, N_F = 16) to over 5600 (DD-PC1, N_F = 30).
- The ABOA uses 855 even-even nuclei for defining the correction function and 12 spherical anchor nuclei in the optimization.

## Context

This work extends the limited attempts at global fitting of covariant energy density functionals, which were previously undertaken only in Refs. AGC.16 and TA.23, and addresses the missing rigorous analysis of numerical errors from basis truncation that has been neglected in prior global calculations. The study contrasts with non-relativistic approaches such as the UNEDF class of functionals (which used N = 20 shells with limited nuclei), the BCPM functional (which used 579 measured masses with reduced basis), and the D1M/D1M* functionals (which used N <= 14 shells with infinite-basis corrections). The results enable the development of next-generation covariant energy density functionals that properly account for infinite basis corrections, which are neglected in present-generation functionals.
