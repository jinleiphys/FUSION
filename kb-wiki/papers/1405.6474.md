# Pole structure from energy-dependent and single-energy fits to $πN$ elastic scattering data

> The pole structure of the current GW/SAID partial-wave analysis of elastic $πN$ scattering and $ηN$ production data is studied. Pole positions and residues are extracted from both the energy-dependent and single-energy fits, using two different methods. For the energy-dependent fits, both contour integration and a Laurent+Pietarinen approach are used. In the case of single-energy fits, the Laurent+Pietarinen approach is used. Errors are estimated and the two sets of results are compared to other recent and older fits to data.

## Key claim

The pole positions and residues extracted from the GW/SAID energy-dependent (ED) and single-energy (SE) partial-wave analyses of elastic πN scattering and ηN production data are compared, with the caveat that the SE amplitudes contain additional structure that can lead to further poles being required for a best Laurent+Pietarinen fit, and that some of these additional poles may be explainable by a complex branch point rather than a new resonance.

## Method

Pole parameters were extracted from the WI08 energy-dependent (ED) partial-wave amplitudes using two independent methods: contour integration of the analytic continuation of the GW/SAID Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix model, and a Laurent+Pietarinen (L+P) expansion that fits a local analytic function with poles and three Pietarinen series (representing left-hand cuts and two inelastic channels) to the amplitude on the real axis. For the single-energy (SE) solutions, which are not smooth functions of energy and cannot be analytically continued by contour integration, only the L+P method was applied, with the fit performed directly to the SE amplitude points at discrete energies.

## Key numbers

- N(1535) 1/2⁻ pole from WI08 ED L+P: Re W_p = 1497 ± 8 ± 1 MeV, -2Im W_p = 85 ± 14 ± 7 MeV, residue modulus = 13 ± 3 ± 1 MeV, phase = -(41 ± 12 ± 4)°
- N(1535) 1/2⁻ pole from WI08 SE L+P: Re W_p = 1507 ± 1 ± 0 MeV, -2Im W_p = 88 ± 3 ± 1 MeV
- RPP range for N(1535) 1/2⁻ pole position: Re W_p = 1490–1510 MeV, -2Im W_p = 90–170 MeV
- The first physical branch point x_Q was fixed at 1077 MeV (the πN threshold)
- Additional poles found in SE amplitude fits include N(1895) 1/2⁻, N(1710) 1/2⁺, N(2100) 1/2⁺, N(1700) 3/2⁻, N(1990) 7/2⁺, and Δ(1900) 1/2⁻

## Context

This work updates the pole parameters from the earlier SP06 GW/SAID solution (which were quoted without error estimates in the Review of Particle Physics) to the current WI08 solution, providing error estimates for the first time. The L+P method was previously introduced by the authors (LP1, LP2, LP3) and is here applied to SE solutions for the first time, where no reliable pole-extraction procedure had been applied before. The additional poles found in the SE fits are similar to those found in the older Karlsruhe-Helsinki analysis (Hoehler 1984) and the Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley analysis, and the results enable comparison of pole parameters from different extraction methods and different partial-wave analyses.
