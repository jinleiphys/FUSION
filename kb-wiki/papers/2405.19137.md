# Towards the understanding of heavy quarks hadronization: from leptonic to heavy-ion collisions

> The formation of hadrons is a fundamental process in nature that can be investigated at particle colliders. As several recent findings demonstrate, with $\mathrm{e^+e^-}$ collisions as a "vacuum-like" reference at one extreme, and central nucleus--nucleus as a dense, extended-size system characterized by flow and local equilibrium at the opposite extreme, different collision systems offer a lever arm that can be exploited to probe with a range of heavy-flavour hadron species the onset of various hadronization processes. In this review, we present an overview of the theoretical and experimental developments. The focus is on open-heavy-flavour measurements. The comparison with model predictions and connections among the results in electron-positron, proton--proton, proton--nucleus, nucleus--nucleus collisions are discussed. After reviewing the current state, we suggest some prospects and future developments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the assumption of universal heavy-quark hadronization, that fragmentation functions and fragmentation fractions measured in e+e- collisions can be applied to any collision system, is challenged by recent LHC measurements showing significantly higher baryon-to-meson ratios in proton-proton collisions compared to e+e- collisions, particularly for heavy-flavour hadrons, indicating that hadronization may depend on the surrounding partonic environment.

## Method

The authors review theoretical models (including the Lund string model in Pythia, the cluster model in Herwig, coalescence/recombination models, and statistical hadronization models) and experimental measurements of open-heavy-flavour hadron production across e+e-, proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collision systems. They compare measured production ratios of heavy-flavour baryons and mesons from the ALICE and LHCb experiments at the LHC with model predictions and with results from e+e- colliders (LEP, B factories) and ep collisions.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass is around 1.5 times the proton mass; beauty quark mass is around 5 times the proton mass
- The fraction of Lambda_c+ yield from feed-down of Sigma_c states decreases with transverse momentum from about 40% to about 28% in proton-proton collisions
- The baryon-to-meson ratio for heavy-flavour hadrons is significantly larger in proton-proton collisions with respect to e+e- collisions, contrary to expectations from universal fragmentation

## Context

This review extends the long-standing line of work on heavy-quark fragmentation functions parameterized from e+e- data (Braaten 1994) and used in pQCD calculations like FONLL (Cacciari 1998, 2012) and GM-VFNS (Kniehl 2004). It addresses the breakdown of universality previously hinted at by CDF in the beauty sector at the Tevatron (Aaltonen 2008) and now confirmed by ALICE and LHCb at the LHC. The paper enables future work on non-universal hadronization models incorporating coalescence and environment-dependent effects, and suggests new measurements to distinguish between possible mechanisms.
