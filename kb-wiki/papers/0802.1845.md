# Dynamical simulation of bound antiproton-nuclear systems and observable signals of cold nuclear compression

> On the basis of the kinetic equation with selfconsistent relativistic mean fields acting on baryons and antibaryons, we study dynamical response of the nucleus to an antiproton implanted in its interior. By solving numerically the time-dependent Vlasov equation, we show that the compressed state is formed on a rather short time scale of about 4-10 fm/c. This justifies the assumption, that the antiproton annihilation may happen in the compressed nuclear environment. The evolution of the nucleus after antiproton annihilation is described by the same kinetic equation including collision terms. We show, that nucleon kinetic energy spectra and the total invariant mass distributions of produced mesons are quite sensitive observables to the antiproton annihilation in the compressed nucleus.

## Key claim
The compressed nuclear state formed by an implanted antiproton develops on a time scale of 4-10 fm/c, which is comparable to the antiproton lifetime in nuclear matter (estimated at ~2 fm/c in vacuum but potentially up to 20 fm/c for deeply bound antiprotons), making it plausible that annihilation occurs within the compressed environment.

## Method
The authors solved the time-dependent Vlasov equation with selfconsistent relativistic mean fields (RMF) for nucleons and antiprotons, using the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model with NL3 parameterization. The antiproton was initialized as a Gaussian wave packet at the nucleus center and propagated without collision terms to study compression, then instantaneously annihilated at a preselected time using a quark-model-based event generator with in-medium phase space corrections. Two scaling factors for antiproton-meson coupling constants were used: ξ=1 (G-parity motivated) and ξ=0.3 (fitting empirical antiproton-nucleus optical potentials).

## Key numbers
- Compression time scale: 4-10 fm/c for compressed state formation
- Antiproton lifetime estimate in nuclear matter at normal density: τ_ann ≈ 2 fm/c (from σ_ann = C + D/v_rel with C=38 mb, D=35 mb·c)
- Maximum central nucleon density for ξ=0.3 in ⁴⁰Ca: ρ_N = 0.30 fm⁻³ at t=10 fm/c, relaxing to ρ_N ≈ 0.26 fm⁻³
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ_0 = 0.148 fm⁻³
- NL3 parameterization nuclear matter properties: binding energy 16.299 MeV/nucleon, compressibility K=271.76 MeV, nucleon effective mass m_N* = 0.60 m_N at ρ_0
- G-parity transformed antiproton optical potential: about -700 MeV at ρ_0; phenomenological range: -(100-350) MeV
- Calculated average pion multiplicity for p̄p annihilation at rest: ⟨n_π⟩ ≈ 4.5 (experimental value ≈ 5.0)

## Context
This work extends static RMF calculations by Buervenich (2002) and Mishustin (2005) that predicted nuclear compression from deeply-bound antiprotons, by adding dynamical evolution via the Vlasov equation. It enables experimental searches for cold nuclear compression signals in antiproton-nucleus interactions, proposing nucleon kinetic energy spectra and total invariant mass distributions of produced mesons as sensitive observables. The approach contrasts with earlier intranuclear cascade simulations by Cahay (1982) that neglected mean field effects entirely.
