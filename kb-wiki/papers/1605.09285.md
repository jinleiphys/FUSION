# A semi-classical recipe for wobbly limp noodles in partonic soup

> We compute the average squared distance, $s^2(t)$, travelled by a light-flavour off-mass-shell coloured parton in a strongly-coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ $SU(N_c)$ super-symmetric Yang Mills plasma using the gauge/string duality. In fact, we derive a closed integral expression for $s^2(t;a)$ in $AdS_3$-Schwarzschild, which interpolates between a heavy quark when $a = 0$ and a light quark when $a = 1$, that we evaluate analytically for small virtualities - labelled $s_\text{small}^2(t;a)$. For arbitrary virtualities, we show that for asymptotically early times the motion is ballistic, $\left.s^2(t;a)\right|_{t\llβ}\sim t^2$, while at asymptotically late times the motion is diffusive, $\left. s^2(t;a) \right|_{t\ggβ} = s_\text{small}^2(t;a) \sim 2D(a) t$, from which we are able to extract the diffusion coefficient $D(a)$. Motivated by the apparent universality of the late time behaviour, we compute $s_{\text{small}}^2(t;a,d)$ and $D(a,d)$ for an arbitrary $AdS_d$-Schwarzschild geometry. From $D(a,d)$ we then compute for the first time the dynamic, time-dependent transverse momentum squared per unit path length picked up by a high momentum light quark in a strongly-coupled plasma, the transport coefficient $\hat{q}(t)$, which is critically important for phenomenology in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors compute the average squared distance travelled by an off-mass-shell light quark in a strongly-coupled plasma using gauge/string duality, finding that the late-time diffusive behavior is universal and determined solely by the plasma temperature, independent of the initial virtuality of the quark or the parameter \(a\) that interpolates between a heavy quark (\(a=0\)) and a light quark (\(a=1\)).

## Method

The authors first derive the leading-order classical motion of a falling string in \(AdS_3\)-Schwarzschild, parameterized by a fraction \(a\) of the local speed of light at which the free endpoint falls, building on the heavy-quark solution of de Boer et al. (2008). They then compute next-to-leading order semi-classical fluctuations on top of this falling string solution induced by thermal Hawking radiation from the black hole, obtaining a closed integral expression for the average transverse distance squared \(s^2(t;a)\). For small initial string lengths (small virtualities compared to temperature), they evaluate this expression analytically; for arbitrary virtualities, they analyze the asymptotic early-time (ballistic) and late-time (diffusive) limits.

## Key numbers

* The early-time motion is ballistic: \(s^2(t;a) \sim t^2\) for \(t \ll \beta\), where \(\beta = 1/T\) is the inverse Hawking temperature.
* The late-time motion is diffusive: \(s^2(t;a) \sim 2D(a) t\) for \(t \gg \beta\), with the diffusion coefficient \(D(a)\) extracted from the calculation.
* The initial virtuality of the light quark is related to the initial string length by \(Q^2 = \lambda \ell_0^2 / (4\pi^2 L^4)\), where \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling and \(L\) the AdS radius.
* The Hawking temperature of the black-brane is \(T = (d-1) r_H / (4\pi L^2)\) in natural units, with \(r_H\) the horizon radius.

## Context

This work extends the foundational study of Brownian motion of an on-shell heavy quark in AdS/CFT by de Boer et al. (2008) to the case of off-shell light quarks, and builds on prior holographic Langevin studies of slow-moving heavy quarks (Gubser 2006, Casalderrey-Solana 2007). The authors compute for the first time the time-dependent transport coefficient \(\hat{q}(t)\) for a light quark in a strongly-coupled plasma, which is critically important for phenomenological modeling of jet quenching in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
