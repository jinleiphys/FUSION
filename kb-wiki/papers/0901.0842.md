# Role of axial-vector mesons near the chiral phase transition

> We present a systematic study of the vector--axial-vector mixing (V-A mixing) in the current correlation functions and its evolution with temperature within an effective field theory. The $a_1$-$ρ$-$π$ coupling vanishes at the critical temperature $T_c$ and thus the V-A mixing also vanishes. A remarkable observation is that even for finite $m_π$ the $ρ$ and $a_1$ meson masses are almost degenerate at $T_c$. The vanishing V-A mixing at $T_c$ stays approximately intact.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even with a finite physical pion mass (m_π = 140 MeV), the ρ and a₁ meson masses become nearly degenerate at the critical temperature T_c, and the a₁-ρ-π coupling almost vanishes at T_c, preserving the vanishing of vector-axial-vector mixing approximately intact despite explicit chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors use a generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) Lagrangian to describe a system including pions, vector mesons (ρ), and axial-vector mesons (a₁) consistently with chiral symmetry. They introduce a temperature-dependent bare axial-vector meson mass with a "flash temperature" T_f = 140 MeV to model the onset of partial chiral symmetry restoration, and compute the vector spectral function at various temperatures up to T_c = 200 MeV, both in the chiral limit and for physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: T_c = 200 MeV
- Flash temperature (onset of partial restoration): T_f = 140 MeV
- At T = T_c, the ρ and a₁ meson masses become degenerate around √s ≈ 1 GeV
- Vector spectral function calculated at T/T_c = 0.8 for m_π = 0 and m_π = 140 MeV
- Temperature dependence shown for T/T_c = 0.6 to 1.0 for both m_π = 0 and m_π = 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends the model-independent low-energy theorem for vector-axial-vector mixing (V-A mixing) by Sasaki, Harada, and Weise (2008), which is valid only for T ≪ 2f_π, to higher temperatures near the chiral phase transition. The study uses lattice QCD results for the chiral crossover transition temperature range (T_c = 200 MeV, T_f = 140 MeV) as input. The findings are relevant for understanding dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC under high-temperature, low-baryon-density conditions.
