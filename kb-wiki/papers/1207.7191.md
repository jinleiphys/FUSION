# Three-body properties of low-lying $^{12}$Be resonances

> We compute the three-body structure of the lowest resonances of $^{12}$Be considered as two neutrons around an inert $^{10}$Be core. This is an extension of the bound state calculations of $^{12}$Be into the continuum spectrum. We investigate the lowest resonances of angular momenta and parities, $0^{\pm}$, $1^{-}$ and $2^{+}$. Surprisingly enough, they all are naturally occurring in the three-body model. We calculate bulk structure dominated by small distance properties as well as decays determined by the asymptotic large-distance structure. Both $0^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ have two-body $^{10}$Be-neutron d-wave structure, while $1^{-}$ has an even mixture of $p$ and d-waves. The corresponding relative neutron-neutron partial waves are distributed among $s$, $p$, and d-waves. The branching ratios show different mixtures of one-neutron emission, three-body direct, and sequential decays. We argue for spin and parities, $0^{+}$, $1^{-}$ and $2^{+}$, to the resonances at 0.89, 2.03, 5.13, respectively. The computed structures are in agreement with existing reaction measurements.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the lowest resonances of $^{12}$Be at 0.89, 2.03, and 5.13 MeV can be assigned spin and parities $0^{+}$, $1^{-}$, and $2^{+}$ respectively, but the authors acknowledge that the computed widths are systematically larger than experimental values, suggesting that more than half of the resonance structure lies beyond the inert-core-plus-two-neutron model.

## Method
The authors used the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method to solve the three-body problem of $^{12}$Be treated as an inert $^{10}$Be core plus two valence neutrons. They applied complex rotation (scaling only the hyperradius by $\exp(i\theta)$) to transform resonance boundary conditions into exponentially falling boundary conditions. A Gaussian three-body force with range 4.25 fm was tuned to match experimental resonance energies, after which the widths were determined without further adjustment.

## Key numbers
- For $1^{-}$ without three-body force: resonance at $(2.99, 0.68)$ MeV; with three-body force tuned to 0.89 MeV gives width 0.22 MeV (experimental width 0.11 MeV); tuned to 2.03 MeV gives width 0.50 MeV (experimental width 0.09 MeV)
- For $0^{+}$ without three-body force: resonance at $(1.97, 0.70)$ MeV; with three-body force tuned to 0.89 MeV gives width 0.32 MeV; tuned to 2.03 MeV gives width 0.84 MeV
- For $2^{+}$ without three-body force: resonance at $(5.36, 1.20)$ MeV; with three-body force the lowest achievable energy is 2.76 MeV with width 0.66 MeV; tuned to 5.13 MeV gives width 1.13 MeV
- The $0^{-}$ state appears as a bound state at about $-1$ MeV without three-body force; when repulsion is increased it passes the $^{11}$Be ground state threshold at $-0.504$ MeV and becomes a very broad resonance or virtual state

## Context
This work extends the bound-state three-body calculations of $^{12}$Be by the same group (Romero-Redondo et al., 2007) into the continuum spectrum. It addresses the experimental resonances observed by Fortune et al. (1994) at 0.89 and 2.03 MeV and by Bohlen et al. (2008) at 5.13 MeV. The results enable future studies of reaction mechanisms populating these resonances and provide a framework for understanding the competition between one-neutron emission, three-body direct decay, and sequential decay in neutron-rich beryllium isotopes.
