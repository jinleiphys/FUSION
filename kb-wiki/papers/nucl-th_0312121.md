# Ground State Bands of the E(5) and X(5) Critical Symmetries Obtained from Davidson Potentials through a Variational Procedure

> Davidson potentials of the form $β^2 +β_0^4/β^2$, when used in the original Bohr Hamiltonian for $γ$-independent potentials bridge the U(5) and O(6) symmetries. Using a variational procedure, we determine for each value of angular momentum $L$ the value of $β_0$ at which the derivative of the energy ratio $R_L=E(L)/E(2)$ with respect to $β_0$ has a sharp maximum, the collection of $R_L$ values at these points forming a band which practically coincides with the ground state band of the E(5) model, corresponding to the critical point in the shape phase transition from U(5) to O(6). The same potentials, when used in the Bohr Hamiltonian after separating variables as in the X(5) model, bridge the U(5) and SU(3) symmetries, the same variational procedure leading to a band which practically coincides with the ground state band of the X(5) model, corresponding to the critical point of the U(5) to SU(3) shape phase transition. A new derivation of the Holmberg-Lipas formula for nuclear energy spectra is obtained as a by-product.

## Key claim

Davidson potentials of the form β² + β₀⁴/β², when used in the Bohr Hamiltonian with a variational procedure that maximizes the rate of change of energy ratios R_L = E(L)/E(2) with respect to β₀ for each angular momentum L separately, produce ground state bands that practically coincide with the E(5) and X(5) critical symmetry models, thereby proving that the infinite well potential used in those models is the optimum choice for describing shape phase transitions.

## Method

The authors used Davidson potentials u(β) = β² + β₀⁴/β² in the Bohr Hamiltonian under two different frameworks: (1) the E(5) framework, assuming the potential is independent of the γ variable, and (2) the X(5) framework, assuming the potential separates as u(β)+u(γ). For each angular momentum L, they computed the energy ratios R_L = E(L)/E(2) as functions of β₀, then located the value β₀,max where the first derivative dR_L/dβ₀ reaches a sharp maximum (where the second derivative vanishes). The collection of R_L values at these β₀,max points for all L forms a band that was compared to the E(5) and X(5) model predictions.

## Context

This work extends the E(5) model of Iachello (2001) and X(5) model of Iachello (2001), which used infinite well potentials to describe shape phase transitions from U(5) to O(6) and from U(5) to SU(3) respectively. The variational procedure is analogous to the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model of Mariscotti, Scharff-Goldhaber, and Buck (1969), where energy is minimized with respect to an angular-momentum-dependent moment of inertia for each L separately. The method enables future application to other physical quantities such as B(E2) transition rates and quadrupole moments, using any potential bridging the relevant symmetry pairs.
