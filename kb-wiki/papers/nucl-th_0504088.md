# Many Body Methods and Effective Field Theory

> In the framework of pionless nucleon-nucleon effective field theory we study different approximation schemes for the nuclear many body problem. We consider, in particular, ladder diagrams constructed from particle-particle, hole-hole, and particle-hole pairs. We focus on the problem of finding a suitable starting point for perturbative calculations near the unitary limit (k_Fa)->infinity and (k_Fr)-> 0, where k_F is the Fermi momentum, a is the scattering length and r is the effective range. We try to clarify the relationship between different classes of diagrams and the large g and large D approximations, where g is the fermion degeneracy and D is the number of space time dimensions. In the large D limit we find that the energy per particle in the strongly interacting system is 1/2 the result for free fermions.

## Key claim

In the unitary limit where the scattering length diverges and the effective range vanishes, the particle-particle ladder sum in the large D (space-time dimensions) limit yields an energy per particle equal to 1/2 the free fermion result, but this conclusion depends on taking the strong coupling limit after the large D limit and on the assumption that other diagram classes are suppressed by powers of 1/D.

## Method

The authors study non-relativistic fermions using pionless effective field theory with contact interactions, computing the energy per particle from resummed particle-particle ladder diagrams, hole-hole ladder diagrams, and particle-hole ring diagrams at finite density. They analyze the scaling behavior of these diagram classes in the limits of large fermion degeneracy g and large space-time dimensions D, using dimensional regularization with the power divergence subtraction scheme to relate coupling constants to scattering length and effective range.

## Key numbers

* Energy per particle in the unitary limit from particle-particle ladder diagrams for g=2: xi=0.44 (f_PP approximated as 2), xi=0.32 (f_PP approximated by its phase space average), xi=0.24 (full numerical calculation)
* Effective range correction to the universal parameter xi: xi(k_F r) = 0.32 + 0.19 (k_F r) + O(k_F/mu, (k_F r)^2) in the unitary limit
* In the large D limit, the particle-particle bubble scales as f_PP^(D)(0,0) = 2/(D-3)
* BCS gap in the large D limit: Delta = (2 e^{-gamma} E_F / D) exp(-1/(D lambda)) where lambda is the dimensionless coupling and gamma is Euler's constant approximately 0.5772
* Pairing energy per particle in the large D limit scales as 1/D relative to the free Fermi energy

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear many-body systems pioneered by Weinberg (1990), Hammer and Furnstahl (2000), and Steele (2000), focusing on the unitary limit relevant to neutron matter and cold atomic gases near Feshbach resonances. The study clarifies the relationship between particle-particle ladder sums (analogous to Brueckner theory from Fetter and Day) and the large g and large D expansions, showing that the large D limit provides a systematic scheme where ladder diagrams dominate and yields a specific prediction of xi=1/2 in the unitary limit. This enables future perturbative calculations of finite effective range corrections and other effects for neutron matter, as well as connections to experimental results from cold atom systems.
