# In-medium properties of a $ΞN$ interaction derived from chiral effective field theory

> The in-medium properties of a baryon-baryon interaction for the strangeness $S=-2$ sector derived within chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order are investigated. The considered $ΞN$ interaction is in line with available empirical constraints on the $ΛΛ$ $S$-wave scattering length and on $ΞN$ elastic and inelastic cross sections. In particular, there are no near-threshold bound states in the $ΞN$ system. A conventional $G$-matrix calculation for this interaction is performed and reveals that the single-particle potential of the $Ξ$-hyperon in nuclear matter is moderately attractive as suggested by recent experimental evidence for the existence of $Ξ$-hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a chiral EFT $\Xi N$ interaction can be constructed which simultaneously satisfies all available experimental constraints on $\Xi^- p$ cross sections and yields a moderately attractive $\Xi$ single-particle potential in nuclear matter, contrary to their earlier interaction (Haidenbauer 2015) which was too repulsive in the medium.

## Method

The authors perform a conventional $G$-matrix calculation using the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the gap choice for the intermediate spectrum, evaluating the $\Xi$ single-particle potential $U_\Xi(p_\Xi,\rho)$ in symmetric nuclear matter. They readjust the low-energy constants (LECs) $\tilde C^{10^*}_{^3S_1}$ and $\tilde C^{10}_{^3S_1}$ in the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial wave for isospin $I=1$, while keeping the $^1S_0$ interaction and the $I=0$ $^3S_1$ interaction fixed from their earlier work (Haidenbauer 2015). The Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved with a regulator cutoff $\Lambda$ varied between 500 and 650 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The updated NLO interaction yields a total $\Xi$ single-particle potential $U_\Xi(0)$ at saturation density ($k_F = 1.35$ fm$^{-1}$) ranging from $-5.5$ MeV (cutoff 500 MeV) to $-4.1$ MeV (cutoff 650 MeV)
- The original NLO interaction (Haidenbauer 2015) gave $U_\Xi(0)$ values from $+22.4$ to $+27.7$ MeV at the same density, indicating strong repulsion
- The $\Xi N$ $^3S_1$ scattering length for $I=1$ in the updated interaction ranges from $(-1.17 + i\,3.44)$ fm (cutoff 500 MeV) to $(-0.90 + i\,4.27)$ fm (cutoff 650 MeV)
- The $\Xi N$ $^1S_0$ scattering length for $I=0$ is large and negative, ranging from $(-7.24 - i\,20.79)$ fm to $(-10.89 - i\,14.91)$ fm depending on cutoff
- The $\Xi$ potential depth of $-3$ to $-5$ MeV is compared to the benchmark value of $-14$ MeV from the BNL-E885 experiment (Khaustov) and to the ESC08c result of $-7$ MeV (Rijken 2015)

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT baryon-baryon interaction for strangeness $S=-2$ (Haidenbauer 2015) by investigating its in-medium properties, motivated by recent experimental evidence for bound $\Xi$-hypernuclei (Nakazawa 2015, Nagae 2017) and lattice QCD results near the physical point (Sasaki 2017). The updated interaction avoids the near-threshold bound state in the $\Xi N$ $^3S_1$ $I=1$ channel that appears in phenomenological potentials like Nijmegen ESC08c (Rijken 2015), while producing a $\Xi$ single-particle potential comparable to those from ESC08c and the quark-model potential fss2 (Fujiwara 2007). The results enable more reliable predictions for $\Xi$-hypernuclei properties and for the role of $\Xi$ hyperons in neutron star matter.
