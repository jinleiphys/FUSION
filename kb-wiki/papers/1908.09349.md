# Towards high-precision nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory

> Chiral effective field theory is being developed into a precision tool for low-energy nuclear physics. I review the state of the art in the two-nucleon sector, discuss applications to few-nucleon systems and address challenges that will have to be faced over the coming years.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory can be developed into a precision tool for low-energy nuclear physics, but maintaining consistency between nuclear forces and currents under finite-cutoff regularization becomes a nontrivial task starting from fourth order in the chiral expansion, and the solution of the long-standing three-nucleon force challenge likely requires pushing the chiral expansion to at least N⁴LO.

## Method

The author reviews the derivation of nuclear forces and currents using the method of unitary transformation applied to the effective chiral Lagrangian, where the unitary operator is determined perturbatively using the chiral expansion. The resulting expressions are regularized using dimensional regularization for loop integrals, then subsequently regularized with a finite momentum-space cutoff for practical calculations in the Schrödinger or Lippmann-Schwinger equation. For the three-nucleon force at N²LO, the low-energy constants c_D and c_E are determined by fitting the triton binding energy and the proton-deuteron differential cross section minimum at E_N = 70 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown scale Λ_b is estimated to be ~600 MeV, confirmed by Bayesian analysis which found it may be somewhat larger
- For the medium cutoff choice Λ = 450 MeV at N⁴LO⁺, the χ²/datum values are 1.06 for neutron-proton and 1.00 for proton-proton scattering data from the 2013 Granada database below E_lab = 300 MeV
- The kinetic energy of the deuteron ranges from 12 to 23 MeV for LO through N⁴LO chiral potentials, compared to ~19 MeV for the Argonne AV18 potential
- The three-nucleon force contribution to the ³H binding energy is estimated at ~650 keV based on power counting with Q ~ M_π/Λ_b and Λ_b ~ 600 MeV
- The deuteron binding energy is ~2.224 MeV, which is much smaller than the naive dimensional analysis estimate
- The coordinate-space cutoff R = 0.9 fm was used for the three-nucleon force calculations at N²LO

## Context

This review extends the chiral EFT program of Weinberg (1990) and the author's own prior work on nuclear forces (Epelbaum 1998, 2006, 2014) by presenting the most precise chiral NN potentials to date at N⁴LO⁺ from Reinert (2017). The work enables systematic improvement of three-nucleon force calculations beyond N²LO, which is needed to resolve long-standing discrepancies in nucleon-deuteron scattering spin observables that persist across high-precision phenomenological potentials and the Tucson-Melbourne and Urbana IX three-nucleon force models.
