# Contribution of the Weinberg-type operator to atomic electric dipole moments

> We report the calculation of the isovector CP-odd $πN$ interaction generated by the Weinberg operator, the CP violating three-gluon operator. This enables to restrict models which induce the operator by using experimental data of atomic electric dipole moment. The QCD sum rules are used to determine the matrix element required to assess the CP-odd $πN$ interactions. The relation between the Weinberg operator and the Hg and Xe EDMs is clarified, and the constraint on the Weinberg operator is obtained using the experimental data for the Hg EDM.

## Key claim
The isovector CP-odd πN interaction generated by the Weinberg operator contributes significantly to atomic electric dipole moments, contrary to the chiral perturbation theory expectation that it would be negligible compared to the neutron EDM contribution. For the Xe EDM, the πN interaction contribution may even exceed that of the neutron EDM.

## Method
The author used QCD sum rules to compute the pion-vacuum transition matrix element ⟨0|L_W|π^0⟩, which is the low-energy constant needed to determine the isovector CP-odd πN coupling constant ḡ^(1)_πNN. The correlation function ⟨0|L_W(x)L_W(0)|0⟩ was expanded using the operator product expansion, and the Borel transformation with differentiation with respect to (m_u - m_d)^2 was applied to isolate the pion pole from glueball resonances. The resulting coupling was then used in chiral perturbation theory to compute the atomic EDMs of Hg and Xe through the Schiff moment mechanism.

## Key numbers
- |⟨0|L_W|π^0⟩| ∈ w · [2.3, 8.3] × 10^(-6) GeV^5 (theoretical uncertainty from Borel mass and threshold parameter variation)
- d^Hg = w(-1.3 × 10^(-2) ± [0.71, 4.4] × 10^(-3)) e MeV (neutron EDM term plus πN interaction term with uncertainty range)
- d^Xe = w(2.7 × 10^(-4) ± [0.52, 3.2] × 10^(-4)) e MeV (neutron EDM term plus πN interaction term with uncertainty range)
- For Hg EDM, the maximal πN interaction contribution is 34% of the neutron EDM contribution
- |w(μ=1 TeV)| < 4 × 10^(-10) GeV^(-2) (constraint from Hg EDM experimental data by Graner (2016))

## Context
This work extends the recent calculations by Osamura (2022) and Yamanaka (2022) on the CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction from the Weinberg operator, which had only been evaluated quite recently. The Weinberg operator was first calculated in the Higgs doublet model by Weinberg (1989), and later found to be generated in supersymmetric models by Dai (1990). While the relation between this operator and the neutron EDM has been widely investigated by Demir (2002), Haisch (2019), and Yamanaka (2020), this work clarifies the connection to atomic EDMs of Hg and Xe, enabling experimental constraints on models that induce the Weinberg operator. The discrepancy between lattice estimates (30 MeV) and phenomenological estimates (60 MeV) of the πN sigma term σ_πN by Yamanaka (2018), Yang (2015), Alexandrou (2019) versus Hoferichter (2015) and Friedman (2019) is noted as an important uncertainty.
