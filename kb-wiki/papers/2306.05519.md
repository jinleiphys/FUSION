# Double particle production in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider and Future Circular Collider

> In this paper we analyze the associated production of a vector meson with a (pseudo)scalar bound state or a dimuon system in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions through the double scattering mechanism for the energies of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and Future Circular Collider (FCC). Our results complement previous studies for the double vector meson production. We present our predictions for the total cross sections and rapidity distributions considering the rapidity ranges covered by the ALICE and LHCb detectors, which indicate that a future experimental analysis of the $ϕ+ η_c$, $ϕ+ μ^+ μ^-$ and $J/Ψ+ μ^+ μ^-$ final states is feasible.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the associated production of a vector meson (J/Ψ or φ) with an ηc meson or a dimuon system in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions via the double scattering mechanism is feasible for future experimental analysis at the LHC and FCC, but only if the final state does not involve a true muonium bound state, which yields cross sections too small to be measured.

## Method

The double differential cross section for producing two distinct particles via simultaneous γPomeron and γγ interactions is computed as the product of single-production probabilities integrated over impact parameter, assuming no correlations. The vector meson photoproduction cross section is estimated using the color dipole formalism with the Gaus-LC model and Glauber-Gribov non-forward scattering amplitude, while the γγ production of ηc, true muonium, or dimuon pairs is calculated using the Low formula or Breit-Wheeler formula, respectively. The equivalent photon spectrum is derived from the Wood-Saxon charge form factor of the lead nucleus.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for φ + ηc production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 11.07 × 10² nb.
* Total cross section for φ + ηc production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the ALICE central rapidity range (−2.5 < y < 2.5): 5.09 × 10² nb.
* Total cross section for φ + ηc production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the LHCb forward rapidity range (2.0 < y < 4.5): 0.41 × 10² nb.
* Total cross section for J/Ψ + ηc production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 29.72 nb.
* Total cross section for φ + μ⁺μ⁻ production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 2.78 × 10⁶ nb.
* Total cross section for J/Ψ + μ⁺μ⁻ production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 7.43 × 10⁴ nb.
* Total cross section for φ + TM (true muonium) production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 1.29 nb.
* Total cross section for J/Ψ + TM production at LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 3.45 × 10⁻² nb.
* Total cross section for φ + ηc production at FCC (√s = 39 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 32.03 × 10² nb.
* Total cross section for φ + μ⁺μ⁻ production at FCC (√s = 39 TeV) in the full rapidity range: 6.75 × 10⁶ nb.
* Expected number of φ + ηc events at LHC with integrated luminosity 7 nb⁻¹: ≥ 10³ (central), ≥ 10² (forward).
* Expected number of φ + μ⁺μ⁻ events at LHC with integrated luminosity 7 nb⁻¹: ≥ 10⁷ (central), ≥ 10⁶ (forward).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of double vector meson production via the double scattering mechanism in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, specifically the φJ/Ψ production analyzed by Azevedo (2022) and the general formalism developed by Klusek-Gawenda (2013) and others. The authors introduce for the first time the mixed case where one particle is produced by γPomeron interactions (vector meson) and the other by γγ interactions (scalar or dilepton), complementing the earlier work that considered only two γPomeron interactions. The results enable future experimental searches by the LHCb and ALICE Collaborations for these mixed final states, which could provide new tests of the double scattering mechanism and QCD dynamics at high energies.
