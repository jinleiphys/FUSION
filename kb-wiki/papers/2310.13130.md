# Describing the speed of sound peak of isospin-asymmetric cold strongly interacting matter using effective models

> The non-monotonic behavior of the speed of sound for isospin imbalanced strongly interacting matter, found by recent lattice QCD simulations, can be reproduced within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and Linear Sigma Model with quarks when the couplings become isospin chemical potential-dependent. The introduction of medium-dependent couplings can potentially affect the equivalence between the thermodynamic relations and their definitions from statistical mechanics. We describe the procedure to compensate for the introduction of medium-dependent couplings to preserve the correct thermodynamic identities. We find the isospin chemical potential dependence for the couplings from the isospin density LQCD data and, after finding the compensating function to correctly describe the pressure, we show that the description of the square of the speed of sound reported by LQCD is well reproduced when using the found medium-dependent couplings in both models.

## Key claim

The non-monotonic peak in the speed of sound for isospin-imbalanced strongly interacting matter, observed in lattice QCD simulations, can be reproduced by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and the Linear Sigma Model with quarks (LSMq) only when the couplings are made dependent on the isospin chemical potential, and a compensating function is introduced to preserve thermodynamic consistency.

## Method

The authors introduced isospin chemical potential-dependent couplings G(μ_I) in the NJL model and g(μ_I) in the LSMq, fitting these couplings to lattice QCD data for the isospin density from Brandt et al. (2022). They then applied a thermodynamic consistency procedure, adding a compensating function B* to the effective pressure to correct for the medium-dependent couplings, ensuring that the statistical mechanics and thermodynamic definitions of number density coincide. The speed of sound squared was computed from the resulting thermodynamically consistent pressure and energy density at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters used: current quark mass m = 4.76 MeV, ultraviolet cutoff Λ = 659 MeV, fixed coupling G₀ = 4.78 GeV⁻², producing pion mass m_π = 131.7 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV, and quark condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩¹/³ = -250 MeV.
- The density-dependent NJL coupling G(μ_I) varies by about 6% from its nominal value, with a maximum of approximately 4.8 GeV⁻² and a minimum of approximately 4.5 GeV⁻² across the considered μ_I range.
- The LSMq parameters used: constituent quark mass m_q = 270 MeV, sigma mass m_σ = 450 MeV, m_π = 131.7 MeV, f_π = 92.4 MeV, yielding λ = 10.84, a = 274.29 MeV, and g = 2.9.
- The onset of pion condensation in the LSMq occurs when μ_I² > m_π², corresponding to μ_I > 131.7 MeV.
- The NJL coupling G(μ_I) reaches a minimum around μ_I ≈ 1.66 m_π.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using medium-dependent couplings previously applied to describe inverse magnetic catalysis in the NJL model by Farias et al. (2014, 2016) and in the LSMq by Ayala et al. (2014, 2021). It directly addresses the speed of sound peak found in lattice QCD simulations by Brandt et al. (2022), which has been interpreted by Chiba et al. (2023) as a signature of quark saturation for the onset of quark matter formation. The thermodynamic consistency procedure follows the framework developed by Gorenstein and Yang (1995) for systems with medium-dependent parameters. The results enable effective model descriptions of isospin-asymmetric matter that can be compared with lattice QCD and applied to dense astrophysical objects such as neutron stars.
