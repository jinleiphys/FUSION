# An alternative way to decipher the nature of the doubly charmed tetraquark $T_{cc}(3875)^+$: its antiparticle $T_{{\bar c}{\bar c}}(3875)^-$ photoproduction off nuclei near threshold

> We study the inclusive photoproduction of $T_{{\bar c}{\bar c}}(3875)^-$ mesons (which are the antiparticles of the doubly charmed tetraquarks $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ discovered recently by the LHCb Collaboration) from nuclei in the near-threshold energy region within the nuclear spectral function approach by considering incoherent direct ($╬│p(n) \to D^+(D^0){T_{{\bar c}{\bar c}}(3875)^-}╬ø^+_c$) photon--nucleon $T_{{\bar c}{\bar c}}(3875)^-$ creation processes as well as five possible different scenarios for their internal structure with the main goal of clarifying the possibility to decipher this structure (and, hence, that of $T_{cc}(3875)^+$) in photoproduction via integral and differential observables. We calculate the absolute and relative excitation functions for $T_{{\bar c}{\bar c}}(3875)^-$ production off $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W target nuclei at near-threshold photon beam energies of 30--38 GeV, the absolute differential cross sections for their production off these target nuclei at laboratory polar angles of 0$^{\circ}$--10$^{\circ}$ as well as the A and momentum dependences of the relative (transparency ratios) cross sections for $T_{{\bar c}{\bar c}}(3875)^-$ production at photon energy of 35 GeV within the adopted scenarios for the $T_{{\bar c}{\bar c}}(3875)^-$ meson intrinsic structure. We demonstrate that the absolute and relative observables considered show a certain sensitivity to these scenarios. Therefore, the measurement of such observables in future experiments at the proposed high-luminosity electron-ion colliders EIC and EicC in the US and China in the near-threshold energy region might shed light on the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ internal structure.

## Key claim

The internal structure of the doubly charmed tetraquark \(T_{cc}(3875)^+\) can be deciphered by measuring photoproduction observables of its antiparticle \(T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-\) off nuclei near threshold, provided that the absolute and relative cross sections show sufficient sensitivity to distinguish between a compact tetraquark, a molecular state, or a hybrid configuration.

## Method

The inclusive photoproduction of \(T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-\) mesons from nuclei is calculated within the nuclear spectral function approach, considering only the direct incoherent elementary processes \(\gamma p \to D^+ T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^- \Lambda_c^+\) and \(\gamma n \to D^0 T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^- \Lambda_c^+\). Five scenarios for the internal structure of \(T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-\) are adopted: a pure compact tetraquark (4q), a pure \(D^- \bar D^{*0} - \bar D^0 D^{*-}\) molecule, and three hybrid mixtures with (80%,20%), (50%,50%), and (20%,80%) probability weights between the compact and molecular components. The \(T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-\)-nucleon absorption cross section is estimated for each scenario and used to compute the nuclear suppression via the nuclear spectral function and a Woods-Saxon or harmonic-oscillator density profile for target nuclei.

## Key numbers

* The free production threshold for the elementary \(\gamma p \to D^+ T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^- \Lambda_c^+\) reaction is approximately 33.9 GeV.
* The free total cross section for \(\gamma p \to D^+ T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^- \Lambda_c^+\) is parametrized as \(\sigma = 2.409\,(1 - s_{\rm th}/s)^{1.745}\) pb, reaching values of order 0.05 pb at center-of-mass energies \(W \le 8.5\) GeV.
* The \(T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-\)-nucleon absorption cross section is taken as 10.7 mb for the compact tetraquark, 15.0 mb for the hybrid (80% compact, 20% molecular), 21.35 mb for the hybrid (50%,50%), 27.7 mb for the hybrid (20%,80%), and 32 mb for the pure molecular state.
* The r.m.s. separation of the anticharmed mesons in the molecular scenario is estimated as \(r_T = 5.3 \pm 0.3\) fm, based on a binding energy of \(\delta = 360 \pm 40^{+4}_{-0}\) keV relative to the \(D^0 D^{*+}\) threshold.
* The kinematically allowed laboratory momenta for the \(T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-\) meson in the direct process on a free proton at rest at \(E_\gamma = 35\) GeV range from 13.748 to 20.066 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to the \(X(3872)\) resonance by Paryev (2024) to the doubly charmed tetraquark antiparticle \(T_{\bar c \bar c}(3875)^-\), using photoproduction off nuclei as a cleaner probe than hadronic collisions. It builds on the LHCb discovery of \(T_{cc}(3875)^+\) (2021) and on theoretical studies of its possible molecular, compact tetraquark, or hybrid nature (e.g., Refs. [11-19, 24, 51-73]). The predictions are intended to be tested at the proposed high-luminosity electron-ion colliders EIC (US) and EicC (China), enabling discrimination among the five structural scenarios via excitation functions, differential cross sections, and transparency ratios.
