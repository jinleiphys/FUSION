# Effect of large neutron excess on the dipole response in the region of the Giant Dipole Resonance

> The evolution of the Dipole Response in nuclei with strong neutron excess is studied in the Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation with Skyrme forces. We find that the neutron excess increases the fragmentation of the isovector Giant Dipole Resonance, while pushing the centroid of the distribution to lower energies beyond the mass dependence predicted by the collective models. The radial separation of proton and neutron densities associated with a large neutron excess leads to non vanishing isoscalar transition densities to the GDR states, which are therefore predicted to be excited also by isoscalar nuclear probes. The evolution of the isoscalar compression dipole mode as a function of the neutron excess is finally studied. We find that the large neutron excess leads to a strong concentration of the strength associated with the isoscalar dipole operator $\sum_ir^3_iY_{10}$, that mainly originates from uncorrelated excitations of the neutrons of the skin.

# Effect of large neutron excess on the dipole response in the region of the Giant Dipole Resonance

## Key claim

The neutron excess increases the fragmentation of the isovector Giant Dipole Resonance and pushes its centroid to lower energies beyond the mass dependence predicted by collective models, but the authors note that the use of other Skyrme interactions may give rather smaller neutron separation energies, leading to different features in the very low energy part of the response, though this should not alter the medium and high energy regions which are the object of the investigation.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with the Skyrme SGII interaction for several isotopes of oxygen and calcium, followed by Random Phase Approximation (RPA) using the full residual interaction. The continuum states needed for the RPA were expanded in oscillator functions of different principal quantum number, a procedure found adequate for these systems because the last neutrons are not too weakly bound. The dipole response was calculated for the isovector operator $\sum_i z_i\tau_i^3$ and the isoscalar operator $\sum_i z_i$, and the spurious center-of-mass state was identified and eliminated.

## Key numbers

- The HF calculations predict the last neutron to be bound by 3.25 MeV in $^{28}$O and 5.1 MeV in $^{60}$Ca.
- In both $^{28}$O and $^{60}$Ca, the most collective state only exhausts approximately 15% of the total Energy-Weighted Sum Rule (EWSR).
- The full width at half maximum (FWHM) increased by about 50% going from the N=Z to the most neutron rich isotope.
- The centroid changes from 19 MeV in $^{40}$Ca to 16 MeV in $^{60}$Ca, a shift which is a factor two larger than the prediction of the Goldhaber-Teller model.
- The states selected for transition density analysis in $^{28}$O and $^{60}$Ca have 18 and 17.4 MeV of excitation energy and carry 8% and 16% of the EWSR, respectively.
- For the isoscalar compression dipole mode, the correction over the $A^{7/3}$ scaling is of the order of few percent even in the extreme case of $^{70}$Ca.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron skin effects on collective states in neutron-rich nuclei (referenced as works 1-4 in the paper) and the possibility of exciting the isovector GDR by isoscalar probes through hadronic interactions (referenced as works 5-7). The authors compare their results to the Goldhaber-Teller model (referenced as work 10) and the hydrodynamical model (referenced as work 11), finding that the centroid shift is larger than the $A^{-1/6}$ dependence of the former and closer to the $A^{-1/3}$ scaling of the latter. The work enables future experimental extraction of the neutron skin via Coulomb-nuclear interference measurements, as suggested by several authors (referenced as works 5-7, 12-13), and extends the study of the compressional isoscalar dipole resonance (referenced as works 14-15) from stable nuclei to neutron-rich systems near the drip line.
