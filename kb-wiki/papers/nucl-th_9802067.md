# Electron-deuteron scattering in a current-conserving description of relativistic bound states: formalism and impulse approximation calculations

> The electromagnetic interactions of a relativistic two-body bound state are formulated in three dimensions using an equal-time (ET) formalism. This involves a systematic reduction of four-dimensional dynamics to a three-dimensional form by integrating out the time components of relative momenta. A conserved electromagnetic current is developed for the ET formalism. It is shown that consistent truncations of the electromagnetic current and the $NN$ interaction kernel may be made, order-by-order in the coupling constants, such that appropriate Ward-Takahashi identities are satisfied. A meson-exchange model of the $NN$ interaction is used to calculate deuteron vertex functions. Calculations of electromagnetic form factors for elastic scattering of electrons by deuterium are performed using an impulse-approximation current. Negative-energy components of the deuteron's vertex function and retardation effects in the meson-exchange interaction are found to have only minor effects on the deuteron form factors.

## Key claim

The paper develops a three-dimensional equal-time (ET) formalism for relativistic two-body bound states that systematically reduces four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter dynamics while preserving current conservation via Ward-Takahashi identities; however, the impulse-approximation calculations presented do not include the full two-body currents needed for exact current conservation when retardation effects are included, and the authors find that including relativistic effects and negative-energy states does not improve agreement with experimental data for deuteron form factors.

## Method

The authors start from a four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation and apply a systematic reduction that integrates out the time component of relative momentum to obtain a three-dimensional equal-time (ET) scattering equation. They construct a conserved electromagnetic current for the ET formalism by gauging the modified four-dimensional propagator that includes an eikonal-approximation term \(G_C\), ensuring correct one-body limits. Deuteron vertex functions are computed from a meson-exchange model (based on Bonn-B parameters with a fitted \(\sigma\)-meson coupling) using both instant interactions and retarded ET interactions, and elastic electron-deuteron scattering observables \(A\), \(B\), and \(T_{20}\) are calculated in the impulse approximation using a simplified conserved current.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy is fitted by adjusting the \(\sigma\)-meson coupling so that the \(NN\) bound-state pole in the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channel appears at the deuteron mass (no explicit numerical value given for the coupling).
* The minimum in the magnetic form factor \(B\) shifts to somewhat larger \(Q^2\) when negative-energy states are included, but the shift is not sufficient to bring impulse-approximation calculations into agreement with experimental data.
* For the tensor polarization \(T_{20}\), all models produce very similar results up to \(Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* Above \(Q^2 \approx 0.5\ \text{GeV}^2\), two-body current contributions become important for the observables.

## Context

This work extends the equal-time formalism developed by Mandelzweig and Wallace (1987) and Wallace and Mandelzweig (1989), and builds on previous three-dimensional reduction techniques by Phillips and Wallace (1996, 1997). It contrasts with the spectator formalism of Gross (1969, 1992) and the Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze (BSLT) quasipotential approach used by Hummel and Tjon (1989, 1990, 1994), noting that the ET propagator has twice the negative-energy state propagation amplitude of either of those approaches. The calculations enable future inclusion of meson-exchange currents (such as \(\rho\pi\gamma\) and \(\omega\sigma\gamma\)) and the full retarded current needed for exact current conservation, which the authors identify as necessary before reliable comparison with experimental data from TJNAF can be made.
