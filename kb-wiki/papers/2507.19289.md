# Determination of the $ϕ$-meson production process and its absorption cross section via directed flow

> We show that the directed flow of $ϕ$-mesons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV, is sensitive on production and the absorption cross section of the $ϕ$ in a nuclear medium. This provides a new observable to constrain the in-medium properties of the $ϕ$ which is independent of its absolute production rate. It is shown that the STAR data disfavor any significant $ϕ$-N absorption in dense nuclear matter and are consistent with a very small cross section of the $ϕ$ comparable to the vacuum cross section. The similarity of the $ϕ$-meson and proton directed flow also indicates that the $ϕ$ is produced in conjunction with a baryon.

## Key claim

The directed flow of $\phi$-mesons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV is sensitive to the in-medium absorption cross section of the $\phi$, and the STAR data disfavor any significant $\phi$-nucleon absorption in dense nuclear matter, being consistent with a very small cross section comparable to the vacuum cross section. The similarity of the $\phi$-meson and proton directed flow also indicates that the $\phi$ is produced in conjunction with a baryon.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (v4.0) to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV, including a realistic equation of state via momentum-dependent potentials from the CMF model. They compared the directed flow $v_1(y)=\langle p_x/p_T \rangle(y)$ of various hadrons and resonances to STAR data for 10-40% most central collisions. For the $\phi$-meson, they compared three scenarios: a default absorption cross section constrained by detailed balance from p+p reactions (not exceeding 2 mb), a fixed constant absorption cross section of 20 mb, and a scenario with production cross section increased by a factor of 100 with consistent absorption via detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- The default $\phi$-nucleon absorption cross section from detailed balance does not exceed 2 mb, even for very small relative momenta.
- The fixed absorption scenario uses a constant cross section of 20 mb, in line with conclusions from Hartmann (2012).
- The $\phi$ vacuum lifetime is approximately $\tau_{\phi}\approx 40$ fm/$c$.
- The collision energy is $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- The centrality selection is 10-40% most central reactions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\phi$ absorption in p+A collisions by Hartmann (2012) and Ishikawa (2004), which suggested a large absorption cross section of about 20 mb based on mass-number scaling of production cross sections. The authors had previously shown in Steinheimer (2015) that the observed reduction of $\phi$ production on different target nuclei could be reproduced without additional in-medium effects. This paper provides an alternative, production-rate-independent observable (directed flow) to distinguish between reduced production and absorption, and proposes extending the study to $J/\psi$ directed flow at the CBM@FAIR experiment.
