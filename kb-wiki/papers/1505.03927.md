# Momentum-space calculation of ${}^4$He triatomic system with realistic potential

> The system of three ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms with realistic interactions is studied in the momentum-space framework. It is demonstrated that short and long-range difficulties encountered in the coordinate-space calculations can be reliably resolved in the momentum-space calculations. Well-converged and accurate results are obtained for the ground and excited trimer energies, atom-dimer scattering length, phase shifts, inelasticity parameters, and elastic and breakup cross sections. A significant correction to previously published results is found for the elastic cross section at very low energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that momentum-space integral equations for the Faddeev and AGS transition operators can reliably resolve both the short-range hard-core and long-range van der Waals tail difficulties that plague coordinate-space calculations of the three-${}^4$He system, but the abstract's claim of "significant correction to previously published results" applies specifically to the elastic cross section at very low energy, where the present work finds strong $l=0$ dominance that contradicts the unusual energy and angular momentum dependence reported by Suno (2008).

## Method

The author solves the symmetrized Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) version of the Faddeev equations in momentum-space partial-wave basis, using the LM2M2 realistic two-atom potential by Aziz and Slaman. The coordinate-space potential is transformed to momentum-space via Bessel transforms with a modified potential that introduces cut-off parameters $r_{\rm min}$ and $r_{\rm max}$ to study convergence, and the resulting integral equations are solved using real-axis integration with subtraction, spline interpolation, and Padé summation, including partial waves up to $L \le 12$.

## Key numbers

- Dimer binding energy $e_d = 1.3035$ mK (converged value)
- Ground trimer binding energy $E_t = 126.40$ mK
- Excited trimer binding energy $E_{t*} = 2.271$ mK
- Atom-dimer scattering length $a_0 = 115.21$ Å
- At $E_k = 40$ mK: $S$-wave phase shift $\delta_0 = 24.93^\circ$, inelasticity $\eta_0 = 0.8283$; $P$-wave $\delta_1 = -73.78^\circ$, $\eta_1 = 0.7676$
- At $E_k = 0.1$ mK: $\delta_0 = 158.05^\circ$, $\eta_0 = 1.0000$ (elastic); $\delta_1 = -2.39^\circ$
- Results become independent of $r_{\rm min}$ for $r_{\rm min} \le 1.7$ Å and independent of $r_{\rm max}$ for $r_{\rm max} \ge 75$ Å, compared to coordinate-space calculations requiring integration above 1000 Å

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space techniques successfully used in three-nucleon scattering calculations (Gloeckle 1996, Deltuva 2003) to the atomic ${}^4$He system, providing benchmark results that agree with the most accurate coordinate-space Faddeev results from Lazauskas (2010) and the variational Gaussian expansion method of Hiyama (2012). The paper resolves discrepancies in low-energy elastic cross sections reported by Suno (2008) and provides the first calculations of semi-inclusive and fully exclusive breakup differential cross sections for the ${}^4$He trimer, enabling future comparisons with nucleon-deuteron breakup studies.
