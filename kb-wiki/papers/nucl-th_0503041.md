# New features of some proton-neutron collective states

> Using a schematic solvable many-body Hamiltonian, one studies a new type of proton-neutron excitations within a time dependent variational approach. Classical equations of motion are linearized and subsequently solved analytically. The harmonic state energy is compared with the energy of the first excited state provided by diagonalization as well as with the energies obtained by a renormalized RPA and a boson expansion procedure. The new collective mode describes a wobbling motion, in the space of isospin, and collapses for a particle-particle interaction strength which is much larger than the physical value. A suggestion for the description of the system in the second nuclear phase is made. We identified the transition operators which might excite the new mode from the ground state.

## Key claim

Using a schematic solvable many-body Hamiltonian and a time-dependent variational approach, the authors identify a new type of proton-neutron collective mode that describes a wobbling motion in isospin space; this mode collapses for a particle-particle interaction strength much larger than the physical value, and the authors suggest it may describe the system in a second nuclear phase.

## Method

The authors employed a time-dependent variational principle (TDVP) with a coherent trial state for the SU(2) isospin group, derived from a schematic Hamiltonian restricted to a single j-shell with proton-neutron scattering terms. Classical equations of motion were linearized around the minimum of the energy function and solved analytically to obtain the harmonic mode frequency. The results were compared with exact diagonalization, a renormalized RPA (frn-RPA), and a Holstein-Primakoff boson expansion procedure.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strengths used were \(G_p = 0.2\) MeV and \(G_n = 0.4\) MeV, applied to a system with 6 protons and 14 neutrons in the \(j = 19/2\) shell.
- The harmonic mode angular frequency is given by \(\omega = 2\frac{2T-1}{T}[-\lambda_2(\lambda_1-2\lambda_2)\stackrel{\circ}{r}(2T-\stackrel{\circ}{r})]^{\frac{1}{2}}\), where \(T\) is the total quasiparticle isospin and \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2\) are interaction parameters defined in the text.
- The renormalized RPA mode energy is \(\omega = [(E_p-E_n+\lambda_1 C)^2 - 4\lambda_2^2 C^2]^{1/2}\), with \(C = 2T - 2 + 2/X^2\) and \(X\) the phonon amplitude.
- The boson expansion harmonic energy is \(\omega_1 = [(E_p-E_n+2\lambda_1 T)^2 - 16\lambda_2^2 T^2]^{1/2}\), which equals the renormalized RPA energy when \(X=1\) (i.e., \(\lambda_2=0\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fully renormalized RPA (frn-RPA) formalism (Raduta et al., 2003), which introduced a new proton-neutron boson involving scattering terms beyond the standard pnQRPA. The semi-classical treatment confirms that the new mode appears naturally in the harmonic approximation and provides an intuitive interpretation as a wobbling motion in isospin space. The approach enables future studies of the second nuclear phase and suggests transition operators that could excite the new mode from the ground state, relevant for double beta decay calculations.
