# Infrared renormalon effects in color dipole TMD PDF at small-$x$

> The uncertainties from the infrared renormalons in the (color dipole) gluon distribution is estimated. It is shown that non-linear saturation effects at small-$x$ shift the first IR pole at the Borel plane from $2/β_2$ to $1/β_2$. As a consequence the estimated uncertainty is found to be ${\cal O}\left(Λ_{\rm QCD}^2\right)$ instead of ${\cal O}\left(Λ_{\rm QCD}^4\right)$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that non-linear saturation effects at small-$x$ shift the first infrared renormalon pole in the Borel plane from \(2/\beta_2\) to \(1/\beta_2\), leading to an estimated non-perturbative uncertainty of \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2)\) rather than the previously expected \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^4)\) for the color dipole gluon distribution.

## Method

The authors start from the relation between the dipole amplitude and the unintegrated dipole gluon distribution, then use a recently derived expression for the gluon distribution at asymptotically small transverse momentum (Abir 2018) that behaves as a logarithmic times log-normal distribution. They expand the QCD running coupling in powers of \(\alpha_s(\mu)\), perform the transverse momentum integration, and apply Borel summation to identify the infrared renormalon pole position and estimate the associated uncertainty.

## Key numbers

* The first infrared renormalon pole in the Borel plane shifts from \(2/\beta_2\) to \(1/\beta_2\) due to small-\(x\) saturation effects, where \(\beta_2\) is the QCD beta function.
* The resulting non-perturbative uncertainty is \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2)\) instead of \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^4)\), with the explicit form \(r_\perp^2 \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2 \ln(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2 / 4Q_s^2) \exp[-\tau \ln^2(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2 / 4Q_s^2)]\), where \(\tau \approx 0.2\).
* The earlier estimate without saturation effects gave uncertainty proportional to \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^4\) (from the pole at \(2/\beta_2\)).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of infrared renormalons in small-\(x\) QCD first addressed by Levin (1994) and builds on the authors' own derivation of the unintegrated color dipole gluon distribution at small transverse momentum (Abir 2018). The result confirms an earlier anticipation by Mueller (1992) that the perturbation series becomes ambiguous at \(b = 1/\beta_2\) and that \(\mathcal{O}(1/Q^2)\) corrections should be pursued. The finding enables improved estimates of non-perturbative uncertainties in small-\(x\) gluon distributions and saturation physics, and shows that saturation effects suppress renormalon contributions through a Sudakov-type form factor.
