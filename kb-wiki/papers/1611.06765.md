# Computational Nuclear Physics and Post Hartree-Fock Methods

> We present a computational approach to infinite nuclear matter employing Hartree-Fock theory, many-body perturbation theory and coupled cluster theory. These lectures are closely linked with those of chapters 9, 10 and 11 and serve as input for the correlation functions employed in Monte Carlo calculations in chapter 9, the in-medium similarity renormalization group theory of dense fermionic systems of chapter 10 and the Green's function approach in chapter 11. We provide extensive code examples and benchmark calculations, allowing thereby an eventual reader to start writing her/his own codes. We start with an object-oriented serial code and end with discussions on strategies for porting the code to present and planned high-performance computing facilities.

## Key claim

This lecture chapter presents a pedagogical computational framework for infinite nuclear matter using Hartree-Fock theory, many-body perturbation theory, and coupled cluster theory, but limits its demonstrations to pure neutron matter with a simplified two-body interaction to avoid the full complexity of nuclear forces, noting that the codes can be extended to include effective field theory interactions and other baryon species.

## Method

The authors define a plane-wave single-particle basis in a periodic box with closed-shell filling, compute the kinetic energy and two-body interaction matrix elements in momentum space, and then apply Hartree-Fock theory, many-body perturbation theory, and coupled cluster theory to the normal-ordered Hamiltonian. The formalism is developed in second quantization with anti-symmetrized matrix elements, and the single-particle basis is constructed from discretized momenta with periodic boundary conditions to approximate infinite matter.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 approximately 0.16 +/- 0.02 fm^{-3}
- Energy per baryon at saturation for symmetric nuclear matter: E_0 = B/A = -15.6 +/- 0.2 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: range 27-38 MeV (citing Horowitz 2014)
- Magic numbers for one spin-1/2 fermion species in Cartesian basis: 2, 14, 38, 54, 66, and 114 particles for filled shells
- Magic numbers for symmetric nuclear matter (protons and neutrons): 4, 28, 76, 108, 132, 228 particles
- Density relation: rho = g * k_F^3 / (6 pi^2), where g = 2 for one spin-1/2 species and g = 4 for symmetric nuclear matter

## Context

This work provides the computational foundation for the three subsequent chapters on Monte Carlo methods (chapter 9), in-medium similarity renormalization group theory (chapter 10), and Green's function approaches (chapter 11) for dense fermionic systems. It extends the long tradition of nuclear matter studies dating back to Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory (Day 1967) and connects to modern ab initio methods using nuclear forces from effective field theory (Weinberg 1990, 1991; Epelbaum 2009; Machleidt 2011). The pedagogical code examples enable readers to implement their own calculations and eventually port them to high-performance computing facilities.
