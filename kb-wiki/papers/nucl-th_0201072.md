# Study of Postacceleration Effects in the Coulomb Dissociation of Neutron Halo Nuclei

> We investgate the breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei in the Coulomb field of a target nucleus. In the post-form distorted wave Born approximation theory of this reaction, with only Coulomb distortions in the entrance and the outgoing channels, an analytic solution for the breakup $T$-matrix is known. We study this $T$-matrix and the corresponding cross-sections numerically. This formula can be related to the first order semiclassical treatment of the electromagnetic dissociation. This theory contains the electromagnetic interaction between the core and the target nucleus to all orders. We show that higher order effects (including postacceleration) are small in the case of higher beam energies and forward scattering. We investigate the beam energy dependence of the postacceleration effects. They are found to be quite important for smaller beam energies (slow collisions), but almost negligible at larger ones.

# Study of Postacceleration Effects in the Coulomb Dissociation of Neutron Halo Nuclei

## Key claim
Higher order electromagnetic effects, including postacceleration, are found to be quite important for beam energies at or below 30 MeV/nucleon but almost negligible at beam energies around 70 MeV/nucleon, contradicting some earlier claims that substantial higher order effects persist even at higher energies.

## Method
The authors used the post-form distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) with pure Coulomb distortions in both entrance and outgoing channels, for which an analytic solution of the breakup T-matrix exists. They calculated triple and double differential cross sections as functions of core fragment energy, as well as relative energy distributions of the outgoing fragments, for the breakup of 11Be and 19C on a 208Pb target. The full finite-range CWBA (Coulomb wave Born approximation) results were compared with a first-order approximation extracted from the same theoretical framework, allowing a consistent assessment of higher order effects across beam energies from 5 to 72 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- For 11Be breakup on 208Pb at 72 MeV/nucleon: higher order and first-order cross sections peak at the same core energy (beam velocity energy), indicating negligible postacceleration.
- For 11Be breakup at 30 MeV/nucleon, 10 MeV/nucleon, and 5 MeV/nucleon: the higher order cross section peaks are shifted to energies above the beam velocity energy, demonstrating postacceleration.
- The ground state binding energy of 11Be was taken as 504 keV; for 19C the separation energy was 0.530 MeV.
- For 19C breakup on 208Pb at 67 MeV/nucleon: higher order effects are weak; at 30 MeV/nucleon and 10 MeV/nucleon they are appreciable.
- The first-order semiclassical scaling formula [Eq. (25)] shows that breakup probability depends on the dimensionless variable x = q/kappa, where kappa is related to the c-n separation energy.

## Context
This work extends the finite-range CWBA formalism developed by Chatterjee, Banerjee, and Shyam (2000) and the analytic T-matrix solution of Baur and Trautmann (1972). It directly addresses the question of higher order electromagnetic effects in Coulomb dissociation, which had been studied by Typel and Baur (2001) and Typel and Shyam (2001) using different approaches. The results support the validity of first-order semiclassical analyses used by Nakamura et al. (1994, 1999) for 11Be and 19C breakup data at beam energies around 70 MeV. The paper provides a theoretical laboratory for relating quantal and semiclassical treatments and clarifies the conditions under which postacceleration effects must be considered in astrophysical radiative capture studies via Coulomb dissociation.
