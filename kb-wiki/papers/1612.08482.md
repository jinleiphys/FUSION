# Leading order relativistic hyperon-nucleon interactions in chiral effective field theory

> We apply a recently proposed covariant power counting in nucleon-nucleon interactions to study strangeness $S=-1$ $ΛN-ΣN$ interactions in chiral effective field theory. At leading order, Lorentz invariance introduces 12 low energy constants, in contrast to the heavy baryon approach, where only five appear. The Kadyshevsky equation is adopted to resum the potential in order to account for the non-perturbative nature of hyperon-nucleon interactions. A fit to the $36$ hyperon-nucleon scattering data points yields $χ^2\simeq 16$, which is comparable with the sophisticated phenomenological models and the next-to-leading order heavy baryon approach. However, one cannot achieve a simultaneous description of the nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon scattering data at leading order.

## Key claim
The authors apply a covariant power counting scheme, previously used for nucleon-nucleon interactions, to strangeness S=-1 hyperon-nucleon interactions at leading order. They achieve a chi-squared of about 16 for 36 scattering data points, comparable to sophisticated phenomenological models and next-to-leading order heavy baryon approaches, but they cannot simultaneously describe nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and hyperon-nucleon scattering data at leading order.

## Method
The authors construct leading order hyperon-nucleon interactions using covariant chiral effective field theory, retaining full Dirac spinors for baryons rather than performing a non-relativistic reduction. The potential includes non-derivative four-baryon contact terms (with 12 independent low energy constants from five Lorentz structures) and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchange potentials derived from the covariant SU(3) meson-baryon Lagrangian. The Kadyshevsky equation is used to resum the potential non-perturbatively, with an exponential regulator applied to the high-momentum region.

## Key numbers
- Chi-squared of 16.1 for 36 hyperon-nucleon scattering data points, achieved at cutoff values between 550-650 MeV
- 12 independent low energy constants at leading order in the covariant approach, compared to 5 in the heavy baryon approach
- Lambda-proton scattering lengths fixed to a_{3S1} = -1.32 fm and a_{1S0} = -2.44 fm, with a_{3S1} uncertainty of +/- 0.02 fm and a_{1S0} uncertainty of +0.16/-0.54 fm at cutoff 600 MeV
- Pseudoscalar meson masses used: m_pi = 138.039 MeV, m_K = 495.645 MeV, m_eta = 547.853 MeV
- Average baryon mass M_B = 1080 MeV used in baryon spinors and energies
- Axial vector couplings: D+F = g_A = 1.277, F/(F+D) = 0.4
- Pseudoscalar meson decay constant f_0 = f_pi = 92.2 MeV

## Context
This work extends the covariant power counting scheme of Ren et al. (2016) from nucleon-nucleon interactions to hyperon-nucleon systems with strangeness S=-1. It improves upon the leading order heavy baryon approach of Polinder et al. (2006) and the EG approach of Epelbaum and Gegelia (2012, 2015), showing mitigated cutoff dependence and better description of scattering data. The results are comparable to the next-to-leading order heavy baryon approach of Haidenbauer et al. (2013) and phenomenological models such as NSC97a-f by Rijken et al. (1998), and provide a foundation for future higher-order calculations and applications to hypernuclear structure studies.
