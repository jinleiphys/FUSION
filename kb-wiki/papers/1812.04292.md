# Renormalization of the GT operator within the realistic shell model

> In nuclear structure calculations, the choice of a limited model space, due to computational needs, leads to the necessity to renormalize the Hamiltonian as well as any transition operator. Here, we present a study of the renormalization procedure and effects of the Gamow-Teller operator within the framework of the realistic shell model. Our effective shell-model operators are obtained, starting from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, by way of the many-body perturbation theory in order to take into account the degrees of freedom that are not explicitly included in the chosen model space. The theoretical effective shell-model Hamiltonian and transition operators are then employed in shell-model calculations, whose results are compared with data of Gamow-Teller transition strengths and double-beta half-lives for nuclei which are currently of interest for the detection of the neutrinoless double-beta decay process, in a mass interval ranging from A=48 up to A=136. We show that effective operators are able to reproduce quantitatively the spectroscopic and decay properties without resorting to an empirical quenching neither of the axial coupling constant gA, nor of the spin and orbital gyromagnetic factors. This should assess the reliability of applying present theoretical tools to this problematic.

## Key claim

The authors show that a perturbative renormalization of the Gamow-Teller operator, derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via many-body perturbation theory up to third order, can reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller transition strengths and double-beta decay half-lives for nuclei from A=48 to A=136 without introducing an empirical quenching factor for the axial coupling constant g_A or for the spin and orbital gyromagnetic factors.

## Method

Starting from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, the authors first soften it via the V_low-k approach with a cutoff of 2.6 fm^-1. They then derive effective shell-model Hamiltonians and Gamow-Teller operators using the Q-box-plus-folded-diagram method within many-body perturbation theory, including diagrams up to third order. The effective one-body transition operators incorporate a density-dependent blocking effect to account for the Pauli principle in systems with more than one valence nucleon. Shell-model calculations are performed for nuclei in the mass regions A=48, A=76, A=82, A=130, and A=136, and the results are compared with experimental data on Gamow-Teller strength distributions, 2nu-beta-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, magnetic dipole moments, and B(M1) transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The calculated 2nu-beta-beta decay nuclear matrix element for 48Ca is 0.026 MeV^-1 with the effective operator (including blocking), compared to the experimental value of 0.038 +/- 0.003 MeV^-1
- The calculated magnetic dipole moment of the 2+_1 state in 130Te is 0.71 mu_N at third order, compared to 0.65 mu_N at second order
- The calculated magnetic dipole moment of the 2+_1 state in 136Xe is 1.15 mu_N at third order, compared to 1.19 mu_N at second order
- The total GT- strength for 48Ca is 24.0 with the bare operator, 23.1 with the effective operator without blocking, and 23.0 with the effective operator including blocking
- The B(M1) strength for the 3+_1 to 2+_1 transition in 48Ca is 0.046 mu_N^2 with the effective operator, compared to the experimental value of 0.023 +/- 0.004 mu_N^2
- The chi_2 contribution to the effective operator is at most 1% of the final results, and chi_3 is estimated to be at least one order of magnitude smaller than chi_2

## Context

This work extends the approach of Suzuki and Okamoto (1995) for deriving effective shell-model operators, building on the authors' previous studies (Coraggio 2017a, Coraggio 2018b) that included only the leading chi_0 term. It contrasts with phenomenological approaches that introduce empirical quenching factors for g_A, as reviewed by Suhonen (2017b), and with the work of Siiskonen (2001) who performed a similar perturbative expansion but neglected diagrams with (G-U)-insertion vertices. The results enable more reliable calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay, where the Gamow-Teller component is a key ingredient, without resorting to empirical quenching parameters.
