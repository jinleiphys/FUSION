# On the dominance of J(P)=0(+) ground states in even-even nuclei from random two-body interactions

> Recent calculations using random two-body interactions showed a preponderance of J(P)=0(+) ground states, despite the fact that there is no strong pairing character in the force. We carry out an analysis of a system of identical particles occupying orbits with j=1/2, 3/2 and 5/2 and discuss some general features of the spectra derived from random two-body interactions. We show that for random two-body interactions that are not time-reversal invariant the dominance of 0(+) states in this case is more pronounced, indicating that time-reversal invariance cannot be the origin of the 0(+) dominance.

## Key claim

The preponderance of J^P=0^+ ground states in even-even nuclei with random two-body interactions is not caused by time-reversal invariance of the Hamiltonian, because breaking time-reversal symmetry (by using Gaussian Unitary Ensembles instead of Gaussian Orthogonal Ensembles) actually increases the percentage of 0^+ ground states rather than decreasing it.

## Method

The authors studied systems of N=2, 4, and 6 identical fermions occupying single-particle orbitals with j=1/2, 3/2, and 5/2 (the sd shell). They randomly generated two-body matrix elements using both the Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE) and the Random Quasiparticle Ensemble (RQE), and introduced time-reversal symmetry breaking by adding an imaginary antisymmetric component to the Hamiltonian matrix, controlled by a parameter epsilon ranging from 0 (GOE, time-reversal invariant) to 1 (GUE, maximally broken). For each random realization, they diagonalized the many-body Hamiltonian and recorded the angular momentum of the ground state over 1000 runs.

## Key numbers

- For N=2 particles with RQE, 64.0% of ground states are J^P=0^+, compared to 15.9% for TBRE and 21.4% expected from the model space dimension alone.
- For N=4 particles with TBRE, the percentage of 0^+ ground states jumps to 55.9%, while the model space contains only 11.1% 0^+ states.
- For N=6 particles with epsilon=0 (time-reversal invariant), RQE gives 74.2% 0^+ ground states and TBRE gives 67.7%.
- For N=6 particles with epsilon=1 (maximally broken time-reversal), RQE gives 85.7% 0^+ ground states and TBRE gives 76.8%.
- The width of the level distribution for J=0 in TBRE for N=6 is 10.16, compared to 9.01 for J=2 and 8.53 for J=1.

## Context

This work extends the surprising finding of Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998) that random two-body interactions produce a preponderance of 0^+ ground states in even-even nuclei, contrary to the traditional explanation that nuclear pairing from the short-range force is responsible. The authors tested the specific hypothesis that time-reversal invariance might be the origin of this effect, and ruled it out by showing that breaking time-reversal symmetry increases the 0^+ dominance. This enables future work to focus on the angular momentum coupling behavior and correlations in the many-body matrix elements as the true origin, which the authors state they are pursuing in an analytically tractable model.
