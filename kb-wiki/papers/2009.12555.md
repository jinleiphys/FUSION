# Associated production in pp and heavy ion collisions

> Associated particle production processes in pp and heavy ion collisions at the LHC are in particular interesting in the sense that they provide unique tools to study double parton scattering (DPS) mechanism. In this talk, I will first review the recent theoretical, phenomenological and experimental developments of DPS in pp collisions. Then, I will focus on the DPS studies in heavy ion collisions, and stress their roles in understanding the cold nuclear matter effects, such as the (poorly known) impact-parameter dependent nuclear parton densities.

## Key claim

Double parton scattering (DPS) in heavy ion collisions can be used to probe the poorly known impact-parameter-dependent nuclear parton densities (nPDFs), but the pocket formula for DPS cross sections is only a starting point and deviations from constant effective cross section sigma_eff may indicate the need for more rigorous treatment.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical developments in DPS factorisation for pp collisions, including the double DGLAP evolution of two-body parton distribution functions and the perturbative splitting contribution (1v2 or 2v1) that requires subtraction to avoid double counting. For heavy ion collisions, the DPS nuclear modification factor R_pPb for associated J/psi + D0 production is derived as a function of the single-particle R_pPb values, the effective cross section sigma_eff, and a parameter a that characterises the spatial dependence of nuclear modification in nPDFs.

## Key numbers

- dDGLAP evolution leads to 10-20% deviation below x1=x2=10^-1 relative to the product of two one-body PDFs, with deviations amplified near the threshold x1+x2=1
- For the same-sign WW final state, a 1-sigma deviation from constant sigma_eff is anticipated at the high-luminosity LHC phase with integrated luminosity 1 ab^-1
- For lead (A=Pb), the parameter r_eff,A is approximately 5.23 times (sigma_eff,pp / 34.8 mb), where sigma_eff,pp is the effective cross section in pp collisions
- The effective cross section sigma_eff,pp from various extractions (including measurements from Aad 2013, Chatrchyan 2013, Abazov 2014, Lansberg 2014, Aaboud 2016, Aaij 2012, Aaij 2015, Shao 2016, Lansberg 2016, Lansberg 2017, Shao 2020) disfavours a constant value

## Context

This talk reviews the status of DPS studies following the factorisation proof for double Drell-Yan by Diehl (2015, 2018) and extends the approach to heavy ion collisions building on earlier work by Strikman (2001) and d'Enterria (2012) on enhanced DPS in nuclear collisions. The proposed use of DPS to extract impact-parameter-dependent nPDFs is new, with the first DPS measurement in pPb collisions recently appearing from Aaij (2020). Future LHC measurements of DPS in heavy ion collisions will enable better understanding of cold nuclear matter effects from multiple perspectives.
