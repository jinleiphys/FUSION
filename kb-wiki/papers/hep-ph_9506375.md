# Dispersion-Theoretical Analysis of the Nucleon Electromagnetic Formfactors

> Dispersion relations allow for a coherent description of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors measured over a large range of momentum transfer, $Q^2 \simeq 0 \ldots 35$ GeV$^2$. Including constraints from unitarity and perturbative QCD, we present a novel parametrisation of the absorptive parts of the various isoscalar and isovector nucleon form factors. Using the current world data, we obtain results for the electromagnetic form factors, nucleon radii and meson couplings. We stress the importance of measurements at large momentum transfer to test the predictions of perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

Dispersion relations provide a largely model-independent framework for describing nucleon electromagnetic form factors across a wide range of momentum transfer, but the analysis requires imposing constraints from unitarity (particularly the two-pion continuum) and perturbative QCD to reduce the number of free parameters; the authors present a novel parametrization of the absorptive parts that respects these constraints, though the onset of asymptotic pQCD behavior remains uncertain and data at large momentum transfer are still needed to test it.

## Method

The authors constructed spectral functions for the isoscalar and isovector Dirac and Pauli form factors using dispersion relations, incorporating the two-pion continuum contribution to the isovector spectral functions via extended unitarity as formulated by Frazer and Fulco (1959), with the pion form factor updated to include rho-omega mixing. They implemented superconvergence relations derived from the asymptotic behavior dictated by perturbative QCD (quark counting rules and logarithmic corrections) and performed simultaneous fits to the world data for both proton and neutron form factors using a minimal number of additional pole terms (three isoscalar and three isovector poles) beyond the fixed two-pion continuum.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range covered: $Q^2 \simeq 0$ to $35$ GeV$^2$
- Proton electric charge radius: $r_E^p = 0.847$ fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- Proton magnetic radius: $r_M^p = 0.853$ fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- Neutron magnetic radius: $r_M^n = 0.889$ fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- Third isoscalar pole mass: $M_{S'} = 1.60$ GeV
- Second isovector pole mass: $M_{\rho''} = 1.65$ GeV
- Boundary parameter between hadronic and pQCD contributions: $\Lambda^2 \simeq 10$ GeV$^2$
- Neutron-electron scattering length used as constraint: $b_{ne} = (-1.308 \pm 0.05) \times 10^{-3}$ fm (from Kopek et al.)
- Proton charge radius from Simon et al. (1980): $<r^2_E>_p = (0.862 \pm 0.012)$ fm$^2$

## Context

This work extends and updates the classical dispersion-theoretical analysis of Hohler et al. (1976) by incorporating updated pion form factor data with rho-omega mixing, implementing pQCD asymptotic constraints similar in spirit to Furuichi and Watanabe (1980s), and using the most recent neutron charge radius determination from low-energy neutron-atom scattering (Leeb and Teichmeister, 1994). The analysis enables improved determinations of nucleon radii and vector meson-nucleon coupling constants, and provides a framework for testing pQCD predictions once more data become available at large momentum transfer; the authors note that their finding of a large Phi-nucleon coupling does not violate the OZI rule but rather accounts for the neglected kaon cloud contribution as discussed by Genz and Hohler (1970s) and Jaffe (1990s).
