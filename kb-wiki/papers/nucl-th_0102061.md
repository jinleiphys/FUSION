# Improved lower bounds for the ground-state energy of many-body systems

> New lower bounds for the binding energy of a quantum-mechanical system of interacting particles are presented. The new bounds are expressed in terms of two-particle quantities and improve the conventional bounds of the Hall-Post type. They are constructed by considering not only the energy in the two-particle system, but also the structure of the pair wave function. We apply the formal results to various numerical examples, and show that in some cases dramatic improvement over the existing bounds is reached.

## Key claim

The paper presents new lower bounds for the ground-state energy of many-body systems that improve upon the conventional Hall-Post bounds by incorporating not just the two-particle ground-state energy but also the structure of the pair wave function, specifically the maximal possible occupation number of a given two-body state in an A-body system.

## Method

The authors start from the Hall-Post decomposition of the A-body Hamiltonian into two-body clusters and the exact equality relating the A-body ground-state energy to a sum over two-body eigenstates weighted by their spectroscopic factors (occupation numbers). They then derive the maximal possible occupation number for a given two-body state in an A-boson or A-fermion system by solving an eigenvalue problem in the many-body space, using techniques from generalized pairing problems. This maximal occupation replaces the trivial upper bound in the Hall-Post inequality, yielding a sequence of improved lower bounds that are always at least as good as the Hall-Post bound and often significantly better.

## Key numbers

- For bosons, the maximal pair occupation \(S_{(2)}^{\text{max}}\) lies between \(a\) and \(\binom{A}{2}\), where \(a = (A-s)/2\) with \(s=0\) for even \(A\) and \(s=1\) for odd \(A\); the lower limit \(a\) is reached in the large-\(\Omega\) limit of a maximally correlated wave function with flat distribution \(x_\alpha = 1/\sqrt{\Omega}\).
- For fermions, the maximal pair occupation \(S_{(2)}^{\text{max}}\) lies between 1 and \(a\), where the upper limit \(a\) is reached in the large-\(\Omega\) limit of a maximally correlated wave function.
- For \(A=3\) bosons, the maximal pair occupation simplifies to \(\lambda = 1 + 2x_\beta^2\).
- For \(A=4\) bosons, the maximal pair occupation satisfies \(\sum_\alpha x_\alpha^2 / (\lambda - 1 - 4x_\alpha^2) = 1\).
- For \(A=3\) fermions, the maximal pair occupation simplifies to \(\lambda = 1 - 2x_\beta^2\).
- For \(A=4\) fermions, the maximal pair occupation satisfies \(2\sum_{\alpha>0} x_\alpha^2 / (\lambda - 1 + 4x_\alpha^2) = 1\).

## Context

This work extends the Hall-Post lower bound method (Hall and Post, 1967) for many-body ground-state energies, which has been used in quark model studies (Bashir and others, 1990s) and for Borromean systems (Richard, Goy, Moszkowski, and others, 1990s). The new bounds improve upon the optimal decomposition approaches for three unequal masses (Bashir, 1990s) and four unequal masses (Bencze, 1980s), as well as the treatment of identical fermions by Juillet (2000). The results enable tighter constraints on ground-state energies of trapped boson systems with pairing forces and self-bound nuclear systems, and the authors note that the method can be extended to systems with external potentials and nonlocal two-body potentials.
