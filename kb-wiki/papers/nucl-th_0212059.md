# Neutrino-deuteron reactions at solar neutrino energies

> In interpreting the SNO experiments, accurate estimates of the νd reaction cross sections are of great importance. In our recent work, we have improved our previous calculation by updating some of its inputs and by incorporating the results of a recent effective-field-theoretical calculation. The new cross sections are slightly (\sim 1%) larger than the previously reported values. It is reasonable to assign 1% uncertainty to the νd cross sections reported here; this error estimate does not include radiative corrections.

# Neutrino-deuteron reactions at solar neutrino energies

## Key claim
The updated neutrino-deuteron cross sections are slightly (~1%) larger than previously reported values (NSGK), with an estimated 1% uncertainty that does **not** include radiative corrections. The authors assign 0.5% accuracy to the ratio of neutral-current to charged-current cross sections.

## Method
The authors performed a standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) calculation of neutrino-deuteron cross sections, using the ANLV18 potential for deuteron and NN scattering wave functions. They updated the axial coupling constant g_A, improved the treatment of the axial-vector exchange current by normalizing the Δ-excitation current to reproduce the tritium β-decay rate, and employed the effective Fermi constant G_F' that includes inner radiative corrections instead of G_F.

## Key numbers
- The new cross sections are ~1% larger than the previously reported NSGK values
- Exchange current contributions to the charged-current cross section are ~2% in NETAL, which is ~3% smaller than the NSGK results
- At E_ν ~ 10 MeV, transitions to higher partial waves account for 1% of the reaction rate; at E_ν ~ 20 MeV, they account for 4%
- The ratio σ_νd(EFT*)/σ_νd(SNPA) for the CC reaction is 1.001 at 10 MeV and 0.998 at 20 MeV
- The ratio σ_νd(EFT*)/σ_νd(SNPA) for the NC reaction is 1.003 at 10 MeV and 1.001 at 20 MeV
- The ratio R = σ_νd(NC)/σ_νd(CC) is assigned 0.5% accuracy

## Context
This work extends the previous SNPA calculation by Nakamura et al. (NSGK) and incorporates insights from effective field theory calculations by Butler et al. (BCK) and Ando et al. (EFT*). The improved treatment of the axial-vector exchange current, constrained by the tritium β-decay rate, resolves discrepancies between different exchange current models and demonstrates that SNPA and EFT* give identical results within 1% accuracy. The results enable more precise interpretation of SNO experimental data for testing neutrino oscillations, though the authors note that radiative corrections not included here are expected to affect cross sections at the few-percent level.
