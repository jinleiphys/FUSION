# Realizing Discontinuous Wave Functions with Renormalized Short-Range Potentials

> It is shown that a potential consisting of three Dirac's delta functions on the line with disappearing distances can give rise to the discontinuity in wave functions with the proper renormalization of the delta function strength. This can be used as a building block, along with the usual Dirac's delta, to construct the most general three-parameter family of point interactions, which allow both discontinuity and asymmetry of the wave function, as the zero-size limit of self-adjoint local operators in one-dimensional quantum mechanics. Experimental realization of the Neumann boundary is discussed. # Realizing Discontinuous Wave Functions with Renormalized Short-Range Potentials

## Key claim
The most general three-parameter family of point interactions in one-dimensional quantum mechanics, which allows both discontinuity and asymmetry of the wave function, can be realized as the zero-distance limit of three (or five) Dirac delta functions with renormalized strengths, provided the strengths are taken to diverge in a specific way as the separation distances vanish. This construction is claimed to be experimentally accessible, not merely a mathematical abstraction.

## Method
The authors construct a potential consisting of three Dirac delta functions placed at positions −a, 0, and +a, with strengths v(a) and u(a) that are allowed to depend on the separation a. By solving the Schrödinger equation in the intervals between the deltas and applying the connection conditions at each delta, they derive relations between the wave function and its derivative on either side of the three-delta system. Taking the limit a → 0 with specific choices of v(a) and u(a) that diverge as 1/a or 1/a² yields finite connection conditions at a single point, including the discontinuity-inducing potential ε(x;c). The full three-parameter family is then built by composing δ(x;v) and ε(x;c) potentials placed at vanishing distances, using matrix multiplication of the corresponding connection matrices.

## Key numbers
- The discontinuity-inducing potential ε(x;c) is obtained with v(a) = 1/(2c) − 1/(2a) and u(a) = −1/a + c/a², producing the connection condition ψ₊ − ψ₋ = 2c ψ′₋.
- The Neumann boundary condition (ψ′(0) = 0) is obtained as the c → ∞ limit of ε(x;c).
- Numerical demonstration uses a = 0.0333 L and s = 0.0012 L (where L = 10 is the system size) for a finite-range approximation with coupling c = 5, showing near-degeneracy between the first and second eigenstates (and between third and fourth) as a signature of approaching the Neumann limit.

## Context
This work extends the mathematical theory of point interactions in one dimension, previously formulated via self-adjoint extensions by Albeverio, Gesztesy, Høegh-Krohn, and Holden (1988) and others, by providing an explicit local potential realization of the full three-parameter connection condition. It addresses the controversy over the "δ′ interaction" discussed by Exner (1996) and Coutinho, Nogami, and Perez (1997), and improves upon earlier non-local or non-Hermitian zero-range limit constructions by Carreau (1993) and Chernoff and Hughes (1993). The construction enables experimental realization of Neumann boundary conditions and other discontinuity-inducing point interactions, with potential applications in nanometer-scale quantum devices and macroscopic wave systems.
