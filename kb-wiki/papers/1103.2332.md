# Relativistic Continuum Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation in Spherical Nuclei

> We have calculated the strength distributions of the dipole response in spherical nuclei, ranging all over the periodic table. The calculations were performed within two microscopic models: the discretized quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and the quasiparticle continuum RPA, which takes into account the coupling of the single-particle continuum in an exact way. Pairing correlations are treated with the BCS model. In the calculations, two density functionals were used, namely the functional PC-F1 and the functional DD-PC1. Both are based on relativistic point coupling Lagrangians. It is explicitly shown that this model is capable of reproducing the giant as well as the pygmy dipole resonance for open-shell nuclei in a high level of quantitative agreement with the available experimental observations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (CQRPA) based on point-coupling density functionals can reproduce both the giant dipole resonance (GDR) and the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in open-shell spherical nuclei with quantitative agreement to experiment, but only when the single-particle continuum is treated exactly rather than discretized with a truncation.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Bethe-Salpeter equation in a response-function formalism using two relativistic point-coupling density functionals (PC-F1 and DD-PC1). For open-shell nuclei, pairing correlations are included via the BCS model with constant gaps. The free response function is constructed by combining a continuum term (using relativistic Green's functions for exact continuum coupling) with a discrete two-quasiparticle term for the pairing-active space, minus a correction term to avoid double counting. The resulting strength distributions for isovector dipole excitations are computed for several spherical nuclei across the periodic table.

## Key numbers

- Energy-weighted sum rule for \(^{124}\)Sn: \(m_1 = 2043.47\) mb·MeV (PC-F1) and \(m_1 = 2151.85\) mb·MeV (DD-PC1), compared to the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule value of \(m_{\text{TRK}} = 1782.32\) mb·MeV (13.7% and 19.7% larger, respectively).
- Centroid energy of the GDR in \(^{124}\)Sn: 14.99 MeV (DD-PC1) and 14.70 MeV (PC-F1) from continuum QRPA, compared to experimental value \(14.34 \pm 0.02\) MeV.
- Centroid energy of the GDR in \(^{208}\)Pb: 14.13 MeV (DD-PC1) and 13.78 MeV (PC-F1) from continuum QRPA, compared to experimental value \(13.52 \pm 0.04\) MeV.
- For the discrete QRPA calculations, an energy cut-off of \(E^{\text{ph}}_{\text{cut}} = 300\) MeV for particle-hole configurations and \(E^{\text{ah}}_{\text{cut}} = 2000\) MeV for antiparticle configurations was used.
- The pairing-active space was limited to quasiparticle energies up to \(E_p = +20\) MeV.
- The smearing parameter used for the GDR width was \(\Gamma_0 = 1.4\) MeV with \(T = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic continuum RPA approach previously developed for doubly magic nuclei (Daoutidis and Ring, 2009) to open-shell nuclei by incorporating BCS pairing correlations, enabling the study of both GDR and PDR in neutron-rich isotopes. The continuum treatment avoids the basis truncation problems of conventional discretized QRPA, which the authors show can shift the PDR position by several MeV depending on the energy cut-off. The results enable more reliable predictions of low-lying dipole strength relevant for astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis, as discussed by Khan (2002), and provide a benchmark for future relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov based continuum calculations near the drip line.
