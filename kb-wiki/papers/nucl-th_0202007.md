# Search for new baryon resonances

> Within a chiral constituent quark formalism, allowing the inclusion of all known resonances, a comprehensive study of the recent eta photoproduction data on the proton up to 2 GeV is performed. This study shows evidence for a new S11 resonances and indicates the presence of an additional missing P13 resonance.

## Key claim
Within a chiral constituent quark formalism, the study shows evidence for a new S11 resonance and indicates the presence of an additional missing P13 resonance, but the evidence for the P13 is weaker and the paper reports preliminary findings rather than a definitive discovery.

## Method
The authors performed a comprehensive fit of differential cross section and polarization asymmetry data for the reaction γp → ηp up to Eγlab ≈ 2 GeV using a chiral constituent quark model based on SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry. They introduced symmetry-breaking factors related to configuration mixing angles and tested three models: Model M1 with all 11 known resonances below 2 GeV, Model M2 adding a third S11 resonance, and Model M3 adding both a third S11 and a third P13 resonance. The free parameters were extracted using the MINUIT minimization code from the CERN Library against roughly 650 data points from MAMI, GRAAL, ELSA, and CLAS.

## Key numbers
- Reduced χ² values: Model M1 = 6.5, Model M2 = 3.1, Model M3 = 2.7
- Extracted mixing angles for Models M2 and M3: θS = −34°, θD = 11°
- Third S11 resonance parameters (Model M3): mass = 1.776 GeV, width = 268 MeV
- Third P13 resonance parameters (Model M3): mass = 1.887 GeV, width = 225 MeV
- Data coverage: 650 data points for Eγlab from 0.716 to 1.925 GeV (W up to 2.2 GeV)

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by the same authors (Saghai and Li, 2001) that first suggested a new S11 resonance using GRAAL data, and now incorporates CLAS data (2001) covering the third resonance region. The extracted S11 parameters agree with predictions from the hypercentral constituent quark model (Giannini, Santopinto, and Vassallo) and with indications from BES Collaboration measurements of J/ψ → p p̄ η decays. The missing P13 resonance is consistent with predictions from the relativized pair-creation ³P₀ approach (Capstick and Roberts, 1992). The configuration mixing angles agree with the Isgur-Karl model predictions based on one-gluon exchange and with large-Nc effective field theory approaches. This work enables further searches for missing baryon resonances predicted by QCD-inspired models.
