# Effects of finite width of excited states on heavy-ion sub-barrier fusion reactions

> We discuss the effects of coupling of the relative motion to nuclear collective excitations which have a finite lifetime on heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies near and below the Coulomb barrier. Both spreading and escape widths are explicitly taken into account in the exit doorway model. The coupled-channels equations are numerically solved to show that the finite resonance width always hinders fusion cross sections at subbarrier energies irrespective of the relative importance between the spreading and the escape widths. We also show that the structure of fusion barrier distribution is smeared due to the spreading of the strength of the doorway state.

# Effects of finite width of excited states on heavy-ion sub-barrier fusion reactions

## Key claim

The finite resonance width of nuclear collective excitations always hinders fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies, regardless of the relative importance between spreading and escape widths. This contradicts earlier claims by Hussein et al. (1994, 1995) that spreading width enhances fusion cross sections, with the discrepancy attributed to the constant coupling approximation and incorrect treatment of complex potentials and weight factors in those earlier works.

## Method

The authors used the exit doorway model to treat both spreading width (coupling to bound particle-hole states) and escape width (coupling to particle continuum states) on equal footing. They numerically solved the coupled-channels equations for the system 16O + 144Sm, assuming a low-lying octupole phonon excitation in 144Sm with an artificially set excitation energy of 2 MeV and a hypothetical total width of 1 MeV, using a collective model coupling form factor with deformation parameter β3 = 0.205. The complete fusion cross section was obtained by excluding particle continuum states from the final states, with the spreading and escape widths introduced via a Breit-Wigner distribution of the non-collective states.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the doorway state: 2 MeV (artificially set)
- Total width of the doorway state: 1 MeV (hypothetical)
- Deformation parameter β3: 0.205, with target radius RT = 1.06 A^(1/3) fm
- Energy spacing D for the non-collective states: 0.2 MeV
- Nuclear potential parameters: Woods-Saxon with depth V = 105.1 MeV, range parameter r0 = 1.1 fm, surface diffuseness a = 0.75 fm
- Energy spacing of 1.8 MeV used in the 3-point difference formula for fusion barrier distributions
- For the case with both spreading and escape widths present simultaneously, each set to 0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the exit doorway model of Hussein et al. (1994, 1995) by performing exact coupled-channels calculations rather than using the constant coupling approximation, which the authors argue is inadequate for heavy-ion fusion where coupling extends outside the Coulomb barrier. The paper contradicts the earlier claim that spreading width enhances fusion cross sections, showing instead that both spreading and escape widths hinder fusion. The results are relevant for understanding fusion reactions of weakly bound nuclei such as 6,7Li and 9Be (Satchler et al. 1983, Hinde et al. 1992, Rehm et al. 1997, Timmers et al. 1997) and nuclei far from stability (Yoshida et al. 1996, Hinde et al. 1992, Dasgupta et al. 1995, Takigawa et al. 1993, Dasso and Vitturi 1994). The work enables more reliable analysis of high-precision fusion measurements for fragile nuclei and provides a framework for treating dissipative quantum tunneling in nuclear reactions.
