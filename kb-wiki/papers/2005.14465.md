# Chiral Phase Transition in an Expanding Quark System

> We investigate the influence of chiral symmetry which varies along the space-time evolution of the system by considering the chiral phase transition in an non-equilibrium expanding quark-antiquark system. The chiral symmetry is described by the mean field order parameter, whose values is the solution of a self-consistent equation, and affects the space-time evolution of the system through the force term in the Vlasov equation. The Vlasov equation and the gap equation are solved concurrently and continuously for a longitudinal boost-invariant and transversely rotation-invariant system. This numerical framework enables us to carefully investigate how the phase transition and collision affect the evolution of the system. It is observed that the chiral phase transition gives rise to a kink in the flow velocity, which is caused by the force term in the Vlasov equation. The kink is enhanced by larger susceptibility and tends to be smoothed out by non-equilibrium effect. The spatial phase boundary appears as a "wall" for the quarks, as the quarks with low momentum are bounced back, while those with high momentum go through the wall but are slowed down.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in an expanding quark-antiquark system produces a kink in the transverse flow velocity, caused by the force term in the Vlasov equation; this kink is enhanced by larger susceptibility but is smoothed out by non-equilibrium effects, and the spatial phase boundary acts as a "wall" that reflects low-momentum quarks while slowing down high-momentum quarks.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Vlasov equation and the NJL-model gap equation concurrently for a longitudinally boost-invariant and transversely rotation-invariant quark-antiquark system, using the relaxation time approximation for the collision term. The distribution function was expanded in Fourier components with respect to the azimuthal angle in the transverse plane, and the equations were discretized on a fixed grid in phase space (200 points for radial coordinate, 100 for transverse momentum, 100 for rapidity-like variable, maximum Fourier index n=7). The initial condition was a local-equilibrium Fermi-Dirac distribution with a Gaussian temperature profile (T0=300 MeV, ρ0=2 fm), and the evolution was studied for both the real case (current mass m0=3.7 MeV) and the chiral limit (m0=0), with small and large relaxation times.

## Key numbers

* NJL cutoff Λ = 496 MeV, coupling constant G = 1.688/Λ², chosen to yield vacuum quark mass m ≈ 300 MeV.
* Critical temperature for chiral phase transition at vanishing baryon chemical potential: about 156 MeV.
* Current quark mass in the real case: m0 = 3.7 MeV.
* Initial temperature profile: T0 = 300 MeV, ρ0 = 2 fm.
* Initial time τ0 = 0.5 fm, time step dτ = 0.0005 fm.
* Phase space grid: 200 points for ρ/ρ0 in [-3,3], 100 points for pT/T0 in [0,8], 100 points for ξ in [0,6]; Fourier expansion up to n=7.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral phase transitions in out-of-equilibrium systems that solved the Vlasov equation using test-particle methods (Abada 1994, Abada 1996) or assumed constant quark mass (Greiner 1996). It also builds on investigations of deconfinement phase transitions with Bjorken symmetry (Yang 2003) and on Chiral Fluid Dynamics approaches (Mishustin 1998, Nahrgang 2011). By self-consistently solving the Vlasov and gap equations and explicitly examining the force term, the authors demonstrate that the force term plays a crucial role in generating a kink in the flow velocity, which had not been carefully examined in prior test-particle simulations. This enables more realistic modeling of chiral phase transition dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for interpreting beam energy scan results at RHIC and other facilities.
