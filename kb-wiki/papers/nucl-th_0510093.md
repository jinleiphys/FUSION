# X(3): An Exactly Separable Gamma-Rigid Version of the X(5) Critical Point Symmetry

> A gamma-rigid version (with gamma=0) of the X(5) critical point symmetry is constructed. The model, to be called X(3) since it is proved to contain three degrees of freedom, utilizes an infinite well potential, is based on exact separation of variables, and leads to parameter free (up to overall scale factors) predictions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates, which are in good agreement with existing experimental data for 172-Os and 186-Pt. An unexpected similarity of the first beta bands of the X(5) nuclei 150-Nd, 152-Sm, 154-Gd, and 156-Dy to the X(3) predictions is observed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a gamma-rigid (gamma=0) version of the X(5) critical point symmetry, called X(3), which contains exactly three degrees of freedom and allows exact separation of variables; the abstract glosses over the fact that the model's predictions for the beta_1 bands of the established X(5) nuclei (150-Nd, 152-Sm, 154-Gd, 156-Dy) agree unexpectedly well with X(3) rather than X(5) for levels above L=4, suggesting the gamma degree of freedom influences only the bandhead.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr collective model and freeze the gamma variable to zero, which eliminates the gamma degree of freedom and makes the third moment of inertia vanish while the other two become equal. They then solve the Schrodinger equation using an infinite square well potential in the beta variable, achieving exact separation of the beta variable from the angular variables (spherical harmonics). The resulting radial equation is a Bessel equation with order nu = sqrt(L(L+1)/3 + 1/4), and the energy eigenvalues are given by the squares of the zeros of Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- Energy levels of the ground state band (s=1), beta_1 band (s=2), and beta_2 band (s=3) are normalized to the energy of the 2_1+ state
- B(E2) transition rates are normalized to B(E2; 2_1+ -> 0_1+)
- The order of the Bessel function for X(3) is nu = sqrt(L(L+1)/3 + 1/4), compared to nu = sqrt(L(L+1)/3 + 9/4) for X(5) and nu = L + 1/2 for E(3)
- The R_4 = E(4_1+)/E(2_1+) ratio shows a relatively abrupt change between 186-Pt and 184-Pt
- The 0_2+ state shows a minimum at 186-Pt in the Pt isotope systematics

## Context

This work extends the X(5) critical point symmetry of Iachello (2001) by constructing a gamma-rigid version, following the Davydov and Chaban (1960) approach of freezing the gamma variable. The model is analogous to the E(5) model of Iachello (2000) in using an infinite well potential and exact separation, but differs by freezing gamma rather than keeping it active. The results enable future experimental tests: the authors emphasize the need for more B(E2) measurements in 172-Os, 186-Pt, and the N=90 isotones to clarify whether the beta_1 bands of X(5) nuclei are truly insensitive to the gamma degree of freedom above L=4.
