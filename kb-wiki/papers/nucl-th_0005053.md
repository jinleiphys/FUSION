# Generalized Faddeev Equations for N-Particle Scattering

> A proposal is made for reducing the solution of the N-particle Lippmann-Schwinger equation to that of smaller sets of particles. This consists of first writing the N-particle equation in terms of all possible $N/2$-particle Lippmann-Schwinger equations. (If N is odd this needs a minor modification.) The second step requires a decoupling of the resolvents for the fewer particle systems so that each can be solved separately. This generalization of the Faddeev approach deals only with connected kernels and the homogeneous solution reproduces the N-particle Schrödinger equation. For four particles the proposed method involves only a $3\times3$ matrix whereas other approaches typically require the solution of at least a $7\times7$ matrix equation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalization of Faddeev's equations to N-particle scattering by successively partitioning the system into two-cluster subsystems of equal size, which reduces the N-particle problem to a hierarchy of smaller problems; the authors claim this approach involves fewer coupled equations than existing methods (e.g., a 3×3 matrix for four particles versus at least a 7×7 matrix in other approaches), but note that the method requires a renormalization of the potential at each partitioning step and a minor modification when N is odd.

## Method

The N-particle Lippmann-Schwinger equation is first decomposed into a sum over all two-cluster partitions of equal (or nearly equal) size, yielding a set of Faddeev-type equations for the transition operator components. Each partition transition operator is then expressed in terms of the transition operators of its two constituent clusters, either through an infinite series (following Sloan's approach) or via a contour integral convolution of cluster resolvents (following Bianchi-Favella and Haberzettl-Sandhas). This decomposition is applied recursively to each cluster until reaching two-particle subsystems, with a renormalization factor v_N applied to the potential at each level to ensure the total potential is correctly distributed among partitions.

## Key numbers

- For N=4, the method involves a 3×3 matrix equation, whereas other approaches typically require at least a 7×7 matrix equation.
- For N=4, the renormalization factor v_4 = 1 (no renormalization needed).
- For N=5, the renormalization factor v_5 = 1/4.
- For N=6, the renormalization factor v_6 = 1/4.
- For N=8, there are 35 two-cluster partitions (each cluster having 4 particles), and the renormalization factor v_8 = 1/15.
- The number of two-cluster partitions for even N is given by (1/2) * (N choose N/2); for odd N, it is (N choose (N-1)/2).

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev approach (Faddeev, 1965) for three-particle scattering to arbitrary N-particle systems, building on prior generalizations by Yakubovsky (1967), Sloan (1972), Bencze (1973), and Redish (1974). The authors claim their method is simpler than the Yakubovsky equations (which organize the four-particle wavefunction into 18 components) and the Sloan-Bencze-Redish equations, and it enables a more efficient numerical procedure by reducing the number of coupled equations at each level. The approach also provides a framework for computing statistical mechanical virial coefficients, as noted by the authors' prior work (Wei and Snider, 1995).
