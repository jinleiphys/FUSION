# Study of light $П•$-mesic nuclei with HAL QCD $П•N$ interactions

> We explore the possible existence of light $П•$-mesic nuclei using HAL QCD $П•N$ interactions for the $^2S_{1/2}$ and $^4S_{3/2}$ channels. Particularly, using the Faddeev formalism in configuration space, the $П•NN$ system, and $^{9}_П•$Be and $^{6}_{П•П•}$He nuclei within the framework of the three-body cluster model, are investigated. The $П•О±$ effective potential, obtained through a folding procedure, involves the HAL QCD $П•N$ interaction in the $^4S_{3/2}$ channel which does not lead to a bound state of the $П•N$ pair while the $П•N$ $^2S_{1/2}$ channel yields the bound state as the $^3_П•$H nucleus. The $^4S_{3/2}$ potential ensures that the folding procedure is appropriate because there are no open channels like $П•+N$ and $П•+2N $ near or below the $П•+ 4N$ threshold, and it utilizes different matter distributions of $^4$He proposed in the literature. The folding potential is approximated by the Woods-Saxon formula. The mirror systems $П•$+$О±$+$О±$ and $П•$+$П•$+$О±$ have energy ranges from 1-11 MeV and 3-10~MeV, respectively. The predicted binding energies represent the minimal values for the hypothetical $П•$ mesic nuclei $^{5}_П•$He, $^{9}_П•$Be and $^{6}_{П•П•}$He. The phenomenological $О±О±$ and $П•П•$ potentials are adopted from the literature.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi-mesic nuclei that used Yukawa-type phi-N potentials (Gao et al., 2001) and earlier Faddeev calculations (Belyaev et al., 2008; Sofianos et al., 2008; Epelbaum et al., 2024), which reported binding energies in the range of approximately 6-39 MeV. The study is enabled by recent lattice QCD results from Lyu et al. (2022) providing HAL QCD phi-N potentials and the finding by Chizzali et al. (2024) of evidence for a phi-N bound state in the spin-1/2 channel. The results provide minimal binding energy predictions that can guide experimental searches for phi-mesic nuclei at facilities such as J-PARC, GSI, and JLab.
