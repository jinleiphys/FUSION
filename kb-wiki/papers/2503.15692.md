# Study of light $Ōē$-mesic nuclei with HAL QCD $ŌēN$ interactions

> We explore the possible existence of light $Ōē$-mesic nuclei using HAL QCD $ŌēN$ interactions for the $^2S_{1/2}$ and $^4S_{3/2}$ channels. Particularly, using the Faddeev formalism in configuration space, the $ŌēNN$ system, and $^{9}_Ōē$Be and $^{6}_{ŌēŌē}$He nuclei within the framework of the three-body cluster model, are investigated. The $ŌēőĪ$ effective potential, obtained through a folding procedure, involves the HAL QCD $ŌēN$ interaction in the $^4S_{3/2}$ channel which does not lead to a bound state of the $ŌēN$ pair while the $ŌēN$ $^2S_{1/2}$ channel yields the bound state as the $^3_Ōē$H nucleus. The $^4S_{3/2}$ potential ensures that the folding procedure is appropriate because there are no open channels like $Ōē+N$ and $Ōē+2N $ near or below the $Ōē+ 4N$ threshold, and it utilizes different matter distributions of $^4$He proposed in the literature. The folding potential is approximated by the Woods-Saxon formula. The mirror systems $Ōē$+$őĪ$+$őĪ$ and $Ōē$+$Ōē$+$őĪ$ have energy ranges from 1-11 MeV and 3-10~MeV, respectively. The predicted binding energies represent the minimal values for the hypothetical $Ōē$ mesic nuclei $^{5}_Ōē$He, $^{9}_Ōē$Be and $^{6}_{ŌēŌē}$He. The phenomenological $őĪőĪ$ and $ŌēŌē$ potentials are adopted from the literature.

## Key claim

The paper predicts minimal binding energies for hypothetical light phi-mesic nuclei (¬≥_ŌÜH, ‚ĀĶ_ŌÜHe, ‚ĀĻ_ŌÜBe, and ‚Ā∂_ŌÜŌÜHe) using HAL QCD phi-N interactions, but only when the spin-1/2 channel potential is employed; the spin-3/2 channel alone does not produce bound states in the phi-N or phi-N-N systems.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations in configuration space for the three-body phi-N-N, phi-phi-N, phi-alpha-alpha, and phi-phi-alpha systems, using HAL QCD phi-N potentials in the ¬≤S‚āĀ/‚āā and ‚ĀīS‚āÉ/‚āā channels. For the phi-alpha potential, they performed a single folding procedure using the ‚ĀīS‚āÉ/‚āā channel HAL QCD phi-N interaction with matter density distributions of ‚ĀīHe, then approximated the result with a Woods-Saxon form. The Malfliet-Tjon MT-I-III potential was used for the N-N interaction, the Ali-Bodmer potential for alpha-alpha, and a Yukawa-type potential from the literature for phi-phi.

## Key numbers

- The phi-N scattering length in the ‚ĀīS‚āÉ/‚āā channel is -1.37 fm with effective radius 2.42 fm, and no bound state is found for phi-N or phi-N-N in this channel
- The phi-N binding energy in the ¬≤S‚āĀ/‚āā channel is 27.7 MeV, and the phi-N-N binding energy in the (S=0, T=0) state is 64.17 MeV
- The mixed spin state (S=1, T=0) using 1/3 of the ¬≤S‚āĀ/‚āā potential and 2/3 of the ‚ĀīS‚āÉ/‚āā potential gives a phi-N-N binding energy of 4.23 MeV
- The mixed spin state (S=1, T=1) using 2/3 of the ¬≤S‚āĀ/‚āā potential and 1/3 of the ‚ĀīS‚āÉ/‚āā potential gives a phi-N binding energy of 0.47 MeV and a phi-N-N binding energy of 6.82 MeV
- The mirror systems phi+alpha+alpha and phi+phi+alpha have energy ranges from 1-11 MeV and 3-10 MeV, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi-mesic nuclei that used Yukawa-type phi-N potentials (Gao et al., 2001) and earlier Faddeev calculations (Belyaev et al., 2008; Sofianos et al., 2008; Epelbaum et al., 2024), which reported binding energies in the range of approximately 6-39 MeV. The study is enabled by recent lattice QCD results from Lyu et al. (2022) providing HAL QCD phi-N potentials and the finding by Chizzali et al. (2024) of evidence for a phi-N bound state in the spin-1/2 channel. The results provide minimal binding energy predictions that can guide experimental searches for phi-mesic nuclei at facilities such as J-PARC, GSI, and JLab.
