# Can we learn anything new from polarization observables of the deuteron disintegration near threshold?

> We discuss polarization characteristics of the deuteron disintegration near the threshold energy. Due to the small relative energy of the outgoing $np$ pair, the dominant amplitude for this process is a 1+ -> 0^+ transition. Relativistic covariance requires only one electromagnetic transition form factor for this amplitude. Subsequently this leads to substantial simplifications in the formulas of the polarization observables and allows to draw conclusions independent of the details of the interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for deuteron electrodisintegration near threshold, where the final \(np\) pair is dominated by the \(^1S_0\) channel, the polarization observables become independent of the detailed nuclear structure (the transition form factor \(V(s,q^2)\)), allowing model-independent tests of the \(^1S_0\) dominance assumption.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant one-photon-exchange amplitude for the reaction \(e + d \rightarrow e' + n + p\), assuming the final \(np\) state is purely \(^1S_0\) (a \(1^+ \rightarrow 0^+\) transition). They derive the hadronic tensor using a single electromagnetic transition form factor \(V(s,q^2)\) and then compute the electron-deuteron spin asymmetries and tensor analyzing powers from the leptonic and hadronic tensors.

## Key numbers

*   For vector-polarized deuterons with polarization parallel to the \(Z\)-axis, the asymmetry \(A_{\parallel} = \frac{3}{2}\kappa\) at backward scattering angle \(\theta_e = 180^\circ\), where \(\kappa\) is the degree of polarization.
*   For tensor-polarized deuterons, the tensor analyzing power at backward scattering is \(A_{zz} = 1\).
*   The general vector asymmetry for arbitrary deuteron polarization direction \((\vartheta,\varphi)\) is given by \(A(\vartheta,\varphi) = \frac{3}{2}\kappa(E+E') \times \frac{(E'\sin\theta_e\sin\vartheta\cos\varphi + (E-E')\cos\theta_e\cos\vartheta)}{(E+E')^2 - 2EE'\cos^2(\theta_e/2)}\).

## Context

This work extends covariant treatments of deuteron electrodisintegration, such as the light-front approach of Carbonell and Karmanov (1995) and the Bethe-Salpeter approach of the same authors (1996), by focusing on the threshold region where the \(^1S_0\) final state dominates. The derived polarization formulas provide a direct experimental test of the \(^1S_0\) dominance assumption; any deviation from the predicted structure-independent values would indicate contributions from other partial waves (e.g., \(^3S_1-^3D_1\), \(^1P_1\)). The results also offer a method to calibrate the polarization degree of a deuteron target.
