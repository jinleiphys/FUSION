# Probing the neutron-skin thickness through $J/ψ$ photoproduction in ultra-peripheral collisions

> We study the impact of neutron-skin thickness on $J/ψ$ photoproduction in ultra-peripheral $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}+{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions. Within the Color Glass Condensate framework, we calculate coherent and incoherent cross sections and examine their dependence on the momentum transfer $|t|$ for different neutron-skin thicknesses. We find a clear imprint of the neutron skin on the $|t|$ spectra: a larger neutron skin leads to a smoother and more extended color-density profile, suppressing the coherent cross section at large $|t|$ while enhancing the incoherent cross section through increased event-by-event configurational fluctuations in the nuclear periphery. We further show that the ratio of incoherent to coherent integrated cross sections provides a particularly sensitive and robust observable, with reduced theoretical uncertainties. These results establish diffractive vector-meson photoproduction in ultra-peripheral collisions as a powerful tomographic tool to constrain the neutron-skin thickness and the transverse gluon distribution at the LHC and future Electron-Ion Colliders.

## Key claim

The neutron-skin thickness of \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) leaves a clear imprint on the \(|t|\) spectra of coherent and incoherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction in ultra-peripheral collisions, and the ratio of integrated incoherent to coherent cross sections provides a particularly sensitive and robust observable with reduced theoretical uncertainties, though the authors note that absolute cross-section values may not perfectly match experimental data due to phenomenological uncertainties in the vector meson wave function and missing higher-order corrections.

## Method

Within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, the authors computed coherent and incoherent differential cross sections for diffractive \(J/\psi\) production in \(\gamma+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) collisions using the color-dipole picture with event-by-event Wilson lines generated by the IP-Glasma model. Five distinct Woods-Saxon parameter sets for the neutron and proton densities were employed to vary the neutron-skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np}\) from \(-0.225\) fm to \(0.445\) fm, and the UPC cross sections were obtained by folding the photon-nucleus cross sections with the equivalent photon flux under the condition \(|\mathbf{b}_\perp| > 2R_{\text{Pb}}\).

## Key numbers

* The coherent cross section at \(|t| = 0.08\;\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(y=0\) for the baseline scenario (Case 2, \(\Delta r_{np}=0.0\) fm) is approximately 2.14 times larger than the thick neutron-skin case (Case 4, \(\Delta r_{np}=0.445\) fm) and about 1.60 times larger than the moderate neutron-skin case (Case 3, \(\Delta r_{np}=0.210\) fm).
* At \(|t| = 0.006\;\mathrm{GeV}^2\), the incoherent cross section for Case 1 (\(\Delta r_{np}=-0.225\) fm) is suppressed to a ratio of 0.83 relative to the baseline, while Case 3 shows a 9.6% increase and Case 4 shows a 23.9% enhancement relative to Case 2.
* The neutron-skin thickness values used are: Case 1 (\(\Delta r_{np}=-0.225\) fm), Case 2 (\(\Delta r_{np}=0.0\) fm), Case 3 (\(\Delta r_{np}=0.210\) fm), Case 4 (\(\Delta r_{np}=0.445\) fm), and Case 5 (\(\Delta r_{np}=0.445\) fm, bulk-only variation).
* The calculations were performed at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02\) TeV for rapidities \(y=-2,-1,0,1,2\), with the \(J/\psi\) mass \(M_V = 3.096\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the color-dipole framework for exclusive vector-meson production in UPCs developed by Mantysaari and collaborators (2023) and Zhang and collaborators (2023) to specifically quantify sensitivity to the neutron-skin thickness. The results establish diffractive \(J/\psi\) photoproduction as a tomographic tool complementary to low-energy parity-violating electron scattering (PREX Collaboration, 2021) and to central heavy-ion collision observables, and they motivate further studies at the LHC and future Electron-Ion Collider to constrain the neutron-skin thickness and the transverse gluon distribution.
