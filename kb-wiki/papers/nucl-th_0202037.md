# The three-nucleon bound state using realistic potential models

> The bound states of $^3$H and $^3$He have been calculated using the Argonne $v_{18}$ plus the Urbana three-nucleon potential. The isospin $T=3/2$ state have been included in the calculations as well as the $n$-$p$ mass difference. The $^3$H-$^3$He mass difference has been evaluated through the charge dependent terms explicitly included in the two-body potential. The calculations have been performed using two different methods: the solution of the Faddeev equations in momentum space and the expansion on the correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis. The results are in agreement within 0.1% and can be used as benchmark tests. Results for the CD-Bonn interaction are also presented. It is shown that the $^3$H and $^3$He binding energy difference can be predicted model independently.

## Key claim

The triton and helion binding energies calculated with the Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX three-nucleon potential agree between the two independent numerical methods to within 0.1%, and the resulting 3H-3He binding energy difference of 744 keV is 20 keV smaller than the experimental value of 764 keV, with the remaining discrepancy attributed to charge-symmetry-breaking three-nucleon force terms or relativistic effects not included in the model.

## Method

The bound states of 3H and 3He were solved using two independent methods: the Faddeev equations in momentum space (Bochum group) and an expansion on a correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis (Pisa group). Both calculations used the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential augmented by the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, included isospin T=1/2 and T=3/2 states, and accounted for the neutron-proton mass difference perturbatively. Additional calculations were performed with the CD-Bonn 2000 potential plus the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force.

## Key numbers

- 3H binding energy with AV18+UIX: 8.485(2) MeV (final averaged value, including n-p mass difference and T=3/2 contributions)
- 3He binding energy with AV18+UIX: 7.741(2) MeV (final averaged value)
- Experimental 3H binding energy: 8.482 MeV; experimental 3He binding energy: 7.718 MeV
- 3H-3He binding energy difference D with AV18+UIX: 744 keV (experimental: 764 keV)
- Contribution breakdown to D: nuclear CSB 65 keV, point Coulomb 677 keV, full Coulomb (including finite size) 648 keV, magnetic moment 17 keV, n-p mass difference 14 keV, total theory 744 keV
- 3H binding energy with CD-Bonn 2000+TM: 8.482 MeV (adjusted to reproduce experiment); 3He: 7.732 MeV; D = 750 keV
- Probability of T=3/2 state in 3H with AV18+UIX: 0.0025%; in 3He: 0.0075%
- D-state probability in 3H with AV18+UIX: 9.301%; in 3He: 9.248%

## Context

This work extends benchmark calculations of three-nucleon bound states from pure two-nucleon forces (Nogga 2000) to include three-nucleon interactions, demonstrating that the same 0.1% accuracy can be achieved with both Faddeev and hyperspherical harmonic methods when three-nucleon forces are present. The results enable a model-independent prediction of the 3H-3He mass difference that traces remaining model sensitivity to the nn scattering length, and show that the AV18+UIX and CD-Bonn 2000+TM predictions for D differ by only 6 keV despite very different electromagnetic treatments. The analysis updates earlier work by Wu (1990, 1993) on charge-symmetry-breaking contributions to the triton-helion mass difference using the new generation of potentials that include CSB terms fitted to NN data.
