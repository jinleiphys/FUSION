# Inner crust of a neutron star at crystallization in a multi-component approach

> The possible presence of amorphous and heterogeneous phases in the inner crust of a neutron star is expected to reduce the electrical conductivity of the crust, with potentially important consequences on the magneto-thermal evolution of the star. In cooling simulations, the disorder is quantified by an impurity parameter which is often taken as a free parameter. We aim to give a quantitative prediction of the impurity parameter as a function of the density in the crust,performing microscopic calculations including up-to-date microphysics of the crust. A multi-component approach is developed at finite temperature using a compressible liquid drop description of the ions with an improved energy functional based on recent microscopic nuclear models and optimized on extended Thomas-Fermi calculations. Thermodynamic consistency is ensured by adding a rearrangement term and deviations from the linear mixing rule are included in the liquid phase. The impurity parameter is consistently calculated at the crystallization temperature as determined in the one-component plasma approximation for the different functionals. Our calculations show that at the crystallization temperature the composition of the inner crust is dominated by nuclei with charge number around $Z \approx 40$, while the range of the $Z$ distribution varies from about 20 near the neutron drip to about 40 closer to the crust-core transition. This reflects on the behavior of the impurity parameter that monotonically increases with density up to around 40 in the deeper regions of the inner crust. Our study shows that the contribution of impurities is non-negligible, thus potentially having an impact on the transport properties in the neutron-star crust. The obtained values of the impurity parameter represent a lower limit; larger values are expected in the presence of non-spherical geometries and/or fast cooling dynamics.

## Key claim

The impurity parameter in the inner crust of a neutron star, calculated at the crystallization temperature using a multi-component plasma approach, increases monotonically with density up to around 40 in the deeper regions; however, the authors explicitly state that these values represent a lower limit, with larger values expected in the presence of non-spherical geometries or fast cooling dynamics.

## Method

The authors developed a multi-component approach at finite temperature using a compressible liquid drop description of ions with an energy functional based on recent microscopic nuclear models (BSk22, BSk24, BSk25, BSk26) optimized on extended Thomas-Fermi calculations. Thermodynamic consistency was ensured by adding a rearrangement term and including deviations from the linear mixing rule in the liquid phase. The impurity parameter was calculated at the crystallization temperature as determined in the one-component plasma approximation for the different functionals, with the calculation limited to spherical nuclei in the density domain from the neutron drip point to 0.04 fm\(^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- The composition of the inner crust at crystallization is dominated by nuclei with charge number around \(Z \approx 40\).
- The range of the \(Z\) distribution varies from about 20 near the neutron drip to about 40 closer to the crust-core transition.
- The impurity parameter monotonically increases with density up to around 40 in the deeper regions of the inner crust.
- The calculation was limited to baryon densities up to \(n_B = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\) due to the onset of non-spherical pasta structures above \(n_B \approx 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The proton fugacity never exceeds \(-20\) MeV in the density and temperature domain studied.

## Context

This work extends the multi-component plasma formalism previously applied to the outer crust by Fantina (2020) and the compressible liquid drop model calculations of Carreau (2020) for the inner crust, which used the same BSk functionals but only considered a one-component plasma. The results complement the outer-crust impurity parameter calculations of Fantina (2020) and provide quantitative predictions that can replace the free-parameter treatment of the impurity factor in cooling simulations such as those by Vigano (2013). The obtained lower-limit values of the impurity parameter enable more realistic modeling of electrical conductivity and magneto-thermal evolution of neutron stars.
