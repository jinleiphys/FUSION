# Spin-dependent structure functions in nuclear matter and the polarized EMC effect

> An excellent description of both spin-independent and spin-dependent quark distributions and structure functions has been obtained with a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, which is free of unphysical thresholds for nucleon decay into quarks - hence incorporating an important aspect of confinement. We utilize this model to investigate nuclear medium modifications to structure functions and find that we are readily able to reproduce both nuclear matter saturation and the experimental F^A_2N / F_2N ratio, that is, the EMC effect. Applying this framework to determine g^A_1p, we find that the ratio g^A_1p / g_1p differs significantly from 1, with the quenching caused by the nuclear medium being about twice that of the spin-independent case. This represents an exciting result, which if confirmed experimentally, will reveal much about the quark structure of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The ratio of the spin-dependent structure function of a bound proton in nuclear matter to that of a free proton, \(g_{1p}^A/g_{1p}\), differs significantly from 1, with the quenching caused by the nuclear medium being about twice that of the spin-independent case (the EMC effect). This prediction is made using a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that incorporates an important aspect of confinement via proper-time regularization and is conditional on experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors use a modified NJL model with proper-time regularization to describe the nucleon as a quark-diquark bound state, including both scalar and axial-vector diquark channels. They calculate the spin-dependent quark distributions in a bound proton by solving the nuclear matter equation of state self-consistently, incorporating the effects of the scalar field (via effective masses) and the vector field (via a scale transformation), and then convoluting with a Fermi smearing function for infinite nuclear matter. The resulting distributions are evolved to a scale of 10 GeV\(^2\) to construct the structure functions \(g_{1p}^A\) and \(F_{2N}^A\).

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation point: \(\rho_B = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and binding energy \(E_B = 15.7\ \text{MeV}\).
* Free constituent quark mass: \(M_0 = 400\ \text{MeV}\); at saturation density the effective constituent quark mass becomes \(M^* = 0.32\ \text{GeV}\).
* Free nucleon mass: \(M_N = 940\ \text{MeV}\); at saturation density the effective nucleon mass becomes \(M_N^* = 0.75\ \text{GeV}\).
* Vector field strength at saturation density: \(V_0 = 0.044\ \text{GeV}\).
* Free scalar diquark mass: \(M_s = 0.65\ \text{GeV}\); at saturation density \(M_s^* = 0.52\ \text{GeV}\).
* Free axial-vector diquark mass: \(M_a = 1.2\ \text{GeV}\); at saturation density \(M_a^* = 1.1\ \text{GeV}\).
* Model scale: \(Q_0^2 = 0.16\ \text{GeV}^2\); results are evolved to \(Q^2 = 10\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* The ratio \(g_{1p}^A/g_{1p}\) is predicted to be about twice as small as the unpolarized EMC ratio \(F_{2N}^A/F_{2N}\) in the valence quark region.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bentz, Thomas and collaborators (2001) and Mineo et al. (2003), who developed a covariant NJL model with proper-time regularization to describe nuclear matter and parton distributions. It also relates to the quark meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon et al. (2004), which achieves nuclear saturation through the scalar polarizability of bound nucleons. The prediction of a large polarized EMC effect, if confirmed experimentally, would provide new insights into quark polarization degrees of freedom in nuclei and motivate measurements on systems like \(^7\text{Li}\), \(^{11}\text{B}\), and \(^{15}\text{N}\).
