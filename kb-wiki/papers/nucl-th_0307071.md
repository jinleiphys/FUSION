# Comment on "Energies of the ground state and first excited $0^{+}$ in an exactly solvable pairing model"

> We comment on a recent application of the RPA method and its extensions to the case of the two-level pairing model by N. Dinh Dang [1].

## Key claim

The paper argues that the RPA and QRPA calculations presented by Dinh Dang (2003) for the two-level pairing model contain several inconsistencies, including an unjustified single-boson approximation in the normal phase and incorrect treatment of the q-term in the superfluid phase that violates the Goldstone theorem.

## Method

The author re-derives the standard boson mapping for the two-level pairing Hamiltonian, keeping two distinct bosons rather than Dinh Dang's single-boson approximation, and diagonalizes both Hamiltonians via RPA (Bogoliubov transformation) to compare eigenvalues. In the superfluid phase, the author calculates the standard QRPA matrix elements including the q-term explicitly, then compares the resulting eigenvalues with those obtained from Dinh Dang's formalism where the q-term factor is incorrectly set to 3 instead of 2.

## Key numbers

- For the normal phase with particle number N=8 and level spin J=7/2, the two-boson RPA eigenvalues are Ω₁ = -g + √(g+ε)√(ε+g(1-2Ω)) and Ω₂ = g + √(g+ε)√(ε+g(1-2Ω)), while the single-boson approximation gives ω = 2ε√(1-2gΩ/ε)
- In the superfluid phase, the standard QRPA yields eigenvalues Ω₁=0 and Ω₂=2Δ√(1-1/(2Ω)), where Δ is the BCS gap
- Dinh Dang's formalism with the incorrect q-term factor of 3 gives Ω₁=Δ√[(1/Ω)(3Δ²/(4g²Ω³)-1)] and Ω₂=2Δ√[(1-3/(4Ω))(1-Δ²/(4g²Ω³))]
- When the q-term is divided by a factor of 2, the eigenvalues become Ω₁=Δ√[(1/Ω)(1-Δ²/(4g²Ω³))] and Ω₂=2Δ√[(1-1/(4Ω))(1+Δ²/(4g²Ω³))], which match Dinh Dang's result when he neglects the q-term

## Context

This comment directly criticizes the work of Dinh Dang (2003) on RPA applications to the two-level pairing model, pointing out that the single-boson approximation produces results unrelated to the exact solution. The author shows that standard pp-RPA and QRPA results agree with earlier work by Hagino and Bertsch, and that Dinh Dang's treatment violates the Goldstone theorem by not producing a zero-energy mode in the superfluid phase. The comment clarifies that pp(hh)-RPA in the non-superfluid phase respects particle number conservation, contrary to Dinh Dang's claim that addition and removal modes can be superposed.
