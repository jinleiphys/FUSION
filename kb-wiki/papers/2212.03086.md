# Phenomenological model for the $γγ\to π^+π^-π^0$ reaction

> We present a phenomenological model for the $γγ\to π^+π^-π^0$ reaction by including the production mechanism of the $a_2(1320)$ resonance, as well as the contributions from the $σ/f_0(500)\, π^0$ and $f_2(1270)\, π^0 $ production channels. Furthermore, the $γγ\toρ^{\pm} π^{\mp}\toπ^{+}π^-π^0$ channel, which is essential for a description in the low-energy region, is investigated carefully by introducing the complete set of gauge invariant and Lorentz-covariant tensors for the $γγ\toρ^\pm π^\mp$ subprocess. The full amplitude is constructed to yield a correct high-energy Regge behavior. Within our model, we achieve a very reasonable description of ARGUS and L3 data of the total cross section, as well as of the $π^\pmπ^0$ and $π^+π^-$ invariant mass distributions. We also predict the invariant mass distributions in the $γγ$ center-of-mass energy range from $0.8$ GeV to $2.0$ GeV, which will be studied by the forthcoming data of the BESIII collaboration.

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological model for the γγ → π⁺π⁻π⁰ reaction that achieves a "very reasonable description" of ARGUS and L3 data, but the abstract's claim is qualified by the fact that the model has a single free parameter (the coupling g_{f₂ργ}) and that the relative phases between the a₂(1320) and f₂(1270) amplitudes are fixed by requiring constructive or destructive interference to match the data, rather than being determined from first principles.

## Method
The total amplitude is constructed as a sum of four contributions: the s-channel a₂(1320) resonance production via ρπ decay, the σ/f₀(500)π⁰ and f₂(1270)π⁰ production channels, and the γγ → ρ±π∓ → π⁺π⁻π⁰ channel. The ρ±π∓ subprocess is described using a complete set of gauge invariant and Lorentz-covariant tensors. To obtain correct high-energy behavior, Feynman propagators for ρ and π mesons in t- and u-channels are replaced by Reggeized propagators, and Blatt-Weisskopf form factors are included for resonance decays with non-zero orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers
- The a₂(1320) two-photon decay width is Γ_{a₂→γγ} = 1.00 ± 0.06 keV, yielding |g_{a₂γγ}| = 0.151 ± 0.005
- The a₂(1320) → ρπ coupling is |g_{a₂ρπ}| = 4.9 ± 0.2, derived from Γ_{a₂→ρπ} = 75.0 ± 4.5 MeV
- The ρπγ coupling is g_{ρπγ} = 0.102 ± 0.005, from Γ_{ρ±→π±γ} = 68 ± 7 keV
- The f₂(1270)ππ coupling is g_{f₂ππ} ≈ 23.67, from Γ_{f₂→ππ} = 157.2 MeV
- The f₂ργ coupling is determined as g_{f₂ργ} = -27.5 by fitting the total cross section at W = 1.85 GeV to approximately 28 nb
- The model covers the γγ center-of-mass energy range from 0.8 GeV to 2.0 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations by Talavera (1995) and Ametller (1999), which focused only on the very low-energy region near the 3π threshold, by providing a phenomenological model that covers the low- and intermediate-energy regions up to 2 GeV. The model is motivated by the need to improve the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2, where three-pion intermediate states are required. The paper provides predictions for the forthcoming BESIII experiment, which will measure the γγ → π⁺π⁻π⁰ reaction with higher statistics than the older ARGUS (1996) and L3 (1997) data.
