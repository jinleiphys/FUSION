# Phenomenological model for the $╬│╬│\to ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0$ reaction

> We present a phenomenological model for the $╬│╬│\to ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0$ reaction by including the production mechanism of the $a_2(1320)$ resonance, as well as the contributions from the $Žā/f_0(500)\, ŽĆ^0$ and $f_2(1270)\, ŽĆ^0 $ production channels. Furthermore, the $╬│╬│\toŽü^{\pm} ŽĆ^{\mp}\toŽĆ^{+}ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0$ channel, which is essential for a description in the low-energy region, is investigated carefully by introducing the complete set of gauge invariant and Lorentz-covariant tensors for the $╬│╬│\toŽü^\pm ŽĆ^\mp$ subprocess. The full amplitude is constructed to yield a correct high-energy Regge behavior. Within our model, we achieve a very reasonable description of ARGUS and L3 data of the total cross section, as well as of the $ŽĆ^\pmŽĆ^0$ and $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ invariant mass distributions. We also predict the invariant mass distributions in the $╬│╬│$ center-of-mass energy range from $0.8$ GeV to $2.0$ GeV, which will be studied by the forthcoming data of the BESIII collaboration.

## Method
The total amplitude is constructed as a sum of four contributions: the s-channel aŌéé(1320) resonance production via ŽüŽĆ decay, the Žā/fŌéĆ(500)ŽĆŌü░ and fŌéé(1270)ŽĆŌü░ production channels, and the ╬│╬│ ŌåÆ Žü┬▒ŽĆŌłō ŌåÆ ŽĆŌü║ŽĆŌü╗ŽĆŌü░ channel. The Žü┬▒ŽĆŌłō subprocess is described using a complete set of gauge invariant and Lorentz-covariant tensors. To obtain correct high-energy behavior, Feynman propagators for Žü and ŽĆ mesons in t- and u-channels are replaced by Reggeized propagators, and Blatt-Weisskopf form factors are included for resonance decays with non-zero orbital angular momentum.

## Context
This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations by Talavera (1995) and Ametller (1999), which focused only on the very low-energy region near the 3ŽĆ threshold, by providing a phenomenological model that covers the low- and intermediate-energy regions up to 2 GeV. The model is motivated by the need to improve the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2, where three-pion intermediate states are required. The paper provides predictions for the forthcoming BESIII experiment, which will measure the ╬│╬│ ŌåÆ ŽĆŌü║ŽĆŌü╗ŽĆŌü░ reaction with higher statistics than the older ARGUS (1996) and L3 (1997) data.
