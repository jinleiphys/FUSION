# Constraints on the quark matter equation of state from astrophysical observations

> We calculate the structure of neutron star interiors comprising both the hadronic and the quark phases. For the hadronic sector we employ a microscopic equation of state involving nucleons and hyperons derived within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory with realistic two-body and three-body forces. For the description of quark matter, we use several different models, e.g. the MIT bag, the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL), the Color Dielectric (CDM), the Field Correlator method (FCM), and one based on the Dyson-Schwinger model (DSM). We find that a two solar mass hybrid star is possible only if the nucleonic EOS is stiff enough.

## Key claim
A hybrid neutron star with a mass of two solar masses is possible only if the nucleonic equation of state is stiff enough and the hadron-quark phase transition occurs without hyperons present in the hadronic phase; the abstract's claim of "possible only if the nucleonic EOS is stiff enough" omits the additional condition that hyperons must be absent.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state for neutron star matter by combining a microscopic hadronic phase described within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory (using Argonne V18, Bonn B, and Nijmegen N93 potentials with compatible three-body forces) with several quark matter models: the MIT bag model, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, Color Dielectric model, Field Correlator method, and Dyson-Schwinger model. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star mass-radius relations and maximum masses for each combination, using both Maxwell and Gibbs constructions for the hadron-quark phase transition.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of purely nucleonic stars with the Bonn B potential: approximately 2.50 solar masses
- Maximum mass of hyperonic stars (with Bonn B): 1.37 solar masses
- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with the MIT bag model: 1.5 solar masses
- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with the Color Dielectric model: between 1.5 and 1.6 solar masses
- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with the Dyson-Schwinger model: approximately 2.0 solar masses (with parameter alpha=2) to nearly 2.5 solar masses (with alpha=0.5)
- Gluon condensate value in the Field Correlator method: G2 approximately 0.006-0.007 GeV^4, giving critical temperature Tc approximately 170 MeV
- Observed mass of pulsar PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 plus or minus 0.04 solar masses

## Context
This work extends earlier Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of neutron star structure (Schulze 1998, Vidana 2000, and others) that found hyperon softening reduces maximum masses below 1.4 solar masses. The paper systematically compares five different quark matter models against the observational constraint from the two-solar-mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010). The results enable future work to constrain quark matter model parameters, particularly the gluon condensate in the Field Correlator method and the asymptotic freedom parameter in the Dyson-Schwinger model, using astrophysical observations.
