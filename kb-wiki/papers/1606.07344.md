# Examining the effect of nonlocality in $(d,n)$ transfer reactions

> Background: In the last year we have been exploring the effect of the explicit inclusion of nonlocality in (d,p) reactions. Purpose: The goal of this work is to extend previous studies to (d,n) reactions, which, although similar to (d,p), have specific properties that merit inspection. Method: We apply our methods (both the distorted wave Born approximation and the adiabatic wave approximation) to $(d,n)$ reactions on $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{126}$Sn, $^{132}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb at $20$ and $50$ MeV. Results: We look separately at the modifications introduced by nonlocality in the final bound and scattering states, as well as the consequences reflected on the differential angular distributions. The cross sections obtained when using nonlocality explicitly are significantly different than those using the local approximation, just as in (d,p). Due to the particular role of Coulomb in the bound state, often we found the effects of nonlocality to be larger in (d,n) than in (d,p). Conclusions: Our results confirm the importance of including nonlocality explicitly in deuteron induced reactions.

## Key claim

The explicit inclusion of nonlocality in (d,n) reactions produces cross sections significantly different from those using the local approximation, and due to the particular role of the Coulomb force in the proton bound state, the effects of nonlocality are often larger in (d,n) than in (d,p).

## Method

The authors applied both the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) and the adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) to (d,n) reactions on six targets at two beam energies. They used the Perey and Buck nonlocal potential and generated phase-equivalent local interactions that reproduce the same elastic scattering and binding energies. They compared cross sections obtained with nonlocal optical potentials to those from the local phase-equivalent interactions, examining separately the effects of nonlocality in the final bound state, the final scattering state, and the entrance deuteron channel.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb(d,n)$^{209}$Bi at 20 MeV within DWBA, including nonlocality in both bound and scattering states produced a total cross section increase of 53.21% at the peak of the angular distribution.
- For $^{208}$Pb(d,n)$^{209}$Bi at 50 MeV within DWBA, the total effect of nonlocality was a 19.51% increase at the peak.
- For $^{126}$Sn(d,n)$^{127}$Sb at 20 MeV within ADWA, the total effect of nonlocality was a 95.01% increase at the peak (nearly a factor of two).
- For $^{126}$Sn(d,n)$^{127}$Sb at 50 MeV within ADWA, the total effect was a 19.31% increase at the peak.
- The percentage difference in the square of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) between nonlocal and local calculations for the proton bound state in $^{208}$Pb was 139%, compared to 33.0% for the neutron bound state in the corresponding (d,p) reaction.
- For $^{48}$Ca(d,n)$^{49}$Sc at 20 MeV within DWBA, the bound state alone produced a 33.89% increase, while the scattering state alone produced an 11.36% decrease.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group on nonlocality in (d,p) reactions (Titus 2014, Ross 2015, Titus 2015) to the complementary (d,n) case, which is important for studying proton orbitals and for astrophysical applications. The results confirm that nonlocality must be included explicitly in deuteron-induced reactions, and show that the Coulomb force in the proton bound state enhances the sensitivity to nonlocality compared to (d,p). The findings enable future (d,n) analyses to incorporate nonlocal potentials and suggest that constraining the ANC from other peripheral reactions would reduce the large model dependences found.
