# alpha-particle production in the scattering of 6He by 208Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier

> New experimental data from the scattering of 6He+208Pb at energies around and below the Coulomb barrier are presented. The yield of breakup products coming from projectile fragmentation is dominated by a strong group of $α$ particles. The energy and angular distributions of this group have been analyzed and compared with theoretical calculations. This analysis indicates that the $α$ particles emitted at backward angles in this reaction are mainly due to two-neutron transfer to weakly bound states of the final nucleus.

## Key claim

The alpha particles observed at backward angles in the scattering of 6He on 208Pb at energies around and below the Coulomb barrier are mainly produced by two-neutron transfer to weakly bound states of the final nucleus, rather than by direct breakup of the projectile.

## Method

The experiment used a 6He beam produced via the 7Li(p,2p)6He reaction, accelerated by the CYCLONE110 cyclotron at Louvain-la-Neuve, incident on enriched 208Pb targets at laboratory energies of 14, 16, 18, and 22 MeV. Reaction products were measured using LEDA detectors in the forward direction and the DINEX telescope array covering backward angles from approximately 132 degrees to 167 degrees, which provided mass and charge separation via Delta E versus total energy identification. The measured alpha particle angular and energy distributions were compared with theoretical calculations using three approaches: transfer to the continuum within the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA), direct breakup using continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations, and one-neutron transfer DWBA calculations.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 14, 16, 18, and 22 MeV (laboratory frame), with average beam intensity of 4 x 10^6 ions per second for the first three energies and 1.5 x 10^5 ions per second at 22 MeV
- At Elab = 22 MeV, the yield of alpha fragments exceeded the elastic yield by a factor of ten at backward angles
- The distance of closest approach for a head-on collision at 14 MeV is 17.3 fm, indicating the mechanism producing alpha particles is effective at large separations
- The angular range covered by the DINEX telescope was 136.3 degrees to 166.6 degrees for the thin target (0.950 mg/cm^2) and 131.8 degrees to 164.5 degrees for the thick target (2.080 mg/cm^2)
- The energy of the alpha particle peak was significantly larger than the value expected from a direct breakup picture (4/6 of the 6He energy), ruling out simple direct breakup as the dominant mechanism

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 6He-induced reactions on medium-heavy targets including 209Bi (Aguilera 2000), 64Zn (Di Pietro 2004), 65Cu (Navin 2004), and 238U (Raabe 2004), all of which reported large alpha particle production cross sections. The elastic scattering data from the same reaction had previously revealed a long-range absorption effect (Sanchez-Benitez 2006, Kakuee 2006), and this analysis shows that the two-neutron transfer mechanism producing alpha particles is responsible for that absorption. The results enable future four-body calculations of the alpha + n + n + 208Pb system to be tested against these precise angular and energy distributions.
