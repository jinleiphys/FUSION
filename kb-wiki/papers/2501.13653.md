# Towards a parameter-free analysis of the QCD chiral phase transition and its universal critical behavior

> To quantify the universal properties of chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD, we use an improved, renormalized order parameter for the chiral symmetry breaking. We construct ratios of this divergence-free order parameter from its values for different pairs of light quark masses. From this, we determine in a parameter-independent manner, the chiral phase transition temperature $T_c$ and the associated critical exponent $δ$ of the universality class. We present first numerical results of these calculations on $N_τ=8$ lattices, with staggered fermions.

# Towards a parameter-free analysis of the QCD chiral phase transition and its universal critical behavior

## Key claim
The chiral phase transition temperature \(T_c\) and critical exponent \(\delta\) can be determined in a parameter-independent manner from ratios of a renormalized order parameter, but only for sufficiently small light quark masses \(m_\ell \leq m_s/40\) where sub-leading corrections to scaling remain negligible at the current level of precision.

## Method
The authors construct an improved, renormalized chiral order parameter \(M(T,H) = M_\ell(T,H) - H\,\chi_\ell(T,H)\) that removes ultraviolet divergent contributions, where \(H \equiv m_\ell/m_s\). They compute ratios \(B(T,H,c) = \ln R(T,H,c)/\ln(c)\) from this order parameter evaluated at two different light quark masses, and locate the unique intersection point of these ratios as a function of temperature to extract \(T_c\) and \(\delta\) without assuming a universality class. Lattice QCD calculations use the HISQ action with Symanzik-improved gauge actions on \(N_\tau=8\) lattices.

## Key numbers
- \(T_c^{N_\tau=8} = 143.7(2)\) MeV, the chiral phase transition temperature determined on \(N_\tau=8\) lattices, consistent with the intersection point of the rescaled order parameter
- \(h_0^{-1/\delta} = 39.2(4)\), the non-universal normalization constant for the magnetic scaling variable, using \(\delta = 4.7798\) from the 3-dimensional O(2) universality class
- The analysis is valid for light quark masses \(m_\ell \leq m_s/40\) (i.e., \(H \leq 1/40\)), above which sub-leading contributions become significant

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the magnetic QCD equation of state by Ejiri (2009), Bazavov (2011), and Ding (2024), and attempts to quantify scaling regimes relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data in terms of chiral critical behavior. The improved order parameter was introduced by Unger (2010) and previously used in lattice QCD by Ding (2024), Kotov (2021), and Dini (2021). The results currently cannot distinguish between O(4) and U(2)×U(2) universality classes, requiring improved precision with more data points near the critical point, finite-volume checks, and continuum extrapolation on finer lattices.
