# Evidence for the general dominance of proton shells in low-energy fission

> A regular pattern, revealing the leading role of the light-fragment nuclear charge, is found to emerge from a consistent analysis of the experimental information collected recently on low-energy asymmetric fission of neutron-deficient nuclei around lead. The observation is corroborated by a theoretical investigation within a microscopic framework, suggesting the importance of proton configurations driven by quadrupole-octupole correlations. This is in contrast to the earlier theoretical interpretations in terms of dominant neutron shells. The survey of a wider area of the nuclear chart by a semi-empirical approach points to the lack of understanding of the competition between the different underlying macroscopic and microscopic forces in a quantitative manner. Combined with previously identified stabilizing forces, the present finding shows a striking connection between the "old" (actinide) and "new" (pre-actinide) islands of asymmetric fission which could steer the strive for an unified theory of fission.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in low-energy asymmetric fission of neutron-deficient nuclei around lead, the light-fragment proton number remains nearly constant at Z ≈ 36, indicating that proton shells (not neutron shells as previously thought) dominate the fission partition; this conclusion is hedged by the observation that the slope in Z_L versus Z_CN is "not very significant statistically" and that two subgroups (Z_L ≈ 35 for N_L < 50 and Z_L ≈ 37 for N_L > 50) are actually present.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental fragment mass and charge distributions from β-delayed, electromagnetic-induced, and fusion-induced fission measurements on neutron-deficient pre-actinide nuclei. They extracted mean light- and heavy-fragment proton and neutron numbers from published distributions, using the Unchanged-Charge-Density (UCD) assumption where only mass was measured, with an assigned uncertainty of 0.8 units. These experimental values were compared to predictions from microscopic energy-density-functional (EDF) calculations and from the semi-empirical GEF2019/V1.1 model.

## Key numbers

- Light-fragment charge Z_L is confined to a narrow range around 36 across a large diversity of fissioning systems (Z_CN from ~78 to ~84).
- For N_L < 50, Z_L values cluster around 35; for N_L > 50, Z_L values cluster around 37.
- The uncertainty introduced by the UCD assumption was taken to be 0.8 units.
- In the actinide region, asymmetric fission starts to be visible for A_CN above approximately 224 and persists up to A_CN approximately 256.
- For the specific case of ²¹⁰Po at an excitation energy 9.5 MeV above the fission barrier, the mass distribution can be accurately described by including both "old" and "new" fission modes attributed to proton configurations.

## Context

This work extends the recent observations of asymmetric fission in neutron-deficient pre-actinides such as ¹⁸⁰Hg (Andreyev 2010) and subsequent measurements (Ghys 2014, Gorbinet 2014, Nishio 2015, Prasad 2015, Gupta 2019) by providing a systematic analysis that reveals a common pattern. It contradicts earlier theoretical interpretations by Mulgin (1998), Ichikawa and Moller (2018), and Scamps and Simenel (2019) that attributed the asymmetry primarily to neutron shells or neutron-driven octupole effects. The finding establishes a connection between the "old" island of asymmetric fission in actinides and the "new" island in pre-actinides, enabling the search for a unified theory of fission; the semi-empirical GEF model (Schmidt 2016, 2019) fails in the region of most neutron-deficient radium to thorium, indicating that the competition between macroscopic and microscopic forces is not yet quantitatively understood.
