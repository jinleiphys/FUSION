# Observed $Ω_b$ spectrum and meson-baryon molecular states

> We observe that four peaks seen in the high energy part of the $Ω_b$ spectrum of the recent LHCb experiment are in remarkable agreement with predictions made for molecular $Ω_b$ states stemming from the meson-baryon interaction, with an approach that applied to the $Ω_c$ states gave rise to three states in good agreement with experiment in masses and widths. While the statistical significance of the peaks prevents us from claims of states at the present time, the agreement found should be an incentive to look at this experiment with increased statistics to give an answer to this suggestive idea.

# Observed $\Omega_b$ spectrum and meson-baryon molecular states

## Key claim
Four peaks seen at higher energies in the LHCb $\Omega_b$ spectrum (at 6402, 6427, 6468, and 6495 MeV) agree remarkably with predictions for molecular $\Omega_b$ states from meson-baryon interactions, but the statistical significance of these peaks prevents claiming them as states at present; the agreement should motivate higher-statistics studies.

## Method
The authors used the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels with an interaction based on vector meson exchange, extending the local hidden gauge approach to the bottom sector. The meson-baryon loop functions were regularized using a cutoff method with $q_{\rm max}=650$ MeV, the same cutoff used in the successful $\Omega_c$ molecular state calculations. The coupled channels included $\Xi_b\bar{K}$, $\Xi^{\prime}_b\bar{K}$, $\Omega_b \eta$, $\Xi \bar B$, $\Xi^*_b \bar K$, $\Omega^*_b \eta$, $\Xi^* \bar B$, $\Xi \bar B^*$, $\Xi_b \bar{K}^*$, and $\Xi^{\prime}_b \bar{K}^*$.

## Key numbers
- Four predicted molecular $\Omega_b$ states: 6405 MeV ($\frac{1}{2}^-$), 6427 MeV ($\frac{3}{2}^-$), 6465 MeV ($\frac{1}{2}^-$), and 6508 MeV ($\frac{1}{2}^-, \frac{3}{2}^-$), matching experimental peaks at 6402, 6427, 6468, and 6495 MeV respectively
- Binding energies: $\Xi^{\prime}_b\bar{K}$ state bound by about 26 MeV; $\Xi_b^* \bar K$ state bound by about 24 MeV; $\Xi \bar B$ state bound by about 133 MeV; $\Xi \bar B^*$ state bound by about 135 MeV
- Widths: 6405 MeV state has $\Gamma \simeq 0.024$ MeV (decays only to $\Xi_b \bar K$); 6427 and 6508 MeV states have $\Gamma \simeq 0$ MeV; 6465 MeV state has $\Gamma \simeq 2.42$ MeV (1.67 MeV to $\Xi_b \bar K$, 0.755 MeV to $\Xi^{\prime}_b \bar K$)
- With $q_{\rm max}=830$ MeV, a $\frac{1}{2}^-$ state appears at 6317 MeV matching $\Omega_b(6316)$ but with width 13 MeV, disagreeing with the experimental upper limit of 2.8 MeV

## Context
This work extends the molecular approach previously applied to $\Omega_c$ states by the same authors (Liang and Oset, 2018), which successfully reproduced three $\Omega_c$ states ($\Omega_c(3050)$, $\Omega_c(3090)$, $\Omega_c(3119)$) in masses and widths. The predictions contrast with quark model calculations (e.g., Hosaka 2019, Qifang 2019) that interpret the lower $\Omega_b$ states as ordinary $1P$ excitations of three-quark states, and with other quark models predicting $2S$ states around 6450 MeV with positive parity. The molecular states have negative parity and much narrower widths than most quark model predictions, enabling future experiments to discriminate between the two interpretations through measurements of widths, partial decay widths, and spin-parity quantum numbers.
