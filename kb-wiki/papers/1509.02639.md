# Estimation of 2p2h effect on Gamow-Teller transition with Second Tamm-Dancoff-Approximation

> Two-particle two-hole (2p2h) effect on the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition for neutron-rich nuclei is studied by the second Tamm-Dancoff approximation (STDA) with the Skyrme interaction. Unstable $^{24}$O and $^{34}$Si, and stable $^{48}$Ca nuclei are chosen to study the quenching and fragmentation of the GT strengths. Correlation of the 2p2h configurations causes $20$\% quenching and downward shift of GT giant resonances (GTGRs). The residual interaction changing relative angular momentum, appeared in the tensor force part, gives a meaningful effect to the GT strength distributions. In this work, $17$ to $26$ \% of the total GT strengths are brought to high energy region above GTGRs. In particular, the tensor force brings strengths to high energy more than $50$ MeV. STDA calculation within a small model space for 2p2h configuration is also performed and experimental data of $^{48}$Ca is reproduced reasonably.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the coupling of two-particle two-hole (2p2h) configurations within the Second Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (STDA) causes about 20% quenching of Gamow-Teller (GT) strength and a downward shift of the GT giant resonance (GTGR), but this downward shift is so strong that unphysical negative-energy resonances appear, especially when the tensor force is included; the authors note that this problem may be due to double counting between the residual interaction and the static ground-state response, as suggested by Tselyaev (2013).

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent STDA calculations using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) method with the SGII Skyrme interaction (central and spin-orbit forces) and the Te1 tensor force, assuming spherical nuclear shape and a box boundary condition. They solved the STDA eigenvalue equation (which couples 1p1h and 2p2h configurations) by direct diagonalization using the FEAST linear algebra solver, and also tested a diagonal approximation (STDA(D)) where the coupling between 2p2h states is omitted. The method was applied to three nuclei: unstable \(^{24}\)O and \(^{34}\)Si, and stable \(^{48}\)Ca, with a model space defined by cutoff energy \(E_{cut}^{2p2h}=100\) MeV and quantum number \(N=13\) for \(^{24}\)O and \(N=12\) for \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{34}\)Si.

## Key numbers

*   For \(^{24}\)O, the total GT strength below 20 MeV is 86.7% of the Ikeda sum rule for STDA with SGII, and 77.8% for STDA with SGII+Te1.
*   For \(^{24}\)O, STDA(D) with SGII brings 20.4% of the Ikeda sum rule to energies above 20 MeV, of which 8.0% lies above 50 MeV; with SGII+Te1, 26.2% lies above 20 MeV, with 17.1% above 50 MeV.
*   For \(^{48}\)Ca, STDA with SGII gives 88.5% of the Ikeda sum rule below 20 MeV, and with SGII+Te1 gives 80.3% below 20 MeV; the experimental value from Yako (2009) is 58.1%.
*   The center-of-mass energy \(E_{cm}\) of GT peaks for \(^{24}\)O (considering only states below 30 MeV) shifts from 8.7 MeV in HF to -0.9 MeV in STDA with SGII+Te1, and to -0.7 MeV in STDA(D) with SGII+Te1.
*   The tensor force brings GT strengths to energies above 50 MeV, as seen in TDA for \(^{24}\)O where SGII+Te1 produces peaks above 50 MeV while SGII alone gives none.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of 2p2h effects on GT transitions, such as the SRPA calculations by Drozdz (1986) and Nishizaki (1988), and the perturbative study by Bertsch and Hamamoto (1982) on \(^{90}\)Zr. It also relates to self-consistent RPA work by Bai (2009) showing that the tensor force can bring GT strength above 50 MeV already at the 1p1h level. The present STDA results for \(^{48}\)Ca are compared with experimental data from Yako (2009) and with PVC calculations by Niu (2014). The paper identifies a known problem of excessive downward shift in higher-order RPA, which Tselyaev (2013) and Gambacurta (2015) addressed via a subtraction method, suggesting that future work should apply that correction to GT transitions.
