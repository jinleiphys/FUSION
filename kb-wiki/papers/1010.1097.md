# The general behavior of $NLO$ unintegrated parton distributions based on the single-scale evolution and the angular ordering constraint

> To overcome the complexity of generalized two hard scale ($k_t$,$μ$) evolution equation, well known as the $Ciafaloni$, $Catani$, $Fiorani$ and $Marchsini$ ($CCFM$) evolution equations, and calculate the unintegrated parton distribution functions ($UPDF$), $Kimber$, $Martin$ and $Ryskin$ ($KMR$) proposed a procedure based on ($i$) the inclusion of single-scale ($μ$) only at the last step of evolution and ($ii$) the angular ordering constraint ($AOC$) on the $DGLAP$ terms (the $DGLAP$ collinear approximation), to bring the second scale, $k_t$ into the $UPDF$ evolution equations. In this work we intend to use the $MSTW 2008$ (Martin et al) parton distribution functions (PDF) and try to calculate $UPDF$ for various values of $x$ (the longitudinal fraction of parton momentum), $μ$ (the probe scale) and $k_t$ (the parton transverse momentum) to see the general behavior of three dimensional $UPDF$ at the $NLO$ level up to the $LHC$ working energy scales ($μ^2)$. It is shown that there exits some pronounced peaks for the three dimensional $UPDF$ $(f_a(x,k_t))$ with respect to the two variables $x$ and $k_t$ at various energies ($μ$). These peaks get larger and move to larger values of $k_t$, as the energy ($μ$) is increased. We hope these peaks could be detected in the $LHC$ experiments at $CERN$ and other laboratories in the less exclusive processes.

## Key claim

The Kimber-Martin-Ryskin (KMR) procedure, which uses only single-scale evolution at the last step and angular ordering constraints on DGLAP terms, produces unintegrated parton distribution functions (UPDF) at NLO level that exhibit pronounced peaks in the three-dimensional (x, k_t) space; these peaks grow and shift to larger k_t as the hard scale mu increases, and the authors hope these peaks could be detected at LHC experiments.

## Method

The authors applied the KMR prescription using MSTW 2008 NLO parton distribution functions as input, implementing the angular ordering constraint (AOC) cutoff Delta = k_t/(mu + k_t) on the DGLAP splitting function integrals, and computed the UPDF via equations (8) and (9) which include the Sudakov-like survival probability factor T_a(k_t, mu). They generated three-dimensional plots of f_a(x, k_t^2, mu^2) for gluons and several quark flavors (u, s, b) at fixed mu^2 values of 10, 10^2, 10^4, and 10^8 GeV^2, covering scales up to LHC working energies.

## Key numbers

- UPDF were computed at mu^2 = 10, 10^2, 10^4, 10^8 GeV^2 for gluons, up quarks, strange quarks, and bottom quarks
- For gluons at x = 0.01 and mu^2 = 100 GeV^2, integrating the UPDF over k_t^2 gives 6.7, while the MSTW 2008 input PDF gives 6.5, a discrepancy of approximately 3%
- The original KMR work (Kimber, Martin, Ryskin) reported 25% discrepancies for the same type of comparison
- At low scales (mu^2 approximately 10 GeV^2), the UPDF become negative when x approaches 1, reflecting negative values in the MSTW 2008 NLO gluon distribution

## Context

This work extends the KMR approach (Kimber, Martin, Ryskin, 2001) for generating UPDF from standard DGLAP evolution with angular ordering constraints, using the more recent MSTW 2008 PDF set (Martin, Stirling, Thorne, Watt) instead of earlier sets like MRST99. The authors previously investigated the stability and reliability of KMR UPDF (Hosseinkhani and Modarres, recent works) and here provide three-dimensional visualizations of the UPDF behavior across a wide range of scales. The results enable phenomenological calculations for less exclusive processes at LHC energies, particularly for predicting jet properties based on the most probable k_t values at given x and mu.
