# Iterated Resolvent Function for the Ladder Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> We developed the formal connection of the field theoretical Bethe-Salpeter equation including the ladder approximation with its representation on the light-front for a bosonic model. We use the light-front Green's function for the N-particle system for two-particles plus N-2 intermediate bosons. We derived an infinite set of coupled hierarchy equations or iterated resolvent from which the Green's function can be calculated. These equations allow a consistent truncation of the light-front Fock-space. We show explicitly the represention of the covariant two-body T-matrix and bound state vertex in the light-front.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a formal connection between the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation and its representation on the light-front for a bosonic model, deriving an infinite hierarchy of coupled equations (iterated resolvents) that allows a consistent truncation of the light-front Fock space; the authors note that this fills a formal gap in the literature, as an explicit systematic expansion of the covariant BS equation in the light-front was previously missing.

## Method

The authors start from a bosonic model with interaction Lagrangian coupling two scalar bosons (phi_1, phi_2) of equal mass m to an intermediate sigma boson of mass mu. They construct the two-body light-front Green's function from the covariant propagator, evaluate it perturbatively up to order g_S^4 (including one-boson exchange and the box diagram), and generalize to order g_S^{2n}. They then derive an infinite set of coupled hierarchy equations for the N-particle light-front Green's functions, where the kernel is built from the interaction vertex V and the free Green's functions G_0^{(N)}. The truncation G^{(N)} -> G_0^{(N)} yields a systematic approximation scheme, with the lowest-order truncation (three-particle intermediate states) recovering the Weinberg equation.

## Key numbers

- Bosonic model with scalar bosons phi_1 and phi_2 of equal mass m, intermediate sigma boson of mass mu, and coupling constant g_S.
- The free two-body Green's function is given by G_0^{(2)}(K^-) = theta(k_1^+) theta(K^+ - k_1^+) / (K^- - K_0^{(2)-} + i epsilon), where K_0^{(2)-} = (k_{1perp}^2 + m^2)/k_1^+ + (k_{2perp}^2 + m^2)/(K^+ - k_1^+).
- The free N-body Green's function is G_0^{(N)}(K^-) = [product_{j=1}^N theta(k_j^+) theta(K^+ - k_j^+)] / (K^- - K_0^{(N)-} + i epsilon), with K_0^{(N)-} = sum_{j=1}^N (k_{jperp}^2 + m_j^2)/k_j^+.
- The bound state eigenvalue equation is written as [(M_0^{(2)})^2 + K^+ V G^{(3)}(K_B^-) V] |Psi_B> = (M_2)^2 |Psi_B>, where (M_2)^2 = K^+ K_B^- - K_perp^2 and M_0^{(2)} = K^+ K_0^{(2)-} - K_perp^2.

## Context

This work extends the light-front approach to the Bethe-Salpeter equation beyond the three-particle truncation originally derived by Weinberg (1966) and numerically solved by Ji (1986, 1994) and by Pauli and collaborators (1990). The hierarchy equations provide a systematic framework for including higher Fock components (up to N particles in intermediate states), enabling comparison between the full covariant ladder BS approximation and its light-front truncations. The authors note that numerical results for the four-particle truncation have been obtained in a companion work (Sales et al., 2000), and the formalism opens the way to study bound state properties and the covariant two-body T-matrix in the light-front representation.
