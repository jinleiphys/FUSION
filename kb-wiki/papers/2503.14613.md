# Electron capture of superheavy nuclei with realistic lepton wave functions

> The superheavy nuclei push the periodic table of the elements and the chart of the nuclides to their limits, providing a unique laboratory for studies of the electron-nucleus interactions. The most important weak decay mode in known superheavy nuclei is electron capture (EC). In the standard calculations of EC, the lepton wave functions are usually considered in the lowest-order approximation. In this work, we investigate the sensitivity of EC rates on the choice of the electron wave functions by (i) assuming the single-particle approximation for the electron wave functions, and (ii) carrying out Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) calculations. The nuclear response is generated based on the state-of-the-art quasiparticle random phase approximation employing relativistic nuclear energy density functional theory. We show that using the improved lepton wave functions reduces the EC rates up to 40\% in the superheavy nucleus oganesson ($Z=118$). Interestingly, because of screening effects, the difference between the EC rates obtained with the DHF and single-particle calculations is fairly small.

## Key claim

Using improved lepton wave functions (Dirac-Hartree-Fock or single-particle Dirac) reduces electron capture rates by up to 40% in the superheavy nucleus oganesson (Z=118) compared to the standard lowest-order approximation, but the difference between Dirac-Hartree-Fock and single-particle Dirac calculations is fairly small due to screening effects.

## Method

The authors computed electron capture rates by solving the bound-state Dirac equation for electron radial wave functions using two approaches: (i) a single-particle Dirac solver (RADIAL) that neglects electron-electron interactions, and (ii) many-body Dirac-Hartree-Fock calculations (GRASP package) that include electron screening. The nuclear response was generated using the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with relativistic nuclear energy density functional theory (DD-PC1 functional), assuming axially-deformed and reflection-symmetric nuclear shapes. The full expression for the EC rate was evaluated by coupling the lepton wave functions to nuclear transition densities within the linear response pnRQRPA framework.

## Key numbers

- EC rates in the superheavy nucleus oganesson (Z=118) are reduced by up to 40% when using improved lepton wave functions compared to the lowest-order approximation
- For medium-mass nuclei, the ratio of EC rates calculated with Dirac-Hartree-Fock wave functions to lowest-order wave functions ranges from approximately 0.85 to 1.05 across iron (Z=26), palladium (Z=46), and dysprosium (Z=66) isotopic chains
- The end-point energy for EC includes a term Δ_nH = 0.782 MeV representing the mass difference between the neutron and hydrogen atom
- The strength function calculations used a small imaginary value η = 0.1 MeV added to energy to obtain finite Lorentzian width of peaks

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of EC rates in superheavy nuclei that used simplified phenomenological mean-field models (Sarriguren 2019, 2021, 2022) or microscopic-macroscopic models (Moller 1997, 1994) with only allowed Gamow-Teller transitions and lowest-order lepton wave functions. It also goes beyond calculations for lighter nuclei up to Z=50 (Niu 2013) that used relativistic DFT and QRPA but only at lowest order. The results enable more accurate predictions of EC rates in superheavy nuclei where EC can compete with alpha-decay and fission, providing guidance for future experimental efforts in the superheavy region.
