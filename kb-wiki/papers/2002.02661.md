# QCD equation of state at finite densities for nuclear collisions

> We construct the QCD equation of state at finite chemical potentials including net baryon, electric charge, and strangeness based on the results of lattice QCD simulations and the hadron resonance gas model. The situation of strangeness neutrality and a fixed charge-to-baryon ratio, which resembles that of heavy nuclei, is considered for the application to relativistic heavy-ion collisions. This increases the values of baryon chemical potential compared to the case of vanishing strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials, modifying the fireball trajectory in the phase diagram. We perform viscous hydrodynamic simulations and demonstrate the importance of multiple chemical potentials for identified particle production in heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC and SPS beam energy scan energies.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a QCD equation of state that includes net baryon, electric charge, and strangeness chemical potentials simultaneously, rather than only baryon chemical potential as in previous work. The authors show that imposing strangeness neutrality and a fixed charge-to-baryon ratio (matching heavy nuclei) increases the baryon chemical potential along fireball trajectories compared to the simpler case of vanishing strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials.

## Method
The equation of state (called NEOS) is constructed by connecting the pressure from Taylor-expanded lattice QCD susceptibilities to that of a hadron resonance gas model using a hyperbolic tangent switching function centered at a baryon-dependent crossover temperature. The authors then perform viscous hydrodynamic simulations with the Music model, using initial conditions from a dynamical Glauber Monte Carlo model and switching to the UrQMD hadronic cascade at a switching energy density of 0.26 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers
- The crossover temperature parameterization is \(T_c(\mu_B) = 0.16\ \mathrm{GeV} - 0.4\times(0.139\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1} \mu_B^2 + 0.053\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3} \mu_B^4)\)
- The switching width is \(\Delta T_c = 0.1 T_c(0)\)
- The charge-to-baryon ratio for gold nuclei is 0.401 (for \({}^{197}_{\ 79}\)Au) and 0.394 for lead (\({}^{208}_{\ 82}\)Pb)
- Constant \(s/n_B\) trajectories of 420, 144, 51, and 30 correspond to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200, 62.4, 19.6, and 14.5 GeV respectively
- The switching energy density used is \(e_\mathrm{sw} = 0.26\) GeV/fm\(^3\), with a preferred range of 0.16-0.36 GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Shear viscosity is set to \(\eta/s = 0.08\)

## Context
This work extends prior lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential (Bazavov 2014, Bazavov 2017) and the NEOS equation of state framework (Monnai 2015, Monnai 2019) to include multiple conserved charges relevant for heavy-ion collisions. The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic modeling of the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and SPS, and imply that the phase diagram explored in these experiments is four-dimensional (\(T, \mu_B, \mu_Q, \mu_S\)) rather than the two-dimensional \(T\)-\(\mu_B\) plane typically assumed. The equation of state is made publicly available for use by other researchers.
