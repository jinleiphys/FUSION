# Strangeness production in heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies

> Kaon production, in particular $K^+$ production in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies is discussed. Main emphasis is put on the question if subthreshold $K^+$ production can serve as a suitable tool to test the high density phase of such reactions and to deliver information on the high density behavior of the nuclear equation of state. It is shown that the $K^+$ excitation function in heavy ($Au+Au$) over light ($C+C$) systems provides a robust observable which, by comparison to data, strongly favors a soft equation of state. A second question of interest is the existence of an in-medium kaon potential as predicted by effective chiral Lagrangiens. Here it is argued that transport calculations support this scenario with, in the meantime, a significant level of consistency.

## Key claim

Subthreshold K+ production in heavy ion collisions can serve as a robust tool to test the high-density phase of nuclear matter and the nuclear equation of state, but the conclusions depend on comparing ratios of heavy (Au+Au) to light (C+C) systems to cancel uncertainties, and the results strongly favor a soft equation of state only when an in-medium kaon potential is included.

## Method

The study uses the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport model with soft and hard Skyrme forces corresponding to compression moduli of K=200 MeV and K=380 MeV, respectively, including momentum dependence adjusted to the empirical optical nucleon-nucleus potential. Kaon production is treated perturbatively via baryon-baryon (BB -> BYK+) and pion-baryon (pi B -> YK+) channels, with elementary cross sections from Sibirtsev (1995) for NN channels and Tsushima (1999) for NDelta and DeltaDelta channels. An in-medium kaon potential based on effective chiral models is included, with a repulsive vector part and an attractive scalar part, and the threshold conditions are shifted using an effective kaon mass defined from the optical potential.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of soft EOS: K=200 MeV; hard EOS: K=380 MeV
- Saturation density: rho_sat = 0.17 fm^-3; binding energy: E_B = -16 MeV
- Free K+ production threshold: E_thr = 1.58 GeV; measurements start at 0.8 A*GeV
- Mean densities at which kaons are created in C+C at 0.8 A*GeV: <rho/rho_sat> = 1.46 (hard EOS) and 1.40 (soft EOS)
- Mean densities at which kaons are created in Au+Au at 0.8 A*GeV: <rho/rho_sat> = 1.47 (hard EOS) and 1.57 (soft EOS)
- In the light system (C+C), the K+ yield is reduced by about 50% due to the repulsive in-medium potential
- Kaons in Au+Au are created at densities up to three times saturation density
- Maximum impact parameters: b_max = 11 fm for Au+Au, b_max = 5 fm for C+C

## Context

This work extends earlier transport model studies by Aichelin and Ko (1985), Li and collaborators (1995), and Bao and Li (1994) that first noted the sensitivity of K+ yields to the nuclear equation of state, but with large uncertainties. The paper builds on systematic measurements by the KaoS Collaboration (Sturm, 2000) of K+ production far below threshold in Au+Au and C+C systems. The results are compared to independent IQMD transport calculations by Hartnack (2001) and are consistent with microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body calculations by Boelting (1999) and with nucleon flow analyses by Danielewicz (2000) and Gaitanos (2001), all favoring a soft equation of state in the density range up to three times saturation density. The work enables future studies of the EOS dependence in asymmetric systems such as C+Au reactions, with forthcoming data from KaoS (Uhlig, 2003).
