# $^3$H/$^3$He ratio as a probe of the nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities

> Within the newly updated version of the Ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model in which the Skyrme potential energy-density functional is introduced, the yield ratio between $^3$H and $^3$He clusters emitted from central $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca, $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr, $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions in the beam energy range from 0.12 to 1 GeV$/$nucleon is studied. The recent FOPI data for the $^3$H$/$$^3$He ratio are compared with UrQMD calculations using 13 Skyrme interactions (all exhibiting similar values of iso-scalar incompressibility but very different density dependences of the symmetry energy). It is found that the $^3$H$/$$^3$He ratio is sensitive to the nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities. Model calculations with moderately soft to linear symmetry energies are in agreement with the experimental FOPI data. This result is in line with both, the recent constraints on the low-density symmetry energy available in the literature and our previous results for the high-density symmetry energy obtained with the elliptic flow of free nucleons and hydrogen isotopes as a sensitive probe.

## Key claim

The yield ratio of ^3H to ^3He clusters emitted from central heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the nuclear symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities, and model calculations with moderately soft to linear symmetry energies are in agreement with the experimental FOPI data, while very soft symmetry energies are ruled out.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model updated with a Skyrme potential energy-density functional to simulate central collisions of ^40Ca+^40Ca, ^96Zr+^96Zr, ^96Ru+^96Ru, and ^197Au+^197Au at beam energies from 0.12 to 1 GeV per nucleon. They selected 13 Skyrme interactions with similar isoscalar incompressibility but very different density dependences of the symmetry energy, and compared the calculated ^3H/^3He ratios with FOPI experimental data. Clusters were constructed using an isospin-dependent minimum spanning tree algorithm with coalescence parameters R0^pp=2.8 fm, R0^nn=R0^np=3.8 fm, and P0=0.25 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

* The 13 Skyrme interactions have incompressibility K0 values ranging from 222.36 MeV (SkO') to 255.79 MeV (SkI5), with most near 230 MeV.
* The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy ranges from 5.75 MeV (Skz4) to 161.05 MeV (SkI1).
* The symmetry energy at density 0.03 fm^-3 ranges from 4.15 MeV (SkI1) to 15.91 MeV (Skz4).
* At beam energy 400 MeV per nucleon for central Au+Au collisions, the ^3H/^3He ratio shows a linear correlation with the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities, with the adjusted R^2 value increasing as density decreases.
* The ^3H/^3He ratio from central Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV per nucleon is sensitive to the symmetry energy, with Skz4 (very soft) giving the largest ratio and SkI1 (stiff) giving the smallest ratio at the asymptotic stage.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using BUU-type and QMD-type transport models (Chen 2003, Chen 2004, Li 2005, Zhang 2005) that showed puzzling inconsistencies in the sensitivity of the ^3H/^3He ratio to the symmetry energy. The results are consistent with constraints on the low-density symmetry energy from Danielewicz (2013), Roca-Maza (2012), Brown, and Zhang (2013), and also with the authors' previous results for the high-density symmetry energy obtained using elliptic flow of free nucleons and hydrogen isotopes. The study enables tighter constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities, though the authors note that large experimental uncertainties and the dependence on cluster recognition criteria require further investigation with full energy spectrum comparisons.
