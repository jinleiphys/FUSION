# True muonium production in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions

> In this paper we investigate the production of a true muonium state, which is an atom consisting of a $μ^+ μ^-$ bound state, by $γγ$ interactions in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions considering an accurate treatment of the absorptive corrections and for the nuclear form factor. The rapidity distributions and cross sections are estimated considering the RHIC, LHC and FCC energies. Our results indicate that the experimental analysis can be useful to observe, for the first time, the true muonium state.

# True muonium production in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions

## Key claim
The production of para-true muonium (the spin-singlet ground state of a muon-antimuon bound atom) via two-photon fusion in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions yields cross sections of order microbarns, making experimental observation feasible at LHC, HE-LHC, and FCC energies, provided that the dominant background from light-by-light scattering can be removed.

## Method
The authors use the equivalent photon approximation to compute the cross section for the process PbPb → Pb ⊗ (μ⁺μ⁻) ⊗ Pb, where ⊗ denotes a rapidity gap. The nuclear photon flux is calculated using a realistic Wood-Saxon charge form factor, and absorptive corrections are included via a probability of hadronic interaction that excludes nuclear overlap. The elementary γγ → (μ⁺μ⁻) cross section is obtained from the Low formula using the two-photon decay width of the para-TM state, which in the non-relativistic approximation is Γ(n¹S₀) = α⁵ m_μ / (2 n³).

## Key numbers
- Cross section for full rapidity range at RHIC (√s = 0.2 TeV): 0.16 μb
- Cross section for full rapidity range at LHC (√s = 5.02 TeV): 1.24 μb
- Cross section for full rapidity range at HE-LHC (√s = 10.6 TeV): 1.70 μb
- Cross section for full rapidity range at FCC (√s = 39 TeV): 2.74 μb
- Cross section for central rapidities (−2.5 < Y < 2.5) at LHC: 0.68 μb
- Cross section for forward rapidities (2 < Y < 4.5) at LHC: 0.26 μb
- Predicted number of events per year at LHC (assuming 350 fb⁻¹ integrated luminosity): > 85 × 10⁹
- Predicted number of events per year at FCC (assuming 1000 fb⁻¹): > 500 × 10⁹
- Nuclear radius parameter R_A = 6.63 fm, diffuseness a = 0.549 fm for the Wood-Saxon form factor

## Context
This work extends the pioneering estimates of Serbo (1975) and Serbo et al. (2005) for true muonium production in ultraperipheral collisions by incorporating a realistic nuclear form factor and absorptive corrections, following the authors' previous study of dilepton production (Azevedo, Goncalves, Moreira, 2019). The results are motivated by recent observations of light-by-light scattering by the CMS and ATLAS Collaborations (2018, 2019), which demonstrated that Z⁴-enhanced photon-photon processes are experimentally accessible. The large predicted event rates enable future searches for this unobserved QED bound state, which could test CPT invariance and probe beyond-Standard-Model physics as discussed by LHCb and fixed-target studies.
