# Axially-symmetric Neutron stars: Implication of rapid rotation

> Models of relativistic rotating neutron star composed of hyperon rich matter is constructed in the framework of an effective field theory in the mean-field approach. The gross properties of compact star is calculated at both static and the mass-shedding limit in the axially symmetric basis. The effect of appearance and abundance of hyperons on equation of state of dense matter and stellar properties is lineated with particular emphasis on the underlying nuclear interactions. We find that the models can explain fast rotations, which supports the existence of millisecond pulsars. An important offshoot of the present investigation is that, irrespective of the model parameters and interaction taken, the star seems to sustain faster rotations (an increase in rotational frequency up to $\approx$ 50%) without any further deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, regardless of the specific model parameters and nuclear interactions used, a neutron star composed of hyperon-rich matter can sustain faster rotations (an increase in rotational frequency up to approximately 50%) without further deformation once a certain rotational frequency is reached, specifically at a Keplerian frequency of approximately 4700 s⁻¹ (about 716 Hz).

## Method

The authors constructed models of relativistic rotating neutron stars composed of hyperon-rich matter using an effective field theory in the mean-field approach, incorporating scalar, vector, and isovector meson interactions with cross-couplings. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static stars and used the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu method (via the Stergioulas code) to construct uniformly rotating star models in an axially symmetric space-time, calculating properties at both the static limit and the mass-shedding (Kepler) limit for five different parameter sets (G2, G1, TM1*, TM1, NL3).

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of static neutron stars lies in the range (1.29 – 1.79) M⊙ across the five models.
- Maximum mass of stars rotating at the Kepler limit lies in the range (1.49 – 2.18) M⊙.
- Increase in stellar mass at the Kepler limit is approximately (15 – 20)% compared to the static case.
- Increase in equatorial radius at maximum mass at the Kepler limit is approximately (38 – 43)% compared to the static case.
- Central density of the star at maximum mass in the static case lies in the range (4 – 10) ρ₀, where ρ₀ is normal nuclear matter density (0.145 – 0.153 fm⁻³).
- Keplerian rotational frequency at maximum mass ranges from 1009 Hz (G1 model) to 1183 Hz (G2 model).
- Moment of inertia at the Kepler limit ranges from I₄₅ = 1.47 (G2) to 3.92 (NL3) in units of 10⁴⁵ g cm².
- The star undergoes maximal deformation at Ωₖ ≈ 4700 s⁻¹ (f ≈ 716 Hz), after which it rotates at much higher frequency (up to approximately 50% increase) without further deformation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperon-rich neutron star matter in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory, comparing five models (G2, G1, TM1*, TM1, NL3) that differ in their nuclear interaction terms and saturation properties. The results are compared with observational constraints from massive stars (approximately 2 M⊙) from quasi-periodic oscillations, the canonical mass of PSR 1913+16 (1.44 M⊙), and the fastest known pulsars (XTE J1739-285 at 1122 Hz and a radio pulsar at 716 Hz). The study enables future work on constraining the equation of state of dense matter through observations of rapidly rotating neutron stars and their potential as sources of gravitational wave radiation.
