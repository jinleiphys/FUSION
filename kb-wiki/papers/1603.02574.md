# From the stable to the exotic: clustering in light nuclei

> A great deal of research work has been undertaken in alpha-clustering study since the pioneering discovery of 12C+12C molecular resonances half a century ago. Our knowledge on physics of nuclear molecules has increased considerably and nuclear clustering remains one of the most fruitful domains of nuclear physics, facing some of the greatest challenges and opportunities in the years ahead. The occurrence of "exotic" shapes in light N=Z alpha-like nuclei is investigated. Various approaches of the superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands associated with quasimolecular resonant structures are presented. Evolution of clustering from stability to the drip-lines is examined: clustering aspects are, in particular, discussed for light exotic nuclei with large neutron excess such as neutron-rich Oxygen isotopes with their complete spectroscopy.

# From the stable to the exotic: clustering in light nuclei

## Key claim
Clustering phenomena, including superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands associated with quasimolecular resonant structures, are a general feature of light N=Z alpha-like nuclei and also appear in neutron-rich isotopes such as oxygen, but the existence of a 4-alpha chain state in 16O and of Bose-Einstein condensate states in heavier alpha-like nuclei remains experimentally unconfirmed despite theoretical predictions.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental and theoretical work on alpha clustering in light nuclei, covering resonant structures in excitation functions for light alpha-cluster nuclei from the Coulomb barrier up to excitation energies of 20–50 MeV, gamma-ray spectroscopy of superdeformed rotational bands in nuclei such as 36Ar and 40Ca, radiative capture reactions measured with the DRAGON spectrometer at TRIUMF, and charged-particle coincidence measurements using the Binary Reaction Spectrometer at the Vivitron facility. The author also discusses results from the 12C(gamma,alpha)8Be reaction at the HIGS facility and from studies of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes using the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) framework.

## Key numbers
- The 2+2 "Hoyle" rotational excitation in 12C appears at around 10 MeV, consistent with an L=2 pattern from angular distributions measured at HIGS.
- A 0+6 state in 16O is predicted at about 15.1 MeV, approximately 700 keV above the 4-alpha-particle breakup threshold.
- The 3+ state of 16O at 11.085 MeV ± 3 keV has a total width Gamma(3+) < 0.23 eV, a value fifty times lower than known previously.
- The 12C+16O radiative capture reaction shows direct feeding of the prolate 4+3 state at 9.16 MeV and the octupole deformed 3- state at 6.88 MeV in 28Si.
- For the 12C+12C radiative capture reaction, enhanced feeding of the 24Mg prolate band was measured for a 4+-2+ resonance at E(c.m.) = 8.0 MeV near the Coulomb barrier.
- Superdeformed shapes in light nuclei correspond to quadrupole deformation parameter beta2 ≈ 0.6 (2:1 axis ratio), while hyperdeformed shapes correspond to beta2 ≈ 1.0 (3:1 axis ratio).

## Context
This review extends the original Ikeda diagram (Ikeda, 1968) for N=Z alpha-conjugate nuclei and the extended Ikeda diagram proposed by von Oertzen (2001) for neutron-rich nuclei, further modifying it to include 14C cluster cores alongside 16O cores. The work builds on decades of research on nuclear molecules since the discovery of 12C+12C molecular resonances, and on the cranked alpha-cluster model (Freer, 2007) and Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations. The paper enables future searches for Bose-Einstein condensate states in 16O and for hyperdeformed configurations in medium-mass nuclei using combined gamma-ray and charged-particle coincidence techniques.
