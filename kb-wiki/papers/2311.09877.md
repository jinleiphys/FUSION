# Measurements of the lightest hypernucleus ($\mathrm{^3_ΛH}$): progress and perspective

> The hyperon-nucleon ($Y$-$N$) interaction is important for the description of the equation-of-state of high baryon density matter. Hypernuclei, the cluster object of nucleons and hyperons, serve as cornerstones of a full understanding of the $Y$-$N$ interaction. Recent measurements of the lightest known hypernucleus, the hypertriton's ($\mathrm{^3_ΛH}$) and anti-hypertriton's ($\mathrm{^3_{\barΛ}\bar{H}}$) lifetime, mass and $Λ$ separation energy have attracted interests on the subject. Its cross section and collective flow parameters have also been measured in heavy-ion collisions, which have revealed new features on its production mechanism. In this article we summarise recent measurements of $\mathrm{^3_ΛH}$, focusing on the heavy-ion collisions. We will discuss their implications for the $\mathrm{^3_ΛH}$ properties and the constrains on the $Y$-$N$ interaction models.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent measurements of the lightest hypernucleus, the hypertriton (³_ΛH), focusing on heavy-ion collision experiments, and argues that while the average lifetime from a common fit of heavy-ion data is 236.4 ± 8.1 ps (about 10% smaller than the free Λ lifetime), the large spread of both lifetime and Λ separation energy values across different experiments indicates unresolved systematic issues that require further investigation.

## Method

The authors compiled and analyzed world data on ³_ΛH lifetime, mass, and Λ separation energy from early nuclear emulsion and helium bubble chamber experiments as well as modern heavy-ion collision measurements by STAR at RHIC and ALICE at LHC. In heavy-ion experiments, ³_ΛH is reconstructed via its mesonic weak decay channels (³He + π⁻, d + p + π⁻) by identifying secondary decay vertices and calculating invariant mass distributions, with machine learning techniques (gradient-boosted decision trees) applied to improve signal-to-background ratios. The lifetime is extracted by fitting the proper decay length distributions with an exponential function, while the Λ separation energy is obtained from precision mass measurements calibrated using Λ decays.

## Key numbers

- Average lifetime of ³_ΛH from world data: 228.5 ± 11.6 ps (uncertainty scaled by S = 1.3)
- Lifetime from common fit of heavy-ion collision data only: 236.4 ± 8.1 ps (uncertainty scaled by S = 1.1)
- Free Λ lifetime for reference: τ_Λ = 263.2 ± 2.0 ps (Particle Data Group 2022)
- Global average of Λ separation energy B_Λ: 0.17 ± 0.06 MeV (uncertainty scaled by S = 1.6)
- Frequently cited earlier value of B_Λ: 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV (Juric 1973)
- Slope parameter dv₁/dy for light nuclei mass dependence: 0.3323 ± 0.0003
- Slope parameter dv₁/dy for hypernuclei mass dependence: 0.27 ± 0.04

## Context

This review extends the long-standing discussion on hypertriton properties that began with early nuclear emulsion and bubble chamber measurements in the 1960s and 1970s (Danysz 1953, Juric 1973). It addresses the puzzle raised by Davis (2005) and Dalitz (2005) regarding the large spread of measured lifetimes, which ranged from about 0.35τ_Λ to 1.08τ_Λ. The paper enables future constraints on hyperon-nucleon interaction models by providing a comprehensive comparison of modern heavy-ion data from STAR (2019, 2021, 2022) and ALICE (2015, 2019, 2022) with theoretical approaches including coalescence models, thermal models (Andronic 2010), and transport models such as JAM, UrQMD, and PHQMD. The measurements of collective flow and production yields open a new direction for studying the in-medium Y-N interaction under finite pressure and its implications for the equation-of-state of high baryon density matter in neutron stars.
