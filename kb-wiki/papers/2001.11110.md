# Electromagnetic radiation from the pre-equilibrium/pre-hydro stage of the quark-gluon plasma

> Photons produced in the pre-equilibrium/pre-hydro stage of the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions were computed using parton distribution functions obtained from solutions of the Boltzmann equation. The effect of the initial gluon momentum anisotropy $ξ$ and the dependence on the saturation momentum $Q_s$ was investigated. We see that small $Q_s$ results in a photon yield enhancement, whereas a larger $Q_s$ results in a pre-equilibrium photon suppression, owing to the strict constraint of matching to experimental energy density.

## Key claim

Photons from the pre-equilibrium stage of the quark-gluon plasma can enhance the total photon yield by 40-80% at low transverse momentum, but this enhancement is strongly suppressed when the saturation momentum \(Q_s\) is increased from 1 GeV to 2 GeV, because matching to experimental energy density forces the initial gluon population to scale as \(f_0 \propto Q_s^{-3}\).

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation for gluon and quark distribution functions in the diffusion approximation (Fokker-Planck form) with \(2\leftrightarrow2\) scattering, initialized with a colour-glass-condensate-inspired gluon distribution at proper time \(t_0 Q_s = 1\). Photon production rates were computed from Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation using a small-angle approximation, with the Coulomb logarithm determined numerically by matching to leading-order AMY rates. The pre-equilibrium photon yield was obtained by integrating over space-time rapidity and proper time, and was added to pQCD and hydrodynamic photon contributions for comparison with experimental data from RHIC at 200 GeV and LHC at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC 200 GeV with \(Q_s = 1\) GeV, adding pre-equilibrium photons increases the total photon yield by about 40% at \(p_T = 1\) GeV.
- At LHC 2.76 TeV with \(Q_s = 1\) GeV, the pre-equilibrium contribution increases the total photon yield by about 80% at \(p_T = 1\) GeV.
- At LHC 5.02 TeV with \(Q_s = 1\) GeV, the enhancement is about 80%; with \(Q_s = 2\) GeV the enhancement drops to about 4%.
- The initial gluon population \(f_0\) is proportional to \(Q_s^{-3}\), so doubling \(Q_s\) from 1 to 2 GeV reduces \(f_0\) by a factor of 8.
- Three initial anisotropy values were studied: \(\xi = 1.0\) (isotropic), \(\xi = 1.5\), and \(\xi = 3.7\) (giving \(P_L/P_T = 0.1\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pre-equilibrium photon production by using distribution functions from a full Boltzmann-equation solution rather than simpler parametric forms. The results are compared to experimental data from STAR, PHENIX, and ALICE, and to calculations of pQCD and hydrodynamic photons from Paquet (2016). The finding that the pre-equilibrium photon yield is sensitive to both the saturation momentum \(Q_s\) and the initial gluon anisotropy \(\xi\) suggests that these photons can serve as a probe of the initial-state properties of heavy-ion collisions, complementing constraints from final-state hadron multiplicities.
