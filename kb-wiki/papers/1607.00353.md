# To fission or not to fission

> The fission-fragments mass-yield of 236U is obtained by an approximate solution of the eigenvalue problem of the collective Hamiltonian that describes the dynamics of the fission process whose degrees of freedom are: the fission (elongation), the neck and the mass-asymmetry mode. The macroscopic-microscopic method is used to evaluate the potential energy surface. The macroscopic energy part is calculated using the liquid drop model and the microscopic corrections are obtained using the Woods-Saxon single-particle levels. The four dimensional modified Cassini ovals shape parametrization is used to describe the shape of the fissioning nucleus. The mass tensor is taken within the cranking-type approximation. The final fragment mass distribution is obtained by weighting the adiabatic density distribution in the collective space with the neck-dependent fission probability. The neck degree of freedom is found to play a significant role in determining that final fragment mass distribution.

## Key claim

The fission-fragment mass yield of 236U can be reproduced by a three-dimensional collective Hamiltonian when the fission probability is made dependent on the neck radius, using a Fermi-function parametrization with two adjustable parameters; the neck degree of freedom is found to play a significant role in determining the final fragment mass distribution, and the authors treat this as a first attempt toward a universal parametrization independent of the specific fission reaction.

## Method

The authors solve the eigenvalue problem of a three-dimensional collective Hamiltonian in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, using the macroscopic-microscopic method with the liquid drop model for the macroscopic energy and Woods-Saxon single-particle levels for microscopic shell and pairing corrections. The nuclear shape is described by the four-dimensional modified Cassini ovals parametrization, and the mass tensor is computed using a cranking-type approximation with diagonal matrix elements omitted to avoid unphysical divergences. The final fragment mass distribution is obtained by weighting the adiabatic density distribution in the collective space with a neck-dependent fission probability, using a Fermi function with parameters κ0 and d fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The fission-fragment mass yield of 236U is calculated for thermal neutron induced fission of 235U.
- The neck radius parameter κ = r_neck/R_0 reaches approximately 0.2 at scission, realized at R12/R0 = 2.0 for α4 = -0.18, R12/R0 = 2.25 for α4 = 0, and R12/R0 > 2.5 for α4 = 0.18 with asymmetry parameter a ≈ 0.2.
- The optimal shape approach gives r_neck ≈ 0.3 R0 and R12^crit ≈ 2.3 R0.
- The fitted parameters for the neck-breaking probability are κ0 = 0.16 and d = 0.09.
- The calculated mass distribution is shifted by approximately 2 mass units toward symmetric fission compared to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the two-dimensional model of Refs. Berlin and APPBS (2015) by adding the neck degree of freedom (α4) and a neck-dependent fission probability, while omitting nonadiabatic and dissipative effects expected to be small at low temperatures. The approach enables future inclusion of nonadiabatic effects beyond the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and energy dissipation, which could modify the theoretical distribution and potentially correct the slight shift toward symmetric fission.
