# Dissociation-driven quarkonium spin alignment in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV

> The observation of spin alignment of quarkonia in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions provides deep insight into the possible formation of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The present study investigates the spin alignment of quarkonia induced by dissociation mechanisms arising from medium effects imposed on quarkonia. We implement an effective Hamiltonian with a medium-modified color-singlet potential to incorporate the coupling of quarkonium spin with medium vorticity. This coupling gives rise to spin-dependent dissociation, which we identify as a plausible mechanism contributing to quarkonium spin alignment. Within the ambit of second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, we calculate the spin-dependent decay widths of charmonium ($J/ψ$, $ψ$(2S)) and bottomonium ($Υ$(1S), $Υ$(2S)) in a rotating thermal medium, including collisional damping and gluonic dissociation effects. We evaluate the observable $ρ_{00}$ for Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV as a function of transverse momentum of the quarkonia, charged particle multiplicity, and medium rotation. The results demonstrate that medium vorticity modifies the quarkonia net decay width and, as a consequence, quarkonia spin alignment gets modified. These findings suggest new directions for understanding spin transport and the microscopic dynamics of vortical QGP.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that spin-dependent dissociation of quarkonia in a rotating quark-gluon plasma, arising from the coupling of quarkonium spin with medium vorticity via a medium-modified color-singlet potential, is a plausible mechanism contributing to quarkonium spin alignment; the authors explicitly state that this mechanism is identified as one plausible mechanism, not the exclusive one, and the results depend on the specific choice of initial thermalization time and hydrodynamic modeling.

## Method

The authors implement an effective Hamiltonian with a medium-modified color-singlet potential to incorporate spin-vorticity coupling for charmonium (J/ψ, ψ(2S)) and bottomonium (Υ(1S), Υ(2S)) states. They solve the radial Schrödinger equation in a rotating frame, where rotation is parameterized by a conserved circulation parameter C, to obtain spin-dependent wave functions and eigenvalues. The spin-dependent decay widths are calculated from collisional damping (via the imaginary part of the in-medium potential) and gluonic dissociation (via thermal gluon cross sections), using a temperature cooling profile from 1+1D second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with boost invariance. The spin alignment observable ρ₀₀ is then computed from the dissociation probabilities of the three spin projection states (m = +1, 0, -1) integrated over the medium evolution from initial temperature to freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Initial thermalization time τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c is adopted for Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV.
- Charged particle multiplicity ranges from ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ = 44.9 ± 3.4 (70-80% centrality) to 2035 ± 52 (0-2.5% centrality), taken from ALICE experimental data.
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.27 GeV and bottom quark mass m_b = 4.18 GeV are used.
- String tension σ = 0.192 GeV² is used in the medium-modified potential.
- Strong coupling at the HTL scale at T = 400 MeV is α_s^T ≈ 0.26; at the soft scale, α_s^s ≈ 0.33 for bottom quark and α_s^s ≈ 0.5 for charm quark; at the ultra-soft scale, α_s^u ≈ 0.76 for bottom quark and α_s^u ≈ 0.87 for charm quark.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quarkonium suppression in the QGP by Singh et al. (2015, 2018, 2021, 2025) that used collisional damping and gluonic dissociation mechanisms, and it builds on the theoretical framework of spin-vorticity coupling for hadron polarization developed by Liang and Wang (2004, 2005) and later applied to vector mesons by Sheng et al. (2019, 2022). The study is motivated by recent ALICE measurements of J/ψ spin alignment in Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (ALICE 2020, 2022), which showed ρ₀₀ < 1/3 in the helicity and event-plane frames but ρ₀₀ > 1/3 in the Collins-Soper frame, and it aims to provide a microscopic mechanism for these observations. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data across different centrality and p_T bins, and suggest new directions for understanding spin transport and vortical QGP dynamics.
