# Re-appearance of the pairing correlations at finite temperature

> Rotational and deformation dependence of isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations at finite temperature are studied in an exactly solvable cranked deformed shell model Hamiltonian. It is shown that isovector pairing correlations, as expected, decrease with increasing deformation and the isoscalar pairing correlations remain constant at temperature, T=0. However, it is observed that at finite temperature both isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations are enhanced with increasing deformation, which contradict the mean-field predictions. It is also demonstrated that the pair correlations, which are quenched at T=0 and high rotational frequency re-appear at finite temperature. The changes in the individual multipole pairing fields as a function of rotation and deformation are analyzed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, contrary to mean-field predictions, both isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations can be enhanced with increasing deformation at finite temperature, and that pairing correlations quenched at zero temperature and high rotational frequency can re-appear at finite temperature. These results are obtained in an exactly solvable cranked deformed shell model Hamiltonian for a single j-shell, and the authors note that the real deformation dependence in realistic potentials may differ from their simple model.

## Method

The authors solved exactly a Hamiltonian consisting of a deformed Nilsson one-body term and a scalar two-body delta-interaction for protons and neutrons in the \(f_{7/2}\) subshell. Pairing correlations were calculated using the canonical ensemble (which has well-defined particle number) by averaging over exact eigenstates weighted by Boltzmann factors. The total isovector (t=1) and isoscalar (t=0) pair-gaps, as well as individual multipole components (J=0,1,2,4,6,7), were computed as functions of rotational frequency, temperature, and deformation parameter \(\kappa\).

## Key numbers

- For the spherical case (\(\kappa=0\)) at zero temperature, the isovector pair-gap drops in steps with increasing rotational frequency; for \(\kappa=3\) the drop is smoother.
- At \(\hbar\omega=2\) and 4 MeV, the pair-gaps increase with increasing temperature, showing re-appearance of pairing correlations quenched at low temperature.
- The monopole field (J=0, t=1) is zero at low temperatures and becomes non-zero at about T=0.6 MeV, then shows a smooth increase with temperature.
- The dipole field (J=1, t=0) is almost constant at lower temperatures but starts increasing at around T=3 MeV.
- At T=0, isovector correlations drop with increasing deformation; at finite temperature, the isovector \(\Delta_{t=1}\) increases from 4.4 MeV at \(\kappa=0\) to 5.0 MeV at \(\kappa=8\) MeV.
- For the deformed case at T=0, the t=1 pair field is smaller than in the spherical case, while the t=0 pairing field is quite similar in both cases.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Baldo et al. (1999) and Baldo et al. (2003), which first reported the re-appearance of pairing correlations at finite temperature using number-parity projection and exact solutions for monopole pairing. The present study generalizes these findings to include higher multipole pairing fields (isovector and isoscalar) and deformation dependence, contradicting mean-field predictions such as those by Goodman (1984) and Sheikh et al. (2002). The results suggest that realistic Cranking Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations at finite temperature, such as those performed for \(^{74}\)Rb by Dean et al., would be valuable to confirm these findings in more realistic systems.
