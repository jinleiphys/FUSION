# Renormalized Field Strength Correlators in SU(N) Gauge Theory and Gauge/String Duality

> We use gauge/string duality to analytically evaluate correlation lengths of the renormalized field strength correlators in pure Yang-Mills theories at zero and finite temperature.

## Key claim
Using gauge/string duality with a deformed AdS₅ background, the paper analytically evaluates correlation lengths of renormalized field strength correlators in pure Yang-Mills theories, but the results are scheme-dependent due to the need for renormalization conditions (e.g., the constant \(c\) must be fixed by matching to lattice data or physical assumptions).

## Method
The authors model pure SU(N) gauge theory via a five-dimensional deformed AdS₅ metric with a deformation parameter \(\s\) (zero temperature) or a deformed Schwarzschild black hole in AdS₅ (finite temperature). They compute the renormalized minimal area \(S\) of a string world-sheet end-surface (Figure 1, right) using the Nambu-Goto action, then relate it to the correlation lengths \(\xi\) (for quark-antiquark correlator \(\Psi\)) and \(\lambda\) (for field strength correlator D) via \(\Psi \sim e^{-S}\) and D \(\sim e^{-2S}\). The integrals over the holographic coordinate are regularized using modified minimal subtraction, and the constant \(c\) is fixed by renormalization conditions.

## Key numbers
- For SU(3), with \(\g \approx 0.62\) and \(\s \approx 0.45\ \text{GeV}^2\) (fixed from heavy quark potential), the correlation length \(\lambda\) is estimated as \(\lambda \approx 0.20\ \text{fm}\).
- Lattice data (Giacomo et al.) give \(\lambda \approx 0.22\ \text{fm}\) with an error of \(0.03\ \text{fm}\).
- Choosing \(\lambda = m_G^{-1}\) with glueball mass \(m_G \approx 3.64\sqrt{\sigma}\) gives \(\lambda \approx 0.13\ \text{fm}\).
- At zero temperature, the relation \(\lambda = \frac{1}{2}\xi\) holds.
- At finite temperature, the magnetic correlation length \(\lambda_s\) approximately satisfies \(\frac{\lambda_s}{\lambda} \approx \sqrt{\frac{\sigma}{\sigma_s}}\), with \(\sigma_s\) given by \(\sigma_s = \sigma \frac{T^2}{\T^2} \exp\left\{\frac{\T^2}{T^2} - 1\right\}\) (valid up to \(3-4\,\T\)).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous effective string model for pure gauge theories, which successfully described the heavy quark potential (Andreev and Zakharov, 2006), the gluon condensate (Andreev and Zakharov, 2007), the spatial string tension (Andreev, 2009), and the renormalized Polyakov loop (Andreev, 2010). It provides the first analytical calculation of field strength correlators from gauge/string duality, enabling comparison with lattice simulations (e.g., Giacomo et al.) and predictions for scheme-independent relations (e.g., \(\lambda_s^{-1}(T_1)/T_1 - \lambda_s^{-1}(T_2)/T_2\)) that can be tested numerically.
