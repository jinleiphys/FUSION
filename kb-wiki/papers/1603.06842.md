# Role of spd- Continuum Components in the Halo Nucleus 6He

> Role of different continuum components in the weakly bound nucleus 6He is studied by coupling unbound spd-waves of 5He by using simple pairing contact-delta interaction. The results show that 6He ground state $0^+$ displays collective nature by taking contribution from five different oscillating continuum states that sum up to give an exponentially decaying bound wavefunction emerging from five different possible configurations i.e. $(s_{1/2})^2$, $(p_{1/2})^2$, $(p_{3/2})^2$, $(d_{3/2})^2$ and $(d_{5/2})^2$. The ground state properties of 6He has been calculated.

## Key claim

The bound ground state of the halo nucleus 6He emerges collectively from the coupling of five different unbound spd-continuum configurations of 5He via a simple contact-delta pairing interaction, with the (p3/2)2 configuration dominating at 89.7% of the ground state composition.

## Method

The author constructs two-particle wavefunctions by tensor-coupling continuum single-particle states of 5He generated with a Woods-Saxon potential (depth V0 = -42.6 MeV, radius r0 = 1.2 fm, diffuseness a = 0.9 fm, spin-orbit coefficient Vls = 8.5 MeV). Continuum wavefunctions are calculated for energies from 0.0 to 10.0 MeV on a radial grid from 0.1 fm to 100.0 fm, and an attractive pairing contact-delta interaction (-gδ(r1 - r2)) is used to couple the five configurations (s1/2)2, (p1/2)2, (p3/2)2, (d3/2)2, and (d5/2)2. The pairing strength is adjusted to reproduce the correct ground state energy of 6He, with the largest basis (N=100, energy spacing 0.1 MeV) providing a fairly dense continuum.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pair separation energy of 6He: 0.975 MeV (from Ajzenberg-Selove, cited in text).
- Experimental resonances in 5He: p3/2 at 0.789 MeV above neutron separation threshold (width 0.648 MeV), p1/2 at 1.27 MeV (width 5.57 MeV).
- Ground state composition of 6He: (p3/2)2 = 0.897 (89.7%), (p1/2)2 = 0.080, (d5/2)2 = 0.009, (2s1/2)2 = 0.008, (d3/2)2 = 0.005.
- Calculated matter radius Rm of 6He: 2.37674 fm.
- Mean square distance between valence neutrons (rNN2): 28.8404 fm2.
- Mean square distance of valence neutron center of mass from core (rc-2N2): 7.21011 fm2.

## Context

This work extends a previous model by Fortunato et al. (cited as FORT) that coupled only unbound p-waves of 5He, now including sd-continuum waves to study the 6He ground state. The results are compared with earlier calculations by Myo (cited as MYO) and Hagino (cited as HAG), showing good agreement in ground state composition and radial properties. The study demonstrates that a simple contact-delta pairing interaction can naturally explain the bound character of Borromean nuclei like 6He while also accounting for resonant structures in the low-lying continuum, motivated by recent experimental measurements at GANIL by Mougeot et al. (cited as MOUG).
