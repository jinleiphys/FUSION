# Classical mappings of the symplectic model and their application to the theory of large-amplitude collective motion

> We study the algebra Sp(n,R) of the symplectic model, in particular for the cases n=1,2,3, in a new way. Starting from the Poisson-bracket realization we derive a set of partial differential equations for the generators as functions of classical canonical variables. We obtain a solution to these equations that represents the classical limit of a boson mapping of the algebra. The relationship to the collective dynamics is formulated as a theorem that associates the mapping with an exact solution of the time-dependent Hartree approximation. This solution determines a decoupled classical symplectic manifold, thus satisfying the criteria that define an exactly solvable model in the theory of large amplitude collective motion. The models thus obtained also provide a test of methods for constructing an approximately decoupled manifold in fully realistic cases. We show that an algorithm developed in one of our earlier works reproduces the main results of the theorem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the classical limit of a boson mapping of the Sp(n,R) algebra provides an exact solution of the time-dependent Hartree approximation, but only for Hamiltonians that are polynomials in the generators of Sp(n,R) (n=1,2,3); the decoupling fails when terms outside this algebra, such as x^4 interactions, are added.

## Method

The authors derive a set of partial differential equations from the Poisson-bracket realization of the Sp(n,R) algebra and solve them to obtain classical mappings of the generators as functions of canonical collective variables (Q,P). They then construct the corresponding density matrices that solve the time-dependent Hartree equation, showing that these define a decoupled classical symplectic manifold. The procedure is carried out explicitly for n=1 (monopole), n=2 (monopole plus two-dimensional quadrupole), and n=3 (monopole plus three-dimensional quadrupole), and the results are compared with an algorithm from their earlier work on large-amplitude collective motion.

## Key numbers

- For Sp(1,R): the Casimir invariant in the Hartree approximation gives C = N^4/4, where N is the number of fermions.
- For Sp(1,R): the collective potential energy is V(Q) = (1/2)Q + (1/2)kappa Q^2 + C/Q, with C = N^4/8 from the kinetic term.
- For Sp(2,R): the constant C in the mapping is C = (2/9)N^3 for a special representation.
- For Sp(2,R): the collective potential energy is V(Q) = Q_0 + (1/2)kappa_0 Q_0^2 + (1/2)kappa_2 (Q_1^2 + Q_2^2) + C Q_0 / (Q_0^2 - Q_1^2 - Q_2^2).
- For Sp(2,R): the intrinsic frequencies are bar_omega_1^2 = |C|/(Q_0 + Q)^2 and bar_omega_2^2 = |C|/(Q_0 - Q)^2, where Q = sqrt(Q_1^2 + Q_2^2).
- For the extended model with an x^4 term added to Sp(1,R): the mass matrix elements are B^{00} = 4Q, B^{04} = 8Q_4, and B^{44} = 16Q_6.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the monopole model (Sp(1,R)) from Klein and Walet (1991) to the full symplectic model algebras Sp(2,R) and Sp(3,R), the latter connecting to the symplectic model of Rowe (1980) and the pseudo-symplectic model used for shell-model truncation in deformed nuclei. The exactly solvable models derived here provide a testing ground for algorithms in the theory of large-amplitude collective motion, which the authors had previously developed and applied to nuclear structure problems. The paper shows that the algorithm from their earlier work reproduces the exact results, validating the method for future applications to more realistic Hamiltonians.
