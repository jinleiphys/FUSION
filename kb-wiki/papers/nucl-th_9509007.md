# Flavor Production in Pb(160AGeV) on Pb Collisions: Effect of Color Ropes and Hadronic Rescattering

> Collective interactions in the preequilibrium quark matter and hadronic resonance gas stage of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied in the framework of the the transport theoretical approach RQMD. The paper reviews string fusion into color ropes and hadronic rescattering which serve as models for these interactions. Hadron production in central Pb(160AGeV) on Pb collisions has been calculated. The changes of the final flavor composition are more pronounced than in previous RQMD studies of light ion induced reactions at 200AGeV. The ratio of created quark pairs $s\bar{s}$/($u\bar{u}$+$d\bar{d}$) is enhanced by a factor of 2.4 in comparison to $pp$ results. Color rope formation increases the initially produced antibaryons to 3 times the value in the `NN mode', but only one quarter of the produced antibaryons survives because of subsequent strong absorption. The differences in the final particle composition for Pb on Pb collisions compared to S induced reactions are attributed to the hadronic resonance gas stage which is baryon-richer and lasts longer.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in central Pb(160AGeV) on Pb collisions, the combination of color rope formation and hadronic rescattering enhances the ratio of created strange to non-strange quark pairs by a factor of 2.4 compared to pp results, and that color rope formation initially increases antibaryon production to three times the value in the 'NN mode', but only one quarter of those antibaryons survive due to subsequent strong absorption in the hadronic resonance gas stage.

## Method

The study uses the transport theoretical approach RQMD (Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) to simulate central Pb(160AGeV) on Pb collisions. The model incorporates string fusion into color ropes (chromoelectric flux-tubes with higher-dimensional SU(3) color charges) during the preequilibrium quark matter stage, and hadronic rescattering including resonance formation and annihilation during the hadronic resonance gas stage. The flavor composition of final-state hadrons is calculated from the simulated events, with particular focus on strange hadron and antibaryon yields.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of created quark pairs s\bar{s}/(u\bar{u}+d\bar{d}) is enhanced by a factor of 2.4 in comparison to pp results.
- Color rope formation increases the initially produced antibaryons to 3 times the value in the 'NN mode'.
- Only one quarter of the produced antibaryons survives because of subsequent strong absorption.
- The transverse rope radius is set to 0.8 fm, fixed by the requirement to get reasonable production rates from elementary string decays in comparison to e+e- hadroproduction data.
- The elementary string tension (triplet flux-tube) is 0.9 GeV/fm, related to the Regge slope parameter.

## Context

This work extends previous RQMD studies of light ion induced reactions at 200AGeV (S on A collisions) to the heavier Pb+Pb system, where collectivity is expected to be strongest due to minimized surface-over-volume ratio. The paper reviews and builds upon earlier work on string fusion in QGSM (Ameodo, 1993), the Spanish version of the dual-parton model (Aichelin, 1995), and quark matter droplet formation in VENUS (Aichelin, 1993). The results enable comparison with experimental data from the CERN-SPS Pb beam program and provide a baseline for studying the transient stages of heavy ion collisions, with a follow-up paper planned to discuss the dynamical evolution of the created source in space-time until freeze-out.
