# Limit on T-Violating P-Conserving $ρN N$ Interaction from the $γ$ Decay of $^{57}$Fe

> We use the experimental limit on the interference of M1 and E2 multipoles in the $γ$-decay of $^{57}$Fe to bound the time-reversal-violating parity-conserving $ρN N$ vertex. Our approach is a large-basis shell-model calculation of the interference. We find an upper limit on the parameter $\bar{g}_ρ$, the relative strength of the T-violating $ρN N$ vertex, of close to $10^{-2}$, a value similar to the best limits from other kinds of experiments.

# Limit on T-Violating P-Conserving ρNN Interaction from the γ Decay of ⁵⁷Fe

## Key claim

The experimental upper limit on M1-E2 interference in the γ-decay of ⁵⁷Fe constrains the time-reversal-violating parity-conserving ρNN coupling parameter $\bar{g}_ρ$ to be close to $10^{-2}$, but the central value extracted is $(2 ± 4) × 10^{-2}$, meaning the result is consistent with zero within uncertainty and provides an upper bound rather than a nonzero measurement.

## Method

The authors performed large-basis shell-model calculations in a model space of 17 valence nucleons in the $pf$ shell, using the Lanczos algorithm implemented in the CRUNCHER code to obtain approximate wave functions for the $3/2^-$ and $5/2^-$ states in ⁵⁷Fe. They treated the TVPC ρ-exchange interaction as a perturbation that mixes higher-lying states into the two states involved in the 137 keV → 14 keV γ-transition, and computed the resulting phase $\varepsilon_{E2} - \varepsilon_{M1}$ from the imaginary part of the multipole mixing ratio. Three different effective $pf$-shell interactions (FPVH, TBLC8, and FPBPN) were used to test sensitivity, and the M1 operator was calibrated by adjusting the isoscalar spin g-factor to reproduce the known M1 matrix element for the first transition.

## Key numbers

- Experimental upper limit on $|\sin\eta|$ (the imaginary part of the multipole mixing ratio): $(3.1 ± 6.5) × 10^{-4}$ from Cheung, Henrikson, and Boehm (1977)
- Extracted limit on $\bar{g}_ρ$: $|\bar{g}_ρ| = (2 ± 4) × 10^{-2}$, implying an upper bound close to $10^{-2}$
- Ratio $|\varepsilon_{E2} - \varepsilon_{M1}| / \bar{g}_ρ = 16.4 × 10^{-3}$ (averaged over FPVH and FPBPN forces in the large space)
- Large model space contained 23604 m-scheme states, compared to the full $pf$-shell dimension of approximately $4.5 × 10^8$
- M1 total strength in the large space was about a factor of 10 larger than in the small space (e.g., B(M1) for $3/2^-_1 → n5/2^-$: 11.56 nuclear magnetons² for FPVH large space vs. 1.154 for small space)
- The M1 resonance in the strength distribution appears at approximately 12 MeV excitation energy

## Context

This work extends the approach of Beyer (1993), who used the same ⁵⁷Fe experiment to constrain the TVPC coupling of the $A_1$ meson but employed a smaller model space that artificially restricted M1 strength by excluding the $1f_{7/2}$-$1f_{5/2}$ spin-flip transition. The resulting limit on $\bar{g}_ρ$ is comparable to limits from electric dipole moment experiments (Haxton, Horing, and Musolf, 1994) and from polarized neutron scattering from aligned ¹⁶⁵Ho (Engel, Gould, and Hnizdo, 1994), all constraining $\bar{g}_ρ$ to about $10^{-2}$. The authors note that upcoming detailed balance experiments (Blanke et al., 1983) may provide limits better by two orders of magnitude, though theoretical expectations from Frampton and Springer and from Conti and Khriplovich suggest $\bar{g}_ρ$ may not exceed $10^{-8}$.
