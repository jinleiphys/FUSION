# Viscosity and boost invariance at RHIC and LHC

> We consider the longitudinal hydrodynamic evolution of the fireball created in a relativistic heavy-ion collision. Nonzero shear viscosity reduces the colling rate of the system and hinders the acceleration of the longitudinal flow. As a consequence, the initial energy density needed to reproduce the experimental data at RHIC energies is significantly reduced. At LHC energies, we expect that shear viscosity helps to conserve a Bjorken plateau in the rapidity distributions during the expansion.

## Key claim

Nonzero shear viscosity reduces the cooling rate and hinders the acceleration of longitudinal flow in the fireball expansion, which significantly lowers the initial energy density needed to reproduce RHIC data; at LHC energies, shear viscosity may help preserve a Bjorken plateau in rapidity distributions, but this outcome depends on the initial conditions and the specific value of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio.

## Method

The author solves 1+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic equations for longitudinal expansion, using a realistic equation of state combining lattice QCD results and a hadron gas model (Chojnacki and Florkowski 2007). The hydrodynamic evolution starts from Gaussian initial energy density profiles in space-time rapidity with Bjorken scaling longitudinal flow at proper time τ₀ = 1 fm/c, and freeze-out is taken at constant temperature T_f = 165 MeV. Hadron spectra are computed using the Cooper-Frye formula with viscous corrections, and the parameters of the initial distribution (σ and ε₀) are adjusted to reproduce pion and kaon rapidity distributions measured by the BRAHMS Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio values considered: η/s = 0.1–0.3
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ₀ = 1 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 165 MeV
- For η/s = 0.1–0.2 at RHIC energies, the initial energy density is reduced by a factor of 2–3 compared to ideal fluid calculations
- At LHC energies, particle multiplicities at central rapidities are assumed to increase by a factor of 2 compared to Au-Au at the highest RHIC energies
- For the LHC plateau scenario, the Bjorken scaling plateau spans 8 units of central rapidities in the initial condition

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations that assumed ideal fluid behavior (Kolb 2003) and previous viscous hydrodynamic studies that focused primarily on transverse flow with boost invariance (Song 2007, Teaney 2003, Baier 2006). The author's prior work (Bozek 2007) introduced longitudinal viscous effects, and this paper applies that framework to both RHIC and LHC energies. The results enable more realistic modeling of initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions and provide a mechanism to explain the narrow Bjorken plateau observed at RHIC (Hirano 2002, Satarov 2006), while predicting that a wider plateau may survive at LHC if shear viscosity is sufficiently large.
