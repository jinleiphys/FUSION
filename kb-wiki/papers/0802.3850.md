# Spin-polarized collision of deuterium and tritium: Relativistic Kinematics

> We investigate the relativistic kinematics of the spin-polarized collision of deuterium incident on tritium, producing ${}^4$He and a neutron. Within the context of special relativity, we apply the conservation of four momentum and the conservation of intrinsic spin, which leads to a system of ten equations. We impose initial conditions such that the deuterium is moving along the x-axis, the tritium is stationary at the origin of coordinates, and the classical spin vector of the deuterium (spin magnitude = 1) is along the +z-axis, while the classical spin vector for tritium (spin magnitude = 1/2) is along the $-$z-axis. We expand the ten conservation equations to second order in velocities and we solve them for the velocity components of the neutron, its unit-spin-orientation vector, and the velocity components of the ${}^4$He nucleus, as a function of the incident deuterium energy. We find that this analytic solution agrees closely with the numerical solution of the ten (unexpanded) equations. For a given energy of deuterium, we find that there are two solutions, each solution having a unique velocity for the emitted neutron and helium nucleus. The two solutions are related to each other by reflection in the plane perpendicular to the deuterium spin and containing the initial deuterium velocity vector.

# Spin-polarized collision of deuterium and tritium: Relativistic Kinematics

## Key claim

For a spin-polarized deuterium-tritium collision with anti-aligned spins and zero impact parameter, there exist exactly two classical relativistic solutions for the emitted neutron and helium velocities, related by reflection in the plane perpendicular to the deuterium spin. The analysis is limited to the zero-impact-parameter case because relaxing this constraint introduces complications in special relativity associated with the speed of propagation of interactions and relativistic invariance.

## Method

The authors apply conservation of four-momentum and conservation of intrinsic spin four-tensor (using Synge's formalism) to the reaction d + t → n + ⁴He within special relativity. They expand the ten conservation equations to second order in velocities and solve analytically for the velocity components of the neutron, its unit-spin-orientation vector, and the velocity components of the ⁴He nucleus as functions of incident deuterium energy. The analytic solutions are compared with numerical solutions of the full (unexpanded) ten equations.

## Key numbers

- The reaction cross section peaks at 5 barns for a deuterium energy of 107 keV (citing Ohlsen (1967) and Bonar (1968)).
- At the maximum cross section (deuteron kinetic energy 107 keV), the neutron emission angle is θ_n = 82.85 degrees and the helium emission angle is θ_h = 89.88 degrees in the laboratory frame.
- Particle masses used: deuterium 2.01410177785 u, tritium 3.01604927767 u, neutron 1.00866491574 u, ⁴He 4.00260325415 u; speed of light 299792458 m/s; conversion factor 931.49402823303 MeV/u.

## Context

This work extends Synge's (1965) relativistic formalism for elastic collisions of particles with intrinsic spin to the fundamentally inelastic reaction T(d,n)⁴He. The results may be compared with quantum calculations by Kulsrud et al. (1982). The analysis assumes zero orbital angular momentum (zero impact parameter) with deuterium and tritium spins anti-aligned, corresponding to the low-energy reaction proceeding via a short-lived intermediate ⁵He resonant state with J=3/2. Future work could relax the zero-impact-parameter constraint to treat the case of aligned spins, which would produce a continuum of possible neutron velocities rather than unique directions.
