# Ground state hyperfine splitting in ^6,7Li atoms and the nuclear structure

> Relativistic and QED corrections were calculated for hyperfine splitting of the $2S_{1/2}$ ground state in $^{6,7}$Li atoms with an exact account for electronic correlations. The resulting theoretical predictions achieved such a precision level that, by comparison with experimental values, they enable determination of the nuclear properties. In particular, the obtained results show that the $^7$Li nucleus, having a charge radius smaller than $^6$Li, has about a 40% larger Zemach radius. Together with known differences in the electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments, this calls for a deeper understanding of the Li nuclear structure.

# Ground state hyperfine splitting in ^6,7Li atoms and the nuclear structure

## Key claim

The theoretical predictions for hyperfine splitting in ^6,7Li atoms achieve few-ppm precision, enabling extraction of nuclear structure effects; the results show that ^7Li, despite having a smaller charge radius than ^6Li, has an approximately 40% larger Zemach radius, which may indicate significant differences in magnetic moment distributions or possibly signal an unknown spin-dependent short-range force between charged hadrons and leptons.

## Method

The authors used the NRQED (nonrelativistic quantum electrodynamics) approach, treating relativistic and QED effects perturbatively in powers of the fine structure constant, with electron correlations accounted for by explicitly correlated basis sets (Hylleraas, Slater, and Gaussian functions). They derived an exact formula for O(α^2) corrections and treated higher orders approximately using hydrogenic results, then compared theoretical hyperfine constants for point nuclei with experimental values to extract the nuclear structure contribution expressed as an effective Zemach radius.

## Key numbers

- For ^7Li: theoretical hyperfine constant for point nucleus A_the = 401.8998(13) MHz; experimental A_exp = 401.7520433(5) MHz; difference (A_exp - A_the)/A_exp = -368(3) ppm
- For ^6Li: theoretical hyperfine constant for point nucleus A_the = 152.1765(5) MHz; experimental A_exp = 152.136839(2) MHz; difference (A_exp - A_the)/A_exp = -261(3) ppm
- Effective Zemach radius for ^7Li: 3.25(3) fm; for ^6Li: 2.30(3) fm
- Charge radius for ^7Li: 2.390(30) fm; for ^6Li: 2.540(28) fm
- Leading coefficient A^(4,0) = 5.81193788(5) (dimensionless, common to both isotopes)
- A^(6) = 70.739(5) (dimensionless, common to both isotopes)

## Context

This work extends high-accuracy hyperfine splitting theory from one-electron systems (H, D, ^3He) to three-electron atoms, building on prior NRQED calculations for Li by Yerokhin (2013) and earlier work on ^3He hyperfine splitting. The results enable determination of nuclear Zemach radii from atomic measurements alone, and the authors note that their nuclear structure contribution for ^6Li disagrees strongly with nuclear calculations performed in Yerokhin (2013), while agreeing for ^7Li. The method can be applied to ^11Be, where a significant neutron halo is expected.
