# Revisiting the Rhoades-Ruffini bound

> We revisit the derivation of the Rhoades-Ruffini bound on the upper limit for the maximum mass of neutron stars and find that the assumption made there for the onset of an ultimately stiff phase of high-density matter is not stringent. Relaxing this assumption and allowing for an onset of stiff non-nucleonic matter under neutron star constraints at the saturation density or below boost the upper limit of the theoretically possible maximum mass to $4~M_\odot$ or higher, in the mass-gap region between neutron stars and stellar-mass black holes. We provide a fit formula for the dependence of this upper limit on the speed of sound and the onset density of the deconfinement transition.

## Key claim

The Rhoades-Ruffini bound on the maximum mass of neutron stars is not stringent because its assumption about the onset density of an ultimately stiff phase of high-density matter can be relaxed. Allowing stiff non-nucleonic matter to appear at or below nuclear saturation density raises the theoretical upper limit to 4 solar masses or higher, placing it in the mass-gap region between neutron stars and stellar-mass black holes.

## Method

The authors revisit the original derivation of the Rhoades-Ruffini bound by examining the assumption that an ultimately stiff phase (with speed of sound equal to the speed of light) sets in only at a high threshold density. They relax this assumption and construct neutron-star models in which the onset of stiff non-nucleonic matter (e.g., deconfined quark matter) occurs at or below nuclear saturation density, while still satisfying neutron-star constraints. They then compute the resulting maximum gravitational mass and provide a fit formula that expresses this upper limit as a function of the speed of sound and the onset density of the deconfinement transition.

## Key numbers

* The revised upper limit for the maximum mass of a neutron star is 4 solar masses or higher, placing it in the mass-gap region between neutron stars and stellar-mass black holes.
* The original Rhoades-Ruffini bound, which the authors revisit, is not given a numerical value in the text, but the paper provides a fit formula for the dependence of the new upper limit on the speed of sound and the onset density of the deconfinement transition.

## Context

This work extends the classic derivation by Rhoades and Ruffini (1974) of an upper bound on neutron-star maximum mass, which assumed that an ultimately stiff equation of state sets in only above a certain density. By relaxing that assumption, the authors show that the bound can be significantly higher, potentially reaching into the mass-gap region. This enables future observational searches for compact objects in the mass gap between neutron stars and black holes, and provides a parametric fit that can be used to interpret gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observations of neutron-star mergers.
