# Algebraic Treatment of Collective Excitations in Baryon Spectroscopy

> We present an algebraic U(7) model for baryons which encompasses both single-particle and collective forms of quark dynamics. The mass operator by construction preserves the permutation symmetry between identical quarks. The underlying geometric structure of baryons is discussed in terms of a rigid rotating and vibrating oblate top shape. The model is applied to the mass spectrum of nonstrange baryons.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a U(7) spectrum generating algebra for baryons that can describe both single-particle and collective quark dynamics, with the mass operator constructed to preserve permutation symmetry among identical quarks. The geometric interpretation of the model corresponds to a rigid rotating and vibrating oblate top (equilateral triangular) shape, but the authors note that the analysis is limited to nonstrange baryons and that spin-flavor dependence in the spatial interaction coefficients is not included.

## Method

The authors construct a U(7) spectrum generating algebra by adding a scalar s-boson to the U(6) harmonic oscillator algebra of two relative Jacobi coordinates, with the total boson number N conserved. They classify all S3-invariant one- and two-body interactions in terms of boson creation and annihilation operators, then obtain a geometric interpretation via coherent states that yields an equilateral triangular equilibrium configuration. The mass operator is decomposed into an intrinsic (vibrational) part and a collective (rotational) part, with normal mode analysis performed in the large N limit to identify the fundamental vibrational modes and rotational bands.

## Key numbers

- The vibrational spectrum in the large N limit gives M²_vib = N[λ₁ n_u + λ₂ (n_v + n_w)], where λ₁ = 4ξ₁R² and λ₂ = 4ξ₂R²/(1+R²)
- The vibrationless ground state (n_u=n_v=n_w=0) is identified with the nucleon N(939)
- The symmetric stretching vibration (n_u=1, n_v+n_w=0) is associated with the Roper resonance N(1440)
- The two-dimensional vibrational mode (n_u=0, n_v+n_w=1) is associated with N(1710)
- The spin-flavor mass operator eigenvalues involve SU(6) Casimir values of 45, 33, and 21 for representations 56, 70, and 20 respectively, and SU(3) flavor Casimir eigenvalues of 9 and 18 for octet and decuplet
- The rotational spectrum includes terms κ₃ L(L+1) + κ₄ K²_y from the SO(3) and SO(2) Casimir operators

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach of Iachello (1991) from mesons (U(4) model) to baryons, and provides an alternative to quark potential models such as those of Isgur and Karl (1978) and Capstick and Isgur (1986) that use harmonic oscillator bases with only a few shells. The U(7) model enables exact diagonalization within a finite-dimensional space while mixing many oscillator shells, analogous to how algebraic models in nuclear physics (IBM) and molecular physics (vibron model) describe collective motion. The same U(7) algebra can also describe rotations and vibrations in triatomic molecules (Bijker, Dieperink, and Leviatan).
