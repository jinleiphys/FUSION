# Efimov physics from the functional renormalization group

> Few-body physics related to the Efimov effect is discussed using the functional renormalization group method. After a short review of renormalization in its modern formulation we apply this formalism to the description of scattering and bound states in few-body systems of identical bosons and distinguishable fermions with two and three components. The Efimov effect leads to a limit cycle in the renormalization group flow. Recently measured three-body loss rates in an ultracold Fermi gas $^6$Li atoms are explained within this framework. We also discuss briefly the relation to the many-body physics of the BCS-BEC crossover for two-component fermions and the formation of a trion phase for the case of three species.

## Key claim

The functional renormalization group (FRG) can describe Efimov physics and few-body bound states, including the limit-cycle scaling of three-body interactions at unitarity, but the authors tune the three-body parameter to zero in the microscopic action for simplicity, meaning that all three-body correlations are generated during the RG evolution rather than being put in by hand.

## Method

The authors apply the Wetterich equation for the scale-dependent effective action to non-relativistic few-body systems of identical bosons and distinguishable fermions (two and three components). They use a vertex expansion truncation, exploiting the hierarchy that n-body flow equations are not influenced by higher-body sectors, and solve the two-body problem analytically with a masslike regulator \(R_\psi = k^2\) to obtain the exact infrared inverse dimer propagator. For three-body systems, they compute the renormalization group flow of the three-body vertex, which exhibits a limit cycle at the unitarity point where the scattering length diverges.

## Key numbers

- For identical bosons, the exact infrared inverse dimer propagator is \(P_{\phi}(Q) = \frac{h^2}{4\pi(3+p)}\left(-a^{-1} + \sqrt{\frac{i q_0}{2} + \frac{\mathbf{q}^2}{4} - \mu_{\psi}}\right)\) with \(p=1\) for bosons, where \(a\) is the s-wave scattering length.
- The s-wave zero-range scattering amplitude is \(f(q) = 1/(-a^{-1} - i q)\), and the scattering length is related to the dimer propagator by \(f(0) = -a = \frac{1}{16\pi} \frac{h^2}{P^{\text{IR}}_\phi(0,0,\mu_\psi=0)}\).
- The three-body loss rates measured in ultracold Fermi gas of \(^6\)Li atoms are explained within this framework (no explicit numerical loss-rate values given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group approach, previously applied to many-body BCS-BEC crossover physics (Diehl 2005, Diehl, Krahl, and Scherer), to few-body Efimov physics. It provides a unified field-theoretic framework that can describe both few-body bound states and many-body phenomena such as the BCS-BEC crossover for two-component fermions and the formation of a trion phase for three species. The method enables future studies connecting few-body Efimov physics to many-body systems, including the explanation of three-body loss rates measured in experiments with \(^6\)Li atoms (Ottenstein 2008, Huckans 2009).
