# Relativistic approach to nuclear spin-isospin excitations including quasiparticle-vibration coupling

> The spin-isospin response of stable and exotic nuclei is investigated in the framework of the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (pn-RQTBA). Based on the Covariant Density Functional Theory, this method extends the proton-neutron Relativistic Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (pn-RQRPA) by including the coupling between single quasiparticles and collective nuclear vibrations. In the charge-exchange channel, this coupling generates a time-dependent effective interaction between proton and neutron quasiparticles. The particle-hole component of this interaction adds to the static pion and rho-meson exchange, while the particle-particle component provides a microscopic and consistent proton-neutron pairing interaction. We find that such dynamical effects induce fragmentation and spreading of the Gamow-Teller transition strength which are important for a better agreement with the experimental measurements and for an accurate description of $β$-decay rates. The new developments include the coupling of single nucleons to isospin-flip vibrations in doubly-magic nuclei. We find that these phonons can have a non-negligible effect on $β$-decay half-lives and "quenching" of the strength.

## Key claim

The inclusion of quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) within the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (pn-RQTBA) systematically induces fragmentation and spreading of the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength, leading to better agreement with experimental data than the simpler proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-RQRPA); however, the framework is often not sufficient to reproduce very fine details of the strength when experimental resolution is high.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) ground state with the NL3 meson parametrization and a monopole-monopole pairing force. They then calculate a set of phonons (collective vibrations) in the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) and couple these phonons to the quasiparticles via the time-blocking approximation (TBA). The resulting Bethe-Salpeter equation for the proton-neutron quasiparticle-quasihole propagator is solved to obtain the GT response, with the effective interaction consisting of static pion and rho-meson exchange plus a dynamical QVC amplitude that introduces 1 quasiparticle-1 quasihole⊗phonon configurations.

## Key numbers

- In ⁶⁸Ni, the first 1⁺ state is found at 330 keV in pn-RQTBA versus 3.88 MeV in pn-RQRPA (experimental ground state of ⁶⁸Cu is 1⁺).
- In ⁷⁸Ni, the cumulative GT strength at ~30 MeV reaches 97% in pn-RQRPA; introducing QVC with isoscalar and isovector phonons (E_win = 30 MeV) leads to a quenching of ~16% of that strength.
- In ⁷⁸Ni, transitions to the Dirac sea of negative-energy states take away about 10% of the total GT⁻ strength.
- In ⁴⁸Ca, around 30 MeV excitation energy, the pn-RQRPA distribution reaches ~98% of the total GT⁻ strength in the particle sector; with QVC (E_win = 100 MeV) it exhausts only ~91% of that value, while the experimental value is only 71%.
- The static meson-exchange interaction uses a Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.6 and a free-space pion coupling constant f_π²/4π = 0.08.

## Context

This work extends the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-RQRPA) by including quasiparticle-vibration coupling, which generates a dynamical proton-neutron pairing interaction without the empirical fitting used in prior pn-(R)QRPA studies by Engel, Mustonen, Bai, and Niksic. The approach is applied to GT transitions in stable and neutron-rich nuclei (⁶⁸Ni, ⁷⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ⁴⁸Ca) and compared to experimental data from Wakasa (2008) and Yako (2009). The new inclusion of isospin-flip (charge-exchange) phonons in doubly-magic nuclei builds on work by Litvinova (2016) on single-nucleon shell structure. Future plans include incorporating higher-order configurations in the response and ground state to further improve the description.
