# Relation between the mass modification of the heavy-light mesons and the chiral symmetry structure in dense matter

> We point out that the study of the density dependences of the masses of heavy-light mesons give some clues to the chiral symmetry structure in nuclear matter. We include the omega meson effect as well as the sigma meson effect at mean field level on the density dependence of the masses of heavy-light mesons with chiral partner structure. It is found that the omega meson affects the masses of the heavy-light mesons and their antiparticles in the opposite way, while it affects the masses of chiral partners in the same way. This is because the omega meson is sensitive to the baryon number of the light degrees included in the heavy-light mesons. We also show that the mass difference between chiral partners is proportional to the mean field of sigma, reflecting the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in the nuclear matter. In addition to the general illustration of the density dependence of the heavy-light meson masses, we consider two concrete models for nuclear matter, the parity doublet model and skyrmion crystal model in the sense of mean field approximation.

## Key claim

The mass difference between chiral partner heavy-light mesons (e.g., the $D$/$D^\ast$ doublet and the $D_0^\ast$/$D_1$ doublet) is proportional to the mean field of the sigma meson, reflecting partial chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter; however, the omega meson contribution splits the masses of particles and antiparticles, so the degenerate mass at high density does not equal the chiral invariant mass (the vacuum average) as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian for heavy-light mesons in the heavy quark limit, including the $H$ doublet ($J^P = 0^-$, $1^-$) and the $G$ doublet ($J^P = 0^+$, $1^+$) as chiral partners. They replace the light meson fields ($\sigma$, $\omega$) by their mean field values in symmetric nuclear matter, using a linear density approximation for $\langle \sigma \rangle$ and $\langle \omega_0 \rangle = (g_{\omega NN}/m_\omega^2) \rho_B$. The effective masses of the $H$ and $G$ doublets (and their antiparticles) are then computed as functions of baryon density. Two concrete nuclear matter models are also considered: the parity doublet model and the skyrmion crystal model, both in mean field approximation.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum masses used are $m_H = 1.97$ GeV (spin average of $D$ and $D^\ast$) and $m_G = 2.40$ GeV (spin average of $D_0^\ast$ and $D_1$), giving $\Delta_M = m_G - m_H = 430$ MeV.
* The omega meson coupling to the heavy-light meson is estimated as $|g_{\omega DD}| = 3.7$, and the omega-nucleon coupling is taken as $|g_{\omega NN}| = 6.23$, yielding $|g_{\omega NN} g_{\omega DD}| = 23$.
* The pion-nucleon sigma term is taken as $\sigma_{\pi N} = 45$ MeV as a reference value, with variations to 30 MeV and 60 MeV explored.
* In the skyrmion crystal model, the $H$ and $G$ doublets become degenerate at a density of approximately $1.2 \rho_0$, where $\rho_0$ is normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy-light meson masses in dense matter (Harada 2003, Suenaga 2014, Sasaki 2014) by including the omega meson effect in addition to the sigma meson effect. Prior analyses (Suenaga 2014) considered only pions and found that the degenerate mass at high density agreed with the chiral invariant mass (the vacuum average). The present paper shows that the omega meson, which couples to baryon number, splits particle and antiparticle masses, so the degenerate mass is shifted. The results are consistent with the earlier finding of Mishra (2003) that the omega meson increases the $D$ meson mass and decreases the $\bar{D}$ meson mass. The work enables experimental tests at J-PARC and FAIR through channels such as $\psi(3770) \to D\bar{D}$ and $J/\psi \to \bar{D} e^+ \nu_e$.
