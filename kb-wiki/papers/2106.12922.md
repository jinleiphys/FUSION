# Electroweak Physics: Summary

> Electroweak probes are potential tool to study the properties of the hot and dense strongly interacting matter produced in relativistic nuclear collisions due to their unique nature. A selection of the new experimental analysis and results from theory calculations on electromagnetic and weak probes presented at the Hard Probes 2020 are discussed in this contribution.

# Electroweak Physics: Summary

## Key claim
Electroweak probes (photons, dileptons, and weak gauge bosons) are efficient tools to study the hot and dense strongly interacting matter produced in relativistic nuclear collisions because they do not suffer strong interaction with the medium and carry undistorted information from their production point to the detector; however, the direct photon puzzle remains an unsolved problem where theoretical model calculations that successfully explain charged particle spectra and anisotropic flow systematically underpredict the experimental data on photon anisotropic flow.

## Method
The paper summarizes a selection of experimental analyses and theory calculations on electromagnetic and weak probes presented at the Hard Probes 2020 conference. It reviews direct photon measurements from RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) and LHC (ALICE) using decay background subtraction methods such as invariant mass analysis, external conversion, and isolation cuts; dilepton measurements from STAR, ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS in ultra-peripheral and hadronic collisions; and W± and Z boson measurements from ATLAS, CMS, LHCb, and ALICE compared to nuclear PDF calculations and Monte Carlo event generators.

## Key numbers
- Direct photon yield from heavy ion collisions shows excess compared to scaled p+p collisions in the region pT < 4 GeV, considered thermal radiation from the hot and dense medium.
- The universal scaling of low pT photon yield with charged particle multiplicity follows (dNch/dη)^α with α about 1.25, independent of energy, centrality, and system size; the scaling coefficient is close to the value of about 1.2 predicted by Cleymans et al.
- ALICE isolated prompt photons from 7 TeV p+p collisions are available in the range 10 < pT < 60 GeV, in good agreement with JETPHOX NLO pQCD calculations.
- STAR dimuon measurement covers the mass range 3.2 to 10 GeV/c² in Au+Au collisions, with significant enhancement relative to cocktail in the 60–80% centrality bin.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section σNN at 5.02A TeV is estimated to be about 41.5 mb (down from 71 mb) when nuclear shadowing is included, affecting nuclear modification factor calculations.
- STAR dielectron data from Au+Au collisions at 27 and 54.4 GeV enhance precision of in-medium rho modification measurements compared to BES-I results.
- ALICE dielectron spectra from Pb+Pb and p+p collisions at 5.02A TeV and soft dielectrons from p+p at 13 TeV with B=0.2 T are reported.

## Context
This summary extends the line of work on electroweak probes in heavy ion collisions reviewed at previous Hard Probes conferences, covering results from PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, CMS, ATLAS, and LHCb experiments. It discusses the direct photon puzzle identified in comparisons between experimental data and hydrodynamic model calculations, and highlights new developments including pre-hydro phase modeling by Paquet et al. (KOMPOST), Bayesian analysis by the JETSCAPE simulation group to constrain transport coefficients, and photon-photon interaction studies in ultra-peripheral collisions. The results enable better constraints on nuclear PDFs, shear and bulk viscosity coefficients, and the initial state of relativistic nuclear collisions, with open questions remaining about the direct photon anisotropic flow puzzle and the high-temperature behavior of η/s and ζ/s.
