# Emergent Kink Statistics at Finite Temperature

> In this paper we use 1D quantum mechanical systems with Higgs-like interaction potential to study the emergence of topological objects at finite temperature. Two different model systems are studied, the standard double-well potential model and a newly introduced discrete kink model. Using Monte-Carlo simulations as well as analytic methods, we demonstrate how kinks become abundant at low temperatures. These results may shed useful insights on how topological phenomena may occur in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that kinks become abundant at low temperatures in 1D quantum mechanical systems with Higgs-like potentials, but this result is conditional on the Euclidean time scale being much larger than the kink size (i.e., \(\sqrt{2}\mu\beta\gg 1\)), which allows well-separated kink configurations to dominate the partition function.

## Method

The authors studied two 1D quantum mechanical systems: the standard double-well potential model and a newly introduced discrete kink model (DKM) defined on a temporal lattice. For the standard model, Monte Carlo Metropolis simulations were performed with cooling procedures to isolate kink content from thermal fluctuations. For the DKM, the partition function was computed analytically by converting the sum over site-index variables into a sum over kink configurations, exploiting the logarithmic form of the potential that allowed exact Gaussian integration.

## Key numbers

- For the standard double-well potential, parameters were fixed to \(\lambda_k=4\) and \(\mu_k/\sqrt{\lambda_k}=1.4\).
- For the DKM, the matching parameters were \(\mu=2\), \(\lambda=2.85\), lattice spacing \(a=0.391\), and \(C=4.105\times10^{-4}\).
- Monte Carlo simulations used \(N_{MC}=10^8\) sweeps for \(\beta=80\) and \(N_{MC}=6\times10^9\) sweeps for \(\beta=0.05\).
- The DKM analytic result for \(\langle x^2\rangle\) is \(1/2\sqrt{2}\mu + \mu^2/\lambda \approx 1.5803\), which is temperature independent.
- The average number of kinks in the DKM is given by \(\langle N_k\rangle = \sqrt{2}\mu\beta \tanh(\sqrt{2}\mu\beta F)\) with \(F = \exp(-\sqrt{2}\mu^3/\lambda)\).
- The kink distribution in the DKM follows \(f(N_k) = (1/\cosh(\xi))(\xi^{N_k}/N_k!)\) with \(\xi = \sqrt{2}\mu\beta F\), approximating a Poisson distribution at low temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical instanton gas approach of Polyakov (1976) by providing an exact analytic treatment of kink statistics in a discrete model, rather than assuming an ensemble of classical solutions. The DKM is a novel model not previously studied, and its analytic results agree well with Monte Carlo simulations of the standard double-well potential. The findings may provide insights into how topological objects such as instantons and calorons emerge in QCD at finite temperature, potentially informing models like the Instanton Liquid Model of Schafer and Shuryak.
