# Updated flexible global parametrization of generalized parton distributions from elastic and deep inelastic inclusive scattering data

> An updated flexible parametrization of the generalized parton distributions in the quark, antiquark and gluon sectors is presented using constraints from high precision electron nucleon deep inelastic scattering data, as well as from the $u$, $d$ quark and gluonic contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic elastic form factors. The latter include recently updated lattice QCD moment calculations. The generalized parton distributions in the vector sector are $H$ and $E$. We rigorously constrain the partonic components, $H_{u_v}$, $H_{d_v}$, $H_{\bar{u}}$, $H_{\bar{d}}$, $H_{\bar{s}}$ and $H_{g}$, and the analogous quantities for $E$, with proper uncertainty quantification. These distributions obey leading order perturbative QCD evolution equations in $α_S$. Parametric forms at the initial scale, $Q_o^2 = 0.58$ $\mathrm{GeV}^2$, for both quarks and gluon distributions are presented as a function of the relevant kinematic variables, namely, the parton momentum fraction, $x$, the skewness, $ξ$, and the invariant, $t$. We also present the Compton form factors entering the deeply virtual Compton scattering process in the kinematic regimes for both fixed target and electron-ion collider settings.

## Key claim
The authors present an updated flexible parametrization of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that is constrained by both deep inelastic scattering data and nucleon electromagnetic elastic form factors including recently updated lattice QCD moment calculations, but the parametrization is limited to leading order perturbative QCD evolution and is defined at a specific initial scale of Q_o^2 = 0.58 GeV^2.

## Method
The authors construct parametric forms for the vector GPDs H and E in the quark (u_v, d_v, anti-u, anti-d, anti-s) and gluon sectors, using a reggeized spectator model as the physics basis. They constrain the parameters using high precision electron-nucleon deep inelastic scattering data for the forward limit and using u, d quark and gluonic contributions to nucleon electromagnetic elastic form factors from updated lattice QCD moment calculations. The GPDs obey leading order perturbative QCD evolution equations in alpha_S, and the authors provide fully analytic forms at the initial scale Q_o^2 = 0.58 GeV^2 as functions of the parton momentum fraction x, skewness xi, and invariant t.

## Key numbers
- Initial scale for PQCD evolution: Q_o^2 = 0.58 GeV^2
- Three mass parameters and two Regge phenomenology-based parameters per flavor are defined in the forward limit for both H and E
- Elastic form factors constrain two extra parameters, consistently with the dipole parametrization
- The parametrization is named UVA2, superseding formulations from Kriesten (2021) and earlier works

## Context
This work extends the flexible GPD parametrization framework from Kriesten (2019) and Kriesten (2021) by providing more stringent constraints on antiquark and gluon distributions using updated lattice QCD form factor values evaluated at a pion mass value closer to the physical one, and by including an entirely new antiquark contribution for the GPD E. The parametrization enables realistic estimates across a wide kinematic regime from fixed target scenarios (dominated by valence structure) to the electron ion collider (EIC) regime (sensitive to antiquark and gluon components), and provides analytic forms that can be used for event generators and Monte Carlo simulations.
