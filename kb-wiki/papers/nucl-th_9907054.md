# Few-nucleon systems in translationally invariant harmonic oscillator basis

> We present a translationally invariant formulation of the no-core shell model approach for few-nucleon systems. We discuss a general method of antisymmetrization of the harmonic-oscillator basis depending on Jacobi coordinates. The use of a translationally invariant basis allows us to employ larger model spaces than in traditional shell-model calculations. Moreover, in addition to two-body effective interactions, three- or higher-body effective interactions as well as real three-body interactions can be utilized. In the present study we apply the formalism to solve three and four nucleon systems interacting by the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. Results of ground-state as well as excited-state energies, rms radii and magnetic moments are discussed. In addition, we compare charge form factor results obtained using the CD-Bonn and Argonne V8' NN potentials.

## Key claim

The paper presents a translationally invariant formulation of the no-core shell model that, by omitting center-of-mass degrees of freedom and using a coupled basis with good total angular momentum and isospin, allows larger model spaces than traditional shell-model calculations; the authors note that the practical applicability of the formalism is limited to light nuclei (A ≤ 6) because the antisymmetrization procedure is computationally involved.

## Method

The authors construct antisymmetrized harmonic-oscillator basis states depending on Jacobi coordinates for A-nucleon systems, using an iterative formula that builds the antisymmetrized basis for A nucleons from that for A-1 nucleons. They derive two-body and three-body effective interactions via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method, solving the two-nucleon and three-nucleon Hamiltonians in large model spaces to construct the transformation operator ω and then the Hermitian effective Hamiltonian. The formalism is applied to solve three- and four-nucleon systems interacting by the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, with calculations performed in model spaces including up to 34ħΩ and 16ħΩ HO excitations, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Three-nucleon system: model spaces include up to 34ħΩ HO excitations above the unperturbed ground state
- Four-nucleon system: model spaces include up to 16ħΩ HO excitations above the unperturbed ground state
- Two-nucleon Hamiltonian solved for all two-nucleon channels up to j=6
- Three-body effective interaction constructed for three-nucleon channels with J₃=1/2±, 3/2± and T₃=1/2
- Three-nucleon solutions obtained using two-body effective interactions corresponding to a large space characterized by N₃ₘₐₓ ≈ 30

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell-model approach previously applied by the authors to three- and four-nucleon systems (Navratil and Barrett, 1998; Navratil, Barrett, and Kamuntavicius, 1999) by simplifying and generalizing the translationally invariant formulation to be applicable to an arbitrary number of nucleons. The approach enables larger model spaces than traditional shell-model calculations and allows the use of three- or higher-body effective interactions as well as real three-body interactions, with the expectation of significant improvement over traditional shell-model results for A ≤ 6. The method connects to the unitary-model-operator approach of Suzuki and Okamoto and provides a framework for future calculations on A=5 and A=6 systems.
