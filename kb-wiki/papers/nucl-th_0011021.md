# Probing meson spectral functions with double differential dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions

> The double differential dilepton spectrum $dN/ (dM_\perp^2 dM^2)$ at fixed transverse mass $M_\perp$ allows a direct access to the vector meson spectral functions. Within a fireball model the sensitivity of $dN/ (dM_\perp^2 dM^2)$ against variations of both the in-medium properties of mesons and the dynamics of the fireball is investigated. In contrast to the integrated invariant-mass spectrum $dN/dM^2$, in the spectrum $dN/dM^2 dM_\perp^2$ with fixed $M_\perp$ the $ω$ signal is clearly seen as bump riding on the $ρ$ background even in case of strong in-medium modifications.

## Key claim

The double differential dilepton spectrum \(dN/(dM_\perp^2 dM^2)\) at a fixed transverse mass \(M_\perp\) provides a direct access to vector meson spectral functions, because fixing \(M_\perp\) freezes the thermal Boltzmann factor and reveals the spectral shape; this is in contrast to the integrated invariant-mass spectrum \(dN/dM^2\), where the steep thermal weighting can mask in-medium modifications even when strong broadening and mass shifts are present.

## Method

The authors employ a fireball (blast-wave) model with constant radial expansion velocity \(v_r = 0.3\), initial temperature \(T_i = 90\) MeV, freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 50\) MeV, initial baryon density \(n_i = 3 n_0\) (\(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)), and freeze-out density \(n_f = 0.3 n_0\). The dilepton rate from vector meson decays (\(V \to e^+ e^-\)) is parameterized with a Breit-Wigner spectral function, and the space-time evolution is integrated to produce both the integrated invariant-mass spectrum \(dN/dM^2\) and the double differential spectrum \(dN/(dM_\perp^2 dM^2)\) at fixed \(M_\perp\). In-medium effects are simulated by varying the meson masses and total widths (e.g., a broadening factor \(b = 3\) or density-dependent shifts), and background contributions from Dalitz decays and post-freeze-out decays are also estimated.

## Key numbers

*   Initial temperature \(T_i = 90\) MeV and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 50\) MeV, derived from an isentropic extrapolation from freeze-out baryon density \(n_f = 0.3 n_0\) back to initial density \(n_i = 3 n_0\).
*   Nuclear saturation density \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
*   Radial flow velocity \(v_r = 0.3\) (constant).
*   Baryon participant number \(N = 330\).
*   For an extreme test case, a \(\rho\) meson mass shift \(\Delta m_\rho = 300\) MeV (downward) and a width broadening factor \(b = 3\) are used; the \(\omega\) mass is kept unchanged (\(\Delta m_\omega = 0\)).
*   For a more realistic parameterization, the \(\omega\) mass shift is \(m_\omega^* = m_\omega (1 - 0.08 \cdot n/n_0)\) and its width broadening is \(\delta \Gamma_\omega = 20\) MeV; the \(\rho\) meson has only a broadening \(\delta \Gamma_\rho = 400\) MeV.
*   The double differential spectrum is shown at fixed \(M_\perp = 0.9\) GeV and \(M_\perp = 1.3\) GeV.
*   The BUU transport calculation selects the interval 850 MeV \(< M_\perp <\) 950 MeV and finds the \(\pi^+ \pi^- \to \rho \to e^+ e^-\) channel dominates in the interval 300 MeV \(< M <\) 850 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard analysis of dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, which previously focused on the integrated invariant-mass spectrum \(dN/dM^2\) (e.g., Rapp and Wambach (1999)), by proposing the double differential spectrum \(dN/(dM_\perp^2 dM^2)\) as a cleaner probe of in-medium vector meson spectral functions. The fireball model is a schematic first step; the authors note that their results should be confirmed by more realistic transport calculations, such as the BUU code of Gyuri (1999) and Wolf (2000). The approach is motivated by experiments at the HADES detector at SIS energies, where densities up to \(3 n_0\) are expected, and aims to disentangle effects like the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) and QCD sum-rule predictions (Hatsuda, 1993) from dynamical smearing due to fireball expansion and cooling.
