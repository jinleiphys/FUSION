# Equilibration in Quark Gluon Plasma

> The hydrodynamic expansion rate of quark gluon plasma (QGP) is evaluated and compared with the scattering rate of quarks and gluons within the system. Partonic scattering rates evaluated within the ambit of perturbative Quantum Choromodynamics (pQCD) are found to be smaller than the expansion rate evaluated with ideal equation of state (EoS) for the QGP. This indicate that during the space-time evolution the system remains out of equilibrium. Enhancement of pQCD cross sections and a more realistic EoS keep the partons closer to the equilibrium.

# Equilibration in Quark Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic expansion rate of QGP is compared with partonic scattering rates; with pQCD cross sections and an ideal equation of state, the scattering rates are smaller than the expansion rate, indicating the system remains out of equilibrium during space-time evolution. However, enhancement of pQCD cross sections by a factor of 2 and use of a more realistic lattice QCD equation of state can keep partons closer to equilibrium.

## Method

The authors solve the hydrodynamic expansion equations for QGP assuming boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry, using initial conditions appropriate for RHIC and LHC energies. They compute scattering time scales for partons (gluons, light quarks, and heavy quarks) using leading-order pQCD cross sections with Debye mass screening, and compare these scattering time scales to the hydrodynamic expansion time scale to assess whether the condition for maintaining thermal equilibrium is satisfied.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature for RHIC: T_i = 400 MeV, initial proper time tau_i = 0.2 fm, giving dN/dy ~ 1100
- Initial temperature for LHC: T_i = 700 MeV, initial proper time tau_i = 0.08 fm, giving dN/dy = 2100
- pQCD cross sections are enhanced by a factor of 2 to study non-perturbative effects on equilibration
- The condition for maintaining equilibrium is tau_exp >= alpha * tau_scatt, where alpha ~ O(1)

## Context

This work extends the study of thermalization in heavy ion collisions by comparing scattering and expansion time scales, following the approach used by Kolb and Turner (1990) for freeze-out in the early universe and by Navarra (1995) for hadron freeze-out in heavy ion collisions. The results support arguments by Kovchegov (2001) that thermalization cannot be achieved without non-perturbative effects, and by Molnar (2002) that large enhancement of pQCD cross sections is needed to reproduce elliptic flow data at RHIC. The findings enable further investigation of how non-perturbative interactions and realistic lattice QCD equations of state affect equilibration dynamics at both RHIC and LHC energies.
