# Momentum transfer dependence of the proton's electric and magnetic polarizabilities

> The Q^2-dependence of the sum of the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the proton is calculated over the range 0 \leq Q^2 \leq 6 GeV^2 using the generalized Baldin sum rule. Employing a parametrization of the F_1 structure function valid down to Q^2 = 0.06 GeV^2, the polarizabilities at the real photon point are found by extrapolating the results of finite Q^2 to Q^2 = 0 GeV^2. We determine the evolution over four-momentum transfer to be consistent with the Baldin sum rule using photoproduction data, obtaining α+ β= 13.7 \pm 0.7 \times 10^{-4}\, \text{fm}^3.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the \(Q^2\)-dependence of the sum of the proton's electric and magnetic polarizabilities using the generalized Baldin sum rule, finding that the evolution is consistent with the real-photon Baldin sum rule when extrapolated to \(Q^2 = 0\) GeV\(^2\), but only after employing a structure function parametrization valid down to \(Q^2 = 0.06\) GeV\(^2\) and extrapolating the results, because the parametrization fails to match the real photon point directly as \(Q^2 \to 0\).

## Method

The authors evaluate the generalized Baldin sum rule for the proton over \(0.06 \leq Q^2 \leq 6\) GeV\(^2\) using the Adelaide-Jefferson Lab-Manitoba (AJM) parametrization of the \(F_1\) structure function, which combines a Christy-Bosted fit (valid down to \(Q^2 = 0.06\) GeV\(^2\)) at low \(W^2\), a Regge-plus-vector-meson-dominance parametrization at high \(W^2\), and parton distribution functions at high \(Q^2\) and \(W^2\). To obtain the polarizability sum at the real photon point, they fit the calculated \(\alpha(Q^2)+\beta(Q^2)\) results over four different low-\(Q^2\) ranges (e.g., \(0.04<Q^2<0.10\) GeV\(^2\)) and extrapolate to \(Q^2 = 0\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

* \(\alpha + \beta = (13.7 \pm 0.7) \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\) at the real photon point (\(Q^2 = 0\) GeV\(^2\)), obtained from extrapolation over the range \(0.06<Q^2<0.10\) GeV\(^2\).
* The root-mean-square radius of the polarizability sum is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{\alpha+\beta}^{1/2} = 0.98 \pm 0.05\,\text{fm}\).
* The calculation covers the range \(0.06 \leq Q^2 \leq 6\) GeV\(^2\).
* The result at the real photon point agrees with Babusci (1998) (\(\alpha+\beta = 13.69 \pm 0.14 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)) and Olmos de Leon (2001) (\(\alpha+\beta = 13.8 \pm 0.4 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Sibirtsev and Blunden (2013), who used a different parametrization of \(F_1\) and obtained a \(Q^2\)-dependence that would overshoot the real-photon value upon extrapolation; the present AJM parametrization resolves this discrepancy by being consistent to much lower \(Q^2\) (0.06 GeV\(^2\)). The results enable more precise modeling of the spatial distribution of polarizabilities inside the proton and can be used to constrain future low-\(Q^2\) electron scattering experiments, though the authors note that increased data at very low \(Q^2\) and low \(W^2\) would further reduce uncertainties.
