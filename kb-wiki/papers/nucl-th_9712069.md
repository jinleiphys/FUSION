# Intermediate mass excess of dilepton production in heavy ion collisions at BEVALAC energies

> Dielectron mass spectra are examined for various nuclear reactions recently measured by the DLS collaboration. A detailed description is given of all dilepton channels included in the transport model UrQMD 1.0, i.e. Dalitz decays of $π^0,η,ω,η'$ mesons and of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance, direct decays of vector mesons and $pn$ bremsstrahlung. The microscopic calculations reproduce data for light systems fairly well, but tend to underestimate the data in $pp$ at high energies and in $pd$ at low energies. These conventional sources, however, cannot explain the recently reported enhancement for nucleus-nucleus collisions in the mass region 0.15 GeV<$M_{ee}$<0.6 GeV. Chiral scaling and $ω$ meson broadening in the medium are investigated as a source of this mass excess. They also cannot explain the recent DLS data.

## Key claim

The conventional dilepton sources implemented in the UrQMD 1.0 transport model cannot explain the enhancement observed by the DLS collaboration in nucleus-nucleus collisions for dielectron invariant masses between 0.15 GeV and 0.6 GeV; furthermore, neither chiral scaling of vector meson masses nor broadening of the omega meson decay width in the medium can account for this excess.

## Method

The authors implemented all relevant dilepton production channels, Dalitz decays of π⁰, η, ω, η' mesons and the Δ(1232) resonance, direct decays of vector mesons (ρ⁰, ω, φ), and pn bremsstrahlung, into the UrQMD 1.0 microscopic n-body transport model. They calculated dielectron mass spectra for pp, pd, and nucleus-nucleus collisions (C+C, Ca+Ca, He-Ca) at BEVALAC beam kinetic energies from 1.04 to 4.9 GeV, applying the DLS acceptance filter (V4.1) and folding with the experimental mass resolution of ΔM/M ≈ 0.1. They then tested two in-medium modification scenarios: a linear scaling law for vector meson masses (m*_V = m_V (1 − 0.18 ρ/ρ₀)) and an enhancement of the ω → ππ decay width by a factor of 500 to approximately 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at T = 1.04 GeV, the model reproduces data at low masses but underestimates them around M_ee = 0.4 GeV.
- For pd collisions at T = 1.27 GeV, the model predicts a dilepton ratio R = 5.2 (integrated from 0.15 GeV to M_max), lower than the DLS data.
- The ratio R_η = σ(pd → η X) / σ(pp → η X) is calculated to be 17 ± 3 at T = 1.27 GeV, dropping to between 2.8 and 2.3 above T = 1.61 GeV.
- To reproduce the nucleus-nucleus data by artificially enhancing the η yield, a factor f_η ≈ 10 is needed for C+C and ≈ 20 for Ca+Ca, corresponding to an η cross section of about 120 mb for Ca+Ca at 1 GeV, while TAPS data give about 20 mb.
- The ω → ππ decay width was increased by a factor of 500 to approximately 100 MeV, but this had almost no effect on the total dilepton yield.
- The vector meson mass scaling law used is m*_V = m_V (1 − 0.18 ρ/ρ₀), where ρ₀ is the ground state density.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport model calculations by Wolf et al. (1990), Xiong et al. (1990), and Bratkovskaya et al. (1996, 1997) that also found discrepancies with the earlier DLS measurements. The paper directly compares with the new DLS data published by Porter et al. (1997) and Wilson et al. (1997), and tests the in-medium modification scenarios proposed by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) for dropping vector meson masses and by Rapp et al. (1997) and Cassing et al. (1997) for dissolving spectral functions. The results show that these in-medium effects cannot explain the DLS enhancement, leaving the puzzle open for future experiments such as HADES at GSI and PHENIX at RHIC.
