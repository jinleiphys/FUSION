# Nuclear physics input for solar models

> We discuss microscopic cluster model descriptions of two solar nuclear reactions, Be7(p,gamma)B8 and He3(He3,2p)He4. The low-energy reaction cross section of Be7(p,gamma)B8, which determines the high-energy solar neutrino flux, is constrained by Be7 and B8 observables. Our results show that a small value of the zero-energy cross section is rather unlikely. In He3(He3,2p)He4 we study the effects of a possible virtual state on the cross section. Although, we have found no indication for such a state so far, its existence cannot be ruled out yet. We calculate the He3(He3,2p)He4 and H3(H3,2n)He4 cross sections in a continuum-discretized coupled channel approximation, and find a good general agreement with the data.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a small value of the zero-energy astrophysical S-factor for Be7(p,gamma)B8 is rather unlikely based on microscopic cluster model constraints from Be7 and B8 observables, but this conclusion depends on the specific effective nucleon-nucleon interaction used and the model space employed.

## Method
The author uses a microscopic eight-body three-cluster model (He4+He3+p) for Be7(p,gamma)B8 and a six-body cluster model (He3+He3, He4+p+p) for He3(He3,2p)He4. Scattering states are treated with the Kohn-Hulthén variational method and bound states with the Siegert variational method, with cross sections expressed as astrophysical S-factors to remove the exponential Coulomb barrier energy dependence.

## Key numbers
- For Be7(p,gamma)B8: the model predicts S17(0) = 25-26.5 eVb, compared to the weighted average of experimental measurements at 22.4 ± 2.1 eVb (Johnson) and a Coulomb dissociation result of 16.7 ± 3.2 eVb (Motobayashi)
- The experimental point-nucleon radius of B8 is 2.50 ± 0.04 fm, giving r^2(B8) - r^2(Be7) ≈ 0.9 fm^2
- The Be7 quadrupole moment Q7 is predicted by the model to lie between -6 e fm^2 and -7 e fm^2
- For He3(He3,2p)He4: the electron screening potential extracted from data is U = 323 eV, and a hypothetical virtual state with strength artificially set to 5 keV is shown for comparison

## Context
This work extends microscopic cluster model calculations of solar fusion reactions, addressing the long-standing uncertainty in the Be7(p,gamma)B8 S-factor that directly affects high-energy solar neutrino flux predictions (Bahcall). The results are slightly higher than the weighted average from direct capture measurements (Johnson) and the Coulomb dissociation result (Motobayashi), and the paper emphasizes the need for further experimental measurements of the Be7 quadrupole moment and precise extraction of the Be7 radius. For He3(He3,2p)He4, the study investigates the possibility of a low-energy resonance or virtual state that could affect solar neutrino fluxes (Fowler) and examines the electron screening puzzle from the LUNA experiment, finding no evidence for such states so far.
