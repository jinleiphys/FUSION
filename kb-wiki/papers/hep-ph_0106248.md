# Instantons in the Saturation Environment

> We show that instanton calculations in QCD become theoretically well defined in the gluon saturation environment which suppresses large size instantons. The effective cutoff scale is determined by the inverse of the saturation scale. We concentrate on two most important cases: the small-x tail of a gluon distribution of a high energy hadron or a large nucleus and the central rapidity region in a high energy hadronic or heavy ion collision. In the saturation regime the gluon density in a single large ultrarelativistic nucleus is high and gluonic fields are given by the classical solutions of the equations of motion. We show that these strong classical fields do not affect the density of instantons in the nuclear wave function compared to the instanton density in the vacuum. A classical solution with non-trivial topological charge is found for the gluon field of a single nucleus at the lowest order in the instanton perturbation theory. In the case of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions a strong classical gluonic field is produced in the central rapidity region. We demonstrate that this field introduces a suppression factor of exp{-c ρ^4 Q_s^4 / [8 α^2 N_c (Q_s τ)^2]} in the instanton size distribution, where Q_s is the saturation scale of both (identical) nuclei, τis the proper time and c = 1 is the gluon liberation coefficient. This factor suggests that gluonic saturation effects at the early stages of nuclear collisions regulate the instanton size distribution in the infrared region and make the instanton density finite by suppressing large size instantons.

## Key claim

Instanton calculations in QCD become theoretically well-defined in the gluon saturation environment because the saturation scale \(Q_s\) provides an effective infrared cutoff that suppresses large-size instantons, but only in the case of heavy-ion collisions (not for a single nucleus) does the classical gluon field actually modify the instanton size distribution.

## Method

The authors use the effective instanton Lagrangian approach of Shuryak and Verbaarschot (1990) to compute the modification of the instanton size distribution in the presence of a slowly varying background gluon field. For a single ultrarelativistic nucleus, they evaluate the classical non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams field and its one-loop corrections, showing that \(G_{\mu\nu}^{a2}=0\) at all orders in the leading-logarithmic \(\ln 1/x\) resummation. For heavy-ion collisions, they compute the classical gluon field produced by two colliding nuclei at lowest order in perturbation theory and evaluate the matrix element \(\langle G_{\mu\nu}^{a}G_{\mu\nu}^{a}\rangle\) in the nuclear wave function to obtain the exponential suppression factor.

## Key numbers

* The suppression factor for instantons in heavy-ion collisions is \(\exp\left(-c\,\rho^{4}Q_{s}^{4}/[8\alpha_{s}^{2}N_{c}(Q_{s}\tau)^{2}]\right)\), where \(c\approx 1\) is the gluon liberation coefficient, \(Q_{s}\) is the saturation scale, \(\tau\) is the proper time, and \(\rho\) is the instanton size.
* The gluon liberation coefficient has been estimated numerically as \(c=1.29\pm 0.09\) (Krasnitz and Venugopalan, 2000) and analytically as \(c\approx 2\ln 2\) (Kovchegov, 2001), while RHIC data (PHOBOS, 2000) suggest \(c=1.23\pm 0.20\) (Kharzeev and Nardi, 2001).
* Saturation effects at RHIC reduce the instanton density in the central rapidity region by three orders of magnitude compared to the instanton density in vacuum as extracted from lattice data (Ringwald and Schrempp, 1999).

## Context

This work extends the instanton-in-background-field formalism of Shuryak and Verbaarschot (1990) and the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) of gluon saturation to the problem of instantons in high-energy nuclear environments. It contradicts the naive expectation that the saturation scale would act like a Debye screening mass \(m_D\sim Q_s\) to produce a \(\exp(-\text{const}\,\rho^2 Q_s^2)\) suppression for a single nucleus, showing instead that the classical Weizsäcker-Williams field has vanishing \(G_{\mu\nu}^{a2}\). The result enables controlled instanton calculations in the saturation regime of heavy-ion collisions and suggests that instanton-induced effects may be suppressed at RHIC but could become important at higher energies, potentially unitarizing the soft pomeron of Kharzeev, Kovchegov, and Levin (2000).
