# Zero Sound Propagation in Femto-Scale Quantum Liquids

> Charge equilibration has been recognized as a dominant process at the early stage of low-energy heavy-ion reactions. The production of exotic nuclei is suppressed under the appearance of charge equilibration, in which the proton-neutron ratios of the final reaction products are inevitably averaged. Therefore charge equilibration plays one of the most crucial roles in the synthesis of chemical elements. Focusing on how and when the charge equilibration takes place, the zero-sound propagation in femto-scale quantum liquids is explained.

## Key claim

The paper argues that fast charge equilibration in low-energy heavy-ion reactions is realized by zero sound propagation (nucleon propagation without density change), not necessarily by the isovector giant dipole resonance. The author hedges that this interpretation applies only below an upper energy limit determined by the Fermi energy, above which zero sound disappears due to increased nucleon collisions.

## Method

The author reviews Landau's Fermi liquid theory and applies it to heavy-ion collisions, deriving an upper energy limit for charge equilibration from the Fermi velocities of the colliding nucleons. Three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations with a Skyrme interaction (SLy4d) are performed for the collision between ⁵²Ca and ³⁶Ca, visualizing the propagation of neutron-rich flow at different impact parameters and incident energies.

## Key numbers

- The fast charge equilibration time scale is a few 10⁻²² s, compared to the typical reaction time of low-energy heavy-ion collisions (1000 fm/c ∼ 10⁻²⁰ s).
- The propagation speed of charge-equilibrating flow is 0.90 |v_F|, where |v_F| is fixed to 1/3 of the speed of light (the nuclear standard value).
- The relative velocity at contact is 0.36 |v_F| for E/A = 2.0 MeV and 0.23 |v_F| for E/A = 1.0 MeV (speeds given at initial time).
- The Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula coefficients are given as a_vol ∼ −16 MeV, a_surf ∼ 20 MeV, a_coul ∼ 0.751 MeV, and a_sym ∼ 21.4 MeV.
- The upper energy limit formula (Eq. 1) includes the Fermi energy term ℏ²(3π²ρ_min)^(2/3)/(2m) and a Coulomb correction term e²Z₁Z₂/(4πε₀r₀) × (A₁+A₂)/(A₁A₂(A₁^(1/3)+A₂^(1/3))).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of charge equilibration beyond the isovector giant dipole resonance framework, which had been studied by Hill-Wheeler (1950s) and Bonche-Ngo (1970s), and more recently by Simenel-Chomaz-de France. The author builds on their own prior work (Iwata-Otsuka-Maruhn-Itagaki) that first identified the upper energy limit for charge equilibration. The paper enables experimental detection of zero sound in femto-scale quantum liquids by observing whether most final products of heavy-ion reactions are in charge equilibrium, and suggests that the fast charge equilibration mechanism is universal across low-energy heavy-ion reactions below the Fermi energy threshold.
