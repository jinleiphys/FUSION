# Structure of hybrid protoneutron stars within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We investigate the structure of protoneutron stars (PNS) formed by hadronic and quark matter in $β$-equilibrium described by appropriate equations of state (EOS). For the hadronic matter, we use a finite temperature EOS based on the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory, with realistic two- and three-body forces. For the quark sector, we employ the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We find that the maximum allowed masses are comprised in a narrow range around 1.8 solar masses, with a slight dependence on the temperature. Metastable hybrid protoneutron stars are not found.

## Key claim

The maximum masses of hybrid protoneutron stars (PNS) are confined to a narrow range around 1.8 solar masses, with a slight dependence on temperature; however, this result holds only when the hadronic phase contains nucleons alone, because the inclusion of hyperons softens the equation of state enough to prevent any hadron-quark phase transition.

## Method

The authors combined a finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) many-body equation of state for hadronic matter (using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus Urbana three-body forces) with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter, imposing beta equilibrium, charge neutrality, and (where applicable) neutrino trapping with electron lepton number fixed at 0.4. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated via the Maxwell construction, and stellar configurations were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for a hot isothermal core joined to a cold outer crust.

## Key numbers

- Maximum PNS masses lie in a narrow range around 1.8 solar masses, with values from 1.76 to 1.99 solar masses depending on temperature and neutrino trapping (Table I).
- For nucleon-only matter without neutrinos at T=0, the maximum mass is 1.8 solar masses, with radius 10.3 km and central density 6.4 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3).
- For nucleon-only matter with neutrino trapping at T=0, the maximum mass is 1.76 solar masses, with radius 9.4 km and central density 7.82 rho_0.
- At T=30 MeV without neutrinos, the maximum mass is 1.9 solar masses (radius 15 km); with neutrino trapping at T=30 MeV, it is 1.86 solar masses (radius 14.6 km).
- The transition density to quark matter in neutrino-free matter occurs at about 6 times normal nuclear matter saturation density.
- Above core temperatures of about 40 MeV, all stellar configurations become unstable.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of PNS with hyperons (Burgio and Plumari, 2006) and their earlier hybrid PNS calculations using the MIT bag model (Burgio and Plumari, 2006), where maximum masses below 1.6 solar masses were found. The results contrast with Cooke (2005), who found metastable hybrid stars using a much stiffer baryonic equation of state including hyperons. The findings confirm that maximum PNS masses remain below 2 solar masses, compatible with observational data on neutron stars, and rule out metastable hybrid PNS configurations.
