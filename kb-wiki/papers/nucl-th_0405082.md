# Charge Form Factors of Quark-Model Pions II

> Experimental data of the pion charge form factor are well represented by Poincaré invariant constituent-quark phenomenology depending on two parameters, a confinement scale and an effective constituent--quark mass. Pion states are represented by eigenfunctions of mass and spin operators and of the light-front momenta. An effective current density is generated by the dynamics from a null-plane impulse current density. A simple shape of the wave function depending only on the confinement scale is sufficient.

# Charge Form Factors of Quark-Model Pions II

## Key claim
Simple relativistic constituent-quark models with null-plane kinematics can represent all existing pion charge form factor data using only two parameters (a confinement scale and an effective constituent-quark mass), but the specific shape of the radial wave function matters for predictions at higher momentum transfers where future measurements may discriminate between models.

## Method
The authors construct Poincaré-invariant constituent-quark models of the pion using eigenfunctions of mass and spin operators together with light-front momenta. An effective current density is generated dynamically from a null-plane impulse current density, and the pion charge form factor is computed as a functional of the radial wave function. Three different radial wave function shapes are compared: a Gaussian, a rational form, and a wave function generated from a QCD-motivated mass operator.

## Key numbers
- Constituent-quark mass: $m_q = 0.23$ GeV used for all calculations.
- Confinement scale parameters: $b = 0.35$ GeV for the Gaussian wave function and $b = 0.43$ GeV for the rational wave function.
- Pion decay constant values obtained with $g_A^q = 1$: 92.5 MeV (Gaussian), 101.5 MeV (rational), and 101.8 MeV (wave function from mass operator).
- Mass operator parameters for the ground-state solution: $\alpha = 0.1$ GeV$^2$, $\beta = 0.4$, $\gamma = 0.229$ GeV, $\lambda = 0.3$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous letter (Coester, Polyzou, and collaborators, 2004) which demonstrated agreement with data at both low $Q^2$ and $Q^2 > 1$ GeV$^2$. It incorporates new precise measurements by Volmer (2001) for $Q^2 < 2$ GeV$^2$ and provides predictions for higher momentum transfers. The results agree with the QCD-based calculations of Maris and Tandy (2000) for $Q^2 < 2$ GeV$^2$ and with their expectations at larger $Q^2$. The approach contrasts with point-form kinematics (Allen, 2000) which produces form factors too small for realistic representation, and with conventional QCD-motivated mass operators (Isgur, 1985) that require substantial modification of the current (Cardarelli and collaborators, 1995).
