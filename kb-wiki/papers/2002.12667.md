# Fractal structure of Yang-Mills fields

> The origin of non-extensive thermodynamics in physical systems has been under intense debate for the last decades. Recent results indicate a connection between non-extensive statistics and thermofractals. After reviewing this connection, we analyze how scaling properties of Yang-Mills theory allow the appearance of self-similar structures in gauge fields. The presence of such structures, which actually behave as fractals, allows for recurrent non-perturbative calculations of vertices. It is argued that when a statistical approach is used, the non-extensive statistics is obtained, and the Tsallis entropic index, $q$, is deduced in terms of the field theory parameters. The results are applied to QCD in the one-loop approximation, resulting in a good agreement with the value of $q$ obtained experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the scaling properties of Yang-Mills theory, specifically renormalization group invariance, lead to self-similar (fractal) structures in gauge fields, and that a statistical description of these structures naturally yields Tsallis non-extensive statistics with the entropic index \(q\) determined entirely by field theory parameters; the claim is hedged by the one-loop approximation used for the QCD comparison and the asymptotic limit \((q-1)\mu \gg \lambda_o / \varepsilon_i\) required for the beta function derivation.

## Method

The authors first review the connection between Tsallis statistics and thermofractals, showing that self-similar energy distributions in thermofractals obey Tsallis statistics. They then analyze the renormalization group scaling properties of Yang-Mills theory, using the Callan-Symanzik equation to relate vertex functions at different scales. By considering multiparticle production in \(pp\) collisions and introducing a statistical description of the partonic state, they derive a probability distribution for parton energies that exhibits self-similarity. This distribution is identified as the effective coupling in vertex functions, and from it the beta function is computed in the one-loop approximation and compared to the QCD beta function.

## Key numbers

* Entropic index from experimental fits to hadron abundances in \(pp\) collisions: \(q = 1.14 \pm 0.01\).
* From the one-loop QCD beta function with \(N_c = 3\) and \(N_f = 6\) (so \(N_f/2 = 3\)), the combination \(\frac{11}{3}c_1 - \frac{4}{3}c_2 = 7\), leading to \(1/(q-1) = 7\) and thus \(q = 1.14\).
* The beta function derived in the paper in the asymptotic limit is \(\beta_{\bar{g}} = -\frac{1}{16\pi^2} \frac{1}{q-1} \bar{g}^{\tilde{N}+1}\) with \(\tilde{N}=2\).

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn self-consistent thermodynamic approach to high-energy collisions (Hagedorn, 1965) by replacing the exponential Boltzmann-Gibbs distribution with a power-law Tsallis distribution, motivated by experimental data. It builds on prior studies connecting non-extensive statistics to thermofractals (Deppman, 2016; Deppman, 2017) and provides a field-theoretic derivation of the Tsallis entropic index \(q\) from QCD parameters, enabling a first-principles justification for using non-extensive thermodynamics in strongly interacting systems such as the quark-gluon plasma and hadron physics.
