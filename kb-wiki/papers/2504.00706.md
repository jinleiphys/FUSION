# Lepton-proton two-photon exchange with improved soft-photon approximation including proton's structure effects in HB$χ$PT

> We present an improved evaluation of the two-photon exchange correction to the unpolarized lepton-proton elastic scattering process at very low-energies relevant to the MUSE experiment, where only the dominant intermediate elastic proton is considered. We employ the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and invoke the soft-photon approximation in order to reduce the intricate 4-point loop-integrals into simpler 3-point loop-integrals. In the present work, we adopt a more robust methodology compared to an earlier work along the same lines for analytically evaluating the loop-integrations, and incorporate important corrections at next-to-next-to-leading order. These include the proton's structure effects which renormalize the proton-photon interaction vertices and the proton's propagator. Finally, our results allow a model-independent estimation of the charge asymmetry for the scattering of unpolarized massive leptons and anti-leptons.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved evaluation of the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to unpolarized lepton-proton elastic scattering at very low energies, relevant to the MUSE experiment, but only considers the dominant intermediate elastic proton state and employs the soft-photon approximation (SPA) to reduce 4-point loop integrals to simpler 3-point forms, meaning the results are not exact but rely on this approximation and the neglect of inelastic intermediate states.

## Method

The authors employed heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) to compute the TPE amplitudes, using the soft-photon approximation (SPA) to simplify the 4-point loop integrals into 3-point loop integrals. They adopted a more robust methodology than earlier work by Talukdar et al. (2019), including a proper treatment of the \(i\epsilon\) prescription in propagators and an improved infrared (IR) subtraction scheme using dimensional regularization with a \(Q^2\)-independent subtraction proportional to \(\ln(m_l^2)\). The proton's structure effects, including its radius and anomalous magnetic moment, were incorporated at one-loop accuracy through renormalization of the proton-photon vertices and the proton propagator.

## Key numbers

* The LO TPE contribution from the exact evaluation in HBChPT is \(\delta^{\rm (0)}_{\gamma \gamma}(Q^2) = \pi \alpha \frac{\sqrt{-Q^2}}{2E}\left(\frac{1}{1+\frac{Q^2}{4E^2}}\right)\), analogous to the McKinley-Feshbach contribution.
* The IR-divergent term from the LO box diagrams is \(\delta^{\rm (box)}_{\rm IR}(Q^2) = \frac{\alpha}{\pi\beta}\left[\frac{1}{\epsilon}-\gamma_E+\ln\bigg(\frac{4\pi \mu^2}{m_l^2}\bigg) \right]\left\{\ln \sqrt{\frac{1+\beta}{1-\beta}}-\frac{\beta}{\beta^\prime}\ln \sqrt{\frac{1+\beta^\prime}{1-\beta^\prime}}\right\}\), where \(\epsilon=(4-D)/2<0\).

## Context

This work extends the SPA-based TPE analysis of Talukdar et al. (2019, 2020) by including NNLO corrections that incorporate proton structure effects (radius and anomalous magnetic moment) and by using a more rigorous loop-integration method with an improved IR-subtraction scheme. It also builds upon the exact TPE evaluation in HBChPT by Choudhary et al. (2023), whose LO result is adopted to restore correct power counting. The results enable a model-independent estimation of the charge asymmetry for unpolarized massive lepton and anti-lepton scattering at low energies relevant to the MUSE experiment at PSI.
