# Negative R-parity scissors modes in the Two-Rotors Model

> In a previous work I constructed R-negative scissors states in the Two-Rotors Model in which the rotors are two-sided classical bodies. Such states have |K| = 1; 0 components and negative space parity. Here I extend this work including reflections in a plane through the rotors symmetry axes obtaining also R-negative states of positive parity. I then associate reflections and R-operations with corresponding operations on internal noncollective variables of the rotors. I evaluate the em transition strengths of these states and compare them with the corresponding strengths of R-positive scissors modes.

# Negative R-parity scissors modes in the Two-Rotors Model

## Key claim

The paper extends the Two-Rotors Model to include negative R-parity scissors states with both negative and positive space parity, by treating the rotors as quantal objects with respect to their internal noncollective position variables rather than fully classical bodies. The negative parity scissors mode can decay to the ground state by an electric dipole transition, which is a purely quantal effect because roto-oscillations of classical rotors cannot produce any electric moment.

## Method

The author constructs R-negative scissors states in the Two-Rotors Model by allowing each rotor to exist in positive or negative R-parity states, and by including reflections in a plane through the rotors' symmetry axes. The wave functions are expressed as products of collective angular functions F^1_{MK}, intrinsic functions χ_K(θ) depending on the angle between rotor axes, and functions f^π of internal noncollective variables. The electromagnetic transition strengths are evaluated by computing matrix elements of the magnetic and electric multipole operators, using the approximation that only the R-negative rotor contributes to the transition matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The electric dipole strength for decay of the 1^-_sc state to the ground state: B(E1; 1_sc^- → 0^+) = (1/12) (Q_{pδ} θ_0)^2
- The electric dipole strength for decay to the 2^+ member of the ground state rotational band: B(E1; 1_sc^- → 2^+) = (1/24) (Q_{pδ} θ_0)^2
- The magnetic dipole strength for decay of the 1^+_sc,i state to the ground state: B(M1; 1^+_sc,i → 0^+) = (1/(16 π^3)) (1/θ_0^2) (e g_i / (2m))^2
- The branching ratio for decay of the 1^+_sc,i state to the 2^+ versus ground state: B(M1; 1^+_sc,i → 2^+) / B(M1; 1^+_sc,i → 0^+) = 1/2 (neglecting the small term H_1)
- The branching ratio including the small term H_1: B(M1; 1^+_sc,i → 2^+) ≈ (1/2) (1 - r_i α) B(M1; 1^+_sc,i → 0^+), where α = (5/2) √π ((J_2 - J_1)/(J_1 + J_2)) θ_0
- The electric quadrupole decay strength: B(E2; 1^+_sc,i → 2^+) = (1/4) θ_0^2 Q_{i2}^2, where Q_{i2} = -(1/π) √(3/(10π)) e_i N_i R^2
- The magnetic dipole strength for the R-positive scissors mode: B(M1; 1^+, R=+1 → 0^+) = (1/(16 π)) (1/θ_0^2) (e (g_1 - g_2) / (2m))^2

## Context

This work extends the author's previous construction of R-negative scissors states in the Two-Rotors Model (Palumbo, 2020), which assumed rotors were even under reflection σ and produced only negative parity states. The present paper allows rotors to be odd under reflection σ, yielding both negative and positive parity R-negative states. The work is motivated by recent experimental observations by Beck and collaborators (2021) of a 1^- state in ¹⁶⁴Dy at the energy of the scissors mode and a 1^+ doublet with |K|=0,1 components of comparable amplitude. The results enable comparison with these experimental findings, though the author notes that quantitative agreement would require the parameter α to be about 0.3 while current values of θ_0 give α < 0.1. The paper also provides branching ratios that differ from the Alaga rules due to K mixing in the 2^+ wave function.
