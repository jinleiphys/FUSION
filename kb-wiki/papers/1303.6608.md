# Consequences of Heavy Quark Symmetries for Hadronic Molecules

> Among the newly observed structures in the heavy quarkonium mass region, some are proposed to be hadronic molecules. We investigate the consequences of heavy quark flavor symmetry on these heavy meson hadronic molecules. The symmetry allows us to predict new hadronic molecules on one hand, and test the hadronic molecular assumption of the observed structures on the other hand. We explore the consequences of the flavor symmetry assuming the X(3872) and $Z_b(10610)$ as a isoscalar $D\bar D^*$ and isovector $B\bar B^*$ hadronic molecule, respectively. A series of hadronic molecules composed of heavy mesons are predicted. In particular, there is an isoscalar $1^{++}$ $B\bar B^*$ bound state with a mass of 10580 MeV which may be searched for in the $Υ(1S,2S)π^+π^-π^0$ mass distribution; the isovector charmonium partners of the $Z_b(10610)$ and the $Z_b(10650)$ are also predicted, one of which probably corresponds to the very recently observed $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4025)$ resonances by the BESIII Collaboration.

# Consequences of Heavy Quark Symmetries for Hadronic Molecules

## Key claim
Assuming the X(3872) and Z_b(10610) are isoscalar D D* and isovector B B* hadronic molecules respectively, heavy quark flavor symmetry predicts a series of new hadronic molecules, but the formation of bound states depends on both the potential strength and the reduced mass of the two-body system, meaning that a bound state in the charm sector does not guarantee a bound state in the bottom sector and vice versa.

## Method
The authors construct a leading-order effective field theory with contact-range interactions for heavy meson-antimeson systems, exploiting heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and heavy quark flavor symmetry (HFS) to relate potentials across different channels. They fix the contact terms C_{0X} and C_{1Z} by reproducing the measured masses of the X(3872) and Z_b(10610), then solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Gaussian regulator at cut-offs of 0.5 GeV and 1 GeV to predict pole positions for partner states, including uncertainties from finite heavy quark masses (20% in charm, 7% in bottom).

## Context
This work extends the application of heavy quark symmetries to hadronic molecules, building on prior studies of HQSS in the Z_b system by Bondar (2011), Voloshin (2011), and Mehen (2011), and on earlier EFT treatments of the X(3872) by Nieves (2012) and Valderrama (2012). The key novelty is the systematic use of heavy flavor symmetry to relate charm and bottom sectors, predicting the X_b(10580) as a test of the molecular hypothesis for X(3872) and identifying the newly observed Z_c(3900) and Z_c(4025) (BESIII 2013) as the charm counterparts of the Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650). The predictions enable experimental searches at hadron colliders or in photon-photon collisions, particularly for the X_b in the Υ(1S,2S)π+π-π0 channel.
