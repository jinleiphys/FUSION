# Quantum entanglement between partons in a strongly coupled quantum field theory

> We perform a first-principles, non-perturbative investigation of quantum entanglement between partonic constituents in a strongly coupled 3+1-dimensional scalar Yukawa theory, using light-front Hamiltonian methods with controlled Fock-space truncations. By explicitly constructing reduced density matrices for (mock) nucleon, pion, and anti-nucleon subsystems from light-front wave functions, we compute key entanglement witnesses, including von Neumann entropy, mutual information, and linear entropy, in both quenched (no sea pairs) and unquenched frameworks. We find that the entanglement entropy is closely related to the Shannon entropy of the transverse momentum dependent distribution, establishing a link between quantum information and parton structure. In contrast, the unquenched theory reveals genuinely non-classical correlations: the entanglement entropy cannot be reduced to any Shannon entropy of normalized parton distributions, demonstrating that the full hadronic wave function encodes quantum information beyond classical probabilities. Our findings highlight the role of entanglement as a fundamental probe of non-perturbative dynamics in relativistic quantum field theory and lay the groundwork for extending these concepts to QCD and future collider phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a strongly coupled scalar Yukawa theory, the entanglement entropy of partons computed from first-principles light-front wave functions is closely related to the Shannon entropy of transverse momentum dependent distributions in the quenched approximation, but in the unquenched theory the entanglement entropy cannot be reduced to any Shannon entropy of normalized parton distributions, revealing genuinely non-classical correlations beyond classical probabilities.

## Method

The authors solved the 3+1-dimensional scalar Yukawa theory using light-front Hamiltonian methods with controlled Fock-space truncations, including up to three-body sectors (|N⟩ + |πN⟩ + |ππN⟩) for the quenched approximation and additionally the |NN\(\bar N\)⟩ sector for the unquenched framework. They constructed reduced density matrices for (mock) nucleon, pion, and anti-nucleon subsystems from the light-front wave functions, then computed von Neumann entropy, mutual information, and linear entropy as entanglement witnesses.

## Key numbers

- Coupling strength defined as α = g²/(16πm²), with the nucleon mass set to m = 0.94 GeV and pion mass μ = 0.14 GeV
- Numerical convergence of the Fock expansion demonstrated at coupling α = 2.0 with Pauli-Villars regulator mass 15 GeV
- The field-strength renormalization constant Z satisfies 0 < Z ≤ 1 for the physical nucleon
- The anti-nucleon component in the unquenched solution contributes only a negligible fraction of the total norm

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Kharzeev (2017, 2021) connecting parton distribution functions to quantum information concepts, and builds upon non-perturbative light-front solutions of scalar Yukawa theory developed by Li (2014, 2015), Karmanov (2016), and Hiller (2016). The approach provides a first-principles derivation of parton entanglement from hadronic wave functions, contrasting with phenomenological models that studied entanglement in the valence-dominated large-x regime (Dumitru 2022, 2023; Qian 2024) or the dense gluon-dominated small-x regime (Kharzeev 2021; Hatta 2024). The results lay groundwork for extending entanglement studies to QCD and future collider phenomenology.
