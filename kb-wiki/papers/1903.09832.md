# Non-quadratic improved Hessian PDF reweighting and application to CMS dijet measurements at 5.02 TeV

> Hessian PDF reweighting, or "profiling", has become a widely used way to study the impact of a new data set on parton distribution functions (PDFs) with Hessian error sets. The available implementations of this method have resorted to a perfectly quadratic approximation of the initial $χ^2$ function before inclusion of the new data. We demonstrate how one can take into account the first non-quadratic components of the original fit in the reweighting, provided that the necessary information is available. We then apply this method to the CMS measurement of dijet pseudorapidity spectra in proton-proton (pp) and proton-lead (pPb) collisions at 5.02 TeV. The measured pp dijet spectra disagree with next-to-leading order (NLO) theory calculations using the CT14 NLO PDFs, but upon reweighting the CT14 PDFs, these can be brought to a much better agreement. We show that the needed proton-PDF modifications also have a significant impact on the predictions for the pPb dijet distributions. Taking the ratio of the individual spectra, the proton-PDF uncertainties effectively cancel, giving a clean probe of the PDF nuclear modifications. We show that these data can be used to further constrain the EPPS16 nuclear PDFs and strongly support gluon nuclear shadowing at small $x$ and antishadowing at around $x \approx 0.1$.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that including non-quadratic (cubic) terms in the Hessian PDF reweighting method improves the approximation of the original fit's χ² function, but the method's reliability depends on the penalty term remaining much smaller than the global tolerance Δχ²; when applied to CMS dijet data, the CT14 PDFs require modifications that push the penalty term to 1.17 times Δχ², indicating the limits of the reweighting approximation.

## Method
The authors develop a cubic-quadratic Hessian reweighting method that incorporates third-order terms in the χ² function and quadratic terms in the observable response, using the known δz±k parameter variations and global tolerance Δχ² from the original fit. They apply this method to CMS dijet pseudorapidity spectra at 5.02 TeV in pp and pPb collisions, using NLOJet++ with anti-kT algorithm (R=0.3) and CT14 NLO PDFs, with factorization and renormalization scales set to μ = pTave. The reweighting minimizes a new χ² function that combines the approximated original χ² with the new data's χ² contribution, using MINUIT for the numerical minimization.

## Key numbers
- The pp dijet spectra with CT14 NLO PDFs yield χ²/Ndata = 7.5 before reweighting, improving to χ²/Ndata = 2.0 after quadratic-quadratic reweighting with Δχ² = 100
- The penalty term for the reweighted CT14 fit is P/Δχ² = 1.17, indicating the reweighting reaches the limits of applicability
- With an artificially low Δχ² = 10, the reweighting achieves χ²/Ndata = 0.9 but with P/Δχ² = 3.61, showing clear tension with the original CT14 analysis
- MMHT14, NNPDF3.1, and 5-flavour ABMP16 NLO PDFs yield χ²/Ndata values of 4.7, 4.0, and 2.7 respectively for the same dijet data

## Context
This work extends the Hessian PDF reweighting method of Paukkunen (2014) by incorporating non-quadratic terms in the χ² function, building on the quadratic approximation used in xFitter and ePump software. The application to CMS dijet data at 5.02 TeV (Sirunyan 2018) shows that the required modifications to CT14 gluon PDFs are consistent with findings from NNPDF3.1 (Ball 2017) and reweighting studies using top-production data (Azizi 2018). The method enables studying the impact of new data on both free-proton PDFs (CT14) and nuclear PDFs (EPPS16) without performing full global fits, though the authors caution that large penalty terms signal tensions that may require complete refits.
