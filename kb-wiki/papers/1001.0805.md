# Possible $Σ({1\over2}^-)$ under the $Σ^*(1385)$ peak in $KΣ^*$ photoproduction

> The LEPS collaboration has recently reported a measurement of the reaction $γn\to K^+Σ^{*-}(1385)$ with linearly polarized photon beam at resonance region. The observed beam asymmetry is sizably negative at $E_γ=1.8-2.4 \mathrm{GeV}$, in contrast to the presented theoretical prediction. In this paper, we calculate this process in the framework of the effective Lagrangian approach. By including a newly proposed $Σ(J^P={1\over2}^-)$ state with mass around 1380~MeV, the experimental data for both $γn$ and $γp$ experiments can be well reproduced. It is found that the $Σ({1\over2}^-)$ and/or the contact term may play important role and deserve further investigation.

# Possible Σ(1/2⁻) under the Σ*(1385) peak in KΣ* photoproduction

## Key claim

The large negative beam asymmetry observed by LEPS in γn → K⁺Σ*⁻(1385) at Eγ = 1.8–2.4 GeV can be explained either by including a Σ(Jᴾ = 1/2⁻) state near 1380 MeV mixed into the detected Λπ final state, or by adjusting the contact-term parameter h from 1.0 to 1.11 for the neutron reaction alone; the data alone cannot distinguish which mechanism is correct.

## Method

The authors calculated γN → KΣ*(1385) and γN → KΣ(1/2⁻) using an effective Lagrangian approach with t-channel kaon exchange, s-channel N and Δ resonances (N*(2080), Δ(1940), Δ(2000)), u-channel Λ and Σ* exchange, and a contact term to maintain gauge invariance. They fitted the unknown coupling constant g_{KNΣ} for the Σ(1/2⁻) state and the contact-term parameter h to the LEPS differential cross sections and beam asymmetry data for γn → K⁺Σ*⁻, and checked consistency with CLAS total cross sections for γp → K⁺Σ*⁰.

## Key numbers

- Beam asymmetry for γn → K⁺Σ*⁻ is sizably negative at Eγ = 1.8–2.4 GeV, in contrast to the original theoretical prediction (χ² ≈ 180 for 39 data points without new ingredients).
- Scheme I (including Σ(1/2⁻) with h = 1.0 fixed): g_{KNΣ} = 1.34, cutoff ΛM = 1.6 GeV for the Σ(1/2⁻) channel, χ² = 97 for 39 data points.
- Scheme II (no Σ(1/2⁻), h adjusted): h = 1.11, χ² = 102 for 39 data points.
- For the Σ(1/2⁻) alone, the beam asymmetry approaches −1 for cosθ_c.m. = 0.6–1.0.
- Coupling constants for the three s-channel resonances: f₁ = −1.25, f₂ = 1.21 for γpN*(2080); f₁ = 0.381, f₂ = −0.256 for γnN*(2080); f₁ = 0.39, f₂ = −0.57 for γNΔ(1940); f₁ = −0.68, f₂ = −0.062 for γNΔ(2000).
- Resonance widths used in fits: Γ_{N*(2080)} = 0.25 GeV, Γ_{Δ(1940)} = 0.15 GeV, Γ_{Δ(2000)} = 0.15 GeV (within PDG ranges).
- Anomalous magnetic moments for Σ(1/2⁻) taken from diquark model: κ_{Σ⁰} = −0.43, κ_{Σ⁻} = −1.74.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Oh, Ko, and Nakayama (2008) for KΣ*(1385) photoproduction, which successfully described CLAS total cross sections for γp → K⁺Σ*⁰ but failed to reproduce the LEPS beam asymmetry for γn → K⁺Σ*⁻. The Σ(1/2⁻) state near 1380 MeV was previously suggested by pentaquark model calculations of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) and Zhang et al. (2005), and by evidence from K⁻p → Λπ⁺π⁻ studies by Wu et al. (2009). The results enable future experiments at higher incident energies to distinguish between the Σ(1/2⁻) interpretation and the alternative contact-term mechanism.
