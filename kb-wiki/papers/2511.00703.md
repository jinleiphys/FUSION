# In-medium mass shifts of $B_c^{(*)}, B_s^{(*)}$ and $D_s^{(*)}$ mesons

> We present our predictions for the Lorentz scalar mass shifts of two-flavored heavy mesons, $B_c^{(*)}, B_s^{(*)}$ and $D_s^{(*)}$ in symmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium mass shifts are estimated by evaluating the lowest order one-loop self-energies of the mesons based on a flavor-SU(5) effective Lagrangian approach. In-medium properties necessary for the estimates are calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The enhanced self-energies of the mesons in symmetric nuclear matter relative to those in free space, yield the negative mass shifts of these mesons.

## Key claim

The paper predicts negative Lorentz scalar mass shifts for the two-flavored heavy mesons \(B_c^{(*)}, B_s^{(*)}\) and \(D_s^{(*)}\) in symmetric nuclear matter, but these predictions depend on the choice of a cutoff mass parameter \(\Lambda\) (varied from 2000 to 6000 MeV) used in vertex form factors to regularize loop integrals, and the results are presented as ranges rather than single values.

## Method

The in-medium mass shifts were estimated by evaluating the lowest order one-loop self-energies of the mesons using a flavor-SU(5) effective Lagrangian approach. The in-medium masses of the intermediate state mesons (\(B^{(*)}, D^{(*)}, K^{(*)}\)) needed for the self-energy calculations were computed using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which describes nucleon binding in nuclear matter via self-consistent exchange of \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) meson fields coupled to light quarks. The self-energy integrals were regularized with dipole form factors using five different cutoff mass values (\(\Lambda = 2000, 3000, 4000, 5000, 6000\) MeV), and the bare masses of the heavy mesons were determined by reproducing their free-space physical masses.

## Key numbers

*   At normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), the predicted mass shift range for \(B_c\) is \(\Delta m_{B_c} = [-101.1, -90.4]\) MeV, depending on the cutoff mass \(\Lambda\).
*   At \(\rho_0\), the predicted mass shift range for \(B^*_c\) is \(\Delta m_{B^*_c} = [-19.7, -14.5]\) MeV.
*   At \(\rho_0\), the predicted mass shift range for \(B_s\) is \(\Delta m_{B_s} = [-178.8, -133.0]\) MeV.
*   At \(\rho_0\), the predicted mass shift range for \(B^*_s\) is \(\Delta m_{B^*_s} = [-20.5, -16.0]\) MeV.
*   At \(\rho_0\), the predicted mass shift range for \(D_s\) is \(\Delta m_{D_s} = [-119.0, -78.0]\) MeV.
*   At \(\rho_0\), the predicted mass shift range for \(D^*_s\) is \(\Delta m_{D^*_s} = [-20.2, -52.1]\) MeV.
*   The QMC model predicts mass shifts at \(\rho_0\) for the intermediate mesons: \((\Delta m_B, \Delta m_{B^*}, \Delta m_D, \Delta m_{D^*}, \Delta m_K, \Delta m_{K^*}) = (-61.13, -61.05, -61.97, -61.66, -63.20, -61.97)\) MeV.
*   The universal coupling constant from SU(5) symmetry is \(g_{B_c B^* D} = g_{B_c B D^*} = g_{B^*_c B D} = g/(2\sqrt{2}) \approx 11.9\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zeminiani 2023) on in-medium mass shifts of heavy mesons, using the same flavor-SU(5) effective Lagrangian and QMC model framework. It provides predictions for the \(B_c^{(*)}, B_s^{(*)}\) and \(D_s^{(*)}\) mesons, which are two-flavored heavy mesons that do not share light quarks with nucleons, making their in-medium interactions primarily gluon-mediated. The results enable future studies of possible meson-nucleus bound states for these mesons, including Coulomb potentials and in-medium widths, with initial results for the \(B_c\)-\(^{12}\)C system already reported by Zeminiani (2024).
