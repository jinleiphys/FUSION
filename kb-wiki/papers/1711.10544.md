# Initial state and hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC BES energies

> We present a fully three-dimensional initial state model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) collision energies. The initial energy and net baryon density profiles are produced based on a classical string deceleration model. The baryon stopping and fluctuations during this early stage of the collision are investigated by studying the net baryon rapidity distribution and longitudinal decorrelation of the transverse geometry.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully three-dimensional initial state model for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies that uses a classical string deceleration model to produce initial energy and net baryon density profiles, but the authors note that at low collision energies (around 10 GeV) the finite longitudinal thickness of the colliding nuclei leads to a substantial overlapping time of 2-3 fm/c, leaving large theoretical uncertainty for the early time dynamics.

## Method

The authors generalized the Monte-Carlo Glauber model by including spatial distributions of nucleons along the longitudinal direction, then employed a string deceleration model where collided nucleons form string endpoints that decelerate according to classical equations of motion with string tension sigma. Longitudinal fluctuations were introduced by treating valence quarks (carrying only a fraction of nucleon momentum) as the colliding participants and by fluctuating the string deceleration time according to the LEXUS model. The decelerated strings were then treated as source terms feeding into hydrodynamic simulations on a local collision-by-collision basis.

## Key numbers

- Overlapping time tau_overlap ~ 2-3 fm/c for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) ~ 10 GeV
- String deceleration time Delta tau = 0.5 fm/c used for the baseline model
- Four collision energies studied: 200 GeV, 19.6 GeV, and 7.7 GeV (with 0-5% central Au+Au collisions)
- Beam rapidity Y_beam = arccosh(sqrt(s_NN)/(2m_p)) used to scale decorrelation ratios

## Context

This work extends the hybrid (viscous hydrodynamics + hadronic transport) framework previously applied by Song (2010) and Karpenko (2015) to heavy-ion collisions over a wide range of energies. It differs from the approach of Karpenko (2015), which used the UrQMD hadron transport model for the pre-equilibrium stage, and from the AMPT transport + hydrodynamic simulations of Pang (2012, 2015). The model enables future Bayesian analysis and deep learning techniques to constrain initial state fluctuations and extract transport properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma in a baryon-rich environment, which is crucial for determining the existence and position of the QCD critical point.
