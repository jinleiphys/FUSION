# Stability conditions and Fermi surface topologies in a superconductor

> Candidate homogeneous, isotropic superfluid or superconducting states of paired fermion species with different chemical potentials, can lead to quasiparticle excitation energies that vanish at either zero, one, or two spheres in momentum space. With no zeroes, we have a conventional BCS superconductor. The other two cases, ``gapless'' superconductors, appear in mean field theory for sufficiently large mismatches and/or sufficiently large coupling strengths. Here we examine several stability criteria for those candidate phases. Positivity of number susceptibility appears to provide the most powerful constraint, and renders all the two-zero states that we have examined mechanically unstable. Our results should apply directly to ultracold fermionic atom systems.

## Key claim

Positivity of the number susceptibility matrix, rather than the Meissner mass, provides the most restrictive stability condition for homogeneous isotropic superconducting states with mismatched Fermi surfaces; all examined states with two effective Fermi surfaces ("breached pairing") are mechanically unstable according to this criterion.

## Method

The authors compute the electric (Debye) and magnetic (Meissner) screening mass matrices, as well as the number susceptibility matrix, from the zero-energy low-momentum limit of the polarization tensor in a mean-field U(1)×U(1) gauge theory of two nonrelativistic fermion species with different chemical potentials and s-wave pairing. They treat the chemical potentials and gap function as free parameters, systematically scanning the parameter space defined by the dimensionless ratios ρ = μ̄/Δ and η = δμ/Δ, and classify the resulting quasiparticle dispersion relations into three Fermi surface topologies (zero, one, or two spheres of gapless excitations).

## Key numbers

- The boundary between one and two effective Fermi surfaces occurs at ρ₊ = 0 and ρ₋ = 0, where ρ± ≡ ρ ± √(η² − 1), with η = δμ/Δ and ρ = μ̄/Δ.
- The condition for any gapless state (one or two Fermi surfaces) requires η > 1, i.e., δμ > Δ.
- The two-Fermi-surface (breached pairing) region F₂ requires ρ₊ > 0 and ρ₋ > 0, which translates to μ̄ > √(δμ² − Δ²).
- The one-Fermi-surface region F₁ requires ρ₊ > 0 and ρ₋ < 0, i.e., −√(δμ² − Δ²) < μ̄ < √(δμ² − Δ²).

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of gapless superconducting phases previously studied in the context of ultracold fermionic atoms (Ketterle, 2003; Hulet, 2004) and color-superconducting quark matter (Huang, 2004; Casalbuoni, 2004), where imaginary Meissner masses had already revealed instabilities in breached pairing phases. The authors find that number susceptibility provides a more powerful constraint than the Meissner mass, and their results suggest that the true ground state for mismatched Fermi surfaces likely involves phase separation (Bedaque, 2003) or a Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) state rather than homogeneous breached pairing.
