# On the possibility of eta-mesic nucleus formation

> A microscopic treatment of eta-nucleus system is presented. Using this, the poles of the amplitude describing elastic scattering of eta-meson off the light nuclei ^2H, ^3H, ^3He, and ^4He have been located in the second quadrant of the complex plane of the eta-nucleus relative momentum. The results obtained indicate that, within the existing uncertainties in the eta-Nucleon input data, a bound state could exist. By a qualitative consideration it is shown that such bound-states, cannot be narrow. It is further shown that scattering length calculations cannot provide a definite answer on the question concerning the existence of eta-nucleus bound states.

## Key claim

Within the existing uncertainties in the eta-Nucleon input data, a bound eta-meson state could exist for light nuclei (H-2, H-3, He-3, He-4), but such quasi-bound states cannot be narrow; the scattering length approach cannot provide a definite answer on the existence of eta-nucleus bound states.

## Method

The authors developed a microscopic Finite-Rank Approximation (FRA) method to calculate the elastic scattering amplitude for eta-mesons off light nuclei (H-2, H-3, He-3, He-4) for any complex value of the relative momentum k, enabling them to locate S-matrix poles in the second quadrant of the complex k-plane. The eta-N interaction was modeled using a separable t-matrix motivated by S11-resonance (N*(1535)) dominance, with the nuclear wave functions obtained from the Malfliet-Tjon NN potential via the integro-differential equation approach (IDEA). The influence of inelastic channels (eta A to pi A) was taken into account via a complex eta-N potential.

## Key numbers

*   For the S11-resonance N*(1535), the resonance energy relative to the eta N threshold is approximately 49 MeV, with a width Gamma of about 150 MeV.
*   The eta N scattering length a_etaN has an uncertainty range: Re a_etaN in [0.27, 0.98] fm and Im a_etaN in [0.19, 0.37] fm.
*   Using a_etaN = (0.55 + i0.30) fm, the pole for eta-He-4 gave a binding energy E = -2.05 MeV and width Gamma = 7.48 MeV; for eta-H-2, E = 31.46 MeV and Gamma = 59.38 MeV.
*   The minimal Re a_etaN required to generate a quasi-bound state (zero-binding) is 0.47 fm for He-4, 0.73 fm for He-3, 0.75 fm for H-3, and 0.91 fm for H-2.

## Context

This work extends earlier optical potential predictions by Haider and Liu (1986) which set a lower bound of A >= 12 for eta-meson binding, and addresses the negative experimental search for narrow eta-nuclear bound states by Chrien et al. (1988). The results contradict the simple scattering length reasoning used by Wilkin (1993) for He-3 and He-4, showing that for a complex potential, large scattering lengths do not necessarily indicate bound states. The method enables future searches for eta-mesic nuclei analogous to hypernuclei, which could be used to study Charge Symmetry Breaking reactions and the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule.
