# Recent developments in effective field theory

> We will give a short introduction to the one-nucleon sector of chiral perturbation theory and will address the issue of a consistent power counting and renormalization. We will discuss the infrared regularization and the extended on-mass-shell scheme. Both allow for the inclusion of further degrees of freedom beyond pions and nucleons and the application to higher-loop calculations. As applications we consider the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass to order O(q^6) and the inclusion of vector and axial-vector mesons in the calculation of nucleon form factors.

# Recent developments in effective field theory

## Key claim

The infrared regularization and extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) schemes both enable a consistent power counting in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), allowing the inclusion of additional degrees of freedom such as vector and axial-vector mesons and the extension to higher-loop calculations, provided that the terms violating power counting are either separated (infrared regularization) or subtracted (EOMS) and absorbed into counter terms.

## Method

The paper reviews two renormalization schemes for baryon ChPT: infrared regularization, which splits the Feynman-parameter integral into an infrared part that satisfies power counting and a regular remainder that is absorbed into counter terms; and the EOMS scheme, which expands the integrand in small quantities and subtracts the integrated terms whose order is smaller than the power counting suggests. Both schemes are applied to one-loop and two-loop self-energy diagrams and to calculations of the nucleon mass, electromagnetic form factors, axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors, and the pion-nucleon form factor.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion of the nucleon mass to order O(q^6) is given by m_N = m + k_1 M^2 + k_2 M^3 + k_3 M^4 ln(M/mu) + k_4 M^4 + k_5 M^5 ln(M/mu) + k_6 M^5 + k_7 M^6 ln^2(M/mu) + k_8 M^6 ln(M/mu) + k_9 M^6, where all coefficients k_i are determined in terms of infrared renormalized parameters.
- Using physical values g_A = 1.2695(29), F_pi = 92.42(26) MeV, mu = m_N = 938.92 MeV, and M = M_pi+ = 139.57 MeV, the term k_5 M^5 ln(M/m_N) equals -4.8 MeV, which is approximately 31% of the leading nonanalytic one-loop contribution k_2 M^3.
- For M approximately 360 MeV, the k_5 term becomes as large as the k_2 term.
- The Dirac and Pauli form factors at Q^2=0: F_1^p(0)=1, F_1^n(0)=0, F_2^p(0)=1.793, F_2^n(0)=-1.913.
- The axial-vector coupling constant g_A = G_A(Q^2=0) = 1.2695(29).
- Lorentz-invariant baryon ChPT up to fourth order fails to describe nucleon form factors for momentum transfers beyond Q^2 approximately 0.1 GeV^2; inclusion of vector mesons improves the description.

## Context

This work extends the baryon ChPT framework of Gasser (1987) by addressing the long-standing problem of power counting in the relativistic formulation, which the heavy-baryon approach of Jenkins and Manohar had circumvented. The infrared regularization method was introduced by Becher and Leutwyler (1999), and the EOMS scheme by Fuchs (2003); the paper shows both schemes are applicable to two-loop calculations (Schindler, 2003) and to the inclusion of vector mesons (Fuchs, 2003) and axial-vector mesons (Hacker, 2005). The two-loop nucleon mass calculation (Schindler, 2006) represents the first complete two-loop result in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon ChPT and agrees with the heavy-baryon results of McGovern (1998). The inclusion of vector mesons in electromagnetic form factors (Schindler, 2005) and axial-vector mesons in axial form factors (Schindler, 2006) enables improved phenomenological descriptions beyond the reach of pure pion-nucleon ChPT.
