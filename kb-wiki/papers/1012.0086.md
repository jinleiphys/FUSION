# A Theoretic Review of Centrality-Dependent Direct Photon pt spectra in Au+Au Collisions at 200 GeV

> The recently published PHENIX data of direct photons at low pt from Au+Au collisions at the BNL relativestic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energy 200 GeV have attracted a lot of theoretic interest. Here I review our theoretical calculation which successfully reproduces the measured pt spectra of direct photons from Au+Au at different centralities over a wide pt range, in order to show a close relation between hadronic observables and those of direct photons.

# A Theoretic Review of Centrality-Dependent Direct Photon pt spectra in Au+Au Collisions at 200 GeV

## Key claim

The theoretical calculation successfully reproduces the measured direct photon pt spectra from Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV across different centralities over a wide pt range, but only when the collision system is described with ideal hydrodynamics constrained by hadronic data and the AMY photon emission rate (which includes the LPM effect) is used instead of the Kapusta rate. The author notes that only the mean production at each pt value has been reproduced, while other observables such as elliptic flow and correlations remain to be understood.

## Method

The calculation considers three sources of direct photons: prompt photons (leading order and fragmentation), thermal photons from the QGP and hadronic gas phases, and jet-photon conversion (JPC) photons from hard parton scattering with thermal partons. The space-time evolution of the hot dense matter is described by ideal hydrodynamics with energy-momentum conservation, and the thermal photon emission rate uses the AMY formalism. Hard parton energy loss in the plasma is included in both fragmentation and JPC contributions, with the energy loss parameter per unit distance determined by fitting pion nuclear suppression factor R_AA data at high pt for all centralities.

## Key numbers

- The pt interval studied is from 1 to 5 GeV/c, based on PHENIX data from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The intermediate pt region at RHIC is identified as approximately [4, 6] GeV/c
- A K factor of 2 is used for higher-order contributions to hard parton production in the fragmentation calculation
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA^gamma shows a strong enhancement at low pt due to thermal contributions, while remaining at the magnitude of unity at high pt

## Context

This work extends theoretical calculations of direct photon production at RHIC energies, following the publication of precise PHENIX data (PHENIX 2008) that generated significant theoretical interest. The calculation reproduces data over a wider pt range than previous approaches such as those by Bauchle (2010). The author notes that at SPS energy (Pb+Pb at 158 GeV), prompt and thermal photons overlap below 5 GeV/c and cannot be separated, whereas at RHIC the three pt regions are distinguishable. The work enables future studies of additional observables such as elliptic flow and correlations, which will provide further information beyond the pt spectra.
