# Rapidity window dependences of higher order cumulants and diffusion master equation

> We study the rapidity window dependences of higher order cumulants of conserved charges observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The time evolution and the rapidity window dependence of the non-Gaussian fluctuations are described by the diffusion master equation. Analytic formulas for the time evolution of cumulants in a rapidity window are obtained for arbitrary initial conditions. We discuss that the rapidity window dependences of the non-Gaussian cumulants have characteristic structures reflecting the non-equilibrium property of fluctuations, which can be observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions with the present detectors. It is argued that various information on the thermal and transport properties of the hot medium can be revealed experimentally by the study of the rapidity window dependences, especially by the combined use, of the higher order cumulants. Formulas of higher order cumulants for a probability distribution composed of sub-probabilities, which are useful for various studies of non-Gaussian cumulants, are also presented.

## Key claim

The rapidity window dependences of higher-order cumulants of conserved charges in relativistic heavy ion collisions can reveal non-equilibrium properties of fluctuations, and these dependences encode information on both the thermal and transport properties of the hot medium, provided the diffusion master equation (DME) is used to describe the time evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuations.

## Method

The author employs the diffusion master equation (DME) to model the time evolution of conserved-charge fluctuations along the rapidity direction, treating the spatial coordinate as one-dimensional discrete cells with particles hopping between adjacent cells at a rate gamma(t). Analytic formulas for the time evolution of cumulants in a rapidity window are derived for arbitrary initial conditions by solving the factorial generating function via the method of characteristics, then taking the continuum limit. The results are extended to multi-particle systems to describe net-particle numbers (e.g., net-baryon and net-electric charge) and to initial conditions with fluctuations using a superposition formula for cumulants.

## Key numbers

- The diffusion constant is given by \( D(t) = \gamma(t) a^2 \), where \( a \) is the cell length and \( \gamma(t) \) is the hopping probability per unit time.
- The diffusion distance is \( d(t) = \sqrt{ 2 \int_0^t dt' D(t') } \), and the dimensionless variable \( X = d(t)/\Delta \) controls the time and rapidity-window dependences of cumulants.
- For a uniform fixed initial condition with density \( M \), the second-order cumulant of particle number in a window \( \Delta \) is \( \langle Q^2 \rangle_c = M \Delta (1 - F_2(X)) \), where \( F_2(0)=1 \) and \( \lim_{X\to\infty} F_2(X)=0 \).
- The fourth-order cumulant for the same initial condition is \( \langle Q^4 \rangle_c = M \Delta (1 - 7F_2(X) + 12F_3(X) - 6F_4(X)) \), which can become negative at intermediate times due to non-equilibrium effects.
- In equilibrium (large \( t \)), all cumulants approach the Poissonian value \( \langle Q^n \rangle_c = M \Delta \).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kitazawa (2015) from Gaussian fluctuations to non-Gaussian fluctuations using the DME, building on earlier studies by Asakawa (2000), Jeon (2000), Shuryak (2000), and Aziz (2004) that considered only second-order fluctuations. The DME approach contrasts with the stochastic diffusion equation, which yields Gaussian fluctuations in equilibrium, whereas the DME produces Poissonian (or Skellam for net charges) higher-order cumulants in equilibrium, matching the hadron resonance gas model. The results enable experimental extraction of diffusion constants and thermal properties from rapidity-window dependences of cumulants measured at RHIC and LHC, particularly addressing the observed deviations from Skellam values and the violation of proportionality to rapidity window size seen by ALICE (2013) and STAR (2012).
