# Charged participants and their electromagnetic fields in an expanding fluid

> We investigate the space-time dependence of electromagnetic fields produced by charged participants in an expanding fluid. To address this problem, we need to solve the Maxwell's equations coupled to the hydrodynamics conservation equation, specifically the relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) equations, since the charged participants move with the flow. To gain analytical insight, we approximate the problem by solving the equations in a fixed background Bjorken flow, onto which we solve Maxwell's equations. The dynamical electromagnetic fields interact with the fluid's kinematic quantities such as the shear tensor and the expansion scalar, leading to additional non-trivial coupling. We use mode decomposition of Green's function to solve the resulting non-linear coupled wave equations. We then use this function to calculate the electromagnetic field for two test cases: a point source and a transverse charge distribution. The results show that the resulting magnetic field vanishes at very early times, grows, and eventually falls at later times.

## Key claim
The paper derives exact analytical solutions for electromagnetic fields generated by charged participants in an expanding fluid, but only under the assumptions that the back-reaction of electromagnetic fields on the fluid flow is negligible (inverse plasma beta parameter much less than 1) and that all dissipative quantities such as conductivity and diffusion are zero.

## Method
The authors solve Maxwell's equations coupled to relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) by fixing the background fluid to a one-dimensional Bjorken flow in Milne coordinates, neglecting back-reaction from the electromagnetic fields. They use mode decomposition of Green's function to solve the resulting nonlinear coupled wave equations, obtaining exact solutions without time-dependent asymptotic expansions. The method is applied to two test cases: a stationary point charge and a transverse Gaussian charge distribution.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field vanishes at very early times, grows, and eventually falls at later times (no specific numerical values given for the field magnitude or time scales in the truncated text).
- The initial time for the point charge calculation is taken as tau_0 = 0.6 fm, motivated by the fact that hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions typically starts after a finite time around 0.5-0.6 fm.
- The point charge magnitude is taken as Z = 79, which is half of the total charged spectators for an Au-Au collision.
- The source location is taken at x_0 = (b/2, 0, 0) with b = 7 fm.
- Electromagnetic fields from spectators in heavy-ion collisions reach magnitudes of approximately 10^18-10^19 G at RHIC-LHC energies (cited from prior work Kharzeev 2007, Skokov 2009).

## Context
This work extends previous analyses that focused on electromagnetic fields generated by moving spectators in heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007, Gursoy 2018), by instead calculating fields produced by charged participants that constitute the bulk of the fluid flow. The authors note that prior work calculated participant fields from the geometric overlap region using spectator-generated fields, which they argue can be improved upon. The results enable exact calculation of electromagnetic fields in expanding fluid without relying on time-dependent asymptotic expansions, though the authors note that full numerical RMHD simulations (Inghirami 2019, Dash 2022) would be needed for a comprehensive study including back-reaction effects.
