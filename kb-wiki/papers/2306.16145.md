# Universal rapidity scaling of entanglement entropy inside hadrons from conformal invariance

> When a hadron is probed at high energy, a nontrivial quantum entanglement entropy inside the hadron emerges due to the lack of complete information about the hadron wave function extracted from this measurement. In the high-energy limit, the hadron becomes a maximally entangled state, with a linear dependence of entanglement entropy on rapidity, as has been found in a recent analysis based on parton description. In this paper, we use an effective conformal field theoretic description of hadrons on the light cone to show that the linear dependence of the entanglement entropy on rapidity found in parton description is a general consequence of approximate conformal invariance and does not depend on the assumption of weak coupling. Our result also provides further evidence for a duality between the parton and string descriptions of hadrons.

## Key claim

The linear dependence of entanglement entropy on rapidity inside hadrons, previously derived from parton model wave functions, is shown to be a general consequence of approximate conformal invariance on the light cone and does not rely on weak coupling assumptions. The result holds for both vacuum and excited states in an effective 2D conformal field theory description, with the leading term independent of the specific string theory details.

## Method

The authors model hadrons using an effective 2D conformal field theory on the light cone, motivated by the effective string description of color-flux tubes. They compute the entanglement entropy for a null interval along the light-cone coordinate \(x^+\) using the replica method and twist operators, then express the result in terms of rapidity \(\eta\) via the relation \(x^\pm = \tau e^{\pm \eta}\). For excited states representing hadrons, they use the first law of entanglement entropy and the modular Hamiltonian for a null segment, evaluating the expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor from three-point functions of conformal primary vertex operators.

## Key numbers

* Entanglement entropy for a null interval in the vacuum: \(S_A = \frac{c}{6} \log\left(\frac{\delta \Delta x_+}{\epsilon^2}\right)\), where \(c\) is the central charge, \(\delta\) is a UV cutoff, and \(\Delta x_+\) is the interval width.
* Vacuum entanglement entropy in terms of rapidity: \(S_A = \frac{c}{6} \Delta\eta + \text{constant}\), with \(\Delta\eta\) the rapidity interval.
* Leading variation of entanglement entropy for excited states: \(\delta S_A = 2\pi C_h \Delta\eta + \cdots\), where \(C_h\) is the OPE coefficient of the vertex operator with conformal weight \(h\).
* For a regulated finite-energy excitation with \(\langle T_{++}(x_+)\rangle = m/(m/k + x_+^2)\), the entanglement entropy gives \(\delta S_A = 2\pi m \left[\log \Delta x_+ - (1 + \log\sqrt{m/k}) + \mathcal{O}(1/\Delta x_+)\right]\), with subleading corrections at order \(\mathcal{O}(e^{-\Delta\eta})\).

## Context

This work extends the parton-model derivation of linear rapidity scaling of entanglement entropy by Kharzeev (2017) to a general conformal field theory framework, showing the result is universal and does not require weak coupling. It supports the effective string description of hadrons (Nambu, Luscher and Weisz, Aharony 2013) and provides further evidence for a duality between parton and string descriptions. The result enables an entropic definition of the hadron structure function at small Bjorken \(x\) that does not rely on the parton model, with \(xG(x) \sim (1/x)^c\) where \(c\) is proportional to the central charge of the effective CFT.
