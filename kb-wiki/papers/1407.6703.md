# Diffractive photoproduction of vector mesons at the LHC

> We confront saturation-based results for diffractive $ψ(2s)$ and $ρ$ production at HERA and $J/ψ$ photoproduction with all available data including recent ones from HERA, ALICE and LHCb, finding a good agreement. We show that the $t$-distribution of differential cross-section of photoproduction of vector mesons offers a unique opportunity to discriminate among saturation and non-saturation models. This is due to emergence of a pronounced dip (or multiple dips) in the $t$-distribution of diffractive photoproduction of vector mesons at relatively large, but potentially accessible $|t|$ that can be traced back to the unitarity features of colour dipole amplitude in the saturation regime. We provide various predictions for exclusive (photo)-production of different vector mesons including the ratio of $ψ(2s)/J/ψ$ at HERA, the LHC and at future colliders.

# Diffractive photoproduction of vector mesons at the LHC

## Key claim

Saturation-based (Color Glass Condensate) predictions for diffractive vector meson photoproduction agree with all available HERA, ALICE, and LHCb data, and the differential cross-section in momentum transfer $t$ can discriminate between saturation and non-saturation models, but only for lighter mesons such as $\rho$ and $\phi$, where multiple dips appear; for heavier mesons like $\psi(2s)$, saturation and 1-Pomeron models give similar results in the energy range studied.

## Method

The authors compute the scattering amplitude for exclusive diffractive $\gamma^* + p \to V + p$ (with $V = J/\psi, \psi(2s), \rho$) using the colour-dipole formalism, convolving the dipole-proton scattering amplitude $\mathcal{N}$ with the overlap of photon and vector meson wave functions. They employ two impact-parameter-dependent saturation models (b-CGC and IP-Sat) whose parameters were previously constrained by combined HERA data, and compare results to a 1-Pomeron model representing leading-order pQCD without saturation. The vector meson wave functions are constructed using boosted Gaussian wave-functions with parameters fitted to leptonic decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Total $J/\psi$ photoproduction cross-section compared at photon-proton centre-of-mass energies $W_{\gamma p}$ up to about 1.3 TeV (LHCb and ALICE data).
- Charm quark mass varied within $m_c = 1.2 \div 1.4$ GeV, extracted from global analysis of small-x data at $x < 0.01$.
- Light quark masses varied within $m_{u,d,s} = 0.01 \div 0.14$ GeV for $\rho$ meson calculations.
- Ratio $R = \psi(2s)/J/\psi$ for diffractive photoproduction at $Q=0$ shown as a function of $W_{\gamma p}$, and as a function of $Q^2$ at fixed $W_{\gamma p} = 95$ GeV.
- Differential cross-section $d\sigma/dt$ predictions for $J/\psi$, $\psi(2s)$, and $\rho$ at $W_{\gamma p} = 1$ TeV and $Q=0$, showing dip structures in saturation models at specific $|t|$ values.

## Context

This work extends previous saturation model studies (b-CGC from Rezaeian et al. 2013, IP-Sat from Kowalski et al. 2003) by confronting them with the 2014 LHCb updated data and combined HERA data that were not available earlier. It also compares with leading-order and next-to-leading-order pQCD results (MNRT) for $J/\psi$ production. The predictions for $\psi(2s)$ production and the $\psi(2s)/J/\psi$ ratio at LHC energies, and the demonstration that $t$-distribution dips are a signature of saturation unitarity effects, enable future experimental discrimination between saturation and non-saturation models at the LHC and LHeC.
