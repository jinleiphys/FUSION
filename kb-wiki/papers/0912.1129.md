# Chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons and dense matter

> We overview two interconnected topics: possible effective restoration of chiral symmetry in highly excited hadrons and possible existence of confined but chirally symmetric matter at low temperatures and high densities.

# Chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons and dense matter

## Key claim

Highly excited hadrons may exhibit effective chiral symmetry restoration, meaning that most of their mass is not generated by the quark condensate of the vacuum; this phenomenon, if correct, implies that confinement and chiral symmetry breaking are not inextricably linked, and a confining but chirally symmetric phase of matter (quarkyonic matter) may exist at low temperatures and high densities.

## Method

The paper reviews spectroscopic patterns of parity doublets in baryon and meson spectra from the Particle Data Group and from antiproton-proton annihilation data at LEAR (CERN), and correlates these patterns with measured decay coupling constants to the pion-nucleon channel. It also summarizes results from a confining, chirally symmetric model (a 3+1 dimensional generalization of the 't Hooft model) that solves the gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation to study chiral restoration in excited mesons and in dense matter.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleon excitation N\_(1520) with no identified chiral partner, the squared decay coupling constant ratio (f\_{B-Nπ}/f\_{NNπ})² is 2.5, indicating a large coupling to pions.
- For the chiral multiplet N\_(1440) – N\_(1535), the squared decay coupling constant ratios are 0.15 and 0.026, respectively, both much smaller than the nucleon value.
- For the highest-spin multiplet N\_(2220) – N\_(2250) (J=9/2), the squared decay coupling constant ratios are 0.000022 and 0.0000020, respectively.
- For the N\_(2600) state (J=11/2), the squared decay coupling constant ratio is 0.000000064.
- Missing J=4 chiral partners in the meson spectrum are expected at mass 2000 ± 50 MeV; their production in the L=4 partial wave of the antiproton-proton system is suppressed by a factor of 10¹ to 10³ relative to the observed L=3 partial wave.

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Glozman and collaborators (Glozman 2000, 2002) on parity doubling in baryon spectra and by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) on quarkyonic matter in the large N_c limit. It contradicts the long-held assumption, based on 't Hooft anomaly matching and models like the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, that confinement necessarily implies chiral symmetry breaking. The paper enables future experimental searches for missing chiral partners in high-spin meson states and motivates lattice QCD calculations of axial charges for excited baryons, such as those begun by Burch (2006) and by Takahashi and Kunihiro (2008).
