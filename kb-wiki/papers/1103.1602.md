# Exclusive pion electroproduction off nucleons and nuclei

> We present our results for the exclusive electroproduction of pions off nucleons and nuclei at high values of $Q^2$. In electroproduction of nuclei $A(e,e'π)$ we consider the Color Transparency (CT) effect recently observed at JLAB. It is shown that the description of $π$ production off nucleons is mandatory for the proof of the CT signal at JLAB. We further develop the models for exclusive production of $π$ off nucleons at JLAB and HERMES. At first we describe a model based on exclusive-inclusive connection. In the second approach we combine a Regge pole approach with the residual effect of nucleon resonances. The nucleon resonances are described using a dual connection between the exclusive form factors and inclusive deep inelastic structure functions. We present the results for the beam spin azimuthal asymmetries measured at JLAB in exclusive electroproduction of charged and neutral pions.

# Exclusive Pion Electroproduction off Nucleons and Nuclei

## Key claim
The observed Color Transparency (CT) effect in semi-exclusive pion electroproduction off nuclei at JLAB is driven primarily by the transverse component of the cross section, not the longitudinal component as previously expected; a correct description of the elementary pion production mechanism off nucleons is mandatory for interpreting the CT signal.

## Method
The authors model exclusive pion electroproduction using two complementary approaches: (1) an exclusive-inclusive connection where the transverse cross section is described via the Lund string fragmentation model for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) events, and (2) a Regge pole approach combined with residual nucleon resonance contributions modeled through a Bloom-Gilman duality connection between exclusive form factors and inclusive deep inelastic structure functions. For nuclear targets, final-state interactions are treated using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equation with a quantum diffusion model for the time-dependent hadron-nucleon cross section.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear transparency data for $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au targets measured at JLAB over $Q^2$ range 1 to 5 GeV$^2$ show a rise in pionic transparency
- Unseparated cross sections for $p(\gamma^*,\pi^+)n$ are described from $Q^2 \simeq 1$ GeV$^2$ up to $5$ GeV$^2$ at average $W \simeq 2.2$ GeV
- At $Q^2=1.1$ GeV$^2$ the forward cross section shows a strong longitudinal component; at $Q^2=4.7$ GeV$^2$ the cross section is flat and totally transverse
- The ratio $d\sigma_{\rm L}/d\sigma_{\rm T}$ shows approximate $\sim Q^2$ scaling only at small Bjorken $x_{\rm B}$; for $x_{\rm B} \gtrsim 0.6$ the ratio is independent of $Q^2$
- Beam spin asymmetry $A^{\sin(\phi)}_{\rm LU}$ in $p(\vec{e},e'\pi^+)n$ is sizable and positive at CLAS/JLAB with $E_e=5.77$ GeV, $W>2$ GeV, $Q^2>1.5$ GeV$^2$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous two-component hadron-parton model (Kaskulov 2008, Kaskulov 2009) for charged pion electroproduction, which resolved the longstanding puzzle of the large transverse cross section that hadronic meson-exchange models alone (Blok 2008) could not explain. The paper also addresses the Color Transparency signal observed at JLAB (2007) and provides a microscopic interpretation contrasting with the expectation that CT should appear in the longitudinal channel (Strikman 2007). The results enable quantitative extraction of pion form factors and generalized parton distributions from JLAB and HERMES data, and provide predictions for beam spin asymmetries in both charged and neutral pion channels that can be tested at the JLAB upgrade.
