# Soft Dipole Modes in Neutron-rich Ni-isotopes in QRRPA

> The soft dipole modes in neutron rich even-even Ni-isotopes are investigated in the quasiparticle relativistic random phase approximation. We study the evolution of strengths distribution, centroid energies of dipole excitation in low-lying and normal GDR regions with the increase of the neutron excess. It is found in the present study that the centroid energies of the soft dipole strengths strongly depend on the thickness of neutron skin along with the neutron rich even-even Ni-isotopes.

## Key claim
The centroid energies of soft dipole modes in neutron-rich even-even Ni-isotopes strongly depend on the thickness of the neutron skin, but this relationship is demonstrated only within the quasiparticle relativistic random phase approximation (QRRPA) using the NL3 effective interaction, and the calculated centroid energies of the normal giant dipole resonance (GDR) deviate from empirical values for nuclei far from the beta-stability line.

## Method
The authors first compute ground-state properties of neutron-rich even-even Ni-isotopes (mass numbers 68–96) using an extended relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) approach with the NL3 parameter set, treating the resonant continuum with a scattering boundary condition. They then construct quasiparticle configurations and solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the response function formalism to obtain the QRRPA isovector dipole response functions, separating the energy region into low-lying (0–10 MeV) and normal GDR (10–60 MeV) intervals.

## Key numbers
- The calculated centroid energy of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) for \(^{96}\)Ni is 15.4 MeV, about 1 MeV smaller than the empirical value from the formula \(E = 31.2A^{-1/3} + 20.6A^{-1/6}\).
- The low-lying dipole strength in \(^{70}\)Ni exhausts 10.2% of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) dipole sum rule; for \(^{96}\)Ni, it exhausts 32.3% of the TRK sum rule.
- The binding energy per nucleon for Ni-isotopes from mass 68 to 96 is reproduced perfectly by the extended RMF+BCS approach with NL3, compared to experimental data from Audi (1995).
- The difference of neutron and proton rms radii increases with mass number, with a kink at \(A = 78\) (neutron number \(N = 50\)).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of soft dipole modes (pygmy dipole resonances) in light neutron-rich nuclei, such as those by Aumann (1996) and Vretenar (2001), to medium-heavy Ni-isotopes using a self-consistent QRRPA framework. It also builds on the quasiparticle phonon model calculations by Tsoneva (2003), which found correlations between low-lying dipole strengths and neutron skin thickness. The results suggest that experimental measurements of low-lying dipole excitation in neutron-rich nuclei could provide information on neutron skin properties, which are relevant to the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star structure.
