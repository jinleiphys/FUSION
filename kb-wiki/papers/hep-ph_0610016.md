# Generalized Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation and Exact Quark Propagators

> A three-dimensional reduction of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation retaining, in contrast to the Salpeter equation, the exact propagators (crucial for, e.g., a proper incorporation of dynamical chiral symmetry breakdown) is proposed. This formalism may be easily extended to Bethe-Salpeter equations for bound states composed of particles that are not, or not all, identical to spin-1/2 fermions as well as to three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation different from Salpeter's equation.

# Generalized Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation and Exact Quark Propagators

## Key claim
The authors propose a three-dimensional reduction of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation that retains exact fermion propagators, unlike the Salpeter equation, but this improvement is contingent on assuming all interactions are instantaneous and that the exact propagator can be approximated with scalar functions depending only on three-momentum.

## Method
The authors construct a three-dimensional reduction of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation under the instantaneous approximation, while keeping the exact quark propagator expressed in terms of scalar functions A(p²) and B(p²) that depend only on three-momentum. For spherically symmetric interactions, the bound-state equation reduces to a set of coupled integral equations for the independent radial components of the bound-state amplitude, which are solved by conversion into an equivalent matrix problem. The formalism is applied to pseudoscalar (J^PC = 0^-+) mesons considered as quark-antiquark bound states with linear confining interactions of time-component Lorentz-vector Dirac structure γ⁰⊗γ⁰, using a light-quark propagator from a Dyson-Schwinger equation solution consistent with the QCD axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers
- The "exact-propagator instantaneous formalism" yields significantly smaller spacings of bound-state mass eigenvalues than those obtained from Salpeter's equation for "reasonable" constituent light-quark masses (no specific numerical values given for the mass spacings or constituent masses in the truncated text).

## Context
This work extends the earlier formalism of Lucha and Schoberl (2000/2001) on instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equations, and contrasts with the standard Salpeter equation which uses approximate propagators. The approach enables proper incorporation of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in bound-state calculations, and the authors cite the quark Dyson-Schwinger model of Maris as the source for the light-quark propagator used in their tentative application. The formalism can be extended to bound states of particles that are not all spin-1/2 fermions, and to other three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation.
