# Spin transport in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions as a probe of in-medium spin-orbit interactions

> The spin up-down splitting of collective flows in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions as a result of the nuclear spin-orbit interaction is investigated within a spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehing-Uhlenbeck transport model SIBUU12. Using a Skyrme-type spin-orbit coupling quadratic in momentum, we found that the spin splittings of the directed flow and elliptic flow are largest in peripheral Au+Au collisions at beam energies of about 100-200 MeV/nucleon, and the effect is considerable even in smaller systems especially for nucleons with high transverse momenta. The collective flows of light clusters of different spin states are also investigated using an improved dynamical coalescence model with spin. Our study can be important in understanding the properties of in-medium nuclear spin-orbit interactions once the spin-dependent observables proposed in this work can be measured.

## Key claim

The spin up-down splitting of directed and elliptic flows in heavy-ion collisions is largest in peripheral Au+Au collisions at beam energies of about 100-200 MeV/nucleon, but the effect is considerable even in smaller systems such as Sn+Sn, especially for nucleons with high transverse momenta. The authors note that this effect depends on using a Skyrme-type spin-orbit coupling quadratic in momentum, and the energy range for maximum splitting could differ if a different momentum dependence of the spin-orbit coupling is used.

## Method

The authors used the spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model SIBUU12 with a quantum description of spin dynamics, incorporating a Skyrme-type spin-orbit interaction with strength W0 = 150 MeVfm^5. They simulated Au+Au and Sn+Sn collisions at beam energies of 50-200 MeV/nucleon and various impact parameters, calculating the directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2 for spin-up and spin-down nucleons separately. For light clusters, they employed an improved dynamical coalescence model that uses explicit nucleon spin and isospin information to determine statistical factors for forming deuterons, tritons, and He-3 nuclei in different spin states.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit coupling strength is set to W0 = 150 MeVfm^5 in the calculations, and reducing it to 80 MeVfm^5 reduces the slope of the differential directed flow by approximately a factor of five and the magnitude of the differential elliptic flow by approximately a factor of two.
- The spin up-down differential directed flow can be as large as 50% of the total directed flow in some cases, and the differential elliptic flow can be as large as 50% of the total elliptic flow in peripheral collisions at beam energies of 100-200 MeV/nucleon.
- For Au+Au collisions at 100 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 8 fm, the y component of the time-odd spin-dependent potential (nabla x j) is two orders of magnitude larger than its x and z components.
- The statistical factor for a spin-up proton and a spin-up neutron to form a spin-up deuteron with Sz = 1 is 1/2, while the statistical factor for a spin-up (spin-down) neutron, a spin-up proton, and a spin-down proton to form a spin-up (spin-down) He-3 nucleus with Sz = 1/2 is 1/6.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies using the SIBUU12 model (Xu 2013, Xia 2014, Xu 2015) by incorporating a quantum description of spin dynamics (Xia 2016), which yields a larger spin splitting effect than previous calculations. A similar approach based on the quantum molecular dynamics model was developed more recently by Guo (2014). The study provides theoretical predictions for spin-dependent collective flow observables that could be measured using spin-polarized beams now producible via nucleon removal or pickup reactions (Ich 2012), and could help constrain the poorly known strength, isospin dependence (Sha 1995), and density dependence (Pea 1994) of the in-medium nuclear spin-orbit interaction.
