# Lepton Scattering on Nuclei at x > 1 and the Nucleon Spectral Function

> We analyze the processes of deep inelastic and quasielastic scattering in the region of x > 1. These processes are found to be very sensitive to the information contained in the nucleon spectral function in nuclei, particularly to the correlations between energy and momentum. Calculations are done using two spectral functions for nuclear matter and one for finite nuclei, ^{16}O. Several approximations are also analyzed and are shown to be inaccurate in this region. These results stress the fact that the region of x > 1 contains important information on nuclear dynamical correlations.

## Key claim

The region of Bjorken x > 1 in lepton-nucleus scattering is highly sensitive to the full nucleon spectral function, particularly the correlation between energy and momentum, and cannot be accurately described using only momentum distributions or other simplified approximations.

## Method

The authors computed the nuclear structure function F2A(x) for deep inelastic scattering and the quasielastic structure function W2A^Q using a relativistic formalism that convolves the nucleon structure function with the hole spectral function. They employed three different spectral functions: two for nuclear matter (a semiphenomenological model using NN experimental cross sections and a microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation with a realistic OBE potential) and one for finite nuclei (16O) that separates quasiparticle and background contributions. They also tested several approximations including a non-interacting Fermi sea, use of the momentum distribution alone, and use of the momentum distribution with the mean energy.

## Key numbers

- At x ≈ 1, the microscopic nuclear matter spectral function gives results about 20% higher than the semiphenomenological one for 16O.
- At x ≈ 1.5, the semiphenomenological approach provides values of F2A(x) about 40% larger than the microscopic one.
- The finite nucleus spectral function for 16O gives results about 8% higher than the nuclear matter approach at x ≈ 1.
- Relativistic corrections reduce F2A(x) by 25% around x = 1 and roughly halve the nonrelativistic results at x ≈ 1.5.
- At x ≈ 1, the non-interacting Fermi sea approximation gives a structure function about a factor of two smaller than the proper spectral function.
- Comparison with BCDMS data for 12C at Q2 = 61, 85, and 150 GeV2 shows theoretical results are on average 40% higher than experiment up to x ≈ 1.05.
- At low Q2 = 1.27 GeV2 for 56Fe, the quasielastic contribution dominates the structure function for x > 1, peaking around x = 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the EMC effect and the rise of the nuclear structure function ratio at x close to 1, which was identified by Bodek as due to Fermi motion. The authors show that approximations using only momentum distributions, as sometimes used in EMC calculations, give severely wrong results in the x > 1 region, and that the full spectral function including energy-momentum correlations is essential. The results enable future experiments at high Q2 to extract information about nuclear dynamical correlations beyond the shell model, and they provide benchmarks for comparing with data from BCDMS (1990) and SLAC (1994).
