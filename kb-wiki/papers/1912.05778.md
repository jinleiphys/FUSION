# Quark Transverse Parton Distribution at the Next-to-Next-to-Next-to-Leading Order

> We report a calculation of the perturbative matching coefficients for the transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions for quark at the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in QCD, which involves calculation of non-standard Feynman integrals with rapidity divergence. We introduce a set of generalized Integration-By-Parts equations, which allows an algorithmic evaluation of such integrals using the machinery of modern Feynman integral calculation.

## Key claim

The authors report the first calculation of the perturbative matching coefficients for the quark transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distribution function at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in QCD, using the exponential regularization scheme for rapidity divergence; the abstract glosses over the fact that the calculation is performed specifically within the rapidity renormalization group formalism based on Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), and that the results are presented in a completely analytic form for all color structures and partonic channels.

## Method

The calculation converts the perturbative TMD beam function into semi-inclusive partonic cross sections, which are expressed as Feynman integrals with an exponential regulator for rapidity divergence. The authors introduce a set of generalized Integration-By-Parts (IBP) equations that incorporate the exponential regulator, allowing algorithmic reduction of the integrals to master integrals using standard tools (LiteRed, Fire). The master integrals are solved via the method of differential equations, expanded around the regulator parameter tau approaching zero, and the boundary constants are determined by considering the threshold limit z to 1 using expansion by regions, relating them to soft-virtual corrections for Higgs production at N3LO.

## Key numbers

* The matching coefficients contain poles in epsilon up to 1/epsilon^6, which cancel after renormalization using the known three-loop splitting kernel.
* The alphabet for the differential equations consists of five letters: {z, 1-z, 1+z, 2-z, z^2 - z + 1}, but after summing virtual-real and real-real contributions, the latter two letters drop out, leaving results expressible in Harmonic Polylogarithms.
* In the threshold limit (z to 1), the non-vanishing component I^{(2)}_{qq} has coefficient (28 zeta_3 - 808/27) C_A C_F + (224/27) C_F N_f T_F, and I^{(3)}_{qq} has coefficient involving terms such as (-128 zeta_3/9 - 7424/729) C_F N_f^2 T_F^2 and others up to zeta_5.
* In the high energy limit (z to 0), the leading term for I^{(2)}_{qg} is (2 C_A T_F / z) (172/27 - 8 zeta_2/3), and for I^{(3)}_{qg} the leading term includes (2 T_F / z) times (208 zeta_2/9 + 32 zeta_3/3 - 17152/243) C_A^2 ln z plus additional color structures.
* The integrated TMD PDF with q_T^max = 10 GeV shows stabilization of the perturbative series at N3LO even for z as small as 10^{-4}.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of TMD matching coefficients at NLO (Collins 1984, Ji 2004, Becher 2010) and NNLO (Catani 2011, Gehrmann 2012, Echevarria 2016, Luo 2019) to N3LO for the first time. The results provide the last missing ingredient for generalizing q_T subtraction (Catani 2007) to N3LO accuracy, and enable precision TMD phenomenology for future Electron Ion Collider measurements. The generalized IBP equations introduced here may also be applicable to other problems involving non-standard Feynman propagators, such as two-loop q_T soft functions for top-quark pair production or thrust soft functions with step functions.
