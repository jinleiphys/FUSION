# Further steps towards next generation of covariant energy density functionals

> The present study aims at further development of covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) towards more accurate description of binding energies across the nuclear chart. For the first time, infinite basis corrections to binding energies in the fermionic and bosonic sectors of the covariant density functional theory have been taken into account in the fitting protocol within the covariant density functional theory. In addition, total electron binding energies have been used in the conversion of atomic binding energies into nuclear ones. Their dependence on neutron excess has been investigated for the first time across the nuclear chart within atomic approach. These factors have been disregarded in previous generation of covariant energy density functionals but their neglect leads to substantial global calculation errors for physical quantities of interest. For example, these errors for binding energies are of the order of 0.8 MeV or higher for the three major classes of covariant energy density functionals.

## Key claim

The paper claims that accounting for infinite basis corrections in both fermionic and bosonic sectors, together with using total electron binding energies to convert atomic binding energies into nuclear ones, substantially improves the accuracy of covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) for binding energies across the nuclear chart. The authors hedge that these corrections were disregarded in previous generations of CEDFs, leading to global calculation errors of order 0.8 MeV or higher for binding energies.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the anchor based optimization approach (ABOA) to fit three major classes of CEDFs: nonlinear meson exchange (NLME), density-dependent meson exchange (DDME), and point coupling (PC) models. They implemented infinite basis corrections for fermionic (up to N_F=40 shells) and bosonic (up to N_B=40 shells) sectors iteratively within the fitting protocol, and subtracted total electron binding energies from atomic binding energies of the AME2020 mass evaluation to obtain nuclear binding energies for 12 spherical anchor nuclei and 882 even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy errors from neglecting the corrections are of order 0.8 MeV or higher for the three major classes of CEDFs
- The atomic binding energy of 208Pb is -1636.4302 MeV, while the nuclear binding energy is -1635.863 MeV, a difference of 0.5672 MeV due to electron binding
- Total electron binding energy for Z=120 atoms reaches approximately 1.6 MeV
- The field shift constant F for Og atoms is 0.118 keV/fm^2, and the difference in total electron binding energies between 290Og and 314Og isotopes is 0.552 keV
- Full convergence in the fermionic basis for DD-MEZ and NL5(Z) functionals is reached at N_F=40 for the heaviest nuclei studied
- The procedure for infinite basis corrections reproduces fully convergent N_F=40 results with global accuracy better than 15 keV for DD-MEZ and 10 keV for NL5(Z) functionals
- For the PC-Z functional, full convergence of binding energy for 240Pu and 290Lv is reached only at N_F=56 in calculations without pairing

## Context

This work extends the anchor based optimization approach developed by Taninah and Afanasjev (2023) and applied in TOAPT (2024), which improved global binding energy description by 0.5-0.8 MeV depending on the CEDF class. The paper addresses limitations of previous CEDF fits that used truncated fermionic bases (typically N_F=20) and ignored electron binding energies, a problem noted in many prior functionals including NL3, DD-ME2, PC-PK1, and the Y-series functionals. The results enable more accurate global mass calculations in covariant density functional theory and provide a pathway for fitting functionals at the beyond mean field level.
