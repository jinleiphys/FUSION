# Spin-orbit interaction in final state as possible reason for T-odd correlation in ternary fission

> A model for ternary fission is discussed in which a third particle (alpha-particle) is emitted due to non-adiabatic change of the nuclear potential at neck rapture. An expression for energy and angular distribution of alpha-particles is proposed. It is shown that an interaction between spin of fissioning system and orbital momentum of alpha-particle (spin-orbit interaction in the final state) results in recently observed asymmetry of alpha-particle emission, which can be formally related to T-odd correlation. No strong dependence of the asymmetry on the angle of alpha-particle emission with respect to the fission axis is predicted by the model in accordance with the experimental data.

# Spin-orbit interaction in final state as possible reason for T-odd correlation in ternary fission

## Key claim
The observed T-odd asymmetry in alpha-particle emission from ternary fission of 233U and 235U by cold polarized neutrons can be explained by a spin-orbit interaction between the spin of the fissioning system and the orbital momentum of the emitted alpha-particle in the final state, without requiring violation of time-reversal invariance. The model predicts no strong dependence of the asymmetry on the emission angle relative to the fission axis, consistent with experimental data, but the author notes that neglecting fragment motion during alpha-particle acceleration prevents faithful reproduction of experimental data.

## Method
The author constructs a quantum model in which the alpha-particle is emitted due to a non-adiabatic change of the nuclear potential at neck rupture during scission. The transition probability from an initial bound or quasibound state to a final continuum state is calculated using first-order time-dependent perturbation theory, with the Hamiltonian changing from an initial potential (including the neck) to a final potential (fragments only). The spin-orbit interaction is included as a small correction to the perturbation operator, and the resulting angular distribution is expanded in Legendre polynomials, yielding an asymmetry term proportional to the cosine of the azimuthal angle.

## Key numbers
- Measured asymmetry D = -(2.52 ± 0.14) × 10^{-3} for 233U and D = +(0.83 ± 0.11) × 10^{-3} for 235U, from experiments with cold polarized neutrons.
- Naive classical estimate using the nuclear magneton gives D ~ (e^2/hbar c)(c/v)(hbar/mcR)^2, where e^2/hbar c = 1/137 is the fine structure constant.
- Nuclear spin-orbit estimate gives D ~ (c/v)(hbar/mcR)^2; taking R ~ 10^2 hbar/mc ~ 10^{-12} cm and v ~ 10^{-2} c yields D ~ 10^{-2}.
- The maximum of the angular distribution of alpha-particles with respect to the light fragment momentum falls at angle theta = 82 degrees due to Coulomb repulsion from the heavy fragment.

## Context
This work extends the study of T-odd correlations in nuclear fission, following experimental observations by the author's collaborators (cited as references 1-3) of asymmetry in ternary fission of 233U and 235U. The author notes that a similar left-right asymmetry (T-odd correlation between neutron spin, neutron momentum, and light fragment momentum) has been known in binary fission by polarized slow neutrons (reference 4). The proposed mechanism parallels spin-orbit interactions in elastic scattering of transversely polarized particles, where the correlation arises from nuclear spin-orbit interaction ~ s·l. The model enables a quantum framework for calculating angular and energy distributions of alpha-particles in ternary fission, though the author acknowledges that neglecting fragment motion limits quantitative agreement with experiment.
