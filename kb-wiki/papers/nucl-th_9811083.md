# Boson expansion methods applied to a two-level model in the study of multiple giant resonances

> We apply boson expansion methods to an extended Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model including anharmonicities in analogy with previous microscopic calculations. We study the effects of different approximations present in these calculations, among which the truncation of the hamiltonian and of the space, in connection with the study of the properties of two-phonon and three-phonon states. By comparing the approximate results on the spectrum with the exact ones we conclude that the approximations made in the microscopic calculations on two-phonon states are well justified. We find also that a good agreement with the exact results for the three-phonon state is obtained by using a bosonic hamiltonian truncated at the fourth order. This result makes us confident that such approximation can be used in realistic calculations, thus allowing a theoretical study of triple excitations of giant resonances.

## Key claim

By comparing approximate boson-expansion results with exact solutions of an extended Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, the authors conclude that the approximations used in previous microscopic calculations of two-phonon states are well justified, and that a bosonic hamiltonian truncated at fourth order and diagonalized in a space including up to three-phonon states yields good agreement with exact results for the third excited state, making such an approximation feasible for realistic studies of triple giant resonances.

## Method

The authors extended the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model by adding terms quadratic in the SU(2) quasi-spin operators to simulate anharmonicities from residual interactions neglected in earlier microscopic calculations. They applied the Holstein-Primakoff boson mapping to this fermionic hamiltonian, truncating the boson expansion at second order (RPA) and at fourth order, then diagonalized the resulting bosonic hamiltonians in spaces containing up to two-phonon and up to three-phonon states. The approximate energy eigenvalues for the first three excited states were compared with the exact eigenvalues of the original fermionic hamiltonian for various values of the anharmonicity parameters \(V_3\) and \(V_4\).

## Key numbers

* For the chosen parameters (\(\varepsilon = 41/A^{1/3}\) MeV, \(V_1 = 1.2\) MeV, \(\Omega = 8\)), the RPA energy difference between successive states is 15.11 MeV, independent of \(n\), \(V_3\), and \(V_4\).
* The exact fermionic hamiltonian with \(V_3 = V_4 = 0\) gives first three energy differences of 15.62 MeV, 14.38 MeV, and 13.03 MeV, respectively.
* When the quartic bosonic hamiltonian is diagonalized in the two-phonon space, the discrepancy for the second excited state is not more than 250 keV.
* For the third excited state (excitation energy about 40 MeV), the approximate results from the quartic hamiltonian in the three-phonon space differ from the exact ones by about 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach of Volpe, Chomaz, Andres, Catara, and Lanza (1996), who applied boson mapping techniques to realistic nuclear calculations of anharmonicities in double giant resonances, but only considered one- and two-phonon states. It also builds on the schematic anharmonic oscillator model of Volpe, Chomaz, Andres, Catara, and Lanza (1994), which did not include Pauli blocking. By using the exactly solvable LMG model, the authors test the validity of truncating the boson expansion at fourth order and of using RPA amplitudes in the quartic hamiltonian, finding these approximations reliable. This enables future theoretical studies of triple giant resonance excitations, for which experimental interest exists.
