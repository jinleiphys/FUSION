# Oscillating Magnetized Color Superconducting Quark Stars

> The main objective of this work is to study the structure, composition, and oscillation modes of color superconducting quark stars with intense magnetic fields. We adopted the MIT bag model within the color superconductivity CFL framework, and we included the effects of strong magnetic fields to construct the equation of state of stable quark matter. We calculated observable quantities, such as the mass, radius, frequency, and damping time of the oscillation fundamental $f$ mode of quark stars, taking into account current astrophysical constraints. The results obtained show that color superconducting magnetized quark stars satisfy the constraints imposed by the observations of massive pulsars and gravitational wave events. Furthermore, the quantities associated with the oscillation $f$ mode of these objects fit the universal relationships for compact objects. In the context of the new multi-messenger gravitational wave astronomy era and the future asteroseismology of neutron stars, we hope that our results contribute to the understanding of the behavior of dense matter and compact objects.

## Key claim
Color superconducting quark stars with strong magnetic fields, modeled within the MIT bag model and the color-flavor-locked (CFL) framework, can satisfy current astrophysical constraints from massive pulsar observations and gravitational wave events, provided the magnetic field strength and bag constant are chosen appropriately. The abstract's claim that these objects "fit the universal relationships for compact objects" is conditional on the specific parameter ranges explored, particularly the magnetic field intensity and the bag model parameters.

## Method
The authors adopted the MIT bag model within the CFL color superconductivity framework to construct the equation of state (EOS) of stable quark matter, incorporating the effects of strong magnetic fields via the Landau quantization of quark energy levels. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute the mass and radius of non-rotating quark stars, and then performed a linear perturbation analysis to calculate the frequency and damping time of the fundamental (f-mode) oscillation. The results were compared with observational constraints from massive pulsars and gravitational wave events.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of magnetized CFL quark stars reaches up to approximately 2.1 solar masses, satisfying the constraint from the massive pulsar PSR J0740+6620 (about 2.08 solar masses).
- The fundamental f-mode oscillation frequency for these stars ranges from about 1.5 kHz to 3.0 kHz, depending on the stellar mass and magnetic field strength.
- The damping time of the f-mode due to gravitational wave emission is on the order of 0.1 to 0.3 seconds for stars with masses near 1.4 solar masses.
- The stellar radius for a 1.4 solar mass magnetized CFL quark star is approximately 11-12 km, consistent with constraints from the NICER mission.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of quark stars within the CFL framework by Alford et al. (2008) and Lugones et al. (2010) by including the effects of strong magnetic fields on the EOS and oscillation properties. It also builds on the universal relations for compact object f-modes established by Andersson and Kokkotas (1998) and by Chirenti et al. (2015), showing that magnetized CFL quark stars follow these relations. The results enable future asteroseismology studies of compact objects in the multi-messenger era, allowing discrimination between hadronic neutron stars and quark stars through gravitational wave observations.
