# Velocity Dependence of Baryon Screening in a Hot Strongly Coupled Plasma

> The L-dependence of the static potential between Nc quarks arranged in a circle of radius L (a "baryon") immersed in the hot plasma of a gauge theory with Nc colors defines a screening length Ls. We use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute this screening length for the case of heavy quarks in the plasma of strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory moving with velocity v relative to the baryon. We find that in the v -> 1 limit, Ls \propto (1-v^2)^{1/4}/T, and find that corrections to this velocity dependence are small at lower velocities. This result provides evidence for the robustness of the analogous behavior of the screening length defined by the static quark-antiquark pair, which has been computed previously and in QCD is relevant to quarkonium physics in heavy ion collisions. Our results also show that as long as the hot wind is not blowing precisely perpendicular to the plane of the baryon configuration that we analyze, the Nc different quarks are not all affected by the wind velocity to the same degree, with those quarks lying perpendicular to the wind direction screened most effectively.

## Key claim
The screening length \(L_s\) for a baryon configuration (Nc quarks arranged in a circle) in a hot strongly coupled plasma moving with velocity \(v\) relative to the baryon scales as \(L_s \propto (1-v^2)^{1/4}/T\) in the ultrarelativistic limit, with corrections to this scaling being small at lower velocities; this provides evidence that the same velocity dependence previously found for quark-antiquark pairs is robust even when a D5-brane (baryon vertex) is introduced.

## Method
The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a baryon as Nc heavy external quarks arranged in a circle of radius L in N=4 super Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling and finite temperature, with the plasma moving at velocity v relative to the baryon. In the gravity dual, each quark is connected by a fundamental string to a D5-brane (the baryon vertex) located at a radial position re in the AdS black hole metric boosted to describe a wind; the total action (Nambu-Goto strings plus D5-brane) is extremized to find static configurations, and the screening length Ls is defined as the maximum L for which such a connected configuration exists.

## Key numbers
- At zero velocity, the baryon screening length is \(L_s(0,T) = 0.094/T\) (comparable to half the meson screening length \(L_s^{\text{meson}}(0,T) = 0.28/T\)).
- For wind perpendicular to the baryon plane, in the \(v \to 1\) limit the screening length is \(L_s(v,T) = 0.083\,(1-v^2)^{1/4}/T\).
- The numerical prefactor varies monotonically from 0.094 at v=0 to 0.083 at v=1, making \(L_s(v,T) \simeq L_s(0,T) (1-v^2)^{1/4}\) a good approximation.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of velocity-dependent screening from quark-antiquark pairs (Liu, 2006; Liu, 2006; Peeters, 2006) to baryon configurations, testing the robustness of the \((1-v^2)^{1/4}\) scaling in a system that includes a D5-brane (baryon vertex), a qualitatively new element compared to the meson case. The results support the interpretation that a moving dipole experiences an effective temperature \(T\sqrt{\gamma}\) due to the blueshifted energy density, and suggest that baryon suppression in heavy ion collisions could depend on transverse momentum similarly to quarkonium suppression, as previously argued by Liu (2006) and Ejaz (2007). The zero-velocity baryon screening length was computed earlier by Brandhuber (1998) for the same circular configuration.
