# Complete calculation of exclusive heavy vector meson production at next-to-leading order in the dipole picture

> Exclusive production of transversely polarized heavy vector mesons in deep inelastic scattering at high energy is calculated at next-to-leading order accuracy in the Color Glass Condensate framework. In addition to the first QCD correction proportional to the strong coupling constant $α_s$, we systematically also include the first relativistic correction proportional to the heavy quark velocity squared $v^2$. When combined with our previously published results for longitudinal vector meson production at next-to-leading order accuracy, these results make phenomenological calculations of heavy vector meson production possible at the order $\mathcal{O}(α_s v^0, α_s^0 v^2)$. When applied to $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ and $Υ$ production at HERA and at the LHC, a good agreement between the next-to-leading order calculations and experimental data is found. Additionally, we demonstrate that vector meson production can provide additional constraints compared to structure function analyses when the nonperturbative initial condition for the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation is extracted.

## Key claim

The authors present the first complete next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of transversely polarized exclusive heavy vector meson production in the Color Glass Condensate framework, systematically including both the first QCD correction proportional to the strong coupling constant αs and the first relativistic correction proportional to the heavy quark velocity squared v², enabling phenomenological calculations at order O(αs v⁰, αs⁰ v²) when combined with their previously published longitudinal results.

## Method

The calculation uses the dipole picture where the virtual photon splits into a quark-antiquark dipole (or a qq̅g state at NLO) that interacts with the target via Wilson lines, with the dipole-target scattering amplitude evolved using the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation including running coupling and resummation schemes (KCBK, ResumBK, TBK). The NLO light-front wave functions for the transverse photon with massive quarks from Beuf et al. (2021, 2022) and the NLO heavy vector meson wave function in the nonrelativistic limit from Escobedo et al. (2019) are combined, with the initial condition for the BK evolution fitted to HERA structure function data by Beuf et al. (2020).

## Key numbers

- J/ψ and Υ production at HERA and the LHC show good agreement between NLO calculations and experimental data
- The running coupling uses Λ_QCD = 0.241 GeV, β₀ = (11N_c − 2N_F)/3 with N_F = 3, and parameters μ₀/Λ_QCD = 2.5, c = 0.2
- The evolution rapidity for TBK uses the relation x_ℙ ≈ (M_V² + Q²)/(W² + Q²) where M_V is the meson mass
- The target transverse momentum scale for the TBK rapidity shift is set to Q₀² = 1 GeV²

## Context

This work completes the NLO calculation of exclusive heavy vector meson production initiated by the authors in previous publications: relativistic corrections ∼ v² αs⁰ were determined in Lappi et al. (2020) and NLO corrections ∼ v⁰ αs to longitudinally polarized production were calculated in Mäntysaari and Penttala (2021). The results enable phenomenological comparisons to HERA data (ZEUS, H1 collaborations) and LHC ultra-peripheral collision data (ALICE, LHCb collaborations), and demonstrate that vector meson production can provide additional constraints beyond structure function analyses when extracting the nonperturbative initial condition for the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation.
