# Field Redefinitions and Wave Function Renormalization to $O(p^4)$ in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Mass- and wave-function renormalization is calculated to order $p^4$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. Two different schemes used in the literature are considered. Several technical issues like field redefinitions, non-transformation of sources as well as subtleties related to the definition of the baryon propagator are discussed. The nucleon axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ is calculated to order $p^4$ as an illustrative example.

## Key claim

The paper calculates mass and wave function renormalization to order \(p^4\) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) for two different lagrangean formulations (BKKM and EM), showing that although intermediate steps differ, the final physical results for the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) agree between the two schemes, as expected.

## Method

The authors compute the nucleon self-energy to one-loop order at \(O(p^4)\) using both the BKKM and EM versions of the HBChPT effective lagrangean. They introduce a new equation-of-motion (EOM) field transformation performed on the relativistic nucleon fields, which yields the simpler EM lagrangean while allowing direct evaluation of the wave function renormalization constant \(Z_N\). The nucleon mass, wave function renormalization, and axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) are extracted from the pole of the relativistic nucleon propagator.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass renormalization: \(m_N = m - \frac{4M^2 a_3}{m} - \frac{M^3}{2(4\pi F)^2}\left[3\pi \ga^2 + \left(32d - \frac{3}{2}a_2 + \frac{21\ga^2}{16}\right)\frac{M}{m}\right] + O(p^5)\), where \(M\) is the pion mass, \(F = 0.093\) GeV, and \(\ga\) is the axial-vector decay constant in the chiral limit.
- Wave function renormalization in the EM scheme: \(Z_N^{\rm EM} = 1 - \frac{3\ga^2 M^2}{2(4\pi F)^2}\left[1 + 3\left(\ln\frac{M}{\mu} + (4\pi)^2 L(\mu)\right) - \frac{3\pi}{2}\frac{M}{m}\right] + O(p^4)\).
- Wave function renormalization in the BKKM scheme: \(Z_N^{\rm BKKM} = 1 - \frac{3\ga^2 M^2}{2(4\pi F)^2} + \frac{9\pi \ga^2 M^3}{4 m (4\pi F)^2} + O(p^4)\).
- Axial-vector coupling constant: \(g_A = \ga + \frac{M^2}{(4\pi F)^2}(4b - \ga^3) + \frac{M^3}{m(4\pi F)^2}\frac{2\pi}{3}\left[\ga(1 - 8a_1 + 8a_5) + \frac{11}{2}\ga^3\right]\), with counter term couplings \(a_1 = -2.6 \pm 0.7\) and \(a_5 = 3.3 \pm 0.8\).
- The \(M^3\) correction to \(g_A\) is \(\Delta g_A|_{M^3} = 0.32 \pm 0.05\) using input parameters \(g_A = 1.26\), \(M = 0.14\) GeV, \(m = 0.939\) GeV, and \(F = 0.093\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the HBChPT calculations of nucleon properties to order \(p^4\), building on the framework established by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and Bernard, Kaiser, Kambor, and Meissner (1992). It addresses subtleties in wave function renormalization previously discussed by Ecker and Mojzis (1997) and provides the renormalized parameters needed for complete \(O(p^4)\) calculations of one-nucleon Green functions. The results enable systematic use of the simpler EM lagrangean from Ecker and Mojzis (1996) in future HBChPT calculations, and the large \(M^3\) correction to \(g_A\) (0.32) indicates that lower-order calculations may carry significant uncertainties from finite renormalization effects.
