# Multiplicity fluctuations and rapidity correlations in ultracentral proton-nucleus collisions

> A collision between a proton and a heavy nucleus at ultrarelativistic energy creates particles whose rapidity distribution is asymmetric, with more particles emitted in the direction of the nucleus than in the direction of the proton. This asymmetry becomes more pronounced as the centrality estimator, defined from the energy deposited in a calorimeter, increases. We argue that for high-multiplicity collisions, the variation of the impact parameter plays a negligible role, and that the fluctuations of the multiplicity and of the centrality estimator are dominated by quantum fluctuations, whose probability distribution can be well approximated by a correlated gamma distribution. We show that this simple model reproduces existing data, and we make quantitative predictions for collisions in the $0-0.1\%$ and $0-0.01\%$ centrality windows. We argue that by repeating the same analysis with a different centrality estimator, one can obtain direct information about the rapidity decorrelation in particle production.

# Multiplicity fluctuations and rapidity correlations in ultracentral proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of the multiplicity in ultracentral p+Pb collisions is not driven by variation of impact parameter but instead reflects the correlation between multiplicity and the centrality estimator (transverse energy), which is a manifestation of long-range rapidity correlations; this holds for the approximately 10% most central collisions where impact parameter variation can be ignored.

## Method

The authors model the joint probability distribution of charged-particle multiplicity per unit pseudorapidity (dNch/dη) and transverse energy (ET) at zero impact parameter as a correlated gamma distribution, which generalizes the negative binomial distribution. They determine the parameters of this distribution by fitting ATLAS data from the 0-1% and 1-5% centrality bins in p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, using the centrality classifier defined by ET deposited in a backward calorimeter (−4.9 < η < −3.1). The average multiplicity at fixed ET is then computed analytically from the correlated gamma distribution, including leading non-Gaussian corrections.

## Key numbers

- The knee of the ET distribution (average ET at b=0) is 60 ± 2 GeV, with standard deviation σ_ET = 24 ± 2 GeV, reconstructed from Bayesian analysis of the ET distribution in p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV.
- Events above the knee represent 7.5% of all p+Pb collisions, compared to only 0.3% in Pb+Pb collisions.
- The dimensionless centrality dependence strength −d ln(ET)/dc|c=0 is 1 in p+Pb collisions versus 4 in Pb+Pb collisions.
- Predictions are made for the 0-0.1% and 0-0.01% centrality windows, corresponding to ET ≈ 133 GeV and ET ≈ 160 GeV respectively.
- When using half of the backward calorimeter (−4.9 < η < −4.0) instead of the full calorimeter, the average dNch/dη for the 1% most central events is reduced to 97-98% of the nominal value.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of centrality fluctuations in small systems pioneered by Rogly (2018) and Pepin (2022), who reconstructed the ET distribution at b=0 in p+Pb collisions using Bayesian methods. The correlated gamma distribution framework was introduced by Pepin (2022) to describe joint fluctuations of multiplicity and transverse energy. The paper argues that repeating the analysis with a different centrality classifier (e.g., covering a different rapidity range) would allow disentangling the Pearson correlation coefficient r from the multiplicity fluctuation σ_Nch, providing direct access to rapidity decorrelation, a phenomenon closely related to the longitudinal decorrelation of azimuthal correlations studied by CMS (2015) and ATLAS (2017). This approach offers a model-independent way to extract information about rapidity correlations without relying on specific microscopic models such as AMPT, EPOS, or Angantyr.
