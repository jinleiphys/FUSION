# Nonexistence of a $Λnn$ bound state

> It has been recently suggested the existence of a neutral bound state of two neutrons and a $Λ$ hyperon, $^3_Λn$. We point out that using either simple separable potentials or a full-fledged calculation with realistic baryon-baryon interactions derived from the constituent quark cluster model there is no possibility for the existence of such a $Λnn$ bound state. For this purpose, we performed a full Faddeev calculation of the $Λnn$ system in the $(I,J^P)=(1,1/2^+)$ channel using the interactions derived from the constituent quark cluster model which describe well the two-body $NN$ and $NY$ data and the $Λnp$ hypertriton.

# Nonexistence of a Λnn bound state

## Key claim

The Λnn system in the (I,J^P) = (1,1/2^+) channel is unbound by at least 5–10 MeV, contradicting the recent suggestion by the HypHI Collaboration of a neutral bound state of two neutrons and a Λ hyperon. This conclusion holds for both simple separable potentials and a full Faddeev calculation using realistic baryon-baryon interactions from the constituent quark cluster model.

## Method

The authors performed a full Faddeev calculation of the Λnn system in the (I,J^P) = (1,1/2^+) channel, using baryon-baryon interactions derived from the constituent quark cluster model that includes confinement, one-gluon exchange, and pseudoscalar and scalar boson exchanges from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The calculation takes full account of the ΛNN – ΣNN coupling and the tensor force coupling S and D waves, with a total of 21 coupled channels contributing to the state. The Fredholm determinant at zero energy was evaluated for twelve different models corresponding to different choices of the ΛN spin-singlet and spin-triplet scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- The Fredholm determinant at zero energy D_F(0) for the (I,J^P) = (1,1/2^+) state ranges between 0.38 and 0.42 for the realistic quark model interactions, and between 0.46 and 0.59 for separable potentials from the Nijmegen group.
- The Λnn system is unbound by at least 5–10 MeV, compared to the 0.13 MeV binding energy of the hypertriton (Λnp).
- The ΛN spin-triplet scattering length is constrained to 1.41 ≤ a_{1/2,1} ≤ 1.58 fm, based on the nonexistence of a bound (I,J^P) = (0,3/2^+) ΛNN state and fits to hyperon-nucleon cross sections.
- The ΛN spin-singlet scattering length is constrained to 2.37 ≤ a_{1/2,0} ≤ 2.48 fm, obtained by requiring the hypertriton binding energy to be B = 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Dalitz and Downs (1958) using a variational approach that the Λnn system should be unbound, and the authors' own previous Faddeev calculation with separable potentials (Garcilazo and Valcarce, 2014). It contradicts the experimental claim by the HypHI Collaboration (2013) of a Λnn bound state based on invariant mass distributions from the reaction 6Li + 12C at 2A GeV. The results enable future experimental searches to focus on alternative explanations for the observed signal in the d+π− and t+π− final states.
