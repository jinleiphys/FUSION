# Peculiarities of sub-barrier fusion with quantum diffusion approach

> With the quantum diffusion approach the unexpected behavior of fusion cross section, angular momentum, and astrophysical S-factor at sub-barrier energies has been revealed. Out of the region of short-range nuclear interaction and action of friction at turning point the decrease rate of the cross section under the barrier becomes smaller. The calculated results for the reactions with spherical nuclei are in a good agreement with the existing experimental data.

# Peculiarities of sub-barrier fusion with quantum diffusion approach

## Key claim

The decrease rate of sub-barrier fusion cross sections changes at energies about 3.5–5.0 MeV below the Coulomb barrier because the external turning point moves outside the region where nuclear forces and friction act, but the authors note that experimental uncertainties at these extreme sub-barrier energies remain large and the effect requires further experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors apply a quantum diffusion approach based on a quantum master-equation for the reduced density matrix, treating the relative distance between colliding nuclei as a single collective variable with friction and diffusion terms that model coupling to internal degrees of freedom. The capture probability is obtained by integrating a propagator for an inverted oscillator that approximates the nucleus-nucleus potential, using a non-Markovian formalism with a dissipation kernel and a random force. Two regimes are distinguished: when the external turning point lies beyond the interaction radius, friction is negligible; when it lies inside, friction hinders the classical motion.

## Key numbers

- Friction coefficient: \(\hbar\lambda = 2\) MeV
- Internal-excitation width: \(\hbar\gamma = 15\) MeV
- Nuclear radius parameter: \(r_0 = 1.15\) fm
- Diffuseness parameter: \(a = 0.55\) fm for \(^{208}\)Pb, \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{22}\)Ne; \(a = 0.53\) fm for \(^{16}\)O
- Interaction radius defined as \(R_{int} = R_b + 1.1\) fm, where nucleon density reaches about 10% of saturation density
- Change of fall rate occurs at energies about 3.5–5.0 MeV below the Coulomb barrier
- The pocket in the nucleus-nucleus potential is washed out at angular momenta \(J > 80\)
- Energy increment of 0.2 MeV is required to extract the logarithmic derivative with its narrow maximum

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously developed by the same group (Sargsyan et al., earlier papers) to the extreme sub-barrier energy region, where conventional coupled-channel calculations with realistic parameters fail to describe capture cross sections (Dasgupta et al., 2007). The predicted change in fall rate and the minimum in average angular momentum are consistent with experimental indications from reactions such as \(^{16}\)O+\(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{22}\)Ne+\(^{208}\)Pb (Tribble et al., Oganessian et al., Trzaska et al.), and the results suggest that the origin of the steep fall-off of fusion cross sections is partly attributed to deviation of the internuclear potential from the Woods-Saxon shape, as proposed by Esbensen (2005). The findings enable improved predictions for astrophysical S-factors and angular anisotropy of fission products following fusion.
