# Hadronic dissipative effects on transverse dynamics at RHIC

> We simulate the dynamics of Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) with a hybrid model that treats the quark-gluon plasma macroscopically as an ideal fluid, but models the hadron resonance gas microscopically using a hadronic cascade. We find that much of the mass-ordering pattern for v_2(p_T) observed at RHIC is generated during the hadronic stage due to build-up of additional radial flow. We also find that the mass-ordering pattern is violated for phi meson due to small interaction cross section in the hadron resonance gas.

# Hadronic dissipative effects on transverse dynamics at RHIC

## Key claim
The mass-ordering pattern for elliptic flow v2(pT) observed at RHIC is largely generated during the hadronic stage due to build-up of additional radial flow, but this pattern is violated for phi mesons because their small interaction cross section in the hadron resonance gas prevents them from participating in that additional radial flow.

## Method
The authors simulate Au+Au collisions at RHIC using a hybrid model that treats the quark-gluon plasma as an ideal fluid in three spatial dimensions, then switches to the JAM hadronic cascade at a switching temperature of 169 MeV. The hadronic cascade is initialized with hadrons distributed according to the hydrodynamic output via the Cooper-Frye formula, and phi mesons are stabilized by turning off their decay channels during the cascade.

## Key numbers
- Switching temperature from ideal hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade: Tsw = 169 MeV
- Critical temperature for QGP phase transition: Tc = 170 MeV
- For protons and phi mesons, v2^p(pT) < v2^phi(pT) in the range 0 < pT < 1.2 GeV/c, violating the mass-ordering expectation since m_phi > m_p
- The total hadronic cross section follows the additive quark model formula: sigma_tot = sigma_NN^tot * (n1/3)*(n2/3)*(1-0.4*ns1/n1)*(1-0.4*ns2/n2), where n_i is the number of constituent quarks and n_si is the number of strange quarks

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous hybrid model studies (Hirano, Heinz, Kharzeev, Lacey, Nara 2006, 2007) that showed "late viscosity" accounts for deviations from ideal hydrodynamics. The results demonstrate that the large integrated v2 and strong mass ordering observed at RHIC arise from interplay between perfect fluid dynamics of the QGP stage and dissipative dynamics of the hadronic stage. The finding that phi mesons violate mass ordering due to weak interactions provides a specific observable signature of hadronic dissipative effects, enabling experimental tests of the transition from ideal fluid to hadronic cascade behavior.
