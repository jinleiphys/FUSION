# Simulation of Transitions between "Pasta" Phases in Dense Matter

> Calculations of equilibrium properties of dense matter predict that at subnuclear densities nuclei can be rodlike or slablike. To investigate whether transitions between phases with non-spherical nuclei can occur during the collapse of a star, we perform quantum molecular dynamic simulations of the compression of dense matter. We have succeeded in simulating the transitions between rodlike and slablike nuclei and between slablike nuclei and cylindrical bubbles. Our results strongly suggest that non-spherical nuclei can be formed in the inner cores of collapsing stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates through quantum molecular dynamics simulations that transitions between non-spherical nuclear "pasta" phases (rodlike to slablike, and slablike to cylindrical bubbles) can occur dynamically during the adiabatic compression of dense matter, strongly suggesting that such phases can be formed in the inner cores of collapsing stars, though the simulations are performed at a compression rate that may be artificially fast.

## Method

The authors performed quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations of symmetric nuclear matter with 16384 nucleons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using a Hamiltonian with a momentum-dependent Pauli potential to reproduce Pauli principle effects. Starting from equilibrated samples of the columnar phase (rodlike nuclei) at density 0.225 ρ₀ and the laminar phase (slablike nuclei) at 0.4 ρ₀ at T ≈ 1 MeV, they simulated adiabatic compression by increasing density in small steps (2×10⁻⁴ ρ₀ or 1×10⁻⁴ ρ₀ every 100 steps) and analyzed the structural evolution using integral mean curvature and Euler characteristic of the nucleon density field on 128³ grid points.

## Key numbers

- System size: 16384 nucleons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions
- Initial densities: 0.225 ρ₀ for rodlike nuclei phase, 0.4 ρ₀ for slablike nuclei phase (where ρ₀ = 0.165 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear density)
- Compression rate: ≈ 1.3×10⁻⁵ ρ₀/(fm/c) for rodlike-to-slablike transition; ≈ 7.1×10⁻⁶ ρ₀/(fm/c) for slablike-to-cylindrical holes transition
- Final densities: 0.405 ρ₀ for slablike nuclei phase; 0.490 ρ₀ for cylindrical bubbles phase
- Temperature in final states: ≈ 1.35 MeV (rodlike-to-slablike); ≈ 1.3 MeV (slablike-to-cylindrical holes)
- Connecting stage duration: 3000–4000 fm/c for both transitions
- Relaxation stage duration: more than 8000 fm/c (rodlike-to-slablike); more than 15000 fm/c (slablike-to-cylindrical holes)
- Individual fusion processes proceed on time scales of order 10² fm/c
- Integral mean curvature plateau values: ≈ 4000 fm for rodlike nuclei; ≈ 0 fm for slablike nuclei; ≈ -2000 fm for cylindrical holes

## Context

This work extends previous equilibrium calculations of pasta phases (Lassaut et al.; Watanabe et al. 2003) and static perturbative studies (Iida) by providing the first dynamical simulation of transitions between non-spherical nuclear shapes during compression, addressing whether such phases can form during the less-than-one-second timescale of stellar collapse. The results support earlier suggestions by Watanabe et al. and Horowitz et al. that pasta phases could significantly increase neutrino opacity in supernova cores through coherent scattering (Freedman; Sato), potentially affecting shock revival mechanisms. A remaining challenge identified by the authors is to simulate the transition from bcc lattice of spherical nuclei to triangular lattice of rodlike nuclei to establish the existence of pasta phases in supernova cores.
