# In-medium omega-meson broadening and S-wave pion annihilation into e^+e^- pairs

> An $ω$-meson in motion with respect to a nuclear medium can couple to a $σ$-meson through a particle-hole excitation. This coupling is large. We investigate its consequences for the width of $ω$-mesons in matter and for the s-wave annihilation of pions into lepton pairs which can take place in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We find that the two pion decay of $ω$-mesons, resulting from the $ω-σ$ transition and the subsequent $2π$ decay of the $σ$-meson, leads to a substantial broadening of $ω$-mesons in matter and possibly to an observable effect in experiments measuring the $e^+e^-$ decay of vector mesons produced in nuclei and in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The inverse process, the s-wave annihilation of pions into $ω$-mesons decaying into $e^+e^-$ pairs, has in general a much smaller cross section than the corresponding p-wave annihilation through $ρ$-mesons and is expected to contribute rather little to the total $e^+e^-$ pair production in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the in-medium mixing of the omega-meson with the sigma-meson through particle-hole excitations leads to a substantial broadening of the omega-meson in nuclear matter, but this effect is conditional on the omega-meson having non-zero momentum relative to the medium; at zero momentum the mixing is forbidden and the broadening vanishes.

## Method

The authors calculate the omega-sigma polarization operator in nuclear matter at finite density and temperature using a one-loop approximation with nucleon-hole intermediate states, treating nuclear matter as a system of noninteracting fermions in the relativistic mean-field approximation. They fit the sigma-pi-pi coupling constant and sigma-meson mass to s-wave pi-pi scattering phase shifts using a model where the sigma-meson dominates the s-channel, including both pion and kaon loops with Pauli-Villars regularization. The in-medium omega width from the omega-to-sigma-to-two-pion decay channel is then computed, along with the cross section for s-wave pion annihilation into e+e- pairs through the same mixing mechanism.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3) and zero temperature, the in-medium omega width from the omega-sigma mixing mechanism is 27 MeV for an omega 3-momentum of 0.5 GeV/c, using a nucleon effective mass M*_N(rho_0) = 0.7 M_N.
- Using M*_N(rho_0) = 0.8 M_N instead reduces the in-medium width by about 8 MeV at rho = rho_0.
- The free omega width is (8.43 +/- 0.10) MeV, corresponding to a propagation length of c*tau_omega = 23.4 fm in free space.
- The free rho width is (151.2 +/- 1.2) MeV, corresponding to c*tau_rho = 1.3 fm.
- The sigma-meson mass is fitted to m_sigma = 0.84 GeV, with g_sigma_pi_pi = 12.8, g_sigma_KK = 8.68, and a form factor cutoff Lambda = 1.4 GeV.
- The difference m_omega^2 - m_sigma^2 = -0.094 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of omega-sigma mixing in matter by Celenza and collaborators, and is motivated by the suggestion of Brown and Rho that in-medium vector meson masses could be order parameters for chiral symmetry restoration at large baryon density. The paper provides a quantitative calculation of the omega-meson broadening mechanism that complements earlier estimates of collisional broadening from the omega N to pi N reaction (about 10 MeV at normal nuclear density, as found by Friman using detailed balance from measured pi N to omega N cross sections by Karami). The results are relevant for planned measurements of e+e- spectra from vector mesons produced in nuclei at TJNAF and GSI (HADES), and for understanding the relative importance of s-wave versus p-wave pion annihilation in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
