# Shear Viscosity of a Gluon Plasma in Perturbative QCD

> We calculate the shear viscosity $η$ to entropy density $s$ ratio $η/s$ of a gluon plasma in kinetic theory including the gg->gg and gg<->ggg processes. Due to the suppressed contribution to $η$ in the gg->gg forward scattering, it is known that the gluon bremsstrahlung gg<->ggg process also contributes at the same order $O(α_{s}^{-2})$ in perturbative QCD. Using the Gunion-Bertsch formula for the gg<->ggg matrix element which is valid for the limit of soft bremsstrahlung, we find that the result is sensitive to whether the same limit is taken for the phase space. Using the exact phase space, the gg<->ggg contribution becomes more important to $η$ than gg->gg for $α_{s}\gtrsim 2\times 10^{-3}$. Therefore, at $α_{s}=0.1$, $η/s\simeq 1.0$, between 2.7 obtained by Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (AMY) and 0.5 obtained by Xu and Greiner. If the soft bremsstrahlung limit is imposed on the phase space such that the recoil effect from the bremsstrahlung gluon is neglected, then the correction from the gg<->ggg process is about 10-30% of the total which is close to AMY's prediction. This shows that the soft bremsstrahlung approximation is not as good as previously expected.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of a gluon plasma depends sensitively on whether the soft bremsstrahlung limit is applied to the phase space of the three-gluon state, not just to the matrix element; the Gunion-Bertsch formula's soft-bremsstrahlung approximation is less reliable than previously thought.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann equation variationally for a gluon plasma, including both elastic \(gg\rightarrow gg\) and inelastic \(gg\leftrightarrow ggg\) processes, using Bose-Einstein statistics. They employed the Gunion-Bertsch formula for the \(gg\rightarrow ggg\) matrix element, which is valid for soft gluon bremsstrahlung, and compared results using either the exact three-body-like phase space or the two-body-like phase space that neglects the recoil of the bremsstrahlung gluon. The Debye mass \(m_D\) or the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect was used to regulate infrared divergences.

## Key numbers

- At \(\alpha_s = 0.1\), the full result \(\eta/s \simeq 1.0\) for the three-body-like phase space, which lies between the value 2.7 obtained by Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (AMY) and 0.5 obtained by Xu and Greiner (XG).
- The \(gg\leftrightarrow ggg\) contribution becomes more important than \(gg\rightarrow gg\) for \(\alpha_s \gtrsim 2 \times 10^{-3}\) when using the three-body-like phase space.
- For the two-body-like phase space, the correction from the \(gg\leftrightarrow ggg\) process is about 10-30% of the total, close to AMY's prediction.
- At \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) and 0.6, \(\eta_{22+23}/s \simeq 0.22\) and 0.15, respectively, for the three-body-like phase space.
- The leading-log result gives \(\eta_{LL}/s \simeq 3.9 / [g^4 \ln(1/g)]\) for non-interacting gluon entropy density.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculation of \(\eta/s\) for a gluon plasma, addressing the discrepancy between the results of Xu and Greiner (2007), who found a large contribution from \(gg\leftrightarrow ggg\) using a parton cascade model with Boltzmann gas statistics, and those of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2003), who found a small contribution using a variational method with Bose-Einstein statistics. The authors show that the choice of phase space for the three-gluon state (three-body-like vs. two-body-like) dramatically changes the result, and that using the exact phase space yields \(\eta/s\) values between the two prior calculations. This work enables future studies using the exact matrix element for \(gg\rightarrow ggg\) to resolve the correct treatment of the phase space.
