# Is a Trineutron Resonance Lower in Energy than a Tetraneutron Resonance?

> We present quantum Monte Carlo calculations of few-neutron systems confined in external potentials based on local chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. The energy and radial densities for these systems are calculated in different external Woods-Saxon potentials. We assume that their extrapolation to zero external-potential depth provides a quantitative estimate of three- and four-neutron resonances. The validity of this assumption is demonstrated by benchmarking with an exact diagonalization in the two-body case. We find that the extrapolated trineutron resonance, as well as the energy for shallow well depths, is lower than the tetraneutron resonance energy. This suggests that a three-neutron resonance exists below a four-neutron resonance in nature and is potentially measurable. To confirm that the relative ordering of three- and four-neutron resonances is not an artifact of the external confinement, we test that the odd-even staggering in the helium isotopic chain is reproduced within this approach. Finally, we discuss similarities between our results and ultracold Fermi gases.

## Key claim
The authors find that the extrapolated trineutron resonance energy is lower than the tetraneutron resonance energy, suggesting a three-neutron resonance may exist below a four-neutron resonance in nature and be potentially measurable. This conclusion depends on the assumption that extrapolating energies from confined systems in external Woods-Saxon potentials to zero well depth provides a quantitative estimate of few-neutron resonances.

## Method
The authors used auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of two, three, and four neutrons confined in external Woods-Saxon potentials, employing local chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in chiral effective field theory. They calculated ground-state energies for various well depths and radii, then extrapolated to zero well depth to estimate resonance energies. The method was benchmarked by exact diagonalization in a two-body model potential with a known resonance, and validated by reproducing the odd-even staggering pattern in the helium isotopic chain.

## Key numbers
- Extrapolated trineutron resonance energy: 1.1(2) MeV
- Extrapolated tetraneutron resonance energy: 2.1(2) MeV
- Energy per particle from quadratic fits: 0.37(7) MeV for three neutrons, 0.53(5) MeV for four neutrons
- Tetraneutron resonance energy from scale-factor extrapolation: 2.0(1.0) MeV, consistent with the trapped-neutron result
- Experimental candidate tetraneutron resonance energy from the double-charge-exchange reaction \isotope[4]He(\isotope[8]He,\isotope[8]Be): 0.83 +/- 0.65 (stat) +/- 1.25 (syst) MeV
- Maximum density of confined systems: approximately 0.01 fm$^{-3}$, indicating extreme diluteness

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical investigations of few-neutron systems, including Green's function Monte Carlo calculations by Pieper (2003) and no-core-shell-model calculations by Shirokov (2016), which suggested a tetraneutron resonance below about 2 MeV. It also addresses the experimental claim by Kisamori (2016) of a candidate four-neutron resonance. The results open the possibility for experimental searches of a trineutron resonance and suggest that few-neutron systems could be simulated using ultracold Fermi gases, drawing analogies to experiments with ultracold atoms by Regal (2003) and Zuern (2013).
