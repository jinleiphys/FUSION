# Bayesian inference of signatures of hyperons inside neutron stars

> The possible signatures of the presence of hyperons inside neutron stars are discussed within a Bayesian inference framework applied to a set of models based on a density-dependent relativistic mean-field description of hadronic matter. Nuclear matter properties, hypernuclei properties, and observational information are used to constrain the models. General properties of neutron stars such as the maximum mass, radius, tidal deformability, proton fraction, hyperon fraction, and speed of sound are discussed. It is shown that the two solar mass constraint imposes that neutron stars described by equations of state that include hyperons have on average a larger radius, $\gtrsim 0.5$km, and a larger tidal deformability, $\gtrsim 150$, than the stars determined from a nucleonic equation of state, while the speed of sound at the center of the star is more than 25\% smaller. If a 1.4 M$_\odot$ star with a radius $\lesssim 12.5$ km is measured it is quite improbable that a massive star described by the same model contains hyperons. A similar conclusion is drawn if a two solar mass star with a radius $\lesssim 11.5$ km or a neutron star with a mass above 2.2 M$_\odot$ is observed: the possible hyperon content of these stars is ruled out or very reduced. The hyperon presence inside neutron stars is compatible with the present NICER mass-radius observations of the pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 and the gravitational wave detection GW170817. {It is shown that if the polytropic index $γ=\partial\ln p/\partial\lnε$ takes values of the order of 1.75 at not too large densities, it may indicate the onset of some kind of exotic matter, but not necessarily of deconfined quark matter.

## Key claim

The presence of hyperons inside neutron stars that satisfy the two solar mass constraint forces the stars to have on average a larger radius (greater than or approximately 0.5 km) and a larger tidal deformability (greater than or approximately 150) than purely nucleonic stars, while the speed of sound at the center is more than 25% smaller; however, these conclusions are conditional on the specific Bayesian priors and the set of nuclear matter, hypernuclei, and observational constraints applied.

## Method

The authors used a Bayesian inference framework applied to a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDH) model of hadronic matter, generating ensembles of approximately 15,000 equations of state for three scenarios: purely nucleonic matter, matter including the Lambda hyperon, and matter including both Lambda and Xi- hyperons. The model parameters were constrained by nuclear matter saturation properties, the pure neutron matter equation of state from chiral effective field theory at N3LO, and the observational requirement that the maximum neutron star mass exceeds 2.0 solar masses, with hyperon-meson couplings fixed by hypernuclei binding energies and SU(6) symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The two solar mass constraint forces hyperonic neutron stars to have a radius larger by approximately 0.5 km and a tidal deformability larger by approximately 150 compared to nucleonic stars.
- The speed of sound at the center of hyperonic stars is more than 25% smaller than in nucleonic stars.
- If a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with a radius less than or approximately 12.5 km is measured, it is quite improbable that a massive star described by the same model contains hyperons.
- If a two solar mass star with a radius less than or approximately 11.5 km is observed, the possible hyperon content is ruled out or very reduced.
- If a neutron star with a mass above 2.2 solar masses is observed, the hyperon content is ruled out or very reduced.
- The polytropic index gamma = d ln p / d ln epsilon taking values of the order of 1.75 at not too large densities may indicate the onset of some kind of exotic matter, but not necessarily deconfined quark matter.
- The Lambda hyperon fraction can reach fractions as high as approximately 50% when only Lambda hyperons are included; the inclusion of Xi- reduces the Lambda fraction to values below 30%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fortin (2014), who compared three nucleonic and 14 hyperonic equations of state and concluded hyperonic models predicted larger radii, but those models did not satisfy chiral effective field theory constraints on pure neutron matter from Hebeler (2013). The present study uses a Bayesian inference approach similar to Traversi (2020) and Sun (2022) but additionally imposes low-density constraints from chiral effective field theory and a broader set of nuclear matter properties. The results enable future observational programs such as NICER and LIGO/Virgo to discriminate between nucleonic and hyperonic compositions by measuring neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities with sufficient precision.
