# Boundary Conditions for Three-Body Scattering in Configuration Space

> The asymptotic behavior of three-body scattering wave functions in configuration space is studied by considering a model equation that has the same asymptotic form as the Faddeev equations. Boundary conditions for the wave function are derived, and their validity is verified by numerical calculations. It is shown that these boundary conditions for the partial differential equation can be used to obtain accurate numerical solutions for the wave function.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the asymptotic boundary conditions derived for three-body scattering wave functions in configuration space, when applied to a model equation with the same asymptotic form as the Faddeev equations, yield accurate numerical solutions at matching radii on the order of 100 fm, though the breakup amplitude for small interparticle distances cannot be extracted directly from the wave function at large radii and instead requires the T-matrix integral.

## Method

The authors studied a model equation that mimics the asymptotic behavior of the Faddeev equations for three identical bosons with spin-independent s-wave pairwise interactions at zero total angular momentum, using a Bargmann two-body potential. They derived asymptotic forms for the wave function in both the deuteron (two-body bound) channel and the breakup (three-body continuum) channel by evaluating the three-body Green's function including one pair interaction, then verified these forms by numerically integrating the Green's function integral representation. Finally, they solved the partial differential equation form of the scattering equation using spline expansion methods with the derived asymptotic expressions as boundary conditions at a finite matching radius.

## Key numbers

- Matching radius of about 100 fm was found sufficient for accurate wave function solutions; the difference between the asymptotic form and the exact result in the elastic channel was less than 2% for y greater than 50 fm and less than 1% for y greater than 75 fm.
- For the model calculations, the two-body bound-state wave number was kappa = 0.2316 fm^{-1}, the Bargmann potential range parameter was lambda = 0.7 fm^{-1}, the laboratory energy was E_lab = 14 MeV, and hbar^2/m = 41.47 MeV·fm^2, giving k_0 = 0.41403 fm^{-1}.
- The asymptotic value of the correction term in the deuteron channel was f_d^corr(infinity) = -23.325 fm^{3/2}.
- For the breakup amplitude A(theta) at theta = 80 degrees, the extracted value at rho_m = 340 fm was -0.0659 + 0.1574i, compared to the exact asymptotic value of -0.0657 + 0.1560i; at theta = 60 degrees the value at rho_m = 340 fm was 0.0068 + 0.1988i versus the exact 0.0066 + 0.1990i; at theta = 30 degrees the value converged to 0.0157 + 0.0642i by rho_m = 140 fm.
- For theta = 80 degrees, the matching radius rho needed to exceed 200 fm for the asymptotic approximation to be valid.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Gloeckle and Payne, 1992) of three-body asymptotic behavior from free propagation to propagation including one pair interaction, as required by the Faddeev formulation. The derived boundary conditions enable accurate configuration-space solutions of the Faddeev equations, complementing momentum-space approaches; the authors note that configuration-space Faddeev calculations using this method gave results in excellent agreement with momentum-space calculations in a benchmark study (Friar, Payne, Gloeckle, Huber, and Witala, 1995). The results also connect to earlier work by Gloeckle (1974) on the asymptotic form of three-body wave functions.
