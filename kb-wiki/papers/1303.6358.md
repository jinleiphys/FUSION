# Infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs in variational calculations with a harmonic oscillator basis

> I abstract from a recent publication [1] the motivations for, analysis in and conclusions of a study of the ultraviolet and infrared momentum regulators induced by the necessary truncation of the model spaces formed by a variational trial wave function. This trial function is built systematically from a complete set of many-body basis states based upon three-dimensional harmonic oscillator (HO) functions. Each model space is defined by a truncation of the expansion characterized by a counting number (N) and by the intrinsic scale ($\hbarω$) of the HO basis. Extending both the uv cutoff to infinity and the ir cutoff to zero is prescribed for a converged calculation. In [1] we established practical procedures which utilize these regulators to obtain the extrapolated result from sequences of calculations with model spaces. Finally, I update this subject by mentioning recent work on our extrapolation prescriptions which have appeared since the submission of [1]. The numerical example chosen for this contribution consists of calculations of the ground state energy of the triton with the "bare" and "soft" Idaho N3LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the convergence of no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using a harmonic oscillator basis can be understood and extrapolated by treating the ultraviolet (UV) and infrared (IR) momentum cutoffs induced by basis truncation on an equal footing, rather than relying primarily on the counting number N. The key hedge is that this extrapolation scheme works provided the UV cutoff is large enough to capture the UV physics of the interaction, and that the IR cutoff definition using λ_sc (scaling cutoff) shows universal behavior for extrapolation.

## Method

The authors performed variational NCSM calculations of the triton (³H) ground state energy using the "bare" and "soft" Idaho N³LO nucleon-nucleon interaction, with basis sizes up to N=36 and various values of ℏω. They defined two pairs of UV and IR cutoffs: Λ = √(m_N(N+3/2)ℏω) for the UV cutoff, and two alternative IR cutoffs, λ = √(m_Nℏω) and λ_sc = √(m_Nℏω/(N+3/2)). They studied the convergence behavior by holding one cutoff fixed while varying the other, and performed exponential extrapolations to the IR limit (λ_sc → 0) at fixed Λ values.

## Key numbers

- The accepted ground state energy of ³H with the Idaho N³LO potential is −7.855 MeV from a 34-channel Faddeev calculation, −7.854 MeV from a hyperspherical harmonics expansion, and −7.85(1) MeV from an NCSM calculation.
- The UV regulator scale of the Idaho N³LO interaction, estimated from the reduced mass in a HO basis emulation with ℏω = 30 MeV and N = 20, is Λ^NN ∼ 780 MeV/c.
- The IR regulator scale λ_sc^NN estimated from the same emulation is ∼ 36 MeV/c.
- The extrapolated ground state energy from five fixed-Λ fits using E_gs(λ_sc) = a exp(−b/λ_sc) + E_gs(λ_sc=0) gave a mean of −7.8511 MeV with a standard deviation of 0.0011 MeV.
- The high-momentum cutoff of the Idaho N³LO regulator is set at Λ_N3LO = 500 MeV/c, but the exponential regulator form allows momentum transfers up to 2Λ_N3LO.

## Context

This work extends the convergence analysis of HO basis expansions originally studied by Delves (1972) and Schneider (1972), who established asymptotic convergence rates (inverse power laws for non-smooth potentials, exponential for smooth potentials). It builds on the introduction of UV and IR cutoffs for NCSM by the EFT approach of Coon et al. (2012) and the work of Furnstahl, Hagen, and Papenbrock on scaling properties. The paper enables practical extrapolation procedures that treat N and ℏω on an equal footing, allowing practitioners to obtain converged results from sequences of finite model-space calculations without requiring computationally prohibitive basis sizes.
