# The $K^- p \to f_1(1285) Λ$ reaction within an effective Lagrangian approach

> The production of the $f_1(1285)$ resonance in the reaction of $K^- p \to f_1(1285) Λ$ is studied within an effective Lagrangian approach. The production process is described by the $t$ channel $K^{*+}$ meson exchange. My theoretical approach is based on the results of chiral unitary theory where the $f_1(1285)$ resonance is dynamically generated from the single channel $\bar{K} K^* - c.c.$ interaction. The total and differential cross sections of the $K^- p \to f_1(1285) Λ$ reaction are evaluated. Within the coupling constant of the $f_1(1285)$ to $\bar{K}K^*$ channel obtained from the chiral unitary theory and a cut off parameter $Λ_c \sim 1.5$ GeV in the form factors, the experimental measurement can be reproduced. This production process would provide further evidence for the $\bar{K} K^* - c.c.$ nature of the $f_1(1285)$ state.

## Key claim

The total cross section of the $K^- p \to f_1(1285) \Lambda$ reaction can be reproduced by a model using only $t$-channel $K^{*+}$ exchange, provided the cutoff parameter $\Lambda_c$ in the form factors is adjusted to approximately 1.46 GeV; this agreement supports the interpretation of the $f_1(1285)$ as a dynamically generated state from the $\bar{K} K^* - c.c.$ interaction, but the calculation is sensitive to the cutoff and does not include final-state interactions or $s$-/$u$-channel contributions.

## Method

The reaction is modeled at tree level with only $t$-channel $K^{*+}$ meson exchange, using an effective Lagrangian for the $\Lambda N K^*$ vertex (vector and tensor couplings) and a contact vertex for $K^- K^{*+} f_1(1285)$ derived from chiral unitary theory. The coupling constant $g_{f_1} = 7555$ MeV for the $f_1(1285)$ to $\bar{K}K^*$ is taken from prior chiral unitary studies (Roca 2005, Aceti 2015), and monopole form factors with a common cutoff $\Lambda_c$ are applied at both vertices. The invariant amplitude is squared and integrated to obtain total and differential cross sections as functions of the center-of-mass energy $W$.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section $\sigma = 11 \pm 3$ $\mu$b at $W = 3.01$ GeV (corresponding to $p_{K^-} = 4.2$ GeV) from the experimental measurement of Gurtu (1978).
- With $\Lambda_c = 1.46$ GeV, the model yields $\sigma = 10.9$ $\mu$b at $W = 3.01$ GeV, reproducing the central experimental value.
- With $\Lambda_c = 1.3$ GeV, the calculated cross section at $W = 3.01$ GeV is $2.1$ $\mu$b; with $\Lambda_c = 1.7$ GeV, it is $50.5$ $\mu$b.
- The $f_1(1285)$ mass is taken as $M_{f_1} = 1281.9 \pm 0.5$ MeV and its total decay width as $\Gamma_{f_1} = 24.2 \pm 1.1$ MeV (Agashe 2014).
- The coupling constant used is $g_{\Lambda N K^*} = -6.41$, with $\kappa_{K^*} = 2.77$; the exchanged $K^{*+}$ mass is $m_{K^*} = 891.66$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular picture of the $f_1(1285)$ resonance, previously studied in chiral unitary approaches (Roca 2005, Aceti 2015) and in decays such as $J/\psi \to \phi f_1(1285)$ (Xie 2015), to a production reaction in kaon-nucleon scattering. It provides a first calculation of the $K^- p \to f_1(1285) \Lambda$ cross section using only $t$-channel exchange, and shows that the single available experimental datum from Gurtu (1978) can be reproduced; future measurements of total and differential cross sections could test the model and distinguish $t$-channel dominance from possible $s$- or $u$-channel contributions.
