# Theoretical study of the $α+d$ $\rightarrow$ $^6$Li + $γ$ astrophysical capture process in a three-body model

> The astrophysical capture process $α+d$ $\rightarrow$ $^6$Li + $γ$ is studied in a three-body model. The initial state is factorized into the deuteron bound state and the $α+d$ scattering state. The final nucleus $^6$Li(1+) is described as a three-body bound state $α+n+p$ in the hyperspherical Lagrange-mesh method. The contribution of the E1 transition operator from the initial isosinglet states to the isotriplet components of the final state is estimated to be negligible. An estimation of the forbidden E1 transition to the isosinglet components of the final state is comparable with the corresponding results of the two-body model. However, the contribution of the E2 transition operator is found to be much smaller than the corresponding estimations of the two-body model. The three-body model perfectly matches the new experimental data of the LUNA collaboration with the spectroscopic factor 2.586 estimated from the bound-state wave functions of $^6$Li and deuteron.

## Key claim

The three-body model of the α+d → ⁶Li+γ capture process, using a spectroscopic factor of 2.586 derived from the overlap integral of the ⁶Li and deuteron bound-state wave functions, perfectly matches the new LUNA experimental data, better than two-body models; however, the E2 transition contribution is found to be much smaller than corresponding two-body model estimations, and the E1 transition from initial isosinglet states to isotriplet components of the final state is negligible.

## Method

The initial state is factorized into the deuteron bound state (solved with the Minnesota NN potential using a Lagrange-Laguerre mesh method) and the α+d scattering state (solved with the Numerov method using a modified Dubovichenko potential). The final ⁶Li(1+) nucleus is described as a three-body α+n+p bound state using the hyperspherical Lagrange-mesh method with the same Minnesota NN potential and a renormalized Voronchev α-N potential. The E1 and E2 transition matrix elements are evaluated between the initial and final states, with the isospin operator algebra allowing E1 transitions from isosinglet initial states to isotriplet components of the final state.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron ground-state energy: Ed = -2.202 MeV, calculated with N=40 mesh points and scaling parameter hd=0.40.
- Norm square of the isotriplet component of the ⁶Li(1+) three-body wave function: 1.13 × 10⁻⁵.
- Matter r.m.s. radius of the ⁶Li ground state: 2.25 fm (with 1.4 fm for the α-particle radius), slightly lower than the experimental value of 2.32 ± 0.03 fm.
- Overlap integral Pαd = 0.3867, yielding a spectroscopic factor C²S = 1/Pαd = 2.586.
- At the resonance energy, the three-body and two-body models differ by a factor of 0.565, consistent with the square of the overlap integral I = 0.748.
- The S-wave contribution to the astrophysical S-factor from the E2 transition increases from 1×10⁻¹² MeV b to 2.02×10⁻¹² MeV b over the energy range 0.1 MeV to 1.0 MeV.
- Any spectroscopic factor value between 1.50 and 4.25 can describe the LUNA data within the error bar.

## Context

This work extends previous two-body potential models (Mukhamedzhanov 2011, Tursunov 2015) and three-body models (Descouvemont 2003, Tursunov 2006) for the α+d → ⁶Li+γ capture process, which is the main production channel for ⁶Li in Big Bang nucleosynthesis. The three-body model addresses the isospin-selection-rule problem that forbids E1 transitions in two-body models, and provides a better description of the new LUNA experimental data (LUNA collaboration 2014) at astrophysical energies (94 keV and 134 keV) than previous two-body approaches. The results enable improved predictions for the primordial ⁶Li abundance, which is currently underestimated by the BBN model by nearly three orders of magnitude compared to observations in metal-poor stars (Asplund 2006).
