# Thermodynamic Laws and Equipartition Theorem in Relativistic Brownian Motion

> We extend the stochastic energetics to a relativistic system. The thermodynamic laws and equipartition theorem are discussed for a relativistic Brownian particle and the first and the second law of thermodynamics in this formalism are derived. The relation between the relativistic equipartition relation and the rate of heat transfer is discussed in the relativistic case together with the nature of the noise term.

## Key claim

The authors extend stochastic energetics to relativistic Brownian motion, deriving the first and second laws of thermodynamics for a relativistic particle, but only under the condition that the noise term transforms separately from the force term under Lorentz boosts, which is shown to be consistent only when the heat bath is homogeneous and static.

## Method

The authors start from a Langevin equation for a relativistic Brownian particle defined in the rest frame of a heat bath, with momentum relaxation parameter ν(p⁰*) and noise strength D(p⁰*) assumed to be Lorentz scalar functions. They then apply a Lorentz boost to a moving frame and determine the transformation property of the noise term by demanding that the equilibrium distribution function be a Lorentz scalar, leading to a generalized fluctuation-dissipation relation. Using this framework, they derive the first law of thermodynamics by identifying heat transfer as the work done by the heat bath on the particle, and the second law by relating the time derivative of Shannon entropy to the average heat transfer.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium distribution is given by the Jüttner distribution ρ_eq ∝ exp(−u^μ p_μ / T), where T is temperature.
- The generalized fluctuation-dissipation relation is ν(u^μ p_μ) = (1/(u^μ p_μ)) (D(u^μ p_μ)/T − α D′(u^μ p_μ)), where α depends on the discretization scheme (α=0 for Hänggi-Klimontovich, α=1/2 for Stratonovich-Fisk, α=1 for Ito).
- For the case D ∝ p⁰*, the relativistic equipartition theorem reduces to ⟨p²/(2p⁰)⟩_{t*} = (3/2)T, valid even out of equilibrium.
- In the ultra-relativistic limit with D ∝ (p⁰*)^q, the generalized equipartition theorem gives ⟨E^q⟩_{t*} = T × (q+2) ⟨E^{q-1}⟩_{t*}, where E = p⁰*.
- In the non-relativistic limit with D ∝ |p|^q, the generalized equipartition theorem gives ⟨|p|^{q+2}⟩_{t*} = T m × (q+3) ⟨|p|^q⟩_{t*}.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic energetics formalism of Sekimoto (1998) from non-relativistic to relativistic Brownian motion, addressing the open problem of defining a Lorentz-covariant noise term that has been discussed by many authors including Debbasch, Hakim, Hänggi, and others. The results enable the study of thermodynamic properties in relativistic systems such as quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, where event-by-event fluctuations are important, and provide a foundation for connecting microscopic dynamics to relativistic thermodynamics and hydrodynamics.
