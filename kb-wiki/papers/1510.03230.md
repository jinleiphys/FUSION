# The long and winding road from chiral effective Lagrangians to nuclear structure

> I review the chiral dynamics of nuclear physics. In the first part, I discuss the new developments in the construction of the forces between two, three and four nucleons which have been partly carried out to fifth order in the chiral expansion. It is also shown that based on these forces in conjunction with the estimation of the corresponding theoretical uncertainties, the need for three-nucleon forces in few nucleon systems can be unambiguously established. I also introduce the lattice formulation of these forces, which allow for truly ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions. I present some pertinent results of the nuclear lattice approach. Finally, I discuss how few-nucleon systems and nuclei can be used to explore symmetries and physics within and beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) based on the symmetries of QCD provides a systematic and rigorous framework for constructing nuclear forces, with theoretical uncertainties that can be quantified, and that this approach has been extended to fifth order in the chiral expansion for two-nucleon forces, though the applicability is restricted to momenta and energies small compared to the hard scale of about 1 GeV.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of chiral effective Lagrangians for pions and nucleons, employing Weinberg power counting to organize the expansion in small momenta and quark masses. Nuclear forces between two, three, and four nucleons are derived from these Lagrangians, with low-energy constants (LECs) fixed from pion-nucleon scattering data and then used to predict two-pion exchange contributions to nucleon-nucleon scattering and three-nucleon forces. The forces are also implemented on a space-time lattice to perform Monte Carlo simulations of nuclear structure and reactions.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant \(F_\pi\) has a physical value of 92.2 MeV, determined from pion decay \(\pi \to \nu \mu\).
* The pion mass satisfies \(M_\pi^2 = (m_u + m_d) G_\pi / F_\pi\), showing it grows as \(\sqrt{m_u + m_d}\).
* The nucleon mass receives about 110 MeV from quark mass contributions (pion-nucleon sigma term and strangeness content), with the bulk coming from gluon field energy.
* The QCD \(\theta\)-parameter is constrained to \(|\theta| < 7.6 \cdot 10^{-11}\) from the neutron electric dipole moment limit.
* The hard scale for chiral effective field theory is of order 1 GeV, setting the breakdown scale of the expansion.

## Context

This review extends the chiral effective field theory program initiated by Weinberg (1978, 1990, 1991) and developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984) for pion interactions, and by Bernard (1995) for pion-nucleon systems. It enables ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions via lattice methods, and allows investigation of how nuclear properties vary with fundamental parameters such as light quark masses and the fine-structure constant, with implications for anthropic arguments about the Universe.
