# Predicting narrow states in the spectrum of a nucleus beyond the proton drip line

> Properties of particle-unstable nuclei lying beyond the proton drip line can be ascertained by considering those (usually known) properties of its mirror neutron-rich system. We have used a multi-channel algebraic scattering theory to map the known properties of the neutron-${}^{14}$C system to those of the proton-${}^{14}$O one from which we deduce that the particle-unstable ${}^{15}$F will have a spectrum of two low lying broad resonances of positive parity and, at higher excitation, three narrow negative parity ones. A key feature is to use coupling to Pauli-hindered states in the target.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the particle-unstable nucleus ${}^{15}$F, lying beyond the proton drip line, will have a spectrum of two low-lying broad positive-parity resonances and, at higher excitation, three narrow negative-parity resonances, based on mapping the known properties of the neutron-${}^{14}$C mirror system using a multi-channel algebraic scattering theory (MCAS) that incorporates coupling to Pauli-hindered states in the target.

## Method

The authors used the multi-channel algebraic scattering theory (MCAS) to analyze the $n$+${}^{14}$C system and then mapped those results to the $p$+${}^{14}$O system by adding a Coulomb field. The MCAS approach incorporates the Pauli principle via the Orthogonalizing-Pseudo-Potential (OPP) method, and this work extends that method to treat Pauli-hindered states (intermediate between Pauli-blocked and Pauli-allowed) by using finite OPP coupling strengths. The same collective-model interaction potentials (central, spin-orbit, and $l^2$ terms with quadrupole deformation) were used for both systems, with only a slight reduction in the central potential strength for the proton case.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of ${}^{15}$C is bound by 1.218 MeV below the $n$+${}^{14}$C threshold, and the first excited state ($\frac{5}{2}^+$) is bound by 0.478 MeV.
* The two observed resonances in ${}^{15}$F are centered at 1.47 and 2.78 MeV above the $p$+${}^{14}$O threshold.
* The OPP coupling strengths for the Pauli-hindered neutron $0p_{\frac{1}{2}}$ orbit in the $0^+_2$ and $2^+$ states of ${}^{14}$C were 3.11 and 3.87 MeV, respectively.
* The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were: undeformed radius 3.1 fm, diffuseness 0.65 fm, with potential strengths $V_0 = -45.0$ MeV, $V_{so} = 7.0$ MeV, and $V_{ll} = 0.42$ MeV for the neutron system; the central strength was reduced to $V_0 = -44.2$ MeV for the proton system.
* The quadrupole coupling strength was $\beta_2 = -0.5$.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS approach (Amos 2003, Canton 2005, Pisent 2005) to include Pauli-hindrance, a concept arising from the Resonating Group Method (Schmid 1978), to describe shell-breaking effects in weakly-bound exotic nuclei. The predictions for narrow negative-parity resonances in ${}^{15}$F above the energy range of existing data from Goldberg (2004) and Guo (2005) provide a specific target for future experiments, and the method can be applied to other proton-rich nuclei beyond the drip line by using their neutron-rich mirror systems.
