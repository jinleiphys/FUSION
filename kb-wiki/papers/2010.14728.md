# Non-local quark models for description of dense matter in the cores of neutron stars

> This thesis work focuses on studying the possible existence of phase transitions in the immediate compact remnants of core collapse supernova, neutron stars, and the theoretical models that describe the interior of dense matter. Specifically, we are interested in analyzing the feasibility of a transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in the cores of these objects. For the phase transition we will use two different formalisms: the Maxwell formalism or abrupt phase transition and the Gibbs formalisms or mixed phase transition. For the description of hadronic matter, we will use different parametrizations of the relativistic mean field model with density-dependent coupling constants. For the description of quark matter we will use an effective nonlocal Nambu Jona-Lasinio model of three flavors with vector interactions, in which we will include the possibility of formation of diquarks to model a superconducting phase of color in $SU(3)$. Phase diagrams and equations of state of quark matter at finite temperature are presented, and the influence of that type of matter on observables associated with neutron stars is investigated. The simplified thermal evolution of compact stars during their formation is studied, from their state of proto-neutron stars to that of cold neutron stars, and the results obtained are compared with recent astrophysical observations. The results obtained indicate that the occurrence of quark matter in the nuclei of these stars remains a promising possibility. The fact that the use of more realistic models for the description of the dense matter in these objects indicates the presence of quark matter inside neutron stars, could be an answer to the question of the behavior of that kind of matter and the determination of its corresponding equation of state.

## Key claim

The thesis investigates whether a phase transition from hadronic matter to quark matter can occur in neutron star cores, using non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nl-NJL) models. The results indicate that quark matter in neutron star cores remains a "promising possibility," but the occurrence depends sensitively on the choice of vector interaction coupling, the inclusion of the Polyakov loop, and whether color superconductivity (2SC+s phase) is considered; notably, including the Polyakov loop suppresses quark matter at finite temperature in Maxwell-type transitions, while including color superconductivity without the Polyakov loop shifts the transition to lower chemical potentials but reduces the maximum stellar mass.

## Method

The author constructs hybrid equations of state (EoS) for neutron stars by combining a density-dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) model for hadronic matter (using the DD2 and GM1L parametrizations) with a non-local three-flavor NJL model for quark matter, including vector interactions and, in some cases, a color superconducting phase (2SC+s) with diquark pairing. Phase transitions between hadronic and quark matter are treated using both the Maxwell (abrupt) and Gibbs (mixed phase) formalisms. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain families of non-rotating, spherically symmetric compact stars, and a schematic thermal evolution from proto-neutron star to cold neutron star is studied by assuming baryonic mass conservation.

## Key numbers

- The vector interaction coupling ranges that allow a phase transition and satisfy the 2 solar mass bound are: \(0.331 < \zeta_v < 0.371\) for the GM1L hadronic parametrization, and \(0.328 < \zeta_v < 0.385\) for the DD2 parametrization.
- For the Gibbs mixed-phase construction with the GM1L parametrization and minimal vector coupling (\(\zeta_V = 0.331\)), an evolutionary stage with entropy per baryon \(s = 0.8\) and central temperature \(T_{\text{central}}^{\text{GM1L}} = 28\) MeV is found as a precursor to the maximum-mass cold hybrid star.
- The observational bound of 2 solar masses is imposed by the pulsars J0348+0432 and J1614-2230.
- The nuclear compressibility modulus from early linear RMF models was \(K_0 = 545\) MeV, while the accepted range is \(K_0 \simeq 200\)–300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hybrid stars by applying non-local NJL models (rather than local ones) to describe quark matter, and by systematically comparing the Maxwell and Gibbs phase transition formalisms. It builds on the density-dependent RMF parametrizations DD2 and GM1L, which are constrained by finite-nuclei properties and nuclear saturation data (Typel 2005, PhysRevC.71.024312). The results show that including the Polyakov loop suppresses quark matter at finite temperature in Maxwell transitions, while color superconductivity (2SC+s) without the Polyakov loop reduces maximum masses, often failing the 2 solar mass bound. The author identifies the unresolved problem of simultaneously including the Polyakov loop and color superconductivity due to color-space rotation, and suggests that future work should investigate the Color Flavor Locked (CFL) phase and temperature-dependent coupling constants. Observations from NICER, Strobe-X, LIGO, and Virgo (including the GW170817 neutron star merger) are noted as key to constraining the dense matter EoS.
