# pi^- decay rates of p-shell hypernuclei revisited

> Explicit expressions for the parity-violating s-wave and the parity-conserving p-wave contributions to pi- weak-decay rates of Lambda hypernuclei in the 1p shell are given in the weak-coupling limit, to update previous shell-model calculations and to compare with recent pi- spectra and total decay rates measured by the FINUDA Collaboration. A useful sum rule for the summed strength of Lambda_{1s} --> pi- + proton_{1p} weak decays is derived. Fair agreement between experiment and calculations is reached, using the primary s-wave amplitude and Cohen-Kurath nuclear wavefunctions. The role of the p-wave amplitude is studied in detail for Lambda-15N and found to be secondary. Previous assignments of ground-state spin-parity values are confirmed and a new assignment is made, spin-parity 3/2+ for Lambda-15N, based on the substantial suppression calculated here for spin-parity 1/2+.

## Key claim

The paper provides explicit expressions for parity-violating s-wave and parity-conserving p-wave contributions to pi- weak-decay rates of Lambda hypernuclei in the 1p shell, updating previous shell-model calculations to compare with FINUDA data; fair agreement is reached using the primary s-wave amplitude and Cohen-Kurath nuclear wavefunctions, with the p-wave amplitude playing a secondary role except for suppressed transitions.

## Method

The author derived explicit formulas for Lambda_{1s} to p_{1p} transition rates in the weak-coupling limit using Cohen-Kurath (CK) nuclear wavefunctions and fractional-parentage coefficients. Pion final-state interactions were incorporated via distorted-wave form factors, with the DW/PW ratio estimated by comparing Motoba et al. (1988) and Motoba et al. (1994) calculations. A sum rule for the summed strength of Lambda_{1s} to p_{1p} transitions was derived, encapsulating Pauli suppression through the factor (1 - N_c^{(1p)}/12). The s-wave amplitude was used for main transitions, with p-wave effects checked explicitly for the suppressed case of Lambda-15N.

## Key numbers

- Free-space Lambda to pi- p decay momentum: q_0 = 100.7 MeV/c (also given as 101 MeV/c)
- Ratio of p-wave to s-wave amplitudes squared: |p_pi/s_pi|^2 ≈ 0.132
- Plane-wave form factor squared at q_0: |F_PW^(s)(q_0)|^2 = 0.0898 (harmonic oscillator), adopted value 0.0876 (two-term Gaussian)
- DW/PW form factor ratio: 1.47 ± 0.16, giving |F_DW^(s)(q)|^2 ≈ 0.129 at q_0
- Calculated total pi- decay rates relative to Gamma_Lambda: Lambda-7Li(1/2+) = 0.356; Lambda-9Be(1/2+) = 0.186; Lambda-11B(5/2+) = 0.196; Lambda-15N(3/2+) = 0.088
- FINUDA experimental total rates: Lambda-7Li = 0.353 ± 0.059; Lambda-9Be = 0.178 ± 0.050; Lambda-11B = 0.249 ± 0.051; Lambda-15N = 0.108 ± 0.038
- For Lambda-15N(3/2+) to pi- + 15O_g.s.: calculated rate = 0.057 (CK) vs. experimental 0.072 ± 0.024; for Lambda-15N(1/2+): calculated rate = 0.019 (CK)
- Sum-rule prediction for Lambda-5He: 0.305, compared to FINUDA value 0.306 ± 0.060

## Context

This work extends the systematic shell-model calculations of Motoba et al. (1988, 1992, 1994) for pionic decays of p-shell hypernuclei, providing explicit formulas in the weak-coupling limit that allow experimenters to check calculations independently. The paper confirms the ground-state spin-parity assignments of Lambda-7Li(1/2+) from Sasao et al. (2004) and Lambda-11B(5/2+) from earlier emulsion work, and establishes a new assignment of 3/2+ for Lambda-15N based on comparison with FINUDA data from Botta et al. (2009). The derived sum rule quantifies Pauli blocking suppression of pi- decay rates as the 1p shell fills, enabling predictions for unmeasured hypernuclei and providing a framework for future spectroscopic studies of hypernuclear ground-state spins.
