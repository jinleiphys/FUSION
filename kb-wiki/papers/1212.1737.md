# Complex masses of resonances and the Cornell potential

> Physical properties of the Cornell potential in the complex-mass scheme are investigated. Two exact asymptotic solutions of relativistic wave equation for the coulombic and linear components of the potential are used to derive the resonance complex-mass formula. The centered masses and total widths of the $ρ$-family resonances are calculated.

# Complex masses of resonances and the Cornell potential

## Key claim

The Cornell potential, when treated in the complex-mass scheme, yields complex momentum eigenvalues whose real and imaginary parts arise separately from the linear and Coulombic components of the potential, producing a unified formula for both the centered masses and total widths of mesonic resonances, but the widths are finite or decreasing with mass, contradicting the common belief that heavy hadronic resonance widths increase linearly with mass.

## Method

The author solves the relativistic semi-classical wave equation separately for the short-distance Coulombic term and the long-distance linear term of the Cornell potential, obtaining exact asymptotic solutions for each. These two independent solutions are combined via a two-point Pade approximant to produce an interpolating complex-mass formula, from which the real part gives the centered mass squared and the imaginary part gives the total width. The method is applied to the leading Regge trajectory of the rho-family resonances, with parameters fitted to PDG-2012 data.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous interpolating mass formula (Sergeenko 1993, 1994, 2010, 2012) which described meson mass spectra across all qqbar and QQbar systems, by incorporating the complex-mass scheme used in loop calculations (Baur et al.). It contrasts with traditional S-matrix scattering approaches to resonances (Siegert 1939, Heitler) and with the widely spread belief that heavy resonance widths grow linearly with mass. The results enable further development of string models of hadrons and improvement of transport codes such as hadron string dynamics by including finite widths of heavy resonances, and are relevant to ATLAS (LHC) physics.
