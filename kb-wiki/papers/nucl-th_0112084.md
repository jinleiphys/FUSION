# Anomalous anapole moment of an exotic nucleus

> Using the information on the nuclear structure of exotic neutron-rich halo nucleus $^{11}$Be, we evaluate the parity violating anapole moment in its ground state. The resulting value $κ(^{11}$Be)$=0.17$ is fifteen times bigger than the typical value of the anapole moment of a normal nucleus of the same mass, and in fact exceeds by few times anapole moments of any known neutron-odd nuclei (e.g., kappa(^{11}Be) > 2|κ(^{207}Pb)|. It is also few times bigger than the neutral current contribution to the lepton-nucleus interaction.

# Anomalous anapole moment of an exotic nucleus

## Key claim
The parity-violating anapole moment of the halo nucleus ¹¹Be is evaluated to be κ = 0.17, which is fifteen times larger than the typical anapole moment of a normal nucleus of the same mass and exceeds the anapole moment of any known neutron-odd nucleus, including ²⁰⁷Pb. This enhancement arises specifically from the halo structure: the small energy gap (0.32 MeV) between opposite-parity states and the extended spatial distribution of the valence neutron.

## Method
The anapole moment is calculated in a single-particle approximation for the external halo neutron of ¹¹Be, using the effective one-body parity-nonconserving weak potential w_PNC = g (G/2√2 m) {(σ·p), ρ} with the core density ρ taken from Hartree-Fock calculations. The sum over opposite-parity intermediate states in the perturbative expression for the anapole moment is dominated by the single bound excited state 1p₁/₂, whose energy separation from the 2s₁/₂ ground state is ΔE = 0.32 MeV. Model wave functions for the 2s and 1p halo states are fitted to reproduce the mean square radii from Hartree-Fock calculations, and the analytic expression for κ is evaluated with parameters r₀ = 1.45 fm and r₁ = 1.80 fm.

## Key numbers
- κ(¹¹Be) = 0.17 (for the neutron weak constant g_n ≃ 1), from Eq. (W-RESULT-NUMERIC)
- Energy splitting between the 1p₁/₂ excited state and the 2s₁/₂ ground state: |ΔE| = 0.32 MeV (Eq. DELTA-E)
- Enhancement factor R_halo = κ(¹¹Be)/κ_norm = 15, where κ_norm is the anapole moment of a normal nucleus of the same mass (Eq. R=15)
- Mean square halo radii: √⟨r²_2s⟩ = 5.9 fm, √⟨r²_1p⟩ = 4.9 fm (from Eq. RADII with r₀ = 1.45 fm, r₁ = 1.80 fm)
- Core density parameters: ρ₀ = 0.2 fm⁻³, R_c = 2 fm (Eq. ANSATZ-CORE)
- κ(²⁰⁷Pb) = -0.08 g_n (from text, for comparison)
- Neutral current contribution: -κ_nc = -0.05 (from text)

## Context
This work extends the study of parity-nonconserving effects in nuclei from normal stable nuclei to exotic halo nuclei, following the earlier demonstration by Hussein, de Toledo Piza, Vorov, and Kerman (2000) that PNC mixing can be enhanced in halo systems. Prior calculations of anapole moments in normal nuclei were performed by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1984-1986) in the single-particle approximation and by Haxton, Henley, and Musolf (1989) with residual interactions; many-body corrections were treated by Dmitriev, Khriplovich, and Telitsin (1994). The result enables future experimental searches for the anapole moment of ¹¹Be using atomic hyperfine structure measurements at ISOL-type facilities, and offers a unique probe of the isospin structure of the weak nucleon-nucleon interaction through the neutron weak constant g_n, which is otherwise masked by the larger proton weak constant in normal nuclei.
