# Low energy nuclear reactions in a crystal lattice

> We extend the recently proposed mechanism for inducing low energy nuclear reactions (LENRs) to a crystal lattice. The process gets a dominant contribution at second order in quantum perturbation theory. The tunnelling barrier is evaded due to the presence of high energy intermediate states. However, the process is found to have a negligible rate in free space due to a delicate cancellation between amplitudes corresponding to different states. The delicate cancellation can be evaded in medium in special circumstances leading to an observable rate. Here we consider this process in a one dimensional crystal lattice and find that the rate depends strongly on the form of interaction. We find that for some allowed choices of interactions, the rate is substantial, while other interactions produce a very small rate.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed second-order perturbation mechanism for low-energy nuclear reactions (LENRs) to a one-dimensional crystal lattice, finding that the reaction rate depends strongly on the form of the interaction Hamiltonian; for some allowed choices of the interaction operator the rate is substantial, while for others it remains negligible due to a delicate cancellation among intermediate-state amplitudes.

## Method

The authors model a proton fusing with a lattice ion in a one-dimensional generalized Kronig-Penney potential that includes a deep narrow nuclear well. They compute the second-order transition amplitude for the process, which proceeds via emission of a scalar particle at each of two vertices, summing over intermediate Bloch states in the periodic lattice. The integrals \(I_1\) and \(I_2\) are evaluated analytically in the dominant spatial regions, and the total rate is obtained by integrating over the energies of the two emitted particles while enforcing energy conservation.

## Key numbers

- The initial state energy eigenvalue is \(2.68 \times 10^{-3}\) atomic units, chosen to be much smaller than the barrier height \(V_0 = 100\) atomic units.
- The final state energy eigenvalue is \(E_f = -10.96\) MeV (second excited state), with the ground state at \(-45.3\) MeV.
- For the modified interaction with operator \({\cal O} = 1/(P^2 - P_0^2)\) and dimensionless coupling \(g'' = 10^{-5}\), the amplitude \(\bar I\) settles to a value of \(-6.89 \times 10^{-11}\) atomic units (real part) and approximately \(2.6 \times 10^{-11}\) atomic units (imaginary part).
- The total reaction rate for this modified interaction, after integrating over the emitted particle momentum \(q_1\), is approximately \(2 \times 10^{-13}\) atomic units.
- For the same parameters, the first-order process rate is of order \(10^{-76}\) atomic units, showing extreme suppression compared to the second-order process.

## Context

This work extends the second-order LENR mechanism proposed by Jain et al. (2020, 2021) and Ramkumar et al. (2022) from free-space and step-potential toy models to a periodic crystal lattice. The earlier studies found that the rate is negligible in free space due to cancellation among continuous-energy eigenstates, but becomes substantial when the spectrum is discretized (e.g., by an infinite potential wall). The present paper confirms that a realistic periodic lattice can also yield a significant rate for certain interaction forms, while showing that the simplest interaction (operator equal to unity) still suffers from cancellation. This enables future work to identify which physical interactions in real solids might produce observable LENR rates.
