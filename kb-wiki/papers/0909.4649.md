# Conditions for Efimov Physics for Finite Range Potentials

> We consider a system of three identical bosons near a Feshbach resonance in the universal regime with large scattering length usually described by model independent zero-range potentials. We employ the adiabatic hyperspherical approximation and derive the rigorous large-distance equation for the adiabatic potential for finite-range interactions. The effective range correction to the zero-range approximation must be supplemented by a new term of the same order. The non-adiabatic term can be decisive. Efimov physics is always confined to the range between effective range and scattering length. The analytical results agree with numerical calculations for realistic potentials.

# Conditions for Efimov Physics for Finite Range Potentials

## Key claim

Efimov physics is confined to the range between the effective range (not the potential range) and the scattering length, but the effective range correction to the zero-range approximation must be supplemented by a new model-dependent term of the same order from the scattering length, and the non-adiabatic term can be decisive in restoring universality.

## Method

The authors employ the adiabatic hyperspherical approximation for three identical bosons interacting via finite-range two-body potentials, deriving a rigorous large-distance transcendental equation for the adiabatic eigenvalue by matching solutions in the inner region (where the potential is nonzero) and the outer region (where it vanishes). They expand the resulting equation using the effective range expansion to second order, obtaining a generalized eigenvalue equation that includes both the effective range term and a new scattering length correction term \(R_V\), and compare analytical results with exact numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation for a model potential with a barrier.

## Key numbers

- The zero-range universal solution gives \(\nu_0 = 1.00624 i\) and \(\lambda_0 = -5.0125\) for \(\rho/\sqrt{\mu} \ll |a|\).
- For the model potential with barrier, the low-energy parameters are \(a = 556.88\, r_0\), \(R_e = -142.86\, r_0\), \(R_V = 73.031\, r_0\), and \(R_0 = -0.71\, r_0\).
- For the finite square well with \(|a| = \infty\), \(R_e = r_0\) and \(R_V = n^2\pi^2 r_0/24\) where \(n\) is the number of bound states.
- For the \(^{39}\)K narrow Feshbach resonance at \(B = 825\) G, the effective range is \(R_e = -2.93 \times 10^4 a_0\) with van der Waals length \(l_{\text{vdW}} = 1.29 \times 10^2 a_0\).
- The coefficient \(c_0\) in the asymptotic expansion is approximately \(-0.671\).

## Context

This work extends zero-range models of Efimov physics (Efimov 1973, Braaten 2006, Platter 2009) by rigorously including finite-range corrections that were previously treated semi-rigorously (Fedorov 2001, Platter 2009). The authors show that the effective range correction alone is insufficient and that a new model-dependent scattering length correction term \(R_V\) must be included, with the non-adiabatic term restoring universality by canceling the model-dependent contributions. The results enable more accurate predictions for cold atom experiments near Feshbach resonances, particularly for narrow resonances where the effective range can be much larger than the potential range (Bruun 2005), and for determining stability conditions of condensates in traps and properties of cold atoms in lattices.
