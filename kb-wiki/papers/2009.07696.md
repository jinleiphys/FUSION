# Prior probability distributions of neutron star crust models

> To make best use of multi-faceted astronomical and nuclear data-sets, probability distributions of neutron star models that can be used to propagate errors consistently from one domain to another are required. We take steps toward a consistent model for this purpose, highlight where model inconsistencies occur and assess the resulting model uncertainty. Using two distributions of nuclear symmetry energy parameters - one uniform, the other based on pure neutron matter theory, we prepare two ensembles of neutron star inner crust models. We use an extended Skyrme energy-density functional within a compressible liquid drop model (CLDM). We fit the surface parameters of the CLDM to quantum 3D Hartree-Fock calculations of crustal nuclei. All models predict more than 50% of the crust by mass and 15% of the crust by thickness comprises pasta with medians of around 62% and 30% respectively. We also present 68% and 95% ranges for the crust composition as a function of density. We examine the relationships between crust-core boundary and pasta transition properties, the thickness of the pasta layers, the symmetry energy at saturation and sub-saturation densities and the neutron skins of 208Pb and 48Ca. We quantify the correlations using the maximal information coefficient, which can effectively characterize non-linear relationships. Future measurements of neutron skins, information from nuclear masses and giant resonances, and theoretical constraints on PNM will be able to place constraints on the location of the pasta and crust-core boundaries and the amount of pasta in the crust.

## Key claim

The paper constructs two ensembles of neutron star inner crust models using different probability distributions for the symmetry energy parameters (one uniform, one informed by chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter), finding that all models predict more than 50% of the crust by mass and 15% of the crust by thickness comprises nuclear pasta, with medians around 62% and 30% respectively. The authors emphasize that these are prior probability distributions intended for future Bayesian inference, and they highlight where model inconsistencies occur and assess the resulting model uncertainty rather than claiming definitive predictions.

## Method

The authors used an extended Skyrme energy-density functional within a compressible liquid drop model (CLDM) to generate ensembles of neutron star inner crust models. They fit the surface parameters of the CLDM to quantum 3D Hartree-Fock calculations of crustal nuclei, and generated two ensembles: one from a uniform distribution of the symmetry energy parameters J, L, and K_sym, and another from a distribution informed by chiral effective field theory constraints on pure neutron matter via the Holt (2018) correlations. They calculated neutron skins of 208Pb and 48Ca using the Sky3D code and quantified correlations between crust properties and nuclear observables using the maximal information coefficient (MIC).

## Key numbers

- The uniform ensemble covers J from 24 to 36 MeV, L from -10 to 100 MeV, and K_sym from -440 to 120 MeV, drawn from a grid of 12x13x9 points (1404 potential models)
- The PNM-informed ensemble draws 9x9x9 models uniformly from ranges of J, a_0 (5.53-6.41 MeV fm^3), and b_12 (0 to 16 MeV fm^2)
- All models predict more than 50% of the crust by mass comprises pasta, with medians around 62%
- All models predict more than 15% of the crust by thickness comprises pasta, with medians around 30%
- The paper presents 68% and 95% ranges for crust composition as a function of density

## Context

This work extends prior systematic surveys of crust properties (Newton 2013, Carreau 2019) by preparing full inner crust model ensembles suitable for Bayesian inference, rather than only crust-core transition properties. It builds on the Holt (2018) correlations between symmetry energy parameters from chiral effective field theory and the Lim (2017) extended Skyrme functional. The results enable future incorporation of neutron skin measurements from PREX-II, CREX, and MREX experiments, along with nuclear mass and giant resonance data, to constrain the location of pasta and crust-core boundaries and the amount of pasta in the crust.
