# Update of the Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator: light-nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions

> We present an update of the event generator based on the three-fluid dynamics (3FD), complemented by Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) for the late stage of the nuclear collision~-- the three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD final State interactions (THESEUS). Two modifications are introduced. The THESEUS table of hadronic resonances is made consistent with that of the underlying 3FD model. The main modification is that the generator is extended to simulate the light-nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, on the equal basis with hadrons. These modifications are illustrated by applications to the description of available experimental data. The first run of the updated generator revealed a good reproduction of the NA49 data on the light nuclei. The reproduction is achieved without any extra parameters, while the coalescence approach in 3FD requires special tuning of the coalescence coefficients for each light nucleus separately.

## Key claim

The updated THESEUS-v2 event generator reproduces NA49 light-nuclei data without any extra fit parameters, whereas the coalescence approach in the underlying 3FD model requires special tuning of coalescence coefficients for each light nucleus separately.

## Method

The authors updated the THESEUS event generator by making its hadronic resonance table consistent with that of the underlying 3FD model, and extended it to simulate light-nuclei production (deuterons, tritons, helium-3, helium-4, and low-lying helium-4 resonances) on an equal thermodynamic basis with hadrons. The light-nuclei momentum distributions are sampled according to phase-space distribution functions at given flow velocity, chemical potentials, and temperature, with a recalculation of the baryon chemical potential to conserve baryon number when nuclei are added to the particle list.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 9.2 GeV: the original extended THESEUS table overshoots the 3FD proton distribution by approximately 10%, with approximately 10% excess for K- and 5% for K+
- At sqrt(s_NN) < 5 GeV, the effect of the resonance table is negligible, amounting to less than 1% in terms of midrapidity proton density
- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7 GeV, total energy is conserved with accuracy of 3% after baryon chemical potential recalculation
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at E_lab = 20A GeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 6.4 GeV), energy is conserved within 1%
- The freeze-out energy density criterion is epsilon_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the THESEUS event generator (Batyuk 2016) based on the three-fluid dynamics model (Ivanov 2005, Ivanov 2013) with UrQMD afterburner (Bass 1993, Bass 1998). It provides an alternative thermodynamic approach to light-nuclei production, contrasting with the coalescence approach used in the 3FD model (Ivanov 2005, Ivanov 2017) and with microscopic transport models such as SMASH (Weil 2016, Oliinychenko 2018) and PHQMD (Aichelin 2019). The updated generator enables studies of light-nuclei production at collision energies of the BES-RHIC, SPS, NICA, and FAIR facilities.
