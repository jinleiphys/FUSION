# Compton Scattering off Pions and Electromagnetic Polarizabilities

> The electric ($Оұ_ПҖ$) and magnetic ($ОІ_ПҖ$) Compton polarizabilities of both the charged and the neutral pion are of fundamental interest in the low-energy sector of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Pion polarizabilities affect the shape of the $ОіПҖ\toОіПҖ$ Compton scattering angular distribution at back scattering angles and $ОіОі\toПҖПҖ$ absolute cross sections. Theory derivations are given of the $ОіПҖ\toОіПҖ$ Compton scattering differential cross section, dispersion relations, and sum rules in terms of the polarizabilities. We review experimental charged and neutral polarizability studies and theoretical predictions. The $ПҖ^0$ polarizabilities were deduced from DESY Crystal Ball $ОіОі\toПҖ^0ПҖ^0$ data, but with large uncertainties. The charged pion polarizabilities were deduced most recently from (1) radiative pion Primakoff scattering $ПҖ^- Z \to ПҖ^-ZОі$ at CERN COMPASS, (2) two-photon pion pair production $ОіОі\toПҖ^+ПҖ^-$ at SLAC Mark II, and (3) radiative pion photoproduction $Оіp\toОіПҖ^+ n$ from the proton at MAMI in Mainz. A stringent test of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) is possible based on comparisons of precision experimental charged pion polarizabilities with ChPT predictions. Only the CERN COMPASS charged pion polarizability measurement has acceptably small uncertainties. Its value $Оұ_{ПҖ^\pm}-ОІ_{ПҖ^\pm} = (4.0\pm 1.8)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$ agrees well with the two-loop ChPT prediction $Оұ_{ПҖ^\pm}-ОІ_{ПҖ^\pm}=(5.7\pm 1.0)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$, strengthening the identification of the pion with the Goldstone boson of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.

## Key claim

The charged pion polarizability difference measured by CERN COMPASS, $\alpha_{\pi^\pm}-\beta_{\pi^\pm} = (4.0\pm 1.8)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$, agrees with the two-loop chiral perturbation theory prediction of $(5.7\pm 1.0)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$, but only the COMPASS measurement has acceptably small uncertainties among the three most recent experimental determinations.

## Method

The authors review theoretical derivations of the $\gamma\pi\to\gamma\pi$ Compton scattering differential cross section, dispersion relations, and sum rules in terms of electric and magnetic polarizabilities. They compare experimental charged pion polarizability results from three processes: radiative pion Primakoff scattering $\pi^- Z \to \pi^-Z\gamma$ at CERN COMPASS using 190 GeV/$c$ negative pions, two-photon pion pair production $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$ at SLAC Mark II, and radiative pion photoproduction $\gamma p\to\gamma\pi^+ n$ at MAMI in Mainz. Neutral pion polarizabilities were deduced from DESY Crystal Ball $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^0\pi^0$ data.

## Key numbers

- Charged pion polarizability difference from CERN COMPASS: $\alpha_{\pi^\pm}-\beta_{\pi^\pm} = (4.0\pm 1.8)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$
- Two-loop chiral perturbation theory prediction: $\alpha_{\pi^\pm}-\beta_{\pi^\pm} = (5.7\pm 1.0)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$
- Two-loop chiral perturbation theory prediction for the sum: $\alpha_{\pi^\pm}+\beta_{\pi^\pm} = 0.16\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$
- Thomson cross section for the charged pion: 8.84 $\mu$barn
- Thomson cross section for the proton: 197 nbarn
- Estimated diamagnetic contribution to the magnetic polarizability of the charged pion: $-4.30\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3$

## Context

This review extends earlier work on pion polarizabilities by Klein (1955), Holstein (1990), Lvov (1993), Moinester (1997), and Scherer (1999), and provides a comprehensive comparison of experimental data with chiral perturbation theory predictions by Gasser (2006). The COMPASS result strengthens the identification of the pion as the Goldstone boson of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, while the large uncertainties in the neutral pion polarizabilities from DESY Crystal Ball data and the charged pion results from SLAC Mark II and MAMI leave room for improved measurements, such as the planned JLab experiment using Primakoff scattering of high-energy gamma rays.
