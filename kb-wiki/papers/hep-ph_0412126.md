# Parametrization of Neutrino Mixing Matrix in Tri-bimaximal Mixing Pattern

> The neutrino mixing matrix is expanded in powers of a small parameter $λ$ in tri-bimaximal mixing pattern. We also present some applications of this parametrization, such as to the expression of the Jarlskog parameter $J$. Comparing with other parametrizations (such as the parametrization in bimaximal mixing pattern), this parametrization converges more quickly, but is of less symmetry.

## Key claim

The neutrino mixing matrix can be expanded in powers of a small parameter λ around the tri-bimaximal mixing pattern, with λ defined by the deviation of the matrix element V_e2 from its tri-bimaximal value; this expansion converges more quickly than expansions around the bimaximal mixing pattern, but has less symmetry.

## Method

The authors define a small parameter λ = √3/3 − V_e2, where √3/3 is the tri-bimaximal value of V_e2, and expand all elements of the PMNS matrix in powers of λ up to order λ². They introduce two additional parameters a and b to describe the deviations of V_μ3 and V_e3 from their tri-bimaximal values, and a CP-violating phase δ. The trigonometric functions of the three mixing angles are then computed order by order, and the full mixing matrix is reconstructed as a series expansion.

## Key numbers

- λ ≈ 0.05, derived from the best fit point V_e2 = 0.53 (the tri-bimaximal value is √3/3 ≈ 0.577)
- Allowed range of λ at 1σ: 0.03 < λ < 0.07, from the 1σ range 0.51 < V_e2 < 0.55
- Allowed range of λ at 3σ: −0.03 < λ < 0.1, from the 3σ range of V_e2
- Parameter a ≈ 0.3, from V_μ3 = √2/2 + aλ with V_μ3 = 0.72 (best fit)
- Parameter b ≈ 1.5, from V_e3 = bλ e^{iδ} with |V_e3| = 0.08 (best fit)
- Jarlskog parameter J ≈ 0.018, estimated using λ ≈ 0.05 and sinδ ≈ 1
- The effective Majorana mass term ⟨m⟩_ee has a coefficient for m_3 of order b²λ² ≈ 10⁻³

## Context

This work extends the approach of expanding the neutrino mixing matrix around a reference pattern, previously applied to the bimaximal mixing pattern by Rodejohann, Giunti and Tanimoto, and the authors themselves. The tri-bimaximal pattern, first conjectured by Wolfenstein (1978) and discussed by several authors, is the best approximation to the measured mixing matrix with angles 45°, 0°, and 35.3°. The parametrization enables simple expressions for the Jarlskog parameter and effective neutrino mass terms, linking the mixing parameters to mass-squared differences and potentially helping to determine the absolute neutrino mass scale.
