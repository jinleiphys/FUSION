# Relativistic Dissipative Hydrodynamic Equations at the Second Order for Multi-Component Systems with Multiple Conserved Currents

> We derive the second order hydrodynamic equations for the relativistic system of multi-components with multiple conserved currents by generalizing the Israel-Stewart theory and Grad's moment method. We find that, in addition to the conventional moment equations, extra moment equations associated with conserved currents should be introduced to consistently match the number of equations with that of unknowns and to satisfy the Onsager reciprocal relations. Consistent expansion of the entropy current leads to constitutive equations which involve the terms not appearing in the original Israel-Stewart theory even in the single component limit. We also find several terms which exhibit thermal diffusion such as Soret and Dufour effects. We finally compare our results with those of other existing formalisms.

## Key claim

The authors derive second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equations for multi-component systems with multiple conserved currents by generalizing the Israel-Stewart theory and Grad's moment method, finding that extra moment equations associated with conserved currents must be introduced beyond the conventional moment equations to consistently match the number of equations with unknowns and satisfy the Onsager reciprocal relations.

## Method

The authors extend the Israel-Stewart second-order theory by considering the third moment of the distribution function and constraining its derivative from the second law of thermodynamics. They introduce additional moment equations of the form ∂_α I_J^{μα} = Y_J^μ for each conserved current, which are derivatives of higher-order moments for charge number conservations, and determine the distortion of distribution functions through a generalized Grad's moment method with 10+4N unknowns. The constitutive equations for dissipative currents are obtained by estimating the derivatives ∂_α I^{μνα} and ∂_α I_J^{μα} and solving the resulting matching conditions using the second law of thermodynamics and Onsager reciprocal relations.

## Key numbers

- The number of additional macroscopic variables in systems with non-vanishing chemical potentials is 10+4N, where N is the number of conserved currents
- The system has 4+N equations from energy-momentum conservation and charge number conservations for ideal hydrodynamics, with 5+N unknowns (e_0, P_0, n_J0, and u^μ with 3 unknowns)
- The energy-momentum tensor has 10 independent components and the conserved currents have 4N independent components in the dissipative case
- The matching matrices J_0, J_1, and J_2 are defined with dimensions (2+N)×(2+N), (1+N)×(1+N), and 1×1 respectively

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart second-order theory (Israel 1979) and Grad's 14-moment method to the previously uninvestigated case of multi-component systems with multiple conserved currents, which is the most important situation for ultra-relativistic systems at very high temperature where particle creation and annihilation occur frequently. The formalism enables the development of viscous hydrodynamic models for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where ideal hydrodynamic models (Hirano 2002, Teaney 2000) have successfully reproduced experimental data but viscous corrections are needed to capture the correct physics. The authors compare their results with other existing formalisms and find that consistent expansion of the entropy current leads to constitutive equations involving terms not appearing in the original Israel-Stewart theory even in the single component limit, including thermal diffusion effects such as Soret and Dufour effects.
