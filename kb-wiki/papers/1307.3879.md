# Nuclear Symmetry Energy: constraints from Giant Quadrupole Resonances and Parity Violating Electron Scattering

> Experimental and theoretical efforts are being devoted to the study of observables that can shed light on the properties of the nuclear symmetry energy. We present our new results on the excitation energy [X. Roca-Maza et al., Phys. Rev. C 87, 034301 (2013)] and polarizability of the Isovector Giant Quadrupole Resonance (IVGQR), which has been the object of new experimental investigation [S. S. Henshaw et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 107, 222501 (2011)]. We also present our theoretical analysis on the parity violating asymmetry at the kinematics of the Lead Radius Experiment [S. Abrahamyan et al. (PREx Collaboration), Phys. Rev. Lett. 108, 112502 (2012)] and highlight its relation with the density dependence of the symmetry energy [X. Roca-Maza et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 106, 252501 (2011)].

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the excitation energies of the isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonances (GQRs) in 208Pb, combined with the symmetry energy at saturation, are correlated with the neutron skin thickness, and that the parity violating asymmetry measured in the Lead Radius Experiment (PREx) is linearly correlated with the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy, but these correlations are derived from macroscopic models and tested within specific families of energy density functionals (EDFs) rather than being model-independent.

## Method

The authors used a Quantum Harmonic Oscillator (QHO) approach and the Droplet Model (DM) to derive analytical expressions relating the neutron skin thickness Δrnp to combinations of the isoscalar and isovector GQR excitation energies (E_x^IS and E_x^IV) and the symmetry energy at saturation J. They then tested these correlations by performing systematic variations of J in two families of EDFs: a non-relativistic SAMi family (J varied from 27 to 31 MeV in 1 MeV steps) and a relativistic DD-ME family (J varied from 30 to 38 MeV in 2 MeV steps). For the parity violating asymmetry, they calculated Apv at the PREx kinematics for a large set of EDFs, accounting for Coulomb distortions, and examined its correlation with the slope parameter L.

## Key numbers

- Experimental weighted average for the isovector GQR excitation energy in 208Pb: E_x^IV = 22.7 ± 0.2 MeV
- Experimental weighted average for the isoscalar GQR excitation energy in 208Pb: E_x^IS = 10.9 ± 0.1 MeV
- Potential contribution to the symmetry energy at density 0.1 fm^-3, derived from the GQR data: S^pot(0.1 fm^-3) = 11.1 ± 0.3 MeV
- Correlation coefficient between the product m_{-1}(Q) * J and the neutron skin thickness Δrnp in 208Pb across a large set of EDFs: r = 0.98
- Correlation coefficient between the parity violating asymmetry Apv at PREx kinematics and the slope parameter L: r = 0.97
- The surface contribution to the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb was found to be almost constant at 0.09 ± 0.01 fm across a large set of EDFs

## Context

This work extends the analysis of isovector properties of nuclei by connecting giant quadrupole resonance data and parity violating electron scattering to the nuclear symmetry energy, building on prior experimental work by Henshaw (2011) on the IVGQR and the PREx collaboration (2012) on parity violating asymmetry. The results enable improved constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the neutron skin thickness, complementing earlier studies by Brown (2000), Tsang (2012), Centelles (2009), and Trippa (2008), and provide a framework for interpreting future experiments at facilities like the High Intensity Gamma-Ray Source (HIγS) and Jefferson Laboratory.
