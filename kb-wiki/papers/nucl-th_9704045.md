# Freeze-Out Time in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions from Coulomb Effects in Transverse Pion Spectra

> The influence of the nuclear Coulomb field on transverse spectra of $π^+$ and $π^-$ measured in $Pb+Pb$ reactions at 158 A GeV has been investigated. Pion trajectories are calculated in the field of an expanding fireball. The observed enhancement of the $π^-/π^+$ ratio at small momenta depends on the temperature and transverse expansion velocity of the source, the rapidity distribution of the net positive charge, and mainly the time of the freeze-out.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at low transverse momenta in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV can be explained by Coulomb acceleration of pions in the field of the positively charged fireball, and the magnitude of this effect is most sensitive to the freeze-out time, providing an independent constraint on this timescale.

## Method

The authors modeled the pion source at freeze-out as a thermal distribution with temperature T and collective transverse flow, superimposed on a longitudinally expanding Bjorken-type fireball. They calculated pion trajectories in the retarded electromagnetic field of the net positive charge distribution, which was parameterized from measured proton rapidity distributions. The final π⁻/π⁺ ratios were obtained by Monte-Carlo sampling over initial conditions and compared to NA44 experimental data for Pb+Pb, S+Pb, and S+S collisions.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out time τ_f = 7 fm/c for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV, extracted from fitting the π⁻/π⁺ ratio
- Temperature T = 120 MeV and mean transverse expansion velocity ⟨β⟩ = 0.42 for Pb+Pb, with participant charge Z_cent = 122 and geometric radius R_geom = 5.8 fm
- For S+S and S+Pb at 200 A GeV: temperature T = 130 MeV and mean transverse velocity ⟨β⟩ = 0.29
- Longitudinal HBT radius R_l ≈ 5.5 fm gives τ_f ≈ 8 fm/c; transverse HBT radii give R = 2R_s ≈ 10 fm, also consistent with τ_f ≈ 8 fm/c
- Rapidity distribution parameters for Pb+Pb: Δy_ch = 0.84, y_1 = 1.1, implying a rapidity loss of 1.7
- Estimated enhancement 4Δ ≈ 0.6 from the approximate formula using dN^ch/dy ≈ 35, R ≈ 10 fm, and f ≈ 2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Coulomb effects in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions (early references) to ultrarelativistic SPS energies, where the NA44 collaboration had recently observed enhanced π⁻/π⁺ ratios at low transverse masses. The extracted freeze-out time of 7 fm/c is compatible with values from HBT analyses (NA44HBT) but substantially smaller than the 15 fm/c predicted by RQMD calculations (Sorge). The results enable using pion charge ratios as an independent probe of freeze-out dynamics, and predict smaller Coulomb effects at RHIC and LHC energies due to reduced stopping and net charge at midrapidity.
