# Study of $^{7}$Li+$^{10}$B elastic scattering and the lithium-induced reaction of one-nucleon transfers from $^{10}$B($^{7}$Li,$^{6}$Li)$^{11}$B

> The angular distributions of elastic scattering of $^{7}$Li, as well as the lithium-induced reaction of one-nucleon transfers $^{10}$B($^{7}$Li,$^{6}$Li)$^{11}$B were measured at $E_\text{lab}$ = 58 MeV. Experiment was done using U-400 accelerator beam of the FLNR JINR, Dubna. Angular distribution for reaction $^{10}$B($^{7}$Li,$^{6}$Li)$^{11}$B with excitation of the 3.56 MeV state ($^{6}$Li*) is presented for the first time. The DWBA analysis of the differential cross section of the $^{10}$B($^{7}$Li,$^{6}$Li)$^{11}$B ground state (g.s.) transition and excited ($J^π$ = 0$^+$, $T$ = 1, $E$ = 3.56 MeV) state of $^{6}$Li transition was performed. The optical model potentials were obtained by fitting of measured elastic scattering data and evaluating parameters for the output reaction channels. Phenomenological approach based on solving an approximate equation for the reaction form factor was used to determine its radial dependence and empirical values of asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC). Obtained values of ANCs for the $^{6}$Li$_\text{g.s.}$ and $^{6}$Li*(3.56 MeV) states are in agreement with the literature ones. Comparison of the radial dependences of form factors shows that the wave function of the $^{6}$Li nucleus in excited ($J^π$ = 0$^+$, $T$ = 1, $E$ = 3.56 MeV) state has increased spatial dimension compared to the ground state. This result is an argument in favor of a halo existence in $^{6}$Li*(3.56 MeV) state, while the question of a halo in $^{6}$Li$_\text{g.s.}$ still leaves open.

# Study of $^{7}$Li+$^{10}$B elastic scattering and the lithium-induced reaction of one-nucleon transfers from $^{10}$B($^{7}$Li,$^{6}$Li)$^{11}$B

## Key claim
The radial dependence of the reaction form factor for the $^{6}$Li*(3.56 MeV) state shows an 8% larger root-mean-square radius compared to the $^{6}$Li ground state, providing evidence for a halo structure in the excited state; however, the question of a halo in $^{6}$Li$_\text{g.s.}$ remains open, and this result contradicts ab initio calculations by Rodkin (2023) which found the radii to be practically equal.

## Method
Angular distributions of elastic scattering $^{7}$Li+$^{10}$B and the one-nucleon transfer reaction $^{10}$B($^{7}$Li,$^{6}$Li)$^{11}$B were measured at $E_\text{lab}$ = 58 MeV using the U-400 cyclotron at FLNR JINR, Dubna, with two groups of $\Delta E-E$ silicon detector telescopes covering laboratory angles from 7 to 30 degrees. DWBA analysis was performed using the FRESCO code, with optical model potentials obtained by fitting elastic scattering data for the input channel and evaluating parameters for the output channels using $^{6}$Li+$^{12}$C scattering data as a reference. A phenomenological approach solving an approximate equation for the reaction form factor with a three-parameter Woods-Saxon potential was used to determine the radial dependence and asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for both the ground state and the 3.56 MeV excited state of $^{6}$Li.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: $E_\text{lab}$ = 58 MeV for $^{7}$Li ions, with energy resolution $\Delta E_\text{(FWHM)}$ = 0.5 MeV
- Target thickness: $t \approx 0.05$ mg/cm$^2$ for the self-supporting $^{10}$B target, containing 9% $^{12}$C and 6% $^{16}$O impurities
- Beam spot diameter: 3.6 mm, angular aperture $\Delta \Theta \approx 0.2^\circ$
- Solid angle of detector telescopes: $\Omega = 1.3 \times 10^{-4}$ sr (first group) and $2.2 \times 10^{-4}$ sr (second group)
- Angular spacing between neighboring telescopes: $\Delta \Theta_\text{LAB} = 3.2^\circ$ (first group) and $1.7^\circ$ (second group)
- Energy resolution of detectors: no worse than $\Delta E = 60$ keV ($^{226}$Ra $\alpha$-source)
- Optical potential parameters for $^{7}$Li+$^{10}$B at 58 MeV: $V_r = 250$ MeV, $W_S = 10.0$ MeV, $W_D = 22.0$ MeV, volume integrals $-J_V = 415$ MeV fm$^3$, $-J_W = 177$ MeV fm$^3$, reaction cross section $\sigma_r = 1468$ mb
- Optical potential parameters for $^{6}$Li$_\text{(g.s.)}$+$^{11}$B at 59.2 MeV: $V_r = 285$ MeV, $W_S = 13.0$ MeV, $W_D = 17.0$ MeV, $\sigma_r = 1365$ mb
- Optical potential parameters for $^{6}$Li*(3.56)+$^{11}$B at 53.7 MeV: $V_r = 285$ MeV, $W_S = 12.0$ MeV, $W_D = 16.0$ MeV, $\sigma_r = 1362$ mb
- ANC for $^{7}$Li(g.s.), n$^{6}$Li(g.s.): $NC^2 = 3.05(46)$ fm$^{-1}$ (from Demyanova 2024), compared to theoretical value 2.94 fm$^{-1}$ (Timofeyuk 2010) and experimental 3.17(53) fm$^{-1}$ (Gulamov 1995)
- ANC for $^{7}$Li(g.s.), n$^{6}$Li*(3.56): $NC^2 = 2.76(39)$ fm$^{-1}$, compared to theoretical 2.47 fm$^{-1}$ (Timofeyuk 2010) and experimental 2.91(35) fm$^{-1}$ (Gulamov 1995) and 3.16(47) fm$^{-1}$ (Demyanova 2024)
- ANC for $^{11}$B(g.s.), n$^{10}$B(g.s.): $NC^2 = 12.4(1.9)$ fm$^{-1}$, compared to theoretical 13.1 fm$^{-1}$ (Timofeyuk 2010) and experimental 31.4(3.0) fm$^{-1}$ (Gulamov 1995)
- Root-mean-square radius of form factor for $^{6}$Li ground state: 3.23 fm
- Root-mean-square radius of form factor for $^{6}$Li*(3.56 MeV) state: 3.50 fm (8% larger than ground state)
- Root-mean-square radius of form factor for $^{11}$B(g.s.), n$^{10}$B(g.s.): 2.89 fm
- Measurement error of absolute cross section: estimated at approximately 15%

## Context
This work extends studies of the isobaric triplet $A=6$ ($^{6}$He, $^{6}$Li, $^{6}$Be) by providing the first simultaneous measurement of direct one-nucleon transfer reactions populating both the ground state and the 3.56 MeV excited state of $^{6}$Li, which is the isobaric analog of $^{6}$He$_\text{g.s.}$. Previous studies by Cortina-Gil (1996, 1998), Brown (1996), and Li (2002) investigated the charge exchange reaction $^{1}$H($^{6}$He,$^{6}$Li)$n$ and concluded a two-nucleon halo structure for the 3.56 MeV state, while Demyanova (2018) estimated the $rms$-radius of this state using the Modified Diffraction Model on $^{3}$He+$^{6}$Li scattering data. The present results provide empirical radial dependences of form factors that can be compared with theoretical calculations by Timofeyuk (2010) and ab initio NCSM calculations by Rodkin (2023), and enable further investigation of the spatial structure of $^{6}$Li states through direct transfer reactions rather than multi-step reaction channels.
