# From Orthogonalizing Pseudopotential to the Feshbach-Schur Projection

> The Pauli exclusion principle imposes an important structural constraint in cluster descriptions of light nuclei and is commonly taken into account using methods such as the Resonating Group Method (RGM), the Orthogonality Condition Model (OCM), and the Orthogonalizing Pseudopotential (OPP) approach. The latter provides a practical implementation for suppressing Pauli-forbidden states in few-body calculations through the introduction of a large auxiliary coupling constant $λ_0$ and an associated limiting procedure. Exact $λ_0$-eliminated formulations have appeared in the literature. It is shown that the OPP method may be interpreted as the singular $λ_0\to\infty$ limit of the Feshbach-Schur projection, and that the Schur complement provides a natural operator-level framework for understanding this connection. In contrast to earlier approaches, the elimination is derived explicitly as a closed Schur-complement operator identity. When formulated in terms of operators and Green's functions, the Feshbach-Schur projection eliminates Pauli-forbidden components algebraically and avoids the explicit introduction of large pseudopotential parameters. This reformulation clarifies the implementation of Pauli projection in cluster models and provides a convenient framework for few-body calculations.

# From Orthogonalizing Pseudopotential to the Feshbach-Schur Projection

## Key claim

The Orthogonalizing Pseudopotential (OPP) method for enforcing the Pauli exclusion principle in cluster models can be interpreted as the singular $\lambda_0 \to \infty$ limit of a Feshbach-Schur projection, where the Schur complement provides an explicit operator-level identity that eliminates the Pauli-forbidden subspace algebraically without introducing a large auxiliary parameter. This connection holds provided the Pauli-forbidden subspace is well defined and linearly independent, and the derivation is presented for a general multi-rank separable interaction containing both allowed and forbidden form factors.

## Method

The paper derives the exact $\lambda_0 \to \infty$ limit of the OPP method by applying the Schur complement formula to the block-structured coupling matrix of an extended separable interaction basis that includes both Pauli-allowed form factors $\{\chi_i\}$ and Pauli-forbidden states $\{\phi_f\}$. The derivation is carried out at three levels: for a separable $T$-matrix in momentum space, for the configuration-space radial Schrödinger equation using projection operators $Q = 1 - P_f$, and for the resolvent (Green's operator) using the Feshbach projection formalism. The resulting Feshbach-Schur projection (FSP) expression removes the forbidden subspace algebraically, yielding a $\lambda_0$-independent effective $\tau$-matrix and projected resolvent.

## Key numbers

- For $^6$He ground-state binding energy: with $\lambda_0 = 10^0$ MeV, $E = -0.449773$ MeV; with $\lambda_0 = 10^7$ MeV, $E = -0.377586$ MeV; at $\lambda_0 \to \infty$ (FSP), $E = -0.377586$ MeV.
- For $^6$Li ground-state binding energy: with $\lambda_0 = 10^0$ MeV, $E = -4.535299$ MeV; with $\lambda_0 = 10^7$ MeV, $E = -4.148903$ MeV; at $\lambda_0 \to \infty$ (FSP), $E = -4.148903$ MeV.
- The residual forbidden-state coupling in finite-$\lambda_0$ calculations scales as $1/(\varepsilon_f - E + \lambda_0)$, where $\varepsilon_f = \langle\phi_f|H|\phi_f\rangle$ is the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the forbidden state.
- The OPP strength $\lambda_0$ is varied from $10^0$ to $10^7$ MeV in the numerical benchmark; convergence to the asymptotic limit requires $\lambda_0 \gtrsim 10^5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formal understanding of the Orthogonalizing Pseudopotential method, originally formulated by Krasnopol'skii and Kukulin (1974, 1975), by providing an explicit operator-level connection to the Feshbach projection formalism (Feshbach, 1958, 1962) and the Schur complement (Schur, 1917). Prior analytic $\lambda_0$-eliminated formulations have appeared in configuration-space calculations (Schellingerhout, 1993), operator-level $T$-matrix formulations (Lehman; Fujiwara, 2004), and momentum-space Faddeev/AGS approaches (Nogga, 2005; Hlophe, 2017), but no prior work presented an explicit block-matrix derivation of the $\lambda_0 \to \infty$ limit for a general multi-rank separable interaction. The FSP formulation enables parameter-free Pauli projection in few-body calculations, avoiding the numerical stiffness and residual $\lambda_0$-dependence documented in three-$\alpha$ calculations (Tursunov, 2022) and studies of the $^6$He spectrum (Kukulin, 1995).
