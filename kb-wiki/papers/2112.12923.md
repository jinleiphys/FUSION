# Possible halo structure of $^{62,72}$Ca by forbidden-state-free locally peaked Gaussians

> In order to efficiently describe nucleon orbits around a heavy core nucleus, we propose locally peaked Gaussians orthogonalized to the occupied bound states in the core. We show the advantage of those functions in both numerical stability and fast convergence by taking examples of touchstone calcium isotopes $^{62,72}$Ca in $^{60,70}{\rm Ca}+n+n$ three-body models. Both weakly bound configurations and continuum coupling effect are taken into account. We evaluate the neutron radii and the occupation probabilities of two-neutron configurations not only for the ground state but also for some particle-bound excited states by varying the strength of the core-neutron interaction. The emergence of the halo structure in the ground state depends on the energy difference between $2s_{1/2}$ and $0g_{9/2}$ orbits. Two-neutron [consisting of $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration] and one-neutron [consisting of $(g_{9/2}s_{1/2})$ configuration] halo structure of $^{62}$Ca can coexist in narrow energy spacing provided that both of $2s_{1/2}$ and $0g_{9/2}$ orbits are almost degenerate and barely bound. The ground-state structure of $^{72}$Ca is likely to be a two-neutron halo, although its emergence depends on the position of the $2s_{1/2}$ level.

## Key claim

The emergence of a halo structure in the ground state of $^{62,72}$Ca depends critically on the energy spacing between the $2s_{1/2}$ and $0g_{9/2}$ single-particle orbits; two-neutron and one-neutron halo configurations can coexist only when these orbits are nearly degenerate and barely bound, not simply when the system is weakly bound overall.

## Method

The authors used a $^{60,70}{\rm Ca}+n+n$ three-body model with a Woods-Saxon core-neutron potential and the Minnesota potential for the neutron-neutron interaction. They introduced forbidden-state-free locally peaked Gaussians (FFLPG) as a single-particle basis, constructed by projecting out the harmonic-oscillator approximations of the occupied bound states in the core, and performed variational calculations using the stochastic variational method to select optimal basis parameters.

## Key numbers

- The $2s_{1/2}$ single-particle energy was set to $-0.01$ MeV by adjusting the Woods-Saxon depth to $V_0=44.03$ MeV (set A) and $41.89$ MeV (set B).
- In the degenerate $sg$ limit (set A, $V_1=11.40$ MeV), the $2s_{1/2}$ and $0g_{9/2}$ orbits have root-mean-square radii of $36.0$ fm and $5.04$ fm, respectively.
- The $0_1^+$ ground state of $^{62}$Ca has energy $-1.19$ MeV from the two-neutron threshold, a two-neutron rms radius of $5.08$ fm, and $94\%$ $(g_{9/2})^2$ configuration.
- The $0_2^+$ state of $^{62}$Ca has energy $-0.14$ MeV, a two-neutron rms radius of $12.8$ fm, and $91\%$ $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration.
- The $4_2^+$ state of $^{62}$Ca has energy $-0.11$ MeV, a two-neutron rms radius of $10.1$ fm, and $87\%$ $(g_{9/2}s_{1/2})$ configuration.
- The $2_{1/2}$ single-particle rms radius is about $36$ fm, but the $0_2^+$ two-neutron rms radius is only about $13$ fm due to correlated motion.

## Context

This work extends the study of two-neutron halo nuclei from light systems like $^{11}$Li (Tanihata 1985) and $^{22}$C (Horiuchi 2006) to heavier calcium isotopes, where the core has many occupied orbits. It addresses the debate between coupled-cluster predictions of a halo in $^{62}$Ca (Hagen 2013) and energy-density-functional or shell-model calculations placing the dripline near $^{70}$Ca (Erler 2012, Coraggio 2020). The FFLPG method enables future studies of core-plus-multinucleon systems with heavy cores, where the orthogonality condition model becomes numerically challenging.
