# A-dependence of phi-meson production in p+A collisions

> A systematic analysis of the A-dependence of phi-meson production in proton-nucleus collisions is presented. We apply different formalisms for the evaluation of the phi-meson distortion in nuclei and discuss the theoretical uncertainties of the data analysis. The corresponding results are compared to theoretical predictions. We also discuss the interpretation of the extracted results with respect to different observables and provide relations between frequently used definitions. The perspectives of future experiments are evaluated and estimates based on our systematical study are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed near-linear A-dependence (α ≈ 1) of φ-meson production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions, which contradicts the expectation from direct production mechanisms where α should be around 0.7, can be quantitatively understood through a two-step production mechanism involving intermediate pions that produce φ-mesons throughout the full nuclear volume.

## Method

The authors apply the eikonal (Glauber) formalism to calculate the A-dependence of φ-meson production in p+A collisions, using the tρ approximation with Wood-Saxon nuclear density distributions. They compute effective nucleon numbers NA from Eq. (4) for direct production and extend the formalism to a two-step mechanism where an incident proton first produces pions (iN→jN) which then produce φ-mesons (jN→fN), accounting for distortion of all particles. The calculations are performed for multiple nuclei (C, Al, Cu, Ag, Au, Pb) and fitted to an Aα power law to extract average slope parameters.

## Key numbers

- For direct production with σi=40 mb and σf=0, the maximal slope α is approximately 0.7, driven by incident proton distortion
- For a 12C target with σ=40 mb, the leading-order effective collision number N_A (ν=1) is 3.4, while multiple scattering corrections give ν=2: 1.5, ν=3: 0.79, ν=4: 0.39, ν=5: 0.176
- For a 207Pb target with σ=40 mb, the leading-order N_A (ν=1) is 9.1, with ν=2: 5.9, ν=3: 5.2, ν=4: 4.9, ν=5: 4.7, ν=6: 4.3
- The average slope α for σi=40 mb and σf=30 mb is 0.35 ± 0.18 when fitted across C, Al, Cu, Ag, Au, and Pb nuclei
- For σi=0 and σf=50 mb (photoproduction case), the average slope α is 0.66
- The effective collision number ratio NPb/NC saturates for σf ≥ 40 mb, making the model insensitive to σf above this limit

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic analysis of φ-meson photoproduction from nuclear targets (Sibirtsev 2007), which showed that observed A-dependence could be understood by including ω-φ mixing in nuclei. The paper addresses the discrepancy between direct production expectations (α ≈ 0.7) and experimental results from Abt, Antipov, Dijkstra, Daum, and Bailey showing α ≈ 1 for φ-meson production in high-energy pA collisions. The two-step mechanism involving intermediate pions provides a framework for interpreting data from the ANKE Collaboration at COSY (Hartmann) and enables future experiments to extract the φ-nucleon forward scattering amplitude and in-medium modification of the φ-meson spectral function.
