# The proton structure via double parton scattering

> In this talk we present the results of the investigation on the so called double parton distribution functions (dPDFs), accessible quantities in high energy proton-proton and proton nucleus collisions, in double parton scattering processes (DPS). These new and almost unknown distributions encode information on how partons inside a proton are correlated among each other and represent a new tool to explore the three dimensional partonic structure of hadrons. In the present contribution, results of the calculations of dPFDs are presented also including phenomenological investigations on the impact of double correlations in experimental observables, showing how the latter could be observed in the next LHC run. In addition we discuss how present information on experimental observables could be related to the transverse proton structure.

# The proton structure via double parton scattering

## Key claim
Double parton correlations (DPC) in double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) cannot be neglected, and their effects on the effective cross section σeff should be observable in the next LHC run at 68% confidence level with a luminosity of approximately 1000 fb⁻¹, provided the departure of σeff from a constant value can be measured.

## Method
The author calculates dPDFs within constituent quark models (CQM) at a low hadronic scale, then applies perturbative QCD evolution to reach the energy scales of experiments. The effective cross section σeff is evaluated from these dPDFs without assuming the factorized ansatz, and its dependence on longitudinal momentum fractions (rapidity) is studied as a signature of double parton correlations. The same-sign W pair production process at LHC is used as a specific channel to quantify the observability of these correlations.

## Key numbers
- The total W-charge summed DPS cross section for same-sign W pair production (both W decays into same sign muons) is σ⁺⁺ + σ⁻⁻ [fb] ∼ 0.69, consistent with results using the factorized ansatz with MSTW parametrization.
- The mean value ⟨σ̃eff⟩ for the same-sign WW process is ∼ 21.04 mb.
- For pion dPDFs, the mean effective cross section is σ̄eff ∼ 40 mb, larger than the proton case.
- The experimental constant value σeff ≃ 15 mb is compatible with data within errors under the uncorrelated ansatz.
- The inequality (σeff)/(3π) ≤ ⟨z⊥²⟩ ≤ (σeff)/(π) relates the effective cross section to the mean transverse distance between active partons.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous CQM calculations of dPDFs (Rinaldi et al., 2015-2018) and provides a path to test the factorized ansatz (Eq. 1) that is commonly assumed in experimental analyses. The results contradict the assumption that double parton correlations can be neglected, as encoded in the fully factorized approximation. The analysis enables future experimental searches at the LHC to extract information on the transverse proton structure from σeff measurements, and provides a model-independent inequality linking σeff to the mean transverse parton separation.
