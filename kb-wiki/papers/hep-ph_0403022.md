# Microscopic description of anisotropic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Anisotropic flow of hadrons is studied in heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. The model was found to reproduce correctly many of the flow features, e.g., the wiggle structure of direct flow of nucleons at midrapidity, or centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum dependences of elliptic flow. Further predictions are made. The differences in the development of the anisotropic flow components are linked to the freeze-out conditions, which are quite different for baryons and mesons.

# Microscopic description of anisotropic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The quark-gluon string model (QGSM), which does not implement quark-gluon plasma formation, can reproduce many observed features of anisotropic flow at SPS and RHIC energies, including the wiggle structure of nucleon directed flow and the centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum dependences of elliptic flow. Discrepancies between QGSM predictions and experimental data should be interpreted as indications for new physics beyond the model's hadronic degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors employed the microscopic quark-gluon string model (QGSM), based on the 1/N_c expansion of QCD amplitudes and Gribov-Regge theory, to simulate heavy ion collisions at SPS (sqrt(s)=17.8 AGeV) and RHIC (sqrt(s)=130 and 200 AGeV) energies. The model treats hadronic interactions via Reggeon and Pomeron exchange, string formation and fragmentation, and subsequent hadron rescattering, without implementing a QGP phase. Anisotropic flow coefficients v_1 (directed flow) and v_2 (elliptic flow) were computed from the Fourier expansion of the invariant particle distribution as functions of rapidity, transverse momentum, and impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- Directed flow of nucleons in Pb+Pb at SPS shows deviations from linear behavior starting at b/b_max = 0.45 in the central rapidity window.
- Elliptic flow of kaons at RHIC rises linearly from near zero at p_T <= 0.25 GeV/c to v_2^K(p_T) approximately 10% within the interval 0.25 <= p_T <= 1.5 GeV/c, then saturates at p_T >= 1.5 GeV/c.
- Elliptic flow at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV is only 10-15% stronger than that at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV.
- The elliptic flow of pions in QGSM calculations at RHIC is twice as large as obtained with RQMD (Sorge, 1999).
- At sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, elliptic flow caused by subprocesses without hard and multichain contributions cannot exceed 3-3.5%.
- The dN/dt distribution of pions has a peak at t=5-6 fm/c, while baryon distributions peak later at t=10-12 fm/c for Au+Au at b=8 fm and sqrt(s)=130 AGeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of collective flow using microscopic transport models, particularly the RQMD model (Sorge, 1999) and hydrodynamic approaches (Csernai and Rischke, 1999). The QGSM predictions for the wiggle structure of nucleon directed flow at SPS were subsequently confirmed by the NA49 Collaboration. The paper links differences in mesonic and baryonic flow to their distinct freeze-out time distributions, contrasting with the sharp freeze-out assumption in hydrodynamic models. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from STAR, PHENIX, and PHOBOS collaborations to identify signatures of QGP formation not captured by hadronic models.
