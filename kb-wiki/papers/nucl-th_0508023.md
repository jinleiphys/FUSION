# Neutron localization induced by the pairing field in an inhomogeneous neutron matter

> It is shown that in an inhomogeneous neutron matter the pairing field bounds neutrons around the Fermi level leading to the formation of localized Andreev states. In the case of the inner crust of neutron stars the localization length has been determined as a function of the nuclear density.

## Key claim

In an inhomogeneous neutron matter, the pairing field always binds neutrons around the Fermi level into localized Andreev states, even for an arbitrarily weak deviation from a homogeneous pairing field, contrary to the behavior of normal three-dimensional potentials.

## Method

The author solves the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation for a spherical pairing well model, first using the Andreev approximation to obtain analytic solutions, then performing variational calculations with a Gaussian-localized wave function ansatz. The pairing field is modeled as a Gaussian well superimposed on a constant background, with parameters taken from two different density-dependent pairing gaps: one from BCS theory with the Argonne v18 interaction, and one from s-wave N-N scattering data that includes polarization corrections.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the localization length to the Wigner-Seitz cell radius, \(R_C/\eta\), exceeds 1 for stronger (BCS) pairing at lower densities, indicating practical localization; for weaker (polarization-corrected) pairing, \(R_C/\eta\) reaches 0.6.
- Throughout the inner crust, \(\sqrt{\langle E^2\rangle}/\Delta_{out} \approx 0.9\) and \(\eta/\xi \approx 50\), where \(\xi\) is the coherence length.
- The pairing gap maximum from BCS theory is around 3 MeV, while inclusion of polarization corrections reduces it to about 1 MeV; non-perturbative calculations for fermions in the unitary limit predict a pairing gap of about \(0.6\epsilon_F\) at \(T=0\).

## Context

This work extends studies of pairing in neutron star crusts, where the pairing field's density dependence had been calculated by KKC (2001) and Schulze (2001), and where the inhomogeneous structure of nuclear impurities had been described by PRA, BMA, and MPH (various years). The results show that the BCS approach, which neglects quasiparticle scattering, is insufficient for determining the neutron band structure in the inner crust, and that a full Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov or BdG treatment is needed, analogous to the requirement noted for nuclei near the neutron drip line by Bulgac (1980). The findings also imply that transport properties may be affected, as the effective mass for bound neutrons can become very large when \(R_C/\eta > 1\).
