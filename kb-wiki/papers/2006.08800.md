# Quark spin - thermal vorticity alignment and the Lambda, anti-Lambda polarization in heavy-ion collisions

> It has been proposed that the $Λ$ and $\overlineΛ$ polarizations observed in heavy-ion collisions are due to the interaction between quark spin and thermal vorticity. In this work we report on a computation of the relaxation time required for this alignment to occur at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, considering quarks with a finite mass. The calculation is performed after modelling the interaction by means of an effective vertex which couples the thermal gluons and quarks within the vortical medium. We show that the effect of the quark mass is to reduce the relaxation time as compared to the massless quark case. An intrinsic global polarization of quarks/antiquarks emerges which is shown to be linked with the $Λ$/$\barΛ$ polarization.

## Key claim

The relaxation time for quark spin to align with thermal vorticity in heavy-ion collisions is reduced when a finite quark mass (100 MeV) is considered, compared to the massless case; however, the computed relaxation times for antiquarks remain larger than those for quarks at the same collision energy and impact parameter.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop quark self-energy at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential using an effective vertex that couples quark spin to thermal vorticity, with the gluon propagator treated in the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation. The interaction rate is obtained by integrating over the available phase space, incorporating spectral densities for longitudinal and transverse gluon modes and a bare quark propagator with finite mass. The relaxation time is defined as the inverse of the total interaction rate, and the calculation is performed for strange quarks with mass 100 MeV at collision energies of 10 and 200 GeV for semicentral collisions at impact parameter b = 10 fm.

## Key numbers

- For massive quarks (m_q = 100 MeV) at √s_NN = 10 GeV and b = 10 fm, the initial angular velocity is ω ≃ 0.072 fm⁻¹; for √s_NN = 200 GeV, ω ≃ 0.051 fm⁻¹.
- For massive quarks, relaxation times τ ≲ 5 fm in the temperature range 150 MeV < T < 200 MeV, where the phase transition is expected.
- For massless quarks at √s_NN = 10 GeV and b = 10 fm, the initial angular velocity is ω ≃ 0.12 fm⁻¹; for √s_NN = 200 GeV, ω ≃ 0.10 fm⁻¹.
- The freeze-out parametrization used is T(μ_B) = 166 - 139μ_B² - 53μ_B⁴ (MeV) and μ_B(√s_NN) = 1308/(1000 + 0.273√s_NN) (MeV).
- The intrinsic global polarization for both quarks and antiquarks tends to 1 for t ≃ 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends the massless quark calculation of Ayala (2020) by incorporating a finite quark mass, finding that mass reduces the relaxation time compared to the massless case. The results use the angular velocity values from Deng and Huang (2020) for semicentral collisions. The computed relaxation times are within the expected lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma, and the authors suggest that further effects during hadronization, such as the core-corona model of Ayala and Cuautle, could enhance anti-Lambda polarization relative to Lambda polarization, potentially explaining experimental observations.
