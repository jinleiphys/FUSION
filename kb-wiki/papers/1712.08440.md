# Model of multiple Dirac eikonal scattering of protons by nuclei

> The model of multiple Dirac eikonal scattering of incident proton by target-nucleus nucleons is developed, in which new expressions for the elastic $pA$-scattering amplitudes are obtained from the multiple scattering Watson series with employing the eikonal approximation for the Dirac propagators of the free proton motion between successive scattering acts on nucleons. Basing on this model, calculations for the complete set of observables of the elastic $p+^{40}$Ca and $p+^{208}$Pb at 800 MeV have been performed with using proton-nucleon amplitudes determined from the phase analysis and the nucleon densities obtained from describing the target-nucleus structure in the relativistic mean-field approximation. A comparison has been made of the results of these calculations with analogous calculations on the basis of the Glauber multiple diffraction theory.

## Key claim

The authors develop a multiple Dirac eikonal scattering (MDES) model for elastic proton-nucleus scattering, deriving new expressions for the scattering amplitudes from the Watson multiple scattering series using the eikonal approximation for Dirac propagators, but the model assumes that scalar and vector nucleon densities are equal and that nucleon correlations can be neglected, which limits its accuracy for light nuclei and large scattering angles.

## Method

The authors start from the Watson multiple scattering series for the proton-nucleus T-matrix, employ the eikonal approximation for the Dirac propagator of the free proton between successive scatterings on target nucleons, and average over the nuclear ground state using relativistic mean-field (RMF) single-particle densities. They derive a symmetrized eikonal amplitude that includes spin-rotation operators at each collision step, and relate the Dirac invariant pN-amplitudes to the standard nonrelativistic central and spin-orbit amplitudes A(q) and C(q) using a simplified forward-scattering relation. Calculations are performed for elastic p+40Ca and p+208Pb at 800 MeV using pN-amplitudes from phase analysis and RMF nucleon densities.

## Key numbers

- Elastic p+40Ca and p+208Pb scattering at 800 MeV (incident proton kinetic energy in the laboratory frame).
- The momentum transfer in the pN and pA center-of-mass frames is taken to coincide: q_c^2 = q^2.
- The eikonal propagator expansion is performed in powers of 1/k, where k is the magnitude of the initial/final momentum in the pA c.m. frame.
- The relationship between Dirac invariant amplitudes and nonrelativistic amplitudes uses the factor k/k_c, where k_c is the momentum in the pN c.m. frame.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber multiple diffraction scattering theory (MDST) by constructing a relativistic eikonal model based on the Dirac equation, building on prior formulations by Bleuler et al. (Ble1, Ble2) and Wall et al. (Wall1, Wall2) that considered corrections to MDST. The model enables comparison with the relativistic impulse approximation approach of McNeil et al. (McN) and Ray et al. (Ray), and provides a framework for analyzing precise measurements of differential cross sections and spin observables in intermediate-energy proton-nucleus scattering to extract nuclear structure information.
