# Energy dependence of the deformed nuclear structure at small-$x$

> We quantify the effect of high-energy JIMWLK evolution on the deformed structure or heavy (Uranium) and intermediate (Ruthenium) nuclei. The soft gluon emissions in the high-energy evolution are found to drive the initially deformed nuclei towards a more spherical shape, although the evolution is slow ,especially for the longest distance-scale quadrupole deformation. We confirm a linear relationship between the squared eccentricity $\varepsilon_n^2$ and the deformation parameter $β_n^2$ in central collisions across the energy range covered by the RHIC and LHC measurements. The applied JIMWLK evolution is found to leave visible signatures in the eccentricity evolution that can be observed if the same nuclei can be collided at RHIC and at the LHC, or in rapidity-dependent flow measurements. Our results demonstrate the importance of including the Bjorken-$x$ dependent nuclear geometry when comparing simulations of the Quark Gluon Plasma evolution with precise flow measurements at high collision energies.

## Key claim

The JIMWLK high-energy evolution drives initially deformed nuclei (Uranium and Ruthenium) toward a more spherical shape, but the evolution is slow, only a few percent effect for the dominant quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ when evolving from RHIC to LHC energies, with stronger evolution for smaller nuclei or higher-order deformations.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the JIMWLK evolution equation to obtain the Bjorken-x dependence of Wilson lines describing the nuclear color fields, starting from an IP-Glasma initial condition at x₀=0.01. They extract effective nuclear radii as a function of angle from the forward elastic dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude for a fixed-size dipole, fit these to a deformed Woods-Saxon distribution to obtain deformation parameters βₙ, and then compute eccentricities in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at zero impact parameter after classical Yang-Mills evolution to τ=0.2 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Uranium β₂ decreases by approximately 1.3% when evolving from RHIC to LHC energies (Y=0 to Y=4.8, corresponding to x from 0.01 to ~8×10⁻⁵)
- Uranium β₄ decreases by approximately 10% over the same energy range
- Uranium nuclear radius grows by approximately 5% from RHIC to LHC energies
- Ruthenium β₂ decreases by approximately 5% over the same energy range
- Ruthenium nuclear radius grows by approximately 5% from RHIC to LHC energies
- Linear relation εₙ² = aₙ + bₙβₙ² holds: for Uranium n=2, a=0.008±0.009, b=0.291±0.147; for Uranium n=4, a=0.006±0.002, b=1.124±0.316; for Ruthenium n=2, a=-0.01±0.008, b=1.376±0.343

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deformed nuclear geometry in high-energy collisions (Schenke 2014, Mantysaari 2017, Giacalone 2021, Zhang 2021) by quantifying how the JIMWLK evolution modifies the deformation parameters themselves, rather than assuming a fixed geometry. It confirms the linear eccentricity-deformation relation proposed by Zhang (2021) and demonstrates that the energy-dependent geometry leaves visible signatures in eccentricities, which are correlated with measurable flow harmonics. The results enable improved interpretation of flow measurements at RHIC and the LHC, and motivate rapidity-dependent flow studies as probes of small-x evolution.
