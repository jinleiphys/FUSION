# Nuclear $β^+$/EC decays in covariant density functional theory and the impact of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing

> Self-consistent proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation based on the spherical nonlinear point-coupling relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory is established and used to investigate the $β^+$/EC-decay half-lives of neutron-deficient Ar, Ca, Ti, Fe, Ni, Zn, Cd, and Sn isotopes. The isoscalar proton-neutron pairing is found to play an important role in reducing the decay half-lives, which is consistent with the same mechanism in the $β$ decays of neutron-rich nuclei. The experimental $β^+$/EC-decay half-lives can be well reproduced by a universal isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a universal isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron pairing strength can reproduce experimental β+/EC-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient isotopes across multiple isotopic chains, but this universality is demonstrated only for nuclei with N-Z < 5, and the strength parameter V0=175 MeV is calibrated specifically to the half-life of 100Cd.

## Method

The authors developed a self-consistent proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) based on spherical nonlinear point-coupling relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory using the PC-PK1 effective interaction. They calculated β+/EC-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient even-even nuclei by including both isovector (T=1) and isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron pairing interactions in the particle-particle channel, with the T=0 pairing strength V0 adjusted to reproduce the half-life of 100Cd. The Gamow-Teller transition strengths and decay energies were computed from the QRPA, and the half-lives were obtained using the allowed approximation with Fermi integrals including positron emission and electron capture contributions.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength V0 was determined to be 175 MeV, calibrated to the β+/EC-decay half-life of 100Cd.
- For 100Cd without T=0 pairing, the dominant GT transition had EQRPA = -1.879 MeV, corresponding to the spin-flip configuration π1g9/2 → ν1g7/2, with occupation probabilities of 0.808 for π1g9/2 and 0.123 for ν1g7/2.
- The pion-nucleon coupling parameters used were mπ = 138.0 MeV and fπ2/4π = 0.08, with the Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.52 adjusted to reproduce the GT resonance excitation energy in 208Pb.
- The decay constant D = 6163.4 s and the effective ratio (gA/gV)eff = 1 were used in the half-life formula.
- The T=0 pairing interaction used a two-range Gaussian form with ranges μ1 = 1.2 fm and μ2 = 0.7 fm, and weights g1 = 1, g2 = -2.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent QRPA approach based on covariant density functional theory (CDFT) from the neutron-rich side to the neutron-deficient side, specifically for β+/EC decays. It builds on prior proton-neutron QRPA calculations using meson-exchange relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory by Paar (2004) and Niksic (2005), and on the isospin-dependent T=0 pairing treatment developed by Niu (2012) for neutron-rich nuclei. The results enable systematic predictions of β+/EC half-lives for neutron-deficient nuclei relevant to nuclear astrophysics and motivate future inclusion of deformation degrees of freedom, as the present spherical calculations underestimate the half-life of the deformed nucleus 96Cd.
