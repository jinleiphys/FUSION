# Exploration of a modified density dependence in the Skyrme functional

> A variant of the basic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) functional is considered dealing with a new form of density dependence. It employs only integer powers and thus will allow a more sound basis for projection schemes (particle number, angular momentum). We optimize the new functional with exactly the same adjustment strategy as used in an earlier study with a standard Skyrme functional. This allows direct comparisons of the performance of the new functional relative to the standard one. We discuss various observables: bulk properties of finite nuclei, nuclear matter, giant resonances, super-heavy elements, and energy systematics. The new functional performs at least as well as the standard one, but offers a wider range of applicability (e.g. for projection) and more flexibility in the regime of high densities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new form of density dependence for the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock functional that uses only integer powers of density (via a rational approximant), which performs at least as well as the traditional non-integer-power form for standard nuclear observables but offers the formal advantage of being suitable for particle-number projection schemes.

## Method

The authors replaced the traditional density-dependent term proportional to rho^(2+alpha) in the Skyrme functional with a rational function of density using only integer powers, specifically (alpha_inv rho + beta_inv rho^2)/(1 + alpha_inv rho). They optimized the new functional using the same least-squares fitting strategy and data set as a previous study (Kluepfel 2009), fitting to binding energies, charge distribution properties, pairing gaps, and spin-orbit splittings in spherical semi-magic nuclei. They produced two variants: RD (rational density dependence alone) and RM (rational plus traditional with alpha=1 for more isovector freedom), each with a free fit (min) and a constrained fit (bas) that fixed nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

* The new functional has a simple pole at rho = -1/alpha_inv approximately -0.067 fm^-3, safely outside the range of physical densities.
* The effective nucleon mass m*/m is determined to be close to 0.9 for the new parameterizations, with the constrained fits fixing this value.
* The incompressibility K shows dramatically larger extrapolation uncertainty for the new density dependence compared to the standard form, due to having two parameters instead of one.
* For symmetric nuclear matter, all parameterizations predict practically the same energies up to about rho = 0.28 fm^-3; for neutron matter, agreement holds up to about rho = 0.15 fm^-3.
* The extrapolation uncertainty for the equation of state is a factor of five smaller for the standard SV-min parameterization than for the new RD-min parameterization.
* The GDR in O-16 remains problematic, with resonance energy staying far below experimental values even with the extended density dependence.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock development tradition reviewed by Bender (2003) and Stone (2007), and directly builds on the fitting strategy and data set of Kluepfel (2009). The new functional addresses a known limitation of the traditional rho^(2+alpha) form (with alpha approximately 0.16-0.3 from Bartel 1982) that prevents particle-number projection due to non-analytic behavior, as noted by Dobaczewski (2007) and Erler (2010). The results enable future work using projection schemes and suggest that the persistent underbinding of heavy deformed nuclei and the mismatch in super-heavy element binding energies (e.g., Hs-264) are not due to insufficient flexibility in density dependence.
