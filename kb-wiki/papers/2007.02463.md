# Relativistic nuclear collisions: Establishing a non-critical baseline for fluctuation measurements

> We study the influence of global baryon number conservation on the non-critical baseline of net baryon cumulants in heavy-ion collisions in a given acceptance, accounting for the asymmetry between the mean-numbers of baryons and antibaryons. We derive the probability distribution of net baryon number in a restricted phase space from the canonical partition function that incorporates exact conservation of baryon number in the full system. Furthermore, we provide tools to compute cumulants of any order from the generating function of uncorrelated baryons constrained by exact baryon number conservation. The results are applied to quantify the non-critical baseline for cumulants of net proton number fluctuations obtained in heavy-ion collisions by the STAR collaboration at different RHIC energies and by the ALICE collaboration at the LHC. Furthermore, volume fluctuations are added by a Monte Carlo procedure based on the centrality dependence of charged particle production as measured experimentally. Compared to the predictions based on the hadron resonance gas model or Skellam distribution a clear suppression of fluctuations is observed due to exact baryon-number conservation. The suppression increases with the order of the cumulant and towards lower collision energies. Predictions for net proton cumulants up to the eight order in heavy-ion collisions are given for experimentally accessible collision energies.

## Key claim
Global baryon number conservation in full phase space produces a significant suppression of net-proton cumulants compared to the hadron resonance gas or Skellam baselines, and this suppression increases with cumulant order and towards lower collision energies. The authors provide a non-critical baseline that must be subtracted before any critical signal (such as a chiral critical endpoint) can be identified in fluctuation measurements.

## Method
The authors derive the probability distribution of net baryon number in a restricted acceptance from the canonical partition function that enforces exact baryon number conservation in the full system. They compute cumulants of any order from the generating function of uncorrelated baryons constrained by global baryon conservation, allowing different acceptance probabilities for baryons and antibaryons. Volume fluctuations are added via a Monte Carlo procedure based on the experimentally measured centrality dependence of charged particle production. The acceptance probabilities for protons and antiprotons are determined from empirical rapidity distributions measured by NA49, BRAHMS, STAR, and ALICE, supplemented by limiting fragmentation extrapolations where data are incomplete.

## Key numbers
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.8\) GeV, the mean number of wounded nucleons for the most central 5% of events is \(\langle N_W \rangle = 352\) (NA49 data).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV, the BRAHMS net baryon data for the most central 10% are normalized to 314 wounded nucleons.
- The pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD is \(T_{pc} \simeq 156.5\) MeV (Bazavov 2018).
- The critical temperature for a second-order chiral transition in the massless limit is \(T_c \simeq 132\) MeV (Ejiri 2009, Sarkar 2019, Ding 2019).
- Predictions for net proton cumulants up to eighth order are given for experimentally accessible collision energies from SPS through LHC.

## Context
This work extends the canonical ensemble approach of Bzdak et al. (2012), who derived the probability distribution for net baryon fluctuations with global conservation but gave analytic results only for equal baryon and antibaryon acceptances. The authors provide general expressions for cumulants of any order with different acceptances for baryons and antibaryons, which is essential because at RHIC energies the acceptance probabilities for protons and antiprotons differ substantially. The results establish a robust non-critical baseline that must be compared to STAR data (Adamczyk 2013, Adam 2020) and ALICE data (Rustamov 2017, Arslandok 2020, Acharya 2019) before any claim of critical behavior can be evaluated; the framework also enables future searches for the chiral critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
