# How does the $S_{11}$ $N^*(890)$ state emerge from a naive $K$ matrix fit?

> We give a pedagogical analysis on $K$ matrix models describing the $πN$ scattering amplitude, in $S_{11}$ channel at low energies. We show how the correct use of analyticity in the $s$ channel and crossing symmetry in $t$ and $u$ channels leads to a much improved analytic behavior in the negative $s$ region, in agreement with the prediction from chiral perturbation amplitudes in its validity region. The analysis leads again to the conclusion that a genuine $N^*(890)$ resonance exists.

## Key claim

A subthreshold resonance \(N^*(890)\) emerges even from a simple \(K\)-matrix fit to \(\pi N\) scattering in the \(S_{11}\) channel, provided one respects analyticity in the \(s\) channel and crossing symmetry in the \(t\) and \(u\) channels; the naive on-shell approximation (using only \(i\rho\) instead of the full Chew-Mandelstam function) artificially suppresses the left-hand cut and hides the resonance.

## Method

The authors perform three successive \(K\)-matrix fits to \(\pi N\) scattering data in the \(S_{11}\) channel from threshold to \(\sqrt{s}=2.1\) GeV, including the \(\eta N\) channel. Fit I uses the standard on-shell \(K\)-matrix formula \(T^{-1}=K^{-1}-i\rho\). Fit II replaces \(i\rho\) with the Chew-Mandelstam function \(B_0(s;m_1^2,m_2^2)\), which restores the dispersive part of the two-body intermediate state and gives the correct left-hand cut. Fit III further adds tree-level \(t\)-channel \(\rho\) meson exchange and \(u\)-channel nucleon exchange to the \(K\)-matrix. The left-hand cut spectral function from each fit is compared with the chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) prediction at \(O(p^3)\).

## Key numbers

* Scattering length from Fit I: \(a \simeq 162.43 \times 10^{-3} \, m_\pi^{-1}\), compatible with the Roy-Steiner result \(a \simeq 169.9(19.4) \times 10^{-3} \, m_\pi^{-1}\) from Hoferichter (2015).
* Scattering length from Fit II: \(a \simeq 164.11 \times 10^{-3} \, m_\pi^{-1}\).
* Scattering length from Fit III: \(a \simeq 167.08 \times 10^{-3} \, m_\pi^{-1}\).
* Interaction range \(R\) from the left-hand cut integral: \(R = 0\) in Fit I, \(R = 0.18\) fm in Fit II, and \(R(\text{left cut}) \simeq 0.25\) fm plus \(R(\text{circular arc}) \simeq 0.10\) fm in Fit III.
* Subthreshold pole from the best model (using Fit III left cuts as input): \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 796(2) - i\,109(8)\) MeV.
* Pole from Fit II on sheet II: \(0.77 - i\,0.23\) GeV; from Fit III on sheet II: \(0.93 - i\,0.27\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Peking University (PKU) representation approach of Wang (2017, 2018), which used \(\chi\)PT input for the left-hand cut and found a subthreshold \(N^*(890)\) resonance. The present paper addresses queries about that result by showing that the same resonance appears in a conventional \(K\)-matrix framework once the correct analytic structure (Chew-Mandelstam function and crossed-channel exchanges) is included. The analysis contradicts the earlier suspicion of Doring and Nakayama (2009) that the subthreshold pole might be a forced pole mocking up the \(u\)- and \(t\)-channel cuts; instead, the resonance provides a positive phase shift that counterbalances the negative contributions from those cuts. The results enable future studies to treat the \(N^*(890)\) as a genuine state and to refine the description of low-energy \(\pi N\) dynamics without relying on cutoff parameters.
