# Thermal monopole condensation in QCD with physical quark masses

> Thermal monopoles, identified after Abelian projection as magnetic currents wrapping non-trivially around the thermal circle, are studied in $N_f = 2+1$ QCD at the physical point. The distribution in the number of wrappings, which in pure gauge theories points to a condensation temperature coinciding with deconfinement, points in this case to around 275 MeV, almost twice the QCD crossover temperature $T_c$; similar indications emerge looking for the formation of a percolating current cluster. The possible relation with other non-perturbative phenomena observed above $T_c$ is discussed.

## Key claim

Thermal monopole condensation in QCD with physical quark masses occurs at around 275 MeV, which is almost twice the QCD crossover temperature of about 155 MeV, rather than coinciding with deconfinement as it does in pure gauge theories.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_f = 2+1 flavors of stout rooted staggered fermions and the Symanzik tree-level improved gauge action, using configurations generated with a Rational Hybrid Monte-Carlo algorithm on GPUs. They identified thermal monopoles after Abelian projection in the generalized Maximal Abelian gauge, then studied the distribution of monopole currents wrapping around the temporal direction and the formation of percolating current clusters across temperatures up to approximately 1 GeV on lattices of sizes 32^3 x 8, 24^3 x 6, and 48^3 x 6.

## Key numbers

- The Bose-Einstein condensation temperature for thermal monopoles is T_BEC = 272(2) MeV (for alpha = 0) and T_BEC = 278(6) MeV (for alpha = 5/2), determined from fits of the chemical potential to a critical behavior.
- The QCD crossover temperature T_c is approximately 155 MeV, based on chiral symmetry restoration.
- The effective scale parameter from fitting the high-temperature monopole density to perturbative behavior is Lambda_eff = 47(5) MeV for full QCD, compared to 48(1) MeV for pure gauge SU(3).
- At high temperatures, the normalized thermal monopole density rho/T^3 in full QCD is about twice that in pure gauge SU(3).

## Context

This work extends the thermal monopole condensation approach previously applied to pure SU(2) and SU(3) gauge theories (D'Alessandro 2010, Bonati 2013), where condensation coincided with the deconfinement temperature, to QCD with physical quark masses. The finding that condensation occurs near 275 MeV, far above the chiral crossover, aligns with other evidence for non-perturbative behavior persisting well above T_c, including deviations from the dilute instanton gas approximation in topological charge distributions (b2_qcd) and color screening properties (Bazavov 2018, Bazavov 2020). The results enable further investigation into whether this temperature corresponds to a real phase transition and whether it relates to proposed intermediate phases such as the stringy fluid phase (Glozman 2019) or the scale-invariant regime suggested by Dirac spectrum studies (Alexandru 2019, Alexandru 2021).
