# Ward identity and electrical conductivity in hot QED

> We study the Ward identity for the effective photon-electron vertex summing the ladder diagrams contributing to the electrical conductivity in hot QED at leading logarithmic order. It is shown that the Ward identity requires the inclusion of a new diagram in the integral equation for the vertex that has not been considered before. The real part of this diagram is subleading and therefore the final expressions for the electrical conductivity at leading logarithmic order are not affected.

## Key claim

The Ward identity for the effective photon-electron vertex in hot QED at leading logarithmic order requires the inclusion of a new diagram involving soft fermions that had not been considered before; however, the real part of this new diagram is subleading, so the final expressions for the electrical conductivity at leading logarithmic order are unaffected.

## Method

The authors study the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the effective photon-electron vertex in the imaginary-time formalism, summing ladder diagrams that contribute to the electrical conductivity in hot QED. They compute the complete thermal width of a hard on-shell fermion to order \(e^4 T \ln(1/e)\), including contributions from both soft photon and soft fermion exchange, and verify that the modified integral equation for the vertex satisfies the Ward identity. The analysis uses spectral representations for propagators, HTL (hard thermal loop) approximations, and sum rules for the spectral functions.

## Key numbers

* Electrical conductivity expression: \(\sigma = -\frac{4e^2}{3} \int_{\mathbf{p}} n_F'(p) \frac{\mathcal{D}(p)}{\Gamma_{\mathbf{p}}}\), where \(\Gamma_{\mathbf{p}}\) is the thermal width and \(\mathcal{D}(p)\) is the real part of the effective vertex.
* Debye mass squared: \(m_D^2 = e^2 T^2 / 3\).
* Fermion thermal mass squared: \(m_f^2 = e^2 T^2 / 8\).
* Soft-photon contribution to thermal width at next-to-leading order: \(\Gamma^{\rm (sp,nlo)}_{\mathbf{p}} = \frac{\alpha\, m_D^2 \ln(1/e)}{2p} \left[-1+2n_F(p)+\frac{p}{6T}+ 2p\,n_F'(p) \right]\).
* Soft-fermion contribution to thermal width at leading order: \(\Gamma^{\rm (sf,lo)}_{\mathbf{p}} = \frac{\alpha\,m_f^2 \ln(1/e)}{p}\left[1+2n_B(p)\right]\).
* Leading-order soft-photon contribution to thermal width: \(\Gamma^{\rm (sp,lo)}_{\mathbf{p}} = 2\alpha T \ln(m_D/\Lambda_{\rm min})\).

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic approach of Valle Basagoiti (2002) for summing ladder diagrams in hot gauge theories, correcting the integral equation for the effective vertex to satisfy the Ward identity. The new diagram involving soft fermions had not been discussed before. The results confirm the equivalence with kinetic theory calculations by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) for the electrical conductivity at leading logarithmic order, and the corrected vertex equation provides a consistent framework for future calculations of transport coefficients in hot gauge theories.
