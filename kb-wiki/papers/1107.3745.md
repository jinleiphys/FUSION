# Femtoscopic scales in central A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies in hydrokinetic model

> A study of the energy behavior of the interferometry radii is carried out for the RHIC and LHC energies within the hydrokinetic model (HKM). The hydrokinetic predictions for the HBT radii at LHC energies are compared with the recent results of the ALICE Collaboration. The role of non-equilibrium and non-hydrodynamic stage of the matter evolution in formation of the femtoscopy scales at the LHC energies is analyzed. For this aim we develop the hybrid hydrokinetic model.

# Femtoscopic scales in central A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies in hydrokinetic model

## Key claim
The energy dependence of femtoscopy scales from RHIC to LHC can be understood within the same hydrokinetic basis used for RHIC, provided it is supplemented by a hadronic cascade model at the latest stage (hybrid HKM). The authors claim that the ALICE results confirm their earlier prediction that the $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio slowly drops with energy and saturates near unity, but note that the longitudinal HBT radius $R_{long}$ is underestimated by about 20% in the pure hydrokinetic model, requiring the hybrid treatment to reproduce the observed interferometry volume.

## Method
The authors use the hydrokinetic model (HKM), which combines hydrodynamic expansion of the system formed in A+A collisions with dynamical continuous decoupling described by escape probabilities. For the hybrid HKM (hHKM), they switch to the UrQMD hadronic cascade at a switching hypersurface defined by $T(\tau=\tau_{sw}, r_T=0)=165$ MeV, using non-equilibrium distribution functions from HKM as input. The initial conditions are boost-invariant Bjorken-like with thermalization time $\tau_0=1$ fm/c, Glauber-like transverse energy density profiles, and a pre-thermal transverse flow linear in radius parameterized by $\alpha$.

## Key numbers
- Maximum initial energy density for top RHIC energy: $\epsilon_0 = 15$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Maximum initial energy density for LHC $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV: $\epsilon_0 = 40$ GeV/fm$^3$, corresponding to charged particle multiplicity $dN_{ch}/d\eta \approx 1500$
- Pre-thermal transverse flow parameter $\alpha=0.28$ for HKM and $\alpha=0.18$ for hHKM at RHIC, with the same values kept for LHC
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: $T_{ch}=165$ MeV
- Switching temperature for hHKM: $T(\tau=\tau_{sw}, r_T=0)=165$ MeV
- Longitudinal HBT radius $R_{long}$ is underestimated in HKM by around 20% compared to ALICE data at LHC
- The $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio approximately follows $R_{out}/R_{side}\approx 1+const/\epsilon_0$

## Context
This work extends the hydrokinetic model previously developed by the same authors (Sinyukov et al., 2006, 2008) from RHIC to LHC energies, and compares predictions with the first ALICE femtoscopy results in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV. The hybrid HKM (hHKM) is introduced to address the finding that pure HKM underestimates the interferometry volume at LHC, which the authors attribute to non-equilibrium and non-hydrodynamic stages of matter evolution. The results contradict the expectation from isentropic and chemically frozen hydrodynamic evolution (Akkelin and Sinyukov, 2005) that interferometry volume increases only moderately with initial energy density; instead, the ALICE data show a significant increase. The authors argue that no new "LHC HBT puzzle" exists, as the behavior can be understood within the same framework used for RHIC when supplemented by the hadronic cascade stage.
