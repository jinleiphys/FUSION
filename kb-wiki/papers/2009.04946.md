# Probing the multi-scale dynamical interaction between heavy quarks and the QGP using JETSCAPE

> The dynamics of shower development for a jet traveling through the QGP involves a variety of scales, one of them being the heavy quark mass. Even though the mass of the heavy quarks plays a subdominant role during the high virtuality portion of the jet evolution, it does affect longitudinal drag and diffusion, stimulating additional radiation from heavy quarks. These emissions partially compensate the reduction in radiation from the dead cone effect. In the lower virtuality part of the shower, when the mass is comparable to the transverse momenta of the partons, scattering and radiation processes off heavy quarks differ from those off light quarks. All these factors result in a different nuclear modification factor for heavy versus light flavors and thus for heavy-flavor tagged jets. In this study, the heavy quark shower evolution and the fluid dynamical medium are modeled on an event by event basis using the JETSCAPE Framework. We present a multi-stage calculation that explores the differences between various heavy quark energy-loss mechanisms within a realistically expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Inside the QGP, the highly virtual and energetic portion of the shower is modeled using the MATTER generator, while the LBT generator models the showers induced by energetic and close-to-on-shell heavy quarks. Energy-momentum exchange with the medium, essential for the study of jet modification, proceeds using a weak coupling recoil approach. The JETSCAPE framework allows for transitions, on the level of individual partons, from one energy-loss prescription to the other depending on the parton's energy and virtuality and the local density. This allows us to explore the effect and interplay between the different regimes of energy loss on the propagation and radiation from hard heavy quarks in a dense medium.

## Key claim

The multi-stage JETSCAPE framework, using the same parameter set (\(\alpha_s=0.25\) and switching virtuality \(Q_0=2\) GeV) previously tuned to light flavor and jet observables, can simultaneously describe \(D^0\) meson nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\{2\}\) in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, with the caveat that the description of \(v_2\{2\}\) below 10 GeV is not shown due to the known absence of a recombination hadronization mechanism.

## Method

The heavy quark shower evolution and fluid dynamical medium were modeled event-by-event using the JETSCAPE Framework, with a (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic background calibrated via Bayesian model-to-data comparison using TRENTo initial conditions and free-streaming. Hard partons from PYTHIA were evolved through the QGP using a multi-stage approach: the MATTER generator (based on higher twist formalism) handled partons with virtuality \(Q > Q_0\), while the LBT generator (solving linearized Boltzmann equation with 2→2 and 2→3 processes) handled partons with \(Q < Q_0\), with energy-momentum exchange via a weak coupling recoil approach and transitions between modules occurring at the individual parton level.

## Key numbers

- Switching virtuality \(Q_0 = 2\) GeV (and alternatively \(\sqrt{2}\) GeV) used to separate MATTER and LBT regimes for heavy flavor evolution, matching the value previously tuned to light flavor observables.
- Effective strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.25\) used to determine \(\hat{q}\) (transverse momentum broadening), tuned using light flavor observables.
- Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV in the 0-10% centrality class.
- \(D^0\) meson \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\{2\}\) described well using the same parameter set (\(\alpha_s=0.25\), \(Q_0=2\) GeV) that describes light flavor and jet observables; a smaller \(Q_0 = \sqrt{2}\) GeV also fits the data well.

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework's multi-stage energy-loss approach, previously applied to light flavor hadronic observables (Amit et al., 2019), to heavy flavor observables for the first time. The hydrodynamic background calibration follows an established Bayesian model-to-data comparison (Bernhard et al., 2019). The study enables future improvements including a better description of heavy quark hadronization at low \(p_T\) (noting that Cao et al., 2015 showed the lack of recombination suppresses heavy quark flow) and extension to bottom flavor observables.
