# The land of deformation south of $^{68}$Ni

> We study the development of collectivity in the neutron-rich nuclei around $N=40$, where experimental and theoretical evidences suggest a rapid shape change from the spherical to the rotational regime, in analogy to what happens at the {\it island of inversion} surrounding $^{31}$Na. Theoretical calculations are performed within the interacting shell model framework in a large valence space, based on a $^{48}$Ca core which encompasses the full $pf$ shell for the protons and the $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, $0g_{9/2}$ and $1d_{5/2}$ orbits for the neutrons. The effective interaction is based on a G-matrix obtained from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential whose monopole part is corrected empirically to produce effective single particle energies compatible with the experimental data. We find a good agreement between the theoretical results and the available experimental data. We predict the onset of deformation at different neutron numbers for the various isotopic chains. The maximum collectivity occurs in the chromium isotopes, where the large deformation regime starts already at $N=38$. The shell evolution responsible for the observed shape changes is discussed in detail, in parallel to the situation in the $N=20$ region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the rapid onset of collectivity in neutron-rich nuclei around N=40 is driven by the same shell-evolution mechanism that creates the "island of inversion" at N=20, but the authors hedge that the wave functions in the N=40 region are more complex (with dominant 4p-4h rather than 2p-2h components) and that the maximum deformation occurs in chromium isotopes, not in iron.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale interacting shell model calculations in a valence space based on a ⁴⁸Ca core, including the full pf shell for protons and the 0f₅/₂, 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0g₉/₂, and 1d₅/₂ orbits for neutrons. They constructed the LNPS effective interaction from realistic G-matrix two-body matrix elements with empirical monopole corrections, and diagonalized matrices up to dimension 10¹⁰ using the m-scheme code ANTOINE, including up to 14 particle-14 hole excitations across the Z=28 and N=40 gaps.

## Key numbers

- The 2⁺ state in ⁶⁴Cr is predicted to have the lowest excitation energy and largest B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) among N=40 isotones, with the measured 2⁺ energy agreeing within 100 keV of theoretical predictions.
- In ⁶⁸Ni, the ground state has 55.5% 0p0h, 35.5% 2p2h, and 8.5% 4p4h neutron configurations, with a correlation energy of -9.03 MeV.
- In ⁶⁴Cr, the ground state has 0% 0p0h, 9% 2p2h, 73% 4p4h, and 18% 6p6h neutron configurations, with a correlation energy of -24.83 MeV.
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment in ⁶²,⁶⁴,⁶⁶Cr is Q_int ≈ 150 e fm², corresponding to β ≈ 0.35.
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment in ⁶⁶,⁶⁸Fe is Q_int = 145 e fm², corresponding to β ≈ 0.3.
- The N=50 neutron gap in ⁷⁸Ni is estimated to be approximately 5 MeV.
- The 0g₉/₂-1d₅/₂ gap in Zr isotopes increases by 3 MeV when the neutron 0g₉/₂ orbital is filled.
- In ⁶⁸Ni, the first excited 0₂⁺ state is calculated at 1.2 MeV and the 0₃⁺ state at 2.4 MeV, with total quadrupole sum rules of 904, 1162, and 2025 e²fm⁴ for the ground state, 0₂⁺, and 0₃⁺ states respectively.

## Context

This work extends the shell model studies of the N=40 region by Caurier (2002), Sorlin (2003), and Kaneko (2008), but includes the neutron 1d₅/₂ orbital which was missing in those earlier calculations. The authors explicitly compare their results to beyond-mean-field HFB+GCM calculations with the Gogny force by Gaudefroy (2009), which showed only moderate collectivity without visible structure changes between iron and chromium chains. The paper enables future experimental tests of the predicted deformation onset at N=38 in chromium isotopes and at N=40 in iron isotopes, particularly through transition probability measurements.
