# $NΩ$ dibaryon from lattice QCD near the physical point

> The nucleon($N$)-Omega($Ω$) system in the S-wave and spin-2 channel ($^5$S$_2$) is studied from the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with nearly physical quark masses ($m_π\simeq 146$~MeV and $m_K \simeq 525$~MeV). The time-dependent HAL QCD method is employed to convert the lattice QCD data of the two-baryon correlation function to the baryon-baryon potential and eventually to the scattering observables. The $NΩ$($^5$S$_2$) potential, obtained under the assumption that its couplings to the D-wave octet-baryon pairs are small, is found to be attractive in all distances and to produce a quasi-bound state near unitarity: In this channel, the scattering length, the effective range and the binding energy from QCD alone read $a_0= 5.30(0.44)(^{+0.16}_{-0.01})$~fm, $r_{\rm eff} = 1.26(0.01)(^{+0.02}_{-0.01})$~fm, $B = 1.54(0.30)(^{+0.04}_{-0.10})$~MeV, respectively. Including the extra Coulomb attraction, the binding energy of $pΩ^-$($^5$S$_2$) becomes $B_{pΩ^-} = 2.46(0.34)(^{+0.04}_{-0.11})$~MeV. Such a spin-2 $pΩ^-$ state could be searched through two-particle correlations in $p$-$p$, $p$-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The NΩ(⁵S₂) system is found to have a quasi-bound state near unitarity, with a binding energy from QCD alone of 1.54 MeV, but this result depends on the assumption that couplings to D-wave octet-baryon pairs (ΛΞ and ΣΞ) below the NΩ threshold are small, and that couplings to inelastic octet-decuplet channels above threshold are also negligible in the range of t adopted.

## Method

The time-dependent HAL QCD method was used to extract the NΩ(⁵S₂) central potential from the two-baryon correlation function computed on (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD configurations with nearly physical quark masses. The potential was obtained under a leading-order derivative expansion, assuming single-channel dominance, and was fitted to a Gaussian plus (Yukawa)² functional form. The Schrödinger equation was then solved with this fitted potential to obtain scattering observables and binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length: a₀ = 5.30(0.44)(⁺⁰·¹⁶₋₀.₀₁) fm for NΩ(⁵S₂) from QCD alone
- Effective range: r_eff = 1.26(0.01)(⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀.₀₁) fm for NΩ(⁵S₂) from QCD alone
- Binding energy from QCD alone: B = 1.54(0.30)(⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀.₁₀) MeV for NΩ(⁵S₂)
- Binding energy including Coulomb attraction: B_pΩ⁻ = 2.46(0.34)(⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀.₁₁) MeV for pΩ⁻(⁵S₂)
- Root mean square distance from QCD alone: √⟨r²⟩ = 3.77(0.31)(⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀.₀₁) fm for NΩ(⁵S₂)
- Root mean square distance including Coulomb: √⟨r²⟩_pΩ⁻ = 3.24(0.19)(⁺⁰·⁰⁶₋₀.₀₀) fm for pΩ⁻(⁵S₂)
- Pion mass used: m_π ≃ 146 MeV; kaon mass: m_K ≃ 525 MeV
- Lattice cutoff: a⁻¹ ≃ 2.333 GeV (a ≃ 0.0846 fm); lattice volume 96⁴ (La ≃ 8.1 fm)
- Hadron masses from lattice: m_N = 954.7(2.7) MeV, m_Ω = 1711.5(1.0) MeV

## Context

This work extends the pilot lattice QCD study of NΩ(⁵S₂) by Etminan et al. (2014) which used a heavy pion mass (m_π ≃ 875 MeV) and found a short-range attraction, to nearly physical quark masses where the binding energy is much smaller (1.54 MeV vs 18.9 MeV). The results are consistent with the picture of a shallow quasi-bound state near unitarity, similar to the deuteron (NN(³S₁)) and the di-Omega (ΩΩ(¹S₀)) studied by Gongyo et al. (2017). The findings enable experimental searches for the spin-2 pΩ⁻ state through two-particle correlations in p-p, p-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, as suggested theoretically by Morita et al. (2016) and reported experimentally by the STAR Collaboration (2018).
