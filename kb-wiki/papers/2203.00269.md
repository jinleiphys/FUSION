# Chiral symmetry restoration and the $Δ$ matter formation in neutron stars

> We analyze the effects of chiral symmetry restoration in hadronic matter, including the lowest-lying baryonic resonance $Δ$ based on the parity doublet model. We study the role of $Δ$ and its chiral partner on the equation of state (EoS) of dense matter under neutron star (NS) conditions of $β$-equilibrium and charge neutrality. We find that the softening of the EoS driven by the early onset of $Δ$ matter due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry allows accommodating the modern multi-messenger astrophysical constraints on the mass, radius, and tidal deformability. The softening above the saturation density is accompanied by subsequent stiffening at high densities. We also find that the matter composition in the NS cores may be different upon variations of the repulsive interactions of $Δ$ baryons in hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the Δ(1232) resonance and its chiral partner within the parity doublet model, with chiral symmetry restoration, can simultaneously satisfy modern multi-messenger constraints on neutron star mass, radius, and tidal deformability, provided that the chirally invariant mass of the Δ (m₀^Δ) is chosen appropriately relative to that of the nucleon (m₀^N). The softening of the equation of state from early Δ onset is followed by stiffening at high densities, which is essential for reaching the 2 M⊙ mass threshold.

## Method

The authors employ the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation, including nucleons (N(938) and N(1535)) and Δ baryons (Δ(1232) and Δ(1700)) as chiral partners, coupled to σ, ω, and ρ meson fields. They solve the thermodynamic potential under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality at zero temperature, fixing couplings to nuclear ground-state properties (saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy -16 MeV, compressibility K = 240 MeV, symmetry energy 31 MeV). The chirally invariant mass of the nucleon m₀^N is varied from 550 to 700 MeV, and the Δ chirally invariant mass m₀^Δ is varied from m₀^N to infinity (purely nucleonic limit), with the vector couplings of Δ set equal to those of nucleons (R_Δ = 1). The resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A - m_N = -16 MeV, compressibility K = 240 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 31 MeV
- Vacuum masses: m_N = 939 MeV, m_N* = 1500 MeV, m_Δ = 1232 MeV, m_Δ* = 1700 MeV, m_π = 140 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 775 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L_sym ≈ 82 MeV at saturation for m₀^N = 550-700 MeV
- For m₀^N = 600 MeV and m₀^Δ = 600 MeV, the onset of positive-parity Δ occurs at μ_B ≈ 950 MeV through a first-order phase transition with a density jump of about 0.5 n₀
- For m₀^N = 600 MeV and m₀^Δ = 650 MeV, the onset of positive-parity Δ occurs around μ_B ≈ 1100 MeV
- Radii of 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars reduce by up to approximately 1 km for m₀^N = 700 MeV and up to almost 2 km for m₀^N = 550 MeV when Δ matter is included compared to purely nucleonic equations of state
- Maximum mass constraint: M_max = 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙ for PSR J0740+6620

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Δ(1232) resonance in dense matter within relativistic mean-field models (Drago 2014) and microscopic approaches (Li 2019) by incorporating chiral symmetry restoration via the parity doublet model, which was previously applied to nucleons alone (Motohiro 2015, Marczenko 2017-2021). The results contrast with earlier findings (Li 2018) where earlier Δ onset yielded larger maximum masses; here, the additional softening from negative-parity N* and Δ* at high densities reduces the maximum mass mildly. The framework enables systematic exploration of how chiral symmetry restoration affects the composition and stiffness of neutron star cores, and can be extended to study the role of repulsive Δ interactions (parametrized by R_Δ) and their impact on the stability of neutron star sequences.
