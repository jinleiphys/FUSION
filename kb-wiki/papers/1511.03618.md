# Bayesian parameter estimation for effective field theories

> We present procedures based on Bayesian statistics for estimating, from data, the parameters of effective field theories (EFTs). The extraction of low-energy constants (LECs) is guided by theoretical expectations in a quantifiable way through the specification of Bayesian priors. A prior for natural-sized LECs reduces the possibility of overfitting, and leads to a consistent accounting of different sources of uncertainty. A set of diagnostic tools are developed that analyze the fit and ensure that the priors do not bias the EFT parameter estimation. The procedures are illustrated using representative model problems, including the extraction of LECs for the nucleon mass expansion in SU(2) chiral perturbation theory from synthetic lattice data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Bayesian framework for estimating low-energy constants (LECs) in effective field theories, where the use of naturalness priors reduces overfitting and enables consistent accounting of truncation uncertainties, but the authors caution that priors can bias parameter extraction if not validated through diagnostic tools.

## Method

The authors formulate LEC estimation using Bayes' theorem, where the posterior probability distribution for LECs is proportional to the product of a Gaussian likelihood (from experimental data) and a prior that encodes the expectation of natural-sized coefficients. They implement marginalization over higher-order EFT terms to account for truncation uncertainty, and develop diagnostic tools including projected posterior plots and comparisons of likelihood versus prior dominance to validate that priors do not bias the fit. The procedures are demonstrated on synthetic data from model problems with controlled naturalness properties, and on the extraction of LECs for the nucleon mass expansion in SU(2) chiral perturbation theory from synthetic lattice data.

## Key numbers

* Model D synthetic data: generated with relative error c = 0.05 (5%) using Gaussian noise, with data points up to x_max = 1
* For the nucleon mass expansion in SU(2) chiral perturbation theory: LECs are extracted up to sixth chiral order from synthetic lattice data
* The prior width parameter abar_0 = 5 is used for the Set C' prior in the model problem demonstrations
* Posterior diagnostics show that for parameter a_1 the posterior is dominated by the likelihood, while for parameter a_3 the posterior is mostly given by the prior (indicating the data do not constrain that coefficient)

## Context

This work extends the model problem approach of Schindler (2008) and Furnstahl (2014) for studying systematic behavior of EFT expansions, and builds upon the Bayesian truncation error framework of Furnstahl (2015). The procedures are designed to replace ad-hoc modifications to least-squares fitting used in contemporary chiral EFT fits, such as those by Carlsson (2015) who add uncertainties in quadrature, and Epelbaum (2014) who limit fit regions and add penalty terms. The framework enables consistent propagation of all three sources of uncertainty (data, truncation, and computational) into LEC estimates and subsequent EFT predictions.
