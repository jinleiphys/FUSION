# Modified combinant analysis of the $e^+e^-$ multiplicity distributions

> As shown recently, one can obtain additional information from the measured multiplicity distributions, $P(N)$, by extracting the so-called modified combinants, $C_j$. This information is encoded in their specific oscillatory behavior, which can be described only by some combinations of compound distributions, the basic part of which is the Binomial Distribution. So far this idea was applied to $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ processes; in this note we show that an even stronger effect is observed in the $C_j$ deduced from $e^+e^-$ collisions. We present its possible explanation in terms of the so called Generalised Multiplicity Distribution (GMD) proposed some time ago.

## Key claim

The modified combinants $C_j$ derived from $e^+e^-$ multiplicity distributions show stronger oscillatory behavior than those from $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ processes, and this effect can be explained by a compound distribution involving the Generalized Multiplicity Distribution (GMD) combined with a Binomial Distribution, but only if the GMD is modified to allow for particle numbers below the parameter $k'$.

## Method

The authors extracted modified combinants $C_j$ from experimental charged-particle multiplicity distributions $P(N)$ measured by the ALEPH and DELPHI collaborations at 91 GeV using a recurrence relation that connects all multiplicities. They fitted the $P(N)$ data and the derived $C_j$ with a compound distribution whose generating function is the product of a Binomial Distribution (representing detection in a limited rapidity window) and a Negative Binomial Distribution, which is equivalent to a modified version of the Generalized Multiplicity Distribution (GMD). The GMD itself originates from a stochastic branching equation describing parton evolution in QCD, with the QCD evolution parameter $t$ defined in terms of the parton invariant mass and hadronization scale.

## Key numbers

- ALEPH data at 91 GeV: GMD fit to $P(N)$ gave parameters $\langle N\rangle = 12.991$, $k = 3.5$, $k' = 0.7348$; the corresponding $C_j$ show oscillations with period 2 and power-law increasing amplitude.
- Compound distribution fit to ALEPH data at 91 GeV: $\alpha = 0.8725$ (detection probability), $k' = 1$, $k = 3.2$, $\kappa = 4.585$; the $C_j$ from this fit also oscillate with period 2.
- DELPHI data at 91 GeV: GMD fit gave $\langle N\rangle = 13.1043$, $k = 4.5$, $k' = 0.29$; the derived $C_j$ were too scattered to discern oscillations due to low statistics (ALEPH had $3 \times 10^5$ events, 5 times more than DELPHI).
- For different rapidity windows ($|y| < 2.0, 1.5, 1.0, 0.5$), the mean rescaled amplitudes $\langle C_j/\xi^j \rangle$ for even and odd $j$ were distinctly different from zero, with $\xi = 6.85, 2.35, 1.2, 0.91$ respectively.

## Context

This work extends the modified combinant analysis previously applied to $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions (Ang et al., 2018; Rybczyński et al., 2018) to $e^+e^-$ annihilation data from ALEPH and DELPHI. The GMD was originally proposed by Chew et al. as an alternative to the Negative Binomial Distribution for describing multiplicity distributions in $p\bar{p}$ and $e^+e^-$ reactions. The authors show that the oscillatory behavior of $C_j$ in $e^+e^-$ (period 2, increasing amplitude) differs markedly from $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions (period ~10, decaying amplitudes), challenging claims of universality in hadron production mechanisms across collision types (Biswas, 2000; Kittel, 2000; Bzdak, 2010). The analysis enables future discrimination between competing models of multiparticle production by using $C_j$ as an additional phenomenological tool beyond the traditional $P(N)$ fits.
