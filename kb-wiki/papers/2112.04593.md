# Deciphering the role of multiple scatterings and time delays in the in-medium emission process

> In this work we use the all-order resummed solution of the BDMPS-Z spectrum to shed light on the dynamics that controls the in-medium radiation process for each kinematical regime. We find that multiple scatterings are essential to correctly describe the radiation process both in the low and mid-energy regime, while in the high-energy region one single hard scattering is enough. Furthermore, we compute the all-order spectrum when the medium is produced with a time delay with respect to the hard process in which the parent parton was created. The propagation of the hard parton though vacuum before the medium formation induces extra medium-induced radiation which might have an impact on phenomenological analyses.

## Key claim

Multiple scatterings are essential to correctly describe the in-medium radiation process in the low and mid-energy regimes, while in the high-energy region one single hard scattering is sufficient; additionally, a time delay between the hard process and medium formation induces extra medium-induced radiation beyond what is captured by simply shortening the medium length.

## Method

The authors numerically compute the all-order resummed solution of the BDMPS-Z single gluon emission spectrum using a Yukawa interaction model for the dipole cross section, without additional approximations. They compare this full solution to the first opacity (GLV N=1) approximation and the improved opacity expansion (IOE) at HO+NLO precision across different energy regimes. They then re-derive the all-order spectrum for a scenario where the hard parton propagates through vacuum from time 0 to t0 before entering a thermalized medium from t0 to L, and compare this to the standard cases where the medium starts at time 0 or at t0.

## Key numbers

* For a Yukawa-type interaction in the low-energy regime, the fully resummed spectrum is well approximated by the first opacity result times a suppression factor accounting for the probability of not experiencing further scatterings, as shown for several values of n0L (linear density of scattering centers times medium length).
* At intermediate gluon energies (below ω̄c = μ²L/2, where μ is the screening mass), the first opacity approximation overpredicts the fully resummed spectrum, while the IOE (HO+NLO) is a good approximation; this is shown for n0L values of 2, 5, and 10.
* For high gluon energies (ω > ω̄c), both the fully resummed and IOE calculations converge to the GLV N=1 spectrum.
* For a time delay scenario with t0/L = 0.2 and n0L = 5, the spectrum for case c (vacuum propagation from 0 to t0, then medium from t0 to L) is larger than case b (medium from t0 to L only), and both are smaller than case a (medium from 0 to L).

## Context

This work extends the all-order resummation framework of Andres (2020) to determine the validity range of common analytical approximations: the harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation, the first opacity expansion (GLV N=1), and the improved opacity expansion (IOE) of Mehtar-Tani (2019). The time-delay analysis addresses the common phenomenological assumption of neglecting jet quenching before the hydrodynamization time, and the authors note that sensitivity of jet quenching observables to initial stages was recently shown by Andres (2019). The results enable more precise phenomenological studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions by properly accounting for multiple scatterings and initial-stage propagation.
