# Physics perspectives with heavy ions in the HL-LHC phase and beyond

> The current state of research on high-energy heavy ion physics, including its motivations and purpose is reviewed from a theorist's perspective. Possible future directions are discussed, in particular the possibility of investigating the regime of small transverse momenta in more detail and an improved interplay between experiments and dedicated theory development.

# Physics perspectives with heavy ions in the HL-LHC phase and beyond

## Key claim
Heavy ion collisions at the HL-LHC and beyond will not produce paradigmatic changes in the physics picture compared to current LHC results, but the regime of low transverse momentum (down to approximately 10 MeV) offers a qualitatively new window into macroscopic fluid properties, thermalization dynamics, and chiral symmetry restoration that has not been systematically explored.

## Method
The paper reviews the current state of high-energy heavy ion physics from a theorist's perspective, focusing on relativistic fluid dynamics as the framework for describing bulk dynamics of heavy ion collisions. It discusses the Israel-Stewart formalism for making dissipative stresses dynamical fields to maintain causality, the role of fluctuating initial conditions from Glauber-type models, and the analogy between heavy ion collisions and cosmology for studying fluctuation evolution.

## Key numbers
- Transverse momentum threshold for future dedicated detectors: approximately 10 MeV, corresponding to a de Broglie wavelength of approximately 1/20 fm
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: approximately 120 MeV
- The chemical freeze-out temperature at small baryon chemical potential agrees with the chiral crossover temperature from lattice QCD simulations (Andronic 2017)
- Lattice QCD calculations now provide the equation of state p(T) at vanishing baryon chemical potential (Borsanyi 2016, Bazavov 2014)
- Cumulants of net baryon number such as χ₂ᴮ = ⟨(N_B − N̄_B)²⟩/(V T³) are now understood from lattice calculations (Bellwied 2015, Bazavov 2017)

## Context
This review extends the ongoing discussion documented in the CERN Yellow Report on heavy ions at the Future Circular Collider (Dainese 2016) and the forthcoming Yellow Report on heavy ions at the HL-LHC. It builds on experimental discoveries at LHC and RHIC including the observation of odd harmonic flow coefficients from initial state fluctuations (Alver 2010), the suppression of all Υ states with stronger suppression for excited states (Khachatryan 2016), and the observation of light-by-light scattering in ultra-peripheral collisions by ATLAS (Aaboud 2017). The paper enables future work on developing a "standard model" for heavy ion collisions that integrates theory and experiment, and on using heavy ion physics insights to understand the relation between microscopic and macroscopic properties of dark matter in cosmology.
