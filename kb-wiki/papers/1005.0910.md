# QCD phase diagram at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials

> The phase structure of two-flavor QCD is explored for thermal systems with finite baryon- and isospin-chemical potentials, mu_B and mu_{iso}, by using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The PNJL model with the scalar-type eight-quark interaction can reproduce lattice QCD data at not only mu_{iso}=mu_B=0 but also mu_{iso}>0 and mu_B=0. In the mu_{iso}-mu_{B}-T space, where T is temperature, the critical endpoint of the chiral phase transition in the mu_B-T plane at mu_{iso}=0 moves to the tricritical point of the pion-superfluidity phase transition in the mu_{iso}-T plane at mu_B=0 as mu_{iso} increases. The thermodynamics at small T is controlled by sqrt{sigma^2+pi^2} defined by the chiral and pion condensates, sigma and pi.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with the scalar-type eight-quark interaction can reproduce lattice QCD data for two-flavor QCD at finite isospin chemical potential, and predicts that the critical endpoint of the chiral phase transition in the baryon chemical potential-temperature plane at zero isospin chemical potential moves to the tricritical point of the pion-superfluidity phase transition in the isospin chemical potential-temperature plane at zero baryon chemical potential as isospin chemical potential increases, but this mapping depends on the inclusion of the eight-quark interaction which significantly shifts the location of the tricritical point.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor PNJL model in Euclidean space-time, incorporating the Polyakov-loop potential fitted to pure gauge lattice QCD data and the scalar-type eight-quark interaction in the NJL sector. They solved the stationary conditions for the mean fields (chiral condensate sigma, pion condensate pi, Polyakov-loop Phi and its conjugate) to obtain the thermodynamic potential, and determined phase boundaries by analyzing discontinuities in order parameters and divergences in the dimensionless susceptibility matrix. The model parameters in the NJL sector (set A) were fixed by reproducing the pion mass (138 MeV), pion decay constant (93.3 MeV), and the pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD at zero chemical potentials, with no additional free parameters for the finite isospin chemical potential calculations.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at vacuum: M_pi = 138 MeV, used to define the critical isospin chemical potential mu_c = M_pi/2 = 69 MeV for the second-order pion-superfluidity phase transition at zero temperature
- Pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potentials: T_c = 173 +/- 8 MeV from lattice QCD, used to normalize temperature in comparisons
- Tricritical point (TCP) location with eight-quark interaction: (mu_I, T) = (0.32 GeV, 0.169 GeV) in the mu_I-T plane at mu_q = 0
- Tricritical point (TCP) location without eight-quark interaction: (mu_I, T) = (0.401 GeV, 0.171 GeV) in the mu_I-T plane at mu_q = 0
- Polyakov-potential parameters: a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_3 = -1.75, with T_0 reset to 212 MeV (from the pure gauge value of 270 MeV) to reproduce the full lattice QCD pseudocritical temperature
- NJL sector parameters for set A: G_s = 4.673 GeV^{-2}, G_8 = 452.12 GeV^{-8}, m_0 = 5.5 MeV, Lambda = 631.5 MeV; for set B (no eight-quark interaction): G_s = 5.498 GeV^{-2}, G_8 = 0, m_0 = 5.5 MeV, Lambda = 631.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous PNJL model studies by Sasaki et al. (2009) and Sakai et al. (2009) that demonstrated the model's ability to reproduce lattice QCD data at imaginary quark chemical potential and at finite isospin chemical potential, respectively. The inclusion of the scalar-type eight-quark interaction, which was shown necessary to reproduce lattice QCD data at finite temperature and zero chemical potentials, is here validated against lattice QCD data at finite isospin chemical potential from Kogut et al. (2002). The results enable predictions for the three-dimensional phase diagram in the space of baryon chemical potential, isospin chemical potential, and temperature, which is relevant for understanding heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as GSI, SPS, RHIC, and LHC where isospin chemical potential is generally non-zero. The finding that the thermodynamics at small temperature is controlled by the approximate order parameter R = sqrt(sigma^2 + pi^2) over both the normal and pion-superfluidity phases provides a unifying framework for understanding the phase structure.
