# Revisit of Coulomb effects on pi^{-}/pi^{+} ratio in heavy-ion collisions

> It is shown that the large $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio at low pion energies recently observed in the reaction of Au+Au at $E_{beam}/A=1.0$ GeV at SIS/GSI is due to the strong Coulomb field of the reaction system.

# Revisit of Coulomb effects on π⁻/π⁺ ratio in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The large π⁻/π⁺ ratio at low pion kinetic energies observed in Au+Au collisions at E_beam/A = 1.0 GeV at SIS/GSI is a manifestation of the strong Coulomb field of the reaction system, not an indication of new physics; the ratio varies with energy due to competition between the Coulomb momentum impulse and phase space distortion.

## Method

A hadronic transport model with explicit isospin degrees of freedom for all hadrons, isospin-dependent hadron-hadron collision cross sections, branching ratios for baryon resonance decays, and a time-dependent self-consistent mean field for baryons including the Coulomb field and an isosymmetry term was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at E_beam/A = 1.0 GeV. The calculated π⁻/π⁺ ratio was compared with experimental data from the KaoS collaboration at SIS/GSI, and the ratio was also computed without Coulomb interactions to isolate the Coulomb effect.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at E_beam/A = 1.0 GeV, the π⁻/π⁺ ratio increases from about 1 at E_kin ≈ 400 MeV to about 2 at E_kin ≈ 100 MeV (experimental observation by KaoS and FOPI collaborations).
- For Ni+Ni at E_beam/A = 1.8 GeV, the π⁻/π⁺ ratio reaches only about 1.15 (experimental observation by Oeschler).
- Without Coulomb interactions, the calculated π⁻/π⁺ ratio for Au+Au is a constant of about 1.9 for E_kin ≤ 500 MeV, close to the isospin estimate of 1.95 from the formula R_I = (5N² + NZ)/(5Z² + NZ).
- With Coulomb interactions, the calculated ratio is larger than the isospin average for E_kin ≤ 150 MeV and decreases as pion energy increases, crossing the no-Coulomb result at E_kin ≈ 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Bertsch (1980), Libbrecht and Koonin (1979), and Gyulassy and Kauffmann (1981) who explained π⁻/π⁺ structures using idealized static charge distributions, by incorporating a dynamically evolving charge distribution in a transport model. The study also addresses the similar large π⁻/π⁺ ratios observed at AGS energies by the E866 collaboration for Au+Au and Si+Au reactions, and enables future separation of Coulomb effects from other isospin phenomena in pion spectra at SIS/GSI and AGS.
