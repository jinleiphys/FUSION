# Color neutral 2SC phase of cold and dense quark matter in the presence of constant magnetic fields

> The color neutral two-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase of cold and dense quark matter is studied in the presence of constant magnetic fields and at moderate baryon densities. In the first part of the paper, a two-flavor effective Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model consisting of a chiral symmetry breaking mass gap sigma_{B}, a color superconducting mass gap Delta_{B} and a color neutrality coefficient mu_{8} is introduced in the presence of a rotated U(1) magnetic field B. To study the phenomenon of magnetic catalysis in the presence of strong magnetic fields, the gap equations corresponding to sigma_{B} and Delta_{B}, as well as mu_{8} are solved in the lowest Landau level approximation. In the second part of the paper, a detailed numerical analysis is performed to explore the effect of any arbitrary magnetic field on the above mass gaps and the color neutrality coefficient. The structure of the chiral symmetry breaking and color superconducting phases is also presented in the mu_{c}-B plane, and the effect of the color neutrality coefficient mu_{8} on the phase structure of the model is explored. As it turns out, whereas the transition from the chiral symmetry breaking to color superconducting phase is of first order, nonvanishing mu_{8} affects essentially the second order phase transition from color superconducting to the normal phase.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the color neutral two-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase of cold and dense quark matter under a constant magnetic field, the transition from the chiral symmetry breaking (χSB) to the color superconducting (CSC) phase remains first order, while a nonvanishing color chemical potential μ₈ changes the second order phase transition from CSC to the normal phase into a first order transition at a specific point in the μc–B plane.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with chiral symmetry breaking mass gap σ_B, color superconducting mass gap Δ_B, and a color chemical potential μ₈, in the presence of a rotated U(1) magnetic field. They derive the one-loop effective action and thermodynamic potential at zero temperature and finite quark chemical potential, then solve the coupled gap equations analytically in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation for strong magnetic fields and numerically for arbitrary magnetic field strengths.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential for the 2SC phase at vanishing magnetic field is μ_c = 342 MeV, with a diquark mass gap Δ₀ ≃ 100 MeV and color chemical potential μ₈ ≃ 10 MeV.
- For strong magnetic fields, the LLL approximation is reliable for rotated magnetic fields eB ≳ 0.45–0.5 GeV².
- The first order phase transition between χSB and CSC phases occurs in the regime μ_c ≃ 350–450 MeV and eB ∈ [0, 0.7] GeV².
- The second order phase transition from CSC to normal phase occurs at μ_c ≃ 750–800 MeV; with μ₈ ≠ 0, this becomes first order at μ_c ≃ 755 MeV and eB ≃ 0.13 GeV².
- For heavy ion collisions, the magnetic field at RHIC is at most eB ≃ 1.3 m_π² ∼ 4.3 × 10¹⁸ Gauss, and at LHC up to 15 m_π² ∼ 5 × 10¹⁹ Gauss (with m_π = 140 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of color neutral 2SC quark matter by Ebert et al. (2005), who found the diquark gap and color chemical potential at zero magnetic field, and by Mandal et al. (2009), who studied chiral and diquark condensates at a fixed magnetic field eB = 0.05 GeV². The paper provides a systematic analytical and numerical survey of the effect of arbitrary magnetic field strength and color chemical potential on the phase structure, relevant for compact star interiors and heavy ion collision physics.
