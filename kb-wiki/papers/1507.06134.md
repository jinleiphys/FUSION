# Self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems vs Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method. Spherical nuclei

> Recent results of the Fayans energy density functional (EDF) for spherical nuclei are reviewed. A comparison is made with predictions of several Skyrme EDFs. The charge radii and characteristics of the first 2^+ excitations in semi-magic nuclei are briefly discussed. The single-particle spectra of doubly magic nuclei are considered in more detail. The phonon-particle coupling effects are analyzed including the so-called tadpole term.

# Self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems vs Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method. Spherical nuclei

## Key claim

The Fayans energy density functional (EDF) reproduces experimental charge radii, excitation energies, and single-particle spectra in spherical nuclei significantly better than several popular Skyrme EDFs, with the advantage attributed to the Fayans functional's more sophisticated density dependence that implicitly incorporates energy-dependence effects from the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS). However, the phonon-particle coupling corrections to single-particle energies, including the tadpole term, improve agreement for heavy nuclei like 208Pb but slightly worsen it for light nuclei such as 40Ca and 48Ca, partly due to an approximation that neglects in-volume components of the phonon vertex.

## Method

The authors compare predictions of the Fayans EDF (versions DF3, DF3-a, and DF3-b) with those of several Skyrme EDFs (SLy4, SkM*, and HFB-17) for spherical nuclei. Charge radii are computed self-consistently for lead isotopes; excitation energies and B(E2) values for the first 2+ states are obtained via QRPA with volume and surface pairing for tin and lead isotopes; single-particle energies in seven doubly magic nuclei are calculated at the mean-field level and then corrected for phonon-particle coupling, including both the pole diagram and the tadpole term, using the DF3-a functional.

## Key numbers

- Charge radii in lead isotopes: Fayans EDF achieves agreement with data at the level of 0.01 fm; SLy4 values are systematically higher by approximately 0.03 fm; HFB-17 fails systematically for A less than 190 with disagreement reaching 0.1 fm.
- For tin isotopes, 2+ excitation energies with volume pairing are systematically higher by 200-400 keV compared to surface pairing, with surface pairing being on average better.
- Average rms deviation of single-particle energies from experiment for all 105 levels across seven doubly magic nuclei: DF3-b = 0.89 MeV, DF3-a = 0.98 MeV, DF3 = 0.89 MeV, HFB-17 = 1.40 MeV.
- For 208Pb specifically: DF3-a without PC corrections gives 0.51 MeV; with PC corrections including tadpole gives 0.38 MeV (Table 4) or 0.34 MeV (Conclusions); HFB-17 gives 1.15 MeV; RMF approach from Litvinova and Ring (no tadpole) gives 0.85 MeV.
- For the 3-1 state in 208Pb: neutron phonon amplitude alpha_L^n = 0.32 fm, proton alpha_L^p = 0.33 fm, BM model alpha_L^BM = 0.30 fm.
- Pole and tadpole contributions from the 3-1 state to neutron SPEs in 208Pb range from -0.708 MeV (pole, 1j15/2) to +0.724 MeV (pole, 2f7/2), with tadpole contributions between 0.012 MeV and 0.211 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach developed by Fayans and coauthors (Fayans 1994, 2000) and contrasts it with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method (Vautherin and Brink 1972) and the Gogny force (Gogny 1975). The comparison with Skyrme EDFs SLy4 and SkM* uses results from BE2-HFB (2010), while HFB-17 predictions are taken from the HFB-17 database. The single-particle energy analysis builds on earlier work by Saperstein and Tolokonnikov (2014) and includes the tadpole term following Khodel and Saperstein (1982), which is often neglected in other approaches such as Litvinova and Ring (2006) and Bortignon et al. (1998). The results enable improved systematic calculations of nuclear structure in spherical nuclei and suggest that the Fayans EDF's implicit treatment of energy dependence via its density dependence is a key advantage over simpler Skyrme functionals.
