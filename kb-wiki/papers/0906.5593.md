# A simple model for the nuclear unintegrated gluon distribution

> The solution of non-linear evolution equations for dense nuclear gluon density has been suggested as one of the relevant mechanisms of pA and AA collisions at collider energies. Here we study a simple parameterization for the unintegrated gluon distribution using the knowledge of asymptotic solutions of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, describing high-energy QCD in the presence of saturation effects. A satisfactory description of nuclear shadowing at small-x is obtained and it allows us to understand the qualitative behavior shown by data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple parameterization for the nuclear unintegrated gluon distribution based on asymptotic solutions of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, claiming a satisfactory description of nuclear shadowing at small-x, but the agreement is qualitative and relies on geometric scaling arguments that may break down in certain kinematic regions.

## Method

The authors start from an existing analytic parameterization of the proton unintegrated gluon distribution (derived from asymptotic solutions of the BK equation fitted to HERA data) and extend it to nuclear targets by replacing the proton saturation scale with a nuclear saturation scale that scales with atomic number A through a power law. The nuclear saturation scale is defined as Q_{s,A}^2(Y) = (A pi R_p^2 / pi R_A^2)^Delta Q_s^2(Y), where Delta is taken from the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) model as Delta = 1/delta with delta = 0.79 +/- 0.02. The model is then compared to experimental data on nuclear structure function ratios R(A/B) for various nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- The critical anomalous dimension from the LO BFKL kernel is gamma_c = 0.6275, with the wavefront velocity v_c = 4.88 * alpha_bar.
- The fit to HERA data uses parameters: v_c = 0.807, chi_c'' = 2.96, k_0^2 = 3.917 x 10^{-3} GeV^2, and R_p = 4.142 GeV.
- The nuclear saturation scale exponent delta = 0.79 +/- 0.02, giving Delta = 1/delta.
- The analysis is restricted to kinematic range x <= 0.01 and 0.045 <= Q^2 <= 150 GeV^2 for the proton structure function fit.

## Context

This work extends the parameterization of the unintegrated gluon distribution for protons developed in Marcos (2007) to nuclear targets, using geometric scaling arguments from the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) model. The model enables calculation of observables in pA and AA collisions within the k_perp-factorization framework, and the authors note that their results are similar to numerical solutions of the BK equation with running coupling corrections. The paper also discusses connections to the RSYZ equation and comparisons with other parameterizations tested in Baier (2008).
