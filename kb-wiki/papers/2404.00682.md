# A new approach for deducing rms proton radii from charge-changing reactions of neutron-rich nuclei and the reaction-target dependence

> We report the charge-changing cross sections ($σ_{\text{cc}}$) of 24 $p$-shell nuclides on both hydrogen and carbon at about 900$A$ MeV, of which $^{8,9}$Li, $^{10\textendash12}$Be, $^{10,14,15}$B, $^{14,15,17\textendash22}$N and $^{16}$O on hydrogen and $^{8,9}$Li on carbon are for the first time. Benefiting from the data set,we found a new and robust relationship between the scaling factor of the Glauber model calculations and the separation energies of the nuclei of interest on both targets.This allows us to deduce proton radii ($R_p$) for the first time from the cross sections on hydrogen. Nearly identical $R_p$ values are deduced from both target data for the neutron-rich carbon isotopes, however, the $R_p$ from the hydrogen target is systematically smaller in the neutron-rich nitrogen isotopes.This calls for further experimental and theoretical investigations.

## Key claim

The paper reports a new empirical correlation between the scaling factor of Glauber model calculations and the separation energy \(S_1\) (defined as the sum of the neutron separation energy of the projectile and the proton separation energy of the one-neutron-removed nucleus) for charge-changing cross sections on both hydrogen and carbon targets, which allows the first extraction of proton radii from hydrogen-target data; however, the deduced proton radii from hydrogen and carbon targets agree for neutron-rich carbon isotopes but show systematic differences for neutron-rich nitrogen isotopes, indicating a target dependence that requires further investigation.

## Method

Charge-changing cross sections (\(\sigma_{\text{cc}}\)) were measured for 24 \(p\)-shell nuclides (Li to O isotopes) at about 900\(A\) MeV using the transmission method with a carbon target and a polyethylene target at the FRS fragment separator at GSI; cross sections on hydrogen were derived by subtracting the carbon-target cross sections from the polyethylene-target results. The zero-range optical-limit approximation (ZROLA) Glauber model was used to calculate \(\sigma_{\text{cc}}\) for ten nuclei with well-known charge radii, and the ratio of experimental to calculated cross sections was found to follow a linear dependence on the separation energy \(S_1\), which was then used as an empirical scaling factor to deduce proton radii for the remaining 14 neutron-rich isotopes.

## Key numbers

* Charge-changing cross sections on carbon (\(\sigma_{\text{cc,C}}\)) and hydrogen (\(\sigma_{\text{cc,H}}\)) are reported for 24 \(p\)-shell nuclides at about 900\(A\) MeV; first-time measurements include \(^{8,9}\)Li, \(^{10-12}\)Be, \(^{10,14,15}\)B, \(^{14,15,17-22}\)N, and \(^{16}\)O on hydrogen, and \(^{8,9}\)Li on carbon.
* For the ten calibration nuclei, the ratio \(\sigma_{\text{cc}}^{\text{exp}}/\sigma_{\text{cc}}^{\text{calc}}\) on hydrogen follows \(f(\mathrm{H}) = -0.0132(10) S_1 + 1.596(23)\) and on carbon follows \(f(\mathrm{C}) = -0.0047(8) S_1 + 1.209(16)\), with \(S_1\) in MeV.
* The scaling factor introduces an uncertainty of typically 1.3% for \(\sigma_{\text{cc}}^{\text{calc}}\) on carbon and 2.4% on hydrogen.
* For neutron-rich nitrogen isotopes, the proton radii from the carbon target (\(R_p^{\text{C}}\)) are systematically larger than those from the hydrogen target (\(R_p^{\text{H}}\)), with the difference \(R_p^{\text{C}} - R_p^{\text{H}}\) for odd-\(Z\) isotopes following a linear trend with neutron excess having a slope of 0.025(6), compared to 0.006(6) for even-\(Z\) isotopes.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charge-changing cross sections and proton radii determinations using Glauber model analyses, such as those by Yamaguchi (2011), Bagchi (2019), and Kanungo (2016), which observed systematic underestimation of cross sections by the model and introduced scaling factors. The new correlation with separation energy \(S_1\) provides an empirical method to account for the proton-evaporation process after neutron removal, as proposed by Tanaka (2022) and Zhao (2023), and enables proton radius extraction from hydrogen-target data for the first time. The observed target dependence for odd-\(Z\) isotopes calls for further theoretical work on nuclear density distributions and reaction mechanisms, and the method is expected to be applicable to future experiments at facilities like FAIR.
