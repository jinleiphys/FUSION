# Cold quark matter in astrophysics of compact stars

> The appearance of quark matter in the centers of compact stars has a number of astrophysical implications. In this contribution I discuss the structure and stability of compact stars, gravitational radiation from non-axisymmetric deformations, and nucleation and dynamics of vortices (flux tubes) in the color superconducting phases.

## Key claim

The appearance of quark matter in the centers of compact stars has a number of astrophysical implications, but the existence of hybrid stars with central cores made of quark matter is not obvious because deconfined quark matter has a larger number of degrees of freedom than nuclear matter, leading to a softening of the equation of state that may be fatal to gravitational stability.

## Method

The author reviews and summarizes recent results concerning the astrophysical implications of deconfinement and color superconductivity in quark matter, centered mainly upon his own recent research. The work describes quark matter in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, a low-energy non-perturbative approximation to QCD, and matches the quark equation of state to a low-density hadronic equation of state via Maxwell construction. The spherically symmetric solutions of Einstein's equations for self-gravitating fluids are given by the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and the stability of configurations is analyzed through the requirement that the derivative dM/dρ_c should be positive.

## Key numbers

- The central densities of massive neutron stars are at about five times the nuclear saturation density or more.
- A stable branch of hybrid stars emerges in the range of central densities 1.3 ≤ ρ_c ≤ 2.5 × 10^15 g cm^-3.
- The masses of the quark cores cover the range 0 ≤ M_core/M_⊙ ≤ 0.75-0.88 for central densities 1.3 × 10^15 ≤ ρ_c ≤ 2 × 10^15.
- The increase in the maximum mass for stable hybrid configurations (in solar mass units) is 2.052 → 2.462 for model A, 2.017 → 2.428 and 2.4174 for model A1, and 1.981 → 2.35 for model B.
- The measured masses of pulsars in the double-pulsar system PSR J0737-3039 are M/M_⊙ = 1.337 for pulsar A and M/M_⊙ = 1.250 for pulsar B.
- Differences of about 20% in the moments of inertia of purely nuclear and hybrid configurations of the same mass are within the accuracy achievable in measurements similar to those for PSR J0737-3039.

## Context

This contribution extends the line of work on hybrid stars with quark matter cores, building on the study of Ippolito (2007) which addressed equilibrium and stability of non-rotating and rapidly rotating hybrid stars. The work discusses how the discovery of the double-pulsar system PSR J0737-3039 by Lyne and Burgay (2003) offers a unique opportunity to place bounds on compact star parameters through moment of inertia measurements, as proposed by Lattimer (2004). The results enable future observational discrimination between purely nuclear and hybrid star configurations, though heavier objects than those currently measured are needed to obtain useful bounds on the moment of inertia of hybrid configurations.
