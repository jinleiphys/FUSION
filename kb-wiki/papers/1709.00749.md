# Efimov states near a Feshbach resonance and the limits of van der Waals universality at finite background scattering length

> We calculate the spectrum of three-body Efimov bound states near a Feshbach resonance within a model which accounts both for the finite range of interactions and the presence of background scattering. The latter may be due to direct interactions in an open channel or a second overlapping Feshbach resonance. It is found that background scattering gives rise to substantial changes in the trimer spectrum as a function of the detuning away from a Feshbach resonance, in particular in the regime where the background channel supports Efimov states on its own. Compared to the situation with negligible background scattering, the regime where van der Waals universality applies is shifted to larger values of the resonance strength if the background scattering length is positive. For negative background scattering lengths, in turn, van der Waals universality extends to even small values of the resonance strength parameter, consistent with experimental results on Efimov states in $^{39}$K. Within a simple model, we show that short-range three-body forces do not affect van der Waals universality significantly. Repulsive three-body forces may, however, explain the observed variation between around $-8$ and $-10$ of the ratio between the scattering length where the first Efimov trimer appears and the van der Waals length.

## Key claim

The paper shows that van der Waals universality for Efimov trimers is more robust than predicted by standard two-channel Feshbach resonance models, because a nonvanishing background scattering length shifts the regime where universality applies: for positive background scattering lengths the universal regime moves to larger resonance strengths, while for negative background scattering lengths universality extends to even small resonance strength parameters, consistent with experimental results in $^{39}$K.

## Method

The authors extend a two-channel Feshbach resonance model by adding a second molecular field that accounts for background scattering, either from direct open-channel interactions or from overlapping Feshbach resonances. They solve the coupled-channel Skornyakov-Ter-Martirosian equation for three-body bound states using a Lorentzian form factor with range set to the mean scattering length $\bar{a}=0.956\,l_{\text{vdw}}$, with all parameters determined by two-body physics (the van der Waals length $l_{\text{vdw}}$, the resonance strength $s_{\text{res}}=\bar{a}/r^{\star}$, and the dimensionless background scattering length $r_{\text{bg}}=a_{\text{bg}}/\bar{a}$). They also introduce a simple model for short-range three-body forces to test their effect on universality.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $|a_{-}|/l_{\text{vdw}}$ for the first Efimov trimer in the open-channel dominated limit ($s_{\text{res}}\gg 1$) is approximately $8.3$, where $a_{-}$ is the scattering length where the first Efimov trimer appears.
- In the closed-channel dominated limit ($s_{\text{res}}\ll 1$), the ratio approaches $a_{-}\to -10.3\, r^{\star}$, with $r^{\star}$ the intrinsic length of the Feshbach resonance.
- The observed ratio $|a_{-}|/l_{\text{vdw}}$ for a number of different alkali atoms and many Feshbach resonances ranges between $-8\,l_{\text{vdw}}$ and $-10\,l_{\text{vdw}}$, with an average value $\langle a_{-}\rangle \approx -9.45\, l_{\text{vdw}}$.
- For the $^{7}$Li resonance at $737\,$G, the observed ratio $|a_{-}|/l_{\text{vdw}}\simeq 7.75$ despite a rather small resonance strength $s_{\text{res}}\simeq 0.56$.
- The effective range of the full two-body scattering amplitude is given by $r_{\rm e} = -2 r^\star (1 - a_{\text{bg}}/a)^2 + 3 \bar{a} (1 - 4\bar{a}/3a)$, which for pure background scattering approaches $r_e^\text{vdw}=2.9179 \bar a (1-2\bar a/a+2\bar a^2/a^2)$.
- The effective range crosses zero near $s_{\text{res}}=2/3$ and approaches $r_e\to-2r^\star$ in the closed-channel dominated limit.

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-channel model by Schmidt et al. (2012) that explained van der Waals universality for open-channel dominated resonances but predicted a strong increase of $|a_{-}|/l_{\text{vdw}}$ for decreasing resonance strength, a trend not observed in experiments on $^{39}$K by Roy et al. (2013) or on $^{7}$Li. The present model resolves this discrepancy by including finite background scattering length, showing that negative background scattering lengths extend van der Waals universality to smaller resonance strengths. The results enable systematic predictions for Efimov spectra near Feshbach resonances with arbitrary background scattering, and the authors show that short-range three-body forces do not significantly affect van der Waals universality, though repulsive three-body forces may explain the observed variation of $a_{-}/l_{\text{vdw}}$ between $-8$ and $-10$.
