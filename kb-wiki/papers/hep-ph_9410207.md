# Parton Distributions in Nuclei

> We discuss two topics in this talk. One is a brief overview of nuclear parton distributions, and the other is SU(2)-flavor-symmetry breaking in {\it nuclear} antiquark distributions. First, we show that nuclear structure functions $F_2^A(x)$ could be explained in a $Q^2$ rescaling model with parton recombination effects. Then, nuclear gluon distributions are discussed in this parton model. We point out other interesting distributions for future investigations. Second, $\bar u-\bar d$ distributions in nuclei are discussed in order to shed light on nuclear modification of the $\bar u-\bar d$ distribution. -------------------- * This talk was supported by the Yamada Science Foundation.

# Parton Distributions in Nuclei

## Key claim
Nuclear structure functions F₂^A(x) can be explained across a wide Bjorken-x range (0.005 < x < 0.8) using a parton model that combines Q² rescaling for the medium-x EMC effect with parton recombination for small-x shadowing, but the shadowing at very small x (below 0.02) is highly sensitive to the input gluon distribution. Additionally, a finite SU(2)-flavor asymmetry in nuclear antiquark distributions can arise purely from neutron-excess effects via parton recombination, even if the nucleon sea is flavor symmetric.

## Method
The author constructs a parton model for nuclei that incorporates two mechanisms: Q² rescaling to explain the EMC effect in the medium-x region, and parton recombination to produce shadowing at small x. Nuclear parton distributions are calculated at a low Q² scale using these ideas, then evolved via the Altarelli-Parisi equations. For the flavor-asymmetry analysis, the author evaluates a recombination formula (Eq. 1) that relates the nuclear modification of (ū − d̄) to the neutron-excess parameter ε = (N−Z)/(N+Z), using MRS-D0 (1993) parton distributions as input for the tungsten nucleus ⁷⁴W₁₁₀.

## Key numbers
- The model reproduces experimental F₂^A/F₂^D data across the range 0.005 < x < 0.8, as shown for calcium.
- For medium-size nuclei, the gluon distribution ratio G_A(x)/G_N(x) in the medium-x region is typically 0.9.
- The neutron-excess parameter ε for tungsten is defined as ε = (N−Z)/(N+Z), with the nucleus specified as ⁷⁴W₁₁₀.
- The recombination kernel K is given by K = 9A^{1/3} α_s(Q²) / (2 R₀² Q²) with R₀ = 1.1 fm.

## Context
This work extends the author’s earlier model (Kumano, 1993, 1994) that combined Q² rescaling and parton recombination to explain the EMC effect and nuclear shadowing in a unified framework, addressing a gap where most prior models treated the medium-x and small-x regions separately. The flavor-asymmetry analysis builds on the NMC discovery of Gottfried sum rule violation and addresses whether Drell-Yan data from heavy targets like tungsten can be directly compared with nucleon results, given potential nuclear modifications. The results point toward needed future measurements: valence-quark shadowing via neutrino experiments (F₃^A/F₃^D), sea-quark shadowing at RHIC, and gluon shadowing via direct-photon experiments at RHIC or improved J/ψ production data.
