# $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\mathrm{B}$ mesons in nuclear medium

> We discuss the mass modifications of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ ($\mathrm{B}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$) mesons in nuclear medium. The heavy quark symmetry for $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ ($\mathrm{B}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$) mesons is adopted, and the interaction between a $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ or $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ ($\mathrm{B}$ or $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$) meson and a nucleon is supplied from the pion exchange. We find the negative mass shifts for $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ meson and $\mathrm{B}$ meson, and hence that the $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\mathrm{B}$ mesons are bound in the nuclear medium. As applications, we consider the atomic nuclei with a $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ meson, $^{40}_{\bar{\mathrm{D}}}\mathrm{Ca}$ and $^{208}_{\bar{\mathrm{D}}}\mathrm{Pb}$, and investigate the energy levels of the $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ meson in each nucleus. We also discuss the mass shifts in the isospin asymmetric nuclear medium, and present the possible phenomenon about distribution of isospin density around a $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ or $\mathrm{B}$ meson in nuclear medium. We find that the mass shifts of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$ mesons have large imaginary parts, which would prevent precise study of the energy levels of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$ mesons in nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors claim that $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\mathrm{B}$ mesons experience negative mass shifts in nuclear medium and are therefore bound, but this result depends on the pion-exchange interaction and heavy quark symmetry framework, and the mass shifts of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$ mesons have large imaginary parts that prevent precise study of their energy levels in nuclei.

## Method

The authors adopt heavy quark symmetry for $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ (or $\mathrm{B}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$) mesons and use pion-exchange interactions to compute the self-energies of these mesons in uniform nuclear matter at zero temperature. They evaluate the leading two-pion-exchange contributions to the self-energies using in-medium nucleon propagators with a Fermi momentum cutoff, regularizing divergent integrals with a three-dimensional momentum cutoff parameter derived from quark model radii.

## Key numbers

- Self-energy of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ meson at normal nuclear matter density $n_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$: $-35.1$ MeV (real part, no imaginary part)
- Self-energy of $\mathrm{B}$ meson at $n_0$: $-106.9$ MeV (real part, no imaginary part)
- Self-energy of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ meson at $n_0$: $-153.9 - i170.1$ MeV
- Self-energy of $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$ meson at $n_0$: $-203.3 - i117.5$ MeV
- Critical density for imaginary part of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ meson self-energy: $n_{\mathrm{cr}} = 2.2$ fm$^{-3}$
- Critical density for imaginary part of $\mathrm{B}$ meson self-energy: $n_{\mathrm{cr}} = 0.28$ fm$^{-3}$
- Binding energy of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ meson ground state in $^{40}_{\bar{\mathrm{D}}}\mathrm{Ca}$: $28.2$ MeV
- Binding energy of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ meson ground state in $^{208}_{\bar{\mathrm{D}}}\mathrm{Pb}$: $32.8$ MeV
- Mass splitting between $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$: approximately $140$ MeV
- Mass splitting between $\mathrm{B}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$: approximately $45$ MeV
- Pion decay constant $f = 132$ MeV; coupling constant $g_\pi = 0.59$; axial coupling $g_A = 1.3$

## Context

This work extends the approach used for pion dynamics in nuclear matter by Kaiser (1997-2007) and in hypernuclear matter by Finelli (2009) to heavy-light mesons containing charm or bottom quarks. It builds on earlier studies by Tsushima (1998, 2002) and Sibirtsev (1999) that suggested $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\mathrm{B}$ mesons could be bound in atomic nuclei, and on two-body $\bar{\mathrm{D}}\mathrm{N}$ and $\mathrm{B}\mathrm{N}$ system calculations by Yasui (2009) and Yamaguchi (2011) that showed attractive interactions from pion exchange. The results enable future experimental searches for $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$-nucleus bound states and studies of isospin density distributions around heavy mesons in nuclear medium.
