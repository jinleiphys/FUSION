# Muon Capture in Deuterium

> Model dependence of the capture rates of the negative muon capture in deuterium is studied starting from potential models and the weak two-body meson exchange currents constructed in the tree approximation and also from an effective field theory. The tree one-boson exchange currents are derived from the hard pion chiral Lagrangians of the $N Δπρωa_1$ system. If constructed in conjunction with the one-boson exchange potentials, the capture rates can be calculated consistently. On the other hand, the effective field theory currents, constructed within the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, contain a low energy constant $\hat d ^R$ that cannot be extracted from data at the one-particle level nor determined from the first principles. Comparative analysis of the results for the doublet transition rate allows us to extract the constant $\hat d ^R$.

## Key claim

The doublet capture rate for negative muon capture in deuterium can be calculated consistently using either tree-approximation one-boson exchange currents or effective field theory currents, but the effective field theory approach contains a low-energy constant \(\hat d^R\) that cannot be determined from first principles or one-body data; a comparative analysis of the doublet transition rate allows extraction of this constant, with the preferred model (NI/Ib) yielding \(\Lambda_{1/2} = 416.3\ \text{s}^{-1}\), subject to a model dependence of about 4% from the choice of potential and current model.

## Method

The authors calculated the capture rates for \(\mu^- + d \rightarrow \nu_\mu + n + n\) using two approaches: (1) the Tree Approximation Approach (TAA), where weak axial meson exchange currents (MECs) of the one-boson exchange type were derived from hard pion chiral Lagrangians of the \(N\Delta\pi\rho\omega a_1\) system and used with Nijmegen I and Nijmegen 93 one-boson exchange potentials; and (2) a hybrid approach combining heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory currents with nuclear wave functions from the Schrödinger equation. The \(\Delta(1232)\) excitation currents were constructed with different off-shell parameter sets (Ia: \(Y=Z=-1/2\); Ib: \(Y=0, Z=1/2\)) and also from gauge-symmetric Lagrangians. The capture rates were computed from multipole expansions of the weak nuclear currents, including contributions from channels \(d \rightarrow {}^{2S+1}L_{j_f}\) with \(L=S,P,D,F\) and \(j_f=0,1,2\).

## Key numbers

- Doublet capture rate for the preferred model NI/Ib: \(\Lambda_{1/2} = 416.3\ \text{s}^{-1}\) (full calculation including all channels and MECs)
- Doublet capture rate for the channel \(d \rightarrow {}^1S_0\) only, model NI/Ib: \(\Lambda_{1/2}^0 = 254.0\ \text{s}^{-1}\)
- Statistical capture rate for model NI/Ib: \(\Lambda_{stat} = 146.4\ \text{s}^{-1}\)
- Quadruplet capture rate for model NI/Ib: \(\Lambda_{3/2} = 11.4\ \text{s}^{-1}\)
- MEC effect in the channel \(d \rightarrow {}^1S_0\) for model NI/Ib: 16.7 s\(^{-1}\) (approximately 4% of the total doublet rate)
- Contribution from weak vector \(\Delta\) excitation currents: approximately 4–6 s\(^{-1}\) (1–1.5% of \(\Lambda_{1/2}\))
- Estimated error from the 3% variation of \((f_{\pi N\Delta}/m_\pi)^2/4\pi = 0.783\ \text{fm}^2\): approximately 1 s\(^{-1}\) (0.25%)
- Cutoff masses for the Nijmegen potentials: \(\Lambda_\pi = 827.5\ \text{MeV}\) (NI), \(\Lambda_\pi = 1177.11\ \text{MeV}\) (N93)
- Previous measured value: \(\Lambda_{1/2} = 409 \pm 40\ \text{s}^{-1}\) (CEAL) and \(\Lambda_{1/2} = 470 \pm 29\ \text{s}^{-1}\) (M)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of muon capture in deuterium by Adam et al. (2005) and Chen et al. (2005) using a consistent treatment of meson exchange currents with one-boson exchange potentials, and by Ando et al. (2007) using a hybrid effective field theory approach with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential. The authors show that the \(\Delta\) excitation currents contribute non-negligibly (1–1.5%) and must be included for the expected experimental precision of about 1% from the MuSun experiment. The extracted low-energy constant \(\hat d^R\) from the comparison of TAA and hybrid calculations can be used in other weak processes in the two-nucleon system, such as neutrino-deuteron disintegration and proton-proton fusion.
