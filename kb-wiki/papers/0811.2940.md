# Elliptic flow of gluon matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Employing a perturbative QCD based parton cascade we calculate the elliptic flow $v_2$ and its transverse momentum dependence $v_2(p_T)$ for the gluon matter created in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. To make comparisons with the experimental data at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), parton-hadron duality is assumed. We find that whereas the integrated $v_2$ matches the experimental data, the gluon (or pion) $v_2(p_T)$ is about 20-50% smaller than the experimental data. Hadronization via gluon fragmentation and quark recombination seems to be the key to explaining the necessary jump of $v_2(p_T)$ from the partonic to the hadronic phase. We also show that the elliptic flow values moderately depend on the chosen freezeout condition, which will thus constrain the shear viscosity to the entropy density ratio of the quark gluon plasma created at RHIC.

## Key claim

Using a pQCD-based parton cascade (BAMPS) with parton-hadron duality, the integrated elliptic flow of gluon matter matches experimental data for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but the differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) is 20–50% smaller than the data, especially at intermediate transverse momenta between 1.5 and 4 GeV.

## Method

The authors employed the Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS) to solve the Boltzmann equation for on-shell gluons with pQCD interactions including elastic scatterings and bremsstrahlung with its backreaction, using a stochastic simulation method with test particles. Initial conditions were minijets with transverse momenta above \(p_0 = 1.4\) GeV from semihard nucleon-nucleon collisions using Glauber geometry, and freezeout occurred when local energy density dropped below a critical value \(e_c\), with gluons converted to pions via parton-hadron duality.

## Key numbers

- Integrated elliptic flow \(v_2\) from BAMPS with \(\alpha_s = 0.6\) and \(e_c = 1\) GeV fm\(^{-3}\) agrees with STAR and PHOBOS data for charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- Differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) from BAMPS is 20–50% smaller than experimental data at intermediate momenta \(1.5\) GeV \(< p_T < 4\) GeV.
- With \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) and \(e_c = 0.6\) GeV fm\(^{-3}\), the final \(v_2\) values are almost identical to those with \(\alpha_s = 0.6\) and \(e_c = 1\) GeV fm\(^{-3}\).
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) extracted from BAMPS lies between 0.15 (for \(\alpha_s = 0.3\)) and 0.08 (for \(\alpha_s = 0.6\)).
- Mean transverse momentum \(\langle p_T \rangle\) of gluons is 40–100% larger than STAR data for charged hadrons.
- Transverse energy per rapidity \(dE_T/d\eta\) from BAMPS with \(\alpha_s = 0.6\) is 7–15% smaller than experimental data.

## Context

This work extends earlier BAMPS calculations by Xu, Greiner, and collaborators (2008) that showed pQCD interactions could explain large elliptic flow buildup at RHIC, and provides detailed comparisons with experimental data from STAR, PHOBOS, and PHENIX. The results contradict the simple parton-hadron duality picture and suggest that hadronization via gluon fragmentation (1.5–2 pions per gluon) and quark recombination is needed to explain the jump of \(v_2(p_T)\) from partonic to hadronic phase. The findings enable future work incorporating quark degrees of freedom and realistic hadronization scenarios (e.g., Cooper-Frye prescription) to better constrain the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma.
