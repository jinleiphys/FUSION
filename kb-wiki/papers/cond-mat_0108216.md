# On "Do the attractive bosons condense?" by N. K. Wilkin, J. M. F. Gunn, R. A. Smith

> Using Perron-Frobenius theorem, we prove that the results by Wilkin, Gunn and Smith [1] for the ground states of N Bose atoms rotating at the angular momentum L in a harmonic atomic trap with frequency omega interacting via attractive delta^2(r) forces, are valid for a broad class of predominantly attractive interactions V(r), not necessarily attractive for any r. The sufficient condition for the interaction is that all the two-body matrix elements <z_1^k z_2^l |V| z_2^m z_1^n> allowed by the conservation of angular momentum k+l = m+n, are negative. This class includes, in particular, the Gaussian attraction of arbitrary radius, -1/r - Coulomb and log(r)-Coulomb forces, as well as all the short-range R << omega^{-1/2} interactions satisfying inequality int d^2r V(r) < 0. There is no condensation at L>> 1, and the angular momentum is concentrated in the collective ``center-of-mass'' mode.

# On "Do the attractive bosons condense?" by N. K. Wilkin, J. M. F. Gunn, R. A. Smith

## Key claim

Using the Perron-Frobenius theorem, the authors prove that the ground-state results of Wilkin, Gunn and Smith (1998) for N Bose atoms in a 2D harmonic trap with attractive delta-function interactions remain valid for a broad class of predominantly attractive interactions V(r), provided all two-body matrix elements allowed by angular-momentum conservation are negative. The key qualifier is that the interaction need not be attractive everywhere; it is sufficient that the interaction is "predominantly attractive" in the sense of this matrix-element condition.

## Method

The authors reformulate the problem of N bosons in a 2D harmonic trap (frequency omega) at fixed angular momentum L in the weak-coupling limit, using the basis of symmetrized monomials of the complex coordinates z_k. They construct the matrix M_{alpha,beta} = -V_{alpha,beta} from the two-body interaction matrix elements and apply the Perron-Frobenius theorem, which requires M to be irreducible with non-negative entries. They then derive the sufficient condition that all two-body matrix elements V_{kl,mn} with l > m must be negative, and show that this condition holds for Gaussian attraction of arbitrary radius, Coulomb (-1/r) and log-Coulomb forces, and all short-range interactions with negative integrated strength.

## Key numbers

- For attractive delta-function interaction: the two-body matrix element is given by V_{klm}(-delta) = -(k+l)! 2^{-(k+l+1)} / [pi sqrt(k! l! m! (k+l-m)!)].
- For short-range interactions with effective radius R much smaller than the oscillator length (R << 1 = sqrt(hbar/(m omega))), the condition reduces to the single inequality: integral d^2 r V_{R<<1}(r) < 0.
- For Gaussian attractive interaction V = -exp(-r^2/R^2)/(pi R^2), the matrix element is expressed in terms of a hypergeometric function and is negative for any R.
- The log-Coulomb and Coulomb (-1/r) cases are obtained by integrating the Gaussian result over R^2 or R respectively, yielding V_{klm} <= 0 in both cases.

## Context

This work extends the results of Wilkin, Gunn and Smith (1998), who derived the yrast states for attractive delta-function interactions in a 2D harmonic trap, to a much wider class of interactions including Gaussian, Coulomb, and log-Coulomb forces. The proof uses the Perron-Frobenius theorem from matrix theory (Lancaster and Tismenetsky, 1985) and builds on a related submission by the same authors (Hussein and Vorov, 2001). The result enables future studies of Bose condensation in rotating traps to consider realistic finite-range interactions rather than only the idealized delta-function case, while confirming that no condensation occurs at large angular momentum L >> 1, with angular momentum concentrated in the center-of-mass mode.
