# Microscopic Description of Rotational Nuclear Fission Elucidates Fragment Spin Generation and Scission Mechanism

> The generation of fission fragment spin as a probe of scission mechanism remains a question of considerable interests. We present here microscopic calculations of rapidly rotating fission of the compound nucleus 240Pu under varying initial conditions within the time-dependent density functional theory framework. The obtained spin ratio of light to heavy nascent fragments is unchanged up to high spins but diminished as excitation energies increase, indicating sawtooth structures in fragment spins would fade away at high excitations rather than high angular momentum. Further analysis elucidates that the bending scission mode is predominated at low excitation energies, which has been under intense debates. Results also show thicker and elongated neck configurations, along with scission nucleons emitted perpendicular to the fission axis, owing to rapid rotations. These findings offer insights into the scission mechanism of rotating compound nuclei that have usually been overlooked in earlier studies.

# Microscopic Description of Rotational Nuclear Fission Elucidates Fragment Spin Generation and Scission Mechanism

## Key claim

The spin ratio of light to heavy fission fragments from 240Pu is unchanged up to high initial angular momentum but diminishes as excitation energies increase, indicating that sawtooth structures in fragment spins fade away at high excitations rather than at high angular momentum. The bending scission mode predominates at low excitation energies, but a mixture of scission modes occurs at high excitations.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations, specifically the time-dependent Hartree-Fock+BCS (TD-BCS) approach, to simulate fission of the compound nucleus 240Pu after the saddle point. Initial wave functions were obtained by deformation-constrained HF+BCS calculations at finite temperatures (T = 0.0, 1.0, and 1.2 MeV), and the system was rotated along the y-axis perpendicular to the splitting direction by applying a driven rotation of the single-particle Hamiltonian for 30 fm/c. The Skyrme force SkM* and a mixing-type density dependent pairing interaction were adopted, and fragment spins were extracted from expectation values of angular momentum along the y-axis when fragments were well separated.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu at T = 0.0 MeV and L_CN = 27.6 ℏ, the fluctuation spin of the heavy fragment S_fH = -1.04 ℏ and of the light fragment S_fL = 1.47 ℏ, with fluctuation portions P_fH = 70.1% and P_fL = 41.8% of total fragment spin.
- At T = 0.0 MeV and L_CN = 95.2 ℏ, S_fH = -3.41 ℏ and S_fL = 4.64 ℏ, with P_fH = 70.3% and P_fL = 40.2%.
- At T = 1.2 MeV and L_CN = 28.8 ℏ, S_fH = -0.03 ℏ and S_fL = 0.91 ℏ, with P_fH = 1.1% and P_fL = 32.3%.
- At T = 1.2 MeV and L_CN = 99.3 ℏ, S_fH = -0.80 ℏ and S_fL = 3.24 ℏ, with P_fH = 9.6% and P_fL = 33.5%.
- Total excitation energies E* ranged from 1.8 MeV (T = 0.0, L_CN = 27.6 ℏ) to 52.6 MeV (T = 1.2, L_CN = 99.3 ℏ).

## Context

This work extends earlier TDDFT studies of fission fragment spins that considered only low or zero angular momentum (Bulgac, 2020; Scamps, 2021) by systematically varying both initial angular momentum and excitation energies. It addresses the debate between the bending scission mode (supported by TDDFT calculations from Bulgac, 2020) and the wriggling mode (favored by the FREYA Monte Carlo model from Randrup, 2020). The results enable future experimental tests in heavy-ion induced fission, particularly regarding scission neutron emission perpendicular to the fission axis and the disappearance of sawtooth structures at high excitation energies.
