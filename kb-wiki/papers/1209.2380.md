# Towards azimuthal anisotropy of direct photons

> Intensive radiation of magnetic bremsstrahlung type (synchrotron radiation) resulting from the interaction of escaping quarks with the collective confining colour field is discussed as a new possible mechanism of observed direct photon anisotropy.

## Key claim

The authors propose that synchrotron radiation from quarks moving in the confining colour field at the surface of the quark-gluon plasma is a significant and previously overlooked mechanism that can explain the large azimuthal anisotropy of direct photons observed by PHENIX, without invoking non-equilibrium dynamics.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma as a hydrodynamically expanding cylinder and calculate the photon yield from the surface using the chromoelectric flux tube model, where quarks crossing the boundary experience a constant confining force. They compare this surface photon yield to the standard volume photon production from Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation, using the Bjorken scaling solution for the plasma evolution and an ideal gas equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The confining force magnitude is taken as \(\sigma \simeq 0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The ratio of surface to volume photon yield is \(N^{\gamma}_{\rm surface}/N^{\gamma}_{\rm volume} \approx 2\) for a plasma cylinder radius \(r = 10\) fm and a reasonably large initial temperature \(T_0\).
- The photon azimuthal anisotropy coefficient \(v_2^{\gamma}\) is proportional to the spatial eccentricity \(\epsilon = b/2R_A\), where \(b\) is the impact parameter and \(R_A\) is the nuclear radius.
- The observed "anomalous" photon radiation temperature is about \(T_{\rm ave}\simeq 220\) MeV at collision energy \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.

## Context

This work addresses the contradiction between the small azimuthal anisotropy predicted by standard photon production mechanisms from quark-gluon plasma and the large anisotropy measured by the PHENIX Collaboration (2011). The proposed surface synchrotron radiation mechanism extends the authors' earlier work (Goloviznin et al., several papers) and offers an alternative to other phenomenological suggestions such as those by Bzdak, van Hees, Kharzeev, Liu, and Linnyk. The result enables a possible resolution of the PHENIX direct photon puzzle without requiring non-equilibrium dynamics.
