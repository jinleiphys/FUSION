# Extended Thomas-Fermi approximation to the one-body density matrix

> The one-body density matrix is derived within the Extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. This has been done starting from the Wigner-Kirkwood distribution function for a non-local single-particle potential. The links between this new approach to the density matrix with former ones available in the literature are widely discussed. The semiclassical Hartree-Fock energy at Extended Thomas-Fermi level is also obtained in the case of a non-local one-body Hamiltonian. Numerical applications are performed using the Gogny and Brink-Boeker effective interactions. The semiclassical binding energies and root mean square radii are compared with the fully quantal ones and with those obtained using the Strutinsky averaged method.

## Key claim

The paper derives the one-body density matrix in the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation starting from the Wigner-Kirkwood distribution function for a non-local single-particle potential, establishing that the ETF density matrix corresponds to summing all momentum-weighted integrals evaluated semiclassically, whereas the Negele-Vautherin (NV) and Campi-Bouyssy (CB) approximations are truncations of this expansion.

## Method

The authors performed the inverse Wigner transform of the Wigner-Kirkwood distribution function expanded to ℏ² order, then expressed the Fermi momentum and its derivatives in terms of the local density and its gradients to obtain the ETF density matrix in coordinate space. For non-local potentials, they derived the ETF density matrix including effective mass corrections, and obtained the semiclassical Hartree-Fock energy at ETF level. Restricted variational calculations using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions were performed for ⁴He, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca nuclei with Gogny and Brink-Boeker effective interactions, minimizing with respect to the oscillator parameter.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca described with harmonic oscillator wavefunctions (α = 0.516 fm⁻¹), the ETF density matrix ratio ρ(R,s)/ρ(R) was compared with quantal, NV, CB, and Strutinsky results for R values from 0 to 4 fm and s values up to approximately 6 fm.
- Using the Gogny interaction, the ETF(KS) binding energies for uncharged nuclei were: ⁴He (-24.0 MeV), ¹⁶O (-122.6 MeV), and ⁴⁰Ca (-335.2 MeV), compared with quantal values of ⁴He (-24.0 MeV), ¹⁶O (-126.0 MeV), and ⁴⁰Ca (-338.4 MeV).
- Using the Brink-Boeker interaction, the ETF(KS) binding energies were: ⁴He (-22.5 MeV), ¹⁶O (-108.4 MeV), and ⁴⁰Ca (-297.8 MeV), compared with quantal values of ⁴He (-22.5 MeV), ¹⁶O (-112.6 MeV), and ⁴⁰Ca (-307.0 MeV).
- The ETF exchange Coulomb energy for ⁴⁰Ca using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions (α = 0.481 fm) was -13.1 MeV, compared with the quantal value of -13.2 MeV.
- For the Gogny interaction, the Strutinsky binding energies (smoothing parameter γ = 1.25 ℏω) were: ⁴He (-22.5 MeV, α = 0.643 fm⁻¹), ¹⁶O (-117.8 MeV, α = 0.567 fm⁻¹), and ⁴⁰Ca (-328.4 MeV, α = 0.516 fm⁻¹).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical ETF approach to the one-body density matrix for non-local potentials, building on the Wigner-Kirkwood ℏ-expansion framework described in Ring (1980) and the recent WK expansion for non-local potentials by Centelles (1998). The authors establish formal connections between their ETF density matrix and the Negele-Vautherin (1972) and Campi-Bouyssy (1978) density matrix expansions, showing that NV and CB are truncations of the ETF expansion when the M₂ moment is computed semiclassically. The ETF approach enables semiclassical Hartree-Fock calculations with finite-range effective interactions (Gogny, Brink-Boeker) without fully quantal computations, though the authors note that ℏ⁴ contributions would be needed to reproduce Strutinsky averaged results accurately, as previously found for kinetic energy functionals by Brack (1976) and Centelles (1990).
