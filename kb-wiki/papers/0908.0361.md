# Dynamical heavy-quark recombination and the non-photonic single electron puzzle at RHIC

> We show that the single, non-photonic electron nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^e$ is affected by the thermal enhancement of the heavy-baryon to heavy-meson ratio in relativistic heavy-ion collisions with respect to proton-proton collisions. We make use of the dynamical quark recombination model to compute such ratio and show that this produces a sizable suppression factor for $R_{AA}^e$ at intermediate transverse momenta. We argue that such suppression factor needs to be considered, in addition to the energy loss contribution, in calculations of $R_{AA}^e$

## Key claim

The suppression of the single non-photonic electron nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^e$ at RHIC is partially caused by a thermal enhancement of the heavy-baryon to heavy-meson ratio in Au+Au collisions relative to p+p collisions, and this suppression factor must be considered *in addition to* the heavy-quark energy loss contribution, not as a replacement for it.

## Method

The authors used the dynamical quark recombination model (DQRM), a variational quantum Monte Carlo simulation with 384 particles (192 quarks and 192 antiquarks, with heavy quark mass set to $m_c = 10 m_u$), to compute the density-dependent probabilities for forming charmed baryons and mesons. These probabilities were then folded with a Bjorken expansion scenario (initial temperature $T_0 = 200$ MeV, final temperature $T_f = 100$ MeV, proper time $\tau_0 = 1$ fm to $\tau_f = 8$ fm) to obtain transverse momentum distributions for charmed hadrons, from which the baryon-to-meson ratio and the suppression factor $T_{AA}^e$ were derived.

## Key numbers

- Heavy quark mass ratio: $m_c = 10 m_u$ used in the simulation
- Initial hadronization temperature: $T_0 = 200$ MeV; final hadronization temperature: $T_f = 100$ MeV
- Hadronization time interval: $\tau_0 = 1$ fm to $\tau_f = 8$ fm
- Charmed baryon mass: $m^B = 2.29$ GeV (corresponding to $\Lambda_c$); charmed meson mass: $m^M = 1.87$ GeV (corresponding to $D$)
- Ratio of branching ratios for charmed baryons vs. mesons decaying inclusively into electrons: $x = 0.14$
- Integrated charmed baryon to meson ratio in p+p collisions from Pythia: $a^{\text{int}} = 0.073$
- At finite transverse expansion velocity $v_T = 0.4$, the charmed baryon to meson ratio exceeds 1 for $p_T \gsim 3.5$ GeV
- The suppression factor $T_{AA}^e$ is below 1 for the chosen evolution parameters, contributing to the suppression of $R_{AA}^e$

## Context

This work extends the qualitative argument of Sorensen (2008) and Martinez (2008) that an enhanced heavy-baryon to heavy-meson ratio could suppress the non-photonic electron yield, by providing a quantitative calculation using the dynamical quark recombination model previously applied to light-flavor ratios by Ayala (2008). The paper addresses the "non-photonic single electron puzzle" at RHIC, where calculations by Djordjevic (2006), Armesto (2006), and Wicks (2007) that successfully describe hadron suppression fail to describe $R_{AA}^e$ using only energy loss mechanisms. The results enable future work that includes both the recombination suppression factor and heavy-quark energy losses without double counting, and can be extended to investigate the $p_T$ region where fragmentation becomes dominant.
