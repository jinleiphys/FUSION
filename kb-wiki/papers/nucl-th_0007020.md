# Transverse energy fluctuations and the pattern of J/Psi suppression in Pb-Pb collisions

> The NA50 collaboration has recently observed that the J/psi production rate in Pb-Pb collisions decreases more rapidly as a function of the transverse energy for the most central collisions than for less central ones. We show that this phenomenon can be understood as an effect of transverse energy fluctuations in central collisions. A good fit of the data is obtained using a model which relates J/psi suppression to the local energy density. Our results suggest that the J/psi is completely suppressed at the highest densities achieved in Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The second drop in J/ψ suppression at high transverse energy in Pb-Pb collisions can be explained by transverse energy fluctuations in central collisions, rather than by the successive melting of charmonium bound states; the authors show that a model relating J/ψ suppression to local energy density, with fluctuations included, reproduces the data, but note that the fit is not perfect and requires assuming complete J/ψ disappearance at the highest densities.

## Method

The authors model J/ψ suppression by assuming that any J/ψ emerging at a point where the local energy density exceeds a critical value ε_c is suppressed, with the energy density proportional to the participant density scaled by the ratio of the actual transverse energy to its average at that impact parameter. Transverse energy fluctuations at fixed impact parameter are described by a Gaussian distribution with parameters q=0.274 GeV (mean transverse energy per participant) and a=1.27 (dimensionless dispersion parameter), fitted to the NA50 minimum-bias E_T distribution. The survival probability is computed by integrating over impact parameter and transverse position, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density with radius 6.52 fm and diffuseness 0.53 fm for lead, and compared to the Drell-Yan pair production rate to construct the ratio N(E_T) that is compared to NA50 data.

## Key numbers

- Critical participant density parameter n_c = 3.7 fm^{-2} for the step-function suppression model
- For the gradual suppression model: n_c = 3.75 fm^{-2} with λ = 2 fm^2
- For the two-threshold scenario: first threshold n_{c1} = 3.3 fm^{-2}, second threshold n_{c2} = 4.0 fm^{-2}
- Mean transverse energy per participant q = 0.274 GeV; dispersion parameter a = 1.27
- The "knee" of the E_T distribution sits at about 108 GeV
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section σ_N = 32 mb; absorption cross section σ_a ≈ 6.4 mb
- Normalization factor σ^{NN}_ψ/σ^{NN}_{DY} = 53.5 ± 3 (NA50 value); fits used values 52 and 53.5
- A 10% fluctuation in E_T decreases J/ψ production by about 30%

## Context

This work extends the model of Blaizot and Ollitrault (1996) that related anomalous J/ψ suppression to local energy density, by now including transverse energy fluctuations that were previously neglected. The authors argue against the interpretation of the NA50 collaboration (2000) and Gupta (1992) that the suppression pattern shows two drops corresponding to successive melting of χ and J/ψ charmonium states. The model is contrasted with the comover absorption scenario of Capella (2000), which produces a much weaker dependence on transverse energy fluctuations. The results suggest that complete J/ψ suppression occurs at the highest densities achieved in Pb-Pb collisions, and enable further discrimination between plasma-based and hadronic suppression mechanisms using the shape of the E_T dependence.
