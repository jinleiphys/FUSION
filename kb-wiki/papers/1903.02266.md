# The symmetry energy of the nuclear EoS: a study of collective motion and low-energy reaction dynamics in semiclassical approaches

> In the framework of mean-field based transport approaches, we discuss recent results concerning collective motion and low-energy heavy ion reactions involving neutron-rich systems. We focus on aspects which are particularly sensitive to the isovector terms of the nuclear effective interaction and the corresponding symmetry energy. As far as collective excitations are concerned, we discuss the mixed nature of dipole oscillations in neutron-rich systems. On the other hand, for reactions close to the Coulomb barrier, we investigate the structure of pre-equilibrium collective dipole oscillations, focusing on their sensitivity to the symmetry energy behavior below normal density. Nucleon emission is also considered within the same context. The possible impact of other relevant terms of the nuclear effective interaction on these mechanisms is also examined. From this analysis we expect to put further constraints on the nuclear Equation of State, of crucial importance also in the astrophysical context.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the mixed isoscalar-isovector nature of dipole excitations in neutron-rich nuclei, together with pre-equilibrium dynamical dipole radiation and nucleon emission in low-energy heavy-ion reactions, can be used to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, particularly below normal density, but the sensitivity depends on the specific effective interaction and the density region probed by each observable.

## Method

The authors employed the semiclassical Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport model, solving the two coupled dynamical equations for neutron and proton distribution functions with Skyrme-like effective interactions. They studied collective dipole excitations in isolated neutron-rich nuclei (68Ni, 132Sn, 208Pb) by applying instantaneous isoscalar or isovector dipole perturbations and following the time evolution of the dipole moment. For low-energy reactions, they investigated pre-equilibrium dynamical dipole oscillations and nucleon emission in heavy-ion collisions near the Coulomb barrier, using both momentum-dependent SAMi-J interactions (with symmetry energy J from 27 to 35 MeV at saturation) and momentum-independent interactions with different symmetry energy slopes.

## Key numbers

- The SAMi-J family allows for symmetry energy values at normal density J = 27, 31, and 35 MeV, with corresponding slope parameters L = 29.9, 74.5, and 115.2 MeV respectively
- The momentum-independent interactions asy-soft, asy-stiff, and asy-superstiff have J values of 30, 30.5, and 30.5 MeV with L values of 14.8, 79, and 106 MeV respectively
- For 132Sn with SAMi-J31, the isovector GDR peak is located at EGDR approximately 14 MeV, with low-energy PDR strength between E1 = 9 MeV and E2 = 11 MeV
- The three SAMi-J parametrizations give the same symmetry energy value C(rho_c) approximately 22 MeV at density rho_c approximately 0.6 rho_0, which represents the average density of intermediate-mass nuclei
- The neutron-proton effective mass splitting for SAMi-J interactions is m*_n - m*_p = 0.023 m I, with an effective isoscalar mass m*(I=0) = 0.67 m

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) and giant dipole resonance (GDR) in neutron-rich nuclei (Har2001, harPRL2000, harPRC2002) and dynamical dipole modes in low-energy reactions (baranPRL2001, simenelPRL2001, pierrPRC2009). The authors use the recently developed SAMi-J Skyrme interaction family (coll4) which isolates isovector effects by keeping isoscalar properties fixed, enabling systematic comparison with momentum-independent interactions (coll2). The results enable future constraints on the nuclear equation of state relevant for neutron skin thickness studies (piek12, GaiPRC2011, GaiPRC2012) and astrophysical applications such as compact star structure (stePR2005, burrelloPRC2015).
