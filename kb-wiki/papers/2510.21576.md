# Relativistic corrections for lepton-nucleus scattering in the short-time approximation

> We present an approach for including relativistic corrections in lepton-nucleus scattering calculations within the Short-Time Approximation (STA). Previous ab-initio studies employed electromagnetic currents expanded in powers of $q/m$, where $q$ is the momentum transfer and $m$ is the nucleon mass, restricting their validity to low-$q$ kinematics. We adopt an expansion scheme that treats the initial nucleon momentum perturbatively while allowing for arbitrary momentum transfer, thereby extending the applicability of the STA to high-$q$ regimes. Additionally, we incorporate a relativistic treatment of the two-nucleon final-state energies. Calculations for $^3$He and $^4$He inclusive electron scattering cross sections show a substantial improvement over previous results, achieving good agreement with experimental data in the quasi-elastic region for both low- and high-momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The authors present a method for including relativistic corrections in lepton-nucleus scattering within the Short-Time Approximation (STA) by expanding the covariant single-nucleon current in powers of the initial nucleon momentum \(p/m\) rather than the momentum transfer \(q/m\), thereby extending the STA's validity to high momentum transfer regimes where previous ab-initio approaches failed.

## Method

The authors perform a nonrelativistic reduction of the covariant single-nucleon electromagnetic current by treating the initial nucleon momentum \(p\) as a small parameter while keeping the momentum transfer \(q\) exact, yielding leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) expressions for the charge and current operators. They incorporate a relativistic treatment of two-nucleon final-state energies and evaluate inclusive electron scattering cross sections for \(^3\)He and \(^4\)He using the STA with a free two-nucleon propagator, employing the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon interaction and Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction for the ground state wave functions.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of longitudinal responses from the high-\(q\) and low-\(q\) expansion schemes, \(R_L^{\rm high-q}/R_L^{\rm low-q} \propto \alpha(q)^2\), gives \(\alpha^2 = 0.9\) for \(q = 700\) MeV/\(c\), \(\alpha^2 = 0.77\) for \(q = 1500\) MeV/\(c\), and approaches \(1/2\) in the limit \(q \gg m\).
* The ratio of transverse responses from the LO high-\(q\) and LO low-\(q\) expansions gives a suppression factor of approximately 0.71 at \(q = 700\) MeV/\(c\) and approximately 0.37 at \(q = 1500\) MeV/\(c\).
* Previous QMC calculations (GFMC and STA) agreed with experimental data only up to momentum transfer values of \(q \simeq 600\) MeV/\(c\).
* The Darwin-Foldy term and the LO high-\(q\) expression for the longitudinal response agree well up to \(q \sim 500\) MeV/\(c\), but at \(q = 1500\) MeV/\(c\) the high-\(q\) result gives a \(\sim 10\%\) enhancement over the Darwin-Foldy result.

## Context

This work extends the Short-Time Approximation (STA) framework developed by Pastore (2019) and Andreoli (2021), which previously used electromagnetic currents expanded in powers of \(q/m\) and was limited to low momentum transfer. The new expansion scheme, following an approach similar to Jeschonnek (1997) for single-nucleon scattering, allows the STA to achieve good agreement with experimental data in the quasi-elastic region for both low and high momentum transfer, addressing a limitation highlighted by comparisons with the Spectral Function method of Benhar (1994) and Rocco (2015). This development is directly relevant for neutrino-nucleus scattering programs such as DUNE and for ongoing electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab, and the authors note that future work will extend the STA to medium-mass nuclei using Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo methods.
