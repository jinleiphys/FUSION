# Constraining the mass and width of the $N^*(1685)$ resonance

> We have examined the existence of the $N^*(1685)$ resonance, which is recently listed by the Particle Data Group as a one-star nucleon resonance, by using a covariant isobar model for kaon photoproduction and assuming that the resonance has $J^p=1/2^+$, in accordance with our previous finding. After the inclusion of this resonance the changes in the total $χ^2$ show two clear minima at $M_{N^*}=1650$ and 1696 MeV, which correspond to two different resonance states. The former corresponds to the narrow nucleon resonance found in our previous investigation, whereas the latter corresponds to a new resonance found as we increase the resonance width. From the latter we derive the mass and width relation of the $N^*(1685)$ resonance. We observe that the properties of both the $N^*(1685)$ and $N^*(1710)P_{11}$ resonances are strongly correlated. Although the best fit of the present work yields $M_{N^*}=1696$ MeV and $Γ_{N^*}=76$ MeV, the apparently small $N^*(1710)P_{11}$ coupling constant to the $K^+Λ$ channel found in previous investigations suggests that $Γ_{N^*}\lesssim 35$ MeV, which, according to the mass and width relation, corresponds to $M_{N^*}\lesssim 1680$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the \(N^*(1685)\) resonance, listed as a one-star state by the Particle Data Group, likely corresponds to a mass below 1680 MeV and a width below 35 MeV when the small coupling of the \(N^*(1710)P_{11}\) resonance to the \(K^+\Lambda\) channel is taken into account, rather than the best-fit values of 1696 MeV mass and 76 MeV width obtained from a pure \(\chi^2\) minimization.

## Method

The author used a fully covariant isobar model for the \(\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda\) reaction, fitting over 3500 data points (differential cross sections, recoil polarization, beam-recoil double polarization, photon \(\Sigma\) asymmetry, and target \(T\) asymmetry) up to a center-of-mass energy \(W = 2200\) MeV. The resonance mass was scanned from 1620 to 1740 MeV and the total width from 1 to 100 MeV, with the changes in total \(\chi^2\) monitored to locate minima. A mass-width relation was then derived by fixing the width and finding the minimum \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for each width value.

## Key numbers

- Two clear \(\chi^2\) minima were found at resonance masses \(M_{N^*} = 1650\) MeV and \(M_{N^*} = 1696\) MeV.
- The best fit of the present work yields \(M_{N^*} = 1696\) MeV and \(\Gamma_{N^*} = 76\) MeV.
- The narrow resonance at 1650 MeV only exists for total width \(\Gamma_{N^*} \le 25\) MeV.
- Considering the small \(N^*(1710)P_{11}\) coupling constant, the largest reasonable total width is \(\Gamma_{N^*} \lesssim 35\) MeV, corresponding to \(M_{N^*} \lesssim 1680\) MeV.
- If the width is fixed to 25 MeV (from \(\eta\) photoproduction experiments), the mass-width relation gives \(M_{N^*} \approx 1675\) MeV.
- The \(N^*(1710)P_{11}\) coupling constant from a previous study is \(G_{N^*(1710)}/4\pi \approx 0.08\), smaller than the \(N^*(1650)S_{11}\) value of \(G_{N^*(1650)}/4\pi \approx 0.14\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigation (Mart, 2011) of a narrow \(J^p = 1/2^+\) resonance in kaon photoproduction, which had limited the resonance width to 10 MeV. It also addresses the \(N^*(1685)\) state recently listed by the Particle Data Group (2012) and compares its extracted properties with those from \(\eta\) photoproduction experiments by the CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration (Jaegle, 2008; Jaegle, 2011) and quasi-free Compton scattering on the neutron by the GRAAL Collaboration (Kuznetsov, 2010). The finding that the \(N^*(1685)\) and \(N^*(1710)P_{11}\) resonances are strongly correlated enables future experiments at MAMI, Mainz, to help resolve the resonance parameters.
