# The quasi-deuteron model at low RG resolution

> The quasi-deuteron model introduced by Levinger is used to explain cross sections for knocking out high-momentum protons in photo-absorption on nuclei. This is within a framework we characterize as exhibiting high renormalization group (RG) resolution. Assuming a one-body reaction operator, the nuclear wave function must include two-body short-range correlations (SRCs) with deuteron-like quantum numbers. In a previous paper, we showed that SRC physics can be naturally accounted for at low RG resolution. Here we describe the quasi-deuteron model at low RG resolution and determine the Levinger constant, which is proportional to the ratio of nuclear photo-absorption to that for photo-disintegration of a deuteron. We extract the Levinger constant based on the ratio of momentum distributions at high relative momentum. We compute momentum distributions evolved under similarity RG (SRG) transformations, where the SRC physics is shifted into the operator as a universal two-body term. The short-range nature of this operator motivates using local-density approximations with uncorrelated wave functions in evaluating nuclear matrix elements, which greatly simplifies the analysis. The operator must be consistently matched to the RG scale and scheme of the interaction for a reliable extraction. We apply SRG transformations to different nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and use the deuteron wave functions and Weinberg eigenvalues to determine approximate matching scales. We predict the Levinger constant for several NN interactions and a wide range of nuclei comparing to experimental extractions. The predictions at low RG resolution are in good agreement with experiment when starting with a hard NN interaction and the initial operator. Similar agreement is found using soft NN interactions when the additional two-body operator induced by evolution from hard to soft is included.

## Key claim

The Levinger constant, which quantifies the ratio of nuclear photo-absorption to deuteron photo-disintegration, can be extracted at low renormalization group (RG) resolution from the ratio of momentum distributions at high relative momentum, provided the reaction operator is consistently matched to the RG scale and scheme of the interaction.

## Method

The authors applied similarity RG (SRG) transformations to evolve momentum distribution operators from high to low RG resolution, truncating at the two-body level. They used a local-density approximation (LDA) with Gogny functional densities to evaluate nuclear matrix elements for uncorrelated wave functions, and extracted the Levinger constant by averaging the ratio of the proton-neutron pair momentum distribution in a nucleus to the proton momentum distribution in the deuteron over a high-momentum interval. To match soft interactions to hard ones, they applied inverse-SRG transformations to the initial operator and determined matching scales by comparing deuteron wave functions and Weinberg eigenvalues at zero energy.

## Key numbers

- The representative low-resolution RG scale used for nuclear ground states is \(\lambda = 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The Levinger constant was averaged over momentum \(q\) from 4 to 5 fm\(^{-1}\) following Weiss (2015), and alternatively from 2.5 to 3.4 fm\(^{-1}\) where factorization holds strongly.
- For the AV18 potential, the extracted Levinger constant values for several nuclei agree with the experimental average of \(L = 5.5\) from Weiss (2014).
- The largest repulsive Weinberg eigenvalue at zero energy for unevolved AV18 is \(-3.06\), and for unevolved SMS N\(^3\)LO 550 MeV it is \(-1.22\); the eigenvalue for unevolved SMS N\(^3\)LO 450 MeV is \(-0.70\).
- Matching scales between hard and soft potentials were found at \(\lambda_m \approx 4\) to 4.5 fm\(^{-1}\) from both deuteron wave function and Weinberg eigenvalue comparisons.

## Context

This work extends the low-RG-resolution framework developed by Tropiano (2021) for short-range correlation physics to the quasi-deuteron model originally introduced by Levinger (1951). It provides an alternative to the generalized contact formalism of Weiss (2014, 2015), where factorization of reaction and structure at low RG resolution replaces factorization of the many-body wave function. The results enable systematic improvements through inclusion of higher-order terms in the density matrix expansion and many-body calculations with SRG-evolved operators.
