# Nonlinearities of the Vlasov equation in the spinodal region

> We estimate the importance of the nonlinear terms in the Vlasov equation for the development of the unstable modes. The results allow to identify the region of wavelength where the linear evolution is justified.

## Key claim

The linear approximation of the Vlasov equation for spinodal decomposition in nuclear matter is justified only for the most unstable modes (wave-vectors around 0.7 fm⁻¹), while long-wavelength modes (below 0.35 fm⁻¹) are always nonlinear before the instability time; the paper provides the first analytic estimate of these nonlinear effects in three dimensions.

## Method

The author expands the Vlasov equation to second order in the deviation from the homogeneous solution, Fourier transforms in space, and inserts the linearized unstable-mode solutions into the mode-mode coupling terms. By taking a one-sided Fourier transform in time and evaluating the inverse transform via contour integration, the amplitude of the nonlinearly driven density modes is obtained analytically for the cases of one unstable mode (wave-vector doubling) and two unstable modes with opposite wave-vectors (intermediate wave-vector generation).

## Key numbers

- The wave-vector doubling mechanism is important only for wave-vectors \(k < 0.35\) fm⁻¹, a value determined mainly by the range of the interaction.
- For the most unstable modes near \(k \sim 0.7\) fm⁻¹, the nonlinear effects at double wave-vector are small; the amplitude of linear modes with \(k > 0.4\) fm⁻¹ reaches \(|\delta A_k \exp(\Gamma_k T)| \simeq \rho\) before the nonlinearly driven mode at \(2k_0\) becomes important.
- For two unstable modes, a small excitation of a rapidly growing mode at \(\sim 0.7\) fm⁻¹ together with a strong excitation of the mode at \(-0.3\) fm⁻¹ leads to strong nonlinear effects at wave-vectors \(\sim 0.4\) fm⁻¹.
- In the long-wavelength limit, the growth time for the nonlinear mode at \(2k_0\) is smaller than the growth time for the linear mode at \(k_0\) (with initial perturbation \(\delta A = 1/50\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of the two-dimensional Vlasov equation for spinodal decomposition in nuclear matter (Chomaz et al., 1991; Colonna et al., 1994) by providing the first analytic estimate of nonlinear mode-mode coupling in three dimensions. The results explain why linear evolution is valid for the most unstable modes but fails for long wavelengths, and they show that replacing noise terms with stronger initial perturbations (as done by Ohta et al., 1982) gives different results due to nonlinearities. The analysis enables better understanding of fragment size distributions in nuclear fragmentation experiments and clarifies the regime where linearized Vlasov dynamics can be trusted.
