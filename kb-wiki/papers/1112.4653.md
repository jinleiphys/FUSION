# Relativistic oscillator model with spin for nucleon resonances

> The relativistic three-body problem is approached via the extension of the SL(2,C) group to the Sp(4,C) one. In terms of Sp(4,C) spinors, a Dirac-like equation with three-body kinematics is composed. After introducing the linear in coordinates interaction, it describes the spin-1/2 oscillator. For this system, the exact energy spectrum is derived and then applied to fit the Regge trajectories of baryon N-resonances in the (E^2,J) plane. The model predicts linear trajectories at high total energy E with some form of nonlinearity at low E.

# Relativistic oscillator model with spin for nucleon resonances

## Key claim
The paper claims that a relativistic three-body oscillator model based on an Sp(4,C) extension of SL(2,C) can reproduce the Regge trajectories of nucleon N-resonances in the (E^2, J) plane, but only for states with spin 1/2 and 3/2 below 2100 MeV; the model explicitly fails to accommodate the Roper resonance N(1440), N(1675), and N(2250), and omits Delta-resonances entirely because the Dirac bispinor representation used cannot describe spin 3/2 ground states.

## Method
The authors extend the SL(2,C) group to Sp(4,C) to construct a Dirac-like equation for three-body kinematics, then introduce a linear-in-coordinates oscillator interaction (generalized momenta) that preserves exact solvability. They derive the energy spectrum by taking the square of the Hamiltonian and diagonalizing, obtaining dispersion relations that yield Regge trajectories parameterized by radial quantum number n, rotational quantum number N, total angular momentum J, and parity P. The model is applied to fit nucleon resonances by fixing the mass parameter m = 0.940 GeV from the nucleon mass and adjusting the slope parameter lambda = 0.345 GeV^2 to match the nucleon trajectory including N(1680) and N(2220).

## Key numbers
- Mass parameter m = 0.940 GeV, determined by the nucleon N(940) ground state with J^P = 1/2^+
- Slope parameter lambda = 0.345 GeV^2, chosen to fit the nucleon Regge trajectory containing N(1680) and N(2220)
- Ground state energy E = m for N = 0, J^P = 1/2^+ (the nucleon)
- Fitted states on the n=0, N=0, P = (-1)^(J+N-1/2) trajectory: N(940), N(1520) with J^P = 3/2^-, N(1680) with 5/2^+, N(2190) with 7/2^-, N(2220) with 9/2^+
- States on n=0, N=1, same parity: N(1535) with 1/2^-, N(1720) with 3/2^+
- States on n=0, N=0, opposite parity P = (-1)^(J+N+1/2): N(1650) with 1/2^-, N(1900) with 3/2^+, N(2200) with 5/2^-
- States on n=0, N=2: N(1710) with 1/2^+, N(2080) with 3/2^-
- State on n=0, N=3: N(2090) with 1/2^-
- State on n=1, N=0: N(2100) with 1/2^+
- States that drop out of the systematics: Roper resonance N(1440) with 1/2^+, N(1675) with 5/2^-, N(2250) with 9/2^-

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous two-body oscillator model with spin (Kulikov, Uvarov, Yaroshenko, earlier work) to the three-body case, introducing a new quantum number N for rotational excitations that has no analog in the two-body quark-diquark model. The approach contrasts with the Feynman-Kislinger-Ravndal (FKR) model (1971), which uses a mass-squared operator with six oscillator degrees of freedom and high degeneracy, and with the interacting boson model (Toki, 1980s) and stringlike collective model (Bijker, 1990s) that remove degeneracy via algebraic methods. The model also differs from the Lorentz-group approach of Rarita-Schwinger type (O4 model, 2000s) that classifies baryons by total J and parity using Casimir operators. The results enable a systematic classification of nucleon resonances below 2100 MeV into trajectories labeled by n, N, and parity, but leave open the problem of the Roper resonance and parity-doubling phenomena that may require incorporating pseudoscalar diquark configurations or mixing effects (Nagata, 2000s).
