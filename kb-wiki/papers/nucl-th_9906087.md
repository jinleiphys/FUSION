# $ρ$ meson broadening and dilepton production in heavy ion collisions

> The modification of the width of the rho meson due to in-medium decays and collisions is evaluated. In high temperature and/or high density hadronic matter, the collision width is much larger than the one-loop decay width. The large width of the $ρ$ meson in matter seems to be consistent with some current interpretations of the $e^+ e^-$ mass spectra measured at the CERN/SPS.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large collision-broadened width of the rho meson in hot and dense hadronic matter, which is much larger than its one-loop decay width, is consistent with some current interpretations of the CERN/SPS dilepton data, but the authors do not claim that this mechanism alone resolves the discrepancy with experiment.

## Method

The authors compute dielectron emission from the three-body process π ρ → π e⁺ e⁻ in a thermal medium of pions and rho mesons, including π, ρ, ω, and a₁ mesons as intermediate states. They calculate the rho meson collision rate from ρπ and ρN scattering using effective Lagrangians and the Manley resonances method, then incorporate this temperature-dependent collision width into the dilepton production rates. The quantum interference effects from coherently adding amplitudes with the same initial and final configurations are explicitly included.

## Context

This work extends earlier two-body dilepton production calculations by Gale and collaborators (1990s) that showed significant disagreement with CERES experimental data from Drees (1998). It contrasts with two existing approaches that successfully fit the CERES data: one invoking a dropping rho meson mass as a precursor to chiral phase transition (Li, Ko, and Brown, 1990s) and another invoking rho meson widening from coupling to baryon resonances (Rapp, 1990s). The paper enables systematic study of whether these mechanisms coexist and provides a more complete calculation of the π ρ → π e⁺ e⁻ process with a consistent treatment of rho collisional broadening.
