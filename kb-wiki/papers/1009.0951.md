# Breakdown of universality in few-boson systems

> We develop a series of resonant short-range two-boson potentials reproducing the same two-body low-energy observables and apply them in three- and four-body calculations. We demonstrate that the universal behavior predicted by effective field theory may be strongly violated and analyze the conditions for this phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that universal behavior predicted by effective field theory (EFT) for few-boson systems may be strongly violated when the underlying two-body potential has a complicated structure with multiple attractive regions, even when all two-body low-energy observables are held fixed. The breakdown occurs when the size of the few-body system becomes smaller than the range of the interaction, violating the Efimov condition \(r_0 \ll R\).

## Method

The authors constructed a series of rank-2 separable short-range two-boson potentials with Gaussian form factors, fitted to reproduce the same two-body binding energy \(B_2 = 1.318\) mK, scattering length \(a_0 = 104.0\) Å, and scattering phase shifts up to about \(50 B_2\) center-of-mass energy. They solved the three- and four-body bound state and scattering equations using both configuration-space Faddeev/Faddeev-Yakubovsky integro-differential equations and momentum-space Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations, with both methods agreeing to well below 0.1%.

## Key numbers

- Two-body scattering length: \(a_0 = 104.0\) Å
- Dimer binding energy: \(B_2 = 1.318\) mK
- Effective range: \(r_0 = 15.0\) Å (about two times larger than realistic \(^4\)He potentials)
- Boson mass parameter: \(\hbar^2/m = 12.12\) K·Å\(^2\)
- Cutoff parameter range: \(\Lambda\) between 0.133 and 0.305 Å\(^{-1}\), corresponding to \(13.8 < a_0\Lambda < 31.7\)
- At \(a_0\Lambda = 26.75\), a second excited tetramer state appears with binding energy \(B_4^{(2)}\) only slightly larger than \(B_3^{(0)}\)
- At \(a_0\Lambda = 28.12\), the second excited trimer state appears; its binding energy at appearance is about \(5.6 B_2\)
- The second excited tetramer state exists only in the range \(26.75 < a_0\Lambda < 28.14\)
- At \(a_0\Lambda = 27.0\), the tetramer ground state radius \(R_4^{(0)} \approx r_0/5\), much smaller than the interaction range

## Context

This work extends the study of few-boson universality initiated by Efimov (1970, 1979) and later developed in EFT frameworks by Platter et al. and Stecher et al., who established universal relations between three- and four-boson observables using contact interactions. The results contradict the finding of Platter et al. and Stecher et al. that no four-body parameter is needed, showing instead that a four-body parameter may be required when the system size becomes smaller than the interaction range. The work is complementary to Delfino et al. (1992), who found a strong collapse of the trimer for rank-1 separable potentials with two-term form factors.
