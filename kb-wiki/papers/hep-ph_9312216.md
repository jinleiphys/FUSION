# Weak $\Lam N\to NN$ Transition in the Direct Quark Mechanism

> The weak $\Lam N\to NN$ transition is studied in the valence quark model approach. The momentum transfer for this transition is so large that the short-distance two baryon dynamics must be taken into account. The two baryon system is described in the quark cluster model and the weak transition amplitude is calculated by evaluating the matrix elements of the effective weak $ΔS= 1$ hamiltonian. The results indicate some qualitative differences when compared with those in conventional meson-exchange calculations. Especially, we conclude that contributions of the $ΔI={3\over2}$ transition are significant and that the discrepancy in the $n-p$ ratio between theory and experiment could be resolved by including the direct-quark processes.

## Key claim

The direct quark mechanism for the weak $\Lambda N \to NN$ transition, computed using a valence quark model with a quark cluster description of the two-baryon system, yields qualitative differences from conventional meson-exchange calculations; in particular, the $\Delta I = 3/2$ component of the weak Hamiltonian contributes significantly, and including direct-quark processes could resolve the long-standing discrepancy between the theoretical and experimental $n$-$p$ ratio in hypernuclear nonmesonic decay.

## Method

The authors employed the quark cluster model to describe the six-quark wave functions of the initial $\Lambda N$ and final $NN$ systems, using harmonic oscillator orbitals with a Gaussian parameter $b = 0.5$ fm for the internal baryon structure and a Gaussian relative wave function for the initial state. The weak transition amplitude was computed by evaluating matrix elements of the effective $\Delta S = 1$ four-quark Hamiltonian (including both $\Delta I = 1/2$ and $\Delta I = 3/2$ operators) between these quark-model wave functions, using a nonrelativistic Breit-Fermi expansion up to order $p/m$ and incorporating SU(3) breaking via the quark mass ratio $m_u/m_s = 0.6$.

## Key numbers

* Final relative momentum of the $NN$ system: $k = 416$ MeV/c, determined by the $Q$ value $\Delta E \equiv M_\Lambda - M_N$ of the decay.
* Two choices of the renormalization scale for the effective Hamiltonian: $\mu = 0.24$ GeV (with $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^{(4)} = 0.10$ GeV) and $\mu = 0.71$ GeV (with $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^{(4)} = 0.316$ GeV), yielding coefficients $K_1$ through $K_6$ that differ by at most 10% in the transition matrix elements.
* Light quark constituent mass: $m_q = m_u = 313$ MeV; mass ratio $m_u/m_s = 0.6$.
* Gaussian parameter for the initial $\Lambda N$ relative wave function: $B = 1.838$ fm (corresponding to $\sqrt{2} \times 1.3$ fm) for a $\Lambda N$ system in $^4$He, or $B = \sqrt{2/3}\,b$ for the $(0s)^6$ dibaryon configuration.

## Context

This work extends the valence quark model approach to the nonmesonic weak decay $\Lambda N \to NN$, contrasting with the traditional meson-exchange mechanism (involving $\pi$, $K$, $\rho$ exchange) which predicts a very small $n$-$p$ ratio $R_{np} \sim 0.1$–$0.4$ due to tensor-force dominance. The authors note that experimental data from light hypernuclei give $R_{np}^{\rm exp} \sim 1$, and that an analysis of $A=3$ and $4$ hypernuclei by Shoe (year not given in text) suggests possible violation of the $\Delta I = 1/2$ rule. The effective weak Hamiltonian used follows the renormalization-group analysis of Paschos (year not given in text) and earlier work by Vainshtein, Zakharov, and Shifman (year not given in text); the quark cluster model wave functions follow the approach of Oka and Yazaki (1980) and others. The results enable future calculations using realistic two-baryon wave functions rather than the simplified Gaussian and plane-wave approximations employed here.
