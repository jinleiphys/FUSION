# Skewed recoil polarization in (e,e'p) reactions from polarized nuclei

> The general formalism describing $\vec{A}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})B$ reactions, involving polarization of the electron beam, target and ejected proton, is presented within the framework of the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation for medium and heavy nuclei. It is shown that the simultaneous measurement of the target and ejected proton polarization can provide new information which is not contained in the separate analysis of the $\vec{A}(\vec{e},e'p)B$ and $A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})B$ reactions. The polarization transfer mechanism in which the electron interacts with the initial nucleon carrying the target polarization, making the proton exit with a fractional polarization in a different direction, is referred to here as ``skewed polarization''. The new observables characterizing the process are identified, and written in terms of polarized response functions and asymmetries which are of tensor nature. The corresponding half-off-shell single-nucleon responses are analyzed using different prescriptions for the electromagnetic vertex and for different kinematics. Numerical predictions are presented for selected perpendicular and parallel kinematics in the case of $^{39}$K as polarized target.

## Key claim

The paper shows that simultaneously measuring target and ejected proton polarization in \((\vec{e},e'\vec{p})\) reactions reveals new "skewed polarization" observables that are absent when only one polarization is measured, but the analysis is restricted to the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and the authors note that final state interactions (FSI) may affect these observables, though they expect the effect to be small for low missing momentum.

## Method

The authors develop the general formalism for \(\vec{A}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})B\) reactions within the relativistic PWIA, extending previous work on \(\vec{A}(\vec{e},e'p)\) and \(A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})\) processes. They compute the single-nucleon tensor using trace techniques with two standard prescriptions (CC1 and CC2) for the off-shell \(\gamma NN\) vertex, then construct the hadronic response functions by combining the single-nucleon responses with the polarized momentum distribution of the target nucleus. Numerical predictions are presented for the case of \(^{39}\)K, described as a proton hole in the \(d_{3/2}\) shell of \(^{40}\)Ca, for selected perpendicular and parallel kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The formalism is applied to \(^{39}\)K as a polarized target, described in shell model as a proton hole in the \(d_{3/2}\) shell of \(^{40}\)Ca.
- The cross section in PWIA factorizes as \(\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega_e d\varepsilon' d\Omega_N} = K f_{rec}^{-1} \sum_{mm'} \sigma^{ep}_{mm'}(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{q};S_N) M_{mm'}(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{\Omega}^*)\), where \(K = p_N M_N M_B / M_A\).
- The polarized single-nucleon responses are expressed in terms of scalar \(\overline{\mathcal{R}}^\alpha\) and vector \(\widehat{\mathcal{R}}^\alpha(S_N)\) components, with the vector components given by the three directions \(x, y, z\) relative to the momentum transfer \(\mathbf{q}\).
- The "skewed polarization" asymmetries are defined as \(A_{\mathbf{s}_N} = [\Sigma(\mathbf{s}_N) - \Sigma(-\mathbf{s}_N)] / [\Sigma(\mathbf{s}_N) + \Sigma(-\mathbf{s}_N)]\), where the dependence on the ejected nucleon spin is linear.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Caballero et al. (1993, 1994) for \(\vec{A}(\vec{e},e'p)\) reactions and of C. Giusti et al. (2002) for \(A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})\) reactions to the case where both target and ejected proton polarizations are measured simultaneously. It fills a gap in the literature for medium and heavy nuclei, as previous studies of triple-polarized reactions were limited to light nuclei, notably the systematic work on deuterium by Arenhövel et al. (1992, 1995, 1998, 2000, 2002, 2004). The paper enables future experimental investigations of these new observables, which could provide information on nuclear structure and reaction mechanisms not accessible through separate measurements of target or recoil polarization alone.
