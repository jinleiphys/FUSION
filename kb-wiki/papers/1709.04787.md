# Towards an empirical unified crust-core description of neutron stars

> Understanding the properties of the crust and the core as well as its interface is essential for accurate astrophysical modeling of phenomena such as glitches, X-ray bursts or oscillations in neutron stars. To study the crust-core properties, it is crucial to develop a unified and consistent scheme to describe both the clusterized matter in the crust and homogeneous matter in the core. The low density regime in the neutron star crust is accessible to terrestrial nuclear experiments. In order to develop a consistent description of the crust and the core of neutron stars within the same formalism, we use a density functional scheme, with the model coefficients in homogeneous matter related directly to empirical nuclear observables. In this work, we extend this scheme to non-homogeneous matter to describe nuclei in the crust. We then test this scheme against nuclear observables.

# Towards an empirical unified crust-core description of neutron stars

## Key claim

The authors develop a unified density functional scheme for both the neutron star crust and core, where the model coefficients in homogeneous matter are directly related to empirical nuclear observables, and they show that for non-homogeneous matter only a single additional effective parameter (the finite-size coefficient $C_{fin}$) is needed to reproduce experimental nuclear masses and charge radii, provided the formalism is restricted to subsaturation densities where the series expansion converges at second order.

## Method

The authors employ a nuclear energy density functional (EDF) in the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation, using an analytical mass formula derived from the ETF approach. The homogeneous matter energy per particle is expanded as a Taylor series in the density parameter $x = (\rho - \rho_{sat})/3\rho_{sat}$ up to second order, with coefficients given by empirical nuclear matter parameters (saturation density, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and its derivatives). For finite nuclei, the energy functional includes local and non-local surface terms; the non-local part depends on a single finite-size parameter $C_{fin}$, which is constrained by comparing calculated surface energy coefficients and nuclear masses against experimental data from the AME2012 mass table and charge radius measurements.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: $\rho_{sat} = 0.1540 \pm 0.0051$ fm$^{-3}$ (average and standard deviation from a compilation of effective models)
- Energy per particle at saturation: $E_{sat} = -16.04 \pm 0.20$ MeV
- Isoscalar incompressibility: $K_{sat} = 255.91 \pm 34.39$ MeV
- Symmetry energy: $E_{sym} = 33.43 \pm 2.64$ MeV
- Symmetry energy slope: $L_{sym} = 77.92 \pm 30.84$ MeV
- Symmetry energy curvature: $K_{sym} = -2.19 \pm 142.71$ MeV
- Effective mass ratio: $m^*/m = 0.7 \pm 0.15$
- Finite-size parameter from surface energy coefficient systematics: $C_{fin} \approx 77.5 \pm 12.5$ MeV fm$^5$
- Finite-size parameter from fit to nuclear masses (AME2012): $C_{fin} = 61$ MeV fm$^5$ (with $C_{so}=40$) or $C_{fin}=59$ MeV fm$^5$ (with $C_{so}=0$), with an uncertainty of approximately $\pm 13$ MeV fm$^5$
- Neutron drip density: $\rho \simeq 4 \times 10^{11}$ g cm$^{-3}$
- Non-unified models produce uncertainty in crust thickness of up to 30% and up to 4% for the radius estimation (citing Fortin)

## Context

This work extends the empirical equation of state approach developed by Casali et al. (2014) for homogeneous nuclear matter to non-homogeneous matter in the crust, using the analytical ETF mass formula framework of Francois et al. (2015). It addresses the problem that non-unified models (e.g., those using different formalisms for crust and core) produce arbitrary results at the crust-core boundary, as demonstrated by Fortin et al. (2016) and Khan and Margueron (2013). The scheme enables systematic investigation of how uncertainties in empirical nuclear matter parameters propagate to predictions of neutron star observables such as charge radii, neutron skin thickness, and crust-core interface properties, and is applied in a companion paper (Chatterjee et al., 2017) to scan the full parameter space under the constraint of nuclear mass reproduction.
