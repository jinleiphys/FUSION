# Holographic thermalization with initial long range correlation

> We studied the evolution of Wightman correlator in a thermalizing state modeled by AdS_3-Vaidya background. We gave a prescription for calculating Wightman correlator in coordinate space without using any approximation. For equal-time correlator $\langle O(v,x)O(v,0)\rangle$, we obtained an enhancement factor $v^2$ due to long range correlation present in the initial state. This was missed by previous studies based on geodesic approximation. We found that the long range correlation in initial state does not lead to significant modification to thermalization time as compared to known results with generic initial state. We also studied spatially integrated Wightman correlator and showed evidence on the distinction between long distance and small momentum physics for an out-of-equilibrium state. We also calculated radiation spectrum of particle weakly coupled to $O$ and found lower frequency mode approaches thermal spectrum faster than high frequency mode.

# Holographic thermalization with initial long range correlation

## Key claim

The paper claims that while the presence of long-range correlations in the initial state modifies the equal-time Wightman correlator by an enhancement factor $v^2$ missed by previous geodesic approximation studies, this does not lead to a significant modification of the thermalization time compared to known results with generic initial states. The thermalization time remains $x + O(1/T)$ for spacelike separated points and $O(1/T)$ for temporally separated points, free from logarithmic corrections.

## Method

The author studies thermalization in a 1+1D conformal field theory modeled by the AdS$_3$-Vaidya background, which describes a lightlike shell collapsing from the boundary at $v=0$, separating empty AdS (dual to vacuum) from BTZ (dual to thermal state). The Wightman correlator for a dimension-2 scalar operator $O$ is computed in coordinate space without approximation by formulating the calculation as an initial value problem on the lightlike hypersurface $v=0$, using the difference between bulk correlators in AdS and BTZ to eliminate singularities, and propagating the initial data using retarded bulk-bulk propagators in BTZ.

## Key numbers

- The equal-time correlator $\langle O(v,x)O(v,0)\rangle$ in the regime $x \gg v \gg 1$ has a power law factor $v^2/x^4$, with the analytic form $\frac{18}{\pi x^4}\left(\frac{v\coth v - 1}{\sinh^2 v}\right)^2$ for the difference from the thermal correlator.
- For $x \ll 1$ and $v \ll 1$, the equal-time correlator difference scales as $\frac{1}{x^2}\mathcal{G}(v/x)$, with the tail numerically consistent with $\mathcal{G}(v/x) \sim (x/v)^4$.
- For spacelike correlator with $x_{43} \gg 1$ and $v_3, v_4 \gg 1$, the result is $\langle O(v_4,x_4)O(v_3,x_3)\rangle \simeq \frac{18}{\pi x_{43}^4} v_3 v_4 e^{-2v_3-2v_4}$, compared to the geodesic approximation result $\sim \frac{1}{x_{43}^4} e^{-2v_3-2v_4}$.
- For the spatially integrated (zero momentum) correlator at early times $v_3, v_4 \ll 1$, the result is $\int dx_{43} \Delta\langle O(v_4,x_4)O(v_3,x_3)\rangle \simeq \frac{3\pi}{128\sqrt{v_3 v_4}}$.
- The thermalization time for spacelike correlator is $x_{43} + O(1)$, confirmed by analytic calculation near the lightcone where $x_{43} - v_4 \equiv \delta \sim O(1) \gg v_3$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic thermalization program using two-point functions, building on the rigorous momentum-space calculation of the Wightman correlator by Keränen and Kleinert (2014). It provides coordinate-space results that allow direct comparison to the general CFT results of Calabrese and Cardy (2006), which assume finite initial correlation length. The author finds that while the initial long-range correlation (power law $1/x^4$ in vacuum) modifies the correlator with an enhancement factor $v^2$ missed by the geodesic approximation of Aparicio and Lopez (2011), the thermalization time remains unchanged. The work also demonstrates a counterexample to the equivalence of low momentum and long distance physics in out-of-equilibrium states, and shows that high frequency modes thermalize slower than low frequency modes in the radiation spectrum of a particle weakly coupled to $O$.
