# Comparative study of the scalar- and tensor-meson production in the reaction $γγ^*(Q^2)\to ηπ^0$

> The prediction of the cross section $σ(γγ^*(Q^2)\to ηπ^0)$ based on the simultaneous description of the Belle data on the $γγ\to ηπ^0$ reaction and the KLOE data on the $ϕ\toηπ^0γ$ decay is presented. The production of the scalar $a_0(980)$ and tensor $a_2(1320)$ is studied in detail. It is shown that the QCD based asymptotics of the $γ^*(Q^2)γ\to a_2(1320)\toηπ^0$ cross section can be reached by the compensation of the contributions of $ρ(770)$ and $ω(782)$ with the contributions of their radial excitations in $Q^2$ channel. At large $Q^2$ the $a_2(1320)$ contribution is expected to be dominant, while at $Q^2=0$ it is similar to the scalars contribution.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the cross section for the reaction $\gamma\gamma^*(Q^2)\to \eta\pi^0$ by simultaneously fitting Belle data on $\gamma\gamma\to \eta\pi^0$ and KLOE data on $\phi\to\eta\pi^0\gamma$, showing that the QCD-based asymptotics of the $a_2(1320)$ production cross section at large $Q^2$ can only be reached through cancellation between the contributions of $\rho(770)$ and $\omega(782)$ and their radial excitations in the $Q^2$ channel.

## Method

The authors construct helicity amplitudes for $\gamma^*\gamma\to\eta\pi^0$ including Born terms from $\rho$, $\omega$, $K^*$, and $K$ exchanges; kaon loop contributions $\gamma^*\gamma\to K^+K^-\to a_0\to\eta\pi^0$; direct couplings of $a_0(980)$ and $a_0'$ to photons; and tensor $a_2(1320)$ production via Vector Dominance Model. They perform a simultaneous fit to Belle data on $\gamma\gamma\to\eta\pi^0$ (36 points) and KLOE data on the $\phi\to\eta\pi^0\gamma$ mass spectrum (24 points), using a model with scalar resonances $a_0(980)$ and $a_0'$ plus the tensor $a_2(1320)$, and then extrapolate to non-zero $Q^2$ using form factors that incorporate vector meson radial excitations.

## Key numbers

- $m_{a_0}=993.9$ MeV (Fit 1), with $g_{a_0K^+K^-}=2.75$ GeV and $g_{a_0\eta\pi}=2.74$ GeV
- $\Gamma_{a_0}(m_{a_0})=116.8$ MeV (Fit 1), with effective width $\Gamma^{eff}_{a_0}=34.6$ MeV
- $\langle\Gamma_{a_0\to\gamma\gamma}\rangle_{\eta\pi^0}=0.23$ keV (Fit 1) for the total averaged $a_0\to\gamma\gamma$ width via rescatterings and direct coupling
- $\Gamma^{(0)}_{a_0\to\gamma\gamma}=0.063$ keV (Fit 1) for the direct $a_0\to\gamma\gamma$ width at $m_{a_0}$
- $m_{a_2}=1318.3$ MeV, $\Gamma_{a_2}^{tot}=105$ MeV, $Br(a_2\to\eta\pi^0)=0.145$, $Br(a_2\to\gamma\gamma)=9.4\times 10^{-6}$
- $\chi^2_{\gamma\gamma}=12.4$ for 36 Belle points and $\chi^2_{sp}=24.5$ for 24 KLOE points, combined $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.}=36.9/46$ (Fit 1)
- At $Q^2=0$, the $a_2(1320)$ contribution is similar to the scalar ($a_0(980)+a_0'$) contribution; at large $Q^2$ the $a_2$ contribution is expected to be dominant

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Achasov et al. (2010, 2011) on $\gamma\gamma\to\eta\pi^0$ by performing a simultaneous fit with KLOE $\phi\to\eta\pi^0\gamma$ data and predicting the $Q^2$ dependence. It supports the four-quark interpretation of $a_0(980)$ by showing that the kaon loop model (without an additional kaon form factor) describes both datasets well, with coupling constants close to the four-quark model predictions of Achasov (1989). The prediction for $\sigma(\gamma\gamma^*(Q^2)\to\eta\pi^0)$ enables future experimental checks at facilities with virtual photons, and the demonstration that radial excitations of $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\phi$ are needed to reach QCD asymptotics for $a_2$ production provides a testable mechanism for tensor meson production.
