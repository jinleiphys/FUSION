# Antihyperon potentials in nuclei via exclusive antiproton-nucleus reactions at FAIR

> The exclusive production of hyperon-antihyperon pairs close to their production threshold in antiproton - nucleus collisions offers a unique and hitherto unexplored opportunity to elucidate the behaviour of antihyperons in nuclei. For the first time we analyse these reactions in a microscopic transport model using the the Gießen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model. The calculation take the delicate interplay between the strong absorption of antihyperons, their rescattering and refraction at the nuclear surface as well as the Fermi motion of the struck nucleon into account. We find a substantial sensitivity of transverse momentum correlations of coincident $Λ\overlineΛ$-pairs to the assumed depth of the $\overlineΛ$-potential. Because of the high cross section for this process and the simplicity of the experimental method our results are highly relevant for future activities at the international Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR).

## Key claim

The transverse momentum asymmetry of coincident Lambda-antilambda pairs produced in antiproton-nucleus collisions near threshold retains substantial sensitivity to the depth of the antilambda potential even when realistic rescattering, refraction, and absorption effects are included in a microscopic transport model, contrary to concerns that secondary effects might wipe out this dependence.

## Method

The authors used the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model (Release 1.5) to simulate exclusive antiproton + neon-20 reactions at beam energies of 0.85 GeV and 1.0 GeV, producing coincident Lambda-antilambda pairs. They varied only the antihyperon potentials by a single scaling factor xi_Lambda applied to the default G-parity-based antilambda potential of -449 MeV, while keeping all other input parameters fixed, and analyzed the resulting transverse momentum asymmetry as a function of longitudinal momentum asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The default antilambda potential from G-parity symmetry is U(antilambda) = -449 MeV.
- At 0.85 GeV beam energy, the excess energy above the elementary antiproton + proton -> antilambda + Lambda threshold is 30.6 MeV.
- The impact parameter distributions for free antilambda-Lambda pair production peak around 3.8 fm, significantly larger than the neon-20 rms-charge radius of 3.0 fm.
- For scaling factors xi_Lambda between 1.0 and 0.5, the number of produced antilambda-Lambda pairs remains fairly constant at about 8700 events; for xi_Lambda = 0, the number decreases by about 15% to approximately 7500 events.
- At maximum interaction rate for a medium-size target like neon-20, approximately 10 reconstructed antilambda-Lambda pairs per second are expected, and a measurement period of about one hour would provide statistics exceeding the GiBUU simulations shown, sufficient to reach a precision of about 10% for the scaling factor xi_Lambda.

## Context

This work extends schematic calculations by Pochodzalla (2008, 2009) that demonstrated sensitivity of transverse momentum correlations to antihyperon potentials but ignored rescattering and refractive effects. The present realistic transport calculations using GiBUU, which reproduces antiproton-nucleus data in the FAIR energy range as shown by Larionov (2009, 2012), confirm that secondary scattering does not destroy the sensitivity. The results are directly relevant for the PANDA experiment at the international Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR), where high-intensity antiproton beams will enable measurements of hyperon-antihyperon pair production with sufficient statistics to constrain the antilambda potential.
