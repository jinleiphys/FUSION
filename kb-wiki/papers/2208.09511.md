# Application of the cranking method to the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model and nuclear molecules

> A particular quantum phase transition (QPT) is studied at excited energies of light nuclei within the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM), using a combination of catastrophe theory and a direct minimization of the potential. A distinct change from a compact nucleus to a nuclear molecule is described as a second order QPT occurring at a certain value of angular momentum. This finding is in accordance with experimental observations when nuclear molecules appear. The method has implication in the fission of heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a second-order quantum phase transition from a compact nucleus to a nuclear molecule occurs at a specific angular momentum within the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model, but the cranking formalism used cannot reproduce the correct rotation axis after the transition, leading to a band with too low moment of inertia and too high lying states.

## Method

The authors apply the cranking method by adding a term \(-\Omega L_x\) to the SACM Hamiltonian and construct a semiclassical potential using coherent states in the variables \((\alpha,\theta,\phi)\), where \(\alpha\) relates to inter-cluster distance and \(\theta,\phi\) to orientation. They minimize this potential for fixed Hamiltonian parameters \(\{a,b,\xi\}\) fitted to low-energy experimental data, then increase the cranking frequency \(\Omega\) to trace trajectories in parameter space and identify where separatrices (second-order transition, stability, bifurcation, Maxwell) are crossed, indicating phase transitions.

## Key numbers

* For the \({}^{12}\mathrm{C}+{}^{12}\mathrm{C}\) system, the minimal cluster separation distance is \(r_0 = 2.36\ \mathrm{fm}\) as determined by \(n_0\).
* After the quantum phase transition, the intercluster axis makes an angle \(\theta_c \approx \pi/4\) with respect to the \(z\)-axis; the two equal semi-axes of the oblate clusters are \(2.75\ \mathrm{fm}\) and the radius along the symmetry axis is \(1.58\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* The moment of inertia for small \(\Omega\) is given by \(I_{\pm} = n_0 / [\frac{3}{4}a \langle Q_{C,0}^a \rangle \mp 2(n_0 -1) \xi] + \mathcal{O}(\Omega)\), which for spherical clusters reduces to \(I_{-} \approx 1/(2\xi)\).
* The Hamiltonian parameters \(\hbar\omega\) are set by \(\hbar\omega = 45 A^{-1/3} - 25 A^{-2/3}\) MeV, where \(A\) is the nucleon number.

## Context

This work extends the SACM phase transition studies of Lohr-Robles (2019, 2021) from the \(\Omega=0\) case to finite rotation, using cranking methods previously applied to the interacting boson model by Cejnar (2002, 2003, 2004) for spherical-deformed transitions in heavy nuclei. The results enable future study of fission processes in heavy nuclei, building on the SACM extension to heavy systems by Chavez (2021), and allow comparison with the geometric nuclear molecule model of NM1 and NM2.
