# The Quark-Meson Coupling model as a description of dense matter

> Quantum Hadrodynamics provides a useful framework for investigating dense matter, yet it breaks down easily when strangeness carrying baryons are introduced into the calculations, as the baryon effective masses become negative due to large meson field potentials. The Quark-Meson Coupling model overcomes this issue by incorporating the quark structure of the nucleon, thus allowing for a feedback between the the nuclei and the interaction with the meson fields. With the inclusion of this feature, QMC provides a successful description of finite nuclei and nuclear matter. We present the latest parameterization of QMC and discuss the predictions for dense nuclear matter and `neutron' stars.

## Key claim

The Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model provides a successful description of finite nuclei and nuclear matter by incorporating the quark structure of the nucleon, which allows a feedback between the nucleon and the meson fields and avoids the negative effective masses that arise in Quantum Hadrodynamics when strangeness-carrying baryons are introduced. However, the maximum predicted neutron star mass using either the 2007 or 2008 parameterization does not exceed 1.57 solar masses, which is in conflict with observational evidence of larger mass neutron stars, though the author cautions that the predictions correspond to static, spherically symmetric, non-rotating objects.

## Method

The author calculates the properties of infinite nuclear matter in beta-equilibrium with leptons using the QMC model, with baryon effective masses parameterized as quadratic functions of the scalar mean-field. The coupling constants of baryons to scalar sigma, vector omega, and rho mesons are determined by fitting the properties of saturated nuclear matter. The equation of state is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to predict neutron star masses and radii for the 2007 and 2008 parameterizations of the effective masses.

## Key numbers

- The free-space bag radius used for the nucleon is \(R_{N}^{\rm free}=0.8~{\rm fm}\).
- The maximum predicted stellar mass calculated using either the 2007 or 2008 parameterization of the effective baryon mass does not exceed 1.57 \(M_\odot\).
- The 2008 parameterization includes one-gluon-exchange hyperfine terms that lead to the \(N\)-\(\Delta\) and \(\Sigma\)-\(\Lambda\) mass splittings in free-space, and results in \(\Sigma\) hypernuclei being unbound in the model.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model from earlier parameterizations (QMC2007, QMC2008) to describe dense nuclear matter and neutron stars, and contrasts with Quantum Hadrodynamics (Serot, 1984) which models a linear dependence of the effective mass on the scalar mean-field and leads to negative effective masses at large baryon densities. The results enable future investigations of the effects of rotation on stellar solutions and inclusion of Fock (exchange) terms to baryon self-energies to improve the description of dense nuclear matter.
