# Re-examining the $X(4630)$ resonance in the reaction $e^+e^-\rightarrow 螞^+_c\bar螞^-_c$

> The reaction $e^+e^-\rightarrow 螞^+_c\bar螞^-_c$ is investigated at energies close to the threshold with emphasis on the role played by the $X(4630)$ resonance. The interaction in the final $螞^+_c \bar螞^-_c$ system, constructed within chiral effective field theory and supplemented by a pole diagram that represents a bare $X(4630)$ resonance, is taken into account rigorously. The pole parameters of the $X(4630)$ are extracted and found to be compatible with the ones of the $X(4660)$ resonance that have been established in the reaction $e^+e^- \to 蟺^+蟺^-蠄(2S)$. The actual result for the $X(4630)$ is $M = (4652.5\pm 3.4)$ MeV and $螕= (62.6\pm 5.6)$ MeV. Predictions for the $螞^+_c$ electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region are presented.

## Key claim
The X(4630) resonance observed in the reaction \(e^+e^-\rightarrow \Lambda^+_c\bar\Lambda^-_c\) is compatible with the X(4660) resonance established in \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\psi(2S)\), but only when final-state interactions in the \(\Lambda^+_c\bar\Lambda^-_c\) system are treated rigorously rather than with a simple Breit-Wigner fit. The authors hedge that the dynamical origin of the state is left aside; they focus only on whether the two observed structures could be the same resonance.

## Method
The authors constructed the \(\Lambda^+_c\bar\Lambda^-_c\) interaction using chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order, with contact terms representing short-range forces and a pole diagram for a bare vector-meson resonance with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=1^{--}\). They solved a relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the coupled \(^3S_1\) and \(^3D_1\) partial waves and computed the \(e^+e^-\rightarrow \Lambda^+_c\bar\Lambda^-_c\) cross section via distorted-wave Born approximation, fitting the free parameters to Belle data up to a kinetic center-of-mass energy of 100 MeV in the \(\Lambda^+_c\bar\Lambda^-_c\) system.

## Key numbers
- Extracted pole mass of the X(4630): \(M = (4652.5 \pm 3.4)\) MeV.
- Extracted pole width of the X(4630): \(\Gamma = (62.6 \pm 5.6)\) MeV.
- The \(\Lambda^+_c\bar\Lambda^-_c\) threshold is at \(\sqrt{s} = 4572.92\) MeV, using the \(\Lambda^+_c\) mass \(M_{\Lambda_c} = 2286.46\) MeV.
- Fits were performed for cutoff masses \(\Lambda\) between 0.45 GeV and 0.85 GeV; the systematic variation in the resonance mass was about 2 MeV and about 8 MeV for the width.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of final-state interactions in \(e^+e^- \to p\bar p\) (Haidenbauer 2014) and \(e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda\) (Haidenbauer 2016) to the charmed baryon sector. It contradicts the simple Breit-Wigner interpretation of the Belle data (Belle 2008) and provides a more rigorous extraction than earlier analyses by Bugg (2009), Cotugno et al. (2010), and Guo et al. (2010). The results enable future comparisons with the X(4660) parameters from the \(\pi^+\pi^-\psi(2S)\) channel and provide predictions for the \(\Lambda^+_c\) electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region.
