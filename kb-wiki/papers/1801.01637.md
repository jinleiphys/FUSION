# Unintegrated dipole gluon distribution at small transverse momentum

> We derive analytical results for unintegrated color dipole gluon distribution function at small transverse momentum. By Fourier transforming the $S$-matrix for large dipoles we derive the results in the form of a series of Bells polynomials. Interestingly, when resumming the series in leading log accuracy, the results showing up striking similarity with the Sudakov form factor with role play of coupling is being done by a constant that stems from the saddle point condition along the saturation line.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical expression for the unintegrated color dipole gluon distribution at small transverse momentum, valid deep inside the saturation region where the Levin-Tuchin solution of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation applies, with the caveat that the result is restricted to the momentum range \(Q_s \gtrsim k_\perp \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) and is expressed as a series of Bell polynomials that, when resummed in leading log accuracy, shows a striking similarity to the Sudakov form factor.

## Method

The authors start from the operator definition of the color dipole gluon distribution and express it in terms of the dipole S-matrix. They adopt the Levin-Tuchin solution for the S-matrix, valid for large dipoles deep inside the saturation region, and perform a Fourier transform to transverse momentum space. The calculation proceeds by expanding the exponential of a squared logarithm into a series, using a dummy variable \(\eta\) to convert logarithmic terms into derivatives, performing the angular integration, and then resumming the resulting series of Bell polynomials of zeta functions. The leading log approximation is then applied to obtain a closed-form expression.

## Key numbers

* \(\tau \approx 0.2\): a constant that stems from the saddle point condition along the saturation line, appearing in the exponent of the Levin-Tuchin S-matrix and in the final resummed expression for the unintegrated dipole gluon distribution.
* The critical anomalous dimension \(\gamma_{\rm cr} \sim 0.63\): the exponent governing the power-law fall of the unintegrated dipole gluon distribution just outside the saturation boundary, where \(x G^{DP} \propto (Q_s^2/k_\perp^2)^{\gamma_{\rm cr}}\).
* The final resummed result for the small momentum range is given by Eq. (1): \(x G^{DP}(x,k_\perp) \approx -\frac{S_\perp N_c \tau}{\pi^3 \alpha_s} \ln\left(\frac{k_\perp^2}{4Q_s^2(Y)}\right) \exp\left[-\tau \ln^2\left(\frac{k_\perp^2}{4Q_s^2(Y)}\right)\right]\), valid for \(Q_s \gtrsim k_\perp \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).
* For the region \(k_\perp \gtrsim Q_s\) at low rapidity \(Y \sim 0\), the result using the McLerran-Venugopalan or Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff model is: \(x G^{DP}(x,k_\perp) \approx \frac{S_\perp N_c}{2\pi^3 \alpha_s} \frac{k_\perp^2}{Q_s^2(Y)} \exp\left(-\frac{k_\perp^2}{Q_s^2(Y)}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends analytical studies of unintegrated gluon distributions within the color dipole formalism, building on the Levin-Tuchin solution (Levin and Tuchin, 1999; 2000) of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, which describes the S-matrix deep inside the saturation region. The results complement earlier work using the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993; 1994) and the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff model (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1999) for the S-matrix, which are valid at lower rapidities and for smaller dipoles. The derived expressions enable phenomenological studies of the unintegrated dipole gluon distribution at small transverse momentum, relevant for future electron-ion collider experiments probing gluon saturation.
