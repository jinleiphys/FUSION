# Hartree Fock Calculations in the Density Matrix Expansion Approach

> The density matrix expansion is used to derive a local energy density functional for finite range interactions with a realistic meson exchange structure. Exchange contributions are treated in a local momentum approximation. A generalized Slater approximation is used for the density matrix where an effective local Fermi momentum is chosen such that the next to leading order off-diagonal term is canceled. Hartree-Fock equations are derived incorporating the momentum structure of the underlying finite range interaction. For applications a density dependent effective interaction is determined from a G-matrix which is renormalized such that the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter are reproduced. Intending applications to systems far off stability special attention is paid to the low density regime and asymmetric nuclear matter. Results are compared to predictions obtained from Skyrme interactions. The ground state properties of stable nuclei are well reproduced without further adjustments of parameters. The potential of the approach is further exemplified in calculations for A=100...140 tin isotopes. Rather extended neutron skins are found beyond 130Sn corresponding to solid layers of neutron matter surrounding a core of normal composition.

## Key claim

The density matrix expansion (DME) can be used to derive a local energy density functional for finite-range interactions that reproduces ground state properties of stable nuclei without further parameter adjustments, while also predicting extended neutron skins in neutron-rich tin isotopes beyond \(^{130}\)Sn. The approach uses a generalized Slater approximation where the effective local Fermi momentum is chosen to cancel the next-to-leading order off-diagonal term, which the abstract does not specify as the key technical condition.

## Method

The authors start from the M3Y G-matrix parameterization of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and introduce density-dependent isoscalar and isovector vertex functions fitted to reproduce the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy, density, compressibility) and the symmetry energy. The density matrix expansion is applied to treat exchange contributions in a local momentum approximation, using a modified effective Fermi momentum \(q_F^2(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{5}{3} \frac{\tau(\mathbf{r}) - \frac{1}{4} \nabla^2 \rho(\mathbf{r})}{\rho(\mathbf{r})}\) that cancels the next-to-leading order term in the DME series. Hartree-Fock equations are then derived and solved for finite nuclei, yielding effective masses and rearrangement potentials determined by the momentum and isospin structure of the interaction.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.159\) fm\(^{-3}\)
* Binding energy per particle at saturation: \(a_V \simeq 16\) MeV
* Compressibility at saturation: \(K_\infty = 230\) MeV (fitted value)
* Symmetry energy coefficient at saturation: \(a_s = \varepsilon_\tau(\rho_0) = 32\) MeV
* Tin isotope calculations performed for mass range \(A = 100 \dots 140\)
* Extended neutron skins predicted for tin isotopes beyond \(^{130}\)Sn

## Context

This work extends the density matrix expansion approach originally developed by Negele and Vautherin (1972) for finite-range interactions, applying it to the M3Y interaction derived from the Paris NN-potential by Toki et al. (1980). The results are compared to predictions from Skyrme interactions, specifically the SLy4 parameterization. The approach enables systematic Hartree-Fock calculations for nuclei far off stability, particularly neutron-rich systems, without the numerical complications of integro-differential equations that arise from finite-range exchange terms in methods like the Gogny interaction.
