# Symmetry energies for $A = 24$ and $48$ and the USD and KB3 shell model Hamiltonians

> Calculations in the sd and pf shells reported some time ago by Satuła\etal\ [Phys.~Lett.~B~407, 103 (1997)] are redone for an extended analysis of the results. As in the original work, we do calculations for one mass number in each shell and consider in each case the sequence of lowest energies for isospins 0, 2, and 4, briefly the symmetry spectrum. Following further the original work we study how this spectrum changes when parts of the two-nucleon interaction are turned off. The variation of its width is explored in detail. A differential combination $ε_\text{W}$ of the three energies was taken in the original work as a measure of the so-called Wigner term in semi-empirical mass formulas, and it was found to decrease drastically when the two-nucleon interaction in the channel of zero isospin is turned off. Our analysis shows that the width of the symmetry spectrum experiences an equally drastic decrease, which can be explained qualitatively in terms of schematic approximations. We therefore suggest that the decrease of $ε_\text{W}$ be seen mainly as a side effect of a narrowing of the symmetry spectrum rather than an independent manifestation of the two-nucleon interaction in the channel of zero isospin.

## Key claim

The drastic decrease of the Wigner term measure \(\epsilon_\text{W}\) when the isospin \(T' = 0\) two-nucleon interaction is turned off, previously interpreted as a direct signature of that interaction channel, is instead mainly a side effect of the narrowing of the overall symmetry spectrum (the sequence of lowest energies for isospins \(T = 0, 2, 4\)).

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for \(A = 24\) with the USD Hamiltonian in the \(sd\) shell and for \(A = 48\) with the KB3 Hamiltonian in the \(pf\) shell, using the code nushellX@msu. The lowest energies for isospins \(T = 0, 2, 4\) were computed for the full Hamiltonian and for variants where two-nucleon interaction components with definite isospin \(T'\) and angular momentum \(J\) were successively turned off, first all \(T' = 0\) components (increasing \(J\)) and then all \(T' = 1\) components (decreasing \(J\)). The width of the symmetry spectrum \(E(4) - E(0)\) and the parameter \(\epsilon_\text{W}\) (defined by \(E(T) = E(0) + \epsilon_\text{W} + \frac12 \kappa T^2\) for \(T = 2,4\)) were extracted and compared with schematic monopole and pairing approximations.

## Key numbers

- For \(A = 48\) with KB3, the width of the symmetry spectrum \(E(4) - E(0)\) is 6.1 MeV when only the \(J = 0\) interaction remains; the monopole-plus-pairing approximation gives 4.8 MeV.
- For \(A = 48\) with KB3, the difference \(E_\text{m}(0) - E(0)\) between the monopole approximation and the full calculation is 13.7 MeV for \(J_\text{cut} = 0\) and 8.3 MeV for \(J'_\text{cut} = 1\).
- For \(A = 24\) with USD, the pairing energy \(E_\text{p}(0)\) from Eq.~(9) is 20.7 MeV for the full \(sd\) shell (\(\Omega = 6\)), 13.2 MeV for the \(1d_{5/2} + 2s_{1/2}\) space (\(\Omega = 4\)), and 9.4 MeV for the \(1d_{5/2}\) space (\(\Omega = 3\)); the additional binding from the \(J = 0\) component is 13.7 MeV.
- The ratio \(r_\text{W} = \epsilon_\text{W} / [E(4) - E(0)]\) is 0.113 for the full Hamiltonian in both \(A = 24\) and \(A = 48\), and 0.063 for \(J'_\text{cut} = 1\) (close to the \(T(T+1)\) value of 1/15 = 0.067).
- For \(A = 24\) with USD, the parameter \(x\) in \(E(T) = E(0) + T(T+x)/(2\theta)\) is 2.05 for the full Hamiltonian, close to values extracted from measured binding energies by Bentley and Frauendorf (2013).

## Context

This work reanalyzes and extends the calculations of Satula et al. (1997), who studied the Wigner term in the USD and KB3 Hamiltonians and concluded that the \(T' = 0\) interaction is responsible for it. The present analysis shows that the decrease of \(\epsilon_\text{W}\) when \(T' = 0\) components are turned off is a side effect of the narrowing of the symmetry spectrum, which is explained by schematic monopole and pairing approximations. The results are consistent with earlier work by Bentley et al. (2014) using the \(1f_{7/2}\) valence space, which corroborates the dominance of the \(1f_{7/2}\) shell in the KB3 results. The study enables a more nuanced interpretation of the Wigner term in semi-empirical mass formulas and suggests that the \(T(T+1)\) proportionality of the symmetry energy is approximately maintained when only the \(J = 0\) interaction remains.
