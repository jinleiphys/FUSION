# Hyperon-rich matter

> The phase diagram of nuclear matter is extended to strangeness and antimatter. It offers fascinating features for heavy ion physics and astrophysics. We will discuss some regions of this phase diagram as strange matter at zero temperature, hypermatter at high densities and the antiworld at high densities and strangeness fraction.

# Hyperon-rich Matter

## Key claim

The phase diagram of nuclear matter, when extended to include strangeness and antimatter, reveals that neutron stars are more likely giant hypernuclei and that antikaon condensation does not occur in neutron star matter when hyperons are present, contrary to previous calculations that disregarded hyperons.

## Method

The author uses the relativistic mean field (RMF) model extended to include hyperons, with SU(6)-symmetry for vector coupling constants and scalar coupling constants fixed to hypernuclear data and kaon-nucleon scattering lengths. Hyperon-hyperon interactions are introduced via additional scalar ($f_0(975)$) and vector ($\phi(1020)$) meson fields. The model is applied to neutron star matter composition and to evaluate the onset of kaon condensation.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons appear in neutron star matter at a density of about 2-3 times normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
- The potential depths chosen are $U_\Lambda^{(N)} = U_\Sigma^{(N)} = -30$ MeV and $U_\Xi^{(N)} = -28$ MeV
- The $\bar K$-optical potential is found to be $U^{\bar K}_{\rm opt} = -(130\div 150)$ MeV at normal nuclear density
- The scalar potential for nucleons is $U_s = -(350\div 400)$ MeV $\rho_N/\rho_0$ and the vector potential is $U_v = (300\div 350)$ MeV $\rho_N/\rho_0$ for moderate densities
- The antinucleon potential is $U_{\bar N} = -(650\div 750)$ MeV $\rho_N/\rho_0$
- The critical density for spontaneous nucleon-antinucleon pair production is $\rho_c = (3-7)\rho_0$
- The $\bar\Lambda$ potential is $U_{\bar\Lambda} = -(430\div 500)$ MeV $\rho_N/\rho_0$
- The additional $\bar\Lambda$ potential from strange fields is $V_{\bar\Lambda} \approx -120$ MeV $\rho_B/\rho_0 \cdot f_s$ where $f_s$ is the total strangeness fraction
- The electrochemical potential never reaches values above 160 MeV, so at least 100 MeV are missing for the onset of kaon condensation

## Context

This work extends the earlier RMF calculations of Glendenning (1987) on hyperons in neutron stars and the kaon condensation calculations of Brown (1994) based on chiral perturbation theory. The paper shows that previous kaon condensation calculations that disregarded hyperons overestimated the possibility of condensation. The results enable future searches for signals of the "antiworld" in heavy ion collisions, including enhanced subthreshold antiproton production (Mishustin, 1990), apparent higher temperatures (Koch, 1991), and enhanced antihyperon production.
