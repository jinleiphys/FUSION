# Monopole oscillations in light nuclei with a molecular dynamics approach

> Collective monopole vibrations are studied in the framework of an antisymmetrized version of molecular dynamics as a function of the vibration amplitude. The giant monopole resonance energy in ${}^{40}Ca$ is sensitive to the incompressibility of the effective interaction, in good agreement with complete time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. The collective response of ${}^{12}C$, ${}^{16}O$ and ${}^{24}Mg$ is also studied. For these lighter nuclei that have an important contribution of an $α$-clustered component, different frequencies are observed, corresponding to two different types of vibrations associated with breathing and moving of the underlying clusters. Possible connections with direct breakup into $α$ clusters at high excitation energy are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in light nuclei with significant α-clustering (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ²⁴Mg), the monopole response contains two distinct oscillation frequencies corresponding to different types of cluster motion, but this finding is conditional on the choice of the Gaussian width parameter in the AMD model and is not observed in FMD calculations where the width is a dynamical variable.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) calculations for ⁴He, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ²⁴Mg, and ⁴⁰Ca, using three effective interactions (SLy4, SIII, and Gogny). The ground state was prepared by energy minimization, and monopole oscillations were excited by radially scaling the Gaussian centroid positions; the time evolution of the root-mean-square radius was Fourier-analyzed to extract oscillation frequencies as a function of excitation amplitude.

## Key numbers

*   For ⁴⁰Ca with SLy4, the AMD monopole peak frequency is 22.1 MeV, in agreement with TDHF (22.1 MeV) and RPA (21.5 MeV) calculations using the same interaction.
*   The ground-state energy of ¹²C with SLy4 is -72.2 MeV (AMD) and -77.4 MeV (FMD), compared to the experimental value of -92.2 MeV; the underbinding is attributed to the neglect of the spin-orbit interaction.
*   The matter root-mean-square radius of ⁴⁰Ca with SLy4 is 3.40 fm in both AMD and FMD, close to the HF value of 3.39 fm.
*   For ¹²C with SLy4, the monopole frequency shows a minimum at an oscillation amplitude of 0.04 fm when the inverse width parameter ν = 0.17 fm⁻², corresponding to the transition between two distinct oscillation regimes.

## Context

This work extends time-dependent mean-field studies of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR), previously applied mainly to medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, to light, clustered nuclei where standard RPA may be inadequate. It builds on TDHF calculations by Chomaz (1987) and Avez (2008) and on RPA results by Colo (2007) for ⁴⁰Ca, and it contrasts the AMD and FMD approaches to assess the role of the Gaussian width degree of freedom. The results suggest that cluster degrees of freedom can produce multiple monopole frequencies and may connect to direct breakup into α clusters at high excitation energy, motivating future inclusion of two-body collisions and quantum branching to describe the resonance width.
