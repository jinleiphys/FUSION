# Extraction of bounds on time-reversal non-invariance from neutron reactions

> Ratios involving on-resonance measurements of the three-fold and five-fold correlation cross sections for which the dependence on some of the unknown spectroscopic data is eliminated are considered. Closed form expressions are derived for the statistical distributions of these ratios. Implications for bounds on the variance of matrix elements of time reversal non-invariant nucleon-nucleon interactions are considered within a Bayesian framework and the competitiveness with bounds from other experiments is evaluated. The prospects for null five-fold correlation measurements improving by an order of magnitude or more upon the current bound on a parity-conserving T-odd interaction are good.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed-form statistical distributions for ratios of on-resonance correlation cross-sections that eliminate dependence on unknown spectroscopic parameters, and shows that null five-fold correlation measurements could improve the bound on a parity-conserving T-odd interaction by an order of magnitude or more compared to the current tightest bound from neutron-proton charge symmetry breaking experiments, provided the two-resonance approximation is valid.

## Method

The authors derived probability density functions for the ratio R_FD (five-fold correlation to deformation effect cross-section at s-wave resonances) and R_TP (three-fold correlation to parity-violating cross-section at p-wave resonances) using a two-level mixing approximation. They assumed Porter-Thomas distributions for neutron partial width amplitudes and Gaussian distributions for the imaginary matrix elements of T-odd interactions, then performed Bayesian statistical analysis to determine bounds on the root-mean-square matrix elements v_T and v_PT from hypothetical null measurements.

## Key numbers

- Current 95% confidence bound on the T-odd coupling ratio g_rho from neutron-proton CSB experiments: 6.7 x 10^-3 (Simonius, 1997)
- Current 95% confidence bound from atomic EDM measurements: 9.3 x 10^-3 (Haxton et al., 1994)
- Current 95% confidence bound from MeV-neutron FC experiments: 5.8 x 10^-2 (Huffman et al., 1997)
- For a 166Ho target with s-wave resonance spacing D = 4.6 eV, a 95% probability bound on v_T better than 50 meV is needed to improve upon the CSB result
- Four null measurements of R_FD with errors of 0.1 yield a 95% probability bound on v_T of about 40 meV
- For 12 null measurements with errors of 0.1, the bound on v_T is an order of magnitude smaller
- Four null measurements of R_TP with errors of 1 x 10^-6 would imply a 95% probability bound of 5 x 10^-6 on v_PT/v_P
- At least M_min = 4 measurements are needed to set a 95.5% probability bound comparable in size to the experimental error

## Context

This work extends the statistical treatment of on-resonance time-reversal tests beyond the p-wave resonance case considered by Bunakov, Davis and Weidenmuller (1990) to include s-wave resonance measurements of the five-fold correlation, as suggested by Huffman, Gould and Haase (1998). The Bayesian framework provides the most comprehensive statistical analysis yet of on-resonance reaction tests of time-reversal invariance, and the results enable quantitative planning of experiments at TUNL and KEK that could compete with bounds from atomic EDM measurements and neutron-proton charge symmetry breaking experiments.
