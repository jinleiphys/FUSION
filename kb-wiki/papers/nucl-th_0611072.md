# Microscopic Determinations of Fission Barriers, (MEAN-Field and Beyond)

> With a help of the selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with the D1S effective Gogny interaction and the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) we incorporate the transverse collective vibrations to the one-dimensional model of the fission-barrier penetrability based on the traditional WKB method. The average fission barrier corresponding to the least-energy path in the two-dimensional potential energy landscape as function of quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom is modified by the influence of the transverse collective vibrations along the nuclear path to fission. The set of transverse vibrational states built in the fission valley corresponding to a successively increasing nuclear elongation produces the new energy barrier which is compared with the least-energy barrier. These collective states are given as the eigensolutions of the GCM purely vibrational Hamiltonian. In addition, the influence of the collective inertia on the fission properties is displayed, and it turns out to be the decisive condition for the possible transitions between different fission valleys.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that incorporating transverse collective vibrations into the one-dimensional WKB fission-barrier model, via the Generator Coordinate Method with Gaussian Overlap Approximation, lowers the effective fission barrier height by approximately 1 MeV for the studied nuclei, but this result carries an uncertainty from the interpolation of the discrete energy landscape and mass tensor components.

## Method
The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the D1S Gogny interaction for the nuclei 226Th and 238U, using quadrupole (q2) and octupole (q3) deformation degrees of freedom. They then applied the Generator Coordinate Method with the Gaussian Overlap Approximation to construct a two-dimensional collective Bohr Hamiltonian, from which they extracted the least-action fission path and the transverse vibrational states by diagonalizing a reduced one-dimensional collective Hamiltonian along the direction perpendicular to the fission path.

## Key numbers
- For 226Th, the least-action path passes through states with about 3-5 MeV higher potential energy than the least-energy path.
- The effective fission barrier height is lowered by approximately 1 MeV for both 226Th and 238U when transverse vibrations are included.
- The study covers two actinide nuclei: 226Th and 238U.
- The potential energy landscapes were computed for quadrupole deformations up to approximately 230 barns for 226Th.

## Context
This work extends the traditional one-dimensional WKB approach to fission barriers, which the authors note gives discrepancies of fission half-lives up to a few orders of magnitude compared to experimental data. The paper builds on prior constrained HFB calculations with the Gogny force (Gogny, 1975) and the Generator Coordinate Method formalism (Hill and Wheeler, 1953; Ring and Schuck, 1980). The results enable more realistic calculations of fission half-lives and cross sections by providing a microscopic method to include transverse collective effects without adjustable parameters, and the authors indicate ongoing work on similar transitions in Fm isotopes.
