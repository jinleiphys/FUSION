# Bag-type Model with Fractal Structure

> In this work we present a bag-type model within a non-extensive statistics applied to the description of the properties of a hadronic system with an underlying fractal structure. The non-extensive ideal gas inside the bag is determined by the grand canonical partition function from which pressure, energy and particle density as well as temperature and chemical potential are obtained for the hadronic system. These quantities are studied in the approximation of fixed mass for all bag constituents but also for discrete and continuum masses. In all cases, the freeze-out line, corresponding to the energy per particle equal to 1 GeV and the lines corresponding to a fractal structure inside the proton volume are obtained. Finally, the pressure on the bag surface of the proton is calculated and the resulting value $(0.135\, {\rm GeV})^4$ obtained.

# Bag-type Model with Fractal Structure

## Key claim

The authors propose that a non-extensive (Tsallis) bag-type model, where hadrons are treated as bags containing an ideal gas with an underlying fractal structure, can consistently describe the proton as a confined system only when the constituent particles have a realistic mass spectrum (discrete or continuous), not when all constituents share a fixed light-quark mass. The model yields a specific internal pressure on the bag surface of (0.135 GeV)^4, matching the MIT bag constant, but only under the condition that the system lies below the freeze-out line defined by energy per particle equal to 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct a grand-canonical partition function for a non-extensive ideal gas inside a fixed spherical volume (radius 1.2 fm) with total energy equal to the proton mass (938 MeV). They compute pressure, energy density, particle density, temperature, and chemical potential using Tsallis statistics with entropic index q=1.14, considering three scenarios: (1) fixed mass (2.3 MeV for all quarks), (2) discrete masses from the known hadron spectrum, and (3) continuous masses following a generalized Hagedorn mass spectrum fitted to PDG data. The freeze-out line is defined by energy per particle equal to 1 GeV, and the confined region is identified as the area below this line in the T versus mu_B phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass used as total energy constraint: m_p = 938 MeV
- Bag volume radius: r_0 = 1.2 fm
- Fixed quark mass in first scenario: m = 2.3 MeV
- Entropic index used throughout: q = 1.14
- Pressure on the proton bag surface: (0.135 GeV)^4
- For the continuous mass scenario, the meson spectrum fit parameters: gamma = 10.36 x 10^(-3), T_o = 51.05 MeV
- For the baryon spectrum fit, the single parameter c = 0.078 GeV
- In the continuous mass scenario, the proton exists at mu_B approximately 0.75 GeV with maximum confinement temperature T approximately 0.05 GeV
- In the discrete mass scenario, the proton exists at mu_B approximately 0.96 GeV with maximum temperature T approximately 0.025 GeV
- Cut-off mass for continuous spectrum: Lambda = 2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn self-consistent thermodynamics and the Chew-Frautschi bootstrap model by incorporating Tsallis statistics to describe the fractal structure of hadrons, building on prior work by Deppman (2016) showing that fractal systems require non-extensive statistics. The model addresses limitations of earlier Hadron Resonance Gas models, which failed to describe high-momentum particle distributions in high-energy collisions (Hagedorn). The results enable future calculations of hadron properties using non-extensive bag models and provide a connection between the MIT bag constant and the internal pressure from a fractal gas, with potential applications to heavy-ion collision phenomenology as studied by Megias and collaborators.
