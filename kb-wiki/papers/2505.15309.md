# $ϕ\to 3π$ and $ϕπ^{0}$ transition form factor from Khuri-Treiman equations

> This work studies the $ϕ\to 3π$ decay and the $ϕ\to π^0 γ^\ast$ transition form factor, utilizing the Khuri-Treiman formalism to account for analyticity, crossing, and unitarity. Using once-subtracted dispersion relations, we perform a simultaneous fit to the $ϕ\to 3π$ Dalitz plot distribution and the $ϕ\to π^0 γ^\ast$ measurements from the KLOE collaboration, finding good agreement with these experimental data. These results reaffirm the applicability of the Khuri-Treiman approach in the analysis of three-body decays. An interesting result is that the subtraction constant appearing in the equations is similar to a sum rule expectation, in contrast to analogous studies of $ω\to 3π$ decays and $ω\to π^0 γ^\ast$, which shows significant deviations. Our results also provide a reasonable description of the trend of the transition form factor data from BaBar in the $e^{+}e^{-}\toϕπ^{0}$ scattering region. These intriguing theoretical differences between the decays of $ϕ$ and $ω$ could encourage further experimental measurements to assess the discrepancies and refine the theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The Khuri-Treiman formalism with once-subtracted dispersion relations provides a good simultaneous description of both the $\phi \to 3\pi$ Dalitz plot distribution and the $\phi \to \pi^0 \gamma^\ast$ transition form factor measured by KLOE, but the key finding is that the subtraction constant for the $\phi$ decay is consistent with the sum-rule expectation, unlike the analogous case of $\omega \to 3\pi$ where significant deviations were observed.

## Method

The authors applied the Khuri-Treiman formalism to construct the $\phi \to 3\pi$ decay amplitude, using once-subtracted dispersion relations with the Omnès function to account for $\pi\pi$ $P$-wave final-state interactions. They performed a simultaneous fit to the KLOE Dalitz plot distribution of $\phi \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ and the KLOE measurements of the $\phi \to \pi^0 \gamma^\ast$ transition form factor, incorporating $\rho^0$-$\omega$ mixing in the $s$-channel isobar amplitude and using a global $\chi^2$ function that also included constraints from the $\phi \to 3\pi$ and $\phi \to \pi^0 \gamma$ decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The $\phi$ meson mass is $M_\phi = 1019.461 \pm 0.016$ MeV and its total width is $\Gamma_\phi = 4.249 \pm 0.013$ MeV (PDG values used as input)
- The branching ratios used are $\mathcal{B}(\phi \to 3\pi) = 15.4 \pm 0.4\%$ and $\mathcal{B}(\phi \to \pi^0 \gamma) = 0.132 \pm 0.05\%$ (PDG values)
- The $\omega$ meson parameters used are $M_\omega = 782.66$ MeV and $\Gamma_\omega = 8.68$ MeV
- The Dalitz plot bins have semi-width $\Delta X = \Delta Y = 8.75$ MeV in the $(X,Y)$ phase-space variables
- The physical decay region for $s$ (the $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass squared) ranges from $s_{\text{min}} = 4m^2$ to $s_{\text{max}} = (M_\phi - m)^2$, where $m$ is the charged pion mass

## Context

This work extends previous Khuri-Treiman analyses of $\omega \to 3\pi$ and $\phi \to 3\pi$ decays by Niecknig et al. (2012) and Danilkin et al. (2014), and directly parallels the study of $\omega \to 3\pi$ and $\omega \to \pi^0 \gamma^\ast$ by the JPAC Collaboration (2020). The key novelty is the simultaneous fit to both the $\phi \to 3\pi$ Dalitz plot and the $\phi \to \pi^0 \gamma^\ast$ transition form factor data from KLOE, which was not available when earlier predictions were made. The results enable comparison between $\phi$ and $\omega$ decays, revealing that the subtraction constant for $\phi$ agrees with the sum-rule expectation while that for $\omega$ does not, motivating further experimental measurements to resolve this discrepancy.
