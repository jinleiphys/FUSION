# Relativistic dynamics of charmonia in strong magnetic fields

> We investigate the properties of charmonium systems in strong external magnetic fields using a relativistic light-front Hamiltonian approach within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework. By solving the eigenvalue problem for the invariant mass squared operator with confinement potentials and one-gluon-exchange interactions, we obtain the mass spectrum and wave functions under varying magnetic fields. Our results reveal significant spectral modifications via the Zeeman effect, including $η_c$-$J/ψ$ mixing and magnetic sublevel splitting. Momentum density analysis demonstrates wave function deformation, with transverse momentum broadening and longitudinal narrowing under strong fields, alongside structural shifts in parton distributions such as double-hump profiles in excited states. Relativistic corrections and center-of-mass coupling critically drive these dynamics, highlighting the necessity of a relativistic framework for QCD bound states in extreme magnetic environments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that relativistic corrections and center-of-mass coupling critically drive the dynamics of charmonia in strong magnetic fields, highlighting the necessity of a fully relativistic framework for QCD bound states in extreme magnetic environments, a condition the abstract presents as essential but which most prior work in the field has neglected by using non-relativistic approximations.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic light-front Hamiltonian approach within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework, constructing an effective light-cone Hamiltonian with confinement potentials (transverse holographic confinement and longitudinal confinement) and one-gluon-exchange interactions. They solved the eigenvalue problem for the invariant mass squared operator in a finite truncated basis space with regulators \(\bar N_{\max}=9\) and \(L_{\max}=8\), incorporating the external magnetic field through minimal coupling, which introduces Zeeman terms, orbital magnetic moment contributions, and a seagull term. The Hamiltonian matrix was diagonalized using LAPACK for different magnetic projection sectors \(m_j = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2\) at various magnetic field strengths.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass \(M_c = 1.57\) GeV and transverse confinement strength \(\kappa = 0.98\) GeV, with longitudinal confinement strength \(\sigma = 0.31\) GeV.
- Basis truncation parameters: \(\bar N_{\max} = 9\) and \(L_{\max} = 8\).
- Mass squared levels computed for \(eB = 0, 0.5, 1.0\) GeV\(^2\).
- Magnetic fields at RHIC estimated at \(\sim m_\pi^2\) where \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV; at LHC fields can reach \(\sim 10 m_\pi^2\).
- The vacuum charmonium spectrum from the same approach agrees with experimental values within 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ framework previously applied to quarkonium spectra in vacuum (Li 2015, Li 2017, Tang 2019) to include strong external magnetic fields relevant for heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with non-relativistic potential models used by Alford (2013), Suzuki (2016), and Bonati (2015), as well as with NRQCD approaches that encounter poor convergence for charmonium radiative transitions even at NNLO (Feng 2015, Feng 2017). The results enable future studies of parton distribution modifications and time-dependent phenomena in extreme magnetic environments, and can be compared with lattice QCD results (Endrodi 2024) and holographic QCD approaches.
