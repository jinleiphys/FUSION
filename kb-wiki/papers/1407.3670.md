# Heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC: analysis of the underlying effects

> Suppressions of light and heavy flavor observables are considered to be excellent probes of QCD matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Suppression predictions of quark and gluon jets appear to suggest a clear hierarchy according to which neutral pions should be more suppressed than D mesons, which in turn should be more suppressed than single electrons. However, joint comparison of neutral pion (light probe) and non-photonic single electron (heavy probe) suppression data at RHIC unexpectedly showed similar jet suppression for these two probes, which presents the well-known heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC. We here analyze which effects are responsible for this unexpected result, by using the dynamical energy loss formalism. We find that the main effect is a surprising reversal in the suppression hierarchy between neutral pions and D mesons, which is due to the deformation of the suppression patterns of light partons by fragmentation functions. Furthermore, we find that, due to the decay functions, the single electron suppression approaches the D meson suppression. Consequently, we propose that these two effects, taken together, provide a clear intuitive explanation of this longstanding puzzle.

## Key claim

The heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC, the unexpected observation that neutral pion and non-photonic single electron suppressions are similar, can be explained within conventional pQCD by two effects acting together: a reversal of the suppression hierarchy between neutral pions and D mesons caused by fragmentation functions, and a convergence of single electron suppression toward D meson suppression caused by decay functions.

## Method

The authors used the dynamical energy loss formalism, which includes radiative and collisional energy losses in a finite-size dynamical QCD medium with finite magnetic mass and running coupling, combined with multigluon and path-length fluctuations. They computed nuclear modification factors (\(R_{AA}\)) for neutral pions, D mesons, B mesons, and non-photonic single electrons at RHIC by folding parton-level energy loss with production, fragmentation, and decay functions using standard literature parameters with no free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Average QGP temperature used: \(T = 221\) MeV, as extracted by PHENIX.
- Charm quark mass: \(M = 1.2\) GeV; bottom quark mass: \(M = 4.75\) GeV.
- Debye mass \(\mu_E \approx 0.7\) GeV obtained self-consistently; magnetic mass ratio range: \(0.4 < \mu_M/\mu_E < 0.6\).
- Effective number of light quark flavors: \(n_f = 2.5\); perturbative QCD scale: \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 0.2\) GeV.
- The model predicts D meson suppression larger than neutral pion suppression at RHIC, reversing the naive hierarchy (Fig. 5).
- Single electron suppression lies between D and B meson suppressions but is closer to D meson suppression (Fig. 6, left panel).
- Theoretical predictions for neutral pion and single electron \(R_{AA}\) show qualitative and quantitative agreement with 0-10% central 200 GeV RHIC data from STAR and PHENIX (Fig. 7).

## Context

This work addresses the heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC, first noted in Djordjevic (2006), where neutral pion and single electron suppressions were unexpectedly similar despite naive expectations of a clear hierarchy. Prior attempts to explain the puzzle invoked mechanisms outside conventional pQCD, such as those by Ficnar, Noronha, and Gyulassy (2011) or Noronha, Gyulassy, and Torrieri (2009). The present analysis extends the authors' own dynamical energy loss formalism (Djordjevic 2009; Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) to include finite magnetic mass and running coupling (Djordjevic and Djordjevic 2014), and demonstrates that the puzzle is fully explainable within pQCD without free parameters. The results enable future tests against high-precision D meson \(R_{AA}\) data from STAR.
