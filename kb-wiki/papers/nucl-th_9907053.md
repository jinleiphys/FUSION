# Relativistic quasipotential equations with u-channel exchange interactions

> Various quasipotential two-body scattering equations are studied at the one-loop level for the case of $t$- and $u$-channel exchange potentials. We find that the quasipotential equations devised to satisfy the one-body limit for the $t$-channel exchange potential can be in large disagreement with the field-theoretical prediction in the case of $u$-channel exchange interactions. Within the spectator model, the description of the $u$-channel case improves if another choice of the spectator particle is made. Since the appropriate choice of the spectator depends strongly on the type of interaction used, one faces a problem when both types of interaction are contained in the potential. Equal-time formulations are presented, which, in the light-heavy particle system corresponding to the mass situation of the $πN$ system, approximate in a reasonable way the field-theoretical result for both types of interactions.

## Key claim

Quasipotential equations that correctly reproduce the one-body limit for t-channel exchange potentials can be in large disagreement with field-theoretical predictions when applied to u-channel exchange interactions; the appropriate choice of spectator particle depends strongly on the type of interaction, creating a problem when both types are present in the potential.

## Method

The authors evaluated the scalar box and crossed-box graphs at one-loop level for both t-channel and u-channel exchange potentials, comparing exact field-theoretical results with various quasipotential approximations including the spectator model (with either nucleon or pion as spectator), equal-time (ET) approximations, symmetrized equal-time prescriptions, and a constant energy-transfer (CET) approximation. Numerical integrations were performed using Gaussian quadrature (320 points for q0 integration, 64 points for q, 8 points for angular integrations), and analytical expressions for forward scattering (t=0) were derived. Phase shifts were computed using the [1,1] Pade approximant to the K-matrix Born series.

## Key numbers

* For the t-exchange case at threshold with mN=1, mπ=0.01, and varying μ, the symmetrized ET formulation achieves the correct one-body limit independently of the exchange particle mass, while the nucleon spectator deviates for large μ and the pion spectator gives an even worse prediction.
* For the u-exchange case with mN=1, mπ=0.01, the nucleon spectator prediction is an order of magnitude larger than the exact QFT result (beyond scale of the figure), while the pion spectator prediction is in good agreement.
* For the u-exchange case at threshold with μ=mN and mπ→0, the box and crossed-box contributions vanish individually for μ≠mN, but at μ=mN the limit yields B[(mN±mπ)²,0] = ±1/(2 mπ mN³).
* For the t-exchange case at threshold with mπ=0.15 mN and μ=0.82 mN (ρ-meson mass), all QP prescriptions except the pion spectator do reasonably well with correct energy dependence.
* For the u-exchange case with mπ=0.15 mN and μ=1.31 mN (Δ-isobar mass), the ET and pion spectator prescriptions both practically "fall on top" of the exact result.
* The coupling strength used in phase-shift calculations was g²/(4π)=2.0, adjusted so rescattering effects match realistic πN calculations.

## Context

This work extends studies of quasipotential approximations by Gross (1969), Todorov (1973), and Mandelzweig and Wallace (1987), which focused on t-channel exchange potentials and the one-body limit. It directly addresses the discrepancy encountered by Gross and Surya (1993) in πN scattering, where the choice of spectator particle (pion vs. nucleon) gave different results; the authors show this difference arises from the type of potential (t-channel vs. u-channel) rather than the mass of the exchanged particle. The findings enable the use of equal-time formulations for systems like πN scattering where both t- and u-channel exchanges are present, and the authors report in a separate publication (Pascalutsa and Tjon, 1999) on a relativistic study of πN dynamics using the equal-time quasipotential formalism with full spin complications.
