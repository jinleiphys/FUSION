# Probing Shadowed Nuclear Sea with Massive Gauge Bosons in the Future Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The production of the massive bosons $Z^0$ and $W^{\pm}$ could provide an excellent tool to study cold nuclear matter effects and the modifications of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) relative to parton distribution functions (PDFs) of a free proton in high energy nuclear reactions at the LHC as well as in heavy-ion collisions (HIC) with much higher center-of mass energies available in the future colliders. In this paper we calculate the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of the vector boson and their nuclear modification factors in p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=63$TeV and in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=39$TeV in the framework of perturbative QCD by utilizing three parametrization sets of nPDFs: EPS09, DSSZ and nCTEQ. It is found that in heavy-ion collisions at such high colliding energies, both the rapidity distribution and the transverse momentum spectrum of vector bosons are considerably suppressed in wide kinematic regions with respect to p+p reactions due to large nuclear shadowing effect. We demonstrate that in the massive vector boson productions processes with sea quarks in the initial-state may give more contributions than those with valence quarks in the initial-state, therefore in future heavy-ion collisions the isospin effect is less pronounced and the charge asymmetry of W boson will be reduced significantly as compared to that at the LHC. Large difference between results with nCTEQ and results with EPS09 and DSSZ is observed in nuclear modifications of both rapidity and $p_T$ distributions of $Z^0$ and $W$ in the future HIC.

## Key claim

The production of massive gauge bosons (Z^0 and W±) in future heavy-ion collisions at center-of-mass energies of √s_NN = 39 TeV (Pb+Pb) and 63 TeV (p+Pb) will be considerably suppressed relative to proton-proton collisions due to large nuclear shadowing effects at small momentum fraction x, with the caveat that the magnitude of suppression depends strongly on which nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) parametrization set is used, and nCTEQ predicts significantly stronger suppression than EPS09 or DSSZ.

## Method

The authors performed perturbative QCD calculations at next-to-leading order (O(α_s)) using the DYNNLO program, incorporating three nPDF parametrization sets (EPS09, DSSZ, nCTEQ) with MSTW2008 proton PDFs as baseline. They computed the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of Z^0 and W± bosons, along with their nuclear modification factors R_AA and R_pA, for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 39 TeV and p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 63 TeV, comparing results to LHC energies (2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV respectively). Leading-order analysis of partonic subprocess contributions was also performed to identify which quark flavors dominate at different energies.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 39 TeV: nCTEQ nPDFs predict more than 30% suppression of Z^0 yield at central rapidity relative to p+p collisions
- p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 63 TeV: persistent suppressions given by EPS09, DSSZ, and nCTEQ except in very backward regime (-4.0 < y^Z < -3.0)
- At LHC energy (2.76 TeV Pb+Pb), x ~ 0.033 for mid-rapidity Z^0 production; at future HIC (39 TeV), x ~ 0.0023, entering the shadowing region of EPS09
- Sea quark initiated processes contribute more than 30% of Z^0 production at mid-rapidity in future HIC at leading order
- For Z^0 p_T distributions in Pb+Pb at 39 TeV: considerable suppressions in region 10 GeV ≲ p_T^Z ≲ 150 GeV from all three nPDF sets
- At LHC (2.76 TeV Pb+Pb): enhancements in region 10 GeV ≲ p_T^Z ≲ 250 GeV from all three nPDF sets

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cold nuclear matter effects in vector boson production at LHC energies (Ru 2014, Kang 2012, Paukkunen 2010) to the much higher energies proposed for the Future Circular Collider (FCC) at CERN and the CEPC-SPPC in China. The authors demonstrate that at these future energies, sea quarks and gluons dominate over valence quarks in the initial state, making the isospin effect less pronounced and the charge asymmetry of W bosons significantly reduced compared to LHC measurements. The large differences between nCTEQ and the other nPDF sets (EPS09, DSSZ) at small x highlight the need for future heavy-ion data to constrain nuclear parton distributions for sea quarks and gluons in the previously unexplored low-x regime.
