# Parity Violation, the Neutron Radius of Lead, and Neutron Stars

> The neutron radius of a heavy nucleus is a fundamental nuclear-structure observable that remains elusive. Progress in this arena has been limited by the exclusive use of hadronic probes that are hindered by large and controversial uncertainties in the reaction mechanism. The Parity Radius Experiment at the Jefferson Laboratory offers an attractive electro-weak alternative to the hadronic program and promises to measure the neutron radius of 208Pb accurately and model independently via parity-violating electron scattering. In this contribution we examine the far-reaching implications that such a determination will have in areas as diverse as nuclear structure, atomic parity violation, and astrophysics.

## Key claim

The Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory promises to measure the neutron radius of 208Pb to within 0.05 fm, model independently via parity-violating electron scattering, and this measurement will have far-reaching implications for nuclear structure, atomic parity violation, and neutron star astrophysics; however, the correlation between the neutron skin of 208Pb and the radius of a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star is model dependent, as the star radius depends on both the low- and high-density components of the equation of state.

## Method

The author uses a relativistic density functional with an isoscalar-isovector mixing coupling constant (Lambda_v) to tune the density dependence of the symmetry energy, then calculates the neutron skin of 208Pb and the properties of neutron stars by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The transition density from the uniform liquid mantle to the non-uniform solid crust is determined via an RPA stability analysis, and a simple polytropic equation of state interpolates between the outer crust and the uniform liquid. Skin-skin correlations between 208Pb and nuclei relevant to atomic parity violation (138Ba, 158Dy, 176Yb) are computed using linear regression.

## Key numbers

- The charge radius of 208Pb has been determined by elastic electron scattering to better than 0.001 fm (Fricke, 1995), while realistic estimates place the uncertainty in the neutron radius at about 0.2 fm.
- PREX aims to measure the neutron radius of 208Pb to within 0.05 fm.
- Varying Lambda_v reduces the neutron skin of 208Pb from Rn-Rp = 0.28 fm to Rn-Rp = 0.20 fm.
- The theoretical spread in the neutron radius of 208Pb is approximately 0.2 to 0.3 fm, driven by differences between relativistic and nonrelativistic models.
- The PREX 1% measurement of the neutron radius in 208Pb (Delta Rn(208Pb) = 0.056 fm) translates into neutron radius uncertainties of Delta Rn(138Ba) = 0.045 fm, Delta Rn(158Dy) = 0.034 fm, and Delta Rn(176Yb) = 0.052 fm.
- The neutron star structure is computed for a "canonical" 1.4 solar-mass neutron star (M = 1.4 M_sun).

## Context

This work extends the correlation between the neutron skin of heavy nuclei and the pressure of neutron-rich matter, first noted by Brown (2000), and the connection between neutron skin thickness and neutron star radii established by Horowitz (2001). The paper builds on the relativistic density functional models of Serot (1984, 1997) and Mueller (1996), and uses the NL3 set of Lalazissis (1996) and the FSUGold set of Todd-Rutel (2005). The results enable a combined measurement from PREX and observational astronomy to provide the most complete information to date on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, and also inform atomic parity violation experiments on isotopes of Barium, Dysprosium, Ytterbium, and Francium by reducing nuclear-structure uncertainties.
