# Dileptons from Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> Disoriented chiral condensates or long wavelength pionic oscillations and their interaction with the thermal environment can be a significant source of dileptons. We calculate the yield of such dilepton production within the linear sigma model, both in a quantal mean-field treatment and in a semi-classical approximation. We then illustrate the basic features of the dilepton spectrum in a schematic model. We find that dilepton yield with invariant mass near and below $2m_π$ due to the soft pion modes can be up to two orders of magnitude larger than the corresponding equilibrium yield.

## Key claim

Dilepton yield with invariant mass near and below \(2m_\pi\) from soft pion modes in a disoriented chiral condensate can be up to two orders of magnitude larger than the corresponding equilibrium yield, but this enhancement is confined to low dilepton momenta (below about 300 MeV) and is strongly reduced when realistic experimental acceptance cuts are applied.

## Method

The authors calculated dilepton production from disoriented chiral condensates within the linear sigma model using two complementary approaches: a quantal mean-field treatment (with mode functions evolved from thermal and quench initial conditions) and a semi-classical approximation (with classical field equations sampled from thermal ensembles). Both methods computed the electromagnetic current-current correlator from the pion fields and extracted the invariant dilepton yield as a function of mass and momentum, comparing thermal equilibrium scenarios to quench scenarios that emulate rapid cooling.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement from quench initial conditions relative to thermal production is about two orders of magnitude at invariant mass \(M \simeq 2 m_\pi\) for the quantal mean-field treatment.
- In the semi-classical treatment, the enhancement around 400 MeV is only about one order of magnitude.
- The enhancement is confined to dilepton momenta below \(q \simeq 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for an invariant mass of \(M = 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The thermal initial condition in the mean-field treatment had energy density \(\epsilon = 13.7\ \mathrm{MeV/fm^3}\) at \(T = 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\); the quench scenario had \(\epsilon = 13.6\ \mathrm{MeV/fm^3}\).
- The pion mass used was \(135\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and the resulting sigma mass was \(340\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- After applying CERES acceptance cuts, the enhancement becomes virtually indistinguishable from the thermal spectrum; only if momentum cuts are pushed down to \(\simeq 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\) does a factor of about ten remain.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) as proposed by Bjorken, Kowalski, and Taylor (1993) and numerical simulations of the linear sigma model by Randrup and others (1993-1996), which showed that rapid cooling can amplify soft pion modes. It also builds on electromagnetic DCC signatures studied by Zhang, Wang, and others (1996), who found that coherent pion field oscillations produce dileptons only at very small invariant masses. The present paper addresses incoherent dilepton production from high-momentum pions annihilating with coherent DCC pions, finding a narrow enhancement near \(2m_\pi\) that may be observable if experimental momentum resolution can be pushed to low values.
