# Intermittency analysis of proton numbers in heavy-ion collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Local density fluctuations near the QCD critical point has been suggested to exhibit a power-law behavior which can be probed by an intermittency analysis on scaled factorial moment (SFM) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The collision energy and centrality dependence of the second-order SFMs are systematically investigated in Au $+$ Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV within the UrQMD model. We estimate the noncritical background in the measurement of intermittency and propose a cumulative variable method to effectively remove the background contributions. We further study the effect of particle detection efficiency by implementing the RHIC (STAR) experimental tracking efficiencies in the UrQMD events. A cell-by-cell method is proposed for experimental application of efficiency corrections on SFM. This work can provide a guidance of background subtraction and efficiency correction for the experimental measurement of intermittency in the search of the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the small but nonzero intermittency indices observed in UrQMD model simulations (which contain no critical fluctuations) arise from noncritical background effects, and that these background contributions can be effectively removed using a cumulative variable method, yielding intermittency indices close to zero.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD model (version 2.3) to generate event samples of Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. They calculated the second-order scaled factorial moment (SFM) of proton multiplicities in two-dimensional transverse momentum space (px, py) at midrapidity (|y|<0.5) with 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c. They applied a cumulative variable transformation to the momentum coordinates to remove distortions from nonuniform single-particle density distributions, and tested the method on critical Monte Carlo (CMC) event samples contaminated with Gaussian background. They also derived a cell-by-cell efficiency correction formula and validated it by embedding RHIC (STAR) experimental tracking efficiencies into UrQMD events.

## Key numbers

- Event statistics: 72.5, 105, 106, 81, 133, 38, and 56 million events at sqrt(sNN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, respectively
- The theoretical prediction for the second-order intermittency index in a 2D transverse momentum space for baryon density is phi2 = 5/6 from a 3D Ising universality class model (Antoniou 2006)
- The cumulative variable method was tested with CMC events contaminated by Gaussian background with mixing probability lambda = 95%
- After applying the cumulative variable method to UrQMD events, the intermittency index values became near zero at all measured energies and centralities

## Context

This work extends previous intermittency studies by the NA49 and NA61 collaborations at CERN SPS, who observed power-law behavior in Si+Si collisions at 158A GeV (NA49 2006) and reported preliminary results from STAR at RHIC. The paper provides a noncritical baseline from the UrQMD transport model, which contains no critical fluctuations, and offers practical methods for background subtraction (cumulative variable method from Ochs 1991 and Bialas and Gazdzicki 1990) and efficiency correction (cell-by-cell method) that can be applied to experimental searches for the QCD critical point in ongoing RHIC Beam Energy Scan II data.
