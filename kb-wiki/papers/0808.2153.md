# Angular Momentum Projected Configuration Interaction with Realistic Hamiltonians

> The Projected Configuration Interaction (PCI) method starts from a collection of mean-field wave functions, and builds up correlated wave functions of good symmetry. It relies on the Generator Coordinator Method (GCM) techniques, but it improves the past approaches by a very efficient method of selecting the basis states. We use the same realistic Hamiltonians and model spaces as the Configuration Interaction (CI) method, and compare the results with the full CI calculations in the sd and pf shell. Examples of 24Mg, 28Si, 48Cr, 52Fe and 56Ni are discussed.

## Key claim

The Projected Configuration Interaction (PCI) method can reproduce full Configuration Interaction (CI) results for low-lying states in deformed sd- and pf-shell nuclei using a much smaller basis, but the agreement is less satisfactory for non-rotational nuclei like ⁵²Fe and ⁵⁶Ni, where the basis selection needs improvement.

## Method

The PCI method starts from a set of deformed Slater Determinants (SDs) obtained from a Nilsson Hamiltonian with quadrupole and hexadecupole deformation parameters, projects them onto good angular momentum using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) technique, and then builds a basis by including low-lying particle-hole (np-nh) excitations on those projected SDs. The Hamiltonian matrix elements are calculated using the same realistic effective interactions (USD for sd-shell, GXPF1A for pf-shell) as in full CI, and the generalized eigenvalue problem is solved for each angular momentum I. Basis states are selected by a criterion based on the energy gain from mixing each np-nh excitation with its parent SD.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁸Si, the projected SD with I=0 gives energy −133.64 MeV at deformation (ε₂,ε₄) = (−0.40,−0.20), which is 2.30 MeV above the full CI ground state energy of −135.94 MeV
- For ²⁴Mg, PCI with 1749 selected SDs yields a ground state energy of −89.94 MeV, compared to −89.91 MeV from QMCD with 800 SDs
- For ⁴⁸Cr, PCI selects 5973 SDs (with N=30 starting configurations, |K|≤4, E_cut=0.2 keV) versus the full CI m-scheme dimension of 1,963,461
- For ⁵⁶Ni, PCI dimensions for I=0 are 2373 compared to the full CI m-scheme dimension of 1.5×10⁷
- For ²⁸Si, PCI with 108 projected SDs (PH:D=108) gives yrast band energies within about 300 keV of full CI results

## Context

This work extends the Projected Shell Model (PSM) of Hara and Sun (1995) and the GCM approach by adding efficient np-nh configuration mixing, enabling the use of realistic Hamiltonians (USD, KB3, FPD6, GXPF1) rather than the schematic multipole-multipole plus pairing Hamiltonians used in PSM. It improves upon the Quantum Monte Carlo Diagonalization (QMCD) method of Honma, Mizusaki, and Otsuka (1996) by providing a more systematic basis selection and by recovering full angular momentum through explicit projection rather than relying on M-projection alone. The method enables calculations for deformed nuclei with much smaller dimensions than full CI, and can describe rotational bands in nuclei like ⁵⁶Ni that require up to 10p-10h excitations.
