# Towards a Unified Understanding of Jet Quenching and Elliptic Flow within Perturbative QCD Parton Transport

> The gluonic contribution to the nuclear modification factor R_AA is investigated for central Au + Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV employing a perturbative QCD-based parton cascade including radiative processes. A flat quenching pattern is found up to transverse momenta of 30 GeV which is slightly smaller compared with results from the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formalism. We demonstrate that the present microscopic transport description provides challenging means of investigating both jet-quenching and a strong buildup of elliptic flow in terms of the same standard perturbative QCD interactions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a perturbative QCD-based parton cascade including radiative processes can simultaneously describe both jet quenching and elliptic flow in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, but the gluonic suppression factor R_AA is found to be approximately 0.053, which is slightly smaller (stronger suppression) than results from the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formalism.

## Method
The authors employ the BAMPS (Boltzmann approach to multi parton scatterings) parton cascade, treating gluons as semi-classical massless Boltzmann particles with elastic gg→gg interactions via leading-order pQCD differential cross sections and inelastic gg↔ggg processes using an effective Bremsstrahlung matrix element. A theta-function cutoff enforces the Bethe-Heitler regime by ensuring the formation time of emitted gluons does not exceed the mean free path of the parent particle, approximating the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. The initial gluon distribution for Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV uses mini-jets with a lower momentum cut-off p0=1.4 GeV from nucleon-nucleon collisions following a Glauber model with Wood-Saxon density profile and Gluck-Reya-Vogt parton distribution functions with a K-factor of 2.

## Key numbers
- Gluonic R_AA for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is approximately 0.053, roughly constant up to transverse momenta of 30 GeV
- Differential energy loss dE/dx for a gluon jet in a static thermal gluon medium at T=400 MeV is approximately 29 GeV/fm at E=40 GeV from radiative gg→ggg processes, compared to approximately 1 GeV/fm from elastic gg→gg interactions
- For comparison, a gluon jet interacting only via isotropic binary scatterings with fixed cross section sigma=10 mb shows dE/dx approximately 94 GeV/fm at E=40 GeV (about a factor of 3-4 larger than pQCD interactions)
- The total average cross section for a gluon jet at E=40 GeV in the thermal medium is approximately 3.5 mb
- Initial gluon density per unit rapidity is dN_g/dy approximately 700, evolving to a final dN_g/dy approximately 800
- Gluon jets with final pT>10 GeV are produced on average 1.7 fm from the surface and undergo on average 0.6 radiative processes, 2.0 elastic processes, and 0.1 annihilation processes

## Context
This work extends the BAMPS framework previously developed by Xu et al. (2007) that demonstrated pQCD Bremsstrahlung processes can drive gluon matter to equilibration within less than 1 fm/c and generate sizeable elliptic flow with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s in the range 0.08-0.15. The results contradict earlier attempts using only binary elastic pQCD collisions with fixed large cross sections (Zhang 1999, Lin 2001, Molnar combined) that could reproduce elliptic flow but would over-suppress high-momentum partons. The gluonic R_AA is compared to analytic results from Wicks et al. (2005) using the GLV formalism, which found R_AA approximately 0.07-0.09 assuming dN_g/dy=1100. The approach enables future inclusion of light quark degrees of freedom and fragmentation schemes to achieve better agreement with experimental pion data from PHENIX and STAR.
