# Single photons from relativistic collision of lead nuclei at CERN SPS: A reanalysis

> We present a reanalysis of single photon production from relativistic collision of lead nuclei at CERN SPS measured by the WA98 experiment. The refinements include use of iso-spin and shadowing corrected NLO pQCD treatment for prompt photon production using an optimized scale for factorization, renormalization, and fragmentation and use of hydrodynamics suited for non-central collisions along with a well tested equation of state admitting a quark-hadron phase transition. A quantitative explanation of the data requires a large initial temperature (at a small formation time of about 0.2 fm/$c$) and a moderate increase in the prompt yield which could perhaps be attributed to the Cronin effect in nuclei. The data can also be explained using a moderate initial temperature (at a formation time of about 1 fm/$c$) with a very large $K$-factor multiplying the prompt yield. We show that different initial times give rise to different values for the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ for thermal photons. We also show that a measurement of $v_2$ for thermal photons could also distinguish between the scenarios with or without a phase transition.

# Single photons from relativistic collision of lead nuclei at CERN SPS: A reanalysis

## Key claim
The WA98 single photon data can be explained either by a large initial temperature (about 420 MeV at a formation time of 0.2 fm/c) with a moderate enhancement of prompt photons (K-factor of 2.7), or by a moderate initial temperature (about 238 MeV at a formation time of 1.0 fm/c) with a very large K-factor of 5.9 multiplying the prompt yield; the measurement of elliptic flow v2 for thermal photons could distinguish between these scenarios and also between scenarios with or without a quark-hadron phase transition.

## Method
The authors performed a reanalysis of single photon production in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV measured by the WA98 experiment, using NLO pQCD with optimized scale Q = p_T/2 for prompt photons including isospin and shadowing corrections, and boost-invariant hydrodynamics with an equation of state (EOS Q) incorporating a first-order quark-hadron phase transition at T_c = 164 MeV. They varied the initial formation time tau_0 from 0.2 to 1.0 fm/c while keeping the rapidity density of total entropy and net baryons fixed, and calculated thermal photon production using the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe rate for quark matter and the Turbide-Rapp-Gale rate for hadronic matter.

## Key numbers
- Initial peak temperatures for tau_0 = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm/c: 420, 330, 284, 257, and 238 MeV respectively; average temperatures: 380, 295, 257, 233, and 215 MeV respectively
- K-factors multiplying NLO pQCD prompt photon yield needed to fit WA98 data at p_T = 2.55 GeV/c: 2.7 (tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c), 4.9 (tau_0 = 0.4 fm/c), 5.4 (tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c), 5.7 (tau_0 = 0.8 fm/c), and 5.9 (tau_0 = 1.0 fm/c)
- Prompt photon production is about 17% of the total single photon yield at this energy
- Phase transition temperature T_c = 164 MeV; freeze-out energy density = 0.075 GeV/fm^3; mixed phase begins at energy density 0.45 GeV/fm^-3
- Isospin and shadowing reduce prompt photon production at p_T = 4 GeV/c by about 30% for Pb+Pb compared to naive scaling of pp data
- For tau_0 = 0.8 fm/c, the initial conditions used K = 2.04 GeV/fm and L = 0.122 GeV^-1 to describe hadron spectra
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-section set to 32 mb; Pb nuclear radius R = 6.5 fm, diffuseness a = 0.54 fm
- Impact parameter range for 0-10% most central collisions: b between 0 and 4.6 fm

## Context
This work reanalyzes the WA98 experiment (WA98 Collaboration, 2000) single photon data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS, extending earlier analyses that used lower-order pQCD or simpler hydrodynamics. The authors incorporate NLO pQCD with optimized scales from Aurenche et al. (2006) and the hydrodynamics code of Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (2000) which had successfully described hadron spectra. The study builds on earlier work by Srivastava (2002) and addresses the long-standing ambiguity between QGP and hadron-gas-only scenarios for SPS energies. The results enable future measurements of photon elliptic flow at SPS or lower RHIC energies to distinguish formation times and determine whether a quark-gluon plasma was created.
