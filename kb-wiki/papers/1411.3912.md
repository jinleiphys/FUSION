# Systematic Investigation of Negative Cooper-Frye Contributions in Heavy Ion Collisions Using Coarse-grained Molecular Dynamics

> In most heavy ion collision simulations involving relativistic hydrodynamics, the Cooper-Frye formula is applied to transform the hydrodynamical fields to particles. In this article the so-called negative contributions in the Cooper-Frye formula are studied using a coarse-grained transport approach. The magnitude of negative contributions is investigated as a function of hadron mass, collision energy in the range of $E_{\rm lab} = 5$--$160A$ GeV, collision centrality and the energy density transition criterion defining the hypersurface. The microscopic results are compared to negative contributions expected from hydrodynamical treatment assuming local thermal equilibrium. The main conclusion is that the number of actual microscopic particles flying inward is smaller than the negative contribution one would expect in an equilibrated scenario. The largest impact of negative contributions is found to be on the pion rapidity distribution at midrapidity in central collisions. For this case negative contributions in equilibrium constitute 8--13\% of positive contributions depending on collision energy, but only 0.5--4\% in cascade calculation. The dependence on the collision energy itself is found to be non-monotonous with a maximum at 10-20$A$ GeV.

## Key claim

The number of actual microscopic particles flying inward through a constant-energy-density hypersurface is smaller than the negative Cooper-Frye contribution one would expect from an equilibrated hydrodynamical scenario, indicating that the system is out of both chemical and kinetic equilibrium at the transition surface.

## Method

Au+Au collisions at laboratory frame energies from 5 to 160A GeV were simulated using the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD 3.3p2) transport approach. The events were coarse-grained on a 3+1D space-time grid to obtain energy-momentum tensors and flow velocities, from which a hypersurface of constant Landau rest frame energy density was constructed. On this hypersurface, particle spectra were calculated both by applying the Cooper-Frye formula with an ideal hadron resonance gas equation of state and by explicitly counting actual UrQMD particles crossing the surface, separating inward and outward crossings.

## Key numbers

- Negative Cooper-Frye contributions for pions at midrapidity in central collisions constitute 8-13% of positive contributions depending on collision energy, but only 0.5-4% in the cascade calculation.
- The ratio of negative to positive contributions shows a non-monotonous dependence on collision energy with a maximum at 10-20A GeV.
- At Elab = 40A GeV, the negative to positive ratio for pions at midrapidity on the epsilon_c = 0.3 GeV/fm^3 surface is significantly larger in Cooper-Frye than in the explicit UrQMD particle counting.
- Energy conservation in the coarse-graining procedure is violated by less than 1% when integrated over the whole collision time at all energies studied.
- The average temperature on the constant energy density surface increases with collision energy, with distributions narrow enough that constant energy density approximately coincides with constant temperature.

## Context

This work extends studies of negative Cooper-Frye contributions in hybrid models, which were previously evaluated at SPS and RHIC energies by Petersen and Huovinen (2012) who found negative contributions around 13% at 160A GeV and 9% at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The present results show smaller negative contributions than those hydrodynamical calculations, indicating that the transport-based approach yields velocity profiles with larger relative velocity between flow and surface. The findings enable improved understanding of the transition from hydrodynamics to transport in hybrid models and highlight the need to account for non-equilibrium effects when constructing particlization surfaces at lower collision energies.
