# Benchmarking nuclear models for Gamow-Teller response

> A comparative study of the nuclear Gamow-Teller response (GTR) within conceptually different state-of-the-art approaches is presented. Three nuclear microscopic models are considered: (i) the recently developed charge-exchange relativistic time blocking approximation (RTBA) based on the covariant density functional theory, (ii) the shell model (SM) with an extended "jj77" model space and (iii) the non-relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with a Brueckner G-matrix effective interaction. We study the physics cases where two or all three of these models can be applied. The Gamow-Teller response functions are calculated for 208-Pb, 132-Sn and 78-Ni within both RTBA and QRPA. The strengths obtained for 208-Pb are compared to data that enables a firm model benchmarking. For the nucleus 132-Sn, also SM calculations are performed within the model space truncated at the level of a particle-hole (ph) coupled to vibration configurations. This allows a consistent comparison to the RTBA where ph+phonon coupling is responsible for the spreading width and considerable quenching of the GTR. Differences between the models and perspectives of their future developments are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper benchmarks three conceptually different microscopic nuclear models (relativistic time blocking approximation, shell model, and quasiparticle random phase approximation) for Gamow-Teller response, finding that while all models exhaust the Ikeda sum rule within their respective model spaces, the RTBA best reproduces the experimental spreading width in 208Pb, but the agreement depends on the specific nucleus and the inclusion of particle-vibration coupling.

## Method

The authors calculated Gamow-Teller strength distributions for three doubly-magic nuclei (208Pb, 132Sn, and 78Ni) using three approaches: (i) the charge-exchange relativistic time blocking approximation (RTBA) based on covariant density functional theory with NL3 parametrization, which includes particle-phonon coupling; (ii) the non-relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with a Brueckner G-matrix effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential and single-particle energies from the SkX mean field; and (iii) the shell model (SM) with an extended "jj77" model space for 132Sn, using Tamm-Dancoff approximation configurations coupled to 1p-1h core vibrations. The RTBA and QRPA calculations were performed for all three nuclei, while SM calculations were only feasible for 132Sn.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the experimentally observed total Gamow-Teller strength is a factor of 0.62 smaller than that obtained in the QRPA without quenching factors
- In the QRPA for 208Pb, the parameter g_ph = 1.15 was adjusted to reproduce the GTR centroid, and 3% of the total B(GT_-) lies beyond the considered 25 MeV energy interval
- In the RRPA for 208Pb, approximately 8% of the B(GT_-) is at large negative energies due to transitions to the Dirac sea
- In the RTBA for 208Pb, an additional 5% of the sum rule goes above the considered energy region, and the total B(GT_+) equals 0.34
- For 132Sn, the RRPA strength distribution is shifted upward by approximately 2 MeV relative to the QRPA strength when related to the ground state of the daughter nucleus
- For 132Sn, the QRPA overestimates the experimental 1+_1 energy by 1.5 MeV
- For 132Sn, 2%, 8%, and 12% of the sum rule lies beyond the considered energy region for QRPA, RTBA, and SM respectively

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent DFT-based studies of Gamow-Teller response that were confined to the QRPA (BTF.90, EBDNS.99, PNVR.04), and earlier relativistic time blocking approximation applications that focused on spin-dipole strength (MLVR.12). The paper enables systematic benchmarking between conceptually different microscopic approaches, showing that the RTBA's particle-phonon coupling mechanism reproduces the spreading width observed in 208Pb data from WOD.12, while the QRPA with realistic forces (STF88, YRFS09) and the shell model with extended jj77 space (hb13) provide complementary descriptions. The comparison identifies specific missing mechanisms in the RTBA (pairing vibrations and ground state correlations from ph-phonon configurations) that need inclusion for improved low-energy strength predictions relevant to beta-decay half-lives.
