# On the factorization of chiral logarithms in the pion form factors

> The recently proposed hard-pion chiral perturbation theory predicts that the leading chiral logarithms factorize with respect to the energy dependence in the chiral limit. This claim has been successfully tested in the pion form factors up to two loops in chiral perturbation theory. In the present paper we explain this factorization property at two loops and even show that it is valid to all orders for a subclass of diagrams. We also demonstrate that factorization is violated starting at three loops.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the factorization of leading chiral logarithms in the pion form factors, which holds up to two loops in chiral perturbation theory and was conjectured to hold to all orders, is violated starting at three loops due to inelastic contributions from four-pion intermediate states.

## Method

The authors analyzed the vector and scalar pion form factors using a dispersive representation combined with chiral counting, studying the elastic contribution (two-pion intermediate states) separately from inelastic contributions (four-pion intermediate states). For the elastic part, they developed a recursive argument based on the dispersion relation to prove factorization to all orders. For the inelastic part at three loops, they evaluated the seven contributing diagrams using a four-body phase space parametrization, performing analytical integrations where possible and numerical integrations with fitting for the remaining diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient of the leading chiral logarithm for the scalar form factor from elastic contributions: \(\alpha_S = -\frac{5}{2}\)
- The coefficient of the leading chiral logarithm for the vector form factor from elastic contributions: \(\alpha_V = -1\)
- The coefficient of the leading chiral logarithm in the imaginary part of the inelastic contribution at three loops: \(\delta = (-0.53 \pm 0.05)\,\pi\), multiplying \(\frac{s^2}{(4\pi F)^4}\)
- The factorization breaking effect from inelastic contributions is about 5% in the range of interest for \(s\)
- For the vector form factor at asymptotic energies, the leading chiral log coefficient is \(-2\), not \(-1\) as predicted by hard-pion chiral perturbation theory

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bijnens and collaborators (2009-2011) who proposed hard-pion chiral perturbation theory and tested factorization up to two loops in the pion form factors calculated by Bijnens (1998). The paper contradicts the claim by Bijnens and Jemos (2010) that factorization might hold to all orders. The results enable future studies of the precise range of validity of hard-pion chiral perturbation theory and connect to the asymptotic analysis of Brodsky-Lepage (1980) and Chen-Stewart (2003), which predict a different coefficient for the leading chiral logarithm at high energies.
