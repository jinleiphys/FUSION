# The relation between nuclear charge radii and other parameters characterizing the nuclear drops

> Correlation between the rms nucleus charge radius and their deformation, isospin asymmetry, mass and charge numbers are presented. Four parameters radius parametrization formula is proposed. Ratio of experimental to theoretical value distribution exhibit variation equal to 0.0044, i.e. it corresponds to the vaule of experimental errors. Observed correlations can impose interesting constraints on the form of the nuclear equation of state.

# The relation between nuclear charge radii and other parameters characterizing the nuclear drops

## Key claim
A four-parameter formula for nuclear charge radii, depending on deformation, isospin asymmetry, and proton number, reproduces experimental radii for 574 heavy nuclei (A>100) with a residual variation of 0.0044, matching the level of experimental errors. The claim is hedged by the observation that some groups of nuclei (notably terbium isotopes) deviate systematically, possibly due to experimental uncertainties rather than physics.

## Method
The author defines a reduced charge radius r = ⟨R²⟩^(1/2) / A^(1/3) and fits a parametric form r = r₀ (1+α_β·β²)(1+α_Z·(Z-50))(1+α_I·I) to experimental data for 574 nuclei with A>100. The deformation parameters β are taken from the model calculations of Moller (1995). The coefficients α_β, α_Z, α_I, and r₀ are determined by minimizing the variance of the distribution of the ratio r_exp / r_theory under the constraint that its mean equals 1.

## Key numbers
- Dataset: 574 nuclei with A > 100, using experimental charge radii from Angeli (2004)
- Fitted parameters: α_β = 0.225, α_Z = -0.00021, α_I = 0.16, r₀ = 0.9681 fm
- Residual variation of the ratio of experimental to theoretical radius: 0.0044
- The reduced radii distribution (without corrections) exhibits variations of about 3%
- Relative error of measured terbium radii with respect to the reference isotope: 0.04%

## Context
This work extends the approach of Sosin (2006) which found systematic variation of charge radii with neutron-proton asymmetry in heavy nuclei. The deformation parameters are taken from the macroscopic-microscopic model of Moller (1995). The observed correlations between radius, deformation, isospin, and proton number are proposed to constrain the nuclear equation of state, a possibility the author states will be addressed in a forthcoming publication. The paper does not resolve the origin of deviations for terbium isotopes and other outlier groups, leaving open the question of whether these reflect experimental errors or special nuclear structure properties.
