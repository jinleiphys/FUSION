# Kaon Condensation in Neutron Star Matter with Hyperons

> Based on the Kaplan-Nelson Lagrangian, we investigate kaon condensation in dense neutron star matter allowing for the explicit presence of hyperons. Using various models we find that the condensate threshold is sensitive to the behavior of the scalar density; the more rapidly it increases with baryon density, the lower is the threshold for condensation. The presence of hyperons, particularly the $Σ^-$, shifts the threshold for $K^-$ condensation to a higher density. In the mean field approach, with hyperons, the condensate amplitude grows sufficiently rapidly that the nucleon effective mass vanishes at a finite density and a satisfactory treatment of the thermodynamics cannot be achieved. Thus, calculations of kaon-baryon interactions beyond the mean field level appear to be necessary.

## Key claim

Using the Kaplan-Nelson Lagrangian, the authors find that the presence of hyperons, particularly the Σ⁻, shifts the threshold for K⁻ condensation to a higher density than in nucleons-only matter; however, in the mean field approach with hyperons, the condensate amplitude grows so rapidly that the nucleon effective mass vanishes at a finite density, making a satisfactory treatment of the thermodynamics impossible.

## Method

The authors employ the Kaplan-Nelson SU(3)×SU(3) chiral Lagrangian for kaon-baryon interactions and a relativistic field theoretical model (Walecka-type) for baryon-baryon interactions, including σ, ω, and ρ meson exchange. They treat kaons in the mean field approximation and consider baryons at both the mean field level (with nonlinear σ³ and σ⁴ terms) and the one-loop Hartree level (modified relativistic Hartree approximation, MRHA). The grand canonical potential is extremized to determine the condensate amplitude, baryon effective masses, and meson fields, with chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality imposed for cold catalyzed neutron star matter.

## Key numbers

- The threshold density for kaon condensation in nucleons-only matter is typically ~4n₀, where n₀ denotes equilibrium nuclear matter density.
- For the mean field models, the critical density ratio u_c = n_c/n₀ ranges from approximately 2.5 to 4.5 depending on the value of a₃mₛ and the model stiffness (compression moduli of 300 MeV for model A and 240 MeV for model B).
- The constant a₃mₛ is used in the range −134 to −310 MeV, corresponding to 0 to 20% strangeness content for the proton; lattice simulations give a₃mₛ = −(220 ± 40) MeV using mₛ = 150 MeV.
- The pion decay constant f = 93 MeV and the kaon mass m_K is given by m_K² = 2Cmₛ/f².
- The electron chemical potential μ_e saturates at about 200 MeV and begins to decrease with density when hyperons are present.
- The binding energy of the lowest Λ level in nuclear matter at saturation yields −28 = x_ω g_ωn ω₀ − x_σ g_σn σ (in MeV), with x_σ = 0.6 suggested from hypernuclear fits.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of kaon condensation by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and subsequent chiral perturbation theory formulations, as well as prior calculations of hyperonic matter by Glendenning (1985) and the authors' own work. The only previous work combining hyperons and kaons (Muto, 1993) considered p-wave interactions leading to quasinucleons through mixing with hyperons. The results show that mean field treatments of kaon-baryon interactions are inadequate when hyperons are present, necessitating calculations beyond the mean field level, such as inclusion of loop corrections and p-wave interactions in the Kaplan-Nelson Lagrangian.
