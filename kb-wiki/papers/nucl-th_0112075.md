# A simple approach to the angular momentum distribution in the ground states of many-body systems

> We propose a simple approach to predict the angular momentum I ground state (I g.s.) probabilities of many-body systems that does not require the diagonalization of hamiltonians with random interactions. This method is found to be applicable to {\bf all} cases that have been discussed: even and odd fermion systems (both in single-j and many-j shells), and boson (both sd and sdg) systems. A simple relation for the highest angular momentum g.s. probability is found. Furthermore, it is suggested for the first time that the 0g.s. dominance in boson systems and in even-fermion systems is given by two-body interactions with specific features.

# A simple approach to the angular momentum distribution in the ground states of many-body systems

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the angular momentum ground state (I g.s.) probabilities for many-body systems with random two-body interactions can be predicted without diagonalizing the full Hamiltonian, by instead setting each two-body matrix element to -1 in turn (with all others zero) and counting how often each angular momentum I yields the lowest eigenvalue. The method is claimed to be universal across fermion systems (single-j and many-j shells, even and odd particle numbers) and boson systems (sd and sdg), but the authors note that the microscopic origin of why certain angular momenta dominate remains unexplained.

## Method

For a system with N independent two-body interaction matrix elements, each matrix element is set to -1 in turn while all others are set to zero, and the angular momentum I that gives the lowest eigenvalue among all states is recorded. The I g.s. probability is then given by the fraction N_I/N, where N_I is the number of times angular momentum I produced the lowest eigenvalue. These predictions are compared against results from diagonalizing Hamiltonians with two-body random ensemble (TBRE) interactions, where the matrix elements are Gaussian random numbers with zero mean and unit variance.

## Key numbers

- For fermions in single-j shells with n=4, the predicted 0g.s. probabilities show staggering with j at intervals of δj=3.
- For the highest angular momentum I_max in single-j shells, the predicted I_max g.s. probability follows 1/N × 100% = 1/(j+1/2) × 100%, valid for all particle numbers (even or odd).
- For sd-boson systems, the predicted I=2n g.s. probability is 1/7 = 14.3%, consistent with previous TBRE results of approximately 15%.
- For a j=9/2 shell with n=4 (even) and n=5 (odd), the predicted I g.s. probabilities agree well with TBRE diagonalization results.
- For a system of 7 fermions in two-j shells (j1=7/2, j2=5/2) and an sd-boson system with 10 bosons, the predicted I g.s. probabilities are reasonably consistent with TBRE results.
- The method was checked for single-j shells with j up to 31/2 and n=4,5,6; two-j shells with various combinations and n=4,5,6; sd-boson systems with n up to 17; and sdg-boson systems with n=4,5,6.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Johnson et al. (1998-2000) who discovered 0g.s. dominance in even fermion systems using the TBRE, and by Bijker and Casten (1998) who confirmed this in sd-boson systems. The paper provides a simpler alternative to previous approaches that addressed only specific cases, such as the work by Bijker (2000) on eigenvalue distribution widths, Zelevinsky (2000) on geometric chaoticity of angular momentum coupling, and Zhao et al. (2000) on diagonal matrix elements. The method enables identification of which specific two-body interactions (not just monopole pairing) are crucial for 0g.s. dominance, and shows that this dominance is not an intrinsic property of the model space as previously assumed.
