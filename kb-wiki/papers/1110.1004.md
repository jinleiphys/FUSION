# Impact of nuclear effects in the measurement of neutrino oscillation parameters

> In recent years the experimental study of neutrino oscillations has much contributed to our knowledge of particle physics by establishing non vanishing neutrino masses and by measuring or constraining the corresponding mixing angles. Within the domain of neutrino oscillations, the main goal of the next generation of facilities is the measurement of the mixing angle theta13 and the observation of leptonic CP violation, for which we have no hints at the moment. We discuss more in detail how various models of neutrino-nucleus cross section affect the forecasted precision measurement of theta13 and the CP violating phase delta.

## Key claim

The precision with which future neutrino facilities can measure the mixing angle θ₁₃ and the CP-violating phase δ depends strongly on which nuclear model is used to describe neutrino-nucleus charged-current quasielastic cross sections; the commonly used Relativistic Fermi Gas model yields sensitivity estimates that are up to a factor of 2 better than more sophisticated nuclear models such as the Spectral Function, Relativistic Mean Field, or Random Phase Approximation approaches.

## Method

The author computed total charged-current quasielastic cross sections for ν_μ on ¹⁶O (and the corresponding antineutrino process) using five nuclear models: the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG), Spectral Function (SF), Relativistic Mean Field (RMF), Random Phase Approximation (RPA), and RPA with two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) contributions. These cross sections were then used as inputs to simulate the sensitivity of a γ=100 β-Beam facility to θ₁₃ and δ, by evaluating the CP discovery potential and θ₁₃ discovery potential at 3σ significance after marginalizing over other oscillation parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the RFG model of oxygen, the Fermi momentum is p_F = 225 MeV and the average binding energy is E_B = 25 MeV, as determined from electron scattering data.
- The RFG prediction for the total QE cross section on ¹⁶O overestimates the SF, RMF, and RPA results by roughly 15% in the energy range E_ν ~ [0, 0.75] GeV.
- For δ_CP ~ ±90°, the RFG model gives a CP discovery potential around a factor of 2 better than the SF, RMF, and RPA models in sin²2θ₁₃, and around 40% better than the RPA-2p2h model.
- For θ₁₃ sensitivity at δ_CP ~ ±90°, the SF, RMF, and RPA predictions differ by up to a factor of ~60% compared to the RFG model.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fernandez-Martinez (2010) by systematically comparing five nuclear models for neutrino-nucleus cross sections and their impact on oscillation parameter extraction. The results contradict the implicit assumption in many earlier sensitivity studies that the RFG model (Smith 1972) is adequate for precision measurements; instead, they show that more realistic models like the Spectral Function approach (Benhar 2005) or RPA with 2p2h contributions (Martini 2009) produce significantly different sensitivity estimates. This enables future experimental collaborations to incorporate nuclear model uncertainties into their sensitivity projections and motivates further development of cross-section models that can reproduce MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010).
