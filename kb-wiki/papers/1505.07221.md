# Lambda Polarization in an Exact Rotating and Expanding fluid dynamical model for peripheral heavy ion reactions

> We calculate the Lambda polarization in an exact analytical, rotating model based on parameters extracted from a high resolution (3+1)D Particle-in-Cell Relativistic hydrodynamics calculation. The polarization is attributed to effects from thermal vorticity and for the first time the effects of the radial and axial acceleration are also studied separately

## Key claim

The paper calculates Lambda polarization in an exact analytical rotating and expanding fluid dynamical model, finding that polarization arises from thermal vorticity and, for the first time, separately from radial and axial acceleration effects. The authors qualify that while average polarization may be as low as 1-2%, consistent with RHIC bounds, in some regions of momentum space the polarization can reach about 5% for momenta in the transverse plane up to 3 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors calibrate an exact analytical rotating and expanding fluid dynamical model using parameters extracted from a (3+1)D Particle-in-Cell Relativistic hydrodynamics (PICR) calculation for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(S_NN)=2.76 A TeV with impact parameter b=0.7 b_Max. They compute the Lambda polarization by evaluating the thermal vorticity and acceleration terms in the quantum-field-theoretical expression for polarization, integrating over the fireball volume with a Fermi-Juttner distribution and assuming a non-relativistic limit where gamma=1. The model uses scaling variables with a linear velocity profile in radial and axial directions, with initial conditions R_0=2.5 fm, Y_0=4.0 fm, dot R_0=0.20 c, dot Y_0=0.25 c, omega_0=0.1 c/fm, kappa=3/2, and T_0=300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial angular momentum magnitude: approximately 1.45 x 10^4 hbar
- Thermal vorticity at t=0.5 fm/c: hbar (nabla x beta) = -0.13 (dimensionless)
- Polarization from the first term (vorticity contribution): constant value of hbar epsilon omega/(4mT) in the y-direction
- Polarization from the second term (acceleration contribution): up to 20% in the corners at p_x=-4 GeV/c, and 12% at p_x=+4 GeV/c
- Total y-component of polarization: -1.5% at CM-momentum (p_x=p_y=0), reaching -16% in the corners
- Time derivatives of velocity components at t=0.5 fm/c and r=1 fm: (hbar/c) partial_t beta_r = 0.024, (hbar/c) partial_t beta_phi = 0.009
- Vorticity decreases slowly with time: about 1-2% per 1 fm/c for the first term, about 0.0005 per 1 fm/c for the acceleration components

## Context

This work extends the exact rotating and expanding fluid dynamical model developed by Csernai and collaborators (CsN14, CsWCs14-2) and builds on previous calculations of energy-weighted thermal vorticity (CsI15). The authors note that Kelvin-Helmholtz instabilities may further enhance rotation and thermal vorticity by 10-20%, and that at LHC energies there may be 5% Lambda polarization due to the corona effect from single nucleon-nucleon collisions outside the reaction zone. The calculation enables experimental searches for Lambda polarization in peripheral heavy ion collisions, particularly for particles emitted in the +/- x direction in the reaction plane, as opposed to earlier RHIC measurements that averaged over all azimuthal angles (BCsW13).
