# Bulk and decay properties of neutron-deficient odd-mass Hg isotopes around A = 185

> Ground and isomeric states of the neutron-deficient odd-$A$ isotopes $^{183}$Hg, $^{185}$Hg, and $^{187}$Hg are described from a microscopic calculation based on a self-consistent, axially-deformed Hartree-Fock mean field with the Skyrme functional and pairing within BCS approximation. For each equilibrium shape and different odd-neutron states, results on mean square charge radii and magnetic dipole moments are given and analyzed in the context of their sensitivity to the nuclear deformation and to the spin and parity. Spin-isospin correlations within proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation are then introduced in the nuclear states to obtain the distributions of Gamow-Teller strength and the $β^+/EC$ half-lives of these isotopes, whose measurements are planned at ISOLDE-CERN using total absorption gamma-ray spectroscopy techniques.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives of neutron-deficient odd-mass Hg isotopes (A=183, 185, 187) carry clear signatures of nuclear deformation that are robust against changes in the Skyrme interaction parametrization, but the actual hedge is that the interpretation in terms of pure shapes is difficult for these transitional nuclei and a more elaborated mixing scenario may be necessary once experimental data become available.

## Method

The authors performed axially-deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy4 Skyrme functional and BCS pairing to obtain energy-deformation curves and equilibrium shapes for the even-even cores of 183Hg, 185Hg, and 187Hg. Odd-neutron states were constructed as one-quasiparticle states upon the even-even core, and spin-isospin correlations were introduced through a deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) with separable particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-plus/electron capture half-lives.

## Key numbers

- For 183Hg ground state (1/2-): calculated half-life 22.0 s (prolate, beta=+0.27) and 9.3 s (oblate, beta=-0.18); experimental half-life 10.6(8) s
- For 185Hg ground state (1/2-): calculated half-life 64.8 s (prolate, beta=+0.25) and 17.8 s (oblate, beta=-0.19); experimental half-life 52.2(12) s
- For 187Hg ground state (3/2-): calculated half-life 375.3 s (prolate, beta=+0.19) and 80.4 s (oblate, beta=-0.17); experimental half-life 114(18) s
- For 185Hg 13/2+ isomeric state (oblate, beta=-0.15): calculated magnetic dipole moment -1.28 mu_N; experimental value -1.017(9) mu_N
- Experimental Q_EC values: 6.387 MeV for 183Hg, 5.674 MeV for 185Hg, 4.910 MeV for 187Hg
- The quenching factor used for the axial coupling is Q = 0.77, with (g_A/g_V) = -1.276

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent mean-field calculations by Sarriguren et al. (2005) and Moreno et al. (2006) that showed beta-decay properties in this mass region carry signatures of nuclear deformation. The calculations are motivated by planned measurements at ISOLDE-CERN using total absorption gamma-ray spectroscopy (TAGS) techniques, following the successful measurement of 186Hg by Algora et al. (2021). The results provide the theoretical framework for interpreting future isomer-selective beta-decay measurements that could distinguish between prolate and oblate shapes in the same nucleus, building on earlier experimental work on shape coexistence in Hg isotopes by Julin et al. (2001) and Andreyev et al. (2000).
