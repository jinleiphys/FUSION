# Gravitational wave spectrum from first-order QCD phase transitions based on a parity doublet model

> We investigate the gravitational wave spectrum from first-order QCD phase transitions using the parity doublet model at finite baryon chemical potential. The model incorporates the chiral invariant mass $m_0$, representing the portion of nucleon mass that persists even when chiral symmetry is restored. Within the model, we identify two first-order phase transition regions: the nuclear liquid--gas transition and the chiral phase transition. By solving the bounce equation and computing the Euclidean action $S_3/T$, we obtain the gravitational wave spectra from both transitions. The liquid--gas transition yields $α\sim \mathcal{O}(1)$ and $β/H \sim \mathcal{O}(10)$--$\mathcal{O}(100)$ near the endpoint of the first-order line, producing signals with peak frequencies from the millihertz to the nanohertz band that can fit the existing data. In contrast, the chiral transition produces signals suppressed by approximately five orders of magnitude, well below the sensitivity of all current and planned detectors. These results connect the chiral invariant mass to the gravitational wave spectrum, offering a novel probe of the origin of nucleon mass through gravitational wave astronomy.

## Key claim
Using the parity doublet model with chiral invariant mass \(m_0 = 800\) MeV, the authors find that the nuclear liquid–gas transition can produce gravitational wave signals with peak frequencies from millihertz to nanohertz that fit existing pulsar timing array data, while the chiral transition yields signals suppressed by approximately five orders of magnitude below all current and planned detector sensitivities.

## Method
The authors compute the finite-temperature thermodynamic potential of the parity doublet model at finite baryon chemical potential, identify two first-order phase transition regions (nuclear liquid–gas and chiral), and solve the \(O(3)\)-symmetric bounce equation to obtain the Euclidean action \(S_3/T\) for bubble nucleation. From the nucleation temperature \(T_n\), they extract the inverse duration parameter \(\beta/H\) and the transition strength \(\alpha\), then compute the gravitational wave spectrum using standard numerical fits for sound waves, MHD turbulence, and bubble collisions.

## Key numbers
- Liquid–gas transition yields \(\alpha \sim \mathcal{O}(1)\) and \(\beta/H \sim \mathcal{O}(10)\)–\(\mathcal{O}(100)\) near the endpoint of the first-order line.
- Chiral transition produces signals suppressed by approximately five orders of magnitude relative to the liquid–gas transition.
- The chiral invariant mass is fixed at \(m_0 = 800\) MeV, consistent with neutron star observations from NICER and LIGO/Virgo.
- Nucleation temperature \(T_n\) is determined from the condition \(\Gamma(t)/H^4 \sim 1\) with reduced Planck mass \(m_p = 2.435 \times 10^{18}\) GeV.
- Bubble wall velocity is taken as the Jouguet detonation velocity \(v_w = v_J = (\sqrt{1/3} + \sqrt{\alpha^2 + 2\alpha/3})/(1 + \alpha)\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of gravitational waves from first-order QCD phase transitions using effective models such as the Friedberg-Lee model and the quark-meson/Polyakov-quark-meson models (Wang 2023, Shao 2024), which typically found large \(\beta/H \sim 10^4\)–\(10^5\) at high temperatures with small chemical potential. By incorporating the parity doublet model at finite baryon chemical potential, the authors connect the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\), a fundamental parameter related to the origin of nucleon mass, to observable gravitational wave spectra, enabling a novel probe of QCD matter through gravitational wave astronomy. The results also link to neutron star constraints from NICER and LIGO/Virgo (Minamikawa 2020, Gao 2024, Gao 2025).
