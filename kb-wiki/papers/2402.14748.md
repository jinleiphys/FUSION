# TMD factorisation for diffractive jets in photon-nucleus interactions

> Using the colour dipole picture and the colour glass condensate effective theory, we study the diffractive production of two or three jets via coherent photon-nucleus interactions at high energy. We consider the hard regime where the photon virtuality and/or the transverse momenta of the produced jets are much larger than the saturation momentum $Q_s$ of the nuclear target. We show that, despite this hardness, the leading-twist contributions are controlled by relatively large parton configurations, with transverse sizes $R\sim 1/Q_s$, which undergo strong scattering and probe gluon saturation. For exclusive dijets, this implies that both final jets have semi-hard transverse momenta ($P_\perp\sim Q_s$) and that one of them is aligned with the photon. The dominant contributions to the diffractive production of hard dijets ($P_\perp\gg Q_s$) rather come from three-jet final states, which are very asymmetric and will be referred to as 2+1 jets: two of the jets are hard, while the third one is semi-hard. We demonstrate that the leading-twist contributions to both exclusive dijets and the diffractive production of 2+1 jets admit transverse-momentum dependent (TMD) factorisation, in terms of quark and gluon diffractive TMD distribution functions, for which we obtain explicit expressions from first principles. We show that the contribution of 2+1 jets to diffractive SIDIS (semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering) takes the form of one step in the DGLAP evolution of the quark diffractive PDF.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that, despite the presence of hard scales (photon virtuality or jet transverse momenta much larger than the saturation momentum Q_s), the leading-twist contributions to diffractive jet production in photon-nucleus interactions are controlled by relatively large parton configurations of transverse size R ~ 1/Q_s that undergo strong scattering and probe gluon saturation, and that these contributions admit transverse-momentum dependent (TMD) factorisation.

## Method
The authors use the colour dipole picture and the colour glass condensate (CGC) effective theory to study diffractive production of two or three jets via coherent photon-nucleus interactions at high energy. They construct light-cone wavefunctions for the q\bar{q} and q\bar{q}g fluctuations of the virtual photon, compute cross-sections for elastic scattering off the nuclear target, and isolate the leading-twist contributions in the regime where the photon virtuality Q^2 and/or jet transverse momenta P_perp are much larger than Q_s^2. They demonstrate TMD factorisation by rewriting the cross-sections in terms of quark and gluon diffractive TMD distribution functions, which they compute from first principles using the dipole scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The saturation momentum Q_s of the nuclear target sets the semi-hard scale; typical transverse momenta of exclusive dijets are P_perp ~ Q_s.
- For hard diffractive dijets with P_perp >> Q_s, the exclusive q\bar{q} contribution is suppressed as 1/P_perp^6 (a higher-twist contribution), while the 2+1 jet contribution scales as 1/P_perp^4 (leading-twist).
- The transverse momentum transferred by the target Delta_perp ~ 1/R_A, with R_A ~ A^{1/3} * 1.1 fm; for A ~ 200 (Pb or Au), Delta_perp ~ 30 MeV.
- The longitudinal momentum fraction of the soft parton in aligned jet configurations is theta_1 ~ Q_s^2/Q^2 << 1.

## Context
This work extends the seminal analysis of Dominguez et al. (2011) on the relation between the CGC approach and collinear factorisation for inclusive dijet production, now applied to diffractive processes. It builds on earlier studies of diffractive structure functions by Wusthoff (1997), Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1999), and others, which showed that the leading-twist contribution to F_2^D at high Q^2 is controlled by saturation. The paper provides the first explicit proof of universality for the quark and gluon diffractive TMDs introduced in recent works by Hatta et al. (2022) and Iancu et al. (2021, 2022), and shows how the DGLAP evolution of diffractive PDFs emerges from the dipole picture. This enables first-principles calculations of diffractive observables relevant for the Electron-Ion Collider and ultra-peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC.
