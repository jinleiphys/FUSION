# Net-baryon multiplicity distribution consistent with lattice QCD

> We determine the net-baryon multiplicity distribution which reproduces all cumulants measured so far by lattice QCD. We present the dependence on the volume and temperature of this distribution. We find that for temperatures and volumes encountered in heavy ion reactions, the multiplicity distribution is very close to the Skellam distribution, making the experimental determination of it rather challenging. We further provide estimates for the statistics required to measure cumulants of the net-baryon and net-proton distributions.

## Key claim

The net-baryon multiplicity distribution consistent with all lattice QCD cumulants up to eighth order is very close to a Skellam distribution for temperatures and volumes typical of heavy ion collisions, making experimental determination of the actual distribution rather challenging.

## Method

The authors used the cluster expansion model (CEM) of Vovchenko et al. (2017), which reproduces all net-baryon number cumulants determined by lattice QCD up to eighth order, to extract the net-baryon multiplicity distribution via a Fourier transform of the fugacity expansion of the QCD pressure. They computed the distribution for two temperatures (T=155 MeV and T=180 MeV) and two volumes (V=5280 fm³ for LHC and V=2100 fm³ for RHIC), then compared the resulting distributions to Skellam distributions with the same variance.

- For T=155 MeV and RHIC volume (VT³=1020), the deviation from Skellam is at best 20% at probability values as small as P(𝒩)≃10⁻¹⁵
- To obtain K₆/K₂ within 5% at T=155 MeV for LHC conditions, one must sum the distribution up to 𝒩=118, where P(𝒩)=2.3×10⁻¹³
- For LHC conditions, at least 2.7×10⁸ events are needed to measure K₄ at 10% accuracy, and 5.3×10¹⁵ events for K₆

- For net-protons at LHC, 4.8×10⁶ events are required for K₄ᵖ and 1.8×10¹² events for K₆ᵖ at 10% accuracy
- For net-protons at RHIC, 2.5×10⁶ events are required for K₄ᵖ and 4.9×10¹¹ events for K₆ᵖ at 10% accuracy

## Context

This work extends the cluster expansion model of Vovchenko et al. (2017) and lattice QCD results from Bazavov et al. (2017) and Borsanyi et al. (2018) to construct the full net-baryon multiplicity distribution, which had not been directly extracted from lattice QCD. The results contradict the expectation that higher-order cumulant deviations from Skellam would produce visibly different multiplicity distributions; instead, the distributions are nearly indistinguishable from Skellam. The authors note that an alternative virial expansion by Almasi et al. (2018) gives nearly identical multiplicity distributions despite different asymptotic behavior of virial coefficients. This work enables realistic estimates of the statistics required for experimental measurements of net-baryon and net-proton cumulants at RHIC and LHC, and provides guidance on how far into the tails of the distribution one must measure to obtain accurate cumulants.
