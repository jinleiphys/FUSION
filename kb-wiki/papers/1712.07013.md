# Fast neutrino flavor conversion: roles of dense matter and spectrum crossing

> The flavor conversion of a neutrino usually occurs at densities $\lesssim G_F^{-1} ω$, whether in the ordinary matter or the neutrino medium, and on time/distance scales of order $ω^{-1}$, where $G_F$ is the Fermi weak coupling constant and $ω$ is the vacuum oscillation frequency of the neutrino. In 2005 Sawyer and more recently both he and other groups have shown that neutrino flavor conversions can occur on scales much shorter than $ω^{-1}$ in a very dense, anisotropic neutrino gas such as that in a core-collapse supernova or a binary neutron star merger. It has also been suggested that the fast neutrino flavor conversion require a crossing in the electron lepton number (ELN) angular distribution or spectrum. We study a few simple analytical models with which we elucidate the roles of the dense matter and the ELN spectrum crossing in neutrino fast flavor conversions. We show that a large matter density can induce fast neutrino flavor conversions in certain astrophysical scenarios such as at the early epoch of a core-collapse supernova.

## Key claim

Fast neutrino flavor conversions can occur even in the presence of large matter densities, contrary to earlier claims that dense matter suppresses such instabilities; the multi-angle matter effect that was previously thought to suppress collective neutrino oscillations can instead induce fast flavor conversions when the electron lepton number (ELN) angular distribution has a crossing.

## Method

The authors performed linear flavor-stability analysis on simplified analytical models, including a four-beam neutrino model and an outward-flowing, axially symmetric neutrino flux, by solving the linearized equation of motion for small perturbations to the neutrino density matrix. They derived characteristic equations for collective oscillation frequencies and identified conditions for exponential growth (instability) by examining the effective ELN spectra in transformed coordinates, relating the results back to the bipolar model and the multi-bulb supernova model of Sawyer (2015).

## Key numbers

- In the bipolar model, flavor instability exists for neutrino potential μ in the range (μ₋, μ₊) where μ₊ = |ω₁−ω₂|/(√|g₁|−√|g₂|)² and μ₋ = |ω₁−ω₂|/(√|g₁|+√|g₂|)², with ω₁ and ω₂ being vacuum oscillation frequencies and g₁, g₂ the ELN coefficients.
- For the four-beam model with δm²→0, the exponential growth rate κ of the symmetric mode is of order λ (the matter potential), which can be much larger than vacuum oscillation frequencies.
- In Sawyer's multi-bulb model with R_νe = 15 km, R_νeb = 0.93×15 km, R_νx = 0.8×15 km, and n_νeb,0 = 10³² cm⁻³, the instability growth rate was calculated to be κ = 0.32 m⁻¹ on the surface of the νₑ sphere.
- The criterion for instability in Sawyer's model was given as R_νeb/R_νe > 0.44 + 0.55(R_νx/R_νe).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fast neutrino flavor conversions first envisioned by Sawyer (2005, 2008) and later studied in multi-bulb models by Sawyer (2015), Chakraborty et al (2016), and Dasgupta et al (2016). It contradicts the conclusion of Dasgupta et al (2016) that background matter suppresses fast instabilities in space, showing instead that large matter density can induce fast oscillations in certain astrophysical scenarios such as the early epoch of a core-collapse supernova. The paper provides an explicit proof that a crossed ELN angular distribution is necessary for fast oscillations in an outward-flowing, axially symmetric neutrino flux, but also demonstrates that fast oscillations can occur without a crossed angular distribution when both inward and outward flowing neutrino fluxes are present.
