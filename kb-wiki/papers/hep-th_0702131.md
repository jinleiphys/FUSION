# Gluon Condensation at Finite Temperature via AdS/CFT

> We consider gluon condensation (GC) at finite temperature using AdS/CFT. We first show that in the presence of regular horizon, the GC is forbidden in high temperature. Then we consider gravity back-reaction to dilaton coupling and show that the back-reaction develops an singularity, and non-vanishing value of gluon condensation is allowed. We also study thermodynamic quantities and the trace anomaly in the presence of the GC. We discuss how to define a temperature in the presence of the singularity which forbids Hawking temperature. Finally we describe the thermodynamics of the gluon condensation including the effect of the Hawking-Page transition.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a simple hard-wall AdS/CFT model, gluon condensation is forced to zero at high temperature by the regularity condition at the black hole horizon; however, when gravity back-reaction to the dilaton is included, the metric develops an essential singularity that cancels the IR divergence in the action, thereby allowing a non-zero gluon condensation even in the deconfined phase.

## Method

The authors construct a two-parameter dilatonic black hole solution in 5D Einstein-dilaton gravity that interpolates between the zero-temperature dilaton-wall solution (Csaki and Reese, 2007) and the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole. They compute the total Euclidean action, renormalize it by subtracting the pure AdS contribution, and derive thermodynamic quantities (free energy, energy density, pressure, entropy, trace anomaly) as functions of the temperature parameter \(a\) and the condensation parameter \(c\). The temperature is identified via the leading \(z^4\) coefficient of the metric expansion, which follows the Stefan-Boltzmann law, even though the metric has an essential singularity that prevents defining a Hawking temperature. The Hawking-Page transition between the thermal dilatonic AdS and the dilatonic black hole is analyzed to determine the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

* The hard-wall model predicts a jump of the gluon condensation from a constant finite value to zero at the critical temperature \(T = 2^{1/4} / (\pi z_m)\), where \(z_m\) is the IR cutoff.
* The dilatonic black hole metric has a singularity at \(z_f = f^{-1/4}\), with \(f^2 = a^2 + c^2\).
* The energy density in the presence of gluon condensation is \(\rho(T,\Lambda) = \frac{3\pi^2 N_c^2}{8} \frac{(T^8 - \frac{1}{3}\Lambda^8)}{\sqrt{T^8 + \Lambda^8}}\), where \(a = (\pi T)^4/4\) and \(c = (\pi \Lambda)^4/4\).
* The pressure is \(p = \frac{\pi^2 N_c^2}{8} \sqrt{T^8 + \Lambda^8}\).
* The trace anomaly is \(\rho - 3p = -4\alpha \frac{c^2}{f} < 0\), which at large temperature behaves as \(\sim -1/T^4\).
* The entropy density is \(s = \frac{\pi^2}{2} N_c^2 T^3 \cdot \frac{T^4}{\sqrt{T^8 + \Lambda^8}}\).
* The critical temperature for the Hawking-Page transition, when \(c \ll c_0\), is \(T_c \simeq \sqrt{2} \Lambda\), with \(c_0 = (\pi \Lambda)^4/4\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature dilaton-wall solution of Csaki and Reese (2007) to finite temperature by including gravity back-reaction, and contrasts with the hard-wall model of Polchinski and Strassler (2002) and the AdS/QCD approach. The result that gluon condensation is non-zero in the deconfined phase qualitatively matches lattice data of Miller (2004) for pure Yang-Mills, unlike the hard-wall model which predicts a jump to zero. The paper enables future studies of thermodynamics with multiple scales (temperature, condensation, and possible additional IR cutoffs) and suggests that the essential singularity in the dual gravity description signals incomplete thermalization when two independent scales coexist.
