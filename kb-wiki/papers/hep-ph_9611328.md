# Low-mass dileptons at the SPS

> We use a simple QCD-based model to study particle production in S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/n. A requisite consistency is met for the hadronic observables (pi0 and pi- spectra) while pursuing estimates for e+e- production. Since radiative decays of initially produced hadrons has accounted for only a portion of the observed dileptons at the CERN SPS, we search for additional mechanisms. By including contributions from prompt secondary hadronic scatterings, pi rho -> pi e+e-, and adding to pi+ pi- annihilation and hadronic decays, the ``excess'' dilepton signal can possibly be interpreted.

## Key claim

By including contributions from prompt secondary hadronic scatterings (πρ → πe+e−) in addition to π+π− annihilation and hadronic decays, the "excess" dilepton signal observed in S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/n can possibly be interpreted, though the model cannot rule out other explanations such as medium modifications of the rho mass.

## Method

The authors developed a QCD-based event generator similar to HIJING, using PYTHIA and JETSET for nucleon-nucleon collisions with Glauber-type geometry to determine collision scaling. Primary scattering produced hadrons whose radiative decays provided a baseline dilepton yield, while secondary scattering was modeled by allowing pions and rhos formed in primary collisions to undergo both resonant (π+π− → ρ0 → e+e−, πρ → a1 → πe+e−) and non-resonant (π0ρ± → π±e+e−) interactions. The non-resonant contribution was computed using an effective Lagrangian with gauge-invariant form factors, and a Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal reduction factor of 1−e−Mλ was applied with λ ≈ 1 fm.

## Key numbers

- The CERES data for S+Au at 200 GeV/n showed an excess over the "cocktail" prediction by a factor of 5 ± 2 when integrated over pair invariant mass up to 1.5 GeV.
- The non-resonant πρ → πe+e− cross section was parameterized as σ(√s) = 1.8 mb + (8/s²) mb·GeV⁴ + 0.5 s (mb/GeV²) for √s ≤ 2.5 GeV.
- The ρ0 full decay width was set to 152 MeV for the π+π− channel; the a1 full decay width was 400 MeV.
- The form factor cutoff parameter Λ was set to 1 GeV, with m set to mρ.
- The largest excess in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum was observed between 200 and 500 MeV (0.2 GeV ≤ M² ≤ 0.5 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the investigation of dilepton production at the CERN SPS, where the CERES collaboration (Agakichiev et al., 1995) had found a significant excess in S+Au collisions beyond predictions from primary hadronic decays. The authors build on prior studies of secondary scattering by Haglin (1996), who examined the role of the a1 resonance through πρ → a1 → πe+e− but found it insufficient. The non-resonant πρ → πe+e− mechanism was suggested as an additional source, complementing alternative explanations such as a shifted rho mass (Li, Ko, and Brown, 1995), modified pion dispersion relations (Song et al., 1996), or a shifted η′ mass (Kapusta, Kharzeev, and McLerran, 1995). The results enable further investigation of whether secondary scattering or medium modifications are responsible for the observed excess, and motivate more detailed calculations with full T-matrix approaches.
