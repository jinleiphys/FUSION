# Phenomenological High Precision Neutron-Proton Delta-Shell Potential

> We provide a successful fit for proton-neutron scattering below pion production threshold up to LAB energies of 350 MeV. We use seven high-quality fits based on potentials with different forms as a measure of the systematic uncertainty. We represent the interaction as a sum of delta-shells in configuration space below the 3fm and a charge dependent one pion exchange potential above 3fm together with magnetic and vacuum polarization effects. Special attention is paid to estimate the errors of the phenomenological interaction.

## Key claim

The paper provides a high-quality fit to neutron-proton scattering data up to 350 MeV laboratory energy using a delta-shell potential with a minimal number of sampling points, but the authors note that the systematic errors are generally larger than those estimated because a definitive assessment would require testing all possible potential forms.

## Method

The interaction is represented as a sum of delta-shells in configuration space for radii below 3 fm, combined with a charge-dependent one-pion exchange potential and electromagnetic effects (including magnetic and vacuum polarization) for radii above 3 fm. The authors fit to the mean phase-shift values at 11 laboratory energies (1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, 200, 250, 300, 350 MeV) using a compilation of the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and six high-quality potentials (Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, Reid93, AV18, CD-Bonn, and Gross) as a measure of systematic uncertainty. The delta-shell parameters are determined with N=5 equidistant sampling points at spacing Δr = 0.6 fm, and errors are obtained from the standard covariance matrix.

## Key numbers

- Fits cover laboratory energies up to 350 MeV, below pion production threshold
- N=5 equidistant delta-shell sampling points with Δr = 0.6 fm between origin and r_c = 3 fm
- χ²/d.o.f. values for individual partial waves range from 0.0088 (¹P₁) to 1.8670 (³G₄) for J ≤ 5
- Overall χ²/d.o.f. = 0.78 when fitting to the mean Wolfenstein parameters averaged over the 11 reference energies
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2(2) MeV (empirical: 2.224575(9) MeV)
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio η = 0.025(2) (empirical: 0.0256(5))
- Deuteron asymptotic S-wave normalization A_S = 0.88(3) fm^{1/2} (empirical: 0.8781(44) fm^{1/2})
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.272(9) fm² (empirical: 0.2859(3) fm²)
- Deuteron D-state probability P_D = 5.7(2)% (empirical: 5.67(4)%)

## Context

This work extends the coarse-grained interaction approach previously developed by Perez, Amaro, and Ruiz Arriola (2011) and connects to the V_low k approach of Bogner, Kuo, and Schwenk (2003) in which effective interactions are built by eliminating momenta above Λ ∼ √(M_N m_π). The delta-shell representation was originally suggested by Aviles (1973) and provides a minimal sampling that avoids statistical correlations between potential strengths, enabling straightforward error propagation in nuclear structure calculations. The paper enables future applications to nuclear structure calculations where the error estimates can be propagated directly, and the method can be extended to proton-proton scattering by coarse-graining long-range Coulomb interactions.
