# On the symmetry improved CJT formalism in the $O(4)$ linear sigma model

> By using the symmetry improved CJT effective formalism developed by Pilaftsis and Teresi, the chiral phase transition is reconsidered in the framework of the $O(4)$ linear sigma model in chiral limit. Our results confirm the restorations of the second-order phase transition and the Goldstone theorem in the Hartree approximation. Finally, we explicitly calculate the effective potentials via the order parameter for various temperatures and address advantages of the present method in comparison with the $O(N)$ model in large-$N$ approximation.

# On the symmetry improved CJT formalism in the $O(4)$ linear sigma model

## Key claim

The symmetry improved CJT formalism, when applied to the $O(4)$ linear sigma model in the chiral limit within the Hartree approximation, restores both a second-order chiral phase transition and the Goldstone theorem (massless pions in the broken phase), but only if a constraint ($\phi M_\pi^2 = 0$) is artificially inserted into the stationarity equations, unlike the large-$N$ approximation where this constraint emerges automatically.

## Method

The authors apply the symmetry improved Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) effective formalism developed by Pilaftsis and Teresi (2013) to the $O(4)$ linear sigma model in the chiral limit at finite temperature, using the imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalism. They solve the system of mass-gap equations in the Hartree approximation with the additional constraint $M_\pi^2 = 0$ in the symmetry-broken phase, using a Newton-Raphson numerical method, and compute the effective potential by integrating with respect to the order parameter. Renormalization is performed using the counter-term scheme of Lenaghan (1999).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral phase transition: $T_c = \sqrt{2} f_\pi \simeq 131.5$ MeV, obtained from the high-temperature limit where all particle masses vanish.
- Sigma mass at zero temperature: $m_\sigma = 500$ MeV (input parameter).
- Pion decay constant at zero temperature: $f_\pi = 93$ MeV (input parameter).
- Coupling constant in chiral limit: $\lambda = 3 m_\sigma^2 / f_\pi^2$.
- Negative mass parameter in chiral limit: $-2 m^2 = m_\sigma^2 > 0$.
- Renormalization scale chosen as $\mu^2 \equiv m_\sigma^2 / e$ to match vacuum masses.
- In the large-$N$ approximation, the critical temperature is $T'_c = \sqrt{3} f_\pi \approx 161$ MeV, higher than the symmetry improved CJT result.

## Context

This work extends the symmetry improved CJT formalism, previously applied to the $O(2)$ scalar model by Pilaftsis and Teresi (2013), to the more phenomenologically relevant $O(4)$ linear sigma model. It directly addresses the long-standing problems in the traditional CJT formalism, where the Hartree approximation yields a first-order phase transition and massive pions in the broken phase, contradicting the universality arguments of Pisarski (1983) for $N_f=2$ QCD. The results are compared with the large-$N$ approximation of the $O(N)$ model (Petropoulos 1998, Lenaghan 1999), which also gives a second-order transition and massless pions but neglects sigma contributions and yields a different critical temperature. The symmetry improved CJT formalism preserves sigma contributions and satisfies the Goldstone theorem at any truncation order, enabling more reliable studies of phenomena such as pion string formation in early universe and heavy-ion collisions.
