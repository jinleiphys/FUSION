# The dependence of information entropy of uniform Fermi systems on correlations and thermal effects

> The influence of correlations of uniform Fermi systems (nuclear matter, electron gas and liquid $^3$He) on Shannon's information entropy, $S$, is studied. $S$ is the sum of the information entropies in position and momentum spaces. It is found that, for three different Fermi systems with different particle interactions, the correlated part of $S$ ($S_{cor}$) depends on the correlation parameter of the systems or on the discontinuity gap of the momentum distribution through two parameter expressions. The values of the parameters characterize the strength of the correlations. A two parameter expression also holds between $S_{cor}$ and the mean kinetic energy ($K$) of the Fermi system. The study of thermal effects on the uncorrelated electron gas leads to a relation between the thermal part of $S$ ($S_{thermal}$) and the fundamental quantities of temperature, thermodynamical entropy and the mean kinetic energy. It is found that, in the case of low temperature limit, the expression connecting $S_{thermal}$ with $K$ is the same to the one which connects $S_{cor}$ with $K$. There are only some small differences on the values of the parameters. Thus, regardless of the reason (correlations or thermal) that changes $K$, $S$ takes almost the same value.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for three different uniform Fermi systems (nuclear matter, electron gas, and liquid ³He), the correlated part of Shannon's information entropy sum depends on correlation parameters or the discontinuity gap of the momentum distribution through simple two-parameter expressions, and that thermal effects on the uncorrelated electron gas produce a similar relation between the thermal part of the entropy and the mean kinetic energy as that found for correlations, but only in the low temperature limit where the expressions are approximately the same with small differences in the parameter values.

## Method

The authors calculated the momentum distributions for nuclear matter using the low order approximation (LOA) with a Jastrow correlation function, for the electron gas using the convex Kulik function based on accurate calculations for r_s values from 1 to 5, and for liquid ³He using results from diffusion Monte Carlo simulations with optimized trial functions. They then computed Shannon's information entropy sum S = S_r + S_k from these momentum distributions and the constant density distributions, extracting the correlated part S_cor by subtracting the ideal Fermi gas entropy S_0. For thermal effects, they applied the Fermi-Dirac distribution to the uncorrelated electron gas in both the low temperature (T << T_F) and high temperature (T >> T_F) limits.

## Key numbers

- For nuclear matter, the correlation parameter β was in the interval 1.01 ≤ β ≤ 2.482, corresponding to wound parameter k_dir values from 0.3 down to 0.02
- For nuclear matter, S_cor(k_dir) fit parameters: s = 2.0575, λ = 0.6364
- For nuclear matter, S_cor(Z_F) fit parameters: s = 2.2766, λ = 0.6164
- For nuclear matter, S_cor(K) fit parameters: s = 3.7413, λ = 1.5911
- For electron gas, S_cor(r_s) fit parameters: s = 0.1312, λ = 0.8648
- For electron gas, S_cor(Z_F) fit parameters: s = 2.0381, λ = 1.6899
- For electron gas, S_cor(K) fit parameters: s = 2.0786, λ = 0.6601
- For liquid ³He, S_cor(ρ) fit parameters: s = 2032.56, λ = 1.4757
- For liquid ³He, S_cor(Z_F) fit parameters: s = 13.0640, λ = 0.2070
- For liquid ³He, S_cor(K) fit parameters: s = 3.0993, λ = 0.5236
- For the electron gas at low temperature, S_thermal = α(T/T_F) with α = 2.5466
- For the electron gas at low temperature, the relation between S_thermal and K gives s = 1.6212, λ = 0.5
- The lower bound for the information entropy sum for a three-dimensional system is S ≥ 3(1 + ln π) ≈ 6.434

## Context

This work extends previous studies of information entropy in nuclei, atomic clusters, and correlated bosonic systems by Moustakidis and Massen to uniform Fermi systems, and is consistent with the earlier finding by Gadre (1984) and Massen (1998) that the entropy sum for ideal infinite Fermi systems follows S_0 = a + b ln N. The results for the electron gas agree with those of Ziesche (1995). The paper enables using information entropy as a common measure of particle correlations across different Fermi systems, and establishes connections between information entropy and thermodynamic quantities such as temperature and thermodynamic entropy, which could be applied to further studies of finite Fermi systems and the relationship between correlation-driven and temperature-driven changes in momentum distributions.
