# NNLL Resummation of Sudakov Shoulder Logarithms in the Heavy Jet Mass Distribution

> The heavy jet mass event shape has large perturbative logarithms near the leading order kinematic threshold at $ρ= \frac{1}{3}$. Catani and Webber named these logarithms Sudakov shoulders and resummed them at double-logarithmic level. A resummation to next-to-leading logarithmic level was achieved recently. Here, we extend the resummation using an effective field theory framework to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic order and show how to combine it with the resummation of dijet logarithms. We also solve the open problem of an unphysical singularity in the resummed momentum space distribution, in a way similar to how it is resolved in the Drell-Yan $q_T$ spectrum: through a careful analysis of the kinematics and scale-setting in position space. The heavy jet mass Sudakov shoulder is the first observable that does not involve transverse momentum for which position space resummation is critical. These advances may lead to a more precise extraction of the strong coupling constant from $e^+ e^-$ data.

## Key claim

The authors extend the resummation of Sudakov shoulder logarithms in the heavy jet mass distribution to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) order using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), and resolve an unphysical singularity (Sudakov Landau pole) in the resummed momentum space distribution by performing scale setting in position space rather than momentum space, analogous to the resolution of similar singularities in the Drell-Yan transverse momentum spectrum.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization theorem for the heavy jet mass distribution near the trijet threshold at ρ = 1/3, separating contributions from gluon and quark channels in the light hemisphere. They perform resummation using renormalization group evolution in SCET, computing the necessary two-loop anomalous dimensions and one-loop finite parts for the hard, jet, and soft functions. The resummation is carried out in position (Fourier) space to avoid spurious Sudakov Landau poles that arise from canonical scale setting in momentum space, and the result is matched to fixed-order NLO calculations and combined with NNLL' dijet resummation.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic threshold for the Sudakov shoulder in heavy jet mass is at ρ = 1/3, with large logarithms appearing on both sides of this value (left shoulder for ρ < 1/3 and right shoulder for ρ > 1/3)
- The leading non-analytic behavior near ρ = 1/3 at NLO contains terms proportional to (α_s/(4π)) × 48 C_F × r θ(r) where r = 1/3 - ρ, with additional logarithmic terms at O(α_s²) involving ln² r and ln²(-r)
- The third derivative d³σ/dρ³ near the shoulder yields a series in distributions of the form [lnⁿ r / r]_+, analogous to the dijet limit where dσ/dρ yields [lnⁿ ρ / ρ]_+ distributions
- The Fourier transform of the integral kernel simplifies to (-iz)^(-a) (iz)^(-b), where a and b are related to the cusp anomalous dimension and scale choices

## Context

This work extends the recent NLL resummation of Sudakov shoulder logarithms by Bhattacharya et al. (2022) to NNLL order, building on the original identification of Sudakov shoulders by Catani and Webber (1997) and Seymour (1997) who achieved double-logarithmic resummation. The resolution of the unphysical singularity through position space resummation parallels the treatment of similar singularities in the Drell-Yan q_T spectrum by Frixione et al. (1998), Chiu et al. (2012), Becher et al. (2010, 2011), Monni et al. (2016), and Ebert et al. (2016). This advance may enable more precise extraction of the strong coupling constant α_s from e⁺e⁻ data, particularly because heavy jet mass has a left shoulder that propagates into the region typically used for α_s fits, unlike thrust which only has a right shoulder.
