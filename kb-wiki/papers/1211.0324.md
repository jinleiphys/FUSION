# PHENIX Highlights

> PHENIX reports on electromagnetic and hadronic observables in large data sets of p+p, d+Au and Au+Au collisions at various cms energies. Initial state effects in cold nuclear matter are quantified by centrality dependent $π^0$, $η$, reconstructed jets and $ψ\prime$ measurements. Using the first run of the new EBIS ion source at RHIC, we report first results for particle flow ($v_1$ and $v_2$) and quarkonium production in U+U and Cu+Au collisions. Hot matter created in Au+Au is characterized using event-plane dependent HBT and dielectrons. Parton-medium interactions are investigated using high $p_T$ single hadrons, $γ$-hadron correlations and heavy flavor decay electrons identified with the newly installed VTX detector.

## Key claim

PHENIX presents new measurements across multiple collision systems and energies, but several results are preliminary or based on limited calibrated data; for example, the Cu+Au flow results use only ~20% of the 2012 data, and the most central bin of the thermal dielectron measurement is still being analyzed.

## Method

PHENIX analyzed large datasets of p+p, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at RHIC, plus first data from U+U and Cu+Au collisions using the new EBIS ion source. Initial-state cold nuclear matter effects were quantified via centrality-dependent nuclear modification factors (R_dA) for π^0, η, reconstructed jets, and ψ′. Hot matter dynamics were characterized using event-plane-dependent HBT correlations and dielectron measurements. Parton-medium interactions were studied via high-p_T single hadrons, γ-hadron correlations, and heavy flavor decay electrons identified with the newly installed VTX silicon vertex detector.

## Key numbers

- Bjorken energy density for 0-2% centrality U+U collisions at √s_NN=193 GeV is ~30% higher than most central Au+Au at 200 GeV.
- Ratio ψ′/(J/ψ) in d+Au collisions is ~0.8%, smaller than in p+p (2.1%).
- Fractional momentum shift (S_loss) for ALICE charged hadrons in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV is ~25% higher than for PHENIX π^0 in Au+Au at 200 GeV.
- S_loss in Au+Au changes by approximately a factor of 4 from 200 GeV to 39 GeV.
- Average HBT radii set to 10 and 5 for Ψ_2 and Ψ_3 event planes, respectively, in 0-10% Au+Au at √s_NN=200 GeV.
- J/ψ R_AA in Cu+Au shows stronger suppression on the Cu-going side than the Au-going side as a function of N_part.

## Context

This work extends PHENIX's program of characterizing the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC, building on earlier measurements of nuclear modification factors and flow in symmetric systems. The new U+U and Cu+Au data provide novel constraints on initial-state geometry and energy density, with the U+U tip-tip events accessing higher energy density than Au+Au. The ψ′ suppression in d+Au contradicts expectations from FNAL fixed-target energy explanations based on the time the c-cbar precursor spends in the nucleus. The γ-hadron angular broadening is qualitatively consistent with hadron-hadron correlations at RHIC and jet shape broadening at LHC. The VTX detector enables separation of charm and bottom contributions to non-photonic electrons, addressing a key question from earlier PHENIX results.
