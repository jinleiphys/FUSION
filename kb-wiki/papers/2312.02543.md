# Stress inside the pion in holographic light-front QCD

> In this work, we propose a method to compute the gravitational form factor $D(Q^2)$ in holographic QCD by exploiting the remarkable correspondence between semi-classical light-front QCD and semi-classical field theories in wrapped spacetime in 5D. The use of light-front holography bridges physics at large $Q^2$ as attained in light-front QCD and physics at small $Q^2$ where the coupling to scalar and tensor fields, i.e. glueballs, are dominant. As an application, we compute the $D$-term for the pion, and compare the results with recent lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to compute the pion gravitational form factor D(Q²) in holographic light-front QCD by combining a light-front wave function representation of the energy-momentum tensor with dressed currents from bulk-to-boundary propagators, and they show that the resulting D-term satisfies the von Laue condition for mechanical stability, which earlier holographic approaches did not.

## Method

The authors start from the light-front wave function representation of the energy-momentum tensor, splitting T⁺⁻ into a kinetic part (with an exact wave function representation) and an interaction part. For the soft-wall holographic model, they localize the light-front quantized potential energy operator to obtain the interaction density, then compute the pion matrix element of T⁺⁻ in the valence Fock sector. To extend the result to low Q², they replace the bare scalar, vector, and tensor currents with dressed bulk-to-boundary propagators from soft-wall AdS/QCD, fixing two coefficients by matching to the UV asymptotics and the chiral perturbation theory prediction Dπ(0) = -1.

## Key numbers

- Soft-wall scale parameter κ = 0.388 GeV, obtained from fitting to the ρ meson mass
- Dπ(0) = -1 from chiral perturbation theory in the chiral limit, used as the IR boundary condition
- Mechanical radius of the pion: r_mech² = (0.60 fm)², compared to the matter radius r_mat² = (0.39 fm)²
- Lattice results for comparison: r_mat² = (0.41 ± 0.01 fm)² and r_mech² = (0.61 ± 0.07 fm)² from Hackett (2023)
- Lowest scalar meson pole mass M = √6 κ = 0.95 GeV, close to the a₀(980) mass
- Scalar glueball pole mass M_G = √8 κ = 1.1 GeV
- Contribution breakdown: scalar mesons Dπ^M(0) = -0.83, scalar glueball Dπ^G(0) = -1/2, tensor current Dπ^T(0) = +1/2, vector-tensor term Dπ^{V-T}(0) = -0.17

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach to gravitational form factors, which previously computed Aπ(Q²) via coupling to a transverse-traceless gravitational field in 5D AdS space (Abidin 2009) but could not access the D-term. It contrasts with the nucleon D-term calculation by Chakrabarti (2020) which did not satisfy the von Laue condition, and with the Sakai-Sugimoto model computation by Fujita (2022) which found contributions from scalar and tensor glueballs. The method enables computation of the D-term for other hadrons, particularly the nucleon, and provides a holographic interpretation of the pQCD counting rules for the pion.
