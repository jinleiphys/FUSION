# Nuclear shadowing in Glauber-Gribov theory with Q2-evolution

> We consider deep inelastic scattering off nuclei in the Regge limit within the Glauber-Gribov model. Using unitarized parton distribution functions for the proton, we find sizeable shadowing effects on the nuclear total and longitudinal structure functions, $F_2^A$ and $F_L^A$, in the low-x limit. Extending a fan-diagram analysis for the large-mass region of coherent diffraction off nuclei to high Q2, we also find significant shadowing effects in this kinematical regime. Finally, we discuss shortcomings of our approach and possible extensions of the model to other kinematical regimes.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear shadowing effects on the total and longitudinal structure functions of nuclei are sizeable at low x and high Q2, but the magnitude of gluon shadowing is weak due to the rapid small-x growth of the gluon PDF in the proton at leading order. The results are valid only for the low-x regime where triple-pomeron diagrams dominate, and the model does not include anti-shadowing or low-mass diffraction.

## Method

The authors extend the Glauber-Gribov model to deep inelastic scattering off nuclei by summing multiple rescattering contributions using the AGK cutting rules. They implement two unitarization schemes, the Schwimmer model (summing fan diagrams) and the eikonal model, and use unitarized parton distribution functions for the proton from their previous work (Armesto et al., 2010) as input. The nuclear structure functions F2^A and FL^A are computed via the Altarelli-Martinelli formula at leading order, and coherent diffraction off nuclei is calculated from fan diagram resummation.

## Key numbers

- Shadowing ratio R_F2 for lead (Pb) at Q2 = 3.5 GeV2 and x ~ 10^-3 is approximately 0.7–0.8 (Schwimmer and eikonal models overlap)
- Shadowing ratio R_F2 for calcium (Ca) at Q2 = 3.5 GeV2 and x ~ 10^-3 is approximately 0.8–0.9
- The diffractive ratio R_D for Pb at Q2 = 3.5 GeV2 and M2 ~ 100 GeV2 is suppressed to about 0.2–0.4 relative to A^2 scaling
- The ratio sigma_D / sigma_tot for Pb at Q2 = 3.5 GeV2 and x ~ 10^-4 is approximately 0.05–0.10, compared to ~0.15 for protons
- The exponential slope B0 for the diffractive cross section is taken as 5.5 GeV^-2 from Aktas et al. (2006)
- The deuteron form factor parameter a = 40 GeV^-2

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-Gribov analysis of nuclear shadowing from earlier non-perturbative calculations (Armesto et al., 2003) to high Q2 by including QCD evolution, building on the unitarized proton PDF model of Capella et al. (2000). The results are compared to experimental data from Amaudruz et al. (1995) and Arneodo et al. (1996), and to other models including the dipole model of Kopeliovich et al. (2008), the FGS model of Frankfurt et al. (2003), and the EPS09 NLO parameterization of Eskola et al. (2009). The authors find stronger shadowing at low x than EPS09, and weaker gluon shadowing than Frankfurt et al. (2003). The work enables predictions for future electron-ion colliders such as eRHIC and LHeC.
