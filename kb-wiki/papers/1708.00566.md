# Lambda beta-decay in-medium

> Under the working hypothesis that the structure of a bound hadron is modified by its interactions with other hadrons, one may expect to see changes in carefully chosen observables. In the light of a recent proposal to measure the axial charge in the strangeness changing beta-decay of a bound Lambda hyperon, we examine the size of the change expected within the quark-meson coupling model. It is predicted to be significant.

## Key claim

Under the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the strangeness-changing axial charge of a bound Lambda hyperon is predicted to be suppressed by about 10% at nuclear matter density, corresponding to a reduction of order 20% in the beta-decay partial width; the authors note this prediction depends on the working hypothesis that the internal structure of a bound hadron is modified by its interactions with other hadrons.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium modification of the Delta S = 1 axial charge for a Lambda hyperon bound in nuclear matter using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, where only the light (u and d) quarks couple to the mean scalar (sigma) and vector (omega, rho) meson fields, while the strange quark does not. The axial charge is evaluated as an integral over the bag volume of the product of the strange-quark wave function (from the MIT bag model) and the light-quark wave function (modified by the effective mass m* from the scalar field), using a local density approximation for the sigma field. The calculation assumes a bag radius of 1 fm and uses three values of the coupling G_sigma that cover the range found across different approximations (Hartree, Hartree-Fock, relativistic, non-relativistic) for reproducing saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Suppression of the axial charge at nuclear matter density: 9 ± 1.6 % (relative change)
- Corresponding reduction in the beta-decay partial width for a bound Lambda hyperon: up to 20%
- Relative variation of the vector charge at nuclear matter density: around 3.5%
- Scalar polarizability parameter d: 0.18 fm (for bag radius 1 fm)
- Quark coupling relation: g_sigma_N = 3 × 0.479 × g_sigma_q (from Eq. 4)

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to hypernuclei (Guichon 2008), the EMC effect (Thomas 1989, Cloet 2006), and nuclear density functionals (Stone 2016), to a new observable: the beta-decay of bound Lambda hyperons. It is inspired by a planned experiment at J-PARC (Tamura, personal communication) and contrasts with existing studies of mesonic and non-mesonic decay modes of Lambda hypernuclei at BNL, KEK, and DAFNE (Botta 2015, Itonaga 2010, Parreno 2007). The prediction enables experimental tests of in-medium hadron structure modification, with the possibility of separating intrinsic axial charge changes from meson exchange currents by comparing hypernuclei of different mass numbers and different hyperon orbital states.
