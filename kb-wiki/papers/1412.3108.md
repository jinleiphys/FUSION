# Soft Theorems from Effective Field Theory

> The singular limits of massless gauge theory amplitudes are described by an effective theory, called soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), which has been applied most successfully to make all-orders predictions for observables in collider physics and weak decays. At tree-level, the emission of a soft gauge boson at subleading order in its energy is given by the Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem, with the angular momentum operator acting on a lower-point amplitude. For well separated particles at tree-level, we prove the Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem using matrix elements of subleading SCET Lagrangian and operator insertions which are individually gauge invariant. These contributions are uniquely determined by gauge invariance and the reparametrization invariance (RPI) symmetry of SCET. RPI in SCET is connected to the infinite-dimensional asymptotic symmetries of the S-matrix. The Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem is generically spoiled by on-shell corrections, including collinear loops and collinear emissions. We demonstrate this explicitly both at tree-level and at one-loop. The effective theory correctly describes these configurations, and we generalize the Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem into a new one-loop subleading soft theorem for amplitudes. Our analysis is presented in a manner that illustrates the wider utility of using effective theory techniques to understand the perturbative S-matrix.

## Key claim

The Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem for subleading soft gauge boson emission at tree level is generically violated in quantum field theory by on-shell corrections from collinear loops and collinear emissions, but a new one-loop subleading soft theorem can be formulated using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) that correctly describes these configurations.

## Method

The authors use soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to compute matrix elements of subleading SCET Lagrangian and operator insertions that are individually gauge invariant, proving the Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem for well-separated particles at tree level. They then demonstrate explicitly at tree level and one-loop that collinear configurations spoil the theorem, and generalize it into a new one-loop subleading soft theorem for amplitudes by including contributions from soft loops dressing the tree-level N-point amplitude, one-loop splitting amplitudes, and loop-level fusion of collinear particles.

## Key numbers

* The leading soft factor for gauge theory is given by \(S_{\text{gauge}}^{(0)}(s) = \sum_{i=1}^N T_i \frac{\epsilon_s\cdot p_i}{p_i\cdot p_s}\), where the sum runs over all external particles and gauge invariance follows from \(\sum_i T_i = 0\).
* The subleading soft factor (Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem) is \(S^{\text{(sub)}}(s) = \sum_{i=1}^N T_i \frac{\epsilon_{s\mu}p_{s\nu}J_i^{\mu\nu}}{p_i\cdot p_s}\), where \(J_i^{\mu\nu}\) is the angular momentum operator of particle \(i\).
* For a positive-helicity soft gauge boson in color-ordered amplitudes, the leading soft factor is \(S_{\text{gauge}}^{(0)}(s^+) = \frac{\langle N1\rangle}{\langle Ns\rangle\langle s1\rangle}\) and the subleading soft factor is \(S^{\text{(sub)}}(s^+) = \frac{\tilde{\lambda}_s^{\dot{a}}}{\langle s1\rangle}\frac{\partial}{\partial\tilde{\lambda}_1^{\dot{a}}} + \frac{\tilde{\lambda}_s^{\dot{a}}}{\langle Ns\rangle}\frac{\partial}{\partial\tilde{\lambda}_N^{\dot{a}}}\).
* The expansion parameter for the soft limit is constrained by \(\epsilon \sim \frac{p_s\cdot p_k}{(p_i+p_j)^2} \ll 1\) for all particles \(i,j,k\) in the amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the tree-level Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem (Low 1958, Burnett and Kroll 1967) and the recent subleading soft theorems for gravity and gauge theory tree amplitudes (Cachazo 2014, Casali 2014) to one-loop order using SCET. The paper shows that the one-loop finite single-minus amplitude \(\mathcal{A}(-,+,\cdots,+)\) in pure Yang-Mills theory provides a counterexample to the general validity of the Low-Burnett-Kroll theorem beyond tree-level (Bern 2014, He 2014), and demonstrates that SCET correctly describes these configurations through a generalized subleading soft theorem. The reparametrization invariance of SCET is connected to the infinite-dimensional asymptotic symmetries of gauge theories (Strominger 2013, He 2014).
