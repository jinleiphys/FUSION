# Abelian Dominance in Wilson Loops

> It has been conjectured that the Abelian projection of QCD is responsible for the confinement of color. Using a gauge independent definition of the Abelian projection which does {\it not} employ any gauge fixing, we provide a strong evidence for the Abelian dominance in Wilson loop integral. In specific we prove that the gauge potential which contributes to the Wilson loop integral is precisely the one restricted by the Abelian projection.

# Abelian Dominance in Wilson Loops

## Key claim
The paper provides a theoretical proof that the gauge potential contributing to the Wilson loop integral is precisely the restricted connection obtained from a gauge-independent Abelian projection, not requiring any gauge fixing. The proof is conditional on the effective action of the restricted QCD being known, and the author notes that the proof by itself does not establish confinement, that requires a separate calculation of the effective action showing monopole condensation.

## Method
The author introduces a gauge-covariant unit iso-triplet scalar field \(\hat{n}(x)\) satisfying \(D_\mu\hat{n}=0\) to select the \(U(1)\) direction at each spacetime point, then projects the full \(SU(2)\) connection \(\vec{A}_\mu\) onto the restricted connection \(\hat{A}_\mu = A_\mu\hat{n} - \frac{1}{g}\hat{n}\times\partial_\mu\hat{n}\). The full connection is decomposed as \(\vec{A}_\mu = \hat{A}_\mu + \vec{X}_\mu\), where \(\vec{X}_\mu\) is a gauge-covariant "valence gluon" field orthogonal to \(\hat{n}\). Using the Diakonov-Petrov functional integral representation of the Wilson loop, the author shows that the loop integral reduces to an integral over the gauge-invariant part of the restricted potential, \(A_\mu + \tilde{C}_\mu\), where \(\tilde{C}_\mu\) is the monopole potential.

## Key numbers
- Lattice calculations cited in the paper show that the Abelian projection contributes about 92% of the string tension for the \(q\bar{q}\) pair (Kronfeld, Stack).
- The Wilson loop integral for an arbitrary representation is expressed with the Casimir invariant \(T\) (the color charge) appearing as a factor multiplying the loop integral of \((A_\mu + \tilde{C}_\mu)\).

## Context
This work extends the Abelian dominance conjecture originally proposed by 't Hooft and developed by Cho (1990s), which posits that only the degrees of freedom corresponding to the maximal Abelian subgroup of QCD dominate in the infrared. The author distinguishes his gauge-independent Abelian projection from the popular maximal Abelian gauge approach of 't Hooft and from the gauge-fixing approach used by Ezawa and Brower. The result enables the reduction of the non-Abelian Wilson loop integral to a quasi-Abelian Stokes integral, which, combined with a proof of monopole condensation in the restricted QCD (to be given in a forthcoming paper by Cho), would provide a field-theoretic demonstration of the dual Meissner effect as the confinement mechanism.
