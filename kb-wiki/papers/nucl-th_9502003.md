# S-wave Meson-Nucleon Interactions and the Meson Mass in Nuclear Matter from Chiral Effective Lagrangians

> Chiral effective lagrangians may differ in their prediction of meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes off-meson-mass-shell, but must yield identical S-matrix elements. We argue that the effective meson mass in nuclear matter obtained from chiral effective lagrangians is also unique. Off-mass-shell amplitudes obtained using the PCAC choice of pion field must therefore not be viewed as fundamental constraints on the dynamics, the determination of the effective meson mass in nuclear matter or the possible existence of meson condensates in the ground state of nuclear matter. This hypothesis is borne out by a calculation of the effective mass in two commonly employed formulations of chiral perturbation theory which yield different meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes off-meson-mass-shell.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the effective meson mass in nuclear matter obtained from chiral effective lagrangians is unique and independent of the choice of meson field, even though off-meson-mass-shell scattering amplitudes differ between formulations; this claim is supported by showing that two commonly employed formulations of chiral perturbation theory yield the same effective pion mass in nuclear matter despite giving different off-mass-shell amplitudes.

## Method

The authors compare two formulations of chiral perturbation theory at tree level to O(Q^2): the Manohar-Georgi formalism (used by Kaplan and Nelson) and the Gasser-Leutwyler formalism (extended to baryons by Gasser, Sainio and Svarc). They compute the in-medium pion propagator in symmetric, isospin-saturated nuclear matter using the mean field approximation for nucleon operators, evaluating the pole position (effective mass) from each formalism. The effective action method and Feynman diagram summation are both employed to derive the propagator in the Gasser-Leutwyler approach.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
* Pion mass: m_pi = 139 MeV
* Pion-nucleon sigma term: sigma = 45 MeV
* Empirical isospin-even pion-nucleon scattering length: a^+_piN = -0.0083 m_pi^{-1}
* Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
* From the empirical scattering length and sigma = 45 MeV, the combination (c_2 + c_3) m_pi^2 = -26 MeV
* The effective pion mass to linear order in density: m_pi^*(rho) = m_pi - 2 pi a^+_piN rho / m_R, where m_R is the reduced mass of the pion-nucleon system

## Context

This work addresses claims by Delorme, Ericson and Ericson (1990s) and others that chiral effective lagrangians are inconsistent with PCAC and current algebra for off-mass-shell amplitudes, and that such inconsistencies would inhibit meson condensation. The authors extend the well-established theorem of Coleman, Wess and Zumino (1969) that S-matrix elements are independent of meson field choice to the case of the effective meson mass in nuclear matter. The paper directly supports the original approach of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) for kaon condensation in neutron stars, while clarifying that the PCAC choice of pion field does not provide fundamental constraints on the dynamics or the existence of meson condensates.
