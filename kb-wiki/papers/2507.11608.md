# Coherent State Path Integral Reveals Unexpected Vacuum Structure in Thermal Field Theory

> We construct the path integral formulation of the partition function for a free scalar thermal field theory using coherent states, first in the ladder operator basis and then in the field operator basis. In so doing, we provide for the first time a mapping in quantum field theory between the field-basis coherent states and ladder-basis coherent states. Using either basis, one finds terms missed in the usual path integral derivation, which we identify as the vacuum energy contribution to the partition function. We then extend the field-basis coherent state method to the interacting $ϕ^4$ theory. In addition to the vacuum energy contribution, one finds a coupling of a vacuum expectation value to the mass term that is absent in the existing literature.

## Key claim

The standard path integral derivation of the thermal partition function for scalar field theory misses vacuum energy contributions and, in the interacting \(\phi^4\) case, a coupling of a vacuum expectation value (VEV) to the mass term; these omissions arise because the commonly used identity operator in terms of field eigenstates is ill-defined, whereas the authors' coherent-state approach yields well-defined expressions that include these terms.

## Method

The authors constructed the path integral for the partition function of a free scalar field in two ways: first using coherent states in the ladder operator basis (keeping the vacuum energy term explicit in the Hamiltonian), and second using coherent states in the field operator basis. They then extended the field-basis coherent state method to the interacting \(\phi^4\) theory. Both derivations were performed in a discretized spacetime with periodic boundary conditions, using a finite-sized box and trotterization of the thermal trace.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum energy contribution to the partition function appears with a dimensionful prefactor arising from the discretized lattice spacing.
* In the free theory, the Hamiltonian in the ladder operator basis is given by \(\hat{H} = \Delta p^n \sum_{\mathbf{k}} \frac{1}{(2\pi)^n 2E_\mathbf{k}} E_\mathbf{k} \left[ \hat{a}^\dagger_\mathbf{p_k}\hat{a}_\mathbf{p_k} + \left(\frac{2\pi}{\Delta p}\right)^n E_\mathbf{k} \right]\), where the second term is the explicit vacuum energy.
* The dispersion relation is \(E_\mathbf{k} = \sqrt{\mathbf{p}_\mathbf{k}^2 + m^2}\), analogous to the continuum limit.
* The volume of the spatial box is \(V = \prod_{j=1}^n L_j\), with \(\left(\frac{\Delta p}{2\pi}\right)^n = 1/V\).

## Context

This work extends the standard path integral formulation of thermal field theory (TFT) by identifying missing vacuum terms that are absent in all previous derivations using field eigenstate identities. The authors note that the usual derivation assumes ill-defined unities \(\mathbbm{1} \equiv \int d\phi \ketbra{\phi}{\phi}\) and \(\mathbbm{1} \equiv \int d\pi \ketbra{\pi}{\pi}\). The results provide a foundation for computing finite-volume corrections to thermodynamic quantities such as pressure, energy density, and entropy density, and for extending the framework to fermionic fields and gauge theories like QED and QCD, with implications for lattice QCD (Umeda 2008, Bazavov 2009) and signals of QGP formation in small systems.
