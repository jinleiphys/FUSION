# Quantum turbulence, superfluidity, non-Markovian dynamics, and wave function thermalization

> While quantum turbulence has been addressed both experimentally (predominantly for superfluid $^4$He and $^3$He) and theoretically, the dynamics of various ensembles of quantized vortices was followed in time only until the vortices decay into phonons. How this ``thermalization'' is achieved is still an unaddressed and thus an unelucidated question. The Unitary Fermi Gas (UFG) is a unique quantum system, which has no classical counterpart and of relevance to neutron stars, cold atoms, condensed matter and nuclear many-body systems. The non-Markovian evolution of an isolated UFG is put in evidence and its entire non-equilibrium evolution can be studied theoretically within a unified theoretical framework. The initial lattice of quantum vortices and anti-vortices evolves through a couple of vortex tangles and excitation of Kelvin waves, where vortices cross and reconnect, until very slowly thermalization sets in.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the thermalization of an isolated Unitary Fermi Gas (UFG) starting from an initial lattice of quantized vortices proceeds through a non-Markovian, piecewise linear evolution of single-particle occupation probabilities, and that the thermalization time is orders of magnitude longer than the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis (ETH) prediction of \(t_{therm} \approx \mathcal{O}(\hbar/\Delta E)\).

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent simulations of an unpolarized UFG using the Time-Dependent Superfluid Local Density Approximation (TDSLDA), solving \(2N_x^3 = 65,536\) coupled, nonlinear, 3D+time partial differential equations on a \(32^3\) spatial lattice with periodic boundary conditions. The initial state consisted of 12 linear vortices and anti-vortices with vanishing total circulation at \(T=0\), and the system was evolved for up to \(3.75 \times 10^6\) time-steps with relative numerical errors of \(10^{-6}\) for total energy conservation and \(10^{-8}\) for total particle number conservation.

## Key numbers

* Total particle number \(N = N_\uparrow + N_\downarrow \approx 1000\) for an unpolarized UFG in a cubic box of size \(L^3 = (32l)^3\) with lattice constant \(l=1\).
* Average spatial number density \(n_0 = N/V \approx 0.03\) (in units where \(\hbar = m = 1\)).
* The fractional occupation probability sum \(\sigma_1(t)\) shows piecewise linear time dependence, with distinct stages: vortex deformation for \(t\epsilon_F < 700\), vortex tangle formation and reconnections for \(700 < t\epsilon_F < 1200\), a four-vortex state persisting until \(t\epsilon_F \approx 4300\), and final vortex annihilation for \(t\epsilon_F > 5000\).
* In the quasi-homogeneous stage (\(t\epsilon_F > 9000\)), the thermalization time extracted from density fluctuations is \(t_{UFG}\,\epsilon_F = \mathcal{O}(10)\), compared to the ETH prediction \(t_{ETH}\,\epsilon_F \approx \mathcal{O}(0.1)\).
* The spatially averaged absolute value of the pairing gap changed by less than \(\approx 5\%\) between the initial vortex state and the final thermalized state.
* In the final thermalized state, currents contribute only about \(0.01\%\) to the total energy, compared to about \(10\%\) in the initial vortex state.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum turbulence beyond the point where vortices decay into phonons, a regime previously unaddressed in the field following Feynman's (1955) conjecture on superfluid turbulence. The non-Markovian dynamics revealed here contrast with the Markovian character assumed in standard Boltzmann kinetic approaches (Boltzmann 1872, Nordheim 1928, Uehling 1933) and in the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis (Srednicki 1994). The UFG is presented as a uniquely tractable system for studying these phenomena, with relevance to neutron stars, cold atoms, condensed matter, and nuclear many-body systems, and the results enable future studies of thermalization in strongly interacting quantum systems where Berry's conjecture (Berry 1977, 1991) does not apply.
