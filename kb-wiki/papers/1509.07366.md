# Quarkonium at finite temperature: Towards realistic phenomenology from first principles

> We present the finite temperature spectra of both bottomonium and charmonium, obtained from a consistent lattice QCD based potential picture. Starting point is the complex in-medium potential extracted on full QCD lattices with dynamical u,d and s quarks, generated by the HotQCD collaboration. Using the generalized Gauss law approach, vetted in a previous study on quenched QCD, we fit ${\rm Re}[V]$ with a single temperature dependent parameter $m_D$, the Debye screening mass, and confirm the up to now tentative values of ${\rm Im}[V]$. The obtained analytic expression for the complex potential allows us to compute quarkonium spectral functions by solving an appropriate Schrödinger equation. These spectra exhibit thermal widths, which are free from the resolution artifacts that plague direct reconstructions from Euclidean correlators using Bayesian methods. In the present adiabatic setting, we find clear evidence for sequential melting and derive melting temperatures for the different bound states. Quarkonium is gradually weakened by both screening (${\rm Re}[V]$) and scattering (${\rm Im}[V]$) effects that in combination lead to a shift of their in-medium spectral features to smaller frequencies, contrary to the mass gain of elementary particles at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first consistent extraction of both bottomonium and charmonium in-medium spectral functions from a complex potential derived from full lattice QCD with dynamical u, d, and s quarks, but the results are obtained in an adiabatic setting where the potential is assumed to be time-independent, and the melting temperatures derived depend on the specific criterion where binding energy equals spectral width.

## Method

The authors extract the complex in-medium heavy quark potential from Euclidean Wilson line correlators on HotQCD lattices using a novel Bayesian reconstruction prescription, then fit the real part of the potential with a generalized Gauss law ansatz that depends on a single temperature-dependent parameter, the Debye screening mass m_D. Using the resulting analytic expression for the complex potential, they solve a Schrodinger equation to compute quarkonium spectral functions for both bottomonium and charmonium S-wave states at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.472 GeV; bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.882 GeV
- Chiral crossover transition temperature on these lattices: T_C = 172.5 MeV
- Temperature range studied: T/T_C = 0.68 to 1.66
- Debye mass values extracted: m_D/T = 0.04(10) at T/T_C = 0.86, rising to m_D/T = 2.29(14) at T/T_C = 1.66
- Vacuum parameters at beta=6.9: strong coupling tilde_alpha_s = 0.456 +/- 0.027, string tension sqrt(sigma) = 0.470 +/- 0.01 GeV
- Vacuum parameters at beta=7.48: strong coupling tilde_alpha_s = 0.385 +/- 0.006, string tension sqrt(sigma) = 0.515 +/- 0.003 GeV

## Context

This work extends the perturbative complex potential derived by Laine et al. (2007) using hard-thermal loop resummation to a non-perturbative lattice QCD setting, confirming that the in-medium potential is complex with both screening (real part) and scattering (imaginary part) effects. The results enable realistic phenomenology of heavy quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, providing melting temperatures for different bottomonium and charmonium states that can be used as input for transport model calculations, and allowing estimates of the Psi' to J/Psi ratio at the chiral crossover and bottomonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions compared to p+p.
