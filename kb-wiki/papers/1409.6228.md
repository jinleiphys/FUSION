# Spin structure of the nucleon on the light front

> We briefly review the spin structure of the nucleon and show that it is best thought in the light-front formulation. We discuss in particular the longitudinal and transverse spin sum rules, the proper definition of canonical orbital angular momentum and the spin-orbit correlation.

## Key claim

The nucleon spin decomposition is not unique, but both the canonical and kinetic families of decompositions are in principle measurable and reflect complementary aspects of the bound system; the light-front formalism provides the most consistent and intuitive picture for describing and interpreting high-energy scattering experiments.

## Method

The paper reviews the two families of nucleon spin decompositions (canonical and kinetic), presenting the operator expressions for quark and gluon spin and orbital angular momentum contributions. It then discusses longitudinal and transverse spin sum rules, showing how quark kinetic orbital angular momentum can be expressed in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and twist-3 GPDs, and how canonical orbital angular momentum relates to generalized transverse-momentum dependent distributions (GTMDs) depending on the Wilson line shape. Finally, it introduces the quark spin-orbit correlation as a new independent quantity and derives its expression in terms of the second moment of the helicity distribution and twist-3 GPDs.

## Key numbers

- For up quarks at scale μ² = 1 GeV²: ∫_{-1}^{1} dx x H̃_u(x,0,0) ≈ 0.19, leading to kinetic spin-orbit correlation Cᵤ_z ≈ -0.9
- For down quarks at scale μ² = 1 GeV²: ∫_{-1}^{1} dx x H̃_d(x,0,0) ≈ -0.06, leading to kinetic spin-orbit correlation C_d_z ≈ -0.53
- Lattice calculations at μ² = 4 GeV² and pion mass m_π = 293 MeV give ∫_{-1}^{1} dx H̃_u(x,0,0) = 0.82(7) and ∫_{-1}^{1} dx H̃_d(x,0,0) = -0.41(7)
- Light-front constituent quark model at scale μ² ≈ 0.26 GeV² predicts Cᵤ_z ≈ -0.8 and C_d_z ≈ -0.55 for the kinetic spin-orbit correlation

## Context

This work extends the ongoing discussion of the proton spin puzzle by clarifying the relationship between canonical and kinetic angular momentum decompositions, building on the foundational work of Ji (1996) who related quark and gluon total angular momentum to GPDs, and the gauge-invariant canonical decomposition of Chen et al. (2008, 2009). The paper enables future experimental extraction of quark orbital angular momentum and spin-orbit correlation from parton distributions, and provides a framework for interpreting lattice QCD calculations such as those by the LHPC collaboration (Bratt et al., 2010).
