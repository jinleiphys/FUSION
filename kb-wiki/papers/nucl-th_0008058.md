# Strange quark matter: mapping QCD lattice results to finite baryon density by a quasi-particle model

> A quasi-particle model is presented which describes QCD lattice results for the 0, 2 and 4 quark-flavor equation of state. The results are mapped to finite baryo-chemical potentials. As an application of the model we make a prediction of deconfined matter with appropriate inclusion of strange quarks and consider pure quark stars.

# Strange quark matter: mapping QCD lattice results to finite baryon density by a quasi-particle model

## Key claim
A quasi-particle model can reproduce QCD lattice results for the equation of state at zero baryo-chemical potential for 0, 2, and 4 quark flavors, and then be extrapolated to finite baryo-chemical potential; however, the model does not predict absolutely stable strange quark matter, and the resulting strange quark stars are light and small, with masses ≤ 1 solar mass and radii ≤ 7 km.

## Method
The authors construct a quasi-particle model where quarks and gluons acquire effective thermal masses from 1-loop self-energies parameterized by an effective coupling g_eff(T, μ), and the thermodynamic densities (entropy density s, baryon density n) are computed from ideal-gas formulae with those effective masses. The effective coupling is constrained by requiring that the model reproduce QCD lattice data at μ = 0 for N_f = 0, 2, and 4, and is then extended to finite μ by solving a partial differential equation derived from the Maxwell relation ∂n/∂T = ∂s/∂μ. The resulting equation of state at T = 0 is applied to compute static properties of pure quark stars with strangeness via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers
- The baryo-chemical potential μ_B = 3μ for the innermost characteristics at T = 0 is 1.4 GeV when choosing the scale T_c = 160 MeV.
- The energy per baryon at the point of vanishing pressure is typically in the order of 1.5 GeV for N_f = 2, 3, 4.
- The energy density–pressure relation for 2+1 flavor matter is approximated by e = 4B̃ + α̃ p with α̃ = 3.1–4.5 and B̃^(1/4) ≥ 200 MeV.
- Strange quark stars have masses M ≤ 1 M_⊙ and radii R ≤ 7 km.
- The strange quark current mass used is m_s0 = 150 MeV.

## Context
This work extends quasi-particle models previously applied to the pure gluon plasma (N_f = 0) and two-flavor QCD lattice results (N_f = 2) by Kämpfer, Peshier, and Soff (2000) to finite baryo-chemical potential and to include strangeness. It builds on ϕ-derivable self-consistent approximations (Baym, 1962) and hard thermal loop resummation (Braaten and Pisarski, 1990; Blaizot et al., 1999) which support a quasi-particle description of deconfined matter. The model enables predictions for the equation of state of deconfined matter at finite density, which can be used to compute static properties of quark stars and hybrid stars, and will be refined when continuum-extrapolated 2+1 flavor lattice data become available.
