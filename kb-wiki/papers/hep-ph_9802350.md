# Scale evolution of nuclear parton distributions

> Using the NMC and E665 nuclear structure function ratios $F_2^A/F_2^D$ and $F_2^A/F_2^{C}$ from deep inelastic lepton-nucleus collisions, and the E772 Drell--Yan dilepton cross sections from proton-nucleus collisions, and incorporating baryon number and momentum sum rules, we determine nuclear parton distributions at an initial scale $Q_0^2$. With these distributions, we study QCD scale evolution of nuclear parton densities. The emphasis is on small values of $x$, especially on scale dependence of nuclear shadowing. As the main result, we show that a consistent picture can be obtained within the leading twist DGLAP evolution, and in particular, that the calculated $Q^2$ dependence of $F_2^{Sn}/F_2^{C}$ agrees very well with the recent NMC data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the observed $Q^2$ dependence of nuclear structure function ratios, particularly $F_2^{\rm Sn}/F_2^{\rm C}$, can be consistently described within leading twist DGLAP evolution without parton fusion corrections, provided that the initial nuclear parton distributions at $Q_0^2 = 2.25$ GeV$^2$ are determined iteratively from deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan data together with baryon number and momentum sum rules.

## Method

The authors first determine nuclear parton distributions at an initial scale $Q_0^2 = 2.25$ GeV$^2$ by iteratively fitting the ratios $R_V^A$, $R_S^A$, and $R_G^A$ to NMC and E665 structure function ratios $F_2^A/F_2^D$ and $F_2^A/F_2^{\rm C}$ from deep inelastic lepton-nucleus collisions, and E772 Drell-Yan dilepton cross sections from proton-nucleus collisions, while imposing baryon number and momentum sum rules. They then evolve these initial distributions to higher $Q^2$ using lowest-order DGLAP equations without parton fusion corrections, using the GRV-LO parton distribution set for the free proton. The nuclear gluon ratio $R_G^A$ is constrained by momentum conservation and by requiring stable scale evolution at small $x$, with the anti-shadowing peak position guided by the results of Gousset and Pirner (1996).

## Key numbers

- Initial scale chosen: $Q_0^2 = 2.25$ GeV$^2$ (the charm-mass threshold for the GRV-LO set)
- For $A=208$, the glue carries about 4% more momentum than in a free nucleon, giving $R_G^A(x,Q_0^2) = 1.04$ if no $x$-dependence is assumed
- If $R_G^A(x,Q_0^2) \approx R_{F_2}^A(x,Q_0^2)$ were used for all $x$, 11% of momentum would be missing for $A=208$
- Baryon number conservation requires that up to 9% of baryon number would be missing for the heaviest nuclei if $R_V^A = R_{F_2}^A$ at all $x$
- The DGLAP evolution is performed from $Q_0^2 = 2.25$ GeV$^2$ up to $Q^2 \sim 10000$ GeV$^2$
- The cross-over region in $R_{DY}^A$ where evolution is very weak depends on the plateau in $R_S^A$ at $x \sim 0.1$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of scale evolution of nuclear effects by Qiu (1987), Frankel, Szwed, and collaborators (1990s), and Eskola (1993) by using improved proton parton distributions from HERA data (1990s) and higher-precision nuclear data from NMC and E665. The paper enables a consistent leading-twist QCD description of nuclear shadowing and its $Q^2$ dependence, and provides a framework for determining nuclear gluon distributions from the measured $Q^2$ evolution of $F_2^A/F_2^{\rm C}$ ratios, following the approach of Gousset and Pirner (1996) who used preliminary NMC data.
