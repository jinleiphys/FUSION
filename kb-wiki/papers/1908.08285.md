# From response functions to cross sections in neutrino scattering off the deuteron and trinucleons

> Response functions, differential cross sections and total cross sections for several (anti)neutrino induced reactions on 2H, 3He and 3H are calculated in momentum space for (anti)neutrino energies up to 160 MeV, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and a single-nucleon weak current operator. This work is a continuation of our investigations presented in J. Golak et al. [Phys. Rev. C 98, 015501 (2018)].

## Key claim

The authors compute response functions, differential cross sections, and total cross sections for (anti)neutrino-induced reactions on deuteron, trinucleon (³He and ³H) targets using only a single-nucleon weak current operator and the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential, with the explicit hedge that two-nucleon current contributions are expected to produce effects smaller than 2-4% at the low energies considered (E ≤ 160 MeV).

## Method

The authors solve the nuclear many-body problem in momentum space using Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon systems and direct integration for the two-nucleon system, with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and a single-nucleon weak current operator. For the trinucleon reactions, they compute response functions on a dense two-dimensional grid in internal nuclear energy and three-momentum transfer using a closure-based Faddeev scheme, then interpolate these stored response functions to obtain differential and total cross sections. The nonrelativistic kinematics are used throughout, with relativistic corrections included only in the single-nucleon current operator.

## Key numbers

- (Anti)neutrino energies considered: up to 160 MeV
- Fermi constant used: G_F = 1.1803 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Cabibbo angle cosine used: cosθ_C = 0.97425
- Maximal relative difference between relativistic and nonrelativistic maximal lepton energy formulas: less than 1% for E ≤ 160 MeV over all scattering angles
- Two-nucleon response function grids contained from 7200 to 17200 points
- Interpolation accuracy criterion: results from three different interpolation methods did not deviate from the average by more than 1%
- Expected two-nucleon current contributions: smaller than 2-4% based on results from Shen et al. (2013)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous momentum-space calculations from Golak et al. (2018), which had only presented total cross sections for deuteron reactions and limited response functions for trinucleons without cross sections. The present paper provides the first full differential and total cross sections for (anti)neutrino-induced reactions on ³He and ³H using this momentum-space framework. The results are compared with coordinate-space calculations by Shen et al. (2013) and Baroni and Schiavilla (2017), and the method of storing response functions on a grid enables efficient computation of cross sections for multiple energies and reaction channels.
