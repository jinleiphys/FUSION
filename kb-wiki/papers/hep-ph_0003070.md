# Wong's equations and the small x effective action in QCD

> We propose a new form for the small x effective action in QCD. This form of the effective action is motivated by Wong's equations for classical, colored particles in non-Abelian background fields. We show that the BFKL equation, which sums leading logarithms in x, is efficiently reproduced with this form of the action. We argue that this form of the action may be particularly useful in computing next-to- leading-order results in QCD at small x.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an alternative gauge-invariant form for the small x effective action in QCD, replacing the Wilson line \(W\) in the interaction term with \(\ln W\), motivated by Wong's equations for classical colored particles. The authors show that this new form reproduces the BFKL equation without needing to invoke the kinematic constraint of static sources that was required for the earlier form.

## Method

The authors start from a many-body world line formalism for the one-loop effective action in QCD, where the equations of motion for point particles satisfy Wong's equations for classical charged particles in a non-Abelian background field. They coarse-grain this particle-based action to obtain a field-theoretic effective action where the coupling between soft gauge fields and the color charge density \(\rho\) of hard modes is given by \(\text{Tr}(\rho \ln W)\) instead of the earlier \(\text{Tr}(\rho W)\) form. They then expand both actions to cubic order in the gauge field \(A^-\) and compare the resulting terms, showing that the difference vanishes under the static-source approximation used in the Wilsonian renormalization group derivation of the BFKL equation.

## Key numbers

- The cubic term in the expansion of \(S_{\ln W}\) yields a coefficient of \(g^3/6\) multiplying the structure constant combination \((f^{adn}f^{bcn} - f^{abn}f^{cdn})\).
- The cubic term in the expansion of \(S_W\) contains an additional term proportional to the totally symmetric tensor \(d_{abcd}\) with coefficient \(I_2/6 = N_c/6\) for SU(\(N_c\)).
- The \(d_{abcd}\) term in \(S_W\) vanishes because the hard-mode propagator gives a factor \(p^-\) and the color charge density satisfies \(\rho \sim \delta(p^-)\), so \(p^- \delta(p^-) = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the effective action approach to small x QCD developed by Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, Leonidov, and Weigert (JKLW) in their 1997-1998 papers, where the interaction term was \(\text{Tr}(\rho W)\). The new \(\ln W\) form is motivated by the authors' own prior work on a many-body world line formalism for the one-loop effective action (Jalilian-Marian, Jeon, Venugopalan, 1999). The paper enables future next-to-leading-order calculations at small x, where the \(d_{abcd}\) terms present in the \(W\) action would become non-zero due to recoil effects, but are naturally absent in the \(\ln W\) action.
