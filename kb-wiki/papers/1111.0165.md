# Review of Heavy Quarkonium at weak coupling

> We review weakly-bound heavy quarkonium systems using effective field theories of QCD. We concentrate on potential Non-Relativistic QCD, which provides with a well founded connection between QCD and descriptions of the heavy quarkonium dynamics in terms of Schrödinger-like equations. This connection is obtained using standard quantum field theory techniques such as dimensional regularization, which is used throughout, and renormalization. Renormalization group equations naturally follow. Certain effort is made to illustrate how computations are performed and the necessary techniques, providing some examples. Finally, we briefly review a selected set of applications, which include spectroscopy, radiative transitions, Non-Relativistic sum rules, inclusive decays, and electromagnetic threshold production.

## Key claim

This review argues that potential Non-Relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) provides a systematic, model-independent connection between QCD and Schrödinger-like descriptions of weakly-bound heavy quarkonium, but only under the condition that the hierarchy of scales \(m \gg mv \gg mv^2\) holds and that the soft scale is much larger than \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) (the weak-coupling regime).

## Method

The paper constructs pNRQCD by sequentially integrating out the hard scale \(m\) to obtain NRQCD, then integrating out the soft scale \(mv\) to obtain pNRQCD, using dimensional regularization throughout. Matching between QCD and NRQCD is performed by equating Green's functions with the same infrared behavior, setting scaleless loop integrals in the effective theory to zero. Renormalization group equations for the Wilson coefficients are derived from the ultraviolet divergences of the soft and ultrasoft sectors.

## Key numbers

* The hierarchy of scales is \(m \gg mv \gg mv^2\), with \(v \ll 1\) and \(v \sim \alpha_s(mv)\).
* The static potential at leading order is \(V_s^{(0)}(r) \simeq -C_f \alpha(\nu)/r\).
* The one-loop renormalized wave-function renormalization constant is \(Z = 1 + C_f \frac{\alpha}{\pi} \left( \frac{3}{4} \ln\frac{m^2}{\nu_{\rm NR}^2} - 1 \right) + \mathcal{O}\left(\left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi}\right)^2\right)\).
* The leading-logarithmic renormalization group equation for the spin-dependent four-fermion coefficient is \(\nu_s \frac{d}{d\nu_s} d_{vv} = -\frac{C_A}{2} \alpha^2 c_F^2\).

## Context

This review extends the effective field theory approach to heavy quarkonium pioneered by Caswell and Lepage (1986) for NRQED and by Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1995) for NRQCD. It focuses on the weak-coupling version of pNRQCD introduced by Pineda and Soto (1998) and Brambilla et al. (1999), which assumes \(mv \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) so that potentials can be computed perturbatively. The work enables systematic resummation of logarithms via renormalization group equations and provides a clean theoretical framework for spectroscopy, radiative transitions, sum rules, inclusive decays, and electromagnetic threshold production.
