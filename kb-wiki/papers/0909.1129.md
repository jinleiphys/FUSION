# Double and single pion photoproduction within a dynamical coupled-channels model

> Within a dynamical coupled-channels model which has already been fixed from analyzing the data of the pi N -> pi N and gamma N -> pi N reactions, we present the predicted double pion photoproduction cross sections up to the second resonance region, W< 1.7 GeV. The roles played by the different mechanisms within our model in determining both the single and double pion photoproduction reactions are analyzed, focusing on the effects due to the direct gamma N -> pi pi N mechanism, the interplay between the resonant and non-resonant amplitudes, and the coupled-channels effects. The model parameters which can be determined most effectively in the combined studies of both the single and double pion photoproduction data are identified for future studies.

## Key claim

The dynamical coupled-channels model, previously fixed using only πN → πN and γN → πN data, predicts double pion photoproduction cross sections that agree well with data up to W = 1.4 GeV but overestimate the data above that energy for γp → π⁺π⁻p and γp → π⁰π⁰p, indicating that the πNΔ coupling constant gπNΔ in the electromagnetic amplitudes needs adjustment.

## Method

The authors employed the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels (EBAC-DCC) model, which includes interactions among γN, πN, ηN, and ππN channels, with the hadronic part fixed from prior fits to πN → πN scattering data. The γN → ππN amplitude was decomposed into four pieces: a direct term T^dir and three terms proceeding through unstable intermediate states πΔ, ρN, and σN, each decaying to ππN. The model parameters for the hadronic sector were kept fixed from previous analyses, while the electromagnetic parameters (bare helicity amplitudes and coupling constants) were varied to study their effects on both single and double pion photoproduction observables.

## Key numbers

- The model predictions cover the energy region up to W < 1.7 GeV (the second resonance region)
- The direct T^dir term is crucial for reproducing the near-threshold behavior of γN → ππN total cross sections up to W = 1.4 GeV
- A ±25% variation of the πNΔ coupling constant gπNΔ in the γN → πΔ transition matrix elements produces large changes in γp → π⁺π⁻p and γp → π⁺π⁰n total cross sections, but negligible changes in γN → πN total cross sections
- A 20% smaller value of gπNΔ (close to the quark model value) is suggested by the analysis
- The S₁₁(1535) and S₃₁(1620) helicity amplitudes varied by ±50% produce up to 20% changes in γp → π⁰π⁰p and γp → π⁺π⁰n total cross sections
- The D₁₃(1520) resonance builds up a large fraction of the first peak in γp → π⁰π⁰p total cross section

## Context

This work extends the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels approach from previous analyses of πN → πN (Julia-Diaz et al., 2007) and γN → πN (Julia-Diaz et al., 2008) reactions to double pion photoproduction, following a similar study of πN → ππN reactions (Kamano et al., 2009). It contrasts with earlier tree-diagram models (Gomez Tejedor and Oset, 1995; Ochi et al., 1997; Nacher et al., 2000; Fix and Arenhoevel, 2005) that emphasized specific resonances, and with chiral perturbation theory studies focused on the near-threshold region. The results enable future combined fits of world data on πN, γN → πN, and ππN reactions to better constrain the couplings of N* resonances to the ππN channel, particularly the πΔ, ρN, and σN channels.
