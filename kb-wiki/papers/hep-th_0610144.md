# Viscous plasma evolution from gravity using AdS/CFT

> We analyze the AdS/CFT dual geometry of an expanding boost-invariant plasma. We show that the requirement of nonsingularity of the dual geometry for leading and subasymptotic times predicts, without any further assumptions about gauge theory dynamics, hydrodynamic expansion of the plasma with viscosity coefficient exactly matching the one obtained earlier in the static case by Policastro, Son and Starinets.

# Viscous plasma evolution from gravity using AdS/CFT

## Key claim
The requirement that the dual AdS/CFT geometry of an expanding boost-invariant plasma be nonsingular, when applied to subasymptotic times, predicts viscous hydrodynamic evolution with a uniquely fixed viscosity coefficient, without any further assumptions about gauge theory dynamics. The viscosity coefficient obtained from this nonsingularity condition exactly matches the value derived from the static case by Policastro, Son and Starinets.

## Method
The author constructs the 5-dimensional dual geometry in Fefferman-Graham coordinates by solving Einstein equations with negative cosmological constant, using the energy-momentum tensor of a boost-invariant plasma as a boundary condition. The metric coefficients are expanded in powers of tau^{-2/3} around the scaling variable v = z / tau^{s/4}, and the curvature invariant R^2 is computed to second order in this expansion. The requirement that this curvature invariant be nonsingular at the horizon v = 3^{1/4} fixes the viscosity coefficient eta_0.

## Key numbers
- The leading-order energy density for ideal hydrodynamics scales as eps(tau) = 1 / tau^{4/3}.
- The first subleading correction from viscosity gives eps(tau) = 1/tau^{4/3} - 2 eta_0 / tau^2 + ...
- The viscosity coefficient required for nonsingular geometry is eta_0^2 = sqrt(3) / 18, equivalently eta_0 = 1 / (2^{1/2} * 3^{3/4}).
- The temperature of the evolving plasma scales as T = sqrt(2) / (pi * 3^{1/4}) * tau^{-1/3}.
- The horizon location in the fifth dimension is z_0 = 3^{1/4} * tau^{1/3}.

## Context
This work extends the framework of Janik and Peschanski (2006), who showed that nonsingularity of the dual geometry for asymptotic times selects perfect fluid hydrodynamics with s=4/3. It also builds on the calculation of shear viscosity from static black hole perturbations by Policastro, Son and Starinets (2001). The result provides a nontrivial consistency check of the AdS/CFT correspondence for time-dependent processes and opens the door to studying non-equilibrium phenomena such as thermalization and black hole formation in the dual description, as suggested by Nastase (2005) and Zahed (2005).
