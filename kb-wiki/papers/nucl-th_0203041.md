# Ground-state correlations and final state interactions in the process 3He(e,e'pp)n

> The two-proton emission process $^3He(e,e'pp)n$ is theoretically investigated using realistic three-nucleon wave functions and taking the final state interaction into account by an approach which can be used when the value of the three-nucleon invariant mass is either below or above the pion emission threshold. Various kinematical conditions which enhance or minimize the effects of the final state interaction are thoroughly analyzed.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether final state interactions (FSI) in the process \(^3He(e,e'pp)n\) can be minimized by a proper choice of kinematics, thereby enabling the study of ground-state correlations (GSC); they find that in certain kinematic conditions, particularly when the neutron momentum satisfies \(|{\bf k}_n| \simeq 2|{\bf p}_{rel}|\) and the emission is collinear, the effects of \(pp\) rescattering become negligible, but this conclusion is limited to the regime where the three-nucleon invariant mass is below the pion production threshold for fully consistent Faddeev treatments.

## Method

The authors use realistic three-nucleon ground-state wave functions from the Pisa group (obtained with the AV18 interaction) and treat the final state interaction by including \(pp\) rescattering via continuum two-proton wave functions from the Schrödinger equation, and three-body rescattering via a Glauber-type eikonal approximation. They analyze two mechanisms: (1) the virtual photon is absorbed by a proton in a correlated \(pp\) pair, and (2) the photon is absorbed by the neutron, with the two spectator protons interacting in the final state. Calculations are performed for symmetric kinematics (\({\bf p}_n=0\), \({\bf p}_1+{\bf p}_2={\bf q}\)) and super-parallel kinematics (all momenta collinear with \({\bf q}\)), and the transition form factors and vector spectral functions are computed to assess FSI effects.

## Key numbers

- At \(\nu = 0.2\) GeV in super-parallel kinematics, \(|{\bf k}_{rel}^{(s.p.)}| \simeq 0.45\) fm\(^{-1}\), whereas in symmetric kinematics \(|{\bf k}_{rel}^{(sym)}| \simeq 2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- At \(\nu = 0.2\) GeV, \(|{\bf q}| \simeq 535\) MeV/\(c\); in super-parallel kinematics \(|{\bf p}_2| \simeq 0.45\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(|{\bf p}_{rel}| \simeq 1.8\) fm\(^{-1}\); in symmetric kinematics \(|{\bf p}_1| = |{\bf p}_2| \simeq 2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\theta_{12} \simeq 105^\circ\), and \(|{\bf p}_{rel}| \simeq 1.7\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- In super-parallel kinematics, there exists a small bin of \(\nu \simeq 0.4\)–\(0.5\) GeV where two-nucleon correlations could be investigated treating \(pp\) rescattering within the Schrödinger equation.
- The neutron spectral function shows that at \(k_n \geq 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\), there are regions peaked at \(E^* \simeq {\bf k}_n^2 / (4M)\) where \(pp\) rescattering does not play any role, corresponding to the relation \(|{\bf k}_n| \simeq 2|{\bf p}_{rel}|\).
- For \(|{\bf p}_{rel}| = 0.75\) fm\(^{-1}\) (\(E^* \simeq 23\) MeV), the relation \(|{\bf k}_n| = 2|{\bf p}_{rel}|\) gives \(|{\bf k}_n| \simeq 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\); for \(|{\bf p}_{rel}| = 1.1\) fm\(^{-1}\) (\(E^* \simeq 50\) MeV), it gives \(|{\bf k}_n| \simeq 2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of two-nucleon emission from few-body systems, particularly the consistent Faddeev treatment of bound and continuum states by Golak et al. (2001) for low momentum transfer (\(Q^2 \sim 0.1\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\)), and the calculations by Laget (1998) using different ground-state wave functions. The authors aim to bridge the gap to higher momentum transfer (\(Q^2 \sim 0.5\)–\(2\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\)) relevant to experiments at TJLab (CEBAF), where Faddeev continuum calculations are not available above the pion production threshold. The results enable future experimental investigation of ground-state correlations in \(^3He\) via the \((e,e'2p)\) reaction in super-parallel kinematics, provided that rescattering with the outgoing neutron is properly accounted for.
