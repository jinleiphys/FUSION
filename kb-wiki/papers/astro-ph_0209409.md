# Limits on cosmological variation of quark masses and strong interaction

> We discuss limits on variation of $(m_q/Λ_{QCD})$. The results are obtained by studying $n-α$-interaction during Big Bang, Oklo natural nuclear reactor data and limits on variation of the proton $g$-factor from quasar absorpion spectra.

## Key claim

The paper derives limits on the cosmological variation of the dimensionless ratio \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) (light quark mass over the QCD scale) by combining constraints from Big Bang nucleosynthesis, the Oklo natural nuclear reactor, and quasar absorption spectra, but the authors caution that some of the limits should be treated as order-of-magnitude estimates due to hard-to-estimate theoretical errors.

## Method

The authors study the dependence of the \(^5\)He \(p_{3/2}\) resonance position on pion mass and nucleon mass using a Woods-Saxon potential with a one-pion-exchange exchange term, then derive BBN limits on quark mass variation. They analyze the shift of the lowest neutron resonance in \(^{149}\)Sm from Oklo reactor data, expressing the resonance energy in terms of nucleon mass, nuclear radius, and potential depth. They also use quasar absorption measurements of the proton \(g\)-factor and the combination \(\alpha^2 g_p m_e/M_p\) to constrain variations of \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) and \(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}\) at redshifts up to \(z=1.8\).

## Key numbers

- BBN limit from nucleon mass variation: \(\delta(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) < 0.25\) (from 5% nucleon mass increase producing bound \(^5\)He).
- BBN limit from pion mass variation: \(\delta(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}) < 0.4\) (from 21.9% pion mass increase producing bound \(^5\)He).
- Oklo limit on combined variation: \(|\delta(M/\Lambda_{QCD})/(M/\Lambda_{QCD}) + 0.6\,\delta(m_\pi/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_\pi/\Lambda_{QCD})| < 4 \times 10^{-10}\).
- Oklo limit on strange and light quark masses: \(|\delta(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) + 0.2\,\delta(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD})| < 2 \times 10^{-9}\).
- Oklo non-zero solution: \(\delta(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) + 0.2\,\delta(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}) = (1 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-8}\).
- Carbon resonance limit: \(|\delta(M/\Lambda_{QCD})/(M/\Lambda_{QCD}) + 0.6\,\delta(m_\pi/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_\pi/\Lambda_{QCD})| < 1.2 \times 10^{-3}\).
- Quasar limit at \(z=0.2467\): \(2\,\delta(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) - \delta(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}) = (-2.0 \pm 4.4) \times 10^{-5}\).
- Quasar limit at \(z=0.6847\): \(2\,\delta(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}) - \delta(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}) = (-1.6 \pm 5.4) \times 10^{-5}\).
- Quasar limit at \(z=1.8\): \(\delta(m_e/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_e/\Lambda_{QCD}) - 0.09\,\delta(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD})/(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}) = (0.7 \pm 1.1) \times 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier discussion (Dmitriev and Flambaum, 2002) of strong scale variation effects on BBN, Oklo, and quasar spectra, and builds on prior limits on fine-structure constant variation (Murphy et al., 2001) and on the Oklo resonance shift (Shlyakhter, 1976). The results enable combined constraints on quark mass and QCD scale variation across cosmological timescales from the Big Bang to the present, and provide a framework for interpreting future measurements of proton \(g\)-factor and molecular transitions in quasar spectra.
