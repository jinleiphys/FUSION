# Smallest drop of QGP: Thermodynamic properties in p-Pb collisions

> The extreme conditions of temperature and density produced in ultrarelativistic collisions of heavy nuclei facilitate the formation of the most fundamental fluid in the universe, the deconfined phase of Quantum Chromodynamics called quark-gluon plasma. Despite the extensive experimental evidence collected over the past decade of its production in colliding systems such as Au-Au and Pb-Pb, establishing quark-gluon plasma formation in the collision of smaller systems, such as p-Pb, remains an open question. In this study, we describe the evolution of matter formed in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV using a state-of-the-art hybrid model based on viscous relativistic hydrodynamics. We investigate the thermodynamic properties of the medium and final state observables. Our findings are compared with experimental data and first-principles calculations derived from lattice quantum chromodynamics. The results support the formation of a collective phase of strongly interacting matter in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The thermodynamic properties extracted from p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV using a hybrid hydrodynamic model agree with lattice QCD calculations only for the 30% of events with the highest multiplicity, supporting QGP formation in those events but not in lower-multiplicity ones.

## Method

The authors simulated p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV using a hybrid model that includes TRENTo initial conditions, free-streaming pre-equilibrium dynamics, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with MUSIC, and a hadronic cascade with UrQMD. They extracted the effective temperature and entropy density from the simulated freeze-out hypersurfaces using the ratio of total energy to total entropy, then compared these thermodynamic quantities to experimental data from ALICE and to first-principles lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

- The effective temperature range extracted from p-Pb collisions is 179 < T_eff < 257 MeV across different multiplicities.
- The relation between mean transverse momentum and effective temperature is ⟨p_t⟩ = 3.05 T_eff for the kinematic cuts p_T > 0.15 GeV and |η| < 0.3.
- The effective volume for p-Pb events is 45 times smaller than for central Pb-Pb events.
- For high-multiplicity events with T_eff > 220 MeV, dN_ch/dη > 20 and V_eff > 10.9 fm^3, giving particle density n > 2.7 fm^{-3} and number of active degrees of freedom ν > 19.
- The entropy per particle used is S/N_ch = 6.3 ± 0.8, with a total uncertainty of 23% for s(T_eff)/T^3_eff.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic extraction method developed by Gardim (2019) for Pb-Pb collisions to the smaller p-Pb system, testing whether QGP forms in such small systems. The results support QGP formation only in the highest-multiplicity p-Pb events, consistent with earlier hydrodynamic simulations by Romatschke (2015) and Bozek (2014) that partially agreed with experimental data. The findings provide a caveat for Bayesian analyses such as those by Nijs (2020) and Moreland (2018) that include small systems: only centrality ranges consistent with QGP formation should be used when extracting transport coefficients.
