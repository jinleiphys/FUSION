# Octet baryon electromagnetic form factor double ratios $(G_E^\ast/G_M^\ast)/(G_E/G_M)$ in a nuclear medium

> The ratios of the baryon electric ($G_E$) to magnetic ($G_M$) form factors, $G_E/G_M$, can provide us with important information on the structure of baryons in vacuum as demonstrated in recent studies for the proton and neutron systems. It has been argued that the corresponding in-medium ratios, $G_E^\ast/G_M^\ast$, can also provide useful information on the electromagnetic structure of baryons in a nuclear medium. Although the ratios may not be measured directly in experiments except for the proton at present, the double ratios $(G_E^\ast/G_M^\ast)/(G_E/G_M)$ can be directly related to the polarization-transfer measurements of bound baryons. In the present work we estimate, for the first time, the double ratios $(G_E^\ast/G_M^\ast)/(G_E/G_M)$ for all the members of octet baryons in symmetric nuclear matter in the range $Q^2=0$--3 GeV$^2$, where $Q^2=- q^2$ with $q$ being the four-momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The authors estimate, for the first time, the double ratios $(G_E^\ast/G_M^\ast)/(G_E/G_M)$ for all octet baryons in symmetric nuclear matter, but note that the estimates for charge neutral baryons (especially $\Lambda$, $\Sigma^0$, and $\Xi^0$) should be taken with caution because the separation between quark core and pion cloud contributions is poorly constrained by lattice QCD data for these systems.

## Method

The authors combine the covariant spectator quark model with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to compute electromagnetic form factors of octet baryons in symmetric nuclear matter. The valence quark contributions are calibrated using lattice QCD electromagnetic form factor data for octet baryons, while the pion cloud contributions are estimated using an $SU(6)$ symmetry-based model with parameters fitted to physical data including nucleon form factors, octet baryon magnetic moments, and electric and magnetic radii. The extension to nuclear medium replaces vacuum masses with in-medium effective masses ($M_B^\ast$) and modifies pion-baryon coupling constants ($g_{\pi B B'}^\ast$) using the QMC model, with nuclear matter densities $\rho = 0$, $0.5\rho_0$, and $\rho_0$ where $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Key numbers

- The proton double ratio at $Q^2=0$ is less than unity for both $0.5\rho_0$ and $\rho_0$, a consequence of the in-medium magnetic form factor enhancement $\frac{G_M^\ast}{G_M} \approx \frac{M_N}{M_N^\ast}$ near $Q^2=0$
- The neutron double ratio increases up to 7% for $0.5\rho_0$ and 15% for $\rho_0$ near $Q^2 = 0.3$ GeV$^2$, before falling slowly up to $Q^2 \simeq 2.8$ GeV$^2$
- The calculations cover the range $Q^2 = 0$--3 GeV$^2$ for all octet baryons
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
- The quark anomalous magnetic moment values used are $\kappa_u = 1.711$, $\kappa_d = 1.987$, and $\kappa_s = 1.462$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factor double ratios in nuclear medium (Dieterich 2001, Strauch 2004, Paolone 2010) to all octet baryons, building on the model framework developed in Ramalho et al. (2015) for octet baryon form factors. The results for the proton double ratio show slower falloff rates with $Q^2$ compared to estimates from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Cloet 2009), while the neutron double ratio enhancement is smaller in magnitude than other studies (Cloet 2009, de Araujo 2018). The predictions enable future experimental tests via polarization-transfer measurements of bound baryons, particularly for the neutron using $^2$H$(\vec{e},e^\prime \vec{n})p$ and $^4$He$(\vec{e},e^\prime \vec{n})^3$He reactions, and provide input for understanding heavy-ion collisions and neutron star cores.
