# Dynamical AdS/Yang-Mills Model

> The AdS/CFT correspondence has provided new and useful insights into the nonperturbative regime of strongly coupled gauge theories. We construct a class of models meant to mimic Yang-Mills theory using the superpotential method. This method allows us to efficiently address the problem of solving all the equations of motion. The conformal symmetry is broken in the infrared by a dilaton field. Using a five-dimensional action we calculate the mass spectrum of scalar glueballs. This spectrum contains a tachyon, indicating an instability in the theory. We stabilize the theory by introducing a cosmological constant in the bulk and a pair of 3-branes, as in the Randall-Sundrum model. The scalar glueball masses computed by lattice gauge theory are then described very well by just a few parameters. Prospects for extending the model to other spins and parities and to finite temperature are considered.

## Key claim

The authors construct a fully dynamical AdS/Yang-Mills model using the superpotential method that reproduces the scalar glueball mass spectrum from lattice gauge theory, but only after stabilizing the theory by introducing a Randall-Sundrum-type UV cutoff; without this stabilization, the model generically contains a tachyonic state indicating an instability.

## Method

The authors use a five-dimensional action in the Einstein frame with a dilaton field and a scalar glueball field, employing the superpotential method to reduce the equations of motion to first-order differential equations. They specify a superpotential W(phi, G) derived from a function U(G), which determines the full potential V(phi, G). The background fields are solved in the coordinate z, and the glueball mass spectrum is obtained by solving a Schrodinger equation derived from fluctuations of the glueball field. The model is stabilized by adding a bulk cosmological constant and two 3-branes as in the Randall-Sundrum model, which introduces a UV cutoff parameter k.

## Key numbers

* The lattice glueball spectrum for pure SU(3) gauge theory shows linear behavior: for n = 2,3,4, m_n^2/sigma = 20.37 n - 1.666, with sigma = (440 MeV)^2.
* The best-fit parameters for the stabilized model are: sqrt(lambda) = 581.8 MeV, k = 0.951 sqrt(lambda), and gamma = 0.2725.
* In the large-gamma limit without stabilization, the mass spectrum is m_n^2 = 8 lambda (n - 3/2), which contains exactly one tachyonic state.
* The UV cutoff parameter l = 1/k is related to the brane tension and renders the four-dimensional vacuum energy density finite.

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up AdS/QCD approach by using the superpotential method to construct a self-consistent potential, addressing a shortfall of previous work by Bartz and Kapusta (2016) where a special term in the potential could only be determined numerically. The model reproduces lattice glueball results from Meyer and Teper (2005) and improves upon earlier holographic models by Csaki et al. (2000) and Ballon-Bayona et al. (2017) by providing an analytic potential with a UV cutoff that eliminates the tachyon. The next step is to extend the model to finite temperature to study the equation of state and transport coefficients.
