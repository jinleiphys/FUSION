# A method of eta' decay product selection to study partial chiral symmetry restoration

> In case of chiral U_A(1) symmetry restoration the mass of the eta' boson (the ninth, would-be Goldstone boson) is decreased, thus its production cross section is heavily enhanced. The eta' decays (through one of its decay channels) into five pions. These pions will not be correlated in terms of Bose-Einsten correlations, thus the production enhancement changes the strength of two-pion correlation functions at low momentum. Preliminary results strongly support the mass decrease of the eta' boson. In this paper we propose a method to select pions coming from eta' decays. We investigate the efficiency of the proposed kinematical cut in several collision systems and energies with several simulators. We prove that our method can be used in all investigeted collision systems.

# A method of eta' decay product selection to study partial chiral symmetry restoration

## Key claim

The paper proposes a kinematical selection method to identify pions originating from eta' decays, based on invariant mass cuts on pion pairs and quadruplets, in order to test whether a measured decrease in the two-pion correlation strength (the lambda parameter) is actually caused by enhanced eta' production due to partial chiral U_A(1) symmetry restoration. The authors claim the method works in all investigated collision systems, but note that efficiency depends strongly on detector geometry and collision system, with the pair-based method outperforming the single-particle method.

## Method

The method selects pions from the decay chain eta' -> eta + pi+ + pi- -> (pi+ + pi- + pi0) + pi+ + pi- by applying invariant mass cuts of 0.075–0.171 GeV^2/c^4 for pi+ pi- pairs and 0.43–0.69 GeV^2/c^4 for quadruplets (pi+ pi- pi+ pi-). The authors tested the method using Pythia 8.135 for proton-proton collisions and Hijing 1.411 for both proton-proton and gold-gold collisions, simulating detector acceptances for STAR and PHENIX at RHIC (200 GeV) and for ALICE and CMS at LHC (14 TeV). They quantified performance via efficiency (fraction of eta' decay pions that pass the cuts) and loss (fraction of non-eta' pions that pass the cuts).

## Key numbers

- The eta' meson has a vacuum mass of 958 MeV.
- The decay chain eta' -> eta + pi+ + pi- -> (pi+ + pi- + pi0) + pi+ + pi- has an overall probability of 10%.
- The average momentum of the five pions from this decay chain is 138 MeV.
- The invariant mass interval chosen for pi+ pi- pairs is 0.075–0.171 GeV^2/c^4.
- The invariant mass interval chosen for quadruplets is 0.43–0.69 GeV^2/c^4.
- Simulations used 1,000,000 events at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and 10,000 events at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV for p+p collisions, and 100 events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Csanad (2005), Vertesi (2009), and Csorgo (2009) that observed a decreasing lambda parameter in two-pion correlation functions at low momentum, which was interpreted as evidence for eta' mass modification and partial chiral symmetry restoration. The proposed method aims to provide a direct experimental test by kinematically selecting eta' decay pions; if the lambda decrease vanishes after removing these pions, the eta' enhancement hypothesis would be confirmed. A similar invariant mass selection method was previously investigated by Kulka (1990) for e+e- collisions. The results enable future experimental analyses at RHIC and LHC to isolate the eta' contribution to correlation measurements.
