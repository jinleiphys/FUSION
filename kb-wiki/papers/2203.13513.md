# Zero- and finite-temperature electromagnetic strength distributions in closed- and open-shell nuclei from first principles

> Ab initio approaches to the nuclear many-body problem have seen their reach considerably extended over the past decade. However, collective excitations have been scarcely addressed so far due to the prohibitive cost of solving the corresponding equations of motion. Here, a numerically efficient method to compute electromagnetic response functions at zero- and finite-temperature in superfluid and deformed nuclei from an ab initio standpoint is presented and applied to $^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si, $^{46}$Ti and $^{56}$Fe. This work opens the path to systematic ab initio calculations of nuclear responses to electroweak probes across a significant portion of the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ab initio Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov quasiparticle random phase approximation (HFB-QRPA), implemented via the numerically efficient finite amplitude method (FAM), can reproduce experimental electromagnetic strength distributions in both closed- and open-shell nuclei, with the caveat that dynamical correlations beyond HFB-QRPA largely cancel between ground and excited states, making the basic HF(B)-RPA a viable approach despite its apparent simplicity.

## Method

The authors employ the quasiparticle finite amplitude method (QFAM) to solve the HFB-QRPA equations, replacing the costly diagonalization of the QRPA matrix with a set of nonlinear equations of similar dimension to the static HFB mean-field problem. Calculations use chiral effective field theory (χEFT) Hamiltonians at NLO, N²LO, and N³LO, softened via similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation with flow parameter α = 0.08 fm⁴, and are applied to compute isovector dipole (IVD) E1 photoabsorption cross sections at zero and finite temperature in ¹⁶O, ²⁸Si, ⁴⁶Ti, and ⁵⁶Fe.

## Key numbers

- In ¹⁶O, the zero-temperature dipole polarizability α_D computed with the N³LO Hamiltonian is 0.61 fm³, compared to the experimental value of 0.58(1) fm³.
- In ¹⁶O, the NNLO_sat Hamiltonian gives α_D = 0.63 fm³ via HFB-QRPA and 0.53 fm³ via IM-SRPA.
- The zero-temperature dipole polarizability α_D for ²⁸Si is 1.33 fm³, for ⁴⁶Ti is 2.50 fm³, and for ⁵⁶Fe is 3.05 fm³, all computed with the N³LO Hamiltonian.
- In ²⁸Si, the experimental isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) at 20 MeV and a shoulder around 26 MeV are accurately reproduced.
- In ⁵⁶Fe at k_BT = 1 MeV, the mean excitation energy of the IVD response is about 22 MeV, compared to the experimental centroid at 18.4 MeV.
- The temperature interval studied for ⁵⁶Fe is k_BT ∈ [0, 4] MeV.

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of electromagnetic response functions, previously limited to doubly closed-shell nuclei via coupled cluster (Bacca 2014, Miorelli 2016, Bonaiti 2021), no-core shell model (Stumpf 2017), and Dyson self-consistent Green's function (Raimondi 2019) approaches, to singly and doubly open-shell nuclei by incorporating pairing and deformation via HFB-QRPA. The QFAM implementation, originally developed for energy density functional calculations (Nakatsukasa 2007, Avogadro 2011), enables systematic ab initio studies of nuclear responses to electroweak probes across a large portion of the nuclear chart, with future work needed to include dynamical correlations beyond HFB-QRPA for quantities sensitive to strength details such as dipole polarizability.
