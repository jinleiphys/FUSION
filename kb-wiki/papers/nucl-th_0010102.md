# Role of N*(1650) in the near threshold pp --> p Lambda K+ and pp --> p Sigma0 K+ reactions

> We investigate the pp --> p Lambda K+ and pp --> p Sigma0 K+ reactions at beam energies near their thresholds within an effective Lagrangian model, where the strangeness production proceeds via the excitation of N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) baryonic resonances. It is found that the $N^*$(1650) resonance dominates both these reactions at near threshold energies. The contributions from this resonance together with the final state interaction among the outgoing particles are able to explain the observed beam energy dependence of the ratio of the cross sections of the two reactions in the near threshold region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the N*(1650) resonance dominates both the pp → pΛK+ and pp → pΣ0K+ reactions at near-threshold energies, and that together with final-state interactions it can explain the observed large ratio of cross sections (R ≈ 28) at excess energies below 13 MeV, but only if the N*(1650) resonance is included in the Σ0K+ channel (which prior work by Sibirtsev et al. (2000) had omitted).

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach (ELA) where the initial nucleon-nucleon interaction is modeled by π-, ρ-, ω-, and σ-meson exchange, with coupling constants fixed by fitting NN scattering T-matrices. Strangeness production proceeds via excitation of N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) intermediate baryonic resonances, with interference terms retained. Final-state interactions among the outgoing particles were included using the Watson-Migdal theory with a Jost function formalism, using scattering lengths and effective ranges from the Jülich-Bonn YN interaction model.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally observed ratio R = σ(pp → pΛK+)/σ(pp → pΣ0K+) at excess energies ε < 13 MeV is about 28 (+6, -9).
- For the N*(1650)Σ0K+ coupling, a value of g²_{N*Σ0K+}/4π = 0.450 was used, determined by fitting πp → KΣ data.
- For the N*(1710) and N*(1720) resonances, the coupling constants g²_{N*Σ0K+}/4π were 8.242 and 0.220 respectively.
- For the Σ-p FSI, the scattering length a0 and effective range r0 were 2.28 fm and 4.96 fm (singlet state), and 0.76 fm and 2.50 fm (triplet states).
- For the K+-Λ system, a0 = -0.065 - i0.040 and r0 = -15.930 - i8.252; for K+-Σ0, a0 = -0.201 - i0.131 and r0 = -1.757 - i0.0835.
- At higher beam energies (ε > 300 MeV), the ratio R drops to about 2.5, and the N*(1710) resonance becomes dominant.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied by Shyam (1999) to the pp → pΛK+ reaction, now including the pp → pΣ0K+ channel. It directly addresses the puzzle raised by Sewerin et al. (1999) of the large cross-section ratio at near-threshold energies, which prior models by Gasparian et al. (2000) and Sibirtsev et al. (2000) could not fully explain without arbitrary normalization or omission of the N*(1650) resonance in the Σ0 channel. The results enable a unified description of both reactions from threshold up to 1 GeV excess energy, and demonstrate that the N*(1650) resonance, not just final-state interactions, is essential for reproducing the observed ratio.
