# Jet Tomography of Harmonic Fluctuations in the Initial Condition of Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this paper we study the jet response (particularly azimuthal anisotropy) as a hard probe of the harmonic fluctuations in the initial condition of central heavy ion collisions. By implementing the fluctuations via cumulant expansion for various harmonics quantified by $ε_n$ and using the geometric model for jet energy loss, we compute the response $χ^h_n=v_n/ε_n$. Combining these results with the known hydrodynamic response of the bulk matter expansion in the literature, we show that the hard-soft azimuthal correlation arising from their respective responses to the common geometric fluctuations reveals a robust and narrow near-side peak that may provide the dominant contribution to the "hard-ridge" observed in experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the azimuthal anisotropy of high-\(p_t\) hadrons from jet quenching responds approximately linearly to initial-state harmonic fluctuations \(\epsilon_n\) in central heavy ion collisions, and that the hard-soft azimuthal correlation arising from this common response produces a robust narrow near-side peak that may provide the dominant contribution to the observed "hard-ridge", but the quantitative comparison with data is not yet ready due to modeling approximations and experimental complications.

## Method

The authors implement harmonic fluctuations in the initial matter density of central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\ \mathrm{GeV}\) via a modified cumulant expansion for harmonics \(n=1\) through \(6\), parameterized by eccentricities \(\epsilon_n\) and a Gaussian width \(\Sigma\). They compute the jet energy loss using a geometric model with a near-\(T_c\) enhancement of the jet-medium interaction, where the suppression factor for a jet path is \(f_{\vec P} = \exp\left\{-\int_{\vec P} \kappa[s(l)]\, s(l)\, l^m dl\right\}\) with \(m=1\). The jet response coefficient \(\chi^h_n = v_n/\epsilon_n\) is extracted from the linear regime \(0.05 < \epsilon_n < 0.1\) for each harmonic, and these coefficients are combined with known hydrodynamic soft response coefficients \(\chi^s_n\) and Monte Carlo values of \(\sqrt{\langle \epsilon_n^2 \rangle}\) to compute the event-averaged hard-soft azimuthal correlation \(C[\Delta\phi]\).

## Key numbers

* For \(\Sigma = \sigma = 4.19\ \mathrm{fm}\), the total jet response coefficients are \(\chi^h_1 = 1.3\), \(\chi^h_2 = 0.18\), \(\chi^h_3 = 0.17\), \(\chi^h_4 = -2.5\times10^{-2}\), \(\chi^h_5 = -4.1\times10^{-2}\), \(\chi^h_6 = -1.5\times10^{-3}\).
* For \(\Sigma = 1.25\sigma\), the coefficients are \(\chi^h_1 = 1.5\), \(\chi^h_2 = 0.25\), \(\chi^h_3 = 0.18\), \(\chi^h_4 = -3.0\times10^{-2}\), \(\chi^h_5 = -4.4\times10^{-2}\), \(\chi^h_6 = -8.8\times10^{-4}\).
* The root-mean-square radii used for the cumulant expansion are \(\langle r^n \rangle^{1/n} = 3.88, 4.19, 4.44, 4.63, 4.80, 4.95\ \mathrm{fm}\) for \(n=1,2,3,4,5,6\).
* The Monte Carlo values of the initial fluctuation amplitudes for central collisions are \(\sqrt{\langle \epsilon_1^2 \rangle} \approx 0.037\), \(\sqrt{\langle \epsilon_2^2 \rangle} \approx 0.068\), \(\sqrt{\langle \epsilon_3^2 \rangle} \approx 0.076\), \(\sqrt{\langle \epsilon_4^2 \rangle} \approx 0.084\), \(\sqrt{\langle \epsilon_5^2 \rangle} \approx 0.091\).
* The soft response coefficients from hydrodynamic modeling are \(\chi^s_1 \approx 0.15\), \(\chi^s_2 \approx 0.26\), \(\chi^s_3 \approx 0.21\), \(\chi^s_4 \approx 0.14\), \(\chi^s_5 \approx 0.086\).
* The near-side peak in the hard-soft correlation has a Gaussian width of about \(0.8\) radian.
* The nuclear modification factor in \(0-5\%\) central collisions is \(R_{AA} \approx 0.18\) at RHIC \(\sqrt{s}=200\ \mathrm{GeV}\), used to fix the jet-medium interaction strength \(\kappa\).

## Context

This work extends the study of harmonic fluctuations in the initial condition of heavy ion collisions, previously shown to generate harmonic flows in the soft bulk matter (Alver 2010, Teaney 2010, Luzum 2011), to the hard probe sector of jet quenching. It builds on the geometric model for jet energy loss with near-\(T_c\) enhancement (Liao 2008, Liao 2011) that successfully described \(R_{AA}\) and \(V_2^{hard}\) data. The computed hard-soft correlation offers a natural explanation for the "hard-ridge" observed in di-hadron correlations at RHIC and LHC, and the framework enables future studies using Monte Carlo initial conditions, extension to non-central collisions, and tighter constraints on jet energy loss models and pre-equilibrium energy loss.
