# Jet Tomography of Harmonic Fluctuations in the Initial Condition of Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this paper we study the jet response (particularly azimuthal anisotropy) as a hard probe of the harmonic fluctuations in the initial condition of central heavy ion collisions. By implementing the fluctuations via cumulant expansion for various harmonics quantified by $ﾎｵ_n$ and using the geometric model for jet energy loss, we compute the response $ﾏⅪh_n=v_n/ﾎｵ_n$. Combining these results with the known hydrodynamic response of the bulk matter expansion in the literature, we show that the hard-soft azimuthal correlation arising from their respective responses to the common geometric fluctuations reveals a robust and narrow near-side peak that may provide the dominant contribution to the "hard-ridge" observed in experimental data.

## Key claim

## Context

This work extends the study of harmonic fluctuations in the initial condition of heavy ion collisions, previously shown to generate harmonic flows in the soft bulk matter (Alver 2010, Teaney 2010, Luzum 2011), to the hard probe sector of jet quenching. It builds on the geometric model for jet energy loss with near-¥(T_c¥) enhancement (Liao 2008, Liao 2011) that successfully described ¥(R_{AA}¥) and ¥(V_2^{hard}¥) data. The computed hard-soft correlation offers a natural explanation for the "hard-ridge" observed in di-hadron correlations at RHIC and LHC, and the framework enables future studies using Monte Carlo initial conditions, extension to non-central collisions, and tighter constraints on jet energy loss models and pre-equilibrium energy loss.
