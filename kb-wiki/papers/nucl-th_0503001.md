# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors and polarization observables in space-like and time-like regions

> We perform a global analysis of the experimental data of the electromagnetic nucleon form factors, in space-like and time-like regions. We give the expressions of the observables in annihilation processes, such as $p+\bar{p}\to \ell^+ +\ell^- $, $\ell=e$ or $μ$, in terms of form factors. We discuss some of the phenomenological models proposed in the literature for the space-like region, and consider their analytical continuation to the time-like region. After determining the parameters through a fit on the available data, we give predictions for the observables which will be experimentally accessible with large statistics, polarized annihilation reactions.

# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors and polarization observables in space-like and time-like regions

## Key claim
The authors perform a global fit of nucleon electromagnetic form factor data across both space-like and time-like regions, but the extracted parameters and predictions for polarization observables depend on which phenomenological model is chosen; the models that fit space-like data equally well give divergent predictions for time-like observables, particularly for the single-spin asymmetry $A_y$ and the double-spin correlation $A_{xz}$.

## Method
The authors collected world data on proton and neutron electric and magnetic form factors in the space-like region (up to $Q^2 \simeq 31$ GeV$^2$) and time-like region (up to $q^2 \simeq 18$ GeV$^2$). They fitted four phenomenological parametrizations, a vector meson dominance model by Iachello, Jackson and Landé (1973), the GKex(02L) extended VMD model by Lomon, the Hohler parametrization (1976), and the Bosted empirical fit (1995), to the combined data set, then analytically continued each model to the time-like region to compute predictions for the differential cross section asymmetry $\mathcal{R}$, the single-spin analyzing power $A_y$, and the double-spin correlation $A_{xz}$ for the reaction $p+\bar{p} \to \ell^+ + \ell^-$.

## Key numbers
- The dipole mass parameter used for normalization is $m_d^2 = 0.71$ GeV$^2$.
- The pQCD-inspired fit to time-like data gives $A(p) = 56.3$ GeV$^4$ for the proton and $A(n) = 77.15$ GeV$^4$ for the neutron, with $\Lambda = 0.3$ GeV.
- The ratio $\mu_p G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}$ from polarization experiments deviates up to 70% at $Q^2 = 5.6$ GeV$^2$ compared to the dipole assumption $G_E = G_M/\mu_p$.
- The Hohler parametrization fit to all space-like form factors yields $\chi^2/\text{ndf} \simeq 1.7$; the Bosted fit yields $\chi^2/\text{ndf} \simeq 2$.
- The models predict large polarization observables in the time-like region, with the Iachello model giving the largest absolute values near $q^2 \simeq 15$ GeV$^2$.

## Context
This work extends earlier global analyses of nucleon form factors by Iachello, Jackson and Landé (1973) and by Lomon (2002) to include both space-like and time-like data simultaneously, and provides the first systematic comparison of polarization observables predicted by different models for the annihilation channel. The results enable future experiments at FAIR (GSI) and Frascati to discriminate between competing VMD and pQCD-inspired descriptions by measuring the angular asymmetry $\mathcal{R}$ and the single-spin asymmetry $A_y$ in $p+\bar{p} \to \ell^+ + \ell^-$, which are sensitive to the relative phase of $G_E$ and $G_M$ in the time-like region. The paper also highlights the unresolved discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization measurements of $G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}$ in the space-like region, as discussed by Jones (2000) and Gayou (2002).
