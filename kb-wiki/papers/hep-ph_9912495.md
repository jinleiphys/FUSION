# P,T-violating electron-nucleon interactions in the R-parity violating minimal supersymmetric standard model

> We show that the present experimental limits on electron-nucleon interactions that violate both parity and time reversal invariance provide new stringent bounds on the imaginary parts of some of the products of the R-parity violating coupling constants in the R-parity violating Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that existing experimental limits on parity- and time-reversal-violating (P,T-violating) electron-nucleon interactions provide new stringent bounds on the imaginary parts of certain products of R-parity violating coupling constants in the R-parity violating Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (RPV-MSSM), specifically for products involving a $\lambda_{1j1}$ and a $\lambda^\prime_{jkk}$ coupling, under the assumption that each such product is the only one with significant size.

## Method

The author derives the tree-level contributions to P,T-violating electron-nucleon interactions arising from combinations of $\lambda_{ijk}$ and $\lambda^\prime_{ijk}$ couplings in the RPV-MSSM, mediated by sneutrino exchange. Using experimental limits on electric dipole moments of $^{205}$Tl, $^{199}$Hg, and the P,T-violating spin-flip parameter of TlF, together with hadronic matrix elements for the quark content of nucleons, the author extracts upper bounds on the imaginary parts of the products $\lambda^*_{1j1}\lambda^\prime_{jkk}$ for $j=2,3$ and $k=1,2,3$.

## Key numbers

- For $|\text{Im}(\lambda^*_{121}\lambda^\prime_{211})|$: upper limit $1.7 \times 10^{-8} (m_{\tilde{\nu}_\mu}/100\text{ GeV})^2$ from $d(^{205}\text{Tl})$
- For $|\text{Im}(\lambda^*_{131}\lambda^\prime_{311})|$: upper limit $1.7 \times 10^{-8} (m_{\tilde{\nu}_\tau}/100\text{ GeV})^2$ from $d(^{205}\text{Tl})$
- For $|\text{Im}(\lambda^*_{121}\lambda^\prime_{222})|$: upper limit $4 \times 10^{-8} (m_{\tilde{\nu}_\mu}/100\text{ GeV})^2$ from $d(^{205}\text{Tl})$
- For $|\text{Im}(\lambda^*_{131}\lambda^\prime_{322})|$: upper limit $4 \times 10^{-8} (m_{\tilde{\nu}_\tau}/100\text{ GeV})^2$ from $d(^{205}\text{Tl})$
- For $|\text{Im}(\lambda^*_{121}\lambda^\prime_{233})|$: upper limit $4 \times 10^{-6} (m_{\tilde{\nu}_\mu}/100\text{ GeV})^2$ from $d(^{205}\text{Tl})$
- For $|\text{Im}(\lambda^*_{131}\lambda^\prime_{333})|$: upper limit $4 \times 10^{-6} (m_{\tilde{\nu}_\tau}/100\text{ GeV})^2$ from $d(^{205}\text{Tl})$
- For $k=3$ and $m_{\tilde{\nu}_j}=100$ GeV, the electron electric dipole moment gives $|\text{Im}(\lambda^*_{1j1}\lambda^\prime_{j33})| \lesssim 6 \times 10^{-7}$, which is more stringent than the atomic limits by an order of magnitude
- The experimental limit on the P,T-violating spin-flip parameter $\nu$ of TlF gives $|0.75 C_{Pp} + 0.25 C_{Pn}| < 3 \times 10^{-4}$ and $|0.75 C_{Tp} + 0.25 C_{Tn}| < 4.5 \times 10^{-7}$

## Context

This work extends the study of P,T-violating electron-nucleon interactions, previously examined in multi-Higgs models by Barr (1992) and He, McKellar, and Pakvasa (1992), and in leptoquark models, to the RPV-MSSM framework. Unlike the R-parity conserving MSSM studied by Fischler, Paban, and Thomas (1991), where such interactions arise only at loop level and are several orders of magnitude below experimental limits, the RPV-MSSM produces tree-level contributions. The bounds derived here for $k=1$ and $k=2$ are the best available on these coupling constant products, while for $k=3$ the electron electric dipole moment provides a stronger constraint. The results enable future experimental improvements in atomic electric dipole moment searches to further constrain or discover CP-violating R-parity violating interactions.
