# Threshold suppression of Lambda spin-orbit splitting

> New experimental data on medium to heavy single Lambda hypernuclei revealed a much larger spin-orbit splitting than observed in older measurements of light hypernuclei. Taking into account particle threshold effects and the density-dependence of in-medium coupling constants the apparent suppression of spin-orbit strength in light hypernuclei as well as the spin-orbit structure observed in medium to heavy nuclei are explained in a unified manner within the density dependent relativistic hadron field theory. It is concluded that the most valuable information on the Lambda spin-orbit dynamics in finite nuclei has to be extracted from medium to heavy mass nuclei.

# Threshold suppression of Lambda spin-orbit splitting

## Key claim
The apparent suppression of Lambda spin-orbit splitting in light hypernuclei and the reduced but sizeable splitting in medium-to-heavy hypernuclei can both be explained within density dependent relativistic hadron (DDRH) field theory without invoking quark substructure or tensor couplings, provided that particle threshold effects and density-dependent in-medium coupling constants are properly included.

## Method
The authors applied density dependent relativistic hadron (DDRH) field theory, an effective Lagrangian approach where medium effects are incorporated by mapping Dirac-Brueckner self-energies onto density-dependent vertices derived from the Bonn A potential and free Lambda-N scattering. They calculated Lambda single-particle spectra for hypernuclei from A=16 to A=208, using localization coefficients to quantify wave function delocalization and artificially varying the Lambda-omega coupling constant to study threshold effects on spin-orbit doublet splitting.

## Key numbers
- For $^{51}_{\Lambda}$V: experimental 1p spin-orbit splitting 1.3±0.3 MeV, theoretical 1.05 MeV; experimental 1d splitting 2.0±0.2 MeV, theoretical 1.81 MeV
- For $^{16}_{\Lambda}$O: reanalysis of older data gave spin-orbit splitting 1.56±0.12 MeV
- For $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C: reanalysis gave spin-orbit splitting ≈0.8 MeV
- Localization coefficients for Lambda 1p$_{3/2}$ states: 0.51 in $^{40}_{\Lambda}$Ca, 0.59 in $^{51}_{\Lambda}$V, 0.68 in $^{89}_{\Lambda}$Y, 0.79 in $^{208}_{\Lambda}$Pb (compared to neutron values 0.71, 0.75, 0.81, 0.88 respectively)
- New KEK experiments show Lambda spin-orbit splitting in medium-to-heavy hypernuclei in the range 1–2 MeV

## Context
This work extends the DDRH field theory of Fuchs (1995) and Keil (2000) to explain the mass dependence of Lambda spin-orbit splitting. It contradicts earlier quark-model explanations by Mares (1994), Chiapparini (1991), and Tsushima (1998) that invoked a Lambda-omega tensor coupling to cancel the spin-orbit force, as well as the conjecture by Pirner (1979) of partial deconfinement. The results reinterpret older emulsion data by Dalitz (1986, 1997) on $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C and $^{16}_{\Lambda}$O and show that the new KEK data by Nagae (1999) on $^{51}_{\Lambda}$V and $^{89}_{\Lambda}$Y provide cleaner information on the generic Lambda spin-orbit strength, enabling reliable extraction of hyperon-nucleon interaction parameters from medium-mass hypernuclei.
