# Production of $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, and $ψ(4415)$ mesons in hadronic matter

> We present the first study of production of $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, and $ψ(4415)$ mesons in hadronic matter. Quark interchange between two colliding charmed mesons leads to the production of the three mesons. We calculate unpolarized cross sections for the reactions, $D\bar{D} \to ρR$, $D\bar{D}^* \to πR$, $D\bar{D}^* \to ρR$, $D^*\bar{D} \to πR$, $D^*\bar{D} \to ρR$, $D^*\bar{D}^* \to πR$, and $D^*\bar{D}^* \to ρR$, where $R$ stands for $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, and $ψ(4415)$. In the temperature region covering hadronic matter the peak cross sections of producing $ψ(4040)$ are similar to or larger than the ones of producing $ψ(4415)$, and the latter are generally larger than those of producing $ψ(4160)$. With the cross sections we establish new master rate equations for $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, and $ψ(4415)$. The equations are solved for central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV at the Large Hadron Collider. Solutions of the equations show that the $ψ(4040)$ number density at kinetic freeze-out of hadronic matter is larger than the $ψ(4415)$ number density which is larger than the $ψ(4160)$ number density.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first study of production of ψ(4040), ψ(4160), and ψ(4415) mesons in hadronic matter via quark interchange between two colliding charmed mesons, but the production is only possible in the temperature range 0.6Tc ≤ T < Tc where the charmonium masses are smaller than the sum of two open-charm mesons, meaning fusion of two open-charm mesons is not allowed.

## Method

The authors calculate unpolarized cross sections for seven reaction channels (D D̅ → ρR, D D̅* → πR, D D̅* → ρR, D* D̅ → πR, D* D̅ → ρR, D* D̅* → πR, and D* D̅* → ρR, where R stands for ψ(4040), ψ(4160), or ψ(4415)) using a quark interchange mechanism with a temperature-dependent potential derived from lattice gauge calculations and perturbative QCD. They then establish new master rate equations incorporating these cross sections and solve them for central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV at the LHC, using relativistic hydrodynamics to provide the temperature and transverse velocity evolution of the hadronic matter.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature Tc = 0.175 GeV used as the boundary between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter
- Constituent quark masses used: up/down quark = 0.32 GeV, strange quark = 0.5 GeV, charm quark = 1.51 GeV
- The confining potential parameter ξ1 = 0.525 GeV, ξ2 = 1.5[0.75+0.25(T/Tc)^10]^6 GeV, ξ3 = 0.6 GeV
- The smearing parameters d1 = 0.15 GeV and d2 = 0.705 in the spin-spin interaction
- The string slope parameter α' = 1.04 GeV^(-2) used in the potential
- At zero temperature, the central spin-independent potential Vc̅c inside ψ(4160) approaches 0.91 GeV at long distances
- The ψ(4040) number density at kinetic freeze-out is larger than the ψ(4415) number density, which is larger than the ψ(4160) number density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium production in hadronic matter by Xu and collaborators (2019) who studied J/ψ, ψ', and ψ(3770) production via similar quark interchange mechanisms, and by the Mark I, DASP, BES, CLEO, Belle, BaBar, and BES III collaborations who measured the masses, decay widths, and decay modes of these three mesons in e+e- collisions. The results enable future experimental searches for ψ(4040), ψ(4160), and ψ(4415) production in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, and provide a framework for understanding how these higher-mass charmonia are regenerated in hadronic matter after the quark-gluon plasma phase.
