# Phase-Space Coalescence for heavy and light quarks at RHIC

> We review the application and successes of a phase-space coalescence plus fragmentation model that has been applied for hadronization at RHIC. The physical concept is discussed together with the practical implementation. The robustness of main predictions is reviewed together with several open issues like relevance of three dimensional calculation, finite width of the wave functions, effects of quark masses, energy-entropy conservation, space-momentum correlation. Eventually the relevance of coalescence also for the study of the microscopic interaction of heavy quarks is highlighted.

## Key claim
The paper reviews a phase-space coalescence plus fragmentation model for hadronization at RHIC, arguing that while coalescence successfully describes several experimental features (enhanced baryon/meson ratios, constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow), the simplest implementation has several open issues including energy-entropy conservation, finite wave function width effects, and space-momentum correlations that require more advanced formulations.

## Method
The model uses a Wigner formalism where hadron transverse momentum spectra are computed from the overlap between hadron wave functions and the product of single-quark phase-space distribution functions, evaluated via Monte Carlo integration in full 6D phase space. For light quarks, thermal distributions (T=170 MeV) with radial flow (β₀=0.5) are used up to p₀=2 GeV, while minijet partons from pQCD with energy loss (GLV model, L/λ=3.5) are used above that threshold, with constituent quark masses (m_{u,d}=300 MeV, m_s=475 MeV) for thermal partons and current quark masses for minijets.

## Key numbers
- Energy density threshold for QGP formation: ε_c ≈ 0.7 GeV/fm³, with RHIC reaching ε ≥ 10 GeV/fm³
- Hadronization temperature: T = 170 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD phase transition temperature
- Constituent quark masses used: m_{u,d}=300 MeV, m_s=475 MeV, m_c=1.4 GeV, m_b=4.8 GeV
- Wave function width parameter: Δ_p = 0.24 GeV for mesons, 0.36 GeV for baryons
- Fireball volume: 950 fm³ (transverse radius R=8.1 fm, longitudinal length τ=4.4 fm)
- Radial flow parameter: β₀ = 0.5
- Chemical potential: μ_q = 10 MeV, giving light antiquark to quark ratio of 0.89
- Effective opacity for minijet energy loss: L/λ = 3.5
- Entropy of the system: dS/dy ≈ 4800
- Energy density at hadronization: 0.8 GeV/fm³
- Transverse energy: 750 GeV
- Number of quarks and antiquarks: dN/dy ≈ 1300 for most central collisions

## Context
This work extends the coalescence approach originally applied to light cluster production in nuclear physics and the ALCOR model by Zimanyi's group, and was developed alongside similar models by Hwa, Fries, and Molnar (2002-2003) to explain RHIC data including the baryon/meson enhancement at intermediate p_T and constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow. The review identifies open issues including energy conservation (later investigated by Ravagli and Rapp, 2007), entropy conservation, finite wave function effects, and space-momentum correlations, while highlighting the model's potential for studying heavy quark in-medium interactions and quarkonia in the QGP.
