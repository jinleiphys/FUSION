# Triaxial deformation in 10Be

> The triaxial deformation in $^{10}$Be is investigated using a microscopic $α+α+n+n$ model. The states of two valence neutrons are classified based on the molecular-orbit (MO) model, and the $π$-orbit is introduced about the axis connecting the two $α$-clusters for the description of the rotational bands. There appear two rotational bands comprised mainly of $K^π= 0^+$ and $K^π= 2^+$, respectively, at low excitation energy, where the two valence neutrons occupy $K^π= 3/2^-$ or $K^π= 1/2^-$ orbits. The triaxiality and the $K$-mixing are discussed in connection to the molecular structure, particularly, to the spin-orbit splitting. The extent of the triaxial deformation is evaluated in terms of the electro-magnetic transition matrix elements (Davydov-Filippov model, Q-invariant model), and density distribution in the intrinsic frame. The obtained values turned out to be $γ= 15^o \sim 20^o$.

# Triaxial deformation in 10Be

## Key claim

The triaxial deformation of 10Be is evaluated to be γ = 15° ∼ 20°, but this result depends on the specific model interpretation (Davydov-Filippov model and Q-invariant model) and is derived from the recoil effect of valence neutrons breaking the axial symmetry of the α-α core, rather than from an intrinsically triaxial core.

## Method

A microscopic α+α+n+n model was used, where the two valence neutrons are described by molecular-orbit (MO) wave functions built from p-orbits around two α-clusters. The wave functions were fully antisymmetrized Slater determinants with Gaussian single-particle wave functions, and the parameters for the Gaussian centers were variationally determined using the cooling method. Angular momentum projection and generator coordinate method (GCM) were applied, mixing three configurations: Φ((3/2⁻)²), Φ((1/2⁻)²), and Φ(3/2⁻ 1/2⁻), with the Volkov No.2 central interaction and G3RS spin-orbit interaction.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic density contour-line analysis gives β ∼ 0.5 and γ = 11° for the Φ((3/2⁻)²) configuration at α-α distance 3 fm.
- The ground 0⁺ state is calculated at −60.5 MeV; the first 2⁺ state (K=0 band) at −56.9 MeV (E_x = 3.1 MeV, experimentally 3.358 MeV); the second 2⁺ state (K=2 band) at −54.9 MeV (E_x = 5.6 MeV, experimentally 5.958 MeV).
- B(E2: 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) = 11.8 e²fm⁴, compared to experimental 10.04 ± 1.2 e²fm⁴.
- B(E2: 2⁺₁ → 2⁺₂) = 3.99 e²fm⁴ (interband transition).
- The ratio B(E2: 2⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) / B(E2: 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) = 0.34, corresponding to γ ≈ 19° in the Davydov-Filippov model.
- The ratio B(E2: 2⁺₂ → 0⁺₁) / B(E2: 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) = 0.059, corresponding to γ = 17° and 22°.
- The Q-invariant model gives γ = 20.1°.
- B(E2: 4⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) = 11.1 e²fm⁴, with ratio to B(E2: 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) of 0.94 (rigid-body limit is 1.43).
- Magnetic dipole moments predicted: μ = 0.72 μ_N for 2⁺₁ and 0.48 μ_N for 2⁺₂.
- With increased spin-orbit strength (V^{ls}_0 = 2500 MeV), B(E2: 2⁺₁ → 2⁺₂) decreases to 2.20 e²fm⁴; with decreased strength (V^{ls}_0 = 1500 MeV), it increases to 9.53 e²fm⁴; with V^{ls}_0 = 1000 MeV, it becomes 17.53 e²fm⁴.

## Context

This work extends the molecular-orbit model previously applied to 10Be by Itagaki et al. (2000) to specifically address triaxial deformation, contrasting with the deformed oscillator model prediction of γ = 34.8° by Harvey (1977). The calculated γ range of 15°–20° is significantly smaller than the Harvey prediction and provides a theoretical basis for interpreting experimental searches for triaxial structure in 10Be by Curtis (2001). The results enable future exploration of triaxial deformations in 12Be and 14Be, which have weakly bound neutrons and deformed cores, and connect to discussions of di-neutron clustering in halo nuclei such as 6He studied by Aoyama (2000).
