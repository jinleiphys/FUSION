# Pseudo-spin Symmetry and Relativistic Single-nucleon Wave Functions

> We show that the occurrence of approximate pseudo-spin symmetry in nuclei is connected with certain similarities in the relativistic single-nucleon wave functions of the corresponding pseudo-spin doublets. We perform a case study in which several examples and the systematics of this connection are explored.

# Pseudo-spin Symmetry and Relativistic Single-nucleon Wave Functions

## Key claim

The authors show that approximate pseudo-spin symmetry in nuclei arises from the near equality in magnitude of attractive scalar and repulsive vector relativistic mean fields, and that this symmetry manifests in the lower components of Dirac single-nucleon wave functions being approximately equal up to a sign for pseudo-spin doublet partners. The symmetry becomes increasingly valid as the pseudo-orbital angular momentum decreases and as the binding energy decreases, but exact symmetry would require no bound Dirac valence states, which would contradict the existence of bound nuclei.

## Method

The authors employ a self-consistent Dirac-Hartree model with contact interactions (point couplings) containing four-, six-, and eight-fermion interactions with derivative terms, using nine coupling constants determined by fitting to ground-state masses, rms charge radii, and spin-orbit splittings for ten spherical closed-shell nuclei from 16O to 208Pb. They calculate the Dirac single-nucleon wave functions and eigenvalues for the six neutron and four proton pseudo-spin doublets in 208Pb, then compare the lower components of the wave functions for doublet partners to test the predicted relation f_{-ℓ̃}(r) = -f_{ℓ̃+1}(r) that holds in the exact pseudo-spin limit.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation established by Ginocchio (1997) showing that pseudo-spin symmetry arises from the near equality of scalar and vector mean fields, which had been discovered empirically by Hecht and Adler (1969) and Arima, Harvey, and Shimizu (1969). The authors use a relativistic point coupling model developed by Nikolaus, Hoch, and Madland (1992) to perform the first realistic calculation demonstrating the connection between pseudo-spin symmetry and the lower components of Dirac wave functions in a heavy nucleus (208Pb). This enables future studies of pseudo-spin symmetry in deformed nuclei and provides a microscopic foundation for the pseudo-spin shell model coupling scheme used to explain identical rotational bands in nuclei.
