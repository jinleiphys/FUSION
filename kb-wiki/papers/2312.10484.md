# The 95 GeV Excess in the Georgi-Machacek Model: Single or Twin Peak Resonance

> In this work, we investigate the possibility to address the excess observed around 95 GeV in the $γγ$, $ττ$, and $b\bar{b}$ channels as a scalar resonance(s) within the Georgi-Machacek (GM) model. In our analysis, we find that the excess can be easily accommodated in the channels ($γγ$ and $b\bar{b}$) simultaneously, where the 95 GeV candidate is a single peak resonance (SPR) due to a light CP-even scalar. We found that the excess in the $ττ$ channel can be addressed simultaneously with $γγ$ and $b\bar{b}$ only if the 95 GeV candidate is a twin peak resonance (TPR), i.e., another CP-odd scalar in addition to the CP-even scalar. We demonstrate that the nature of the 95 GeV scalar resonance candidate (SPR or TPR) can be probed via the properties of its di-$τ$ decay.

## Key claim

The 95 GeV excess observed in the γγ, ττ, and b̅b channels can be accommodated within the Georgi-Machacek (GM) model, but the ττ channel can only be simultaneously addressed with the other two channels if the 95 GeV candidate is a twin peak resonance (TPR) consisting of both a CP-even scalar (η) and a CP-odd scalar (H₃⁰), rather than a single peak resonance (SPR) from a CP-even scalar alone.

## Method

The author performed a numerical scan of the GM model parameter space, imposing theoretical constraints (tree-level unitarity, boundedness from below, vacuum stability) and experimental constraints (Higgs signal strength modifiers, Higgs total decay width, oblique parameter S, LEP and LHC negative searches for light scalars, doubly-charged Higgs searches, and b→sγ flavor constraints). Signal strength modifiers for the 95 GeV candidate were computed within the narrow width approximation for both the SPR case (only η) and TPR case (η plus H₃⁰), and a χ² analysis was used to identify viable benchmark points that match the observed excess values within 2σ.

## Key numbers

- Experimental excess values used: μ_{γγ}^{exp} = 0.27_{-0.09}^{+0.10}, μ_{ττ}^{exp} = 1.2 ± 0.5, μ_{b̅b}^{exp} = 0.117 ± 0.057
- Best fit SPR benchmark point BP₁: χ²₍₃₎ = 3.487, with μ_{γγ}^{(95)} = 0.257, μ_{ττ}^{(95)} = 0.562, μ_{b̅b}^{(95)} = 0.095
- Best fit TPR benchmark point BP₂: χ²₍₃₎ = 1.796, with μ_{γγ}^{(95)} = 0.251, μ_{ττ}^{(95)} = 0.278, μ_{b̅b}^{(95)} = 0.104
- Best matching of γγ and b̅b channels only (BP₃): χ²₍₂₎ = 3.7 × 10⁻⁶, with μ_{γγ}^{(95)} = 0.270, μ_{b̅b}^{(95)} = 0.117
- In the TPR case, the H₃⁰ contribution to μ_{ττ}^{(95)} ranges from 14% to 58% (ρ_{ττ} = 0.14 to 0.58)
- Higgs coupling modifiers for viable BPs lie in ranges 0.90584 < κ_F < 1.0591 and 0.89045 < κ_V < 0.99083
- Only 0.0059% of SPR BPs achieve χ²₍₃₎ < 3.53 (1σ for three channels), while 27.5% of TPR BPs achieve this

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the 95 GeV excess in various beyond-Standard-Model frameworks, including the two-Higgs-doublet model and other extensions (Biekotter 2023, Crivellin 2017, Cao 2019, and many others). It builds on prior GM model analyses (Ahriche 2022, Bairi 2022) that established viable parameter space for a light CP-even scalar. The key advance is demonstrating that the ττ excess requires a twin peak resonance with a CP-odd scalar H₃⁰, and that the acoplanarity angle distribution in di-τ decays can distinguish between the SPR and TPR scenarios. Future ATLAS results on the di-τ channel with improved precision could confirm or rule out the TPR interpretation.
