# Pigmy resonances in artificial nuclei: far-infrared absorption by electron-hole droplets

> The structure of E1 resonances is examined in a microscopic random phase approximation calculation for neutral, symmetric, closed shell, electron-hole systems in a quantum dot. The number of electron-hole pairs, N, is varied from 6 to 42. The ocurrence of small, but distinct, E1 peaks in the far infrared spectra located in the low energy tail of the giant dipole resonance and consisting of highly coherent electron-hole excitations is predicted. These pigmy resonances account for about 2 % of the dipole energy-weighted photoabsorption sum rule. A very weak dependence of the average pigmy resonance energy on the number of electron-hole pairs is found.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of pygmy resonances in neutral, symmetric, closed-shell electron-hole systems confined in a quantum dot, analogous to those observed in atomic nuclei, but only for systems with more than two electron-hole pairs (N greater than 2).

## Method

The authors performed microscopic random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for a two-dimensional disk-shaped quantum dot with parabolic confinement, using a large harmonic oscillator basis of approximately 20 Planck quanta. They considered neutral systems with equal electron and hole masses, varying the number of electron-hole pairs N from 6 to 42, and computed the dipole strength distribution for far-infrared absorption at a Coulomb coupling parameter beta equal to 1.

## Key numbers

- Pygmy resonances account for about 2 percent of the dipole energy-weighted photoabsorption sum rule.
- The average pygmy resonance energy lies at about 0.5 to 0.6 times the giant dipole resonance (GDR) energy.
- The GDR energy follows the approximate dependence Delta E_GDR minus 1 equals 0.7 plus 0.4 times N to the one-fourth power.
- The average pygmy resonance energy follows the approximate dependence Delta E_pygmy minus 1 equals 0.7 minus 0.1 times N to the one-fourth power.
- The total number of pygmy resonances is equal to N_s minus 1, where N_s is the last filled two-dimensional harmonic oscillator shell.
- No pygmy resonances are obtained for the case of two confined pairs (N equals 2).

## Context

This work extends the analogy between electron-hole droplets in quantum dots and atomic nuclei, previously established by the same authors (Delgado et al., 2000) for the giant dipole resonance, to the phenomenon of pygmy resonances. The results are compared to experimental observations of pygmy resonances in lead isotopes (Enders et al., 2000) and other nuclei such as Ba, Sn, Ce, and Y studied by Herzberg et al. (1997, 1999) and Govaert et al. (1998). The findings enable the study of collective dipole oscillations in artificial quantum systems as a testbed for nuclear structure phenomena.
