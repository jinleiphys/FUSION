# A note on inequalities for the masses of the lightest pi-pi resonances in large N_c QCD

## Key claim
The authors derive inequalities relating the masses of the lightest pi-pi resonances (rho and sigma) to low-energy constants of the effective chiral Lagrangian in the large-N_c limit, showing that the sigma meson mass is bounded from above by approximately 770 MeV under the assumption 2L_2+L_3=0, with the bound sensitive to the sign of the parameter Delta defined from the LECs.

## Method
The authors start from large-N_c sum rules derived in prior work (BolPolVer, BolManPolVer) that relate the low-energy constants L_i of the fourth-order effective chiral Lagrangian to resonance spectrum parameters (masses M_I and residues V_I of pi-pi resonances with isospin I). They manipulate these sum rules using inequalities for the sums over resonance contributions, isolating the contributions of the lightest resonances (rho and sigma) to obtain upper bounds on M_sigma. They then enhance the inequality by including known excited resonances (f_2(1275) and rho_3(1690)) and express the bound as a function of the parameter Delta = -(2L_2+L_3)/L_2.

## Key numbers
- F_pi ≈ 93 MeV (pion decay constant)
- F_0 ≈ 88 MeV (pion decay constant in the chiral limit)
- L_2 = (1.6 ± 0.1) × 10^{-3} (large-N_c value from Bij)
- L_3 = -(4 ± 1) × 10^{-3} (large-N_c value from Bij)
- alpha m_pi^2 ≈ 0.18 × 10^{-3} (sixth-order LEC estimate from PolVer)
- beta m_pi^2 ≈ 0.05 × 10^{-3} (sixth-order LEC estimate from PolVer)
- Lower bound on L_2 from inequality: L_2 > 1.66 × 10^{-3}
- Upper bound on M_sigma assuming 2L_2+L_3=0: M_sigma < 770 MeV
- Enhanced upper bound including f_2(1275) and rho_3(1690): M_sigma < 665 [1 + 0.44 Delta + 0.33 Delta^2 + O(Delta^3)] MeV
- Experimental constraint on Delta from D-wave scattering lengths: Delta ≈ -0.2 ± 0.6

## Context
This work extends the large-N_c sum rules derived by BolPolVer and BolManPolVer, which relate effective chiral Lagrangian constants to resonance parameters, and applies them to the controversial question of the light scalar sigma meson (reviewed by Spanier and Tornqvist in the Review of Particle Physics). The inequalities provide model-independent constraints that can be used to test various models of low-energy QCD in the large-N_c limit, including those from DiaEid, Bal, And, DiaPet, Espriu, and Bij. The results enable consistency checks of the nature of low-lying mesons, particularly distinguishing q\bar q components from exotic states like glueballs or four-quark states whose contributions are suppressed by powers of 1/N_c.
