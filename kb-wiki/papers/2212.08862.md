# Quantum computing of the pairing Hamiltonian at finite temperatures

> In this work, we study the pairing Hamiltonian with four particles at finite temperatures on a quantum simulator and a superconducting quantum computer. The excited states are obtained by the variational quantum deflation (VQD). The error-mitigation methods are applied to improve the noisy results. The simulation of thermal excitation states is performed using the same variational circuit as at zero temperature. The results from quantum computing become close to exact solutions at high temperatures, and demonstrate a smooth superfluid-normal phase transition as a function of temperatures as expected in finite systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) approach, using the same variational circuit for both ground and excited states, can simulate the finite-temperature pairing Hamiltonian for small systems (4 particles in degenerate shells), with results approaching exact solutions at high temperatures and showing a smooth superfluid-normal phase transition, but only when the system is small enough that the false phase transition predicted by finite-temperature BCS theory is absent.

## Method

The authors mapped the pairing Hamiltonian for N=4 particles in degenerate j-shells (Omega=3 and Omega=4) onto 3-qubit and 4-qubit systems using quasi-spin operators and the Jordan-Wigner transformation. They obtained ground states via VQE and excited states via variational quantum deflation (VQD), then constructed thermal states as superpositions of these eigenstates weighted by Boltzmann factors, with variational parameters optimized by minimizing the free energy. Calculations were performed on the Qiskit simulator and on the IBM_oslo superconducting quantum processor, with readout error mitigation and zero-noise extrapolation applied to noisy results.

## Key numbers

- For Omega=3 (3 qubits): ground state energy from Qiskit simulator was -4.013 G, exact value -4.0 G; overlap between simulated and exact ground state wavefunction was 0.9994
- For Omega=3: first excited state energies from Qiskit were -1.024 G and -1.004 G, exact value -1.0 G
- For Omega=3 on IBM_oslo: raw ground state energy was -3.751 G (6% deviation from simulator); after readout error mitigation, -3.871 G; after zero-noise extrapolation with 2 additional CNOT gates, -4.04 G
- For Omega=4 (4 qubits): ground state energy from Qiskit was -5.958 G, exact value -6.0 G; on IBM_oslo raw value was -4.534 G (24% deviation)
- IBM_oslo processor: median CNOT error about 8.3e-3, median readout error about 2.2e-2; 15000 shots per measurement
- Critical temperature in FT-BCS theory: kT_c = 0.5 * Delta_0, where Delta_0 = G*Omega/2

## Context

This work extends prior quantum computing studies of nuclear pairing Hamiltonians and the Lipkin model (Lipkin1, Lipkin2, 2020-2021) by adding finite-temperature treatment via VQE/VQD. It contrasts with finite-temperature BCS theory (Goodman, 1981), which predicts a false pairing phase transition in finite systems that is washed out by exact treatment. The approach enables future quantum simulations of thermal properties of small nuclear systems, such as level densities and shape transitions, using near-term quantum devices with error mitigation.
