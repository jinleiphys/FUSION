# Non-extensive Fokker-Planck transport coefficients of heavy quarks

> In presence of the non-ideal plasma effects, Heavy Quarks (HQs) carry out non linear random walk inside Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) and in the small momentum transfer limit, the evolution of the HQ distribution is dictated by the Non Linear Fokker-Planck Equation (NLFPE). Using the NLFPE, we calculate the transport coefficients (drag and diffusion) of heavy quarks travelling through QGP. We observe substantial modification in the momentum and temperature variation of the transport coefficients; and this will modify the physical picture we are having about the transport of heavy quarks inside QGP, and hence, about the characterisation of the plasma.

## Key claim

The paper derives non-extensive (Tsallis-type) Fokker-Planck transport coefficients for heavy quarks in quark-gluon plasma, showing that introducing a correlation between the heavy quark and medium particles (parameterized by the Tsallis parameter δq) substantially increases the drag and diffusion coefficients compared to the standard extensive case, with the modification vanishing as δq → 0.

## Method

Starting from the non-linear Boltzmann transport equation with a modified collision term that incorporates a correlated two-particle distribution function within the Tsallis non-extensive framework, the authors perform a space averaging using a temperature profile and then take the small momentum transfer (Landau kinetic) limit to derive expressions for the non-extensive Fokker-Planck drag and diffusion coefficients. The transport coefficients are evaluated numerically for charm and bottom quarks scattering elastically with light quarks and gluons, using the collisional matrix element from Combridge (1979), with the medium temperature profile parameters T_p = 290 MeV, a = 5.99, and r_0 = 7.96 fm.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass taken as 1.3 GeV and bottom quark mass as 4.2 GeV.
* Medium temperature T_q = 350 MeV used for momentum-dependent plots; incoming momentum of 5 GeV used for temperature-dependent plots.
* For charm or bottom quarks with 10 GeV momentum in a 350 MeV medium, the non-extensive drag coefficient and transverse diffusion coefficient are approximately 3 times larger than their extensive counterparts; the parallel diffusion coefficient is approximately 2.5 times larger.
* The Tsallis parameter δq = 0.01 is used for the transport coefficient plots; at δq = 0.001 the non-extensive functions approach the extensive medium distribution f(q).

## Context

This work extends the standard linear Fokker-Planck treatment of heavy quark transport (Svetitsky 1988) by incorporating non-ideal plasma effects via the Tsallis non-extensive statistical framework (Tsallis 1988). It builds on prior derivations of the non-linear Boltzmann equation (Lavagno 2002) and its application to Fokker-Planck equations (Wolschin 2006; Lavagno 2009). The results enable future calculation of heavy quark stopping power and nuclear modification factor R_AA within the non-extensive formalism, which can be compared with experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
