# A Diquark Chiral Effective Theory and Exotic Baryons

> A chiral effective theory of quarks and diquarks is formulated and applied to exotic tetraquarks and pentaquarks. The effective theory is similar to the chiral quark effective theory with the addition of diquark degrees of freedom and couplings to quarks. Chiral symmetry through generalized Goldberger-Treiman relations, fixes the mass splitting between the even and odd parity diquarks to be about 600 MeV. We provide an estimate of the parameters of the effective theory from both the random instanton model and known data on the low lying scalar nonet. We assess the static properties of the exotic baryons, such as their masses, magnetic moments and decay widths. We show that the small decay widths of the newly reported exotics are largely due to a large tunnelling suppression of a quark between a pair of diquarks. We find $Γ(Θ^+\to K^+n)\simeq 2.5\sim7.0 MeV$ and $Γ(Ξ^{--}\toΞ^- π^-)\simeq 1.7\sim4.8 MeV$.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a chiral effective theory of quarks and diquarks to describe exotic tetraquarks and pentaquarks, arguing that the small decay widths of the newly reported exotics (such as the Θ⁺ and Ξ⁻⁻) are largely due to a large tunneling suppression of a quark between a pair of diquarks, rather than being a generic feature of all strong decays.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian for scalar and tensor diquarks (color antitriplet, flavor antitriplet and sextet) coupled to quarks and pseudoscalar mesons, analogous to the chiral quark effective theory of Georgi and Manohar. They estimate the parameters of the effective theory using both the random instanton model of the QCD vacuum and empirical data on the low-lying scalar nonet (treated as tetraquark bound states of diquark–anti-diquark). They then compute static properties (masses, magnetic moments, decay widths) of exotic baryons in the antidecuplet, using a WKB approximation for the tunneling amplitude in the decay process.

## Key numbers

* Mass splitting between even and odd parity diquarks (scalar and pseudoscalar, or magnetic and electric tensor) is about 600 MeV, fixed by chiral symmetry via generalized Goldberger–Treiman relations.
* Scalar diquark mass from the random instanton model: M_S = 420 ± 30 MeV; tensor diquark mass: M_T = 570 ± 20 MeV; scalar coupling G_S = 0.22 ± 0.01 GeV²; tensor coupling G_T = 0.13 ± 0.00 GeV².
* From the scalar nonet data, for M_S = 420 MeV, the extracted scalar coupling squared is g_S² ≈ 3.03, and the binding energy B ≈ 0.28 GeV.
* Mass difference between Ξ_{3/2}⁻⁻ and Θ⁺ is predicted to be 180–320 MeV.
* Decay width Γ(Θ⁺ → K⁺ n) ≈ 2.5–7.0 MeV.
* Decay width Γ(Ξ⁻⁻ → Ξ⁻ π⁻) ≈ 1.7–4.8 MeV.
* Magnetic moment of Θ⁺ for J^P = 1/2⁺: μ_m(Θ⁺) = 0.71 n.m. for M_S = 400 MeV, and 0.56 n.m. for M_S = 450 MeV; for J^P = 3/2⁺: μ_m(Θ⁺) = 2.21–2.06 n.m. for the same mass range.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark effective theory of Georgi and Manohar (1983) by adding diquark degrees of freedom, motivated by earlier quark-diquark models for multiquark states (Jaffe 2003, Shuryak 2003, Karliner 2003). It provides a framework to compute static properties of exotic baryons (antidecuplet and 27-plet) and explains the narrow widths of the Θ⁺ and Ξ⁻⁻ as a tunneling suppression unique to diquark-containing exotics. The results enable future calculations of magnetic moments, axial charges, and bound states in tetraquark and pentaquark channels, and can be compared with lattice QCD calculations (Csikor 2003) and experiments (NA49, LEPS).
