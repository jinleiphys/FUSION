# Deuteron-proton charge exchange reaction at small transfer momentum

> The charge-exchange reaction pd -> npp at 1 GeV projectile proton energy is studied. This reaction is considered in a special kinematics, when the transfer momentum from the beam proton to fast outgoing neutron is close to zero. Our approach is based on the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formulation of the multiple-scattering theory for the three-nucleon system. The matrix inversion method has been applied to take account of the final state interaction (FSI) contributions. The differential cross section, tensor analyzing power $C_{0,yy}$, vector-vector $C_{y,y}$ and vector-tensor $C_{y,xz}$ spin correlation parameters of the initial particles are presented. It is shown, that the FSI effects play a very important role under such kinematical conditions. The high sensitivity of the considered observables to the elementary nucleon-nucleon amplitudes has been obtained.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the deuteron-proton charge exchange reaction at 1 GeV with near-zero transfer momentum, the final state interaction (FSI) between the two slow protons is significant for the differential cross section even at very small proton momenta, while polarization observables are insensitive to FSI and D-wave effects only for proton momenta up to 10–15 MeV/c, beyond which model-dependent corrections become necessary.

## Method

The authors employed the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formulation of multiple-scattering theory for the three-nucleon system, using a matrix inversion method to include final state interaction contributions. The nucleon-nucleon amplitudes were described using the Love and Franey parametrization fitted to the SP00 phase shift data, and the Paris NN-potential with the Paris deuteron wave function was used. Calculations were performed for the special collinear kinematics where the transfer momentum from the beam proton to the fast outgoing neutron is set to zero, and the differential cross section, tensor analyzing power \(C_{0,yy}\), vector-vector spin correlation \(C_{y,y}\), and vector-tensor spin correlation \(C_{y,xz}\) were computed.

## Key numbers

- Transfer momentum is approximately \(q \approx 1.8\) MeV/c due to mass differences and deuteron binding energy, but set to \(q=0\) in calculations.
- FSI effects on polarization observables become visible for proton momentum \(p_2 \ge 10-15\) MeV/c.
- The vector-tensor spin correlation \(C_{y,xz}\) has a very small magnitude, \(|C_{y,xz}| \approx 0.06\).
- The vector-vector spin correlation \(C_{y,y}\) is close to the theoretical limit of \(-2/3\), indicating approximate equality of the nucleon-nucleon spin-flip amplitudes \(|B| \approx |F|\) and a relative phase near zero.
- The tensor analyzing power \(C_{0,yy}\) is near zero in the momentum interval of interest, consistent with \(|B| \approx |F|\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the deuteron-proton charge exchange reaction at 1 GeV, building on the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) approach suggested by Pomeranchuk (1951) and developed by Dean, Wilkin, and Car (various years). The study is motivated by an experiment planned at the ANKE setup at COSY, which aims to extract spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon charge exchange amplitudes in an energy region where phase-shift analysis data are absent. The results show that FSI corrections are essential for extracting absolute values of spin-flip amplitudes from the differential cross section, but that polarization observables at very small proton momenta (below 10–15 MeV/c) can provide a relatively model-independent determination of the ratio and phase of the nucleon-nucleon amplitudes.
