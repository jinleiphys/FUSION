# Anisotropic magnetized neutron star

> As we know, the effect of strong magnetic field causes the anisotropy for the magnetized compact objects. Therefore, in this paper, we have studied the structure properties of anisotropic case of magnetized neutron star. We have derived the equation of state (EoS) of neutron star matter for two forms of magnetic fields, one uniform and one density dependent. We have solved the generalized Tolman Oppenheimer Volkoff equations to examine the maximum mass and corresponding radius, Schwarzschild radius, graviitational redshift, Kretschmann scalar, and Buchdahl theorem for this system. It was shown that the maximum mass and radius of neutron star are increasing functions of the magnetic field. Also redshift, strength of gravity, and Kretschmann scalar increase as the magnetic field increases. In addition, the dynamical stability of anisotrop neutron star has been investigated, and finally a comparison with the empirical results has been made.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the maximum mass, radius, gravitational redshift, compactness, and Kretschmann scalar of a neutron star all increase with increasing magnetic field strength, but only under the assumption that the magnetic field induces a pressure anisotropy (radial pressure differs from tangential pressure). The abstract glosses over the fact that these results are obtained for a pure neutron matter equation of state computed via the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method, and that the anisotropy is a direct consequence of the magnetic field contribution to the energy-momentum tensor.

## Method

The authors used the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to compute the energy per particle of spin-polarized neutron matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field, including the magnetic energy contribution from the neutron magnetic moment. From this energy, they derived the equation of state (EoS) with two pressure components: radial pressure (matter pressure minus magnetic pressure) and tangential pressure (matter pressure plus magnetic pressure). They then solved the generalized Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for an anisotropic fluid to obtain the mass, radius, Schwarzschild radius, compactness, gravitational redshift, Kretschmann scalar, and Buchdahl-Bondi bound for two magnetic field profiles: a uniform field (constant B) and a density-dependent Gaussian field.

## Key numbers

*   For zero magnetic field (B=0): maximum mass M_max = 1.68 solar masses, radius R = 9.0 km.
*   For uniform B = 1e17 G: M_max = 1.92 solar masses, R = 9.8 km.
*   For uniform B = 5e17 G: M_max = 2.05 solar masses, R = 9.8 km.
*   For uniform B = 8e17 G: M_max = 2.11 solar masses, R = 9.9 km.
*   For the Gaussian (density-dependent) magnetic field: M_max = 2.09 solar masses, R = 10.03 km.
*   Maximum Schwarzschild radius obtained: R_Sch = 6.2 km (for B = 8e17 G).
*   Maximum gravitational redshift: z = 0.64 (for B = 8e17 G).
*   Maximum Kretschmann scalar: K = 0.24e-7 m^{-2} (for B = 8e17 G).
*   Maximum stable magnetic field for the most massive star (M=2.11 solar masses, R=9.9 km): B_max = 9.35e17 G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of isotropic neutron stars (Bordbar et al., various years) by incorporating pressure anisotropy induced by a strong magnetic field, following the framework of Bowers and Liang (1974) for anisotropic fluids. It also builds on earlier calculations of spin-polarized neutron matter in magnetic fields using the LOCV method (Bordbar et al., 2013, 2014). The results are compared with observational data for several neutron stars, including PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie et al., 2020), PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013), and PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010), showing that the Gaussian magnetic field model yields masses and radii consistent with these observations. The work enables future studies of anisotropic magnetized neutron stars with more realistic nuclear interactions or including additional particle species.
