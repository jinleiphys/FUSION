# Constraining the high-density behavior of nuclear equation of state from strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of pions and strange particles in heavy-ion collisions in the region of 1A GeV energies is investigated by the lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics model for probing the nuclear equation of state at supra-saturation densities. The total multiplicities and the ratios obtained in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au over $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C systems are calculated for selected Skyrme parameters SkP, SLy6, Ska and SIII, which correspond to different modulus of incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter and different cases of the stiffness of symmetry energy. A decrease trend of the excitation functions of the ratios for strange particle production with increasing incident energy was observed. The available data of K$^{+}$ production measured by KaoS collaboration are described well with the parameter SkP, which results in a soft equation of state. The conclusions can not be modified by an in-medium kaon-nucleon potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the excitation functions of double ratios for strange particle production (K⁺, Λ) in heavy-ion collisions are sensitive to the stiffness of the nuclear equation of state, and that comparison with KaoS data favors a soft equation of state; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific Skyrme parameter SkP giving the best description, and the authors note that the conclusions cannot be modified by an in-medium kaon-nucleon potential.

## Method

The authors used the Lanzhou Quantum Molecular Dynamics (LQMD) model to simulate central collisions of ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au and ¹²C+¹²C at incident energies around 1A GeV. They calculated total multiplicities and double ratios (ratios of yields per mass number between the two systems) for pions, kaons, and Λ hyperons using four different Skyrme parameter sets (SkP, SLy6, Ska, SIII) that produce different incompressibility moduli and symmetry energy stiffnesses. The calculations were performed both with and without an in-medium kaon-nucleon potential to test the robustness of the conclusions.

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility modulus K∞ for the four Skyrme forces: SkP = 200 MeV, SLy6 = 230 MeV, Ska = 262 MeV, SIII = 353 MeV (Table 1).
- The saturation density ρ∞ for the four forces: SkP = 0.162 fm⁻³, SLy6 = 0.16 fm⁻³, Ska = 0.155 fm⁻³, SIII = 0.145 fm⁻³ (Table 1).
- The symmetry energy at saturation Esym: SkP = 30.9 MeV, SLy6 = 32.0 MeV, Ska = 33.0 MeV, SIII = 28.2 MeV (Table 1).
- The repulsive kaon-nucleon potential strength is 25.5 MeV and the attractive antikaon-nucleon potential strength is -96.8 MeV at saturation baryon density.
- The double ratios for K⁺ production at 0.8A GeV and 1.2A GeV are compared with KaoS data, with SkP (soft EoS) giving the best agreement (Figures 4-6).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies using transport models such as the QMD model by Fuchs (2001) and the Isospin-QMD (IQMD) model by Hartnack (2006), which also favored a soft equation of state from kaon production data. The paper builds on the LQMD model previously applied to fusion reactions and superheavy nuclei, now extended to strangeness production. The results enable further constraints on the high-density behavior of the nuclear equation of state, with implications for neutron star structure and supernova nucleosynthesis.
