# Parameter symmetries of quantum many-body systems

> We analyze the occurrence of dynamically equivalent Hamiltonians in the parameter space of general many-body interactions for quantum systems, particularly those that conserve the total number of particles. As an illustration of the general framework, the appearance of parameter symmetries in the interacting boson model-1 and their absence in the Ginocchio SO(8) fermionic model are discussed.

## Key claim

Parameter symmetries (isospectral mappings of the Hamiltonian parameter space) exist for general many-body Hamiltonians that conserve particle number, but their occurrence depends on the specific structure of the Hamiltonian and the constraints from conserved quantities and symmetry generators, not simply on the dimensional comparison of parameters versus independent eigenvalues.

## Method

The authors analyze the conditions under which a unitary transformation maps a Hamiltonian at one point in parameter space to an isospectral partner at another point, restricting to transformations that conserve particle number and commute with all parameter-independent constants of motion and symmetry generators. They apply this framework to two concrete nuclear models: the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1) with its seven-parameter Casimir-operator Hamiltonian, and the Ginocchio SO₈ fermionic model with its six-parameter Hamiltonian built from pair and multipole operators.

- IBM-1: The allowed phase values for the s-boson gauge transformation are φₛ = 0, π for k₆ ≠ 0 and φₛ = 0, ±π/2, π for k₆ = 0

- Ginocchio SO₈: The maximum number of nucleon pairs in a fully occupied shell is 2Ω = 4(2k+1)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of "parameter symmetries" introduced by Shirokov et al. (2000) for the IBM-1, who showed that each IBM Hamiltonian has an isospectral partner at a different point in parameter space. The authors demonstrate that the IBM-1 has exactly one parameter symmetry (the gauge transformation of boson operators), contradicting Shirokov et al.'s claim of an additional symmetry. The absence of parameter symmetries in the Ginocchio SO₈ model is explained by the fact that the SO₈ parameter space maps injectively (not surjectively) into the IBM-1 space, so at most one member of each IBM equivalence class has a fermionic counterpart. This result clarifies the relationship between the two models and constrains the possible fermion-boson mapping procedures.
