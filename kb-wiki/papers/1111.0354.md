# Nuclear mass predictions with radial basis function approach

> With the help of radial basis function (RBF) and the Garvey-Kelson relation, the accuracy and predictive power of some global nuclear mass models are significantly improved. The rms deviation between predictions from four models and 2149 known masses falls to about 200 keV. The AME95-03 and AME03-Border tests show that the RBF approach is a very useful tool for further improving the reliability of mass models. Simultaneously, the differences from different model predictions for unknown masses are remarkably reduced and the isospin symmetry is better represented when the RBF extrapolation is combined.

## Key claim

The radial basis function (RBF) approach, combined with the Garvey-Kelson relation, can reduce the rms deviation between predictions from four global nuclear mass models and 2149 known masses to about 200 keV, but this result depends on using the known masses themselves for training the RBF in a cross-validation test.

## Method

The authors treat nuclear mass prediction as a surface reconstruction problem: they use the RBF approach with a natural spline basis function (φ(r)=r) to reconstruct the difference R(N,Z)=M_exp−M_th between experimental masses and those calculated by five global mass models (WS3, DZ28, FRDM, HFB17, WS*). The reconstructed function S(N,Z) is then added to the model predictions for unmeasured nuclei. Three tests are performed: a leave-one-out cross-validation on 2149 nuclei from AME2003, an AME95-03 test using 1760 masses from AME1995 to predict 389 new masses in AME2003, and an AME03-Border test using 1700 nuclei near the β-stability line to predict 449 masses approaching the drip lines.

## Key numbers

- Rms deviation between predictions from four models (WS3, DZ28, FRDM, WS*) and 2149 known masses falls to about 200 keV when RBF and GK12 relation are combined (Table I: WS3+RBF+GK12 = 184 keV, DZ28+RBF+GK12 = 187 keV, FRDM+RBF+GK12 = 216 keV, WS*+RBF+GK12 = 217 keV)
- In the AME95-03 test, rms deviation for WS3 model reduces from 378 keV to 311 keV (18% reduction) when RBF is combined; for FRDM from 536 keV to 351 keV (35% reduction)
- In the AME03-Border test, rms deviation for FRDM reduces from 855 keV to 582 keV (32% reduction); for DZ28 from 491 keV to 392 keV (20% reduction)
- Rms deviation for b coefficients in the isobaric multiplet mass equation for 62 pairs of mirror nuclei reduces from 159 keV to 97 keV for WS3 model, and from 274 keV to 144 keV for FRDM, when RBF and GK are used

## Context

This work extends the image reconstruction technique (CLEAN algorithm) previously applied by Mora et al. (2009) to nuclear mass predictions, but demonstrates that the RBF approach is more efficient: in the AME95-03 test, the RBF approach achieves about 23% rms reduction for the DZ31 model compared to about 12% with CLEAN reconstruction. The method also improves upon the Garvey-Kelson relations alone, which suffer from error accumulation when used iteratively as noted by Mora et al. (2009). The approach enables more reliable mass predictions for nuclei far from stability, particularly near the neutron drip line, and reduces the divergence between different global mass models.
