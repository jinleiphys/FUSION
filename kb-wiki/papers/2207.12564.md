# QCD Equilibrium and Dynamical Properties from Holographic Black Holes

> By using gravity/gauge correspondence, we employ an Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model to compute the equilibrium and out-of-equilibrium properties of a hot and baryon rich strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma. The family of 5-dimensional holographic black holes, which are constrained to mimic the lattice QCD equation of state at zero density, is used to investigate the temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of the equation of state. We also obtained the baryon charge conductivity, and the bulk and shear viscosities with a particular focus on the behavior of these observables on top of the critical end point and the line of first order phase transition predicted by the model.

## Key claim

Using an Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic model constrained to match lattice QCD thermodynamics only at zero baryon chemical potential, the authors predict a critical end point at T = 89 MeV and μB = 724 MeV, and compute transport coefficients that remain finite at that point but exhibit divergent slopes, placing the model in the type B dynamical universality class rather than the type H class expected for QCD.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the equations of motion for a family of 5-dimensional asymptotically Anti-de Sitter black hole backgrounds in an Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model, where the dilaton potential V(φ) and gauge-dilaton coupling f(φ) are fixed by matching the holographic equation of state and second-order baryon susceptibility to lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential. Linear perturbations of the black hole background fields are then used to compute the baryon conductivity, bulk viscosity, and shear viscosity via gauge and diffeomorphism invariant combinations classified under the SO(3) symmetry group of spatial rotations.

## Key numbers

- Critical end point located at T^C = 89 MeV and μB^C = 724 MeV
- Equation of state computed over the rectangle T ∈ [2, 550] MeV and μB ∈ [0, 1100] MeV
- Holographic equation of state agrees with lattice QCD results up to μB/T = 3.5
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 1/(4π) at μB = 0
- Baryon conductivity σB/T remains finite at the critical end point
- Bulk viscosity ζT/(ε+P) remains finite at the critical end point
- Normalized shear viscosity ηT/(ε+P) reduces to η/s = 1/(4π) at μB = 0

## Context

This work extends the holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton approach previously developed by Critelli et al. (2017) and Grefa et al. (2021) to compute transport coefficients over the crossover region, critical point, and first-order phase transition line. The model's prediction of finite bulk viscosity at the critical point contrasts with the divergent bulk viscosity predicted by effective models such as Monnai et al. (2016) and Buchel (2009), and the type B dynamical universality class differs from the type H class expected for QCD by Son (2004). The results provide transport coefficients needed as inputs for hydrodynamical simulations of relativistic heavy ion collisions at finite baryon density, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan program.
