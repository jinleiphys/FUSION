# Relativistic corrections in (e,e'p) knockout reactions

> A consistent comparison between nonrelativistic and relativistic descriptions of the (e,e'p) reaction is presented. We use the nonrelativistic DWEEPY code and develop a fully relativistic model starting from the effective Pauli reduction for the scattering state and the relativistic mean field theory for the bound state. Results for the 16O(e,e'p) differential cross section and structure functions are compared in various kinematical conditions. A limit in energy of the validity of the nonrelativistic approach is established. The effects of spinor distortion and of the effective momentum approximation for the scattering state are discussed. A satisfactory agreement with data of differential cross sections, structure functions, and polarization observables is achieved.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic DWEEPY code can be used with confidence at proton kinetic energies around 100 MeV and, with caution, up to about 200 MeV, but a fully relativistic calculation is necessary above 300 MeV; the abstract's claim of establishing "a limit in energy" is specified here as a gradual transition rather than a sharp threshold.

## Method

The authors performed a consistent comparison between the nonrelativistic DWEEPY code and a newly developed fully relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) model for the 16O(e,e'p) reaction. The relativistic model used the effective Pauli reduction for the scattering state (solving a Schrödinger-like equation with Dirac scalar and vector optical potentials) and Dirac-Hartree bound-state wave functions from relativistic mean-field theory, while DWEEPY used the upper component of the Dirac spinor for the bound state and the same Schrödinger-equivalent optical potential for the scattering state. Calculations were performed in constant-(q,ω) kinematics at outgoing proton energies of 100, 200, and 300 MeV, and in parallel kinematics at 100 and 200 MeV, comparing differential cross sections and the four nuclear structure functions (R_L, R_T, R_LT, R_TT).

## Key numbers

- At T_p = 100 MeV, the transverse structure function R_T in RDWIA is reduced by about 15% compared to the nonrelativistic calculation; the reduction grows to about 25% at 200 MeV and 40% at 300 MeV.
- The Darwin factor gives a reduction of about 5–10% at 100 MeV, consistent with the qualitative prediction of Cannata.
- The normalization factor (E+M)/2E has the value 0.95 at 100 MeV and decreases to 0.87 at 300 MeV.
- Spectroscopic factors from RDWIA are approximately 10–20% larger than those from nonrelativistic DWIA analyses, consistent with prior RDWIA work.
- The effective momentum approximation (EMA) produces a sensible difference at 100 MeV but becomes negligible at about 400 MeV, the energy of the TJNAF experiment.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between nonrelativistic DWIA (as implemented in the DWEEPY code, used extensively at Saclay and NIKHEF) and fully relativistic RDWIA models developed by groups such as Pick, Jin, Udias, and others. It directly addresses the problem that previous comparisons were indirect, using different optical potentials and bound-state wave functions. The results enable a clear understanding of when relativistic corrections become essential, particularly for the analysis of new higher-energy data from TJNAF at Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)² and ω ≈ 439 MeV, and for polarization transfer measurements. The upper limit of |q| ≈ 600 MeV/c for nonrelativistic calculations, stated earlier by Giusti and Pacati (1993), is confirmed and refined.
