# Role of the symmetry energy and the neutron-matter stiffness on the tidal deformability of a neutron star with unified equations of state

> The role of the symmetry energy and the neutron-matter stiffness on the tidal deformability of a cold nonaccreted neutron star is studied using a set of unified equations of state. Based on the nuclear energy-density functional theory, these equations of state provide a thermodynamically consistent treatment of all regions of the star and were calculated using functionals that were precision fitted to experimental and theoretical nuclear data. Predictions are compared to constraints inferred from the recent detection of the gravitational-wave signal GW170817 from a binary neutron-star merger and from observations of the electromagnetic counterparts.

## Key claim

The tidal deformability of a neutron star depends on the symmetry energy only indirectly through the stellar radius, while the tidal Love number \(k_2\) is essentially insensitive to the symmetry energy; the dominant nuclear-physics uncertainty affecting both \(k_2\) and the radius is the stiffness of the neutron-matter equation of state at supersaturation densities, not the symmetry energy itself.

## Method

The authors computed the structure and tidal deformability of cold nonaccreted neutron stars using seven unified equations of state (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21, BSk22, BSk24, BSk25, BSk26) based on Brussels-Montreal nuclear energy-density functionals fitted to experimental atomic masses and to different neutron-matter equations of state from many-body theory. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the differential equation for the tidal perturbation were integrated with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method using analytic fits to the unified EoSs that consistently describe the outer crust, inner crust, and core.

## Key numbers

* The tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) of a \(1.4\,M_\odot\) neutron star is correlated with the radius \(R_{1.4}\) by relations of the form \(\Lambda_{1.4}\propto R_{1.4}^{\,\alpha}\) with \(\alpha\) ranging from 5 to 7.71 in the literature; the unified Brussels-Montreal EoSs reproduce most of these relations to within a few percent, except those from Zhou (2019) and Tews (2019) which deviate by 10–20%.
* The LIGO-Virgo 90% confidence constraint on the tidal deformability using method (ii) is \(\Lambda = 190^{+390}_{-120}\) for a \(1.4\,M_\odot\) star.
* The upper limit \(\Lambda_{1.4}<580\) from the LIGO-Virgo collaboration using method (ii) translates to a radius constraint \(R_{1.4} \lesssim 12.6\)–\(13.1\) km.
* Using method (ii), the LIGO-Virgo collaboration estimated the radii of the two neutron stars as \(R_1=10.8^{+2.0}_{-1.7}\) km and \(R_2=10.7^{+2.1}_{-1.5}\) km at 90% confidence.
* Using method (iii) with the requirement \(M_\text{max} \geq 1.97\,M_\odot\), the radii were estimated as \(R_1=11.9^{+1.4}_{-1.4}\) km and \(R_2=11.9^{+1.4}_{-1.4}\) km at 90% confidence.
* The symmetry-energy coefficient \(J\) ranges from 29 to 32 MeV across the functionals; the slope parameter \(L\) ranges from 31.9 MeV (BSk19) to 68.5 MeV (BSk22).
* The incompressibility coefficient \(K_v\) for all functionals lies in the range 236.0–245.9 MeV, consistent with the experimental range \(K_v=240\pm10\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tidal deformability correlations (Malik 2018, Fattoyev 2018, Zhang and Li 2019) by using unified equations of state that treat the crust and core consistently, unlike earlier work that employed separate crust models such as those of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) and Negele and Vautherin (1973) or polytropic approximations. The results show that the symmetry energy has negligible direct effect on the Love number \(k_2\), consistent with the findings of Malik (2018) and Raithel (2019) for different sets of EoSs, and that the dominant uncertainty comes from the neutron-matter stiffness at high densities. The predictions are compared to constraints from GW170817, and the analysis enables more reliable extraction of neutron-star radii from future gravitational-wave detections by clarifying which nuclear-matter properties must be better constrained.
