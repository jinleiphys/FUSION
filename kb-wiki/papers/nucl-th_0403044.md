# Rapidity dependent momentum anisotropy at RHIC

> In Au+Au collisions at RHIC, elliptic flow was found to rapidly decrease as a function of rapidity. We argue that the origin of this phenomenon is incomplete thermalization of the initial fireball outside the midrapidity region and show that it can be quantitatively related to the analogous effect at midrapidity in peripheral collisions and in collisions at lower beam energies.

# Rapidity dependent momentum anisotropy at RHIC

## Key claim

The rapid decrease of elliptic flow ($v_2$) with increasing rapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is caused by incomplete thermalization of the initial fireball outside the midrapidity region, and this effect can be quantitatively related to the analogous suppression of elliptic flow seen at midrapidity in peripheral collisions and in collisions at lower beam energies. The authors argue that the degree of thermalization is controlled by the local initial energy density, with a linear scaling between the measured-to-hydrodynamic $v_2$ ratio and the transversally averaged initial energy density.

## Method

The authors initialize a (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model with a longitudinal structure that correlates transverse position with space-time rapidity using a double-Gaussian parametrization of the charged particle rapidity distribution, fitted to PHOBOS data. They evolve the system with a locally boost-invariant hydrodynamic code using an equation of state from Kolb and Rapp (2003), starting at proper time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/$c$ and stopping at decoupling energy density $e_{\rm dec} = 0.075$ GeV/fm$^3$, then correct the hydrodynamic $v_2$ values by multiplying with a "thermalization coefficient" $F(x) = 0.15 + 0.85x$ (for $x \leq 1$) that depends on the ratio of the local initial energy density to a reference value $e_0 = 9.5$ GeV/fm$^3$.

## Key numbers

- The double-Gaussian width parameter $a = 1.9 \pm 0.05$ for 200 $A$ GeV Au+Au data, $a = 1.8 \pm 0.05$ for 130 $A$ GeV, and $a = 1.15 \pm 0.05$ for 19.6 $A$ GeV
- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/$c$ and stops at decoupling energy density $e_{\rm dec} = 0.075$ GeV/fm$^3$, both independent of rapidity
- The reference energy density $e_0 = 9.5$ GeV/fm$^3$ is the average initial energy density in central Au+Au collisions at 130 $A$ GeV
- The thermalization coefficient is $F(x) = 0.15 + 0.85x$ for $x \leq 1$, with $x = 1$ corresponding to $(1/S) dN_{\rm ch}/dy = 25$ fm$^{-2}$ from the NA49 data
- The average impact parameter used for minimum bias Au+Au is $b = 6.8$ fm
- The average transverse momentum for charged pions is taken as $\langle p_\perp \rangle \approx 0.4$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the scaling observation by STAR (2003) and NA49 (2002) that the discrepancy between hydrodynamic and measured elliptic flow at midrapidity scales with $(1/S) dN_{\rm ch}/dy$, and applies it to explain the rapidity dependence of $v_2$ first observed by PHOBOS (2002) and confirmed by STAR (2003). The authors support the earlier conclusion by Hirano (2001) that local thermal equilibrium is only achieved at midrapidity, and they show that the same incomplete thermalization effects seen in peripheral and lower-energy collisions also describe the rapidity dependence at RHIC. The work enables predictions for higher collision energies (LHC) and suggests testing the scenario using deformed Uranium-Uranium collisions at RHIC.
