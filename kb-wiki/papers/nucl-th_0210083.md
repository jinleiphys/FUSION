# Infinite Component Relativistic Wave Equations

> We construct an infinite component relativistic wave equation which is a linear first order differential equation identical in form to a Dirac like equation, describing composite fields possessing multiple spin and energy states. The main motivation for such a construction is to give a description of Hadronic fields moving along their so called Regge Trajectories, however this may be generalized to other composite fields. In order to construct the equation so that it may accommodate physical states the concept of Spin Frames is introduced, and it is found that such an equation may propagate physical fields whose spin states differ by two units of angular momentum namely $ΔJ=2$. The solution for the free field case is given by boosting a rest frame spinor with the infinite dimensional Lorentz Boost which are constructed as well. Finally we discuss the relevance of the groups GL(4R), and GL(3,1,R) and their appearance with regards to the wave equation at hand.

# Infinite Component Relativistic Wave Equations

## Key claim

The paper constructs an infinite component relativistic wave equation, identical in form to a Dirac-like first-order linear equation, that can describe composite fields with multiple spin and energy states; however, the equation only propagates physical fields whose spin states differ by exactly two units of angular momentum (ΔJ=2), and only one of the two possible spin trajectories (odd or even) satisfies the Klein-Gordon equation while the other does not, depending on the choice of metric convention.

## Method

The author introduces the concept of "spin frames" as an infinite-dimensional index labeling different spin states of a composite field, analogous to tetrads in general relativity. Representations of the four-vector matrices X^μ are constructed using the Wigner-Eckart theorem in a spherical basis, with coefficients determined by enforcing the mass shell condition (p² = m²) for each spin state. The matrices are split into odd and even projection operators, yielding two sets of operators (L^μ and N^μ) that mix states differing by one unit of angular momentum, and the full X^μ matrices are assembled in a chiral-like 2×2 block form using a metric ε_ab that alternates sign between odd and even spin states.

## Key numbers

- The wave equation propagates fields whose spin states differ by ΔJ = 2 along a Regge trajectory.
- The coefficients for the X^i representations are given by l^-(α,j) = l^+(α,j-1) = η/√(2j+1) and n^+(α,j) = n^-(α,j+1) = η/√(2j+1), where η is a complex number with modulus unity (η = i makes X^i anti-Hermitian).
- The time component condition requires n^0(α,j) = l^0(α,j) = 1.
- The commutator (i/2)[X^1,X^2]_{11} = m gives the representation of the J_3 angular momentum generator.
- For odd spin states (j_o), the field satisfies the Klein-Gordon equation (∂²_α + M²_α)ψ_{j_o} = 0; for even spin states (j_e), it satisfies ((∂⁰)²_α + ∇²_α + M²_α)ψ_{j_e} = 0, which is not the standard Klein-Gordon equation.

## Context

This work extends the infinite component wave equation approach originally pioneered by Majorana (1932) and Dirac (1971), who constructed equations describing particles with multiple spin states. The paper specifically addresses the problem that Majorana's equation gave a mass spectrum m = M/(j+1) which does not match observed hadron spectra, and that Majorana's X^0 being positive definite excluded negative frequencies, making quantization problematic. The construction is motivated by the need to describe hadronic fields moving along Regge trajectories, where excited states differ by ΔJ = 2, and builds on earlier work by Ne'eman (1990s) on quadrupole moments and structural deformations of hadrons. The paper also discusses the relevance of the groups GL(4R) and GL(3,1,R) to the wave equation formalism.
