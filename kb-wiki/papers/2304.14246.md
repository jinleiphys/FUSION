# Hexadecapole strength in the rare isotopes $^{74,76}$Kr

> In the Ge-Sr mass region, isotopes with neutron number $N \leq 40$ are known to feature rapid shape changes with both nucleon number and angular momentum. To gain new insights into their structure, inelastic proton scattering experiments in inverse kinematics were performed on the rare isotopes $^{74,76}$Kr. This work focuses on observables related to the $J^π = 4^+_1$ states of the Kr isotopes and, in particular, on the hexadecapole degree of freedom. By performing coupled-channels calculations, hexadecapole deformation parameters $β_4$ were determined for the $J^π = 4^+_1$ states of $^{74,76}$Kr from inelastic proton scattering cross sections. Two possible coupled-channels solutions were found. A comparison to predictions from nuclear energy density functional theory, employing both non-relativistic and relativistic functionals, clearly favors the large, positive $β_4$ solutions. These $β_4$ values are unambiguously linked to the well deformed prolate configuration. Given the $β_2 - β_4$ trend, established in this work, it appears that $β_4$ values could provide a sensitive measure of the nuclear shell structure.

## Key claim
The paper reports that hexadecapole deformation parameters β₄ for the 4⁺₁ states of ⁷⁴,⁷⁶Kr, determined from inelastic proton scattering, have two possible coupled-channels solutions differing in sign; comparison with nuclear density functional theory clearly favors the large, positive β₄ solutions, which are unambiguously linked to the well deformed prolate configuration.

## Method
Inelastic proton scattering experiments in inverse kinematics were performed on secondary beams of ⁷⁴,⁷⁶Kr at a center-of-mass energy of about 100 MeV at the NSCL Coupled Cyclotron Facility. The NSCL/Ursinus Liquid Hydrogen Target, GRETINA γ-ray tracking array, and S800 magnetic spectrograph were used to measure γ-ray yields and inelastic scattering cross sections. Coupled-channels calculations with the program CHUCK3 were performed to extract hexadecapole deformation parameters β₄ from the measured cross sections to the 4⁺₁ states, accounting for one-step and two-step excitation processes.

## Key numbers
- Inelastic scattering cross sections to the 4⁺₁ state: 5.1(5) mb for ⁷⁶Kr and 6.1(11) mb for ⁷⁴Kr.
- Positive β₄ solutions: β₄ = 0.201 ± 0.009 (stat.) ± 0.016 (sys.) for ⁷⁶Kr; β₄ = 0.23 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.02 (sys.) for ⁷⁴Kr.
- Negative β₄ solutions: β₄ = −0.127 ± 0.009 (stat.) ± 0.022 (sys.) for ⁷⁶Kr; β₄ = −0.17 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.02 (sys.) for ⁷⁴Kr.
- B(E4; 4⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) strengths from positive β₄: 22.7 ± 1.0 (stat.) ± 1.8 (sys.) W.u. for ⁷⁶Kr; 29 ± 2 (stat.) ± 2 (sys.) W.u. for ⁷⁴Kr.
- Quadrupole deformation parameters β₂: 0.40 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.03 (sys.) for ⁷⁶Kr; 0.35 ± 0.06 (stat.) ± 0.02 (sys.) for ⁷⁴Kr.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence and rapid shape changes in the Ge-Sr mass region, where isotopes with neutron number N ≤ 40 exhibit prolate-oblate ground-state shape transitions around N = 36 (Lec80b, Gad05a, Lju08a, Iwa14a, Hen18a, Wim20a). The results support the two-state mixing analysis of Clement et al. (2007), which attributed large quadrupole deformations to prolate configurations in ⁷⁴,⁷⁶Kr, and demonstrate that β₄ values can serve as a sensitive probe of prolate shapes and nuclear shell structure. The findings enable future experiments at rare isotope beam facilities to investigate hexadecapole moments in even more neutron-deficient isotopes such as ⁷⁰,⁷²Kr.
