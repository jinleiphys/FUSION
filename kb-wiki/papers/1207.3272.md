# Collective properties of nucleus-nucleus collisions from AGS to LHC energies

> The azimuthal anisotropies of the collective transverse flow of charged hadrons are investigated in a wide range of heavy-ion collision energies within the microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach which incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom in terms of strongly interacting quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) in line with an equation-of-state from lattice QCD as well as the dynamical hadronization and hadronic collision dynamics in the final reaction phase. The experimentally observed increase of the elliptic flow $v_2$ of charged hadrons with collision energy is successfully described in terms of the PHSD approach. The analysis of higher-order harmonics $v_3$ and $v_4$ in the azimuthal angular distribution shows a similar tendency of growing deviations between partonic and purely hadronic models with increasing collision energy. This demonstrates that the excitation functions of azimuthal anisotropies reflect the increasing role of quark-gluon degrees of freedom in the early phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Furthermore, the specific variation of the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ with respect to bombarding energy, centrality and transverse momentum is found to provide valuable information on the underlying partonic dynamics.

# Collective properties of nucleus-nucleus collisions from AGS to LHC energies

## Key claim

The increase of elliptic flow $v_2$ with collision energy is successfully described by the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) approach, which incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom and a lattice QCD equation-of-state, but only when the partonic phase is treated consistently with dynamical hadronization and repulsive mean-fields; purely hadronic models (UrQMD, HSD) and simplified partonic models (AMPT) fail to reproduce this energy dependence.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic transport calculations using the PHSD model for Au+Au collisions across a beam energy range from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ GeV to 200 GeV, computing the Fourier coefficients $v_2$, $v_3$, and $v_4$ of charged hadron azimuthal distributions at midrapidity. They compared these results to experimental data from the RHIC Beam-Energy-Scan program and to calculations from purely hadronic models (HSD, UrQMD) and the AMPT model with string melting, using the same centrality selections and analysis methods (two-sub-events method for event plane determination).

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow $v_2$ increases from approximately 0.02 at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2$ GeV to approximately 0.06 at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV in PHSD, matching the experimental trend from the STAR collaboration; hadronic models (UrQMD, HSD) give approximately constant $v_2 \approx 0.02$ across this energy range.
- The parton fraction of the total energy density at midrapidity reaches approximately 0.8 at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV for impact parameter $b = 1$ fm, decreasing to approximately 0.2 at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2$ GeV.
- The ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ from PHSD is approximately constant at about 0.8 across $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ to 200 GeV, significantly deviating from the ideal fluid estimate of 0.5; the HSD model gives a monotonically increasing ratio from about 0.5 to 1.2 over the same range.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, PHSD results for $v_4/(v_2)^2$ versus transverse momentum $p_T$ (20-30% centrality) overestimate the STAR data by up to 20%, while hydrodynamic models from Ko (2003) and Borghini and Ollitrault (2006) significantly underestimate the data.

## Context

This work extends the PHSD model studies previously reported by Konchakovski et al. (2012) and Bratkovskaya et al. (2011) for longitudinal rapidity distributions, transverse mass spectra, and elliptic flow at SPS and RHIC energies, now covering the full energy range of the RHIC Beam-Energy-Scan program from AGS to LHC energies. The results contradict the earlier failure of traditional hadronic models (UrQMD, HSD) and the AMPT model to explain the observed increase of $v_2$ with collision energy, as noted by Nasim et al. (2010). The analysis enables the use of higher-order harmonics $v_3$ and $v_4$ and the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ as sensitive probes of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ in the partonic phase, and provides a baseline for interpreting LHC flow measurements.
