# Deconfinement and chiral phase transitions in quark matter with chiral imbalance

> We study the thermodynamics of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model considering the effects of an effective chiral chemical potential. We offer a new parametrization of the Polyakov-loop potential depending on temperature and the chiral chemical potential which, when used together with a proper regularization scheme of vacuum contributions, predicts results consistent with those from lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can reproduce lattice QCD results for both chiral and deconfinement pseudocritical temperatures as increasing functions of the chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\), but only when two conditions are met simultaneously: the medium separation scheme (MSS) regularization is used for vacuum contributions, and a new parametrization of the Polyakov-loop potential that depends explicitly on both temperature and \(\mu_5\) is introduced.

## Method

The authors study the thermodynamics of the PNJL model with an effective chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\) at zero baryon chemical potential. They compare two regularization schemes for the divergent momentum integrals: the traditional three-dimensional sharp cutoff (TRS) and the medium separation scheme (MSS) that isolates finite medium contributions from divergent vacuum integrals. To match lattice results, they propose a new polynomial parametrization of the Polyakov-loop potential where the coefficient \(\bar{b}_2\) gains additional terms linear, quadratic, and cubic in \(\mu_5/T\), with coefficients \(k_1 = 0.08602\), \(k_2 = -1.39646\), and \(k_3 = 0.3381\) fitted to lattice data from Braguta et al. (2015).

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperatures at \(\mu_5 = 0\) for the chiral transition: \(T_{\rm pc}^c = 0.229\) GeV (TRS) and \(0.233\) GeV (MSS); for the deconfinement transition: \(T_{\rm pc}^d = 0.225\) GeV (TRS) and \(0.227\) GeV (MSS).
* The chiral density \(\langle n_5\rangle\) in the MSS is exactly \(\langle n_5\rangle = 4 f_\pi^2 \mu_5\) at zero temperature, matching the chiral perturbation theory prediction for two flavors.
* The TRS produces a first-order phase transition for \(\mu_5 \gtrsim 0.44\) GeV, while the MSS shows no critical end point.
* The NJL model parameters used are \(\Lambda = 651\) MeV, \(G = 5.04\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(m_c = 5.5\) MeV, reproducing \(f_\pi = 92.3\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 139.3\) MeV, and the quark condensate \(-\langle \bar q q \rangle^{1/3} = 251\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the medium separation scheme (MSS) regularization, first applied to the chiral imbalance problem by Farias et al. (2016), from the NJL model to the Polyakov-extended NJL (PNJL) model. It addresses the longstanding discrepancy where effective models predicted decreasing critical temperatures with \(\mu_5\), opposite to lattice QCD results from Braguta et al. (2015). The new \(\mu_5\)-dependent Polyakov-loop parametrization, analogous to prior modifications for baryon chemical potential (Schaefer, 2007), isospin chemical potential (Adhikari, 2018), and magnetic field (Ferreira, 2013), enables simultaneous agreement with lattice data for both chiral and deconfinement transitions, which the authors state has not been achieved before.
