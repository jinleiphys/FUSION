# Cluster sum rules for three-body systems with angular-momentum dependent interactions

> We derive general expressions for non-energy weighted and energy-weighted cluster sum rules for systems of three charged particles. The interferences between pairs of particles are found to play a substantial role. The energy-weighted sum rule is usually determined by the kinetic energy operator, but we demonstrate that it has similar additional contributions from the angular momentum and parity dependence of two- and three-body potentials frequently used in three-body calculations. The importance of the different contributions is illustrated with the dipole excitations in $^6$He. The results are compared with the available experimental data.

# Cluster sum rules for three-body systems with angular-momentum dependent interactions

## Key claim

The authors derive general expressions for non-energy weighted and energy-weighted cluster sum rules for three charged particles, demonstrating that the energy-weighted sum rule receives additional contributions from the angular momentum and parity dependence of two- and three-body potentials, beyond the usual kinetic energy term. The interferences between pairs of particles play a substantial role, and for three identical particles with equal charge-to-mass ratio the dipole sum rules vanish entirely.

## Method

The authors start from the definition of electric multipole transition strengths and use the double-commutator formalism to derive sum rules for three-body systems described in hyperspherical coordinates with Faddeev decomposition. They separate the Hamiltonian into kinetic energy, partial-wave dependent two-body potentials (which depend on relative orbital angular momentum between pairs), and J-pi dependent three-body potentials, then evaluate each contribution to the energy-weighted sum rule analytically. The formalism is applied to dipole excitations in He-6 using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method with box-discretized continuum states.

## Key numbers

- For a system of three equal-mass particles with one charged particle (charge z) and lambda=1: S_0 = 3 z^2 <|r_p - R|^2>/(4 pi) and S_1^(T) = 3 hbar^2 z^2/(4 pi m)
- For three identical charged particles with lambda=1: both S_0 and S_1^(T) are zero
- For the illustrative three-body model with mass m=4 times nucleon mass, one charge equal to twice proton charge, and angular-momentum independent potentials: S_1 = 9.90 e^2 MeV fm^2
- The He-6 ground state two-neutron separation energy matches the experimental value of -0.97 +/- 0.04 MeV, achieved with a gaussian three-body force of range 2.9 fm and strength -7.55 MeV
- The box boundary condition for continuum states uses rho_max = 50 fm
- The dipole strength function is computed using energy bins with centers at 0.3, 0.9, 1.5, 2.3, 3.2, 4.2, 5.4, 6.7, and 8.2 MeV

## Context

This work generalizes the dipole two-body cluster sum rules from Sagawa (1990) and Suzuki (2003) to three-body systems for any multipolarity, incorporating the complications of partial-wave dependent pair interactions and state-dependent three-body potentials that are standard in modern three-body calculations (Garrido 2002, Garrido 2004). The results enable quantitative separation of contributions from kinetic energy, angular-momentum dependent two-body forces, and J-pi dependent three-body forces to sum rules for halo nuclei and other weakly bound three-body systems, with direct application to electromagnetic excitation studies of He-6 and similar borromean nuclei.
