# Connecting the Pygmy Dipole Resonance to the neutron skin

> We study the correlation between the neutron skin development and the low-energy dipole response associated with the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in connection with the properties of symmetry energy. We perform our investigation within a microscopic transport model based on the Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation by employing three different equations of state in the isovector sector. Together with the giant dipole resonance (GDR) for all studied systems, we identify a PDR collective mode whose energy centroid is very well described by the parametrization E_{PDR}=41 A^{-1/3}. A linear correlation between the energy weighted sum rule (EWSR) associated to PDR and the neutron skin thickness is evidenced. An increase of 15 MeVfm^2 of EWSR, in correspondence to a change of 0.1fm of the neutron skin size, is obtained. We conjecture that different nuclei having close neutron skin sizes will exhaust the same EWSR in the pygmy region. This suggests that a precise experimental estimate of the total EWSR exhausted by the PDR allows the determination of the neutron skin size, constraining the slope parameter of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims a universal, linear correlation between the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) exhausted by the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) and the neutron skin thickness, with the caveat that this correlation holds above a threshold of approximately 0.15 fm for the neutron skin; below this value the EWSR shows a saturation tendency.

## Method

The authors employed a semiclassical microscopic transport model based on the Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation, using three different equations of state in the isovector sector (asysoft, asystiff, and asysuperstiff) that differ in the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy. They simulated the dipole response of nine nuclei (⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁸Ni, ⁸⁶Kr, ¹⁰⁸Sn, ¹¹⁶Sn, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹³²Sn, ¹⁴⁰Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb) by applying a boost perturbation and extracting the strength function from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent dipole moment. The neutron skin thickness was determined from the equilibrium neutron and proton root-mean-square radii obtained after a gentle monopolar perturbation.

## Key numbers

- The PDR energy centroid follows the parametrization E_PDR = 41 A^(-1/3) MeV, with the experimental value for ²⁰⁸Pb at 7.36 MeV and for ⁶⁸Ni at 9.9 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the neutron skin thickness ranges from 0.19 fm (asysoft) to 0.27 fm (asysuperstiff); for ⁶⁸Ni, from 0.17 fm to 0.20 fm.
- The dipole polarizability for ⁶⁸Ni varies from 4.1 fm³ (asysoft) to 5.7 fm³ (asysuperstiff); for ²⁰⁸Pb, from 21.1 fm³ to 28.6 fm³.
- The fraction of EWSR exhausted by the PDR (f_y) ranges from 1% to 2% for ²⁰⁸Pb, 1.8% to 3.5% for ⁶⁸Ni, and 2.2% to 4.2% for ¹³²Sn, depending on the EOS.
- An increase of 15 MeV fm² in the EWSR exhausted by the PDR corresponds to a change of 0.1 fm in the neutron skin thickness, with a slope of s = 150 MeV fm.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of the correlation between the PDR and neutron skin, notably the relativistic RPA study by Piekarewicz (2006) which found a linear dependence for Sn isotopes up to A=120, and the covariance analysis by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010) which questioned such a correlation. The results are consistent with the systematic RPA calculations by Inakura et al. (2011) using a SkM* Skyrme functional, though the present model finds a continuous rise of EWSR with neutron skin rather than the mild anti-correlation reported for very neutron-rich systems by Piekarewicz (2006) and by Liang et al. (2007). The findings suggest that precise experimental determination of the EWSR exhausted by the PDR could constrain the neutron skin size and the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy.
