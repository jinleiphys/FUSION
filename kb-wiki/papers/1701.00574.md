# Effects of mean-field and the softening of equation of state on elliptic flow in Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5 GeV from JAM model

> We perform a systematic study of elliptic flow ($v_2$) in Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5$ GeV by using a microscopic transport model JAM. The centrality, pseudorapidity, transverse momentum and beam energy dependence of $v_2$ for charged as well as identified hadrons are studied. We investigate the effects of both the hadronic mean-field and the softening of equation of state (EoS) on elliptic flow. The softening of EoS is realized by imposing attractive orbits in two body scattering, which can reduce the pressure of the system. We found that the softening of EoS leads to the enhancement of $v_2$, while the hadronic mean-field suppresses $v_2$ relative to the cascade mode. It indicates that elliptic flow at high baryon density regions is highly sensitive to the EoS and the enhancement of $v_2$ may probe the signature of a first-order phase transition in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies of a strong baryon stopping region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a softening of the equation of state (EoS) enhances elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) GeV, while the hadronic mean-field suppresses it relative to the cascade mode; this enhancement of \(v_2\) may serve as a signature of a first-order phase transition, but only at beam energies below about 7 GeV, and the scenario of a phase transition appears ruled out at energies below 5.0 GeV based on existing data.

## Method

The authors used the JAM (Jet AA Microscopic Transport Model) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) GeV in three modes: standard cascade, mean-field (with Skyrme-type density-dependent and Lorentzian-type momentum-dependent potentials for baryons), and attractive orbit (which softens the EoS by imposing attractive orbits in all two-body scatterings, reducing the system pressure). They computed elliptic flow \(v_2\) using the true reaction plane from the model and compared results for charged hadrons and identified particles (protons, pions, kaons and their antiparticles) as functions of centrality, pseudorapidity, and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement of charged hadron \(v_2\) in the attractive orbit mode is largest in semi-central collisions (20-30% centrality) at mid-rapidity (\(|\eta| < 0.2\)).
- The mean-field mode suppresses \(v_2\) for all particles at mid-central collisions relative to the cascade mode; the mean-field result agrees with experimental data from the top AGS energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.7\) GeV.
- The attractive orbit mode enhances \(v_2\) for pions and kaons, but proton \(v_2\) is similar to the cascade mode at mid-pseudorapidity.
- The enhancement of \(v_2\) from the attractive orbit mode is seen only for beam energies up to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.0\) GeV; above 7.7 GeV no enhancement relative to cascade is observed.
- The mean-field mode suppresses pion yield by 5% and the attractive orbit mode enhances proton yield by 8% and pion yield by 2% at mid-rapidity.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at high baryon density, particularly using transport models such as RQMD, UrQMD, and AMPT. The authors compare their results to experimental data from the E895 collaboration at the AGS and to STAR Beam Energy Scan (BES) results. The study enables future experimental searches for a first-order phase transition signal in the beam energy range between 5.0 and 7.7 GeV, where no data currently exist, and suggests that measurements at FAIR (CBM at SIS100), J-PARC, and NICA could test the predicted enhancement of \(v_2\).
