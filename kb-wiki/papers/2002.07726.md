# Manifestation of kaonium in the $e^+e^- \to K^+K^-$ process

> We analyze the precise data obtained by the CMD-3 experiment on the $e^+e^-$ annihilation into two charged kaons in the vicinity of the $ϕ$ peak. A perfect fit is obtained only if a pole on the real axis below the reaction threshold is assumed. This can be interpreted as proof of the existence of the 2p state of kaonium, a compound of $K^+$ and $K^-$. The BaBar Collaboration data on the same process supports this conclusion and, in addition, points to the strong interaction as a dominant source of the binding energy. The possibility of discovering 2p kaonium in the $e^+e^-\rightarrow π^+π^-$ process is discussed. The 2p state of $K^0$-onium is indicated on the basis of the CMD-3 $e^+e^-\rightarrow K^0_SK^0_L$ data.

# Manifestation of kaonium in the $e^+e^- \to K^+K^-$ process

## Key claim

Analysis of CMD-3 and BaBar data on $e^+e^- \to K^+K^-$ near the $\phi(1020)$ resonance is consistent with a pole on the real axis below the $K^+K^-$ threshold, interpreted as the 2p state of kaonium ($K^+K^-$ bound state); however, the binding energy is not conclusively determined, with the Coulombic value of 1.64 keV and a strong-interaction-dominated value of 10 MeV both yielding acceptable fits depending on the dataset.

## Method

The cross section for $e^+e^- \to K^+K^-$ was modeled using a vector-meson-dominance formula with two resonances, where the second resonance was replaced by a bound-state pole with zero width and mass $M_2 = 2m_{K^+} - b$, with $b$ the binding energy. Fits were performed to 24 CMD-3 data points near the $\phi$ peak, and separately to 48 BaBar data points up to 1.065 GeV, using MINUIT minimization of $\chi^2$. The same approach was applied to 25 CMD-3 data points on $e^+e^- \to K^0_S K^0_L$ to test for neutral kaonium.

## Key numbers

- CMD-3 $K^+K^-$ one-resonance fit: $\chi^2 = 341.8$ with NDF = 21, confidence level 0%; resonance-plus-kaonium fit with $b = 1.64$ keV: $\chi^2 = 4.6$ with NDF = 19, confidence level 100%
- CMD-3 $K^+K^-$ fit with binding energy as free parameter: $b = (10.8^{+14.8}_{-9.1})$ MeV
- BaBar $K^+K^-$ data (48 points, $\sqrt{s} < 1.065$ GeV): one-resonance fit $\chi^2 = 244.4$ with NDF = 45 (0% C.L.); kaonium with $b = 1.64$ keV: $\chi^2 = 184.5$ with NDF = 43 (0% C.L.); kaonium with $b = 10$ MeV: $\chi^2 = 48.1$ with NDF = 43 (27.4% C.L.)
- CMD-3 $K^0_S K^0_L$ data (25 points): one-resonance fit $\chi^2 = 36.7$ with NDF = 22 (2.5% C.L.); resonance-plus-kaonium with $b = 10$ MeV: $\chi^2 = 7.5$ with NDF = 20 (99.5% C.L.)
- Coulombic binding energy of 2p kaonium: 1.64 keV (from hydrogen-atom formula with reduced mass $m_{K^+}/2$)
- Ground-state kaonium Coulombic binding energy: 6.57 keV

## Context

This work extends the search for meson-antimeson bound states (onia) beyond pionium, which was discovered at Serpukhov (1993) and studied by DIRAC at CERN. The DIRAC Collaboration recently observed long-lived 2p pionium with $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ (Lifetime 2019), which can be produced in $e^+e^-$ collisions unlike the 0++ ground state. Prior theoretical work on kaonium by Krewald (2004) found the ground-state energy shifted above the Coulomb value, while Zhang (2006) found stronger binding (7.05 keV). The present analysis uses CMD-3 data (Kozyrev 2018) and BaBar data (2013) to provide the first experimental indication of kaonium. The results enable combined analyses of $e^+e^- \to K^+K^-$, $K^0_S K^0_L$, and $\pi^+\pi^-$ data to extract a consistent picture of kaonia, with new CMD-3 $\pi^+\pi^-$ data expected soon.
