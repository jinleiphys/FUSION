# Average ground-state energy of finite Fermi systems

> Semiclassical theories like the Thomas-Fermi and Wigner-Kirkwood methods give a good description of the smooth average part of the total energy of a Fermi gas in some external potential when the chemical potential is varied. However, in systems with a fixed number of particles N, these methods overbind the actual average of the quantum energy as N is varied. We describe a theory that accounts for this effect. Numerical illustrations are discussed for fermions trapped in a harmonic oscillator potential and in a hard wall cavity, and for self-consistent calculations of atomic nuclei. In the latter case, the influence of deformations on the average behavior of the energy is also considered.

## Key claim

Semiclassical methods like Thomas-Fermi and Wigner-Kirkwood overbind the true average ground-state energy when evaluated at fixed particle number N rather than at fixed chemical potential; the correct average is obtained by adding a positive correction proportional to the variance of the fluctuating part of the accumulated level density.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic expression for the average of the ground-state energy at fixed particle number by relating the canonical-ensemble energy to the grand-canonical grand potential and expanding the chemical potential around its smooth Wigner-Kirkwood value. They compute the variance of the fluctuating accumulated level density from periodic-orbit sums and evaluate the resulting correction for fermions in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential (using Wigner-Kirkwood theory) and in a spherical hard-wall cavity (using the Weyl expansion). For self-bound systems they compare fully minimised quantum harmonic-oscillator energies and relativistic mean-field nuclear calculations with the spherical semiclassical results.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical treatment of finite Fermi systems by resolving a known discrepancy between the smooth Wigner-Kirkwood (or Weyl) energy at fixed particle number and the true average of the quantum ground-state energy, previously noted in Refs. [BB, RS, March, BCKT, PS, CVBS, cent06]. The correction formula (Eq. (105)) is derived from the connection between grand-canonical and canonical ensembles, an approach related to the particle-number-conserving shell-correction methods of Ivanyuk and Pomorski. The results enable more accurate mass formulas for nuclei and metal clusters, and the analysis of deformations shows that for self-bound systems the minimised quantum energies lie below the spherical semiclassical curve, in contrast to the overbinding found for fixed external potentials.
