# The effects of the QCD critical point on the spectra and flow coefficients of hadrons

> The space-time evolution of the hot and dense fireball of quarks and gluons produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at non-zero baryonic chemical potential and temperature has been studied by using relativistic viscous causal hydrodynamics. For this purpose a numerical code has been developed to solve the relativistic viscous causal hydrodynamics in (3+1)-dimensions with the inclusion of QCD critical point (CP) through the equation of state and scaling behaviour of the transport coefficients. We have evaluated the transverse momentum spectra, directed and elliptic flow coefficients of pions and protons to comprehend the effect of CP on these observables by using this code. It is found that the integration over the entire space-time history of the fireball largely obliterates the effects of CP on the spectra and flow coefficients.

## Key claim

The authors find that the space-time integration over the entire fireball history "largely obliterates" the effects of the QCD critical point on the transverse momentum spectra and flow coefficients of hadrons, even though the critical point substantially alters the local hydrodynamic evolution (e.g., producing non-monotonic velocity profiles at high space-time rapidity).

## Method

The authors developed a FORTRAN code to solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous causal hydrodynamics (Israel-Stewart formalism) in Milne coordinates, using the relativistic HLLE algorithm. The QCD critical point was incorporated through an equation of state constructed by mapping the 3D Ising model onto the QCD phase diagram (following Parotto et al., 2020) and through scaling behavior of the shear and bulk viscosities with the correlation length (ζ ∼ ξ³, η ∼ ξ⁰·⁰⁵). The code was validated against the analytic Gubser solution and benchmarked against AZHYDRO, MUSIC, and vHLLE codes without the critical point.

## Key numbers

- Critical point coordinates taken as (μBc, Tc) = (350 MeV, 143.2 MeV)
- Correlation length threshold defining the critical region: ξ₀ = 1.75 fm
- Hydrodynamic grid: 201 × 201 × 71 space points with Δx = Δy = Δη = 0.2 fm; time step Δτ = 0.05 fm
- Freeze-out surface defined at constant energy density ε = 0.3 GeV/fm³
- pT integration range for flow coefficients: pT,min = 0.2 GeV to pT,max = 3 GeV
- Rapidity integration range for flow coefficients: ymin = −1 to ymax = 1
- Collision energies studied: √sNN = 14.5 GeV and 62.4 GeV at impact parameter b = 5 fm for Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of the QCD critical point by incorporating both a critical equation of state (from the 3D Ising universality class mapping of Parotto et al., 2020) and critical scaling of transport coefficients into a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulation. It contrasts with earlier studies that focused on fluctuation observables (Stephanov, 2004; Stephanov, 2009) or spin polarization (Singh and Alam, 2021) as CP signals. The finding that space-time integration washes out CP effects in spectra and flow motivates searches for more differential observables (e.g., at specific rapidities or using event-shape engineering) to isolate critical point signatures in Beam Energy Scan experiments at RHIC, CBM, and NICA.
