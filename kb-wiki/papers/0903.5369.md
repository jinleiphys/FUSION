# Exclusive photoproduction of $f_1(1285)$ meson off proton in the JLab kinematics

> Within the Regge approach, we calculated the exclusive $f_1(1285)$ meson photoproduction cross section at energy of few GeV. The calculation shows that the cross section is sizable, being in the range of 100 nb, and much larger than the the expected cross section of the $η(1295)$ meson photoproduction at the same energy. These two facts make possible to use this reaction to study the poor known properties of the $f_1(1285)$ meson in the JLab kinematics.

## Key claim

Within the Regge approach, the exclusive photoproduction cross section of the \(f_1(1285)\) meson off a proton at photon energies of a few GeV is calculated to be sizable, on the order of 100 nb, and significantly larger than the estimated cross section for the \(\eta(1295)\) meson, making the reaction feasible for study at JLab kinematics without requiring complicated partial wave analysis.

## Method

The authors used a Regge model with \(t\)-channel exchange of \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) meson trajectories to calculate the differential cross section for the reaction \(\gamma p \to p f_1(1285)\) at a photon energy of 3.1 GeV. The coupling constants for the vector meson-proton vertex were taken from the Bonn model, and the \(f_1\)-vector meson-photon coupling was fixed from the measured decay width \(\Gamma_{f_1 \to \rho\gamma} \simeq 1.3\) MeV. The \(\eta(1295)\) cross section was estimated using a constituent quark model to relate its two-photon width to known data, and the model was validated by comparing the calculated \(\eta(548)\) photoproduction cross section with SAPHIR data.

## Key numbers

- The integrated cross section for \(\gamma p \to p f_1(1285)\) at \(E_\gamma = 3.1\) GeV is 68 nb.
- The integrated cross section for \(\gamma p \to p \eta(1295)\) at \(E_\gamma = 3.1\) GeV is 18 nb, about 25% of the \(f_1(1285)\) cross section.
- The \(\rho\) meson trajectory parameters are \(\alpha_\rho(t) = 0.55 + 0.8t\) and the \(\omega\) meson trajectory parameters are \(\alpha_\omega(t) = 0.44 + 0.9t\).
- The coupling constant \(g_{\rho f_1\gamma} = 0.94\) GeV\(^{-2}\) was fixed from the measured width \(\Gamma_{f_1 \to \rho\gamma} \simeq 1.3\) MeV.
- The estimated two-photon width for the \(\eta(1295)\) meson is \(\Gamma(\eta(1295) \to 2\gamma) \approx 0.091\) keV.

## Context

This work extends the application of Regge theory to the photoproduction of axial vector mesons, specifically the \(f_1(1285)\), which has been discussed in the context of the \(U(1)_A\) gluon axial anomaly by Kochelev and collaborators (prior work) and as a dynamically generated resonance by Oset and collaborators. The calculation enables experimental searches for the \(f_1(1285)\) meson using the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab, and it also provides a method to study the properties of the \(a_0(980)\) meson, a candidate for a four-quark state, via the \(f_1(1285)\) decay to \(a_0(980)\pi\). The model was validated by comparison with SAPHIR data for \(\eta(548)\) photoproduction.
