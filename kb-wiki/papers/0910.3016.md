# A pQCD-based description of heavy and light flavor jet quenching

> We present a successful description of the medium modification of light and heavy flavor jets within a perturbative QCD (pQCD) based approach. Only the couplings involving hard partons are assumed to be weak. The effect of the medium on a hard parton, per unit time, is encoded in terms of three non-perturbative, related transport coefficients which describe the transverse momentum squared gained, the elastic energy loss and diffusion in elastic energy transfer. A fit of the centrality dependence of the suppression and the azimuthal anisotropy of leading hadrons tends to favor somewhat larger transport coefficients for heavy quarks. Imposing additional constraints based on leading order (LO) Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) effective theory, leads to a worsening of the fit.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a perturbative QCD (pQCD) based approach, where only the coupling of the hard jet to the medium is assumed weak, can successfully describe both light and heavy flavor jet suppression and azimuthal anisotropy at RHIC, provided the transport coefficients are treated as parameters rather than computed from leading-order Hard Thermal Loop (LO-HTL) effective theory; imposing LO-HTL constraints worsens the fit considerably, implying that LO-HTL is insufficient for computing these coefficients.

## Method

The authors model the medium modification of jets using a higher-twist factorization scheme, encoding the medium's effect via three non-perturbative transport coefficients: the transverse momentum squared gained per unit time (hat-q), the elastic energy loss per unit time (hat-e), and the diffusion in elastic energy transfer per unit time (hat-e_2). These coefficients are related via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, and only hat-q is treated as a free parameter, assumed to scale with temperature as T^3. The medium-modified fragmentation functions are evolved using in-medium splitting functions that incorporate both radiative and collisional energy loss, with heavy quark masses included via the dead-cone effect; the final hadron spectra are obtained by convolving nuclear parton distribution functions, leading-order partonic cross sections with K-factors, and the modified fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

* The jet transport parameter hat-q_0 is approximately 1.3 GeV^2/fm for a quark jet at a temperature T = 400 MeV.
* The fit with hat-q proportional to T^3 yields chi^2/d.o.f. = 24/20 (approximately 1.2) for heavy flavor data.
* The fit using LO-HTL transport coefficients yields chi^2/d.o.f. = 110/20 (approximately 5.5) for heavy flavor data.
* Heavy quark masses used: M_c = 1.3 GeV, M_b = 4.2 GeV; heavy meson masses: M_D = 1.9 GeV, M_B = 5.3 GeV.
* The medium is assumed to thermalize at proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c with a peak temperature T_0 = 400 MeV in central collisions.
* K-factors for partonic cross sections: 1.7 for light flavor, 2.4 for heavy flavor.

## Context

This work extends prior pQCD-based calculations of light flavor suppression (Bass 2008) and addresses the long-standing difficulty in describing heavy flavor modification, where earlier efforts by Armesto (2005) required a large hat-q of about 14 GeV^2/fm for gluon jets, and Wicks (2005) could not quantitatively describe non-photonic electron suppression at RHIC. The authors' successful description of both light and heavy flavor observables with a single parameter supports the applicability of pQCD-based jet tomography, while the failure of LO-HTL estimates (consistent with Wicks 2005) indicates that transport coefficients must be computed beyond LO-HTL, enabling future work with more realistic three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations and non-Glauber initial profiles.
