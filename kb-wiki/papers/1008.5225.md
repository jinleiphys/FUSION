# The realistic QCD equation of state in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the early Universe

> The realistic equation of state of strongly interacting matter, that has been successfully applied in the recent hydrodynamic studies of hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, is used in the Friedmann equation to determine the precise time evolution of thermodynamic parameters in the early Universe. A comparison with the results obtained with simple ideal-gas equations of state is made. The realistic equation of state describes a crossover rather than the first-order phase transition between the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter. Our numerical calculations show that small inhomogeneities of strongly interacting matter in the early Universe are moderately damped during such crossover.

## Key claim

The realistic QCD equation of state (EOS) describing a crossover rather than a first-order phase transition between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter, when used in the Friedmann equation, yields small effects on the time evolution of energy density but noticeable differences in pressure and temperature evolution compared to the bag EOS; small inhomogeneities of strongly interacting matter in the early Universe are moderately damped (by about 30%) during such crossover, contrary to the large amplification expected from a first-order transition.

## Method

The author constructed a realistic QCD EOS by interpolating between a hadron-gas model at low temperatures and lattice QCD results at high temperatures, following the approach of Chojnacki (2007). This EOS was then used in the Friedmann equation, assuming isentropic expansion, to compute the time evolution of temperature, energy density, and pressure in the early Universe from an initial temperature of 500 MeV down to 53 MeV. The evolution of energy density fluctuations was studied using the system of equations from Schmid, Schwarz, and Widerin (1996, 1998), solving five coupled ordinary differential equations for density contrasts and fluid velocities of the QCD matter and the electro-weak sector.

## Key numbers

- Initial condition: temperature \(T_0 = 500\) MeV, corresponding to total energy density \(\varepsilon_{R,0} = 138\) GeV/fm\(^3\) at initial time \(t_0 = 1.35\ \mu\)s.
- The sound velocity \(c_s^2(T)\) has a characteristic minimum at \(T \sim 170\) MeV.
- For the bag EOS with two flavors, two values of the bag constant were used: \(B = (235\ \text{MeV})^4\) (critical temperature \(T_c = 169\) MeV) and \(B = (165\ \text{MeV})^4\) (critical temperature \(T_c = 119\) MeV).
- The scale factor \(a(t)/a(t_0)\) is approximated by \((t/t_0)^p\) with \(p = 0.517\) over the time interval from \(t = 1.35\ \mu\)s to \(t = 220\ \mu\)s.
- The final temperature at \(t = 220\ \mu\)s is 53 MeV.
- The amplitude of the density contrast \(\delta\) of strongly interacting matter decreases by about 30% during the crossover transition for subhorizon modes with \(\xi = 10\) and \(\xi = 5\).

## Context

This work extends the use of a realistic QCD EOS, previously applied successfully in 2+1 and 3+1 hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC by Chojnacki (2007) and Bozek (2009), to cosmological calculations via the Friedmann equation. It contrasts with earlier cosmological studies that used simple bag EOS models with a first-order phase transition, and differs from the approach of Sassi (2009) by using different lattice results from Aoki (2005) and combining them with a hadron-gas model at low temperatures. The results indicate that the crossover transition produces only moderate damping of density fluctuations, in contrast to the large peaks and dips predicted for first-order transitions by Schmid, Schwarz, and Widerin (1996, 1998), suggesting small chances for observing exotic phenomena associated with first-order QCD phase transitions.
