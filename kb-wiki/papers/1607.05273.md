# Holographic heavy ion collisions with baryon charge

> We numerically simulate collisions of charged shockwaves in Einstein-Maxwell theory in anti-de Sitter space as a toy model of heavy ion collisions with non-zero baryon charge. The stress tensor and the baryon current become well described by charged hydrodynamics at roughly the same time. The effect of the charge density on generic observables is typically no larger than 15\%. %The rapidity profile of the charge is wider than the profile of the local energy density. We find significant stopping of the baryon charge and compare our results with those in heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The stress tensor and baryon current become well described by charged hydrodynamics at roughly the same time, with the effect of charge density on generic observables typically no larger than 15%, though this result is obtained in a toy model using Einstein-Maxwell theory in anti-de Sitter space and may depend on the specific shock width and charge parameters chosen.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Einstein-Maxwell equations in five-dimensional anti-de Sitter space to simulate collisions of charged gravitational shock waves, which are dual via gauge-gravity duality to collisions of energy lumps carrying baryon charge. They evolved both the metric and Maxwell field simultaneously for symmetric collisions of shocks with Gaussian profiles of energy and charge density, working first in the probe approximation (e=0) and then including full backreaction of the charge on the metric. The simulations extracted the stress tensor and baryon current, from which local energy density, charge density, fluid velocity, and chemical potential were computed and compared to predictions from charged hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The effect of charge density on generic observables is typically no larger than 15% for thin shocks and 6% for thick shocks, based on backreaction scaling with e^2 (maximum e=1.12 for thin shocks, e=2.85 for thick shocks)
- For thin shocks (m w = 0.1), hydrodynamization times for the stress tensor are m thy = {2.0, 2.4, 3.6} at m z = {0, 1.5, 3.0}; for the current, m thy^J = {2.0, 3.5} at m z = {1.5, 3.0}
- For thick shocks (m w = 1.9), hydrodynamization times for the stress tensor are m thy = {2.1, 4.7, 16} at m z = {0, 5, 15}; the current is always well predicted by hydrodynamics (thy^J = 0 at m z = {5, 15})
- In asymmetric collisions where only the left-moving shock carries charge, about 41% of the charge ends up at positive z, indicating strong interactions
- The charge density in backreacted simulations reaches up to 80% of the maximum possible value (rho_max / E^(3/4) = 2/3) for thick shocks
- For thin shocks with e=1.07, the hydrodynamization time is 3% shorter than in the neutral case; for thick shocks with e=1.7, it is 6% longer

## Context

This work extends the holographic heavy ion collision simulations of Chesler (2010) and Casalderrey-Solana (2013) from neutral plasmas to plasmas with non-zero baryon charge, using the Einstein-Maxwell theory previously applied to homogeneous relaxation by Fuini (2015). The results enable future studies of baryon stopping and charge-dependent observables in strongly coupled plasmas, and suggest that comparing collisions with different baryon charges but similar energies (e.g., proton versus antiproton collisions) could test these predictions experimentally.
