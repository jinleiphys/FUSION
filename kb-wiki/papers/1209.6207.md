# Production of doubly charmed tetraquarks with exotic color configurations in electron-positron collisions

> Structure and production of doubly charmed tetraquarks T_cc (cc ubar dbar) are studied from the viewpoint of color configurations. Based on the diquark correlation, the tetraquark T_cc with I(JP)=0(1+) is considered to be stable against strong decay. We discuss that the mixing probability of color antitriplet and sextet cc components in T_cc is suppressed by 1/m_c^2, so the two configurations are separately realized in the heavy quark limit. Utilizing the nonrelativistic QCD framework, we evaluate the production cross sections of T_cc in electron-positron collisions. The momentum dependence of the cross section of color antitriplet is found to be different from that of sextet, which can be used to discriminate the color structure of the T_cc states in experimental measurements.

# Production of doubly charmed tetraquarks with exotic color configurations in electron-positron collisions

## Key claim

The doubly charmed tetraquark T_cc can exist in two distinct color configurations (color antitriplet and color sextet for the cc pair) that do not mix in the heavy quark limit due to suppression by 1/m_c^2, and these two configurations produce measurably different momentum distributions in electron-positron collisions, allowing experimental discrimination of the color structure.

## Method

The authors use nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) at leading order in both α_s and velocity expansion to factorize the production cross section into short-distance perturbative amplitudes and long-distance nonperturbative matrix elements. The short-distance part is computed from Feynman diagrams for e⁺e⁻ → [cc] + c̄ + c̄ with color-spin projections for the cc pair in either the antitriplet (³S₁) or sextet (¹S₀) configuration. The nonperturbative matrix elements h_α are estimated using a harmonic oscillator quark model to obtain the cc radial wave function at the origin, with parameters fixed to reproduce charmonium wave functions from the Cornell potential model of Barnes (2005).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of T_cc[3̄,³S₁] relative to DD* threshold: 71 MeV, estimated from color-spin interaction with parameters C_B/m_u² = 193 MeV, C_M/m_u² = 635 MeV, m_u = 300 MeV, m_c = 1500 MeV, and C_cc/m_c² = 39 MeV
- Mass splitting between T_cc[6,¹S₀] and T_cc[3̄,³S₁]: 125 MeV from color-spin interaction
- Total cross section at √s = 10.6 GeV with m_c = 1.8 GeV, α_s = 0.212: σ[3̄,³S₁] = 13.8 fb, σ[6,¹S₀] = 4.1 fb
- Total cross section at √s = 10.6 GeV with m_c = 1.5 GeV, α_s = 0.26: σ[3̄,³S₁] = 65 fb, σ[6,¹S₀] = 21 fb
- Nonperturbative matrix elements from harmonic oscillator model: h[3̄,³S₁] = 0.089 GeV³, h[6,¹S₀] = 0.054 GeV³
- Momentum distribution peak for T_cc[3̄,³S₁] at p ≈ 3.5 GeV; for T_cc[6,¹S₀] at p ≈ 2.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of doubly charmed tetraquarks from earlier quark model calculations by Lee (2007, 2009) and Vijande (2009), which established the diquark picture and predicted T_cc stability. It applies the NRQCD factorization approach previously used for double-charmonium production by Braaten (2002), Liu (2002), and Zhang (2005, 2008), and for doubly charmed baryon Ξ_cc production by Ma (2003) and Jiang (2012). The key new feature is the separate treatment of the color sextet configuration, which cannot appear in ordinary hadrons, and the prediction that its momentum distribution differs qualitatively from the conventional antitriplet case. This enables future experimental searches at Belle and BaBar to distinguish the color structure of T_cc states through momentum-dependent cross section measurements.
