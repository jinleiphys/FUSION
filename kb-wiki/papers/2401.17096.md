# Heavy flavour hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC in EPOS4HQ

> Employing the recently developed EPOS4HQ event generator, we study the production of different heavy-flavor mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The transverse momentum spectra, yield ratio, nuclear modification factor, and elliptic flow can be well described in the EPOS4HQ framework. We furthermore analyze the processes which modify these observables as compared to $pp$ collisions and are at the origin of the experimentally determined nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$.

## Key claim

The EPOS4HQ event generator can simultaneously describe the transverse momentum spectra, yield ratios, nuclear modification factor, and elliptic flow of multiple heavy-flavor meson species in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, but only when the heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma is modeled with a specific infrared regulator parameter \(\kappa = 0.2\) that reproduces lattice QCD diffusion coefficients.

## Method

The authors extended the EPOS4 event generator with heavy quark production via hard processes, gluon splitting, and flavor excitation, then propagated heavy quarks through a hydrodynamically evolving QGP medium using pQCD elastic and radiative scattering cross sections with a running coupling and Debye-screened gluon propagators. Hadronization was modeled as a combination of coalescence with thermal light quarks at the freeze-out hypersurface (temperature \(T_{\rm FO} = 167\) MeV) and fragmentation using heavy quark effective theory, with subsequent hadronic rescattering handled by UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV and bottom quark mass set to \(m_b = 4.5\) GeV
- Freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm FO} = 167\) MeV and freeze-out energy density \(\epsilon_{\rm FO} = 0.57\) GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Effective Debye mass regulator \(\kappa = 0.2\) used to match lattice QCD spatial diffusion coefficient \(2\pi T \mathcal{D}_s\)
- Root-mean-square radius of in-medium \(D^0\) meson taken as \(\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle} = 0.85\) fm, corresponding to width \(\sigma = 3.725\) GeV\(^{-1}\)
- Light quark masses: \(m_{u/d} = 0.1\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.3\) GeV
- Number of excited states considered for hadronization: 9 for \(D^0\), 91 for \(\Lambda_c\), 91 for \(\Lambda_b\), among others
- Fragmentation ratios from \(e^+e^-\) collisions: \(D^0\) 60.8%, \(D^+\) 24.0%, \(D_s\) 8.0%, \(\Lambda_c\) 6.0%

## Context

This work extends the EPOS4 framework (Werner, 2023) for light hadron observables to heavy flavor physics, improving upon earlier EPOS2/EPOS3/EPOSLHC heavy quark calculations by including baryons and excited meson states, conserving energy-momentum at interaction vertices, and preserving \(c\bar{c}\) momentum correlations. The approach enables quantitative comparison with ALICE data for \(D^0\), \(D^+\), \(D_s\), and \(\Lambda_c\) mesons, and provides a unified model where the same QGP evolution simultaneously describes both light and heavy flavor observables, unlike models such as TAMU (He, 2019), Duke (Cao, 2015), or Torino (Beraudo, 2022) that focus solely on heavy quark physics.
