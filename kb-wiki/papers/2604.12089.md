# Dynamics of density fluctuations in atomic nuclei

> We study the spatiotemporal patterns of density fluctuations in $^{16,24}$O and $^{48}$Ca using nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory and the time-dependent coupled-cluster method. We find that two-particle-two-hole excitations generate small-amplitude fluctuations that are fast, short-ranged and of stochastic character.

# Dynamics of density fluctuations in atomic nuclei

## Key claim

Two-particle-two-hole excitations generate small-amplitude, fast, short-ranged density fluctuations in atomic nuclei that appear stochastic in character, but these fluctuations are only visible in *ab initio* time-dependent coupled-cluster computations and cannot be detected in mean-field approaches; the fluctuations are model-dependent (sensitive to the interaction cutoff) yet universal across different nuclei for a given Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors employed time-dependent coupled-cluster theory at the singles-and-doubles (CCSD) and doubles-only (CCD) levels, starting from a Hartree-Fock reference state and using chiral effective field theory interactions (NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ with 450 MeV/$c$ cutoff and $\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$(394) with 394 MeV/$c$ cutoff). They computed the time-dependent one-body density for $^{16,24}$O and $^{48}$Ca in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with $N_{\rm max}=8$ and $\hbar\Omega = 16$ MeV, then subtracted the time-averaged density to isolate fluctuations. The equations of motion were integrated using the adaptive CVODE solver with time steps of 0.2 fm/$c$, recording results at 1 fm/$c$ increments.

## Key numbers

- Density fluctuation amplitude in CCSD for $^{48}$Ca: about 6% of saturation density $\rho_0\approx0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ at small radii, with slow oscillation period of about 50 fm/$c$
- Shortest fluctuation period observed for NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ interaction: 3 fm/$c$, corresponding to energy scale $E \sim \pi\hbar c/(3\ \mathrm{fm}) \approx 207$ MeV, consistent with maximum kinetic energy $\Lambda^2/(2\mu) \approx 215$ MeV for momentum cutoff $\Lambda = 450$ MeV/$c$
- Short-range fluctuations in CCD extend up to 1-2 fm spatially and occur on time scales of 3-4 fm/$c$ for NNLO$_{\rm sat}$, with longer periods up to 10 fm/$c$ for $\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}$(394)
- Time step used in integration: 0.2 fm/$c$, chosen to be much smaller than the time scale associated with the interaction cutoff
- Time-averaged density computed using integration limits $\tau_{\rm min}=100$ fm/$c$ and $\tau_{\rm max}=200$ fm/$c$

## Context

This work extends time-dependent *ab initio* computations beyond the mean-field level, contrasting with time-dependent Hartree-Fock simulations of fission by Goddard (2015), Sekizawa (2016), and others that show only slow oscillations on tens of fm/$c$ scales and cannot capture two-particle-two-hole induced fluctuations. The short-range fluctuations are consistent with static short-range correlation studies by Subedi (2008), Fomin (2012), and Hen (2014), but this work reveals their spatial and temporal patterns simultaneously. The findings enable future *ab initio* studies of fission and fusion dynamics, though the authors note that such computations require further advances beyond the present capabilities.

Review-type paper: no original numerics.
