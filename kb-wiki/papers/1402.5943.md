# Geometric scaling of Efimov states in a $^{6}\textrm{Li}$-$^{133}\textrm{Cs}$ mixture

> In few-body physics, Efimov states are an infinite series of three-body bound states that obey universal discrete scaling symmetry when pairwise interactions are resonantly enhanced. Despite abundant reports of Efimov states in recent cold atom experiments, direct observation of the discrete scaling symmetry remains an elusive goal. Here we report the observation of three consecutive Efimov resonances in a heteronuclear Li-Cs mixture near a broad interspecies Feshbach resonance. The positions of the resonances closely follow a geometric series $1$, $λ$, $λ^2$. The observed scaling constant $λ_{\rm exp} = 4.9(4)$ is in good agreement with the predicted value of 4.88.

## Key claim

The authors report the observation of three consecutive Efimov resonances in a heteronuclear lithium-cesium mixture, whose positions closely follow a geometric series 1, λ, λ², providing a model-independent test of the discrete scaling symmetry of Efimov states. The abstract glosses over that the first resonance may be shifted by finite-range corrections and that practical factors such as finite temperature and finite trap size could contribute to differences between experiment and theory.

## Method

The experiment used a Fermi-Bose mixture of ⁶Li and ¹³³Cs atoms near quantum degeneracy in an optical dipole trap, prepared in their lowest spin states. Efimov resonances were identified as enhanced three-body recombination losses by measuring Li and Cs atom numbers as a function of magnetic field near a broad interspecies Feshbach resonance at 842.75 G. The resonance positions were determined using Lorentzian and Gaussian fits with linear background, and the scattering lengths at resonance were extracted using an updated scattering model for the Li-Cs Feshbach resonance.

## Key numbers

- Three Efimov resonance positions: B₁ = 848.55(12)stat(3)sys G, B₂ = 843.82(4)stat(3)sys G, B₃ = 842.97(3)stat(3)sys G
- Feshbach resonance position: B₀ = 842.75(1)stat(3)sys G
- Separations from Feshbach resonance: ΔB₁ = 5.80(12) G, ΔB₂ = 1.07(4) G, ΔB₃ = 0.22(3) G
- Efimov resonances in scattering length: a₋⁽¹⁾ = -323(8) a₀, a₋⁽²⁾ = -1635(60) a₀, a₋⁽³⁾ = -7850(1100) a₀
- Scaling constants: λ₂₁ = a₋⁽²⁾/a₋⁽¹⁾ = 5.1(2), λ₃₂ = a₋⁽³⁾/a₋⁽²⁾ = 4.8(7)
- Averaged scaling constant: λ_exp = 4.9(4), in agreement with predicted value λ = 4.88

## Context

This work extends previous observations of Efimov resonances in homonuclear systems (Kraemer 2006, Pollack 2009, Zaccanti 2009) and heteronuclear K-Rb mixtures (Barontini 2009, Bloom 2013) by providing the first model-independent proof of discrete scaling symmetry through three consecutive resonances. The use of a Li-Cs mixture with large mass ratio yields a smaller scaling constant (λ ≈ 4.88) than homonuclear systems (λ ≈ 22.7), making multiple resonances experimentally accessible. The results enable future tests of whether discrete scaling symmetry persists in the unitary Bose gas regime, in contrast with the continuous scaling symmetry of a unitary Fermi gas.
