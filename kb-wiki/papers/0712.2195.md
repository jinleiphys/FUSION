# Quark and Gluon Degrees of Freedom in High-Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> I discuss some recent progress in our understanding of high energy nuclear collisions. I will focus on two topics which I was lucky to co-pioneer in the recent past. One is recombination of quarks and its interpretation as a signal for deconfinement, the second is electromagnetic radiation from jets passing through a quark gluon plasma. This talk was given during the award ceremony for the 2007 IUPAP Young Scientist Award.

# Quark and Gluon Degrees of Freedom in High-Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Quark recombination explains the baryon/meson ratio of order unity at intermediate transverse momentum (around 4 GeV/c) at RHIC, and the resulting scaling law for elliptic flow with the number of valence quarks provides the best direct signal for deconfinement. However, the recombination picture is only valid for momenta above about 1.5 GeV/c and below the fragmentation-dominated region, and a completely satisfying dynamical description is still lacking due to its non-perturbative nature.

## Method
The author reviews two co-pioneered topics: (1) quark recombination, where hadron production proceeds through instant projection of thermal quark states onto hadron states using the hadron wave function, preserving 3-momentum but not energy; (2) jet-medium photon production, where jets traveling through the quark gluon plasma radiate photons via leading-order channels (annihilation and Compton scattering), with cross sections proportional to the input jet spectra.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature for the QCD phase transition is around T_c ≈ 180 MeV, with critical energy density ε_c ≈ 1 GeV/fm³.
- At RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV), the proton/pion ratio reaches p/π ≈ 1 at P_T ≈ 4 GeV/c.
- The transition from recombination-dominated to fragmentation-dominated hadron production occurs around P_T ≈ 4 GeV/c for mesons and about 6 GeV/c for baryons.
- Recombination is only valid for P_T above about 1.5 GeV/c.
- Jet-medium photon elliptic flow v₂ can reach negative values as large as -5%.
- At the LHC (√s_NN = 5.5 TeV), jet-medium dileptons remain the dominant source at intermediate masses even for P_T > 8 GeV/c.

## Context
This talk extends the recombination approach first proposed around 2003 by Fries and collaborators at Duke University, as well as by other groups (Greiner, Ko, Levai, Hwa, Yang), which resolved the baryon anomaly observed at RHIC that contradicted the fragmentation picture from QCD jets (Collins, 1981). The jet-medium photon work (Fries, 2002; Gale, 2004) combines electromagnetic probes with jet energy loss studies (PHENIX, 2003) to provide complementary information about the medium. The scaling law for elliptic flow builds on earlier work by Voloshin (2002) and has been confirmed by STAR (2003) and PHENIX (2003) data. Future measurements at the RHIC luminosity upgrade and LHC will enable jet-photon and hadron-photon correlations to definitively establish the importance of jet-medium photons.
