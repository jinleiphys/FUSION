# Stiffening of matter in quark-hadron continuity

> We discuss stiffening of matter in quark-hadron continuity. We introduce a model that relates quark wave functions in a baryon and the occupation probability of states for baryons and quarks in dense matter. In a dilute regime, the confined quarks contribute to the energy density through the masses of baryons, but do not directly contribute to the pressure; hence, the equations of state are very soft. This dilute regime continues until the low momentum states for quarks get saturated; this may happen even before baryons fully overlap, possibly at density slightly above the nuclear saturation density. After the saturation the pressure grows rapidly while changes in energy density are modest, producing a peak in the speed of sound. If we use baryonic descriptions for quark distributions near the Fermi surface, we reach a description similar to the quarkyonic matter model of McLerran and Reddy. With a simple adjustment of quark interactions to get the nucleon mass, our model becomes consistent with the constraints from 1.4-solar mass neutron stars, but the high density part is too soft to account for two-solar mass neutron stars. We delineate the relation between the saturation effects and short range interactions of quarks, suggesting interactions that leave low density equations of state unchanged but stiffen the high density part.

## Key claim

The paper argues that stiffening of dense matter in quark-hadron continuity arises from saturation of low-momentum quark states, which may begin at densities slightly above nuclear saturation density (1-3 times nuclear saturation density) even before baryon cores fully overlap, but the model without additional short-range interactions is too soft to account for two-solar-mass neutron stars.

## Method

The author constructs a model that relates quark wave functions inside a baryon to the occupation probability of quark states in dense matter, using a Gaussian momentum distribution for quarks within baryons characterized by a scale Lambda related to the baryon radius. The model computes the quark occupation probability by integrating over baryon momentum states, then derives the equation of state from the quark energy density and chemical potential, with a postulated form for the occupation probability after saturation that includes a fully occupied Fermi sea at low momenta plus a partially occupied tail.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Baryon size scale: 0.5-0.8 fm, corresponding to Lambda = 0.2-0.3 GeV
- Quark saturation at p=0 occurs at nB = 0.5-2 n0 for Lambda = 0.2-0.3 GeV
- In the large Nc limit, the critical baryon Fermi momentum for saturation is P_F^c = 1.1 Lambda
- For Nc=3, Mq=0.3 GeV, and Lambda=0.25 GeV, the baryon mass is MB = 1.26 GeV
- The chemical potential jump at saturation in the relativistic regime is approximately 0.18 MB
- For Nc=3, Mq=0.3 GeV, and Lambda=0.25 GeV, the chemical potential jump is about 0.1 GeV
- The model is consistent with constraints from 1.4-solar-mass neutron stars but too soft for two-solar-mass neutron stars

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter model of McLerran and Reddy (2018) by describing the crossover from baryonic to quark matter using only quark degrees of freedom, avoiding double-counting issues present in hybrid descriptions. The paper builds on earlier phenomenological interpolation work by Masuda et al. (2012-2015) and the Quark-Hadron-Crossover framework of Baym et al. (2017-2019), and it aims to provide a microscopic foundation for the peak in the speed of sound that these earlier works identified as necessary to satisfy neutron star observations including the 2-solar-mass constraint from Demorest et al. (2010) and radius measurements from NICER.
