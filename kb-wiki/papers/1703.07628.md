# Influence of complex configurations on properties of pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich Ca isotopes

> Starting from the quasiparticle random phase approximation based on the Skyrme interaction SLy5, we study the effects of phonon-phonon coupling~(PPC) on the low-energy electric dipole response in $^{40-58}$Ca. Using the same set of parameters we describe available experimental data for $^{40,44,48}$Ca and give prediction for $^{50-58}$Ca. The inclusion of the PPC results in the formation of low-energy $1^-$ states. There is an impact of the PPC effect on low-energy $E1$~strength of $^{40,44,48}$Ca. The PPC effect on the electric dipole polarizability is discussed. We predict a strong increase of the summed $E1$~strength below 10MeV, with increasing neutron number from $^{48}$Ca till $^{58}$Ca.

# Influence of complex configurations on properties of pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich Ca isotopes

## Key claim
The inclusion of phonon-phonon coupling (PPC) beyond the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) is necessary to reproduce the low-energy electric dipole strength in Ca isotopes, particularly the formation of low-lying 1⁻ states that the QRPA alone fails to produce. The authors predict a strong increase of summed E1 strength below 10 MeV with increasing neutron number from ⁴⁸Ca to ⁵⁸Ca, but note that the QRPA calculations alone fail to reproduce experimental data for lighter Ca isotopes, requiring the PPC extension.

## Method
Starting from Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations with the Skyrme energy density functional SLy5 (and SLy5+T including tensor terms), the authors built QRPA phonon states using a finite-rank separable approximation for the residual interaction. They then constructed wave functions as linear combinations of one-phonon and two-phonon configurations, solving the resulting set of linear equations to include phonon-phonon coupling effects on the 1⁻ states in even-even ⁴⁰⁻⁵⁸Ca isotopes.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴⁸Ca, the PPC calculation gives a total dipole strength of 0.063 e²fm² below 10 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.0687 ± 0.0075 e²fm² (Hartmann 2004)
- The electric dipole polarizability α_D for ⁴⁸Ca is 2.28 fm³ with SLy5 and 2.20 fm³ with SLy5+T, both within the experimental range 2.07 ± 0.22 fm³ (Birkhan 2016)
- The PPC increases the GDR width in ⁴⁸Ca from 6.9 MeV to 7.3 MeV in the energy region 10-26 MeV, with a 300 keV downward shift of the GDR energy from 19.3 MeV (RPA) to match the experimental GDR energy of 19.5 MeV (Keefe 1987)
- For ⁴⁸Ca, the candidate two-phonon 1⁻ state is expected at 7.298 MeV with B(E1) = 18.6 ± 1.8 × 10⁻³ e²fm² (Hartmann 2000)
- The neutron skin thickness ΔR_np of ⁴⁸Ca is 0.16 fm with SLy5 and 0.14 fm with SLy5+T, within the experimental range 0.14-0.20 fm (Birkhan 2016)
- The M1 contribution to the integrated photo-absorption cross section in ⁴⁸Ca is less than 1%

## Context
This work extends the finite-rank separable approximation approach previously applied to Sn isotopes (Arsenyev 2012, 2015, 2016) and to Ni, Zr, and Mo nuclei (Severyukhin 2014, 2012). It addresses the known failure of standard QRPA calculations (Terasaki 2006, Vretenar 2001) to reproduce low-energy dipole strength in Ca isotopes, and the results are in good agreement with relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) calculations (Egorova 2016). The study enables improved predictions for radiative neutron capture cross sections relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis and provides constraints on the symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness through the electric dipole polarizability.
