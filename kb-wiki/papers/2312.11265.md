# Lattice-based equation of state with 3D Ising critical point

> The BEST Collaboration equation of state combining lattice data with the 3D Ising critical point encounters limitations due to the truncated Taylor expansion up to $\frac{μ_B}{T} \sim 2.5$. This truncation consequently restricts its applicability at high densities. Through a resummation scheme, the lattice results have been extended to $\frac{μ_B}{T} = 3.5$. In this article, we amalgamate these ideas with the 3D-Ising model, yielding a family of equations of state valid up to $μ_B=700 \text{MeV}$ with the correct critical behavior. Our equations of state feature tunable parameters, providing a stable and causal framework-a crucial tool for hydrodynamics simulations.

# Lattice-based equation of state with 3D Ising critical point

## Key claim
The authors extend the BEST Collaboration equation of state to baryon chemical potentials up to 700 MeV by combining a resummation scheme for lattice data with the 3D Ising critical point, but the construction remains a parametric family with tunable parameters rather than a unique prediction, and the critical point location is chosen rather than determined from first principles.

## Method
The authors map the 3D Ising model critical behavior to QCD coordinates using a two-step mapping that first transforms to an effective temperature coordinate T' where the first-order transition line is constant, then maps to physical (T, μ_B) coordinates. They merge the critical contribution with lattice data by defining a full effective temperature T'_full that combines the lattice-based resummation (kept to O((μ_B/T)^2)) with the critical part from the Ising mapping, using Eq. (1) to reconstruct the baryon density from the zero-chemical-potential baryon susceptibility evaluated at T'_full.

## Key numbers
- The equation of state is valid up to μ_B = 700 MeV, extending beyond the previous BEST Collaboration limit of μ_B ≲ 450 MeV
- The resummation scheme extends lattice results from μ_B/T ∼ 2.5 to μ_B/T = 3.5
- Example parameters shown: μ_BC = 500 MeV, T_C = 116.49 MeV, w = 15.0, ρ = 0.3, α_1 = 11.19 degrees, α_12 = α_1
- The baryon density and second-order baryon susceptibility are computed, with the susceptibility showing divergence at the critical point and the density showing a discontinuity marking the first-order transition

## Context
This work extends the BEST Collaboration equation of state (Parotto 2020) which was limited to μ_B ≲ 450 MeV due to unphysical wiggles from Taylor expansion extrapolation. It incorporates the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration resummation scheme (Borsanyi 2021) that uses a chemical-potential-dependent effective temperature to achieve smooth behavior at higher densities. The resulting family of equations of state provides a stable and causal framework suitable for hydrodynamics simulations in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan regime, with the critical line following the expected QCD curvature and reducing the number of free parameters compared to the earlier BEST construction.
