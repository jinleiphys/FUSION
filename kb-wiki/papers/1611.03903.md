# Parity Breaking Medium and Squeeze Operators

> The transition between a Minkowski space region and a parity breaking medium domain is thoroughly discussed. The requirement of continuity of the field operator content across the separating boundary of the two domains leads to Bogolyubov transformations, squeezed pairs states and squeeze operators that turn out to generate a functional SU(2) algebra. According to this algebraic approach, the reflection and transmission probability amplitude across the separating boundary are computed. The probability rate of the emission or absorption of squeezed pairs out of the vacuum (generalization of the Sauter-Schwinger-Nikishov formula) is obtained.

## Key claim

The transition between a Minkowski space region and a parity-breaking medium containing a Chern-Simons term requires continuity of the field operator content across the boundary, which leads to Bogolyubov transformations and squeezed pair states; the resulting squeeze operators generate a functional SU(2) algebra, and the probability rate for emission or absorption of squeezed pairs from the vacuum generalizes the Sauter-Schwinger-Nikishov formula specifically for the case of a space-like Chern-Simons vector orthogonal to a flat boundary.

## Method

The authors consider massive Abelian vector fields (Proca-Stückelberg type) on a flat spacetime divided by a three-dimensional hyper-plane, with a Chern-Simons interaction active on one half-space and ordinary vacuum on the other. They impose continuity of the quantum field operators across the boundary, derive the Bogolyubov transformations linking creation and destruction operators on the two sides, and construct the corresponding squeeze operator algebra. The reflection and transmission probability amplitudes across the boundary are computed using this algebraic approach, and the rate of squeezed pair emission and absorption from the vacuum is evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-scalar-vector coupling involves a mass parameter \(M\) (the axion decay constant) with a natural size varying between \(10^{9}\) and \(10^{17}\) GeV.
- The cold axion dark matter density is given as \(\rho \simeq 10^{-30}\ \mathrm{g\ cm}^{-3} \simeq 10^{-46}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{4}\).
- The dispersion relations for the two chiral modes in the Maxwell-Chern-Simons medium are \(\omega_{\mathbf{k},\pm} = \sqrt{\mathbf{k}^{2} + m^{2} + \frac12 \zeta_{x}^{2} \pm \zeta_{x} \sqrt{k_{x}^{2} + m^{2} + \frac14 \zeta_{x}^{2}}}\) for a Chern-Simons vector \(\zeta^{\mu} = (0, \zeta_{x}, 0, 0)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lorentz and CPT invariance violation in quantum electrodynamics initiated by Carroll, Field, and Jackiw (1989), who introduced the Chern-Simons term in the action. It builds on prior studies of Maxwell-Chern-Simons electrodynamics in bounded volumes (AACGS 2010) and on the treatment of squeezed pairs in the presence of a boundary (AKS 2011). The results enable a quantum field-theoretic description of particle production at interfaces between parity-violating media and vacuum, with potential applications to heavy-ion collision experiments (such as those at RHIC and LHC) where local parity breaking may occur via pseudo-scalar condensates.
