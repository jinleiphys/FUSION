# Implications of Feebly Interacting Dark Sector on Neutron Star Properties and Constraints from GW170817

> We investigate the effect of feeble interaction of dark matter (DM) with hadronic matter on the equation of state (EoS) and structural properties of neutron stars (NSs) in static conditions. For the purpose we adopt the effective chiral model for the hadronic sector and for the first time in the context of possible existence of DM inside NSs, we introduce DM-SM interaction through light new physics mediator. Moreover, the mass of DM fermion, the mediator and the coupling are adopted from the self-interaction constraint from Bullet cluster and from present day relic abundance. Within the considered framework, the work highlights the underlying stiffening of EoS in presence of DM fermion of mass of the order of a few GeV compared to the no-DM scenario. Consequently, the maximum gravitational mass of NS is obtained consistent with the bounds from the most massive pulsars which were not satisfied with the hadronic matter EoS alone. The estimates of radius and tidal deformability of 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ NS and the tidal deformabilities of the individual components of the binary neutron stars (BNS) associated with GW170817 are all in good agreement with the individual constraints obtained from GW170817 observation of BNS merger.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a feebly interacting dark matter (DM) sector with a light scalar mediator, where the DM fermion mass is of order a few GeV, stiffens the neutron star equation of state compared to the pure hadronic case, thereby allowing the model to satisfy the maximum mass constraints from massive pulsars that the pure hadronic equation of state alone could not meet.

## Method

The authors adopt an effective chiral model for the hadronic sector and introduce a fermionic dark matter particle that interacts with baryons via a light scalar mediator that mixes with the Standard Model Higgs boson. The dark matter fermion mass, mediator mass, and coupling are chosen to satisfy self-interaction constraints from the Bullet cluster and present-day relic abundance. The total equation of state is computed in mean-field approximation, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain neutron star structural properties, including tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

* Maximum gravitational mass of DM-admixed neutron stars reaches values consistent with PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses) and PSR J0740+6620 (2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} solar masses at 68.3% confidence), whereas the pure hadronic equation of state alone could not satisfy these bounds.
* Dark matter fermion masses considered range from 1 GeV to 50 GeV, with corresponding mediator masses from 4 MeV to 60 MeV and Yukawa couplings from 0.06 to 0.32, as listed in Table 2 of the paper.
* The DM Fermi momentum is taken as constant between 0.01 GeV and 0.06 GeV, corresponding to a DM number density roughly 1000 times smaller than the average neutron number density, with DM mass fraction inside the neutron star of order 0.01.
* The radius and tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, and the tidal deformabilities of the individual binary components associated with GW170817, are all in good agreement with the constraints from the GW170817 observation.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dark matter in neutron stars by introducing a light mediator for DM-baryon interaction, in contrast to earlier approaches that either used only gravitational interaction (two-fluid method) or direct Higgs-mediated coupling. The authors note that previous work by Panotopoulos (2017) using 200 GeV DM softened the equation of state, whereas the present lighter DM (few GeV) stiffens it. The model revives a hadronic parameter set from Jha (2009) that was previously ruled out by massive pulsar constraints, and the results are compared with constraints from the NICER experiment for PSR J0030+0451 and from the GW170817 binary neutron star merger.
