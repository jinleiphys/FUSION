# Dimensional Effects on the Momentum distribution of Bosonic Trimer States

> The momentum distribution is a powerful probe of strongly-interacting systems that are expected to display universal behavior. This is contained in the contact parameters which relate few- and many-body properties. Here we consider a Bose gas in two dimensions and explicitly show that the two-body contact parameter is universal and then demonstrate that the momentum distribution at next-to-leading order has a logarithmic dependence on momentum which is vastly different from the three-dimensional case. Based on this, we propose a scheme for measuring the effective dimensionality of a quantum many-body system by exploiting the functional form of the momentum distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a two-dimensional Bose gas with short-range interactions, the next-to-leading-order (NLO) term in the momentum distribution has a logarithmic dependence on momentum (proportional to \(\ln^3(k)/k^6\)), which is radically different from the oscillatory log-periodic behavior seen in three dimensions, and proposes using this functional form as a probe of the effective dimensionality of a quantum many-body system.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev integral equations for three identical bosons in two dimensions with zero-range attractive interactions parameterized by the dimer binding energy \(E_2\). They compute the spectator functions \(f(q)\) analytically in the large-momentum limit, finding \(f(q) \to A_m \ln(q)/q^2\), and then evaluate the four components of the single-particle momentum distribution \(n(q)\) to extract the leading-order (LO) and NLO asymptotic behavior. Numerical checks are performed by solving the coupled integral equations and comparing with the analytical asymptotic forms.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state trimer energy: \(E_3 = 16.52 E_2\); excited-state trimer energy: \(E_3 = 1.270 E_2\).
- Leading-order two-body contact: \(C_2/E_3 = 0.222 \pm 0.003\), independent of the state (ground or excited) to within numerical accuracy of about 2%.
- Ground-state NLO three-body contact: \(C_3^0 = 52.07\); excited-state NLO three-body contact: \(C_3^1 = 1.01\).
- Asymptotic spectator function constants: \(A_0 \approx 1.800\) for the ground state; \(A_1 \approx 0.251\) for the excited state.
- LO tail for ground state: \(n_3^0(q) \to 3.71 E_2 / q^4\); for excited state: \(n_3^1(q) \to 0.28 E_2 / q^4\).
- Normalized LO tail: \(3.71 E_2 / 16.52 E_2 = 0.224\) for ground state; \(0.28 E_2 / 1.270 E_2 = 0.219\) for excited state.

## Context

This work extends the universal contact relations derived by Tan (2008) for three-dimensional systems to two-dimensional bosonic trimers, and contrasts with the 3D case where the NLO term shows log-periodic oscillations due to the Efimov effect (Castin and Werner, 2011; Braaten et al., 2011). The results enable experimental determination of effective dimensionality in squeezed Bose gases by measuring the functional form of the momentum distribution tail, and provide a foundation for studying dimensional crossover in quasi-2D systems where transverse confinement must be explicitly included.
