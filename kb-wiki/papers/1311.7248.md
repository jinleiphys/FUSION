# QCD symmetries in eta and etaprime mesic nuclei

> We discuss the role of QCD symmetries and confinement in understanding eta and etaprime mesic nuclei. Eta and etaprime bound states in nuclei are sensitive to the flavour-singlet component in the meson. The bigger the singlet component, the more attraction and the greater the binding. Recent results on the etaprime mass in nuclei from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration are very similar to the prediction of the Quark Meson Coupling model. In the model eta-etaprime mixing induces a factor of two enhancement of the eta-nucleon scattering length relative to the prediction with a pure octet eta, with real part about 0.8 fm.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the flavour-singlet component in the eta and eta' mesons, arising from non-perturbative gluon dynamics (the QCD axial anomaly), is essential for understanding their binding in nuclei; the Quark Meson Coupling model predicts an eta-nucleon scattering length with real part about 0.8 fm when eta-eta' mixing is included, which is a factor of two larger than the prediction for a pure octet eta.

## Method

The authors use the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model, where meson wavefunctions are described by the MIT Bag model and in-medium mass modifications come from coupling light quarks and antiquarks to the scalar sigma mean-field in the nucleus in mean-field approximation. The in-medium meson mass is obtained by solving the s-wave self-energy equation at zero three-momentum, using the Ericson-Ericson-Lorentz-Lorenz double scattering correction. The model incorporates eta-eta' mixing with a density-independent mixing angle, and the strange-quark component does not couple to the sigma field.

## Key numbers

- Eta-nucleon scattering length real part: 0.85 fm for mixing angle -20 degrees (QMC model prediction)
- Eta-nucleon scattering length real part: 0.43 fm for pure octet eta (QMC model prediction)
- Eta' mass in medium at nuclear matter density: 921 MeV for mixing angle -20 degrees (QMC model prediction)
- CBELSA/TAPS measurement of eta' optical potential real part at nuclear matter density: -37 ± 10 (stat.) ± 10 (syst.) MeV
- CBELSA/TAPS measurement of eta' optical potential imaginary part at nuclear matter density: -10 ± 2.5 MeV
- Gluonic mass term in free space: 0.73 GeV^2
- Gluonic mass term in medium at nuclear matter density: 0.68 GeV^2 (derived from QMC predictions with mixing angle -20 degrees)
- Eta' width in nuclei at nuclear matter density: 20 ± 5.0 MeV (from transparency ratio measurements)
- COSY-11 upper bound on eta'-nucleon scattering length real part: |Re a_eta'N| < 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the Quark Meson Coupling model (reviewed by the authors in earlier work) to eta and eta' mesic nuclei, building on previous studies of medium modifications of hadron properties. The results are compared to chiral coupled channels models (which treat the eta as a pure octet and predict smaller scattering lengths around 0.2 fm) and to NJL and linear sigma model calculations (which predict larger eta' binding up to 150 MeV). The paper enables future experimental searches for eta bound states in Helium at COSY and eta' bound states in Carbon at ELSA and GSI/FAIR, and suggests extending the approach to charmed mesic nuclei.
