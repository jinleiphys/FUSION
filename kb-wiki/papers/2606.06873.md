# Measurement of energy-level splitting from Charge-Symmetry Breaking in $A$ = 4 mirror hypernuclei

> Breaking of fundamental symmetries is a ubiquitous phenomenon in physics, underlying the origin of mass and the emerging structure in the universe. The charge symmetry of $螞$ hyperon-nucleon interactions can be probed through the difference in the $螞$ binding energy ($B_螞$) between mirror hypernuclei. In this paper, the $B_螞$ of mirror hypernuclei with atomic mass number $A$ = 4, $\rm ^4_螞H$ and $\rm ^4_螞He$, are measured in Au+Au collisions at the center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 3 GeV with the STAR experiment at RHIC. For the ground states, we obtain $B_螞$($\rm ^4_螞H$) = 2.24 $\pm$ 0.02 (stat.) $\pm$ 0.04 (syst.) MeV and $B_螞$($\rm ^4_螞He$) = 2.39 $\pm$ 0.05 (stat.) $\pm$ 0.05 (syst.) MeV, yielding a charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) effect at the level of 0.15 $\pm$ 0.05 (stat.) $\pm$ 0.04 (syst.) MeV. In combination with previous measurements of $纬$-ray transitions from their $1^+$ excited states, the CSB in excited states is determined to be $-$0.17 $\pm$ 0.05 (stat.) $\pm$ 0.04 (syst.) MeV. These measurements provide a precise determination of CSB in the hypernuclear system, and establish that the $螞$ binding energy differences in ground and excited states are comparable in magnitude but opposite in sign, offering new insight to the CSB effect in $螞$-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim
The charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) effect in the \(A=4\) mirror hypernuclei \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{H}\) and \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{He}\) is measured with significantly improved precision, establishing that the \(\Lambda\) binding energy differences in the ground and excited states are comparable in magnitude but opposite in sign (\(\Delta B_\Lambda^4(1^+) \approx -\Delta B_\Lambda^4(0^+) < 0\)), consistent with a symmetric energy-level splitting from CSB. The abstract does not emphasize that the statistical significance of the nonzero CSB is at the 2.3\(\sigma\) level for the ground state and 2.6\(\sigma\) for the excited state.

## Method
The STAR experiment reconstructed \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{H}\) and \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{He}\) hypernuclei produced in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 3\) GeV via their two-body (\(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{H} \to {}^4\mathrm{He} + \pi^-\)) and three-body (\(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{He} \to {}^3\mathrm{He} + p + \pi^-\)) weak-decay channels. Particle identification used ionization energy loss in the Time Projection Chamber and time-of-flight measurements; decay topologies were reconstructed with a Kalman-filter-based package (KFParticle). Invariant masses were corrected for track momentum scale (99.8% factor from \(K_S^0\) calibration) and energy loss in detector material, with systematic uncertainties evaluated from variations in correction factors and selection criteria.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state \(\Lambda\) binding energy of \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{H}\): \(B_\Lambda = 2.24 \pm 0.02\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.04\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Ground-state \(\Lambda\) binding energy of \(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{He}\): \(B_\Lambda = 2.39 \pm 0.05\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.05\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Charge-symmetry breaking in ground states (\(0^+\)): \(\Delta B_\Lambda^4(0^+) = 0.15 \pm 0.05\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.04\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Charge-symmetry breaking in excited states (\(1^+\)): \(\Delta B_\Lambda^4(1^+) = -0.17 \pm 0.05\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.04\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Measured masses: \(m(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{H}) = 3922.36 \pm 0.02\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.04\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\); \(m(^4_\Lambda\mathrm{He}) = 3921.68 \pm 0.05\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.05\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\).

## Context
This work extends the pilot STAR measurement from 2018 (STAR 2022) by using a dataset of approximately 2 billion events (versus 300 million), reducing total uncertainty by about 63% and superseding earlier nuclear emulsion results from Juric (1973) and the A1 Collaboration at Mainz (2016). The results provide precise constraints for theoretical models of \(\Lambda N\) CSB, including \(ab\ initio\) no-core shell model calculations (Gazda 2015, 2016) and \(\Lambda\)-\(\Sigma\) mixing approaches (Nogga 2001, Haidenbauer 2007, Gal 2015), and enable tighter bounds on the \(\Lambda\)-neutron scattering length (Haidenbauer 2021) and on hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interactions relevant to the neutron-star hyperon puzzle.
