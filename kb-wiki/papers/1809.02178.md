# Conductivities of magnetic quark-gluon plasma at strong coupling

> In the presence of a strong magnetic field, the quark gluon plasma is magnetized, leading to anisotropic transport coefficients. In this work, we focus on the effect of magnetization on electric conductivity, ignoring the possible contribution from the axial anomaly. We generalize longitudinal and transverse conductivities to finite frequencies. For transverse conductivity, a separation of contribution from fluid velocity is needed. We study the dependence of the conductivities on the magnetic field and frequency using a holographic magnetic brane model. The longitudinal conductivity scales roughly linearly in the magnetic field, while the transverse conductivity is rather insensitive to the magnetic field. Furthermore, we find the conductivities can be significantly enhanced at large frequency. This can possibly extend the lifetime of the magnetic field, which is a key component of the chiral magnetic effect.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma with a strong magnetic field, the longitudinal electric conductivity scales roughly linearly with the magnetic field while the transverse conductivity is rather insensitive to it; however, this result explicitly ignores the possible contribution from the axial anomaly (Chern-Simons term), which is known to lead to negative magnetoresistance.

## Method

The authors use a holographic magnetic brane model, a solution to five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell theory with a negative cosmological constant, to compute the retarded current-current correlators. They derive Kubo formulas for both longitudinal and transverse conductivities that generalize to finite frequencies, solving the linearized perturbation equations numerically in the hydrodynamic regime and at arbitrary frequency. For the transverse conductivity, a careful subtraction of the fluid velocity contribution is performed to extract the correct correlator.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal conductivity \(\sigma_\parallel\) grows linearly with magnetic field \(B\) at large \(B\) (e.g., Fig. 3 shows \(\sigma_\parallel\) increasing from about 0.5 at \(B/T^2 \approx 0\) to about 2.5 at \(B/T^2 \approx 8\)).
- Transverse conductivity \(\sigma_\perp\) tends to a constant at large \(B\) (approximately 0.5 at \(B/T^2 \approx 8\) in Fig. 3).
- At small \(B\), \(\sigma_\parallel\) and \(\sigma_\perp\) are numerically consistent with each other (Fig. 3).
- At large frequency \(\omega\), both conductivities can be significantly enhanced; for example, at \(\omega/(2\pi T) \approx 0.57\) (corresponding to a magnetic field lifetime \(\tau \approx 1\) fm), the conductivities are enhanced by a factor of about 3 (Fig. 4).
- For a magnetic field lifetime \(\tau \approx 0.2\) fm, the enhancement factor for conductivity reaches about 10 (from Fig. 4).
- The large \(\omega\) limit of \(|\sigma_\perp|\) shows a non-monotonic dependence on \(B\), with a minimum at \(B/T^2 \sim O(1)\) (Fig. 6).
- The large \(\omega\) limit of \(\text{Arg}[\sigma_\perp]\) approaches \(-\pi/2\) numerically at large \(B\) (Fig. 5).

## Context

This work extends the magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) framework developed by Hernandez and Kovtun (2017) by providing explicit calculations of anisotropic electric conductivities in a strongly coupled plasma. It contrasts with weak-coupling calculations using the lowest Landau level approximation by Hattori et al. (2016) and beyond-lowest-Landau-level work by Fukushima et al. (2017), as well as with earlier strong-coupling calculations of longitudinal conductivity by Arciniega et al. (2013) and Patino et al. (2012). The results enable more realistic modeling of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, where the rapid decay of the magnetic field requires finite-frequency conductivities to evaluate the magnetic field lifetime.
