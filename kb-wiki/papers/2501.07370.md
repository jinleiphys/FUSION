# Novel way of evaluating $g_A$ quenching in $β^+$/EC decays : Introducing the Branching-Ratio Method (BRM)

> The novel Branching-Ratio Method (BRM) for the determination of effective value $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ of the weak axial coupling $g_{\rm A}$ for forbidden non-unique (FNU) $β^+$/electron-capture(EC) decays is introduced. The method offers new possibilities for testing the fitness of nuclear Hamiltonians in modeling the physics of complex $β^+$/EC decays. The constraint of simultaneously reproducing the branching to $β^+$ and EC transitions offers an additional constraint in tackling the problem of $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ determination. In the BRM the ambiguity in the choice of the values of $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ and s-NME (small relativistic vector nuclear matrix element) is lifted when constraints of the branching ratios of $β^+$ and EC decays are applied. As an example, in the present work we apply BRM to the case of second FNU $β^+$/EC decay of $^{59}$Ni. This decay is treated with three different nuclear shell-model (NSM) Hamiltonians demonstrating the effects of different nuclear-structure aspects in application of the BRM.

# Novel way of evaluating $g_A$ quenching in $\beta^+$/EC decays : Introducing the Branching-Ratio Method (BRM)

## Key claim

The Branching-Ratio Method (BRM) can determine the effective axial-vector coupling $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ for forbidden non-unique (FNU) $\beta^+$/electron-capture (EC) decays by using the relative branching ratio between the $\beta^+$ and EC sub-branches as an additional constraint, but the method's success depends on the quality of the nuclear Hamiltonian used, as demonstrated by the fact that not all shell-model Hamiltonians can simultaneously reproduce the total half-life and the relative branching ratio even when the small relativistic vector nuclear matrix element (sNME) is treated as a free parameter.

## Method

The authors computed the integrated shape functions for both the $\beta^+$ and EC branches of the second-forbidden non-unique ground-state-to-ground-state transition $^{59}{\rm Ni}(3/2^-) \to ^{59}{\rm Co}(7/2^-)$ using three different nuclear shell-model (NSM) Hamiltonians. For each Hamiltonian, they treated $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ as a free parameter and the sNME as an additional free parameter, then searched for pairs of values that simultaneously reproduce the experimental total branching ratio of the $\beta^+$/EC transition and the relative branching ratio ${\rm BR}_{\beta^+:EC}$ defined as the ratio of the $\beta^+$ branching to the EC branching. The sNME values obtained were compared to the conserved-vector-current (CVC) based reference value sNME$_{\rm CVC}$.

## Key numbers

* For the KB3GPN Hamiltonian with $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=0.6$, the sNME solution (1) for the $\beta^+$/EC branch is -0.07981, yielding a total branching ratio of 1.64e-6; the corresponding $\beta^+$ branch sNME(1) is -0.02426 with ${\rm BR}_{\beta^+}=9.52$e-6.
* For the KB3GPN Hamiltonian with $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=1.0$, the sNME(1) for $\beta^+$/EC is -0.09594 with total BR 1.87e-6, and the $\beta^+$ branch sNME(1) is -0.03317 with ${\rm BR}_{\beta^+}=2.91$e-6.
* The CVC-based reference sNME for the KB3GPN Hamiltonian is sNME$_{\rm CVC}=-0.115125671$.
* For the FPD6PN Hamiltonian with $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=0.6$, the sNME(1) for $\beta^+$/EC is -0.04350 with total BR 6.74e-8, and the $\beta^+$ branch sNME(1) is -0.07244 with ${\rm BR}_{\beta^+}=1.50$e-7.
* For the FPD6PN Hamiltonian with $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=1.0$, the sNME(1) for $\beta^+$/EC is -0.04058 with total BR 5.60e-8, and the $\beta^+$ branch sNME(1) is -0.07144 with ${\rm BR}_{\beta^+}=1.44$e-7.
* The CVC-based reference sNME for the FPD6PN Hamiltonian is sNME$_{\rm CVC}=0.0692797750$.

## Context

This work extends the study of $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ phenomenology from the well-studied $\beta^-$ forbidden non-unique decays, where the enhanced Spectral Shape Method (SSM) and Spectral Moment Method (SMM) have been applied by Kostensalo, Suhonen, and collaborators (2021-2024), into the previously unexplored domain of combined $\beta^+$/EC forbidden non-unique decays. The BRM enables $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ determination even for decays whose positron spectral shapes are insensitive to $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ and sNME values, which would remain intractable under the SSM alone; the method also provides a stringent test of nuclear Hamiltonians by requiring simultaneous reproduction of the total branching ratio, the relative $\beta^+$/EC branching, and potentially the positron spectral shape as a cross-check.
