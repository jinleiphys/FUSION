# Self-consistent tensor effects on nuclear matter system under relativistic Hartree-Fock approach

> With the relativistic representation of the nuclear tensor force that is included automatically by the Fock diagrams, we explored the self-consistent tensor effects on the properties of nuclear matter system. The analysis were performed within the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory. The tensor force is found to notably influence the saturation mechanism, the equation of state and the symmetry energy of nuclear matter, as well as the neutron star properties. Without introducing any additional free parameters, the DDRHF approach paves a natural way to reveal the tensor effects on the nuclear matter system.

## Key claim

The nuclear tensor force components, which are automatically included in the Fock diagrams of the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory without additional free parameters, notably influence the saturation mechanism, equation of state, symmetry energy, and neutron star properties of nuclear matter, with the effects becoming more pronounced at supranuclear densities.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory with the functionals PKA1, PKO1, PKO2, and PKO3 to study nuclear matter. They extracted the tensor force contributions from the Fock diagrams of the meson-nucleon couplings (sigma-S, omega-V, rho-V, rho-T, and pi-PV) using a recently proposed relativistic formalism. To isolate the tensor effects, they performed two self-consistent calculations for each functional: one with the full energy density functional (EDF) and one with the EDF that drops the tensor force components.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density rho_0 for PKA1: 0.160 fm^-3 with tensor, 0.148 fm^-3 without tensor (a reduction of about 8%).
* Binding energy per nucleon E/A for PKA1: -15.83 MeV with tensor, -14.18 MeV without tensor (a change of about 1.7 MeV).
* Incompressibility K for PKA1: 229.96 MeV with tensor, 203.56 MeV without tensor (an enhancement of about 26 MeV).
* Symmetry energy J at saturation for PKA1: 36.02 MeV with tensor, 35.95 MeV without tensor.
* Slope L of symmetry energy for PKA1: 103.50 MeV with tensor, 115.49 MeV without tensor.
* Curvature K_sym of symmetry energy for PKA1: 212.90 MeV with tensor, 317.31 MeV without tensor.
* At supranuclear densities, the tensor force contributes about 40 MeV to the energy functional, making the equation of state softer.
* The tensor contributions to the symmetry energy at high density counteract about 20% of the contributions from other channels.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic representation of nuclear tensor force components proposed by Jiang et al. (2014) to study nuclear matter and neutron star properties. It contrasts with earlier relativistic mean field (RMF) approaches (Walecka, 1974; Serot, 1986; Reinhard, 1989; Ring, 1996; Bender, 2003; Meng, 2006; Niksic, 2011) that miss the tensor degrees of freedom from pi and rho fields in the Hartree limit. The results enable a self-consistent understanding of tensor effects on the symmetry energy at supranuclear densities, which had been studied previously by Baoan (2010) and Vidana (2011), and suggest that tensor effects make the direct Urca process less favorable in neutron stars, in better agreement with observational soft X-ray data.
