# Calculating the critical exponents of the chiral phase transition

> We calculate the critical exponents of the chiral phase transition at nonzero temperature using the thermal and chiral susceptibilities. We show that within a class of confining Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) models the transition is mean field, and that an accurate determination of the critical exponents requires extremely small values of the current-quark mass, several order of magnitude smaller than realistic up- and down-quark masses. In general, rainbow truncation models of QCD exhibit mean field exponents as a result of the gap equation's fermion substructure.

## Key claim

Within a class of confining Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) models, the chiral phase transition at nonzero temperature is mean field, but an accurate determination of the critical exponents requires extremely small current-quark masses, several orders of magnitude smaller than realistic up- and down-quark masses, and the scaling relations are not valid until the reduced mass satisfies \(\log_{10}(h/h_u) < -7\) for Model B and \(\log_{10}(h/h_u) < -5\) for Model C.

## Method

The author calculated the chiral and thermal susceptibilities \(\chi_h(t,h)\) and \(\chi_t(t,h)\) from the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) in the rainbow truncation (leading term in a \(1/N_c\)-expansion of the vertex) for three confining DSE models (A, B, C) with different ultraviolet behaviour. The critical exponents \(\beta\) and \(\delta\) were extracted from the scaling of the pseudocritical points and the peak susceptibilities with reduced mass \(h = m/T\), using local critical exponents \(z_i(h) = -h \, \partial \ln \chi_i^{\rm pc} / \partial h\) and extrapolating to \(h \to 0^+\). The order parameter used was the scalar function \(B(p^2=0,\omega_0)\) and its ratio with \(C(p^2=0,\omega_0)\).

## Key numbers

- Model A: critical temperature \(T_c = 169\) MeV; critical exponents \(z_h = 0.666\) (mean field value 2/3) and \(z_t = 0.335\) (mean field value 1/3).
- Model B: critical temperature \(T_c = 174\) MeV; critical exponents \(z_h = 0.67 \pm 0.01\) and \(z_t = 0.33 \pm 0.02\).
- Model C: critical temperature \(T_c = 120\) MeV; critical exponents \(z_h = 0.667 \pm 0.001\) and \(z_t = 0.333 \pm 0.001\).
- For Model B, the scaling relations are not valid until \(\log_{10} (h/h_u) < -7\), where \(h_u = m_R/T_c\) corresponds to the current-quark mass giving \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV.
- For Model C, the scaling relations are only valid for \(\log_{10} (h/h_u) < -5\).
- The mass-scale parameter in Model B is \(m_t = 0.69\) GeV = \(1/0.29\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral symmetry restoration in DSE models (Maris, Roberts, and collaborators, late 1990s) and directly addresses the conjecture by Pisarski (1984) that the transition is in the \(O(4)\) universality class (with exponents \(\beta^H = 0.38 \pm 0.01\), \(\delta^H = 4.82 \pm 0.05\)). The author shows that rainbow truncation DSE models yield mean field exponents, contrary to the \(O(4)\) expectation, and argues that the compositeness of mesons (as in the Gross-Neveu model discussed by Kogut and Stephanov) may affect the universality class. The results imply that lattice QCD calculations of the critical exponents will be extremely difficult because they require current-quark masses far smaller than the physical up and down masses.
