# Two-Photon-Exchange Effects and $Δ(1232)$ Deformation

> The two-photon-exchange (TPE) contribution in $ep\rightarrow epπ^0$ with $W=M_Δ$ and small $Q^2$ is calculated and its corrections to the ratios of electromagnetic transition form factors $R_{EM} = E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)} $ and $R_{SM} = S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}$, are analysed. A simple hadronic model is used to estimate the TPE amplitude. Two phenomenological models, MAID2007 and SAID, are used to approximate the full $ep\rightarrow epπ^0$ cross sections which contain both the TPE and the one-photon-exchange (OPE) contributions. The genuine the OPE amplitude is then extracted from an integral equation by iteration. We find that the TPE contribution is not sensitive to whether MAID or SAID is used as input in the region with $Q^2<2$ GeV$^2$. It gives small correction to $R_{EM}$ while for $R_{SM}$, the correction is about -10\% at small $ε$ and about $1\%$ at large $ε$ for $Q^2\approx2.5$ GeV$^2$. The large correction from TPE at small $ε$ must be included in the analysis to get a reliable extraction of $R_{SM}$.

# Two-Photon-Exchange Effects and Δ(1232) Deformation

## Key claim
The two-photon-exchange (TPE) contribution to the reaction \(ep\rightarrow ep\pi^0\) at the Δ(1232) resonance peak (\(W=M_\Delta\)) and low momentum transfer (\(Q^2<2\) GeV\(^2\)) gives a small correction to the ratio \(R_{EM}=E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\), but a correction of about -10% at small \(\epsilon\) and about 1% at large \(\epsilon\) for \(R_{SM}=S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) at \(Q^2\approx2.5\) GeV\(^2\), which must be included for a reliable extraction of \(R_{SM}\). The TPE contribution is not sensitive to whether the MAID2007 or SAID phenomenological model is used as input in the region \(Q^2<2\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method
The TPE amplitude was estimated using a simple hadronic model with elastic intermediate states, including the Born term in the one-photon-exchange (OPE) amplitude, which was omitted in prior work by Pascalutsa (2006). The full \(ep\rightarrow ep\pi^0\) cross sections were approximated using two phenomenological models, MAID2007 and SAID, which contain both TPE and OPE contributions. The genuine OPE amplitude was then extracted from an integral equation by iteration, fixing the phases of the multipoles \(E_{1+}\), \(M_{1+}\), and \(S_{1+}\) to the input model as required by unitarity, and minimizing a \(\chi^2\) fit to determine their absolute values.

## Key numbers
- TPE correction to \(R_{SM}\) is about -10% at small \(\epsilon\) and about 1% at large \(\epsilon\) for \(Q^2\approx2.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The TPE contribution is not sensitive to the input model (MAID2007 or SAID) in the region \(Q^2<2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The TPE corrections to \(M_{1+}\) are very small (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
- The TPE corrections to \(R_{EM}\) are much smaller than those to \(R_{SM}\) (no explicit numerical value given in the text).

## Context
This work extends the study of TPE effects from elastic \(ep\) scattering, where they are known to be non-negligible for extracting the nucleon form factor ratio \(G_E/G_M\) (Arrington, 2011), to the inelastic channel of Δ(1232) excitation at low \(Q^2\). It contrasts with the partonic approach of Pascalutsa (2006), which considered only the Δ pole term and omitted the Born term in the OPE amplitude, leading to different TPE corrections for \(R_{SM}\) in the \(Q^2\sim 2-4\) GeV\(^2\) region. The results enable more reliable extraction of the electromagnetic transition form factors \(R_{EM}\) and \(R_{SM}\) from precise experimental cross sections, which are now measured to near 1% precision.
