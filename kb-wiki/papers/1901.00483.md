# Lattice QCD and Three-particle Decays of Resonances

> Most strong-interaction resonances have decay channels involving three or more particles, including many of the recently discovered $X$, $Y$ and $Z$ resonances. In order to study such resonances from first principles using lattice QCD, one must understand finite-volume effects for three particles in the cubic box used in calculations. Here we review efforts to develop a three-particle quantization condition that relates finite-volume energies to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes. We describe in detail the three approaches that have been followed, and present new results on the relationship between the corresponding results. We show examples of the numerical implementation of all three approaches and point out the important issues that remain to be resolved.

## Key claim

The paper reviews three distinct theoretical approaches to deriving a three-particle quantization condition that relates finite-volume energy levels from lattice QCD to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes, but notes that all derivations assume negligible exponentially-suppressed volume dependence and, for the alternative approaches, restrict to purely s-wave dimer interactions.

## Method

The authors present a diagrammatic, all-orders analysis in a generic relativistic quantum field theory (RFT approach) for three identical spinless particles with a Z₂ symmetry forbidding 2↔3 transitions, then compare this to two alternative approaches: a non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) method and a finite-volume unitarity (FVU) approach. The derivation proceeds by first establishing the two-particle quantization condition through analysis of finite-volume correlators and Bethe-Salpeter kernels, then extending to three particles by showing that the sum-integral difference for loops can project kernels onto their on-shell values up to exponentially-suppressed corrections.

## Key numbers

- The threshold expansion for the lowest two-particle energy at zero total momentum gives E₀(L) = 2m + 4πa/(m L³) + O(1/L⁴), where a is the scattering length
- The two-particle unitarity relation in 1+1 dimensions yields M₂ = 1/(K₂⁻¹ - i/(8q*E)), with q* the center-of-mass momentum
- The phase-space factor for two particles is ρ(s) = q*/(16π√s) for s ≥ 4m², and analytically continues to ρ(s) = i|q*|/(16π√s) below threshold
- Lattice calculations discussed use mπ ≳ 230 MeV (heavier-than-physical quarks) and require MπL ≳ 4 for finite-volume uncertainties to become subleading

## Context

This review extends the seminal two-particle quantization condition developed by Lüscher (1990) to three-particle systems, motivated by the need to study resonances such as the a₁(1260) decaying to three pions, the Roper resonance N(1440), and the recently discovered X, Y, and Z resonances that have decay channels involving three or more particles. The work enables first-principles lattice QCD calculations of three-particle resonances, building on prior numerical spectra from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (2013) and Bulava and Morningstar (2016), while noting that the HALQCD collaboration's approach (2011) remains restricted to the nonrelativistic domain and is not directly applicable to most QCD resonances of interest.
