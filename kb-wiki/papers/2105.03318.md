# Fluctuations in the pasta phase

> Baryonic matter close to the saturation density is very likely to present complex inhomogeneous structures collectively known under the name of pasta phase. At finite temperature, the different geometric structures are expected to coexist, with potential consequences on the neutron star crust conductivity and neutrino transport in supernova matter. In the framework of a statistical multi-component approach, we calculate the composition of matter in the pasta phase considering density, proton fraction, and geometry fluctuations. Using a realistic energy functional from relativistic mean field theory and a temperature and isospin dependent surface tension fitted from Thomas-Fermi calculations, we show that different geometries can coexist in a large fraction of the pasta phase, down to temperatures of the order of the crystallization temperature of the neutron star crust. Quantitative estimates of the charge fluctuations are given.

## Key claim

Using a statistical multi-component approach with a realistic relativistic mean-field energy functional, the authors show that different pasta geometries (droplets, rods, slabs) can coexist over a large fraction of the pasta phase down to temperatures near the crystallization temperature of the neutron star crust, contrary to the single-nucleus approximation which assumes only one geometry at each thermodynamic condition.

## Method

The authors employed a compressible liquid-drop model within a relativistic mean-field framework using the IUFSU parametrization, with a temperature- and isospin-dependent surface tension fitted from Thomas-Fermi calculations. They extended the single-nucleus approximation by constructing a grand-canonical partition function that allows fluctuations in cluster density, proton fraction, and geometry across different Wigner-Seitz cells, while keeping the nucleon gas and electron densities fixed from the equilibrium solution. The probability of each pasta structure was then computed from the resulting cluster number densities.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(n_{sat} \approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Crystallization temperature in the pasta region: \(T_m \approx 700\) KeV (approximately 1 MeV)
- Probabilities for pasta structures were computed at temperatures T = 1, 3, and 5 MeV for proton fractions \(Y_p = 0.1\) and 0.3
- Proton number variance \(\Delta Z\) was calculated at T = 1 MeV for \(Y_p = 0.1\) and 0.3, shown as an increasing function of baryonic density and temperature

## Context

This work extends the statistical multi-component approach previously applied to nuclear clusters by Gulminelli (2015) and Carreau (2020) to the pasta phase, using a realistic microscopic energy functional rather than simplified models. The results confirm earlier suggestions by Nandi (2018) and Schneider (2016) that impurities and shape coexistence may characterize the catalyzed neutron star crust, and provide the necessary input for future quantitative calculations of transport coefficients (electrical and thermal conductivity) in the pasta phase, which affect neutrino opacity and magneto-thermal evolution of compact stars.
