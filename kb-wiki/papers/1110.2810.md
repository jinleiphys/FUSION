# Hadron production in pA collisions at the LHC from the Color Glass Condensate

> We investigate the contribution of inelastic and elastic processes to single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton (deuteron)-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC. Using the hybrid formulation which includes both elastic and inelastic contributions, supplemented with the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, we get a good description of RHIC data. It is shown that inclusion of the inelastic terms makes the transverse momentum dependence of the production cross section steeper in the mid-rapidity region but does not affect the cross section in the very forward region. The inelastic processes also lead to a sharper increase of the nuclear modification factor R_{pA} with increasing p_T. We also make predictions for the nuclear modification factor in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC (\sqrt{s}=4.4 and 8.8 TeV) at various rapidities using the Color Glass Condensate framework.

## Key claim
Including inelastic contributions to the single inclusive hadron production cross section makes the transverse momentum dependence steeper at mid-rapidity and causes the nuclear modification factor R_{pA} to increase faster with p_T, but these inelastic terms do not affect the cross section in the very forward region.

## Method
The authors used the hybrid formulation of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, which treats the projectile proton via collinear factorization (with NLO MSTW 2008 PDFs and NLO KKP fragmentation functions) and the target via the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation. They numerically evaluated the cross section including both elastic and inelastic contributions, using the McLerran-Venugopalan type initial condition for the rcBK dipole amplitude with γ=1.119, and compared results to the DHJ dipole parametrization. The free parameter was the initial saturation scale Q_{0s} at x_0=0.01, fixed by fits to RHIC data.

## Key numbers
- Initial saturation scale for proton: Q_{0s}^2 = 0.168 GeV^2 (preferred from HERA data, χ^2/d.o.f.=1.104)
- Initial saturation scale for gold nucleus in dAu collisions: Q_{0s}^2 = 0.5 to 0.67 GeV^2 (consistent with RHIC data)
- LHC collision energies considered: √s = 4.4 TeV and 8.8 TeV
- Number of binary collisions for pA: N_coll = 6.5 at √s=4.4 TeV, N_coll = 7.4 at √s=8.8 TeV
- Strong coupling in inelastic term: α_s = 0.1 (used for most results), with variations shown for α_s = 0.05, 0.15
- DHJ dipole model parameters: λ=0.3, x_{s0}=10^{-4}, d=1.2, γ_s=0.628

## Context
This work extends the CGC-based hybrid formulation of Dumitru, Hayashigaki, and Jalilian-Marian (2006) by including the inelastic contributions derived by Altinoluk and Kovner (2011), which were previously omitted as higher-order corrections. The authors show that these inelastic terms improve the description of RHIC data at mid-rapidity (η=1) for both pp and dAu collisions. The predictions for R_{pA} at the LHC forward rapidities provide a test of gluon saturation dynamics that can distinguish between different initial saturation scales and evolution models, with uncertainties minimized at very forward rapidities.
