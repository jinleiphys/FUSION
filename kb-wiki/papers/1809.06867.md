# A new parametrization for the scalar pion form factors

> We derive a new parametrization for the scalar pion form factors that allows us to analyze data over a large energy range via the inclusion of resonances, and at the same time to ensure consistency with the high-accuracy dispersive representations available at low energies. As an application the formalism is used to extract resonance properties of excited scalar mesons from data for $\bar B^0_s\to J/ψππ$. In particular we find for the pole positions of $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(2020)$ $1465\pm 18 - i (50\pm 9)\,\text{MeV}$ and $1910\pm 50 - i(199\pm 40)\,\text{MeV}$, respectively. In addition, from their residues we calculate the respective branching ratios into $ππ$ to be $(58\pm31)\%$ and $(1.3\pm1.8)\%$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new parametrization for scalar pion form factors that combines a dispersive low-energy description (capturing the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$) with explicit resonance exchange at higher energies, ensuring unitarity and analyticity; the formalism is applied to extract pole parameters of excited scalar mesons from LHCb data on $\bar B^0_s\to J/\psi\pi\pi$, but the extracted branching ratios into $\pi\pi$ carry large uncertainties (e.g., $(58\pm31)\%$ for $f_0(1500)$).

## Method

The authors derive a coupled-channel parametrization for the scalar isoscalar pion and kaon form factors using a Bethe-Salpeter equation with a two-potential formalism: a low-energy part $T_0$ (fixed by dispersive $\pi\pi$--$K\bar K$ input) and a resonance part $V_R$ for states above $1\,\text{GeV}$. The form factors are expressed via an Omnès matrix $\Omega$ (built from $T_0$) and a $P$-vector source term $M$, with the full form factor given by $\Gamma^s_i = \Omega_{im}[1-V_R\Sigma]^{-1}_{mn} M_n$, where $\Sigma$ is a dressed loop operator. The model includes three channels ($\pi\pi$, $K\bar K$, and an effective $4\pi$ channel modeled as either $\sigma\sigma$ or $\rho\rho$) and is fitted to LHCb angular moments for $\bar B^0_s\to J/\psi\pi\pi$ and $\bar B^0_s\to J/\psi K\bar K$ up to about $2\,\text{GeV}$.

## Key numbers

* Pole position of $f_0(1500)$: $1465\pm 18 - i(50\pm 9)\,\text{MeV}$
* Pole position of $f_0(2020)$: $1910\pm 50 - i(199\pm 40)\,\text{MeV}$
* Branching ratio of $f_0(1500)$ into $\pi\pi$: $(58\pm31)\%$
* Branching ratio of $f_0(2020)$ into $\pi\pi$: $(1.3\pm1.8)\%$
* Reduced $\chi^2$ values for fits: best fit (Fit 3 with $\rho\rho$ channel) gives $\chi^2/\text{ndf}=335.4/(384-35-1)=0.96$; with $\sigma\sigma$ channel gives $366.9/(384-35-1)=1.05$
* Branching ratios used for normalization: $\mathcal{B}(\bar B^0_s\to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-) = (2.09\pm0.23)\times10^{-4}$ and $\mathcal{B}(\bar B^0_s\to J/\psi K^+K^-) = (7.9\pm0.7)\times10^{-4}$
* $\bar B^0_s$ lifetime: $\tau_{\bar B^0_s} = (1.509\pm0.004)\times10^{-12}\,\text{s}$

## Context

This work extends the dispersive coupled-channel analysis of Daub et al. (2015), which was limited to energies below $1.05\,\text{GeV}$, by adding a resonance-exchange mechanism that preserves unitarity and matches smoothly onto the low-energy amplitudes. The formalism generalizes the approach of H_1 (for the pion vector form factor) to the scalar isoscalar channel, where the strong $\pi\pi$--$K\bar K$ coupling via the $f_0(980)$ necessitates a coupled-channel treatment. The extracted pole parameters for $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(2020)$ provide input for glueball searches in the scalar sector, where the lightest $0^{++}$ glueball is predicted between $1600$ and $1700\,\text{MeV}$ (Bali 1993, Morningstar 1997). Future exclusive data on $4\pi$ final states would allow refinement of the effective third channel description.
