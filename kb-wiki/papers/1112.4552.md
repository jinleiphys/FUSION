# Single Spin Asymmetry through QCD Instantons

> We revisit the effects of QCD instantons in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS). We show that large single spin asymmetry (SSA) effects can be induced in longitudinally and transversely polarized proton targets. The results are in agreement with most of the reported data for pion and kaon production. The same effects are found to be important in polarized proton on proton scattering for both charged and chargeless pion productions. The results agree with the reported data in a wide range of \sqrt{s} = 19.4-200 GeV. We predict the SSA for π^\pm production in p_\uparrow p in the collider range of \sqrt{s} = 62.4-500 GeV. The backward π^{\pm} and π^0 productions for the SSA in p_\uparrow p collisions are predicted to coincide at large \sqrt{s}.

## Key claim

QCD instantons can generate large single spin asymmetry (SSA) effects in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and polarized proton-proton collisions, with the results depending on the instanton size and quark zero-mode virtuality being fixed to their QCD vacuum values; the agreement with data is achieved under the assumption that the longitudinal and transverse spin-polarized quark distributions are equal.

## Method

The authors compute the T-odd contribution to the SIDIS cross section from a single instanton insertion in the quark-photon vertex, using the instanton parameters (size ρ ≈ 1/3 fm and mean quark zero-mode virtuality λ̄ ≈ 1/(0.2 GeV)³) from the QCD instanton vacuum. They extend the same instanton mechanism to polarized proton-proton collisions by considering one instanton insertion tagged to a single gluon exchange between valence quarks, deriving analytic expressions for the Sivers-type spin asymmetry for π⁺, π⁻, and π⁰ production. The partonic results are converted to hadronic level using mean kinematics and empirical parametrizations of spin structure functions and parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- Instanton size set to ρ ≈ 1/3 fm (1.7/GeV) for SIDIS analysis
- Mean quark zero-mode virtuality λ̄ ≈ 1/(0.2 GeV)³, giving 16π²ρ²/N_cλ ≈ 1.22 GeV
- For CLAS comparison: Q² ≈ (0.9–5.4) GeV², x ≈ (0.12–0.48), z ≈ (0.4–0.7), K⊥ ≈ (0–1.12) GeV, with mean values ⟨z⟩ ≈ 0.55 and ⟨K⊥⟩ ≈ 0.56 GeV
- Spin structure function ratio g₁ᵖ/F₁ᵖ ≈ 0.354 at (x, Q²) ≈ (0.274, 3.35 GeV²)
- For HERMES transverse: ⟨x⟩=0.09, ⟨y⟩=0.54, ⟨z⟩=0.36, ⟨K⊥⟩=0.41 GeV, ⟨Q²⟩=2.41 GeV²
- For p↑p collisions: results agree with data for √s = 19.4–200 GeV
- Instanton size varied: ρ ≈ 0.72 fm at √s = 19.4 GeV, ρ ≈ 0.43 fm at √s = 62.4 GeV, ρ ≈ 0.65 fm at √s = 200 GeV
- Predictions made for SSA at √s = 62.4–500 GeV
- For √s > 100 GeV, deviation of asymptotic formulas from non-asymptotic values is less than 1%

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based approach to SSA originally developed by Kochelev (2000), Dorokhov (2009), and Ostrovsky (2005) for SIDIS, correcting and improving the earlier analysis of Ostrovsky (2005). The instanton mechanism provides a non-perturbative QCD origin for T-odd amplitudes, complementing the Sivers effect (Sivers, 1990, 1991) and Collins effect (Collins, 1993, 1994) which are induced in initial-state or fragmentation functions. The results enable predictions for future STAR measurements at √s = 500 GeV and establish that all backward pion productions (π⁺, π⁻, π⁰) in p↑p collisions coincide at large √s.
