# Finite-temperature equations of state of compact stars with hyperons: three-dimensional tables

> We construct tables of finite temperature equation of state (EoS) of hypernuclear matter in the range of densities, temperatures, and electron fractions that are needed for numerical simulations of supernovas, proto-neutron stars, and binary neutron star mergers and cast them in the format of {\sc CompOSE} database. The tables are extracted from a model that is based on covariant density functional (CDF) theory that includes the full $J^P=1/2^+$ baryon octet in a manner that is consistent with the current astrophysical and nuclear constraints. We employ a parameterization with three different values of the slope of the symmetry energy $L_{\rm sym}=30$, 50 and 70 MeV and fixed skewness $Q_{\rm sat}= 400$ MeV for above saturation matter. A model for the EoS of inhomogeneous matter is matched at sub-saturation density to the high-density hypernuclear EoS. We discuss the generic features of the resulting EoS and the composition of matter as a function of density, temperature, and electron fraction. The nuclear characteristics and strangeness fraction of these models are compared to the alternatives from the literature. The integral properties of static and rapidly rotating compact stars in the limit of zero temperature are discussed and confronted with the multimessenger astrophysical constraints.

## Key claim

The paper constructs finite-temperature equations of state for hypernuclear matter in three-dimensional tables (density, temperature, electron fraction) suitable for astrophysical simulations, but the tables are limited to three dimensions by excluding muons and neutrinos, and the matching to low-density inhomogeneous matter at 0.04 fm\(^{-3}\) introduces small discontinuities in hyperon fractions that the authors acknowledge will be improved in future versions.

## Method

The authors employ covariant density functional (CDF) theory including the full \(J^P = 1/2^+\) baryon octet (neutrons, protons, \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma^{0,\pm}\), \(\Xi^{0,-}\)) with density-dependent couplings, using three values of the symmetry energy slope \(L_{\rm sym} = 30, 50, 70\) MeV and fixed skewness \(Q_{\rm sat} = 400\) MeV. They solve the mean-field equations for meson fields, scalar densities, and baryon densities self-consistently at fixed temperature, baryon density, and electron fraction, then match the high-density homogeneous hypernuclear EoS to the low-density inhomogeneous HS(DD2) EoS from Hempel (2010) at a matching density of \(n_B = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

* Saturation density \(n_{\rm sat} = 0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy per particle \(E_B = -16.14\) MeV, compressibility \(K = 251\) MeV, and effective nucleon mass \(m^*_{n,p}/m_N = 0.57\) for all DDLS models.
* Symmetry energy values: \(E_{\rm sym} = 30.1\) MeV for \(L_{\rm sym} = 30\) MeV, \(E_{\rm sym} = 32.2\) MeV for \(L_{\rm sym} = 50\) MeV, and \(E_{\rm sym} = 34.0\) MeV for \(L_{\rm sym} = 70\) MeV.
* Hyperonic potentials in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: \(U_\Lambda (n_{\rm sat}) = -30\) MeV, \(U_\Xi (n_{\rm sat}) = -14\) MeV, and \(U_\Sigma (n_{\rm sat}) = +30\) MeV.
* Matching density between high-density homogeneous and low-density inhomogeneous EoS: \(n_B = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* Temperature range covered: \(0.1 \lesssim T \lesssim 100\) MeV; baryon density range: \(10^{-12} \lesssim n_B \lesssim 1\) fm\(^{-3}\); electron fraction range: \(0.01 \lesssim Y_e \lesssim 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature CDF models for hypernuclear matter from Colucci (2013), van Dalen (2014), Marques (2017), Fortin (2018), Raduta (2020), Sedrakian (2021, 2022), and Kochankovski (2022) by using new parametrizations from Li (2023) that allow variation of the symmetry energy slope and skewness, and by producing tables in the CompOSE database format. The tables enable numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae, proto-neutron stars, and binary neutron star mergers that require general-purpose EoS covering a wide range of temperatures, densities, and electron fractions, and the authors note that future work should include hyperons in the low-density matching and add muons as a fourth dimension.
