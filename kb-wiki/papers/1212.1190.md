# In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group with Chiral Two- Plus Three-Nucleon Interactions

> We use the recently proposed In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IM-SRG) to carry out a systematic study of closed-shell nuclei up to $\nuc{Ni}{56}$, based on chiral two- plus three-nucleon interactions. We analyze the capabilities of the IM-SRG by comparing our results for the ground-state energy to Coupled Cluster calculations, as well as to quasi-exact results from the Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model. Using chiral two- plus three-nucleon Hamiltonians whose resolution scales are lowered by free-space SRG evolution, we obtain good agreement with experimental binding energies in $\nuc{He}{4}$ and the closed-shell oxygen isotopes, while the calcium and nickel isotopes are somewhat overbound.

## Key claim
The In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IM-SRG) with chiral two- plus three-nucleon interactions yields good agreement with experimental binding energies for helium-4 and closed-shell oxygen isotopes, but systematically overbinds calcium and nickel isotopes when using Hamiltonians whose resolution scales are lowered by free-space SRG evolution.

## Method
The authors start from chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, evolve them to lower resolution scales via free-space Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution, then solve Hartree-Fock equations to obtain reference states. The Hamiltonian is normal-ordered with respect to the Hartree-Fock reference, and the IM-SRG flow equations are integrated using the White generator in a truncation that retains zero-, one-, and two-body operators (IM-SRG(2)), with the residual three-body term neglected. Ground-state energies are computed for closed-shell nuclei up to nickel-56 and compared to Coupled Cluster and Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model results.

## Context
This work extends the recently proposed IM-SRG method (Tsukiyama 2011, Tsukiyama 2012) to include chiral three-nucleon interactions in a systematic study of closed-shell nuclei up to nickel-56. The results are benchmarked against Coupled Cluster calculations at various truncation levels and against quasi-exact Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model results (Roth 2007, Roth 2009). The IM-SRG approach enables ab initio calculations of medium-mass nuclei with three-nucleon forces, where the normal-ordered two-body approximation has been shown to be very good (Roth 2012, Binder 2012, Hagen 2007). The method's size-extensivity means errors from truncation scale linearly with mass number A, allowing quality assessment based on comparisons with exact methods in light nuclei.
