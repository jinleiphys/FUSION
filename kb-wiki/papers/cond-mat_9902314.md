# Correlations of Eigenvectors for Non-Hermitian Random-Matrix Models

> We establish a general relation between the diagonal correlator of eigenvectors and the spectral Green's function for non-hermitian random-matrix models in the large-N limit. We apply this result to a number of non-hermitian random-matrix models and show that the outcome is in good agreement with numerical results.

## Key claim

The paper proves that, in the large-\(N\) limit, the diagonal correlator of left and right eigenvectors for non-Hermitian random-matrix models equals the product of the off-diagonal elements of the spectral Green’s function, specifically \(O(z) = -\frac{N}{\pi} \mathcal{G}_{q\bar{q}} \mathcal{G}_{\bar{q}q}\). This relation holds for any non-Hermitian ensemble where the factorization theorem applies, and it generalizes earlier results by Chalker and Mehlig (1998) that were limited to Ginibre’s ensemble.

## Method

The authors derive an exact representation for the diagonal eigenvector correlator \(O(z)\) using a limiting procedure involving traces of resolvent-like operators, then apply the large-\(N\) factorization to split the average into a product of two one-point Green’s functions. They compute the off-diagonal Green’s functions \(\mathcal{G}_{q\bar{q}}\) and \(\mathcal{G}_{\bar{q}q}\) via the generalized Blue’s function technique for several non-Hermitian random-matrix models: Ginibre’s ensemble, the elliptic ensemble, a “Ginibre plus deterministic two-level” model, and the Haake–Sommers–Weidenmüller model for open chaotic scattering. Numerical simulations for matrices of size \(N=20\) to \(N=120\) are compared to the analytical predictions.

## Key numbers

- For Ginibre’s ensemble: \(O(z) = \frac{N}{\pi}(1 - |z|^2)\) inside the unit disc, matching Chalker and Mehlig (1998).
- For the elliptic ensemble with parameter \(\tau\): \(O(z) = \frac{N}{\pi} \frac{1}{(1-\tau^2)^2} \left[(1-\tau^2)^2 - (1+\tau^2)|z|^2 + 2\tau \operatorname{Re} z^2\right]\); numerical results shown for \(\tau = 0.5\) and \(\tau = 0.7\) with matrices of size \(N=20, 40, 100\).
- For the “Ginibre plus deterministic” model with parameter \(a\): \(O(z) = -\frac{N}{\pi} \left[ |z|^2 + a^2 - \frac12(1 + \sqrt{1 + 4a^2(z+\bar{z})^2}) \right]\); critical splitting occurs at \(a=1\); numerical results for \(a=0.5, 1, 2\) with \(N=60\).
- For the open chaotic scattering model with \(m = M/N = 0.25\): the critical coupling is \(g_{\text{crit}} = 4.44\); numerical results for \(N=60\) and \(N=120\) at various \(g\) values.

## Context

This work extends the analytical results of Chalker and Mehlig (1998), who derived exact expressions for eigenvector correlators only for Ginibre’s ensemble, to a broad class of non-Hermitian random-matrix models. It also builds on the generalized Blue’s function formalism developed by Janik, Nowak, Papp, and Zahed (1997) and on the hermitization method of Feinberg and Zee (1997). The results enable the study of eigenvector reorganization accompanying structural changes in the complex spectrum, such as the splitting of eigenvalue domains in open chaotic scattering (Haake, Sommers, and coworkers, 1992) and in the “Ginibre plus deterministic” model. The relation provides a practical tool for computing the diagonal eigenvector correlator without requiring microscopic-level calculations, which are technically involved and limited to rotationally symmetric spectra.
