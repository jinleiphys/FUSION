# The continuum description with pseudo-state wave functions

> Benchmark calculations are performed aiming to test the use of two different pseudo-state bases on the the Multiple Scattering expansion of the total Transition amplitude (MST) scattering framework. Calculated differential cross sections for p-6He inelastic scattering at 717 MeV/u show a good agreement between the observables calculated in the two bases. This result gives extra confidence on the pseudo-state representation of continuum states to describe inelastic/breakup scattering.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two different pseudo-state bases (Gauss-Laguerre and Transformed Harmonic Oscillator) produce essentially the same inelastic differential cross sections for p-⁶He scattering at 717 MeV/u, provided enough states are included, supporting the reliability of the pseudo-state method for describing continuum states in breakup reactions.

## Method

The authors performed benchmark calculations within the Multiple Scattering expansion of the total Transition amplitude (MST) framework, using the single scattering approximation (SSA). They represented the ⁶He continuum states using two different square-integrable pseudo-state bases: a Gauss-Laguerre (GL) basis and a Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis, both expanded in Hyperspherical Harmonics. The three-body Hamiltonian included the Gogny-Pires-Tourreil n-n potential, a n-⁴He potential from Bang (1979), and an effective three-body potential tuned to reproduce the experimental ²He separation energy and the 2⁺₁ resonance energy. The calculated angular differential cross sections for p-⁶He inelastic scattering at 717 MeV/u were compared between the two bases for final states with Jπ = 0⁺, 1⁻, 1⁺, and 2⁺.

## Key numbers

- The ground state energy after diagonalization was -0.9781 MeV for the GL basis and -0.9549 MeV for the THO basis.
- The three-body force parameters were V₀³ᴮ = -2.4 MeV for J=0 and VJ³ᴮ = -0.85 MeV for J>0, with maximum hyperangular momentum Kmax = 20.
- The GL basis used N=20 and range parameter ρ₀ = 0.25 fm, extending to about 20 fm in hyperradius; the THO basis used N=4.
- The number of pseudo-states in the GL (THO) basis was: 31 (30) for 0⁺, 63 (86) for 1⁻, 53 (49) for 1⁺, and 79 (81) for 2⁺.
- Only eigenstates below 10 MeV excitation energy were included in the summed cross sections.
- The calculations considered forward angles θc.m. < 30°, where the SSA is expected to work well.

## Context

This work extends the MST scattering framework previously applied to elastic scattering by Crespo (2001, 2006) and inelastic scattering by Crespo (2002, 2006b) by testing the sensitivity of breakup observables to the choice of pseudo-state basis. The THO basis was recently introduced for three-body systems by Rodriguez-Gallardo (2005). The results support the use of pseudo-state methods in other scattering frameworks such as the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method, as implemented by Moro (2006) and Matsumoto (2004). The analysis could be extended to other pseudo-state bases and reactions.
