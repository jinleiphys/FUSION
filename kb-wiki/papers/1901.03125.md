# Performance studies of anisotropic flow with MPD at NICA

> The Multi-Purpose Detector (MPD) at NICA collider has a substantial discovery potential concerning the exploration of the QCD phase diagram in the region of high net-baryon densities and moderate temperatures. The anisotropic transverse flow is one of the key observables to study the properties of dense matter created in heavy-ion collisions. The MPD performance for anisotropic flow measurements is studied with Monte-Carlo simulations of gold ions at NICA energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4-11$ GeV using different heavy-ion event generators. Different combinations of the MPD detector subsystems are used to investigate the possible systematic biases in flow measurements, and to study effects of detector azimuthal non-uniformity. The resulting performance of the MPD for flow measurements is demonstrated for directed and elliptic flow of identified charged hadrons as a function of rapidity and transverse momentum in different centrality classes.

## Key claim
The MPD detector at NICA is capable of measuring directed and elliptic flow of identified charged hadrons with good performance across a wide centrality range (0-80%) at collision energies sqrt(s_NN) = 4-11 GeV, provided that systematic biases from detector azimuthal non-uniformity and event generator choice are accounted for.

## Method
Monte-Carlo simulations of Au+Au collisions at NICA energies were generated using UrQMD (4 million events) and LAQGSM (100k events). The detector response was simulated with GEANT3 and GEANT4 toolkits, and the event plane method was used to measure anisotropic flow coefficients v1 and v2 with respect to the first-order collision symmetry plane, using energy deposition in the forward hadron calorimeters (FHCal) for reaction plane estimation.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy range: sqrt(s_NN) = 4-11 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Track selection criteria: |eta| < 1.5, 0.2 < p_T < 2 GeV/c, N_hits^TPC > 32
- Centrality range for flow measurements: 0% to 80% for all energies
- Event statistics: 4 million events (UrQMD) and 100k events (LAQGSM)
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 11 GeV, GEANT4 simulation shows increased energy deposition in FHCal compared to GEANT3, attributed to more realistic hadronic shower simulation

## Context
This work extends prior MPD performance studies (Svintsov 2017, Parfenov 2017) by incorporating full reconstruction chains including realistic particle identification via combined TPC dE/dx and TOF m^2 methods. The results demonstrate that the MPD detector can reliably measure anisotropic flow at NICA energies, enabling future experimental exploration of the QCD phase diagram at high net-baryon densities. The study uses the event plane method (Poskanzer 1998) and compares two event generators (UrQMD and LAQGSM) to assess systematic uncertainties.
