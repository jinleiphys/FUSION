# Kaon squeeze-out in heavy ion reactions

> The squeeze-out phenomenon of $K^+$ and $K^-$ mesons, i.e. the azimuthal asymmetry of $K^+$ and $K^-$ mesons emitted at midrapidity in heavy ion reactions, is investigated for beam energies of 1-2 A.GeV. It is found that the squeeze-out signal is strongly affected by in-medium potentials of these mesons. The repulsive $K^+$-nucleus potential gives rise to a pronounced out-of-plane emission of $K^+$'s at midrapidity. With the $K^+$ potential we reproduce well the experimental data of the $K^+$ azimuthal distribution. It is found that the attractive $K^-$-nucleus potential cancels to a large extent the influence of rescattering and reabsorption of the $K^-$ mesons on the projectile and target residuals (i.e. shadowing). This results in an azimuthally isotropic emission of the midrapidity $K^-$ mesons with transverse momentum up to 0.8 GeV/c. Since it is well accepted that the shadowing alone would lead to a significant out-of-plane preference of particle emission, in particular at high transverse momenta, the disappearance of the out-of-plane preference for the $K^-$ mesons can serve as an unambiguous signal of the attractive $K^-$ potential. We also apply a covariant formalism of the kaon dynamics to the squeeze-out phenomenon. Discrepancies between the theory and the experiments and possible solutions are discussed.

## Key claim

The squeeze-out signal (azimuthal asymmetry at midrapidity) for both K+ and K- mesons in heavy ion reactions at 1-2 A.GeV is strongly affected by in-medium potentials, but the conventional non-covariant treatment neglecting the spatial components of the baryon current reproduces experimental data, while a covariant treatment including the Lorentz-like force destroys agreement with both azimuthal asymmetry and in-plane flow data.

## Method

The authors used a Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1-2 A.GeV, incorporating kaon production from baryon-baryon and pion-baryon collisions. Kaon in-medium potentials were constructed from the lowest-order terms of chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg-Tomozawa and Kaplan-Nelson terms), with the K+ potential including range term and medium-modified pion decay constant, and the K- potential using a reduced sigma term of 350 MeV. The azimuthal asymmetry was quantified by the ratio Rout/in of particle multiplicity perpendicular to the reaction plane over that parallel to the plane, and the authors compared calculations with and without in-medium potentials, as well as with and without the Lorentz-like force from a covariant treatment.

## Key numbers

*   For semi-central (b=6 fm) Au+Au at 1 A.GeV with transverse momentum cut PT > 0.2 GeV/c and midrapidity cut -0.2 < (Y/Yproj)cm < 0.2, the full calculation yields Rout/in = 1.5 for K+, close to the experimental value of 1.7 from the KaoS Collaboration.
*   The K- absorption cross section is larger than 50 mb for K- momenta below 0.2 GeV/c, while the elastic K+N cross section is approximately 10 mb.
*   At nuclear matter saturation density rho0 = 0.16 fm-3, the K- potential used is about -100 MeV, while the potential extracted from kaonic atoms is about -185 +/- 15 MeV.
*   The K- mesons show nearly isotropic azimuthal emission up to a transverse momentum of Pt = 0.8 GeV/c when the attractive in-medium potential is included.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of azimuthal asymmetry in heavy ion reactions, which had shown that nucleons and pions exhibit out-of-plane emission (squeeze-out) due to shadowing by spectator matter at these energies, with transitions between in-plane and out-of-plane preferences occurring at specific beam energies (FOPI Collaboration, 1997; Ollit et al., 1998). The study specifically builds on earlier work by Li et al. (1996) on kaon azimuthal asymmetry at projectile/target rapidity, and by the Stony Brook group (Shin et al., 1998) on K+ squeeze-out. The paper enables future investigations into higher-order chiral contributions and correlation effects to resolve discrepancies between the covariant and non-covariant treatments, and suggests that studying kaon squeeze-out at beam energies above 6 A.GeV could provide cleaner information on in-medium potentials by minimizing shadowing effects.
