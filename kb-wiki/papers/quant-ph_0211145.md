# Investigation of phase-equivalent potentials by a halo transfer reaction

> Using the supersymmetric quantum mechanics we investigate the wave function-sensitive properties of the supersymmetric potentials which have received a lot of attention in the literature recently. We show that a superdeep potential and its phase-equivalent shallow-partner potential give very similar "rms" values for the weakly bound systems such as the deuteron and 11Be nuclei. Although the corresponding eigenstates differ in the node-number, our investigation on the 11Be(p,d)10Be single nucleon halo transfer reaction at 35 MeV show that also other physical quantities such as the cross section angular distributions calculated using these wave functions reflect the nodal structure rather weakly. This lends support to two nearly equivalent treatments of the Pauli principle.

## Key claim

A superdeep potential and its phase-equivalent shallow partner potential give very similar root-mean-square radii for weakly bound systems such as the deuteron and ¹¹Be, and the cross section angular distributions for the ¹¹Be(p,d)¹⁰Be halo transfer reaction at 35 MeV calculated using these wave functions reflect the nodal structure rather weakly, lending support to two nearly equivalent treatments of the Pauli principle.

## Method

The authors used supersymmetric quantum mechanics to construct phase-equivalent partner potentials from a two-parameter sech-squared superdeep potential, first for the deuteron system and then for the ¹¹Be one-neutron halo system. They then calculated the ¹¹Be(p,d)¹⁰Be single nucleon halo transfer reaction at 35 MeV using the adiabatic model in zero-range approximation, employing both the deep potential and its phase-equivalent shallow partner for the deuteron and the transferred neutron bound state wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron matter radius: 1.953 fm for the superdeep potential and 1.955 fm for the phase-equivalent partner potential.
- For the deuteron system, the superdeep potential parameters were Ã = 3.146 and β = 1.587 fm⁻¹, with ħ²/(2μₙ₋ₚ) = 41.47 MeV fm²; the ground state binding energy was about 481 MeV (unphysical) and the physically meaningful deuteron bound state had a binding energy of 2.226 MeV.
- For the ¹¹Be system, the root-mean-square radius was 6.70 fm for the initial deep potential and 6.78 fm for the phase-equivalent partner potential; the intermediate non-phase-equivalent potential gave 6.17 fm.
- For the ¹¹Be system, the superdeep potential parameters were Ã = 3.124 and β = 0.694 fm⁻¹, with ħ²/(2μ) = 22.81 MeV fm²; the neutron separation energy was 0.503 MeV.
- The deuteron wave function node from the superdeep potential was located around 0.56 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstrations by Baye (1990) that deep and shallow phase-equivalent potentials exist for nucleus-nucleus scattering, and by Van Dijk (1999) and Ridikas (1999) who showed similar rms values for model deuteron and one-neutron halo systems. The paper goes beyond those studies by calculating a specific transfer reaction, ¹¹Be(p,d)¹⁰Be at 35 MeV, to test whether the wave function differences affect reaction observables. The results enable further investigation of whether deep or shallow potential descriptions are preferable for reactions involving weakly bound halo nuclei, and support the equivalence of two treatments of the Pauli principle in local potential models.
