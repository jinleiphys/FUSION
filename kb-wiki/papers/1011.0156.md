# Relativistic point-form approach to hadron properties

> We present a review of the description of hadron properties along an invariant mass operator in the point form of Poincaré-invariant relativistic dynamics. The quark-quark interaction is furnished by a linear confinement, consistent with the QCD string tension, and a hyperfine interaction derived from Goldstone-boson exchange. The main advantage of the point-form approach is the possibility of calculating manifestly covariant observables, since the generators of Lorentz transformations remain interaction-free. We discuss the static properties of the mass-operator eigenstates, such as the invariant mass spectra of light- and heavy-flavor baryons, the characteristics of the eigenstates in terms of their spin, flavor, and spatial dependences as well as their classification into spin-flavor multiplets. Regarding dynamical observables we address the electroweak structures of the nucleon and hyperon ground states, including their electric radii, magnetic moments as well as axial charges, and in addition a recently derived microscopic description of the $πNN$ as well as $πNΔ$ interaction vertices. Except for hadronic resonance decays, which are not addressed here due to space limitations, all of these observables are obtained in good agreement with existing phenomenology. Relativistic (boost) effects are generally sizable. We conclude that low-energy hadrons can be well described by an effective theory with a finite number of degrees of freedom, as long as the symmetries of low-energy quantum chromodynamics (spontaneously broken chiral symmetry) as well as special relativity (Poincaré invariance) are properly taken into account. The latter requirement is particularly well and efficiently met in the point-form approach.

## Key claim
Low-energy hadron properties can be well described by an effective theory with a finite number of degrees of freedom, provided the symmetries of low-energy QCD (spontaneously broken chiral symmetry) and special relativity (Poincare invariance) are properly taken into account; the point-form approach meets this requirement particularly well and efficiently, though the author notes the reasons for its good performance are not yet fully understood.

## Method
The authors formulate a relativistic constituent-quark model (RCQM) using an invariant mass operator in the point form of Poincare-invariant relativistic dynamics, where baryons are treated as three-quark systems. The quark-quark interaction combines a linear confinement potential consistent with the QCD string tension with a hyperfine interaction derived from Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE), specifically a pseudoscalar coupling. The eigenvalue problem of the mass operator is solved, and the resulting eigenstates are used to calculate electroweak observables via the point-form spectator model (PFSM), where the exchange boson couples to only one constituent quark while momentum is transferred to the baryon as a whole.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon ground state spatial probability distribution is maximal at Jacobi coordinates of approximately xi ≈ eta ≈ 0.3 fm.
- Electric radius squared of the proton: PFSM prediction 0.824 fm^2 versus experimental 0.769 ± 0.012 fm^2.
- Electric radius squared of the neutron: PFSM prediction -0.135 fm^2 versus experimental -0.1161 ± 0.0022 fm^2.
- Proton magnetic moment: PFSM prediction 2.70 n.m. versus experimental 2.792847356 ± 0.000000023 n.m.
- Neutron magnetic moment: PFSM prediction -1.70 n.m. versus experimental -1.9130427 ± 0.0000005 n.m.
- Axial charge g_A^N: EGBE RCQM prediction 1.15 versus experimental 1.2694 ± 0.0028.
- Axial charge g_A^Sigma: EGBE RCQM prediction 0.65.
- Axial charge g_A^Xi: EGBE RCQM prediction -0.21.
- Axial charge g_A^Delta: EGBE RCQM prediction -4.48.
- piNN coupling constant f_piNN extracted from the GBE RCQM agrees with the phenomenological value of 0.075.

## Context
This work reviews and extends the Goldstone-boson exchange relativistic constituent quark model (GBE RCQM) originally developed by Glozman and Riska for baryons, and later constructed in nonrelativistic form by the Graz group (GPP). It contrasts the GBE approach with other modern RCQMs: the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) RCQM of Bhaduri-Cohler-Nogami (Theussl, 2000) and the instanton-induced (II) RCQM (Loring, 2001). The point-form approach enables manifestly covariant calculations of electroweak observables, overcoming frame-dependence issues found in the instant-form spectator model (IFSM). The results enable further studies of baryon structure, particularly comparisons with lattice QCD calculations (Lin and Orginos, 2007; Erkol, Oka, and Takahashi, 2009) and chiral perturbation theory (Jiang and Tiburzi, 2008, 2009), as well as microscopic descriptions of piNN and piNDelta interaction vertices.
