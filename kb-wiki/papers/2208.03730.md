# Exact factorization of the many-body Green's function theory of electrons and nuclei

> We combine the recently developed many-body Green's function theory for electrons and nuclei with the exact factorization of the wave function. The existing Born-Oppenheimer Green's functions are shown to be special cases of our exact approach. We consider the limitations of the laboratory frame formulation of the Green's function theory and discuss why the body-fixed frame formulation is needed in order to go beyond the Born-Oppenheimer theory. We give exact forms of the electronic and nuclear Green's functions written in terms of the exact factorized states, providing a systematic approach beyond the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. The lowest order approximation to the exact electronic Green's function is found to be an expected value of the Born-Oppenheimer electronic Green's function with respect to the nuclear density.

## Key claim

The paper combines the exact factorization of the wave function with many-body Green's function theory for electrons and nuclei, showing that the existing Born-Oppenheimer Green's functions are special cases of this exact approach, but only when the laboratory frame formulation is used with additional approximations that break translational symmetry.

## Method

The author starts from the full electron-nuclear Hamiltonian and writes the exact wave function as a product of a conditional electronic wave function and a nuclear wave function (exact factorization). The electronic and nuclear Green's functions are then re-expressed in terms of these factorized states, with the trace operations separated into the electronic and nuclear Hilbert spaces. A perturbation theory in the nuclear kinetic energy is developed using the exact factorized states as the unperturbed reference, and the lowest-order approximation to the exact electronic Green's function is derived as an expectation value of the Born-Oppenheimer electronic Green's function with respect to the nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the many-body Green's function theory of electrons and nuclei originally introduced by Baym (1961) and later developed for electron-phonon systems by Giustino (2017) and others. It addresses the fundamental problem that the laboratory frame formulation of the Green's function theory, as used in the Hedin-Baym equations, does not describe bound states due to continuous translational symmetry, requiring either the imposition of periodic boundary conditions (as in the Born-von Karman approach) or a transformation to the body-fixed frame as introduced by Harkonen (2020). The exact factorization approach provides a systematic way to go beyond the Born-Oppenheimer approximation while maintaining computational tractability by separating the electronic and nuclear degrees of freedom.
