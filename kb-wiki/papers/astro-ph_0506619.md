# An approach toward the successful supernova explosion by physics of unstable nuclei

> We study the explosion mechanism of collapse-driven supernovae by numerical simulations with a new nuclear EOS based on unstable nuclei. We report new results of simulations of general relativistic hydrodynamics together with the Boltzmann neutrino-transport in spherical symmetry. We adopt the new data set of relativistic EOS and the conventional set of EOS (Lattimer-Swesty EOS) to examine the influence on dynamics of core-collapse, bounce and shock propagation. We follow the behavior of stalled shock more than 500 ms after the bounce and compare the evolutions of supernova core.

# An approach toward the successful supernova explosion by physics of unstable nuclei

## Key claim

The new relativistic nuclear equation of state (Shen's EOS) based on unstable nuclei does not by itself produce a successful supernova explosion in spherically symmetric simulations; the shock still stalls below 200 km and recedes, similar to the conventional Lattimer-Swesty EOS, but quantitative differences in central density, lepton fraction, and thermal structure arise from the different nuclear physics inputs.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic hydrodynamics simulations coupled with Boltzmann neutrino transport in spherical symmetry, using a 15 solar mass progenitor from Woosley and Weaver (1995). They compared two nuclear equations of state: the new relativistic mean-field EOS (Shen's EOS, parameter set TM1) and the conventional compressible liquid drop EOS (Lattimer-Swesty EOS with incompressibility 180 MeV), following the evolution for more than 500 ms after core bounce.

## Key numbers

- Peak central density at bounce: 3.3 × 10^14 g/cm^3 for Shen's EOS, compared to 4.2 × 10^14 g/cm^3 for Lattimer-Swesty EOS
- Trapped lepton fraction at bounce center: 0.35 for Shen's EOS, slightly larger than 0.34 for Lattimer-Swesty EOS
- Central density at 600 ms after bounce: 3.9 × 10^14 g/cm^3 for Shen's EOS, compared to 6.0 × 10^14 g/cm^3 for Lattimer-Swesty EOS
- Peak temperature at around 10 km at 600 ms after bounce: approximately 40 MeV for Shen's EOS and approximately 50 MeV for Lattimer-Swesty EOS
- Incompressibility of uniform nuclear matter in Shen's EOS: 281 MeV; symmetry energy: 36.9 MeV
- Symmetry energy in Lattimer-Swesty EOS: 29.3 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass from Shen's EOS: 2.2 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the series of spherically symmetric neutrino-transfer hydrodynamics simulations by Rampp and Janka (2000), Mezzacappa et al. (2001), Liebendoerfer et al. (2001), and Thompson et al. (2003), which also found no explosion. The new Shen's EOS had previously been applied to prompt supernova explosions by Sumiyoshi et al. (2001) and to r-process simulations by Sumiyoshi et al. (2000); this study provides the first detailed comparison of post-bounce evolution between the relativistic and conventional EOS over hundreds of milliseconds. The observed negative gradients in entropy and lepton fraction suggest that multi-dimensional convection may be necessary for explosion, and the differences in thermal structure will affect supernova neutrino signals and proto-neutron star cooling in future studies.
