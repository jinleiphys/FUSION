# Evidence for higher nodal band states with $^3$He cluster structure in $^{19}$Ne and prerainbows in $^3$He+$^{16}$O scattering

> The existence of a higher nodal band state with a $^{3}$He cluster structure, i.e. a vibrational mode in which the inter-cluster relative motion is excited, in $^{19}$Ne in addition to those with the $α$ cluster structure in $^{20}$Ne and the $^{16}$O cluster structure in $^{32}$S, is suggested, which reinforces the importance of the concept of $^3$He-clustering in nuclei. This conclusion was reached by investigating $^{3}$He scattering from $^{16}$O in a wide range of incident energies and prerainbow oscillations.

## Key claim

The paper suggests the existence of a higher nodal band (N=8) with a $^3$He+$^{16}$O cluster structure in $^{19}$Ne, where the inter-cluster relative motion is excited, based on a unified description of $^3$He+$^{16}$O scattering and bound states using a double folding potential, contrary to a previous negative conclusion by Waltham et al. (1983).

## Method

The authors performed a double folding model analysis of $^3$He+$^{16}$O elastic scattering angular distributions at incident energies from 15 to 60 MeV, using the DDM3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction and ground state densities of $^3$He and $^{16}$O. They introduced a normalization factor $\lambda$ for the real potential and phenomenological Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials, then decomposed the scattering amplitudes into farside/nearside and internal-wave/barrier-wave contributions to identify prerainbow oscillations and Airy minima. The same potential was used to calculate bound and quasi-bound states of $^{19}$Ne, including a spin-orbit potential with strength $V_{so}=0.011$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The double folding potential at $E_L=15$ MeV with $\lambda=1.34$ ($J_V=466.4$ MeV fm$^3$) locates the lowest Pauli-allowed ground state of $^{19}$Ne at -4.97 MeV from the $^3$He threshold, compared to the experimental energy of -8.44 MeV.
- The calculated rms radius of the real potential is 3.74 fm for all incident energies.
- At $E_L=60$ MeV, the first order Airy minimum appears at $\theta\approx27^\circ$.
- The $\frac{1}{2}^+$ state at $E_x=12.69$ MeV observed in $^3$He+$^{16}$O elastic scattering at $E_L=5.05$ MeV has a $^3$He decay width $\Gamma_{c.m.}/\Gamma_{tot}=0.43$ with $\Gamma_{c.m.}=0.18$ MeV.
- States observed in $^{16}$O($^3$He,$\gamma$) capture reactions at $E_x=13.8$, 14.88, and 16.24 MeV have widths $\Gamma_{c.m.}=0.67$, 0.62, and 0.40 MeV respectively, with dimensionless reduced widths $\theta^2=0.20$, 0.13, and 0.07 at a channel radius of 5 fm assuming $L=2$.
- The state at $E_x=18.4$ MeV has $\Gamma_{c.m.}=4.4$ MeV and $\theta^2=1.0$ assuming $L=4$.
- The rotational constant of the calculated $N=8$ band is $k\simeq0.21$, comparable to $k\simeq0.24$ estimated from experimental states.

## Context

This work extends the concept of higher nodal band states (vibrational excitation of inter-cluster relative motion) previously established for $\alpha$ cluster structure in $^{20}$Ne, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{44}$Ti (Michel 1998, Yamaya 1998) and for $^{16}$O cluster structure in $^{32}$S (Ohkubo 2002) to the $^3$He cluster in $^{19}$Ne. It contradicts the negative conclusion of Waltham et al. (1983), who used a cosh potential with an unphysical long tail (volume integral 695 MeV fm$^3$) that the authors argue is inappropriate for highly excited cluster states. The findings enable future searches for similar higher nodal bands with t+$^{16}$O cluster structure in the mirror nucleus $^{19}$F and prerainbow oscillations in t+$^{16}$O scattering.
