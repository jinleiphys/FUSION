# Incoherent diffractive J/Psi-production in high energy nuclear DIS

> We compute cross sections for incoherent diffractive J/Psi production in lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering. The cross section is proportional to A in the dilute limit and to A^(1/3) in the black disc limit, with a large nuclear suppression due to saturation effects. The t-dependence of the cross section, if it can be measured accurately enough, is sensitive to the impact parameter profile of the gluons in the nucleus and their fluctuations, a quantity that determines the initial conditions of a relativistic heavy ion collision. The nuclear suppression in incoherent diffraction shows how the transverse spatial distribution of the gluons in the nucleus gradually becomes smoother at high energy. Since the values of the momentum transfer |t| involved are relatively large, this process should be easier to measure in future nuclear DIS experiments than coherent diffraction.

## Key claim

The cross section for incoherent diffractive J/Psi production in lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering is proportional to A in the dilute limit and to A^(1/3) in the black disc limit, with a large nuclear suppression due to saturation effects that is sensitive to the impact parameter profile of gluons in the nucleus and their fluctuations.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections using the dipole picture of deep inelastic scattering within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, employing two dipole cross section parametrizations: the IIM parametrization (which includes features of Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution) and a factorized approximation of the IPsat parametrization (which uses an eikonalized DGLAP-evolved gluon distribution). The nuclear S-matrix is constructed using the independent scattering approximation (Glauber theory) where the S-matrix for a nucleus is the product of proton S-matrices at the transverse positions of the nucleons, and the average over nucleon positions is performed using a Woods-Saxon distribution. The incoherent cross section is obtained as the variance of the scattering amplitude over nucleon configurations, while the coherent cross section comes from the squared average amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The proton impact parameter profile width B_p is 4.0 GeV^(-2) for IPsat and 5.59 GeV^(-2) for IIM
- The ratio between coherent cross section at t=0 and incoherent cross section extrapolated to t=0 is 270 (IPsat) and 300 (IIM) at Q^2=0 for gold (A=197), and 250 (IPsat) and 270 (IIM) at Q^2=10 GeV^2
- The n=2 contribution to the incoherent cross section is typically less than 2% of the n=1 contribution, reaching only 5% at -t greater than 0.5 GeV^2
- The nuclear radius is given by R_A = (1.12 A^(1/3) - 0.86 A^(-1/3)) fm with surface thickness d = 0.54 fm
- The cross section ratio (nuclear transparency) decreases by approximately 0.04 when using the "gaus-LC" wavefunction compared to the "boosted Gaussian" wavefunction

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of nuclear modifications to diffractive structure functions (Kowalski, 2008) and coherent vector meson production (Goncalves, 2009) by providing a more detailed treatment of the t-dependence of incoherent diffraction. The authors build on the Good-Walker picture of diffraction and the dipole model framework that has successfully described HERA data on diffractive J/Psi production. The results enable future nuclear DIS experiments (such as the EIC or LHeC) to use incoherent diffractive J/Psi production as a quantitative probe of gluon saturation effects and the transverse spatial distribution of gluons in nuclei, which determines the initial conditions of relativistic heavy ion collisions.
