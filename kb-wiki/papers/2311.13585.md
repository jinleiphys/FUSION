# Nuclear Structure Effects on Hyperfine Splittings in Ordinary and Muonic Deuterium

> Precision spectroscopy of hyperfine splitting (HFS) is a crucial tool for investigating the structure of nuclei and testing quantum electrodynamics (QED). However, accurate theoretical predictions are hindered by two-photon exchange (TPE) effects. We propose a novel formalism that accounts for nuclear excitations and recoil in TPE, providing a model-independent description of TPE effects on HFS in light ordinary and muonic atoms. Combining our formalism with pionless effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order, the predicted TPE effects on HFS are 41.2(2.6) kHz and 0.116(9) meV for the 1S state in deuterium and the 2S state in muonic deuterium. These results are within 1.4-1.7 standard deviation from recent measurements and highlight the importance of nuclear structure effects on HFS and indicate the value of more precise measurements in future experiments.

# Nuclear Structure Effects on Hyperfine Splittings in Ordinary and Muonic Deuterium

## Key claim
The paper presents a new formalism for two-photon exchange (TPE) effects on hyperfine splitting that accounts for nuclear excitations and recoil, and when combined with pionless effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order, predicts TPE contributions of 41.2(2.6) kHz for the 1S state in deuterium and 0.116(9) meV for the 2S state in muonic deuterium. These predictions fall within 1.4-1.7 standard deviations of recent measurements, indicating that the formalism captures the dominant nuclear structure effects but that higher-order corrections or more precise measurements are still needed.

## Method
The authors develop a formalism for the two-photon exchange contribution to hyperfine splitting that separates the effect into elastic, polarizability, and single-nucleon components, with the nuclear response functions expressed through charge-magnetic and convection-magnetic sum rules. They evaluate these response functions using pionless effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order, computing deuteron bound and scattering states with dimensional regularization and power-divergence subtraction renormalization, including S-to-D-wave mixing operators. The elastic TPE is computed from deuteron electric and magnetic form factors, while the polarizability contributions are obtained by integrating the response functions weighted by kinematic kernels, and single-nucleon TPE effects are incorporated using effective proton and neutron Zemach radii from independent determinations.

## Key numbers
- Predicted TPE effect on HFS in deuterium 1S state: 41.7(2.6) kHz, compared to the experimental-QED discrepancy of 45 kHz
- Predicted TPE effect on HFS in muonic deuterium 2S state: 0.118(9) meV, compared to the experimental-QED discrepancy of 0.0966(73) meV
- Theoretical deuteron Zemach radius from pionless EFT: 2.605(91) fm, consistent with the experimental value 2.593(16) fm within 1 sigma
- Effective proton Zemach radius for ordinary atoms: 0.883(2) fm; for muonic atoms: 0.906(2) fm
- Effective neutron Zemach radius for ordinary atoms: 0.347(38) fm; for muonic atoms: 0.102(39) fm
- Elastic TPE contribution in deuterium 1S: -41.9(1.5) kHz; polarizability contribution: 109.8(3.8) kHz
- Elastic TPE contribution in muonic deuterium 2S: -0.123(4) meV; polarizability contribution: 0.358(13) meV

## Context
This work extends previous efforts to compute TPE effects on hyperfine splitting in deuterium and muonic deuterium, which had been attempted using the zero-range approximation by Khriplovich (1995, 2003) and the Low-term formalism by Friar (2005). The zero-range approximation agreed with experiment within 5% but introduced an unquantified model-dependent uncertainty through an arbitrary energy cutoff. The Low-term formalism, which uses the closure approximation without explicit nuclear excitations, gave 46 kHz for deuterium but this agreement was accidental due to omission of single-nucleon recoil and polarizability effects; when those were added the prediction became 64 kHz, disagreeing with experiment by 43%. For muonic deuterium, the Low-term formalism extended by Kalinowski (2018) predicted only 0.0383 meV, accounting for only 40% of the observed discrepancy. The present formalism enables model-independent, uncertainty-quantified predictions and can be applied to other light atomic systems, though further improvement requires higher-order chiral perturbation theory calculations for single-nucleon TPE and more precise measurements of hyperfine splitting in both ordinary and muonic deuterium.
