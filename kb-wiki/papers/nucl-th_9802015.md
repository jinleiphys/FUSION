# Partons in Phase Space

> Within QED, we examine several issues related to constructing a parton-model-based QCD transport theory. We rewrite the QED analog of the parton model, the Weizsaecker-Williams Approximation, entirely in terms of phase-space quantities and we study the phase-space photon and electron densities created by a classical point charge. We find that the densities take a distinctive ``source-propagator'' form. This form does not arise in a conventional derivation of the semiclassical transport equations because of the overuse of the gradient approximation. We do not apply the gradient approximation and so derive the phase-space analog of the Generalized Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem. Together, this theorem and the expression for the phase-space particle self-energies give a set of coupled phase-space evolution equations. We illustrate how these evolution equations can be used perturbatively or to derive semiclassical transport equations. Our work relies on phase-space propagators and sources, so we describe them in detail when calculating the photon and electron phase-space densities. We use these tools to discuss the shape of a nucleon's parton cloud.

## Key claim

Within QED, the authors show that the phase-space densities of photons and electrons take a distinctive "source-propagator" form, and they derive the phase-space analog of the Generalized Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem without applying the gradient approximation, which is normally used in conventional derivations of semiclassical transport equations.

## Method

The authors rewrite the Weizsacker-Williams Approximation (the QED analog of the parton model) entirely in terms of phase-space quantities by Wigner transforming the reaction probabilities for photon exchange and photon absorption. They calculate the phase-space photon and electron densities created by a classical point charge using Feynman perturbation theory, then use time-ordered nonequilibrium methods to derive coupled phase-space evolution equations without making the gradient approximation.

## Key numbers

- The primary hadronic collisions at RHIC occur at sqrt(s) ~ 200A GeV, providing the energy scale for the QGP context
- Large-Q^2 partons extend an additional hbar c / (x P_L) from the bag surface in the longitudinal direction, where P_L is the longitudinal momentum of the nucleon
- Small-x partons extend at least an additional hbar c sqrt(-q^2) from the bag, making the small-x parton cloud substantially larger than the large-Q^2 cloud

## Context

This work extends the standard semiclassical transport equation derivations of Danielewicz (1984) and Mrowczynski (1990, 1992) by removing the gradient approximation, which normally ignores small-scale structure of particle phase-space densities. The authors build on the Weizsacker-Williams Approximation as described by Jackson (1975) and Bertulani and Baur (1988), and they connect their phase-space formalism to the parton model approach used by Geiger (1996) for QCD transport. The work enables a systematic treatment of both hard and soft modes in a transport framework and provides tools for discussing the coordinate-space structure of a nucleon's parton cloud.
