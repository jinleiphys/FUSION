# Causality violations in realistic simulations of heavy-ion collisions

> Causality is violated in the early stages of state-of-the-art heavy-ion hydrodynamic simulations. Such violations are present in up to 75% of the fluid cells in the initial time and only after 2-3 fm/$c$ of evolution do we find that 50% of the fluid cells are definitely causal. Superluminal propagation reaches up to 15% the speed of light in some of the fluid cells. The inclusion of pre-equilibrium evolution significantly reduces the number of acausal cells. Our findings suggests that relativistic causality may place constraints on the available parameter space of heavy-ion collision simulations when factored into more thorough statistical analyses.

## Key claim

Causality violations occur in up to 75% of fluid cells at the earliest times of state-of-the-art heavy-ion hydrodynamic simulations, and only after 2-3 fm/c of evolution do 50% of fluid cells become definitely causal; the inclusion of pre-equilibrium evolution significantly reduces but does not eliminate these violations.

## Method

The authors simulated central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using two open-source frameworks: T_RENTo+free-streaming+VISHNU (TFV) and IP-Glasma+(K_O_MP_O_ST)+MUSIC (IKM). They evaluated necessary and sufficient causality conditions from Bemfica (2020) at each fluid cell and time step, classifying cells as causal (sufficient conditions hold), acausal (necessary conditions violated), or indeterminate (necessary conditions satisfied but sufficient conditions fail). For the IKM framework, three pre-equilibrium scenarios were compared: no pre-equilibrium, free-streaming K_O_MP_O_ST, and effective kinetic theory (EKT) K_O_MP_O_ST.

## Key numbers

- Up to 75% of fluid cells explicitly violate causality at the initial time in the IKM framework without pre-equilibrium evolution
- Only after 2-3 fm/c of evolution do 50% of fluid cells become definitely causal
- Superluminal propagation reaches up to 15% the speed of light in some fluid cells
- Inclusion of K_O_MP_O_ST pre-equilibrium reduces acausal cells to approximately 1/3 of fluid cells
- The TFV framework uses freeze-out energy density epsilon_FO = 0.265 GeV/fm^3
- The IKM framework uses eta/s = 0.12 and freeze-out at epsilon_FO = 0.18 GeV/fm^3 or T_FO = 145 MeV
- Acausal cells account for only about 1-2% of the total number of cells in the simulation throughout the evolution

## Context

This work extends the causality constraints derived by Bemfica (2020) for nonlinear relativistic hydrodynamics to realistic heavy-ion collision simulations, and is consistent with findings from Cheng (2021) on causality in AA collisions. The results suggest that causality constraints should be incorporated into Bayesian analyses such as those by Bernhard (2019) that extract QGP transport coefficients, and that improvements in pre-equilibrium modeling beyond conformal and boost-invariant approximations are needed to resolve acausal behavior.
