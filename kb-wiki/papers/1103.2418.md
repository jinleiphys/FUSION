# Probing internal structure of Lambda(1405) in meson-baryon dynamics with chiral symmetry

> The internal structure of the resonant Lambda(1405) state is investigated based on meson-baryon coupled-channels chiral dynamics, by evaluating density distributions obtained from the form factors of the Lambda(1405) state. The form factors are extracted from current-coupled scattering amplitudes in which the current is coupled to the constituent hadrons inside Lambda(1405). Using several probe interactions and channel decomposition, we find that the resonant Lambda(1405) state is dominantly composed of widely spread Kbar around N, with a small fraction of the escaping pi Sigma component.

## Key claim

The resonant Lambda(1405) state is dominantly composed of a widely spread antikaon (Kbar) around a nucleon (N), with a small fraction of an escaping pi Sigma component, based on evaluations of density distributions obtained from form factors at the Lambda(1405) pole position in the complex energy plane.

## Method

The authors used meson-baryon coupled-channels chiral dynamics (the chiral unitary approach) to dynamically generate the Lambda(1405) state. They extracted form factors of the Lambda(1405) from current-coupled scattering amplitudes, where the conserved probe current was coupled to the constituent hadrons inside the resonance, and evaluated density distributions at the pole position of the Lambda(1405) in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- The KbarN (I=0) channel generates 0.994 + 0.048i of the baryonic and strangeness charges of the Lambda(1405), giving more than 99% of the total charges.
- The antikaon (Kbar) distribution inside the Lambda(1405) largely exceeds typical hadronic size of less than 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to studying the internal structure of the Lambda(1405), which was previously considered as a KbarN quasi-bound state by Dalitz (1960) and dynamically generated in meson-baryon coupled-channels by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), Oller (2000), Lutz (2001), and Jido (2003). The results enable future experimental surveys of the Lambda(1405) structure, such as those proposed by Jido (2009) and Cho (2010), and provide a microscopic understanding of the resonance's spatial composition beyond the cut-off scheme study by Yamagata-Sekihara (2010).
