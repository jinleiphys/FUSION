# Thermal dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at beam-energy-scan (BES) energies

> The lepton pair production rate at finite temperature and at next-to-leading-order (NLO) is calculated for quark-gluon plasma at non-zero baryon density. Yields are obtained using a (3+1)D multicomponent simulation capable of reproducing hadronic observables measured in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. Spectra of intermediate invariant mass dileptons are compared with measurements from the STAR collaboration. The result of a study where the temperature information obtained from the dilepton spectrum from heavy-ion collisions performed at different energies and centralities is presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first derivation of thermal dilepton production rates at next-to-leading-order (NLO) in the strong coupling and at finite baryon chemical potential, and shows that the effective temperature extracted from dilepton spectra in the intermediate mass region (1 < M < 3 GeV) correlates linearly with the true initial temperature from hydrodynamical simulations, though the yields reported are not yet corrected for viscous effects.

## Method

The authors calculated the NLO dilepton production rate for quark-gluon plasma at non-zero baryon chemical potential by combining two-loop and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) diagrams, re-expanding the LPM contribution to avoid double-counting at finite chemical potential for the first time. These rates were integrated over a (3+1)D multicomponent fluid dynamical simulation that reproduces hadronic observables from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, with a hadronic afterburner evolving the late states to kinetic freeze-out. The resulting dilepton spectra in the intermediate invariant mass region (1 < M < 3 GeV) were compared with STAR collaboration measurements.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies range from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV (RHIC Beam Energy Scan)
- Dilepton yields are reported in the mass region 1 < M < 3 GeV (intermediate mass region between m_phi and m_J/psi)
- The spectral density is shown for a scaled momentum k/T = 2 pi at a temperature T = 0.3 GeV for several values of baryon chemical potential
- The Debye mass modification at finite chemical potential: m_D^2 -> g^2 [(1/2 n_f + N_c) T^2/3 + n_f mu^2/(2 pi^2)] where mu = mu_B/3
- The asymptotic quark mass modification: m_infinity^2 -> (g^2/3)(T^2 + mu^2/pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculations of thermal dilepton production to finite baryon chemical potential, building on prior work by Gale (1990) and Weldon (1990) on the relation between dilepton rates and the photon self-energy, and on NLO estimates of real photon production by Gervais (2012). The results enable extraction of QGP temperature information from dilepton spectra at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, which is rare in heavy-ion physics, and the authors note that viscous corrections studied previously by Vujanovic (2013) will be incorporated in a future study.
