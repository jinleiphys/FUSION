# Novel implementation of the multipole expansion to quarkonium hadronic transitions

> We compute hadronic transitions between heavy quarkonium states with two, or one, pion/eta particles in the final state. We use the multipole expansion but not the twist expansion. The latter cannot be justified for the energy release of hadronic transitions between heavy quarkonium states with different principal quantum numbers. Instead, we use a counting based on the dimension of the interpolating field of the hybrid. This alternative counting allows us to still use chiral low-energy theorems to compute the pion production by local gluonic operators. We explore the phenomenological impact of this counting. Remarkably enough, for the two-pion transitions, we obtain the same predictions for the normalized differential decay rate as those obtained assuming the twist expansion. We implement this computational scheme using the hadronic representation of the effective theory potential NRQCD. We assume that the inverse Bohr radius of the heavy quarkonium is much larger than $Λ_{\rm QCD}$ but do not impose any constraint on the relative size of $Λ_{\rm QCD}$ and the typical kinetic energy of the bound state.

## Key claim

The authors compute hadronic transitions between heavy quarkonium states with two or one pion/eta in the final state using the multipole expansion but not the twist expansion, which cannot be justified for the energy release of such transitions. They instead use a counting based on the dimension of the interpolating field of the hybrid, which allows them to still use chiral low-energy theorems and yields the same predictions for normalized differential decay rates of two-pion transitions as the twist expansion, without imposing constraints on the relative size of Λ_QCD and the typical kinetic energy of the bound state.

## Method

The authors construct a hadronic representation of the effective theory potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) by projecting onto a Fock subspace spanned by singlet, hybrid, and pion fields, organized in a combined expansion in chiral counting and the multipole, 1/m_Q, and 1/N_c expansions. They match correlation functions between the partonic and hadronic representations of the theory to determine the couplings, using a hypothesis that higher-dimension interpolating operators for the gluelump are subleading, and then compute transition amplitudes for quarkonium decays via intermediate hybrid states.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as F = 92.419 MeV.
- For the ψ(2S) → J/ψ π⁺π⁻ transition, the chiral fit to the normalized differential decay width yields ratios a₁/a₂ = -0.20^{+3.93}_{-0.98}, a₃/a₂ = 3.12^{+3.78}_{-15.05}, with χ²/d.o.f. = 0.13.
- For the Υ(2S) → Υ(1S) π⁺π⁻ transition, the chiral fit yields ratios a₁/a₂ = 0.26^{+5.29}_{-2.30}, a₃/a₂ = 1.84^{+8.48}_{-20.43}, with χ²/d.o.f. = 0.13.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gottfried (1977) and Yan (1980) who used the multipole expansion for quarkonium hadronic transitions, and Voloshin (1978) who introduced the twist expansion, which the authors argue cannot be justified for the energy release in these transitions as shown by Luty (1993). The new counting based on hybrid interpolating field dimension provides an alternative framework that remains valid even when Λ_QCD is much larger than the kinetic energy of the bound state, and enables systematic computation of one-pion transitions and ratios of decay widths where certain uncertainties cancel.
