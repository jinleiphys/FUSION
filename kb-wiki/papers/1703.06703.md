# Up, down, and strange nucleon axial form factors from lattice QCD

> We report a calculation of the nucleon axial form factors $G_A^q(Q^2)$ and $G_P^q(Q^2)$ for all three light quark flavors $q\in\{u,d,s\}$ in the range $0\leq Q^2\lesssim 1.2\text{ GeV}^2$ using lattice QCD. This work was done using a single ensemble with pion mass 317 MeV and made use of the hierarchical probing technique to efficiently evaluate the required disconnected loops. We perform nonperturbative renormalization of the axial current, including a nonperturbative treatment of the mixing between light and strange currents due to the singlet-nonsinglet difference caused by the axial anomaly. The form factor shapes are fit using the model-independent $z$ expansion. From $G_A^q(Q^2)$, we determine the quark contributions to the nucleon spin and axial radii. By extrapolating the isovector $G_P^{u-d}(Q^2)$, we obtain the induced pseudoscalar coupling relevant for ordinary muon capture and the pion-nucleon coupling constant. We find that the disconnected contributions to $G_P$ form factors are large, and give an interpretation based on the dominant influence of the pseudoscalar poles in these form factors.

## Key claim

This lattice QCD calculation reports the nucleon axial form factors for up, down, and strange quarks using a single ensemble with pion mass 317 MeV, finding that disconnected contributions to the induced pseudoscalar form factors are large and dominated by pseudoscalar poles, while the results for the axial form factors are obtained with systematic uncertainty estimates from excited-state contamination.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon two-point and three-point functions on a 32^3 x 96 lattice with 2+1 flavors of clover-improved Wilson fermions at a lattice spacing of 0.11403(77) fm, using 1028 gauge configurations with five source-sink separations. Connected diagrams were evaluated exactly using sequential propagators through the sink, while disconnected loops were computed stochastically using hierarchical probing with color and spin dilution. Nonperturbative renormalization of the axial current was performed including treatment of singlet-nonsinglet mixing, and the form factor shapes were fit using the model-independent z expansion with the pseudoscalar pole removed before fitting.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 317(2)(2) MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.11403(77) fm
- Lattice volume: 32^3 x 96, with m_pi * L_s approximately 5.9
- Momentum transfer range: 0 <= Q^2 <= 1.2 GeV^2
- Number of gauge configurations: 1028, yielding 24672 samples for connected diagrams and 6168 timeslice samples for disconnected loops
- Source-sink separations used: T/a in {6, 8, 10, 12, 14}
- For the isovector axial form factor G_A^{u-d}(Q^2), the primary analysis used the summation method with T=6a
- For the isovector induced pseudoscalar form factor G_P^{u-d}(Q^2), the primary analysis used the summation method with T=8a
- For disconnected axial form factors, the primary results used T=10a (ratio method, averaged over three points near tau=T/2)
- The z expansion used t_cut = (3*m_pi)^2 for isovector form factors and k_max = 5

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors by including all three light quark flavors (up, down, strange) with both connected and disconnected diagrams, using the same dataset that previously produced a high-precision calculation of strange nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Green, 2015). The results are timely for comparison with upcoming MicroBooNE experimental measurements of the strange axial form factor at momentum transfers as low as Q^2 = 0.08 GeV^2 (Papavassiliou, 2009), and the use of the model-independent z expansion follows the approach used by Bhattacharya (2011, 2015) and Meyer (2016) for axial form factors from neutrino scattering data.
