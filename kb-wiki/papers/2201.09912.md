# Universal Properties of Weakly Bound Two-Neutron Halo Nuclei

> We construct an effective field theory of a two-neutron halo nucleus in the limit where the two-neutron separation energy $B$ and the neutron-neutron two-body virtual energy $ε_n$ are smaller than any other energy scale in the problem, but the scattering between the core and a single neutron is not fine-tuned, and the Efimov effect does not operate. The theory has one dimensionless coupling which formally runs to a Landau pole in the ultraviolet. We show that many properties of the system are universal in the double fine-tuning limit. The ratio of the mean-square matter radius and charge radius is found to be $\langle r^2_m \rangle/\langle r^2_c\rangle = A f(ε_n/B)$, where $A$ is the mass number of the core and $f$ is a function of the ratio $ε_n/B$ which we find explicitly. In particular, when $B\ggε_n$, $\langle r^2_m\rangle/\langle r^2_c\rangle = \frac23 A$. The shape of the the $E1$ dipole strength function also depends only on the ratio $ε_n/B$ and is derived in explicit analytic form. We estimate that for the $^{22}$C nucleus higher-order corrections to our theory are of order 20% or less if the two-neutron separation energy is less than 100 keV and the $s$-wave scattering length between a neutron and a $^{20}$C nucleus is less than 2.8 fm.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective field theory for two-neutron halo nuclei that is valid when both the two-neutron separation energy \(B\) and the neutron-neutron virtual energy \(\epsilon_n\) are small, but crucially *without* requiring a fine-tuned core-neutron resonance or the Efimov effect, making it applicable to systems like \({}^{22}\)C where such a resonance is absent.

## Method

The authors build a nonrelativistic effective field theory with a neutron field, a dimer (dineutron) field, a core field \(\phi\), and a halo field \(h\). The theory contains a single dimensionless coupling \(g\) for the \(h^\dagger \phi d\) vertex, which runs logarithmically with energy and reaches a Landau pole in the ultraviolet. All calculations are performed to all orders in \(g^2\) using Feynman diagrams for the charge form factor, neutron form factor, and the \(E1\) dipole strength function.

## Key numbers

* The neutron-neutron two-body virtual energy is \(\epsilon_n = \hbar^2/(m_n a^2) \approx 120\) keV, where \(a\) is the \(s\)-wave scattering length.
* In the limit \(B \gg \epsilon_n\), the ratio of mean-square matter radius to charge radius is \(\langle r_m^2 \rangle / \langle r_c^2 \rangle = \frac{2}{3} A\), where \(A\) is the core mass number.
* For the \({}^{22}\)C nucleus, higher-order corrections are estimated to be 20% or less if the two-neutron separation energy is less than 100 keV and the \(s\)-wave scattering length between a neutron and a \({}^{20}\)C nucleus is less than 2.8 fm.
* For \({}^{11}\)Li, using \(B = 247\) keV and \(\epsilon_n = 116.04\) keV, the theory predicts \(\sqrt{\langle r_c^2 \rangle} = 0.86\) fm and \(\sqrt{\langle r_n^2 \rangle} = 4.7\) fm.
* For \({}^{6}\)He, the theory predicts \(\langle r_m^2 \rangle / \langle r_c^2 \rangle \approx 0.686 A\), which differs from the value 0.862\(A\) derived by Danilin (2005) by about 20%.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei without relying on a core-neutron resonance, which was assumed in prior EFT treatments by Canham (2008) and Hammer (2017). The theory is specifically designed for systems like \({}^{22}\)C, where the core-neutron scattering length is small (Mosby 2013), and may also apply to the \({}^{3}\)He\({}^{4}\)He\(_2\) molecular trimer. The results enable quantitative predictions for charge radii, matter radii, and \(E1\) dipole strength functions in the double fine-tuning limit, with explicit analytic formulas that depend only on the ratio \(\epsilon_n / B\).
