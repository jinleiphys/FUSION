# Vector-meson production and vector meson dominance

> We consider the fidelity of the vector meson dominance (VMD) assumption as an instrument for relating the electromagnetic vector-meson production reaction $e + p \to e^\prime + V + p$ to the purely hadronic process $V + p \to V+p$. Analyses of the photon vacuum polarisation and the photon-quark vertex reveal that such a VMD Ansatz might be reasonable for light vector-mesons. However, when the vector-mesons are described by momentum-dependent bound-state amplitudes, VMD fails for heavy vector-mesons: it cannot be used reliably to estimate either a photon-to-vector-meson transition strength or the momentum dependence of those integrands that would arise in calculations of the different reaction amplitudes. Consequently, for processes involving heavy mesons, the veracity of both cross-section estimates and conclusions based on the VMD assumption should be reviewed, e.g., those relating to hidden-charm pentaquark production and the origin of the proton mass.

## Key claim

Vector meson dominance (VMD) might be a reasonable approximation for light vector-mesons, but it fails for heavy vector-mesons when the mesons are described by momentum-dependent bound-state amplitudes; consequently, cross-section estimates and conclusions based on VMD for processes involving heavy mesons should be reviewed.

## Method

The authors analyze the photon vacuum polarisation and the photon-quark vertex using two truncation schemes for QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations: a symmetry-preserving contact interaction (SCI) that produces pointlike bound-states, and a realistic momentum-dependent interaction in rainbow-ladder truncation that incorporates QCD constraints. They compute the ratio R_V(0) that measures the deviation of the VMD assumption from the exact result at Q^2=0 for vector-mesons of different flavours.

## Key numbers

- For the contact interaction, R_V(0) values: ρ = 0.42, φ = 0.37, J/ψ = 0.15, Υ = 0.12; these indicate VMD overestimates the connection between electromagnetic and hadronic cross-sections by a factor of six for light mesons and over fifty for heavy mesons
- Using the realistic momentum-dependent interaction, computed vector-meson masses and decay constants (in GeV): m_ρ = 0.735, f_ρ = 0.146; m_φ = 1.074, f_φ = 0.183; m_J/ψ = 3.12, f_J/ψ = 0.300; m_Υ = 9.33, f_Υ = 0.505; with mean absolute relative difference from experiment of 4.5%
- The SCI yields vector-meson masses (in GeV): ρ = 0.93, φ = 1.03, J/ψ = 3.19, Υ = 9.49; and decay constants (in GeV): ρ = 0.13, φ = 0.12, J/ψ = 0.20, Υ = 0.38

## Context

This work extends earlier objections to VMD raised by Feldman (1963) regarding the tachyonic photon problem, and by Maris (1999) who studied the ρ-meson case specifically. The paper provides a systematic QCD-based analysis across all vector-meson flavours, showing that the failure of VMD becomes dramatically worse for heavy mesons. This has direct implications for interpreting hidden-charm pentaquark production experiments at facilities like JLab and for planned measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider, as well as for studies of the proton mass origin via the QCD trace anomaly.
