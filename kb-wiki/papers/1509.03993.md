# Scaling properties of inclusive W$^\pm$ production at hadron colliders

> We consider the hadroproduction of W gauge bosons in their leptonic decay mode. Starting from the leading-order expressions, we show that by defining a suitable scaling variable the centre-of-mass dependence of the cross sections at the LHC energies can be essentially described by a simple power law. The scaling exponent is directly linked to the small-$x$ behaviour of parton distribution functions (PDF) which, at the high virtualities involved in W production, is largely dictated by QCD evolution equations. This entails a particularly simple scaling law for the lepton charge asymmetry and also predicts that measurements in different collision systems (p-p, p-$\bar{\rm p}$, p-Pb Pb-Pb) are straightforwardly related. The expectations are compared with the existing data and a very good overall agreement is observed. It is shown that the PDF uncertainty in certain cross-section ratios between nearby centre-of-mass energies can be significantly reduced by taking the ratios at fixed value of scaling variable instead of fixed rapidity.

## Key claim

The centre-of-mass energy dependence of inclusive W boson production cross sections at LHC energies can be described by a simple power law when expressed in terms of a suitable scaling variable, with the scaling exponent reflecting the small-x behaviour of parton distribution functions; this scaling holds approximately at leading order and is validated against NLO calculations and existing data, though the exponent is not exactly constant and shows some variation depending on lepton charge and kinematic region.

## Method

Starting from the leading-order expression for W production in the leptonic decay mode, the authors define a scaling variable xi_1 = (M_W / sqrt(s)) e^y for forward rapidity (y >> 0) and xi_2 = (M_W / sqrt(s)) e^{-y} for backward rapidity (y << 0). They assume the small-x sea-quark densities at Q^2 = M_W^2 follow a power law x q(x) ~ x^{-alpha}, and show that at fixed xi the cross section scales as s^alpha. They test this scaling against full NLO calculations using the MCFM Monte Carlo code with CT10NLO PDFs, and compare with experimental data from D0, ATLAS, CMS, ALICE, and LHCb across multiple collision systems (p-p, p-anti-p, p-Pb, Pb-Pb) and centre-of-mass energies (1.96, 2.76, 5.02, 7, 8 TeV).

## Key numbers

* Scaling exponent extracted from NLO calculations at large xi_1,2 is approximately alpha_eff ~ 0.35, independent of lepton charge.
* The scaling exponent for W- production tends to be somewhat larger than for W+ at small xi_1,2 (midrapidity), with the difference Delta = alpha- - alpha+ > 0.
* Cross-section data from p-p at sqrt(s) = 7 and 8 TeV, p-Pb at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, and Pb-Pb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV are compared after scaling by (s/GeV^2)^{-0.40}.
* For the lepton charge asymmetry, the reference centre-of-mass energy chosen for the master plot is sqrt(s_ref) = 5.02 TeV.
* The LHCb collaboration reported ratios of cross sections integrated over rapidity interval 2 < y < 4.5 at sqrt(s) = 7 and 8 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of W production systematics at hadron colliders, building on fixed-order perturbative QCD calculations by Anastasiou (2003) and Catani (2009). The scaling laws provide a simple way to relate measurements across different collision systems (p-p, p-anti-p, p-Pb, Pb-Pb) and centre-of-mass energies, complementing the detailed PDF studies by the CTEQ and NNPDF collaborations (Lai 2010, Ball 2014). The results enable direct comparisons of data from different experiments (D0, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb, ALICE) and suggest that PDF uncertainties in cross-section ratios between nearby centre-of-mass energies can be reduced by taking ratios at fixed scaling variable instead of fixed rapidity, which could increase sensitivity to physics beyond the Standard Model.
