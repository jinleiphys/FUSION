# Electric dipole transitions in the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation at finite temperature

> Finite temperature results in various effects on the properties of nuclear structure and excitations of relevance for nuclear processes in hot stellar environments. Here we introduce the self-consistent finite temperature relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (FT-RQRPA) based on relativistic energy density functional with point coupling interaction for describing the temperature effects in electric dipole (E1) transitions. We perform a study of E1 excitations in the temperature range $T=$ 0-2 MeV for the selected closed- and open-shell nuclei ranging from $^{40}$Ca to $^{60}$Ca and $^{100}$Sn to $^{140}$Sn by including both thermal and pairing effects. The isovector giant dipole resonance strength is slightly modified for the considered range of temperature, while new low-energy peaks emerge for $E<$12 MeV with non-negligible strength in neutron-rich nuclei at high temperatures. The analysis of relevant two-quasiparticle configurations discloses how new excitation channels open due to thermal unblocking of states at finite temperature. The study also examines the isospin and temperature dependence of electric dipole polarizability ($α_D$), resulting in systematic increase in the values of $α_D$ with increasing temperature, with a more pronounced effect observed in neutron-rich nuclei. The FT-RQRPA introduced in this work will open perspectives for microscopic calculation of $γ$-ray strength functions at finite temperatures relevant for nuclear reaction studies.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the self-consistent finite temperature relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (FT-RQRPA) for electric dipole transitions, but the abstract's claim of describing temperature effects is qualified by the restriction to temperatures up to 2 MeV and the caveat that the isovector giant dipole resonance strength is only "slightly modified" over this range, while new low-energy peaks emerge only in neutron-rich nuclei at high temperatures.

## Method

The authors developed a fully self-consistent finite temperature relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (FT-RQRPA) based on the relativistic energy density functional with point coupling DD-PCX interaction, using the finite temperature Hartree-Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (FT-HBCS) framework for ground state properties. The FT-RQRPA matrix was diagonalized to obtain excitation energies and transition strengths for electric dipole (E1) transitions in even-even nuclei, with calculations performed for the isotopic chains 40-60Ca and 100-140Sn at temperatures from 0 to 2 MeV. The discrete FT-RQRPA spectrum was averaged with a Lorentzian of width 1.0 MeV, and the analysis included both non-energy-weighted (m0) and energy-weighted (m1) moments of the strength function.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: T = 0 to 2 MeV for all nuclei
- Critical temperatures for pairing phase transition: 44Ca at 0.862 MeV, 52Ca at 0.528 MeV, 56Ca at 0.743 MeV, 108Sn at 0.872 MeV, 116Sn at 0.834 MeV, 124Sn at 0.764 MeV, 140Sn at 0.644 MeV
- Low-energy region defined as E < 12 MeV for Ca isotopes and E < 10 MeV for Sn isotopes
- High-energy region defined as E > 12 MeV for Ca isotopes and E > 10 MeV for Sn isotopes
- For 56Ca at T = 0 MeV, the main high-energy E1 peak is at 17.76 MeV with total B(E1) = 2.463 fm2; at T = 2 MeV, the peak shifts to 17.59 MeV with total B(E1) = 1.760 fm2
- New low-energy excitations in 56Ca at T = 2 MeV appear at energies including 1.88 MeV (B(E1) = 0.022 fm2), 2.19 MeV (B(E1) = 0.122 fm2), 2.27 MeV (B(E1) = 0.064 fm2), 2.42 MeV (B(E1) = 0.054 fm2), 2.73 MeV (B(E1) = 0.014 fm2), 2.96 MeV (B(E1) = 0.124 fm2), 3.26 MeV (B(E1) = 0.212 fm2), 3.42 MeV (B(E1) = 0.018 fm2), 3.46 MeV (B(E1) = 0.042 fm2), 3.59 MeV (B(E1) = 0.045 fm2), 4.18 MeV (B(E1) = 0.020 fm2), and 4.42 MeV (B(E1) = 0.015 fm2)
- Quasiparticle cut-off energy for configuration space: Ecut = 100 MeV
- 20 oscillator shells used in FT-HBCS calculations

## Context

This work extends the finite temperature relativistic RPA (FT-RRPA) based on meson-exchange interaction developed by Niu (2017) and the finite temperature quasiparticle RPA (FT-QRPA) based on Skyrme functionals by Yuksel (2017, 2019), while also going beyond the finite temperature relativistic time blocking approximation (FT-RTBA) by Litvinova (2013, 2017) which was limited to closed-shell nuclei. The new FT-RQRPA framework enables microscopic calculation of gamma-ray strength functions at finite temperatures for both closed- and open-shell nuclei, which is relevant for nuclear reaction studies in hot stellar environments and for the ongoing first experimental study of pygmy dipole strength in Ni isotopes at temperatures up to approximately 2 MeV by Wieland (2023).
