# Theoretical Modeling of J/psi Yield Modifications in Proton (Deuteron) - Nucleus Collisions at High Energy

> Understanding the detailed production and hadronization mechanisms for heavy quarkonia and their modification in a nuclear environment presents one of the major challenges in QCD. Calculations including nuclear-modified parton distribution functions (nPDFs) and fitting of break-up cross sections (sigma_breakup) as parameters have been successful at describing many features of J/psi modification in proton(deuteron)-nucleus collisions. In this paper, we extend these calculations to explore different geometric dependencies of the modification and confront them with new experimental results from the PHENIX experiment. We find that no combination of nPDFs and sigma_breakup, regardless of the nPDF parameter set and the assumed geometric dependence, can simultaneously describe the entire rapidity and centrality dependence of J/psi modifications in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. We also compare the data with coherence calculations and find them unable to describe the full rapidity and centrality dependence as well. We discuss how these calculations might be extended and further tested, in addition to discussing other physics mechanisms including initial-state parton energy loss.

## Key claim

No combination of nuclear-modified parton distribution functions (nPDFs) and breakup cross section (sigma_breakup), regardless of the nPDF parameter set and the assumed geometric dependence (linear, exponential, or quadratic), can simultaneously describe the entire rapidity and centrality dependence of J/psi modifications in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors employed a Monte Carlo Glauber model with full event-by-event fluctuations to simulate d+Au collisions, incorporating the EPS09 nPDF parameter set with 31 Hessian basis variations and a factorized breakup cross section (sigma_br) for the J/psi precursor traversing the nucleus. They tested three geometric dependencies for the nPDF modification (linear, exponential, and quadratic functions of the density-weighted longitudinal thickness) and used a modified-chi-squared fit to the PHENIX R_CP data (0-20% / 60-88% centrality) to find the best combination of EPS09 set and sigma_br value.

## Key numbers

* The best fit for the linear geometric dependence used EPS09 nPDF parameter set 17 with sigma_br = 3.2 mb, yielding a modified-chi-squared of 41.5 (fit probability less than 10^-4).
* The best fit for the exponential geometric dependence used EPS09 set 17 with sigma_br = 4.2 mb, yielding a modified-chi-squared of 50.6.
* The best fit for the quadratic geometric dependence used EPS09 set 30 with sigma_br = 3.4 mb, yielding a modified-chi-squared of 46.3.
* For collisions near the center of the gold nucleus (r_T approximately 0), the average number of target nucleons within a tube of radius 2 x 0.87 fm was approximately 20 with an RMS of approximately 5.
* The PHENIX experimental data include a global scale uncertainty of plus or minus 7.8% and an additional 8.2% global scale uncertainty for the R_CP ratio.

## Context

This work extends the factorized nPDF-plus-breakup framework previously used by Lourenco (2008), Adare (2007), Vogt (2004), and Kharzeev (1996) by incorporating full geometric fluctuations and testing multiple geometric dependencies for the nPDF modification. The authors find that this standard picture fails to describe the PHENIX data (Adare 2010), and they also compare with coherence calculations that similarly fail. The results motivate further theoretical work including initial-state parton energy loss and more sophisticated treatments of J/psi production kinematics, and they highlight the need for better constraints on low-x gluon nPDFs, particularly for future p+A and A+A measurements at the Large Hadron Collider.
