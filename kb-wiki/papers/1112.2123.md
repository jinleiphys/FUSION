# Meson-exchange currents and quasielastic antineutrino cross sections in the SuperScaling Approximation

> We evaluate quasielastic double-differential antineutrino cross sections obtained in a phenomenological model based on the superscaling behavior of electron scattering data and estimate the contribution of the vector meson-exchange currents in the 2p-2h sector. We show that the impact of meson-exchange currents for charge-changing antineutrino reactions is much larger than in the neutrino case.

## Key claim

The impact of vector meson-exchange currents (MEC) in the 2p-2h sector on charge-changing antineutrino cross sections is much larger than in the neutrino case, because the vector-axial (VA) interference is destructive for antineutrinos (reducing the quasielastic contribution) while it is constructive for neutrinos.

## Method

The authors applied the SuperScaling Approach (SuSA) model, which uses scaling functions extracted from inclusive electron scattering data, to compute quasielastic (QE) double-differential antineutrino cross sections on carbon-12. They then added the contribution from 2p-2h meson-exchange currents in the transverse vector sector, obtained from a relativistic model, and integrated the results over the MiniBooNE antineutrino flux. The calculations were performed for various bins of the muon scattering angle and kinetic energy, and the results were compared with the corresponding neutrino cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For the angular bin \(0.8 < \cos \theta_\mu < 0.9\), the 2p-2h MEC contribute about 38% to the total antineutrino cross section at the peak.
- For the angular bin \(0 < \cos \theta_\mu < 0.1\), the MEC contribution rises to about 44% at the peak.
- At an antineutrino energy \(E_{\overline\nu_\mu}= 1\) GeV, the MEC increase the total cross section by 60%.
- At an antineutrino energy \(E_{\overline\nu_\mu}= 2\) GeV, the MEC increase the total cross section by 70%.
- The mean energy of the MiniBooNE antineutrino flux is \(\langle E_{\overline\nu_\mu}\rangle\simeq 0.66\) GeV, compared to \(\langle E_{\nu_\mu}\rangle\simeq 0.79\) GeV for neutrinos.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA model with MEC, previously applied to neutrino scattering by Amaro et al. (2010, 2011), to the antineutrino case. It addresses the puzzle raised by MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2010) that the measured charged-current quasielastic cross section is larger than predictions from the Relativistic Fermi Gas model and impulse approximation calculations. The results suggest that measurements of both neutrino and antineutrino reactions on different nuclear targets could provide a stringent test of the 2p-2h MEC modeling and help disentangle the QE and MEC contributions.
