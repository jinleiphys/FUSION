# Competition between Normal Superfluidity and Larkin-Ovchinnikov Phases of Polarized Fermi Gases in Elongated Traps

> By applying the recently proposed antisymmetric superfluid local density approximation (ASLDA) to strongly interacting polarized atomic gases at unitarity in very elongated traps, we find families of Larkin-Ovchinnikov (LO) type of solutions with prominent transversal oscillation of pairing potential. These LO states coexist with a superfluid state having a smooth pairing potential. We suggest that the LO phase could be accessible experimentally by increasing adiabatically the trap aspect ratio. We show that the local asymmetry effects contained in ASLDA do not support a deformed superfluid core predicted by previous Bogoliubov-de Gennes treatments.

## Key claim

The authors find that in strongly interacting polarized Fermi gases at unitarity in very elongated traps, families of Larkin-Ovchinnikov (LO) type solutions with prominent transversal oscillation of the pairing potential coexist with a superfluid state having a smooth pairing potential; they suggest the LO phase could be accessed experimentally by increasing the trap aspect ratio adiabatically, but note that the local asymmetry effects contained in the antisymmetric superfluid local density approximation (ASLDA) do not support a deformed superfluid core predicted by previous Bogoliubov-de Gennes treatments.

## Method

The authors solved the self-consistent Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations of superfluid density functional theory (DFT) using the coordinate-space axial solver HFB-AX, based on B-splines, for spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases in elongated harmonic traps. They compared results from the standard superfluid local density approximation (SLDA) with those from the antisymmetric superfluid local density approximation (ASLDA), which incorporates local polarization effects via effective masses and Hartree potentials fitted to Monte Carlo data. The calculations used a finite energy cutoff and a local regularized pairing interaction, with initial pairing potentials parameterized as oscillatory functions along the trap axis to explore multiple self-consistent solutions.

## Key numbers

- For a system of 1000 atoms with polarization P=0.37 in a trap with aspect ratio eta=20, the ground-state SLDA solution has q=0, while excited LO-type solutions appear at q=1.05 and q=2.2; the trap excitation energy along the z-axis is Delta E_z = 1/(2*eta) = 0.025, approximately 0.5% of the Fermi energy.
- For 2000 polarized atoms with P=0.37 in a trap with eta=10, the LO wave numbers for ASLDA solutions evolved to higher aspect ratios are q_LO = 1.32 for eta=20, q_LO = 1.18 for eta=30, and q_LO = 1.13 for eta=40.
- The energy cutoff was 35 in SLDA and 25 in ASLDA (in trap units where hbar=m=omega=1), with the condition E_c > 5*lambda_up.

## Context

This work extends previous DFT-based studies of polarized Fermi gases, specifically the SLDA approach of Bulgac (2003, 2007) and the ASLDA formalism of Bulgac (2008), which found evidence for a stable LO phase in the thermodynamic limit. It addresses the conflicting experimental results from the Rice experiment (Partridge, 2006) which observed a spherical distortion of the superfluid core in a trap with aspect ratio ~50, and the MIT experiment (Shin, 2006) with aspect ratio ~5, as well as later experiments (Salomon, 2009) with aspect ratio ~22. The results enable future experimental searches for the LO phase by adiabatically increasing trap elongation, and suggest that multireference DFT beyond the BdG level could mix nearly degenerate solutions to lower the energy of the LO state relative to the ground state.
