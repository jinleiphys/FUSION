# Exact mapping from the $(3+1)$-dimensional Skyrme model to the $(1+1)$-dimensional sine-Gordon theory and some applications

> A remarkable exact mapping, valid for low-enough energy scales and close to a sharp boundary distribution of hadronic matter, from the $(3+1)$-dimensional Skyrme model to the sine-Gordon theory in $(1+1)$ dimensions in the attractive regime is explicitly constructed. Besides the intrinsic theoretical interest to be able to describe the prototype of nonintegrable theories (namely, quantum chromodynamics in the infrared regime) in terms of the prototype of integrable relativistic field theories (namely, sine-Gordon theory in $(1+1)$ dimensions), we will show that this mapping can be extremely useful to analyze both equilibrium and out-of-equilibrium features of baryonic distributions in a cavity.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an exact mapping from the (3+1)-dimensional Skyrme model to the (1+1)-dimensional sine-Gordon theory, but this mapping is only valid for low-enough energy scales (below the scale 1/L) and close to a sharp boundary distribution of hadronic matter where the energy and baryon densities are homogeneous in two spatial directions.

## Method

The authors consider the SU(2) Skyrme model in a box-shaped cavity with metric ds² = -dt² + dx² + L²(d𝔶² + d𝔷²), where Lx is the length in the x-direction and L sets the transverse size. They impose an Ansatz where the Euler-angle fields F and G are linear in the homogeneous coordinates 𝔶 and 𝔷, while the profile H depends only on time t and the longitudinal coordinate x. This reduces the three coupled nonlinear Skyrme field equations to a single sine-Gordon equation in (1+1) dimensions for the rescaled field φ = (4/β)H, with effective coupling constants M₀ and β derived from the original Skyrme parameters and the box geometry.

## Key numbers

- The effective sine-Gordon coupling constant is β = 2 / [π √(K(2L² + Bλ))], where K and λ are the Skyrme couplings and B = p² is the baryon number squared
- The effective mass parameter is M₀ = π²K B²λ / (8L²)
- The condition for the attractive regime (which always holds) is 1/[π³K(2L² + Bλ)] < 1, and since Kλ ≈ 1/6 and π³ > 27, the effective theory is always in the attractive phase
- The number of breathers 1/ξ = (1-β²)/β² is always bigger than two for baryon number B ≥ 4
- The baryon density for the Ansatz is ρ_B = J₀ = -3 B (∂_x H) sin(2H)

## Context

This work extends the analytic techniques developed by Canfora and collaborators (2018-2022) for describing nonhomogeneous baryonic condensates at finite density, which previously allowed analytic descriptions of baryonic layers. The mapping enables importing exact results from integrable sine-Gordon theory, such as those on entanglement entropy dynamics after quantum quenches (Calabrese and Cardy, 2016-2019) and finite-temperature correlation functions (Koch and collaborators, 2023), into the nonintegrable context of low-energy QCD. This opens the possibility of obtaining exact analytic predictions for out-of-equilibrium properties of baryonic distributions in cavities, including the time evolution of von Neumann and Renyi entropies, which display undamped oscillations with frequencies determined by the sine-Gordon breather spectrum.
