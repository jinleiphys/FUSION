# Parton Equilibration in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the processes leading to phase-space equilibration of parton distributions in nuclear interactions at collider energies. We derive a set of rate equations describing the chemical equilibration of gluons and quarks including medium effects on the relevant QCD transport coefficients, and discuss their consequences for parton equilibration in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper derives a parameter-free set of rate equations, based on perturbative QCD in a dense partonic medium, that describes the evolution of quark and gluon distributions toward chemical equilibrium in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but only after an initial phase where arbitrary cut-off parameters are still required for primary semi-hard parton scattering.

## Method
The authors used the HIJING Monte Carlo model to estimate initial minijet production and parton densities, then derived rate equations for chemical equilibration of gluons and quarks via the dominant processes \(gg \leftrightarrow ggg\) and \(gg \leftrightarrow q\bar{q}\). They incorporated medium effects such as Debye screening and the Landau-Pomeranchuk suppression of gluon radiation, and solved the rate equations numerically assuming longitudinal (Bjorken) scaling expansion with a thermal but not chemically equilibrated initial state.

## Key numbers
- Initial gluon density at RHIC: \(n_0 = 2.4\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and initial energy density \(\varepsilon_0 = 3.5\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\) (gluons only); total initial energy density including quarks estimated at \(5\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\).
- Initial gluon density at LHC: \(n_0 = 23\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and initial energy density \(\varepsilon_0 = 52\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\) (gluons only); total initial energy density estimated at \(70\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\).
- Time to achieve local momentum isotropy: \(t_{\text{iso}} = 0.31\ \text{fm}/c\) at RHIC and \(t_{\text{iso}} = 0.23\ \text{fm}/c\) at LHC.
- Mean free path of gluons at isotropization: \(\Lambda_f = 0.4\ \text{fm}\) (RHIC) and \(\Lambda_f = 0.25\ \text{fm}\) (LHC), assuming \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).
- Perturbative parton plasma lifetime: \(1-2\ \text{fm}/c\) at RHIC and \(5-6\ \text{fm}/c\) at LHC.
- Analytical fit for gluon equilibration rate: \(R_3 = 2.1 \alpha_s^2 T (2\lambda_g - \lambda_g^2)^{1/2}\).
- Light quark production rate: \(R_2 \approx 0.064 N_f \alpha_s^2 \lambda_g T (\ln(7.5/\lambda_g))^2\) for \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).

## Context
This work extends the parton cascade model simulations of Geiger and Kapusta (1991) and others by providing analytic rate equations that clarify the dependence of equilibration time scales on parameters and model assumptions. It builds on the earlier study by Biró, Müller, and Wang (1992) which showed that screening effects may provide natural infrared cut-offs, and incorporates the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect as described by Gyulassy and Wang (1993). The results enable a parameter-free description of the approach to a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma after the initial semi-hard scattering phase, and suggest that the scenario is uncertain at RHIC energies but very likely at LHC energies.
