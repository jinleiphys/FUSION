# Quantum decoherence of hyperon spin correlations in QCD hadronization

> Hadronization, the transition of quarks and gluons into hadrons, lies beyond the reach of perturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and is commonly described by the semiclassical Lund string model. Yet this very success raises a fundamental question: where does the quantumness go during hadronization? In this Letter, we propose an approach inspired by quantum information science, in which (i) quark-antiquark pairs excited from the QCD vacuum inherit its quantum numbers, giving rise to spin entanglement at their creation, and (ii) subsequent string breaking generates environmental degrees of freedom that induce quantum decoherence of the spin state. This framework simultaneously describes the $Λ$ hyperon spin-correlation data measured at RHIC [Nature 650, 65-71 (2026)] and at the LHC, establishing a quantitative connection between the QCD vacuum, spin entanglement and decoherence, and hadronization.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the observed spin correlations of Lambda hyperon pairs arise from quantum entanglement of strange quark-antiquark pairs excited from the QCD vacuum, with subsequent string breaking introducing environmental degrees of freedom that cause quantum decoherence; the framework simultaneously describes data from both RHIC and LHC, but the quantitative connection relies on phenomenological parameters including a feed-down dilution factor of 1/3 and a scaling relation for the decoherence strength based on charged-hadron yields.

## Method
The authors construct a Vacuum Spin Chain (VSC) model where single s\bar{s} pairs excited from the QCD vacuum carry J^{PC}=0^{++} and form a maximally entangled Bell state, while two-pair ss\bar{s}\bar{s} channels are organized into diquark-antidiquark configurations with two allowed color-spin configurations. Decoherence is modeled as a "Witness effect" where environmental hadrons produced during string breaking suppress off-diagonal density matrix elements, with the decoherence factor taking an exponential form exp(-k* Delta R) where k* scales with charged-hadron multiplicity per unit pseudorapidity. The model is fitted to STAR Lambda\bar{Lambda} spin correlation data at 200 GeV and CMS preliminary data at 13 TeV in proton-proton collisions.

## Key numbers
- Spin correlation for single s\bar{s} pair: P^{(1)}_{s\bar{s}} = 1/3
- Spin correlation for ss (or \bar{s}\bar{s}) in color-symmetric configuration (a): P^{(2)}_{ss(\bar{s}\bar{s}),(a)} = -1
- Spin correlation for s\bar{s} in color-symmetric configuration (a): P^{(2)}_{s\bar{s},(a)} = 0
- Spin correlation for ss (or \bar{s}\bar{s}) in color-antisymmetric configuration (b): P^{(2)}_{ss(\bar{s}\bar{s}),(b)} = 1/3
- Spin correlation for s\bar{s} in color-antisymmetric configuration (b): P^{(2)}_{s\bar{s},(b)} = -2/3
- Feed-down dilution factor: A_{fd} = 1/3
- Decoherence factor at RHIC (STAR): k* = 0.53 +/- 0.29
- One-/two-pair fraction from STAR fit: F = 1.0 +/- 0.72
- Color-configuration fraction from STAR fit: f = 0.93 +/- 0.51
- Strange hadron yields in pp at 200 GeV: dN/dy(K_S^0) = 0.134 +/- 0.011, dN/dy(Lambda + \bar{Lambda}) = 0.0834 +/- 0.0056
- Strange hadron yields in pp at 13 TeV: dN/dy(K_S^0) = 0.3192 +/- 0.0111, dN/dy(Lambda + \bar{Lambda}) = 0.1807 +/- 0.0102

## Context
This work extends the Lund string model (Andersson 1983) by introducing quantum information concepts to address the question of how classical probabilistic behavior emerges from quantum dynamics during hadronization. It builds on simulations of string breaking in the (1+1)-dimensional Schwinger model (Grieninger 2025) that revealed strong quantum entanglement in color flux tubes, and directly describes the Lambda hyperon spin correlation data from STAR (2025) and CMS (preliminary). The framework enables quantitative predictions for decoherence effects across different collision energies and provides a new experimental handle on the quantum-to-classical transition during hadronization through the Delta R dependence of spin correlations.
