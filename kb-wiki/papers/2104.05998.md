# Investigating high energy proton proton collisions with a multi-phase transport model approach based on PYTHIA8 initial conditions

> The striking resemblance of high multiplicity proton-proton (pp) collisions at the LHC to heavy ion collisions challenges our conventional wisdom on the formation of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). A consistent explanation of the collectivity phenomena in pp will help us to understand the mechanism that leads to the QGP-like signals in small systems. In this study, we introduce a transport model approach connecting the initial conditions provided by PYTHIA8 with subsequent AMPT rescatterings to study the collective behavior in high energy pp collisions. The multiplicity dependence of light hadron productions from this model is in reasonable agreement with the pp $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV experimental data. It is found in the comparisons that both the partonic and hadronic final state interactions are important for the generation of the radial flow feature of the pp transverse momentum spectra. The study also shows that the long range two particle azimuthal correlation in high multiplicity pp events is sensitive to the proton sub-nucleon spatial fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a transport model that connects PYTHIA8 initial conditions with AMPT final-state rescatterings to study collective behavior in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, finding that both partonic and hadronic final-state interactions are necessary to reproduce the multiplicity-dependent transverse momentum spectra and that the long-range two-particle azimuthal correlation is sensitive to proton sub-nucleon spatial fluctuations.

## Method

The authors generate initial conditions for pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8 with the Angantyr model, then apply the AMPT string melting mechanism to convert excited strings into constituent partons. The parton system undergoes partonic rescatterings via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) with elastic two-body scattering, followed by hadronization through a spatial quark coalescence model and final-state hadronic rescatterings via the ART model. Two proton geometry models are tested for initial transverse spatial distributions: an overlap function weighting method based on an exponential matter distribution, and a constituent quark Glauber Monte Carlo method with three constituent quarks per proton.

## Key numbers

- Parton rescattering cross sections tested: 0 mb, 0.2 mb, and 3 mb in ZPC
- Constituent scattering cross section σ_cc = 25.2 mb, reproducing the inelastic pp cross section at √s = 13 TeV
- Exponential matter distribution parameter R = 0.2 fm for the proton spatial density
- Baryon-to-meson ratio parameter r_BM = 0.53 for the quark coalescence model
- Energy density above the critical value (~1 GeV/fm³) within t < 1 fm/c for central pp collisions at b = 0 fm
- Maximum impact parameter from PYTHIA/Angantyr rises up to 5 fm for pp at √s = 13 TeV
- Parton number in full phase space can exceed 600 in high-multiplicity events (N_parton > 600)

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model framework (Lin 2004) by replacing HIJING initial conditions with PYTHIA8/Angantyr, enabling a more realistic treatment of multiple parton interactions and sub-nucleon geometry in small systems. It addresses limitations of previous approaches: the full AMPT model could not easily reproduce multiplicity-dependent transverse momentum spectra until recent improvements (Zhang 2021), while PYTHIA-based models with color reconnection or rope hadronization (Bierlich 2015, 2017) struggle to reproduce dynamical correlations like the near-side ridge. The approach enables simultaneous description of pT spectra and multi-particle correlations in pp collisions, paving the way for further investigations of azimuthal anisotropy in small systems.
