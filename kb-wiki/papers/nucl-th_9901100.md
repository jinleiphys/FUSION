# Proton Single Particle Energy Shifts due to Coulomb Correlations

> A theoretically consistent approach to the calculation of the Coulomb correlation corrections to the single--particle energies is presented. New contributions to the single--particle energies previously overlooked in the literature are now identified and taken into account. We show that the interplay between the Coulomb interaction and the strong interaction, which is enhanced in the nuclear surface, leads to an upward shift of the proton single--particle levels. This shift affects the position of the calculated proton drip line, a shift towards decreasing $Z$. We describe briefly a similar mechanism which is at work for neutron levels. The same mechanism is responsible for significant corrections to the mass difference of the mirror nuclei (Nolen--Schiffer anomaly) and to the effective proton mass.

## Key claim

The interplay between the Coulomb interaction and the strong nuclear interaction, which is enhanced in the nuclear surface region, leads to an upward shift of proton single-particle levels by 0.1–0.3 MeV, affecting the calculated proton drip line position by shifting it towards decreasing Z; the authors identify a new diagrammatic contribution to this shift that was previously overlooked in the literature.

## Method

The authors use density functional theory with a Skyrme-type functional for the strong interaction and decompose the Coulomb energy functional into Hartree, Fock, and correlation terms. They compute the proton single-particle energy shift by taking the functional derivative of the Coulomb correlation energy with respect to occupation numbers, solving the resulting RPA-type equations with a separable residual interaction model for the particle-hole interaction in spherical nuclei. The calculations include collective modes with multipolarities up to approximately 2A^(1/3) and energies up to approximately 225 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Calculated proton single-particle energy shifts Δε_πl are in the interval 0.1–0.3 MeV for light (A=16) and medium (A=40–48) nuclei
- The Coulomb correlation energy is estimated as approximately 0.4 × Z MeV, leading to a typical single-particle shift of approximately 0.4 MeV
- The Fayans parametrization uses ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, σ = 1/3, and h_Coul = 0.941 from a fit to 100 medium and heavy spherical nuclei
- An upward shift of 0.4 MeV for the last occupied proton level near the proton drip line is equivalent to shifting the drip line by a few units in Z
- A similar shift for a neutron level would change the mass number by up to 5 units

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration (Bulgac and Shaginyan, 1996) that a specific many-body mechanism enhances Coulomb energy in the nuclear surface region, accounting for a major part of the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in mirror nuclei binding energies. The approach contrasts with earlier treatments by Auerbach and others that considered only two of the three diagrams of the same order in e², and with the phenomenological Skyrme-type functionals of Brown et al. (1999) that can fit the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly but lack unique theoretical underpinning. The results enable more theoretically grounded nuclear density functionals and suggest that the proton effective mass becomes smaller than the neutron effective mass due to Coulomb correlations, with implications for the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and proton drip line predictions.
