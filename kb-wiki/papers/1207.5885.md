# On the bound state of the antiproton-deuterium-tritium ion

> The properties of the weakly-bound $S(L = 0)-$state in the $\bar{p}dt$ ion are investigated with the use of the results of highly accurate computations. The hyperfine structure splitting of this ion is investigated. We also evaluate the life-time of the $\bar{p}dt$ ion against the nuclear $(d,t)-$fusion and discuss a possibility to evaluate the corresponding annihilation rate(s).

## Key claim

The $\overline{p}dt$ ion has only one bound $S(L=0)$ state, which is weakly-bound (binding energy to total energy ratio $\tau < 0.01$), but the authors explicitly ignore all contributions from strong interactions between antiproton, deuteron, and triton, stating that these will be included in a future study.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for the Coulomb three-body $\overline{p}dt$ system using an exponential variational expansion in relative coordinates with up to 3840 basis functions. Non-linear parameters were optimized at dimensions of 1800 and 2200, then the basis size was increased to 3500–3840 without further optimization. Expectation values of interparticle distances, delta-functions, and correlation functions were computed from the resulting highly accurate wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy ratio $\tau = \varepsilon / E \ll 1$; for weakly-bound states in Coulomb few-body systems $\tau$ must be less than 0.01 (1%)
- Interparticle distances in the ground state are approximately $2.5 - 2.6 \times 10^{-12}$ cm, which is about 20–30 times larger than nucleon-nucleon distances in light nuclei
- The antiproton annihilation rate at the triton is approximately 5.86213 times more likely than annihilation at the deuteron (assuming $\xi = 1$)
- Hyperfine structure splittings between the four groups of spin states: $1.112524269 \times 10^8$ MHz, $6.853155051 \times 10^9$ MHz, and $5.977402022 \times 10^7$ MHz
- Life-time against nuclear $(d,t)$-fusion: $\tau_f \approx 4 \times 10^{-14} - 1 \times 10^{-13}$ sec (fusion rate $\approx 1 \times 10^{13} - 2.5 \times 10^{13}$ sec$^{-1}$)
- Probability of forming a bound $\overline{p}{}^4$He system after fusion is approximately 5%

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Frolov and collaborators (FrBi, FrTa) that established the $\overline{p}dt$ ion is bound, and it provides the first highly accurate variational computation of its ground state properties. The $\overline{p}dt$ ion is unique among Coulomb three-body systems with unit charges because it contains three heavy particles yet has very small binding energy. The results enable future inclusion of strong interaction potentials and provide predictions for hyperfine structure that can be tested experimentally, as well as a method to measure the ratio of neutron-antiproton to proton-antiproton annihilation rates at zero energy.
