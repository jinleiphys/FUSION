# Photon and dilepton spectra from nonlinear QED effects in supercritical magnetic fields induced by heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss properties of photons in extremely strong magnetic fields induced by the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We investigate the vacuum birefringence, the real-photon decay, and the photon splitting which are all forbidden in the ordinary vacuum, but become possible in strong magnetic fields. These effects potentially give rise to anisotropies in photon and dilepton spectra.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nonlinear QED effects (vacuum birefringence, real-photon decay, and photon splitting), which are forbidden in ordinary vacuum, become possible in the supercritical magnetic fields generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and these effects could produce observable anisotropies in photon and dilepton spectra. The authors hedge that these effects are "potential" sources of anisotropy, and that the regime of large photon momentum and strong field relevant to heavy-ion collisions had not been fully covered by prior general results.

## Method

The authors compute the resummed polarization tensor in a strong external magnetic field using the proper-time method, obtaining analytic expressions in terms of associated Laguerre polynomials and Landau-level sums. They then extract the refractive index from the pole of the resummed photon propagator, focusing on the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation to obtain explicit forms for the dielectric constant and its real and imaginary parts. For photon splitting, they evaluate the triangle diagram with resummed propagators, showing that the leading contribution in the strong-field limit comes from configurations with two LLL fermions and one higher-Landau-level fermion.

## Key numbers

- The critical field strength is given as \(B_c = m^2/e\).
- The real part of the refractive index is \(\sim 1.4\) for \(B/B_c = 500\), which is compared to air (1.0003) and water (1.333).
- The imaginary part of the refractive index is shown for \(B/B_c = 500\) and photon energy \(\omega^2/(4m^2) = 3\), with the decay rate largest at propagation angles \(\sim \pm \pi/4, \pm 3\pi/4\).
- The photon-splitting amplitude in the strong-field limit is of order \(\mathcal{O}(e^3 (eB)^0)\), indicating the rate is independent of the magnetic field strength.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of QED in strong magnetic fields, which had been applied to astrophysical contexts such as magnetars (e.g., work by Harding and Lai, and by Baring) and intense-laser physics, to the regime of heavy-ion collisions. The authors note that earlier attempts at the exact resummed polarization tensor were missing for decades (citing earlier work by KRDG), and their general result covers the full parameter space of field strength and photon momentum. The paper enables future calculations of anisotropic photon and dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, potentially providing a new probe of early-time dynamics.
