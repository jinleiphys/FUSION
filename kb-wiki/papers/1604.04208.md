# Understanding the shortened lifetime of \boldmath$\isotope[3][Λ]{H}$\unboldmath

> The lifetime of the hypertriton $\isotope[3][Λ]{H}$ has been recently measured as significantly shorter than that of the free $Λ$. We present an explanation based on a change of the intrinsic hyperon decay due to the nuclear environment.

# Understanding the shortened lifetime of \(^3_{\Lambda}\text{H}\)

## Key claim

The shortened lifetime of the hypertriton \(^3_{\Lambda}\text{H}\) relative to the free \(\Lambda\) is explained by a breakdown of the fine-tuned cancellation between two pole terms in the weak decay amplitude, which occurs because the nuclear environment (spectator nucleons) affects those pole terms differently. The mechanism is demonstrated for light hypernuclei, not requiring heavy nuclear systems.

## Method

The authors calculate the hadronic weak decay of \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^\pm\) hyperons using a constituent quark model (CQM) with baryon internal conversion (pole) diagrams, where the weak transition operator contains a \(\delta(\vec{r}_1-\vec{r}_2)\) contact interaction sensitive to short-distance quark structure. They then extend this to the hypertriton decay \(^3_{\Lambda}\text{H} \to ^3\text{He} + \pi^-\) using the impulse approximation with harmonic-oscillator wavefunctions for the three-body nuclei, where the intermediate \([p,n,n]\) and \([p,n,\Sigma^+]\) states are treated with explicit anti-symmetrization and nonrelativistic propagators. Parameters for the strong couplings (\(C_{B_i B_f\pi}\)) and the harmonic-oscillator strength (\(\alpha_h\)) are fitted to experimental hyperon decay widths.

## Key numbers

* Free \(\Lambda\) lifetime: \(\tau(\Lambda) = (2.63 \pm 0.020) \times 10^{-10}\) s (from Agashe:2014kda)
* Fitted harmonic-oscillator strength: \(\alpha_h = 305.12 \pm 0.75\) MeV
* Fitted SU(3) symmetry-breaking parameters: \(C_{NN\pi} = 0.843 \pm 0.001\), \(C_{\Lambda\Sigma\pi} = 1.400 \pm 0.086\), \(C_{\Sigma\Sigma\pi} = 1.128 \pm 0.002\)
* Partial width for \(\Lambda \to p\pi^-\): \((1.62^{+0.50}_{-0.43}) \times 10^{-6}\) eV (fitted), compared to experimental \((1.60 \pm 0.02) \times 10^{-6}\) eV
* Partial width for \(^3_{\Lambda}\text{H} \to ^3\text{He} + \pi^-\): \(2.18 \times 10^{-6}\) eV (Jülich model), larger than the free \(\Lambda\) partial width
* Estimated two-body decay width of \(^3_{\Lambda}\text{H}\): \(3.27 \times 10^{-6}\) eV
* Predicted hypertriton lifetime: \(\tau(^3_{\Lambda}\text{H}) \simeq 2.0 \times 10^{-10}\) s (200 ps), with error \(\pm 23\) ps from varying \(\beta_\Lambda\) by 10%
* Experimental hypertriton lifetimes compared: \(217^{+19}_{-16}\) ps (Rappold:2014jqa), \(183^{+42}_{-32}\pm 37\) ps (Rappold:2013fic), \(181^{+54}_{-39}\pm 33\) ps (Adam:2015yta), \(155^{+25}_{-22}\pm 29\) ps (STAR)

## Context

This work extends the understanding of hypernuclear weak decay by identifying a novel mechanism for lifetime shortening in light hypernuclei, in contrast to earlier studies such as Kamada (1997) which assumed a frozen weak decay vertex and found little departure from the free \(\Lambda\) case. The authors build on their previous three-baryon calculation (Richard:2014pwa) and use nuclear wavefunctions from the Jülich (Polinder:2007mp, Haidenbauer:2013oca) and Nijmegen potential models. The mechanism enables future refinements with more realistic wavefunctions (e.g., superpositions of Gaussians) and can be extended to other light hypernuclei such as \(^4_{\Lambda}\text{H}\).
