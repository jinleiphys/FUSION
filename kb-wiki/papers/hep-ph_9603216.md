# Quantisation of Global Isospin in the Skyrme Crystal

> A quantisation of chiral symmetry within the Skyrme crystal is carried out. The definition of global isospin in the crystal is explored, and found to be ambiguous. However, the state corresponding to a neutron crystal is identified, and the leading quantum correction to the classical mass is computed. The results are compared to those of Klebanov for a crystal of whole skyrmions.

## Key claim

The paper identifies that global isospin in the Skyrme crystal is ambiguously defined because the high-density half-skyrmion phase may correspond to restored chiral symmetry, so that the energy spectrum depends on which field is assigned to the trivial irreducible representation of the cubic point group; however, the energy of a neutron crystal is uniquely defined in the infinite-volume limit.

## Method

The author performs a semi-classical quantisation of chiral SO(4) symmetry for the minimum-energy Skyrme crystal (a simple cubic lattice of half-skyrmions), using analytic field approximations from Kugler and Shtrikman (1990) and Castillejo et al. (1989). The isospin inertia tensor is computed numerically per unit cell, its symmetry constraints are derived from the cubic point groups, and the Hamiltonian is quantised canonically in terms of left and right SU(2) operators. Two different choices of diagonal subgroup (assigning either the sigma field or a pion field to the trivial irrep) are compared, and the infinite-crystal limit is taken for the neutron crystal state.

## Key numbers

- Classical energy minimum per unit cell: 491.81 (dimensionless units); after quantisation of global isospin, the minimum rises to 513.84 per unit cell, a difference of approximately 4%.
- In physical units (with Skyrme parameters fitted to nucleon and delta masses, zero pion mass), the energy difference is 32 MeV; the length unit is 0.561 fm and hbar = 59.4 in the paper's units.
- The lattice parameter at the minimum increases from 2.35 to 2.54 (dimensionless units), corresponding to a density decrease from 0.218 baryons/fm^3 (classical) to 0.173 baryons/fm^3 (quantised), a reduction of almost 25%.
- The principal moments of inertia per unit cell as functions of lattice parameter L: A = 6.6667 L^3 + 69.4944 L, B = 9.3333 L^3 + 74.9876 L.
- The energy per baryon for an infinite neutron crystal is E/B = (1/4) M_cl + hbar^2/(2B), where M_cl is the classical mass per unit cell and B is the relevant moment of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical quantisation approach of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) to the minimum-energy Skyrme crystal, which was first studied by Klebanov (1985) for a simple cubic array of whole skyrmions. The paper addresses the unresolved problem that skyrmion crystals predict too high a density for nuclear matter, and finds that the true Skyrme crystal (identified by Kugler and Shtrikman (1990) and Castillejo et al. (1989)) has both lower energy and lower density than Klebanov's crystal after quantisation, making it more likely to remain bound. The results motivate a full quantisation of the remaining 6N modes (soft isospin and vibrational waves), which the author notes is an extremely difficult but worthwhile problem.
