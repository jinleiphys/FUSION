# Vortex-nucleus interaction in the inner crust of neutron stars

> The structure of a vortex in the inner crust of neutron stars is calculated within the framework of quantum mean field theory taking into account the interaction with the nuclei composing the Coulomb lattice. Making use of the results obtained with different nuclear interactions, the pinning energy, relevant in the study of glitches, is worked out. Quantal size and density dependent effects are found to be important.

## Key claim

The pinning energy of a vortex to a nucleus in the inner crust of neutron stars is calculated using quantum mean-field theory, revealing that quantal finite-size effects and density-dependent pairing are important, but the results depend qualitatively on the chosen nuclear interaction (e.g., SkM* versus SLy4), and the study is limited to spherical nuclei at densities below n0/3, axial symmetry, and a single nucleus interacting with the vortex.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in a cylindrical box with reflecting walls, using Skyrme forces (SkM*, SLy4, SII, SGII) for the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force fitted to reproduce the pairing gap of neutron matter from the Argonne V18 interaction. They imposed axial symmetry and a vortex number ν=1, comparing two limiting configurations: a vortex axis passing through the center of a nucleus (pinned) and a vortex far from the nucleus (interstitial), with the pinning energy defined as the difference between the energy cost to build the vortex on a nucleus and in uniform neutron matter.

## Key numbers

* The inner crust density range studied is between 4 × 10^11 g/cm^3 (0.0014 n0) and 1.6 × 10^14 g/cm^3 (0.57 n0), where n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 = 2.8 × 10^14 g/cm^3 is nuclear saturation density.
* The cylindrical box used for HFB calculations has radius ρ_box = 30 fm and height h_box = 40 fm.
* The pairing force parameters are V0 = -481 MeV fm^3, η = 0.7, and α = 0.45, with an energy cutoff E_cut = 60 MeV.
* The average distance between vortices is of order 10^-3 cm for a star with angular velocity Ω ~ 1 ms^-1, from the Onsager-Feynman formula.
* The study assumes a fixed proton number Z = 40 for the nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic study of neutron star crusts initiated by Negele and Vautherin (1973) using Skyrme functionals and the Wigner-Seitz approximation, and it provides the first detailed quantum HFB calculation of vortex-nucleus interaction, going beyond semiclassical estimates by Epstein and Baym (1988) and Donati and Pizzochero (2004). The results are a starting point for understanding glitch phenomena in neutron stars, but the authors note limitations including the neglect of medium polarization effects on pairing (studied by Gori et al., 2004), restriction to spherical nuclei (excluding pasta phases at densities above n0/3), and the assumption of a single isolated nucleus rather than a full lattice.
