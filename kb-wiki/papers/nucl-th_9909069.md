# A Linear Approximation for the Excitation Energies of single and double analog states in the f_{7/2} shell

> We find that the excitation energies of single analog states for odd-even nuclei in the f$_{7/2}$ shell with J=j=7/2$^{-}$ and the J=0$^{+}$ double analog states in the even-even nuclei are well described by the formulas $E^{*}(j,T+1) = b (T+X)$ and $E^{*}(0^{+},T+2) = 2b (T+X+0.5)$,respectively, where $T=\mid N-Z\mid /2$ is usually the ground state isospin. It is remarkable to note that the parameter X accounts for the departures from the symmetry energy based predictions.

# A Linear Approximation for the Excitation Energies of Single and Double Analog States in the f_{7/2} Shell

## Key claim

The excitation energies of single analog states (J=j=7/2⁻) in odd-even nuclei and double analog states (J=0⁺) in even-even nuclei within the f_{7/2} shell follow linear formulas E*(S.A.) = b(T+X) and E*(D.A.) = 2b(T+X+0.5), where the parameter X accounts for departures from symmetry energy predictions; however, the fit is "very good but not perfect," and the values of b and X differ between single-j shell calculations (b=2.32 MeV, X=1.30) and experimental data (b=1.94 MeV, X=2.00 for titanium isotopes).

## Method

The authors performed single-j shell model calculations using the spectrum of ⁴²Sc to determine two-body matrix elements for the f_{7/2} orbital, then fitted linear formulas E*(S.A.) = b(T+X) and E*(D.A.) = 2b(T+X+0.5) to the calculated excitation energies of single analog and double analog states across multiple nuclei in the f_{7/2} shell. They compared these results to experimental data for titanium isotopes and compiled experimental excitation energies grouped by isospin T = |N-Z|/2.

## Key numbers

- Single-j shell fit parameters: b = 2.32 MeV, X = 1.30
- Experimental fit for titanium double analog states: b = 1.94 MeV, X = 2.00
- ⁴⁴Ti double analog state: experimental 9.340 MeV, linear formula 9.70 MeV
- ⁴⁶Ti double analog state: experimental 14.153 MeV, linear formula 13.58 MeV
- ⁴⁸Ti double analog state: experimental 17.379 MeV, linear formula 17.46 MeV
- ⁴⁵Sc and ⁴⁷Ti single analog states differ by only 10 keV in the single-j shell calculation (6.601 MeV vs 6.590 MeV)
- ⁴⁶Ti double analog (13.204 MeV) and ⁴⁷Ti single analog (6.590 MeV) yield ratio 2.0036 in single-j shell
- ⁵²Fe double analog state: 8.559 MeV (experimental)
- ⁴⁸Cr double analog state: 8.75 MeV (experimental)
- ⁴⁹Ti single analog state: 8.724 MeV (experimental)
- ⁵³Co single analog state: 4.390 MeV (experimental)

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-to-one relation between spectra of even-even and odd-A nuclei noted by McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1964) and further explored by Zheng and Zamick (1992) and Zamick and Devi (1999). The linear approximation successfully reproduces the two-to-one ratios for pairs such as (⁴⁴Ti, ⁴³Ti) and (⁴⁸Ti, ⁴⁹Ti) where the single-j shell also gives exact ratios, and approximately reproduces ratios for cases like (⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁷Ti) where the single-j shell does not give an exact two-to-one relation. The results enable future investigation of why the parameter X differs from unity, and motivate extended calculations incorporating configuration mixing, Coulomb interactions, and A-dependent parameters (with b tentatively proportional to 1/√A rather than the 1/A dependence of the symmetry energy in the semi-empirical mass formula).
