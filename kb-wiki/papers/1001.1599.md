# Effects of density and parametrization on scattering observables

> We calculate the density distribution of protons and neutrons for $^{40,42, 44,48}Ca$ in the frame-work of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with NL3 and G2 parameter sets. The microscopic proton-nucleus optical potential for $p+^{40}Ca$ system is evaluted from Dirac NN-scattering amplitude and the density of the target nucleus using Relativistic-Love-Franey and McNeil-Ray-Wallace parametrizations. Then we estimate the scattering observables, such as elastic differential scattering cross-section, analysing power and the spin observables with relativistic impulse approximation. We compare the results with the experimental data for some selective cases and found that the use of density as well as the scattering matrix parametrization is crucial for the theoretical prediction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the choice of target density (from RMF with NL3 vs. E-RMF with G2 parameter sets) and the choice of NN-scattering amplitude parametrization (Relativistic-Love-Franey vs. McNeil-Ray-Wallace) both significantly affect the predicted optical potential and scattering observables for proton-nucleus elastic scattering, with the NL3 density and MRW parametrization giving better agreement with experimental data at 1000 MeV.

## Method

The authors first calculated proton and neutron density distributions for 40,42,44,48Ca using relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set and effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) with the G2 parameter set. They then constructed a microscopic proton-nucleus optical potential for the p+40Ca system by folding these target densities with Dirac NN-scattering amplitudes at Elab=1000 MeV using both the Relativistic-Love-Franey (RLF) and McNeil-Ray-Wallace (MRW) parametrizations. Finally, they solved the scattering wave function using the Numerov algorithm and extracted the elastic differential cross-section, analysing power, and spin observable Q.

## Key numbers

- Extremum real scalar potential: -442.2 MeV for RLF with G2 density, -372.4 MeV for RLF with NL3 density; -219.8 MeV for MRW with G2, -175.1 MeV for MRW with NL3
- Extremum imaginary scalar potential: 113.6 MeV for RLF with G2, 109.1 MeV for RLF with NL3; 32.8 MeV for MRW with G2, 33.2 MeV for MRW with NL3
- Extremum real vector potential: 361.3 MeV for RLF with G2, 279.2 MeV for RLF with NL3; 128.1 MeV for MRW with G2, 99.2 MeV for MRW with NL3
- Extremum imaginary vector potential: -179.2 MeV for RLF with G2, -164.8 MeV for RLF with NL3; -87.4 MeV for MRW with G2, -76.6 MeV for MRW with NL3
- The optical potential with NL3 extends to r ~ 8 fm, while with G2 it ends at r ~ 6 fm
- Incident proton energy: Elab = 1000 MeV for all calculations

## Context

This work extends the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) framework developed by earlier authors (Furnstahl 1987, Pardy 1983) and compares two established density functionals (NL3 from Lalazissis 1997 and G2 from Tang 1996) for calcium isotopes. The results confirm the earlier finding by Neilsen 1983 and Horowitz 1990 that MRW parametrization performs better at energies above 400 MeV while RLF is superior at lower energies, and demonstrate that the choice of target density is equally important as the choice of scattering parametrization for accurate predictions. The work enables more reliable calculations of elastic scattering observables for medium-mass nuclei and suggests that the NL3 parameter set may be preferred over G2 for reaction studies at these energies.
