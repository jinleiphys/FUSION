# Harmonics suppression effect of the quasi-periodic undulator in SASE free-electron-laser

> In this paper, the harmonics suppression effect of QPUs in SASE FEL is investigated. The numerical results show that the harmonics power is reduced by using QPUs, but the fundamental radiation power also has a pronounced decrease as the saturation length gets very long. The cases of employing QPUs as parts of undulators are studied. The calculations show that if the fraction of QPUs and their offgap are appropriate in an undulator system, the harmonics radiation could be suppressed remarkably, meanwhile the fundamental saturation length does not increase too much.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using quasi-periodic undulators (QPUs) in a SASE free-electron laser can suppress high-order harmonic radiation, but only if the fraction of QPUs and their offgap are chosen appropriately; otherwise, the fundamental radiation power drops significantly and the saturation length becomes impractically long.

## Method

The authors simulated the magnetic fields of a Fibonacci-sequence QPU using the RADIA code, computed the spontaneous radiation spectrum with the SPECTRA code, and modeled the SASE FEL process with the GENESIS code. They compared a system of ten normal undulators against systems where some or all undulators were replaced by QPUs with offgaps of 3 mm or 5 mm, using electron beam parameters based on the VUV-FEL design at NSRL (electron energy 405.2 MeV, peak current 500 A, energy spread 0.01%, emittance 1.9 mm-mrad).

## Key numbers

- For spontaneous radiation from a QPU with offgap=3 mm, the 3rd harmonic is suppressed by 22.6% and the 5th harmonic by 13.4% compared to a normal undulator.
- For spontaneous radiation from a QPU with offgap=5 mm, the 3rd harmonic is suppressed by 24.5% and the 5th harmonic by 20.8%.
- In the SASE FEL with ten normal undulators at 70 nm resonant wavelength, the saturated fundamental power is 313.5 MW, the 3rd harmonic power is 7.2 MW, and the 5th harmonic power is 46.3 kW.
- In the SASE FEL with five normal undulators and five QPUs (offgap=5 mm), the fundamental power reaches 333.8 MW, the 3rd harmonic power is about 98% lower than in the normal-undulator case, and the 5th harmonic power is 72% lower.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of QPUs as insertion devices for spontaneous radiation (Hashimoto and Sasaki, and the Halbach QPU tested at ESRF) into the regime of SASE FELs, where coherent amplification is critical. The authors show that using QPUs for the entire undulator system prevents FEL startup, but a hybrid scheme with normal undulators in the front and QPUs in the rear can suppress harmonics while maintaining fundamental power. This enables future FEL designs that require cleaner harmonic spectra for user experiments, though the optimal fraction of QPUs and offgap must be tuned for each specific beam energy and wavelength.
