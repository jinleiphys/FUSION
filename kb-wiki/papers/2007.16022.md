# Low mass strange stars and the compact star 1E1207.4-5209 in the Field Correlator Method

> We investigate the possible existence of anomalous mass defects in the low mass region of stellar sequences of strange stars. We employ the nonperturbative equation of state derived in the framework of the Field Correlator Method to describe the hydrostatic equilibrium of the strange matter. The large distance static $Q{\bar Q}$ potential $V_1$ and the gluon condensate $G_2$ are the main parameters of the model. We use the surface gravitational redshift measurements as a probe to determine the ratio $({\cal P}/{\cal E})_C$ at the center of strange stars. For $V_1=0$ and $G_2\gappr0.035\,{\rm GeV}^4$\,, we show that $({\cal P}/{\cal E})_C\simeq0.262$ and the corresponding redshift $z_S\simeq0.47$ are limiting values, at the maximum mass of the highest mass stellar sequence. As a direct application of our study, we try to determine the values of $V_1$ and $G_2$ from astrophysical observations of the compact star 1E\,1207.4-5209. Due to the uncertainties in the surface redshift determination, we made two attempts to obtain the model parameters. Our findings show that $({\cal P}/{\cal E})_C=0.073^{+0.029}_{+0.024}$\, at 68\% confidence, $V_1=0.44\pm0.10$\,GeV at 90\% confidence and $G_2=0.008\pm0.001\,{\rm GeV}^4$ at 95\% confidence \, in the first attempt; and $({\cal P}/{\cal E})_C=0.073^{+0.029}_{+0.024}=0.087\pm0.028$\, at 71\% confidence, $V_1=0.43\pm0.085$\,GeV at 94\% confidence and $G_2=0.0093\pm0.00092\,{\rm GeV}^4$ at 94\% confidence in the second attempt. These values of $V_1$ and $G_2$ are in reasonable agreement with the lattice and QCD sum rules calculations. As a consequence of the high values of $V_1$ and $G_2$, the anomalous mass defects of 1E\,1207.4-5209 are $|Δ_2M|\simeq2.56\times10^{53}$\,erg\, in the first attempt and $|Δ_2M|\simeq2.94\times10^{53}$\,erg\, in the second attempt.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the surface gravitational redshift of a strange star can be used as a probe to determine the central pressure-to-energy density ratio (P/E)_C, and that for the compact star 1E1207.4-5209 the Field Correlator Method parameters V1 and G2 can be constrained from astrophysical observations, but only within significant uncertainties due to the degeneracy between mass and radius in determining redshift.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations for hydrostatic equilibrium using a nonperturbative equation of state derived from the Field Correlator Method (FCM), which depends on two parameters: the large distance static Q\bar{Q} potential V1 and the gluon condensate G2. They generate theoretical stellar sequences for various (V1, G2) pairs and construct an interpolating curve relating the central pressure-to-energy density ratio (P/E)_C to the surface redshift z_S for non-constant energy density profiles. They then apply this relation to observational data of the compact star 1E1207.4-5209, using two attempts: the first based only on mass, radius, and redshift measurements, and the second incorporating the (P/E)_C versus z_S relation to narrow the parameter space.

## Key numbers

* For V1=0 and G2 >= 0.035 GeV^4, the limiting values at maximum mass are (P/E)_C ≈ 0.262 and z_S ≈ 0.47.
* In the first attempt for 1E1207.4-5209: (P/E)_C = 0.073^{+0.029}_{+0.024} at 68% confidence, V1 = 0.44 ± 0.10 GeV at 90% confidence, G2 = 0.008 ± 0.001 GeV^4 at 95% confidence.
* In the second attempt for 1E1207.4-5209: (P/E)_C = 0.087 ± 0.028 at 71% confidence, V1 = 0.43 ± 0.085 GeV at 94% confidence, G2 = 0.0093 ± 0.00092 GeV^4 at 94% confidence.
* The anomalous mass defects for 1E1207.4-5209 are |Δ2M| ≈ 2.56 × 10^53 erg in the first attempt and |Δ2M| ≈ 2.94 × 10^53 erg in the second attempt.
* For V1=0 and G2 >= 0.035 GeV^4, the maximum mass star has M ≈ 0.58 M_sun, R ≈ 3.19 km ≈ 1.85 R_S, and R_S/(2R) ≈ 0.27.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigations of strange stars using the FCM nonperturbative equation of state (Pereira 2019, 2020) and applies the method to the specific compact star 1E1207.4-5209, which was previously studied within the MIT Bag Model by RXX (2010) and whose mass, radius, and redshift were determined by Zhang (2011). The study enables future work on strange stars with crusts and magnetic fields, and provides a method to constrain QCD parameters from astrophysical observations, complementing lattice QCD and QCD sum rule calculations.
