# An overview of neV probes of PeV scale physics --- and of what's in between

> Low-energy experiments which would identify departures from the Standard Model (SM) rely either on the unexpected observation of symmetry breaking, such as of CP or B, or on an observed significant deviation from a precise SM prediction. We discuss examples of each search strategy, and show that low-energy experiments can open windows on physics far beyond accessible collider energies. We consider how the use of a frequentist analysis framework can redress the impact of theoretical uncertainties in such searches --- and how lattice QCD can help control them.

## Key claim

Low-energy precision experiments can probe new physics at energy scales far beyond those accessible to colliders, but the interpretation of such searches requires careful handling of theoretical uncertainties from non-perturbative QCD; the authors advocate that a frequentist R-fit framework can remove the impact of Standard Model theoretical errors on the allowed new-physics parameter space.

## Method

The paper reviews two search strategies for physics beyond the Standard Model: null tests of symmetry breaking (such as searches for permanent electric dipole moments, neutrinoless double-beta decay, or neutron-antineutron oscillations) and precision measurements of observables with precise SM predictions (such as parity-violating electron scattering, the muon anomalous magnetic moment, and angular correlations in neutron and nuclear beta decay). The authors illustrate their proposed frequentist analysis framework using Monte Carlo pseudodata of neutron beta decay observables, fitting simultaneously for the Standard Model parameter lambda (g_A/g_V) and a Fierz interference term b_BSM that would indicate scalar or tensor interactions.

## Key numbers

- The baryon asymmetry of the universe is eta_B = (5.96 +/- 0.28) x 10^(-10), determined from the observed deuterium abundance and big-bang nucleosynthesis (Steigman 2012)
- The Standard Model estimate of the baryon asymmetry yields eta_B < 10^(-26), many orders of magnitude too small (Huet 1994)
- The most stringent experimental EDM limit is from diamagnetic Hg-199: |d| < 3.1 x 10^(-29) e-cm at 95% C.L. (Griffith 2009)
- The evasion of electron shielding in Ra-225 is estimated to be about 700 times larger than in Hg-199 (Dobaczewski 2005)
- A neutrino mass of 0.1 eV, if generated via the seesaw mechanism, implies a new-physics scale Lambda_BSM ~ 10^(14-15) GeV (de Gouvea 2013)
- Lattice QCD calculations of the axial coupling g_A typically lie 5-15% below the values from beta decay, with much larger errors (Bhattacharya 2013)

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of low-energy probes of new physics presented at the MENU 2013 conference, building on the effective field theory framework of Buchmuller and Wyler (1985) and the CKMFitter analysis methodology of Hocker (2001) and Charles (2004). The work specifically addresses how lattice QCD calculations, such as those by Bhattacharya (2011) for scalar and tensor form factors, can sharpen constraints on non-Standard Model interactions, while also highlighting the unresolved problem of g_A where lattice results disagree with experiment. The analysis framework enables future searches for new physics through neutron beta decay, heavy-atom EDMs, and radiative beta decay correlations, potentially distinguishing between different mechanisms for baryogenesis such as electroweak baryogenesis, leptogenesis, or asymmetric dark matter models.
