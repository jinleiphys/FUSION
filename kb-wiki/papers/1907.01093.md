# Electroweak pion-production on nuclei within the extended factorization scheme

> We have applied the extended factorization scheme to investigate the electroweak pion production on nuclei. The ANL-Osaka model, which was obtained by analyzing the data of $πN$, $γN$, $N(e,e'π)$ and $N(ν,μ\,π) $ reactions up to invariant mass $W=$ 2 GeV, is used to generate the matrix elements of current operators relevant to pion-production off the nucleon. Medium effects on the $Δ$ (1232) component of meson-exchange current are included by using a $Δ$-nucleus potential determined from the previous $Δ$-hole model studies of pion-nucleus reactions. Nuclear correlations in the initial target state and in the spectator system(s) are modeled using realistic hole spectral functions. As a first step, we show that the data of $^{12}$C$(e,e')$ up to the $Δ$ (1232) region can be described reasonably well. The interplay between the pion production and two-body meson-exchange mechanisms is shown to be essential in improving the agreement with the data in the ``dip'' region, between the quasielastic and the $Δ$ (1232) peaks. Predictions for $^{12}$C$(ν,μ\,π)$ have also been made. They can be used to estimate pion-emission rates in neutrino-nucleus cross section, which constitutes an important systematic uncertainty to the reconstructed neutrino energy. With further improvements of the Metropolis Monte-Carlo techniques to account for final states comprised of more than two particles, our approach can be employed up to $W=$ 2 GeV, where two-pion production and higher mass nucleon resonances must be included for analyzing the data from accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

The extended factorization scheme, which combines realistic hole spectral functions with the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channel model, can describe electron-carbon scattering data up to the Δ(1232) region reasonably well, but only when the interplay between pion-production and two-body meson-exchange mechanisms is included; the approach is currently limited to final states with at most two particles and requires further Metropolis Monte-Carlo improvements to reach invariant mass W = 2 GeV where two-pion production and higher resonances become important.

## Method

The authors implemented electroweak pion-production amplitudes from the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channel model into the extended factorization scheme (EFS), which uses realistic hole spectral functions from correlated-basis function theory to model nuclear correlations in the initial target state and spectator system. Medium effects on the Δ(1232) component of the two-body meson-exchange current were included via a Δ-nucleus potential derived from a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation based on a coupled-channel NN⊕NΔ⊕πNN model. The one-body current contributions were computed within the impulse approximation with final-state interactions treated through a folding scheme based on a generalization of Glauber theory, while two-body currents included pion-in-flight, seagull, pion-pole, and Δ-excitation diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The ANL-Osaka model was fit to approximately 26,000 data points of πN, γN → πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ reactions from channel thresholds up to W ≤ 2.1 GeV
- The Fermi coupling constant used is G_F = 1.1803 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻², from analysis of 0⁺ → 0⁺ nuclear β-decays
- The axial-vector coupling constant is g_A = 1.2694 and the axial mass is m_A = 1.049 GeV
- The Fermi momentum for ¹²C is taken as k_F = 225 MeV
- The πNΔ cutoff parameter is Λ_{πNΔ} = 1150 MeV and f* = 2.14 for the Δ coupling
- The real Δ resonance mass is M_Δ = 1232 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of pion emission in inclusive ¹²C(e,e') scattering that used tree-diagram models (Blomqvist 1977, Koch 1985, Chen 1988) and the Sato-Lee dynamical model (Szczerbinska 2006), by employing the more comprehensive ANL-Osaka model that includes about 20 nucleon resonances up to W = 2 GeV. It also improves upon the treatment of medium effects in the Δ-component of the two-body current compared to Chen (1988), using a momentum-dependent Δ-nucleus potential from coupled-channel calculations. The approach enables predictions for neutrino-induced ¹²C(ν,μ π) cross sections that can be used to estimate pion-emission rates, which constitute an important systematic uncertainty for reconstructed neutrino energy in accelerator-based oscillation experiments such as T2K, MicroBooNE, MINERνA, NOνA, and DUNE.
