# Mass and radius relations of quarkyonic stars using an excluded volume model

> Inspired by the excluded volume model for isospin symmetric quarkyonic matter, we construct an `excluded volume' model for a charge neutral quarkyonic phase whose hadronic sector contains only neutrons. We refer to this model as quarkyonic neutron matter. We compute the equation of state for this model and solve the Tolman-Oppenhermer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass and radius relations relevant for neutron stars. The most straightforward extension of the model for symmetric quarkyonic matter to quarkyonic neutron matter does not satisfy the mass radius constraints from neutron star measurements. However, we show that by incorporating appropriate nuclear interactions in the excluded volume model one can produce mass-radius relations that lie within the constraints obtained from gravitational waves of binary neutron star mergers and maximum mass measurements of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors show that a straightforward extension of the excluded volume model for isospin-symmetric quarkyonic matter to charge-neutral quarkyonic neutron matter does not satisfy neutron star mass-radius constraints, but that incorporating appropriate nuclear interactions into the excluded volume model can produce mass-radius relations consistent with gravitational wave and maximum mass measurements.

## Method

The authors construct an excluded volume model for charge-neutral quarkyonic matter where the hadronic sector contains only neutrons, with up and down quarks constrained by charge neutrality. They compute the equation of state by minimizing an energy density functional that treats nucleons as hard spheres of volume v0 and quarks as non-interacting point particles, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations. To avoid an abrupt quark onset that produces unphysical speed-of-sound behavior, they introduce a density-dependent regulator for the quark density of states, and they incorporate nuclear interactions consistent with microscopic calculations of neutron matter from PhysRevC.85.032801.

## Key numbers

- Hard core density n0 is set to 4ρ0, where ρ0 is the nuclear saturation density.
- The regulator Λ is chosen to be zero at nB = 0 and increases to a maximum value Λ0 near the hardcore density, with a representative value of Λ³ = 10⁻⁵ × (3π²n0/2) shown in one figure.
- The number of colors Nc is taken as 3 throughout the analysis.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical excluded volume model for isospin-symmetric quarkyonic matter developed by PhysRevC.101.035201 to the charge-neutral case relevant for neutron stars. It contrasts with approaches that use Maxwell constructions between nuclear and quark matter, such as Duarte (2020), arguing that such constructions are not well motivated because the quarkyonic model is designed to avoid a phase transition. The paper enables future work incorporating beta equilibrium and interactions between quarks and nucleons, and it builds on the quarkyonic matter framework of McLerran (2007) and Hidaka (2008) which was found to naturally explain neutron star mass and radius measurements.
