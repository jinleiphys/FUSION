# Color superconductivity in weak coupling

> We derive perturbatively the gap equations for a color-superconducting condensate with total spin J=0 in dense QCD. At zero temperature, we confirm the results of Son for the dependence of the condensate on the coupling constant, and compute the prefactor to leading logarithmic accuracy. At nonzero temperature, we find that to leading order in weak coupling, the temperature dependence of the condensate is identical to that in BCS-like theories. The condensates for total spin J=1 are classified; to leading logarithmic accuracy these condensates are of the same order as those of spin J=0.

## Key claim

The paper confirms Son's result for the zero-temperature color-superconducting condensate's dependence on the coupling constant and computes the prefactor to leading logarithmic accuracy, but with an undetermined constant \(b_0'\) in the prefactor; at nonzero temperature, the ratio \(\phi(T)/\phi_0\) is identical to BCS-type theories to leading order in weak coupling, even though the detailed form of the gap function differs.

## Method

The authors derive perturbative gap equations for a color-superconducting condensate with total spin \(J=0\) in dense QCD with \(N_f=2\) massless quark flavors, using the hard dense loop (HDL) approximation for the gluon propagator. They solve these equations to leading logarithmic accuracy at zero temperature by isolating the dominant logarithmic terms \(\sim \ln(\mu/\phi_0)\) and \(\sim \ln(\mu/m_g)\), and then extend the analysis to nonzero temperature using spectral representations and Matsubara sums. The spin-\(J=1\) condensates are classified and their gap equations solved for \(N_f=1\) using the same leading-logarithmic approach.

## Key numbers

* The zero-temperature spin-zero condensate scales as \(\phi_0 = 2 \, (b_0/g^5) \, \mu \, \exp(-\pi/(2\bar{g}))\) where \(\bar{g} = g/(3\sqrt{2}\pi)\) and \(b_0 = 256\pi^4 (2/N_f)^{5/2} b_0'\) with an undetermined constant \(b_0'\).
* The ratio of critical temperature to zero-temperature condensate is \(T_c/\phi_0 \simeq 0.567\), identical to BCS theory.
* The spin-one condensate scales as \(\phi_1 = 2 \, (b_1/g^5) \, \mu \, \exp(-\pi/(2\bar{g}))\) with \(b_1 \equiv (b_0/b_0') b_1'\), where \(b_1'\) is an undetermined constant.
* The gluon mass is \(m_g^2 \equiv N_f \, g^2 \mu^2/(6\pi^2) + (N_c + N_f/2) g^2 T^2/9\).

## Context

This work extends Son's (1999) renormalization-group analysis of the zero-temperature color-superconducting gap by computing the prefactor to leading logarithmic accuracy, and agrees with independent analyses by Schäfer and Wilczek (1999) and Hong et al. (1999). The finding that spin-one condensates are not exponentially suppressed relative to spin-zero, unlike in BCS-like theories, enables the possibility that higher-spin condensates may be relevant for phenomenology of quark stars where chemical potentials differ between flavors, suppressing the usual spin-zero color-flavor locked condensates.
