# The Vortex State in a Strongly Coupled Dilute Atomic Fermionic Superfluid

> We show that in a dilute Fermionic superfluid, when the Fermions interact with an infinite scattering length, a vortex state is characterized by a strong density depletion along the vortex core. This feature can make a direct visualization of vortices in Fermionic superfluids possible.

## Key claim

In a dilute fermionic superfluid at infinite scattering length, a vortex state exhibits a strong density depletion along the core (to about 30% of the asymptotic density), contrary to earlier expectations that density variations would be negligible; this depletion could enable direct visualization of vortices in fermionic superfluids.

## Method

The authors constructed a local density approximation (LDA) energy density functional (EDF) for fermions with infinite scattering length, using ab initio results for the energy per particle of homogeneous normal and superfluid phases from Carlson et al. (2003). They solved the Bogoliubov–de Gennes equations self-consistently for a vortex with net angular momentum ℏ/2 per particle, using two parameter sets for the EDF, and computed the density, pairing field, and velocity profiles as functions of radial distance from the vortex axis.

## Key numbers

- Density at the vortex core is lowered to approximately 0.3 times the asymptotic density n∞ (Fig. 1).
- For the normal phase energy per particle, the coefficient αN ≈ 0.54 in the expression E_N = αN (3/5) εF.
- For the superfluid phase energy per particle, the coefficient αS ≈ 0.44 in the expression E_S = αS (3/5) εF.
- Using weak-coupling BCS condensation energy, the estimated superfluid gap Δ ≈ 0.4 εF.
- In the weakly coupled BCS limit, the gap formula is Δ = (2/e)^(7/3) εF exp(π/(2kFa)).
- For the EDF parameter set I: m/m* = αN, β = 0, γ ≈ -6.64.
- For the EDF parameter set II: m/m* = 1, β = 3(αN - 1)(3π^2)^(2/3)/5, γ ≈ -7.20.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of vortex structure in low-density nuclear matter (where a similar density depletion was found) to dilute atomic Fermi gases, and contradicts the earlier expectation from Nicolai (date not given) that density variations from vortices would be negligible. The results suggest that by tuning the scattering length to infinity via a Feshbach resonance, vortices in fermionic superfluids could be visualized after trap expansion, similar to techniques used in Bose-Einstein condensates by Madison et al. (date not given) and the MIT group (date not given). The approach builds on the BCS-to-BEC crossover framework of Leggett (1980) and Nozieres (1985), but uses ab initio results from Carlson et al. (2003) rather than the ladder-diagram approximations used in prior BCS-based analyses.
