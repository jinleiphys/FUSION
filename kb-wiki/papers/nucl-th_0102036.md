# Polarization observables in (${\vec γ},{\vec N}N$) reactions

> The formalism of (${\vec γ},{\vec N}N$) reactions is given where the incident photon is polarized and the outgoing nucleon polarization is detected. Sixteen structure functions and fifteen polarization observables are found in the general case, while only eight structure functions and seven polarizations observables survive in coplanar kinematics. Numerical examples are presented for the $^{16}$O($γ,pn$) and $^{16}$O($γ,pp$) reactions. The transitions to the ground state of $^{14}$C and $^{14}$N are calculated in a model where realistic short-range and tensor correlations are taken into account for the $pn$ pair, while short-range and long-range correlations are included in a consistent way for $pp$ pairs. The effects of the one-body and two-body components of the nuclear current and the role of correlations in cross sections and polarizations are studied and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper provides the general formalism for ($\vec{\gamma},\vec{N}N$) reactions with polarized photons and detected outgoing nucleon polarization, but the numerical results are presented as illustrative examples within a specific model that includes short-range and tensor correlations, with the caveat that the predictions are sensitive to the theoretical treatment of the two-nucleon overlap and the choice of kinematics.

## Method

The authors derive the structure functions and polarization observables for ($\vec{\gamma},\vec{N}N$) reactions from the hadron tensor components, considering both general and coplanar kinematics. They then perform numerical calculations for the $^{16}$O($\gamma,np$)$^{14}$N and $^{16}$O($\gamma,pp$)$^{14}$C reactions using a direct knockout framework where the nuclear current includes one-body (convective and spin) and two-body (seagull, pion-in-flight, and $\Delta$ isobar) components, with final-state interactions treated via phenomenological optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Sixteen structure functions and fifteen polarization observables are found in the general case; only eight structure functions and seven polarization observables survive in coplanar kinematics.
- For the $^{16}$O($\gamma,np$)$^{14}$N reaction, calculations are shown at incident photon energies of $E_\gamma = 100$ MeV and 400 MeV in coplanar symmetrical kinematics.
- For the detailed polarization study, a coplanar kinematics at $E_\gamma = 120$ MeV is chosen, with the outgoing neutron energy fixed at 45 MeV and scattering angle at $45^\circ$.
- The recoil momentum in the calculated angular range varies between approximately 90 and 290 MeV/$c$.
- For the $^{16}$O($\gamma,np$)$^{14}$N reaction, the two-nucleon overlap includes a ($p_{1/2}$)$^{-2}$ component with strength 0.60 and an interference component ($p_{1/2}p_{3/2}$)$^{-1}$ equal to $-0.45$.
- In out-of-plane kinematics with azimuthal angle $\phi = 30^\circ$, the cross section is an order of magnitude lower than in the peak region of the coplanar kinematics, and recoil momentum values below 160 MeV/$c$ are forbidden.

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously developed for ($\vec{e},e'\vec{N}N$) reactions by Giusti and Pacati (2000) to the case of real photons, and builds on earlier studies of ($\gamma,NN$) reactions by Gottfried (1963) and the Oxford group. The numerical results for $^{16}$O target connect to recent high-resolution experiments at MAMI and MAXlab in Lund, and the authors note that first measurements with polarized photon beams have been performed at LEGS on $^3$He and $^{16}$O and at MAMI on $^{12}$C, though without sufficient energy resolution to separate discrete final states. The work enables future combined measurements of cross sections and polarization observables to disentangle reaction mechanisms and study short-range and tensor correlations in nucleon pairs.
