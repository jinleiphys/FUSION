# Photon Spin and the Shape of the Two-Photon Correlation Function

> We use the covariant current formalism to derive the general form of the 2-photon correlation function for fully chaotic sources. Motivated by the recent discussion in the literature we concentrate on the effects from the photon spin on the correlator. We show that for locally thermalized expanding sources, like those expected to be created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, the only change relative to 2-pion interferometry is a statistical factor 1/2 for the overall strength of the correlation which results from the experimental averaging over the photon spin.

## Key claim

For locally thermalized expanding sources like those created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, the only effect of photon spin on the two-photon correlation function is a statistical factor of 1/2 for the overall correlation strength, with no additional momentum-dependent shape modifications; the authors argue that previously suggested momentum-dependent factors in the correlator are artifacts arising from neglecting charge conservation constraints (Neuhauser, 1986) or from incorrect tensor structure parametrization (Razumov and Feldmeier, 1995).

## Method

The authors used the covariant current formalism, generalizing earlier work on two-pion correlations (Chapman and Heinz, 1994) to vector fields, to derive the two-photon correlation function for fully chaotic sources. They modeled the source as an ensemble of elementary classical electromagnetic currents with random phases, then performed ensemble averages including integration over the random orientation angle of the current polarization vectors. The tensor structure of the Wigner density of source currents was analyzed under the constraint of charge conservation (current transversality) to isolate spin effects.

## Key numbers

* The correlation function for spin-averaged photons takes the form C = 1 + (1/2) * |S(q,K)|^2 / (S(0,ka) * S(0,kb)), where the factor 1/2 accounts for spin averaging in the detector.
* The factor 1/2 reflects that only photons with identical spin polarization contribute to Bose-Einstein correlations.
* The result applies to photon sources at ultrarelativistic energies where incoherent (chaotic) emission processes dominate, as opposed to low energies (E_lab < 100 MeV/nucleon) where coherent mechanisms complicate photon production.

## Context

This work extends the covariant current formalism previously applied to two-pion correlations (Chapman and Heinz, 1994) and resolves a controversy in the literature regarding photon spin effects in two-photon interferometry. The authors argue that the momentum-dependent prefactor 1/2 [1 + k_a_hat * k_b_hat] proposed by Neuhauser (1986) arises from neglecting charge conservation constraints, while the additional tensor structure terms in Razumov and Feldmeier (1995) result from incorrectly parametrizing the emission function in terms of ka and kb separately rather than only the average momentum K. The result supports the simpler normalization prescription used by Srivastava and Kapusta (1993) and enables the direct application of standard two-pion interferometry analysis techniques to two-photon correlations, with only the overall correlation strength reduced by the spin-averaging factor.
