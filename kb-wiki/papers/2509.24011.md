# Resonance Contributions to Radiative Corrections in Charged-Current Elastic (Anti)Neutrino-Nucleon Scattering at GeV Energies

> We present the first evaluation of virtual resonance contributions to the charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering at GeV energies, focusing on the dominant $Δ(1232)$ resonance. We approximate the vector part of the $N \to Δ$ transition by the leading magnetic dipole term. Our results for the cross-section corrections at fixed neutrino energy indicate the permille-level contribution of resonance intermediate states to the elastic and radiative scattering cross sections. This calculation exhibits the expected infrared behavior of the invariant amplitudes and unpolarized cross sections. Our findings provide important insights into inelastic excitations in the charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering at GeV energies.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first evaluation of virtual Δ(1232) resonance contributions to radiative corrections in charged-current elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering at GeV energies, finding that these contributions are at the permille level and do not exceed this magnitude even with kinematic enhancement near the resonance pole.

## Method
The authors evaluate one-loop direct and crossed box diagrams with a Δ(1232) intermediate state, modeling the N→Δ vector transition using the leading magnetic dipole term in the Jones-Scadron convention and the axial-vector transition using form factors from Lalakulich (2005). They perform two calculations: one with form factors evaluated at the momentum transfer q² (default model) and one with form factors evaluated at (q±L)² (hadronic model), and also compare two axial-vector form factor parametrizations (Δ_I and Δ_II models). The loop integrals are evaluated in d=4 dimensions with a photon-mass regulator, and the imaginary parts are verified through an independent unitarity calculation.

## Key numbers
- The Δ(1232) resonance contribution to the unpolarized charged-current elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering cross section is at the permille level for incoming neutrino energies of 600 MeV, 1 GeV, 1.2 GeV, and 2 GeV
- The magnetic transition form factor at zero momentum transfer is G*_M(0) = 3.02
- The axial-vector transition form factor parameter C_5^A(0) = 1.2 with M_A² = 1.05 GeV²
- The Δ resonance mass is M_Δ = 1.232 GeV with width Γ_Δ = 0.117 GeV
- The pion mass used in the Breit-Wigner weighting is m_π ≈ 0.135 GeV
- The dipole form factor parameter Λ = 0.843 GeV for the proton magnetic form factor

## Context
This work extends the factorization framework for radiative corrections to charged-current elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering developed by Tomalak (2021, 2022), which previously evaluated only the nucleon intermediate state. The calculation addresses the uncertainty from inelastic intermediate states that was estimated in those earlier works. The results enable precision analyses of neutrino scattering data from experiments such as DUNE and MINERvA, and complement similar dispersion-relation approaches used in electron-proton scattering by Gorchtein (2006) and Borisyuk (2008, 2012, 2013).
