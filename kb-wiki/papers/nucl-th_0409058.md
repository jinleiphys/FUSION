# Partonic effects on anisotropic flows at RHIC

> We report recent results from a multiphase transport (AMPT) model on the azimuthal anisotropies of particle momentum distributions in heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. These include higher-order anisotropic flows and their scaling, the rapidity dependence of anisotropic flows, and the elliptic flow of charm quarks.

# Partonic effects on anisotropic flows at RHIC

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with string melting, the measured anisotropic flows $v_2$, $v_4$, and $v_6$ of charged hadrons at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV can be described by a parton scattering cross section of about 10 mb, but this cross section is an order of magnitude larger than perturbative QCD predictions, implying that nonperturbative effects are important in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The AMPT model uses minijet partons from hard processes and strings from soft processes in the HIJING model as initial conditions, with parton evolution described by Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) model including only elastic scatterings with an in-medium cross section. In the string melting scenario, hadrons from string fragmentation are converted to valence quarks and antiquarks, which then interact via ZPC before hadronizing through a simple coalescence model that combines nearest quarks and antiquarks into hadrons. Final-state hadronic scatterings are modeled by a relativistic transport (ART) model.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb were used for minimum bias Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV
- The 3 mb cross section underestimates data at higher $p_T$ ($>1$ GeV/$c$), while 10 mb gives a better fit to the data
- Current quark masses used: $d$ quark = 10 MeV, $u$ quark = 6 MeV, $s$ quark = 200 MeV, $c$ quark = 1.35 GeV
- The scaling relations for meson anisotropic flows from the naive coalescence model give $v_{4,M}(p_T)/v_{2,M}^2(p_T) \approx 3/4$ and $v_{6,M}(p_T)/v_{2,M}^3(p_T) \approx 1/2$, which are smaller than the experimental scaling factor of about 1.2
- Parton anisotropic flows satisfy the scaling relation $v_{n,q}(p_T) \sim v_{2,q}^{n/2}(p_T)$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of anisotropic flows at RHIC using the AMPT model (Lin 2001, Zhang 2000) and addresses discrepancies between theoretical models and experimental data on rapidity dependence of anisotropic flows (Hirano 2001, Heinz 2004, Csanad 2004). The results show that a larger parton cross section (10 mb) is needed at midrapidity while a smaller cross section (3 mb) works at large pseudorapidity, suggesting that partonic matter forms only around midrapidity while strings dominate at large rapidities. The charm quark elliptic flow results connect to the debate between perturbative QCD spectra without final-state interactions versus complete thermalization scenarios (Batsouli 2003, Greco 2004).
