# Semi-contact 3-body interaction for nuclear density functional theory

> To solve difficulties related to the use of nuclear density functional theory applied in its beyond mean-field version, we introduce a semi-contact 3-body effective interaction. We show that this interaction is a good candidate to replace the widely used density dependent effective interaction. The resulting new functionals are able to describe symmetric, neutron, polarized and neutron polarized nuclear matter as well as the effective mass properties simultaneously.

# Semi-contact 3-body interaction for nuclear density functional theory

## Key claim

A semi-contact 3-body effective interaction, which is density-independent and has finite range in the relative coordinate of two particles but contact in the third particle's coordinate, can replace the widely used density-dependent effective interactions in nuclear density functional theory, thereby solving pathologies that arise in beyond-mean-field calculations such as symmetry restoration. The authors show this replacement works for symmetric nuclear matter, neutron matter, spin-polarized matter, and spin-polarized neutron matter simultaneously, but note that for very small density exponents (α ≤ 1/6) the fit starts to deteriorate.

## Method

The authors introduce a 3-body semi-contact interaction where the interaction is contact in the Jacobi coordinate R^k_ij (the position of the third particle relative to the center-of-mass of the other two) but has finite range in the relative coordinate r_ij, with the finite-range part taken as a normalized Gaussian function of range a = 1.2 fm. They derive the Hartree-Fock energy functional for infinite nuclear matter in four spin/isospin channels (symmetric matter, neutron matter, spin-polarized matter, and spin-polarized neutron matter) and fit the 3-body strength parameters (v_0, v_σ, v_τ, v_στ) along with the 2-body interaction parameters to reproduce the equations of state and effective masses of the SLy5 Skyrme functional and the D1M Gogny functional, replacing the density-dependent term in each.

## Key numbers

- Range of the semi-contact 3-body interaction: a = 1.2 fm, chosen as optimal for fitting D1M results.
- Saturation density for SLy5^3b: ρ_sat = 0.161 fm^-3 (target SLy5: 0.160 fm^-3).
- Binding energy per particle for SLy5^3b: B/A = -15.42 MeV (target SLy5: -15.98 MeV).
- Compression modulus for SLy5^3b: K_∞ = 236.59 MeV (target SLy5: 229.92 MeV).
- Isoscalar effective mass for SLy5^3b: m^*/m = 0.691 (target SLy5: 0.697).
- Saturation density for D1M^3b: ρ_sat = 0.165 fm^-3 (target D1M: 0.165 fm^-3).
- Binding energy per particle for D1M^3b: B/A = -15.82 MeV (target D1M: -16.02 MeV).
- Compression modulus for D1M^3b: K_∞ = 228.58 MeV (target D1M: 224.98 MeV).
- Isoscalar effective mass for D1M^3b: m^*/m = 0.744 (target D1M: 0.746).
- The 3-body interaction can reproduce density dependence ρ^(1+α) for 0 ≤ α ≤ 1, with deterioration for α ≤ 1/6.

## Context

This work addresses the known pathology that density-dependent functionals with non-integer powers of density (as used in standard Skyrme and Gogny interactions) lead to violations of the Pauli principle and self-interaction problems when used in beyond-mean-field calculations such as symmetry restoration (Duguet 2009). The semi-contact 3-body interaction extends earlier efforts to construct density-independent functionals, including the zero-range 3-body force proposed by Gezerlis and Carlson (2010) for low-density fermions and the zero-range density-independent interaction with 3-body velocity-dependent terms introduced by Sadoudi et al. (2013). The new interaction enables the construction of functionals (SLy5^3b and D1M^3b) that can be used in multi-reference DFT frameworks for symmetry restoration and configuration mixing without the pathologies of density-dependent terms.
