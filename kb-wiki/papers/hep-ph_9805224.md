# A new technique for computing the spectral density of sunset-type diagrams: integral transformation in configuration space

> We present a new method to investigate a class of diagrams which generalizes the sunset topology to any number of massive internal lines. Our attention is focused on the computation of the spectral density of these diagrams which is related to many-body phase space in $D$ dimensional space-time. The spectral density is determined by the inverse $K$-transform of the product of propagators in configuration space. The inverse $K$-transform reduces to the inverse Laplace transform in any odd number of space-time dimensions for which we present an explicit analytical result.

## Key claim

The spectral density of sunset-type (water melon) diagrams with any number of massive internal lines can be computed via an inverse \(K\)-transform of the product of propagators in configuration space, which reduces to an inverse Laplace transform in any odd number of space-time dimensions where an explicit analytical result is available; in even dimensions the method yields a one-dimensional integral representation suitable for numerical treatment, but the final integration cannot be performed in closed form due to products of Bessel functions.

## Method

The polarization function \(\Pi(x)\) for a water melon diagram with \(n\) internal lines is written as the product of configuration-space propagators \(D(x,m_i)\) in \(D\)-dimensional Euclidean space-time. The spectral density \(\rho(s)\) is obtained by applying the inverse \(K\)-transform to the dispersion representation \(\Pi(x)=\int_0^\infty\rho(s)D(x,\sqrt{s})\,ds\), which for odd \(D\) becomes an inverse Laplace transform whose evaluation reduces to computing residues at the origin. For even \(D\) the contour integral is closed to the left, and the cut along the negative real axis is handled by analytic continuation of the McDonald functions, leading to a non-oscillatory one-dimensional integral representation.

## Key numbers

* In three-dimensional space-time (\(D=3\)), the spectral density for a water melon diagram with \(n\) internal lines of masses \(m_i\) and total mass \(M=\sum_i m_i\) is \(2m\rho(m^2) = (m-M)^{n-2} / [(4\pi)^{n-1}(n-2)!]\,\theta(m-M)\) for \(n>1\).
* For the degenerate one-loop case (two internal lines with equal mass \(m\)) in general \(D=2\lambda+2\) dimensions, the spectral density is \(\rho(s) = (s-4m^2)^{\lambda-1/2} / [2^{4\lambda+1}\pi^{\lambda+1/2}\Gamma(\lambda+1/2)\sqrt{s}]\) for \(s>4m^2\).
* The three-dimensional scalar propagator is \(D_3(x,m)=e^{-mx}/(4\pi x)\); the five-dimensional propagator is \(D_5(x,m)=e^{-mx}(1+mx)/(8\pi^2 x^3)\).

## Context

This work extends the method of Groote, Körner, and Pivovarov (1998) for water melon diagrams (generalized sunset topology to any number of internal lines) by providing a configuration-space integral transformation that simplifies the computation of the spectral density. It contrasts with the convolution approach of Narison and Pivovarov (1995), which becomes cumbersome for large numbers of internal lines, and with the direct Fourier-transform discontinuity method of Groote, Körner, and Pivovarov (1998) which involves oscillatory integrands. The technique enables efficient numerical evaluation of multi-loop massive diagrams in even dimensions and yields closed-form results in odd dimensions, with applications to phase-space integrals in three-dimensional QCD and jet physics.
