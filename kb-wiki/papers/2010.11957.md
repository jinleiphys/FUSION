# The Multiplicity Dependence of Pion Interferometry in Hydrodynamics

> Understanding the origins of collective, fluid-like behavior in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions constitutes one of the biggest open challenges in the field. In this Letter, it is argued that certain features in the multiplicity dependence of the source sizes extracted using pion interferometry may be understood quite naturally if small systems evolve hydrodynamically at sufficiently large multiplicities. Interferometry may therefore provide a baseline for probing and constraining the nature of collective behavior in nuclear collisions.

# The Multiplicity Dependence of Pion Interferometry in Hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The paper argues that the observed non-universality and hierarchy in the slopes of HBT radii as functions of the cube root of charged multiplicity across different collision systems (p+p, p+Pb, Pb+Pb) are natural consequences of hydrodynamic behavior, arising from enhanced collective flow in small systems due to larger initial density gradients. The claim is hedged by the use of a simple heuristic hydrodynamic model that provides qualitative but not fully quantitative agreement with experimental data.

## Method
The author used the boost-invariant hydrodynamic framework iEBE-VISHNU with smooth, event-averaged MC-Glauber initial conditions to model p+p collisions at 7 TeV, p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. The two-pion correlation function was computed numerically from integrals over each system's freeze-out hypersurface and then fit to a Gaussian parameterization to extract the HBT radii $R_o$, $R_s$, and $R_l$ as functions of transverse pair momentum $K_T$ and charged multiplicity $dN_\text{ch}/d\eta$. The source variances entering the pocket relations were also analyzed to connect the radii to the space-time structure of the pion source.

## Key numbers
- The HBT radii were computed for three collision systems: p+p at 7 TeV, p+Pb at 5.02 TeV, and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV.
- Freeze-out hypersurfaces were shown for fixed multiplicities $dN_\text{ch}/d\eta = 10$, 25, and 50.
- The HBT radii were analyzed at a transverse pair momentum $K_T = 450$ MeV.
- The slope magnitudes of $R_o$ as a function of $(dN_\text{ch}/d\eta)^{1/3}$ at $K_T = 450$ MeV are strongly ordered by collision size, with the smallest slopes in p+p and the largest in Pb+Pb.
- Virtually no splitting is visible in the $R_s$ slopes across the three collision systems.
- The model overestimates the magnitudes of $R_o$ and $R_s$ but underestimates $R_l$, attributed to the absence of preequilibrium flow.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of HBT interferometry in small collision systems, building on prior studies of collectivity in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions (Nagle 2018, Adolfsson 2020) and on the theoretical framework for HBT radii (Wiedemann 1999, Lisa 2005, Heinz 1999). It directly addresses the observed slope non-universality and hierarchy in experimental data from ALICE (Aamodt 2011, Adam 2015a, Adam 2015b) and earlier hydrodynamic studies (Kisiel 2008, Plumberg 2020). The results enable future work to use the multiplicity dependence of HBT radii as a sensitive test to discriminate between different models of collectivity, particularly when combined with momentum-space observables.
