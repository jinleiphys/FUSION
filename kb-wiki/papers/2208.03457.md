# Nonperturbative Casimir effects: Vacuum structure, Confinement, and Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> The review of vacuum and matter restructuring in space-time with boundaries is presented. We consider phase properties of confining gauge theories and strongly interacting fermion systems. In particular, the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions properties in the presence of Casimir plates. We also discuss mass scale shifts in such systems and their possible dynamical and geometrical nature.

# Nonperturbative Casimir effects: Vacuum structure, Confinement, and Chiral Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim

The paper reviews how boundaries in quantum field theories can restructure the vacuum through three distinct mechanisms: purely geometrical constraints on topological objects (monopoles), non-perturbative gluon dynamics that shift mass scales below the glueball mass, and dynamical temporal boundaries in bound states that break time-translation invariance for individual constituents. The author argues that these effects go beyond the traditional Casimir picture of infrared restriction of the vacuum spectrum.

## Method

The paper surveys three systems using lattice field theory simulations and analytic methods: (1) a strongly interacting fermion system with a four-fermion interaction Lagrangian, studied via the phase diagram of the chiral condensate as a function of inter-plate distance and temperature; (2) compact QED in (2+1) dimensions, where monopole configurations between Casimir wires are analyzed numerically; (3) SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1) dimensions, where the Casimir potential and Polyakov loop are computed on the lattice. For bound states, the author derives the temporal distribution of constituents from the Bethe-Salpeter equation and relates it to the EMC effect.

## Key numbers

- Critical interwire distance for deconfinement in compact QED: \( R_c = 0.72(1)/g^2 \), where \( g \) is the electric charge
- Casimir mass in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory: \( M_{Cas} = 1.38(3) \sqrt{\sigma} \), where \( \sigma \) is the string tension
- Anomalous power in the Casimir potential fit: \( \nu_\infty = 0.05(2) \)
- Lowest glueball mass in (2+1) SU(2): \( M_{0^{++}} \approx 4.7 \sqrt{\sigma} \)
- Temporal size of the deuteron bound state: \( \tau_0 \approx 1.4 \, \text{MeV}^{-1} \), corresponding to 276 Fm or \( 9.2 \times 10^{-22} \) seconds
- Casimir energy for a spherical geometry: \( \langle E_{\text{Sphere}} \rangle = +0.0461765 / R \)

## Context

This review extends the work of Chernodub (2017, 2018) on deconfinement phase transitions induced by Casimir boundaries in gauge theories, and the work of Flachi (2012, 2013) on chiral symmetry breaking in fermionic systems with boundaries. It also connects to the Scharnhorst (1990, 1993) prediction of superluminal light propagation in Casimir vacuum. The analysis of bound state temporal structure provides a geometric explanation for the EMC effect, which was discovered experimentally by the EMC collaboration. The paper enables further study of how finite-volume and boundary effects can mimic or modify dynamical mass generation in both Abelian and non-Abelian gauge theories.
