# Analysis of the data for $γp \to f_1(1285) p$ photoproduction

> The photoproduction of $f_1(1285)$ meson off proton is investigated within an effective Lagrangian approach. The $t$-channel $ρ$- and $ω$-exchange diagrams, $u$-channel nucleon-exchange diagram, generalized contact term, and $s$-channel pole diagrams of nucleon and a minimal number of nucleon resonances are taken into account in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the experimental data. Three different models, i.e., the Feynman model, the Regge model, and the interpolated Regge model, are employed where the $t$-channel reaction amplitudes are constructed in Feynman type, Regge type, and interpolated Regge type, respectively. The results show that in neither Feynman model with two nucleon resonances introduced nor interpolated Regge model with one nucleon resonances included, can the available data for $γp \to f_1(1285) p$ be satisfactorily reproduced. Nevertheless, in the Regge model, when any one of the $N(1990){7/2}^+$, $N(2000){5/2}^+$, $N(2040){3/2}^+$, $N(2060){5/2}^-$, $N(2100){1/2}^+$, $N(2120){3/2}^-$, $N(2190){7/2}^-$, $N(2300){1/2}^+$, and $N(2570){5/2}^-$ resonances is considered, the data can be well described. The resulted resonance parameters are consistent with those advocated in Particle Data Group (PDG) review. Further analysis shows that in high-energy region, the peaks of $γp \to f_1(1285) p$ differential cross sections at forward angles are dominated by the contributions from $t$-channel $ρ$- and $ω$-exchange diagrams, while in low-energy region, the $s$-channel pole diagrams of resonances also provide significant contributions to the $γp \to f_1(1285) p$ cross sections.

## Key claim

The available CLAS differential cross-section data for $\gamma p \to f_1(1285) p$ can be well described in the Regge model when any one of nine specific nucleon resonances is included, but cannot be satisfactorily reproduced in either the Feynman model (even with two nucleon resonances) or the interpolated Regge model (even with one nucleon resonance).

## Method

The authors constructed reaction amplitudes within an effective Lagrangian approach, including $t$-channel $\rho$- and $\omega$-exchange diagrams, $u$-channel nucleon-exchange diagram, a generalized contact term to ensure gauge invariance, and $s$-channel pole diagrams of the nucleon and a minimal number of nucleon resonances. They implemented three different treatments of the $t$-channel amplitudes: the Feynman model (standard Feynman propagators), the Regge model (Reggeized propagators with degenerate trajectories), and the interpolated Regge model (smooth transition between Feynman and Regge forms via an interpolating form factor). For each model, they systematically tested combinations of up to two of the nine PDG-listed nucleon resonances with spins $J=1/2$ to $7/2$ to fit the CLAS differential cross-section data at five energy points.

## Key numbers

- The $f_1(1285)$ meson has mass $M=1281.9\pm 0.5$ MeV and width $\Gamma=22.7\pm 1.1$ MeV.
- The coupling constant $g_{\rho f_1\gamma}=0.94$ GeV$^{-2}$ was determined from the decay width $\Gamma_{f_1 \to \rho\gamma} \simeq 0.45$ MeV from the CLAS experiment.
- In the Regge model, the best fit (lowest $\chi^2/N$) was obtained with the $N(2000){5/2}^+$ resonance, yielding $\chi^2/N = 4.0$; other resonances gave $\chi^2/N$ values ranging from $4.1$ to $6.5$.
- In the Feynman model with $N(2000){5/2}^+$ and $N(2060){5/2}^-$ resonances, the fitted mass of $N(2000){5/2}^+$ was $2250\pm3$ MeV (PDG range: $\sim2000$ MeV) and width $126\pm30$ MeV (PDG range: $\sim300$ MeV); the $N(2060){5/2}^-$ had fitted mass $2030\pm7$ MeV (PDG range: $2030$–$2200$ MeV) and width $450\pm52$ MeV (PDG range: $\sim400$ MeV).
- The CLAS differential cross-section data cover the energy range from the $f_1(1285)p$ threshold up to center-of-mass energy $W\approx 2.8$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical analyses of $\gamma p \to f_1(1285) p$ by Wangxiaoyun (2017) and Wangyanyan (2017), both of which used the interpolated Regge model but reached conflicting conclusions about the need for nucleon resonances and the role of the $N(2300){1/2}^+$ resonance. The present study systematically compares three different $t$-channel treatments (Feynman, Regge, interpolated Regge) to resolve these discrepancies, and finds that only the Regge model can describe the CLAS data with a single nucleon resonance. The results enable future investigations of higher-mass nucleon resonances in the less-explored energy region above 2.2 GeV, where the $f_1(1285)p$ channel acts as an isospin filter isolating $I=1/2$ resonances from $\Delta$ resonances.
