# Weak decays of medium and heavy Lambda-hypernuclei

> We have made a new evaluation of the Lambda decay width in nuclear matter within the Propagator Method. Through the Local Density Approximation it is possible to obtain results in finite nuclei. We have also studied the dependence of the widths on the N-N and Lambda-N short range correlations. Using reasonable values for the parameters that control these correlations, as well as realistic nuclear densities and Lambda wave functions, we reproduce, for the first time, the experimental non-mesonic widths in a wide range of mass numbers (from medium to heavy hypernuclei).

## Key claim

The authors reproduce, for the first time, the experimental non-mesonic decay widths of medium to heavy Lambda-hypernuclei across a wide range of mass numbers, but only after adjusting the Landau parameters for short-range correlations (g' = 0.8, g'_Lambda = 0.4) to fit the non-mesonic width of 12-Lambda-C, and using a Wood-Saxon potential that reproduces the s and p Lambda single-particle levels.

## Method

The Lambda decay width in nuclear matter was calculated using the Propagator Method within Random Phase Approximation, which provides a unified treatment of mesonic, one-body non-mesonic, and two-body non-mesonic (three-nucleon) decay channels. The results were extended to finite nuclei via the Local Density Approximation, using Fermi distributions for nuclear density and Wood-Saxon potentials for the Lambda wave function that reproduce experimental s and p single-particle levels. The short-range correlations were controlled by Landau parameters g' (for NN interactions) and g'_Lambda (for Lambda-N interactions), with the pion and rho meson exchange modulated by these correlations.

## Key numbers

- Non-mesonic width of 12-Lambda-C: 0.98 (new Wood-Saxon wave function), compared to experimental values of 1.14 ± 0.20 (BNL) and 0.89 ± 0.18 (KEK)
- Total width of 12-Lambda-C: 1.23 (new Wood-Saxon), compared to experimental 1.25 ± 0.18 (both BNL and KEK)
- Mesonic width of 12-Lambda-C: 0.25 (new Wood-Saxon), compared to experimental 0.11 ± 0.27 (BNL) and 0.36 ± 0.15 (KEK)
- One-body non-mesonic width of 12-Lambda-C: 0.82; two-body non-mesonic width: 0.16
- Total width of 208-Lambda-Pb: 1.40, with mesonic width 1 × 10^{-4}, one-body non-mesonic 1.21, two-body non-mesonic 0.19
- Landau parameters used: g' = 0.8, g'_Lambda = 0.4
- Cut-off for the pion form factor: Lambda_pi = 1.2 GeV
- Weak coupling constants: G = 2.211 × 10^{-7} / m_pi^2, A = 1.06, B = -7.10

## Context

This work extends the Propagator Method of Oset and Salcedo (1985) and the calculations of Ramos et al. (1995) for Lambda decay in nuclear matter, using improved treatment of short-range correlations and more realistic nuclear densities and Lambda wave functions. The authors reproduce experimental non-mesonic widths across a wide mass range (from 12-Lambda-C to 208-Lambda-Pb), which previous calculations using the parameters of Ramos et al. (1995) (g' = 0.615, g'_Lambda = 0.62) overestimated by about 50% for carbon. The results enable improved predictions for the neutron-to-proton ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p and nucleon energy spectra, which remain an open problem as theoretical calculations still underestimate experimental values (experimental ratios 0.5 to 2, while One Pion Exchange models give 0.1 to 0.2).
