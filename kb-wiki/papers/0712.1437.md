# Internal energy in dissipative relativistic fluids

> Liu procedure is applied to a first order weakly nonlocal special relativistic fluid. It is shown, that a reasonable relativistic theory is and extended one, where the basic state space contains the momentum density. This property follows from the structure of the energy-momentum balance and the Second Law of thermodynamics. Moreover, the entropy depends on the energy density and the momentum density on a given specific way, indicating that the local rest frame energy density cannot be interpreted as the internal energy, the local rest frame momentum density should be considered, too. The corresponding constitutive relations for the stress and the energy flux are derived.

# Internal energy in dissipative relativistic fluids

## Key claim

A first-order weakly nonlocal (local equilibrium) relativistic fluid theory cannot yield a complete solution of the entropy inequality under natural assumptions; instead, a viable relativistic theory must be an extended one where the basic state space includes the momentum density, and the local rest frame energy density cannot be interpreted as the internal energy, the internal energy is the absolute value of the energy four-vector, \(E = \sqrt{|e^2 - q_\alpha q^\alpha|}\).

## Method

The Liu procedure (Lagrange-Farkas multiplier method) was applied to enforce the Second Law of thermodynamics as a constraint on the energy-momentum balance in a first-order weakly nonlocal special relativistic fluid, with the constitutive state space spanned by the energy density, the velocity field, and their first derivatives. The resulting Liu equations and dissipation inequality were solved under assumptions of local Lagrange-Farkas multipliers, local entropy, and no additional entropy flux term, leading to a specific functional form for the entropy. An extended irreversible thermodynamics theory was then constructed by introducing the energy flux as an independent variable and deriving constitutive relations for the viscous stress and heat flux from the entropy production.

## Key numbers

- The internal energy is defined as \(E = \sqrt{|e^2 - q_\alpha q^\alpha|}\), where \(e\) is the local rest frame energy density and \(q^\alpha\) is the momentum density/energy flux.
- The entropy depends on \(e\) and \(q^\alpha\) as \(\hat{s} = \tilde{s}(e^2 - q^\alpha q_\alpha) + B\), with \(B\) constant.
- The temperature is defined as \(\partial_e \hat{s} = \Lambda = 1/T\).
- The potential relation is \(e - q^\alpha q_\alpha / e = T s - p\), where \(p\) is the thermostatic pressure.
- The series expansion of the internal energy gives \(E \approx e - \mathbf{q}^2/(2e) + \dots\), with the relaxation time fixed as \(\tau = 1/e\).
- The shear viscosity \(\eta\), bulk viscosity \(\eta_v\), and heat conduction coefficient \(\lambda\) are nonnegative.

## Context

This work extends the classical Eckart (1940) theory of relativistic fluids, which is known to produce generic instabilities (Hiscock and Lindblom, 1985), and contrasts with the more developed Müller-Israel-Stewart extended theory (Israel, 1976; Israel and Stewart, 1980). The paper shows that the quadratic term in the internal energy expansion matches the Müller-Israel-Stewart result but with a fixed relaxation time \(\tau = 1/e\) and as only the first approximation. The derived system yields a stable homogeneous equilibrium in linear stability investigations (Van and Biró, 2007), offering a minimal viable extension of local equilibrium theory without the complexity of the Müller-Israel-Stewart approach.
