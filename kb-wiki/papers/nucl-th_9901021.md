# Phi-meson photoproduction and strangeness of the nucleon

> The polarization observables in phi photoproduction are suggested for probing the nucleon strangeness. Based on models for phi photoproduction, some double polarization observables are shown to be very sensitive to the strangeness content of the proton because of the different spin structures of the amplitudes associated with different mechanisms.

# Phi-meson photoproduction and strangeness of the nucleon

## Key claim
Double polarization observables in phi photoproduction, particularly the beam-target asymmetry C^BT_zz, are highly sensitive to the hidden strangeness admixture B^2 of the proton, even at values as low as 1%, because the different production mechanisms (vector-meson dominance, one-pion-exchange, and s\bar{s} knockout) have distinct spin structures that become separable in polarization measurements, unlike in unpolarized cross sections.

## Method
The author computed helicity amplitudes for three contributing mechanisms in gamma p -> phi p: diffractive vector-meson dominance (VDM), one-pion-exchange (OPE), and direct s\bar{s} knockout from a Fock-space proton wavefunction containing a [uud] core plus an s\bar{s} cluster with spin 0 or 1 and orbital angular momentum L=1. Using these amplitudes, the double polarization observable C^BT_zz (where both photon beam and target proton are polarized along the photon momentum direction) was calculated as a function of momentum transfer at fixed center-of-mass energy W = 2.155 GeV, for various values of the strangeness admixture B^2 = b_0^2 + b_1^2.

## Key numbers
- At forward scattering angles, C^BT_zz approaches zero with B^2 = 0%, but with B^2 = 1% its magnitude reaches 0.45.
- Calculations were performed at W = 2.155 GeV for B^2 values of 0%, 0.25%, and 1%, assuming |b_0| = |b_1|.
- The optimal initial photon energy range for measuring B^2 is around 2-3 GeV.
- The theoretical upper bound on B^2 from earlier relativistic quark model work by Titov et al. is less than 5%.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Henley et al. (nonrelativistic harmonic oscillator quark model, assuming B^2 = 10-20%) and Titov et al. (relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model, finding B^2 < 5%) for phi electroproduction, moving to photoproduction and focusing on polarization observables rather than cross sections. The key advance is showing that double polarization observables can distinguish the s\bar{s} knockout contribution from VDM and OPE backgrounds, which have similar momentum-transfer dependence in unpolarized cross sections. This enables experimental searches for nucleon strangeness at facilities like JLab and RCNP, where several experiments have been proposed.
