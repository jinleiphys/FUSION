# Global hyperon polarization at local thermodynamic equilibrium with vorticity, magnetic field and feed-down

> The system created in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions is known to behave as an almost ideal liquid. In non-central collisions, due to the large orbital momentum, such a system might be the fluid with the highest vorticity ever created under laboratory conditions. Particles emerging from such a highly vorticous fluid are expected to be globally polarized with their spins on average pointing along the system angular momentum. Vorticity-induced polarization is the same for particles and antiparticles, but the intense magnetic field generated in these collisions may lead to the splitting in polarization. In this paper we outline the thermal approach to the calculation of the global polarization phenomenon for particles with spin and we discuss the details of the experimental study of this phenomenon, estimating the effect of feed-down. A general formula is derived for the polarization transfer in two-body decays and, particularly, for strong and electromagnetic decays. We find that accounting for such effects is crucial when extracting vorticity and magnetic field from the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general formula for polarization transfer in two-body decays and demonstrates that accounting for feed-down effects from heavier particle decays is crucial when extracting vorticity and magnetic field from experimental data on global hyperon polarization, contrary to the abstract's implication that the thermal approach alone is sufficient.

## Method

The authors develop a thermal approach to global polarization by extending the Cooper-Frye formula to include spin degrees of freedom at local thermodynamic equilibrium, using the thermal vorticity tensor (rather than kinematical vorticity) as the fundamental quantity. They derive the mean spin vector for spin-1/2 particles at leading order in thermal vorticity, then generalize to higher spins and include electromagnetic field effects by replacing thermal vorticity with a combination of thermal vorticity and the electromagnetic field tensor scaled by the magnetic moment. The formalism is applied to calculate polarization transfer in two-body decays, particularly for strong and electromagnetic decays of heavier resonances into Lambda hyperons.

## Key numbers

* Estimated non-relativistic vorticity in heavy ion collisions: approximately 0.02 fm^-1 ≈ 10^22 s^-1, based on a velocity difference of 0.1c across a transverse size of about 5 fm
* Thermal vorticity estimated as ω/T ~ 0.01 (percent level) using T ~ 100 MeV and the above vorticity estimate
* Lambda decay constant α_Λ = 0.642 (with α_Λbar = -0.642)
* Lambda magnetic moment μ_Λ ≈ -0.61 μ_N = -0.61 e/(2m_p)
* A 0.1% difference in polarization between primary Lambda and anti-Lambda would correspond to a magnetic field of order 10^-2 m_π^2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Becattini (2007, 2013) that linked global polarization to thermal vorticity at local thermodynamic equilibrium, and provides the quantitative tools needed to interpret experimental measurements from the STAR collaboration and other experiments. The paper enables extraction of both vorticity and magnetic field from polarization measurements of Lambda hyperons, provided feed-down corrections are applied, and builds on earlier qualitative proposals by Liang (2004) and Voloshin (2004) for polarization in peripheral heavy ion collisions. The formalism contrasts with alternative approaches without local thermodynamic equilibrium proposed by Ayala, Baznat (2015), and Aristova (2016).
