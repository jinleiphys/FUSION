# Neutron Star Properties with Hyperons

> In the light of the recent discovery of a neutron star with a mass accurately determined to be almost two solar masses, it has been suggested that hyperons cannot play a role in the equation of state of dense matter in $β$-equilibrium. We re-examine this issue in the most recent development of the quark-meson coupling model. Within a relativistic Hartree-Fock approach and including the full tensor structure at the vector-meson-baryon vertices, we find that not only must hyperons appear in matter at the densities relevant to such a massive star but that the maximum mass predicted is completely consistent with the observation.

## Key claim

The authors find that hyperons can appear in neutron star matter at densities relevant to a nearly two-solar-mass star, and that the maximum mass predicted by the quark-meson coupling model is fully consistent with the observed 1.97 solar mass pulsar, provided the full tensor structure of vector-meson-baryon couplings is included in a relativistic Hartree-Fock treatment.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which derives baryon properties from a quark-level MIT bag description, within a relativistic Hartree-Fock approach. They included the full tensor structure at the vector-meson-baryon vertices for all octet baryons, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for neutron star masses and radii. The model parameters were fixed by fitting symmetric nuclear matter properties (saturation density, binding energy, symmetry energy) and tested against hypernuclear data, with additional scenarios varying the cutoff mass in form factors, the tensor-to-vector coupling ratios, and the free nucleon radius.

## Key numbers

* The observed pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has a mass of 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses (Demorest et al., 2010).
* The baseline scenario (κ_I) yields a maximum neutron star mass of 1.93 solar masses, with a radius of 12.27 km and central density 5.52 times nuclear saturation density (n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}).
* The maximum mass across all scenarios ranges from 1.90 to 2.14 solar masses, with the highest value (2.23 solar masses) corresponding to a cutoff Λ = 1.3 GeV that exceeds the nuclear matter incompressibility limit of K < 315 MeV.
* The Ξ- hyperon threshold occurs at baryon density 0.42 fm^{-3} in the baseline scenario, while the Λ threshold occurs at higher density (0.91 fm^{-3} for Ξ0).
* The nuclear matter incompressibility K ranges from 283 MeV (Hartree Only) to 329 MeV (Λ = 1.3 GeV), with the baseline value at 298 MeV.
* The nucleon-only scenario (hyperons artificially excluded) gives a maximum mass of 2.26 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Hartree-Fock study of Stone et al. (2007), who predicted neutron stars containing hyperons with masses up to 2 solar masses, by including the full tensor structure at vector-meson-baryon vertices. It also extends the work of Krein et al. (1998), who considered nucleons only, to all octet baryons, and complements the calculations of Miyatsu et al. (2011) by using the latest QMC model that reproduces key hypernuclear properties without additional parameter adjustments. The results contradict the suggestion that the discovery of a two-solar-mass neutron star rules out hyperons in dense matter, and enable further studies of cooling processes (direct URCA) and condensate formation in neutron stars.
