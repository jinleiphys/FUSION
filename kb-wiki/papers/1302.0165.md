# Shear viscosity of nuclear matter

> In this talk I report my recent study on the shear viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter from a relaxation time approach. An isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction is used in the study. Effects of density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry of nuclear matter on its shear viscosity have been discussed. Similar to the symmetry energy, the symmetry shear viscosity is defined and its density and temperature dependence are studied.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity and specific shear viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter increase with isospin asymmetry and roughly satisfy a parabolic approximation, with the second-order coefficient defined as the symmetry shear viscosity; the specific shear viscosity at higher temperatures is about 4–5 times the lower limit from AdS/CFT, close to that of quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The author used a relaxation time approach linearizing the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation with an isospin- and momentum-dependent mean-field potential (MDI). In-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections were obtained from free-space parameterizations (pp/nn and np) modified by the in-medium effective mass ratio. The shear viscosity was then calculated from the relaxation time and the local momentum distribution near the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers
- Specific shear viscosity at higher temperatures is about 4–5 times the lower limit from AdS/CFT calculation (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005).
- Free-space pp(nn) scattering cross section parameterization: σ_pp(nn) = 13.73 − 15.04/v + 8.76/v² + 68.67v⁴ (in mb, where v is projectile velocity relative to target).
- Free-space np scattering cross section parameterization: σ_np = −70.67 − 18.18/v + 25.26/v² + 113.85v (in mb).
- In-medium NN cross section modification: σ_medium_NN = σ_NN × (μ*_NN / μ_NN)², where μ_NN (μ*_NN) is the free-space (in-medium) reduced mass.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of shear viscosity in nuclear matter (Danielewicz, 1984; Shi and Danielewicz, 2003; Chen et al., 2007; Pal, 2010; Li et al., 2011) by incorporating isospin asymmetry effects using the MDI interaction, which had previously been applied to nuclear symmetry energy constraints (Chen et al., 2005; Li et al., 2005; Xiao et al., 2009). The results enable future studies of transport properties of neutron-rich nuclear matter formed in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, particularly the newly defined symmetry shear viscosity and symmetry specific shear viscosity.
