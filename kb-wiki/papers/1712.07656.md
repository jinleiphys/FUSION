# Resummed Photon Spectra for WIMP Annihilation

> We construct an effective field theory (EFT) description of the hard photon spectrum for heavy WIMP annihilation. This facilitates precision predictions relevant for line searches, and allows the incorporation of non-trivial energy resolution effects. Our framework combines techniques from non-relativistic EFTs and soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), as well as its multi-scale extensions that have been recently introduced for studying jet substructure. We find a number of interesting features, including the simultaneous presence of SCET$_{\text{I}}$ and SCET$_{\text{II}}$ modes, as well as collinear-soft modes at the electroweak scale. We derive a factorization formula that enables both the resummation of the leading large Sudakov double logarithms that appear in the perturbative spectrum, and the inclusion of Sommerfeld enhancement effects. Consistency of this factorization is demonstrated to leading logarithmic order through explicit calculation. Our final result contains both the exclusive and the inclusive limits, thereby providing a unifying description of these two previously-considered approximations. We estimate the impact on experimental sensitivity, focusing for concreteness on an SU(2)$_{W}$ triplet fermion dark matter - the pure wino - where the strongest constraints are due to a search for gamma-ray lines from the Galactic Center. We find numerically significant corrections compared to previous results, thereby highlighting the importance of accounting for the photon spectrum when interpreting data from current and future indirect detection experiments.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective field theory (EFT) framework that unifies previously separate exclusive and inclusive approximations for the hard photon spectrum from heavy WIMP annihilation, enabling resummation of both electroweak Sudakov double logarithms and endpoint logarithms that appear when the photon energy approaches the kinematic endpoint, with the condition that the framework applies when the final state X recoiling against the photon has invariant mass much smaller than the WIMP mass.

## Method

The authors combine non-relativistic dark matter effective theory (NRDM) with soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) and its multi-scale extensions to factorize the dynamics at four distinct scales: the hard scale M_chi, the jet scale M_chi sqrt(1-z), the soft scale M_chi (1-z), and the electroweak scale m_W. They derive a factorization formula that separates Sudakov logarithms, Sommerfeld enhancement effects, and collinear-soft mode contributions, then perform renormalization group evolution between scales to resum all large logarithms to leading logarithmic order. The calculation is performed explicitly for the case of an SU(2)_W triplet fermion (pure wino) dark matter annihilating to photons.

## Key numbers

* For a 3 TeV wino, the cumulative cross section at z_cut ~ 0.8-0.9 (motivated by HESS energy resolution) falls approximately halfway between the fully inclusive (z_cut=0) and fully exclusive (z_cut=1) limits, with a factor of 2.2 difference between the exclusive and inclusive calculations
* The wino mass from relic density calculations (including Sommerfeld enhancement) is around 3 TeV
* The DM velocity in the halo is v ~ 10^{-3}, justifying the non-relativistic description
* The energy resolution parameter z_cut varies from 0.83 to 0.89 as M_chi goes from 500 GeV to 10 TeV for HESS

## Context

This work extends and unifies two previously separate approaches to WIMP annihilation photon spectra: the exclusive calculations of Bauer et al. (2014) and Ovanesyan et al. (2014, 2016) that resummed logarithms for gamma-gamma or gamma-Z final states, and the inclusive operator product expansion (OPE) approach of Baumgart et al. (2014, 2015) that assumed a gamma+X final state. The paper resolves the differing conclusions between these approaches by showing that the physically relevant region lies between the two extremes, and that proper treatment of finite detector energy resolution requires the endpoint EFT developed here. The framework enables precision predictions for current experiments (HESS, HAWC, VERITAS, MAGIC) and future experiments (CTA) searching for gamma-ray lines from dark matter annihilation.
