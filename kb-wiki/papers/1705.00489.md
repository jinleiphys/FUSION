# Nucleon-anti-nucleon intruder state of Dirac equation for nucleon in deep scalar potential well

> We solve the Dirac radial equation for a nucleon in a scalar Woods-Saxon potential well of depth $V_0$ and radius $r_0$. A sequence of values for the depth and radius are considered. For shallow potentials with $-1000 MeV\lesssim V_0 < 0$ the wave functions for the positive-energy states $Ψ_+(r)$ are dominated by their nucleon component $g(r)$. But for deeper potentials with $V_0 \lesssim -1500 MeV $ the $Ψ_+(r)$s begin to have dominant anti-nucleon component $f(r)$. In particular, a special intruder state enters with wave function $Ψ_{1/2}(r)$ and energy $E_{1/2}$. We have considered several $r_0$ values between 2 and 8 fm. For $V_0 \lesssim -2000 MeV$ and the above $r_0$ values, $Ψ_{1/2}$ is the only bound positive-energy state and has its $g(r)$ closely equal to $-f(r)$, both having a narrow wave-packet shape centered around $r_0$. The $E_{1/2}$ of this state is practically independent of $V_0$ for the above $V_0$ range and obeys closely the relation $E_{1/2}=\frac{\hbar c}{r_0}$.

## Key claim

For a nucleon in a scalar Woods-Saxon potential well deeper than about -1500 MeV, the lowest positive-energy state becomes a special "intruder state" with wave function \(\Psi_{1/2}\) and energy \(E_{1/2}\) that is practically independent of the well depth \(V_0\) and obeys \(E_{1/2} = \hbar c / r_0\), but the abstract's claim that this state is "the only bound positive-energy state" for \(V_0 \lesssim -2000\) MeV is conditional on the specific range of radii considered (2 to 8 fm) and on the definition of bound states as those with \(|E| < mc^2\).

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac radial equation for a nucleon (mass \(mc^2 = 938\) MeV, \(\hbar c = 197.3\) MeV-fm) in a scalar Woods-Saxon potential \(V_s(r) = V_0 / (1 + e^{(r - r_0)/\delta})\) with diffuseness \(\delta = 0.1\) fm, considering the \(l=0\), \(j=1/2\) (k = -1) state. They used a numerical matching condition based on the ratio \(f/g\) at a matching radius to determine bound-state eigenvalues, and varied the well depth \(V_0\) from about -50 MeV to -5400 MeV and the radius \(r_0\) from 2 to 8 fm.

## Key numbers

* For \(V_0 = -50\) MeV and \(r_0 = 4\) fm, the lowest positive-energy state has energy \(E_{1+} = 897.954\) MeV with an anti-nucleon fraction \(\langle f|f \rangle = 0.004645\); the highest negative-energy state has \(E_{1-} = -908.101\) MeV with \(\langle f|f \rangle = 0.9908\).
* For \(V_0 = -1500\) MeV and \(r_0 = 4\) fm, the intruder state has energy \(E_{1+} = 50.7829\) MeV and anti-nucleon fraction \(\langle f|f \rangle = 0.4960\); for \(V_0 = -1700\) MeV, \(E_{1+} = 49.9197\) MeV and \(\langle f|f \rangle = 0.4973\).
* For \(V_0 \lesssim -1500\) MeV and \(r_0 = 4\) fm, the intruder state energy plateaus at about 50 MeV, and the effective mass ratio \(m^*/m \equiv E_{1+}/(mc^2)\) flattens at about 0.05, remaining flat down to \(V_0 = -5400\) MeV.
* For the intruder state with \(V_0 = -1400\) MeV and \(r_0 = 4\) fm, the norms of the combinations are \(N_+ = \langle f+g|f+g \rangle = 0.00004\) and \(N_- = \langle f-g|f-g \rangle = 0.99996\); for \(V_0 = -1500\) MeV, \(N_+ = 0.00002\) and \(N_- = 0.99998\).
* The energy relation \(E_{1/2} \simeq \hbar c / r_0\) is verified for \(V_0 = -3000\) MeV and \(r_0\) values from 2 to 7 fm, with the approximation agreeing remarkably well with full numerical solutions.

## Context

This work extends the study of intruder states in nuclear shell-model effective interactions, as pioneered by Brown and collaborators (Brown and Kuo, 1967; Kuo and Brown, 1966), to the Dirac equation for a nucleon in a strong scalar potential. The authors draw an analogy to the intruder \(2^+\) state in \(^{18}O\) found in shell-model calculations using \(V_{lowk}\) interactions (Dong et al., 2014), where a \(4p2h\) state is pushed down into the \(2p0h\) model space. The results suggest that in extremely deep scalar potentials, such as those estimated near medium-mass black holes (gravitational potential approximately -1400 MeV), nucleons may exist in a special "half-nucleon" intruder state with equal nucleon and anti-nucleon components, which could motivate future studies using the Dirac equation in curved spacetime.
