# Revisiting model relations between T-odd transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions and generalized parton distributions

> We revisit the connection between generalized parton distributions in impact parameter space and T-odd effects in single spin asymmetries of the semi-inclusive deep inelastic process. We show that nontrivial relations can be established only under very specific conditions, typically realized only in models that describe hadrons as two-body bound systems and involving a helicity-conserving coupling between the gauge boson and the spectator system. Examples of these models are the the scalar-diquark spectator model or the quark-target model for the nucleon, and relativistic models for the pion at the lowest order in the Fock-space expansion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nontrivial relations between T-odd transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) and generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in impact parameter space can be established only under very specific conditions, typically realized only in models that describe hadrons as two-body bound systems with a helicity-conserving coupling between the gauge boson and the spectator system.

## Method

The authors analyze the formal connection between the average transverse momentum of T-odd effects in single spin asymmetries and impact-parameter dependent distributions (IPDs) by examining the operator structure of final-state interactions (FSIs) in the light-cone gauge. They derive the conditions under which the FSI operator factorizes into a lensing function and an IPD, requiring that the FSI connects Fock states with the same number of constituents, transfers transverse momentum proportionally to each spectator's longitudinal momentum fraction, and does not transfer longitudinal momentum. They then test these conditions explicitly in model calculations for the pion described as a quark-antiquark bound state and for the proton described as a three-quark system and as a quark-diquark system.

## Key numbers

- The Boer-Mulders function for the pion in the quark-antiquark model is expressed as \( k_{\perp}^k h_{1,\pi}^\perp(x, \mathbf{k}^2) = \frac{2\alpha_s}{(2\pi)^4} \frac{4}{3} T_\pi^2 M_\pi \int \frac{d\mathbf{q}}{\mathbf{q}^2} G^k(x, \mathbf{k} | x, \mathbf{k} - \mathbf{q}) \), where \(\alpha_s\) is the strong coupling constant and the factor 4/3 arises from color factors.
- The relation between the gluon transverse momentum \(\mathbf{q}\) and the GPD momentum transfer \(\mathbf{\Delta}\) is given by \( -\mathbf{q} = (1-x)\mathbf{\Delta} \), where \(x\) is the light-cone momentum fraction of the active quark.
- The chiral-odd IPD for the pion is obtained as \( \frac{i b_\perp^k}{M_\pi} (\widetilde{\mathcal{H}}_{T,\pi}(x, \mathbf{b}_\perp^2))' = \frac{T_\pi^2}{2(2\pi)^5(1-x)^2} \mathcal{G}^k(x, \frac{\mathbf{b}}{1-x} | x, \frac{\mathbf{b}}{1-x}) \).

## Context

This work extends the earlier model relations proposed by Burkardt (2002, 2003) connecting the Sivers effect to GPDs via a chromodynamic lensing function, and by Meissner, Metz, and Goeke (2007) for spectator models. It contradicts the assumption that such relations hold generally, showing they are violated in three-quark model calculations for the nucleon by Pasquini, Boffi, and collaborators (2008, 2010). The paper enables a precise understanding of when phenomenological extractions of the Sivers function, such as that by Bacchetta et al. (2011), can be justified theoretically, and clarifies the limitations of extending these relations beyond two-body bound-state models.
