# Diagnosing the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Brief review of the hadronic probes that are used to diagnose the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions and interrogate its properties. Emphasis is placed on probes that have significantly impacted our understanding of the nature of the quark-gluon plasma and confirmed its formation.

## Key claim

Hadronic probes have moved from speculative "smoking gun" signatures to a portfolio of rigorous, theory-based analyses that quantitatively confirm the formation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with unconfined, collectively flowing quarks and near-perfect fluidity, though the extraction of QGP properties still carries quantifiable uncertainties that future higher-statistics data will reduce.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical and experimental development of hadronic probes for diagnosing the QGP produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions, focusing on three categories: quark-sensitive observables (strangeness equilibration, hadronization via recombination, and fluctuations of conserved quantum numbers), and gluon-sensitive observables (shear and bulk viscosity extracted via Bayesian model-to-data comparisons, jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$, and color screening of heavy quarkonia). The review synthesizes results from fixed-target experiments at the CERN SPS and BNL AGS, collider experiments at RHIC and the LHC, and modern multi-parameter Bayesian analyses that connect transport parameters to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature limit for hadronic matter: approximately 160 MeV.
- Rafelski's original prediction: $\overline{s}/\overline{q} \approx 5$ in QGP with stopped nuclear matter.
- 't Hooft coupling at experimentally relevant temperatures (few hundred MeV): $\lambda \approx 10$.
- WA97 experiment at SPS: enhancement of $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ production by about a factor of 20 compared to proton-proton collisions.
- STAR experiment at RHIC: Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV; SPS WA97 results for Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$ GeV.
- Specific shear viscosity $\eta/s$ of QGP: close to the quantum bound $1/(4\pi)$, much smaller than common materials like helium or water.
- Normalized jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}/T^3$: in the range of four, deduced from RHIC and LHC data on jet suppression.

## Context

This review extends the original "smoking gun" signatures identified by the Nuclear Science Advisory Committee in 1996 (NSAC, 1996; Harris and Muller, 1996) and the early strangeness enhancement predictions by Rafelski (1980, 1982). It builds on the quark recombination model developed by Fries, Nonaka, Bass, and Muller (2003) and the valence quark scaling of elliptic flow proposed by Molnar and Voloshin (2003). The paper enables future quantitative extraction of QGP transport parameters using Bayesian analyses (Bernhard, 2019; Everett, 2020) and open quantum system approaches for heavy quarkonia (Yao, 2020; Akamatsu, 2020; Brambilla, 2020), with improved data expected from the RHIC beam energy scan, sPHENIX, and LHC Run 3.
