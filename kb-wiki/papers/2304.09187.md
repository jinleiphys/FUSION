# Soft gluon self-energy at finite temperature and density: hard NLO corrections in general covariant gauge

> We compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) hard correction to the gluon self-energy tensor with arbitrary soft momenta in a hot and/or dense weakly coupled plasma in Quantum Chromodynamics. Our diagrammatic computations of the two-loop and power corrections are performed within the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) framework and in general covariant gauge, using the real-time formalism. We find that after renormalization our individual results are finite and gauge-dependent, and they reproduce previously computed results in Quantum Electrodynamics in the appropriate limit. Combining our results, we also recover a formerly known gauge-independent matching coefficient and associated screening mass in a specific kinematic limit. Our NLO results supersede leading-order HTL results from the 1980s and pave the way to an improved understanding of the bulk properties of deconfined matter, such as the equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper computes the next-to-leading order (NLO) hard correction to the gluon self-energy tensor in a hot and/or dense weakly coupled QCD plasma, but this NLO refers specifically to the expansion within the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) effective theory, not to the complete self-energy; the results are individually gauge-dependent and only recover gauge independence in specific kinematic limits.

## Method

The authors performed diagrammatic computations of two-loop and power corrections to the gluon self-energy within the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) framework using the real-time formalism in general covariant gauge (R_xi class). They expanded the self-energy in small coupling and external momentum, separating contributions into one-loop HTL, two-loop HTL, and power correction terms, then projected the tensor components onto a four-dimensional basis (transverse, longitudinal, C, and D) using the rest frame of the thermal medium. The internal zero-component integrals were performed using the residue theorem on Delta^d_n propagators, and the remaining spatial integrals were expanded in powers of K/T and evaluated with dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

* The effective thermal mass scale m_E^2 is of order g_s^2 T^2, setting the scale for the HTL expansion.
* The one-loop HTL self-energy (Pi_{1,HTL}) scales as m_E^2, the two-loop HTL correction (Pi_{2,HTL}) scales as m_E^2 g_s^2, and the power correction (Pi_{1,Pow}) scales as K^2 g_s^2.
* The Slavnov-Taylor identity requires the self-energy component Pi_D to satisfy Pi_D = -Pi_C^2 / (K^2 + Pi_L), which is verified perturbatively at O(g_s^4) as Pi_D^(2-loop) = -(Pi_C^(1-loop))^2 / K^2.
* In the HTL limit, Pi_D vanishes at O(g_s^4) but becomes nonzero at O(g_s^6) due to finite HTL contributions to Pi_C and Pi_L.

## Context

This work extends previous diagrammatic calculations of HTL corrections in Quantum Electrodynamics at high temperature (Manuel, 2016; Carignano, 2017, 2019) and high chemical potential (Gorda, 2022) to the non-Abelian case of QCD in general covariant gauge, and complements a recent kinetic-theory approach in Feynman gauge (Ekstedt, 2023). The results supersede leading-order HTL results from the 1980s (Braaten, 1989) and enable improved calculations of bulk properties of deconfined matter such as the equation of state, particularly for the N3LO pressure of QCD at zero temperature where these corrections have already been partially applied (Gorda, 2021).
