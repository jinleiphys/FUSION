# Theoretical estimates of the width of light-meson states in the SO(4) (2+1)-flavor limit

> The low-energy sector of the mesonic spectrum exhibits some features which may be understood in terms of the SO(4) symmetry contained in the QCD-Hamiltonian written in the Coulomb Gauge. In our previous work we have shown that this is indeed the case when the Instantaneous Color-Charge Interaction (ICCI) is treated by means of non-perturbative many-body linearization techniques. Continuing along this line of description in this work we calculate the width of meson states belonging to the low portion of the spectrum (E <1 GeV ). In spite of the rather simple structure of the Hamiltonian used to calculate the spectra of pseudoscalar and vector mesons, the results for the width of these states follow the pattern of the data.

# Theoretical estimates of the width of light-meson states in the SO(4) (2+1)-flavor limit

## Key claim
The widths of low-lying pseudoscalar and vector meson states (E < 1 GeV) calculated from a minimal SO(4)-symmetric QCD Hamiltonian in the Coulomb Gauge, treated via the Random Phase Approximation (RPA), reproduce the experimental pattern of widths despite the model's simple structure. The calculated widths are parameter-free once the spectrum of physical and background states is defined, but the identification of which RPA eigenstates correspond to which physical mesons relies on analyzing their quark-antiquark pair content.

## Method
The authors diagonalized an SO(4)-symmetric QCD Hamiltonian in the Coulomb Gauge using the RPA method, with four different parameter sets (Set-1 through Set-4) for the Hamiltonian coefficients. They then calculated the width of each meson-like state by coupling it to a background of less-collective or non-collective RPA states via a residual interaction V, using the formalism of Bes and Mottelson. The matrix elements V_{a,alpha} were taken as constants for each state and parameter set, and the width Gamma was computed from the energy variance of the resulting mixed eigenstates.

## Key numbers
- Experimental widths from the Particle Data Group (2016): eta (1.3 MeV), rho (147.8 MeV), omega (8.5 MeV), K* (50.8 MeV), eta' (0.2 MeV), phi (4.3 MeV)
- Calculated rho/omega width: 147.84 MeV (Set-1), 147.46 MeV (Set-2), 147.14 MeV (Set-3), 147.23 MeV (Set-4)
- Calculated eta width: 1.32 MeV (Set-1), 1.32 MeV (Set-2), 1.30 MeV (Set-3), 1.32 MeV (Set-4)
- Calculated K/K* (low) width: 50.82 MeV (Set-1), 50.90 MeV (Set-2), 50.63 MeV (Set-4)
- Calculated K/K* (high) width: 50.74 MeV (Set-1), 50.81 MeV (Set-2), 50.99 MeV (Set-3), 50.99 MeV (Set-4)
- Calculated phi width: 4.30 MeV (Set-1), 4.32 MeV (Set-2), 4.29 MeV (Set-3), 4.32 MeV (Set-4)
- RPA energies for pion-like states: 184.81 MeV (Set-1), 164.49 MeV (Set-2), 187.27 MeV (Set-3), 201.37 MeV (Set-4)
- RPA energies for rho/omega-like states: 579.10 MeV (Set-1), 590.31 MeV (Set-2), 364.98 MeV (Set-3), 622.56 MeV (Set-4)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Yepez-Martinez 2016, Yepez-Martinez 2017) where the SO(4) symmetry of the QCD Hamiltonian in the Coulomb Gauge was used to describe the low-lying meson spectrum, and where the RPA method was applied to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors for pseudoscalar and vector meson states. It also builds on the work of Amor (2017), which extended the SO(4) formalism to a larger basis with a confining Coulomb-plus-linear potential. The present calculation of widths tests the physical validity of the RPA wave functions by comparing with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2016). The results enable future application of the same width-calculation procedure to more involved situations, such as the non-SO(4)-restricted RPA treatment of Amor (2017).
