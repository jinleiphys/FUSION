# Electromagnetic modulation of monochromatic neutrino beams

> A possibility to produce a modulated monochromatic neutrino beam is discussed. Monochromatic neutrinos can be obtained in electron capture by nuclei of atoms or ions, in particular, by nuclei of hydrogen-like ions. It is shown that monochromatic neutrino beam from such hydrogen-like ions with nuclei of non-zero spin can be modulated because of different probabilities of electron capture from hyperfine states. Modulation arises by means of inducing of electromagnetic transitions between the hyperfine states. Requirements for the hydrogen-like ions with necessary properties are discussed. A list of the appropriate nuclei for such ions is presented.

# Electromagnetic modulation of monochromatic neutrino beams

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a monochromatic neutrino beam from electron-capture decay in hydrogen-like ions with non-zero nuclear spin can be modulated into pulses by inducing electromagnetic transitions between hyperfine states, but only for nuclei satisfying specific Gamow–Teller transition conditions and with energy release small enough to suppress competing beta-plus decay.

## Method

The authors identified candidate nuclei by requiring: non-zero nuclear spin, a Gamow–Teller transition with spin change of ±1 and same parity, branching fraction ≥98% to a single final nuclear state, and half-life less than 10⁶ seconds. They calculated hyperfine splitting energies using the point-like nucleus approximation, determined resonant wavelengths for M1 transitions, and computed spontaneous emission lifetimes for the upper hyperfine state. They classified ions as type A (electron capture allowed from the lower hyperfine state) or type F (electron capture allowed only from the upper state) based on the sign of the nuclear magnetic moment and the spin relation.

## Context

This work extends the concept of monochromatic neutrino beams from electron-capture decay (EC-beams), originally proposed by Sato (2005) and Bernabeu (2005), by adding the possibility of time modulation. It builds on the hyperfine effect observed in GSI storage-ring experiments with hydrogen-like ions of ¹⁴⁰Pr, ¹⁴²Pm, and ¹²²I by Litvinov (2007, 2008) and Winckler (2009), where the electron-capture decay rate depended on the hyperfine state. The modulation idea was first stated for muonic atoms by Batkin (1977) and rediscovered for hydrogen-like ions by Folan (1995). The paper provides a specific list of seven candidate nuclei that satisfy the stringent requirements for producing a modulated monochromatic beam, enabling future experiments to suppress constant background noise through pulsed neutrino signals.
