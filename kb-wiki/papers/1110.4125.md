# Glimpsing Colour in a World of Black and White

> The past 40 years have taught us that nucleons are built of constituents that carry colour charges with interactions governed by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). How experiments (past, present and future) at Jefferson Lab probe colourless nuclei to map out these internal colour degrees of freedom is presented. When combined with theoretical calculations, these will paint a picture of how the confinement of quarks and gluons, and the structure of the QCD vacuum, determine the properties of all (light) strongly interacting states.

## Key claim

Jefferson Lab experiments can probe the internal colour degrees of freedom of colourless nuclei, and when combined with theoretical calculations, these will reveal how confinement of quarks and gluons and the structure of the QCD vacuum determine the properties of all light strongly interacting states. The paper hedges that while the underlying theory (QCD) is confirmed at short distances, accurate theoretical calculations at the distance scale of ~1 fm where the strong interaction is strong are much more difficult, so experiment provides critical guidance.

## Method

The paper reviews how Jefferson Lab uses an electron beam to probe protons and neutrons, both alone and within nuclei, via deep inelastic scattering to study parton momentum distributions and generalized parton distributions (GPDs). It also reviews hadron spectroscopy efforts, including lattice QCD calculations of the baryon spectrum and hybrid meson spectrum, and coupled-channel analyses (such as the EBAC effort) that fit differential cross-sections and polarization asymmetries for meson, photon, and electron induced baryon production channels to determine masses, widths, and couplings of excited baryon resonances.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD calculations of the baryon spectrum were performed at a pion mass of 520 MeV, with states grouped into SU(6)×O(3) multiplets in units of the Ω mass
- Lattice QCD calculations predict 1⁻⁺ hybrid meson states below 2 GeV, with 0⁺⁻ and 2⁺⁻ states heavier by several hundred MeV
- The EBAC analysis of πN partial waves shows that when ηN and πΔ decay channels are switched off in the P₁₁ channel, the N*(1440) (Roper) and N*(1710) poles move to the real axis merging as a single entity at ~1800 MeV
- The VES experiment at 37 GeV/c found an ηπ enhancement around 1400 MeV and a broad enhancement ~1600 MeV in η'π
- COMPASS at 190 GeV saw a broad 1⁻⁺ signal in 3π
- The BNL E852 experiment claimed a π₁(1600) peak, but its signal in the 3π channel could change by a factor of 4 depending on treatment of dipion final state interactions

## Context

This review extends the work of lattice QCD calculations by Dudek et al. (2009) on hybrid meson spectra and the EBAC coupled-channel analyses by Julia-Diaz et al. (2007) and Lee (2008) on baryon resonances. It also references the experimental results from GAMS, VES, BNL-E852, and COMPASS on exotic meson candidates. The paper enables future searches for hybrid mesons and gluonic degrees of freedom using the JLab 12 GeV upgrade with the GlueX detector and CLAS12, as well as analyses of data from BaBar, Belle, BESIII, LHCb, and PANDA@FAIR.
