# Comparative Regge analysis of $Λ, Σ^0, Λ(1520)$ and $Θ^+$ production in $γp$, $πp$ and $pp$ reactions

> Using the Quark-Gluon Strings Model -- combined with Regge phenomenology -- we perform a comparative analysis of $Λ$, $Σ^0$, $Λ(1520)$ and $Θ^+$ production in binary reactions induced by photon, pion and proton beams on the nucleon. We find that the existing experimental data on the $γp \to K^+ Λ$ differential and total cross sections can be described very well by the model for photon energies $1 - 16$ GeV and $-t < 2$ GeV$^2$ assuming a dominant contribution of the $K^*$ Regge trajectory. Moreover, using the same parameters we also reproduce the total $γp \to K^+ Σ^0$ and $γp \to K^+ Λ(1520)$ cross sections suggesting a 'universality' of the Regge model. In order to check the consistency of the approach we evaluate the differential and total cross sections for the reaction $π^- p \to K^0 Λ$ which is also found to be dominated by the $K^*$ Regge trajectory. Using the apparent 'universality' of the Regge model we extend our scheme to the analysis of the binary reactions $γp \to \bar{K}^0 Θ^+$, $π^- p \to K^- Θ^+$ and $pp \to Σ^+ Θ^+$ as well as the exclusive and inclusive $Θ^+$ production in the reactions $pp \to p \bar{K}^0 Θ^+$ and $pp \to Θ^+ X$. Our detailed studies demonstrate that $Θ^+$ production does not follow the 'universality' principle thus suggesting an essentially different internal structure of the exotic baryon relative to conventional hyperons or hyperon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Quark-Gluon Strings Model (QGSM) combined with Regge phenomenology can describe existing data for conventional hyperon production (Λ, Σ⁰, Λ(1520)) via a "universality" principle for the K* Regge trajectory couplings, but that Θ⁺ production violates this universality by almost three orders of magnitude, suggesting a fundamentally different internal structure for the exotic pentaquark.

## Method

The authors applied the QGSM with a logarithmic K* Regge trajectory to compute differential and total cross sections for binary reactions γp → K⁺Λ, γp → K⁺Σ⁰, γp → K⁺Λ(1520), and π⁻p → K⁰Λ, fixing coupling constants and form factor parameters from fits to γp → K⁺Λ data. They then extended the same model with identical parameters to predict cross sections for Θ⁺ production in γp → K̄⁰Θ⁺, π⁻p → K⁻Θ⁺, pp → Σ⁺Θ⁺, and inclusive/exclusive pp → Θ⁺X reactions, comparing predictions against experimental upper limits from SAPHIR, CLAS, and COSY-TOF collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The γp → K⁺Λ differential cross section is well described for photon energies 1–16 GeV and −t < 2 GeV² with coupling constants g_{ρKK*} = 5.8 and g_{pK*Λ} ≈ 3.5, and form factor parameters B₁ = 5 GeV⁻², R²₁ = 1.13 GeV⁻².
- The γp → K⁺Λ(1520) total cross section is described within 40% using g_{pK*Y} = 4.14, consistent with the universality principle.
- The SAPHIR collaboration estimated σ(γp → K̄⁰Θ⁺) ≈ 200 nb at an average photon energy of ~2 GeV, requiring g_{pK*Θ} ≈ 1.8 (0.4 times the hyperon coupling).
- The CLAS collaboration preliminary upper limit is σ(γp → K̄⁰Θ⁺) ≤ 1–4 nb, requiring g_{pK*Θ} ≈ 0.25 (0.06 times the hyperon coupling), a violation of universality by almost 3 orders of magnitude.
- For π⁻p → K⁰Λ, the model uses g_{πK*K} = 5.8, g_{pK*Λ} ≈ 4.5, B = 0, R²₁ = 2.13 GeV⁻², and describes data at p_lab = 4.5, 6, 8, 10.7, and 15.7 GeV/c.
- The predicted π⁻p → K⁻Θ⁺ cross section reaches about 10 μb (using SAPHIR coupling) or 0.2 μb (using CLAS coupling) at maximum.
- For pp → Θ⁺Σ⁺, the COSY-TOF collaboration measured σ = 0.4 ± 0.1(stat) ± 0.1(syst) μb at beam momentum 2.95 GeV/c; the model with CLAS coupling gives ~16 nb, a factor ~25 smaller.
- The inclusive pp → Θ⁺X cross section via kaon exchange with G_{ΘKN} ≤ 1.4 is about 1.5 μb at high energies.

## Context

This work extends the QGSM approach previously applied by Kaidalov (1990s) to binary hadronic reactions and by Grishina et al. (2004) to deuteron photodisintegration, now applied to strangeness production. It contradicts earlier isobar model studies by Oh, Nam, Li, Ko, and others (2003–2004) that predicted Θ⁺ photoproduction cross sections ranging from several nb to almost 1 μb. The finding that Θ⁺ production violates the universality observed for conventional hyperons enables future experimental searches to distinguish between the pentaquark and conventional baryon production mechanisms, and provides constraints on models of the Θ⁺ internal structure such as the quark-soliton model of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) or the diquark correlation model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003).
