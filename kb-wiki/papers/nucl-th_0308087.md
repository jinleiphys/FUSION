# Thermal production of the $ρ$ meson in the $π^+π^-$ channel

> Recent measurements of the pi^+pi^- invariant mass distribution at RHIC show a shifted peak for the rho meson in 100A GeV in peripheral Au + Au and even in p + p collisions. A recent theoretical study based on a picture of in-medium production rates of pions, showed that a large shift could result from a combination of the Boltzmann factor and the collisional broadening of the rho. Here we argue that the two-pion density of states is the appropriate quantity if one assumes a sudden break-up of the system. Methods for calculating the density of states which include Bose effects are derived. The resulting invariant mass distributions are significantly enhanced at lower masses and the rho peak is shifted downward by ~ 35 MeV.

## Key claim

If one assumes a sudden break-up of the system, the two-pion density of states (rather than the in-medium production rate) is the appropriate quantity for predicting the π⁺π⁻ invariant mass distribution; the resulting ρ peak is shifted downward by ~35 MeV, but the authors caution that this shift depends critically on the assumptions of sudden breakup and neglect of finite-size effects, and that the experimental shift appears 10–20 MeV stronger than their model predicts.

## Method

The authors calculate the two-pion density of states from π⁺π⁻ phase shifts, including contributions from s-wave (I=0 and I=2), p-wave (ρ meson), and d-wave channels, using experimental phase shift parametrizations. They then convolute this density of states with a Boltzmann factor e⁻ᴹ/ᵀ and integrate over the three-momentum of the pair to obtain the thermal invariant mass distribution. Bose-Einstein corrections are incorporated by modifying the τ matrix (tan δ) with factors (1+f(q))(1+f(−q)) that account for phase-space filling, and the resulting density of states is recalculated for each total pair momentum.

## Key numbers

- The ρ peak in the π⁺π⁻ invariant mass distribution is shifted downward by ~35 MeV relative to the nominal ρ mass (771.1 MeV) when using the two-pion density of states with a breakup temperature of 110 MeV and chemical potential of 90 MeV.
- The ρ spectral function itself peaks ~5 MeV below the nominal mass; additional form factor choices may shift it another ~5 MeV.
- For T=170 MeV (appropriate for pp collisions) and zero chemical potential, the shift is ~20 MeV.
- The s-wave I=0 scattering length used is a=0.204/m_π; the coefficient b=9.1×10⁻⁴ GeV⁻¹ reproduces experimental phase shifts.
- The d-wave I=0 coefficient is c=6.2 GeV⁻¹; the I=2, ℓ=2 phase shift is δ=−8.4 q⁵ + 12.5 q⁶ GeV⁻¹.
- The I=2, ℓ=0 effective range parameters are a=−0.13 MeV⁻¹ and R=1.0 MeV⁻¹.
- Bose effects lead to a near doubling of the distribution at low masses but shift the peak by only ~1 MeV.
- The mean Bose enhancement factor ⟨(1+f₁)(1+f₂)⟩ exceeds 2.0 for low pair momentum and low invariant mass at T=110 MeV, μ=90 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of ρ meson production in heavy-ion collisions beyond the in-medium production rate approach of Kolb and Prakash (2000) and Rapp (2001), which used decay rates corrected for collisional broadening and Bose effects. The authors argue that for a sudden breakup scenario (supported by HBT measurements from STAR and PHENIX), the two-pion density of states is the appropriate quantity rather than the decay rate. The paper enables future studies to incorporate finite-size effects, the influence of other resonances, and experimental acceptances, and it highlights the need for higher-statistics experimental analyses to distinguish thermal effects from possible in-medium mass shifts of the ρ.
