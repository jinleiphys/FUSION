# N* Mass Spectrum from an Anisotropic Action

> Results are reported for N* masses in the 1/2+ and 1/2- sectors on an anisotropic lattice. The gauge action is the usual plaquette plus rectangle type, the quark action is of the D234 type, both having tadpole-improved tree-level coefficients. Clear splittings from the nucleon ground state are observed with smeared operators and 500 configurations. The first excited states in each channel are further isolated, using a correlation matrix and the variational method. The basic pattern of these splittings is consistent with experimental observations.

# N* Mass Spectrum from an Anisotropic Action

## Key claim
The pattern of splittings between nucleon excited states and the ground state, computed on an anisotropic lattice with tadpole-improved actions, is mostly consistent with experimental observations, but the ordering of the Roper N(1440) and the N(1535) is inverted at the quark masses studied, and this ordering might reverse at smaller quark masses where meson cloud effects become important.

## Method
The authors used a 10^3 × 30 anisotropic lattice with spatial spacing a_s ≈ 0.24 fm and anisotropy ξ = a_s/a_t = 3, employing a tadpole-improved plaquette-plus-rectangle gauge action and a D234 quark action. Two independent local interpolating fields (χ_1 and χ_2) were used to construct a 2×2 correlation matrix, and the variational method was applied to isolate ground and first excited states in both the 1/2+ and 1/2- parity sectors. Gaussian smearing was applied at source and sink, and 500 configurations were analyzed with quark masses ranging from approximately 780 to 90 MeV, corresponding to π/ρ ratios from 0.93 to 0.60.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: 10^3 × 30 with a_s ≈ 0.24 fm and anisotropy ξ = 3
- 500 configurations analyzed
- Quark masses ranged from approximately 780 to 90 MeV
- π/ρ mass ratio ranged from 0.93 to 0.60
- Tadpole factor from plaquette mean link: u_s = 0.81 (u_t set to 1)
- The splitting of the Roper N(1440) from the nucleon is roughly 5 times that of the N(1620) from the N(1535)

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of the N* spectrum by Lee (1999) using an O(a^2) improved next-nearest-neighbor action, by Sasaki et al. (2000) using domain-wall fermions, and by Dong et al. (2000) using the non-perturbatively O(a) improved clover action. The use of an anisotropic lattice provides finer temporal resolution for studying extended and heavy states, and the variational method with two interpolating fields enables separation of the first excited states. The results enable future comparisons with chiral perturbation theory extrapolations as proposed by Leinweber (1999), and point toward the need for simulations with domain-wall or overlap quarks to address the chiral limit directly.
