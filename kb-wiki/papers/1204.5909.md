# Quark matter nucleation with a microscopic hadronic equation of state

> The nucleation process of quark matter in cold (T = 0) stellar matter is investigated using the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach to describe the hadronic phase, and the MIT bag model, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio, and the Chromo Dielectric models to describe the deconfined phase of quark matter. The consequences of the nucleation process for neutron star physics are outlined. Hyperonic stars are metastable only for some of the quark matter equations of state considered. The effect of an hyperonic three body force on the metastability of compact stars is estimated, and it is shown that, except for the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and the MIT bag model with a large bag pressure, the other models predict the formation of hybrid stars with a maximum mass not larger than \sim 1.62 M\odot .

## Key claim

Using a microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock hadronic equation of state, hyperonic stars are metastable to quark matter nucleation only for some quark matter models; specifically, only the MIT bag model with a low bag constant (B=85 MeV fm⁻³) enables nucleation, and even then the resulting hybrid stars have maximum masses not exceeding approximately 1.62 M☉, which is below the measured mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 M☉).

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach to compute the hadronic equation of state for both nucleonic and hyperonic matter, using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon force with three-body forces (Model 1) and a separate BHF calculation with phenomenological hyperonic three-body force terms (Model 2). The quark phase was described using three models: the MIT bag model, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and the Chromo Dielectric model (CDM). The quantum nucleation time for quark matter droplets in cold (T=0) stellar matter was calculated via a semi-classical tunneling approach, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations were integrated to obtain stellar masses and critical masses for metastable hadronic stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of pure hadronic star sequence (Model 1, hyperonic matter): M_HS_max = 1.37 M☉
- Critical mass for MIT bag model (B=85 MeV fm⁻³) with Model 1 hyperonic matter: M_cr = 1.272 M☉, producing a hybrid star of M_fin = 1.233 M☉ with maximum hybrid star mass M_max_HybS = 1.544 M☉
- Maximum mass of pure hadronic star sequence (Model 1, only nucleons): M_HS_max = 2.27 M☉, with critical masses M_cr ~ 2.2 M☉ for all quark models, but all such stars collapse to a black hole
- For Model 2 parametrization P3 (K∞=285 MeV, x=1) with MIT bag (B=85 MeV fm⁻³): M_cr = 1.504 M☉, M_fin = 1.456 M☉, M_max_HybS = 1.574 M☉
- For Model 2 parametrization P3 with CDM: M_cr = 1.472 M☉, M_fin = 1.440 M☉, M_max_HybS = 1.624 M☉
- Energy released in stellar conversion for Model 2 P3 with MIT bag (B=85): E_conv = 85.57 × 10⁵¹ erg
- Measured mass of pulsar PSR J1614-2230: M = (1.97 ± 0.04) M☉

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark matter nucleation in neutron stars that used phenomenological relativistic mean field models (Glendenning and Moszkowski, 1991) by instead employing a microscopic BHF approach for the hadronic phase. It builds on earlier work by Bombaci et al. (2004, 2007) that studied the effect of hyperon couplings on critical masses and stellar conversion energy, and on a recent study by Bombaci et al. (2011) that applied the NJL and CDM models to the quark phase. The results show that with microscopic hadronic equations of state, the formation of hybrid stars is more restricted than previously thought, and that the 1.97 M☉ pulsar PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010) cannot be a hyperonic hybrid star under these models, suggesting it is likely a purely nucleonic hadronic star.
