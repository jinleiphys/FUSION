# Role of the Low-Lying Isoscalar Dipole Modes in the Polarization Potential

> An analysis of the real and imaginary part of the polarization potential in terms of the relative contributions of the single collective states for the $ ^{208}Pb + ^{208}Pb $ system has been done. The polarization potential has been calculated within the Feshbach formalism taking into account the collective states calculated with the Wigner function moments method. The contribution of the isoscalar giant dipole resonance states has been estimated being of the order of 10 - 20 \% of the total at relatively low incident energy.

## Key claim

The contribution of the low-lying isoscalar giant dipole resonance (GDR) states to the polarization potential for the \( ^{208}\text{Pb} + ^{208}\text{Pb} \) system is estimated to be 10–20% of the total at relatively low incident energy, a result that depends on the specific transition densities derived from the Wigner Function Moments method.

## Method

The polarization potential was calculated within the Feshbach formalism, using collective states obtained from the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method with a Skyrme-type effective force (SGII). Transition densities for the excited states were extracted from the WFM results via linear response theory, and the form factors were computed by double folding the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction with the ground state density of one nucleus and the transition density of the other. The non-local polarization potential was made local using a standard WKB approximation and a Bessel-function expansion.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of the isoscalar GDR states to the polarization potential is 10–20% of the total at relatively low incident energy.
- The ground state density was parametrized with a Fermi distribution: radius \( R = 1.115\,A^{1/3} - 0.53\,A^{-1/3} \) fm and diffuseness \( a = 0.568 \) fm.
- The polarization potential was evaluated at a fixed relative distance \( R = 16 \) fm as a function of incident energy.
- The low-lying \( 3^- \) state (LEOR) provides the most important contribution to both the real and imaginary parts of the polarization potential.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the polarization potential (e.g., Andres et al. (1991, 1992)) by including the newly identified low-lying isoscalar dipole modes, which were recently observed experimentally via \( (\alpha,\alpha^\prime \gamma) \) reactions in \( ^{208}\text{Pb} \), \( ^{90}\text{Zr} \), \( ^{58}\text{Ni} \), and \( ^{40}\text{Ca} \) (Holt et al. (1993)). The results show that the contribution of these compressional modes is comparable to that of the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR), especially for the imaginary part, and should be observable in elastic cross-section measurements. The findings differ quantitatively from earlier work (Andres et al. (1991, 1992)) due to the use of different transition densities.
