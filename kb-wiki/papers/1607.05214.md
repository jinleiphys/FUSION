# Lattice Prediction for Deeply Bound Doubly Heavy Tetraquarks

> We investigate the possibility of $qq^\prime \bar b \bar b$ tetraquark bound states using $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD ensembles with pion masses $\simeq 164$, $299$, and $415$ MeV. Motivated by observations from heavy baryon phenomenology, we consider two lattice interpolating operators both of which are expected to couple efficiently to tetraquark states: one with diquark-antidiquark and one with a meson-meson structure. Using nonrelativistic QCD to simulate the bottom quarks, we study the $ud\bar b \bar b$, $\ell s\bar b \bar b$ channels with $\ell=u,d$, and find unambiguous signals for strong-interaction-stable $J^P=1^+$ tetraquarks. These states are found to lie $189(10)$ and $98(7)$ MeV below the corresponding free two-meson thresholds.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of strong-interaction-stable doubly bottom tetraquarks in the $ud\bar b \bar b$ and $\ell s\bar b \bar b$ channels, with binding energies of 189(10) MeV and 98(7) MeV below their respective two-meson thresholds, but the authors note that these results depend on the validity of the heavy quark limit for bottom quarks and on a linear chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using $n_f=2+1$ Wilson-Clover ensembles from the PACS-CS collaboration at three pion masses (164, 299, and 415 MeV) with a fixed lattice spacing. Bottom quarks were simulated using nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD), and two interpolating operators were employed: one with a diquark-antidiquark structure and one with a meson-meson ($BB^*$ or $B_sB^*$) structure. A $2\times2$ generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) analysis was used to extract the ground state binding energies, followed by a linear chiral extrapolation in $m_\pi^2$ to the physical point.

## Key numbers

- $ud\bar b \bar b$ tetraquark binding energy: 189(10)(3) MeV below the $BB^*$ threshold at the physical point
- $\ell s\bar b \bar b$ tetraquark binding energy: 98(7)(3) MeV below the $B_sB^*$ threshold at the physical point
- $ud\bar b \bar b$ tetraquark mass: 10.415(10) GeV
- $\ell s\bar b \bar b$ tetraquark mass: 10.594(8) GeV
- Pion masses used: 164, 299, and 415 MeV on ensembles with $m_\pi L$ values of 2.4, 4.4, and 6.1 respectively
- Lattice spacing: $a^{-1}=2.194(10)$ GeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of doubly heavy tetraquarks, which all employed pion masses above 330 MeV and mostly used the static approximation for heavy quarks. Prior lattice studies by Bicudo et al. (2015) using $n_f=2$ ensembles and a Born-Oppenheimer approach found an estimated binding of $90^{+43}_{-36}$ MeV for the $ud\bar b \bar b$ channel after chiral extrapolation. The present calculation improves on these by using NRQCD for bottom quarks (avoiding the static approximation) and ensembles with near-physical pion masses, enabling a controlled chiral extrapolation. The results enable experimental searches for these states through weak decay modes with displaced vertices, and motivate further investigations into lighter tetraquark systems such as $qq^\prime \bar c \bar c$, $qq^\prime \bar c \bar b$, and $qq^\prime \bar s \bar b$.
