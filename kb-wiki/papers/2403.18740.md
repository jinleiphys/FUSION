# Effects of Dark Matter on $f$-mode oscillations of Neutron Stars

> The effect of dark matter (DM) on $f$-mode oscillations in DM admixed neutron stars (NSs) is investigated in a comprehensive analysis with particular attention to the role of the nuclear equation of state. Hadronic matter is modeled by the relativistic mean field model and the DM model is based on the neutron decay anomaly. The non-radial $f$-mode oscillations for such DM admixed NS are studied in a full general relativistic framework. We investigate the impact of DM, DM self-interaction, and DM fraction on the $f$-mode characteristics. We derive relations encoding the effect of DM on $f$-mode parameters. We then perform a systematic study by varying all the model parameters within their known uncertainty range and obtain a universal relation for the DM fraction based on the total mass of the star and DM self-interaction strength. We also perform a correlation study among model parameters, NS observables, in particular, $f$-mode parameters. Finally, we check the $f$-mode universal relations (URs) for the case of DM admixed NSs and demonstrate the existence of a degeneracy between purely hadronic NSs and DM admixed NSs.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how dark matter (DM) admixed in neutron stars (NSs) affects their non-radial \(f\)-mode oscillations, finding that the DM fraction is constrained to low values (90% quantile below 5.79% for a 2 \(M_\odot\) star) under current astrophysical constraints, and that a degeneracy exists between purely hadronic NSs and DM admixed NSs in \(f\)-mode universal relations.

## Method

The authors model hadronic matter using the relativistic mean field (RMF) model and DM using the neutron decay anomaly model, where neutrons can decay into dark sector particles. They solve the full general relativistic perturbation equations for non-radial quadrupolar (\(l=2\)) \(f\)-modes, including both interior fluid perturbations and exterior metric perturbations via the Zerilli equation, to find complex quasi-normal mode frequencies. A systematic parameter study is performed by varying the effective nucleon mass (\(m^*/m\)), DM self-interaction strength (\(G\)), and other nuclear parameters within their known uncertainty ranges, applying constraints from chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT), the \(2 M_\odot\) maximum mass observation, and the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement.

## Key numbers

- The 90% quantile for DM fraction in a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) DM admixed NS is 3.97%, and for a 2 \(M_\odot\) DM admixed NS it is 5.79%, after applying \(\chi\)EFT, GW170817, and \(2 M_\odot\) constraints.
- When a higher maximum mass constraint of 2.3 \(M_\odot\) is imposed, the 90% quantile for DM fraction reduces to 3.03% for 1.4 \(M_\odot\) and 4.29% for 2 \(M_\odot\) DM admixed NSs.
- The change in \(f\)-mode frequency \(\Delta f\) scales as \(\sqrt{M}\) and quadratically with DM fraction \(f_{DM}\) as \((a f_{DM} + b f_{DM}^2)\), with fit coefficients \(C_{f2}\) ranging from \((1.01 \pm 0.11) \times 10^{-2}\) to \((1.10 \pm 0.14) \times 10^{-2}\) kHz/\(\sqrt{M_\odot}\) and \(C_{f3}\) ranging from \((1.22 \pm 0.87) \times 10^{-4}\) to \((1.78 \pm 1.42) \times 10^{-4}\) kHz/\(\sqrt{M_\odot}\) depending on the effective mass \(m^*/m\).
- The change in damping time \(\Delta \tau\) scales as \(M^{-2}\) and decreases linearly with \(f_{DM}\), with fit coefficient \(C_{\tau}\) ranging from \((-8.80 \pm 0.39) \times 10^{-3}\) to \((-7.88 \pm 0.49) \times 10^{-3}\) \(M_\odot^2\) s depending on \(m^*/m\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(f\)-mode oscillations in NSs by Pradhan and Chatterjee (2022) that used full general relativity but did not include DM, and by Das et al. (2021) that studied DM admixed NSs using a Higgs-interaction model within the Cowling approximation. The authors note that the Cowling approximation can overestimate \(f\)-mode frequencies by up to 30% as shown by Pradhan and Chatterjee (2022), and that Flores et al. (2024) performed a similar full-GR study but with a fixed nuclear equation of state and a different DM model. The results enable future gravitational wave asteroseismology to potentially distinguish between purely hadronic and DM admixed NSs, though the degeneracy in universal relations complicates this distinction.
