# Meson masses in electromagnetic fields with Wilson fermions

> We determine the light meson spectrum in QCD in the presence of background magnetic fields using quenched Wilson fermions. Our continuum extrapolated results indicate a monotonous reduction of the connected neutral pion mass as the magnetic field grows. The vector meson mass is found to remain nonzero, a finding relevant for the conjectured $ρ$-meson condensation at strong magnetic fields. The continuum extrapolation was facilitated by adding a novel magnetic field-dependent improvement term to the additive quark mass renormalization. Without this term, sizable lattice artifacts that would deceptively indicate an unphysical rise of the connected neutral pion mass for strong magnetic fields are present. We also investigate the impact of these lattice artifacts on further observables like magnetic polarizabilities and discuss the magnetic field-induced mixing between $ρ$-mesons and pions. We also derive Ward-Takashi identities for QCD+QED both in the continuum formulation and for (order $a$-improved) Wilson fermions.

## Key claim

The paper resolves a discrepancy in the literature regarding the magnetic field dependence of the neutral pion mass: while previous quenched Wilson fermion simulations had shown a turning point and subsequent increase of the connected neutral pion mass at strong magnetic fields, the authors demonstrate that this rise is a lattice artifact caused by a magnetic field-dependent additive quark mass renormalization, and that after correcting for this artifact the neutral pion mass monotonously decreases with increasing magnetic field.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using unimproved Wilson fermions at three lattice spacings (β = 5.845, 6.0, 6.26) with fixed physical volume, applying constant background magnetic fields via the symmetric gauge. They determined the magnetic field-dependent critical hopping parameter κc,f(B) by requiring that the neutral axial Ward-Takahashi identity current quark mass vanishes for each flavor, then tuned the bare quark mass along a line of constant physics (LCP) that keeps the renormalized quark mass constant as B varies. Meson masses were extracted from correlation functions projected to zero momentum in the z-direction and summed over the x,y-plane.

## Key numbers

- The neutral pion mass (connected contribution) monotonously decreases with magnetic field over the range 0 < eB < 4 GeV², in contrast to the unphysical rise seen without the B-dependent improvement term.
- The ρ-meson mass remains nonzero for all magnetic fields studied, up to eB = 4 GeV², contradicting the conjecture of ρ-meson condensation at strong fields.
- The coefficient d_m of the leading-order B-dependent additive mass renormalization is d_m = -1/2 in the free case (Appendix 1), and the numerical results show that the shift Δm_{c;f}(a,B) can be as large as the quark mass corresponding to a 415 MeV pion at B=0.
- The proportionality constant C_f relating the current quark mass to the bare quark mass approaches unity toward the continuum limit and also toward the free fermion case at large B, with values shown for eB up to about 2 GeV² where the increase levels off.

## Context

This work extends the quenched overlap fermion results of Luschevskaya et al. (2014) and resolves the discrepancy with the quenched unimproved Wilson fermion results of Hidaka and Yamamoto (2012), which had reported a turning point in m_{π⁰}(B). The finding that the ρ-meson mass remains nonzero is relevant for the conjectured ρ-meson condensation scenario of Schramm et al. (1991) and the superconducting vacuum proposal of Chernodub (2011). The improvement scheme developed here enables future lattice studies of magnetic polarizabilities and magnetic moments, which suffer from enhanced lattice artifacts that can be eliminated using the B-dependent line of constant physics.
