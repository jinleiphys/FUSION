# Scattering and Femtoscopic Correlation Functions of the $危_c^{++}蟺^{+}$, $危_c^{0}蟺^{-}$ and $危_b^{+}蟺^{+}$ Systems

> We present predictions for scattering observables and femtoscopic correlation functions (CFs) of the $I=2$ $危_c^{++}蟺^{+}$, $危_c^{0}蟺^{-}$ systems and its heavy-flavor counterpart $危_b^{+}蟺^{+}$. In both heavy-quark sectors, the strong interaction is formulated within two distinct theoretical frameworks, each constrained to reproduce the lowest-lying odd-parity isoscalar spin-$1/2$ resonances, $螞_c(2595)$ and $螞_b(5912)$, respectively. While the $危_c^{0}蟺^{-}$ pair is governed solely by the strong interaction, electrostatic contributions are included in the other two channels involving charged particles through relativistic Coulomb wave functions. We show that the differences observed in the scattering observables between the two strong-interaction models arise mainly from the specific ultraviolet regularization schemes employed. The inclusion of Coulomb effects induces only a very small increase in both the scattering length and the effective range. The resulting CFs in the charm and bottom sectors display analogous global features, in agreement with expectations from heavy-quark flavor symmetry. Both, the $危_c^{++}蟺^+$ and $危_b^{+}蟺^{+}$ CFs, when computed including only the strong interaction, exhibits substantial discriminating power among the different models. However, once Coulomb effects are incorporated, the CFs become largely affected by the repulsive electrostatic interaction, which diminishes their sensitivity to the details of the underlying strong dynamics, thereby reducing the capability to differentiate between theoretical descriptions. Thus, the $危_c^{0}蟺^{-}$ CF-being free from Coulomb effects-provides the most suitable observable for constraining the strong dynamics of the isotensor $危_c蟺$ system.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the femtoscopic correlation function of the electrically neutral $\Sigma_c^{0}\pi^{-}$ system, being free from Coulomb effects, provides the most suitable observable for constraining the strong dynamics of the isotensor $\Sigma_c\pi$ system, while the Coulomb-repelled $\Sigma_c^{++}\pi^{+}$ and $\Sigma_b^{+}\pi^{+}$ correlation functions lose sensitivity to the details of the underlying strong interaction models once electrostatic effects are included.

## Method

The authors solve the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $I=2$ $\Sigma_c\pi$ and $\Sigma_b\pi$ systems using two distinct strong-interaction frameworks: an SU(4)-symmetric Weinberg-Tomozawa potential with coupled-channel dynamics including $ND$ channels, and a chiral Weinberg-Tomozawa potential supplemented by a constituent quark model exchange term. Both models are constrained to reproduce the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ and $\Lambda_b(5912)$ resonances in the $I=0$ sector, and the resulting ultraviolet cutoffs are then applied to the isotensor channel. Coulomb effects are incorporated through relativistic Coulomb wave functions using the Gell-Mann-Goldberger decomposition.

## Key numbers

- UV cutoff for the SU(4)-WT model: $\Lambda = 653$ MeV, fixed by the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance
- For the $\Sigma_c\pi$ [WT and CQM] model, two limiting parameter pairs reproduce the $\Lambda_c(2595)$: ($d_c=0.69$, $\Lambda=650$ MeV) and ($d_c=1.15$, $\Lambda=400$ MeV)
- Strong scattering length for the SU(4)-WT model with $\Lambda=653$ MeV: $a_0 = 0.177$ fm; with Coulomb included: $a_0^{SC} = 0.181$ fm
- Strong scattering length for the $\Sigma_c\pi$ [WT and CQM] model with $\Lambda=650$ MeV: $a_0 = 0.177$ fm; with Coulomb included: $a_0^{SC} = 0.181$ fm
- Effective range for the SU(4)-WT model with $\Lambda=653$ MeV: $r_0 = 8.26$ fm (fit); with Coulomb: $r_0^{SC} = 8.42$ fm (fit)
- The difference between the standard and Coulomb-dressed loop functions at threshold is approximately $-1.9$ MeV, corresponding to a 2-3% increase in the scattering length
- The WT coefficient in the $I=2$ sector is $C_{\Sigma_c\pi; I=2}^{\textrm{WT}} = 2$ (repulsive), compared to $C_{\Sigma_c\pi; I=0}^{\textrm{WT}} = -4$ (attractive) in the isoscalar sector

## Context

This work extends theoretical femtoscopic studies of charmed hadron interactions, following experimental measurements by the ALICE Collaboration (2024, 2022) of $D\pi$, $DK$, and $Dp$ correlation functions in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV. The authors build on prior theoretical frameworks for $\Sigma_c\pi$ interactions developed by Hofmann (2005), Mizutani (2006), and Jimenez-Tejero (2009) using SU(4)-coupled channels, and by Nieves (2024) using the constituent quark model approach. The Coulomb treatment follows the formalism of Albaladejo (2025) originally applied to $pp$ correlation functions. The results enable future experimental discrimination between strong-interaction models through measurement of the $\Sigma_c^{0}\pi^{-}$ correlation function, which is unaffected by Coulomb distortions.
