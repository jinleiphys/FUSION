# The beta-beta two neutrino decay in 48Ca

> A schematic study of the $ββ2ν$-decay of $^{48}Ca$ is made in a shell-model approach. The emphasis is especially put on the role of the spin-orbit potential in relation with the contribution of other terms in the strong interaction. This is discussed with a particular attention to the behavior of these ones under the SU(4) symmetry. Different methods in calculating the transition amplitude are also looked at with the aim to determine their reliability and, eventually, why they don't work. Further aspects relative to the failure of the Operator Expansion Method to reproduce the results of more elaborate calculations are examined.

# The beta-beta two-neutrino decay in 48Ca

## Key claim
The Operator Expansion Method (OEM) fails to reproduce the correct sign of the double-beta decay transition amplitude for 48Ca when more than two valence nucleons are present, because it omits contributions from Pauli antisymmetrization and many-body operators that arise from the non-commutativity of the SU(4)-symmetric and SU(4)-breaking parts of the Hamiltonian.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations in the full fp shell using the OXBASH code, employing the Bertsch-Hamamoto force and a modified version with adjusted parameters. They computed the Gamow-Teller matrix element for the ββ2ν decay of 48Ca using three methods: the standard sum-over-intermediate-states approach (M_GT^St), the Operator Expansion Method (M_GT^OEM), and a first-order SU(4) symmetry-breaking approximation (M_GT^SU(4)). The spin-orbit potential strength was varied by a factor γ from 0 to 1 to isolate its contribution relative to the central force.

## Key numbers
- Experimental half-life of 48Ca: T_{1/2}^{2ν} = (4.3 {+2.4}{-1.1} [Stat] ± 1.4 [Syst]) × 10^{19} y (Balysh et al.)
- Standard method matrix element for B-H potential with full spin-orbit (γ=1): M_GT^St = 0.044 MeV^{-1}
- Standard method matrix element for modified B-H potential with full spin-orbit (γ=1): M_GT^St = 0.15 MeV^{-1}
- SU(4) approximation matrix element for B-H potential: varies from -0.072 MeV^{-1} (γ=0) to -0.053 MeV^{-1} (γ=1)
- SU(4) approximation matrix element for modified B-H potential: varies from approximately -0.05 MeV^{-1} (γ=0) to approximately -0.04 MeV^{-1} (γ=1)
- Single-particle energies used: ε_{f7/2}=0, ε_{p3/2}=2.1 MeV, ε_{p1/2}=3.9 MeV, ε_{f5/2}=6.5 MeV
- Coulomb displacement parameter Δ_c fitted from relative ground-state positions of 48Ca and 48Ti

## Context
This work extends the discussion of SU(4) symmetry breaking in double-beta decay previously studied by Bernabeu et al. and Krmpotic et al., and directly addresses the failure of the Operator Expansion Method demonstrated by Engel et al. (2000). The authors show that the OEM, proposed by Simkovic et al. and Ching et al., gives the wrong sign for the transition amplitude in the first-order SU(4) breaking limit when more than two valence nucleons are present, because it neglects contributions from three-body and higher operators that arise from the non-commutativity of H_0 and H_1. The work enables a clearer understanding of which approximations are reliable for calculating double-beta decay matrix elements, and highlights the importance of the spin-orbit potential as an SU(4)-breaking term that significantly affects the transition amplitude.
