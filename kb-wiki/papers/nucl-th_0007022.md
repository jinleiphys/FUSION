# Classifying Reported and "Missing" Resonances According to Their P and C Properties

> The Hilbert space H^3q of the three quarks with one excited quark is decomposed into Lorentz group representations. It is shown that the quantum numbers of the reported and ``missing'' resonances fall apart and populate distinct representations that differ by their parity or/and charge conjugation properties. In this way, reported and ``missing'' resonances become distinguishable. For example, resonances from the full listing reported by the Particle Data Group are accommodated by Rarita-Schwinger (RS) type representations (k/2,k/2)*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] with k=1,3, and 5, the highest spin states being J=3/2^-, 7/2^+, and 11/2^+, respectively. In contrast to this, most of the ``missing'' resonances fall into the opposite parity RS fields of highest-spins 5/2^-, 5/2^+, and 9/2^+, respectively. Rarita-Schwinger fields with physical resonances as lower-spin components can be treated as a whole without imposing auxiliary conditions on them. Such fields do not suffer the Velo-Zwanziger problem but propagate causally in the presence of electromagnetic fields. The pathologies associated with RS fields arise basically because of the attempt to use them to describe isolated spin-J=k+1/ 2 states, rather than multispin-parity clusters. The positions of the observed RS clusters and their spacing are well explained trough the interplay between the rotational-like (k/2)(k/2 +1)-rule and a Balmer-like -(k+1)^{-2}-behavior.

## Key claim

Reported and "missing" baryon resonances are not randomly distributed but populate distinct Lorentz group representations that differ by their parity or charge conjugation properties, making them distinguishable from each other. The claim is conditional on the decomposition of the three-quark Hilbert space with one excited quark into Lorentz group representations, where reported resonances occupy Rarita-Schwinger type representations with highest spins 3/2-, 7/2+, and 11/2+, while most "missing" resonances fall into opposite parity Rarita-Schwinger fields with highest spins 5/2-, 5/2+, and 9/2+.

## Method

The Hilbert space of three quarks with one excited quark was decomposed into Lorentz group representations using a j-j coupling shell model scheme rather than the traditional LS coupling of SU(6)SF x O(3)L quark models. The quantum numbers of baryon resonances from the Particle Data Group listing were then mapped onto Rarita-Schwinger multispinor fields of the type {k/2, k/2} x [(1/2,0) + (0,1/2)] with k=1,3,5, and the parity and charge conjugation properties of these representations were compared between reported and "missing" states. The analysis considered the single-particle shells 1s, 1p, and 2s as the simplest configuration space.

## Key numbers

- The three Rarita-Schwinger clusters accommodating reported resonances have highest spin states J=3/2-, 7/2+, and 11/2+ for k=1, 3, and 5 respectively
- Most "missing" resonances fall into opposite parity Rarita-Schwinger fields with highest spins 5/2-, 5/2+, and 9/2+ respectively
- The first nucleon cluster (psi_mu) contains the first P11, S11, and D13 resonances; the second cluster (psi_mu eta rho) contains the second P11, S11, D13 states plus first P13, D15, F15, F17 states; the third cluster (psi_mu eta rho tau nu) contains the third S11, P11, D13 resonances, second P13, D15, F15, F17 states, plus first G17, G19, H19, H1,11 states
- The F17 resonance is the only nonstrange state with mass below 2000 MeV that is "missing" in the Rarita-Schwinger classification scheme
- Four additional states with masses above 2000 MeV are "missing": H1,11, P31, P33, and D33
- The approximately equidistant cluster spacing between mass centers of Rarita-Schwinger clusters is about 200 to 300 MeV
- The maximal mass splitting within the 2(1,+), 2(3,+), 4(1,-), and 4(3,-) multiplets is 50-70 MeV
- All seven Delta baryon resonances (S31, P31, P33, D33, D35, F35, F37) from the 4(3,-) cluster are squeezed within the narrow mass region from 1900 MeV to 1950 MeV
- The I=1/2 resonances paralleling them are located around 1700(+20/-50) MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Kirchbach, 1997-1998) which found spin and parity clustering of Breit-Wigner masses on the M/J plane and showed that resonance quantum numbers fit into Rarita-Schwinger representations. It contradicts the traditional SU(6)SF x O(3)L classification scheme (Particle Data Group; Bhaduri) where states from different Rarita-Schwinger clusters separated by about 200 MeV are assigned to the same multiplet, while states from the same Rarita-Schwinger cluster separated by only few MeV are distributed over three different SU(6)SF x O(3)L representations. The work enables treating Rarita-Schwinger fields with physical resonances as lower-spin components without imposing auxiliary conditions, avoiding the Velo-Zwanziger problem of acausal propagation in electromagnetic fields, and provides a framework for understanding why some resonances may be suppressed.
