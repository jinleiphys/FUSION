# Structure and Width of the d*(2380) Dibaryon

> In this contribution, dedicated to the memory of Walter Greiner, we discuss the structure and width of the recently established d*(2380) dibaryon, confronting the consequences of our Pion Assisted Dibaryons hadronic model with those of quark motivated calculations. In particular, its relatively small width of about 70 MeV favors hadronic structure for the d*(2380) dibaryon rather than a six-quark structure.

## Key claim

The d*(2380) dibaryon's relatively small width of about 70 MeV favors a hadronic structure (specifically a pion-assisted dibaryon model with a diffuse \(\pi N\Delta\) component) over a six-quark structure, but the observed width is actually much larger than what a compact, deeply bound \(\Delta\Delta\) state would produce upon decay, contradicting quark-based calculations that predict a tightly bound configuration.

## Method

The author applies a three-body \(\pi N\Delta\) Faddeev hadronic model using separable pairwise interactions, where the coupled-channel \(B\Delta - \pi D\) eigenvalue problem reduces to a single integral equation for the \(B\Delta\) \(T\) matrix. The \(\Delta\) resonance is treated as a stable isobar in the three-body calculation, with its decay width considered at the penultimate stage. The bound-\(\Delta\) decay width is then evaluated by averaging the momentum-dependent \(\Gamma_{\Delta\to N\pi}\) over the \(\Delta\Delta\) bound-state momentum-space distribution, using the uncertainty relation \(P_{\Delta\Delta}R_{\Delta\Delta} \geq 3/2\) to relate the r.m.s. momentum to the r.m.s. radius.

## Key numbers

- d*(2380) resonance width: \(\Gamma_{d^*} \approx 70\) MeV (abstract) or \(\Gamma_{d^*(2380)} = 80 \pm 10\) MeV (from WASA-at-COSY and SAID, Ref. [wasa14])
- Free-space \(\Delta\) width: \(\Gamma_{\Delta} \approx 115\) MeV (twice this is 230 MeV, the naive expectation for a \(\Delta\Delta\) configuration)
- \(\Delta\Delta\) binding energy: \(B_{\Delta\Delta} = 2 \times 1232 - 2380 = 84\) MeV
- For a compact \(\Delta\Delta\) state with r.m.s. radius \(R_{\Delta\Delta} = 0.76\) fm (from quark-based RGM calculations), the calculated pionic width upper limit is about 47 MeV, incompatible with the experimental value
- For a diffuse \(\Delta\Delta\) state with \(R_{\Delta\Delta} = 1.5\) fm, the calculated width \(\overline{\Gamma}_{\Delta\Delta\to NN\pi\pi} = 112.3\) MeV; for \(R_{\Delta\Delta} = 2.0\) fm, it is 122.0 MeV
- Hadronic model Faddeev calculation with \(r_{\Delta} \approx 1.3\) fm gives \({\cal D}_{03}\) pole position: \(2383 - i41\) MeV (mass 2383 MeV, width 82 MeV)
- Quark-based RGM calculation (Ref. [dong16]) gives \({\cal D}_{03}\) pole: \(2380 - i36\) MeV (width 72 MeV)
- Quark-based RGM calculation (Ref. [wang14]) gives \({\cal D}_{03}\) pole: \(2393 - i75\) MeV (width 150 MeV)
- ELPH experiment \(\gamma d \to d\pi^0\pi^0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2.39\) GeV: resonance mass 2370 MeV, width 68 MeV (low statistical significance)

## Context

This work extends the author's prior hadronic model of pion-assisted dibaryons (Gal and Garcilazo, 2013-2014) and confronts it with quark-based calculations. The d*(2380) dibaryon was predicted by Dyson and Xuong (1964) using SU(6) symmetry and was experimentally established by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (2011-2014). The paper argues that recent quark-based RGM calculations (Wang et al., 2014; Dong et al., 2016) produce a compact \(\Delta\Delta\) state with r.m.s. radius 0.76 fm that cannot reproduce the observed width, whereas the hadronic model's diffuse \(\pi{\cal D}_{12}\) component (size 1.5-2 fm) resolves this discrepancy. The analysis enables future precise measurements of the \(d^*\to NN\pi\) single-pion decay branch to constrain the \(\pi{\cal D}_{12}-\Delta\Delta\) mixing parameter.
