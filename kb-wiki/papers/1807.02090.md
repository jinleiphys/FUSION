# Ordered arrays of Baryonic tubes in the Skyrme model in (3+1) dimensions at finite density

> A consistent ansatz for the Skyrme model in (3+1)-dimensions which is able to reduce the complete set of Skyrme field equations to just one equation for the profile in situations in which the Baryon charge can be arbitrary large is introduced: moreover, the field equation for the profile can be solved explicitly. Such configurations describe ordered arrays of Baryonic tubes living in flat space-times at finite density. The plots of the energy density (as well as of the Baryon density) clearly show that the regions of maximal energy density have the shape of a tube: the energy density and the Baryon density depend periodically on two spatial directions while they are constant in the third spatial direction. Thus, these topologically non-trivial crystal-like solutions can be intepreted as configurations in which most of the energy density and the baryon density are concentrated within tube-shaped regions. The positions of the energy-density peaks can be computed explicitly and they manifest a clear crystalline order. A non-trivial stability test is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an ansatz that reduces the full set of Skyrme field equations in (3+1) dimensions to a single integrable equation for the profile, enabling explicit analytic construction of ordered arrays of baryonic tubes at finite density, but only under the condition that the function \(G\) satisfies \(\nabla_\mu G \nabla^\mu G = 0\), which is achieved by a specific time-periodic choice that keeps the energy density static.

## Method

The author adopts a parametrization of the \(SU(2)\) Skyrme field in terms of three scalar functions \(\alpha\), \(F\), and \(G\), and proposes the ansatz \(\alpha = \alpha(r)\), \(F = q\theta\) with \(q\) an odd integer, and \(G = p(t/L - \phi)\) with \(p\) a nonzero integer, in a flat metric \(ds^2 = -dt^2 + A(dr^2 + d\theta^2) + L^2 d\phi^2\) where coordinates range periodically. This ansatz makes the three coupled Skyrme field equations reduce to a single ordinary differential equation for \(\alpha(r)\), which is solved explicitly via a quadrature involving elliptic functions, with the boundary condition \(\alpha(2\pi) - \alpha(0) = n\pi\) yielding baryon charge \(B = np\).

## Key numbers

- Baryon charge is given by \(B = np\), where \(n\) is the integer from the profile boundary condition \(\alpha(2\pi) - \alpha(0) = n\pi\) and \(p\) is the integer from the ansatz \(G = p(t/L - \phi)\).
- The spatial coordinates have ranges \(0 \leq r \leq 2\pi\), \(0 \leq \theta \leq \pi\), and \(0 \leq \phi \leq 2\pi\), with the volume of the box being \(4\pi^3 L A\).
- The integer \(q\) in \(F = q\theta\) must be an odd integer (\(q = 2v+1\) with \(v \in \mathbb{N}\)) to obtain non-vanishing topological charge; even \(q\) leads to zero baryon charge.
- The integration constant \(E_0\) in the profile equation is determined by \(\int_0^{n\pi} d\alpha / \eta(\alpha, E_0) = 2\pi\), and for large \(n\) both \(\eta\) and \(E_0\) are of order \(n\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized hedgehog ansatz methods of Canfora (2013, 2014) and collaborators to construct analytic multi-Skyrmions with crystalline order, going beyond the rational map approach of Houghton, Manton, and Sutcliffe (1998) which only yields approximate solutions for multi-Skyrmions. The configurations describe the "nuclear spaghetti" phase of the nuclear pasta phase, previously only studied numerically (see pasta phase references), and enable analytic study of transitions where configurations with taller but fewer tubes are energetically favored over those with shorter but more numerous tubes.
