# Eta-nucleon coupling constant in QCD with SU(3) symmetry breaking

> We study the $η$NN coupling constant using the method of QCD sum rules starting from the vacuum-to-eta correlation function of the interpolating fields of two nucleons. The matrix element of this correlation has been taken with respect to nucleon spinors to avoid unwanted pole contribution. The SU(3)-flavor symmetry breaking effects have been accounted for via the $η$-mass, s-quark mass and eta decay constant to leading order. Out of the four sum rules obtained by taking the ratios of the two sum rules in conjunction with the two sum rules in nucleon mass, three are found to give mutually consistent results. We find the SU(3) breaking effects significant, as large as 50% of the SU(3) symmetric part.

## Key claim

The eta-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\eta NN}\) is determined via QCD sum rules with leading-order SU(3)-flavor symmetry breaking included; the breaking effects are significant, as large as 50% of the SU(3) symmetric part, and the most reliable result comes from the odd-odd sum rule, yielding \(g_{\eta NN} = 4.20 \pm 1.05\).

## Method

The authors compute the vacuum-to-eta correlation function of two nucleon interpolating fields, then take its matrix element with respect to nucleon spinors to avoid unwanted pole contributions. They separate the projected correlation function into even and odd parts, derive two independent sum rules for \(g_{\eta NN}\), and combine them with two nucleon mass sum rules to obtain four independent sum-rule equations. SU(3)-flavor symmetry breaking is incorporated to leading order via the eta mass, strange-quark mass, and eta decay constant, using Gell-Mann–Okubo-like relations for the octet pseudoscalar meson parameters.

## Key numbers

*   \(g_{\eta NN} = 4.20 \pm 1.05\) from the odd-odd sum rule, the main result.
*   In the SU(3) symmetric limit, the odd-odd sum rule gives \(g_{\eta NN} = 5.31\).
*   The eta decay constant used is \(f_\eta = 0.1695\ \text{GeV}\); the eta mass is \(m_\eta = 0.547\ \text{GeV}\); the nucleon mass is \(m_N = 0.939\ \text{GeV}\).
*   The continuum threshold for the coupling sum rules is \(s_\eta = 2.57\ \text{GeV}^2\); for the mass sum rules it is \(s_0 = 2.00\ \text{GeV}^2\).
*   Changing the quark condensate \(<\bar q q>\) by \(\pm 0.025\ \text{GeV}^3\) around \(-(0.23\ \text{GeV})^3\) changes \(g_{\eta NN}\) by \(9.6\%\) in the odd-odd sum rule.
*   The SU(3) breaking effect in the odd-odd sum rule is a decrease of about \(21\%\) relative to the symmetric result.

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum-rule calculations of \(g_{\eta NN}\) in the SU(3) symmetry limit by Kim et al. (2000) and Doi et al. (2000), and improves upon the approach of Kondo and Morimatsu (2003) by including leading-order SU(3) breaking. The result is compared with values derived from the Goldberger–Treiman relation by Feldmann (2000) and Shore (2000), and with potential-model fits by Stoks et al. (1999) and Dumbrajs et al. (1983). The determination enables more reliable modeling of eta production off nucleons and of isospin violation in nuclear potentials, and may help constrain the strange content of the nucleon via the Sullivan process.
