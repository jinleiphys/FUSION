# Spin-dependent dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism

> We study electron and positron pair production from the vacuum by a strong slow electric field superimposed by a weak fast electric field pointing in an arbitrary direction as a perturbation (the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism). An analytical formula for the production number is derived on the basis of the perturbation theory in the Furry picture. The formula is found to be in good agreement with non-perturbative results obtained by numerically solving the Dirac equation if the perturbation is sufficiently weak and/or is not very slow. We also find analytically/numerically that the Schwinger mechanism becomes spin-dependent if the perturbation has a transverse component with respect to the strong electric field. The number difference between spin up and down particles is strongly suppressed by an exponential of the critical field strength if the frequency of the perturbation is small, while it is only weakly suppressed by powers of the critical field strength if the frequency is large enough. We also find that the spin-imbalance exhibits non-trivial oscillating behaviors in terms of the frequency of the perturbation, the azimuthal angle, and the momentum of produced particles.

## Key claim

The Schwinger mechanism becomes spin-dependent when a weak fast electric field with a transverse component (relative to the strong slow field) is superimposed, with the spin-imbalance suppressed exponentially by the critical field strength for small perturbation frequencies but only by powers of the critical field strength for sufficiently large frequencies.

## Method

The authors employed a perturbation theory in the Furry picture, treating the strong constant electric field non-perturbatively and the weak monochromatic perturbation to lowest order, to derive an analytical formula for the momentum distribution of produced electrons and positrons. They also performed non-perturbative numerical calculations by directly solving the Dirac equation using a Bogoliubov transformation technique to compute the spin-imbalance without approximations, and compared these results with the analytical formula.

## Key numbers

- The critical field strength is given by \(e E_{\rm cr} \equiv m^2 \sim \sqrt{10^{28}\;{\rm W/cm^2}}\).
- The current strongest laser (HERCULES) reaches \(eE \sim \sqrt{10^{22}\;{\rm W/cm^2}}\), and planned facilities (ELI, HiPER) are expected to reach \(eE \sim \sqrt{10^{24}\;{\rm W/cm^2}}\).
- The Keldysh parameter for the weak field is \(\gamma_{\rm K} \equiv m\Omega/e|{\bm {\mathcal E}}|\), with perturbation theory valid for \(\gamma_{\rm K} \gtrsim 1\).
- For small \(\Omega \lesssim \sqrt{e\bar{E}}, \sqrt{m^2+p_{\perp}^2}\), the spin-imbalance \(\Delta n^{(\mp)}_{{\bm p}}\) scales as \(\Omega^2/(4e\bar{E})\) times the leading exponential factor.
- For large \(\Omega \gtrsim \sqrt{e\bar{E}}, \sqrt{m^2+p_{\perp}^2}\), the spin-imbalance is proportional to \(\sqrt{e\bar{E}/(m^2+p_{\perp}^2)} \times (p_{\perp}/\Omega)({\mathcal E}_{\perp}/\bar{E})\) times an oscillatory phase factor.

## Context

This work extends the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism, originally proposed by Schutzhold et al. (2008) and Dunne et al. (2009), by demonstrating that a transverse weak field component introduces spin-dependence absent in the original Schwinger formula (Schwinger, 1951). The spin-dependent effect is analogous to the Franz-Keldysh effect in semiconductor physics (Franz, 1958; Keldysh, 1958) and builds on perturbative approaches in the Furry picture developed by Greiner et al. (2017, 2019) and Taya (2019). The results enable experimental searches for spin-polarized pair production in strong-field laser facilities, where the spin-imbalance could serve as a signature of the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism.
