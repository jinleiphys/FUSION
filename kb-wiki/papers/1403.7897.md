# Low-lying $Ω$ states with negative parity in an extended quark model with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio interaction

> Here we investigate mixing of the low-lying three- and five-quark $Ω$ states with spin-parity quantum numbers $\frac{1}{2}^{-}$ and $\frac{3}{2}^{-}$, employing the quark-antiquark creation triggered by Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) interaction. Wave functions of the three- and five-quark configurations are constructed using the extended constituent quark model, within which the hyperfine interaction between quarks is also taken to be the NJL interaction induced one. Numerical results show that the NJL-induced pair creation results in vanishing mixing between three- and five-quark $Ω$ configurations with spin-parity $1/2^{-}$, but mixing between three- and five-quark $3/2^{-}$ $Ω$ states should be very strong. And the mixing decreases energy of the lowest $3/2^{-}$ $Ω$ state to be $1785\pm25$~MeV, which is lower than energy of the lowest $1/2^{-}$ state in this model. This is consistent with our previous predictions within the instanton-induced quark-antiquark creation model.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) induced quark-antiquark pair creation results in exactly vanishing mixing between three- and five-quark $\Omega$ configurations with spin-parity $1/2^{-}$, but strong mixing for $3/2^{-}$ states; the mixing lowers the lowest $3/2^{-}$ $\Omega$ state to $1785\pm25$ MeV, which is below the lowest $1/2^{-}$ state in this model, consistent with previous instanton-induced predictions.

## Method

The authors constructed an extended constituent quark model Hamiltonian with diagonal terms for three-quark ($s^3$) and five-quark ($s^4\bar{s}$ and $s^3q\bar{q}$) $\Omega$ configurations, using the NJL interaction to derive both the hyperfine interaction between quarks and the quark-antiquark pair creation transition operator. They computed the matrix elements for spin-parity $1/2^{-}$ and $3/2^{-}$ states, diagonalized the Hamiltonian, and extracted the resulting energies and probability amplitudes for the mixed states.

## Key numbers

- The diagonal matrix element for the three-quark $\Omega$ state with both $1/2^{-}$ and $3/2^{-}$ is $1942$ MeV (using NJL hyperfine interaction).
- The lowest $3/2^{-}$ $\Omega$ state energy after mixing is $1785\pm25$ MeV, obtained by varying $g_{qs}$ from $25$ to $75$ MeV.
- The coupling strengths are $g_{qq}=69$ MeV, $g_{qs}=51$ MeV, $g_{ss}=38$ MeV, fitted to the $\Sigma$-$\Lambda$ mass splitting.
- The constituent quark masses used are $m=310$ MeV for light quarks and $m_s=460$ MeV for strange quarks.
- The confinement potential parameter $V_0^{(3)}=-188$ MeV (fitted to the ground state $\Omega(1672)$) and $V_0^{(5)}=-294$ MeV (fitted to the lowest five-quark $\Omega$ configuration at $\sim1810$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (An and Zou, 2013) which used instanton-induced quark-antiquark creation with three different hyperfine interactions (one-gluon exchange, Goldstone boson exchange, and instanton-induced). Here they replace both the hyperfine interaction and the pair creation mechanism with NJL-induced versions for model consistency, finding similar qualitative behavior: negligible mixing for $1/2^{-}$ states and strong mixing for $3/2^{-}$ states. The results enable predictions for future BESIII experiments searching for $\Omega$ resonances through $\psi(2S)\to\bar\Omega\Omega^*$ reactions.
