# The Simple Spectrum of $c\bar c c\bar c$ States in the Dynamical Diquark Model

> We develop the spectroscopy of $c\bar c c\bar c$ and other all-heavy tetraquark states in the dynamical diquark model. In the most minimal form of the model (e.g., each diquark appears only in the color-triplet combination; the non-orbital spin couplings connect only quarks within each diquark), the spectroscopy is extremely simple. Namely, the $S$-wave multiplets contain precisely 3 degenerate states ($0^{++}$, $1^{+-}$, $2^{++}$) and the 7 $P$-wave states satisfy an equal-spacing rule when the tensor coupling is negligible. When comparing numerically to the recent LHCb results, we find the best interpretation is assigning $X(6900)$ to the $2S$ multiplet, while a lower state suggested at about $6740$ MeV fits well with the members of the $1P$ multiplet. We also predict the location of other multiplets ($1S$, $1D$, etc.) and discuss the significance of the $cc$ open-flavor threshold.

## Key claim

The dynamical diquark model predicts that the $S$-wave $c\bar c c\bar c$ multiplet contains exactly three degenerate states ($0^{++}$, $1^{+-}$, $2^{++}$) when diquarks appear only in the color-triplet combination and spin couplings connect only quarks within each diquark; the authors find that assigning $X(6900)$ to the $2S$ multiplet and a lower state near 6740 MeV to the $1P$ multiplet provides the best interpretation of recent LHCb data, but this assignment depends on the specific LHCb fit scenario used.

## Method

The authors apply the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to the dynamical diquark model, treating the $cc$ diquark and $\bar c \bar c$ antidiquark as heavy color-triplet sources connected by a gluon field. They solve the Schrodinger equation using lattice-computed static potentials for the $\Sigma_g^+$ configuration, then add spin-dependent corrections: a spin-spin term $\kappa_{cc}$ coupling quarks within each diquark (taken from a fit to $c\bar c s\bar s$ candidates), and spin-orbit and tensor terms $V_{LS}$ and $V_T$ (taken from fits to $P$-wave $c\bar c q\bar q^\prime$ candidates). The mass eigenvalues for $S$-wave and $P$-wave states are computed parametrically and compared to the two LHCb fit scenarios for the observed resonances.

## Key numbers

- $\kappa_{cc} = 114.2$ MeV: the spin-spin coupling within the $cc$ diquark, taken from a fit to $c\bar c s\bar s$ candidates in Giron (2020)
- $V_{LS} = 42.9$ MeV and $V_T = 5.5$ MeV (Case 3), or $V_{LS} = 49.0$ MeV and $V_T = 3.8$ MeV (Case 5): spin-orbit and tensor couplings from $P$-wave $c\bar c q\bar q^\prime$ fits in Giron (2020)
- $M_0(1P) = 6931.3$ MeV or $6954.0$ MeV: the multiplet-average mass for the $\Sigma_g^+(1P)$ state, depending on which $V_{LS}, V_T$ values are used
- $\Delta m_{1P-1S} = +343.3$ MeV and $\Delta m_{1P-2S} = -156.9$ MeV: the $M_0$ mass differences between $1P$ and lower multiplets
- $X(6900)$ mass: $6905 \pm 11$ MeV (Model I) or $6886 \pm 11$ MeV (Model II) from LHCb
- Lower state masses: $6490 \pm 15$ MeV (Model I, labeled $X(6500)$) or $6741 \pm 6$ MeV (Model II, labeled $X(6740)$) from LHCb
- Mass splittings between peaks: $\Delta m_{\rm I} = 415 \pm 19$ MeV (Model I) or $\Delta m_{\rm II} = 145 \pm 15$ MeV (Model II)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical diquark model previously applied to $c\bar c q\bar q^\prime$ states (Giron 2019) and $c\bar c s\bar s$ states (Giron 2020) to the all-heavy $c\bar c c\bar c$ system, following the LHCb observation of resonances in the $J/\psi$-pair spectrum (Aaij 2020). The model's predictions contrast with other approaches such as Berezhnoy (2011) which treats all quark spin-spin interactions on equal footing, and Liu (2020) which includes couplings between all quarks and color-sextet combinations. The observation of a $1^{++}$ state in the lowest $S$-wave multiplet would provide direct evidence of dynamics beyond the most restrictive diquark models, specifically indicating the presence of color-sextet diquark components.
