# Energy minimization of paired composite fermion wave functions in the spherical geometry

> We perform the energy minimization of the paired composite fermion (CF) wave functions, proposed by Möller and Simon (MS) [PRB 77, 075319 (2008)] and extended by Yutushui and Mross (YM) [PRB 102, 195153 (2020)], where the energy is minimized by varying the CF pairing function, in the case of an approximate model of the Coulomb interaction in the second Landau level for pairing channels $\ell = -1, 3, 1$ which are expected to be in the Pfaffian, anti-Pfaffian and particle-hole symmetric (PH) Pfaffian phases respectively. It is found that the energy of the $\ell = -1$ MS wave function can be reduced substantially below that of the Moore-Read wave function at small system sizes, however, in the $\ell = 3$ case the energy cannot be reduced much below that of the YM trial wavefunction. Nonetheless, both our optimized and unoptimized wavefunctions with $\ell=-1,3$ extrapolate to roughly the same energy per particle in the thermodynamic limit. For the $\ell = 1$ case, the optimization makes no qualitative difference and these PH-Pfaffian wave functions are still energetically unfavourable. The effective CF pairing is analyzed in the resulting wave functions, where the effective pairing for the $\ell = -1, 3$ channels is found to be well approximated by a weak-pairing BCS ansatz and the $\ell = 1$ wave functions show no sign of emergent CF pairing.

## Key claim

The energy minimization of paired composite fermion wave functions for the Pfaffian (ℓ = -1) and anti-Pfaffian (ℓ = 3) channels yields optimized states that extrapolate to roughly the same energy per particle in the thermodynamic limit as the unoptimized trial wave functions, despite showing substantial energy reduction at small system sizes for the ℓ = -1 case; for the particle-hole symmetric Pfaffian (ℓ = 1) channel, optimization makes no qualitative difference and these wave functions remain energetically unfavourable.

## Method

The authors performed energy minimization of the Möller-Simon (MS) paired composite fermion wave functions in the spherical geometry by varying the CF pairing function parameters \(g_l\) (taken as real numbers) for pairing channels ℓ = -1, 3, and 1, using an approximate model of the Coulomb interaction in the second Landau level. The effective interaction was modelled as \(V^{\text{eff}}(r) = a_0/r + a_1 e^{-\alpha_1 r^2} + a_2 r^2 e^{-\alpha_2 r^2}\) with parameters fit to reproduce the Haldane pseudopotential coefficients of the second Landau level Coulomb interaction. Energies were estimated using Monte Carlo sampling with approximately \(5 \times 10^9\) samples per system size, and thermodynamic limits were obtained by fitting polynomials in \(1/N\) to the adjusted energy per particle \(\tilde{E}/N\).

## Context

This work extends the paired composite fermion wave function approach of Möller and Simon (2008) and Yutushui and Mross (2020) by performing systematic energy minimization for all three candidate pairing channels (ℓ = -1, 3, 1) relevant to the ν = 5/2 fractional quantum Hall state. The results confirm that the ℓ = -1 (Pfaffian) and ℓ = 3 (anti-Pfaffian) channels are energetically competitive in the thermodynamic limit, while the ℓ = 1 (PH-Pfaffian) channel remains unfavourable even after optimization, consistent with prior numerical studies by Morf (1998), Rezayi (2000, 2011, 2017, 2021), and Feiguin (2008). The finding that the effective CF pairing for ℓ = -1 and ℓ = 3 can be approximated by a weak-pairing BCS ansatz enables future studies of larger system sizes using these optimized trial wave functions, potentially helping to resolve the discrepancy between numerical predictions favouring Pfaffian or anti-Pfaffian phases and experimental heat conductivity measurements by Banerjee (2018) and Dutta (2021) that are more consistent with the PH-Pfaffian state.
