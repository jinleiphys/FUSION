# SU(3) Breaking in Neutral Current Axial Matrix Elements and the Spin Content of the Nucleon

> We examine the effects of SU(3) breaking in the matrix elements of the flavour-diagonal axial currents between octet baryon states and find that SU(3) breaking may be substantial for some matrix elements. We estimate the strange axial matrix element in the proton to be between -0.35 and 0 and the matrix element of the flavour-singlet current in the proton to be between -0.1 and +0.3 from the E-143 measurement g_1(x) . The up-quark content of the $Ξ^-$ is discussed and its implications for nonleptonic weak processes discussed. We also estimate the matrix element of the axial current coupling to the $Z^0$ between all octet baryon states. This may be important for neutrino interactions in dense nuclear environments, where hyperons may play an important role.

## Key claim

SU(3) breaking in the flavour-diagonal axial matrix elements between octet baryon states may be substantial for some matrix elements, and the strange axial matrix element in the proton determined from measurements of \(g_1(x)\) has large uncertainties and might yet be zero, contrary to the more precise central values often quoted in the literature.

## Method

The authors compute the leading non-analytic SU(3) breaking contributions of the form \(m_s \log m_s\) to the axial matrix elements using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with octet and decuplet baryon intermediate states in kaon, eta, and pion loops. They perform a fit to semileptonic hyperon decay data and beta-decay data to extract the axial coupling constants \(D, F, C, H\) at both tree-level and one-loop level, using a \(\chi^2\) minimization with a theoretical uncertainty of \(\sim 0.2\) assigned to each data point. The unknown counterterms of order \(\mathcal{O}(m_s)\) are set to zero, and results are presented for three values of the decuplet-octet mass splitting \(\Delta_0 = 0, 200\ \mathrm{MeV}, \infty\).

## Key numbers

- Strange axial matrix element in the proton: \(-0.35 \lesssim \Delta s \lesssim 0\) (estimated range from E-143 measurement)
- Flavour-singlet axial matrix element in the proton: \(-0.1 \lesssim \Sigma \lesssim +0.3\) (estimated range from E-143 measurement)
- E-143 measurement used: \(\int_0^1 dx\ g_1(x, Q^2 = 3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2) = 0.127 \pm 0.004 \pm 0.010\)
- Tree-level fit values: \(D = 0.79 \pm 0.10\), \(F = 0.47 \pm 0.07\), with \(D+F = 1.26 \pm 0.08\)
- Loop-level fit values for \(\Delta_0 = 0\): \(D = 0.64 \pm 0.05\), \(F = 0.42 \pm 0.04\), \(|C| = 1.39 \pm 0.06\), \(H = -2.7 \pm 0.6\)
- Loop-level estimate of \(\mathcal{O}_8\) matrix element in the proton: \(0.78 \pm 0.24\) (\(\Delta_0 = 0\)), \(0.45 \pm 0.20\) (\(\Delta_0 = 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), \(0.60 \pm 0.22\) (\(\Delta_0 = \infty\))
- Tree-level quark contributions to proton spin: \(\Delta u = 0.77 \pm 0.04\), \(\Delta d = -0.49 \pm 0.04\), \(\Delta s = -0.18 \pm 0.09\)
- Loop-level (\(\Delta_0 = 0\)) quark contributions: \(\Delta u = 0.79 \pm 0.04\), \(\Delta d = -0.48 \pm 0.04\), \(\Delta s = -0.23 \pm 0.10\)
- Loop-level (\(\Delta_0 = 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\)) quark contributions: \(\Delta u = 0.76 \pm 0.04\), \(\Delta d = -0.51 \pm 0.04\), \(\Delta s = -0.10 \pm 0.09\)
- Loop-level up-quark content of \(\Xi^-\): \(\Delta u_\Xi = -0.18 \pm 0.14\) (for \(\Delta_0 = 0\))

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of SU(3) breaking in axial matrix elements by Jaffe and Manohar (1990), Luty and Liu (1991), and Dai, Dey, Dey, and Jaffe (1995), using a more systematic chiral perturbation theory approach that includes the leading non-analytic \(m_s \log m_s\) contributions from kaon and eta loops. The results show that the strange quark content of the nucleon is highly sensitive to SU(3) breaking corrections, with uncertainties large enough that \(\Delta s\) could be zero, and the authors emphasize that planned parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Laboratory and neutrino-proton scattering at LSND will circumvent the need for SU(3) symmetry to extract the strange content of the nucleon. The analysis also provides estimates of the axial current matrix elements coupling to the \(Z^0\) for all octet baryon states, which may be important for neutrino interactions in dense nuclear environments such as neutron stars.
