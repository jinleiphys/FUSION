# Proton halo structures and 22Al

> Inspired by the recent debate as to whether the proton drip-line nucleus 22Al demonstrates a halo structure in its ground state and in order to assess such a possibility, we have analyzed theoretical results obtained within the relativistic density functional theory in 22Al and in a number of neighboring nuclei especially along isotopic, isotonic, and isobaric chains. The theory includes self-consistently the effects of pairing, deformation and the continuum. We employ two different functional parameterizations, PC-F1 and PC-PK1. Although the valence proton of the 22Al nucleus is found very loosely bound, in concordance with experimental data, its spatial distribution is found to hardly penetrate the potential barrier. Its wave function is found to consist predominately of l=2 components, for which halo formation is disfavored. Comparisons with results for isobars reveal a somewhat more extended density distribution than that of the stable or neutron-rich counterparts, but comparisons along isotopic, isotonic, and isobaric chains reveal no discontinuities in size evolution, which, if present, might have signaled exotic structures.

## Key claim

The valence proton of 22Al is very loosely bound, consistent with experimental data, but its spatial distribution hardly penetrates the potential barrier and consists predominantly of l=2 components, for which halo formation is disfavored; comparisons along isotopic, isotonic, and isobaric chains reveal no discontinuities in size evolution that would signal exotic structures.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc), which self-consistently includes pairing, axial deformation, and continuum effects, using two point-coupling density functionals (PC-PK1 and PC-F1). Selected results were also obtained with the triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (TRHBc) to investigate triaxial deformation effects. The calculations were performed for 22Al and neighboring nuclei along isotopic, isotonic, and isobaric chains.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of 22Al is predicted prolately deformed with quadrupole deformation parameter β2 = 0.29 (PC-PK1) and 0.31 (PC-F1) within DRHBc.
* The least bound proton state in 22Al is at about -1 MeV, with all less bound proton orbitals being predominately d states with no s contribution.
* Direction-specific root-mean-square radii for the prolate ground state of 22Al perpendicular to the z-axis: r_tot = 2.88 fm, r_cor = 2.70 fm, r_val = 3.60 fm, giving r_val = 1.33 r_cor.
* The proton and neutron r.m.s. radii in 22Al equal 3.077 and 2.735 fm, respectively, giving a proton skin thickness of 0.342 fm.
* The valence proton in the oblate state of 22Al (PC-PK1) has occupancy v² = 0.12 at an energy of 130 keV, with direction-specific radii r_val = 4.03 fm along the z-axis (r_val = 1.45 r_cor) and r_val = 4.51 fm perpendicular to the z-axis (r_val = 1.41 r_cor).

## Context

This work addresses the recent debate initiated by Lee (2020) claiming a halo structure in 22Al based on mirror transition asymmetries, which was challenged by ZPW (2024) who argued that the large asymmetry does not necessarily imply a ground-state halo and that typical experimental signatures are absent. The analysis extends the theoretical investigation of ZPW (2024) using the DRHBc framework, comparing results with the earlier analytical work of RJM (1992) on halo formation conditions, and finds no evidence for a proton halo in 22Al, consistent with the mirror energy difference analysis of Yu (2024).
