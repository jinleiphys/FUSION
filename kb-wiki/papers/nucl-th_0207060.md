# Deformed Nuclei in a Chiral Model

> We investigate the deformation properties of atomic nuclei in a hadronic chiral SU_f(3) model approach. The parameters are fitted to hadron mass properties and adjustments for spherical finite nuclei have been performed. Using these parameters the deformation of a series of light and heavy nuclei are obtained in a two-dimensional self-consistent calculation. In addition we discuss a case of superdeformation in a heavy nucleus.

# Deformed Nuclei in a Chiral Model

## Key claim

A hadronic chiral SU(3) model, with parameters fitted to spherical nuclei and including the isovector scalar delta meson, can reproduce nuclear groundstate quadrupole deformations across the nuclear chart with a quality comparable to standard relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, but the model still misses the experimental binding-energy precision of macroscopic-microscopic calculations by a factor of about 4 to 5, and the calculated superdeformed excitation energy for 194Hg is somewhat low at 4.3 MeV versus the experimental estimate of 6.02 MeV.

## Method

The author solves the mean-field equations of a chiral SU(3) hadronic model on a two-dimensional axially symmetric grid, including the baryon octet, scalar mesons (sigma, zeta, delta), vector mesons (omega, rho), the glueball field chi, and the Coulomb field. A zero-range pairing force is added, and the model parameters are fitted to the binding energies of a set of spherical nuclei; the resulting parameter set is called chi_m. For each even-even nucleus, the groundstate deformation is obtained by comparing converged solutions from prolate, oblate, and spherical initial configurations and selecting the energetically lowest one.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter compressibility: kappa = 215.33 MeV.
- Asymmetry energy: E_asym = 31.9 MeV.
- Maximum neutron star mass (non-rotating): M = 1.46 solar masses (reduced from 1.64 solar masses without the delta meson).
- Average binding-energy deviation for nuclei with A >= 50: epsilon_B = 0.21% for the chi_m parameter set, compared to 0.25% for the NL3 RMF parameter set.
- Average binding-energy deviation for nuclei with A >= 100: epsilon_B = 0.14% for chi_m, compared to 0.16% for NL3.
- Superdeformed excitation energy for 194Hg: E = 4.3 MeV (experimental estimate 6.02 MeV).
- Superdeformed excitation energy for 192Hg: E = 3.1 MeV.
- Groundstate quadrupole deformation of 252No: beta_2 = 0.29 (experimental value 0.31 +/- 0.02).
- Groundstate quadrupole deformation of 254No: beta_2 = 0.29 (experimental value 0.32 +/- 0.02).

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) hadronic model approach of earlier studies (Schramm, 2001; Beckmann, 2000) by including the isovector scalar delta meson and performing two-dimensional axially symmetric calculations for deformed nuclei. The results are compared with standard RMF calculations using the NL3 parameter set (Bender, 1999; Ring, 1996) and with macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Moller and Nix (1995). The model enables future three-dimensional studies and configuration-mixing calculations for shape-coexisting nuclei, particularly in the Z ~ 70 region where oblate and prolate minima are nearly degenerate.
