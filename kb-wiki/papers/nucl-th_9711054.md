# The $π^+$--emission puzzle in $^4_Λ$He decay

> We re-examine the puzzling $π^+$ emission from the weak decay of $^4_Λ$He and propose an explanation in terms of a three-body decay of the virtual $Σ^+$. Such a resolution of the $π^+$ decay puzzle is consistent with the calculated $Σ^+$ probability in light $Λ$ hypernuclei as well as the experimentally observed $π^+$ energy spectrum and $s$--wave angular distribution.

# The π⁺-emission puzzle in ⁴_ΛHe decay

## Key claim
The observed π⁺ emission from ⁴_ΛHe weak decay, which has been a puzzle for over 30 years, can be explained by a three-body decay of a virtual Σ⁺ (Σ⁺ + N → π⁺ + n + N) rather than by the two-body free Σ⁺ decay mechanism previously considered. This explanation is consistent with the calculated Σ⁺ probability in ⁴_ΛHe and the experimentally observed flat π⁺ energy spectrum and s-wave angular distribution, but the authors note that a more realistic model calculation including both the charge-exchange channel and the Σ⁰ decay mechanism is still needed.

## Method
The authors re-examine the π⁺ emission puzzle by analyzing the experimental π⁺ energy spectrum from ⁴_ΛHe decay, which is flat and s-wave, in contrast to the peaked π⁻ spectra. They propose that the virtual Σ⁺ component of the ⁴_ΛHe wave function, arising from Λ-Σ conversion, undergoes a three-body rescattering reaction Σ⁺ + N → π⁺ + n + N rather than the free two-body decay Σ⁺ → π⁺ + n. The branching ratio is estimated by combining the theoretically estimated Σ⁺ probability in ⁴_ΛHe with phase-space factors, Pauli suppression effects, and the vacuum decay rate ratio of Σ⁺ to Λ.

## Key numbers
- The experimental π⁺ to π⁻ branching ratio R(π⁺/π⁻) for ⁴_ΛHe decay is 4.3(1.7)% from Keyes et al. (1976), with other measurements in the range 5.4⁺¹·⁵₋₁·₇% to 6.9⁺¹·⁸₋₂·₁%.
- The Λ separation energy B_Λ(⁴_ΛHe) is 2.39(3) MeV, compared to B_Λ(³_ΛH) = 0.13(5) MeV and B_Λ(⁵_ΛHe) = 3.10(2) MeV.
- The Σ⁺ probability in ⁴_ΛHe is estimated as P(Σ⁺) = 9(4)%, derived from a model calculation giving total Σ probability |β|² = 14(6)%.
- The estimated branching ratio from the three-body virtual Σ⁺ decay mechanism is R(π⁺/π⁻) = 5(3)%, compared to von Hippel's earlier estimate of 0.2% (or 0.4% with higher average π⁺ momentum).
- The vacuum decay rate ratio Γ(Σ⁺ → π⁺ + n) / Γ(Λ → π⁻ + p) ≈ 2.5, with a phase-space factor of 70/185 for in-medium decay.

## Context
This work extends the earlier investigations by Dalitz and von Hippel (1962, 1964, 1966) who considered two-body decay processes and charge-exchange mechanisms but concluded neither could account for more than about 1% π⁺ decay rate. It also relates to the more recent charge-exchange calculation by Cieplý and Gal (1997) which yielded a 1.2% branching ratio, still insufficient to explain the experimental value. The proposed mechanism relies on the significant Λ-Σ coupling in light hypernuclei, which Glöckle and coworkers (1995) showed is essential for binding the hypertriton, and which also explains charge symmetry breaking in the A=4 isodoublet. The resolution enables direct confirmation of virtual baryon octet transitions in hypernuclei and suggests that similar Λ-Σ mixing signatures should be visible in hypernuclear magnetic moments, as pointed out by Dover (1995).
