# Role of pairing interaction in neutron rich odd and even Zr nuclei

> Neutron rich Zr nuclei with number of neutrons between $N$= 50 and 82 are investigated in the relativistic mean field approach in co-ordinate space. The resonant levels in the positive energy continuum have been explicitly included in the calculation. Odd nuclei have been treated in the blocking approximation. Our calculation indicates that the dripline for odd mass isotopes is far away from that for the even mass ones. Pairing interaction plays a significant role in stabilizing the even isotopes, thus extending the dripline for them.

## Key claim

The odd-mass neutron dripline for Zr isotopes is at \(N=77\) (force NL3) or \(N=81\) (force NLSH), far lower than the even-mass dripline at \(N=96\), because pairing interaction stabilizes even-even isotopes much more strongly than odd-mass ones.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations in coordinate space with exact boundary conditions, using the forces NL3 and NLSH. Resonant states in the positive-energy continuum were included via the scattering approach and a zero-range delta pairing interaction was treated with resonant-BCS (rBCS) equations. Odd nuclei were handled using the blocking approximation for different single-particle states.

## Key numbers

- Odd-mass neutron dripline for Zr isotopes: \(N=77\) with force NL3, \(N=81\) with force NLSH.
- Even-mass neutron dripline for Zr isotopes: \(N=96\) (from earlier relativistic calculations, confirmed in this work).
- In \(^{97}\)Zr, the predicted ground state is \(5/2^+\) with a \(1/2^+\) state approximately 270 keV above it (NL3); with NLSH the \(5/2^+\) state is about 290 keV below the \(s_{1/2}\) state.
- In \(^{101}\)Zr, the predicted ground state is \(1/2^+\) with the observed \(3/2^+\) state at an excitation energy of 530 keV.
- Correlation energy from pairing does not vanish at \(N=82\) but has a value of 1.65 MeV.
- For nuclei with \(N \ge 113\), the correlation energy difference between even and odd isotopes exceeds 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous coordinate-space RMF studies of neutron-rich Ca and Ni isotopes (Bhattacharya and Gangopadhyay, 2006) to the Zr isotopic chain, where no mean-field calculation for odd-mass Zr nuclei had been reported. It confirms the even-mass dripline at \(N=96\) found by earlier relativistic calculations (Sharma et al., Sandulescu et al., Zhang et al.) and shows that the odd-mass dripline is much lower, analogous to the Ca case but unlike the Ni case. The results enable future studies of deformation effects and three-quasiparticle configurations in this mass region, and highlight the reduction of the \(N=82\) magic gap due to decreased spin-orbit splitting in neutron-rich nuclei.
