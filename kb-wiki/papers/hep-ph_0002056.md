# How precisely can we determine the pion-nucleon coupling constant from the isovector GMO sum rule?

> The isovector GMO sum rule for zero energy forward pion-nucleon scattering is critically studied to obtain the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant using the precise negatively charged pion-proton and pion-deuteron scattering lengths deduced recently from pionic atom experiments. This direct determination leads to a pseudoscalar charged pion-nucleon coupling constant of 14.23 +- 0.09 (statistic) +- 0.17 (systematic). We obtain also accurate values for the pion-nucleon scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The charged pion-nucleon coupling constant can be determined directly from the isovector GMO sum rule with a precision of about 1%, but this result depends on the assumption of charge symmetry (specifically that the π⁺p scattering length equals the π⁻n scattering length) and on a theoretical description of the π⁻-deuteron scattering length accurate to about 30%.

## Method

The authors used the precisely measured π⁻p scattering length from pionic hydrogen experiments and the π⁻d scattering length from pionic deuterium experiments, together with a multiple scattering theory analysis of the deuteron to extract the isoscalar πN scattering length a⁺. They then combined this with a re-evaluation of the total cross section integral J⁻ from the GMO sum rule, using phase-shift analyses below 2 GeV/c and Regge extrapolations at high energies, to determine the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Charged pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling constant: g²_c/4π = 14.23 ± 0.09 (statistic) ± 0.17 (systematic), or f²_c/4π = 0.0786(11)
- π⁻p scattering length: 0.0883(8) m_π⁻¹ from pionic hydrogen energy shift (Cha97)
- π⁻d scattering length: -0.0261(5) m_π⁻¹ from pionic deuterium 1s energy level (Hau98)
- Isoscalar scattering length a⁺ = (a_π⁻p + a_π⁻n)/2 = (-17 ± 3 (statistic) ± 9 (systematic)) × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹
- Isovector scattering length a⁻ = (a_π⁻p - a_π⁻n)/2 = (900 ± 12) × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹
- Cross section integral J⁻ = (-1.095 ± 0.008 (statistic) ± 0.030 (systematic)) mb, with an additional systematic uncertainty from Coulomb penetration of ±0.017 mb

## Context

This work extends the earlier GMO sum rule analysis by Koch (1985), who obtained g²_c/4π = 14.2(4) with larger uncertainties, by using new precise pionic atom data from PSI experiments. The result is intermediate between the low value deduced from large NN and πN scattering data banks (Pavan, 1999; de Swart, 1997) and the high value from np charge exchange cross sections (Rahm, 1998), and is fully compatible with the latter within about one standard deviation. The analysis enables a more precise test of charge symmetry in the pion-nucleon system and provides a benchmark for future determinations of the coupling constant.
