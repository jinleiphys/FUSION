# Hot neutron matter from a Self-Consistent Green's Functions approach

> A systematic study of the microscopic and thermodynamical properties of pure neutron matter at finite temperature within the Self-Consistent Green's Function approach is performed. The model dependence of these results is analyzed by both comparing the results obtained with two different microscopic interactions, the CD-BONN and the Argonne V18 potentials, and by analyzing the results obtained with other approaches, such as the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approximation, the variational approach and the virial expansion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) approach provides a systematic study of hot pure neutron matter properties, but only for temperatures above the critical temperature of the pairing transition, thus neglecting superfluidity effects.

## Method

The authors solved the ladder approximation to the in-medium T-matrix self-consistently with the Dyson equation at finite temperature, using two realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (CD-BONN and Argonne V18) with partial waves up to J=8. The self-consistent set of equations included the single-particle spectral function, self-energy, and momentum distribution, with the chemical potential adjusted to fix the density. The entropy was computed using the dynamical quasi-particle approximation within the Luttinger-Ward formalism, and results were compared to finite-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF), variational calculations, and the virial expansion.

## Key numbers

- At T=10 MeV and ρ=0.16 fm⁻³, the SCGF energy per particle is E/A=18.0 MeV for Argonne V18 and E/A=16.9 MeV for CD-BONN
- At T=10 MeV and ρ=0.32 fm⁻³, the difference between Argonne V18 and CD-BONN in SCGF energy per particle becomes 6.4 MeV
- At T=10 MeV and ρ=0.16 fm⁻³, inclusion of hole-hole propagation increases the energy per particle by 1.6 MeV for Argonne V18 and 0.6 MeV for CD-BONN
- At T=10 MeV and ρ=0.32 fm⁻³, inclusion of hole-hole propagation increases the energy per particle by 4.0 MeV for Argonne V18 and 1.5 MeV for CD-BONN
- At ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ and T=4 MeV, the zero-momentum occupation is n(0)=0.974 for CD-BONN and n(0)=0.959 for Argonne V18
- At T=10 MeV, deviations in entropy per particle between different approximations above 0.16 fm⁻³ are at most 0.15 Boltzmann units
- At T=20 MeV, deviations in entropy per particle between approaches are at most 0.25 Boltzmann units

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature SCGF calculations of neutron matter by Dewulf et al. (2003) and finite-temperature SCGF studies by Bozek (1999, 2002) and Frick et al. (2003, 2005), providing the first systematic finite-temperature study within the SCGF approach. The results are compared to the variational calculations of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), finite-temperature BHF extensions, and the virial expansion of Schwenk (2006). The paper enables improved modeling of neutron star structure and evolution, particularly for the hot early stages after formation, and provides a benchmark for testing other many-body approaches at finite temperature.
