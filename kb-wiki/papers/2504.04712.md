# Perturbation theory of rotating scalar fields and vacuum insensitivity to rotation

> We formulate the finite-temperature perturbation theory of interacting scalar fields under external rotation. Because of the translational non-invariance in the radial direction, Green's functions are described using the Fourier-Bessel basis, instead of the conventional Fourier basis. We derive the leading-order correction to the partition function and the one-loop self-energy. The Feynman rules obtained in our perturbation theory shows that due to the finite-size effect required by the causality constraint, the zero-temperature thermodynamics in the perturbation theory is unaffected by rotation, similarly to that in the noninteracting theory.

## Key claim

The paper formulates finite-temperature perturbation theory for interacting scalar fields under rigid rotation using the Fourier-Bessel basis, and shows that the zero-temperature thermodynamics is unaffected by rotation up to at least O(λ²), because the finite-size effect required by the causality constraint (ΩR ≤ 1) ensures that all poles in the relevant contour integrals lie outside the integration contours.

## Method

The authors construct the finite-temperature path integral for a λφ⁴ theory in a cylindrical geometry with Dirichlet boundary conditions at radius R, expanding fields in Fourier-Bessel modes (with Bessel function zeros ξ_{l,k} defining radial momenta p_{l,k} = ξ_{l,k}/R). They compute the leading-order correction to the partition function (ln Z₁) and the one-loop self-energy Π₁, performing Matsubara sums via contour integration with l-dependent contours C_l. The radial integrals I involving products of four Bessel functions are evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The causality constraint requires ΩR ≤ 1, with R being the cylinder radius.
- The infrared energy gap satisfies ε_ν − Ω|l| ≥ (ξ_{l,k} − |l|)/R > 0, where ξ_{l,k} is the k-th positive root of J_l(ξ) and ξ_{l,1} > |l| for all l.
- The Cartesian one-loop self-energy is Π_{1c} = λT²; the cylindrical self-energy Π_{1r} converges to this value as R → ∞.
- For m = 0 and Ω = 0, the diagonal self-energy for mode (l=0, k=1) approaches the Cartesian limit at large RT, with deviations up to ~40% at RT ≈ 1.
- The off-diagonal self-energy elements converge to zero as R → ∞, with slower convergence for larger |l|.

## Context

This work extends the noninteracting rotating scalar theory of Vilenkin (1980) and the Fourier-Bessel treatment of rotating systems by Ambrus (2015) and Ebihara (2016) to include interactions at the perturbative level. It provides the first systematic perturbation theory for rotating scalar fields beyond mean-field, enabling future studies of rotational effects on phase transitions and vacuum structure in interacting quantum field theories, including applications to QCD matter. The formalism clarifies that the vacuum insensitivity to rotation persists in the interacting theory due to the finite-size effect, analogous to the Silver-Blaze phenomenon but with a different underlying mechanism involving the competition between the infrared energy gap and the rotation-induced effective chemical potential.
