# Impact of the Nuclear Modification of the Gluon Densities on J/Psi production in pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5 TeV

> We update our previous studies of nuclear-matter effects on J/Psi production in proton-nucleus for the recent LHC pPb runs at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV. We have analysed the effects of the modification of the gluon PDFs in nucleus, using an exact kinematics for a 2->2 process, namely g+g->J/Psi+g as expected from LO pQCD. This allows to constrain the transverse-momentum while computing the nuclear modification factor for different rapidities, unlike with the usual simplified kinematics. Owing to the absence of measurement in pp collisions at the same sqrt(s_NN) and owing to the expected significant uncertainties in yield interpolations which would hinder definite interpretations of nuclear modification factor --R_pPb--, we have derived forward-to-backward and central-to-peripheral yield ratios in which the unknown proton-proton yield cancel. These have been computed without and with a transverse-momentum cut, e.g. to comply with the ATLAS and CMS constraints in the central-rapidity region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear modification of gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) in lead nuclei significantly affects J/Psi production in proton-lead collisions at 5 TeV, but that the standard nuclear modification factor R_pPb cannot be reliably measured because no pp reference data exist at the same energy and interpolations introduce large uncertainties. The authors therefore propose forward-to-backward and central-to-peripheral yield ratios as alternative observables that cancel the unknown pp yield.

## Method

The authors used a probabilistic Glauber Monte-Carlo framework (JIN) to encode a 2-to-2 partonic process (g+g to J/Psi+g) as expected from leading-order pQCD, rather than the simplified 2-to-1 kinematics used in earlier studies. They employed the EPS09 and nDSg parametrizations for nuclear gluon PDFs, including a spatial dependence proportional to the local nuclear density. The calculations were performed for J/Psi production in pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV, with and without a transverse-momentum cut of P_T > 6 GeV, to produce predictions for the forward-to-backward ratio R_FB and the central-to-peripheral ratio R_CP.

## Key numbers

- The forward-to-backward ratio R_FB for prompt J/Psi measured by LHCb is 0.66 +/- 0.08 for 2.5 < |y_CM| < 4
- The forward-to-backward ratio R_FB for inclusive J/Psi measured by ALICE is 0.60 +/- 0.07 for 3 < |y_CM| < 3.5
- The formation time of the J/Psi in the lead rest frame ranges from 6 fm at y=-4.0 to 6.0 x 10^4 fm at y=4.5
- The effective break-up cross sections considered for the pre-resonant pair are 1.5 mb and 2.8 mb
- The factorisation scale uncertainty was evaluated using mu_R = (0.75, 1, 2) x m_T

## Context

This work updates earlier studies by Ferreiro et al. (2008) of nuclear-matter effects on J/Psi production in proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC, extending them to LHC energies. It goes beyond other J/Psi production studies in pA collisions that assumed a 2-to-1 partonic process where colliding gluons carried intrinsic transverse momentum. The predictions can be compared to preliminary LHCb and ALICE data from the 2013 LHC pPb run, and the authors note that unless there is unexpectedly large anti-shadowing, the measured R_FB values support the presence of significant gluon shadowing stronger than the central value of EPS09LO.
