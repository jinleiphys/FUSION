# Sensitivity of flow harmonics to sub-nucleon scale fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

> In this paper a new procedure to smooth out the initial energy densities of hydrodynamics is employed to show that the initial spatial eccentricities $\varepsilon_{m,n}$, which drive the final state flow harmonics $v_n$, are remarkably robust with respect to variations of the underlying scale of initial energy density spatial gradients, $λ$, in nucleus-nucleus collisions. For $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions, the $\varepsilon_{m,n}$'s (across centrality classes) change by less than $10\%$ if the scale of fluctuations is varied from $0.1$ to $1$ fm. We show, using the 2+1 Lagrangian hydrodynamic code, v-USPhydro, that this robustness is transferred to the final $v_n$'s computed within event by event viscous hydrodynamics. This indicates that the flow harmonics in nucleus-nucleus collisions are not yet particularly sensitive to the underlying microscopic sub-nucleon physics below the confinement scale. On the other hand, the eccentricities of top $1\%$ high multiplicity $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV p+Pb collisions are found to be very sensitive to sub-nucleonic scale fluctuations, which should be contrasted with the robustness found in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions with the same multiplicity.

## Key claim

The initial spatial eccentricities that drive final-state flow harmonics in nucleus-nucleus collisions change by less than 10% when the smoothing scale of initial energy density fluctuations is varied from 0.1 fm to 1 fm, indicating that flow harmonics are not yet particularly sensitive to sub-nucleon physics below the confinement scale; however, this robustness does not hold for high-multiplicity p+Pb collisions, where eccentricities are very sensitive to sub-nucleonic scale fluctuations.

## Method

The authors introduced a smoothing procedure based on a cubic spline kernel to systematically remove fluctuations below a given scale λ from initial energy density profiles generated by the MCKLN model for Pb+Pb collisions at √s=2.76 TeV. They computed initial spatial eccentricities ε_{m,n} across centrality classes as a function of λ, and then evolved the smoothed initial conditions using the 2+1 Lagrangian viscous hydrodynamic code v-USPhydro with a simplified Israel-Stewart relaxation equation for shear stress, setting bulk viscosity to zero. For p+Pb collisions at √s=5.02 TeV, they used the Trento code to generate initial conditions and studied the λ-dependence of eccentricities for top 1% high-multiplicity events.

## Key numbers

- For √s=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, the ε_{m,n} values across centrality classes change by less than 10% when λ is varied from 0.1 fm to 1 fm.
- The smoothing kernel used has finite support such that it strictly vanishes for |r|/λ > 2.
- The initial energy densities before smoothing were defined on a square lattice of size λ₀ = 0.06 fm.
- For the shear relaxation time, using η/s = 0.1 and T ~ 400 MeV gives τ_π ~ 0.25 fm.
- The study used 150 events per centrality class for Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of how different length scales in initial conditions affect hydrodynamic observables, such as those by Petersen (2010), Floerchinger (2013), and Konchakovski (2014), by introducing a systematic smoothing procedure that preserves the relative peaks and valleys of the energy density. The results qualitatively agree with the mode-by-mode hydrodynamics analysis of Floerchinger (2013) that azimuthal anisotropies are more sensitive to intermediate mesoscopic scales than to microscopic sub-nucleon scales. The finding that p+Pb eccentricities are sensitive to sub-nucleonic fluctuations while peripheral Pb+Pb collisions with comparable multiplicity are not suggests that the interpretation of collectivity in small systems may require different theoretical treatment than in large nucleus-nucleus collisions.
