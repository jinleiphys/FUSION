# Stable hybrid stars within a SU(3) Quark-Meson-Model

> The inner regions of the most massive compact stellar objects might be occupied by a phase of quarks. Since the observations of the massive pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and of PSR J0348+0432 with about two solar masses, the equations of state constructing relativistic stellar models have to be constrained respecting these new limits. We discuss stable hybrid stars, i.e. compact objects with an outer layer composed of nuclear matter and with a core consisting of quark matter (QM). For the outer nuclear layer we utilize a density dependent nuclear equation of state and we use a chiral SU(3) Quark-Meson model with a vacuum energy pressure to describe the objects core. The appearance of a disconnected mass-radius branch emerging from the hybrid star branch implies the existence of a third family of compact stars, so called twin stars. Twin stars did not emerge as the transition pressure has to be relatively small with a large jump in energy density, which could not be satisfied within our approach. This is, among other reasons, due to the fact that the speed of sound in QM has to be relatively high, which can be accomplished by an increase of the repulsive coupling. This increase on the other hand yields too high transition pressures for twins stars to appear.

## Key claim

The authors find that within their SU(3) Quark-Meson-Model approach, stable hybrid stars (with a quark matter core and nuclear matter outer layer) can reach masses up to approximately 2 solar masses, but twin stars (a disconnected third family of compact stars) do not emerge because the required combination of low transition pressure and large energy density discontinuity cannot be satisfied.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star models by combining a density-dependent nuclear equation of state (DD2) for the outer layer with a chiral SU(3) Quark-Meson-Model equation of state for the quark matter core, using a Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations, varying four parameters: the constituent quark mass, the vector coupling constant, the sigma meson mass, and the bag constant representing vacuum energy pressure.

## Key numbers

- For B=60 MeV and g_omega=1: transition occurs at p_t approximately 1.5 MeV/fm^3 and epsilon_t approximately 102 MeV/fm^3, with energy density discontinuity Delta epsilon approximately 122 MeV/fm^3; stable hybrid stars reach up to approximately 1.7 solar masses
- For B=60 MeV and g_omega=2: hybrid star solutions reach masses greater than or equal to 2 solar masses
- For B=100 MeV and g_omega=0: transition at p_t approximately 15 MeV/fm^3, epsilon_t approximately 230 MeV/fm^3, Delta epsilon approximately 90 MeV/fm^3; stable up to approximately 1.6 solar masses at radius approximately 11 km
- For B=100 MeV and g_omega=1: transition at p_t approximately 75 MeV/fm^3, epsilon_t approximately 380 MeV/fm^3, Delta epsilon approximately 100 MeV/fm^3; stable up to approximately 1.7 solar masses
- Speed of sound in quark matter for physically relevant cases lies between c_s^2 = 0.3 and c_s^2 = 0.5
- For g_omega=2, the speed of sound reaches c_s^2 approximately 0.5

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pure quark stars within the SU(3) Quark-Meson-Model (Zacchi, 2015) to hybrid star configurations, and builds on the general framework for empirical testing of hybrid stars developed by Alford et al. (2014, 2015) using a constant speed of sound parametrization. The results show that the SU(3) Quark-Meson-Model can produce hybrid stars reaching the 2 solar mass limit set by observations of PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, but unlike the constant speed of sound approach of Alford et al., this model does not produce twin stars, suggesting that the existence of twin stars depends sensitively on the specific quark matter equation of state used.
