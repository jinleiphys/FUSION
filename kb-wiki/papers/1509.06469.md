# Photoproduction of the $f'_2(1525)$ and $K^*_2(1430)$

> Assuming that the $f'_2(1525)$ and $K^*_2(1430)$ resonances are dynamically generated states from the vector meson-vector meson interactions in $S$-wave with spin $S=2$, we study the $纬p \to f'_2(1525) p$ and $纬p \to K^*_2(1430) 螞(危)$ reactions. These reactions proceed in the following way: the incoming photon first mutates into a $蟻^0$, $蠅$, or $蠒$ meson via vector meson dominance, which then interacts with the $蟻^0$, $蠅$ or $K^*$ emitted by the incoming proton to form the tensor mesons $f'_2(1525)$ and $K^*_2(1430)$. The picture is simple and has no free parameters, as all the parameters of the mechanism have been fixed in previous studies. We predict the differential and total cross sections of these reactions. The results can be tested in future experiments and therefore offer new clues on the nature of these tensor states.

## Key claim

Assuming the \(f'_2(1525)\) and \(K^*_2(1430)\) resonances are dynamically generated states from vector meson-vector meson interactions in \(S\)-wave with spin \(S=2\), the authors predict the differential and total cross sections for \(\gamma p \to f'_2(1525) p\) and \(\gamma p \to K^*_2(1430) \Lambda(\Sigma)\) reactions with no free parameters, offering a testable check of the molecular picture of these tensor states.

## Method

The photoproduction proceeds via vector meson dominance: the incoming photon converts into a \(\rho^0\), \(\omega\), or \(\phi\) meson, which then rescatters with a vector meson (\(\rho^0\), \(\omega\), or \(K^*\)) emitted from the proton to dynamically generate the tensor resonance. The amplitudes are constructed using the couplings of the tensor states to vector meson pairs (taken from previous unitary coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter calculations), the \(\gamma\)-\(V\) vertex from hidden gauge Lagrangians, and the \(VNN\) vertices. The differential cross sections are computed as functions of the Mandelstam \(t\) and the invariant mass of the decay products (\(K\bar{K}\) for \(f'_2(1525)\), \(K\pi\) for \(K^*_2(1430)\)), with \(D\)-wave decay widths included.

## Key numbers

- The \(f'_2(1525)\) resonance is seen in the \(K^+K^-\) invariant mass distribution at \(E_{\gamma} = 3.4\) GeV and \(t = -1.2\) GeV\(^2\), peaking around \(M_{\rm inv} = 1525\) MeV with an apparent width of about 60 MeV.
- The total decay width of the \(f'_2(1525)\) is taken as \(\Gamma^{\rm on}_{f'_2} = 73\) MeV, with the \(K\bar{K}\) partial width \(\Gamma^{\rm on}_{K\bar{K}} = 32.5\) MeV (89% of total).
- The total decay width of the \(K^*_2(1430)\) is taken as \(\Gamma^{\rm on}_{K^*_2} = 104\) MeV, with the \(K\pi\) partial width \(\Gamma^{\rm on}_{K\pi} = 34.7\) MeV (50% of total).
- The coupling constants of the \(f'_2(1525)\) to \(\rho\rho\) are \((-2443, i649)\) MeV; to \(\omega\omega\) are \((-2709, i8)\) MeV; to \(\phi\omega\) are \((5016, -i17)\) MeV.
- The coupling constants of the \(K^*_2(1430)\) to \(\rho K^*\) are \((10901, -i71)\) MeV; to \(\omega K^*\) are \((2267, -i13)\) MeV; to \(\phi K^*\) are \((-2898, i17)\) MeV.
- The exchanged vector meson mass is taken as \(m_V = 780\) MeV for \(\rho^0\) and \(\omega\); the \(K^*\) mass is taken as \(m_{K^*} = 893.1\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously applied to the \(\gamma p \to p f_2(1270)\) reaction (Xie, 2014) to the photoproduction of the \(f'_2(1525)\) and \(K^*_2(1430)\) tensor states. It tests the molecular picture of these states, which were dynamically generated from vector-vector interactions in unitary approaches (Molina, 2008; Geng, 2008; Geng, 2009), in a baryonic reaction for the first time. The predictions can be checked by future experiments such as those at CLAS, providing new clues on the nature of these tensor resonances.
