# Complex scaling of the hyper-spheric coordinates and Faddeev equations

> We implement complex scaling of Faddeev equations using hyper-spheric coordinates and adiabatic expansion. Complex scaling of coordinates allows convenient calculations of three-body resonances. We derive the necessary equations and investigate the adiabatic spectrum at large distances. We illustrate the viability of the implementation by calculations of several three-body resonances: a $0^+$ resonance in a model benchmark system of three identical bosons; the $2^+$ resonance in the $^6$He nucleus within the $α+n+n$ model; and the two $0^+$ resonances in $^{12}$C within the three-$α$ model.

## Key claim
Complex scaling of Faddeev equations can be implemented using hyper-spheric coordinates and adiabatic expansion, enabling convenient calculation of three-body resonances. The method is demonstrated to be viable for several benchmark systems, though the accuracy depends on the specific system and resonance parameters.

## Method
The authors implement complex scaling of the Faddeev equations by expressing them in hyper-spheric coordinates and applying an adiabatic expansion. They derive the necessary equations and investigate the adiabatic spectrum at large distances to identify resonance behavior. The method is tested on three systems: a model benchmark of three identical bosons, the 6He nucleus modeled as alpha+n+n, and 12C modeled as three alpha particles.

## Context
This work extends the application of complex scaling methods to three-body Faddeev equations formulated in hyper-spheric coordinates, building on prior developments in complex scaling for few-body systems. The approach enables calculation of three-body resonances that are difficult to access with bound-state methods alone. The benchmark calculations on 6He and 12C demonstrate the method's applicability to nuclear physics problems, potentially enabling future studies of other three-body resonances in light nuclei.
