# Thermonuclear reaction rate of $^{18}$Ne($α$,$p$)$^{21}$Na from Monte-Carlo calculations

> The $^{18}$Ne($α$,$p$)$^{21}$Na reaction impacts the break-out from the hot CNO-cycles to the $rp$-process in type I X-ray bursts. We present a revised thermonuclear reaction rate, which is based on the latest experimental data. The new rate is derived from Monte-Carlo calculations, taking into account the uncertainties of all nuclear physics input quantities. In addition, we present the reaction rate uncertainty and probability density versus temperature. Our results are also consistent with estimates obtained using different indirect approaches.

## Key claim

The revised thermonuclear reaction rate for $^{18}$Ne($\alpha$,$p$)$^{21}$Na is derived from Monte-Carlo calculations that incorporate the latest experimental data on resonance energies, spins, parities, and partial widths, but the rate remains consistent with estimates from indirect approaches only within a factor of approximately 3 uncertainty, and the earlier forward reaction data of Groombridge et al. (2002) were excluded due to a dramatic disagreement with reverse reaction data.

## Method

Resonance strengths $\omega\gamma_{\alpha p}$ for the $^{18}$Ne($\alpha$,$p$)$^{21}$Na reaction were determined for over 30 resonances in the compound nucleus $^{22}$Mg by grouping states into four categories (A through D) based on the availability of spectroscopic information from the mirror nucleus $^{22}$Ne, including $\alpha$-capture data, $\alpha$-transfer data, and elastic scattering measurements. For states without spectroscopic information (category D), $\alpha$-particle partial widths were sampled from a Porter-Thomas distribution with a mean reduced width of $\left< \theta_\alpha^2 \right> = 0.03 \pm 0.01$, extrapolated from systematics of Pogrebnyak et al. (2013). The reaction rate and its probability density were then computed using a Monte-Carlo sampling method that simultaneously accounts for uncertainties in resonance energies, resonance strengths, and ambiguous spin-parity assignments.

## Key numbers

- The $Q$-value of the $^{18}$Ne($\alpha$,$p$)$^{21}$Na reaction is positive, with the $\alpha$-particle binding energy in $^{22}$Mg given by $S_\alpha = 8142.5 \pm 0.5$ keV.
- The resonance at $E^\ast = 10052$ keV in $^{22}$Mg (corresponding to $E = 1567$ keV in the $^{18}$Ne+$\alpha$ channel) has a resonance strength of $\omega\gamma_{\alpha p} = 65$ eV with an estimated uncertainty factor of two.
- The $0^+$ state at $E^\ast = 10052$ keV in $^{22}$Mg has a dimensionless reduced $\alpha$-particle width of $\theta_\alpha^2 = 0.46$, averaged from $\alpha$-capture ($\theta_\alpha^2 = 0.27 \pm 0.09$) and $\alpha$-transfer ($\theta_\alpha^2 = 0.64$) measurements.
- The reaction rate covers many orders of magnitude in the temperature range $T_9 = 0.25 - 3$, with deviations between the narrow-resonance formalism and a numerical integration of the cross section remaining below 5% for $T_9 = 1-2$ and below 10% for $T_9 = 0.25 - 3$.
- The reaction rates obtained by two independent methods in Mohr and Matic (2013) differ by a factor of approximately 3, with estimated uncertainties of a factor of approximately 2 for each approach.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Mohr and Matic (2013) by incorporating new spin-parity assignments from resonant proton scattering experiments by He et al. (2013) and Zhang et al. (2014), and by applying a Monte-Carlo sampling method that provides the first statistically meaningful rate probability densities for the $^{18}$Ne($\alpha$,$p$)$^{21}$Na reaction. The revised rate impacts the break-out from hot CNO-cycles to the $rp$-process in type I X-ray bursts, and the methodology enables future studies to determine the break-out temperature and its uncertainty under typical burst conditions.
