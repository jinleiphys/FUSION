# Instantons and the Chiral Phase Transition at non-zero Baryon Density

> We study an interacting ensemble of instantons at finite baryon chemical potential. We emphasize the importance of fermionic zero modes and calculate the fermion induced interaction between instantons at non-zero chemical potential. We show that unquenched simulations of the instanton ensemble are feasible in two regimes, for sufficiently small and for very large chemical potential. At very large chemical potential chiral symmetry is restored and the instanton ensemble is dominated by strongly correlated chain-like configurations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that unquenched simulations of the instanton ensemble at finite baryon chemical potential are feasible only in two regimes: for sufficiently small chemical potential and for very large chemical potential; at very large chemical potential chiral symmetry is restored and the instanton ensemble becomes dominated by strongly correlated chain-like configurations, but the sign problem makes simulations difficult at intermediate chemical potentials.

## Method

The author constructs the free Euclidean propagators for bosons and fermions at non-zero chemical potential, then derives exact zero-mode solutions of the Dirac operator in an instanton field at finite chemical potential by solving the Dirac equation with a chemical potential term. Overlap matrix elements of the Dirac operator between instanton and anti-instanton zero modes are calculated numerically, and the fermion determinant is evaluated in the zero-mode basis. Simulations of the instanton ensemble are performed using the modulus of the determinant, with the phase of the determinant incorporated into observables via reweighting.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative instanton density at finite chemical potential is suppressed by a factor exp(-N_f (ρμ)^2) relative to the zero-density rate n(ρ,0)
- The quark condensate at small baryon density behaves as ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ ⟨q̄q⟩_0 (1 - 0.3 (ρ_B/ρ_B^0)) where ρ_B^0 = 0.14 fm^{-3} is nuclear matter density
- For N_f=2, the density dependence of the 't Hooft operator estimated via factorization gives ⟨O_{'tH}⟩ ≈ ⟨O_{'tH}⟩_0 (1 - 0.6 (ρ_B/ρ_B^0))
- The πN sigma term used is Σ_{πN} ≈ 46 MeV
- Simulations were performed with instanton density (N/V) = 1 fm^{-4}, quark masses m = 20, 30, 40 MeV, and volumes V = 16, 32, 64 fm^4
- The pion masses at μ=0 for these quark masses are m_π ≈ 240, 280, 360 MeV with statistical errors Δm_π ≈ 20 MeV
- The onset chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration appears consistent with μ_0 ≈ m_π/2
- Simulations at μ = 0.5 fm^{-1} showed ⟨e^{iφ}⟩ still differs significantly from zero; for μ > 1 fm^{-1} both real and imaginary parts of ⟨e^{iφ}⟩ are close to zero

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model, which has provided a phenomenologically successful description of the QCD vacuum and chiral symmetry breaking (Schäfer and Shuryak, 1997), to finite baryon chemical potential. It contrasts with random matrix models of QCD at finite density (Stephanov, 1996; Janik et al., 1996; Halasz et al., 1997) where the chemical potential acts as a simple external field, and with the quenched approximation which incorrectly predicts chiral symmetry restoration at arbitrarily small chemical potential (Gibbs, 1988). The study enables future simulations of the instanton ensemble at finite density and provides a mechanism for chiral symmetry restoration at large chemical potential based on chain-like instanton-anti-instanton configurations, analogous to the role of instanton-anti-instanton molecules in the finite-temperature chiral phase transition (Schäfer et al., 1995).
