# Electromagnetic proton-neutron knockout off 16O: new achievements in theory

> Results for the cross sections of the exclusive 16O(e,e'pn)14N and 16O(gamma,pn)14N knockout reactions are presented and discussed in different kinematics. In comparison with earlier work, a complete treatment of the center-of-mass (CM) effects in the nuclear one-body current is considered in connection with the problem of the lack of orthogonality between initial bound and final scattering states. The effects due to CM and orthogonalization are investigated in combination with different treatments of correlations in the two-nucleon overlap function and for different parametrizations of the two-body currents. The CM effects lead in super-parallel kinematics to a dramatic increase of the 16O(e,e'pn) cross section to the 1_2^+ excited state (3.95 MeV) of 14N. In all the situations considered the results are very sensitive to the treatment of correlations. A crucial role is played by tensor correlations, but also the contribution of long-range correlations is important.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including a complete treatment of center-of-mass (CM) effects in the nuclear one-body current, combined with orthogonalization of initial bound and final scattering states, produces a dramatic increase (over one order of magnitude) in the calculated cross section for the \(^{16}\text{O}(e,e'pn)^{14}\text{N}\) reaction to the \(1_2^+\) excited state in super-parallel kinematics, which can resolve previous large discrepancies with experimental data. However, the magnitude of these CM effects depends strongly on the treatment of correlations in the two-nucleon overlap function, with tensor correlations playing a crucial role.

## Method

The authors compute exclusive cross sections for \(^{16}\text{O}(e,e'pn)^{14}\text{N}\) and \(^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,pn)^{14}\text{N}\) reactions using a direct knockout model where the transition matrix element is built from a two-nucleon overlap function (TOF), a nuclear current operator (one-body plus two-body meson-exchange and \(\Delta\)-isobar currents), and a two-nucleon scattering wave function. The key theoretical improvement is enforcing orthogonality between single-particle bound and scattering states via Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization, which automatically includes all CM effects from the transformation to Jacobi coordinates, rather than subtracting the uncorrelated one-body current contribution as in prior work. Calculations are performed in super-parallel kinematics with three different TOF models (SF-B, SF-CC, SM-SRC) and two parametrizations of the two-body currents (unregularized and Bonn parametrization).

## Key numbers

- The CM effects in the orthogonalized approach produce a huge enhancement of the one-body current contribution to the \(^{16}\text{O}(e,e'pn)^{14}\text{N}\) cross section to the \(1_2^+\) state (3.95 MeV), larger than one order of magnitude at low missing momenta, only slightly reduced at higher momenta.
- In the final cross section (one-body plus two-body currents) for the \(1_2^+\) state, the difference between the orthogonalized approach and the previous approach remains large, a bit less than one order of magnitude at low momenta in the maximum region.
- With the Bonn parametrization for the two-body currents, the \(\Delta\)-current contribution is dramatically reduced compared to the unregularized parametrization; the Bonn parametrization reduces the total cross section by at most 30–40%.
- For the \(1_2^+\) state, the SF-B overlap function gives a cross section up to a factor of 6 larger than the SF-CC overlap function in the maximum region; the SM-SRC cross section is up to about two orders of magnitude lower than SF-B in the maximum region.
- The SM-SRC one-body current contribution is up to about three orders of magnitude lower than the SF-B one-body current contribution.
- The calculations use incident electron energy \(E_0 = 855\) MeV, energy transfer \(\omega = 215\) MeV, momentum transfer \(q = 316\) MeV/\(c\), and electron scattering angle \(\theta_e = 18^\circ\).
- For the \(^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,pn)^{14}\text{N}\) reaction, calculations use incident photon energy \(E_\gamma = 215\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Pavia group's model for two-nucleon knockout, which previously treated CM effects and orthogonalization only for proton-proton knockout (Giusti et al., 2005), to the more complex proton-neutron case where tensor correlations and meson-exchange currents are both important. The results directly address the large discrepancies found between the earlier theoretical model (Barbieri, 2005) and the first exclusive \(^{16}\text{O}(e,e'pn)^{14}\text{N}\) measurements at MAMI (Middleton, 2006), showing that the CM effects included here can give much better agreement. The work enables a systematic comparison with experimental data across multiple kinematical settings, which is promised in a forthcoming paper, and highlights the need for consistent treatment of both short-range and tensor correlations in the two-nucleon overlap function.
