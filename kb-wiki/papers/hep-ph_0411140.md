# The Possible $J^{PC}I^G=2^{++}2^+$ State X(1600)

> The interesting state X(1600) with $J^{PC}I^G=2^{++}2^+$ can't be a conventional $q \bar q$ meson in the quark model. Using a mixed interpolating current with different color configurations, we investigate the possible existence of X(1600) in the framework of QCD finite energy sum rules. Our results indicate that both the "hidden color" and coupled channel effects may be quite important in the multiquark system. We propose several reactions to look for this state.

## Key claim

Using QCD finite energy sum rules (FESR) with a mixed interpolating current that is a linear combination of two different color configurations, the authors find evidence for a resonance near 1.65 GeV with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}I^G=2^{++}2^+\), consistent with the experimentally observed X(1600) state. The analysis requires a specific mixing of color structures to achieve convergence of the operator product expansion, indicating that neither a pure "hidden color" nor a pure diquark-type current alone yields a reliable sum rule.

## Method

The authors construct a general interpolating current for a tetraquark state as a linear combination of the \(\eta^{\bar 3}\) (diquark-type) and \(\eta^{6}\) color configurations, parameterized by a complex mixing parameter \(Y = a + bi\). They compute the two-point correlation function of this current using the operator product expansion up to dimension-8 condensates, extract the spectral density, and apply finite energy sum rules (FESR) to determine the resonance mass. The mixing parameters are tuned to suppress higher-dimensional condensate contributions and ensure convergence of the OPE series, with the mass extracted from the ratio of successive FESR moments.

## Key numbers

- Extracted mass of X(1600): \(M_X = (1.65 \pm 0.15)\ \text{GeV}\)
- Condensate values used: \(\langle \bar q q \rangle = -(0.24\ \text{GeV})^3\), \(\langle g_s^2 GG \rangle = (0.48 \pm 0.14)\ \text{GeV}^4\), \(m_0^2 = (0.8 \pm 0.2)\ \text{GeV}^2\) for the mixed quark-gluon condensate \(\langle g_s \bar q \sigma G q \rangle = -m_0^2 \langle \bar q q \rangle\)
- For the chosen mixing parameters \(\epsilon_1 = -0.02\), \(\epsilon_2 = -2\), the zeroth FESR moment requires \(s_0 > 2.3\ \text{GeV}^2\) for positivity and convergence
- The experimentally observed X(1600) has mass \(1600 \pm 100\ \text{MeV}\) from the reaction \(\gamma\gamma \rightarrow \rho^0\rho^0 \rightarrow 4\pi\)

## Context

This work extends the study of multiquark states beyond conventional quark model mesons, following experimental discoveries of candidates such as the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark (LEPS Collaboration, 2003), the \(D_{sJ}(2317)\) and \(D_{sJ}(2457)\) charm-strange mesons (BABAR, CLEO, BELLE), and the X(3872) charmonium-like state (BELLE). The X(1600) state was previously observed by the ARGUS collaboration in \(\gamma\gamma \rightarrow \rho^0\rho^0\) and interpreted as a tetraquark candidate. Prior theoretical work includes potential scattering models (Bajc, 2000), diquark cluster model calculations (Maiani et al., 2004), and potential model estimates giving 1544 MeV (Vijande et al., 2004). The present FESR analysis demonstrates that both "hidden color" and coupled channel effects are important for multiquark systems, and proposes specific experimental reactions for further searches, including \(J/\psi\) decays, charge exchange hadron reactions, and antiproton annihilation.
