# Accurate nuclear masses from a three parameter Kohn-Sham DFT approach (BCPM)

> Given the promising features of the recently proposed Barcelona-Catania-Paris (BCP) functional \cite{Baldo.08}, it is the purpose of this paper to still improve on it. It is, for instance, shown that the number of open parameters can be reduced from 4-5 to 2-3, i.e. by practically a factor of two. One parameter is tightly fixed by a fine-tuning of the bulk, a second by the surface energy. The third is the strength of the spin-orbit potential on which the final result does not depend within the scatter of the values used in Skyrme and Gogny like functionals. An energy rms value of 1.58 MeV is obtained from a fit of these three parameters to the 579 measured masses reported in the Audi and Waspra 2003 compilation. This rms value compares favorably with the one obtained using other successful mean field theories. Charge radii are also well reproduced when compared with experiment. The energies of some excited states, mostly the isoscalar giant monopole resonances, are studied within this model as well.

# Accurate nuclear masses from a three parameter Kohn-Sham DFT approach (BCPM)

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Kohn-Sham DFT functional for nuclear masses can be reduced to only two or three adjustable parameters, one for bulk nuclear matter binding energy, one for surface energy, and a spin-orbit strength that does not affect results within typical scatter, while still achieving a mass rms of 1.58 MeV comparable to Skyrme and Gogny functionals that use 10–12 or more parameters.

## Method

The bulk part of the energy density functional is taken from microscopic Brueckner Hartree-Fock calculations of symmetric and neutron matter equations of state, fitted with fifth-order polynomials in density. The surface term uses Gaussian form factors whose strengths are fixed by requiring that the bulk limit of the surface term reproduces the quadratic density term of the bulk energy, leaving only the Gaussian range as a free parameter. The functional is applied in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction, including rotational energy corrections for deformed nuclei, and fitted to the 579 measured even-even nuclear masses from the Audi and Wapstra 2003 compilation.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of 1.58 MeV from a fit of three parameters to 579 measured masses (Audi and Wapstra 2003 compilation)
- Optimal saturation energy per particle: -15.98 MeV (fine-tuned in steps of 0.01 MeV over the interval 15.97–16.03 MeV)
- Saturation density: 0.16 fm⁻³
- Nuclear matter incompressibility modulus K₀ = 212.4 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient J = 31.90 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L = 52.96 MeV
- Symmetry energy curvature K_sym = -96.75 MeV
- Skewness coefficient K' = 879.6 MeV
- Effective mass m/m* = 1.00
- Spin-orbit strength W_LS = 90.5 MeV (consistent with effective mass of 1 and Gogny force value of 130 MeV for m* = 0.7m)

## Context

This work extends the Barcelona-Catania-Paris (BCP) functional of Baldo (2008) by reducing the number of adjustable parameters from 4–5 to 2–3, eliminating the subtraction term in the finite-range surface energy and fixing surface strengths from the microscopic bulk calculation. The approach follows the Kohn-Sham DFT strategy previously applied in nuclear physics by Fayans (1998) and Steiner (2004), but uses a more modern microscopic equation of state. The resulting BCPM functional enables large-scale mass table calculations on a personal computer and provides predictions for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to stellar nucleosynthesis, where experimental data are unavailable.
