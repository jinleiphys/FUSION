# Transport Properties of a Non-relativistic Delta-Shell Gas with Long Scattering Lengths

> The coefficients of diffusion, thermal conductivity, and shear viscosity are calculated for a system of non-relativistic particles interacting via a delta-shell potential V(r)=-v δ(r-R) when the average distance between particles is smaller than R. The roles of resonances and long scattering lengths including the unitary limit are examined. Results for ratios of diffusion to viscosity and viscosity to entropy density are presented for varying scattering lengths.

# Transport Properties of a Non-relativistic Delta-Shell Gas with Long Scattering Lengths

## Key claim

The paper calculates transport coefficients for a dilute gas of non-relativistic particles interacting via a delta-shell potential, showing that even at the two-body level, the coefficients of diffusion, thermal conductivity, and shear viscosity acquire a significantly larger temperature dependence as the scattering length approaches infinity (the unitary limit), but the authors stress that their study is mainly pedagogical and that analyses beyond what they offer, particularly regarding higher than two-body interactions, are needed.

## Method

The authors use the Chapman-Enskog method to calculate transport coefficients in first and second order of deviations from the equilibrium distribution function, based on two-body quantum mechanical inputs from the delta-shell potential V(r) = -v δ(r-R). The transport cross sections are expressed as infinite sums over partial waves, with proper symmetrization for spin-1/2 particles, and the transport coefficients are normalized to hard-sphere-like values. The scattering length a_sl and effective range r_0 are tuned by varying the single dimensionless parameter g = 2μvR/ħ², with the unitary limit occurring at g = 1.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-proton and neutron-neutron scattering lengths are a_sl ≈ -23.8 fm and -18.5 fm respectively, much longer than the few fm ranges of strong interactions.
- For g → 1 (unitary limit), a_sl → ∞, while r_0/R → 4/3 and the shape parameter P → 3/80.
- For T ≪ T̃ (characteristic temperature T̃ = 2πħ²/(k_B m R²)), the diffusion coefficient asymptotically behaves as D/D̃ → 8π(T/T̃)^{3/2} for g=1 and D/D̃ → (4/17)(T/T̃)^{1/2} for g=3.
- For T ≪ T̃, the shear viscosity asymptotically behaves as η/η̃ → 6π(T/T̃)^{3/2} for g=1 and η/η̃ → (1/6)(T/T̃)^{1/2} for g=3.
- For g ≠ 1,3 and T ≪ T̃, the ratio mnD/η approaches 6/5 = 1.20; for g=1 it approaches 4/5 = 0.80; for g=3 it approaches 72/85 = 0.85.
- The contribution from the second approximation to viscosity is small, with |δ_η| < 0.15.
- In the dilute gas limit, η/s for the delta-shell gas remains above (1/4π)ħ/k_B.

## Context

This work extends the quantum mechanical treatment of the delta-shell potential by Gottfried (1966, 2004) to transport properties of a dilute gas, and connects to atomic physics experiments where the unitary limit (a_sl → ∞) is accessible, as in the work of Kinast (2005). The results for the unitary limit viscosity coefficient were obtained earlier by Massignan, and the paper compares its findings to those of intermediate and extreme degeneracies by Massignan and Rupak (2007) which highlight the additional roles of superfluidity and superconductivity. The study enables future work on many-body effects beyond the two-body level, particularly for densities where the average inter-particle distance is smaller than the range R.
