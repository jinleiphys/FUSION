# On photoexcitation of baryon antidecuplet

> We show that the photoexcitation of the baryon antidecuplet, suggested by the soliton classification of low-lying baryons, is strongly suppressed on the proton target. The process occurs mostly on the neutron target. This qualitative prediction can be useful in identifying the non-exotic members of the antidecuplet in the known baryon spectrum. We also analyze the interrelation between photocouplings of various baryon multiplets in the soliton picture and in the nonrelativistic quark model.

## Key claim

The photoexcitation of the baryon antidecuplet is strongly suppressed on the proton target and occurs mostly on the neutron target; in the chiral limit, the octet-antidecuplet electromagnetic transition from the proton or Σ⁺ target does not occur at all, and can only arise from SU(3) symmetry breaking effects.

## Method

The authors use the chiral soliton picture, where all low-lying baryons are treated as rotational excitations of a classical soliton, and compute magnetic dipole transition amplitudes between the octet and antidecuplet baryon multiplets by sandwiching the photon-soliton coupling operator between rotational wave functions expressed as Wigner SU(3) functions. The universal constants v₁, v₂, v₃ in the photon-soliton coupling are fixed using the chiral quark soliton model calculations and the nonrelativistic limit, with (v₁ − v₂/2) adjusted to reproduce the proton magnetic moment, and SU(3) breaking effects estimated from earlier work.

## Key numbers

- The mass formula for antidecuplet members is M = [1890 − Y × 180] MeV.
- The exotic Z⁺ (S=+1) is predicted to have mass around 1530 MeV and total width less than 15 MeV.
- The ratio v₂/(v₁ − v₂/2) = −0.3 ± 0.08 from chiral quark soliton model calculations.
- The ratio v₃/(v₁ − v₂/2) = −0.22 ± 0.07 from chiral quark soliton model calculations.
- The octet-antidecuplet dipole magnetic transition for the nucleon-like member (identified with P₁₁(1710)) is μ_{pp*} = −0.15 to 0.15 nuclear magnetons for the proton target.
- The octet-antidecuplet dipole magnetic transition for the neutron target is μ_{nn*} = −1 to −0.3 nuclear magnetons.
- The ratio |μ_{nn*}/μ_{pp*}| > 2 for the octet-antidecuplet transition, compared to ∼ −2/3 for the octet-octet transition.

## Context

This work extends the soliton classification of baryons proposed at the ITEP Winter School (1984) and developed by Diakonov, Petrov, Polyakov (1997), which identified the P₁₁(1710) resonance as the nucleon-like member of the antidecuplet and predicted the anomalously narrow exotic Z⁺ pentaquark. The paper provides a qualitative experimental test for identifying antidecuplet members through photoproduction, showing that photoexcitation from neutrons should dominate over protons, and that the couplings vanish in the nonrelativistic quark model limit, making antidecuplet baryons a probe of relativistic quark interactions. The results enable targeted experimental searches at facilities like SPring-8, JLab, ELSA, MAMI, and GRAAL, particularly in reactions such as γ n → K⁺ Σ⁻, γ n → η n, and γ n → (ππ)_{I=1} N.
