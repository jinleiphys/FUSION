# Effective-Range Dependence of Resonantly Interacting Fermions

> We extract the leading effective range corrections to the equation of state of the unitary Fermi gas from ab initio fixed-node quantum Monte Carlo (FNQMC) calculations in a periodic box using a density functional theory (DFT), and show them to be universal by considering several two-body interactions. Furthermore, we find that the DFT is consistent with the best available unbiased QMC calculations, analytic results, and experimental measurements of the equation of state. We also discuss the asymptotic effective-range corrections for trapped systems and present the first QMC results with the correct asymptotic scaling.

## Key claim

The leading effective-range correction to the equation of state of the unitary Fermi gas is universal, with the coefficient \(\zeta_e = 0.127(4)\) in the thermodynamic limit, extracted by fitting fixed-node quantum Monte Carlo results directly with a finite-range density functional theory rather than first extrapolating each particle number to zero range.

## Method

The authors performed fixed-node quantum Monte Carlo (FNQMC) simulations for systems of 4 to 130 fermions in a periodic box, using three different two-body potentials (modified-Pöschl-Teller, double-Gaussian, and double-exponential) all tuned to infinite scattering length, with effective ranges \(k_F r_e \lesssim 0.3\). They directly fitted the QMC energies with a finite-range extension of the superfluid local density approximation (SLDA) that parametrizes the effective-range dependence of the three dimensionless parameters \(\alpha\), \(\xi\), and \(\eta\) as quadratic functions of \(k_F r_e\), allowing simultaneous consistency over all particle numbers and ranges.

## Key numbers

* Bertsch parameter at zero range from FNQMC extrapolation: \(\xi^* = 0.3897(4)\) (thermodynamic limit, upper bound)
* Leading effective-range coefficient from FNQMC: \(\zeta_e^* = 0.127(4)\) (average of \(V_{PT}\) and \(V_{2G}\) results)
* Best fit to unbiased zero-range results (exact diagonalizations, auxiliary-field QMC, and experimental data): \(\xi_S = 0.3742 \pm 0.0005\), \(\alpha = 1.104 \pm 0.008\), \(\eta = 0.651 \pm 0.009\)
* For \(N_+ = 66\) particles, quadratic fits gave \(\xi_{66} = 0.3898(4)\) (PT), \(0.3885(4)\) (2G), \(0.3902(5)\) (2E) and \(\zeta_e(66) = 0.14(1)\), \(0.14(1)\), \(0.12(1)\) respectively
* Experimental effective range in \(^6\)Li: \(r_e \approx 4.7\) nm at densities where \(k_F r_e \approx 0.01\)

## Context

This work extends the zero-range SLDA density functional theory developed by Bulgac (2007) and the finite-size analysis of Forbes, Gandolfi, and Gezerlis (2010) by incorporating effective-range dependence directly into the functional, avoiding the need for separate zero-range extrapolations per particle number. The universal coefficient \(\zeta_e\) was previously estimated by Bhattacharyya (2006) as 0.046(7) from two-particle trap solutions and by Carlson (2011) as 0.11(3) for 66 particles using auxiliary-field QMC. The results enable direct use of finite-range QMC data to extract thermodynamic properties of neutron matter, where \(k_F r_e \approx 3\) is much larger than in cold-atom systems.
