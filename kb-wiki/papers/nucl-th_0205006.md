# Asymptotic Behavior of the Wave Packet Propagation through a Barrier: the Green's Function Approach Revisited

> To model the decay of a quasibound state we use the modified two-potential approach introduced by Gurvitz and Kalbermann. This method has proved itself useful in the past for calculating the decay width and the energy shift of an isolated quasistationary state. We follow the same approach in order to propagate the wave-packet in time with the ultimate goal of extracting the momentum-distribution of emitted particles. The advantage of the method is that it provides the time-dependent wave function in a simple semi-analytic form. We intend to apply this method to the modeling of metastable states for which no direct integration of the time-dependent Schroedinger equation is available today.

## Key claim

The modified two-potential approach (TPA) of Gurvitz and Kalbermann can be extended to provide a semi-analytic expression for the time-dependent wave function of a particle tunneling through a spherically-symmetric barrier, enabling extraction of the momentum distribution of emitted particles without direct numerical integration of the time-dependent Schroedinger equation. The derivation assumes a short-range potential that vanishes at large distances and that the initial bound state of the auxiliary Hamiltonian is well isolated from other bound states with the same quantum numbers.

## Method

The authors decompose the full potential V(r) into an auxiliary potential U(r) that produces a bound state at energy E0 and a perturbative "closing" potential W(r). They expand the time-dependent wave function in the basis of the bound state and continuum states of the auxiliary Hamiltonian, then apply Laplace transforms to obtain integral equations for the expansion coefficients. The resulting expressions are evaluated using the residue theorem, with the Green's function approximated by neglecting the projection operator on the bound state and replacing V(r) with the closing potential W(r). The final integral is expressed in closed form using the Moshinsky function after applying the Mittag-Leffler theorem to obtain a pole expansion of the S-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The decay width and energy shift of the quasistationary state are given by epsilon_0 = Delta - i Gamma/2, where epsilon_0 = - sqrt(pi/2) * |phi_0(R)|^2 / (2m k_0) * [alpha chi_{l k_0}(R) + chi_{l k_0}'(R)] * [alpha chi^{(+)}_{l k_0}(R) + chi^{(+)}_{l k_0}'(R)], with alpha = sqrt(2m(V_0 - E_0)) and k_0 = sqrt(2m(E_0 + epsilon_0))
- The time-dependent radial wave function for r < R from the pole of the Laplace-transformed coefficient is psi_{II,a}(r<R, t) = sqrt(pi/2) * phi_0(R)/(k_0 r) * [alpha chi_{l k_0}^{(+)}(R) + chi_{l k_0}^{(+)}'(R)] * chi_{l k_0}(r) * exp(-i (E_0 + epsilon_0) t)
- For r > R, the contribution from psi_I is exactly canceled by the first term in the expression for psi_{II,a}, leaving psi_{II,a}(r>R, t) = sqrt(pi/2) * phi_0(R)/(k_0 r) * [alpha chi_{l k_0}(R) + chi_{l k_0}'(R)] * chi_{l k_0}^{(+)}(r) * exp(-i (E_0 + epsilon_0) t)

## Context

This work extends the two-potential approximation originally introduced by Gurvitz and Kalbermann (1987, 1988) for calculating decay widths and energy shifts of isolated quasistationary states, which was previously applied to nuclear physics problems by Aberg, Semmes, and Nazarewicz (1997). The method provides a semi-analytic framework for time-dependent wave packet propagation that avoids direct numerical integration of the time-dependent Schroedinger equation, which the authors note is not available for many metastable states. The derivation uses the Mittag-Leffler theorem and Moshinsky functions following the approach of van Dijk and Nogami (1999), and the results are expressed in terms of the S-matrix poles in the complex k-plane as described by Baz, Zeldovich, and Perelomov (1969) and Newton (1966).
