# Field Theory of the Fermi Function

> The Fermi function $F(Z,E)$ accounts for QED corrections to beta decays that are enhanced at either small electron velocity $β$ or large nuclear charge $Z$. For precision applications, the Fermi function must be combined with other radiative corrections and with scale- and scheme-dependent hadronic matrix elements. We formulate the Fermi function as a field theory object and present a new factorization formula for QED radiative corrections to beta decays. We provide new results for the anomalous dimension of the corresponding effective operator complete through three loops, and resum perturbative logarithms and $π$-enhancements with renormalization group methods. Our results are important for tests of fundamental physics with precision beta decay and related processes.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates the Fermi function as a field theory object in effective field theory, providing a new factorization formula for QED radiative corrections to beta decays. The authors claim that the existing heuristic estimate for order \(Z^2\alpha^3\) corrections is incorrect, with shifts ranging from \(1.1\times 10^{-4}\) to \(1.4\times 10^{-3}\) for the nine most precisely measured superallowed transitions, which is an order of magnitude larger than the previously estimated uncertainty on the outer radiative correction.

## Method

The authors formulate the Fermi function as a field theory object using effective field theory (EFT) techniques, separating the beta decay process into soft, hard, and UV components via a factorization theorem. They compute the hard function to all orders in \(Z\alpha\) using dimensional regularization with \(D=3-2\epsilon\) dimensions, derive the anomalous dimension of the effective operator through three loops, and resum perturbative logarithms and \(\pi\)-enhancements using renormalization group methods. The analysis covers three regimes: large \(Z\) asymptotics, intermediate \(Z\) (relevant for superallowed decays), and neutron beta decay (\(Z=0\)).

## Key numbers

* The anomalous dimension through three-loop order at arbitrary \(Z\) is given by \(\gamma_{\cal O} = {\alpha \over 4\pi} \gamma_0^{(1)} + \left({\alpha \over 4\pi}\right)^2 \left[ -8\pi^2 Z(Z+1) + \gamma_1^{(2)} \right] + \left({\alpha \over 4\pi}\right)^3 \left[ 16 \pi^2 \, Z(Z+1) \left( 6 - {\pi^2\over 3}\right) + \gamma_2^{(3)} \right]\), where \(\gamma_0^{(1)}=-3\), \(\gamma_1^{(2)}= -16 \zeta_2 + \frac52 + \frac{10}{3} n_e\), and \(\gamma_2^{(3)}\) is given explicitly with \(n_e=1\) for one dynamical charged lepton.

* For the 9 transitions with smallest \({\cal F}t\) uncertainty (at or below permille level), shifts from the corrected coefficient range from \(1.1\times 10^{-4}\) for \(^{14}{\rm O}\) to \(1.4 \times 10^{-3}\) for \(^{54}{\rm Co}\).

* The CKM unitarity discrepancy is \(|V_{ud}|^2 + |V_{us}|^2 + |V_{ub}|^2 - 1 = -0.0015(6)\) from Hardy (2020).

* The leading series involving \((Z\alpha)^n\) sums to \(\gamma^{(0)} = \sqrt{1-(Z\alpha)^2} - 1\).

* The \(\pi\)-enhanced resummation for neutron beta decay gives \(| {\cal M}_H(\mu_{S+}^2)|^2 = \exp\big[{ \pi \alpha \over \beta } \big] | {\cal M}_H(\mu_{S-}^2) |^2\).

## Context

This work extends the classic Fermi function formalism of Fermi (1934) and the radiative correction framework of Sirlin (1967) and Sirlin and Zucchini (1986), providing a rigorous EFT treatment that resolves ambiguities in the scale \(r^{-1}\) appearing in the traditional Fermi function. The authors find that the heuristic estimate of Sirlin and Zucchini (1986) for order \(Z^2\alpha^3\) corrections is incorrect, disagreeing with Jaus (1986) which did not include the full set of relevant diagrams. The results enable systematic inclusion of higher-order QED corrections for precision beta decay experiments, with implications for CKM unitarity tests and new physics constraints from superallowed nuclear beta decays and neutron beta decay.
