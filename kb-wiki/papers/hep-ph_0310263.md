# Diffusion and local deconfinement in relativistic systems

> In relativistic systems at RHIC energies, new deconfinement signatures emerge and indicate an increasingly clear separation between soft hadronic processes, and hard partonic interactions in a locally deconfined subsystem. Here the emphasis is on longitudinal variables, namely, net-baryon rapidity distributions. As described in a Relativistic Diffusion Model, they change from bell-shaped at the lower to double-humped at the higher SPS-energy, but do not reach local statistical equilibrium. At sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV in the Au-Au system, however, they are shown to consist of three components. In addition to the nonequilibrium contributions, a third fraction close to midrapidity containing Z_eq = 22 protons reaches local statistical equilibrium in a discontinuous transition. It may be associated with a deconfinement of the participant partons and thus, serve as a signature for Quark-Gluon Matter formation.

## Key claim

At sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV in central Au-Au collisions, net-baryon rapidity distributions show a third component near midrapidity that reaches local statistical equilibrium in a discontinuous transition, which may serve as a signature for Quark-Gluon Matter formation; this is a stronger claim than the abstract suggests because the discontinuity is presented as a key indicator for deconfinement, not merely a possibility.

## Method

The author applies a Relativistic Diffusion Model (RDM) based on a Fokker-Planck equation in rapidity space with linear drift and constant diffusion coefficient, using delta-function initial conditions at the beam rapidities. For the Au-Au system at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV, the net-proton rapidity distribution is decomposed into a superposition of two nonequilibrium Gaussian components from soft hadronic processes and a third local equilibrium component at midrapidity from hard partonic interactions. The diffusion coefficient is calculated from a dissipation-fluctuation theorem in the weak-coupling limit, then enhanced by a factor g(sqrt(s)) to account for strong-coupling memory and collective effects.

## Key numbers

- Net-proton rapidity density at midrapidity in central Au-Au at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV: dN/dy = 7.1 +/- 0.7 (stat.) +/- 1.1 (sys.) from BRAHMS (Lee 2002)
- A fraction of Z_eq = 22 net protons near midrapidity reaches local statistical equilibrium
- Weak-coupling rapidity width coefficient from dissipation-fluctuation theorem: sqrt(D_y tau_y) = 7.6 x 10^-2 at T = 170 MeV
- Enhancement factor for the weak-coupling rapidity width coefficient due to collective and memory effects: g(sqrt(s)) = 3.7 at RHIC energies
- Nonextensivity parameter q = 1.4 for the nonlinear model at RHIC, with mean transverse mass <m_perp> = 1.2 GeV and T = 170 MeV
- At SPS energies (sqrt(sNN) = 17.3 GeV), the enhancement factor is g(sqrt(s)) = 2.6 and the nonextensivity parameter q = 1.25 for Pb-Pb
- For produced pions in Pb-Pb at SPS energies, q = 1.038 from transverse mass spectra
- Beam rapidity at p = 100 GeV/c per nucleon: y_b = +/- 5.36
- Total number of net protons for central Au-Au: 158

## Context

This work extends the Relativistic Diffusion Model previously applied to AGS and SPS data (Wolschin 1996, 1999) to RHIC energies, where a clear separation between nonequilibrium and equilibrium components becomes possible. The finding of a discontinuous transition to local equilibrium at midrapidity contrasts with the continuous evolution seen at lower energies and with the results of numerical microscopic models that struggle to reproduce the central valley. The interpretation builds on earlier deconfinement signatures such as J/Psi suppression at SPS (Matsui and Satz 1986) and jet quenching at RHIC, but offers a new longitudinal observable. The work enables future investigation of baryon stopping mechanisms including the gluon junction mechanism (Rossi and Veneziano 1980) and nonperturbative QCD thermodynamics, and suggests that similar signatures may be observable at LHC energies.
