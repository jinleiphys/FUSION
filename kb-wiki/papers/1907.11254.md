# A renormalized approach to neutrinoless double-beta decay

> The process at the heart of neutrinoless double-beta decay, $nn \rightarrow p p\, e^- e^-$ induced by a light Majorana neutrino, is investigated in pionless and chiral effective field theory. We show in various regularization schemes the need to introduce a short-range lepton-number-violating operator at leading order, confirming earlier findings. We demonstrate that such a short-range operator is only needed in spin-singlet $S$-wave transitions, while leading-order transitions involving higher partial waves depend solely on long-range currents. Calculations are extended to include next-to-leading corrections in perturbation theory, where to this order no additional undetermined parameters appear. We establish a connection based on chiral symmetry between neutrinoless double-beta decay and nuclear charge-independence breaking induced by electromagnetism. Data on the latter confirm the need for a leading-order short-range operator, but do not allow for a full determination of the corresponding lepton-number-violating coupling. Using a crude estimate of this coupling, we perform ab initio calculations of the matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay for $^6$He and $^{12}$Be. We speculate on the phenomenological impact of the leading short-range operator on the basis of these results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the leading-order (LO) neutrino potential for neutrinoless double-beta decay ($0\nu\beta\beta$) requires a short-range lepton-number-violating (LNV) contact operator in the $^1S_0$ channel to achieve regulator-independent amplitudes, contradicting the naive expectation that such an operator appears only at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in chiral effective field theory.

## Method

The authors analyze the $nn \rightarrow pp\, e^- e^-$ scattering amplitude induced by light Majorana-neutrino exchange using both pionless effective field theory ($\slashpi$EFT) and chiral effective field theory ($\chi$EFT). They compute the matrix element of the long-range neutrino potential between $^1S_0$ two-nucleon wavefunctions obtained from solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with LO $\chi$EFT potentials, varying the coordinate-space cutoff $R_S$ to study regulator dependence. The analysis is extended to higher partial waves ($^3P_J$, $^1D_2$) and to next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbation theory, and a chiral-symmetry-based connection is established between the $0\nu\beta\beta$ short-range operator and charge-independence breaking (CIB) in nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Key numbers

- The $^1S_0$ $NN$ scattering length is $a = -23.714$ fm, used to determine the strong-interaction low-energy constant $C(R_S)$.
- The effective range in the $^1S_0$ channel is $r_0 = 2.7$ fm, which enters at NLO in $\slashpi$EFT.
- The nucleon axial coupling is $g_A = 1.27$, appearing in the long-range neutrino potential.
- The pion mass is $m_\pi \simeq 140$ MeV and the pion decay constant is $F_\pi \simeq 92.2$ MeV, defining the chiral breakdown scale $\Lambda_\chi \sim 4\pi F_\pi$.
- The $0\nu\beta\beta$ amplitude $\mathcal{A}_\nu(E)$ shows a logarithmic cutoff dependence $\propto \ln R_S$ for small $R_S$, fitted as $\mathcal{A}_\nu = a + b\ln R_S$.
- The $^1S_0$ neutrino potential projected onto the $^1S_0 \rightarrow {}^1S_0$ channel is $V_{\nu\, \rm L}^{^1S_0}(\vec q) = \tau^{(1)+}\tau^{(2)+} / \vec q^2 \left[1 + 2g_A^2 + g_A^2 m_\pi^4 / (\vec q^2 + m_\pi^2)^2\right]$.
- The $\slashpi$EFT LECs obey renormalization-group equations: $C = (4\pi/m_N) / (1/a - \mu)$ and $C_2 = (2\pi/m_N) r_0 / (1/a - \mu)^2$ in the PDS scheme.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Cirigliano et al. (2017, 2018) that a short-range LNV operator is required at LO in $\slashpi$EFT and $\chi$EFT, confirming the need for such an operator in multiple regularization schemes. The paper establishes a novel connection between $0\nu\beta\beta$ and charge-independence breaking in $NN$ scattering (e.g., from Argonne $v_{18}$ and CD-Bonn potentials), showing that CIB data confirm the need for a LO short-range operator but cannot fully determine the corresponding LNV coupling. This enables future lattice QCD calculations (e.g., Shanahan et al., 2017; Tiburzi et al., 2017) to determine the short-range coupling, and provides a framework for ab initio calculations of $0\nu\beta\beta$ matrix elements in light nuclei such as $^6$He and $^{12}$Be.
