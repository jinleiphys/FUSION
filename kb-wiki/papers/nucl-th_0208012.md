# The Origin of Large-p_T pi^0 Suppression at RHIC

> We present results for inclusive $π^0$ production in proton-proton and in Au-Au at RHIC energy $\sqrt s=200$ GeV. We use next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculation and we include nuclear effects such as parton energy loss and nuclear shadowing. We consider the ratio of $π^0$ distribution in Au-Au and p-p collisions for $p_T > 3$ GeV for three cases of parton energy loss: 1) constant parton energy loss per parton scattering, $ε^a_n=const$, 2) Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal energy-dependent energy loss, $ε^a_n \sim \sqrt E^a_n$ and 3) Bethe-Heitler energy-dependent energy loss, $ε^a_n \sim E^a_n$. We show that recently observed suppression of $π^0$ production in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, which is found to increase with $p_T$ increasing from 3GeV to 8GeV, can be reproduced by $ε^a_n=0.06 E^a_n$. We show that the ratio of prompt photons to neutral pions produced in Au-Au collisions at RHIC has a strong $p_T$ dependence approaching one at $p_T\sim 10$GeV.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of $\pi^0$ production in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, which increases with $p_T$ from 3 GeV to 8 GeV, can be reproduced by a Bethe-Heitler-type energy-dependent parton energy loss where each parton loses a constant fraction (6%) of its energy per scattering, rather than by constant or Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal energy loss forms.

## Method

The authors used next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations for inclusive $\pi^0$ and prompt photon production in proton-proton and Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt s = 200$ GeV, incorporating nuclear shadowing via the EKS98 parameterization and medium-induced parton energy loss by modifying the final-state fragmentation functions. They considered three forms of parton energy loss: constant energy loss per scattering, LPM energy-dependent loss ($\epsilon^a_n \sim \sqrt{E^a_n}$), and Bethe-Heitler energy-dependent loss ($\epsilon^a_n \sim E^a_n$), and compared the resulting ratio of Au-Au to p-p cross sections with PHENIX data.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of $\pi^0$ production in Au-Au to p-p collisions at $\sqrt s = 200$ GeV increases from 76% at $p_T = 3$ GeV to 84% at $p_T = 8$ GeV when using $\epsilon^a_n = 0.06 E^a_n$ (Bethe-Heitler type), in agreement with PHENIX data.
- For constant energy loss $\epsilon^a_n = 0.3$ GeV, the ratio increases with $p_T$; for LPM energy loss with $E_{LPM} = 0.35$ GeV, the ratio also increases with $p_T$; for Bethe-Heitler with $\kappa = 10\%$, the ratio decreases from about 50% at $p_T = 3$ GeV to 35% at $p_T = 12$ GeV.
- Nuclear shadowing effects are small, contributing less than 10% to the suppression.
- The ratio of prompt photons to $\pi^0$ in Au-Au collisions approaches 1 at $p_T \sim 10$ GeV.
- Prompt photon suppression in Au-Au relative to p-p decreases from 40% at $p_T = 3$ GeV to 15% at $p_T = 12$ GeV, while $\pi^0$ suppression increases from 76% to 88% over the same $p_T$ range.
- Bremsstrahlung processes contribute 24% to the prompt photon cross section at $p_T = 3$ GeV and 6% at $p_T = 12$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends NLO pQCD calculations of $\pi^0$ production (Aurenche et al.) to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, addressing the unexpected $p_T$ dependence of the suppression observed by PHENIX (2002). The authors show that a simple Bethe-Heitler energy loss model reproduces the data, whereas constant or LPM energy loss forms (previously expected from theoretical work by Wang, Gyulassy, and others) do not match the observed trend. The results enable predictions for prompt photon production and the photon-to-pion ratio, which is important for separating direct photon signals from hadronic backgrounds in searches for quark-gluon plasma formation.
