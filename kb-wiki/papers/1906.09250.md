# Quantum chromodynamics axion in a hot and magnetized medium

> It is analyzed the effects of a hot and magnetized medium on the axion mass, self-coupling and topological susceptibility when in presence of an anisotropic external magnetic field along the $z$-direction, within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio effective model for quantum chromodynamics. The effects of both Magnetic Catalysis and Inverse Magnetic Catalysis are explicitly taken into account through appropriate matching of parameters with those from lattice Monte-Carlo numerical simulations. It is also analyzed the dependence of the results with respect to different model parametrizations in the context of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model.

## Key claim

The axion mass, self-coupling, and topological susceptibility are computed in a hot and magnetized medium within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective model, explicitly incorporating both Magnetic Catalysis (MC) and Inverse Magnetic Catalysis (IMC) effects through parameter matching to lattice QCD data, with results showing significant dependence on the choice of model parametrization and the value of the coupling parameter \(c\) that controls the axion interaction strength.

## Method

The thermodynamic potential for the QCD axion was derived within a two-flavor NJL model including the 't Hooft determinant interaction term, using mean-field approximation with scalar and pseudoscalar condensates. An external anisotropic magnetic field along the \(z\)-direction was incorporated via Landau level quantization of quark momenta, and the thermodynamic potential was separated into vacuum, magnetic field-dependent, and medium parts using the Magnetic Field Independent Regularization (MFIR) procedure. The axion mass and self-coupling were obtained from the second and fourth derivatives of the effective potential at vanishing axion field, respectively, and the topological susceptibility was extracted from the axion mass relation.

## Key numbers

- Axion mass ratio \(m_a(T)/m_a(0)\) at vanishing magnetic field shows a sharp drop near the pseudo-critical temperature \(T_c \sim 170\) MeV, with the exact behavior depending on the parameter set (Set I, II, or III from Table 1).
- The critical value of the coupling parameter \(c_{\rm min}\) for the onset of spontaneous CP violation at \(a/f_a = \pi\) ranges from approximately 0.1 to 0.4 depending on temperature and magnetic field strength, as shown in the \(T-c_{\rm min}\) phase diagram for \(eB = 0, 0.2, 0.4\) GeV\(^2\).
- The fitting parameters for the IMC coupling \(G_s(eB,T)\) at \(eB = 0.2\) GeV are \(b = 1.226\) GeV, \(T_a = 0.168\) GeV, \(s = 3.262\) GeV, \(\beta = 34.117\) GeV; at \(eB = 0.4\) GeV: \(b = 1.769\) GeV, \(T_a = 0.169\) GeV, \(s = 2.294\) GeV, \(\beta = 22.988\) GeV.
- The zero-temperature magnetic field dependent coupling \(G_0(eB)\) is fitted as \(G_0(eB) = \alpha + \beta e^{-\gamma (eB)^2}\) with \(\alpha = 1.44373\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\beta = 3.06\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\gamma = 1.31\) GeV\(^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD axion properties in hot medium by Lu et al. (2018) to include external magnetic fields, and contrasts with the dilute instanton gas approximation which is unreliable near the chiral transition temperature. The results enable predictions for axion phenomenology in early universe cosmology and neutron star environments, and the parametrization dependence quantified here provides guidance for future lattice QCD comparisons and experimental searches for axions.
