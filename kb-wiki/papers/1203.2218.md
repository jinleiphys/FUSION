# Helium nuclei around the neutron drip line

> Neutron rich He nuclei have been investigated using relativistic mean field approach in co-ordinate space. Elastic partial scattering cross sections for proton scattering in inverse kinematics have been calculated using the theoretically obtained density for $^{6,8}$He and compared with experiment. The energies of the low-lying resonance states in the neutron unstable nuclei $^{5,7}$He have also been calculated and compared with experimental observations.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field (RMF) approach in coordinate space, including the effect of the width of resonant continuum states, can reasonably describe the structure of neutron-rich He nuclei near the drip line, but the predicted neutron radii and resonance energies depend significantly on the chosen Lagrangian parametrization (NLSH, FSU Gold, NL3, NL2), with FSU Gold providing the best overall agreement for resonance energies in the odd isotopes.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations in coordinate space with a BCS pairing approximation, using a mesh size of 0.04 fm and a nuclear interaction cutoff at 14 fm. For the even-even isotopes 6,8He, they calculated ground state binding energies, proton and neutron densities, and root-mean-square radii using four different Lagrangian parametrizations (NLSH, FSU Gold, NL3, NL2). They then computed elastic partial scattering cross sections for proton scattering in inverse kinematics by folding the theoretical densities with two effective interactions (DDM3Y and JLM) in the optical model. For the odd neutron-unstable isotopes 5,7He, they calculated the energies of low-lying single-particle resonance states (3/2- and 1/2-) in the tagged approximation, taking into account the widths of positive energy levels.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for 6He: experimental 4.878 MeV; FSU Gold gives 5.507 MeV, NL2 gives 5.161 MeV.
- Binding energy per nucleon for 8He: experimental 3.926 MeV; FSU Gold gives 4.222 MeV, NL2 gives 3.765 MeV.
- Proton radius for 6He: experimental 2.068(11) fm; FSU Gold gives 1.88 fm, NL2 gives 1.89 fm.
- Neutron radius for 6He: FSU Gold gives 3.12 fm, NL2 gives 3.63 fm.
- RMS matter radius for 6He: experimental 2.48(3) fm; FSU Gold gives 2.76 fm, NL2 gives 3.16 fm.
- RMS matter radius for 8He: experimental 2.52(3) fm; FSU Gold gives 2.81 fm, NL2 gives 3.33 fm.
- For 5He, the 3/2- resonance is calculated at 0.92 MeV (experimental value around 0.798 MeV from Tilley et al. or 0.741(4) MeV from Aksyutina et al.); the 1/2- resonance is calculated at 4.47 MeV.
- For 7He, the 3/2- resonance is calculated at 0.63 MeV (experimental value approximately 0.45 MeV from Wuosmaa et al. or 0.388(2) MeV from Aksyutina et al.); the 1/2- resonance is calculated at 2.09 MeV resonance energy (1.46 MeV excitation energy).
- Including the width of the resonant νp1/2 level in 6He increases the neutron radius by 0.5%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF+BCS coordinate-space study of exotic even-even Be and C nuclei (Bhattacharya et al., earlier work) to the He isotopic chain near the neutron drip line. The calculated densities are tested against elastic proton scattering data from Kiselev et al., Tanihata et al., and others, providing a model-dependent alternative to direct electron scattering measurements. The resonance energy calculations for 5,7He are compared with experimental results from Tilley et al., Aksyutina et al., Wuosmaa et al., and Ryezayeva et al., as well as with continuum shell model calculations by Volya and Zelevensky and by Halderson; the results highlight the need for further experimental clarification of the 1/2- resonance in 7He.
