# Baryons as Relativistic Bound States of Quark and Diquark

> In this thesis a model framework for describing baryons as diquark-quark bound states is presented which is formulated in an explicitly covariant manner. The relativistic bound state problem for three quarks is considered. Diquarks are introduced as separable correlations in the two-quark correlation function and thereby baryons are described by a Bethe-Salpeter equation for bound states of quark and diquark which interact by quark exchange. In analogy to the meson spectrum, scalar and axialvector diquarks are considered to be the most important two-quark configurations within baryons. The numerical solutions of the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation are subsequently employed in the calculation of electromagnetic, strong and axial form factors of the nucleons. The construction of the electromagnetic current operator in the diquark-quark model respects gauge invariance. Invariance under chiral symmetry transformations is slightly violated for vector diquarks have been neglected, leading to a modest violation of the Goldberger-Treiman relation. The possibility of an effective parametrization of confinement by suitable modifications of the quark and diquark propagators is investigated. It is shown that the modelling of confinement chosen here is suitable for the calculation of the octet and devuplet spectrum and spacelike nucleon properties, but its applicability breaks down for processes where large energies are transferred to the nucleon as it is the case in certain meson production processes. Model solutions for nucleon wave functions and observables are compared between the full relativistic treatment and a widely used semi-relativistic approximation. The considerable deviations in the results illustrate the inadequacy of employing the semi-relativistic treatment.

## Key claim

This thesis presents a fully covariant framework for describing baryons as bound states of a quark and a diquark, where the diquark is introduced as a separable two-quark correlation. The approach is intended to bridge the gap between soft and high-energy hadronic physics, but the author acknowledges that the model's applicability breaks down for processes involving large energy transfers, such as certain meson production reactions.

## Method

The three-quark bound state problem is reduced to an effective two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation by introducing diquarks as separable correlations in the two-quark correlation function. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-diquark bound states is solved without any non-relativistic or semi-relativistic reduction, using massive constituent quarks with free-fermion propagators. The resulting nucleon wave functions are then employed to calculate electromagnetic, strong (pion-nucleon), and axial form factors, with the electromagnetic current operator constructed to respect gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: approximately 0.3 GeV, as used in the harmonic oscillator potential of non-relativistic quark models
- Lowest-lying energy level for a massless quark in a spherical static bag: approximately 400 MeV for a bag radius of 1 fm
- Static baryon observables in soliton models are accurate only at the 20-30% level in leading order of N_C
- For the N-Delta mass splitting in bag models, the QCD fine structure constant alpha_s must be greater than 2 to account for the mass difference, questioning the validity of perturbative treatment

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev bound state approach to relativistic three-quark systems, contrasting with non-relativistic constituent quark models (Faiman 1968, Isgur 1978), soliton models (Skyrme 1961, Holzwarth 1986), and bag models (Hasenfratz 1978). The key advantage claimed over these approaches is manifest covariance, which the author argues is lost in semi-relativistic potential models and limits the calculation of dynamical observables. The framework enables calculation of nucleon electromagnetic, axial, and pion-nucleon form factors within a single covariant formalism, though the author notes that chiral symmetry is slightly violated because vector diquarks were neglected, leading to a modest violation of the Goldberger-Treiman relation.
