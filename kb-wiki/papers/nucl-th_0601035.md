# Inelastic form factors to alpha particle condensate states in 12C and 16O: what can we learn ?

> In order to discuss the spatial extention of the second 0+ state of 12C (Hoyle state), we analyze the inelastic form factor of electron scattering to the Hoyle state, which our 3 alpha condensate wave function reproduces very well like previous 3 alpha RGM/GCM models. The analysis is made by varying the size of the Hoyle state artificially. As a result, we find that only the maximum value of the form factor sensitively depends on its size, while the positions of maximum and minimum are almost unchanged. This size dependence is found to come from a characteristic feature of the transition density from the ground state to the Hoyle state. We further show the theoretical predictions of the inelastic form factor to the second 2+ state of 12C, which was recently observed above the Hoyle state, and of the inelastic form factor to the calculated third 0+ state of 16O, which was conjectured to correspond to the 4 alpha condensed state in previous theoretical work by the present authors.

## Key claim

The inelastic form factor for electron scattering to the Hoyle state (0₂⁺ state of ¹²C) is sensitive in its maximum value to the spatial size of that state, while the positions of maximum and minimum are almost unchanged; this size dependence arises from a characteristic feature of the transition density from the ground state to the Hoyle state.

## Method

The authors solved the Hill-Wheeler equation for 3α condensate wave functions to obtain the ground and Hoyle states of ¹²C, then computed the inelastic form factor via Fourier transformation of the transition density. They artificially varied the size parameter β of the Hoyle state wave function Ψ⊥(β) and calculated the resulting form factors, analyzing the sensitivity of the form factor's maximum height to the root-mean-square (r.m.s.) radius. Predictions were also made for the 2₂⁺ state of ¹²C and the 0₃⁺ state of ¹⁶O using the same α-condensate framework.

## Key numbers

- Elastic form factor |F(q)₀₁⁺→₀₁⁺| at q = 0.25 fm⁻¹: 9.4 × 10⁻¹; at q = 1.00 fm⁻¹: 3.3 × 10⁻¹; at q = 2.00 fm⁻¹: 0.15 × 10⁻¹ (for ¹²C)
- Inelastic form factor |F(q)₀₁⁺→₀₂⁺| at q = 0.25 fm⁻¹: 0.98 × 10⁻²; at q = 1.00 fm⁻¹: 5.5 × 10⁻²; at q = 2.00 fm⁻¹: 0.069 × 10⁻² (for ¹²C Hoyle state)
- The squared overlap between the full Hill-Wheeler Hoyle state Ψλ=2^(J=0) and the projected wave function Ψ⊥(β₁) is 99.2% at β₁ = (βx=βy, βz) = (5.27 fm, 1.37 fm)
- The r.m.s. radius R₀ of the Hoyle state corresponding to Ψ⊥(β₁) is 3.78 fm
- The 2₂⁺ state in ¹²C was observed at 2.6 ± 0.3 MeV above the three-alpha threshold
- The calculated 0₃⁺ state in ¹⁶O (the 4α condensate candidate) lies at E = 14.1 MeV
- A candidate 4α condensate state was observed at 13.5 MeV with an alpha decay width of 0.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Funaki et al., 2005; Tohsaki et al., 2001) on α-particle condensate states in light nuclei, where the Hoyle state was interpreted as a dilute 3α Bose condensate. The inelastic form factor calculations agree closely with earlier resonating group method (RGM) results by Kamimura et al. (1981). The analysis shows that measuring the inelastic form factor can determine the radius of α-condensate states, and the predictions for ¹⁶O provide a testable signature for the 4α condensate state conjectured in prior theoretical work by the same authors.
