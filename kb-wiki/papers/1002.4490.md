# Symmetry preserving regularization with a cutoff

> A Lorentz and gauge symmetry preserving regularization method is proposed in 4 dimension based on momentum cutoff. We use the conditions of gauge invariance or freedom of shift of the loop-momentum to define the evaluation of the terms carrying Lorentz indices, e.g. proportional to k_μk_ν. The remaining scalar integrals are calculated with a four dimensional momentum cutoff. The finite terms (independent of the cutoff) are unambiguous and agree with the result of dimensional regularization.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a four-dimensional momentum cutoff regularization method that preserves Lorentz and gauge symmetries by using consistency conditions derived from gauge invariance or freedom of loop-momentum shifting to define the evaluation of terms carrying Lorentz indices (such as \(k_{\mu}k_{\nu}\)), rather than relying on dimensional regularization; the finite terms are unambiguous and agree with dimensional regularization, but the method works directly in four dimensions.

## Method

The authors start with the standard Wick rotation, Feynman parameterization, and loop-momentum shift, but replace the naive substitution \(k_{\mu}k_{\nu} \rightarrow \frac{1}{4}g_{\mu\nu}k^{2}\) with a set of symmetry-preserving identifications: for divergent integrals, \(l_{E\mu}l_{E\nu}/(l_{E}^{2}+\Delta)^{n+1} \rightarrow \frac{1}{2n}g_{\mu\nu}/(l_{E}^{2}+\Delta)^{n}\) (and generalizations for four-index tensors). The remaining scalar integrals are then evaluated with a sharp four-dimensional momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\). The conditions are derived from requiring gauge invariance (the Ward identity) and freedom of loop-momentum shifting, which are shown to be equivalent to vanishing surface terms.

## Key numbers

* Quadratic divergences in the vacuum polarization function cancel only when the \(k_{\mu}k_{\nu}\) term is evaluated with \(d=2\) (instead of \(d=4\)), giving the matching relation \(4\pi\mu^{2}(1/(\epsilon-1)+1) = \Lambda^{2}\).
* Logarithmic divergences match via \(\frac{1}{\epsilon} - \gamma_{E} + \ln(4\pi\mu^{2}) + 1 = \ln\Lambda^{2}\).
* The vacuum polarization function \(\Pi(0)\) for general fermion masses \(m_a, m_b\) is given by \(\Pi(0) = \frac{1}{4}(m_a^2+m_b^2) - \frac{1}{2}(m_a-m_b)^2\ln(\Lambda^2/(m_a m_b)) - \frac{m_a^4+m_b^4-2m_a m_b(m_a^2+m_b^2)}{4(m_a^2-m_b^2)}\ln(m_b^2/m_a^2)\).
* The first derivative \(\Pi'(0)\) is \(\Pi'(0) = -\frac{2}{9} - \frac{4m_a^2 m_b^2 - 3m_a m_b(m_a^2+m_b^2)}{6(m_a^2-m_b^2)^2} + \frac{1}{3}\ln(\Lambda^2/(m_a m_b)) + \frac{(m_a^2+m_b^2)(m_a^4-4m_a^2 m_b^2+m_b^4)+6m_a^3 m_b^3}{6(m_a^2-m_b^2)^3}\ln(m_b^2/m_a^2)\).
* Basic scalar integrals are listed in the appendix, e.g., \(\int d^4k/(i(2\pi)^4) \cdot 1/(k^2-m^2) = -1/(4\pi)^2 (\Lambda^2 - m^2\ln(\Lambda^2/m^2))\) and \(\int d^4k/(i(2\pi)^4) \cdot 1/(k^2-m^2)^2 = 1/(4\pi)^2 (\ln(\Lambda^2/m^2)-1)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to define symmetry-preserving cutoff regularization, including the observation by Veltman (1970s) that quadratic divergences in dimensional regularization can be identified with poles in \(d=2\), and the subsequent matching procedure by Hagiwara et al. (1990s) and Harada and Yamawaki (1990s). The method is closely related to the operator regularization approach of Oleszczuk (1990s) and the implicit regularization of Nemes et al. (1990s), which also use consistency conditions to maintain gauge invariance. The proposed regularization is advantageous for effective field theories, Wilsonian renormalization group applications, supersymmetric theories, and extra-dimensional models where an explicit cutoff is physically meaningful; it has been successfully tested in the Fermion Condensate Model for oblique radiative corrections (S and T parameters) by the same authors.
