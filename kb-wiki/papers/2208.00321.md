# Impact of Symmetry Energy on Sound Speed and Spinodal Decomposition in Dense Neutron-Rich Matter

> Using a meta model for nuclear Equation of State (EOS) with its parameters constrained by astrophysical observations and terrestrial nuclear experiments, we examine effects of nuclear EOS especially its symmetry energy \esym term on the speed of sound squared $C^2_s(ρ)$ and the critical density $ρ_t$ where $C^2_s(ρ_t)$ vanishes (indicating the onset of spinodal decomposition) in both dense neutron-rich nucleonic matter relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the cold $n+p+e+μ$ matter in neutron stars at $β$-equilibrium. Unlike in nucleonic matter with fixed values of the isospin asymmetry $δ$, in neutron stars with a density dependent isospin profile $δ(ρ)$ determined consistently by the $β$ equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, the $C^2_s(ρ)$ almost always show a peak and then vanishes at $ρ_t$. The latter strongly depends on the high-density behavior of \esym if the skewness parameter $J_0$ characterizing the stiffness of high-density symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) EOS is not too far above its currently known most probable value of about $-190$ MeV inferred from recent Bayesian analyses of neutron star observables. Moreover, in the case of having a super-soft \esym that is decreasing with increasing density above about twice the saturation density of nuclear matter, the $ρ_t$ is significantly lower than the density where the \esym vanishes (indicating the onset of isospin-separation instability and pure neutron matter formation) in neutron star cores.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in neutron stars at beta-equilibrium, the squared speed of sound almost always exhibits a peak and then vanishes at a critical density indicating spinodal decomposition, with this vanishing density strongly depending on the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy if the skewness parameter J0 of symmetric nuclear matter is not too far above its most probable value of about -190 MeV.

## Method

The authors use a meta model for the nuclear Equation of State (EOS) where the symmetric nuclear matter EOS and symmetry energy are parameterized via Taylor expansions around saturation density, with parameters constrained by astrophysical observations and terrestrial nuclear experiments. They compute the squared speed of sound in both nucleonic matter with fixed isospin asymmetry (relevant for heavy-ion collisions) and in cold n+p+e+mu matter at beta-equilibrium (relevant for neutron stars), varying the high-density EOS parameters within their current uncertainty ranges while fixing low-density parameters at their most probable values.

## Key numbers

- The most probable value of the skewness parameter J0 characterizing the stiffness of high-density symmetric nuclear matter is about -190 MeV, inferred from recent Bayesian analyses of neutron star observables
- The slope parameter L of nuclear symmetry energy is fixed at 60 +/- 20 MeV, consistent with the chiral EFT prediction of L = 59.8 +/- 4.1 MeV
- The incompressibility K0 of symmetric nuclear matter is fixed at 240 MeV
- The magnitude of symmetry energy at saturation density is fixed at E_sym(rho0) = 31.7 MeV
- The curvature parameter K_sym is around -100 +/- 100 MeV
- The skewness parameter J_sym ranges from about -200 to +800 MeV based on theoretical predictions
- The most probable value of J0 from Bayesian analysis of nuclear collective flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is J0 = -180 (+100, -110) MeV at 68% confidence level
- To support a neutron star of mass 2.17 solar masses, a minimum value of about J0 = -180 MeV is needed

## Context

This work extends studies of spinodal decomposition in neutron-rich matter, which at low densities is used to identify the crust-core transition in neutron stars (Lattimer 2000, Kubis, Xu 2009) or liquid-gas phase transitions in heavy-ion reactions (Bertsch and Siemens, Lopez, Goodman). The authors build on earlier findings by Kubis that spinodal decomposition can occur at supra-saturation densities in neutron star cores when the symmetry energy is very soft. The study enables future work on the nature of the high-density phase transition and properties of new phases that may appear above the instability onset density, complementing Bayesian analyses of hadron-quark transition densities (Xie 2022) and studies of core g-mode oscillations in neutron stars (Dong and Lai).
