# Effects of odderon spin on helicity amplitudes in $pp$ elastic scattering

> In recent years, the discovery of the odderon, a colorless $C$-odd gluonic compound, has been confirmed in the TOTEM and D0 collaborations. However, the spin quantum number of the odderon remains unidentified. In this work, we aim to attribute a spin of $J=3$ to the odderon in $pp$ elastic scattering by calculating the helicity amplitudes and the corresponding complex parameter $r_5$, the ratio of helicity's single-flip to non-flip amplitudes, for the spin-3 tensor odderon with the standard spin-2 tensor pomeron exchanges. Then, we apply these results to the constraints obtained from the STAR experiment at RHIC. By comparing to the contributions of the spin-1 vector odderon and spin-2 tensor pomeron, we demonstrate that the spin-3 tensor odderon, i.e. $J=3$, provides a better explanation for the observable in $pp$ elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that attributing a spin of \(J=3\) to the odderon provides a better explanation for the helicity amplitude ratio \(r_5\) in \(pp\) elastic scattering than a spin-1 odderon, when both are combined with a standard spin-2 tensor pomeron exchange and compared to constraints from the STAR experiment at RHIC.

## Method

The authors calculated the helicity amplitudes and the complex parameter \(r_5\) (the ratio of single-flip to non-flip helicity amplitudes) for a combined-exchange model consisting of a spin-2 tensor pomeron and either a spin-1 vector odderon or a spin-3 tensor odderon, using the Donnachie-Landshoff ansatz for the propagators. They then compared the resulting \(r_5\) values to the experimental constraints obtained from single-spin asymmetry measurements by the STAR experiment at RHIC at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* The central value of the experimental constraint on \(r_5\) from the STAR experiment at RHIC is \((0.0017 + i\,0.007)\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV, with statistical and statistical+systematic uncertainties.
* The TOTEM and D0 collaborations reported the discovery of odderon exchange with a combined significance exceeding \(5\sigma\).
* A subsequent model-dependent theoretical study increased the statistical significance of odderon exchange to at least \(7.08\sigma\) by comparing differential cross sections at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) and 7 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the spin-dependent analysis of the pomeron by Ewerz et al. (2016), which studied the spin-2 tensor pomeron and found its \(r_5\) value consistent with STAR data, by adding the odderon contribution. It builds on the spin-3 tensor odderon model of Magallanes et al. (2022), which fitted total cross sections but lacked spin-dependent analysis, and contrasts with the spin-1 vector odderon approach of Ewerz et al. (2013). The results enable future identification of the odderon's spin quantum number through spin-dependent observables in high-energy elastic scattering.
