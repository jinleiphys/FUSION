# Effective Lagrangians and Parity-Conserving Time-Reversal Violation at Low Energies

> Using effective Lagrangians, we argue that any time-reversal-violating but parity-conserving effects are too small to be observed in flavor-conserving nuclear processes without dramatic improvement in experimental accuracy. In the process we discuss other arguments that have appeared in the literature.

# Effective Lagrangians and Parity-Conserving Time-Reversal Violation at Low Energies

## Key claim

Under conservative assumptions about the high-energy scale of new physics (at least 100 GeV) and regardless of how time-reversal invariance is broken, effective parity-conserving time-reversal-violating (TVPC) couplings in flavor-conserving nuclear processes lie at or below 10^{-8} times the strong coupling g, making them unobservable without dramatic improvements in experimental accuracy.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory expansion in powers of 1/Λ, where Λ is the scale of new T-violating physics (taken conservatively as 100 GeV), and identify the lowest-dimensional gauge-invariant TVPC flavor-diagonal operators in standard-model fields. They then estimate the size of TVPC effects by counting powers of (p/Λ) from operator dimensions, with p taken as the proton mass (~1 GeV), and further constrain operator coefficients using the measured limit on the neutron electric dipole moment.

## Key numbers

- The best published limit on the effective TVPC ρNN coupling is about 10^{-2} times the normal strong ρNN coupling
- The conservative momentum scale p is taken as the proton mass, m_p ≈ 1 GeV
- The conservative high-energy scale Λ is taken as ~100 GeV (the Z^0 mass)
- Without experimental input, dimensional analysis gives TVPC effects suppressed by at least (p/Λ)^3 = 10^{-6} relative to strong interactions
- The neutron electric dipole moment limit d_n/e ≤ 10^{-25} cm constrains the dimension-seven operator coefficient C_7 ≤ 10^{-4}
- The most natural bound from dimension-eight operators gives suppression of (m_p/Λ)^4 = 10^{-8} relative to strong effects

## Context

This work extends the theorem of Herczeg et al. (1995) showing that TVPC flavor-conserving quark-quark interactions require more than two non-QCD non-QED vertices in renormalizable gauge theories, and addresses the approach of Conti and Khriplovich (1992), who argued that electric dipole moment limits constrain TVPC vertices to 10^{-10} times strong interactions. The paper provides a more conservative bound of 10^{-8} using effective field theory, and enables future experimental searches to set target sensitivities, noting that a measurement between 10^{-6} and 10^{-8} would suggest either unknown symmetries in the high-energy theory or accidental cancellations preventing TVPV contributions to the neutron electric dipole moment.
