# Quarkonium spectral function in medium at next-to-leading order for any quark mass

> The vector channel spectral function at zero spatial momentum is calculated at next-to-leading order in thermal QCD for any quark mass. It corresponds to the imaginary part of the massive quark contribution to the photon polarization tensor. The spectrum shows a well defined transport peak in contrast to both the heavy quark limit studied previously, where the low frequency domain is exponentially suppressed at this order and the naive massless case where it vanishes at leading order and diverges at next-to-leading order. From our general expressions, the massless limit can be taken and we show that no divergences occur if done carefully. Finally, we compare the massless limit to results from lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The vector channel spectral function at zero spatial momentum is calculated at next-to-leading order (NLO) in thermal QCD for any quark mass, showing a well-defined transport peak at low frequencies that is absent in both the previously studied heavy-quark limit (where it is exponentially suppressed) and the naive massless limit (where it diverges at NLO); the massless limit can be taken carefully from the general expressions without divergences.

## Method

The Euclidean correlator for the massive quark vector current was computed at NLO using finite-temperature Feynman rules, and the spectral function was obtained by taking the discontinuity of the analytically continued Euclidean correlator along the imaginary axis. The calculation required resummation of the thermal mass shift to avoid threshold divergences, and the final result was split into vacuum, bosonic thermal, and fermionic thermal contributions, with the remaining integrals evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The spectral function is plotted for masses ranging from \(M = 0.5T\) to \(M = 11.9T\) at \(T = 1.5 T_c\), where \(T_c\) is the critical temperature.
- For the charm quark at \(T = 1.5 T_c\) (\(M = 3.3T\)), the extracted diffusion coefficient gives \(2\pi T D \approx 0.1\), compared to lattice results suggesting \(2\pi T D \approx 2\) and the perturbative heavy quark limit \(2\pi T D \approx 10\).
- For a massless fermion with thermal mass \(\delta M_T^2 = g^2 C_F T^2/4\), the transport coefficient is \(2\pi T D \sim 0.3\), while perturbative resummation gives \(2\pi T D \sim 25\) and lattice results range from \(2\pi T D \sim 1\) to \(6\).
- The asymptotic behavior at high frequency (\(\omega \gg M, T\)) is \(-\rho^T_{NLO} = 4 N_c C_F g^2 \left( \frac{\pi T^4}{36\omega^2} + \frac{T^2 M^2}{8\pi\omega^2} \right)\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the vector channel spectral function from the heavy-quark limit (Burnier, 2008) and the massless case (Altherr, 1989) to arbitrary quark mass, resolving the divergence at zero frequency that appeared in the earlier massless NLO calculation. The results enable comparison with lattice simulations of the Euclidean correlator (Ding, 2010) and provide a perturbative baseline for extracting transport coefficients such as the heavy-flavor diffusion coefficient and electric conductivity, though the transport peak is found to be broad and not well separated from other physics at this order.
