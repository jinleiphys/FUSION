# Double beta decay searches of Xe-134, Xe-126 and Xe-124 with large scale Xe detectors

> The sensitivity for double beta decay studies of Xe-134 and Xe-124 is investigated assuming a potential large scale Xe experiment developed for dark matter searches depleted in Xe-136. The opportunity for an observation of the 2nu double beta decay of Xe-134 is explored for various scenarios. A positive observation should be possible for all calculated nuclear matrix elements. The detection of 2$ν$ ECEC of Xe-124 can be probed in all scenarios covering the theoretical predicted half-life uncertainties and a potential search for Xe-126 is discussed. The sensitivity to beta+/EC decay of Xe-124 is discussed and a positive observation might be possible, while beta+/beta+ decay still remains unobservable. The performed studies take into account solar pp-neutrino interactions, $^{85}$Kr beta decay and remaining Xe-136 double beta decay as background components in the depleted detector.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether large-scale liquid xenon detectors built for dark matter searches (depleted in Xe-136) can also observe the two-neutrino double beta decay of Xe-134 and the double electron capture of Xe-124, finding that a positive observation of Xe-134 should be possible for all calculated nuclear matrix elements, while the detection of Xe-124 ECEC can be probed across the full range of theoretical half-life uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed GEANT4 Monte Carlo simulations of a 20-tonne liquid xenon detector based on the DARWIN concept, using a modified DECAY0 code to generate decays of Xe-134, Xe-124, and Xe-126. They defined three scenarios for residual Xe-136 abundance (0.01%, 0.05%, and 0.1%) in depleted xenon and calculated sensitivities using the Feldman-Cousins approach, including backgrounds from solar pp-neutrino electron recoils, Kr-85 beta decay, and remaining Xe-136 double beta decay.

## Key numbers

- Xe-134 double beta decay Q-value: 825.8 ± 0.9 keV
- Current experimental limit on Xe-134 2νββ half-life: 1.1 × 10^16 years
- Estimated Xe-134 2νββ half-life from Q^11 scaling: approximately 1.71 × 10^25 years
- Estimated Xe-134 2νββ half-life from IBM-2 model (varying g_A between 1 and 1.269): 3.7-4.7 × 10^24 years
- Sensitivity for Xe-134 2νββ: half-lives above 10^26 years accessible in 5 years (ideal scenario without additional backgrounds)
- With solar pp-neutrino background included: sensitivity limited to about 2 × 10^26 years in 5 years
- With Kr-85 at 0.1 ppt Kr/Xe purification level: sensitivity reduced by a factor of two
- Xe-124 2ν ECEC theoretical half-life predictions span: 0.4 × 10^21 to 986 × 10^21 years
- Current experimental lower limit on Xe-124 ECEC half-life: 1.1 × 10^17 years
- Xe-124 natural abundance: 0.1%
- Xe-126 Q-value: 897 keV, natural abundance: 0.09%
- Xe-126 2ν ECEC half-life estimate (from equal matrix elements and Q^5 scaling): approximately 340 times longer than Xe-124
- Xe-124 2ν β+EC half-life predictions: 3.0 × 10^22 to 3.0 × 10^24 years
- Xe-124 2ν β+β+ half-life predictions: 10^27 to 10^28 years (unobservable, less than one event per 10 years)

## Context

This work extends the study of double beta decay to isotopes with lower Q-values that are typically unobservable in dedicated double beta decay experiments, following the approach used for tellurium isotopes by Barabash (2010). The paper proposes using future large-scale liquid xenon dark matter detectors (XENON1T, LZ, DARWIN) as multipurpose instruments, similar to how geochemical experiments measured both Te-128 and Te-130 half-lives. The results enable searches for the rare 2ν ECEC mode of Xe-124, which has never been observed in any isotope, and could provide nuclear matrix element tests by comparing Xe-134 and Xe-136 decays from the same element.
