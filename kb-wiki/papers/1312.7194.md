# Extension of the HAL QCD approach to inelastic and multi-particle scatterings in lattice QCD

> We extend the HAL QCD approach, with which potentials between two hadrons can be obtained in QCD at energy below inelastic thresholds, to inelastic and multi-particle scatterings. We first derive asymptotic behaviors of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function at large space separations for systems with more than 2 particles, in terms of the one-shell $T$-matrix consrainted by the unitarity of quantum field theories. We show that its asymptotic behavior contains phase shifts and mixing angles of $n$ particle scatterings. This property is one of the essential ingredients of the HAL QCD scheme to define "potential" from the NBS wave function in quantum field theories such as QCD. We next construct energy independent but non-local potentials above inelastic thresholds, in terms of these NBS wave functions. We demonstrate an existence of energy-independent coupled channel potentials with a non-relativistic approximation, where momenta of all particles are small compared with their own masses. Combining these two results, we can employ the HAL QCD approach also to investigate inelastic and multi-particle scatterings.

## Key claim

The HAL QCD approach, previously limited to elastic two-hadron scatterings below inelastic thresholds, can be extended to inelastic and multi-particle scatterings by deriving asymptotic behaviors of Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for more than two particles and by constructing energy-independent coupled-channel potentials above inelastic thresholds, provided a non-relativistic approximation holds where all particle momenta are small compared to their masses.

## Method

The author first derives the asymptotic behavior of NBS wave functions for systems of n scalar particles (all with the same mass m but different flavors) at large spatial separations, using the unitarity constraint on the T-matrix and expressing the wave function in hyper-spherical coordinates in D = 3(n-1) dimensions. The author then constructs energy-independent but non-local coupled-channel potentials for inelastic scatterings (e.g., NN → NN + nπ) by defining a potential matrix that satisfies a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation under a non-relativistic approximation where kinetic energies are small compared to rest masses.

## Key numbers

- For n=2 particles, the T-matrix in the non-relativistic limit takes the form T_L(Q) = -(4√2)/(mQ) e^(iδ_L(Q)) sin δ_L(Q), with Q = (p₁ - p₂)/√2.
- The asymptotic form of the n-particle NBS wave function at large hyper-radius R contains the factor sin(Q_A R - Δ_L + δ_[N](Q_A)), where Δ_L = (2L + D - 3)π/4 and D = 3(n-1).
- The inelastic energy interval considered for NN → NN + π is m_π < W - 2m_N < 2m_π.
- The non-relativistic approximation for the inelastic case uses kinetic energy E_W^k = p₁²/(2m_N) + p₂²/(2m_N) + Σ k_i²/(2m_π) with total momentum zero in the center-of-mass frame.

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method, previously applied to elastic two-nucleon scatterings below the inelastic threshold (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2008, Aoki 2009, Aoki 2012), to inelastic and multi-particle scatterings. The results are relevant for three-nucleon forces (Doi 2011) and coupled-channel potentials between hyperons (Sasaki). A remaining open question is how to treat bound states (e.g., a deuteron forming in three-nucleon scattering) within this extended framework.
