# Diffusion as a leading dissipative mechanism in superconducting neutron stars

> Despite the fact that different particle species can diffuse with respect to each other in neutron star (NS) cores, the effect of particle diffusion on various phenomena associated with NS oscillations is usually ignored. Here we demonstrate that the diffusion can be extremely powerful dissipative mechanism in superconducting NSs. In particular, it can be much more efficient than the shear and bulk viscosities. This result has important implications for the damping times of NS oscillations, development and saturation of dynamical instabilities in NSs, and for the excitation and coupling of oscillation modes during the late inspiral of binary NSs.

# Diffusion as a leading dissipative mechanism in superconducting neutron stars

## Key claim
Particle diffusion can be an extremely powerful dissipative mechanism in superconducting neutron stars, much more efficient than shear and bulk viscosities, but only when protons are superconducting; in normal (non-superconducting) matter, diffusion is generally less effective than shear viscosity except for g-modes.

## Method
The authors derived expressions for the energy dissipation rate due to particle diffusion in both normal and superconducting npe/npeμ matter, using the momentum transfer rates between particle species and the chemical potential imbalance δμ. They then calculated damping times for sound waves and for global oscillation modes (f-, p-, g-, and r-modes) in a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star with the BSk24 equation of state, comparing diffusion damping times to shear viscosity damping times.

## Key numbers
- In superconducting matter, diffusion is more efficient than in normal matter by a factor of ~J_np/J_en ~ 10^5
- For p-modes in superconducting matter, τ_η exceeds τ_diff by approximately two orders of magnitude
- For g-modes in superconducting matter, the difference between τ_η and τ_diff is almost four orders of magnitude
- For the main g-mode harmonic in normal matter, τ_η/τ_diff ≈ 4.8
- Momentum transfer rates J_ni ~ (10^24–10^30) (T/10^8 K)^2 g cm^−3 s^−1
- For sound waves in superconducting matter, τ_diff and τ_η obey almost the same temperature dependence (∝ T^2) and their ratio is almost temperature-independent
- For r-modes in the Newtonian framework, 1/τ_diff ∝ ν^2 while 1/τ_η does not depend on ν

## Context
This work extends the study of dissipative mechanisms in neutron star oscillations beyond the traditionally considered shear and bulk viscosities (Clarke & Lindblom 1987; Miralda-Escude et al. 1988). The authors build on the multi-fluid hydrodynamic framework developed by Gusakov et al. (2017) and Dommes et al. (2020, 2021), and use momentum transfer rates from Shternin (2018) and Dommes et al. (2020). The results imply that damping times of all oscillation modes need to be revisited, with consequences for r-mode instability windows (Andersson 1998; Friedman & Morsink 1998), tidal instabilities in binary inspirals (Weinberg et al. 2016; Arras & Hindmarsh 2018), and glitch models based on hydrodynamic instabilities.
