# Separable potential model for meson-baryon interaction beyond the S-wave

> A model for low-energy meson-baryon interaction in the strange sector is presented. The interaction is described in terms of separable potentials with multiple partial waves considered. A general solution of Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the scattering of spin zero and spin one-half particles is derived. Next, the general framework is applied to the $\bar{K}N$ sector in a simple model with only the S- and P-waves taken into account. The separable potential is designed to match the chiral perturbation theory at lowest nontrivial order. It is shown that although a simple model with three free parameters works well for the S-wave, it fails to reproduce the P-wave features of kaon-nucleon physics. Most importantly, the P-wave interaction is too weak to express a resonant behavior that could be identified as $Σ(1385)$ resonance.

## Key claim

A chirally motivated separable potential model that includes both S- and P-waves for low-energy meson-baryon interaction in the strange sector is presented; although the model reproduces S-wave data well, the P-wave interaction derived from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian is too weak to generate the Σ(1385) resonance, and the model fails to reproduce the angular distribution asymmetries for the K⁻p and K̄⁰n channels.

## Method

The author derives a general solution of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for scattering of spin-zero and spin-one-half particles using separable potentials with multiple partial waves. The general formalism is then applied to the K̄N sector with only S- and P-waves, where the separable potential is constructed to match chiral perturbation theory at lowest nontrivial order O(p¹), using Yamaguchi-type form factors with inverse range parameters α^S and α^P, and the pion decay constant f_π as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters: α^S = 736 MeV, f_π = 116.6 MeV, α^P = 1353 MeV; overall χ²/N = 4.3
- Threshold data fit: γ = 2.36 (experimental 2.36 ± 0.04), R_c = 0.637 (experimental 0.664 ± 0.011), R_n = 0.178 (experimental 0.189 ± 0.015)
- Kaonic hydrogen: ΔE = -296 eV (experimental -283 ± 42 eV), Γ = 761 eV (experimental 541 ± 111 eV)
- For the P-wave sector, a resonant structure in the isovector πΣ 1⁺ amplitude appears only when the P-wave potential is artificially scaled by P_scale ≈ -3.9 (for α^P = 1353 MeV), with the resonance moving to higher energies as the strength increases
- For α^P = 1100 MeV, the resonant structure appears at P_scale ≈ -6.7; for α^P = 900 MeV, at P_scale ≈ -10.5

## Context

This work extends the separable potential model of Cieplý and Smejkal (2010, 2012), which considered only S-wave contributions and successfully fit low-energy K̄N data including the Λ(1405) resonance, to include P-wave effects. The model fails to dynamically generate the Σ(1385) resonance, suggesting that higher-order chiral contributions (O(p²)) or a scaling parameter away from χPT are needed; the author notes that better measurements of angular distribution asymmetries would be required to constrain an extended model with more free parameters.
