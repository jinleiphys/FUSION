# Hybrid states

> Theoretical arguments are given in favour of existence of gluonic degrees of freedom at the constituent level. Models for hybrid mesons are discussed, and the predictions are compared with the data on meson spectroscopy in the light quark sector. It is demonstrated that the $q\bar q g$ content might be responsible for the properties of some recently found mesonic states.

# Hybrid States

## Key claim

The paper argues that gluonic degrees of freedom at the constituent level are likely responsible for the properties of several recently observed mesonic states in the 1.5–2.0 GeV mass range, but the claim is hedged by the recognition that both the flux-tube model and the constituent string model are still in their infancy theoretically, and that the existing hybrid candidates are all P-odd, which prevents distinguishing between the two competing model predictions.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares two theoretical approaches for hybrid mesons: the flux-tube model, motivated by strong coupling expansion, where the string connecting a quark and antiquark is replaced by a set of "beads" coupled by linear forces; and the constituent string model, motivated by the Vacuum Background Correlators method, where a constituent gluon is introduced as a perturbation over the confining background, with two straight-line strings connecting the gluon to the quark and antiquark. The predictions of both models are compared with experimental data on meson spectroscopy in the light quark sector, focusing on P-odd hybrid candidates in the mass range 1.5–2.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground state hybrid mass with light quarks: M(q qbar g) = 1.7–1.8 GeV (up to spin-dependent effects), predicted by both the flux-tube and constituent string models.
- The ratio of partial widths for hybrid decay into pi b1 versus pi f1 is predicted as pi b1 : pi f1 = 4:1 in the constituent model.
- A clear hybrid signal in the pseudoscalar sector was reported by VES with mass about 1800 MeV and width about 200 MeV.
- The pi(1300), believed to be the first radially excited pion, has a very broad width of Gamma ~ 500 MeV, in contrast to the narrower pi(1800) candidate.
- The eta2(1870) has a width about 200 MeV with decay modes pi a2 and eta f0(980).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of hybrid mesons from earlier flux-tube model developments (Isgur and Paton, 1985) and constituent string model calculations (Kalashnikova and colleagues, 1990s), and compares predictions with experimental results from BNL (1990s) and VES (1990s) collaborations. The paper enables future dedicated qualitative analysis of hybrid candidates appearing in multiplets, and emphasizes the need to study P-even hybrids to distinguish between the flux-tube and constituent gluon models, as well as the importance of understanding q qbar – q qbar g mixing for the physical interpretation of observed states.
