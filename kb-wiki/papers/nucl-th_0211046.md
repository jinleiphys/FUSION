# Compact stars with color superconducting quark matter

> We study compact stars that contain quark matter. We look at the effect of color superconductivity in the quark matter on the nuclear-quark matter transition density, mass-radius relationship, and the density discontinuity at the boundary between nuclear and quark matter. We find that color superconducting quark matter will occur in compact stars at values of the bag constant where ordinary quark matter would not be allowed. We are able to construct ``hybrid'' stars with a color superconducting quark matter interior and nuclear matter surface, with masses in the range 1.3-1.6 M_solar and radii 8-11 km. Our results are consistent with recent mass-radius limits based on absorption lines from EXO0748-676.

## Key claim
Color superconducting quark matter can occur in compact stars at values of the bag constant where ordinary unpaired quark matter would not be allowed, enabling hybrid stars with a superconducting quark matter interior and nuclear matter surface. The effect is significant only in a narrow window of quark chemical potential near the value where kinetic and bag pressures cancel, making the pairing contribution to the pressure dominant over the otherwise much larger kinetic contribution.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state for nuclear matter using the Walecka model (calibrated to five empirical saturation properties) and the APR98 variational equation of state, and for quark matter using a free Fermi gas with a bag constant plus a CFL pairing contribution to the free energy. They imposed local or global charge neutrality, allowed sharp interfaces or mixed phases between nuclear and quark matter, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain mass-radius relations for compact stars.

## Key numbers
- Hybrid star masses: 1.3–1.6 solar masses, radii 8–11 km (for CFL quark matter interior with nuclear matter surface)
- For bag constant B^(1/4)=190 MeV and gap Delta=100 MeV: transition from nuclear to CFL quark matter occurs at chemical potential 365.4 MeV, pressure 33.79 MeV/fm^3, nuclear density 2.149 n_sat, CFL density 4.295 n_sat
- For bag constant B^(1/4)=170 MeV and gap Delta=50 MeV: transition at chemical potential 338.4 MeV, pressure 10.77 MeV/fm^3, nuclear density 1.382 n_sat, CFL density 3.033 n_sat
- Maximum masses for superconducting stars with Delta=100 MeV and m_s=200 MeV: 1.33 solar masses (B^(1/4)=185 MeV, sharp interface), 1.35 solar masses (B^(1/4)=185 MeV, mixed phase), 1.44 solar masses (B^(1/4)=175 MeV), 1.52 solar masses (B^(1/4)=170 MeV)
- Bag constant range studied: B^(1/4)=150–200 MeV (B=66–210 MeV/fm^3), corresponding to mu_0 approximately 320–400 MeV
- Charge density comparison at mu=400 MeV, m_s=150 MeV, mu_e=100 MeV: unpaired quark matter Q=-0.32 fm^-3, CFL phase Q=-0.09 fm^-3

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of unpaired quark matter in compact stars (Burgio 2002, Schertler 2000, Steiner 2000) by incorporating color-flavor locked pairing, and complements studies of transport signatures of color superconductivity in stars (reviewed by Alford 2002). The results are consistent with mass-radius limits from absorption lines in EXO0748-676 (Cottam 2002). The paper enables future work on how the CFL-nuclear mixed phase, with its distinct charge susceptibility, affects transport and cooling phenomena in compact stars.
