# Relativistic Mean-Field Theory Equation of State of Neutron Star Matter and a Maxwellian Phase Transition to Strange Quark Matter

> The equation of state of neutron star matter is examined in terms of the relativistic mean-field theory, including a scalar-isovector $δ$-meson effective field. The constants of the theory are determined numerically so that the empirically known characteristics of symmetric nuclear matter are reproduced at the saturation density. The thermodynamic characteristics of both asymmetric nucleonic matter and $β$-equilibrium hadron-electron $npe$-plasmas are studied. Assuming that the transition to strange quark matter is an ordinary first-order phase transition described by Maxwell's rule, a detailed study is made of the variations in the parameters of the phase transition owing to the presence of a $δ$-meson field. The quark phase is described using an improved version of the bag model, in which interactions between quarks are accounted for in a one-gluon exchange approximation. The characteristics of the phase transition are determined for various values of the bag parameter within the range $B\in[60,120]$ $MeV/fm^{3}$ and it is shown that including a $δ$-meson field leads to a reduction in the phase transition pressure $P_{0}$ and in the concentrations $n_{N}$ and $n_{Q}$ at the phase transition point.

## Key claim

Including a scalar-isovector δ-meson field in the relativistic mean-field theory description of neutron star matter leads to a reduction in the phase transition pressure \(P_{0}\) and in the baryon concentrations \(n_{N}\) and \(n_{Q}\) at the hadron-quark phase transition point, assuming the transition follows Maxwell's rule for a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state for neutron star matter using relativistic mean-field theory with \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), and \(\delta\) meson fields, determining the coupling constants by fitting to empirical properties of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density (saturation concentration \(n_{0}=0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(f_{0}=-16.3\) MeV, compression modulus \(K=300\) MeV, symmetry energy \(E_{sym}^{(0)}=32.5\) MeV). They then computed the thermodynamic properties of \(\beta\)-equilibrium \(npe\) plasma and applied Maxwell's rule for a first-order phase transition to strange quark matter, which was described using an improved MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange interactions for \(u\), \(d\), and \(s\) quarks.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(n_{0}=0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Specific binding energy at saturation: \(f_{0}=-16.3\) MeV
- Compression modulus: \(K=300\) MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{sym}^{(0)}=32.5\) MeV
- Effective nucleon mass ratio: \(\gamma = m_{N}^{\ast}/m_{N}=0.78\)
- Bag parameter range studied: \(B\in[60,120]\) MeV/fm\(^{3}\)
- For the \(\sigma\omega\rho\delta\) model: \(a_{\sigma}=9.154\) fm\(^{2}\), \(a_{\omega}=4.828\) fm\(^{2}\), \(a_{\delta}=2.5\) fm\(^{2}\), \(a_{\rho}=13.621\) fm\(^{2}\), \(b=1.654\times10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(c=1.319\times10^{-2}\)
- For the \(\sigma\omega\rho\) model (no \(\delta\)): \(a_{\delta}=0\) fm\(^{2}\), \(a_{\rho}=4.794\) fm\(^{2}\)

## Context

This work extends prior relativistic mean-field theory studies of nuclear matter (Walecka 1974, Serot and Walecka 1986, 1997) by including the scalar-isovector \(\delta\)-meson field, which had been studied at low densities by Kubis (1997), Liu (2002), and Grefe and Colonna (2003). The phase transition treatment follows the Maxwell construction approach, in contrast to the mixed-phase formalism of Glendenning (1992, 2000). The results enable future calculations of hybrid neutron star structure with quark cores, comparing the \(\sigma\omega\rho\delta\) and \(\sigma\omega\rho\) models to determine how the \(\delta\)-meson affects stellar mass-radius relations and maximum mass.
