# Optimized Dirac Woods-Saxon basis for covariant density functional theory

> The Woods-Saxon basis has achieved great success in both nonrelativistic and covariant density functional theories in recent years. Due to its nonanalytical nature, however, applications of the Woods-Saxon basis are numerically complicated and computationally time consuming. In this paper, based on the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc), we check in detail the convergence with respect to the basis space in the Dirac sea. An optimized Dirac Woods-Saxon basis is proposed, whose corresponding potential is close to the nuclear mean field. It is shown that the basis space of the optimized Dirac Woods-Saxon basis required for convergence is substantially reduced compared with the original one. In particular, it does not need to contain the bases from continuum in the Dirac sea. The application of the optimized Woods-Saxon basis would greatly reduce computing resource for large-scale density functional calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an optimized Dirac Woods-Saxon (ODWS) basis for covariant density functional theory, showing that when the basis-generating potential is close to the converged mean-field potential, the required basis space is substantially reduced and does not need to include bases from the continuum in the Dirac sea, unlike the original DWS basis.

## Method

The authors performed convergence checks of the total energy against the energy cutoff in the Dirac sea using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) for three representative nuclei: light 20Ne, medium-heavy 112Mo, and heavy 300Th. They then constructed an optimized basis by solving the Dirac equation with angle-averaged converged mean-field potentials, and compared convergence behavior of total energy and single-particle levels between the original DWS basis and the ODWS basis.

## Key numbers

- For the original DWS basis, an energy cutoff in the Dirac sea of E_cut^D = 50 MeV yields total energy accuracy on the order of 10^-5 relative difference from converged results for 20Ne, 112Mo, and 300Th.
- For the ODWS basis applied to 300Th, the difference between results from E_cut^F = 200 MeV, E_cut^D = 0 MeV and E_cut^F = 300 MeV, E_cut^D = 50 MeV is only 0.005 MeV, about 10^-6 of the total energy.
- For the ODWS basis with E_cut^F = 200 MeV and E_cut^D = 0 MeV, the largest difference in single-neutron energies near the continuum threshold for 300Th is less than 0.01 MeV compared to the converged result.
- The depth of the V+S potential for protons in 300Th differs by about 10 MeV between the Woods-Saxon basis and the converged mean field, while the difference in the V-S potential exceeds 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Woods-Saxon basis approach originally proposed by Zhou et al. (2003) for solving relativistic Hartree equations, which has been applied in the DRHBc theory for studying deformed halo nuclei and constructing mass tables (Zhang et al., 2020, 2022). The optimized basis is conceptually similar to the method of Zhang et al. (1988, 1991) where deformed relativistic Hartree equations were solved using basis functions from the self-consistent spherical Hartree potential, but the ODWS basis offers the practical advantage of requiring only one basis reconstruction during iteration. The reduction in basis space enables more efficient large-scale DRHBc mass table calculations, particularly for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei where blocking effects make computations extremely time consuming.
