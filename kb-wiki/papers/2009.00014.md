# The Strangest Proton?

> We present an improved determination of the strange quark and anti-quark parton distribution functions of the proton by means of a global QCD analysis that takes into account a comprehensive set of strangeness-sensitive measurements: charm-tagged cross sections for fixed-target neutrino-nucleus deep-inelastic scattering, and cross sections for inclusive gauge-boson production and $W$-boson production in association with light jets or charm quarks at hadron colliders. Our analysis is accurate to next-to-next-to leading order in perturbative QCD where available, and specifically includes charm-quark mass corrections to neutrino-nucleus structure functions. We find that a good overall description of the input dataset can be achieved and that a strangeness moderately suppressed in comparison to the rest of the light sea quarks is strongly favored by the global analysis.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a global QCD analysis strongly favors a strange quark PDF that is moderately suppressed compared to the other light sea quarks, contradicting earlier indications from ATLAS W and Z data alone that suggested strangeness might be nearly equal to the non-strange sea. The result depends on including NNLO charm-quark mass corrections to neutrino-nucleus structure functions and a comprehensive set of LHC measurements.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in perturbative QCD, using the NNPDF methodology with Monte Carlo replica fitting. They included dimuon neutrino-nucleus deep-inelastic scattering data from NuTeV and NOMAD experiments, plus inclusive W and Z boson production, W+c production, and W+jets measurements from ATLAS and CMS at the LHC. Theoretical predictions incorporated NNLO charm-quark mass corrections for neutrino DIS via K-factors in the FONLL scheme, and NNLO K-factors for collider observables where available, with a correlated theory uncertainty for W+c production where NNLO corrections were not yet implemented.

## Key numbers

- Total dataset: 4096 data points included in the final fit (str fit)
- NOMAD dataset: 19 data points as a function of neutrino beam energy E_nu, with kinematic coverage roughly 0.03 less than x less than 0.7
- NOMAD ratio chi^2 per data point: approximately 9.0 in the baseline fit (str_base) without NOMAD data, decreasing to 0.55 in the final fit (str) that includes NOMAD
- NuTeV dimuon chi^2 per data point: 0.70 in str_base, 0.53 in str fit
- ATLAS W,Z (7 TeV) chi^2 per data point: 3.22 in str_base (not fitted), 1.67 in str fit
- NNLO K-factors for NOMAD: the NNLO cross section is about 10% smaller than the LO cross section in the highest energy bin
- Nuclear corrections for NOMAD ratio: relative difference between Fe and proton predictions ranges from 3% at lowest E_nu to a fraction of percent at highest E_nu
- Effective number of replicas after reweighting with NOMAD E_nu data: N_eff = 105 (from 850 initial replicas)
- Effective number of replicas after reweighting with NOMAD sqrt(s-hat) data: N_eff = 135

## Context

This work extends the NNPDF3.1 analysis (Ball et al., 2017) by incorporating new strangeness-sensitive measurements and theoretical improvements, particularly NNLO charm-mass corrections to neutrino DIS structure functions that were also studied by the MMHT collaboration (Thorne et al., 2019). The analysis resolves tensions between earlier neutrino-DIS results favoring suppressed strangeness (R_s approximately 0.5) and ATLAS W,Z data that suggested R_s approximately 1, finding that a moderately suppressed strangeness is favored when all data are combined. The results enable more precise predictions for electroweak measurements at the LHC, such as the W boson mass and the Weinberg angle.
