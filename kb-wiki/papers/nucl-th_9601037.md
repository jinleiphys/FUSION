# Finite-size Effects in Multiquark Droplets; An Anatomical Study

> Strutinsky's averaging(SA) method is applied to multiquark droplets to systematically extract the smooth part of the exact quantal energy and thereby the shell correction energies. It is shown within the bag model that the semi-phenomenological density of states expression given upto curvature order is almost equivalent to the SA method. A comparative study of the bag model and the relativistic harmonic oscillator potential for quarks is done to investigate the quark mass dependence of the finite-size effects. It is found that there is an important difference between these two cases, which may be related to the presence/non-presence of the net spin-orbit effect.

# Finite-size Effects in Multiquark Droplets; An Anatomical Study

## Key claim
The semi-phenomenological density of states expression up to curvature order (the quark liquid drop model of Madsen) is shown to be almost equivalent to Strutinsky's averaging method for the bag model, but the quark mass dependence of surface and curvature energies is strongly model-dependent, the bag model shows significant variation with quark mass while the relativistic harmonic oscillator potential does not, likely due to the presence or absence of net spin-orbit splitting.

## Method
Strutinsky's averaging (SA) method was applied to single-flavor quark systems confined in either a spherical cavity (infinite square well with bag boundary conditions) or a relativistic harmonic oscillator potential. The smooth part of the total quantal energy was extracted by Gaussian smoothing of the single-particle level density, and shell correction energies were computed as the difference between exact and smoothed energies. The SA results were compared to the quark liquid drop (QLD) model (Madsen's density of states up to curvature order) for the bag model, and to the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion up to O(ℏ²) for the harmonic oscillator.

## Key numbers
- For the relativistic harmonic oscillator potential, the difference between the smoothed energy from SA and the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion is about 10 MeV for massless quarks and about 17 MeV for massive quarks.
- For the bag model with massless quarks (m_q = 1 MeV, B^{1/4} = 145 MeV), the surface energy coefficient a_s is zero and the curvature coefficient a_cv is about 220 MeV.
- For the bag model with m_q = 150 MeV, the curvature coefficient decreases relative to the massless case (the curvature term in the DOS is nearly zero at this mass), while a_s becomes non-zero.
- For the bag model with m_q = 450 MeV, a_s increases further while a_cv remains more or less the same as at 150 MeV.
- For the relativistic harmonic oscillator, the surface coefficient is independent of quark mass (nearly zero for all m_q values), and the curvature coefficient increases from 150 MeV to 220 MeV as m_q varies from 0 to 150 MeV, with a slight decrease at higher m_q.

## Context
This work extends the quark liquid drop model of Madsen (1993) by quantitatively testing its density of states expression against Strutinsky's averaging method, a technique well-established in nuclear physics for extracting shell corrections. The study also compares the bag model (used by Farhi and Jaffe (1984) for surface effects and Mardor and Svetitsky (1991) for curvature effects) with the relativistic harmonic oscillator potential. The finding that finite-size effects depend strongly on the confining potential and on the presence of spin-orbit splitting has implications for stability calculations of strangelets (small lumps of strange quark matter) and for studies of the quark-hadron phase transition, as the model-dependence of surface and curvature energies affects the binding of small multiquark droplets with baryon number A ≤ 100.
