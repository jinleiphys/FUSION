# Relation between exponential behavior and energy denominators -- Weak Coupling Limit

> We show some interesting properties of tridiagonal and pentadiagonal matrices in the weak coupling limits. In the former case of this limit the ground state wave function amplitudes are identical to the Taylor expansion coefficients of the exponential function e$^{(-v/E)}$. With regards to transition rates a dip in the pentadiagonal case which is not present in the tridiagonal case is explained. An intimate connection between energy denominators and exponential behavior is demonstrated.

## Key claim

In the weak coupling limit (very small v/E), the ground-state wave function amplitudes of a tridiagonal matrix with equally spaced diagonal energies (E_n = nE) become exactly the Taylor expansion coefficients of e^{-v/E}, demonstrating that the exponential behavior arises directly from the factorial energy denominators produced by nth-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors diagonalized tridiagonal and pentadiagonal Hamiltonian matrices of dimension 11x11, with diagonal elements E_n = nE (E = 1 MeV) and constant off-diagonal coupling v. They computed ground-state wave function coefficients a_n in the weak coupling limit (v/E very small) using Mathematica, then derived analytic expressions via nth-order perturbation theory by summing all paths connecting the unperturbed ground state to state n. For the pentadiagonal case, they also computed transition strengths for two operators T_1 (constant matrix element) and T_2 (matrix element proportional to sqrt(n+1)) between the ground state and excited states.

## Key numbers

- For the tridiagonal case with v/E = 0.01, the ground-state coefficients were: a_0 = 0.99995, a_1 = -0.009999, a_2 = 0.0000499933, a_3 = -1.6664e-7, a_4 = 4.16592e-10, a_5 = -8.33171e-13, a_6 = 1.3886e-15, a_7 = -1.98269e-18, a_8 = 2.47958e-21, a_9 = -2.75506e-24, a_10 = 2.75504e-27
- For the pentadiagonal case with v/E = 10^{-10}, the ground-state coefficients were: a_0 = 1, a_1 = -1.00006e-10, a_2 = -5.0e-11, a_3 = 5.00091e-21, a_4 = 1.25e-21, a_5 = -1.25071e-31, a_6 = -2.08333e-32, a_7 = 2.08951e-42, a_8 = 2.60417e-43, a_9 = -2.63376e-53, a_10 = -2.60417e-54
- For the tridiagonal case, the analytic form of the ground-state coefficients is a_n = (-1)^n (v/E)^n / n!
- For the pentadiagonal case, a_1 = -v/E, a_2 = -v/(2E), a_3 = -v/(2E), a_4 = (1/8)(v/E)^2, a_5 = -(1/8)(v/E)^3
- For T_1 transitions in the pentadiagonal case with v = 0.1 MeV, a dip occurs at the second excited state due to cancellation of leading terms: a_0*b_1 = v/E and a_1*b_2 = -v/E

## Context

This work extends previous matrix-model studies of strength distributions by Wolfe and Zamick (2015, 2016) to the weak coupling limit, where analytic results become tractable. The tridiagonal matrix model is related to the schematic model of Brown and Bolsterli for giant dipole resonances, and to the matrix models used by Bohr and Mottelson to derive the Breit-Wigner formula. The results show that equally spaced unperturbed energies generate the factorial denominators needed for exponential behavior, and explain the dip in pentadiagonal transition rates that does not appear in the tridiagonal case. The work enables analytic understanding of how energy denominators produce exponential strength distributions in simple matrix models, and suggests that similar mechanisms may underlie exponential behavior seen in empirical data.
