# Photon angular distribution and nuclear-state alignment in nuclear excitation by electron capture

> The alignment of nuclear states resonantly formed in nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) is studied by means of a density matrix technique. The vibrational excitations of the nucleus are described by a collective model and the electrons are treated in a relativistic framework. Formulas for the angular distribution of photons emitted in the nuclear relaxation are derived. We present numerical results for alignment parameters and photon angular distributions for a number of heavy elements in the case of E2 nuclear transitions. Our results are intended to help future experimental attempts to discern NEEC from radiative recombination, which is the dominant competing process.

## Key claim

The alignment of nuclear states formed via NEEC can be used to derive photon angular distributions that are substantially different from those of the competing radiative recombination process, potentially enabling experimental discrimination between the two channels; however, the numerical results are presented only for E2 nuclear transitions in a limited set of heavy even-even nuclei, and the calculations assume capture into bare or He-like ions with specific electronic configurations.

## Method

A density matrix formalism was applied to describe the magnetic sublevel population (alignment) of nuclear states excited by NEEC, with the electron-nucleus interaction treated via a multipole expansion of the Coulomb interaction in the Coulomb gauge. Nuclear states were described using a phenomenological collective model for vibrational excitations of deformed even-even nuclei, while electrons were treated with relativistic Dirac four-component wave functions, including partial wave decomposition of continuum electrons and numerical evaluation of radial integrals. Alignment parameters and photon angular distributions were then computed for the subsequent radiative decay of the excited nuclear state, specifically for E2 transitions in the case of capture into the 1s orbital of bare ions and the 2s orbital of initially He-like ions.

## Key numbers

- For the even-even nuclei considered (174Yb, 170Er, 154Gd, 162Dy), the reduced transition probabilities B(E2,0→2) and nuclear transition energies were taken from Raman (year not specified in text).
- The nuclear radius was calculated using the semi-empirical formula R0 = (1.0793 A^(1/3) + 0.73587) fm, where A is the atomic mass number.
- For capture into the 2s orbital of initially He-like ions, the difference in Coulomb phase shifts between the Dirac-Fock approximation and the Zeff = Z-2 full screening approximation was less than 0.01 rad, with negligible effect on alignment parameters.
- The angular distribution for the 2+ → 0+ nuclear decay (E2 transition) is given by W(θ) = 1 - (√70/14) A2 P2(cos θ) - (2√14/7) A4 P4(cos θ), where A2 and A4 are the alignment parameters of the excited 2+ nuclear state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical study of total NEEC cross sections for heavy isotopes (Pálffy et al., 2006) and investigates the quantum interference of NEEC with radiative recombination (Pálffy et al., 2006). The density matrix approach for alignment and angular distributions parallels similar treatments for dielectronic recombination (Chen, 1995; Gail, 1998; Zakowicz, year not specified; Kandler, 1995; Ma, year not specified) and for Coulomb excitation of nuclei by charged particles (Breit, year not specified). The results are intended to guide future experimental attempts to observe NEEC by detecting emitted radiation, where the distinct photon angular distribution may help separate NEEC from the dominant background of radiative recombination.
