# The QCD phase transition in the inhomogeneous Universe

> We investigate a new mechanism for the cosmological QCD phase transition: inhomogeneous nucleation. The primordial temperature fluctuations, measured to be $δT/T \sim 10^{-5}$, are larger than the tiny temperature interval, in which bubbles would form in the standard picture of homogeneous nucleation. Thus the bubbles nucleate at cold spots. We find the typical distance between bubble centers to be a few meters. This exceeds the estimates from homogeneous nucleation by two orders of magnitude. The resulting baryon inhomogeneities may affect primordial nucleosynthesis.

# The QCD phase transition in the inhomogeneous Universe

## Key claim

The cosmological QCD phase transition may proceed via inhomogeneous nucleation rather than homogeneous nucleation, because primordial temperature fluctuations (δT/T ∼ 10⁻⁵) exceed the tiny temperature interval (Δ_nuc ∼ 10⁻⁶) in which bubbles would form in the standard picture. This conclusion depends on assuming a first-order QCD transition with parameters from quenched lattice QCD and a COBE-normalized primordial fluctuation spectrum.

## Method

The authors compare the rms temperature fluctuation Δ_T^rms (derived from COBE data and scaled for the QCD epoch) with the nucleation temperature interval Δ_nuc (calculated from quenched lattice QCD values for surface tension σ = 0.015 T_c³ and latent heat l = 1.4 T_c⁴, scaled to l = 3 T_c⁴ for physical QCD). They model bubble nucleation as occurring preferentially in cold spots of the temperature distribution, compute the fraction of space converted to hadron phase using a Gaussian probability distribution for temperature fluctuations, and derive the mean distance between nucleation centers from the number density of cold spots that act as nucleation sites.

## Key numbers

- Primordial temperature fluctuation amplitude measured by COBE: δT/T ∼ 10⁻⁵
- COBE-normalized rms temperature fluctuation at QCD transition: Δ_T^rms ≈ 2 × 10⁻⁵ (for Harrison-Zel'dovich spectrum with 3c_s² = 0.1)
- Supercooling from homogeneous nucleation: Δ_sc = 2.3 × 10⁻⁴
- Nucleation temperature interval: Δ_nuc = 1.5 × 10⁻⁶
- Duration of nucleation period: Δt_nuc = 1.5 × 10⁻⁵ t_H (with t_H the Hubble time at QCD transition)
- Homogeneous nucleation distance: d_nuc,hom = 2.9 × 10⁻⁶ d_H (for v_heat = 0.1), corresponding to ∼ 1 cm
- Smoothing scale from neutrino diffusion: l_smooth ≈ 1 m (from l_smooth = 10⁻⁴ d_H with d_H ∼ 10 km)
- Inhomogeneous nucleation distance: d_nuc,ihn = 1.4 to 4.8 × l_smooth (for l_heat/l_smooth = 1 to 10), giving a few meters
- Quenched lattice QCD values: surface tension σ = 0.015 T_c³, latent heat l = 1.4 T_c⁴ (scaled to l = 3 T_c⁴ for physical QCD)
- Sound speed at QCD transition: 3c_s²(T_c) = O(0.1) from lattice QCD

## Context

This work extends studies of the cosmological QCD phase transition by Witten (1984) and homogeneous nucleation calculations by Ignatius and collaborators, which gave nucleation distances ∼ 1 cm. It contrasts with heterogeneous nucleation via ad hoc impurities proposed by Christiansen and collaborators, which also gives meter-scale distances but by a different mechanism. The resulting baryon inhomogeneities at the meter scale may affect primordial nucleosynthesis predictions, as previously studied by Mathews and collaborators and Kainulainen and collaborators. The work enables future investigation of whether observable deviations from homogeneous nucleosynthesis element abundances can arise from this mechanism.
