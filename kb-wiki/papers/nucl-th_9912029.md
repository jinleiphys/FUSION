# Nuclear symmetry energy in presence of hyperons in the nonrelativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation

> We generalise the finite range momentum and density dependent Seyler-Blanchard nucleon-nucleon effective interaction to the case of interaction between two baryons. This effective interaction is then used to describe dense hadronic matter relevant to neutron stars in the nonrelativistic Thomas-Fermi approach. We investigate the behaviour of nuclear symmetry energy in dense nuclear and hyperon matter relevant to neutron stars. It is found that the nuclear symmetry energy always increases with density in hyperon matter unlike the situation in nuclear matter. This rising characteristic of the symmetry energy in presence of hyperons may have significant implications on the mass-radius relationship and the cooling properties of neutron stars. We have also noted that with the appearance of hyperons, the equation of state calculated in this model remains causal at high density.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy in hyperon matter always increases with density, unlike in pure nuclear matter where it falls at high density (around 4 times normal nuclear matter density); this rising behavior is attributed to the reduction of nucleon Fermi momenta when hyperons appear, causing the repulsive term in the interaction part of the symmetry energy to dominate over the attractive term.

## Method

The authors generalized the finite-range, momentum- and density-dependent Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) nucleon-nucleon effective interaction to all baryons of the SU(3) octet. They then applied this interaction in a nonrelativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation at zero temperature to describe dense hadronic matter in beta-equilibrium with leptons, solving for particle fractions, chemical potentials, and the nuclear symmetry energy in both nucleons-only and hyperon matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of normal nuclear matter: 0.1533 fm^-3
- Volume energy coefficient for symmetric nuclear matter: -16.1 MeV
- Asymmetry energy coefficient at saturation: 34 MeV
- Surface energy coefficient of symmetric nuclear matter: 18.01 MeV
- Incompressibility of normal nuclear matter: 260 MeV
- Effective mass ratio (m_N*/m_N) at normal nuclear matter density: 0.61
- Hyperons first appear at 1.5 times normal nuclear matter density
- Nuclear symmetry energy in nuclear matter starts falling around 4 times normal nuclear matter density (4 rho_0)
- The direct URCA threshold (proton fraction exceeding 11 percent) is reached around 3.0 rho_0 in hyperon matter
- Lambda particle potential in normal nuclear matter: approximately -30 MeV
- Xi particle potential in symmetric nuclear matter: approximately -25 MeV
- Hyperon-hyperon well depth: approximately -40 MeV

## Context

This work extends nonrelativistic studies of the symmetry energy by Wiringa (1988), Kutschera (1994), and Pandharipande and Garde (1972), who found that the symmetry energy falls at high density in nucleons-only matter due to tensor force and isospin-dependent repulsion. It contrasts with relativistic mean field models by Glendenning (1985) and Kutschera (1994), where the symmetry energy always increases with density. The inclusion of hyperons resolves two problems in nonrelativistic nucleons-only models: the falling symmetry energy (and consequent proton disappearance) and causality violation at high density. The rising symmetry energy in hyperon matter enables future calculations of neutron star mass-radius relationships and cooling properties via the direct URCA process.
