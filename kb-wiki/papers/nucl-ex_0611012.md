# Quark-Gluon Matter

> A concise review of the experimental and phenomenological progress in high-energy heavy-ion physics over the past few years is presented. Emphasis is put on measurements at BNL-RHIC and CERN-SPS which provide information on fundamental properties of QCD matter at extreme values of temperature, density and low-x. The new opportunities accessible at the LHC, which may help clarify some of the current open issues, are also outlined.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the experimental and phenomenological progress in high-energy heavy-ion physics, emphasizing that measurements at BNL-RHIC and CERN-SPS provide information on fundamental properties of QCD matter at extreme temperature, density, and low-x, while noting that the LHC may help clarify open issues.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the four RHIC experiments (PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS, BRAHMS) studying AuAu, dAu, and pp collisions at center-of-mass energies up to sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, along with results from NA60 InIn reactions at the CERN-SPS at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV. The review compares observables measured in heavy-ion collisions to those in proton-proton and deuteron-nucleus collisions using nuclear modification factors R_AA and R_dA to isolate hot and dense QCD medium effects from cold nuclear matter effects.

## Key numbers

* Central AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV produce dN_ch/deta at eta=0 of approximately 700 charged particles
* Bjorken energy density estimates at mid-rapidity: approximately 5 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC and 10 GeV/fm^3 at LHC, compared to the QCD critical energy density epsilon_crit approximately 1 GeV/fm^3
* The saturation momentum Q_s^2 scales as A^(1/3) x^(-lambda) with lambda approximately 0.2-0.3, making non-linear effects approximately 6 times larger in heavy nuclei (A approximately 200) than in protons
* Hard cross sections in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV cover 9 orders of magnitude from 10 mb down to 10 pb, with transverse momenta up to approximately 45 GeV/c
* At mid-rapidity in dAu collisions, nuclear modification factors deviate from unity by at most approximately 20 percent

## Context

This review extends the experimental program from CERN-SPS fixed-target experiments (NA38, NA50, NA60) to RHIC collider experiments, covering the period when RHIC provided the first definitive evidence for strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma properties. The paper contextualizes results within theoretical frameworks including lattice QCD predictions for deconfinement, perturbative QCD calculations by Vogelsang and Cacciari, Color Glass Condensate approaches by Kharzeev and Armesto, and AdS/CFT correspondence calculations of transport properties by Kovtun and Wiedemann. The review enables future comparisons with LHC heavy-ion data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV.
