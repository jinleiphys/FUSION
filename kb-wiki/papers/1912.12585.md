# Many-body correlations in nuclear superfluidity

> The two-fermion two-point correlation function in the pairing channel is discussed in the equation of motion framework. Starting from the bare two-fermion interaction, we derive the equation of motion for the two-fermion pair propagator in a strongly-correlated medium. The resulting equation is of the Dyson type with the kernel having a static and a one frequency dependent components and, thus, can be regarded as Dyson Bethe-Salpeter equation (Dyson-BSE). The many-body hierarchy generated by the dynamical interaction kernel is truncated on the level of two-body correlation functions, thus neglecting the explicit three-body and higher-rank correlations. The truncation is performed via a cluster expansion of the intermediate three-particle-one-hole correlation function irreducible in the particle-particle channel, that leads to the coupling between single fermions and emergent collective modes of excitation. The latter couplings are, thus, derived in terms of the exact mapping of the in-medium two-fermion correlation functions onto the domain of bosonic quasibound states (phonons) without introducing new parameters. The approach is applied to calculations of the pairing gaps in medium-mass nuclear systems, that include calcium, nickel and tin isotopic chains.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Dyson Bethe-Salpeter equation (Dyson-BSE) for the two-fermion pair propagator in the pairing channel, where the many-body hierarchy is truncated at the level of two-body correlation functions via a cluster expansion of the intermediate three-particle-one-hole correlation function, thereby coupling single fermions to emergent bosonic phonons without introducing new parameters.

## Method

The authors start from the bare two-fermion interaction and derive the equation of motion for the two-time two-fermion propagator in the particle-particle channel. The resulting Dyson-type equation contains a static kernel (composed of the bare interaction, screening via particle-hole correlations, and single-particle energy renormalization) and a one-frequency dependent dynamical kernel. The dynamical kernel is approximated by factorizing the irreducible three-particle-one-hole propagator into products of particle-particle and particle-hole correlation functions, truncating the hierarchy at the two-body level. The approach is applied to calculate pairing gaps in medium-mass nuclear systems.

## Context

This work extends the equation-of-motion framework previously applied to the particle-hole channel in Litvinova and Schuck (2019) to the particle-particle channel for nuclear pairing. It provides a microscopic foundation for phenomenological nuclear field theories (NFT) and quasiparticle-phonon models (QPM) such as those of Bohr and Mottelson (1969, 1975), Broglia (1976), and Soloviev (1992), connecting the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction to emergent collective modes. The approach enables systematic inclusion of higher-order correlations beyond the conventional particle-vibration coupling models, such as two-quasiparticle-plus-two-phonon configurations, which are necessary for spectroscopic accuracy in medium-mass and heavy nuclei.
