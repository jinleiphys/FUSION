# Forced Tunneling and Turning State Explosion in Pure Yang-Mills Theory

> We consider forced tunneling in QCD, described semiclassically by instanton-antiinstanton field configurations. By separating topologically different minima we obtain details of the effective potential and study the turning states, which are similar to the sphaleron solution in electroweak theory. These states are alternatively derived as minima of the energy under the constraints of fixed size and Chern-Simons number. We study, both analytically and numerically, the subsequent evolution of such states by solving the classical Yang-Mills equations in real time, and find that the gauge field strength is quickly localized into an expanding shell of radiating gluons. The relevance to high-energy collisions of hadrons and nuclei is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Forced tunneling in pure Yang-Mills theory, described by instanton-antiinstanton configurations, produces unstable "turning states" that evolve into expanding shells of radiating gluons; the paper shows that these states are energy minima under constraints of fixed size and Chern-Simons number, but the analysis is limited to spherically symmetric SU(2) fields and the Yung ansatz, which the authors note is only one possible set of forced paths.

## Method

The authors construct instanton-antiinstanton (I-antiI) configurations using the Yung ansatz, which solves the streamline equation and provides a set of forced tunneling paths. They extract static turning states at t=0 (where the chromoelectric field vanishes) and compute their energy and Chern-Simons number as functions of the I-antiI separation T. Separately, they derive turning states via constrained minimization of the Yang-Mills energy functional with fixed r.m.s. size and Chern-Simons number, solving the resulting Euler-Lagrange equations analytically. Finally, they use these static configurations as initial conditions for real-time classical Yang-Mills evolution in 3+1 dimensions, solving the equations of motion numerically.

## Key numbers

- The instanton size is taken as ρ ∼ 1/3 fm, corresponding to the semi-hard scale Q ∼ 1–2 GeV.
- The cross section for instanton-induced cluster production peaks at an invariant cluster mass in the range 2.5–3 GeV (citing Kharzeev, Kovchegov, Levin and Nowak, Shuryak, Zahed).
- The Yung ansatz yields Chern-Simons numbers N_CS spanning the full range [0,1] as the I-antiI separation T varies from ∞ to 0.
- The analytic turning state solution gives potential energy E_B = 3π² (1−κ²)² / (g²ρ), where κ is a Lagrange multiplier with −1 < κ < 1.
- The corrected Chern-Simons number for the analytic solution is Ñ_CS = (1/4) sign(κ) (2+|κ|)(1−|κ|)².
- The sphaleron solution corresponds to κ=0, with φ(r)=|r²−ρ²|/(r²+ρ²) and α(r)=πθ(r−ρ).

## Context

This work extends the study of instanton-induced processes in QCD, building on earlier electroweak theory results from the 1980s and 1990s (Manton, Klinkhamer and Manton) and on QCD instanton studies by Callan, Dashen, and Gross (1978) and Shuryak (1988). It directly follows proposals by Kharzeev, Kovchegov, and Levin and by Nowak, Shuryak, and Zahed that topological tunneling underlies the "soft" pomeron in high-energy hadronic collisions. The paper provides a semiclassical description of the produced colored clusters, turning states analogous to electroweak sphalerons, and their subsequent real-time decay into gluon shells, enabling future calculations of multi-gluon production in hadron and nucleus collisions.
