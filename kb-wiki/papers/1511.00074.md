# Nuclear Matrix Elements for Double-Beta Decay

> Recent progress in nuclear-structure theory has been dramatic. I describe recent and future applications of ab initio calculations and the generator coordinate method to double-beta decay. I also briefly discuss the old and vexing problem of the renormalization of the weak nuclear axial-vector coupling constant "in medium" and plans to resolve it.

## Key claim

Recent progress in nuclear-structure theory has been dramatic, but the nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay computed in different models still vary by factors of two or three; the paper argues that linking model Hamiltonians and decay operators to data through ab initio calculations can both quantify and reduce this uncertainty.

## Method

The paper describes two complementary approaches: (1) a program to derive effective shell-model interactions and decay operators from ab initio coupled-cluster calculations in closed-shell-plus-one and closed-shell-plus-two nuclei via a Lee-Suzuki mapping, starting with oxygen isotopes; (2) an extension of the generator coordinate method (GCM) that incorporates proton-neutron pairing correlations by mixing mean fields with constraints on both quadrupole moment and proton-neutron pairing amplitude, using a semi-realistic Hamiltonian in a two-oscillator-shell model space for 36 nucleons in ⁷⁶Ge.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear radius used to make the matrix element dimensionless is defined as R ≡ 1.2 A^(1/3) fm.
- For ⁷⁶Ge decay, the GCM collective wave functions for the proton-neutron pairing amplitude φ = ⟨P₀ + P₀†⟩ peak around φ = 4 or 5 in both ⁷⁶Ge and ⁷⁶Se.
- The QRPA matrix element for ⁷⁶Ge decay "blows up" when the proton-neutron pairing strength g_pn is near 1.5 (a realistic value), while the GCM behaves smoothly at that point.
- The ab initio derived shell-model interaction for oxygen isotopes, using only data in two- and three-nucleon systems, produces spectra at least as good as the phenomenological USD interaction that was fit to data in the sd shell itself.

## Context

This work extends the venerable shell model and generator coordinate method approaches to double-beta decay matrix elements, which have previously been computed in models that vary by factors of two or three. The ab initio shell-model program builds on coupled-cluster calculations by Jansen (2011) and the in-medium similarity renormalization group by Tsukiyama (2012), while the GCM with proton-neutron pairing extends earlier GCM calculations by Rodriguez (2010, 2011) that used the Gogny interaction but omitted proton-neutron correlations. The paper also addresses the long-standing problem of g_A quenching in beta decay, known since Brown (1988), and argues that chiral effective field theory many-body currents and model-space truncation effects can now be investigated systematically to resolve whether neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements are similarly quenched.
