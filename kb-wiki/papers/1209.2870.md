# The strangeness content of the nucleon from effective field theory and phenomenology

> We revisit the classical relation between the strangeness content of the nucleon, the pion-nucleon sigma term and the $SU(3)_F$ breaking of the baryon masses in the context of Lorentz covariant chiral perturbation theory with explicit decuplet-baryon resonance fields. We find that a value of the pion-nucleon sigma term of $\sim$60 MeV is not necessarily at odds with a small strangeness content of the nucleon, in line with the fulfillment of the OZI rule. Moreover, this value is indeed favored by our next-to-leading order calculation. We compare our results with earlier ones and discuss the convergence of the chiral series as well as the uncertainties of chiral approaches to the determination of the sigma terms.

# The strangeness content of the nucleon from effective field theory and phenomenology

## Key claim
A pion-nucleon sigma term of approximately 60 MeV is not necessarily incompatible with a small strangeness content of the nucleon (consistent with the OZI rule), provided that relativistic corrections and explicit decuplet-baryon resonance contributions are properly accounted for in the chiral expansion of sigma_0.

## Method
The authors calculate sigma_0 (the nucleon expectation value of the octet operator) up to next-to-leading order (NLO) using Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BchiPT) renormalized in the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme, including explicit decuplet-baryon resonance fields. They apply the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to the chiral expansion of the baryon masses, using experimental baryon-octet mass splittings to determine the low-energy constants b_D and b_F, and then use two phenomenological determinations of sigma_piN (45(7) MeV and 59(7) MeV) as input to extract the remaining constant b_0 and predict y and sigma_s. Systematic higher-order uncertainties are estimated by partial calculation of NNLO pieces.

## Key numbers
- sigma_0 = 58(8) MeV from Lorentz covariant BchiPT with octet+decuplet at NLO (compared to 27 MeV at tree level)
- For sigma_piN = 59(7) MeV as input: y = 0.02(13)(10) and sigma_s = 16(80)(60) MeV
- For sigma_piN = 45(7) MeV as input: y = -0.28(13)(10) and sigma_s = -150(80)(60) MeV
- Estimated NNLO systematic uncertainties: Delta_sigma_piN^EOMS = 6 MeV, Delta_sigma_s^EOMS = 60 MeV
- Estimated NNLO systematic uncertainties in HB: Delta_sigma_piN^HB = 20 MeV, Delta_sigma_s^HB = 140 MeV
- Decuplet contributions produce approximately 10 MeV rise in sigma_0 compared to octet-only calculations
- b_D = 0.161(4) GeV^{-1} and b_F = -0.502(2) GeV^{-1} in the covariant octet+decuplet scheme

## Context
This work revisits the classical relation between sigma_piN, sigma_s, and the strangeness content y, originally derived from the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relation by Cheng (1975). It extends earlier calculations by Gasser (1980) and Borasoy (1996, 1998) who obtained sigma_0 around 35-36 MeV using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBchiPT) with or without decuplet resonances. The authors show that those earlier results suffer from poor convergence of the HB expansion in the SU(3)_F sector, and that the covariant EOMS approach with explicit decuplet fields yields a larger sigma_0, resolving the apparent tension between modern sigma_piN determinations near 60 MeV and a small strangeness content. This enables consistent interpretation of both piN scattering data and lattice QCD results (Durr 2011, Alexandrou) that favor small y.
