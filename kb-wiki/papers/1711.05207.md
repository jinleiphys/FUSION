# Effect of the QCD equation of state and strange hadronic resonances on multiparticle correlations in heavy ion collisions

> The QCD equation of state at zero baryon chemical potential is the only element of the standard dynamical framework to describe heavy ion collisions that can be directly determined from first principles. Continuum extrapolated lattice QCD equations of state have been computed using 2+1 quark flavors (up/down and strange) as well as 2+1+1 flavors to investigate the effect of thermalized charm quarks on QCD thermodynamics. Lattice results have also indicated the presence of new strange resonances that not only contribute to the equation of state of QCD matter but also affect hadronic afterburners used to model the later stages of heavy ion collisions. We investigate how these new developments obtained from first principles calculations affect multiparticle correlations in heavy ion collisions. We compare the commonly used equation of state S95n-v1, which was constructed using what are now considered outdated lattice results and hadron states, to the current state-of-the-art lattice QCD equations of state with 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavors coupled to the most up-to-date hadronic resonances and their decays. New hadronic resonances lead to an enhancement in the hadronic spectra at intermediate $p_T$. Using an outdated equation of state can directly affect the extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$, of the quark-gluon plasma and results for different flow observables. The effects of the QCD equation of state on multiparticle correlations of identified particles are determined for both AuAu $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and PbPb $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV collisions. New insights into the $v_2\{2\}$ to $v_3\{2\}$ puzzle in ultracentral collisions are found. Flow observables of heavier particles exhibit more non-linear behavior regardless of the assumptions about the equation of state, which may provide a new way to constrain the temperature dependence of $η/s$.

## Key claim

The choice of QCD equation of state (EoS) and the inclusion of updated strange hadronic resonances significantly affect multiparticle correlations in heavy ion collisions, with the commonly used S95n-v1 EoS leading to an extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio that can differ by nearly 50% compared to using state-of-the-art lattice QCD EoS with 2+1 or 2+1+1 flavors, though the absolute values of eta/s depend on simplifying assumptions such as equal chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures.

## Method

The authors constructed new QCD equations of state by combining continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (for 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavors) with a Hadron Resonance Gas model using the most up-to-date PDG16+ particle list (including *-**** states) at low temperatures, matched via a smoothing function. They performed event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the v-USPhydro code with TRENTO initial conditions, followed by hadronic decays from the full PDG16+ resonance list, and compared results to those obtained using the older S95n-v1 EoS with PDG05 resonances for both AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The extracted eta/s for AuAu 200 GeV is 0.05 for all three equations of state (PDG05/S95n-v1, PDG16+/2+1[WB], PDG16+/2+1+1[WB])
- For PbPb 5.02 TeV, the extracted eta/s values are: 0.025 for PDG05/S95n-v1 (with tau_0=0.4 fm, T_FO=145 MeV), 0.047 for PDG16+/2+1[WB], and 0.04 for PDG16+/2+1+1[WB]
- Comparing PDG05/S95n-v1 to PDG16+/WB2+1 at LHC run 2 requires an 88% increase in eta/s to match experimental data
- The ratio v_2{2}/v_3{2} in ultracentral collisions (0-1% centrality) at LHC run 2 is still 15-20% above experimental data when using the updated equation of state
- The matching temperature between HRG and lattice is T_HRG/Latt=153 MeV, with smoothing parameter a=0.1 MeV^-1
- The transition to pion gas occurs at T_HRG+Latt/pi=33.5 MeV with smoothing parameter b=1 MeV^-1
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at tau_0=0.6 fm for both RHIC and LHC (except PDG05/S95n-v1 at LHC where tau_0=0.4 fm)
- Freeze-out temperature T_SW=150 MeV for all cases except PDG05/S95n-v1 at LHC where T_SW=145 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies that used the S95n-v1 EoS from Huovinen (2009), which was based on non-continuum-extrapolated lattice results and an outdated PDG 2005 hadron list. The authors incorporate recent continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2013, Borsanyi 2016) and the PDG16+ resonance list, building on earlier work by Alba (2017) that showed the need for additional strange states to match lattice susceptibilities. The findings enable more accurate extraction of transport coefficients like eta/s from experimental data and provide new insights into the v_2{2}/v_3{2} puzzle in ultracentral collisions, while also suggesting that flow observables of heavier particles may help constrain the temperature dependence of eta/s.
