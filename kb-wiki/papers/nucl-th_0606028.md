# Small eta-N scattering lengths favour eta-d and eta-alpha states

> Unstable states of the eta meson and the 3He nucleus predicted using the time delay method were found to be in agreement with a recent claim of eta-mesic 3He states made by the TAPS collaboration. Here, we extend this method to a speculative study of the unstable states occurring in the eta-d and eta-4He elastic scattering. The T-matrix for eta-4He scattering is evaluated within the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of few body equations. For the evaluation of time delay in the eta-d case, we use a parameterization of an existing Faddeev calculation and compare the results with those obtained from FRA. With an eta-N scattering length, $a_{ηN} = (0.42, 0.34)$ fm, we find an eta-d unstable bound state around -16 MeV, within the Faddeev calculation. A similar state within the FRA is found for a low value of $a_{ηN}$, namely, $a_{ηN} = (0.28, 0.19)$ fm. The existence of an eta-4He unstable bound state close to threshold is hinted by $a_{ηN} = (0.28, 0.19)$ fm, but is ruled out by large scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The existence of unstable bound states (quasibound states) in the η-d and η-⁴He systems depends critically on the value of the η-N scattering length, with smaller scattering lengths favoring such states while larger scattering lengths rule them out.

## Method

The authors extended the time delay method (based on the energy derivative of scattering phase shifts) to negative energies to search for unstable bound states in η-d and η-⁴He elastic scattering. For the η-d system, they used both a Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of few-body equations and a parameterization of an existing relativistic Faddeev calculation. For the η-⁴He system, they evaluated the T-matrix within the FRA using two different models of the η-N interaction that yield different η-N scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- η-d unstable bound state at approximately -16 MeV (binding energy) found using the Faddeev calculation with η-N scattering length a_ηN = (0.42, 0.34) fm
- η-d unstable bound state around -12 MeV found within the FRA with a_ηN = (0.28, 0.19) fm
- η-⁴He scattering lengths: a_η⁴He = (-3.94, 5.575) fm for a_ηN = (0.88, 0.41) fm; a_η⁴He = (1.678, 1.524) fm for a_ηN = (0.28, 0.19) fm
- η-d scattering lengths: a_ηd = (1.52, 2.57) fm for a_ηN = (0.88, 0.41) fm; a_ηd = (0.67, 0.42) fm for a_ηN = (0.28, 0.19) fm
- η-⁴He unstable bound state hinted close to threshold for a_ηN = (0.28, 0.19) fm, but ruled out by large scattering lengths

## Context

This work extends the time delay method previously applied by the same authors to the η-³He system (Kelkar et al., 2005), which had found agreement with the TAPS collaboration's claim of η-mesic ³He states. The study addresses the long-standing uncertainty in the η-N interaction, where different models (such as those by Bhalerao and Liu (1985) giving a_ηN = (0.28, 0.19) fm, and Fix et al. giving a_ηN = (0.88, 0.41) fm) produce conflicting predictions for η-mesic nuclei. The results enable future experimental searches for η-mesic states in light nuclei, particularly with the COSY-GEM collaboration's ongoing program, and suggest that the FRA approximation is reasonable for small η-N scattering lengths but unreliable for larger ones.
