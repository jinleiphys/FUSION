# Gamma-delayed deuteron emission of the 6Li (0+;T=1) halo state

> M1 transitions from the $^6$Li($0^+;T=1$) state at 3.563 MeV to the $^6$Li($1^+$) ground state and to the $α+d$ continuum are studied in a three-body model. The bound states are described as an $α+n+p$ system in hyperspherical coordinates on a Lagrange mesh. The ground-state magnetic moment and the gamma width of the $^6$Li(0$^+$) resonance are well reproduced. The halo-like structure of the $^6$Li$(0^+)$ resonance is confirmed and is probed by the M1 transition probability to the $α+d$ continuum. The spectrum is sensitive to the description of the $α+d$ phase shifts. The corresponding gamma width is around 1.0 meV, with optimal potentials. Charge symmetry is analyzed through a comparison with the $β$-delayed deuteron spectrum of $^6$He. In $^6$He, a nearly perfect cancellation effect between short-range and halo contributions was found. A similar analysis for the $^6$Li($0^+;T=1$) $γ$ decay is performed; it shows that charge-symmetry breaking at large distances, due to the different binding energies and to different charges, reduces this effect. The present branching ratio $Γ_γ(0^+\to α+d)/Γ_γ(0^+\to1^+)\approx 1.3\times 10^{-4}$ should be observable with current experimental facilities.

## Key claim

The M1 transition from the \(^6\)Li(0\(^+\);T=1) halo state to the \(\alpha+d\) continuum has a branching ratio of approximately \(1.3\times 10^{-4}\) relative to the ground-state transition, but this value is sensitive to the choice of \(\alpha+d\) potential and the cancellation between short-range and halo contributions is less complete than in the analogous \(^6\)He \(\beta\)-delayed deuteron emission due to charge-symmetry breaking at large distances.

## Method

The authors describe the \(^6\)Li bound states (ground state and 0\(^+\) excited state) as an \(\alpha+n+p\) three-body system using hyperspherical coordinates solved on a Lagrange mesh, with the Minnesota potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and a renormalized \(\alpha+N\) potential from Voronchev et al. (1995). The \(\alpha+d\) continuum wave functions are factorized into a deuteron ground-state wave function and an \(\alpha+d\) scattering wave function derived from four different two-body potentials (deep Gaussian, supersymmetric transform, and two folding potentials). The M1 transition probability per time and energy units to the continuum is computed from reduced matrix elements of the M1 operator between the three-body bound state and the two-body scattering states, integrated over the \(\alpha+d\) relative energy up to the Q-value of 2.089 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The \(^6\)Li(0\(^+\)) state lies at 3.563 MeV excitation energy; its matter r.m.s. radius is 2.56 fm (with 1.4 fm \(\alpha\) radius), confirming its halo structure.
- The ground-state magnetic moment is calculated as 0.86 \(\mu_N\) (experimental: 0.82 \(\mu_N\)); the M1 transition to the ground state gives \(\Gamma_{\gamma}=7.5\) eV (experimental: \(8.19\pm 0.17\) eV).
- The integrated \(\gamma\) width for the transition to the \(\alpha+d\) continuum is 0.90 meV with the recommended potential \(V_a\), 1.08 meV with \(V_{f2}\), and 2.27 meV with the supersymmetric potential \(V^{S1}_a\).
- The recommended branching ratio \(\Gamma_{\gamma}(0^+\rightarrow \alpha+d)/\Gamma_{\gamma}(0^+\rightarrow 1^+)\) is \(1.3\times 10^{-4}\), averaged from the \(V_a\) and \(V_{f2}\) results (\(1.2\times 10^{-4}\) and \(1.4\times 10^{-4}\) respectively).
- The overlap between the \(^6\)He and \(^6\)Li(0\(^+\)) wave functions is 0.996, close to the value 0.995 from Arai et al. (1995).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body study of \(^6\)He \(\beta\)-delayed deuteron emission (Tursunov et al., 2006) to the analogous \(\gamma\)-delayed process in \(^6\)Li, testing charge symmetry in halo nuclei. The results are compared with an earlier estimate by Csoto et al. (1998) who obtained a branching ratio of \(0.8\times 10^{-4}\) using a simplified model. The calculation shows that the halo contribution dominates the matrix element in \(^6\)Li, unlike the near-perfect cancellation seen in \(^6\)He, and that the branching ratio of about \(1.3\times 10^{-4}\) should be observable with current experimental facilities, enabling a combined test of halo structure in isobaric analog states.
