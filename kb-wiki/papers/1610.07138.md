# Higher moments of multiplicity fluctuations in a hadron-resonance gas with exact conservation laws

> Higher moments of multiplicity fluctuations of hadrons produced in central nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied within the hadron-resonance gas model in the canonical ensemble. Exact conservation of three charges, baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, is enforced in the large volume limit. Moments up to the forth order of various particles are calculated at SPS, RHIC and LHC energies. The asymptotic fluctuations within a simplified model with only one conserved charge in the canonical ensemble are discussed where simple analytical expressions for moments of multiplicity distributions can be obtained. Moments products of net-proton, net-kaon, and net-charge distributions in Au + Au collisions at RHIC energies are calculated. The pseudo-rapidity coverage dependence of net-charge fluctuation is discussed.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first calculation of higher-order (third and fourth) moments of multiplicity fluctuations in a hadron-resonance gas model using the canonical ensemble with exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, rather than the more common grand canonical ensemble. The canonical ensemble results show systematically larger suppression of fluctuations relative to Poisson expectations than grand canonical results, with the suppression increasing for higher-order moments.

## Method
The authors extended the THERMUS thermal model program to compute the first four cumulants of multiplicity distributions in the canonical ensemble, enforcing exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in the large-volume limit. They calculated moments up to fourth order for various hadron species and for net-proton, net-kaon, and net-charge distributions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, using freeze-out parameters determined from the phenomenological relation between collision energy and chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential from Cleymans (2006). Resonance decay contributions to final-state particle fluctuations were included using generating function techniques.

## Key numbers
- Primordial net-proton $\kappa\sigma^2$ in the CE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV: -0.036, compared to 0.952 in the GCE
- Final net-proton $\kappa\sigma^2$ in the CE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV: 0.664, compared to 0.993 in the GCE
- Final net-kaon $\sigma^2/M$ in the CE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV: 1.328, compared to 2.012 in the GCE
- Final net-charge $\sigma^2/M$ in the CE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV: 7.299, compared to 75.705 in the GCE
- Final net-charge $\kappa\sigma^2$ in the CE at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV: 1.325, compared to 1.806 in the GCE
- Temperature at chemical freeze-out ranges from 94.7 MeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.32$ GeV to 163.8 MeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760$ GeV
- Baryon density ranges from 0.093 fm$^{-3}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.32$ GeV to 0.0008 fm$^{-3}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760$ GeV

## Context
This work extends previous hadron-resonance gas calculations of multiplicity fluctuations, which were mostly performed in the grand canonical ensemble by Jinghua (2009, 2013), Subhasis (2014), Nahrgang (2014, 2015), Mohanty (2016), and Karsch and Redlich (2016). The canonical ensemble treatment is motivated by the fact that at lower collision energies a larger fraction of conserved charge is observed, making exact conservation laws important. The results provide a theoretical baseline for interpreting Beam Energy Scan data from the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations at RHIC, where measurements of net-proton, net-kaon, and net-charge moments products have been reported by STAR (2014) and PHENIX (2016). The canonical ensemble results show that higher-order moments are more strongly suppressed than lower-order ones, which could help distinguish baseline hadronic physics from critical point signatures in future experimental comparisons.
