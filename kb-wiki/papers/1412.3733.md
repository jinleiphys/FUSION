# Dilepton Production in Transport-based Approaches

> We investigate dilepton production in transport-based approaches and show that the baryon couplings of the $ρ$ meson represent the most important ingredient for understanding the measured dilepton spectra. At SIS energies, the baryon resonances naturally play a major role and affect already the vacuum spectra via Dalitz-like contributions, which can be captured well in transport simulations. Recent pion-beam measurements at GSI will help to constrain the properties of the involved resonances further.

# Dilepton Production in Transport-based Approaches

## Key claim
The baryon couplings of the rho meson are the most important ingredient for understanding measured dilepton spectra at SIS energies, but the authors' model relies on the assumption of strict vector-meson dominance (VMD) for the electromagnetic transition form factors of baryonic resonances, which are essentially unknown in the time-like region from experiment.

## Method
The authors used the GiBUU hadronic transport model to simulate dilepton production in p+p, Ar+KCl, and pion-induced collisions. Dalitz decays of baryonic resonances (N* and Delta*) into a lepton pair and a nucleon were treated as a two-step process: first an R -> rho N decay using branching ratios from the partial-wave analysis by Manley (1992), then a subsequent rho -> e+e- conversion under strict VMD. The VMD assumption was extended to the Delta(1232) state in this work.

## Key numbers
- p+p collisions at E_kin = 3.5 GeV: the simulated dilepton mass spectrum shows a clear omega peak and a hint of a phi peak, with the intermediate-mass regime dominated by baryonic contributions from N* and Delta* resonances
- Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76 AGeV: the model underestimates data at intermediate masses and shows a slight excess in the vector-meson pole region
- Pion-induced reactions at E_kin = 0.566 GeV (p_lab = 0.690 GeV, sqrt(s) = 1.49 GeV): the D13(1520) resonance dominates the high-mass yield, with contributions from other resonances like S11(1535) and Delta(1232) suppressed by roughly an order of magnitude in elementary pi-p reactions

## Context
This work extends previous GiBUU transport investigations by Weil (2012) that showed baryonic N* and Delta* resonances give important Dalitz-like contributions at SIS energies. It builds on the NA60 experiment at CERN SPS, which revealed strong collisional broadening of the rho spectral function driven by baryonic effects as calculated by van Hees (2006). The results contradict claims by Bratkovskaya (2013) of a much more dominant role of the Delta(1232). The pion-beam measurements at GSI may help constrain resonance properties and possibly measure the electromagnetic transition form factor of the D13(1520), which could test the VMD assumption against models that predict strong destructive interference effects (Lutz, 2002; Zetenyi, 2012).
