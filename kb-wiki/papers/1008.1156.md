# Chaotic behavior in classical Yang-Mills dynamics

> Understanding the underlying mechanisms causing rapid thermalization deduced for high-energy heavy ion collisions is still a challenge. To estimate the thermalization time, entropy growth for classical Yang-Mills theories is studied, based on the determination of Lyapunov exponents. Distinct regimes for short, medium and long sampling times are characterized by different properties of their spectrum of Lyapunov exponents. Clarifying the existence of these regimes and their implications for gauge-field dynamics is one of the results of this contribution. As a phenomenological application we conclude that for pure gauge theories with random initial conditions thermalization occurs within few fm/c, an estimate which can be reduced by the inclusion of fermions, specific initial conditions, etc.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for classical Yang-Mills theories with random initial conditions, thermalization occurs within a few fm/c, but this estimate is explicitly conditional: it applies only to pure gauge theories and can be reduced by the inclusion of fermions or specific initial conditions.

## Method

The authors numerically simulate classical Yang-Mills (CYM) evolution on a spatial lattice using the non-compact formulation in the temporal gauge, with SU(2) and SU(3) gauge groups. They compute three types of Lyapunov exponents: local Lyapunov exponents (LLE) from the instantaneous Hessian eigenvalues, intermediate Lyapunov exponents (ILE) from the time-evolution operator constructed via the Trotter formula over finite time intervals, and global Lyapunov exponents (GLE) for ergodic time scales. The Kolmogorov-Sinai (KS) entropy is estimated as the sum of positive ILEs, and the equilibration time is obtained from the ratio of equilibrium entropy density to KS entropy density.

## Key numbers

- On a 4^3 lattice with energy density ε = 0.014 (lattice units), the exponential growth rate of the distance between trajectories in the intermediate time region (50 < t < 120) is λ_D ~ 0.04.
- The maximum ILE for t > 50 is λ_max^ILE ~ 0.04, matching the distance growth rate λ_D.
- The KS entropy in lattice units scales as S_KS^(L) = c_KS L^3 (ε^L)^(1/4), with c_KS ≈ 0.75 for SU(2).
- The physical equilibration time is given by τ_eq ≥ 2(N_c^2 - 1) (4 C_L θ)/(3 c_KS T) (ε^L)^(-1/4), where θ is a numerical constant of order unity and C_L is a numerical coefficient of order unity.
- The classical energy density on the lattice relates to temperature as ε_cl(T) = 2(N_c^2 - 1) C_L T / a^3, while the quantum thermal energy density is ε(T) = 2(N_c^2 - 1) (π^2/30) T^4.

## Context

This work extends the KMOS framework (Kunihiro, Muller, Ohnishi, Schafer, 2008) from simple one-degree-of-freedom systems to realistic classical Yang-Mills dynamics with many degrees of freedom. It builds on earlier studies of chaos in Yang-Mills fields by Matinyan (1986), Muller (1992), and Bolte (1999), and on the observation by Fries, Muller and Schafer (2008) that decoherence alone generates only a fraction of the entropy needed for hydrodynamic treatment. The results enable estimation of thermalization times in heavy-ion collisions and support a picture of rapid pre-thermalization via infrared mode growth, consistent with bottom-up thermalization scenarios (Baier, 2000).
