# Recent progress constraining the nuclear equation of state from astrophysics and heavy ion reactions

> The quest for the nuclear equation of state (EoS) at high densities and/or extreme isospin is one of the longstanding problems of nuclear physics. Ab initio calculations for the nuclear many-body problem make predictions for the density and isospin dependence of the EoS far away from the saturation point of nuclear matter. On the other hand, in recent years substantial progress has been mode to constrain the EoS both, from the astrophysical side and from accelerator based experiments. Heavy ion experiments support a soft EoS at moderate densities while recent neutron star observations require a ``stiff'' high density behavior. Both constraints are discussed and shown to be in agreement with the predictions from many-body theory.

# Recent progress constraining the nuclear equation of state from astrophysics and heavy ion reactions

## Key claim
Heavy ion reactions support a soft equation of state (EoS) at moderate densities (up to 2-3 times saturation density), while neutron star observations require a stiff EoS at high densities; these constraints are complementary rather than contradictory, and both are consistent with ab initio many-body calculations.

## Method
The paper reviews constraints on the nuclear EoS from two complementary sources: (1) subthreshold K+ meson production in relativistic heavy ion collisions (Au+Au and C+C at SIS100/GSI energies 0.1-2 AGeV), analyzed with RQMD and IQMD transport models using Skyrme-type forces with empirical momentum dependence; and (2) neutron star mass measurements, particularly PSR J0751+1807, combined with the Direct Urca cooling constraint. The results are compared to non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF), Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF), and variational many-body calculations.

## Key numbers
- The most massive neutron star observed (PSR J0751+1807) has a mass of 2.1 ± 0.2 (1σ) or +0.4/-0.5 (2σ) solar masses, requiring a reliable EoS to describe neutron star masses of at least 1.9 solar masses (1σ) or 1.6 solar masses (2σ).
- Heavy ion data from the KaoS Collaboration favor a soft EoS with compression modulus K = 200 MeV over a hard EoS with K = 380 MeV, based on the ratio of K+ multiplicities in Au+Au over C+C reactions.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to approximately 31 MeV from NSCL-MSU heavy ion data and low energy charge exchange reactions.
- Microscopic many-body calculations (DBHF, BHF, variational) yield maximum neutron star masses between 2.1 and 2.3 solar masses.
- The Direct Urca process should not occur below an upper mass limit of 1.5 solar masses (1.35 solar masses in a weak interpretation) for typical neutron stars.
- Heavy ion reactions probe densities up to 2-3 times saturation density (rho_0) at bombarding energies between 0.1 and 2 AGeV.

## Context
This review (Fuchs, 2007) synthesizes constraints from two traditionally separate lines of research: heavy ion reaction studies (KaoS Collaboration data on subthreshold kaon production) and neutron star astrophysics (PSR J0751+1807 mass measurement by Nice et al., 2005). The paper reconciles the apparent tension between a soft EoS from heavy ion data and a stiff EoS required by massive neutron stars by noting they constrain different density regimes. It extends earlier work by Danielewicz et al. (2000) on flow observables and Lattimer et al. (1991) on the Direct Urca process, and shows consistency with ab initio calculations from Akmal et al. (1998) and van Dalen et al. (2004). The review enables future work at FAIR/GSI, SPIRAL2/GANIL, and RIA to further constrain the symmetry energy at supra-normal densities, which remains largely unconstrained.
