# Chiral-even axial twist-3 GPDs of the proton from lattice QCD

> This work presents the first lattice-QCD calculation of the twist-3 axial quark GPDs for the proton using the large-momentum effective theory approach. We calculate matrix elements with momentum-boosted proton states and a non-local axial operator. The calculation is performed using one ensemble of two degenerate light, a strange and a charm quark ($N_f=2+1+1$) of maximally twisted mass fermions with a clover term. The ensemble has a volume $32^3\times64$, lattice spacing 0.0934 fm, and corresponds to a pion mass of 260 MeV. The matrix elements are calculated for three values of the proton momentum, namely 0.83, 1.25, and 1.67 GeV. The light-cone GPDs are defined in the symmetric frame, which we implement here with a (negative) 4-momentum transfer squared of 0.69, 1.38, and 2.76 GeV$^2$, all at zero skewness. We also conduct several consistency checks, including assessing the local limit of the twist-3 GPDs and examining the Burkhardt-Cottingham-type as well as Efremov-Teryaev-Leader-type sum rules.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice-QCD calculation of the chiral-even axial twist-3 generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton, using the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach, but only for the specific case of zero skewness and at a single lattice spacing and pion mass (260 MeV), with results that are not yet extrapolated to the physical point or continuum limit.

## Method

The authors computed off-forward matrix elements of a non-local axial operator with momentum-boosted proton states on a single \(N_f=2+1+1\) ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions with a clover term (volume \(32^3\times64\), lattice spacing 0.0934 fm, pion mass 260 MeV). Matrix elements were calculated for three proton momenta (0.83, 1.25, and 1.67 GeV) and three values of 4-momentum transfer squared (0.69, 1.38, and 2.76 GeV\(^2\)) at zero skewness in the symmetric frame. The quasi-GPDs were extracted from the matrix elements using a numerical inversion of the decomposition equations, renormalized non-perturbatively in the RI' scheme, and converted to the light-cone GPDs via a one-loop matching kernel in LaMET, with the \(x\)-dependence reconstructed using the Backus-Gilbert method.

## Key numbers

*   Pion mass of the ensemble: 260 MeV.
*   Lattice spacing: 0.0934 fm; volume: \(32^3 \times 64\).
*   Proton momenta used: 0.83, 1.25, and 1.67 GeV.
*   Values of (negative) 4-momentum transfer squared: 0.69, 1.38, and 2.76 GeV\(^2\), all at zero skewness.
*   Renormalization scale: 2 GeV (modified \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior lattice calculations of twist-3 parton distribution functions (PDFs) like \(g_T\) and \(h_L\) (Bhattacharya, 2020; Bhattacharya, 2021) to the more complex case of generalized parton distributions, and builds on their recent progress in computing twist-2 GPDs using asymmetric frames (Bhattacharya, 2022). It provides the first lattice-QCD determination of the four twist-3 axial GPDs (\(\widetilde{G}_1\), \(\widetilde{G}_2\), \(\widetilde{G}_3\), \(\widetilde{G}_4\)), enabling future studies of quark orbital angular momentum, transverse forces, and spin-orbit correlations in the nucleon, and can be compared with phenomenological extractions from deeply-virtual Compton scattering experiments.
