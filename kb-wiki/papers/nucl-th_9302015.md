# Effective Hamiltonians with Relativistic Corrections II: Application to Compton Scattering by a Proton

> We discuss two different methods of obtaining ``effective $2 \times 2$ Hamiltonians'' of the electromagnetic interaction which include relativistic corrections. One is the standard Foldy--Wouthuysen transformation which we compare with the Hamiltonian obtained from a direct reduction of the matrix element of the interaction Hamiltonian between positive--energy solutions of the free Dirac equation. The two approaches are applied to Compton scattering by a proton for which a low--energy theorem exists. It is found that the Foldy--Wouthuysen Hamiltonian yields the same result as a covariant calculation. This is not true for the direct reduction method which will in general lead to incorrect results even after restoring the gauge invariance property of the Hamiltonian. Furthermore, it is shown that an identification of the Z--diagrams of the usual Dirac representation with the contact graphs of the Foldy--Wouthuysen representation is incorrect beyond the order of the low--energy theorem.

# Effective Hamiltonians with Relativistic Corrections II: Application to Compton Scattering by a Proton

## Key claim
The Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation yields an effective Hamiltonian that reproduces the covariant calculation result for low-energy Compton scattering by a proton, whereas the direct Pauli reduction method (evaluating the matrix element of the interaction Hamiltonian between positive-energy solutions of the free Dirac equation) leads to incorrect results even after restoring gauge invariance. Furthermore, the identification of Z-diagrams with contact graphs of the Foldy-Wouthuysen representation is incorrect beyond the order of the low-energy theorem.

## Method
The authors performed a covariant calculation of Compton scattering by a proton to second-order perturbation theory using the Dirac equation with an anomalous magnetic moment (kappa = 1.79), splitting the Feynman propagator into positive- and negative-frequency contributions to identify ordinary diagrams and Z-diagrams. They then compared this reference result with two nonrelativistic approaches: (1) a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation applied to the Dirac Hamiltonian to obtain a block-diagonal effective 2x2 Hamiltonian to order 1/M^3, and (2) a direct Pauli reduction where the matrix element of the interaction Hamiltonian between free positive-energy Dirac solutions was reduced to two-component form and expanded in 1/M. The T-matrix elements were expanded to order omega^2/M^3 and expressed in terms of eight amplitudes A_i in the laboratory frame using radiation gauge.

## Key numbers
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the proton used in the calculation: kappa = 1.79
- The expansion was carried out to order 1/M^3 in the nucleon mass and to order omega^2/M^3 in photon energy
- The Foldy-Wouthuysen Hamiltonian reproduced the covariant result exactly to the order of the low-energy theorem (order 1/M and omega/M^2)
- The direct reduction method failed to reproduce even the leading-order Thomson limit term
- Beyond the low-energy theorem (order omega^2/M^3), the Z-diagram contribution to amplitudes A_1, A_5, and A_6 differed from the contact terms of the Foldy-Wouthuysen calculation

## Context
This work extends the generic analysis of Fearing (1993) by applying the comparison of effective Hamiltonian methods to a specific process with a known low-energy theorem from Gell-Mann and Low (1954). The paper contradicts the common practice of using direct Pauli reduction of relativistic matrix elements to obtain effective Hamiltonians, as done in many low- and intermediate-energy nuclear physics calculations. The results enable a critical assessment of which effective Hamiltonian methods are reliable for processes requiring higher than first-order perturbation theory, and show that gauge invariance alone is insufficient to determine terms beyond the Thomson limit, consistent with the observation of Low (1954).
