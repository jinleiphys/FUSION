# Coupled nuclear and leptonic longitudinal collective modes in neutron star matter : a covariant Vlasov approach

> A covariant relativistic approach based on the Vlasov equation is used to study collective modes in neutron-star matter. The analysis is carried out within relativistic mean-field models describing charge-neutral and $β$-equilibrated matter composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons. We investigate the conditions under which nuclear collective excitations couple to electron and muon plasmon modes, a phenomenon relevant for neutron stars and supernova matter. The study is undertaken considering relativistic mean field models with different isoscalar and isovector properties. It is shown that the nuclear-leptonic coupling can be sufficiently strong to modify the onset of nuclear collective modes and to affect their isoscalar or isovector character.

## Key claim
The coupling between nuclear collective excitations and lepton (electron and muon) plasmon modes in neutron-star matter can be sufficiently strong to modify the onset of nuclear collective modes and alter their isoscalar or isovector character, but this effect depends on the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the equation of state encoded in the chosen relativistic mean-field model.

## Method
The authors employed a covariant relativistic Vlasov equation formalism to derive dispersion relations for longitudinal collective modes in charge-neutral, β-equilibrated matter composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons. They solved the linearized Vlasov equation coupled to the equations of motion for meson (σ, ω, ρ) and electromagnetic fields, obtaining a 4×4 matrix equation for density fluctuations whose determinant yields the collective mode frequencies. The analysis was performed using three relativistic mean-field models (NL3, NL3ωρ, and FSU2H) with different isoscalar and isovector properties, at zero temperature.

## Key numbers
- NL3 model predicts proton fractions up to y_p ≈ 0.25 for npeμ matter and y_p ≈ 0.225 for npe matter at around three times saturation density
- NL3ωρ and FSU2H models give proton fractions saturating at approximately y_p ≈ 0.14 for npeμ matter and y_p ≈ 0.12 for npe matter
- At saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1480 fm⁻³ (NL3, NL3ωρ) or 0.1505 fm⁻³ (FSU2H), nuclear modes persist up to model-dependent momentum limits: about 30 MeV for NL3, 80 MeV for NL3ωρ, and above 90 MeV for FSU2H
- The plasmon frequency at zero-momentum transfer is given by ω₀ = (e²ρₑ/E_Fₑ)^(1/2) for npe matter and ω₀ = (e²(ρₑ/E_Fₑ + ρ_μ/E_F_μ))^(1/2) for npeμ matter
- In np matter with Coulomb interactions, the undamped mode changes from isovector at low momentum to isoscalar above q ≈ 10 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous covariant Vlasov study of asymmetric nuclear matter (Rabhi 2025) to fully β-equilibrated neutron-star matter including muons, building on earlier relativistic mean-field treatments of collective modes by Chin (1977), Lim (1989), Nielsen (1990, 1993), Greco (2003), Avancini (2005), and Providência (2006). The inclusion of both electrons and muons provides a more realistic description of the neutron-star outer core, enabling investigation of how composition and equation of state influence collective excitations at supra-saturation densities. The results enable improved predictions for neutrino transport in dense astrophysical environments, as neutrino opacity is significantly influenced by coherent scattering off density fluctuations.
