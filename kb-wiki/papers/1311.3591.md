# Nuclear Entalpies

> In a compressed Nuclear Matter (NM) an increasing pressure between the nucleons starts to increase the ratio of a nucleon Fermi to average single particle energy and in accordance with the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem the longitudinal Momentum Sum Rule (MSR) is broken in a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach. We propose to benefit from the concept of enthalpy in order to show how to fulfill the MSR above a saturation density with pressure corrections. As a result a nucleon mass can decrease with density, making the Equation of State (EoS) softer. The course of the EoS in our modified RMF model is close to a semi-empirical estimate and to results obtained from extensive DBHF calculations with a Bonn A potential, which produce the EoS stiff enough to describe neutron star properties (mass-radius constraint), especially the most massive known neutron star. The presented model has proper saturation properties, including good values of a compressibility and a spin-orbit term.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that including volume corrections to the nucleon rest energy via the concept of enthalpy (adding a pressure-volume work term) can restore the longitudinal Momentum Sum Rule (MSR) in Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models above saturation density, while simultaneously softening the Equation of State (EoS) by allowing the nucleon mass to decrease with density.

## Method

The author modifies the standard linear (σ,ω) RMF model by replacing the constant nucleon mass with a density-dependent nucleon mass M_pr(ρ) = M_N − p_H(ρ)Ω_N, where p_H is the nuclear pressure and Ω_N is the nucleon volume (taken as a sphere of constant radius R_0). This introduces a differential-recursive set of equations for the energy per nucleon ε_A above saturation density, coupling the RMF self-consistency equations with the pressure-dependent mass. The nucleon enthalpy H_N = M_N is kept constant, meaning the nucleon mass decreases as pressure increases, and the total nuclear energy is taken as the nuclear enthalpy H_A = M_A + p_HΩ_A rather than the nuclear mass M_A.

## Key numbers

- The original linear RMF model gives compressibility K⁻¹ ∼ 550 MeV; the modified model with R_0 = 0.55 fm and set S_2 reduces this to K⁻¹ = 290 MeV.
- For the constant nucleon enthalpy model with R_0 = 0.7 fm and set S_2, the EoS follows a good course up to density ρ = 0.6 fm⁻³.
- The bag constant B(ρ₀) ≈ 60 MeV fm⁻³ is the pressure at which de-confinement is expected, occurring when p_H = B(ρ₀) at ρ ≈ (0.5–0.6) fm⁻³.
- Two saturation densities are used: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (set S₁) and ρ₀ = 0.19 fm⁻³ (set S₂).

## Context

This work extends the standard Walecka linear RMF model (Walecka, 1974; Serot, 1986) by introducing finite nucleon volume effects, which are absent in point-like nucleon treatments. It addresses the problem that the original linear RMF produces an EoS that is too stiff and has unrealistically large compressibility, which nonlinear extensions (Stocker, 1981; Hansel, 1995) fix by adding self-interaction terms for the σ-field. The results are compared to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations with a Bonn A potential and to semi-empirical flow constraints from heavy ion collisions (Pawel, 2013). The model enables improved descriptions of neutron star properties, particularly the masses of the most massive known neutron stars PSR J1614–2230 and PSR J0348+0432 (∼2 M_⊙).
