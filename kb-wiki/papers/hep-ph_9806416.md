# Chiral symmetry and the pion gas virial expansion

> In this work we study the thermodynamic properties of the pion gas starting from the realistic elastic scattering phase-shifts obtained from Chiral Perturbation Theory and using the virial expansion. In particular we study the state equation and the behavior of the quark condensate as a function of the temperature and the density.

# Chiral symmetry and the pion gas virial expansion

## Key claim
The thermodynamic properties of a pion gas, including the equation of state and the quark condensate, can be derived from elastic scattering phase shifts using the virial expansion, provided the temperature and density remain within a regime where the fugacity is small and Bose-Einstein condensation does not occur. The approach is valid for temperatures below approximately 300 MeV and densities where the second-order virial expansion yields less than 5% error relative to exact calculations.

## Method
The authors apply the relativistic virial expansion to a pion gas, expressing the pressure as a series in the fugacity with virial coefficients computed from the S-matrix. The second virial coefficient is obtained from elastic ππ scattering phase shifts in the (I,J) = (0,0), (1,1), and (2,0) channels, using both standard Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) to O(p⁴) and the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) which extends fits to approximately 1 GeV. The quark condensate is then derived from the pressure via its derivative with respect to the pion mass, following the relation of Gerber and Leutwyler (1989).

## Key numbers
- The virial expansion is applicable with less than 5% error for chemical potential μ ≤ 135 MeV and temperatures below 10 MeV (from free gas comparison)
- The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration at μ = 0 is estimated around 220 MeV
- Numerical difference between standard ChPT and IAM results is approximately 5% at T = 300 MeV and n = 2 fm⁻³
- The pion decay constant used is F = 88.3 ± 1.1 MeV, with parameter c = 0.90 ± 0.05
- Chiral parameters used: l̄₁ = -0.62 ± 0.94, l̄₂ = 6.28 ± 0.48, l̄₃ = 2.9 ± 2.4, l̄₄ = 4.3 ± 0.9

## Context
This work extends the finite-temperature ChPT calculations of Gerber and Leutwyler (1989), who computed the pion gas free energy up to O(T⁶/F⁶), by using the virial expansion to incorporate interactions at higher temperatures and including density effects. The approach complements finite-temperature QCD sum rule studies and other alternative methods for studying the chiral phase transition. The results enable construction of phase diagrams for the pion gas in the (T,μ) and (T,n) planes, showing the interplay between temperature-driven condensate melting and density-driven enhancement of chiral symmetry breaking, though the authors note that extension to SU(3) with kaons and etas, as well as baryons, would be needed for more complete calculations.
