# Pseudoscalar Meson Nonet at Zero and Finite Temperature

> Theoretical understanding of experimental results from relativistic heavy-ion collisions requires a microscopic approach to the behavior of QCD n-point functions at finite temperatures, as given by the hierarchy of Dyson-Schwinger equations, properly generalized within the Matsubara formalism. The convergence of sums over Matsubara modes is studied. The technical complexity of finite-temperature calculations mandates modeling. We present a model where the QCD interaction in the infrared, nonperturbative domain, is modeled by a separable form. Results for the mass spectrum of light quark flavors (u, d, s) and for the pseudoscalar bound-state amplitudes at finite temperature are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a separable-rank Dyson-Schwinger model for light pseudoscalar mesons (u, d, s) that reproduces the correct chiral behavior and Goldstone theorem at zero temperature, and extends this to finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism, but the results for the temperature dependence of the η-η' complex depend critically on the assumed relationship between the chiral restoration temperature T_Ch and the temperature of melting of the topological susceptibility T_χ, with different scenarios yielding qualitatively different predictions.

## Method

The authors model the nonperturbative QCD interaction in the infrared by a separable rank-2 Ansatz for the effective gluon propagator, with Gaussian-type form factors. They solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar meson bound states in the rainbow-ladder truncation, both at zero temperature and at finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism with discrete fermion frequencies ω_n = (2n+1)πT. The model parameters are fixed by fitting the pion and kaon masses and decay constants, and the η-η' complex is treated by combining the non-anomalous qq̅ contributions with the gluon axial anomaly.

## Key numbers

- Bare quark masses: m̃_u = m̃_d = 5.5 MeV, m̃_s = 115 MeV
- Model parameters: Λ_0 = 758 MeV, Λ_1 = 961 MeV, p_0 = 600 MeV, D_0Λ_0² = 219, D_1Λ_1⁴ = 40
- Dynamical quark masses at p²=0: m_u(0) = 398 MeV, m_s(0) = 672 MeV
- Chiral quark-antiquark condensate at T=0: ⟨qq̅⟩_0 = (-217 MeV)³
- Chiral restoration temperature: T_Ch = 128 MeV
- Pseudoscalar meson masses at T=0 (in GeV): M_π = 0.140 (exp. 0.1396), M_K = 0.495 (exp. 0.4937), M_s\bar{s} = 0.685, M_η = 0.543 (exp. 0.5473), M_η' = 0.933 (exp. 0.9578)
- Pseudoscalar decay constants at T=0 (in GeV): f_π = 0.092 (exp. 0.0924±0.0003), f_K = 0.110 (exp. 0.1130±0.0010), f_s\bar{s} = 0.119

## Context

This work extends the separable Dyson-Schwinger approach previously applied to the scalar σ meson at finite temperature (Kalinovsky 2005) by including the strange quark flavor and studying the full pseudoscalar nonet. It builds on the Dyson-Schwinger studies of QCD at finite temperature initiated by Bender (1996) and reviewed by Roberts (2000). The results enable predictions for the temperature behavior of the η-η' complex, where the relationship between T_Ch and T_χ (the temperature of topological susceptibility melting) determines whether η becomes light near the chiral transition (as in the Pisarski-Wilczek scenario) or remains heavy, with η' becoming very heavy and potentially serving as a signal from hot QCD matter.
