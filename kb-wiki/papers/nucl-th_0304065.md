# On the physical significance of q-deformation to isovector pairing interactions in nuclei

> The quantum deformation concept is applied to a study of isovector pairing correlations in nuclei of the mass 40<A<100 region. While the non-deformed (q -> 1) limit of the theory provides a reasonable global estimate for strength parameters of the pairing interaction, the results show that the q-deformation plays a significant role in understanding higher-order effects in the interaction.

## Key claim

The q-deformation of the Sp(4) symmetry introduces higher-order, many-body terms into the isovector pairing interaction that cannot be reproduced by simply adjusting the two-body interaction strengths in the non-deformed (q→1) limit; the deformation parameter is decoupled from the interaction strength parameters and provides a uniformly superior fit to experimental 0⁺ state energies across the mass 40≤A≤100 region.

## Method

The authors constructed a q-deformed Sp_q(4) algebra using q-deformed fermion creation and annihilation operators, with generators representing proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-neutron pair creation and annihilation operators coupled to J^π=0⁺. They fit the eigenvalues of the q-deformed Hamiltonian to experimental 0⁺ state energies for groups of 36 and 100 nuclei in the mass 40≤A≤100 region, first determining global parameters for the non-deformed (q=1) limit, then allowing both the deformation parameter q and the interaction strength parameters to vary in a second global fit.

## Key numbers

- The global parameters determined for the non-deformed limit include: ε=13.851 MeV, F/Ω=0.296 MeV, G/Ω=0.352 MeV, C=0.190 MeV, D=-0.796 MeV, E/(2Ω)=-0.489 MeV (for the ⁵⁶Ni core analysis)
- The ratio R^{pp(nn)} = E_q^{±}/E^{±} = 1 + (ϰ²/6)((8n_{±1}²-5+4Ω²)/(8Ω²) + (E^{±}/(F n_{±1}))²) + ... for like-particle pairing
- The ratio R^{pn} = E_q^{pn}/E^{pn} = 1 + (ϰ²/6)((n₀²-1-4Ω²)/(4Ω²) + (E^{pn}/(G n₀))²) + ... for proton-neutron pairing
- The quadratic coefficient in R^{pp(nn)} is positive, while the one in R^{pn} is negative

## Context

This work extends the earlier non-deformed Sp(4) pairing model (Helmers, Szpikowski, Hecht, Engel et al. 1996) by introducing a q-deformation that accounts for higher-order many-body effects in the isovector pairing interaction. The approach builds on earlier applications of q-deformation in nuclear physics to rotational bands using SU_q(2) (Raychev, Roussev, Smirnov 1990) and to pairing interactions (Sharma, Sharma). The results enable future studies where a local q value that varies smoothly with nuclear mass number can be used to investigate specific features of nuclear structure, particularly for nuclei just beyond closed shells and with N₊ ≈ N₋.
