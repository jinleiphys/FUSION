# Hybrid stars with color superconductivity within a nonlocal chiral quark model

> The equation of state for quark matter is derived for a nonlocal, chiral quark model within the mean field approximation. Special emphasis is on the occurrence of a diquark condensate which signals a phase transition to color superconductivity and its effects on the equation of state. We present a fit formula for the Bag pressure, which is density dependent in case when the quark matter is color superconducting. We calculate the quark star configurations by solving the Tolman- Oppenheimer- Volkoff equations and demonstrate the effects of diquark condensation on the stability of hybrid stars for different formfactors of the quark interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that color-superconducting (2SC) quark matter can support stable hybrid star configurations, but only for certain nonlocal formfactors of the quark interaction (Gaussian and Lorentzian), while the standard NJL cutoff formfactor yields no stable quark cores. The stability and mass-radius relation depend sensitively on the diquark coupling strength and the formfactor choice, with the Gaussian formfactor giving the hardest equation of state.

## Method

The authors derive the equation of state for quark matter using a nonlocal chiral quark model in the mean-field approximation, with both scalar (mass gap) and diquark (pairing gap) order parameters. They impose beta equilibrium, electric charge neutrality, and color neutrality, constructing a mixed phase of superconducting and normal quark matter via the Glendenning construction. The quark-hadron phase transition is treated with a Maxwell construction using a relativistic mean-field (nonlinear Walecka) model for hadronic matter, and hybrid star configurations are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- For the Gaussian formfactor with color superconductivity (eta=1), the critical mass for a hybrid star with a quark core shifts from 1.04 solar masses to 0.62 solar masses; the maximum hybrid star mass decreases from 1.83 solar masses to 1.79 solar masses.
- For the Lorentzian formfactor with 2SC superconducting quark cores, stable hybrid stars exist in the mass range between 1.55 solar masses and a maximum of 1.72 solar masses.
- The volume fraction of the 2SC condensate phase in the mixed phase ranges from 65% to 85%, depending on the formfactor.
- The color chemical potential mu_8 is about 5 to 7 MeV in the relevant density region (mu_q approximately 300 to 500 MeV).
- The Lorentzian model with normal quark matter gives marginally stable quark cores with radii less than 2 km in the mass range 1.91 to 1.92 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color superconductivity in quark matter (Rapp 1997, Alford 1999) and its application to compact stars (Blaschke 1999, Page 2000) by systematically comparing different nonlocal formfactors within a single model framework. It provides polynomial fit formulas for the density-dependent bag pressure and diquark gap that enable phenomenological applications such as neutron star cooling (Grigorian 2003) and merging (Oechslin 2004). The results are compared with observational constraints from the compact object RX J185635-3754 and the surface redshift measurement of EXO 0748-676 (Cottam 2002).
