# A contact interaction model for the $η$ and $η'$ mesons in a SDE-BSE approach to QCD: masses, decay widths and transition form factors

> We construct a contact interaction model for the $η$ and $η'$ mesons in the SDE-BSE approach to QCD and compute several static properties of these mesons and their transition form factors. We find that this model gives an excellent description of the $η$ and $η'$ static properties, namely their masses, decay width and decay constants. However, a contact interaction disagrees with experimental data for $Q^2$ greater than 2 GeV$^2$, and produces transition form factors in conflict with perturbative QCD prediction. This is not surpring and the reasons for this are explained

## Key claim

The contact interaction model, when augmented with a phenomenological non-Abelian anomaly kernel, provides an excellent description of the static properties (masses, decay widths, decay constants) of the eta and eta' mesons, but the model's transition form factors disagree with experimental data for photon virtualities Q^2 greater than 2 GeV^2 and conflict with perturbative QCD predictions.

## Method

The authors constructed a contact interaction model within the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equation (SDE-BSE) framework of QCD, using rainbow-ladder truncation with a symmetry-preserving vector-vector contact interaction. They extended this by adding a phenomenological kernel representing the non-Abelian anomaly, with parameters xi and theta fitted to experimental eta and eta' masses and two-photon decay widths. The model parameters for the rainbow-ladder part were fixed from pion and kaon static properties, and the quark-photon vertex was determined from its own SDE constrained by the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: 141 MeV (experimental: 140 MeV); pion decay constant: 94 MeV (experimental: 93 MeV)
* Kaon mass: 500 MeV (experimental: 497 MeV); kaon decay constant: 96 MeV (experimental: 110 MeV)
* Light quark dynamical mass: M_l = 410 MeV; strange quark dynamical mass: M_s = 557 MeV
* Eta mass from fit-II: 558 MeV (experimental: 548 MeV); eta' mass from fit-II: 920 MeV (experimental: 958 MeV)
* Eta two-photon decay width from fit-II: 0.418 keV (experimental: 0.516 keV); eta' two-photon decay width from fit-II: 4.16 keV (experimental: 4.35 keV)
* The contact interaction disagrees with experimental data for Q^2 greater than 2 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the contact interaction SDE-BSE approach, previously applied to pions and kaons by Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010) and others, to the eta and eta' mesons which require treatment of the non-Abelian anomaly and flavor mixing. The model enables investigation of U_A(1) symmetry restoration at finite temperature and density within the SDE-BSE framework, which the authors state will be reported elsewhere. The results are compared with those from Ding (2018), Osipov (2014), and Takizawa (1995), and the model's limitations at higher Q^2 are attributed to the contact interaction's inability to reproduce the momentum dependence required by perturbative QCD.
