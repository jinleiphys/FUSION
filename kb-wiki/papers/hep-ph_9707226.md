# Strange Magnetic Moment and Isospin Symmetry Breaking

> The small mass difference $m_n-m_p=1.3 MeV$ between the proton and neutron leads to an excess of $n=π^- p$ over $p=π^+ n$ fluctuations which can be calculated by using a light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model of intrinsic quark-antiquark pairs of the nucleon sea. The Gottfried sum rule violation may partially be explained by isospin symmetry breaking between the proton and neutron and the same effect introduces correction terms to the anomalous magnetic moments and the anomalous weak magnetic moments of the proton and neutron. We also evaluated the strange magnetic moment of the nucleon from the lowest strangeness $K Λ$ fluctuation and found a non-trivial influence due to isospin symmetry breaking in the experimental measurements of the strange magnetic moment of the nucleon.

# Strange Magnetic Moment and Isospin Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim
Isospin symmetry breaking between the proton and neutron, arising from the 1.3 MeV mass difference, introduces correction terms to the anomalous magnetic moments and anomalous weak magnetic moments that cause a non-trivial shift in the experimentally extracted strange magnetic moment of the nucleon. The measured "strange magnetic moment" may therefore differ from the actual strange magnetic moment by an amount that could change its sign from negative to positive.

## Method
The author uses a light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model of intrinsic quark-antiquark pairs in the nucleon sea to calculate the relative probabilities of pion-nucleon fluctuations in the proton and neutron. The excess of n = π⁻ p over p = π⁺ n fluctuations due to the neutron-proton mass difference is estimated using Gaussian-type wavefunctions with an invariant mass parameter α, and the resulting correction to the neutron anomalous magnetic moment is computed from the magnetic moment of the π⁻ p intermediate state. The same framework is applied to the weak magnetic moment via the neutral weak vector current.

## Key numbers
- The neutron-proton mass difference is m_n − m_p = 1.3 MeV.
- The ratio of fluctuation probabilities is r^{π}_{p/n} = P(p = π⁺ n)/P(n = π⁻ p) = 0.986 for α = 330 MeV, corresponding to a 0.2% excess of n = π⁻ p over p = π⁺ n fluctuations.
- With α = 205 MeV for the neutron and α = 200 MeV for the proton, the ratio becomes r^{π}_{p/n} = 0.820, corresponding to a 3% excess.
- The excess of n = π⁻ p over p = π⁺ n fluctuation probabilities lies in the range δP^{π} = 0.002 to 0.03.
- The correction to the neutron anomalous magnetic moment is δG^{n}_{M} ≈ −0.006 to −0.088 n.m.
- The strange magnetic moment from the K Λ fluctuation is G^{s}_{M} ≈ −0.066 n.m.
- The correction to the measured strange magnetic moment from the proton weak measurement is −δG^{s}_{M} ≈ 0.006 to 0.088 n.m.
- The correction to the measured strange magnetic moment from the neutron weak measurement is (1 − 4 sin²θ_W)δG^{n}_{M} − 4δG^{Zn}_{M} ≈ 0.004 to 0.054 n.m., using sin²θ_W = 0.2315.

## Context
This work extends the light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model of Brodsky and collaborators (1996) to include isospin symmetry breaking effects from the neutron-proton mass difference. It builds on the suggestion by Ma (1992, 1993) that the Gottfried sum rule violation measured by the New Muon Collaboration (1991) could be partially explained by isospin symmetry breaking between the proton and neutron sea. The paper shows that the same mechanism introduces corrections to the extraction of the strange magnetic moment from parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as those by the SAMPLE Collaboration, which reported G^{s}_{M}(Q² = 0.1 GeV²) = +0.23 ± 0.37 ± 0.15 ± 0.19 n.m. This enables future experimental measurements of neutral weak vector matrix elements to constrain the uncertainties in the strange magnetic moment determination.
