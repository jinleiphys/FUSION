# Threshold Resonances - a Lattice Perspective

> The calculation of the masses of the lightest nucleon resonances using lattice QCD is surveyed. Recent results for the mass of the first radial excitation of the nucleon, the Roper resonance, are reviewed and the interpretation in terms of models of hadronic resonances, such as the quark model and hadronic molecules, discussed. The talk concludes with an outline of prospects for future calculations.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of nucleon resonances, when performed at quark masses near the strange quark mass, yield a spectrum broadly consistent with quark-model expectations (e.g., the Roper resonance above 2 GeV), but calculations using chiral fermions at pion masses as low as 180 MeV show a dramatic cross-over between the lightest negative-parity state and the radial nucleon excitation, recovering the experimental ordering of states.

## Method

The author surveys lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon spectrum, focusing on the extraction of masses from Euclidean two-point correlators using interpolating operators with specific overlaps onto spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 states. Studies employ both quenched and dynamical fermion actions, with some using Bayesian fitting methods to extract excited-state masses from a matrix of correlators. The talk also reviews calculations of multiquark and molecular states, including the H dibaryon and heavy-quark systems, using volume-dependence methods and adiabatic potentials.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance mass is found to be in excess of 2 GeV in quenched calculations at quark masses around the strange quark mass.
- Pion masses as low as 180 MeV are attained using overlap fermions, enabling exploration of the region where pion cloud effects can emerge.
- A cross-over between the lightest negative-parity state and the radial nucleon excitation occurs for quark masses around 300–400 MeV.
- The H dibaryon was originally predicted in the bag model to be O(100) MeV below the ΛΛ threshold.
- Light-hadron spectrum calculations in the quenched approximation agree with experiment to around 10% in most channels.

## Context

This talk reviews and extends the lattice QCD studies of nucleon resonances by Lee (1998), the BNL group (2002), the LHPC collaboration (2002), and the Adelaide group (2002), all of which found quark-model-like ordering at heavy quark masses. The work by Lee (2002) using overlap fermions and Bayesian methods enables exploration of the light-quark regime where the experimental ordering emerges, and the author emphasizes the need to continue studies to physical quark masses and to investigate molecular states using methods such as Lüscher’s (1986) volume-dependence technique for scattering lengths.
