# A new perspective on the Faddeev equations and the $\bar{K}NN$ system from chiral dynamics and unitarity in coupled channels

> We review recent work concerning the $\bar{K}N$ interaction and Faddeev equations with chiral dynamics which allow us to look at the $\bar{K}NN$ from a different perspective and pay attention to problems that have been posed in previous studies on the subject. We show results which provide extra experimental evidence on the existence of two $Λ(1405)$ states. We then show the findings of a recent approach to Faddeev equations using chiral unitary dynamics, where an explicit cancellation of the two body off shell amplitude with three body forces stemming from the same chiral Lagrangians takes place. This removal of the unphysical off shell part of the amplitudes is most welcome and renders the approach unambiguous, showing that only on shell two body amplitudes need to be used. With this information in mind we use an approximation to the Faddeev equations within the fixed center approximation to study the $\bar{K}NN$ system, providing answers within this approximation to questions that have been brought before and evaluating binding energies and widths of this three body system. As a novelty with respect to recent work on the topic we find a bound state of the system with spin S=1, like a bound state of $\bar{K}$-deuteron, less bound that the one of S=0, where all recent efforts have been devoted. The width is relatively large in this case, suggesting problems in a possible experimental observation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\bar{K}NN$ system with spin S=1 forms a bound state that is less bound than the S=0 state, but with a relatively large width that makes experimental observation problematic. This finding is presented as a novelty relative to prior work that had focused exclusively on the S=0 channel.

## Method

The authors use the fixed center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, treating the $\bar{K}$ as scattering against a bound $NN$ cluster (deuteron-like for S=1). The two-body $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes are taken from the chiral unitary approach, which generates the two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles. The formalism incorporates charge exchange processes ($K^- p \rightarrow \bar{K}^0 n$) and explicitly includes the $\pi \Sigma N$ intermediate channel, while relying on the finding that off-shell two-body amplitudes cancel with three-body forces from the same chiral Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $\bar{K}NN$ (S=1) system: 9 MeV, with a width of about 30 MeV (from the calculation including charge exchange with normal NN size)
- $K^-d$ scattering length: $a \simeq (-1.54 + i 1.82)$ fm
- The two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles from chiral unitary theory: one around 1390 MeV (wide, $\Gamma > 100$ MeV, couples mostly to $\pi \Sigma$) and one around 1420 MeV (narrow, $\Gamma = 30$ MeV, couples mostly to $\bar{K}N$)
- Experimental peak in $K^- d \to \pi^+ \Sigma^- n$ observed around 1420 MeV, consistent with the higher $\Lambda(1405)$ pole

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to the $\bar{K}NN$ three-body system, building on prior Faddeev calculations by Ikeda (2010) and Shevchenko (2006) that gave binding energies of 50-70 MeV, and variational calculations by Dote (2008) that gave around 20 MeV binding. The authors note that the energy-dependent chiral potential produces less binding than energy-independent kernels used in AGS equations. The paper also addresses claims of a deeply bound $K^- pp$ state by Agnello (2005), which was reinterpreted by Magas (2006) as conventional reaction mechanisms. The results enable future experimental searches for the S=1 $\bar{K}NN$ state, though the large width suggests such observation would be challenging.
