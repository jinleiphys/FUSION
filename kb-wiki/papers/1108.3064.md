# Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance from the 3D Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory for Superfluid Nuclei

> A fully symmetry unrestricted Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory extended to include pairing correlations is used to calculate properties of the isovector giant dipole resonances of the deformed open-shell nuclei 172Yb (axially deformed), 188Os (triaxially deformed), and 238U (axially deformed), and to demonstrate good agreement with experimental data on nuclear photo-absorption cross-sections for two different Skyrme force parametrizations of the energy density functional: SkP and SLy4.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully symmetry-unrestricted Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory extended to include pairing correlations (TDSLDA) can reproduce experimental photo-absorption cross-sections for the isovector giant dipole resonance in deformed open-shell nuclei, but only when the external perturbation uses a finite wavevector (|k|=2π/L) rather than the ideal |k|→0 limit, leading to an underestimate of the cross-section by approximately 25% that must be corrected.

## Method

The authors placed each nucleus on a 3D spatial lattice with periodic boundary conditions, first solving the stationary superfluid local density approximation (SLDA) to obtain the ground state, then applying a time-dependent external one-body potential to excite the system and solving the time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations using a fifth-order multistep method with a time step of 0.12 fm/c. The response was propagated for approximately 1600 fm/c, and the strength function was extracted from the Fourier transform of the expectation value fluctuation, with the energy resolution set by the propagation time to about 0.8 MeV. Calculations were performed for two Skyrme force parametrizations (SkP and SLy4) for the normal part of the energy density functional, with the pairing part treated using a single coupling constant for both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution: δE ≈ 0.8 MeV, from propagation time T ≈ 1600 fm/c
- Lattice box: L = 32.5 fm on a side with lattice constant 1.25 fm
- Time step: Δt ≈ 0.12 fm/c, maintaining 10⁻⁷ relative accuracy
- Energy deposited into nucleus: 45-50 MeV
- Proton and neutron number changes during time evolution: |ΔZ| < 10⁻⁴ and |ΔN| < 10⁻⁴
- Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule values: 2.49 MeV·barn (¹⁷²Yb), 2.81 MeV·barn (¹⁸⁸Os), 3.38 MeV·barn (²³⁸U)
- Energy-integrated photo-absorption cross-sections (8-20 MeV) for experiment: 2.47 (¹⁷²Yb), 2.73 (¹⁸⁸Os), 3.06 (²³⁸U) in MeV·barn
- Energy-integrated cross-sections for SkP: 1.94 (¹⁷²Yb), 2.26 (¹⁸⁸Os), 2.49 (²³⁸U) in MeV·barn
- Energy-integrated cross-sections for SLy4: 1.84 (¹⁷²Yb), 2.01 (¹⁸⁸Os), 2.35 (²³⁸U) in MeV·barn
- Corrected energy-weighted sum rule for SkP: 2.63(6) (¹⁷²Yb), 3.11(7) (¹⁸⁸Os), 3.55(8) (²³⁸U) in MeV·barn
- Corrected energy-weighted sum rule for SLy4: 2.50(5) (¹⁷²Yb), 2.77(5) (¹⁸⁸Os), 3.35(8) (²³⁸U) in MeV·barn
- Isovector effective mass: m*_iv = 0.74 m (SkP) and m*_iv = 0.8 m (SLy4)

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent density functional theory approach to superfluid nuclei (TDSLDA) beyond previous applications to spherical systems, demonstrating its feasibility for axially deformed (¹⁷²Yb, ²³⁸U) and triaxially deformed (¹⁸⁸Os) open-shell nuclei. The approach avoids the severe truncation issues and spurious state admixtures that plague conventional (Q)RPA calculations in deformed nuclei, as noted in prior work by Terasaki and Engel (2008) and others. The results enable future studies of large-amplitude nuclear motion and dissipation through stochastic extensions of TDSLDA, and provide a framework for investigating the spreading width (Γ↓) that has been difficult to treat microscopically in earlier approaches such as nuclear field theory (Mottelson, 1975) and diagrammatic methods (Bertsch, 1983).
