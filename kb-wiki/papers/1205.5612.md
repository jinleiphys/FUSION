# $π^0$ and $η$ meson exchange interactions in the coherent $η$ meson production in the nucleus

> The energy transfer spectra have been calculated for the coherent $η$ meson production in the proton nucleus reaction. The elementary reaction occurring in the nucleus is considered as $ pN \to pN^*(1535) $; $ N^*(1535) \to Nη$. The $N^*(1535)$ excitation for the forward going proton and $η$ meson is occurred due to the $π^0$ and $η$ meson exchange interactions, other mesons do not contribute in this process. The cross sections are calculated for the $π^0$ and $η$ meson exchange interactions, and the effect of their interference on the cross section is also studied. The initial and final state interactions as well as the $N^*(1535)$ nucleus interaction are addressed by the optical potentials. The distorted wave functions for proton and $η$ meson are described by the eikonal form. The sensitivity of the cross section to the hadron nucleus potential is presented.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the coherent η meson production cross section in proton-nucleus reactions on a scalar-isovector nucleus (¹⁴C), showing that both π⁰ and η meson exchange interactions contribute to the N*(1535) excitation, contrary to the isoscalar case where only η exchange contributes. The interference between these two exchange mechanisms is distinctly visible in the calculated cross section.

## Method

The elementary reaction is modeled as pN → pN*(1535) followed by N*(1535) → Nη, with the N*(1535) excitation occurring through π⁰ and η meson exchange interactions between the projectile proton and a nucleon inside the nucleus. The distorted wave functions for the incoming proton, outgoing proton, and η meson are described using the eikonal form, while the initial and final state interactions as well as the N*(1535)-nucleus interaction are treated via optical potentials calculated using the "tρ(r)" approximation. The calculations are performed for a ¹⁴C target at a beam energy of 2.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_π = 13.4, g*_π = 0.71, g_η = 7.93, g*_η = 1.86
- Form factor cutoff parameters: Λ_π = 1.3 GeV, Λ_η = 1.5 GeV
- Meson masses: m_π⁰ ≈ 135 MeV, m_η ≈ 547 MeV
- N*(1535) mass: m_N* = 1.535 GeV; total width at resonance: Γ_N*(m_N*) = 0.15 GeV
- N*(1535) decay branching ratios: Nπ (∼48%), Nη (∼42%), Nππ (∼10%)
- Nuclear density parameters for ¹⁴C: w = 1.38, c = 1.73 fm
- Beam energy for the calculation: 2.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Alvaredo and Oset (2003) on coherent η meson production in (p,p') reactions on spin-isospin saturated (isoscalar) nuclei, where only η meson exchange contributed. The present paper considers a scalar-isovector nucleus (¹⁴C) where both π⁰ and η meson exchange interactions contribute to the N*(1535) excitation, enabling the study of their interference. The results show that the π⁰ exchange contribution is much smaller than the η exchange contribution, and that hadron-nucleus optical potentials drastically reduce the cross section compared to plane wave calculations.
