# Multi-body correlations in SU(3) Fermi gases

> We investigate strong-coupling effects in a three-component atomic Fermi gas. It is a promising candidate for simulating quantum chromodynamics (QCD), and furthermore, the emergence of various phenomena such as color superfluidity and Efimov effect are anticipated in this system. In this paper, we study the effects of two-body and three-body correlations by means of the many-body $T$-matrix approximation (TMA) as well as the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation with medium corrections. We investigate the effects of finite temperature and chemical potential on the trimer binding energy at the superfluid critical point of the unitarity limit.

## Key claim

The paper shows that medium corrections (Pauli-blocking effects) suppress the binding energy of Efimov trimers in a three-component Fermi gas at unitarity, with the trimer binding energy disappearing around a negative effective range of \(r_{\rm e}k_{\rm F}\simeq -0.17\), though the authors note this does not necessarily mean the disappearance of trimer states, as positive-energy "Cooper triple" states may still exist.

## Method

The authors use the many-body \(T\)-matrix approximation (TMA) to calculate the superfluid phase transition temperature \(T_{\rm c}\) and critical chemical potential \(\mu_{\rm c}\) for a symmetric three-component Fermi gas with a two-channel model describing a narrow resonance with finite negative effective range at unitarity (\(1/a=0\)). They then solve the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation with medium corrections, incorporating Pauli-blocking effects via statistical factors that include particle-particle (pp) and hole-hole (hh) pair contributions, using the previously calculated \(T_{\rm c}\) and \(\mu_{\rm c}\) as input.

## Key numbers

- Superfluid phase transition temperature in the narrow resonance limit: \(T_{\rm c}^{\rm NRL} = 0.133 T_{\rm F}\).
- Efimov trimer binding energy in vacuum: \(E_{\rm 3}^{\rm V} = -0.0138542 / (4 m r_{\rm e}^2)\).
- The Efimov trimer binding energy in medium \(E_{\rm 3}^{\rm M}\) disappears around \(r_{\rm e}k_{\rm F} \simeq -0.17\).
- The BEC temperature of molecules in the strong-coupling limit at zero effective range is approximately \(T_{\rm BEC} \simeq 0.137 T_{\rm F}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of strong-coupling effects in three-component Fermi gases, which are analogous to quantum chromodynamics (QCD) where quarks with three colors interact. The authors build on prior theoretical work on the crossover from a trimer phase to a color superfluid phase (Floerchinger, Nishida, Kirk) and on the Efimov effect (Efimov, Naidon). The results enable future investigation of "Cooper triple" states, which generalize the Cooper problem to three fermions, and suggest a self-consistent treatment of two-body and three-body correlations is needed for a complete understanding.
