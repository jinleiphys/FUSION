# Time-Reversal Invariance Violation in Heavy and in Few-body Nuclei

> Time reversal invariance violating (TRIV) effects in neutron scattering are very important in a search for new physics, being complementary to neutron and atomic electric dipole moment measurements. In this relation, a sensitivity of TRIV observables to different models of CP-violation and their dependencies on nuclear structure, which can lead to new enhancement factors, are discussed.

## Key claim

Time-reversal invariance violating (TRIV) effects in neutron transmission through polarized targets are free from false signals due to final state interactions, making them as reliable as electric dipole moment (EDM) experiments for probing CP-violation, but with the potential for enhancement factors of up to 10^4 relative to current EDM limits if the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant is smaller than the DDH "best value."

## Method

The authors analyze TRIV observables in neutron transmission through polarized nuclear targets, specifically the difference in total cross sections and the neutron spin rotation angle for the correlation \(\vec{\sigma}_n\cdot(\vec{p}\times\vec{I})\). They compare these effects to parity-violating (PV) observables in the same systems, using the ratio \(\lambda\) of TRIV to PV effects to cancel nuclear structure uncertainties, and relate this ratio to meson-nucleon coupling constants within the DDH one-meson-exchange model. They then connect these coupling constants to experimental limits from neutron and atomic EDM measurements to estimate the discovery potential of future neutron scattering experiments at spallation sources.

## Key numbers

- The TRIV cross section difference in neutron-deuteron scattering is given by \(P_{\not{T}\not{P}} = (-0.185 \text{ b})/(2\sigma_{tot}) [\bar{g}_\pi^{(0)}+0.26 \bar{g}_\pi^{(1)} -0.0012 \bar{g}_\eta^{(0)}+0.0034 \bar{g}_\eta^{(1)} -0.0071 \bar{g}_\rho^{(0)}+0.0035 \bar{g}_\rho^{(1)} +0.0019 \bar{g}_\omega^{(0)}-0.00063 \bar{g}_\omega^{(1)}]\).
- The PV cross section difference in neutron-deuteron scattering is given by \(P_{\not{P}} = (0.395 \text{ b})/(2\sigma_{tot}) [h_\pi^1+h_\rho^0(0.021)+h_\rho^1(0.0027) +h_\omega^0(0.022)+h_\omega^1(-0.043) +h_\rho^{'1}(-0.012)]\).
- The ratio \(\lambda = \Delta\sigma_{\not{T}\not{P}}/\Delta\sigma_{\not{P}} \simeq (-0.47)(\bar{g}^{(0)}_\pi/h_\pi^1 + 0.26 \bar{g}^{(1)}_\pi/h_\pi^1)\).
- The neutron EDM limit (Baker 2006) constrains \(\bar{g}^{(0)}_\pi < 2.5 \times 10^{-10}\).
- The \({}^{199}\)Hg atomic EDM limit (Romalis 2000) constrains \(\bar{g}^{(1)}_\pi < 0.5 \times 10^{-11}\) (Dmitriev 2003).
- The DDH "best value" for the PV coupling constant is \(h_\pi^1 \sim 4.6 \times 10^{-7}\).
- TRIV effects in neutron-induced reactions are enhanced by a factor of about \(10^6\) due to nuclear structure.
- If the PV coupling constant \(h_\pi^1\) is much smaller than the DDH best value, the discovery potential for TRIV experiments could be about \(10^4\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for TRIV searches in neutron scattering developed by Gudkov (1991) and Bunakov (1982), building on the theorem by Ryndin (1965) that final state interactions cannot mimic TRIV effects in zero-angle elastic scattering. The authors compare their calculations for neutron-deuteron scattering (Song 2010, 2011) to earlier estimates for complex nuclei (Gudkov 1990), finding good agreement for the ratio \(\lambda\). The results enable experimental planning at new spallation neutron facilities such as the SNS at Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the J-SNS at J-PARC, and suggest that neutron transmission experiments could improve current limits on TRIV coupling constants from EDM experiments by two to four orders of magnitude.
