# Impact of phonon coupling on the radiative nuclear reaction characteristics

> The pygmy dipole resonance and photon strength functions (PSF) in stable and unstable Ni and Sn isotopes are calculated within the microscopic self-consistent version of the extended theory of finite fermi systems in the quasiparticle time blocking approximation. The approach includes phonon coupling (PC) effects in addition to the standard QRPA approach. The Skyrme force SLy4 is used. A pygmy dipole resonance in 72Ni is predicted at the mean energy of 12.4 MeV exhausting 25.7% of the total energy-weighted sum rule. With our microscopic E1 PSFs in the EMPIRE 3.1 code, the following radiative nuclear reaction characteristics have been calculated for several stable and unstable even-even Sn and Ni isotopes: 1) neutron capture cross sections, 2) corresponding neutron capture gamma-spectra, 3) average radiative widths of neutron resonances. Here, three variants of the microscopic nuclear level density models have been used and a comparison with the phenomenological generalized superfluid model (GSM) has been performed. In all the considered properties, including the recent experimental data for PSF in Sn isotopes, the PC contributions turned out to be significant, as compared with the QRPA one, and necessary to explain the available experimental data. The phenomenological GSM NLD model used in EMPIRE3.1 gives, on the whole, the worse results than the microscopic HFB plus combinatorial NLD model.

# Impact of phonon coupling on the radiative nuclear reaction characteristics

## Key claim

Phonon coupling (PC) effects, included via the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) on top of the standard QRPA approach, are necessary to explain experimental photon strength function data and radiative nuclear reaction characteristics in stable and unstable Ni and Sn isotopes; the phenomenological generalized superfluid model (GSM) for nuclear level densities gives worse results than the microscopic HFB plus combinatorial model.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent version of the extended theory of finite Fermi systems (ETFFS) in the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) with the Skyrme force SLy4, which includes both QRPA and phonon coupling effects self-consistently with the single-particle continuum treated in discrete form. The ground state was calculated within the HFB method using the spherical code HFBRAD, and the residual interaction was derived as the second derivative of the Skyrme functional. The resulting microscopic E1 photon strength functions were then used as input in the EMPIRE 3.1 reaction code to calculate neutron capture cross sections, capture gamma-ray spectra, and average radiative widths for several stable and unstable even-even Sn and Ni isotopes, employing three variants of nuclear level density models.

## Key numbers

- Pygmy dipole resonance in 72Ni predicted at mean energy 12.4 MeV exhausting 25.7% of the total energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR)
- For 68Ni, the PDR was measured experimentally in the range 7-13 MeV with maximum at 11 MeV exhausting about 5% of the EWSR; the QTBA calculation gave 10.8 MeV and 8.7% of EWSR
- For 58Ni, QRPA gave PDR mean energy 13.3 MeV with 6.0% EWSR, while QTBA gave 14.0 MeV with 11.7% EWSR
- For 68Ni, QRPA gave PDR mean energy 11.0 MeV with 4.9% EWSR, while QTBA gave 10.8 MeV with 8.7% EWSR
- For 72Ni, QRPA gave PDR mean energy 12.4 MeV with 14.7% EWSR, while QTBA gave 12.4 MeV with 25.7% EWSR
- Average radiative width for 118Sn: experimental value 117 (20) meV; QTBA with HFB+combinatorial NLD gave 58.1 meV; QRPA gave 40.3 meV; EGLO gave 106.7 meV
- Average radiative width for 120Sn: experimental value 100 (16) meV; QTBA with HFB+combinatorial NLD gave 61.5 meV
- Average radiative width for 60Ni: experimental value 2200 (700) meV; QTBA with HFB+combinatorial NLD gave 2800 meV
- Average radiative width for 62Ni: experimental value 2000 (300) meV; QTBA with HFB+combinatorial NLD gave 2117 meV
- M1 contribution to average radiative widths was estimated to be of order (10-12)% for Sn isotopes and 4%, 3%, 22%, and 16% for 58Ni, 62Ni, 68Ni, and 72Ni respectively

## Context

This work extends the microscopic self-consistent approach of the extended theory of finite Fermi systems (PhysRep, 2011) and the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (Tselyaev, 2007) to calculate radiative nuclear reaction characteristics, building on earlier studies that showed phonon coupling effects are necessary to explain experimental PSF structures in Sn isotopes measured by the Oslo method (Toft et al., 2010, 2011) and the PDR in 68Ni (Wieland et al., 2009). The results enable more reliable predictions for neutron capture cross sections and gamma-ray spectra in unstable nuclei relevant to nuclear astrophysics and nuclear engineering, particularly for nuclei where phenomenological systematics from stable nuclei cannot be applied due to small neutron separation energies.
