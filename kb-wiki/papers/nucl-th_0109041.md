# Superfluid states with moving condensate in nuclear matter

> Superfluid states of symmetric nuclear matter with finite total momentum of Cooper pairs (nuclear LOFF phase) are studied with the use of Fermi-liquid theory in the model with Skyrme effective forces. It is considered the case of four-fold splitting of the excitation spectrum due to finite superfluid momentum and coupling of T=0 and T=1 pairing channels. It has been shown that at zero temperature the energy gap in triplet-singlet (TS) pairing channel (in spin and isospin spaces) for the SkM$^*$ force demonstrates double-valued behavior as a function of superfluid momentum. As a consequence, the phase transition at the critical superfluid momentum from the LOFF phase to the normal state will be of a first order. Behavior of the energy gap as a function of density for TS pairing channel under increase of superfluid momentum changes from one-valued to universal two-valued. It is shown that two-gap solutions, describing superposition of states with singlet-triplet (ST) and TS pairing of nucleons appear as a result of branching from one-gap ST solution. Comparison of the free energies shows that the state with TS pairing of nucleons is thermodynamically most preferable.

## Key claim

At zero temperature, the energy gap in the triplet-singlet (TS) pairing channel for the SkM$^*$ force demonstrates double-valued behavior as a function of superfluid momentum, which implies that the phase transition from the LOFF phase to the normal state will be of first order, contrary to the second-order transition found in earlier work by Sedrakian (2001) for weakly asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The author uses Fermi-liquid theory with Skyrme effective forces (SkM$^*$ and SkP parametrizations) to study superfluid states of symmetric nuclear matter where Cooper pairs have finite total momentum (the nuclear LOFF phase). The self-consistent equations for the order parameters $\Delta_{30}$ (TS pairing) and $\Delta_{03}$ (ST pairing) are solved numerically at zero temperature, including the coupling between $T=0$ and $T=1$ pairing channels and the renormalization of the single-particle spectrum through the normal Fermi-liquid amplitude, which introduces an effective mass $m^*$ in the linear-in-momentum term.

## Key numbers

- For the SkM$^*$ force at density $\varrho = 0.03\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ and zero temperature, the energy gap in the TS pairing channel shows double-valued behavior as a function of superfluid momentum, while for the SkP force the gap is single-valued.
- The critical superfluid momentum for the disappearance of TS superfluidity is $q_m \approx 0.108\,p_F$ for the SkM$^*$ force.
- The ratio $q/p_F$ at which the phase curve transitions from single-valued to double-valued behavior is $q_1/p_F$; at $q_c \approx 0.106\,p_F$ the two double-valued regions merge, and above $q_c$ the phase curves become closed oval curves.
- Two-gap TS-ST solutions exist for the SkM$^*$ force at $\varrho = 0.03\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ and zero temperature, branching from the one-gap ST solution, but no such solutions exist for the SkP potential.

## Context

This work extends the study of the nuclear LOFF phase by Sedrakian (2001), who considered only two-fold splitting of the quasiparticle spectrum and neglected coupling between isospin singlet and triplet pairing channels. The author incorporates the coupling of $T=0$ and $T=1$ pairing channels, leading to four-fold splitting and the possibility of two-gap superfluid states, as previously suggested by Isayev (2000). The results enable further investigation of first-order phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter and the thermodynamic competition between different pairing channels in the LOFF phase.
