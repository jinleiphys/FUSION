# Modern applications of covariant density functional theory

> Modern applications of Covariant Density Functional Theory (CDFT) are discussed. First we show a systematic investigation of fission barriers in actinide nuclei within constraint relativistic mean field theory allowing for triaxial deformations. In the second part we discuss a microscopic theory of quantum phase transitions (QPT) based on the relativistic generator coordinate method.

# Modern applications of covariant density functional theory

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT), when extended beyond axial symmetry to include triaxial deformations, reproduces inner fission barrier heights in actinide nuclei with an average deviation of 0.76 MeV from experiment, a level of accuracy comparable to the macroscopic+microscopic method. The paper also claims that the relativistic generator coordinate method (GCM) can describe quantum phase transitions at the critical point, specifically that the shape phase transition in the Nd isotope chain occurs precisely at 150Nd, in agreement with empirical evidence for X(5) symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculations allowing for triaxial deformations to map potential energy surfaces in the beta-gamma plane for even-even actinide nuclei, using the NL3* parameter set and a monopole pairing force with coupling constants adjusted to experimental odd-even mass differences. For quantum phase transitions, they applied the one-dimensional generator coordinate method with angular-momentum and particle-number projection of axially symmetric RMF+BCS wave functions generated by constraints on the axial mass quadrupole moment, using the relativistic point-coupling model PC-F1.

## Key numbers

- Inner fission barrier heights in actinide nuclei are reduced by 1-4 MeV when triaxial deformations are allowed, compared to axially symmetric solutions.
- The average deviation between calculated and experimental inner fission barrier heights is 0.76 MeV.
- The ground state of 240Pu has a deformation beta ~ 0.28, the axial saddle point at beta ~ 0.52, and a superdeformed minimum at beta ~ 0.96.
- A change of 1 MeV in the inner fission barrier height changes the calculated survival probability of a synthesized nucleus by about one order of magnitude or more.
- The typical uncertainty in experimental fission barrier values, as suggested by differences among compilations, is of the order of +/- 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic studies of fission barriers that demonstrated triaxiality reduces barrier heights (SBD 2009, WERP 2002), and addresses the slower progress in CDFT compared to Skyrme and Gogny-based approaches. The GCM treatment of quantum phase transitions builds on prior phenomenological boson model studies (Iachello 2001) and earlier relativistic GCM work (Niksic, Vretenar, Ring 2007), showing that the N=90 isotone 150Nd realizes X(5) symmetry. The results enable systematic microscopic studies of shape transitions across multiple mass regions and provide a foundation for comparing different energy-density functionals.
