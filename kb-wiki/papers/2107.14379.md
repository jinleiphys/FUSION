# Quantum Phase Transitions within a nuclear cluster model and an effective model of QCD

> The catastrophe theory is applied to a nuclear cluster model and an effective model for QCD at low energy. The study of quantum phase transitions in the cluster model was considered in an earlier publication, but restricted to spherical clusters and on a semi-classical level. In the present contribution, we include the case of deformed clusters and determine the spectrum numerically as a function of an interaction parameter, where signatures of a quantum phase transition can be seen. It is shown that in this more complicated case, with deformation of the clusters, the catastrophe theory can be applied with some interesting consequences. A further example of a many-body problem is considered, namely an effective model of QCD, which is able to describe the low energy hadron spectrum and, when temperature is introduced, even ratios of particle-antiparticle productions. The catastrophe theory is able to provide useful information on the phase transition from a perturbative to a non-perturbative vacuum. This contributions shows the universal usefulness of catastrophe theory, while more examples of applications to different fields are mentioned in the Introduction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that catastrophe theory can be applied to both a nuclear cluster model with deformed clusters and an effective model of QCD to describe quantum phase transitions, but the signatures of phase transitions in finite quantum systems are smoother than in the semi-classical description, with the exact quantum mechanical treatment showing continuous changes rather than discrete jumps.

## Method

The authors extended the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) to include deformed clusters by incorporating the expectation value of the quadrupole operator for each cluster, then obtained the semi-classical potential as the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in a coherent state basis. They applied catastrophe theory to determine the essential parameters and separatrices (bifurcation and Maxwell sets) in parameter space, and performed numerical diagonalizations of the Hamiltonian to obtain spectra as functions of interaction parameters. For the QCD model, they used a Hamiltonian describing quark-antiquark pairs and gluon pairs with a coherent state approach to derive the semi-classical potential and study the phase transition from perturbative to non-perturbative vacuum.

## Key numbers

- For the QCD effective model, the fermionic state energy scale is set at ω_q = 0.33 GeV, chosen because three times this value gives approximately the nucleon mass of 1 GeV
- Each fermionic state in the QCD model has a degeneracy of 2Ω = 18 (3 color, 3 flavour and 2 spin degrees of freedom)
- The gluon state energy is at 1.6 GeV, corresponding to two gluons with one-gluon energy of 0.8 GeV
- For the 16O+α → 20Ne system, the minimal number of relative oscillation quanta is n_0 = 8, with up to four excitation quanta N = 4 considered in numerical diagonalisation
- For the 12C+12C → 24Mg system, n_0 = 12, β_12C = -0.38, with N = 4 excitation quanta
- The phase transition in the SU(3)-SO(4) mixed Hamiltonian for 16O+α → 20Ne occurs at about c = 0.2

## Context

This work extends the earlier application of catastrophe theory to the SACM by Hess and collaborators (2019), which was restricted to spherical clusters and semi-classical analysis. The inclusion of deformed clusters (as in the 12C+12C system) and numerical diagonalization represents a significant advance. The application to an effective QCD model builds on prior work by the same group (QCD-0, QCD-1, QCD-2, QCD-3) that used standard methods to describe the low energy hadron spectrum and particle-antiparticle production ratios. The paper demonstrates that catastrophe theory provides a universal framework applicable to many-body problems ranging from nuclear cluster models to QCD vacuum structure, with the caveat that finite quantum systems show smoother transitions than the semi-classical description predicts.
