# Ultraperipheral vs ordinary nuclear interactions

> It is argued that the cross sections of ultraperipheral interactions of heavy nuclei can become comparable in value to those of their ordinary hadronic interactions at high energies. Simple estimates of corresponding "preasymptotic energy thresholds" are provided.The~method of equivalent photons is compared with the perturbative approach. The~situation at NICA/FAIR energies is discussed.

# Ultraperipheral vs ordinary nuclear interactions

## Key claim

Ultraperipheral cross sections for heavy nuclei can become comparable to ordinary hadronic cross sections at high energies, but only if the preasymptotic energy threshold (determined by the parameter \(u\) and the mass of the produced particle) is exceeded; for proton-proton collisions the required energy is unrealistically high (\(\sqrt{s_0} \approx e^{3500}\) GeV), whereas for lead-lead or gold-gold collisions the threshold can be as low as \(\gamma > 3.6\) per nucleon.

## Method

The author compares the energy dependence of ultraperipheral cross sections (calculated using the method of equivalent photons and the perturbative Born approximation) with measured inelastic hadronic cross sections for proton-proton collisions, and with geometrical estimates for heavy-ion collisions. For the exclusive production of a resonance \(R\) in two-photon collisions, the cross section is expressed analytically as \(\sigma_{AA}(R) \propto Z^4 \alpha^2 \ln^3(2um\gamma/m_R)\), where the parameter \(u\) encodes the formfactor-dependent cutoff on transverse momentum. The preasymptotic threshold is defined by the condition \(\gamma \gg m_R/(2um)\), and the value of \(u\) is taken from fits to formfactors (Vyzh, 2019) as \(u_{pp} \approx 0.2\) and \(u_{PbPb} \approx 0.02\).

## Key numbers

- For \(pp\) collisions at 13 TeV, the ultraperipheral cross section for \(\mu^+\mu^-\) production is 0.22 \(\mu\)b; after fiducial cuts (invariant mass, transverse momentum, pseudorapidity) it reduces to 3.35 pb, and after absorptive corrections to 3.06 \(\pm\) 0.05 pb, in agreement with the ATLAS measurement of 3.12 \(\pm\) 0.07 (stat.) \(\pm\) 0.10 (syst.) pb.
- For \(pp\) collisions, the energy at which ultraperipheral \(\pi^0\) production equals the hadronic partial cross section is estimated as \(\sqrt{s_0} \approx e^{3500}\) GeV, from the equality \(2.7 \times 10^{-9} \ln^2 \sqrt{s_0} \approx 3.28 \times 10^{-2}\).
- For \(AuAu\) collisions at 4.5 GeV per nucleon (\(\gamma = 4.5\)), the ultraperipheral \(e^+e^-\) cross section from the Racah formula is approximately 1 kb, exceeding the geometrical hadronic cross section of about 1500 mb.
- The preasymptotic threshold for heavy nuclei is \(\gamma > 3.6\) (from \(\gamma \gg m_R/(2um)\) with \(m_R = 2m_e\) and \(u \approx 0.02\)).
- The Coulomb correction to the Racah formula at NICA energies replaces the coefficient of the \(L^2\) term from \(-2.2\) to \(-4.2\), where \(L = \ln \gamma^2\).

## Context

This work extends the classic result of Landau and Lifshitz (1934) on ultraperipheral \(e^+e^-\) pair production and the method of equivalent photons (Fermi, 1924; Weizsacker, 1934; Williams, 1935) to preasymptotic energies relevant for NICA and FAIR. It contrasts with the more restrictive cutoff used by KMR (2011) and Geom (2018), which gave a four-times-larger preasymptotic threshold and excluded NICA energies. The analysis enables experimental searches for ultraperipheral \(\pi^0\) and \(e^+e^-\) production at NICA/FAIR, and suggests that dedicated detectors for low-energy electrons and photons in the central rapidity region could separate these signals from hadronic background.
