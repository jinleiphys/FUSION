# The hidden charm pentaquark states and $Σ_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ interaction in chiral perturbation theory

> In this work, we employ the heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (HHChPT) to calculate the $Σ_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ potentials to the next-to-leading order. The contact, the one-pion exchange and the two-pion exchange interactions are included. Besides, the mass splittings between the heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) multiplets are kept in calculations. Our result shows that neglecting the heavy quark symmetry (HQS) violation effect may be misleading to predict the potentials between the charmed hadrons. We perform numerical analysis with three scenarios. In the first scenario, we relate the low-energy constants (LECs) in the contact terms of $Σ_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ to those of nucleon systems, and reproduce the $P_c(4312)$ and $P_c(4440)$ as loosely bound states. In the second scenario, we vary the unknown LECs and find a small parameter region in which $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ can coexist as molecular states. In the third scenario, we include the coupled-channel effect on the basis of scenario II, and notice that the three $P_c$ states can be reproduced as molecular states simultaneously in a large region of parameters. Our analytical results can be used for the chiral extrapolations in lattice QCD. With the lattice QCD results in the future as inputs, the identification of the $P_c$ states and predictions for other systems would be more reliable.

## Key claim

The paper shows that neglecting heavy quark symmetry (HQS) violation effects may be misleading when predicting potentials between charmed hadrons, and that the three observed $P_c$ states (4312, 4440, 4457) can be reproduced as molecular states in chiral perturbation theory, but only when the mass splittings between heavy quark spin symmetry multiplets are properly included.

## Method

The authors employed heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (HHChPT) to calculate the $\Sigma_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ potentials to next-to-leading order, including contact, one-pion exchange, and two-pion exchange interactions while keeping the mass splittings between heavy quark spin symmetry multiplets. They performed numerical analysis with three scenarios: relating low-energy constants to those of nucleon systems (scenario I), varying unknown low-energy constants (scenario II), and including coupled-channel effects (scenario III), then solved the Schrödinger equation to identify bound states.

## Key numbers

- $P_c(4312)$ observed with 7.3$\sigma$ significance
- $\delta_a = M_{\Sigma_c^*} - M_{\Sigma_c} = 0.064$ GeV (mass splitting between $\Sigma_c$ and $\Sigma_c^*$)
- $\delta_b = M_{\bar{D}^*} - M_{\bar{D}} = 0.141$ GeV (mass splitting between $\bar{D}^*$ and $\bar{D}$)
- $m_\pi = 0.139$ GeV, $F_\pi = 0.092$ GeV (pion mass and decay constant)
- $g_a = -1.47$, $g_b = -0.59$ (axial coupling constants)
- Cutoff $\Lambda$ varied from 0.5 GeV to 0.8 GeV
- Minimum of potential function that generates loosely bound states ranges from -0.06 GeV to -0.15 GeV
- HQS violation correction changes the $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ potential in coordinate space by approximately 0.02-0.03 GeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to nuclear forces, originally developed by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and applied to nucleon systems by Epelbaum (2008) and Machleidt (2011), to the heavy hadron sector involving $\Sigma_c$ and $\bar{D}^{(*)}$ systems. It builds upon previous studies of heavy meson interactions using chiral perturbation theory by Valderrama (2012), Wang (2013), and Baru (2015), and directly addresses the newly observed $P_c$ states reported by LHCb (2015, 2019). The results provide analytical expressions that can be used for chiral extrapolations in lattice QCD, enabling more reliable identification of the $P_c$ states and predictions for other heavy hadron systems once lattice QCD inputs become available.
