# Self-consistent quantal treatment of decay rates within the perturbed static path approximation

> The framework of the Perturbed Static Path Approximation (PSPA) is used to calculate the partition function of a finite Fermi system from a Hamiltonian with a separable two body interaction. Therein, the collective degree of freedom is introduced in self-consistent fashion through a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. In this way all transport coefficients which dominate the decay of a meta-stable system are defined and calculated microscopically. Otherwise the same formalism is applied as in the Caldeira-Leggett model to deduce the decay rate from the free energy above the so called crossover temperature $T_0$.

## Key claim

The paper derives a decay rate for metastable Fermi systems within the Perturbed Static Path Approximation (PSPA) that is valid only above a crossover temperature \(T_0\); below this temperature the Gaussian integrals in the PSPA correction factor diverge, and the formalism cannot describe genuine quantum tunneling.

## Method

The partition function of a finite Fermi system with a separable two-body interaction is computed using the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a collective degree of freedom self-consistently. Small fluctuations around the static path are treated to second order, yielding a Gaussian correction factor that involves the retarded response function of the operator \(\hat{F}\). Dissipation is introduced by replacing the discrete independent-particle response function with a continuous Lorentzian model, from which local transport coefficients (inertia, stiffness, damping) are extracted. The decay rate is then obtained from the imaginary part of the free energy via the saddle-point approximation at the barrier and minimum of the SPA free energy.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature for damped motion is given by \(T_0(q_0) = \frac{\hbar |\varpi(q_0)|}{2\pi} \left(\sqrt{1 + \eta^2(q_0)} - \eta(q_0)\right)\), where \(\eta(q_0) = \Gamma(q_0)/(2|\varpi(q_0)|)\) is the dimensionless damping parameter.
- For strong damping \(\eta(q_0) \gg 1\), the local crossover temperature scales as \(T_0(q_0) \sim 1/(2\eta(q_0))\).
- The decay rate above \(T_0\) is expressed as \(R = \frac{2T_0}{\hbar} \frac{\operatorname{Im} Z(\beta)}{\operatorname{Re} Z(\beta)}\), where the partition functions are evaluated within the PSPA.
- The PSPA correction factor for damped systems is \(\mathcal{C}^{\text{PSPA}}(\beta, q_0) = \prod_{r>0} \frac{\nu_r^2 + \Gamma(q_0) \nu_r + \Omega^2(q_0)}{\nu_r^2 + \Gamma(q_0) \nu_r + \varpi^2(q_0)}\), with Matsubara frequencies \(\nu_r = 2\pi r T / \hbar\).

## Context

This work extends the Caldeira-Leggett model (Caldeira and Leggett, 1983) for dissipative tunneling to self-bound Fermi systems by introducing the collective degree of freedom self-consistently through a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, rather than assuming a phenomenological coupling to a linear heat bath. It builds on the Static Path Approximation (SPA) and its perturbative extension (PSPA) developed by Alhassid et al. (1984), Attias et al. (1997), and Rossignoli and Canosa (1997), and connects to the linear response formulation of nuclear transport theory reviewed by Hofmann (1997). The formalism enables microscopic calculation of all transport coefficients (inertia, stiffness, damping) from a separable two-body interaction, and provides a decay rate formula valid above the crossover temperature that reproduces Kramers' high-temperature limit.
