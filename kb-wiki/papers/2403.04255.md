# Non-equilibrium Green's function approach to low-energy fission dynamics: fluctuations in fission reactions

> We present a microscopic modeling for a decay of a heavy compound nucleus, starting from a nucleonic degree of freedom. To this end, we develop an approach based on a non-equilibrium Green's function, which is combined with a configuration interaction (CI) approach based on a constrained density-functional theory (DFT). We apply this approach to a barrier-top fission of $^{236}$U, restricting the model space to seniority zero configurations of neutrons and protons. We particularly focus on the distribution of the fission probability. We find that it approximately follows the chi-squared distribution with the number of degrees of freedom $ν$ of the order of 1, which is consistent with the experimental finding. We also show that $ν$ corresponds to the number of eigenstates of the many-body Hamiltonian whose energy is close to the excitation energy of the system and at the same time which have significant components on both sides of a fission barrier.

## Key claim

The fission probability distribution for barrier-top fission of $^{236}$U approximately follows a chi-squared distribution with a small number of degrees of freedom $\nu$ (of order 1), consistent with experimental findings, but only when the model space is restricted to seniority-zero configurations of both neutrons and protons; the authors note that the calculated fission-to-capture branching ratio underestimates the empirical value by a factor of about 40, indicating the need for a larger model space including seniority non-zero configurations.

## Method

The authors combined a non-equilibrium Green's function (NEGF) approach with a configuration interaction (CI) method based on constrained density-functional theory (DFT) using the UNEDF1 Skyrme functional. They discretized the fission path of $^{236}$U into 13 blocks from $Q=14$ b to $Q=79$ b, constructed many-particle many-hole configurations with seniority-zero neutrons and protons up to 5 MeV excitation, and computed transmission coefficients for capture and fission using the Datta formula with a block tri-diagonal Hamiltonian matrix including GOE random matrices for the compound nucleus and pre-fission configurations.

## Key numbers

* The dimension of each $Q$-block in the Hamiltonian kernel ranges from 2520 to 15088 across the 13 blocks from $Q=14$ b to $Q=79$ b.
* The pairing strength $G=0.16$ MeV and the diabatic interaction strength $h_2=1.5$ MeV were used.
* The GOE matrices had dimension $N_{\rm GOE}=1000$ and level density $\rho_0=31.8$ MeV$^{-1}$.
* The fission-to-capture branching ratio $\alpha^{-1}=0.071$ at $E=6.5$ MeV, underestimating the empirical value $\alpha^{-1}\simeq3$ by a factor of about 40.
* The extracted degrees of freedom $\nu=1.25$ at $E=6.5$ MeV, close to the experimental estimate of Porter and Thomas ($\nu=2.3\pm1.1$ at $E=6.536$ MeV).
* The level density for $K^\pi=0^+$ seniority-zero configurations at $E=6.5$ MeV was $\rho_{\nu=0}=220$ MeV$^{-1}$, while the total level density was $\rho_{\rm tot}=3.87\times10^5$ MeV$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the preliminary calculation of Bertsch (2023), which considered only neutron seniority-zero configurations and only the first fission barrier, by including both neutron and proton excitations and both the first and second fission barriers of $^{236}$U. The small $\nu$ values obtained are consistent with the transition state hypothesis of Bohr and Wheeler (1939) and with experimental analyses of Porter and Thomas (1956) and Leal (1999) for the $^{235}$U$(n,f)$ reaction. The approach enables microscopic investigation of the competition between radiative capture and fission in low-energy induced fission, with potential applications to r-process nucleosynthesis where heavy neutron-rich nuclei may undergo fission recycling.
