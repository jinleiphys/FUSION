# Two coexisting families of compact stars: observational implications for millisecond pulsars

> It is usually thought that a single equation of state (EoS) model "correctly" represents cores of all compact stars. Here we emphasize that two families of compact stars, viz., neutron stars and strange stars, can coexist in nature, and that neutron stars can get converted to strange stars through the nucleation process of quark matter in the stellar center. From our fully general relativistic numerical computations of the structures of fast-spinning compact stars, known as millisecond pulsars, we find that such a stellar conversion causes a simultaneous spin-up and decrease in gravitational mass of these stars. This is a new type of millisecond pulsar evolution through a new mechanism, which gives rise to relatively lower mass compact stars with higher spin rates. This could have implication for the observed mass and spin distributions of millisecond pulsars. Such a stellar conversion can also rescue some massive, spin-supported millisecond pulsars from collapsing into black holes. Besides, we extend the concept of critical mass $M_{\rm cr}$ for the neutron star sequence (Berezhiani et al. 2003; Bombaci et al. 2004) to the case of fast-spinning neutron stars, and point out that neutron star EoS models cannot be ruled out by the stellar mass measurement alone. Finally, we emphasize the additional complexity for constraining EoS models, for example, by stellar radius measurements using X-ray observations, if two families of compact stars coexist.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two families of compact stars, neutron stars and strange stars, can coexist in nature, and that the conversion of a neutron star into a strange star via quark matter nucleation in the stellar center causes a simultaneous spin-up and decrease in gravitational mass, provided the equation of state models allow the phase transition to occur (which depends on the specific EoS pair and the quark matter surface tension).

## Method

The authors performed fully general relativistic numerical computations of rapidly spinning compact star structures using the Cook et al. (1994) method, solving Einstein's field equations to obtain stellar parameters such as gravitational mass, spin frequency, equatorial radius, moment of inertia, rest mass, and angular momentum. They used two neutron star EoS models (TM1-2 with hyperons, and APR with only nucleons) and two strange star EoS models (extended MIT bag model with different bag constants and perturbative QCD corrections). For each EoS pair, they determined whether the quark deconfinement phase transition was energetically possible, computed the critical central density for nucleation (assuming a nucleation time of 1 year), and then calculated the pre- and post-conversion stellar configurations assuming conservation of rest mass and angular momentum during the conversion.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational masses for non-spinning configurations: NS1 (TM1-2) = 1.98 M_sun, NS2 (APR) = 2.19 M_sun, SS1 = 2.04 M_sun, SS2 = 2.48 M_sun
- For NS1+SS1 EoS models with surface tension sigma = 10 MeV/fm^2: critical density rho_cr = 6.8 x 10^14 g cm^-3; critical mass M_cr(nu) ranges from 1.61 M_sun (non-spinning) to 2.03 M_sun (mass-shed limit at 925 Hz)
- Post-conversion spin increase: nu increases by approximately 21-35%; gravitational mass decreases by approximately 7-9%; radius decreases by approximately 20-24%; moment of inertia decreases by approximately 17-26%
- For NS2+SS1 with sigma = 10 MeV/fm^2: M_cr = 2.16 M_sun, M_fin = 2.03 M_sun (very small range of metastable NS configurations)
- Effective maximum masses for NS1 at specific MSP spin rates: 1.62 M_sun for PSR J0437-4715 (nu = 173.6 Hz) and 1.64 M_sun for PSR J1614-2230 (nu = 317.5 Hz); corresponding post-conversion minimum SS masses: 1.48 M_sun and 1.49 M_sun respectively

## Context

This work extends the concept of a critical mass for the neutron star sequence, previously introduced by Berezhiani et al. (2003) and Bombaci et al. (2004), to the case of fast-spinning neutron stars. It builds on the idea of two coexisting families of compact stars (Bombaci & Datta 2000; Berezhiani et al. 2003; Bombaci et al. 2004, 2008, 2016; Drago et al. 2016) and the nucleation process of quark matter in neutron star cores. The paper enables future population studies of millisecond pulsars that include this conversion mechanism, and it complicates the standard approach of constraining a single equation of state from mass and radius measurements (e.g., with NICER), because two separate EoS models must be constrained and the conversion threshold identified.
