# Quasi-parton distribution functions: a study in the diquark spectator model

> A set of quasi-parton distribution functions (quasi-PDFs) have been recently proposed by Ji. Defined as the matrix elements of equal-time spatial correlations, they can be computed on the lattice and should reduce to the standard PDFs when the proton momentum $P_z$ is very large. Since taking the $P_z\to \infty$ limit is not feasible in lattice simulations, it is essential to provide guidance for what values of $P_z$ the quasi-PDFs are good approximations of standard PDFs. Within the framework of the spectator diquark model, we evaluate both the up and down quarks' quasi-PDFs and standard PDFs for all leading-twist distributions (unpolarized distribution $f_1$, helicity distribution $g_1$, and transversity distribution $h_1$). We find that, for intermediate parton momentum fractions $x$, quasi-PDFs are good approximations to standard PDFs (within $20-30%$) when $P_z\gtrsim 1.5-2$ GeV. On the other hand, for large $x\sim 1$ much larger $P_z > 4$ GeV is necessary to obtain a satisfactory agreement between the two sets. We further test the Soffer positivity bound, and find that it does not hold in general for quasi-PDFs.

## Key claim

Within the spectator diquark model, quasi-PDFs approximate standard PDFs to within 20-30% for intermediate parton momentum fractions x when the proton momentum P_z is at least 1.5-2 GeV, but for large x near 1, much larger P_z greater than 4 GeV is required for satisfactory agreement; additionally, the Soffer positivity bound does not hold in general for quasi-PDFs.

## Method

The authors computed both standard PDFs and quasi-PDFs for unpolarized (f1), helicity (g1), and transversity (h1) distributions at leading twist within the spectator diquark model, using scalar and axial-vector diquark spectators with dipolar form factors and previously fitted parameters from Bacchetta (2008). They evaluated the collinear distributions by integrating over transverse momentum k_perp, then compared the quasi-PDFs at finite P_z values (1, 2, 3, and 4 GeV) to the standard PDFs, and tested the Soffer inequality for the quasi-PDFs numerically.

## Key numbers

- Quasi-PDFs approximate standard PDFs within 20-30% for intermediate x (0.1 less than or similar to x less than or similar to 0.4-0.5) when P_z is greater than or similar to 1.5-2 GeV
- For large x near 1, P_z greater than 4 GeV is necessary for satisfactory agreement between quasi-PDFs and standard PDFs
- The unpolarized quasi-PDF for u and d quarks at P_z = 1 GeV shows significant deviation from the standard PDF, while at P_z greater than or equal to 2 GeV the shapes become similar
- The Soffer bound is saturated for the scalar diquark contribution to standard PDFs: h1^s(x) = (1/2)(f1^s(x) + g1^s(x)) = (g_s^2/(2pi)^2) * ((m+xM)^2 (1-x)^3)/(12 L_s^6(Lambda_s^2))
- For the axial-vector diquark contribution to standard PDFs, the Soffer bound is satisfied: (1/2)(f1^a(x) + g1^a(x)) - |h1^a(x)| = (g_a^2/(2pi)^2) * ((1-x)^3)/(24 L_a^4(Lambda_a^2)) greater than or equal to 0 for 0 less than or equal to x less than or equal to 1

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Ji (2013) to compute PDFs on the lattice via quasi-PDFs, providing model-based guidance on the required proton momentum for lattice simulations. The spectator diquark model, previously used by Bacchetta (2008) and others to study standard PDFs, allows direct comparison between quasi-PDFs and standard PDFs. The results indicate that lattice calculations at accessible momenta (around 2 GeV) may be sufficient for intermediate x, but the large-x region requires much higher momenta, and the violation of the Soffer bound for quasi-PDFs suggests that positivity constraints from standard PDFs do not directly apply to quasi-PDFs.
