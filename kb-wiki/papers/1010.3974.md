# The Decay Properties of the 1^{-+} Hybrid State

> Within the framework of the QCD sum rules, we consider the three-point correlation function, work at the limit q^2 -> 0 and m_π-> 0, and pick out the singular term ~ {1\over q^2} to extract the pionic coupling constants of the 1^{-+} hybrid meson. Then we calculate the decay widths of different modes. The decay width of the S-wave modes b_1 π, f_1πincreases quickly as the hybrid meson mass and decay momentum increase. But for the low mass hybrid meson around 1.6 GeV, the P-wave decay mode ρπis very important and its width is around 180 MeV, while the widths of ηπand η^\prime πare strongly suppressed. We suggest the experimental search of π_1(1600) through the decay chains at BESIII: e^+e^- -> J/ψ(ψ') -> π_1 +γor e^+e^- -> J/ψ(ψ') -> π_1 +ρwhere the π_1 state can be reconstructed through the decay modes π_1 -> ρπ-> π^+π^-π^0 or π_1 -> f_1(1285)π^0. It is also interesting to look for π_1 using the available BELLE/BABAR data through the process e^+e^- -> γ^\ast -> ρπ_1, b_1π_1, γπ_1 etc.

## Key claim

The decay width of the S-wave modes \(b_1 \pi, f_1\pi\) increases quickly as the hybrid meson mass and decay momentum increase, but for the low mass hybrid meson around 1.6 GeV, the P-wave decay mode \(\rho \pi\) is very important with a width around 180 MeV, while the widths of \(\eta \pi\) and \(\eta^\prime \pi\) are strongly suppressed.

## Method

Within the framework of QCD sum rules, the authors considered the three-point correlation function, worked at the limit \(q^2 \to 0\) and \(m_\pi \to 0\), and picked out the singular term \(\sim 1/q^2\) to extract the pionic coupling constants of the \(1^{-+}\) hybrid meson. They performed operator product expansion calculations, applied Borel transformations to suppress higher order contributions, and then calculated the decay widths of different modes including \(\rho \pi\), \(\eta \pi\), \(\eta^\prime \pi\), \(b_1 \pi\), and \(f_1 \pi\).

## Key numbers

- The decay width of \(\pi_1 \rightarrow \rho \pi\) is around 180 MeV for a hybrid meson mass of 1.6 GeV.
- The decay width of \(\pi_1 \rightarrow \eta \pi\) is around 0.9 MeV.
- The decay width of \(\pi_1 \rightarrow \eta^\prime \pi\) is about 0.1 MeV.
- The coupling constant \(g_{\rho \pi}\) is around 4 GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The coupling constant \(g^{\rm D}_2\) for the \(b_1 \pi\) mode is around \(-1.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The coupling constant \(g^{\rm S}_{b_1 \pi}\) is around 0.02 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of the \(1^{-+}\) hybrid meson mass by Chen et al. (2008) and provides a systematic calculation of decay properties using a formalism first developed by Reinders (1982). The results support the identification of \(\pi_1(1600)\) as a hybrid candidate and suggest experimental search strategies at BESIII through decay chains such as \(e^+e^- \rightarrow J/\psi(\psi') \rightarrow \pi_1 + \gamma\) or \(\pi_1 + \rho\), as well as using available BELLE/BABAR data. The calculations contradict earlier flux tube model predictions that the lowest-lying \(1^{-+}\) hybrid cannot decay into two ground states, and instead show that the \(\rho \pi\) mode is significant.
