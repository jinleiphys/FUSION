# Perturbative application of next-to-leading order pionless EFT for $A\le3$ nuclei in a finite volume

> Lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) calculations with physical pion mass would revolutionize nuclear physics by enabling predictions based on the fundamental theory of the strong force. To bridge the gap between finite-volume LQCD results and free-space physical observables, two primary extrapolation methods have been employed so far. The traditional approach relies on the Lüscher formula and its extensions, while a recent alternative employs effective field theories (EFTs) fitted directly to the finite volume data. In this study, we fit pionless EFT with perturbative inclusion of the next-to-leading order to finite-volume energies generated from a phenomenological $NN$ interaction. The theory is then used to extrapolate the finite-volume results into free space as well as to predict new few-body observables. As a benchmark, we also apply the Lüscher formalism directly to the finite-volume data. Through a comprehensive analysis, we explore the characteristics of order-by-order predictions of the pionless EFT fitted within a finite volume, investigate the limitations of the different extrapolation techniques used, and derive recommended box sizes required for reliable predictions.

## Key claim

Pionless effective field theory (EFT) with perturbative next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections, fitted to finite-volume energies generated from a phenomenological NN interaction, can extrapolate to free-space observables with high accuracy when box sizes L ≳ 7 fm are used, but the method introduces high-momentum physics outside the EFT's breakdown scale for smaller boxes.

## Method

The authors solved the few-body Schrödinger equation for A ≤ 3 nucleons in a periodic box using the stochastic variational method (SVM) with correlated Gaussian basis functions, employing the phenomenological Minnesota potential to generate artificial lattice QCD-like finite-volume data. They fitted leading-order (LO) and NLO pionless EFT low-energy constants (LECs) to these finite-volume energies using two approaches: one using ground-state and first-excited-state energies from a single box size, and another using ground-state energies from two adjacent box sizes. The NLO momentum-dependent terms were treated within first-order perturbation theory to maintain proper renormalization, and the fitted EFT was then solved in free space to predict binding energies and scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy from the Minnesota potential is E_d ≃ -2.202 MeV.
- For box sizes L ≳ 7 fm, NLO pionless EFT yields free-space deuteron binding energies with residual cutoff variation uncertainty of ∼3%, surpassing the expected NLO truncation error of (r_NN^1/a_NN^1)^2 ≃ 10%.
- The NN spin-triplet scattering length from the Minnesota potential is a_NN^1 ≃ 5.427 fm, and the effective range is r_NN^1 ≃ 1.758 fm.
- The Lüscher bound-state formula, Eq. (1), fitted to two adjacent box sizes (L_avg ± 1 fm), provides a solution only for L_avg ≳ 7 fm, and requires L_avg ≳ 12 fm to achieve ∼5% accuracy.
- Lüscher's scattering formula yields deuteron binding energies with ∼7% accuracy at L ≳ 11 fm using ground and first excited states from the same box, and 5% accuracy for average box sizes L_avg ≳ 8 fm using two adjacent ground-state energies.
- The two-body finite-volume first-excited-state energies exceed the pionless EFT breakdown scale for box sizes L ≲ 7 fm (deuteron channel) and L ≲ 8 fm (dineutron channel); ground-state energies exceed the breakdown scale for L ≲ 4 fm in both channels.

## Context

This work extends the approach of ElBaz et al. (2016), who first proposed fitting pionless EFT to finite-volume lattice QCD data, by including perturbative NLO corrections that were previously applied only in free-space calculations by Vanasse et al. (2013) and in few-body systems by SB (2023) and BSBB (2023). The study contrasts with DetRomShan (2023), who implemented NLO terms non-perturbatively and faced renormalization issues due to the Wigner bound. The results enable systematic order-by-order extrapolation of future lattice QCD calculations at physical pion mass to free-space observables, and provide recommended minimum box sizes (L ≳ 7 fm) for reliable NLO predictions.
