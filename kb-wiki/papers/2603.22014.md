# Jet quenching and its substructure dependence due to color decoherence

> Motivated by color coherence and decoherence effects in the QCD medium, we propose a theoretical framework that combines vacuum-like emissions and medium-induced radiation to study jet quenching and its dependence on jet cone sizes and substructure. In our approach, a jet produced at a hard scale $Q$ first undergoes vacuum-like evolution, as described by the well-established generating-function method in the double logarithmic approximation. These vacuum-like emissions generate subjets at an infrared momentum scale $Q_0$. Each subjet then experiences medium-induced energy loss as described by the BDMPS-Z formalism. By modeling the QCD bulk medium using OSU (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics and treating $Q_0$ together with the jet-quenching parameters at the initial proper time of the hydrodynamic evolution as free parameters, our approach provides a very good description of the inclusive jet modification factor $R_{AA}$ for large-radius jets and its dependence on jet substructure in 0-10% PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02~\rm{TeV}$, as measured by the ATLAS experiment.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions can be described by a two-stage process where a jet first undergoes vacuum-like parton shower evolution down to a scale \(Q_0\), and only then do the resulting resolved subjets each independently lose energy via medium-induced radiation, with the scale \(Q_0\) treated as a free parameter to be determined from experimental data rather than predicted from first principles.

## Method

The authors combine vacuum-like parton shower evolution in the double logarithmic approximation (DLA) with the BDMPS-Z formalism for medium-induced radiative energy loss. The QCD bulk medium is modeled using OSU (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics. The jet cross section in PbPb collisions is computed by convoluting the \(pp\) baseline with energy-loss probability distributions, where the number of independent subjets that lose energy is determined by the parton multiplicity at the decoherence scale \(Q_0\). The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is then calculated for 0-10% central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02~\rm{TeV}\) and compared with ATLAS measurements.

## Key numbers

* The jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) is defined via \(\omega_c \equiv \frac{1}{2} \hat{q} L^2\), where \(\omega_c\) sets the typical energy scale of medium-induced radiation for a static medium of length \(L\).
* The mean medium-induced energy loss for a single color charge is \(\Delta E_i = \frac{\alpha_s C_i}{2}\omega_c\), with \(C_i = 4/3\) for quarks and \(C_i = 3\) for gluons.
* Parton multiplicity distributions are computed for jet transverse momenta ranging from \(100~\rm{GeV}\) to \(1000~\rm{GeV}\), for jet radii \(R = 0.4\) and \(R = 1.0\), and for cutoff scales \(Q_0 = 20~\rm{GeV}\) and \(Q_0 = 35~\rm{GeV}\).
* The inclusive jet \(R_{AA}\) is computed for 0-10% PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02~\rm{TeV}\) and compared with ATLAS data for jet \(p_T\) up to \(1~\rm{TeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of KNO scaling in QCD jets for \(pp\) collisions (Duan 2025) to heavy-ion collisions by incorporating medium-induced energy loss. It contrasts with approaches that treat the jet as a single color charge (Baier 2001) and with frameworks such as JEWEL (Zapp 2013), Hybrid (Casalderrey-Solana 2014), LBT (He 2015), JETSCAPE (2017), and LIDO (Ke 2018), which incorporate vacuum-like emissions and medium-induced radiation in different ways. The framework enables systematic study of how jet substructure and cone-size dependence of jet quenching arise from color decoherence effects, providing a pathway to reduce model discrepancies in the theoretical description of jet energy loss.
