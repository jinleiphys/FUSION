# Probing high-density symmetry energy using heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> The nuclear symmetry energy, which describes the energy difference of per proton and neutron in nuclear matter, has been extensively studied within the last two decades. Around saturation density, both the value and the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy have been roughly constrained, its high-density behavior is now still in argument. Probing high-density symmetry energy at terrestrial laboratories is being carried out at facilities that offer radioactive beams worldwide. While relevant experiments are being conducted, we theoretically developed more advanced isospin-dependent transport model including new physics such as nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations and in-medium isospin-dependence of baryon-baryon scattering cross section. New sensitive probes of high-density symmetry energy are provided, such as squeezed-out neutron to proton ratio, photon and light cluster as well as the production of mesons with strangeness or hidden strangeness. The blind spots of probing the high-density symmetry energy by sensitive observable are demonstrated. Model dependence of frequently used sensitive probes of the symmetry energy has been studied thoroughly based on different transport models. A qualitative observable of neutron to proton ratio at high emitting energy is proposed to probe the high-density symmetry energy qualitatively. The probed density regions of the symmetry energy are carefully studied. Effects of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations on the some sensitive observables of the symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions are explored carefully. Probing the curvature of the symmetry energy by involving the slope information of the symmetry energy at saturation point in the transport model is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the value and slope of the nuclear symmetry energy around saturation density are roughly constrained, its high-density behavior remains uncertain; the authors develop an improved isospin-dependent transport model incorporating nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations and in-medium isospin-dependent baryon-baryon scattering to provide new sensitive probes, but caution that a less density-dependent high-density symmetry energy creates a "blind spot" where it cannot be effectively probed in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent single-nucleon mean-field potential parameterized by a stiffness parameter \(x\) to mimic different density-dependent symmetry energies. They incorporated in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross sections reduced by the square of the ratio of effective to free reduced masses, and included nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations via a high-momentum tail in the nucleon momentum distribution. Simulations were performed for various heavy-ion reactions (e.g., \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn, \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au) at beam energies from 50 MeV/nucleon to 1 GeV/nucleon, and results were compared with experimental data from FOPI and FOPI-LAND.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0) = 30\) MeV (model input, consistent with the literature value \(31.7\pm 3.2\) MeV).
- Slope of symmetry energy at saturation: \(L = 58.7\pm 28.1\) MeV (cited from literature).
- Saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\); binding energy \(E_0 = -16\) MeV; incompressibility \(K_0 = 230\) MeV.
- In the \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon, the squeezed-out neutron-to-proton ratio shows a symmetry energy effect as high as 40% at high transverse momenta.
- The \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio in central \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn at 400 MeV/nucleon saturates at about 25 fm/c, indicating chemical freeze-out.
- In Au+Au reactions, \(\eta\) meson multiplicity saturates at incident energy of about 10 GeV/nucleon.
- The FOPI pion data supports a softer symmetry energy (parameter \(x = 1, 2, \text{ or } 3\)); combining with FOPI-LAND elliptic flow data yields \(x = 1\) (mildly soft symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the nuclear symmetry energy using heavy-ion collisions, particularly building on the IBUU04 model (Lyzenga et al., 2005) and the UrQMD model (Bass et al., 1998; Bleicher et al., 1999). It addresses the ongoing debate on the high-density symmetry energy behavior, which affects neutron star properties (Lattimer and Prakash, 2001; Steiner et al., 2005) and gravitational-wave signals from neutron star mergers (GW170817). The paper enables future experiments at facilities such as FAIR (Germany), HIRFL-CSR (China), FRIB (USA), RIBF (Japan), and RISP (Korea) to use the proposed qualitative observable (normal vs. abnormal isospin fractionation of energetic nucleons) and to cross-check constraints from multiple probes to reduce model dependence.
