# Probing the Odderon in coherent hadron - hadron interactions at CERN LHC

> One of the open questions of the strong interaction theory is the existence of the Odderon, which is an unambiguous prediction of Quantum Chromodynamics, but still not confirmed in the experiment. In this paper we propose the study of the diffractive $η_c$ photoproduction in coherent interactions as a new alternative to probe the Odderon in $pp$ and $PbPb$ collisions at CERN - LHC. As the Pomeron exchange cannot contribute to this process, its observation would indicate the existence of the Odderon. We predict total cross sections of order of $pb \, (μb)$ for $pp \, (PbPb)$ collisions and large values for the event rates/year, which makes, in principle, the experimental analysis of this process feasible at LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that diffractive $\eta_c$ photoproduction in coherent hadron-hadron collisions at the LHC can serve as a clean probe of the Odderon, because the Pomeron cannot contribute to this process. However, the feasibility depends on the experimental ability to separate the signal from backgrounds, particularly from photon-photon interactions and multiple hadron-hadron collisions per bunch crossing at high luminosities.

## Method

The total cross section for coherent $h_1 h_2 \rightarrow h_1 \otimes \eta_c \otimes h_2$ is factorized into the equivalent photon flux of one hadron and the $\gamma h \rightarrow \eta_c h$ photoproduction cross section. The photoproduction amplitude is computed using the impact factor representation, convolving the $\gamma \eta_c$ impact factor (calculated perturbatively) and the proton impact factor (modeled non-perturbatively) with the Odderon Green function from the Bartels-Kwiecinski-Praszalowicz (BKP) equation. Two Odderon models are used: the Bartels-Lipatov-Vacca (BLV) solution with intercept exactly one (BBCV model) and a simplified three-gluon exchange model (CKMS model), with a realistic strong coupling $\alpha_s = 0.3$.

## Key numbers

- For $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 14$ TeV, the total cross section is predicted to be $0.65$ pb (CKMS model) and $13.90$ pb (BBCV model).
- For $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8$ TeV, the total cross section is $0.55$ pb (CKMS) and $10.10$ pb (BBCV).
- For $PbPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV, the total cross section is $0.40$ $\mu$b (CKMS) and $23.59$ $\mu$b (BBCV).
- For $PbPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, the total cross section is $0.30$ $\mu$b (CKMS) and $14.25$ $\mu$b (BBCV).
- The predicted event rate/year for $pp$ at 14 TeV is larger than 65000 (using design luminosity $10^{34}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$), but a more realistic luminosity of $10^{32}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$ reduces this by a factor $10^2$.
- The maximum center-of-mass energy for the $\gamma h$ system at LHC is $\approx 8390$ GeV for $pp$ and $\approx 950$ GeV for $PbPb$, compared to about 200 GeV at HERA.

## Context

This work extends the search for the Odderon beyond previous attempts at HERA (where cross sections were too small to observe) and elastic $pp$ scattering studies (Dosch and Ewerz, 2007). It proposes coherent hadron-hadron collisions as a new experimental avenue, complementing the exclusive vector meson hadroproduction approach of other authors (2012). The results enable future experimental searches at the LHC, provided forward detectors can identify the scattered hadrons and reject backgrounds from photon-photon interactions and pile-up events.
