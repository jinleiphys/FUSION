# Neutron-proton mass difference in finite nuclei and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly

> The neutron-proton mass difference in finite nuclei is studied in the framework of a medium-modified Skyrme model. The possible interplay between the effective nucleon mass in finite nuclei and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly is discussed. In particular, we find that a correct description of the properties of mirror nuclei leads to a stringent restriction of possible modifications of the nucleon's effective mass in nuclei.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton mass difference in finite nuclei is slightly increased compared to free space, and a correct description of mirror nuclei properties imposes a stringent restriction on possible modifications of the nucleon's effective mass in nuclei, though the model predicts a strongly decreased effective nucleon mass at the center of a nucleus.

## Method

The authors study the neutron-proton mass difference in finite nuclei using a medium-modified Skyrme model Lagrangian, where the influence of the surrounding nuclear environment is encoded in medium functionals depending on local neutron and proton densities. They solve the classical equations of motion for a skyrmion placed at the center of a spherical nucleus (spherically symmetric case) and also develop a variational approximation with trial functions for nonspherical configurations where the nucleon is displaced from the nuclear center. The electromagnetic part of the mass difference is computed from the electric and magnetic form factors using the Cottingham formula.

## Key numbers

- Free-space proton mass: 938.27 MeV; free-space neutron mass: 939.56 MeV (PDG values used as input)
- Free-space neutron-proton mass difference: 1.291 MeV (exact solution) or 1.313 MeV (variational approximation)
- In-medium proton mass at center of ¹⁶O: 585.487 MeV (exact solution); at center of ⁴⁰Ca: 557.621 MeV
- In-medium neutron-proton mass difference at center of ¹⁶O: 1.697 MeV; at center of ⁴⁰Ca: 1.804 MeV
- Electromagnetic part of neutron-proton mass difference in free space: -0.686 MeV; at center of ¹⁶O: -0.517 MeV; at center of ⁴⁰Ca: -0.478 MeV
- Pion-decay constant used: Fπ = 108.783 MeV; Skyrme constant: e = 4.854
- Input parameters for medium functionals: b̃₀ = -1.206 mπ⁻¹, b̃₁ = -1.115 mπ⁻¹, c₀ = 0.21 mπ⁻³, c₁ = 0.165 mπ⁻³, g′ = 0.47

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the neutron-proton mass difference in infinite asymmetric nuclear matter (Meissner, 2007) to the case of finite nuclei, incorporating density gradients and p-wave pion-nucleus scattering contributions. The study addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, a long-standing puzzle in nuclear physics (Nolen and Schiffer, 1969) that has been approached by many theoretical methods (Shlomo, 1982; Auerbach, 2001; He, 1989; and others) but remains not fully understood. The results enable future calculations of isospin-breaking effects in mirror nuclei and may have applications in nuclear astrophysics.
