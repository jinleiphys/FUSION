# Classical Gluon Production Amplitude for Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions: First Saturation Correction in the Projectile

> We calculate the classical single-gluon production amplitude in nucleus-nucleus collisions including the first saturation correction in one of the nuclei (the projectile) while keeping multiple-rescattering (saturation) corrections to all orders in the other nucleus (the target). In our approximation only two nucleons interact in the projectile nucleus: the single-gluon production amplitude we calculate is order-g^3 and is leading-order in the atomic number of the projectile, while resumming all order-one saturation corrections in the target nucleus. Our result is the first step towards obtaining an analytic expression for the first projectile saturation correction to the gluon production cross section in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the classical single-gluon production amplitude in nucleus-nucleus collisions at order \(g^3\), which represents the first saturation correction in the projectile nucleus while resumming all multiple-rescattering (saturation) corrections in the target nucleus; this is only the first step toward the full cross section, as the complete expression also requires the calculation of an additional \(g^5\) amplitude contribution left for future work.

## Method

The calculation is performed in the light-cone gauge \(A^+ = 0\) of the projectile, modeling the projectile nucleus with two interacting quarks (from two different nucleons) and the target nucleus as a shock wave that resums all multiple interactions via Wilson lines. The authors compute the order-\(g^3\) amplitude by evaluating Feynman diagrams with retarded gluon Green functions (which incorporate certain contributions from the complex conjugate amplitude) and by replacing products of fundamental color matrices with commutators where necessary, thereby isolating the surviving diagrams \(B_9\) and \(B_{11}\) (and their symmetric \(C\)-graph counterparts) that contribute to the amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The single-gluon production amplitude is calculated at order \(g^3\), which is leading-order in the atomic number of the projectile (order \(\as^3 A_1^{2/3}\)).
* The cross section expansion parameter for the projectile is \(\as^2 A_1^{1/3} \ll 1\), while for the target \(\as^2 A_2^{1/3} \sim 1\).
* The gluon dipole \(S\)-matrix in the quasi-classical MV/Glauber–Mueller approximation is given by \(S_G ({\vec x}_\perp , {\vec y}_\perp) = \exp \left[ - \frac{1}{4} ({\vec x}_\perp - {\vec y}_\perp)^2 \, Q_{sG}^2 \left( \frac{{\vec x}_\perp + {\vec y}_\perp}{2} \right) \, \ln \frac{1}{|{\vec x}_\perp - {\vec y}_\perp| \, \Lambda} \right]\), where \(Q_{sG}^2 ({\vec b}_\perp) = 4 \pi \as^2 \, T({\vec b}_\perp)\).

## Context

This work extends the known analytic result for proton-nucleus (\(p+A\)) collisions (Kovchegov, 1998), which gives the coefficients \(c_{1,n}\) in the expansion of the gluon production cross section, toward the full nucleus-nucleus (\(A+A\)) case by calculating the first projectile saturation correction (coefficients \(c_{2,n}\)). The result is a step toward obtaining an analytic expression for the function \(f(Q_{s1}^2/k_T^2, Q_{s2}^2/k_T^2)\) that governs classical gluon production in heavy-ion collisions, which has so far only been studied numerically (Krasnitz et al., 1998-2003; Lappi, 2003; Blaizot et al., 2010); an analytic form would facilitate the inclusion of small-\(x\) evolution and running-coupling corrections needed for phenomenological applications.
