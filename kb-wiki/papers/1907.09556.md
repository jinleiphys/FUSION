# Heavy quark dynamics in a hot magnetized QCD medium

> The heavy quark drag and momentum diffusion have been investigated in a hot magnetized quark-gluon plasma, along the directions parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field. The analysis is done within the framework of Fokker-Planck dynamics by considering the heavy quark scattering with thermal quarks and gluons at the leading order in the coupling constant. An extended quasiparticle model is adopted to encode the thermal QCD medium interactions in the presence of a magnetic field. Further, the higher Landau level effects on the temperature behaviour of the parallel and perpendicular components of the drag force and diffusion coefficients have studied. It has been observed that both the equation of state and the magnetic field play key roles in the temperature dependence of the heavy quark dynamics.

## Key claim

The heavy quark drag and momentum diffusion in a hot magnetized quark-gluon plasma are anisotropic, with the anisotropy and the magnitude of the transport coefficients depending significantly on both the equation of state (EoS) of the medium and the inclusion of higher Landau levels (HLLs), not just the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation used in prior work.

## Method

The authors employed the Fokker-Planck equation to describe heavy quark (HQ) dynamics, considering leading-order scattering of HQs with thermal gluons and quarks via one-gluon exchange in the presence of a strong magnetic field. The thermal QCD medium interactions were encoded using an effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) that incorporates a realistic (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equation of state, with the magnetic field affecting quarks through Landau level quantization and gluons indirectly through the Debye screening mass. The longitudinal and transverse components of the HQ drag force and momentum diffusion coefficients were computed separately for gluon and quark contributions, including the effects of higher Landau levels (up to l=4).

## Key numbers

*   The gluon contribution to the longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficient at leading order is \(K^{gluon}_{\parallel} = \frac{8\pi}{9}\alpha_{eff}^2 N_c C_R^{HQ} \frac{1}{d_{HQ}} T^3 \left[\log\left(\frac{2T}{m_D}\right) + 2\xi\right]\), where \(\xi = \frac{1}{2} - \gamma_E + \frac{\zeta'(2)}{\zeta(2)}\).
*   The transverse quark contribution to the HQ drag force is given by \(A_{\perp}^{quark} = 2 T \alpha^2_{eff} N_c C_R^{HQ} \frac{\sqrt{|eB|}}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\infty} dx \frac{\sqrt{x} N(T,x)}{(x + 2\frac{\alpha_{eff}}{\pi} N(T,x))^2}\), where \(N(T,x)\) is defined in Eq. (4.7).
*   The transverse quark contribution to the momentum diffusion coefficient is \(K_{\perp}^{quark} = 4 T \alpha^2_{eff} N_c C_R^{HQ} \frac{|eB|}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\infty} dx \frac{x N(T,x)}{(x + 2\frac{\alpha_{eff}}{\pi} N(T,x))^2}\).
*   Numerical results are presented for a magnetic field strength of \(|eB| = 15 m_{\pi}^2\) and a temperature range near the transition temperature \(T_c = 0.17\) GeV.
*   The quark contribution to the longitudinal drag and diffusion vanishes at leading order in the strong magnetic field limit.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of heavy quark transport in a magnetized QGP beyond the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation used by Fukushima (2015) to include higher Landau levels (HLLs) and a realistic equation of state. It also builds on the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) developed by Chandra (2011, 2007) to encode thermal QCD medium effects. The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy quark observables, such as directed flow (\(v_1\)), in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where strong transient magnetic fields are present.
