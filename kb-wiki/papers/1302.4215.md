# Description of the ψ(3770) resonance interfering with the background

> The parameters of the interfering $ψ(3770)$ resonance should be determined from the data on the reactions $e^+e^-$\,$\to$\,$D\bar D$ with the use of the models satisfying the elastic unitarity requirement. The selection of such models can be realized by comparing their predictions with the relevant data on the shape of the $ψ(3770)$ peak in the non-$D\bar D$ decay channels. Here, we illustrate this unitarity approach by the example of the most simple variant of the model of the mixed $ψ(3770)$ and $ψ(2S)$ resonances. When new high-statistics data become available, it will be interesting to test this clarity variant.

## Key claim

The parameters of the interfering ψ(3770) resonance should be determined from e+e− → D D̅ data using models that satisfy elastic unitarity; the simplest working variant of the mixed ψ(3770) and ψ(2S) resonance model is presented as an illustration, but the fitted values of the ψ(3770) mass and couplings can depend essentially on the model used.

## Method

The authors construct a unitarized model of the e+e− → D D̅ reaction by representing the D-meson isoscalar electromagnetic form factor F0D in terms of mixed ψ(3770) and ψ(2S) inverse propagators, with the imaginary part of the ψ(3770)–ψ(2S) mixing amplitude arising from real D D̅ intermediate states. They fit this model to existing e+e− → D D̅ cross-section data from BES, CLEO, BaBar, and Belle, then use the fitted parameters to predict cross sections for non-D D̅ channels (γχc0, J/ψη, φη, γηc, γη′) in the ψ(3770) region, estimating coupling constants from single-energy-point CLEO measurements where available.

## Key numbers

- ψ(3770) mass mψ′′ = 3.794 GeV (fitted)
- ψ(3770) coupling to D D̅: gψ′′D D̅ = ±14.35, giving Γψ′′D D̅(m2ψ′′) ≈ 56.8 MeV
- ψ(3770) coupling to virtual photon: gψ′′γ = ±0.1234 GeV2, giving Γψ′′e+e− ≈ 0.062 keV
- ψ(2S) mass fixed at mψ(2S) = 3.6861 GeV, with Γψ(2S)e+e− = 2.35 keV and gψ(2S)γ = ±0.7262 GeV2
- ψ(2S) coupling to D D̅: gψ(2S)D D̅ = ±20.11
- For e+e− → γχc0 at √s = 3773 MeV: σ ≈ 72 ± 9 pb (CLEO single-point measurement)
- For e+e− → J/ψη at √s = 3773 MeV: σ ≈ 5.5 ± 2.5 pb (CLEO single-point measurement)
- For e+e− → φη at √s = 3773 MeV: σ ≈ 2.4 ± 0.6 pb (CLEO single-point measurement)
- Estimated gψ′′γχc0 ≈ ±0.608 GeV−1 (case +−+) or ±0.721 GeV−1 (case −+−)
- Estimated gψ′′J/ψη ≈ ±0.0375 GeV−1 (case +−+) or ±0.065 GeV−1 (case −+−)
- Estimated gψ′′φη ≈ ±1.1×10−2 GeV−1 (case +−+) or ±1.11×10−2 GeV−1 (case −+−)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Achasov and Shestakov, 2012) of unitarized models for e+e− → D D̅ in the ψ(3770) region, which revealed spurious ambiguities in resonance parameters when using unitarily uncorrected parametrizations (as noted by experimental groups including BES, CLEO, BaBar, and Belle, and in the PDG). The simplest mixed-resonance variant presented here enables testing of theoretical models by comparing their predictions with high-statistics data expected from CLEO-c and BESIII on both D D̅ and non-D D̅ channels, which will impose severe restrictions on dynamical models for the ψ(3770) resonance interfering with background.
