# Description of the Ōą(3770) resonance interfering with the background

> The parameters of the interfering $Ōą(3770)$ resonance should be determined from the data on the reactions $e^+e^-$\,$\to$\,$D\bar D$ with the use of the models satisfying the elastic unitarity requirement. The selection of such models can be realized by comparing their predictions with the relevant data on the shape of the $Ōą(3770)$ peak in the non-$D\bar D$ decay channels. Here, we illustrate this unitarity approach by the example of the most simple variant of the model of the mixed $Ōą(3770)$ and $Ōą(2S)$ resonances. When new high-statistics data become available, it will be interesting to test this clarity variant.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Achasov and Shestakov, 2012) of unitarized models for e+e‚ąí ‚Üí D DŐÖ in the Ōą(3770) region, which revealed spurious ambiguities in resonance parameters when using unitarily uncorrected parametrizations (as noted by experimental groups including BES, CLEO, BaBar, and Belle, and in the PDG). The simplest mixed-resonance variant presented here enables testing of theoretical models by comparing their predictions with high-statistics data expected from CLEO-c and BESIII on both D DŐÖ and non-D DŐÖ channels, which will impose severe restrictions on dynamical models for the Ōą(3770) resonance interfering with background.
