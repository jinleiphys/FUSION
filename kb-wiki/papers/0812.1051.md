# Heavy-quark energy loss and thermalization in a strongly coupled SYM plasma

> Using the AdS/CFT correspondence, we compute the radiative energy loss of a slowly decelerating heavy quark with mass M moving through a supersymmetric Yang Mills (SYM) plasma at temperature T at large t'Hooft coupling λ. The calculation is carried out in terms of perturbation in \sqrtλT/M, and the rate of the energy loss is computed up to second order. We explain the physical meaning of each correction and estimate the thermalization time of a heavy quark moving in a strongly-coupled plasma.

## Key claim

The authors compute the radiative energy loss of a heavy quark decelerating in a strongly coupled SYM plasma, treating the deceleration as a perturbation in the small parameter \(\sqrt{\lambda}T/M\). The leading-order result from the stationary trailing-string picture is corrected at first and second order in this expansion, and the corrections are positive (increasing the energy loss rate) but suppressed by an additional factor \(\gamma^{-3/2}\) in the ultrarelativistic limit.

## Method

The calculation uses the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a heavy quark (mass \(M\)) moving through an \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma at temperature \(T\) and large 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\). Starting from the stationary trailing-string solution of Herzog et al. (2006) and Gubser (2006), the authors turn off the external drag force and solve the Nambu-Goto equations of motion perturbatively in \(\dot{v} \sim \sqrt{\lambda}T^2/M\). The string shape is expanded to first and second order in \(\dot{v}\), and the energy flow at the string horizon \(u_s = \sqrt{\gamma}\,\pi T\) (the scale separating the quark's wave function from emitted radiation) gives the radiative energy loss.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order radiative energy loss is \(\Phi(t,u_s) = \frac{\sqrt{\lambda}}{2\pi}\gamma v^2 (\pi T)^2\) (Eq. labeled Phi_us_1stOrder_SYM).
* The first-order correction to the energy loss is proportional to \(\frac{\sqrt{\lambda} T}{2M}\) and is suppressed by \(\gamma^{-3/2}\) in the ultrarelativistic limit \(\gamma \gg 1\) (Eq. labeled Phi_us_1stOrder_SYM).
* The deceleration at first order is \(\dot{v}(t) = -\frac{v\, u_h^2}{\gamma^2 (u_m - u_s)}\) where \(u_h = \pi T\) and \(u_m\) is related to the quark mass (Eq. vdot_1stOrder).
* The expansion parameter is \(\frac{\sqrt{\lambda} T}{2M}\) (derived from \(M = \frac{\sqrt{\lambda}}{2\pi}(u_m - u_s)\)).

## Context

This work extends the stationary trailing-string model of Herzog et al. (2006) and Gubser (2006) to the physically realistic case of a decelerating heavy quark, where the external drag force is turned off. It provides the first perturbative calculation of the radiative energy loss beyond the infinite-mass limit, showing that lighter quarks lose energy faster. The results enable estimates of the thermalization time of a heavy quark in a strongly coupled plasma and can be compared to pQCD-based energy-loss calculations (e.g., Baier et al. (1996), Zakharov (1996), Gyulassy (2000)). The perturbative expansion breaks down at very low velocities, indicating that the heavy quark thermalizes before the string picture becomes invalid.
