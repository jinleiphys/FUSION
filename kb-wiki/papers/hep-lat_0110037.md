# Topological Charge Fluctuations and Low-Lying Dirac Eigenmodes

> We discuss the utility of low-lying Dirac eigenmodes for studying the nature of topological charge fluctuations in QCD. The implications of previous results using the local chirality histogram method are discussed, and the new results using the overlap Dirac operator in Wilson gauge backgrounds at lattice spacings ranging from a~0.04 fm to a~0.12 fm are reported. While the degree of local chirality does not change appreciably closer to the continuum limit, we find that the size and density of local structures responsible for chiral peaking do change significantly. The resulting values are in disagreement with the assumptions of the Instanton Liquid Model. We conclude that the fluctuations of topological charge in the QCD vacuum are not locally quantized.

## Key claim

The paper argues that topological charge fluctuations in the QCD vacuum are not locally quantized, contradicting the instanton-based semiclassical picture; the observed double-peaked local chirality distribution does not imply the dominance of instanton-like objects, and quantitative comparisons with the Instanton Liquid Model (ILM) show clear disagreement.

## Method

The authors used the overlap Dirac operator on Wilson gauge backgrounds at lattice spacings from approximately 0.04 fm to 0.12 fm to compute low-lying Dirac eigenmodes. They analyzed the local chirality parameter X(n) distribution and identified local maxima of the fermion density, selecting structures that decay along axis directions and contribute to the peaks of the X-distribution, then measured their size and density.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings studied: a ≈ 0.04 fm to a ≈ 0.12 fm
- At a ≈ 0.042 fm, the typical size of the chirality correlator in the lowest modes (upper bound on average size of individual structures) is about 0.23 fm
- ILM parameters used for comparison: average instanton radius ρ ≈ 1/3 fm and density n ≈ 1 fm⁻⁴
- The size and density of local structures found are significantly below the ILM assumptions close to the continuum limit (specific numerical values from the figures are not given in the text)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using the local chirality histogram method with Wilson fermions at a ≈ 0.17 fm (Horvath et al., 2001), which found results inconsistent with instanton-like vacuum, and later follow-up works that observed visible double-peaked behavior at smaller lattice spacings. The results contradict the Instanton Liquid Model (ILM) and challenge the semiclassical picture of the QCD vacuum (Witten, 1979), suggesting that topological charge fluctuations are not locally quantized and enabling further investigation of the vacuum structure using fermionic smoothing.
