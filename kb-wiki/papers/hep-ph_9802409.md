# Mixing and Decay Constants of Pseudoscalar Mesons

> We propose a new eta-eta' mixing scheme where we start from the quark flavor basis and assume that the decay constants in that basis follow the pattern of particle state mixing. On exploiting the divergences of the axial vector currents - which embody the axial vector anomaly - all basic parameters are fixed to first order of flavor symmetry breaking. That approach naturally leads to a mass matrix, quadratic in the masses, with specified elements. We also test our mixing scheme against experiment and determine corrections to the first order values of the basic parameters from phenomenology. Finally, we generalize the mixing scheme to include the eta(c). Again the divergences of the axial vector currents fix the mass matrix and, hence, mixing angles and the charm content of the eta and eta'.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the decay constants of pseudoscalar mesons in the quark flavor basis (non-strange and strange) follow the same pattern as particle state mixing, which reduces the number of free parameters to three and, to first order of flavor symmetry breaking, leaves no free parameter at all.

## Method

The authors start from the quark flavor basis (non-strange $\eta_q$ and strange $\eta_s$ states) and assume that the decay constants in this basis follow the pattern of particle state mixing with a single mixing angle $\phi$. They exploit the divergences of the axial vector currents, which incorporate the axial anomaly, to relate the mixing angle to the ratio of decay constants $f_q/f_s$ and to fix the mass matrix elements using flavor symmetry relations connecting $m_{qq}^2$ and $m_{ss}^2$ to pion and kaon masses. The scheme is then generalized to include the $\eta_c$ meson using a three-angle parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Averaged mixing angle $\overline{\phi} = 39.3^\circ \pm 1.0^\circ$ from seven different physical processes
- Ratio of decay constants $f_q/f_s = y = 0.81 \pm 0.03$ from the mass matrix and mixing angle
- $f_q = (1.07 \pm 0.02) f_\pi$ and $f_s = (1.34 \pm 0.06) f_\pi$ from two-photon decays and the ratio $y$
- From $J/\psi \to \rho P$ decays: $\phi = 39.9^\circ \pm 2.9^\circ$ using the experimental ratio $0.54 \pm 0.11$ for $\Gamma[J/\psi \to \eta'\rho] / \Gamma[J/\psi \to \eta\rho]$
- From $J/\psi \to P\gamma$ decays: $\phi = 39.0^\circ \pm 1.6^\circ$ using the measured ratio $R_{J/\psi} = 5.0 \pm 0.6$
- From $a_2 \to P\pi$ decays: $\phi = 43.1^\circ \pm 3.0^\circ$ using the experimental ratio $0.039 \pm 0.008$ for $\Gamma[a_2 \to \eta'\pi] / \Gamma[a_2 \to \eta\pi]$
- From $\eta' \to \rho\gamma$ and $\rho \to \eta\gamma$ decays: $\phi = 35.3^\circ \pm 5.5^\circ$ from the coupling constant ratio $1.41 \pm 0.29$
- From $D_s \to Pe\nu$ decays: $\phi = 41.3^\circ \pm 5.3^\circ$ from the form factor ratio $1.14 \pm 0.17 \pm 0.13$
- From $\pi^-p \to Pn$ scattering: $\phi = 36.5^\circ \pm 1.4^\circ$ and $39.3^\circ \pm 1.2^\circ$ from two experiments
- $\Gamma[\eta \to \gamma\gamma] = (0.51 \pm 0.026)$ keV and $\Gamma[\eta' \to \gamma\gamma] = (4.26 \pm 0.19)$ keV used in the analysis
- $f_{\eta_c} \simeq 405 \pm 30$ MeV for the $\eta_c$ decay constant

## Context

This work extends the earlier phenomenological analysis by FeKr97b and the chiral perturbation theory framework of Leutwyler (1997), which showed that the conventional octet-singlet mixing scheme with a single mixing angle $\theta$ (yielding values from $-10^\circ$ to $-23^\circ$ in analyses by Gilman (1987) and others) cannot correctly describe the decay constants. The new scheme resolves the long-standing discrepancy between different determinations of the mixing angle and provides a natural explanation for why vector and tensor mesons have mixing angles close to the ideal value $\theta_{ideal} = \arctan \sqrt{2}$. The generalization to include $\eta_c$ mixing enables estimates of the charm content of $\eta$ and $\eta'$, which is relevant for understanding the large branching ratio for $B \to K\eta'$ measured by CLEO.
