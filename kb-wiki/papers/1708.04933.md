# Features of the QCD phase diagram from small, noisy, fluctuating systems

> Current heavy-ion collision experiments might lead to the discovery of a first-order chiral symmetry breaking phase-transition line, ending in a second-order critical point. Nevertheless, the extraction of information about the equilibrium thermodynamic properties of baryonic matter from the highly dynamic, small, noisy and fluctuating environment formed in such collisions is an extremely challenging task. We address some of the limitations present in the experimental search for the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The extraction of equilibrium thermodynamic information about the QCD phase diagram from heavy-ion collisions is severely limited because the systems formed are small, short-lived, noisy, and fast-evolving; the paper argues that even robust signatures of a critical point, such as enhanced fluctuations, are significantly restrained by critical slowing-down and experimental effects like finite acceptance, resonance decay, and spurious fluctuations.

## Method

The authors model the long-range fluctuations of the chiral order parameter near a second-order critical point using a probability distribution with a Gaussian approximation, where the mass term scales as the inverse correlation length. They then compute critical contributions to averages and correlations of particle multiplicities by expanding single-particle energies in powers of mass corrections from order-parameter fluctuations, and incorporate experimental limitations such as acceptance windows, resonance decay probabilities (using phase-space volume), and spurious fluctuations from imperfect control of freeze-out temperature, chemical potential, and system volume.

## Key numbers

- The pion-sigma coupling constant is estimated to be around G ~ 300 MeV.
- The initial correlation length at proper time t = -tau is fixed at xi_0 ~ 1.6 fm.
- Critical exponents used: alpha = 0.11, nu = 0.63, z = 2 + alpha/nu, beta = 0.326, delta = 4.80, from universality class arguments.
- Causality constrains xi/xi_0 to below 1.3 for tau = 1 fm and below 1.9 for tau = 5.5 fm, significantly restraining signatures of criticality.
- Results for the variance-over-mean ratio of charged pion multiplicity are shown for an acceptance window of 0.3 GeV < p_T < 1.0 GeV and |eta| < 0.5, with temperature fluctuations of 5% width and geometric fluctuations from the centrality bin width for the 5% most central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for critical point signatures in heavy-ion collisions developed by Stephanov (1999, 2008) and Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1999), and builds on the authors' own prior work (Hippert 2017). It addresses limitations in the experimental search for the QCD critical point at the RHIC Beam-Energy Scan and future facilities, and enables future calculations of more interesting signatures connected to protons and higher-order moments of particle multiplicities.
