# Tera-Leptons Shadows over Sinister Universe

> The role of Sinister Heavy Fermions in recent Glashow's SU(3)*SU(2)*SU(2)'*U(1) model is to offer in a unique frame relic Helium-like products (an ingenious candidate to the dark matter puzzle), a solution to the See-Saw mechanism for light neutrino masses as well as to strong CP violation problem in QCD. The Sinister model requires a three additional families of leptons and quarks, but only the lightest of them Heavy U-quark and E-"electron" are stable. Final neutral Helium-like UUUEE state is an ideal evanescent dark-matter candidate. However it is reached by multi-body interactions along a tail of more manifest secondary frozen blocks. They should be now here polluting the surrounding matter. Moreover, in opposition to effective pair quark annihilations, there is no such an early or late tera-lepton pairs suppressions, because:a) electromagnetic interactions are "weaker" than nuclear ones and b) helium ion 4He++ is able to attract and capture, E-, fixing it into a hybrid tera helium "ion" trap. This leads to a pile up of relic (4HeE)+ traces, a lethal compound for any Sinister Universe. This capture leaves no Tera-Lepton frozen in Ep relic (otherwise an ideal catalyzer to achieve effective late E+E- annihilations possibly saving the model). The (4HeE)+ Coulomb screening is also avoiding the synthesis of the desired UUUEE hidden dark matter gas. The e(4HeE)+ behave chemically like an anomalous hydrogen isotope.Also tera-positronium (eE+) relics are over-abundant and they behave like an anomalous hydrogen atom: these gases do not fit by many orders of magnitude known severe bounds on hydrogen anomalous isotope, making grave shadows over a Sinister Universe. However a surprising and resolver role for Tera-Pions in UHECR astrophysics has been revealed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Glashow "Sinister" model, while offering a novel dark matter candidate in the form of neutral (UUUEE) tera-helium atoms, inevitably produces overabundant charged relic tera-lepton and tera-hadron species that violate observational bounds on anomalous isotopes by many orders of magnitude, casting grave doubt on the cosmological viability of the model.

## Method

The authors trace the thermal history of tera-fermions (U-quarks and E-leptons) in the early Universe, calculating freeze-out abundances of particle-antiparticle pairs and following their subsequent binding into hadrons, atoms, and ions. They compare the predicted abundances of intermediate relic species such as (4HeE-)+ ions, (eE+) tera-positronium, and various hybrid tera-hadron atoms against experimental upper limits on anomalous hydrogen and helium isotopes in terrestrial and astrophysical matter.

## Key numbers

- The minimal S-factor is S6 = 0.2, giving U-quark mass m_U = 3.5 S6 TeV and E-lepton mass m_E = 500 S6 GeV.
- The required tera-baryon excess for dark matter is r_B' = 4 x 10^(-14) (3.5 TeV / m), corresponding to U-quark excess kappa_U = 1.2 x 10^(-13) / S6.
- The required tera-lepton excess is r_L' = 8 x 10^(-14) / S6.
- At S6 = 1, frozen-out U-quark pair concentration is 6 times larger than the U excess; frozen-out E E+ pair concentration is 2 orders of magnitude larger than the E excess.
- The abundance of (eE+) and (4HeE-e) exceeds terrestrial upper limits for anomalous hydrogen by 27 orders of magnitude.
- The abundance of (UUUEe) exceeds anomalous hydrogen bounds by 25.5 orders, (Uude) by at least 20 orders, (UUUee) by 14.5 orders, and (UUuee) and (Uuuee) by 10 orders relative to anomalous helium.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Glashow (2005) "Sinister" SU(3) x SU(2) x SU(2)' x U(1) model, which proposed (UUUEE) tera-helium as a dark matter candidate. The paper identifies a fatal flaw: ordinary 4He from Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis captures free E- into (4HeE-)+ ions at T ~ 15 keV, preventing both E+ E- annihilation and the formation of the desired (UUUEE) dark matter. This contradicts earlier hopes expressed in Glashow (2005) that (Ep) catalytic annihilation could eliminate the problematic relics. The results enable future searches for metastable tera-fermions in accelerators or cosmic rays if the model is modified to allow tera-particle decay before the present epoch.
