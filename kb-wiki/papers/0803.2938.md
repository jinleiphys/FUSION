# Neutrino Mass Differences and Nonunitarity of Neutrino Mixing Matrix from Interfering Recoil Ions

> We show that the recent observation of the time modulation of two-body weak decays of heavy ions reveals the mass content of the electron neutrinos via interference patterns in the recoiling ion wave function. From the modulation period we derive the difference of the square masses $ Δm^2\approx 22.5\times 10^{-5}$ eV${}^2$, which is about 2.8 times larger than that derived from a combined analysis of KamLAND and solar neutrino oscillation experiments. It is, however, compatible with a data regime to which the KamLAND analysis attributes a smaller probability. The experimental results imply that the neutrino mixing matrix violates unitarity by about 10%.

## Key claim

The time modulation observed in two-body weak decays of heavy ions at GSI reveals the mass content of electron neutrinos via interference patterns in the recoiling ion wave function, yielding a squared mass difference that is about 2.8 times larger than the KamLAND-favored value but compatible with a data regime to which the KamLAND analysis attributes a smaller probability.

## Method

The authors analyzed the time-dependent decay rates of fully ionized heavy ions undergoing electron capture or bound-state beta decay in the GSI storage ring ESR, extracting the modulation frequency from the oscillatory component of the decay rate. They derived the neutrino mass-squared difference from the observed beat frequency using the relation Δω = Δm²/(2M), where M is the mass of the initial ion, and interpreted the modulation amplitude as evidence for nonunitarity of the neutrino mixing matrix by summing over all neutrino flavors in the outgoing wave function.

## Key numbers

- Δm² ≈ 22.5 × 10⁻⁵ eV², derived from the modulation period of the decay rate
- The modulation period is roughly 7 seconds for both decays studied
- For ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺ → ¹⁴⁰Ce⁵⁸⁺: Δω ≈ 0.890(11) sec⁻¹ (Q = 3386 keV)
- For ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺ → ¹⁴²Nd⁶⁰⁺: Δω ≈ 0.885(31) sec⁻¹ (Q = 4470 keV)
- The modulation amplitude a = 0.18(3)
- The derived Δm² is a factor of approximately 2.8 larger than the KamLAND result Δm² ≈ 7.58⁺⁰·³₋₀·₃ × 10⁻⁵ eV²
- The nonunitarity parameter u₂₁/u₀ ≈ 10%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of GSI storage ring experiments on two-body weak decays of heavy ions reported by the GSI collaboration (2008), interpreting the observed time modulation as a neutrino interference effect rather than a nuclear phenomenon. The result contradicts the standard three-neutrino unitary mixing framework favored by KamLAND and solar neutrino oscillation experiments, and the authors suggest that the unitarity violation could arise from additional lepton families beyond the three known ones, with fourth-family mass bounds of m_{τ'} ≥ 100.8 GeV and m_{ν_{τ'}} ≥ 90.3 GeV.
