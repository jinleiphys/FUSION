# Source function from two-particle correlation function through entropy-regularized Richardson-Lucy deblurring

> Source functions are obtained from $p$-$p$ and $d$--$α$ correlation functions by applying the Richardson-Lucy (RL) deblurring to the Koonin-Pratt (KP) equation. To prevent fitting of noise in the correlation function, total-variation (TV) regularization is employed that has been effective in ordinary image restoration. TV alone cannot ensure normalization of the source functions. To ensure the latter, we propose a maximum-entropy regularized RL algorithm (MEM-RL). We outline the MEM-RL formalism and optimization strategy for the KP equation, demonstrating its effectiveness on both simulated and experimental data, including the $p$-$p$ and $d$-$α$ correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a maximum-entropy regularized Richardson-Lucy (MEM-RL) algorithm to extract source functions from two-particle correlation functions via the Koonin-Pratt equation, claiming that this method preserves the normalization of the source function, a property that total-variation (TV) regularization alone cannot guarantee, while preventing over-fitting of noise in the correlation data.

## Method

The authors discretize the Koonin-Pratt equation into a matrix form and apply the Richardson-Lucy deblurring algorithm iteratively to recover the source function from measured correlation functions. To suppress noise amplification, they add a maximum-entropy regularization term with a floating default constructed from a Gaussian-smearing kernel, and they optimize the regularization parameters (strength α, smoothing width σ_r, and purity λ) by minimizing the χ² of the predicted correlation function against the data. The algorithm is tested on simulated Gaussian source functions and then applied to experimental p-p correlation data from ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions at 80 MeV/nucleon and d–α correlation data from ⁴⁰Ca+¹¹²Sn collisions at 140 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

* For a Gaussian test source with R_G = 2.5 fm and λ_G = 0.65, the MEM-RL algorithm recovered a source with FWHM r_{1/2} = 4.25 ± 0.61 fm (true value 4.16 fm) and purity λ = 0.64 ± 0.04.
* For experimental p-p correlations from ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca at 80 MeV/nucleon, MEM-RL gave r_{1/2} = 4.17 ± 0.38 fm and λ = 0.66 ± 0.05, compared to a Gaussian fit yielding r_{1/2} = 4.20 (+0.29, -0.21) fm and λ = 0.61 (+0.11, -0.08).
* For experimental d–α correlations from ⁴⁰Ca+¹¹²Sn at 140 MeV/nucleon, MEM-RL gave r_{1/2} = 5.39 ± 0.52 fm and λ = 0.98 ± 0.02, compared to a Gaussian fit yielding r_{1/2} = 5.44 ± 0.06 fm.
* The algorithm converged in less than 30,000 iterations using a convergence threshold of 10⁻³ on the relative update.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to extract source functions from two-particle correlations, including parametric Gaussian fitting (Henzl et al., 2011) and Bayesian imaging (Dave et al., 2001; Henzl et al., 2011), as well as a previous application of Richardson-Lucy deblurring with TV regularization to d–α correlations (Pierre et al., 2023). The proposed MEM-RL method addresses the normalization failure of TV regularization and enables source reconstruction for both identical (p-p) and non-identical (d–α) pairs, though the authors note that the current framework cannot handle momentum-dependent source functions that arise from collective motion.
