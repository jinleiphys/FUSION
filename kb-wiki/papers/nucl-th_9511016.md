# Low-energy QCD: Chiral coefficients and the quark-quark interaction

> A detailed investigation of the low-energy chiral expansion is presented within a model truncation of QCD. The truncation allows for a phenomenological description of the quark-quark interaction in a framework which maintains the global symmetries of QCD and permits a $1/N_c$ expansion. The model dependence of the chiral coefficients is tested for several forms of the quark-quark interaction by varying the form of the running coupling, $α(q^2)$, in the infrared region. The pattern in the coefficients that arises at tree level is consistent with large $N_c$ QCD, and is related to the model truncation.

## Key claim

Within a model truncation of QCD that includes only a two-point quark-quark interaction and describes mesons as fluctuations about the saddle point of the effective action, the pattern in the chiral coefficients at tree level is consistent with large \(N_c\) QCD; however, the mass-dependent coefficients \(L_5\) and \(L_8\) are most sensitive to the infrared form of the running coupling and require a sufficiently large integrated strength to produce dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, with the overall scale fixed by the pion decay constant.

## Method

The authors start from a global color symmetry model (GCM) truncation of QCD that retains only the gluon two-point function, then bosonize the quark-quark interaction via a Fierz reordering and a Gaussian integration over bilocal meson fields. They expand about the saddle point (the classical vacuum) to obtain the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark self-energy and the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the Goldstone modes, then perform a gradient expansion of the effective action to fourth order in chiral counting, keeping only the Nambu-Goldstone boson fluctuations and neglecting higher-mass meson states. Three different two-parameter models for the running coupling \(\alpha(q^2)\) in the infrared are tested, each matched to the one-loop perturbative form at large momentum, with the pion decay constant fixed at \(f_\pi = 86\) MeV to set the overall scale.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant fixed at \(f_\pi = 86\) MeV (zero-momentum value, rather than the pion-mass-shell value of 93 MeV).
* The one-loop perturbative running coupling parameters: \(\Lambda = 0.2\) GeV and \(d = 12/(33-2N_F) = 12/27\).
* For model \(\alpha_1\), previous parameter values were \(\Delta = 0.002\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\chi = 1.14\) GeV.
* The constituent quark mass from the quark self-energy peaks at \(M(q^2_{\text{peak}}) \approx 300\)–\(400\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Roberts and collaborators (Roberts 1988, Roberts 1994) that extracted second-order and some fourth-order chiral coefficients for \(\pi\)-\(\pi\) scattering, by calculating the full set of coefficients \(L_1\)–\(L_8\) and systematically testing their sensitivity to the infrared form of the quark-quark interaction. The Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Nambu 1961) is recovered as a limiting case when the gluon propagator becomes a delta function, and the results are consistent with earlier findings by Hatsuda and Meissner (1995) that the running coupling must be strong in the infrared to achieve convergence of the chiral series in the strange quark sector. The approach enables future work to include higher-mass meson states and to compute the remaining coefficients \(L_9\) and \(L_{10}\), as initiated by Frank (1994).
