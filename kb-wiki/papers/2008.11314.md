# Reduction for one-loop tensor Feynman integrals in the relativistic quantum field theories at finite temperature and/or finite density

> The \emph{conventional} Passarino-Veltman reduction is a systematic procedure based on the Lorentz covariance, which can efficiently reduce the one-loop tensor Feynman integrals in the relativistic quantum field theories (QFTs) at zero temperature and zero density. However, the Lorentz covariance is explicitly broken when either of the temperature and density is finite, due to a rest reference frame of the many-body system in which the temperature and density are measured, rendering the \emph{conventional} Passarino-Veltman reduction not applicable anymore to reduce the one-loop tensor Feynman integrals therein. In this paper, we report a \emph{generalized} Passarino-Veltman reduction which can efficiently simplify the one-loop tensor Feynman integrals in the relativistic QFTs at finite temperature and/or finite density. The \emph{generalized} Passarino-Veltman reduction can analyze the one-loop tensor Feynman integrals in a wide range of physical systems described by the relativistic QFTs at finite temperature and/or finite density, such as quark-gluon plasma in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper reports a *generalized* Passarino-Veltman reduction that can efficiently simplify one-loop tensor Feynman integrals in relativistic quantum field theories at finite temperature and/or finite density, where the Lorentz covariance is explicitly broken due to the rest reference frame of the many-body system. The generalization is achieved by introducing an extra constant vector to account for the broken Lorentz symmetry and by including purely temporal components of the tensor integrals as additional building blocks.

## Method

The author generalizes the conventional Passarino-Veltman reduction by expanding generic one-loop tensor Feynman integrals into a complete set of tensor structures that include both Lorentz-covariant tensors (such as the metric tensor and external momenta) and non-Lorentz-covariant tensors constructed from a constant vector u^ρ = (1; 0, ..., 0) in energy-momentum space. The reduction procedure expresses the tensor integrals in terms of generic one-loop scalar Feynman integrals and the purely temporal components of the tensor integrals, with the form factors determined by solving systems of linear equations derived from contracting the tensor integrals with the available vectors and the metric.

## Key numbers

* The Gram determinant for the rank-one two-point reduction is Δ_B^(1)(p_1) = p_1^2 - (p_1^0)^2 = -|p_1|^2.
* The Gram matrix for the rank-two two-point reduction is a 4x4 matrix with entries involving D (spacetime dimension), p_1^2, p_1^0, and (p_1^0)^2.
* The highest rank (r_h) of tensor integrals in renormalizable theories does not exceed the number of external momenta N, i.e., r_h ≤ N.

## Context

This work extends the conventional Passarino-Veltman reduction (Passarino and Veltman, 1979), which is only applicable at zero temperature and zero density, to finite temperature and/or finite density systems such as quark-gluon plasma. It addresses the incompleteness of previous attempts (Rehberg, 1996; Chang, 2018) by providing a systematic reduction procedure for generic one-loop tensor integrals up to three-point, enabling efficient calculation of one-loop Feynman diagrams in relativistic many-body systems where Lorentz symmetry is broken.
