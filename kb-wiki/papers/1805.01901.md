# Statistical hadron-gas treatment of systems created in proton-proton interactions at CERN SPS

> We analyze the newest data from the NA61/SHINE collaboration which, in addition to previous results on pions and kaons, include mean multiplicities of $p$, $Λ$, and $ϕ$-mesons produced in inelastic proton-proton (p+p) interactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.3-17.3$~GeV. The canonical ensemble formulation of the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model is used with exact conservation of net baryon number $B=2$, electric charge $Q=2$, and strangeness $S=0$. The chemical freeze-out parameters in p+p interactions are obtained and compared to those in central nucleus-nucleus collisions. Several features of p+p interactions at the CERN SPS within a statistical model are studied: 1) the inclusion of the $ϕ$-meson yields in thermal fits worsens significantly the fit quality; 2) the data show large event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in inelastic p+p interactions which can not be explained by a single fireball described by a statistical model; 3) the fits within the canonical ensemble formulation of HRG do not give any improvement over the fits within the grand canonical ensemble formulation, i.e., there are no indications for existence of a single statistical system in p+p inelastic interactions in the considered energy range.

## Key claim

The canonical ensemble hadron resonance gas model cannot adequately describe the full set of hadron multiplicities in inelastic p+p interactions at SPS energies when phi-meson yields are included, and the data show no evidence for a single statistical system with exactly conserved charges; the grand canonical ensemble actually provides a better fit than the canonical ensemble, contrary to expectations.

## Method

The authors fit the canonical ensemble (CE) formulation of the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to mean hadron multiplicities measured by NA61/SHINE in inelastic p+p collisions at center-of-mass energies sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3–17.3 GeV, with exact conservation of baryon number B=2, electric charge Q=2, and strangeness S=0. The fit parameters are temperature T, strangeness saturation factor gamma_S, and system radius R (derived from volume V = 4πR³/3). Fits are performed both including and excluding the phi-meson yields, and the CE results are compared to grand canonical ensemble (GCE) fits of the same data.

## Key numbers

- The chi² per degree of freedom (chi²/N_dof) for CE fits without phi-meson data is approximately 1, but increases to approximately 10 when phi-meson yields are included in the fit.
- The average energy of the statistical system in p+p collisions at SPS energies is 5–8 GeV, approximately half of the total available energy sqrt(s_NN).
- The measured scaled variance for negatively charged particle fluctuations in inelastic p+p reactions at sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 17.3 GeV is approximately 1.4, compared to CE predictions of approximately 0.8 and MCE predictions of approximately 0.4.
- The GCE prediction for the scaled variance of negatively charged particles is approximately 1.1, still smaller than the measured value of 1.4.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CE HRG analysis of NA61/SHINE p+p data (Begun et al., 2016) by including newly measured p, Lambda, and phi-meson multiplicities. The difficulties in describing phi-meson yields within the CE were previously noted by Becattini (2008) for p+p and e+e- collisions at higher energies, and the present analysis shows these persist at lower SPS energies. The results contradict the expectation that the CE should improve over the GCE for small systems, and instead suggest that multiple fluctuating subclusters described by the micro-canonical ensemble may be needed, enabling future experimental tests using centrality-selected p+p events.
