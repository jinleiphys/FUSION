# Derivative-free optimization for parameter estimation in computational nuclear physics

> We consider optimization problems that arise when estimating a set of unknown parameters from experimental data, particularly in the context of nuclear density functional theory. We examine the cost of not having derivatives of these functionals with respect to the parameters. We show that the POUNDERS code for local derivative-free optimization obtains consistent solutions on a variety of computationally expensive energy density functional calibration problems. We also provide a primer on the operation of the POUNDERS software in the Toolkit for Advanced Optimization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the POUNDERS derivative-free optimization code obtains consistent local solutions for calibrating nuclear energy density functionals, even when starting from very different initial parameterizations, but notes that this consistency may depend on the starting points being in reasonable regions of parameter space and the objective function not being highly multimodal in those regions.

## Method

The authors formulate parameter estimation as a nonlinear least squares (NLS) optimization problem and apply the POUNDERS derivative-free method, which exploits the sum-of-squares structure of the objective without requiring gradient information. They test the method on calibration problems for Skyrme energy density functionals using the HFBTHO code, which solves Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations under axial and time-reversal symmetry, and compare results across different starting points (SLy4, SkM*, UNEDF0, UNEDF1) and data sets.

## Key numbers

- UNEDF0 problem: 12 parameters, 108 observables across 72 even-even nuclei; each function evaluation required 12 minutes on 72 cores, with 300-evaluation runs taking 2.5 days
- UNEDF0 bound-constrained POUNDERS run: final objective value f(x) = 67.31 after 300 evaluations; two parameters (K^NM and 1/M_s^*) were active at bounds (230 and 0.9 respectively)
- UNEDF0 unconstrained POUNDERS run: final objective value f(x) = 41.87 after 268 evaluations (terminated by criticality measure)
- UNEDF1 problem: 12 parameters, 115 observables across 79 nuclei; POUNDERS achieved f(x) = 51.06 after 218 evaluations starting from UNEDF0
- UNEDF1ex (adding alpha_ex parameter): f(x) = 49.34 after 253 evaluations
- UNEDF0 rerun with updated HFBTHO code (Ver 201): both SLy4 and SkM* starting points converged to solutions within one standard deviation of original UNEDF0 parameters; final f(x) values of 67.90 and 67.57
- UNEDF1 with 17 additional Argonne mass data: two runs from different starting points gave parameters within 0.07 standard deviations of each other; f(x) = 54.01 and 54.05
- Removing bounds on nuclear matter properties in the AM data run reduced chi-squared from 0.450 to 0.386 but changed a_sym^NM by up to 5 standard deviations

## Context

This work extends the UNEDF collaboration's parameter estimation efforts (UNEDF0, UNEDF1, UNEDF2 parameterizations) by systematically testing the robustness of derivative-free optimization for calibrating Skyrme energy density functionals. The paper provides empirical validation of the sensitivity analysis conducted in those earlier UNEDF studies and demonstrates that POUNDERS obtains consistent solutions across different starting points and code versions. The results enable future work on including additional observables (such as the Argonne masses from Van Schelt 2013) and examining the effect of bound constraints on nuclear matter properties, while also providing practical guidance for using the POUNDERS software in the Toolkit for Advanced Optimization (TAO).
