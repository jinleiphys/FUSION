# A Dynamical Model of Light Halo Nuclei

> We present a review of theoretical studies of the structure and reactions of N=7 and N=8 nuclei in the vicinity of 11Li, carried out within a framework based on Nuclear Field Theory. The coupling of valence nucleons to low-lying surface vibrations of the spherical core plays a central role, giving rise to self-energy processes that renormalize single-particle states and transfer form factors, as well as to an induced pairing interaction arising from the exchange of collective vibrations, which renormalizes the bare pairing force. Excitation spectra and cross sections for one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions populating states in the quasi-continuum are calculated and compared with available experimental data. Collective excitations in the particle-particle channel are investigated, with particular emphasis on Giant Pairing Vibrations and on their damping mechanisms arising from coupling to more complex configurations and continuum states. Comparisons with other theoretical schemes are also presented. We conclude that a coherent understanding of experimental data requires the detailed consideration of particle-vibration coupling effects.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a coherent understanding of the structure and reactions of light halo nuclei near 11Li requires the detailed consideration of particle-vibration coupling effects, going beyond frozen-core or mean-field approaches. The authors claim that while two-body and three-body models with frozen cores can reproduce several observables, they encounter difficulties with phenomena involving core dynamics, and that surface fluctuations of the core play a central role that must be included explicitly.

## Method

The authors employ Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) to couple valence nucleons to low-lying surface vibrations of a spherical core, using a Woods-Saxon single-particle basis fitted to experimental properties including particle-vibration coupling effects. For A+1 systems, they construct a Hamiltonian matrix including particle states, particle-plus-phonon states, and hole-plus-phonon states, then apply Bloch-Horowitz projection to obtain energy-dependent self-energy corrections. For A+2 systems, they build an extended particle-particle Random Phase Approximation (pp-RPA) matrix that includes configurations with two valence particles, particle-particle-plus-phonon states, particle-hole-plus-phonon states, and hole-hole states, incorporating both a bare pairing interaction and an induced pairing interaction arising from phonon exchange.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameters β_L and phonon energies ħω_L of low-lying collective vibrations are taken from experimental data, not calculated from effective interactions
- The self-energy normalization factor Z_k for a quasiparticle state is given by Z_k = (1 - ∂Σ(k,ω)/∂ω)^{-1} evaluated at ω = E
- The induced pairing interaction matrix element V_ind involves sums over multipolarity L with factors β_L^2 and energy denominators (ω - (ε_k' + ε_q + ħω_L)) or (ω - (ε_k + ε_q' + ħω_L))
- The monopole strength function S_k(R_box) for pair addition is defined with radial form factor f(r) = dV/dr and includes sums over forward amplitudes X(k)_pp' and backward amplitudes Y(k)_hh'
- The extended pp-RPA matrix includes a pp-TDA submatrix connecting pp-states among themselves and a conventional pp-RPA submatrix connecting pp- and hh-states

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of N=7 and N=8 nuclei around 11Li using Nuclear Field Theory, building on the framework developed by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the NFT formalism of Broglia and collaborators. The approach contrasts with frozen-core two-body and three-body models that introduce parity-dependent potentials, and with self-consistent approaches using Skyrme or Gogny effective interactions that are difficult to refit including PVC effects. The paper discusses comparisons with other theoretical schemes and notes that cluster models are beyond the present scope. The work enables calculation of excitation spectra and cross sections for one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions populating states in the quasi-continuum, and investigates Giant Pairing Vibrations and their damping mechanisms, with particular reference to a recent two-neutron transfer experiment on 14C that observed a bump interpreted as a possible GPV signature.
