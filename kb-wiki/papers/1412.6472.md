# Variational Principle of Hydrodynamics and Quantization by Stochastic Process

> The well-known hydrodynamical representation of the Schrödinger equation is reformulated by extending the idea of Nelson-Yasue's stochastic variational method. The fluid flow is composed by the two stochastic processes from the past and the future, which are unified naturally by the principle of maximum entropy. We show that this formulation is easily applicable to the quantization of scalar fields.

# Variational Principle of Hydrodynamics and Quantization by Stochastic Process

## Key claim
The Schrödinger equation can be derived from a variational principle combining two Brownian motions (forward and backward in time) with a maximum entropy condition, without assuming quantum mechanics a priori. The derivation requires that the noise intensity parameter \(\nu\) and the constant \(\kappa\) satisfy the specific relation \(\kappa = 4\alpha \nu m\), and if this condition is not met, the resulting dynamics becomes nonlinear rather than linear.

## Method
The authors extend the Nelson-Yasue stochastic variational method by introducing two stochastic differential equations: one forward in time (FSDE) and one backward in time (BSDE), each describing Brownian motion with Gaussian white noise. They impose a maximum entropy principle on the product of forward and backward trajectory densities \(\rho_F \rho_B\) to enforce consistency between the two stochastic processes, yielding \(\rho_F = \rho_B\). The resulting fluid action includes a translational kinetic energy term from the mean velocity \(\mathbf{u}_T\) and an internal energy term from the relative velocity \(\mathbf{u}_r = \mathbf{u}_F - \mathbf{u}_B\), which is proportional to \((\nabla \ln \sqrt{\rho})^2\). The action is then rewritten in terms of a complex wave function \(\psi = \sqrt{\rho} e^{i\lambda}\), and variation yields the Schrödinger equation when \(\kappa\) is identified with \(\hbar\).

## Key numbers
- The noise intensity \(\sigma^2 = \nu |dt|\) characterizes the Gaussian white noise, where \(\nu\) has dimensions of \(L^2/T\).
- The consistency condition from the maximum entropy principle gives \(\mathbf{u}_F - \mathbf{u}_B = 2\nu \nabla \ln \rho\).
- The relation \(\kappa = 4\alpha \nu m\) must hold to obtain the linear Schrödinger equation, where \(\alpha\) is an undetermined constant from the effective mass \(m_{eff} = \alpha^2 m\).
- For field quantization, the universal constant becomes \(\kappa c = \hbar c\).

## Context
This work reformulates the well-known hydrodynamical representation of the Schrödinger equation (de Broglie, Bohm, Vigier) within the stochastic variational method originally developed by Nelson (1966) and Yasue. It extends prior work by Kodama and Koide (referenced as KK, KK2) by incorporating the maximum entropy principle to unify forward and backward stochastic processes. The approach enables straightforward application to scalar field quantization via a functional Schrödinger equation, addressing the Takabayashi-Wallstrom criticism about multi-valued phases in the hydrodynamic representation by noting that vorticity in functional space is unrelated to phase multivaluedness. The paper suggests that the quantum noise may originate from spacetime fluctuations, potentially linking quantum mechanics and relativity.
