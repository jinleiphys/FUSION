# Orbital Angular Momentum and Generalized Transverse Momentum Distribution

> We show that, when boosted to the infinite momentum frame, the quark and gluon orbital angular momentum operators defined in the nucleon spin sum rule of X. S. Chen et al. are the same as those derived from generalized transverse momentum distributions. This completes the connection between the infinite momentum limit of each term in that sum rule and experimentally measurable observables. We also show that these orbital angular momentum operators can be defined locally, and discuss the strategies of calculating them in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) operators defined in the gauge-invariant nucleon spin sum rule of Chen et al. (2008, 2009) become identical, in the infinite momentum frame (IMF) limit, to the OAM derived from generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs). This equivalence holds under the specific condition that the non-Abelian Coulomb condition is used to fix the decomposition of the gauge field into physical and pure-gauge parts.

## Method

The authors analytically boost the quark and gluon OAM operators from the Chen et al. sum rule to the IMF using light-cone coordinates, solving for the pure-gauge field component \(A^\mu_{\text{pure}}\) from the non-Abelian Coulomb condition. They then show that the resulting IMF expressions match the OAM definitions obtained from the first \(k_T\)-moment of quark and gluon GTMD functions \(F^q_{1,4}\) and \(g(x,\vec{k}_T,\vec{\Delta}_T)\), following the formalism of Hatta (2011). Finally, they demonstrate that the Chen et al. operators can be constructed locally on a Euclidean lattice using a Coulomb gauge-fixing procedure, enabling lattice QCD calculations via large-momentum effective theory (LaMET).

## Key numbers

- The polarized gluon distribution \(\Delta g(x,Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 = 10\ \text{GeV}^2\) is found positive in the momentum fraction range \(0.05 \leq x \leq 0.2\), with the integral over that range contributing about \(40\%\) of the proton spin (de Florian et al., 2014).
- The quark spin measured in deep-inelastic scattering contributes about \(25\%\) to the proton spin (de Florian et al., 2009).
- The quark OAM \(\mathcal{L}_q\) is expressed in terms of the GTMD \(F^q_{1,4}\) as \(\mathcal{L}_q(x) = -\int d^2k_\perp (\vec{k}_\perp^2 / M^2) F^q_{1,4}(x, 0, \vec{k}_\perp^2, 0, 0)\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ji et al. (2013), who showed that the gluon spin operator in the Chen et al. sum rule equals the first moment of the polarized gluon distribution \(\Delta g\) in the IMF, enabling lattice calculations of \(\Delta G\). The present paper completes the connection by showing the same for the OAM operators, linking them to GTMDs that are in principle measurable in hard exclusive processes (Ji, 2012; Kanazawa, 2014). This enables future lattice QCD calculations of quark and gluon OAM using local operators, avoiding the renormalization challenges of non-local Wilson lines in direct GTMD calculations (Musch, 2010, 2011; Engelhardt, 2014).
