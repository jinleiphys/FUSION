# Exploring the relationship between nuclear matter and finite nuclei with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces

> We address the connection between the saturating behavior of infinite nuclear matter and the description of finite nuclei based on state-of-the-art chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. We observe that chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions (at N2LO and at N3LO) which have been found to predict realistic binding energies and radii for a wide range of finite nuclei (from p-shell nuclei up to nickel isotopes) are unable to saturate infinite nuclear matter. On the other hand, it has been shown that, when the fits of the cD and cE couplings of the chiral three-nucleon interactions include the constraint of nuclear matter saturation in addition to, as is typically the case, the triton binding energy, medium-mass nuclei are underbound and their radii are sytematically too large. We discuss this apparent inconsistency and perform test calculations for various scenarios to shed light on the issue.

## Key claim

Chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions that predict realistic binding energies and radii for finite nuclei from p-shell up to nickel isotopes fail to saturate infinite nuclear matter, while interactions constrained by nuclear matter saturation underbind medium-mass nuclei and produce radii that are systematically too large. The authors demonstrate that the c_D coupling of the three-nucleon force behaves very differently in infinite matter compared to finite nuclei, and that no simultaneously optimal c_D and c_E values have been found for few-nucleon systems, medium-mass nuclei, and nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter using chiral two-nucleon forces at N2LO and N3LO (from Entem, Machleidt, Nosyk 2017) combined with three-nucleon forces at N2LO and N3LO, expressed as density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions. They compared equations of state obtained with 3NF low-energy constants fitted to the triton binding energy plus nuclear matter saturation (from Drischler, Hebeler, Schwenk 2019) against those fitted to triton and 16O ground-state energies (from Huther et al. 2020). Additionally, they performed finite-nucleus calculations using a mass-formula-based density functional with a Thomas-Fermi distribution, directly inputting the nuclear matter equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The 3NF couplings from Drischler et al. (2019) at N2LO with cutoff 450 MeV: c_D = 2.25, 2.50, 2.75 and c_E = 0.07, 0.10, 0.13 (cases a, b, c respectively)
- The 3NF couplings from Huther et al. (2020) at N2LO with cutoff 450 MeV: c_D = 10.0, c_E = 0.909
- For 16O with the Drischler et al. (2019) couplings (case a, cutoff 450 MeV): E/A = -6.83 MeV, charge radius = 2.89 fm (experimental: -7.98 MeV, 2.73 fm)
- For 40Ca with the Drischler et al. (2019) couplings (case a, cutoff 450 MeV): E/A = -7.60 MeV, charge radius = 3.57 fm (experimental: -8.55 MeV, 3.49 fm)
- The N3LO portion of the 3NF is moderately attractive and parameter-free
- With ad hoc modifications to the EoS below saturation density, 16O binding energy reached -7.99 MeV (experimental -7.98 MeV) but charge radius increased to 3.03 fm (experimental 2.73 fm)

## Context

This work extends the puzzle identified by Hoppe et al. (2019) and Huther et al. (2020) regarding the incompatibility between chiral 3NF constraints from finite nuclei versus nuclear matter saturation. The authors compare their BHF results with the many-body perturbation theory calculations of Drischler et al. (2019) and find agreement within 2-3% at N3LO. They contrast their findings with the successful "1.8/2.0 (EM)" interaction from the SRG-evolved chiral forces, which achieves good finite-nuclei results but involves induced 3NFs not included in the bare interactions. The work enables future searches for bare chiral interactions that combine a very soft two-nucleon force with a repulsive three-nucleon force, or a semi-soft 2NF with a 3NF that is attractive at low densities but repulsive around and above saturation.
