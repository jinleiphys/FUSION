# Medium-induced coherent gluon radiation for $2\to 2$ processes with general kinematics

> High-energy proton-nucleus (pA) collisions offer valuable insight into the role of cold nuclear matter effects in hadron production. In particular, multiple rescatterings of an incoming parton by the nuclear target are known to induce the radiation of many soft gluons. Hadron production is affected by fully coherent energy loss (FCEL), owing to those radiated gluons with a long formation time. Here we present a recently derived formula for the induced single soft gluon radiation spectrum beyond leading logarithmic accuracy, whose main features are demonstrated with the example of $q\, g \to q\, g$ scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents a newly derived formula for the medium-induced single soft gluon radiation spectrum for all \(2\to 2\) QCD scattering processes that goes beyond the leading-logarithmic approximation (LLA), valid for the full kinematic range of the underlying scattering rather than only the symmetric \(\xi \sim 1/2\) limit where the LLA applies.

## Method

The authors derive the induced soft gluon spectrum \(\mathrm{d}I/\mathrm{d}x\) (with \(x = \omega/E\)) as a trace over a color density matrix \(\Phi\) and a soft radiation matrix \(S(x)\), where \(\Phi\) encodes the entanglement between color components of the \(2\to 2\) partonic scattering amplitude decomposed into \(s\)-channel irreducible representations. The soft radiation matrix \(S(x)\) contains logarithmic factors \(\mathcal{L}_\xi\) and \(\mathcal{L}_{\bar\xi}\) that depend on the kinematic variable \(\xi \equiv (m^2-t)/s\), and the matrix elements \(B_{\alpha\beta}\) and \(\bar B_{\alpha\beta}\) represent soft color connections between initial and final state gluon radiation. The general formula is demonstrated explicitly for the specific case of \(q\,g \to q\,g\) scattering.

## Key numbers

- For \(qg\to qg\) at \(\xi = 1/2\) with \(N_c=3\), the \(s\)-channel probabilities are \(\rho_{\mathbf{3}} = 25/88\), \(\rho_{\mathbf{\bar 6}} = 9/44\), and \(\rho_{\mathbf{15}} = 45/88\).
- For \(qg\to qg\) at \(\xi \to 0\), the spectrum equals \(\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi x} \mathcal{L}_{\bar\xi=1} (-1/N_c)\) with \(N_c=3\), giving a negative value indicating energy gain.
- For \(qg\to qg\) at \(\xi \to 1\), the spectrum equals \(\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi x} \mathcal{L}_{\xi=1} N_c\) with \(N_c=3\), giving a positive value indicating energy loss.
- For \(qg\to qg\) at \(\xi = 1/2\) with \(N_c=3\), the spectrum equals \(\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi x} \mathcal{L}_{\xi=1/2} \frac{(N_c^2-1)(4N_c^2-1)}{N_c(5N_c^2-1)}\).
- The general expression for \(qg\to qg\) for all \(\xi\) is \(\frac{\mathrm{d}I}{\mathrm{d}x} = \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi x} \frac{1}{C_F \xi^2 + N_c \bar\xi} \left[ (N_c^2 - \xi^2) \mathcal{L}_\xi + \left( \frac{\xi^2}{N_c^2} - 2\bar\xi \right) \mathcal{L}_{\bar\xi} \right]\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-logarithmic approximation (LLA) for fully coherent energy loss (FCEL) previously derived for \(2\to 1\) forward processes by Peigne (2014) and for \(2\to 2\) processes by Peigne (2014), which were limited to the symmetric \(\xi \sim 1/2\) regime. The new formula enables more accurate evaluation of FCEL effects on hadron production in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions across a broad kinematic range, which is crucial for disentangling energy loss from nuclear parton distribution function effects in precision studies at LHC and RHIC. The results provide a baseline for building quenching weights for phenomenological applications to single hadron and dihadron/dijet production.
