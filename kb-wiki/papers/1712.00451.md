# Implications from GW170817 and I-Love-Q relations for relativistic hybrid stars

> Gravitational wave observations of GW170817 placed bounds on the tidal deformabilities of compact stars allowing one to probe equations of state for matter at supranuclear densities. Here we design new parametrizations for hybrid hadron-quark equations of state, that give rise to low-mass twin stars, and test them against GW170817. We find that GW170817 is consistent with the coalescence of a binary hybrid star--neutron star. We also test and find that the I-Love-Q relations for hybrid stars in the third family agree with those for purely hadronic and quark stars within $\sim 3\%$ for both slowly and rapidly rotating configurations, implying that these relations can be used to perform equation-of-state independent tests of general relativity and to break degeneracies in gravitational waveforms for hybrid stars in the third family as well.

## Key claim
GW170817 is consistent with the coalescence of a binary hybrid star-neutron star (HS-NS), not just a binary neutron star; this conclusion depends on the existence of a first-order hadron-quark phase transition that produces a third family of compact stars at low masses around 1.5 solar masses.

## Method
The authors constructed new parametrizations of hybrid hadron-quark equations of state (EOSs) using two sets: Set I employed a constant speed-of-sound parametrization for quark matter matched to a covariant density functional hadronic EOS via Maxwell construction; Set II used a piecewise polytropic representation with four polytropes to describe the nuclear EOS, a first-order phase transition, and stiff quark matter. They computed sequences of nonrotating and rotating hybrid stars, calculating the dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda, moment of inertia I, and quadrupole moment Q using the Hartle-Thorne formalism for slow rotation and a self-consistent rotating equilibrium code for rapid rotation. These results were compared against the tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 and the universal I-Love-Q relations previously established for neutron stars and quark stars.

## Key numbers
- The chirp mass of GW170817 is 1.188 solar masses.
- The I-Love-Q relations for hybrid stars in the third family agree with those for purely hadronic and quark stars within about 3% for both slowly and rapidly rotating configurations.
- The ACS-I models produce maximum masses up to 2.47 solar masses (j=0.10) and as low as 2.05 solar masses (j=0.60).
- The ACS-II models produce maximum masses of 2.08 solar masses (j=0.80) and 1.97 solar masses (j=1.00).
- The ACB4 model has a maximum mass of 2.11 solar masses on the hybrid branch and is inconsistent with GW170817 at 90% confidence; ACB5 has a maximum mass of 2.00 solar masses on the hybrid branch and satisfies the GW170817 constraints when one star is a hybrid star.
- The ACB5 model has a phase transition onset at a nucleon number density of 0.284 fm^{-3} (roughly 2 times nuclear saturation density); ACB7 has onset at 0.309 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars with a third family (Gerlach 1968, Kampfer 1981, Schertler 2000, Glendenning 2000) and the universal I-Love-Q relations (Yagi 2013, Yagi 2016) to the specific constraints from GW170817. It contradicts the assumption that the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass compact object can be approximated as a linear function of mass, as was done in earlier GW170817 analyses (PhysRevD.85.123007, PhysRevLett.111.071101). The paper enables future binary HS-NS simulations in full general relativity and combined gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observations (e.g., NICER) to distinguish between a first-order hadron-quark phase transition and a smooth crossover.
