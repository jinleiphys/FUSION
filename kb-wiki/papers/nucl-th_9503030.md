# Effects of Pairing in the Pseudo-SU(3) Model

> An extended version of the pseudo-SU(3) model which includes both spin and proton-neutron degrees of freedom is used to study the influence of the pairing interaction on K-band mixing, B(E2) values and quadrupole moments. Using the asymmetric rotor model as a backdrop, specific consequences of a many-particle shell-model based description of these collective properties are demonstrated and fundamental limits of the collective model's approach are investigated. Finally, the pseudo-SU(3) model, including representation mixing induced by pairing, is used to calculate the energies of 140Ce and the results are compared to experimental data and other theories.

## Key claim

Pairing-induced mixing of different SU(3) irreducible representations drives the system toward triaxiality and increases K-band mixing, but the effect is constrained by the Pauli Exclusion Principle which limits the available K values in a way that the phenomenological asymmetric rotor model cannot capture.

## Method

The authors extended the pseudo-SU(3) model to include spin and proton-neutron degrees of freedom, then calculated expectation values of the K^2 operator and mapped SU(3) irrep labels (λ, μ) onto the geometric deformation parameter γ using Casimir invariants. They compared pseudo-SU(3) results for a (2π, 2ν) configuration in the Nπ=4, Nν=5 shells against asymmetric rotor model predictions for K-band mixing, B(E2) values, and quadrupole moments as functions of the pairing strength G.

## Key numbers

- For the (2π, 2ν) configuration with no pairing (G=0), the leading SU(3) irrep is (λ, μ) = (14,0), giving γ = 3.2° for all yrast states
- The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength parameter used was χ = 4.32 keV, with a = 202 keV and b = 9.26 keV for the K_J^2 and J^2 terms
- For pairing strengths G ≤ 0.1 MeV, the yrast eigenstates show less triaxiality at higher angular momentum, attributed to rotational stretching
- The maximum average K value for each configuration in the pseudo-SU(3) model occurs at roughly one third of the maximum possible angular momentum
- For γ values between about 3° and 17° (the range induced by increasing pairing from weak to strong), the asymmetric rotor model B(E2; I → I+2) values are essentially independent of γ, while the B(E2; 0 → 2) strength shows a moderate decrease

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) model calculations reported in Troltenier, Bahri, and Draayer (1994) by investigating pairing effects on K-band mixing, B(E2) values, and quadrupole moments, using the asymmetric rotor model of Davydov and Filippov (1958) as a comparative backdrop. The results demonstrate fundamental limitations of the geometric collective model, which neglects single-particle degrees of freedom and thus cannot reproduce the Pauli-driven K-value restrictions found in the shell-model approach. The formalism enables future applications to semi-magic nuclei such as 140Ce, where the authors calculate excitation energies for comparison with experiment and other theories.
