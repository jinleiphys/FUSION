# A simple model for NN correlations in quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering

> We present a covariant extension of the relativistic Fermi gas model which incorporates correlation effects in nuclei. Within this model, inspired by the BCS descriptions of systems of fermions, we obtain the nuclear spectral function and from it the superscaling function for use in treating high-energy quasielastic electroweak processes. Interestingly, this model has the capability to yield the asymmetric tail seen in the experimental scaling function.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant extension of the relativistic Fermi gas model that incorporates nucleon-nucleon correlation effects, and shows that this BCS-inspired model can produce the asymmetric tail seen in the experimental scaling function, but only when the parameter controlling the momentum distribution tail is near a critical value; the model approaches first-kind scaling from below in the scaling region, contrary to experimental findings.

## Method

The authors construct a BCS-inspired wavefunction for the nuclear ground state and daughter nucleus, using creation operators that add relativistic on-shell spinors with back-to-back momenta and opposite helicities. They compute the nuclear spectral function from these states, then derive the superscaling function by integrating the spectral function over missing momentum and excitation energy, using a phenomenological three-parameter expression for the BCS coefficients v(k) that controls the momentum distribution tail. The parameters are fixed by requiring particle number conservation on average and stability of the daughter nucleus energy.

## Key numbers

* Fermi momentum k_F = 228 MeV/c used throughout the calculations
* Critical value of the correlation parameter beta_crit = 0.017 c/MeV, below which the system becomes strongly disrupted by correlations
* Onset of first-kind scaling at the quasielastic peak occurs at momentum transfers of order 500 MeV/c
* Away from the quasielastic peak, onset of first-kind scaling occurs only at momentum transfers of order 2 GeV/c
* For beta = 0.01 c/MeV, first-kind scaling is reached only for q approximately 1.5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model of Alberico (1988) by incorporating nucleon-nucleon correlations through a BCS-inspired approach, building on the superscaling framework established by Day and Sick. The model aims to provide predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for neutrino oscillation studies. The results are compared with experimental scaling function data from Maieron (2001) and Jourdan, and the authors note that their model approaches first-kind scaling from below, similar to the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model of Martin, but in contrast to relativistic mean-field theory of Caballero (2005) which approaches from above and agrees better with experiment.
