# Dependence of nuclear magnetic moments on quark masses and limits on temporal variation of fundamental constants from atomic clock experiments

> We calculate the dependence of the nuclear magnetic moments on the quark masses including the spin-spin interaction effects and obtain limits on the variation of the fine structure constant $α$ and $(m_q/Λ_{QCD})$ using recent atomic clock experiments examining hyperfine transitions in H, Rb, Cs, Yb$^+$ and Hg$^+$ and the optical transition in H, Hg$^+$ and Yb$^+$ .

## Key claim

The paper calculates how nuclear magnetic moments depend on quark masses, including the previously neglected spin-spin interaction between nucleons, and uses these calculations to set limits on the temporal variation of the fine structure constant alpha and the dimensionless ratio (m_q/Lambda_QCD) from atomic clock experiments. The authors note that the coefficient linking variations in (m_q/Lambda_QCD) to variations in alpha is model-dependent but generically large in grand unification models, making variations in quark masses potentially easier to detect than variations in alpha.

## Method

The authors first used chiral perturbation theory results for the dependence of free proton and neutron magnetic moments on light and strange quark masses (delta mu_p/mu_p = -0.087 delta m_q/m_q, etc.). They then calculated the dependence of nuclear magnetic moments on quark masses using two approaches: a simple valence shell model (method A) and a more realistic approach incorporating core nucleon contributions via the spin-spin interaction (method B), using experimental magnetic moments to determine the spin contributions of valence and core nucleons. Finally, they estimated the additional effect of the quark-mass dependence of the spin-spin interaction itself using a one-boson-exchange model with pi-meson and rho-meson exchange contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the free proton: delta mu_p/mu_p = -0.087 delta m_q/m_q (light quark mass dependence) and -0.013 delta m_s/m_s (strange quark mass dependence)
- For the free neutron: delta mu_n/mu_n = -0.118 delta m_q/m_q and +0.0013 delta m_s/m_s
- For Cs-133 (valence proton, including core polarization): delta mu/mu = 0.0441 delta (m_q/Lambda_QCD)/(m_q/Lambda_QCD)
- For Hg-199 (valence neutron, including core polarization): delta mu/mu = -0.116 delta (m_q/Lambda_QCD)/(m_q/Lambda_QCD)
- The ratio S1/S for the pi-meson exchange integral was calculated as 2.17, giving delta b/b = (1/3)(2 - S1/S) delta m_q/m_q
- The pi-meson mass is m_pi = 135 MeV = 0.68 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Flambaum (2003, 2004) on the quark-mass dependence of nucleon magnetic moments to include core polarization effects in nuclei, and builds on the suggestion by Karshenboim (2000) that hyperfine structure comparisons are sensitive to nuclear magnetic moment variations. The results enable interpretation of atomic clock experiments comparing hyperfine transitions in H, Rb, Cs, Yb+ and Hg+ (Bize 2005, Peik 2004, Prestage 1995) and optical transitions in H, Hg+ and Yb+ (Bize 2003, Fischer 2004, Peik 2005) to set limits on fundamental constant variation. The authors also note that effects may be enhanced by 2-3 orders of magnitude in diatomic molecules like LaS, LaO, LuS, LuO (Flambaum 2006).
