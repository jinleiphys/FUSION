# Relativistic Contribution of the Final-State Interaction to Deuteron Photodisintegration

> The contribution of the final-state interaction to the differential cross-section of deuteron photodisintegration at laboratory photon energies below 50 MeV is analyzed in the framework of Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a phenomenological rank-one separable interaction. The approximations made are the neglect of two-body exchange currents, negative-energy components of the bound-state vertex function and the scattering T matrix. It has been found that the gross effect of the final-state interaction with J=0,1 comes from the net contributions of the spin-triplet final states and spin-singlet (1S0+)-state. The relativistic results are compared with the nonrelativistic ones in every partial-wave channel. It is found that the relativistic effects change the magnitude of the final-state interaction from several percent to several tens of percent.

## Key claim

The relativistic Bethe-Salpeter treatment of the final-state interaction in deuteron photodisintegration changes the magnitude of the differential cross section by several percent at 5 MeV and up to twenty percent at 50 MeV laboratory photon energy, but the results cannot be compared to experimental data because the model neglects two-body exchange currents and negative-energy components of the bound-state vertex function and scattering T matrix.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two Dirac particles in Minkowski space using a rank-one separable interaction kernel with Yamaguchi-type covariant form factors, restricting to partial-wave channels with total angular momentum J ≤ 1 and positive-energy states only. They computed the half off-mass-shell T matrix for elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering and used it to construct the final-state interaction contribution to the invariant transition amplitude for deuteron photodisintegration, then performed the remaining two-dimensional numerical integration in FORTRAN after analytic integration over the relative energy variable.

## Context

This work extends the plane-wave approximation calculations of the same authors (Bondarenko et al., previous works) by incorporating the relativistic final-state interaction via the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, and confirms the nonrelativistic finding of Hadjimichael, Lee, and Walecka (previous work) that the ³S₁⁺–³D₁⁺ final state becomes pronounced at about 50 MeV. The results enable future development of a rigorous relativistic theory of deuteron photodisintegration at higher energies, but the authors note that comparison with experiment requires inclusion of two-body exchange currents and negative-energy components which were omitted.
