# The $a_0(980)$ physics in semileptonic $D^0$ and $D^+$ decays

> The decays $D^0\to d\bar u\,e^+ν\to a^-_0(980)\, e^+ν\toπ^-η\, e^+ν$ and $D^+\to d\bar d\,e^+ν\to a^0_0(980)\, e^+ν\toπ^0η\, e^+ν$ (and the charge conjugated ones) is the direct probe of the two-quark components in the $a^\pm_0(980)$ and $a^0_0(980)$ wave functions. Recent BESIII experiment is the first step in experimental study of these decays. We present a possible variant of $ηπ$ invariant mass distribution when $a_0(980)$ has no $q\bar q$ component at all.

## Key claim

The authors present a scenario where the $a_0(980)$ meson has **no constituent two-quark component at all**, being produced entirely through mixing with a heavier $a_0'$ meson (mass ~1400 MeV). This is a specific variant of the data description, not a unique conclusion; the authors note the results are "very preliminary due to large experimental errors."

## Method

The authors model the semileptonic decays $D^0 \to d\bar u\, e^+\nu \to a_0^-(980)\, e^+\nu \to \pi^-\eta\, e^+\nu$ and $D^+ \to d\bar d\, e^+\nu \to a_0^0(980)\, e^+\nu \to \pi^0\eta\, e^+\nu$ using a two-channel mixing formalism between the $a_0(980)$ and a heavier $a_0'$ resonance. They simultaneously fit data from three independent processes: the $\gamma\gamma \to \eta\pi^0$ cross section (Belle), the $\phi \to \eta\pi^0\gamma$ decay (KLOE), and the $K^0_S K^+$ correlation in Pb-Pb collisions (ALICE), using a modified parametrization of the $K\bar K$ scattering background phase. The $D$ decay data from BESIII are then compared to the model predictions with only the overall normalization as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- $m_{a^0_0} = 988.3$ MeV, $m_{a'_0} = 1423.9$ MeV (fitted resonance masses)
- $g^{(0)}_{a^0_0 \gamma\gamma} = 0$ (direct two-quark coupling to $\gamma\gamma$ set to zero)
- Signal branching for $D^0 \to \pi^-\eta\, e^+\nu$: $1.45^{+0.43}_{-0.40} \times 10^{-4}$ (compared to BESIII result $(1.33^{+0.33}_{-0.29}(stat) \pm 0.09(syst)) \times 10^{-4}$)
- Signal branching for $D^+ \to \pi^0\eta\, e^+\nu$: $1.94 \times 10^{-4}$
- $g_{a_0\eta\pi} = 3.99$ GeV, $g_{a_0K^+K^-} = 4.06$ GeV (coupling constants)
- $g_{a_0\eta'\pi} = -4.24$ GeV
- Combined $\chi^2$ for 99 degrees of freedom: $107.8$ (from $\gamma\gamma$, $\phi$ spectrum, and correlation data)
- $R = 6.3$ fm (source radius parameter for $K^0_S K^+$ correlation)

## Context

This work extends the program proposed by Achasov and Kiselev (2013, 2014) to study light scalar mesons in semileptonic $D$ and $B$ decays, following the first experimental measurements by BESIII (2018). It builds on the four-quark model of light scalar mesons originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) and Weinberg, and on previous analyses of $\phi \to a_0\gamma$ decays (Achasov 1989, 1997, 2003) and $\gamma\gamma \to \eta\pi^0$ reactions (Achasov and Shestakov 1988). The results enable future high-statistics tests of the $a_0(980)$ internal structure through semileptonic $D$ and $B$ decays, and suggest that the $a_0'$ meson around 1400 MeV plays a crucial role in understanding the $a_0(980)$ production mechanism.
