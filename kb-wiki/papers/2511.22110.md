# Symmetry energy in dilute matter and the neutron skin

> Energy density functional (EDF) theory provides a unified framework for the description of nuclei and of infinite nuclear matter. In principle, it facilitates direct connections between nuclear data and the nuclear equation of state (EoS). Although in practice traditional nuclear EDF theory has strained to describe finite nuclei and infinite systems at the same time, recently developed extended EDF models overcome many of the limitations of traditional models in that respect. A recent challenge to EDF and EoS studies has come entirely from within nuclear structure, namely how to account both for the relatively thin neutron skin in 48Ca as extracted by the CREX experiment and the relatively thick neutron skin of 208Pb exctracted by the PREX-II experiment. The discrepancy suggests a surface and structure effect. The present study shows that the puzzle can be resolved in a non-relativistic framework by revisiting the nuclear surface tension and diffuseness, as driven in part by the EoS in dilute matter well below the saturation point and in part by the isovector gradient terms and spin-orbit potential. Such effects have no bearing on the EoS near and above saturation.

# Symmetry energy in dilute matter and the neutron skin

## Key claim

The PREX-CREX puzzle, the discrepancy between the relatively thin neutron skin of ⁴⁸Ca and the relatively thick neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb, can be resolved within non-relativistic energy density functional theory by modifying the symmetry energy in dilute matter well below saturation density, without affecting the equation of state near and above saturation.

## Method

The author performed a parameter-space survey using the KIDS energy density functional framework, varying the symmetry energy parameters (J, L, K_sym, Q_sym) along with the isovector gradient term D₁₂ and spin-orbit coupling W₀. An additional density-dependent term with exponential decay was introduced to modify the equation of state only at low densities, and acceptable parameter combinations were selected by requiring 1% average accuracy for 17 data points of energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- CREX experiment result for ⁴⁸Ca neutron skin thickness: 0.121 ± 0.050 fm (1σ confidence interval)
- PREX-II experiment result for ²⁰⁸Pb neutron skin thickness: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm (1σ confidence interval)
- Symmetric-matter EoS parameters fixed at ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and (E₀, K₀) = (−16, 240) MeV
- Isoscalar effective mass and isovector enhancement factor fixed at (m*/m, κ) = (0.70, 0.40)
- Isoscalar gradient term fixed at C₁₂ = −75 MeV·fm⁵
- Example successful parameter set: (J, L, −K_sym, Q_sym) = (32.5, 65, 180, 1000) MeV with D₁₂ = 25 and W₀ = 140 MeV·fm⁵
- For this example, neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca reduced from 0.230 fm (original KIDS) to 0.171 fm (modified); ²⁰⁸Pb reduced from 0.277 fm to 0.212 fm
- Charge radii for the example: ⁴⁸Ca 3.507 fm (modified) vs 3.497 fm (original); ²⁰⁸Pb 5.463 fm (modified) vs 5.450 fm (original); experimental values 3.477 fm and 5.501 fm respectively

## Context

This work extends the KIDS functional framework (Papakonstantinou, 2022) and addresses the PREX-CREX puzzle that has challenged both non-relativistic Skyrme functionals and relativistic mean-field theory. A prior pilot study (Papakonstantinou, 2022) indicated that extreme values of higher-order symmetry energy parameters Q_sym and R_sym could achieve reconciliation, but with unrealistic dense-matter behavior. The present approach instead decouples the dilute-matter equation of state from the saturation regime, enabling reconciliation while maintaining realistic dense-matter properties. The results suggest that isoscaling experiments (Natowitz, 2010; Wada, 2012) have measured symmetry energy values of order 10 MeV at densities between 0.002 and 0.05 fm⁻³, attributed to clustering correlations. This work enables further exploration of isovector gradient terms and spin-orbit coupling in nuclear structure.
