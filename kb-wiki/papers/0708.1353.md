# Non-localities in nucleon-nucleus potentials

> Two causes of non-locality inherent in nucleon-nucleus scattering are considered. They are the results of two-nucleon antisymmetry of the projectile with each nucleon in the nucleus and the dynamic polarization potential representation of channel coupling. For energies $\sim 40 - 300$ MeV, a g-folding model of the optical potential is used to show the influence of the knock-out process that is a result of the two-nucleon antisymmetry. To explore the dynamic polarization potential caused by channel coupling, a multichannel algebraic scattering model has been used for low-energy scattering.

## Key claim
The non-localities in nucleon-nucleus optical potentials arising from the Pauli principle (knock-out exchange) and from channel coupling (dynamic polarization potential) are shown to be strongly energy-dependent and have significant magnitudes, but their specific effects on scattering observables can be masked by parameter adjustments in local phenomenological models.

## Method
The authors used two distinct theoretical frameworks: a g-folding model (based on the Kerman-McManus-Thaler theory) with the Melbourne effective NN interaction for medium energies (40-300 MeV), and the multichannel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method for low-energy scattering. The MCAS method was applied to the n+12C system using a collective model with quadrupole deformation, truncating the target spectrum to the 0+ ground state, the 2+ state at 4.43 MeV, and the 0+ state at 7.96 MeV, to extract the dynamic polarization potential (DPP) in coordinate space.

## Key numbers
- The central well depth of the 0s1/2 wave DPP for n+12C is -170 MeV-fm^-1 at 1.5 MeV and -240 MeV-fm^-1 at 2.73 MeV.
- The 0p3/2 wave DPP maximal depths are -22 MeV-fm^-1 at 1.5 MeV and -23 MeV-fm^-1 at 2.73 MeV.
- The 0d5/2 wave DPP maximal depths are -28 MeV-fm^-1 at 1.5 MeV and -32 MeV-fm^-1 at 2.73 MeV.
- The Perey-Buck potential was originally fitted to differential cross sections for 7.0 and 14.5 MeV neutrons elastically scattered from 208Pb.
- The MCAS calculations for n+12C were performed for energies up to approximately 6 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic optical model approach of Amos (2000) and the MCAS method of Amos (2003) by explicitly visualizing the non-local dynamic polarization potential in coordinate space. It contrasts with the phenomenological approach of Perey and Buck (1962), which used an energy-independent non-local form, by showing that the actual non-locality from channel coupling is strongly energy dependent. The results enable future studies to use these non-local potentials directly in scattering calculations rather than relying on local-equivalent approximations, which the authors note can violate the Pauli principle as shown by Canton (2005).
