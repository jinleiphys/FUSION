# Causal viscous hydrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We explore the effects of shear viscosity on the hydrodynamic evolution and final hadron spectra of Cu+Cu collisions at ultrarelativistic collision energies, using the newly developed (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1. Based on the causal Israel-Stewart formalism, this code describes the transverse evolution of longitudinally boost-invariant systems without azimuthal symmetry around the beam direction. Shear viscosity is shown to decelerate the longitudinal and accelerate the transverse hydrodynamic expansion. For fixed initial conditions, this leads to a longer quark-gluon plasma (QGP) lifetime, larger radial flow in the final state, and flatter transverse momentum spectra for the emitted hadrons compared to ideal fluid dynamic simulations. We find that the elliptic flow coefficient v_2 is particularly sensitive to shear viscosity: even the lowest value allowed by the AdS/CFT conjecture, eta/s=1/4pi, suppresses v_2 enough to have significant consequences for the phenomenology of heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. A comparison between our numerical results and earlier analytic estimates of viscous effects within a blast-wave model parametrization of the expanding fireball at freeze-out reveals that the full dynamical theory leads to much tighter constraints for the specific shear viscosity eta/s, thereby supporting the notion that the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC exhibits almost ``perfect fluidity''.

## Key claim

Shear viscosity suppresses elliptic flow \(v_2\) enough that even the conjectured AdS/CFT lower bound \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) has significant phenomenological consequences for Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC, and the full dynamical theory yields much tighter constraints on specific shear viscosity than earlier analytic blast-wave model estimates, supporting the notion of nearly perfect fluidity in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors developed VISH2+1, a (2+1)-dimensional causal viscous hydrodynamic code based on the Israel-Stewart formalism, solving the coupled evolution equations for the energy-momentum tensor and shear viscous pressure tensor for longitudinally boost-invariant systems with transverse expansion. They initialized Cu+Cu collisions using a Glauber model with zero initial transverse flow and a peak energy density of 30 GeV/fm³ at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\), evolved with a specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) and relaxation time \(\tau_\pi = 3\eta/(sT)\), and computed final hadron spectra via a modified Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure at \(T_\mathrm{dec} = 130\) MeV using two different equations of state (EOS I and SM-EOS Q).

## Key numbers

- Specific shear viscosity set to \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi \approx 0.08\), the conjectured AdS/CFT lower bound.
- Initial peak energy density in central Cu+Cu collisions: \(e_0 = 30\) GeV/fm³ at initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_\mathrm{dec} = 130\) MeV.
- For SM-EOS Q: critical temperature \(T_c = 164\) MeV, latent heat \(\Delta e_\mathrm{lat} = 1.15\) GeV/fm³, mixed phase between \(e_\mathrm{H} = 0.45\) GeV/fm³ and \(e_\mathrm{Q} = 1.6\) GeV/fm³.
- Nuclear radius for Cu: \(R_\mathrm{Cu} = 4.2\) fm, diffuseness \(\xi = 0.596\) fm, nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: \(\sigma = 40\) mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier ideal hydrodynamic studies of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (Kolb and Heinz, 1999) by incorporating shear viscosity via the causal Israel-Stewart formalism, addressing the breakdown of ideal fluid dynamics observed in peripheral collisions and at high transverse momenta. It builds on prior viscous hydrodynamic developments in 1+1 and 2+1 dimensions (Teaney, 2004; Muronga, 2004; Chaudhuri, 2005; Baier, 2006; Romatschke, 2007) and provides a more complete dynamical treatment than the analytic blast-wave model estimates of Teaney (2003), enabling tighter constraints on the specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma.
