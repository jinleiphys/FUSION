# Continuity from neutron matter to two-flavor quark matter with $^1 S_0$ and $^3 P_2$ superfluidity

> This study is performed with the aim of gaining insights into the possible applicability of the quark-hadron continuity concept, not only in the idealized case of three-flavor symmetric quark matter, but also for the transition from neutron matter to two-flavor quark matter. A key issue is the continuity between neutron superfluidity and a corresponding superfluid quark phase produced by $d$-quark pairing. Symmetry arguments are developed and relevant dynamical mechanisms are analyzed. It is pointed out that the $^3P_2$ superfluidity in dense neutron matter has a direct analogue in the $^3P_2$ pairing of $d$-quarks in two-flavor quark matter. This observation supports the idea that the quark-hadron continuity hypothesis may be valid for such systems. Possible implications for neutron stars are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The quark-hadron continuity hypothesis, previously established for idealized three-flavor symmetric quark matter, may also apply to the transition from neutron matter to two-flavor quark matter, provided that the $^3P_2$ superfluidity in dense neutron matter has a direct analogue in the $^3P_2$ pairing of $d$-quarks in two-flavor quark matter.

## Method

The authors develop symmetry arguments comparing the spontaneous symmetry breaking patterns in neutron superfluid matter and in two-flavor quark matter with diquark condensates. They analyze the chemical potentials of neutrons, protons, and quarks under $\beta$-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions using a relativistic mean-field model with scalar and vector interactions, and they rearrange order-parameter operators to demonstrate how neutron pairing operators can be factorized into diquark condensates.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$
- $^1S_0$ neutron superfluidity occurs at baryon densities $\nB < 0.5\,n_0$
- $^3P_2$ neutron pairing becomes dominant for $\nB > n_0$
- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u = 312.3\ \text{MeV}$, $m_d = 313.6\ \text{MeV}$
- Vector coupling in hadronic phase: $G_v \sim 4\ \text{fm}^2$, $G_\tau \sim 1\ \text{fm}^2$
- Vector coupling in quark phase: $g_v \sim G_v/9 = 0.44\ \text{fm}^2$
- Scalar condensate drops to about half its vacuum value at $\nB \sim 5\,n_0$
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi \simeq 92\ \text{MeV}$

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron continuity concept originally proposed by Schafer and Wilczek (1998) for three-flavor color-flavor locked matter to the more realistic two-flavor case relevant for neutron stars. It builds on prior studies of $^3P_2$ neutron superfluidity (Hoffberg 1970, Tamagaki 1970) and two-flavor NJL model investigations (Kitazawa 2003, Zhang 2008). The results suggest that the 2SC phase with an additional $\langle dd \rangle$ condensate (termed 2SC+X) can provide a continuous connection to neutron superfluid matter, with implications for neutron star cooling and equation of state modeling.
