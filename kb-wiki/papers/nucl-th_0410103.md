# Antisymmetrized Green's function approach to $(e,e')$ reactions with a realistic nuclear density

> A completely antisymmetrized Green's function approach to the inclusive quasielastic $(e,e')$ scattering, including a realistic one-body density, is presented. The single particle Green's function is expanded in terms of the eigenfunctions of the nonhermitian optical potential. This allows one to treat final state interactions consistently in the inclusive and in the exclusive reactions. Nuclear correlations are included in the one-body density. Numerical results for the response functions of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a completely antisymmetrized Green's function approach to inclusive quasielastic $(e,e')$ scattering that includes nuclear correlations through a realistic one-body density matrix, but the numerical results are limited to the direct contribution to the hadronic tensor while the interference contribution is neglected, and the continuum states of the residual nucleus are omitted despite being acknowledged as conceptually important.

## Method

The single-particle Green's function is expanded in a biorthogonal basis of eigenfunctions of the nonhermitian optical potential, allowing consistent treatment of final state interactions in both inclusive and exclusive reactions. The hadronic tensor is decomposed into direct and interference contributions using extended projection operators, with nuclear correlations incorporated through the natural expansion of the one-body density matrix (ODM) and its occupation numbers. The spectral function for excited states of the residual nucleus is approximated by the ground-state spectral function shifted by an average energy shift.

## Key numbers

- For $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca, numerical results for the longitudinal and transverse response functions are presented and discussed.
- The one-body density matrix has a finite trace equal to $Z+1$, where $Z+1$ is the number of protons in the target nucleus.
- Occupation numbers $n_\nu$ satisfy $0 \leq n_\nu \leq 1$ and $\sum_\nu n_\nu = Z+1$.
- For momentum transfer $|\q| > 2 k_{\mathrm F}$, the coherent contribution to the non-energy-weighted sum rule is negligible, giving $\Sigma^{00}(\q) \simeq (Z+1)$.
- The incoherent contribution to the non-energy-weighted sum rule for the scalar component is $\Sigma_{\mathrm{inc}}^{00}(\q) = Z+1$.

## Context

This work extends previous Green's function approaches by Capuzzi, Giusti, Pacati, and Meucci (2000-2003) that neglected correlations, incorporating a realistic one-body density matrix to include partial occupation numbers and nuclear correlations. The approach contrasts with treatments using Feshbach projection operators (Feshbach, 1958-1962) and addresses the longstanding problem of simultaneously describing longitudinal and transverse response functions, which has been investigated by Fabrocini (1998), Co (1998), and others using correlation effects, and by Amaro (1998) and Meucci (2001) using relativistic frameworks. The method enables future calculations that can include both two-body currents and tensor correlations, as suggested by Leidemann (1997), Fabrocini (1998), and Sick (1998).
