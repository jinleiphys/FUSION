# A high-precision variational approach to three- and four-nucleon bound and zero-energy scattering states

> The hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method has been widely applied in recent times to the study of the bound states, using the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle, and of low-energy scattering processes, using the Kohn variational principle, of A=3 and 4 nuclear systems. When the wave function of the system is expanded over a sufficiently large set of HH basis functions, containing or not correlation factors, quite accurate results can be obtained for the observables of interest. In this paper, the main aspects of the method are discussed together with its application to the A=3 and 4 nuclear bound and zero-energy scattering states. Results for a variety of nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) local or non-local interactions are reported. In particular, NN and 3N interactions derived in the framework of the chiral effective field theory and NN potentials from which the high momentum components have been removed, as recently presented in the literature, are considered for the first time within the context of the HH method. The purpose of this paper is two-fold. First, to present a complete description of the HH method for bound and scattering states, including also detailed formulas for the computation of the matrix elements of the NN and 3N interactions. Second, to report accurate results for bound and zero-energy scattering states obtained with the most commonly used interaction models. These results can be useful for comparison with those obtained by other techniques and are a significant test for different future approaches to such problems.

## Key claim

The hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method, combined with the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle for bound states and the Kohn variational principle for zero-energy scattering states, can produce accurate results for A=3 and 4 nuclear systems when the wave function is expanded over a sufficiently large set of HH basis functions, with or without correlation factors; the paper presents this method and reports results for a variety of nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, including chiral effective field theory potentials and low-momentum potentials (Vlow-k) considered for the first time within the HH framework.

## Method

The wave function of the A=3 or 4 system is expanded in hyperspherical harmonic (HH) basis functions constructed from Jacobi coordinates and hyperspherical angles, with the hyperradius ρ and a set of angular variables Ω_N. For bound states, the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle is used to determine the expansion coefficients; for zero-energy scattering states, the Kohn variational principle is applied, with the scattering wave function expanded in the HH basis while explicitly including the asymptotic part describing the relative motion of the incident nucleon and the target. The method handles both local and non-local NN and 3N interactions, including those from chiral perturbation theory and Vlow-k potentials, and computes matrix elements of the interactions using detailed formulas provided in the paper.

## Key numbers

- The hyperradius ρ is defined as ρ² = (2/A) Σ_{i<j} (r_i - r_j)² = 2 Σ_{i=1}^A (r_i - X)², where X is the center-of-mass coordinate.
- For the grand angular momentum operator Λ_N², the eigenvalue equation is Λ_N² Y_[G] = -G(G + D - 2) Y_[G] with D = 3N, where N = A - 1.
- The grand angular momentum quantum number G is given by G = 2n_N + ℓ_N + G_{N-1} for the recursive construction, with G_j = Σ_{i=1}^j (ℓ_i + 2n_i) and n_1 ≡ 0.
- The normalization factor for the hyperspherical polynomials is 𝒩^{ℓ_j,ν_j}_{n_j} = [2ν_j Γ(ν_j - n_j) n_j! / (Γ(ν_j - n_j - ℓ_j - 1/2) Γ(n_j + ℓ_j + 3/2))]^{1/2}.
- For the plane wave expansion in 3N-dimensional space: e^{i Σ q_i·x_i} = (2π)^{D/2} / (Qρ)^{D/2 - 1} Σ_{[G]} i^G Y*_{[G]}(Ω^q_N) Y_{[G]}(Ω_N) J_{G + D/2 - 1}(Qρ).

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical harmonic method previously developed by the same authors (Kievsky, Rosati, Viviani, and collaborators) for bound and scattering states of A=3 and 4 nuclei, and provides a comprehensive description of the technique including detailed formulas for matrix elements. The paper reports results for the first time using chiral effective field theory NN and 3N interactions (from the work of Epelbaum, Entem, and others) and Vlow-k potentials (from Bogner, Schwenk, and collaborators) within the HH framework, enabling comparison with other methods such as Faddeev equations (Glockle, 1996), Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations (Nogga, 2000; Lazauskas, 2005), quantum Monte Carlo (Pieper, 2001), no-core shell model (Navratil, 2000), and coupled-rearrangement-channel Gaussian basis (Kamimura, 1988). The results serve as benchmarks for future approaches to few-nucleon problems.
