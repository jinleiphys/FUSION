# Equations of State and Maximum Mass of Neutron Stars in Light of PSR J1614-2230

> We shall examine various types of equations of state for neutron stars, which determine the structure of neutron stars. In particular, the relation between mass and radius of neutron stars is of primary consideration. By combining an equation of state (EOS) with the Tolmann-Oppenheimer-Volkoff structure equations, we can determine the theoretical maximum mass of a neutron star for a given equation of state. One question we seek to answer is whether quark matter can exist in the core of a neutron star. In light of the discovery of pulsar PSR J1614-2230, the mass of which is observed to be 1.97 solar masses, a valid equation of state must achieve a maximum mass that is greater than 2 solar masses. To try to solve this problem, we experiment with different sets of parameters for the quark matter to try to meet the lower limit 2-solar-mass criterion. It is found that certain parameters contribute significantly to the maximum mass of a neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a hybrid equation of state including a quark matter core can satisfy the 2-solar-mass maximum mass criterion set by PSR J1614-2230, but only for a specific set of quark matter parameters; most quark matter parameter choices produce maximum masses below 2 solar masses.

## Method

The authors construct a comprehensive neutron star equation of state by stitching together tabulated EOS for the outer crust (Haensel and Pichon, 1994) and inner crust (Douchin and Haensel, 2001), a Quantum Monte Carlo neutron matter EOS from Gandolfi et al. with Urbana IX parameters, and a quark matter EOS parameterized by three constants (a4, a2, and the bag constant B). They compute threshold pressures for the phase transition between neutron matter and quark matter by matching chemical potentials and pressures, then solve the Tolmann-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each hybrid EOS to obtain mass-radius relations and maximum masses.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses (from Demorest et al.)
- Outer crust EOS valid from 3.833 × 10^24 dyn cm^-2 to 6.2149 × 10^29 dyn cm^-2
- Inner crust EOS valid from 6.2150 × 10^29 dyn cm^-2 to 5.3711 × 10^32 dyn cm^-2
- Neutron matter core EOS uses parameters: a = 13.4 MeV, b = 5.62 MeV, α = 0.514, β = 2.436, with saturation density n0 = 0.16 × 10^39 cm^-3
- Quark matter parameter set d) (a4 = 0.6, a2 = (150 MeV)^2, B^1/4 = 141.2 MeV) produces a maximum mass safely above 2 solar masses
- Parameter sets a) and b) produce maximum masses around 1.5 solar masses; set c) produces roughly 1.85 solar masses
- The EOS without quark matter reaches 2.5 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutron star structure following the discovery of the massive pulsar PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest et al., which set a new lower bound of 2 solar masses for the maximum neutron star mass and ruled out many previously viable equations of state. The paper specifically investigates whether hybrid stars with quark matter cores, which previous research had shown to produce softer equations of state with lower maximum masses, could still satisfy the new observational constraint. The parameter choices for quark matter are inspired by Alford et al., and the results show that the a4 parameter most significantly affects the maximum mass, while a2 mainly influences the threshold pressure for the neutron-to-quark matter phase transition.
