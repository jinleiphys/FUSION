# Phenomenological neutron star equations of state: 3-window modeling of QCD matter

> We discuss the 3-window modeling of cold, dense QCD matter equations of state at density relevant to neutron star properties. At low baryon density, n_B < ~ 2n_s (n_s: nuclear saturation density), we utilize purely hadronic equations of state that are constrained by empirical observations at density n_B ~ n_s and neutron star radii. At high density, n_B > ~ 5n_s, we use the percolated quark matter equations of state which must be very stiff to pass the two-solar mass constraints. The intermediate domain at 2 < n_B/n_s < 5 is described as neither purely hadronic nor percolated quark matter, and the equations of state are inferred by interpolating hadronic and percolated quark matter equations of state. Possible forms of the interpolation are severely restricted by the condition on the (square of) speed of sound, 0 < c_s^2 < 1. The characteristics of the 3-window equation of state are compared with those of conventional hybrid and self-bound quark matters. Using a schematic quark model for the percolated domain, it is argued that the two-solar mass constraint requires the model parameters to be as large as their vacuum values, indicating that the gluon dynamics remains strongly non-perturbative to n_B ~ 10n_s. The hyperon puzzle is also briefly discussed in light of quark descriptions.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a "3-window" modeling of QCD matter, using purely hadronic equations of state at low density (n_B < ~2n_s), percolated quark matter equations of state at high density (n_B > ~5n_s), and an interpolated description in the intermediate region, can produce stiff enough equations of state to satisfy the two-solar-mass neutron star constraint, provided the quark matter model parameters are as large as their vacuum values, indicating strongly non-perturbative gluon dynamics up to n_B ~ 10n_s. The hedge is that the paper's purpose is not to predict equations of state but to delineate properties of strongly correlated QCD matter and develop a plausible picture.

## Method
The author constructs equations of state using the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) purely nucleonic equation of state for n_B < 2n_s and a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector and diquark interactions for percolated quark matter at n_B > 5n_s. For the intermediate domain (2 < n_B/n_s < 5), the pressure is interpolated as a smooth function of chemical potential using a polynomial form that matches the hadronic and quark pressures up to second derivatives, subject to the condition that the square of the speed of sound satisfies 0 < c_s^2 < 1. The resulting equations of state are used to compute neutron star mass-radius relations via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n_s ≃ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Low-density hadronic domain: n_B < ~2n_s
- High-density percolated quark matter domain: n_B > ~5n_s
- Intermediate interpolation domain: 2 < n_B/n_s < 5
- The two-solar-mass constraint requires model parameters as large as their vacuum values, indicating strongly non-perturbative gluon dynamics up to n_B ~ 10n_s
- For the bag model, the maximum star mass is M_max = 2.033 × (56 MeV·fm⁻³ / B)^(1/2) M_⊙; if B ≥ 56 MeV·fm⁻³, self-bound quark matter can pass the 2M_⊙ constraint, with threshold chemical potential μ_th = 274 MeV and baryon density n_B(μ_th) = 0.273 fm⁻³ ≃ 1.7 n_s
- The pQCD calculations to O(α_s²) show interactions are important already at μ ~ 1 GeV or n_B ~ 10² n_s

## Context
This work extends the 3-window approach introduced by Masuda, Hatsuda, and Takatsuka (2012) and discussed in slightly different versions by Alvarez-Castillo (2013), Hell (2014), and Kojo (2014). It contrasts with conventional hybrid equations of state that use Maxwell construction for first-order phase transitions between hadronic and weakly interacting quark matter, which typically soften the equation of state and struggle with the two-solar-mass constraint. The paper also discusses the hyperon puzzle, the conflict between hyperon softening and the 2M_⊙ constraint, in light of quark descriptions. The approach enables exploration of a new class of stiff quark matter equations of state that are not accessible in conventional hybrid constructions, and draws an analogy to finite-temperature QCD where interpolating between hadron resonance gas and pQCD successfully describes lattice data.
