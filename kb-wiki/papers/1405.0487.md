# Worldline holographic Schwinger effect

> The decay of the vacuum due to the presence of an electric field is expected to be delayed by a confining force. We demonstrate that this feature is captured by our model [arXiv:1404.0011 [hep-ph],arXiv:1312.5718 [hep-ph]] for hadrons based on the worldline formalism. Our model, while based entirely in four-dimensional quantum field theory, shares many features with holographic approaches: it appears intrinsically quantum mechanical; as an auxiliary fifth dimension Schwinger's proper time combines with the physical four spacetime dimensions into an AdS$_5$ geometry; conformal-symmetry breaking contributions lead to warping; hidden local symmetry emerges; four-dimensional sources are extended to five-dimensional fields by a Wilson flow (gradient flow); and a variational principle for this flow reproduces the corresponding holographic calculation. The approach also yields the higher-dimensional description in the non-relativistic case.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a confining force delays vacuum decay (the Schwinger effect) to higher electric field strengths, and that this delay is captured by the author's worldline-based model of hadrons, which reproduces features of holographic approaches without relying on gauge/gravity duality.

## Method

The author computes the one-loop effective action in the worldline formalism, first for a constant electric field alone and then with an additional confining interaction term proportional to the area enclosed by the worldline trajectory. The path integral over periodic trajectories is evaluated using Fourier decomposition, and the vacuum persistence amplitude is obtained from the imaginary part of the effective action. The confining interaction is incorporated by averaging over a gauge field component that yields an area-law term, and the analysis uses both exact path integration and worldline-instanton saddle-point approximations.

## Key numbers

* The standard Schwinger exponent for scalar matter is \(-\pi l \frac{m^2}{|E|}\) for the \(l\)-th pole, with the dominant \(l=1\) term giving \(\mathrm{Im}\, w \propto 2\pi E^2 \frac{(-)^{l+1}}{(l\pi)^2} e^{-\frac{m^2}{|E|}l\pi}\) (Equation (13)).
* With the confining interaction of strength \(\Lambda\), the saddle-point exponent becomes \(-\pi \frac{m^2}{|E|-\Lambda}\) for \(|E|>\Lambda\), and the saddle-point radius is \(R \rightarrow \frac{m}{|E|-\Lambda}\) (Equations (23) and (22)).
* For higher winding number \(l\), the threshold condition becomes \(|E| > l\Lambda\) with exponent \(-\pi l \frac{m^2}{|E|-l\Lambda}\) (derived from Equation (26)).
* The exact pole positions from the constrained Fourier analysis give \(T = \frac{n\pi}{|E|-n\Lambda}\) leading to the exponential factor \(e^{-n\pi\frac{m^2}{|E|-n\Lambda}}\) (Equation (27)).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous model of hadrons (Dietrich 2014) based on the worldline formalism, which shares features with holographic approaches like AdS/QCD (Karch 2006) and soft-wall models. The delayed onset of vacuum decay in the presence of confinement was previously found in holographic studies by Sato (2013), Kawai (2013), and Hashimoto (2013), and the present paper reproduces this behavior from a purely four-dimensional quantum field theory framework. The approach enables further study of nonperturbative phenomena in QCD without relying on gauge/string duality, and the worldline-instanton method connects to the Gutzwiller trace formula for quantum mechanical systems.
