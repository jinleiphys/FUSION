# How neutron stars constrain the nuclear equation of state

> Recent neutron star observations set new constraints for the equation of state of baryonic matter. A chiral effective field theory approach is used for the description of neutron-dominated nuclear matter present in the outer core of neutron stars. Possible hybrid stars with quark matter in the inner core are discussed using a three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an equation of state derived from chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) with baryonic and mesonic degrees of freedom, including three-body forces, is compatible with recent neutron star observations, even without exotic matter in the core, but notes that questions about higher-order terms beyond three-body forces remain open.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for neutron star matter using two complementary approaches: chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) for nucleonic matter, and a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a vector interaction for quark matter. They combine these with a phenomenological SLy crust equation of state at low densities (below about 0.8 times nuclear saturation density) and use polytropic extrapolations at higher densities, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations. For hybrid stars, they consider both a smooth quark-hadron crossover and a first-order phase transition using Gibbs conditions.

## Key numbers

- The pulsars J1614-2230 and J0348+0432 have masses of 1.97 +/- 0.04 and 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses, respectively
- Neutron star radii are constrained to an allowed range of about 10 to 15 km
- Nuclear saturation density is taken as rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The central density of a typical two-solar-mass neutron star using the ChEFT equation of state does not exceed about 5 rho_0
- For the first-order transition scenario with zero vector coupling (G_v=0), the quark-hadron coexistence region lies between 4 rho_0 and 9 rho_0
- The typical nucleon Fermi momenta under these conditions are not larger than about 4 m_pi and much smaller than the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking scale of 4 pi f_pi ~ 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hebeler (2010) and Hebeler (2013) for constraining the neutron star equation of state using observational data, and builds on the chiral effective field theory framework of Kaiser (2001) and Kaiser (2011). The results support a stiff equation of state without substantial exotic-matter admixtures, consistent with the variational many-body calculations of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998), while the hybrid star scenarios follow the quark-hadron crossover picture of Schafer (1998), Baym (2008), and Maeda (2009), as well as the first-order transition approach of Glendenning (1992).
