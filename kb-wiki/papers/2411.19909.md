# Gravitational form factors of the deuteron

> The gravitational form factors of the deuteron are calculated in the framework of non-relativistic chiral effective field theory. Non-relativistic reduction of the matrix element of the energy-momentum tensor operator for spin-one systems is worked out, and the gravitational form factors of the deuteron are extracted from the three-point function of the energy-momentum tensor using the LSZ reduction formula. The obtained form factors are compared to results of model calculations available in the literature.

## Key claim

The gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the deuteron are calculated in non-relativistic chiral effective field theory at leading order in the nucleon-nucleon potential, with the caveat that the regularization procedure violates energy-momentum tensor conservation, though the resulting effects appear to be rather small.

## Method

The authors extract the deuteron GFFs from the three-point function of the energy-momentum tensor operator and deuteron interpolating fields using the LSZ reduction formula, working in the Breit frame with static approximation. The calculation employs the leading-order nucleon-nucleon potential consisting of derivative-less contact interactions and one-pion-exchange, regularized with a smooth cutoff parameter Λ varied between 400 and 600 MeV. The deuteron structure functions are obtained by solving integral equations for the S- and D-state components, and the matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor are computed numerically including tree-level diagrams and two-nucleon connected diagrams up to zeroth chiral order.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff parameter Λ is varied in the range 400–600 MeV, with central value Λ = 500 MeV
- The effective axial coupling constant is set to g_A = 1.29 to account for the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy
- The low-energy constant c_8 is fixed to c_8 = -2.77 GeV^{-1} by matching the calculated value of D_0(0) to that of He and Zahed (2024)
- The deuteron binding momentum is p_B = sqrt(m_N E_b), where E_b is the deuteron binding energy and m_N is the nucleon mass
- The form factor E_0 shows a deviation from 1 at q=0 that is "tiny," indicating small effects from regularization violation and neglected relativistic corrections

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of deuteron structure, previously applied to electromagnetic form factors by Kaplan (1998), Phillips (1999, 2003, 2006), Walzl (2001), Kolling (2012), Epelbaum (2013), and Filin (2019, 2020), to the gravitational form factors. The only prior calculations of deuteron GFFs were model calculations using a phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potential by He and Zahed (2023, 2024), with which the present results are compared. The results enable future systematic studies of nuclear gravitational form factors with controlled accuracy and can be extended by applying symmetry-preserving regularization as developed by Djukanovic (2004, 2006) and Krebs (2023).
