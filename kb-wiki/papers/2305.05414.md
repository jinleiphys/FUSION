# Investigation of unbound hydrogen isotopes with the Gamow shell model

> Although they are part of the lightest nuclei, the hydrogen isotopes are not well understood both experimentally and theoretically. Indeed, besides deuteron and triton, all known hydrogen isotopes are resonances of complex structure. Even more elusive is 7H, which may have been observed experimentally and has been claimed to be a narrow resonance. Nevertheless, even its existence is controversial, and its theoretical study is difficult due to both its unbound character and large number of interacting valence nucleons. It is then the object of this paper to theoretically study the hydrogen isotopes {4-7}H with the Gamow shell model, which is, up to our knowledge, the first direct calculation of unbound resonance hydrogen isotopes up to 7H. As the Gamow shell model includes both continuum coupling and inter-nucleon correlations, useful information can be obtained about poorly known unbound hydrogen isotopes. Our present calculations indicate that {4,6}H ground states are fairly broad resonances, whereas those of {5,7}H are narrow, which is in accordance with current experimental data. The results then suggest that, in particular, {5,7}H should be more heavily studied, as they might well be among the most narrow neutron resonances of the light nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first direct calculation of unbound resonance hydrogen isotopes up to 7H using the Gamow shell model, showing that 4H and 6H ground states are fairly broad resonances while 5H and 7H are narrow, in accordance with current experimental data. However, the authors note that the theoretical errors are significant for 7H, with energy uncertainties of 400-600 keV depending on the interaction used, and the results depend on varying the Woods-Saxon core potential depth within a range determined by experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the Gamow shell model (GSM) with a core+valence neutron picture employing a 3H core, utilizing the Berggren basis in the complex momentum plane to treat unbound states. Two-body interactions were taken from the Minnesota (MN) and Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) forces, with parameters optimized using 4He as a core to reproduce neutron-rich helium isotopes, while the Woods-Saxon potential depth mimicking the 3H core was varied to assess theoretical uncertainties. For 7H, natural orbitals were generated using 3p-3h truncations to reduce the Hamiltonian matrix dimensions and enable calculations without further truncations.

## Key numbers

- 5H ground state: energy of about 1.4 MeV and width of about 500 keV (with theoretical errors of about 100 keV for energy and 200 keV for width using FHT and MN interactions)
- 6H ground state: energy of about 3.2 MeV and width of about 2 MeV across all considered potential depths and interactions
- 7H ground state neutron-emission width: between 15 and 240 keV with the MN interaction and between 10 and 140 keV with the FHT interaction
- 7H energy theoretical error: about 400 keV when using the FHT interaction and about 600 keV when using the MN interactions
- 4H width in the model is larger than that obtained in PhysRevC.104.024319 by a factor of 2, attributed to the oversimplified one-body nature of 4H in this core+valence picture

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of unbound hydrogen isotopes, which had been limited to Faddeev-Yakubovski equations (Lazauskas, 2019) and cluster models (PhysRevC.63.027001, ADAHCHOUR2008252), and contrasts with those calculations that predicted 5H as a broad resonance of width around 2.5-3.5 MeV. The results enable future experimental focus on 5H and 7H as potentially among the most narrow neutron resonances of the light nuclear chart, comparable to 10He, and could provide new information about the nucleon-nucleon interaction under extreme conditions. The small width of 7H is attributed to the closure of the 0p3/2 neutron shell, similar to the origin of the abnormally large binding energy of 8He.
