# Anisotropy in the equation of state of strongly magnetized quark matter within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> In this article, we calculate the magnetization and other thermodynamical quantities for strongly magnetized quark matter within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at zero temperature. We assume two scenarios, chemically equilibrated charge neutral matter present in the interior of compact stars and zero-strangeness isospin-symmetric matter created in nuclear experiments. We show that the magnetization oscillates with density but in a much more smooth form than what was previously shown in the literature. As a consequence, we do not see the unphysical behavior in the pressure in the direction perpendicular to the magnetic field that was previously found. Finally, we also analyze the effects of a vector interaction on our results.

## Key claim

The authors show that, contrary to claims in Menezes (2015), strong magnetic fields in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model do not generate unphysical behavior in the magnetization or perpendicular pressure, provided the derivatives of the scalar condensates with respect to the magnetic field are correctly taken as zero in the magnetization calculation.

## Method

The authors compute the equation of state of three-flavor quark matter within the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at zero temperature, including a vector interaction term. They consider two scenarios: zero-strangeness isospin-symmetric matter and chemically equilibrated charge-neutral matter with leptons (electrons and muons). The magnetization is calculated as the partial derivative of the parallel pressure with respect to the magnetic field, enforcing thermodynamic consistency by imposing stationary conditions on the grand potential with respect to both the scalar condensates and the total quark density.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: density-independent values of 10^17 G and 10^18 G, and a chemical-potential-dependent profile producing fields between 1.03 x 10^17 G and 5.31 x 10^17 G for a star with baryon mass 1.6 solar masses
- Model parameters: cutoff Lambda = 631.4 MeV, coupling G = 1.835/Lambda^2, K = 9.29/Lambda^5, current quark masses m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 135.7 MeV
- The magnetization for realistic magnetic fields is about three orders of magnitude lower in strength than in Menezes (2015)
- Up to magnetic field strengths of 10^18 G, there is no change in population and no pressure anisotropy generated by the magnetic field
- For magnetic field strengths slightly larger than 10^18 G, the parallel and perpendicular pressures start to differ at any baryon number density

## Context

This work corrects previous results by Menezes (2015) that found unphysical behavior in the magnetization and perpendicular pressure of magnetized quark matter within the NJL model. The authors show that when the magnetization is calculated correctly, the oscillations are much smoother and the perpendicular pressure does not become discontinuous. The results are relevant for modeling magnetars and compact stars, and the authors note that self-consistent general-relativity calculations (Chatterjee 2014, Franzon 2015, Dexheimer 2016) do not present solutions for stars with central magnetic fields beyond 1 or 2 times 10^18 G. The inclusion of a vector interaction stiffens the equation of state, which may allow larger stellar central magnetic fields in future calculations.
