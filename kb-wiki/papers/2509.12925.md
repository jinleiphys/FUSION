# Coupled-channel analysis for vector charmonia and their nature

> High-precision $e^+e^-\to c\bar{c}$ data (20 final states) from the BESIII and Belle in $\sqrt{s}=3.75-4.7$ GeV are analyzed with a semi three-body unitary coupled-channel model. Vector charmonium poles are extracted from the amplitudes obtained from the fit. We find well-known $ψ$ states listed in the PDG, and also several states near open-charm thresholds. The compositeness of the near-threshold poles suggests that $ψ(4040)$ could mainly consist of a $D^*\bar{D}^*$-molecule component, rather than a conventionally accepted quark-model $ψ(3S)$ state. Also, $ψ(4230)$ and $ψ(4360)$ might be substantial mixtures of $D_1(2420)\bar{D}$, $D_1(2420)\bar{D}^*$, $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$, and $c\bar{c}$ components.

# Coupled-channel analysis for vector charmonia and their nature

## Key claim
The paper claims that the well-established $\psi(4040)$ state, conventionally assigned as the quark-model $\psi(3S)$, may instead be predominantly a $D^*\bar{D}^*$ hadron molecule, and that $\psi(4230)$ and $\psi(4360)$ are not simple $D_1\bar{D}$ or $D_1\bar{D}^*$ molecules but rather substantial mixtures of multiple molecular and $c\bar{c}$ components. These conclusions are hedged by the caveat that the compositeness measure is generally complex and depends on the choice of form factors, making its interpretation non-trivial.

## Method
The author performed a global coupled-channel analysis of 20 final states of $e^+e^-\to c\bar{c}$ cross-section data from BESIII and Belle over $\sqrt{s}=3.75-4.7$ GeV, using a semi three-body unitary coupled-channel model. The model includes quasi two-body $Rc$ channels (such as $D_1(2420)\bar{D}^{(*)}$, $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$, $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^{(*)}$, and others) and incorporates bare $\psi$ states, particle-exchange mechanisms, and short-range contact interactions to satisfy three-body unitarity. Vector charmonium poles were extracted by analytically continuing the fitted amplitudes onto unphysical sheets of open channels.

## Key numbers
- The fit achieved $\chi^2/{\rm ndf}=2320/(1635-200)\simeq 1.6$ with 200 fitting parameters.
- The analysis covered the energy range $\sqrt{s}=3.75-4.7$ GeV.
- Fourteen vector charmonium states were found in the range $3.75 < M < 4.7$ GeV with $\Gamma\equiv -2\times{\rm Im}[E_\psi] < 0.2$ GeV.
- The compositeness of $\psi(4040)$ in the $D^*\bar{D}^*$ channel is $X_{D^*\bar{D}^*}=0.86+0.22i$.
- The compositeness sum for $\psi(4040)$ is $0.86+0.19i$; for the state labeled ${}^{\rm v}D_{s}^*\bar{D}^*_s$ it is $0.83+0.12i$; for $\psi(4230)$ it is $0.65-0.13i$; for $\psi(4360)$ it is $0.61-0.27i$.
- Cusp structures appear at thresholds including $D_1(2420)\bar{D}^*$ (4431 MeV), $D_1(2420)\bar{D}$ (4289 MeV), and $\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ (4573 MeV).

## Context
This work extends prior coupled-channel analyses of vector charmonia by performing the first global fit of BESIII and Belle data over this energy range, going beyond the simple Breit-Wigner fits to inclusive $R$ values that previously determined the properties of $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, and $\psi(4415)$ (BES Collaboration, 2002). The results challenge the conventional quark-model assignment of $\psi(4040)$ as $\psi(3S)$ and provide a more complex picture of $\psi(4230)$ and $\psi(4360)$ than the simple $D_1\bar{D}$ and $D_1\bar{D}^*$ molecule proposals of Ji (2022) and Peng (2023). The analysis enables future studies of how process-dependent $Y$ lineshapes arise and provides pole positions with generally smaller uncertainties than previous single-channel experimental analyses.
