# Precision calculation of the pi^- deuteron scattering length and its impact on threshold pi-N scattering

> We present a calculation of the pi^- d scattering length with an accuracy of a few percent using chiral perturbation theory. For the first time isospin-violating corrections are included consistently. Using data on pionic deuterium and pionic hydrogen atoms, we extract the isoscalar and isovector pion-nucleon scattering lengths and obtain a^+=(7.6 +/- 3.1) x 10^{-3} mpi^{-1} and a^-=(86.1 +/- 0.9) x 10^{-3} mpi^{-1}. Via the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule, this leads to a charged-pion-nucleon coupling constant g_c^2/4 pi = 13.69 +/- 0.20.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the isoscalar and isovector pion-nucleon scattering lengths from a combined analysis of pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium data, achieving a non-zero isoscalar scattering length at better than 95% confidence level for the first time, but only after including isospin-violating corrections and three-body dynamics consistently in chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors calculate the real part of the π⁻-deuteron scattering length using chiral perturbation theory, decomposing it into two-body and three-body contributions. The three-body part includes strong contributions from multiple-scattering diagrams, photon-exchange contributions with momenta of order M_π, and dispersive corrections including Δ(1232) intermediate states. The two-body part incorporates isospin-violating shifts from the pion-nucleon sector. The calculated π⁻-deuteron scattering length is then combined with experimental data on the 1s level shift and width of pionic hydrogen and the 1s level shift of pionic deuterium via improved Deser formulas to extract the scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- π⁻-deuteron strong three-body contribution: a^str = (−22.6 ± 1.1 ± 0.4) × 10⁻³ M_π⁻¹
- Electromagnetic contribution to three-body part: a^EM = (0.94 ± 0.01) × 10⁻³ M_π⁻¹
- Dispersive plus Delta contribution: a^{disp+Δ} = (−0.6 ± 1.5) × 10⁻³ M_π⁻¹
- Pionic deuterium 1s level shift: ε_{1s}^D = (2.356 ± 0.031) eV
- Extracted isovector scattering length: a⁻ = (86.1 ± 0.9) × 10⁻³ M_π⁻¹
- Extracted isoscalar scattering length: a⁺ = (7.6 ± 3.1) × 10⁻³ M_π⁻¹
- Charged-pion-nucleon coupling constant from GMO sum rule: g_c²/4π = 13.69 ± 0.20

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pionic hydrogen by HKM (2009) to include pionic deuterium data, reducing the uncertainty on the isoscalar scattering length by more than a factor of two compared to hydrogen-only analyses. The calculation improves upon earlier work by Beane (2004) by including isospin-violating corrections and a systematic treatment of three-body dynamics, and enables a more precise determination of the pion-nucleon sigma-term and the charged-pion-nucleon coupling constant via the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule.
