# Shell Migration at N = 32, 34 around Ca Region

> The neutron numbers N = 32 and 34 are new magic numbers suggested in neutron-rich $pf$-shell nuclei. In this article, we discuss the experimental observables and state-of-the-art theoretical calculations that characterize and explain the shell evolution leading to new magic numbers. Particular focus shall be afforded to the experimental progress of the shell migration study at and beyond N = 32, 34 in Ar, K, Ca, and Sc isotopes at the RIBF using direct reactions with liquid hydrogen targets over the past ten years. The results prove the double magicity of \ts{52,54}Ca, and support the persistence of the N = 34 subshell closure below Z = 20 with a sharp weakening beyond Z = 20. Future measurements of intruder bands of N = 32, 34 nuclei and shell evolution towards N = 40 are discussed within the context of an upgraded RIBF facility and the development of novel detection systems.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental evidence that the neutron numbers N=32 and 34 act as subshell closures in neutron-rich pf-shell nuclei, but with the important qualifier that the N=34 subshell closure persists strongly only below Z=20 and weakens sharply above Z=20, while the N=32 subshell closure peaks at doubly magic ⁵²Ca and gradually vanishes for heavier isotones.

## Method

The authors review experimental results from quasi-free one-nucleon knockout reactions (p,pn) and (p,2p) performed at the RIBF facility using the MINOS liquid hydrogen target coupled with the DALI2 gamma-ray spectrometer, at beam energies around 230-250 MeV/nucleon. Measured cross sections, gamma-ray energies, and momentum distributions were compared with distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations folded with shell-model and ab initio nuclear structure calculations including GXPF1Br, GXPF1Bs, SPDF-MU, and VS-IMSRG interactions.

## Key numbers

- The first 2⁺ excitation energy of ⁵⁴Ca is 2043(19) keV, approximately 0.5 MeV lower than that of ⁵²Ca but much higher than ⁵⁶Ti
- The ⁵²Ca(p,pn) reaction at ~230 MeV/nucleon used an average beam intensity of 4.4 particles per second (pps)
- The extracted root-mean-square radii for neutron orbitals in ⁵²Ca are 4.74(18) fm for the 1p₃/₂ orbital and 4.13(14) fm for the 0f₇/₂ orbital, giving a difference of 0.61(23) fm
- The ratio of measured to calculated single-particle cross sections for ⁵²Ca(p,pn) is close to unity below the Fermi level and very small above, matching the pattern seen in ⁴⁸Ca and ⁵⁴Ca
- For ⁵²Ca(p,2p)⁵¹K, the ratio of measured to calculated cross sections is 0.75(6) for the 3/2⁺ state and 0.45(5) for the 1/2⁺ state
- The experimental momentum resolution was 49.5 MeV/c for parallel and 76.9 MeV/c for perpendicular momentum distributions

## Context

This review extends the work of Otsuka (2001) who first suggested N=34 as a new magic number from tensor-force arguments, and Steppenbeck (2013) who measured the 2⁺ energy of ⁵⁴Ca. It synthesizes results from the SEASTAR campaigns at RIBF, including the ⁵²Ca(p,pn) study by Enciu (2022) and the ⁵⁴Ca(p,pn) study by Chen (2019), which together demonstrate that the N=32 and N=34 subshell closures are as strong as the N=28 closure in ⁴⁸Ca. The work enables future studies of intruder bands in N=32,34 nuclei and shell evolution toward N=40 using the upgraded RIBF facility and new detection systems.
