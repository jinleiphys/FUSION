# Resolving the $Λ$ hypernuclear overbinding problem in pionless effective field theory

> We address the $Λ$-hypernuclear `overbinding problem' in light hypernuclei which stands for a 1--3 MeV excessive $Λ$ separation energy calculated in $_Λ^5$He. This problem arises in most few-body calculations that reproduce ground-state $Λ$ separation energies in the lighter $Λ$ hypernuclei within various hyperon-nucleon interaction models. Recent pionless effective field theory nuclear few-body calculations are extended in this work to $Λ$ hypernuclei. At leading order, the $ΛN$ low-energy constants are associated with $ΛN$ scattering lengths and the $ΛNN$ low-energy constants are fitted to $Λ$ separation energies ($B_Λ^{\rm exp}$) for $A\leq 4$. The resulting pionless-EFT interaction reproduces in few-body stochastic variational method calculations the reported value $B_Λ^{\rm exp}(_Λ^5 $He)=3.12$\pm$0.02 MeV within a fraction of MeV over a broad range of momentum-space cut-off parameters. Possible consequences and extensions to heavier hypernuclei and to neutron-star matter are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the Λ hypernuclear overbinding problem in ⁵_ΛHe (a 1–3 MeV excessive Λ separation energy) can be largely resolved at leading order in pionless effective field theory, but only when the momentum-space cut-off parameter λ is chosen above approximately 2 fm⁻¹; at lower cut-offs the overbinding persists.

## Method
The authors constructed a leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian with contact two-body and three-body interactions, regularized by a local Gaussian regulator with momentum cut-off λ. The ΛN low-energy constants were fitted to spin-singlet and spin-triplet ΛN scattering lengths from several models (Alexander, NSC97f, χEFT), and the three ΛNN low-energy constants were fitted to the experimental Λ separation energies of ³_ΛH, ⁴_ΛH_gs, and ⁴_ΛH_exc. The stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussian basis functions was used to solve the A-body Schrödinger equation for s-shell hypernuclei up to ⁵_ΛHe.

## Key numbers
- Experimental Λ separation energy of ⁵_ΛHe: B_Λ^exp(⁵_ΛHe) = 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV
- For cut-off λ = 4 fm⁻¹, calculated B_Λ(⁵_ΛHe) values: Alexander[B] = 2.59(3) MeV, NSC97f = 2.32(3) MeV, χLO = 2.99(3) MeV, χNLO = 2.40(3) MeV
- Extrapolated to λ → ∞, calculated B_Λ(⁵_ΛHe) values: Alexander[B] = 3.01(10) MeV, NSC97f = 2.74(11) MeV, χLO = 3.96(08) MeV, χNLO = 3.01(06) MeV
- Calculated B(⁴He) in the λ → ∞ limit using a_s(NN) = −18.63 fm: 29.2 ± 0.5 MeV (experimental B(⁴He) = 28.3 MeV)
- Typical momentum scale in ⁵_ΛHe: p_Λ ≈ 83 MeV/c, giving expansion parameter (Q/2m_π) ≈ 0.3 and LO accuracy ≈ 9%
- For version Alexander[B], B_Λ in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ρ_A = 0.16 fm⁻³ is ≤ 27 MeV

## Context
This work extends recent pionless effective field theory studies of light nuclei (Bazak 2016, Contessi 2017, Kirscher 2015) to the full hypernuclear s-shell, addressing the overbinding problem first identified by Dalitz, Herndon and Tang (1972). Previous few-body calculations using phenomenological ΛN+ΛNN models (AFDMC by Lonardoni 2014) or chiral EFT (Wirth 2014, Wirth 2018) overbound ⁵_ΛHe by 1–3 MeV. The present approach reproduces the experimental B_Λ(⁵_ΛHe) within a fraction of an MeV for most ΛN scattering-length versions, and the resulting repulsive ΛNN terms may have implications for the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star matter and for p-shell hypernuclear calculations.
