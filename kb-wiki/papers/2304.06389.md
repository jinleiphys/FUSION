# Kinematical higher-twist corrections in $γ^* \to M_1 M_2 γ$: Neutral meson production

> We carry out the calculation of kinematical higher-twist corrections to the cross section of $γ^* \to M_1 M_2 γ$ up to twist 4, where $M_i$ is a scalar or pseudoscalar neutral meson. The three independant helicity amplitudes are presented in terms of the twist-2 generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), which are important non-perturbative quantities for understanding the 3D structure of hadrons. Since this process can be measured by BESIII in $e^+ e^-$ collisions, we perform the numerical estimate of the kinematical higher-twist corrections by using the kinematics of BESIII. We adopt the $ππ$ GDA extracted from Belle measurements and the asymptotic $ππ$ GDA to study the size of the kinematical corrections in the case of pion meson pair, and a model $ηη$ GDA is used to see the impact of target mass corrections $\mathcal O(m^2/s)$ for $γ^* \to ηηγ$. Our results show that the kinematical higher-twist corrections account for $\sim 20\%$ of the cross sections at BESIII on the average, and it is necessary to include them if one tries to extract GDAs from experimental measurements precisely. We also comment the case of $π^0 η$ production which is important for the search of hybrid mesons.

## Key claim

The kinematical higher-twist corrections to the cross section of $\gamma^* \to M_1 M_2 \gamma$ account for approximately 20% of the cross sections at BESIII on average, and must be included for precise extraction of generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) from experimental measurements, but the exact magnitude depends on the meson mass and the invariant mass of the meson pair.

## Method

The authors calculated the kinematical higher-twist corrections to the three independent helicity amplitudes for $\gamma^* \to M_1 M_2 \gamma$ up to twist-4 accuracy using the operator product expansion of two electromagnetic currents, where the kinematical contributions are separated from dynamical ones following the formalism of Braun et al. (2011-2022). The helicity amplitudes were expressed in terms of twist-2 generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), and numerical estimates were performed using the kinematics of the BESIII experiment with $\sqrt{s}=12$ GeV$^2$, employing the $\pi\pi$ GDA extracted from Belle measurements, the asymptotic $\pi\pi$ GDA, and a model $\eta\eta$ GDA.

## Key numbers

* The kinematical higher-twist corrections account for approximately 20% of the cross sections at BESIII on average, for the kinematics studied with $s=12$ GeV$^2$ and $W$ in the range 0.5-2.0 GeV.
* For the asymptotic $\pi\pi$ GDA, the kinematical higher-twist corrections account for more than 40% of the cross section around $W\sim 2$ GeV at $s=12$ GeV$^2$.
* For the model $\eta\eta$ GDA, the kinematical higher-twist corrections account for less than 40% of the cross section around $W\sim 2$ GeV at $s=12$ GeV$^2$, with the target mass corrections of order $m^2/s$ being negative and diminishing the positive corrections of order $\hat{s}/s$.
* The center-of-mass energy used for BESIII kinematics is $\sqrt{s}=3-5$ GeV, while for Belle (Belle II) it is $\sqrt{s}=8-10$ GeV.
* The differential cross section was evaluated at $u=-3$ GeV$^2$ and $u=-6$ GeV$^2$ with $W$ ranging from 0.5 GeV to 2.1 GeV for pions and from 1.2 GeV to 2.1 GeV for $\eta$ mesons.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of kinematical higher-twist corrections, previously applied to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) by Braun et al. (2012, 2014) and to $\gamma^* \gamma \to M \bar M$ by Lorce et al. (2022), to the crossed reaction $\gamma^* \to M_1 M_2 \gamma$. The process can be measured at BESIII in $e^+ e^-$ collisions and complements the Belle measurements of $\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ (Belle, 2015) and $\gamma^* \gamma \to K \bar K$ (Belle, 2017), enabling cross-checks of GDA universality between spacelike and timelike virtual photons. The results enable more precise extraction of GDAs and, through them, the energy-momentum tensor form factors of mesons, including the shear viscosity form factor $E_q(\hat{s})$ accessible via $P$-wave GDAs in $\pi\eta$ production relevant for hybrid meson searches.
