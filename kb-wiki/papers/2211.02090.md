# Compositeness and several applications to exotic hadronic states with heavy quarks

> Several methods for studying the nature of a resonance are applied to resonances recently discovered in the bottonomium and charmonium sectors. We employ the effective-range expansion, the saturation of the width and compositeness of a resonance, as well as direct fits to data. The latter stem from generic $S$-matrix parameterization that account for relevant dynamical features associated to channels that couple strongly in an energy region around the resonance masses, in which their thresholds also lie. We report on results obtained with these methods for the resonances $Z_b(10610)$, $Z_b(10650)$, $Z_{cs}(3985)$, $Z_c(3900)$, $X(4020)$, $X(6900)$, $X(6825)$, and $P_{cs}(4459)$.

# Compositeness and several applications to exotic hadronic states with heavy quarks

## Key claim

The compositeness of several recently discovered heavy-quark exotic resonances is extracted using multiple complementary methods (effective-range expansion, width saturation, and direct S-matrix fits), with the finding that while some states like the Zb resonances show moderate compositeness (0.5-0.8), others like the X(6900) have very small compositeness (X<0.2) indicating a predominantly elementary (non-molecular) nature, and the Pcs(4459) is identified as a molecular state dominated by the Xi_c Dbar* channel.

## Method

The authors apply three methods to determine the compositeness of resonances: (1) the effective-range expansion (ERE) using the formula X = k_i/k_r from the resonance pole momentum in the second Riemann sheet; (2) saturation of the total and partial decay widths combined with compositeness relations using the formula X_i = |g_i^2 dG_i^II(s_R)/ds|; and (3) direct fits to experimental event distributions using coupled-channel S-matrix parameterizations with unitarization formulas that incorporate CDD poles and heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints.

## Key numbers

- Zb(10610): compositeness X = 0.75 ± 0.15 from ERE; X = 0.66 ± 0.11 from partial width saturation
- Zb(10650): compositeness X = 0.67 ± 0.16 from ERE; X = 0.51 ± 0.10 from partial width saturation
- Zc(3900): compositeness X = 0.45 ± 0.06 from ERE; X = 0.438 (+0.021/-0.047) from coupled-channel analysis
- X(4020): compositeness X = 0.39 ± 0.14 from ERE
- Zcs(3985): compositeness X = 0.38 ± 0.18 (using D_s- D*0 threshold) or 0.46 ± 0.19 (using D_s*- D0 threshold) from ERE
- X(6900): total compositeness X = 0.17 (+0.04/-0.07) for Fit I; X = 0.13 ± 0.03 for Fit II; mass 6907 (+5/-3) MeV (Fit I) or 6892 ± 2 MeV (Fit II)
- X(6825): pole at 6827.0 (+1.6/-4.8) - i 1.1 (+1.3/-1.0) MeV (Fit I); 6820.6 (+3.0/-2.7) - i 4.0 (+1.7/-1.6) MeV (Fit II)
- Pcs(4459): from ERE with Xi_c Dbar* threshold, scattering length a = -1.79 ± 0.23 fm, effective range r = -0.94 ± 0.13 fm; from direct fits, J=3/2 pole at 4466.6 (+1.9/-2.7) - i 1.3 (+1.3/-3.7) MeV with X_3 = 1.0 (+0.2/-0.2)

## Context

This work extends the program of applying compositeness analysis to exotic hadrons, building on the methods reviewed in the companion paper Oller (these proceedings) and prior applications to the Zb resonances by Kang (2016). The results for the charmonium sector (Zc, X(4020), Zcs) follow the unified study of Guo (2020), while the X(6900) analysis follows Guo (2020) and the Pcs(4459) study follows Du (2021). The findings for the Pcs(4459) show it shares a molecular nature analogous to its non-strange pentaquark partners Pc(4312), Pc(4380), Pc(4440), and Pc(4457) studied by Guo (2019) and Du (2019, 2021). The small compositeness of X(6900) and the presence of a CDD pole near the resonance mass are consistent with Morgan's pole-counting criterion (Morgan 1990, 1992), indicating a predominantly elementary component. The identification of X(6825) as a virtual state in the 4th Riemann sheet suggests a molecular nature for this state.
