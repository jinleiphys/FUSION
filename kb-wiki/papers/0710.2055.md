# Anatomy of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We show that, within the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) and the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) based on the Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon interaction, the competition between the pairing and the neutron-proton particle-particle and particle-hole interactions causes contributions to the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element to nearly vanish at internucleon distances of more than 2 or 3 fermis. As a result, the matrix element is more sensitive to short-range/high-momentum physics than one naively expects. We analyze various ways of treating that physics and quantify the uncertainty it produces in the matrix elements, with three different treatments of short-range correlations.

## Key claim

Within the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) and renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) based on the Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon interaction, the competition between like-particle pairing and neutron-proton particle-particle and particle-hole interactions causes contributions to the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element to nearly vanish at internucleon distances greater than 2 or 3 fm, making the matrix element more sensitive to short-range and high-momentum physics than previously expected.

## Method

The authors used the QRPA and RQRPA methods with a G matrix derived from the realistic Bonn CD nucleon-nucleon force, solving the many-body Hamiltonian with this interaction. They analyzed the matrix element by decomposing it into contributions from different angular momenta of the two decaying neutrons and from intermediate nuclear states, and examined the dependence on internucleon distance and momentum transfer. Three different treatments of short-range correlations were compared: the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), a phenomenological Jastrow function, and a microscopically-derived Jastrow function from hypernetted-chain calculations.

## Key numbers

- The integrated matrix element M^0nu is 5.35 for ^76Ge, 4.46 for ^100Mo, and 4.09 for ^130Te (without short-range correlations, with g_pp values 1.030, 1.096, and 0.994 respectively)
- The UCOM method reduces M^0nu by less than 5% (from 5.32 to 5.01 for ^76Ge)
- The phenomenological Jastrow function reduces M^0nu by about 20% (from 5.32 to 3.98 for ^76Ge)
- For ^76Ge, the range of M'^0nu with Jastrow short-range correlations is (3.33, 4.68) and with UCOM is (3.92, 5.73)
- The corresponding half-life ranges for ^76Ge with <m_betabeta> = 50 meV are (6.01, 11.9) x 10^26 years (Jastrow) and (4.01, 8.57) x 10^26 years (UCOM)

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA studies by Rodin et al. (2003, 2006) and provides a detailed analysis of the competition between pairing and neutron-proton correlations that was not sufficiently discussed before. The finding that only internucleon distances below 2-3 fm contribute explains the sensitivity to higher-order nucleon currents, form factors, and short-range repulsion. The results are compared with Large-Scale Shell Model calculations by Poves (2007) and others, showing reasonable agreement for some nuclei but discrepancies for ^76Ge and ^82Se. The paper enables more reliable estimates of the uncertainty in neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements and corresponding half-life predictions.
