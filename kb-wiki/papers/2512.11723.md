# The neutron dripline in calcium isotopes from a chiral interaction

> Interactions derived from effective field theories of quantum chromodynamics have thus far failed to bind calcium nuclei beyond neutron number $N=40$, while nuclear density functionals typically place the neutron dripline near $^{70}$Ca, at $N=50$. We present the chiral interaction N$^3$LO$_{\rm Texas}$, a combination of two- and three-nucleon potentials at fourth and third chiral order, respectively, with low-energy constants optimized using emulator-accelerated fits to few- and many-body data. This interaction accurately reproduces binding energies and charge radii of key nuclei with mass number $A=3$ to $208$, important excited states, and nuclear matter near saturation. Using ab-initio methods, we find that the calcium two-neutron dripline extends to $^{71}$Ca.

## Key claim
The new chiral interaction N³LO_Texas, optimized using emulator-accelerated fits to few- and many-body data, predicts that the calcium two-neutron dripline extends to ⁷¹Ca, resolving a long-standing tension where previous chiral interactions failed to bind calcium beyond neutron number N=40 while density functionals placed the dripline near ⁷⁰Ca.

## Method
The authors constructed a chiral interaction combining a two-nucleon potential at fourth chiral order (N³LO) and a three-nucleon potential at third chiral order (NNLO), with a cutoff of 394 MeV. They optimized 28 low-energy constants using eigenvector continuation emulators for neutron-proton scattering, deuteron, ⁴He, and ¹⁶O observables, then validated against binding energies of ²²,²⁴O and ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca. Many-body calculations for calcium and oxygen isotopes employed valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) and coupled-cluster methods with Hartree-Fock bases in spherical harmonic oscillator spaces up to e_max=14.

## Key numbers
- The calcium two-neutron dripline extends to ⁷¹Ca (neutron number N=51), a significant extension from previous ab initio limits near ⁶⁰Ca.
- The interaction uses a cutoff Λ = 394 MeV with regulator power n=4.
- Binding energies and charge radii for closed-shell nuclei from ⁴He to ²⁰⁸Pb are reproduced with deviations below 1.5%.
- For oxygen isotopes, ²⁵O and ²⁶O are unbound by approximately 830 keV and 18 keV respectively relative to ²⁴O, consistent with experimental data.
- VS-IMSRG calculations used e_max=14, E_3max=28, and ħω=12 MeV for calcium isotopes.
- The many-body uncertainty for oxygen and calcium isotopes is approximately 1%, quantified as the ground-state energy difference between IMSRG(3f2)+Triple and IMSRG(2).

## Context
This work resolves the discrepancy between ab initio chiral interaction predictions, which previously placed the calcium dripline near ⁶⁰Ca (Stroberg 2021), and nuclear density functional predictions that place it near ⁷⁰Ca (Meng 2002, Erler 2012). The results are consistent with the experimental discovery of ⁶⁰Ca (Tarasov 2018) and spectra of ⁵⁶,⁵⁸Ca and ⁶²Ti (Chen 2023, Cortes 2020). The interaction enables future Bayesian analyses incorporating effective field theory and many-body uncertainties for systematic predictions of neutron-rich nuclei and the rapid neutron-capture process.
