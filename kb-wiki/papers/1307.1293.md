# Strangeness Production in Light and Intermediate size Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Within the statistical model, the net strangeness conservation and incomplete total strangeness equilibration lead to the suppression of strange particle multiplicities. Furthermore, suppression effects appear to be stronger in small systems. By treating the production of strangeness within the canonical ensemble formulation we developed a simple model which allows to predict the excitation function of $K^+/π^+$ ratio in nucleus-nucleus collisions. In doing so we assumed that different values of $K^+/π^+$, measured in p+p and Pb+Pb interactions at the same collision energy per nucleon, are driven by the finite size effects only. These predictions may serve as a baseline for experimental results from NA61/SHINE at the CERN SPS and the future CBM experiment at FAIR.

## Key claim

The difference in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio between p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at the same collision energy per nucleon can be explained solely by finite-size effects from net strangeness conservation and incomplete total strangeness equilibration, assuming that other physical differences between the statistical systems created in these collisions are negligible.

## Method

The authors developed a statistical model within the canonical ensemble (CE) formulation that treats strangeness production with two suppression effects: net strangeness conservation (enforced by the CE) and incomplete strangeness equilibration (regulated by a parameter $\gamma_S$). They extracted the two model parameters $X = \gamma_S^p z_p$ and $Y = \gamma_S^p / \gamma_S^{Pb}$ by solving transcendental equations using experimental data on $\langle K^+\rangle_{pp}$ and the ratio $\eta_p = R_p / R_{Pb}$ as functions of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. They then used these parameters to predict the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio for light and intermediate nucleus-nucleus collisions by scaling the volume according to the number of wounded nucleons $N_W$ and assuming a functional form for $\gamma_S^A$ dependent on $N_W$ and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$.

## Key numbers

- The fit function for $\langle K^+\rangle_{pp}$ as a function of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ uses parameters $a=0.028$ and $b=0.736$ (power-law fit).
- The fit function for the p+p data used to calculate $\eta_p$ has parameters $a=-3.397$, $b=3.384$, and $c=0.009$.
- The functional form for $\gamma_S^A$ uses parameters $\alpha=1.015$ and $\beta=0.189$, obtained by fitting the $\gamma_S^p / \gamma_S^{Pb}$ ratio.
- The $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio becomes approximately independent of the number of wounded nucleons for $N_W > 40$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.6$ GeV.
- Predictions are presented for $N_W = 6$, $10$, $20$, and $40$ across SPS energies.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach to strangeness production by treating finite-size effects within the canonical ensemble, building on prior work by Rafelski (quark-gluon plasma signature), Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (the "horn" as a deconfinement signal), and Becattini (strangeness suppression factor $\gamma_S$ in different systems). The predictions serve as a baseline for the NA61/SHINE experimental program at CERN SPS, which has already recorded Be+Be data at beam momenta from 13A to 158A GeV/c and plans energy scans with p+Pb, Ar+Ca, and Xe+La collisions through 2016, as well as the future CBM experiment at FAIR.
