# Spin-dependent effective interactions for halo nuclei

> We discuss the spin-dependence of the effective two-body interactions appropriate for three-body computations. The only reasonable choice seems to be the fine and hyperfine interactions known for atomic electrons interacting with the nucleus. One exception is the nucleon-nucleon interaction imposing a different type of symmetry. We use the two-neutron halo nucleus 11Li as illustration. We demonstrate that models with the wrong spin-dependence are basically without predictive power. The Pauli forbidden core and valence states must be consistently treated.

## Key claim

The spin-dependence of effective two-body interactions in three-body halo-nucleus computations must be chosen to conserve the nucleon total angular momentum \(j_n\) as a good quantum number, consistent with the mean-field description of core nucleons; otherwise, the treatment of Pauli-forbidden states becomes inconsistent and predictive power is lost, even if ground-state properties can be fitted.

## Method

The authors compare three forms of spin-dependent nucleon-core interactions: one conserving total spin \(s\) and total angular momentum \(j\) (Eq. 2), one conserving \(\ell\) and \(j_n\) (Eq. 4), and one conserving \(j_n\) via a hyperfine-like term (Eq. 5). They solve the Faddeev equations with the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method for the two-neutron halo nucleus \(^{11}\)Li, using Gaussian radial form factors for the \(^9\)Li-neutron and neutron-neutron interactions. The Pauli principle is treated either by using shallow potentials without bound states or by constructing phase-equivalent potentials that remove the forbidden \(s_{1/2}\) and \(p_{3/2}\) states.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{11}\)Li two-neutron separation energy is set to 0.3 MeV (experimental value).
- The low-lying \(p_{1/2}\) resonances in \(^{10}\)Li are placed at 0.3 MeV and 0.5 MeV.
- A low-lying virtual \(s\)-state in \(^{10}\)Li is placed at 50 keV.
- With the interaction of Eq. (10) (conserving \(j_n\)), the computed r.m.s. radius of \(^{11}\)Li is 3.3 fm and the \(p\)-wave content is slightly above 30%.
- With the interaction of Eq. (11) (conserving total spin \(s\)), the r.m.s. radius is 3.4 fm but the \(p\)-wave content is less than 7%; after parameter adjustment the \(p\)-wave content reaches 27%.
- The \(p_{3/2}\)-occupation probability in the \(^{11}\)Li ground state is 7% with Eq. (10) and around 13% with Eq. (11).
- With Eq. (10), the \(1/2^+\) excited state of \(^{11}\)Li is a resonance at 0.4 MeV with a width of 0.3 MeV; with Eq. (11), the same state is bound by 1.3 MeV.
- Using phase-equivalent potentials with a range of 2 fm, the \(^{11}\)Li ground state has a two-neutron separation energy of 0.30 MeV, a \(p\)-wave content of around 40%, and an r.m.s. radius of 3.2 fm; the \(1/2^+\) resonance appears at about 0.6 MeV with a width of 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier three-body calculations of halo nuclei (Garrido 1997, Garrido 2002) by systematically comparing different spin-dependent forms for the nucleon-core effective interaction. It contradicts the approach of Ban (1992), Thompson (1994), and Fedorov (1995), who used the total-spin-conserving form (Eq. 2), by showing that this choice leads to uncontrolled mixing of Pauli-forbidden \(p_{3/2}\) states and produces incorrect excited-state properties. The results enable reliable predictions for halo-nucleus structure and reactions, provided the interaction conserves the nucleon total angular momentum \(j_n\) as in the hyperfine-like form (Eq. 5), which is consistent with the mean-field description of the core.
