# Error reduction technique using covariant approximation and application to nucleon form factor

> We demonstrate the new class of variance reduction techniques for hadron propagator and nucleon isovector form factor in the realistic lattice of $N_f=2+1$ domain-wall fermion. All-mode averaging (AMA) is one of the powerful tools to reduce the statistical noise effectively for wider varieties of observables compared to existing techniques such as low-mode averaging (LMA). We adopt this technique to hadron two-point functions and three-point functions, and compare with LMA and traditional source-shift method in the same ensembles. We observe AMA is much more cost effective in reducing statistical error for these observables.

# Error reduction technique using covariant approximation and application to nucleon form factor

## Key claim
All-mode averaging (AMA) reduces statistical noise for hadron two-point and three-point functions more effectively than low-mode averaging (LMA) or traditional source-shift methods, but the actual gain depends on the observable and time separation; for nucleon correlators at short time separations, LMA degrades significantly while AMA maintains error reduction close to the ideal 1/sqrt(N_G) factor.

## Method
The authors implement covariant approximation averaging (CAA) using all-mode averaging (AMA), where the approximate quark propagator is computed with a sloppy conjugate gradient (CG) solver at stopping condition epsilon_AMA = 3e-3 combined with low-mode deflation using 180-400 eigenmodes. The improved estimator averages over N_G = 32 different source locations on a 24^3 x 64 lattice with N_f = 2+1 domain-wall fermions at quark masses m = 0.005 and 0.01, and compares results to low-mode averaging (LMA) and the traditional source-shift method from Yamazaki (2009).

## Key numbers
- N_G = 32 source locations used for the averaging in both LMA and AMA estimators
- CG iteration count for AMA: 70-90 iterations at m=0.005, 90-130 iterations at m=0.01, compared to 350-630 iterations for the exact solver at epsilon = 1e-8
- Nucleon mass from AMA at m_pi = 0.33 GeV: 1.139(4) GeV, compared to 1.148(10) GeV from Yamazaki (2009)
- Computational gain factor for AMA: up to 15 times compared to traditional many-source method without deflation, and 4 times compared with deflation
- Error reduction ratio for AMA remains close to the ideal 1/sqrt(32) ~ 0.18 across all channels and distances, while LMA degrades significantly at short time separations

## Context
This work extends the covariant approximation averaging framework proposed by Blum (2012) and applies it to nucleon observables where low-mode averaging (LMA), introduced by Giusti (2002, 2004) and DeGrand (2004), had shown limited effectiveness for nucleon propagators and resonance states as reported by Giusti (2005), Li (2010), and Bali (2010). The technique enables more precise calculations of nucleon electric dipole moment and hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment without generating new gauge configurations.
