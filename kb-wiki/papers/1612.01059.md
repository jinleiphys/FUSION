# Short range correlations - The important role of few-body dynamics in many-body systems

> For many-body systems with short range interaction a series of relations were derived connecting many properties of the system to the dynamics of a closely packed few-body subsystems. Some of these relations were experimentally verified in ultra cold atomic gases. Here we shall review the implications of these developments on our understanding of nuclear one and two-body momentum distributions, and on the electron scattering Coulomb sum rule.

# Short range correlations - The important role of few-body dynamics in many-body systems

## Key claim
The Tan contact formalism, originally developed for zero-range interactions in cold atomic gases, can be generalized to nuclear systems despite the finite range of the nuclear force and the presence of multiple interaction channels (proton-proton, neutron-neutron, neutron-proton with various partial waves). The generalized contact matrices govern the asymptotic behavior of nuclear one-body and two-body momentum distributions and the Coulomb sum rule, but the asymptotic tail of nuclear momentum distributions is not expected to take on Tan's simple form of $C/k^4$.

## Method
The authors review the derivation of Tan's relations for one-body and two-body momentum distributions in single-channel systems (two-component fermions, one-component fermions, and bosons). They then generalize the contact formalism to nuclear systems by defining nuclear contact matrices $C_{ij}^{\alpha\beta}$ that account for multiple interaction channels, including different spin, angular momentum, and isospin quantum numbers for nucleon pairs ($pp$, $nn$, $np$). Using the asymptotic factorization of the many-body wave function when two nucleons approach each other, they derive relations connecting the nuclear contacts to the one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions and to the Coulomb sum rule for electron scattering.

## Key numbers
- For two-component fermions with $s$-wave zero-range interaction: $C = \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty} k^4 n(k)$, where $C$ is the contact and $n(k)$ is the momentum distribution.
- For one-component fermions with $p$-wave interaction: $C = \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty} k^2 n(k)$.
- For bosons with zero-range force, the high-momentum tail of the momentum distribution acquires a $1/k^5$ component modulated by a log-periodic function associated with the Efimov effect.
- The relation between one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions for protons: $n_p(\bs{k}) = 2F_{pp}(\bs{k}) + F_{pn}(\bs{k})$ for $k \rightarrow \infty$.
- The relation between one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions for neutrons: $n_n(\bs{k}) = 2F_{nn}(\bs{k}) + F_{pn}(\bs{k})$ for $k \rightarrow \infty$.
- The Coulomb sum rule for $q \rightarrow \infty$: $CSR = Z + \sum_{\alpha\beta} \frac{2 C^{\alpha\beta}_{pp}}{16\pi^2} h_{pp}^{\alpha\beta}(\qvec) - \rho_c^2(\qvec)$, where $h_{pp}^{\alpha\beta}$ is a universal proton-proton function.

## Context
This review extends the Tan contact formalism (Tan, 2008) from cold atomic gases to nuclear physics, building on prior work by Weiss et al. (2015) that defined neutron-proton $s$-wave nuclear contacts and related them to the Levinger photoabsorption constant (Levinger, 1951) and high-energy inclusive electron scattering (Hen et al., 2015; Ciofi degli Atti, 2016). The generalized contact matrices developed by Weiss et al. (2015) account for finite-range interactions and all partial waves, unlike earlier approaches that assumed a single contact number (Ciofi degli Atti and Simula, 1996). The derived relations between one-body and two-body momentum distributions were verified using numerical data from Wiringa (2014). The connection between the Coulomb sum rule and proton-proton contacts provides a principle way to extract these contacts from experimental electron scattering data, enabling further study of short-range correlations in nuclei.
