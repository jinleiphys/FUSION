# Excited collective states of nuclei within Bohr Hamiltonian with Tietz-Hua potential

> In this paper, we present new analytical solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian problem that we derived with the Tietz-Hua potential, here used for describing the β-part of the nuclear collective potential plus harmonic oscillator one for the γ-part. Also, we proceed to a systematic comparison of the numerical results obtained with this kind of β-potential with others which are widely used in such a framework as well as with the experiment. The calculations are carried out for energy spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities for γ-unstable and axially symmetric deformed nuclei. In the same frame, we show the effect of the shape flatness of the β-potential beyond its minimum on transition rates calculations.

## Key claim

The Tietz-Hua potential, used for the β-part of the nuclear collective potential within the Bohr Hamiltonian, yields analytical solutions for energy spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities that show excellent agreement with experimental data for γ-unstable nuclei (87% of 54 studied nuclei have σ < 1), but only moderate agreement for axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei (50% of 59 studied nuclei have σ < 1), with the discrepancy attributed to the β-band predictions.

## Method

The authors solved the Bohr Hamiltonian by separating variables using a Tietz-Hua potential for the β-vibrational part and a harmonic oscillator potential for the γ-part, applying the Nikiforov-Uvarov method to obtain closed analytical expressions for energy eigenvalues and wave functions. The model was applied to both γ-unstable nuclei (with seniority quantum number τ) and axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei (with angular momentum projection K), and the theoretical results for energy spectra and B(E2) transition probabilities were compared with experimental data and with results from Davidson, Kratzer, Morse, and Manning-Rosen potentials.

## Key numbers

- For γ-unstable nuclei: 87% of 54 studied nuclei (from ⁹⁸Ru to ²⁰⁰Pt) were reproduced with σ < 1, where σ is the root-mean-square deviation normalized to E(2₁⁺)
- For axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei: only 50% of 59 studied nuclei (from ¹⁵⁰Nd to ²⁵⁰Cf) had σ < 1
- For ¹⁶²Dy and ¹⁶⁶Er, theoretical energy spectra and B(E2) transitions showed overall agreement with experimental data (specific numerical values shown in figures)
- The Tietz-Hua potential predicted three nuclei (¹⁵⁸Gd, ¹⁷⁸Hf, ²⁵⁰Cf) satisfying the Alhassid-Whelan arc of regularity criterion |E(2γ⁺)-E(0β⁺)|/E(2γ⁺) ≤ 0.05, while not predicting ¹⁵⁸Dy and ²³⁶U which were predicted in prior work with Davidson potential

## Context

This work extends the Bohr Hamiltonian approach to collective nuclear states by introducing the Tietz-Hua potential, which was previously used in molecular physics and is considered more realistic than the Morse potential for describing molecular dynamics at moderate and high quantum numbers. The results are compared with prior models using Davidson potential (Bonatsos et al., 2011), Kratzer potential (Bonatsos et al., 2013), Morse potential (Inci et al., 2011), and Manning-Rosen potential (Chabab et al., 2016), showing that Tietz-Hua yields superior results for transition probabilities due to the flatness of its shape beyond the potential minimum. The authors suggest that the model's accuracy for axially symmetric nuclei could be improved by incorporating the deformation dependent mass formalism (DDMF), as was done with the Davidson potential by Bonatsos et al. (2010).
