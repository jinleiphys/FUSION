# Ridge from jet-medium interaction in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN} }$ = 5.02 TeV

> In this paper we report the effect of the jet-medium interplay as implemented in EPOS 3 on the ridge like structure observed in high multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 5.02 TeV. EPOS 3 takes into account hydrodynamically expanding bulk matter, jets and the jet-medium interaction. The basis of this model is multiple scatterings where each scattering finally produces flux tube / string. In the higher multiplicity event classes where the flux tube/string density is higher, there is a finite probability that the strings will pick up quarks and antiquarks (or diquarks) from the bulk (core) for flux tube breaking to produce jet hadrons (corona) instead of producing them via usual Schwinger mechanism. This will eventually create a correlation between core and corona and also influence the corona-corona correlation as the corona particles containing quarks and antiquarks (or diquarks) from the bulk also carry the fluid information. We report the relative contributions of the core-core, core-corona, corona-core and corona-corona correlations towards the ridge in the high and low multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 5.02 TeV using the data generated by EPOS 3. The multiplicity evolution of the ridges in all the cases is also reported.

## Key claim

The ridge-like structure observed in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV receives a non-zero contribution from fluid-jet interaction (core-corona and corona-core correlations) in addition to the dominant hydrodynamical core-core contribution, as implemented in the EPOS 3 model.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS 3 event generator, which implements a 3+1D event-by-event hydrodynamical model with flux tube initial conditions, to simulate p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. They performed two-particle angular correlation analyses in Δη-Δφ, separating particles into "core" (bulk hydrodynamical matter) and "corona" (jet hadrons) categories, and computed the ridge yield (|Δη| ≥ 1.2, near-side |Δφ| < π/2) for core-core, core-corona, corona-core, and corona-corona correlations across multiplicity classes.

## Key numbers

- In the 0-20% event class, approximately 68% of trigger particles (2.0 < pT < 4.0 GeV/c) originate from corona and 32% from core; in the 60-100% event class, the fractions are 77% corona and 23% core.
- The core-to-corona ratio in the associated particle pT region (1.0 < pT < 4.0 GeV/c) changes from 37% core : 63% corona in the 60-100% event class to 56% core : 44% corona in the 0-20% event class.
- The analysis uses trigger particles with 2.0 < pT < 4.0 GeV/c and associated particles with 1.0 < pT < 4.0 GeV/c, with pseudorapidity restricted to -0.8 < η < 0.8.
- Multiplicity classes are defined based on charged particle yield with pT > 0.05 GeV/c within 2.8 < η < 5.1.

## Context

This work extends previous EPOS 3 studies of the ridge in pp collisions (Werner et al., various years) and p-Pb collisions (EPOS mass ordering and flow papers) by explicitly separating the contributions from hydrodynamical evolution and fluid-jet interaction. It builds on the core-corona separation formalism and the fluid-jet interaction mechanism previously applied to Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (EPOS PbPb lambda/K-short enhancement paper). The results enable future studies with identified particles to understand the chemical composition of the ridge in small collision systems at LHC energies.
