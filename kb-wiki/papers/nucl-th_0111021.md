# Flavour asymmetry of anti-quarks in nuclei

> A novel nuclear effect on the flavour asymmetry of anti-quarks in the nucleon bound in a nucleus is discussed in terms of the meson cloud model and the Pauli exclusion principle. It is expected that the flavour asymmetry is enhanced in a nucleus.

# Flavour asymmetry of anti-quarks in nuclei

## Key claim
The flavour asymmetry of anti-quarks (excess of \(\bar{d}\) over \(\bar{u}\)) is expected to be enhanced in a bound proton inside a nucleus compared to a free proton, due to Pauli blocking at the hadron level affecting virtual pion emission. This enhancement is conditional on the nucleus having unequal numbers of valence protons and neutrons; if the numbers are equal, the asymmetry vanishes.

## Method
The author uses the meson cloud model, where the physical nucleon contains virtual meson-baryon Fock components (primarily \(\pi^+\)-\(n\) and \(\pi^-\)-\(p\)), and applies the Pauli exclusion principle at the hadron level to a pair of mirror nuclei \((A, A')\) with opposite proton-neutron excess. For a proton-rich nucleus \(A\), the virtual emission of \(\pi^-\) from a neutron is suppressed because the final-state proton is Pauli-blocked, while \(\pi^+\) emission from a proton is less suppressed; the opposite holds for a neutron-rich nucleus \(A'\). The flavour asymmetry is extracted from the nonsinglet difference of structure functions \(F_2^A(x) - F_2^{A'}(x)\).

## Key numbers
- The admixture of the \(\pi\)-\(N\) component in the physical proton is about 20%, while \(\rho\)-\(N\) and \(\pi\)-\(\Delta\) components are about 10% and 5%, respectively.
- For the \(A=3\) mirror pair \(^3\)He-\(^3\)H, the change of pion fields predicts a reduction of the nuclear Gottfried sum rule by about 10% compared to the free-space value.
- In a two-proton 6-quark state (exotic configuration), the naive counting estimate gives \(\bar{d}/\bar{u} \sim 2\).
- For the free proton, the naive counting estimate from Pauli blocking at quark level gives \(\bar{d}/\bar{u} \sim 5/4\).

## Context
This work extends the meson cloud model explanation of the flavour asymmetry in the free proton, which was established by measurements from the New Muon Collaboration (NMC, 1991), NA51 (1994), E866/NuSea (1998), and HERMES (1998). The author proposes that measuring the nonsinglet structure function difference of mirror nuclei (e.g., \(^3\)He-\(^3\)H, and potentially \(^8\)B-\(^8\)Li or \(^{17}\)Ne-\(^{17}\)N for halo nuclei) can reveal this novel nuclear enhancement. The effect is distinct from the Pauli blocking at quark level discussed by Field and Feynman (1977) and others, and could be tested with future collider experiments on unstable nuclei, as suggested by the MUSES project and Tanihata (1996).
