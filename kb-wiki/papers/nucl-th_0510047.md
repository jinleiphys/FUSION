# The triton and three-nucleon force in nuclear lattice simulations

> We study the triton and three-nucleon force at lowest chiral order in pionless effective field theory both in the Hamiltonian and Euclidean nuclear lattice formalism. In the case of the Euclidean lattice formalism, we derive the exact few-body worldline amplitudes corresponding to the standard many-body lattice action. This will be useful for setting low-energy coefficients in future nuclear lattice simulations. We work in the Wigner SU(4)-symmetric limit where the S-wave scattering lengths {1}S{0} and {3}S{1} are equal. By comparing with continuum results, we demonstrate for the first time that the nuclear lattice formalism can be used to study few-body nucleon systems.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that the nuclear lattice formalism can be used to study few-body nucleon systems, but only in the Wigner SU(4)-symmetric limit where the S-wave scattering lengths in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels are equal, and only at lowest chiral order in pionless effective field theory.

## Method

The authors study the triton and three-nucleon force using both a Hamiltonian lattice formalism with the Lanczos method for exact diagonalization and a Euclidean lattice formalism with Monte Carlo sampling of fermion worldlines. In the Euclidean approach, they derive exact few-body worldline amplitudes corresponding to the standard many-body lattice action by constructing the transition operator matrix elements in the occupation number basis and using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to handle two-body interactions. The two-nucleon binding energy is set to 1 MeV in the SU(4)-symmetric limit.

## Key numbers

- Two-nucleon binding energy is set to B2 = 1 MeV in the SU(4)-symmetric limit
- Lattice spacings used in the Hamiltonian formalism: a^{-1} = 30, 40, 50 MeV
- Lattice lengths used: L = 4, 5, 6, 7 (in lattice units)
- The three-body coupling D0 scales roughly as Lambda^{-2} for triton energies much less than -1 MeV
- The pole in D0 as a function of triton energy occurs close to the -1 MeV continuum threshold for a dimer plus nucleon

## Context

This work extends the nuclear lattice methods previously applied to neutron matter by Lee and collaborators (2004-2005) to include protons and three-nucleon systems. It provides the first measurement of the three-nucleon force on the lattice in the Wigner symmetry limit, which enables future many-body nuclear simulations with arbitrary numbers of neutrons and protons including three-body effects. The continuum regularization results from Bedaque and collaborators (1998-1999) serve as a reference for comparison with the lattice calculations.
