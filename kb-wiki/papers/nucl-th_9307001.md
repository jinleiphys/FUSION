# A Full $PF$ Shell Model Study of a~=~48 Nuclei

> Exact diagonalizations with a minimally modified realistic force lead to detailed agreement with measured level schemes and electromagnetic transitions in $^{48}$Ca, $^{48}$Sc, $^{48}$Ti, $^{48}$V, $^{48}$Cr and $^{48}$Mn. Gamow-Teller strength functions are systematically calculated and reproduce the data to within the standard quenching factor. Their fine structure indicates that fragmentation makes much strength unobservable. As a by-product, the calculations suggest a microscopic description of the onset of rotational motion. The spectroscopic quality of the results provides strong arguments in favour of the general validity of monopole corrected realistic forces, which is discussed.

## Key claim

Exact diagonalizations in the full $pf$ shell with the minimally modified realistic interaction KB3 yield detailed agreement with measured level schemes and electromagnetic transitions for A=48 nuclei, but the agreement is conditional on adopting the standard Gamow-Teller quenching factor of $(0.77)^2$ and on the claim that fragmentation makes much of the calculated strength unobservable in experiment.

## Method

The authors performed full $pf$ shell model diagonalizations using the Lanczos algorithm implemented in the code ANTOINE, working in the $m$-scheme with dimensions up to 1,963,461 for $^{48}$Cr. They used the KB3 interaction, a minimally modified realistic force derived from the Kuo-Brown interaction with centroid corrections, and calculated energy levels, electromagnetic transitions, and Gamow-Teller strength functions for six A=48 nuclei ($^{48}$Ca, $^{48}$Sc, $^{48}$Ti, $^{48}$V, $^{48}$Cr, $^{48}$Mn).

## Key numbers

- $^{48}$Ti: rms deviation for 33 excited states is 110 keV
- $^{48}$Ti: calculated $B(E2,2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 92.1$ e$^2$ fm$^4$ (experimental: $142 \pm 6$ e$^2$ fm$^4$)
- $^{48}$Ti: calculated $B(M1,0^+_1 \rightarrow 1^+_1) = 0.54$ $\mu_N^2$ (experimental: $0.50 \pm 0.08$ $\mu_N^2$)
- $^{48}$Ca: total observed $M1$ strength between 7.5 and 12.5 MeV is $5.2 \pm 0.5$ $\mu_N^2$, with observed-to-calculated ratio $5.2/8.96 = (0.76)^2$
- $^{48}$Ca: calculated $B(E2,2^+ \rightarrow 0^+) = 10.2$ e$^2$ fm$^4$ (experimental: $17.2$ e$^2$ fm$^4$)
- $^{48}$Ca $2\nu$ double beta decay half-life corrected to $T_{1/2} = 3.7 \times 10^{19}$ yr
- $^{48}$V: calculated ground state magnetic moment $\mu(4^+) = 1.90$ $\mu_N$ (experimental: $2.01(1)$ $\mu_N$)
- $^{48}$V: calculated half-life $T_{1/2} = 8.85$ d (experimental: $15.97$ d)
- $^{48}$Cr: calculated half-life $T_{1/2} = 21.8$ h (experimental: $21.56(3)$ h)
- $^{48}$Cr: calculated $B(E2,2^+ \rightarrow 0^+) = 228$ e$^2$ fm$^4$ (experimental: $321 \pm 41$ e$^2$ fm$^4$)
- $^{48}$Mn: full calculation total half-life 142 ms (experimental: $158(22)$ ms)
- $^{48}$Mn: full calculation partial GT half-life 340 ms (experimental: $372(20)$ ms)

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of $pf$ shell nuclei using exact diagonalizations, building on earlier truncation studies by Pasquini and Zuker (1980s) and the MBZ model of McCullen, Bayman and Zamick. The calculations demonstrate that monopole-corrected realistic forces (KB3) are valid in general, a fact easier to detect in the $pf$ shell than in the $sd$ shell where phenomenologically fitted matrix elements had been necessary. The results enable future studies of rotational motion in heavier nuclei, particularly in regions around N=60, 90, and 136 where Nilsson diagrams predict deformation onset, and suggest that $t=3$ truncations are acceptable for calculations up to A=58.
