# Collisional Energy Loss of Non Asymptotic Jets in a QGP

> We calculate the collisional energy loss suffered by a heavy (charm) quark created at a finite time within a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) in the classical linear response formalism as in Peigne {\it et al.} \cite{peigne}. We pay close attention to the problem of formulating a suitable current and the isolation of binding and radiative energy loss effects. We find that unrealistic large binding effects arising in previous formulations must be subtracted. The finite time correction is shown to be important only for very short length scales on the order of a Debye length. The overall energy loss is similar in magnitude to the energy loss suffered by a charge created in the asymptotic past. This result has significant implications for the relative contribution to energy loss from collisional and radiative sources and has important ramifications for the ``single electron puzzle'' at RHIC.

## Key claim

The collisional energy loss of a heavy quark created at a finite time in a QGP is similar in magnitude to the energy loss of a charge created in the asymptotic past, contradicting earlier claims of a large suppression. The finite time correction is important only for very short path lengths on the order of a Debye length, and the previously reported suppression arose from unrealistic binding effects that must be subtracted.

## Method

The authors calculate collisional energy loss using the classical linear response formalism of Peigne et al., modeling the heavy (charm) quark as a classical color current created at finite time within a Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed medium. They formulate a conserved two-particle current to satisfy charge conservation, then carefully separate the induced field contributions into self-interaction terms (the physical energy loss) and shared binding terms (which must be subtracted). The energy loss is computed by integrating the work done by the induced electric field on the moving quark, with the vacuum field subtracted to isolate medium effects.

## Key numbers

- Debye screening mass: \( m_D^2 = 4\pi \alpha_s T^2 (1 + N_F/6) \), with \( N_F = 0 \) (pure gluonic plasma) and \( \alpha_s = 0.3 \)
- Charm quark mass: \( M = 1.2 \) GeV used for all numerical calculations
- Medium temperature: \( T = 250 \) MeV used for typical medium particle momentum \( q \sim T \)
- Path length: \( L = 5 \) fm used for energy loss fraction calculations
- Maximum momentum transfer: \( k_{\text{max}} = \min\left\{ E, \frac{2 q (E+p)}{\sqrt{M^2 + 2 q (E+p)}} \right\} \), where \( E \) is the incident particle energy, \( p \) its momentum, and \( q \) the typical medium particle momentum

## Context

This work extends the finite-time collisional energy loss calculation of Peigne et al. (2005) by identifying and removing unrealistic binding effects that caused an artificial suppression. It reconciles the linear response approach with the quantum mechanical formalism of Djordjevic (2005), finding consistent results. The finding that collisional energy loss is comparable to radiative loss supports the approach of Wicks et al. (2005), who achieved better agreement with RHIC single non-photonic electron data by including both elastic and radiative energy loss with geometric and gluon number fluctuations.
