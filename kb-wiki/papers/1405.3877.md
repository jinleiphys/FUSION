# Multipole solution of hydrodynamics and higher order harmonics

> The time evolution of the medium created in heavy ion collisions can be described by hydrodynamical models. After expansion and cooling, the hadrons are created in a freeze-out. Their distribution describes the final state of this medium. In particular their azimuthal asymmetry, characterized by the elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$, is one of the most important observables in heavy ion physics. In recent years it has been revealed that if measuring relative to higher order event planes $Ψ_n$, higher order flow coefficients $v_n$ for $n>2$ can be measured. This is due to initial state fluctuations, previously not described by analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics. In this paper we show the first solutions that utilize higher order asymmetries and thus yield realistic $v_n$ flow coefficients. It is a clear consequence of this that different flow patterns may lead to the same observed flow coefficients. We also compare our results to PHENIX measurements and determine a possible parameter set corresponding to these data.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first exact analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics that incorporate higher-order azimuthal asymmetries (beyond elliptic flow), enabling the calculation of realistic \(v_n\) flow coefficients for \(n>2\). However, the solutions are restricted to a special class of initial conditions defined by a Hubble-flow velocity profile and a specific form of the scale variable, and the authors explicitly note that different flow patterns can lead to the same observed flow coefficients.

## Method
The authors extend a known (1+3)-dimensional ellipsoidal hydrodynamic solution by generalizing the scale variable \(s\) to include higher-order multipole symmetries of order \(N\) (e.g., \(s = r^N/R^N (1 + \epsilon \cos(N\phi))\) in 1+2 dimensions). They assume a perfect fluid with a constant speed of sound (constant \(\kappa\)), a Hubble-flow velocity profile \(u^\mu = x^\mu/\tau\), and an exponential scale function \(\nu(s) = e^{-bs}\). Observables (transverse momentum spectra and flow coefficients \(v_n\)) are computed via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at a fixed proper time \(\tau_f\), and the model is fitted to PHENIX 200 GeV Au+Au data for \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) across five centrality bins.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out proper time \(\tau_f = 7.7\) fm/\(c\) (fixed from prior work).
- Central freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 200\) MeV (fixed from prior work).
- Transverse expansion velocity \(u_t\) fitted values range from \(740 \pm 3\) permil (0-10% centrality) to \(774 \pm 3\) permil (40-50% centrality).
- Elliptic eccentricity \(\epsilon_2\) fitted values range from \(175 \pm 2\) permil (0-10%) to \(621 \pm 6\) permil (40-50%).
- Triangular eccentricity \(\epsilon_3\) fitted values range from \(99 \pm 2\) permil (0-10%) to \(182 \pm 4\) permil (40-50%).
- Quadrupole eccentricity \(\epsilon_4\) fitted values range from \(44 \pm 2\) permil (0-10%) to \(125 \pm 12\) permil (40-50%).
- Temperature gradient parameter \(b\) is constrained to the interval \(0.05 - 0.2\) (confidence interval, not a single value).
- Fits are restricted to \(p_t < 2\) GeV, above which non-hydrodynamic effects become important.

## Context
This work extends prior exact analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics, particularly the ellipsoidal solution of Csorgo et al. (2003) which only described elliptic flow (\(v_2\)). It provides the first analytic framework capable of describing higher-order harmonics (\(v_3\), \(v_4\)) that arise from event-by-event initial-state fluctuations, previously only accessible via numerical hydrodynamic calculations (e.g., Petersen et al. 2010, Schenke et al. 2010). The results enable analytic extraction of average triangular and quadrupole anisotropies from data, though the authors note that the parameter extraction is ambiguous due to correlations between \(b\) and \(u_t\), and that more observables are needed to resolve this degeneracy.
