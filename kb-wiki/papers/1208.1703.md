# Dynamic Microscopic Theory of Fusion Using DC-TDHF

> The density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) theory is a fully microscopic approach for calculating heavy-ion interaction potentials and fusion cross sections below and above the fusion barrier. We discuss recent applications of DC-TDHF method to fusion of light and heavy systems.

# Dynamic Microscopic Theory of Fusion Using DC-TDHF

## Key claim
The density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method provides a fully microscopic approach for calculating heavy-ion interaction potentials and fusion cross sections both below and above the fusion barrier, but the calculated cross sections for the 40Ca+48Ca system at higher energies are larger than experimental values by about a factor of two, indicating that the method works best at sub-barrier energies where dynamical rearrangements are most complete.

## Method
The DC-TDHF approach performs unrestricted TDHF time-evolution and, at selected time steps (every 20 steps out of a few thousand total), uses the instantaneous TDHF density to perform a static Hartree-Fock minimization with the neutron and proton densities constrained to match the TDHF densities. The resulting density-constrained energy is renormalized by subtracting the binding energies of the two individual nuclei to obtain the ion-ion interaction potential. Fusion cross sections are then calculated by numerically integrating the Schrodinger equation using the Incoming Wave Boundary Condition method, with a coordinate-dependent mass parameter derived from TDHF dynamics via energy conservation at zero impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- The DC-TDHF potential barrier for 12C+16O has a peak energy of 7.77 MeV.
- The barrier for 12C+24O has a peak energy of 6.64 MeV and occurs at a slightly larger internuclear distance R.
- The calculated cross sections for 40Ca+48Ca at higher energies are larger than experimental values by about a factor of two.
- The density constraint is applied every 20 time steps during a typical DC-TDHF run utilizing a few thousand time steps.

## Context
This work extends the DC-TDHF method developed by Umar and Oberacker (2012) to calculate fusion barriers in the mean-field limit, applying it to light systems (carbon-oxygen isotopes) and medium-mass systems (calcium isotopes). The results for 40Ca+48Ca address a long-standing problem where standard models failed to reproduce sub-barrier cross sections, as noted in experimental studies by Montagnoli (2011). The method enables fully microscopic predictions for fusion of neutron-rich nuclei relevant to neutron star crust composition and heating, and points to the need for improved Skyrme force parametrizations that incorporate deformation and reaction data.
