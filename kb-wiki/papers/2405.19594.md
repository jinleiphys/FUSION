# Factorized Approximation to the IMSRG(3)

> We describe an approximation to the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) method in which we include the effects of intermediate three-body operators arising within nested commutators. As an initial step, we present the relevant equations for two nested commutators, all of which can be factorized so that the method scales like the standard IMSRG(2) approximation, enabling large-scale calculations. We test the accuracy of this approximation scheme, and apply it to the isotopic chains of carbon, sulfur and nickel isotopic chains. We obtain an improved description of spectroscopy, and a reduced dependence on the choice of the valence space. In addition, we provide an explanation of the relative importance of the diagram topologies included, with an eye toward assessing the impact of remaining omitted terms.

## Key claim

The paper presents a factorized approximation to the IMSRG(3) method, called IMSRG(3f₂), which includes effects of intermediate three-body operators arising within nested commutators while scaling like the standard IMSRG(2) approximation; the authors hedge that this is an initial step treating only two nested commutators, and that not all diagrams in this scheme can be factorized to scale as N⁶.

## Method

The authors modify the Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff nested commutator expansion by adding a correction term Δ that captures the effect of induced three-body operators contracting back down to one- and two-body operators in double commutators. They factorize the resulting expressions using intermediate one-body objects (e.g., χᵅ) to reduce the computational scaling from N⁷ to N⁶, comparable to IMSRG(2). The approximation is implemented within the Magnus formulation of the IMSRG, using a hunter-gatherer scheme to split the unitary transformation operator.

## Key numbers

- IMSRG(3f₂) required 15 seconds of CPU time for a valence-space IMSRG decoupling of ¹²C within e_max=4, compared to 12 seconds for IMSRG(2), 8.5 hours for IMSRG(3N7), and 94 hours for full IMSRG(3)
- Including factorized commutator terms at s=∞ shifted the ¹²C ground state energy by +5.5 MeV, while perturbative triples contributed -2.5 MeV
- The IMSRG(3N7) approximation added an additional -2.06 MeV to the ¹²C ground state energy, of which -1.47 MeV was captured by the perturbative triples correction
- The factorized double commutator expressions reduce scaling from N⁷ to N⁴+N⁵ for the one-body part and to N⁶ overall

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG framework beyond the standard IMSRG(2) approximation (Hergert 2016) toward a full three-body treatment (IMSRG(3)), which was previously explored in small spaces by Heinz (2021) but was computationally infeasible for large-scale calculations. The factorized approach is similar to the shell model coupled cluster (SMCC) method (Sun 2018), but produces Hermitian interactions suitable for direct use with standard shell model codes. The approximation enables large-scale calculations of carbon, sulfur, and nickel isotopic chains with improved spectroscopy and reduced dependence on valence-space choice, while the analysis of diagram topologies provides guidance for assessing which omitted terms may be most important in future extensions.
