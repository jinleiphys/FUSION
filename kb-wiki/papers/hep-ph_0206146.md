# A renormalisation group approach to two-body scattering in the presence of long-range forces

> We apply renormalisation-group methods to two-body scattering by a combination of known long-range and unknown short-range potentials. We impose a cut-off in the basis of distorted waves of the long-range potential and identify possible fixed points of the short-range potential as this cut-off is lowered to zero. The expansions around these fixed points define the power countings for the corresponding effective field theories. Expansions around nontrivial fixed points are shown to correspond to distorted-wave versions of the effective-range expansion. These methods are applied to scattering in the presence of Coulomb, Yukawa and repulsive inverse-square potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that renormalisation-group methods applied to two-body scattering with a combination of known long-range and unknown short-range potentials can identify fixed points of the short-range interaction, and expansions around nontrivial fixed points correspond to distorted-wave versions of the effective-range expansion, provided the long-range potential can be rescaled to become scale independent.

## Method

The authors impose a sharp momentum cutoff on the basis of distorted waves of the long-range potential and derive a Wilsonian renormalisation-group equation for the short-range potential by demanding that the off-shell K-matrix be independent of the cutoff. They identify fixed points of the short-range potential as the cutoff is lowered to zero and linearise the RG equation around these fixed points to determine the scaling behaviour of perturbations, which defines the power counting for the corresponding effective field theory. For potentials more singular than r^{-1} at the origin, they introduce a delta-shell regulator as a factorisation scale.

## Key numbers

- For the trivial fixed point (no scattering), RG eigenvalues are ν = 2(l+m+n)+1, where l, m, n are non-negative integers, leading to Weinberg power counting with order d = ν-1.
- For the nontrivial (effective-range) fixed point, energy-dependent perturbations have RG eigenvalues ν = -1, 1, 3, ..., with the negative eigenvalue indicating an unstable fixed point; the corresponding power counting assigns orders d = ν-1 = -2, 0, 2, ...
- The first two coefficients in the expansion around the nontrivial fixed point relate to the effective-range expansion as C_{-1} = -π/(2a) and C_1 = π r_e/4, where a is the scattering length and r_e is the effective range.
- The critical scale at which the unstable perturbation takes over is the inverse of the scattering length, 1/a.

## Context

This work extends the RG treatment of two-body scattering by short-range forces from Birse et al. (1999) to include known long-range potentials such as Coulomb, Yukawa, and inverse-square forces. It provides a systematic framework for determining power counting in effective field theories for nucleon-nucleon scattering, addressing the schemes of Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998) and Weinberg/van Kolck (1992, 1999). The approach enables a distorted-wave effective-range expansion and can be applied to three-body systems where Efimov (1970) showed inverse-square potentials are relevant.
