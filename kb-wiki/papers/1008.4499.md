# The triton in a finite volume

> Understanding the volume dependence of the triton binding energy is an important step towards lattice simulations of light nuclei. We calculate the triton binding energy in a finite cubic box with periodic boundary conditions to leading order in the pionless effective field theory. Higher order corrections are estimated and the proper renormalization of our results is verified explicitly. We present results for the physical triton as well as for the pion-mass dependence of the triton spectrum near the ``critical'' pion mass, Mpi_c ~ 197 MeV, where chiral effective field theory suggests that the nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths in the singlet- and triplet-channels diverge simultaneously. An extension of the Luescher formula to the three-body system is implicit in our results.

## Key claim
The triton binding energy in a finite cubic volume is calculated to leading order in pionless effective field theory, with the claim that proper renormalization is verified explicitly and that an extension of the Luscher formula to the three-body system is implicit in the results. The hedge is that higher-order corrections from effective ranges are estimated to be of order 30-40% and that contributions from higher partial waves are neglected in this first study.

## Method
The authors place the three-nucleon system in a finite cubic box with periodic boundary conditions and periodic boundary conditions, using the pionless effective field theory Lagrangian with auxiliary dinucleon fields and an SU(4)-symmetric three-body force. They derive coupled integral equations for the triton amplitude in the spin-1/2 channel, project onto the G1+ representation of the cubic group, and solve numerically for the bound state energies while neglecting partial waves above J=1/2. The three-body coupling constant H(Lambda) is fixed by reproducing the physical triton binding energy in infinite volume, and renormalization is verified by comparing results from two different cutoff schemes.

## Key numbers
- Physical triton binding energy in infinite volume: 8.48 MeV (from the horizontal dashed line in Fig. 1, implied by the text)
- Critical pion mass: M_pi^crit ≈ 197 MeV, where nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths in the singlet and triplet channels diverge simultaneously
- For the physical triton, the volume dependence fit gives L0 = 2.9 fm and c = 39 fm in the function E3(L) = E3(∞)[1 + (c/L) exp(-L/L0)]
- Slopes of triton energy vs. pion mass near critical pion mass: ΔE3/ΔM_pi = 0.0783 (infinite volume), 0.0467 (L=4.925 fm), 0.064 (L=3.94 fm), and 0.08 (L=2.955 fm)
- Edge lengths studied range from 17 fm down to 2 fm for the physical triton
- For volumes near L=6 fm, the finite volume shift exceeds 100% of the infinite volume binding energy

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of three-boson bound states in finite volumes (Kreuzer 2008, 2009) to the three-nucleon system, and provides the finite-volume framework needed for lattice QCD simulations of light nuclei. The results are compared to a nuclear lattice calculation using chiral EFT at N2LO by Epelbaum and collaborators (2009), with agreement for L ≥ 10 fm but discrepancies at smaller volumes attributed to neglected higher partial waves. The work enables extraction of infinite-volume three-body observables from lattice data and provides the foundation for studying the conjectured Efimov limit cycle near the critical pion mass, though the authors find that present-day volumes are too small to observe the predicted excited triton states as bound states.
