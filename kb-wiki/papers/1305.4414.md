# On Collective Properties of Turbulent QED Plasma

> Polarization properties of turbulent stochastically inhomogeneous ultrarelativistic QED plasma are studied. It is shown that the sign of nonlinear turbulent Landau damping corresponds to an instability of the spacelike modes and, for sufficiently large turbulent fields, to an actual instability of a system. Modification of plasmon dispersion relations due to turbulent effects are studied.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the sign of nonlinear turbulent Landau damping in ultrarelativistic QED plasma corresponds to an instability of spacelike modes, and for sufficiently large turbulent fields this leads to an actual instability of the system; the effect depends on the synthetic correlation scale \(l = \sqrt{2} \tau a / \sqrt{\tau^2 + a^2}\) and on the strengths of the turbulent electric and magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors model turbulent QED plasma as a perturbation of equilibrium ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasma by weak stochastic Gaussian turbulent fields characterized by a two-point correlator with temporal correlation length \(\tau\) and spatial correlation length \(a\). They solve the Vlasov kinetic equation coupled to Maxwell's equations in the linear response approximation, expanding the distribution function to first order in the regular field and second order in the turbulent field, then average over the turbulent ensemble. The resulting turbulent contributions to the polarization tensor are computed in a gradient expansion in the small parameter \(|\mathbf{k}| l\), yielding explicit analytic expressions for the longitudinal and transverse components at first and second order.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass: \(m_D^2 = e^2 T^2 / 3\).
- Plasma frequency: \(\omega_{\mathrm{pl}}^2 = m_D^2 / 3\).
- For purely magnetic turbulent fields, the instability criterion is \(\frac{4}{\pi^2 \sqrt{\pi}} \frac{|\mathbf{k}| l}{\mathbf{k}^2} \frac{e^2 \langle B^2 \rangle}{T^2} \Phi(x) > 1\), where \(\Phi(x)\) is defined in the text and \(x = \omega / |\mathbf{k}|\).
- For mixed instability with \(\langle B^2 \rangle = \langle E^2 \rangle\), the criterion is \(\frac{4}{\pi^2 \sqrt{\pi}} \frac{|\mathbf{k}| l}{\mathbf{k}^2} \frac{e^2 \langle E^2 \rangle}{T^2} \Theta(x) > 1\), where \(\Theta(x)\) is defined in the text.
- The critical value of \(x\) for sign change of the magnetic contribution to the transverse imaginary part is \(x^* \sim 0.43\).
- Turbulent modifications to the plasmon dispersion relations in the deeply timelike regime: \((\omega^{\mathrm{turb}}_{\mathrm{pl}\;L})^2 = \omega_{\mathrm{pl}}^2 - \frac{e^4 l^2}{6 \pi^2} \left( \frac{16}{3} \langle E^2 \rangle + \frac{8}{3} \langle B^2 \rangle \right)\); \((\omega^{\mathrm{turb}}_{\mathrm{pl}\;T})^2 = \omega_{\mathrm{pl}}^2 - \frac{e^4 l^2}{6 \pi^2} \left( \frac{128}{15} \langle E^2 \rangle + \frac{8}{3} \langle B^2 \rangle \right)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of turbulent QED plasma initiated by Tsytovich (1970s) and Ichimaru (1991), and builds on the formalism for turbulent contributions to shear viscosity developed by Asakawa, Bass, and Muller (2006, 2007, 2011). The results are relevant to understanding instabilities and collective modes in the early stages of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, where turbulent glasma states with Kolmogorov spectra have been studied by Fukushima and Gelis (2012) and others. The paper provides the Abelian foundation for a forthcoming non-Abelian generalization (Kirakosyan, Leonidov, Muller 2013) and enables future studies of turbulent effects on transport coefficients and plasmon properties in QCD plasma.
