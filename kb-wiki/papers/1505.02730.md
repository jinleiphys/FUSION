# Regularity and chaos in 0+ states of the interacting boson model using quantum measures

> Statistical measures of chaos have long been used in the study of chaotic dynamics in the framework of the interacting boson model. The use of large number of bosons renders additional studies of chaos possible, that can provide a direct comparison with similar classical studies of chaos. We intend to provide complete quantum chaotic dynamics at zero angular momentum in the vicinity of the arc of regularity and link the results of the study of chaos using statistical measures with those of the study of chaos using classical measures. Statistical measures of chaos are applied on the spectrum and the transition intensities of 0+ states in the framework of the interacting boson model. The energy dependence of chaos is provided for the first time using statistical measures of chaos. The position of the arc of regularity was also found to be stable in the limit of large boson numbers. The results of the study of chaos using statistical measures are consistent with previous studies using classical measures of chaos, as well as with studies using statistical measures of chaos, but for small number of bosons and states with angular momentum greater than 2.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first energy-dependent study of quantum chaos in 0+ states of the interacting boson model using statistical measures, enabled by the large boson numbers (up to N_B=175) handled by the new IBAR code. The authors find that the Alhassid-Whelan arc of regularity remains stable for large boson numbers, but note that for N_B=25 (close to the boson numbers of actual nuclei near the arc, around N_B=14) the arc location may differ from the large-N_B limit.

## Method

Statistical measures of chaos were applied to the spectrum and B(E0) transition intensities of 0+ states in the interacting boson model (IBM) using the Hamiltonian H(η,χ)=c[η n_d + ((η-1)/N_B) Q_χ·Q_χ]. The nearest neighbor level spacing distribution P(S) (fitted to the Brody distribution with parameter ω), the Δ_3 spectral rigidity statistic of Dyson and Mehta (fitted with parameter q), and the P(y) distribution of transition intensities (fitted with parameter ν) were calculated for points in the IBM symmetry triangle, using the IBAR code with up to N_B=175 bosons for the spectral measures and N_B=50 bosons for the transition intensities.

## Key numbers

- For N_B=175, 2640 0+ states were produced for the P(S) and Δ_3(L) fluctuation measures
- For N_B=50, 234 0+ states (producing 54,756 possible B(E0) transition strengths) were used for the P(y) fluctuation measure
- The point on the arc of regularity had parameters (η, χ) = (0.632, -0.803), with the arc given by χ(η) = ((√7-1)/2)η - (√7/2)
- For the total spectrum at N_B=175, the Brody parameter ω values were: SU(3) vertex ω=0.17, arc ω=0.18, χ=-0.7 point ω=0.34, χ=-1.1 point ω=0.53
- For the total spectrum at N_B=175, the Δ_3 parameter q values were: SU(3) vertex q=0.39, arc q=0.44, χ=-0.7 point q=0.63, χ=-1.1 point q=0.76
- For the total spectrum at N_B=50, the P(y) parameter ν values were: arc ν=0.34, χ=-0.7 point ν=0.42, χ=-1.1 point ν=0.49
- For the arc point at N_B=25, the ω value was greater than at neighboring points (χ=-0.7 and χ=-1.1), contrary to the behavior for N_B≥50

## Context

This work extends the classical and quantum chaos studies of Alhassid and Whelan (using N_B=25 and J≥2) by applying statistical measures to 0+ states with much larger boson numbers, enabled by the IBAR code. The results are consistent with previous classical studies using Poincaré sections by Macek et al. (2010) and with quantum studies using Peres's visual method by Macek et al. (2011, 2013). The energy dependence of chaos (increased regularity at low and high energies, reduced regularity in between) agrees with these prior studies. The work enables direct comparison between quantum statistical measures and classical measures of chaos in the IBM, and provides a foundation for studying the stability of the arc of regularity for nuclei with different boson numbers.
