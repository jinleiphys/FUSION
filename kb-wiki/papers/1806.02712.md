# Dissecting reaction calculations using Halo EFT and ab initio input

> We present a description of the break-up of halo nuclei in peripheral nuclear reactions by coupling a model of the projectile motivated by Halo Effective Field Theory with a fully dynamical treatment of the reaction using the Dynamical Eikonal Approximation. Our description of the halo system reproduces its long-range properties, i.e., binding energy and asymptotic normalization coefficients of bound states and phase shifts of continuum states. As an application we consider the break-up of 11Be in collisions on Pb and C targets. Taking the input for our Halo-EFT-inspired description of 11Be from a recent ab initio calculation of that system yields a good description of the Coulomb-dominated breakup on Pb at energies up to about 2 MeV, with the result essentially independent of the short-distance part of the halo wave function. However, the nuclear dominated break-up on C is more sensitive to short-range physics. The role of spectroscopic factors and possible extensions of our approach to include additional short-range mechanisms are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that coupling a Halo Effective Field Theory (Halo-EFT) description of the projectile with the Dynamical Eikonal Approximation (DEA) for the reaction dynamics provides a good description of Coulomb-dominated breakup of 11Be on Pb at energies up to about 2 MeV, with results essentially independent of the short-distance part of the halo wave function; however, the nuclear-dominated breakup on C is more sensitive to short-range physics, indicating limitations of the approach for reactions where nuclear interactions dominate.

## Method

The authors construct Gaussian-regulated Halo-EFT potentials for the 10Be-neutron interaction in the s1/2 and p1/2 partial waves, with parameters fitted to reproduce the binding energies and asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from an ab initio No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) calculation by Calci et al. (2016). These potentials are then used within the Dynamical Eikonal Approximation (DEA) to compute breakup cross sections of 11Be on Pb and C targets at approximately 70 MeV/nucleon, with the range of the Gaussian potential varied (1.2, 1.5, and 2.0 fm) to test sensitivity to short-distance physics. A third set of potentials ("NLO EFT + resonances") adds interactions in the p3/2, d5/2, and d3/2 channels to reproduce known 11Be resonances.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of 11Be ground state: S1n = 503 keV (experimental value from Ajzenberg-Selove (1990))
- One-neutron separation energy of 11Be first excited state (1/2-): S*1n = 184 keV (experimental value)
- Expansion parameter for Halo-EFT description: Rcore/Rhalo ~ 0.4
- Gaussian potential ranges considered: 1.2, 1.5, and 2.0 fm
- 5/2+ resonance in 11Be continuum at E = 1.274 MeV
- Coulomb-dominated breakup on Pb described well up to about 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Halo-EFT treatment of 11Be by Hammer et al. (2011), which computed E1 dissociation strength using a simplified reaction model, by incorporating a fully dynamical reaction treatment via the DEA. The approach uses ab initio input from the NCSMC calculation of Calci et al. (2016) to determine ANCs and phase shifts, building on the finding by Capel and Nunes (2007) that breakup calculations are sensitive primarily to the tail of the wave function. The method enables systematic identification of which nuclear-structure inputs are needed for reaction calculations at a given accuracy, and can be extended to include additional short-range mechanisms such as core excitation.
