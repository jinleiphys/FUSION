# Non-perturbative Gluons and Pseudoscalar Mesons in Baryon Spectroscopy

> We study baryon spectroscopy including the effects of pseudoscalar meson exchange and one gluon exchange potentials between quarks, governed by $α_s$. The non-perturbative, hyperspherical method calculations show that one can obtain a good description of the data by using a quark-meson coupling constant that is compatible with the measured pion-nucleon coupling constant, and a reasonably small value of $α_s$.

## Key claim

A non-perturbative hyperspherical calculation including both one-gluon exchange and pseudoscalar meson exchange can describe the light-quark baryon spectrum, with the quark-meson coupling constant close to the value derived from the measured pion-nucleon coupling constant and a reasonably small value of the strong coupling constant α_s, provided one allows the meson coupling strength to vary away from the value of 0.67 used by Glozman and Riska.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic constituent quark model Hamiltonian with kinetic energy (quark mass 336 MeV), a linear confining potential, a one-gluon exchange potential (with a Yukawa cutoff of range 0.238 fm to account for finite quark size), and a pseudoscalar meson exchange potential from the SU(3)_F octet. They solved the three-body Schrödinger equation non-perturbatively using the hyperspherical method with an optimal subset of potential harmonics, constructing spatial wave functions symmetric under permutations and using standard SU(6) spin-flavor wave functions. The radial wave functions for nucleon and Δ states were obtained by solving a differential equation using the renormalized Numerov method.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass for u/d quarks: 336 MeV
- Cutoff range r_0 for one-gluon exchange Yukawa: 0.238 fm
- Cutoff range Λ for meson exchange Yukawa: 0.238 fm
- In the model with only one-gluon exchange (no mesons), α_s ≈ 2.2 was needed with A=0.10 GeV/fm, and α_s ≈ 1.4 with A=0.45 GeV/fm
- In the model with only pseudoscalar meson exchange (no gluons), α_{qπ} ≈ 1.4 was needed (compared to the expected value of 0.67)
- In the full model with both interactions, α_s ≈ 0.7 and α_{qπ} ≈ 1.0
- The derived α_{qπ} from the measured pion-nucleon coupling constant (g^2_{πN}/4π = 14.2) and g_A = 1.26 gives α_{qπ} = 1.1
- The confining string tension A_L = 0.17 GeV/fm yielded a nucleon rms radius of 0.46 fm
- Most mass differences between states were described within accuracy of 10% or better

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Glozman and Riska (1994-1995), who argued that pseudoscalar meson exchange alone could describe baryon spectra and that gluon exchange was unnecessary. It also builds on the earlier one-gluon exchange models of DeRujula, Georgi and Glashow (1975) and Isgur and Karl (1978-1979), which had difficulty simultaneously describing the Roper resonance and P-wave states. The non-perturbative hyperspherical method of Fabre de la Ripelle, Fiedeldey and Sofianos (1987) allowed the authors to treat both interactions without perturbation theory. The results enable future work including tensor and spin-orbit forces, relativistic recoil corrections, meson cloud effects on the nucleon radius, decay properties of excited states, and extension to strange baryons.
