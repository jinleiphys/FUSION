# Theoretical and practical progresses in the HAL QCD method

> In this report, we discuss some theoretical and practical progresses in the HAL QCD potential method. We first clarify the issue of the derivative expansion for the non-local potential in the HAL QCD method. As the non-local potential in the original literature is not uniquely defined, we propose a procedure to define a non-local potential from NBS wave functions in terms of the derivative expansion. We then demonstrate how this definition works by using quantum mechanics with a separable potential. Secondly we discuss an issue of Hermiticity of the HAL QCD potential. Since the NBS wav functions are not orthogonal to each other in general, the HAL QCD potential is necessary to be non-Hermitian. We consider the next-to-leading order potential, which can be made Hermitian exactly by the change of variables. In general we can also make the higher order HAL QCD potential Hermitian order by order in the derivative expansion. An explicit example on how the procedure works is given for lattice QCD calculations. Finally we discuss how we can extract the HAL QCD potential from the NBS wave function in the boosted system. An explicit formula for this is derived.

## Key claim
The HAL QCD potential method can be made well-defined through a derivative expansion scheme that fixes the non-local potential uniquely, and the resulting non-Hermitian potentials can be made Hermitian order by order in the expansion, though the practical utility of this Hermitization depends on the specific system.

## Method
The author proposes a scheme to define the non-local HAL QCD potential by expanding it in terms of \((\nabla^2)^j (\mathbf{L}^2)^{n-j}\) and determining the coefficient functions \(V_{j,n-j}^{(N)}(x)\) from a system of equations using Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions at multiple energies up to the inelastic threshold \(W_{\text{th}}\). For the Hermitization procedure, a change of variables \(\psi = R\phi\) is applied to the Schrödinger equation, and \(R\) is solved for analytically to cancel the non-Hermitian part of the next-to-leading order (NLO) potential. The method for extracting potentials from boosted systems uses a Lorentz transformation to relate the NBS wave function in the moving frame to the center-of-mass frame, with an explicit formula derived for the potential in the equal-time scheme at \(\mathbf{x}_\parallel = 0\).

## Key numbers
- The separable potential test uses parameters \(\omega/\mu^4 = -0.017\), \(m/\mu = 3.3\), and \(R\mu = 2.5\), chosen to mimic the \(NN\) (\({}^1S_0\)) channel with \(m \simeq m_N/2\) and \(\mu \simeq m_\pi\).
- The NLO scattering phase shift from the derivative expansion reproduces the exact phase shift at both \(k^2=0\) and \(k^2=\mu^2\), providing a reasonable interpolation between these two points.
- For the \(\Xi\Xi\) (\({}^1S_0\)) system, lattice QCD calculations were performed at lattice spacing \(a = 0.09\) fm and pion mass \(m_\pi = 510\) MeV.
- The Hermitized NLO potential \(\tilde{V}_0 + \nabla^i V_2 \nabla_i\) gives identical \(k\cot\delta_0(k)/m_\pi\) results to the original non-Hermitian NLO potential \(V_0 + V_2\nabla^2\) by construction.

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD potential method, originally developed by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009, 2012), by addressing three theoretical issues: the non-uniqueness of the non-local potential definition, the non-Hermiticity arising from non-orthogonal NBS wave functions, and the extension to boosted systems. The derivative expansion scheme and Hermitization procedure build on prior work by Iritani (2016, 2017) that clarified differences between the HAL QCD method and the finite volume method of Lüscher (1990). The boosted system formalism enables future studies of resonances like the \(\sigma\) meson by reducing vacuum state contamination, with the author noting ongoing tests on the \(I=2\) \(\pi\pi\) system in the moving frame.
