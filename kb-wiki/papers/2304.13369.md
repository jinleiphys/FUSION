# Generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method for small and large amplitude collective motion

> An implementation of the generalized time-dependent generator coordinated method (TD-GCM) is developed, that can be applied to the dynamics of small- and large-amplitude collective motion of atomic nuclei. Both the generator states and weight functions of the GCM correlated wave function depend on time. The initial generator states are obtained as solutions of deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field equations, and are evolved in time by the standard mean-field equations of nuclear density functional theory (TD-DFT). The TD-DFT trajectories are used as a generally non-orthogonal and overcomplete basis in which the TD-GCM wave function is expanded. The weights, expressed in terms of a collective wave function, obey a TD-GCM (integral) equation. In this explorative paper, the generalized TD-GCM is applied to the excitation energies and spreading width of giant resonances, and to the dynamics of induced fission. The necessity of including pairing correlations in the basis of TD-DFT trajectories is demonstrated in the latter example.

# Generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method for small and large amplitude collective motion

## Key claim

The generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TD-GCM), which uses time-dependent generator states from TD-DFT trajectories as a non-orthogonal and overcomplete basis, can describe both the spreading widths of giant resonances and induced fission dynamics, but the inclusion of pairing correlations in the basis of TD-DFT trajectories is necessary for realistic modeling of fission observables.

## Method

The authors implement a generalized TD-GCM where both the generator states and weight functions depend on time. Initial generator states are obtained as solutions of deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field equations using the point-coupling relativistic energy density functional PC-PK1, then evolved in time by the standard time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT) equations. The TD-DFT trajectories form a non-orthogonal and overcomplete basis in which the TD-GCM wave function is expanded, with the collective wave function obeying a time-dependent Griffin-Hill-Wheeler equation. For small-amplitude motion, the method is applied to monopole, quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecapole vibrations in 208Pb; for large-amplitude motion, a schematic two-trajectory TD-GCM description of induced fission of 240Pu is presented without pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- The calculated equilibrium binding energy of 208Pb is 1637.97 MeV and the corresponding matter radius is 5.617 fm, in excellent agreement with data.
- The TD-GCM monopole strength function for 208Pb with four basis trajectories shows excellent agreement with the experimental ISGMR at 13.7 MeV.
- The TD-GCM calculation for 208Pb uses a lattice mesh spacing of 1 fm for all directions, lattice size 24 x 24 x 24 fm3, and time step 0.2 fm/c (6.67 x 10-4 zs).
- For the quadrupole response in 208Pb, the TD-GCM strength function shows a peak at 12.3 MeV (ISGQR) and a low-energy peak at 4.9 MeV.
- The TD-DFT octupole strength function peak for 208Pb appears at 3.1 MeV, compared to the lowest experimental 3- state at 2.61 MeV.
- The TD-DFT hexadecapole strength function for 208Pb shows a main peak at 4.9 MeV, compared to the lowest experimental 4+ state at 4.32 MeV.
- For 240Pu fission, the initial points are at beta20 = 1.05 and beta30 = 2.37 for the first trajectory, and beta20 = 1.05 and beta30 = 2.39 for the second trajectory.
- The 240Pu fission calculation uses a lattice size of 20 x 20 x 60 fm3 with mesh spacing 1 fm and time step 0.2 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent generator coordinate method beyond the adiabatic approximation by combining it with TD-DFT trajectories, following an approach originally introduced by Reinhard (1983) and more recently applied to particle number restoration by Regnier (2019) and to collective multi-phonon states by Marevie (2023). The method directly addresses the limitation of standard TD-DFT, which cannot describe spreading widths of one-body observables (Reinhard, 1983) and can only simulate single fission events without quantitative estimates of fragment distribution widths. The TD-GCM monopole strength function for 208Pb is almost identical to that calculated by Litvinova (2023) using a microscopic theory of nuclear response with quasiparticle-vibration coupling. The approach enables fully quantum mechanical description of collective dynamics with dissipation, going beyond the random phase approximation (RPA) level to describe spreading widths of giant resonances, and provides a framework for future calculations of fission observables that will require inclusion of pairing correlations.
