# Quarks to Cosmos: Particles and Plasma in Cosmological evolution

> We describe in the context of the particle physics (PP) standard model (SM) `PP-SM' the understanding of the primordial properties and composition of the Universe in the temperature range $130\GeV>T>20\keV$. The Universe evolution is described using FLRW cosmology. We present a global view on particle content across time and describe the different evolution eras using deceleration parameter $q$. We follow the arrow of time in the expanding and cooling Universe: After the PP-SM heavies $(t, h, W, Z)$ diminish in abundance below $T\simeq 50\GeV$, the PP-SM plasma in the Universe is governed by the strongly interacting Quark-Gluon content. Once the temperature drops below $T\simeq 150\MeV$, quarks and gluons hadronize into strongly interacting matter particles. Rapid disappearance of baryonic antimatter completes at $T_\mathrm{B}=38.2\MeV$. We study the ensuing disappearance of strangeness and mesons in general. We show that the different eras defined by particle populations are barely separated from each other with abundance of muons fading out just prior to $T=\mathcal{O}(2.5)\MeV$, the era of emergence of the free-streaming neutrinos. We discuss the two relevant fundamental constants controlling the decoupling of neutrinos. We subsequently follow the primordial Universe as it passes through the hot dense electron-positron plasma epoch. The high density of positron antimatter disappears near $T=20.3\keV$: Nuclear reactions occur in the presence of a highly mobile and relatively strongly interacting electron-positron plasma phase. We apply plasma theory methods to describe the strong screening effects between heavy dust particle (nucleons). We analyze the paramagnetic characteristics of the electron-positron plasma when exposed to an external primordial magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper provides a continuous description of the primordial Universe's particle and plasma composition across the temperature range 130 GeV > T > 20 keV, arguing that within this epoch the unknown cold dark matter and dark energy content of LambdaCDM have negligible influence, allowing a reliable understanding based solely on the particle physics standard model (PP-SM) energy-momentum.

## Method

The authors integrate the Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker (FLRW) cosmological equations backward in time from the present-day composition (69% dark energy, 26% dark matter, 5% baryons) to study the primordial plasma. They apply equilibrium statistical physics with Fermi and Bose distributions to describe particle abundances, and use plasma theory methods to analyze screening effects in the electron-positron plasma and paramagnetic characteristics under primordial magnetic fields. The evolution is tracked through distinct epochs defined by the deceleration parameter q and the changing particle content as the Universe cools.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 130 GeV > T > 20 keV
- Baryonic antimatter disappearance completes at T_B = 38.2 MeV
- Muon abundance fades out just prior to T = O(2.5) MeV
- Positron antimatter disappears near T = 20.3 keV
- Energy density at the end of the QGP epoch (T ≈ 150 MeV): epsilon = 1 GeV/fm^3 = 1.8 × 10^15 g/cm^3
- Corresponding pressure at that epoch: P ≈ (1/3) epsilon = 0.52 × 10^30 bar
- Universe expansion time constant at energy density rho_0 = 1 GeV/fm^3: tau_U = 14 microseconds
- Planck mass (cosmology convention): M_p c^2 = 2.4353 × 10^18 GeV
- Planck mass (particle data group convention): M_p^PDG c^2 = 1.2209 × 10^19 GeV
- Temperature conversion: 100 MeV ≡ 116 × 10^10 K
- Hagedorn temperature: T_H ≈ 150 MeV (range 150-180 MeV)
- Hadronization temperature: T_0 ≈ 150 MeV
- Recombination temperature: T_recomb ≈ 0.25 eV

## Context

This work extends the authors' ongoing program (Rafelski 2023, Birrell 2014, Grayson 2024, Steinmetz 2023, Yang 2024) to create a continuous description of the primordial Universe connecting different particle and plasma epochs. It builds on the Cabibbo-Parisi (1975) proposal of a deconfinement phase boundary at high temperature and the Hagedorn bootstrap model of hadronic matter melting into quark-gluon plasma (Rafelski and Hagedorn 2015). The paper enables future study of how PP-SM physics in the primordial epoch may impact extrapolations of the Hubble parameter from recombination to the current epoch, and provides a framework for understanding primordial magnetic field generation from the electron-positron plasma.
