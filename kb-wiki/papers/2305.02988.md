# Kinetic approach of light-nuclei production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We develop a kinetic approach to the production of light nuclei up to mass number $A$ $\leqslant$ $4$ in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions by including them as dynamic degrees of freedom. The conversions between nucleons and light nuclei during the collisions are incorporated dynamically via the breakup of light nuclei by a nucleon and their inverse reactions. We also include the Mott effect on light nuclei, i.e., a light nucleus would no longer be bound if the phase-space density of its surrounding nucleons is too large. With this kinetic approach, we obtain a reasonable description of the measured yields of light nuclei in central Au+Au collisions at energies of $0.25$ - $1.0A~\rm GeV$ by the FOPI collaboration. Our study also indicates that the observed enhancement of the $α$-particle yield at low incident energies can be attributed to a weaker Mott effect on the $α$-particle, which makes it more difficult to dissolve in nuclear medium, as a result of its much larger binding energy.

## Key claim

The authors develop a kinetic approach that includes light nuclei (up to mass number 4) as dynamic degrees of freedom in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, and they claim that the observed enhancement of alpha-particle yield at low incident energies can be attributed to a weaker Mott effect on the alpha-particle due to its much larger binding energy, making it more difficult to dissolve in the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors derive coupled kinetic equations for nucleons and light nuclei (deuteron, triton, helium-3, and alpha-particle) using real-time many-body Green's-function formalism, where light nuclei appear as poles of the A-particle Green's function. They include nucleon-induced catalytic reactions (NNN↔Nd, NNNN↔Nt(h), NNNNN↔Nα, NNt(h)↔Nα) and the two-body inelastic channel Nα↔dt(h), with transition amplitudes determined from experimental cross sections and detailed balance using the impulse approximation. The Mott effect is implemented through a phase-space cutoff condition where A free nucleons can form a nucleus only if the average nucleon phase-space density around them is less than a cutoff value f_A^cut, and the kinetic equations are solved using the test particle ansatz with a lattice Hamiltonian method and stochastic treatment of the collision integral.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range studied: 0.25 to 1.0 A GeV for central Au+Au collisions
- Cutoff parameters used: f_{A=2}^cut = 0.11 for deuteron, f_{A=3}^cut = 0.16 for triton and helium-3, and f_{A=4}^cut = 0.25 for alpha-particle
- Normal nuclear matter density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} used as reference
- Temperature T = 30 MeV used for calculating Mott momentum of alpha-particle in nuclear matter
- Two values of f_{A=4}^cut tested: 0.25 and 0.15, showing that decreasing f_{A=4}^cut significantly reduces the alpha-particle yield

## Context

This work extends previous dynamical attempts to describe light nuclei in transport models by Danielewicz and collaborators (1990s), which did not include the alpha-particle, and responds to new measurements from the INDRA and FOPI collaborations (Reisdorf et al., 2010) showing enhanced alpha-particle yields at low incident energies. The approach enables future studies of light-nuclei phenomena in nuclear reactions including iso-scaling, alpha-clusters on heavy nuclei surfaces, and the role of light nuclei in core-collapse supernovae and compact star mergers, while also calling for extension to include fluctuations for describing heavy fragments with mass number A ≥ 5.
