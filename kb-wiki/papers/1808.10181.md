# Symmetries of the interacting boson model

> In this contribution the symmetry properties of the interacting boson model of Arima and Iachello are reviewed. While the concept of a dynamical symmetry is by now a familiar one, this is not necessarily so for the extended notions of partial dynamical symmetry and quasi dynamical symmetry, which can be beautifully illustrated in the context of the interacting boson model. The main conclusion of the analysis is that dynamical symmetries are scarce while their partial and quasi extensions are ubiquitous.

# Symmetries of the interacting boson model

## Key claim
While exact dynamical symmetries in the interacting boson model are restricted to small regions of parameter space, the extended notions of partial dynamical symmetry and quasi dynamical symmetry are ubiquitous across the parameter space of the extended consistent-Q formalism Hamiltonian, as illustrated by wave-function entropy calculations for boson number N_b = 15 and angular momentum L = 0.

## Method
The author reviews the symmetry properties of the interacting boson model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello, focusing on the extended consistent-Q formalism (ECQF) Hamiltonian with parameters ξ and χ. Wave-function entropy is computed in U(5), SU±(3), and SO(6) bases for eigenstates with angular momentum L = 0 and boson number N_b = 15, and a measure Ω_r of quasi dynamical symmetry is evaluated for yrast states with L = 0,2,…,10. The analysis distinguishes three types of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS-1, PDS-2, PDS-3) based on which subset of eigenstates and which quantum numbers are conserved.

## Key numbers
- The ECQF Hamiltonian parameter space spans 0 ≤ ξ ≤ 1 and −√7/2 ≤ χ ≤ +√7/2, covering the U(5), SU±(3), and SO(6) dynamical symmetry limits.
- Wave-function entropy calculations are performed for boson number N_b = 15 and angular momentum L = 0, with the maximum entropy value being ln D where D is the basis dimension.
- For the SO(6) partial dynamical symmetry of type PDS-3, an entire band of ECQF Hamiltonians shows close to exact SO(6) symmetry in the ground state, as reported by Kremer (2014).
- The U(5)–SO(6) transitional Hamiltonian shows exact conservation of the SO(5) label υ for all eigenstates (PDS-2), a result known since Leviatan (1986).
- The measure Ω_r of quasi dynamical symmetry is evaluated for yrast eigenstates with angular momenta L = 0,2,…,10 and boson number N_b = 15, with vanishing Ω_r indicating perfect correlation between expansion coefficients for different L.

## Context
This review extends the well-known dynamical symmetry concept in the IBM, originally formulated by Arima and Iachello (1975), to the more general notions of partial dynamical symmetry (Alhassid and Leviatan, 1992; Leviatan, 1986; Leviatan, 2002) and quasi dynamical symmetry (Rowe, 1988; Rowe, 1998). The work builds on earlier studies of quantum phase transitions in nuclei (Cejnar, 2010) and catastrophe theory analyses of the IBM energy surface (Gilmore, 1981; López-Moreno, 1996). The results enable future investigations of the full symmetry content of the IBM with up to three-body interactions, which the author notes remains to be uncovered more than forty years after the model's proposal.
