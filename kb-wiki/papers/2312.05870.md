# Critical dynamics within the real-time fRG approach

> The Schwinger-Keldysh functional renormalization group (fRG) developed in [1] is employed to investigate critical dynamics related to a second-order phase transition. The effective action of model A is expanded to the order of $O(\partial^2)$ in the derivative expansion for the $O(N)$ symmetry. By solving the fixed-point equations of effective potential and wave function, we obtain static and dynamic critical exponents for different values of the spatial dimension $d$ and the field component number $N$. It is found that one has $z \geq 2$ in the whole range of $2\leq d\leq 4$ for the case of $N=1$, while in the case of $N=4$ the dynamic critical exponent turns to $z < 2$ when the dimension approach towards $d=2$.

## Key claim

The authors use the Schwinger-Keldysh functional renormalization group (fRG) to compute static and dynamic critical exponents for model A with O(N) symmetry, finding that the dynamic critical exponent z is always greater than or equal to 2 for N=1 across dimensions 2 to 4, but for N=4 the exponent becomes less than 2 when the spatial dimension approaches d=2.

## Method

The real-time fRG formalism developed by Tan et al. (2021) is applied to the dissipative relaxation model (model A) with O(N) symmetry. The effective action is expanded to O(∂²) in the derivative expansion, and fixed-point equations for the effective potential and wave function are solved numerically using both a conventional grid method and a high-precision direct integration method. The static anomalous dimension η and dynamic anomalous dimension η_t are extracted from the flow equations, and the dynamic critical exponent is obtained as z = 2 - η + η_t.

## Key numbers

- For N=1, the dynamic critical exponent satisfies z ≥ 2 over the entire range 2 ≤ d ≤ 4.
- For N=4, the dynamic critical exponent becomes z < 2 when the dimension falls below d = 2.5.
- The static anomalous dimension η and dynamic anomalous dimension η_t both vanish (η_t = 0) at d = 4, indicating no critical slowing down at the Gaussian fixed point.
- Results from derivative expansion and LPA' truncations are comparable with three-loop ε-expansion results for dimensions d ≳ 3.5, while LPA overestimates the dynamic critical exponent.

## Context

This work extends the real-time fRG approach developed by Tan et al. (2021) to study critical dynamics of model A, which was classified by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977). The results are compared with three-loop ε-expansion calculations by Antonov (1984) and Folk and Moser (2006). The method enables nonperturbative computation of dynamic critical exponents across dimensions, and the authors note that for N ≥ 2 the Mermin-Wagner-Hohenberg theorem prevents phase transitions in d=2, which is reflected in the non-monotonic behavior of the anomalous dimensions.
