# Pion valence quark distribution from current-current correlation in lattice QCD

> We extract the pion valence quark distribution $q^π_{\rm v}(x)$ from lattice QCD (LQCD) calculated matrix elements of spacelike correlations of one vector and one axial vector current analyzed in terms of QCD collinear factorization, using a new short-distance matching coefficient calculated to one-loop accuracy. We derive the Ioffe time distribution of the two-current correlations in the physical limit by investigating the finite lattice spacing, volume, quark mass, and higher-twist dependencies in a simultaneous fit of matrix elements computed on four gauge ensembles. We find remarkable consistency between our extracted $q^π_{\rm v}(x)$ and that obtained from experimental data across the entire $x$-range. Further, we demonstrate that the one-loop matching coefficient relating the LQCD matrix computed in position space to the $q_{\rm v}^π(x)$ in momentum space has well-controlled behavior with Ioffe time. This justifies that LQCD calculated current-current correlations are good observables for extracting partonic structures by using QCD factorization, which complements to the global effort to extract partonic structure from experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the pion valence quark distribution \(q^\pi_{\rm v}(x)\) from lattice QCD (LQCD) matrix elements of spacelike vector-axial current correlations using a new one-loop matching coefficient, finding consistency with experimental data across the entire \(x\) range, but only when the spatial separation \(\xi\) between currents is kept sufficiently small (\(\lvert\xi\rvert \le 0.56\) fm) to ensure the validity of short-distance QCD factorization and minimize higher-twist contributions.

## Method

The authors computed pion matrix elements of two local, spacelike-separated, gauge-invariant currents (one vector, one axial-vector) on four 2+1 flavor isotropic-clover fermion ensembles with lattice spacings \(a = 0.127\) fm and \(0.094\) fm and pion masses ranging from 278 MeV to 413 MeV. They performed a simultaneous \(z\)-expansion fit of the Ioffe-time distribution across all ensembles, including corrections for finite lattice spacing, volume, quark mass, and higher-twist effects, then extracted \(q^\pi_{\rm v}(x)\) by numerically convolving the physical Ioffe-time distribution with the next-to-leading order perturbative matching kernel.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings: \(a = 0.127(2)\) fm and \(a = 0.094(1)\) fm; pion masses: \(m_\pi = 278(4)\) MeV, 358(3) MeV, and 413(4–5) MeV on four ensembles.
- Spatial separations used: \(\lvert\xi\rvert \le 0.56\) fm (specifically \(\xi = 2 \times 0.094\) fm for the shortest separation).
- 2-parameter fit results: \(\alpha = -0.17(7)_{\rm stat}(2)_{\rm sys}\), \(\beta = 1.24(22)_{\rm stat}(7)_{\rm sys}\) with \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f} = 1.41\).
- 3-parameter fit results: \(\alpha = -0.22(11)_{\rm stat}(3)_{\rm sys}\), \(\beta = 2.12(56)_{\rm stat}(14)_{\rm sys}\), \(\gamma = 4.28(1.73)_{\rm stat}(25)_{\rm sys}\) with \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f} \approx 1.29\).
- Strong coupling used: \(\alpha_s = 0.303\) at scale \(\mu_0 = 2\) GeV, with systematic uncertainty estimated by 10% variation.

## Context

This work extends the lattice cross section (LCS) approach of Ma (2014, 2017) and the authors' previous determination of the pion valence PDF (Sufian, 2019) by including a full one-loop matching coefficient and a simultaneous fit across four ensembles to control systematic errors. It contrasts with earlier phenomenological analyses (Aicher, 2010; Barry, 2018) that showed disagreement on the large-\(x\) behavior of the pion PDF, and with other LQCD methods such as quasi-PDFs (Ji, 2013) and reduced pseudo-ITDs (Radyushkin, 2017). The results enable future comparisons with upcoming Jefferson Lab tagged deep-inelastic scattering experiments, COMPASS Drell-Yan measurements, and the Electron-Ion Collider.
