# Universality of multi-particle production in QCD at high energies

> By studying the color structure of multi-particle production processes in p+A-type (dilute-dense) collisions, we find that higher-point functions beyond typical dipoles and quadrupoles, e.g., sextupoles, octupoles, etc., naturally appear in the cross sections, but are explicitly suppressed in the large-N_c limit. We evaluate the sextupole in the McLerran-Venugopalan model and find that, in general, its analytical form cannot be written as combination of dipoles and quadrupoles. Within the Color Glass Condensate framework, we present a proof that in the large-N_c limit, all multi-particle production processes in the collision of a dilute system off a dense can, up to all orders in α_s, be described in terms of only dipoles and quadrupoles.

## Key claim

The paper proves that in the large-\(N_c\) limit, all multi-particle production processes in dilute-dense (p+A-type) collisions can be described using only dipole and quadrupole correlators, up to all orders in \(\alpha_s\). This universality holds despite the fact that higher-point functions (sextupoles, octupoles, etc.) naturally appear in the cross sections and cannot generally be written as combinations of dipoles and quadrupoles outside the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Method

The authors analyze the color structure of multi-particle production cross sections in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, using eikonal Wilson lines to describe multiple scattering of partons off a dense target. They employ a graphical approach based on the double-line (large-\(N_c\)) representation and Fierz identities to identify the leading-\(N_c\) contributions, working explicitly with processes involving up to three final-state particles and then proving by induction that all higher-multiplicity processes reduce to dipoles and quadrupoles. The sextupole correlator is evaluated explicitly in the McLerran-Venugopalan model to demonstrate that it cannot be factorized into dipoles and quadrupoles in general.

## Key numbers

- The sextupole correlator (six Wilson lines) is evaluated in the McLerran-Venugopalan model and shown to have an analytical form that cannot be written as a combination of dipoles and quadrupoles.
- The large-\(N_c\) suppression of higher-point functions is explicitly demonstrated: terms with sextupoles, octupoles, etc. are suppressed by powers of \(N_c\) relative to dipole and quadrupole terms.
- For the \(g \to q\bar{q}\) process, the Fierz identity yields a leading term \(\frac{1}{2}\text{Tr}[U_1U^\dagger_4]\text{Tr}[U^\dagger_2U_3]\) and a subleading term \(-\frac{1}{2N_c}\text{Tr}[U_1U^\dagger_2U_3U^\dagger_4]\), explicitly showing the \(1/N_c\) suppression of the quadrupole relative to the dipole product.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of two-particle production processes studied by Jalilian-Marian and Kovchegov (2004), Marquet (2007), and Dominguez et al. (2011), which showed that quadrupole amplitudes appear in di-jet production and cannot be reduced to dipoles. The paper provides a general proof that the large-\(N_c\) limit ensures universality across all multiplicities, enabling simplified calculations of multi-jet observables in p+A collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The result also establishes the foundation for factorization proofs in single-inclusive processes, as shown by Chirilli et al. (2011), and provides initial conditions for the small-\(x\) evolution of higher-point correlators studied by Iancu and Triantafyllopoulos (2011) and Dumitru et al. (2011).
