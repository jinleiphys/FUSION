# Relativistic energy density functional from momentum space to coordinate space within a coherent density fluctuation model

> In this theoretical study, we have derived a simplified analytical expression for the binding energy per nucleon as a function of density and isospin asymmetry within the relativistic mean-field model. We have generated a new parameterization for the density-dependent DD-ME2 parameter set using the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov approach. Moreover, this work attempts to revisit the prior polynomial fitting in [Phy. Rev. C 103, 024305 (2021)] for the non-linear NL3 force parameter to provide a simplified set of equations for the energy density functional which is used for calculating the surface properties of finite nuclei. The current study improves the existing fitting procedure by effectively proposing a simpler model that provides comparably precise results while lowering the computational expense. To study the surface properties of finite nuclei with these parameterizations, we have adopted the coherent density fluctuation model, which effectively translates the quantities of nuclear matter from momentum space to coordinate space at local density. The isospin properties, such as symmetry energy and its surface and volume components, slope parameter, finite nuclear incompressibility, and surface incompressibility for even-even nuclei, are calculated for different mass regions. Moreover, we have studied the effect of density, weight function, and choice of relativistic force parameters on the surface properties. The consequence of this work will help to determine the properties of nuclei along the nuclear landscape and can facilitate an improved understanding of the island of stability, heavy-ion collision, and nucleosynthesis, among others.

# Relativistic energy density functional from momentum space to coordinate space within a coherent density fluctuation model

## Key claim

The authors derive a simplified 8-term analytical expression for the binding energy per nucleon as a function of density and isospin asymmetry within the relativistic mean-field model, reducing the number of fitting coefficients to about one-third of the 24 parameters used in the prior polynomial fitting of Kumar (2021), while maintaining comparably precise results and lowering computational expense. The new parameterization is generated for the density-dependent DD-ME2 set and revisits the NL3 set, but the authors note that individually fitting each parameter set is necessary because finding a single fitting procedure that simultaneously defines both sets is time-consuming and resource-intensive due to differences in the curvature of their energy-density graphs.

## Method

The authors employ principal component analysis (PCA) to minimize the number of ad-hoc terms in the relativistic energy density functional (EDF), using correlation matrices to identify and reduce strongly correlated predictors. They perform the fitting using the Levenberg-Marquardt iteration algorithm, taking 12 iterations for NL3 and 13 iterations for the DD-ME2 parameter set within the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach. The resulting EDF is then used within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), which translates infinite nuclear matter quantities from momentum space to coordinate space at local density via a weight function derived from the nuclear density distribution, to calculate surface properties of finite even-even nuclei.

- Root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) for the binding energy per nucleon: 0.07704 MeV for DD-ME2 and 0.19146 MeV for the NL3 parameter set using the 8-term fitting.
- The fitting procedure took 12 iterations for NL3 and 13 iterations for the DD-ME2 parameter set.
- For DD-ME2, the finite nuclear incompressibility K^A ranges from 208.1 MeV (^208Pb) to 327.3 MeV (^56Ni); symmetry energy S ranges from 22.7 MeV (^16O) to 25.4 MeV (^56Ni); slope parameter L^A_sym ranges from 38.7 MeV (^16O) to 42.7 MeV (^56Ni); surface incompressibility K^A_sym ranges from -77.9 MeV (^56Ni) to -65.7 MeV (^16O).
- For NL3, K^A ranges from 228.6 MeV (^208Pb) to 382.7 MeV (^56Ni); S ranges from 26.7 MeV (^40Ca) to 32.1 MeV (^56Ni); L^A_sym ranges from 85.4 MeV (^40Ca) to 103.8 MeV (^56Ni); K^A_sym ranges from 27.0 MeV (^16O) to 60.9 MeV (^208Pb).
- The saturation incompressibility K_0 for NL3 is 271.53 MeV at saturation energy -16.24 MeV; for DD-ME2 it is 250.92 MeV at saturation energy -16.14 MeV, citing Dutra (2014).
- The volume symmetry energy S_V for DD-ME2 ranges from 25.5 MeV (^208Pb) to 29.9 MeV (^56Ni); surface symmetry energy S_S ranges from 13.4 MeV (^208Pb) to 19.9 MeV (^56Ni).
- For NL3, S_V ranges from 30.2 MeV (^208Pb) to 37.9 MeV (^56Ni); S_S ranges from 15.9 MeV (^208Pb) to 26.2 MeV (^56Ni).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic energy density functional approach of Kumar (2021), which used 24 ad-hoc parameters with polynomial fitting, by applying principal component analysis to reduce the number of terms to eight while maintaining accuracy. It also revisits the prior fitting procedure for the NL3 force parameter set from the same 2021 work. The CDFM formalism was previously applied using non-relativistic Brueckner's prescription by Antonov (1978, 1980, 1982) and Gaidarov (2011), and later with relativistic inputs by Bhuyan (2018) and Yadav (2022), but those works still employed the original Brueckner prescription which suffers from the Coester-band problem (Coester 1970, Brockmann 1990), the failure to reproduce the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter (E/A approximately -16 MeV at density approximately 0.15 fm^-3). The present relativistic-EDF resolves this issue, as demonstrated in Pattnaik (2022) for the Pb isotopic chain where the relativistic-EDF correctly predicts the symmetry energy peak at N=126 instead of the incorrect peak at N=120 from the Brueckner functional. This work enables improved determination of symmetry energy and its coefficients for studying the island of stability, neutron star physics, nucleosynthesis, and heavy-ion collisions.
