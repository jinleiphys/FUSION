# Femtoscopic scales in $p+p$ and $p+$Pb collisions in view of the uncertainty principle

> A method for quantum corrections of Hanbury-Brown/Twiss (HBT) interferometric radii produced by semi-classical event generators is proposed. These corrections account for the basic indistinguishability and mutual coherence of closely located emitters caused by the uncertainty principle. A detailed analysis is presented for pion interferometry in $p+p$ collisions at LHC energy ($\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV). A prediction is also presented of pion interferometric radii for $p+$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV. The hydrodynamic/hydrokinetic model with UrQMD cascade as 'afterburner' is utilized for this aim. It is found that quantum corrections to the interferometry radii improve significantly the event generator results which typically overestimate the experimental radii of small systems. A successful description of the interferometry structure of $p+p$ collisions within the corrected hydrodynamic model requires the study of the problem of thermalization mechanism, still a fundamental issue for ultrarelativistic $A+A$ collisions, also for high multiplicity $p+p$ and $p+$Pb events.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that quantum corrections based on the uncertainty principle, accounting for partial indistinguishability and mutual coherence of closely located emitters, can significantly reduce the overestimated interferometry radii from semi-classical event generators in small systems like p+p collisions, but the success of this corrected hydrodynamic model for p+p collisions still depends on resolving the fundamental issue of the thermalization mechanism.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid hydrokinetic model (HKM) with UrQMD hadronic cascade as an afterburner, using (2+1)D boost-invariant relativistic hydrodynamics with a lattice QCD-inspired equation of state matched to a hadron-resonance gas. They extract standard interferometry radii from Gaussian fits to correlation functions generated by the semi-classical event generator, then apply quantum corrections in the source rest frame using the formalism of partially coherent phases and overlap integrals of emitted wave packets, with the thermal de Broglie wavelength defining the coherence length. The corrected radii are transformed back to the longitudinally co-moving system (LCMS) for comparison with experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- For p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, the initial Gaussian energy density profile has width (rms) R=0.3 fm attributed to initial proper time tau_0=0.1 fm/c.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature is T_ch=165 MeV; the primary momentum spectrum dispersion is k=0.16 GeV/c (T=0.18 GeV) for the gamma=2.05 case and k=0.18 GeV/c for the gamma=1 case.
- For p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, the prediction uses dN_ch/d_eta=35 for centrality 0-20%, with initial transverse sizes R=0.9 fm and R=1.5 fm.
- The quantum correction factor for side and long radii is given by 4k_0^2 R_S^2/(1+4k_0^2 R_S^2), which tends to unity for large sources like A+A collisions.
- The coherence suppression parameter lambda_coh is calculated to be 0.9-0.95 for not very small multiplicities.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of SinShap (2013) that first identified the uncertainty principle's effect on partial coherence of closely located emitters, applying it to LHC p+p data from the ALICE Collaboration. It addresses the systematic overestimate of interferometry radii found in prior approaches including the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) and UrQMD, as well as pure hydrodynamic models by Bozek (2012) and Werner (2012). The results enable testing of the scaling hypothesis by Lisa (2005) and support the Landau (1953) suggestion of using relativistic hydrodynamics for high-multiplicity hadron collisions, while raising open questions about the thermalization mechanism in small systems.
