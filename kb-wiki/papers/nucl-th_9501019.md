# Correlation Effects in Nuclear Transparency

> The Glauber approximation is used to calculate the contribution of nucleon correlations in high-energy $A(e,e'N)$ reactions. When the excitation energy of the residual nucleus is small, the increase of the nuclear transparency due to correlations between the struck nucleon and the other nucleons is mostly compensated by a decrease of the transparency due to the correlations between non detected nucleons. We derive Glauber model predictions for nuclear transparency for the differential cross section when nuclear shell level excitations are measured. The role of correlations in color transparency is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleon correlations affect nuclear transparency in high-energy $A(e,e'N)$ reactions in two opposing ways, and that when the excitation energy of the residual nucleus is small (below about 50 MeV), these two effects largely cancel, reducing the net correlation effect to the level of a few percent.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber approximation to calculate the amplitude for coherent final-state interactions in exclusive $(e,e'N)$ reactions, decomposing the nuclear ground-state wave function into a Slater determinant plus Jastrow-type two-nucleon correlation functions. They derived expressions for the nuclear transparency when a specific nuclear shell level is fixed, treating correlations between the struck nucleon and spectators separately from correlations among spectator nucleons, and applied the formalism to $^{12}C$ using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock single-nucleon wave functions and realistic parameterizations of the $NN$ scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The net effect of nucleon correlations on nuclear transparency for coherent final-state interactions is at the level of "few percent" (stated for $^{12}C$ with a proton knocked out from the $s$ shell).
- The correlation length $l_c$ is defined via $l_c = -\int_0^\infty g(r) dr$, where $g(r)$ is the correlation function.
- For the uniform-density model, the ratio of correlated to uncorrelated transparency scales as $T_r/T_r^{\text{noncorr}} \approx (1 + L/\Lambda)$, where $L \equiv l_c/R$ and $\Lambda \equiv \lambda/R$ (with $\lambda$ the mean free path and $R$ the nuclear radius).
- For coherent final-state interactions, the transparency scales as $T_r^{\text{coh}} \sim A^{-2/3}$ in the uniform-density model.
- The quantum diffusion model uses $\Delta M^2 = 0.7\ \text{GeV}^2$ and $\langle r_t(Q^2)^2\rangle / \langle r_t^2\rangle \approx 1\ \text{GeV}^2 / Q^2$ for the color transparency calculation.
- The standard nuclear density used for the correlation function is $\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nucleon correlations in hadron-nucleus scattering by Moniz, Nixon, and Walecka (early 1970s) and by Yennie to the case of $(e,e'N)$ reactions with fixed missing energy. It addresses conflicting estimates in the literature: Benhar et al. (1992) and Lee and Miller had suggested correlations could increase the $(e,e'p)$ cross section by about 20%, while other groups (Frankfurt et al., Nikolaev, and others) estimated effects no larger than 5%. The present calculation resolves this discrepancy by showing that the two competing correlation mechanisms nearly cancel for coherent final-state interactions. The results provide a baseline Glauber prediction for interpreting color transparency experiments at facilities such as CEBAF and HERMES, and the authors note that fixing the final nuclear shell level makes $(e,e'p)$ reactions more sensitive to color coherent effects than measurements summed over final states.
