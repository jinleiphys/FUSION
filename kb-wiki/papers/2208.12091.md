# The high-density equation of state in heavy-ion collisions: Constraints from proton flow

> A set of different equations of state is implemented in the molecular dynamics part of a non-equilibrium transport simulation (UrQMD) of heavy-ion collisions. It is shown how different flow observables are affected by the density dependence of the equation of state. In particular, the effects of a phase transition at high density are explored, including an expected reduction in mean $m_T$. We also show that an increase in $v_2$ is characteristic for a strong softening of the equation of state. The phase transitions with a low coexistence density, $n_{\mathrm{CE}}<4 n_0$, show a distinct minimum in the slope of the directed flow as a function of the beam energy, which would be a clear experimental signal. By comparing our results with experimental data, we can exclude any strong phase transition at densities below $4n_0$.

## Key claim

By comparing transport simulations with experimental data, the authors exclude any strong phase transition at baryon densities below 4 times nuclear saturation density (4n0), while phase transitions with a low coexistence density (nCE < 4n0) produce a distinct minimum in the slope of directed flow as a function of beam energy that would serve as a clear experimental signal.

## Method

The authors implemented a set of different equations of state (Skyrme hard and soft, Vector Density Functional with three parametrizations, and Chiral Mean Field with two phase transition scenarios) into the molecular dynamics part of the non-equilibrium transport simulation UrQMD. They calculated the density-dependent average field energy per baryon and its derivative for each EoS, then solved the equations of motion for baryons in Au-Au collisions at beam energies from sqrt(sNN) = 2 to 10 GeV. The resulting proton flow observables (mean transverse kinetic energy, elliptic flow v2, and directed flow slope) were compared across EoSs and against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density n0 = 0.160 fm^-3 and binding energy E0 = -16.3 MeV used for all VDF parametrizations
- VDF1: critical density of QGP-like transition n_c^(Q) = 3.0 n0, critical temperature T_c^(Q) = 100 MeV, incompressibility K0 = 261 MeV
- VDF2: n_c^(Q) = 4.0 n0, T_c^(Q) = 50 MeV, K0 = 279 MeV
- VDF3: n_c^(Q) = 6.0 n0, T_c^(Q) = 50 MeV, K0 = 356 MeV
- CMF model: incompressibility K0 = 267 MeV, asymmetry energy S0 = 31.9 MeV
- CMF PT1: cut density n_B^cut = 2.1 n0, shift Delta n_B = 2.0 n0
- CMF PT2: cut density n_B^cut = 2.6 n0, shift Delta n_B = 3.0 n0
- Skyrme hard EoS: incompressibility K0 = 380 MeV
- Skyrme soft EoS: incompressibility K0 = 200 MeV
- Time step for molecular dynamics evolution: Delta t = 0.2 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD equation of state at high baryon density, where lattice QCD calculations are unavailable due to the fermion sign problem (deForcrand, 2009) and expansion schemes to finite density have limited reliability (Fodor, 2002; Allton, 2002). The authors build on earlier predictions that a phase transition would produce a stalled radial expansion (Van Hove, 1982) and a minimum in directed flow slope (Stoecker, 1986; Brachmann, 1999), but note that these signals were often washed out in hybrid model approaches (Steinheimer, 2014). The present fully microscopic transport study enables systematic comparison of different EoSs with experimental flow data from RHIC-BES and future SIS18/SIS100 facilities, and complements constraints from neutron star observations (Tolman, 1939; Oppenheimer, 1939; LIGO collaboration).
