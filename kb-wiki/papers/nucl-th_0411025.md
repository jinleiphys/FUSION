# Neutrino Physics and Nuclear Axial Two-Body Interactions

> We consider the counter-term describing isoscalar axial two-body currents in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, L1A, in the effective field theory approach. We determine this quantity using the solar neutrino data. We investigate the variation of L1A when different sets of data are used.

## Key claim

The isoscalar axial two-body counter-term \(L_{1A}\) in the nucleon-nucleon effective field theory can be constrained using solar neutrino data, and the best-fit value is around 5 fm\(^3\) with a one-sigma error of 5 fm\(^3\), though the extracted value varies between 4 and 8 fm\(^3\) depending on which data sets are included.

## Method

The authors extracted \(L_{1A}\) by fitting the neutrino-deuteron breakup cross sections \(\sigma(E) = \sigma_0(E) + L_{1A} \sigma_1(E)\) to solar and reactor neutrino data, using the MSW survival probabilities calculated with the Standard Solar Model of Bahcall and collaborators. They performed a \(\chi^2\) analysis marginalized over the neutrino parameters \(\delta m_{12}^2\) and \(\theta_{12}\), using 93 data points from the Homestake chlorine experiment, SAGE, GALLEX, GNO, SuperKamiokande zenith-angle-spectrum, SNO day-night-spectrum, SNO neutral-current flux with dissolved salt, and the KamLAND spectrum. The mixing angle \(\theta_{13}\) was taken to be zero for the main analysis, and its effects were explored separately.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit value of \(L_{1A}\) around 5 fm\(^3\) from global fit to solar and reactor neutrino data.
- \(L_{1A}\) values change between 4 and 8 fm\(^3\) with a one-sigma error of 5 fm\(^3\).
- Naive dimensional analysis predicts \(|L_{1A}| \sim 6\) fm\(^3\) when the renormalization scale is set to the muon mass.
- Chen, Heeger, and Robertson found \(L_{1A} = 4.0 \pm 6.3\) fm\(^3\) using SNO and SK data.
- State-of-the-art \(pp\) fusion cross section calculations imply \(L_{1A} = 4.2 \pm 2.4\) fm\(^3\) in third order of power counting.
- Helioseismology gives \(L_{1A} = 4.8 \pm 6.7\) fm\(^3\) in third order.
- Reactor antineutrino-deuteron breakup data yields \(L_{1A} = 3.6 \pm 5.5\) fm\(^3\).
- CHOOZ and Palo Verde experiments limit \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13} < 0.19\) at 90% C.L. for \(\delta m^2_{\rm atmos} = 0.002\) eV\(^2\).
- K2K experiment provides a limit of \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13} < 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to neutrino-deuteron reactions, building on the framework developed by Kaplan (1998) and the calculation of two-body counter-terms by Butler et al. (1999, 2000) and Ando et al. (2002). The extracted \(L_{1A}\) values are comparable to those from traditional nuclear physics calculations by Ying et al. (1991), Doi et al., and Nakamura et al. (2000), as well as to helioseismic constraints from Brown et al. (2002). The results enable improved calculations of the \(pp\) fusion cross section (reaction \(p + p \rightarrow d + \nu_e + e^+\)), which is impossible to measure directly at solar-core energies, and demonstrate that the uncertainty in \(L_{1A}\) has a smaller effect on SNO data interpretation than a maximal \(\theta_{13}\) or solar density fluctuations.
