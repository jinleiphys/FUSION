# Effects of symmetry energy on the radius and tidal deformability of neutron stars in relativistic mean-field model

> The radii and tidal deformabilities of neutron stars are investigated in the framework of relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with different density-dependent behaviors of symmetry energy. To study the effects of symmetry energy on the properties of neutron stars, an $ω$ meson and $ρ$ meson coupling term is included in a popular RMF Lagrangian, i.e. the TM1 parameter set, which is used for the widely used supernova equation of state (EoS) table. The coupling constants relevant to the vector-isovector meson, $ρ$, are refitted by a fixed symmetry energy at subsaturation density and its slope at saturation density, while other coupling constants remain the same as the original ones in TM1 so as to update the supernova EoS table. The radius and mass of maximum neutron stars are not so sensitive to the symmetry energy in these family TM1 parameterizations. However, the radii at intermediate mass region are strongly correlated with the slope of symmetry energy. Furthermore, the dimensionless tidal deformabilities of neutron stars are also calculated within the associated Love number. We find that its value at $1.4 M_\odot$ has a linear correlation to the slope of symmetry energy being different from the previous studied. With the latest constraints of tidal deformabilities from GW170817 event, the slope of symmetry energy at nuclear saturation density should be smaller than $60$ MeV in the family TM1 parameterizations. This fact supports the usage of lower symmetry energy slope for the update supernova EoS, which is applicable to simulations of neutron star merger. Furthermore, the analogous analysis are also done within the family IUFSU parameter sets. It is found that the correlations between the symmetry energy slope with the radius and tidal deformability at $1.4 M_\odot$ have very similar linear relations in these RMF models.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the slope of symmetry energy at saturation density should be smaller than 60 MeV in the family TM1 parameterizations to satisfy the tidal deformability constraints from the GW170817 event, supporting the use of a lower symmetry energy slope for updating the supernova equation of state table.

## Method

The authors constructed a family of relativistic mean-field (RMF) parameter sets based on the TM1 interaction by introducing an omega-rho meson coupling term, refitting the rho-meson coupling constants to fix the symmetry energy at subsaturation density (0.11 fm^-3) to 28.05 MeV while varying the slope of symmetry energy L from 40 to 110.8 MeV. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations with these equations of state to compute neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities, and also performed analogous calculations with the family IUFSU parameter sets to check model dependence.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at subsaturation density (rho_B = 0.11 fm^-3) was fixed to 28.05 MeV for all family TM1 parameter sets
- The original TM1 parameter set has L = 110.8 MeV; family sets were constructed with L = 40, 50, ..., 100 MeV
- Maximum neutron star masses range from 2.12 to 2.18 solar masses across the family TM1 sets
- Radius at 1.4 solar masses decreases from 14.20 km (L=110.8 MeV) to 12.86 km (L=40 MeV)
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses is 496 for L=40 MeV and 1045 for L=110.8 MeV
- The linear relation between radius at 1.4 solar masses and L is R_1.4 = 12.091 + 0.0183L with correlation coefficient 0.991
- The linear relation between tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses and L is Lambda_1.4 = 208.23 + 7.16L with correlation coefficient 0.982
- The constraint Lambda_1.4 < 800 from GW170817 corresponds to L < 80 MeV; the 90% credible level from updated analysis requires L < 60 MeV
- The direct Urca process threshold occurs at Y_p = 0.1298 at rho = 0.210 fm^-3 for L=110.8 MeV and at Y_p = 0.1389 at rho = 0.669 fm^-3 for L=40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the family TM1 parameterizations previously developed by Bao et al. (2014) for studying crust properties, applying them to neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities. It directly addresses the need to update the widely used Shen equation of state table (Shen et al., 1998, 2011), which employs the original TM1 parameter set with large symmetry energy slope, to be consistent with constraints from the GW170817 event (Abbott et al., 2017). The linear correlations found between symmetry energy slope and both radius and tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses provide a systematic framework for choosing an improved interaction for supernova and neutron star merger simulations, and the similar linear relations found in the IUFSU family suggest these correlations are not strongly model-dependent.
