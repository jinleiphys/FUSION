# Elementary excitations in homogeneous neutron star matter

> We study the collective density modes which can affect neutron-star thermodynamics in the baryonic density range between nuclear saturation ($ρ_0$) and $3ρ_0$. In this region, the expected constituents of neutron-star matter are mainly neutrons, protons and electrons ($npe$ matter), under the constraint of beta equilibrium. The elementary excitations of this $npe$ medium are studied in the RPA framework. We emphasize the effect of Coulomb interaction, in particular the electron screening of the proton plasmon mode. For the treatment of the nuclear interaction, we compare two modern Skyrme forces and a microscopic approach. The importance of the nucleon effective mass is observed.

## Key claim

The paper studies collective density modes in homogeneous neutron star matter between nuclear saturation density and three times that density, finding that the electron-proton Coulomb coupling suppresses the proton plasmon mode into a soundlike mode due to electron screening, while the nuclear interaction model choice (Skyrme forces versus microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock) significantly affects the excitation spectrum through differences in effective masses and residual interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the random-phase approximation (RPA) equations for a three-fluid system of neutrons, protons, and electrons in beta equilibrium, using a nonrelativistic Lindhard function for nucleons and an ultrarelativistic Vlasov (semiclassical) approximation for electrons. They compare results from two modern Skyrme forces (SLy230a and NRAPR) with a microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach using the Argonne v18 two-body interaction plus Urbana IX three-body force, extracting the residual interaction in the Landau monopolar approximation from the energy-density curvature or single-particle potential derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density range studied: between saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and 3ρ₀
- Proton fraction at saturation density: 3.7% (from microscopic calculation)
- Proton fraction at 2ρ₀: 8.6%; at 3ρ₀: 13.0%
- Electron plasmon frequency at zero momentum at saturation density: 6.15 MeV
- Electron plasmon frequency at 2ρ₀: 10.25 MeV; at 3ρ₀: 13.48 MeV
- Screening wave number q_c satisfies q_c² = 3(ω₀ₑ/V_Fe)², with q_c much smaller than the proton Fermi momentum k_Fp
- Energy-weighted sum rules satisfied with accuracy better than 10⁻³
- Neutron effective mass from SLy230a at saturation density: 0.544 m; from NRAPR: 0.806 m; from microscopic: 0.926 m
- Proton effective mass from SLy230a at saturation density: 0.969 m; from NRAPR: 0.610 m; from microscopic: 0.856 m

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective modes in asymmetric nuclear matter (Haensel 1978, Matera 1994, Greco 2003) and relativistic mean-field studies of neutron star matter (Providencia 2006) by including dynamical electron screening and comparing multiple nuclear interaction models. The results provide the excitation spectra and strength functions needed for calculating neutrino emissivity and mean free paths in neutron star interiors, with the authors noting that pairing effects and neutrino processes are left to future work.
