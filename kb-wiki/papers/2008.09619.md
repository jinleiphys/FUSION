# Distribution Amplitudes of Heavy Mesons and Quarkonia on the Light Front

> The ladder kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation is amended by introducing a different flavor dependence of the dressing functions in the heavy-quark sector. Compared with earlier work this allows for the simultaneous calculation of the mass spectrum and leptonic decay constants of light pseudoscalar mesons, the $D_u$, $D_s$, $B_u$, $B_s$ and $B_c$ mesons and the heavy quarkonia $η_c$ and $η_b$ within the same framework at a physical pion mass. The corresponding Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are projected onto the light front and we reconstruct the distribution amplitudes of the mesons in the full theory. A comparison with the first inverse moment of the heavy meson distribution amplitude in heavy quark effective theory is made.

## Key claim

The authors extend the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation by introducing a flavor-dependent infrared interaction kernel, which allows them to simultaneously compute masses and leptonic decay constants of light, heavy, and heavy–light pseudoscalar mesons at a physical pion mass, a result not achieved in earlier work with a flavor-independent kernel.

## Method

The dressed quark propagators for each flavor are obtained by solving the Dyson–Schwinger equation on the complex plane using Cauchy’s theorem, with a flavor-dependent effective interaction model that combines an infrared Gaussian term and a perturbative running coupling. The homogeneous Bethe–Salpeter equation is then solved in a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder truncation, using an averaged interaction kernel for the two valence quarks. The resulting Bethe–Salpeter amplitudes are projected onto the light front to reconstruct the leading-twist distribution amplitudes of the mesons.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 0.140 GeV (used as input to fix light-quark parameters).
- Mass of η_b: 9.392 GeV (used as input to fix bottom-quark parameters).
- Light-quark current masses at μ = 2 GeV: m_u = m_d = 0.018 GeV, m_s = 0.166 GeV.
- Charm-quark current mass at μ = 2 GeV: m_c = 1.272 GeV.
- Bottom-quark current mass at μ = 2 GeV: m_b = 4.370 GeV.
- Euclidean constituent quark masses: M^E_u = 0.408 GeV, M^E_s = 0.562 GeV, M^E_c = 1.342 GeV, M^E_b = 4.259 GeV.
- Interaction parameters: ω_u = ω_s = 0.50 GeV, κ_u = κ_s = 0.80 GeV; ω_c = 0.70 GeV, κ_c = 0.60 GeV; ω_b = 0.64 GeV, κ_b = 0.56 GeV.
- Variation between decay constants f_D and f_B obtained from the Bethe–Salpeter wave function and from the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation is about 3%.

## Context

This work extends earlier continuum studies of heavy–light mesons and quarkonia (Rojas 2014, El-Bennich 2016, Mojica 2017, Bedolla 2015, Raya 2017, Fischer 2014, Hilger 2017) by introducing a flavor-dependent interaction in the ladder kernel, motivated by the different dressing of the quark–gluon vertex for light versus heavy quarks. The approach enables a unified description of the mass spectrum and decay constants of π, K, D, D_s, B, B_s, B_c, η_c, and η_b at a physical pion mass, and provides light-front distribution amplitudes that can be compared with heavy-quark effective theory predictions for the first inverse moment.
