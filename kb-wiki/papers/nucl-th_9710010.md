# Central Coulomb Effects on Pion Interferometry

> Using a simple final-state rescattering model coupled with a simple Coulomb model, the effects of the central Coulomb potential on pion interferometry measurements in 158 GeV/nucleon Pb+Pb collisions are explored. Although the central Coulomb potential does not introduce correlations between pions, it does prevent an accurate measurement of the momentum difference. This momentum difference smearing effect leads to a reduction in the measured correlation radii and lambda parameters. These distortions are important in 158 GeV/nucleon Pb+Pb collisions because of large source sizes and the strong central Coulomb potential.

## Key claim

The central Coulomb potential in 158 GeV/nucleon Pb+Pb collisions does not introduce correlations between pions, but it prevents an accurate measurement of the momentum difference; this momentum difference smearing effect leads to a reduction in the measured correlation radii and lambda parameters, with the effect being most important for low-transverse-momentum pions due to large source sizes and the strong central Coulomb potential.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo cascade rescattering model that assumes strong binary collisions between hadrons (including pions, kaons, nucleons, and resonances such as rho, omega, eta, eta-prime, phi, Delta, and K*) to generate freeze-out positions and momenta for pions in 158 GeV/nucleon Pb+Pb collisions. Coulomb interactions were added after freeze-out using an analytic classical Coulomb potential with an average value V_s extracted from fitting the NA44 single-particle pi-/pi+ ratio data. The correlation functions were then calculated in the LCMS frame using the procedure described in Humanic (1994, 1996), with the directional dependence of the Coulomb shift added by assuming the central Coulomb charge is spherically symmetric.

## Key numbers

- The average Coulomb potential V_s extracted from the NA44 single particle data is approximately 5 MeV (a lower limit since the data was measured over rapidity range 3.1 < y < 4.1 while the formula is the mid-rapidity limit y=2.9).
- For negative pion pairs (V_s = -5 MeV), the mean momentum difference shifts are: Delta Q_{t_s} = -2.4 MeV, Delta Q_{t_o} = -1.6 MeV, Delta Q_l = -1.3 MeV; the RMS deviations are: 6.4 MeV, 6.0 MeV, and 5.0 MeV respectively.
- For positive pion pairs (V_s = 5 MeV), the mean momentum difference shifts are: Delta Q_{t_s} = 2.5 MeV, Delta Q_{t_o} = 1.7 MeV, Delta Q_l = 1.5 MeV; the RMS deviations are: 6.3 MeV, 5.9 MeV, and 4.8 MeV respectively.
- Without Coulomb potential (V_s = 0): R_{t_s} = 5.55 +/- 0.12 fm, R_{t_o} = 6.37 +/- 0.26 fm, R_l = 8.13 +/- 0.63 fm, lambda = 0.63 +/- 0.03.
- With negative Coulomb potential (V_s = -5 MeV, pi-pi-): R_{t_s} = 5.53 +/- 0.09 fm, R_{t_o} = 6.17 +/- 0.06 fm, R_l = 6.73 +/- 0.13 fm, lambda = 0.54 +/- 0.01.
- With positive Coulomb potential (V_s = 5 MeV, pi+pi+): R_{t_s} = 5.33 +/- 0.10 fm, R_{t_o} = 6.14 +/- 0.16 fm, R_l = 7.38 +/- 0.22 fm, lambda = 0.62 +/- 0.02.
- The rapidity and p_T range for the correlation function calculations are 0 < p_T < 400 MeV/c and 3.1 < y < 4.1, with mean p_T approximately 150 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the analytical Coulomb treatment of Baym and Braun-Munzinger (1996) by explicitly including the directional dependence of the Coulomb shift and demonstrating that the momentum difference smearing effect (characterized by RMS deviations) is more important than the net momentum difference shift. It contradicts the qualitative prediction of Barz (1996) that negatively charged pairs should measure a source size larger than the true source size; instead, both positive and negative pairs measure a smaller source size due to the smearing effect. The results are relevant for interpreting pion interferometry measurements from the NA44 collaboration and should be considered when comparing theoretical predictions to experimental data from 158 GeV/nucleon Pb+Pb collisions, with qualitative applicability to Au+Au collisions at 10.8 GeV/c where a similar pi-/pi+ ratio enhancement is observed.
