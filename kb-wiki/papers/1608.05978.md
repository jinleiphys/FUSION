# Chiral EFT based nuclear forces: Achievements and challenges

> During the past two decades, chiral effective field theory has become a popular tool to derive nuclear forces from first principles. Two-nucleon interactions have been worked out up to sixth order of chiral perturbation theory and three-nucleon forces up to fifth order. Applications of some of these forces have been conducted in nuclear few- and many-body systems---with a certain degree of success. But in spite of these achievements, we are still faced with great challenges. Among them is the issue of a proper uncertainty quantification of predictions obtained when applying these forces in {\it ab initio} calculations of nuclear structure and reactions. A related problem is the order by order convergence of the chiral expansion. We start this review with a pedagogical introduction and then present the current status of the field of chiral nuclear forces. This is followed by a discussion of representative examples for the application of chiral two- and three-body forces in the nuclear many-body system including convergence issues.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory provides a systematic framework for deriving nuclear forces from first principles, but despite successes in describing two-nucleon interactions up to sixth order and three-nucleon forces up to fifth order, the approach still faces major challenges including proper uncertainty quantification and establishing order-by-order convergence of the chiral expansion in ab initio calculations.

## Method

The authors review the chiral effective field theory (ChPT) approach to nuclear forces, starting from the QCD Lagrangian and its symmetries, then constructing effective Lagrangians using the CCWZ formalism with pions and nucleons as degrees of freedom. They present the power counting scheme that organizes contributions by powers of (Q/Λχ) where Q is a small momentum or pion mass and Λχ ≈ 1 GeV is the chiral symmetry breaking scale, and discuss how two-nucleon forces, three-nucleon forces, and contact interactions emerge at successive orders (LO, NLO, NNLO, N3LO, etc.).

## Key numbers

- Up and down quark masses in the MS scheme at μ ≈ 2 GeV: m_u = 2.3 ± 0.7 MeV, m_d = 4.8 ± 0.5 MeV
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 95 ± 5 MeV
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λχ ≈ 1 GeV, identified with the rho meson mass m_ρ = 0.78 GeV
- Pion mass sets the soft scale: Q ~ m_π
- Two-nucleon forces appear at ν = 0 (leading order), three-nucleon forces at ν = 2 (but cancel at that order), and four-nucleon forces at ν = 4
- The power counting formula for irreducible diagrams: ν = -2 + 2A - 2C + 2L + Σ_i Δ_i, where Δ_i = d_i + n_i/2 - 2

## Context

This review extends the long history of nuclear force modeling from phenomenological potentials like the Reid potential (1975) and meson-exchange models (Paris and Bonn potentials) toward a first-principles approach rooted in QCD via Weinberg's effective field theory concept (1979, 1991). The work summarizes the current status of chiral nuclear forces and their applications, while identifying open problems in uncertainty quantification and convergence that must be addressed before the approach can provide fully predictive ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions.
