# Investigation of interaction of circularly and linearly polarized photon beams with polarized 3He target

> Photodisintegration of polarized 3He by linearly or circularily polarized photons offers a rich choice of observables which can be calculated with high precision using a rigorous scheme of three-nucleon Faddeev equations. Using the (semi)phenomenological AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force we investigate sensitivity of 3He photodisintegration observables to underlying currents taken in the form of a single-nucleon current supplemented by two-body contributions for $π$- and $ρ$-meson exchanges or incorporated by the Siegert theorem. Promising observables to be measured for two- and three-body fragmentation of 3He are identified. These observables form a challenging test ground for consistent forces and currents being under derivation within the framework of chiral perturbation theory. For thre-body 3He photodisintegration several kinematicaly complete configurations, including SST and FSI, are also discussed.

## Key claim

Using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus Urbana IX three-nucleon force, the authors identify promising observables in two- and three-body photodisintegration of polarized 3He that are sensitive to the choice of electromagnetic current operator (explicit meson-exchange currents versus Siegert theorem), but the unpolarized cross section and the photon analyzing power A_x^gamma show only small sensitivity to that choice.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equations for 3He photodisintegration at photon energies of 15, 20, and 30 MeV, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. The electromagnetic current was taken either as a single-nucleon current supplemented by explicit pi- and rho-meson exchange two-body currents, or as a single-nucleon current with many-body contributions incorporated via the Siegert theorem. All possible polarization observables for two-body (proton-deuteron) and three-body (proton-proton-neutron) fragmentation were calculated, including analyzing powers and spin correlation coefficients.

## Key numbers

* Photon energies studied: E_gamma = 15, 20, and 30 MeV.
* For two-body photodisintegration at E_gamma = 30 MeV, the 3He analyzing power A^{3He}_y reaches a value of approximately 0.2.
* For two-body photodisintegration, changing the photon energy from 15 MeV to 30 MeV diminishes the unpolarized cross section by a factor of approximately 3.
* For semi-inclusive three-body photodisintegration, changing the photon energy from 15 to 30 MeV reduces the unpolarized cross section by a factor of approximately 4.
* For the symmetric space-star (SST) configuration in three-body photodisintegration, the cross section drops from approximately 2 mb/(sr^2 MeV) at E_gamma = 15 MeV to approximately 0.3 mb/(sr^2 MeV) at E_gamma = 30 MeV.
* For the final-state interaction (FSI) configuration, increasing the photon energy from 15 to 30 MeV diminishes the cross section by a factor of approximately 2.
* The initial photon laboratory energies correspond to center-of-mass kinetic energies of the ppn system of 7.2, 12.2, and 22.1 MeV for E_gamma = 15, 20, and 30 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous Faddeev calculations of 3He photodisintegration (reviewed in Phys. Rep. 2005) by systematically investigating the full set of polarization observables accessible with modern polarized photon beams and polarized 3He targets. The results are intended to guide experiments that will test consistent chiral forces and currents currently being derived within chiral perturbation theory (Skibinski 2003, Pastore 2008, Krebs 2017). The identification of the symmetric space-star (SST) configuration in three-body photodisintegration is motivated by unresolved discrepancies between theory and data for the corresponding nucleon-deuteron breakup SST geometry (Koln 2013, Sagara 2012).
