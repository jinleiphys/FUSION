# Towards Reconstructing the Final Stage of Heavy Ion Collisions

> A Fourier inversion problem lies at the heart of determining spatio-temporal characteristica of the final stage of a heavy ion collision: From the measured two-particle momentum correlations C(p_1,p_2) of identical particles, pions say, a Hanbury-Brown /Twiss (HBT) interferometric analysis aims at extracting as much information as possible about the Wigner phase space density S(x,p) of pion emitting sources in the collision region. Here, we discuss how this analysis allows to separate the effects of temperature and transverse flow which cannot be disentangled completely on the basis of single-particle spectra.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while single-particle spectra cannot separate temperature from transverse flow effects, two-particle HBT correlations can disentangle them, but only if resonance decay contributions are properly accounted for and if deviations from a Gaussian correlation shape are quantified via higher-order q-moments rather than relying on standard Gaussian fits.

## Method

The author constructs a model pion emission function that includes direct thermal pions and pions from resonance decays (ρ, Δ, K*, Σ*, ω, η, η', K_S^0, Σ, Λ with branching ratios >5%). The model assumes local thermalization at freeze-out with a Boltzmann factor containing temperature T and collective flow velocity u(x), plus Gaussian spatial and temporal profiles. Two-particle correlation functions are computed numerically from this emission function, and the resulting HBT radii are extracted using both standard Gaussian fits and a set of shape-independent q-variances (second and fourth q-moments) that quantify non-Gaussianity via a kurtosis parameter.

## Key numbers

- Transverse flow parameter values considered: η_f = 0 (no flow) and η_f = 0.3 (with flow)
- The approximate expression for the side radius: R_s²(K_⊥) ≈ R² / [1 + (m_⊥/T) η_f²], where the m_⊥-dependence is governed by the prefactor η_f²/T
- The kurtosis Δ_i = ⟨⟨q_i⁴⟩⟩ / (3 ⟨⟨q_i²⟩⟩²) - 1 is proposed as a quantitative measure of deviations from Gaussian shape
- Gaussian widths R, Δη, τ_0, and Δτ parameterize the spatial and temporal extent of the source in the emission function

## Context

This work extends the standard HBT interferometric analysis framework (S. Pratt 1986, G. Bertsch 1994) by incorporating resonance decay contributions that were previously neglected. It directly addresses the degeneracy between temperature and transverse flow in single-particle spectra, a problem noted in earlier analyses of NA49 data (Foka 1996). The paper enables future data-driven extraction of source parameters from NA49 two-particle correlations by providing a quantitative method to distinguish flow from resonance lifetime effects using non-Gaussianity measures.
