# Strange sea distributions of the nucleon

> The strange and antistrange quark distributions of the nucleon are less constrained by experimental data than the non-strange quark sea. The combination of light quark sea distributions, $Δ(x)=\dbar(x)+\ubar(x)-s(x)-\sbar(x)$, originates mainly from non-perturbative processes and can be calculated using non-perturbative models of the nucleon. We have calculated $Δ(x)$ using the meson cloud model, which, when combined with the relatively well known non-strange light antiquark distributions obtained from global analysis of available experimental data, enables us to make new estimates of the total strange sea distributions of the nucleon and the strange sea suppression factor.

## Key claim

The strange sea distributions of the nucleon can be estimated by combining a meson cloud model (MCM) calculation of the difference between non-strange and strange sea distributions, Δ(x) = d̄(x) + ū(x) − s(x) − s̄(x), with well-known non-strange antiquark distributions from global fits, under the assumption that perturbative gluon splitting is SU(3) flavour symmetric and thus does not contribute to Δ(x).

## Method

The authors calculated Δ(x) using time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame within the meson cloud model, considering Fock states involving pseudoscalar and vector meson octets (π, ρ, ω, K, K*) and baryon octet and decuplet (N, Δ, Λ, Σ). They combined this Δ(x) with the non-strange light antiquark distribution (d̄ + ū) from the CTEQ6.6M global PDF set to extract the total strange sea distribution S⁺(x) = s(x) + s̄(x) and the strange sea suppression factor r(x) = (s + s̄)/(d̄ + ū). Parton distributions for the mesons and baryons in the Fock states were taken from the Glück-Reya-Schienbein parameterization for pions and from a variant of the MIT bag model for baryons, all evolved to Q² = 2.5 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The cut-off parameters for the form factors in the MCM are Λ_oct = 0.8 GeV for fluctuations involving octet baryons and Λ_dec = 1.0 GeV for decuplet baryons.
- The model scale for the bag model parton distributions is μ₀² = 0.23 GeV², evolved to Q² = 2.5 GeV² for comparison with data.
- The coupling constants used include g_{Nπ/N} = 13.07, g_{ΛK/N} = −13.98, g_{ΣK/N} = 2.69, f_{Δπ/N} = 12.43 GeV⁻¹, and f_{Δρ/N} = 20.82 GeV⁻¹.
- The CTEQ6.5 PDF set required the momentum fraction carried by the strange sea to be in the range 0.018 → 0.040 at 90% confidence.
- The MCM calculation for x(s + s̄) becomes negative for x > 0.25, indicating unreliability in that region.

## Context

This work extends previous MCM calculations of Δ(x) by Kumano (1991) and Carvalho (2000) by using time-ordered perturbation theory to avoid ambiguities with off-mass-shell structure functions, and by including vector meson Fock states (ρ, ω, K*) in addition to pseudoscalar mesons. The results agree with the HERMES (2008) measurements and the CTEQ6.6M PDF set for x < 0.07, but are larger than the NuTeV NLO analysis (Mason, 2007) and the CTEQ6.5 and MSTW2008 global fits, suggesting that the suppression of the strange sea relative to the non-strange light antiquark sea may be smaller than previously believed. The calculation could be extended to helicity-dependent strange distributions, though current uncertainties in helicity-dependent non-strange sea data may limit the precision.
