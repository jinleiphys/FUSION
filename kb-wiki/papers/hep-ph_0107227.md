# Color Transparency versus Quantum Coherence in Electroproduction of Vector Mesons off Nuclei

> So far no theoretical tool for the comprehensive description of exclusive electroproduction of vector mesons off nuclei at medium energies has been developed. We suggest a light-cone QCD formalism which is valid at any energy and incorporates formation effects (color transparency), the coherence length and the gluon shadowing. At medium energies color transparency (CT) and the onset of coherence length (CL) effects are not easily separated. Indeed, although nuclear transparency measured by the HERMES experiment rises with Q^2, it agrees with predictions of the vector dominance model (VDM) without any CT effects. Our new results and observations are: (i) the good agreement with the VDM found earlier is accidental and related to the specific correlation between Q^2 and CL for HERMES kinematics; (ii) CT effects are much larger than have been estimated earlier within the two channel approximation. They are even stronger at low than at high energies and can be easily identified by HERMES or at JLab; (iii) gluon shadowing which is important at high energies is calculated and included; (iv) our parameter-free calculations explain well available data for variation of nuclear transparency with virtuality and energy of the photon; (v) predictions for electroproduction of ρand ϕare provided for future measurements at HERMES and JLab.

## Key claim

The apparent agreement between HERMES nuclear transparency data and the vector dominance model (VDM) without color transparency is accidental, arising from a specific correlation between photon virtuality \(Q^2\) and coherence length in HERMES kinematics; when coherence length is held constant, color transparency effects are much larger than previously estimated in the two-channel approximation and can be clearly identified at HERMES or JLab.

## Method

The authors develop a light-cone QCD formalism for exclusive electroproduction of vector mesons off nuclei that incorporates color transparency via a Green function approach for the \(\bar qq\) pair propagation, the coherence length effect, and gluon shadowing. The dipole cross section for \(\bar qq\)-nucleon interaction is parameterized to reproduce hadronic cross sections and DIS data up to \(Q^2 \approx 10\ \text{GeV}^2\), and the light-cone wave functions of the photon and vector meson include a nonperturbative \(\bar qq\) interaction via an oscillator potential. The formalism is applied to both incoherent and coherent production, with absorption included via an imaginary part in the light-cone Schrödinger equation for the Green function.

## Key numbers

- The dipole cross section parameterization uses \(\sigma_0(s) = \sigma^{\pi p}_{tot}(s) \left[1 + \frac{3}{8} \frac{r_0^2(s)}{\langle r^2_{ch}\rangle}\right]\ \text{mb}\), with \(\langle r^2_{ch}\rangle = 0.44\ \text{fm}^2\) and \(r_0(s) = 0.88 \left(\frac{s}{s_0}\right)^{-0.14}\ \text{fm}\) for \(s_0 = 1000\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The pion-proton total cross section is fitted as \(\sigma^{\pi p}_{tot}(s) = 23.6 \left(\frac{s}{s_0}\right)^{0.079} + 0.032 \left(\frac{s}{s_0}\right)^{-0.45}\ \text{mb}\).
- The meson wave function uses parameters \(R = 0.59\ \text{fm}\) and quark mass \(m_q = 0.15\ \text{GeV}\).
- The light-cone potential parameters satisfy \(a_0^2 = v^{1.15} (0.112)^2\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(a_1^2 = (1-v)^{1.15} (0.165)^2\ \text{GeV}^2\) for any \(0 < v < 1\), with results varying by only \(1\%\).
- The formalism is valid for \(Q^2 \lesssim 10\ \text{GeV}^2\) for light vector mesons.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone dipole approach of Kopeliovich and collaborators (1991) and the hadronic basis description of Hufner and Kopeliovich (1997) by providing a unified formalism valid at any energy that simultaneously accounts for color transparency, coherence length effects, and gluon shadowing. It contradicts the earlier conclusion that HERMES data agree with VDM without color transparency, showing that this agreement is accidental. The results enable future experiments at HERMES and JLab to isolate color transparency effects by mapping events at fixed coherence length, and provide parameter-free predictions for \(\rho\) and \(\phi\) electroproduction off nuclei.
