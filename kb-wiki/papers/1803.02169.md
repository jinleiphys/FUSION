# Electric structure of shallow D-wave states in Halo EFT

> We compute the electric form factors of one-neutron halo nuclei with shallow D-wave states up to next-to-leading order and the E2 transition from the S-wave to the D-wave state up to leading order in Halo Effective Field Theory (Halo EFT). The relevant degrees of freedom are the core and the halo neutron. The EFT expansion is carried out in powers of $R_{core}/R_{halo}$, where $R_{core}$ and $R_{halo}$ denote the length scales of the core and the halo, respectively. We propose a power counting scenario for weakly-bound states in one-neutron Halo EFT and discuss its implications for higher partial waves in terms of universality. The scenario is applied to the $\frac{5}{2}^+$ first excited state and the $\frac{1}{2}^+$ ground state of $^{15}\text{C}$. We obtain several universal correlations between electric observables and use data for the E2 transition $\frac{5}{2}^+\to \frac{1}{2}^+$ together with ab initio results from the No-Core Shell Model to predict the quadrupole moment.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a power counting scenario for shallow D-wave states in one-neutron Halo EFT that requires only one fine-tuned combination of coupling constants, in contrast to alternative scenarios that require two fine tunings; this scenario is then applied to the 5/2+ first excited state and 1/2+ ground state of 15C, with the claim that the minimal fine-tuning assumption is more compatible with experimental data for this nucleus.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic Halo EFT Lagrangian with core and halo neutron degrees of freedom, introducing auxiliary dimer fields for both S-wave and D-wave interactions. They compute the dressed D-wave propagator by summing bubble diagrams using a momentum cutoff regularization to identify all divergences, then switch to dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS). The electric form factors are computed up to next-to-leading order and the E2 transition strength from the S-wave to D-wave state up to leading order, with local gauge-invariant operators added where needed for renormalization.

## Key numbers

- The D-wave self-energy contains divergences scaling as Lambda^5, Lambda^3, and Lambda^1, requiring three effective range parameters (a_2, r_2, P_2) for renormalization at leading order
- The wave function renormalization constant for the D-wave state at LO is Z_d = -15pi/(m_R^2 g_2^2) * 1/(r_2 + P_2 gamma_2^2)
- The LO B(E2: 1/2+ -> 5/2+) transition strength is given by (4/(5 pi)) * (Z_eff^2 e^2 gamma_0)/(-r_2 - P_2 gamma_2^2) * [(3gamma_0^2 + 9gamma_0 gamma_2 + 8gamma_2^2)/(gamma_0 + gamma_2)^3]^2
- The effective charge for arbitrary multipolarity lambda is Z_eff^(lambda) = Z_n (M/M_nc)^lambda + Z_c (-m_n/M_nc)^lambda
- For the D-wave state, the scaling relations are a_2 ~ R_halo^4 R_core, r_2 ~ 1/(R_halo^2 R_core), and P_2 ~ 1/R_core

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT formalism for electric properties from P-wave systems (hammer2011electric) to shallow D-wave systems, following the approach used for 11Be. The power counting scenario builds on arguments by Bedaque (2003) about minimal fine tuning in higher partial waves, but differs from that work beyond P-waves by counting higher effective range parameters differently. The results are applied to 15C and combined with data for the E2 transition strength and ab initio results from the Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model (IT-NCSM) by Roth (2009) to predict the quadrupole moment, with comparisons to rotational model correlations by Calci (2016).
