# Dilepton and photon production in the presence of a nontrivial Polyakov loop

> We calculate the production of dileptons and photons in the presence of a nontrivial Polyakov loop in QCD. This is applicable to the semi-Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), at temperatures above but near the critical temperature for deconfinement. The Polyakov loop is small in the semi-QGP, and near unity in the perturbative QGP. Working to leading order in the coupling constant of QCD, we find that there is a mild enhancement, ~ 20%, for dilepton production in the semi-QGP over that in the perturbative QGP. In contrast, we find that photon production is strongly suppressed in the semi-QGP, by about an order of magnitude, relative to the perturbative QGP. In the perturbative QGP photon production contains contributions from 2->2 scattering and collinear emission with the Landau- Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. In the semi-QGP we show that the two contributions are modified differently. The rate for 2->2 scattering is suppressed by a factor which depends upon the Polyakov loop. In contrast, in an SU(N) gauge theory the collinear rate is suppressed by 1/N, so that the LPM effect vanishes at infinite N. To leading order in the semi-QGP at large N, we compute the rate from 2->2 scattering to the leading logarithmic order and the collinear rate to leading order.

## Key claim
Dilepton production is mildly enhanced (~20%) in the semi-Quark Gluon Plasma (semi-QGP) relative to the perturbative QGP, while photon production is strongly suppressed by about an order of magnitude; these results hold to leading order in the QCD coupling constant and depend on the value of the Polyakov loop, which is small near the critical temperature for deconfinement.

## Method
The authors compute dilepton and photon production rates in the presence of a nontrivial Polyakov loop by working in a matrix model of the semi-QGP, where the temporal component of the gluon field is treated as a constant, diagonal background. They perform analytic continuation from Euclidean to Minkowski spacetime and evaluate the rates to leading order in the QCD coupling, using the double line basis for color factors and incorporating the Polyakov loop dependence into modified statistical distribution functions for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons.

## Key numbers
- Dilepton production in the semi-QGP shows a mild enhancement of ~20% over the perturbative QGP at low temperatures T ~ 300 MeV for back-to-back dileptons with total energy E = 1 GeV (three colors).
- Photon production in the semi-QGP is suppressed by about an order of magnitude relative to the perturbative QGP.
- The Polyakov loop from lattice QCD is ~0.1 at the pseudo-critical temperature T_c ~ 170 MeV.
- In the confined phase at infinite N_c, the ratio of dilepton production to the perturbative QGP for back-to-back dileptons at low energy (E << T) scales as 4T/E.

## Context
This work extends the matrix model approach to the semi-QGP developed by Pisarski (2006), Hidaka (2008-2009), and Dumitru (2010-2012) to electromagnetic probes. It contrasts with perturbative QCD calculations at high temperature (e.g., Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe 2001-2002) and addresses puzzles in heavy-ion collision data, including the enhancement of dileptons at invariant masses below the rho meson mass observed by Rapp (2013) and the unexpectedly large elliptic flow for photons seen by Adare (2011) and Lohner (2012). The results enable comparison with Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasino model calculations by Islam, Majumder, Haque, and Mustafa (2014) and with earlier work on dilepton enhancement by Lee, Wirstram, Zahed, and Hansson (1998).
