# Sequency Hierarchy Truncation (SeqHT) for Adiabatic State Preparation and Time Evolution in Quantum Simulations

> We introduce the Sequency Hierarchy Truncation (SeqHT) scheme for reducing the resources required for state preparation and time evolution in quantum simulations, based upon a truncation in sequency. For the $λϕ^4$ interaction in scalar field theory, or any interaction with a polynomial expansion, upper bounds on the contributions of operators of a given sequency are derived. For the systems we have examined, observables computed in sequency-truncated wavefunctions, including quantum correlations as measured by magic, are found to step-wise converge to their exact values with increasing cutoff sequency. The utility of SeqHT is demonstrated in the adiabatic state preparation of the $λϕ^4$ anharmonic oscillator ground state using IBM's quantum computer ${\textit ibm\_sherbrooke}$. Using SeqHT, the depth of the required quantum circuits is reduced by $\sim 30\%$, leading to significantly improved determinations of observables in the quantum simulations. More generally, SeqHT is expected to lead to a reduction in required resources for quantum simulations of systems with a hierarchy of length scales.

## Key claim

SeqHT reduces quantum circuit depth for adiabatic state preparation by truncating high-sequency operators in the Hamiltonian decomposition, with the condition that observables converge step-wise to exact values as the cutoff sequency is increased; the method is demonstrated for the $\lambda\phi^4$ anharmonic oscillator, where the depth reduction of approximately 30% is achieved while maintaining fidelity above 0.99 for the prepared ground state.

## Method

The authors decompose the digitized $\lambda\phi^4$ Hamiltonian on a single spatial site into Walsh-basis (Pauli-Z tensor product) operators indexed by sequency, the digital analog of frequency. They derive upper bounds on the sequency coefficients for polynomial interactions and truncate operators above a cutoff sequency $\nu_{\rm cut}=14$ for the $\hat{\phi}^4$ term (and $\nu_{\rm cut}=30$ for $\hat{\phi}^2$ in larger systems), then perform adiabatic state preparation by evolving from the free-theory ground state to the interacting ground state using Trotterized time evolution on IBM's ibm_sherbrooke quantum processor.

## Key numbers

- Circuit depth reduced by $\sim 30\%$ for adiabatic state preparation of the $\lambda\phi^4$ anharmonic oscillator ground state on IBM's ibm_sherbrooke quantum computer
- For five qubits with $\phi_{\rm max}=4$ and $\lambda=10$, the SeqHT Hamiltonian eigenvalues deviate from the full Hamiltonian eigenvalues at the $5\%$ level
- Adiabatic state preparation fidelity of $0.9999$ achieved for $\lambda=10$ with total time $t=5$ and five adiabatic steps (five qubits, $\phi_{\rm max}=4$)
- For $\lambda=60$ with same parameters, fidelity is $0.9978$
- For twelve qubits with $\phi_{\rm max}=4$ and $\lambda=10$, Trotterized SeqHT ASP achieves fidelity $0.9923$ with $\nu_{\rm cut}=14$ for $\hat{\phi}^4$ only, and $0.9897$ when $\hat{\phi}^2$ is also truncated to $\nu_{\rm cut}=30$
- For twelve qubits, the number of four-body operators for $\hat{\phi}^4$ is reduced from ${12 \choose 4}=495$ to one operator; two-body operators for $\hat{\phi}^2$ reduced from $66$ to $10$ with $\nu_{\rm cut}=14$
- Initial overlap between free theory and interacting theory ground states is $0.9729$ (for $\lambda=10$, five qubits, $\phi_{\rm max}=4$)

## Context

This work extends the foundational quantum simulation framework for $\lambda\phi^4$ theory developed by Jordan, Lee, and Preskill (2012-2017), who established adiabatic state preparation and the local quantum Fourier transform for digitization. The sequency truncation approach builds on earlier Walsh-function decompositions by Klco et al. (2021) and Kane et al. (2022), but provides rigorous upper bounds on sequency coefficients that eliminate the need to compute the full decomposition classically. SeqHT is expected to reduce quantum resources for simulating lattice gauge theories including QCD, where gauge-field wavefunctions are smooth and localized, and complements other truncation strategies such as confinement-based truncation by Farrell et al. (2023, 2024) and perturbative approaches by Cohen et al. (2021) and Davoudi et al. (2020, 2022).
