# Chiral condensates for neutron stars in hadron-quark crossover; from a parity doublet nucleon model to an NJL quark model

> In this contribution, we summarize our recent studies on the chiral invariant mass and the chiral condensates in neutron star matter. We construct a unified equations of state assuming the crossover phase transition from hadronic matter described by a parity doublet model to quark matter by an Nambu--Jona-Lasinio type quark model. We first show that the chiral invariant mass is constrained to be 600 MeV $\lesssim m_0 \lesssim$ 900 MeV from recent observations of neutron stars. We then determine the density dependence of the chiral condensate in the crossover description, and show that the chiral condensates are actually smoothly connected from the hadronic matter where the change is driven by the positive chiral scalar charge in a nucleon, to the quark matter where the change is by the modification of the quark Dirac sea, reflecting the hadron-quark crossover.

## Key claim

The chiral invariant mass of nucleons is constrained to 600 MeV ≲ m₀ ≲ 900 MeV from recent neutron star observations, implying that the majority of nucleon mass comes from the chiral invariant component rather than from chiral symmetry breaking, and that the chiral condensate evolves smoothly from hadronic to quark matter through a crossover transition.

## Method

The authors construct a unified equation of state by interpolating between a parity doublet model (PDM) for hadronic matter at baryon densities n_B ≤ 2n₀ and an Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quark model for n_B ≥ 5n₀, using a sixth-order polynomial in baryon chemical potential to connect the two regimes. The interpolation is constrained by neutron star observables (mass-radius relations from PSR J0740+6620 and tidal deformability from GW170817) and the causality condition that sound velocity must be less than light velocity. The generating functional approach is extended with external sources to compute the chiral condensate via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem, differentiating the thermodynamic potential with respect to current quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Chiral invariant mass constraint: 600 MeV ≲ m₀ ≲ 900 MeV, derived from comparing M-R relations with neutron star observations.
- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, used as the reference density for the hadronic-quark boundary at 2n₀ and 5n₀.
- Neutron star mass constraint: PSR J0740+6620 has mass 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙ and radius ≈ 12.4 ± 0.7 km, requiring stiff EOS at high density.
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: R₁.₄ ≲ 13 km for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star.

## Context

This work extends the 3-window modeling approach of Baym (2017) for neutron star equations of state, updating it from purely thermodynamic quantities to microscopic condensates. The parity doublet model builds on the work of Detar (1988) and Jido (2001), which introduced chiral invariant masses for nucleons, while the lattice simulations of Aarts (2017) showed that nucleon mass remains large even when the chiral condensate is substantially reduced. The results enable future studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensates and the role of strange quarks in neutron star cores, and suggest that further neutron star observations can more strictly constrain the chiral invariant mass.
