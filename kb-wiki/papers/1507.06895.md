# Novel technique to extract experimental symmetry free energy information of nuclear matter

> A new method of accessing information on the symmetry free energy from yields of fragments produced in Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions is proposed. Furthermore, by means of quantum fluctuation analysis techniques, correlations between extracted symmetry free-energy coefficients with temperature and density were studied. The obtained results are consistent with those of commonly used isoscaling techniques.

## Key claim

The authors propose that symmetry free energy coefficients can be extracted from fragment yields in Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions using Landau's free-energy approach, rather than the commonly used isoscaling technique. The results are consistent with isoscaling methods, but the paper notes that the density-dependent symmetry free energy values with Coulomb corrections significantly deviate from those obtained without Coulomb corrections and from other methods.

## Method

Fragment yields from 35 MeV/A beams of 64Zn, 70Zn, and 64Ni incident on matched targets were measured using the 4π NIMROD-ISiS array at Texas A&M University. The quasiprojectile source was reconstructed and events were sorted into eight excitation energy bins from 2.5 to 9.5 MeV/A. The free energy per nucleon as a function of fragment neutron-proton asymmetry was fitted with a sixth-order Landau free-energy expansion, and the symmetry free energy coefficient was extracted from the fit parameters. Temperature and density of the fragmenting source were determined independently using the quantum-fluctuation method applied to proton momentum quadrupole fluctuations, with and without Coulomb corrections.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures and densities were obtained for 8 excitation energy bins, each 1 MeV/A wide, spanning 2.5 to 9.5 MeV/A.
- At an excitation energy of 5.5 MeV/A, the Landau fit parameters (with c fixed to 115) gave a = 16.289 ± 0.024 and b = -102.871 ± 0.058, yielding F_sym/T = 1.593 ± 0.019.
- With all Landau parameters free at the same excitation energy, a = 15.527 ± 0.041, b = -91.786 ± 0.484, c = 100.81 ± 0.615, yielding F_sym/T = 1.619 ± 0.16.
- A 15% variation of the F/T value at m = -1 resulted in a 19% change of the F_sym value, giving a systematic uncertainty less than 20%.
- Coulomb corrections lowered temperature values by almost 2 MeV, with estimated errors of ±2% for densities and ±6% for temperatures.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used isoscaling techniques to extract symmetry free energy coefficients from heavy-ion collisions, such as those by the same group (PhysRevC.87.017603, 2013) and others (PhysRevLett.104.202501, 2010; PhysRevC.85.064618, 2012). The Landau free-energy approach had previously been applied to study the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (PhysRevLett.101.122702, 2008; PhysRevC.81.044618, 2010), but this is the first time it is used to determine symmetry free energy coefficients in a self-consistent manner. The results enable future work to disentangle entropic contributions and derive symmetry energy coefficients, and suggest that precise measurement of neutron distribution functions could resolve discrepancies arising from different Coulomb correction treatments.
