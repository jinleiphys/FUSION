# Systematic Analysis of Flow Distributions

> The information of the event-by-event fluctuations is extracted from flow harmonic distributions and cumulants, which can be done experimentally. In this work, we employ the standard method of Gram-Charlier series with the normal kernel to find such distribution, which is the generalization of recently introduced flow distributions for the studies of the event-by-event fluctuations. Also, we introduce a new set of cumulants $j_n\{2k\}$ which have more information about the fluctuations compared with other known cumulants. The experimental data imply that not only all of the information about the event-by-event fluctuations of collision zone properties and different stages of the heavy-ion process are not encoded in the radial flow distribution $p(v_n)$, but also the observables describing harmonic flows can generally be given by the joint distribution $\mathcal{P}(v_1,v_2,...)$. In such a way, we first introduce a set of joint cumulants $\mathcal{K}_{nm}$, and then we find the flow joint distribution using these joint cumulants. Finally, we show that the Symmetric Cumulants $SC(2,3)$ and $SC(2,4)$ obtained from ALICE data are explained by the combinations $\mathcal{K}_{22}+\frac{1}{2}\mathcal{K}_{04}-\mathcal{K}_{31}$ and $\mathcal{K}_{22}+4\mathcal{K}_{11}^2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the standard Gram-Charlier series method with a normal kernel provides a systematic technique to extract flow harmonic distributions and their cumulants from event-by-event fluctuations, and that the joint distribution \(\mathcal{P}(v_1,v_2,\ldots)\) is necessary to capture all fluctuation information because the radial distribution \(p(v_n)\) alone is insufficient.

## Method

The author employs the Gram-Charlier series expansion of the characteristic function to derive probability density functions for flow harmonics, first for odd harmonics using two-dimensional standardized cumulants and then for even harmonics by introducing shifted cumulants \(j_n\{2k\}\) based on the shifted flow vector \(W_n = V_n - \bar{v}_n\). The method is extended to a joint distribution of multiple flow harmonics using joint cumulants \(\mathcal{K}_{nm}\) derived from the joint moment-generating function. The approach is validated against iEBE-VISHNU event generator simulations for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV with MC-Glauber initial conditions, \(\eta/s = 0.08\), and zero bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

* Shifted cumulants \(j_2\{2k\}\) for \(k=2,3,4\) are computed from iEBE-VISHNU simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, with 14000 events per centrality class in sixteen centrality classes between 0-80%.
* The relation \(j_n\{2\} \gg j_n\{4\} \gg j_n\{6\} \gg j_n\{8\} \gg \cdots\) holds in peripheral collisions, with differences becoming sensible in peripheral centralities.
* The Symmetric Cumulants \(SC(2,3)\) and \(SC(2,4)\) from ALICE data are explained by the combinations \(\mathcal{K}_{22} + \frac{1}{2}\mathcal{K}_{04} - \mathcal{K}_{31}\) and \(\mathcal{K}_{22} + 4\mathcal{K}_{11}^2\), respectively.
* The \(\chi^2/\)NDF values for the Bessel-Gaussian distribution and \(p_q(v_n;\bar{v}_n)\) for \(q=2,3,4\) are compared in 65-70%, 70-75%, and 75-80% centrality collisions, showing that \(p_q(v_n;\bar{v}_n)\) values are closer to 1.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Navid (2019) and Hadi (2019), who introduced flow distributions for odd harmonics using two-dimensional standardized cumulants and Gram-Charlier A series with orthogonal polynomials, respectively. It also generalizes the Bessel-Gaussian distribution used by Voloshin (2006, 2007) and the ALICE collaboration (2013) for even flow harmonics, which fails in peripheral collisions. The method enables a systematic extraction of event-by-event fluctuation information from joint flow distributions, with applications to interpreting ALICE data on symmetric cumulants and to hydrodynamic simulations such as iEBE-VISHNU.
