# Asymptotic channels and gauge transformations of the time-dependent Dirac equation for extremely relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss the two-center, time-dependent Dirac equation describing the dynamics of an electron during a peripheral, relativistic heavy-ion collision at extreme energies. We derive a factored form, which is exact in the high-energy limit, for the asymptotic channel solutions of the Dirac equation, and elucidate their close connection with gauge transformations which transform the dynamics into a representation in which the interaction between the electron and a distant ion is of short range. We describe the implications of this relationship for solving the time-dependent Dirac equation for extremely relativistic collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact factored forms for asymptotic channel solutions of the two-center time-dependent Dirac equation in the high-energy limit (γ → ∞), showing that these solutions are accurate to order γ⁻² for finite γ, and that the phase transformations defining the "short-range representation" constitute well-defined gauge transformations that remove the long-range asymptotic interaction between the electron and a distant ion.

## Method

The authors analyze the two-center, time-dependent Dirac equation for an electron in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions, deriving asymptotic channel solutions by factoring the wavefunction into a space-time dependent phase factor and a single-center eigenstate. They examine two reference frames: the target frame (where the electron is asymptotically near the target) and the collider frame (where the electron is distant from both ions), and show that the asymptotic electron-projectile interaction can be canceled exactly only in the β → 1 limit, with residual terms of order γ⁻² remaining for finite γ. The phase transformations that define the short-range representation are then shown to constitute gauge transformations, connecting the approach to the Coulomb-boundary conditions of Eichler and co-workers (1994, 1987, 1990) and the gauge transformations used by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser.

## Key numbers

- Collision velocities at RHIC: β_C ≈ 0.9999 (γ correspondingly large)
- Collision velocities at LHC: β_C ≈ 0.9999999
- SPS fixed-target experiments: equivalent collider-frame collision velocity exceeds 0.99c
- The asymptotic solutions are accurate to order γ⁻² for finite γ
- The sharp-limit interaction requires |r_⊥|, b ≪ γ for validity
- The short-range interaction in the sharp limit contains a Dirac-delta function δ(t-z) in the light-front coordinate τ₋ = (t-z)/2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for electron-positron pair production in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the high-energy limit analyses of Baltz (1997), Segev and Wells (1998), and others who obtained closed-form expressions for pair production amplitudes. The paper formalizes the connection between the Coulomb-boundary conditions developed by Eichler and co-workers (1994, 1987, 1990) and the gauge transformations first used by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser in numerical coupled-channel solutions of the two-center Dirac equation. This enables a rigorous treatment of asymptotic channel states for extremely relativistic collisions at facilities like RHIC and LHC, where the high-energy limit (γ → ∞) becomes a meaningful approximation for interpreting experimental results.
