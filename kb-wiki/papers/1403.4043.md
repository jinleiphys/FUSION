# The Spin-orbit force, recoil corrections and possible $B \bar{B}^{*}$ and $D \bar{D}^{*}$ molecular states

> In the framework of the one boson exchange model, we have calculated the effective potentials between two heavy mesons $B \bar{B}^{*}$ and $D \bar{D}^{*}$ from the t- and u-channel $π$, $η$, $ρ$, $ω$ and $σ$ meson exchange with four kinds of quantum number: $I=0$, $J^{PC}=1^{++}$; $I=0$, $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$; $I=1$, $J^{PC}=1^{++}$; $I=1$, $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$. We keep the recoil corrections to the $B \bar{B}^{*}$ and $D \bar{D}^{*}$ system up to $O(\frac{1}{M^2})$. The spin orbit force appears at $O(\frac{1}{M})$, which turns out to be important for the very loosely bound molecular states. Our numerical results show that the momentum-related corrections are unfavorable to the formation of the molecular states in the $I=0$, $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ and $I=1$, $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ channels in the $D \bar{D}^{*}$ systems.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that momentum-related recoil corrections (including the spin-orbit force at order \(O(1/M)\)) are unfavorable to the formation of molecular states in the \(D \bar{D}^{*}\) system with quantum numbers \(I=0, J^{PC}=1^{++}\) and \(I=1, J^{PC}=1^{+-}\), but the abstract does not specify that these corrections can be comparable to or even larger than the binding energy itself for very loosely bound states such as X(3872).

## Method

The authors used the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model to derive effective potentials for the \(B \bar{B}^{*}\) and \(D \bar{D}^{*}\) systems, including t- and u-channel exchange of \(\pi\), \(\eta\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\sigma\) mesons. They kept recoil corrections up to order \(O(1/M^2)\) by explicitly retaining the external momenta of initial and final states in the relativistic Lagrangian, and solved the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for S-wave and D-wave components using a variational method with Laguerre polynomial basis functions.

## Key numbers

* For the \(D \bar{D}^{*}\) system with \(I=0, J^{PC}=1^{++}\) (X(3872) candidate) at cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.1\) GeV: binding energy with recoil corrections is \(0.054\) MeV, while without recoil corrections it is \(0.276\) MeV; the recoil correction (\(0.222\) MeV) exceeds the binding energy itself.
* For the same system at \(\Lambda = 1.3\) GeV: binding energy with recoil corrections is \(7.131\) MeV, without is \(9.686\) MeV; the spin-orbit force contributes \(0.573\) MeV.
* For the \(D \bar{D}^{*}\) system with \(I=0, J^{PC}=1^{+-}\) at \(\Lambda = 1.4\) GeV: binding energy with recoil corrections is \(2.386\) MeV, the total recoil correction is \(-0.447\) MeV, and the spin-orbit force contributes \(0.9\) MeV.
* The \(\pi\) meson mass is \(134.98\) MeV, the \(\sigma\) meson mass is taken as \(600\) MeV, and the heavy meson masses used are \(m_D = 1864.9\) MeV, \(m_{D^*} = 2010.0\) MeV, \(m_B = 5279.0\) MeV, \(m_{B^*} = 5325\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous OBE model calculations for \(D \bar{D}^{*}\) and \(B \bar{B}^{*}\) molecular states (e.g., Liu (2008), Sun (2011), Li (2012)) by including recoil corrections and the spin-orbit force that were omitted in the heavy quark symmetry limit. The results show that these corrections are significant for loosely bound systems like X(3872) and \(Z_c(3900)\), and they enable more precise assessments of whether observed XYZ states can be interpreted as heavy meson molecules. The paper also notes that the \(\pi\) meson exchange provides the most attractive force for the \(D \bar{D}^{*}\) system, while the \(\sigma\) meson attraction is relatively small.
