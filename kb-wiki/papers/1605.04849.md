# Study of the Low-Energy Characteristics of Neutron-Neutron Scattering in the Effective-Range Approximation

> The influence of the mass difference between the charged and neutral pions on the low-energy characteristics of nucleon-nucleon interaction in the $^{1}S_{0}$ spin-singlet state is studied within the framework of the effective-range approximation. By making use of the experimental singlet neutron-proton scattering parameters and the experimental value of neutron-neutron virtual-state energy, the following values were obtained for the neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range: $a_{nn}=-16.59(117)$fm, $r_{nn}=2.83(11)$fm. The calculated neutron-neutron scattering length $a_{nn}$ is in good agreement with one of the two well known and differing experimental values of this quantity, and the calculated effective range $r_{nn}$ is also in good agreement with present-day experimental results.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within the effective-range approximation and assuming nuclear forces arise primarily from one-pion exchange, the neutron-neutron scattering length \(a_{nn}\) and effective range \(r_{nn}\) can be derived from the experimental neutron-proton parameters and the neutron-neutron virtual-state energy, yielding a value of \(a_{nn}\) that agrees with one of the two conflicting experimental determinations but not the other.

## Method

The authors used the effective-range approximation (Eq. 1) to relate the scattering length \(a_{NN}\), effective range \(r_{NN}\), and the nuclear-force range \(R_{NN}\) for nucleon-nucleon systems in the \(^{1}S_{0}\) state. They assumed that the nuclear-force range for identical nucleons (nn, pp) is longer than for np by the ratio of the average pion mass to the neutral-pion mass (Eqs. 16-17), accounting for the mass difference between charged and neutral pions. Using the experimental singlet np scattering parameters \(a_{np}\) and \(r_{np}\) from Sitenko and Tartakovskii (1975) and the experimental nn virtual-state energy from Konobeevskii et al. (2009-2011), they solved for \(R_{np}\), then \(R_{nn}\), and finally \(a_{nn}\) and \(r_{nn}\) via Eqs. (12) and (29).

## Key numbers

*   Neutron-neutron scattering length: \(a_{nn} = -16.59(117)\ \text{fm}\).
*   Neutron-neutron effective range: \(r_{nn} = 2.83(11)\ \text{fm}\).
*   Neutron-proton singlet scattering length (input): \(a_{np} = -23.71(1)\ \text{fm}\) from Sitenko and Tartakovskii (1975).
*   Neutron-proton singlet effective range (input): \(r_{np} = 2.70(9)\ \text{fm}\) from Sitenko and Tartakovskii (1975).
*   Neutron-neutron virtual-state energy (input): \(\varepsilon_{nn} = 0.1293(158)\ \text{MeV}\) from Konobeevskii et al. (2009-2011).
*   Nuclear-force range for nn and pp: \(R_{nn} = R_{pp} = 0.6549(208)\ \text{fm}\).
*   Nuclear-force range for np: \(R_{np} = 0.6404(204)\ \text{fm}\).
*   Purely nuclear proton-proton effective range (calculated): \(r_{pp} = 2.82(10)\ \text{fm}\), in agreement with the recommended value \(r_{pp} = 2.85(4)\ \text{fm}\) from Miller, Nefkens, and Slaus (1990).

## Context

This work extends the effective-range analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering by explicitly incorporating the pion mass difference to relate the np, pp, and nn systems. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between two experimental values of \(a_{nn}\) (e.g., \(-16.61(54)\ \text{fm}\) from Kuhn (1975) versus \(-18.7(7)\ \text{fm}\) from Gonzalez Trotter et al. (2006)), finding support for the less negative value. The results enable a more precise test of charge independence of nuclear forces, but the authors note that the errors on the nn parameters are still too large to draw a firm conclusion on charge symmetry violation.
