# Exotic $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^0$ and $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^{++}$ states in Born-Oppenheimer approximation

> We employ Born-Oppenheimer approximation to the $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^0$ and $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^{++}$ states observed by the LHCb Collaboration and study mass spectrum and root-mean-square radius values. For this purpose, we use dynamical diquark model. We assume that strange quark is a heavy for the usage of Born-Oppenheimer approximation. Our results strongly indicate that the $T_{c\overline{s}0}^{a}(2900)$ states are best described as composed of axial-vector (spin-1) diquark pairs. Furthermore, the calculated root-mean-square radius, $\langle r^{2}\rangle^{1/2} \approx 0.70-0.80$ fm, which is significantly less than 1 fm, provides compelling evidence that these are compact tetraquarks rather than loosely bound hadronic molecules.

## Key claim

The Born-Oppenheimer approximation, applied under the assumption that the strange quark can be treated as a heavy source, predicts that the $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^0$ and $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^{++}$ states observed by LHCb are best described as compact tetraquarks composed of axial-vector (spin-1) diquark pairs, rather than scalar (spin-0) diquark configurations or loosely bound hadronic molecules.

## Method

The author employed the Born-Oppenheimer approximation within a dynamical diquark model, treating the tetraquark as a color-antitriplet diquark $(cq)$ and antidiquark $(\bar{s}\bar{q})$ pair connected by a confining flux tube. The radial Schrodinger equation was solved numerically using a finite-difference method with a Born-Oppenheimer potential $V_{\Sigma_g^+}(r)$ derived from lattice QCD simulations, and the total tetraquark mass was obtained by summing the constituent diquark masses (taken from QCD sum rules) with the computed binding energy.

## Key numbers

*   For the $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^0$ state with spin-1 diquark configuration, the predicted mass is 2881-2894 MeV (depending on parameter set), compared to the experimental value of $2892 \pm 14 \pm 15$ MeV.
*   For the $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^{++}$ state with spin-1 diquark configuration, the predicted mass is 2938-2952 MeV (depending on parameter set), compared to the experimental value of $2921 \pm 17 \pm 20$ MeV.
*   The calculated root-mean-square radius for all spin-1 diquark configurations is $\langle r^{2}\rangle^{1/2} \approx 0.77$ fm, which is significantly less than 1 fm.
*   For the spin-0 diquark configuration, the predicted masses fall approximately 150-160 MeV below the experimental values (e.g., 2715-2730 MeV for the neutral state versus the experimental $2892 \pm 14 \pm 15$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical diquark model and Born-Oppenheimer approximation, previously applied to hidden-charm tetraquarks by Giron and Lebed (2019) and to hidden-strangeness systems by Jafarzade (2025), to the open-flavor charm-strange sector. The results contradict molecular interpretations of these states, such as the $D^\ast K^\ast$ hadronic molecule picture proposed by Chen (2022) and Agaev (2022), and instead support the compact tetraquark interpretation found in studies by Liu (2022), Yang (2023), and Ortega (2023). The finding enables future experimental searches for the predicted $I_3=0$ member of the isovector triplet and further tests of the Born-Oppenheimer framework in heavy-light systems.
