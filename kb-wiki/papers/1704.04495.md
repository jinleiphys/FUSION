# Effective Field Theory Models for Thermal QCD

> We present an effective field theory model for QCD at finite temperature with quarks. We discuss the mean field theory, the fixing of parameters, and a prediction for the curvature of the critical line. We proceed to write down a pionic theory of fluctuations around the mean field, and discuss how the parameters of this pionic effective theory descend from the model with quarks.

## Key claim

The authors construct an effective field theory (EFT) for QCD at finite temperature with quarks, organized by mass dimension and using a temperature scale \(T_0\), which differs from the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by including all symmetry-allowed terms up to dimension 6 rather than only a single four-fermi coupling. The mean-field prediction for the curvature of the critical line in the chiral limit is parameter-free but larger than current lattice determinations, which are not in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors build a Euclidean finite-temperature EFT with SU\(_V(N_f) \times\) SU\(_A(N_f)\) flavour symmetries, including all operators up to mass dimension 6 invariant under O(3)\(\times\)Z\(_2\) spatial and time-reversal symmetries. They apply a mean-field approximation via the fermion operator identity \(\overline\psi_\alpha\psi_\beta = \delta_{\alpha\beta}\langle\overline\psi\psi\rangle\), perform Fierz transformations to reduce the four-fermi terms, and compute the free energy using dimensional regularization. The parameters are fixed by setting the chiral transition temperature \(T_c\) and the renormalization scale \(M\), and the pion effective theory for fluctuations is obtained by matching the quark EFT to a quadratic pion Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The number of fermion field components is \(\mathcal{N}=4N_c N_f = 24\) for \(N_c=3\) and \(N_f=2\).
- The renormalization scale \(M\) is chosen between 500 and 700 MeV, between the pseudoscalar and vector meson mass scales.
- For \(T_c\) between about 140 MeV and 125 MeV, the coupling \(\lambda/\mathcal{N}\) is attractive and of order one.
- The mean-field prediction for the curvature of the critical line in the chiral limit is \(T_c(0)\kappa = 3/\pi^2\).
- Lattice results for the curvature correspond to the range \(T_c(0)\kappa \simeq 0.01\)–0.05.
- In the chiral limit, the parameter \(c^4\) in the pion effective theory vanishes linearly as \(T \to T_c\).

## Context

This work extends the NJL model (Klevansky, 1992) by constructing a systematic EFT that includes all operators up to dimension 6, respecting the reduced O(3)\(\times\)Z\(_2\) symmetry of finite temperature rather than full Lorentz invariance. The quadratic pion theory examined earlier (Son and Stephanov, 2002) is recovered as the low-energy limit. The parameter-free prediction for the curvature of the critical line provides a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations, which currently exist only for heavier-than-physical quark masses, and the matching procedure enables future refinement beyond the mean-field and chiral limits.
