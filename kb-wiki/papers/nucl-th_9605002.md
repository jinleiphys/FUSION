# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering from Effective Field Theory

> We perform a nonperturbative calculation of the 1S0 NN scattering amplitude using an effective field theory (EFT) expansion. The expansion we advocate is a modification of what has been used previously; it is no a chiral expansion in powers of the pion mass. We use dimensional regularization throughout and the MS-bar subtraction scheme; our final result depends only on physical observables. We show that the EFT expansion of the quantity |p|cot delta(p) converges at momenta much greater than the scale that characterizes the derivative expansion of the EFT Lagrangian. Our conclusions are optimistic about the applicability of an EFT approach to the quantitative study of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified effective field theory (EFT) expansion for nucleon-nucleon scattering that is not a chiral expansion in powers of the pion mass, and shows that expanding the quantity |p|cot delta(p) in powers of the EFT expansion parameter converges at momenta much greater than the scale Lambda that characterizes the derivative expansion of the EFT Lagrangian, contrary to what one would expect from naive power counting.

## Method

The authors perform a nonperturbative calculation of the 1S0 NN scattering amplitude using dimensional regularization and the MS-bar subtraction scheme. They sum ladder diagrams with contact interactions (four-nucleon operators) to all orders, first in a theory with nucleons alone, then including one-pion exchange. Instead of expanding the potential V or the amplitude A in the EFT expansion, they advocate expanding the quantity |p|cot delta(p) in powers of the expansion parameter nu, which preserves unitarity and yields a faster-converging series.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length a = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm, effective range r0 = 2.73 ± 0.03 fm for 1S0 np scattering (from Preston and Bhaduri, 1975)
- Leading-order coupling C = 4πa/M = -(1/25 MeV)^2, where M = 940 MeV is the nucleon mass
- The scale Lambda from the derivative expansion is 1/Lambda^2 = (1/2) r0 a = -(1/35 MeV)^2, corresponding to a failure scale of |p| ~ 35 MeV (lab kinetic energy T_lab = 2.6 MeV) in the theory with nucleons alone when expanding the potential
- The nu=2 expansion of |p|cot delta(p) reproduces effective range theory and agrees with Nijmegen partial wave analysis data beyond |p| = m_pi, unlike the potential expansion which fails above |p| ~ 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's (1990, 1991, 1992) proposal to apply effective field theory to nucleon-nucleon scattering and nuclear physics, and the subsequent work by Ordonez, van Kolck, and Ray (1992, 1994, 1996) using chiral Lagrangians with momentum cutoffs. The paper addresses the fundamental difficulty that NN interactions are nonperturbative and that the large scattering length (a ~ -24 fm) introduces a new length scale not associated with any physical threshold. The proposed expansion of |p|cot delta(p) enables semi-analytical approaches to two- and many-body problems that were previously only approached numerically, and is optimistic about applying EFT to the quantitative study of nuclear matter.
