# KDAR neutrino scattering for $^{12}$C target via charged current and muon angular distribution

> We calculate muon-neutrino ($ν_μ$) scattering off $^{12}$C via charged current (CC) by exploiting the 236 MeV ${ν_μ}$ from the kaon-decay-at-rest (KDAR). In this energy region, since both inelastic scattering below the quasielastic (QE) region and the QE scattering contribute simultaneously, we combine the inelastic scattering obtained by the QRPA and the QE scattering obtained by distorted wave born approximation (DWBA) based on the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. We compare the results to the data from MiniBooNE. Further, since the KDR $ν_μ$ CC scattering may have angle dependence of outgoing muon, we investigate the differential angular dependent cross section in the ${ν_μ}$-$^{12}$C scattering and compare to the results by $ν_e$-$^{12}$C scattering. These results could be useful for the calibration of the forthcoming KDAR neutrino cross section experiments.

## Key claim

The authors calculate muon-neutrino scattering off carbon-12 via charged current at the specific KDAR energy of 236 MeV, combining two separate theoretical frameworks (QRPA for inelastic scattering and DWBA based on RMF for quasielastic scattering) to cover the full energy region where both processes contribute simultaneously. The abstract's claim of "comparing to MiniBooNE data" is hedged: the comparison is qualitative, with the experimental data shown as upper and lower curves rather than as discrete data points with uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compute charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections using the weak current operator expanded in multipole operators (Coulomb, longitudinal, electric, and magnetic). For inelastic scattering below the quasielastic region, they use the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) to describe nuclear excitations up to J=4, including allowed Gamow-Teller (1+), spin dipole (0-, 1-, 2-), and higher multipole transitions. For quasielastic scattering, they use distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) based on two relativistic mean field models: a simple RMF model and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The two approaches are combined to cover the full muon kinetic energy range for 236 MeV KDAR neutrinos on carbon-12.

## Key numbers

- Incident neutrino energy: 236 MeV (monoenergetic KDAR muon-neutrino)
- Energy transfer range covered: up to approximately 130 MeV (E_nu - m_mu)
- Differential cross section peaks appear around muon kinetic energy T_mu = 112 MeV region
- Cross section distributed mostly in T_mu range 90 MeV to 125 MeV
- Forward scattering peak for muon appears around theta_mu = 40 degrees at 236 MeV
- At lower incident energy E_nu_mu = 180 MeV, peak position shifts to theta_mu = 50 degrees
- About 20% increase in cross section obtained by including transitions with J greater than 2
- Excitation energy range in compound nucleus N-12: E* = 5 to 40 MeV (corresponding to T_mu = 90 to 125 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the same group (Kim 2009, Ch09-1, Ch09-2) on neutrino-nucleus scattering and responds to the MiniBooNE measurement of monoenergetic KDAR neutrino interactions on carbon-12 (Aqui 2018). The combined QRPA-plus-DWBA approach addresses the challenge that at 236 MeV both inelastic scattering and quasielastic scattering contribute simultaneously, unlike lower-energy experiments such as LSND (Atha 1997) and KARMEN (Bodm 1994) or higher-energy DIF neutrino experiments. The results are intended to support calibration of forthcoming KDAR neutrino experiments including JSNS2 at J-PARC and the NuMI beamline absorber at Fermilab, and to inform supernova neutrino studies where neutrino energies lie in the tens-of-MeV region.
