# Relativistic unitary description of pi pi scattering

> A unitary framework based on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of relativistic quantum mechanics is used to describe two-pion scattering from threshold to 1400 MeV. The framework properly includes unitarity cuts for one-, two- and three-hadron states and provides an excellent description of the available data for pi pi phase shifts and inelasticities. The role and importance of three-hadron cuts are calculated and discussed.

# Relativistic unitary description of pi pi scattering

## Key claim

The Bakamjian-Thomas relativistic quantum mechanics framework, when truncated to one-, two-, and three-hadron states, provides an excellent description of pi pi scattering phase shifts and inelasticities from threshold to 1400 MeV, although the authors acknowledge that inelasticities due to open three- or four-particle states (such as rho pi pi and pi pi pi pi) do not appear to be significant for this process, as their effects seem overwhelmed by the opening of the two-body K anti-K channel.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic Hamiltonian framework based on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction, where interactions are introduced into the mass operator and the Hilbert space is truncated to include only one-, two-, and three-body states of finite-sized hadrons. The coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations are solved in the center-of-momentum frame, with the potential matrix elements describing couplings between hadron states via meson-exchange interactions, and the three-body unitarity cuts are explicitly included through two-body self-energies and kernels derived from three-body intermediate states. The model is applied to the S-wave and P-wave partial waves of pi pi scattering, including coupling to the K anti-K channel.

## Key numbers

- The framework describes pi pi scattering from the two-pion threshold up to 1400 MeV.
- In a study of the pion-loop contribution to the rho-meson self-energy, two-pion intermediate states contributed more than 95% of the total, while four-hadron states contributed less than 5%.
- The numerical methods maintain unitarity of the framework to better than one part in a million.
- The isoscalar-scalar channel near 1000 MeV is dominated by mixing between pi pi and K anti-K channels and the f0(980) meson resonance.
- Three additional scalar meson resonances above this region are established: f0(1370), f0(1500), and f0(1710).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson scattering dynamics based on potential models, such as the Julich group model (Speth, 1995) which uses instantaneous approximations of meson-exchange models and the Krakow group separable-potential model (Kaminski, 1994) which incorporates few-body dynamical effects through effective two-body channels like sigma sigma. The framework enables a fully unitary treatment of hadron reactions beyond the lowest-order valence quark picture, which is needed for analyzing baryon resonance data from experiments at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (TJNAF) Hall B.
