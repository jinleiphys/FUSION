# BBGKY Hierarchy in Scalar QFT

> This work is dedicated to obtaining of analog of Bogoliubov's chain for the case of complex scalar field in QFT and renormalization problem of obtained equations is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic generalization of the BBGKY hierarchy for a complex scalar field with quartic self-interaction, but the resulting chain equations are only valid under the condition that the system volume is finite and the number of particles is large yet finite, with momentum integrals cut off at upper and lower limits determined by the Heisenberg uncertainty principle.

## Method

The author starts from the Wigner function formalism for a complex scalar field in quantum field theory, using the grand canonical Gibbs distribution with a chemical potential and hydrodynamical velocity. The kinetic equation for the one-particle Wigner function is obtained by substituting the interaction current from the Lagrangian density \(\mathcal{L} = (\partial_\mu\varphi^\dagger\partial_\mu\varphi) - m^2(\varphi^\dagger\varphi) + \lambda(\varphi^\dagger\varphi)^2\) into the Lorentz-invariant transport equation. The hierarchy is then extended to n-particle Wigner functions by induction, and renormalization is addressed by expanding the Wigner function in terms of initial-state reduced density matrices with a finite completeness condition that introduces a cutoff-dependent factor \(Z\).

## Key numbers

- The interaction Lagrangian is \(\lambda (\varphi^\dagger\varphi)^2\) with coupling constant \(\lambda\).
- The initial equilibrium Wigner function is the relativistic Bose-Einstein distribution without spin: \(\mathcal{F}_{\rm in}(k,x) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3[e^{\beta(k_\nu U^\nu(x)-\mu(x))}-1]}\).
- The number of produced particles scales as \(N(E) \sim \ln s\), where \(E \sim \sqrt{s}\).
- The maximal pion four-momentum is \(p_{max}^{\mu} = (p_{max}^{0} = \sqrt{\vec p_{max}^2+m^2} \sim \frac{E}{N} \sim \frac{\ln s}{\sqrt{s}}, \vec p_{max})\).
- The system volume is Lorentz-contracted as \(V \sim \frac{1}{m_{\pi}^3} \frac{2M}{E}\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic BBGKY hierarchy of Bogoliubov (no year given in text) to relativistic quantum field theory, building on the relativistic kinetic theory formalism of de Groot (no year given in text). The renormalization approach using a finite-volume cutoff and expansion in initial-state correlation functions parallels the virial expansion in classical statistical mechanics. The chain equations (4.16) are intended for computing kinetic coefficients in nonlinear regimes where particle-cluster correlations are essential, such as phase transitions in relativistic plasma or multiple meson production in ultra-high energy collisions, referencing the multiperipheral model of Pignotti (no year given in text) and the hydrodynamic description of Landau (no year given in text).
