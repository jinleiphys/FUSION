# Suppression of composition $g$-modes in chemically-equilibrating warm neutron stars

> We investigate the impact of chemical equilibration and the resulting bulk viscosity on non-radial oscillation modes of warm neutron stars at temperatures up to $T\approx 5$ MeV, relevant for protoneutron stars and neutron-star post-merger remnants. In this regime, the relaxation rate of weak interactions becomes comparable to the characteristic frequencies of composition $g$-modes in the core, resulting in resonant damping. To capture this effect, we introduce the dynamical sound speed, a complex, frequency-dependent generalization of the adiabatic sound speed that encodes both the restoring force and the dissipative effects of bulk compression. Using realistic weak reaction rates and three representative equations of state, we compute the complex frequencies of composition $g$-modes with finite-temperature profiles. We find that bulk viscous damping becomes increasingly significant with temperature and can completely suppress composition $g$-modes. In contrast, the $f$-mode remains largely unaffected by bulk viscosity due to its nearly divergence-free character. Our results highlight the sensitivity of $g$-mode behavior to thermal structure, weak reaction rates, and the equation of state, and establish the dynamical sound speed as a valuable descriptor characterizing oscillation properties in dissipative neutron star matter.

## Key claim
Bulk viscous damping from chemical equilibration can completely suppress composition $g$-modes in warm neutron stars at temperatures up to $T\approx 5$ MeV, but only when the beta equilibration relaxation rate $\gamma$ becomes comparable to the mode frequency; the $f$-mode remains largely unaffected due to its nearly divergence-free character.

## Method
The authors computed the complex frequencies of non-radial composition $g$-modes (and the $f$-mode) for three relativistic mean-field equations of state (QMC-RMF3, IUFSU, and IOPB-I) by solving the general relativistic non-radial oscillation equations in the Cowling approximation. They introduced a complex, frequency-dependent "dynamical sound speed" that encodes both the restoring force and the dissipative effects of bulk viscosity, using microphysically-calculated, temperature-dependent Urca reaction rates (direct and modified) consistent with each equation of state. The background stellar models used a zero-temperature equation of state, while the weak interaction rates and constant-entropy temperature profiles included finite-temperature effects.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range considered: up to $T\approx 5$ MeV.
- Direct Urca threshold densities: $n_{\rm{B},\mathrm{thr.}}=0.414~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ for IOPB-I and $n_{\rm{B},\mathrm{thr.}}=0.655~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ for IUFSU; QMC-RMF3 has no direct Urca threshold.
- The beta equilibration relaxation rate $\gamma$ scales as $T^4$ for direct Urca and $T^6$ for modified Urca in the Fermi surface approximation.
- The imaginary part of the dynamical sound speed squared, related to bulk viscosity, peaks at the resonant condition $\gamma = \omega$.
- The bulk viscosity coefficient is given by $\zeta = 4.5\times 10^{29} \rm{~g~cm}^{-1}\rm{~s}^{-1} \left(\omega/600 \rm{~s^{-1}}\right)^{-1} \times \left((\ep+P)/c^2 / (3\times 10^{14}\rm{~g~ cm}^{-3})\right)\left({\rm Im}\left[c_{\rm dy}^2\right]/0.001\right)$.

## Context
This work extends the local plane-wave analysis of the Brunt-Vaisala frequency by Andersson (2019) and the global mode calculations of Counsell (2023) by using microphysically-calculated, temperature-dependent weak reaction rates that are self-consistent with the equation of state, rather than a parametrized viscosity. It builds on studies of bulk viscosity in neutron star mergers by Alford (2019, 2023) and Most (2022). The results enable more accurate predictions for the gravitational-wave signal from protoneutron stars and neutron-star post-merger remnants, where the damping and potential suppression of $g$-modes must be accounted for.
