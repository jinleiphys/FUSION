# Calculations of widths in the problem of decay by proton emission

> We develop a new fully quantum method for determination of widths for nuclear decay by proton emission where multiple internal reflections of wave packet describing tunneling process inside proton--nucleus radial barrier are taken into account. Exact solutions for amplitudes of wave function, penetrability $T$ and reflection $R$ are found for $n$-step barrier (at arbitrary $n$) which approximates the realistic barrier. In contrast to semiclassical approach and two-potential approach, we establish by this method essential dependence of the penetrability on the starting point $R_{\rm form}$ in the internal well where proton starts to move outside (for example, for $^{157}_{73}{\rm Ta}$ the penetrability is changed up to 200 times; accuracy is $|T+R-1| < 1.5 \cdot 10^{-15}$). We impose a new condition: in the beginning of the proton decay the proton starts to move outside from minimum of the well. Such a condition provides minimal calculated half-life and gives stable basis for predictions. However, the half-lives calculated by such an approach turn out to be a little closer to experimental data in comparison with the semiclassical half-lives. Estimated influence of the external barrier region is up to 1.5 times for changed penetrability.

## Key claim

The authors develop a fully quantum method for calculating proton-decay widths that accounts for multiple internal reflections of the wave packet inside the proton-nucleus barrier, and they find that the penetrability depends strongly on the starting point \(R_{\rm form}\) in the internal well, a dependence that is absent in semiclassical and two-potential approaches. The method yields half-lives that are "a little closer" to experimental data than semiclassical half-lives, but the improvement is modest and the approach introduces a new free parameter (the starting point) that must be fixed by an additional condition.

## Method

The authors approximate the realistic proton-nucleus radial barrier by an \(n\)-step rectangular barrier (for arbitrary \(n\)) and solve the stationary Schrödinger equation exactly using the method of multiple internal reflections (MIR). They derive recurrence relations for the transmission and reflection amplitudes at each step, sum the contributions from all internal reflections, and compute the penetrability \(T\) and reflection \(R\) from the total amplitudes. The width \(\Gamma\) is then obtained via the Gurvitz–Kälbermann formula \(\Gamma = P_p F (\hbar^2 / 4m) T\), using a normalization factor \(F_1\) from Buck (1993), and the half-life follows as \(\tau = \hbar \ln 2 / \Gamma\). The method is tested against the standard quantum-mechanical approach for two-step barriers, showing agreement to 15 digits.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{157}_{73}{\rm Ta}\), the penetrability changes by up to a factor of 200 when the starting point \(R_{\rm form}\) is varied (accuracy of the calculation is \(|T+R-1| < 1.5 \times 10^{-15}\)).
- For \(^{157}_{73}{\rm Ta}\), including the external barrier region (beyond the third turning point) changes the half-life by up to a factor of 1.5 (from \(\tau_{\rm min} = 0.20\) sec to \(\tau_{\rm as} = 0.30\) sec at \(R_{\rm max} = 250\) fm).
- For \(^{157}_{73}{\rm Ta}\), the starting point that gives agreement with experimental data is \(R_{\rm form} = 7.2127\) fm.
- The half-lives calculated by MIR are "a little closer" to experimental data than those from the semiclassical WKB approach for the three nuclei studied (\(^{157}_{73}{\rm Ta}\), \(^{161}_{75}{\rm Re}\), \(^{167}_{77}{\rm Ir}\)), but no explicit numerical values for the MIR half-lives are given in the truncated text beyond the qualitative statement.

## Context

This work extends the method of multiple internal reflections (MIR), previously applied to simple rectangular barriers and alpha decay by Maydanyuk (2000, 2002, 2003, 2006), to arbitrary-shaped radial barriers for proton emission. It directly challenges the semiclassical WKB approach (Landau and Lifshitz, 1989) and the two-potential approach (TPA), which assume that the penetrability is independent of the internal well depth and the starting point. The paper enables future fully quantum calculations of proton-decay widths that can incorporate more realistic nuclear potentials and deformations, and it provides a benchmark for assessing the accuracy of semiclassical approximations.
