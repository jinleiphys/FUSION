# Antikaons in neutron star studied with recent versions of relativistic mean-field models

> We study the impact of additional couplings in the relativistic mean field (RMF) models, in conjunction with antikaon condensation, on various neutron star properties. We analyze different properties such as in-medium antikaon and nucleon effective masses, antikaon energies, chemical potentials and the mass-radius relations of neutron star (NS). We calculate the NS properties with the RMF (NL3), E-RMF (G1, G2) and FSU2.1 models, which are quite successful in explaining several finite nuclear properties. Our results show that the onset of kaon condensation in NS strongly depends on the parameters of the Lagrangian, especially the additional couplings which play a significant role at higher densities where antikaons dominate the behavior of equation of state.

# Antikaons in neutron star studied with recent versions of relativistic mean-field models

## Key claim

The onset of kaon condensation in neutron stars strongly depends on the parameters of the Lagrangian, especially the additional couplings (non-linear scalar-vector and tensor couplings) which play a significant role at higher densities where antikaons dominate the behavior of the equation of state; however, the results are conditional on the chosen value of the kaon optical potential (fixed at U_K = -160 MeV for most comparisons) and the specific parameter sets (NL3, G1, G2, FSU2.1) which are successful for finite nuclear properties but yield different predictions for antikaon onset densities and maximum neutron star masses.

## Method

The authors extended three classes of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, the standard RMF (NL3 parameter set), the extended RMF (E-RMF with G1 and G2 parameter sets, containing four additional couplings), and the FSU2.1 model (with three additional couplings), by including antikaons (K^- and anti-K^0) via a minimal coupling scheme. They solved the coupled equations for chemical potentials, charge neutrality, and baryon densities to determine the onset conditions for s-wave antikaon condensation, then computed the equation of state (EoS) and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Antikaon mass used: m_K = 495 MeV; nucleon mass: m_n = 939 MeV.
- Kaon optical potential fixed at U_K = -160 MeV for most calculations.
- For the G1 parameter set with U_K = -160 MeV, the effective mass ratio m_K*/m_K decreases with density but less strongly than the nucleon effective mass ratio m_n*/m_n.
- The maximum value of the electron chemical potential (mu_e) is larger for G1 and G2 parameter sets, and lower for NL3 and FSU2.1 parameter sets.
- The change in maximum neutron star mass (Delta M) due to antikaon condensation is largest for the NL3 parameter set and smallest for the FSU2.1 parameter set; anti-K^0 plays no role in the FSU2.1 case.
- The radius corresponding to maximum mass is lower for a softer EoS and can change in the presence of antikaons; the relation is Delta M proportional to U_K and R proportional to 1/rho_c (central baryon density).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars, first demonstrated by Kaplan and Nelson within a chiral SU(3)_L x SU(3)_R model, and later studied by groups such as Ellis (1995), Brown (1995), Thorsson (1994), Banik (2002), and others. The paper builds on the success of RMF models (NL3), E-RMF models (G1, G2 from Furnstahl and collaborators), and the FSU2.1 model (Shen 2011) in describing finite nuclei, and investigates how the additional couplings in these models, which soften the EoS at high density and are consistent with experimental flow data, affect the onset and consequences of antikaon condensation. The results enable more precise predictions for neutron star mass-radius relations that can be compared with observational constraints from neutron star mass measurements (Demorest 2010) and other astrophysical data.
