# Weak reactions on 12C within the Continuum Random Phase Approximation with partial occupancies

> We extend our previous studies of the neutrino-induced reactions on 12C and muon capture to include partial occupation of nuclear subshells in the framework of the continuum random phase approximation. We find, in contrast to the work by Auerbach et al., that a partial occupation of the p1/2 subshell reduces the inclusive cross sections only slightly. The extended model describes the muon capture rate and the 12C(nu_e,e-)12N cross section very well. The recently updated flux and the improved model bring the calculated 12C(nu_mu,mu^-)12N cross section (~ 17.5 10^{-40} cm^2) and the data (12.4 +/- 0.3(stat.) +/- 1.8(syst.) 10^{-40} cm^2) closer together, but does not remove the discrepancy fully.

# Weak reactions on 12C within the Continuum Random Phase Approximation with partial occupancies

## Key claim

The inclusion of partial occupation of the p1/2 subshell in the 12C ground state, together with an updated neutrino flux, reduces the calculated 12C(nu_mu,mu-)12N cross section from about 19-20 x 10^{-40} cm^2 to about 17.5 x 10^{-40} cm^2, bringing it closer to the experimental value of 12.4 x 10^{-40} cm^2 but not fully removing the discrepancy, in contrast to the work by Auerbach et al. (1997) who claimed the discrepancy could be essentially fully removed with partial occupancy.

## Method

The authors extended their previous continuum random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for neutrino-induced reactions on 12C and muon capture by allowing partial occupation numbers for nuclear subshells, multiplying the relevant particle-hole matrix elements by the occupation numbers n_h for hole states. They performed shell model calculations using the OXBASH code with the Cohen-Kurath interaction to determine partial occupancies of 0.75 for protons and neutrons in the p1/2 subshell, then solved the continuum RPA equations using both a finite-range G-matrix derived from the Bonn NN potential (BP) and an empirical zero-range Landau-Migdal force (LM) as residual interactions.

## Key numbers

- Partial occupation of the p1/2 subshell: 0.75 for both protons and neutrons, determined from shell model calculations with the Cohen-Kurath interaction
- Inclusive muon capture rate on 12C: calculated value 32.7 x 10^3 s^{-1} (BP force) and 31.3 x 10^3 s^{-1} (LM force), compared to experimental value 32.8 +/- 0.8 x 10^3 s^{-1}
- 12C(nu_e,e-)12N* inclusive cross section: calculated 5.4 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (BP) and 5.6 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (LM), compared to experimental values 5.1 +/- 0.6 +/- 0.5 x 10^{-42} cm^2 and 5.7 +/- 0.6 +/- 0.6 x 10^{-42} cm^2
- Total 12C(nu_mu,mu-)12N cross section: calculated 17.8 x 10^{-40} cm^2 (BP) and 17.5 x 10^{-40} cm^2 (LM), compared to experimental value 12.4 +/- 0.3 (stat.) +/- 1.8 (syst.) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Reduction in the 12C(nu_mu,mu-)12N cross section: approximately half (0.8 x 10^{-40} cm^2 for BP, 1.9 x 10^{-40} cm^2 for LM) due to revised flux and half (0.7 x 10^{-40} cm^2 for BP, 0.9 x 10^{-40} cm^2 for LM) due to partial p1/2 occupation
- Rescaling factors N for exclusive Gamow-Teller transitions reduced from about 4 (old calculation) to less than 2 (new calculation with partial occupancies)

## Context

This work extends previous continuum RPA studies by Kolbe, Langanke, and Vogel (1994, 1995) on neutrino-induced reactions on 12C and muon capture. It directly addresses the claim by Auerbach et al. (1997) that partial occupation of the p1/2 subshell allows simultaneous description of all three inclusive weak processes on 12C, finding instead that the reduction is only slight for inclusive processes. The results are relevant for interpreting oscillation search experiments by the KARMEN and LSND collaborations, and the authors provide tabulated cross sections for 12C(nu_e,e-)12N at neutrino energies from 20 to 300 MeV to aid the LSND oscillation analysis. The work also notes that Singh et al. (1998) obtained a total muon capture rate of (3.6 +/- 0.22) x 10^4 s^{-1} and a 12C(nu_mu,mu-)12N cross section of (16.5 +/- 1.3) x 10^{-40} cm^2 using the local density approximation, consistent with the present results.
