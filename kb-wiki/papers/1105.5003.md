# Nuclear matter within a dilatation-invariant parity doublet model: the role of the tetraquark at nonzero density

> We investigate the role of a scalar tetraquark state for the description of nuclear matter within the parity doublet model in the mirror assignment. In the dilatation-invariant version of the model a nucleon-nucleon interaction term mediated by the lightest scalar tetraquark field naturally emerges. At nonzero density one has, beyond the usual chiral condensate, also a tetraquark condensate. The behavior of both condensates and the restoration of chiral symmetry at high density are studied. It is shown that this additional scalar degree of freedom affects non negligibly the properties of the medium.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including a light scalar tetraquark field (identified with the $f_0(600)$ resonance) in a dilatation-invariant parity doublet model resolves previous inconsistencies between vacuum physics and finite-density nuclear matter properties, specifically allowing the bare nucleon mass parameter $m_0$ extracted from vacuum decays ($460\pm130$ MeV) to also reproduce nuclear matter saturation and compressibility within the experimentally allowed range.

## Method
The authors construct a chirally symmetric linear sigma model with $N_f=2$ including scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons, plus a scalar-isoscalar tetraquark field $\chi$. Baryons are introduced as a parity doublet $(N,N^*)$ in the mirror assignment, with a dilatation-invariant interaction term coupling the tetraquark to the nucleons. The thermodynamic potential is computed in the mean-field approximation, retaining only the $\sigma$, $\chi$, and $\omega$ fields, and the coupled gap equations are solved to obtain the condensates and nuclear matter properties at finite baryon density.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ with nucleon binding energy $16\ \text{MeV}$ (for $m_0=500\ \text{MeV}$).
- Compressibility at saturation: $K = 194\ \text{MeV}$ (for $m_0=500\ \text{MeV}$), within the phenomenological range $200$-$300\ \text{MeV}$.
- Bare nucleon mass parameter from vacuum fit: $m_0 = 460 \pm 130\ \text{MeV}$; combined with compressibility constraint, the allowed range narrows to $500 \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 600\ \text{MeV}$.
- Physical scalar masses after diagonalization: tetraquark-dominated state $m_h = 600\ \text{MeV}$ (identified with $f_0(600)$) and quarkonium-dominated state $m_s = 1300\ \text{MeV}$ (identified with $f_0(1370)$).
- Coupling constants for $m_0=500\ \text{MeV}$: $g_\omega = 4.87$, $g = 450\ \text{MeV}$, $m_\sigma = 1294\ \text{MeV}$, $m_\chi = 612\ \text{MeV}$.
- Strong first-order chiral phase transition at chemical potential $\mu \sim 1\ \text{GeV}$, with density jump ratio $\rho_2/\rho_1 = 2$-$4$ within the allowed $m_0$ range.

## Context
This work extends the parity doublet model of Gallas (2009) by incorporating a light tetraquark field, addressing inconsistencies noted in earlier studies by Hatsuda (1988), Zschiesche (2006), Dexheimer (2007), and Sasaki (2010), where the compressibility was too large and the required $m_0\sim800\ \text{MeV}$ disagreed with vacuum fits. The results align with the Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential of Machleidt (2000), which uses two scalar mesons with masses near $500$ and $1200\ \text{MeV}$. The work enables future studies of asymmetric nuclear matter, neutron star equations of state, and extension to $N_f=3$, as well as inclusion of the dilaton/glueball field.
