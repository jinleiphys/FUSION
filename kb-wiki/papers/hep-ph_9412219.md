# Off-Shell Axial Anomaly via the γ^* π^0 -> γTransition

> The $γ^* π^0 \rightarrow γ$ form factor, including the extension off the pion mass-shell, is obtained from a generalized impulse approximation within a QCD-based model field theory known to provide an excellent description of the pion charge form factor. This approach implements dressing of the vertex functions and propagators consistent with dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, gauge invariance, quark confinement and perturbative QCD. Soft nonperturbative behavior, dictated by the axial anomaly, is found to evolve to the perturbative QCD limit only for \mbox{$Q^2 \geq 20~{\rm GeV}^2$}.

## Key claim

The gamma* pi0 -> gamma transition form factor, including its extension off the pion mass-shell, is calculated within a QCD-based model field theory; the soft nonperturbative behavior dictated by the axial anomaly evolves to the perturbative QCD limit only for Q^2 >= 20 GeV^2, meaning that non-leading contributions still provide 50% of the strength at Q^2 = 3 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors compute the gamma* pi0 -> gamma vertex using a generalized impulse approximation (quark triangle diagram) within the Global Color-symmetry Model (GCM), a QCD-based model field theory. The dressed quark propagator, pion-quark Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and dressed quark-photon vertex are all mutually consistent with dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, gauge invariance, confinement, and perturbative QCD; the Ball-Chiu ansatz is used for the photon vertex and the pion vertex is approximated by B(p^2,m)/f_pi. The integral is evaluated numerically with no parameter adjustment, using a parameterized quark propagator that reproduces soft chiral physics quantities.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 0 and the pion mass-shell, the coupling constant g(0,-m_pi^2) = 0.497, in agreement with the experimental value 0.504 +/- 0.019
- The chiral limit reproduces the exact axial anomaly result g(0,0) = 1/2
- The asymptotic form F(Q^2) ~ A [Q^2 + B + C ln(Q^2/Q_0^2)/Q^2]^{-1} with A = 0.53 GeV^2, B = 3.1 GeV^2, C = -3.8 GeV^4, and Q_0 = 1 GeV^2, fits numerical results for Q^2 >= 10 GeV^2
- The normalization A/f_pi^2 = 75 agrees with the leading asymptotic behavior 8 pi^2 = 79 from perturbative QCD factorization
- The off-shell suppression factor f(P^2) = 1/[1 + (P^2 + m_pi^2)/a^2 - (P^2 + m_pi^2)^2/b^4] with a = 1.38 GeV and b = 1.55 GeV fits to within 7% for -m_pi^2 <= P^2 <= 1 GeV^2 and Q^2 <= 2.5 GeV^2
- The quark propagator parameters include lambda = 0.516 GeV, m = 6.1 MeV, and fitted quantities f_pi = 83.9 MeV, m_pi = 127 MeV, r_pi^em = 0.596 fm

## Context

This work extends previous quark triangle diagram studies of the gamma* pi0 -> gamma form factor, such as Ito, Buck, and Gross (1992), by including nonperturbative dressing of the photon-quark vertex, confining dynamical self-energy amplitudes rather than a constant constituent mass, and a pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude determined by the quark propagator as required by dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The same model previously provided an excellent description of the space-like pion charge form factor (Roberts 1994). The results enable extraction of the physical transition form factor from planned experiments at CEBAF via virtual Compton scattering on a proton target, where an extrapolation to the pion mass shell will be needed.
