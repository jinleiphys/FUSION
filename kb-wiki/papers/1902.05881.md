# Theoretical calculation of the $p-{}^6{\rm Li}$ radiative capture reaction

> We present a new calculation of the ${}^6{\rm Li}(p,γ){}^7{\rm Be}$ radiative capture astrophysical $S$-factor in a cluster model framework. We consider several intercluster potentials, adjusted to reproduce the ${}^7{\rm Be}$ bound state properties and the $p-{}^6{\rm Li}$ elastic scattering phase shifts. Using these potentials, we calculate the astrophysical $S$-factor, obtaining a good agreement with available data, and the photon angular distribution. Finally, we discuss the consequences of a hypothetical resonance-like structure on the $S$-factor.

## Key claim

The authors present a new cluster-model calculation of the \({}^{6}{\rm Li}(p,\gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}\) astrophysical \(S\)-factor that achieves good agreement with available data, but they note that the theoretical uncertainty from model dependence is significant and of the same order as the experimental errors. They also investigate the possibility of a hypothetical resonance-like structure near 195 keV, finding that it can be accommodated only if the \({}^{7}{\rm Be}\) ground state contains a small spin-3/2 component.

## Method

The calculation treats the proton and \({}^{6}{\rm Li}\) as structureless particles interacting via a Gaussian nuclear potential plus point-like Coulomb term, with parameters fitted to reproduce the \({}^{7}{\rm Be}\) bound-state binding energies and the \(p-{}^{6}{\rm Li}\) elastic scattering phase shifts from partial-wave analysis. The radiative capture cross section is computed using electric dipole (E1) and electric quadrupole (E2) operators in the long-wavelength approximation, and spectroscopic factors are introduced to account for the internal cluster structure of \({}^{7}{\rm Be}\). The photon angular distribution is also calculated and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

*   The \({}^{7}{\rm Be}\) ground state binding energy is reproduced as \(-5.606800\) MeV and the first excited state as \(-5.176700\) MeV, both to the sixth digit.
*   The asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for the ground state and first excited state are calculated as 2.654 and 2.528, respectively (no experimental data available for comparison).
*   The zero-energy astrophysical \(S\)-factor from the present work is \(S(0)=103.9\) eV b for the sum of ground-state and first-excited-state captures.
*   Combining all phenomenological potential results, the authors quote \(S(0)= (103.5\pm 4.5)\ {\rm eV\ b}\).
*   The polynomial fit coefficients for the total \(S\)-factor are \(S_1(0)=-105.1\) eV b/MeV and \(S_2(0)=45.0\) eV b/MeV\(^2\).
*   For the hypothetical resonance, the authors obtain a resonance energy \(E_R=197\) keV and a width \(\Gamma\sim15\) keV when introduced in the \({}^{4}S_{3/2}\) partial wave, with a spectroscopic factor \({\cal{S}}_{\rm{res}}\sim0.011\).

## Context

This work extends the cluster-model studies of the \({}^{6}{\rm Li}(p,\gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}\) reaction by using a two-body phenomenological potential similar to that of Souza (2011), but also calculating the photon angular distribution for comparison with data from Chasteler (1987). The results are consistent with earlier phenomenological calculations by He (2010), Souza (2011), and Barker (1980), and with the four-cluster model of Kadyrov (2002), but disagree with the energy dependence of the Gamow shell model calculation by Giraud (2017). The study enables improved estimates of systematic uncertainties in experimental \(S\)-factor measurements, particularly the correction from angular distribution effects at BBN energies, and provides a framework for testing the resonance-like structure proposed by He (2013) that the LUNA Collaboration is currently investigating.
