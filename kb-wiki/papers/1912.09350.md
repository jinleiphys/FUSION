# Heavy-light flavour correlations of anisotropic flows at LHC energies within event-by-event transport approach

> The heavy quarks (HQs) are unique probe of the hot QCD matter properties and their dynamics is coupled to the locally thermalized expanding quark gluon plasma. We present here a novel study of the event by event correlations between light and heavy flavour flow harmonics at LHC energy within a transport approach. Interaction between heavy quarks and light quarks have been taken into account exploring the impact of different temperature dependence of the transport coefficients $D_s$ and $Γ$. Our study indicates that $v^{heavy}_n-v^{light}_n$ correlation and the relative fluctuations of anisotropic flows, $σ_{v_{n}}/\langle v_n \rangle$, are novel observables to understand the heavy quark-bulk interaction and are sensitive to the temperature dependence even to moderate differences of $D_s(T)$, or $Γ(T)$. Hence a comparison of such new observables for HQ to upcoming experimental data at both RHIC and LHC can put further constraints on heavy quark transport coefficients and in particular on its temperature dependence toward a solid comparison between the phenomenological determination and the lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that event-by-event correlations between heavy and light flavour flow harmonics ($v_n^{heavy}$-$v_n^{light}$) and the relative fluctuations of anisotropic flows ($\sigma_{v_n}/\langle v_n \rangle$) are novel observables sensitive to the temperature dependence of heavy quark transport coefficients $D_s(T)$ or $\Gamma(T)$, even for moderate differences in that temperature dependence.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic Boltzmann transport equation for charm quarks interacting with a bulk medium of quarks and gluons, where the bulk evolution is also solved via transport with a fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. Event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions are generated using a Monte Carlo Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon nucleon distribution, and charm quarks are initialized according to the number of binary collisions with a FONLL momentum distribution. Heavy quark hadronization to D mesons is treated via a hybrid approach of coalescence plus fragmentation, using a Peterson fragmentation function and a Wigner formalism for coalescence.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon-nucleon cross section used in the Monte Carlo Glauber model is $\sigma_{NN}=7.0\ \text{fm}^2$.
* The Gaussian width for smearing nucleon positions into parton density is fixed to $\sigma_{xy}=0.5\ \text{fm}$.
* The quark-hadron transition temperature is set to $T_c=155\ \text{MeV}$.
* The correlation coefficient $C(2,2)$ between heavy and light quark $v_2$ is approximately $0.95$ and remains almost flat and independent of impact parameter.
* The number of events used is $N_{event}=5000$ per centrality class with $N_{test}=2\times 10^6$ test particles per event.
* The bulk viscosity is fixed to $4\pi\eta/s=1$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Das 2013, Das 2015, Scardina 2017) by introducing event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions that allow study of odd harmonics, and focuses for the first time on $v_n^{heavy}$-$v_n^{light}$ correlations up to 5th order. It builds on prior work showing that a quasi-particle model (QPM) can reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics (Plumari 2011) and that the temperature dependence of transport coefficients is key for simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ (Das 2015). The results enable future comparison with upcoming experimental data at RHIC and LHC to further constrain the temperature dependence of heavy quark transport coefficients, complementing Bayesian model-to-data analyses (Xu 2017) and T-matrix approaches (TAMU group, He 2012).
