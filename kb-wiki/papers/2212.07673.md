# Beyond-Mean-Field with an Effective Hamiltonian Mapped from an Energy Density Functional

> A method for beyond-mean-field calculations based on an energy density functional is described. The main idea is to map the energy surface for the nuclear quadrupole deformation, obtained from an energy density functional at the mean-field level, into an effective Hamiltonian expressed as a many-body operator. The advantage of this procedure is that one avoids the problems with density dependence which can arise in beyond-mean-field methods. The effective Hamiltonian is then used in a straightforward way in the generator-coordinate-method with the inclusion of projections onto good particle numbers and angular momentum. In the end, both spectra and wave functions are obtained. As an example of the method, calculations for the nucleus $ ^{62} $Zn is performed with three different parametrizations of the Skyrme functional. The results are compared with experiment.

## Key claim
The paper presents a method that maps the energy surface for quadrupole deformation from an energy density functional (EDF) into an effective Hamiltonian with no density dependence, thereby avoiding formal issues that arise in beyond-mean-field methods when using density-dependent interactions directly. The method is demonstrated for the nucleus ⁶²Zn, and the authors note that while the overall structure agrees with experiment, the energies increase too fast with angular momentum for the UNE1 parametrization.

## Method
The authors first perform constrained Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using an EDF (Skyrme functional) to obtain the binding energy as a function of quadrupole deformation E(β₂). They then postulate an effective Hamiltonian H_eff containing spherical single-particle energies, a seniority pairing term with strength G, and a modified quadrupole interaction with strength χ; the parameter χ is fitted so that H_eff reproduces the E(β₂) curve from the EDF. With χ fixed, they use H_eff in the generator-coordinate-method (GCM) with cranking, sampling the (β,γ) deformation plane, and perform projections onto good particle numbers and angular momentum before solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain spectra and wave functions.

## Key numbers
- The E(β₂) curve was fitted between β₂ = -0.15 and β₂ = 0.30 for all three Skyrme parametrizations (UNE1, SLy4, SKM*).
- For UNE1, the (β,γ)-plane was sampled with 250 points within -30° ≤ γ ≤ 150° and β ≤ 0.36; only states below excitation energy E* = 16 MeV were kept, resulting in a basis of 209 states.
- For SLy4 and SKM*, the (β,γ)-plane was sampled with 400 points within -30° ≤ γ ≤ 150° and β ≤ 0.60 (SLy4) or β ≤ 0.48 (SKM*); a quadratic cut E* = [8 + 0.07 × I_x(I_x+1)] MeV was used, resulting in basis sizes of 151 (SLy4) and 145 (SKM*) states.
- For angular momentum projection, 28 points for each Euler angle were used for UNE1; 32 points were used for SLy4 and SKM*. All spins up to 10ℏ were projected out.
- The experimental binding energy of ⁶²Zn is E = -538.1 MeV (given for comparison; HF energies are higher because pairing is not included).
- The maximal spin from valence nucleons in the fp-shell is 10ℏ; a configuration with two neutrons excited into g_9/2 terminates at 16ℏ.

## Context
This work extends beyond-mean-field methods that use energy density functionals, addressing formal issues such as ultraviolet divergences (Carlsson, 2013) and the ambiguous treatment of density dependence in the mixing process (Satula; Duguet; Waroquier; Bender, 2009). The method enables straightforward many-body calculations for heavy and superheavy nuclei, and the authors plan to use the resulting wave functions as input for constructing optical potentials for reaction studies (Idini, in this volume). The results for ⁶²Zn are compared to experiment and to the authors' previous work (Ljungberg), which did not include odd-spin states.
