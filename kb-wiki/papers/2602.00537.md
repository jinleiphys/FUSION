# Model Study of Eigen-Microstate Signatures of Criticality in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present a comprehensive model study of the eigen-microstate approach (EMA) for identifying critical fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using UrQMD and two stochastic baseline models, we demonstrate that EMA is insensitive to conventional short-range correlations and effectively filters out non-critical backgrounds. Critical fluctuations embedded via event-level or particle-level replacement with CMC events generate characteristic cluster-like eigen-microstate patterns and enhanced leading eigenvalues, with event-level criticality producing stronger responses. The eigen microstates exhibit the same pattern across different scales, demonstrating that the fractal nature of critical fluctuations is captured by the eigen microstates. Finite-size scaling of eigenvalue ratios exhibits fixed-point behavior, confirming the largest eigenvalue as an effective order-parameter-like quantity. These results demonstrate that EMA offers a robust and background-independent method for critical-point searches in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan and future heavy-ion experiments.

# Model Study of Eigen-Microstate Signatures of Criticality in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The eigen-microstate approach (EMA) can identify critical fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by filtering out non-critical backgrounds, but its sensitivity depends strongly on whether criticality is embedded at the event level (where a fraction of events are fully critical) versus the particle level (where only a fraction of particles in each event are critical), with event-level criticality producing detectable signatures at much smaller fractions.

## Method
The authors generated 20,000 events of 0-5% most-central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV using the UrQMD model (v3.4) as a non-critical baseline, plus two stochastic models sharing the same multiplicity distribution but with and without kinetic constraints. They embedded critical fluctuations using the Critical Monte Carlo (CMC) model via two hybrid construction methods: event-level replacement (fraction alpha_e of UrQMD events replaced entirely by CMC events) and particle-level replacement (fraction alpha_p of particles in each UrQMD event replaced by CMC particles with a p_t-matching condition). The EMA was applied by discretizing the (p_x, p_y) plane into N = L x L bins and diagonalizing the event-event correlation matrix to obtain eigenvalues and eigen-microstates.

## Key numbers
- UrQMD baseline: 0-5% most-central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, with acceptance cuts of |eta| < 0.5, 0.2 < p_t < 1.6 GeV/c for pions and kaons, 0.4 < p_t < 2.0 GeV/c for protons and antiprotons
- Each ensemble contains M = 20,000 events
- For event-level replacement: cluster-like patterns appear in EM2 and EM3 at alpha_e = 0.1%, clear patch structures appear at alpha_e = 0.8%, and strong stable patterns at alpha_e = 4%
- For particle-level replacement: embryonic cluster patterns appear at alpha_p = 3%, more visible development at alpha_p = 9%, and clearly developed patterns at alpha_p = 12%
- Stochastic model II used a flow-modulated azimuthal distribution with coefficient 0.08 cos(2*phi)
- Particle-level replacement used p_t-matching condition |p_t^CMC - p_t^UrQMD| < 0.2 GeV/c
- Finite-size scaling examined at L = 10, 40, 60, and 100 bins per dimension
- Fixed-point behavior of ratio w_3/w_1 observed for alpha_e >= 4% and alpha_p >= 9%

## Context
This work extends the eigen-microstate approach previously applied to equilibrium spin models (ChenXS, 2020-2022), nonequilibrium living systems, vortex streets, and atmospheric dynamics, and recently to relativistic heavy-ion collisions (eigen-1, 2024). It directly addresses the challenge of searching for the QCD critical point in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, where prior observables such as higher-order cumulants of conserved charges (Stephanov, 1999; STAR, 2014), factorial moments (WuYF, 2017), and multi-particle correlations have been strongly affected by non-critical backgrounds from resonance decays, global conservation laws, hadronic rescatterings, and collective flow. The results provide direct guidance for applying EMA to experimental data from RHIC BES II and future experiments at FAIR, NICA, and HIRFL-CSR, suggesting that scanning collision energy and centrality for the onset of cluster-like EM patterns and fixed-point behavior in eigenvalue ratios may locate the QCD critical point.
