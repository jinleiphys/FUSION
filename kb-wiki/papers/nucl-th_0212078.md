# High Spin Proton Emitters in Odd-Odd Nuclei and Shape Changes

> We present a formalism to describe proton emission from odd-odd nuclei based on a scattering like approach. Special emphasis is given to the case of transitions between states with different deformations. As an example we estimate the proton half-life of the odd-odd nucleus $^{58}$Cu. Our calculations show that the change of deformation in the decay process has a significant influence on the half-life. In addition, the angular momentum coupling of proton and neutron orbitals can result in an important $K$-hindrance of the decay. To fully account for the observed half-life in $^{58}$Cu, one has to consider a shape mixing in the final state.

# High Spin Proton Emitters in Odd-Odd Nuclei and Shape Changes

## Key claim

The formalism shows that proton emission from odd-odd nuclei can involve transitions between states with different deformations, and that the measured half-life of ⁵⁸Cu can only be reproduced by assuming shape mixing in the daughter nucleus ⁵⁷Ni, where a small spherical component (amplitude ≈0.1) coexists with a dominant oblate component (amplitude ≈0.9). The abstract glosses over the fact that neither a pure prolate-to-spherical nor a pure prolate-to-oblate transition alone reproduces the experimental half-life; only a mixed final state works.

## Method

The authors developed a scattering-like formalism for proton emission from odd-odd axially symmetric deformed nuclei, solving the Schrödinger equation for the outgoing proton in a Woods-Saxon mean field with universal parametrization. They computed the decay width using a formation amplitude that includes the overlap of initial and final BCS core states, Nilsson wave functions, and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for angular momentum coupling. Total Routhian surface (TRS) calculations with Lipkin-Nogami pairing were performed to determine the deformations of ⁵⁸Cu (prolate, β₂=0.35) and ⁵⁷Ni (oblate, β₂≈-0.2), and Coriolis mixing was included to account for K-mixing in the initial and final states.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole deformation of the mother nucleus ⁵⁸Cu is β₂=0.35 (prolate), with hexadecapole deformation β₄=0.05.
- The daughter nucleus ⁵⁷Ni has an oblate deformation β₂≈-0.2 for the J_f=9/2⁺ state, which is approximately 600 keV lower in energy than the spherical minimum.
- Assuming the same prolate deformation for mother and daughter gives a half-life T≈0.8×10⁻¹⁶ s, which underestimates the experimental value T_exp≈0.5×10⁻¹² s (Rud01) by four orders of magnitude.
- Assuming a prolate mother and spherical daughter gives T≈0.24×10⁻¹⁴ s (overlap I²≈3.2×10⁻²), still two orders of magnitude below experiment.
- Assuming a prolate mother and oblate daughter gives T≈0.7×10⁻¹⁴ s (overlap I²≈10⁻²).
- With Coriolis mixing and K_i=2, K_f=K_ν=5/2, the half-life becomes T≈1.8×10⁻¹⁴ s; including the neutron preformation factor P²_ν≈10⁻⁵ gives T≈1.8×10⁻⁹ s, overestimating experiment by three orders of magnitude.
- The experimental half-life is reproduced when the daughter 9/2⁺ state has a spherical component amplitude X_sph≈0.1 and an oblate component amplitude X_obl≈0.9.

## Context

This work extends prior scattering-like approaches to proton emission (Bug89, Kad96, Abe97, Dav00, Esb01) and R-matrix formalisms (Mag99, Kru00, Bar00), which were restricted to odd-even to even-even decays with identical shapes in mother and daughter. It specifically addresses the case of ⁵⁸Cu, whose prompt proton decay from a highly deformed state to a near-spherical state was reported by Rud98. The formalism enables analysis of shape changes and K-hindrance in odd-odd proton emitters, and the authors conclude that proton decay is a sensitive probe of intrinsic nuclear structure at the drip line, including shape mixing and Coriolis effects.
