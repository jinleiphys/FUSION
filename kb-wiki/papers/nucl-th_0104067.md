# Determination of pi-N scattering lengths from pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium data

> The pi-N s-wave scattering lengths have been inferred from a joint analysis of the pionic hydrogen and the pionic deuterium x-ray data using a non-relativistic approach in which the pi-N interaction is simulated by a short-ranged potential. The pi-d scattering length has been calculated exactly by solving the Faddeev equations and also by using a static approximation. It has been shown that the same very accurate static formula for pi-d scattering length can be derived (i) from a set of boundary conditions; (ii) by a reduction of Faddeev equations; and (iii) through a summation of Feynman diagrams. By imposing the requirement that the pi-d scattering length, resulting from Faddeev-type calculation, be in agreement with pionic deuterium data, we obtain bounds on the pi-N scattering lengths. The dominant source of uncertainty on the deduced values of the pi-N scattering lengths are the experimental errors in the pionic hydrogen data.

## Key claim

The paper infers pi-N s-wave scattering lengths from a joint analysis of pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium x-ray data, using a non-relativistic potential model where the pi-N interaction is simulated by a short-ranged potential with a free range parameter; the dominant source of uncertainty on the deduced values is the experimental errors in the pionic hydrogen data, not the theoretical model assumptions.

## Method

The author solves the pionic hydrogen bound state problem exactly using a two-channel isospin-invariant separable potential, expressing the pi-N scattering lengths analytically in terms of the range parameter and the measured 1s level shift and width. For the pi-deuteron system, the pi-d scattering length is calculated both by solving the Faddeev equations numerically with separable potentials (including s-wave and P33 p-wave pi-N interactions, and NN interactions from a Hulthen-Yamaguchi or PEST potential) and by deriving a static approximation formula from boundary conditions, reduction of Faddeev equations, or summation of Feynman diagrams. The requirement that the Faddeev-calculated pi-d scattering length agrees with pionic deuterium data yields bounds on the pi-N scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- The experimental error on the pi-d scattering length is at the level of 2%.
- The imaginary part of the pi-d scattering length constitutes only about a quarter of the real part of a_pi_d.
- The P33 scattering volume is taken to be 0.64 fm^3.
- The p-wave form factor inverse range parameter beta_Delta = 5.33 fm^{-1}.
- The NN Hulthen-Yamaguchi potential inverse range parameter beta_N = 6.01162 sqrt(M B) where B is the deuteron binding energy.

## Context

This work extends prior pi-N phase shift analyses (SAID, GIB98, Gashi) and pionic x-ray measurements on pionic hydrogen (Sigg, SCH99) and pionic deuterium (HAU98) by using only atomic data to extract scattering lengths without recourse to scattering data. It improves upon earlier multiple-scattering expansion approaches (Baru, PETROV, AFN74, MIZ77) and the second-order formula for pi-d scattering length (ERI88) by providing an exact Faddeev solution and a static approximation that accounts for binding energy corrections. The results enable more precise input for the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule and for chiral perturbation theory calculations of low-energy pi-N parameters.
