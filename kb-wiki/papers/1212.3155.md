# Consequence of total lepton number violation in strongly magnetized iron white dwarfs

> The influence of neutrinoless electron to positron conversion on cooling of strongly magnetized iron white dwarfs is studied. It is shown that they can be good candidates for soft gamma-ray repeaters and anomalous X-ray pulsars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that strongly magnetized iron white dwarfs (SMIWDs) could be good candidates for soft gamma-ray repeaters and anomalous X-ray pulsars, but only if the neutrinoless electron-to-positron conversion process (a lepton number violating reaction analogous to neutrinoless double beta decay) can occur in their interiors. This requires that the electron Fermi energy exceeds the threshold energy for the double charge exchange reaction, which is only possible in the presence of a strong magnetic field that allows the Fermi energy to overcome the inverse beta decay threshold that would otherwise terminate the iron white dwarf.

## Method

The authors analyze the energetics of the neutrinoless electron capture reaction e- + X(A,Z) -> X(A,Z-2) + e+ for the specific case of 56Fe to 56Cr in strongly magnetized iron white dwarfs, using the threshold energies from atomic mass evaluation and comparing them with the electron Fermi energies achievable in the presence of strong magnetic fields (B >= B_c = 4.414 x 10^13 G). They survey the existing literature on strongly magnetized white dwarfs, including the Landau quantization formalism for electron motion in strong magnetic fields, and discuss the competing inverse beta decay process that would normally prevent the double charge exchange reaction from occurring.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field B_c = 4.414 x 10^13 G, above which the magnetic field is considered strong enough to enable the proposed mechanism
- Threshold energy for the double charge exchange reaction 56Fe -> 56Cr: Delta^{beta beta}_Z = 6.33 MeV
- Threshold energy for inverse beta decay of 56Fe: Delta^{beta}_Z = 3.794 MeV (with an additional 109 keV needed to excite 56Mn*(1+))
- Critical density for onset of inverse beta decay in iron: rho_B = (1.14 - 1.18) x 10^9 g/cm^3, which terminates the iron white dwarf
- For 12C, the critical density for inverse beta decay is 3.97 x 10^10 g/cm^3, while the critical density for gravitational instability is 2.12 x 10^10 g/cm^3
- The non-equilibrium heating from electron capture by 56Mn releases 476 keV per capture
- Without non-equilibrium heating, cooling from T = 5.5 x 10^6 K requires 4 x 10^8 yr; with heating, the white dwarf cools to approximately 10^6 K over 20 Gyr
- For the 56Fe -> 56Cr pair, the inverse beta decay threshold Delta^{beta}_Z = 3.794 MeV is smaller than the double charge exchange threshold of 6.33 MeV plus the electron mass

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Isern et al. (various years) that white dwarf luminosity functions could be used to study elementary particle physics, specifically applying it to the lepton number violating process of neutrinoless double beta decay analogues. The paper builds on extensive calculations of iron white dwarf properties by Bravo et al. (various years) and the two-step inverse beta decay chain studied by Bisnovatyi-Kogan and collaborators (various years). The work enables future studies of whether strongly magnetized white dwarfs can serve as astrophysical laboratories for testing lepton number violation, though the authors note that microscopic models of strongly magnetized white dwarf development, internal structure, and cooling have not yet reached general acceptance.
