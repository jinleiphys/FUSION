# $^2$H$(e,e'p)$ observables using a Regge model parameterization of final state interactions

> In previous papers we have presented a calculation describing electrodisintegration of the deuteron at GeV energies. The model is fully relativistic and incorporates full spin dependence of the final state interactions (FSI), which were obtained from the SAID analysis. It was, however, limited kinematically due to lack of availability of the SAID amplitudes. This work rectifies this problem by implementing a Regge model to describe the FSI. We present an outline of the model and show comparisons between the two approaches in a region of overlap. We see good agreement between the models, and note observables which can provide additional insight due to model sensitivity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Regge model parameterization of final state interactions (FSI) can replace the previously used SAID partial wave analysis for deuteron electrodisintegration calculations at GeV energies, extending the kinematic range beyond the SAID limit of 1.3 GeV laboratory kinetic energy. The authors find good agreement between the two approaches in the overlapping kinematic region, but note that certain observables (particularly \(A_{TT}\), \(A^{l'}_{p}\), and \(A^{l'}_{ep}\)) show significant model sensitivity that could provide additional insight into FSI effects.

## Method

The authors calculate deuteron electrodisintegration observables using a fully relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) model based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation, incorporating full spin dependence of the \(np\) scattering matrix via Fermi invariant parameterization. They replace the previously used SAID helicity amplitudes (limited to laboratory kinetic energies below 1.3 GeV) with a Regge model fit to \(NN\) scattering data from \(s=5.4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) to \(s=4000\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), parameterizing the five Fermi invariants in terms of Regge pole exchanges. The comparison between SAID and Regge FSI is performed for two kinematic settings (Bjorken \(x=1.0\) and \(x=1.3\)) using the onshell approximation for FSI, and results are presented for unpolarized cross sections, response functions, and various single and double spin asymmetries for polarized deuteron target and polarized ejected proton.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic set 1: \(x=1.0\), \(Q^2=2.4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), beam energy \(6.25\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(s=5.91\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), electron scattering angle \(15.97^\circ\)
- Kinematic set 2: \(x=1.3\), \(Q^2=4.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), beam energy \(8.6\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(s=5.93\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), electron scattering angle \(16.0^\circ\)
- For \(x=1\) kinematics at missing momentum \(p_m=1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}\), the SAID FSI cross section ratio to PWIA is approximately 8, while the Regge FSI ratio is approximately 5
- For \(x=1\) kinematics at \(p_m<0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\), SAID and Regge results differ by up to 50 percent from the PWIA result
- The Regge model fit covers \(NN\) scattering data from \(s=5.4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) to \(s=4000\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)
- The SAID partial wave analysis is reliable up to laboratory kinetic energy of 1.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of deuteron electrodisintegration (Van Orden et al., 2008; Jeschonnek et al., 2009) which were limited by the kinematic range of SAID partial wave amplitudes. The Regge model approach, previously fitted to \(NN\) scattering data by Ford et al. (2013), removes this limitation and enables calculations at higher energies, including those accessible after the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade. The paper identifies specific observables (\(A_{TT}\), \(A^{l'}_{p}\), and \(A^{l'}_{ep}\)) where model sensitivity is largest, suggesting these as promising targets for future experimental measurements to discriminate between FSI models and improve understanding of the reaction mechanism.
