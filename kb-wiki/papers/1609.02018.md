# Practical quasi parton distribution functions

> A completely new strategy to calculate parton distribution functions on the lattice has recently been proposed. In this method, lattice calculable observables, called quasi distributions, are related to normal distributions. The quasi distributions are known to contain power-law UV divergences arise from a Wilson line in the non-local operator, while the normal distributions only have logatithmic UV divergences. We propose possible method to subtract the power divegence to make the matching of the quasi with the normal distributions well-defined. We also demonstrate the matching of the quasi quark distribution between continuum and lattice implementing the power divergence subtraction. The matching calculations are carried out by one-loop perturbation.

## Key claim

The authors propose a nonperturbative subtraction method for the power-law UV divergences arising from the Wilson line in quasi parton distribution functions, which is necessary to make the perturbative matching between quasi and normal distributions well-defined; the matching between continuum and lattice quasi distributions is demonstrated at one-loop order, but the power divergence subtraction must be performed nonperturbatively to avoid ruining perturbative accuracy.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop perturbative corrections to the non-local matrix element defining quasi quark distributions in covariant gauge, identifying the linear UV divergence originating from the Wilson line in the tadpole-type diagram. They propose subtracting this power divergence using the static q\bar{q} potential extracted from Wilson loops, where the mass renormalization parameter δm is determined by imposing a renormalization condition on the potential at a chosen reference distance R₀. The matching between continuum and lattice quasi distributions is performed in coordinate space (before Fourier transform over δz) using one-loop lattice perturbation theory with naive fermions and plaquette gluon action.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop amplitude for the non-local matrix element at zero external momentum is expressed as: ℳ_{δz}(P₃=0) = [1 + (g/4π)² C_F 𝒜(δz) + O(g⁴)] × [ℳ_{δz}(P₃=0)]^{tree}, where C_F = (N_c²-1)/(2N_c) for SU(N_c)
- The subtracted one-loop amplitude after removing the linear divergence is: ℳ_{δz}^S(P₃=0) = [1 + (g/4π)² C_F (𝒜^{subt}(δz) - 2π|δz|/R₀) + O(g⁴)] × [ℳ_{δz}^S(P₃=0)]^{tree}
- The continuum one-loop static potential is: V(R) = -g²C_F/(4πR) + g²C_F ∫_{k_{⊥0}} 1/k_{⊥0}² + O(g⁴)
- The d-1 dimensional tadpole integral used in the subtraction is: T_{d-1} = ∫_{k_{⊥z}} 1/k_{⊥z}²

## Context

This work extends Ji's original proposal (Ji, 2013) for quasi parton distribution functions and the collinear factorization approach introduced by Ma and Qiu (Ma, 2014a; Ma, 2014b), which treats quasi distributions as lattice cross sections factorizable into hard parts and normal PDFs. The paper addresses the UV inconsistency between quasi distributions (power-law divergent) and normal distributions (logarithmically divergent), which was identified in earlier work by Xiong et al. (2013). The subtraction method using the static potential follows techniques developed for Wilson line renormalization in the 1980s (Dotsenko, 1979; Arefeva, 1980; Craigie, 1980; Dorn, 1986) and applied to heavy-light systems (Boucaud, 1989; Eichten, 1989; Maiani, 1991; Martinelli, 1998), with the specific implementation adapted from Musch et al. (2010). This enables future lattice QCD calculations of PDFs with controlled systematic uncertainties from power divergences, higher twist effects (O(Λ_QCD²/μ̃²)), and lattice artifacts (O(Λ_QCD²a²)).
