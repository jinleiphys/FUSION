# Couplings of light I=0 scalar mesons to simple operators in the complex plane

> The flavour and glue structure of the light scalar mesons in QCD are probed by studying the couplings of the I=0 mesons $σ(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ to the operators $\bar{q}q$, $α_s G^2$ and to two photons. The Roy dispersive representation for the $ππ$ amplitude $t_0^0(s)$ is used to determine the pole positions as well as the residues in the complex plane. On the real axis, $t_0^0$ is constrained to solve the Roy equation together with elastic unitarity up to the $K\Kbar$ threshold leading to an improved description of the $f_0(980)$. The problem of using a two-particle threshold as a matching point is discussed. A simple relation is established between the coupling of a scalar meson to an operator $j_S$ and the value of the related pion form-factor computed at the resonance pole. Pion scalar form-factors as well as two-photon partial-wave amplitudes are expressed as coupled-channel Omnès dispersive representations. Subtraction constants are constrained by chiral symmetry and experimental data. Comparison of our results for the $\bar{q}q$ couplings with earlier determinations of the analogous couplings of the lightest I=1 and $I=1/2$ scalar mesons are compatible with an assignment of the $σ$, $κ$, $a_0(980)$, $f_0(980)$ into a nonet. Concerning the gluonic operator $α_s G^2$ we find a significant coupling to both the $σ$ and the $f_0(980)$.

## Key claim

The paper uses Roy dispersive representations to extract the couplings of the I=0 scalar mesons σ(600) and f0(980) to quark and gluon operators, finding that the results are compatible with assigning these mesons together with the κ and a0(980) into a flavor nonet, but the conclusions depend on the specific choice of the inelasticity function η00(s) used to constrain the Roy equation solutions.

## Method

The author solves the Roy integral equation for the ππ S-wave amplitude t00(s) up to the KKbar threshold, using a modified Schenk parametrization with up to ten parameters to achieve an accuracy of |ε(s)| ≈ 5×10−4 below the matching point. The amplitude is then continued to the second Riemann sheet to extract pole positions and residues, and coupled-channel Omnès dispersive representations are used to compute pion scalar form-factors and two-photon partial-wave amplitudes at the resonance poles.

## Context

This work extends the Roy equation analysis of the ππ amplitude from Caprini, Colangelo, and Leutwyler (2006) by pushing the matching point up to the KKbar threshold to improve the description of the f0(980). The results for σ and f0 couplings to q̄q operators are compared with earlier determinations by Maltman (1999) for I=1 and I=1/2 scalar mesons, and the two-photon couplings are studied using the dispersive framework of Garcia-Martin and Moussallam (2010) with updated phase-shifts from the Roy solutions. The findings enable a more precise classification of the light scalar nonet and provide input for lattice QCD studies of scalar meson structure.
