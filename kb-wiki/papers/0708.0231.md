# Forward inclusive dijet production and azimuthal correlations in pA collisions

> We derive forward inclusive dijet production in the scattering of a dilute hadron off an arbitrary dense target, whose partons with small fraction of momentum x are described by a Color Glass Condensate. Both multiple scattering and non-linear QCD evolution at small-x are included. This is of relevance for measurements of two-particle correlations in the proton direction of proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The azimuthal angle distribution is peaked back to back and broadens as the momenta of the measured particles gets closer to the saturation scale.

## Key claim
The paper derives the forward inclusive dijet production cross-section in proton-nucleus collisions using the Color Glass Condensate framework, but the azimuthal angle distribution only broadens as momenta approach the saturation scale, it does not produce a double-peak structure, which the author indicates would require final-state effects not included in this initial-state calculation.

## Method
The author computes the inclusive quark-gluon production cross-section for the process qT → qgX at leading order in α_S and to all orders in the strong coupling times the CGC color field, using light-cone perturbation theory and eikonal scattering off a dense target described by classical color fields. The cross-section is expressed in terms of 2-, 4-, and 6-point functions of Wilson-line operators, which are averaged over a Gaussian distribution of color sources and evolved using the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation in the large-N_c limit, starting from the McLerran-Venugopalan model at x_0 = 0.01.

## Key numbers
- The initial saturation scale for a Gold or Lead nucleus is set to 2π Q²_{s_0} = 2 GeV² at x_0 = 0.01.
- The saturation scale evolves as Q²_s(x) = Q²_{s_0} (x_0/x)^{v\barα} with v = 4.88 (γ_c = 0.6275) in the leading-logarithmic BFKL approximation.
- The BK evolution kernel uses the strong coupling combination \barα = α_S N_c/π.

## Context
This work extends the CGC treatment of forward particle production in pA collisions beyond the soft-gluon approximation used by Braun et al. (2005) and beyond the k_T-factorization approach of Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2003), showing that dijet production requires up to 6-point Wilson-line correlators rather than single gluon distributions. The result is similar to that of Nikolaev, Schafer, Zakharov, and Zoller (2005) but treats the n-point functions differently, and it enables future studies of whether azimuthal decorrelation in pA collisions at RHIC and LHC arises from initial-state saturation or final-state medium effects.
