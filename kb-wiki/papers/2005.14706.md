# Probing short-range correlations in the deuteron via incoherent diffractive $J/ψ$ production with spectator tagging at the EIC

> Understanding the role of Quantum Chromodynamics in generating nuclear forces is important for uncovering the mechanism of short-ranged nuclear interactions and their manifestation in short range correlations (SRC). The future Electron-Ion-Collider (EIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory in the US will provide an unprecedented opportunity to systematically investigate the underlying physics of SRC for energies and kinematic regions that are otherwise impossible to reach. We study SRCs in electron-deuteron scattering events using the Monte Carlo event generator BeAGLE. Specifically, we investigate the sensitivity of observables to high internal nucleon momentum in incoherent diffractive $J/ψ$ vector meson production. In a plane wave impulse approximation, the initial state deuteron wavefunction can be accessed directly from the four-momentum of the spectator nucleon. We use realistic physics simulations and far-forward detector simulations of the EIC to fully reveal the physics potential of this exclusive process. In particular, we provide the luminosity and detector requirements necessary to study SRCs in the deuteron at an EIC.

## Key claim

Incoherent diffractive $J/\psi$ production in electron-deuteron collisions at the EIC, combined with spectator tagging, can directly access the initial-state deuteron wavefunction via the plane wave impulse approximation, enabling systematic study of short-range correlations (SRC) in the high-momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution, provided that detector and beam-related smearing effects are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors used the BeAGLE Monte Carlo event generator to simulate incoherent diffractive $J/\psi$ production in electron-deuteron scattering at a center-of-mass energy corresponding to 18 GeV electrons on 110 GeV per nucleon deuterons, with kinematic cuts of 1 < Q^2 < 10 GeV^2 and 0.01 < y < 0.95. The deuteron wavefunction was implemented in a light-front formalism based on the non-relativistic nucleon momentum distribution from Ciofi degli Atti (1995), and the spectator nucleon four-momentum was used to reconstruct the initial-state leading nucleon momentum via the plane wave impulse approximation. Realistic detector simulations were performed for four far-forward detector systems (B0 tracker, off-momentum detectors, Roman Pots, and zero-degree calorimeter) including beam-related effects such as angular divergence, crab cavity rotation, and beam energy spread.

## Key numbers

* The spectator three-momentum distribution shows the mean-field region below 300 MeV/c and the SRC region above 300 MeV/c, with the high-momentum tail extending to approximately 1 GeV/c.
* Neutron spectator acceptance is nearly 100% up to 600 MeV/c and about 80% at p_m approximately 1 GeV/c.
* The ZDC energy resolution used was sigma_E/E = 50%/sqrt(E) + 5%, with angular resolution sigma_theta = 3 mrad/sqrt(E).
* The B0 silicon tracker covers polar angles from 5.5 to 25.0 mrad with transverse momentum resolution of 15-30 MeV/c; off-momentum detectors and Roman Pots cover 0.0 to 5.0 mrad with 20-30 MeV/c resolution.
* Hadron beam angular divergence was 65 microrad horizontal and 229 microrad vertical; vertex smearing from crab cavity was 1.25 mm in x and 0.2 mm in y.
* 98% of dissociative events can be experimentally rejected using both main detector and forward detectors.

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations from fixed-target experiments at JLab (Egiyan 2007, Schmookler 2019) and the theoretical framework for spectator tagging in inclusive deep inelastic scattering developed by Strikman (2017) to the exclusive channel of incoherent diffractive J/psi production at the EIC. The proposed measurement addresses open questions about the role of gluons in SRCs and the connection between SRCs and the EMC effect, which were previously studied by Hen (2016) and others. The results enable future experimental programs at the EIC to probe the nucleon-nucleon potential at extremely high momenta and to investigate whether the NN potential differs when probed via exclusive vector meson production compared to inclusive scattering.
