# HERA Anomaly and Hard Charm in the Nucleon

> We explore the possibility that the excess neutral and charged current events seen by the H1 and ZEUS Collaborations at HERA at large x and Q^2 could arise from a hard charm component of the nucleon. While the symmetric intrinsic charm hypothesis is unable to account for the HERA anomaly, a non-symmetric charm distribution generated non-perturbatively, for which c-bar is much harder than c, can produce significant enhancement of cross sections at the HERA kinematics.

# HERA Anomaly and Hard Charm in the Nucleon

## Key claim

A non-symmetric charm distribution in the nucleon, where the anti-charm component is significantly harder than the charm component, can produce a substantial enhancement of neutral and charged current deep-inelastic scattering cross sections at the large x and Q^2 values where the H1 and ZEUS Collaborations observed an excess of events, while the symmetric intrinsic charm hypothesis cannot account for the anomaly.

## Method

The authors model the non-perturbative charm content of the nucleon using a meson cloud framework, where the nucleon fluctuates into a virtual D- meson and Lambda_c baryon configuration. The charm and anti-charm quark distributions are computed via convolution integrals over the light-cone momentum distributions of the D- and Lambda_c, with the anti-charm distribution approximated as the D- distribution in the nucleon and the charm distribution related to the Lambda_c distribution. These non-perturbative distributions are then added to standard CTEQ global parton distribution fits and evolved to Q^2 = 20000 GeV^2 to compute neutral and charged current cross sections for comparison with HERA kinematics.

## Key numbers

- For Q^2 > 35000 GeV^2, ZEUS observed 2 events where approximately 0.15 were expected from the standard DIS model
- For x > 0.55 and y > 0.25, ZEUS found 4 events with approximately 0.9 anticipated
- For Q^2 > 15000 GeV^2, H1 observed 12 neutral current events (approximately 4.7 expected) and 4 charged current events (approximately 1.8 expected)
- The meson cloud model with a cutoff Lambda approximately 2.2 GeV gives an integrated charm probability of approximately 1%; a cutoff of approximately 1.7 GeV gives 0.5%
- With a 0.5% (1%) intrinsic charm component, the charged current cross section increases by a factor of approximately 2 (3) for invariant mass M between 200 and 250 GeV at Q^2 = 20000 GeV^2

## Context

This work addresses the excess of events at large x and Q^2 reported by the H1 and ZEUS Collaborations at HERA, which had prompted explanations involving leptoquarks or contact interactions. The authors extend the intrinsic charm model of Gunion and Vogt (1997) and Brodsky et al. (1980, 1981), which assumed symmetric charm and anti-charm distributions and proved too soft to explain the anomaly. The meson cloud model, previously applied to describe the anti-d excess in the proton and strangeness content by Thomas (1983) and Holtmann, Szczurek and Speth (1996), naturally predicts asymmetric charm distributions. The results enable future experimental discrimination between new physics and modified parton distributions through comparison of neutral and charged current cross sections in both e+ p and e- p collisions, and through charm tagging in J/psi production.
