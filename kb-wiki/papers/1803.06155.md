# Few-body quark dynamics for doubly-heavy baryons and tetraquarks

> We discuss the adequate treatment of the 3- and 4-body dynamics for the quark model picture of double-charm baryons and tetraquarks. We stress that the variational and Born-Oppenheimer approximations give energies very close to the exact ones, while the diquark approximation might be rather misleading. The Hall-Post inequalities also provide very useful lower bounds that exclude the possibility of stable tetraquarks for some mass ratios and some color wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a careful treatment of the few-body problem is required before drawing conclusions about the existence of stable doubly-heavy baryons and tetraquarks in quark models; the variational and Born-Oppenheimer approximations give energies very close to the exact ones, while the diquark approximation systematically overbinds and can be misleading, especially for states near the dissociation threshold.

## Method

The authors compare several few-body methods for solving the Schrodinger equation for 3-body (QQq) and 4-body (QQqq) systems with chromoelectric potentials: variational methods using correlated Gaussian expansions and hyperspherical expansions, the Born-Oppenheimer approximation (freezing heavy quarks and solving for light degrees of freedom), the diquark approximation (solving sequentially for clusters), and Hall-Post inequalities (which provide rigorous lower bounds). They test these methods on toy models with linear, soft (r^0.1), Coulomb, and Coulomb-plus-linear potentials as a function of the heavy-to-light quark mass ratio M/m, using frozen color wavefunctions and including color mixing between the 33 and 66 color configurations.

## Key numbers

- For a linear potential with frozen 33 color, the critical mass ratio for tetraquark binding below the two-meson threshold is (M/m)_c greater than or approximately 40; this is reduced to (M/m)_c approximately 32 by including color mixing.
- For a Coulomb-plus-linear potential (kappa=0.4, sigma=0.2 GeV^2, light mass m=0.3 GeV), the critical mass ratio is approximately 18 with color mixing, compared to approximately 28 with a simple Gaussian expansion.
- For a pure Coulomb interaction, the critical mass ratio is (M/m)_c approximately 7.
- For a soft potential r^0.1, the critical mass ratio is (M/m)_c approximately 15.
- For a linear potential with M=5 and m=1, the exact tetraquark energy (frozen 33) is 4.331, while the diquark approximation gives 3.899, showing significant overbinding.
- For the same linear model with M=5 and m=1, the Eichten-Quigg relation (QQqq = QQq + Qqq - Qq) gives 4.357 for the right-hand side versus 4.331 for the left-hand side.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of flavor-exotic tetraquarks initiated by Ader (1981) and Ballot (1983), and addresses contradictory results in the recent literature stimulated by the discovery of the double-charm baryon by Aaij (2017). The paper shows that the diquark approximation, which was used in many earlier studies including those by Fredriksson and Jandel (1981), systematically overbinds tetraquarks and can produce spurious bound states; the authors recommend using variational methods with correlated Gaussians or hyperspherical expansions, or the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, which give results close to exact solutions. The Hall-Post inequalities provide rigorous lower bounds that can exclude the possibility of stable tetraquarks for certain mass ratios and color wavefunctions, enabling more reliable predictions for future experimental searches.
