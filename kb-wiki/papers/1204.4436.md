# Onset of deformation at $N = 112$ in Bi nuclei

> The high spin states in $^{195}$Bi has been studied by $γ$-ray spectroscopic method using the $^{181}$Ta($^{20}$Ne, 6n) fusion evaporation reaction at 130 MeV. The $γγ$ coincidence data were taken using an array of 8 clover HPGe detectors. The spin and parity assignments of the excited states have been made from the measured directional correlation from oriented states (DCO) ratios and integrated polarization asymmetry (IPDCO) ratios. The results show, for the first time, the evidence of a rotational like band based on a 13/2$^+$ band head in this nucleus, indicating the onset of deformation at neutron number $N = 112$ for the Bismuth isotopes. The results obtained were found to be consistent with the prediction of the total Routhian surface calculations using Woods Saxon potential. The same calculations also predict a change in shape from oblate to triaxial in $^{195}$Bi at high rotational frequency.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first observation of a rotational-like band based on a 13/2⁺ band head in ¹⁹⁵Bi, which indicates that the onset of deformation in odd-A bismuth isotopes occurs at neutron number N = 112, extending the previously established border of N = 110. The claim is hedged by the fact that the band is interpreted as rotational based on its similarity to bands in lighter isotopes, and the deformation is inferred from total Routhian surface calculations rather than directly measured.

## Method
High-spin states in ¹⁹⁵Bi were populated via the ¹⁸¹Ta(²⁰Ne, 6n) fusion-evaporation reaction at 130 MeV beam energy (degraded from 145 MeV). Gamma-gamma coincidence data were collected using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) with 8 clover HPGe detectors. Spin and parity assignments were made using directional correlation from oriented states (DCO) ratios and integrated polarization asymmetry (IPDCO) ratios, and the level scheme was built from coincidence relations.

## Key numbers
- The 13/2⁺ band head in ¹⁹⁵Bi lies at 886.7 keV excitation energy, decaying to the 9/2⁻ ground state via an 886.7-keV M2 transition.
- The 29/2⁻ isomer is proposed at 2396 keV excitation energy, decaying via an 86.3-keV E2 transition to a 25/2⁻ state at 2309.5 keV.
- The 13/2⁺ state has a half-life of 32 ns; additional isomers with half-lives of 80 ns and 750 ns were identified by Lönnroth et al. (1986).
- The kinematic moment of inertia J⁽¹⁾ for the rotational band in ¹⁹⁵Bi is closer to the oblate band in ¹⁹⁰Pb than to prolate bands, with values intermediate between those of ¹⁹¹Bi and ¹⁹³Bi.
- Total Routhian surface calculations yield an oblate deformation of β₂ = 0.13 for the 13/2⁺ configuration at zero rotational frequency.

## Context
This work extends the observation of rotational bands based on the πi₁₃/₂ configuration from ¹⁹¹Bi and ¹⁹³Bi (N = 108 and 110, reported by Nie et al., 2004) to ¹⁹⁵Bi at N = 112, pushing the neutron-number border for deformed shapes in odd-A bismuth isotopes. The results are consistent with total Routhian surface calculations using a Woods-Saxon potential, which also predict a shape change from oblate to triaxial at rotational frequencies above ℏω ≈ 0.4 MeV. The study enables future experimental tests of this predicted shape transition and further systematic mapping of deformation onset in the lead region.
