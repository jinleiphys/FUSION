# Exact solution of the spin-isospin proton-neutron pairing Hamiltonian

> The exact solution of proton-neutron isoscalar-isovector (T=0,1) pairing Hamiltonian with non-degenerate single-particle orbits and equal pairing strengths (g_{T=1}= g_{T=0}) is presented for the first time. The Hamiltonian is a particular case of a family of integrable SO(8) Richardson-Gaudin (RG) models. The exact solution of the T=0,1 pairing Hamiltonian is reduced to a problem of 4 sets of coupled non linear equations that determine the spectral parameters of the complete set of eigenstates. The microscopic structure of individual eigenstates is analyzed in terms of evolution of the spectral parameters in the complex plane for system of A=80 nucleons. The spectroscopic trends of the exact solutions are discussed in terms of generalized rotations in isospace.

# Exact solution of the spin-isospin proton-neutron pairing Hamiltonian

## Key claim

The paper presents the first exact solution of the proton-neutron isoscalar-isovector (T=0,1) pairing Hamiltonian with non-degenerate single-particle orbits, but only for the special case where the pairing strengths are equal (g_{T=1} = g_{T=0}), which imposes a conserved U(4) symmetry that organizes eigenstates into degenerate Wigner multiplets.

## Method

The Hamiltonian is realized as a particular linear combination of the integrals of motion of an SO(8) Richardson-Gaudin integrable model. The exact solution reduces to solving four sets of coupled nonlinear Richardson equations for the spectral parameters (e, omega, eta, gamma), which are solved numerically by starting from the weak-coupling limit and iteratively increasing the pairing strength, using an imaginary term in the single-particle energies to avoid singularities from real-axis crossings. The method is applied to a system of A=80 nucleons moving in L=50 equidistant, fourfold degenerate single-particle levels with l=0.

## Key numbers

- System of A=80 nucleons in L=50 equidistant fourfold degenerate levels with single-particle energies epsilon_i = (i-1)/2 for i=1,...,50.
- At weak coupling g=0.16, approximately 6 correlated pairs (15% of pairs) are formed; at intermediate coupling g=0.22, approximately 14 correlated pairs (35% of pairs) are formed, irrespective of isospin.
- The inverse moment of inertia in isospace (1/J_T) converges to the single-particle splitting delta_epsilon = 1/2 at high isospin for all models.
- In the SO(8) case, the linear enhancement factor lambda reaches the Wigner supermultiplet limit lambda ~ 4 for large g (g=0.22) and drops to unity for large T.
- For pure T=1 pairing, signature splitting delta_e_T is approximately constant at -1.5.

## Context

This work extends the Richardson-Gaudin integrable models from rank 1 (SU(2)) and rank 2 algebras (SO(5) for isovector pairing, SO(3,2) for boson pairing, SU(3) for three-level atoms) to the rank 4 SO(8) algebra. The specific Hamiltonian generalizes the isoscalar-isovector pairing model introduced for a single degenerate shell by Flowers (1952) and developed further by Pang (1969) and Dussel (1977). The exact solutions enable microscopic analysis of alpha-like quartet structures and spectroscopic interpretation in terms of generalized rotations in isospace, following the iso-cranking model of Satula (2001). The results confirm the predicted trends of the iso-cranking model and provide a benchmark for understanding the competition between isoscalar and isovector pairing in proton-rich nuclei near the N=Z line.
