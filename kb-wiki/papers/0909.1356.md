# Disentangling the dynamical origin of P11 Nucleon Resonances

> We show that two almost degenerate poles near the $πΔ$ threshold and the next higher mass pole in the $P_{11}$ partial wave of $πN$ scattering evolve from a single bare state through its coupling with $πN$, $ηN$ and $ππN$ reaction channels. This finding provides new information on understanding the dynamical origins of the Roper $N^*(1440)$ and $N^*(1710)$ resonances listed by Particle Data Group. Our results for the resonance poles in other $πN$ partial waves are also presented.

# Disentangling the dynamical origin of P11 Nucleon Resonances

## Key claim
The two almost degenerate poles near the πΔ threshold (associated with the Roper N*(1440)) and the higher mass pole near 1800 MeV (associated with N*(1710)) all evolve from a single bare state at 1763 MeV through its coupling with πN, ηN, and ππN reaction channels, implying that these two PDG-listed resonances originate from the same excited nucleon state rather than from distinct bare states.

## Method
The authors used the JLMS dynamical coupled-channels model, formulated within a Hamiltonian approach, to describe meson-baryon reactions including πN, ηN, and ππN channels (with πΔ, ρN, and σN resonant components). They performed analytic continuation to search for resonance poles on unphysical Riemann sheets by finding zeros of the determinant of the dressed N* propagator inverse, and traced the evolution of pole positions as functions of the coupling strengths to each reaction channel from zero to full strength.

## Key numbers
- Three P11 poles found from a single bare state at 1763 MeV: (1357, 76) MeV, (1364, 105) MeV, and (1820, 248) MeV (listed as Re MR, -Im MR)
- The two lowest poles (1357, 76) and (1364, 105) lie within the PDG range for N*(1440): (1350-1380, 80-110) MeV
- The higher pole (1820, 248) is close to the PDG N*(1710) range: (1670-1770, 40-190) MeV
- Branch points for unstable channels: πΔ at (1365.40, -32.46) MeV, ρN at (1704.08, -74.98) MeV, σN at (1907.57, -323.62) MeV
- Residues for the three P11 poles: (R=36 MeV, φ=-111°), (R=64 MeV, φ=-99°), (R=20 MeV, φ=-168°)
- Branching ratios to πN: 49% for the (1357, 76) pole, 61% for the (1364, 105) pole, and 8% for the (1820, 248) pole, compared to PDG values of 60-70% for N*(1440) and 10-20% for N*(1710)
- No molecular-type resonances found from meson-exchange amplitudes in any partial wave

## Context
This work extends the earlier JLMS model analysis (Suzuki et al., 2007) by performing analytic continuation to extract resonance poles and study their dynamical evolution. It confirms the two-pole structure near the πΔ threshold first found by Arndt, Ford and Roper (1984) and later by Cutkosky and Wang (1990), and also seen in analyses by the GWU/VPI and Jülich groups. The key advance is demonstrating that three P11 poles originate from a single bare state, which has implications for interpreting large Nc QCD and quenched lattice QCD calculations where coupling to the meson-baryon continuum is not fully implemented. The results also confirm most 3- and 4-star PDG resonances but find no evidence for P13(1720), P31(1910), or P33(1600).
