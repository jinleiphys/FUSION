# WHAT DO WE LEARN FROM ATOMIC PHYSICS ABOUT FUNDAMENTAL SYMMETRIES IN NUCLEI AND PARTICLES

> Atomic experiments bring meaningful and valuable information on fundamental symmetries. The hypothesis of a large ($\sim 100$ eV) P-odd weak matrix element between single-particle states in heavy nuclei is inconsistent with the results of atomic PNC experiments. Upper limits on CP-violation obtained in atomic and molecular spectroscopy are as informative as those established in neutron physics. Very strict upper limits on T-odd, P-even interactions (nucleon-nucleon, electron-nucleon, electron-electron, and $β$-decay) are derived from the same atomic and neutron experiments.

## Key claim

Atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) experiments rule out the hypothesis that the weak interaction matrix element between single-particle states in heavy nuclei is as large as ~100 eV, which had been invoked to explain the common sign of neutron asymmetries in ²³²Th and large P-odd correlations in Mössbauer transitions.

## Method

The author compares existing experimental upper limits on the nuclear-spin-dependent (NSD) part of the PNC optical activity in atomic lead vapour with the weak mixing matrix element expected in ²⁰⁷Pb. The NSD P-odd effects in heavy atoms are dominated by the contact electromagnetic interaction of electrons with the nuclear anapole moment, which is induced by PNC nuclear forces. The measured bound on the NSD-to-spin-independent ratio is translated into constraints on the anapole constant, the effective neutron PNC constant, and the weak mixing matrix element for the specific single-particle states in ²⁰⁷Pb.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on the ratio of nuclear-spin-dependent to nuclear-spin-independent PNC optical activity in atomic lead vapour at 95% confidence level: P_NSD / P < 0.02.
- Bound on the dimensionless anapole constant for ²⁰⁷Pb: κ(²⁰⁷Pb) < 1.
- Bound on the effective neutron PNC constant: g_n < 10.
- Simple estimate for a typical weak mixing matrix element: ⟨W⟩ < 20 eV.
- More sophisticated Woods-Saxon calculation gives upper limit on the specific matrix element ⟨3d 5/2⁺|W|2f 5/2⁻⟩ in ²⁰⁷Pb: < 14 eV.
- Best experimental upper limit on neutron electric dipole moment (EDM): d(n)/e < 7 × 10⁻²⁶ cm.
- Thallium experiment result for electron EDM: d(e)/e = (1.8 ± 1.2 ± 1.0) × 10⁻²⁷ cm.
- Record upper limit on atomic EDM of ¹⁹⁹Hg: d(¹⁹⁹Hg)/e < 9.1 × 10⁻²⁸ cm.
- Upper limit on neutron EDM derived from mercury result: d(n)/e < 6 × 10⁻²⁵ cm.
- Bound on dimensionless CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction constant ξ from mercury limit: ξ < 1.7 × 10⁻³.
- Standard model prediction for neutron EDM: d(n)/e ∼ 10⁻³² – 10⁻³¹ cm.
- Standard model prediction for electron EDM: d(e)/e < 10⁻⁴⁰ cm.
- Standard model prediction for ξ: < 10⁻⁹.
- New limits on T-odd, P-even nucleon-nucleon interaction: < 10⁻⁴ G (where G is the Fermi constant).
- New limits on T-odd, P-even electron-nucleon interaction: < 10⁻⁴ G.
- New limits on T-odd β-decay constants: Im(C_S + C'_S) < 4 × 10⁻³, Im(C_T + C'_T) < 5 × 10⁻⁴, Im(C_P + C'_P) < 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fundamental symmetry tests by showing that atomic PNC experiments provide independent and stringent constraints on the weak mixing in nuclei, contradicting the statistical-model-based explanation of common-sign neutron asymmetries in ²³²Th (Sushkov and Flambaum, 1982) and large P-odd correlations in Mössbauer transitions (Tsinoev and Shvydko, 1985). The derived upper limits on CP-violating and T-odd, P-even interactions from atomic and neutron experiments are complementary to direct kaon decay measurements and rule out many models of new physics beyond the Standard Model. The results enable future experiments, such as the proposed measurement of PNC asymmetry in the M4 γ-transition in ²⁰⁷Pb (Sushkov and Bodrov, 1993), to be interpreted with known constraints on the weak mixing matrix element.
