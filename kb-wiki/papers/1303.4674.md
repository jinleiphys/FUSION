# An optimized chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order

> We optimize the nucleon-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next- to-leading order. The resulting new chiral force NNLOopt yields χ^2 \approx 1 per degree of freedom for laboratory energies below approximately 125 MeV. In the A = 3, 4 nucleon systems, the contributions of three-nucleon forces are smaller than for previous parametrizations of chiral interactions. We use NNLOopt to study properties of key nuclei and neutron matter, and demonstrate that many aspects of nuclear structure can be understood in terms of this nucleon-nucleon interaction, without explicitly invoking three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

The optimized NNLO chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction (NNLOopt) achieves a chi-squared per degree of freedom of approximately 1 for laboratory energies below 125 MeV, and many aspects of nuclear structure can be understood using this two-body interaction alone without explicitly invoking three-nucleon forces, though the authors note that three-nucleon forces are still required for a complete description at NNLO and that their calculations with three-nucleon forces were not fully converged.

## Method

The authors optimized the chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using the derivative-free POUNDerS algorithm, fitting 3 pion-nucleon couplings (c1, c3, c4) and 11 partial-wave contact parameters to reproduce Nijmegen phase shifts. The optimization minimized a weighted sum of squared deviations between NNLO phase shifts and experimental Nijmegen phase shifts, with the actual chi-squared calculated afterward against scattering databases. The three-nucleon force parameters cD and cE were determined from binding energies of 3H, 3He, and 4He, with cD = -0.20 guided by the triton half-life.

## Key numbers

- Chi-squared per datum for NNLOopt: 0.85 (np, 0-35 MeV), 1.17 (np, 35-125 MeV), 1.11 (pp, 0-35 MeV), 1.56 (pp, 35-125 MeV)
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.224582 MeV (NNLOopt) versus 2.224575(9) MeV (experiment)
- 3H ground-state energy with NNLOopt plus 3NF: -8.469 MeV versus experimental -8.482 MeV
- 4He ground-state energy with NNLOopt plus 3NF: -28.417 MeV versus experimental -28.296 MeV
- 4He point proton radius: 1.43(8) fm (NNLOopt with and without 3NF) versus 1.467(13) fm (experiment)
- Ground-state variational upper bounds for 10B: -54.35 MeV (1+ state) and -54.32 MeV (3+ state), with level spacing near zero compared to 1.2 MeV for N3LO EM
- First 2+ state in 48Ca with NNLOopt plus 3NF: 4.8(7) MeV
- Calcium isotopes overbound by approximately 1.03-1.06 MeV per nucleon with NNLOopt

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach of Epelbaum et al. (2004) and Entem and Machleidt (2003) by applying modern derivative-free optimization (POUNDerS) to achieve a much-improved chi-squared at NNLO compared to previous NNLO parametrizations. The resulting interaction reproduces key nuclear structure features (oxygen drip line, calcium shell closures, neutron matter equation of state) that previously required three-nucleon forces when using the N3LO EM interaction, as demonstrated in work by Otsuka et al. (2010), Hagen et al. (2012), and Holt et al. (2012). This enables future quantification of theoretical uncertainties and optimization at higher chiral orders.
