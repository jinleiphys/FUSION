# Solving the NLO BK equation in coordinate space

> We present results from a numerical solution of the next-to-leading order (NLO) Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation in coordinate space in the large Nc limit. We show that the solution is not stable for initial conditions that are close to those used in phenomenological applications of the leading order equation. We identify the problematic terms in the NLO kernel as being related to large logarithms of a small parent dipole size, and also show that rewriting the equation in terms of the "conformal dipole" does not remove the problem. Our results qualitatively agree with expectations based on the behavior of the linear NLO BFKL equation.

## Key claim

The NLO BK equation in coordinate space is numerically unstable for initial conditions close to those used in leading-order phenomenology, because a double-logarithmic term in the kernel causes the evolution speed to diverge as \(\partial_y N / N \sim \ln r\) for small parent dipole size \(r\), and rewriting the equation in terms of the "conformal dipole" does not remove the problem.

## Method

The authors solved the NLO BK equation numerically in coordinate space in the large-\(N_c\) limit, using the "Balitsky" running coupling prescription for the first kernel and a parent-dipole running coupling for the other NLO kernels. They used an initial condition parametrized as \(N(r) = 1 - \exp\left[ -\frac{(r^2 Q_{s0}^2)^\gamma}{4} \ln\left(\frac{1}{r \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}} + e\right) \right]\) with tunable parameters \(\gamma\) and \(Q_{s0}/\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\), and enforced \(N(r) \geq 0\) by hand to avoid negative amplitudes at small \(r\).

## Key numbers

- For the MV model initial condition (\(\gamma = 1\)), the evolution speed \(\partial_y N(r)/N(r)\) is negative at all \(r\) for small values of \(Q_s/\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) (i.e., effectively large couplings), meaning the scattering amplitude decreases with energy.
- The evolution speed diverges as \(\partial_y N / N \sim \ln r\) for small dipoles, signaling an instability.
- The anomalous dimension \(\gamma(r) = d\ln N(r)/d\ln r^2\) increases with rapidity for \(\gamma=1\) and \(\gamma=0.8\) initial conditions, but is not noticeably increased within the studied rapidity range for \(\gamma=0.6\).

## Context

This work presents the first numerical solution of the full NLO BK equation directly in coordinate space, extending the leading-order BK phenomenology used in fits to HERA data (Albacete 2010, Lappi 2013). The results qualitatively agree with expectations from the linear NLO BFKL equation. The problematic double-logarithmic term was previously noted by Balitsky and Chirilli (2009) in the context of conformal \(N=4\) Super Yang-Mills theory, and the authors show that the proposed "composite conformal dipole" operator does not stabilize the equation. The work motivates the need to resum large logarithms before the NLO BK equation can be used in practical phenomenological applications.
