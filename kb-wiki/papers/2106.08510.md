# Causality and stability analysis of first-order field redefinition in relativistic hydrodynamics from kinetic theory

> In this work, the causality and stability of a first-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic theory, that redefines the hydrodynamic fields from a first principle microscopic estimation, have been analyzed. A generic approach of gradient expansion for solving the relativistic transport equation has been adopted using the Chapman-Enskog iterative method. Next, the momentum dependent relaxation time approximation (MDRTA) has been employed to quantify the collision term for analytical estimation of the field correction coefficients from kinetic theory. At linear regime, in local rest frame the dispersion relations are observed to produce a causal propagating mode. However, the acausality and instability reappear when a boosted background is considered for linear analysis. These facts point out relevant aspects regarding the methodology of extracting the causal and stable first order hydrodynamics from kinetic theory and indicate the appropriate approach to construct a valid first order theory with proper justification.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a first-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic theory with thermodynamic field redefinition derived from kinetic theory produces a causal propagating mode in the local rest frame, but acausality and instability reappear when a Lorentz-boosted background is considered, regardless of the values of the field correction coefficients.

## Method

The author adopts the Chapman-Enskog iterative method for gradient expansion of the relativistic Boltzmann equation, employing a momentum-dependent relaxation time approximation (MDRTA) to quantify the collision term and analytically estimate field correction coefficients. The conservation equations for particle four-flow and energy-momentum tensor, including first-order out-of-equilibrium corrections to thermodynamic fields, are linearized around a hydrostatic equilibrium state first in the local rest frame and then in a Lorentz-boosted background. Dispersion relations are derived from plane-wave perturbations and analyzed in the small and large wave-number limits to assess causality and stability.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic group velocity in the local rest frame is given by \( v_g = \sqrt{D/A} \), where the causality condition requires \( 0 < v_g < 1 \), satisfied for small mass (typically \( z = m/T \) values below 0.25) and non-zero negative exponent \( n \) in the MDRTA power law
- At small \( k \) in the local rest frame, the sound modes are \( \omega^{\paral}_{1,2} = \frac{i}{2}\left[\frac{4\eta/3+\zeta}{(\epsilon_0+P_0)}\right]k^2 \pm k c_s \) and the heat-diffusion mode is \( \omega^{\paral}_3 = i \left[\frac{\lambda T}{(\epsilon_0+P_0)}\right] k^2 \)
- At large \( k \) in the local rest frame, the propagating modes become \( \omega^{\paral}_{1,2} = \frac{i}{2}\left\{\frac{B}{A}-\frac{E}{D}\right\} \pm k\sqrt{\frac{D}{A}} \)
- In the boosted frame at large \( k \), the shear modes become \( \omega^{\perp}_{1,2} = \frac{1}{\textbf{v}}k \), which is acausal for any acceptable background velocity \( 0 < \textbf{v} < 1 \)
- In the boosted frame at large \( k \), two new sound-channel roots appear with group velocities \( v_{g,4,5}^{\paral} = \pm 1/\textbf{v} \), which are always acausal for \( 0 < \textbf{v} < 1 \)

## Context

This work extends the recent BDNK formalism (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha and Kovtun, 2017-2019) for causal and stable first-order relativistic hydrodynamics by deriving field redefinition coefficients from kinetic theory using the Chapman-Enskog method. The analysis contradicts the expectation that first-order field redefinition alone can resolve the causality and stability problems that plague the original Landau-Lifshitz (Landau and Lifshitz) and Eckart (1940) first-order theories, which were previously addressed by second-order theories such as Israel-Stewart (Israel and Stewart, 1979) and DNMR (Denicol, Niemi, Molnar and Rischke, 2012). The results indicate that a full nonlinear analysis with thermodynamic field redefinition is needed to properly assess causality and stability, and that the homogeneous part of the non-equilibrium distribution function may be essential for constructing a valid first-order theory.
