# Resurgence and Hydrodynamic Attractors in Gauss-Bonnet Holography

> We study the convergence of the hydrodynamic series in the gravity dual of Gauss-Bonnet gravity in five dimensions with negative cosmological constant via holography. By imposing boost invariance symmetry, we find a solution to the Gauss-Bonnet equation of motion in inverse powers of the proper time, from which we can extract high order corrections to Bjorken flow for different values of the Gauss-Bonnet parameter $λ_{GB}$. As in all other known examples the gradient expansion is, at most, an asymptotic series which can be understood through applying the techniques of Borel-Padé summation. As expected from the behaviour of the quasi-normal modes in the theory, we observe that the singularities in the Borel plane of this series show qualitative features that interpolate between the infinitely strong coupling limit of $\mathcal{N}=4$ Super Yang Mills theory and the expectation from kinetic theory. We further perform the Borel resummation to constrain the behaviour of hydrodynamic attractors beyond leading order in the hydrodynamic expansion. We find that for all values of $λ_{GB}$ considered, the convergence of different initial conditions to the resummation and its hydrodynamization occur at large and comparable values of the pressure anisotropy.

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic gradient expansion in Gauss-Bonnet holography is an asymptotic series whose Borel-plane singularities interpolate between the infinite-coupling limit of N=4 Super Yang-Mills theory and the expectation from kinetic theory, but the resummation approaches first-order hydrodynamics at comparable values of the viscosity-rescaled gradient for all values of the Gauss-Bonnet parameter λGB considered.

## Method

The authors impose boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow symmetry on the five-dimensional Gauss-Bonnet gravity dual with negative cosmological constant and solve the equations of motion as an expansion in inverse powers of proper time τ, equivalent to a gradient expansion in the Knudsen number. They compute up to 94 series coefficients for the energy density and pressure anisotropy for λGB = -0.1, -0.2, -0.5, and -1.0, then apply Borel-Padé summation to analytically continue the Borel transform and resum the asymptotic hydrodynamic series, extracting the hydrodynamic attractor by analyzing the leading trans-series contributions.

## Key numbers

- Number of gradient expansion coefficients computed: 94 for λGB = -0.1, 86 for λGB = -0.2, 80 for λGB = -0.5, 66 for λGB = -1.0
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is given by η/s = (1 - 4λGB)/(4π)
- The Gauss-Bonnet parameter is constrained to λGB ≤ 1/4 for real energy densities, with additional causality constraints -7/36 < λGB ≤ 9/100
- The leading-order anisotropy in first-order hydrodynamics is R^(1st)_hyd = 8(η/s)(1/w)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hydrodynamic series convergence from N=4 SYM at infinite coupling (Heller 2013) and kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation (Heller 2016) to intermediate coupling via Gauss-Bonnet holography. It builds on prior studies of quasi-normal modes in Gauss-Bonnet gravity (Grozdanov 2016) and the first few orders of the gradient expansion (DiNunno 2017). The results enable connecting the hydrodynamic attractor concept, previously studied at extreme coupling limits, to finite-coupling regimes relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
