# The optical potential of $^{6}$He in the eikonal approximation

> The new data of the elastic scattering of $^{6}$He+$^{12}$C at about 40 MeV/nucleon are analyzed in the eikonal approximation. The $^{6}$He+$^{12}$C phase-shift function is evaluated completely without any {\it ad hoc} assumption by a Monte Carlo integration, which makes it possible to use a realistic 6-nucleon wave function for a halo nucleus $^{6}$He. The effect of the breakup of $^6$He on the elastic differential cross sections as well as the optical potential is studied at different energies from 40 to 800 MeV/nucleon. PACS number(s): 24.10.-i; 21.60.Ka; 25.60.Bx; 25.10.+s Keywords: Eikonal; Glauber; Monte Carlo; Halo; Breakup

## Key claim

The full Glauber model using a realistic 6-nucleon wave function for $^{6}$He reproduces the elastic scattering data only up to 7 degrees; beyond that angle it gives much smaller cross sections than measured, indicating that free-space NN scattering parameters are insufficient and that an effective phase calculated from a nucleon-target optical potential is needed to describe the data.

## Method

The authors evaluated the $^{6}$He+$^{12}$C phase-shift function by Monte Carlo integration using a variational Monte Carlo (VMC) 6-nucleon wave function for $^{6}$He and a three-$\alpha$ cluster-model wave function for $^{12}$C, without any ad hoc approximation. They replaced the free NN profile function with a nucleon-target (NT) optical potential phase-shift function, computed the effective eikonal phase via Monte Carlo sampling, and then inverted the phase-shift function to obtain a local optical potential $U(R)$ via the Glauber inversion formula. The dynamic polarization potential was extracted as the difference between $U(R)$ and the single-folding potential $U_{\rm f}(R)$.

## Key numbers

* $^{6}$He r.m.s. radii from VMC wave function: 2.56 fm (nucleon), 1.96 fm (proton), 2.81 fm (neutron); $^{12}$C r.m.s. radius: 2.36 fm.
* At 40 MeV/nucleon, the eikonal approximation overestimates the exact quantum-mechanical cross section by at most 15% up to 30 degrees, but the discrepancy grows to about five times at 60 degrees.
* Reaction cross sections for $^{6}$He+$^{12}$C predicted by different p-$^{12}$C optical potentials: 1193 mb (Fannon et al.), 1147 mb (Becchetti and Greenlees), 1139 mb (Rapaport); experimental p+$^{12}$C reaction cross section is 371$\pm$9 mb.
* At 800 MeV/nucleon, the central depth of the folding potential imaginary part ($-314.1$ MeV) is reduced to $-49.3$ MeV by the dynamic polarization potential.

## Context

This work extends earlier eikonal and Glauber calculations for halo nuclei (Al-Khalili, 1995; Yabana, 1992) by using a realistic VMC wave function for $^{6}$He rather than simple density distributions, and by performing the Monte Carlo integration to all orders. It addresses the failure of the double-folding model reported by Lapoux (2003) to reproduce $^{6}$He+$^{12}$C elastic scattering over the full angular range. The approach enables direct extraction of the dynamic polarization potential due to breakup of $^{6}$He, and can be applied to future radioactive beam data at larger angles.
