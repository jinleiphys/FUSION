# Fission Fragments Mass Yields of Actinide Nuclei

> A new, rapidly convergent Fourier over spheroid parametrization is developed to describe the shape of a fissioning nucleus: its elongation, non-axiality and left-right asymmetry and neck formation. The 4D Potential Energy Surfaces (PES) of even-even actinide nuclei are evaluated within the macro-micro model. The Langevin trajectories generated on such PESs allow for obtaining the fission fragments' mass and kinetic energy yields. The charge equilibration at the scission configuration and the post-scission neutron emission are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) shape parametrization, combined with a 3D Langevin model on 4D potential energy surfaces, can describe the main features of low-energy fission fragment mass and kinetic energy yields, but the results are shown only for the specific case of thermal neutron-induced fission of ²³⁵U and the model's quality is described as "reasonable" rather than exact.

## Method

The authors developed a Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) shape parametrization to describe nuclear shapes in terms of elongation, non-axiality, left-right asymmetry, and neck formation. They evaluated 4D Potential Energy Surfaces (PES) for even-even actinide nuclei using the macro-micro model with the Lublin-Strasbourg-Drop (LSD) formula for the macroscopic part and Yukawa-folded single-particle potential for microscopic corrections. Langevin trajectories were generated on these PESs to obtain fission fragment mass and total kinetic energy yields, with the inertia and friction tensors evaluated in the irrotational flow and wall approximation respectively. A charge equilibration model based on liquid-drop and pairing correlation energy was added at scission, and neutron emission from fragments was estimated using the Weisskopf formula.

## Key numbers

- The PES of ²³⁶U is shown on the (c, a₄) plane, with the upper value of the neck-parameter a₄ = 0.72 corresponding to the neck radius approximately equal to the nucleon radius.
- The zero-point collective energy E₀ = 1 MeV is used in the generalized Einstein relation for the diffusion tensor.
- The total kinetic energy (TKE) is calculated using the formula TKE = (3e²/5r₀)[Z²/A^(1/3) B_Coul(q_sc) - Z_h²/A_h^(1/3) B_Coul(q_h) - Z_l²/A_l^(1/3) B_Coul(q_l)].
- The charge equilibration model for ²⁴⁰Pu at scission uses a Wigner distribution with energy width E_W comparable to the harmonic oscillator shell spacing ħω₀.
- The neutron inverse cross-section formula uses σ_inv(ε) = [0.76 + 1.93/A^(1/3) + (1.66/A^(2/3) - 0.050)/ε] π (1.70A^(1/3))².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using Fourier shape parametrization (Pomorski 2017, Pomorski 2020, Pomorski 2021) by adding a charge equilibration mode and a Master-type equation for neutron multiplicity, and introducing the FoS parametrization better adapted for grid calculations. The model is compared to experimental data for ²³⁵U + n_th from D. K. T. (1969) for mass yields, A. T. J. (2020) for TKE, M. S. (2017) for charge yields, and G. H. O. (2018) for neutron multiplicities. The approach enables future calculations for wider mass and excitation energy ranges of fissioning nuclei.
