# Scattering of Klein-Gordon particles in the background of mixed scalar-vector generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential

> Recently, it has been shown that the generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential energy, in which surface interaction terms are taken into account, describes the physical processes better than the standard form. Therefore in this study, we investigate the scattering of Klein-Gordon particles in the presence of both generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon vector and scalar potential. In one spatial dimension we obtain the solutions in terms of hypergeometric functions for spin symmetric or pseudo-spin symmetric cases. Finally, we plot transmission and reflection probabilities for incident particles with negative and positive energy for some critical arbitrary parameters and discuss the correlations for both cases.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact transmission and reflection probabilities for Klein-Gordon particles scattering from a generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential in one spatial dimension, but only for the special cases of spin symmetry (SS) or pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) where the vector and scalar potentials are linearly related by constants \(\varepsilon^+\) or \(\varepsilon^-\), which are taken as zero for finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the one-dimensional time-independent Klein-Gordon equation with a generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential that includes surface interaction terms. They treat the spin-symmetric case (\(V_v = g V_s\)) and pseudo-spin-symmetric case (\(V_v = -g V_s\)) separately, transforming the differential equation into a hypergeometric form via a coordinate change. Solutions are matched at the origin using continuity of the wave function and its derivative, yielding closed-form expressions for the reflection and transmission coefficients in terms of hypergeometric functions and Gamma functions.

## Key numbers

- Neutral kaon rest mass energy \(m_0 c^2\) is used as the energy scale; nuclear radius \(L = 6\ \mathrm{fm}\) and reciprocal diffusion parameter \(a = 1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) are chosen such that \(aL \gg 1\).
- For the spin-symmetric case with \(V_0 = m_0 c^2 / 2\) and \(W = 0\), particles with positive energy (\(E > m_0 c^2\)) and negative energy (\(E < -m_0 c^2\)) can scatter; the forbidden gap lies between \(-m_0 c^2\) and \(m_0 c^2\).
- For the spin-symmetric case with \(V_0 = 3 m_0 c^2 / 2\), the forbidden gap expands so that negative-energy particles require \(E < -2 m_0 c^2\) to scatter.
- For the pseudo-spin-symmetric case with \(V_0 = m_0 c^2 / 2\) and \(W = 0\), the scattering probability for negative-energy particles becomes unity after a shift in the threshold energy.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Klein-Gordon particle scattering with Woods-Saxon potentials by Rojas et al. and Hassanabadi et al. (2013) to the generalized symmetric form that includes surface interaction terms, as proposed by Candemir and Bayrak (2014) and others. The results enable analysis of how surface effects (parameter \(W\)) modify transmission and reflection probabilities for both positive and negative energy particles, and can be used to study resonant states via Siegert boundary conditions. The paper also corrects an omission in earlier works (Panella, 2010; Lutfuoglu and Akdeniz, 2016) regarding the derivative of hypergeometric functions in continuity conditions.
