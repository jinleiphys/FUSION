# $N^\ast$ Form Factors based on a Covariant Quark Model

> We discuss the results of the covariant spectator quark model for the several $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast$ transitions, where $N$ is the nucleon and $N^\ast$ a nucleon excitation. More specifically, we present predictions for the form factors and transition amplitudes associated with the resonances $N(1440)1/2^+$, $N(1535)1/2^-$, $N(1520)3/2^-$, $Δ(1620)1/2^-$ and $N(1650)1/2^-$. The estimates based on valence quark degrees of freedom are compared with the available data, particularly with the recent Jefferson Lab data at low and large momentum transfer ($Q^2$). In general, the estimates are in good agreement with the empirical data for $Q^2 >2$ GeV$^2$, with a few exceptions. The results are discussed in terms of the role of the valence quarks and the meson cloud excitations for the different resonances $N^\ast$. We also review our results for the resonance $Δ(1232)3/2^+$ and discuss the relevance of the pion cloud component for the magnetic dipole form factor and the electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, based solely on valence quark degrees of freedom, provides a good description of the available Jefferson Lab data for several gamma* N to N* transitions for Q^2 > 2 GeV^2, with the exceptions of the Pauli form factor for N(1535) and the A_{3/2} amplitude for N(1520), which are interpreted as requiring meson cloud contributions.

## Method

The covariant spectator quark model treats baryons as three-quark systems with wave functions based on SU(6) x O(3) symmetry, reduced to a quark-diquark system in the covariant spectator theory. The electromagnetic interaction is described by photon coupling to constituent quarks in relativistic impulse approximation, with quark electromagnetic form factors parametrized according to vector meson dominance. For the N(1535) and N(1520) resonances, a semirelativistic approximation is used where the radial wave functions of the resonances are assumed equal to the nucleon radial wave function, yielding parameter-free predictions for transition form factors and helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For N(1535)1/2^-: Dirac form factor F_1* is well described for Q^2 > 2 GeV^2; Pauli form factor F_2* is compatible with zero (F_2* approx 0) for Q^2 > 2 GeV^2
- For N(1520)3/2^-: semirelativistic approximation describes data well for Q^2 > 1.5 GeV^2, except for A_{3/2} amplitude and G_E form factor at small Q^2
- For N(1440)1/2^+: covariant spectator quark model results are close to empirical data for Q^2 > 1.5 GeV^2
- For N(1535)1/2^-: the relation S_{1/2} = - sqrt(1+tau)/sqrt(2) * (M_R^2 - M_N^2)/(2 M_R Q) * A_{1/2} is confirmed with precision in the region Q^2 = 1.5-4.2 GeV^2
- Jefferson Lab data are available up to Q^2 = 6 GeV^2, with JLab-12 GeV upgrade expected to reach Q^2 approx 12 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously developed for the nucleon electromagnetic structure (Ramalho, 2010) to multiple nucleon resonances, including N(1440), N(1535), N(1520), Delta(1620), and N(1650). The results are compared with Jefferson Lab data from CLAS analyses (Aznauryan, 2009; Mokeev, 2012, 2016) and with bare core estimates from the EBAC coupled-channel dynamical model. The findings enable future tests with JLab-12 GeV upgrade data and provide a framework for separating valence quark contributions from meson cloud effects in nucleon excitation transitions.
