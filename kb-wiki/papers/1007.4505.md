# Influence of scalar-isovector delta-meson field on quark phase structure in neutron stars

> The deconfinement phase transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in the interior of compact stars is investigated. The hadronic phase is described in the framework of relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, when also the scalar-isovector delta-meson effective field is taken into account. The MIT bag model for describing a quark phase is used. The changes of the parameters of phase transition caused by the presence of delta-meson field are explored. Finally, alterations in the integral and structural parameters of hybrid stars due to both deconfinement phase transition and inclusion of delta-meson field are discussed.

## Key claim

Including the scalar-isovector delta-meson field in the relativistic mean-field description of hadronic matter alters the threshold parameters of the hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars, but the direction of the change depends on the bag constant: for B=60 MeV/fm^3 the lower threshold baryon density increases while for B=100 MeV/fm^3 it decreases, contrary to the abstract's implication of a uniform effect.

## Method

The hadronic phase was described using relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with sigma, omega, rho, and delta meson fields, with coupling constants fixed to reproduce saturation properties (n0=0.153 fm^-3, binding energy -16.3 MeV, incompressibility 300 MeV, asymmetry energy 32.5 MeV). The quark phase was described using the MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange corrections for u, d, s quarks (masses 5, 7, 150 MeV respectively, alpha_s=0.5). The deconfinement phase transition was treated via the Glendenning construction (global charge neutrality, mixed phase) and compared with the Maxwell construction (separate charge neutrality, first-order transition). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

- For B=60 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: lower mixed phase threshold baryon density n_N = 0.0771 fm^-3, upper threshold n_Q = 1.083 fm^-3; without delta field: n_N = 0.0717 fm^-3, n_Q = 1.083 fm^-3
- For B=100 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: n_N = 0.2409 fm^-3, n_Q = 1.448 fm^-3; without delta field: n_N = 0.2596 fm^-3, n_Q = 1.436 fm^-3
- For B=60 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: lower threshold pressure P_N = 0.434 MeV/fm^3, upper threshold P_Q = 327.745 MeV/fm^3
- For B=100 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: P_N = 16.911 MeV/fm^3, P_Q = 474.368 MeV/fm^3
- Maximum mass of hybrid star for B=60 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: M_max = 1.853 solar masses, radius R = 10.71 km, central density 2.322 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- Maximum mass for B=100 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: M_max = 1.780 solar masses, radius R = 11.190 km
- Minimum mass of hybrid star for B=100 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: M_min = 0.997 solar masses, radius R = 14.354 km; without delta field: M_min = 0.955 solar masses, R = 13.960 km
- For B=60 MeV/fm^3 with delta field: quark core radius r_Q = 0.83 km, mixed phase layer thickness r_MP = 9.43 km, nuclear matter layer r_N = 0.45 km

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Alaverdyan 2009) which used the Maxwell construction, by now applying the Glendenning construction (Glendenning 1992, 2000) for mixed phase formation. It builds on RMF models including the delta meson developed by Kubis (1997), Liu (2002), and Greco (2003), and uses the MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange corrections from Farhi and Jaffe (1984). The results enable comparison of two phase transition scenarios (Maxwell vs. Glendenning) and show that for B=100 MeV/fm^3, stable hybrid stars can contain a mixed phase core but not a pure quark core, while for B=60 MeV/fm^3 a pure strange quark matter core is possible.
