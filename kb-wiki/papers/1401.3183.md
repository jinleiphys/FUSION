# Pion cloud effects on baryon masses

> In this work we explore the effect of pion cloud contributions to the mass of the nucleon and the delta baryon. To this end we solve a coupled system of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator, a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion and a three-body Faddeev equation for the baryons. In the quark-gluon interaction we explicitly resolve the term responsible for the back-coupling of the pion onto the quark, representing rainbow-ladder like pion cloud effects in bound states. We study the dependence of the resulting baryon masses on the current quark mass and discuss the internal structure of the baryons in terms of a partial wave decomposition. We furthermore determine values for the nucleon and delta sigma-terms.

## Key claim

The authors find that including explicit pion cloud contributions via a pion-exchange kernel in the quark-gluon interaction reduces the nucleon mass by about 150 MeV compared to the quark-core calculation (RL2), but when comparing the full calculation (RL2+π) to the standard rainbow-ladder calculation (RL1) that already implicitly encodes some pion effects, the mass shift is about 80 MeV; the delta baryon shows a much smaller shift of about 60 MeV when comparing RL2+π to RL2, but an increase of about 70 MeV when comparing to RL1 due to the different interaction scale needed to reproduce the physical pion decay constant.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled system of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator, a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion, and a three-body Faddeev equation for the nucleon and delta baryons. The quark-gluon interaction kernel is decomposed into a rainbow-ladder gluonic part and an additional pion-exchange kernel representing the back-coupling of the pion onto the quark, where the internal pion is approximated by its leading Bethe-Salpeter amplitude Eπ while the external pion amplitude includes all four tensor components. The interaction parameters are fixed by reproducing the experimental pion mass and decay constant, with the scale Λ increased from 0.72 GeV (RL1) to 0.84 GeV (RL2+π) when pion cloud effects are treated explicitly.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: 0.94 (1) GeV for RL1, 1.01 (3) GeV for RL2 (quark-core), 0.86 (1) GeV for RL2+π (with pion cloud), compared to experimental 0.94 GeV
- Delta mass: 1.23 (1) GeV for RL1, 1.36 (1) GeV for RL2, 1.30 (3) GeV for RL2+π, compared to experimental 1.23 GeV
- Pion mass: 0.138 (1) GeV for RL1, 0.144 (1) GeV for RL2, 0.138 (1) GeV for RL2+π, compared to experimental 0.140 GeV
- Pion decay constant: 0.093 (1) GeV for RL1, 0.098 (1) GeV for RL2, 0.093 (1) GeV for RL2+π, compared to experimental 0.093 GeV
- Nucleon sigma term: 30 (3) MeV (RL1), 26 (3) MeV (RL2), 31 (3) MeV (RL2+π)
- Delta sigma term: 24 (2) MeV (RL1), 23 (3) MeV (RL2), 24 (3) MeV (RL2+π)
- Chiral condensate (1/3 power at μ=19 GeV): 0.281 (2) GeV for RL1, 0.300 (3) GeV for RL2, 0.280 (3) GeV for RL2+π
- Interaction scale Λ: 0.72 GeV for RL1, 0.84 GeV for RL2+π
- Current quark mass m_u/d(μ²)=3.7 MeV for all setups

## Context

This work extends the covariant Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to baryons by including explicit pion cloud effects, building on the framework developed by Fischer et al. (2007, 2008) for mesons where pion back-coupling effects were studied. The pure rainbow-ladder calculations for nucleon and delta masses were previously reported by Eichmann et al. (2009) and Sanchis-Alepuz et al. (2011). The results show that while the standard rainbow-ladder truncation (RL1) gives excellent agreement with experimental baryon masses, it fails to reproduce low-momentum properties of form factors as shown by Eichmann et al. (2011) and Sanchis-Alepuz et al. (2013); the inclusion of pion cloud effects improves the description of internal structure but introduces discrepancies of about 10% in the masses, indicating the need for further improvements such as gluon self-interaction effects and two-pion intermediate states.
