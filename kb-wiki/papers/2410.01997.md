# Role of neutron pairing with density-gradient dependence in the semi-microscopic treatment of the inner crust of neutron stars

> Using the fourth-order extended Thomas-Fermi method with Strutinsky-integral shell and pairing corrections, we calculate the inner crust of neutron stars with the BSk31 functional, whose pairing has two terms: i) a term that is fitted to the results of microscopic calculations on homogeneous nuclear matter (accounting for both medium polarization and self-energy effects) that are more realistic than those of our earlier functionals; ii) an empirical term that is dependent on the density gradient, which permits an excellent fit to nuclear masses. Both proton and neutron pairing are taken into account, the former in the BCS theory and the latter in the local density approximation. We found that the equilibrium value of the proton number $Z$ remains 40 over the entire density range considered, whether or not neutron pairing is included. The new equation of state and the composition are very similar to those of our previously preferred functional, BSk24. However, the predicted neutron pairing fields are quite different. In particular, clusters are found to be impermeable to the neutron superfluid. The implications for the neutron superfluid dynamics are briefly discussed. Since the new pairing is more realistic, the functional BSk31 is better suited for investigating neutron superfluidity in neutron-star crusts.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the new BSk31 functional, which includes a density-gradient-dependent pairing term fitted to more realistic microscopic calculations (including self-energy and medium polarization effects), predicts an inner crust composition and equation of state very similar to the earlier BSk24 functional, but produces substantially different neutron pairing fields, most notably showing that clusters are impermeable to the neutron superfluid, with implications for superfluid dynamics.

## Method

The authors used the fourth-order extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method with Strutinsky-integral shell corrections for protons and pairing corrections for both protons (BCS theory) and neutrons (local density approximation) to calculate the inner crust of neutron stars with the BSk31 functional. The neutron pairing energy was included before optimization of the ETF energy by adding a condensation energy density term, and the density-gradient-dependent pairing force required solving the BCS gap equations locally using an effective pairing strength that incorporates both the homogeneous matter gap and the gradient term. Calculations were performed for spherical Wigner-Seitz cells at mean densities from the neutron drip point up to 0.06 fm\(^{-3}\), with the proton number \(Z\) varied to find the equilibrium configuration.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium proton number \(Z\) remains 40 over the entire density range considered (from neutron drip up to \(\bar{n} = 6.0 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\)), whether or not neutron pairing is included.
- Neutron pairing contributes at most about 0.1 MeV per nucleon to the total energy, and the relative contribution to the pressure does not exceed 3% at all densities.
- The root-mean-square error of BSk31 for 2353 measured nuclear masses is 0.571 MeV, compared to 0.549 MeV for BSk24.
- At mean density \(\bar{n} = 3.0 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\), the optimal neutron number for BSk31 is \(N = 108\) (with \(Z = 40\), \(A = 148\)); at \(\bar{n} = 4.0 \times 10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(N = 653\) (\(A = 693\)); at \(\bar{n} = 5.0 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(N = 1182\) (\(A = 1222\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous inner crust calculations using the BSk24 functional (Pearson et al., 2018) by incorporating a more realistic pairing treatment based on the BSk30-32 family of functionals (Goriely et al., 2016), which include both a term fitted to the microscopic pairing gaps of Cao et al. (2006) with self-energy corrections and a density-gradient-dependent surface term. The new functional enables more reliable studies of neutron superfluidity in neutron-star crusts, as the predicted neutron pairing fields differ significantly from those of BSk24, with clusters found to be impermeable to the neutron superfluid, a result with implications for superfluid dynamics and the physics of neutron star crusts.
