# Cross-shell excitations in doubly magic 132Sn and its nearest neighbours

> Large scale shell model calculations have been performed to study the excitation spectra of 132Sn and its nearest neighbours with a new cross-shell interaction constructed from two widely used interactions, sn100pn and CWG, of this mass region. A few of the two-body matrix elements have been tuned to reproduce the low-lying multiplet states of 132Sn. This is the first full scale shell model study of 132Sn energy spectra as well as transition probabilities. The excitation spectra for other nearest neighbours are reproduced reasonably well. The most important observable calculated are the E1 transition probabilities, which were so far beyond the scope of calculations with the existing interactions.

# Cross-shell excitations in doubly magic 132Sn and its nearest neighbours

## Key claim
The authors construct a new cross-shell effective interaction (named sm56fph) by combining the sn100pn and CWG interactions and tuning selected two-body matrix elements, enabling the first full-scale shell-model calculation of excitation spectra and E1 transition probabilities for 132Sn and its neighbours. However, the interaction is limited by a truncated model space that omits the π1h11/2 orbital and some neutron orbitals, and the tuning is specific to reproducing low-lying multiplet states of 132Sn.

## Method
Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the NUSHELLX@MSU and OXBASH codes with a valence space comprising two proton orbitals (π1g7/2, π2d5/2) and eight neutron orbitals (ν1g7/2, ν2d5/2, ν2d3/2, ν3s1/2, ν1h11/2, ν1h9/2, ν2f7/2, ν3p3/2) above a 100Sn core. The two-body matrix elements were taken from the sn100pn and CWG interactions, with cross-shell ν-ν matrix elements tuned using experimental binding energies and excitation energies of 132Sn multiplet states, and additional tuning of π-π and ν-ν matrix elements to reproduce binding energies of 134Te and 134Sn. Calculations for nuclei with N≤82 allowed up to 3p3h excitations into the N>82 orbitals, while for N>82 nuclei, up to 2p2h excitations were typically feasible.

## Key numbers
- Neutron single-particle energies for orbitals below N=82: -9.74 MeV (ν1g7/2), -8.97 MeV (ν2d5/2), -7.31 MeV (ν2d3/2), -7.62 MeV (ν3s1/2), -7.38 MeV (ν1h11/2)
- Neutron single-particle energies for orbitals above N=82: 11.06 MeV (ν1h9/2), 5.718 MeV (ν2f7/2), 8.287 MeV (ν3p3/2)
- Proton single-particle energies: 0.8072 MeV (π1g7/2), 1.5623 MeV (π2d5/2)
- Monopole interaction energy for ν1h11/2-1-ν2f7/2 coupling: -0.1374 MeV (neutron-neutron) compared to -0.2317 MeV (neutron-proton from CWG)
- First excited state of 132Sn: 2+ at approximately 4.041 MeV (used as reference energy)
- 132Sn 3-1 state overpredicted by approximately 600 keV in theory
- 131Sn 11/2-1 isomer predicted at 36 keV (experimental values: 69(14) keV from β spectrum, 65.1(3) keV from level scheme)
- 132Sb 3+1 state predicted at 135 keV (experimental: 85.55(6) keV)
- 134Te 8+1 state predicted at 4.843 MeV (experimental: 4.558 MeV)
- 134Te 10+1 state predicted at 5.85 MeV (experimental: 5.621 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the capabilities of shell-model calculations in the A≈130 region beyond the limitations of the widely used sn100pn and CWG interactions (Brown, 2001), which could only describe low-energy states and failed to reproduce E1 transitions due to the absence of opposite-parity orbitals in their model spaces. The new interaction enables calculation of cross-shell excitations and hindered E1 transition probabilities in 132Sn and neighbouring nuclei (131,132Sn, 132-134Sb, 133-135Te, 135I), which were previously inaccessible. The results are compared with experimental data from Bhattacharyya (2009), Fogelberg (2004), Bocchi (2016), and others; the failure to reproduce the 3-1 state in 132Sn and the need for larger model spaces including the π1h11/2 orbital point to remaining open questions about collective excitations and the need for further computational resources to enable 3p3h calculations in heavier neighbours.
