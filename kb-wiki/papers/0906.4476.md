# Deformation effects and neutrinoless positron $ββ$ decay of $^{96}$Ru, $^{102}$Pd, $^{106}$Cd, $^{124}$Xe, $^{130}$Ba and $^{156}$Dy isotopes within Majorona neutrino mass mechanism

> The $(β^{+}β^{+})_{0ν}$ and $(\varepsilon β^{+})_{0ν}$ modes of $^{96}$Ru, $^{102}$Pd, $^{106}$Cd, $^{124}$Xe, $^{130}$Ba and $^{156}$Dy isotopes are studied in the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition. The reliability of the intrinsic wave functions required to study these decay modes has been established in our earlier works by obtaining an overall agreement between the theoretically calculated spectroscopic properties, namely yrast spectra, reduced $B(E2$:$0^{+}\to 2^{+})$ transition probabilities, quadrupole moments $Q(2^{+})$ and gyromagnetic factors $g(2^{+})$ and the available experimental data in the parent and daugther even-even nuclei. In the present work, the required nuclear transition matrix elements are calculated in the Majorana neutrino mass mechanism using the same set of intrinsic wave functions as used to study the two neutrino positron double-$β$ decay modes. Limits on effective light neutrino mass $< m_ν >$ and effective heavy neutrino mass $< M_{N} >$ are extracted from the observed limits on half-lives $T_{1/2}^{0ν}(0^{+}\to 0^{+})$ of $(β^{+}β^{+})_{0ν}$ and $(\varepsilon β^{+})_{0ν}$ modes. We also investigate the effect of quadrupolar correlations vis-a-vis deformation on NTMEs required to study the $(β^{+}β^{+})_{0ν}$ and $(\varepsilon β^{+})_{0ν}$ modes.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear transition matrix elements (NTMEs) for neutrinoless positron double-beta decay modes in six nuclei using the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) framework, but the available experimental half-life limits are not large enough to provide stringent limits on effective neutrino masses, so the authors instead predict half-lives for future experiments.

## Method

The authors employ the PHFB model with a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole (PQQ) effective two-body interaction to generate intrinsic wave functions for parent and daughter nuclei, then project states of good angular momentum. They calculate the four NTMEs (Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and their heavy-neutrino counterparts) for the 0+ to 0+ transition of the (β+β+)0ν and (εβ+)0ν modes in the Majorana neutrino mass mechanism, using the same wave functions previously validated against spectroscopic properties and two-neutrino decay rates. The NTMEs are computed under several approximations: point nucleons, point nucleons plus Jastrow-type short-range correlations (SRC), finite nucleon size (FNS) with dipole form factor, and FNS plus SRC.

## Key numbers

- Phase space factors G01 for the β+β+ mode (in yr−1): 2.243×10−18 for 96Ru, 2.532×10−18 for 106Cd, 3.048×10−18 for 124Xe, 5.114×10−19 for 130Ba
- Phase space factors G01 for the εβ+ mode (in yr−1): 2.664×10−17 for 96Ru, 3.635×10−17 for 106Cd, 5.654×10−17 for 124Xe, 4.901×10−17 for 130Ba, 6.0×10−19 for 102Pd, 3.250×10−17 for 156Dy
- For 96Ru with point nucleons and average energy denominator A̅: MF = 0.4983, MGT = −2.4780, MFh = 35.8917, MGTh = −169.321
- For 102Pd with point nucleons and A̅: MF = 0.6464, MGT = −2.7663, MFh = 43.3140
- NTMEs MF and MGT change by 7.8–9.8% when using A̅/2 versus A̅ in the energy denominator
- With FNS plus SRC, NTMEs change by 21.7–25.8% for light neutrinos; for heavy neutrinos, MFh and MGTh reduce by 65.0–68.5% with FNS plus SRC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHFB studies of two-neutrino positron double-beta decay (Rath et al., 2009; Raina et al., 2006; Singh et al., 2007) to the neutrinoless modes, using the same wave functions and PQQ interaction. It complements earlier deformed QRPA calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay (Pace et al., 2004; Rodin and Faessler, 2008) and the ISM studies of pairing and quadrupolar correlations (Caurier et al., 2008; Menendez et al., 2008). The predicted half-lives for the six nuclei are intended to guide future experimental searches for positron-emitting neutrinoless double-beta decay, which could help resolve degeneracies between mass mechanism and right-handed current parameters as noted by Hirsch et al. (1994).
