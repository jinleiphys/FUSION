# Photon and dilepton production in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The recent results on direct photons and dileptons in high energy heavy ion collisions, obtained particularly at RHIC and LHC are reviewed. The results are new not only in terms of the probes, but also in terms of the precision. We will discuss the physics learned from the results.

# Photon and dilepton production in high energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The review finds that while hard (high-pT) direct photons and Z-bosons are well described by perturbative QCD and show no suppression in heavy ion collisions, the soft (low-pT) direct photon excess and its unexpectedly large elliptic flow (v2) cannot be explained by models considering only quark-gluon plasma (QGP) photons, requiring significant contributions from hadron-gas interactions. The low-mass dilepton excess observed at RHIC shows a factor of 3–4 discrepancy between the PHENIX and STAR experiments, though the energy dependence of the STAR data is consistent with in-medium modification of the rho meson spectral function.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental results from RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) and LHC (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS) on direct photons and dileptons (virtual photons decaying to e+e- or mu+mu- pairs) in heavy ion collisions. Direct photons are measured via internal conversion (Kroll-Wada formalism relating e+e- pairs to real photons) and external conversion techniques, while dilepton invariant mass spectra are compared to hadronic cocktail calculations of known decay sources. Elliptic flow (v2) of direct photons is extracted by subtracting the v2 of hadron decay photons from inclusive photon v2 measurements.

## Key numbers
- Direct photon slope parameter in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: ~220 MeV, nearly independent of centrality (PHENIX)
- Ratio of slope parameters between LHC Pb+Pb (2.76 TeV) and RHIC Au+Au (200 GeV): 1.38
- Bjorken energy density ratio LHC/RHIC: ~14 GeV/fm^3 / ~5.7 GeV/fm^3 = 2.6
- Low mass region (LMR, 0.15 < M_ee < 0.75 GeV/c^2) dilepton excess in 0-10% central Au+Au at 200 GeV: factor 7.6 ± 0.5 (stat) ± 1.3 (syst) ± 1.5 (model) for PHENIX; factor 1.72 ± 0.10 ± 0.50 for STAR
- Minimum bias LMR excess: factor 4.7 ± 0.4 (stat) ± 1.5 (syst) ± 0.9 (model) for PHENIX; factor 1.53 ± 0.07 ± 0.41 for STAR
- Background photons from pi0 and eta decays constitute ~95% of inclusive photons; signal-to-background ratio at 1-3 GeV/c is ~10%

## Context
This review extends the program of electromagnetic probes in heavy ion collisions following the first thermal photon signal reported by WA98 at SPS (Aggarwal, 2000) and the early direct photon measurements by PHENIX (Adler, 2005). The results challenge earlier theoretical expectations that thermal photons from QGP would dominate the 1-3 GeV/c pT range; instead, models by van Hees (2011) and Shen (2013) that increase hadron-gas photon contributions or reduce QGP photon rates are needed to explain the large v2. The dilepton results build on CERES measurements at SPS (Agakichiev, 2005) and in-medium rho meson modification calculations by Rapp (1999). The discrepancy between PHENIX and STAR LMR excesses remains unresolved, with PHENIX's 2010 hadron blind detector (HBD) run showing consistency with their 2004 results. Future measurements of dilepton flow and triangular flow (v3) are needed to discriminate between hydrodynamic expansion and magnetic field scenarios for the large photon v2.
