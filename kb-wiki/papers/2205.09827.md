# Exploring the effects of Delta Baryons in magnetars

> Strong magnetic fields can modify the microscopic composition of matter with consequences on stellar macroscopic properties. Within this context, we study, for the first time, the possibility of the appearance of spin-3/2 $Δ$ baryons in magnetars. We make use of two different relativistic models for the equation of state of dense matter under the influence of strong magnetic fields considering the effects of Landau levels and the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) proportional to the spin of all baryons and leptons. In particular, we analyze the effects of the AMM of $Δ$ baryons in dense matter for the first time. {We also obtain global properties corresponding to the EoS models numerically and study the corresponding role of the $Δ$ baryons.} We find that they are favored over hyperons, which causes an increase in isopin asymmetry and a decrease in spin polarization. We also find that, contrary to what generally occurs when new degrees of freedom are introduced, the $Δ$s do not make the EoS significantly softer and magnetars less massive. Finally, the magnetic field distribution inside a given star is not affected by the presence of $Δ$s.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin-3/2 Δ baryons can appear in magnetars and are favored over hyperons, but contrary to typical behavior when new degrees of freedom are introduced, the Δs do not significantly soften the equation of state or reduce magnetar masses, a result that depends on the specific coupling choices (scalar coupling ratios x_σΔ = 1.0 or 1.2 for the L3ωρ model and x_ωΔ = 1.25 for the CMF model) and the inclusion of anomalous magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors used two relativistic mean-field models (the L3ωρ nonlinear Walecka model and the chiral mean-field model) to describe dense baryonic matter under strong magnetic fields, incorporating Landau quantization and anomalous magnetic moments for all baryons and leptons, including Δ baryons for the first time. They solved the coupled Einstein-Maxwell equations to obtain anisotropic stellar configurations, using a non-magnetic matter contribution to the equation of state for global structure calculations while accounting for the pure electromagnetic field contribution. The Δ baryon couplings were treated as free parameters, with scalar coupling ratios x_σΔ = 1.0 and 1.2 for L3ωρ and x_ωΔ = 1.25 for CMF, constrained by transport models and lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- Δ baryon vacuum masses: 1232 MeV for all four charge states (Δ⁺⁺, Δ⁺, Δ⁰, Δ⁻)
- Anomalous magnetic moment coefficients κ_b/μ_N: Δ⁺⁺ = 3.47, Δ⁺ = 1.73, Δ⁰ = 0.06, Δ⁻ = −1.69
- Single measurement of Δ⁺ magnetic moment: μ_Δ⁺/μ_N = 2.7 ⁺¹·⁰₋₁·₃ ± 1.5 from γp → pπγ' reaction
- Δ⁺⁺ magnetic moment range from π⁺p → π⁺pγ bremsstrahlung: μ_Δ⁺⁺/μ_N = 3.7–7.5
- Compressibility at saturation for isospin-symmetric matter: 256 MeV (L3ωρ model) and 300 MeV (CMF model)
- Hyperon potential depths used: U_Λ = −28 MeV (L3ωρ), U_Λ = −27 MeV (CMF); U_Σ = +21.8 MeV (L3ωρ), U_Σ = 6 MeV (CMF); U_Ξ = +35.3 MeV (L3ωρ), U_Ξ = −17 MeV (CMF)
- Δ potentials for L3ωρ model: U_Δ = −66.25 MeV (equal to nucleon potential) for x_σΔ = x_ωΔ = 1.0, and −79.50 MeV for x_σΔ = x_ωΔ = 1.2
- Δ potential for CMF model: U_Δ = −64 MeV for x_ωΔ = 1.25
- Magnetic field range for magnetars: surface fields 10¹¹–10¹⁵ G, interior fields up to ~10¹⁸ G according to scalar virial theorem
- Leptons AMMs: κ_e/μ_Be = 1.15965 × 10⁻³, κ_μ/μ_Bμ = 1.16592 × 10⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Δ baryons in neutron stars by Dexheimer (2021) and Thapa (2020) by including for the first time the effects of anomalous magnetic moments of Δ baryons in magnetized matter and solving the full Einstein-Maxwell equations for anisotropic stellar configurations. It builds on the L3ωρ parametrization from L3wr (2021) and the chiral mean-field model from Dexheimer (2008), and uses coupling constraints from lattice QCD by Cloet (2003) and transport models by Cozma (2021). The results enable future studies of magnetar structure with self-consistent magnetic field profiles and Δ baryon populations, though the authors note that the effects of Landau quantization and AMMs on global properties remain to be checked explicitly.
