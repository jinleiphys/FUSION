# A data-driven analysis for the temperature and momentum dependence of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> By applying a Bayesian model-to-data analysis, we estimate the temperature and momentum dependence of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient in an improved Langevin framework. The posterior range of the diffusion coefficient is obtained by performing a Markov chain Monte Carlo random walk and calibrating on the experimental data of $D$-meson $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$ and $v_2$ in three different collision systems at RHIC and the LHC: AuAu collisions at 200 GeV, PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. The spatial diffusion coefficient is found to be consistent with lattice QCD calculations and comparable with other models' estimation. We demonstrate the capability of our improved Langevin model to simultaneously describe the $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$ and $v_2$ at both RHIC and the LHC energies, as well as the higher order flow coefficient such as $D$-meson $v_3$. We show that by applying a Bayesian analysis, we are able to quantitatively and systematically study the heavy flavor dynamics in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors use a Bayesian model-to-data analysis to extract the temperature and momentum dependence of the heavy quark spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) from \(D\)-meson \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) and \(v_2\) data, but the extracted posterior range is conditional on a specific three-parameter ansatz that interpolates between a linear temperature-dependent form at low momentum and a perturbative QCD calculation at high momentum, with the transition controlled by a free parameter \(\gamma\).

## Method

The authors simulate heavy quark evolution in a QGP medium using an improved Langevin framework that includes both collisional and radiative energy loss, with the QGP medium evolved via (2+1)-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNEW). They generate 60 training points in the three-dimensional parameter space \((\alpha, \beta, \gamma)\) using a Latin hypercube design, compute \(D\)-meson \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) and \(v_2\) for AuAu collisions at 200 GeV, PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, and train Gaussian process emulators to interpolate the model output. A Markov chain Monte Carlo random walk is then performed to calibrate the parameters against 69 experimental data points, yielding posterior distributions for the diffusion coefficient.

## Key numbers

* Spatial diffusion coefficient at \(T_c\) (zero momentum): parameter \(\alpha\) prior range 0.1–7.0 (dimensionless \(D_s 2\pi T\)).
* Slope of the linear component above \(T_c\): parameter \(\beta\) prior range 0–5.0.
* Transition parameter between linear and pQCD components: \(\gamma\) prior range 0.0–0.6.
* Training set: 60 design points in 3-dimensional parameter space.
* Experimental calibration data: 69 data points from three collision systems (AuAu at 200 GeV, PbPb at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV).
* Soft medium thermalization time: \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c.
* Critical temperature for hadronization: \(T_c = 154\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian model-to-data analysis, previously applied to extract the specific shear viscosity of the QGP from soft hadron data (Bernhard 2016), to the heavy flavor sector for the first time. It addresses the "heavy quark \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) and \(v_2\) puzzle" by using a parametrized diffusion coefficient that combines a linear temperature-dependent form (inspired by the shear viscosity parametrization of Bernhard 2015) with a leading-order pQCD calculation, enabling a quantitative comparison with lattice QCD calculations (Ding 2012, Francis 2015, Banerjee 2011). The results enable future simultaneous extraction of soft and heavy-flavor transport coefficients within a unified Bayesian framework.
