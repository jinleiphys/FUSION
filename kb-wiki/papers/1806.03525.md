# Nuclear charge radii of $^{229}$Th from isotope and isomer shifts

> The isotope $^{229}$Th is unique in that it possesses an isomeric state of only a few eV above the ground state, suitable for nuclear laser excitation. An optical clock based on this transition is expected to be a very sensitive probe for variations of fundamental constants, but the nuclear properties of both states have to be determined precisely to derive the actual sensitivity. We carry out isotope shift calculations in Th$^+$ and Th$^{2+}$ including the specific mass shift, using a combination of configuration interaction and all-order linearized coupled-cluster methods and estimate the uncertainty of this approach. We perform experimental measurements of the hyperfine structure of Th$^{2+}$ and isotopic shift between $^{229}$Th$^{2+}$ and $^{232}$Th$^{2+}$ to extract the difference in root-mean-square radii as $δ\langle r^{2} \rangle^{232,229}=0.299(15)$ fm$^2$. Using the recently measured values of the isomer shift of lines of $^{229\textrm{m}}$Th, we derive the value for the mean-square radius change between $^{229}$Th and its low lying isomer $^{229\textrm{m}}$Th to be $δ\langle r^2 \rangle^{229\textrm{m},229} = 0.0105(13)\,{\rm fm}^2$.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the difference in root-mean-square charge radii between \(^{229}\)Th and \(^{232}\)Th and between \(^{229}\)Th and its low-lying isomer \(^{229\textrm{m}}\)Th, using a combination of theoretical calculations and experimental measurements; the authors note that the smallness of the isomer–ground-state radius difference makes it challenging to determine the difference in Coulomb energy because quadrupole and higher-order deformations lead to significant contributions.

## Method

The authors carried out isotope shift calculations in Th\(^+\) and Th\(^{2+}\) using a hybrid approach combining configuration interaction with all-order linearized coupled-cluster methods (CI+all-order), including the specific mass shift. They performed experimental measurements of the hyperfine structure of Th\(^{2+}\) and the isotopic shift between \(^{229}\)Th\(^{2+}\) and \(^{232}\)Th\(^{2+}\) for three transitions using saturated absorption spectroscopy with trapped ions. The difference in root-mean-square radii was extracted by combining the experimental isotope shifts with the calculated field shift and specific mass shift coefficients.

## Key numbers

- \(\delta \langle r^{2} \rangle^{232,229} = 0.299(15)\) fm\(^2\) for the difference in mean-square charge radius between \(^{232}\)Th and \(^{229}\)Th
- \(\delta \langle r^2 \rangle^{229\textrm{m},229} = 0.0105(13)\) fm\(^2\) for the mean-square radius change between \(^{229}\)Th and its low-lying isomer \(^{229\textrm{m}}\)Th
- The specific mass shift contributes 3–4% of the total isotope shift in the two Th\(^{2+}\) transitions of interest, and only 1% for the Th\(^+\) transition
- The uncertainty from the spherical contribution \(\delta \langle r^2 \rangle^{229\textrm{m},229}\) to the Coulomb energy difference is about 30 keV

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of thorium isotope shifts by Kal et al. (1989) and provides a threefold improvement in precision for \(\delta \langle r^{2} \rangle^{232,229}\) compared to the value of 0.33(5) fm\(^2\) from that work. The results enable more precise determination of the sensitivity of the \(^{229}\)Th nuclear clock transition to variations of fundamental constants, resolving controversy between predictions by Hayes (2008), Berengut (2009), and Litvinova (2009). The authors note that determining the differences in deformation parameters \(\beta_2, \beta_3, \beta_4\) with uncertainties in the low \(10^{-3}\) range will be needed to reach similar uncertainty for the Coulomb energy difference in the deformed nucleus.
