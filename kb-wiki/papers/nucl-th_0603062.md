# Self-consistent description of multipole strength: systematic calculations

> We use the quasiparticle random phase approximation with a few Skyrme density functionals to calculate strength functions in the Jpi = 0+, 1-, and 2+ channels for even Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes, from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line. We show where and how low-lying strength begins to appear as N increases. We also exhibit partial energy-weighted sums of the transition strength as functions of N for all nuclei calculated, and transition densities for many of the interesting peaks. We find that low-energy strength increases with N in all multipoles, but with distinctive features in each. The low-lying 0+ strength near the neutron at large N barely involves protons at all, with the strength coming primarily from a single two-quasineutron configuration with very large spatial extent. The low-lying 1- strength is different, with protons contributing to the transition density in the nuclear interior together with neutrons at large radii. The low-lying 2+ transition strength goes largely to more localized states. The three Skyrme interactions we test produce similar results, differing most significantly in their predictions for the location of the neutron drip line, the boundaries of deformed regions, energies of and transition strengths to the lowest 2+ states between closed shells, and isovector energy-weighted sum rules.

## Key claim

Low-energy multipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei increases with neutron number across all multipoles (0+, 1-, 2+), but the character of this strength differs qualitatively by multipole: 0+ strength near the drip line is almost purely neutron-based and non-collective, 1- strength involves both protons and neutrons, and 2+ strength remains more localized and collective even at large neutron excess. The three Skyrme interactions tested produce similar qualitative trends but differ in predictions for the neutron drip line location, deformed-region boundaries, and isovector sum rules.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in coordinate space with a 20-fm box, then diagonalized the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) Hamiltonian in a canonical basis truncated by occupation probability or single-particle energy thresholds. They computed isoscalar and isovector strength functions for Jpi = 0+, 1-, and 2+ channels in even Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line using three Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SLy4, and SkP) with volume-type delta pairing. Partial energy-weighted sums up to 10, 20, 30, and 100-150 MeV were evaluated for all nuclei, and radial transition densities were extracted for representative low-lying states.

## Key numbers

- The largest QRPA Hamiltonian matrix encountered was about 14,000 (in the 1- channel for Sn near the neutron drip line); the minimum was about 2,000 (in the 0+ channel for Ca near the proton drip line).
- In 76Ca, 45% of the isoscalar 1- energy-weighted strength lies below 10 MeV.
- For 80Ni (SkM*), the IV strength in the lowest 2+ peak at 400 keV is 5% of the IS strength.
- The low-energy 0+ bump first appears at 10 MeV in 50Ca and at 6 MeV in 80Ni; in Sn it first appears at 8 MeV in 136Sn.
- For 60Ca, the IV 0+ energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) is 19,575 e^2 MeV fm^4 for SkM* and 17,602 e^2 MeV fm^4 for SLy4; the SkM* value of C_c is 68.74 MeV fm^5 versus 32.47 MeV fm^5 for SLy4.
- The QRPA basis truncation kept states with v_i^2 >= 10^{-12} for 0+ and 1- channels, and v_i^2 >= 10^{-8} for the 2+ channel; in unpaired cases, single-particle states were kept up to 150 MeV (0+, 1-) or 100 MeV (2+).

## Context

This work extends systematic QRPA calculations of multipole strength (beyond the 1- channel emphasized in prior work) to three isotopic chains and three multipolarities, complementing earlier studies by Catara et al. (1997), Sagawa et al. (2001), and Matsuo (2005) that focused on specific nuclei or channels. The results enable systematic comparison of low-energy strength emergence across multipoles and isotopic chains, and identify nuclei such as 50Ca and 80Ni where the low-energy mode first appears and could be tested experimentally. The paper also provides a benchmark for distinguishing Skyrme interactions through their predictions for the neutron drip line, deformed regions, and isovector sum rules.
