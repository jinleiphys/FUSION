# Probing the quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon spectrum for electromagnetic dissociation of relativistic uranium projectiles

> Electromagnetic fission cross sections for the reactions U + (Be, C, Al, Cu, In, Au, U) at E/A = 0.6 and 1.0 GeV are compared to theoretical calculations using recently proposed quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon spectra. In contrast to semi-classical calculations, systematically lower cross sections are obtained that cannot reproduce the experimental results. Furthermore, we point out that the study of electromagnetic fission cross sections or electromagnetic 1-neutron removal cross sections alone cannot provide unambiguous information on the excitation of the double giant dipole resonance.

# Probing the quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon spectrum for electromagnetic dissociation of relativistic uranium projectiles

## Key claim

The quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon spectra proposed by Benesh, Hayes, and Friar (1996) produce systematically lower electromagnetic fission cross sections than the semi-classical Weizsäcker-Williams method and cannot reproduce experimental data for uranium projectiles at 0.6 and 1.0 GeV/nucleon, unless the momentum-space cutoff parameter is artificially increased by 30%. Additionally, neither electromagnetic fission cross sections nor 1-neutron removal cross sections alone can provide unambiguous information on the excitation of the double giant dipole resonance.

## Method

Electromagnetic fission cross sections were measured for 238U projectiles incident on seven targets (Be, C, Al, Cu, In, Au, U) at beam energies of 0.6 and 1.0 GeV/nucleon using the ALADIN spectrometer at GSI, Darmstadt. The experimental cross sections were compared to calculations using both the semi-classical Weizsäcker-Williams method and the quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon spectra of Benesh, Hayes, and Friar (1996), employing the same parameterizations for photodissociation cross sections, fission probability, and cutoff parameters.

## Key numbers

- Electromagnetic fission cross sections for 238U + 238U at 1.0 GeV/nucleon: the quantum-mechanical calculation requires an artificially increased cutoff parameter in momentum space by 30%, yielding b_min = 12.5 fm compared to b_min = 16.3 fm from the standard parameterization
- The Kox parametrization of the cutoff parameter results in approximately 15% lower cross sections relative to semi-classical calculations
- Use of photodissociation cross sections from Caldwell (1980), known to be systematically high, results in only 15% higher cross sections
- Peak-to-valley ratio of the fission fragment charge distribution: 7.6 ± 2.6 and 7.1 ± 1.0 from two independent experiments, compared to a calculated value of 16 ± 3 for single phonon excitation alone

## Context

This work extends previous electromagnetic dissociation studies at relativistic energies (Bertulani and Baur, 1988; Aumann et al., 1993, 1995) and directly tests the quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon formalism proposed by Benesh, Hayes, and Friar (1996). The results contradict the claim by Benesh, Hayes, and Friar that their quantum-mechanical spectra leave little room for multiphonon mechanisms. The paper points to alternative observables that provide clearer evidence for double giant dipole resonance excitation, including the peak-to-valley ratio of fission fragment charge distributions (Rubehn et al., 1995; Armbruster et al., 1996), multi-neutron removal cross sections (Aumann et al., 1993, 1995), and direct excitation energy reconstruction via gamma-ray and neutron measurements (Boretzky et al., 1996).
