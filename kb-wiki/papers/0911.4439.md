# Non-equilibrium approaches to the pre-thermal and post-hadronisation stages of A+A collisions

> The results related to non-equilibrium phenomena at the very early and late stages of the processes of A+A collisions are presented. A good description of the hadron momentum spectra as well as pion and kaon interferometry data at RHIC is reached within the realistic dynamical picture of A+A collisions: HydroKinetic Model (HKM). The model accumulates the following features: not too early thermalization time; $τ\geq 1$ fm/c; a developing of the pre-thermal transverse flows; the effectively more hard, than in the case of chemical equilibrium, equation of state of expanding chemically non-equilibrated multi-hadronic gas; a continuous non-equilibrated emission of hadrons. All these factors lead to a good description of the mentioned RHIC data, in particular, the observed $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratios, solving, therefore, the HBT puzzle in detailed realistic model.

## Key claim

The HydroKinetic Model (HKM) achieves a good simultaneous description of hadron momentum spectra and pion/kaon interferometry radii at RHIC without requiring early thermalization (before 1 fm/c), provided that pre-thermal transverse flows are developed and a chemically non-equilibrated equation of state is used for the hadronic stage.

## Method

The authors construct the HydroKinetic Model (HKM) which couples a pre-thermal stage (described via a phenomenological relaxation-time approach motivated by Boltzmann equations) to ideal hydrodynamics for the equilibrated QGP and hadronic phases, and then to a continuous non-equilibrated hadron emission stage. The hadronic phase below T_ch = 165 MeV is treated as a chemically non-equilibrated multi-component gas of 359 hadron species with coupled equations for particle number densities including resonance decays and (optionally) recombination. The emission function for pions is computed from the gain term in Boltzmann equations and escape probabilities using UrQMD cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time: τ ≥ 1 fm/c (chosen as τ_th = 1 fm/c for the calculations)
- Initial maximal energy density at thermalization: ε₀ = 17 GeV/fm³
- Initial transverse rapidity slope parameter: α = 0.35
- Transverse radius of the fireball: R = 5.4 fm for Au+Au collisions
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_ch = 165 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out chemical potentials: μ_B = 29 MeV, μ_S = 7 MeV, μ_E = -1 MeV
- Maximal pion emission occurs at proper time τ ≈ 13 fm/c and transverse radius r ≈ 8.4 fm
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios at the emission region: η_π/s_π = 4.2 × (1/4π), η_p/s_p = 0.68 × (1/4π), total η/s = 6.27 × (1/4π)
- Mean free paths at emission region: λ_π = 3 fm, λ_p = 1.2 fm
- Knudsen numbers at emission region: Kn_π ≈ 0.5, Kn_p ≈ 0.2

## Context

This work extends the HydroKinetic Model previously developed by the same group (Sinyukov et al., 2008, 2009) to include resonance decays in the expanding chemically non-equilibrated hadronic system. It addresses the "HBT puzzle" at RHIC by showing that pre-thermal flow development and continuous non-equilibrated hadron emission reduce the R_out/R_side ratios to match experimental data, without requiring the very early thermalization (τ ~ 0.5 fm/c) needed in standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamics (Heinz et al., 2006; Teaney, 2003). The approach enables systematic inclusion of dissipative effects at the hadronic stage and provides estimates of viscosity and Knudsen numbers to assess the applicability of viscous hydrodynamics.
