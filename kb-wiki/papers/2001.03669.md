# Bayesian inference of the incompressibility, skewness and kurtosis of nuclear matter from empirical pressures in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Within the Bayesian statistical framework we infer the incompressibility $K_0$, skewness $J_0$ and kurtosis $Z_0$ parameters of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) at its saturation density $ρ_0$ using the constraining bands on the pressure in cold SNM in the density range of 1.3$ρ_0$ to 4.5$ρ_0$ from transport model analyses of kaon production and nuclear collective flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. As the default option assuming the $K_0$, $J_0$ and $Z_0$ have Gaussian prior probability distribution functions (PDFs) with the means and variances of $235\pm 30$, $-200\pm 200$ and $-146\pm 1728$ MeV, their posterior most probable values are narrowed down to 192$^{+12}_{-16}$ MeV, -180$^{+100}_{-110}$ MeV and 200$^{+250}_{-250}$ at 68\% confidence level, respectively. The results are largely independent of the prior PDFs of $J_0$ and $Z_0$ used. However, if one adopts the strong belief that the incompressibility $K_0$ has a uniform prior PDF within its absolute boundary of 220-260 MeV as one can find easily in the literature, the posterior most probable values of $K_0$, $J_0$ and $Z_0$ shift to $K_0=220^{+6}_{-0}$ MeV, $J_0=-390^{+60}_{-70}$ MeV and $Z_0=600^{+200}_{-200}$ MeV, respectively. While the posterior PDFs of the SNM EOS parameters depend somewhat on the prior PDF of $K_0$ used, the results from using different prior PDFs are qualitatively consistent. The uncertainties of all three parameters are significantly reduced especially for the $J_0$ and $Z_0$ parameters compared to their current values.

## Key claim

Within a Bayesian framework, the incompressibility \(K_0\), skewness \(J_0\), and kurtosis \(Z_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) at saturation density are inferred from empirical pressure bands extracted from heavy-ion collisions, but the posterior most probable values depend somewhat on the chosen prior probability distribution function (PDF) for \(K_0\); using a Gaussian prior yields \(K_0 = 192^{+12}_{-16}\) MeV, while using a uniform prior for \(K_0\) between 220 and 260 MeV shifts the result to \(K_0 = 220^{+6}_{-0}\) MeV.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference using the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm for Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling, adopting a Taylor-like parameterization of the SNM equation of state \(E_0(\rho)\) up to the fourth-order term in \((\rho - \rho_0)/(3\rho_0)\). They used empirical pressure constraints from transport model analyses of kaon production (density range 1.3\(\rho_0\) to 2.2\(\rho_0\)) and nuclear collective flow (2.0\(\rho_0\) to 4.5\(\rho_0\)), treating the width of the constraining bands as 3\(\sigma\) error bars. The likelihood function was constructed from the digitized pressure data points, and posterior PDFs were obtained by marginalizing over the three parameters with Gaussian priors as the default option.

## Key numbers

*   With default Gaussian priors (means and variances: \(K_0 = 235 \pm 30\) MeV, \(J_0 = -200 \pm 200\) MeV, \(Z_0 = -146 \pm 1728\) MeV), the posterior most probable values from combined kaon and flow data are \(K_0 = 192^{+12}_{-16}\) MeV, \(J_0 = -180^{+100}_{-110}\) MeV, and \(Z_0 = 200^{+250}_{-250}\) MeV at 68% confidence level.
*   Using a uniform prior for \(K_0\) between 220 and 260 MeV (with Gaussian priors for \(J_0\) and \(Z_0\)), the posterior most probable values shift to \(K_0 = 220^{+6}_{-0}\) MeV, \(J_0 = -390^{+60}_{-70}\) MeV, and \(Z_0 = 600^{+200}_{-200}\) MeV at 68% confidence level.
*   From the kaon data alone (1.3\(\rho_0\) to 2.2\(\rho_0\)), the 68% credible intervals are \(K_0 = 202^{+14}_{-16}\) MeV, \(J_0 = -260^{+110}_{-160}\) MeV, and \(Z_0 = -200^{+750}_{-1000}\) MeV.
*   From the flow data alone (2.0\(\rho_0\) to 4.5\(\rho_0\)), the 68% credible intervals are \(K_0 = 208^{+16}_{-20}\) MeV, \(J_0 = -240^{+110}_{-120}\) MeV, and \(Z_0 = 250^{+300}_{-250}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian analyses of the SNM equation of state by simultaneously constraining the incompressibility, skewness, and kurtosis using empirical heavy-ion collision pressure data, going beyond earlier studies that focused mainly on \(K_0\) from giant monopole resonance data (e.g., Khan et al., Shlomo et al., Garg and Colo). The results are qualitatively consistent with but more loosely constrained than the \(J_0 = -190^{+40}_{-40}\) MeV found by Xie and Li (2019) from neutron star observations, and they significantly narrow the prior ranges for \(J_0\) and \(Z_0\) compared to the wide spreads from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock, relativistic mean-field, and chiral effective field theory predictions (e.g., Margueron et al., Dutra et al., Drischler et al.). The inferred parameters provide a benchmark for future studies of super-dense nuclear matter and can be used to test predictions from nuclear many-body theories.
