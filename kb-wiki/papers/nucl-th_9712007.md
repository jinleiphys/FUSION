# Photons from Hadronic Matter at Finite Temperature

> Temperature dependence of hadronic decay widths and masses are studied within the framework of an effective Lagrangian approach. At finite temperature the hadronic masses do not seem to follow a universal scaling law. Considering an exhaustive set of hadronic reactions and vector meson decays we have estimated the photon spectrum emitted from hot hadronic matter taking into account medium effects through thermal loop corrections on the hadronic decay widths and masses. An enhancement in photon emission rate is obtained when we use the in-medium masses of vector mesons in our calculations. It is observed that the effect of $ρ$ decay width on the photon spectra is negligible.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including temperature-dependent hadronic masses (particularly for the rho and omega mesons) leads to an enhancement in the photon emission rate from hot hadronic matter, but the effect of the temperature-dependent rho decay width on the photon spectra is negligible. The authors qualify that their calculations apply to high temperature and zero net baryon density, relevant to future RHIC and LHC experiments rather than present SPS data.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach within the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model to calculate the temperature dependence of vector meson masses and decay widths. They computed the rho and omega meson self-energies from thermal nucleon and pion loops using finite-temperature field theory with imaginary time formalism, including both vacuum polarization (Dirac sea) and medium-dependent contributions. The photon emission rates from hot hadronic matter were then calculated using relativistic kinetic theory for an exhaustive set of reactions (pi pi -> rho gamma, pi rho -> pi gamma, pi pi -> eta gamma, pi eta -> pi gamma) and vector meson decays (rho -> pi pi gamma, omega -> pi gamma), incorporating the in-medium masses and widths.

## Key numbers

* Effective nucleon mass (M*) remains almost constant up to a temperature of 140 MeV, then starts falling; above 180 MeV it decreases very sharply.
* The overall decrease in effective rho mass is approximately 32% at T = 180 MeV from its free space value of 770 MeV.
* The effective omega mass decreases by about 200 MeV at T = 180 MeV from its free space value.
* The in-medium contribution to the rho mass from N anti-N polarisation increases very little (approximately 5-10 MeV) with temperature.
* The effective rho mass due to pi pi polarisation shows a very small (2%) increase at T = 180 MeV.
* At T = 180 MeV, the total photon emission rate with finite temperature effects is a factor of approximately 3 higher than the rate calculated without medium effects.
* At low photon energy (E less than or equal to 0.5 GeV), the photon yield from pi rho -> pi gamma with medium effects increases by an order of magnitude; at higher energies the enhancement is by a factor of approximately 2-3.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronic properties at finite temperature using QCD sum rules by Hatsuda and collaborators (1990s) and Brown and Rho (1991), who predicted a universal scaling of hadronic masses with temperature. The authors find that hadronic masses do not follow such a universal scaling law, and their calculated masses fall at a slower rate than predicted by Brown-Rho scaling. The paper also builds on calculations of photon production from hadronic matter by Gale and collaborators (1990s) and Rapp and collaborators (1990s), incorporating medium effects through thermal loop corrections. The results enable more accurate estimates of the background photon spectrum from hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions, which is essential for identifying signals from quark-gluon plasma formation.
