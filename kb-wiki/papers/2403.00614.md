# A Light-Front Model for the Transition Distribution Amplitudes for Backward Timelike Compton Scattering

> To access information on the internal structure of the nucleon, data from a variety of scattering experiments can be analyzed, in regimes where the information factorizes from an otherwise known scattering amplitude. A recent development, promising new insight, is the study of exclusive reactions in the backward kinematical region, where the information can be encoded in Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs). We model the photon-to-nucleon TDAs, entering the factorized description of backward Timelike Compton Scattering, using techniques of light-front dynamics to integrate information from a quark model for the photon and the nucleon. We include the results of numerical predictions that could inform further experiments at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron--Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors model photon-to-nucleon Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) for backward Timelike Compton Scattering using light-front dynamics, but only in the DGLAP I support region where the photon is treated as a quark-antiquark pair and the nucleon is truncated to its three valence quarks, with the nucleon model previously fitted to electromagnetic form factors at an energy scale of about 0.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct light-front wave functions for the photon (as a quark-antiquark pair at tree level) and for the proton (using a constituent quark model with six orbital-angular-momentum components fitted to electromagnetic form factors). They then evaluate the matrix elements of a trilocal operator between the initial photon and final nucleon states, focusing on the leading-twist contributions in the DGLAP I support region where one antiquark is annihilated and two quarks are created. The resulting helicity amplitudes are combined into four independent TDAs (V, A, T1, T2) for the exactly backward case with zero transverse momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Light-quark mass: m = 0.263 GeV
- Proton model parameter: betatilde = 0.607 GeV
- Proton model parameter: gammatilde = 3.5
- Proton model normalization: Ntilde = 0.047 GeV^{-4}
- The energy scale at which the nucleon is well represented by constituent valence quarks is about 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the light-front dynamical approach previously applied to Generalized Parton Distributions (Boffi, 2002-2007) and nucleon-to-neutral-pion TDAs (Pasquini, 2009) to the photon-to-nucleon TDAs needed for backward Timelike Compton Scattering. The model provides numerical predictions that could inform experiments at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider, building on the first experimental TCS data published by the CLAS collaboration in 2021 for the forward region. The authors note that the Bethe-Heitler background is significantly suppressed in backward kinematics, making this channel especially promising for phenomenological studies.
