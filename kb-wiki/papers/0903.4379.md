# Equation of state and QCD transition at finite temperature

> We calculate the equation of state in 2+1 flavor QCD at finite temperature with physical strange quark mass and almost physical light quark masses using lattices with temporal extent Nt=8. Calculations have been performed with two different improved staggered fermion actions, the asqtad and p4 actions. Overall, we find good agreement between results obtained with these two O(a^2) improved staggered fermion discretization schemes. A comparison with earlier calculations on coarser lattices is performed to quantify systematic errors in current studies of the equation of state. We also present results for observables that are sensitive to deconfining and chiral aspects of the QCD transition on Nt=6 and 8 lattices. We find that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration happen in the same narrow temperature interval. In an Appendix we present a simple parametrization of the equation of state that can easily be used in hydrodynamic model calculations. In this parametrization we also incorporated an estimate of current uncertainties in the lattice calculations which arise from cutoff and quark mass effects. We estimate these systematic effects to be about 10 MeV

## Key claim

The paper reports lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state in 2+1 flavor QCD at finite temperature, finding that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration occur in the same narrow temperature interval, with systematic uncertainties from cutoff and quark mass effects estimated at about 10 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two different O(a^2) improved staggered fermion actions (asqtad and p4) on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=8, using physical strange quark mass and light quark masses set to one-tenth of the strange quark mass. They calculated the trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T^4 from which pressure, energy density, and entropy density were derived via thermodynamic integration, and compared results with earlier Nτ=6 calculations to quantify cutoff effects.

## Key numbers

- Light pseudo-scalar Goldstone mass: about 220 MeV (corresponding to light quark masses one-tenth of the strange quark mass)
- Non-Goldstone masses in the transition region: about 400 MeV for asqtad action, about 500 MeV for p4 action
- Scale r0 = 0.469(7) fm determined from the Υ(2S-1S) splitting
- Ratio r0/r1 = 1.4636(60) (p4 action) and 1.474(7)(18) (asqtad action)
- Cutoff dependence of (ε-3p)/T^4 near its maximum: about 15% for p4 action, about half that size for asqtad action
- Systematic effects estimated at about 10 MeV
- Peak height of (ε-3p)/T^4 about 15% smaller with asqtad action than with p4 action
- Fit parameters for high-temperature trace anomaly (p4 action, Nτ=8, T≥300 MeV): d2 = 0.22(3) GeV^2, d4 = 0.008(4) GeV^4

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the QCD equation of state performed with asqtad and p4 actions on coarser Nτ=4 and 6 lattices by the MILC Collaboration (2007) and RBC-Bielefeld Collaboration (2007). The results provide a controlled lattice determination of bulk thermodynamic observables needed for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the parametrization given in the appendix enables direct use in such models. The comparison between two independent improved staggered fermion actions establishes the current systematic uncertainty in lattice calculations of the QCD equation of state.
