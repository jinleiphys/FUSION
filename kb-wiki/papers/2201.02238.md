# Information content of the differences in the charge radii of mirror nuclei

> Differences in the charge radii of mirror nuclei have been recently suggested to contain information on the slope of the symmetry energy L. To test this hypothesis, we perform statistical correlation analysis using quantified energy density functionals that are consistent with our previous knowledge on global nuclear observables such as binding energies and charge radii. We conclude that the difference in charge radii between a mirror pair, R_mir, is an inferior isovector indicator compared to other observables, such at the neutron skin or electric dipole polarizability. In particular, this quantity correlates poorly with both the neutron skin and L. We demonstrate that R_mir is influenced by pairing correlations in the presence of low-lying proton continuum in the proton-rich mirror-partner nucleus. Considering the large theoretical uncertainties on R_mir, we conclude that the precise data on mirror charge radii cannot provide a stringent constraint on L.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the difference in charge radii between mirror nuclei (R_mir) is an inferior isovector indicator compared to other observables like neutron skin or electric dipole polarizability, and that precise data on mirror charge radii cannot provide a stringent constraint on the symmetry energy slope L, contrary to earlier suggestions by Brown (2017) and Yang (2018).

## Method

The authors performed statistical correlation analysis using quantified nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs) within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework, employing the Skyrme parametrizations SV-min and Fy(Δr, HFB) that were calibrated to global nuclear observables. They computed bivariate correlation coefficients and multiple correlation coefficients between mirror radius differences and various model parameters, comparing results from HFB, BCS, and Hartree-Fock with equal filling approximation treatments of pairing.

## Key numbers

- For R_mir(⁵⁴Ni/Fe) with SV-min: CoD_HFB = 0.69, CoD_BCS = 0.44, CoD_HF+EFA = 0.77
- For neutron skin in ²⁰⁸Pb with SV-min: CoD = 0.99; for electric dipole polarizability in ²⁰⁸Pb: CoD = 0.98
- Uncertainties: σ_L(BCS) = 26.5 MeV, σ_L(HFB) = 27.2 MeV, σ_Rmir(BCS) = 0.0065 fm, σ_Rmir(HFB) = 0.0041 fm
- Proton Fermi levels for ³⁶Ca: -1.0 MeV (SV-min) and -0.7 MeV (Fy); for ⁴⁸Ni: +1.9 MeV (SV-min) and +3.5 MeV (Fy); for ⁵⁴Ni: -2.6 MeV (SV-min) and -1.3 MeV (Fy)
- Expected accuracy for precision calculation of charge radii: 0.015 fm
- Statistical errors on predicted R_mir vary between 0.005-0.02 fm depending on |N-Z|

## Context

This work directly tests the hypothesis proposed by Brown (2017) and Yang (2018) that mirror charge radii differences can provide stringent constraints on the symmetry energy slope L. The authors extend prior correlation analysis frameworks (Reinhard 2010, Fattoyev 2011) to specifically examine mirror nuclei, finding that earlier studies overestimated correlations by neglecting pairing correlations and using models with fewer isovector parameters. The results enable more careful interpretation of recent measurements for ³⁶Ca/S (Brown 2020) and ⁵⁴Ni/Fe (Pineda 2021), and suggest that neutron skin in ²⁰⁸Pb and electric dipole polarizability remain superior isovector indicators for constraining nuclear symmetry energy.
