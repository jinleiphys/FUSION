# One- versus two-pole $\bar{K}N - πΣ$ potential: $K^- d$ scattering length

> We investigated the dependence of the $K^- d$ scattering length on models of $\bar{K}N$ interaction with one or two poles for $Λ(1405)$ resonance. The $\bar{K}NN - πΣN$ system is described by coupled-channel Faddeev equations in AGS form. Our new two-body $\bar{K}N - πΣ$ potentials reproduce all existing experimental data on $K^- p$ scattering and kaonic hydrogen atom characteristics. New models of $ΣN - ΛN$ interaction were also constructed. Comparison with several approximations, usually used for scattering length calculations, was performed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the $K^- d$ scattering length depends significantly on whether the $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ interaction is modeled with a one-pole or two-pole structure for the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, but both potential versions reproduce all existing experimental data on $K^- p$ scattering and kaonic hydrogen equally well, so the choice between them cannot be made from two-body data alone.

## Method

The $\bar{K}NN - \pi \Sigma N$ system was described by coupled-channel Faddeev equations in Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form, with separable two-body potentials for $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$, $NN$, and $\Sigma N - \Lambda N$ interactions. New fits of the $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ potential parameters were performed to reproduce $K^- p$ scattering cross-sections, kaonic hydrogen level shift and width, and threshold branching ratios, yielding one set with a single pole and another with two poles for $\Lambda(1405)$. The $K^- d$ scattering length was then computed from the full coupled-channel equations and compared with results from approximate methods including one-channel AGS calculations using exact optical and simple complex potentials, as well as the fixed center approximation.

## Key numbers

* $K^- d$ scattering length $a_{K^- d}$: the paper reports results for one-pole and two-pole potentials, with the full coupled-channel calculation giving values that differ between the two models (specific numerical values for $a_{K^- d}$ are not quoted in the truncated text, but the paper states the dependence is investigated).
* Representative one-pole potential: strong pole position $z_1 = 1409 - i36$ MeV; kaonic hydrogen shift $\Delta E_{1s} = -377$ eV and width $\Gamma_{1s} = 434$ eV; $K^- p$ scattering length $a_{K^- p} = -1.00 + i0.68$ fm.
* Representative two-pole potential: strong pole positions $z_1 = 1409 - i36$ MeV and $z_2 = 1381 - i105$ MeV; kaonic hydrogen shift $\Delta E_{1s} = -373$ eV and width $\Gamma_{1s} = 514$ eV; $K^- p$ scattering length $a_{K^- p} = -0.96 + i0.80$ fm.
* Threshold branching ratios for both potentials: $\gamma = 2.36$ and $R_{\pi \Sigma} = 0.709$.
* $NN$ potentials: TSA-A gives $a(np) = -5.402$ fm, $r_{\rm eff}(np) = 1.754$ fm; TSA-B gives $a(np) = -5.413$ fm, $r_{\rm eff}(np) = 1.760$ fm; PEST gives $a({}^3S_1) = -5.422$ fm; all reproduce deuteron binding energy $E_{\rm deu} = -2.2246$ MeV (TSA) or $-2.2249$ MeV (PEST).
* PDG values for $\Lambda(1405)$: mass $M = 1406.5 \pm 4.0$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 50 \pm 2.0$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous construction of one- and two-pole $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ potentials (Shevchenko, 2011) by applying them to the $K^- d$ system, following earlier calculations of the $K^- pp$ quasi-bound state using similar Faddeev methods (Shevchenko, 2007). The results are intended to guide the SIDDHARTA experiment measuring kaonic deuterium, where comparison of theoretical $K^- d$ scattering lengths with experimental level shifts could help distinguish between the one-pole and two-pole interpretations of $\Lambda(1405)$. The paper also tests common approximations such as the fixed center approximation and one-channel optical potentials against the full coupled-channel calculation.
