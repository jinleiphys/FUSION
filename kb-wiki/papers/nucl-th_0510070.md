# CEM03 and LAQGSM03 - new modeling tools for nuclear applications

> An improved version of the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) of nuclear reactions realized in the code CEM2k and the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM) have been developed recently at LANL to describe reactions induced by particles and nuclei for a number of applications. Our CEM2k and LAQGSM merged with the GEM2 evaporation/fission code by Furihata have predictive powers comparable to other modern codes and describe many reactions better than other codes; therefore both our codes can be used as reliable event generators in transport codes for applications. During the last year, we have made a significant improvements to the intranuclear cascade parts of CEM2k and LAQGSM, and have extended LAQGSM to describe photonuclear reactions at energies to 10 GeV and higher. We have produced in this way improved versions of our codes, CEM03.01 and LAQGSM03.01. We present a brief description of our codes and show illustrative results obtained with CEM03.01 and LAQGSM03.01 for different reactions compared with predictions by other models, as well as examples of using our codes as modeling tools for nuclear applications.

## Key claim

The improved codes CEM03.01 and LAQGSM03.01 describe many nuclear reactions as well as or better than other modern codes, but the authors explicitly note that the agreement with experimental data is "comparable to" rather than strictly superior to other models like FLUKA, INCL, GNASH, and TALYS, and that the results "do not coincide exactly" with measured spectra.

## Method

The authors improved the intranuclear cascade parts of the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) and the Los Alamos Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM) by incorporating new systematics for angular and energy distributions of particles from nucleon-nucleon and photon-proton elementary interactions, replacing 36-year-old Dubna INC approximations with systematics from Cugnon et al. (for the Liege INC) and Duarte (for the BRIC code). They also refined the coalescence model for complex-particle emission, improved the preequilibrium stage description, and re-fitted the level-density parameter ratio a_f/a_n for fission calculations. LAQGSM was extended to describe photonuclear reactions up to tens of GeV by incorporating 56 channels for gamma-proton and 56 channels for gamma-neutron elementary interactions from the Moscow high-energy photonuclear model.

## Key numbers

* CEM03.01 and LAQGSM03.01 describe reactions at energies from 10 MeV to 800 GeV per nucleon.
* LAQGSM was extended to describe photonuclear reactions at energies up to tens of GeV (previously limited to about 1.5 GeV).
* CEM03.01 describes photonuclear reactions at incident energies from about 30 MeV to about 1.5 GeV, and can be used for estimation up to about 5 GeV.
* The improved CEM03.01 reproduces proton-induced fission cross sections on W isotopes (186, 184, 183, 182) measured at Uppsala.
* Using evaluated cross sections based on CEM2k calculations instead of phenomenological systematics reduced the derived Galactic halo size uncertainty from 3-7 kpc to 4-6 kpc.
* LAQGSM03.01 describes cumulative particle spectra from 400 GeV p + 181Ta reactions measured at FNAL 26 years prior, including K+, K-, antiprotons, d, t, 3He, and 4He spectra, simultaneously within a single approach for the first time.
* LAQGSM03.01 describes proton spectra at 30, 60, 90, and 150 degrees from bremsstrahlung gamma quanta of maximum energy 4.5 GeV with C, Al, Cu, and Pb targets, measured 28 years prior at Yerevan, for the first time.

## Context

This work extends the Cascade-Exciton Model lineage from CEM95 and CEM2k and the Los Alamos Quark-Gluon String Model from earlier LAQGSM versions, improving upon the Dubna intranuclear cascade approximations used for 36 years. The codes are used as event generators in transport codes including MCNPX, MARS, GEANT4, and MCNP6, and have been applied to medical isotope production studies for the Accelerator Production of Tritium project and to astrophysical cosmic-ray propagation modeling with the GALPROP code by Strong and Moskalenko. The improved codes enable more reliable evaluated excitation functions for astrophysical needs, reducing uncertainties in derived Galactic halo size compared to phenomenological systematics by Webber et al. (1990) and Silberberg et al. (1998).
