# Nuclear final-state interactions in deep inelastic scattering off the lightest nuclei

> We review recent progress in studies of nuclear final-state interactions in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off the lightest nuclei tagged by a recoil nucleon. These processes hold a lot of potential for resolving the outstanding issues related to the dynamics of hadronization in QCD. Within the minimal Fock component framework, valid at large Bjorken $x$, the main features of the theoretical approach based on the virtual nucleon approximation are elaborated. In this approach, the strong final-state interaction of the DIS products with the nuclear fragments is described by an effective eikonal amplitude, whose parameters can be extracted from the analysis of semi-inclusive DIS off the deuteron target. The extraction of the $Q^2$ and $W$ mass dependences of these parameters gives a new observable in studying the QCD structure of DIS final states. Another important feature of tagged DIS off the lightest nuclei is the possibility of performing pole extrapolation with a high degree of accuracy. Such extrapolation allows an extraction of the neutron structure function in a model independent way due to suppression of the final-state interaction in the on-shell limit of the struck nucleon propagator. We review the first application of the pole extrapolation to recent experimental data. Finally, we outline the extension of the framework to inclusive DIS, including a polarized deuteron target as well as its application to the tagged DIS reactions for future experiments at fixed target and collider energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off the lightest nuclei (deuteron, ³He/³H) with detection of a recoil nucleon in the target fragmentation region can resolve the dynamics of hadronization in QCD, but this requires highly accurate estimates of conventional nuclear effects and the approach is valid primarily at large Bjorken x where the minimal Fock component framework applies.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework based on the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) for describing deep inelastic scattering off bound nucleons in the deuteron, treating the spectator nucleon as on-mass shell and accounting only for positive energy projections of the bound nucleon propagator. For final-state interactions at large Bjorken x, they employ the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), where the strong interaction of DIS products with the spectator nucleon is described by an effective diffractive amplitude with parameters (total cross section, real part, slope factor) that depend on Q² and the invariant mass W of the DIS products. The framework is applied to calculate cross sections for the reaction e + d → e' + N_s + X and compared with experimental data from Jefferson Lab.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron wave function uncertainty is confined to ~5% for internal momenta up to 400 MeV/c, based on NN interaction potentials and high-energy deuteron electro-disintegration experiments.
- The internucleon distances accessible with the deuteron target range from ~4 fm down to 1.2 fm, corresponding to internal momenta of 0–400 MeV/c.
- The VNA factorization assumption for final-state interactions was verified for Q² ~ 4 GeV² and recoil nucleon momenta up to 400 MeV/c.
- The spectator nucleon momentum range considered is up to 700 MeV/c, below which only the pn component of the deuteron wave function dominates.
- The kinematic requirement for the VNA is Q² > 1–2 GeV² for inelastic scattering.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of final-state interactions in nuclear deep inelastic scattering, which historically focused on hadron suppression in e+A scattering (Osborne 1978, Ashman 1991, Airapetian 2003) and jet-quenching in heavy-ion collisions (Arsene 2004, Adams 2005). The authors build on the virtual nucleon approximation developed for quasi-elastic nuclear breakup reactions (Frankfurt 1994, Sargsian 2001) and the generalized eikonal approximation for high-energy scattering. The approach enables future experiments at fixed target and collider energies (including the Electron-Ion Collider) to extract the neutron structure function via pole extrapolation and to study medium modification of parton distribution functions (EMC effect) by varying the virtuality of the interacting nucleon through spectator momentum measurements.
