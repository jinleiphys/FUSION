# Quark angular momentum and the Sivers asymmetry

> The determination of quark angular momentum requires the knowledge of the generalized parton distribution $E$ in the forward limit. We assume a connection between this function and the Sivers transverse-momentum distribution, based on model calculations and theoretical considerations. Using this assumption, we show that it is possible to fit at the same time nucleon anomalous magnetic moments and semi-inclusive single-spin asymmetries. This imposes additional constraints on the Sivers function and opens a plausible way to quantifying the quark angular momentum.

## Key claim

The authors assume a model-inspired connection between the generalized parton distribution \(E\) in the forward limit and the Sivers transverse-momentum distribution, and show that this assumption is compatible with existing data on nucleon anomalous magnetic moments and semi-inclusive single-spin asymmetries, thereby opening a plausible but not proven way to quantify quark angular momentum.

## Method

The authors performed a combined \(\chi^2\) fit to the proton and neutron anomalous magnetic moments and to semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) Sivers asymmetry data with identified hadrons from the HERMES, COMPASS, and Jefferson Lab experiments. They parametrized the Sivers function using a Gaussian transverse-momentum distribution and a flavor-dependent functional form, and connected it to the forward limit of the GPD \(E\) via a flavor-independent lensing function \(L(x) = K/(1-x)^{\eta}\). They neglected TMD evolution, assumed a flavor- and scale-independent Gaussian transverse-momentum distribution, and included standard DGLAP evolution only in the collinear part of the parametrizing functions.

## Key numbers

- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: \(\kappa^p = 1.793 \pm 0.001\); neutron anomalous magnetic moment: \(\kappa^n = -1.913 \pm 0.001\).
- Best-fit \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom: approximately 1.34.
- Lensing function parameters: \(K \approx 1.86\) GeV, \(\eta \approx 0.4\); Sivers transverse-momentum width parameter \(M_1 \approx 0.34\) GeV.
- Up valence Sivers function node parameter: \(\alpha^{u_v} \approx 0.78\).
- At \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\): \(J^u = 0.229 \pm 0.002^{+0.008}_{-0.012}\), \(J^d = -0.007 \pm 0.003^{+0.020}_{-0.005}\), \(J^{\bar{u}} = 0.015 \pm 0.003^{+0.001}_{-0.000}\), \(J^{\bar{d}} = 0.022 \pm 0.005^{+0.001}_{-0.000}\), \(J^s = 0.006^{+0.002}_{-0.006}\), \(J^{\bar{s}} = 0.006^{+0.000}_{-0.005}\).
- Total quark plus antiquark contribution to nucleon spin at 4 GeV\(^2\): \(0.271 \pm 0.007^{+0.032}_{-0.028}\), of which 85% is carried by the up quark.
- Gluon total angular momentum estimate (assuming zero gluon Sivers function): \(J^g = 0.215\) at 4 GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Burkardt (2002), who proposed a connection between the GPD \(E\) and the Sivers function based on theoretical considerations and spectator model calculations. The results agree well with other extractions of the GPD \(E\) by Guidal (2004), Diehl (2004), Goloskokov (2008), and Goldstein (2010), as well as with lattice simulations by Hagler (2007), Bratt (2010), Brommel (2007), and Wakamatsu (2009). The approach enables a flavor-decomposed estimate of quark and antiquark total angular momentum, but the authors note that the results are subject to systematic uncertainties from the rigid functional forms assumed and that direct measurements of sea-quark and gluon Sivers functions are necessary to improve the constraints.
