# Elliptic flow of thermal photons and formation time of quark gluon plasma at RHIC

> We calculate the elliptic flow of thermal photons from Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies for a range of values for the formation time $τ_0$ but fixed entropy (or particle rapidity density). The results are found to be quite sensitive to $τ_0$. The $v_2$ for photons decreases as $τ_0$ decreases and admits a larger contribution from the QGP phase which has a smaller $v_2$. The elliptic flow coefficient for hadrons, on the other hand, is only marginally dependent on $τ_0$.

# Elliptic flow of thermal photons and formation time of quark gluon plasma at RHIC

## Key claim

The elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$ of thermal photons from Au+Au collisions at RHIC is highly sensitive to the formation time $\tau_0$ of the quark-gluon plasma, while the $v_2$ of hadrons is only marginally dependent on $\tau_0$. This sensitivity arises because smaller $\tau_0$ admits a larger contribution from the QGP phase, which has a smaller $v_2$, making the photon $v_2$ a potential experimental probe of the formation time.

## Method

The authors assume a chemically and thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma formed at time $\tau_0$, with initial entropy density profiles from a Glauber model (wounded-nucleon and binary-collision contributions with $\alpha = 0.75$), normalized to fixed total entropy ($s_0 \tau_0$ constant). They evolve the system using boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics with an equation of state incorporating a phase transition at $T \approx 164$ MeV and a resonance gas below 0.45 GeV/fm$^3$, then compute thermal photon production by folding the evolution history with leading-order QGP photon rates and hadronic gas photon rates, and calculate the differential elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ for photons and hadrons for $\tau_0$ values from 0.2 fm/$c$ to 1.0 fm/$c$.

## Key numbers

- Central entropy density at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm and $b = 0$: $s_0 = 117$ fm$^{-1}$ for Au+Au at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ A GeV.
- Impact parameter for non-central calculations: $b = 6$ fm.
- Phase transition temperature: $T \approx 164$ MeV.
- Freeze-out energy density: $e_f = 0.075$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- At $p_T = 6$ GeV/$c$, the total thermal photon yield increases by more than four orders of magnitude when $\tau_0$ drops from 1.0 fm/$c$ to 0.2 fm/$c$.
- At $p_T = 6$ GeV/$c$, the hadronic matter photon contribution increases by an order of magnitude as $\tau_0$ drops from 1.0 fm/$c$ to 0.2 fm/$c$.
- The $v_2$ for thermal photons shows large sensitivity to $\tau_0$ for $p_T$ greater than about 1.5 GeV/$c$.
- NLO pQCD calculations nearly fully account for photon production at $p_T \geq 3$ GeV/$c$ in 10-20% most central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by the same authors (Chatterjee and Srivastava, 2006) that elliptic flow of thermal photons and dileptons can probe azimuthal anisotropy of parton momenta in the early QGP phase. It contrasts with hadronic observables: the authors show that hadron $v_2$ is insensitive to $\tau_0$, consistent with the recombination model and quark-number scaling arguments from Bass (2003). The results enable experimental determination of the QGP formation time at RHIC by measuring thermal photon $v_2$, and the authors note similar sensitivity was found for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS (Chatterjee, 2008). The work also connects to parton cascade models (Koch, 1990; Geiger, 1992; Xu, 2005) and saturation models (Eskola, 2000) that predict different $\tau_0$ values.
