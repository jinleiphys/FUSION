# Nonperturbative QED Effective Action at Finite Temperature

> We advance a novel method for the finite-temperature effective action for nonequilibrium quantum fields and find the QED effective action in time-dependent electric fields, where charged pairs evolve out of equilibrium. The imaginary part of the effective action consists of thermal loops of the Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein distribution for the initial thermal ensemble weighted with factors for vacuum fluctuations. And the real part of the effective action is determined by the mean number of produced pairs, vacuum polarization, and thermal distribution. The mean number of produced pairs is equal to twice the imaginary part. We explicitly find the finite-temperature effective action in a constant electric field.

# Nonperturbative QED Effective Action at Finite Temperature

## Key claim

The paper claims that the finite-temperature QED effective action in time-dependent electric fields can be obtained from the trace of the evolution operator weighted with the initial thermal ensemble, and that this resolves the controversy over whether thermal effects on pair production exist, but only in the weak-field limit (qE << m²) does twice the imaginary part equal the mean number of produced pairs.

## Method

The authors extend the zero-temperature in- and out-state formalism to finite temperature by expressing the evolution operator in terms of Bogoliubov coefficients and taking the thermal expectation value of the evolution operator using thermofield dynamics. They compute the one-loop effective action for both spinor and scalar QED in time-dependent electric fields, expanding the real and imaginary parts in terms of Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein distributions weighted by the Bogoliubov coefficients. The method is applied explicitly to a constant electric field and discussed for a Sauter-type pulsed electric field.

## Key numbers

- For a constant electric field in the weak-field limit, the mean number of produced pairs is |ν_{kσ}|² = exp(-π(m² + k⊥²)/(qE)), independent of spin states.
- The leading factor of the imaginary part at zero temperature is ((1+2|σ|)/2)(qE/(2π))² exp(-π m²/(qE)).
- In the low-temperature limit (βm >> 1), the leading thermal contribution to the imaginary part for a constant electric field is proportional to (m²/(qE)) exp(-βm)/(βm + 2π m²/(qE)).
- For spinor QED in the low-temperature limit, the real part of the thermal contribution to the effective action in a constant electric field is approximately -(2π)²/(45 m⁴)(qE/(2π))⁴ × [(-3/(βm)) ln(1 + exp(-βm))].

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature QED effective action formalism of Sauter, Heisenberg, Euler, Weisskopf, and Schwinger to finite temperature, and builds on the evolution operator method of Kim, Lee, and Yoon (2008) for time-dependent electric fields. The paper directly addresses the controversy between Loewe and Rojas, Hallin and Liljenberg (who found thermal effects on pair production) and Elmfors and Skagerstam (who found no thermal effects), claiming that thermal effects do exist for any non-zero electric field. The method enables calculation of the effective action for general time-dependent electric fields at finite temperature and may be applied to other nonequilibrium quantum field systems.
