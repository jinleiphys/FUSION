# Variations of the crossover and first-order phase transition curve in modeling the QCD equation of state

> Lattice QCD calculations have shown that the transition from hadrons to quarks and gluons is a rapid crossover at $T = 155-160$ MeV at vanishing chemical potential. Many model calculations show that the transition is first-order at sufficiently high baryon chemical potential. It is then natural to expect the existence of a critical point where the crossover and first-order phase transition lines meet. We show how to embed a phase boundary that terminates at the critical point in a smooth background equation of state, using several different but closely related criteria, so as to yield the critical exponents and critical amplitude ratios expected of a transition in the 3D Ising and liquid-gas universality class. The crossover curves can be tuned to pass through experimental freeze-out data from heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The resulting equations of state can be used in hydrodynamic simulations of these collisions to probe the existence of a critical point and corresponding first-order phase transition.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates how to embed a critical point and first-order phase transition line into a smooth QCD equation of state using several different criteria for the phase coexistence boundary, yielding critical exponents from the 3D Ising universality class; the crossover curves can be tuned to pass through experimental freeze-out data from RHIC and LHC, but the location of the critical point is suggestive rather than conclusive.

## Method
The authors start from a background equation of state constructed by combining an excluded volume hadron model (exI) with perturbative QCD, fitted to lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential. They embed a critical point using a Schofield parametrization of the 3D Ising model, mapping Ising variables to QCD baryon density and chemical potential via the phase coexistence boundary function μ_x(T). They then test three different conditions to determine μ_x(T): a constant background density condition from prior work, a combined entropy-density squared condition (Condition A), and a constant energy density condition (Condition B), and compare the resulting crossover curves to experimental freeze-out data from heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD crossover temperature at vanishing chemical potential: T = 155-160 MeV
- Critical point chosen at T_c = 120 MeV and μ_c = 670 MeV, with critical density n_c = 0.2957 fm^{-3}
- 3D Ising critical exponents used: β = 0.3264, γ = 1.2371
- Schofield parametrization coefficients: h_3 = -0.762, h_5 = 0.008, with θ_0 ≈ 1.154
- Background equation of state fit parameters: C_S = 4.55, C_T = 3.67, M_0 = 2283.22 MeV, ε_0 = 1732.18 MeV, C_μ = 0.9; combined χ^2 per degree of freedom = 0.188
- Window function parameters chosen for heuristic calculations: P_0 = 0.05 P_c, n_0 = 0.1 n_c, m_0 = 0.5, h_0 = 0.2, j = 2, c_* = 1.0
- Λ_MSbar = 290 MeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Kapusta (2022) for embedding a QCD critical point into a smooth equation of state, which itself built on the Schofield parametrization (1969) and Widom scaling (1965). The new conditions for the phase coexistence boundary (Conditions A and B) produce crossover curves that intersect the temperature axis, unlike the prior constant-density condition, and can be tuned to approximately match freeze-out data from Andronic (2017). The resulting equations of state are intended for use in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan II and for neutron star merger simulations, enabling searches for the QCD critical point.
