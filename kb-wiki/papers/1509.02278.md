# Indication of change of phase in high-multiplicity proton-proton events at LHC in String Percolation Model

> We analyze high multiplicity proton-proton ($pp$) collision data in the framework of the String Percolation Model (SPM) that has been successful in describing several phenomena of multiparticle production, including the signatures of recent discovery of strongly interacting partonic matter, the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Our study in terms of the ratio of shear viscosity and entropy density ($η/s$) and the (LQCD) predicted signature of QCD change of phase, in terms of effective number of degrees of freedom ($ε/T^4$), reiterates the possibility of strongly interacting collective medium in these events.

## Key claim

The String Percolation Model (SPM) applied to high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies yields a ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) and an effective number of degrees of freedom (ε/T⁴) that are consistent with a QCD phase transition, but the paper presents this as an "indication" and "possibility" rather than a definitive discovery, pending higher-statistics data from LHC Run-2 at 13 TeV.

## Method

The authors fit the SPM to measured charged-particle transverse momentum spectra from CMS at √s = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV for various multiplicity classes, extracting the color suppression factor F(ζᵗ) and string density ζᵗ for each class. Using the Schwinger mechanism, they derive an effective temperature T from the string tension fluctuations, then compute η/s via relativistic kinetic theory and ε/T⁴ via the Bjorken energy density formula, comparing these to the lattice QCD critical temperature Tc = 154 ± 9 MeV from the HotLQCD collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Critical string density for 2D percolation: ζᶜₜ ≈ 1.2–1.5 (depending on profile function)
- LQCD critical temperature: Tc = 154 ± 9 MeV (HotLQCD collaboration)
- Single string transverse size: r₀ ≈ 0.25 fm; proton transverse size: Rp ≈ 1 fm
- Normalization factor κ ≈ 0.63; constant parameter λ ≈ 0.201
- Average transverse momentum of single string: ⟨pₜ⟩₁ ≈ 190.25 ± 11.12 MeV/c
- Fit parameters for minimum bias pp at √s = 7 TeV: a = 33.12 ± 9.30, p₀ = 2.32 ± 0.88, α = 9.78 ± 2.53
- Fit parameters for minimum bias pp at √s = 2.76 TeV: a = 22.48 ± 4.20, p₀ = 1.54 ± 0.46, α = 7.94 ± 1.41
- Fit parameters for minimum bias pp at √s = 0.9 TeV: a = 23.29 ± 4.48, p₀ = 1.82 ± 0.54, α = 9.40 ± 1.80
- Longitudinal extension of source L = 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the String Percolation Model, previously successful in describing collectivity and phase change in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Pajares and collaborators, 2000s), to the unexpected "ridge" phenomenon observed in high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC by CMS (2010). It provides a unified explanation for the same signatures of strongly interacting matter (η/s near the perfect fluid bound and ε/T⁴ behavior) that were seen in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (2005) and LHC, suggesting that high-multiplicity pp events may also produce a collective partonic medium. The results enable future tests with higher-statistics pp data at 13 TeV from LHC Run-2.
