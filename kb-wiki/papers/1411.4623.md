# The role of strangeness in hybrid stars and possible observables

> We study the effects of strangeness on the quark sector of a hybrid star equation of state. Since the model we use to describe quarks is the same as the one we use to describe hadrons, we can also study the effects of strangeness on the chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement phase transitions (first order or crossover). Finally, we analyze combined effects of hyperons and quarks on global properties of hybrid stars, like mass, radius and cooling profiles. It is found that a large amount of strangeness in the core is related to the generation of twin-star solutions, which can have the same mass as the lower or zero strangeness counterpart, but with smaller radii.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a large amount of strangeness in the core of hybrid stars is related to the generation of twin-star solutions, which can have the same mass as the lower or zero strangeness counterpart but with smaller radii; this result depends on the strength of the strange quark coupling to the vector meson, parameterized by the ratio xi = g_{q phi} / g_{q omega}.

## Method

The authors use a chiral SU(3) sigma model in the mean-field approximation that includes both hadrons (baryon octet) and quarks (u, d, s) as dynamical degrees of freedom, with an excluded volume for baryons to suppress them at high density. They vary the coupling strength of the strange quark to the phi meson (parameter xi) to control the stiffness of the equation of state and the nature of the phase transition (crossover or first order). The resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure and to compute thermal evolution via general relativistic heat transport and neutrino emission.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear saturation properties used for the model: density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, binding energy B/A = -15.65 MeV, nucleon effective mass M*_N = 0.67 M_N, compressibility K = 318.76 MeV, asymmetry energy E_sym = 32.43 MeV with slope L = 102.77 MeV.
* For xi = 3.62: maximum mass 2.31 M_sun with radius 12.58 km.
* For xi = 0: maximum mass 2.05 M_sun with radius 13.07 km.
* For xi = -1.29: maximum mass 1.97 M_sun with radius 13.61 km (normal branch); twin branch stars have radii from 9.60 to 10.22 km and maximum mass 1.68 M_sun.
* The density discontinuity across the first-order phase transition for xi = -1.29 is Delta rho_B = 0.18 fm^{-3} (energy density discontinuity Delta epsilon = 238.40 MeV/fm^3).
* For xi = -1.29, a star of mass 1.68 M_sun has central strangeness f_s = 0.01 in the normal branch and f_s = 1.68 in the twin branch.
* The ratio of the energy density jump to the transition energy density is Delta epsilon / epsilon_trans = 0.33; the ratio of transition pressure to transition energy density is P_trans / epsilon_trans = 0.16.

## Context

This work extends the study of hybrid stars with both hyperons and quarks, building on prior chiral model calculations by Dexheimer (2008, 2009) and others. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" discussed by Zdunik (2012) and the possibility of twin-star solutions previously explored by Glendenning (1998), Schertler (2000), and Alvarez-Castillo (2013). The results show that varying the strange quark coupling can produce either smooth crossovers or first-order phase transitions, and that twin-star configurations with enhanced strangeness could be observable through simultaneous mass and radius measurements, providing a test of first-order deconfinement at low temperature.
