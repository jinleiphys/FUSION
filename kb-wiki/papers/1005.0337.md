# Axial charges of octet and decuplet baryons

> We present a study of axial charges of baryon ground and resonant states with relativistic constituent quark models. In particular, the axial charges of octet and decuplet $N$, $Σ$, $Ξ$, $Δ$, $Σ^*$, and $Ξ^*$ baryons are considered. The theoretical predictions are compared to existing experimental data and results from other approaches, notably from lattice quantum chromodynamics and chiral perturbation theory. The relevance of axial charges with regard to $π$-dressing and spontaneous chiral-symmetry breaking is discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic constituent quark models (RCQMs) predict axial charges for octet and decuplet baryon ground and resonant states that agree well with available experimental data and lattice QCD results, but the authors note that the predicted nucleon axial charge of about 1.15 falls slightly below the experimental value of 1.2695, which may indicate missing pion-dressing effects in the models.

## Method

The authors calculated axial charges using three relativistic constituent quark models with different hyperfine interactions: one-gluon-exchange (OGE), pseudoscalar Goldstone-boson-exchange (psGBE), and an extended version including all force components from pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector exchanges (EGBE). The calculations were performed in Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics using the spectator model approximation, where the weak-interaction gauge boson couples to only one constituent quark in the baryon, with the axial current operator evaluated at zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon (N) ground state axial charge: 1.15 (EGBE and psGBE), 1.11 (OGE), compared to experimental value 1.2695 +/- 0.0029
- Sigma ground state axial charge: 0.65 (all three RCQMs), compared to lattice QCD result 0.636 +/- 0.068 (Lin and Orginos)
- Xi ground state axial charge: -0.21 (EGBE), -0.22 (psGBE and OGE), compared to lattice QCD result -0.277 +/- 0.034
- Delta ground state axial charge: -4.48 (EGBE), -4.47 (psGBE), -4.30 (OGE)
- N(1535) resonance axial charge: 0.02 (EGBE), 0.09 (psGBE), 0.13 (OGE)
- N(1650) resonance axial charge: 0.51 (EGBE), 0.46 (psGBE), 0.44 (OGE)
- Nonrelativistic limit of EGBE for nucleon: 1.65, deviating grossly from the relativistic result

## Context

This work extends previous RCQM studies of nucleon axial charges (Glozman 2001, Boffi 2001, Merten 2002) to a comprehensive set of octet and decuplet baryons including resonances, and provides the first predictions for Sigma* and Xi* axial charges. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations by Lin and Orginos (2007) and Erkol, Oka, and Takahashi (2009), as well as chiral perturbation theory results by Jiang and Tiburzi (2009). The findings contradict the argument by Glozman (2007, 2008) that axial charges should become small for nearly degenerate parity-partner resonances, as the RCQM predictions show no such trend even for high-lying states like N(1680) and N(1675). The work enables future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations for baryon resonances beyond the N(1535) and N(1650).
