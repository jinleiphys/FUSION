# An Extended Approximation for the Lowest-lying States in Odd-mass Nuclei

> An enhanced model, based on the Extended Boson Approximation, for the lowest-lying states in odd-mass nuclei is presented. Our approach is built on the Quasiparticle Phonon Model, extending it to take into account the ground state correlations due to the action of the Pauli principle more accurately than in the conventional theory. The derived interaction strengths between the quasiparticles and the phonons in this model depend on the quasiparticle occupation numbers explicitly coupling the odd-mass nucleus equations with those of the even-even core. Within this model we calculated the transition probabilities in several Te, Xe and Ba isotopes with A$\approx$130.

# An Extended Approximation for the Lowest-lying States in Odd-mass Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors present an enhanced model for odd-mass nuclei that extends the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPM) by incorporating ground state correlations from the Pauli principle more accurately than conventional theory, but the improvement is conditional: the calculated energies of the even-even core still remain higher than experimental values, and the model is limited to one-phonon configurations with only quadrupole-quadrupole interactions.

## Method

The authors start from the Extended Random Phase Approximation (ERPA) for even-even nuclei and extend it to odd-mass nuclei within the Quasiparticle Phonon Model framework. They derive renormalized quasiparticle-phonon interaction strengths that depend explicitly on quasiparticle occupation numbers, coupling the odd-mass nucleus equations with those of the even-even core through a nonlinear system of equations. The wave functions for odd-mass nuclei are constructed as mixed states of pure quasiparticle and quasiparticle-phonon configurations including backward-going amplitudes, and the resulting generalized eigenvalue problem is solved with a diagonal approximation for the Pauli correction terms.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference of particle occupation numbers between QPM and EQPM for the proton sub-shell $2d_{5/2}$ in $^{130}$Ba reaches up to 5%
- The contribution of higher-lying phonons to the backward matrix elements $W(Jj\lambda i)$ can be up to 25%
- The energy $\omega_{2_1^+}$ at which experimental spectroscopic factors are reproduced is lower in EQPM_BCK_P than in QPM_BCK_P by about 50 keV for $^{131}$Ba, and by 50-150 keV across the studied nuclei
- For $^{123}$Te, the experimental spectroscopic factor of 0.5 is reproduced at $\omega_{2_1^+}=1.4$ MeV (QPM_BCK_P) versus 1.3 MeV (EQPM_BCK_P)
- For $^{125}$Te, the experimental spectroscopic factor of 0.46 is reproduced at $\omega_{2_1^+}=1.5$ MeV (QPM_BCK_P) versus 1.3 MeV (EQPM_BCK_P)
- For $^{127}$Te, the experimental spectroscopic factor of 0.38 is reproduced at $\omega_{2_1^+}=1.5$ MeV (QPM_BCK_P) versus 1.35 MeV (EQPM_BCK_P)
- For $^{131}$Ba, the experimental spectroscopic factor of 0.25 is reproduced at $\omega_{2_1^+}=1.05$ MeV (QPM_BCK_P) versus 1.0 MeV (EQPM_BCK_P)
- The peak values of $B(E2|3/2_1^+ \rightarrow 1/2_1^+)$ in EQPM_BCK_P occur at $\omega_{2_1^+}$ values about 100 keV lower than in QPM_BCK_P
- The maximum transition probabilities in models with backward amplitudes are about three times larger than in the model without backward amplitudes

## Context

This work extends the earlier QPM studies by Sluys (1993) and Mishev (2008) that demonstrated the importance of backward amplitudes for spectroscopic factors, but which were based on the Quasi-Boson Approximation (QBA). The authors improve upon those results by incorporating the Extended Boson Approximation (EBA) developed by Hara and later applied to even-even nuclei by Karadjov, which accounts for Pauli principle effects more accurately. The approach differs from the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) introduced by Iachello (1979), where core excitations are treated as pure bosons and the core-particle interaction contains free parameters; in contrast, the QPM derives this interaction from the underlying nucleon dynamics. The model enables improved calculations of spectroscopic factors and transition probabilities in odd-A Te, Xe, and Ba isotopes near A=130, though the authors note that further weakening of the quasiparticle-phonon interaction through inclusion of higher multipolarities and multi-phonon configurations is needed to resolve remaining discrepancies with experiment.
