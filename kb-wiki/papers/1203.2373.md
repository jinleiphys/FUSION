# The JLab 12GeV Upgrade and the Initial Science Program

> An overview is presented of the upgrade of JLab's cw electron accelerator from a maximum beam energy of currently 6 GeV to 12 GeV. Construction of the 12 GeV upgrade project has started in 2008. A broad experimental program has been developed to map the nucleon's intrinsic correlated spin and momentum distribution through measurements of deeply exclusive and semi-inclusive processes, and to probe the quark and gluon confinement by studying the spectrum of mesons with exotic quantum numbers. Other programs include the forward parton distribution function at large xB, the quark and gluon polarized distribution functions, the measurements of electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon ground state and of nucleon resonance transitions at high Q2, and the exploration of physics beyond the Standard Model in high precision parity violating processes. The 12 GeV electron beam is also well suited to explore quark hadronization properties using the nucleus as a laboratory.

## Key claim

The JLab 12 GeV upgrade will enable a comprehensive program to map the nucleon's multi-dimensional structure through generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMDs), but the full extraction of these quantities requires combining measurements of multiple asymmetries on both proton and neutron targets, and the dominance of the handbag mechanism in meson production must first be established at the upgrade energy before those channels can be incorporated into global fits.

## Method

The paper describes the upgrade of the Jefferson Lab continuous wave electron accelerator from 6 GeV to 12 GeV by adding five accelerating cryo-modules with higher gradients to both linear accelerators, plus one arc and an additional pass through the north linac to reach 12 GeV for the new Hall D. The baseline experimental equipment under construction includes the CLAS12 large acceptance spectrometer in Hall B, the SHMS spectrometer in Hall C, a hermetic solenoid detector in Hall D for hybrid meson spectroscopy, and retention of the HRS2 pair in Hall A. The science program will measure deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production for GPD extraction, semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) for TMD studies, inclusive structure functions at large x, elastic and transition form factors at high Q^2, and parity-violating processes.

## Key numbers

- Maximum beam energy upgrade from 6 GeV to 12 GeV; construction started in 2008 with baseline equipment completion scheduled for fall 2014
- Beam spin asymmetry A_LU projected for DVCS at Q^2=2 GeV^2 and x=0.25 for transverse target spin asymmetry A_UT
- Projected measurement of F^n_2/F^p_2 and d/u ratio at 11 GeV beam energy using spectator proton detection down to 70 MeV/c
- Spin structure function A_1^p projected up to x less than or equal to 0.9 with W greater than 2 GeV constraint
- First moment Gamma_1^p measurable up to Q^2=6 GeV^2 with statistical accuracy improved several fold over existing world data
- Total point-to-point uncorrelated uncertainty on twist-4 term f_2^{p-n} for Bjorken sum decreases by factor of 5-6 compared to results in Deur (2004)
- Neutron magnetic form factor G_Mn measurements extended to higher Q^2 with CLAS12
- Nucleon resonance excitations accessible with Q^2 less than or equal to 12 GeV^2 at 12 GeV beam energy

## Context

This paper extends the GPD framework developed by Mueller (1994), Ji (1996), and Radyushkin (1996-1997) and the TMD formalism to higher energies and precision, building on first DVCS experiments at JLab and DESY (HERMES). The 12 GeV program enables spatial imaging of the nucleon through tomographic techniques, extraction of gravitational form factors from GPD moments, and discrimination between competing models for parton distributions at large x, including pQCD predictions and quark-hadron duality scenarios. The results will feed into global QCD analyses of polarized parton densities and enable extraction of higher-twist parameters representing quark correlations in the nucleon.
