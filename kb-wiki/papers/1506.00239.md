# Tsallis $p_\perp$ distribution from statistical clusters

> It is shown that the transverse momentum distributions of particles emerging from the decay of statistical clusters, distributed according to a power law in their transverse energy, closely resembles that following from the Tsallis non-extensive statistical model. The experimental data are well reproduced with the cluster temperature $T\approx 160$ MeV.

# Tsallis $p_\perp$ distribution from statistical clusters

## Key claim

The Tsallis-like transverse momentum distribution observed in high-energy collisions can be reproduced, to good accuracy, by a model where particles emerge from the decay of statistical clusters that follow standard Boltzmann statistics, provided the clusters' transverse Lorentz factors follow a power-law distribution. This suggests the experimental success of the Tsallis formula may be interpreted as confirmation of the standard statistical model rather than of non-extensive Tsallis statistics.

## Method

The author assumes that final-state hadrons are decay products of clusters that emit particles according to the Boltzmann distribution, with the cluster moving at some transverse velocity. The cluster transverse Lorentz factor $\gamma_\perp$ is taken to follow a power-law distribution $d\gamma_\perp \, \gamma_\perp^{-\kappa}$. The resulting single-particle transverse momentum distribution is computed numerically by integrating over the cluster velocity and rapidity distributions, and compared to the Tsallis distribution forms $D_1 = c \, m_\perp [1+(q-1)m_\perp/T_{ts}]^{q/(1-q)}$ and $D_2 = c [1+(q-1)m_\perp/T_{ts}]^{1/(1-q)}$.

## Key numbers

- Cluster temperature $T \approx 160$ MeV (from abstract); numerical calculations use $T = 155$ MeV.
- Power-law exponent for cluster Lorentz factor: $\kappa = 6.5$.
- Agreement between cluster-decay and Tsallis distributions holds from $p_\perp \approx 100$ MeV up to $p_\perp = 200$ GeV.
- For charged particles, the Tsallis parameters giving good agreement are $q-1 = 0.150$ and $T_{ts} = 76$ MeV, consistent with the analysis of Cleymans et al. (2013).
- The cluster temperature range explored is 100 to 180 MeV, and the power $\kappa$ range is 4 to 7.

## Context

This work extends the idea from Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2014) and Cleymans et al. (2014) that power-law tails in transverse momentum spectra can arise from fluctuations of Boltzmann spectrum parameters, here applied specifically to the statistical cluster model of Becattini (1996) and Bialas (1999). It offers an alternative to the non-extensive Tsallis interpretation of the same data, and suggests that measurements above 200 GeV may eventually distinguish the two approaches. The author notes that the scaling properties observed by McLerran and Praszalowicz (2010) could be investigated within this cluster framework.
