# Thermostatistics of q-deformed boson gas

> We show that a natural realization of the thermostatistics of q-bosons can be built on the formalism of q-calculus and that the entire structure of thermodynamics is preserved if we use an appropriate Jackson derivative in place of the ordinary thermodynamics derivative. This framework allows us to obtain a generalized q-boson entropy which depends on the q-basic number. We study the ideal q-boson gas in the thermodynamic limit which is shown to exhibit Bose-Einstein condensation with higher critical temperature and discontinuous specific heat.

## Key claim

The authors show that a fully consistent thermostatistics of q-deformed bosons can be constructed by replacing ordinary thermodynamic derivatives with Jackson derivatives, preserving the entire structure of thermodynamics. The abstract's claim that the framework "allows us to obtain a generalized q-boson entropy" is conditioned on the specific prescription that the Jackson derivative applies only to variables in exponential form (such as fugacity z or y_i = e^{-βϵ_i}), and that the ordinary chain rule must be used before applying the Jackson derivative.

## Method

The authors start from the q-deformed boson algebra (q-Heisenberg algebra) with creation/annihilation operators satisfying aa† − q a†a = 1, and construct the q-Fock space using q-basic numbers [n] = (q^n − 1)/(q − 1). They compute thermal averages in the grand canonical ensemble using the standard (undeformed) density matrix e^{−βH} with Hamiltonian H = Σ_i (ϵ_i − μ) N_i, then derive the mean occupation number from the relation [n_i]/[n_i + 1] = e^{−β(ϵ_i−μ)}. To maintain thermodynamic consistency, they replace ordinary derivatives with respect to exponential variables by the Jackson derivative D_z^{(q)} = ((q−1)/log q) ∂_z^{(q)}, where ∂_z^{(q)} f(z) = (f(qz)−f(z))/(z(q−1)), and apply this prescription to derive the entropy, internal energy, and all thermodynamic functions.

## Key numbers

- For q = 1.01, the critical temperature T_c^q increases by 18% relative to the standard boson critical temperature T_c.
- For q = 1.1, T_c^q increases by 75% relative to T_c.
- For q = 1.05, the critical temperature T_c^q is increased by 48% relative to the standard boson case.
- The classical limit of the specific heat per particle is (C_v/N)|_{cl} = (3/2) · (q−1)/log q.
- The undeformed function g_{3/2}(1) = 2.61 (value at z = 1 for standard bosons).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of q-deformed boson thermodynamics by Lee (1995), Su (1995), Tuszynski (1995), Song (1995), Narayana Swamy (1995), and others, which had not demonstrated that the full structure of thermodynamics is preserved. The authors establish a formal connection between q-deformed quantum groups and nonextensive Tsallis statistics (Tsallis, 1988) through the common language of q-calculus, and show that the q-deformed entropy remains deformed even in the classical limit, similar to Greenberg's infinite statistics (Greenberg, 1991). The results enable future investigation of q-fermion thermostatistics and suggest that experimental data on Bose-Einstein condensation in dilute rubidium atoms (Ensher, 1996) could be interpreted as evidence of q-deformation effects.
