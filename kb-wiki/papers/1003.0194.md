# Collision geometry fluctuations and triangular flow in heavy-ion collisions

> We introduce the concepts of participant triangularity and triangular flow in heavy-ion collisions, analogous to the definitions of participant eccentricity and elliptic flow. The participant triangularity characterizes the triangular anisotropy of the initial nuclear overlap geometry and arises from event-by-event fluctuations in the participant-nucleon collision points. In studies using a multi-phase transport model (AMPT), a triangular flow signal is observed that is proportional to the participant triangularity and corresponds to a large third Fourier coefficient in two-particle azimuthal correlation functions. Using two-particle azimuthal correlations at large pseudorapidity separations measured by the PHOBOS and STAR experiments, we show that this Fourier component is also present in data. Ratios of the second and third Fourier coefficients in data exhibit similar trends as a function of centrality and transverse momentum as in AMPT calculations. These findings suggest a significant contribution of triangular flow to the ridge and broad away-side features observed in data. Triangular flow provides a new handle on the initial collision geometry and collective expansion dynamics in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observed "ridge" and "broad away-side" features in two-particle azimuthal correlations in heavy-ion collisions arise from a triangular anisotropy in the initial collision geometry caused by event-by-event fluctuations, which then produces a triangular flow signal through collective expansion of the medium, rather than from non-flow effects such as jets or resonances.

## Method

The authors define participant triangularity (epsilon_3) and triangular flow (v_3) analogously to participant eccentricity and elliptic flow, using the positions of participating nucleons from Glauber Monte Carlo simulations. They analyze the AMPT (multi-phase transport model) to extract v_2 and v_3 as functions of eccentricity and triangularity, and compute the Fourier coefficients V_2 and V_3 of two-particle azimuthal correlation functions for particle pairs at large pseudorapidity separations (2 < |Delta_eta| < 4). They compare these model results to experimental correlation data from the PHOBOS and STAR collaborations at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- In the AMPT model for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, more than 80% of the third Fourier coefficient V_3 of azimuthal correlations can be accounted for by triangular flow with respect to the minor axis of triangularity.
- The average magnitude of triangularity is observed to be of the same order as eccentricity in Glauber Monte Carlo calculations for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The ratio of triangular flow to elliptic flow (mean v_3 / mean v_2) increases with centrality and transverse momentum in AMPT calculations for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- A linear relationship is observed between v_2 and eccentricity, and between v_3 and triangularity, in AMPT calculations for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV across bins of number of participating nucleons.

## Context

This work extends the concept of participant eccentricity introduced by Alver et al. (2007) to explain elliptic flow in Cu+Cu collisions, and the suggestion by Sorensen that initial geometry fluctuations could produce higher-order Fourier components. It provides a unified framework for understanding the ridge and broad away-side structures observed by PHOBOS and STAR, which had previously been attributed to non-flow correlations. The results enable future studies of initial-state fluctuations and collective expansion dynamics using triangular flow as a new observable, and suggest that hydrodynamic calculations should incorporate event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions rather than smooth overlap geometries.
