# Light hyperclusters and hyperons in low-density hot stellar matter

> The abundance of light nuclei and hyperons, that are produced in stellar environments such as supernova or binary mergers, is calculated within a relativistic mean-field model with density dependent couplings in low-density matter. Five light nuclei are considered, together with three light hyper-nuclei. We show that the presence of hyperons shifts the dissolution of clusters to larger densities, and increases the amount of clusters. This effect is larger the smaller the charge fraction, and the higher the temperature. The abundance of hyperons is also affected by the cluster formation: neutral and positively charged hyperons suffer a reduction, and the negatively charged ones an increase. We also observe that the dissolution of the less-abundant clusters occurs at larger densities due to smaller Pauli-blocking effects. Overall, hyper-nuclei set in at temperatures above 25 MeV, and depending on the temperature and chemical composition, they may be more abundant than $α$-particles, or even more abundant than other heavier clusters.

## Key claim
The presence of hyperons shifts the dissolution of light clusters to larger densities and increases the total amount of clusters, with this effect being larger for smaller charge fractions and higher temperatures; hypernuclei set in at temperatures above 25 MeV and may become more abundant than alpha-particles depending on temperature and chemical composition.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with density-dependent couplings (DD2) to calculate the abundances of five light nuclei (²H, ³H, ³He, ⁴He, ⁶He), three light hypernuclei (³_ΛH, ⁴_ΛH, ⁴_ΛHe), and the full baryonic octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰) in low-density stellar matter. The clusters were treated as point-like particles with medium-dependent binding energy shifts and a calibrated σ-meson coupling fraction (x_s = 0.93 ± 0.02) that reproduces INDRA experimental equilibrium constants. The system was solved under fixed charge fraction (Y_Q = 0.1 or 0.3) and temperature (T = 10–100 MeV) conditions relevant to supernova and binary neutron star merger environments.

## Key numbers
- The σ-meson coupling fraction for clusters was calibrated to x_s = 0.93 ± 0.02 for the DD2 RMF model, based on INDRA data (Custodio 2020).
- Vacuum masses: M(³_ΛH) = 2990.89 MeV, M(⁴_ΛH) = 3922.49 MeV, M(⁴_ΛHe) = 3921.70 MeV, with corresponding vacuum binding energies B⁰ = 2.793 MeV, 10.198 MeV, and 10.981 MeV respectively.
- Hyperon coupling ratios: R_σΛ = 0.621, R_σΣ = 0.474, R_σΞ = 0.320, with the Σ potential in symmetric nuclear matter fixed at U_Σ^(N)(n₀) = 30 MeV.
- The experimental production ratio ³_ΛH/³H = 0.75 ± 0.07 (She 2020) was used to validate the σ-hypercluster coupling definition.
- At T = 10 MeV and Y_Q = 0.1, ⁶He becomes more abundant than ³He; at T = 30 MeV, the deuteron is the most abundant cluster at all densities due to its smaller mass.
- At T = 100 MeV and Y_Q = 0.1, the fraction of ⁶He remains below 10⁻⁴.
- At density n_B = 0.1 fm⁻³ and Y_Q = 0.1, deuteron abundance surpasses proton abundance for 25 ≲ T ≲ 70 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Pais (2018, 2020) and Custodio (2020), who introduced medium-dependent binding energy shifts and calibrated σ-meson couplings for light clusters using NIMROD and INDRA experimental data. It builds on the RMF models with hyperons developed by Fortin (2017) and Marques (2017), which included the full baryonic octet but did not simultaneously treat light clusters and hypernuclei. The study also relates to the work of Sedrakian (2020), who considered light clusters and heavy baryons including hyperons and deltas, and to earlier nuclear statistical equilibrium models by Hempel (2010) and Raduta (2010). The results enable more accurate equations of state for core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger simulations, where temperatures up to 50–100 MeV and non-β-equilibrium conditions occur.
