# Microcanonical studies concerning the recent experimental evaluations of the nuclear caloric curve

> The microcanonical multifragmentation model from [Al. H. Raduta and Ad. R. Raduta, Phys. Rev. C 55, 1344 (1997); 56, 2059 (1997); 59, 323 (1999)] is refined and improved by taking into account the experimental discrete levels for fragments with $A \le 6$ and by including the stage of sequential decay of the primary excited fragments. The caloric curve is reevaluated and the heat capacity at constant volume curve is represented as a function of excitation energy and temperature. The sequence of equilibrated sources formed in the reactions studied by the ALADIN group ($^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at 600, 800 and 1000 MeV/nucleon bombarding energy) is deduced by fitting simultaneously the model predicted mean multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments ($M_{IMF}$) and charge asymmetry of the two largest fragments ($a_{12}$) versus bound charge ($Z_{bound}$) on the corresponding experimental data. Calculated HeLi isotopic temperature curves as a function of the bound charge are compared with the experimentally deduced ones.

# Microcanonical studies concerning the recent experimental evaluations of the nuclear caloric curve

## Key claim

The microcanonical multifragmentation model, when refined with experimental discrete levels for light fragments and sequential decay, reproduces the ALADIN HeLi isotopic temperature curves with excellent agreement across the entire bound-charge range, but only after accounting for a bombarding-energy-dependent nonequilibrium component in the experimentally measured excitation energies. The model predicts a smaller nonequilibrium energy contribution than the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM), bringing the equilibrated source sequence closer to the 600 MeV/nucleon experimental line.

## Method

The authors refined their sharp microcanonical multifragmentation model by replacing the level density formula with experimental discrete energy levels for fragments with A ≤ 6 (using only levels with width Γ ≤ 1 MeV) and by adding a factor exp(-ε/τ) with τ=9 to the level density for A > 6 fragments to account for the decrease of excited-state lifetimes. They then included a sequential decay stage using Weisskopf evaporation for A > 6 fragments and secondary break-up for 4 ≤ A ≤ 6 fragments when excitation energy exceeds B(A,Z)/3, simulating the full decay chain via Monte Carlo. The sequence of equilibrated sources for the ALADIN reactions (197Au+197Au at 600, 800, and 1000 MeV/nucleon) was deduced by simultaneously fitting the calculated mean intermediate mass fragment multiplicity ⟨M_IMF⟩ and charge asymmetry ⟨a_12⟩ as functions of bound charge Z_bound to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The constant volume heat capacity C_V(E*) shows a sharp maximum around 4.5 MeV/nucleon excitation energy for both freeze-out radii considered (R = 2.25 A^{1/3} and R = 2.50 A^{1/3} fm), indicating a phase transition in that region.
- The HeLi isotopic temperature is calculated using T_HeLi = 13.33 / ln[2.18 × (Y_6Li/Y_7Li) / (Y_3He/Y_4He)], with experimental predictions divided by f_T = 1.2 (the factor used by ALADIN to average QSM, GEMINI, and MMMC model predictions).
- The plateau-like region in the caloric curve is situated at around 5 MeV temperature, lasting from 3 to 10 MeV/nucleon excitation energy (referencing the original ALADIN result from Pochodzalla 1995).
- Corrections to neutron kinetic energies brought changes of about 10% for the 197Au+197Au reaction at 600 MeV/nucleon, and spectator energies grow by approximately 30% over the bombarding energy interval 600 to 1000 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own microcanonical multifragmentation model (Raduta and Raduta 1997, 1999) by adding experimental discrete levels and sequential decay, and directly addresses the discrepancies between the ALADIN group's original caloric curve (Pochodzalla 1995) and later results from EOS (Hauger 1996) and INDRA (Ma 1997). The model enables separation of equilibrium and nonequilibrium contributions to measured excitation energies, a problem highlighted by Trautmann (1998) and Xi (1997), and provides a benchmark for comparing with SMM calculations (Bondorf 1995) which predicted larger nonequilibrium energy components. The excellent agreement with ALADIN HeLi temperature data suggests the refined microcanonical approach can reliably identify the equilibrated source sequence, enabling future studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclear systems.
