# A Non-extensive Equilibrium analysis of $π^{+}$ $p_{T}$ Spectra at RHIC

> By analyzing the dynamical properties of particle production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, it is possible to characterize the final stage of the equilibration process occurring in the collision fireball. In this work, we use the Hagedorn model coupled with non-extensive statistics to evaluate the transverse momentum spectra of positive pions for various event centrality classes in Au+Au collisions at RHIC with center-of-mass energies of 62.4GeV/A and 200GeV/A. We find that, by assuming an energy distribution that incorporates particle correlations, it is possible to explain the entire $π^{+}$ $p_{T}$ spectrum as measured by RHIC. We find that spectra from central collisions, when compared to peripheral collisions, are consistent with a system that has smaller values of the non-extensivity parameter "q" and higher values of temperature. Comparison between different beam energies also shows a variation of the "q" parameter. The result is discussed using the interpretation that the "q" parameter is a measure of particle correlations within the system. Under this assumption, our results show that more central collisions are consistent with a system with less particle correlations.

# A Non-extensive Equilibrium analysis of π⁺ p₇ Spectra at RHIC

## Key claim
By applying the Hagedorn model with Tsallis non-extensive statistics, the entire transverse momentum spectrum of positive pions from RHIC can be described with a single function, but only under the assumption that the non-extensivity parameter q reflects the degree of particle correlations in the system. The claim that more central collisions have less particle correlations depends on interpreting q as a measure of correlation, with the dynamical model mapping higher q to higher correlation parameter r.

## Method
The authors fitted the Hagedorn gas model with Tsallis energy distribution (equation 3) to published STAR π⁺ transverse momentum spectra from Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV/A and 200 GeV/A across multiple centrality classes, as well as p+p data. They also constructed a discrete dynamical toy model with N=100 particles exchanging energy quanta, where a parameter r controls the probability of introducing two-particle correlations, and fitted the resulting energy distributions with the Tsallis distribution to establish the relationship between r and the q parameter.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies studied: 62.4 GeV/A and 200 GeV/A for Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- The dynamical model used N=100 particles with average energy per particle ⟨E⟩ = 180 (in units of energy quantum ΔE), requiring at least 10⁶ time steps to reach stationary distribution
- Event-by-event energy fluctuations in the dynamical model increased by roughly 20-25% when increasing the correlation parameter r
- The transverse momentum range where parameters converge ideally is roughly 4-5 GeV/c
- The Hagedorn+Tsallis model produced the best χ² values compared to other full-range candidate functions for all centrality bins at both beam energies

## Context
This work extends the Hagedorn model with Tsallis statistics previously applied to e⁺e⁻ → hadrons by Bediaga et al. to heavy ion collisions at RHIC. It contrasts with approaches using percolation of strings (Pajares) or temperature fluctuations (Wilk) to describe full-range p₇ spectra. The results enable interpretation of the q parameter as a measure of dynamical correlations, suggesting jet quenching in central collisions, and invite further studies of other particle species and comparison with blast wave models (Zhangbu).
