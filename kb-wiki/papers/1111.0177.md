# Flux Tube Model Signals for Baryon Correlations in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The flux tube model offers a pictorial description of what happens during the deconfinement phase transition in QCD. The 3-point vertices of a flux tube network lead to formation of baryons upon hadronisation. Therefore, correlations in the baryon number distribution at the last scattering surface are related to the preceding pattern of the flux tube vertices, and provide a signature of the nearby deconfinement phase transition. I discuss the nature of the expected signal, which should be observable in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The flux tube model predicts that baryon-antibaryon correlations at the kinetic freeze-out surface in heavy ion collisions can serve as a signature of the nearby deconfinement phase transition, even in the crossover region where no sharp transition exists, provided that the angular positions of (anti)baryons can be backtracked to the (anti)vertex pattern on the fireball surface at chemical freeze-out.

## Method

The author formulates the flux tube model on a cubic lattice with spacing \(a\), where flux tubes live on links and quarks/vertices live on sites, with variables taking values \(0,\pm1\). The grand canonical partition function is expressed by imposing Gauss's law via a Kronecker delta constraint at every site, which is then factorized using an integral representation, allowing the sum over flux, quark, and vertex variables to be carried out explicitly. The resulting partition function is mapped to an XY spin model with \(Z(N)\)-symmetric magnetic field terms, establishing a duality between the flux tube description and the Polyakov loop description of deconfinement.

## Key numbers

* The critical energy density for hadronization of the (quasi-)equilibrated QGP is \(\epsilon_{\rm cr} \simeq 1\ {\rm GeV/fm}^3\) and the critical temperature is \(T_{\rm cr} \simeq 175\ {\rm MeV}\).
* Chemical freeze-out occurs at \(T_{\rm chem} \simeq 170\ {\rm MeV}\), and kinetic freeze-out at \(T_{\rm kin} \simeq 120\ {\rm MeV}\).
* For \(N_f=3\) QCD at small chemical potentials, the first-order deconfinement phase transition ends in a critical line around \(m = 1.5\ {\rm GeV}\) (the quark mass value at which the transition terminates).

## Context

This work extends the flux tube model developed by Patel (2011) to predict baryon number correlations in heavy ion collisions, drawing an analogy to cosmic microwave background radiation analysis as described by Weinberg (2008). The approach contrasts with thermal hadron resonance gas models, which fit single-particle multiplicities but miss multi-particle correlations, and with combined Polyakov loop–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models (Ogilvie, Fukushima, Ratti) that treat the Polyakov loop only as a mean field without full dynamics. The predicted correlations enable a new experimental observable for deconfinement dynamics at RHIC and LHC, complementing existing signals such as direct photons, leptons, and elliptic flow.
