# Q-PYTHIA: a medium-modified implementation of final state radiation

> We present a Monte Carlo implementation, within PYTHIA, of medium-induced gluon radiation in the final state branching process. Medium effects are introduced through an additive term in the splitting functions computed in the multiple-soft scattering approximation. The observable effects of this modification are studied for different quantities as fragmentation functions and the hump-backed plateau, and transverse momentum and angular distributions. The anticipated increase of intra-jet multiplicities, energy loss of the leading particle and jet broadening are observed as well as modifications of naive expectations based solely on analytical calculations. This shows the adequacy of a Monte Carlo simulator for jet analyses. Effects of hadronization are found to wash out medium effects in the soft region, while the main features remain. To show the performance of the implementation and the feasibility of our approach in realistic experimental situations we provide some examples: fragmentation functions, nuclear suppression factors, jet shapes and jet multiplicities. The package containing the modified routines is available for public use. This code, which is not an official PYTHIA release, is called Q-PYTHIA. We also include a short manual to perform the simulations of jet quenching.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Monte Carlo implementation of medium-induced gluon radiation in final-state parton showers, where medium effects are introduced as an additive term to the vacuum splitting functions within the multiple-soft scattering (BDMPS) approximation; however, the authors explicitly note that a formal proof of the factorization of vacuum and medium contributions into separate splitting probabilities and Sudakov form factors is still missing, and that the probabilistic interpretation of radiation in a medium requires phenomenological assumptions.

## Method

The authors modified the t-ordered final-state radiation routine PYSHOW in PYTHIA v6.4.18 by replacing the approximate vacuum splitting functions with exact leading-order vacuum splitting functions computed numerically, then adding a medium-induced correction term ΔP(z,t,hat{q},L,E) derived from the BDMPS gluon radiation spectrum. The implementation includes a hybrid treatment of the interplay between virtuality ordering and the finite medium size, where the formation length of each emitted gluon is computed and subtracted from the remaining medium length for subsequent branchings, and energy degradation is tracked by using the parent parton's current energy at each splitting. Hadronization was performed using standard PYTHIA routines after the medium-modified parton shower.

## Key numbers

- For a gluon jet of initial energy E_jet = 100 GeV, the medium-modified shower was studied with medium lengths L = 2 fm and 5 fm, and transport coefficients hat{q} = 0, 1, and 10 GeV^2/fm.
- The lower virtuality cutoff for the parton shower was set to t_0 = 1 GeV^2.
- For the nuclear suppression factor R_AA calculation, 10^6 pp events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV were generated with a minimum p_T^min = 8 GeV requirement in PYTHIA.
- Using the PQM model with k = 6 x 10^6 fm (corresponding to average <hat{q}> = 14 GeV^2/fm), the suppression factor was of order 5 at p_T > 5 GeV for 0-10% central PbPb collisions.
- For jet shapes at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV with anti-k_T algorithm (R^j = 1.5), approximately 80% of the initial jet transverse energy was contained within a subjet of radius R^sj = 0.3 in vacuum, while approximately 60% was contained within the same subjet radius for <hat{q}> = 14 GeV^2/fm.

## Context

This work extends the analytical BDMPS formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation (Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigné-Schiff, 1997) into a full Monte Carlo implementation with energy-momentum conservation, building on the medium-modified splitting function approach of Armesto, Salgado, and Wiedemann (2008). It enables the study of jet observables beyond single-inclusive measurements, such as jet shapes, jet multiplicities, and multiparticle intra-jet correlations, which are essential for interpreting heavy-ion data from RHIC and the LHC. The code Q-PYTHIA is publicly available and provides a tool for experimental collaborations to incorporate medium effects in their jet energy calibration and background subtraction procedures, complementing other Monte Carlo implementations of radiative energy loss.
