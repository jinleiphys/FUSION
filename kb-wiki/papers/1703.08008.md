# Dynamical evolution of critical fluctuations and its observation in heavy ion collisions

> We study time evolution of critical fluctuations of conserved charges near the QCD critical point in the context of relativistic heavy ion collisions. A stochastic diffusion equation is employed in order to describe the diffusion property of the critical fluctuation arising from the coupling of the order parameter field to conserved charges. We show that the diffusion property gives rise to a possibility of probing the early time fluctuations through the rapidity window dependence of the second-order cumulant and correlation function of conserved charges. It is pointed out that their non-monotonic behaviors as functions of the rapidity interval are robust experimental signals for the existence of the critical enhancement around the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The paper shows that non-monotonic behavior of the second-order cumulant of conserved charges as a function of rapidity window width, or of the correlation function as a function of rapidity separation, is a robust experimental signal for the existence of critical enhancement around the QCD critical point, provided the system's susceptibility has a local maximum as a function of proper time.

## Method

The authors employ a stochastic diffusion equation (SDE) in Milne coordinates to describe the time evolution of conserved charge density fluctuations in a boost-invariant Bjorken expansion model. They derive formal analytic solutions for the second-order cumulant and correlation function, then numerically evaluate these observables using a phenomenological parametrization where the susceptibility and diffusion coefficient are decomposed into singular and regular parts, with the singular part modeled using the 3D Ising universality class (model H) and a linear mapping between Ising variables and QCD temperature.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 220 MeV at proper time τ0 = 1.0 fm; critical temperature Tc = 160 MeV; kinetic freeze-out temperature Tf = 100 MeV
- Sound velocity cs^2 = 0.15 used in the Bjorken expansion relation T(τ) = T0 (τ0/τ)^(cs^2)
- Regular diffusion coefficients: D_C^Q = 2.0 fm (quark-gluon plasma) and D_C^H = 0.6 fm (hadronic)
- Susceptibility ratio χ^Q / χ^H ≈ 0.5 between high and low temperature limits
- Critical exponents β = 1/3 and δ = 5 for the 3D Ising universality class; dynamic exponents χ_η ≈ 0.04 and χ_λ ≈ 0.916 for model H
- Parameter ΔT/ΔH = 10 MeV controls the width of the critical region; crossover width δT = 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of critical slowing down in heavy ion collisions by Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1999), Nonaka and Asakawa (2004), and Mukherjee, Venugopalan, and Yin (2015), which analyzed the uniform sigma mode, by instead treating the critical mode as a diffusion mode consistent with conservation laws as established by Fujii (2003, 2004), Son and Stephanov (2004), and Minami and Kunihiro (2011). The paper enables experimental searches for the QCD critical point through rapidity window dependence of fluctuation observables in the Beam-Energy Scan program at RHIC and future programs at J-PARC, FAIR, and NICA.
