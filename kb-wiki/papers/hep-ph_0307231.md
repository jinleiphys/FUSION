# Deep Electroproduction of Photons and Mesons on the Deuteron

> We study deeply virtual Compton scattering and deep exclusive meson electroproduction on a deuteron target. We model the Generalized Quark Distributions in the deuteron by using the impulse approximation for the lowest Fock-space state on the light-cone. We study the properties of the resulting GPDs, and verify that sum rules violations are quite small in the impulse approximation. Numerical predictions are given for the unpolarized cross sections and polarization asymmetries for the kinematical regimes relevant for JLab experiments and for HERMES at HERA. We conclude that the signal of coherent scattering on the deuteron is comparable to the one on the proton at least for low momentum transfer, providing support to the feasibility of the experiments. The short distance structure of the deuteron may thus be scrutinized in the near future.

## Key claim

The authors model deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deep exclusive meson electroproduction (DEMP) on a deuteron target using the impulse approximation for the lowest Fock-space state on the light-cone, and find that the coherent scattering signal on the deuteron is comparable to that on the proton at low momentum transfer, supporting experimental feasibility, but only for Bjorken x values not smaller than 0.1, and with the caveat that the impulse approximation becomes too rough for large skewness (ξ), where higher Fock-space components would become dominant.

## Method

The authors constructed Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) for the deuteron by evaluating helicity amplitudes in the impulse approximation, using a convolution of the deuteron light-cone wave function (Paris potential with S-wave and 5.8% D-wave component) with isoscalar nucleon GPDs. The nucleon GPDs were modeled using a factorized ansatz for the t-dependence (dipole form factors) and double distributions based on MRST 2001 NLO unpolarized parton densities and LSS01 polarized densities, with the helicity-flip nucleon GPD E^IS included but found negligible. The resulting deuteron GPDs were then used to compute unpolarized cross sections and polarization asymmetries for kinematics relevant to JLab and HERMES experiments.

## Key numbers

- The D-wave component of the deuteron wave function has a probability of 5.8%.
- For the axial form factor, the dipole mass parameter M_A = 1.06 GeV was used.
- The GPDs were evaluated at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, ξ = 0.1, and t = -0.25 GeV^2.
- The longitudinal momentum distribution of the nucleon in the deuteron is strongly peaked at α = 0.5, with a width of the order of the binding energy divided by the nucleon mass.
- For Bjorken x > 0.1, the difference between the initial and final longitudinal momentum fractions of the active nucleon exceeds the width of the momentum distribution, causing cross sections to decrease very fast with x_Bj.
- The minimal momentum transfer for a given ξ is t_0 = -4 ξ^2 M^2 / (1 - ξ^2), and for a given t, the upper bound on ξ is ξ^2 ≤ -t / (4 M^2 - t).
- Sum rule violations in the impulse approximation are quite small, with the integrated GPDs I_i(ξ) showing only mild ξ-dependence, indicating the deuteron is essentially a two-nucleon state in the studied kinematic regime.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Generalized Parton Distributions, previously developed for the nucleon (Guichon, 1998) and for spin-1 targets (Berger, 2001), to the deuteron as the simplest nuclear system. It provides numerical predictions for coherent DVCS and DEMP on the deuteron, complementing existing deep inelastic scattering studies of nuclear partonic structure. The results enable experimental scrutiny of the short-distance structure of the deuteron at facilities like JLab and HERMES, and highlight the need for a deuteron recoil detector to distinguish coherent from incoherent scattering. The authors note that at larger skewness, contributions from higher Fock-space components (such as N anti-N pairs) not included in the impulse approximation would become important, as previously discussed by Frankfurt (1979, 1993) and Brodsky (2001).
