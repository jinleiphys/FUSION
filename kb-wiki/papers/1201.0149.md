# The $DN$, $πΣ_c$ interaction in finite volume and the $Λ_c(2595)$ resonance

> In this work the interaction of the coupled channels $DN$ and $πΣ_c$ in an SU(4) extrapolation of the chiral unitary theory, where the $Λ_c(2595)$ resonance appears as dynamically generated from that interaction, is extended to produce results in finite volume. Energy levels in the finite box are evaluated and, assuming that they would correspond to lattice results, the inverse problem of determining the phase shifts in the infinite volume from the lattice results is solved. We observe that it is possible to obtain accurate $πΣ_c$ phase shifts and the position of the $Λ_c(2595)$ resonance, but it requires the explicit consideration of the two coupled channels. We also observe that some of the energy levels in the box are attached to the closed $DN$ channel, such that their use to induce the $πΣ_c$ phase shifts via Lüscher's formula leads to incorrect results.

# The $DN$, $\pi\Sigma_c$ interaction in finite volume and the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance

## Key claim

The $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance can be dynamically generated from the coupled $DN$ and $\pi\Sigma_c$ channels in an SU(4) extrapolation of chiral unitary theory, and its properties can be extracted from finite-volume lattice energy levels, but only if both coupled channels are explicitly considered; using only the open $\pi\Sigma_c$ channel with Lüscher's formula yields incorrect phase shifts because some low-lying levels are tied to the closed $DN$ channel.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(4) chiral unitary approach for the coupled $DN$ and $\pi\Sigma_c$ channels to finite volume by replacing the continuous loop function $G$ with a discrete sum $\tilde{G}$ over momenta $\vec{q} = 2\pi\vec{n}/L$ in a cubic box of side $L$. They generate synthetic lattice energy levels by solving $\det(1-V\tilde{G})=0$ for various $L$, then solve the inverse problem by fitting these levels with a parameterized potential $V_{ij}=a_{ij}+b_{ij}(s-(m_\pi+M_{\Sigma_c})^2)$ and using the fitted potential in the infinite-volume Bethe-Salpeter equation to extract phase shifts and the resonance pole.

## Key numbers

* The $\Lambda_c(2595)$ resonance pole is found at $\sqrt{s_R} = (2596.3 - i1.6)$ MeV from the two-channel unitary approach.
* The squared couplings at the pole are $g^2_{\pi\Sigma_c} = 0.65 - i0.10$ and $g^2_{DN} = 43.3 + i3.6$.
* The compositeness sum rule gives $g^2_{DN} \frac{dG^D_{DN}}{dE} = -0.91 - i0.08$ at the pole, indicating about 91% of the state is a $DN$ bound state.
* The width extracted from the two-channel inverse analysis is $\Gamma = 3.3 \pm 1.9$ MeV.
* The second energy level in the box is nearly constant around 2600 MeV across box sizes $L$ from $1.7$ to $3.3$ $m_\pi^{-1}$.
* The fit to synthetic data with $\pm 10$ MeV errors using the two-channel potential yields a minimum $\chi^2$ of $2.5 \times 10^{-2}$.
* The CDD pole test gives $Z = 0.15 \pm 0.07$ when fitting the two-channel levels with a CDD potential, compared to $Z=0.96$ for a genuine state.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume coupled-channel formalism of Döring et al. (2011) and the Jülich approach (M. Döring et al., 2011) to the charm sector, specifically the $DN$ and $\pi\Sigma_c$ system where the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ was previously studied in infinite volume by Mizutani and Tomaradze (2006), Tolos et al. (2008), and Garcia-Recio et al. (2009). It demonstrates a novel pitfall: levels tied to a closed channel can mislead single-channel Lüscher analyses, as also noted for the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ by Martinez Torres et al. (2011). The results provide a roadmap for future lattice QCD calculations of the $\Lambda_c(2595)$ and show that the level spacing can distinguish between a dynamically generated state and a genuine (CDD pole) state.
