# Study of corrections to the eikonal approximation

> For the last decades, multiple international facilities have developed Radioactive-Ion Beams (RIB) to measure reaction processes including exotic nuclei. These measurements coupled with an accurate theoretical model of the reaction enable us to infer information about the structure of these nuclei. The partial-wave expansion provides a precise description of two-body collisions but has a large computational cost. To cope with this issue, the eikonal approximation is a powerful tool as it reduces the computational time and still describes the quantum effects observed in reaction observables. However, its range of validity is restricted to high energy and to forward scattering angles. In this work, we analyse the extension of the eikonal approximation to lower energies and larger angles through the implementation of two corrections. These aim to improve the treatment of the nuclear and Coulomb interactions within the eikonal model. The first correction is based on an expansion of the $T$-matrix while the second relies on a semi-classical approach. They permit to better account for the deflection of the projectile by the target, which is neglected in the standard eikonal model. The gain in accuracy of each correction is evaluated through the analyses of angular cross sections computed with the standard eikonal model, its corrections and the partial-wave expansion. These analyses have been performed for tightly bound projectiles ($^{10}\mathrm{Be}$) from intermediate energies (67 MeV/nucleon) down to energies of interest of future RIB facilities such as HIE-ISOLDE and ReA12 at MSU (10 MeV/nucleon).

## Key claim

The eikonal approximation can be extended to lower energies (down to 10 MeV/nucleon) and larger scattering angles by implementing two corrections that account for projectile deflection by the target, but both corrections fail to reproduce the absorption at large angles, leaving cross sections overestimated there.

## Method

The authors computed elastic scattering angular cross sections for the two-body collision of ¹⁰Be off ¹²C using the standard eikonal model, two corrected eikonal models, and a fully converged partial-wave expansion (taken as exact). The first correction (Wallace) is derived from a perturbation expansion of the T-matrix around the eikonal propagator, improving the treatment of the nuclear interaction; the second correction is a semi-classical approach that replaces the impact parameter in the eikonal phase by the distance of closest approach from the classical trajectory, applied either to the Coulomb interaction alone or to both Coulomb and nuclear interactions.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering of ¹⁰Be off ¹²C computed at 67 MeV/nucleon (high energy) and 10 MeV/nucleon (low energy).
- Wallace's correction yields nearly exact results at 67 MeV/nucleon but shows discrepancies at 10 MeV/nucleon.
- At 10 MeV/nucleon, the semi-classical Coulomb correction combined with Wallace's correction cancels the forward-angle shift induced by Wallace's correction alone.
- The semi-classical correction applied to both Coulomb and nuclear interactions shows a small improvement at forward angles below 20° at 10 MeV/nucleon, but worsens accuracy at 67 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Wallace's correction (Wallace, various years) and semi-classical corrections (Aguiar, Zardi, and Vitturi 1997; Lenzi, Vitturi, and Zardi 1995; Fukui, Ogata, and Capel 2014) from Coulomb-dominated collisions to nuclear-dominated reactions relevant for light halo nuclei. The analysis is a first step toward generalizing these corrections to three- and four-body collisions, which are needed to describe reactions of exotic nuclei at future RIB facilities such as HIE-ISOLDE and ReA12 at MSU (targeting 10 MeV/nucleon). The authors propose that using a complex distance of closest approach computed from the full optical potential could improve the absorption at large angles.
