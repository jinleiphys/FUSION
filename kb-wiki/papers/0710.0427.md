# Effective Interactions In Neutron-Rich Matter

> Properties of effective interactions in neutron-rich matter are reflected in the medium's equation of state (EOS), which is a relationship among several state variables. Spin and isospin asymmetries play an important role in the energy balance and could alter the stability conditions of the nuclear EOS. The EOS has far-reaching consequences for numerous nuclear processes in both the terrestrial laboratories and the cosmos. Presently the EOS, especially for neutron-rich matter, is still very uncertain. Heavy-ion reactions provide a unique means to constrain the EOS, particularly the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. On the other hand, microscopic, self-consistent, and parameter-free approaches are ultimately needed for understanding nuclear properties in terms of the fundamental interactions among the basic constituents of nuclear systems. In this talk, after a brief review of our recent studies on spin-polarized neutron matter, we discuss constraining the changing rate of the gravitational constant $G$ and properties of (rapidly) rotating neutron stars by using a nuclear EOS partially constrained by the latest terrestrial nuclear laboratory data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, partially constrained by terrestrial heavy-ion reaction data, can be used to set an upper limit on the time variation of the gravitational constant G and to constrain the mass of rapidly rotating neutron stars, but the authors note that the equation of state for neutron-rich matter remains very uncertain and that no ferromagnetic transition is observed in spin-polarized neutron matter up to 10 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors apply the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach with the Bonn B one-boson-exchange potential to compute the equation of state for spin-polarized neutron matter up to 10 times normal nuclear density. They then use the gravitochemical heating formalism of Jofre, Reisenegger, and Fernandez (2006) with several nucleonic equations of state, including the MDI interaction with symmetry energy constrained by isospin diffusion data and neutron skin measurements, to compute stationary surface temperatures of neutron stars and derive limits on the time variation of G. Additionally, they construct two-dimensional stationary configurations of rapidly rotating neutron stars using the RNS code to constrain the mass of the pulsar in XTE J1739-285.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on the relative time variation of the gravitational constant: |dot-G/G| in the range (4.5-21) x 10^{-12} yr^{-1}, obtained using the gravitochemical heating formalism with symmetry energy constrained by terrestrial nuclear data.
- Maximum gravitational mass for rapidly rotating neutron stars at the Kepler frequency: 2.25 solar masses for the MDI(x=0) equation of state, 2.30 solar masses for MDI(x=-1), 2.61 solar masses for APR, and 2.69 solar masses for DBHF+Bonn B.
- Kepler (mass-shedding) frequencies for maximum-mass models: 1742 Hz for MDI(x=0), 1512 Hz for MDI(x=-1), 1963 Hz for APR, and 1685 Hz for DBHF+Bonn B.
- Mass of the neutron star in XTE J1739-285 constrained between 1.7 and 2.1 solar masses, based on models rotating at 1122 Hz with equations of state consistent with terrestrial nuclear data.
- The DBHF calculation of spin-polarized neutron matter extends to densities as high as 10 times rho_0 (with rho_0 approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}), and no ferromagnetic transition is observed up to that density.

## Context

This work extends the gravitochemical heating method introduced by Jofre, Reisenegger, and Fernandez (2006) by incorporating equations of state whose symmetry energy is partially constrained by recent isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions and neutron skin measurements in lead-208, as reported by Shi (2003), Tsang (2004), Chen (2004), Steiner (2005), and Li (2005). The study also builds on the DBHF calculations of Alonso (2003), Sammarruca (2004, 2006), and Krastev (2006) for isospin asymmetric matter and neutron star properties. The results enable more reliable constraints on the time variation of G and on the mass of rapidly rotating neutron stars like XTE J1739-285, while the spin-polarized neutron matter analysis suggests that ferromagnetic instabilities may only occur at densities beyond those considered, possibly requiring inclusion of quark degrees of freedom.
