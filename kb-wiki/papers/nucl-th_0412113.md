# Magnetic moment of the Delta(1232)-resonance in chiral effective field theory

> We perform a relativistic chiral effective field theory calculation of the radiative pion photoproduction ($γp \to π^0 p γ'$) in the $Δ$-resonance region, to next-to-leading order in the ``delta-expansion''. This work is aimed at a model-independent extraction of the $Δ^+$ magnetic moment from new precise measurements of this reaction. It also predicts the chiral behavior of $Δ$'s magnetic moment, which can be used to extrapolate the recent lattice QCD results to the physical point.

## Key claim

A relativistic chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) calculation of radiative pion photoproduction (gamma p -> pi0 p gamma') in the Delta-resonance region, performed to next-to-leading order in the delta-expansion, can extract the Delta+ magnetic moment from experimental data with only one free parameter (mu_Delta+), provided the outgoing photon energy is of order m_pi while the incident photon energy is near the Delta resonance.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic chiral effective Lagrangian including pion, nucleon, and explicit Delta(1232) fields, organized according to the delta-expansion scheme where the Delta-nucleon mass splitting (Delta ~ 293 MeV) counts as one power of the small parameter delta and the pion mass counts as two powers. They compute the gamma p -> pi0 p gamma' amplitude at next-to-leading order from diagrams involving Delta propagators with one-loop self-energy corrections, using dimensional regularization and on-mass-shell renormalization. The calculation maintains exact electromagnetic gauge invariance and determines the imaginary part of the gamma-Delta-Delta vertex from the Delta width, leaving only the real part of the Delta magnetic moment as a free parameter to be extracted from experimental observables.

## Key numbers

- Delta-nucleon mass splitting: Delta = M_Delta - M_N = 293 MeV, treated as a light scale compared to Lambda ~ 1 GeV
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Transition coupling constants: g_M = 2.94, g_E = -0.96
- Delta width: Gamma_Delta = 115 MeV, which fixes the pi-N-Delta coupling h_A = 2.85
- The ratio R = (1/sigma_pi) * E'_gamma * dsigma/dE'_gamma approaches 1 in the soft-photon limit (E'_gamma -> 0), confirming the low-energy theorem
- The Delta magnetic moment develops an imaginary part when m_pi < Delta = M_Delta - M, with a pronounced cusp in the real part at m_pi = Delta

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1989) by including the Delta resonance explicitly via the delta-expansion scheme developed by Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003). It provides a model-independent alternative to the phenomenological model of Drechsel (2001) and Chiang (2004) used in the first experimental extraction of mu_Delta+ by Kotulla (2002), which gave mu_Delta+ = 2.7 (+1.0/-1.3) (stat.) +/- 1.5 (syst.) +/- 3 (theor.) nuclear magnetons. The calculation enables chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD results for the Delta magnetic moment from larger pion masses to the physical point, improving upon the heavy-baryon result of Butler (1993) by using a manifestly relativistic framework. The dedicated second-generation experiments by the Crystal Ball Collaboration at MAMI, with approximately two orders of magnitude better statistics than the pioneering experiment, will allow extraction of mu_Delta+ using this EFT calculation.
