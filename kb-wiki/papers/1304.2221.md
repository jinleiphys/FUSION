# Heavy quark pair production in high energy pA collisions: Quarkonium

> Quarkonium production in high-energy proton (deuteron)-nucleus collisions is investigated in the color glass condensate framework. We employ the color evaporation model assuming that the quark pair produced from dense small-x gluons in the nuclear target bounds into a quarkonium outside the target. The unintegrated gluon distribution at small Bjorken x in the nuclear target is treated with the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling corrections. For the gluons in the proton, we examine two possible descriptions, unintegrated gluon distribution and ordinary collinear gluon distribution. We present the transverse momentum spectrum and nuclear modification factor for J/psi production at RHIC and LHC energies, and those for Upsilon(1S) at LHC energy, and discuss the nuclear modification factor and the momentum broadening by changing the rapidity and the initial saturation scale.

## Key claim

The paper investigates quarkonium production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions within the color glass condensate framework, using the color evaporation model with the condition that the quark pair produced from dense small-x gluons in the nuclear target binds into a quarkonium only after leaving the target nucleus.

## Method

The authors compute the quark-pair production cross section using the CGC formalism for dilute-dense (proton-nucleus) collisions at leading order, treating the nuclear target's unintegrated gluon distribution with the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation including running coupling corrections (rcBK equation). For the proton side, they examine two descriptions: a full uGD treatment and a collinear approximation using CTEQ6LO parton distribution functions. The quarkonium production cross section is then obtained by integrating the pair cross section over the invariant mass range from twice the charm quark mass to the open-charm threshold, multiplied by a phenomenological nonperturbative transition factor.

## Key numbers

- J/psi production at RHIC energy sqrt(s)=200 GeV: transverse momentum spectrum shown for rapidity ranges |y|<0.35 and 1.2<y<2.2, with charm quark mass varied between m_c=1.2 GeV and 1.5 GeV
- J/psi production at LHC energy sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV: transverse momentum spectrum and nuclear modification factor presented
- Upsilon(1S) production at LHC energy: transverse momentum spectrum and nuclear modification factor shown
- Initial saturation scale for the proton at x_0=0.01: Q_{s0,p}^2=0.1597 GeV^2 for the g1118 parameter set, with gamma=1.118
- Nuclear saturation scale at x_0=0.01: Q_{s0,A}^2 = (4-6) x Q_{s0,p}^2 for a heavy nucleus with A~200
- Lambda_QCD=0.241 GeV fixed in the rcBK evolution
- Charm quark mass values used: m_c=1.2 GeV and 1.5 GeV
- D-meson mass M_D=1.864 GeV for J/psi threshold; B-meson mass M_B=5.280 GeV for Upsilon threshold
- Phenomenological transition factor F_J/psi=0.02 used as representative value
- Proton radius R_p=0.9 fm used for absolute normalization

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark production in pA collisions by Fujii, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2006-2007) by incorporating the full rapidity evolution of the nuclear unintegrated gluon distribution via the rcBK equation, which was previously constrained by global fits to HERA e+p data by Albacete et al. (2009-2010). The results provide a benchmark for separating cold nuclear matter effects from hot medium modifications in quarkonium production in AA collisions, and enable quantitative comparison with J/psi data from RHIC and LHC experiments to constrain the saturation scale in heavy nuclei.
