# Helicity separation in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the P-odd effects related to the vorticity of the medium formed in noncentral heavy ion collisions. Using the kinetic Quark-Gluon Strings Model we perform the numerical simulations of the vorticity and hydrodynamical helicity for the various atomic numbers, energies and centralities. We observed the vortical structures typically occupying the relatively small fraction of the fireball volume. In the course of numerical simulations the noticeable hydrodanamical helicity was observed manifesting the specific mirror behaviour with respect to the reaction plane. The effect is maximal at the NICA and FAIR energy range.

## Key claim

Using the kinetic Quark-Gluon Strings Model, the authors observe that the hydrodynamical helicity in noncentral heavy-ion collisions exhibits a mirror behavior with respect to the reaction plane, with the effect being maximal at the NICA and FAIR energy range; the abstract glosses over the condition that the helicity is zero when calculated over all cells and only becomes nonzero when selecting cells with a definite sign of velocity components orthogonal to the reaction plane.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of noncentral heavy-ion collisions using the Quark-Gluon Strings Model (QGSM), a kinetic model based on the 1/N_c expansion of binary process amplitudes and Regge theory. The coordinate space was divided into 50×50×100 cells of volume dx dy dz with dx=dy=0.6 fm and dz=0.6/γ fm, and velocity fields were computed from the momentum and energy of particles in each cell averaged over about 10000 collisions with identical initial conditions. Vorticity was calculated using discrete partial derivatives, and the hydrodynamical helicity was computed as the volume integral of the scalar product of velocity and vorticity.

## Key numbers

- Vorticity is concentrated in a relatively thin layer of 2–3 fm at the boundary of the participant region for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 5 GeV with impact parameter b = 8 fm.
- The coordinate space grid used 50×50×100 cells with cell dimensions dx=dy=0.6 fm and dz=0.6/γ fm, covering 25 time moments over an interval of 10 fm/c.
- The simulations averaged results over about 10000 heavy ion collisions with identical initial conditions.
- The helicity separation effect shows saturation in time and grows with impact parameter for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 5 GeV.
- The maximal value of helicity is achieved around the NICA energy range (approximately √s_NN = 5–11 GeV based on the energy dependence shown).

## Context

This work extends the study of P-odd effects in heavy-ion collisions from the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Vortical Effect (CVE) proposed by Kharzeev (2007) to the specific phenomenon of hydrodynamical helicity separation. The results are consistent with calculations in the hybrid UrQMD model by Steinheimer (2013), and the vorticity localization at the participant-spectator boundary is interpreted in the spirit of core-corona type models by Aichelin (2012) and St. & Bl. (2012). The findings enable further investigation of hyperon polarization and the relativistic generalization of Beltrami flows, as noted by Sorin and Teryaev (2013).
