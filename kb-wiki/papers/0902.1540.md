# Five Easy Pieces: The Dynamics of Quarks in Strongly Coupled Plasmas

> We revisit the analysis of the drag a massive quark experiences and the wake it creates at a temperature T while moving through a plasma using a gravity dual that captures the renormalisation group runnings in the dual gauge theory. Our gravity dual has a black hole and seven branes embedded via Ouyang embedding, but the geometry is a deformation of the usual conifold metric. In particular the gravity dual has squashed two spheres, and a small resolution at the IR. Using this background we show that the drag of a massive quark receives corrections that are proportional to powers of log T when compared with the drag computed using AdS/QCD correspondence. We use the perturbation produced by the quark strings to compute the wake and compare with the results obtained using AdS/QCD correspondence. We also study the shear viscosity with running couplings, analyze the viscosity to entropy ratio and compare the result with the known bound. In the presence of higher order curvature square corrections from the back-reactions of the embedded D7 branes, we argue the possibility of the entropy to viscosity bound being violated. Finally, we show that our set-up could in-principle allow us to study a family of gauge theories at the boundary by cutting off the dual geometry respectively at various points in the radial direction. All these gauge theories can have well defined UV completions, and more interestingly, we demonstrate that any thermodynamical quantities derived from these theories would be completely independent of the cut-off scale and only depend on the temperature at which we define these theories. Such a result would justify the holographic renormalisabilities of these theories which we, in turn, also demonstrate. We give physical interpretations of these results and compare them with more realistic scenarios.

## Key claim

Using a gravity dual that incorporates renormalization group running (a deformed conifold with squashed two-spheres and a small IR resolution, plus Ouyang-embedded D7 branes), the drag force on a massive quark receives corrections proportional to powers of log T compared to the standard AdS/QCD result, and the shear viscosity to entropy ratio may violate the conjectured 1/(4π) bound when higher-order curvature-squared corrections from D7 brane back-reactions are included.

## Method

The authors construct a non-AdS gravity dual based on a deformed Klebanov-Strassler geometry with a black hole and seven branes embedded via the Ouyang embedding, featuring squashed two-spheres and a small IR resolution. They compute the drag force on a massive quark (mapped to a fundamental string in the dual gravity) by analyzing the string's trailing configuration in this background, and compute the wake produced by the quark using the perturbation from these strings. They also compute the shear viscosity from the dual gravity description and analyze the viscosity-to-entropy ratio, including the effect of higher-order curvature-squared corrections from D7 brane back-reactions.

## Key numbers

- The drag force on a massive quark receives corrections proportional to powers of log T when compared with the drag computed using AdS/QCD correspondence (no explicit numerical coefficient given in the truncated text).
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s in the standard AdS background yields a minimum value of ℏ/(4π k_B) ≡ 1/(4π), as established by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001) and Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005).
- In the presence of higher-order curvature-squared corrections from D7 brane back-reactions, the authors argue for the possibility of violating the η/s ≥ 1/(4π) bound.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to strongly coupled plasmas beyond the conformal AdS/QCD correspondence by using a gravity dual that captures renormalization group running, building on the Klebanov-Strassler construction (2000) and its extensions by various authors (referred to as "1, 4, sully, cotrone" in the text). The results enable more realistic modeling of quark-gluon plasma properties relevant to RHIC and LHC observables, including jet quenching, elliptic flow, and heavy quark energy loss, by providing a framework where thermodynamic quantities are independent of the UV cutoff and depend only on temperature, justifying holographic renormalization for this family of gauge theories.
