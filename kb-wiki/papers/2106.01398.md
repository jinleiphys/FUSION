# Worldline Path Integrals for Gauge Fields and Quantum Computing

> We study different aspects the worldline path integrals with gauge fields using quantum computing. We use the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) and Evolution of Hamiltonian (EOH) quantum algorithms and IBM QISKit to perform our computations. We apply these methods to the path integral of a particle moving in a Abelian and non-Abelian background gauge field associated with a constant magnetic field and the field of a chromo-magnetic field. In all cases we found excellent agreement with the classical computation. We also discuss the insertion of vertex operators into the worldline path integrals to study scattering and show how to represent them using unitary operators and quantum gates on near term quantum computers.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that worldline path integrals for both Abelian and non-Abelian background gauge fields can be simulated on near-term quantum computers using the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) and Evolution of Hamiltonian (EOH) algorithms, with the caveat that the VQE results for the non-Abelian case are less accurate than for the Abelian case, likely due to poor overlap of the variational form with the ground state.

## Method

The authors map the Hamiltonian of a particle moving in a constant magnetic field (Abelian) and a chromo-magnetic field (non-Abelian SU(2) Wu-Yang monopole) onto qubit operators using a harmonic oscillator basis for VQE and a position basis for EOH. They implement the VQE algorithm with a Sequential Least SQuares Programming (SLSQP) optimizer and an R_y variational form with fully entangled circuits of depth 3, and the EOH algorithm using the Trotter-Suzuki approximation, both run on the IBM QISKit statevector simulator. For the non-Abelian case, worldline fermions are introduced to implement path ordering, and the Hamiltonian is expanded about a reference point using the field strength tensor.

## Key numbers

*   Constant magnetic field (Cartesian coordinates): VQE ground state energy = 1.0000003553, exact = 1.0, using 8 qubits.
*   Constant magnetic field (polar coordinates): VQE ground state energy = 0.99859198, exact = 0.9980452, using 4 qubits.
*   Non-Abelian SU(2) gauge field with g_m = 2: VQE ground state energy = -1.89074415, exact = -2.53854786, using 9 qubits.
*   Non-Abelian SU(2) gauge field with g_m = 0.2: VQE ground state energy = 0.32279234, exact = 0.31120022, using 9 qubits.
*   The Hamiltonian for the non-Abelian case is represented by a 512 × 512 matrix (9 qubits).

## Context

This work extends the worldline formalism for gauge fields, previously studied analytically by Pisarski (2006), Korthals-Altes (1994), and others, into the domain of quantum computing. It contrasts with full quantum field simulations that require many qubits by using the worldline approach to simulate particle propagation in 3+1 dimensions with fewer qubits. The results enable future quantum simulations of scattering amplitudes via vertex operators, as demonstrated for a nonlinear Klein-Gordon equation with a phi^3 interaction, and open the door to studying chiral fermions, background gravitational fields, and Kaluza-Klein theory on near-term quantum hardware.
