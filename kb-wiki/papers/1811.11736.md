# Thermal effective potential for the Polyakov loop to higher loop order

> This is a progress report on the calculation of the effective potential for the Polyakov loop in $SU(N)$ pure gauge theory beyond two-loop order. We introduce a new approach using the Poisson resummation formula to compute sum-integrals with the holonomy. We show partial results for the free energy at order $g^3$ and $g^4$.

## Key claim

The authors present a progress report on computing the effective potential for the Polyakov loop in SU(N) pure gauge theory beyond two-loop order, introducing a new Poisson resummation technique to handle sum-integrals with nontrivial holonomy; they show only partial results for the free energy at order g^3 and g^4, with the full gauge-invariant result requiring future completion of the "insert" term from delta-function constraints.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy part V_free of the effective potential for the Polyakov loop in SU(N) pure Yang-Mills theory using a background field method with constant diagonal A_4. They introduce Poisson resummation to evaluate sum-integrals with holonomy, compute one-loop self-energies in the Feynman gauge, and then perform ring-diagram resummation for the g^3 contribution. For the g^4 order, they reduce three-loop Feynman diagrams (3a-3f) to two master integrals I_2 and I_3 in the large-N limit, using double-line notation and color-index summation.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order effective potential up to order g^2 is V = -(N^2 T^4/pi^2)(1 - 5 g^2 N/(16 pi^2)) sum_{n != 0} L_n L_{-n}/n^4
* The g^3 contribution from neutral gluons is V^(3)_n.g. = -(T^4/(12 pi)) sum_{d=1}^{N-1} (Lambda^d_4/T^2)^(3/2)
* For charged gluons at g^3, the ring-diagram contribution involves terms with (theta^2_ij T^2 + Lambda^ij_alpha)^(3/2) minus |theta_ij T|^3, and Lambda^ij_alpha |theta_ij T|
* The one-loop self-energy for charged gluons in the static limit gives Lambda^ij_4 = (4 pi/3) g^2 T^2 sum_{k=1}^N [B_3(theta_ik/(2pi)) - B_3(theta_jk/(2pi))]/theta_ij
* For trivial holonomy, the Debye mass is m^2_D = (1/3) N g^2 T^2

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of the Polyakov loop effective potential beyond the two-loop order previously established by Gross-Pisarski-Yaffe-Weiss (1980) and the next-to-leading order results of Korthals-Altes (1993) and Dumitru (2013). The new Poisson resummation technique provides an alternative to standard Matsubara sum methods and analytic continuation approaches used by Kapusta (1979). The calculation enables future determination of the deconfinement phase transition order at large N, building on the work of Nishimura (2017), and requires eventual combination with the V_insert term from delta-function constraints to verify gauge invariance.
