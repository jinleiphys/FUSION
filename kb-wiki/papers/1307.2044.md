# Relativistic description of magnetic moments in nuclei with doubly closed shells plus or minus one nucleon

> Using the relativistic point-coupling model with density functional PC-PK1, the magnetic moments of the nuclei $^{207}$Pb, $^{209}$Pb, $^{207}$Tl and $^{209}$Bi with a $jj$ closed-shell core $^{208}$Pb are studied on the basis of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The corresponding time-odd fields, the one-pion exchange currents, and the first- and second-order corrections are taken into account. The present relativistic results reproduce the data well. The relative deviation between theory and experiment for these four nuclei is 6.1% for the relativistic calculations and somewhat smaller than the value of 13.2% found in earlier non-relativistic investigations. It turns out that the $π$ meson is important for the description of magnetic moments, first by means of one-pion exchange currents and second by the residual interaction provided by the $π$ exchange.

## Key claim

The relativistic point-coupling model with density functional PC-PK1, including time-odd fields, one-pion exchange currents, and first- and second-order configuration mixing, reproduces the magnetic moments of four nuclei near the doubly magic core 208Pb with a relative deviation of 6.1% from experiment, which is smaller than the 13.2% deviation found in earlier non-relativistic investigations; however, the second-order corrections require a truncation of the configuration space to avoid divergence, and the residual interaction provided by pion exchange is essential for obtaining significant first-order corrections.

## Method

The authors performed spherical relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations in coordinate space for the nuclei 207Pb, 209Pb, 207Tl, and 209Bi using the relativistic point-coupling density functional PC-PK1. They computed magnetic moments from the RMF ground state including time-odd fields via triaxially deformed calculations, then added one-pion exchange current corrections and first- and second-order configuration mixing corrections using Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory with experimental single-particle energies for the intermediate states. The configuration space for second-order corrections was truncated to one major shell above the closed core (neutron quantum numbers 2(n-1)+l <= 6, proton quantum numbers 2(n-1)+l <= 5) to avoid divergent results.

## Key numbers

* Relative deviation between theory and experiment for the four nuclei (207Pb, 209Pb, 207Tl, 209Bi): 6.1% for the relativistic calculations, compared to 13.2% for earlier non-relativistic investigations.
* First-order configuration mixing correction to the magnetic moment of 209Bi: 0.52 mu_N (total) from relativistic PC-PK1 with pion exchange included, compared to non-relativistic values ranging from 0.43 mu_N to 0.80 mu_N depending on the interaction used.
* Experimental excitation energies used for first-order corrections: Delta E_p = 5.6 MeV for the (1h_9/2, 1h_11/2) proton spin-orbit partners, and Delta E_n = 5.86 MeV for the (1i_11/2, 1i_13/2) neutron spin-orbit partners.
* Pion mass used: m_pi = 138 MeV; pion-nucleon coupling constant: f_pi = 1.
* Box size for spherical RMF calculations: 15 fm; step size: 0.1 fm; triaxial deformed RMF calculations used 10 major shells in a harmonic oscillator basis.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic descriptions of magnetic moments in LS closed-shell nuclei (such as 16O and 40Ca cores) to the jj closed-shell core 208Pb, where first-order configuration mixing (the Arima-Horie effect) is expected to be important. The study builds on prior non-relativistic calculations by Arima (1972) and others that used interactions such as Kallio-Kolltveit, Gillet, Kim-Rasmussen, Brueckner, Hamada-Johnston, and M3Y, and it addresses the long-standing problem that relativistic mean field theory without pion exchange and configuration mixing fails to reproduce isovector magnetic moments. The results enable future investigations of magnetic moments in heavier jj closed-shell nuclei and motivate the development of beyond-mean-field theories that can treat the divergent second-order corrections without artificial truncation.
