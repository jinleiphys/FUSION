# Molecular structure of highly-excited resonant states in $^{24}$Mg and the corresponding $^8$Be+$^{16}$O and $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C decays

> Exotic $^8$Be and $^{12}$C decays from high-lying resonances in $^{24}$Mg are analyzed in terms of a cluster model. The calculated quantities agree well with the corresponding experimental data. It is found that the calculated decay widths are very sensitive to the angular momentum carried by the outgoing cluster. It is shown that this property makes cluster decay a powerful tool to determine the spin as well as the molecular structures of the resonances.

# Molecular structure of highly-excited resonant states in $^{24}$Mg and the corresponding $^8$Be+$^{16}$O and $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C decays

## Key claim

The calculated cluster decay widths for high-lying resonances in $^{24}$Mg are very sensitive to the angular momentum carried by the outgoing cluster, making cluster decay a powerful tool to determine both the spin and the molecular structure of the resonances, but only under the assumption that the preformation factor $P \approx 0.5$ for both decay channels and that the resonances are quasibound states satisfying the cluster model conditions.

## Method

The authors applied an extreme cluster model (Buck et al., 1990) using a Woods-Saxon potential with Chepurnov parametrization and a folded mean-field nuclear potential for the cluster. The Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition was used to determine the folding factor and radius parameter, with the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C Coulomb barrier fitted to experimental data from Kovar (1979). Decay widths were calculated using the expression $\Gamma = P \times \Gamma_{\rm p}$, where the penetration width $\Gamma_{\rm p}$ depends on the angular momentum $l$ carried by the outgoing cluster, and the preformation factor $P$ was taken as 0.5 for both $^8$Be and $^{12}$C channels based on experimental excitation functions.

## Key numbers

- $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C Coulomb barrier: $V_B = 5.8 \pm 0.3$ MeV, $R_B = 6.5 \pm 0.4$ fm (experimental from Kovar, 1979)
- Adjusted radius parameter for $^{12}$C-cluster potential: $r_0 = 1.44$ fm (compared to Chepurnov value of 1.24 fm)
- Folding factor for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C channel: $\lambda = 0.500$ (with $Q_0 = -13.93$ MeV)
- Folding factor for $^8$Be+$^{16}$O channel: $\lambda = 0.509$ (with $Q_0^{\rm expt} = -14.14$ MeV)
- Global quantum number $G = 20$ for $^{12}$C$_{\rm gs}$+$^{12}$C$_{\rm gs}$ and $G = 16$ for $^8$Be$_{\rm gs}$+$^{16}$O$_{\rm gs}$ (ignoring inner quanta)
- Calculated width for 23.9 MeV ($J=8$) state: $\Gamma^{\rm cal} = 136$ keV (experimental: 200 keV)
- Calculated width for 33.4 MeV state: $\Gamma^{\rm cal} = 76$ keV for $J=13$ (experimental: 230 keV, with $J=12$ or 13)
- For the 23.9 MeV state, $^{12}$C$_{\rm gs}$+$^{12}$C($2_1^+$) channel width: 0.3 keV
- For the 23.9 MeV state, $^{12}$C$_{\rm gs}$+$^{12}$C(Hoyle state) channel width: less than 1 keV
- Experimental energy range of resonances: $\approx 23-34$ MeV (main study) and $20-23$ MeV (additional test)
- Experimental energy resolution: $\approx 100$ keV (Fletcher, 1976; James, 1978) and 87 keV (Curtis, 1995)

## Context

This work extends the cluster model approach of Buck et al. (1990) to highly-excited resonant states in $^{24}$Mg, where previous calculations by Buck et al. (1990) using a $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster model had been limited to excitation energies $\leq 20$ MeV. The study addresses the long-standing question from Bromley (1960) and Vogt (1960) about whether resonances observed in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering represent true cluster states in $^{24}$Mg or merely scattering states in the ion-ion potential, as debated by Beck (2009). The results enable spin assignments for resonances where experimental angular correlation data were ambiguous (Free, 1998; Free, 2001), and provide a method to distinguish between $^8$Be+$^{16}$O and $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C molecular configurations in the same resonance.
