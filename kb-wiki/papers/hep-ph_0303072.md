# The Quark Gluon Plasma: lattice computations put to experimental test

> I describe how lattice computations are being used to extract experimentally relevant features of the quark gluon plasma. I deal specifically with relaxation times, photon emissivity, strangeness yields, event by event fluctuations of conserved quantities and hydrodynamic flow. Finally I give evidence that the plasma is liquid-like in some ways.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD computations can provide experimentally testable predictions for quark-gluon plasma properties in the temperature range \(1.5 \le T/T_c \le 3\), where the coupling \(g = \mathcal{O}(1)\) and perturbation theory is not numerically accurate; the plasma exhibits liquid-like behavior rather than gas-like behavior in this regime.

## Method
The author reviews lattice QCD computations of screening masses, the equation of state (pressure, energy density, entropy density), quark number susceptibilities, and transport coefficients (electrical conductivity, relaxation times, photon emissivity) for the quark-gluon plasma. These lattice results are compared to experimental data from RHIC, including elliptic flow, strangeness yields (Wroblewski parameter), and event-by-event fluctuations of conserved quantities. The liquidity parameter \(\ell = \tau S^{1/3}\) is introduced to classify the plasma phase.

## Key numbers
- The screening mass ratio in Polyakov loop correlators is consistent with 3/2 in the range \(1.25 \le T/T_c \le 3\) (quenched QCD).
- Pressure, entropy, and energy density deviate from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit by about 20% even at the highest lattice temperatures (about \(4T_c\)).
- The speed of sound \(c_s\) is close to its ideal gas value at the highest temperatures, with a dip near \(T_c\).
- The Wroblewski parameter \(\lambda_s\) (ratio of strange to light quark production) is computed from quenched lattice QCD and compared to experimental data.
- The scaled soft photon emissivity gives \(\sigma/T \approx 7 C_{EM}\) for \(1.5 \le T/T_c \le 3\), where \(C_{EM} = 4\pi\alpha \sum_f e_f^2\).
- The relaxation time for quarks is \(\tau_R \approx 1/(7T)\), much smaller than 1 fm for \(1.5 \le T/T_c \le 3\).
- The fireball at RHIC is marginally transparent to soft photons, with a photon mean free path of about 2–3 fm.
- The liquidity parameter \(\ell < 1.5\) for experimental conditions (\(E \approx 1\) GeV/fm\(^3\), \(\tau < 1\) fm), indicating liquid-like behavior.

## Context
This review extends earlier lattice work on the QCD phase transition (e.g., precise \(T_c\) extraction by the MILC collaboration) and Debye screening (Olaf Kaczmarek, 2002) to dynamical quantities like transport coefficients and fluctuations. It enables direct comparisons between lattice QCD predictions and RHIC experimental data on elliptic flow, strangeness yields, and photon production, and motivates future lattice computations of transport coefficients such as \(\hat{q}\) for jet quenching. The work contrasts with perturbative approaches (e.g., hard thermal loop resummation by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe, 2001) which describe a dilute gaseous plasma, while the lattice results indicate a liquid-like plasma for \(T \le 3T_c\).
