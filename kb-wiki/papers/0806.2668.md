# Universal Fermi gases in mixed dimensions

> We investigate a two-species Fermi gas in which one species is confined in a two-dimensional plane (2D) or one-dimensional line (1D) while the other is free in the three-dimensional space (3D). We discuss the realization of such a system with the interspecies interaction tuned to resonance. When the mass ratio is in the range 0.0351<m_2D/m_3D<6.35 for the 2D-3D mixture or 0.00646<m_1D/m_3D<2.06 for the 1D-3D mixture, the resulting system is stable against the Efimov effect and has universal properties. We calculate key quantities in the many-body phase diagram. Other possible scale-invariant systems with short-range few-body interactions are also elucidated.

## Key claim

A two-species Fermi gas in mixed dimensions (one species confined to 2D or 1D, the other free in 3D) can be tuned to a scale-invariant resonance with universal properties, provided the mass ratio lies within specific stability windows that avoid the Efimov effect: 0.0351 < m_2D/m_3D < 6.35 for the 2D-3D mixture and 0.00646 < m_1D/m_3D < 2.06 for the 1D-3D mixture.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Schrodinger equation for an A atom (confined by a harmonic potential) and a B atom (free in 3D) with a zero-range interspecies interaction characterized by the s-wave scattering length a. By imposing the short-range boundary condition and extracting the asymptotic form at large separation, they obtain an effective scattering length a_eff that depends on the ratio l_perp/a, where l_perp is the harmonic oscillator length of the confinement. They then analyze three-body problems (AAB and ABB) in hyperspherical coordinates to determine the scaling exponent gamma of the wave function; when gamma becomes complex, the Efimov effect occurs, setting the critical mass ratios u_min and u_max. For the many-body phase diagram, they use a variational wave function with single particle-hole excitations to compute the critical chemical potentials at the phase boundaries.

## Key numbers

- For the 2D-3D mixture, the system is stable against the Efimov effect for mass ratios in the range 0.0351 < m_2D/m_3D < 6.35.
- For the 1D-3D mixture, the stability window is 0.00646 < m_1D/m_3D < 2.06.
- The critical mass ratio for the Efimov effect in the p-wave channel of the AAB system in the 2D-3D mixture is u_max = 6.35.
- The critical mass ratio for the odd-parity channel of the AAB system in the 1D-3D mixture is u_max = 2.06.
- The critical mass ratio for the ABB system in the 2D-3D mixture is u_min = 0.0351.
- The critical mass ratio for the ABB system in the 1D-3D mixture is u_min = 0.00646.
- The binding energy of the shallow bound state for a_eff > 0 is E_binding = -hbar^2/(2 m_AB a_eff^2), where m_AB is the reduced mass.

## Context

This work extends the concept of unitary Fermi gases from pure three dimensions to mixed-dimensional systems, building on prior studies of confinement-induced resonances (Massignan, 2006) and the Efimov effect in three-component Fermi gases (Efimov, 1972). The authors identify seven additional classes of scale-invariant short-range few-body interactions in mixed dimensions beyond the well-known s-wave two-body resonance in pure 3D. The results enable experimental realization of novel unitary Fermi gases using cold atomic mixtures such as Li-6 and K-40, and open the door to studying many-body physics in systems with reduced-dimensional components, including possible universal Fermi gases with three-body or four-body resonances.
