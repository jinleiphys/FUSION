# Ab initio leading order effective potential for elastic proton scattering based on the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model

> Based on the Watson expansion of the multiple scattering series, we employ a nonlocal translationally invariant nuclear density derived within the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) framework from a chiral next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) nucleon-nucleon interaction and the very same interaction for a consistent full-folding calculation of the effective (optical) potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering for medium-heavy nuclei. The leading order effective (optical) folding potential is computed by integrating over a translationally invariant SA-NCSM one-body scalar density, spin-projected momentum distribution, and the Wolfenstein amplitudes $A$, $C$, and $M$. The resulting nonlocal potentials serve as input for a momentum space Lippmann-Schwinger equation, whose solutions are summed up to obtain nucleon-nucleus scattering observables. In the SA-NCSM, the model space is systematically up-selected using $\SpR{3}$ symmetry considerations. For the light nucleus of $^6$He, we establish a systematic selection scheme in the SA-NCSM for scattering observables. Then, we apply this scheme to calculations of scattering observables, such as differential cross sections, analyzing powers, and spin rotation functions for elastic proton scattering from $^{20}$Ne and $^{40}$Ca in the energy regime between 65 and 200 MeV, and compare to available data. Our calculations show that the leading order effective nucleon-nucleus potential in the Watson expansion of multiple scattering theory obtained from an up-selected SA-NCSM model space describes $^{40}$Ca elastic scattering observables reasonably well to about 60 degrees in the center-of-mass frame, which coincides roughly with the validity of the NNLO chiral interaction used to calculate both the nucleon-nucleon amplitudes and the one-body scalar and spin nuclear densities.

## Key claim

The leading order effective nucleon-nucleus potential derived from the Watson expansion of multiple scattering theory, using symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) densities from a chiral NNLO nucleon-nucleon interaction, describes elastic proton scattering observables for $^{40}$Ca reasonably well only up to about 60 degrees in the center-of-mass frame, which coincides with the validity range of the underlying NNLO chiral interaction.

## Method

The authors computed a leading order effective (optical) folding potential by integrating a translationally invariant SA-NCSM one-body scalar density and spin-projected momentum distribution with Wolfenstein amplitudes $A$, $C$, and $M$ from the same chiral NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ nucleon-nucleon interaction. The resulting nonlocal potentials served as input for a momentum space Lippmann-Schwinger equation, whose solutions were summed to obtain nucleon-nucleus scattering observables. The SA-NCSM model space was systematically up-selected using Sp(3,R) symmetry considerations, with a selection cutoff $\varepsilon_{\rm max}$ defined as the fraction of the complete model space retained.

## Key numbers

- For $^6$He, the complete model space dimension grows from 905 at $N_{\rm max}=4$ to 1,024,654 at $N_{\rm max}=12$ (three orders of magnitude increase)
- For $^6$He, a selection cutoff of $\varepsilon_{\rm max} \sim 0.26$-$0.29$ (constructed from $w_{\rm norm}>10^{-2}$) was sufficient to reproduce complete model space results for structure and scattering observables
- For $^{20}$Ne, the rms matter radius from the calculation was 2.6(1) fm compared to the experimental value of 2.87(3) fm
- For $^{40}$Ca, the complete $N_{\rm max}=6$ model space for $J=0$ has a dimension of 327,125,599
- Calculations were performed for elastic proton scattering from $^{20}$Ne and $^{40}$Ca in the energy regime between 65 and 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pioneering ab initio effective interaction approach for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering, previously limited to light nuclei with masses up to $A\approx 16$ using the no-core shell model (NCSM) by Burrows (2018, 2020) and Baker (2023), to medium-mass nuclei up to $A\approx 48$ by employing the symmetry-adapted NCSM framework. The SA-NCSM approach, reviewed by Launey (2016, 2021), uses shape-related symmetries to drastically reduce basis states, enabling calculations for heavier nuclei. This work enables future studies of elastic scattering from medium-mass nuclei using ab initio structure inputs, with the possibility of including three-nucleon forces and higher-order terms in the spectator expansion.
