# The unitarity expansion for light nuclei

> I is argued here that (at least light) nuclei may reside in a sweet spot: bound weakly enough to be insensitive to the details of the interaction, but dense enough to be insensitive to the exact values of the large two-body scattering lengths as well. In this scenario, a systematic expansion of nuclear observables around the unitarity limit converges. In particular, in this scheme the nuclear force is constructed such that the gross features of states in the nuclear chart are determined by a very simple leading-order interaction, whereas---much like the fine structure of atomic spectra---observables are moved to their physical values by small perturbative corrections. Explicit evidence in favor of this conjecture is shown for the binding energies of three and four nucleons.

## Key claim
Light nuclei may occupy a "sweet spot" where they are bound weakly enough to be insensitive to interaction details, yet dense enough to be insensitive to the exact values of the large two-body scattering lengths, enabling a systematic perturbative expansion of nuclear observables around the unitarity limit (infinite scattering lengths). The paper argues that nature is close enough to unitarity that this expansion converges quantitatively for at least three- and four-nucleon systems.

## Method
The unitarity expansion is constructed as a variant of pionless effective field theory (pionless EFT), where at leading order (LO) the two-body interaction is set to the unitarity limit (infinite scattering lengths) with no free two-body parameters, and a single three-body parameter is adjusted to reproduce the triton binding energy. Finite physical scattering lengths, effective range corrections, and electromagnetic effects are added as perturbative corrections at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). The calculations use separable potentials with a Gaussian regulator, solving Faddeev equations for three nucleons and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for four nucleons.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy: fixed at 8.48 MeV (experimental value) at all orders, used as input to tune the three-body interaction.
- Triton-helion energy splitting at NLO: predicted as (0.92 ± 0.18) MeV, compared to experimental value 0.764 MeV.
- Deuteron binding energy at NNLO: 1.41 ± 1.12 MeV, compared to experimental value 2.22 MeV.
- 4He ground state binding energy at LO (unitarity limit): 39 ± 12 MeV, with uncertainty of O(r_{s,t}/a_{s,t}) ≈ 30%.
- 4He binding energy at incomplete NLO (finite scattering lengths only, no range or electromagnetic corrections): 30 ± 9 MeV.
- Experimental 4He binding energy: 28.3 MeV.
- Ratio B_4/B_3 for four-boson tetramers at unitarity: 4.611 (ground state) and 1.002 (excited state), compared to experimental values for 4He/3He of approximately 3.66 and 1.05.

## Context
This work extends the universal physics of systems with large scattering lengths, placing light nuclei in the same class as cold atomic gases near Feshbach resonances and Efimov states. It builds on the pionless EFT framework reviewed by Hammer (2018) and on prior work by König (2015, 2016) establishing the unitarity expansion. The approach contrasts with standard pionless EFT where finite scattering lengths are included at LO, and it enables a simplified description where the gross features of light nuclei are determined by a parameter-free leading-order interaction, with fine structure added perturbatively. The results suggest connections to recent single-parameter models for helium isotopes by Fossez (2018) and to calculations of nuclear matter guided by unitarity by Kievsky (2018).
