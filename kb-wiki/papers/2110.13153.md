# Predictions and postdictions for relativistic lead and oxygen collisions with Trajectum

> We introduce a global analysis of relativistic heavy ion collisions using Trajectum of a significantly higher precision and including a new option to vary the normalization of the centrality estimator. We use the posterior distribution of our parameters to generate a set of high statistics samples that allows us to make precise predictions including statistical and systematic uncertainties estimated from the model parameter distribution. The results are systematically compared with experiment whereby we also include many observables not included in the global analysis. This includes in particular (extremely) ultracentral anisotropic flow and mean transverse momentum, whereby we find satisfactory agreement with experiment where data is available. Lastly, we compute spectra and anisotropic flow for oxygen-oxygen collisions performed at RHIC and to be performed at the LHC and comment on how these collisions may inform us on properties of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma.

## Key claim

The authors perform a high-precision global Bayesian analysis of relativistic heavy-ion collisions using the Trajectum framework, finding that the model's predictions for ultracentral anisotropic flow and mean transverse momentum agree satisfactorily with existing experimental data, though the posterior distributions for some parameters (e.g., nucleon width and shear viscosity curvature) shift substantially when the centrality normalization is allowed to vary, indicating that small discrepancies between model and experimental centrality determination can affect parameter extraction.

## Method

The authors use version 1.1 of the Trajectum code with a T R ENTo initial condition model modified to include continuous number of constituents, free-streaming pre-hydrodynamic stage, 14-moment approximation hydrodynamics with temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner. They perform a Bayesian analysis with Gaussian Process emulators on 750 design points (15k events each) for PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, fitting to charged particle multiplicity, transverse energy, identified particle yields and mean pT, pT fluctuations, and integrated and pT-differential anisotropic flow coefficients, while introducing a new parameter that rescales the centrality estimator normalization.

## Key numbers

- 750 design points with 15k events each for PbPb analysis; 1500 design points with 40k events each for OO analysis
- Total computational time approximately 1M CPU hours, roughly equally divided over PbPb and OO collisions
- UrQMD leads to about 1% higher multiplicities and about 1.5% higher mean pT for kaons and protons compared to SMASH; UrQMD elliptic flow is about 1% higher across all centrality classes
- The fitted free streaming velocity v_fs is substantially smaller than previous results from Nijs (2020); the fitted minimum shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (eta/s)_min is slightly higher than previous results
- The 90% confidence interval for cent_norm is 93.8^{+7.6}_{-8.8} when varied, consistent with 100%
- Improvement in posterior precision compared to Nijs (2020): N PbPb_2.76 improved by 50%, N PbPb_5.02 by 56%, (eta/s)_slope by 97%, T_switch by 66%, sigma_fluct by 44%
- The value for tau_pi sT/eta from holography (approximately 2.61) is clearly favored over the weakly coupled value (5) for fixed cent_norm, and slightly favored with varying cent_norm

## Context

This work extends the global Bayesian analysis approach of Bernhard (2019) and the previous Trajectum analyses by Nijs (2020) with higher precision emulation, continuous number of constituents, and a new centrality normalization parameter. The results enable predictions for upcoming oxygen-oxygen collisions at RHIC (0.2 TeV, 402M events in May 2021) and LHC (6.8 TeV, planned likely in 2024), which are at the threshold where hydrodynamic QGP may be present but significant non-hydrodynamic effects are also expected, potentially informing on the limits of hydrodynamics and the nature of the pre-hydrodynamic phase.
