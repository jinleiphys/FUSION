# Ab initio NCSM/RGM for three-body cluster systems and application to $^4$He+n+n

> We introduce an extension of the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) in order to describe three-body cluster states. We present results for the $^6$He ground state within a $^4$He+n+n cluster basis as well as first results for the phase shifts of different channels of the $^4$He+n+n system which provide information about low-lying resonances of this nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper extends the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) to describe three-body cluster states, presenting results for the $^6$He ground state and continuum phase shifts within a $^4$He+n+n basis, but the $^4$He core is described only by its ground state wave function, ignoring its excited states, which is the only limitation in the model space used.

## Method

The many-body wave function is expanded over a basis of three-body cluster channel states built from NCSM wave functions of each cluster, and the Schrodinger equation is projected onto these basis states to obtain a system of coupled-channel integral-differential equations. These equations are orthogonalized and transformed to the hyperspherical harmonics basis to obtain non-local integral-differential equations in the hyper-radial coordinate, which are solved using the microscopic R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. The calculations use similarity-renormalization-group evolved potentials from the chiral N$^3$LO NN interaction with $\Lambda$ = 1.5 fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- $^6$He ground state energy from NCSM/RGM at $N_{\rm max}=12$: $-28.70$ MeV, compared to NCSM at $N_{\rm max}=12$: $-29.75$ MeV, and extrapolated NCSM: $-29.84(4)$ MeV
- $^4$He ground state energy from NCSM/RGM at $N_{\rm max}=12$: $-28.22$ MeV, compared to NCSM at $N_{\rm max}=12$: $-28.23$ MeV, and extrapolated NCSM: $-28.23(1)$ MeV
- The difference between NCSM/RGM and NCSM $^6$He ground state energies is approximately 1 MeV, attributed to polarization effects of the $^4$He core
- Calculations performed with $\hbar\Omega = 14$ MeV harmonic oscillator model space at $N_{\rm max}=12$

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM approach previously developed for two-body cluster bases (Quaglioni 2008, Quaglioni 2009, Navratil 2011, Navratil 2012) to three-body cluster systems, enabling study of Borromean nuclei like $^6$He, the lightest Borromean nucleus (Tanihata 1995). The method produces wave functions with appropriate asymptotic behavior, unlike bound-state ab initio methods such as the NCSM, and the authors plan to include core excitations in future work through the no-core shell model with continuum approach (NCSMC) (PhysRevLett.110.022505, PhysRevC.87.034326). Preliminary continuum results identify the experimentally known $2^+_1$ resonance and a second low-lying $2^+$ resonance recently measured at Ganil (Mougeot 2012), with no low-lying resonances found in the $0^+$ or $1^-$ channels.
