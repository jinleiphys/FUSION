# T-noninvariant effect in muon capture by 6-Li nucleus with decay to a continuum

> The T-noninvariant transverse polarization of neutrons is considered for muon capture by a 6-Li nucleus with decay into a quasistationary 2+ state of the three particle alpha+n+n continuum. This polarization is orthogonal to the plane spanned by the polarization axis of the initial mesic atom and the neutron momentum. The situation in which neutrons that are emitted in the plane orthogonal to the axis of the mesic-atom polarization and which have oppositely directed momenta equal in magnitude are selected is analyzed. The wave function of the final state is constructed by using the method of hyperspherical harmonics. In the approximation of the allowed Gamow-Teller transition 1+ to 2+, this neutron polarization is expressed in terms of the T-noninvariant relative phase of reduced matrix elements for transitions from the 6-Li ground state to various final-state configurations.

# T-noninvariant effect in muon capture by 6-Li nucleus with decay to a continuum

## Key claim
The transverse polarization of neutrons emitted in muon capture by 6-Li, decaying into the 2+ resonance of the alpha+n+n continuum, is sensitive to T-invariance violation and can be expressed in terms of the T-noninvariant relative phase of reduced matrix elements for transitions to different final-state configurations. The effect is estimated to be on the order of 0.1 times the mesoatom polarization times the T-noninvariant phase, with the caveat that this estimate relies on restricting the final-state wave function to only two leading hyperspherical harmonics components and considering only the allowed Gamow-Teller transition.

## Method
The wave function of the final alpha+n+n continuum state was constructed using the method of hyperspherical harmonics, restricting to the two dominant components (hypermoment K=2, configurations gamma1={0220} and gamma2={1111}) that form the 2+ resonance, as identified by Danilin (1993). The non-relativistic transition Hamiltonian was taken in zero-order approximation for the Gamow-Teller transition 1+ to 2+. The analysis selected events where the two emitted neutrons have equal and opposite momenta perpendicular to the mesoatom polarization axis, allowing integration over unobserved variables and expression of the neutron polarization in terms of the imaginary part of the product of reduced matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- The 2+ resonance in the alpha+n+n continuum has energy 1.8 MeV and width 0.1 MeV.
- The binding energy of 6-He is 0.975 MeV.
- For the hyperfine state F=3/2 of the muonic atom with J_i=1, the coefficient C(1,3/2)=1.5 and D(1,3/2)=0.237.
- The mesoatom polarization p_1(F) for muon+6-Li in the F=3/2 state is about 0.05 (Favart 1970).
- The T-noninvariant phase phi is less than 10^{-3} (Herczeg 1988).
- The constant lambda_mu equals 1.005 x 10^3 s^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends the proposals of Deutsch (1992) and Oziewicz (1994) for T-invariance tests in muon capture, and specifically follows the approach of Ciechanowicz (1993) who discussed neutron polarization in muon capture on deuterium and 3-He. It builds on the microscopic description of A=6 nuclei as three-body alpha+N+N systems using hyperspherical harmonics developed by Danilin (1989-1993) and on the calculation of muon capture to the 6-He ground state by Shulgina (1993). The formalism enables future calculation of reduced matrix elements to relate the T-noninvariant effect to parameters of nucleon-nucleon potentials violating time reversal invariance, as discussed by Gudkov (1992).
