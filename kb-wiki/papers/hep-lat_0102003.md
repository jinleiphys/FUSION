# Evidence Against Instanton Dominance of Topological Charge Fluctuations in QCD

> The low-lying eigenmodes of the Dirac operator associated with typical gauge field configurations in QCD encode, among other low-energy properties, the physics behind the solution to the $U_A(1)$ problem (i.e. the origin of the $η'$ mass), the nature of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, and the physics of string-breaking, quark-antiquark pair production, and the OZI rule. Moreover, the space-time chiral structure of these eigenmodes reflects the space-time topological structure of the underlying gauge field. We present evidence from lattice QCD on the local chiral structure of low Dirac eigenmodes leading to the conclusion that topological charge fluctuations of the QCD vacuum are not instanton-dominated. The result supports Witten's arguments that topological charge is produced by confinement-related gauge fluctuations rather than instantons.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD evidence that topological charge fluctuations in the QCD vacuum are not dominated by instantons, supporting Witten's argument that confinement-related gauge fluctuations rather than semiclassical instantons produce topological charge. The hedge is that the evidence comes from studying the local chiral structure of low-lying Dirac eigenmodes, and the conclusion is conditional on the interpretation that instanton dominance would require these modes to exhibit locally self-dual or anti-self-dual behavior.

## Method

The authors used quenched lattice QCD gauge configurations from the Fermilab ACPMAPS b-ensemble and computed low-lying eigenmodes of the Wilson-Dirac operator. They analyzed the local chirality properties of these eigenmodes, specifically testing whether the right- and left-handed components of the wave functions are concentrated in separate lumps (as expected in an instanton-dominated vacuum) or whether they clump together around the same gauge fluctuations (as expected in a confining vacuum). The test avoids smoothing or cooling procedures that would artificially alter the gauge configurations.

## Key numbers

* Topological susceptibility ratio: $\chi_t^{1/4} / \sqrt{\sigma} = 0.455 \pm 0.015$ in $SU(3)_c$ and $0.487 \pm 0.012$ in $SU(2)_c$, showing near equality within errors for $N_c=2$ and $N_c=3$
* The energy density of confining vacuum fluctuations is of order $1$ GeV/fm$^3$, generating "hot spots" in both $({\bf E}\cdot{\bf E} + {\bf B}\cdot{\bf B})$ and ${\bf E}\cdot{\bf B}$

## Context

This work extends Witten's arguments (1979) that instantons are not required for resolving the $U_A(1)$ problem and that the $\eta'$ mass can arise from confinement-related fluctuations, as demonstrated by the Witten-Veneziano formula. The results contradict the Instanton Liquid Model (ILM) of Shuryak and others, which posits that the QCD vacuum consists of locally self-dual or anti-self-dual gauge field lumps. The paper suggests the Strong Coupling Quark Model (SCQM) as an alternative framework where confinement plays a central role, and notes that the AdS/CFT correspondence (Witten, 1998) provides additional theoretical support for non-instanton $\theta$-dependence in large-$N_c$ gauge theory.
