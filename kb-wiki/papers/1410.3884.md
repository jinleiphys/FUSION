# Forward-backward multiplicity correlations in pp collisions at high energy in Monte Carlo model with string fusion

> The correlations between multiplicities in two separated rapidity windows, is studied in the framework of the Monte Carlo model based on the picture of string formation in elementary collisions of colour dipoles. The hardness of the elementary collisions is defined by a transverse size of the interacting dipoles. The dependencies of the forward-backward correlation strength on the width and position of the pseudorapidity windows, as well as on transverse momentum range of observed particles were studied. It is demonstrated that taking into account of the string fusion effects improves the agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating string fusion effects into a Monte Carlo model improves the agreement with experimental data on forward-backward multiplicity correlations in pp collisions, but notes that at midrapidity and current energies it is hard to distinguish between the model versions with and without string fusion.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo model based on the partonic picture of nucleon interaction with colour dipole collisions, where strings are stretched between projectile and target partons. String fusion was implemented via a discrete lattice approach with cell area equal to the string transverse area, and the mean multiplicity and transverse momentum of particles from a cluster of \(k\) overlapping strings were scaled as \(\mu = \sqrt{k} \mu_1\) and \(p_T = \sqrt[4]{k} {p_T}_1\). The hardness of elementary collisions was defined by the transverse size of interacting dipoles, and the model parameters were constrained using data on total inelastic cross-section and multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- The string radius used in the model with string fusion was \(r_{\mathrm{str}} = 0.2\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- The intrinsic string transverse momentum was set to \(p_0 = 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\).
- The correlation coefficient \(b_{\mathrm{corr}}\) was studied at collision energies \(\sqrt{s}\) from 200 to 7000 GeV.
- Transverse momentum cuts of \(0.3 < p_T < 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\) were applied for comparison with ALICE data.
- The model overestimates the correlation coefficient in the hard transverse momentum area above approximately \(1\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\).

## Context

This work extends the study of forward-backward multiplicity correlations, previously measured by ALICE (2013), ATLAS (2011), and UA5 (1989), by using a Monte Carlo model that includes string fusion effects based on the string fusion model (SF). The results enable further investigation of collective phenomena in pp collisions and indicate that the soft string approach is applicable for \(p_T\) below about \(1\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\), while hard processes like jet fragmentation become relevant at higher \(p_T\).
