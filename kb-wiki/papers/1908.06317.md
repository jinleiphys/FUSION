# Is the Momentum Sum Rule Valid for Nuclear Structure Functions ?

> We address the validity of the momentum sum rule for deep inelastic nuclear structure functions.

# Is the Momentum Sum Rule Valid for Nuclear Structure Functions?

## Key claim
The momentum sum rule derived from the operator product expansion (OPE) is invalid for nuclear parton distribution functions because shadowing and anti-shadowing arise from Glauber interference processes involving multiple nucleons, which cannot be reduced to the handbag diagram where two currents act on a single quark propagator. The authors argue that shadowing and anti-shadowing do not need to compensate each other to restore the momentum sum rule, contrary to conventional expectations.

## Method
The authors analyze the forward virtual Compton scattering amplitude on a nucleus using a Glauber multiple-scattering formalism, comparing one-step amplitudes (standard deep inelastic scattering on an interior nucleon) with two-step amplitudes involving diffractive deep inelastic scattering (DDIS) on a front-face nucleon followed by inelastic scattering of the produced vector system on an interior nucleon. They examine the phase structure of Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges in DDIS and evaluate whether the OPE can be applied when the two currents in the Compton amplitude act on different nucleons separated by inter-nucleon distances.

## Key numbers
- Anti-shadowing is absent in neutrino charged-current data in the domain 0.1 < x_Bj < 0.2, in contrast to charged lepton deep inelastic scattering measurements
- Approximately 10% of high energy DIS events are diffractive (Derrick 1993, Ahmed 1994)
- The Reggeon contribution to DDIS has phase (1/√2)(-i + 1) with α_R = 1/2
- The Kuti-Weisskopf prediction states F_2^p(x,Q^2) - F_2^n(x,Q^2) ∝ √x

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous arguments (Brodsky 1989, Brodsky 2004) that final-state rescattering corrections give nontrivial dynamical contributions to measured PDFs. It directly contradicts the conventional expectation from Nikolaev (1975) and Frankfurt (1990) that anti-shadowing must compensate shadowing to restore the momentum sum rule. The paper explains the puzzling NuTeV measurement (Tzanov 2005) showing no anti-shadowing in neutrino-nucleus scattering, in contrast to electron-nucleus measurements from BCDMS (Benvenuti 1987) and NMC (Amaudruz 1995, Arneodo 1996). The analysis enables future experimental tests via charge-exchange DDIS reactions γ* p → n X+ with rapidity gaps, and suggests that sum rule derivations may also fail for the proton at small x_Bj as noted by Mueller.
