# Higher-point Energy Correlators: Factorization in the Back-to-Back Limit & Non-perturbative Effects

> N-point energy correlators are powerful observables for studying strong interactions, with applications ranging from extractions of the strong coupling $α_s$ to probes of jet modification in heavy-ion collisions and determination of the top-quark mass. Their practical use has, however, been limited by the complicated phase space for large N. Using a recently introduced parametrization that simplifies this structure, we study projected N-point correlators in two regimes: factorization in the back-to-back limit and leading non-perturbative effects in the collinear limit. While results in the back-to-back regime were previously limited to the energy-energy correlator, our approach allows us to derive the factorization theorem for arbitrary N. We compute the new ingredient, a one-loop jet function, needed for the next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic resummation, which enables future $α_s$ extractions with complementary systematics. We further determine the analytic structure of leading non-perturbative power corrections for arbitrary N, including their dependence on the center-of-mass energy Q, the value of N, and the angular scale $x$. We present the first results for non-integer N<1, finding that the classical scaling in $x$ acquires an N-dependent modification, and that a new non-perturbative matrix element $\tildeΩ^{[N]}$ appears. In a certain approximation, $\tildeΩ^{[N]}$ can be related to the standard parameter $Ω_1$ relevant for N>1. Our analytic predictions are tested against the hadronization model in Pythia, finding good agreement. The results presented in this paper demonstrate the significant advancements enabled through our new parametrization of energy correlators.

## Key claim

The paper derives a factorization theorem for projected N-point energy correlators (PENCs) in the back-to-back limit for arbitrary N, which was previously only available for the energy-energy correlator (N=2), and computes the leading non-perturbative power corrections for arbitrary N including the first results for non-integer N<1, finding that the classical scaling in the angular variable x acquires an N-dependent modification and a new non-perturbative matrix element appears.

## Method

The authors use soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive the factorization formula for PENCs in the back-to-back limit, where the observable factorizes into a hard function, jet functions describing collinear radiation within each jet, and a soft function capturing wide-angle soft emissions. They compute the one-loop jet function J_{N-1} for the jet not containing the special particle using QCD splitting functions, which is the only new ingredient needed for next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic (NNLL) resummation. For the non-perturbative corrections in the collinear limit, they generalize arguments from Lee (2024) and Schindler (2023) to characterize the dependence on N, center-of-mass energy Q, and angular scale x, and test their analytic predictions against the hadronization model in Pythia.

## Key numbers

- The computational cost for projected correlators using the new parametrization is reduced to O(M^2 ln M) independent of N, compared to the traditional O(M^N) scaling
- For N=3, differences between the new and traditional parametrization are at the few percent level in the perturbative collinear region and up to about 10% in the back-to-back regime, as shown using Pythia simulations at Q = 1 TeV
- The hard function at NLO is given by H(Q; mu) = 1 + (alpha_s(mu) C_F/pi)(-2 ln^2(mu/Q) - 3 ln(mu/Q) - 4 + (7/2) zeta_2)
- The soft function in Fourier space at NLO is S(b^2; mu, nu) = 1 + (alpha_s(mu) C_F/pi)[-(1/2) ln^2(b^2 mu^2 e^{2 gamma_E}/4) + ln(b^2 mu^2 e^{2 gamma_E}/4) ln(mu/nu) - 2 zeta_2]
- The cumulative distribution obeys the sum rule (1/sigma) Sigma^{[N]}(pi) = 2^N

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework previously developed for the energy-energy correlator (EEC) in the back-to-back limit by de Florian (2004), Tulipant (2017), Kardos (2018), Moult (2018), Ebert (2020), Duhr (2022), Aglietti (2024), Kang (2024), and Jaarsma (2025) to arbitrary N-point projected correlators. It also generalizes the non-perturbative power correction analysis of Lee (2024) and Schindler (2023) to arbitrary N and non-integer N<1. The new parametrization introduced by Alipour-Fard (2024) enables these generalizations by simplifying the phase-space structure. The results enable future alpha_s extractions with complementary systematics in the back-to-back regime, complementing the collinear-regime analysis by CMS (2024) that provided the most precise alpha_s determination from jet substructure.
