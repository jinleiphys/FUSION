# Energy dependence of pion in-medium effects on π^-/π^+ ratio in heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of a thermal model with its parameters fitted to the results from an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, we study the pion in-medium effect on the charged-pion ratio in heavy-ion collisions at various energies. We find that due to the cancellation between the effects from pion-nucleon s-wave and p-wave interactions in nuclear medium, the π^-/π^+ ratio generally decreases after including the pion in-medium effect. The effect is larger at lower collision energies as a result of narrower pion spectral functions at lower temperatures.

## Key claim

The pion in-medium effect generally reduces the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in heavy-ion collisions compared to using free pions, but this reduction is only about 4% at 200 AMeV due to cancellation between the s-wave interaction (which reduces the ratio) and the p-wave interaction (which increases it); the effect is larger at lower collision energies because of narrower pion spectral functions at lower temperatures.

## Method

The authors used a thermal model whose parameters (baryon density, isospin asymmetry, temperature) were fitted to results from the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model for central Au+Au collisions at 200 AMeV, 400 AMeV, and 1 AGeV. Pion spectral functions and Δ resonance mass distributions were calculated self-consistently including both pion-nucleon s-wave and p-wave interactions, and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio was computed with and without these in-medium effects for two different symmetry energy parametrizations (supersoft x=1 and stiff x=0).

## Key numbers

- At 200 AMeV with free pions: π⁻/π⁺ = 4.34 (x=1) and 4.05 (x=0); with in-medium effects: 4.15 (x=1) and 3.85 (x=0)
- At 400 AMeV with free pions: π⁻/π⁺ = 2.74 (x=1) and 2.63 (x=0); with in-medium effects: 2.70 (x=1) and 2.59 (x=0)
- At 1 AGeV with free pions: π⁻/π⁺ = 1.79 (x=1) and 1.76 (x=0); with in-medium effects: 1.80 (x=1) and 1.77 (x=0)
- Average baryon densities in the high-density phase: 1.3 ρ₀ at 200 AMeV, 1.5 ρ₀ at 400 AMeV, 1.8 ρ₀ at 1 AGeV (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- Temperatures from thermal model: 32.6 MeV (200 AMeV), 53.3 MeV (400 AMeV), 90.8 MeV (1 AGeV) for x=1
- At 200 AMeV, the s-wave interaction alone reduces the π⁻/π⁺ ratio by about 14%, the p-wave interaction alone increases it by about 15%, and the combined effect is a 4% reduction

## Context

This work extends the earlier thermal model study by Xu et al. (2010) which examined pion in-medium effects only at 400 AMeV, to a range of beam energies from 200 AMeV to 1 AGeV. The results address contradictory conclusions in the literature about the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy from π⁻/π⁺ ratios: Xiao et al. (2009) using IBUU favored a supersoft symmetry energy, while Feng et al. (2010) using an improved quantum molecular dynamics model required a stiff symmetry energy. The findings indicate that including isospin-dependent pion in-medium effects is important for quantitatively extracting the symmetry energy from pion ratios, especially at lower collision energies where both the symmetry energy sensitivity and the in-medium effects are largest.
