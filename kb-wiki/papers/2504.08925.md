# Universality of $SU(\infty)$ relaxation dynamics for $SU(n_f)$-symmetric spin-models

> Spin-models, where the $N$ spins interact pairwise with a $SU(n_f)$ symmetry preserving hamiltonian, famously simplify in the large $n_f$, $N$ limits, as derived by Sachdev and Ye when exploring mean-field behavior of spin-glasses. We present numerical evidence that for a large class of models, the large $n_f$ limit is not necessary: the same dynamical equations can describe the relaxation processes at high temperatures for a set of classical models inspired from mean-field treatments of interacting dense neutrino gases, up to times set by the radius of convergence of the perturbation series for the correlation function. After a simple rescaling of time, the dynamics display a surprising universality, being identical for any value of $n_f$ as long as the rank of the coupling matrix is small. As a corollary of our results, we find that the direct interaction approximation originating from the study of stochastic flows in fluid turbulence should be thought of as only a short-time approximation for generic random coupling systems.

## Key claim
The authors present numerical evidence that for a large class of SU(n_f)-symmetric spin models with rank-deficient coupling matrices, the short-time relaxation dynamics at high temperatures are governed by the same mean-field equations derived for the n_f → ∞ limit, regardless of the actual value of n_f, up to a time scale set by the radius of convergence of the perturbation series for the correlation function.

## Method
The authors numerically integrate the classical Landau-Lifshitz equations of motion for SU(2) and SU(3) spins with all-to-all random couplings, using two specific coupling matrix ensembles: a random orthogonal model with tunable rank and a neutrino-inspired model with rank 4. They compute the spin-spin autocorrelation function G_ss(t) at various temperatures above the phase transition, rescale time to match the direct interaction approximation (DIA) closure equations derived for the n_f → ∞ limit, and compare the numerical results to the DIA prediction up to the radius of convergence of its Taylor series.

## Key numbers
- The time scale where the DIA breaks down is α(β)t_th ≈ 1.78, determined from the radius of convergence of the Taylor series for the DIA correlation function.
- For the random orthogonal model with SU(2) symmetry, N=1500, and rank J=92, the phase transition occurs at β = 3/2 (inverse temperature).
- For the random orthogonal model with SU(3) symmetry, N=1000, and rank J=61, the phase transition occurs at β = 4.
- For the neutrino model with SU(2) symmetry, the phase transition occurs at β = 9.
- The large-N extrapolation gives α(β)t_δ = 1.8 ± 0.1 for linear rank scaling and 1.7 ± 0.1 for square-root rank scaling, with δ = 0.03.

## Context
This work extends the mean-field results of Sachdev and Ye (1993) for SU(n_f)-symmetric spin models to finite n_f values, building on the observation by Neill and Liu (2024) that rank-deficient coupling matrices suppress spin-glass behavior. The authors show that the direct interaction approximation (DIA), originally developed by Kraichnan (1959) for fluid turbulence, describes the short-time dynamics of these spin systems, but should be understood as only a short-time approximation for generic random coupling systems. The results enable future studies of relaxation time scales in dense neutrino systems, where the SU(n_f) symmetry is broken by matter backgrounds and mass-induced vacuum oscillations.
