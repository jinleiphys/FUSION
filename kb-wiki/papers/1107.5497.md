# Polyakov loops, Gross-Witten like point and Hagedorn states

> The phase transition for a finite volume system that incorporates the Polyakov loops and maintains the colorless state is explored using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The order parameter for Polyakov loops is demonstrated to signal the appearance of a transition for $SU(3)_{c}$ analogous to Gross-Witten (GW-) phase transition instead of the deconfinement phase transition to quark-gluon plasma. The asymptotic restoration of Polyakov loops is conjectured to be a threshold production for meta-stable Hagedorn (or semi-QGP) states and this does not imply a direct deconfinement phase transition. In this context, the GW-like point is the point where the colorless states switches from the low-lying hadronic states to the meta-stable high-lying Hagedorn states. The chiral phase transition takes place within an extended GW-like point depending on the fireball's size. The deconfinement phase transition is determined by Hagedorn's temperature above GW-like temperature.

## Key claim

The Polyakov loop order parameter signals a transition for SU(3)c analogous to the Gross-Witten phase transition, which is not a deconfinement phase transition to quark-gluon plasma but rather a threshold for producing meta-stable Hagedorn (or semi-QGP) states; the deconfinement phase transition occurs only at Hagedorn's temperature above the Gross-Witten-like temperature.

## Method

The authors use the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for a finite volume system that maintains the colorless state via the invariance Haar measure of SU(3)c. They evaluate the canonical ensemble using the non-Gaussian stationary points method for Polyakov loops in the strong coupling limit and the Gaussian saddle point approximation in the weak coupling limit, interpolating between the low-lying and high-lying energy solutions. The stationary points are found by extremizing the sum of the effective Polyakov VanderMonde potential and the quark-antiquark-gluon grand potential.

## Key numbers

* The UV-cutoff for momentum integration is taken as Lambda = 631.5 MeV in the calculations.
* For massless flavors at mu_q = 0, the quark-antiquark grand potential contains a term (7 pi^2 / 180 beta^4) N_f N_c for the Stefan-Boltzmann contribution.
* The gluon grand potential in the phenomenological form uses omega_g = (N_c^2 - 1) pi^2 / 45.
* When Polyakov loops vanish (Phi, anti-Phi -> 0), the gluon grand potential is reduced to -(1/beta^4) (1/N_c^2) (N_c^2 - 1) pi^2 / 45; when Polyakov loops are restored (Phi, anti-Phi -> 1), it becomes -(1/beta^4) (N_c^2 - 1) pi^2 / 45.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model approach of Fukushima (2003) and the subsequent studies by Ratti (2005), Roessner (2006), and others that compared PNJL results with lattice QCD data. It builds on the matrix saddle point method of Brezin, Itzykson, Parisi, and Zuber (1977) and the Gross-Witten transition (1980) from the large N_c limit, applying the concept to finite N_c = 3. The paper also connects to the work of Elze, Greiner, and Rafelski (1983-1986) on the non-perturbative effects of the colorless state leading to gradual freezing of internal degrees of freedom. This enables future investigation of the width of the phase transition and intermediate processes such as Hagedorn states, quarkyonic matter, and semi-classical QGP phases.
