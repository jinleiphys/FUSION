# Coriolis coupling effects in proton-pickup spectroscopic factors from $^{12}$B

> Spectroscopic factors to low-lying negative-parity states in $^{11}$Be extracted from the $^{12}$B($d$,$^3$He)$^{11}$Be proton-removal reaction are interpreted within the rotational model. Earlier predictions of the $p$-wave proton removal strengths in the strong coupling limit of the Nilsson model underestimated the spectroscopic factors to the $3/2^-_1$ and $5/2^-_1$ states and suggested that deviations in the $1^+$ ground state of the odd-odd $^{12}$B due to Coriolis coupling should be further explored. In this work we use the Particle Rotor Model to take into account these effects and obtain a good description of the level scheme in $^{11}$B, with a moderate $K$-mixing of the proton Nilsson levels [110]1/2 and [101]3/2. This mixing, present in the $1^+$ bandhead of $^{12}$B, is key to explaining the proton pickup data.

# Coriolis coupling effects in proton-pickup spectroscopic factors from $^{12}$B

## Key claim

Earlier Nilsson model predictions in the strong coupling limit underestimated the spectroscopic factors to the $3/2^-_1$ and $5/2^-_1$ states in $^{11}$Be; the Particle Rotor Model (PRM) incorporating Coriolis-induced $K$-mixing of the proton Nilsson levels [110]1/2 and [101]3/2 in the $1^+$ ground state of $^{12}$B resolves this discrepancy, though the PRM underestimates the measured quadrupole moment of $^{11}$B.

## Method

The authors applied the Particle Rotor Model (PRM) to describe the structure of $^{11}$B and $^{12}$B, using a $^{10}$Be core with deformation $\epsilon_2 \approx 0.45$ ($\beta_2 \approx 0.6$). They included three Nilsson orbits ([110]1/2, [101]3/2, [101]1/2) with pairing parameters from a BCS calculation ($G=1.9$ MeV, $\lambda=45.73$ MeV, $\Delta=3.3$ MeV) and a core moment of inertia $\mathscr{I}=0.57\ \hbar^2$/MeV. Spectroscopic factors for the $^{12}$B($d$,$^3$He)$^{11}$Be proton-removal reaction were then calculated using the Coriolis-mixed wavefunctions of the $^{12}$B ground state, with amplitudes $\mathscr{A}_1$, $\mathscr{A}_2$, and $\mathscr{A}_3$ fit to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Deformation $\epsilon_2 \approx 0.45$ ($\beta_2 \approx 0.6$) adopted for the $^{10}$Be core, where the [220]1/2 and [101]1/2 Nilsson levels cross
- Core moment of inertia $\mathscr{I}=0.57\ \hbar^2$/MeV, approximately 60% of the rigid body value
- Pairing gap $\Delta = 3.3$ MeV and Fermi level $\lambda = 45.73$ MeV from BCS calculation with coupling constant $G=1.9$ MeV
- PRM fit to $^{11}$B yrast negative-parity states gives $A=978$ keV, $B=-17$ keV, and $A_3 \approx 25$ keV from the Coriolis term
- Calculated magnetic moment of $^{11}$B ground state: $\mu_{3/2^-}=2.66\ \mu_N$ (experimental: $2.6886489(10)\ \mu_N$)
- PRM quadrupole moment $Q_{3/2^-}=0.028$ eb, smaller than the experimental value $0.04065(26)$ eb
- Fitted amplitudes for $^{12}$B $1^+$ ground state: $\mathscr{A}_1 = -0.60(3)$, $\mathscr{A}_2 = 0.70(3)$, $\mathscr{A}_3 = 0.40(4)$
- Relative spectroscopic factor for $^{12}$B($d$,$^3$He)$^{11}$Be to $3/2^-_1$: experimental $2.6(10)$, strong coupling $0.8$, PRM $2.6$
- Relative spectroscopic factor to $5/2^-_1$: experimental $1.7(6)$, strong coupling $0.2$, PRM $1.7$

## Context

This work extends the collective model approach previously applied by the same group (Macchiavelli et al., 2018-2020) to nuclei in the $N=8$ Island of Inversion, including $^{11}$Be and $^{12}$Be. It directly addresses the discrepancy noted in Chen (2020) where strong-coupling Nilsson predictions underestimated spectroscopic factors from the $^{12}$B($d$,$^3$He)$^{11}$Be reaction. The PRM results bring the collective model into agreement with shell-model and Variational Monte Carlo calculations discussed in that earlier work. The analysis enables future application of a two-particle-plus-rotor model to $^{12}$B and suggests that extending the ab initio no-core shell model study of Petr (2020) to spectroscopic factors would be valuable.
