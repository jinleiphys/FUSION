# Constituent quark model study of light- and strange-baryon spectra

> We investigate the structure of the SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons employing a constituent quark model designed for the study of the baryon-baryon interaction and successfully applied to the meson spectra. The model considers through the interacting potential perturbative, one-gluon exchange, and non-perturbative, boson exchanges and confinement, aspects of the underlying theory, QCD. We solve the three-quark problem by means of the Faddeev method in momentum space. We analyze the effect of the different terms in the interaction and make contact with the use of relativistic kinematics. We find an explanation to the strong contribution of the pseudoscalar forces in the semirelativistic approach for the octet baryons. A phenomenological recipe for the regularization parameter of the one-gluon exchange is found.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a constituent quark model incorporating one-gluon exchange, boson exchanges, and confinement can simultaneously describe the light- and strange-baryon spectra, but the authors hedge that the model parameters are mostly fixed from other observables (meson spectra and baryon-baryon interaction) rather than fitted to the baryon spectra themselves, and that the regularization parameter of the one-gluon exchange must be flavor-dependent to avoid collapse.

## Method

The authors solved the three-quark bound-state problem using the Faddeev method in momentum space, with the quark-quark interaction consisting of a screened confining potential, a regularized one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potential with flavor-dependent cutoff, and pseudoscalar (π, K, η) and scalar (σ) meson-exchange potentials derived from chiral symmetry breaking. For baryons with three identical quarks, they also used the hyperspherical harmonic expansion method up to K=24 for cross-check and to compute root mean square radii.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constant α_s is 0.54 for the light-quark sector, 0.49 for a light-strange pair, 0.44 for the strange sector, and 0.127 at the Z_0 mass.
- The regularization parameter r_0(μ) scales with reduced mass: for ĥr_0=0.35 fm, r_0^{nn}=0.35 fm, r_0^{ns}=0.28 fm, and r_0^{ss}=0.22 fm.
- Differences between the Faddeev method and hyperspherical harmonic expansion for 3q bound-state energies are at most 5 MeV.
- The reverse of level ordering between positive and negative parity excited states (e.g., Roper resonance below negative parity states) is obtained for a cutoff increase Λ_0 around 3.2 fm^{-1}, giving Λ_π=7.4 fm^{-1} and Λ_K=8.4 fm^{-1}.
- The decuplet-octet mass differences from pseudoscalar forces alone are of order 100-200 MeV, with the remaining splitting provided by OGE.
- For ĥr_0=0.1 fm, the N(1/2^+) and Ξ(1/2^+) masses decrease by 500-600 MeV relative to their asymptotic value.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model of Vijande et al. (2004), which was successfully applied to meson spectra and baryon-baryon interaction, to the full SU(3) octet and decuplet baryon spectra. It contrasts with models based solely on pseudoscalar boson exchanges (e.g., Glozman et al. 1996, Furui et al. 2002) that produce very small baryon radii and overly strong pseudoscalar forces; the inclusion of OGE in the present model reduces the pseudoscalar contribution and yields larger radii. The results enable future simultaneous studies of baryon spectra and baryon-baryon interaction within a single framework, and the flavor-dependent OGE regularization provides a recipe for other constituent quark model calculations.
