# New correlations induced by nuclear supersymmetry

> We show that the nuclear supersymmetry model (n-susy) in its extended version, predicts correlations in the nuclear structure matrix elements which characterize transfer reactions between nuclei that belong to the same supermultiplet. These correlations are related to the fermionic generators of the superalgebra and if verified experimentally can provide a direct test of the model.

## Key claim

The extended nuclear supersymmetry model predicts correlations between spectroscopic intensities of different one-nucleon transfer reactions within a supermultiplet, and these correlations are partially fulfilled by available experimental data but require more experimental work to confirm their full validity.

## Method

The authors used the fermionic generators of the superalgebra to construct transfer operators for one-proton and one-neutron reactions, deriving closed-form expressions for intensity ratios between different transfer channels within the quartet of nuclei (194Pt, 195Pt, 195Au, 196Au) described by the Uν(6/12)⊗Uπ(6/4) dynamical supersymmetry in its Spin(6) limit. For two-nucleon transfer, they applied the Glendenning formalism to calculate spectroscopic strengths for the reaction 198Hg(d,α)196Au, using a simple two-nucleon transfer operator with parameters determined by least-squares fitting to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For one-proton transfer 194Pt → 195Au, the ratio R2 = 1.12 for N = 5 bosons in the odd-odd nucleus
- For one-neutron transfer 194Pt → 195Pt, calculated ratio R(ee→oe) = 0.034 for the [N+2]⟨N+2,0,0⟩ state, compared to experimental values of 0.264 (j=3/2) and 0.052 (j=5/2)
- For one-neutron transfer 194Pt → 195Pt, calculated ratio R(ee→oe) = 0.148 for the [N+1,1]⟨N,0,0⟩ state, compared to experimental value 0.087 (j=3/2)
- For the inverse reaction 195Pt → 194Pt, calculated ratio R(oe→ee) = 0.511 for the [N+2]⟨N+2,0,0⟩ state
- For the inverse reaction 195Pt → 194Pt, calculated ratio R(oe→ee) = 0.498 for the [N+2]⟨N,0,0⟩ state

## Context

This work extends the extended nuclear supersymmetry model previously applied to the quartet of 194Pt, 195Pt, 195Au, and 196Au using the Spin(6) limit of Uν(6/12)⊗Uπ(6/4) (Barea, Bijker, Frank 2002; Metz et al. 1999). The correlations predicted for one-nucleon transfer reactions provide a direct experimental test of the supersymmetry framework, while the two-nucleon transfer analysis of 198Hg(d,α)196Au represents the first susy analysis of such reactions. The results enable future searches for other experimental examples where extended n-susy can be applied, potentially relaxing the constraints of dynamical symmetry as suggested by Frank, Van Isacker, and Warner.
