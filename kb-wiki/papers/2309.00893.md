# Electrical conductivity of warm neutron star crust in magnetic fields: Neutron-drip regime

> We compute the anisotropic electrical conductivity tensor of the inner crust of a compact star at non-zero temperature by extending a previous work on the conductivity of the outer crust. The physical scenarios, where such crust is formed, involve proto-neutron stars born in supernova explosions, binary neutron star mergers and accreting neutron stars. The temperature-density range studied covers the transition from a non-degenerate to a highly degenerate electron gas and assumes that the nuclei form a liquid, i.e., the temperature is above the melting temperature of the lattice of nuclei. The electronic transition probabilities include (a) the dynamical screening of electron-ion interaction in the hard-thermal-loop approximation for the QED plasma, (b) the correlations of the ionic component in a one-component plasma, and (c) finite nuclear size effects. The conductivity tensor is obtained from the Boltzmann kinetic equation in relaxation time approximation accounting for the anisotropies introduced by a magnetic field. The sensitivity of the results towards the matter composition of the inner crust is explored by using several compositions of the inner crust which were obtained using different nuclear interactions and methods of solving the many-body problem. The standard deviation of relaxation time and components of the conductivity tensor from the average are below $\le 10\%$ except close to crust-core transition, where non-spherical nuclear structures are expected. Our results can be used in dissipative magneto-hydrodynamics (MHD) simulations of warm compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper computes the anisotropic electrical conductivity tensor of the inner crust of a compact star at non-zero temperature, extending prior work on the outer crust, but the results apply only in the liquid regime where the temperature is above the melting temperature of the lattice of nuclei (T > T_m ≈ 1 MeV) and below the neutrino trapping temperature (T_tr ≈ 5 MeV), and assume spherical nuclei, excluding the pasta phases near the crust-core transition.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann kinetic equation for electrons in the relaxation time approximation, accounting for electron-ion collisions with a matrix element that includes dynamical screening of the electron-ion interaction via the hard-thermal-loop approximation for the QED plasma, ion-ion correlations via the structure factor of a one-component plasma, and finite nuclear size effects via a nuclear form factor. The conductivity tensor is obtained for five different zero-temperature compositions of inner crust matter (NV, D1M, D1M*, Bsk24, Sly9) across a density range from above neutron drip (4.3 × 10^11 g cm^-3) to the crust-core transition, for magnetic fields below B_c ≈ 10^14 G where Landau quantization is negligible.

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip density: ρ_drip = 4.3 × 10^11 g cm^-3
- Melting temperature of crustal lattice: T_m ≈ 1 MeV; neutrino trapping temperature: T_tr ≈ 5 MeV
- Magnetic field threshold below which Landau quantization is negligible: B_c ≈ 10^14 G
- Standard deviation of relaxation time and conductivity tensor components from the average across five compositions: ≤ 25% except near the crust-core transition
- Plasma coupling parameter for solidification: Γ_m ≈ 160
- Relaxation time scaling (neglecting form factor and structure factor effects): τ ∼ Z^{-4/3} n_i^{-1/3}
- Temperature range studied: T_m ≤ T ≤ T_tr, with T_m ≈ 1 MeV and T_tr ≈ 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the calculation of electrical conductivity from the outer crust (Harutyunyan 2016) to the inner crust where unbound neutrons are present, using the same formalism. The results are intended for use in dissipative magneto-hydrodynamics simulations of warm compact stars, including proto-neutron stars, binary neutron star mergers, and accreting neutron stars. The paper complements studies of conductivity in the solid regime at low temperatures (Itoh 1984) and studies of pasta phases (Pelicer 2023), and provides inputs for assessing the breakdown of ideal magnetohydrodynamics in binary neutron star mergers (Harutyunyan 2018).
