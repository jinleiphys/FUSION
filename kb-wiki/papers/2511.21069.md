# Simultaneous Inference of Effective Range Parameters and EFT Truncation Uncertainty in $^{3}$He-$α$ Scattering

> We extend previous halo effective field theory analyses of low-energy elastic scattering of $^{3}$He-$^{4}$He, including the $\frac{7}{2}^{-}$ $f$-wave resonance as an explicit degree of freedom. The presence of this resonance necessitates a changing power counting scheme depending on the kinematic region. Therefore, we construct a theory uncertainty model at the partial wave amplitude level, allowing us to generate a sophisticated theory covariance matrix that captures the way the theory error structure changes as energy increases. We then perform a Bayesian analysis and simultaneously estimate the joint posterior distributions of the effective range theory parameters and the parameters that characterize the effective field theory truncation uncertainty. We compare two different analyses: no $f$-wave interactions for data up to $E_{\text{max}} = 2.6$ MeV, and including $f$-wave interactions for data up to $E_{\text{max}} = 5.5$ MeV. The inferred breakdown scales in each analysis are consistent with previous work. We find that $f$-wave interactions are needed to describe data for $E_{lab} \gtrapprox 3.6$ MeV.

## Key claim
The paper extends previous halo EFT analyses of low-energy $^{3}$He-$^{4}$He elastic scattering by including the $\frac{7}{2}^{-}$ $f$-wave resonance as an explicit degree of freedom, which requires a changing power counting scheme depending on the kinematic region (below the resonance window versus within the resonance window). The authors simultaneously estimate the joint posterior distributions of effective range theory parameters and EFT truncation uncertainty parameters, finding that $f$-wave interactions are needed to describe data for $E_{lab} \gtrapprox 3.6$ MeV.

## Method
The authors constructed a halo effective field theory Lagrangian including $s$-wave, $p$-wave, and $f$-wave dimer fields, with the $\frac{7}{2}^{-}$ resonance included explicitly and the $\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ channel included by fitting its effective range and scattering length to Boykin (1972) phase shifts. They developed a theory covariance matrix at the partial wave amplitude level that captures the angular dependence of the cross section and defines model uncertainty in two different regions (below and within the resonance window). A Bayesian analysis was performed to simultaneously sample the model parameters and EFT truncation uncertainty parameters, comparing two analyses: one without $f$-waves for data up to $E_{\text{max}} = 2.6$ MeV, and one including $f$-waves for data up to $E_{\text{max}} = 5.5$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- EFT breakdown scale without $f$-waves: $\Lambda_{B} = 0.88^{+0.24}_{-0.22} \text{ fm}^{-1}$ for data up to $E_{\text{max}} = 2.6$ MeV
- EFT breakdown scale with $f$-waves: $\Lambda_{B} = 0.90^{+0.24}_{-0.21} \text{ fm}^{-1}$ for data up to $E_{\text{max}} = 5.5$ MeV
- The $\frac{7}{2}^{-}$ resonance occurs at momentum $k_{R} = 0.495 \text{ fm}^{-1}$, corresponding to energy $E_{R} = 2.983$ MeV above threshold (Tilley 2002)
- The $\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ resonance is at 5.143 MeV above threshold (Tilley 2002)
- Beam energies in the Paneru dataset range from 0.7 to 5.5 MeV, with 10 energy bins (one measured twice at 2.6 MeV)
- Angular coverage of detectors: $30^{\circ} < \theta_{cm} < 139^{\circ}$
- MAP values for key ERPs in the full $f$-wave analysis: $1/a_{0} = 0.018 \text{ fm}^{-1}$, $r_{0} = 0.868 \text{ fm}$, $r_{3}^{+} = 0.674 \text{ fm}^{-5}$, $P_{3}^{+} = -4.268 \text{ fm}^{-3}$

## Context
This work extends the halo EFT analysis of Poudel and Phillips (2022), who analyzed $^{3}$He-$^{4}$He elastic scattering but included the $\frac{7}{2}^{-}$ resonance via a phenomenological $R$-matrix method that did not provide clean uncertainty quantification. The present work directly includes the resonance in the EFT framework and develops a sophisticated uncertainty model, enabling simultaneous inference of physics parameters and truncation uncertainties. The methodology can be applied to other reactions of interest, with a natural next step being the $^{7}$Be + $p$ reaction in the $pp$-II chain, and future work could include Barnard (1964) scattering data and $^{3}$He$(\alpha, \gamma)$ capture data to better constrain effective range parameters and predict the astrophysical $S$-factor $S_{34}(0)$.
