# Zone Determinant Expansions for Nuclear Lattice Simulations

> We introduce a new approximation to nucleon matrix determinants that is physically motivated by chiral effective theory. The method involves breaking the lattice into spatial zones and expanding the determinant in powers of the boundary hopping parameter.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a zone determinant expansion that approximates nucleon matrix determinants by breaking the lattice into spatial zones and expanding in powers of the boundary hopping parameter; the method is physically motivated by chiral effective theory, which suggests that spatial hopping of nucleons appears at subleading order and can therefore be treated as a perturbation, provided the temperature is high enough to regulate infrared singularities.

## Method

The authors partition the spatial lattice into zones whose size is chosen to be much larger than the nucleon localization length \(l\sim\sqrt{\beta h}\). The nucleon matrix \(M\) is decomposed into a block-diagonal part \(M_0\) (containing only intra-zone hopping) and an off-diagonal part \(M_E\) (containing inter-zone hopping). The determinant is then expanded as \(\det(M)=\det(M_0)\exp\bigl(\sum_{p=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{p-1}}{p}\operatorname{tr}((M_0^{-1}M_E)^p)\bigr)\), and the expansion is truncated at finite order \(m\). The spectral radius \(R\) of \(M_0^{-1}M_E\) controls convergence, with rigorous bounds given by Ipsen (2003). For bipartite zone partitionings, odd-order terms vanish, giving an extra order of accuracy for free.

## Key numbers

* Localization length estimate: \(l\sim\sqrt{\beta h}=0.57\) (dimensionless lattice units) at \(T=37.5\) MeV and lattice spacing \(a^{-1}=150\) MeV.
* Spectral radius \(R\) for \([1,1,1]\) zones at \(T=37.5\) MeV and density \(\rho=0.57\rho_{\text{nucl}}\): 0.538, 0.523, 0.534 for three independent configurations.
* Spectral radius \(R\) for \([1,1,1]\) zones at \(T=37.5\) MeV and density \(\rho=1.67\rho_{\text{nucl}}\): 0.520, 0.478, 0.536 for three independent configurations.
* Spectral radius \(R\) for \([1,1,1]\) zones at varying temperature (fixed chemical potential \(\mu=0.8m_N\)): 0.5122 at 37.5 MeV, 0.6742 at 25.0 MeV, 0.7631 at 18.8 MeV, 0.8638 at 12.5 MeV.
* Spectral radius \(R\) for different zone sizes on a \(6^3\times6\) lattice at \(T=37.5\) MeV: 0.5122 for \([1,1,1]\), 0.3013 for \([2,2,2]\), 0.3014 for \([3,3,3]\).
* Empirical convergence rate: \(\frac{|\det(M)-\Delta_{2m}|}{|\Delta_{2m}|}\sim R^{2m+1}\).
* Lattice parameters: \(a^{-1}=150\) MeV, \(a_t^{-1}=225\) MeV, \(C=-4.0\times10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), \(g=6.8\times10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) (from Goldberger-Treiman relation).

## Context

This work extends the zone method previously applied to the chiral condensate in massless QED in three dimensions (Lee, 2003) to the problem of nucleon matrix determinants in nuclear lattice simulations. It addresses the computational bottleneck identified by Muller, Koonin, Seki, and van Kolck (1999), whose LU-factorization-based simulations scaled as \((V\beta)^3\) and were limited to \(4^3\) spatial volumes. The zone expansion enables simulations on larger lattices by replacing the costly full determinant calculation with a product of smaller submatrix determinants plus a perturbative correction; the authors note that a full account of large-volume results and the combined chiral-effective-theory/lattice formalism is forthcoming (Borasoy, 2003).
