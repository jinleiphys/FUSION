# Behavior of the collective rotor in nuclear chiral motion

> The behavior of the collective rotor in the chiral motion of triaxially deformed nuclei is investigated using the particle rotor model by transforming the wave functions from the $K$-representation to the $R$-representation. After examining the energy spectra of the doublet bands and their energy differences as functions of the triaxial deformation, the angular momentum components of the rotor, proton, neutron, and the total system are investigated. Moreover, the probability distributions of the rotor angular momentum ($R$-plots) and their projections onto the three principal axes ($K_R$-plots) are analyzed. The evolution of the chiral mode from a chiral vibration at the low spins to a chiral rotation at high spins is illustrated at triaxial deformations $γ=20^\circ$ and $30^\circ$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the transition from chiral vibration to chiral rotation in triaxially deformed nuclei is driven by the increase of the rotor angular momentum along the intermediate axis, and that this evolution can be visualized through probability distributions of the rotor angular momentum (R-plots and K_R-plots) obtained by transforming particle rotor model wave functions from the K-representation to the R-representation.

## Method

The particle rotor model (PRM) Hamiltonian for a system of one h_{11/2} proton particle and one h_{11/2} neutron hole coupled to a triaxial rigid rotor was diagonalized in the strong-coupling basis (K-representation). The resulting wave functions were then transformed to the R-representation using an orthogonal transformation based on Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and Wigner functions, allowing calculation of probability distributions for the rotor angular momentum R and its projections K_R onto the three principal axes. Calculations were performed for triaxial deformation parameters gamma from 0 degrees to 30 degrees with quadrupole deformation beta = 0.23 and moments of inertia of irrotational flow type with J_0 = 30 hbar^2/MeV.

## Key numbers

- Energy difference at I=20 hbar decreases from about 1.0 MeV to about 0.3 MeV when gamma is varied from 10 degrees to 20 degrees
- At gamma=30 degrees, energy differences between doublet bands become less than 200 keV in the spin region 12 hbar to 20 hbar
- Very good degeneracy and condition for chiral rotation occurs at gamma approximately 23 degrees for I=15 hbar
- At gamma=20 degrees, for I greater than or equal to 13 hbar, the R-plots for yrast and yrare bands are quite similar with energy differences less than 300 keV
- At gamma=30 degrees, the R-plots of yrast and yrare bands become very similar for I greater than or equal to 14 hbar
- At gamma=30 degrees, the K_Ri distributions show chiral vibration for I less than or equal to 12 hbar and chiral rotation for I greater than or equal to 13 hbar, with indistinguishable distributions in the spin region 15 hbar to 17 hbar

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of nuclear chirality that primarily calculated expectation values of rotor angular momentum components (Zhang 2007, Qi 2009, Lawrie 2010, Chen 2010, Hamamoto 2013) by providing detailed wave function information through R-plots and K_R-plots. The technique was previously applied to wobbling motion in 135Pr (Streck 2018) and is here extended to chiral bands based on a two-quasiparticle configuration. The results confirm the established picture that chiral rotation exists only above a critical spin I (Olbratowski 2004, Olbratowski 2006, Zhao 2017, Grodner 2018, Chen 2018) and enable detailed comparison between the rotor behavior in chiral vibration versus chiral rotation regimes.
