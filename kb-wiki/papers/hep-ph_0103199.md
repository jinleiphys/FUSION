# Muon and tau anomalies updated

> We present a new independent evaluation of the hadronic vacuum polarization contributions a_l{had}(l.o)=(1/2)(g-2)_l{had}(l.o) to the anomalous magnetic moment (anomaly) of the muon and tau leptons using tau-decays and e+e- data. The alone theoretical input used for describing the high-energy region not accessible experimentally is perturbative QCD plus (negligible) additional effects due to the QCD vacuum condensates. We obtain: a_mu{had}(l.o)= 7020.6(75.6)10^{-11} and a_tau{had}(l.o)= 353.6(3.8)10^{-8}, which we compare with previous determinations. Our analysis leads to the Standard Model (SM) prediction: a_mu^{SM}=116~591~846.9(78.9)10^{-11}.Confronting a_mu^{SM} with the recent BNL measurement leads to a_mu{new}= a_mu{exp}-a_mu{SM}=176(170)10^{-11}. Combined with the mean existing determinations of a_mu^{had}(l.o), this leads to the conservative range: -42 < a_mu{new}10^{11} < 413 at 90% CL, from which we derive conservative lower bounds on the scales of some new physics. We also update our old predictions of the SM contributions to a_tau. Including QED to sixth order, higher order hadronic and electroweak contributions, we obtain a_tau{SM}=117 755(7)10^{-8}, waiting for a future precise measurement of a_tau.

## Key claim

The paper presents an independent evaluation of the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon and tau anomalous magnetic moments using tau-decay and e+e- data, finding that the difference between the experimental muon anomaly and the Standard Model prediction is 176(170) x 10^{-11}, which indicates only about a one-sigma window for new physics rather than the 2.6-sigma deviation claimed elsewhere.

## Method

The author uses a dispersion relation to relate the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the lepton anomaly to the integral over the e+e- -> hadrons total cross-section multiplied by a QED kernel function. For the low-energy region, the analysis employs the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis to relate tau-decay data from ALEPH and OPAL to the electromagnetic spectral function, supplemented by e+e- data for the isoscalar channel. The high-energy region not accessible experimentally is described using perturbative QCD with negligible corrections from QCD vacuum condensates, including light quark mass effects, tachyonic gluon contributions, and dimension-four non-perturbative corrections.

## Key numbers

* a_mu^{had}(l.o) = 7020.6(75.6) x 10^{-11} (final estimate from combined tau-decay and e+e- data)
* a_tau^{had}(l.o) = 353.6(3.8) x 10^{-8} (hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to tau anomaly)
* a_mu^{SM} = 116,591,846.9(78.9) x 10^{-11} (full Standard Model prediction for muon anomaly)
* a_mu^{new} = a_mu^{exp} - a_mu^{SM} = 176(170) x 10^{-11} (difference between experiment and Standard Model)
* Conservative 90% CL range: -42 < a_mu^{new} x 10^{11} < 413
* a_tau^{SM} = 117,755(7) x 10^{-8} (Standard Model prediction for tau anomaly)
* a_mu^{had}(l.o) from e+e- data alone: 7015.7(119.1) x 10^{-11}
* Light isovector contribution (4m_pi^2 to 0.8 GeV^2): 4794.6(60.7) x 10^{-11} from tau+e+e- data
* QCD continuum contribution (3 GeV^2 to infinity): 522.8(2.4) x 10^{-11}

## Context

This work updates the author's earlier estimate from Calmet et al. (1977) and Narison (1978), and responds to criticisms by Yndurain regarding the claimed 2.6-sigma deviation of the muon anomaly from the Standard Model. The results are compared with determinations by Eidelman-Jegerlehner (1995), Alemany-Davier-Hocker (1997), and Davier-Hocker (1998), finding general agreement within errors. The analysis provides conservative lower bounds on the scales of new physics such as supersymmetry, and establishes that improved measurements of the rho-meson region below 0.8 GeV^2 and of inclusive tau-decay near M_tau are needed to reduce the dominant uncertainties before a definitive claim of new physics can be made.
