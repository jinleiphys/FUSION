# Spurious Isospin Breaking in the In-medium Similarity Renormalization Group

> Robustly quantifying the uncertainty in the isospin-related theoretical correction $δ_C$ to superallowed beta decay rates is vital for a correct assessment of CKM unitarity. To this end, we identify the sources of artificial or \textit{spurious} isospin symmetry breaking introduced by the IMSRG many-body framework at a computational level and provide remedies. We test our best policy for preventing spurious ISB by evaluating $δ_C$.

## Key claim

The In-medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IMSRG) framework introduces artificial (spurious) isospin symmetry breaking at the computational level, which must be identified and remedied to reliably quantify the isospin-related theoretical correction δ_C for superallowed beta decay and thus correctly assess CKM unitarity.

## Method

The authors computed spurious isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) by evaluating the commutator of the Hamiltonian H with the squared isospin operator T^2, decomposed into one-body and two-body contributions, for the isospin-averaged DeltaGO interaction in both Hartree-Fock and harmonic oscillator bases. They tested multiple generator choices (White, imaginary, SMW, SMit, and their isospin-conserving "Iso" variants) within the IMSRG(3f2) framework and the Magnus formulation, using complete decoupling with IsoAv generators at oscillator parameter ℏω=16, cLS=-2, and e_max=3.

## Key numbers

- For 14O in the Hartree-Fock basis with the SR White (EN) generator, the spurious ISB measure ⟨T^2⟩-2 was 0.0087 at s=0 and 0.0026 at s→∞; the Iso White (EN) variant reduced this to 0.0074 at s=0 and 0.0021 at s→∞.
- For 14O in the harmonic oscillator basis with the SR White (EN) generator, ⟨T^2⟩-2 was 0.000e+00 at s=0 and 0.0024 at s→∞; the Iso White (EN) variant gave 0.0032 at s→∞.
- For 14N in the Hartree-Fock basis with the SR White (EN) generator, ⟨T^2⟩-3/4 was 1.332e-15 at s=0 and -5.688e-10 at s→∞; the Iso White (EN) variant gave 1.332e-15 at both s=0 and s→∞.
- For 14N in the harmonic oscillator basis with the SR White (EN) generator, ⟨T^2⟩-3/4 was 0.000e+00 at s=0 and -5.170e-10 at s→∞; the Iso White (EN) variant gave 0.000e+00 at both s=0 and s→∞.
- The two-body commutator [η, T^2]_2B for 14O in the Hartree-Fock basis with SR White (EN) was 3.0895 at s=0 and 256.3086 at s→∞; the Iso White (EN) variant reduced this to 0.1161 at s=0 and 58.5530 at s→∞.

## Context

This work extends the program of quantifying the isospin-breaking correction δ_C for superallowed beta decays, which is critical for testing CKM unitarity in the Standard Model. The authors identify and quantify spurious ISB introduced by the IMSRG framework itself, complementing earlier work by Towner and Hardy (2008) on nuclear-structure-dependent corrections. The results enable more reliable uncertainty quantification for δ_C by providing a best-policy approach to prevent artificial isospin breaking in IMSRG calculations, which can then be applied to the full set of superallowed emitters.
