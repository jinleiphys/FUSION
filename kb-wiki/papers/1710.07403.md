# Topology and symmetry of surface Majorana arcs in cyclic superconductors

> We study the topology and symmetry of surface Majorana arcs in superconductors with nonunitary "cyclic" pairing. Cyclic $p$-wave pairing may be realized in a cubic or tetrahedral crystal, while it is a candidate for the interior $^3P_2$ superfluids of neutron stars. The cyclic state is an admixture of full gap and nodal gap with eight Weyl points and the low-energy physics is governed by itinerant Majorana fermions. We here show the evolution of surface states from Majorana cone to Majorana arcs under rotation of surface orientation. The Majorana cone is protected solely by an accidental spin rotation symmetry and fragile against spin-orbit coupling, while the arcs are attributed to two topological invariants: the first Chern number and one-dimensional winding number. Lastly, we discuss how topologically protected surface states inherent to the nonunitary cyclic pairing can be captured from surface probes in candidate compounds, such as U$_{1-x}$Th$_{x}$Be$_{13}$. We examine tunneling conductance spectra for two competitive scenarios in U$_{1-x}$Th$_{x}$Be$_{13}$---the degenerate $E_u$ scenario and the accidental scenario.

## Key claim

The paper shows that surface Majorana arcs in cyclic superconductors are protected by two distinct topological invariants (the first Chern number and a one-dimensional winding number), while the Majorana cone is protected only by an accidental spin rotation symmetry and is fragile against spin-orbit coupling; this distinction is glossed over in the abstract's claim that both surface states are "topologically protected."

## Method

The authors use the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) formalism and quasiclassical theory to compute the surface density of states and tunneling conductance for cyclic p-wave (E_u) superconductors. They analyze the topological invariants, the first Chern number and a one-dimensional winding number associated with mirror reflection symmetry, and study how surface states evolve from a Majorana cone to Majorana arcs as the surface orientation is rotated relative to the crystal axes.

## Key numbers

- The cyclic state has eight Weyl points, each with monopole charge q_m = ±1, located at the vertices of a tetrahedron (e.g., one at k_0 = k_F(1,1,1)/√3).
- The superconducting transition temperature for U_1-xTh_xBe_13 is T_c ~ 0.85 K at x = 0, with a local maximum of T_c1(x) at x ~ 0.03.
- The effective low-energy Hamiltonian around a Weyl point is given by a Weyl-type Hamiltonian with velocity tensor v^a_b = diag(Δ̄/k_F, Δ̄/k_F, qv_F), where Δ̄ = Δω².

## Context

This work extends the study of topological surface states in nonunitary superconductors, specifically the cyclic p-wave (E_u) state, which may be realized in cubic or tetrahedral crystals and in the ³P₂ superfluids of neutron stars. It contrasts with prior work on cyclic d-wave (E_g) states by Ishikawa (2013), where the topology of surface Fermi arcs differs, and with studies of nematic phases in ³P₂ superfluids by Mizushima (2017). The results enable surface probe experiments (e.g., tunneling conductance) to distinguish between competing scenarios for U_1-xTh_xBe_13, such as the degenerate E_u scenario and the accidental scenario proposed by Sigrist (1989) and Shimizu (2017).
