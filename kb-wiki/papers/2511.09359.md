# Nuclear surface energy solving Hartree-Fock equations with Gogny interactions using Lagrange mesh

> Hartree Fock equations for finite range interactions in a slab of nuclear matter are presented and solved using an algorithm based on the Lagrange mesh method. This approach is faster and more efficient than the Numerov algorithm commonly used in the literature. Thanks to the improved numerical accuracy, we were able to perform calculations with sufficiently large boxes to minimize the impact of Friedel oscillations on the final results, achieving a precision on the surface energy within a few dozens of keV. Results are presented for several Gogny interactions that have not been previously discussed. In addition, the inclusion of the spin orbit term is examined, showing a net reduction of 1.2-1.9 MeV in the surface energy.

## Key claim

The authors present a new numerical method (Lagrange mesh) for solving Hartree-Fock equations in semi-infinite nuclear matter with finite-range Gogny interactions, claiming it is faster and more accurate than the previously used Numerov algorithm. The precision on the surface energy is within a few tens of keV, but the results depend on averaging over box sizes and integration points to minimize the impact of Friedel oscillations.

## Method

The authors solve the Hartree-Fock integro-differential equations for a slab of nuclear matter in a box of size \([-L,L]\) along the \(z\)-direction, using a Lagrange-Fourier basis to expand the single-particle wavefunctions. The Fock potential is evaluated directly at grid points without numerical integration, and the resulting matrix equation is solved by diagonalization. The surface energy coefficient \(a_s\) is extracted from the energy density by subtracting the infinite nuclear matter contribution and averaging over box sizes from 26 fm to 46 fm.

## Key numbers

- Surface energy coefficient for D1 interaction: \(19.74 \pm 0.02\) MeV (with spin-orbit), \(20.92 \pm 0.02\) MeV (without spin-orbit)
- Surface energy coefficient for D1S interaction: \(17.91 \pm 0.01\) MeV (with spin-orbit), \(19.35 \pm 0.02\) MeV (without spin-orbit)
- Surface energy coefficient for D1M interaction: \(18.14 \pm 0.01\) MeV (with spin-orbit), \(19.44 \pm 0.01\) MeV (without spin-orbit)
- Surface energy coefficient for D1P interaction: \(17.06 \pm 0.02\) MeV (with spin-orbit), \(18.97 \pm 0.02\) MeV (without spin-orbit)
- Inclusion of the spin-orbit term reduces the surface energy by 1.2-1.9 MeV across all interactions
- For D1 interaction, the saturation density is \(\rho_0 = 0.166\) fm\(^{-3}\) and the energy per particle at saturation is \(-16.30\) MeV
- The typical length scale of Friedel oscillations is \(\pi/k_F \simeq 2.3\) fm at saturation density
- Numerical accuracy below 50 keV requires at least \(N_{GL}=64\) integration points and boxes larger than 30 fm

## Context

This work extends the semi-infinite nuclear matter calculations of Cote and Pearson (1978) and Davesne et al. (2023), who used the Numerov method with a localization procedure for the Fock potential. The new Lagrange mesh method overcomes convergence problems that occurred for interactions like D3G3 where strong cancellation between Hartree and Fock terms caused the previous algorithm to break down. The results enable a systematic study of fission barriers for Gogny interactions analogous to the analysis performed for Skyrme forces by Ryssens et al. (2019), who showed that surface energy coefficients larger than 20 MeV lead to overestimated fission barriers.
