# Nuclear $β$- and $γ$- Collective Bands in the SU$_q$(2) Rotator Model

> The SU$_q$(2) rotator model is used for describing the $β_1$- and $γ_1$-bands of even-even rare earth and actinide collective nuclei. Good results are obtained in nuclei with valence pair number $N>10$. It is shown that in the excited bands the violation of the exact SU(2) symmetry is generally stronger than in the ground state bands, indicating the presence of a nonadiabatic perturbation caused by the excited vibrational degrees of freedom. The physical content of the parameter $q$ is discussed. Predictions of the SU$_q$(2) model for B(E2) intraband transitions in excited bands are presented and the need for specific experimental data is pointed out.

# Nuclear β- and γ- Collective Bands in the SU_q(2) Rotator Model

## Key claim
The SU_q(2) rotator model successfully describes β₁- and γ₁-bands of even-even rare earth and actinide nuclei, but only for nuclei with valence pair number N > 10. The deformation parameter τ is systematically larger in excited bands than in ground state bands, indicating that the q-deformation "detects" the presence of additional nonrotational (vibrational) degrees of freedom as a nonadiabatic perturbation.

## Method
The authors fit the SU_q(2) energy formula E_I = (1/2θ) sin(τI) sin(τ(I+1)) / sin²(τ) + E₀ to experimental level energies of β₁-bands (K=0, bandhead I=0) and γ₁-bands (K=2, bandhead I=2) for 28 rare earth and 3 actinide nuclei, using an autoregularized Gauss-Newton method. They then compute q-deformed B_q(E2) transition probabilities using q-generalized Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for intraband transitions with ΔI=2 and ΔI=1.

## Key numbers
- τ values for β₁- and γ₁-bands lie in the region 0.03–0.07, close to typical ground state band τ_g values of 0.03–0.06, but systematically shifted above the corresponding gsb values for almost all nuclei considered.
- Some τ_γ values reach ≥ 0.1 for nuclei with valence pair number N = 10–13 (e.g., ¹⁵⁴Sm: τ_γ = 0.1306; ¹⁵⁸Gd: τ_γ = 0.1345; ¹⁶⁰Er: τ_γ = 0.1158; ¹⁸²W: τ_γ = 0.1140).
- Fit quality σ ranges from 0.07 keV (¹⁶⁸Er γ-band, n=7) to 22.57 keV (¹⁵²Sm β-band, n=8); inertial parameters 1/(2θ) range from 6.92 keV (²³⁴U β-band) to 22.53 keV (¹⁶⁰Er γ-band).
- The ground state band τ_g follows the approximate relation τ = √3 (8N² + 22N − 15)^(−1/2), and a similar decreasing trend with increasing N is observed for τ_γ in Er and Yb isotopes.

## Context
This work extends the SU_q(2) rotator model, previously applied only to ground state bands (Raychev et al. 1990; Bonatsos et al. 1990, 1992), to excited β₁- and γ₁-bands for the first time. The deformation parameter τ had been connected to the softness parameter of the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model (Mariscotti et al. 1969) and to the axial deformation parameter β (Minkov et al. 1995). The results show that SU_q(2) is a one-band model limited to a single intrinsic state, motivating future development of a SU_q(3) model with bandmixing, though such an extension remains complicated due to difficulties with the SU_q(3) ⊃ SO_q(3) reduction (Quesne 1993; Van der Jeugt 1993). The predictions for B_q(E2) transitions in excited bands identify specific experimental data needed for testing, particularly ΔI=1 transitions in γ-bands at I > 10–12.
