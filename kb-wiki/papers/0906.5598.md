# Meson-exchange currents and final-state interactions in quasielastic electron scattering at high momentum transfers

> The effects of meson-exchange currents (MEC) are computed for the one-particle one-hole transverse response function for finite nuclei at high momentum transfers $q$ in the region of the quasielastic peak. A semi-relativistic shell model is used for the one-particle-emission $(e,e')$ reaction. Relativistic effects are included using relativistic kinematics, performing a semi-relativistic expansion of the current operators and using the Dirac-equation-based (DEB) form of the relativistic mean field potential for the final states. It is found that final-state interactions (FSI) produce an important enhancement of the MEC in the high-energy tail of the response function for $q\geq 1$ GeV/c. The combined effect of MEC and FSI goes away when other models of the FSI, not based on the DEB potential, are employed.

## Key claim

Meson-exchange currents (MEC) produce a significant enhancement of the transverse response in the high-energy tail for momentum transfers \(q \geq 1\) GeV/c, but this effect only appears when final-state interactions (FSI) are modeled with the Dirac-equation-based (DEB) potential; the combined MEC and FSI enhancement disappears when other FSI models, such as a Woods-Saxon potential, are used.

## Method

The authors computed the one-particle one-hole (1p-1h) transverse response function for quasielastic electron scattering on finite nuclei using a semi-relativistic (SR) shell model. Relativistic kinematics and a semi-relativistic expansion of the current operators were employed, with hole states described by a Woods-Saxon potential and final-state continuum states described using the Dirac-equation-based (DEB) form of the relativistic mean field potential. The MEC contributions included seagull, pion-in-flight, and \(\Delta\)-current diagrams, with a dynamical (energy-dependent) pion propagator used in the calculations.

## Key numbers

- At \(q = 500\) MeV/c, destructive interference between 1-body and 2-body contributions yields a 12% reduction of the total transverse response.
- At \(q = 1\) GeV/c, the reduction rises to about 20%.
- For \(q = 1.5\) GeV/c, the MEC contribution changes sign for transferred energies \(\omega \simeq 1\) GeV and above, producing a plateau-like shape in the transverse response.
- The MEC bump in the high-energy tail is only observed when using the DEB potential for final states; calculations with a Woods-Saxon potential show negligible MEC effects in the same kinematic region.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of MEC in quasielastic electron scattering, which had previously been performed for low-to-intermediate momentum transfers (Amaro 2002, Amaro 2003, Alberico 1989) and within a relativistic Fermi gas model (Amaro 2001, Amaro 2003). The results confirm MEC as an important source of scaling violations in the transverse channel at high momentum transfers, complementing earlier scaling analyses (Donnelly 1999, Sick 1999) that demonstrated scaling in the longitudinal channel. The findings have implications for neutrino reaction studies (Umino 1995), where these MEC contributions must also be included.
