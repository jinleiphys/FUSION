# Anatomy of nuclear matter fundamentals

> The bridge between finite and infinite nuclear system is analyzed for the fundamental quantities like binding energy, density, compressibility, giant monopole excitation energy and effective mass of both nuclear matter and finite nuclei systems. It is shown quantitatively that by knowing one of the fundamental property of one system one can estimate the same in its counter part, only approximately

## Key claim

The paper shows that while binding energy, compressibility, and other fundamental quantities of finite nuclei can be approximately related to those of infinite nuclear matter at the same density, the connection is only approximate and there is no exact route to derive one from the other; the leptodermous expansion for compressibility and mass formulae have no physical merit for predictions in unknown nuclear territory.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the Boguta-Bodmer Lagrangian including nonlinear sigma-meson couplings and cross coupling between rho and omega mesons, employing the FSUGold parameter set. They calculate nuclear matter properties (binding energy per nucleon, compressibility modulus, asymmetry coefficient) as functions of density using both quantal Hartree and semi-classical relativistic Thomas-Fermi (RTF) and relativistic extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) approximations, and compare these with the same quantities evaluated for finite nuclei (40P, 40S, 40Ca, 112Sn, 116Sn, 120Sn, 124Sn, 208Pb) using scaling and constrained calculations for giant monopole resonance energies.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter compressibility K_infinity ranges from approximately 210 MeV (NL1 parameter set) to 400 MeV (NL2 parameter set), with FSUGold at 230 MeV, NL3 at 271 MeV, NL-SH at 355 MeV, and NL3* at 258 MeV.
* For 208Pb, the sum rule weight factor sqrt(m1/m_{-1}) for giant monopole resonance is 13.44 MeV (RETF calculation) compared to the experimental value of 13.5 +/- 0.1 MeV.
* The finite nucleus compressibility K_A for 208Pb is 147.37 MeV (scaling calculation) and 134.57 MeV (constrained calculation), while the nuclear matter compressibility at the finite nucleus density (0.0990 fm^-3) is 138.42 MeV.
* The binding energy per nucleon for 40Ca is 10.589 MeV (finite nucleus) versus 12.997 MeV (nuclear matter at density 0.0780 fm^-3).

## Context

This work extends the correlation studies between finite and infinite nuclear systems previously explored by Mario (2009) on density connections, and by Blaizot (1980) on the leptodermous expansion of compressibility. The paper contradicts the assumption that leptodermous expansions for binding energy (as in the mass formula of Meyers) and compressibility can reliably predict properties of nuclei away from the beta-stability line, a failure previously noted by Mittig and confirmed by Satpathy (2012). The results enable a more cautious approach to using nuclear matter properties for finite nucleus predictions, particularly for neutron-rich systems and giant monopole resonances.
