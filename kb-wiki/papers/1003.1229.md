# Description of nuclear octupole and quadrupole deformation close to the axial symmetry: Octupole vibrations in the X(5) nuclei 150Nd and 152Sm

> The model, introduced in a previous paper, for the description of the octupole and quadrupole degrees of freedom in conditions close to the axial symmetry, is used to describe the negative-parity band based on the first octupole vibrational state in nuclei close to the critical point of the U(5) to SU(3) phase transition. The situation of 150Nd and 152Sm is discussed in detail. The positive parity levels of these nuclei, and also the in-band E2 transitions, are reasonably accounted for by the X(5) model. With simple assumptions on the nature of the octupole vibrations, it is possible to describe, with comparable accuracy, also the negative parity sector, without changing the description of the positive-parity part.

## Key claim

The model can describe the negative-parity octupole band in the X(5) critical-point nuclei 150Nd and 152Sm with comparable accuracy to the X(5) model's description of the positive-parity sector, using only one additional adjustable parameter (Δ₁), under the assumption that the octupole deformation β₃ remains a small fraction of the quadrupole deformation β₂.

## Method

The authors extend their previously introduced model for coupled quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom near axial symmetry, expressing both deformation amplitudes in terms of new variables β and δ (where β₂ = β cos δ, β₃ = β sin δ). They assume a square-well potential in β (as in the X(5) model) and treat the octupole motion as small-amplitude vibrations around a reflection-symmetric shape, leading to separable differential equations whose solutions for the negative-parity band depend on a constant Δ₁ = A′₁ − A′₀, which is treated as an adjustable parameter (Δ₁ = 15 for 150Nd, Δ₁ = 20 for 152Sm).

## Key numbers

- For 150Nd: Δ₁ = 15 (constant) reproduces the negative-parity band; the average value of ⟨sin² δ⟩ is about 0.09 for the positive-parity part and 0.14 for the negative-parity part of the K = 0 band.
- For 152Sm: Δ₁ = 20 (constant) reproduces the negative-parity band; the average value of ⟨sin² δ⟩ is about 0.05 for the positive-parity part and 0.10 for the negative-parity part of the K = 0 band.
- In 152Sm, the experimental B(E1) for the 1⁻ → 0⁺ transition is 4.2 (4) × 10⁻³ W.u. (used for normalization); the calculated B(E1) for the 1⁻ → 2⁺ transition is 9.8 × 10⁻³ W.u. compared to the experimental 7.7 (7) × 10⁻³ W.u.
- In 152Sm, the experimental B(E2) for the 2⁺ → 0⁺ transition is 144 (4) W.u. (used for normalization); the calculated B(E2) for the 4⁺ → 2⁺ transition is 230 W.u. compared to the experimental 209 (7) W.u.
- The first 1⁻ level in both nuclei lies between the lowest 6⁺ and 8⁺ states, at 963 keV in 152Sm and at approximately the same relative position in 150Nd.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Bizzeti 2004) for octupole-quadrupole coupling near axial symmetry, which was previously applied to transitional Ra-Th isotopes and to the X(5)-like nuclei 224Ra and 224Th (Bizzeti 2008). It applies the model to the well-known X(5) critical-point nuclei 152Sm (Casten 2001) and 150Nd (Kruecken 2002), which were the first examples of nuclei at the U(5) to SU(3) phase transition. The results enable comparison with more detailed models such as the spdf-IBM (Garrett 2009) and the coherent-coupling model (Minkov 2006), and suggest that a slightly different potential well (as discussed by Caprio 2004) could improve agreement for inter-band transitions.
