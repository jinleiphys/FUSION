# Dynamical Eightfold Way in Strongly Coupled Lattice QCD

> We obtain from the quark-gluon dynamics, the Gell'Mann-Ne'eman eightfold way baryons in an imaginary-time functional integral formulation of 3+1 lattice QCD in the strong coupling regime (small hopping parameter $κ>0$). The model has ${\rm SU}(3)_c$ gauge and global ${\rm SU}(3)_f$ flavor symmetries. In the subspace of the physical Hilbert space of vectors with an odd number of quarks, the baryons are associated with isolated dispersion curves in the energy-momentum spectrum. The spin 1/2 octet and spin 3/2 decuplet baryons have asymptotic mass $-3\lnκ$ and for each baryon there is an antibaryon with identical spectral properties. All the masses have the form $M=-3\lnκ-3κ^3/4+κ^6 r(κ)$, with $r(κ)$ real analytic. For each member of the octet $r(κ)$ is the same; for each member of the decuplet, $r(0)$ is the same. So, there is no mass splitting within the octet, and within the decuplet up to and including ${\cal O}(κ^6)$. However, there is an octet-decuplet mass difference of $3κ^6/4+{\cal O}(κ^7)$. The baryon and antibaryon spectrum is the only one up to near the meson-baryon threshold of nearly $-5\lnκ$. A decoupling of hyperplane method is used to naturally unveil the form of the baryon composite fields (no a priori guesswork), to show the existence of particles and their multiplicities using a spectral representation for the two-baryon correlation. We also obtain the (anti-)baryon dispersion curves which admit the representation $w(κ,\vec p)= -3\lnκ-3κ^3/4+κ^3\sum_{j=1,2,3} (1-\cos ^j)/4+r(κ,\vec p)$, where $r(κ,\vec p)$ is of ${\cal O}(κ^6)$.

## Key claim
The authors derive the Gell'Mann-Ne'eman eightfold way baryons (spin-1/2 octet and spin-3/2 decuplet) directly from the quark-gluon dynamics of 3+1 lattice QCD in the strong coupling regime, but only under the condition that the hopping parameter κ is small and the plaquette coupling β satisfies β ≪ κ ≪ 1, and with exact SU(3)_f flavor symmetry (equal quark masses).

## Method
Using an imaginary-time functional integral formulation of lattice QCD with SU(3)_c gauge and global SU(3)_f flavor symmetries, the authors apply a decoupling of hyperplane method to analyze the two-baryon correlation function. They obtain a spectral representation for the correlation, compute its Fourier transform, and locate isolated dispersion curves by finding zeros of the determinant of the inverse two-point function in momentum space. The baryon composite fields emerge naturally from the third derivative of the hopping parameter expansion, without a priori guesswork, and are classified using SU(3)_f symmetry into the octet and decuplet representations.

## Key numbers
- Baryon asymptotic mass: M = -3 ln κ (leading order in the strong coupling expansion)
- Mass formula: M = -3 ln κ - 3κ³/4 + κ⁶ r(κ), with r(κ) real analytic
- Octet-decuplet mass difference: 3κ⁶/4 + O(κ⁷)
- Number of independent baryon fields: 56 (8 octet × 2 spin states + 10 decuplet × 4 spin states)
- Meson-baryon threshold energy: approximately -5 ln κ
- Analyticity strip for the inverse two-point function: |Im p⁰| < -(5-ε) ln κ, with 0 < ε ≪ 1
- Octet dispersion curve representation: w(κ, p) = -3 ln κ - 3κ³/4 + κ³ Σ_{j=1,2,3} (1 - cos p^j)/4 + r(κ, p), where r(κ, p) is O(κ⁶)

## Context
This work extends the rigorous mathematical treatment of lattice QCD spectra from earlier studies by Osterwalder and Seiler (1978) and Seiler (1982), and from the authors' own previous work on simpler SU(3)_c models (CMP, da Veiga and O'Carroll, Longo). It provides the first dynamical derivation of the eightfold way baryon classification from first principles in lattice QCD, complementing the phenomenological quark model of Gell-Mann and Ne'eman (1961). The method enables future rigorous analysis of meson-baryon bound states and pentaquarks, and can be extended to SU(N)_f flavor symmetry and broken flavor symmetry with different quark masses.
