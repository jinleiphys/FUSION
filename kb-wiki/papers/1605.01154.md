# Quark Matter at High Density based on Extended Confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass Model

> We investigate the effect of the inclusion of relativistic Coulomb terms in a confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass (CIDDM) model of strange quark matter (SQM). We found that if we include Coulomb term in scalar density form, SQM equation of state (EOS) at high densities is stiffer but if we include Coulomb term in vector density form is softer than that of standard CIDDM model. We also investigate systematically the role of each term of the extended CIDDM model. Compared with what was reported in Ref.~\cite {ref:isospin}, we found the stiffness of SQM EOS is controlled by the interplay among the the oscillator harmonic, isospin asymmetry and Coulomb contributions depending on the parameter's range of these terms. We have found that the absolute stable condition of SQM and the mass of 2 $M_\odot$ pulsars can constrain the parameter of oscillator harmonic $κ_1$ $\approx 0.53$ in the case Coulomb term excluded. If the Coulomb term is included, for the models with their parameters are consistent with SQM absolute stability condition, the $2.0~M_{\odot}$ constraint more prefer the maximum mass prediction of model with scalar Coulomb term than that of model with vector Coulomb term. On contrary, the high densities EOS predicted by model with vector Coulomb is more compatible with recent pQCD result \cite{ref:pressure} than that predicted by model with scalar Coulomb. Furthermore, we also observed the quark composition in a very high density region depends quite sensitively on the kind of Coulomb term used.

# Quark Matter at High Density based on Extended Confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass Model

## Key claim

The inclusion of a relativistic Coulomb term in the CIDDM model can either stiffen or soften the strange quark matter equation of state at high densities depending on whether the Coulomb term is expressed in scalar density form (model I) or vector density form (model II), but neither form simultaneously satisfies both the 2 solar mass pulsar constraint and the perturbative QCD pressure prediction at high densities; the scalar Coulomb term is preferred by the mass constraint while the vector Coulomb term is more compatible with pQCD results.

## Method

The authors extended the confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass (CIDDM) model by adding relativistic Coulomb interaction terms in two forms: a scalar density Coulomb term (model I) where the Coulomb contribution appears inside the quark mass term, and a vector density Coulomb term (model II) where the Coulomb contribution appears outside the quark mass term in the energy density. They solved the equations of state for strange quark matter under charge neutrality and beta equilibrium conditions, then computed mass-radius relations for strange stars and compared the high-density pressure predictions with perturbative QCD results from Fraga, Kurkela, and Vuorinen (2014).

## Key numbers

- The absolute stability condition for SQM constrains the harmonic oscillator parameter to $0.53 \lesssim \kappa_1 \lesssim 0.65$ when the Coulomb term is excluded ($\kappa_2^{(1\ \text{or}\ 2)} = 0$)
- For model II with vector Coulomb parameter $\kappa_2^{(2)} = -0.8$, the allowed $\kappa_1$ range shifts to $0.63 \lesssim \kappa_1 \lesssim 0.83$
- For model I with scalar Coulomb parameter $\kappa_2^{(1)} = 0.3$, the allowed $\kappa_1$ range shifts to $0.45 \lesssim \kappa_1 \lesssim 0.54$
- The isospin asymmetry parameter must satisfy $\kappa_3 \gtrsim 20\ \text{fm}^{3a-1}$ to fulfill the absolute stability condition for u-d quark matter
- The 2 solar mass constraint combined with absolute stability yields $\kappa_1 \approx 0.53$ when Coulomb term is excluded, and $0.45 \lesssim \kappa_1 \lesssim 0.46$ for model I with $\kappa_2^{(1)} = 0.3$
- The quark fraction analysis covers baryon number densities in the range $0.75\ \text{fm}^{-3} < n_B < 4.55\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ where star formation is relevant

## Context

This work extends the CIDDM model of Chu and Chen (2014), which showed that strong isospin asymmetry dependence could yield strange star masses of 2 solar masses. The authors build on the Cornell potential formalism and compare with pQCD results from Fraga, Kurkela, and Vuorinen (2014) and Kurkela, Romatschke, and Vuorinen (2010). The scalar Coulomb term was previously investigated by Xia, Peng, Chen, Lu, and Xu (2014) but with focus on thermodynamic consistency rather than the systematic comparison of Coulomb term forms performed here. The results enable future discrimination between Coulomb term types once pQCD calculations including charm quarks at very high densities become available.
