# Heavy-Meson Decay Constants: QCD Sum-Rule Glance at Isospin Breaking

# Heavy-Meson Decay Constants: QCD Sum-Rule Glance at Isospin Breaking

## Key claim
QCD sum rules predict much less isospin breaking in the decay constants of $B$ mesons than lattice QCD does, but yield good agreement with lattice QCD for $D$ mesons. The analysis relies on modeling the effective threshold as a polynomial function of the Borel parameter to estimate systematic uncertainties.

## Method
The authors apply a modified QCD sum-rule formalism in which the effective threshold $s_{\rm eff}$ is treated as a function of the Borel parameter $\tau$, modeled by polynomial ansätze of order $n=1,2,3$. They compute the decay constants of pseudoscalar and vector heavy-light mesons ($B$, $B^*$, $D$, $D^*$) as functions of the light-quark mass $m_q$, using $\overline{\rm MS}$ quark masses and standard vacuum condensates, and extract the isospin-breaking differences by evaluating the slope $(f_{M_d}-f_{M_u})/(m_d-m_u)$.

## Key numbers
- $f_{B^0} - f_{B^\pm} = (0.79 \pm 0.14)\ \text{MeV}$
- $f_{B^{*0}} - f_{B^{*\pm}} = (0.65 \pm 0.10)\ \text{MeV}$
- $f_{D^\pm} - f_{D^0} = (0.78 \pm 0.13)\ \text{MeV}$
- $f_{D^{*\pm}} - f_{D^{*0}} = (1.41 \pm 0.42)\ \text{MeV}$
- Normalized isospin-breaking ratio $(f_{M_d} - f_{M_u})/f_M(0)$: $B$ meson $(4.1 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-3}$, $B^*$ meson $(3.6 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-3}$, $D$ meson $(3.8 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-3}$, $D^*$ meson $(5.7 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-3}$
- Input light-quark masses: $m_u(2\ \text{GeV}) = (2.3^{+0.7}_{-0.5})\ \text{MeV}$, $m_d(2\ \text{GeV}) = (4.8^{+0.5}_{-0.3})\ \text{MeV}$, $\overline{m} = (m_u+m_d)/2 = 3.5^{+0.7}_{-0.2}\ \text{MeV}$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous improved QCD sum-rule formalism (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2014) for extracting heavy-meson decay constants, which had already predicted $f_{B^*} < f_B$ later confirmed by lattice QCD (HPQCD Collaboration). The results for $B$ mesons are lower than lattice-QCD isospin-breaking results (Lattice Isospin Breaking Collaboration), while for $D$ mesons they agree with lattice QCD; the findings are of similar size to lattice-QCD results for $K$ mesons. A more detailed analysis including light-quark mass uncertainties is presented in a companion paper (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2016).
