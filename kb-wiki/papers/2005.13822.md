# Finite-Density Massless Two-Color QCD at Isospin Roberge-Weiss Point and 't Hooft Anomaly

> We study the phase diagram of two-flavor massless two-color QCD (QC$_2$D) under the presence of quark chemical potentials and imaginary isospin chemical potentials. At the special point of the imaginary isospin chemical potential, called the isospin Roberge--Weiss (RW) point, two-flavor QC$_2$D enjoys the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ center symmetry that acts on both quark flavors and the Polyakov loop. We find a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ 't Hooft anomaly of this system, which involves the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ center symmetry, the baryon-number symmetry, and the isospin chiral symmetry. Anomaly matching, therefore, constrains the possible phase diagram at any temperatures and quark chemical potentials at the isospin RW point, and we compare it with previous results obtained by chiral effective field theory and lattice simulations. We also point out an interesting similarity of two-flavor massless QC$_2$D with $(2+1)$d quantum anti-ferromagnetic systems.

## Key claim

The authors find a mixed 't Hooft anomaly involving the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ center symmetry, the baryon-number symmetry, and the isospin chiral symmetry in massless two-flavor two-color QCD at the isospin Roberge-Weiss point ($\theta_I = \pi/2$). Anomaly matching then forces at least one of these three symmetries to be spontaneously broken at any temperature and quark chemical potential, providing a rigorous constraint on the phase diagram that the abstract implies but does not explicitly state as a conditional necessity.

## Method

The authors analyze the global symmetries of massless two-flavor QC$_2$D using Weyl spinors to exploit the Pauli-Gürsey $\SU(4)/\mathbb{Z}_2$ chiral symmetry. They introduce a real quark chemical potential $\mu$ and an imaginary isospin chemical potential $\mu_I = i\theta_I/L$, and identify the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ center symmetry that emerges at the isospin Roberge-Weiss point $\theta_I = \pi/2$ by combining an isospin flavor rotation with the RW periodicity. They then compute the 't Hooft anomaly of this $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry together with the baryon-number and chiral symmetries, and apply anomaly matching to constrain the possible phase diagram.

## Key numbers

* The isospin Roberge-Weiss point occurs at $\theta_I = \pi/2$, where $\mu_I = i\theta_I/L$ and $L$ is the length of the thermal circle.
* The RW periodicity is $\theta_I \sim \theta_I + \pi$, giving a shortened period compared to the naive $2\pi$.
* The chiral symmetry breaking pattern is $\SU(2N_f)/\mathbb{Z}_2 \to \Sp(N_f)/\mathbb{Z}_2$, producing $2N_f^2 - N_f - 1$ Nambu-Goldstone bosons; for $N_f=2$, this gives $2(2)^2 - 2 - 1 = 5$ NG bosons.
* The quark chemical potential explicitly breaks $\SU(4)/\mathbb{Z}_2$ to $[\SU(2)_\mathrm{L} \times \SU(2)_\mathrm{R} \times \U(1)_\mathrm{V}]/(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2)$ at $\theta_I = 0$ mod $\pi$.

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft anomaly matching approach previously applied to pure Yang-Mills theories (Gaiotto 2017) and to QCD with center-twisted boundary conditions (Kouno 2012, Sakai 2012, Cherman 2017) to the finite-density regime of two-color QCD. It provides rigorous constraints that can be tested by lattice Monte Carlo simulations, which are sign-problem-free for this setup, and contrasts with earlier chiral effective field theory and lattice results (Hands 2006, Braguta 2016, Bornyakov 2017, Astrakhantsev 2018, Iida 2019, Boz 2019) that lacked such symmetry-based constraints. The work also draws an analogy between the discrete anomaly structure and that of $(2+1)$d quantum anti-ferromagnetic systems, opening a connection to condensed matter physics.
