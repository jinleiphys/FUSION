# Fluctuations in Relativistic Causal Hydrodynamics

> Formalism to calculate the hydrodynamic fluctuations by applying the Onsager theory to the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation is already known. In this work, we calculate hydrodynamic-fluctuations within the framework of the second order hydrodynamics of Müller, Israel and Stewart and its generalization to the third order. We have also calculated the fluctuations for several other causal hydrodynamical equations. We show that the form for the Onsager-coefficients and form of the correlation-functions remains same as those obtained by the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation and it does not depend on any specific model of hydrodynamics. Further we numerically investigate evolution of the correlation function using the one dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow. We compare the correlation functions obtained using the causal hydrodynamics with the correlation-function for the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation. We find that the qualitative behavior of the correlation-functions remain same for all the models of the causal hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors show that the form of the Onsager coefficients and the resulting noise correlation functions for causal relativistic hydrodynamics (Müller-Israel-Stewart second-order theory, third-order extensions, and other models) are identical to those obtained from the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation, provided the entropy production rate takes the same quadratic form; the relaxation times and higher-order transport coefficients do not appear explicitly in the correlation functions, though they affect the dynamical evolution of those correlations.

## Method

The authors apply Onsager's fluctuation theory to the relativistic causal hydrodynamic equations by identifying the dissipative fluxes as the thermodynamic fluxes and the conjugate forces from the entropy production rate, then extracting the Onsager coefficients γ_ik from the relation dS/dt = γ_ik X_k X_i. They perform this procedure for the Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) equations in both Landau-Lifshitz and Eckart frames, for the third-order hydrodynamics of Ajel et al. (2011), for the Jaiswal-Bhalerao-Pal (JBP) model, and for the Denicol-Koide-Rischke (DKR) model. They then numerically evolve the correlation functions using a one-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow to compare the causal theories with the relativistic Navier-Stokes result.

## Key numbers

- The noise correlation function for shear and bulk viscosity in all causal models is: ⟨S^{μν}_{vis}(x₁) S^{αβ}_{vis}(x₂)⟩ = 2T [η(Δ^{μα}Δ^{νβ}+Δ^{μβ}Δ^{να}) + (ζ − 2/3 η)Δ^{μν}Δ^{αβ}] δ(x₁−x₂), where η is shear viscosity, ζ is bulk viscosity, and T is temperature.
- For the heat conductivity channel in the Landau-Lifshitz frame (MIS): ⟨I^{μ}(x₁) I^{ν}(x₂)⟩ = −2λ T² Δ^{μν} δ(x₁−x₂), where λ is the heat conductivity coefficient.
- For the JBP model, the heat-noise correlation differs by a factor of T: ⟨I^{μ}(x₁) I^{ν}(x₂)⟩ = −2λ T Δ^{μν} δ(x₁−x₂).
- For the third-order hydrodynamics (shear only, no bulk or heat), the correlation function is identical to the second-order result with Π=0: ⟨S^{μν}_{vis}(x₁) S^{αβ}_{vis}(x₂)⟩ = 2T [η(Δ^{μα}Δ^{νβ}+Δ^{μβ}Δ^{να}) − (2/3)η Δ^{μν}Δ^{αβ}] δ(x₁−x₂).

## Context

This work extends the fluctuation-dissipation approach previously applied to relativistic Navier-Stokes theory by Kapusta et al. (2012) to the full class of causal relativistic hydrodynamic models, including the Müller-Israel-Stewart theory (MIS), its third-order generalization by Ajel et al. (2011), the Jaiswal-Bhalerao-Pal (JBP) model, and the Denicol-Koide-Rischke (DKR) model. The key result, that the noise correlations are universal and independent of the relaxation times, enables the use of the same fluctuation formalism in numerical simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, regardless of which causal hydrodynamic model is employed, while the dynamical evolution of those correlations will differ between models due to the different relaxation equations.
