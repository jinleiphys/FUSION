# Equation of state of quark-nuclear matter

> Quark-nuclear matter (QNM) is a many-body system containing hadrons and deconfined quarks. Starting from a microscopic quark-meson coupling (QMC) Hamiltonian with a density dependent quark-quark interaction, an effective quark-hadron Hamiltonian is constructed via a mapping procedure. The mapping is implemented with a unitary operator such that composites are redescribed by elementary-particle field operators that satisfy canonical commutation relations in an extended Fock space. Application of the unitary operator to the microscopic Hamiltonian leads to effective, hermitian operators that have a clear physical interpretation. At sufficiently high densities, the effective Hamiltonian contains interactions that lead to quark deconfinement. The equation of state of QNM is obtained using standard many-body techniques with the effective quark-hadron Hamiltonian. At low densities, the model is equivalent to a QMC model with confined quarks. Beyond a critical density, when quarks start to deconfine, the equation of state predicted for QNM is softer than the QMC equation of state with confined quarks.

## Key claim

The paper claims that beyond a critical density, the equation of state predicted for quark-nuclear matter (QNM) is softer than the standard quark-meson coupling (QMC) equation of state with confined quarks, but this result depends on a postulated density-dependent confining potential and several simplifying approximations in the calculation of the baryon fraction Z.

## Method

Starting from a relativistic quark potential model with a linearly rising confining potential, the authors construct a unitarily equivalent effective Hamiltonian containing both quark and hadron degrees of freedom using the Fock-Tani mapping formalism. They then calculate the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter in the mean-field approximation, introducing a density-dependent confining potential that weakens above three times normal nuclear matter density, and estimate the fraction of baryons versus deconfined quarks using a perturbative formula with additional approximations (neglecting the lower Dirac spinor component, momentum dependence of the energy denominator, and using an average value for momentum transfer).

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³ (k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹)

- Coupling constants: g^q_σ = 6.1355, 3 g^q_ω = 6.285
- Incompressibility: K = 248 MeV
- The density-dependent potential parameter μ is zero for densities below 3ρ_0 and increases linearly as μ = ρ_B/3ρ_0 - 1 for higher densities

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model originally proposed by Guichon and subsequently improved by Saito and Thomas, which describes matter at low density as nonoverlapping MIT bags interacting through meson fields. The authors introduce a generalization that allows quark deconfinement at high density, using a mapping formalism from Ref. FT (Fock-Tani representation) previously developed for composite particle systems. The softening of the equation of state when quarks deconfine is noted as potentially relevant for neutron-star phenomenology, where soft equations of state may be required to explain recent observational data of compact stellar objects.
