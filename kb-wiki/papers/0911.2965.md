# Neutrino oscillations: Inevitability of non-standard interactions or a sterile neutrino

> We conjecture that the neutrino mixing matrix must be such that in the flavour oscillation process the energy expectation values enjoy flavour independence. Based on this conjecture we present unitarity and energy conservation constraints on neutrino oscillation parameters. In our analysis θ_{13} depends on the ratio of the mass-squared differences ζand on θ_{12} alone; while δ_{CP} is completely determined by ζ, θ_{12}, and θ_{23}. The standard neutrino oscillation phenomenology (SNOP) suggests ζ\approx 30. The resulting prediction for θ_{13} conflicts with SNOP expectation for all values of θ_{12}. This leads to the suggestion that some non-standard neutrino interactions may be intervening to invalidate the SNOP inferred ζand θ_{13}. An alternate inference is to go beyond the three flavours of SNOP and incorporate sterile neutrinos in the analysis.

# Neutrino oscillations: Inevitability of non-standard interactions or a sterile neutrino

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that the neutrino mixing matrix must be such that the energy expectation values for each flavour are equal in the oscillation process, and show that enforcing this flavour-independence of energy expectation values forces a conflict with the standard neutrino oscillation phenomenology (SNOP) value of ζ ≈ 30, leading to the conclusion that either non-standard neutrino interactions must be present or a sterile neutrino must be incorporated.

## Method

The authors impose the conditions ⟨E_e⟩ = ⟨E_μ⟩ and ⟨E_μ⟩ = ⟨E_τ⟩ on the standard three-flavour mixing matrix parameterised by θ₁₂, θ₁₃, θ₂₃, and δ_CP, using the relativistic approximation E_i = p + m_i²/(2p) for the mass eigenstate energies. They solve these constraints analytically to obtain expressions for θ₁₃ as a function of ζ (the ratio of mass-squared differences Δm²₃₂/Δm²₂₁ with a sign) and θ₁₂, and for δ_CP as a function of ζ, θ₁₂, and θ₂₃, then compare the resulting θ₁₃ prediction with the SNOP-inferred value.

## Key numbers

- ζ := −(Δm²₃₂ / Δm²₂₁); the SNOP-inferred value is ζ ≈ 30, taken from the analysis of GonzalezGarcia (2007).
- The SNOP value for θ₁₃ at 90% confidence level, assuming CP conservation and the standard MSW effect, is θ¹³_snop = −0.07⁺⁰·¹⁸₋₀·₁₁ (from Escamilla 2008).
- When the authors' constraint on θ₁₃ is applied with the SNOP value of θ₁₃, the required ζ is approximately 1, not 30.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of energy conservation in neutrino oscillations, originally motivated by Pauli's neutrino conjecture, and directly addresses the paradox that different flavour states carry different energy expectation values. The authors' conjecture leads to a conflict with the standard three-flavour phenomenology summarised by GonzalezGarcia (2007) and Escamilla (2008), and they propose that either non-standard interactions (NSI) of the type studied by Grossman (1995), Gavela (2008), and others must be invoked, or that a sterile neutrino must be added to the analysis. The paper calls for short-baseline experiments free of matter effects to test these alternatives and for re-analysis of existing data incorporating both NSI and the derived constraints.
