# Fluctuating initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions from the Glauber approach

> In the framework of the Glauber approach we analyze the shape parameters of the early-formed system and their event-by-event fluctuations. We test a variety of models: the conventional wounded nucleon model, a model admixing binary collisions to the wounded nucleons, a model with hot spots, as well as the hot-spot model where the deposition of energy occurs with a superimposed probability distribution. We look in detail at the so-called participant multipole moments, obtained by an averaging procedure where in each event the system is translated to its center of mass and aligned with the major principal axis of the ellipse of inertia. Quantitative comparisons indicate substantial relative effects for eccentricity in variants of Glauber models. On the other hand, the dependence of the scaled standard deviation of the participant eccentricity on the chosen model is weak. For all models the values range from about 0.5 for the central collisions to about 0.3-0.4 for peripheral collisions, both for the gold-gold and copper-copper collisions. They are dominated by statistics and change only by 10-15% from model to model. We provide an approximate analytic expansion for the multipole moments and their fluctuations given in terms of the fixed-axes moments. For central collisions and in the absence of correlations it gives the simple formula for the scaled standard deviation of the participant eccentricity: sqrt(4/pi-1). Similarly, we obtain expansions for the radial profiles of the multipole distributions. We investigate the relevance of the shape-fluctuation effects for jet quenching and find them important only for very central events. Finally, we argue how smooth hydro leads to the known result v_4 ~ v_2^2, and further to the prediction Delta v_4/v_4 = 2 Delta v_2/v_2.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the fixed-axes eccentricity and its scaled standard deviation vary significantly across different Glauber-like models, the scaled standard deviation of the participant (variable-axes) eccentricity is remarkably insensitive to model choice, ranging only from about 0.5 for central collisions to 0.3-0.4 for peripheral collisions, with changes of only 10-15% between models.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations for gold-gold and copper-copper collisions, testing four models: the conventional wounded nucleon model, a mixed model adding binary collisions to wounded nucleons, a hot-spot model with rare semi-hard collisions, and a hot-spot model with a superimposed gamma distribution for energy deposition. They computed fixed-axes and variable-axes (participant) harmonic moments and radial profiles by shifting each event to its center of mass and aligning with the major principal axis of the ellipse of inertia, then analyzed the event-by-event fluctuations of these shape parameters.

## Key numbers

- Scaled standard deviation of participant eccentricity for central collisions: approximately 0.5 for all models, with analytic formula sqrt(4/pi - 1) = 0.52
- Scaled standard deviation of participant eccentricity for peripheral collisions: 0.3-0.4 for both gold-gold and copper-copper collisions
- Model-to-model variation in scaled standard deviation of participant eccentricity: only 10-15%
- Woods-Saxon parameters for gold: R = 6.37 fm, a = 0.54 fm (197Au)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for copper: R = 4.14 fm, a = 0.57 fm (62Cu)
- Inelastic nucleon cross section used: sigma_w = 42 mb
- Hot-spot cross section: sigma_hot-spot = 0.5 mb
- Mixed model parameter alpha = 0.145 for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV collisions
- Gamma distribution parameter kappa = 0.5, giving variance var(w) = 5
- At b=0, participant eccentricity ranges from 0.047 (k=0 weighting) to 0.121 (k=6 weighting), while scaled standard deviation stays constant at 0.52

## Context

This work extends earlier event-by-event hydrodynamic studies by Aguiar (2000, 2001) that first showed fluctuations of the initial fireball shape lead to important effects for azimuthal asymmetry. It provides a systematic comparison of multiple Glauber-like models (wounded nucleon model of Bialas (1976), mixed model of Back (2001, 2004), and hot-spot model inspired by Gyulassy (1996)) and shows that the participant eccentricity fluctuations are dominated by statistical effects rather than model details. The results enable quantitative predictions for elliptic flow fluctuations, including the relation Delta v4/v4 = 2 Delta v2/v2, and provide a framework for initial conditions in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations.
