# Eigenvalue spectrum for single particle in a spheroidal cavity: A Semiclassical approach

> Following the semiclassical formalism of Strutinsky et al., we have obtained the complete eigenvalue spectrum for a particle enclosed in an infinitely high spheroidal cavity. Our spheroidal trace formula also reproduces the results of a spherical billiard in the limit $η\to1.0$. Inclusion of repetition of each family of the orbits with reference to the largest one significantly improves the eigenvalues of sphere and an exact comparison with the quantum mechanical results is observed upto the second decimal place for $kR_{0}\geq{7}$. The contributions of the equatorial, the planar (in the axis of symmetry plane) and the non-planar(3-Dimensional) orbits are obtained from the same trace formula by using the appropriate conditions. The resulting eigenvalues compare very well with the quantum mechanical eigenvalues at normal deformation. It is interesting that the partial sum of equatorial orbits leads to eigenvalues with maximum angular momentum projection, while the summing of planar orbits leads to eigenvalues with $L_z=0$ except for L=1. The remaining quantum mechanical eigenvalues are observed to arise from the 3-dimensional(3D) orbits. Very few spurious eigenvalues arise in these partial sums. This result establishes the important role of 3D orbits even at normal deformations.

## Key claim

The authors present a complete semiclassical trace formula for a particle in an infinitely high spheroidal cavity that reproduces the full quantum mechanical eigenvalue spectrum, including the contributions from three-dimensional (3D) orbits which were previously thought to be negligible at normal deformation. The formula works in the spherical limit only as η→1.0 (not at exactly η=1.0, where it diverges), and achieves agreement to the second decimal place for kR₀≥7.

## Method

The authors generalized the Strutinsky et al. (1977) trace formula for spheroidal cavities by deriving an explicit analytical expression for the Jacobian of transformation and including repetitions of each family of periodic orbits up to a maximum determined by the longest orbit. They separated the periodic orbits into three families: equatorial planar orbits (Type I, in the plane perpendicular to the symmetry axis), planar orbits in the axis-of-symmetry plane (Type II, with lz=0), and non-planar 3D orbits, each with appropriate Maslov indices and degeneracy factors. The oscillating part of the level density was summed over these orbit families and combined with the smooth part from Weyl's expansion to obtain the eigenvalue spectrum.

## Key numbers

- At η=1.0001 (near-spherical limit), eigenvalues agree with quantum mechanical results to the second decimal place for kR₀≥7.
- At η=1.3 (normal deformation), the difference between Type I orbit eigenvalues and quantum mechanical eigenvalues for L=Lz states is nearly constant at approximately 0.26 in kR₀ units.
- The minimum deformation for 3D orbit existence is given by η_min = sin(π n_φ/n_v)/sin(π n_u/n_v).
- The averaging width γ was chosen as 0.02R₀ for the spherical limit and 0 for the η=1.3 calculations.
- Quantum mechanical eigenvalues listed include values such as 3.1927 (N=1, L=0, Lz=0), 4.2817 (N=1, L=1, Lz=0), and 4.6990 (N=1, L=1, Lz=1) at η=1.3.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical trace formula approach of Strutinsky et al. (1977) and addresses limitations in the calculations of Frisk (1990) and Magner et al. (1997), particularly the divergence in the spherical limit and the neglect of 3D orbit contributions. The authors show that contrary to earlier beliefs, 3D orbits play an essential role in reproducing the full eigenvalue spectrum even at normal deformation (η=1.3), not just at superdeformation. This enables a "semiclassical shell model" where each quantum level can be identified with a specific type of periodic orbit, with potential applications to nuclear fission, metal clusters, and quantum dots.
