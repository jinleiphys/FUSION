# Microscopic description of Gamow-Teller transitions in middle pf--shell nuclei by a realistic shell model calculation

> GT transitions in $N=28\sim 30$ nuclei are studied in terms of a large-scale realistic shell-model calculation, by using Towner's microscopic parameters. $B({\rm GT})$ values to low-lying final states are reproduced with a reasonable accuracy. Several gross properties with respect to the GT transitions are investigated with this set of the wavefunctions and the operator. While the calculated total GT$^-$ strengths show no apparent disagreement with the measured ones, the calculated total GT$^+$ strengths are somewhat larger than those obtained from charge-exchange experiments. Concerning the Ikeda sum-rule, the proportionality of $S_{\rm GT}$ to $(N-Z)$ persists to an excellent approximation, with a quenching factor of 0.68. For the relative GT$^-$ strengths among possible isospin components, the lowest isospin component gathers greater fraction than expected by the squared CG coefficients of the isospin coupling. It turns out that these relative strengths are insensitive to the size of model space. Systematics of the summed $B({\rm GT})$ values are discussed for each isospin component.

## Key claim

The paper claims that realistic shell-model calculations using Towner's microscopic single-particle parameters can reproduce Gamow-Teller transition strengths in middle pf-shell nuclei (N=28-30) with reasonable accuracy, but the calculated total GT+ strengths are somewhat larger than those obtained from charge-exchange experiments, suggesting either incomplete experimental detection or overestimation in the calculation.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations in the pf-shell above a 40Ca inert core, using a configuration space restricted to k ≤ 2 (where k is the number of nucleons excited from the 0f7/2 orbit), leading to dimensions over 100,000 in the M-scheme for several N=30 nuclei. They diagonalized the Kuo-Brown hamiltonian using the code VECSSE and employed Towner's microscopic single-particle parameter set for the GT transition operator, which incorporates core-polarization and meson-exchange-current effects with no adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- The quenching factor for the Ikeda sum-rule S_GT is 0.68 of the bare value
- The effective g_A quenching is g_A^eff/g_A = 0.82 for the 0f orbit and 0.81 for the 1p orbit
- Energy levels of N=28-30 nuclei are reproduced within typical deviation of 0.3 MeV
- Calculated B(GT-) for 52Ti to 52V 1_1+ is 0.361 compared to experimental 0.56 ± 0.04
- Calculated B(GT-) for 53V to 53Cr (5/2)_1- is 0.156 compared to experimental 0.15
- Calculated B(GT+) for 56Ni to 56Co 1_1+ is 0.058 compared to experimental 0.25
- Calculated total ∑B(GT-) for 54Fe is 14.3 compared to experimental 12.4 ± 3.0
- Calculated total ∑B(GT+) for 54Fe is 7.7 compared to experimental 5.6 ± 0.8
- Calculated total ∑B(GT-) for 56Fe is 19.1 compared to experimental 15.7 ± 3.8
- Calculated total ∑B(GT+) for 56Fe is 6.2 compared to experimental 4.6 ± 0.5
- Calculated total ∑B(GT-) for 58Ni is 15.8 compared to experimental 11.7 ± 2.9
- Calculated total ∑B(GT+) for 58Ni is 9.5 compared to experimental 6.0 ± 0.6

## Context

This work extends the realistic shell-model approach previously applied successfully to sd-shell nuclei (Brown and Wildenthal, 1985) into the middle pf-shell region, building on the authors' own spectroscopic calculations for N=28-30 nuclei (Nakada, Sebe, and Otsuka, 1994) that reproduced energy levels and electromagnetic properties. The study enables systematic predictions of GT strength distributions by isospin component, which are needed for understanding double-beta decay intermediate states and astrophysical s- and r-process nucleosynthesis, and provides a comparison point for full pf-shell Monte-Carlo calculations (Langanke et al., 1995) using the KB3 interaction.
