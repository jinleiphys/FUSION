# On the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for the deuteron

> The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule is evaluated for the deuteron by explicit integration up to a photon energy of 550 MeV including contributions from the photodisintegration channel and from coherent and incoherent single pion production as well. The photodisintegration channel converges fast enough in this energy range and gives a large negative contribution, essentially from the $^1S_0$ resonant state near threshold. Its absolute value is about the same size than the sum of proton and neutron GDH values. It is only partially cancelled by the single pion production contribution. But the incoherent channel has not reached convergence at 550 MeV.

## Key claim

The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule for the deuteron is evaluated by explicit integration up to 550 MeV, finding a total value of -183 μb from the model calculation, but a more realistic estimate of -19 μb when using experimental multipole analysis data for the incoherent pion production channel; the sum rule is not yet converged at this energy because the incoherent channel has not reached convergence.

## Method

The GDH integral was evaluated by explicit integration of the difference between total photoabsorption cross sections for circularly polarized photons on a deuteron target with spin parallel and antiparallel to the photon spin, up to a photon energy of 550 MeV. Three channels were included: photodisintegration (γd → np) using a nonrelativistic framework with the Bonn potential including one-body currents, meson exchange contributions, isobar configurations, and leading order relativistic contributions up to multipole order L=4; coherent single pion production (γd → π⁰d) using a model with Δ-resonance excitation and pion rescattering via coupled NΔ, NNπ, and NN channels; and incoherent single pion production (γd → πNN) using a spectator nucleon approach with pseudovector Born terms and Δ-resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

- Photodisintegration channel GDH integral: I^GDH_γd→np(550 MeV) = -413 μb
- Coherent pion production GDH integral: I^GDH_γd→dπ⁰(550 MeV) = 63 μb
- Incoherent pion production GDH integral (model): I^GDH_γd→NNπ(550 MeV) = 167 μb
- Sum of free proton and neutron GDH values: I^GDH_p = 204.8 μb, I^GDH_n = 233.2 μb
- Deuteron anomalous magnetic moment: κ_d = -0.143, giving GDH prediction I^GDH_d = 0.65 μb
- Total deuteron GDH from model integration up to 550 MeV: I^GDH_d(550 MeV) = -183 μb
- More realistic estimate using experimental multipole analysis (fit SM95): I^GDH_d(550 MeV) = -19 μb
- With nucleon anomalous magnetic moment switched off in one-body current: I^GDH_γd→np(550 MeV) = 7.3 μb

## Context

This work extends the GDH sum rule evaluation from nucleons (proton and neutron) to the deuteron, following the original derivations by Gerasimov (1965) and Drell and Hearn (1966). The deuteron provides a unique test because its small anomalous magnetic moment (κ_d = -0.143) predicts a tiny GDH value of 0.65 μb, yet the individual nucleon contributions are large (438 μb combined), requiring near-perfect cancellation between the photodisintegration channel and pion production channels. The calculation uses the Bonn potential for the deuteron and n-p scattering wave functions, and the results enable future experimental tests of the GDH sum rule for the deuteron, particularly the low-energy photodisintegration contribution near threshold, while also highlighting the need for improved elementary pion photoproduction operators above the Δ-resonance region and inclusion of two-pion production contributions.
