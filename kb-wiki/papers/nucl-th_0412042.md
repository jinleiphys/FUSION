# Bosonization and IBM

> We derive a boson Hamiltonian from a Nuclear Hamiltonian whose potential is expanded in pairing multipoles and determine the fermion-boson mapping of operators. We use a new method of bosonization based on the evaluation of the partition function restricted to the bosonic composites of interest. By rewriting the partition function so obtained in functional form we get the euclidean action of the composite bosons from which we can derive the Hamiltonian. Such a procedure respects all the fermion symmetries.

# Bosonization and IBM

## Key claim

The paper derives a boson Hamiltonian from a nuclear Hamiltonian with pairing-multipole interactions using a new bosonization method based on evaluating the partition function restricted to bosonic composites, but the derivation of the Hamiltonian requires an expansion in the inverse of the shell degeneracy \(\Omega\) and is valid only when the number of bosons is much smaller than the index of nilpotency (i.e., \(n \ll \Omega\)).

## Method

The authors evaluate the partition function restricted to fermionic composites (boson-like pairs with fermion number 2) by inserting an operator \(\mathcal{P}\) that approximates the identity in the composite subspace, then rewrite the partition function in functional form to obtain the Euclidean action of the composite bosons. The Hamiltonian is derived from this action by omitting time-derivative and chemical-potential terms and replacing the bosonic variables with creation-annihilation operators satisfying canonical commutation relations, after performing an expansion in inverse powers of \(\Omega\) (the shell degeneracy). The calculation is carried out explicitly for a single \(j\)-shell with pairing-multipole interactions, where the structure matrices are determined by angular momentum and normalization conditions.

## Key numbers

- The shell degeneracy is \(\Omega = j + 1/2\) for a single \(j\)-shell.
- The structure matrices satisfy \(\mbox{tr} (B^{\dagger}_J \, B_K) = 2 \, \delta_{J,K}\) and have dimension \(2\Omega\).
- The potential form factors are normalized as \(\mbox{tr} (F_K^{\dagger} F_K) = 2 \, \Omega\).
- The single-boson energy matrix becomes diagonal as \(\omega_{I_1 I_2} = (2 \, \overline{e} - g_{I_1} \Omega) \delta_{I_1 I_2}\) under the assumption \(e_{m_1 m_2} = \overline{e} \, \delta_{m_1 m_2}\).
- The operator \(\mathcal{P}\) approximates the identity with an error of order \(\nu / (\Omega - \overline{n})\) for states with \(\nu\) bosons above a reference number \(\overline{n}\), and in the many-composite case the error is of order \(1/\Omega\).

## Context

This work extends the line of research connecting the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello to underlying nucleon Hamiltonians, following earlier attempts by Beliaev and Zelevinsky (which used Bogoliubov transformations violating nucleon number conservation) and by Otsuka, Arima, and Iachello (who obtained exact results for pairing in a single \(j\)-shell). The method avoids the truncation of the nucleon space required by mapping approaches (Klein and others) and instead trades it for a decoupling problem among bosons. The closed-form action opens the possibility of numerical simulations of fermionic systems using bosonic variables, avoiding the sign problem, and can be extended to other many-fermion systems such as the Hubbard model and color superconductivity in QCD.
