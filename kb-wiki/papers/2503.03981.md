# Precise constraint on properties of neutron stars through new universal relations and astronomical observations

> In view of the great uncertainty of the equation of state (EOS) of high-density nuclear matter, establishing EOS-independent universal relations between global properties of neutron stars provides a practical way to constrain the unobservable or difficult-to-observe properties through astronomical observations. It is common to construct universal relations between EOS-dependent properties (e.g., moment of inertia, tidal deformation, etc.) or combined properties (e.g., compactness). Improving the precision of the universal relations may provide stricter constraint on the properties of neutron star. We find that in 3-dimensional space with mass and radius as the base coordinates, the points corresponding to a certain property of neutron star described by different EOSs are almost located in the same surface. Thus the universal relation between the property and the stellar mass-radius can be expressed through describing the surface. It is shown that the resulting universal relations have higher precisions. As an example, we construct high-precision universal relations for the moment of inertia, the $f$-mode frequency, and the dimensionless tidal deformation respect to the mass-radius. As the observational data of neutron star mass and radius from NICER grows in data and accuracy, these universal relations allow for more precise constraints on the unobservable or difficult-to-observe properties.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by projecting neutron star properties onto a 3-dimensional space with mass and radius as base coordinates, the points for different equations of state lie on a single surface, yielding universal relations with higher precision than traditional two-variable relations; the hedge is that this holds for the specific set of 63 piecewise-polytropic and 18 nucleonic/hybrid EOSs tested, and the method is demonstrated only for static (non-rotating) neutron stars.

## Method

The authors constructed a set of 63 piecewise-polytropic EOSs plus 18 microscopic and phenomenological nuclear EOSs (including APR, SLy, WFF, and hybrid models with first-order phase transitions). For each EOS, they solved the TOV equations to obtain mass-radius sequences and then computed the moment of inertia, f-mode frequency, and dimensionless tidal deformation for each neutron star configuration. They fitted polynomial surfaces in the 3-dimensional space of mass and radius to obtain universal relations, and compared the mean relative errors against traditional universal relations (e.g., I/M^3 vs. compactness, omega_f vs. sqrt(M/R^3), Lambda vs. compactness).

## Key numbers

- Mean relative error for the moment of inertia universal relation I_*(M_*, R_*) is 0.019, compared to 0.774 for the traditional I/M^3 vs. compactness relation.
- Mean relative error for the f-mode frequency universal relation omega_f(M_*, R_*) is 0.0698, compared to 0.106 for the traditional omega_f vs. sqrt(M/R^3) relation.
- Mean relative error for the dimensionless tidal deformation universal relation Lambda(M_*, R_*) is 0.0798, compared to 0.213 for the traditional Lambda vs. compactness relation.
- For PSR J0030+0451 (mass 1.44 +/- 0.15 M_sun, radius 11.2-13.3 km at 90% credibility), the moment of inertia is constrained to [0.980, 3.408] x 10^45 g cm^2, the f-mode frequency to [1.296, 2.111] kHz, and the tidal deformation to [284.713, 2833.076].
- For PSR J0740+6620 (mass 2.08 +/- 0.07 M_sun, radius 12.2-16.3 km at 95% credibility), the moment of inertia is constrained to [1.859, 7.364] x 10^45 g cm^2, the f-mode frequency to [0.966, 2.479] kHz, and the tidal deformation to [less than 1885.358].
- Using the GW170817 tidal deformation constraint Lambda_{1.4} = 190_{-120}^{+390}, the radius of a 1.4 M_sun canonical neutron star is constrained to 10.707_{-0.668}^{+1.967} km.

## Context

This work extends the long line of universal relations research beginning with Ravenhall and Pethick (1994) on the I-C relation, refined by Lattimer and Prakash (2001) and Bejger and Haensel (2002), and the I-LOVE-Q relations of Yagi and Yunes (2013). It directly addresses the concern raised by Sulieman and Read (2024) that existing universal relations suffer from insufficient precision for constraining neutron star properties, particularly radius. The new mass-radius projection method enables more precise constraints on moment of inertia, f-mode frequency, and tidal deformation using NICER mass-radius observations, and the authors suggest the method can be extended to rotating neutron stars by adding rotation frequency as a fourth dimension.
