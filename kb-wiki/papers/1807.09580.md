# Zero-sound in nuclear matter with the asymmetry parameter $-1\leq β\leq 1$

> Results for the frequencies of zero-sound excitations in the isospin asymmetric nuclear matter are presented for the different parameters of asymmetry -1\leqβ\leq 1. The dispersion equations are constructed within the random phase approximation. We use the effective Landau-Migdal quasiparticle interaction with the special isospin dependence of the phase volume in the normalization factor C_0. In the paper we present zero-sound branches of the dispersion equation solutions in the symmetric, asymmetric and neutron matter. The branches correspond to the different channels of decay of the zero-sound excitation in the nuclear matter or nuclei. In the asymmetric nuclear matter we obtain three branches of solutions on the physical and unphysical sheets of the complex frequency plane, ω_{s\,τ}(k,β), τ=p,n,np. We demonstrate the change of branches with βand conversion of the three branches into two branches in symmetric nuclear matter (β=0) and into one branch in the neutron matter (β=1).

# Zero-sound in nuclear matter with the asymmetry parameter $-1\leq \beta\leq 1$

## Key claim

The paper presents frequencies of zero-sound excitations in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter for asymmetry parameters $-1\leq \beta\leq 1$, finding three branches of solutions on the physical and unphysical sheets of the complex frequency plane that convert into two branches in symmetric nuclear matter ($\beta=0$) and into one branch in neutron matter ($\beta=1$), with the caveat that the branches $\omega_{sn}(k,\beta>0)$ and $\omega_{sp}(k,\beta<0)$ can be connected at all $k$ except a special interval where no solutions exist at $\beta=0$.

## Method

The dispersion equations for zero-sound excitations were constructed within the random phase approximation (RPA) using the effective Landau-Migdal quasiparticle interaction, with a modified normalization factor $C_0$ that incorporates the isospin-dependent phase volume via $C_{0p} = \pi^2/(m_0 p_{Fp})$ and $C_{0n} = \pi^2/(m_0 p_{Fn})$. The polarization operators $A^p$ and $A^n$ were integrated over particle-hole loops to obtain Migdal functions, and solutions were sought on both the physical and unphysical sheets of the complex frequency plane by analytically continuing through logarithmic cuts formed by free particle-hole pair energies.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density: $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$; Fermi momentum $p_0 = 0.268$ GeV.
- Effective nucleon mass: $m^* = 0.8 m_0$, with $m_0 = 0.94$ GeV.
- Isovector Landau-Migdal constant: $F' = 1.0$.
- In symmetric nuclear matter at $\rho = \rho_0$: $k_t = 0.34 p_0$ (wave vector where collective mode meets the particle-hole cut) and $k_c = 0.52 p_0$ (wave vector where the second branch $\omega_{s1}(k)$ starts).
- At $\beta < 0.24$ the ordering of characteristic wave vectors is $k^p(\beta) < k_t(\beta) < k_1^{np}(\beta) < k_c$.
- For $\beta \to 1$, $[\text{Re}\,\omega_{sp}(k) - k^2/(2m)]/p_0 \to 0$ at least as quickly as $p_{Fp}/p_0$, and $\text{Im}\,\omega_{sp}(k)/p_0$ also goes to zero.

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Migdal theory of zero-sound in symmetric nuclear matter (Sadovnikova, earlier work) to asymmetric nuclear matter with arbitrary isospin asymmetry. It builds on prior studies of collective excitations in asymmetric matter using kinetic theory (Morawetz, 2001; Morawetz, 2002) and local isospin density approximation (Lipparini and Stringari, 1989), and on the RPA-based approach to zero-sound in symmetric matter (Sadovnikova, 2006; Sadovnikova, 2008). The results enable calculation of zero-sound frequencies and widths in neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron stars and exotic nuclei, and provide a framework for connecting collective excitations in symmetric matter, asymmetric matter, and pure neutron matter through continuous variation of the asymmetry parameter.
