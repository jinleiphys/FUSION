# Relativity constraints on the two-nucleon contact interaction

> We construct the most general, relativistically invariant, contact Lagrangian at order Q^2 in the power counting, Q denoting the low momentum scale. A complete, but non-minimal, set of (contact) interaction terms is identified, which upon non-relativistic reduction generate 2 leading independent operator combinations of order Q^0 and 7 sub-leading ones of order Q^2 - a result derived previously in the heavy-baryon formulation of effective field theories (EFT's). We show that Poincare covariance of the theory requires that additional terms with fixed coefficients be included, in order to describe the two-nucleon potential in reference frames other than the center-of-mass frame. These terms will contribute in systems with mass number A>2, and their impact on EFT calculations of binding energies and scattering observables in these systems should be studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Poincaré covariance of the two-nucleon contact interaction in effective field theory requires specific boost-correction terms with fixed coefficients, which are not independent low-energy constants but are instead determined by the leading-order contact couplings \(C_S\) and \(C_T\) and the nucleon mass \(m\).

## Method

The authors constructed the most general relativistically invariant contact Lagrangian at order \(Q^2\) in the power counting, using fermion bilinears with derivatives and enforcing parity and charge conjugation invariance. They performed a non-relativistic reduction of the 36 resulting operators to obtain the corresponding two-nucleon potential in an arbitrary frame, and then verified that the same boost corrections arise from requiring the commutation relations of the Poincaré group generators to be satisfied order by order in the low-energy expansion.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order (LO, \(Q^0\)) contact Lagrangian contains 2 independent operator combinations, with low-energy constants \(C_S\) and \(C_T\).
- At next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO, \(Q^2\)), the non-relativistic reduction yields 7 independent operator combinations of order \(Q^2\).
- The boost-correction coefficients are fixed as: \(C_1^* = (C_S - C_T)/(4 m^2)\), \(C_2^* = C_T/(2 m^2)\), \(C_3^* = -C_S/(4 m^2)\), \(C_4^* = -C_T/(4 m^2)\), \(C_5^* = 0\), where \(m\) is the nucleon mass.
- In \(A=3\) and \(A=4\) systems, these boost corrections give repulsive contributions of about 400 keV and 1.9 MeV, respectively, to binding energies (citing Carlson (1993)).

## Context

This work extends the heavy-baryon formulation of chiral effective field theory (Weinberg (1990), Ordonez (1996), Epelbaum (1998)) by showing that relativistic covariance imposes additional constraints on the two-nucleon contact interaction beyond those from chiral symmetry alone. The derived boost corrections, previously obtained in relativistic quantum mechanics (Krajcik (1974), Carlson (1993)), are now placed on an EFT footing, enabling their systematic inclusion in calculations of nuclei with mass number \(A>2\) and in three-nucleon scattering observables (Witala (2008)).
