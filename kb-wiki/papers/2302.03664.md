# Predicting the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element of $^{136}$Xe using a statistical approach

> Calculation of the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for double-beta decay is of paramount importance for guiding experiments and for analyzing and interpreting the experimental data, especially for the search of the neutrinoless double beta decay mode ($0νββ$). However, there are currently still large differences between the NME values calculated by different methods, hence a quantification of their uncertainties is very much required. In this paper we propose a statistical analysis of $0νββ$ NME for the $^{136}Xe$ isotope, based on the interacting shell model, but using three independent effective Hamiltonians, emphasizing the range of the NMEs' most probable values and its correlations with observables that can be obtained from the existing nuclear data. Consequently, we propose a common probability distribution function for the $0νββ$ NME, which has a range of (1.55 - 2.65) at 90\% confidence level, with a mean value of 1.99 and a standard deviation of 0.37.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a common probability distribution function for the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element of $^{136}$Xe, with a range of (1.55 – 2.65) at 90% confidence level, a mean value of 1.99, and a standard deviation of 0.37, based on a statistical analysis using three independent shell-model effective Hamiltonians with random perturbations.

## Method

The authors performed interacting shell-model calculations in the jj55 model space (consisting of the $0g_{7/2}, 1d_{5/2}, 1d_{3/2}, 2s_{1/2}$ and $0h_{11/2}$ orbitals above a $^{100}$Sn core) for $^{136}$Xe. They generated 1000 perturbed Hamiltonians from each of three starting effective Hamiltonians (SVD, GCN5082, and jj55t) by applying random perturbations of up to ±10% to the two-body matrix elements, and calculated 24 observables including the $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME, $2\nu\beta\beta$ NME, Gamow-Teller strengths, B(E2) transition probabilities, excitation energies, and occupation probabilities. A Bayesian Model Averaging method was then applied to combine the distributions from the three Hamiltonians, using experimental data for correlated observables to determine weighting factors.

## Key numbers

*   $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME common probability distribution: range (1.55 – 2.65) at 90% confidence level, mean value 1.99, standard deviation 0.37.
*   $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME values for the three starting Hamiltonians: SVD = 1.763, GCN5082 = 2.645, jj55t = 2.314.
*   $2\nu\beta\beta$ NME experimental value: 0.018 ± 0.001 MeV$^{-1}$; calculated values: SVD = 0.025, GCN5082 = 0.069, jj55t = 0.060.
*   Pearson correlation coefficient between $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME and $2\nu\beta\beta$ NME for the SVD Hamiltonian: R = 0.8.
*   Pearson correlation coefficients between $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME and excitation energies of $2^+$, $4^+$, and $6^+$ states in both $^{136}$Xe and $^{136}$Ba: R between 0.64 and 0.78.
*   Closure energy used for all $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME calculations: 3.5 MeV.
*   Quenching factor used for Gamow-Teller strengths and $2\nu\beta\beta$ NME: q = 0.70.
*   Effective charges used for B(E2) calculations: $e_p = 1.5$, $e_n = 0.5$.

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach previously applied to $^{48}$Ca by Horoi et al. (2022) to the $^{136}$Xe isotope, which is among the most promising for experimental neutrinoless double-beta decay searches. The study addresses the persistent problem of large differences (factors of 3-4) between NME values calculated by different nuclear methods, as reviewed by Engel and Menendez (2017), by providing uncertainty quantification through a Bayesian Model Averaging method that was not possible for $^{48}$Ca due to lack of correlated experimental data. The results enable more reliable interpretation of experimental half-life limits from current and future experiments such as nEXO, KamLAND-Zen, PandaX, XENONnT, and DARWIN, and provide a framework for similar statistical analyses of other candidate isotopes.
