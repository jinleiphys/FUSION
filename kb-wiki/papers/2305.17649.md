# The asymptotic behaviour of the many-body coupled cluster amplitudes

> We analyze the asymptotic behaviour of the coupled cluster many-body wave-function in the limit of highly excited two- and three-particles states. We find that in this limit the different coupled cluster amplitudes exhibit a recurring behaviour, factorizing into a common asymptotic two- or three-body term. These asymptotic terms depend on the potential and in general are system specific. We also suggest that the knowledge of the asymptotic behaviour can potentially help solving the coupled cluster equations in a more efficient way.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the limit of highly excited two- or three-particle states, the n-body coupled cluster amplitudes factorize into a product of a common asymptotic two-body or three-body term (which depends on the potential and is system specific) times a residual low-energy amplitude, generalizing previous results for nuclear matter to arbitrary n-body amplitudes.

## Method

The authors analyze the coupled cluster equations for a system of identical fermions interacting via a two-body potential, projecting the n-body CC equation onto n-particle-n-hole states and identifying the leading terms in the limit where two or three particle states become highly excited (momenta going to infinity). They separate the kinetic energy term (which diverges) from terms where the high-momentum indices are contracted with the potential, neglect subleading terms, and derive asymptotic equations that admit factorized solutions for the amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic two-body operator is given by $\hat{\tau}_2 = \frac{1}{1-\hat{Q}_2\hat{G}_0(0)\hat{V}}\hat{Q}_2\hat{G}_0(0)\hat{V}$, where $\hat{Q}_2$ is the two-body projection operator into the particle subspace and $\hat{G}_0(E)$ is the Green's function
* The asymptotic three-body operator is given by $\hat{\tau}_3 = \frac{1}{1-\hat{Q}_3\hat{G}_0(0)\hat{V}}\hat{Q}_3\hat{G}_0(0)\hat{V}\hat{\tau}_2$

## Context

This work extends the factorization ansatz used in short-range correlation studies (Tan 2008, Braaten 2012, Weiss 2014-2017) to the coupled cluster framework, and generalizes the authors' previous results for nuclear matter (Beck, Weiss, Barnea 2023) to arbitrary n-body amplitudes. The findings suggest that knowledge of the asymptotic behavior could enable more efficient solution of the coupled cluster equations by eliminating the asymptotic part of the amplitudes.
