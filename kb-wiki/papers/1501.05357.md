# Forward Compton Scattering with weak neutral current: constraints from sum rules

> We generalize forward real Compton amplitude to the case of the interference of the electromagnetic and weak neutral current, formulate a low-energy theorem, relate the new amplitudes to the interference structure functions and obtain a new set of sum rules. We address a possible new sum rule that relates the product of the axial charge and magnetic moment of the nucleon to the 0th moment of the structure function $g_5(ν,0)$. We apply the GDH and the finite energy sum rule for constraining the dispersive $γZ$-box correction to the proton's weak charge.

# Forward Compton Scattering with weak neutral current: constraints from sum rules

## Key claim
The authors generalize forward real Compton scattering to include electromagnetic-weak neutral current interference, deriving sum rules that can constrain the dispersive γZ-box correction to the proton's weak charge. The new sum rules are conditional on the high-energy behavior of the parity-violating structure function g5, which remains inconclusive from existing polarized deep inelastic scattering data.

## Method
The authors formulate a low-energy theorem for the forward γZ-interference Compton amplitude, relating the new parity-conserving and parity-violating amplitudes to the interference structure functions F1, F3, g1, and g5 at Q²=0 via dispersion relations. They apply the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule and finite energy sum rule (FESR) to test and adjust resonance parametrizations of the electromagnetic and interference structure functions, using isospin rotation to relate electromagnetic data to γZ-interference observables.

## Key numbers
- The product of the nucleon axial charge and magnetic moment for the isoscalar sum rule evaluated with the Δ(1232) resonance gives r.h.s. ≈ 5.93, compared to l.h.s. gA(μp-μn) = 5.96
- The parity-violating polarizability δ2γZ for proton and neutron from narrow Δ(1232) model: δ2γZ,p = δ2γZ,n ≈ -2.0 × 10⁻³ fm³
- The parity-violating polarizability δ1γZ for proton and neutron from narrow Δ(1232) model: δ1γZ,p = δ1γZ,n ≈ 4.5 × 10⁻³ fm²
- Model I (original Bosted parametrization) GDH sum rule evaluation gives κp² ≈ 0.9; Model II (adjusted to PDG) gives κp² ≈ 3.28, close to the sum rule value κp² = 1.793² ≈ 3.215
- Model I γZ-GDH evaluation gives κpγκpZ ≈ 2.247; Model II gives κpγκpZ ≈ 3.615, compared to l.h.s. κpγκpZ ≈ 3.666
- Resonance contribution to Re□γZV: Model I gives 2.24⁺⁰·⁵³₋₀·₄₃ × 10⁻³; Model II gives 2.23⁺⁰·²⁸₋₀·²³ × 10⁻³
- FESR evaluation for electromagnetic J=0 pole: C∞ = -10.2 ± 3.8(stat.) ± 2.0(syst.) μb GeV
- FESR evaluation for γZ-interference J=0 pole (Model II): C∞γZ = 28.5 ± 22.0(back.)⁺¹⁰·¹₋₈·₅(res.) μb GeV

## Context
This work extends the classic electromagnetic forward Compton scattering sum rules of Baldin (Baldin) and Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDHsr) to the electroweak interference case, building on earlier studies of parity-violating Compton scattering by Lukaszuk (2002) and Kurek (2004) that considered hadronic parity violation rather than γ-Z⁰ interference. The results directly impact the calculation of the dispersive γZ-box correction to the proton's weak charge for the Q-Weak experiment (Androic 2013), where current theory uncertainty estimates range from 0.5% (Hall 2013) to 2.8% (Gorchtein 2011). The analysis enables improved constraints on parametrizations of interference structure functions and suggests that existing models may overestimate the γZ-interference structure functions at Q²=0, consistent with recent parity-violation measurements on deuterium by Wang (2013).
