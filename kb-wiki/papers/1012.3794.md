# Simplified modeling of cluster-shell competition in $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg

> We investigate properties of the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) on a collective basis of Antisymmetrized Quasi-Cluster (AQC) states to describe the cluster-shell competition in $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg due to the spin-orbit interaction. By introducing a single additional parameter in the antisymmetrized-cluster basis function, a continuous transformation of the $α$ cluster(s) into independent nucleons can be described. We apply this GCM trial wave function to study in details the transition from cluster states $[ ^{16}$O+$α]$ and $[ ^{16}$O+$α+ α]$ to shell model (SM) states $[ ^{16}$O+${\rm 4N} ]$ and $[ ^{16}$O+${\rm 8N} ]$, respectively. An optimal value of the strength of the spin-orbit interaction is deduced by reproducing level spacing in $^{20}$Ne. A possible connection to the group theoretical understanding of the cluster-shell configuration transition is also discussed.

## Key claim

The Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) on a basis of Antisymmetrized Quasi-Cluster (AQC) states, controlled by a single parameter Lambda, can describe the continuous transition from alpha-cluster states to shell-model states in 20Ne and 24Mg, but the spin-orbit strength that reproduces alpha+N scattering (V0 = 2000 MeV) is too strong for 20Ne; the optimal value V0 = 1000 MeV is deduced by fitting the level spacing in 20Ne, possibly because the tensor interaction is not explicitly included.

## Method

The authors construct GCM trial wave functions by superposing AQC basis states parameterized by a control parameter Lambda (describing cluster dissolution) and a distance parameter R between the quasi-cluster and the 16O core. For 20Ne, one quasi-alpha cluster is placed on the x-axis around a tetrahedral 16O core; for 24Mg, two quasi-alpha clusters are placed around the 16O core with Gaussian centers shifted in the z direction. The Hamiltonian uses the Volkov2 central force and a two-range G3RS spin-orbit potential, and the GCM diagonalization is performed after parity and angular momentum projection for various Lambda and R values.

## Key numbers

- The optimal spin-orbit strength for 20Ne is V0 = 1000 MeV, which reproduces both the 0+-2+-4+ and 0+-8+ level spacings; V0 = 2000 MeV (from alpha+N scattering) gives too large a 0+-8+ spacing.
- For the 0+ state of 20Ne with V0 = 1000 MeV, the minimum energy occurs at R = 3 fm and Lambda approximately 0.2, with a spin-orbit contribution of about -2.5 MeV at the minimum and up to -8 MeV in the inner region.
- For the negative parity 1- state of 20Ne, the energy gain from alpha breaking (finite Lambda) is only 0.3 MeV for V0 = 1000 MeV and 0.8 MeV for V0 = 2000 MeV, much smaller than for positive parity states.
- The Gaussian wave-packet size parameter is b = 1.6 fm (b = 1/sqrt(2 nu)), and the Majorana parameter is M = 0.62.

## Context

This work extends the AQC approach of Itagaki (2005) and Masui (2009) to a full GCM treatment of cluster-shell competition in 20Ne and 24Mg, complementing earlier hybrid models (Tomoda, Suzuki) and AMD calculations (Enyo, Taniguchi) for these nuclei. The method enables a transparent study of how the spin-orbit interaction breaks alpha clusters into independent nucleons, and the authors discuss connections to algebraic models of quantum phase transitions (phase-rev, varna9) and to group-theoretical descriptions of cluster-shell transitions. The results suggest that explicit inclusion of the tensor interaction, which is known to affect spin-orbit splitting in 5He (Terasawa, Myo), would modify the effective spin-orbit strength needed in these calculations.
