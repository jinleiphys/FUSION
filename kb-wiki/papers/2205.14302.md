# Examination of background effects on light-nuclei yield ratio in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The light-nuclei yield ratio is one of the candidates to probe the critical fluctuations of hot QCD matter. In this paper, we investigate the \textit{background effects}, namely the non-critical effects coming from the non-trivial thermal background, on the light-nuclei production within the framework of the coalescence model. Specifically, we analyze the impact of the equilibrium phase-space distribution function of nucleons, $f(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{p})$, on the light-nuclei yield ratio $N_tN_p/N_d^2$, where $N_t$, $N_p$, and $N_d$ denote triton, proton, and deuteron yields. By considering the characteristic function of the phase-space distribution, we systematically expand the yield of light nuclei of $A$-constituent nucleons, $N_A$, in terms of the \textit{phase-space cumulants}, $\langle\mathbf{r}^n\mathbf{p}^m\rangle_c$. We find that the cumulants up to the second-order are canceled out in the generalized ratio $N_p^{B-A} N_B^{A-1}/N_A^{B-1}$. This means that the dominant background effects including the fireball size, the kinetic freeze-out temperature, and the coordinate--momentum correlations caused by the radial expansion play an insignificant role in the yield ratio, which supports the yield ratio as a useful tool for the critical-point search. We also show several examples of background phase-space distributions for the qualitative illustration. The higher-order cumulants, which correspond to the non-Gaussian shape of the phase-space profile, play an important role in the variation of the yield ratio particularly for the smaller fireball sizes. Qualitatively, the spatial structure of the background decreases the yield ratio, and the azimuthal anisotropy $v_n$ increases it. The higher order of the azimuthal anisotropy causes a larger effect on the yield ratio. These results call for the comprehensive future studies of the yield ratio using sophisticated dynamical models.

## Key claim

The authors show that the background (non-critical) effects from the phase-space distribution of nucleons, including fireball size, kinetic freeze-out temperature, and coordinate-momentum correlations from radial expansion, cancel out in the light-nuclei yield ratio up to second-order phase-space cumulants, but higher-order cumulants corresponding to non-Gaussian shapes do not cancel and can significantly affect the ratio, especially for smaller fireball sizes.

## Method

The authors use the coalescence model to compute light-nuclei yields from nucleon phase-space distributions, expressing the yields in terms of phase-space cumulants via the characteristic function of the distribution. They expand the yield of a nucleus with A constituent nucleons, N_A, in a series of phase-space cumulants ⟨r^n p^m⟩_c, and examine which cumulant orders cancel in the generalized yield ratio N_p^(B-A) N_B^(A-1)/N_A^(B-1) for nuclei with mass numbers A and B. They then illustrate the effects of non-Gaussian backgrounds using example distributions including Gaussian, Woods-Saxon, double-Gaussian, radial expansion, and anisotropic flow profiles.

## Key numbers

- The yield ratio for a simple Gaussian phase-space distribution reduces to a constant N_t N_p/N_d^2 = g_t/g_d^2 = 4/9, fixed solely by the spin factors of deuterons and tritons.
- Width parameters for the Wigner function are σ_d = 2.26 fm for deuteron, σ_t = 1.59 fm for triton, and σ_⁴He = 1.37 fm for helium-4.
- The binding energy is 2.2 MeV for deuteron and 8.4 MeV for triton.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model analyses of Sun et al. (2017, 2018, 2020), which assumed small coordinate-momentum correlations and found the yield ratio sensitive to relative density fluctuations near the critical point. The present paper analytically examines the background contributions that were not fully considered in those earlier studies, particularly the effects of non-uniform, finite-size fireballs with flow and anisotropic shapes. The results establish a baseline for interpreting the non-monotonic behavior of the yield ratio observed by NA49, STAR, and ALICE, and call for future studies using sophisticated dynamical models that include critical fluctuations, as planned in a companion paper by Wu et al. (2022).
