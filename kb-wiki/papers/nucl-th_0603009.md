# Pentaquark, cusp and rescattering in single kaon photoproduction off deuterium

> In very well defined part of the phase space, $K N$ and $ΛN$ rescattering depend on on-shell elementary matrix elements and on the low momentum components of the deuteron wave function. This provides us with the unique opportunity to study details in the scattering amplitudes that may have escaped the analysis of reactions induced on a nucleon target by Kaon and Hyperon beams at low energies. When folded with a typical experimental mass resolution, a narrow state with a width of 1 MeV would contribute by no more than 10% to the $KN$ mass spectrum. On the contrary, a cusp would be easily detected near the $Σ$ production threshold in the $ΛN$ mass spectrum.

# Pentaquark, cusp and rescattering in single kaon photoproduction off deuterium

## Key claim

In very well-defined parts of phase space where kaon-nucleon and lambda-nucleon rescattering depend only on on-shell elementary matrix elements and low-momentum components of the deuteron wave function, a narrow pentaquark state with a width of 1 MeV would contribute no more than 10% to the KN mass spectrum when folded with typical experimental mass resolution, whereas a cusp near the Sigma production threshold in the Lambda N mass spectrum would be easily detected.

## Method

The author computes the fully differential cross section for the reaction gamma + deuteron -> K+ + n + Lambda, coherently summing three amplitudes: quasi-free kaon production, kaon-nucleon rescattering, and Lambda-nucleon rescattering. The elementary gamma p -> K+ Lambda amplitude uses a Regge model with K and K* exchange, fitted to recent JLab data with a monopole form factor (cut-off mass 2 GeV). The KN rescattering amplitude uses experimental phase shifts expanded into S and P partial waves, while the Lambda N amplitude uses a low-energy expansion based on the Nijmegen potential solution D, including S-wave contributions near the Sigma threshold.

## Key numbers

- A pentaquark with mass 1540 MeV and width 1 MeV contributes a signal of 5% (positive parity, J^pi = 1/2+) or 15% (negative parity, J^pi = 1/2-) in the high recoil momentum sample (neutron momentum ~500 MeV/c), when integrated over a mass bin of 8 MeV (the CLAS resolution)
- The same pentaquark contributes about 0% (positive parity) or 5% (negative parity) in the low recoil momentum sample (neutron momentum ~300 MeV/c)
- The cusp in the Lambda n scattering amplitude appears at the Sigma production threshold at W_nLambda = 2129 MeV
- The width of each bin in the Lambda n mass distribution is 6 MeV, comparable to the CLAS mass resolution
- The elementary gamma p -> K+ Lambda cross section is described using a monopole form factor with cut-off mass Lambda_k = 2 GeV
- The ratio of radiative constants g_gamma K* K0 / g_gamma K* K+ = 1.542

## Context

This work extends the author's previous high-energy predictions for pion production (Laget 2005) to lower-energy kaon production, providing a framework to analyze the recent high-statistics JLab experiment (g10 collaboration) designed to search for pentaquark states. The calculation is more stringent than the pentaquark production cross section alone evaluated by Guidal (2004), because the nonresonant background is generated by the same reaction mechanism and interferes with the resonant signal. The analysis enables precise determination of the Lambda N scattering amplitude near the Sigma threshold, which would provide stringent constraints on hyperon-nucleon potentials such as the Nijmegen potential, and complements earlier work on kaon production in pp scattering (Laget 1991, 1993) where Lambda and Sigma channels could not be disentangled.
