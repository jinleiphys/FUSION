# Violation of Quark-Hadron Duality (The missing oscillation in the OPE)

> The origin of quark-hadron Duality Violations (DVs) can be related to the sigularities of the Laplace transform of the spectral function. With the help of rather generic properties of the large-$N_c$ approximation and a generalized form for the radial trajectories found in Regge Theory, we may locate these singularities in the complex plane and obtain an expression for the DVs which turns out to agree with general expectations. Using the two-point vector correlator as a test laboratory, we show how the usual dispersion relation may give rise to perturbation theory, the power corrections from the condensate expansion and DVs.

## Key claim

Quark-hadron Duality Violations (DVs) arise from singularities of the Laplace transform of the spectral function in the complex plane, and their leading contribution can be derived from the large-\(N_c\) expansion combined with generalized Regge trajectories, yielding an oscillatory exponential form that agrees with phenomenological parametrizations.

## Method

The author starts from the dispersion relation for the Adler function of the vector two-point correlator and expresses it via the Laplace transform (Borel transform) of the spectral function. Using the large-\(N_c\) approximation and a generalized Regge spectrum with subasymptotic corrections (including logarithmic and power-law terms), the singularities of the Borel transform are located in the complex \(\sigma\) plane. The Duality Violating contribution is obtained by analytic continuation of the Borel transform across these singularities, using the residue theorem and the Converse Mapping Theorem. The analysis is extended from \(N_c=\infty\) (where poles lie on the imaginary axis) to finite \(N_c=3\) (where branch points shift off the axis by an angle \(\sim a/N_c\)).

## Key numbers

* For the radial Regge trajectories fit to the meson spectrum: \(\Lambda^2 \simeq 1.35(4)\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\Gamma/M \simeq 0.12(8)\) (interpreted as \(a/N_c\)).
* Predicted DV oscillation frequency: \(\beta = 2\pi/\Lambda^2 \left[1 + \mathcal{O}(1/N_c)\right] \simeq 4.7(2)\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
* Predicted DV exponential damping: \(\gamma = (2\pi/\Lambda^2)(a/N_c) \left[1 + \mathcal{O}(1/N_c)\right] \simeq 0.6(4)\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
* Comparison fit from tau decay data (Boito et al.): \(\beta = 3.81(26)\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\gamma = 0.57(17)\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the model-based understanding of Duality Violations, which began with Poggio, Quinn and Weinberg (1976), by reconciling the Regge-inspired spectrum with the perturbative structure of QCD (including renormalons) and the operator product expansion (OPE). It provides a theoretical derivation of the phenomenological DV parametrization used in determinations of \(\alpha_s\) from tau decays by Cata, Golterman, Maltman, Boito and collaborators (2005-2020). The result enables a first-principles justification for the exponential-oscillatory form of DVs and predicts the presence of a \(\log q^2\) correction in the oscillation argument, which had not been included in earlier fits.
