# Centrality dependence of nuclear suppression of D mesons in $p$+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV

> In this paper we have shown theoretical model comparisons with ALICE results for average D mesons (D$^0$, D$^+$, D$^{*+}$ and $D^+_s$) in $p$+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV for various centralities. Transport calculations of AMPT and calculations from heavy quark pQCD model, NLO(MNR) have been used for the study of $p_T$ dependent nuclear modification factors in terms of collision centrality ($\displaystyle{Q_{pPb}}$) and the central-to-peripheral ratios ($\displaystyle{Q_{cp}}$) of D mesons. It is found that NLO model with its parametrized $\displaystyle{k_T}$ broadening scheme produces results those closely match with the published D-meson data of $p$+Pb collisions from ALICE. Likewise AMPT transport calculation shows a strong centrality dependence in results but underestimates the experimental data. The differences of both models with experimental data have been discussed.

## Key claim

The NLO pQCD model with a parametrized $k_T$ broadening scheme (Cronin effect) reproduces the ALICE data for D-meson nuclear modification factors in $p$+Pb collisions across centralities, while the AMPT transport model systematically underestimates the data, suggesting that initial-state cold nuclear matter effects dominate over final-state effects in this system.

## Method

The authors used two theoretical approaches: (1) the string-melting version of the AMPT model (v2.6t5) with HIJING initial conditions, Zhang's Parton Cascade for partonic interactions, and a quark coalescence model for hadronization; and (2) a next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD model (MNR) with CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions, EPS09 nuclear shadowing, and a parametrized $k_T$ broadening scheme to account for multiple re-scattering (Cronin effect). They computed the nuclear modification factor $Q_{pPb}$ and the central-to-peripheral ratio $Q_{cp}$ for average D mesons (D$^0$, D$^+$, D$^{*+}$, D$_s^+$) in five centrality classes (0-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-100%) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV and compared with ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- The NLO model with $k_T$ broadening reproduces ALICE data for $Q_{pPb}$ up to $p_T$ of 10 GeV/c for centralities 0-40%, where data show a hint of enhancement at 2 < $p_T$ < 6 GeV/c.
- For peripheral collisions (40-100%), NLO explains the data within uncertainties across the full $p_T$ range.
- AMPT underestimates the experimental $Q_{pPb}$ and $Q_{cp}$ data for all centralities and the entire $p_T$ range.
- A significant rise of $Q_{cp}$ is observed in central collisions (0-40%) within 3 < $p_T$ < 7 GeV/c.
- The nuclear overlap function $T_{pPb}$ used is 9.8334 $\times$ 10$^{-5}$ $\mu$b$^{-1}$; the $p$+$p$ normalization uses $T_{pp}$ = 1.39 $\times$ 10$^{-5}$ $\mu$b$^{-1}$.
- The Peterson fragmentation parameter $\epsilon_p$ is taken as 0.12 for charm quark fragmentation into D mesons.
- The average squared momentum kick per scattering $\delta^2$ is approximately 1 GeV$^2$/c$^2$, and the lower limit of re-scattering parameter $n$ is taken as 4.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of cold nuclear matter effects on heavy quark production in small collision systems, specifically comparing with ALICE measurements of D-meson suppression in $p$+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV (ALICE Collaboration, 2019). The NLO model had previously been used for minimum-bias $R_{pPb}$ calculations by Baral (2016), while the present work extends those calculations to centrality-dependent $Q_{pPb}$ and $Q_{cp}$. The results are compared with other transport models including POWLANG (Beraudo, 2015) and Duke (Xu, 2015), which assume QGP formation in $p$+Pb collisions; the finding that the initial-state NLO model describes the data better than these final-state models suggests that cold nuclear matter effects dominate over possible hot medium effects in this system. The work enables future studies of hadronization mechanisms and their effects on freeze-out surfaces for heavy meson production.
