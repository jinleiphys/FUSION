# Small-$x$ Asymptotics of the Quark Helicity Distribution: Analytic Results

> In this Letter, we analytically solve the evolution equations for the small-$x$ asymptotic behavior of the (flavor singlet) quark helicity distribution in the large-$N_c$ limit. These evolution equations form a set of coupled integro-differential equations, which previously could only be solved numerically. This approximate numerical solution, however, revealed simplifying properties of the small-$x$ asymptotics, which we exploit here to obtain an analytic solution. We find that the small-$x$ power-law tail of the quark helicity distribution scales as $Δq^S (x, Q^2) \sim \left(\tfrac{1}{x} \right)^{α_h}$ with $α_h = \tfrac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \sqrt{\tfrac{α_s N_c}{2π}}$, in excellent agreement with the numerical estimate $α_h \approx 2.31\sqrt{\tfrac{α_s N_c}{2π}}$ obtained previously. We then verify this solution by cross-checking the predicted scaling behavior of the auxiliary "neighbor dipole amplitude" against the numerics, again finding excellent agreement.

## Key claim

The small-x power-law tail of the flavor-singlet quark helicity distribution scales as Δq^S(x, Q^2) ~ (1/x)^{α_h} with α_h = (4/√3) √(α_s N_c / (2π)), derived analytically under the assumption of emergent scaling behavior observed in prior numerical solutions, and valid in the large-N_c limit.

## Method

The authors analytically solved the large-N_c helicity evolution equations for the polarized dipole amplitude G and the auxiliary neighbor dipole amplitude Γ, which previously had only been solved numerically. They exploited the emergent scaling property from the numerical solution of Kovchegov, Pitonyak, and Sievert (2016) that G depends only on the combination ζ = η - s_{10} rather than on η and s_{10} separately, used Laplace transforms to convert the integro-differential equations into ordinary differential equations, and imposed consistency conditions and boundary conditions to determine the analytic form of the solution.

## Key numbers

- The helicity intercept α_h = (4/√3) √(α_s N_c / (2π)) ≈ 2.3094 √(α_s N_c / (2π)), in agreement with the numerical estimate α_h ≈ 2.31 √(α_s N_c / (2π)) from Kovchegov, Pitonyak, and Sievert (2016).
- The asymptotic form of the polarized dipole amplitude is G(ζ) ≈ (1/3) e^{(4/√3) ζ}.
- The ratio ln[Γ(s_{10}, s_{21}, η)/G(s_{21}, η) + 3] = ln4 + (1/√3)(s_{21} - s_{10}) was verified numerically: the vertical intercept of 1.386 agrees with ln4 ≈ 1.3863, and the slope of ≈ 0.52 is within 10% of the expected 1/√3 ≈ 0.577.

## Context

This work extends the numerical solution of the large-N_c helicity evolution equations by Kovchegov, Pitonyak, and Sievert (2016) to an analytic solution, confirming the helicity intercept α_h ≈ 2.31 √(α_s N_c / (2π)). The results build on the dipole-model formulation of small-x helicity evolution developed by Kovchegov, Pitonyak, and Sievert (2015, 2016), which itself extends earlier work on polarized evolution by Bartels, Ermolaev, and Ryskin (1995, 1996). The analytic solution enables precise predictions for the small-x behavior of the quark helicity distribution and the polarized structure function g_1, which can be tested against future experimental data from facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider.
