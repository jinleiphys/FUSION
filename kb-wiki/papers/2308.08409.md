# X-PSI Parameter Recovery for Temperature Map Configurations Inspired by PSR J0030+0451

> In the last few years, the NICER collaboration has provided mass and radius inferences, via pulse profile modeling, for two pulsars: PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. Given the importance of these results for constraining the equation of state of dense nuclear matter, it is crucial to validate them and test their robustness. We therefore explore the reliability of these results and their sensitivity to analysis settings and random processes, including noise, focusing on the specific case of PSR J0030+0451. We use X-PSI, one of the two main analysis pipelines currently employed by the NICER collaboration for mass and radius inferences. With synthetic data that mimic the PSR J0030+0451 NICER data set, we evaluate the recovery performances of X-PSI under conditions never tested before, including complex modeling of the thermally emitting neutron star surface. For the test cases explored, our results suggest that X-PSI is capable of recovering the true mass and radius within reasonable credible intervals. This work also reveals the main vulnerabilities of the analysis: a significant dependence on noise and the presence of multi-modal structure in the posterior surface. Noise particularly impacts our sensitivity to the analysis settings and widths of the posterior distributions. The multi-modal structure in the posterior suggests that biases could be present if the analysis is unable to exhaustively explore the parameter space. Convergence testing, to ensure an adequate coverage of the parameter space and a suitable representation of the posterior distribution, is one possible solution to these challenges.

## Key claim

X-PSI is capable of recovering the true mass and radius of PSR J0030+0451 within reasonable credible intervals when analyzing synthetic data that mimic the NICER data set, but the analysis shows significant dependence on Poisson noise and reveals multi-modal structure in the posterior surface that could introduce biases if the parameter space is not exhaustively explored.

## Method

The authors used the X-PSI (X-ray Pulse Simulation and Inference) pipeline with the MultiNest nested sampling algorithm to perform Bayesian parameter estimation on synthetic data designed to replicate the PSR J0030+0451 NICER data set. They generated synthetic data using two different emission models (ST-U and ST+PST) with known input parameters, then tested recovery performance under varying conditions including different noise realizations, MultiNest settings (sampling efficiency, evidence tolerance, number of live points), and X-PSI resolution settings. The analysis employed relativistic ray tracing with the Oblate Schwarzschild plus Doppler approximation, a fully-ionized hydrogen atmosphere model (NSX), and a background-marginalized likelihood function for phase-folded and binned events across 32 phase bins and PI channels corresponding to 0.3-3 keV.

## Key numbers

- The PSR J0030+0451 radius inferred by Riley (2019) using X-PSI was 12.74 +1.14 -1.19 km (16% and 84% quantiles in marginal posterior mass)
- The PSR J0030+0451 mass inferred by Riley (2019) using X-PSI was 1.34 +0.15 -0.16 solar masses
- The spin period of PSR J0030+0451 is 4.87 ms
- The NICER PI channel range used was [30, 300), corresponding to nominal energies of [0.3-3] keV
- The upper limit on energy in the response matrix was 3.715 keV (compared to 3.6 keV in Riley 2019)
- The high resolution X-PSI settings used sqrt_num_cells=32, max_sqrt_num_cells=64, num_leaves=64, and num_energies=128
- The low resolution X-PSI settings used sqrt_num_cells=18, max_sqrt_num_cells=32, num_leaves=32, and num_energies=64
- The reference MultiNest configuration used sampling efficiency 0.3, evidence tolerance 0.1, and 1000 live points
- The analysis consumed approximately 1.0 x 10^6 core-hours on the Dutch national supercomputer Cartesius/Snellius

## Context

This work extends the parameter recovery simulations reported in Riley (thesis) and Bogdanov (2021) by testing X-PSI under conditions never examined before, including complex surface temperature patterns like the ST+PST model that was the preferred geometry in Riley (2019). The study validates the NICER collaboration's mass and radius inferences for PSR J0030+0451, which are crucial for constraining the equation of state of dense nuclear matter. The results establish a baseline for analyzing a forthcoming larger PSR J0030+0451 NICER data set and identify convergence testing as a necessary solution to the challenges posed by multi-modal posterior structure and noise sensitivity.
