# The energy dependence of the $πN$ amplitude and the three-nucleon interaction

> By calculating the contribution of the $π-π$ three-body force to the three-nucleon binding energy in terms of the $πN$ amplitude using perturbation theory, we are able to determine the importance of the energy dependence and the contribution of the different partial waves of the $πN$ amplitude to the three-nucleon force. A separable representation of the non-pole $πN$ amplitude allows us to write the three-nucleon force in terms of the amplitude for $NN\rightarrow NN^*$, propagation of the $NNN^*$ system, and the amplitude for $NN^*\rightarrow NN$, with $N^*$ being the $πN$ quasi-particle amplitude in a given state. The division of the $πN$ amplitude into a pole and non-pole gives a procedure for the determination of the $πNN$ form factor within the model. The total contribution of the three-body force to the binding energy of the triton for the separable approximation to the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential (PEST) is found to be very small mainly as a result of the energy dependence of the $πN$ amplitude, the cancellation between the $S$- and $P$-wave $πN$ amplitudes, and the soft $πNN$ form factor.

## Key claim

The total contribution of the π-π three-body force to the triton binding energy is found to be very small, primarily due to the energy dependence of the πN amplitude, cancellation between S- and P-wave πN amplitudes, and the soft πNN form factor, but this result is qualified by the fact that the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential used is energy-independent, making the overall magnitude of the three-body force contribution potentially not significant.

## Method

The authors used a separable representation of the non-pole πN amplitude to write the three-nucleon force as the product of the amplitude for NN → NN*, propagation of the NNN* system, and the amplitude for NN* → NN, where N* is the πN quasi-particle amplitude in a given state. They calculated the contribution of this three-body force to the triton binding energy in the Born approximation using perturbation theory, employing the separable approximation to the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential (PEST) for the three-nucleon wave function. The πN amplitude was parameterized to fit experimental πN phase shifts in all S- and P-waves, with the nucleon pole contribution removed from the P11 channel to avoid double counting.

## Key numbers

- The experimental triton binding energy is 8.48 MeV (stated as the reference value that three-nucleon calculations aim to reproduce).
- The πNN coupling constant is set to f²_πNN(k₀)/4π = 0.079, determined as the residue of the πN amplitude at the nucleon pole.
- The subthreshold energy region where the πN amplitude is needed extends approximately 145 MeV below the πN threshold (m_π + E_T ≈ 145 MeV).
- Two parameterizations of the P11 amplitude were used, corresponding to monopole (n₀=1) and dipole (n₀=2) bare πNN form factors, with parameters adjusted to give phase shifts below pion production threshold, the nucleon pole position, and the πNN coupling constant of 0.079.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on three-nucleon forces derived from πN dynamics, comparing with three established approaches: the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) potential (Co79, CG81) which uses current algebra constraints; effective chiral Lagrangian approaches (CD83, RI84, RC86, CF86, We90); and the N-Δ coupled channel approach (HSS83, HSY83, S86, PHS90, PRB91). The paper addresses open questions about the relative contributions of different πN partial waves to the three-body force, the cancellation between partial waves, and the sensitivity of these cancellations to the energy dependence of the amplitudes. The results suggest that future work must treat the two- and three-nucleon interactions consistently within a meson-nucleon theory to obtain reliable magnitudes for the three-body force contribution.
