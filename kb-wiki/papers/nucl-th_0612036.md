# Kaon Absorption from Kaonic Atoms and Formation Spectra of Kaonic Nuclei

> We considered the kaon absorption from atomic states into nucleus. We found that the nuclear density probed by the atomic kaon significantly depends on the kaon orbit. Then, we reexamined the meanings of the observed strengths of one-body and two-body kaon absorption, and investigated the effects to the formation spectra of kaon bound states by in-flight ($K^-,p$) reactions. As a natural consequence, if the atomic kaon probes the smaller nuclear density, the ratio of the two-body absorption at nuclear center is larger than the observed value, and the depth of the imaginary potential is deeper even at smaller kaon energies as in kaonic nuclear states because of the large phase space for the two-body processes.

## Key claim

The authors reexamine the meaning of the experimentally observed 80% one-body and 20% two-body kaon absorption branching ratios, showing that these values reflect absorption at the nuclear surface from specific atomic orbits; if the atomic kaon probes smaller nuclear density, the two-body absorption ratio at the nuclear center is larger than the observed value and the imaginary potential is deeper even at the smaller kaon energies characteristic of kaonic nuclear states.

## Method

The authors calculate the overlapping densities of kaonic atom wavefunctions with nuclear density for \(^{12}\)C using both a chiral unitary optical potential and a phenomenological potential, extracting the effective nuclear density probed by kaons in each atomic state. They then parameterize the imaginary part of the kaon-nucleus optical potential with separate one-body and two-body terms, fix the parameters using the experimental branching ratio (80:20) at the effective density of the absorbing atomic state, and compute the energy-dependent imaginary potential. Finally, they calculate the (\(K^-,p\)) reaction spectra for kaonic nucleus formation using the Green function method with these potentials.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Green function calculations of (\(K^-,p\)) spectra (Yamagata et al., 2005, 2006) and addresses the interpretation of kaon absorption data from Vander Velde-Wilquet et al. (1977), which gave the 80:20 branching ratio. The analysis contrasts with the approach of Batty et al. (1997), who performed comprehensive phenomenological fits to kaonic atom data, and with the chiral unitary model of Ramos et al. The results show that the strong atomic-state dependence of the probed nuclear density for kaonic atoms is completely different from pionic atoms (Yamazaki et al., 2003), where the probed density is nearly independent of quantum numbers. The findings support the critical analyses of Oset and Toki (2006) and Magas et al. (2006), who argued that structures in experimental spectra can be explained by well-known processes, and are consistent with the latest data from Kishimoto et al. (2006). The work enables more reliable evaluation of the imaginary potential for kaonic nuclear states and emphasizes the need for careful background subtraction in future experimental searches.
