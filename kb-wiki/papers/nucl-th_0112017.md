# Staggering behavior of the low lying excited states of even-even nuclei in a Sp(4,R) classification scheme

> We implement a high order discrete derivative analysis of the low lying collective energies of even-even nuclei with respect to the total number of valence nucleon pairs N in the framework of F- spin multiplets appearing in a symplectic sp(4,R) classification scheme. We find that for the nuclei of any given F- multiplet the respective experimental energies exhibit a Delta N=2 staggering behavior and for the nuclei of two united neighboring F- multiplets well pronounced Delta N=1 staggering patterns are observed. Those effects have been reproduced successfully through a generalized sp(4,R) model energy expression and explained in terms of the step-like changes in collective modes within the F- multiplets and the alternation of the F-spin projection in the united neighboring multiplets. On this basis we suggest that the observed Delta N=2 and Delta N=1 staggering effects carry detailed information about the respective systematic manifestation of both high order alpha - particle like quartetting of nucleons and proton (neutron) pairing interaction in nuclei.PACS number(s):21.10.Re, 21.60.Fw

# Staggering behavior of the low lying excited states of even-even nuclei in a Sp(4,R) classification scheme

## Key claim

The experimental low-lying collective energies of even-even nuclei exhibit both ΔN=2 staggering (within individual F-spin multiplets) and ΔN=1 staggering (in united neighboring F-spin multiplets), and these effects are successfully reproduced by a generalized Sp(4,R) model energy expression, suggesting that the staggering patterns carry information about high-order alpha-particle-like quartetting and proton/neutron pairing interactions, though the staggering amplitude depends strongly on the nuclear shell region and the position within the multiplet.

## Method

The authors applied high-order discrete derivative analysis (a five-point staggering filter proportional to the fourth derivative of the energy difference) to experimental 2₁⁺, 4⁺, and 6⁺ yrast energies of even-even nuclei classified into F₀-multiplets within an Sp(4,R) symplectic classification scheme. The staggering functions Stg(2N) for ΔN=2 sequences and Stg(1N) for ΔN=1 sequences were computed for nuclei across multiple major shells, and the results were compared with theoretical energies obtained from a generalized Sp(4,R) model expression E_yrast = α{h_k} I(I+ω), where ω is a geometrical parameter characterizing the collective mode (vibrational to rotational) and α is a dynamical coefficient depending on N and F₀.

## Key numbers

- For the F₀=3/2 multiplet from the (28,50|50,82)₋ shell, the Stg(2N) function oscillates between -0.8 and 0.6 MeV
- For the F₀=0,1,2 multiplets from the (50,82|82,126)₊ rare-earth shell, Stg(2N) drops to the interval [-0.06, 0.04] MeV
- For the F₀=-4 multiplet from the (50,50|82,82)₊ shell, Stg(2N) lies in the interval [-0.03, 0.05] MeV
- For the odd-even multiplet F₀={-5/2,-2} from the (28,28|50,50)₊ shell, the maximal ΔN=1 staggering amplitude is 0.2 MeV
- For the odd-even multiplet F₀={-5/2,-2} from the (82,126|126,...)₊ shell, the maximal ΔN=1 staggering amplitude drops to 0.003 MeV
- For the Os isotopic chain from (50,82|82,126), the maximal ΔN=1 staggering amplitude is 0.006 MeV, two orders smaller than the odd-even multiplet staggering in the same shell
- The geometrical parameter ω varies in the range ω = 1 to 30 across the classified nuclei

## Context

This work extends the Sp(4,R) classification scheme previously developed by the same group (Drenska, Georgieva, Minkov, and collaborators, e.g., dggr, girr, pvio) which classified even-even nuclei into F-spin multiplets based on valence boson numbers. The staggering analysis is analogous to earlier studies of ΔI=1,2,4 staggering in rotational bands (e.g., MDRRB99, BDDMRR1, BDDMRR2) and odd-even staggering in nuclear binding energies (e.g., HiT, DOBNAZ, nbes). The results enable a systematic interpretation of how alpha-like quartetting (ΔN=2) and alternating proton/neutron pairing (ΔN=1) manifest in collective excitation energies across the nuclear chart, and provide a framework for testing the Sp(4,R) classification against future data on exotic and proton-rich nuclei.
