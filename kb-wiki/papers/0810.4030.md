# Cold compression of nuclei induced by antiprotons

> On the basis of a dynamical Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model we study the response of a nucleus on the antiproton implanted in its interior. We solve the Vlasov equation for the antiproton-nuclear system and show assuming a moderately attractive antiproton optical potential that the compressed state is formed on a rather short time scale of about 4-10 fm/c. The evolution of the system after antiproton annihilation is simulated using the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model. Finally, several sensitive observables to the antiproton annihilation in a compressed nuclear configuration are proposed, e.g. the nucleon kinetic energy spectra and the total invariant mass distributions of produced mesons.

# Cold compression of nuclei induced by antiprotons

## Key claim

The authors show, using dynamical Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) simulations, that an antiproton implanted at rest in a nucleus can compress the surrounding nuclear matter to 2–3 times normal nuclear density within 4–10 fm/c, *provided* the antiproton does not annihilate too early; the compression effect survives only if the annihilation is delayed by in-medium suppression of the annihilation rate.

## Method

The authors solve the Vlasov equation (mean-field only, no collisions) for a system of nucleons and a single antiproton using the RMF model with NL3 parameter set, with antiproton–meson couplings scaled by a factor ξ (0.3 for realistic potentials, 1.0 for G-parity-motivated potentials). After a preselected annihilation time, the antiproton is suddenly annihilated with the nearest nucleon using the JETSET string model, and the subsequent evolution of nucleons and mesons is followed with the full GiBUU transport model including collision terms, pion rescattering, and absorption.

## Key numbers

- Central nucleon density reaches a maximum of ρ_N^max ≃ 2ρ_0 for ξ = 0.3 and ρ_N^max ≃ 3ρ_0 for ξ = 1, where ρ_0 = 0.148 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The compressed state forms within 4–10 fm/c and persists numerically up to 100 fm/c without annihilation.
- For a central ¯p + ¹²C collision at E_lab = 180 MeV, only about 5% of simulated runs (1 out of 20) show the antiproton reaching the central zone and producing compression up to ≃ 2ρ_0.
- The vacuum annihilation lifetime estimate for a stopped antiproton at normal density is τ ≃ 2 fm/c, but in-medium effects may delay annihilation up to ∼ 20 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends static RMF calculations by Buer02 and Mish05, which predicted strong nuclear compression by antiprotons and anti-lambdas, by adding dynamical evolution and annihilation modeling. It addresses the key question of whether early annihilation kills the compression effect, which earlier static studies could not answer. The results enable experimental searches for compressed nuclear configurations at the future FAIR facility, using proposed observables such as nucleon kinetic energy spectra and total invariant mass distributions of emitted mesons, and suggest that the event-by-event transverse momentum correlation method proposed by Pochodzalla for anti-lambda potentials could also determine the antiproton optical potential.
