# Quark Confinement and Deconfinement in QCD from the Viewpoint of Abelian-Projected Effective Gauge Theory

> We give another derivation of quark confinement in QCD from the viewpoint of the low-energy effective Abelian gauge theory of QCD obtained via Abelian projection. It is based on the recently discovered Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless phase transition in the four-dimensional Abelian gauge theory. Moreover, we show that there exists a critical gauge coupling constant in QCD, above which confinement set in and below which there is no confinement. In a SU(N) gauge theory with $N_f$ flavored fermions, we argue that this leads to a critical value of fermion flavors $N_f^c$ for the confinement as well as the chiral symmetry breaking, which separates the deconfinement and chiral symmetric phase. A finite-temperature deconfinement transition is also discussed briefly.

## Key claim

Quark confinement in QCD follows from the existence of a critical gauge coupling constant \(g_c\) (or \(\alpha_c = \pi/4\)) in the low-energy effective Abelian gauge theory obtained via Abelian projection, above which confinement sets in and below which there is no confinement; this result does not require assuming monopole condensation, but instead relies on a Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) phase transition in the four-dimensional Abelian gauge theory.

## Method

The author derives the Abelian-projected effective gauge theory (APEGT) of QCD by first performing a Cartan decomposition of the non-Abelian gauge field into diagonal and off-diagonal pieces, then fixing the gauge for the coset \(G/H\) (maximal Abelian gauge), and finally integrating out the off-diagonal gluon fields to obtain an effective Abelian gauge theory with gauge group \(H_e \times H_m\). The resulting APEGT action includes an antisymmetric tensor field \(B_{\mu\nu}\) and a dual gauge field \(b_\mu\) that couples to a magnetic monopole current. By eliminating the \(B_{\mu\nu}\) field via Gaussian integration, the theory reduces to a compact Abelian gauge theory whose coupling runs according to the same beta function as the original Yang-Mills theory. The author then applies the result of a previous paper (Kondo, 1998) showing that four-dimensional Abelian gauge theory has a confining phase in the strong coupling region \(g > g_c\), where the critical coupling \(g_c = \pi\) is determined from the BKT transition temperature of the two-dimensional O(2) nonlinear sigma model.

## Key numbers

- Critical gauge coupling for confinement: \(g_c = \pi\) (equivalently \(\alpha_c = \pi/4\)).
- Running coupling constant at one-loop: \(g(\mu)^2 = g(\mu_0)^2 / [1 + (b_0 / 8\pi^2) g(\mu_0)^2 \ln(\mu/\mu_0)]\) with \(b_0 = 11 C_2(G)/3 > 0\).
- Critical coupling for chiral symmetry breaking (CSB) from Schwinger-Dyson equation: \(\alpha_c' = \pi / [3 C_2(R)] = [2N / (3(N^2-1))] \pi\).
- For SU(2): \(\alpha_c' = 4\pi/9\); for SU(3): \(\alpha_c' = \pi/4\) (coincides with \(\alpha_c\)).
- Critical number of fermion flavors for deconfinement/confinement transition: for SU(2), \(8 \ge N_f^c \ge 6\); for SU(3), \(12 \ge N_f^c \ge 8\).
- Lower bounds from two-loop beta function: \(N_f^c \ge 5.55\) for SU(2) and \(N_f^c \ge 8.05\) for SU(3).
- Specific estimate using \(\alpha_c = \pi/4\): \(N_f^c = 8.64\) for SU(2) and \(N_f^c = 11.9\) for SU(3).
- The two critical scales \(m_A\) (off-diagonal gluon mass) and \(\mu_c\) (confinement scale) are expected to be of the same order, approximately 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous reformulation of Yang-Mills theory as a deformation of a topological quantum field theory (Kondo, 1997-1998) and provides an alternative derivation of quark confinement that avoids assuming monopole condensation. It builds on the Abelian projection idea of 't Hooft (1981) and the dual superconductor picture of the QCD vacuum (Nambu, 1974). The approach enables a unified discussion of confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, and finite-temperature deconfinement, and yields estimates for the critical number of fermion flavors that are consistent with lattice simulations (Iwasaki et al., 1998). The paper also connects to the work of Appelquist, Terning, and Wijewardhana (1996) on infrared fixed points and the Schwinger-Dyson approach to chiral symmetry breaking.
