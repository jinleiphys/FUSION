# Baryon Number and Electric Charge Fluctuations in Pb+Pb Collisions at SPS energies

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the net baryon number and electric charge in nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied in Pb+Pb at SPS energies within the HSD transport model. We reveal an important role of the fluctuations in the number of target nucleon participants. They strongly influence all measured fluctuations even in the samples of events with rather rigid centrality trigger. This fact can be used to check different scenarios of nucleus-nucleus collisions by measuring the multiplicity fluctuations as a function of collision centrality in fixed kinematical regions of the projectile and target hemispheres. The HSD results for the event-by-event fluctuations of electric charge in central Pb+Pb collisions at 20, 30, 40, 80 and 158 A GeV are in a good agreement with the NA49 experimental data and considerably larger than expected in a quark-gluon plasma. This demonstrate that the distortions of the initial fluctuations by the hadronization phase and, in particular, by the final resonance decays dominate the observable fluctuations.

## Key claim

The HSD transport model reproduces the NA49 experimental data for event-by-event electric charge fluctuations in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, but these fluctuations are considerably larger than expected in a quark-gluon plasma, demonstrating that distortions from the hadronization phase and final resonance decays dominate the observable fluctuations rather than any signal from deconfinement.

## Method

The authors used the HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics) transport model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, generating minimum bias events and then selecting samples with fixed numbers of projectile participants (N_P^proj) to study fluctuations of net baryon number and electric charge. They calculated scaled variances (omega = Var/mean) for baryon number and the quantity X_Q = Var(Q)/<N_ch> for electric charge fluctuations in different rapidity intervals and centrality classes, comparing their results to NA49 experimental data and to predictions from transparency (T-), mixing (M-), and reflection (R-) models of baryon flow.

## Key numbers

- The scaled variance of target participant fluctuations, omega_P^targ, exceeds 2 for N_P^proj between 10 and 80 in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV
- For the net baryon number in full phase space, omega_B = 0.5 * omega_P^targ due to only half the participants fluctuating when N_P^proj is fixed
- In the transparency model, omega_B^p(T) = 0 and omega_B^t(T) = omega_P^targ; in the mixing model, omega_B^p(M) = omega_B^t(M) = 0.5 + 0.25 * omega_P^targ
- For electric charge fluctuations in the full acceptance at 158 AGeV, X_Q is approximately 3 times larger than the naive estimate of 0.04 * omega_B
- For the most central collisions (impact parameter b < 2 fm, about 2% of minimum bias), X_Q approaches 1 as the rapidity interval Delta Y goes to 0 and decreases toward zero as full acceptance is reached
- The HSD results for X_Q were compared to NA49 data at 20, 30, 40, 80 and 158 AGeV in the forward rapidity interval 1.1 < y < 2.6

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Konchakovski et al., 2005) of charged particle number fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV using both UrQMD and HSD transport approaches, now focusing on net baryon number and electric charge fluctuations. The paper contradicts predictions from Refs. [Ko.1, As.1] that electric charge fluctuations would be suppressed in a quark-gluon plasma relative to a hadron gas, showing instead that hadronization and resonance decays dominate the observed fluctuations. The results enable future experimental tests of different baryon flow scenarios (transparency, mixing, or reflection models) by measuring multiplicity fluctuations as a function of collision centrality in fixed kinematical regions of the projectile and target hemispheres.
