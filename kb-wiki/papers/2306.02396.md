# Perturbative QCD concerning light and heavy flavor in the EPOS4 framework

> We recently introduced new concepts, implemented in EPOS4, which allow to consistently accommodate factorization and saturation in high energy proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions, in a rigorous parallel scattering framework. EPOS4 has a modular structure and in this paper, we present in detail how the "single scattering module" (the main EPOS4 building block) is related to perturbative QCD, and how these calculations are performed, with particular care being devoted to heavy flavor contributions. We discuss similarities and differences compared to the usual pQCD approach based on factorization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the EPOS4 framework can recover factorization at large transverse momentum in a rigorous parallel scattering scenario with energy-momentum conservation, but only for $p_t$ values larger than the relevant saturation scales of the multiple scattering contributions, not universally as the abstract might suggest.

## Method

The authors relate the EPOS4 "single scattering module" (cut Pomeron $G$) to perturbative QCD via a function $G_{\mathrm{QCD}}$ that contains a cut parton ladder based on DGLAP parton evolutions from projectile and target sides with an elementary QCD cross section in the middle. They implement a backward parton evolution method for the first time in the EPOS framework, and compute integrated and differential parton-parton cross sections for 12 different reaction classes, treating heavy flavor contributions (charm and bottom) with distinct kinematics from light-flavor processes like $gg\to gg$.

## Key numbers

- The low virtuality cutoff $Q^2$ in the DGLAP evolution is usually taken constant around $1$ GeV, but the framework allows any value
- The saturation scale $Q_{\mathrm{sat}}^2$ depends on the connection number $N_{\mathrm{conn}}$ and "compensates" the $N_{\mathrm{conn}}$ dependence of the deformation function $R_{\mathrm{deform}}$
- The Pomeron energy fraction $x_{\mathrm{PE}} = x^+ x^- = M_{\mathrm{Pom}}^2 / s$ characterizes cut Pomerons, with larger values favoring high $p_t$ jet production
- The cut parton ladder $G_{\mathrm{QCD}}^{\mathrm{hard,ij}}$ equals the dijet production cross section $\sigma_{\mathrm{hard}}^{ij}$ times a Gaussian impact parameter dependence $\frac{1}{4\pi R_{\mathrm{hard}}^2}\exp(-b^2/4R_{\mathrm{hard}}^2)$

## Context

This work extends the EPOS4 framework introduced by Werner and Guiot (2023) that accommodates factorization and saturation in high-energy collisions, contrasting with the usual multiple parton interactions (MPI) approach which treats hard parton scattering based on multiple parton distribution functions. The paper enables consistent computation of inclusive cross sections as convolutions of EPOS PDFs with elementary pQCD cross sections at large $p_t$, and provides detailed treatment of heavy flavor that was previously absent in the EPOS framework.
