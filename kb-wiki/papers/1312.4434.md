# Electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma and soft photon spectrum in heavy-ion collisions

> We extract the electrical conductivity $σ_0$ of the quark gluon plasma(QGP) and study the effects of magnetic field and chiral anomaly on soft photon azimuthal anisotropy, $v_2$, based on the thermal photon spectrum at $0.4GeV<p_{\perp}<0.6GeV$ at RHIC energy. As a basis for our analysis, we derive the behavior of retarded photon self energy of a strongly interacting neutral plasma in hydrodynamic regime in the presence of magnetic field and chiral anomaly. By evolving the resulting soft thermal photon production rate over the realistic hydrodynamic background and comparing the results with the preliminary data from the PHENIX Collaboration, we found that the electrical conductivity at QGP temperature is in the range: $0.4<σ_0/(e^{2}T) <1.1$, which is comparable with recent studies on lattice. We also compare the contribution from the magnetic field and chiral anomaly to soft thermal photon $v_{2}$ with the data. We argue that at LHC, the chiral magnetic wave would give negative contribution to photon $v_2$.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is extracted from soft thermal photon spectrum data at RHIC, yielding a range of 0.4 < σ₀/(e²T) < 1.1, but this extraction assumes the hydrodynamic expression for the photon emission rate applies at the low transverse momentum bin of 0.4–0.6 GeV and neglects magnetic field effects in the conductivity determination.

## Method

The author derives the retarded photon self-energy in the hydrodynamic regime for a neutral strongly coupled plasma in the presence of a homogeneous magnetic field and chiral anomaly, obtaining the soft thermal photon production rate. This rate is then evolved over a realistic hydrodynamic background using the VISH2+1 viscous hydro code with boost invariance, lattice-based equation of state s95p-PCE, and η/s = 0.20, and the resulting photon spectrum is compared with PHENIX Collaboration data at √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Electrical conductivity range: 0.4 < σ₀/(e²T) < 1.1 at typical QGP temperature, extracted from photon data at 0.4 GeV < p_T < 0.6 GeV at RHIC energy
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at τ_i = 0.4 fm and ends on an isothermal surface at T_dec = 130 MeV
- For magnetic field lifetime τ_B > 2–3 fm, the magnetic field contribution to soft photon v₂ becomes comparable to experimental results
- The speed of chiral magnetic wave v_χ at RHIC is around 0.1–0.4 using lattice-measured susceptibility
- At LHC, v_χ approaches 1 due to much larger magnetic field, giving negative contribution to photon v₂

## Context

This work provides the first direct estimation of QGP electrical conductivity based on soft photon production with realistic hydrodynamic simulation, offering an alternative to lattice QCD extractions that require non-trivial analytic continuation (Ding 2010, Brandt 2012, Amato 2013, Aarts 2007). The results are comparable with lattice measurements finding 0.33 C_EM ≤ σ/T ≤ 1 C_EM at T = 1.45 T_c, and also consistent with transport approach results (Cassing 2013). The study extends previous work on magnetic field effects in photon production (Basar 2012, Tuchin 2010) and enables future investigation of chiral magnetic wave signatures through photon v₄ measurements.
