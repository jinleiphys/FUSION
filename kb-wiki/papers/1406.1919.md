# Effect of temperature on the effective mass and the neutron skin of nuclei

> We study the finite temperature Hartree-Fock-BCS approximation for selected stable Sn nuclei with zero-range Skyrme forces. Hartree Fock BCS approximation allows for a straightforward interpretation of the results since it involves u and v's which are not matrices as in HFB. Pairing transitions from superfluid to the normal state are studied with respect to the temperature. The temperature dependence of the nuclear radii and neutron skin are also analyzed. An increase of proton and neutron radii is obtained in neutron rich nuclei especially above the critical temperature. Using different Skyrme energy functionals, it is found that the correlation between the effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter and the critical temperature depends on the pairing prescription. The temperature dependence of the nucleon effective mass is also investigated, showing that proton and neutron effective masses display different behavior below and above the critical temperature, due to the small temperature dependence of the density.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the correlation between the effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter and the critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal phase transition depends on the pairing prescription, and that proton and neutron effective masses display different behavior below and above the critical temperature due to the small temperature dependence of the density.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-BCS (FT-HFBCS) calculations for even-even Tin isotopes (A=106 to A=124) using zero-range Skyrme forces (SLy5, SGII, SIII, SkM*, and 14 different Skyrme interactions for the effective mass study) with a zero-range density-dependent surface pairing interaction. The pairing strength was fixed for each nucleus according to the phenomenological rule Δ = 12/√A MeV, and calculations were carried out for temperatures up to 1.5 MeV in a 20 fm box with spherical symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for 120Sn and 112Sn nuclei was found at around 0.65 MeV
- The critical temperature and pairing gap at zero temperature follow the relation Tc ≈ 0.57 Δ(T=0) for the Skyrme interactions used
- The neutron skin of 120Sn increases by about 3% at temperatures above 1 MeV
- The change in proton effective mass is about 1.13% from T=0 to T=1.5 MeV
- The change in effective k-mass across all calculations ranges between 0.15% and 1.1% from T=0 MeV to T=1.5 MeV
- The pairing strength was fixed at V0 = 680 MeV·fm³ for the effective mass correlation study
- The pairing window was taken as 20 levels for both protons and neutrons, with an energy difference of around 15 MeV between the Fermi level and the last considered level

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature mean-field studies, including the finite-temperature HFB calculations of Khan et al. (2007) which found critical temperatures around 0.7 MeV for 124Sn with SLy4, and the earlier Hartree-Fock-BCS work of Sano and Yamasaki (1963) which established the Tc ≈ 0.567 Δ(T=0) relation. The results enable more accurate modeling of nuclear properties at astrophysically relevant temperatures (1-2 MeV) for applications such as electron capture rates in stellar core collapse and r-process nucleosynthesis, where the temperature dependence of effective masses and neutron skins may be significant.
