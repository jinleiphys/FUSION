# Foundations of Strangeness Nuclear Physics derived from chiral Effective Field Theory

> Dense compact objects like neutron stars or black holes have always been one of Gerry Brown's favorite research topics. This is closely related to the effects of strangeness in nuclear physics. Here, we review the chiral Effective Field Theory approach to interactions involving nucleons and hyperons, the possible existence of strange dibaryons, the fate of hyperons in nuclear matter and the present status of three-body forces involving hyperons and nucleons.

## Key claim

This review argues that chiral Effective Field Theory (EFT) provides a systematic and theoretically well-founded framework for describing hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, but the approach remains exploratory due to scarce scattering data (approximately 35 data points) and the need to impose SU(3) symmetry on contact interactions, with SU(3) breaking introduced only at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors construct baryon-baryon potentials using Weinberg power counting in chiral perturbation theory, where the potential is calculated order-by-order and then inserted into a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation to generate scattering and bound states. At leading order (LO), the potential includes one-meson-exchange graphs and six four-baryon contact terms without derivatives; at next-to-leading order (NLO), two-meson-exchange diagrams and contact interactions with two derivatives are added. The low-energy constants are fitted to the available hyperon-nucleon scattering data and nucleon-nucleon phase shifts, with physical meson and baryon masses used throughout to account for SU(3) breaking.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 35 data points exist for hyperon-nucleon (YN) scattering, supplemented by binding energies of a few light hyper-nuclei
- The cutoff variation range for NLO calculations is Λ = 500...650 MeV, while for LO it is Λ = 550...700 MeV
- The Λ single-particle potential in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is U_Λ(k=0) = -28...-24 MeV, consistent with the empirical value of about -28 MeV
- The Scheerbaum factor for the Λ hyperon is S_Λ = -3.7 MeV·fm^5, within the phenomenological range of -4.6 to -3.0 MeV·fm^5
- For the neutral Σ hyperon, the Scheerbaum factor is S_Σ = -15...-18 MeV·fm^5 at NLO
- The ΛΛ scattering length in the ¹S₀ channel is a ≈ -0.65 fm with effective range r ≈ 6 fm
- The three thresholds for ΛΛ, ΣΣ, and ΞN are located at 2231.2, 2257.7, and 2385.0 MeV, respectively
- When adjusting the H-dibaryon binding energy to the NPLQCD lattice value at M_π = 389 MeV, the binding energy is reduced by 7 MeV when going to the physical point
- SU(3) breaking reduces the H-dibaryon binding energy by as much as 60 MeV compared to an SU(3) symmetric interaction with degenerate two-baryon thresholds

## Context

This review extends the chiral EFT approach previously applied successfully to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Epelbaum 2008, Machleidt 2011) into the strangeness sector, building on earlier work by Polinder and the authors (2006) at LO and Haidenbauer et al. (2013) at NLO. The work addresses the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star physics, where the equation of state with hyperons is too soft to allow two-solar-mass neutron stars, and provides the theoretical foundation for interpreting upcoming experimental data from J-PARC and FAIR. The three-body forces derived here, using decuplet saturation to estimate low-energy constants following the resonance saturation hypothesis of Bernard (1996), are intended for future calculations of hyper-nuclei and neutron star matter.
