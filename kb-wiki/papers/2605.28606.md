# Fractional short-time dynamics in driven quantum gases

> Quantum gases with short-range attractive interaction have a tendency to form pairs. For time-dependent interaction we find that the pairing amplitude at small separation satisfies a fractional differential equation (FDE). We derive analytic solutions of the pairing evolution for sudden interaction quenches and power-law drives toward resonant scattering. We observe universal short-time dynamics governed by a conformal fixed point at which the momentum distribution exhibits nonthermal, self-similar scaling in time, in quantitative agreement with experiment. At longer times, many-body effects induce relaxation toward an equilibrium state. In this limit, the FDE turns into a Müller-Israel-Stewart type equation that describes a hydrodynamic attractor approaching equilibrium.

## Key claim

For dilute quantum gases with time-dependent short-range attractive interactions, the pairing amplitude at small separation satisfies a fractional differential equation (FDE) that is nonlocal in time, and the short-time dynamics is universal and governed by a conformal fixed point, with the caveat that this universality holds specifically for the short-time regime before many-body effects induce relaxation.

## Method

The authors derive a boundary Schrödinger equation for the pairing amplitude η(t) by evaluating the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in the limit r→0, which yields a fractional differential equation with a Riemann-Liouville half-derivative operator D_t^{1/2}. They solve this FDE analytically for two dynamical protocols: a sudden quench to constant scattering length and a continuous power-law drive a^{-1}(t) = A t^{-B} toward resonance. For the many-body regime, they introduce a tempered fractional derivative with phenomenological decay time τ to model dissipation, and show that the FDE reduces to a Müller-Israel-Stewart type ordinary differential equation at long times.

## Key numbers

- For a quench from noninteracting to unitarity (1/a→0), the pairing amplitude follows conformal scaling η(t) = (2/√π)√(iD₀t) with D₀ = ħ/m
- The speed limit for correlation growth is d|η(t)|²/dt ≤ (4/π)D₀
- The nonthermal fixed point scaling exponents are α=3 and β=1/2 for the momentum distribution n(k,t) = t^α f_s(kt^β)
- The contact density at equilibrium for the experimental comparison is C = 0.085(5)k_F⁴ at T/T_F = 0.26, yielding τ = 0.95(5)τ_F
- For a quench from noninteracting to resonance with finite density, the pairing amplitude solution is η(t) = √(iD₀τ) erf(√(t/τ))

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of quench dynamics in ultracold quantum gases, building on the contact formalism of Tan (2008) and the boundary condition approach of Qi (2021). The fractional differential equation description provides a unified treatment connecting early-time nonthermal fixed point scaling, previously studied in the context of heavy-ion collisions by Heller (2015) and Berges (2021), with late-time hydrodynamic attractor behavior predicted for ultracold gases by Fujii (2024) and Mazeliauskas (2026). The predicted scaling of the momentum distribution shows quantitative agreement with experimental data from Yi (2025), and the approach enables analytic solutions for arbitrary power-law drives of the scattering length.
