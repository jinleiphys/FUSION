# Axial-vector neutral-current measurements in coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments

> Coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) is predominantly governed by vector neutral-current interactions, with subleading contributions arising from the axial current in nuclei with non-zero ground-state spin. Experimentally, the extraction of axial-current contributions has been so far of little interest, mainly because of the challenges its measurement entail. In this work, we investigate the relative size of the vector and axial components for target materials currently employed by the neutrino and dark matter experimental communities. We identify fluorine-based compounds as the most promising targets for probing the axial-current event rate. Among them, octafluoropropane ($\text{C}_3\text{F}_8$) emerges as a particularly suitable candidate, given its widespread use in spin-dependent dark matter searches and its relevance for upcoming dedicated CE$ν$NS experiments. Considering both pion decay-at-rest and reactor neutrino fluxes, we show that such measurements can allow an indirect determination of the axial coupling at the $\sim 10\%$ level, depending on flux uncertainties and detector thresholds. We further emphasize that measurements of the axial current will allow to probe spin-dependent new physics scenarios through CE$ν$NS.

## Key claim
The axial-vector neutral-current contribution to coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS), while subdominant, can be experimentally accessed using fluorine-based targets like octafluoropropane (C3F8), enabling an indirect determination of the axial-vector coupling gA at the ~10% level depending on flux uncertainties and detector thresholds.

## Method
The authors calculated vector and axial event rates for various target materials using neutrino fluxes from pion decay-at-rest (ESS-like spallation source) and nuclear reactors, employing the Helm parametrization for the weak-charge form factor and ab initio shell-model calculations for the axial form factor. They compared the relative size of vector and axial components across heavy targets (CsI, NaI, Xe, Ge) and light targets (fluorine compounds), then performed a chi-squared forecast analysis for gA extraction using C3F8 detectors with varying fiducial masses, recoil thresholds, and flux uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- For heavy targets with 50 kg fiducial mass, 2 keV threshold, and 3 years exposure at ESS: vector events range from 25967 (Ge) to 39773 (Xe), while axial events range from 4 (Xe) to 17 (Ge)
- For C3F8 at spallation source: axial-to-vector suppression factor of ~25; for reactor neutrinos: suppression factor of ~10
- Reference value for axial-vector coupling: gA = 1.27641 (from Markisch 2018)
- Reference value for strange axial-vector coupling: gA^s = -0.085 (from HERMES 2006)
- With 350 kg C3F8, 2 keV threshold, and 1% flux uncertainty: gA extraction at ~10% precision level
- Flux uncertainty scenarios considered: realistic 5% and optimistic 1%

## Context
This work extends the study of subleading effects in CEvNS, which has been dominated by vector neutral-current interactions since the first observation by COHERENT (2017). It builds on spin-dependent dark matter search techniques from PICO (2019) using C3F8 bubble chambers, and on ab initio axial form factor calculations by Hoferichter (2020) and Klos (2013). The paper enables future measurements of the axial-vector coupling complementary to cold neutron experiments (Markisch 2018), and opens the possibility of probing spin-dependent new physics scenarios through CEvNS that would remain hidden in standard vector-dominated analyses.
