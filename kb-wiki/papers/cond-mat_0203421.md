# Universality in the Three-Body Problem for 4He Atoms

> The two-body scattering length a for 4He atoms is much larger than their effective range r_s. As a consequence, low-energy few-body observables have universal characteristics that are independent of the interaction potential. Universality implies that, up to corrections suppressed by r_s/a, all low-energy three-body observables are determined by a and a three-body parameter Λ_*. We give simple expressions in terms of a and Λ_* for the trimer binding energy equation, the atom-dimer scattering phase shifts, and the rate for three-body recombination at threshold. We determine Λ_* for several 4He potentials from the calculated binding energy of the excited state of the trimer and use it to obtain the universality predictions for the other low-energy observables. We also use the calculated values for one potential to estimate the effective range corrections for the other potentials.

# Universality in the Three-Body Problem for 4He Atoms

## Key claim

Low-energy three-body observables for 4He atoms are determined by only the two-body scattering length a and a three-body parameter Λ_*, up to corrections suppressed by r_s/a (the ratio of effective range to scattering length). The authors determine Λ_* from the calculated binding energy of the excited trimer state and use it to predict other observables, but note that the universality predictions have an accuracy of about 10% at leading order, improving to about 1% when effective range corrections are included.

## Method

The authors collect calculated binding energies for the 4He trimer from Faddeev equation solutions for four interatomic potentials (LM2M2, TTY, HFDHE2, HFD-B) and use the excited state binding energy B_3^(1) to determine the three-body parameter Λ_* via Efimov's equation. They then use this parameter to predict the ground state trimer binding energy B_3^(0), atom-dimer scattering phase shifts, and the three-body recombination rate at threshold, comparing these predictions to direct numerical calculations. Effective range corrections are estimated by assuming the deviation from leading-order universality scales linearly with r_s/a, using the HFD-B potential as a reference.

## Key numbers

- The 4He two-body scattering length a = 104 Å (derived from experiment), with effective range r_s ≈ 7.3 Å, giving r_s/a ≈ 0.07
- The dimer binding energy derived from experiment: B_2 = (1.1^{+0.3}_{-0.2}) mK
- The universal ratio of successive Efimov binding energies approaches 515 for deeper states
- For the LM2M2 potential: calculated B_3^(0) = 125.9 mK, B_3^(1) = 2.28 mK; leading-order universality prediction for B_3^(0) = 130.3 mK (using B_2 as input) or 147.4 mK (using a as input)
- For the TTY potential: calculated B_3^(0) = 125.8 mK, B_3^(1) = 2.28 mK; next-to-leading order universality prediction for B_3^(0) = 127 ± 2 mK
- The three-body parameter Λ_* ≈ 0.0107 Å^{-1} for LM2M2 and TTY potentials (using a_B and B_3^(0) as input), with values for other potentials differing by less than 3%
- The universal function Δ(ξ) parametrizations deviate from numerical results by less than 0.013, with discontinuities at ξ boundaries less than 0.016

## Context

This work extends the Efimov theory (Efimov, 1971) of universal three-body states for systems with large scattering length, applying it specifically to 4He atoms where the scattering length is accidentally large. The authors go beyond earlier work by Frederico, Tomio, Delfino, and Amorim (1999, 2000) by providing explicit parametrizations for multiple low-energy observables including atom-dimer scattering phase shifts and three-body recombination rates, and by estimating effective range corrections. The results enable quantitative predictions for cold atom experiments with 4He and establish a framework for describing three-body observables in other systems with large scattering lengths, such as those near Feshbach resonances.
