# Pauli-principle driven correlations in four-neutron nuclear decays

> Mechanism of simultaneous non-sequential four-neutron ($4n$) emission (or `true' $4n$-decay) has been considered in phenomenological five-body approach. This approach is analogous to the model of the direct decay to the continuum often applied to $2n$- and $2p$-decays. It is demonstrated that $4n$-decay fragments should have specific energy and angular correlations reflecting strong spatial correlations of `valence' nucleons orbiting in their $4n$-precursors. Due to the Pauli exclusion principle, the valence neutrons are pushed to the symmetry-allowed configurations in the $4n$-precursor structure, which causes a `Pauli focusing' effect. Prospects of the observation of the Pauli focusing have been considered for the $4n$-precursors $^7$H and $^{28}$O. Fingerprints of their nuclear structure or/and decay dynamics are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in true (simultaneous, non-sequential) four-neutron (4n) decays, the Pauli exclusion principle forces valence neutrons into symmetry-allowed orbital configurations, producing distinctive energy and angular correlation patterns ("Pauli focusing") that can serve as fingerprints of the decaying quantum state; however, the authors emphasize that for general cases the model provides only a starting point for qualitative discussion, while for low-energy decays (E_T < 300 keV) the predictions are considered reliable.

## Method

The authors developed a phenomenological five-body direct-decay model, generalizing the improved direct two-proton decay model of Golubkova (2016) to core+4n systems. The decay amplitude is constructed as an antisymmetrized product of single-particle R-matrix-type amplitudes for each core-neutron channel, with total spin J fixed, and the differential decay probability is computed via Monte Carlo integration over the 8-dimensional phase space. For estimating the upper limit of neutron-neutron final-state interaction (FSI) effects, a zero-range s-wave scattering amplitude with the singlet scattering length a_s = -18.9 fm was included.

## Key numbers

- The phase-space energy distribution for any single Jacobi energy has a maximum at ε_1 = 1/8 and a mean value of ε_1 = 1/4.
- The correlated phase-space distribution of two Jacobi energies has a maximum at ε_1 = ε_2 = 1/6.
- For the [s²_{1/2}p²_{3/2}]_0 configuration, the effective n-n scattering length extracted from the FSI-modified distributions is a_s(eff) = -12.5 fm, corresponding to about 44% of the "vacuum intensity" (a_s = -18.9 fm).
- The n-n FSI effect on energy distributions is reasonably small for decay energies E_T < 300 keV and can be neglected for E_T < 100 keV.
- The model was applied to the 4n-precursors ⁷H and ²⁸O, with assumed dominant configurations [p⁴_{3/2}]_0 and [d⁴_{3/2}]_0, respectively, and decay energy E_T = 500 keV for the pure-configuration calculations.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Pauli focusing, previously established for bound three-body core+n+n systems (Danilin 1988, Zhukov 1993) and for three-body decays of ⁶Be and ⁴⁵Fe (Grigorenko 2003, 2009), to the unexplored regime of five-body core+4n decays. The approach is analogous to the direct-decay model widely applied to 2n and 2p decays (Grigorenko 2007, Golubkova 2016). The predicted correlation patterns provide a framework for interpreting future experiments on ⁷H and ²⁸O, which are actively studied as candidates for true 4n emission, and could enable extraction of detailed information about the decay dynamics and nuclear structure of systems beyond the neutron dripline.
