# Particle Number Fluctuations for van der Waals Equation of State

> The van der Waals (VDW) equation of state describes a thermal equilibrium in system of particles, where both repulsive and attractive interactions between them are included. This equation predicts an existence of the 1st order liquid-gas phase transition and the critical point. The standard form of the VDW equation is given by the pressure function in the canonical ensemble (CE) with a fixed number of particles. In the present paper the VDW equation is transformed to the grand canonical ensemble (GCE). We argue that this procedure can be useful for new physical applications. Particularly, the fluctuations of number of particles, which are absent in the CE, can be studied in the GCE. For the VDW equation of state in the GCE the particle number fluctuations are calculated for the whole phase diagram, both outside and inside the liquid-gas mixed phase region. It is shown that the scaled variance of these fluctuations remains finite within the mixed phase and goes to infinity at the critical point. The GCE formulation of the VDW equation of state can be also an important step for its application to a statistical description of hadronic systems, where numbers of different particle species are usually not conserved.

## Key claim

The van der Waals equation of state, originally formulated in the canonical ensemble with a fixed particle number, is transformed to the grand canonical ensemble to enable the study of particle number fluctuations; the scaled variance of these fluctuations remains finite within the liquid-gas mixed phase region and diverges only at the critical point.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical ensemble pressure function for the van der Waals gas, \( p = NT/(V - bN) - a N^2/V^2 \), and reconstruct the free energy \( F(V,T,N) \) by integrating the pressure with respect to volume, using the ideal gas free energy as a boundary condition at infinite volume. From the free energy they derive the chemical potential, which yields a transcendental equation for the particle number density \( n(T,\mu) \) in the grand canonical ensemble. The scaled variance \( \omega[N] = \text{Var}[N]/\langle N \rangle \) is then computed from the density derivative with respect to chemical potential, both for pure phases and for the mixed phase region defined by the Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers

- Critical point parameters: \( T_c = 8a/(27b) \), \( n_c = 1/(3b) \), \( p_c = a/(27b^2) \)
- Scaled variance in pure phases: \( \omega[N] = \left[ 1/(1-bn)^2 - 2an/T \right]^{-1} \)
- At the critical point along \( \rho = 0 \) (where \( \rho = \tilde{n} - 1 \)): \( \omega[N] \cong (4/9) \tau^{-1} \) with \( \tau = \tilde{T} - 1 \)
- At the critical point along \( \tau = 0 \): \( \omega[N] \cong (16/27) \rho^{-2} \)
- Inside the mixed phase near the critical point: \( \omega[N] \cong (16/9) t^{-1} \) with \( t = 1 - \tilde{T} \)
- At \( \tilde{T} = 0.25 \), the gaseous phase density at the phase boundary is \( \tilde{n}_g \cong 5 \times 10^{-5} \) and the liquid phase density is \( \tilde{n}_l \cong 2.75 \)

## Context

This work extends the excluded volume model of hadron repulsion, previously formulated in the grand canonical ensemble by VdW-1, VdW-2, and the mean-field approach of mf-1992, mf-1995, mf-2014, by including attractive interactions via the full van der Waals equation. The particle number fluctuations in the excluded volume model were calculated by GHN, and the present paper generalizes those results to the van der Waals case. The grand canonical formulation enables application to multi-component hadron gas descriptions in heavy-ion collisions, where particle numbers are not conserved, and provides a framework for studying fluctuation observables near the critical point in nuclear matter phase diagrams.
