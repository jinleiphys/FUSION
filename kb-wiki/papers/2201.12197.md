# New tool for kinematic regime estimation in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering

> We introduce a new phenomenological tool based on momentum region indicators to guide the analysis and interpretation of semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering measurements. The new tool, referred to as "affinity", is devised to help visualize and quantify the proximity of any experimental kinematic bin to a particular hadron production region, such as that associated with transverse momentum dependent factorization. We apply the affinity estimator to existing HERMES and COMPASS data and expected data from Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider. We also provide an interactive notebook based on Machine Learning for fast evaluation of affinity.

## Key claim

The paper introduces "affinity," a new phenomenological tool that quantifies the proximity of any experimental kinematic bin in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) to a particular hadron production region (such as the transverse momentum dependent factorization region), but the tool's reliability depends on prior assumptions about partonic momenta and the choice of threshold values for the region indicator ratios, which are tuned to match existing phenomenological analyses rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors define region indicators (ratios \(R_i\) of partonic and hadronic momenta) that characterize current, target, and central fragmentation regions in SIDIS, then construct a Bayesian estimator called "affinity" that marginalizes over parton-level momenta using Monte Carlo sampling with Gaussian priors for parton virtualities and intrinsic transverse momenta (mean 0.5 GeV, width 0.5 GeV) and flat priors for momentum fractions and angular variables. The proximity function \(\Theta\) selects regions in \(R_i\)-space using threshold values (e.g., \(R_i^{\rm max}=0.3\) for "small" and \(R_i^{\rm min}=0.9\) for "large"), tuned so that kinematic bins where the analysis of Scimemi (2019) found good TMD phenomenology yield affinity near 100%. The tool is applied to existing HERMES and COMPASS data and expected data from Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider, and a neural network trained with TensorFlow is provided in a Google Colab interactive notebook for fast evaluation.

## Key numbers

- Threshold for "small" region indicator values: \(R_i^{\rm max}({\rm TMD}) = 0.3\) for \(i=0,1,2\), tuned to match the cuts \(Q > 2\) GeV and \(q_T/Q < 0.25\) from Scimemi (2019)
- Threshold for "large" region indicator values: \(R_i^{\rm min}({\rm region}) = 0.9\), defined as 3 times the "small" threshold
- Gaussian prior parameters for parton virtualities and intrinsic transverse momenta: mean \(m = 0.5\) GeV, width \(\Delta = 0.5\) GeV, guided by nonperturbative QCD fits of TMD widths from Anselmino (2013) and Signori (2013)
- Momentum fraction prior window: \(\epsilon = 0.1\) around \(\xb\) and \(z_h\)

## Context

This work extends the quantitative criteria for classifying fragmentation regions introduced by Boglione (2016) and Boglione (2019), who proposed region indicator ratios \(R_i\) to delineate current, target, and central fragmentation in SIDIS beyond simple cuts on \(P_{hT}\) or \(q_T\). The affinity tool enables systematic assessment of whether data at given kinematics are suitable for TMD factorization analyses, addressing the need identified by the HERMES Collaboration (Airapetian, 2020) for such region classification. The tool enables future extractions of TMD parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions from Jefferson Lab, COMPASS, and the Electron-Ion Collider to be performed with quantified confidence in the applicability of TMD factorization, and can be refined as more sophisticated priors for partonic kinematics become available from nonperturbative QCD or lattice calculations.
