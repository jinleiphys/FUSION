# Extracing the number of short-range corerlated nucleon pairs from inclusive electron scattering data

> The extraction of the relative abundances of short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs from inclusive electron scattering is studied using the generalized contact formalism (GCF) with several nuclear interaction models. GCF calculations can reproduce the observed scaling of the cross-section ratios for nuclei relative to deuterium at high-$x_B$ and large-$Q^2$, $a_2=(σ_A/A)/(σ_d/2)$. In the non-relativistic instant-form formulation, the calculation is very sensitive to the model parameters and only reproduces the data using parameters that are inconsistent with ab-initio many-body calculations. Using a light-cone GCF formulation significantly decreases this sensitivity and improves the agreement with ab-initio calculations. The ratio of similar mass isotopes, such as $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca, should be sensitive to the nuclear asymmetry dependence of SRCs, but is found to also be sensitive to low-energy nuclear structure. Thus the empirical association of SRC pair abundances with the measured $a_2$ values is only accurate to about $20\%$. Improving this will require cross-section calculations that reproduce the data while properly accounting for both nuclear structure and relativistic effects.

## Key claim

The empirical association of SRC pair abundances with measured $a_2$ values from inclusive electron scattering is only accurate to about 20%, because the cross-section ratio is sensitive to both low-energy nuclear structure and relativistic effects that are not captured by the simplistic interpretation of $a_2$ as a direct count of deuteron-like $np$ pairs.

## Method

The authors used the generalized contact formalism (GCF) in both non-relativistic instant-form and light-cone formulations to calculate inclusive $(e,e')$ cross-section ratios for nuclei relative to deuterium at high $x_B$ and large $Q^2$. They integrated previously derived GCF $(e,e'N)$ and $(e,e'NN)$ cross-sections over knocked-out nucleons to obtain inclusive cross-sections, then compared these calculations to experimental data while varying model parameters including nuclear contacts, pair center-of-mass momentum distribution width $\sigma_{CM}$, and excitation energy of the residual $A-2$ system $E^*_{A-2}$.

## Key numbers

- The empirical association of SRC pair abundances with measured $a_2$ values is only accurate to about $20\%$.
- For $^4$He, the light-cone GCF fitted contact ratios for deuteron-like $np$ pairs are only $20-30\%$ higher than Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations, while instant-form fitted contacts are $50-150\%$ higher.
- A 15 MeV change in $E^*_{A-2}$ changes the instant-form cross-section by approximately $20\%$ for $^4$He.
- For the $^{48}$Ca/$^{40}$Ca ratio, a 10 MeV change in either $\Delta E^*$ or $\Delta \sigma_{CM}$ induces a large change in the extracted contact ratio.

## Context

This work extends the generalized contact formalism (GCF) developed by Weiss et al. (2015, 2016, 2018) and Cruz-Torres et al. (2019) to study inclusive electron scattering, building on earlier SRC studies by Frankfurt et al. (1993), Egiyan et al. (2002, 2006), and Fomin et al. (2012). The results contradict the traditional interpretation that $a_2$ directly measures the number of deuteron-like $np$ SRC pairs, and show that previous corrections for pair center-of-mass motion used by Fomin et al. (2012) were oversimplified. The findings enable more precise extraction of SRC pair abundances by demonstrating that future analyses must properly account for both nuclear structure and relativistic effects, with direct implications for studies of the nuclear symmetry energy, neutron-star properties, and the EMC effect.
