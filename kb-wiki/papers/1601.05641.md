# Partial Conservation Law in a Schematic Single j Shell Model

> We report the discovery of a partial conservation law obeyed by a schematic Hamiltonian of two protons and two neutrons in a j shell. In our Hamiltonian the interaction matrix element of two nucleons with combined angular momentum J is linear in J for even J and constant for odd J. It turns out that in some stationary states the sum J_p + J_n of the angular momenta J_p and J_n of the proton and neutron pairs is conserved. The energies of these states are given by a linear function of J_p + J_n. The systematics of their occurrence is described and explained.

# Partial Conservation Law in a Schematic Single j Shell Model

## Key claim
The authors report the discovery of a partial conservation law in a schematic Hamiltonian of two protons and two neutrons in a single j shell, where the interaction matrix element is linear in J for even J and zero for odd J. The conservation of J_p + J_n holds only in some stationary states (called "special" states), not all states, and the claim is conditional on the specific schematic interaction chosen.

## Method
The authors constructed a schematic Hamiltonian for two protons and two neutrons in a single j shell, with two-nucleon interaction matrix elements E^J = J for even J and E^J = 0 for odd J. They performed numerical diagonalizations for various j values up to j = 15/2, identifying stationary states where J_p + J_n is conserved. They then derived analytic expressions for the wave functions of these special states using vector coupling coefficients and 9-j symbols, and explained the systematics through a combinatorial analysis of the dimensions of subspaces with fixed J_p + J_n and fixed isospin T = 0 versus T = 2.

## Key numbers
- For j = 9/2, the even I yrast band from I = 8 to 16 has absolute energies E = 3I/2, with wave functions containing only components with J_p + J_n = I.
- For j = 9/2, special states with odd I = 7, 9, 11, 13 have absolute energies E = 15, 15, 18, 21 respectively, all equal to 3(J_p + J_n)/2.
- Special states occur for all j from 3/2 to 15/2, with the highest total angular momentum I = 4j - 2 (e.g., I = 28 for j = 15/2).
- The special state with (J_p + J_n, I) = (10, 3) for j = 7/2 is one of four degenerate levels familiar from Robinson and Zamick (prior work).
- For j = 5/2, the (J_p + J_n, I) = (4, 2) special state predicted by rule 2 does not appear.

## Context
This work extends the schematic model approach of Brown and Bolsterli (Physical Review Letters, early work on dipole states) and the seniority scheme of Racah to a system of two protons and two neutrons in a single j shell. The specific interaction is motivated by empirical matrix elements from the 1f_{7/2} shell, as extracted by Zamick and Robinson (prior work) from the spectrum of 42Sc. The results connect to earlier studies by Robinson and Zamick (prior work) who found degenerate states with J_p + J_n = 10 for j = 7/2 using properties of 9-j symbols. The findings enable further analytic understanding of partial conservation laws in schematic nuclear models and may guide studies of more realistic interactions.
