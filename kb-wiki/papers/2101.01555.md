# A model investigation of the longitudinal broadening of the transverse momentum two-particle correlator

> The Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) is used to investigate the longitudinal broadening of the transverse momentum two-particle correlator $C_{2}\left(Δη,Δφ\right)$, and its utility to extract the specific shear viscosity, $η/s$, of the quark-gluon plasma formed in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The results from these model studies indicate that the longitudinal broadening of $C_{2}\left(Δη,Δφ\right)$ is sensitive to the magnitude of $η/s$. However, reliable extraction of the longitudinal broadening of the correlator requires the suppression of possible self-correlations associated with the definition of the collision centrality.

## Key claim

The longitudinal broadening of the transverse momentum two-particle correlator C2(Δη,Δφ) is sensitive to the specific shear viscosity η/s of the quark-gluon plasma, but reliable extraction of this broadening requires suppression of self-correlations that arise when the same particles used to compute the correlator are also used to define collision centrality.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING model and the AMPT model (v2.26t9b) to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, with approximately 7 million and 5 million events respectively. In AMPT, the input η/s was varied over the range 0.1–0.3 by adjusting the screening mass μ while fixing αs = 0.47 and T = 378 MeV, using charged particles with 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and |η| < 1.0. The longitudinal width (RMS) of C2(Δη) was extracted for different centrality selections, and the broadening Δσ² = σc² − σ0² was computed as the difference between the most central and most peripheral collision RMS values.

## Key numbers

- η/s values studied: 0.1, 0.2, and 0.3, varied via the screening mass μ in AMPT with αs = 0.47 and T = 378 MeV
- Charged particle selection: 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and |η| < 1.0
- The pT distributions for 30–40% central collisions showed less than 9% difference when η/s varied from 0.1 to 0.3
- Δσ² shows a monotonic decrease with increasing η/s over the range 0.1–0.3
- The RMS values of C2(Δη) show a linear increase with log(⟨Nch⟩), with a smaller slope for η/s = 0.3 compared to η/s = 0.1

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the C2 correlator by Gavin (2006) and Sharma (2008), who proposed linking its longitudinal broadening to η/s via the relation Δσ² = (4/Tc)(η/s)(1/τ0 − 1/τc,f). The study uses the AMPT model, which has been widely employed for heavy-ion collision simulations by Lin (2004) and others, and benchmarks its η/s sensitivity against experimental v2(pT) data from PHENIX (Adare 2011). The results suggest that experimental measurements of the longitudinal broadening of C2(Δη) could provide additional constraints for precision extraction of η/s, complementing existing constraints from anisotropic flow measurements.
