# Production of cold fragments in nucleus-nucleus collisions in the Fermi-energy domain

> The reaction mechanism of nucleus-nucleus collisions at projectile energies around the Fermi energy is investigated with emphasis on the production of fragmentation-like residues. The results of simulations are compared to experimental mass distributions of elements with Z = 21 - 29 observed in the reactions 86Kr+124,112Sn at 25 AMeV. The model of incomplete fusion is modified and a component of excitation energy of the cold fragment dependent on isospin asymmetry is introduced. The modifications in the model of incomplete fusion appear consistent with both overall model framework and available experimental data. A prediction is provided for the production of very neutron-rich nuclei using a secondary beam of 132Sn where e.g. the reaction 132Sn+238U at 28 AMeV appears as a possible alternative to the use of fragmentation reactions at higher energies.

## Key claim

The authors modify the incomplete fusion model by introducing an isospin-asymmetry-dependent component of excitation energy for the cold fragment, which improves agreement with experimental mass distributions of neutron-rich residues below nickel (Z=21-29) in reactions of 86Kr with 124,112Sn at 25 AMeV, where the standard model overpredicts yields of the most neutron-rich products.

## Method

The authors compare simulations using the incomplete fusion (ICF) model, combined with the deep-inelastic transfer model for peripheral collisions and the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) for de-excitation, to experimental mass distributions of elements Z=21-29 measured at 4 degrees in the reactions 86Kr+124,112Sn at 25 AMeV. They modify the ICF model by adding an excitation energy term for the cold spectator fragment that depends on the difference between its mass and the mass corresponding to the initial N/Z of the projectile, scaled by the relative velocity between hot and cold fragments and the kinetic energy per nucleon above the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers

- Reactions studied: 86Kr+124,112Sn at 25 AMeV projectile energy, with experimental mass distributions of elements Z=21-29 observed around 4 degrees
- Prediction for 132Sn+238U at 28 AMeV as an alternative to fragmentation reactions at higher energies
- For the reaction 132Sn+238U at 28 AMeV, production cross sections for Z=46 are comparable to EPAX and COFRA fragmentation predictions, while for lower atomic numbers the Fermi-energy reaction yields more favorable cross sections
- In-target yields calculated assuming 132Sn secondary beam intensity of 10^12 s^-1 and target thickness 40 mg/cm^2 for the 132Sn+238U reaction at 28 AMeV
- For Z=40, the in-target yield from the modified ICF model exceeds the COFRA fragmentation value

## Context

This work extends the incomplete fusion model previously applied to reactions such as 86Kr+64Ni (Souliotis et al., 2003) and 124Sn+27Al (Veselsky et al., 2004), where the N/Z trend of hot projectile-like fragments was reproduced, to now address the N/Z trend of cold fragmentation-like residues. The modification is motivated by the overprediction of neutron-rich yields below nickel in the standard ICF model, and the results suggest that the reaction 132Sn+238U at 28 AMeV could compete with high-energy fragmentation of 132Sn beams (using codes EPAX and COFRA) for producing very neutron-rich nuclei around N=82, especially if fragmentation cross sections are overpredicted by EPAX as seen in measurements at MSU (Stolz et al., 2005).
