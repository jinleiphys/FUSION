# Chiral Magnetic Effect from Q-balls

> We apply a generic framework of linear sigma models for revealing a mechanism of the mysterious phenomenon, the chiral magnetic effect, in quark-gluon plasma. An electric current arises along a background magnetic field, which is given rise to by Q-balls (non-topological solitons) of the linear sigma model with axial anomaly. We find additional alternating current due to quark mass terms. The hadronic Q-balls, baby boson stars, may be created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) in quark-gluon plasma can be generated by Q-balls (non-topological solitons) of a linear sigma model, producing an electric current along a background magnetic field; the current includes a small alternating component due to quark mass terms and the U(1)A anomaly, with the magnitude set dynamically by the Q-ball frequency.

## Method

The authors construct a generic linear sigma model for scalar mesons (Phi) with chiral symmetry, a U(1)A anomaly term, and electromagnetic couplings via anomalous terms. They restrict to diagonal pseudo-scalars and the sigma field, then consider a one-flavor model to construct spherically symmetric Q-ball solutions with a time-dependent phase (eta' = omega t). The electric current arising from the anomalous coupling is computed for a constant background magnetic field, and the effect of a small explicit symmetry-breaking term (quark mass and anomaly) is treated perturbatively to find corrections to the current.

## Key numbers

- The magnitude of the CME current is estimated as |J| ~ 10^5 [MeV^3] ~ 10^{-2} [fm^{-3}], using the expected magnetic field eB ~ 10^4 [MeV^2] in heavy-ion collisions and assuming omega_0 ~ Lambda_QCD.
- The Q-ball frequency omega_0 is determined by the linear sigma model potential via omega_0^2 = V'(sigma_0^2) and omega_0^2 sigma_0^2 = V(sigma_0^2), independent of the Q-charge.
- The natural size of the Q-ball is ~ Q^{1/3} [fm] when all dimensionful parameters are of order Lambda_QCD ~ 1 [fm^{-1}].
- The perturbation parameter epsilon = h / (sigma_0 omega_0^2) controls the stability; the condition for stability is h sigma_0 << V(sigma_0^2).

## Context

This work extends the study of the chiral magnetic effect, previously investigated using the P-NJL model (Fukushima 2010), holographic QCD (Rebhan 2009), and lattice QCD (Buividovich 2009), by providing a universal mechanism based on Q-balls in a linear sigma model. The approach relates the CME current to the Q-ball frequency, which plays the role of the chiral chemical potential mu_5 introduced by Fukushima (2008). The prediction of hadronic Q-balls (baby boson stars) that may be created in heavy-ion collisions opens a new direction for experimental searches at RHIC, LHC, and FAIR, and connects to cosmological boson stars studied by Jetzer (1991).
