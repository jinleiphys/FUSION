# Fermion and Photon gap-equations in Minkowski space within the Nakanishi Integral Representation method

> The approach based on the Nakanishi integral representation of n-leg transition amplitudes is extended to the treatment of the self-energies of a fermion and an (IR-regulated) vector boson, in order to pave the way for constructing a comprehensive application of the technique to both gap- and Bethe-Salpeter equations, in Minkowski space. The achieved result, namely a 6-channel coupled system of integral equations, eventually allows one to determine the three Källén-Lehman weights for fully dressing the propagators of fermion and photon. A first consistency check is also provided. The presented formal elaboration points to embed the characteristics of the non-perturbative regime at a more fundamental level. It yields a viable tool in Minkowski space for the phenomenological investigation of strongly interacting theories, within a QFT framework where the dynamical ingredients are made transparent and under control.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Nakanishi integral representation (NIR) method to treat fermion and photon self-energies in Minkowski space, deriving a 6-channel coupled system of integral equations for the Nakanishi weight functions (NWFs) that determine the three Kallen-Lehman weights for fully dressing both propagators, but only for couplings below the critical value α_c ≤ π/3 and within a RI'/MOM renormalization scheme rather than the standard on-shell scheme.

## Method

The authors introduce NIRs for the regularized fermion self-energy (two scalar functions ρ_A and ρ_B) and photon self-energy (ρ_γ), then derive relations between these NWFs and the Kallen-Lehman spectral weights of the corresponding propagators. They construct a vertex function combining the Ball-Chiu longitudinal part (Ball 1980) with a minimal transverse Ansatz (Qin 2013) that ensures multiplicative renormalizability, and apply dimensional regularization with momentum-subtraction renormalization and an IR regulator for the gauge boson. The gap-equations are then transformed into a coupled system of integral equations for the three NWFs, with a first consistency check performed through a single iteration.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling threshold: α_c ≤ π/3, below which the analysis is valid
- Photon multiparticle threshold: s^p_th = 4m^2(ζ), where m(ζ) is the renormalized fermion mass
- The transverse projector T^{μν}(q) satisfies T^μ_μ(q) = 3 in the Landau gauge
- The renormalization conditions are set at the renormalization point ζ: A_R(ζ;ζ) = 0 and B_R(ζ;ζ) = 0

## Context

This work extends the NIR-based approach previously applied to Bethe-Salpeter equations (Kusaka, CK2006, CK2006b, CK2010, FSV2, FSV3) and to fermion self-energies with simpler vertex approximations (Sauli 2001, Sauli 2002, Sauli 2003). It differs from earlier Minkowski-space QED studies such as Jia (2017) (which used quenched approximation with Gauge Technique vertex) by introducing a fully dressed vertex with transverse terms that enforce multiplicative renormalizability. The formalism enables future solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with dressed propagators directly in Minkowski space, providing a tool for phenomenological investigation of strongly interacting theories within a QFT framework where dynamical ingredients are transparent.
