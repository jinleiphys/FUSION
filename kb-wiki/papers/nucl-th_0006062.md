# Effect of differences in proton and neutron density distributions on fission barriers

> The neutron and proton density distributions obtained in constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations with the Gogny force along the fission paths of 232Th, 236U, 238U and 240Pu are analyzed. Significant differences in the multipole deformations of neutron and proton densities are found. The effect on potential energy surfaces and on barrier heights of an additional constraint imposing similar spatial distributions to neutrons and protons, as assumed in macroscopic-microscopic models, is studied.

# Effect of differences in proton and neutron density distributions on fission barriers

## Key claim
The multipole deformations of proton and neutron density distributions along fission paths differ significantly (relative differences often exceed 10%), and imposing equal deformations for protons and neutrons, as assumed in macroscopic-microscopic models, raises fission barrier heights by approximately 1 MeV, which could lead to systematic overestimation of spontaneous fission lifetimes by factors up to 22.

## Method
Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny effective interaction (parameter set D1S) were performed along the fission paths of four actinide nuclei, assuming axial symmetry. Multipole deformation parameters (quadrupole β₂, octupole β₃, hexadecapole β₄) were extracted separately for neutron and proton densities using a surface multipole moment expansion. A second set of calculations imposed an additional constraint forcing the neutron and proton density matrices to be proportional (ρ_n = (N/Z) ρ_p), and the resulting energy differences and barrier height changes were evaluated.

## Key numbers
- Relative neutron-proton deformation difference |β^n_λ − β^p_λ|/β_λ reaches 4% for quadrupole and exceeds 10% for octupole and hexadecapole deformations in ²³²Th beyond the second minimum.
- The energy difference δE_den between constrained and unconstrained HFB calculations is approximately 1.5 MeV on average in ²⁴⁰Pu, ²³⁶U, and ²³⁸U, with fluctuations of order 1 MeV.
- The height of the first fission barrier increases by 1 MeV in ²³⁸U when imposing equal neutron-proton deformations.
- First barrier height increases of approximately 0.4–0.7 MeV are seen in ²³²Th, ²³⁶U, and ²⁴⁰Pu.
- Spontaneous fission lifetimes increase by factors of 9.8 (²³²Th), 6.6 (²³⁶U), 22.4 (²³⁸U), and 2 (²⁴⁰Pu) when the constraint is applied.

## Context
This work extends earlier self-consistent studies of actinide fission using the Gogny force by Berger et al. (1989) and builds on the analysis of neutron-proton deformation differences by Pomorski (1997). It directly tests the assumption in macroscopic-microscopic Strutinsky-type models (e.g., Strutinsky 1968, Sobiczewski, Möller) that neutron and proton potentials share identical multipole deformations. The results suggest that such models may systematically overestimate fission barrier heights by about 1 MeV and spontaneous fission lifetimes by orders of magnitude, motivating future generalization of macroscopic-microscopic approaches to allow independent proton and neutron deformations.
