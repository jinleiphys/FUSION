# Extraction of jet-medium interaction details through jet substructure for inclusive and gamma-tagged jets

> We present a comprehensive study of jet substructure modifications in high-energy heavy-ion collisions using both inclusive jets and $γ$-tagged jets, based on a multi-stage jet evolution model within the Monte Carlo framework JETSCAPE. To investigate hard parton splittings inside jets, we focus on Soft Drop observables. Our results for the groomed splitting radius and groomed jet mass distributions of inclusive jets show a slight narrowing compared to proton-proton baselines. We demonstrate that this apparent narrowing is primarily a selection bias from energy loss, rather than a direct modification of the splitting structure, by analyzing $γ$-tagged jets, where such bias is eliminated or significantly reduced. We also show that quark jets exhibit genuine modifications in their splitting structure, which is not seen in gluon jets. These effects are clearly visible in the substructure of $γ$-tagged jets, which are dominated by quark jets, but are not apparent for inclusive jets. This demonstrates that $γ$-tagged jets offer a powerful probe of medium-induced modifications to the hard splitting structure of jets.

## Key claim

The apparent narrowing of groomed jet substructure observables (splitting radius and jet mass) in inclusive jets is primarily a selection bias from energy loss rather than a direct modification of the splitting structure; genuine medium-induced broadening of hard splittings is revealed only in quark-dominated gamma-tagged jets, where this bias is eliminated or significantly reduced.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using the multi-stage in-medium jet evolution model MATTER+LBT within the JETSCAPEv3.5 AA22 tune, applied to PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with a (2+1)-dimensional freestreaming evolution followed by viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNU) with TRENTO initial conditions. They analyzed Soft Drop observables (groomed splitting radius r_g and groomed jet mass m_g) with parameters z_cut = 0.2 and beta = 0, comparing inclusive jets and gamma-tagged jets, and also performed controlled single-parton-initiated jet simulations with fixed initial parton energy E_init = 140 GeV for both quark and gluon jets.

## Key numbers

- Initial parton energy for single-parton-initiated jet simulations: E_init = 140 GeV
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for PbPb collisions
- Centrality: 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions
- Soft Drop parameters: z_cut = 0.2, beta = 0
- p_T^jet trigger thresholds investigated: 112, 84, 56, and 28 GeV
- p_T^gamma threshold for gamma-tagged jet analysis: 140 < p_T^gamma < 160 GeV
- x_Jgamma = p_T^jet / p_T^gamma used as variable trigger threshold for gamma-tagged jets

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE collaboration's multi-stage jet evolution framework (JETSCAPE 2022, 2023, 2025) by systematically separating selection bias effects from genuine medium modifications in jet substructure. The Soft Drop grooming algorithm (Larkoski 2014) provides access to hard parton splittings, and the authors demonstrate that gamma-tagged jets, which are dominated by quark jets, reveal medium-induced broadening that is obscured in inclusive jet measurements. This enables future experimental studies to extract flavor-dependent jet-medium interaction details by comparing inclusive and gamma-tagged jet substructure observables.
