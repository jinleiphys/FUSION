# Particle ratios at SPS, AGS and SIS

## Key claim
The chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from hadronic abundances at CERN/SPS, BNL/AGS and GSI/SIS all correspond to a fixed energy per hadron of approximately 1 GeV in the local rest frame, independent of beam energy and colliding species. This generalizes Hagedorn's limiting temperature concept to include finite baryon density, yielding a freeze-out curve in the temperature–baryon chemical potential plane rather than a single limiting temperature.

## Method
The authors analyzed fully integrated particle multiplicities from heavy-ion collisions at SPS, AGS and SIS energies using a thermal hadronic gas model. They demonstrated analytically that particle ratios are insensitive to excluded volume corrections, superposition of fireballs with identical temperature and chemical potential, transverse flow, and longitudinal expansion with transverse flow, provided yields are integrated over transverse momentum. Freeze-out temperatures and baryon chemical potentials were extracted by fitting measured particle ratios to the thermal model predictions.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature for Au-Au collisions at 1 A·GeV (GSI/SIS): approximately 50 MeV, with a freeze-out baryon chemical potential of approximately 850 MeV.
- The freeze-out curve corresponds to a fixed energy per hadron of approximately 1 GeV per particle in the hadronic gas rest frame; a dashed line in the figure corresponds to 0.94 GeV per hadron.
- The unified description covers beam energies from 200 A·GeV (CERN/SPS) down to below 1 A·GeV (GSI/SIS).
- For Ni-Ni collisions at 1.9 A·GeV, freeze-out parameters are shown as an open triangle in the T-μB plane; additional points for Au-Au at 0.8 and 1.0 A·GeV and Ni-Ni at 1.0 and 1.8 A·GeV correspond to the lowest temperatures in the dataset.
- The η/π0 ratio at GSI/SIS is a notable exception that does not fit the thermal model expectations.

## Context
This work extends the concept of Hagedorn's limiting temperature (Hagedorn, 1960s) from p-p collisions to heavy-ion collisions by incorporating finite baryon density via the baryon chemical potential. It builds on the observation by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (1990s) that a freeze-out curve exists in the T-μB plane. The analysis unifies results from SPS, AGS and SIS accelerators, and the same thermal description has been applied to LEP e+e- annihilation (Becattini, 1990s) and to p-p and pbar-p collisions at CERN, with a slightly different treatment of the strangeness sector to account for strangeness under-saturation. This enables a unified description of hadronic final states across a wide range of collision energies and systems.
