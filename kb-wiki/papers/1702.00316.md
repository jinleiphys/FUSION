# Exclusive neutrino-production of a charmed meson

> We calculate the leading order in $α_s$ QCD amplitude for exclusive neutrino and antineutrino production of a $D$ pseudoscalar charmed meson on an unpolarized nucleon. We work in the framework of the collinear QCD approach where generalized parton distributions (GPDs) factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. We include both $O(m_c)$ terms in the coefficient functions and $O(M_D)$ mass term contributions in the heavy meson distribution amplitudes. We emphasize the sensitivity of specific observables on the transversity quark GPDs.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the leading-order QCD amplitude for exclusive neutrino and antineutrino production of a charmed D meson on an unpolarized nucleon, including both O(m_c) terms in the coefficient functions and O(M_D) mass term contributions in the heavy meson distribution amplitudes, and they emphasize that specific observables (azimuthal moments) are sensitive to the transversity quark GPDs, though the quantitative predictions depend on the choice of model for these poorly known GPDs.

## Method

The authors work in the collinear QCD factorization framework, where generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. They compute the hard-scattering amplitude for the subprocesses W^+ d → D^+ d, W^+ d → D^0 u, and W^+ g → D^+ g (and the corresponding charge-conjugate and antineutrino channels) at leading order in α_s, including heavy quark mass m_c effects in the propagators and meson mass M_D terms in the D-meson distribution amplitude. They then evaluate the longitudinal and transverse cross sections and the azimuthal moments ⟨cos φ⟩ and ⟨sin φ⟩ using the Goloskokov-Kroll model for chiral-even GPDs and three extreme models for the chiral-odd transversity GPDs H_T, E_T, and \tilde H_T.

## Key numbers

- D-meson decay constant: f_D = 0.223 GeV.
- D-meson distribution amplitude parameter: C_D ≈ 1.5, with a maximum around z = 0.7.
- Cross sections are evaluated at s = 20 GeV^2, y = 0.7, Δ_T = 0 (or 0.5 GeV for azimuthal moments), and Q^2 values ranging from about 0.5 to 3 GeV^2.
- The transverse contribution to dσ/(dy dQ^2 dt) for ν n → l^- D^0 p is of the same order of magnitude as the longitudinal one and even dominates for y large enough (e.g., y > 0.7).
- The longitudinal cross section for ν p → l^- D^+ p and ν n → l^- D^+ n is dominated by gluon GPD contributions; the quark-only contribution is smaller than the total (quark+gluon) at all Q^2 shown.
- The ⟨cos φ⟩ and ⟨sin φ⟩ moments for D^+ production on a proton and neutron target at Δ_T = 0.5 GeV, y = 0.7, s = 20 GeV^2 range from about -0.1 to +0.1 depending on the transversity GPD model and Q^2.

## Context

This work extends the collinear QCD factorization approach for exclusive meson production, previously applied to electroproduction of light mesons (e.g., by Goloskokov and Kroll, 2011), to the case of neutrino-production of charmed mesons. It builds on the framework for weak GPDs (Pire and Szymanowski, 2005) and on the proof of factorization for heavy quark production (Collins, 1998). The results enable future extraction of transversity GPDs from neutrino experiments, complementing the information accessible through electroproduction and providing a new probe of the flavor and spin structure of the nucleon.
