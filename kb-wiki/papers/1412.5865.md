# Unified Description of Classical and Quantum Behaviours in a Variational Principle

> We give a pedagogical introduction of the stochastic variational method and show that this generalized variational principle describes classical and quantum mechanics in a unified way.

# Unified Description of Classical and Quantum Behaviours in a Variational Principle

## Key claim

The stochastic variational method (SVM) yields the Schroedinger equation from a generalized variational principle when the noise strength parameter nu is set to hbar/(2m), but the same action reduces to Newton's equation of motion in the limit nu -> 0, thereby unifying classical and quantum mechanics within a single variational framework only when the kinetic term is chosen with time-reversal symmetry (alpha_1 = 0, alpha_2 = 1/2).

## Method

The authors define forward and backward stochastic differential equations (SDEs) for a particle trajectory, with forward and backward mean derivatives (D and tilde D) introduced by Nelson. They construct a stochastic action using a quadratic kinetic term expressed in terms of these mean derivatives, then perform a variation of the action with respect to the trajectory. The resulting Euler-Lagrange equation for stochastic variables, together with the Fokker-Planck equation for the probability density, yields a coupled system that is transformed via a complex function into the Schroedinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The noise strength parameter nu is set to nu = hbar/(2m) to obtain the Schroedinger equation.
- The Wiener process correlation satisfies E[(dW^i_t)(dW^j_t)] = |dt| delta^{ij} for spatial indices i,j = x,y,z.
- The mean forward derivative D r(t) equals u(r(t),t) and the mean backward derivative tilde D r(t) equals tilde u(r(t),t) for the forward and backward SDEs respectively.
- The consistency condition relating forward and backward velocities is u(x,t) = tilde u(x,t) + 2 nu grad ln rho.

## Context

This work provides a pedagogical introduction to the stochastic variational method originally proposed by Yasue to reformulate Nelson's stochastic quantization (Nelson, 1966). It extends prior formulations by explicitly showing how the choice of parameters in the kinetic term leads to either classical Newtonian dynamics (nu -> 0) or quantum mechanics (nu = hbar/(2m)). The method enables alternative quantization schemes and has been applied to the Klein-Gordon field (Koide, 2013) and abelian gauge fields, and can also derive the Navier-Stokes-Fourier equation and Gross-Pitaevskii equation. The authors list open problems including quantization of fermions, extension to curved coordinates, and the criticism by Takabayashi and Wallstrom.
