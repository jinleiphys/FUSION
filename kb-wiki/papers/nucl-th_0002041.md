# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of six-quark states

> The variational Monte Carlo method is used to find the ground state of six quarks confined to a cavity of diameter R_c, interacting via an assumed non-relativistic constituent quark model (CQM) Hamiltonian. We use a flux-tube model augmented with one-gluon and one-pion exchange interactions, which has been successful in describing single hadron spectra. The variational wave function is written as a product of three-quark nucleon states with correlations between quarks in different nucleons. We study the role of quark exchange effects by allowing flux-tube configuration mixing. An accurate six-body variational wave function is obtained. It has only ~13% rms fluctuation in the total energy and yields a standard deviation of ~<.1%; small enough to be useful in discerning nuclear interaction effects from the large rest mass of the two nucleons. Results are presented for three values of the cavity diameter, R_c=2, 4, and 6 fm. They indicate that the flux-tube model Hamiltonian with gluon and pion exchange requires revisions in order to obtain agreement with the energies estimated from realistic two-nucleon interactions. We calculate the two-quark probability distribution functions and show how they may be used to study and adjust the model Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The variational Monte Carlo method can produce a six-quark wave function with only ~13% rms fluctuation in total energy and a standard deviation of less than 0.1%, but the flux-tube model Hamiltonian with gluon and pion exchange requires revisions to obtain agreement with energies estimated from realistic two-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors used the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) method to find the ground state of six quarks confined to a spherical cavity of diameter R_c, interacting via a non-relativistic constituent quark model Hamiltonian. The Hamiltonian includes a Y-junction flux-tube confinement potential, one-gluon exchange (OGE) interactions, and one-pion exchange (OPE) interactions between constituent quarks. The variational wave function was constructed as a product of three-quark nucleon states with correlation operators applied to quarks in different nucleons, and flux-tube configuration mixing was studied by allowing color-nondiagonal matrix elements with a Gaussian suppression factor.

## Key numbers

- String tension sigma = 0.88 GeV/fm, obtained from fitting the nucleon Regge trajectory
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.61, fixed to reproduce the N-Delta splitting
- Pion mass mu = 138 MeV, the average mass of the three charge states of the pion
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant f_piNN^2/4pi = 0.075, used to fix the quark-pion coupling as f_piQQ = (3/5) f_piNN
- Momentum space cutoff Lambda = 5 fm^-1 for both OGE and OPE form factors
- RMS quark radius for the nucleon = 0.44 fm
- Proton RMS charge radius = 0.67 fm (smaller than observed 0.79 fm)
- Neutron RMS charge radius = -0.015 fm (smaller magnitude than observed -0.34 fm)
- Results presented for three cavity diameters: R_c = 2, 4, and 6 fm
- The deuteron binding energy for R_c approaching infinity is -2.24 MeV from the Argonne v18 potential

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Isgur (1978), Oka and Yazaki (1984), and Robson (1985) by using quantum Monte Carlo methods to solve the six-body problem without the approximation schemes used in earlier studies. The authors aim to eventually calculate the two-nucleon interaction energy with computational errors below 1 MeV, which would enable refinement of the CQM Hamiltonian and study of the quark overlap region. The approach is a first step toward applying these methods to hyperon-nucleon interactions such as Lambda-N and Sigma-N, where scattering data is limited.
