# Suppression of Quarkonium Production in Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC and LHC

> A model for the production of quarkonium states in the midrapidity region at RHIC and LHC energy range is presented which explores well understood properties of QCD only. An increase of the quarkonium hadronisation time with the initial energy leads to a gradual change of the most important phenomena from fixed target- to collider-energies. We evaluate nuclear effects in the quarkonium production due to medium modification of the momentum distribution of the heavy quarks produced in the hard interactions, i.e. due to the broadening of the transverse momentum distribution. Other nuclear effects, i.e. nuclear shadowing and parton energy loss, are also evaluated.

# Suppression of Quarkonium Production in Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
At collider energies (RHIC and LHC), the hadronisation time of heavy quarkonium becomes much larger than the nuclear radius, so the production process must be described in partonic rather than hadronic degrees of freedom; the dominant nuclear effect is then the broadening of the transverse momentum distribution of the heavy quark pair, which modifies the overlap integral with the bound-state wave function, while nuclear shadowing and parton energy loss also contribute but are subleading in cold matter.

## Method
The authors compute the survival probability S of quarkonium states in nucleus-nucleus collisions by evaluating the overlap integral between the two-body wave function of the produced Q\bar{Q} pair (parametrized with Gaussian transverse and longitudinal momentum distributions) and the bound-state wave function of the J/ψ or Υ. The transverse momentum broadening Δp_t^2 of the Q\bar{Q} pair in the nuclear medium is related to the slope parameters B(AB) and B(pp) extracted from D-meson and J/ψ data at Fermilab. Nuclear effects on the gluon distribution functions are evaluated using two different parametrizations (EKS and FS), and parton energy loss in cold and hot QCD matter is estimated using the model of Baier et al.

## Key numbers
- Production time τ_p = 0.13 fm/c for c\bar{c} and 0.05 fm/c for b\bar{b} in the pair rest frame.
- Lorentz factor γ at midrapidity: ≈10 (SPS), 20 (Fermilab fixed target), 100 (RHIC), 3000 (LHC).
- Hadronisation coherence length l_c/γ = 0.44 fm for J/ψ and 0.34 fm for Υ.
- B(pAu) = 0.91 ± 0.12 GeV^{-2} from E789 for D-meson transverse momentum distribution in pAu collisions.
- n = 0.55 ± 0.01 GeV^{-2} from E789 for J/ψ transverse momentum distribution in pAu.
- B(pp) = 1.18 (1.5) GeV^{-2} for J/ψ; B(pp) = 0.77 GeV^{-2} for Υ (from <p_t^2> = 2.59 GeV^2).
- Δp_t^2 = 0.48 GeV^2 for J/ψ in pAu at Fermilab.
- Parton energy loss in cold QCD matter: ΔE = -2 GeV for L = 7 fm (gold nucleus); in hot QCD matter: ΔE = -30 GeV for L = 7 fm.
- Suppression factor from parton energy loss in hot matter for AB collisions: 0.76 for J/ψ, 0.88 for Υ.
- Gluon distribution ratio G_A/(A G_N) at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): 0.96–1.0 for J/ψ (EKS), 1.0 for J/ψ (FS); at LHC (√s = 6000 GeV): 0.84 (EKS) and 0.65 (FS) for J/ψ.

## Context
This work extends the treatment of quarkonium production from fixed-target energies (where hadronic descriptions apply, as in Farrar (1988) and Gerland (1998)) to collider energies where the coherence length exceeds the nuclear diameter, requiring a partonic description. The model predicts a gradual transition from hadronic to partonic phenomena as energy increases. The results enable predictions for J/ψ and Υ suppression at RHIC and LHC that can be tested against future data, and the authors emphasize that pA collisions are needed to distinguish between the effects of transverse momentum broadening, nuclear shadowing, and parton energy loss. The approach complements the parton energy loss model of Baier (1997) and the nuclear shadowing parametrizations of Eskola (1999) and Frankfurt-Strikman (1999).
