# Temperature Dependence of Heavy Quark Diffusion from (2+1)-flavor Lattice QCD

> We present a lattice determination of the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient in (2+1)-flavor QCD with almost physical quark masses. The momentum and spatial diffusion coefficients are extracted for a wide temperature range, from $T=163$ MeV to $10$ GeV. The results are in agreement with previous works from the HotQCD collaboration, and show fast thermalization of the heavy quark inside the QGP. Near the chiral crossover temperature $T_c\simeq150$ MeV, our results are close to the AdS/CFT estimation computed at strong coupling.

# Temperature Dependence of Heavy Quark Diffusion from (2+1)-flavor Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The heavy-quark diffusion coefficient is extracted from lattice QCD across a temperature range from 163 MeV to 10 GeV, with results near the chiral crossover temperature close to the AdS/CFT strong-coupling estimate, but the extraction relies on quasi-particle models for charm and bottom degrees of freedom to convert the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic diffusion coefficients into momentum and spatial diffusion coefficients.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations of chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic correlators using gradient flow, with continuum extrapolations performed at each temperature. The spectral functions were reconstructed using multiple Ansätze (max, smooth max, power law, sum) that combine an infrared transport peak with perturbative NLO or LO ultraviolet behavior, and the diffusion coefficients were extracted by fitting these spectral functions to the lattice correlator data.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: from T = 163 MeV to T = 10 GeV
- Chiral crossover temperature: T_c approximately 150 MeV
- At T = 163 MeV: kappa_E/T^3 = 14.3^{+3.1}_{-3.3}, kappa_B/T^3 = 8.0^{+4.4}_{-3.4}
- At T = 163 MeV: spatial diffusion coefficient for charm 2pi T D_s^c = 1.0^{+0.3}_{-0.2}
- At T = 163 MeV: spatial diffusion coefficient for bottom 2pi T D_s^b = 0.9^{+0.3}_{-0.2}
- At T = 1000 MeV: kappa_E/T^3 = 1.2^{+0.8}_{-0.8}, kappa_B/T^3 = 0.7^{+0.7}_{-0.5}
- At T = 10000 MeV: kappa_E/T^3 = 0.5^{+0.3}_{-0.5}, kappa_B/T^3 = 0.3^{+0.4}_{-0.2}
- Light quark masses used: m_l = m_s/20 (m_pi approximately 160 MeV) for low temperatures, m_l = m_s/5 (m_pi approximately 320 MeV) for high temperatures

## Context

This work extends previous HotQCD collaboration calculations (Altenkort 2023) to the physical point for low temperatures and expands the temperature range to 163 MeV through 10 GeV. The analysis uses the correct high-energy behavior of the chromo-electric spectral function from recent perturbative calculations (delaCruz 2024, Scheihing-Hitschfeld 2023), which resolves the large rescaling factor K_E puzzle seen in earlier quenched QCD studies (Brambilla 2020, Banerjee 2022). The results provide first-principles QCD benchmarks for heavy-flavor phenomenology in heavy-ion collisions, showing consistency with NLO perturbative QCD predictions (Caron-Huot 2007) at high temperatures and with strongly coupled AdS/CFT estimates (Casalderrey-Solana 2006, Herzog 2006) near T_c.
