# Heavy- and light-flavor symmetry partners of the $T_{cc}^+(3875)$, the $X(3872)$ and the $X(3960)$ from light-meson exchange saturation

> The spectrum of the charmed meson-(anti)meson system is a fundamental tool for disentangling the nature of a few exotic hadrons, including the recently discovered $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ tetraquark, the $X(3960)$, or the $X(3872)$, the nature of which is still not clear after almost two decades of its discovery. Here we consider that the charmed meson-(anti)meson short-range interaction is described by the exchange of light-mesons ($σ$, $ρ$, $ω$). The effects of light-meson exchanges are recast into a simple contact-range theory by means of a saturation procedure, resulting in a compact description of the two-hadron interaction. From this, if the $T_{cc}^+$ were to be an isoscalar $D^* D$ molecule, then there should exist an isoscalar $J=1$ $D^* D^*$ partner, as constrained by heavy-quark spin symmetry. Yet, within our model, the most attractive two charmed meson configurations are the isovector $J=0$ $D^* D^*$ molecule and its sextet $D_s^* D^*$ and $D_s^* D_s^*$ flavor partners. Finally, we find a tension between the molecular descriptions of the $T_{cc}^+$ and that of the $X(3872)$ and $X(3960)$, where most parameter choices suggest that if the $T_{cc}^+$ is purely molecular then the $X(3872)$ overbinds (or conversely, if the $X(3872)$ is a molecule the $T_{cc}^+$ does not bind). This might be consequential for determining the nature of these states.

## Key claim

If the $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ is a purely molecular $D^* D$ bound state, then within the authors' light-meson exchange saturation model the $X(3872)$ becomes overbound (or conversely, if the $X(3872)$ is a molecule the $T_{cc}^+$ does not bind), suggesting that at least one of these states requires non-molecular components to explain its mass.

## Method

The authors model the short-range interaction of charmed meson-(anti)meson systems by the exchange of light mesons ($\sigma$, $\rho$, $\omega$), recasting these exchanges into a contact-range theory via a saturation procedure. They determine the unknown proportionality constant of the saturated couplings by choosing a reference state (e.g., the $X(3872)$ as a $1^{++}$ $D^*\bar{D}$ bound state) and then predict binding energies for other molecular configurations by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Gaussian regulator at cutoff $\Lambda = 1$ GeV. Uncertainties are estimated from the scalar meson mass variation ($m_S = 475 \pm 75$ MeV) and the contact-range approximation error $\gamma_2 / m_V$.

## Key numbers

- $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ binding energy from unitarized Breit-Wigner fit: $\delta m_{\rm U} = -360 \pm 40 {}^{+4}_{-0}$ keV below the $D^{0*} D^{+}$ threshold (LHCb 2021)
- $X(3872)$ mass: $3871.65 \pm 0.06$ MeV (Review of Particle Physics)
- $X(3960)$ mass from Breit-Wigner fit: $3956 \pm 5 \pm 10$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 43 \pm 13 \pm 8$ MeV (LHCb 2022)
- Using the $X(3872)$ as reference, the $D^* D$ system (candidate for $T_{cc}^+$) yields a virtual state at $3852^{+17}_{-24}$ MeV, i.e., no binding
- Using the $X(3872)$ as reference, the $2^{++}$ $D^* \bar{D}^*$ partner is predicted at $4011.6 \pm 0.7$ MeV, compatible with Belle's $4014.3 \pm 4.0 \pm 1.5$ MeV
- Using the $T_{cc}^+$ as reference, the $X(3872)$ is predicted a few dozen MeV below threshold (overbinding)
- Using the $T_{cc}^+$ as reference, the $X(3960)$ is predicted at $3931.6^{+1.8}_{-2.7}$ MeV, compatible with the virtual/bound pole at $3928 \pm 3$ MeV from Ji (2022)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous saturation model (Peng 2021) to simultaneously address the $T_{cc}^+(3875)$, $X(3872)$, and $X(3960)$ as molecular candidates. The tension found between the molecular descriptions of the $T_{cc}^+$ and the $X(3872)$ echoes the earlier calculation by Janc and Rosina (2004), who argued that binding the $T_{cc}^+$ below the $D^* D$ threshold requires both molecular and quark components. The predicted $2^{++}$ $D^* \bar{D}^*$ partner of the $X(3872)$ at about 4012 MeV was conjectured a decade ago by Valderrama (2012) and Nieves (2012) as a consequence of heavy-quark spin symmetry. The results enable future studies of mixing between molecular and compact tetraquark or charmonium components to resolve the binding discrepancies.
