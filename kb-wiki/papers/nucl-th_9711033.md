# Screening effects on the elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section in relativistic nuclear matter

> We investigate the screening effects on the nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section inside nuclear matter at zero and finite temperature. The N-N interaction is described phenomenologically via meson exchanges ($σ$, $π$ and $ω$) in the framework of a relativistic lagrangian model. The expressions for the in-medium meson propagators, which take into account the effects of matter polarization and renormalized vacuum polarization, are used in the calculation of the various meson contributions to the total cross section in the one-boson exchange approximation. The final expressions allow a satisfactory fit of the Coulomb substracted proton-proton cross section data in vacuum. At finite density or temperature the cross section is reduced as compared to the vacuum values and compatible with the existing experimental constraints. At high density the $p$-$p$ differential cross section gets increasingly forward and backward peaked. The $σ$-$ω$ mixing plays a key role in the understanding of the results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that screening effects in nuclear matter reduce the elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section compared to vacuum values, with the reduction being compatible with existing experimental constraints, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of meson exchanges (σ, π, ω) and the omission of ρ-meson contributions, which would significantly affect the proton-neutron cross section.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section using a relativistic Lagrangian model with σ, π, and ω meson exchanges in the one-boson exchange (OBE) approximation. They incorporate dressed meson propagators that include both matter polarization and renormalized vacuum polarization effects at finite density and temperature, with phenomenological monopolar form factors to remove unphysical tachyonic poles. The model parameters are fitted to Coulomb-subtracted proton-proton scattering data in vacuum over the energy range T_lab = 40-1000 MeV using a weighted least-squares method.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density is attained at P_f0 = 1.40 fm^{-1} with binding energy E_b = -15.85 MeV
- Meson masses fixed at μ_σ = 550 MeV, μ_ω = 783 MeV, μ_π = 138 MeV, nucleon mass m = 939 MeV
- Best-fit coupling constants (set A): g_σ = 3.80, g_ω = 9.31, with cutoffs Λ_σ = 1298.8 MeV, Λ_ω = 1240.5 MeV, Λ_π = 362.1 MeV
- For densities n ≥ 2 n_sat, the total p-p cross section settles around approximately 17 mb for parameter sets A, B, C across all T_lab
- For parameter sets D and F at n ≥ 2 n_sat, the cross section settles to approximately 15.5 mb
- The fit used 12 data points for total cross section over T_lab ∈ [40-1000] MeV and 8 differential cross section points per energy at T_lab = 60, 100, 250, 350, 460, 560, 660, 800, 1000 MeV
- χ² values obtained in the range 10-12 for the fitting procedure

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic Brueckner formalism calculations of in-medium N-N cross sections (F89, GZ93) to a fully relativistic treatment including finite temperature effects, which had only been considered previously by AR94. The results are intended for use in transport models of heavy ion collisions such as BUU (KW93, W93, MSFRS92, L93, AR95), QMD (KO92), and AMD (TOHME95), where a reduced in-medium cross section is favored by analyses of transverse flow. The paper notes that the ρ-meson exchange, necessary for proton-neutron cross sections, is not included and will be treated in future work, and that Δ(1232) resonance contributions to inelastic channels become important above T_lab ≥ 350 MeV.
