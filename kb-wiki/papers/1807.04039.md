# Beyond--mean--field effective masses in the nuclear Fermi liquid from axial breathing modes

> Axial breathing modes are studied within the nuclear energy--density--functional theory to discuss the modification of the nucleon effective mass produced beyond the mean--field approximation. This analysis is peformed with the subtracted second random--phase--approximation (SSRPA) model applied to two nuclei, $^{48}$Ca and $^{90}$Zr. Analyzing the centroid energies of axial breathing modes obtained with the mean--field--based random--phase approximation and with the beyond--mean--field SSRPA model, we estimate the modification (enhancement) of the effective mass which is induced beyond the mean field. This is done by employing a relation, obtained with the Landau's Fermi liquid theory, between the excitation frequency of axial modes to $\sqrt{m/m^*}$, where $m$ ($m^*$) is the bare (effective) mass. Such an enhancement of the effective mass is discussed in connection with the renormalization of single--particle excitation energies generated by the energy--dependent SSRPA self-energy correction. We find that the effective beyond--mean--field compression of the single--particle spectrum produced by the self--energy correction is coherent with the increase of the effective mass estimated from the analysis of axial breathing modes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that beyond-mean-field (BMF) effects enhance the nucleon effective mass in nuclear matter, as estimated from the centroid energies of isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances (axial breathing modes) in two nuclei, with the enhancement depending on the nucleus and the Skyrme parametrization used.

## Method

The authors performed random-phase-approximation (RPA) and subtracted second random-phase-approximation (SSRPA) calculations for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances in ⁴⁸Ca and ⁹⁰Zr using four Skyrme parametrizations (SkP, SGII, SLy4, Ska) with different mean-field effective masses. They plotted the RPA centroid energies against √(m/m*) for each nucleus, performed a linear fit, and then placed the SSRPA centroid energies on the same fit line to extract the BMF effective mass. The relation between excitation frequency and effective mass comes from Landau's Fermi liquid theory.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca with SLy4, the effective mass increases from 0.7 (mean-field) to 0.834 (beyond-mean-field).
- For ⁹⁰Zr with SLy4, the effective mass increases from 0.7 (mean-field) to 0.769 (beyond-mean-field).
- For ⁴⁸Ca with SGII, the effective mass increases from 0.79 (mean-field) to 0.837 (beyond-mean-field).
- For ⁹⁰Zr with SGII, the effective mass increases from 0.79 (mean-field) to 0.842 (beyond-mean-field).
- The average E-mass (the energy-dependent part of the effective mass) increases from 1 (mean-field) to values ranging from 1.06 to 1.19, corresponding to a 6 to 16 percent increase depending on nucleus and parametrization.
- For ⁴⁸Ca with SLy4, the SSRPA model induces an 11.6 percent reduction of the diagonal matrix element A₁₁ for the third 1p1h configuration relative to the RPA result.
- For the third 1p1h configuration in ⁴⁸Ca with SLy4, the estimated E-mass is 1.16.

## Context

This work extends the relation between axial breathing mode frequencies and effective mass, previously used for atomic gases (Nascimbene 2009) and for nuclear giant quadrupole resonances (Blaizot 1980, Bohigas 1979), to the beyond-mean-field level using the SSRPA model. The SSRPA model (Gambacurta 2015) avoids instabilities and double counting of correlations in the energy-density-functional framework. The results enable future extraction of BMF effective masses from experimental data on isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances and connect the effective mass enhancement to compression of the single-particle spectrum.
