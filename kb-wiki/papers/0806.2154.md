# The Momentum Kick Model Description of the Near-Side Ridge and Jet Quenching

> In the momentum kick model, a near-side jet emerges near the surface, kicks medium partons, loses energy, and fragments into the trigger particle and fragmentation products. The kicked medium partons subsequently materialize as the observed ridge particles, which carry direct information on the magnitude of the momentum kick and the initial parton momentum distribution at the moment of jet-(medium parton) collisions. The initial parton momentum distribution extracted from the STAR ridge data for central AuAu collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV has a thermal-like transverse momentum distribution and a rapidity plateau structure with a relatively flat distribution at mid-rapidity and sharp kinematic boundaries at large rapidities. Such a rapidity plateau structure may arise from particle production in flux tubes, as color charges and anti-color charges separate at high energies. The centrality dependence of the ridge yield and the degree of jet quenching can be consistently described by the momentum kick model.

## Key claim

The momentum kick model can consistently describe both the near-side ridge phenomenon and jet quenching in central AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, but only after the initial parton momentum distribution is parametrized with a thermal-like transverse distribution and a rapidity plateau structure that respects kinematic boundaries, rather than a simple Gaussian rapidity distribution.

## Method

The model assumes a near-side jet emerges near the medium surface, collides with medium partons, imparts a momentum kick \(q\) to each, loses energy, and fragments into the trigger particle and jet component. The kicked medium partons subsequently materialize as ridge particles via parton-hadron duality. The initial parton momentum distribution was extracted by fitting the model to STAR ridge data for central AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, using a parametrization with a thermal transverse factor \(e^{-\sqrt{m^2+p_{ti}^2}/T}\), a modified denominator \(\sqrt{m_d^2+p_{ti}^2}\), and a rapidity plateau factor \((1-x)^a\) with kinematic boundaries.

## Key numbers

- The transverse slope parameter \(T\) for the initial parton distribution extracted from STAR ridge data is slightly higher than the corresponding quantity of evolution products at the end-point of the nucleus-nucleus collision (no explicit numerical value given for \(T\) in the truncated text).
- The jet component in \(pp\) collisions is parametrized with \(T_{\rm jet}=0.55\) GeV, \(\sigma_{\phi 0}=0.50\), \(m_a=1.1\) GeV, and \(N_{\rm jet}=0.75\).
- The average jet fragment attenuation factor is taken as \(f_J = 0.632\), derived from an absorption length model.
- The ridge particle distribution per trigger is given by \(\frac{1}{N_{\rm trig}} \frac{dN_{\rm ridge}^{AA}}{d{\bf p}} = \langle f_R \rangle \frac{2}{3} \langle N_{\rm k} \rangle \langle \frac{dF}{d{\bf p}} \rangle\), where \(\langle N_{\rm k} \rangle\) is the average number of kicked medium partons per trigger.

## Context

This work extends the momentum kick model previously proposed by Wong (2007) to provide a quantitative comparison with STAR experimental data on the near-side ridge in central AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. The extracted initial parton momentum distribution exhibits a rapidity plateau structure, which the author connects to theoretical work on particle production in flux tubes by Casher, Bjorken, and others (1974, 1983) and to QED2 field theory connections. The model enables a consistent description of both the ridge yield and jet quenching as a function of centrality, and paves the way for a future Monte Carlo implementation of the momentum kick model.
