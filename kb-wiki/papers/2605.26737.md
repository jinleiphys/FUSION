# Cumulants of mean transverse momentum and elliptic flow in the hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions

> Higher order cumulants between the mean transverse momentum and elliptic flow are calculated in a relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results of the hydrodynamic simulations are compared with calculations using event-by-event predictors of the final collective observables constructed from the initial state entropy distribution. The predictors describes quantitatively centrality dependence of the higher cumulants considered in the paper. We derive a quantitative relations between the cumulants of the mean transverse momentum and different moments of the harmonic flow. The hydrodynamic simulations satisfy those relation very well. Those relations could be used to test experimentally the collective origin of the observed correlations between the mean transverse momentum and harmonic flow.

# Cumulants of mean transverse momentum and elliptic flow in the hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Higher-order cumulants between the mean transverse momentum and elliptic flow, calculated in a relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model, can be approximated by predictors based on initial-state entropy distributions, and a simple Bessel-Gaussian ansatz yields quantitative relations between cumulants of different order in harmonic flow that the hydrodynamic simulations satisfy very well; these relations could be used to test experimentally the collective origin of the observed correlations, but the simple ansatz fails to describe cumulants of higher order in the mean transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution of Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV using the MUSIC framework, with initial conditions from the TRENTo model parameterizing the initial entropy density profile. The hydrodynamic evolution started at proper time τ0 = 0.4 fm/c with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.08 and a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity profile, using a lattice QCD-based equation of state; freeze-out was performed at Tfr = 145 MeV using the Cooper-Frye prescription with resonance decays. Scaled cumulants ρk,1([pT],v2²) and ρ1,k([pT],v2²) were calculated from the final-state hadrons, with corrections applied for multiplicity fluctuations within each centrality bin.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamic evolution start time: τ0 = 0.4 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.08
- Freeze-out temperature: Tfr = 145 MeV
- Collision system: Pb+Pb at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- The scaled cumulant ρ1,2([pT],v2²) is approximately 8κ to leading order in the correlation parameter κ, while ρ1,3([pT],v2²) is approximately 12κ
- The relation ρ1,3([pT],v2²) ≃ (3/2) ρ1,2([pT],v2²) holds to leading order in κ
- The cumulant c{4} = ⟨v⁴⟩c = -v0⁴ and c{6} = ⟨v⁶⟩c = 4v0⁶ under the Bessel-Gaussian distribution

## Context

This work extends previous studies of correlations between mean transverse momentum and harmonic flow coefficients, including calculations by Bozek (2016), Schenke (2020), and the ATLAS Collaboration (2019), as well as higher-order cumulant studies by the CMS Collaboration (2024) and Nielsen (2025). The paper derives quantitative relations between cumulants of different order in harmonic flow that explain previous numerical results and could be tested experimentally to confirm the collective-flow origin of observed correlations. The findings enable experimental tests of whether higher-order cumulants arise from collective expansion rather than nonflow effects, and suggest that measurements of five-particle cumulants ⟨[pT] V2² V2*²⟩c and ⟨[pT] V2³ V2*³⟩c could help estimate nonflow contributions in the lower-order cumulant ⟨[pT] v2²⟩c.
