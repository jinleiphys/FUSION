# Two-flavored heavy mesons' nuclear bound states

> We calculate the $B_c$- and $B_s$-nucleus bound state energies and coordinate space radial wave functions by solving the Klein-Gordon equation in momentum space. The attractive strong potentials for the $B_c$ and $B_s$ mesons in nuclei are calculated from the respective mass shifts of these mesons in nuclear matter using a local density approximation. This negative mass shift may be regarded as a signature of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in medium in an empirical sense, because the origin of the negative mass shift in the present study is not directly related to the chiral symmetry mechanism.

## Key claim

The attractive strong potentials derived from the in-medium mass shifts of $B_c$ and $B_s$ mesons in symmetric nuclear matter are sufficient to form bound states with atomic nuclei, although the authors note that the widths and momenta of the mesons have been neglected and the negative mass shift is not directly related to chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium mass shifts of $B_c$ and $B_s$ mesons from the enhancement of meson loop contributions to their self-energies, using an SU(5) effective Lagrangian density with a universal coupling constant determined by vector meson dominance. The intermediate-state meson masses ($B$, $B^*$, $D$, $D^*$, $K$, $K^*$) in nuclear matter were computed using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The nuclear potentials for meson-nucleus systems were obtained via a local density approximation mapping the density-dependent mass shifts to nuclear density distributions, and the bound-state energies and coordinate-space radial wave functions were found by solving the Klein-Gordon equation in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- $B_c$ mass shift at saturation density ($\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$) ranges from -90.4 to -101.1 MeV depending on the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$
- $B_s$ mass shift at saturation density ranges from -133.0 to -178.8 MeV depending on $\Lambda$
- $B^-_c$-$^{12}$C 1s bound state energy: -79.12 MeV ($\Lambda = 2000$ MeV), -80.63 MeV ($\Lambda = 4000$ MeV), -87.03 MeV ($\Lambda = 6000$ MeV)
- $B^0_s$-$^{4}$He 1s bound state energy: -139.31 MeV ($\Lambda = 2000$ MeV), -153.09 MeV ($\Lambda = 4000$ MeV), -192.81 MeV ($\Lambda = 6000$ MeV)
- $B^0_s$-$^{208}$Pb 1s bound state energy: -122.08 MeV ($\Lambda = 2000$ MeV), -133.47 MeV ($\Lambda = 4000$ MeV), -166.62 MeV ($\Lambda = 6000$ MeV)
- $B^-_c$-$^{208}$Pb 1s bound state energy: -95.88 MeV ($\Lambda = 2000$ MeV), -97.39 MeV ($\Lambda = 4000$ MeV), -103.79 MeV ($\Lambda = 6000$ MeV)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zeminiani (2023) and Zeminiani (2024) for heavy meson-nucleus bound states to the two-flavored heavy mesons $B_c$ and $B_s$, using the QMC model developed by Guichon (1987) and Saito (2005) for in-medium meson masses. The results enable future studies that include the effects of meson widths and finite meson momenta, which the authors state they plan to incorporate.
