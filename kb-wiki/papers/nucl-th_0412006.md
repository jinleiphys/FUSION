# Combined nonrelativistic constituent quark model and heavy quark effective theory study of semileptonic decays of Λ_b and Ξ_b baryons

> We present the results of a nonrelativistic constituent quark model study of the semileptonic decays $Λ_b^0 \to Λ_c^+ l^- {\bar ν}_l$ and $Ξ_b^0 \to Ξ_c^+ l^- {\bar ν}_l$. We work on coordinate space, with baryon wave functions recently obtained from a variational approach based on heavy quark symmetry . We develop a novel expansion of the electroweak current operator, which supplemented with heavy quark effective theory constraints, allows us to predict the baryon form factors and the decay distributions for all $q^2$ (or equivalently $w$) values accessible in the physical decays. Our results for the partially integrated longitudinal and transverse decay widths, in the vicinity of the $w=1$ point, are in excellent agreement with lattice calculations. Comparison of our integrated $Λ_b-$decay width to experiment allows us to extract the $V_{cb}$ Cabbibo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element for which we obtain a value of $|V_{cb}| = 0.040\pm 0.005 ({\rm stat}) ^{+0.001}_{-0.002} ({\rm theory})$ also in excellent agreement with a recent determination by the DELPHI Collaboration from the exclusive ${\bar {\rm B}^0_{\rm d}} \to {\rm D}^{*+}l^-{\bar ν}_l$ decay. Besides for the $Λ_b (Ξ_b)-$decay, the longitudinal and transverse asymmetries, and the longitudinal to transverse decay ratio are $<a_L >=-0.954\pm 0.001 (-0.945\pm 0.002)$, $<a_T >=-0.665\pm 0.002 (-0.628\pm 0.004)$ and $R_{L/T}=1.63\pm 0.02 (1.53\pm 0.04)$, respectively.

## Key claim
The authors combine a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) with heavy quark effective theory (HQET) constraints to predict form factors and decay distributions for the semileptonic decays of Λ_b and Ξ_b baryons over the full kinematic range of q^2, not just near the zero-recoil point (w=1) where previous NRCQM calculations were limited.

## Method
The authors use baryon wave functions obtained from a variational approach based on heavy quark symmetry, solving the nonrelativistic three-body problem in coordinate space. They develop a novel expansion of the electroweak current operator that keeps terms up to first order in the internal heavy quark momentum but retains all orders in the transferred momentum q, allowing predictions for all accessible q^2 values. The spectator model results are then improved by imposing O(1/m_Q) accuracy HQET constraints among the form factors, using correction factors from the literature.

## Key numbers
- Extracted CKM matrix element: |V_cb| = 0.040 ± 0.005 (stat) +0.001/-0.002 (theory) from comparison of integrated Λ_b decay width to experiment
- Longitudinal asymmetry for Λ_b (Ξ_b) decay: ⟨a_L⟩ = -0.954 ± 0.001 (-0.945 ± 0.002)
- Transverse asymmetry for Λ_b (Ξ_b) decay: ⟨a_T⟩ = -0.665 ± 0.002 (-0.628 ± 0.004)
- Longitudinal to transverse decay ratio for Λ_b (Ξ_b) decay: R_L/T = 1.63 ± 0.02 (1.53 ± 0.04)
- Baryon masses used: m_Λ_b = 5624 MeV, m_Ξ_b = 5800 MeV, m_Λ_c = 2285 MeV, m_Ξ_c = 2469 MeV
- Fermi decay constant: G = 1.1664 × 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}

## Context
This work extends previous NRCQM studies of heavy baryon semileptonic decays by enabling predictions across the full q^2 range, whereas earlier approaches (lattice calculations, HQET-based methods) were reliable only near q^2_max and conventional sum rules only near q^2=0. The results agree with lattice calculations for partially integrated longitudinal and transverse decay widths near w=1, and the extracted |V_cb| agrees with the DELPHI Collaboration determination from exclusive B_d^0 → D*+ l ν_l decay. The approach enables independent determination of V_cb from baryon decays and provides predictions for asymmetry parameters that can be tested by future experiments.
