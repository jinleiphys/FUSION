# Long-time tails in the SYK chain from the effective field theory with a large number of derivatives

> We study the nonlinear energy diffusion through the SYK chain in the framework of Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory. We analytically construct the interacting effective Lagrangian up to $40^{th}$ order in the derivative expansion. According to this effective Lagrangian, we calculate the first order loop correction of the energy density response function, the pole of which is the dispersion relation of energy diffusion. As expected, we see that the standard derivative expansion of that dispersion relation, $ω=-i D_{(1)} k^2- i D_{(2)} k^4+\mathcal{O}(k^6)$, breaks down due to the long-time tails. However, we find that the nonlinear contribution of order $n$ to the self-energy is proportional to $\left(k^{2}\right)^{n+1/2}$. This suggests to modify the dispersion relation by splitting it into two dispersion relations and double the number of transport coefficients at any order as $ω=-i k^2\big( D_{(1,1)}\pm i D_{(1,2)} \left(k^2\right)^{1/2}\big)-i k^4\big( D_{(2,1)}\pm i D_{(2,2)} \left(k^2\right)^{1/2}\big)+\mathcal{O}(k^6)$. We find that the modified series, which include the effect of long-time tails, are convergent. The radius of convergence is proportional to the ratio of thermal conductivity to diffusion constant.

## Key claim

The standard derivative expansion of the diffusion dispersion relation, ω = -i D_(1) k^2 - i D_(2) k^4 + O(k^6), breaks down due to long-time tails; however, a modified series that splits the dispersion relation into two branches and doubles the number of transport coefficients at each order (e.g., ω = -i k^2 ( D_(1,1) ± i D_(1,2) (k^2)^(1/2) ) + ...) is convergent, with a radius of convergence proportional to the ratio of thermal conductivity to diffusion constant.

## Method

The author constructs the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory (EFT) for nonlinear energy diffusion in the SYK chain, analytically building the interacting effective Lagrangian up to 40th order in the derivative expansion. Using this Lagrangian, the first-order loop correction to the energy density response function is computed, and the pole of this function yields the dispersion relation for energy diffusion. The analysis focuses on the large N limit (N >> 1) to suppress higher-loop contributions, allowing a systematic study of one-loop effects on the classical Green's functions at high derivative order.

## Key numbers

- The interacting effective Lagrangian was constructed analytically up to 40th order in the derivative expansion.
- The nonlinear contribution of order n to the self-energy is proportional to (k^2)^(n + 1/2).
- The modified dispersion relation introduces 2n transport coefficients at order n in derivatives (e.g., D_(1,1) and D_(1,2) at leading order).
- The radius of convergence of the modified series is proportional to the ratio of thermal conductivity to diffusion constant.

## Context

This work extends the study of long-time tails in hydrodynamics, previously examined at first order in derivatives by Chen-Lin (2018) and others, to high orders in the derivative expansion within the SYK chain. It builds on the Schwinger-Keldysh EFT framework developed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015) and others, and on the classical hydrodynamic description of the SYK chain by Gu, Kitaev, and Maldacena (2016). The results enable a systematic understanding of how hydrodynamic fluctuations modify the derivative expansion at large order, and suggest that the modified dispersion relation series converges, providing a controlled resummation of long-time tail effects.
