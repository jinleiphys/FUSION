# Building a testable shear viscosity across the QCD phase diagram

> Current experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) are probing finite baryon densities where the shear viscosity to enthalpy ratio $ηT/w$ of the Quark Gluon Plasma remains unknown. We use the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model with the most up-to-date hadron list to calculate $ηT/w$ at low temperatures and at finite baryon densities $ρ_B$. We then match $ηT/w$ to a QCD-based shear viscosity calculation within the deconfined phase to create a table across $\left\{T,μ_B\right\}$ for different cross-over and critical point scenarios at a specified location. We find that these new $ηT/w(T,μ_B)$ values would require initial conditions at significantly larger $ρ_B$, compared to ideal hydrodynamic trajectories, in order to reach the same freeze-out point.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a table of the shear viscosity to enthalpy ratio \(\eta T/w\) across the QCD phase diagram \(\{T,\mu_B\}\) by matching a hadron resonance gas (HRG) calculation at low temperatures to a QCD-based parametrization in the deconfined phase, but the authors explicitly state that they do **not** incorporate any critical scaling of the shear viscosity itself, arguing that criticality would not considerably affect it because the shear viscosity scales as \(\xi^{(4-d)/19}\) (where \(d\) is the number of spatial dimensions) compared to the bulk viscosity which scales as \(\xi^3\).

## Method

The authors used the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model with the most up-to-date Particle Data Group hadron list (PDG16+) and an excluded volume correction (single hard-core radius \(r\) constrained by lattice QCD entropy data up to \(T\sim 150\) MeV) to calculate \(\eta T/w\) at low temperatures and finite baryon densities \(\rho_B\). They then matched this to a parametrized form of \(\eta/s(T,\mu_B=0)\) from Christiansen et al. (2014) and Dubla et al. (2018) in the deconfined phase, using a switching temperature \(T_{sw}\) that follows a Taylor expansion in \(\mu_B\) with coefficients \(\kappa_2\) and \(\kappa_4\). Four scenarios were constructed: a smooth crossover, a sharp crossover, a critical point at \(\{T,\mu_B\} = \{143, 350\}\) MeV (matching the BEST collaboration equation of state), and a critical point at \(\{T,\mu_B\} = \{89, 724\}\) MeV (matching a holography prediction from Critelli et al. (2017)).

## Key numbers

- For the PDG16+ hadron list with excluded volume radius \(r=0.25\) fm, \(\eta/s(T) \sim 0.8\) at \(T=165\) MeV and \(\eta/s(T) \sim 0.34\) at \(T=199\) MeV (at \(\mu_B=0\)).
- The pseudo-critical temperature at \(\mu_B=0\) for the chiral transition is taken as \(T_{ch,0} = 158\) MeV, with curvature coefficients \(\kappa_2 = 0.0149\) and \(\kappa_4 = 0.00032\).
- For the sharp crossover scenario, the switching temperature at \(\mu_B=0\) is set to \(T_{sw,0} = 199\) MeV.
- For the critical point at \(\{T,\mu_B\} = \{143, 350\}\) MeV, the crossover-side parameters are \(T_{sw,0}=199\) MeV, \(\kappa_2=0.08\), \(\kappa_4=0.005\); the first-order side uses \(T_{sw,0}=158\) MeV, \(\kappa_2=0.0149\), \(\kappa_4=0.001\).
- For the critical point at \(\{T,\mu_B\} = \{89, 724\}\) MeV, the crossover-side parameters are \(T_{sw,0}=199\) MeV, \(\kappa_2=0.038\), \(\kappa_4=0.0005\); the first-order side uses \(T_{sw,0}=158\) MeV, \(\kappa_2=0.0149\), \(\kappa_4=0.00032\).
- The minimum value of \(\eta T/w\) at \(\mu_B\sim 600\) MeV is approximately twice as large for the smooth crossover scenario compared to the sharp crossover scenario.

## Context

This work extends previous HRG-based shear viscosity calculations at finite baryon density (Denicol et al. 2013, Kadam 2014) by using the most up-to-date hadron list (PDG16+) and by matching to a QCD-based parametrization in the deconfined phase, enabling the construction of a \(\eta T/w(T,\mu_B)\) table that can be used in relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations at finite baryon densities. The framework is designed to be easily updated if more realistic QCD-based calculations become available at finite \(\mu_B\). The authors note that the new \(\eta T/w(T,\mu_B)\) values would require initial conditions at significantly larger \(\rho_B\) compared to ideal hydrodynamic trajectories to reach the same freeze-out point, and they demonstrate that lattice QCD isentropes provide reasonable estimates of the hydrodynamic path at the phase transition but not at higher temperatures, using a single hydrodynamic event at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6\) GeV from Shen et al. (2018).
