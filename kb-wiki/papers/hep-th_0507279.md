# Spinning Skyrmions and the Skyrme Parameters

> The traditional approach to fixing the parameters of the Skyrme model requires the energy of a spinning Skyrmion to reproduce the nucleon and delta masses. The standard Skyrme parameters, which are used almost exclusively, fix the pion mass to its experimental value and fit the two remaining Skyrme parameters by approximating the spinning Skyrmion as a rigid body. In this paper we remove the rigid body approximation and perform numerical calculations which allow the spinning Skyrmion to deform and break spherical symmetry. The results show that if the pion mass is set to its experimental value then the nucleon and delta masses can not be reproduced for any values of the Skyrme parameters; the commonly used Skyrme parameters are simply an artifact of the rigid body approximation. However, if the pion mass is taken to be substantially larger than its experimental value then the nucleon and delta masses can be reproduced. This result has a significant effect on the structure of multi-Skyrmions.

## Key claim

If the pion mass is set to its experimental value (138 MeV), the nucleon and delta masses cannot be reproduced for any values of the Skyrme parameters once the rigid body approximation is removed; the commonly used Skyrme parameters are an artifact of that approximation. However, if the pion mass is taken to be substantially larger than its experimental value (at least more than twice the experimental value), then the nucleon and delta masses can be reproduced.

## Method

The authors performed numerical computations of spinning Skyrmions in the full field theory using an axially symmetric ansatz, removing the rigid body approximation and allowing the Skyrmion to deform and break spherical symmetry. They used a simulated annealing algorithm on a grid of 250 × 500 points with lattice spacing 0.06 in cylindrical polar coordinates to minimize the total energy E = E₀ + J²/(2Λ) for given spin values corresponding to the nucleon (j = 1/2) and delta (j = 3/2). The calculations were repeated for a range of the Skyrme parameter e and for different values of the pion mass mπ.

## Key numbers

- Experimental values: Fπ = 186 MeV, mπ = 138 MeV
- Standard Skyrme parameters (rigid body approximation): mπ = 138 MeV, Fπ = 108 MeV, e = 4.84, rescaled pion mass m = 0.526
- With mπ = 138 MeV, the axial deformation data for nucleon and delta do not cross, so no values of e and Fπ fit both masses
- With mπ = 276 MeV (twice experimental), the nucleon and delta data still do not cross
- With mπ = 345 MeV (two and a half times experimental), the nucleon and delta curves intersect, yielding parameters e = 4.90 and Fπ = 90.5 MeV, with rescaled pion mass m = 1.56
- The critical pion mass for a fit to be possible lies in the range 276 MeV ≤ mπ ≤ 345 MeV
- The maximal rotation frequency constraint is Ω² ≤ mπ²

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and Adkins and Nappi (1984) that used the rigid body approximation to fix the standard Skyrme parameters, and improves upon early papers by Biedenharn and Rothe (1984) and others that allowed deformation within a spherically symmetric hedgehog ansatz. The results support recent work by Battye and Sutcliffe (2005) on multi-Skyrmions, which found that a larger pion mass yields multi-Skyrmions whose qualitative features (such as A^(1/3) scaling of nuclear size) are closer to real nuclei. The findings also align with studies of the Roper resonance and comparisons between vibrational frequencies of multi-Skyrmions and nuclear gamma ray spectra, which produce improved results with a larger pion mass.
