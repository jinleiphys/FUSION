# Redundant Components in the 3 alpha Faddeev Equation Using 2 alpha RGM Kernel

> The 3 alpha Faddeev equation using 2 alpha RGM kernel involves redundant components whose contribution to the total wave function completely cancels out. We propose a practical method to solve this Faddeev equation, by eliminating the admixture of such redundant components. A complete equivalence between the present Faddeev approach and a variational approach using the translationally invariant harmonic-oscillator basis is numerically shown with respect to the 3 alpha bound state corresponding to the ground state of 12C.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the 3-alpha Faddeev equation using the 2-alpha resonating-group method (RGM) kernel contains redundant components of [21] permutation symmetry that cancel out of the total wave function, and proposes a practical elimination method that yields numerical equivalence to a variational approach for the ground state of 12C.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equation for three identical bosons using a two-cluster T-matrix derived from the RGM kernel, identifying redundant components as eigenstates of the rearrangement matrix S with eigenvalue tau = -1. They eliminate these components by modifying the Faddeev equation with a projection term that pushes the trivial solutions to zero eigenvalue, then solve the resulting equation using discretization of momentum variables with Gauss-Legendre quadrature and the Arnoldi-Lanczos algorithm for diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 3-alpha system without Coulomb force: -11.4187 MeV (with partial waves up to l_max = 8 and 20-10-10 discretization points)
- Ground-state energy of 3-alpha system with shielded Coulomb force (R_C = 6 fm): -6.1217 MeV (with l_max = 8 and 20-10-10 discretization points)
- 2-alpha subsystem energy expectation value epsilon: 8.4465 MeV without Coulomb, 9.3490 MeV with shielded Coulomb (l_max = 8)
- Overlap with SU3 (04) shell-model wave function c_(04): 0.8211 without Coulomb, 0.7860 with shielded Coulomb
- Difference between Faddeev and variational calculations: less than 1 keV for 3-alpha energies in both Coulomb on and off cases
- Harmonic oscillator constant for alpha cluster: nu = 0.275 fm^{-2}
- Shielded Coulomb cutoff radius: R_C = 6 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-cluster equation (Fujiwara et al., 2001) which employed the orthogonality condition model of Horiuchi (1974) but used the two-cluster RGM kernel as the interaction potential. The method enables practical solution of the 3-alpha Faddeev equation with realistic quark-exchange kernels, and the numerical equivalence to the variational approach using translationally invariant harmonic-oscillator basis (Fukushima and Kamimura, 1978) validates the formalism for application to few-baryon systems such as the hypertriton.
