# Protoneutron Stars with Kaon Condensation and their Delayed Collapse

> Properties of protoneutron stars are discussed in the context of kaon condensation. Thermal and neutrino trapping effects are very important ingredients to study them. By solving the TOV equation, we discuss the static properties of protoneutron stars and the possibility of the delayed collapse during their evolution.

# Protoneutron Stars with Kaon Condensation and their Delayed Collapse

## Key claim

The delayed collapse of a protoneutron star into a low-mass black hole is possible due to kaon condensation, but only under specific conditions: the neutrino-trapping effect (rather than the thermal effect) provides the dominant contribution to the collapse within the chiral model, contrary to earlier meson-exchange model results that emphasized thermal effects.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state for kaon-condensed matter at finite temperature using a nonlinear chiral Lagrangian based on SU(3) chiral symmetry, treating thermal and quantum fluctuations around the kaon condensate via a one-loop Hartree approximation. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for protoneutron stars under both isothermal and isentropic conditions, with and without neutrino trapping, using the Maxwell construction for the first-order phase transition. The kaon-nucleon sigma term was taken as the most reasonable value, with the parameter a3ms = -222 MeV (giving Sigma_KN = 344 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of cold (S=0) neutrino-free kaon-condensed star: 1.59 solar masses at central density 13.2 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3)
- Maximum gravitational mass of cold (S=0) neutrino-trapped star (Y_le=0.4): 1.64 solar masses at central density 10.0 rho_0
- Maximum gravitational mass of hot (S=3) neutrino-trapped star (Y_le=0.4): 1.86 solar masses at central density 5.3 rho_0 (on the normal branch)
- Maximum total baryon number for stable evolution to cold neutron star: 2.14 x 10^57, corresponding to a maximum mass of 1.54 solar masses
- Critical density for kaon condensation is shifted upward by both neutrino trapping and thermal effects, with the neutrino-trapping effect dominant at low temperatures

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of kaon condensation in cold neutron stars by Thorsson et al. and the numerical simulations of Baumgarte et al. (who used zero-temperature equations of state) by consistently including finite temperature effects within a chiral symmetry framework. The authors contrast their chiral model approach with the meson-exchange model used by Prakash et al. and Pons et al., noting that the chiral model gives stronger condensation and thus a qualitatively different scenario for delayed collapse. The results enable more realistic predictions for the maximum mass of cold neutron stars by accounting for the evolutionary path through the deleptonization era, and provide a mechanism for low-mass black hole formation that could explain the non-detection of a neutron star in SN1987A.
