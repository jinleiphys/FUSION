# The entrainment matrix of a superfluid neutron-proton mixture at a finite temperature

> The entrainment matrix (also termed the Andreev-Bashkin matrix or the mass-density matrix) for a neutron-proton mixture is derived at a finite temperature in a neutron star core. The calculation is performed in the frame of the Landau Fermi-liquid theory generalized to account for superfluidity of nucleons. It is shown, that the temperature dependence of the entrainment matrix is described by a universal function independent on an actual model of nucleon-nucleon interaction employed. The results are presented in the form convenient for their practical use. The entrainment matrix is important, e.g., in kinetics of superfluid nucleon mixtures or in studies of the dynamical evolution of neutron stars (in particular, in the studies of star pulsations and pulsar glitches).

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the entrainment matrix (Andreev-Bashkin matrix) for a superfluid neutron-proton mixture in a neutron star core is described by a universal function independent of the actual model of nucleon-nucleon interaction employed, provided one works in the linear approximation in superfluid velocities and assumes singlet-state pairing of both species.

## Method

The authors generalized the Landau Fermi-liquid theory to account for superfluidity of nucleons, following the approach of Larkin and Migdal (1963) and Leggett (1965). They derived the entrainment matrix by expanding the energy density of a neutron-proton mixture with superfluid currents to linear order in the superfluid velocities, minimizing the thermodynamic potential, and solving the resulting set of linear equations for the matrix elements on the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- The function Phi_alpha, which governs the temperature dependence, was calculated numerically and approximated by Gnedin and Yakovlev (1995); its fit is given in Appendix A of the paper.
- The effective mass relation: m_alpha* / m_alpha = 1 + (N_0_alpha / 3) [ f_1^(alpha alpha) + (m_beta / m_alpha) (p_F_beta / p_F_alpha)^2 f_1^(alpha beta) ], for alpha != beta.
- The entrainment matrix elements are expressed in terms of the Landau parameters F_1^(alpha beta) = f_1^(alpha beta) sqrt(N_0_alpha N_0_beta), where N_0_alpha = m_alpha* p_F_alpha / pi^2.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature calculations of the entrainment matrix by Borumand et al. (1996) and Comer and Joynt (2003) to finite temperatures, which are needed for analyzing kinetic properties of neutron star matter (bulk and shear viscosity, diffusion) and for studying pulsations of warm neutron stars or stars with pulsation energy comparable to thermal energy. The results enable practical calculations of the entrainment matrix for any nucleon-nucleon interaction model, and the authors note that the results can be extended to triplet-state (^3P_2) neutron pairing as shown in Section 4.1.
