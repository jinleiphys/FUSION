# Relativistic Structure of the Deuteron: 1.Electro-disintegration and y-scaling

> Realistic solutions of the spinor-spinor Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron with realistic interaction kernel including the exchange of pi, sigma, omega, rho, eta and delta mesons, are used to systematically investigate relativistic effects in inclusive quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering within the relativistic impulse approximation. Relativistic y-scaling is considered by generalising the non relativistic scaling function to the relativistic case, and it is shown that y-scaling does occur in the usual relativistic scaling variable resulting from the energy conservation in the instant form of dynamics. The present approach of y-scaling is fully covariant, with the deuteron being described by eight components, viz. the 3S_1^{++}, 3S_1^{--}, 3D_1^{++}, 3D_1^{--}, 3P_1^{+-}, 3P_1^{-+}, 1P_1^{+-}, 1P_1^{-+} waves. It is demonstrated that if the negative relative energy states 1P_1, 3P_1 are disregarded, the concept of covariant momentum distributions N(p_0,p), with p_0=M_D/2-\sqrt{p^2+m^2}, can be introduced, and that calculations of lectro-disintegration cross section in terms of these distributions agree within few percents with the exact calculations which include the 1P_1, 3P_1 states, provided the nucleon three momentum |p|\<= 1 GeV/c; in this momentum range, the asymptotic relativistic scaling function is shown to coincide with the longitudinal covariant momentum distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic y-scaling occurs in inclusive quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering using a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach with realistic meson-exchange interactions, but only if the negative relative energy states (the P-wave components) are included; when these states are disregarded, the covariant momentum distributions reproduce the exact cross section within a few percent only for nucleon three-momentum up to 1 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors solved the spinor-spinor Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron with a realistic one-boson exchange interaction kernel (including pi, sigma, omega, rho, eta, and delta mesons) to obtain eight-component deuteron wave functions. They then computed the inclusive quasi-elastic electron-deuteron cross section within the relativistic impulse approximation, generalizing the non-relativistic scaling function to the relativistic case using the instant-form relativistic scaling variable derived from energy conservation. The hadronic tensor was constructed by contracting the leptonic tensor with a nucleonic operator averaged over Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, with the nucleon pole approximation applied for the spectator nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The covariant momentum distribution approach agrees with exact calculations (including P-wave states) within "few percents" for nucleon three-momentum |p| ≤ 1 GeV/c
- The non-relativistic approximation for the scaling function is valid within few percent provided Q² > 0.5 GeV²/c²
- The conditions for non-relativistic y-scaling are: 2m/3 ≤ ν < |q| and |q| ≥ 2m (where m is the nucleon mass)
- The minimum nucleon momentum is given by |p|_min = |y| = (1/2)|(M_D + ν)√(1 − 4m²/s) − |q||, where s = M_D(M_D + 2ν) − Q²

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic y-scaling analysis of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1994) and the relativistic light-front treatment of Polyzou (1997) to a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter framework with realistic interactions. The approach enables a consistent relativistic definition of nucleon momentum distributions and scaling functions for the deuteron, and provides the foundation for future work including final state interaction effects and comparison with experimental data from Bosted et al.
