# Heavy quark radiation in an anisotropic hot QCD medium

> The impact of momentum anisotropy on the heavy quarks (HQs) dynamics has been investigated in a hot QCD medium while considering both collisional and radiative processes within the ambit of the Fokker-Planck approach. The relative orientation of the HQs motion (momentum vector) with respect to the direction of anisotropy is responsible for the character of transport coefficients. Therefore, the drag and diffusion coefficients of the HQs are decomposed, respectively, into two and four components by considering a general tensor basis. Each component of the drag and diffusion coefficient of the HQs has been analyzed in detail. It is observed that the anisotropy has a significant impact on the transport coefficients of the HQ for both the collisional and the radiational processes. The nuclear suppression factor, $R_{AA}$, has been computed considering the anisotropic medium. It is observed that the momentum anisotropy affects the $R_{AA}$ of the HQs significantly in both elastic and inelastic cases.

## Key claim

The paper claims that momentum anisotropy in the hot QCD medium significantly affects heavy quark transport coefficients and the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) for both collisional and radiative (gluon bremsstrahlung) processes, but this conclusion is explicitly conditioned on the weak anisotropy limit (\(\xi \ll 1\)) and on a specific relative orientation between the heavy quark momentum and the anisotropy direction (here taken as \(\theta = \pi/4\)).

## Method

The authors model heavy quark propagation in an anisotropic QGP using the Fokker-Planck equation derived from the Boltzmann equation under the soft momentum transfer approximation. They decompose the drag coefficient into two components and the diffusion tensor into four components using a general tensor basis that accounts for the relative orientation of the heavy quark momentum with respect to the anisotropy vector \(\mathbf{n}\). The anisotropic medium is described by a non-equilibrium distribution function expanded to first order in the anisotropy parameter \(\xi\) (weak anisotropy limit), and the radiative contribution is included via a factorized form that multiplies the elastic transport coefficients by an integral over the gluon emission phase space, incorporating the dead-cone effect and a simplified treatment of Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass taken as 1.3 GeV; two-loop running coupling constant \(\alpha_s\) used throughout.
* Anisotropy parameter values considered: \(\xi = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4\) (consistent with hydrodynamics estimates from Strickland (2014)).
* Temperature fixed at \(T = 360\) MeV (initial maximum temperature at RHIC energies).
* The nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) is computed assuming a final time \(\tau_f = 6\) fm/c for the time evolution.
* The drag coefficient in the anisotropic medium is suppressed relative to the isotropic case for both elastic and inelastic processes at \(T = 360\) MeV and \(\theta = \pi/4\).
* The additional drag component \(\gamma_1^{(\text{aniso})}\) becomes negligible at higher momentum for radiative processes.
* The diffusion components \(D_1^{(\text{aniso})}\) and \(D_0^{(\text{aniso})}\) are nearly equal in the limit \(p \rightarrow 0\); at higher momentum, \(D_1^{(\text{aniso})}\) dominates over \(D_0^{(\text{aniso})}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark transport in isotropic QGP (Mazumder (2013), Prakash (2023)) and previous anisotropic treatments limited to collisional processes (Chandra (2015)) by including radiative (gluon bremsstrahlung) contributions. It builds on the tensor decomposition formalism for anisotropic transport coefficients developed by Kumar (2021) and uses the anisotropic distribution function approach of Romatschke and Strickland (2003). The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy quark observables like \(R_{AA}\) in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the rapidly expanding QGP is expected to develop momentum anisotropy.
