# Systematics analyses on nucleon isovector observables in 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall lattice QCD near physical mass

> Analyses on possible systematics in some isovector nucleon observables in the RBC+UKQCD 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall fermion (DWF) lattice-QCD are presented. The vector charge, axial charge, quark momentum and helicity fractions, and transversity are discussed using mainly the Iwasaki\(\times\)DSDR ensemble at pion mass of 170 MeV. No autocorrelation issue is observed in the vector charge and quark momentum and helicity fractions. Blocked Jack-knife analyses expose significant growth of estimated error for the axial charge with increasing block sizes that are similar to or larger than the known autocorrelation time of the gauge-field topological charge. Similar growth is seen in the transversity. These two observables, however, do not seem correlated with the topological charge.

# Systematics analyses on nucleon isovector observables in 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall lattice QCD near physical mass

## Key claim
The approximately 10% deficit in the calculated ratio of isovector axial charge to vector charge (g_A/g_V) compared to the experimental value of 1.2723(23) persists even at near-physical pion mass, and the deficit does not appear to be caused by autocorrelation with gauge-field topological charge or low-mode deflation, but may instead arise from finite-size effects related to spatial fluctuations of the calculation.

## Method
The authors used conventional 3-point to 2-point correlation function ratios to calculate isovector nucleon observables on four RBC+UKQCD 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall fermion (DWF) ensembles, with the lightest ensemble at pion mass 170 MeV using Iwasaki×DSDR gauge action. They employed AMA (all-mode averaging) technology to enhance statistics for the 170-MeV and 330-MeV ensembles, and performed blocked jackknife analyses with block sizes up to 4 to investigate autocorrelation effects. Spatial dependence was explored by dividing AMA samples into two spatial halves in each of the three spatial directions.

## Key numbers
- The ratio g_A/g_V from the 170-MeV ensemble shows values of 1.26(5) for the first half of trajectories (748-1084) and 1.07(5) for the second half (1100-1420), differing by almost four standard deviations
- Blocked jackknife analysis for g_A gives 1.66(6) at block size 1, 1.66(7) at block size 2, 1.71(8) at block size 3, and 1.65(4) at block size 4, showing error growth with block size
- Blocked jackknife analysis for transversity ⟨1⟩_{δu-δd} gives 1.42(4) at block size 1, 1.42(6) at block size 2, 1.42(6) at block size 3, and 1.41(3) at block size 4, showing weaker error growth
- The experimental value of g_A/g_V is 1.2723(23) from PDG 2014
- The 170-MeV ensemble has inverse lattice spacing a^{-1}=1.371(10) GeV with Iwasaki×DSDR gauge action at β=1.75
- The 420-MeV and 330-MeV ensembles used Iwasaki gauge action at β=2.13 with a^{-1}=1.729(4) GeV

## Context
This work extends the RBC+UKQCD collaboration's ongoing investigation of nucleon structure using 2+1-flavor dynamical DWF ensembles, following earlier reports of the g_A/g_V deficit by Yamazaki (2008) and Ohta (2013). The authors note that earlier phenomenological analyses such as the MIT bag model (Chodos, 1974) estimated g_A/g_V = 1.09 without pion contributions, and the Skyrmion model (Adkins, 1983) gave a conditionally convergent result of 0.61 strongly dependent on pion geometry. The results suggest that larger spatial volumes may stabilize the calculation, and the systematics appears to be a finite-size effect rather than a topological or low-mode issue, enabling future work with larger volumes to resolve the deficit.
