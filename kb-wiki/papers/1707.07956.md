# Evolution of pressures and correlations in the Glasma produced in high energy nuclear collisions

> We consider the SU(2) Glasma with gaussian fluctuations and study its evolution by means of classical Yang-Mills equations solved numerically on a lattice. Neglecting in this first study the longitudinal expansion we follow the evolution of the pressures of the system and compute the effect of the fluctuations in the early stage up to $t\approx 2$ fm/c, that is the time range in which the Glasma is relevant for high energy collisions. We measure the ratio of the longitudinal over the transverse pressure, $P_L/P_T$, and we find that unless the fluctuations carry a substantial amount of the energy density at the initial time, they do not change significantly the evolution of $P_L/P_T$ in the early stage, and that the system remains quite anisotropic. We also measure the longitudinal fields correlators both in the transverse plane and along the longitudinal direction: while at initial time fields appear to be anticorrelated in the transverse plane, this anticorrelation disappears in the very early stage and the correlation length in the transverse plane increases; on the other hand, we find that the longitudinal correlator decreases for a small longitudinal separation while being approximately constant for larger separation, which we interpret as a partial loss of longitudinal correlation induced by the dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors find that unless fluctuations carry a substantial fraction of the initial energy density (approximately 30% or more), they do not significantly change the evolution of the longitudinal-to-transverse pressure ratio \(P_L/P_T\) in the early stage up to \(t \approx 2\) fm/c, and the system remains quite anisotropic.

## Method

The authors solve classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations numerically on a lattice for an SU(2) Glasma in a static three-dimensional box with periodic boundary conditions, neglecting longitudinal expansion. Gaussian fluctuations of the color electric field are added on top of the Glasma initial condition, which is generated within the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. The evolution of the pressures and gauge-invariant field correlators (both in the transverse plane and along the longitudinal direction) is computed up to \(g^2\mu t \approx 10\), corresponding to about 2 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- The physical length of the box in the transverse plane is set to \(L = 2\) fm, with \(g^2\mu = 1\) GeV.
- For \(\Delta=0\) (no fluctuations), the ratio \(P_L/P_T\) remains below approximately 0.3 for \(0.5 \lesssim g^2\mu t \lesssim 10\) (corresponding to \(0.1~\mathrm{fm/c} \lesssim t \lesssim 2~\mathrm{fm/c}\)).
- With fluctuations carrying about 30% of the total initial energy (\(\Delta^2/g^2\mu a = 2.2\times 10^{-4}\)), \(P_L/P_T\) lies between 0.3 and 0.5 in the same time range.
- With fluctuations carrying about 50% of the total initial energy (\(\Delta^2/g^2\mu a = 5\times 10^{-4}\)), \(P_L/P_T\) lies between 0.5 and 0.73.
- The magnetic correlator in the transverse plane becomes negative for \(g^2\mu x \approx 0.4\) at initial time.

## Context

This work extends studies of Glasma isotropization and instabilities by Romatschke and Venugopalan (2006), Fukushima and Gelis (2011), and Iida et al. (2014), who examined fluctuations and entropy production but placed less emphasis on isotropization. It also connects to Gelis et al. (2013), who found that quantum fluctuations with a larger QCD coupling (\(g=0.5\)) produce more substantial isotropization than with a smaller coupling (\(g=0.1\)). The results suggest that early isotropization via fluctuation-induced instabilities requires fluctuations to carry a large fraction of the initial energy, and they enable future studies of the dependence of isotropization times on the fluctuation seed in long-time CYM simulations.
