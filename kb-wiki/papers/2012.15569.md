# Microscopic-Macroscopic Approach for Ground-State Energies Based on the Gogny Force with the Wigner-Kirkwood Averaging Scheme

> In the previous paper I \cite{bhagwat20} we have shown that self-consistent Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) potentials and densities associated with a given finite-range interaction can be parametrized by generalized Fermi distributions. As a next step, a comprehensive calculation of ground-state properties of a large number of spherical and deformed even-even nuclei is carried out in the present work using the Gogny D1S force within the ETF scheme. The parametrized ETF potentials and densities of paper I are used to calculate the smooth part of the energy and the shell corrections within the Wigner-Kirkwood semiclassical averaging scheme. It is shown that the shell corrections thus obtained, along with a simple liquid drop prescription, yield a good description of ground-state masses and potential energy surfaces for nuclei spanning the entire periodic table.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a microscopic-macroscopic (Mic-Mac) model using self-consistent Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) potentials derived from the Gogny D1S force, combined with Wigner-Kirkwood (WK) semiclassical averaging for shell corrections and a fitted liquid drop model, can reproduce experimental ground-state masses of even-even nuclei with an rms deviation of 834 keV, provided the macroscopic part is fitted to experimental data rather than to the HFB energies from the same interaction.

## Method

The authors first parametrized the self-consistent ETF central and spin-orbit potentials (obtained with the Gogny D1S force) as generalized Fermi distributions, as described in a previous paper (Bhagwat 2020). They then computed the smooth part of the energy using a liquid drop model with deformation-dependent surface, curvature, and Coulomb terms, and calculated shell corrections via the Wigner-Kirkwood \(\hbar\)-expansion of the one-body partition function up to fourth order. Pairing correlations were included through the Lipkin-Nogami model, and the total energy was minimized with respect to deformation parameters \(\beta_2\), \(\beta_4\), and \(\gamma\) for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

* The Gogny-based WK Mic-Mac model fitted to experimental energies of 551 even-even spherical and deformed nuclei yields an rms deviation of 834 keV from experiment.
* When the same model is fitted to reproduce the Gogny D1S HFB energies of the same 551 nuclei, the rms deviation from experiment increases to 3.95 MeV.
* The renormalization factor \(\eta\) for the microscopic energy was set to 0.67 to ensure that the isotopes \(^{182,184,186}\)Pb remain spherical in their ground state.
* The fitted liquid drop parameters (exp fit) include: volume coefficient \(a_v = -15.9030\) MeV, surface coefficient \(a_s = 20.2648\) MeV, curvature coefficient \(a_{cur} = -3.7770\) MeV, and charge radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.1919\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous WK Mic-Mac calculations (Bhagwat 2010, 2012) that used the phenomenological Wyss potential, by instead using self-consistent potentials derived from the Gogny D1S effective interaction. The approach provides a link between mean-field calculations using effective forces and traditional Mic-Mac models such as the Finite-Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Moller and Nix (1997) or the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) of Pomorski (2003). The results show that the Mic-Mac model removes the well-known energy drift for neutron-rich nuclei that plagues HFB calculations with the D1S force (Pillet 2017), and the method could be applied to other Gogny parametrizations like D1M (Goriely 2009) or to fitting new finite-range interactions.
