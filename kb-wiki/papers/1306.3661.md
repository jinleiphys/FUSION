# Efimov Physics around the neutron rich Calcium-60 isotope

> We calculate the neutron-Calcium-60 S-wave scattering phase shifts using state of the art coupled-cluster theory combined with modern ab initio interactions derived from chiral effective theory. Effects of three-nucleon forces are included schematically as density dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions. This information is combined with halo effective field theory in order to investigate the Calcium-60-neutron-neutron system. We predict correlations between different three-body observables and the two-neutron separation energy of Calcium-62. This provides evidence of Efimov physics along the Calcium isotope chain. Experimental key observables that facilitate a test of our findings are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper provides evidence that the neutron-rich calcium isotope chain, specifically the $^{60}$Ca-$n$-$n$ system, may exhibit Efimov physics, but this conclusion is conditional on the computed S-wave scattering length being large (about six times larger than the effective range) and on the uncertainties in the coupled-cluster calculations due to truncation errors and the schematic treatment of three-nucleon forces.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-cluster calculations in the singles-and-doubles approximation (CCSD) for $^{60,61}$Ca using chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order with a 500 MeV cutoff, combined with a schematic density-dependent three-nucleon force. They computed neutron-$^{60}$Ca S-wave elastic scattering phase shifts by matching overlap functions to asymptotic forms, extracted scattering lengths and effective ranges via the effective range expansion, and used these as input for halo effective field theory to analyze the three-body $^{60}$Ca-neutron-neutron system.

## Key numbers
- S-wave scattering length for $^{60}$Ca-$n$: $a_{cn} = 54(1)$ fm (average of results for $\hbar\omega = 20$ and 24 MeV).
- Effective range for $^{60}$Ca-$n$: $r_{cn} = 9.0(2)$ fm (average of results for $\hbar\omega = 20$ and 24 MeV).
- Neutron separation energy of $^{61}$Ca: 8 keV for $\hbar\omega = 20$ MeV and 5 keV for $\hbar\omega = 24$ MeV.
- Discrete scaling factor for the $^{62}$Ca system: approximately 256.
- Relative charge radius of $^{61}$Ca: $\langle r_E^2\rangle_{\rm rel} = 0.39(2)$ fm$^2$.
- Relative matter radius of $^{61}$Ca: $\langle r_{mat}^2\rangle_{\rm rel} = 24(2)$ fm$^2$.
- Breakdown energy scale $S_{\rm deep}$: 544 keV for $\hbar\omega = 20$ MeV and 509 keV for $\hbar\omega = 24$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of Efimov physics in nuclear systems beyond the well-known candidate $^{22}$C, which was studied by Tanaka (2010) and Horiuchi (2006), and builds on coupled-cluster calculations of neutron-rich calcium isotopes by Hagen (2012) that suggested a large S-wave scattering length might occur in $^{61}$Ca. The results enable experimental tests at facilities like FRIB, which might provide access to calcium isotopes as heavy as $^{68}$Ca as noted by Sherrill (2012), and predict correlations between three-body observables and the two-neutron separation energy of $^{62}$Ca that could be used to confirm Efimov physics.
