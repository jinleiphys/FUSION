# Numerical modelling of the quantum-tail effect on fusion rates at low energy

> Results of numerical simulations of fusion rate d(d,p)t, for low-energy deuteron beam, colliding with deuterated metallic matrix (Raiola et al. Phys. Lett.B 547 (2002) 193 and Eur. Phys J. A 13 (2002) 377) confirm analytical estimate given in Coraddu et al. nucl-th/0401043, taking into account quantum tails in the momentum distribution function of target particles, and predict an enhanced astrophysical factor in the 1 keV region in qualitative agreement with experiments.

# Numerical modelling of the quantum-tail effect on fusion rates at low energy

## Key claim
Numerical simulations confirm that quantum corrections to the momentum distribution function of target deuterons, producing a power-law tail instead of an exponential Maxwellian tail, can explain the enhanced d(d,p)t fusion rates observed in deuterated metallic matrix experiments at beam energies below 2 keV, without requiring unrealistically large screening potentials.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo integration of the full reaction-rate integral (Eq. 4) using a generalized distribution function that includes a Lorentzian spectral profile for target particles in a non-ideal plasma. They compared results from four models: the standard cross-section formula (K1), a simplified integral with delta-function approximation (K2), a model using the power-law tail decomposition (K3), and the full integral (K), for beam energies from 1 to 15 keV at target temperature T = 2.44×10⁻² eV and target concentration Nₐ = 5×10²³ cm⁻³.

## Key numbers
- At beam energy 1 keV, the full model rate K = 2.82×10⁻⁷ (units of ⟨σv⟩) gives a ratio K/K₁ = 3.42×10², meaning the quantum-tail effect enhances the rate by a factor of 342 over the standard cross-section model.
- At beam energy 1.2 keV, K/K₁ = 2.17×10¹ (enhancement factor 21.7); at 1.5 keV, K/K₁ = 1.43; at 2 keV and above, K/K₁ ≤ 1.09, showing negligible enhancement.
- With screening potential Uₑ = 28 eV included, the ratio K₃/K₁ at 1 keV is 25.8, compared to 3.42×10² without screening, indicating screening alone cannot account for the observed enhancement.
- Target particle concentration used: Nₐ = 5×10²³ cm⁻³; target temperature: T = 2.44×10⁻² eV.

## Context
This work extends the analytical estimates of Coraddu et al. (2004) and the quantum-correction framework of Starostin et al. (2000, 2002) to numerically model the specific experimental conditions of Raiola et al. (2002), who observed enhanced fusion rates in deuterated metals. The results contradict the earlier hypothesis that an unrealistically large screening potential could explain the data, and instead support the quantum-tail mechanism as the dominant cause. This enables future calculations of low-energy fusion rates in dense plasmas and metallic environments where non-ideal plasma effects produce power-law momentum distributions.
