# No-recoil approximation to knock-on exchange potential in the double folding model for heavy-ion collisions

> We propose the no-recoil approximation, which is valid for heavy systems, for a double folding nucleus-nucleus potential. With this approximation, the non-local knock-on exchange contribution becomes a local form. We discuss the applicability of this approximation for the elastic scattering of $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca system. We find that, for this system and heavier, the no-recoil approximation works as good as another widely used local approximation which employs a local plane wave for the relative motion between the colliding nuclei. We also compare the results of the no-recoil calculations with those of the zero-range approximation often used to handle the knock-on exchange effect.

# No-recoil approximation to knock-on exchange potential in the double folding model for heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The no-recoil approximation, which neglects the recoil effect arising from nucleon exchange between projectile and target, yields a local exchange potential that works as well as the local momentum approximation for the $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca system and heavier systems, but only when the bombarding energy is relatively low and the inverse reduced mass $\alpha$ is small; at high energies (e.g., $E_{\rm lab}=156.0$ MeV) the approximation fails because the local momentum becomes large.

## Method
The authors start from the double folding model Schr\"odinger equation containing a non-local knock-on exchange term. They set the recoil parameter $\alpha = 1/A_P + 1/A_T$ to zero in the exchange potential expression, yielding a local potential that requires no self-consistent iteration, unlike the local momentum approximation. They test the approximation by computing elastic scattering angular distributions for $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca at three laboratory energies (30.0, 50.6, and 156.0 MeV) using the CDM3Y6 density-dependent M3Y interaction, and compare to exact solutions of the integro-differential equation, to the local momentum approximation, and to the zero-range approximation.

## Key numbers
- $\alpha = 0.192$ for $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca; $\alpha = 0.0673$ for $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb.
- Barrier height in no-recoil approximation: 8.44 MeV for $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca.
- Barrier heights in local momentum approximation: 8.45 MeV at $E_{\rm lab}=30$ MeV, 8.44 MeV at $E_{\rm lab}=50.6$ MeV, and 8.50 MeV at $E_{\rm lab}=156$ MeV for $^6$Li + $^{40}$Ca.
- For a lighter system, $\alpha$ + $^{90}$Zr ($\alpha=0.261$, barrier height ~11.7 MeV), the no-recoil approximation fails even at $E_{\rm lab}=40.0$ MeV.
- Zero-range approximation strength: $J(E) = -590 (1 - 0.002 \, E_{\rm lab}/A_P)$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$.

## Context
This work extends the double folding model treatment of the knock-on exchange term beyond the zero-range approximation of Love (1975) and the local momentum approximation of Kobos et al. (1988, 1994) and Khoa et al. (1997, 2001). The no-recoil approximation was previously discussed in general terms by Brink and Stancu (1997) and by Gareev et al. (1976), and for transfer reactions by Brink and Winther (1991), but this paper provides the first systematic numerical test against exact non-local calculations. The approximation enables simpler calculations for heavy systems where the local momentum approximation may fail in the classically forbidden region below the Coulomb barrier, and opens the possibility of incorporating full resonating group method exchange effects for heavy systems as noted by Fliessbach (1974).
