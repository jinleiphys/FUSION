# Impact of W and Z Production Data and Compatibility of Neutrino DIS Data in Nuclear Parton Distribution Functions

> Vector boson production and neutrino deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) data are crucial for constraining the strange quark parton distribution function (PDF) and more generally for flavor decomposition in PDF extractions. We extend the nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs (nPDFs) by adding the recent $W$ and $Z$ production data from the LHC in a global nPDF fit. The new nPDF set, referred to as nCTEQ15WZ, is used as a starting point for a follow-up study in which we assess the compatibility of neutrino DIS data with charged lepton DIS data. Specifically, we re-analyze neutrino DIS data from NuTeV, Chorus, and CDHSW, as well as dimuon data from CCFR and NuTeV. To scrutinize the level of compatibility, different kinematic regions of the neutrino data are investigated. Fits to the neutrino data alone and a preliminary global fit are performed and compared to nCTEQ15WZ.

## Key claim

The paper extends the nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs by adding LHC W and Z production data (creating the nCTEQ15WZ set) and then assesses the compatibility of neutrino DIS data with charged lepton DIS data, finding that tensions between these data sets are most pronounced at low x (x ≤ 0.1) and that applying a kinematic cut of x ≥ 0.1 on neutrino data allows all neutrino data sets to satisfy the authors' compatibility criteria.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order using the nCTEQ15 fitting framework, parametrizing nuclear PDFs in polynomial form with mass-number dependence at the PDF parameter level and using the ACOT scheme for heavy quark treatment. They first fitted LHC W/Z boson production data together with the nCTEQ15 data set to produce the nCTEQ15WZ nPDF set, then separately fitted neutrino DIS data from NuTeV, CDHSW, and Chorus along with dimuon data from CCFR and NuTeV (the DimuNeu fit), and finally performed combined global fits with and without a kinematic cut of x ≥ 0.1 on the neutrino data to assess compatibility using a hypothesis-testing method with a tolerance of Δχ² ≤ 45.

## Key numbers

- The nCTEQ15WZ fit includes 853 data points from the baseline set, plus 2366 from NuTeV, 929 from CDHSW, 824 from Chorus, and 174 from charm dimuon data, all after kinematic cuts of Q > 2 GeV and W > 3.5 GeV
- The compatibility test shows Δχ²_S = 92 for NuTeV and Δχ²_S = 106 for the combined DimuNeu data set, both exceeding the tolerance of T = 45
- After applying the x ≥ 0.1 cut, Δχ²_S values drop to 17 for NuTeV and 28 for DimuNeu, with all neutrino data sets satisfying the compatibility criteria
- The Chorus data set shows Δχ²_S = 17 (passing) and CDHSW shows Δχ²_S = 44 (marginally passing) in the full analysis
- The nCTEQ15WZ fit used 19 actively varied PDF parameters and three normalization parameters for LHC W/Z data sets (CMS Run I, CMS Run II, and ATLAS)

## Context

This work extends the nCTEQ15 nuclear PDF analysis (Kovarik 2015) by incorporating LHC W/Z boson production data, following a reweighting study (Kusina 2016) that suggested these data could better constrain strange and gluon nPDFs. The paper directly addresses the known tension between neutrino DIS data and charged lepton DIS data previously observed in nCTEQ studies (Kovarik 2010, Schienbein 2009) but not found in analyses by De Florian (2011) or Paukkunen (2013) that used different treatments of systematic uncertainties. The results enable future global nPDF fits that can include neutrino data with appropriate kinematic cuts, and the identified low-x tension motivates further investigation into nuclear shadowing differences, higher-twist contributions, and PDF parametrization assumptions.
