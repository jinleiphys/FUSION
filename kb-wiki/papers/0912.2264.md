# Relativistic corrections of one-nucleon current in low-energy three-nucleon photonuclear reactions

> Proton-deuteron radiative capture and two- and three-body photodisintegration of 3He at low energy are described using realistic hadronic dynamics and including the Coulomb force. The sensitivity of the observables to the relativistic corrections of one-nucleon electromagnetic current operator is studied. Significant effects of the relativistic spin-orbit charge are found for the vector analyzing powers in the proton-deuteron radiative capture and for the beam-target parallel-antiparallel spin asymmetry in the three-body photodisintegration of 3He.

## Key claim

The relativistic spin-orbit correction to the one-nucleon charge operator produces significant effects on vector analyzing powers in low-energy proton-deuteron radiative capture and on the beam-target parallel-antiparallel spin asymmetry in three-body photodisintegration of 3He, even though the hadronic dynamics used is nonrelativistic and the authors expect relativistic hadronic dynamics effects to be small at these energies.

## Method

The authors solved exact Faddeev and Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-particle equations in momentum space with partial wave decomposition, using the CD Bonn + Δ coupled-channel potential that allows virtual nucleon excitation to a Δ isobar and includes the Coulomb interaction via screening and renormalization. The electromagnetic current was expanded in electric and magnetic multipoles, with electric multipoles using the Siegert form without long-wavelength approximation, and calculations were performed with and without relativistic corrections to the one-nucleon charge (spin-orbit and Darwin-Foldy) and spatial current.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron vector analyzing power A_y(d) for pd radiative capture at deuteron lab energies E_d = 10, 17.5, and 29 MeV shows sizable spin-orbit charge effect that improves description of experimental data
- Proton vector analyzing power A_y(p) at E_p = 5 MeV proton lab energy shows strong and beneficial spin-orbit charge effect
- Deuteron tensor analyzing power A_yy at E_d = 45 MeV shows spin-orbit charge effect at forward and backward angles
- For 3He two-body photodisintegration at E_γ = 15.2 MeV, the beam-target parallel-antiparallel spin asymmetry A_{P-A} is strongly affected by spin-orbit charge at forward and backward angles
- For 3He three-body photodisintegration at E_γ = 11.4, 15.2, and 20.6 MeV, the spin-orbit charge yields significant effect on A_{P-A} at all scattering angles of the detected nucleon
- The 3He binding energies used with AV18 and N3LO potentials are 6.923 MeV and 7.128 MeV respectively, with D-state probabilities 8.47% and 6.32%, and internal kinetic energies 45.68 MeV and 33.78 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of relativistic spin-orbit charge effects in pd radiative capture (Deltuva 2005) to include two- and three-body photodisintegration of 3He, and provides predictions for experiments being performed at the HIGS facility (Weller 2009). The nonrelativistic results are consistent with those of Marcucci (2005) derived from different hadronic interactions and electromagnetic currents, while the spin-orbit charge effect in deuteron photodisintegration was previously found by Cambi (1982), Ritz (1998), and Arenhoevel (1999). The results enable experimental tests of the chosen hadronic and electromagnetic dynamics using upcoming polarization data from HIGS.
