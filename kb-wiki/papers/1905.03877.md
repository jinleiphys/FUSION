# Production of exotic tetraquarks $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

> We investigate the production of exotic tetraquarks, $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q} \equiv T_{QQ}$ ($Q=c$ or $b$ and $q=u$ or $d$), in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using the quark coalescence model. The $T_{QQ}$ yield is given by the overlap of the density matrix of the constituents in the emission source with the Wigner function of the produced tetraquark. The tetraquark wave function is obtained from exact solutions of the four-body problem using realistic constituent models. The production yields are typically one order of magnitude smaller than previous estimations based on simplified wave functions for the tetraquarks. We also evaluate the consequences of the partial restoration of chiral symmetry at the hadronization temperature on the coalescence probability. Such effects, in addition to increasing the stability of the tetraquarks, lead to an enhancement of the production yields, pointing towards an excellent discovery potential in forthcoming experiments. We discuss further consequences of our findings for the search of exotic tetraquarks in central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The production yields of exotic tetraquarks $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ in heavy-ion collisions are typically one order of magnitude smaller than previous estimations that used simplified single-Gaussian wave functions, but partial restoration of chiral symmetry at the hadronization temperature enhances the yields and increases tetraquark stability, pointing towards an excellent discovery potential in forthcoming experiments.

## Method

The authors employed the dynamical quark coalescence model, where the tetraquark yield is given by the overlap of the density matrix of the constituent quarks in the emission source with the Wigner function of the produced tetraquark. The tetraquark wave function was obtained from exact solutions of the four-body problem using two realistic constituent quark models: the chiral constituent quark model ($\chi$CQM) and the Cornell-like interaction model (AL1). The Wigner function was computed analytically using a generalized Gaussian variational method, and finite-temperature effects of partial chiral symmetry restoration were incorporated by modifying the masses of light constituent quarks and mesons according to Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $(I)J^P=(0)1^+$ $T_{bb}$ tetraquark in vacuum: $\Delta E_{T_{bb}} = -202$ MeV (below the $\bar B \bar B^*$ threshold)
- Binding energy of the $T_{bb}$ at hadronization temperature $T=156$ MeV: $\Delta E_{T_{bb}} = -348$ MeV
- Root-mean-square radius of $T_{bb}$: $0.22$ fm (vacuum), remaining nearly constant with temperature
- Yield of $T_{bb}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV without chiral restoration: $N_{T_{bb}} = 6.2 \times 10^{-9}$
- Yield of $T_{bb}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV without chiral restoration: $N_{T_{bb}} = 1.6 \times 10^{-8}$
- Yield of $T_{bb}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV with chiral restoration: $N_{T_{bb}} = 1.3 \times 10^{-8}$
- Yield of $T_{bb}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV with chiral restoration: $N_{T_{bb}} = 3.4 \times 10^{-8}$
- Yield of $T_{cc}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV without chiral restoration: $N_{T_{cc}} = 3.8 \times 10^{-5}$
- Binding energy of $T_{cc}$ in vacuum: $\Delta E_{T_{cc}} = -60$ MeV
- Hadronization temperature used: $T = 156$ MeV; source volume: $V = 5380$ fm$^3$; light quark number: $N_q = 700$
- Number of $b$ quarks per unit rapidity at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV: $N_b = 0.44$; at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV: $N_b = 0.71$
- Number of $c$ quarks per unit rapidity at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV: $N_c = 11$; at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV: $N_c = 14$

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model studies of exotic hadron production in heavy-ion collisions previously applied by Cho et al. (2011, 2017) and Hong et al. (2018), who used simplified single-Gaussian wave functions for tetraquarks. The authors improve upon those estimates by using realistic four-body wave functions from the $\chi$CQM and AL1 constituent quark models, which correctly reproduce low-lying meson and baryon spectra. The results enable more reliable predictions for the discovery potential of doubly-heavy tetraquarks in central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, and the inclusion of chiral symmetry restoration effects provides a framework for assessing in-medium stability of these exotic states against subsequent interactions in the hadronic medium.
