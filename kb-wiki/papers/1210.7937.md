# Fitting Skyrme functionals using linear response theory

> Recently, it has been recently shown that the linear response theory in symmetric nuclear matter can be used as a tool to detect finite size instabilities for different Skyrme functionals. In particular it has been shown that there is a correlation between the density at which instabilities occur in infinite matter and the instabilities in finite nuclei. In this article we present a new fitting protocol that uses this correlation to add new additional constraint in Symmetric Infinite Nuclear Matter in order to ensure the stability of finite nuclei against matter fluctuation in all spin and isospin channels. As an application, we give the parameters set for a new Skyrme functional which includes central and spin-orbit parts and which is free from instabilities by construction.

## Key claim

The authors present a new fitting protocol for Skyrme functionals that uses the correlation between poles in the linear response of symmetric nuclear matter and finite-nucleus instabilities to enforce stability, but the resulting functional (SLy5*) is only guaranteed to be free from instabilities up to 1.4 times saturation density, not at all densities.

## Method

The authors added a constraint from linear response theory to the standard Saclay-Lyon fitting protocol: for each candidate functional, they computed the poles of the RPA response function in symmetric nuclear matter for all spin/isospin channels and required that no pole appear below 1.4 times the saturation density. Starting from the SLy5 functional, they iteratively refitted the Skyrme parameters while enforcing this stability criterion, producing a new functional called SLy5*. They then tested the new functional by comparing binding energies, charge radii, and stability against spin polarization in self-consistent cranking calculations for a set of semi-magic nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The pole in the (0,0,1) channel for the SkP functional occurs at a density of 0.205 fm$^{-3}$ and a transferred momentum of 2.88 fm$^{-1}$.
- The stability criterion requires that the pole density $\rho_\mathrm{pole}$ be greater than 1.4 times the saturation density $\rho_\mathrm{sat}$.
- The new SLy5* functional has a Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn enhancement factor $\kappa_v = 0.418$, compared to $\kappa_v = 0.250$ for the original SLy5.
- The isoscalar coupling constant $C^{\tau}_0$ is 55.18 MeV fm$^5$ for SLy5* and 56.25 MeV fm$^5$ for SLy5; the isovector coupling constant $C^{\tau}_1$ is 1.23 MeV fm$^5$ for SLy5* and 23.95 MeV fm$^5$ for SLy5.
- The saturation density for both SLy5* and SLy5 is 0.160 fm$^{-3}$, and the binding energy per nucleon is -16.02 MeV for SLy5* and -15.98 MeV for SLy5.
- The average pairing gap is 1.201 MeV for SLy5 and 1.164 MeV for SLy5*.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Pastore et al. (2012) and Lesinski et al. (2006) that linear response theory can detect finite-size instabilities in Skyrme functionals, particularly those caused by the $\rho_1\Delta\rho_1$ gradient term which is invisible to Landau parameter constraints. The new fitting protocol is more powerful than standard Landau parameter constraints because those are only valid in the long-wavelength limit and cannot detect finite-size instabilities. The resulting SLy5* functional cures the spin instability found in SLy5 by Fracasso et al. (2012) using time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations, while maintaining comparable accuracy for binding energies and charge radii, enabling the construction of future functionals that are stable by construction.
