# Beam energy scan using a viscous hydro+cascade model

> Following the experimental program at BNL RHIC, we perform a similar "energy scan" using 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics coupled to the UrQMD hadron cascade, and study the collision energy dependence of pion and kaon rapidity distributions and $m_T$-spectra, as well as charged hadron elliptic flow. To this aim the equation of state for finite baryon density from a Chiral model coupled to the Polyakov loop is employed for hydrodynamic stage. 3D initial conditions from UrQMD are used to study gradual deviation from boost-invariant scaling flow. We find that the inclusion of shear viscosity in the hydrodynamic stage of evolution consistently improves the description of the data for Pb-Pb collisions at CERN SPS, as well as of the elliptic flow measurements for Au-Au collisions in the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at BNL RHIC. The suggested value of shear viscosity is $η/s\ge0.2$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.3\dots39$ GeV.

## Key claim

The inclusion of shear viscosity in the hydrodynamic stage of a hybrid model consistently improves the description of pion and kaon rapidity distributions, transverse-mass spectra, and charged hadron elliptic flow for heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. The suggested effective shear viscosity is η/s ≥ 0.2 for √sNN = 6.3 to 39 GeV, which is at least twice the typical value of about 0.08 obtained for √sNN = 200 GeV RHIC energy.

## Method

The authors used a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code in the Israel-Stewart framework, coupled to the UrQMD hadron cascade for the initial stage and afterburner. Initial conditions were generated from UrQMD events averaged to produce smooth energy, momentum, baryon, and charge densities, with the hydrodynamic starting time set to when the two nuclei have passed through each other. The equation of state from a Chiral model coupled to the Polyakov loop was employed for finite baryon density, and the transition from fluid to particles (particlization) was made at a constant energy density of 0.5 GeV/fm³. Only shear viscosity was considered, with bulk viscosity fixed to zero and no baryon or charge diffusion included.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s ≥ 0.2 for the collision energy range √sNN = 6.3 to 39 GeV.
- Duration of the hydrodynamic phase decreases from about 6.5 fm/c at √sNN = 17.3 GeV to about 4.5 fm/c at √sNN = 6.3 GeV.
- At the lowest SPS energy (√sNN = 6.3 GeV), the pre-hydrodynamic stage lifetime is 4.1 fm/c, comparable to the hydrodynamic stage duration.
- The transition from fluid to particles occurs at constant energy density εsw = 0.5 GeV/fm³.
- The relaxation time for shear is taken as τπ = 3η/(sT).
- The typical value of η/s ≈ 0.08 from Heinz (year not given) for √sNN = 200 GeV RHIC energy is cited for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the application of viscous hydrodynamics coupled to a hadron cascade, previously successful at top RHIC and LHC energies, to lower collision energies in the SPS and RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. It uses 3D initial conditions from UrQMD to study deviations from boost-invariant scaling flow, unlike earlier studies at higher energies. The results enable systematic extraction of the shear viscosity at finite baryon density and show that the effective η/s at these lower energies is significantly larger than the value of about 0.08 found at √sNN = 200 GeV by Heinz (year not given) using Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions.
