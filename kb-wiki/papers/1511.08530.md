# Isovector and isoscalar dipole excitations in $^{9}$Be and $^{10}$Be studied with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> Isovector and isoscalar dipole excitations in $^9$Be and $^{10}$Be are investigated in the framework of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, in which angular-momentum and parity projections are performed. In the present method, 1p-1h excitations on the ground state and large amplitude $α$-cluster mode are incorporated. The isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) in $E>20$ MeV shows the two peak structure which is understood by the dipole excitation in the 2$α$ core part with the prolate deformation. Because of valence neutron modes against the $2α$ core, low-energy E1 resonances appear in $E<20$ MeV exhausting about $20\%$ of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule and $10\%$ of the calculated energy-weighted sum. The dipole resonance at $E\sim 15$ MeV in $^{10}$Be can be interpreted as the parity partner of the ground state having a $^6$He+$α$ structure and has the remarkable E1 strength because of coherent contribution of two valence neutrons. The ISD strength for some low-energy resonances are significantly enhanced by the coupling with the $α$-cluster mode. The calculated E1 strength of $^9$Be reasonably describes the global feature of experimental photonuclear cross sections consisting of the low-energy strength in $E<20$ MeV and the GDR in $E>20$ MeV.

## Key claim

The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations incorporating both 1p-1h excitations and large-amplitude $\alpha$-cluster motion reproduce the global features of experimental photonuclear cross sections for $^{9}$Be, including the low-energy strength below 20 MeV and the giant dipole resonance (GDR) above 20 MeV, but the calculated GDR peak energy and strength are somewhat overestimated compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with angular-momentum and parity projections (AMD+VAP) to obtain ground state wave functions for $^{8}$Be, $^{9}$Be, and $^{10}$Be. They then applied a shifted AMD method (sAMD) to incorporate 1p-1h excitations by shifting Gaussian centroids of single-nucleon wave functions, and combined this with the generator coordinate method (GCM) for $\alpha$-cluster configurations to describe large-amplitude cluster motion. The effective nuclear interaction consisted of the MV1 central force, the G3RS spin-orbit force, and the Coulomb force.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy E1 resonances below 20 MeV exhaust about 20% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule and about 10% of the calculated energy-weighted sum.
- The calculated root mean square radii of point-proton distribution are 2.73 fm for $^{8}$Be, 2.69 fm for $^{9}$Be, and 2.43 fm for $^{10}$Be (experimental values are 2.39 fm for $^{9}$Be and 2.22 fm for $^{10}$Be).
- The calculated magnetic moment of $^{9}$Be is $\mu = -1.06\ \mu_N$ (experimental: $-1.1778(9)\ \mu_N$) and the electric quadrupole moment is $Q = 6.9\ e\ \text{fm}^2$ (experimental: $5.288(38)\ e\ \text{fm}^2$).
- The calculated energy-weighted sum of the E1 strength is enhanced from the TRK sum rule value by a factor of 1.7 to 1.8.
- The dipole resonance in $^{10}$Be appears at $E \sim 15$ MeV.
- The GDR in $^{8}$Be shows a two-peak structure in the energy range 20-40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of monopole excitations in $^{16}$O (Kanada-En'yo, 2013) to dipole excitations in neutron-rich Be isotopes, and addresses limitations of both cluster models (which cannot describe high-energy GDR strength) and mean-field RPA approaches (which neglect large-amplitude motion and parity-asymmetric structures). The calculations reproduce the low-energy E1 strength around 10 MeV observed in photodisintegration measurements by Utsunomiya et al. (2015) and Goryachev et al. (1992), as well as the GDR data from Ahrens et al. (1975). The method enables simultaneous description of pigmy dipole resonances and giant dipole resonances in light nuclei with cluster structures, and can be applied to investigate the role of valence neutrons and $\alpha$-cluster coupling in dipole excitations of other neutron-rich nuclei.
