# Possible explanation of the irregular energy dependence of the rapidity width of $ϕ$ mesons observed in Pb+Pb collisions

> Experimental data from the NA49 collaboration show an unexpectedly steep rise of the rapidity width of the $ϕ$ meson as function of beam energy, which was suggested as possible interesting signal for novel physics. In this work we show that the Ultra-relativistic Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (UrQMD) model is able to reproduce the shapes of the rapidity distributions of most measured hadrons and predicts a common linear increase of the width for all hadrons. Only when following the exact same analysis technique and experimental acceptance of the NA49 and NA61/SHINE collaborations, we find that the extracted value of the rapidity width of the $ϕ$ increases drastically for the highest beam energy. We conclude that the observed steep increase of the $ϕ$ rapidity width is a problem of limited detector acceptance and the simplified Gaussian fit approximation.

## Key claim

The steep increase of the rapidity width of the phi meson observed by NA49 in Pb+Pb collisions at the highest beam energy is not a signal of novel physics but an artifact of limited detector acceptance combined with the simplified Gaussian fit approximation used to extract the width.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate 0-5% central Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies from 20 AGeV to 158 AGeV. They calculated rapidity distributions of reconstructable phi mesons (where both decay kaons escape without further interactions) and compared two extraction methods: a direct calculation of the width from the full simulated data set, and the experimental technique of fitting single and double Gaussians within the limited rapidity acceptance (0 ≤ y ≤ 1.5) used by NA49 and NA61/SHINE.

## Key numbers

- The UrQMD model reproduces the rapidity distributions of pi, K+, K-, and anti-Lambda+anti-Sigma0 in Pb+Pb collisions from Elab=20 AGeV to Elab=158 AGeV, showing a linear increase of the rapidity width with beam rapidity for all these hadrons.
- For phi mesons at Elab=158 AGeV (sqrt(sNN)=17.3 GeV), the reduced chi-squared of the UrQMD rapidity distribution compared to NA49 data is chi2/d.o.f. = 0.16, and a single Gaussian fit to the data gives chi2/d.o.f. = 0.10.
- The UrQMD simulation of phi mesons was scaled by a factor of 2.1 to match the experimental yield, but this scaling does not affect the shape or width of the rapidity distribution.
- The rapidity width extracted using the experimental technique (sigma_Exp.) shows a strong dependence on the fit range; smaller acceptance windows artificially increase the extracted width, while the true width from direct calculation follows the same linear trend as other hadrons.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of rapidity widths reported by the NA49 collaboration (NA49, 2008) and the NA61/SHINE collaboration (NA61/SHINE, 2019), who observed that phi mesons in Pb+Pb collisions deviated from the linear trend seen for pions, kaons, and anti-lambdas. The authors show that the UrQMD transport model, which successfully describes the other hadrons, predicts a common linear increase for all hadrons including the phi when the full rapidity acceptance is used. The result disfavors interpretations of novel physics at sqrt(sNN) ≈ 20 GeV and advises caution in fitting Gaussian functions to data with limited acceptance.
