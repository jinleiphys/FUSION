# A closer look at the indications of q-generalized Central Limit Theorem behavior in quasi-stationary states of the HMF model

> We give a closer look at the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) behavior in quasi-stationary states of the Hamiltonian Mean Field model, a paradigmatic one for long-range-interacting classical many-body systems. We present new calculations which show that, following their time evolution, we can observe and classify three kinds of long-standing quasi-stationary states (QSS) with different correlations. The frequency of occurrence of each class depends on the size of the system. The different microsocopic nature of the QSS leads to different dynamical correlations and therefore to different results for the observed CLT behavior.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, three distinct classes of quasi-stationary states (QSS) can arise from the same water-bag initial condition with unitary magnetization, and the observed Central Limit Theorem (CLT) behavior, whether standard Gaussian or q-Gaussian, depends on which class of QSS is realized, with the frequency of each class varying with system size.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of the HMF model for N=20000 rotors at energy density U=0.69, starting from water-bag initial conditions with magnetization M0=1. They classified single-event temperature time evolutions into three classes of QSS, then constructed probability density functions (Pdfs) of the quantity y_i = (1/√n) Σ_{k=1}^n p_i(k), where p_i(k) are rotational velocities sampled at fixed intervals δ along single-rotor trajectories, and compared the resulting Pdfs to Gaussian and q-Gaussian fits.

## Key numbers

- System size N=20000, energy per particle U=0.69, initial magnetization M0=1
- Critical temperature T_c=0.5, critical energy per particle U_c=0.75
- For class 1 events, the Pdf of y/σ is well fitted by a q-Gaussian with parameters A=0.66, q=1.5, β=1.8
- Sampling parameters used: δ=100 and n=2000, giving total simulation time of 200000 steps after a transient of 200 steps
- Relative frequency of the three event classes was studied using m=20 realizations for each system size N, with uncertainty ±Δ=1/√m

## Context

This work extends previous studies of CLT behavior in the HMF model by Pluchino, Rapisarda, and Tsallis (2007) and by Tsallis, Rapisarda, Pluchino, and Borges (2007), which had reported indications of q-generalized CLT behavior in long-range Hamiltonian systems. The paper clarifies that those earlier results are not universal, particularly for large system sizes, because the mixture of QSS classes, including events that produce Gaussian Pdfs (similar to homogeneous QSS described by Lynden-Bell or Vlasov approaches from Antoniazzi, Fanelli, Barre, Chavanis, Dauxois, and Ruffo (2007)), affects the ensemble-averaged Pdf. The findings enable future investigation of whether class 1 events disappear in the thermodynamic limit and whether class 2 events exhibit a crossover between Gaussian and q-Gaussian behavior for larger systems.
