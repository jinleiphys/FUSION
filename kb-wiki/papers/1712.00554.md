# Meson properties in magnetized quark matter

> We study neutral and charged meson properties in magnetic field. Taking bosolization method in a two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, we derive effective meson Lagrangian density with minimal coupling to the magnetic field, by employing derivative expansion for both the meson fields and Schwinger phases. We extract from the effective Lagrangian density the meson curvature, pole and screening masses. As the only Goldstone mode, the neutral pion controls the thermodynamics of the system and propagates the lang range quark interaction. The magnetic field breaks down the space symmetry, and the quark interaction region changes from a sphere in vacuum to a ellipsoid in magnetic field.

# Meson properties in magnetized quark matter

## Key claim

The neutral pion remains the only Goldstone mode in a magnetized two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, controlling the thermodynamics and long-range quark interaction, while charged pions become significantly heavier due to the lowest Landau level contribution; however, the magnetic field breaks the original SU_L(2)×SU_R(2) chiral symmetry down to U_L(1)×U_R(1), reducing the number of Goldstone modes from three to one.

## Method

The authors employ the bosonization method in a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with a constant magnetic field along the z-axis, deriving an effective meson Lagrangian density by performing a derivative expansion of both the meson fields and the Schwinger phases in the quark propagators. From the kinetic part of the effective Lagrangian, they extract the meson curvature masses and wave function renormalization constants, which together determine the pole and screening masses for neutral mesons (sigma and neutral pion) and charged pions. The calculation uses a Pauli-Villars regularization with N=8 to handle divergences, with parameters fitted to vacuum pion mass, decay constant, and chiral condensate.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV, fitted as input parameter
- Vacuum sigma mass: m_sigma = 440 MeV at zero magnetic field
- Quark condensate in vacuum: <psi-bar psi> = (-246 MeV)^3, used as fitting target
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV, used as fitting target
- Model parameters: cutoff Lambda = 1.505 GeV, coupling G = 3.44 (GeV)^{-2}, current quark mass m_0 = 5.3 MeV
- Critical temperature at B=0: T_c = 165 MeV for chiral phase transition
- Magnetic field strength considered: up to eB/m_pi^2 = 20 (corresponding to approximately 10^19 Gauss)
- Summation over Landau levels performed up to n=2000 for eB = 2 m_pi^2 to ensure convergence

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties in magnetic fields using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, addressing the Schwinger phase problem for charged mesons that earlier treatments (Liu 2016, Zhang 2016) had simplified by neglecting the phase. The approach builds on the bosonization method of Eguchi (1976), Klevansky (1992), and Imai (2012), and the derivative expansion technique of Gusynin (1995) and Fayazbakhsh (2012). The results enable systematic calculation of meson pole and screening masses in magnetized quark matter, relevant for understanding the phase structure of QCD in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions where magnetic fields reach eB ~ 10 m_pi^2 (Kharzeev 2007, Skokov 2009, Voronyuk 2011). The finding that the neutral pion screening mass determines an ellipsoidal interaction region provides a concrete prediction for how magnetic fields modify quark-quark correlations.
