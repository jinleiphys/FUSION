# Transport model analysis of particle correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions at femtometer scales

> The pion source as seen through HBT correlations at RHIC energies is investigated within the UrQMD approach. We find that the calculated transverse momentum, centrality, and system size dependence of the Pratt-HBT radii $R_L$ and $R_S$ are reasonably well in line with experimental data. The predicted $R_O$ values in central heavy ion collisions are larger as compared to experimental data. The corresponding quantity $\sqrt{R_O^{2}-R_S^{2}}$ of the pion emission source is somewhat larger than experimental estimates.

# Transport model analysis of particle correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions at femtometer scales

## Key claim
The UrQMD transport model reproduces the transverse momentum, centrality, and system size dependence of the Pratt-HBT radii $R_L$ and $R_S$ reasonably well, but the calculated $R_O$ values in central heavy ion collisions are about 25% too large compared to experimental data, and the quantity $\sqrt{R_O^{2}-R_S^{2}}$ is about twice as large as measured for the most central collisions.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v2.2) transport model in cascade mode (without Coulomb or other potential interactions) to simulate heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies, then analyzed two-pion correlations using the CRAB v3.0beta program. The three-dimensional correlation functions were fit with a standard Gaussian form in Pratt's longitudinal co-moving system (Out-Side-Long) convention to extract the Pratt radii $R_L$, $R_O$, and $R_S$ for negatively charged pions at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers
- $R_O$ values for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies are about 25% larger than experimental data
- The quantity $\sqrt{R_O^{2}-R_S^{2}}$ for the most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV is about twice as large as measured by experiments
- Calculated $R_O$ values deviate from data for central reactions by approximately 20%, while they agree at midcentral and peripheral collisions
- The Pratt-radii ratios between Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV approach approximately 1.4, equal to the ratio between the initial radii of the nuclei
- The freeze-out volume $V_f = (2\pi)^{3/2}R_L R_S^2$ shows a linear increase with number of participants $N_{part}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

## Context
This work extends previous HBT (Hanbury-Brown and Twiss) interferometry studies at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, addressing the so-called "HBT-puzzle" where model calculations incorporating a phase transition to a Quark Gluon Plasma over-predict observed source sizes. The authors compare their UrQMD results with experimental data from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations (Adams 2004, Adler 2001, Adcox 2002, Back 2004) and discuss similar findings from other transport models including RQMD (Lisa 2000, 2005), the Hadronic Rescattering Model (Humanic 2005), and AMPT (Lin 2002). The study enables further investigation of whether the HBT-puzzle originates from incorrect modeling of elementary p+p dynamics rather than from QGP effects, and suggests that space-momentum correlations from jet-like structures in p+p and flow in AA drive the $k_T$ dependence of Pratt radii.
