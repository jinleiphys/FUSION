# Dissipative quantum dynamics in low-energy collisions of complex nuclei

> Model calculations that include the effects of irreversible, environmental couplings on top of a coupled-channels dynamical description of the collision of two complex nuclei are presented. The Liouville-von Neumann equation for the time-evolution of the density matrix of a dissipative system is solved numerically providing a consistent transition from coherent to decoherent (and dissipative) dynamics during the collision. Quantum decoherence and dissipation are clearly manifested in the model calculations. Energy dissipation, due to the irreversible decay of giant-dipole vibrational states of the colliding nuclei, is shown to result in a hindrance of quantum tunneling and fusion.

# Dissipative quantum dynamics in low-energy collisions of complex nuclei

## Key claim
Environmental couplings, specifically the irreversible decay of giant-dipole vibrational states, can cause decoherence and energy dissipation that hinders quantum tunneling and fusion in low-energy nuclear collisions, even at sub-barrier energies where such effects are typically neglected. The model shows that this dissipation occurs before the nuclei encounter the fusion barrier, unlike the short-range absorption assumed in standard coupled-channels treatments.

## Method
The authors solve the Liouville-von Neumann equation for the time-evolution of a reduced density matrix representing a coupled-channels system (ground state plus giant-dipole resonance excitation) coupled to two distinct environments: a short-range capture state inside the fusion barrier and a long-range bath representing the decay of the giant-dipole resonance into complex surrounding states. The Lindblad dissipative Liouvillian is used to model irreversible Markovian couplings, with the density matrix propagated numerically using a Faber polynomial expansion of the time-evolution superoperator and the Fourier method for kinetic energy commutators.

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channels framework (as implemented in the code ccfull by Hagino and collaborators) by incorporating open quantum system dynamics, addressing the known inability of standard coupled-channels calculations to simultaneously describe elastic scattering and fusion measurements (Newton, Angeli) and the failure to consistently describe below-barrier tunneling and above-barrier fusion yields (Nanda). The approach builds on earlier Lindblad-type treatments of nuclear dissipation for single-channel problems (Scheid, Stefanescu, Adamian, Genkin) and on the suggestion by Broglia that giant resonances act as doorway-states to irreversible energy loss in deep-inelastic reactions. The model enables future inclusion of non-zero orbital angular momenta, additional intrinsic channels, and multi-nucleon transfer processes to confront experimental measurements.
