# Fission barriers in actinides in covariant density functional theory: the role of triaxiality

> Relativistic mean field theory allowing for triaxial deformations is applied for a systematic study of fission barriers in the actinide region. Different pairing schemes are studied in details and it is shown that covariant density functional theory is able to describe fission barriers on a level of accuracy comparable with non-relativistic calculations, even with the best phenomenological macroscopic+microscopic approaches. Triaxiality in the region of the first saddle plays a crucial role in achieving that.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with triaxial deformations can describe inner fission barriers in actinides with accuracy comparable to non-relativistic calculations and macroscopic+microscopic approaches, but only when triaxiality in the region of the first saddle is included; the lowering of the barrier due to triaxiality ranges from 1 to 4 MeV depending on the nucleus.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial relativistic mean field (RMF) plus BCS calculations using the NL3* parameterization of the RMF Lagrangian, with monopole pairing forces and a cutoff energy of 120 MeV in the pairing channel. Constrained calculations on the axial and triaxial mass quadrupole moments were used to map potential energy surfaces in the beta-gamma plane for even-even actinide nuclei with Z=90-100 and N-Z=42-66. The inner fission barrier height was determined as the maximum energy along the lowest-energy fission path connecting the normal deformed minimum to the superdeformed minimum.

## Key numbers

- The lowering of inner fission barrier heights due to triaxiality ranges from 1 to 4 MeV compared with axially symmetric solutions, depending on proton and neutron numbers.
- The average gamma deformation of the triaxial fission path is close to 10 degrees.
- For the basis truncation with N_F=20 and N_B=20, binding energies are described with an accuracy of approximately 200 keV and inner fission barriers with approximately 100 keV compared with the exact solution.
- The empirical pairing gap parameters used are Delta_n_emp = 4.8 / N^(1/3) MeV and Delta_p_emp = 4.8 / Z^(1/3) MeV.
- For the nucleus 240Pu, the normal deformed minimum is at beta approximately 0.28, the axial saddle at beta approximately 0.52, and the superdeformed minimum at beta approximately 0.96.

## Context

This work extends earlier axially symmetric RMF+BCS and RHB calculations of fission barriers (KALR 2010) by systematically including triaxial degrees of freedom, which had previously been studied only in specific nuclei such as 264Hs (BRRMG 1998) and 240Pu (BHR 2003, LNV 2010) within CDFT. The results are compared with non-relativistic Skyrme DFT calculations (SBDN 2009) and macroscopic+microscopic approaches (MSI 2004, SK 2006, DPB 2007, MSI 2009, DNPB 2009), showing that CDFT achieves similar accuracy. The findings enable reliable predictions of fission barriers for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to the r-process of stellar nucleosynthesis and for superheavy element synthesis, where experimental data are scarce.
