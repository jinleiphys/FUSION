# Tracing the Trace Anomaly of Dense Matter inside Neutron Stars

> The trace anomaly $Δ$ is an important quantity that measures the broken conformal symmetry in neutron star matter. In this work, we present quasi-universal relations that connect the stellar profile of $Δ$ to the compactness, moment of inertia, and tidal deformability of neutron stars. We apply the quasi-universal relations to determine the trace anomaly profiles for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 based on their mass-radius measurements. We also analyze PSR J0737-3039A according to its moment of inertia inferred from Bayesian modeling of nuclear equation of state. A recent multimessenger constraint on the tidal deformability is also studied, resulting in an estimate value of the trace anomaly $Δ_c = 0.1770^{+0.0365}_{-0.0432}$ at the center of a $1.4M_\odot$ canonical neutron star. It is expected that more precise observations from both electromagnetic and gravitational-wave channels in the future will provide tighter constraints on the behavior of $Δ$ inside neutron stars.

# Tracing the Trace Anomaly of Dense Matter inside Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The authors present quasi-universal relations connecting the radial profile of the normalized QCD trace anomaly Δ inside neutron stars to the compactness, dimensionless moment of inertia, and dimensionless tidal deformability, with the caveat that the relations reproduce the pressure-to-energy-density ratio X = P/ρ to within about 10% for any given one of these global observables, and that the central trace anomaly Δ_c can become negative for some EOS models near the maximum mass limit, contradicting an earlier conjecture that Δ ≥ 0 in neutron star interiors.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for 45 cold neutron star EOS models from the CompOSE database, selected to be consistent with NICER mass-radius observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and compute the moment of inertia and tidal deformability for each stellar configuration. They fit the resulting three-dimensional correlation spaces (C, z, X(z)), (ln Ī, z, X(z)), and (ln Λ, z, X(z)) to an eighth-order bivariate polynomial in u = 1 - z and the global observable, where z = r/R is the normalized radial coordinate, enforcing the surface boundary condition X(u=0)=0. Validation tests are performed using five additional EOS models including two phenomenological parametrizations with non-monotonic speed of sound.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square errors of the fitting surfaces: σ = 7.62 × 10⁻³ for the (C, z, X) correlation, 6.02 × 10⁻³ for the (ln Ī, z, X) correlation, and 6.38 × 10⁻³ for the (ln Λ, z, X) correlation
- Central trace anomaly for PSR J0030+0451: Δ_c = 0.2126⁺⁰·⁰³⁷⁰₋₀.₀₆₃₀, based on compactness C = 0.156⁺⁰·⁰³⁵₋₀.₀₃₀ from mass M = 1.34⁺⁰·¹⁵₋₀.₁₆ M⊙ and radius R = 12.71⁺¹·¹⁴₋₁.₁₉ km (Riley 2019)
- Central trace anomaly for PSR J0740+6620: Δ_c = 0.0653⁺⁰·⁰⁸²⁸₋₀.₁₃₆₈, based on compactness C = 0.247⁺⁰·⁰³⁰₋₀.₀₃₁ from mass M = 2.072⁺⁰·⁰⁶⁷₋₀.₀₆₆ M⊙ and radius R = 12.39⁺¹·³⁰₋₀.₉₈ km (Riley 2021)
- Central trace anomaly for PSR J0737-3039A: Δ_c = 0.1993⁺⁰·⁰²³⁸₋₀.₀₅₈₄, based on dimensionless moment of inertia Ī = 13.137 (most probable) from the range 10.046 < Ī < 14.586 at 95% credibility (PhysRevC.100.035802)
- Δ_c passes through zero in the compactness range C ∼ 0.26–0.28 and can reach Δ_c ≈ −0.2 near the maximum mass limits for some EOS models
- Relative difference ΔX_c/X_c between numerical data and fitting curves is within 10% for a large range of compactness

## Context

This work extends the concept of the normalized QCD trace anomaly Δ, proposed by Fujimoto et al. (2022) as a measure of conformal symmetry breaking in dense matter, by establishing practical quasi-universal relations that connect Δ profiles to observable neutron star quantities. The results build on the I-Love-Q universal relations of Yagi and Yunes (2013) and the I-C relations of Jiang and Yagi (2020), extending the universality concept to the internal thermodynamic profile. The finding that Δ_c can become negative for high-compactness stars supports recent studies by Brandes et al. (2025) and Fukushima et al. (2025) that challenge the conjecture Δ ≥ 0 proposed by Fujimoto et al. (2022). The relations enable reconstruction of the trace anomaly profile from current NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, from moment of inertia estimates for PSR J0737-3039A, and from tidal deformability constraints such as those from GW170817, with future electromagnetic and gravitational-wave observations expected to provide tighter constraints.
