# Effective Field Theory for jet substructure in heavy ion collisions

> I develop an Effective Field Theory (EFT) framework to compute jet substructure observables for heavy ion collision experiments. As an illustration, I consider dijet events that accompany the formation of a weakly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma(QGP) medium in a heavy ion collision and look at an observable insensitive to jet selection bias: the simultaneous measurement of jet mass along with the transverse momentum imbalance between the jets that are groomed to remove soft radiation. Treating the jet as an open quantum system, I write down a factorization formula within the SCET(Soft Collinear Effective Theory) framework in the forward scattering regime. The physics of the medium is encoded in a universal soft field correlator while the jet-medium interaction is captured by a medium induced jet function. The factorization formula leads to a Lindblad type equation for the evolution of the reduced density matrix of the jet in the Markovian approximation. The solution for this equation allows a resummation of large logarithms that arise due to the final state measurements imposed while simultaneously summing over multiple incoherent interactions of the jet with the medium.

## Key claim

The paper develops an Effective Field Theory (EFT) framework for computing jet substructure observables in heavy ion collisions, specifically proposing that the simultaneous measurement of groomed jet mass and transverse momentum imbalance between dijets is insensitive to jet selection bias, thereby enabling an apples-to-apples comparison of jet evolution in proton-proton versus heavy ion collisions for the same hard events.

## Method

The author treats the jet as an open quantum system interacting with a weakly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) medium, using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) in the forward scattering regime. The factorization formula separates the physics of the medium into a universal soft field correlator and a medium-induced jet function, with the jet-medium interaction mediated by t-channel exchange of Glauber gluons. The evolution of the reduced density matrix of the jet is derived in the Markovian approximation, leading to a Lindblad-type equation that allows resummation of large logarithms arising from final state measurements while summing over multiple incoherent jet-medium interactions.

## Key numbers

- The temperature of the QGP medium is stated to lie in the range 150–500 MeV for current heavy ion collision experiments.
- The expansion parameter of the EFT is defined as λ = q_T/Q ∼ √e ∼ T/Q << 1, where q_T is the transverse momentum imbalance, e is the cumulative jet mass, T is the plasma temperature, and Q ∼ 2E_J is the hard scale.
- The Debye screening mass is given as m_D ∼ g T, with the weakly coupled regime requiring m_D to be much smaller than T.
- The QGP medium is stated to exist for approximately 10 fm/c.
- The jet radius R is taken to be ∼ 1 for the anti-kT jet algorithm.
- The energy cut-off for grooming is set at z_c E_J, with the hierarchy Q ∼ z_c Q >> T.

## Context

This work extends the SCET framework for jet substructure from proton-proton collisions (e.g., the work of Larkoski et al. (2014) on soft-drop grooming and Gutierrez-Reyes et al. (2019) on dijet observables) to heavy ion collisions by incorporating medium effects. It contrasts with previous approaches such as SCET_G (Ovanesyan et al. (2011), Chien et al. (2015)) which treated the medium as a background gauge field, and with hybrid AdS/CFT models (Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2014)) that map strong-coupling computations to weak-coupling gravity. The formalism enables rigorous operator definitions for QGP medium structure functions analogous to Parton Distribution Functions in Deep Inelastic Scattering, and opens the door to precision jet substructure studies for both light parton and heavy quark jets (e.g., bottom quark jets to be studied at LHCb and sPHENIX).
