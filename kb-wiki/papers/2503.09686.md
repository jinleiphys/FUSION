# Dominance of gluonic scale anomaly in confining pressure inside nucleon and D-term

> We explore the confining pressure inside the nucleon and the related gravitational form factor referred to as the D-term, using the skyrmion approach based on the scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory, where the skyrmion is described as the nucleon and a scalar meson couples to the scale anomaly through the low energy theorem. Within this model framework, the current quark mass and gluonic quantum contributions to the scale anomaly can be described by the pion and scalar meson masses, respectively, through matching with the underlying QCD. By considering the decomposition of the energy momentum tensor of nucleon, we examine the role of the scale anomaly contributions in the pressure inside the nucleon. As a result, the gluonic scale anomaly is found to dominate the confining pressure. Compared to the result based on the conventional chiral perturbation theory in the chiral limit, our result for the total pressure is capable of qualitatively improving the alignment with lattice QCD observations. Moreover, the pressure from the gluonic scale anomaly is widely distributed in position space, leading to its substantial contribution to the D-term.

## Key claim

The gluonic scale anomaly (the quantum correction from gluons to the trace of the energy-momentum tensor) dominates the confining pressure inside the nucleon and the D-term, while the current-quark-mass contribution to the scale anomaly is negligible; this conclusion is drawn within the skyrmion approach based on scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory (sChPT), where the scalar meson mass in the chiral limit mimics the gluonic part of the scale anomaly.

## Method

The authors construct a skyrmion model of the nucleon using scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory (sChPT), which incorporates a scalar meson (dilaton) that couples to the dilatation current and satisfies the partially conserved dilatation current (PCDC) relation. They numerically solve the coupled differential equations for the skyrmion profile function \(F(r)\) and the scalar-meson profile function \(C(r)\) under hedgehog ansatz, then compute the static energy-momentum tensor (EMT) decomposed into a traceless part (dynamical energy) and a trace part (scale anomaly), further separating the trace part into a quark-mass-induced piece (pion mass) and a gluonic piece (scalar meson mass in the chiral limit). The pressure \(p(r)\) and the D-term are extracted from the spatial distributions of the EMT components.

## Key numbers

* Nucleon mass from the skyrmion in sChPT: \(M_N = 927.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The scale anomaly contributes \(25\%\) of the nucleon mass analytically; in the model, the gluonic scale anomaly accounts for \(24.2\%\) of the nucleon mass.
* The D-term predicted by the model: \(D = -4.12\).
* Tensor mean square radius: \(\langle r^2 \rangle_t = (0.30\ \mathrm{fm})^2\).
* Scalar mean square radius from the gluonic scale anomaly: \(\langle r^2 \rangle_s^g = (0.52\ \mathrm{fm})^2\).
* Scalar mean square radius from the quark-mass scale anomaly: \(\langle r^2 \rangle_s^q = (0.097\ \mathrm{fm})^2\).
* Model parameters: \(f_\pi = 68\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(e = 5.45\), \(m_\pi = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(f_\phi = 240\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_{\phi 0} = 720\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\gamma_m = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion approach of Adkins (1983) and the scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory framework of Lanik (1984) and Ellis (1984) to study the mechanical properties of the nucleon. It directly addresses the role of the scale anomaly, which was not captured in conventional chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculations of the pressure by Cebulla (2007) and Garcia Martin-Caro (2023). The results improve agreement with lattice QCD observations of the pressure by Shanahan (2018) and provide a prediction for the D-term consistent with the lattice results of Hackett (2023). The work enables future studies of the scale anomaly decomposition in other hadrons and motivates extensions to include vector mesons via hidden local symmetry.
