# Low Energy Proton-Deuteron Scattering in Configuration Space

> A new method for solving the configuration-space Faddeev equations for elastic p-d scattering below the deuteron-breakup threshold is described. Numerical solutions that demonstrate the convergence and accuracy of the method are given. The number of channels and the value of the matching radius required to obtain an accurate solution are also investigated. These calculations demonstrate that this method can efficiently solve the large matrix equations required for the three-body scattering problem.

# Low Energy Proton-Deuteron Scattering in Configuration Space

## Key claim

The authors present a new iterative method for solving the configuration-space Faddeev equations for elastic p-d scattering below the deuteron-breakup threshold, demonstrating that with a maximum pair angular momentum of j_max=10 and a matching radius of ρ_max=90 fm, the method yields accurate K-matrix solutions. The method is claimed to be efficient for the large matrix equations required, though the paper only demonstrates results for laboratory energies up to 3.0 MeV and for specific total angular momentum and parity states.

## Method

The authors solve the configuration-space Faddeev equations for elastic p-d scattering using Jacobi coordinates and a partial-wave expansion in the j-J coupling scheme, with the total wave function decomposed into Faddeev amplitudes. The coupled partial differential equations are transformed to hyperspherical coordinates (ρ,θ) and solved using Hermite spline expansions with orthogonal collocation, where the matrix equation is split into a part (A) that can be inverted directly and a part (B) with larger bandwidth that requires an iterative Gram-Schmidt procedure. The K-matrix is extracted from the asymptotic form of the scattered wave using regular and irregular Coulomb wave functions, with the deuteron bound-state wave function factored out of the open channels.

## Key numbers

- For J=1/2⁺ p-d scattering at E_lab=1.0 MeV with j_max=10 and ρ_max=90 fm: K₁₁ = -2.3076×10⁻¹, K₁₂ = -3.9645×10⁻³, K₂₂ = -1.3722×10⁻² (converged to 4-5 significant figures after 14-16 iterations)
- For J=1/2⁺ p-d scattering at E_lab=3.0 MeV with j_max=10 and ρ_max=90 fm: K₁₁ = -6.2250×10⁻¹, K₁₂ = -1.0887×10⁻², K₂₂ = -6.2874×10⁻²
- For J=1/2⁻ p-d scattering at E_lab=1.0 MeV with j_max=10 and ρ_max=90 fm: K₁₁ = 1.4445×10⁻¹, K₁₂ = -6.0499×10⁻², K₂₂ = -4.0592×10⁻²
- For J=5/2⁺ p-d scattering at E_lab=2.0 MeV with j_max=10 and ρ_max=90 fm: K₁₁ = -8.45541×10⁻³, K₂₂ = -1.37725×10⁻², K₃₃ = -1.40337×10⁻³
- The number of channel states N_c required for j_max=10 is 82 for J=1/2⁺ and J=1/2⁻, and 226 for J=5/2⁺
- The iterative method typically requires 10-20 iterations to obtain an accurate solution
- The nucleon-nucleon potential used is the complete AV18 potential with ħ²/M = 41.47108 MeV·fm²

## Context

This work extends the configuration-space Faddeev approach previously used by the authors for scattering length calculations (Chen, 1999) to full elastic p-d scattering below breakup, providing an alternative to the Pair-correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) method of the Pisa group (Kievsky, 1997). The method enables detailed comparisons of three-nucleon scattering calculations with experimental data using realistic two-body potentials like AV18, which is important for studying the three-nucleon force since bound-state calculations (Triton, 1995) show that two-body interactions alone underbind the triton and ³He. The authors note that while most scattering data can be reproduced with two-body interactions alone (Report, 1996), discrepancies in analyzing powers like A_y and iT₁₁ may be sensitive to three-nucleon forces, and this method can be extended to higher energies using boundary conditions from a separate reference (Chapter, 1999).
