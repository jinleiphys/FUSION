# Particle-unstable nuclei in the Hartree-Fock theory

> Ground state energies and decay widths of particle unstable nuclei are calculated within the Hartree-Fock approximation by performing a complex scaling of the many-body Hamiltonian. Through this transformation, the wave functions of the resonant states become square integrable. The method is implemented with Skyrme effective interactions. Several Skyrme parametrizations are tested on four unstable nuclei: 10He, 12O, 26O and 28O.

# Particle-unstable nuclei in the Hartree-Fock theory

## Key claim

The complex scaling method can be combined with the Hartree-Fock approximation to calculate both ground state energies and decay widths of particle-unstable nuclei, but the resulting widths are not in agreement with experimental data, partly because the effective nuclear interactions were not constructed for this domain.

## Method

The authors apply a complex scaling transformation to the many-body Hamiltonian, which rotates the continuous spectrum into the complex energy plane and makes resonant state wave functions square integrable. A bi-variational principle is used to derive complex scaled Hartree-Fock (CSHF) equations for spherical, even-even doubly closed shell nuclei with Skyrme effective interactions. The eigenvalue problem of the complex scaled Fock operator is solved by direct numerical integration with an iterative technique, using initial orbits from a complex scaled radial Schrodinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The numerical integration accuracy gives an imaginary part less than 10^-4 MeV for bound isotopes, setting a lower limit on detectable decay widths.
- For 10He, the SIII interaction gives a decay width of 0.4 MeV, in good agreement with the experimental value of 0.3 MeV.
- For 10He, the Skm1 interaction gives a decay width of 3.8 MeV, overestimating the experimental value by an order of magnitude.
- For 12O, the Skm1 interaction gives a decay width less than 10^-3 MeV, while the experimental value is 0.578 MeV.
- For 26O and 28O, the Skm1 interaction gives decay widths of 0.4 MeV and 0.5 MeV respectively, with no experimental values available for comparison.
- The binding energy of 10He from Skm1 is 25.0 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 30.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method, previously applied in nuclear physics to cluster models (Kruppa and Kato, 1990; Csoto, 1994) and in atomic and molecular physics (Bently, 1990), to the Hartree-Fock framework with Skyrme interactions. It addresses the problem of describing nuclei beyond the drip lines where the last particle orbitals are unbound and have a width, a complication not handled by conventional HF methods or by the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach on a mesh (Dobaczewski, Flocard and Treiner, 1984; Nazarewicz, 1996). The results reveal weaknesses in the Skm1 interaction not apparent on stable nuclei, and the authors plan to generalize the method by including pairing correlations.
