# Non-collapsing renormalized QRPA with proton-neutron pairing for neutrinoless double beta decay

> Using the renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA), we calculate the light neutrino mass mediated mode of neutrinoless double beta decay of Ge76, Mo100, Te128 and Te130. Our results indicate that the simple quasiboson approximation is not good enough to study the neutrinoless double beta decay, because its solutions collapse for physical values of g_pp. We find that extension of the Hilbert space and inclusion of the Pauli Principle in the QRPA with proton-neutron pairing, allows us to extend our calculations beyond the point of collapse, for physical values of the nuclear force strength. As a consequence one might be able to extract more accurate values on the effective neutrino mass by using the best available experimental limits on the half-life of neutrinoless double beta decay.

# Non-collapsing renormalized QRPA with proton-neutron pairing for neutrinoless double beta decay

## Key claim

The standard quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) collapses for physical values of the particle-particle interaction strength $g_{pp}$ when a sufficiently large model space is used, but the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) that includes Pauli principle effects and proton-neutron pairing avoids this collapse, yielding more stable nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay.

## Method

The authors applied four variants of the QRPA, pn-QRPA, full-QRPA (with proton-neutron pairing), pn-RQRPA, and full-RQRPA, to calculate the nuclear matrix element $M^{0\nu}_{mass}$ for the light neutrino mass mechanism of neutrinoless double beta decay. They used a nuclear matter G-matrix derived from the Bonn one-boson-exchange potential, with model spaces ranging from 9 to 21 levels (covering 0–5 $\hbar\omega$ major oscillator shells), and solved the HFB equations including proton-neutron pairing. The renormalization factors ${\cal D}_{k\mu l\nu J^\pi}$ were determined self-consistently from coupled non-linear equations to account for ground-state correlations beyond the quasiboson approximation.

## Key numbers

- For $^{76}$Ge, the full-RQRPA yields an upper limit on the effective neutrino mass $<m_\nu> < 1.3$ eV, using the experimental half-life limit $T^{0\nu-exp}_{1/2} > 5.6 \times 10^{24}$ years from the Heidelberg-Moscow Collaboration (1995).
- For $^{100}$Mo, the full-RQRPA gives $<m_\nu> < 2.4$ eV, using $T^{0\nu-exp}_{1/2} > 4.4 \times 10^{22}$ years from Alston-Garnjost et al. (1993).
- For $^{128}$Te, the full-RQRPA gives $<m_\nu> < 1.1$ eV, using $T^{0\nu-exp}_{1/2} > 7.3 \times 10^{24}$ years from Bernatowicz et al. (1992).
- For $^{130}$Te, the full-RQRPA gives $<m_\nu> < 4.5$ eV, using $T^{0\nu-exp}_{1/2} > 2.3 \times 10^{22}$ years from Moe and Vogel (1994).
- The Ikeda Sum Rule $S^- - S^+$ is violated in the full-RQRPA, yielding $0.8-0.9 \times 3(N-Z)$ for $^{76}$Ge and $^{76}$Se in the physically acceptable region of $g_{pp}$.
- The nuclear radius parameter used is $r_0 = 1.1$ fm, and the dipole nucleon form factor cutoff is $\Lambda = 0.85$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the renormalized QRPA approach previously applied to two-neutrino double beta decay by Schwieger, Simkovic and Faessler (1996) to the neutrinoless mode, and incorporates proton-neutron pairing following the HFB framework of Cheoun et al. (1993–1995). It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the strong suppression of matrix elements found by Engel, Vogel and Zirnbauer (1988) using zero-range forces and the weaker suppression found by Tomoda and Faessler (1987) and Muto, Bender and Klapdor (1989) with realistic interactions, attributing the difference partly to model-space size. The results enable more reliable extraction of limits on the effective Majorana neutrino mass from experimental half-life limits, and identify $^{100}$Mo as a promising candidate for further experimental study due to its large energy release and matrix element.
