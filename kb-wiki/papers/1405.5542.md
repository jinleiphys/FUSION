# Role of diquark correlations and the pion cloud in nucleon elastic form factors

> Electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in the space-like region are investigated within the framework of a covariant and confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The bound state amplitude of the nucleon is obtained as the solution of a relativistic Faddeev equation, where diquark correlations appear naturally as a consequence of the strong coupling in the colour $\bar{3}$ $qq$ channel. Pion degrees of freedom are included as a perturbation to the "quark-core" contribution obtained using the Poincaré covariant Faddeev amplitude. While no model parameters are fit to form factor data, excellent agreement is obtained with the empirical nucleon form factors (including the magnetic moments and radii) where pion loop corrections play a critical role for $Q^2 \lesssim 1\,$GeV$^2$. Using charge symmetry, the nucleon form factors can be expressed as proton quark sector form factors. The latter are studied in detail, leading, for example, to the conclusion that the $d$-quark sector of the Dirac form factor is much softer than the $u$-quark sector, a consequence of the dominance of scalar diquark correlations in the proton wave function. On the other hand, for the proton quark sector Pauli form factors we find that the effect of the pion cloud and axialvector diquark correlations overcomes the effect of scalar diquark dominance, leading to a larger $d$-quark anomalous magnetic moment and a form factor in the $u$-quark sector that is slightly softer than in the $d$-quark sector.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a covariant NJL model, with no parameters fitted to form factor data, can reproduce empirical nucleon electromagnetic form factors when pion loop corrections are included as a perturbation to the "quark-core" Faddeev amplitude, with the caveat that pion loop effects are critical only for momentum transfers \(Q^2 \lesssim 1\,\text{GeV}^2\).

## Method

The nucleon bound-state amplitude is obtained by solving a relativistic Faddeev equation in the NJL model, where scalar and axialvector diquark correlations emerge naturally from the strong coupling in the colour \(\bar{3}\) quark-quark channel. Pion degrees of freedom are then included perturbatively as loop corrections to the quark-photon vertex, while the quark-core contribution uses the Poincaré covariant Faddeev amplitude in the static approximation for the quark exchange kernel. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing the physical masses of the pion, rho, omega, nucleon, and Delta baryon, plus the pion decay constant, with no subsequent adjustment to form factor data.

## Key numbers

- Scalar diquark mass: \(M_s = 0.768\,\text{GeV}\); axialvector diquark mass: \(M_a = 0.903\,\text{GeV}\).
- Nucleon vertex function coefficients: \((\alpha_1,\,\alpha_2,\,\alpha_3) = (0.55,\,0.05,\,-0.40)\).
- Infrared cutoff: \(\Lambda_{IR} = 0.240\,\text{GeV}\); ultraviolet cutoff: \(\Lambda_{UV} = 0.645\,\text{GeV}\); dressed quark mass: \(M = 0.4\,\text{GeV}\).
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 140\,\text{MeV}\); pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92\,\text{MeV}\); rho mass: \(m_\rho = 770\,\text{MeV}\); omega mass: \(m_\omega = 782\,\text{MeV}\); nucleon mass: \(M_N = 940\,\text{MeV}\); Delta mass: \(M_\Delta = 1232\,\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies of baryons as quark-diquark bound states (Ishii 1995, Mineo 1999, Mineo 2002) by including pion loop corrections to the quark-photon vertex, which are essential for describing form factors at low \(Q^2\). The results contradict the earlier expectation that the ratio \(\mu_p G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}\) is constant, consistent with polarization transfer experiments (Jones 1999, Gayou 2001, Puckett 2010). The flavour decomposition enables a direct comparison with the quark-sector form factors extracted from experiment (Cates 2011) and provides a framework for interpreting the softness of the \(d\)-quark Dirac form factor relative to the \(u\)-quark sector.
