# Coupled-channels analyses for large-angle quasi-elastic scattering in massive systems

> We discuss in detail the coupled-channels approach for the large-angle quasi-elastic scattering in massive systems, where many degrees of freedom may be involved in the reaction. We especially investigate the effects of single, double and triple phonon excitations on the quasi-elastic scattering for $^{48}$Ti,$^{54}$Cr,$^{56}$Fe,$^{64}$Ni and $^{70}$Zn$+^{208}$Pb systems, for which the experimental cross sections have been measured recently. We show that the present coupled-channels calculations well account for the overall width of the experimental barrier distribution for these systems. In particular, it is shown that the calculations taking into account single quadrupole phonon excitations in $^{48}$Ti and triple octupole phonon excitations in $^{208}$Pb reasonably well reproduce the experimental quasi-elastic cross section and barrier distribution for the $^{48}$Ti$+^{208}$Pb reaction. On the other hand, $^{54}$Cr,$^{56}$Fe,$^{64}$Ni and $^{70}$Zn$+^{208}$Pb systems seem to require the double quadrupole phonon excitations in the projectiles in order to reproduce the experimental data.

## Key claim

The coupled-channels approach with multi-phonon excitations can reproduce experimental quasi-elastic scattering data for massive systems leading to super-heavy elements, but the required number of phonon excitations differs depending on the projectile: single quadrupole phonon excitations in $^{48}$Ti suffice while $^{54}$Cr, $^{56}$Fe, $^{64}$Ni, and $^{70}$Zn projectiles require double quadrupole phonon excitations, with the caveat that this difference may reflect ambiguity in the subtraction of deep-inelastic components from experimental yields.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations using the CCFULL code with the iso-centrifugal approximation, solving the coupled equations for large-angle quasi-elastic scattering at $\theta_{\rm c.m.}=170^\circ$. They included single, double, and triple phonon excitations of quadrupole ($2^+$) states in the projectiles and octupole ($3^-$) states in the $^{208}$Pb target, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential with surface diffuseness $a=0.63$ fm and radius parameter $r_0=1.22$ fm, with the depth adjusted per system to reproduce experimental cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For $^{48}$Ti+$^{208}$Pb, the calculation with single quadrupole phonon in $^{48}$Ti and triple octupole phonon in $^{208}$Pb gave $\chi^2=7.12$ for quasi-elastic cross sections, compared to $\chi^2=19.15$ for the [1,1] coupling scheme (single phonon in both nuclei).
- For $^{54}$Cr+$^{208}$Pb, the [2,3] coupling scheme (double quadrupole in projectile, triple octupole in target) gave $\chi^2=11.78$, versus $\chi^2=52.47$ for the [1,2] scheme.
- For $^{56}$Fe+$^{208}$Pb, the [2,3] scheme gave $\chi^2=10.28$, versus $\chi^2=28.46$ for [1,2].
- For $^{64}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb, the [2,3] scheme gave $\chi^2=30.64$, versus $\chi^2=57.45$ for [1,2].
- For $^{70}$Zn+$^{208}$Pb, the [2,3] scheme gave $\chi^2=6.87$, versus $\chi^2=26.52$ for [1,2].
- The bare Coulomb barrier heights extracted were: $^{48}$Ti+$^{208}$Pb: 190.50 MeV; $^{54}$Cr+$^{208}$Pb: 205.50 MeV; $^{56}$Fe+$^{208}$Pb: 222.50 MeV; $^{64}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb: 236.25 MeV; $^{70}$Zn+$^{208}$Pb: 249.30 MeV.
- The surface diffuseness $a=0.63$ fm was preferred over $a=1.0$ fm; calculations with $a=1.0$ fm could not simultaneously reproduce cross sections below and above the Coulomb barrier for $^{64}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels analysis of quasi-elastic barrier distributions, previously applied to intermediate-mass systems and the $^{86}$Kr+$^{208}$Pb system by Ntshangase et al. (2007), to a systematic study of five massive systems leading to super-heavy elements with atomic numbers Z=104, 106, 108, 110, and 112. The results demonstrate that the coupled-channels approach remains valid for massive systems, consistent with the earlier finding by Rowley et al. (2006) for $^{100}$Mo+$^{100}$Mo, and enable future higher-precision measurements to study the detailed structure of barrier distributions and the role of anharmonicity in multi-phonon excitations.
