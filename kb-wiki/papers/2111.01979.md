# Efficacy of the symmetry-adapted basis for ab initio nucleon-nucleus interactions for light- and intermediate-mass nuclei

> We study the efficacy of a new ab initio framework that combines the symmetry-adapted (SA) no-core shell-model approach with the resonating group method (RGM) for unified descriptions of nuclear structure and reactions. We obtain ab initio neutron-nucleus interactions for $^4$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{20}$Ne targets, starting with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. We discuss the effect of increasing model space sizes and symmetry-based selections on the SA-RGM norm and direct potential kernels, as well as on phase shifts, which are the input to calculations of cross sections. We demonstrate the efficacy of the SA basis and its scalability with particle numbers and model space dimensions, with a view toward ab initio descriptions of nucleon scattering and capture reactions up through the medium-mass region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the symmetry-adapted (SA) basis, when combined with the resonating group method (RGM), can reproduce ab initio nucleon-nucleus interaction kernels and phase shifts obtained using complete model spaces, while using significantly reduced model spaces; this efficacy is shown for light nuclei and extended to intermediate-mass nuclei, with the caveat that the validation focuses on benchmarking against complete model spaces rather than reproducing experimental phase shifts with all RGM kernels.

## Method

The authors combine the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with the resonating group method (RGM) to compute norm and potential kernels for neutron-nucleus scattering. Target wavefunctions for 4He, 16O, and 20Ne are constructed in the SA basis using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (JISP16 for 4He, NNLOopt for 20Ne), and the RGM kernels are evaluated using group-theoretical algorithms that exploit SU(3) symmetry to reduce computational complexity. The kernels are then used to calculate phase shifts via a microscopic R-matrix approach.

## Key numbers

- For 4He + n with JISP16 interaction at ℏΩ = 25 MeV and ηmax = 10: the selected model space Nmax = ⟨6⟩14 yields potential kernels indistinguishable from the complete Nmax = 14 model space for both 2S1/2 and 2P3/2 partial waves.
- For 20Ne + n with NNLOopt interaction at ℏΩ = 15 MeV and ηmax = 10: exchange norm and potential kernels are computed for selected model spaces Nmax = ⟨2⟩4, ⟨2⟩6, and ⟨2⟩8, with phase shift differences between ⟨2⟩4 and ⟨2⟩8 shown as a function of center-of-mass projectile energy.
- The 4He wavefunctions in Nmax = 14 complete and Nmax = ⟨6⟩14 selected spaces have been shown to converge for binding energy and ground-state root-mean-square matter radius.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM framework of Quaglioni and Navratil (2008, 2009) by incorporating the symmetry-adapted basis from the SA-NCSM approach of Launey et al. (2016) and Dytrych et al. (2020). It enables ab initio reaction calculations for deformed and intermediate-mass nuclei such as 20Ne, which were previously inaccessible to complete NCSM/RGM calculations due to prohibitive model space sizes. The approach builds on earlier SU(3)-based RGM work by Hecht (1977, 1978) and Suzuki (1986, 1987), and opens the path toward ab initio descriptions of nucleon scattering and capture reactions up through the medium-mass region.
