# Statistical Fluctuations of Electromagnetic Transition Intensities in pf-Shell Nuclei

> We study the fluctuation properties of E2 and M1 transition intensities among T=0,1 states of A = 60 nuclei in the framework of the interacting shell model, using a realistic effective interaction for pf-shell nuclei with a Ni56 as a core. It is found that the B(E2) distributions are well described by the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble of random matrices (Porter-Thomas distribution) independently of the isobaric quantum number T_z. However, the statistics of the B(M1) transitions is sensitive to T_z: T_z=1 nuclei exhibit a Porter-Thomas distribution, while a significant deviation from the GOE statistics is observed for self-conjugate nuclei (T_z=0).

## Key claim
The distributions of B(E2) transition intensities in A=60 nuclei follow the Porter-Thomas distribution (GOE prediction) regardless of isospin projection T_z, but the B(M1) distributions show a significant deviation from GOE statistics specifically for self-conjugate nuclei (T_z=0), where the M1 transitions are purely isoscalar and relatively weak.

## Method
The authors performed interacting shell-model calculations in the pf shell using a Ni56 core, with the F5P effective interaction, considering configurations in the 0f5/2, 1p3/2, and 1p1/2 orbitals. They computed B(E2) and B(M1) transition intensities for 2+, T → 2+, T transitions (T=0 or T=1) in A=60 nuclei, sampling 3136 intensities for T_z=0 and 4356 intensities for T_z=1, and fitted the resulting distributions to a χ² distribution in ν degrees of freedom to test agreement with the Porter-Thomas distribution (ν=1).

## Key numbers
- 56² = 3136 intensities sampled for T_z=0 nuclei (60Zn) and 66² = 4356 intensities for T_z=1 nuclei (60Co, 60Cu, 60Zn)
- The smoothing parameter γ used in the average strength function was γ=4
- For the deviating M1 distribution in self-conjugate nuclei, a χ² fit gave ν=0.64, indicating a larger number of weak transitions compared to the Porter-Thomas distribution
- The study examined transitions in nuclei with A=60, using the F5P interaction successful for the mass range A=57-68

## Context
This work extends previous statistical fluctuation studies of electromagnetic transitions from lighter sd-shell nuclei (A<40) to the pf-shell region (A~60), following earlier shell-model analyses of B(M1) and B(E2) transitions in 22Na by the same research community. The results contradict the expectation that all electromagnetic transition distributions follow Porter-Thomas statistics, showing isospin sensitivity in M1 transitions that was not observed in lighter nuclei. This enables future investigations into how the isoscalar and isovector components of transition operators contribute differently to statistical fluctuations, and how the chaoticity of nuclear states may depend on spin and isospin quantum numbers.
