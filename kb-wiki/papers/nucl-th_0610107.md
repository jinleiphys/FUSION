# Stress-free BCS pairing in color superconductors is impossible

> Cold, asymptotically dense three-flavor quark matter is in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, in which all quarks pair in a particularly symmetric fashion. At smaller densities, taking into account a nonzero strange quark mass and electric and color neutrality, the CFL phase requires pairing of quarks with mismatched Fermi momenta. We present a classification of all other possible, less symmetric, pairing patterns and prove that none of them can avoid this mismatch. This result suggests unconventional, e.g., spatially inhomogeneous, superconducting phases for moderate densities.

## Key claim

The paper proves that no conventional (spatially homogeneous, zero-momentum Cooper pair) color-superconducting pairing pattern can simultaneously satisfy electric and color neutrality while avoiding the Fermi-surface mismatch that stresses BCS pairing, given a nonzero strange quark mass. This impossibility holds for all 512 possible pairing patterns (reduced to 149 distinct sets of connected components) except unpaired quark matter.

## Method

The authors systematically classified all possible pairing patterns by representing each of the 512 possible 3×3 order parameter matrices (with entries set to zero or nonzero) as a graph whose vertices are the nine quark species and whose edges represent Cooper pairing. They then reduced these to 149 distinct sets of connected components under color rotations. For each set, they derived the common Fermi momenta from the effective chemical potentials (including the strange quark mass shift) and searched numerically for a simultaneous solution of the electric and color neutrality conditions (Eq. 1) in terms of the chemical potentials μ_e, μ_3, and μ_8.

## Key numbers

- The CFL phase becomes stressed when the mismatch parameter m_s^2/μ reaches 2Δ, where Δ is the energy gap.
- The cost in free energy from filling mismatched Fermi surfaces to a common Fermi momentum is of order m_s^4.
- The condensation energy gained from pairing is of order Δ^2 μ^2.
- The breakdown of all conventional pairing patterns occurs at a quark chemical potential μ of order m_s^2/Δ.
- The quark chemical potential in compact star interiors is at most about 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Rajagopal (2005) on neutrality conditions in color superconductors and contradicts the possibility of finding a stress-free BCS phase at moderate densities, as might have been hoped from analogies with the A-phase in spin-1 color superconductors (Schmitt, 2004) or in superfluid 3He. The result motivates the study of unconventional phases such as the gapless CFL phase (which is unstable to counter-propagating currents), crystalline color superconductivity (Rajagopal, 2006), and single-flavor pairing (Schmitt, 2004), whose transport properties could be probed via astrophysical observables like neutron star cooling curves (Schmitt, 2005).
