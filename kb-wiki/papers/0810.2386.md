# Form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics: constraints from space-time translations

> The comparison of form factors calculated from a single-particle current in different relativistic quantum mechanic approaches evidences tremendous discrepancies. The role of constraints coming from space-time translations is considered here with this respect. It is known that invariance under these translations implies the energy-momentum conservation relation that is usually assumed to hold globally. Transformations of the current under these translations, which lead to this result, also imply constraints that have been ignored so far in relativistic quantum mechanic approaches. An implementation of these constraints is discussed in the case of a model with two scalar constituents. It amounts to incorporate selected two-body currents to all orders in the interaction. Discrepancies for form factors in different approaches can thus be removed, contributing to restore the equivalence of different approaches. Results for the standard front-form approach ($q^+=0$) are found to fulfill the constraints and are therefore unchanged. The relation with results from a dispersion-relation approach is also made.

# Form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics: constraints from space-time translations

## Key claim
The authors show that the large discrepancies between form factors calculated in different relativistic quantum mechanic (RQM) approaches using a single-particle current can be removed by enforcing constraints from space-time translation invariance that go beyond the standard energy-momentum conservation relation. These constraints require the introduction of many-body currents to all orders in the interaction, and when implemented, the results from different approaches converge to the same values.

## Method
The authors consider a model of two scalar constituents (a Wick-Cutkosky model) and examine the transformation properties of currents under space-time translations, deriving double-commutator relations that must be satisfied. They implement the constraints by introducing a multiplicative factor alpha that modifies the momentum transfer variable in the current operator, determined by requiring that the squared momentum transferred to the system equals that transferred to the constituents. This procedure effectively incorporates selected two-body currents to all orders in the interaction without explicitly computing them.

## Key numbers
- Mass of the system (pion-like): M = 0.14 GeV
- Mass of the constituents: m = 0.3 GeV
- The standard front-form approach (q^+ = 0) yields alpha = 1, so its form factor results are unchanged by the correction

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Poincaré covariance in RQM approaches, following earlier work by Dirac (1949) on forms of relativistic dynamics and by Keister and Polyzou on RQM implementations. The authors address discrepancies previously documented by Amghar (2002) and Desplanques (2004) between form factors calculated in different RQM approaches (front form, instant form, point form). The results converge to those of a dispersion-relation approach developed by Anisovich (1992), Krutov (2001), and Melikhov (2001), which the authors show can be obtained from the standard front-form results by a change of variables. This work enables reliable form factor calculations where remaining discrepancies with experiment can be attributed to the underlying dynamics rather than the implementation of relativity.
