# Study of the core-crust transition in neutron stars with finite-range interactions: the dynamical method

> The properties of the core-crust transition in neutron stars are investigated using effective nuclear forces of finite-range. Special attention is paid to the so-called dynamical method for locating the transition point, which, apart from the stability of the uniform nuclear matter against clusterization, also considers contributions due to finite-size effects. In particular, contributions to the transition density and pressure from the direct and exchange energies are carefully analyzed. To this end, finite-range forces of Gogny, Modified Gogny Interaction (MDI) and Simple Effective Interaction (SEI) types are used in the numerical applications. The results from the dynamical approach are compared with those from the popular thermodynamical method that neglects the surface and Coulomb effects in the stability condition. The dependence of the core-crust transition on the stiffness of the symmetry energy of the finite-range models is also addressed. Finally, we analyze the impact of the transition point on the mass, thickness and fraction of the moment of inertia of the neutron star crust. Prominent differences in these crustal properties of the star are found between using the transition point obtained with the dynamical method or the thermodynamical method. It is concluded that the core-crust transition needs to be ascertained as precisely as possible in order to have realistic estimates of the observed phenomena where the crust plays a significant role.

## Key claim

The core-crust transition density and pressure in neutron stars depend significantly on whether one uses the dynamical method (which includes finite-size surface and Coulomb effects) or the simpler thermodynamical method (which neglects them), and this choice produces prominent differences in predicted crustal properties such as mass, thickness, and moment of inertia.

## Method

The authors applied the dynamical method for locating the core-crust transition by analyzing the stability of homogeneous neutron star matter against small density fluctuations, using the curvature matrix of the energy density functional derived from the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation of the density matrix. They employed three families of finite-range nuclear interactions (Gogny forces D1S, D1M, D1M*, D1N; Modified Gogny Interaction MDI; and Simple Effective Interaction SEI) and compared the resulting transition densities and pressures with those obtained from the thermodynamical method that neglects surface and Coulomb contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the Gogny D1S force, the slope parameter of the symmetry energy L = 22.43 MeV; for D1M, L = 24.83 MeV; for D1N, L = 35.58 MeV; for D1M*, L = 43.18 MeV.
- The transition density predicted by the dynamical method is always lower than that from the thermodynamical method for all finite-range interactions studied.
- A lower value of the symmetry energy slope L correlates with a higher core-crust transition density, consistent with previous findings in the literature.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the core-crust transition that used the dynamical method primarily for zero-range Skyrme forces (Xu 2009a) or only for the MDI interaction (Xu 2009a, Xu 2010b). By applying the ETF-based density matrix expansion to Gogny and SEI interactions, the authors provide a systematic comparison across multiple finite-range force families. The results enable more realistic estimates of neutron star crust properties, which are essential for interpreting observations such as pulsar glitches and crustal oscillations.
