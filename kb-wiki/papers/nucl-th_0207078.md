# On the asymmetry of Gamow-Teller beta-decay rates in mirror nuclei in relation with second-class currents

> The theoretical evaluation of major nuclear structure effects on the asymmetry of allowed Gamow-Teller beta-decay rates in light mirror nuclei is presented. The calculations are performed within the shell model, using empirical isospin-nonconserving interaction and realistic Woods-Saxon radial wave functions. The revised treatment of p-shell nuclei is supplemented by systematic calculations for sd-shell nuclei and compared to experimental asymmetries when available. The results are important in connection with the possible existence of second-class currents in the weak interaction.

# On the asymmetry of Gamow-Teller beta-decay rates in mirror nuclei in relation with second-class currents

## Key claim
The paper presents updated shell-model calculations of nuclear structure contributions to the asymmetry of Gamow-Teller beta-decay rates in mirror nuclei, concluding that the current level of accuracy in B(GT)-values is still insufficient to extract possible contributions of second-class currents from the asymmetries, even with improved isospin-nonconserving interactions and realistic Woods-Saxon radial wave functions.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for p-shell and sd-shell mirror nuclei using empirical isospin-nonconserving (INC) Hamiltonians and Woods-Saxon radial wave functions. They computed the asymmetry delta = (ft)_+/(ft)_- - 1 arising from two nuclear structure effects: isospin mixing in nuclear states (via INC interactions from Ormand and Brown) and differences in radial wave functions between protons and neutrons (using Woods-Saxon potentials with single-particle energies fitted to experimental separation energies). The calculations used both the full parent-state formalism (including up to 100 states) and the one-body transition density approximation, and tested wave functions against experimental magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments.

## Key numbers
- The experimental asymmetry for mirror transitions varies on average from 1% to several tens of percent, being about (5 +/- 4)% on average.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were: r_0 = 1.27 fm, a = 0.75 fm, V_0 = 50.5 MeV, V_NZ = 32 MeV, V_ls = 0.22.
- The bare ratio (g_A/g_V) used was 1.26, as obtained from neutron decay.
- The INC interaction reproduces level shifts in mirror partners within 100 keV precision compared to experimental values.
- The Woods-Saxon potential reproduces charge radii for p-shell nuclei within 5%, except for the halo nucleus 8B.
- Theoretical magnetic dipole moments for p-shell nuclei agree with experiment within 20%.
- Electric quadrupole moments calculated with effective charges e(pi)=1.35, e(nu)=0.35 agree with measurements within 20%.

## Context
This work extends the earlier shell-model calculations of Towner (1973) and others (Bohr, 1971; Wilkinson, 1971) for p-shell mirror nuclei asymmetries, which were limited to A=8,9,12,13. The authors provide the first systematic calculations for sd-shell mirror nuclei, using improved INC interactions from Ormand and Brown (1989) and a more consistent method for fitting single-particle energies. The results are important for interpreting experimental asymmetries in the search for second-class currents, complementing correlation experiments such as the recent re-measurement of beta-particle angular distributions in aligned 12B and 12N by Minami and the beta-alpha angular correlation method proposed for the A=8 system by DeBra and by AmBe.
