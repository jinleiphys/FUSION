# Model Predictions for the Phenomenology of Isoscalar Heavy Baryons

> We study certain physical observables of isoscalar heavy baryons using potential models based on hadronic degrees of freedom. The goal is to compare these results with those from an effective theory obtained in a combined heavy quark and large $N_{c}$ expansion and thereby test the convergence of the effective theory. We study the excitation energy of $Λ_{b}$ and semileptonic decay form factors. The models tend to confirm the usefulness of the effective theory for most observables.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the combined heavy quark and large \(N_c\) expansion converges for isoscalar heavy baryon observables by comparing its next-to-leading-order (NLO) predictions to those from "realistic" potential models; the models tend to confirm the expansion's usefulness for most observables, but the authors caution that these models are not nature and convergence in a model does not guarantee convergence in the real world.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for single-channel and coupled-channel potential models describing a heavy baryon as a bound state of a light baryon (the N*(1535) resonance) and a heavy meson (spin-averaged D/D* or B/B* masses). They compute the excitation energy of the first excited state of \(\Lambda_b\) and semileptonic decay form factors for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) transitions, then compare these model results to the NLO predictions of the effective theory derived in prior work (Cohen et al., 2000-2002). For coupled-channel models, they treat the H and H* mesons as distinct states and include an excited light baryon N**(2090) to maintain correct parity.

## Key numbers

- Reduced mass in the charm sector: \(\mu \approx 863\) MeV
- Reduced mass in the bottom sector: \(\mu \approx 1191\) MeV
- The typical momentum scale \(\Lambda \equiv (\mu_c \Delta m_{\Lambda_c}^2)^{1/3} \approx 410\) MeV
- The light baryon mass used is the N*(1535) resonance mass (1535 MeV)
- The electromagnetic coupling constant used: \(e^2 \approx 1/137\)

## Context

This work extends the combined heavy quark and large \(N_c\) expansion developed by Cohen et al. (2000-2002) for isoscalar heavy baryons, which predicts a contracted O(8) symmetry connecting orbitally excited states to the ground state. The paper tests convergence by comparing NLO predictions to potential models based on the bound-state picture of heavy baryons (Rho et al., 1992; Manohar et al., 1993). The results suggest that the model-independent NLO predictions for spectroscopy and semileptonic decays are reliable, but electromagnetic decay widths are expected to have large \(1/N_c\) corrections and are not reported. Future experimental measurements of additional observables (beyond the known \(\Lambda_c\) excitation energy) would enable full NLO predictions.
