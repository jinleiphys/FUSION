# Model-independent determination of the two-photon exchange contribution to hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen

> We obtain a model-independent prediction for the two-photon exchange contribution to the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen. We use the relation of the Wilson coefficients of the spin-dependent dimension-six four-fermion operator of NRQED applied to the electron-proton and to the muon-proton sectors. Their difference can be reliably computed using chiral perturbation theory, whereas the Wilson coefficient of the electron-proton sector can be determined from the hyperfine splitting in hydrogen. This allows us to give a precise model-independent determination of the Wilson coefficient for the muon-proton sector, and consequently of the two-photon exchange contribution to the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen, which reads $δ\bar E_{pμ,\rm HF}^{\rm TPE}(nS)=-\frac{1}{n^3}1.161(20)$ meV. Together with the associated QED analysis, we obtain a prediction for the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen that reads $E^{\rm th}_{pμ,\rm HF}(1S)=182.623(27)$ meV and $E^{\rm th}_{pμ,\rm HF}(2S)=22.8123(33)$ meV. The error is dominated by the two-photon exchange contribution.

## Key claim

The authors obtain a model-independent prediction for the two-photon exchange contribution to the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen by relating the Wilson coefficients of the spin-dependent dimension-six four-fermion operator in NRQED for the electron-proton and muon-proton sectors, using the fact that their difference can be reliably computed with chiral perturbation theory while the electron-proton coefficient is fixed by the hydrogen hyperfine measurement.

## Method

The authors use the relation between the Wilson coefficients \(c_4^{pe}\) and \(c_4^{p\mu}\) of the NRQED Lagrangian, where the difference \(c_{4,\rm TPE}^{p\mu} - c_{4,\rm TPE}^{pe}\) is computed using chiral perturbation theory. The electron-proton coefficient \(\bar c_{4,\rm TPE}^{pe}\) is determined from the experimental hydrogen hyperfine splitting after subtracting all known QED contributions. This then yields the muon-proton coefficient and the corresponding two-photon exchange energy shift for muonic hydrogen. The analysis also includes electron vacuum polarization corrections and recoil corrections at \({\cal O}(m_r\alpha^6)\).

## Key numbers

* Two-photon exchange contribution to hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: \(\delta \bar E_{p\mu,\rm HF}^{\rm TPE}(nS) = -\frac{1}{n^3} 1.161(20)\) meV
* Theoretical prediction for 1S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: \(E^{\rm th}_{p\mu,\rm HF}(1S) = 182.623(27)\) meV
* Theoretical prediction for 2S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: \(E^{\rm th}_{p\mu,\rm HF}(2S) = 22.8123(33)\) meV
* Fermi term for 1S: 182.65604 meV
* Fermi term for 2S: 22.832005 meV
* Electron vacuum polarization correction \(V^{(2,1)}\times V_{\rm VP}^{(0,2)}\) for 1S: 0.73449 meV; for 2S: 0.074474 meV
* Electron vacuum polarization correction \(V_{\rm VP}^{(2,2)}\) for 1S: 0.37465 meV; for 2S: 0.048275 meV
* Breit correction for 1S: 0.01457 meV; for 2S: 0.002581 meV
* Recoil correction for 1S: 0.01752 meV; for 2S: 0.001846 meV
* Wilson coefficient from hydrogen: \(\bar c_{4,\rm TPE}^{pe} = -48.69(3)\)
* Wilson coefficient from muonic hydrogen experiment: \(\bar c_{4,\rm TPE}^{p\mu} = -46.5(1.6)_{\rm exp}(0.3)_{\rm th}\)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Pineda (2002) and Peset (2015) for muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting by providing a model-independent determination of the two-photon exchange contribution. It uses the measured hydrogen hyperfine splitting (Karshenboim, 2005) to fix the electron-proton Wilson coefficient, then relates it to the muon-proton sector via chiral perturbation theory. The result enables precise theoretical predictions for ongoing muonic hydrogen experiments at PSI (Antognini, 2013) and resolves discrepancies between earlier computations by Pachucki and Martynenko (2004) for the 2S state.
