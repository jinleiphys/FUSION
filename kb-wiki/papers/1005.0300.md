# Density functional for pairing with particle number conservation

> In this work, a new functional is introduced to treat pairing correlations in finite many-body systems. Guided by the projected BCS framework, the energy is written as a functional of occupation numbers. It is shown to generalize the BCS approach and to provide an alternative to Variation After Projection framework. Illustrations of the new approach are given for the pairing Hamiltonian for various particle numbers and coupling strengths. In all case, a very good agreement with the exact solution is found.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a new density functional for pairing correlations that approximately restores particle number conservation without explicit projection, claiming it generalizes BCS and provides an alternative to Variation After Projection (VAP) while remaining computationally simpler. The hedge is that the functional is derived from an approximate mapping of the projected BCS parameters onto occupation numbers, not an exact projection.

## Method
The authors start from the projected BCS (PBCS) state for a pairing Hamiltonian and derive expressions for occupation numbers \(n_i\) and correlation matrix elements \(C_{ij}\) as functionals of the \(\{x_i\}\) parameters. They then approximate these parameters as functions of the \(n_i\) using expansions involving moments \(s_p = (1/N)\sum n_i^p\), leading to a closed-form expression for \(C_{ij}\) in terms of \(n_i\) and the particle number \(N\). The energy functional is minimized directly using this expression for the correlation term.

## Key numbers
- For the picket fence pairing Hamiltonian with constant level spacing \(\Delta\varepsilon\), results are shown for \(A=8\) and \(A=16\) particles (i.e., \(N=4\) and \(N=8\) pairs).
- The functional reproduces exact energies across coupling strengths, with the new functional (filled circles) closely matching the exact solid line in Figure 2, while BCS (dashed line) deviates significantly at intermediate coupling.
- The BCS limit is recovered when \(a_0=1\) and \(a_1=0\); the weak-coupling (Hartree-Fock) limit gives \(C_{ij} \rightarrow \sqrt{n_i n_j}\) when \(s_2 = s_3 = 1\).

## Context
This work extends the Density Matrix Functional Theory approach (Gilbert 1975) to pairing in finite nuclear systems, building on prior applications of natural orbitals and occupancies in nuclear physics (Papakonstantinou 2007, Lacroix 2009). It addresses the self-interaction problem in projected energy density functionals identified by Anguiano (2001) and Dobaczewski (2007), and offers a computationally lighter alternative to the VAP framework of Sheikh (2000) for pairing correlations, potentially enabling applications to nuclear dynamics and thermodynamics where explicit projection is impractical.
