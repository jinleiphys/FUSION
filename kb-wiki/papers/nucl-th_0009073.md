# The Next Yukawa Phase of QCD at RHIC

> QCD predicts the existence of a new partonic Yukawa phase above a critical temperature $T_c\sim 150$ MeV. I review some of the key observables which will be used in heavy ion reactions to search for this new phase at RHIC. Systematics of collective observables ($N_{ch},E_\perp)$, flow patterns, meson interferometry, jet quenching, and $J/ψ$ suppression are discussed. This talk is updated with the first data from RHIC.

## Key claim

QCD predicts a new partonic Yukawa phase above a critical temperature \(T_c \sim 150\) MeV, but the author argues that SPS data have not demonstrated deconfinement because at those energies the quark and gluon degrees of freedom are not resolvable; RHIC energies are needed to test whether the QCD predictions for the Yukawa phase are correct.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD results for the heavy quark potential in both confined and deconfined phases, showing the transition from a Luscher confining form to a screened Yukawa form above \(T_c\). It then examines pQCD-based estimates of initial gluon densities and energy densities in heavy ion collisions using the HIJING and EKRT models, and discusses how collective observables (multiplicity, transverse energy, elliptic flow, pion interferometry, and \(J/\psi\) suppression) can be used to search for signatures of the Yukawa phase at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the Yukawa phase: \(T_c \sim 150\) MeV
- For \(T > 2 T_c\) in SU(2) quenched QCD, the Debye screening mass \(\mu_L \approx 2.5 T\), while the effective exponent \(d_L \approx 1.5\) (below the pQCD value of 2)
- Near \(T_c\), the exponent \(d < 1\) and \(\mu \sim T/2\), indicating a longer range interaction
- Initial energy density at RHIC: \(\epsilon(\tau_0 \sim 0.5\ \text{fm/c}) > 10\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(p_0 \sim 1-2\) GeV
- EKRT estimates yield up to 1500 gluons per unit rapidity at RHIC; HIJING with fixed \(p_0 = 2\) GeV gives \(< 500\) gluons per unit rapidity
- HIJING predicts initial transverse energy density scaling: \(dE_\perp/dy \approx 1\ \text{GeV}\ A^{1.3}\ (1 + \log(\sqrt{s}/200))\)
- EKRT predicts \(\epsilon(\tau = 1/p_{sat}) \approx 0.1 A^{0.5} s^{0.38}\)
- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) at RHIC is about twice as large as at SPS, rising linearly with \(p_\perp\) up to 2 GeV/c
- \(J/\psi\) suppression in Pb+Pb shows an excess 25% suppression above the normal \((AB)^{0.9}\) scaling

## Context

This talk extends the line of work on QCD phase transitions and the search for deconfinement in heavy ion collisions, building on lattice QCD calculations by Karsch et al. (1999) and the MILC collaboration (1997). It contrasts the author's view with the CERN press release claiming evidence for deconfinement at SPS, arguing that RHIC is the first facility where the Yukawa phase can be resolved. The paper enables future experimental tests of the QCD equation of state through measurements of transverse energy systematics, elliptic flow, pion interferometry (the QGP stall), and \(J/\psi\) suppression patterns at RHIC, particularly by PHENIX and STAR.
