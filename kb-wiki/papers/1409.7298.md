# Positive and negative parity states of the Two-Rotor Model and scissors modes

> In previous investigations of the Two-Rotor Model with axially symmetric rotors the wave functions were assumed to be invariant under inversion of the axes of the rotors, which restricted the spectrum to positive parity states. We relax this condition requiring only that the wave functions be invariant under the square of the inversion operators. As a result we get positive as well as negative parity states that are all split by tunneling effect.

# Wiki Page: Positive and Negative Parity States of the Two-Rotor Model and Scissors Modes

## Key claim

The Two-Rotor Model (TRM) Hamiltonian possesses a D₂ point symmetry that produces eigenstates in quadruplets containing both positive and negative parity states, but the actual existence of these new negative parity states in atomic nuclei depends on whether excited states of the neutron and proton fluids separately exist that are odd under inversion of the intrinsic coordinates.

## Method

The author relaxes the previous assumption that wave functions must be invariant under inversion of each rotor's symmetry axis, requiring only invariance under the square of the inversion operators. The intrinsic Hamiltonian is quantized and transformed using Euler angles and the variable θ (half the angle between rotor axes), then solved in the harmonic approximation for the two lowest quadruplets with total angular momentum I=0 and I=1. The eigenstates are classified by parity π and the eigenvalue r₁ of the inversion operator for rotor 1.

## Key numbers

- The scissors excitation energy ω is given by ω = √(C/ℐ), where C is the restoring force constant and ℐ = ℐ₁ℐ₂/(ℐ₁+ℐ₂) is the reduced moment of inertia
- The two lowest quadruplets have intrinsic energies ℏω and 2ℏω respectively
- The phenomenological value of θ₀² is approximately 0.01 in the rare earth region
- The energy splitting from tunneling is bounded by δE << exp(-π/(2θ₀²)) × E, which for θ₀² ≈ 0.01 gives δE << exp(-100) × E
- For atomic nuclei, the energies of the new states are ω+e and ω+2e, where e is the excitation energy of a single rotor with negative parity
- The B(M1) strength for transitions between negative parity states is (1/2) × B↓_scissors
- Nonvanishing M1 transitions occur only between states whose energies differ by ω

## Context

This work extends the original Two-Rotor Model developed by LoIudice and Palumbo (1978) and the scissors mode predictions by Hilton (1981), which were experimentally confirmed in deformed nuclei by Bohle et al. The paper completes the determination of the two lowest D₂ quadruplets that were only partially studied previously, where only the ground state and the positive parity scissors mode had been investigated. The results enable experimental searches for negative parity scissors states through two-step cascade experiments as suggested by Krticka and Adekolla, and provide a framework for testing the predicted entanglement of scissors modes through B(M3) strength measurements in the scissors rotational band.
