# Isospin Properties of ($K^-$, $N$) Reactions for the Formation of Deeply-bound Antikaonic Nuclei

> The formation of deeply-bound antikaonic $K^-/\bar{K}^0$ nuclear states by nuclear ($K^-$, $N$) reactions is investigated theoretically within a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA), considering the isospin properties of the Fermi-averaged $K^-+ N \to N + \bar{K}$ elementary amplitudes. We calculate the formation cross sections of the deeply-bound $\bar{K}$ states by the ($K^-$, $N$) reactions on the nuclear targets, $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si, at incident $K^-$ lab momentum $p_{K^-}$ = 1.0 GeV/c and $Î¸_{\rm lab} = 0^{\circ}$, introducing a complex effective nucleon number $N_{\rm eff}$ for unstable bound states in the DWIA. The results show that the deeply-bound $\bar{K}$ states can be populated dominantly by the ($K^-$, $n$) reaction via the total isoscalar $Î”T=0$ transition owing to the isospin nature of the $K^-+ N \to N + \bar{K}$ amplitudes, and that the cross sections described by ${\rm Re}N_{\rm eff}$ and ${\rm Arg}N_{\rm eff}$ enable to deduce the structure of the $\bar{K}$ nuclear states; the calculated inclusive nucleon spectra for a deep $\bar{K}$-nucleus potential do not show distinct peak structure in the bound region. The few-body $\bar{K}\otimes [NN]$ and $\bar{K}\otimes [NNN]$ states formed in ($K^-$, $N$) reactions on $s$-shell nuclear targets, $^3$He, $^3$H and $^4$He, are also discussed.

## Key claim

The formation of deeply-bound antikaonic nuclear states via ($K^-$, $N$) reactions is dominated by the ($K^-$, $n$) channel through a total isoscalar $\Delta T=0$ transition, but the calculated inclusive nucleon spectra for a deep $\bar{K}$-nucleus potential do not show distinct peak structures in the bound region, indicating that experimental identification remains challenging even if such bound states exist.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations for nuclear ($K^-$, $N$) reactions on $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si targets at incident $K^-$ lab momentum $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/c and $\theta_{\rm lab}=0^\circ$, using Fermi-averaged $K^-+N\to N+\bar{K}$ elementary amplitudes derived from the $K^-N$ scattering amplitudes of Gopal et al. (1977). They introduced a complex effective nucleon number $N_{\rm eff}$ for unstable bound states and also analyzed the isospin properties of the formation cross sections for few-body $\bar{K}\otimes[NN]$ and $\bar{K}\otimes[NNN]$ states on $s$-shell targets $^3$He, $^3$H, and $^4$He.

## Key numbers

- At $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/c and $\theta_{\rm lab}=0^\circ$, the free-space lab differential cross sections for elementary reactions are: $24.5$ mb/sr for $K^-+n\to n+K^-$, $13.1$ mb/sr for $K^-+p\to n+\bar{K}^0$, and $9.4$ mb/sr for $K^-+p\to p+K^-$.
- After Fermi-averaging for $^{12}$C, the forward cross sections at $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/c become: $13.9$ mb/sr for $K^-+n\to n+K^-$, $7.5$ mb/sr for $K^-+p\to n+\bar{K}^0$, and $3.5$ mb/sr for $K^-+p\to p+K^-$.
- The momentum transfer $q(0^\circ)$ at $p_{K^-}=1.0$ GeV/c and $\theta_{\rm lab}=0^\circ$ is negative, with values ranging from approximately $-200$ to $-400$ MeV/c depending on the $\bar{K}$ binding energy $B_{\bar{K}}$.
- For $B_{\bar{K}}=50$ MeV, the minimum momentum transfer $q_{\rm min}$ is $-173.4$ MeV/c at $p_{K^-}=192.6$ MeV/c; for $B_{\bar{K}}=100$ MeV, $q_{\rm min}$ is $-279.2$ MeV/c at $p_{K^-}=310.0$ MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for ($K^-$, $N$) reactions proposed by Kishimoto (1999) and follows earlier calculations by Ikuta et al. (2002) and Yamagata et al. (2005, 2006) using Green's function techniques. The study is motivated by the KEK PS-E548 experiment (Kishimoto et al., 2005) which found no clear evidence for deeply-bound $\bar{K}$ states, and by the J-PARC E15 experiment (Iwasaki et al., 2006) searching for the $K^-pp$ state via $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$, $n$) reactions. The results enable quantitative predictions for upcoming experiments and clarify the isospin selectivity that favors ($K^-$, $n$) over ($K^-$, $p$) reactions for populating deeply-bound antikaonic states.
