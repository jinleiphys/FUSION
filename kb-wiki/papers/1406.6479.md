# Time-reversal symmetry violation in molecules induced by nuclear magnetic quadrupole moments

> Recent measurements in paramagnetic molecules improved the limit on the electron electric dipole moment (EDM) by an order of magnitude. Time-reversal (T) and parity (P) symmetry violation in molecules may also come from their nuclei. We point out that nuclear T,P-odd effects are amplified in paramagnetic molecules containing deformed nuclei, where the primary effects arise from the T,P-odd nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM). We perform calculations of T,P-odd effects in the molecules TaN, ThO, ThF$^+$, HfF$^+$, YbF, HgF, and BaF induced by MQMs. We compare our results with those for the diamagnetic TlF molecule, where the T,P-odd effects are produced by the nuclear Schiff moment. We argue that measurements in molecules with MQMs may provide improved limits on the strength of T,P-odd nuclear forces, on the proton, neutron and quark EDMs, on quark chromo-EDMs, and on the QCD $θ$-term and CP-violating quark interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that time-reversal (T) and parity (P) violating effects from nuclear magnetic quadrupole moments (MQMs) are amplified in paramagnetic molecules containing deformed nuclei, and that measurements in such systems could improve limits on hadronic CP-violation parameters, provided experimental sensitivity can be improved by about an order of magnitude beyond the recent ThO electron EDM experiment.

## Method

The authors performed molecular electronic structure calculations to determine the parameter \(W_M\) (the molecular constant for the MQM interaction) for the molecules TaN, ThO, ThF\(^+\), HfF\(^+\), YbF, HgF, and BaF. For ThO, HfF\(^+\), and ThF\(^+\), they used previously calculated values of the electron EDM parameter \(W_d\) together with a scaling relation based on relativistic factors to estimate \(W_M\). For TaN, they made a rough estimate based on the YbF value with adjustments for atomic number and orbital composition. They then combined these molecular constants with nuclear MQM values derived from a valence nucleon model, including collective enhancement for deformed nuclei, to compute frequency shifts in terms of underlying CP-violating quantities such as the proton EDM \(d_p\), the QCD \(\theta\)-term, and quark chromo-EDMs.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{229}\)ThO in the \(^3\Delta_1\) state: \(|W_M| = 1.9 \times 10^{33}\) Hz/(e·cm\(^2\)); the frequency shift from the MQM is \(|W_M M S| \approx 72 \times 10^{10} \tilde{\theta}\) μHz and \(\approx 44 \times 10^{27} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d)/\text{cm}\) μHz.
- For \(^{181}\)TaN in the \(^3\Delta_1\) state: \(|W_M| \approx 1 \times 10^{33}\) Hz/(e·cm\(^2\)); the frequency shift from the MQM is \(|W_M M S| \approx 50 \times 10^{10} \tilde{\theta}\) μHz and \(\approx 30 \times 10^{27} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d)/\text{cm}\) μHz.
- For \(^{173}\)YbF in the \(^2\Sigma_{1/2}\) state: \(|W_M| = 2.1 \times 10^{33}\) Hz/(e·cm\(^2\)); the frequency shift is \(|W_M M S| \approx 42 \times 10^{10} \tilde{\theta}\) μHz.
- For \(^{177}\)HfF\(^+\) in the \(^3\Delta_1\) state: \(|W_M| = 0.5 \times 10^{33}\) Hz/(e·cm\(^2\)); the frequency shift is \(|W_M M S| \approx 33 \times 10^{10} \tilde{\theta}\) μHz.
- Current experimental accuracy in ThO is 700 μHz for the eEDM energy shift; the corresponding limits on the proton EDM are \(|d_p| < 8.6 \times 10^{-25}\) e·cm, on \(\tilde{\theta}\) are \(|\tilde{\theta}| < 2.4 \times 10^{-10}\), and on the quark chromo-EDM difference are \(|\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d| < 6 \times 10^{-27}\) cm (from Ref. [Hg]).
- For \(^{181}\)TaN, the current limits on \(d_p\), \(\tilde{\theta}\), and \((\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d)\) correspond to shifts \(|W_M M| < 260\) μHz, 120 μHz, and 180 μHz respectively.
- For the diamagnetic molecule TlF, the T,P-odd frequency shift from the Schiff moment is \(\nu(\tilde{\theta}) = 1.0 \times 10^5\) Hz \(\tilde{\theta}\), giving a limit \(|\tilde{\theta}| < 4 \times 10^{-9}\) from experiment.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1984) on nuclear T,P-odd moments in molecules, and builds on recent experimental advances in paramagnetic molecules with \(^3\Delta_1\) states, particularly the ThO electron EDM experiment that improved the limit by an order of magnitude. The paper contrasts the MQM approach with the Schiff moment approach used in diamagnetic molecules like TlF (Cho et al.) and atomic \(^{199}\)Hg, noting that MQM effects are less sensitive to many-body nuclear corrections and are collectively enhanced in deformed nuclei. The results enable future experiments to search for hadronic CP-violation using molecules such as TaN, ThO, HfF\(^+\), and ThF\(^+\), potentially improving limits on the QCD \(\theta\)-term, quark chromo-EDMs, and nucleon EDMs.
