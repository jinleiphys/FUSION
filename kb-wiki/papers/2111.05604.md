# Calculations of the alpha decay half-lives of some Polonium isotopes using the double folding model

> The calculations of the alpha decay half-lives of some Polonium isotopes in the mass range 186 - 218 have been carried out using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) semiclassical approximation. The alpha-nucleus effective potential used contains the Coulomb potential, centrifugal potential, and the nuclear potential. The nuclear potential is obtained via the double folding model, with the microscopic NN effective interactions derived from relativistic mean field theory Lagrangian (termed R3Y). Different parametrizations of the R3Y interactions have been employed in the computation of the nuclear potentials. The results obtained using the R3Y NN interactions are compared with the ones obtained using the famous Michigan-3-Yukawa (M3Y) interactions. The use of density-dependent NN interaction is also considered. When compared to available experimental data, there are improvements in the results when density-dependent interaction potentials are used compared to when density-independent interactions are employed.

## Key claim

The R3Y nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from relativistic mean field theory, when used in the double folding model with the WKB semiclassical approximation, give better descriptions of alpha decay half-lives for Polonium isotopes in the mass range 186-218 than the standard M3Y interactions, with further improvement when density-dependent interactions are employed.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha decay half-lives using the WKB semiclassical approximation with an effective alpha-nucleus potential comprising Coulomb, centrifugal, and nuclear terms. The nuclear potential was obtained via the double folding model, folding the matter density distributions of the alpha particle (Gaussian form) and daughter nucleus (Fermi form) with microscopic NN effective interactions derived from relativistic mean field theory Lagrangian (R3Y), using four parametrizations: R3Y-HS, R3Y-L1, R3Y-W, and R3Y-Z. The pre-formation probability was included using an empirical formula, and results were compared with those using M3Y-Paris and M3Y-Reid interactions, for both density-independent (DD0) and density-dependent (DDM3Y1) cases.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation sigma for density-independent R3Y models (with pre-formation factor): R3Y-HS = 0.4278, R3Y-L1 = 0.4328, R3Y-W = 0.4440, R3Y-Z = 0.4159, for 33 Polonium isotopes in mass range 186-218.
- Standard deviation sigma for density-dependent DDM3Y R3Y models (with pre-formation factor): R3Y-HS = 0.3970, R3Y-L1 = 0.3627, R3Y-W = 0.3651, R3Y-Z = 0.3626.
- Standard deviation sigma for density-independent (DD0) M3Y models without pre-formation factor: M3Y-Paris = 0.8044, M3Y-Reid = 0.8099; for R3Y models: R3Y-HS = 0.7807, R3Y-L1 = 0.5729, R3Y-W = 0.5595, R3Y-Z = 0.5950.
- Experimental Q-alpha values range from 4.9792 MeV (Po-209) to 8.9542 MeV (Po-212); experimental log half-lives range from -6.5240 (Po-212) to 9.5070 (Po-209).
- The maximum calculated half-life occurs at N=125 (parent Po-209) and the minimum at N=128 (daughter Pb-208 with neutron magic number N=126).

## Context

This work extends the application of R3Y NN interactions, previously introduced by Singh et al. (2010) for cluster decay studies, to alpha decay of Polonium isotopes using the double folding model. It compares the R3Y results with the established M3Y-Paris and M3Y-Reid interactions used in prior studies by Ni et al. (2009) and Xu et al. (2005, 2006), and demonstrates that density-dependent interactions improve agreement with experimental data from the NUBASE2020 database. The findings enable more accurate theoretical predictions of alpha decay half-lives for heavy nuclei and support further testing of R3Y interactions across different isotopic chains.
