# Evidence for charmonium generation at the phase boundary in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> We investigate the transition from suppression to enhancement of J/psi mesons produced in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions in the framework of the statistical hadronization model. The calculations are confronted with the most recent data from the RHIC accelerator. This comparison yields first direct evidence for generation of J/psi mesons at the phase boundary. Based on the success of this approach we make specific predictions for LHC energy.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first direct evidence that J/psi mesons are generated at the phase boundary (hadronization) via statistical hadronization, rather than being suppressed by Debye screening in the quark-gluon plasma. This evidence is conditional on the model's ability to reproduce the rapidity dependence of the nuclear modification factor, which shows a maximum at midrapidity that is opposite to the trend expected from melting models.

## Method

The authors employed the statistical hadronization model (SHM) to calculate J/psi yields in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, using the charm production cross section from perturbative QCD and the J/psi production cross section in pp collisions from PHENIX data. They compared the model predictions for the nuclear modification factor R_AA^J/psi as a function of rapidity and centrality to the comprehensive PHENIX data set, fitting the pp rapidity distribution with a gaussian of width sigma_y=1.63 plus or minus 0.05.

## Key numbers

- The rapidity width of the J/psi distribution in pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV is sigma_y=1.63 plus or minus 0.05, obtained from a gaussian fit to PHENIX data.
- The rapidity density of initially produced charm quark pairs at RHIC is dN_c\bar{c}/dy=1.6.
- At LHC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV), the charm production cross section is expected to be about an order of magnitude larger than at RHIC.
- The model calculations approach the pp value around N_part=50, corresponding to a minimal QGP volume of 400 fm^3.
- For LHC central collisions, R_AA^J/psi is predicted to exceed unity, with a factor of about 2 enhancement if the charm production cross section is two times larger than presently assumed.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model approach previously developed by the same authors (Andronic et al., 2006) and contrasts with the kinetic model of Thews et al. and transport model calculations by Zhang et al. and Bratkovskaya et al., which describe J/psi production via dynamical melting and regeneration over the whole QGP evolution. The results provide the first unambiguous evidence for charmonium generation at chemical freeze-out and enable specific predictions for LHC energy, where the centrality dependence of R_AA^J/psi is expected to reverse trend and exceed unity for central collisions, serving as a fingerprint of deconfined and thermalized heavy quarks in the QGP.
