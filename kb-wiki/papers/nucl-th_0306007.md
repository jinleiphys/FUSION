# String Parton Models in Geant4

> Dual parton and quark gluon string model are the by now almost standard theoretical techniques by which one can arrive at precision description of high energy, soft, inclusive reactions. These reactions make the part of jets at energies that contribute strongly to discovery channels such as H$\to$WWjj, or search for compositeness at the highest transverse momenta. The above modeling approach is available with Geant4 for nucleon induced reactions since the first release. Its object oriented design and parameter set was recently extended to allow for simulation of pion and kaon induced reactions, as well as heavy ion reactions. We will briefly describe the theory and algorithmic approaches that underly the modeling, show the object oriented designs and component structure of the string parton sub-systems of Geant4, present validation/verification results pertaining to these models, as well as results concerning their usage in calorimeter simulation.

# String Parton Models in Geant4

## Key claim
The dual parton and quark gluon string models, implemented in Geant4, can provide a precision description of high energy, soft, inclusive reactions, but the paper presents validation results primarily for hadron-nucleus collisions and calorimeter simulation, with the caveat that the cascade model for secondary interactions with the excited nucleus was not yet completed at the time of writing.

## Method
The paper describes the object-oriented implementation of two string excitation models (diffractive string excitation and quark gluon string model) within Geant4, which simulate inelastic reactions of high energy particles with nuclei. The nucleus is modeled as an ensemble of protons and neutrons with positions sampled from Wood-Saxon or harmonic oscillator density distributions and momenta from a Fermi gas model, then collapsed into two dimensions perpendicular to the primary particle direction. String fragmentation into hadrons is handled by longitudinal string fragmentation models (Lund or QGSM fragmentation functions), while nuclear de-excitation is simulated by fragmentation, precompound, and de-excitation models.

## Context
This work extends the string parton model approach, previously available in Geant4 for nucleon induced reactions since the first release, to include simulation of pion and kaon induced reactions as well as heavy ion reactions. The diffractive scattering model follows an approach similar to Fritiof (FRITIOF), while the quark gluon string model uses the Regge Gribov approach with Pomeron exchange as described by Capella (1978) and Kaidalov (1982, 1987). The implementation enables simulation of high energy hadron-nucleus reactions and electro- and gamma-nuclear reactions within the Geant4 framework, with applications to calorimeter simulation and background estimation for discovery channels such as H→WWjj.
