# Neutrino masses or new interactions

> Recent proposals to study the mass of the "electron" neutrino at a sensitivity of 0.3 eV can be used to place limits on the right handed and scalar charged currents at a level which improves on the present experimental limits. Indeed the neglect of the possibility of such interactions can lead to the inference of an incorrect value for the mass, as we illustrate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that future tritium beta-decay experiments aiming for a neutrino mass sensitivity of 0.3 eV can also set limits on right-handed and scalar charged currents that improve on present experimental bounds, but that neglecting the possibility of such non-standard interactions could lead to inferring an incorrect value for the neutrino mass.

## Method

The authors construct a general effective Hamiltonian for low-energy semileptonic beta decay that includes scalar, tensor, vector, and axial-vector currents beyond the Standard Model V-A structure. They derive the differential and integral electron energy spectra for tritium decay including interference terms from right-handed vector and right-chiral scalar currents, parameterized by coupling strengths ρ_R and ρ_{S_R} and separate mixing angles for each interaction type. They then compute the relative difference ΔG between spectra with and without these additional interactions, using the Large Mixing Angle solution to the solar neutrino problem as the standard mixing scheme.

## Key numbers

- Current experimental upper bound on the electron anti-neutrino mass: m_{ν_e} ≤ 2.5 eV
- Future KATRIN experiment sensitivity: 0.3 eV (sub-eV)
- Atmospheric neutrino mass difference from Super-Kamiokande: δm²_atm ∼ 3 × 10⁻³ eV², implying m₃ ≥ 0.05 eV
- Present limits on non-standard coupling strengths (assuming mass and weak eigenstates identical): ρ_R ≤ 0.07, ρ_{S_R} ≤ 0.1, ρ_{S_L} ≤ 0.01
- The 0.3 eV mass sensitivity corresponds to about a 0.02% shift in the doubly integrated spectrum G₀(E_l) at a neutrino energy E_ν ≈ 40 eV
- The approximation for the doubly integrated spectrum is accurate to about one part in a thousand for cutoff energies E_ν > 35 eV, and deteriorates rapidly for E_ν < 10 eV

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Stephenson et al. (2000) and Stephenson et al. (1998) on the negative mass-squared anomaly of the electron anti-neutrino, and builds on the formalism for general beta-decay interactions described in those references. The paper shows that future experiments like KATRIN, which aim for 0.3 eV sensitivity, can simultaneously probe new Lorentz structures, and that the presence of neutrino mixing with non-standard currents can produce interference effects that completely distort conclusions about the neutrino mass if not properly accounted for. The authors recommend that future experimental analyses include fits with interference terms incorporated.
