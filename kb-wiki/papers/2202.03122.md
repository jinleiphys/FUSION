# Lorentz transformation in Maxwell equations for slowly moving media

> We use the method of field decomposition, a technique widely used in relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, to study the small velocity approximation (SVA) of the Lorentz transformation in Maxwell equations for slowly moving media. The "deformed" Maxwell equations derived under the SVA in the lab frame can be put into the conventional form of Maxwell equations in the medium's comoving frame. Our results show that the Lorentz transformation in the SVA up to $O(v/c)$ ($v$ is the speed of the medium and $c$ is the speed of light in vacuum) is essential to derive these equations: the time and charge density must also change when transforming to a different frame even in the SVA, not just the position and current density as in the Galilean transformation. This marks the essential difference of the Lorentz transformation from the Galilean one. We show that the integral forms of Faraday and Ampere equations for slowly moving surfaces are consistent with Maxwell equations. We also present Faraday equation the covariant integral form in which the electromotive force can be defined as a Lorentz scalar independent of the observer's frame. No evidences exist to support an extension or modification of Maxwell equations.

## Key claim

The "deformed" Maxwell equations for slowly moving media proposed by Wang (2021) can be derived from the Lorentz transformation under the small velocity approximation (SVA) up to O(v/c), provided that time and charge density are also transformed (not just position and current density as in the Galilean transformation). The authors show that no evidence exists to support an extension or modification of Maxwell equations beyond standard special relativity.

## Method

The authors use the field decomposition method from relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, expressing the electromagnetic field strength tensor F^{μν} in terms of four-vectors E^{μ} and B^{μ} relative to a chosen four-velocity u^{μ}. They apply the Lorentz transformation under the SVA (neglecting O(v²/c²) terms, setting γ≈1) to derive Maxwell equations in the lab frame for slowly moving media, then show these equations can be rewritten in the conventional Maxwell form in the medium's comoving frame using the SVA-transformed quantities.

## Key numbers

- The SVA is taken up to O(v/c), where v is the speed of the medium and c is the speed of light in vacuum; all O(v²/c²) terms are neglected by setting γ≈1
- The Lorentz factor γ = (1 - v²/c²)^{-1/2} is approximated as γ≈1 in the SVA
- Terms proportional to (1-γ) = -[γ²/(1+γ)](v²/c²) ~ v²/c² are neglected in the field transformations
- The charge conservation law in the lab frame is ∂ρ_f/∂t + ∇·J_f = 0, with all O(v/c) terms canceling in the derivation

## Context

This work directly addresses the extended Maxwell equations proposed by Wang (2021) for charged moving media in triboelectric nanogenerators (TENGs). The authors show that the equations of Wang (2021) can be derived from standard Lorentz transformation under SVA, contrary to claims that they require modification of Maxwell equations. The paper extends the relativistic treatment of moving media from Minkowski (1908, 1910) and Einstein (1908), and uses field decomposition techniques from relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (Giacomazzo, 2005; Huang, 2011; Pu, 2016; Denicol, 2018). The results enable consistent application of Maxwell equations to slowly moving dielectric and magnetic media without requiring new physics.
