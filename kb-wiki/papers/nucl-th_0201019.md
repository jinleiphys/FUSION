# Final-state interactions in the response of nuclear matter

> Final-state interactions in the response of a many-body system to an external probe delivering large momentum are normally described using the eikonal approximation, for the trajectory of the struck particle, and the frozen approximation, for the positions of the spectators. We propose a generalization of this scheme, in which the initial momentum of the struck particle is explicitly taken into account. Numerical calculations of the nuclear matter response at 1 $< |{\bf q}| <$ 2 GeV/c show that the inclusion of this momentum dependence leads to a sizable effect in the low energy tail. Possible implications for the analysis of existing electron-nucleus scattering data are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors propose a generalization of the Correlated Glauber Approximation (CGA) for final-state interactions in nuclear matter response that explicitly includes the initial momentum of the struck nucleon, whereas the standard CGA assumes the struck particle's momentum is approximately equal to the momentum transfer. The inclusion of this momentum dependence leads to a sizable effect in the low-energy tail of the response, though the paper does not claim this resolves the discrepancy with experimental data without also invoking color transparency effects.

## Method

The authors develop a nonrelativistic treatment of the nuclear matter response to a scalar probe, starting from the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) and improving upon it by including final-state interactions through the frozen approximation for spectator nucleons and the eikonal approximation for the struck particle's trajectory. They compute a generalized folding function that depends explicitly on both the initial and final momenta of the struck nucleon, using the Fermi-HyperNetted Chain (FHNC) approach to evaluate the many-body correlations, and compare the resulting response functions and y-scaling functions with those obtained from the standard CGA at different values of momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range studied: 1 < |q| < 2 GeV/c
- The inclusion of initial momentum dependence produces a "sizable effect" in the low-energy tail of the response, specifically in the region where the Bjorken scaling variable x = (Q²/2mω) > 2
- Color transparency effects were found in prior work (Benhar 1991, 1993, 1994) to improve agreement with data for Q² > 1.5 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends the Correlated Glauber Approximation developed in Benhar (1991) (referred to as I) and applied to electron-nucleus scattering data in Benhar (1991, 1993, 1994), which successfully described inclusive (e,e') cross sections for momentum transfers in the 1-2 GeV/c range but overestimated the extreme low-energy loss tail. The improved treatment of final-state interactions is motivated by the need to firmly establish the occurrence of color transparency in (e,e') processes, as suggested by the model of CT (1991) which involves no adjustable parameters. The approach is analogous to treatments of final-state interactions in neutron scattering from quantum liquids, as proposed in Sil and Clark (1999).
