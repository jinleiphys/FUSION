# Charged multiplicity density and number of participant nucleons in relativistic nuclear collisions

> The energy and centrality dependences of charged particle pseudorapidity density in relativistic nuclear collisions were studied using a hadron and string cascade model, JPCIAE. Both the relativistic $p+\bar p$ experimental data and the PHOBOS and PHENIX $Au+Au$ data at RHIC energy could be fairly reproduced within the framework of JPCIAE model and without retuning the model parameters. The predictions for $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the LHC energy were also given. We computed the participant nucleon distributions using different methods. It was found that the number of participant nucleons is not a well defined variable both experimentally and theoretically. Thus it may be inappropriate to use the charged particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair as a function of the number of participant nucleons for distinguishing various theoretical models. A discussion for the effect of different definitions in nuclear radius (diffused or sharp) was given.

## Key claim

The JPCIAE hadron and string cascade model can reproduce the energy and centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density in both p+pbar and Au+Au collisions at RHIC without retuning parameters; however, the number of participant nucleons is not a well-defined variable either experimentally or theoretically, making it inappropriate to use the charged particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair as a function of participant number for distinguishing between theoretical models.

## Method

The authors used the JPCIAE (Jet Plus Cascade In A E) model, which is based on PYTHIA for hadron-hadron collisions and includes a cascade of nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-produced particle, and produced particle-produced particle collisions. Nucleon positions in the nucleus were sampled according to a Woods-Saxon distribution, and collisions were selected by least collision time with a requirement that the distance of closest approach be smaller than sqrt(sigma_tot/pi). For collisions with center-of-mass energy above 4 GeV, strings were formed and PYTHIA handled particle production including QCD parton-parton scatterings; below that threshold, two-body collisions were used. The participant nucleon number was computed using geometry methods, the Glauber model, and dynamical simulations (FRITIOF, HIJING, JPCIAE, AMPT), and the effect of using either a diffused or sharp nuclear radius was examined.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair at mid-pseudorapidity in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_nn)=130 GeV: JPCIAE reproduces PHENIX and PHOBOS data fairly well (no single number given for the data value itself; the model matches the observed trend).
- For Au+Au at sqrt(s_nn)=130 GeV, the mapping between centrality bin and impact parameter b uses b_max = R_A + R_B with R_Au = 7.5 fm for diffused radius (corresponding to sigma_geo = 7.2 b) and R_Au = 6.50 fm for sharp radius (R_A = 1.12 A^{1/3} fm).
- The nuclear diffusion edge parameter alpha is approximately 0.54 fm in the Woods-Saxon distribution.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used in the Glauber model at RHIC energies is sigma_in approximately 40 mb.
- For the most central bin (below 6% for PHOBOS, below 5% for PHENIX) with diffused radius: b below 3.67 fm, average b = 2.45 fm; with sharp radius: b below 3.20 fm, average b = 2.13 fm.
- Rescattering in JPCIAE leads to only a few percent increase in charged multiplicity at sqrt(s_nn)=130 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of charged particle pseudorapidity density using the JPCIAE model, which had previously been applied to AA collisions, and compares with results from the EKRT model (Eskola et al.), the KN model (Kharzeev and Nardi), HIJING (Wang et al.), and the Dual Parton Model (Capes et al.). The paper shows that the observed slight increase of charged particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair with participant number, reported by PHENIX and PHOBOS, can be reproduced by JPCIAE, but argues that the model dependence of participant number extraction makes this observable unsuitable for distinguishing between theoretical models. The work enables future studies to focus on other observables less sensitive to the definition of participant number, and suggests that using a diffused nuclear radius is preferable to a sharp one when mapping centrality to impact parameter.
