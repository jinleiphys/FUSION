# Analysis of flux-integrated cross sections for quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering off $^{12}$C at MiniBooNE energies

> Flux-averaged and flux-integrated cross sections for quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering on nucleus are analyzed. It is shown that the flux-integrated differential cross sections are nuclear model-independent. We calculate these cross sections using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation and relativistic Fermi gas model with the Booster Neutrino Beamline flux and compare results with the recent MiniBooNE experiment data. Within these models an axial mass $M_A$ is extracted from a fit of the measured $dσ/dQ^2$ cross section. The extracted value of $M_A$ is consistent with the MiniBooNE result. The measured and calculated double differential cross sections $dσ/dTd\cosθ$ generally agree within the error of the experiment. But the Fermi gas model predictions are completely off of the data in the region of low muon energies and scattering angles.

## Key claim
The paper shows that flux-integrated differential cross sections for quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering are nuclear model-independent, unlike flux-averaged cross sections which depend on the nuclear model through the flux normalization. The extracted axial mass values from fits to the MiniBooNE data are consistent with the MiniBooNE result, but the relativistic Fermi gas model predictions fail completely in the region of low muon energies and scattering angles.

## Method
The author calculated flux-integrated cross sections using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) and the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM) with the Booster Neutrino Beamline flux. For the RDWIA, bound nucleon wave functions were obtained from the sigma-omega model with occupancy factors from JLab electron scattering data, and final-state interactions were treated using a relativistic optical potential with only the real part for inclusive cross sections. The axial mass M_A was extracted from a shape-only fit to the measured flux-integrated dσ/dQ² cross section, and then used to calculate double differential cross sections d²σ/dTdcosθ for comparison with MiniBooNE data.

## Key numbers
- Extracted axial mass M_A = 1.37 ± 0.05 GeV/c² from RDWIA fit to MiniBooNE flux-integrated dσ/dQ² data
- Extracted axial mass M_A = 1.36 ± 0.05 GeV/c² from RFGM fit to same data
- MiniBooNE result: M_A = 1.35 ± 0.17 GeV/c² with κ = 1.007 ± 0.012 from shape-only fit
- Measured flux-integrated total cross section: 8.447 × 10⁻³⁹ cm² (for T ≥ 0.2 GeV, -1 ≤ cosθ ≤ 1)
- RDWIA total cross section: 8.208 × 10⁻³⁹ cm²; RFGM total cross section: 8.310 × 10⁻³⁹ cm²
- Measured total cross section for range -1 < cosθ < 1, 0 < T < 2 GeV: 9.429 × 10⁻³⁹ cm²
- Total normalization error on MiniBooNE measurement: 10.7%
- Occupancy of IPSM orbitals of ¹²C: average 89%, with S(1p₃/₂) = 84% and S(1s₁/₂) = 100%

## Context
This work extends the analysis of MiniBooNE CCQE data (MiniBooNE Collaboration, 2009) by testing both the RDWIA and RFGM predictions against the flux-integrated differential cross sections. The RDWIA approach successfully resolves the so-called low-Q² problem that plagued the RFGM description, where the RFGM overestimates the cross section at Q² ≤ 0.2 (GeV/c)². The results enable more reliable model comparisons for future experiments such as MINERvA, MicroBooNE, T2K, and NOvA, which require accurate characterization of CCQE cross sections for precision oscillation measurements.
