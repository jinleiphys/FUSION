# Psi(2S) production in proton-proton collisions at RHIC, Tevatron and LHC energies

> We briefly review the existing psi(2S) data taken at RHIC, the Tevatron and the LHC. We systematically compare them with colour-singlet-model predictions as a function of the center-of-mass energy, of the quarkonium rapidity and of the quarkonium transverse momentum. The overall agreement is good except for large transverse momenta. This points at the existence of large NNLO corrections or points at colour-octet dominance.

## Key claim

The colour-singlet model (CSM) predictions for ψ(2S) production show good overall agreement with data from RHIC, the Tevatron, and the LHC as a function of center-of-mass energy and rapidity, but the agreement fails at large transverse momenta, which may indicate either large NNLO corrections or colour-octet dominance.

## Method

The author compared existing ψ(2S) production data from PHENIX (RHIC), CDF (Tevatron), and LHCb and CMS (LHC) with CSM predictions at LO, NLO, and approximate NNLO (NNLO*) accuracy. The cross sections were computed using the CSM with a wave function at the origin |R(0)|² = 0.67 GeV³ for the ψ(2S), with uncertainty bands from combined variations of the charm-quark mass (1.5 ± 0.1 GeV), factorisation and renormalisation scales, and the CTEQ6 parton distribution functions. NLO and NNLO* predictions were only computed for sufficiently large transverse momenta due to instability at low PT.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (√s = 200 GeV), the PT-integrated cross section at y=0 from PHENIX data is compared to LO CSM predictions.
- At the Tevatron (√s = 1960 GeV), the CDF data give σ(|y| < 0.6, PT > 2 GeV) × Br = 2.6 ± 0.1 nb and σ(|y| < 0.6, PT < 2 GeV) × Br = 2.1 ± 0.1 nb.
- For the ψ(2S), |R(0)|² = 0.67 GeV³ was used, compared to 1.01 GeV³ for the J/ψ.
- The branching ratio Br(ψ(2S) → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻) = 0.0075 was used.
- Below PT = 15 GeV, the upper uncertainty band of the NNLO* CSM predictions agrees with the data across all collider energies.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous CSM comparisons for J/ψ and Υ production (Lansberg 2010, Artoisenet 2008, Lansberg 2008) to the ψ(2S) state, confirming that the PT-integrated yield is well described without colour-octet contributions. The observed discrepancy at large PT points to the need for a full NNLO CSM calculation, and the results suggest that low-PT quarkonium data could be used to constrain gluon parton distribution functions, as also noted by Brodsky (2009) and Lansberg (2010).
