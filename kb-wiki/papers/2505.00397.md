# Relativistic orbital-free kinetic energy density functional for one-particle nuclear systems

> This letter aims to derive the exact relativistic orbital-free kinetic energy density functional for one-particle nuclear systems in one-dimensional case. The kinetic energy is expressed as a functional of both vector and scalar densities. The functional derivatives of the kinetic energy density functional are also derived. Both the kinetic energy density functional and its functional derivatives are validated to be correct. This serves as a foundation for further exploration of more general relativistic orbital-free kinetic energy density functionals.

## Key claim

The paper derives the exact relativistic orbital-free kinetic energy density functional for one-particle nuclear systems in one dimension, but only for this specific case; the functional is expressed in terms of both vector and scalar densities, and its correctness is validated numerically for a Woods-Saxon potential.

## Method

Starting from the one-dimensional Dirac equation with vector and scalar potentials, the authors express the kinetic energy density in terms of the vector density ρ_v and scalar density ρ_s. They handle the sign-changing point of the lower-component wavefunction ψ₂ by relating the sign factor S_ign to the derivative of ρ₊ = ρ_v + ρ_s. The functional derivatives with respect to ρ₊ and ρ₋ = ρ_v − ρ_s are derived, and their finite limits at the sign-changing point are obtained via Taylor expansion. The functional and its derivatives are verified by numerically solving the Dirac equation for a Woods-Saxon potential and comparing the kinetic energy and the Euler-Lagrange equations.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic energy from wavefunction: T^wavefunction = 0.775780 MeV
- Kinetic energy from the derived functional: T^functional = 0.775773 MeV (consistent within numerical error)
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: U₀ = −67 MeV, x₀ = 5.5 fm, a₀ = 0.6 fm, λ = 11

## Context

This work provides the first exact relativistic orbital-free kinetic energy density functional, extending the non-relativistic von Weizsäcker functional (Weizsaecker 1935) to the relativistic Dirac framework. It serves as a foundation for developing more general relativistic orbital-free KEDFs, which could enable linear-scaling calculations for large nuclear systems such as neutron-star crusts, where the current Kohn-Sham relativistic DFT scales as O(N³).
