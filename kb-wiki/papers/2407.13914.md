# Qutrit and Qubit Circuits for Three-Flavor Collective Neutrino Oscillations

> We explore the utility of qutrits and qubits for simulating the flavor dynamics of dense neutrino systems. The evolution of such systems impacts some important astrophysical processes, such as core-collapse supernovae and the nucleosynthesis of heavy nuclei. Many-body simulations require classical resources beyond current computing capabilities for physically relevant system sizes. Quantum computers are therefore a promising candidate to efficiently simulate the many-body dynamics of collective neutrino oscillations. Previous quantum simulation efforts have primarily focused on properties of the two-flavor approximation due to their direct mapping to qubits. Here, we present new quantum circuits for simulating three-flavor neutrino systems on qutrit- and qubit-based platforms, and demonstrate their feasibility by simulating systems of two, four and eight neutrinos on IBM and Quantinuum quantum computers.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both qutrit-based and qubit-based quantum circuits can feasibly simulate three-flavor collective neutrino oscillations for systems of up to eight neutrinos on current quantum hardware, but the results are limited to the forward-scattering approximation and require error mitigation techniques such as decoherence renormalization to obtain reliable observables from noisy intermediate-scale quantum devices.

## Method

The authors map each three-flavor neutrino to either a single qutrit (using Gell-Mann matrices as generators) or to two qubits (with the unphysical |11⟩ state excluded), then implement the Hamiltonian containing vacuum oscillation and neutrino-neutrino self-interaction terms using Trotterized time evolution. They construct swap networks to achieve all-to-all connectivity with linear depth, and run the circuits on IBM ibm_torino and Quantinuum H1-1 quantum computers for N=2, 4, and 8 neutrinos, applying decoherence renormalization error mitigation with Pauli twirling and dynamical decoupling on the IBM device.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutrino qutrit circuit uses 4 CX gates per two-body interaction with depth 4 on both all-to-all and linear chain architectures
- Qubit circuit B uses 18 CNOT gates per two-body interaction with depth 12 on all-to-all architecture, and 30 CNOTs with depth 25 on linear chain
- PMNS mixing angles used: θ12 = 33.67°, θ23 = 42.3°, θ13 = 8.58°, δCP = 232°
- Mass splittings: Δm²21 = 7.41 × 10⁻¹⁷ MeV², Δm²31 = 2.505 × 10⁻¹⁵ MeV²
- Systems simulated: N = 2, 4, and 8 neutrinos on both IBM and Quantinuum platforms

## Context

This work extends prior two-flavor quantum simulations of neutrino oscillations (Hall 2021, Illa 2022, Amitrano 2022) to the full three-flavor case, building on the theoretical framework of Balantekin (2006) for mapping neutrinos to spin-triplets using Gell-Mann matrices. The results enable future quantum simulations of collective neutrino oscillations in core-collapse supernovae with more neutrinos and beyond the forward-scattering approximation, though current NISQ devices still require significant error mitigation for reliable results.
