# Effect of Octupole correlations on Fission of Light Nuclei

> Fission of $^{180}$Hg produces mass asymmetric fragments which are expected to be influenced by deformed shell-effects at N=56 in the heavy fragment and Z=34 in the light fragment [G. Scamps and C. Simenel, arXiv:1904.01275 (2019)]. To investigate both shell-effects and to determine which one has the main influence on the asymmetry in the region of the $^{180}$Hg, we produce a systematic of Constraint-Hartree-Fock calculations in nuclei with similar N/Z ratio than the $^{178}$Pt. It is found that N=56 determines the asymmetry of systems in this region of the nuclear chart.

# Effect of Octupole correlations on Fission of Light Nuclei

## Key claim
The N=56 deformed shell gap in the heavy fragment, rather than the Z=34 gap in the light fragment, is the dominant factor determining fission fragment asymmetry in the region of ¹⁸⁰Hg, based on constrained Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy4d functional and a specific surface pairing interaction.

## Method
Self-consistent constrained Hartree-Fock plus BCS (CHF+BCS) calculations were performed using the ev8 code modified to allow only one plane of symmetry, with constraints on the quadrupole moment Q₂₀ and octupole moment Q₃₀. The Skyrme functional SLy4d was used with surface-type pairing strengths V₀ⁿⁿ = 1256 MeV·fm³ and V₀ᵖᵖ = 1462 MeV·fm³. A systematic survey was conducted across nine nuclei (¹⁷⁰W, ¹⁷⁴Os, ¹⁷⁸Pt, ¹⁸⁰Hg, ¹⁸²Hg, ¹⁸⁴Hg, ¹⁸⁸Pb, ¹⁹²Po, and ¹⁹⁶Rn) with N/Z ratios between 1.25 and 1.3, tracking the average proton and neutron numbers in fragments just before scission.

## Key numbers
- For all systems up to A=186, heavy fragments have N ≈ 56, while light fragment Z varies with no stabilization at Z ≈ 34.
- In ¹⁷⁸Pt, the energy gap between symmetric and asymmetric fission paths around scission (Q₂₀ ≈ 90 b) is about 5 MeV.
- In ¹⁷⁰W, the energy difference between symmetric and asymmetric paths is approximately 2.5 MeV, with N_H ≈ 54 and Z_L ≈ 32.5.
- In ¹⁹⁶Rn, the asymmetric mode yields N_H ≈ 60 and Z_L ≈ 38.5, while the symmetric path produces fragments with N ≈ 55.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of octupole-driven asymmetry in ¹⁸⁰Hg fission (Scamps and Simenel, 2019) and the earlier demonstration that octupole deformation explains the Z=54 peak in actinide fission (Scamps and Simenel, 2018). The results contradict the expectation that ¹⁸⁰Hg would fission symmetrically into two ⁹⁰Zr nuclei, and instead support the experimental finding of asymmetric fission (Andreyev et al., 2010). The systematic approach across nuclei with constant N/Z ratio enables disentangling the relative strengths of the N=56 and Z=34 shell gaps, showing that N=56 is the primary driver of asymmetry in this mass region.
