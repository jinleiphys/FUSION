# A hybrid-chiral soliton model with broken scale invariance for nuclear matter

> We present a model for describing nuclear matter at finite density based on quarks interacting with chiral fields, sigma and pion. The chiral Lagrangian also includes a logarithmic potential, associated with the breaking of scale invariance. We provide results for the soliton in vacuum and at finite density, using the Wigner-Seitz approximation. We show that the model can reach higher densities respect to the Linear-sigma model, up to approximately 3 rho_0 for m_sigma=1200 MeV.

## Key claim
The authors claim that a chiral Lagrangian including a logarithmic potential from broken scale invariance allows nuclear matter to reach higher densities (up to approximately 3 times nuclear saturation density) compared to the Linear-sigma model, but only for a specific sigma meson mass of 1200 MeV; the improvement is attributed entirely to the different dynamics imposed by the logarithmic potential on the chiral fields.

## Method
Quarks are coupled to chiral sigma and pion fields in a mean-field approximation, with the dilaton field frozen at its vacuum value. The hedgehog ansatz is used for the quark spinor and meson fields, and the Wigner-Seitz approximation is applied to describe nuclear matter at finite density by placing each soliton in a spherical cell of radius R with specific boundary conditions. Self-consistent solutions of the coupled field equations are obtained by decreasing the cell radius from the vacuum value (R=4 fm) down to the smallest radius for which solutions exist.

## Key numbers
- Soliton mass in vacuum: 1175.6 MeV (compared to experimental value of 1085 MeV)
- Isoscalar charge radius: (0.73 fm)^2 (experimental: (0.72 fm)^2)
- Isovector magnetic radius: (0.91 fm)^2 (experimental: (0.80 fm)^2)
- Axial coupling constant g_A: 1.27 (experimental: 1.26)
- Maximum density reached: approximately 3 rho_0 for m_sigma = 1200 MeV
- Vacuum energy parameter |epsilon_vac|^(1/4): 236 MeV
- Quark-meson coupling constant g: 5
- Sigma meson mass in vacuum: 550 MeV
- Pion decay constant sigma_0: 93 MeV
- Dilaton field vacuum value phi_0: 175.23 MeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Carter (1996, 1997, 1998) and Heide (1992, 1994) on scale invariance in QCD, and builds on the hadronic model of Bonanno (2008) that described nuclear physics near saturation density. The authors compare their results directly to the Linear-sigma model calculations of Weber (1998) and Birse (1985), showing that the logarithmic potential allows higher densities than the Mexican hat potential. The model enables future studies including vector mesons for repulsion at high density, accurate band structure calculations in the soliton crystal, and finite-temperature studies that could recover the phase diagram proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007).
