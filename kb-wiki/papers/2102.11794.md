# Newly Evaluated Neutron Reaction Data on Chromium Isotopes

> Neutron reaction data for the set of major chromium isotopes were reevaluated from the thermal energy range up to 20 MeV. In the low energy region, updates to the thermal values together with an improved $R$-matrix analysis of the resonance parameters characterizing the cluster of large $s$-wave resonances for $^{50,53}$Cr isotopes were performed. In the intermediate and high energy range up to 20 MeV, the evaluation methodology used statistical nuclear reaction models implemented in the EMPIRE code within the Hauser-Feshbach framework to evaluate the reaction cross sections and angular distributions. Exceptionally, experimental data were used to evaluate relevant cross sections above the resonance region up to 5 MeV in the major $^{52}$Cr isotope. Evaluations were benchmarked with Monte Carlo simulations of a small suite of critical assemblies highly sensitive to Chromium data, and with the Oktavian shielding benchmark to judge deep penetration performance with a 14-MeV D-T neutron source. A significant improvement in performance is demonstrated compared to existing evaluations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent set of resonance parameters for chromium isotopes, derived by fitting neutron capture yields (not cross sections) with proper multiple-scattering and self-shielding corrections, significantly improves agreement with integral criticality and shielding benchmarks compared to existing evaluations in ENDF/B-VIII.0 and BROND-3.1, though unresolved discrepancies between the Stieglitz et al. (1971) and Guber et al. (2011) capture yield data sets remain unexplained.

## Method

The authors performed a reevaluation of neutron reaction data for the stable chromium isotopes 50,52,53,54Cr from thermal energies up to 20 MeV using an improved R-matrix analysis (via the SAMMY code) of resonance parameters in the resolved resonance region, with particular attention to multiple-scattering and self-shielding corrections for the cluster of s-wave resonances in 50,53Cr. For the intermediate and high energy range up to 20 MeV, statistical nuclear reaction models within the Hauser-Feshbach framework implemented in the EMPIRE code were used to evaluate cross sections and angular distributions, except for 52Cr where experimental data were used above the resonance region up to 5 MeV. The evaluations were benchmarked with Monte Carlo simulations of critical assemblies (including the KBR-15 assembly) and the Oktavian shielding benchmark with a 14-MeV D-T neutron source.

## Key numbers

- The measured capture yield data from Guber et al. (2011) for 53Cr were found to be affected by a mistake in the sample density calculation; after correction, the capture yield in EXFOR (subentry 14324006) is decreased by a factor of two relative to the data published in 2011.
- The sample thickness for the Guber et al. (2011) 53Cr oxide measurement was corrected from n=0.0137 atom/barn to n=0.0133 atom/barn, a reduction of 3%.
- The discrepancy between finite-size and infinite-slab approximations for multiple-scattering corrections was about 15% for the natural metal sample and about 30% for the oxide enriched samples.
- The largest disagreement in the most chromium-sensitive critical assembly (KBR-15, HEU-MET INTER-015) was 11% when using the ENDF/B-VII.1 or ENDF/B-VIII.0 library, traced to underestimated neutron capture on natural chromium in the keV region.
- The 53Cr capture yield data from Stieglitz et al. (1971) and Guber et al. (2011) differ by a constant value of about 50% in the energy region of the cluster of s-wave resonances between 1-10 keV.

## Context

This work extends the chromium evaluation efforts that followed the CIELO project's reevaluation of 56Fe, which identified chromium uncertainties as a major unresolved issue for steel performance in zero-power reactor simulations (Brown et al., 2018). It addresses the poor description of the cluster of large s-wave resonances in 53Cr near 5 keV that affected all major nuclear data libraries, and corrects the earlier BROND-3.1 evaluation by Koscheev et al. (2017) which had incorrectly interpreted digitized capture yield data as cross sections, leading to an overestimated thermal capture cross section. The new evaluations enable improved modeling of neutron leakage and criticality safety for stainless steel applications, and provide a foundation for future work to resolve the remaining discrepancies between the Stieglitz et al. (1971) and Guber et al. (2011) measurements.
