# Bayesian calibration of viscous anisotropic hydrodynamic (VAH) simulations of heavy-ion collisions

> A Bayesian calibration, using experimental data from 2.76 $A$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, of a novel hybrid model is presented in which the usual pre-hydrodynamic and viscous relativistic fluid dynamic (vRFD) stages are replaced by a viscous anisotropic hydrodynamic (VAH) core that smoothly interpolates between the initial expansion-dominated, approximately boost-invariant longitudinally free-streaming and the subsequent collision-dominated (3+1)-dimensional standard vRFD stages. This model yields meaningful constraints for the temperature-dependent specific shear and bulk viscosities, $(η/s)(T)$ and $(ζ/s)(T)$, for temperatures up to about $700$ MeV (i.e. over twice the range that could be explored with earlier models). With its best-fit model parameters the calibrated VAH model makes highly successful predictions for additional $p_T$-dependent observables for which high-quality experimental data are available that were not used for the model calibration.

## Key claim
The VAH hybrid model, which replaces the separate pre-hydrodynamic free-streaming and viscous relativistic fluid dynamic stages with a single viscous anisotropic hydrodynamic core, yields meaningful constraints for the temperature-dependent specific shear and bulk viscosities up to about 700 MeV, over twice the temperature range that could be explored with earlier models such as JETSCAPE SIMS.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian calibration of the VAH model using experimental data from 2.76 A TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The VAH model smoothly interpolates between an initial expansion-dominated, approximately boost-invariant longitudinally free-streaming stage and the subsequent collision-dominated (3+1)-dimensional standard viscous relativistic fluid dynamic stage, eliminating the free-streaming time parameter and an associated discontinuity in the equation of state. The calibrated model was then tested by predicting pT-differential observables (identified hadron spectra and anisotropic flows) that were not used in the calibration, and compared against predictions from the JETSCAPE SIMS model with three different particlization prescriptions (GRAD, CE, and PTB).

## Key numbers
- The VAH model yields constraints for specific shear and bulk viscosities up to temperatures of about 700 MeV, more than double the temperature range accessible with earlier models.
- The calibration used experimental data from 2.76 A TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.
- The VAH model predictions for pT-spectra of pions, kaons, and protons were tested at three collision centralities: 0-5%, 5-10%, and 10-20%.
- The pT-differential elliptic (v2), triangular (v3), and quadrangular (v4) flow predictions were tested at 20-30% centrality.
- The Kullback-Leibler divergence D_KL, quantifying information gain from experimental data, is non-zero up to around 700 MeV in the VAH model.

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian calibration approach previously applied to the JETSCAPE SIMS model (Bernhard 2016, Bernhard 2018, JETSCAPE 2020) by replacing the free-streaming plus viscous relativistic fluid dynamic stages with a single viscous anisotropic hydrodynamic module. The VAH approach builds on the observation by Jaiswal (2021) that for Bjorken flow, which approximates the first 1-2 fm/c in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, VAH accurately reproduces the microscopic kinetic evolution of the full energy-momentum tensor. The calibrated VAH model shows consistently better agreement with experimental data across collision centralities compared to JETSCAPE SIMS, suggesting that more accurate modeling of the far-off-equilibrium early evolution stage is important for heavy-ion collision simulations.
