# S-matrix and R-matrix determination of the low-energy He-5 and Li-5 resonance parameters

> We study the low-energy 3/2- and 1/2- states of He-5 and Li-5 in a microscopic cluster model. The scattering phase shifts of Bond (alpha+n) and of Schwandt (alpha+p), respectively, are reproduced well. We determine the resonance parameters by localizing the poles of the analytically continued S-matrix at complex energies. Our results differ from conventional R-matrix resonance parameters, which were extracted from experimental data using the definition of a resonance based on the positions and widths of reaction cross section peaks. However, they nicely agree with the results of an extended R-matrix method which works at complex energies.

## Key claim

The resonance parameters (position and width) of the low-energy 3/2- and 1/2- states in He-5 and Li-5, when determined from the complex-energy poles of the S-matrix, differ from conventional R-matrix parameters extracted from real-energy cross section peaks, but agree with an extended R-matrix method that also works at complex energies.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic alpha+N Resonating Group Method (RGM) with the Minnesota effective N-N interaction to reproduce the alpha+n scattering phase shifts of Bond (1977) and alpha+p phase shifts of Schwandt (1971). They determined resonance parameters by analytically continuing the S-matrix to complex energies and searching for poles, solving the Schrodinger equation for alpha-N relative motion at complex energies with outgoing Coulomb wave boundary conditions. They also extracted resonance parameters using the extended R-matrix method from multichannel analyses of reactions in the A=5 systems, including two-body channels N+alpha and d+t or d+He-3 along with pseudo two-body breakup configurations.

## Key numbers

* He-5 3/2- state: S-matrix pole RGM gives E_r = 0.76 MeV, Gamma = 0.63 MeV; extended R-matrix gives E_r = 0.80 MeV, Gamma = 0.65 MeV
* He-5 1/2- state: S-matrix pole RGM gives E_r = 1.89 MeV, Gamma = 5.20 MeV; extended R-matrix gives E_r = 2.07 MeV, Gamma = 5.57 MeV
* Li-5 3/2- state: S-matrix pole RGM gives E_r = 1.67 MeV, Gamma = 1.33 MeV; extended R-matrix gives E_r = 1.69 MeV, Gamma = 1.23 MeV
* Li-5 1/2- state: S-matrix pole RGM gives E_r = 2.70 MeV, Gamma = 6.25 MeV; extended R-matrix gives E_r = 3.18 MeV, Gamma = 6.60 MeV
* When Barker's n-alpha R-function parameters were used in the S-matrix pole prescription, the resulting resonance parameters (E_{3/2-}=0.77 MeV, Gamma_{3/2-}=0.65 MeV; E_{1/2-}=2.10 MeV, Gamma_{1/2-}=5.37 MeV) agreed with the RGM and extended R-matrix values
* Recent work by Efros and Oberhummer (1996) gave ground state parameters from S-matrix poles: He-5 3/2- E_r = 0.8 +/- 0.02 MeV, Gamma = 0.65 +/- 0.02 MeV; Li-5 3/2- E_r = 1.72 +/- 0.03 MeV, Gamma = 1.28 +/- 0.03 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ahmed and Shanley (1976), who first suggested extracting alpha+N resonance parameters from observables continued to complex energies, and the extended R-matrix method of Hale, Brown, and Jarmie (1987). The results contradict the conventional R-matrix resonance parameters of Barker and Woods (1985, 1988), which were based on real-energy cross section peak definitions and showed strong dependence on channel radius and reaction mechanism. The paper demonstrates that the S-matrix pole prescription gives consistent resonance parameters independent of the method used to describe nuclear dynamics or the reaction in which the resonance is observed, and recommends using this prescription for specifying resonance parameters in all cases, especially for broad resonances in light systems.
