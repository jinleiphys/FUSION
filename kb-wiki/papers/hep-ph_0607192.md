# Replay to hep-ph/0606266

> The ϕ\toγππamplitude is found from the e+e-\toγππamplitude suggested in Ref. hep-ph/0606266. It is shown that the found amplitude differs essentially from that studied in Refs. hep-ph/0606266 and [3]. It is noticed that the suggestion of the author of Ref. hep-ph/0606266 about the phase of the ϕ\toγππamplitude is misleading.

## Key claim

The $\phi\to\gamma\pi\pi$ amplitude derived from the $e^+e^-\to\gamma\pi\pi$ amplitude proposed by Anisovich (2006) differs essentially from the amplitude studied in that work and in Anisovich (2005), and the suggestion about the phase of the $\phi\to\gamma\pi\pi$ amplitude equaling the $\pi\pi$ scattering phase shift is misleading because the Watson theorem does not apply when other open intermediate channels (such as $\phi\to K^+K^-\to\gamma\pi\pi$) are present.

## Method

The author rewrites the $e^+e^-\to\gamma\pi\pi$ amplitude from Anisovich (2006) including resonance widths, applies the regularity condition at $s_V=s_S$, and solves the resulting constraint to extract the $\phi\to\gamma\pi\pi$ amplitude. The derived amplitude is then compared analytically and numerically with the amplitude from Anisovich (2006) and Anisovich (2005), using the same parameter values for the $f_0$ resonance and background functions.

## Context

This work is a direct reply to Anisovich (2006) and extends the critique of Anisovich (2005) regarding the treatment of the $\phi\to\gamma\pi\pi$ decay amplitude in the context of $e^+e^-\to\gamma\pi\pi$ processes. The author shows that the backgrounds in the Anisovich amplitude are not uniquely determined by resonance properties but are instead phenomenological additions, and that the claimed phase relation with $\pi\pi$ scattering is invalid due to the $\phi\to K^+K^-\to\gamma\pi\pi$ channel. This enables future experimental investigations of the $\phi\to\gamma\pi\pi$ decay to distinguish between the competing amplitude formulations.
