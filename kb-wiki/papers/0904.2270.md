# Dynamical Density Fluctuations around QCD Critical Point Based on Dissipative Relativistic Fluid Dynamics-possible fate of Mach cone at the critical point-

> The purpose of this paper is twofold. Firstly, we study the dynamical density fluctuations around the critical point(CP) of Quantum Chromodynamics(QCD) using dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics in which the coupling of the density fluctuations to those of other conserved quantities is taken into account. We show that the sound mode which is directly coupled to the mechanical density fluctuation is attenuated and in turn the thermal mode becomes the genuine soft mode at the QCD CP. We give a speculation on the possible fate of a Mach cone in the vicinity of the QCD CP as a signal of the existence of the CP on the basis of the above findings. Secondly, we clarify that the so called first-order relativistic fluid dynamic equations have generically no problem to describe fluid dynamic phenomena with long wave lengths contrary to a naive suspect whereas even Israel-Stewart equation, a popular second-order equation, may not describe the hydrodynamic mode in general depending on the value of the relaxation time.

## Key claim

The authors show that at the QCD critical point, the sound mode (which is directly coupled to mechanical density fluctuations) becomes attenuated and dies out, while the diffusive thermal mode becomes the genuine soft mode; this conclusion is based on an extrapolation from the fluid dynamical regime ($k\xi \ll 1$) to the critical region ($k\xi \gg 1$), which is known to work for non-relativistic fluids.

## Method

The authors derived the spectral function of dynamical density fluctuations by linearizing dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics equations (Landau, Eckart, Tsumura-Kunihiro-Ohnishi, and Israel-Stewart) around thermal equilibrium, then performing Fourier-Laplace transformations to obtain the time-dependent density fluctuation. They analyzed how the singular behavior of thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients near the QCD critical point affects the widths of the Rayleigh peak (thermal mode) and Brillouin peaks (sound mode) in the density fluctuation spectrum.

## Key numbers

- For a massless classical ideal gas EoS, the relativistic correction to the Brillouin peak width in the Landau equation is $\delta \Gamma_{\rm B}^{\rm La} = -\kappa/(24 n_0)$, showing the thermal conductivity contribution cancels with the minimal relativistic part.
- For the TKO equation in the particle frame with massless ideal gas EoS, the relativistic correction to the Brillouin peak width is $\delta \Gamma_{\rm B}^{\rm TKO} = -(1/(24 n_0))(\kappa + 3\zeta'/T_0)$.
- The Rayleigh peak width is $\Gamma_{\rm R} = \chi$, the thermal diffusivity, identical to the non-relativistic case.
- The Brillouin peak width in the Landau equation is $\Gamma_{\rm B} = \frac{1}{2}[\chi(\gamma-1)+\nu_l + c_s^2 T_0 (\kappa/w_0 - 2\chi \alpha_P)]$, where $\nu_l = (\zeta + 4\eta/3)/w_0$.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic analysis of critical dynamics (Stanley, Kawasaki, Onuki) to the relativistic case relevant for QCD, and builds on the finding by Fujii and Ohtani that both thermal mode and density oscillation play roles as soft modes at the QCD critical point. The authors' analysis suggests that the divergent behavior of bulk viscosity (Karsch, Moore) may not be observable through density fluctuations, and that the suppression or disappearance of Mach cones in relativistic heavy-ion collisions could serve as a signal for the existence of the QCD critical point.
