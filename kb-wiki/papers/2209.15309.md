# NLO finite system size corrections to $2\to2$ scattering in $ϕ^4$ theory using newly derived sum of sinc functions

> Previously an equation of state for the relativistic hydrodynamics encountered in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC has been calculated using lattice gauge theory methods. This leads to a prediction of very low viscosity, due to the calculated trace anomaly. Finite system corrections to this trace anomaly could challenge this calculation, since the lattice calculation was done in an effectively infinite system. In order to verify this trace anomaly it is sensible to add phenomenologically relevant finite system corrections. We investigate massive $ϕ^4$ theory with periodic boundary conditions on $n$ of the 3 spatial dimensions. $2\to2$ NLO scattering is then computed. Using a newly derived formula for an arbitrary dimensional sum of sinc functions, we show that the NLO finite size corrections preserve unitarity.

## Key claim

The authors show that next-to-leading-order (NLO) finite-size corrections to \(2\to2\) scattering in massive \(\phi^4\) theory preserve unitarity (the optical theorem holds) regardless of how many spatial dimensions are compactified, provided the system has periodic boundary conditions on \(n\) of the 3 spatial dimensions.

## Method

The authors consider massive \(\phi^4\) theory with periodic boundary conditions on \(n\) of the three spatial dimensions, discretizing the corresponding momenta. They compute the NLO scattering amplitude \(\mathcal{M}\) for \(2\to2\) scattering, obtaining the renormalized vertex function \(\overline{V}(p^2,\{L_i\})\) which contains an infinite-volume logarithmic term plus a finite-size correction expressed as a sum over modified Bessel functions \(K_0\). They then verify unitarity by showing the equivalence of the total cross section \(\sigma_\text{tot}\) and twice the imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude \(2\,\text{Im}[\mathcal{M}]\), using a newly derived formula for an arbitrary-dimensional sum of sinc functions.

## Key numbers

* The trace anomaly in QCD is relevant just above the transition temperature \(T = 180\) MeV.
* The total cross section at NLO is given by \(\sigma_{tot} = \frac{\lambda^2}{16\pi}\frac{\pi^\frac{1-m}{2}}{\Gamma\left(\frac{3-m}{2}\right) }\frac{1}{L\sqrt{s}} \,\sideset{}{^*}\sum_{0\leq l<R^2} \frac{r_m(l)}{\sqrt{R^2-l}^{m-1}}\), where \(m\) is the number of finite dimensions and \(L\) is the compactification length.
* The imaginary part of the amplitude at NLO is \(2\,\text{Im}[\mathcal{M}]=\frac{\lambda^2}{16\pi}\frac{2R}{L\sqrt{s}}\frac{1}{2 R \pi^\frac{m-1}{2} \,\Gamma\left(\frac{3-m}{2}\right)}\,\sideset{}{^*}\sum_{0\leq l<R^2}\frac{r_m(l)}{\sqrt{R^2-l}^{m-1}}\).
* The equivalence of these two expressions demonstrates that the optical theorem holds independent of the number of compact dimensions.

## Context

This work extends the study of finite-size effects in quantum field theory, motivated by lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state for relativistic hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC. Prior lattice calculations (e.g., PhysRevC.87.064906) were performed in effectively infinite systems, while quenched lattice QCD calculations by Kitazawa (2019) showed significant possible corrections dependent on system size. The authors develop mathematical techniques (generalizing a formula of Ramanujan for the \(m=2\) case) that are intended as a step toward a full treatment of finite-temperature, finite-size QCD, enabling analytic derivation of finite-size corrections to the trace anomaly and equation of state.
