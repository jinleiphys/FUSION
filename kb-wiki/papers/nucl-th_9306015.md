# Exotic Heavy Baryons at LHC?

> We speculate about a heavy bottom-charm six-quark baryon. A semiclassical and a gaussian estimate reveal that the octet-octet bbb-ccc configuration can be energetically favored with respect to the singlet-singlet one. This result suggests that a confined bbb-ccc six-quark state may exist. Such objects may be produced in appreciable amount in heavy ion collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper speculates that a confined six-quark bbb-ccc dibaryon in an octet-octet color configuration may be energetically favored over the singlet-singlet configuration, but the authors state they have not yet found a decisive quantitative result and that the possibility cannot be excluded based on phenomenological knowledge of heavy meson masses.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic potential model with a Hamiltonian containing quark rest masses, kinetic energy terms, and a color-color interaction including a linear confining term and a Coulomb-like term. They performed two estimates: a semiclassical calculation using a fit to the J/ψ meson, and a variational calculation using a gaussian wave function ansatz, both applied to the bbb-ccc system with the bbb core treated as a single heavy particle in either a color singlet or octet state.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ mass: 3.096 GeV (experimental value used for fit), with string tension σ = 0.192 GeV² and coupling α = 0.47, charm quark mass m = 1.32 GeV
- Semiclassical estimate: singlet core configuration gives E¹ = 4.71 GeV at r = 2.15 GeV⁻¹; octet core gives E⁸ = 4.78 GeV at r = 1.6 GeV⁻¹
- Gaussian estimate with standard parameters (σ = 0.192 GeV², α = 0.47): J/ψ = 3.09 GeV, Ω_c = 4.54 GeV, bbb-ccc (8,6) = 4.57 GeV, bbb-ccc (8,3̅) = 4.74 GeV (not counting 15 GeV bbb rest mass)
- Gaussian estimate with altered parameters (σ = 0.22 GeV², α = 0.54): J/ψ = 3.097 GeV, Ω_c = 4.544 GeV, bbb-ccc (8,6) = 4.515 GeV, bbb-ccc (8,3̅) = 4.716 GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark combinatoric model for multi-heavy baryon production in heavy-ion collisions, which includes a phenomenological penalty factor for charm and bottom flavors. The authors reference lattice QCD potential calculations and heavy meson physics studies of octet confining forces, and note that such exotic baryons could be observed through like-charge muon triplets at LHC energies. The paper calls for more advanced calculations to resolve whether the bbb-ccc system is energetically bound.
