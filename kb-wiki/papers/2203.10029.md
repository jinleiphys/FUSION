# Scaling properties of background- and chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV

> The Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics model, AVFD, is used in concert with the charge-sensitive correlator $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ to investigate the scaling properties of background- and chiral-magnetically-driven (CME) charge separation ($ΔS$), characterized by the inverse variance $\mathrm{σ^{-2}_{R_{Ψ_2}}}$ of the $R_{Ψ_{2}}(ΔS)$ distributions obtained in collisions at $\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV. The $\mathrm{σ^{-2}_{R_{Ψ_2}}}$ values for the background are observed to be event-shape-independent. However, they scale with the reciprocal charged-particle multiplicity $(1/\left< N_{\rm ch} \right>)$, indicating an essential constraint for discerning background from the signal and a robust estimate of the difference between the backgrounds in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions. By contrast, the $\mathrm{σ^{-2}_{R_{Ψ_2}}}$values for signal + background show characteristic $1/\left< N_{\rm ch} \right>$ scaling violations that characterize the CME-driven contributions. Corrections to recent ${R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)}$ measurements \cite{STAR:2021mii} that account for the background difference in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions indicate a charge separation difference compatible with the CME. The results further suggest that $\mathrm{σ^{-2}_{R_{Ψ_2}}}$ measurements for peripheral and central collisions in concert with $1/\left< N_{\rm ch} \right>$ scaling, provides a robust constraint to quantify the background and aid characterization of the CME.

## Key claim

The inverse variance of the $R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)$ distribution for background-driven charge separation scales linearly with $1/\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle$ and is independent of event shape, while signal-plus-background shows scaling violations that characterize the CME contribution; correcting recent STAR measurements for the background difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions using this scaling yields a charge separation difference compatible with the CME.

## Method

The Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model, with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions and VISHNU hydrodynamic evolution followed by a URQMD hadron cascade, was used to simulate Au+Au and isobar collisions at $\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV. The model incorporates charge-dependent backgrounds from local charge conservation (LCC) and resonance decays, and CME currents generated by a time-dependent magnetic field $B(\tau) = B_0/(1+(\tau/\tau_B)^2)$ with $\tau_B=0.6$ fm/c and an initial axial charge density $n_5/s$. The $R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)$ correlator was constructed using charged particles with $0.35<p_T<2.0$ GeV/$c$ and event-plane sub-events with $0.1<|\eta|<1.0$, with event-shape selection via $q_2$ flow vector cuts to study $v_2$ dependence.

## Key numbers

- For 30-40% central Au+Au collisions with $n_5/s=0.1$ and LCC=33%, the CME fraction $f_{\rm CME} \approx 60\%$, indicating the fraction of total charge separation attributable to the CME.
- Background $\sigma^{-2}_{R_{\Psi_2}}$ values scale linearly with $1/\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle$ across centrality selections, with no dependence on $q_2$ (event-shape selection) for either background or signal-plus-background.
- For the same centrality, $\sigma^{-2}_{R_{\Psi_2}}$ values for Ru+Ru collisions are larger than those for Au+Au collisions, indicating reduced sensitivity to the CME signal in isobar collisions.
- The $\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle$-scaled ratio $\sigma^{-1}_{\rm Ru+Ru}/\sigma^{-1}_{\rm Zr+Zr}$ exceeds 1.0 for several centrality selections, consistent with larger CME-driven charge separation in Ru+Ru than in Zr+Zr collisions.

## Context

This work extends AVFD model studies (Shi 2017, Jiang 2016) to systematically characterize the $R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)$ correlator's response to CME and background contributions, addressing ambiguities in recent STAR isobar measurements (STAR 2021). The finding that background $\sigma^{-2}_{R_{\Psi_2}}$ scales as $1/\langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle$ and is $v_2$-independent provides a method to quantify the background difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, enabling future CME searches in other collision systems at RHIC and the LHC.
