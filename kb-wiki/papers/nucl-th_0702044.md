# Neutrino Breakup of A=3 Nuclei in Supernovae

> We extend the virial equation of state to include 3H and 3He nuclei, and predict significant mass-three fractions near the neutrinosphere in supernovae. While alpha particles are often more abundant, we demonstrate that energy transfer cross-sections for muon and tau neutrinos at low densities are dominated by breakup of the loosely-bound 3H and 3He nuclei. The virial coefficients involving A=3 nuclei are calculated directly from the corresponding nucleon-3H and nucleon-3He scattering phase shifts. For the neutral-current inelastic cross-sections and the energy transfer cross sections, we perform ab-initio calculations based on microscopic two- and three-nucleon interactions and meson-exchange currents.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that mass-three nuclei (³H and ³He) can reach significant fractions (up to 10%) near the neutrinosphere in supernovae, and that their neutral-current inelastic energy transfer cross-sections dominate over those of alpha particles for muon and tau neutrinos at low densities, but only under the condition that the matter temperature is above approximately 4 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the virial equation of state to include ³H and ³He nuclei, calculating the second virial coefficients from nucleon-³H and nucleon-³He scattering phase shifts. For the neutral-current inclusive inelastic cross-sections and energy transfer cross-sections, they performed ab-initio calculations using the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions with meson-exchange currents, solving the three-nucleon continuum problem via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method with the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) approach.

## Key numbers

- Total mass-three fraction reaches up to approximately 0.1 (10%) in symmetric matter at a density of 10¹² g/cm³.
- At temperatures T ≥ 5 MeV and low proton fractions, the ³H mass fraction exceeds the alpha particle fraction.
- Averaged neutral-current inclusive inelastic cross-sections per nucleon for ³H at neutrino temperature 4 MeV: 2.68×10⁻⁴² cm²; at 6 MeV: 2.09×10⁻⁴¹ cm².
- Averaged energy transfer cross-sections per nucleon for ³H at neutrino temperature 4 MeV: 3.37×10⁻⁴² MeV cm²; at 6 MeV: 2.99×10⁻⁴² MeV cm².
- Cross-sections on mass-three nuclei are approximately a factor of 20 larger than on ⁴He at neutrino temperature 4 MeV, and a factor of 10 larger at 6 MeV.
- Energy transfer cross-sections on mass-three nuclei are approximately 8 times larger than on ⁴He at 4 MeV and 2 times larger at 6 MeV.
- Meson-exchange currents contribute about 16% to the cross-section at 1 MeV neutrino temperature, decreasing to less than 2% above 4 MeV.
- The asymmetry between neutrino and anti-neutrino energy transfer grows from 3% at 3 MeV to over 50% at 10 MeV neutrino temperature.

## Context

This work extends the virial equation of state approach of Horowitz and Schwenk (2006) and Horowitz et al. (2005) for matter composed of neutrons, protons, and alpha particles to include ³H and ³He nuclei. It builds on the inelastic neutrino-⁴He cross-section calculations of Gazit and Barnea (2007) and the earlier proposal by Haxton and Bruenn (1981) that muon and tau neutrinos can exchange energy via inelastic excitations of nuclei. The results enable supernova simulations to include the neutrino breakup of A=3 nuclei, which may improve the treatment of neutrino energy deposition and shock revival.
