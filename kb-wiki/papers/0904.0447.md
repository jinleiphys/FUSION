# A systematic study of the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions with the same neutron/proton ratio but different masses

> A systematic study of the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions with the same neutron/proton ratio but different masses can help single out effects of the nuclear mean field on pion production. Based on simulations using the IBUU04 transport model, it is found that the $π^-$ / $π^+$ ratio in head-on collisions of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, $^{124}$Sn +$^{124}$Sn and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at beam energies from 0.25 to 0.6 \agev increases with increasing the system size or decreasing the beam energies. A comprehensive analysis of the dynamical isospin fractionation and the \rpi ratio as well as their time evolution and spatial distributions demonstrates clearly that the \rpi ratio is an effective probe of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions is an effective probe of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, but only when comparing systems with the same neutron/proton ratio but different masses; the ratio's sensitivity to the symmetry energy increases with system size and decreases with beam energy, and the effect is dominated by the supra-saturation density region rather than sub-saturation densities.

## Method

The authors used the IBUU04 transport model with a modified Gogny Momentum Dependent Interaction (MDI) to simulate head-on collisions of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at beam energies from 0.25 to 0.6 GeV/nucleon. They varied the parameter $x$ in the symmetry energy to represent different stiffnesses (x=1 for soft, x=0 for intermediate resembling APR, x=-2 for stiff) and analyzed the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio, the degree of isospin fractionation ($R_{\pi}-R_{\rm isob}$), and the density and time evolution of $\Delta$ resonances. They also performed calculations with separate $x$ parameters for sub-saturation ($x_L$) and supra-saturation ($x_H$) densities to isolate the density region responsible for the signal.

## Key numbers

- The $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio in head-on collisions of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at beam energies from 0.25 to 0.6 GeV/nucleon increases with increasing system size or decreasing beam energy.
- The isospin asymmetries of the three systems are approximately the same: 1.40 for $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, 1.48 for $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn, and 1.49 for $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au.
- The sensitivity of the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio to the symmetry energy (defined as the double ratio of results with x=1 over x=0) increases from several percent to approximately twenty percent at the maximum isospin fractionation.
- Changing the $x$ parameter from -2 (stiff) to 1 (soft) at all densities changes the saturated $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio by about 7% in Au+Au collisions at 0.4 GeV/nucleon.
- Varying the sub-saturation $x_L$ between 1 and -2 while fixing $x_H$ changes the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio by less than 2%; varying $x_H$ while fixing $x_L$ produces about a 7% change.
- The majority of $\Delta$ resonances are produced in the high-density region, with a major peak around $2\rho_0$ and a minor bump around $0.1\rho_0$.
- The $N_{\Delta}$ reaches its maximum at about 20 fm/c when the colliding system is still in the compressed phase.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Zhang et al. (2008) that a soft symmetry energy (x=1) reproduces FOPI collaboration data (Reisdorf et al., 2007) better than the stiffer APR prediction (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) or the MDI x=0 case. By using systems with the same neutron/proton ratio but different masses, the authors isolate the effect of the nuclear mean field from initial isospin asymmetry. The results enable future experimental tests using heavy systems at relatively low beam energies to probe the high-density symmetry energy, while noting that in-medium effects on NN inelastic collisions (Larionov et al., 2001, 2003) and pions (Xu et al., 2009) deserve further study.
