# Investigation of the high momentum component of nuclear wave function using hard quasielastic A(p,2p)X reactions

> We present theoretical analysis of the first data on the high energy and momentum transfer (hard) quasielastic $C(p,2p)X$ reactions. The cross section of hard $A(p,2p)X$ reaction is calculated within the light-cone impulse approximation based on two-nucleon correlation model for the high-momentum component of the nuclear wave function. The nuclear effects due to modification of the bound nucleon structure, soft nucleon-nucleon reinteraction in the initial and final states of the reaction with and without color coherence have been considered. The calculations including these nuclear effects show that the distribution of the bound proton light-cone momentum fraction $(α)$ shifts towards small values ($α< 1$), effect which was previously derived only within plane wave impulse approximation. This shift is very sensitive to the strength of the short range correlations in nuclei. Also calculated is an excess of the total longitudinal momentum of outgoing protons. The calculations are compared with data on the $C(p,2p)X$ reaction obtained from the EVA/AGS experiment at Brookhaven National Laboratory. These data show $α$-shift in agreement with the calculations. The comparison allows also to single out the contribution from short-range nucleon correlations. The obtained strength of the correlations is in agreement with the values previously obtained from electroproduction reactions on nuclei.

# Investigation of the high momentum component of nuclear wave function using hard quasielastic A(p,2p)X reactions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hard quasielastic A(p,2p)X reactions at high energy and momentum transfer can probe short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations in nuclei, provided that competing nuclear effects (medium modification of bound nucleons, initial and final state interactions, and color transparency) are properly accounted for. The authors show that the observed shift of the bound proton light-cone momentum fraction α toward values less than 1, previously derived only within plane wave impulse approximation, survives when these nuclear effects are included, and that the α-distribution is largely insensitive to soft initial and final state reinteractions due to an approximate conservation law for the light-cone momentum component.

## Method

The cross section for hard A(p,2p)X reactions was calculated within the light-cone impulse approximation using a two-nucleon correlation model for the high-momentum component of the nuclear wave function. For target proton momenta below the Fermi sea level (p₂ < 250 MeV/c), the nonrelativistic limit of the light-cone spectral function was used with a mean-field momentum distribution; for momenta between the Fermi momentum and 0.7 GeV/c, the spectral function was modeled assuming dominance of two-nucleon short-range correlations, with the correlated pair density matrix set equal to the light-cone density matrix of the deuteron using the Paris potential. Nuclear effects considered included modification of bound nucleon structure via color screening (EMC effect), soft nucleon-nucleon reinteractions in initial and final states calculated within the eikonal approximation for uncorrelated nucleons and within the Generalized Eikonal Approximation for rescattering within the short-range correlated pair, and color transparency effects estimated within the quantum diffusion model. The calculations were compared with data from the EVA/AGS experiment at Brookhaven National Laboratory on C(p,2p)X reactions.

## Key numbers

- The hard elastic pp scattering cross section exhibits a strong energy dependence of approximately s⁻¹⁰ for s ≥ 12 GeV² at θ_cm ≈ 90°
- The missing energy resolution of the available high-energy A(p,2p)X data is about 240 MeV
- The Fermi momentum threshold for the two-nucleon correlation model is p_Ferm ≈ 250 MeV/c, with the SRC model applied for momenta in the range p_Ferm < p₂ < 0.7 GeV/c
- The parameter a₂(¹²C) = 5 was used for the probability of finding two-nucleon correlations in carbon
- The color screening correction uses ΔE ≈ 0.6-1 GeV for the typical excitation energy of a bound nucleon and t₀ ≈ 2 GeV² for the momentum transfer threshold where point-like configurations dominate
- The quantum diffusion model uses ΔM² = 0.7 GeV² and ⟨r_t(Q²)²⟩/⟨r_t²⟩ ∼ 1 GeV²/Q² for Q² ≥ 1.5 GeV²
- The analysis concentrates on incoming proton momenta in the range 5.9 ≤ p₁ ≤ 7.5 GeV/c
- The slope parameter for elastic NN amplitude is b/2, with γ = 1.6 and ρ₁ = 0.08 in the pp cross section parameterization

## Context

This work extends the plane wave impulse approximation predictions of Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) and Frankfurt et al. (FLFS) for high-momentum transfer A(p,2p)X reactions by including competing nuclear effects not previously discussed. The calculations are compared with data from the EVA experiment at Brookhaven National Laboratory (kn:I101), and the obtained strength of short-range correlations agrees with values previously extracted from electroproduction reactions on nuclei. The paper enables future extraction of short-range correlation properties from hard quasielastic scattering data and demonstrates that the α-distribution measured in A(p,2p)X reactions reflects the original α-distribution of the target proton in the nucleus due to the approximate conservation of light-cone momentum in high-energy soft reinteractions.
