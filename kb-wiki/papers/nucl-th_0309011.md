# Temperature Dependent Neutron Scattering Sections for Polyethylene

> This note presents neutron scattering cross sections for polyethylene at 296 K, 77 K and 4 K derived from a new scattering kernel for neutron scattering off of hydrogen in polyethylene. The kernel was developed in ENDF-6 format as a set of S(alpha,beta) tables using the LEAPR module of the NJOY94 code package. The polyethylene density of states (from 0 to sub eV) adopted to derive the new kernel is presented. We compare our calculated room temperature total scattering cross sections and double differential cross sections at 232 meV at various angles with the available experimental data (at room temperature), and then extrapolate the calculations to lower temperatures (77K and 4K). The new temperature dependent scattering kernel gives a good quantitative fit to the available room temperature data and has a temperature dependence that is qualitatively consistent with thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new temperature-dependent neutron scattering kernel for polyethylene at 296 K, 77 K, and 4 K that gives a good quantitative fit to available room-temperature experimental data, but the temperature dependence at lower temperatures is only qualitatively consistent with thermodynamics and is not directly validated against experimental data at those temperatures.

## Method

The authors combined the epithermal part of the Sprevak and Koppel (1970) density of states with the low-energy part of the Swaminathan and Tewari (1990) spectrum for crystalline polyethylene, then ran this combined spectrum through the LEAPR module of the NJOY94 code package to generate S(alpha,beta) tables in ENDF-6 format. They compared calculated room-temperature total scattering cross sections and double differential cross sections at 232 meV incident neutron energy at 25, 60, and 90 degrees against experimental data from Armstrong and Bischoff, then extrapolated the calculations to 77 K and 4 K.

## Key numbers

- Total scattering cross section for hydrogen in polyethylene: 81.67 barns, with coherent scattering accounting for only 2.2% of this total
- Hydrogen absorption cross section subtracted from experimental data: 0.334 barn per H atom at 0.0253 eV
- Constant carbon scattering cross section subtracted from experimental data: 4.74 barns
- Incident neutron energy for double differential cross section measurements: 232 meV (equivalent to T_n = 2700 K)
- Sharp peak in density of states from bond stretching: 0.36 eV
- Energy range of other intra-molecular excitations: 0.08 to 0.19 eV
- For CH2 at 4 K, nuclear absorption overwhelms inelastic scattering for all neutrons with energies less than 70 K
- Optimal 4 K CH2 thicknesses for the LANL UCN source: 10-20 mm range

## Context

This work extends the earlier polyethylene scattering kernel developed by Sprevak and Koppel (1970) using the GASKET code, and incorporates the more detailed low-energy density of states from Swaminathan and Tewari (1990). The new kernels enable Monte Carlo simulations of the prototype solid deuterium Ultra Cold Neutron source at Los Alamos National Laboratory using MCNP, particularly for the cold moderator stage where polyethylene at 4 K replaces liquid deuterium or hydrogen for safety reasons.
