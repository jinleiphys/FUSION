# Electric polarizability of neutral hadrons from dynamical lattice QCD ensembles

> We present a valence calculation of the electric polarizability of the neutron, neutral pion, and neutral kaon on two dynamically generated nHYP-clover ensembles. The pion masses for these ensembles are 227(2) MeV and 306(1) MeV, which are the lowest ones used in polarizability studies. This is part of a program geared towards determining these parameters at the physical point. We carry out a high statistics calculation that allows us to: (1) perform an extrapolation of the kaon polarizability to the physical point; we find $α_K =0.269(43)\times10^{-4}$fm$^{3}$, (2) quantitatively compare our neutron polarizability results with predictions from $χ$PT, and (3) analyze the dependence on both the valence and sea quark masses. The kaon polarizability varies slowly with the light quark mass and the extrapolation can be done with high confidence.

## Key claim

The paper presents a valence calculation of electric polarizabilities for neutral hadrons on dynamical lattice QCD ensembles with the lowest pion masses used in polarizability studies to date (227 and 306 MeV), but the sea quarks are kept electrically neutral throughout, and the neutral pion calculation omits disconnected contributions.

## Method

The authors use the background field method with an imaginary electric field and Dirichlet boundary conditions in the field direction to allow arbitrarily small field strengths, avoiding the quantization constraint of periodic boundary conditions. They compute zero-field, plus-field, and minus-field two-point correlation functions on two nHYP-clover dynamical ensembles with pion masses 227(2) MeV and 306(1) MeV, extracting energy shifts via simultaneous correlated fits among the three correlators. For the neutron, the static polarizability is corrected for the magnetic moment contribution using an extrapolated fit to magnetic moment data from an independent lattice study (Lee 2005).

## Key numbers

*   Kaon polarizability extrapolated to the physical point: α_K = 0.269(43) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
*   Dynamical pion masses for the two ensembles: 227(2) MeV and 306(1) MeV
*   Lattice spacing for the 306 MeV ensemble: 0.1245(16) fm; for the 227 MeV ensemble: 0.1215(11) fm
*   Electric field strength parameter η = 10⁻⁴ used for all calculations, chosen from scaling checks on a single configuration

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD polarizability studies (Engelhardt 2007, Detmold 2010, Detmold 2009, Alexandru 2008) by using the lowest pion masses to date and including dynamical sea quarks, though the sea quarks remain electrically neutral. The results enable chiral extrapolation of the kaon polarizability to the physical point and quantitative comparison with chiral perturbation theory predictions (Griesshammer 2012, Lensky 2009) for the neutron; the authors find disagreement with both χPT curves at the 227 MeV pion mass, which they attribute to finite-volume effects and the neglect of sea quark electric charges.
