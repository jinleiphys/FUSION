# Two-particle two-hole excitations in semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering

> A calculation of the two-particle two-hole contribution to the semi-inclusive $(ν_μ,μ^-N)$ cross section on carbon is performed in the framework of the relativistic Fermi gas model. The process is driven by meson-exchange currents encompassing all contributions involving the exchange of a single pion and the excitation of a $Δ$ resonance. The calculation is validated against the inclusive $(ν_μ,μ^-)$ response functions already existing in the literature, and then extended for the first time to the semi-inclusive channel. Results are presented both at fixed neutrino energy and folded with the T2K neutrino flux.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully microscopic calculation of the two-particle two-hole (2p2h) contribution to the semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section \((\nu_\mu,\mu^-N)\) on carbon, extending a previously developed electromagnetic formalism to the weak interaction sector; the authors note that while the calculation uses the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model as a wave-function basis, the 2p2h nuclear tensor goes beyond the pure Fermi gas approximation because the two-body current introduces dynamical correlations between nucleons.

## Method

The calculation is performed within the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, using meson-exchange currents that include pion-in-flight, seagull, pion-pole, and Delta-resonance forward and backward contributions. The semi-inclusive weak nuclear tensor for 2p2h excitations is computed by evaluating a nine-dimensional integral that is reduced to five dimensions through analytic manipulation of the Dirac delta function, with Pauli blocking enforced via step functions on final-state momenta. The model is validated by comparing inclusive 2p2h response functions against existing results in the literature, then extended to the semi-inclusive channel where the cross section is expressed as a function of the final proton kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum for carbon: \(p_F = 225\) MeV
- Energy shift for 2p2h channel: \(E^{2p2h}_{shift} = 40\) MeV (with \(E_{shift} = 20\) MeV for the quasielastic channel)
- Nucleon mass: \(m_N = 0.939\) GeV; pion mass: \(m_\pi = 0.1395\) GeV; Delta mass: \(M_\Delta = 1.232\) GeV
- Vector mass: \(M_V = 0.84\) GeV; axial mass: \(M_A = 1.05\) GeV
- At fixed neutrino energy \(E_{\nu_\mu} = 750\) MeV, transferred energy \(\omega = 200\) MeV, and scattering angle \(\theta_\mu = 15^\circ\), the momentum transfer is \(|{\bf q}| \simeq 268\) MeV
- The proton-proton channel contribution to the inclusive 2p2h response is about six times larger than the proton-neutron channel contribution

## Context

This work extends the electromagnetic \((e,e'p)\) formalism developed by Belocchi (2024) to the weak interaction sector for semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering. It addresses a gap identified by the authors: previous semi-inclusive 2p2h calculations from the Ghent group (Van Cuyck, 2016, 2017) did not include Delta contributions to meson-exchange currents, while event generators have relied on extracting exclusive predictions from inclusive calculations using strong approximations (Dolan, 2019; Hayato, 2021). The results enable direct implementation of 2p2h contributions in Monte Carlo event generators for experiments such as T2K, MINERvA, and MicroBooNE, and can be compared with semi-inclusive measurements of \((\nu_\mu, \mu p)\) cross sections.
