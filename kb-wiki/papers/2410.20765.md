# The elliptic flow difference between baryons and anti-baryons in heavy collision with SMASH Model

> A significant difference in the elliptic flow $v_2$ for particles and their corresponding antiparticles, which is more pronounced for baryons and anti-baryons, was observed in the STAR experiment during the Beam Energy Scan I (BES-I) at RHIC. By employing the SMASH model, we study the $v_2$ difference between protons and anti-protons, as well as between $Î›$ and $\barÎ›$, in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV as a function of evolution time. It finds that as the evolution time increases, the $v_2$ difference between protons and anti-protons becomes more pronounced compared to that between $Î›$ and $\barÎ›$. This phenomenon can be explained by the different constituent quarks of protons and $Î›$. Since some of the $u$ quarks and $d$ quarks come from the colliding nuclei and are transported to mid-rapidity, they undergo more interactions than produced quarks, resulting in the proton $Î”v_2$ being larger than the $Î›$ $Î”v_2$. We compare the SMASH calculations with STAR BES-I data and argue that higher precision data from BES-II will provide constraints on theoretical frameworks to interpret the $v_2$ differences of particles and anti-particles.

## Key claim

Using the SMASH hadronic transport model, the authors find that the elliptic flow difference between protons and antiprotons is larger than that between Lambda and anti-Lambda in Au+Au collisions at 7.7 GeV, and they attribute this to the fact that protons contain more transported (rather than produced) constituent quarks which undergo more interactions; however, the existing STAR BES-I data have statistical uncertainties too large to confirm or refute this baryon-type dependence.

## Method

The authors performed SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) model simulations of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, extracting the elliptic flow v2 for protons, antiprotons, Lambda, and anti-Lambda at evolution times of 2 fm/c, 10 fm/c, and 50 fm/c after collision. They calculated the v2 difference (Delta v2) between each baryon and its antibaryon by fitting the per-pT-bin differences over a pT range of 0.6 to 2.3 GeV/c, and compared these results across four centrality bins (0-80%, 0-10%, 10-40%, 40-80%) with STAR BES-I experimental data.

## Key numbers

* At evolution time 10 fm/c and 50 fm/c, the proton v2 difference exceeds the Lambda v2 difference across all centrality bins in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV.
* At evolution time 2 fm/c, both the proton v2 difference and the Lambda v2 difference are near zero for each centrality bin.
* The SMASH model results are compared with STAR BES-I data using pT fitting ranges of 0.46-1.64 GeV/c for protons in 0-80% and 10-40% centrality, 0.78-1.49 GeV/c for protons in 0-10% centrality, and 0.87-1.53 GeV/c for Lambda in 0-80% centrality.
* The default evolution time for the final SMASH comparison with STAR data is 200 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon-antibaryon elliptic flow differences observed by the STAR collaboration during the Beam Energy Scan I at RHIC, which found the effect increases at lower collision energies and is more pronounced for baryons than mesons. Prior explanations using the UrQMD model (tracing initial quark number in protons) and hybrid models (baryon-number current propagation in hydrodynamics) are referenced, as is the quark coalescence model approach. The authors argue that higher-precision data from STAR BES-II, which recorded 12-25 times more events for Au+Au collisions at 7.7-19.6 GeV, will be needed to constrain theoretical interpretations of the particle-antiparticle v2 differences.
