# Finite range effects of nuclear force in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> Within the framework of an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model, the zero-range 2-body part of the Skyrme interaction is replaced by a finite-range Gaussian 2-body interaction. From the transverse momentum analysis in the reaction of system $^{93}$Nb + $^{93}$Nb at energy of 400 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter $b$=3 fm, it is shown that the finite-range nuclear force enhances the transverse momentum of the reaction system and it can partly replace the momentum dependent part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

Replacing the zero-range two-body Skyrme interaction with a finite-range Gaussian two-body interaction in an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model enhances the transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions and can partly replace the momentum-dependent part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the finite-range parameter must be set to approximately 0.5 fm to achieve agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors modified an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model by replacing the zero-range delta-function two-body Skyrme interaction with a finite-range Gaussian interaction characterized by a range parameter Delta. They then simulated the reaction of Nb-93 on Nb-93 at 400 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b=3 fm, calculating the transverse momentum distribution for five different equation-of-state parameter sets: hard EOS (H), soft EOS (S), soft EOS with momentum-dependent interaction (SM), soft EOS with Delta=0.1 fm (S 0.1), and soft EOS with Delta=0.5 fm (S 0.5). Clusters were constructed using a coalescence model with coalescence parameters R0=3.5 fm and P0=260 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum in the case of soft EOS with Delta=0.5 fm (S 0.5) was smaller than that in the case of soft EOS with momentum-dependent interaction (SM) by an amount of 8 MeV/c.
- The finite-range parameter Delta=0.5 fm was found to be reasonable for modeling the finite-range effect of nuclear force, with Delta expected to have the same order as the radius of the repulsive core, approximately 0.4 to 0.6 fm.
- The Gaussian wave packet width L was set to 2.0 fm^2 throughout the calculations.
- Normal nuclear matter density rho_0 was taken as 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The hard EOS had an incompressibility of K=380 MeV, while the soft EOS had K=200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the isospin-dependent QMD model developed by Chen, Zhang, and collaborators (1997-2000) by introducing finite-range effects into the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, which had previously used zero-range Skyrme forces. The approach is similar to using the finite-range Gogny interaction, which employs a superposition of two Gaussian functions with different ranges and spin-isospin mixtures. The results enable further investigation of finite-range effects in heavy-ion collisions, with the authors suggesting that future work should adopt finite-range Gogny forces for the two-body interaction and include finite-range three-body forces.
