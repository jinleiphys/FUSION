# On Theoretical Problems in Synthesis of Superheavy Elements

> Towards precise predictions of residue cross sections of the superheavy elements, recent theoretical developments of reaction mechanisms are presented, together with the remaining problems which give rise to ambiguities in absolute values of cross sections predicted.

## Key claim

The paper argues that precise predictions of superheavy element residue cross sections require a consistent treatment of two distinct fusion-hindrance mechanisms, energy dissipation during the Coulomb barrier passage and dissipation during shape evolution from a pear-shaped configuration to a spherical compound nucleus, but that quantitative predictions remain ambiguous due to incomplete theoretical understanding of barrier passage under friction and uncertainties in shell damping parameters.

## Method

The authors decompose the fusion probability into a sticking probability \(P_{\text{stick}}\) (passage over the Coulomb barrier under friction) and a formation probability \(P_{\text{form}}\) (passage over the conditional saddle point to spherical shape). They derive an analytic formula for barrier passage under friction using a Langevin equation with an inverted parabolic barrier, obtaining an expression for the formation probability in terms of barrier height, initial kinetic energy, friction coefficient, and temperature. For the approaching phase, they apply the surface friction model (SFM) to compute sticking probabilities and radial momentum distributions for \(^{48}\text{Ca}\) + actinide systems, and then solve a multi-dimensional Langevin equation for shape evolution using the two-center parameterization with Werner-Wheeler inertia and one-body wall-and-window friction. Survival probabilities are calculated with the statistical code KEWPIE using Hauser-Feshbach formalism, Ignatyuk's prescription for shell damping, and Bohr-Wheeler fission widths.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{48}\text{Ca}+^{244}\text{Pu}\), the sticking probability reaches 1/2 about 12 MeV above the Coulomb barrier.
- The shell damping parameter \(E_d\) is taken as 18 MeV, based on single-particle model calculations of the free energy.
- The reduced friction parameter \(x = \beta/(2\omega)\) for the one-body wall-and-window formula yields a factor of about 10 in the extra-push energy formula \(K_c = (\sqrt{x^2+1}+x)^2 \cdot B\).
- For the rolling friction limit, the limiting orbital angular momentum \(L_{st} = (5/7)L_0\).
- The friction strengths in SFM are given as \(K_r^0 = C_r = 4\) and \(K_\phi^0 = (7/2)C_T = 0.01\) in units of \(10^{-23}\) s/MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for fusion-hindrance in superheavy element synthesis beyond the systematics-based approaches used in prior experiments (e.g., those cited as bib4). It builds on the surface friction model of Gross and Kalinowski (bib6) and the one-body wall-and-window dissipation model (bib18), and provides a consistent treatment of both the approaching phase and shape evolution that reproduces experimental fusion excitation functions for \(^{48}\text{Ca}+^{238}\text{U}\), \(+^{244}\text{Pu}\), and \(+^{248}\text{Cm}\) from GSI (bib26) and Dubna (bib27). The results enable predictions for unexplored systems such as \(^{48}\text{Ca}+^{252}\text{Cf}\), and the analytic formula for barrier passage under friction (the "inverse Kramers formula") provides a new tool for understanding fusion hindrance, though the authors note that multi-dimensional effects, neck formation, and time-dependent shell corrections remain to be fully incorporated.
