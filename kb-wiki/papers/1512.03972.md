# Novel Topological Effects in Dense QCD in a Magnetic Field

> We study the electromagnetic properties of dense QCD in the so-called Magnetic Dual Chiral Density Wave phase. This inhomogeneous phase exhibits a nontrivial topology that comes from the fermion sector due to the asymmetry of the lowest Landau level modes. The nontrivial topology manifests in the electromagnetic effective action via a chiral anomaly term $~θF^{μν}\tilde{F}_{μν}$, with a dynamic axion field $θ$ given by the phase of the dual chiral density wave condensate. The coupling of the axion with the electromagnetic field leads to several macroscopic effects that include, among others, an anomalous, nondissipative Hall current, an anomalous electric charge, magnetoelectricity, and the formation of a hybridized propagating mode known as an axion polariton. Connection to topological insulators and Weyls semimetals, as well as possible implications for heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars are all highlighted.

## Key claim

The Magnetic Dual Chiral Density Wave (MDCDW) phase of dense QCD in a magnetic field exhibits nontrivial topology from the asymmetry of the lowest Landau level modes, which manifests as a chiral anomaly term in the electromagnetic effective action; however, the authors explicitly state they cannot claim this phase is the most energetically favorable in the intermediate-density region, as that would require an energy comparison of all possible chiral and color-superconducting phases.

## Method

The authors model dense QCD plus QED using a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian with a background magnetic field and a baryon chemical potential. They apply a local chiral transformation to remove the spatial modulation of the dual chiral density wave condensate, then use Fujikawa's method with heat-kernel regularization to compute the Jacobian of the fermion measure, obtaining the chiral anomaly contribution to the effective action. The resulting axion electrodynamics equations are derived, and the anomalous electric charge, Hall current, and magnetoelectric properties are calculated from the effective action expanded in powers of the electromagnetic fluctuation field.

## Key numbers

- The axion coupling constant is $\kappa/4 = e^2/(8\pi^2) = \alpha/(2\pi)$, where $\alpha$ is the fine-structure constant.
- The lowest Landau level (LLL) energy spectrum is $E^0 = \epsilon\sqrt{m^2 + k_3^2} + q/2$ with $\epsilon = \pm$, showing asymmetry about zero energy.
- The higher Landau level ($l \neq 0$) spectrum is $E^l = \epsilon\sqrt{(\xi\sqrt{m^2 + k_3^2} + q/2)^2 + 2|e_f B|l}$ with $\epsilon = \pm$, $\xi = \pm$, and $l = 1,2,3,...$.
- Magnetic fields in neutron stars are estimated to range from $10^{18}$ G for nuclear matter to $10^{20}$ G for quark matter; in heavy-ion collisions, fields are of order $eB \simeq O(2m_\pi^2) \sim 10^{18}$ G at RHIC and $eB \simeq O(15m_\pi^2) \sim 10^{19}$ G at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the study of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in dense QCD, which have been found in the large-N limit of QCD (largeNQCD), in quarkyonic matter (q-chiralspirals), and in NJL models (Nickel, 2009; PRD85, 2012). The MDCDW phase differs from the DCDW phase at zero magnetic field (DCDW) in both symmetry and transport properties. The results connect to topological insulators and Weyl semimetals (Weyl-SM, 2011; burkov-2017), which share the axion term $\frac{\kappa}{4}\theta F_{\mu\nu}\widetilde{F}^{\mu\nu}$ in their effective action, enabling tabletop experiments to mimic effects that would be harder to detect in heavy-ion collisions or neutron star observations.
