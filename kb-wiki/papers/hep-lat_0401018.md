# Numerical Portrait of a Relativistic BCS Gapped Superfluid

> We present results of numerical simulations of the 3+1 dimensional Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a non-zero baryon density enforced via the introduction of a chemical potential mu not equal to 0. The triviality of the model with a number of dimensions d>=4 is dealt with by fitting low energy constants, calculated analytically in the large number of colors (Hartree) limit, to phenomenological values. Non-perturbative measurements of local order parameters for superfluidity and their related susceptibilities show that, in contrast to the 2+1 dimensional model, the ground-state at high chemical potential and low temperature is that of a traditional BCS superfluid. This conclusion is supported by the direct observation of a gap in the dispersion relation for 0.5<=(mu a)<=0.85, which at (mu a)=0.8 is found to be roughly 15% the size of the vacuum fermion mass. We also present results of an initial investigation of the stability of the BCS phase against thermal fluctuations. Finally, we discuss the effect of splitting the Fermi surfaces of the pairing partners by the introduction of a non-zero isospin chemical potential.

## Key claim

The 3+1 dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at high chemical potential and low temperature exhibits a conventional BCS superfluid ground state with a non-vanishing diquark condensate and a measurable gap in the quasiparticle dispersion relation, in contrast to the 2+1 dimensional model which shows unconventional Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless behavior; however, this conclusion depends on fitting low energy constants to phenomenological values to handle the model's triviality in d>=4 dimensions.

## Method

The authors performed hybrid Monte Carlo simulations of the lattice-regularized NJL model in 3+1 dimensions with a non-zero baryon chemical potential, using staggered fermions with N_c=8 colors and N_f=2 flavors. The bare parameters (coupling g^2 and fermion mass m) were fixed by fitting the large-N_c (Hartree) analytic predictions for the constituent fermion mass, pion decay constant, and pion mass to phenomenological values of 400 MeV, 93 MeV, and 138 MeV respectively, yielding an inverse lattice spacing a^{-1}=720 MeV. Measurements of the chiral condensate, baryon density, diquark condensates, diquark susceptibilities, and the Gor'kov propagator were performed on lattices of sizes 12^3x12, 16^3x16, and 20^3x20 for chemical potentials mu a between 0.0 and 1.2, with results extrapolated to infinite volume.

## Key numbers

* The chiral restoration transition occurs at mu_c ~ 0.75 in lattice units, with the lattice data showing the condensate and onset mu_o roughly 15% smaller than the large-N_c analytic prediction.
* At mu a = 0.8, the BCS gap Delta is roughly 15% of the vacuum fermion mass Sigma_0, translating to approximately 60 MeV in physical units.
* The inverse lattice spacing is a^{-1} = 720 MeV, giving a lattice spacing a ~ 0.3 fm.
* The bare fermion mass is ma = 0.006 and the inverse coupling is beta = 0.495, from fitting to f_pi = 93 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV, and Sigma* = 400 MeV.
* At (mu a, n_B a^3) ~ (0.65, 0.072), the physical density is 0.88 N_c N_f fm^{-3}, compared to nuclear matter onset at n_q ~ 0.15 N_c fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous numerical studies of the NJL model in 2+1 dimensions (Hands, 2000; Hands, 2001), which found no evidence for a conventional BCS condensate but rather an unconventional Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless superfluid mode with critical behavior for all mu > mu_c. The 3+1 dimensional results presented here show the expected BCS signal with a non-vanishing diquark condensate and gap, consistent with self-consistent gap equation estimates from Berges (1998) and Nardulli (2002). The study enables future investigations of the stability of the BCS phase against thermal fluctuations and the effect of splitting Fermi surfaces via a non-zero isospin chemical potential.
