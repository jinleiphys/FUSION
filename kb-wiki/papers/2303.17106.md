# Probing into the Possible Range of the U Bosonic Coupling Constants in Neutron Stars Containing Hyperons

> The range of the U bosonic coupling constants in neutron star matter is a very interesting but still unsolved problem which has multifaceted influences in nuclear physics, particle physics, astrophysics and cosmology. The combination of the theoretical numerical simulation and the recent observations provides a very good opportunity to solve this problem. In the present work, the range of the U bosonic coupling constants is inferred based on the three relations of the mass-radius, mass-frequency and mass-tidal deformability in neutron star containing hyperons using the GM1, TM1 and NL3 parameter sets under the two flavor symmetries of the SU(6) and SU(3) in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory. Combined with observations from PSRs J1614-2230, J0348+0432, J2215-5135, J0952-0607, J0740+6620, J0030-0451, J1748-2446ad, XTE J1739-285, GW170817 and GW190814 events, our numerical results show that the U bosonic coupling constants may tend to be within the range from 0 to 20 GeV$^{-2}$ in neutron star containing hyperons. Moreover, the numerical results of the three relations obtained by the SU(3) symmetry are better in accordance with observation data than those obtained by the SU(6) symmetry. The results will help us to improve the strict constraints of the equation of state for neutron stars containing hyperons.

## Key claim

The U bosonic coupling constants in neutron stars containing hyperons may tend to be within the range from 0 to 20 GeV$^{-2}$, based on comparisons of theoretical mass-radius, mass-frequency, and mass-tidal deformability relations with observational data from multiple pulsars and gravitational wave events, with the SU(3) flavor symmetry providing better agreement than SU(6).

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean field theory with GM1, TM1, and NL3 parameter sets, including $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\sigma^{*}$, and $\phi$ mesons, to compute equations of state for neutron star matter containing hyperons (npH matter) under SU(6) and SU(3) flavor symmetries. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius, mass-frequency, and mass-tidal deformability relations for U bosonic coupling constants $g_u$ ranging from 0 to 40 GeV$^{-2}$, then compared these results against observational constraints from PSRs J1614-2230, J0348+0432, J2215-5135, J0952-0607, J0740+6620, J0030-0451, J1748-2446ad, XTE J1739-285, GW170817, and GW190814.

## Key numbers

- The U bosonic coupling constants may tend to be within the range from 0 to 20 GeV$^{-2}$ in neutron stars containing hyperons.
- Maximum masses of npH matter across GM1, TM1, and NL3 parameter sets lie in the scope of 1.667 to 2.492 $M_{\odot}$ for $g_u$ values from 0 to 40 GeV$^{-2}$.
- Corresponding radii for maximum mass configurations lie in the range 11.019 to 15.268 km.
- For np matter at $g_u = 0$ GeV$^{-2}$, maximum masses range from 2.094 to 2.774 $M_{\odot}$ and radii from 11.530 to 13.295 km.
- The radius at the canonical mass of 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ lies in the scope of 12.957 to 17.825 km.
- The mass-radius relations simultaneously satisfy mass and radius constraints of six pulsars only in the SU3 case for the NL3 parameter set with $g_u = 0, 4, 7,$ and 10 GeV$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of U boson effects in neutron stars by Krivoruchenko et al. (2009), Yu et al. (2011), Zhang et al. (2011), and Zhang et al. (2016), who showed that U boson interactions modify equations of state and increase maximum masses. The authors build on the earlier constraint by Wen et al. (2009) that $g_u$ should be located in 0-100 GeV$^{-2}$ based on FOPI/GSI data, narrowing this range to 0-20 GeV$^{-2}$ using multi-messenger observations. The results enable stricter constraints on the equation of state for neutron stars containing hyperons and inform future searches for new bosonic degrees of freedom in dense matter.
