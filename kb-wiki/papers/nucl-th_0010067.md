# Quark-meson coupling model for a nucleon

> We considered the quark-meson coupling model for a nucleon. The model describes a nucleon as an MIT bag, in which quarks are coupled to the scalar and the vector mesons. A set of coupled equations for the quark and the meson fields are obtained and are solved in a self-consistent manner. We show that the mass of a dressed MIT bag interacting with $σ$- and $ω$-meson fields differs considerably from the mass of the free MIT bag. The effects of the density-dependent bag constant are investigated. The results of our calculations imply that the self-energy of the bag in the quark-meson coupling model is significant and needs to be considered in doing the calculations for nuclear matter or finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The mass of a single dressed MIT bag in free space, when calculated with the bag constant and zero-point parameter fixed from the free MIT bag, deviates considerably from the physical nucleon mass of 939 MeV; this self-energy effect has been neglected in previous quark-meson coupling (QMC) model calculations for nuclear matter and needs to be accounted for when choosing model parameters.

## Method

The authors solve a set of coupled equations for quark fields and the scalar (σ) and vector (ω) meson fields inside a static spherical MIT bag, using the mean-field approximation and keeping only the time component of the ω field. The equations are solved self-consistently for the quark eigenvalue, the meson field profiles, and the bag energy, with the nucleon mass obtained from the energy-momentum tensor after correcting for spurious center-of-mass motion. The density-dependent bag constant is introduced via a phenomenological parametrization B(σ) = B₀ [1 − g_σ^B (4/δ)(σ/M_N⁰)]^δ, with M_N⁰ = 939 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Free MIT bag mass: 939 MeV at bag radius R = 0.6 fm, using B₀^{1/4} = 188.1 MeV and Z = 2.030.
- With g_σ^q = 0, g_ω^q = 3.00, g_σ^B = 8.45, and δ = 8: dressed nucleon mass M_N = 848 MeV at R = 0.694 fm.
- With g_σ^q = 1.0, g_ω^q = 3.08, g_σ^B = 7.26, and δ = 8: M_N = 834 MeV at R = 0.674 fm.
- With g_σ^q = 2.0, g_ω^q = 2.85, g_σ^B = 5.65, and δ = 8: M_N = 830 MeV at R = 0.659 fm.
- With only ω coupling (g_σ^q = 0, g_ω^q = 2, g_σ^B = 0): M_N = 957 MeV at R = 0.595 fm (repulsive).
- With only σ coupling (g_σ^q = 2, g_ω^q = 0, g_σ^B = 0): M_N = 926 MeV at R = 0.601 fm (attractive).
- With equal couplings (g_σ^q = g_ω^q = 2, g_σ^B = 0): M_N = 948 MeV at R = 0.598 fm (near cancellation).
- σ meson mass: 550 MeV; ω meson mass: 783 MeV; quark mass taken as zero.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model originally proposed by Guichon (1988) for nuclear matter, where nucleons are treated as non-overlapping MIT bags interacting through σ and ω meson exchange in the mean-field approximation. Prior QMC calculations (e.g., Fleck et al., 1990; Jin and Jennings, 1997) fixed the bag constant B and zero-point parameter Z from the free MIT bag and used those same values in nuclear matter calculations, neglecting the self-energy shift of the single nucleon mass due to meson coupling. The present results show that this shift is about 100 MeV, implying that parameters must be re-adjusted before applying the model to nuclear matter or finite nuclei. The density-dependent bag constant is shown to further increase the bag radius and enhance attraction at larger radii.
