# Realization, Characterization, and Detection of Novel Superfluid Phases with Pairing between Unbalanced Fermion Species

> In this chapter we review recent experimental and theoretical work on various novel superfluid phases in fermion systems, that result from pairing fermions of different species with unequal densities. After briefly reviewing existing experimental work in superconductors subject to a strong magnetic field and trapped cold fermionic atom systems, we discuss how to characterize the possible pairing phases based on their symmetry properties, and the structure/topology of the Fermi surface(s) formed by the unpaired fermions due to the density imbalance. We also discuss possible experimental probes that can be used to directly detect the structure of the superfluid order parameter in superconductors and trapped cold atom systems, which may establish the presence of some of these phases unambiguously.

## Key claim

This review argues that the various proposed superfluid phases for pairing between unbalanced fermion species, FFLO, deformed Fermi surface pairing (DFSP), and breached pairing (BP), can be unified and classified based on their symmetry properties as different phases of a liquid crystal (smectic, nematic, and isotropic, respectively), with the caveat that at zero temperature an additional classification based on the topology of Fermi surfaces formed by unpaired fermions may be necessary for a complete description.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical proposals for pairing phases in systems with unequal densities of two fermion species, including superconductors under Zeeman splitting and trapped cold atom mixtures. It classifies these phases by their spatial symmetry properties (crystal, smectic, nematic, isotropic) and, at zero temperature, by the number and topology of Fermi surfaces of unpaired fermions, using a generalized Luttinger theorem. The review also discusses experimental probes for detecting these phases, including Josephson junction measurements, vortex lattice structure analysis, Andreev surface bound state spectroscopy, and time-of-flight imaging in cold atom systems.

## Key numbers

- The FFLO order parameter has a spatial oscillation period of \(2\pi\hbar/p\), where \(p \approx p_{F\uparrow} - p_{F\downarrow}\) is the net momentum of Cooper pairs formed from fermions on mismatched Fermi surfaces.
- For CeCoIn\(_5\), a quasi-2D d-wave superconductor, the upper critical field is comparable to or exceeds the Pauli paramagnetic limit, a field at which Zeeman splitting becomes comparable to the superconducting gap.
- In the generalized Luttinger theorem for superfluids with imbalance, the difference in Fermi surface volumes is conserved: \(\Delta N = N_\uparrow - N_\downarrow = \frac{A}{(2\pi)^d}(\Omega_\uparrow - \Omega_\downarrow)\).
- For a 2D superconductor with tilted magnetic field, the kinetic energy eigenvalues of the order parameter are \(-(2n+1)/\ell^2\), where \(\ell = \sqrt{\hbar c/2eB}\) is the Cooper pair magnetic length and \(n = 0, 1, 2, \dots\) is the Landau level index; the order parameter momentum is estimated as \(q \approx \sqrt{2n+1}/\ell\).
- In the FFLO state, the periodicity of spin modulation (from unpaired electrons localized at domain walls) should be one-half that of the superconducting order parameter.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of Fulde and Ferrell (1964) and Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) for pairing with mismatched Fermi surfaces, and connects it to more recent proposals for deformed Fermi surface pairing (Mears and Sheehy; Mears, Sheehy, and Yang) and breached pairing (Liu and Wilczek; Gubankova, Liu, and Wilczek; Forbes, Gubankova, Liu, and Wilczek). It enables experimental searches for these phases by proposing specific "phase-sensitive" probes, including Josephson junction measurements (Yang, 2000), vortex lattice structure analysis (Yang and Ma), and Andreev surface bound state spectroscopy (Cui and Yang), as well as time-of-flight and shot noise correlation methods for cold atom systems (Yang, 2005).
