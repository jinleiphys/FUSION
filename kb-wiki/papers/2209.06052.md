# Polytropic fits of modern and unified equations of state

> Equations of state for a cold neutron star's interior are presented in three-column tables that relate the baryonic density, the energy density, and the pressure. A few analytical expressions for those tables have been established these past two decades, as a convenient way to present a large number of nuclear models for neutron star matter. Some of those analytical representations are based on nonunified equations of state, in the sense that the high and the low density part of the star are not computed with the same nuclear model. Fits of equations of state based on a piecewise polytropic representation are revised by using unified tables of equations of state, that is to say models which have been calculated consistently for the core and the crust. A set of 52 unified equations of state is chosen. Each one is divided in seven polytropes via an adaptive segmentation, and two parameters per polytrope are fitted to the tabulated equation of state. The total mass, radius, tidal deformability and moment of inertia of neutron stars are modelled from the fits and compared with the quantities calculated from the original tables to ensure the accuracy of the fits on macroscopic parameters. We provide the polytropes parameters for 15 nucleonic relativistic mean field models, seven hyperonic relativistic mean field models, five hybrid relativistic mean-field models, 24 nucleonic Skyrme models, and one ab initio model. The fit error on the macroscopic parameters of neutron stars is small and well within the estimated measurement accuracy from current and next generation telescopes.

## Key claim

The paper revises piecewise polytropic fits of neutron-star equations of state by using unified tables (where core and crust are computed consistently with the same nuclear model), rather than the nonunified constructions used in prior work. The authors claim that the fit error on macroscopic parameters is small and well within the estimated measurement accuracy from current and next-generation telescopes, but this accuracy depends on using unified rather than nonunified equations of state.

## Method

The authors selected 52 unified equations of state covering nucleonic relativistic mean-field models, hyperonic relativistic mean-field models, hybrid relativistic mean-field models, nucleonic Skyrme models, and one ab initio model. Each equation of state was divided into seven polytropes via an adaptive segmentation, and two parameters per polytrope were fitted to the tabulated equation of state. The total mass, radius, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia of neutron stars were then computed from the fits and compared with the quantities calculated from the original tables to verify accuracy.

## Key numbers

- 52 unified equations of state were fitted: 15 nucleonic relativistic mean-field models, 7 hyperonic relativistic mean-field models, 5 hybrid relativistic mean-field models, 24 nucleonic Skyrme models, and 1 ab initio model.
- Each equation of state was divided into 7 polytropes, with 2 parameters fitted per polytrope (14 parameters total per equation of state).
- The maximum mass selection criterion used the pulsar J1614-2230 with mass 1.908 ± 0.016 solar masses.
- The pulsar J0740+6620 has a revised mass of 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses (Fonseca 2021).
- NICER measurements of J0030+0451 give radius 12.71 (+1.14, -1.19) km (Riley 2019) and 13.02 (+1.24, -1.06) km (Miller 2019) within 1-sigma precision.
- NICER measurements of J0740+6620 give radius 13.7 (+2.6, -1.5) km (Riley 2021) and 12.39 (+1.3, -0.98) km (Miller 2021) within 1-sigma precision.
- The saturation density is n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The symmetry energy slope L from PREX-II data is 106 ± 37 MeV, with symmetry energy J = 38.1 ± 4.7 MeV (Reed 2021).
- The symmetry energy slope L from the Finite Range Droplet Model is 70 ± 15 MeV with J = 32.5 ± 0.5 MeV (Moller 2012).
- Heavy ion collision constraints give 42 < L < 117 MeV at 95% confidence level (Tsang 2012).
- The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is constrained to the interval [10.4, 12.9] km at 95% confidence (Steiner 2013).

## Context

This work extends the piecewise polytropic fitting approach of Read (2009) and Lindblom (2018) by using unified equations of state rather than nonunified constructions, which Suleiman (2021) showed can produce large errors in macroscopic parameter modeling. The fits enable convenient analytical representations of 52 modern unified equations of state for use in neutron-star simulations, with parameters that are easily comparable across different nuclear models. The results are relevant for interpreting observations from NICER, the upcoming eXTP mission (scheduled for 2027), ATHENA, and gravitational wave detectors including LIGO, Virgo, KAGRA, and future instruments like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer.
