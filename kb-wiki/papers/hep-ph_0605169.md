# Photoproduction of Xi off nucleons

> The photoproduction reaction $γN \to K K Ξ$ is investigated based on a relativistic meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions. The production amplitude is calculated in the tree-level approximation from relevant effective Lagrangians, whose (coupling constant) parameters are mostly fixed from the empirical data and/or quark models together with SU(3) symmetry considerations. Gauge invariance of the resulting amplitude is maintained by introducing the contact currents by extending the gauge-invariant approach of Haberzettl for one-meson photoproduction to two-meson photoproduction. The role of the intermediate low-lying hyperons and of the intermediate higher-mass hyperon resonances are analyzed in detail. In particular, the basic features of the production of $Ξ^-(1318)$ in $γp \to K^+ K^+ Ξ^-$ and their possible manifestations in the forthcoming experimental data are discussed.

## Key claim

The photoproduction reaction $\gamma N \to K K \Xi$ is investigated using a relativistic meson-exchange model, but the authors note that many issues such as final-state interactions and the roles of many high-mass resonances cannot be addressed due to a complete lack of independent information about the dynamics, so the work is limited to using only currently available information.

## Method

The production amplitude was calculated in the tree-level approximation from effective Lagrangians, with coupling constants mostly fixed from empirical data, quark models, and SU(3) symmetry considerations. Gauge invariance was maintained by extending the gauge-invariant approach of Haberzettl for one-meson photoproduction to two-meson photoproduction via contact currents. The model considered diagrams involving intermediate low-lying hyperons [Lambda(1116), Lambda(1405), Lambda(1520), Sigma(1193), Sigma(1385)] and two higher-mass resonances [Lambda(1800)1/2- and Lambda(1890)3/2+] as representatives of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 resonances in the 1.8-2.0 GeV region.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state cascade Xi(1318) has mass 1318.0 MeV; the Xi(1530) resonance has mass 1533.0 MeV with width 9.5 MeV
- The baryonic form-factor parameters were adjusted to Lambda_B = 1225 MeV with exponent n = 2 for low-mass resonance calculations
- For higher-mass resonance calculations, the form-factor parameters were readjusted to Lambda_B = 1170 MeV with n = 2
- The product of coupling constants for higher-mass resonances was fixed to g_{N Lambda K} * g_{Xi Lambda K} = 2 for both Lambda(1800) and Lambda(1890)
- The pseudoscalar-pseudovector mixing parameter lambda was considered at both extremes: lambda = 0 (pseudovector coupling) and lambda = 1 (pseudoscalar coupling)
- Incident photon energies were studied up to about 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the only previous theoretical investigation of cascade photoproduction off nucleons, that of Liu (no year given), which was devoted to pentaquark Xi_5 production. The study was motivated by the CLAS Collaboration at Jefferson Lab having initiated a cascade-physics program and established feasibility for reactions like gamma p -> K+ K+ Xi- and gamma p -> K+ K+ pi- Xi0. The results enable future experimental data from the ongoing dedicated CLAS experiment to be interpreted, particularly for extracting information on high-mass hyperon resonances with strangeness S = -1, since the reaction lacks t-channel meson-exchange processes that complicate other reactions.
