# Antikaon-induced $K_1(1270)^+$ meson production on nuclei near threshold

> We study the inclusive strange axial-vector meson $K_1(1270)^+$ production in ${K^-}A$ reactions at near-threshold laboratory incident antikaon momenta within a nuclear spectral function approach, which describes incoherent direct $K_1(1270)^+$ meson production in $K^-$ meson--proton ${K^-}p \to {K_1(1270)^+}Ξ^-$ production processes and accounts for three different options for its in-medium mass shift (or for its effective scalar potential) at central density $ρ_0$. We calculate the absolute differential and total cross sections for the production of $K_1(1270)^+$ mesons on $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W target nuclei at laboratory angles of 0$^{\circ}$--45$^{\circ}$ by $K^-$ mesons with momenta of 2.5, 2.8 and 3.5 GeV/c, which are close to the threshold momentum ($\approx$ 2.95 GeV/c) for $K_1(1270)^+$ meson production off the free target proton at rest. The intrinsic properties of carbon and tungsten target nuclei have been described in terms of their spectral functions, which take into account the momenta of target protons and the energies of their separation from the considered nuclei. We show that the differential and total (absolute and relative) $K_1(1270)^+$ antikaon-induced production cross sections at initial momenta not far from threshold -- at momenta $\sim$ 2.8--3.5 GeV/c, at which there is yet no strong drop in their strength, reveal a distinct sensitivity to changes in the in-medium shift of the $K_1(1270)^+$ mass, studied in the paper, both in the $K_1(1270)^+$ meson low-momentum (0.1--1.0 GeV/c) and in its full-momentum ranges. This would permit evaluating this shift. Experimental data necessary for this aim can be obtained in a dedicated experiment at the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility.

## Key claim

The inclusive production cross sections of $K_1(1270)^+$ mesons in $K^-A$ reactions at near-threshold incident antikaon momenta (2.5–3.5 GeV/c) show a distinct sensitivity to the in-medium mass shift of the $K_1(1270)^+$ meson, but only for beam momenta close to the free threshold of approximately 2.95 GeV/c where the cross section has not yet dropped strongly; this sensitivity is present both in the low-momentum (0.1–1.0 GeV/c) and full-momentum ranges of the produced meson.

## Method

The inclusive $K_1(1270)^+$ production in $K^-A$ reactions on $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W targets was calculated using a nuclear spectral function approach that describes incoherent direct $K^-p \to K_1(1270)^+\Xi^-$ production processes. The model accounts for the Fermi motion and separation energies of target protons via spectral functions, incorporates attenuation of the incident $K^-$ and outgoing $K_1(1270)^+$ in nuclear matter using total cross sections and in-medium widths, and considers three options for the $K_1(1270)^+$ effective scalar potential at normal nuclear density ($V_0 = -35$, $-20$, and $0$ MeV). The differential and total cross sections were computed for laboratory angles of $0^\circ$–$45^\circ$ at incident $K^-$ momenta of 2.5, 2.8, and 3.5 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Free threshold momentum for $K^-p \to K_1(1270)^+\Xi^-$: approximately 2.95 GeV/c.
- Three options for $K_1(1270)^+$ in-medium mass shift $V_0$ at normal nuclear density $\rho_0$: $-35$ MeV, $-20$ MeV, and $0$ MeV.
- Vacuum total cross section of $K^-p \to K_1(1270)^+\Xi^-$ at excess energy 0.4 GeV: $(14.8 \pm 2.6)$ $\mu$b and $(10.6 \pm 4.6)$ $\mu$b from two decay channels.
- On-shell cross section $\sigma_{K^-p \to K_1(1270)^+\Xi^-}$ at incident $K^-$ momentum 3.5 GeV/c: approximately 16 $\mu$b.
- Vacuum total decay width of $K_1(1270)^+$: 90 MeV; maximum in-medium width change $\Gamma_0$ at $\rho_0$: +38 MeV (for $V_0 = 0$ MeV), giving total in-medium width 128 MeV; for $V_0 = -35$ MeV, total in-medium width remains 90 MeV.
- $K_1(1270)^+$ decay mean free path: 1.5 fm for $V_0 = 0$ MeV (total width 128 MeV), 2.2 fm for $V_0 = -35$ MeV (total width 90 MeV).
- Ratio of $K_1(1270)^+\Xi^-$ to $K^+\Xi^-$ production cross sections at same excess energy of 0.4 GeV: approximately 0.1.
- $K_1(1270)^+$ low-momentum range studied: 0.1–1.0 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the collision model previously applied by Paryev (2020) to $K^*(892)^+$ production in $\pi^-A$ reactions, now adapted to $K_1(1270)^+$ production in $K^-A$ reactions. The study is motivated by QCD sum rule predictions from a 2015 work that the upper limit of the $K_1(1270)^+$ mass shift in nuclear matter is $-35$ MeV, and by the expectation that the in-medium mass difference between chiral partners $K^*(892)$ and $K_1(1270)$ is sensitive to the chiral order parameter. The results enable future experimental evaluation of the $K_1(1270)^+$ in-medium mass shift through dedicated measurements at the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility, and comparison with previous calculations for $K^*(892)^+$ production could shed light on partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter.
