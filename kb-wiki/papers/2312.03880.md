# On the Destabilization of High-Mass Neutron Stars by the Emergence of $d^*$-Hexaquarks

> We study the effects of the first nontrivial hexaquark, $d^*$(2380), on the equation of state of dense neutron star matter and investigate the consequences of its existence for neutron stars. The matter in the core regions of neutron stars is described using density-dependent relativistic mean-field theory. Our results show that within the parameter spaces examined in our paper, (i) the critical density at which the $d^*$ condensate emerges lies between 4 and 5 times the nuclear saturation density, (ii) $d^*$ hexaquarks are found to exist only in rather massive neutron stars, (iii) only relatively small fractions of the matter in the core of a massive neutron star may contain hexaquarks.

## Key claim
Within the specific parameter spaces of the density-dependent relativistic mean-field models examined, the $d^*$(2380) hexaquark condensate emerges only at densities between 4 and 5 times nuclear saturation density, and only in rather massive neutron stars, where it occupies relatively small fractions of the core matter and has a destabilizing effect that truncates the mass-radius sequence before the maximum-mass configuration.

## Method
The authors used density-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with three hadronic parametrizations (DD2, GM1L, SW4L) that include nucleons, hyperons, and $\Delta$ resonances, and added the $d^*$(2380) dibaryon as a spin-0 scalar field with density-independent coupling constants. They solved the coupled mean-field equations under chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality, varying the $d^*$ meson coupling ratios $x_{\sigma d^*}$ from 0 to 2 and $x_{\omega d^*}$ from -2 to 0, for two choices of the $\Delta$-meson couplings ($x_{\sigma\Delta}=x_{\omega\Delta}=1.05$ and 1.25), and computed the resulting equations of state and mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Critical density for $d^*$ emergence lies between 4 and 5 times nuclear saturation density $n_0$ (where $n_0$ = 0.149 fm$^{-3}$ for DD2, 0.153 fm$^{-3}$ for GM1L, 0.150 fm$^{-3}$ for SW4L).
- In representative maximum-mass configurations with $x_{\sigma d^*}=0.3$ and $x_{\omega d^*}=-0.4$, the $d^*$ appears at $n \simeq 3.60\, n_0$ for DD2 and SW4L, and at $n \simeq 3.75\, n_0$ for GM1L, with total stellar masses of $M = 1.95\, M_\odot$, $M = 1.87\, M_\odot$, and $M = 1.77\, M_\odot$ respectively.
- The $d^*$ particle appears only in the innermost core at $R_{d^*} \sim 2$ km.
- For the SW4L parametrization with $x_{\sigma d^*} = 0.7$, $x_{\omega d^*} = -0.9$, and $x_{\sigma\Delta}=x_{\omega\Delta}=1.05$, the EoS becomes truncated due to instabilities.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Mantziris (2020) on $d^*$(2380) in neutron stars by using a broader set of density-dependent RMF parametrizations (DD2, GM1L, SW4L) that satisfy the 2 $M_\odot$ constraint and incorporate hyperons and $\Delta$ resonances, following the approach of Malfatti (2020). The results confirm the destabilizing effect of $d^*$ hexaquarks found by Mantziris (2020) and show that the $d^*$ suppresses $\Lambda$ hyperons through two-body forces, in contrast to chiral effective field theory explanations (Weise 2020) that attribute $\Lambda$ suppression to higher-order three-body forces. The study enables future work on density-dependent $d^*$ coupling constants and is informed by upcoming photoproduction experiments (Bashkanov 2020) that could constrain the $d^*$ coupling to nuclear matter.
