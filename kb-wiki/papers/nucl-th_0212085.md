# Theoretical approaches to hadrons in nuclear matter

> We discuss recent developments concerning the in-medium properties of hadrons in dense and hot matter. The theoretical approaches are discussed in connection with the interpretation of experimental data from intermediate energy machines up to relativistic heavy ion collisions. Special emphasis is put on chiral restoration and its interplay with the substructure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry restoration in dense and hot matter is expected to occur, but the relationship between the observed reshaping of hadronic spectral functions and chiral restoration is not an absolute one; the modification of spectral functions is not restricted to a simple shift of mass centroids but involves a broadening and reshaping of the entire spectral function, with the notable exception of the sigma meson which may soften and sharpen.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches incorporating chiral dynamics into the many-body problem of interacting hadrons, using effective Lagrangians constrained by lattice data and experimental information from intermediate energy machines to relativistic heavy ion collisions. The author discusses the in-medium behavior of hadronic spectral functions, particularly for pions, the sigma meson, and the rho meson, using chiral effective field theory, the linear sigma model, and the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model, with calculations of the in-medium chiral condensate, pion decay constant, and scalar susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- The chiral quark condensate in vacuum is approximately -(240 MeV)^3, derived from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation with f_pi = 94 MeV.
- The critical temperature for deconfinement and chiral restoration from lattice simulations is T_c = 170 MeV.
- The pionic fluctuation contributes approximately 20% dropping of the chiral condensate at nuclear saturation density rho_0, and the scalar piece also contributes approximately 20% dropping at rho_0.
- The in-medium isovector scattering length b_1^* / b_1 is approximately 1 + 0.18 (rho / rho_0), from the combined effect of energy dependence and in-medium renormalization.
- The convergence of scalar and pseudoscalar susceptibilities in the model occurs at densities larger than 3 rho_0.
- The matching scale for the HLS model is Lambda ~ 1 GeV.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on chiral symmetry restoration in hadronic matter, including the Rho-Brown dropping scenario (Brown and Rho, 1991) and the axial-vector mixing mechanism (Dey, Eletsky, and Ioffe, 1990; Chanfray, Davesne, and Ericson, 1999). It discusses the interpretation of experimental data from the TAPS collaboration on two-pion photoproduction (Messerchendorp, 2002) and the CERES collaboration on dilepton production (Agakichiev, 2001). The paper enables future work connecting effective field theories to QCD via Wilsonian renormalization group methods (Harada and Yamawaki, 2001) and lattice QCD at finite chemical potential, and identifies open questions about the nature of the phase transition at high baryonic density and the chiral status of resonances such as the N*(1520).
