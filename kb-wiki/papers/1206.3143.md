# Quark phase-space distributions and orbital angular momentum

> We discuss the Wigner functions of the nucleon which provide multi-dimensional images of the quark distributions in phase space. They combine in a single picture all the information contained in the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs). In particular, we present results for the distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon obtained in a light-cone constituent quark model. We show how quark orbital angular momentum can be extracted from these distributions and compare it with alternative definitions given in terms of the GPDs and the TMDs.

## Key claim

The paper shows that quark Wigner distributions, which combine information from GPDs and TMDs, can be calculated in a light-cone constituent quark model free of relativistic corrections, and that the quark orbital angular momentum extracted from these distributions depends on the choice of gauge link (straight vs. staple-like), leading to different values for separate quark flavors even though the total quark OAM remains the same across definitions.

## Method

The authors computed quark Wigner distributions as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) in impact-parameter space, using a light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) and a light-front version of the chiral quark-soliton model (LFχQSM) restricted to the three-quark sector. They extracted the quark orbital angular momentum from the Wigner distributions using both a straight gauge link (giving the kinetic OAM of Ji) and a staple-like gauge link (giving the canonical OAM of Jaffe-Manohar), and compared these with a TMD-based definition.

## Key numbers

* In the LFCQM, the kinetic OAM (Ji) for u quarks is 0.071, for d quarks is 0.055, and total is 0.126.
* In the LFCQM, the canonical OAM (Jaffe-Manohar) for u quarks is 0.131, for d quarks is -0.005, and total is 0.126.
* In the LFCQM, the TMD-based OAM for u quarks is 0.169, for d quarks is -0.042, and total is 0.126.
* In the LFχQSM, the kinetic OAM (Ji) for u quarks is -0.008, for d quarks is 0.077, and total is 0.069.
* In the LFχQSM, the canonical OAM (Jaffe-Manohar) for u quarks is 0.073, for d quarks is -0.004, and total is 0.069.
* In the LFχQSM, the TMD-based OAM for u quarks is 0.093, for d quarks is -0.023, and total is 0.069.
* The distortion of the Wigner distribution for unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized proton was shown for a fixed transverse momentum k_perp = 0.3 GeV.
* The d-quark OAM changes sign around 0.25 fm away from the transverse center of momentum.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Wigner distributions in QCD, first explored by Ji (2003) where relativistic effects were neglected, by using the impact-parameter representation of GTMDs identified by Meissner (2009) to obtain five-dimensional Wigner distributions free of relativistic corrections. The results enable comparison of different definitions of quark orbital angular momentum: the kinetic OAM from Ji's sum rule (1996) involving GPDs, the canonical OAM from Jaffe and Manohar (1989), and a TMD-based definition, showing that in quark models the total OAM is the same across definitions but flavor-separated contributions differ. The LFχQSM predicts a negative sign for u-quark OAM, in agreement with lattice calculations by Hagler (2007), unlike the LFCQM.
