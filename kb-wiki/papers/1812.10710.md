# On the cancellation of radiative corrections to the cross section of electron-proton scattering

> The largest radiative corrections to the cross section of electron-proton scattering at high energies are associated with emission of photons, real and virtual, by electron. They contain large logarithms coming from soft and collinear photons. Cancellation of the contributions of the soft photons to virtual and real corrections is well known. Less known is the fact that the contributions of the photons collinear to scattered electrons are also cancelled for the most of experiments. On the contrary, contributions of the photons collinear to initial electrons as a rule are not cancelled. It is shown, however, that these contributions are cancelled for the experimental set up suggested by A.A. Vorobev for measurement of proton radius.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the specific experimental setup proposed by A.A. Vorobev for measuring the proton radius, where the recoil proton is detected instead of the scattered electron, in the region of low momentum transfer \(Q^2 \ll E_l^2\) and \(Q^2 \ll E_l M\), the largest radiative corrections (those containing collinear logarithms \(\ln(Q^2/m^2)\)) cancel completely in the one-loop approximation, and this cancellation extends to higher orders when electron-positron pair production is forbidden.

## Method

The authors analyze one-loop radiative corrections to electron-proton scattering, separating virtual vertex corrections and real photon emission contributions (soft and hard). They apply the quasi-real electron method of Baier et al. (1973) with logarithmic accuracy to compute hard photon emission from initial and final electrons, and use exact results from Baier (197?) for photon emission in muon-electron collisions to demonstrate cancellation beyond logarithmic accuracy. For higher orders, they employ the parton distribution formalism developed for deep inelastic scattering by Gribov and Lipatov (1972) and Altarelli and Parisi (1977), treating the process as inclusive proton-electron scattering and using the Callan-Gross relation (1969).

## Key numbers

- The proposed experiment aims to measure \(Q^2\) in the range from 0.001 to 0.04 GeV\(^2\), with a target precision of 0.6 percent for the proton radius and 0.2 percent accuracy for the cross section \(d\sigma/(d Q^2)\).
- The one-loop vertex correction for the electron contains the term \(\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \left[-\frac{1}{2}\ln^2(Q^2/m^2) + \frac{3}{2}\ln(Q^2/m^2) + \pi^2/6 - 2\right]\) (with \(\lambda\) regularization), and the soft photon contribution includes \(\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \left[-\frac{1}{4}\ln^2(4E_l^2/m^2) - \frac{1}{4}\ln^2(4E_l'^2/m^2) + \cdots\right]\).
- The hard photon emission from the final electron, integrated with logarithmic accuracy, gives \(\frac{\alpha}{\pi}\ln(Q^2/m^2)\left(\ln(E'_l/\omega_0) - 3/4\right) d\sigma_B\).
- The exact result for photon emission by the electron in the experimental conditions yields \(d\sigma^{e.e.} = \frac{d\sigma_B}{dQ^2}\frac{\alpha}{\pi}\left[2\left(\ln(Q^2/m^2)-1\right)\ln(E_l/\omega_0) - \frac{3}{2}\ln(Q^2/m^2) + 2\right]\).

## Context

This work addresses the "proton radius puzzle" arising from the discrepancy between muonic hydrogen measurements at PSI (Pohl, 2010; Antognini, 2013) and electron-proton scattering and hydrogen spectroscopy (Mohr, 2008; Bernauer, 2014). It extends prior calculations of radiative corrections by Tsai (1961) and Maximon (2000), which were developed for experiments detecting scattered electrons, to the new recoil-proton detection setup of Vorobev at MAMI. The cancellation property enables a simpler treatment of radiative corrections for this specific experiment, potentially allowing the 0.2 percent cross-section accuracy needed to resolve the puzzle.
