# Fermionic excitations at finite temperature and density

> We study fermionic excitations in a hot and dense strongly interacting medium consisting of quarks and (pseudo-)scalar mesons. In particular, we use the two-flavor quark-meson model in combination with the Functional Renormalization Group approach, which allows to take into account the effects from thermal and quantum fluctuations. The resulting fermionic excitation spectrum is investigated by calculating the quark spectral function at finite temperature, quark chemical potential, and spatial momentum. This involves an analytic continuation from imaginary to real energies by extending the previously introduced analytically-continued FRG (aFRG) method to the present case. We identify three different collective excitations in the medium: the ordinary thermal quark, the plasmino mode, and an ultra-soft "phonino" mode. The dispersion relations of these modes are extracted from the quark spectral function. When compared to corresponding results from an FRG-improved one-loop calculation remarkable agreement is found.

# Fermionic excitations at finite temperature and density

## Key claim
The quark spectral function at finite temperature and density, computed via the analytically-continued Functional Renormalization Group (aFRG) method in the two-flavor quark-meson model, reveals three distinct collective excitations, the ordinary thermal quark, the plasmino mode, and an ultra-soft "phonino" mode, whose dispersion relations show remarkable agreement with an FRG-improved one-loop calculation, though the agreement is primarily qualitative in the thermal continuum regime at smaller energies.

## Method
The authors applied the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) approach to the two-flavor quark-meson model, using the Wetterich equation with a three-dimensional regulator to compute the scale-dependent effective potential and quark two-point function. They performed an analytic continuation from imaginary to real energies by extending the analytically-continued FRG (aFRG) method, which exploits the periodicity of bosonic and fermionic occupation numbers with respect to Euclidean Matsubara frequencies and then replaces the Euclidean frequency by a continuous real energy. The resulting flow equations for the dressing functions of the retarded quark propagator were solved numerically, and the quark spectral function was extracted from the imaginary part of the propagator.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 93.5$ MeV; pion mass: $m_\pi = 138$ MeV; sigma mass: $m_\sigma = 509$ MeV; quark mass: $m_\psi = 299$ MeV (infrared values from the FRG calculation).
- Quark pole mass in vacuum: $m_\psi^p \approx 320$ MeV; at $T = 100$ MeV: $m_\psi^p \approx 300$ MeV.
- Chiral crossover temperature at zero chemical potential: $T_c \approx 170$ MeV.
- At $T = 300$ MeV, the phonino mode mass: $m_{\text{phonino}} \approx 15$ MeV; the thermal quasi-particle peak mass: $m_\psi^p \approx 345$ MeV.
- Spectral weight of the phonino mode: approximately 50% of the total spectral weight.
- Critical endpoint (CEP) location: $T_{\text{CEP}} \approx 9$ MeV and $\mu_{\text{CEP}} \approx 292$ MeV.
- UV cutoff: $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the aFRG method, previously applied to mesonic spectral functions (Tripolt 2014, Tripolt 2014a) and to quark spectral functions at zero temperature and zero chemical potential (Tripolt 2018), to finite temperature, finite chemical potential, and finite spatial momentum. The results confirm the existence of the phonino mode predicted by earlier beyond-HTL calculations (Kitazawa, Kunihiro, Nemoto 2006; Hidaka, Satow, Kunihiro 2012; Blaizot, Satow 2014) and show that the FRG-improved one-loop calculation (Wang and He 2018) reproduces the FRG results remarkably well. The study enables future investigations of quark spectral functions at chemical potentials beyond the critical endpoint, though this region is currently hampered by negative-entropy regimes within FRG calculations.
