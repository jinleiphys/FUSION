# Neutrino-12C scattering in the ab initio shell model with a realistic three-body interaction

> We investigate cross sections for neutrino-12C exclusive scattering and for muon capture on 12C using wave functions obtained in the ab initio no-core shell model. In our parameter-free calculations with basis spaces up to the 6 hbarOmega we show that realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, like e.g. the CD-Bonn, under predict the experimental cross sections by more than a factor of two. By including a realistic three-body interaction, Tucson-Melbourne TM'(99), the cross sections are enhanced significantly and a much better agreement with experiment is achieved. At the same time,the TM'(99) interaction improves the calculated level ordering in 12C. The comparison between the CD-Bonn and the three-body calculations provides strong confirmation for the need to include a realistic three-body interaction to account for the spin-orbit strength in p-shell nuclei.

# Neutrino-12C scattering in the ab initio shell model with a realistic three-body interaction

## Key claim

Realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions alone under-predict experimental neutrino-12C exclusive scattering cross sections by more than a factor of two, but including the Tucson-Melbourne TM'(99) three-body interaction significantly enhances the cross sections and achieves much better agreement with experiment, though the predicted electron scattering form factor still peaks at too large a momentum transfer and is too large at momentum transfers relevant to the LSND decay-in-flight cross section.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 12C using basis spaces up to 6 hbarOmega, employing the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction alone and the AV8' interaction combined with the TM'(99) three-nucleon interaction. They derived effective interactions appropriate for the finite basis space using a cluster approximation with a Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation, solved the three-nucleon system for all possible channels, and computed transition densities for evaluating electron scattering form factors, muon capture rates, and neutrino scattering cross sections to the T=1 1+ states in mass 12.

## Key numbers

- CD-Bonn interaction (without three-body force) under-predicts the neutrino scattering cross section from decay-at-rest pions by a factor of 2.4 at 6 hbarOmega
- AV8' plus TM'(99) three-body interaction at 4 hbarOmega predicts a neutrino cross section only 30% lower than experiment
- CD-Bonn muon capture rate at 6 hbarOmega under-predicts experiment by a factor of 2.6
- AV8' plus TM'(99) at 4 hbarOmega gives muon capture rate 34% lower than experiment
- For the transverse magnetic form factor at the first maximum, including the three-body interaction increased the magnitude by about 75% compared to the CD-Bonn calculation at 4 hbarOmega
- The position of the first minimum in the form factor shifted from q_min ~ 400 MeV/c (CD-Bonn) to 360 MeV/c (with three-body interaction)
- CD-Bonn at 6 hbarOmega under-predicts the (nu_mu, mu-) cross section by a factor of 1.8 compared with experiment
- AV8' plus TM'(99) at 4 hbarOmega agrees with experiment for the (nu_mu, mu-) cross section

## Context

This work extends previous no-core shell model calculations for 12C (C12_NCSM) that used only two-nucleon interactions, and addresses the well-established problem that realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions alone account for only half the observed p-shell splitting (Pieper, Carlson, Wiringa). The Gamow-Teller transition from 12C ground state to the T=1 1+ isobar triplet is a sensitive test of spin-orbit strength, with the pure SU(4) alpha-cluster limit forbidding the transition and the pure jj-coupling closed p3/2 shell overestimating it by almost a factor of 6 (Kolbe, nu_C12_SM). The results confirm the need for three-body forces to account for spin-orbit strength in p-shell nuclei, and the authors note that a single experiment/theory scale factor cannot be defined for all three weak processes examined due to the different momentum transfers involved.
