# Strong-interaction isospin-symmetry breaking within the density functional theory

> The conventional Skyrme interaction is generalized by adding zero-range charge-symmetry-breaking and charge-independence-breaking terms, and the corresponding energy density functional is derived. It is shown that the extended model accounts for experimental values of mirror and triplet displacement energies (MDEs and TDEs) in sd-shell isospin triplets with, on average, about 100~keV precision using only two additional adjustable coupling constants. Moreover, the model is able to reproduce, for the first time, the A=4n versus A=4n+2 staggering of the TDEs.

## Key claim

The extended Skyrme density functional, with only two additional adjustable coupling constants for charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) and charge-independence-breaking (CIB) zero-range terms, can reproduce experimental mirror and triplet displacement energies in sd-shell isospin triplets to about 100 keV precision on average, and for the first time accounts for the staggering of triplet displacement energies between A=4n and A=4n+2 nuclei.

## Method

The authors generalized the conventional isospin-invariant Skyrme interaction by adding zero-range class II (CIB) and class III (CSB) terms, derived the corresponding energy density functional contributions, and implemented them in the HFODD code using a pn-mixed density functional theory approach with isocranking to control the isospin degree of freedom. The two new coupling constants (t0^II = 20 MeV and t0^III = -8 MeV) were fitted perturbatively to the residuals between experimental and conventional-model values of triplet displacement energies and mirror displacement energies, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The fitted coupling constants are t0^II = 20 MeV and t0^III = -8 MeV.
- Without hadronic ISB forces, discrepancies between experimental and theoretical MDEs (the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly) are of order 1 MeV; for TDEs, discrepancies average 0.3 MeV.
- With the extended model, the average disagreement between experiment and theory is reduced to about 100 keV for TDEs and 130 keV for MDEs.
- The extended model reproduces, for the first time, the staggering of TDEs between A=4n and A=4n+2 isospin triplets in the sd-shell.

## Context

This work extends the conventional Skyrme mean-field approach, which is isospin-invariant and relies on the Coulomb interaction as the sole source of isospin-symmetry breaking, as studied by Nolen and Schiffer (1969) and others. The results address the long-standing Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and the inability of conventional models to reproduce triplet displacement energy staggering. The extended model enables future studies of isospin-sensitive phenomena within density functional theory, and the authors note that full three-dimensional isospin projection is under development for more rigorous treatment.
