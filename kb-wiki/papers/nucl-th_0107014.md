# In-medium nucleon-nucleon potentials in configuration space

> Based on the thermodynamic Green function approach two-nucleon correlations in nuclear matter at finite temperatures are revisited. To this end, we derive phase equivalent effective $r$-space potentials that include the effect of the Pauli blocking at a given temperature and density. These potentials enter into a Schrödinger equation that is the $r$-space representation of the Galitskii-Feynman equation for two nucleons. We explore the analytical structure of the equation in the complex $k$-plane by means of Jost functions. We find that despite the Mott effect the correlation with deuteron quantum numbers are manifested as antibound states, i.e., as zeros of the Jost function on the negative imaginary axis of the complex momentum space. The analysis presented here is also suited for Coulombic systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, above the Mott density, the deuteron-like two-nucleon correlation does not disappear but instead manifests as an *antibound state* (a zero of the Jost function on the negative imaginary axis of the complex momentum plane), rather than as a resonance in the first quadrant; this result is conditional on the use of an effective-mass approximation and a local bare potential (Malfliet-Tjon I+III) at a fixed temperature of 10 MeV.

## Method

The authors start from the thermodynamic Green function approach and derive an effective in-medium Schrödinger-type equation for two nucleons, where the Pauli blocking is incorporated via an energy-dependent factor (1-2f(E)) multiplying the bare potential. They then use the Marchenko inverse scattering method (fixed-ℓ inversion) to construct phase-equivalent, energy-independent local potentials from the calculated phase shifts. Finally, they analytically continue these potentials into the complex r-plane and locate the zeros of the Jost function in the complex k-plane to identify bound, antibound, and resonant states.

## Key numbers

* The bare Malfliet-Tjon I+III potential gives a three-nucleon binding energy of 8.595 MeV, close to the experimental 8.48 MeV.
* Normal nuclear matter density is taken as ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³; the study focuses on densities near the Mott transition at roughly ρ₀/10.
* Five in-medium potentials are constructed at T = 10 MeV for densities: 0.003, 0.007, 0.009, 0.017, and 0.034 fm⁻³.
* The pole on the imaginary k-axis moves from +0.2324i fm⁻¹ (bound state for the bare MT potential) to -0.2640i fm⁻¹ (antibound state) for the highest density (0.034 fm⁻³).
* The effective mass m_eff ranges from 925.43 MeV (ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³) down to 809.65 MeV (ρ = 0.034 fm⁻³).
* The Marchenko inversion uses phase shifts up to a cut-off energy of ~1500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier momentum-space studies of in-medium two-nucleon correlations (Schmidt, Röpke, and Schulz 1990; Beyer and Röpke 1997) by constructing equivalent local r-space potentials, enabling analytic continuation to complex momenta. It enables future three- and four-body in-medium calculations using AGS-type equations and provides a method applicable to Coulombic systems with Debye screening. The approach contrasts with self-consistent Galitskii-Feynman solutions (Bozek 1999) that yield finite lifetimes for clusters, whereas the present work finds that the antibound state retains a quasiparticle character with no imaginary part.
