# Bulk viscosity in 2SC and CFL quark matter

> The bulk viscosities of two color-superconducting phases, the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase and the 2SC phase, are computed and compared to the result for unpaired quark matter. In the case of the CFL phase, processes involving kaons and the superfluid mode give the largest contribution to the bulk viscosity since all fermionic modes are gapped. In the case of the 2SC phase, ungapped fermionic modes are present and the process u+d <-> u+s provides the dominant contribution. In both cases, the bulk viscosity can become larger than that of the unpaired phase for sufficiently large temperatures (T >~ 1 MeV for CFL, T >~ 0.1 MeV for 2SC). Bulk viscosity (as well as shear viscosity) is important for the damping of r-modes in compact stars and thus can potentially be used as an indirect signal for the presence or absence of color-superconducting quark matter.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of the CFL phase can become larger than that of unpaired quark matter for temperatures above about 1 MeV, and the bulk viscosity of the 2SC phase can become larger than that of unpaired quark matter for temperatures above about 0.1 MeV, but these results depend on the poorly known kaon mass and chemical potential in the CFL case and on the assumption that no kaon condensate is present.

## Method

The bulk viscosity was computed from the dissipated power per volume during periodic volume oscillations, using the standard relation ζ = (B²/C) × γ/(γ²+ω²) where γ is the inverse time scale of the relevant microscopic process and ω is the oscillation frequency. For the 2SC phase, the rate of the nonleptonic process u+d ↔ u+s was calculated from weak-interaction diagrams, accounting for the fact that only one out of nine color combinations involves only unpaired quarks at low temperatures. For the CFL phase, the dominant contributions come from the processes φ ↔ φ+φ (superfluid mode) and K⁰ ↔ φ+φ (kaon decay), computed within effective theories of Goldstone bosons.

## Key numbers

- For the 2SC phase at low temperatures (T ≪ Δ), the bulk viscosity is ζ_2SC = ζ_unp / 9, where ζ_unp is the bulk viscosity of unpaired quark matter.
- The CFL bulk viscosity from the φ ↔ φ+φ process in the ω → 0 limit is ζ^φ_CFL = 0.011 × M_s⁴ / T, where M_s is the strange quark mass.
- The CFL phase bulk viscosity becomes larger than that of unpaired quark matter for T ≳ 1 MeV; the 2SC phase bulk viscosity becomes larger for T ≳ 0.1 MeV.
- The oscillation period used in the calculations is τ = 2π/ω = 1 ms, corresponding to typical r-mode frequencies.
- A critical temperature of T_c = 30 MeV is assumed for the 2SC phase.
- For T < 0.1 MeV in the CFL phase, the φ mean free path becomes on the order of the size of the star, so the hydrodynamic result ceases to be meaningful.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of bulk viscosity in unpaired quark matter by Madsen (1999) and in conventional neutron star matter by Haensel and collaborators (2000-2001), as well as hyperonic matter calculations by Lindblom and Owen (2001). It provides the first systematic comparison of bulk viscosities between the 2SC and CFL color-superconducting phases and unpaired quark matter, enabling predictions for r-mode damping in compact stars that could serve as indirect signals for the presence of color-superconducting quark matter. The results depend on parameters such as the kaon mass and chemical potential that are poorly constrained at compact-star densities, motivating future work on kaon-condensed phases and on the collisionless-to-hydrodynamic transition at very low temperatures.
