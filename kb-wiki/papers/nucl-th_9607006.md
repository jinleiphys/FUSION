# Neutron and proton spectra from the decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei

> We have determined the spectra of neutrons and protons following the decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei through the one- and two-nucleon induced mechanisms. The momentum distributions of the primary nucleons are calculated and a Monte Carlo simulation is used to account for final state interactions. From the spectra we calculate the number of neutrons ($N_n$) and protons ($N_p$) per $Λ$ decay and show how the measurement of these quantities, particularly $N_p$, can lead to a determination of $Γ_n / Γ_p$, the ratio of neutron to proton induced $Λ$ decay. We also show that the consideration of the two-nucleon induced channel has a repercussion in the results, widening the band of allowed values of $Γ_n / Γ_p$ with respect to what is obtained neglecting this channel.

## Key claim

The measurement of the number of protons per Lambda decay (N_p) is more selective for determining the ratio Gamma_n / Gamma_p than either the number of neutrons per decay (N_n) or the ratio N_n/N_p, but the inclusion of the two-nucleon induced decay channel widens the band of allowed values of Gamma_n/Gamma_p compared to analyses that neglect this channel, and the results remain compatible with the one-pion exchange model predictions.

## Method

The authors calculated the momentum distributions of primary nucleons from one-nucleon (1N) and two-nucleon (2N) induced Lambda decay using a local density approximation formalism for finite hypernuclei, starting from the Lambda self-energy in infinite nuclear matter. They then used a Monte Carlo simulation to account for final state interactions of nucleons as they propagate through the nucleus, including quasielastic collisions, pion production, and charge exchange, with the ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- For a threshold cut of 30 MeV, the experimental values from Szymanski et al. (1991) gave N_n^{TOT} = 3400 ± 1100 and N_p^{TOT} = 1270 ± 180, leading to allowed Gamma_n/Gamma_p values in the range 0.15–2.0 when including the 2N induced channel.
- For a threshold cut of 40 MeV, the experimental values gave N_n^{TOT} = 2530 ± 1050 and N_p^{TOT} = 1112 ± 130, leading to allowed Gamma_n/Gamma_p values in the range 0.0–1.65.
- For Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.5 and a 30 MeV cut, the calculated numbers per Lambda decay were N_n = 1.28 and N_p = 0.62.
- The 2N induced channel contributes about 20% of the total Lambda width in the nucleus, or 30% of the free Lambda width.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ramos, Oset, and Salcedo (1994) on the two-nucleon induced Lambda decay channel by including final state interactions and providing full nucleon energy spectra, rather than assuming all emitted particles are detected. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the one-pion exchange model predictions (Gamma_n/Gamma_p ~ 0.1) and earlier experimental claims of values near unity, showing that the large experimental errors and the inclusion of the 2N channel make the data compatible with OPE predictions. The results enable future experiments, particularly those measuring N_p as advocated by the collaboration of Hashimoto (private communication), to determine Gamma_n/Gamma_p more reliably.
