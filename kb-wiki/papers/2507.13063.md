# Systematic study of the propagation of uncertainties to transfer observables

> A systematic study of parametric uncertainties in transfer reactions is performed using the recently developed uncertainty quantified global optical potential (KDUQ). We consider reactions on the doubly-magic spherical nucleus $^{48}$Ca and explore the dependence of the predicted $(d,p)$ angular distribution uncertainties at different beam energies and for different properties of the final single-particle state populated by the reaction. Our results show that correlations between the uncertainties associated with the bound state potential and with the optical potentials may be important for correctly determining the uncertainty in the transfer cross sections (in our case, these do not add in quadrature). In general, we find small uncertainties in the predicted transfer observables: half-width of the 68% credible interval is roughly $5-10$%, which is comparable to the experimental error on the transfer data. Finally, our results show that the relative magnitude of the parametric uncertainty in transfer observables increases with the beam energy and does not depend strongly on the properties of the final state.

# Systematic study of the propagation of uncertainties to transfer observables

## Key claim

The parametric uncertainty in transfer (d,p) cross sections predicted using the global optical potential KDUQ is small (half-width of the 68% credible interval roughly 5–10%), but this result depends on the consistent treatment of correlations between the bound-state and optical potentials; when these correlations are neglected, the uncertainties do not add in quadrature and the total uncertainty can be larger than the quadrature sum would suggest.

## Method

The authors performed adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) calculations for the reaction ⁴⁸Ca(d,p)⁴⁹Ca(g.s.) using the uncertainty-quantified global optical potential KDUQ, which provides 416 posterior samples for 46 optical-model parameters. They propagated these samples through the reaction model to obtain credible intervals for the transfer angular distributions, and compared results obtained when all three nucleon-nucleus optical potentials (neutron-target, proton-target, proton-residual) were taken consistently from the same KDUQ sample versus from independent samples. For the bound-state potential, they either used a fixed standard geometry or took the geometry from the real part of KDUQ (KDUQ-real), refitting the depth to reproduce the neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

- Half-width of the 68% credible interval for the ⁴⁸Ca(d,p)⁴⁹Ca(g.s.) angular distribution at 19 MeV: 5% using original KDUQ, 16% using KDUQ-n (rescaled neutron potential).
- The empirical coverage for neutron elastic scattering on ⁴⁸Ca at 12 MeV required rescaling the KDUQ neutron-potential covariance matrix by a factor of 38 (i.e., uncertainties scaled by √38 ≈ 6) to achieve correct 68% coverage.
- The ANC-squared predicted by KDUQ-real for the ⁴⁸Ca(d,p)⁴⁹Ca(g.s.) reaction is 28.6 ± 1.3 fm⁻¹, consistent with the literature value 32.1 ± 3.2 fm⁻¹.
- The relative half-width ε₆₈% increases with beam energy; at the highest beam energies studied, the shift in ε₆₈% when correlations between optical potentials are neglected is about 25%.
- Uncertainties remain below 10% for all final-state properties studied (separation energies from 1.146 to 15.146 MeV, angular momenta l=0 and l=1, and number of radial nodes n_r=0 or 1).

## Context

This work extends the uncertainty quantification framework developed for the KDUQ global optical potential (KDUQ, 2023) to a systematic study of transfer reactions, building on earlier Bayesian studies of (d,p) uncertainties by Lovell (2018), King (2018), Lovell (2021), and Catacora-Robles (2023). Unlike those studies, which used local optical potentials fitted to single projectile-target combinations and treated each optical potential independently, this work uses a global parametrization that includes correlations between all optical potentials. The results enable more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients from transfer data, and show that the global approach yields smaller and more realistic uncertainties than local-potential approaches. The finding that correlations between bound-state and optical potentials prevent simple quadrature addition of uncertainties contradicts the assumption made in Catacora-Robles (2023) and highlights the need for consistent treatment of all potential parameters.
