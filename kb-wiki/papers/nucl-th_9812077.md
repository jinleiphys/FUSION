# Two-photon processes in two-nucleon systems as a tool of searching for exotic six-quark resonances

> The reaction $pp \to pp2γ$, proposed earlier to probe for the NN--decoupled dibaryon resonances, has been studied by the DIB$2γ$ Collaboration (JINR) and their preliminary data seem to give evidence for the resonance effect at about 1920 MeV. We discuss some other two-photon processes: the double radiative capture reaction in pionic deuterium, photoabsorption sum rules and the nuclear Compton scattering, which we consider to be especially feasible for further test and invesigation of possible low-lying, exotic six-quark states.

## Key claim

Preliminary data from the DIB$2\gamma$ Collaboration (JINR) on the $pp \to pp2\gamma$ reaction give evidence for a narrow exotic dibaryon resonance at about 1920 MeV, but this result requires confirmation through other two-photon processes such as double radiative capture in pionic deuterium, photoabsorption sum rules, and nuclear Compton scattering, which are proposed as feasible tests.

## Method

The paper analyzes the $pp \to pp2\gamma$ reaction data at a proton energy of approximately 200 MeV, where a distinct enhancement at photon energy about 42 MeV was observed. The author then develops a phenomenological model using a Hulthen-type radial wave function for the $d_{1}^{\ast}\Delta N$-vertex to estimate cross sections and branching ratios for the double radiative capture reaction in pionic deuterium ($\pi^{-}d \to \gamma d_{1}^{\ast} \to \gamma\gamma X$). The model treats the reaction as a two-step process via an intermediate $N\Delta$-state, using the long-wave approximation for electric-dipole and magnetic-dipole transitions, and estimates the resonance contribution to the deuteron Compton scattering cross section and the polarized photon-deuteron sum rule.

## Key numbers

- The observed peak in the $pp \to pp2\gamma$ reaction at proton energy $\sim 200$ MeV corresponds to a dibaryon mass $M_B = 1923.5 \pm 4.5$ MeV with FWHM $= 31.3 \pm 5.0$ MeV; statistical significance exceeds $8\sigma$.
- The estimated differential cross section for the $pp \to \gamma d_{1}^{\ast}$ process in the center-of-mass frame is approximately $40$ nb/sr, compared to an experimental estimate of $65$ nb/sr from the DIB$2\gamma$ data.
- The estimated branching ratio for the radiative transition $(\pi^{-}d)_{atom} \to \gamma d_{1}^{\ast}$ in the $1S$-state is $0.6\%$, close to the experimental upper bound of $\le 0.4\%$ for undetected channels.
- The non-resonant background branching ratio for $(\pi^{-}d)_{atom} \to \gamma\gamma X$ is estimated as $3.4 \times 10^{-5}$, based on the ratio $BR(\pi^{-}p \to 2\gamma/1\gamma) \simeq 1.3 \times 10^{-4}$ from Beder.
- The static magnetic polarizability contribution from the $d_{1}^{\ast}$ resonance is $\beta_{stat} \simeq 2 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, with the resonance photon energy $\omega_r \simeq 45$ MeV.
- The contribution of the $d_{1}^{\ast}$ resonance to the polarized photon-deuteron sum rule is about $-10$ $\mu$b.

## Context

This work extends the search for NN-decoupled dibaryon resonances, specifically the $d_{1}^{\ast}$ candidate seen in the $pp \to pp2\gamma$ reaction by the DIB$2\gamma$ Collaboration, to other radiative processes. It builds on earlier proposals by Gerasimov and collaborators (refs. a3, a4) for using double photon emission in proton-proton interactions, and on the TRIUMF measurements of pionic deuterium capture branching ratios (refs. a8, a9). The paper also references the chiral soliton model prediction by Kopeliovich (1995) for a $IJ^P=11^{+}$ state near 1940 MeV, and the GDH sum rule applied to deuterons by Gerasimov (1967). The proposed measurements of double radiative pion capture in deuterium and deuteron Compton scattering could discriminate between isospin assignments ($I=0$ vs $I=2$) for the candidate resonance, and enable further tests of six-quark exotic states.
