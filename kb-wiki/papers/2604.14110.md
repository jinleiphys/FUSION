# Non-Gaussian fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics: Confluent equations for three-point correlations

> We derive deterministic equations for the evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuations in relativistic stochastic hydrodynamics. This is achieved by defining the average local Landau frame and corresponding fluctuating hydrodynamic variables. Fully nonlinear stochastic hydrodynamics is expressed in a unified multi-component matrix form. A novel relativistic formalism, also manifestly covariant under SO(3) rotations of the local spatial basis in the average local Landau frame, is introduced. The equations describe correlators of all hydrodynamic variables, including fluctuating velocity (or momentum density) -- a nontrivial problem in relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors derive deterministic evolution equations for three-point (non-Gaussian) correlation functions of all hydrodynamic variables in relativistic stochastic hydrodynamics, including fluctuating velocity (or momentum density), which had been neglected in prior work on non-Gaussian fluctuations. The derivation relies on defining an average local Landau frame that does not fluctuate, and on truncating the expansion of the fully nonlinear stochastic equations at bilinear order in fluctuations.

## Method

The authors begin by defining the average local Landau frame via the condition that the average momentum density vanishes, and express the fluctuating energy-momentum tensor and charge current in this non-fluctuating frame. They write the fully nonlinear stochastic hydrodynamic equations in a unified multi-component matrix form, truncated to second order in spatial gradients (the lowest dissipative order). Using a confluent formalism with parallel transport (boosts and local SO(3) rotations) to handle the space-time dependence of the average flow velocity, they expand the equations in fluctuations around the mean and obtain deterministic evolution equations for the confluent (SO(3)-covariant) two-point and three-point correlation functions. The Wigner transform is then applied to obtain equations for generalized Wigner functions describing correlations of wavelets at given wave-vectors.

## Key numbers

* The hierarchy of scales assumed is \(k \sim q^2 \ll q \ll 1\) (in microscopic units), where \(k\) is the wavenumber of background inhomogeneities and \(q\) is the wavenumber of fluctuations, with fluctuations at \(q \sim q_* \sim \sqrt{k}\) evolving at rates comparable to the fluid flow.
* The noise correlator is given by \(\langle \eta^{\widehat c}(x_1) \eta^{\widehat d}(x_2) \rangle = 2 \delta^{\widehat c \widehat d} \delta^{(4)}(x_1 - x_2)\).
* The paper focuses on three-point correlators (\(N=3\)), the lowest order of non-Gaussianity, motivated by STAR collaboration measurements of factorial cumulants up to order \(N \le 4\) in gold-gold collisions reported in Ref. STAR (2025).

## Context

This work extends prior derivations of deterministic equations for Gaussian fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics (An 2019, An 2019) and for non-Gaussian fluctuations in simpler systems such as nonlinear diffusion of charge density (An 2021, An 2023), where velocity fluctuations were neglected. The new equations for three-point correlators including all hydrodynamic modes, especially fluctuating velocity, are the first of their kind. The results enable more realistic modeling of non-equilibrium evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, which is crucial for interpreting critical point signatures in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, particularly the STAR collaboration's recent measurements of proton multiplicity fluctuations (STAR 2025).
