# A digital quantum simulation of the Agassi model

> A digital quantum simulation of the Agassi model from nuclear physics is proposed and analyzed. The proposal is worked out for the case with four different sites. Numerical simulations and analytical estimations are presented to illustrate the feasibility of this proposal with current technology. The proposed approach is fully scalable to a larger number of sites. The use of a quantum correlation function as a probe to explore the quantum phases by quantum simulating the time dynamics, with no need of computing the ground state, is also studied. Evidence is given showing that the amplitude of the time dynamics of a correlation function in this quantum simulation is linked to the different quantum phases of the system. This approach establishes an avenue for the digital quantum simulation of useful models in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a digital quantum simulation of the Agassi model with four sites (j=1) is feasible with current trapped-ion technology, but only for this minimal case; for the four-site system the Hamiltonian reduces to a single effective control parameter (g+V), meaning the simulation can only distinguish between a symmetric phase and a broken-symmetry phase, not between the superconducting and parity-broken phases that exist in the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors map the Agassi Hamiltonian for four fermionic sites (j=1) onto a spin Hamiltonian via the Jordan-Wigner transformation, then decompose the time evolution operator using a Lie-Trotter-Suzuki digital protocol. They numerically simulate the Trotterized dynamics for a specific initial state (|↓↓↑↑⟩) and compare the fidelity of the digital approximation to exact evolution, while also computing the time-dependent correlation function σ_z(1,2) as a phase probe. The implementation is analyzed in terms of gate counts for trapped-ion platforms using Mølmer-Sørensen gates.

## Key numbers

- The four-site (j=1) Agassi model has a Hilbert space dimension of 2^4 = 16.
- The critical point separating the symmetric phase from the broken-symmetry phase occurs at g+V = 1 (equivalently g = V = 0.5 with ε = 1).
- For a Trotter expansion with n_T = 10 Trotter steps, the digital fidelity remains high over a large time interval; with n_T = 5, the estimated total gate error E_G ≃ 0.28, implying a fidelity above 70%.
- The proposed 4-qubit implementation requires 52 single-qubit gates and 50 two-qubit gates per Trotter step, assuming single-qubit gate error of 0.0001 and two-qubit gate error of 0.001.
- The maximum of the correlation function σ_z(1,2) reaches the value 1 at the critical point g = V = 0.5 and remains at 1 in the broken-symmetry phase (g+V ≥ 1).

## Context

This work extends the study of the Agassi model, originally introduced by Agassi (1968) as an extension of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, and whose phase diagram was derived by Davis and Heiss (1986) using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov methods. The paper provides a first digital quantum simulation proposal for this nuclear physics model, enabling future studies of finite-N effects that are inaccessible to mean-field methods and classical computation for larger systems. The approach enables the use of time-domain correlation functions as phase probes without computing the ground state, and is scalable to many sites with polynomial resources, potentially outperforming classical supercomputers for generalized Agassi models with inhomogeneous couplings.
