# Pressure inequalities for nuclear and neutron matter

> We prove several inequalities using lowest-order effective field theory for nucleons which give an upper bound on the pressure of asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter. We prove two types of inequalities, one based on convexity and another derived from shifting an auxiliary field.

## Key claim

The paper proves rigorous upper bounds on the pressure of asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter using lowest-order effective field theory, with the key condition that the inequalities rely on positivity of the Euclidean functional integral measure and are therefore valid only when the effective action remains positive.

## Method

The author derives two types of pressure inequalities for systems with two fermion species (SU(2) symmetry, representing neutron spin states) and four fermion species (SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry, representing nucleon isospin and spin states). The first inequality uses convexity of the pressure as a function of asymmetric chemical potential combined with the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality applied to the grand canonical partition function expressed via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations. The second inequality shifts an auxiliary Hubbard-Stratonovich field to absorb the asymmetric chemical potential, then bounds the resulting expression using the positivity of the fermion determinant.

## Key numbers

- For the two-fermion (neutron) system at density ρ ~ 0.1ρ_N and temperature T < 10 MeV, the maximum coupling |C|_max is about 3 fm².
- For the four-fermion (nucleon) system at density ρ ~ 0.1ρ_N and temperature T < 10 MeV, the maximum coupling |C'_U|_max is about 0.2 fm².
- The shifted-field inequality for the two-fermion system gives P(μ,μ₃) ≤ P(μ,0) + 3μ₃²/(2|C|_max).
- The shifted-field inequality for the four-fermion system with isospin chemical potential gives P(μ,μ₃ᴵ) ≤ P(μ,0) + (μ₃ᴵ)²/(2|C'_U|_max).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous inequality results for two-nucleon correlation functions in symmetric nuclear matter (Lee, 2004) to pressure bounds for asymmetric systems. The inequalities are analogous to QCD inequalities proved using quark-gluon degrees of freedom by Weingarten (1983), Vafa and Witten (1984), and others. The results enable consistency checks for phenomenological models of asymmetric nuclear matter and provide guidance for lattice simulations of nuclear matter using effective field theory, particularly in the Wigner SU(4) symmetry limit where simulations have no sign problem.
