# Extended Glauber Model of Antiproton-Nucleus Annihilation for All Energies and Mass Numbers

> Previous analytical formulas in the Glauber model for high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions developed by Wong are utilized and extended to study antiproton-nucleus annihilations for both high and low energies, after taking into account the effects of Coulomb and nuclear interactions, and the change of the antiproton momentum inside a nucleus. The extended analytical formulas capture the main features of the experimental antiproton-nucleus annihilation cross sections for all energies and mass numbers. At high antiproton energies, they exhibit the granular property for the lightest nuclei and the black-disk limit for the heavy nuclei. At low antiproton energies, they display the effect of the antiproton momentum increase due to the nuclear interaction for the light nuclei, and the effect of the magnification due to the attractive Coulomb interaction for the heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The extended Glauber model, incorporating Coulomb focusing and nuclear-interaction momentum shifts, reproduces experimental antiproton-nucleus annihilation cross sections across all energies and mass numbers, but the agreement relies on approximating the interior thickness function \(T'(b')\) by the unperturbed \(T(b')\) and on using a simple \(1/v\) parametrization for the elementary \(\bar{p}p\) annihilation cross section.

## Method

The authors extended Wong's analytical Glauber-model formulas for high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions to antiproton-nucleus systems by including (i) the initial-state Coulomb interaction as a magnifying lens that maps the impact parameter at nuclear contact to the asymptotic impact parameter, and (ii) the change of antiproton momentum inside the nucleus due to the nuclear potential, which modifies the elementary \(\bar{p}p\) annihilation cross section \(\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}p} = \sigma_0/v\). The \(\bar{p}n\) cross section was related to \(\bar{p}p\) via the quark-model ratio \(\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}n} = (4/5)\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}p}\). For light nuclei (\(A<40\)) Gaussian thickness functions were used; for heavy nuclei a sharp-cut-off uniform density distribution was employed.

## Key numbers

* \(\sigma_0 = 43\ \text{mb}\) in the \(\bar{p}p\) annihilation cross section parametrization \(\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}p} = \sigma_0/v\), fitted to experimental data.
* For \(\bar{p}^2\)H at high momenta, the Glauber model predicts \(\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}^2\text{H}}/\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}p} \sim 2\); at low momenta the ratio becomes \(\sim 3/2\) before Coulomb and nuclear corrections.
* The \(\bar{p}p\) annihilation cross section decreases by an order of magnitude as the antiproton momentum increases from 30 to 600 MeV/\(c\).
* The quark-model ratio \(\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}n}/\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}p} = 4/5\) is compared to experimental deuteron data giving an average of \(0.804 \pm 0.022\).
* The contact radius for heavy nuclei is \(R_c = R_A + R_B + R_{p\bar{p}}\) with \(R_{p\bar{p}} = \sqrt{3\sigma_{\rm ann}^{\bar{p}p}/2\pi}\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical Glauber model of Wong (1984, 1994) for high-energy nucleon-nucleus collisions to low-energy antiproton-nucleus annihilations, addressing puzzles in the mass-number and energy dependence of annihilation cross sections observed in LEAR (Bianconi et al., 2011) and Dubna (Kuzichev et al., 1994) experiments. It contrasts with the optical-potential approach of Batty, Friedman, and Gal (2001), which worked poorly for light nuclei like He and Li, and with the earlier Glauber-model study by Kuzichev et al. (1994) that deviated for heavy nuclei at low momenta. The extended model enables systematic predictions for antiproton annihilation on any nucleus across the full energy range, supporting interpretation of data from FAIR, AD at CERN, PAMELA, and AMS.
