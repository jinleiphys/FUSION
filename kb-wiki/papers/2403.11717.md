# Variational calculations of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter with the tensor-optimized Fermi Sphere (TOFS) method: many-body effects and short-range correlation

> The equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are investigated with the tensor-optimized Fermi Sphere method (TOFS) up to the density $ρ=0.5$~fm$^{-3}$. This method is based on a linked-cluster expansion theorem, and the energy per particle of nuclear matter ($E/A$) is calculated variationally with respect to the correlated nuclear matter wave function. We can study the density dependence of the many-body terms arising from the operator products, which contribute to $E/A$. In order to clarify the relation between the many-body effects and short-range correlation, we take the spin-isospin dependent central {\it NN} interaction with a few GeV repulsion in the inner region. The EOS obtained by the TOFS method is reasonably reproduced, compared with other \textit{ab initio} many-body methods. We found that the many-body terms from the 2-body to 6-body ones) give sizable effects on $E/A$ at higher density, and they play an important role in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The TOFS method yields equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter that are reasonably reproduced compared to other ab initio methods, but the TOFS results are systematically a bit stiffer (giving an upper limit) because the method is variational with respect to the correlated wave function; the many-body terms from 2-body up to 6-body give sizable effects on energy per particle at higher density, particularly beyond approximately 0.3 fm^-3.

## Method

The TOFS method uses an exponential-type correlated nuclear matter wave function, exp(F) Phi_0, where the correlation function F includes central and tensor components expanded in Gaussian basis functions. The energy per particle is calculated variationally by minimizing with respect to the expansion coefficients of the correlation functions, using only linked diagrams guaranteed by a linked-cluster expansion theorem. In this work, a first-order (N=1) TOFS calculation is performed with the AV4' central NN potential (spin-isospin dependent with a few GeV repulsion in the inner region) for both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter up to density rho = 0.5 fm^-3.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter at rho = 0.4 fm^-3: e_0 = 13.9 MeV, e_2b = -70.4 MeV, e_3b = 78.2 MeV, e_4b = -77.7 MeV, e_5b = 25.3 MeV, e_6b = -9.8 MeV (attractive contributions from e_2b, e_4b, e_6b; repulsive from e_0, e_3b, e_5b)
- The TOFS EOS for symmetric nuclear matter is similar to other methods below rho approximately 0.2 fm^-3, but becomes a little stiffer at higher density
- For pure neutron matter, the TOFS result is almost the same as other methods at rho = 0.03 fm^-3, but becomes a bit stiffer at higher densities
- Many-body contributions from e_5b and e_6b become non-negligible for pure neutron matter beyond rho approximately 0.4 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the TOFS method (previously applied only to symmetric nuclear matter up to rho = 0.2 fm^-3 in Yamada (2019, 2021)) to higher density (rho approximately 3 rho_0) and to pure neutron matter. The results are compared with other ab initio methods including BHF, BBG, FHNC/SOC, SCGF, and AFDMC as reported in Baldo (2012). The study clarifies the density dependence of individual many-body contributions (2-body through 6-body) and their relation to short-range correlation, which has not been clear even for the case of short-range correlation alone. This enables future higher-order TOFS calculations (up to 10-body terms) and provides insight into the role of many-body effects in nuclear matter at high density relevant to neutron star structure.
