# Superfluid Properties of the Inner Crust of Neutron Stars

> Superfluid properties of the inner crust matter of neutron stars, formed by nuclear clusters immersed in a dilute neutron gas, are analysed in a self- consistent HFB approach. The calculations are performed with two pairing forces, fixed so as to obtain in infinite nuclear matter the pairing gaps provided by the Gogny force or by induced interactions. It is shown that the nuclear clusters can either suppress or enhance the pairing correlations inside the inner crust matter, depending on the density of the surrounding neutrons. The profile of the pairing field in the inner crust is rather similar for both pairing forces, but the values of the pairing gaps are drastically reduced for the force which simulates the polarisation effects in infinite neutron matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nuclear clusters in the inner crust can either suppress or enhance pairing correlations depending on the density of the surrounding neutron gas, but the magnitude of the pairing gaps is drastically reduced when using a pairing force that simulates polarisation effects in infinite neutron matter (maximum gap of about 1 MeV) compared to a force fitted to the Gogny force.

## Method

Self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed in coordinate space for spherical Wigner-Seitz cells representing the inner crust matter, using the Skyrme-type interaction SLy4 for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent contact pairing force for the particle-particle channel. Two sets of pairing force parameters were used: one reproducing the pairing gaps of neutron matter given by the Gogny force, and another reduced in strength (V0 = -330 MeV fm^3) to simulate the polarisation effects that yield a maximum gap of about 1 MeV. The calculations were performed for four representative Wigner-Seitz cells taken from Negele (1973), with proton numbers Z=40 and Z=50 and varying neutron gas densities.

## Key numbers

* Neutron drip density: ρ_d ≈ 1.4 × 10^{-3} ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is the saturation density.
* Maximum density for the inner crust considered: ρ_max = 0.0789 fm^{-3}, corresponding to a cell with 32 protons and 950 neutrons.
* For the first pairing force (fitted to Gogny force): V_0 = -430.0 MeV fm^3, η = 0.7, α = 0.45, with a cut-off energy E_c = 60 MeV.
* For the second pairing force (simulating polarisation effects): V_0 = -330 MeV fm^3, with the same η = 0.7, α = 0.45, and E_c = 60 MeV.
* For the cell ^{1800}Sn (Z=50, ρ = 0.0204 fm^{-3}), the pairing field in the center is about two times smaller than in the uniform neutron gas region.
* For the cell ^{500}Zr (Z=40, ρ = 0.00159 fm^{-3}), the number of bound neutrons increases by 12 compared to the dripline nucleus ^{122}Zr.

## Context

This work extends earlier semiclassical pairing models based on a local density approximation (Broglia, 1994) and earlier HFB calculations with a fixed Woods-Saxon mean field and Gogny or Argonne pairing forces (Barranco, 1998), which overestimated the influence of nuclear clusters on pairing properties. The fully self-consistent approach allows the authors to study how the strong local variations of particle density and effective mass affect pairing correlations, and to analyse how nuclear clusters are modified by the surrounding neutron gas. The results have implications for the thermal properties and cooling of neutron stars, and the authors indicate these will be analysed in a forthcoming paper.
