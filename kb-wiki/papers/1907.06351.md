# Baryon-baryon interactions at short distances -- constituent quark model meets lattice QCD

> The interaction energies between two baryons at short distance in different flavor channels are calculated from the constituent quark model (CQM) and are compared with the recent lattice QCD (LQCD) results for baryon-baryon potentials at short distance. We consider the six-quark system with two strange quarks and focus on the quantum numbers, (Flavor,Spin)=(1,0),(8,1),(10,1),($\overline{10}$,1) and (27,0). The interaction energy is defined by subtracting out isolated baryon masses and relative kinetic energy of two baryons from the total energy of a compact six-quark state. We introduce interaction energy ratio between different flavors as a useful measure to test the prediction of CQM. We find that the ratios in CQM show good agreement with those in LQCD, which indicates that the short range part of the baryon-baryon interaction can be understood qualitatively in terms of the Pauli principle and spin-dependent color interaction among constituent quarks.

## Key claim

The short-range part of baryon-baryon interactions in different flavor channels, as extracted from lattice QCD, can be understood qualitatively in terms of the Pauli principle and spin-dependent color interactions among constituent quarks, provided one compares ratios of interaction energies rather than absolute magnitudes to cancel operator and wave-function ambiguities.

## Method

The authors calculated the static interaction energy of compact six-quark states in the constituent quark model (CQM) by subtracting the sum of isolated baryon masses and relative kinetic energy from the total energy of the six-quark system, using a Hamiltonian with spin-independent and spin-dependent color interactions. They then formed ratios of these interaction energies between different flavor channels (with two strange quarks) and compared them to corresponding ratios extracted from lattice QCD (LQCD) potentials at short distance, for both flavor SU(3) symmetric and non-symmetric cases.

## Key numbers

- For the flavor SU(3) symmetric case (pseudoscalar meson mass ~469 MeV, octet baryon mass ~1161 MeV), the CQM ratios versus LQCD ratios at r~0 were: F1: -0.33 vs -0.53(3); F27: 1 vs 1; F10: 0.78 vs 0.93(1); F10bar: 0.78 vs 0.81(1); F8: 0.28 vs 0.20(1).
- For the flavor SU(3) non-symmetric case (nearly physical quark masses with M_pi ~146 MeV, M_K ~525 MeV, M_N ~956 MeV, M_Lambda ~1121 MeV, M_Sigma ~1201 MeV, M_Xi ~1328 MeV), the CQM ratios versus LQCD ratios were: F1: -0.19 vs -0.43(14); F27: 1 vs 1; F10: 0.77 vs 0.97(4); F10bar: 0.80 vs 0.82(1); F8: 0.42 vs 0.26(2).
- The total six-quark energies in CQM (in MeV) for each flavor state were: F1: 2596.2; F27: 2900.7; F10: 2889.2; F10bar: 2900.3; F8: 2735.2, with variational parameters a1, a4, a5 in fm^-2 units ranging from 1.73 to 3.11.

## Context

This work provides the first quantitative comparison between constituent quark model predictions and lattice QCD results for baryon-baryon interactions at short distances, extending earlier qualitative discussions reviewed by Hatsuda (2018) and Oka (2000). The ratio method introduced here enables a less ambiguous test than comparing absolute potentials, and the results support the interpretation that short-range attraction or repulsion in different flavor channels arises from color-spin dynamics and Pauli blocking among quarks, with implications for the existence of dibaryon states such as the H-dibaryon. Future work should incorporate non-zero quark orbital states and non-perturbative dynamics beyond the short-distance regime.
