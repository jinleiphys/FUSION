# Inhomogeneous Shadowing Effects on J/ψProduction in dA Collisions

> We study the effect of spatially homogeneous and inhomogeneous shadowing on $J/ψ$ production in deuterium-nucleus collisions. We discuss how the shadowing and its spatial dependence may be measured by comparing central and peripheral $dA$ collisions. These event classes may be selected by using gray protons from heavy ion breakup and events where the proton or neutron in the deuterium does not interact. We find that inhomogenous shadowing has a significant effect on central $dA$ collisions, larger than is observed in central $AA$ collisions. The inhomogeneity may be measured by comparing the rapidity dependence of $J/ψ$ production in central and peripheral collisions. Results are presented for $d$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and $d$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.2$ TeV.

# Inhomogeneous Shadowing Effects on J/ψ Production in dA Collisions

## Key claim

Spatially inhomogeneous nuclear shadowing produces a significantly larger effect on J/ψ production in central deuterium-nucleus collisions than homogeneous shadowing models predict, and this effect is larger in central dA collisions than in central AA collisions; the spatial dependence can be measured by comparing J/ψ production in central and peripheral dA collisions selected using grey protons from heavy ion breakup or events where one deuteron nucleon does not interact.

## Method

The authors calculate J/ψ production in dAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and dPb collisions at √s_NN = 6.2 TeV using the color evaporation model at leading order, with nuclear parton densities constructed as products of nucleon density distributions, free nucleon parton densities (MRST LO), and shadowing functions. Two shadowing parameterizations are compared: EKS98 (based on GRV LO parton densities) and FGS (based on CTEQ5M parton densities with Gribov theory), each implemented with two spatial dependence models: a local density approximation where shadowing is proportional to the local nuclear density, and a path-length approximation where shadowing depends on the integrated density along the parton trajectory. The calculations are performed for central (b < 0.2R_A), peripheral (0.9R_A < b < 1.1R_A), and minimum-bias impact parameter bins.

## Key numbers

- dAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC) and dPb collisions at √s_NN = 6.2 TeV (LHC) are studied
- Nuclear radii used: R_Au = 6.38 fm, R_Pb = 6.62 fm (Woods-Saxon distributions)
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.2 GeV; scale Q = M (the c-cbar pair mass)
- The rapidity y_c where shadowing disappears (S_P^g ≈ 1 at x ≈ 0.025) is y_c ≈ -0.2 at RHIC and y_c ≈ -3.2 at the LHC
- Central collisions defined as b < 0.2R_A; peripheral as 0.9R_A < b < 1.1R_A
- The FGS parameterization predicts significantly stronger gluon shadowing at small x than EKS98, with the difference especially large for gluons
- Inhomogeneous shadowing in central dA collisions is stronger than that predicted for central AA collisions in prior work by Emel'yanov, Khodinov, Klein and Vogt (2000)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear shadowing in deep inelastic scattering (Aubert 1987, Arneodo 1994) and in AA collisions (Emel'yanov, Khodinov, Klein and Vogt 2000) by proposing dA collisions as a cleaner probe of the spatial dependence of gluon shadowing. The authors build on the EKS98 parameterization (Eskola, Kolhinen and Salgado 1999) and the FGS parameterization (Frankfurt, Guzey and Strikman 2003) which represent extremes of gluon shadowing at low x. The techniques described enable future measurements at RHIC (using PHENIX and STAR detectors) and the LHC (using ALICE and CMS detectors) to distinguish between homogeneous and inhomogeneous shadowing models, and could be applied to other hard probes including Υ, open heavy flavor, Drell-Yan, W±, Z0, and jets.
