# Microscopic Calculation of Fission Product Yields with Particle Number Projection

> Fission fragments' charge and mass distribution is an important input to applications ranging from basic science to energy production or nuclear non-proliferation. In simulations of nucleosynthesis or calculations of superheavy elements, these quantities must be computed from models, as they are needed in nuclei where no experimental information is available. Until now, standard techniques to estimate these distributions were not capable of accounting for fine-structure effects, such as the odd-even staggering of the charge distributions. In this work, we combine a fully-microscopic collective model of fission dynamics with a recent extension of the particle number projection formalism to provide the highest-fidelity prediction of the primary fission fragment distributions for the neutron-induced fission of $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu. We show that particle number projection is an essential ingredient to reproduce odd-even staggering in the charge yields and benchmark the performance of various empirical probability laws that could simulate its effect. This new approach also enables for the first time the realistic determination of two-dimensional isotopic yields within nuclear density functional theory.

## Key claim

Particle number projection (PNP) is an essential ingredient for reproducing odd-even staggering in charge yields of fission fragments, and the authors demonstrate that combining PNP with a fully-microscopic collective model (the time-dependent generator coordinate method under the Gaussian overlap approximation) enables the first realistic determination of two-dimensional isotopic yields within nuclear density functional theory, specifically for neutron-induced fission of 235U and 239Pu.

## Method

The authors solve a time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) under the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) generator states in a two-dimensional collective space spanned by the quadrupole moment (q0) and octupole moment (q1). Scission configurations are identified using a neck operator that counts nucleons in the neck region, and the population of each scission configuration is obtained from the time-integrated flux density of the collective wave packet. The probability distribution of particle numbers in the fragments is then computed using particle-number projection (PNP) via gauge angle integrals with a Fomenko quadrature using 41 integration points, rather than the previous approach of convoluting with a Gaussian distribution.

## Key numbers

- The neck dispersion parameter aneck is chosen to be equal to one nucleon.
- The pairing part of the energy functional uses a cut-off of 60 MeV.
- The Fomenko quadrature for the gauge angle integrals uses 41 integration points.
- The study covers neutron-induced fission of 235U and 239Pu at low energy.

## Context

This work extends the seminal particle-number projection method introduced by Simenel (2010) and Scamps (2015) for heavy-ion reactions, which was previously applied to a single scission configuration of 239Pu(n,f) by Verriere (2019). It also builds on the strongly-damped random walk approach of Verriere (2020) that combined PNP with semi-classical potential energy surfaces. The present paper integrates PNP into a fully quantum-mechanical TDGCM framework, enabling for the first time two-dimensional isotopic yield predictions within nuclear density functional theory, and benchmarks various empirical probability laws that could approximate the exact PNP results.
