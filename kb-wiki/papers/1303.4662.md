# Equation of state and neutron star properties constrained by nuclear physics and observation

> Microscopic calculations of neutron matter based on nuclear interactions derived from chiral effective field theory, combined with the recent observation of a 1.97 +- 0.04 M_sun neutron star, constrain the equation of state of neutron-rich matter at sub- and supranuclear densities. We discuss in detail the allowed equations of state and the impact of our results on the structure of neutron stars, the crust-core transition density, and the nuclear symmetry energy. In particular, we show that the predicted range for neutron star radii is robust. For use in astrophysical simulations, we provide detailed numerical tables for a representative set of equations of state consistent with these constraints.

## Key claim

Microscopic calculations of neutron matter based on chiral effective field theory interactions, combined with the observation of a 1.97 solar mass neutron star, robustly constrain the equation of state of neutron-rich matter up to nuclear saturation density and, through a general polytropic extension, also at supranuclear densities, with the predicted range for neutron star radii being robust against variations in the many-body treatment and the high-density extension.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of neutron matter using chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions from chiral effective field theory, with renormalization-group evolution to improve many-body convergence. They extended these results to asymmetric nuclear matter in beta equilibrium using a Skyrme-like parametrization fitted to the neutron matter energy and pressure bands up to 1.1 times saturation density. For higher densities, they employed a general piecewise polytropic extension with three density regions, varying the polytropic exponents and transition densities systematically, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for each equation of state, retaining only those that satisfy causality and support a neutron star of a given maximum mass.

## Key numbers

* Neutron star mass constraint: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses, from the observation of Demorest (2010), used as the primary observational constraint.
* Symmetry energy S_v predicted range: 29.7 to 33.2 MeV for gamma = 4/3 (incompressibility K = 236 MeV).
* L parameter (density derivative of symmetry energy) predicted range: 32.5 to 57.0 MeV for gamma = 4/3.
* Crust-core transition density: approximately 0.55 to 0.625 times saturation density without Coulomb and gradient terms, and 0.475 to 0.55 times saturation density with those terms included (Q = 75 MeV fm^5).
* Maximal central density for neutron stars supporting 1.97 solar masses: approximately 8.3 times saturation density.
* Maximal central density for neutron stars supporting 2.4 solar masses: approximately 5.8 times saturation density.
* Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K = 236 MeV for the fiducial parametrization.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Hebeler et al., 2013) by incorporating the updated 1.97 solar mass neutron star observation of Demorest (2010), generalizing the microscopic neutron matter calculations to include unevolved chiral interactions, improving the treatment of beta equilibrium, explicitly including the crust equation of state, and using a more flexible three-polytrope extension. The results agree well with Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of Gandolfi et al. (2012) and with empirical constraints from nuclear masses, the 208Pb dipole polarizability of Tamii et al. (2011), and astrophysical modeling of X-ray bursts and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries. The constraints rule out many commonly used equations of state, including those based on exotic degrees of freedom, and enable more reliable predictions for neutron star structure and gravitational wave signals from binary mergers.
