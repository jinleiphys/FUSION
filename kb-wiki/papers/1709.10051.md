# Prospects for Improved Understanding of Isotopic Reactor Antineutrino Fluxes

> Predictions of antineutrino fluxes produced by fission isotopes in a nuclear reactor have recently received increased scrutiny due to observed differences in predicted and measured inverse beta decay (IBD) yields, referred to as the 'reactor antineutrino flux anomaly.' In this paper, global fits are applied to existing IBD yield measurements to produce constraints on antineutrino production by individual plutonium and uranium fission isotopes. We find that fits including measurements from highly 235U-enriched cores and fits including Daya Bay's new fuel evolution result produce discrepant best-fit IBD yields for 235U and 239Pu. This discrepancy can be alleviated in a global analysis of all datasets through simultaneous fitting of 239Pu, 235U, and 238U yields. The measured IBD yield of U238 in this analysis is (7.02 +/- 1.65) x 10^-43 cm2/fission, nearly two standard deviations below existing predictions. Future hypothetical IBD yield measurements by short-baseline reactor experiments are examined to determine their possible impact on global understanding of isotopic IBD yields. It is found that future improved short-baseline IBD yield measurements at both high-enriched and low-enriched cores can significantly improve constraints for 235U, 238U, and 239Pu, providing comparable or superior precision to existing conversion- and summation-based antineutrino flux predictions. Systematic and experimental requirements for these future measurements are also investigated.

## Key claim

Global fits to existing inverse beta decay (IBD) yield measurements reveal a discrepancy between fits that include data from highly 235U-enriched cores and fits that include Daya Bay's fuel evolution result, producing inconsistent best-fit IBD yields for 235U and 239Pu; this tension can be resolved by simultaneously fitting 239Pu, 235U, and 238U yields, but the resulting 238U IBD yield sits nearly two standard deviations below existing predictions.

## Method

The authors performed global chi-squared fits to existing IBD yield measurements from multiple reactor experiments, including data from highly 235U-enriched cores and Daya Bay's fuel evolution measurement. They compared fits that included different subsets of data to assess consistency, and then performed a simultaneous three-isotope fit for 239Pu, 235U, and 238U yields. They also simulated hypothetical future short-baseline IBD yield measurements at both high-enriched and low-enriched cores to evaluate the potential improvement in isotopic yield constraints.

## Key numbers

- Measured IBD yield of 238U from the global analysis: (7.02 +/- 1.65) x 10^-43 cm^2/fission, nearly two standard deviations below existing predictions.
- Future improved short-baseline IBD yield measurements at both high-enriched and low-enriched cores can provide constraints on 235U, 238U, and 239Pu with precision comparable or superior to existing conversion-based and summation-based antineutrino flux predictions.

## Context

This work extends global fitting approaches previously applied to reactor antineutrino data (Giunti 2017, Giunti 2017b) by explicitly incorporating the Daya Bay fuel evolution result (Daya Bay Collaboration 2016) and examining tensions between datasets. The analysis builds on the "reactor antineutrino flux anomaly" identified when updated beta-converted predictions (Mueller 2011, Huber 2011) disagreed with measured fluxes. The results enable future short-baseline experiments (PROSPECT, STEREO, SoLid) to design measurement strategies that can resolve the origin of the flux anomaly and determine whether it stems from incorrect modeling of individual fission isotopes or from sterile neutrino oscillations.
