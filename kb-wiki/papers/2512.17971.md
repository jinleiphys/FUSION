# Achieving angular-momentum conservation with physics-informed neural networks in computational relativistic spin hydrodynamics

> We propose physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) as a numerical solver for relativistic spin hydrodynamics and demonstrate that the total angular momentum, i.e., the sum of orbital and spin angular momentum, is accurately conserved throughout the fluid evolution by imposing the conservation law directly in the loss function as a training target. This enables controlled numerical studies of the mutual conversion between spin and orbital angular momentum, a central feature of relativistic spin hydrodynamics driven by the rotational viscous effect. We present two physical scenarios with a rotating fluid confined in a cylindrical container: one case in which initial orbital angular momentum is converted into spin angular momentum in analogy with the Barnett effect, and the opposite case in which initial spin angular momentum is converted into orbital angular momentum in analogy with the Einstein-de Haas effect. We investigate these conversion processes governed by the rotational viscous effect by analyzing the spacetime profiles of thermal vorticity and spin potential. Our PINNs-based framework provides the first numerical evidence for spin-orbit angular momentum conversion with fully nonlinear computational relativistic spin hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) can accurately conserve total angular momentum (orbital plus spin) in relativistic spin hydrodynamics by directly imposing the conservation law as a term in the loss function, but only for the specific case of a rotating fluid confined in a cylindrical container with translational invariance along the axial direction and no parity-odd tensor components.

## Method

The authors formulate (3+1)-dimensional relativistic spin hydrodynamics up to first order in the gradient expansion, adopting a totally antisymmetric pseudo-gauge for the spin tensor and the Landau frame, then derive Müller-Israel-Stewart-type relaxation equations for the couple-stress tensor from an entropy current analysis. They reduce the system to a two-dimensional problem in time and radial coordinate by assuming translational invariance along the cylinder axis and rotational symmetry in the transverse plane, and solve the resulting five evolution equations (for energy density, radial and azimuthal fluid velocity, spin density, and couple-stress tensor) using a multilayer perceptron trained on collocation points with a loss function that incorporates the differential equations, initial conditions, and boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- The equation of state is \( P = \frac{1}{3} e = \frac{29}{45} \pi^2 T^4 \), reflecting the conformal nature of a massless two-flavor fermion and gluon gas.
- The heat capacity is \( C_v = 4 \beta e \), where \( \beta = 1/T \).
- The spin susceptibility is \( \chi = \frac{19}{6} \left( \frac{15e}{29\pi^2} \right)^{1/3} \).
- The relaxation time for the couple-stress tensor is \( \tau_\phi = 2\gamma A \), where \( \gamma \) is the rotational viscosity and \( A \) is an undetermined constant from the entropy current ansatz.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of relativistic spin hydrodynamics developed by Hattori (2019), Fukushima (2020), Gallegos (2021), and others, moving beyond linearized analyses and spin dynamics on a fixed background flow as studied by Singh (2024) and Sapna (2025). The PINNs-based approach addresses the difficulty of maintaining angular momentum conservation in conventional finite-volume methods, which is essential for studying the spin-orbit conversion processes analogous to the Barnett effect and the Einstein-de Haas effect in quark-gluon plasma and other rotating systems. The framework enables fully nonlinear simulations of relativistic spin hydrodynamics that incorporate the mutual coupling between spin and flow, potentially contributing to resolving the "spin sign puzzle" observed by ALICE (2019).
