# Electrical conductivity and relaxation via colored noise in a hadronic gas

> Motivated by the theory of relativistic hydrodynamic fluctuations we make use of the Green-Kubo formula to compute the electrical conductivity and the (second-order) relaxation time of the electric current of an interacting hadron gas. We use the recently developed transport code SMASH to numerically solve the coupled set of Boltzmann equations implementing realistic hadronic interactions. In particular, we explore the role of the resonance lifetimes in the determination of the electrical relaxation time. As opposed to a previous calculation of the shear viscosity we observe that the presence of resonances with lifetimes of the order of the mean-free time does not appreciably affect the relaxation of the electric current fluctuations. We compare our results to other approaches describing similar systems, and provide the value of the electrical conductivity and the relaxation time for a hadron gas at temperatures between T=60 MeV and T=150 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, contrary to previous findings for shear viscosity, the presence of hadronic resonances with lifetimes comparable to the mean-free time does not appreciably affect the relaxation of electric current fluctuations in a hadron gas, and that the exponential ansatz for the current-current correlation function provides a good approximation for extracting both the electrical conductivity and the relaxation time.

## Method

The authors used the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) transport code to numerically solve the coupled set of Boltzmann equations for an interacting hadron gas in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. They employed the Green-Kubo formalism to compute the electrical conductivity and the second-order relaxation time of the electric current by fitting the exponential decay of the current-current autocorrelation function, extracting the intercept C(0) and the relaxation time tau_Q from the fit.

## Key numbers

- Electrical conductivity and relaxation time are provided for a hadron gas at temperatures between T=60 MeV and T=150 MeV.
- For a massive gas of particles with mass m=138 MeV, constant isotropic cross section sigma=30 mb, and charges q={+1,-1,0}, the electrical conductivity over temperature was computed and compared to kinetic theory at temperatures including T=0.12, 0.16, and 0.2 GeV.
- The correlation function at the origin C(0)V for a massive gas was computed analytically using the integral I(m_a,T,mu_Q) = integral_0^infinity dp p^4/(m_a^2+p^2) exp(-sqrt(m_a^2+p^2)-q_a e mu_Q)/T), with the prefactor sum over species of g_a q_a^2 e^2/(6 pi^2).
- For massless particles, the correlation function at the origin reduces to C(0) = (q e n_Q)/(3V), where n_Q = q e n is the charge density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group on shear viscosity in a hadronic gas using SMASH (Rose 2017, Rose 2017, Wesp 2011) to now compute the electrical conductivity. It contrasts with earlier semianalytical calculations of electrical conductivity in a pion gas using chiral perturbation theory (FernandezFraile 2005, Torres-Rincon 2012) and with partonic transport model calculations (Greif 2014, Puglisi 2014). The results enable improved inputs for relativistic magnetohydrodynamics and for understanding low-mass dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions.
