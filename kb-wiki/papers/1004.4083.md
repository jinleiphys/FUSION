# Calculation of the spectrum of 12Li by using the multistep shell model method in the complex energy plane

> The unbound nucleus $^{12}$Li is evaluated by using the multistep shell model in the complex energy plane assuming that the spectrum is determined by the motion of three neutrons outside the $^9$Li core. It is found that the ground state of this system consists of an antibound $1/2^+$ state and that only this and a $1/2^-$ and a $5/2^+$ excited states are physically meaningful resonances.

## Key claim

The ground state of the unbound nucleus 12Li is an antibound (virtual) 1/2+ state, and only three physically meaningful resonances exist in its spectrum: the antibound ground state plus a 1/2- and a 5/2+ excited state; this conclusion depends on the assumed energy of the 0p1/2 single-particle resonance in 10Li and on the binding energy of 11Li(gs).

## Method

The authors used the multistep shell model in the complex energy plane (CXMSM), which extends the shell model to the complex energy plane using the Berggren representation. They assumed 12Li consists of three valence neutrons outside a frozen 9Li core, and built the three-particle basis from one-particle states (antibound 1s1/2, resonances 0p1/2 and 0d5/2) and two-particle states (bound 0+ ground state and 2+1 resonance of 11Li). The separable pairing interaction was fitted to reproduce the experimental binding energy of 11Li(gs), and the resulting complex energies were obtained by solving the three-particle eigenvalue equations after orthogonalizing the overcomplete CXMSM basis.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle antibound state 1s1/2 in 10Li lies at -0.050 MeV.
- The single-particle resonance 0p1/2 in 10Li has energy (0.195, -0.047) MeV or alternatively (0.563, -0.252) MeV depending on the Woods-Saxon potential parameters.
- The single-particle resonance 0d5/2 in 10Li lies at (2.731, -0.545) MeV.
- The bound ground state of 11Li is at -0.369 MeV (adopting the value from Smith et al. (2008)).
- The 2+1 resonance in 11Li lies at (2.300, -0.372) MeV, corresponding to a width of 744 keV.
- For the preferred parameters (0p1/2 at (0.563, -0.252) MeV and 11Li(gs) at -0.369 MeV), the three-particle states in 12Li are: 1/2+ antibound at (-0.466, -0.011) MeV; 1/2- at (1.116, -0.411) MeV; 5/2+ at (1.148, -0.243) MeV.
- The 1/2- resonance has a width of about 800 keV; the 5/2+ resonance has a width of about 500 keV.

## Context

This work extends the complex shell model (CXSM) and multistep shell model (MSM) formalisms, previously applied to stable nuclei like 205Pb (Bloqvist and Liotta, 1984), to the very unstable neutron-rich nucleus 12Li. It builds on prior calculations of 11Li using the CXSM including antibound states (Id Betan et al., 2004) and on experimental measurements of 12Li by Aksyutina et al. (2008), Patterson et al. (2010), and Hall et al. (2010). The results enable future experimental tests by predicting not only resonance energies but also their widths, which standard shell-model calculations using harmonic oscillator bases (e.g., Hall et al. 2010) cannot provide.
