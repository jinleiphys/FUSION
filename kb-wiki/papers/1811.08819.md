# Strongly intensive fluctuations and correlations in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions in the model with string fusion

> The several types of strongly intensive correlation variables are studied in nuclear collisions at LHC energy. These quantities are expected not to depend on centrality class width. They have been calculated in the dipole-based parton-string Monte Carlo model with string fusion. The centrality dependence of the mean transverse momentum correlation coefficient and strongly intensive quantity $Σ$ between multiplicity and $P_T$ have been obtained. Dynamical charge fluctuation $ν_{dyn}$ has been also calculated and compared with experimental data. It is shown that string fusion improves agreement with the experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including string fusion effects in a dipole-based parton-string Monte Carlo model improves agreement with experimental data for dynamical charge fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy, while also predicting centrality-dependent behavior for strongly intensive correlation variables that are expected to be independent of centrality class width.

## Method

The authors use a Monte Carlo model based on colour dipole scattering and quark-gluon string formation, where nucleons contain valence quark-diquark pairs and sea quark-antiquark pairs with positions sampled from two-dimensional Gauss distributions. Strings are formed between colliding dipoles, and when strings overlap in transverse space they fuse into sources with higher tension, modifying the mean charged multiplicity per rapidity as \(\langle \mu \rangle_k = \mu_0 \sqrt{k}\) and mean transverse momentum as \(\langle p_t \rangle_k = p_0 \sqrt[4]{k}\). The model calculates forward-backward correlation coefficients and strongly intensive variables \(\Sigma\) between multiplicity and transverse momentum in separated rapidity windows, as well as the dynamical charge fluctuation measure \(\nu_{dyn}\), for Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe collisions at LHC energy.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear density parameters: for Pb, \(R = 6.62\) fm and \(d = 0.546\) fm; for Xe, \(R = 5.42\) fm and \(d = 0.57\) fm (Woods-Saxon distribution)
- The strongly intensive quantity \(\Sigma_{NN}\) decreases with centrality for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, with results shown for both 2% and 5% centrality class binning
- The mean transverse momentum correlation coefficient \(b_{p_t-p_t}\) shows non-monotonic behavior with centrality for Pb-Pb collisions, with a peak attributed to saturation of the color field; for Xe-Xe collisions the saturation region is not reached even in most central collisions
- Dynamical charge fluctuation \(\nu_{dyn}\) and \(N_{ch} \nu_{dyn}\) are calculated for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and compared with experimental data from Abelev (2012), showing that string fusion improves agreement compared to the model without fusion

## Context

This work extends the string-parton Monte Carlo approach previously developed by Kovalenko (2012, 2014) to include string fusion effects from Braun (1992, 1997) and Amelin (1993), and applies it to strongly intensive variables proposed by Gorenstein (2011) and Gazdzicki (2013). The non-monotonic behavior of the pt-pt correlation coefficient was previously predicted by Kovalenko (2013) and confirmed by experimental data from Altsybeev (2017), but other models have difficulty reproducing this effect. The results enable further experimental studies of these strongly intensive variables at LHC energy and at lower energies such as those planned for the MPD detector at NICA, where non-monotonic behavior could indicate the critical point of the QCD phase transition.
