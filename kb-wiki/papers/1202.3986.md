# Exact Eigenvalues of the Pairing Hamiltonian Using Continuum Level Density

> The pairing Hamiltonian constitutes an important approximation in many- body systems, it is exactly soluble and quantum integrable. On the other hand, the continuum single particle level density (CSPLD) contains information about the continuum energy spectrum. The question whether one can use the Hamiltonian with constant pairing strength for correlations in the continuum is still unanswered. In this paper we generalize the Richardson exact solution for the pairing Hamiltonian including correlations in the continuum. The resonant and non-resonant continuum are included through the CSPLD. The resonant correlations are made explicit by using the Cauchy theorem. Low lying states with seniority zero and two are calculated for the even Carbon isotopes. We conclude that energy levels can indeed be calculated with constant pairing in the continuum using the CSPLD. It is found that the nucleus $^{24}$C is unbound. The real and complex energy representation of the continuum is developed and their differences are shown. The trajectory of the pair energies in the continuum for the nucleus $^{28}$C is shown.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Richardson exact solution of the pairing Hamiltonian to include correlations in the continuum, demonstrating that energy levels can indeed be calculated with constant pairing strength in the continuum using the continuum single particle level density (CSPLD), with the caveat that the agreement with experimental data worsens as the number of neutrons increases and that the nucleus $^{24}$C is found to be unbound.

## Method

The author reformulates the Richardson equations for the pairing Hamiltonian by replacing the discrete sum over single-particle states with an integral over the continuum single particle level density (CSPLD), which is constructed from the derivative of the phase shift for continuum partial waves. The CSPLD is split into resonant and non-resonant (background) contributions, and the resonant part is analytically continued to the complex energy plane using the Cauchy theorem, yielding Richardson equations that include bound states, Gamow (resonant) states, and background continuum contributions. Low-lying states with seniority zero and two are calculated for even Carbon isotopes ($^{14}$C to $^{24}$C) by solving the resulting coupled equations with boundary conditions that determine the pair energies.

## Key numbers

* Pairing strength for $^{14}$C: $G = 0.7786$ MeV, determined by reproducing the experimental pairing energy $P_{\rm Exp}(^{14}{\rm C}) = 1.516$ MeV with a cutoff energy at 30 MeV.
* Ground state energy of $^{14}$C relative to $^{12}$C: $E_0 = -11.398$ MeV, obtained from a single pair energy $E_{p_1} = -11.398$ MeV.
* Ground state energy of $^{24}$C relative to $^{12}$C: $E_0 = -19.605$ MeV, with one pair energy $E_6 = 2.630$ MeV lying in the continuum, indicating the nucleus is unbound.
* Half-life of the $0d_{3/2}$ resonant state: $T_{1/2} = 5.485 \times 10^{-22}$ sec, which is 9.21 times the characteristic nuclear time $\tau_c = 5.953 \times 10^{-23}$ sec.
* Half-life of the $0f_{7/2}$ resonant state: $T_{1/2} = 7.505 \times 10^{-23}$ sec, which is 1.26 times the characteristic time $\tau_c$.

## Context

This work extends the Richardson exact solution of the pairing Hamiltonian, originally developed for discrete bound states by Richardson (1963, 1964) and revived by Von Delft and Braun (1999), to include continuum correlations. It builds on prior studies of continuum effects in pairing by Sandulescu (2000), Kruppa (2001), and Hasegawa (2003), and provides an exact treatment that overcomes the drawbacks of the BCS approximation near the drip line, as noted by Dobaczewsky (1996) and Id Betan (2012). The method enables the calculation of exact eigenvalues for nuclei beyond the neutron drip line, such as $^{24}$C and $^{28}$C, and allows the analysis of pair energy trajectories in the complex plane as a function of pairing strength.
