# Phase transitions in dense matter

> As the density of matter increases, atomic nuclei disintegrate into nucleons and, eventually, the nucleons themselves disintegrate into quarks. The phase transitions (PT's) between these phases can vary from steep first order to smooth crossovers, depending on certain conditions. First-order PT's with more than one globally conserved charge, so-called non-congruent PT's, have characteristic differences compared to congruent PT's. In this conference proceeding we discuss the non-congruence of the quark deconfinement PT at high densities and/or temperatures relevant for heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars, proto-neutron stars, supernova explosions, and compact-star mergers.

## Key claim

The quark deconfinement phase transition in dense matter can be either congruent or non-congruent depending on the number of globally conserved charges and the specific conditions (such as isospin symmetry or charge neutrality assumptions), with non-congruent transitions producing extended phase coexistence regions that are relevant for heavy-ion collisions and neutron-star interiors.

## Method

The authors used the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, an extended non-linear realization of the SU(3) sigma model that includes baryon octet, leptons, and quarks as degrees of freedom, with a Polyakov-loop-like field Phi controlling the transition between hadronic and quark phases. They computed phase diagrams for isospin-symmetric matter, neutron-star matter (charge neutral and chemically equilibrated), and isospin-asymmetric matter with fixed charge fraction, comparing the resulting coexistence lines and critical points.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement critical points were obtained under the assumption that for finite temperature there are quarks in the hadronic phase and hadrons in the quark phase, with each phase defined by the value of the order parameter Phi.
- Astrophysical events such as supernova explosions can reach temperatures of T=30 MeV, and neutron-star mergers can reach T=80 MeV.
- For lower energy heavy-ion collisions with significant net baryon density, the matter created in Au-Au or Pb-Pb collisions has a charge fraction of about Y_Q=0.4.
- The non-congruent phase diagram was computed for charge fraction Y_Q=0.3, showing an extended coexistence region between the confinement and deconfinement curves.

## Context

This work extends the study of non-congruent phase transitions in dense matter, building on prior work by Iosilevskiy (2010), Hempel (2013, 2015), and Glendenning (1992) on Gibbs and Maxwell constructions for neutron-star matter. The CMF model provides a unified description that connects astrophysical systems (supernovae, neutron-star mergers) with laboratory heavy-ion collisions (HADES, FAIR, NICA, RHIC beam energy scan), enabling future searches for observational signatures of non-congruent deconfinement transitions in both settings.
