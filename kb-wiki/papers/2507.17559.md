# Solving two and three-body systems with deep neural networks

> We develop a new method for solving two- and three-body bound state problems using unsupervised machine learning techniques. We use a deep neural network to calculate both simple and realistic potentials, obtaining the properties of the deuteron and triton bound states for the chiral effective field theory NN potential. Our results provide significant accuracy with no prior assumptions about the behaviour of the wave function. This neural network technique, which extends from two-body to three-body, may provide insight into potential solutions to the nuclear and hadronic many-body problems.

## Key claim
The authors develop an unsupervised deep neural network method for solving two- and three-body bound state problems that requires no prior assumptions about the wave function's behavior, but the three-body triton calculation is restricted to only three partial waves and is presented as a capability demonstration rather than a full accurate calculation.

## Method
For two-body systems, the radial Schrödinger equation is discretized on a uniform mesh using central finite differences, and a deep neural network with two hidden layers of 16 nodes each outputs the reduced radial wave function, with the energy expectation value serving as the loss function minimized by the Adam optimizer. For three-body systems, a single Jacobi coordinate channel is selected and the potentials and wave functions from the other two rearrangement channels are expanded via multipole expansions in terms of the chosen channel's coordinates, allowing the total wave function to be predicted on a single two-dimensional grid.

## Key numbers
- Two-body harmonic oscillator ground state energy: 1.5018 MeV (exact 1.5 MeV), with wave function deviation less than 2%
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2241 MeV (experimental 2.224575(9) MeV)
- Deuteron matter radius: 1.867 fm (experimental 1.971(6) fm)
- Deuteron D-wave probability: 4.10% (compared to 4.03% from chiral EFT calculation)
- Three-body harmonic oscillator ground state energy: 2.998 MeV (exact 3.0 MeV), with wave function deviation less than 1%
- For deuteron with M=1000 mesh points and 16 nodes per hidden layer: average kinetic energy 11.7242 MeV, average potential energy -13.9479 MeV

## Context
This work extends the application of deep neural networks from two-body systems to three-body bound states, building on variational Monte Carlo methods (McMillan 1965, Ceperley 1977) and the Gaussian expansion method (Kamimura 1988, Hiyama 2003) which require explicit basis expansions. The approach enables solving nuclear bound state problems with chiral effective field theory potentials (Entem 2003) without constructing large Hamiltonian matrices, potentially scaling to the nuclear many-body problem. The triton calculation is limited to three partial waves and does not include three-nucleon forces, leaving full-precision calculations as an open direction.
