# Baby Skyrmion Strings

> We provide analytical and numerical evidence of the existence of classically stable, string-like configurations in a 2+1 dimensional analog of the Skyrme model. The model contains a conserved topological charge usually called the baryon number. Our strings are non-topological solitons which have a constant baryon number per unit length. The energy per length containing one baryon is, however, less than the energy of an isolated baryon (radially symmetric ``baby Skyrmion") in a region of the parameter space, which suggests a degree of stability for our configurations. In a limiting case, our configuration saturates a Bogomolnyi-type bound and is degenerate in energy per baryon with the baby Skyrmion. In another limiting case, the energies are still degenerate but do not saturate the corresponding Bogomolnyi-type bound. Nonetheless, we expect the string to be stable here. Both limiting cases are solvable analytically.

## Key claim

The authors provide analytical and numerical evidence for classically stable, string-like configurations in a 2+1 dimensional analog of the Skyrme model, but these strings are non-topological solitons whose stability is only guaranteed against disintegration into single baryons for a specific region of the parameter space (0 < mu < 1), not against decay into multi-baryon configurations.

## Method

The authors construct string-like configurations by reinterpreting the radial and angular coordinates of the baby Skyrmion ansatz as two Cartesian coordinates, yielding a configuration with constant baryon number per unit length. They then compute the energy per baryon for the string configuration using an integral expression that avoids solving the differential equation directly, and compare it to the energy of the radially symmetric baby Skyrmion obtained via numerical shooting methods. Two analytically solvable limiting cases are examined: the conformally invariant O(3) non-linear sigma model (mu approaching 0) and the large mass limit (mu approaching infinity).

## Key numbers

- For mass parameter mu^2 = 0.1, the string energy per baryon is E_string = 1.55356 x 4pi with optimal length L_string = 3.4542, compared to the baby Skyrmion energy E_Skyrmion = 1.564 x 4pi
- The string is energetically stable against disintegration into single baryons in the region 0 < mu < 1, with the upper limit mu = 1 determined numerically with error less than 0.002%
- In the large mass limit, both the string and baby Skyrmion have energy E = (4/3) x 4pi mu per baryon, and both occupy area A = 4pi/mu per baryon
- For mu^2 = 0.1, the baby deuteron (B=2) binding energy is Delta = 0.191 x 4pi

## Context

This work extends studies of baby Skyrmions in 2+1 dimensions, which were previously shown to exist as stable localized solitons by Piette, Mueller-Kirsten, Tchrakian, and Zakrzewski (1994) and Piette, Schroers, and Zakrzewski (1995). The string configurations provide a new class of solutions that may be relevant for understanding low-energy excitations of lumps with large baryon number, potentially corresponding to a different low-energy phase for the matter described by this energy functional. The authors note that unlike usual cosmic strings which utilize topology in the transverse direction for stability, these strings lack topological protection and may quantum mechanically tunnel into configurations of isolated lumps with B=2, though calculation of the tunneling rate requires further work.
