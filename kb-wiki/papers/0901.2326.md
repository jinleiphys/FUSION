# Edge states in graphene quantum dots: Fractional quantum Hall effect analogies and differences at zero magnetic field

> We investigate the way that the degenerate manifold of midgap edge states in quasicircular graphene quantum dots with zig-zag boundaries supports, under free-magnetic-field conditions, strongly correlated many-body behavior analogous to the fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE), familiar from the case of semiconductor heterostructures in high magnetic fields. Systematic exact-diagonalization (EXD) numerical studies are presented for the first time for 5 <= N <= 8 fully spin-polarized electrons and for total angular momenta in the range of N(N-1)/2 <= L <= 150. We present a derivation of a rotating-electron-molecule (REM) type wave function based on the methodology introduced earlier [C. Yannouleas and U. Landman, Phys. Rev. B 66, 115315 (2002)] in the context of the FQHE in two-dimensional semiconductor quantum dots. The EXD wave functions are compared with FQHE trial functions of the Laughlin and the derived REM types. It is found that a variational extension of the REM offers a better description for all fractional fillings compared with that of the Laughlin functions (including total energies and overlaps), a fact that reflects the strong azimuthal localization of the edge electrons. In contrast with the multiring arrangements of electrons in circular semiconductor quantum dots, the graphene REMs exhibit in all instances a single (0,N) polygonal-ring molecular (crystalline) structure, with all the electrons localized on the edge. Disruptions in the zig-zag boundary condition along the circular edge act effectively as impurities that pin the electron molecule, yielding single-particle densities with broken rotational symmetry that portray directly the azimuthal localization of the edge electrons.

## Key claim

Under zero magnetic field, the degenerate manifold of midgap edge states in quasicircular graphene quantum dots with zig-zag boundaries supports strongly correlated many-body behavior analogous to the fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE), but the rotating-electron-molecule (REM) type wave functions offer a better description for all fractional fillings than Laughlin trial functions, reflecting strong azimuthal localization of edge electrons rather than liquid-like behavior.

## Method

The authors performed systematic exact-diagonalization (EXD) numerical studies for 5 ≤ N ≤ 8 fully spin-polarized electrons in the graphene-dot lowest Landau level (gd-LLL) for total angular momenta N(N-1)/2 ≤ L ≤ 150. They derived analytic REM trial wave functions using angular-momentum projection techniques from Yannouleas and Landman (2002), introducing a single variational parameter α to create variational REM (vREM) functions. The EXD wave functions were compared with FQHE trial functions of the Laughlin type and the derived REM/vREM types through total energies, relative errors, and wave function overlaps.

## Key numbers

- Maximum relative error of vREM total energies compared to EXD is less than 0.045% for all N=5-8 electrons examined
- vREM overlaps with EXD solutions are larger than 0.985 for N=5 and larger than 0.95 for N=8
- Optimal variational parameter α values are smaller than 0.4, decreasing to about 0.16 for L=150 for all 5 ≤ N ≤ 8
- Laughlin relative errors are substantially larger than vREM ones, on average by a factor of 5
- EXD ground-state energies exhibit cusp states at magic angular momenta L_m = N(N-1)/2 + kN, with k=0,1,2,...
- For L → ∞, EXD energies approach the classical electrostatic energy of N point-like electrons in a (0,N) configuration with radius R

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Wunsch et al. (2008) that covered limited ranges of electron numbers (2 ≤ N ≤ 5) and total angular momenta, suggesting Wigner-crystal-type ansatzes may be more promising than quantum-liquid-type trial functions for graphene dots. The paper contrasts with semiconductor quantum dot studies where multiring arrangements of electrons occur; in graphene dots only single (0,N) polygonal-ring molecular structures appear. The findings enable further theoretical and experimental exploration of strongly correlated electron behavior in graphene nanostructures and may help resolve questions about the appropriateness of liquid-type versus molecular-type trial functions for finite 2D electron systems.
