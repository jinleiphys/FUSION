# Extended-soft-core Baryon-Baryon ESC08 model III. S=-2 Hyperon-hyperon/nucleon Interaction

> This paper presents the Extended-Soft-Core (ESC) potentials ESC08c for baryon-baryon channels with total strangeness S=-2. The potential models for S=-2 are based on SU(3) extensions of ESC potential models for the S=0 and S=-1 sectors, which are fitted to experimental data. Flavor SU(3)-symmetry is broken only kinematically by the masses of the baryons and the mesons. For the S=-2 channels no experimental scattering data exist, and also the information from hypernuclei is rather limited. Nevertheless, in the fit to the S=0 and S=-1 sectors information from the so called NAGARA event and the $Ξ$-well-depth has been used as constraints to determine the free parameters in the simultaneous fit of the $NN \oplus YN \oplus YY$ data. Therefore, the potentials for the S=-2 sector are determined mainly by the NN-, YN-data, and SU(3)-symmetry.Various properties of the potentials are illustrated by giving results for scattering lengths, effective ranges, bound states, elastic and inelastic phase parameters, and total cross sections. Notably is the prediction of a bound state D$^*$ in the $ΞN(^3S_1,I=1)$-channel with a binding energy $B_E=1.56$ MeV. This state is deuteron-like, i.e. a member of the $\{10^*\}$-decuplet. As for the normal deuteron $D= pn(^3S_1-^3D_1)$ the strong tensor force is responsible for this state. The features of $Ξ$ hypernuclei predicted by ESC08c are studied on the basis of the G-matrix approch. The well-depth $U_Ξ= -7.0$ MeV, and the $Ξ-ΛΛ$ conversion width is $Γ_Ξ^c= 4.5$ MeV.

## Key claim

The ESC08c potentials for the S=-2 baryon-baryon channels are determined primarily by fits to NN and YN scattering data combined with SU(3) symmetry, rather than by direct experimental data in the S=-2 sector, because no scattering data exist for these channels and hypernuclear information is very limited.

## Method

The authors constructed the ESC08c potentials for S=-2 baryon-baryon interactions by extending the SU(3)-symmetric framework from the S=0 and S=-1 sectors, which were fitted to experimental NN and YN data. They included one-boson-exchange, two-pseudoscalar-exchange, meson-pair-exchange, and diffractive contributions, with constraints from the NAGARA event for the Lambda-Lambda interaction and from the assumed Xi well-depth. The potentials were then used in a multi-channel Schrodinger equation in configuration space to compute scattering lengths, bound states, phase parameters, and cross sections, and the G-matrix approach was applied to study Xi hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the deuteron-like bound state D* in the Xi N(3S1, I=1) channel: B_E = 1.56 MeV
- Xi well-depth from G-matrix approach: U_Xi = -7.0 MeV
- Xi N to Lambda-Lambda conversion width: Gamma^c_Xi = 4.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ESC08 model series (Nagels, Rijken, Yamamoto 2012a,b) and the earlier ESC04 models (Rijken 2005, Rijken and Yamamoto 2005) to the S=-2 strangeness sector, following the Nijmegen soft-core NSC97a-f models (Stoks and Rijken 1999) for multi-strange baryon-baryon systems. The predictions enable future experimental searches for Xi hypernuclei and the deuteron-like bound state, which could be accessible in K- K+ production experiments at JPARC.
