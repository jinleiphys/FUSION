# Neutron stars as a dense liquid drop at equilibrium within the effective surface approximation

> The macroscopic model is formulated for a neutron star (NS) as a perfect liquid drop at the equilibrium. We use the leptodermic approximation $a/R\ll 1$, where $a$ is the crust thickness of the effective NS surface (ES), and $R$ is the mean radius of the ES curvature. Within the approximate Schwarzschild metric solution to the general relativity theory equations for the spherically symmetric systems, the macroscopic gravitation is taken into account in terms of the total separation particle energy and incompressibility. Density distribution $ρ$ across the ES in the normal direction to the ES was obtained analytically for a general form of the energy density $\mathcal{E}(ρ)$. For the typical crust thickness, and effective radius, one finds the leading expression for the density $ρ$. NS masses are analytically calculated as a sum of the volume and surface terms, taking into account the radial curvature of the metric space, in reasonable agreement with the recently measured masses for several neutron stars. We derive the simple macroscopic equation of state (EoS) with the surface correction. The analytical and numerical solutions to Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the pressure are in good agreement with the volume part of our EoS.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a macroscopic model of a neutron star as a perfect liquid drop at equilibrium using the leptodermic approximation (crust thickness much smaller than the effective surface curvature radius), and claims that the analytical solutions for density, mass, and equation of state obtained within this effective surface approximation show reasonable agreement with recently measured masses for several neutron stars, though the model relies on the condition that the ratio of crust thickness to effective radius is small (a/R ≪ 1).

## Method

The authors apply the effective surface approximation (ESA) from nuclear physics to neutron stars, using a variational principle to minimize the total energy (including gravitational, nuclear, and surface gradient terms) under constraints of fixed particle number, within the Schwarzschild metric solution to general relativity. They expand the energy density functional in powers of the density gradient and solve the resulting one-dimensional differential equation for the particle number density across the effective surface in the normal direction, obtaining analytical expressions for the density distribution, neutron star mass (as sum of volume and surface terms), and a macroscopic equation of state with surface corrections. The analytical results are compared with numerical solutions to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Crust thickness a ≈ 1 km and effective radius R ≈ 10 km for typical neutron stars, giving a/R ≈ 0.1 as the small leptodermic parameter
- For nuclear matter: saturation density ρ̄ = 0.16 fm⁻³, effective radius r₀ = (4πρ̄/3)⁻¹/³ ≈ 1.14 fm, and diffuseness parameter a ≈ 0.8 fm
- Nuclear incompressibility modulus K ∼ 200 MeV (for nuclear matter)
- Nuclear spin-orbit constant W₀ = 100–130 MeV fm⁵, with spin-orbit coefficient D = −(9m/16ℏ²)W₀²
- The gradient correction to kinetic energy in the ETF approach gives Γ = ℏ²/18m, where m is the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends the effective surface approximation previously developed for nuclear physics (Magner et al., 2009; Basu and Mahapatra, 2013, 2015) to spherical neutron stars, following Tolman's original concept of a neutron star as a dense liquid drop at equilibrium. The approach builds on the leptodermic approximation used in liquid-drop models and the extended Thomas-Fermi approach with Skyrme forces (Brack et al., 1985; Centelles et al., 1998), and enables analytical calculation of neutron star properties including surface corrections to the equation of state, which can be compared with numerical TOV solutions and observational mass measurements.
