# On-shell approximation for the s-wave scattering theory

> We investigate the scattering theory of two particles in a generic $D$-dimensional space. For the s-wave problem, by adopting an on-shell approximation for the $T$-matrix equation, we derive analytical formulas which connect the Fourier transform ${\tilde V}(k)$ of the interaction potential to the s-wave phase shift. In this way we obtain explicit expressions of the low-momentum parameters ${\tilde g}_0$ and ${\tilde g}_2$ of ${\tilde V}(k)={\tilde g}_0+{\tilde g}_2k^2 +...$ in terms of the s-wave scattering length $a_s$ and the s-wave effective range $r_s$ for $D=3$, $D=2$, and $D=1$. Our results, which are strongly dependent on the spatial dimension $D$, are a useful benchmark for few-body and many-body calculations. As a specific application, we derive the zero-temperature pressure of a 2D uniform interacting Bose gas with a beyond-mean-field correction which includes both scattering length and effective range.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical formulas connecting the Fourier transform of the interaction potential to the s-wave phase shift by adopting both an s-wave approximation and an on-shell approximation for the T-matrix equation, which the abstract presents as a straightforward derivation but which the full text clarifies is expected to be reliable only in the regime of low momentum and becomes exact only for k approaching zero.

## Method

The authors start from the T-matrix equation for two particles in D-dimensional space, apply a partial wave decomposition, and then impose two approximations: restricting to the s-wave (l=0) component and assuming that the potential and T-matrix in the integrand can be evaluated on-shell (at k''=k). This reduces the integral equation to an algebraic relation T_0(k) = 1/(1/V_0(k) - C(k)), where C(k) is a dimension-dependent function regularized via dimensional regularization. The resulting expressions for V_0(k) are then linked to the s-wave phase shift δ_0(k) using standard scattering theory relations specific to each dimension D=1,2,3.

## Key numbers

* For D=3: g_0 = (4πħ²/m) a_s and g_2 = (2πħ²/m) a_s² r_s, where a_s is the s-wave scattering length and r_s the s-wave effective range.
* For D=1: g_0 = -(2ħ²)/(m a_s) and g_2 = -(ħ²/m) r_s.
* For D=2: g_0 = -(4πħ²/m) / ln(Λ² a_s² e^γ) and g_2 = (2π²ħ²/m) r_s² / ln²(Λ² a_s² e^γ), where Λ is an ultraviolet cutoff wavenumber and γ ≈ 0.5572 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
* For D=2, the zero-temperature pressure of a uniform interacting Bose gas is P(μ) = (m/(8πħ²)) μ² ln(4ħ²/(m μ a_s² e^{2γ+1/2})) + (3m²/(16ħ⁴)) r_s² μ³.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory (EFT) approach of Hammer and Braaten (various years) for nonuniversal corrections in three dimensions, and resolves a discrepancy with the Born-approximation-based results of Gao, Massignan, Ketterle, and Roth (various years) by showing that the on-shell approximation reproduces the EFT results. The derived formulas enable systematic inclusion of effective range corrections in few-body and many-body calculations across dimensions, with the specific application to the 2D Bose gas pressure providing a benchmark for future Monte Carlo studies and experimental comparisons with ultracold atomic gases.
