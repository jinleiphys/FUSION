# u-Channel Color Transparency Observables

> We propose to study the onset of color transparency in hard exclusive reactions in the backward regime. Guided by the encouraging JLab results on backward pion and omega electro-production data at moderate Q^2, which may be interpreted as the signal of an early scaling regime where the scattering amplitude factorizes in a hard coefficient function convoluted with nucleon to meson transition distribution amplitudes, we show that the study of these channels on nuclear targets opens a new opportunity to test the appearance of nuclear color transparency for a fast moving nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that studying hard exclusive reactions in the backward regime on nuclear targets can provide a new test of nuclear color transparency for a fast moving nucleon, building on the interpretation of recent JLab results on backward pion and omega electroproduction at moderate Q^2 as possible signals of an early scaling regime.

## Method

The authors analyze the factorized scattering amplitude for hard exclusive backward reactions, where the amplitude is expressed as a convolution of a hard coefficient function with nucleon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs). They then extend this formalism to nuclear targets, showing that the nuclear medium can act as a filter to probe the onset of color transparency for the fast recoiling nucleon produced in the backward reaction.

## Context

This work extends the color transparency program from traditional forward-angle exclusive reactions to the backward regime, where the struck nucleon is produced at large momentum transfer. It builds on the transition distribution amplitude formalism developed for backward meson electroproduction and is motivated by recent JLab results on backward pion and omega production. The proposed measurements on nuclear targets would enable tests of color transparency for a fast nucleon, complementing existing studies that have focused on meson propagation through nuclear matter.
