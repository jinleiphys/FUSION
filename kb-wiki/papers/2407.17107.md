# The nuclear matter effect on parton distribution functions

> Parton distribution functions (PDFs) are important quantities in describing nucleon structures. They are universal and process-independent. As a matter of fact, nucleon PDFs are inevitably affected by nuclear matter during nuclear scattering process. In order to study the nuclear PDFs (nPDFs), in this paper, we introduce the nucleon pair PDFs (dPDFs) to describe parton distributions in the nucleon pair which is confined to a nucleus. We first of all construct the nuclear state in terms of the nucleonic states and calculate the operator definition of nPDFs. Neglecting the higher order corrections or multi-nucleon correlations, we find that nPDFs can be written as a sum of two terms which respectively correspond to PDFs and dPDFs. Nucleon pair PDFs which stem from nucleon-nucleon correlation are proportional to common nucleon PDFs but suppressed by a factor. Compared with the experimental data, we find that dPDFs can explain the behaviour of the EMC effect. We further calculate the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio to study the nuclear matter effect on the extraction of weak mixing angle or $\sin^2θ_W$ by using dPDFs.

## Key claim

The authors claim that nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) can be expressed as a sum of free-nucleon PDFs and nucleon-pair PDFs (dPDFs), and that this dPDF contribution can explain the behavior of the EMC effect, but with a deviation of about 4% and with uncertainties that depend strongly on the nPDFs used to determine parameters.

## Method

The authors construct the nuclear state in terms of nucleonic states using light-cone perturbation theory, then calculate the operator definition of nPDFs. Neglecting higher-order corrections and multi-nucleon correlations, they expand the nuclear state to include nucleon pair states and derive nPDFs as a sum of two terms corresponding to free nucleon PDFs and dPDFs. The dPDFs are taken to be proportional to common nucleon PDFs but suppressed by a factor Λ, and the resulting expressions are applied to calculate the EMC effect ratio and the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio.

## Key numbers

- The dPDF model can describe the EMC effect behavior with a deviation of about 4%.
- For the EMC effect calculation using nCTEQ15, the parameter combination n^d Z/n^A is taken as 0.88; using EPPS21, it is taken as 0.91.
- The critical distance for nucleon-nucleon correlation is taken as R ∼ 1.7 fm (twice the radius of a nucleon).
- For the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio, the central value -0.005 is the difference of sin²θ_W between the expected value and the NuTeV measurement.
- The kinematic variable y is set as 0.5 for the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio calculations.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear PDFs by introducing nucleon pair PDFs (dPDFs) to account for nucleon-nucleon correlations, building on the concept of short-range correlations (SRC) studied by Frankfurt (1988), Arrington (2012), and Hen (2014). The approach is similar to that of Osborne (2002) in using light-cone perturbation theory but differs from Frankfurt (1988) by not including non-nucleonic components such as pion mesons. The results enable a partonic-level description of the EMC effect, first observed by the European Muon Collaboration (1983), and provide a framework for studying nuclear matter effects on the extraction of the weak mixing angle, potentially explaining part of the discrepancy between the NuTeV measurement and the Standard Model expectation.
