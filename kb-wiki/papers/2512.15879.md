# Two-proton emission as source of spin-entangled proton pairs

> We show that a two-proton emitter with a diproton-correlated initial state can act as a source of spin-correlated proton pairs. Using a time-dependent three-body model, we investigate the two-proton emission of $^{16}$Ne ($^{14}$O$+2p$) and analyze the spin correlation of the emitted protons. We find that, when the emission proceeds as a democratic three-body process from an initial state containing a spin-singlet diproton correlation, the emitted protons exhibit a pronounced spin-correlation pattern exceeding the local-hidden-variable bound. This spin correlation closely resembles that of a pure spin-singlet pair. In contrast, this pattern is lost when the process is dominated by the sequential emission or when the initial diproton correlation is absent. These results demonstrate that a certain class of two-proton emitters can deliver spin-entangled proton pairs, and their spin correlation reflects the diproton correlation embedded in the initial state.

## Key claim

The authors show that a two-proton emitter with an initial diproton correlation can act as a source of spin-entangled proton pairs, but only when the emission proceeds as a democratic three-body process; the spin correlation is lost when sequential emission dominates or when the initial diproton correlation is absent.

## Method

The authors employed a time-dependent three-body model of the $^{14}$O+2p system for the two-proton decay of $^{16}$Ne, solving the three-body Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon and Coulomb potentials for core-proton interactions and a realistic proton-proton interaction including vacuum and additional medium-effect terms. The initial state was constructed as a confined two-proton state inside the Coulomb barrier, and the time evolution was calculated as $\ket{\Psi(t)} = \exp ( -it \hat{H}_{3B} / \hbar ) \ket{\Psi(0)}$, with the decaying state extracted by subtracting the survival component. The spin correlation $S(\Phi)$ was evaluated using the Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt (CHSH) formulation by projecting the decaying state onto the $S_{12}=0$ and $S_{12}=1$ spin channels.

## Key numbers

- The experimental two-proton emission energy of $^{16}$Ne is $Q_{2p} = 1.4$ MeV.
- For the democratic-emission case, the main-resonance width was $\Gamma_{2p} = 1.4 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV, corresponding to a mean lifetime $\tau = 4.7 \times 10^{-18}$ seconds.
- For the sequential-emission case, the mean lifetime was $\tau = 9.1 \times 10^{-20}$ seconds.
- The spin correlation $S(\Phi)$ for the democratic case reaches a maximal value of approximately $2\sqrt{2}$ at specific angles, exceeding the local-hidden-variable bound of $S(\Phi) = 2$.
- The two-proton density distributions were shown at times corresponding to the same decay probability $P_{\rm decay} (t)= 0.68\%$: at $ct=1000$ fm for the democratic case and $260$ fm for the sequential case.
- The first-excited state of $^{14}$O is at $5.17$ MeV, higher than the $Q_{2p}=1.4$ MeV of $^{16}$Ne.

## Context

This work extends the experimental demonstration by Sakai et al. (2006) of Bell-CHSH inequality violation in the reaction $^2\mathrm{H}(p,{}^2\mathrm{He})n$, proposing two-proton emitters as a natural source of spin-entangled proton pairs. The study builds on prior theoretical work on two-proton decay of $^{16}$Ne by Oishi et al. (2025) and on diproton correlations by Dean (2003), Matsuo (2006), and others. The results enable future investigations of "natural entanglement" in two-proton-emitting nuclides that may be generated in astrophysical processes, as discussed by Gorres (1995) and Schatz (2001), and open the question of whether core excitation in $^{14}$O disrupts the spin correlation.
