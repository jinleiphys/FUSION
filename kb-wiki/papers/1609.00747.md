# Novel quantum phenomena induced by strong magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions

> The relativistic heavy-ion collisions create both hot quark-gluon matter and strong magnetic fields, and provide an arena to study the interplay between quantum chromodynamics and quantum electrodynamics. In recent years, it has been shown that such an interplay can generate a number of interesting quantum phenomena in hadronic and quark-gluon matter. In this short review, we first discuss some properties of the magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions and then give an overview of the magnetic field-induced novel quantum effects. In particular, we focus on the magnetic effect on the heavy flavor mesons, the heavy-quark transports, and the phenomena closely related to chiral anomaly.

## Key claim

This review argues that strong magnetic fields created in noncentral heavy-ion collisions, with strengths comparable to the QCD scale (eB ~ 10^18 Gauss), can induce novel quantum phenomena in quark-gluon plasma, but the precise time evolution of these magnetic fields in the expanding, conducting medium remains an unsolved problem.

## Method

The authors review numerical simulations using the HIJING event generator and Lienard-Wiechert potentials to compute magnetic fields in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, and compare these to an analytical model treating nuclei as extended charge distributions with Woods-Saxon form. They also discuss the magnetohydrodynamic equations incorporating finite electric conductivity to describe the time evolution of the magnetic fields in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

* The event-averaged magnetic field at t=0 and the center of the overlapping region in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV reaches about 10^19 Gauss.
* The event-averaged magnetic field at t=0 and the center of the overlapping region in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV reaches about 10^20 Gauss.
* The magnetic fields in LHC Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV are roughly 13.8 times larger than in RHIC Au+Au at 200 GeV, scaling linearly with collision energy.
* The magnetic fields from spectators decay on a time scale tau_B = R_A / gamma, approximately 2 m_N R_A / sqrt(s).
* In the ideal magnetohydrodynamic limit with Bjorken flow, the magnetic field decays as B_y(t) proportional to (t_0/t) B_y(t_0), slower than the 1/t^3 decay in the insulating case.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions by Kharzeev (2007), Skokov (2009), and Deng (2012), and discusses anomalous transport phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008) and chiral separation effect (Son 2004, Metlitski 2005). The paper enables future experimental searches for these effects at RHIC and LHC, and motivates further theoretical work on the interplay between QCD and QED in strong magnetic fields.
