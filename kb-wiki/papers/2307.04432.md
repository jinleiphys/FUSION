# Density-dependent relativistic mean field approach and its application to single-$Λ$ hypernuclei in Oxygen isotopes

> The in-medium feature of nuclear force which includes both nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and hyperon-nucleon ($ΛN$) interactions impacts the description of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei. With the alternated mass number or isospin of hypernuclei, such effects could be unveiled by analyzing systematical evolution of the bulk and single-particle properties. From a density-dependent meson-nucleon/hyperon coupling perspective, a new $ΛN$ effective interaction in the covariant density functional (CDF) theory, namely DD-LZ1-$\Lambda1$, is obtained by fitting the experimental data of $Λ$ separation energies for several single-$Λ$ hypernuclei. It is then adopted to study the structure and transition properties of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei in Oxygen isotopes, comparing with several selected CDF Lagrangians. Discrepancy is observed explicitly in the isospin evolution of $\Lambda1p$ spin-orbit splitting with various effective interactions, ascribed to their divergence of the meson-hyperon coupling strengths with increasing density. In particular, the density-dependent CDFs introduce an extra contribution to enhance the isospin dependence of the splitting, which is originated from the rearrangement terms of $Λ$ self-energies. In addition, the characteristics of hypernuclear radii are studied along the isotopic chain. Owing to the impurity effect of $Λ$ hyperon, a size shrinkage is observed in the matter radii of hypernuclei as compared to their cores of normal nuclei, while its magnitude is elucidated further to correlate with the incompressibility of nuclear matter. Besides, there exists a sizable model-dependent trend that $Λ$ hyperon radii evolve with the neutron number, which is decided partly by the in-medium $NN$ interactions as well as the core polarization effects.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a new density-dependent relativistic mean-field effective interaction for the Λ-nucleon channel, DD-LZ1-Λ1, which reproduces experimental Λ separation energies for several single-Λ hypernuclei; however, the agreement is notably poorer for light-mass Carbon hyperisotopes and deformed ²⁸_ΛSi, and the authors note that more accurate descriptions of light Carbon hyperisotopes could be obtained by limiting the mass region of fitting and including deformation effects.

## Method
The authors extended the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) Lagrangian DD-LZ1 to include Λ hyperon degrees of freedom, fitting the ratio g_σΛ/g_σN (with g_ωΛ/g_ωN fixed at 0.666 from the naive quark model) to reproduce experimental Λ separation energies of ¹⁶_ΛO, ⁴⁰_ΛCa, and ²⁰⁸_ΛPb via Levenberg-Marquardt minimization. They then solved the Dirac equation for spherical single-Λ hypernuclei in Oxygen isotopes using the new DD-LZ1-Λ1 interaction, comparing results with several other covariant density functional (CDF) Lagrangians including PKDD-Λ1, DD-ME2, TW99, DDV, PKO1-Λ1, PKO2-Λ1, PKO3-Λ1, NL-SH, and PK1.

## Key numbers
- The fitted ratio g_σΛ/g_σN for DD-LZ1-Λ1 is 0.615, with a root-mean-square deviation Δ₁ = 0.319 MeV for the three fitted hypernuclei (¹⁶_ΛO, ⁴⁰_ΛCa, ²⁰⁸_ΛPb) and Δ₂ = 1.810 MeV for all hypernuclei considered.
- For ¹⁵_ΛO with the Λ in the 1s₁/₂ ground state, the single-particle energy ε_s.p. = -14.330 MeV, the hyperon radius R_Λ = 2.115 fm, and the hypernuclear matter radius R_m = 2.458 fm.
- For ¹⁷_ΛO with the Λ in the 1s₁/₂ ground state, the single-particle energy ε_s.p. = -13.086 MeV, the hyperon radius R_Λ = 2.323 fm, and the hypernuclear matter radius R_m = 2.555 fm.
- The B(E1) value for the transition from the excited Λ1p state to the ground Λ1s state in ¹⁵_ΛO is 0.095 e²fm² for the 1p₁/₂ state and 0.119 e²fm² for the 1p₃/₂ state; in ¹⁷_ΛO it is 0.109 e²fm² for the 1p₁/₂ state and 0.121 e²fm² for the 1p₃/₂ state.

## Context
This work extends the recently developed density-dependent relativistic mean-field Lagrangian DD-LZ1 (Wei 2020) to hypernuclear systems, following earlier efforts to construct ΛN effective interactions within the DDRMF framework (Rong 2021) which uncovered a linear correlation between the ratios R_σ and R_ω. The study enables systematic investigation of isospin evolution of hypernuclear properties such as Λ1p spin-orbit splitting and hyperon radii along the Oxygen isotopic chain, and provides predictions relevant to planned hypernuclear experiments at J-PARC on neutron-rich Sn hyperisotopes (Choi 2022, Aoki 2021).
