# Kinetics of the chiral phase transition in a linear $σ$ model

> We study the dynamics of the chiral phase transition in a linear quark-meson $σ$ model using a novel approach based on semiclassical wave-particle duality. The quarks are treated as test particles in a Monte-Carlo simulation of elastic collisions and the coupling to the $σ$ meson, which is treated as a classical field. The exchange of energy and momentum between particles and fields is described in terms of appropriate Gaussian wave packets. It has been demonstrated that energy-momentum conservation and the principle of detailed balance are fulfilled, and that the dynamics leads to the correct equilibrium limit. First schematic studies of the dynamics of matter produced in heavy-ion collisions are presented.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their semiclassical wave-particle duality approach (DSLAM) can correctly reproduce thermal and chemical equilibrium limits for the chiral phase transition in a linear sigma model, but note that the final approach to exact chemical equilibrium is very slow when the driving force from the sigma potential is weak, particularly near the cross-over transition.

## Method

The quarks are treated as test particles in a Monte-Carlo simulation with elastic two-body collisions (constant cross section of 15 mb), while the sigma meson is treated as a classical mean field on a spatial grid. The exchange of energy and momentum between particles and the field for the processes q-anti-q annihilation and sigma decay is implemented using Gaussian wave packets, with the decay process modeled via a coarse-graining approach that maps the local field energy-momentum to a Maxwell-Juettner distribution of sigma mesons. The algorithm is validated in box calculations with periodic boundary conditions and then applied to an expanding fireball with Woods-Saxon temperature profile.

## Key numbers

- Total energy conserved with numerical accuracy of |ΔE_tot/⟨E⟩| ≲ 5 × 10^(-5) in equilibrium box calculations
- Number of test particles N_test = 3 × 10^6, time steps Δt = 2 × 10^(-3) a.u., spatial grid N_σ = 128^3, volume V = (6 fm)^3 for equilibrium tests
- Interaction volume of Gaussian wave packets is 32^3 grid points, approximately 1.5% of system volume
- For thermal quench: initial quark temperature T_q = 140 MeV, sigma field at equilibrium for T_σ = 180 MeV, coupling g = 3.3 (cross-over), N_test = 2 × 10^6, Δt = 0.002 fm/c
- Equilibrium temperature after thermal quench: T_eq ≃ 144 MeV
- For expanding fireball: R_0 = 0.45 fm, surface thickness α = 0.1 fm, system size 5 fm, cutoff radius r_c = 2.75 fm, initial temperature T_initial = 175 MeV, N_test = 10^5, Δt = 2 × 10^(-3) fm/c
- Coupling values studied: g = 3.3 (cross-over), 3.63 (second-order), 5.5 (first-order)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed DSLAM (Dynamical Simulation of a Linear Sigma Model) approach (Wesp 2014) for studying non-equilibrium dynamics of the chiral phase transition. It provides a microscopic kinetic alternative to viscous hydrodynamics and Langevin treatments (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013) for studying fluctuations near the QCD critical point. The method enables first schematic studies of matter produced in heavy-ion collisions, with the potential to investigate signatures of first-order, second-order, and cross-over transitions in dynamically expanding systems.
