# Multi-stage jet evolution through QGP using the JETSCAPE framework: inclusive jets, correlations and leading hadrons

> The JETSCAPE Collaboration has recently announced the first release of the JETSCAPE package that provides a modular, flexible, and extensible Monte Carlo event generator. This innovative framework makes it possible to perform a comprehensive study of multi-stage high-energy jet evolution in the Quark-Gluon Plasma. In this work, we illustrate the performance of the event generator for different algorithmic approaches to jet energy loss, and reproduce the measurements of several jet and hadron observables as well as correlations between the hard and soft sector. We also carry out direct comparisons between different approaches to energy loss to study their sensitivity to those observables.

## Key claim

The JETSCAPE framework can reproduce inclusive hadron and jet suppression measurements at the LHC using a multi-stage approach that couples a high-virtuality parton shower (MATTER) to one of three low-virtuality energy loss models (LBT, MARTINI, or AdS/CFT), with the different model combinations yielding compatible results for single hadron and jet observables.

## Method

Initial hard partons were generated by PYTHIA and fed into the MATTER module for virtuality-ordered shower evolution in vacuum or medium. When parton virtuality dropped below a separation scale Q0 = 2 GeV, the evolution was handed off to one of three low-virtuality modules (LBT, MARTINI, or AdS/CFT) for time-ordered evolution. Hadronization was performed using the Lund-model-based fragmentation model without tracking color information, and the event-averaged hydrodynamic background was provided by 2+1D VISHNU with T R ENTo initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Separation scale Q0 between high- and low-virtuality modules: 2 GeV
- MATTER vacuum shower termination virtuality: 1 GeV
- pp collisions at 2.76 TeV used for baseline validation of inclusive hadron and jet production
- PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV used for RAA calculations in 0-5% and 20-30% centrality classes
- Charged hadron and jet elliptic flow v2 calculated for 20-30% PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework benchmarked in Cao (2017) for brick geometry calculations to full heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. It enables systematic comparisons between different energy loss approaches (MATTER coupled with LBT, MARTINI, or AdS/CFT) within a unified multi-stage framework. The results suggest that event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations, rather than the event-averaged background used here, would improve agreement with jet v2 data, pointing to future work with fluctuating media.
