# Coupling constants of the $S_{11}$ resonances to pseudoscalar mesons and octet baryons

> Coupling constants of $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ resonances to meson-baryon ($πN$, $ηN$, $η^\prime N$, $KY$) are investigated in a chiral quark model, complemented by inclusion of five-quark components in those resonances. The known strong decay partial widths of both resonances are well reproduced, provided sizeable strangeness components in the relevant wave functions. %to excellent description for the strong decays of the above two resonances, which %cannot be reproduced very well in the traditional chiral quark model with or without %$SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ breaking. Finally, we find that $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ %couple strongly to the channels with strangeness.

## Key claim

The coupling constants of the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ resonances to pseudoscalar mesons and octet baryons can be well reproduced in an extended chiral constituent quark model, but only if sizeable five-quark (strangeness) components are included in the resonance wave functions, with the mixing angle and five-quark probabilities constrained to narrow ranges.

## Method

The authors employed an Extended Chiral Constituent Quark Model (E$\chi$CQM) in which the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ resonances are treated as admixtures of three-quark and strangeness five-quark components. Strong decay partial widths were calculated by evaluating matrix elements of non-relativistic meson emission operators for both the three-quark and five-quark sectors, using constituent quark masses $m = 290$ MeV and $m_s = 430$ MeV, and oscillator parameters $\omega_3 = 340$ MeV and $\omega_5 = 600$ MeV. The three adjustable parameters (mixing angle $\theta_S$ and five-quark probabilities $P_{5q}$ and $P^\prime_{5q}$) were varied over their full ranges to find the subspace that simultaneously reproduces the known partial decay widths for both resonances.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: $m = 290$ MeV, $m_s = 430$ MeV; oscillator parameters: $\omega_3 = 340$ MeV, $\omega_5 = 600$ MeV.
- Allowed mixing angle range: $26.9^\circ \leq \theta_S \leq 29.8^\circ$.
- Five-quark probability in $S_{11}(1535)$: $21\% \leq P_{5q} \leq 31\%$.
- Five-quark probability in $S_{11}(1650)$: $11\% \leq P^\prime_{5q} \leq 18\%$.
- Calculated partial width for $S_{11}(1535) \to \pi N$: $58 \pm 5$ MeV (PDG value: $68 \pm 15$ MeV).
- Calculated partial width for $S_{11}(1535) \to \eta N$: $79 \pm 11$ MeV (PDG value: $79 \pm 11$ MeV).
- Calculated partial width for $S_{11}(1650) \to \pi N$: $143 \pm 5$ MeV (PDG value: $128 \pm 29$ MeV).
- Calculated partial width for $S_{11}(1650) \to \eta N$: $4.5 \pm 2.8$ MeV (PDG value: $3.8 \pm 3.6$ MeV).
- Calculated partial width for $S_{11}(1650) \to K\Lambda$: $4.5 \pm 0.5$ MeV (PDG value: $4.8 \pm 0.7$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier E$\chi$CQM studies that successfully described the electromagnetic and strong decays of the $P_{31}(1232)$ (Li and Riska, 2006), $P_{11}(1440)$ (Li and Riska, 2006), $S_{11}(1535)$ (An and Zou, 2009), and $S_{01}(1405)$ (An et al., 2010) resonances. It addresses the failure of traditional chiral constituent quark models (with or without $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ breaking) to simultaneously reproduce the measured decay widths of both $S_{11}$ resonances, and provides coupling constant predictions for seven meson-baryon channels that can be used in future studies of meson production processes, particularly $\eta$ production where the $S_{11}(1535)\eta N$ coupling is found to be large and sensitive to five-quark components.
