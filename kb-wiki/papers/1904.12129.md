# Emergence of nuclear clustering in electric-dipole excitations of $^6$Li

> Nuclear clustering plays an important role, especially in the dynamics of light nuclei. The importance of the emergence of the nuclear clustering was discussed in the recent measurement of the photoabsorption cross sections as it offered the possibility of the coexistence of various excitation modes which are closely related to the nuclear clustering. To understand the excitation mechanism, we study the electric-dipole ($E1$) responses of $^6$Li with a fully microscopic six-body calculation. The ground-state wave function is accurately described with a superposition of correlated Gaussian (CG) functions with the aid of the stochastic variational method. The final-state wave functions are also expressed by a number of the CG functions including important configurations to describe the six-body continuum states excited by the $E1$ field. We found that the out-of-phase transitions occur due to the oscillations of the valence nucleons against the $^4$He cluster at the low energies around 10 MeV indicating ``soft'' giant-dipole-resonance(GDR)-type excitations, which are very unique in the $^6$Li system but could be found in other nuclear systems. At the high energies beyond $\sim 30$ MeV typical GDR-type transitions occur. The $^3$He-$^3$H clustering plays an important role to the GDR phenomena in the intermediate energy regions around 20 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the electric-dipole (E1) response of $^6$Li exhibits three distinct excitation regimes, low-energy "soft" giant-dipole-resonance (GDR)-type excitations around 10 MeV, intermediate-energy GDR phenomena around 20 MeV dominated by $^3$He-$^3$H clustering, and high-energy typical GDR transitions beyond ~30 MeV, but these conclusions depend on the choice of the $u$ parameter in the Minnesota potential, with quantitative differences being small up to ~40 MeV across the three $u$ values tested.

## Method

The authors performed a fully microscopic six-body calculation using correlated Gaussian basis functions with the stochastic variational method, employing the Minnesota effective nucleon-nucleon potential with three different values of the $u$ parameter (1.00, 0.93, 0.87) that control the odd-parity interaction strength. The ground-state wave function of $^6$Li ($J^\pi=1^+$) was expanded with 600 basis functions, and the final-state continuum wave functions for $J^\pi=0^-, 1^-, 2^-$ states were constructed from 18,490 basis functions including single-particle excitation, $\alpha+p+n$ cluster, and $^3$He-$^3$H cluster configurations. The E1 transition strengths and transition densities were calculated by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and analyzing spectroscopic factors for cluster configurations.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy of $^6$Li: -34.63 MeV ($u=1.00$), -33.63 MeV ($u=0.93$), -32.94 MeV ($u=0.87$); experimental value is -31.99 MeV
- Two-nucleon separation energy $S_{pn}$: 4.7 MeV ($u=1.00$), 3.7 MeV ($u=0.93$), 3.1 MeV ($u=0.87$); experimental value is 3.70 MeV
- Root-mean-square point-proton radius $r_p$: 2.20 fm ($u=1.00$), 2.34 fm ($u=0.93$), 2.46 fm ($u=0.87$); experimental value is 2.452 fm
- Spectroscopic factor for $\alpha+d$ configuration $S_{\alpha d}^2$: 0.856 ($u=1.00$), 0.869 ($u=0.93$), 0.882 ($u=0.87$)
- Largest B(E1) value in low-energy region: 0.140 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=12.1$ MeV ($u=0.93$)
- Largest B(E1) value in intermediate-energy region: 0.249 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=22.8$ MeV ($u=0.93$)
- Largest B(E1) value in high-energy region: 0.236 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=30.6$ MeV ($u=0.93$)
- Non-energy-weighted sumrule (NEWSR) value: 4.49 $e^2$fm$^2$ with full model space, 99.6% of the theoretical sumrule satisfied
- Cumulative sum with only $\alpha+p+n$ configurations: 2.45 $e^2$fm$^2$, satisfying 55% of total sumrule value

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach previously applied to $^6$He by Mikami et al. (2014) to the $^6$Li system, using transition densities and spectroscopic factors rather than proton-proton distances to quantify clustering. The study was motivated by the recent photoabsorption cross-section measurements by Yamagata et al. (2017) that showed a two-peak structure in $^6$Li, and by earlier work on the $^6$Li($\gamma,n$) reaction by Costa et al. (1963). The results enable future investigations of similar cluster-driven dipole excitation modes in other light nuclei with well-developed $\alpha$-cluster structure, and provide a microscopic foundation for interpreting experimental photoabsorption data up to ~60 MeV.
