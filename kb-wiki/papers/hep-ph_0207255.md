# Chiral and Color-superconducting Phase Transitions with Vector Interaction in a Simple Model

> We investigate effects of the vector interaction on chiral and color superconducting (CSC) phase transitions at finite density and temperature in a simple Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. It is shown that the repulsive density-density interaction coming from the vector term, which is present in the effective chiral models but has been omitted, enhances the competition between the chiral symmerty breaking (chiSB) and CSC phase transition, and thereby makes the thermodynamic potential have a shallow minimum over a wide range of values of the correlated chiral and CSC order parameters. We find that when the vector coupling is increased, the first order transition between the chiSB and CSC phases becomes weaker, and the coexisting phase in which both the chiral and color-gauge symmetry are dynamically broken comes to exisit over a wider range of the density and temperature. We also show that there can exist two endpoints, which are tricritical points in the chiral limit, along the critical line of the first order transition in some range of values of the vector coupling. Although our analysis is based on a simple model, the nontrivial interplay between the chiSB and CSC phases induced by the vector interaction is expected to be a universal phenomenon and might give a clue to understanding results obtained with two-color QCD on the lattice.

## Key claim

The repulsive density-density interaction from the vector term, which is present in effective chiral models but has been omitted in most prior work, enhances the competition between chiral symmetry breaking and color superconducting phase transitions; when the vector coupling is increased, the first-order transition between these phases becomes weaker and the coexisting phase occupies a wider region of density and temperature, with the possibility of two tricritical points along the first-order critical line for some values of the vector coupling.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor, three-color Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, incorporating scalar, vector, and diquark interaction terms. They solve the coupled gap equations for the dynamical quark mass M_D and the diquark gap Delta as functions of temperature T and chemical potential mu, with the vector coupling G_V varied as a free parameter in the range G_V/G_S = 0 to 0.5. The thermodynamic potential is minimized to determine the equilibrium phase, and the quark number density is obtained from the thermodynamic relation rho_q = -d(Omega/V)/d mu.

## Key numbers

* The scalar coupling constant G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2} and three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 631 MeV are chosen to reproduce m_pi = 139 MeV and f_pi = 93 MeV with current quark mass m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV.
* The diquark coupling ratio is fixed at G_C/G_S = 0.6, which reproduces the phase diagram obtained with the instanton-induced interaction.
* Without vector interaction, the endpoint of the first-order chiral transition is at (T_e, mu_Be) = (47, 990) MeV.
* At T = 0 and G_V = 0, the critical chemical potential for the first-order transition between chiSB and CSC phases is mu_B0 = 1035 MeV.
* The critical temperature for the crossover chiral transition at mu = 0 is 187 MeV.
* The vector coupling is varied in the range G_V/G_S = 0 to 0.5, encompassing the physical values from the instanton-anti-instanton molecule model (G_V = 0.25 G_S) and renormalization-group analysis (G_V = 0.5 G_C).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the chiral-to-CSC transition using NJL-type models (Berges and Rajagopal 1999; Rapp et al. 2000) by incorporating the vector interaction that had been omitted in most earlier work, with the exception of very recent studies (Lawley and Rischke 2002; Bedaque et al. 2002). The authors show that the vector interaction induces a nontrivial interplay between the chiSB and CSC phases through the density jump accompanying chiral restoration, and they note that the resulting phase diagram has a good correspondence with results from two-color QCD lattice simulations (Kogut et al. 2002). The work enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram that must include the vector interaction to be complete, particularly for understanding the competition between chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity at finite density.
