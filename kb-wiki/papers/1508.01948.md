# The Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen and the proton radius from effective field theories

> We comprehensively analyse the theoretical prediction for the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen, and the associated determination of the proton radius. We use effective field theories. This allows us to relate the proton radius with well-defined objects in quantum field theory, eliminating unnecessary model dependence. The use of effective field theories also helps us to organize the computation so that we can clearly state the parametric accuracy of the result. In this paper we review all (and check several of) the contributions to the energy shift of order $α^5$, as well as those that scale like $α^6\times$logarithms in the context of non-relativistic effective field theories of QED.

## Key claim

The paper provides a model-independent theoretical prediction for the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen using effective field theories, obtaining a proton radius of 0.8413(15) fm that remains at 6.8 sigma variance with the CODATA value, confirming that the proton radius puzzle persists even after removing model-dependent assumptions.

## Method

The authors use potential non-relativistic QED (pNRQED) to systematically organize the computation by integrating out scales from the proton mass down to the muonic hydrogen binding energy, following the path from Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory through NRQED to pNRQED. They compute all contributions to the Lamb shift at order alpha^5 and alpha^6 times logarithms, including the static potential with vacuum polarization up to three loops, light-by-light scattering diagrams, and the 1/m^2 potentials from tree-level and one-loop matching. The hadronic effects are encoded in Wilson coefficients such as c_D^(p) (related to the proton radius) and c_3^(had) (from two-photon exchange), with the latter computed from chiral perturbation theory including pion and Delta contributions.

## Key numbers

* Experimental Lamb shift: Delta E_L^exp = 202.3706(23) meV for the 2P_3/2 to 2S_1/2 transition in muonic hydrogen
* Theoretical expression: Delta E_L^th = [206.070(13) - 5.2270(7) * r_p^2/fm^2] meV
* Proton radius from this analysis: r_p = 0.8413(15) fm, at 6.8 sigma variance with the CODATA value of 0.8775(51) fm
* Hadronic contribution to the spin-independent four-fermion Wilson coefficient: c_3^(had) = alpha^2 * (m_mu/m_pi) * 47.2(23.6) (pions only) or 56.7(20.6) (pions plus Delta)
* Hadronic contribution to the spin-dependent four-fermion Wilson coefficient: c_4^(had) approximately -46 alpha^2
* Hadronic vacuum polarization derivative: Pi_h' approximately 9.3 * 10^(-3) GeV^(-2)
* Proton anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_p = 1.792847356(23)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Letter (Peset and Pineda, 2014) by providing full details of the QED-related computations, building on the pNRQED framework developed by Pineda (1997) and its application to muonic hydrogen (Pineda, 2004). The analysis contradicts the CODATA value (Mohr et al., 2012) derived from hydrogen spectroscopy and electron-proton scattering, and enables future higher-order computations using EFTs by providing the complete formalism for arbitrary charges applicable to other muonic atoms. The hadronic matching computation between HBET and NRQED was detailed in a separate paper (Peset and Pineda, 2014), with partial results from Nevado and Pineda (2007) and Alarcon et al. (2013).
