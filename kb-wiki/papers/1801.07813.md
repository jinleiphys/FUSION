# Matter-neutrino resonance in a multi-angle neutrino bulb model

> Simulations of neutrino flavor evolution in compact merger environments have shown that neutrino flavor, and hence nucleosynthesis, can be strongly affected by the presence of matter-neutrino resonances (MNRs), where there is a cancelation between the matter and the neutrino potential. Simulations performed thus far follow flavor evolution along a single neutrino trajectory, but self-consistency requires all trajectories to be treated simultaneously, and it has not been known whether MNR phenomena would still occur in multi-angle models. In this paper, we present the first fully multi-angle calculations of MNR. We find that familiar MNR phenomena, where neutrinos transform to a greater extent than anti-neutrinos and a feedback mechanism maintains the cancellation between the matter and neutrino potential, still occurs for a subset of angular bins, although the flavor transformation is not as efficient as in the single-angle case. In addition, we find other types of flavor transformation that are not seen in single-angle simulations. These flavor transformation phenomena appear to be robust and are present for a wide range of model parameters, as long as an MNR is present. Although computational constraints currently limit us to models with spherical symmetry, our results suggest that the presence of an MNR generally leads to large-scale neutrino flavor evolution in multi-angle systems.

# Matter-neutrino resonance in a multi-angle neutrino bulb model

## Key claim

Matter-neutrino resonance (MNR) phenomena, where neutrinos transform more than anti-neutrinos and a feedback mechanism maintains cancellation between matter and neutrino potentials, still occur in fully multi-angle calculations for a subset of angular bins, but flavor transformation is less efficient than in the single-angle approximation; additional types of flavor transformation not seen in single-angle simulations also appear.

## Method

The authors performed fully multi-angle, single-energy neutrino flavor evolution calculations using a spherically symmetric "bulb model" with semi-isotropic neutrino emission from a spherical neutrinosphere, tracking all outward-directed neutrino trajectories simultaneously. They adopted the small-angle approximation for radii much larger than the neutrinosphere and solved the coupled integro-differential equations for neutrino and anti-neutrino flavor density matrices binned by emission angle, using adaptive Runge-Kutta methods with implicit integrators where stiffness occurred near resonance. The matter potential was calculated self-consistently from an equilibrium electron fraction that depends on neutrino survival probabilities through capture rates on neutrons and protons.

## Key numbers

- The benchmark model uses an anti-neutrino to neutrino flux ratio of alpha = 1.4, with a neutrino potential of 4.6 x 10^5 km^-1 at the neutrinosphere.
- The neutrinosphere radius is R_NS = 15 km, and the radially-emitted neutrinos cross the MNR at R_MNR = 60 km.
- The matter potential at the neutrinosphere is H_M(R_NS) = 1.15 x 10^5 km^-1, corresponding to baryon number density n_B,NS = 3.9 x 10^32 cm^-3.
- The baryon number density at the MNR crossing decreases to 6.1 x 10^30 cm^-3.
- The flavor transformation occurs over a radial range of approximately 100 km in the multi-angle case, compared to approximately 300 km in the single-angle case.
- The calculation required O(4000) or more angular bins for convergence, especially at high matter and neutrino densities.
- The initial electron fraction for untransformed spectra is Y_e = 0.45, derived from the expression Y_e = (P_S + 0.51)/(P_S + 1.44*P_bar_S + 0.90).

## Context

This work extends previous single-angle MNR calculations by Malkus (2014), Zhu (2016), Frensel (2016), and Tian (2017), which showed that MNR could alter neutrino flavor content in compact object merger winds but assumed all neutrinos follow the same flavor evolution. One prior multi-angle calculation by Shalgar (2017) examined MNR for a neutrino beam with nonzero opening angle through constant matter, but this paper presents the first fully multi-angle MNR calculations in an astrophysically motivated bulb geometry. The results suggest that MNR generally leads to large-scale flavor evolution in multi-angle systems, though computational constraints currently limit the models to spherical symmetry, and future work could address the full three-dimensional merger geometry with accretion disks.
