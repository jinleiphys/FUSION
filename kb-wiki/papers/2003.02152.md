# Improved neutrino-nucleon interactions in dense and hot matter for numerical simulations

> Neutrinos play an important role in compact star astrophysics: neutrino-heating is one of the main ingredients in core-collapse supernovae, neutrino-matter interactions determine the composition of matter in binary neutron star mergers and have among others a strong impact on conditions for heavy element nucleosynthesis and neutron star cooling is dominated by neutrino emission except for very old stars. Many works in the last decades have shown that in dense matter medium effects considerably change the neutrino-matter interaction rates, whereas many astrophysical simulations use analytic approximations which are often far from reproducing more complete calculations. In this work we present a scheme which allows to incorporate improved rates, for charged current interactions, into simulations and show as an example some results for core-collapse supernovae, where a noticeable difference is found in the location of the neutrinospheres of the low-energy neutrinos in the early post-bounce phase.

## Key claim

The authors present a scheme to incorporate improved, fully energy-dependent charged-current neutrino-nucleon opacities into astrophysical simulations, including nuclear correlations via the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and full phase space effects, but they restrict the analysis to non-relativistic kinematics and neglect weak magnetism corrections, which will be addressed in future work.

## Method

The authors compute neutrino and anti-neutrino absorption opacities from charged-current reactions on nucleons by evaluating the imaginary part of the polarization function, first in the elastic approximation, then in mean field with full phase space using the Lindhard function, and finally with RPA correlations using a Landau approximation for the residual interaction. For the axial channel, they employ a microscopically motivated momentum-dependent residual interaction involving pion and rho meson exchange to avoid instabilities present in standard Skyrme forces. The resulting opacities are tabulated as functions of neutrino energy, baryon number density, temperature, and electron fraction, and are made available on the Compose database together with the underlying equation of state data.

## Key numbers

- The axial-vector coupling ratio is taken as g_A/g_V = 1.2695 from free neutron decay.
- The pion mass is m_pi = 140 MeV, the rho meson mass is m_rho = 770 MeV, the pion-nucleon coupling is f_piNN = 1.01, and the Landau-Migdal parameter is g' = 0.6.
- The cutoff parameters for the form factors are Lambda_pi = 550 MeV and Lambda_rho = 1 GeV.
- RPA correlations can reduce opacities by up to a factor five in high density matter, as cited from prior work (Reddy 1998, Burrows 1998).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Roberts (2016), who provided charged-current opacities with full relativistic phase space and mean field corrections via the nuOpac library, by additionally including RPA correlations with full energy dependence rather than grey correction factors. It also builds on earlier work by Reddy (1998) and Burrows (1998) who studied RPA effects but only provided energy-independent correction factors. The tabulated opacities enable simulations of core-collapse supernovae, binary neutron star mergers, and proto-neutron star cooling to use state-of-the-art neutrino-nucleon interaction rates that are consistent with the underlying equation of state.
