# Anisotropic flow of the fireball fed by hard partons

> In nuclear collisions at highest accessible LHC energies, often more than one dijet pairs deposit momentum into the deconfined expanding medium. With the help of 3+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulation we show that this leads to measurable contribution to the anisotropy of collective transverse expansion. Hard partons generate streams in plasma which merge if they come close to each other. This mechanism correlates the resulting contribution to flow anisotropy with the fireball geometry and causes an increase of the elliptic flow in non-central collisions.

## Key claim

Hard partons from multiple dijet pairs in a single LHC heavy-ion collision deposit momentum into the deconfined medium, generating streams that merge when close together; this mechanism produces a measurable contribution to elliptic flow that is correlated with the fireball geometry, increasing \(v_2\) in non-central collisions beyond what the almond-shaped initial overlap alone would produce.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic simulations (SHASTA algorithm) of Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV, using smooth optical Glauber initial conditions with no event-by-event fluctuations. Hard partons (gluons with \(p_t > 3\) GeV) were generated according to a parametrized cross-section, and their momentum loss into the medium was implemented via source terms in the energy-momentum conservation equation, with energy loss scaling with entropy density. Freeze-out was handled by the Cooper-Frye prescription at \(T = 150\) MeV using THERMINATOR2, and anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_1\) through \(v_4\) for charged hadrons were obtained via the two-particle cumulant method.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) of order 0.015 generated in ultra-central collisions (\(b = 0\) fm) by hard partons, for charged hadrons with energy loss \(dE/dx|_0 = 4\) or 7 GeV/fm.
- In non-central collisions (30–40% centrality), \(v_2\) increases by more than a factor of 1.5 compared to the event-averaged almond-shape contribution alone, for charged hadrons with \(dE/dx|_0 = 4\) GeV/fm.
- Initial energy density \(\epsilon_0 = 60\) GeV/fm\(^3\) at proper time \(\tau = 0.55\) fm/\(c\); nucleon-nucleon cross-section 62 mb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV.
- Hard parton production parametrized with \(B = 14.7\) mb/GeV\(^2\), \(p_0 = 6\) GeV, \(n = 9.5\); dijet pairs generated with \(p_t\) above 3 GeV.
- Two values of energy loss tested: \(dE/dx|_0 = 4\) GeV/fm and 7 GeV/fm, with scaling reference at energy density 20.0 GeV/fm\(^3\) (\(T = 324\) MeV, \(s = 78.2\)/fm\(^3\)).

## Context

This work extends the toy-model study of Tomasik (2011) which suggested that multiple minijet wakes could merge and produce elliptic flow correlated with the reaction plane, and the static-medium hydrodynamic demonstration by Schulc (2013). It contrasts with the HYDJET++ model of Bravina (2014), which treats soft and hard production as independent parts, by focusing on the interplay between the fluid and jets. The results enable future studies that include shear and bulk viscosities and fluctuating initial conditions to disentangle the various mechanisms generating azimuthal anisotropies in LHC data.
