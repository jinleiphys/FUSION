# A quark-model based study of the triton binding energy

# A quark-model based study of the triton binding energy

## Key claim
The triton binding energy obtained from a nucleon-nucleon potential fully derived from a quark-quark interaction is comparable to those predicted by conventional meson-exchange models of the NN force, but the calculation neglects genuine three-body forces from the quark substructure that could add approximately 0.2 MeV of additional binding.

## Method
The authors derived a nonlocal NN potential using the resonating group method (RGM) from a chiral quark cluster model that includes pseudoscalar and scalar exchange between constituent quarks, one-gluon exchange, and a coupling to the NΔ channel. They performed a five-channel Faddeev calculation in momentum space, using separable finite-rank expansions of rank 6 for the ¹S₀-(⁵D₀) and ³S₁-³D₁ partial waves via the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) method, to compute the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers
- The triton binding energy predicted by the quark-model based NN potential is comparable to those from conventional NN potentials such as the Bonn and Nijmegen models (exact numerical value not given in the text).
- The quark-model potential reproduces NN experimental data up to laboratory energies of 250 MeV.
- The NΔ coupling provides an isospin-dependent mechanism generating additional attraction in the ¹S₀ channel.
- An estimation from Hecht (reference in text) suggests that genuine three-body forces from the quark substructure could yield additional binding on the order of 0.2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the NN interaction using the chiral quark cluster model (Juliá-Díaz et al., 1999; Buchmann et al., 1997) to the three-nucleon bound state, which had rarely been done with fully quark-derived potentials. It contrasts with earlier approaches that supplemented quark-model interactions with meson-exchange potentials (Oka, 1988; Tuebingen group) or that derived only the short-range part from quarks (Takeuchi et al., 1992). The results enable future few-body calculations with quark-model interactions and motivate more consistent three-nucleon RGM treatments that include genuine three-body forces from quark degrees of freedom.
