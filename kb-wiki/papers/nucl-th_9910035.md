# Small size boundary effects on two-pion interferometry

> The Bose-Einstein correlations of two identically charged pions are derived when these particles, the most abundantly produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions, are confined in finite volumes. Boundary effects on single pion spectrum are also studied. Numerical results emphasize that conventional formulation usually adopted to describe two-pion interferometry should not be used when the source size is small, since this is the most sensitive case to boundary effects. Specific examples are considered for better illustration.

## Key claim

The conventional formulation of two-pion interferometry, which treats the pion source as a classical current and assumes the momentum is the only relevant quantum number, should not be used when the source size is small because boundary effects become significant and the semiclassical approach leads to inconsistent results.

## Method

The authors derived the single-pion spectrum and two-pion correlation function for identically charged pions confined in finite volumes using a density matrix approach with a Hamiltonian and number operator expressed in terms of creation and annihilation operators for eigenstates of the confining geometry. They considered two specific boundary conditions: pions confined in a sphere with an infinite potential well (solutions of the Klein-Gordon equation with vanishing wavefunction at the boundary) and pions confined in a cubic box with either periodic or infinite-wall boundary conditions. The correlation function was computed from the ratio of the two-particle distribution to the product of single-particle distributions, where the two-particle distribution includes the symmetrization term from Bose-Einstein statistics.

## Key numbers

- For a spherical source of radius R = 3 fm at temperature T = 0.12 GeV, the single-pion spectrum at zero momentum is given by P_1(p=0) = V * sum_{n=1}^{infinity} [1/(exp(E_n/T)-1)] * (3/(4*pi^5*n^2)), where E_n = sqrt((n*pi/R)^2 + m_pi^2) and V = (4*pi/3)*R^3.
- The freeze-out temperature range considered is 0.1 to 0.2 GeV, with T = 0.12 GeV chosen as the default value, consistent with experimental estimates.
- In the limit of very large volume (R approaching infinity), the correlation function reduces to C_2 = 1 for p_1 not equal p_2 and C_2 = 2 for p_1 = p_2.
- For a cubic box with infinite-wall boundary conditions in the limit of very large volume, C_2 = 2 when p_1 = +/- p_2 and C_2 = 1 otherwise.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Makhlin and Sinyukov (1993, 1995) and Andreev and Sinyukov (1997), who modeled pions in a quasi-bound liquid phase with a reflecting boundary, and contradicts the results of Yano and Suzuki (1994, 1999), who found an enhancement of the low-momentum spectrum rather than the depletion observed here. The paper enables more accurate HBT interferometry analyses for small emission volumes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for systems where quantum statistics effects become important and semiclassical treatments fail.
