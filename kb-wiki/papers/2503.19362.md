# I-C-Q relations for rapidly rotating stable hybrid stars

> A number of hadronic equations of state for neutron stars have been investigated for the purpose of the present paper, considering the fact that at sufficiently high density, heavy baryons and quark phases may appear. The observational limits from NICER, GW170817, etc., are obeyed by our choice of equations of state. The universal relations are investigated for both slowly and rapidly rotating neutron stars with heavy baryons present inside the core. For slowly rotating stars, the universality of the I-Love-Q relations is verified, and the I-C-Q relations are inferred to be universal for rapidly rotating stars. Further, we extend the investigation to obtain the universal relations for compact stars containing the quark core, where the connected stable branch of such hybrid stars is considered. The parameters of the I-Love-Q and I-C-Q universal relations are obtained for slowly rotating and rapidly rotating hybrid stars, respectively. These relations would enable extracting information, within the context of general relativity, from astrophysical systems involving rapidly rotating neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that I-C-Q (moment of inertia, compactness, quadrupole moment) relations are approximately universal for rapidly rotating neutron stars and hybrid stars containing a quark core, but this universality holds only for the connected stable branch of hybrid stars constructed via Maxwell phase transition with a small energy-density jump.

## Method

The authors used 12 equations of state from the CompOSE database, including nucleonic (DD2), hyperonic (DD2Λ, DD2Λφ, DD2Y), and Δ-admixed (DD2YΔ(a)-(c)) hadronic models, plus hybrid models combining hadronic EoSs with perturbative QCD quark matter via Maxwell construction. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static stars, the Hartle-Thorne slow-rotation formalism for slowly rotating configurations, and a rapid-rotation code for spin parameters χ = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, and the Keplerian limit. They then fitted polynomial relations between dimensionless moment of inertia (Ī), compactness (C), and dimensionless quadrupole moment (Q̄) for each rotation sequence.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass range for static hadronic configurations: 2.0 M⊙ ≤ Mmax ≤ 2.4 M⊙
- Radius range for 1.4 M⊙ static configurations: 12 km ≲ R1.4 ≲ 13.5 km
- Maximum mass range for static hybrid stars: 1.95 M⊙ ≤ Mmax ≤ 2.2 M⊙
- Three Δ potential depths used: UΔ(ρsat) = -83, -124, -57 MeV for DD2YΔ(a)-(c) respectively
- Spin parameters investigated: χ = 10⁻³, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, and the Keplerian sequence

## Context

This work extends previous studies of I-Love-Q universal relations by Yagi (2013) and others from slowly rotating neutron stars to rapidly rotating configurations with heavy baryons and quark cores. It builds on the hadronic EoS families DD2 (Hempel 2010), DD2Y (Marques 2017), and DD2YΔ (Raduta 2022), and uses the pQCD quark matter EoS of Fraga (2014) with Maxwell construction following Alford (2013). The results enable extracting neutron star properties from gravitational wave and pulsar observations of rapidly rotating systems, complementing the slow-rotation universal relations already in the literature.
