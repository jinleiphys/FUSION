# Antiferrosmectic ground state of two-component dipolar Fermi gases -- an analog of meson condensation in nuclear matter

> We show that an antiferrosmectic-C phase has lower energy at high densities than the non-magnetized Fermi gas and ferronematic phases in an ultracold gas of fermionic atoms, or molecules, with large magnetic dipole moments. This phase, which is analogous to meson condensation in dense nuclear matter, is a one-dimensional periodic structure in which the fermions localize in layers with their pseudospin direction aligned parallel to the layers, and staggered layer by layer.

# Antiferrosmectic ground state of two-component dipolar Fermi gases

## Key claim

The authors propose that an antiferrosmectic-C (AFSC) phase, a one-dimensional layered structure with staggered magnetization parallel to the layers, has lower energy than both the non-magnetized Fermi gas and the ferronematic phase at high densities in ultracold two-component dipolar Fermi gases, but only within a specific region of the coupling constant plane bounded by an instability line where the compressibility vanishes.

## Method

The authors construct a variational wavefunction for the AFSC phase using Gaussian-localized basis states in the z-direction with staggered spinors and plane waves in the transverse directions. They compute the energy density of this state analytically in a one-mode approximation, including kinetic energy, dipole-dipole interaction (direct and exchange terms), and short-range contact repulsion, then minimize with respect to the layer spacing and Gaussian width parameters. The resulting minimum energy is compared to the energies of the non-magnetized Fermi gas and the fully polarized ferronematic phase to construct the zero-temperature phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless dipole coupling constant is defined as λ_d = n μ²/ε_F, and the short-range coupling constant as λ_s = n g/ε_F, where n is the average fermion density and ε_F is the Fermi energy.
- The critical line for instability of the non-magnetized Fermi gas against spatially varying magnetization is given by (1 − 3/4 λ_s − 2π λ_d)(1 + π/2) − π²/2 λ_d² = 0.
- The AFSC phase becomes unstable due to collapse when λ_d reaches approximately 0.40–0.43 (for the parameters studied).
- For laboratory systems, λ_d = 2.7 × 10⁻³ m₁₀₀ n₁₂^{1/3} μ₁₀² and λ_s = 2.6 × 10⁻² n₁₂^{1/3} a₁₀, where m₁₀₀ is mass in units of 100 proton masses, n₁₂ is density in 10¹² cm⁻³, μ₁₀ is magnetic moment in 10 μ_B, and a₁₀ is scattering length in 10 nm.
- The interlayer distance d in the AFSC phase is approximately 10² n₁₂^{-1/3} μm.
- Reaching the AFSC phase with dysprosium atoms would require n ∼ 5 × 10¹⁷ cm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the study of dipolar Fermi gases beyond the ferronematic phase previously identified by Fregoso et al. (2009) and the RPA stability analyses by Yamaguchi (2010) and Sogo (2012). The AFSC phase is motivated by the classical dipole configuration on a cubic lattice found by Luttinger and Tisza (1946) and by meson-condensed states in nuclear physics studied by Kunihiro (1978), Matsui (1978), Baym (1979), and Tamagaki (1993). The results open the possibility of using cold atom experiments to study analogs of dense nuclear matter in neutron stars, where the spatially varying magnetization in the AFSC phase corresponds to spontaneous formation of a vector potential analogous to ρ-meson condensation.
