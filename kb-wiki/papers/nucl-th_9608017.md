# K^+ momentum spectrum from (K^-,K^+) reactions in intranuclear cascade model

> In a framework of intranuclear cascade (INC) type calculation, we study a momentum spectrum in reactions \KK at a beam momentum of 1.65 GeV/c. INC model calculations are compared with the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) calculations to perform the detailed study of the reaction mechanism. We find that the INC model can reproduce the experimental data on various targets. Especially, in the low-momentum region, the forward-angle cross sections of the $(K^-,K^+)$ reaction on from light to heavy targets are consistently explained with the two-step strangeness exchange and production processes with various intermediate mesons, and $ϕ$, $a_0$ and $f_0$ productions and their decay into $K^+K^-$. In the two-step processes, inclusion of meson and hyperon resonances is found to be essential.

## Key claim

The intranuclear cascade (INC) model can reproduce the experimental forward-angle cross sections of the (K⁻,K⁺) reaction on targets from light to heavy nuclei at a beam momentum of 1.65 GeV/c, but only when two-step strangeness exchange and production processes with various intermediate mesons, as well as φ, a₀ and f₀ production and their decay into K⁺K⁻, are included; the inclusion of meson and hyperon resonances in these two-step processes is essential.

## Method

The authors performed intranuclear cascade (INC) model calculations for (K⁻,K⁺) reactions on ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁶³Cu, ¹⁰⁷Ag and ²⁰⁸Pb targets at a beam momentum of 1.65 GeV/c, using a Monte Carlo procedure where the leading particle propagates through a Fermi gas nucleon momentum distribution. Elementary cross sections for direct double strangeness exchange (K⁻p → KΞ, KΞ*), heavy meson production (φ, a₀, f₀) and their decay into K⁺K⁻, and two-step processes involving intermediate mesons (π, ρ, η, ω, η') and hyperon resonances were included, with unknown cross sections estimated using a generalized Breit-Wigner formula assuming s-channel dominance. The INC results were compared with relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) calculations that treat only one-step quasifree processes with eikonal distortion.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: 1.65 GeV/c for the incident K⁻ meson
- Detector angular range: θ_min = 1.7° to θ_max = 13.6° for forward-angle cross sections
- Fermi momentum used in RIA: P_F = 0.27 GeV/c for the proton momentum distribution in nuclei
- K⁻-nucleon total cross section for distortion: σ_K⁻ = 29.0 mb
- K⁺-nucleon total cross section for distortion: σ_K⁺ = 18.4 mb
- Experimental low-momentum region A-dependence: A^(0.56 ± 0.02)
- Experimental high-momentum region A-dependence: A^(0.38 ± 0.03)
- φ N total cross section used: 8.3 mb (from photoproduction experiment) or 16.0 mb (additive-quark model prediction)
- Number of Monte Carlo events generated: 1,000,000 per calculation

## Context

This work extends the analysis of (K⁻,K⁺) reactions on nuclei beyond the quasifree one-step processes considered in earlier distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations by Iijima et al. (1993), which could not explain the large low-momentum bump in the K⁺ spectrum. The authors address the proposal by Gobbi et al. (1994) that heavy-meson production (φ, a₀, f₀) followed by decay into K⁺K⁻ dominates the low-momentum region, but they find that two-step strangeness exchange and production processes with intermediate mesons and hyperon resonances are also essential. The INC model enables systematic treatment of multi-step processes across multiple target masses, providing a framework for understanding the A-dependence of the K⁺ momentum spectrum and for future studies of double-strangeness nuclear systems.
