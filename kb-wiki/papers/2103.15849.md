# The role of $U(1)_A$ symmetry breaking in the QCD corrections to the pion mass difference

> The charged and neutral pion mass difference can be attributed to both the QED and QCD contributions. The current quark mass difference ($Δm$) is the source of the QCD contribution. Here, in a two-flavour non-local NJL model, we try to estimate the QCD contribution. Interestingly, we find that the strength of the $U(1)_A$ symmetry-breaking parameter $c$ plays a crucial role in obtaining the pion mass difference while intertwined with the current quark mass difference. To obtain the QCD contribution for the pion mass difference, we scan the parameter space in $\{Δm,\; c\}$, and by comparing this with the existing results, we constrained the parameter space. Further, using a fitted value of $c$, we determine the allowed range for the $Δm$ in the model. The model estimated $Δm$ ranges enable us to extract the chiral perturbation theory low-energy constant, $l_7$ and verify the dependence of the pion mass difference on $Δm$. We also find out its dependence on $c$ \textemdash\, it increases with the decreasing value of $c$, i.e., toward an axial anomaly restored phase.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the QCD contribution to the charged-neutral pion mass difference depends not only on the current quark mass difference Δm but also crucially on the strength c of U(1)_A symmetry breaking in a non-local NJL model, a dependence that had not been explored before. The authors find that the pion mass difference increases as c decreases (toward an axial anomaly restored phase), and they use existing χPT results to constrain the allowed parameter space in {Δm, c}.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Gaussian form factor g(q)=exp(-q²/Λ²) and a general interaction that includes both U(1)_A symmetric and breaking terms controlled by a parameter c. They solve coupled gap equations for the mean fields σ and Δσ to obtain momentum-dependent constituent quark masses for u and d quarks, then compute the charged and neutral pion masses from the poles of the meson propagators, accounting for π⁰-η* mixing which depends on both c and Δm. The parameter space in {Δm, c} is scanned, and the resulting pion mass differences are compared with existing χPT results to constrain Δm.

## Key numbers

- Physical pion masses from PDG: M_π⁰ = 134.9770 ± 0.0005 MeV, M_π⁺ - M_π⁰ = 4.5936 ± 0.0005 MeV
- Fitted model parameters: m₀ = 6.94 MeV, G = 29.38/Λ², Λ = 605.05 MeV, with Σ_Ave = 221.1 MeV and f_π = 92.9 MeV in the isospin-symmetric limit
- Fitted U(1)_A breaking parameter: c_fit = 0.149^{+0.103}_{-0.029}
- χPT result for QCD contribution to pion mass difference: (M_π⁺ - M_π⁰)_QCD = 0.17(03) MeV (Gasser and Leutwyler 1984) and 0.32(0.20) MeV (Amoros et al. 2001)
- Allowed Δm ranges from the model: Δm = 2.59(_{-0.24}^{+0.22})(_{-0.35}^{+1.26}) MeV for the Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) constraint, and Δm = 3.55(_{-1.37}^{+0.97})(_{-0.48}^{+1.73}) MeV for the Amoros et al. (2001) constraint

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies of isospin breaking by Dmitrasinovic (1995) and Fujihara et al. (2005) by incorporating the role of U(1)_A symmetry breaking through the 't Hooft determinant term, which had been shown to affect the condensate difference in the presence of external magnetic fields by Ali et al. (2020). The results enable extraction of the chiral perturbation theory low-energy constant l₇ from the model and provide constraints on the current quark mass difference Δm that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations such as those by Blum et al. (2010) and de Divitiis et al. (2013).
