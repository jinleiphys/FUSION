# Effects of isospin and momentum dependent interactions on thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter

> Thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter are studied within a self-consistent thermal model using an isospin and momentum dependent interaction (MDI) constrained by the isospin diffusion data in heavy-ion collisions, a momentum-independent interaction (MID), and an isoscalar momentum-dependent interaction (eMDYI). In particular, we study the temperature dependence of the isospin-dependent bulk and single-particle properties, the mechanical and chemical instabilities, and liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter. Our results indicate that the temperature dependence of the equation of state and the symmetry energy are not so sensitive to the momentum dependence of the interaction. The symmetry energy at fixed density is found to generally decrease with temperature and for the MDI interaction the decrement is essentially due to the potential part. It is further shown that only the low momentum part of the single-particle potential and the nucleon effective mass increases significantly with temperature for the momentum-dependent interactions. For the MDI interaction, the low momentum part of the symmetry potential is significantly reduced with increasing temperature. For the mechanical and chemical instabilities as well as the liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter, our results indicate that the boundary of these instabilities and the phase-coexistence region generally shrink with increasing temperature and is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the isospin and momentum dependence of the nuclear interaction, especially at higher temperatures.

# Effects of isospin and momentum dependent interactions on thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the equation of state and the symmetry energy is not very sensitive to the momentum dependence of the nuclear interaction, but the boundaries of mechanical and chemical instabilities and the liquid-gas phase coexistence region are sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the isospin and momentum dependence of the interaction, especially at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent thermal model calculations for asymmetric nuclear matter using three different interactions: the isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction (constrained by isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions), a momentum-independent MID interaction, and an isoscalar momentum-dependent eMDYI interaction. For fixed density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry, the chemical potentials and Fermi distribution functions were determined numerically via a self-consistency iteration scheme, from which the energy per nucleon, entropy per nucleon, and pressure were computed using thermodynamic relations.

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility of cold symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density $\rho_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is set to $K_0 = 211$ MeV for all three interactions.
- The symmetry energy at zero temperature and normal density is fixed at $31.6$ MeV for the MDI interaction, with density dependence parameterized as $31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.69}$ for $x=0$ (softer) and $31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^{1.05}$ for $x=-1$ (stiffer).
- The MDI interaction parameters include $C_{\tau,-\tau} = -103.4$ MeV and $C_{\tau,\tau} = -11.7$ MeV for the momentum-dependent terms, with $\sigma = 4/3$.
- The symmetry energy at fixed density generally decreases with increasing temperature for all interactions; for the MDI interaction at $\rho = 1.0\rho_0$, the total symmetry energy and its potential part both decrease with temperature while the kinetic part increases slightly at low temperature then decreases at high temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cold asymmetric nuclear matter (e.g., Chen et al. 2005, Li et al. 2005) by incorporating temperature effects, and addresses the lack of theoretical attention to momentum-dependent interactions in hot asymmetric nuclear matter noted by Xu et al. (2007) and Mou et al. (2007). The MDI interaction used is constrained by isospin diffusion data from NSCL/MSU (Tsang et al. 2004, Chen et al. 2005), which previously yielded a slope parameter $L = 88 \pm 25$ MeV and isospin-dependent incompressibility $K_{\text{asy}} = -500 \pm 50$ MeV. The results enable better understanding of the liquid-gas phase transition in neutron-rich matter and have implications for supernova explosion mechanisms and neutron star properties.
