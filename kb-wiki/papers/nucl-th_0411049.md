# Pion re-scattering operator in the S-matrix approach

> The pion re-scattering operator for pion production, derived recently in time-ordered perturbation theory, is compared with the one following from the simple S-matrix construction. We show that this construction is equivalent to the on-shell approximation introduced in previous papers. For a realistic NN interaction, the S-matrix approach, and its simplified fixed threshold-kinematics version, work well near threshold.

## Key claim

The S-matrix construction of the pion re-scattering operator is equivalent to the on-shell approximation introduced in previous papers, and for a realistic NN interaction the S-matrix approach and its simplified fixed threshold-kinematics version work well near threshold, provided the excess energy does not exceed approximately 30 MeV.

## Method

The authors compare the pion re-scattering operator derived from time-ordered perturbation theory with the operator obtained from the S-matrix technique, using both a simple sigma-exchange potential and the realistic Bonn B NN potential. They evaluate the amplitudes and cross sections for the NN → (NN)π transition in the partial wave channel 3P0 → (1S0)s0, testing the S-matrix prescription, fixed threshold-kinematics, and static approximations against the full time-ordered reference result.

## Key numbers

- The fixed threshold-kinematics version of the re-scattering operator works well for small excess energy Q but deviates rapidly as Q increases; the static approximation overestimates the amplitude significantly.
- For the realistic Bonn B potential, the difference between the S-matrix approach and its fixed threshold-kinematics version is not very important near threshold provided the excess energy does not exceed approximately 30 MeV (Q/mπ ∼ 0.2).
- The first term of the Taylor expansion of the effective pion propagator (the Klein-Gordon form) gives corrections that are negligible only for low momentum transfer, specifically in the range qk < 100 MeV.
- Approximations applied only to the pion propagator Gπ and the vertex f(ωπ) but not to the kinematical factors overestimate the cross section by a factor of 5.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Malafaia, Adam, and Pena (2004) on the irreducible pion re-scattering operator for the reaction pp → ppπ0, where different approximations yielded rather different results. The paper shows that the S-matrix approach, previously used successfully below pion production threshold, also gives results close to the full time-ordered perturbation theory above threshold. The findings explain why the calculation of S. (2002) artificially enhanced the isoscalar re-scattering term, and support the choices made in PRS, ASP, and PCK for different production operators. The investigation of charged pion production channels with the isovector Weinberg-Tomozawa term is stated to be in progress.
