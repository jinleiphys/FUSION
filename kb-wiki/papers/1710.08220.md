# Chiral interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order and nuclear saturation

> We present an efficient Monte Carlo framework for perturbative calculations of infinite nuclear matter based on chiral two-, three-, and four-nucleon interactions. The method enables the incorporation of all many-body contributions in a straightforward and transparent way, and makes it possible to extract systematic uncertainty estimates by performing order-by-order calculations in the chiral expansion as well as the many-body expansion. The versatility of this new framework is demonstrated by applying it to chiral low-momentum interactions, exhibiting a very good many-body convergence up to fourth order. Following these benchmarks, we explore new chiral interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO). Remarkably, simultaneous fits to the triton and to saturation properties can be achieved, while all three-nucleon low-energy couplings remain natural. The theoretical uncertainties of nuclear matter are significantly reduced when going from next-to-next-to-leading order to N$^3$LO.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that chiral nuclear interactions up to N$^3$LO can simultaneously fit the triton binding energy and nuclear saturation properties while keeping all three-nucleon low-energy couplings natural, but this success is conditional on using interactions with cutoffs of 450 or 500 MeV and is achieved only for specific combinations of the three-nucleon couplings \(c_D\) and \(c_E\).

## Method
The authors developed a Monte Carlo framework for many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) calculations of infinite nuclear matter, working directly in a single-particle product basis without partial-wave decompositions. They computed the energy per particle in symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter up to fourth order in MBPT, including contributions from chiral two-, three-, and four-nucleon interactions. The three-nucleon couplings \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) were first constrained by fitting to the triton binding energy, then nuclear matter calculations were performed for the resulting range of couplings to identify combinations that reproduce empirical saturation properties.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density empirical region: \(n_0 = 0.164 \pm 0.007\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and energy per particle \(E/A = -15.86 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- For the Hebeler+ interaction with \(\lambda/\Lambda_\text{3N} = 1.8/2.0\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) at \(n_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): second-order NN-only contribution \(-2.30\ \mathrm{MeV}\), NN+3N contribution \(-2.54\ \mathrm{MeV}\), residual 3N contribution \(-0.10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), third-order NN+3N contribution \(-0.10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), fourth-order NN-only contribution \(-0.20\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- For the N\(^3\)LO interaction with \(\Lambda = 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(c_D = +0.25\) at \(n_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): second-order NN-only contribution \(-8.84\ \mathrm{MeV}\), NN+3N contribution \(-14.52\ \mathrm{MeV}\), residual 3N contribution \(-0.32\ \mathrm{MeV}\), third-order NN+3N contribution \(-2.28\ \mathrm{MeV}\), fourth-order NN-only contribution \(+0.61\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Four-nucleon Hartree-Fock energies at saturation density are approximately \(-(150-200)\ \mathrm{keV}\) in both neutron matter and symmetric matter
- Most diagrams up to fourth order can be evaluated within about 10 minutes to a precision of \(\lesssim 10\ \mathrm{keV}\)

## Context
This work extends previous many-body perturbation theory calculations of nuclear matter that had only been applied up to third order (Cora14nmat, Holt16eos3pt) and addresses the challenge of incorporating higher-order contributions from chiral three-nucleon forces beyond the normal-ordered two-body approximation. The new Monte Carlo framework enables systematic uncertainty estimates by performing order-by-order calculations in both the chiral expansion and the many-body expansion. The results enable future fitting of nuclear interactions that incorporate nuclear matter properties directly, and the predicted equations of state can be used for astrophysical applications and for calculations of medium-mass and heavy nuclei.
