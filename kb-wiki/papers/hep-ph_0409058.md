# Quark-antiquark production from classical fields in heavy ion collisions: 1+1 dimensions

> Classical color fields produced by the small-x wave functions of colliding ultrarelativistic nuclei have been numerically computed. We set up the framework for computing the production of small-mass quark-antiquark pairs in these color fields by numerically integrating the Dirac equation. This computation is essential for understanding the conversion of the initial gluonic state to chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma. To illustrate and overcome technical difficulties associated with the longitudinal dimension, we first consider numerically the case of one time + one longitudinal space dimension.

## Key claim

The authors set up a framework for computing quark-antiquark pair production from classical color fields in heavy ion collisions by numerically integrating the Dirac equation, but they only present numerical results for a 1+1 dimensional toy model to establish the viability of the method before tackling the full 3+1 dimensional computation.

## Method

The authors start from the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the color glass condensate, where classical color fields are generated by the small-x wave functions of colliding ultrarelativistic nuclei. They formulate the Dirac equation for quarks in these external color fields, derive the initial condition at proper time τ=0 from the Abelian limit, and then numerically integrate the Dirac equation in 1+1 dimensions using an implicit discretization scheme in (τ,z) coordinates. The pair production amplitude is obtained by projecting the evolved spinor onto positive energy states at finite proper time.

## Key numbers

* The free Dirac equation in 1+1 dimensions yields an analytic amplitude of ±i/cosh(½(y−y′)) for the two branches when projecting onto positive energy states
* The numerical amplitude for the free case matches the analytical value 1/cosh(y−y′) for the imaginary part, with the real part being zero
* The initial condition at τ=0 involves exponentials of the form exp(i m e^y (|z|−z)/2) and exp(i m e^{-y} (|z|+z)/2) for the two branches

## Context

This work extends the classical field computation of gluon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Krasnitz, Lappi; 2000-2003) to include quark-antiquark pair production, which is essential for understanding chemical equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma. The approach differs from Schwinger mechanism calculations (Matsui, Mottola) by treating pair production via multiple interactions of quasi-real Fourier components of the color fields, corresponding to the two-gluon fusion mechanism g*+g*→q+qbar. The 1+1 dimensional toy model enables testing of numerical techniques for handling the longitudinal dimension before proceeding to the full 3+1 dimensional computation in future work.
