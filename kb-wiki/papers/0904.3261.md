# Effect of the $δ$-meson on the instabilities of nuclear matter under strong magnetic fields

> We study the influence of the isovector-scalar meson on the spinodal instabilities and the distillation effect in asymmetric non-homogenous nuclear matter under strong magnetic fields, of the order of $10^{18}-10^{19}$ G. Relativistic nuclear models both with constant couplings (NLW) and with density dependent parameters (DDRH) are considered. A strong magnetic field can have large effects on the instability regions giving rise to bands of instability and wider unstable regions. It is shown that for neutron rich matter the inclusion of the $δ$ meson increases the size of the instability region for NLW models and decreases it for the DDRH models. The effect of the $δ$ meson on the transition density to homogeneous $β$-equilibrium matter is discussed. The DDRH$δ$ model predicts the smallest transition pressures, about half the values obtained for NL$δ$.

# Effect of the δ-meson on the instabilities of nuclear matter under strong magnetic fields

## Key claim
The inclusion of the isovector-scalar (δ) meson increases the size of the thermodynamic instability region for neutron-rich matter in non-linear Walecka (NLW) models but decreases it for density-dependent relativistic hadronic (DDRH) models, with the effect becoming significant only under strong magnetic fields of order 10^18–10^19 G where Landau quantization modifies the spinodal structure.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic mean-field calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature under constant external magnetic fields, using four models: NLρ and NLδ (constant couplings) and TW and DDRHδ (density-dependent couplings). They computed the thermodynamic spinodal surface from the curvature matrix of the free energy density as a function of proton and neutron densities, and determined the transition density and pressure at the inner crust of a compact star by finding the crossing of the β-equilibrium equation of state with the spinodal boundary. The nucleon anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) were included in a subset of calculations.

## Key numbers
- For B=0, the transition density for NLρ is 0.067 fm⁻³ with pressure 0.255 MeV fm⁻³; for NLδ it is 0.063 fm⁻³ with pressure 0.175 MeV fm⁻³ (Table 4, no AMM).
- For B* = 10^5 (B ≈ 4.4×10^18 G), the transition pressure for NLδ is 0.896 MeV fm⁻³, while for DDRHδ it is 0.404 MeV fm⁻³, about half the value (Table 4, no AMM).
- For B* = 5×10^5 with AMM included, the transition density for DDRHδ is 0.170 fm⁻³ with pressure 4.356 MeV fm⁻³, compared to NLδ at 0.143 fm⁻³ and 2.245 MeV fm⁻³ (Table 5).
- The proton fraction at the crossing for B* = 5×10^5 reaches 0.367 for NLρ and 0.422 for DDRHδ (Table 4, no AMM).
- For B* = 5×10^4 with AMM, TW predicts a ~15% increase in fractional moment of inertia of the crust, while NLW models predict increases of 120% (without δ) and 220% (with δ) (from text discussion).

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Aziz et al. (2009) on magnetic field effects on nuclear matter instabilities by adding the δ meson, which had previously been shown to soften the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities and harden it above saturation (Kubis and collaborators, 1997; Liu and collaborators). The results connect to the work of Link et al. (1999) on using glitch observations to constrain neutron star radii through the fractional moment of inertia of the crust. The findings enable more precise predictions for the inner crust structure of magnetars with interior fields up to 10^18 G, and suggest that differences between models in the predicted crustal moment of inertia could help constrain the nuclear equation of state when combined with future observations of magnetar glitches.
