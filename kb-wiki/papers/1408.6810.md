# Influence of the density of states on the odd-even staggering in the charge distribution of the emitted fragments

> The fragmentation of thermalized sources is studied using a version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model which employs state densities that take the pairing gap in the nuclear levels into account. Attention is focused on the properties of the charge distributions observed in the breakup of the source. Since the microcanonical version of the model used in this study provides the primary fragment excitation energy distribution, one may correlate the reduction of the odd-even staggering in the charge distribution with the increasing occupation of high energy states. Thus, in the frame- work of this model, such staggering tends to disappear as a function of the total excitation energy of the source, although the energy per particle may be small for large systems. We also find that, although the deexcitation of the primary fragments should, in principle, blur these odd-even effects as the fragments follow their decay chains, the consistent treatment of pairing may significantly enhance these staggering effects on the final yields. In the framework of this model, we find that odd-even effects in the charge distributions should be observed in the fragmentation of relatively light systems at very low excitation energies. Our results also suggest that the odd-even staggering may provide useful information on the nuclear state density.

# Influence of the density of states on the odd-even staggering in the charge distribution of the emitted fragments

## Key claim

Odd-even staggering in fragment charge distributions tends to disappear as the total excitation energy of the source increases, even when the energy per nucleon is small for large systems; however, the consistent treatment of pairing in the nuclear state density can significantly enhance these staggering effects in the final yields after deexcitation, contrary to the expectation that deexcitation would blur them.

## Method

The authors used a microcanonical version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) with a modified density of states that incorporates the pairing gap via the Gilbert-Cameron parameterization at low excitation energies, smoothly transitioning to the standard SMM Fermi-gas density at higher energies. The primary fragment distributions were calculated using recurrence relations for statistical weights, and the final fragment yields were obtained by applying a generalized Fermi breakup model to simulate the sequential deexcitation of primary fragments. Calculations were performed for the breakup of 40Ca and 80Zr sources at various excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca at E*/A = 1.5 MeV, the breakup temperature is T = 3.8 MeV; at E*/A = 3.5 MeV, T = 5.0 MeV.
- Odd-even effects in the primary distribution for 40Ca are barely noticeable at E*/A ≈ 2.5 MeV when using the improved state density.
- For 80Zr at E*/A = 1.5 MeV, the breakup temperature is T = 3.89 MeV, very close to T = 3.74 MeV for 40Ca at the same energy per nucleon.
- The pairing amplitude in the SMM mass formula is Δ̄ = 11.86/A^(1/2) MeV; an alternative parameterization uses Δ̄ = 34.0/A^(3/4) MeV.
- The level density parameter used for all nuclei is a = A/8.0 MeV^(-1).
- The transition energy between low-energy and high-energy regimes is defined as U_x = 2.5 + 150/A MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) developed by Bondorf (1995) and others by incorporating pairing effects into the nuclear state density, building on the approach of Souza (2013) and the generalized Fermi breakup model of Farias (2012). It addresses experimental observations of odd-even staggering reported by Ricciardi (2005) at relativistic energies and by Casini (2012, 2013) at lower bombarding energies, where staggering was attributed to late-stage evaporation. The results suggest that odd-even staggering can serve as a probe of nuclear state densities and pairing parameterizations, enabling future comparisons with experimental data to constrain these quantities.
