# Application range of perfect spin hydrodynamics

> The application range of perfect spin hydrodynamics is studied in two cases: one based on the classical spin description and the other using a quantum spin density matrix (Wigner function). Different forms of the conditions connecting the components of the spin polarization tensor, particle mass, temperature, and hydrodynamic flow are introduced, and their mutual relations are explained. The results obtained are important for practical applications of spin hydrodynamics to model heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives precise mathematical criteria that define when perfect spin hydrodynamics is internally consistent, showing that the applicability depends on the mass-to-temperature ratio and hydrodynamic flow velocity, with the classical and quantum formulations differing only by a constant factor related to the spin magnitude.

## Method

The authors analyze the convergence of scalar density integrals in two formulations of perfect spin hydrodynamics: one using classical distribution functions in an extended phase space that includes the spin four-vector, and another using a quantum Wigner function approach with a spin density matrix. They examine the asymptotic behavior of the integrands at large momentum to derive conditions that ensure the integrals converge, then maximize these conditions over all possible orientations of the momentum vector to obtain the most restrictive bounds. The analysis is performed both in the fluid rest frame and in the laboratory frame, with Lorentz transformations connecting the two.

## Key numbers

- The classical spin criterion requires: $\spin \, \sqrt{ {\bv}^2 + {\ev}^2 + 2 |\ev \times \bv|} < m/T$ (in the fluid rest frame), where $\spin = \sqrt{3/4}$
- The Wigner function criterion requires: $\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{ {\bv}^2 + {\ev}^2 + 2 |\ev \times \bv|} < m/T$ (in the fluid rest frame)
- The maximum-magnitude criterion in the laboratory frame for the classical case: $\spin \, \omega_{\rm max} (2 + \sqrt{2}) \sqrt{(1+v)/(1-v)} < m/T$
- The maximum-magnitude criterion in the laboratory frame for the Wigner case: $\frac{1}{2} \omega_{\rm max} (2 + \sqrt{2}) \sqrt{(1+v)/(1-v)} < m/T$
- The upper bound on the quantity $\sqrt{ {\bv}^2 + {\ev}^2 + 2 |\ev \times \bv|}$ is $(2+\sqrt{2})\omega_{\rm max}$, proven by analyzing the six-dimensional cube $[-\omega_{\rm max}, \omega_{\rm max}]^6$

## Context

This work extends and refines the applicability criterion first introduced in Bhadury (2025), which gave a single inequality involving $\omega_{\rm max}$, $v$, $m$, and $T$. The authors provide finer constraints that involve all components of the spin polarization tensor (the electric-like vector $\ev$ and magnetic-like vector $\bv$) rather than just their maximum component, and they clarify the relationship between the classical spin description from Florkowski (2018) and the quantum Wigner function approach from Bhadury (2025). The results enable practical implementation of spin hydrodynamics in 3D numerical codes for heavy-ion collisions, such as those recently developed by Singh (2024) and Sapna (2025), by providing clear bounds on when the formalism is internally consistent.
