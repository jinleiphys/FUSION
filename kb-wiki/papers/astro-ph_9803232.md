# Neutron Star Constraints on the H Dibaryon

> We study the influence of a possible H dibaryon condensate on the equation of state and the overall properties of neutron stars whose population otherwise contains nucleons and hyperons. In particular, we are interested in the question of whether neutron stars and their masses can be used to say anything about the existence and properties of the H dibaryon. We find that the equation of state is softened by the appearance of a dibaryon condensate and can result in a mass plateau for neutron stars. If the limiting neutron star mass is about that of the Hulse-Taylor pulsar a condensate of H dibaryons of vacuum mass 2.2 GeV and a moderately attractive potential in the medium could not be ruled out. On the other hand, if the medium potential were even moderately repulsive, the H, would not likely exist in neutron stars. If neutron stars of about 1.6 solar mass were known to exist, attractive medium effects for the H could be ruled out. Certain ranges of dibaryon mass and potential can be excluded by the mass of the Hulse-Taylor pulsar which we illustrate graphically.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the existence and properties of the H dibaryon can be constrained by neutron star masses, but only under specific conditions: if the limiting neutron star mass is about that of the Hulse-Taylor pulsar (1.44 solar masses), a condensate of H dibaryons with a vacuum mass of 2.2 GeV and a moderately attractive potential in the medium could not be ruled out, while a moderately repulsive medium potential would likely prevent the H from existing in neutron stars.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field model (extended sigma-omega-rho model) to describe baryons (nucleons and hyperons from the octet) interacting through scalar and vector mesons, with the H dibaryon coupled to the mean fields via minimal coupling. They solved the equations of motion for s-wave Bose condensation of the H dibaryon in neutron star matter, incorporating two different nuclear parameterizations (GM91 and TM1) and two hyperon coupling schemes (universal and SU(6) relations), then computed the equation of state and solved the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass sequences.

## Key numbers

- The Hulse-Taylor pulsar mass of 1.44 solar masses is used as the observational lower bound for the maximum neutron star mass.
- The H dibaryon vacuum mass considered is approximately 2.2 GeV.
- For the parameter set GM91 with SU(6) hyperon couplings, an attractive potential of U_H(rho_0) = -30 MeV is about as attractive as compatible with the Hulse-Taylor pulsar mass.
- For the parameter set TM1 with SU(6) hyperon couplings and U_H(rho_0) = -30 MeV, the maximum neutron star mass reaches a plateau at 1.44 solar masses.
- The critical density for H dibaryon condensation with a free mass of 2.2 GeV and no medium modification is about 0.36 fm^{-3} (approximately 2 times nuclear saturation density).
- For the TM1 parameter set with U_H(rho_0) = -30 MeV, the equation of state shows a plateau where pressure stays nearly constant once condensation starts.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hyperon-rich neutron star matter by Glendenning (1985) and recent investigations of H dibaryon condensation in neutron stars without hyperons by Faessler et al. (1997a, 1997b). The paper shows that including hyperons (which appear at 2-3 times nuclear saturation density) significantly alters the conclusions about H dibaryon presence compared to models with only nucleons and leptons. The results enable future observational constraints: if neutron stars of about 1.6 solar masses were known to exist, attractive medium effects for the H dibaryon could be ruled out.
