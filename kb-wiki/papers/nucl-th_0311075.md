# Algebraic Solutions of an Extended Pairing Model for Well-Deformed Nuclei

> A Nilsson mean-field plus extended pairing interaction Hamiltonian with many-pair interaction terms is proposed. Eigenvalues of the extended pairing model are easy to obtain. Our investigation shows that one- and two-body interactions continue to dominate the dynamics for relatively small values of the pairing strength. As the strength of the pairing interaction grows, however, the three- and higher many-body interaction terms grow in importance. A numerical study of even-odd mass differences in the $^{154-171}$Yb isotopes shows that the extended pairing model is applicable to well-deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a Nilsson mean-field plus extended pairing Hamiltonian, which includes many-pair interaction terms beyond the standard two-body pairing, is exactly solvable via a simple algebraic ansatz involving only a single variable, and that this model can reproduce even-odd mass differences in well-deformed nuclei. The hedge is that the model's applicability is demonstrated specifically for the Yb isotope chain, and the authors note small deviations for some isotopes.

## Method

The authors construct an extended pairing Hamiltonian that adds infinite sums of many-pair hopping terms (μ ≥ 2) to the standard Nilsson mean-field plus pairing interaction. They derive an algebraic ansatz for k-pair eigenstates, where the expansion coefficients depend on a single c-number x^(ζ) determined by a single algebraic equation, in contrast to the coupled nonlinear Bethe ansatz equations of the standard pairing model. They then apply the model to fit even-odd mass differences in ¹⁵⁴–¹⁷¹Yb isotopes using Nilsson single-particle energies with experimentally evaluated deformation parameters.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength G for ¹⁵⁴Yb (k=1 pair): 245 keV; for ¹⁷⁰Yb (k=9 pairs): 0.0948 keV.
- Pairing strength G for ¹⁵⁵Yb (k=1 pair): 270.7 keV; for ¹⁷¹Yb (k=9 pairs): 0.16113 keV.
- For the illustrative 10-level system with k=5 pairs, single-particle energies range from ε₁=1.179 to ε₁₀=10.201 (arbitrary units), with G varied from 0.01 to 0.10 (arbitrary units).
- The extended pairing model shows a sharp transition from mean-field to pairing-dominated ground states as G increases, while the standard pairing model shows a much slower transition.

## Context

This work extends the exact-solution methodology for pairing Hamiltonians beyond the standard Richardson-Bethe ansatz approach, which requires solving highly nonlinear coupled equations and is impractical for large systems like well-deformed nuclei. The authors build on earlier algebraic solutions for Heisenberg algebra Hamiltonians (Feng, 1999) and on the Bethe ansatz developments for pairing (Dukelsky, 2001; Zhou, 2002). The model enables exact treatment of pairing in deformed nuclei with many valence pairs, and may also be applicable to pairing in nano-scale metallic clusters.
