# Nuclear Forces and Few-Nucleon Studies Based on Chiral Perturbation Theory

> After a brief review on the status of few--nucleon studies based on conventional nuclear forces, we sketch the concepts of the effective field theory approach constrained by chiral symmetry and its application to nuclear forces. Then first results for few--nucleon observables are discussed.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory provides a systematic and consistent framework for deriving nuclear forces, where three-nucleon forces appear naturally at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and are less important than two-nucleon forces, but the approach is only valid for low momenta (Q much less than the chiral symmetry breaking scale) and requires regularization to remove unphysical high-momentum behavior.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian constrained by chiral symmetry, order terms by a power-counting scheme based on the parameter Δ = d + n/2 − 2 (where d is the number of derivatives and n the number of nucleon field operators), and derive nuclear forces by eliminating pion degrees of freedom via a unitary transformation. The resulting nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) forces are organized by powers of (Q/Λ), where Q is external momentum and Λ is the regularization cutoff, with contributions at leading order (LO, ν=0), next-to-leading order (NLO, ν=2), and NNLO (ν=3). Low-energy constants (LECs) are adjusted to NN S- and P-wave phase shifts and to the 3H binding energy and doublet nd scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Regularization cutoff range: 500 MeV/c ≤ Λ ≤ 600 MeV/c, chosen to preserve pion-exchange physics while excluding uncontrolled high-energy contributions
- 4He binding energy at NNLO: 29.51 MeV (Λ = 500 MeV/c) and 29.98 MeV (Λ = 600 MeV/c), compared to the experimental value of 29.8 MeV (corrected for np forces only)
- 3H binding energy from NN forces only: 7.74 MeV (Nijmegen), 8.01 MeV (CD-Bonn), 7.60 MeV (AV18), compared to experimental 8.48 MeV
- 4He binding energy from NN forces only: 24.98 MeV (Nijmegen), 26.26 MeV (CD-Bonn), 24.10 MeV (AV18), compared to experimental 28.30 MeV
- 4He binding energy with NN plus 3N forces: 28.40 MeV (CD-Bonn + TM), 28.36 MeV (AV18 + TM), 28.50 MeV (AV18 + URB), compared to experimental 28.30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier chiral perturbation theory approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1991), Ordonez (1996), and Kaiser (1997), and contrasts with conventional meson-theoretic forces such as AV18 (Wiringa, 1995), Nijmegen I/II (Stoks, 1994), and CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 1996), which underbind light nuclei when used alone. The chiral EFT framework provides a systematic explanation for the hierarchy of nuclear forces (2N forces dominate over 3N forces, which dominate over 4N forces) and enables consistent calculations of few-nucleon observables including 3H, 4He, 6Li, and 3N scattering, with ongoing work at NNNLO (ν=4) expected to introduce new parameter-free 3N forces.
