# Unitarization Technics in Hadron Physics with Historical Remarks

> We review a series of unitarization techniques that have been used during the last decades, many of them in connection with the advent and development of current algebra and later of Chiral Perturbation Theory. Several methods are discussed like the generalized effective-range expansion, K-matrix approach, Inverse Amplitude Method, Padé approximants and the N/D method. More details are given for the latter though. We also consider how to implement them in order to correct by final-state interactions. In connection with this some other methods are also introduced like the expansion of the inverse of the form factor, the Omnés solution, generalization to coupled channels and the Khuri-Treiman formalism, among others.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys unitarization techniques in hadron physics, arguing that methods such as the N/D approach, the Inverse Amplitude Method, and the Khuri-Treiman formalism are essential for enforcing unitarity in scattering amplitudes derived from chiral perturbation theory and current algebra, but the paper itself presents no new numerical results or original calculations.

## Method

The paper reviews several unitarization techniques historically used in hadron physics, including the generalized effective-range expansion, K-matrix approach, Inverse Amplitude Method, Pade approximants, and the N/D method (with the latter receiving the most detailed treatment). It also discusses methods for incorporating final-state interactions, such as the expansion of the inverse of the form factor, the Omnes solution, coupled-channel generalizations, and the Khuri-Treiman formalism.

## Key numbers

Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This review extends the methodological lineage connecting current algebra and Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) to modern unitarization schemes, building on decades of work by practitioners such as those who developed the N/D method and the Inverse Amplitude Method. It provides a pedagogical resource for researchers needing to apply unitarization to meson-meson scattering, form factors, and coupled-channel problems, and it identifies open questions regarding the optimal choice of technique for different energy regimes and the treatment of crossing symmetry.
