# Reply to the Comment on 'Pygmy dipole response of proton-rich argon nuclei in random-phase approximation and no-core shell model'

> We respond to a Comment on our recent paper (Phys.Rev.C77:024304,2008) by Paar (arXiv:0803.0274).

## Key claim

The authors defend their use of the V_UCOM interaction for proton-rich argon nuclei near the dripline, arguing that the interaction's known overestimation of giant dipole resonance (GDR) centroid energies in stable nuclei does not apply to dripline nuclei because the relevant degrees of freedom are dominated by the proximity to the continuum, leading to smaller shell gaps and a qualitatively different behavior.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations, random-phase approximation (RPA), and no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for the proton-rich isotopes 32Ar and 34Ar using the V_UCOM interaction. They compared the resulting proton separation energies and dipole strength distributions, including the low-energy pygmy dipole strength and the GDR centroid, with experimental data where available and with previous theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- The HF calculation for 32Ar yields a proton separation energy of 2.13 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.40 MeV.
- The V_UCOM interaction produces much smaller gaps between the two major shells for dripline nuclei than for stable nuclei, due to the position of the next orbits being dominated by kinetic energy.
- Second RPA (SRPA) calculations with V_UCOM noticeably lower the GDR centroid energy for stable nuclei, bringing them into better agreement with data, but SRPA calculations for dripline nuclei have not yet been performed.

## Context

This reply addresses a Comment by Paar (2008) on the authors' earlier paper (Barbieri et al., 2008, Phys.Rev.C77:024304). Paar argued that the V_UCOM interaction used by the authors is inconsistent with a previous study by Paar (2006) that showed the interaction disagrees with data on dipole strength distributions for stable nuclei. The authors clarify that their work focuses on proton-rich dripline nuclei where the continuum proximity changes the behavior, and they note that their shell model study shows inclusion of 2-particle-2-hole configurations does not alter the energies of the enhanced low-lying strength or the GDR centroid energy. The work enables future second RPA calculations for dripline nuclei to test whether the continuum affects those results.
