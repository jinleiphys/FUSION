# Empirical determination of the Bohr-Weisskopf effect in cesium and improved tests of precision atomic theory in searches for new physics

> The finite distribution of the nuclear magnetic moment across the nucleus gives a contribution to the hyperfine structure known as the Bohr-Weisskopf (BW) effect. We have obtained an empirical value of -0.24(18)% for this effect in the ground and excited s states of atomic Cs-133. This value is found from historical muonic-atom measurements in combination with our muonic-atom and atomic many-body calculations. The effect differs by 0.5% in the hyperfine structure from the value found using the uniform magnetization distribution, which has been commonly employed in the precision heavy-atom community over the last several decades. We also deduce accurate values for the BW effect in other isotopes and states of cesium. These results enable cesium atomic wave functions to be tested in the nuclear region at an unprecedented 0.2% level, and are needed for the development of precision atomic many-body methods. This is important for increasing the discovery potential of precision atomic searches for new physics, in particular for atomic parity violation in cesium.

## Key claim

The authors empirically determine the Bohr-Weisskopf (BW) effect in cesium-133 to be -0.24(18)%, resolving a 0.5% tension between nuclear magnetization models in favor of the single-particle model, but with the caveat that the uncertainty is overwhelmingly experimental and new muonic-atom or H-like ion measurements are needed to reduce it further.

## Method

The authors extract the BW effect from historical muonic-atom hyperfine measurements for Cs-133, Tl-203, Tl-205, and Bi-209 by subtracting calculated point-nucleus hyperfine constants and QED corrections from experimental values. They then translate the muonic-atom BW effect to the corresponding H-like ion using a procedure from Elizarov (2006) that employs single-particle magnetization models to account for nuclear model dependence, and finally convert the H-like result to neutral cesium using electronic screening factors from Roberts (2021).

## Key numbers

- Empirical BW effect for the 6s state of atomic Cs-133: -0.24(18)%
- The uniform magnetization distribution gives a BW contribution of -0.7% to the hyperfine structure, while the single-particle model yields -0.2%, a difference of 0.5% in the hyperfine structure constant
- Absolute BW correction for the Cs-133 ground state hyperfine constant: -5.5(4.2) MHz, yielding a total value of 2293.1 MHz (experiment is 2298.157... MHz)
- BW effects for Cs-133 p states: -0.015(12)% for np_{1/2} states and -0.065(50)% for np_{3/2} states
- BW effects for other cesium isotopes from differential anomalies: Cs-131: -0.69(19)%, Cs-134: -0.41(18)%, Cs-135: -0.27(18)%
- Muonic-atom BW effect for Cs-133: -0.134(103) keV (relative correction 18(14)%)
- H-like Cs-54+ BW effect from muonic data: -0.23(17)%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Bohr-Weisskopf effect from muonic-atom measurements by Lee (1969) and the review by Buttgenbach (1984), correcting a factor-of-10 error in the uncertainty for cesium in that review. The results contradict the common practice in the precision heavy-atom community of using a uniform magnetization distribution (as in Dzuba (1984)), showing it is inadequate for cesium and bismuth. The empirical BW values enable testing of cesium atomic wave functions in the nuclear region at the 0.2% level, which is needed for next-generation atomic many-body calculations and for improving the discovery potential of atomic parity violation searches in cesium, particularly for the ongoing experimental program at Purdue (Toh 2019).
