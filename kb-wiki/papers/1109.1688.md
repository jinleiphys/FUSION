# Noncommutative fluid dynamics in the Kähler parametrization

> In this paper, we propose a first order action functional for a large class of systems that generalize the relativistic perfect fluids in the Kähler parametrization to noncommutative spacetimes. We calculate the equations of motion for the fluid potentials and the energy-momentum tensor in the first order in the noncommutative parameter. The density current does not receive any noncommutative corrections and it is conserved under the action of the commutative generators $P_μ$ but the energy-momentum tensor is not. Therefore, we determine the set of constraints under which the energy-momentum tensor is divergenceless. Another set of constraints on the fluid potentials is obtained from the requirement of the invariance of the action under the generalization of the volume preserving transformations of the noncommutative spacetime. We show that the proposed action describes noncommutative fluid models by casting the energy-momentum tensor in the familiar fluid form and identifying the corresponding energy and momentum densities. In the commutative limit, they are identical to the corresponding quantities of the relativistic perfect fluids. The energy-momentum tensor contains a dissipative term that is due to the noncommutative spacetime and vanishes in the commutative limit. Finally, we particularize the theory to the case when the complex fluid potentials are characterized by a function $K(z,\bar{z})$ that is a deformation of the complex plane and show that this model has important common features with the commutative fluid such as infinitely many conserved currents and a conserved axial current that in the commutative case is associated to the topologically conserved linking number.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a first-order action functional that generalizes relativistic perfect fluids in the Kähler parametrization to noncommutative spacetimes, but only at first order in the noncommutative parameter \(\lambda^{\mu\nu}\); the density current receives no noncommutative corrections and remains conserved under commutative translations, while the energy-momentum tensor is not divergenceless unless specific constraints on the fluid potentials are imposed.

## Method

The authors construct an action on a noncommutative spacetime \(M_\lambda\) (defined by \([x^\mu, x^\nu] = i\lambda^{\mu\nu}\)) using the Moyal star product, with fluid potentials \(\{\theta(x), z(x), \bar{z}(x)\}\) and two arbitrary functions: a generalized Kähler potential \(K(z,\bar{z})\) and a characteristic function \(f(\sqrt{-j^\mu * j_\mu})\). They derive the Euler-Lagrange equations of motion and the energy-momentum tensor to first order in \(\lambda^{\mu\nu}\), then impose constraints from the requirement of invariance under volume-preserving transformations and from the condition that the energy-momentum tensor be divergenceless. The theory is particularized to the case \(K(z,\bar{z}) = z * \bar{z}\) and \(f = \frac{c}{2} \rho^2\).

## Key numbers

- The noncommutative spacetime is defined by \([x^\mu, x^\nu] = i\lambda^{\mu\nu}\), where \(\lambda^{\mu\nu}\) is a constant antisymmetric matrix (no numerical value given).
- The action is expanded to first order in \(\lambda^{\mu\nu}\); all results are valid only at this order.
- In the simpler model with \(K(z,\bar{z}) = z * \bar{z}\) and \(f = \frac{c}{2} \rho^2\), the equations of motion for \(\theta\), \(z\), and \(\bar{z}\) receive no noncommutative corrections (equations \(\partial_\mu j^\mu = 0\), \(j^\mu \partial_\mu \bar{z} = 0\), \(j^\mu \partial_\mu z = 0\)).
- The energy-momentum tensor contains a dissipative term proportional to \(\lambda^{\alpha\beta}\) that vanishes in the commutative limit \(\lambda^{\alpha\beta} \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the Kähler parametrization of relativistic perfect fluids developed by Nyawelo et al. (2003) to noncommutative spacetimes, building on earlier connections between noncommutative Chern-Simons theory and fluid dynamics established by Susskind (2001) and others. The paper enables the study of noncommutative corrections to relativistic fluid dynamics, including dissipative effects arising purely from spacetime noncommutativity, and provides a framework for investigating volume-preserving symmetries in noncommutative fluid models.
