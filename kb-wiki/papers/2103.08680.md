# Anti-$k_T$ jet function at next-to-next-to-leading order

> Jets constructed via clustering algorithms (e.g., anti-$k_T$, soft-drop) have been proposed for many precision measurements, such as the strong coupling $α_s$ and the nucleon intrinsic dynamics. However, the theoretical accuracy is affected by missing QCD corrections at higher orders for the jet functions in the associated factorization theorems. Their calculation is complicated by the jet clustering procedure. In this work, we propose a method to evaluate jet functions at higher orders in QCD. The calculation involves the phase space sector decomposition with suitable soft subtractions. As a concrete example, we present the quark-jet function using the anti-$k_T$ algorithm with E-scheme recombination at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of the anti-$k_T$ quark-jet function at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in QCD using E-scheme recombination, but the result is obtained in the narrow jet approximation (small jet radius $R \ll 1$) and the method relies on sector decomposition of phase space with specially designed soft subtraction terms to handle singularities.

## Method

The calculation uses sector decomposition of the three-body collinear phase space combined with Laurent expansion in the dimensional regularization parameter $\epsilon = (4-d)/2$ to isolate infrared poles. For the real-real corrections, the authors introduce subtraction terms that remove the $z_j \to 0$ singularities from the jet clustering conditions and a matrix-element subtraction term to regulate the single-soft limit, enabling numerical evaluation of the finite remainder. The real-virtual corrections are computed from one-loop corrections to the splitting kernel, and the full two-loop result is validated against the renormalization group equation predictions for the leading poles.

## Context

This work extends the NLO jet function calculations of Liu (2012) and Arratia (2020) to NNLO for the anti-$k_T$ algorithm, addressing a gap where two-loop jet function information was missing and limiting resummation accuracy to next-to-leading logarithm (NLL). The method builds on phase space sector decomposition techniques from Anastasiou (2003) and Boughezal (2015), and the results enable improved theoretical precision for jet-based measurements of the strong coupling $\alpha_s$, parton distribution functions, and nucleon tomography at the LHC, RHIC, and future Electron-Ion Collider. The approach can be extended to other jet algorithms (Cambridge-Aachen, soft-drop grooming) and to semi-inclusive jet functions as in Kang (2016) and Dai (2016).
