# A method to test the coupling strength of the linear and nonlinear contributions to higher-order flow harmonics via Event Shape Engineering

> A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model is used to study the efficacy of shape-engineered events to delineate the degree of coupling between the linear and nonlinear contributions to the higher-order flow harmonics $v_{4}$ and $v_{5}$. The study shows that the nonlinear contributions are strongly shape-dependent while the linear contributions are shape-independent, indicating little if any, coupling between the linear and nonlinear flow coefficients. The experimental verification of such patterns could be an invaluable tool for robust extraction of the linear and mode-coupled flow coefficients, especially for beam energies where the charged particle multiplicity and the event statistics precludes the use of current methods to establish the coupling strength.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that Event-Shape Engineering (ESE) can test the degree of coupling between linear and nonlinear contributions to higher-order flow harmonics \(v_{4}\) and \(v_{5}\), with the specific pattern that for no coupling, the linear contributions should be event-shape independent while the nonlinear contributions should be event-shape dependent. The study uses the AMPT model to demonstrate this pattern, but the abstract's claim of "little if any coupling" is hedged by the fact that this conclusion applies specifically to the AMPT model and requires experimental verification.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string melting to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, selecting particles with transverse momentum \(0.1 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\). Events are analyzed using multi-particle cumulant techniques with two-subevent method (separated by \(|\Delta\eta| > 0.8\)) to suppress non-flow correlations, combined with Event-Shape Engineering via selections on the magnitude of the second-order reduced flow vector \(q_{2}\) calculated from particles within \(|\eta| < 0.3\). The linear and nonlinear contributions to \(v_{4}\) and \(v_{5}\) are extracted using the relations \(v_{4}^{\rm nL} = C_{4,22}/\sqrt{\langle v_2^2 v_2^2 \rangle}\), \(v_{5}^{\rm nL} = C_{5,23}/\sqrt{\langle v_2^2 v_3^2 \rangle}\), and \(v_{n}^{\rm L} = \sqrt{(v_n)^2 - (v_n^{\rm nL})^2}\).

## Key numbers

* The two-subevent cumulant method shows agreement within 2% between AMPT with hadronic cascade on and off for \(v_{2}\) and \(v_{3}\) measurements, demonstrating non-flow suppression.
* Non-flow contributions to \(v_{2}\) and \(v_{3}\) range from approximately 5% to 15% when comparing standard and two-subevent methods.
* Non-flow contributions to the three-particle correlators \(C_{4,22}\) and \(C_{5,23}\) range from approximately 10% to 40%.
* The study uses Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with centrality classes 0-5%, 30-40%, 60-70%, and 0-40% for different analyses.
* Particle selection uses transverse momentum range \(0.1 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the standard approach using Pearson correlation coefficients for higher and lower order flow harmonics (Bhalerao 2014) which requires high statistical power and large event multiplicity, making it limited for lower beam energies. The proposed ESE method provides an alternative validation scheme that could enable robust extraction of linear and mode-coupled flow coefficients for beam energies where current methods are precluded by limited charged particle multiplicity and event statistics. The results enable more reliable extraction of the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) as a function of baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) and temperature \(T\).
