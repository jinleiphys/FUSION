# Coherent-Nuclear Pion Photoproduction and Neutron Radii

> { {\bf Background:} Knowing the difference between the neutron and proton densities of nuclei is a significant topic because of its importance for understanding neutron star structures and cooling mechanisms. The coherent-nuclear photoproduction of pions, $({\g,π^0})$, combined with elastic electron scattering, has been suggested to be a very accurate probe of density differences. {\bf Purpose:} Study the ${(\g,π^0})$ reaction mechanism so as to better access the uncertainties involved in extracting the neutron density. {\bf Methods:} Include the effects of final-state pion-nucleus charge-exchange reactions on the cross section and study the influence of the non-zero spatial extent of the proton. {\bf Results:} The effects of final-state charge-exchange increase the cross section between 6\% and 5\%, generally decreasing as the momentum transfer increases. This leads to an increase of the extracted neutron skin distance by about 50\%. The validity of the previous treatments of the proton size is confirmed. {\bf Conclusion:} The model dependence of the theoretically computed cross section increases the total systematic uncertainty (experiment plus theory) in extracting the neutron skin from the ${(\g,π^0})$ cross section by at least a factor of three. }

# Coherent-Nuclear Pion Photoproduction and Neutron Radii

## Key claim

The model dependence of the theoretically computed cross section increases the total systematic uncertainty (experiment plus theory) in extracting the neutron skin from the (gamma, pi0) cross section by at least a factor of three, and including final-state charge-exchange effects leads to a computed increase of the extracted neutron skin distance by about 50% relative to the value reported in Tarbert (2013).

## Method

The author computed the coherent photoproduction cross section for pi0 mesons on 208Pb using a plane-wave approximation for photon energies between 180 and 190 MeV, including both the standard one-body impulse approximation and two-body diagrams representing charged pion production followed by final-state pion-nucleon charge-exchange reactions via s-wave and p-wave interactions. The nuclear density matrices were evaluated using a local density approximation following Negele and Vautherin (1975), and the effect of the non-zero spatial extent of the proton was re-assessed by comparing the exact point-proton form factor using the dipole parametrization with the approximate form used in prior work.

## Key numbers

- The effects of final-state charge-exchange increase the cross section between 6% and 5%, generally decreasing as the momentum transfer increases.
- Including charge-exchange effects leads to an increase of the extracted neutron skin distance from 0.143 fm to 0.229 fm, an increase of about 50%.
- The neutron skin from the one-body mechanism alone, using the density parameters of Tarbert (2013) with c_n = 6.70 fm and a_n = 0.55 fm, gives a skin of 0.143 fm.
- Using a_n = 0.61 fm with c_n = 6.70 fm to account for charge-exchange effects gives R_n = 5.79 fm and a neutron skin of 0.229 fm.
- The proton rms radius used was 0.84 fm, corresponding to Lambda = 3.93 fm^{-1} in the dipole parametrization.
- The momentum transfer range relevant to the experiment is between 0.3 and 0.9 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Tarbert (2013), which established coherent photoproduction of pi0 mesons from 208Pb as a probe of the neutron skin and reported a skin thickness of 0.15 +/- 0.03 (stat.) +0.02/-0.03 (sys.) fm. The present study identifies systematic uncertainties in the reaction theory not included in that analysis, specifically final-state charge-exchange effects and the treatment of proton finite size. The author notes that the pion-nucleus optical potential used by Drechsel (1999) was constrained only by nuclei with equal numbers of neutrons and protons and was not tested against pion-Pb elastic scattering data, and that alternate reaction theories exist such as Peters (1998). The findings suggest that the total systematic error was underestimated by at least a factor of three, and that the neutron skin result could be written as 0.23 +/- 0.03 (stat.) +0.02/-0.03 (sys.) +/- 0.07 (th. sys.) fm.
