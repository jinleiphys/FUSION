# The U(5)-O(6) transition in the Interacting Boson Model and the E(5) critical point symmetry

> The relation of the recently proposed E(5) critical point symmetry with the interacting boson model is investigated. The large-N limit of the interacting boson model at the critical point in the transition from U(5) to O(6) is obtained by solving the Richardson equations. It is shown explicitly that this algebraic calculation leads to the same results as the solution of the Bohr differential equation with a $β^4$ potential.

## Key claim

The large-\(N\) limit of the Interacting Boson Model at the critical point in the U(5)–O(6) transition corresponds to a \(\beta^4\) potential in the geometrical Bohr Hamiltonian, not to the infinite-square-well potential that defines the E(5) critical point symmetry originally proposed by Iachello (2001). The results from the two potentials are similar but not close.

## Method

The authors derived the general energy surface of the IBM Hamiltonian (up to two-body terms) for the U(5)–O(6) transition and imposed the critical-point condition \(d^2E/d\beta^2|_{\beta=0}=0\) to obtain the critical energy surface, whose leading term is \(\beta^4\). They then solved the Bohr differential equation numerically for a \(\beta^4\) potential and computed energy levels and B(E2) ratios. To determine the large-\(N\) limit of the IBM, they solved Richardson’s equations exactly for two schematic IBM Hamiltonians (\(H_I\) and \(H_{II}\)) at the critical point for boson numbers up to \(N=1000\) (energy ratios) and \(N=40\) (B(E2) ratios).

## Context

This work extends the study of critical point symmetries in nuclear structure, specifically the E(5) symmetry proposed by Iachello (2001) for the Bohr Hamiltonian with an infinite square well in \(\beta\). The authors show that the large-\(N\) limit of the IBM at the U(5)–O(6) critical point yields a \(\beta^4\) potential rather than the infinite square well, contradicting the assumption that E(5) is the natural IBM critical-point symmetry. The results enable future IBM calculations with finite-\(N\) corrections, as demonstrated for Ru isotopes by Arias et al. (2003), to robustly identify critical nuclei.
