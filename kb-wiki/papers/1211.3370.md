# Hydrodynamic noise and Bjorken expansion

> Using the Bjorken expansion model we study the effect of intrinsic hydrodynamic noise on the correlations observed in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using the Bjorken expansion model, the authors show that intrinsic hydrodynamic noise (local thermal fluctuations) generates correlations between particles produced in heavy-ion collisions, and that these correlations are distinct from those arising from initial-state geometry fluctuations. The magnitude of the noise-induced long-range correlations is proportional to viscosity, which could potentially be used to measure or constrain this transport coefficient.

## Method

The authors linearize the relativistic stochastic hydrodynamic equations around the Bjorken expansion solution, treating the energy density and four-velocity fluctuations as small perturbations. They compute the two-point correlation function of entropy density fluctuations by integrating the noise source (with magnitude set by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem) over all Bjorken time slices, propagated via linear response Green's functions. Finally, they translate the hydrodynamic fluctuations into observable particle-number fluctuations using the Cooper-Frye freezeout formalism.

## Key numbers

- The singular contribution to the correlation appears at rapidity separation $\Delta\xi = 2v_s \ln(\tau_f/\tau)$, where $v_s^2 = 1/3$ and $\ln(\tau_f/\tau) = 4$ is used in the example plot.
- The width of the smearing of the sound-front singularity due to viscosity follows $\sigma^2 = (\nu/\alpha)(1/(\tau T) - 1/(\tau_f T_f))$, where $\alpha \equiv (1-v_s^2)/2$.
- The final particle correlation formula contains the factor $(45 d_s)/(4\pi^4 N_{\rm eff}(T_0)) \cdot (\nu/(T_f \tau_f)) \cdot (T_0^2/T_f^2)^{v_s^{-2}-2}$, where $d_s = 2$ for charged pions and $T_f = 150$ MeV is used in the example plot.
- The slowest mode dispersion relation is $\omega_- \sim -ik^2$ for $k \to 0$, with $\alpha \equiv (1-v_s^2)/2$ appearing in the dispersion $\omega = i\alpha \pm \sqrt{v_s^2 k^2 - \alpha^2}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Kapusta 2011) on hydrodynamic noise in heavy-ion collisions by applying it to the Bjorken expansion model. It contrasts with the understanding (Alver 2010) that long-range rapidity-independent correlations arise solely from fluctuations of the initial collision geometry propagated hydrodynamically. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on the $\Delta\eta$ and $\Delta\phi$ dependence of fluctuations, and could be extended to three-dimensional event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations (Schenke 2010) for quantitative determination of viscosity.
