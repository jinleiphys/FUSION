# Construction of energy-independent potentials above inelastic thresholds in quantum field theories

> We construct energy independent but non-local potentials above inelastic thresholds, in terms of Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions defined in quantum field theories such as QCD. As an explicit example, we consider NN --> NN + n pi scattering processes for n=0,1,2,.... We show an existence of energy-independent coupled channel potentials with a non-relativistic approximation, where momenta of all particles are small compared with their own masses. In the case of two-body inelastic scatterings such as Lambda Lambda --> Lambda Lambda, N Xi, Sigma Sigma, on the other hand, we show that energy-independent potentials can be constructed without relying on non-relativistic approximations. We also propose a method to extract these potentials using time-dependence of general correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper proves that energy-independent, non-local potentials can be constructed above inelastic thresholds in quantum field theories, but this construction requires either a non-relativistic approximation (momenta of all particles small compared to their masses) for multi-particle final states, or restriction to two-body inelastic scattering channels where no such approximation is needed.

## Method

The authors define equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for coupled channels (e.g., NN and NN+π) and construct a norm kernel from these wave functions. Using the inverse of this norm kernel, they build dual basis vectors and define an energy-independent non-local potential matrix U that satisfies coupled-channel Schrödinger equations for all energies below a chosen maximum threshold. For the time-dependent extraction method, they generalize correlation functions to include multiple pion fields and derive a formula using the inverse of the correlation matrix to extract U directly.

## Key numbers

- The n-th inelastic threshold for NN scattering with pion production is given by W_th^n = 2 m_N + n × m_π, where m_N is the nucleon mass and m_π is the pion mass.
- The non-relativistic approximation allows treatment of up to NN + 6π with roughly 5% relativistic corrections, derived from the condition n-1 ≤ (m_N/m_π)(√7.6 - 2) ≈ 5.
- For the special case of two-body inelastic scatterings (e.g., ΛΛ → ΛΛ, NΞ, ΣΣ), energy-independent potentials can be constructed without non-relativistic approximations for total energy W satisfying 2 m_Σ ≤ W < 2 m_Σ + m_π.

## Context

This work extends the HALQCD method, previously established for elastic scattering below inelastic thresholds (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2010), to energies above particle production thresholds. It provides the theoretical foundation for extracting coupled-channel potentials from lattice QCD simulations, enabling studies of inelastic processes such as NN → NNπ and baryon-baryon interactions with strangeness (e.g., ΛΛ, NΞ, ΣΣ channels). The time-dependent extraction method generalizes the approach of the HALQCD collaboration (2012) to handle multi-particle final states.
