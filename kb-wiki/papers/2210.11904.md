# Chiral extrapolation of hadronic vacuum polarization and isospin-breaking corrections

> By far the biggest contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) arises from the two-pion channel. Its quark-mass dependence can be evaluated by combining dispersion relations with chiral perturbation theory, providing guidance on the functional form of chiral extrapolations, or even interpolations around the physical point. In addition, the approach allows one to estimate in a controlled way the isospin-breaking (IB) corrections that arise from the pion mass difference. As an application, we present an updated estimate of phenomenological expectations for electromagnetic and strong IB corrections to the HVP contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. In particular, we include IB effects in the $\bar K K$ channel, which are enhanced due to the proximity of the $\bar K K$ threshold and the $ϕ$ resonance. The resulting estimates make it unlikely that the current tension between lattice-QCD and data-driven evaluations of the HVP contribution is caused by IB corrections.

# Chiral extrapolation of hadronic vacuum polarization and isospin-breaking corrections

## Key claim
Isospin-breaking (IB) corrections to the hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment are unlikely to explain the current tension between lattice-QCD and data-driven evaluations, even after including resonance-enhanced IB effects in the $\bar K K$ channel that had not been previously accounted for.

## Method
The authors combine dispersion relations with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to evaluate the quark-mass dependence of the two-pion HVP contribution, using the inverse amplitude method (IAM) at one- and two-loop order for the $\pi\pi$ phase shift. For the $\bar K K$ channel, they analyze the isoscalar form factor in terms of $\phi$ resonance parameters, decomposing kaon masses into strong and electromagnetic components using the Cottingham formula. The IB corrections are separated into $\mathcal{O}(e^2)$ (electromagnetic) and $\mathcal{O}(\delta)$ (strong, proportional to $m_u-m_d$) contributions and decomposed into short-distance, intermediate, and long-distance Euclidean windows.

## Key numbers
- Pion-mass difference effect on $a_\mu^\text{HVP}[\pi\pi]$: $-7.67(22) \times 10^{-10}$ (total uncertainty, combining ChPT convergence, polynomial choice, charge radius, and low-energy constant $r_{V1}^r$)
- FSR contribution to $2\pi$ channel: $4.24(2) \times 10^{-10}$ (Born) and $0.18(4) \times 10^{-10}$ (non-Born)
- $\rho$–$\omega$ mixing contribution: $3.68(17) \times 10^{-10}$
- $\phi$ resonance residues: $c_\phi^{K^+K^-} = 0.977(6)$, $c_\phi^{\bar K^0 K^0} = 1.001(6)$
- Kaon mass decomposition: $M_{K^\pm} = (494.58 - 3.05_\delta + 2.14_{e^2})\,\text{MeV}$, $M_{K^0} = (494.58 + 3.03_\delta)\,\text{MeV}$
- Total $\mathcal{O}(e^2)$ IB correction to full HVP: $-0.68(29) \times 10^{-10}$
- Total $\mathcal{O}(\delta)$ IB correction to full HVP: $4.04(39) \times 10^{-10}$

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological IB estimates from Borsanyi (2020) and Jegerlehner (2017) by improving the treatment of the pion-mass difference in the $2\pi$ channel using dispersion relations and ChPT from Colangelo (2018, 2021, 2022). It adds new results for the $\bar K K$ channel from Stamen (2022), where IB effects are enhanced by the proximity of the $\bar K K$ threshold and the $\phi$ resonance. The results enable lattice-QCD practitioners to perform more accurate chiral extrapolations and to test whether IB corrections could resolve the $4.2\sigma$ discrepancy between the experimental muon $g-2$ and Standard Model predictions.
