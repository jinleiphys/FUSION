# Reduced regulator dependence of neutron-matter predictions with chiral interactions

> We calculate the energy per particle in infinite neutron matter perturbatively using chiral N3LO two-body potentials plus N2LO three-body forces. The cutoff dependence of the predictions is investigated by employing chiral interactions with different regulators. We find that the inclusion of three-nucleon forces, which are consistent with the applied two-nucleon interaction, leads to a strongly reduced regulator dependence of the results.

## Key claim

The inclusion of chiral N²LO three-nucleon forces that are consistent with the applied N³LO two-nucleon interaction (sharing the same low-energy constants and cutoff parameters) strongly reduces the regulator dependence of the neutron-matter energy per particle, whereas calculations using only two-nucleon forces show considerable dependence on the choice of regulator and cutoff.

## Method

The authors calculate the ground-state energy per particle of infinite neutron matter using many-body perturbation theory up to third order in the Goldstone expansion, employing three different chiral N³LO two-nucleon potentials with cutoff parameters of 414 MeV (sharp cutoff), 450 MeV (Gaussian regulator, n=3), and 500 MeV (Gaussian regulator, n=2). A density-dependent two-body potential derived from the N²LO three-nucleon force is added to each two-nucleon potential, using exactly the same low-energy constants c₁ and c₃ and the same cutoff parameters as in the corresponding two-nucleon potential; the V_D and V_E terms of the three-nucleon force vanish in pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- The three cutoff parameters used are Λ = 414 MeV (sharp cutoff), Λ = 450 MeV (Gaussian, n=3), and Λ = 500 MeV (Gaussian, n=2).
- The low-energy constants for the dimension-two πN Lagrangian are c₁ = -0.81 GeV⁻¹ for all three potentials; c₃ = -3.00 GeV⁻¹ (414 MeV), -3.40 GeV⁻¹ (450 MeV), and -3.20 GeV⁻¹ (500 MeV).
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is taken as Λ_χ = 700 MeV; the pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV; the pion mass m_π = 138.04 MeV; the axial coupling g_A = 1.29.
- The N³LO potential with Λ = 600 MeV was found to have unsatisfactory perturbative behavior and was not used.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Hebeler and Schwenk (2010) and Tews et al. (2012), who found uncomfortably large uncertainties in neutron-matter predictions using chiral forces; the authors attribute those uncertainties to the use of a range of values for c₁ and c₃ from πN scattering rather than values uniquely fixed in peripheral NN partial waves. The results demonstrate that consistent treatment of two- and three-nucleon forces can provide model-independent many-body predictions, and the authors note that future work should investigate regulator dependence in symmetric nuclear matter, where the V_D and V_E terms of the three-nucleon force also contribute.
