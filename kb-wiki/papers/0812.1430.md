# Quasi-particle model for lattice QCD: quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions

> We propose a quasi-particle model to describe the lattice QCD equation of state for pure SU(3) gauge theory in its deconfined state, for $T \ge 1.5T_c$. The method involves mapping the interaction part of the equation of state to an effective fugacity of otherwise non-interacting quasi-gluons. We find that this mapping is exact. Using the quasi-gluon distribution function, we determine the energy density and the modified dispersion relation for the single particle energy, in which the trace anomaly is manifest. As an application, we first determine the Debye mass, and then the important transport parameters, {\it viz}, the shear viscosity, $η$ and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/{\mathcal S}$. We find that both $η$ and $η/{\mathcal S}$ are sensitive to the interactions, and that the interactions significantly lower both $η$ and $η/\mathcal S$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a quasi-particle model that exactly maps the lattice QCD equation of state for pure SU(3) gauge theory to a system of non-interacting quasi-gluons with an effective fugacity, but this exact mapping is demonstrated only for temperatures T ≥ 1.5T_c, with deviations from lattice results being of order 10^{-6}.

## Method

The authors extract an effective fugacity z_g from the lattice QCD equation of state by expanding the grand canonical partition function around the ideal gas limit (z_g=1) to second order in δz = z_g-1 and matching the resulting pressure expression to the lattice pressure. Using this fugacity, they determine the quasi-gluon distribution function, the modified dispersion relation E_p = p + T^2 ∂_T ln(z_g) that incorporates the trace anomaly, and the number density. They then employ the non-abelian Vlasov transport equation with the quasi-gluon distribution to compute the Debye mass, shear viscosity η, and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/S.

## Key numbers

* The quasi-particle model reproduces lattice results with deviations of O(10^{-6}) for T ≥ 1.5T_c
* The Debye mass is matched to the lattice parametrized expression M_D^L = (1.40) g(T) T
* The dissociation temperature for J/Ψ is T_d/T_c = 1.24, for Υ is T_d/T_c = 2.56, and for χ_c and Ψ' is T_d/T_c = 1.00
* The ratio η/S may violate the AdS/CFT bound of 1/(4π) marginally for T ≤ 1.5T_c
* Interactions from the lattice equation of state decrease η/S by approximately 35% near 1.5T_c and approximately 5% near 3T_c relative to the ideal gas value
* The gluon quenching parameter at T_0 ∼ (337 ± 10) MeV is estimated to be in the range 1-2 GeV^2/fm from twist expansion approaches

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle approach previously introduced by the same authors (Chandra and Ravishankar, earlier works) for perturbative QCD equations of state to the non-perturbative lattice QCD regime. It contrasts with earlier effective mass descriptions by Peshier et al. and others, showing that the effective mass picture emerges only as a limiting case of the more general effective fugacity framework. The determination of transport parameters enables comparison with experimental observations from RHIC suggesting the quark-gluon plasma behaves as a near-perfect fluid, and the results differ from predictions by Arnold et al. and Asakawa et al. who assumed ideal gas equilibrium distributions. The work enables future extension to full QCD with quarks and to studies of bulk viscosity.
