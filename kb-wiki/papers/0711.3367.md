# The high density equation of state: constraints from accelerators and astrophysics

> The nuclear equation of state (EoS) at high densities and/or extreme isospin is one of the longstanding problems of nuclear physics. In the last years substantial progress has been made to constrain the EoS both, from the astrophysical side and from accelerator based experiments. Heavy ion experiments support a soft EoS at moderate densities while the possible existence of high mass neutron star observations favors a stiff EoS. Ab initio calculations for the nuclear many-body problem make predictions for the density and isospin dependence of the EoS far away from the saturation point. Both, the constraints from astrophysics and accelerator based experiments are shown to be in agreement with the predictions from many-body theory.

## Key claim

Heavy ion experiments support a soft equation of state (EoS) at moderate densities (up to 2-3 times saturation density), while the possible existence of high-mass neutron star observations favors a stiff EoS at high densities; these constraints are complementary rather than contradictory, and both are shown to be in agreement with predictions from many-body theory, provided the maximum neutron star mass remains below about 2.3 solar masses.

## Method

The paper reviews constraints on the nuclear equation of state from two complementary sources: subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions (using RQMD and IQMD transport calculations compared to KaoS Collaboration data) and neutron star mass-radius measurements from astrophysical observations (solving the TOV equations for various hadronic EoS models). These are compared to predictions from ab initio many-body calculations, including non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) with three-body forces, relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF), and variational calculations.

## Key numbers

- Heavy ion data favor a soft EoS with compression modulus K=200 MeV over a hard EoS with K=380 MeV, based on subthreshold kaon production in Au+Au and C+C reactions at bombarding energies between 0.1 and 2 AGeV
- The isolated neutron star RX J1856.5-3754 has a radius R > 12 km (Trumper 2003)
- The compact object in LMXB 4U 1636-536 has mass M=2.0 ± 0.1 solar masses (Barret 2005)
- The LMXB EXO 0748-676 has mass M ≥ 2.10 ± 0.28 solar masses and radius R ≥ 13.8 ± 0.18 km (Ozel 2006)
- The millisecond pulsar PSR J1748-2021B has mass M=2.74 ± 0.21 solar masses at 1 sigma confidence (Freire 2007)
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to E_sym ≈ 31 MeV at ρ₀
- Microscopic many-body calculations (BHF, DBHF, variational) yield maximum neutron star masses between 2.1 and 2.3 solar masses
- The Direct Urca process should not occur below M_DU ≥ 1.5 solar masses for typical neutron stars

## Context

This work extends the longstanding effort to constrain the nuclear equation of state, which began with the first relativistic heavy ion beams. It reconciles the apparent tension between heavy-ion results favoring a soft EoS (from the KaoS Collaboration's subthreshold kaon measurements and collective flow data) and astrophysical observations requiring a stiff EoS to support high-mass neutron stars. The paper shows that ab initio many-body calculations (BHF, DBHF, variational) naturally predict a soft EoS at moderate densities and a stiff EoS at high densities, consistent with both constraints. The results enable future work at facilities like FAIR/GSI, SPIRAL2/GANIL, and RIA to further constrain the isospin dependence of the EoS, particularly the symmetry energy at supra-normal densities. If a neutron star mass above 2.5 solar masses is confirmed, it would require new physical pictures beyond current many-body approaches.
