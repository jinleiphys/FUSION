# Kaons and antikaons in isospin asymmetric dense resonance matter at finite temperature

> We study the in-medium properties of kaons and antikaons in isospin asymmetric hot and dense resonance matter within the chiral SU(3) hadronic mean field model. Along with nucleons and hyperons, the interactions of $K$ and $\bar K$ mesons with all decuplet baryons ($Δ^{++,+,0,-}, Σ^{*\pm,0},Ξ^{*0,-}, Ω^{-}$) are explicitly considered in the dispersion relations. The properties of mesons in the chiral SU(3) model are modified at finite density and temperature of asymmetric resonance matter through the exchange of scalar fields $σ, ζ$ and $δ$ and the vector fields $ω, ρ$ and $ϕ$. The presence of resonance baryons in the medium at finite temperature is observed to modify significantly the effective masses of $K$ and $\bar{K}$ mesons. We also calculated the optical potentials of kaons and antikaons as a function of momentum in resonance matter. The present study of in-medium masses and optical potentials of kaons and antikaons will be important for understanding the experimental observables from the heavy-ion collision experiments where hot and dense matter may be produced. Our results indicate that when resonance baryons are present within the medium at finite baryonic density, the mass reduction of kaons and antikaons becomes more pronounced as the temperature of the medium increases from zero to 100 and 150 MeV. The study of the optical potentials of kaons and antikaons reveals a stronger correlation with strangeness fraction compared to isospin asymmetry.

## Key claim

The in-medium mass reduction of kaons and antikaons becomes more pronounced when resonance baryons (decuplet baryons) are present in the medium, and this effect increases as the temperature rises from zero to 100 and 150 MeV, with the optical potentials showing a stronger correlation with strangeness fraction than with isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The authors employed the chiral SU(3) hadronic mean field model to compute the in-medium effective masses and optical potentials of kaons (K+, K0) and antikaons (K-, anti-K0) in isospin asymmetric dense matter. The medium included both spin-1/2 octet baryons (nucleons and hyperons) and spin-3/2 decuplet baryons (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega-), with meson properties modified through scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta) and vector fields (omega, rho, phi) at finite density and temperature. The coupled equations of motion for the scalar and vector fields were solved self-consistently using the thermodynamic potential under the mean-field approximation, with baryon scalar and vector densities computed via Fermi-Dirac distributions.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum values used for scalar fields were sigma_0 = 93.3 MeV and zeta_0 = 106.7 MeV, with pion decay constant f_pi = 93.3 MeV and kaon decay constant f_K = 122 MeV
- The effective nucleon mass at saturation density was set to approximately 0.65 times the free nucleon mass (m_N = 939 MeV)
- Nuclear saturation properties used: binding energy per baryon (E/rho_B - m_N) = -16 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 32 MeV, at saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Temperature values studied ranged from zero to 100 and 150 MeV
- Spin degeneracy factor gamma_i = 2 for spin-1/2 baryons and gamma_i = 4 for spin-3/2 baryons

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon and antikaon properties in nuclear matter (Mishra 2006, 2008) and strange matter (Kumar 2020) within the chiral SU(3) model by explicitly including all decuplet baryon resonances (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega-) in addition to the octet baryons. The study is relevant for interpreting experimental observables from heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as RHIC, FAIR, J-PARC, and NICA, and the results on antikaon properties in dense resonance matter may inform future studies of antikaon condensation in compact stars under charge-neutral and beta-equilibrated conditions.
