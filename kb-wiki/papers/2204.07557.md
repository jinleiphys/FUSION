# Neutral-Current Electroweak Physics and SMEFT Studies at the EIC

> We study the potential for precision electroweak (EW) measurements and beyond-the-Standard Model (BSM) searches using cross-section asymmetries in neutral-current (NC) deep inelastic scattering at the electron-ion collider (EIC). Our analysis uses a complete and realistic accounting of systematic errors from both theory and experiment and considers the potential of both proton and deuteron beams for a wide range of energies and luminosities. We also consider what can be learned from a possible future positron beam and a potential ten-fold luminosity upgrade of the EIC beyond its initial decade of running. We use the SM effective field theory (SMEFT) framework to parameterize BSM effects and focus on semi-leptonic four-fermion operators, whereas for our precision EW study, we determine how well the EIC can measure the weak mixing angle. New features of our study include the use of an up-to-date detector design of EIC Comprehensive Chromodynamics Experiment (ECCE) and accurate running conditions of the EIC, the simultaneous fitting of beam polarization uncertainties and Wilson coefficients to improve the sensitivity to SMEFT operators, and the inclusion of the weak mixing angle running in our fit template. We find that the EIC can probe BSM operators at scales competitive with and in many cases exceeding LHC Drell-Yan bounds while simultaneously not suffering from degeneracies between Wilson coefficients.

## Key claim

The Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) can probe beyond-the-Standard Model (BSM) semileptonic four-fermion operators at scales competitive with and often exceeding LHC Drell-Yan bounds, while avoiding degeneracies between Wilson coefficients, provided that a complete and realistic accounting of systematic errors from both theory and experiment is included and that beam polarization uncertainties are fitted simultaneously with the Wilson coefficients.

## Method

The authors performed a projection study using cross-section asymmetries in neutral-current deep inelastic scattering at the EIC, including both parity-violating (PV) and lepton-charge (LC) asymmetries. They used the SM effective field theory (SMEFT) framework to parameterize BSM effects via seven semileptonic four-fermion operators, and generated pseudodata with an up-to-date detector design of the EIC Comprehensive Chromodynamics Experiment (ECCE) and accurate running conditions. The analysis included simultaneous fitting of beam polarization uncertainties and Wilson coefficients, and considered both proton and deuteron beams across a range of energies and luminosities, including a potential ten-fold luminosity upgrade and a possible future positron beam.

## Key numbers

- For the Wilson coefficient C_eu, 95% CL bounds become 15 to 20% weaker when the luminosity difference between electron and positron runs is treated as a fitting parameter (with correlations |rho_r| less than or equal to 0.4 between A_lum and any C_r).
- For unpolarized hadron data sets, bounds on Wilson coefficients become 30 to 50% stronger when beam polarization is fitted simultaneously; for polarized hadron asymmetries, bounds become 15 to 20% weaker.
- In six-dimensional fits of Wilson coefficients using the P4 data set (10 GeV x 275 GeV, 100 fb^-1), bounds become 25 to 40% weaker compared to one-parameter fits, and 20 to 30% weaker for two-parameter projections.
- The number of pseudoexperiments required for stable fits scales from 10^3 for 2d fits to 10^7 for 6d fits.
- The assumed absolute luminosity error is sigma_lum = 0.02.

## Context

This work extends previous EIC projections by incorporating an up-to-date ECCE detector design, simultaneous fitting of beam polarization uncertainties with Wilson coefficients, and inclusion of weak mixing angle running in the fit template. It builds on the EIC Yellow Report (Abdul Khalek et al., 2021) for nominal luminosity assumptions and adapts LHC Drell-Yan fitting results from Boughezal et al. (2020) and Boughezal et al. (2021) for comparison. The study enables future precision electroweak measurements and BSM searches at the EIC, demonstrating that the facility can probe new physics scales without the degeneracies that affect LHC Drell-Yan analyses.
