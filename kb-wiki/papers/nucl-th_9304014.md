# Nucleon polarization in deuteron electrodisintegration

> Outgoing nucleon polarization in exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration is studied at the quasi-elastic peak in the standard theory with emphasis on the effect of nucleonic and pionic relativistic corrections. The cases of polarized beam or/and polarized target are considered. Sizeable relativistic effects are pointed out in several polarization components. The sensitivity of nucleon polarization to the neutron charge form factor $G_E^n$ is discussed. In particular, it is shown that the longitudinal component of neutron polarization with vector polarized deuterons is as sensitive to $G_E^n$ as the sideways beam polarization transfer.

# Nucleon polarization in deuteron electrodisintegration

## Key claim

Outgoing nucleon polarization in exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration at the quasi-elastic peak shows sizeable relativistic corrections in several polarization components, with the longitudinal component of neutron polarization from vector polarized deuterons being as sensitive to the neutron charge form factor $G_E^n$ as the sideways beam polarization transfer, though the calculations are limited to terms of order $(v/c)^2$ in a perturbative expansion approach.

## Method

The authors computed outgoing nucleon polarization in exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration using a standard non-relativistic impulse approximation (IA) with meson exchange currents (MEC) and isobar excitation (IC) currents, then added nucleonic and pionic relativistic corrections (RC) in a perturbation expansion approach to order $(v/c)^2$. They used the Paris potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the dipole fit and Galster model for the neutron charge form factor $G_E^n$, and evaluated Coulomb, electric, and magnetic multipoles up to order $L=6$ with final state interactions (FSI), with higher multipoles calculated in plane wave (PW) approximation. The calculations were performed at the quasi-elastic peak ($x=1$) at $Q^2 = 12$ fm$^{-2}$ with an electron scattering angle of $60^\circ$, corresponding to an incident beam energy of 820 MeV and scattered electron energy of 570 MeV.

## Key numbers

- $Q^2 = 12$ fm$^{-2}$: the four-momentum transfer squared at which calculations were performed, corresponding to a relative n-p energy of about 120 MeV
- Electron scattering angle $\vartheta_e = 60^\circ$: the fixed scattering geometry used
- Incident electron beam energy of 820 MeV and scattered electron energy of 570 MeV: the kinematic conditions chosen
- Multipole expansion truncated at order $L=6$ with FSI, with higher multipoles calculated in PW approximation
- Galster model parameter $p=5.6$: used for the $G_E^n$ model in sensitivity studies

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of relativistic corrections in deuteron electrodisintegration (Mosconi and Ricci, 1992) and the earlier work by Arenhövel and collaborators (1980s-1990s) on nucleon polarization, who showed that the sideways component of beam polarization transfer $P^h_{0x}(n)$ is almost independent of NN potential models and meson exchange effects in parallel kinematics. The paper also builds on the suggestion by Arnold et al. (1980) that neutron polarization could determine $G_E^n$, and addresses the open problem of whether to use Dirac ($F_1$) or Sachs ($G_E$) form factors in the nucleon charge operator, noting that calculations including RC should be little dependent on this choice for processes dominated by nucleonic degrees of freedom. The results enable future experiments with polarized deuteron targets, which the authors argue are becoming feasible with advances in atomic beam sources and laser-driven sources for storage rings.
