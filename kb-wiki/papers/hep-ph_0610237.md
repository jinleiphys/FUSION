# Superfluidity in many fermion systems: Exact renormalisation group treatment

> The application of the exact renormalisation group to symmetric as well as asymmetric many-fermion systems with a short-range attractive force is studied. Assuming an ansatz for the effective action with effective bosons, describing pairing effects a set of approximate flow equations for the effective coupling including boson and fermionic fluctuations has been derived. The phase transition to a phase with broken symmetry is found at a critical value of the running scale. The mean-field results are recovered if boson-loop effects are omitted. The calculations with two different forms of the regulator are shown to lead to a similar results. We find that, being quite small in the case of the symmetric many-fermion system the corrections to mean field approximation becomes more important with increasing mass asymmetry.

# Superfluidity in many fermion systems: Exact renormalisation group treatment

## Key claim

The exact renormalisation group (ERG) treatment of symmetric and asymmetric many-fermion systems with short-range attraction shows that mean-field theory provides a reliable description in the unitary limit for all mass asymmetries, but corrections from boson loops become significant (approximately 40%) at large mass asymmetry (M_b > 5 M_a) when the parameter p_F a approaches 1, making the mean-field approximation inadequate in that regime.

## Method

The author applies the Average Effective Action (AEA) variant of the Exact Renormalisation Group to symmetric and asymmetric many-fermion systems with a short-range attractive force. A Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation introduces a boson field describing correlated fermion pairs, and approximate flow equations for the effective coupling are derived that include both bosonic and fermionic fluctuations. The evolution equations are solved numerically using two different regulator functions (a smoothed step-function and a sharp cutoff) for parameters relevant to neutron matter, with the phase transition to broken symmetry identified when the coefficient u_1 in the effective potential expansion reaches zero.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric neutron matter (M = 4.76 fm^{-1}, p_F = 1.37 fm^{-1}), the critical scale where the phase transition occurs is k_crit approximately 1.2 fm^{-1} with the smooth cutoff and approximately 0.7 fm^{-1} with the sharp cutoff.
- The physical gap becomes scale independent at approximately k = 0.5 fm^{-1} (smooth cutoff) and approximately k = 0.2 fm^{-1} (sharp cutoff).
- Boson loop contributions to the gap in symmetric matter are at the level of 1%.
- The numerical values of the chemical potential obtained with different regulators differ by approximately 20%.
- For asymmetric systems in the unitary limit (infinite scattering length), boson loop corrections to the gap are 2-4% in the broken phase.
- At large mass asymmetry (M_b = 10 M_a) and p_F a approximately 1, boson loop corrections become approximately 40%.
- At p_F a approximately 1, the effect of boson fluctuations becomes approximately 10% already for M_b = 5 M_a.

## Context

This work extends the ERG approach to many-fermion systems previously developed by Birse, Krippa, and others, applying it specifically to asymmetric fermion matter with two fermion species. The study recovers mean-field results when boson-loop effects are omitted, consistent with standard BCS theory. The results enable future investigations of mismatched Fermi surfaces, Sarma phases, mixed phases, and LOFF phases in asymmetric systems, as well as applications to fermionic atoms and non-dilute systems where three-body force effects may become important.
