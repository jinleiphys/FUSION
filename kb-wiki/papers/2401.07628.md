# Fusion of $^{7}$Li with $^{205}$Tl at near barrier energies

> The complete and incomplete fusion cross sections for the $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl reaction were measured at near barrier energies by online characteristic $γ$ ray detection technique. The complete fusion (CF) cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier were found to be suppressed by $\sim$ 26 \% compared to the coupled channel calculations. Reduced fusion cross sections for the present system at energies normalised to the Coulomb barrier were also found to be systematically lower than those with strongly bound projectiles forming a similar compound nucleus. The suppression observed in CF cross sections is found to be commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections. In the ICF cross sections, t capture is found to be dominant than $α$ capture at all the measured energies. The systematic study of available CF, ICF and total fusion (TF) data with $^7$Li projectile is performed.

## Key claim

Complete fusion (CF) cross sections for the $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl reaction at energies above the Coulomb barrier are suppressed by approximately 26% compared to coupled channel calculations, and this suppression is commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections; the abstract states the suppression as "$\sim$ 26 %" but the full text specifies the suppression factor as 26 $\pm$ 4% and notes that the uncertainty was estimated from uncertainties in the barrier height and CF cross section.

## Method

Complete and incomplete fusion cross sections were measured using the online characteristic $\gamma$-ray detection technique with nine Compton-suppressed HPGe Clover detectors from the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA). A $^{7}$Li beam from the BARC-TIFR Pelletron LINAC Facility was incident on a $^{205}$Tl target of 1 mg/cm$^2$ on carbon backing at beam energies from 25 to 40 MeV. Cross sections for evaporation residues following CF ($^{208,209}$Po) and ICF ($^{207,208}$Bi from $\alpha$-capture, $^{206,207}$Pb from t-capture) were extracted from $\gamma$-ray yields normalized to Rutherford scattering measured by Si monitor detectors at $\pm$ 25$^\circ$.

## Key numbers

* CF suppression factor: 26 $\pm$ 4% for $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl at above-barrier energies, determined by scaling coupled channel calculations by a factor of 0.74.
* Coulomb barrier parameters for $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl: barrier height V$_b$ = 29.4 MeV, barrier radius R$_b$ = 11.2 fm, curvature $\hbar\omega$ = 4.46 MeV (from Akyüz-Winther potential).
* Measured CF cross section at E$_{c.m.}$ = 38.6 MeV: 729 $\pm$ 67 mb; at E$_{c.m.}$ = 24.0 MeV: 0.50 $\pm$ 0.18 mb.
* Total t-capture cross section at E$_{lab}$ = 39.9 MeV: 48.0 $\pm$ 2.4 mb ($^{207}$Pb) + 276 $\pm$ 4 mb ($^{206}$Pb); total $\alpha$-capture at same energy: 16.4 $\pm$ 0.5 mb ($^{208}$Bi) + 65.6 $\pm$ 1.8 mb ($^{207}$Bi).
* From universal fusion function (UFF) reduction: CF data matched by UFF multiplied by 0.70; t-capture by 0.26; $\alpha$-capture by 0.08; total ICF by 0.34.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of CF suppression with weakly bound $^{7}$Li projectiles on medium and heavy targets, confirming the target-independent suppression factor of approximately 26% observed in prior work by Dasgupta (2004), Kundu (2016), and Wang (2014). The simultaneous measurement of CF and ICF cross sections for $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl fills a gap noted by Rout (2013), who studied triton transfer in the same system but did not measure fusion cross sections. The comparison with strongly bound projectile systems forming similar compound nuclei ($^{12}$C+$^{198}$Pt from Shrivastava (2016) and $^{18}$O+$^{192}$Os from Charity (1986)) demonstrates that CF cross sections with weakly bound projectiles are systematically lower. The comprehensive systematic of CF, ICF, and TF data with $^{7}$Li across targets including $^{124}$Sn, $^{159}$Tb, $^{197}$Au, $^{198}$Pt, and $^{209}$Bi enables future studies of breakup and cluster transfer mechanisms in fusion reactions.
