# A new mass model for nuclear astrophysics: crossing 200 keV accuracy

> By using a machine learning algorithms, we present an improved nuclear mass table with a root mean square deviation of less than $200$keV. The model is equipped with statistical error bars in order to compare with available experimental data. We use the resulting model to predict the composition of the outer crust of a neutron star. By means of simple Monte Carlo methods, we propagate the statistical uncertainties of the mass model to the equation of state of the system.

## Key claim

By augmenting the Duflo-Zucker (DZ10) mass model with a Gaussian process (GP) trained on the residuals, the authors achieve a root mean square deviation of 178 keV for measured nuclear masses, but this accuracy is conditional on the GP being effective only for extrapolations up to approximately 8–10 neutrons beyond the training set; for nuclei further away, the underlying DZ10 model dominates.

## Method

The authors take the DZ10 mass model (10 parameters) and fit a Gaussian process with a radial basis function kernel to the residuals between DZ10 predictions and experimental binding energies from the AME2016 database. The GP has three adjustable parameters (η, ρ_N, ρ_Z) plus a fixed nugget σ_n = 0.2 MeV, optimized via likelihood maximization and Markov Chain Monte-Carlo sampling. The combined model, DZ10-GP, is then used to predict the composition of the outer crust of a neutron star by minimizing the Gibbs free energy per nucleon, with statistical uncertainties propagated via Monte-Carlo sampling of 10^4 mass tables.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square deviation of DZ10-GP for measured masses: σ = 178 keV (compared to ≈0.6 MeV for DZ10 alone)
- For extrapolated masses (≈750 nuclei from indirect measurements): DZ10 RMS = 1.426 MeV, DZ10-GP RMS = 1.100 MeV
- For 74 new isotopes from AME2020: DZ10 RMS = 701 keV, DZ10-GP RMS = 299 keV
- GP correlation lengths: ρ_N = 2.67, ρ_Z = 2.00 (in units of neutron/proton number)
- Nugget fixed at σ_n = 0.2 MeV
- 61% of nuclei fall within 1σ error bars of DZ10-GP for the full nuclear chart; 54.4% for the region 20 ≤ Z ≤ 50

## Context

This work extends the approach of Neufcourt et al. (2018) by applying Gaussian process regression to the DZ10 mass model, achieving lower RMS than neural network methods such as those of Utama et al. (2016) and Pastore et al. (2020), which gave ≈350 keV. The model enables statistically quantified predictions for the outer crust composition of neutron stars, improving upon earlier work by Pearson et al. (2011) and Wolf et al. (2013) that highlighted discrepancies between mass models. The GP correction is shown to be effective for extrapolations near known data but reverts to the DZ10 baseline for very neutron-rich nuclei, leaving the transition to the inner crust model-dependent.
