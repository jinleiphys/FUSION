# Probing Saturation Effect in Heavy Meson Pair Correlation in Forward $pA$ Collisions

> Forward two-particle angular correlations in $pA$ collisions have long been recognized as a particularly sensitive observable for exploring gluon saturation effects. In the back-to-back regime, two-particle correlations receive substantial contributions from both soft-gluon radiation and saturation effects. In this work, we study heavy meson pair correlation in forward proton-nucleus collisions by incorporating a unified Sudakov resummation for heavy meson pair correlations in the Color Glass Condensate effect theory. Our results are in good agreement with the $Δϕ$ data measured by the LHCb Collaboration for $D^0 \bar D^0$ pairs in forward $pp$ and $pA$ collisions, as well as $J/ψ$ pairs from $b\bar b$ decays in forward $pp$ collisions. Furthermore, we present predictions for $D\bar D$ and $B\bar B$ correlations in the forward rapidity regions at the Large Hadron Collider. A pronounced mass-hierarchy is observed in the nuclear modification factor, $R_{pA}\big|_{m_b}<R_{pA}\big|_{m_c}$, indicating stronger sensitivity to saturation effects at small $x$. As the rapidity increases, the suppression becomes more pronounced while the mass hierarchy remains robust. This study will help us to search for the saturation signal via heavy-meson pair correlations in forward $pA$ collisions.

# Probing Saturation Effect in Heavy Meson Pair Correlation in Forward $pA$ Collisions

## Key claim

The authors claim that heavy-quark mass effects enhance the sensitivity of heavy-meson pair production to gluon saturation dynamics, resulting in a clear mass-dependent hierarchy where saturation effects become stronger for $B$-meson pairs than for $D$-meson pairs, provided the soft-gluon radiation (Sudakov) effects are properly resummed within the Color Glass Condensate framework.

## Method

The authors compute the azimuthal angle correlation of heavy meson pairs in forward proton-nucleus collisions by incorporating a unified Sudakov resummation scheme for heavy quarks within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. The cross-section is factorized into collinear parton distribution functions for the proton, gluon transverse-momentum-dependent distributions for the nucleus obtained from the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation, and fragmentation functions for heavy quarks into mesons, with the soft function encoding multi-pole soft gluon radiation effects from initial and final-state partons.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-quark masses set to $m_c = 1.2$ GeV for charm and $m_b = 4.5$ GeV for bottom
- Saturation scale for the proton: $Q_{s0}^2 = 0.16$ GeV$^2$; for the nucleus: $Q_{s0,A}^2 = 5Q_{s0,p}^2$
- Transverse area: $S^{pA}_{\perp}=1830$ mb for $p$Pb collisions, $S^{pp}_{\perp}=51$ mb for $pp$ collisions
- Non-perturbative Sudakov parameter $\mathcal{C}_{\rm NP}=1 + C_A/C_F$ with $Q^2_0=2.4$ GeV$^2$
- $b_{\max}=1.5$ GeV$^{-1}$ in the $b_{*}$ prescription
- Anomalous dimension $\gamma = 1.118$ and $\Lambda = 0.24$ GeV in the initial McLerran-Venugopalan model at $x_0=0.01$
- Nuclear modification factor hierarchy: $R_{pA}\big|_{m_b}<R_{pA}\big|_{m_c}$, indicating stronger saturation sensitivity for bottom than charm

## Context

This work extends the unified Sudakov resummation scheme for heavy meson pair correlations developed in photoproduction by Marquet et al. (2025) to proton-nucleus collisions, building on the two-particle correlation framework first calculated for light hadrons by Marquet (2007). The results agree with LHCb data for $D^0\bar{D}^0$ pairs in forward $pp$ and $p$Pb collisions (LHCb 2012, 2020) and for $J/\psi$ pairs from $b\bar{b}$ decays in forward $pp$ collisions (LHCb 2017). The predictions for $D\bar{D}$ and $B\bar{B}$ correlations in forward rapidity regions at the LHC enable future searches for saturation signals via heavy-meson pair correlations, with the mass hierarchy in the nuclear modification factor providing a robust signature that persists as rapidity increases.
