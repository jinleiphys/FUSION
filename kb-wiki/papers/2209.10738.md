# Pion superfluid phase transition under external magnetic field including inverse magnetic catalysis effect

> Pion superfluid phase transition under external magnetic field including the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect is investigated by the Pauli-Villars regularized NJL model. Based on the Goldstone's theorem, we apply the massless Goldstone boson ($π^+$ meson) to determine the onset of pion superfluid phase. The inverse magnetic catalysis effect is introduced by the magnetic field dependent coupling $G(eB)$, which is a decreasing function of magnetic field. At fixed temperature and baryon chemical potential, the critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluid phase transition including IMC effect increases as the magnetic field grows, which is similar as the case without IMC effect. This demonstrates that magnetic field disfavors the pion superfluid phase when considering or ignoring IMC effect. The critical isospin chemical potential at fixed magnetic field, temperature and baryon chemical potential is shifted to higher value by the IMC effect. Since it is more difficult to form pion superfluid with weaker coupling.

## Key claim

The critical isospin chemical potential for the pion superfluid phase transition increases with magnetic field even when the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect is included, showing that magnetic field disfavors the pion superfluid phase; however, the IMC effect shifts the critical isospin chemical potential to higher values compared to the case without IMC, because the weaker coupling makes pion condensate formation more difficult.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Pauli-Villars regularized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant \(G(eB)\), fitted to lattice QCD data on the decreasing pseudo-critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration. The onset of the pion superfluid phase is determined by the Goldstone theorem: the condition that the \(\pi^+\) meson becomes massless, \(m_{\pi^+}(eB,T,\mu_B,\mu_I)=0\), is solved via the pole of the meson propagator in the random phase approximation, including the interaction between charged mesons and the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic-field-dependent coupling \(G(eB)/G(eB=0)\) reduces by 20% at \(eB/m^2_{\pi} = 35\) (with \(m_{\pi}=134\) MeV).
* At \(eB/m^2_{\pi} = 35\), the critical isospin chemical potential \(\mu^{\pi}_I\) with IMC effect is up to 10% higher than without IMC effect.
* At \(eB/m^2_{\pi}=10\) and vanishing baryon chemical potential, the difference in critical isospin chemical potential between cases with and without IMC is \(\delta \mu^{T}_I \simeq (8\sim 11)\) MeV at fixed temperature.
* At \(eB/m^2_{\pi}=20\) and vanishing baryon chemical potential, the difference is \(\delta \mu^{T}_I \simeq (14\sim 25)\) MeV at fixed temperature.
* At \(eB/m^2_{\pi}=10\) and vanishing temperature, the difference is \(\delta \mu^{\mu_B}_I \simeq (10\sim 19)\) MeV at fixed baryon chemical potential.
* At \(eB/m^2_{\pi}=20\) and vanishing temperature, the difference is \(\delta \mu^{\mu_B}_I \simeq (14\sim 48)\) MeV at fixed baryon chemical potential.
* The model parameters are: current quark mass \(m_0=5\) MeV, coupling \(G(eB=0)=3.44\) GeV\(^{-2}\), Pauli-Villars mass \(\Lambda=1127\) MeV, fitted to vacuum chiral condensate \(-(250\ \text{MeV})^3\), pion mass \(m_\pi=134\) MeV, and pion decay constant \(f_\pi=93\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pion superfluid phase transitions under magnetic fields (Mao, 2020) by incorporating the inverse magnetic catalysis effect, which was observed in lattice QCD simulations (Lattice QCD collaborations, 2012-2016). The IMC effect is modeled via a magnetic-field-dependent coupling, following approaches used in effective models to reproduce the decreasing pseudo-critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration. The results show that the qualitative conclusion that magnetic field disfavors pion superfluidity remains unchanged, but the IMC effect quantitatively raises the critical isospin chemical potential, making the phase transition harder to achieve. This enables future comparisons with other methods of implementing IMC in NJL models and with lattice QCD results at finite isospin chemical potential and magnetic field.
