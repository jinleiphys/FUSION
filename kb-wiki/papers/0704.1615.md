# Dynamical Coupled-Channel Model of $πN$ Scattering in the W $\leq$ 2 GeV Nucleon Resonance Region

> As a first step to analyze the electromagnetic meson production reactions in the nucleon resonance region, the parameters of the hadronic interactions of a dynamical coupled-channel model, developed in {\it Physics Reports 439, 193 (2007)}, are determined by fitting the $πN$ scattering data. The channels included in the calculations are $πN$, $ηN$ and $ππN$ which has $πΔ$, $ρN$, and $σN$ resonant components. The non-resonant meson-baryon interactions of the model are derived from a set of Lagrangians by using a unitary transformation method. One or two bare excited nucleon states in each of $S$, $P$, $D$, and $F$ partial waves are included to generate the resonant amplitudes in the fits. The parameters of the model are first determined by fitting as much as possible the empirical $πN$ elastic scattering amplitudes of SAID up to 2 GeV. We then refine and confirm the resulting parameters by directly comparing the predicted differential cross section and target polarization asymmetry with the original data of the elastic $π^{\pm} p \to π^{\pm} p$ and charge-exchange $π^- p \to π^0 n$ processes. The predicted total cross sections of $πN$ reactions and $πN\to ηN$ reactions are also in good agreement with the data. Applications of the constructed model in analyzing the electromagnetic meson production data as well as the future developments are discussed.

## Key claim
The authors determine the hadronic interaction parameters of a dynamical coupled-channel model by fitting πN scattering data up to W = 2 GeV, but this is explicitly a first-stage determination that neglects the complex three-body ππN interaction term h_{ππN}, meaning the extracted bare N* parameters are preliminary and will require refinement in a second stage that also fits πN → ππN reaction data.

## Method
The authors solve coupled-channel equations for meson-baryon (MB) channels πN, ηN, πΔ, ρN, and σN, where the non-resonant interactions v_{MB,M'B'} are derived from a set of Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method. One or two bare excited nucleon states (N*) in each of S, P, D, and F partial waves are included to generate resonant amplitudes, with the bare N* parameters fitted to the empirical πN elastic scattering amplitudes from SAID up to 2 GeV. The resulting parameters are then refined and confirmed by comparing predicted differential cross sections and target polarization asymmetry with original data for elastic π±p → π±p and charge-exchange π−p → π0n processes.

## Key numbers
- The model fits πN scattering data up to invariant mass W = 2 GeV
- Cutoff parameters Λ for form factors are allowed to vary in the range 500 MeV < Λ < 2000 MeV
- Bare N* states with bare mass M⁰_{N*} above 2.5 GeV are removed from the fit
- At W = 1.7 GeV, the effect of the Z^{(E)} term on πN scattering amplitudes is shown: for example, in P33 partial wave, Re[t_{πN,πN}] changes from 0.0306 (without Z^{(E)}) to 0.0506 (with Z^{(E)}), and Im[t_{πN,πN}] changes from 0.457 to 0.510
- The predicted total cross sections of πN reactions and πN → ηN reactions agree well with data

## Context
This work extends the dynamical coupled-channel model (MSL) developed in Physics Reports 439, 193 (2007) by the same authors, and is comparable to recent πN models such as the Jülich model (Jülich-4, Jülich-5) and models by Gross, Slaus, Afnan, and others. The constructed model enables a first-stage extraction of γN → N* parameters by analyzing photo- and electro-production of single π meson, and provides a starting point for a second-stage determination that will also fit πN → ππN reaction data.
