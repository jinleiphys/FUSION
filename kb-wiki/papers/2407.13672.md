# Systematic input scheme of many-boson Hamiltonians with applications to the two-dimensional $ϕ^4$ theory

> We develop a novel, systematic input scheme for many-boson Hamiltonians in order to solve field theory problems within the light-front Hamiltonian formalism via quantum computing. We present our discussion of this input scheme based on the light-front Hamiltonian of the two-dimensional $ϕ^4$ theory. In our input scheme, we employ a set of quantum registers, where each register encodes the occupation of a distinct boson mode as binaries. We squeeze the boson operators of each mode and present the Hamiltonian in terms of unique combinations of the squeezed boson operators. We design the circuit modules for these unique combinations. Based on these circuit modules, we block encode the many-boson Hamiltonian utilizing the idea of quantum walk. For demonstration purposes, we present the spectral calculations of the Hamiltonian utilizing the hybrid quantum-classical symmetry-adapted quantum Krylov subspace diagonalization algorithm based on our input scheme, where the quantum computations are performed with the IBM Qiskit quantum simulator. The results of the hybrid calculations agree with exact results.

## Key claim

The authors develop a systematic input scheme for many-boson Hamiltonians in quantum computing that works directly with Fock states and does not require specific oracles, but the demonstration is limited to spectral calculations performed on a classical simulator (IBM Qiskit) rather than on actual quantum hardware, and the scheme is only illustrated for the two-dimensional φ⁴ theory.

## Method

The authors squeeze (scale) boson operators by the maximal occupation per mode consistent with the total longitudinal momentum K, then rewrite the Hamiltonian in terms of eight unique combinations of these squeezed boson operators. They design circuit modules for each combination that encode the scaled normalization factors as rotation angles on phase qubits and update occupation numbers using quantum adders or subtractors. Using these modules, they construct forward and backward quantum walk states to block encode the many-boson Hamiltonian, then perform spectral calculations via the hybrid quantum-classical symmetry-adapted quantum Krylov subspace diagonalization (SA-QKSD) algorithm on the IBM Qiskit quantum simulator.

## Key numbers

* The maximal occupation per mode k is Λ_k = floor(K/k), where K is the total longitudinal momentum and k is the mode index.
* The scaled normalization factors for the squeezed boson operators range in (0, 1] for nonvanishing cases, encoded via R_y gates with angles θ_{r_k} = 2 arccos(ξ_{r_k}).
* The Hamiltonian coefficients after squeezing become B'_j_0 = Λ_k/k for the one-to-one term, and B'_j_1 = (λ/4π) × (1/N²_kl N²_mn) × sqrt(Λ_k Λ_l Λ_m Λ_n / (klmn)) × δ_{m+n, k+l} for the two-to-two term.
* The coefficient scaling factor Ξ is chosen such that Ξ ≥ max_j |B'_j|, with ρ_j = |B'_j/Ξ| ≤ 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior systematic input scheme for many-fermion Hamiltonians (Du 2023, Du 2024) to the many-boson case, addressing the difficulty of multiple-occupancy normalization factors that arise from Bose-Einstein statistics. The approach builds on the light-front Hamiltonian formalism for field theory problems, following earlier work on the (φ⁴)₂ theory by Harindranath (1987) and others, and utilizes the quantum walk concept from Childs (2010) and Berry (2012). The scheme enables future quantum computations of bound-state and scattering properties in quantum field theory, particularly for studying the critical coupling of spontaneous symmetry breaking in the (φ⁴)₂ theory.
