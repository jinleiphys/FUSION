# Relativistic second-order viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory with extended relaxation-time approximation

> We use the extended relaxation time approximation for the collision kernel, which incorporates a particle-energy dependent relaxation time, to derive second-order viscous hydrodynamics from the Boltzmann equation for a system of massless particles. The resulting transport coefficients are found to be sensitive to the energy dependence of the relaxation time and have significant influence on the fluid's evolution. Using the derived hydrodynamic equations, we study the evolution of a fluid undergoing (0+1)-dimensional expansion with Bjorken symmetry and investigate the fixed point structure inherent in the equations. Further, by employing a power law parametrization to describe the energy dependence of the relaxation time, we successfully reproduce the stable free-streaming fixed point for a specific power of the energy dependence. The impact of the energy-dependent relaxation time on the processes of isotropization and thermalization of an expanding plasma is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors derive second-order viscous hydrodynamics from the Boltzmann equation using an extended relaxation-time approximation (ERTA) where the relaxation time depends on particle energy, and show that the resulting transport coefficients and fixed point structure are sensitive to this energy dependence; the stable free-streaming fixed point is reproduced only for a specific power of the energy dependence in a power-law parametrization.

## Method

The authors employ a Chapman-Enskog-like expansion to iteratively solve the Boltzmann equation with the ERTA collision kernel, which incorporates a particle-energy dependent relaxation time, for a system of massless, chargeless particles. They impose the Landau frame condition and matching condition to determine the second-order corrections to the fluid four-velocity and temperature, then derive the second-order constitutive relation for the shear stress tensor. The resulting hydrodynamic equations are applied to a (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion to study the fixed point structure and the evolution of pressure anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity coefficient: \(\eta = K_{3,2}/T = \frac{4\kappa T^3}{5\pi^2} \left[\frac{\Gamma(5+\ell)}{24}\right]\), valid for \(\ell > -5\), where \(\ell\) is the exponent in the relaxation time parametrization \(\tau_R = \tau_{\rm eq}(x) (u\cdot p/T)^\ell\)
- Second-order relaxation time: \(\tau_\pi = \frac{\kappa}{T} \left[ \frac{\Gamma(2\ell+5)}{\Gamma(\ell+5)}\right]\), valid for \(\ell > -5/2\)
- Second-order coefficient: \(\mathcal{C} = \frac{10+4\ell}{7}\)
- Hydrodynamic fixed point: \(g_* = -4/3\)
- Free-streaming fixed points: \(g_{\rm fp} = -\left(\frac{8+\mathcal{C}}{6}\right) \pm \sqrt{\frac{4\beta_\pi}{3\e} + \frac{\mathcal{C}^2}{36}}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous first-order ERTA framework (Dash, 2021) to second order, addressing the acausality issue inherent in first-order relativistic hydrodynamics in the Landau frame. The ERTA approach allows tuning of interaction characteristics via the energy dependence of the relaxation time, enabling a partial description of the transition from deconfined quark-gluon plasma at high temperatures to a weakly interacting hadron gas at lower temperatures. The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy-ion collision dynamics by providing second-order transport coefficients that depend on microscopic interaction details, in contrast to purely phenomenological parametrizations used in Bayesian analyses (Bernhard, 2015; JETSCAPE, 2020).
