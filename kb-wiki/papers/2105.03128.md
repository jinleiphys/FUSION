# Pairing and nonaxial-shape correlations in $N=150$ isotones

> Background: The $K^Ď€=2^-$ excited band emerges systematically in $N=150$ isotones raging from Pu to No with even-$Z$ numbers, and a sharp drop in energies was observed in Cf. Purpose: I attempt to uncover the microscopic mechanism for the appearance of such a low-energy $2^-$ state in $^{248}$Cf. Furthermore, I investigate the possible occurrence of the low-energy $K^Ď€=2^+$ state to elucidate the mechanism that prefers the simultaneous breaking of the reflection and axial symmetry to the breaking of the axial symmetry alone in this mass region. Method: I employ a nuclear EDF method: the Skyrme-Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation are used to describe the ground state and the transition to excited states. Results: The Skyrme-type SkM* and SLy4 functionals reproduce the fall in energy, but not the absolute value, of the $K^Ď€=2^-$ state at $Z=98$, where the proton 2qp excitation $[633]7/2 \otimes [521]3/2$ plays a decisive role for the peculiar isotonic dependence. I find interweaving roles by the pairing correlation of protons and the deformed shell closure at $Z=98$. The SkM* model predicts the $K^Ď€=2^-$ state appears lower in energy in $^{246}$Cf than in $^{248}$Cf as the Fermi level of neutrons is located in between the $[622]5/2$ and $[734]9/2$ orbitals. Except for $^{250}$Fm in the SkM* calculation, the $K^Ď€=2^+$ state is predicted to appear higher in energy than the $K^Ď€=2^-$ state because the quasi-proton $[521]1/2$ orbital is located above the $[633]7/2$ orbital. Conclusions: A systematic study of low-lying collective states in heavy actinide nuclei provides a rigorous testing ground for microscopic nuclear models. The present study shows a need for improvements in the EDFs to describe pairing correlations and shell structures in heavy nuclei, that are indispensable in predicting the heaviest nuclei.

# Pairing and nonaxial-shape correlations in $N=150$ isotones

## Key claim

The Skyrme-type SkM* and SLy4 functionals reproduce the observed drop in energy of the $K^\pi=2^-$ state at $Z=98$ in the $N=150$ isotones, but not the absolute values; the proton two-quasiparticle excitation $[633]7/2 \otimes [521]3/2$ plays a decisive role, with interweaving contributions from proton pairing correlations and the deformed shell closure at $Z=98$.

## Method

The author employed a nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) method, solving the Skyrme-Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov equation in coordinate space with cylindrical coordinates to describe the ground state, and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) to describe excited states as one-phonon excitations built on the ground state. The SkM* and SLy4 functionals were used for the normal (particle-hole) part of the EDF, with a mixed-type pairing interaction fitted to reproduce pairing gaps in $^{244}$Cm.

## Key numbers

- Calculated quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta_2$ for protons: 0.29 ($^{244}$Pu), 0.29 ($^{246}$Cm), 0.30 ($^{248}$Cf), 0.30 ($^{250}$Fm), 0.30 ($^{252}$No) using SkM*, in agreement with experimental values of 0.29 for $^{244}$Pu and 0.30 for $^{246}$Cm
- Pairing gaps for protons: 0.59 MeV (SkM*) and 0.53 MeV (SLy4) in $^{244}$Cm, with experimental values of 0.63 MeV (neutrons) and 0.57 MeV (protons)
- Excitation energy of the $K^\pi=2^-$ state in $^{248}$Cf: 0.84 MeV (SkM* with standard pairing), rising to 1.00 MeV when proton pairing strength is increased to $V_0^\pi=-320$ MeV fm$^3$
- Isoscalar intrinsic transition strengths to the $K^\pi=2^-$ state: 6.83, 6.18, 6.11, 4.74, and 4.45 (in $10^5$ fm$^6$) for $^{244}$Pu, $^{246}$Cm, $^{248}$Cf, $^{250}$Fm, and $^{252}$No, respectively
- Stiffness parameter $C_{\beta_{32}}$ for the $K^\pi=2^-$ state: 418, 402, 306, 502, and 548 MeV in $^{244}$Pu, $^{246}$Cm, $^{248}$Cf, $^{250}$Fm, and $^{252}$No, respectively
- SkM* model predicts $K^\pi=2^-$ state at 0.65 MeV in $^{246}$Cf, lower than in $^{248}$Cf, with isoscalar strength of $7.55 \times 10^5$ fm$^6$
- Energy shift due to dynamical pairing: 0.04-0.06 MeV for the $K^\pi=2^-$ state and 0.06-0.12 MeV for the $K^\pi=2^+$ state

## Context

This work extends previous studies of low-lying collective states in heavy actinide nuclei, particularly addressing the anomalous drop in energy of the $K^\pi=2^-$ state in $^{248}$Cf observed by Yates et al. (1975). Prior QRPA calculations using the Gogny-D1M functional by Rezai et al. (2018) showed only a smooth isotonic dependence, while calculations using the Nilsson potential by Robledo et al. (2008) described the anomalous behavior. The present study reveals the crucial role of proton pairing correlations and deformed shell closure at $Z=98$, and predicts that the $K^\pi=2^-$ state in $^{246}$Cf should appear even lower than in $^{248}$Cf, providing a testable prediction for future experiments. The work also demonstrates the need for improved energy-density functionals to describe pairing correlations and shell structures in heavy nuclei, which are essential for predicting the stability of superheavy nuclei.
