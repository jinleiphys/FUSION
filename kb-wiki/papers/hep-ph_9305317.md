# Natural Color Transparency in High Energy (p,pp) Reactions

> New parameter free calculations including a variety of necessary kinematic and dynamic effects show that the results of BNL $(p,2p)$ measurements are consistent with the expectations of color transparency.

## Key claim

The BNL (p,2p) measurements of color transparency, which show an oscillatory pattern of the transparency ratio T as beam momentum increases from 5 to 10 GeV/c, are consistent with the expectations of color transparency when all known kinematic and dynamic effects are included in a parameter-free calculation, contrary to earlier claims that the data could not be reproduced quantitatively.

## Method

The authors performed quantum mechanical calculations of nuclear (p,pp) cross sections using the color transparency formalism developed in their earlier work (Jennings and Miller, 1991; Jennings and Miller, 1992), which treats the expansion of the point-like configuration (PLC) using measured proton diffractive dissociation and electron deep inelastic scattering data to constrain the baryon mass distribution. They included three essential effects: proper treatment of the longitudinal momentum component (k_z) of the detected protons, integration over the transverse momentum of the struck proton, interference between PLC and non-PLC (blob-like configuration, BLC) configurations produced in proton-proton elastic scattering using the Ralston-Pire parameterization, and a realistic treatment of the baryonic components of the PLC using a continuous mass distribution rather than a single average state.

## Key numbers

- Transparency T for Al target shows an oscillatory pattern: increases as beam momentum increases from 5 to 10 GeV/c, then decreases at higher momentum
- The proton-proton scattering cross section varies approximately as s^{-10}, where s = 2M^2 + 2M E_LAB - 2 k_z P_LAB
- Integration over transverse momenta k_x, k_y was limited to about 250 MeV/c in the experiment
- Using Hartree-Fock wave functions instead of harmonic oscillator (hbar-omega = 41 MeV/A^{1/3}) gives at most a 20% increase in dsigma/dsigma^B for Glauber optical model calculations for Pb target, but negligible effect for CT calculations
- The ratio of reaction cross section to total cross section sigma_r/sigma is approximately 0.85 for the energy range of interest
- Nucleon-nucleon short range correlations give a 20% increase for C-12 but smaller effects for heavier nuclei
- The SLAC NE-18 experiment covers Q^2 between 1 and 7 GeV^2 with systematic errors of about +/- 10%
- The authors predict CT enhancements for Q^2 between 7 and 15 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of color transparency in (p,2p) reactions beyond earlier calculations by Farrar et al. (1988), Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1991), and Frankfurt, Strikman, and Zhalov (1993), who obtained only qualitative agreement with data at some energies. The authors incorporate the Ralston-Pire (1988) mechanism of PLC-BLC interference and the Brodsky-de Teramond (1988) charm threshold mechanism, along with a proper treatment of baryonic mass distributions from their own recent work (Jennings and Miller, 1992). The calculations enable predictions for the SLAC NE-18 (e,e'p) experiment and for higher-energy BNL experiments (EVA, 1991), suggesting that measurable color transparency effects should appear for Q^2 between 7 and 15 GeV^2.
