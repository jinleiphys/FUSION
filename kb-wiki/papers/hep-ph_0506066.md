# Gluons at finite temperature in Landau gauge Yang--Mills theory

> The infrared behavior of Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature provides access to the role of confinement. In this review recent results on this topic from lattice calculations and especially Dyson-Schwinger studies are discussed. These indicate persistence of a residual confinement even in the high-temperature phase. The confinement mechanism is very similar to the one in the vacuum for the chromomagnetic sector. In the chromoelectric sector screening occurs at the soft scale g^2T, although not leading to a perturbative behavior.

## Key claim

The review argues that in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory, a residual form of gluon confinement persists even in the high-temperature (deconfined) phase, with the chromomagnetic sector remaining confining via the same mechanism as in the vacuum, while the chromoelectric sector develops screening at the soft scale \(g^2T\) without becoming fully perturbative.

## Method

The paper reviews results from Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) solved self-consistently for the ghost and gluon propagators at finite temperature, using the Matsubara formalism and a truncation scheme validated against lattice and renormalization group calculations. The propagators are decomposed into transverse (chromomagnetic) and longitudinal (chromoelectric) dressing functions, and their infrared behavior is analyzed via power-law ansätze. The infinite-temperature limit is treated via dimensional reduction to a 3d Yang-Mills theory coupled to an adjoint Higgs field.

## Key numbers

* Ghost dressing function diverges in the infrared at all temperatures, satisfying the Kugo-Ojima condition \(\lim_{p^2 \to 0} p^2 D_G(p^2) \to \infty\).
* Transverse gluon propagator vanishes at zero momentum at all temperatures, satisfying the Oehme-Zwanziger condition \(\lim_{p^2 \to 0} D(p^2) = 0\).
* In the vacuum, the infrared exponent for the ghost dressing function is \(\kappa \approx 0.595\) from DSE and RG methods, compatible with lattice results suggesting \(\kappa \approx 0.53\).
* In the high-temperature phase, two infrared solutions are found for the transverse sector: \(\kappa = 1/2\) and \(\kappa \approx 0.39\); the latter is favored by thermodynamic considerations and lattice data.
* Longitudinal gluon propagator in the high-temperature phase goes to a finite value in the infrared, corresponding to a screening mass of order \(g^2T\) (not \(gT\) as in perturbative HTL calculations).
* Critical temperature from lattice calculations is \(T_c = 269 \pm 1\) MeV.
* Hard modes have effective masses of at least \(2\pi T\) and behave as massive particles with \(\kappa_h = t_h = l_h = -1\).

## Context

This review extends the understanding of gluon confinement from the vacuum (studied via DSEs by von Smekal (1997), Fischer (2002), and lattice calculations by Bowman (2004) and Oliveira (2004)) to finite temperature. It contradicts the expectation that the high-temperature phase consists of weakly interacting quasi-particles, instead supporting the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario (Zwanziger, 2003) and the Kugo-Ojima mechanism (Kugo, 1995). The results agree with lattice calculations by Cucchieri (2001, 2003) and with dimensional reduction arguments by Appelquist (1981) and Kajantie (1995). The work enables further investigation of the nature of excitations in the quark-gluon plasma and the role of quarks near the phase transition.
