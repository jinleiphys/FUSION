# Pion-Nucleus Elastic Scattering, DCX and Sub-Threshold Resonances

> In the scattering of positive pions by nuclei the double-charge-exchange (DCX) reaction creates the possibility of formation of pionic atom states in the vicinity of the threshold of this reaction. These quasi stationary states can manifest themselves as resonances in the elastic scattering cross section. The strength and shape of these resonances is strongly affected by an instability of the nucleus created in the DCX channel. It is shown that this mechanism can explain oscillation structures in the excitation function observed in scattering of low-energy positive pions from $^{12}\text{C}$.

# Pion-Nucleus Elastic Scattering, DCX and Sub-Threshold Resonances

## Key claim
Oscillation structures observed in the excitation function for low-energy positive pion elastic scattering from 12C at energies around 35 MeV can be explained by the formation of quasi-stationary pionic atom states below the threshold of the double-charge-exchange (DCX) reaction channel, provided that the decay width of the unstable 12O nucleus (approximately 0.4 MeV) is much larger than the elastic widths of the sub-threshold resonances.

## Method
The unitary scattering theory (UST) formalism was used to calculate pion-nucleus phase shifts, separating them into a potential part from multiple scattering and an absorption correction. The effect of sub-threshold resonances was incorporated by modifying the partial S-matrix with a resonance phase shift derived from Baz' theory, then approximated by a single-term Breit-Wigner formula because the 12O decay width dominates over the elastic widths. The free parameter gamma (ratio of total elastic width to nuclear decay width) was adjusted to fit the experimental excitation function data at 83 degrees, then applied to all measured angles.

## Key numbers
- The threshold energy for the DCX channel in pi+ + 12C scattering is approximately 35.45 MeV, calculated as the sum of the reaction Q-value (32.06 MeV) and the Coulomb repulsion barrier (approximately 3.43 MeV).
- The lowest resonance energy corresponds to the 1s-state in the (pi-, 12O) atom at approximately 35.26 MeV, shifted from threshold by the Coulomb binding energy of approximately -0.23 MeV.
- The width of the 12O ground state is 0.40 +/- 0.25 MeV, with the best fit using gamma = 0.1 giving a total elastic width of approximately 0.04 MeV.
- The elastic strong interaction width of the 1s-state of (pi-, 12O) is about 10^-3 MeV.
- The 2s-energy level in the (pi-, 12O) atom is separated by 0.18 MeV from the 1s level.
- For T_pi = 35 MeV and l=0, the partial cross section at resonance reaches its kinematic maximum of approximately 500 mb (4pi/k^2).
- The reaction cross section can reach approximately 300 mb at the sub-threshold resonance region.

## Context
This work extends the unitary scattering theory (UST) developed by Khankhasayev (1989) by adding the effect of sub-threshold resonances first described by Baz' (1959, 1971). It addresses oscillations in the excitation function observed by Alex (1998) in pi+ + 12C elastic scattering at energies 18-44 MeV, which the standard UST approach could not reproduce. A systematic study by Pasyuk (1997) of the pi+ + d -> p + p reaction in the same energy range showed no oscillations, ruling out a diproton resonance explanation. The mechanism enables future investigation of how DCX reaction channels can amplify pion-nucleus interactions at specific energies, potentially making positive pions effective "burners" of nuclei when their energy matches sub-threshold resonance energies.
