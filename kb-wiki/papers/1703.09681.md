# Paths to equilibrium in non-conformal collisions

> We extend our previous analysis of holographic heavy ion collisions in non-conformal theories. We provide a detailed description of our numerical code. We study collisions at different energies in gauge theories with different degrees of non-conformality. We compare four relaxation times: the hydrodynamization time (when hydrodynamics becomes applicable), the EoSization time (when the average pressure approaches its equilibrium value), the isotropization time (when the longitudinal and transverse pressures approach each other) and the condensate relaxation time (when the expectation value of a scalar operator approaches its equilibrium value). We find that these processes can occur in several different orderings. In particular, the condensate can remain far from equilibrium even long after the plasma has hydrodynamized and EoSized. We also explore the rapidity distribution of the energy density at hydrodynamization. This is far from boost-invariant and its width decreases as the non-conformality increases. Nevertheless, the velocity field at hydrodynamization is almost exactly boost-invariant regardless of the non-conformality. This result may be used to constrain the initialization of hydrodynamic fields in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper extends previous holographic analyses of non-conformal heavy ion collisions to show that the relaxation time of a non-conserved scalar condensate can be much longer than the hydrodynamization and EoSization times, meaning that one-point functions of non-conserved operators can remain far from equilibrium even after the plasma has become well described by hydrodynamics and has satisfied the equilibrium equation of state.

## Method
The authors perform numerical simulations of shockwave collisions in a five-dimensional holographic Einstein-scalar model with a potential characterized by a single parameter phi_M, which controls the degree of non-conformality. They solve the gravitational equations of motion in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates using a nested sequence of radial ODEs at each null time slice, extracting gauge theory expectation values including the stress tensor and scalar operator condensate from near-boundary expansions. They compare four relaxation times: hydrodynamization time (when hydrodynamics becomes applicable), EoSization time (when average pressure approaches its equilibrium value), isotropization time (when longitudinal and transverse pressures equalize), and condensate relaxation time (when the scalar operator expectation value equilibrates).

## Key numbers
- For phi_M = 2, 3, 5, and 20, the maximum bulk viscosity to shear viscosity ratios are 0.37, 0.32, 0.26, and 0.19 respectively, occurring at temperatures T/Lambda = 0.299, 0.230, 0.220, and 0.218 respectively.
- The entropy density ratio s_R approaches 1 at high temperature and (L_IR/L)^3 at low temperature, where L_IR/L = 1/(1 + phi_M^2/6).
- The scalar operator at the IR fixed point has dimension Delta_IR = 6(1 + phi_M^2/9)(1 + phi_M^2/6)^{-1}.
- The width of the rapidity distribution of energy density at hydrodynamization decreases as non-conformality increases, while the velocity field remains almost exactly boost-invariant regardless of non-conformality.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous holographic studies of non-conformal collisions (Attems 2016) based on models introduced in Attems 2016, which themselves built on conformal holographic heavy ion collision studies by Chesler (2010, 2013, 2015, 2016) and Casalderrey-Solana (2013). The key new finding is that condensate relaxation can be much slower than hydrodynamization and EoSization, which was not possible to study in conformal theories where the scalar operator expectation value is absent. The result that the velocity field at hydrodynamization is nearly boost-invariant regardless of non-conformality may be used to constrain the initialization of hydrodynamic fields in heavy ion collision simulations.
