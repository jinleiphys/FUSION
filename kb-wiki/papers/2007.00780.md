# Possible octupole deformation of $^{208}$Pb and the ultracentral $v_2$ to $v_3$ puzzle

> Recent measurements have established the sensitivity of ultracentral heavy-ion collisions to the deformation parameters of non-spherical nuclei. In the case of ${}^{ 129}$Xe collisions, a quadrupole deformation of the nuclear profile led to an enhancement of elliptic flow in the most central collisions. In ${}^{ 208}$Pb collisions a discrepancy exists in similar centralities, where either elliptic flow is over-predicted or triangular flow is under-predicted by hydrodynamic models; this is known as the $v_2$-to-$v_3$ puzzle in ultracentral collisions. Motivated by low-energy nuclear structure calculations, we consider the possibility that $^{208}$Pb nuclei could have a pear shape deformation (octupole), which has the effect of increasing triangular flow in central PbPb collisions. Using the recent data from ALICE and ATLAS, we revisit the $v_2$-to-$v_3$ puzzle in ultracentral collisions, including new constraints from recent measurements of the triangular cumulant ratio $v_3\left\{4\right\}/v_3\left\{2\right\}$ and comparing two different hydrodynamic models. We find that, while an octupole deformation would slightly improve the ratio between $v_2$ and $v_3$, it is at the expense of a significantly worse triangular flow cumulant ratio. In fact, the latter observable prefers no octupole deformation, with $β_3\lesssim 0.0375$ for ${}^{ 208}$Pb, and is therefore consistent with the expectation for a doubly-magic nucleus even at top collider energies. The $v_2$-to-$v_3$ puzzle remains a challenge for hydrodynamic models.

## Key claim
While a nonzero octupole deformation (pear shape) of 208Pb would slightly improve the ratio of elliptic to triangular flow (v2/v3) in ultracentral collisions, it simultaneously worsens the agreement of the triangular flow cumulant ratio v3{4}/v3{2} with data; the latter observable prefers no octupole deformation, with β3 ≤ 0.0375, and the v2-to-v3 puzzle remains unresolved.

## Method
The authors modified the Trento initial-condition model to include a nonzero octupole deformation parameter β3 for 208Pb, generating events with both geometric-mean (√(TA TB)) and linear (TA TB) entropy deposition. They then performed full event-by-event relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using two different codes (v-USPhydro and MUSIC) with distinct viscosities, equations of state, and hadronic afterburners, and compared the resulting flow harmonics v2 and v3 to ALICE and ATLAS data at √sNN = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit on octupole deformation from the triangular cumulant ratio: β3 ≤ 0.0375 for 208Pb, based on ATLAS data at 5.02 TeV.
- The Minimization After Projection model predicted β3 ≈ 0.0375 for 208Pb, with an expected ±25% error; a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation gave β3 = 0.
- For the geometric-mean initial condition (√(TA TB)), the ratio ε3{2}/ε2{2} in 0-1% centrality is 10-20% larger than for linear scaling (TA TB), which gives only a few percent enhancement.
- The difference between the final-state ratio v3{4}/v3{2} and the initial-state ratio ε3{4}/ε3{2} was less than 1% in 0-1% centrality (based on 11,000 events).

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear deformation effects in ultracentral heavy-ion collisions, previously applied to quadrupole-deformed 129Xe (Giacalone 2017) and 238U (Adamczyk 2015), to the possibility of octupole deformation in 208Pb. It directly addresses the "v2-to-v3 puzzle" identified in earlier comparisons of hydrodynamic models to LHC data (Luzum 2012, Shen 2015), where models assuming spherical 208Pb overpredict v2 or underpredict v3 in ultracentral collisions. The new constraint from the v3{4}/v3{2} ratio (ATLAS 2019) rules out octupole deformation as a solution, leaving the puzzle as an open challenge for initial-state and hydrodynamic modeling.
