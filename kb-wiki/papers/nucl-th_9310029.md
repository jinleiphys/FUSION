# Deep Inelastic Scattering of Polarized Electrons off Polarized $^3$HE: Nuclear Effects and the Neutron Spin Structure Function

> Nuclear effects in Deep Inelastic Scattering of polarized electrons off polarized $^3$He are analyzed in terms of a spin dependent spectral function taking into account $S'$ and $D$ waves in $^3$He, as well as Fermi motion and binding effects. A simple and reliable equation relating the neutron and $^3$He spin structure functions is proposed.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects in deep inelastic scattering of polarized electrons off polarized $^3$He can be reliably accounted for by considering only the effective nucleon polarizations arising from $S'$ and $D$ wave components of the $^3$He wave function, with Fermi motion and binding effects contributing at most a 4% correction for $x \leq 0.9$.

## Method

The authors computed the nuclear spin structure functions $g_1^3$ and $g_2^3$ using a spin-dependent spectral function for $^3$He that incorporates $S'$ and $D$ wave components, Fermi motion, and binding effects. They evaluated the longitudinal asymmetry $A_{||}$ in the Bjorken limit and at finite $Q^2$, comparing three models of increasing complexity: no nuclear effects, effective nucleon polarizations from $S'$ and $D$ waves only, and the full convolution approach. The neutron structure function was extracted from the $^3$He asymmetry using an equation that subtracts the proton contribution weighted by the effective polarizations $p_p$ and $p_n$.

## Key numbers

- Effective neutron polarization in $^3$He: $p_n = 0.86 \pm 0.02$ (from world three-body calculations) or $p_n = 0.88$ (from the spin-dependent spectral function)
- Effective proton polarization in $^3$He: $p_p = -0.028 \pm 0.004$ (from world three-body calculations) or $p_p = -0.030$ (from the spin-dependent spectral function)
- The difference between the free neutron asymmetry and the asymmetry extracted from $^3$He using the effective polarization formula is at most 4% for $x \leq 0.9$
- At finite $Q^2$, $g_1^3(x,Q^2)$ differs from its Bjorken limit $g_1^3(x)$ by at most 15%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear effects in polarized $^3$He targets beyond earlier calculations by Woloshyn (which included $S'$ and $D$ waves but omitted binding effects and $Q^2$-dependent terms). The authors show that the simple effective polarization formula, previously used in a more limited context, remains valid when Fermi motion and binding are included. This enables reliable extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_1^n$ from future experiments using polarized $^3$He targets, such as those planned at HERMES, and provides first theoretical predictions for $g_2^3$ and $g_2^n$.
