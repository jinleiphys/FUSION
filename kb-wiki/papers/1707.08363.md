# Muon capture reaction on $^{100}Mo$ to study nuclear responses for double beta decays and astro-neutrinos

> The negative-muon capture reaction (MCR) on the enriched $^{100}Mo$ isotope was studied for the first time to investigate neutrino nuclear response for neutrino-less double beta decays and supernova neutrino nuclear interactions. MCR on $^{100}Mo$ proceeds mainly as $^{100}Mo(mu,xn)^{100-x}Nb$ with $x$ being the number of neutrons emitted from MCR. The Nb isotope mass distribution was obtained by measuring delayed gamma-rays from radioactive $^{100-x}Nb$. By using the neutron emission model after MCR, the neutrino response (the strength distribution) for MCR was derived. Giant resonance (GR)-like distribution at the peak energy around 11-14 MeV, suggests concentration of the MCR strength at the muon capture GR region.

## Key claim
Muon capture on $^{100}$Mo provides a probe for the $\beta^+$ side of double beta decay nuclear matrix elements and astro anti-neutrino responses, but the extracted strength distribution is a gross structure derived from the mass distribution of residual niobium isotopes rather than a direct measurement of individual nuclear states.

## Method
Negative muons with momentum 28 MeV/c from the J-PARC MLF D-beam line were stopped in a thick $^{100}$Mo target (40 mg/cm$^2$) for 7 hours. The resulting $^{100-x}$Nb isotopes produced via $^{100}$Mo($\mu$,xn)$^{100-x}$Nb were identified by measuring delayed gamma-rays from short-lived and long-lived radioactive isotopes using on-line and off-line HPGe detectors. The mass distribution of niobium isotopes was then converted into the muon capture strength distribution as a function of excitation energy using a statistical neutron emission model incorporating pre-equilibrium and equilibrium neutron evaporation.

## Key numbers
- Muon beam momentum: 28 MeV/c
- Target thickness: 40 mg/cm$^2$ of enriched $^{100}$Mo
- Irradiation time: 7 hours
- Observed niobium isotopes: $^{100}$Nb (2.99 s half-life, 535.0 keV gamma), $^{99}$Nb (2.6 m, 137.0 keV), $^{98}$Nb (1.23 h, 722.0 and 787.0 keV), $^{97}$Nb (0.85 h, 657.9 keV), $^{96}$Nb (23.3 h, 459.0, 568.7, 778.0 keV), $^{95}$Nb (34.9 d, 765.0 keV), $^{94}$Nb (51.8 m, 75.5, 366.9, 891.7 keV), $^{93}$Nb (6.85 h, 2424.9 keV)
- Giant resonance peak energy: 11-14 MeV (first GR at $E_{G1}$=12 MeV with width $\Gamma_1$=8 MeV)
- Second GR at $E_{G2}$=30 MeV with width $\Gamma_2$=8 MeV
- Cross section ratio $\sigma_1/\sigma_2$ = 1/6
- Chi-squared fit quality: $\chi^2$ = 0.06
- MCR energy and momentum regions: $E$=5-50 MeV, $p$=95-50 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the use of muon capture reactions to study $\beta^+$ nuclear responses, complementing extensive studies of $\beta^-$ responses via high energy-resolution ($^{3}$He,t) charge exchange reactions at RCNP (reviewed by Ejiri and collaborators). The approach follows the methodology suggested in 1972 and developed in 2001 by Ejiri and collaborators for deriving strength distributions from residual isotope yields. The results enable comparison with theoretical nuclear matrix element calculations for neutrino-less double beta decay and supernova neutrino interactions, and the authors note that similar muon capture experiments on all double beta decay nuclei are under progress.
