# Crystallization of Carbon Oxygen Mixtures in White Dwarf Stars

> We determine the phase diagram for dense carbon/ oxygen mixtures in White Dwarf (WD) star interiors using molecular dynamics simulations involving liquid and solid phases. Our phase diagram agrees well with predictions from Ogata et al. and Medin and Cumming and gives lower melting temperatures than Segretain et al. Observations of WD crystallization in the globular cluster NGC 6397 by Winget et al. suggest that the melting temperature of WD cores is close to that for pure carbon. If this is true, our phase diagram implies that the central oxygen abundance in these stars is less than about 60%. This constraint, along with assumptions about convection in stellar evolution models, limits the effective S factor for the $^{12}$C($α,γ$)$^{16}$O reaction to S_{300} <= 170 keV barns.

## Key claim
If Winget et al.'s observations that the melting temperature of white dwarf cores is close to that of pure carbon are correct, then the central oxygen abundance in these stars is less than about 60%, which in turn limits the effective S factor for the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction to S300 ≤ 170 keV barns under specific assumptions about convection in stellar evolution models.

## Method
The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations with 27648 ions in a two-phase configuration (solid above liquid) to directly determine the phase diagram for carbon/oxygen mixtures in white dwarf interiors. The simulations used screened Coulomb interactions between ions with a degenerate electron gas background, evolving the system first at constant temperature then at constant energy to allow carbon and oxygen ions to diffuse across liquid-solid interfaces. The composition of the liquid and solid phases was measured by dividing the simulation volume into regions and calculating the average ion charge in each region.

## Key numbers
- Melting Γ value for pure carbon: Γm = 178.4 ± 0.2 (slightly larger than the one-component plasma value of 175)
- Electron density for all simulations: ne = 5.026 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³
- Simulation time for mixture runs: up to 2 × 10⁹ fm/c (runs c75, c50, c25)
- For run c25 (overall carbon fraction 0.25): solid carbon fraction 0.192(4), liquid carbon fraction 0.284(4), temperature 0.0256(1) MeV
- For run c75 (overall carbon fraction 0.75): solid carbon fraction 0.727(5), liquid carbon fraction 0.766(5), temperature 0.0193(1) MeV
- Oxygen concentration limit from phase diagram: xo < 0.57 by number (Xo < 0.64 by mass) if melting temperature T < 1.13 Tc

## Context
This work extends the phase diagram calculations of Ogata et al. and Medin and Cumming, who used Monte Carlo or molecular dynamics simulation free energies, and contradicts the higher melting temperatures and spindle-type phase diagram of Segretain et al. The results enable constraints on the central oxygen abundance in white dwarfs from observations of crystallization in the globular cluster NGC 6397 by Winget et al., and consequently provide limits on the astrophysical S factor for the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction, which remains one of the most important unsettled rates in nuclear astrophysics. Future work will extend these simulations to three-component systems including neon.
