# Chiral Limit of Nuclear Physics

> We study nuclear physics in the chiral limit ($m_u$, $m_d=0$) in which the pion mass vanishes. We find that the deuteron mass is changed little, but that P--wave nucleon--nucleon scattering volumes are infinite. This motivates an investigation of the possibilities that there could be a two--nucleon $^3P_0$ bound state, and that the nuclear matter ground state is likely to be a condensed state of nucleons paired to those quantum numbers. However, the short distance repulsion in the nucleon--nucleon potential is not affected by the chiral limit and prevents such new chiral possibilities. Thus the chiral limit physics of nuclei is very similar to that of nature. Using the chiral limit to simplify QCD sum rule calculations of nuclear matter properties seems to be a reasonable approximation.

# Chiral Limit of Nuclear Physics

## Key claim

The authors find that in the chiral limit (where pion mass vanishes), the deuteron mass changes little and P-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering volumes become infinite, but the short-distance repulsion in the nucleon-nucleon potential is unaffected by the chiral limit and prevents the formation of a $^3P_0$ bound state or a condensed state of nucleons paired to those quantum numbers. Thus the chiral limit physics of nuclei is very similar to that of nature, making the chiral limit a reasonable approximation for QCD sum rule calculations of nuclear matter properties.

## Method

The authors set the pion mass to zero in the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) while keeping the short-range part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction unchanged, using the Bonn one-boson exchange model and the Argonne V18 potential to represent short-distance physics. They compute the change in deuteron binding energy using first-order perturbation theory with Paris and Bonn potential wave functions, and analyze P-wave scattering volumes from the Born approximation. They also solve the BCS gap equation for $^3P_0$ pairing in infinite nuclear matter using the chiral-limit OPEP.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy reduction: approximately 1.1 MeV loss for the Paris potential (2 MeV lost from central OPEP, 0.9 MeV gained from tensor OPEP)
- Deuteron binding energy reduction: approximately 0.7 MeV loss for the Bonn potential (2 MeV lost, 1.3 MeV gained)
- The $^3P_0$ channel tensor potential uses a monopole form factor cutoff $\Lambda = 1.8$ GeV
- The gap equation for $^3P_0$ pairing yields $\Delta(k)$ of several MeV for $k \approx k_F$ when $\Lambda \approx 1000$ MeV/c, with significantly larger values for larger $\Lambda$
- The energy penalty to create a zero-mass pion localized inside a large nucleus is of order $\pi/R_A \approx 100$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear properties in the chiral limit, addressing the implicit assumption in chiral Lagrangian models and QCD sum rule calculations (Cohen, 1996; reviews by Cohen and others) that the chiral limit does not qualitatively change nuclear properties. The authors use the Bonn one-boson exchange model (Machleidt, 1989) and the Paris potential (Lacombe, 1980) for realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, and the Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa, 1995) for short-range repulsion. The results enable confidence in using the chiral limit to simplify QCD sum rule calculations of nuclear matter properties, and rule out the possibility of $^3P_0$ pairing analogous to that in bulk $^3$He (Leggett, 1975) or pion condensates as suggested by Migdal (1978).
