# Studies of the hydrodynamic evolution of the dense baryonic matter produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We use the relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics to describe the space-time evolution of dense baryonic matter produced in the central nucleus-nucleus collisions at HIRFL-CSR, HIAF, FAIR-CBM, NICA-MPD, and RHIC-BES. The transverse flow of the fireball with cylindrical symmetry and boost invariant along the longitudinal direction is also analyzed. Based on the relativistic kinetic theory, we also present the first preliminary calculation on the production of low-mass dileptons and low-pT photons can be considered as the signals of the dense baryonic matter produced in the central Au-Au and U-U collisions. We find that the rapid cooling of the expanding dense baryonic matter with transverse flow effect can lead to the suppression of the low-mass dileptons and low-pT photons production at HIRFL-CSR, HIAF, FAIR-CBM, NICA-MPD, and RHIC-BES energies.

## Key claim

The authors use relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics to model the space-time evolution of dense baryonic matter produced in low-energy central nucleus-nucleus collisions, and present a first preliminary calculation showing that rapid cooling of the expanding fireball with transverse flow effects leads to suppression of low-mass dilepton and low-pT photon production at HIRFL-CSR, HIAF, FAIR-CBM, NICA-MPD, and RHIC-BES energies.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations for a fireball with cylindrical symmetry along the transverse direction and boost invariance along the longitudinal direction, using a grand canonical ensemble partition function for baryons and mesons to obtain thermodynamic quantities. They compute the temperature evolution T(tau,r) as a function of proper time and radial distance, then calculate yields of low-mass dileptons from pi pi -> V -> l+ l- processes and low-pT photons from pi pi -> gamma gamma processes using relativistic kinetic theory. ## Key numbers

- Initial baryon chemical potential values range from mu_B = 422 MeV (RHIC-BES, sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV) to mu_B = 836 MeV (HIRFL-CSR, sqrt(s_NN)=2.07 GeV)
- Initial temperatures range from T_i = 43.2 MeV (HIRFL-CSR, sqrt(s_NN)=2.07 GeV) to T_i = 186.2 MeV (RHIC-BES, sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV)
- Initial energy densities range from epsilon_i = 0.3383 GeV/fm^3 (HIRFL-CSR) to epsilon_i = 1.0563 GeV/fm^3 (RHIC-BES)
- Initial proper times range from tau_i = 0.35 fm/c (RHIC-BES) to tau_i = 1.2 fm/c (HIRFL-CSR, HIAF, and FAIR-CBM at 2.7 GeV)
- The speed of sound is taken as c_s^2 = 0.05-0.15
- Rapidity distributions dN/dy range from 21 (RHIC-BES) to 400 (HIRFL-CSR)
- Freeze-out energy density threshold: rho0 = 0.16 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken (1983) longitudinal expansion model and the Landau (1953) hydrodynamic model to include transverse flow effects for dense baryonic matter at lower collision energies (sqrt(s_NN) < 10 GeV), where baryon chemical potential is significant. The calculations are preliminary and intended for comparison with upcoming experimental data from the CBM experiment at FAIR, the MPD experiment at NICA, and the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, as well as existing facilities HIRFL-CSR and HIAF. The suppression of dilepton and photon yields due to transverse flow provides a baseline for identifying signals of dense baryonic matter in these collision systems.
