# Susceptibilities and the Phase Structure of an Effective Chiral Model with Polyakov Loops

> In the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loops, we explore the relation between the deconfinement and chiral phase transitions within the mean-field approximation. We focus on the phase structure of the model and study the susceptibilities associated with corresponding order parameters.

## Key claim

The deconfinement and chiral phase transitions do not coincide in the PNJL model within mean-field approximation; their simultaneous occurrence is accidental and occurs at only one point in the phase diagram, contrary to lattice QCD results suggesting they appear along the same critical line.

## Method

The authors extended the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by coupling quarks to a uniform temporal background gauge field expressed via Polyakov loops, using an effective gluon potential with polynomial form fitted to pure gauge lattice data. They computed the chiral susceptibility χ_mm and Polyakov loop susceptibilities χ_l\bar{l}, χ_ll, and χ_\bar{l}\bar{l} as functions of temperature and chemical potential within the mean-field approximation, and used the peak positions to map phase boundaries in the (T,μ) plane.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point (TCP) is located at (T_c = 157 MeV, μ_c = 266 MeV) in the chiral limit.
- The pure gauge deconfinement transition temperature is T_0 = 270 MeV, used to fix the effective gluon potential parameters.
- The effective Polyakov loop potential with μ-independent coefficients is considered a good approximation only for μ/T < 1.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model introduced by Meisinger et al. (2002) and Ratti et al. (2006) to study fluctuations near phase transitions, complementing lattice gauge theory calculations by Karsch et al. (2001) that show peak structures in susceptibilities even with finite quark masses. The results enable improved modeling of QCD thermodynamics at finite density, but the authors note that the polynomial form of the Polyakov loop potential yields unphysical negative diagonal susceptibilities χ_ll, which is corrected by the improved logarithmic potential of Roessner et al. (2007) that respects the full SU(3) group structure.
