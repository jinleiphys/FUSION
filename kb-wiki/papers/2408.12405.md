# Non-extensive (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A non-extensive (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model for multi-particle production processes, NEX-CLVisc, is developed in the framework of CLVisc where the viscous corrections are turned off. It assumes that the non-extensive effects consistently exist in the initial conditions set by the optical Glauber model, the equation of state and the hadron kinetic freeze-out procedure. The model is then applied to simulate the pseudo-rapidity ($η$) distribution, the transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) spectra and the $p_{\rm T}$-differential elliptic flow ($v_2$) of charged particles in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV, respectively. It is found that the model can reasonably well reproduce the experimental data of the $η$ distribution and the charged-particle spectra in a $p_{\rm T}$ range up to 6-8 GeV/c. When compared with the ideal hydrodynamic model, the $p_{\rm T}$-differential $v_2$ of charged particles is suppressed in the NEX-CLVisc model, which is similar to that observed in the hydrodynamic model with a shear viscous correction. Moreover, due to the lack of the viscous corrections and the event-by-event fluctuation, the model can only describe the $p_{\rm T}$-differential $v_2$ up to 3-4 GeV/c, which is smaller than its applicable range for the particle $p_{\rm T}$ spectra.

## Key claim

The NEX-CLVisc model, which applies a single non-extensive parameter \(q\) consistently to initial conditions, equation of state, and kinetic freeze-out while turning off viscous corrections, can reproduce charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions and transverse momentum spectra up to 6–8 GeV/c in Pb-Pb collisions, but its description of elliptic flow is limited to \(p_{\rm T} < 3\)–4 GeV/c due to the absence of viscous corrections and event-by-event fluctuations.

## Method

The authors developed a (3+1)-dimensional non-extensive hydrodynamic model (NEX-CLVisc) based on the CLVisc framework with viscous corrections turned off. Non-extensive effects were incorporated via the Tsallis \(q\)-parameter into: (i) the initial energy density profile from the optical Glauber model, using a \(q\)-exponential modification of the longitudinal profile; (ii) the equation of state, constructed by embedding \(q\)-corrections into the hadron resonance gas model and a parton resonance gas model; and (iii) the kinetic freeze-out procedure, using a \(q\)-extended Cooper-Frye formula. The model was applied to Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV and 5.02 TeV, with \(q = 1.05\) and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 150\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density \(\varepsilon_0 = 111.8\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(q=1\) and 133.24 GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(q=1.05\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV in 0–5% centrality.
- Maximum initial energy density \(\varepsilon_0 = 138.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(q=1\) and 165.19 GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(q=1.05\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV in 0–5% centrality.
- Kinetic freeze-out time for 0–5% centrality at 2.76 TeV: 14.32 fm/c (\(q=1\)) and 9.23 fm/c (\(q=1.05\)); at 5.02 TeV: 14.71 fm/c (\(q=1\)) and 9.55 fm/c (\(q=1.05\)).
- The non-extensive model (\(q=1.05\)) reproduces charged-particle \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra up to 6–8 GeV/c, while the extensive model (\(q=1\)) describes data only up to 2–3 GeV/c.
- The \(p_{\rm T}\)-differential elliptic flow \(v_2\) is described by the non-extensive model only up to 3–4 GeV/c, smaller than the applicable range for the \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra.

## Context

This work extends prior (1+1)-dimensional and (2+1)-dimensional non-extensive hydrodynamic models (non_ext_hydro, 2018; eos, 2020) to full (3+1) dimensions, enabling the study of both longitudinal and transverse observables simultaneously. The model is built on the CLVisc framework (clvisc, 2018) and uses the optical Glauber model for initial conditions. The suppression of elliptic flow in NEX-CLVisc resembles that seen in hydrodynamic models with shear viscous corrections, but the lack of viscous corrections and event-by-event fluctuations limits its applicability for \(v_2\) to lower \(p_{\rm T}\) than for spectra. The results confirm the finding of eos (2020) that the \(q\) value is approximately independent of collision energy, but show a centrality dependence where the same \(q\) works less well for peripheral collisions.
