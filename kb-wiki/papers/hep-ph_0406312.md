# Resonances, and mechanisms of Theta-production

> After explaining necessity of exotic hadrons, we discuss mechanisms which could determine production of the exotic Theta-baryon. A possible important role of resonances (producing the Theta in real or virtual decays) is emphasized for various processes. Several experimental directions for studies of such resonances, and the Theta itself, are suggested. We briefly discuss also recent negative results on the Theta-baryon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the production of the exotic $\Theta^+$ baryon in various processes is likely governed by different mechanisms, with a special and possibly dominant role played by intermediate resonances (real or virtual), rather than simple exchange diagrams; the authors take a "conservative position" that $\Theta$ does exist, but its production intensity depends strongly on the specific reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors qualitatively analyze possible production mechanisms for the $\Theta^+$ baryon, focusing on photoproduction ($\gamma N \to K \Theta$) and related electroproduction. They compare the energy dependence of $t$-channel meson exchanges (K, K*) and $u$-channel baryon exchanges, which decrease with energy, to the potential role of $s$-channel resonances, drawing analogy to known strangeness photoproduction ($\gamma N \to K \Lambda/\Sigma$). They then discuss the properties of a specific candidate resonance, $N^*(2400)$, observed by CLAS in the $K^-\Theta^+$ system, and use $SU(3)_F$ and $U$-spin arguments to suggest experimental tests (e.g., comparing photoexcitation on neutron vs. proton) that could discriminate its flavor multiplet assignment (octet, antidecuplet, or 27-plet).

## Key numbers

- The $N^*(2400)$ resonance is observed as a narrow peak in the mass distribution of the $(K^-\Theta^+)$ system near **2400 MeV**.
- The $K$ meson exchange contribution to the amplitude scales as $\sim s^{2\alpha_K(0)-1}$ with $2\alpha_K(0)-1 \approx -1.5$, while the $K^*$ exchange scales as $\sim s^{2\alpha_{K^*}(0)-1}$ with $2\alpha_{K^*}(0)-1 \approx -0.6$.
- The reaction $\pi^- + p \to K^- + \Theta^+$ with a real pion is predicted to show a narrow enhancement at a pion kinetic energy $T_{\pi} \approx 2.45$ GeV.
- For photoproduction via the $s$-channel $N^*(2400)$ resonance, the required photon energy is $E_{\gamma} \approx 2.6$ GeV.
- The BES upper limit for the branching ratio $Br(J/\psi \to \Theta \bar{\Theta})$ is $< 0.44 \times 10^{-4}$, which is suppressed by a factor $< 0.034$ compared to $Br(J/\psi \to \Lambda \bar{\Lambda}) = (13.0 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-4}$; the dynamical suppression factor is estimated to be $< 0.23$.
- The BES upper limit for $Br(\psi(2S) \to \Theta \bar{\Theta})$ is $< 0.34 \times 10^{-4}$, with a dynamical suppression factor $< 0.27$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion following the prediction of a narrow $\Theta^+$ baryon by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) using the chiral soliton approach, and the subsequent positive experimental evidence from collaborations like LEPS, DIANA, and CLAS. The paper specifically addresses the need to understand production mechanisms, contrasting with the approach of Karliner and Lipkin (2004) who suggested ways to clarify the problem. It enables future experimental searches for the $\Theta^+$ and related resonances (especially $N^*(2400)$) in photo- and electroproduction, $e^+e^-$ annihilation, and diffraction excitation, and provides a framework for interpreting negative results, such as those from BES (2004), by arguing that different mechanisms dominate in different processes.
