# Hyperon-Quark Mixed Phase in Dense Matter

> We investigate the properties of the hadron-quark mixed phase in compact stars using a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework for hadronic matter and the MIT bag model for quark matter. We find that the equation of state of the mixed phase is similar to that given by the Maxwell construction. The composition of the mixed phase, however, is very different from that of the Maxwell construction; in particular, hyperons are completely suppressed.

## Key claim

The equation of state of the hadron-quark mixed phase in compact stars is similar to that given by the Maxwell construction, but the composition is very different; in particular, hyperons are completely suppressed in the mixed phase due to the relaxation of the charge-neutrality condition, whereas they are always present in the Maxwell construction.

## Method

The authors used a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with nonrelativistic potentials and nucleonic three-body forces to describe hadronic matter, and the MIT bag model with massless u and d quarks, a massive s quark (m_s = 150 MeV), and bag constant B = 100 MeV/fm^3 for quark matter. They employed a Wigner-Seitz approximation with spherical, cylindrical, and slab geometries to model the mixed phase, including Coulomb interaction and surface tension (sigma = 40 MeV/fm^2), and solved for the minimum energy density configuration at each baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: rho_0 ≈ 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Surface tension parameter used: sigma = 40 MeV/fm^2
- Bag constant: B = 100 MeV/fm^3
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV
- Maximum mass of purely nucleonic star: about 1.82 solar masses
- Maximum mass of purely hyperonic star: about 1.32 solar masses
- Maximum mass of hybrid star (with quark matter): about 1.5 solar masses
- Transition density for hyperon appearance in charge-neutral hadronic matter: 0.34 fm^{-3}
- Density above which hyperons appear in symmetric nuclear matter (without charge neutrality): 1.15 fm^{-3}
- Wigner-Seitz cell radius at rho_B = 0.4 fm^{-3}: R_W = 26.7 fm, droplet radius R = 17.3 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark mixed phase (Maruyama et al., earlier work) by applying the Gibbs conditions with pasta structures to the specific case of hyperon-quark deconfinement. The hyperonic EOS from BHF calculations is so soft that purely hyperonic stars cannot reach the canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star mass, but hybrid stars including quark matter can. The suppression of hyperons in the mixed phase has implications for neutrino opacity (Reddy, earlier work) and cooling via hyperon Urca processes (Pethick, earlier work), directly affecting thermal evolution of compact stars.
