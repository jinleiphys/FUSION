# Tidal Deformability as a Probe of Dark Matter in Neutron Stars

> The concept of boson stars (BSs) was first introduced by Kaup and Ruffini-Bonazzola in the 1960s. Following this idea, we investigate an effect of self-interacting asymmetric bosonic dark matter (DM) according to Colpi et al. model for BSs (1986) on different observable properties of neutron stars (NSs). In this paper, the bosonic DM and baryonic matter (BM) are mixed together and interact only through gravitational force. The presence of DM as a core of a compact star or as an extended halo around it is examined by applying different boson masses and DM fractions for a fixed coupling constant. The impact of DM core/halo formations on a DM admixed NS properties is probed through the maximum mass and tidal deformability of NSs. Thanks to the recent detection of Gravitational-Waves (GWs) and the latest X-ray observations, the DM admixed NS's features are compared to LIGO/Virgo and NICER results.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how self-interacting bosonic dark matter (DM) that interacts with baryonic matter only through gravity can alter the maximum mass and tidal deformability of neutron stars (NSs), and shows that whether DM forms a core or a halo around the NS depends on the DM particle mass, with the transition occurring around 200 MeV for a fixed coupling constant.

## Method

The authors use a two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) formalism where bosonic DM and baryonic matter (BM) are treated as separate fluids interacting only gravitationally. The DM is modeled as a complex scalar field with repulsive self-interaction (Colpi et al. model, 1986) using an equation of state derived in the strong-coupling regime, while the BM is described by the induced surface tension (IST) equation of state. The authors solve the two-fluid TOV equations for different DM particle masses and DM fractions at a fixed coupling constant, and compute the tidal deformability parameter from the resulting mass-radius profiles.

## Key numbers

* The heaviest known pulsar PSR J0740+6620 has mass \(2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M_{\odot}\) (NICER observation), setting a constraint that maximum stellar mass must be at least \(M_{max}=2M_\odot\).
* The GW170817 merger event gives an upper bound on dimensionless tidal deformability \(\Lambda \leq 580\) for a star of mass \(M=1.4 M_\odot\).
* The pure baryonic NS (IST EoS) has maximum mass \(M_{max}=2.08 M_{\odot}\) and radius of the \(1.4 M_\odot\) star \(R_{1.4}=11.37\) km.
* The pure baryonic NS has tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4} \approx 285\) for \(M=1.4 M_\odot\).
* For DM halo formation with \(m_{\chi}=100\) MeV and \(F_{\chi}=5\%\), the tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) exceeds 580.
* The maximum compactness of a boson star based on the Colpi et al. equation of state is \(\mathcal{C}_{(max)} \simeq 0.16\), independent of boson mass and coupling constant.
* The strong coupling regime condition is \(\lambda \gg 8.43 \times 10^{-36} (m_{\chi}/100\,\text{MeV})^2\).

## Context

This work extends the two-fluid formalism previously applied to DM admixed NSs by Sandin (2009), Ciarcelluti (2011), and others, using the specific bosonic DM model of Colpi et al. (1986) for boson stars. The results agree with earlier studies by Nelson (2019) and Ellis (2018) showing that a DM core decreases maximum mass and radius while a DM halo increases these quantities. The paper enables future constraints on DM particle mass and DM fraction by comparing to observational limits from LIGO/Virgo (GW170817) and NICER (PSR J0740+6620), and suggests that the model could explain unusually massive compact objects such as the secondary in GW190814.
