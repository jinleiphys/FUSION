# In medium T matrix with realistic nuclear interactions

> We calculate the self-consistent in-medium T matrix for symmetric nuclear matter using realistic interactions with many partial waves. We find for the interactions used (CDBonn and Nijmegen) very similar results for on-shell quantities. The effective mass and the renormalization factor Z_F at the Fermi momentum are given for a range of densities.

# In medium T matrix with realistic nuclear interactions

## Key claim

The self-consistent in-medium T-matrix approach, when applied with realistic CDBonn and Nijmegen interactions including many partial waves, yields very similar results for on-shell quantities across a range of densities, despite differences in the off-shell behavior of the self-energy and in the Hartree-Fock contributions.

## Method

The authors solved the self-consistent in-medium T-matrix equations for symmetric nuclear matter using the CDBonn and Nijmegen nucleon-nucleon potentials, incorporating all partial waves with total angular momentum J less than 9. The Green's functions were dressed by the full spectral functions (allowing off-shell propagation), and the equations were iterated for nuclear densities in the range 0.2 to 2.4 times normal nuclear density. A separable parameterization of the interaction was used, with 8 eigen-vectors for uncoupled partial waves and 24 eigen-vectors for coupled partial waves.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per particle at normal nuclear density: -14.3 MeV for CDBonn and -14.1 MeV for Nijmegen interaction
- BHF binding energies at normal density are 3.3-3.5 MeV lower than the T-matrix results
- Normal nuclear density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Density range studied: 0.2 to 2.4 rho_0
- Effective mass parameterization: m*/m = 1.43 - 0.623 (rho/rho_0) + 0.146 (rho/rho_0)^2
- Renormalization factor parameterization: Z_F = 0.57 + 0.2 (rho/rho_0) - 0.05 (rho/rho_0)^2
- At normal nuclear density: Z_F approximately 0.72, giving a reduction of the effective interaction between quasiparticles by a factor Z_F^2 approximately 0.5

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent T-matrix calculations (Bozek 1998, Bozek 2001, Bozek 2002, Dewulf 2002) from simplified separable parameterizations of the Paris potential to the more realistic CDBonn and Nijmegen potentials with many partial waves. The results enable more reliable calculations of the effective interaction between quasiparticles (Dickhoff 1999, Schwenk 2002) and the superfluid gap (Bozek 2000, Bozek 2002), as the uncertainties from technical simplifications in the interaction parameterization are now removed.
