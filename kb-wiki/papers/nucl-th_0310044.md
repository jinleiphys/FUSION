# Medium induced jet absorption at relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The dense medium created in Au + Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) significantly suppresses particle production from hard scattering processes and their characteristic back-to-back angular correlation. We present a simple model of jet absorption in dense matter which incorporates a realistic nuclear geometry. Our calculations are performed at the jet level and assume independent jet fragmentation in the vacuum. This model describes quantitatively the centrality dependence of the observed suppression of the high $p_T$ hadron yield and of the back-to-back angular correlations. The azimuthal anisotropy of high $p_T$ particle production can not be accounted for using a realistic nuclear geometry.

## Key claim
A simple model of jet absorption in an extremely opaque medium, assuming most energy is lost in a single scattering and using a realistic Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry, can quantitatively describe the centrality dependence of high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression and back-to-back correlation suppression in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but it cannot account for the measured azimuthal anisotropy \(v_2\) unless an unrealistic nuclear density profile is used.

## Method
The authors model jet absorption by generating back-to-back parton pairs in the transverse plane with a distribution proportional to the nucleon-nucleon collision density \(\rho_{\text{coll}}(x,y)\) from a Monte Carlo Glauber simulation, and propagating them through a medium with density proportional to the participant density \(\rho_{\text{part}}(x,y)\). The survival probability of a parton is given by \(f = \exp(-\kappa I)\), where \(\kappa\) is a single free absorption strength fixed to reproduce the central collision suppression, and \(I\) is a matter integral along the parton path that incorporates a quadratic dependence on path length in a longitudinally expanding medium. The model assumes independent jet fragmentation in vacuum and is applied to the \(p_T\) range 4.5–10 GeV/\(c\).

## Key numbers
- Suppression factor of 4.35 for the hadron yield in 0–5% most central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, used to fix \(\kappa = 0.7\).
- Absorption length \(\lambda \sim 3.4\) fm for a parton traversing an expanding medium with constant participant density of 2.48 fm\(^{-2}\).
- Calculated suppression of back-to-back correlation strength by a factor of 7 for the most central bin, compared to a factor of 4.35 for single inclusive yield.
- Maximum calculated \(v_2\) of 6% for collisions with impact parameter \(\sim 9\) fm (approximately 100 participants), below the measured value.
- For a hard-sphere nuclear profile, the model yields \(v_2^{\text{max}} = 10\%\) at \(b = 9\) fm for optimal \(\kappa\).
- Predicted high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression factor of \(\sim 3\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV and a factor of 2 at SPS energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17\) GeV), assuming the same \(\kappa\).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Shuryak (2002) for setting limits on azimuthal anisotropy from jet quenching, and contrasts with more detailed energy-loss models by Baier et al. (1997), BDMPS (1997), and Wang (2001) that include explicit gluon radiation calculations. The model demonstrates that the centrality dependence of \(R_{AA}\) and \(D_{AA}\) is largely determined by geometry and average density, while the failure to reproduce \(v_2\) with a realistic Woods-Saxon profile suggests either a sharper medium boundary than expected from the Glauber model or contributions from non-jet fragmentation mechanisms such as hydrodynamics (Kolb, 2003), quark coalescence (Greco, 2003), or viscosity corrections (Teaney, 2003).
