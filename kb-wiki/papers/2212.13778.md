# Testing the $ϕ$-nuclear potential in pion-induced $ϕ$ meson production on nuclei near threshold

> The near-threshold pion-induced $ϕ$ production off nuclei has been studied in the kinematical conditions of the HADES experiment using a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function. Starting from the elementary reaction ${π^-}p \to ϕn$, absolute differential and integral cross sections for the production of $ϕ$ mesons off a light carbon and a heavy tungsten target have been calculated and compared to the recently reported experimental cross sections. The absolute cross section values are shown to be sensitive to the effective nuclear scalar $ϕ$ and neutron potentials while the transparency ratio is governed by the $ϕN$ absorption cross section. The experimental data are found to be consistent with an attractive $ϕ$-nucleus potential of $\approx$ -(50--100) MeV at normal nuclear matter density $ρ_0$ and an effective $ϕN$ absorption cross section of $\approx$ 12--25 mb. The extracted $ϕ$-nucleus potential is deeper than previous theoretical and experimental findings as well as those obtained in the present work within the ($tρ$) approximation with accounting for Pauli correlations from the spin-averaged $ϕ$-$p$ scattering length reported in a recent ALICE experiment.

# Testing the $\phi$-nuclear potential in pion-induced $\phi$ meson production on nuclei near threshold

## Key claim

The HADES experimental data on near-threshold pion-induced $\phi$ production on carbon and tungsten targets are consistent with an attractive $\phi$-nucleus potential of approximately -(50-100) MeV at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0$ and an effective $\phi N$ absorption cross section of approximately 12-25 mb, but only when the $\phi$ potential is deeper than the -(35$\pm$7) MeV extracted from the earlier KEK-PS E325 experiment and deeper than the value obtained within the ($t\rho$) approximation with Pauli correlations using the ALICE spin-averaged $\phi$-$p$ scattering length.

## Method

The author employed a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function to calculate absolute differential and integral cross sections for $\phi$ meson production via the elementary reaction $\pi^- p \to \phi n$ on $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W targets at an incident pion momentum of 1.7 GeV/c, corresponding to an excess energy of 66 MeV above the $\phi n$ production threshold. The model incorporated in-medium modifications through average effective masses for the final $\phi$ meson and neutron, using effective scalar nuclear potentials at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0$, and accounted for $\phi$ meson absorption by target nucleons via the $\phi N$ absorption cross section. The calculations imposed the HADES spectrometer acceptance cuts on rapidity (0.4 $\le y <$ 1.0) and transverse momentum (150 $\le p_T <$ 650 MeV/c) and compared the resulting cross sections and transparency ratios to the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The HADES experimental integrated $\phi$ production cross sections in the acceptance window are $\Delta\sigma_{\rm C}^{\phi} = (41 \pm 6.2)$ $\mu$b for carbon and $\Delta\sigma_{\rm W}^{\phi} = (112 \pm 19.5)$ $\mu$b for tungsten at an incident pion momentum of 1.7 GeV/c.
- The extracted $\phi$-nucleus potential consistent with the data is $U_{\phi} \approx$ -(50-100) MeV at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The effective $\phi N$ absorption cross section consistent with the data is $\sigma_{\phi N} \approx$ 12-25 mb.
- The $\phi$ potential depth estimated from the ALICE spin-averaged $\phi$-$p$ scattering length $f_{\phi p} = 0.85 \pm 0.37$ fm using the ($t\rho$) approximation without Pauli correlations is $U_{\phi} \approx$ -(70$\pm$30) MeV, and with Pauli correlations is $U_{\phi} \approx -32.0^{+5.5}_{-8.4}$ MeV.
- The maximum laboratory polar angles for $\phi$ mesons and neutrons in the free-space reaction at 1.7 GeV/c are $\theta_{\phi}^{\rm max} = 17.427^{\circ}$ and $\theta_{n}^{\rm max} = 18.963^{\circ}$.
- The kinematically allowed $\phi$ meson laboratory momenta in the free-space reaction range from 0.548 to 1.217 GeV/c at the given pion momentum.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of $\phi$ meson production in nuclei using the spectral function approach previously developed by the author (Paryev, 2022) and applies it to the first pion-induced $\phi$ production data from the HADES Collaboration (2022). The extracted $\phi$-nucleus potential of -(50-100) MeV is deeper than the -(35$\pm$7) MeV reported by the KEK-PS E325 experiment (2007) from proton-induced $\phi$ production on copper, and deeper than the value obtained from the ($t\rho$) approximation with Pauli correlations using the ALICE scattering length (2021). The results enable improved constraints on the in-medium modification of the $\phi$ meson mass and width in cold nuclear matter, complementing information from proton-nucleus studies by the ANKE Collaboration (2011) and photon-nucleus studies by the LEPS (2005) and CLAS (2010) Collaborations.
