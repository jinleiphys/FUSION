# Forward sum rule for the $2γ$-exchange correction to the charge radius extraction from elastic electron scattering

> Two-photon exchange (TPE) contributions to elastic electron-proton scattering in the forward regime and in leading log $\sim t\ln|t|$ approximation in the momentum transfer $|t|$ are considered. The imaginary part of TPE amplitude in these kinematics is related to the total photo absorption cross section. The real part of the TPE amplitude is obtained from the imaginary part by means of a fixed-$t$ dispersion relation. The dispersion integrals for the relevant elastic $ep$-scattering amplitude converge and do not need subtraction. This allows us to make clean a prediction for the real part of the TPE amplitude at forward angles with the leading term $\sim t\ln|t|$. Numerical estimates are comparable to the experimental precision in extracting the charge radius from experimental data.

# Forward sum rule for the $2\gamma$-exchange correction to the charge radius extraction from elastic electron scattering

## Key claim
The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to elastic electron-proton scattering at low momentum transfer produces a leading term proportional to $t\ln|t|$ whose coefficient can be calculated model-independently from the total photoabsorption cross section; this correction introduces a nonlinearity in the reduced cross section that is comparable to or exceeds the experimental precision in charge radius extraction, particularly at beam energies of 1-2 GeV.

## Method
The imaginary part of the TPE amplitude in forward kinematics was related to the total photoabsorption cross section via the optical theorem. The real part was obtained from an unsubtracted fixed-$t$ dispersion relation. The elastic and inelastic contributions to the TPE box diagram were separated, with the inelastic part evaluated using a phenomenological fit to world data on real photoabsorption on proton and deuteron targets. The leading logarithmic behavior $\sim t\ln|t|$ was isolated by expanding form factors and performing the angular integrals over the intermediate electron phase space.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius from Mainz A1 experiment: $R_E^p = 0.879 \pm 0.008$ fm (combined uncertainty)
- Proton charge radius from hydrogen spectroscopy: $R_E^p = 0.8775 \pm 0.0051$ fm
- Proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift: $R_E^p = 0.84087 \pm 0.00039$ fm
- Experimental sensitivity for proton: $\delta\sigma_R^{R_E, exp.}/|t| = -6.61(12)$ GeV$^{-2}$ (derived from $R_E = 0.879(8)$ fm)
- Experimental sensitivity for deuteron: $\delta\sigma_R^{R_E, exp.}/|t| = -39.278(196)$ GeV$^{-2}$ (based on $R_E^d = 2.1424(21)$ fm and 0.25% projected precision)
- Minimum momentum transfer accessed in A1 experiment: $|t|_{min} = 4 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$
- Relative uncertainty in reduced cross section at $|t|_{min}$: $5 \times 10^{-4}$
- The TPE correction at JLab energies (1.1-2.2 GeV) is roughly three times the experimental sensitivity for $|t|$ between $10^{-4}$ and $5 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^2$

## Context
This work extends the approach of Gorchtein (2006) which concentrated on high-energy TPE corrections, now applied to the low-energy kinematics of charge radius experiments. It provides a model-independent calculation of the leading logarithmic TPE correction that complements the ground-state-only TPE calculations of Borisyuk (2006, 2008) and Tomalak (2014). The result is directly relevant to the "proton radius puzzle" arising from the discrepancy between muonic hydrogen measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) and electron scattering/spectroscopy results. The calculation enables improved extraction of the proton and deuteron charge radii from elastic electron scattering data at Mainz (Bernauer 2010) and proposed JLab experiments, by providing a correction that must be included when aiming for sub-1% precision.
