# New insights into the analytic structure of correlation functions via kinetic theory

> The way a relativistic system approaches fluid dynamical behaviour can be understood physically through the signals that will contribute to its linear response to perturbations. What these signals are is captured in the analytic structure of the retarded correlation function. The non-analyticities can be grouped into three types based on their dimension in the complex frequency plane. In this paper, we will use kinetic theory to find how we can calculate their corresponding signals. In the most general case of a system with particles that have a continuum of thermalization rates, we find that a non-analytic region appears. To calculate its signal, we introduce the non-analytic area density that describes the properties of this region, and we construct a method to calculate it. Further, to take into account the ambiguity present in signal analysis, following from manipulations of the non-analyticities, we will identify two specific choices called pictures with interesting analytic properties and compare in what scenarios each picture is most useful.

# New insights into the analytic structure of correlation functions via kinetic theory

## Key claim

The paper claims that in momentum-dependent relaxation time approximation (RTA) kinetic theory, a two-dimensional non-analytic region appears in the retarded correlation function when particles have a continuum of thermalization rates, and this region can be characterized by a newly defined "non-analytic area density", but only for the specific case of particle diffusion in the linear response regime, and only when the relaxation time grows with momentum in an unrestricted way.

## Method

The author solves the linearized Boltzmann equation in the Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) for particle diffusion, first with a constant relaxation time and then with a momentum-dependent relaxation time of the form τ_R(p) = t_R p/T. The retarded correlation function G^{0,0}(ω,k) is computed analytically in Fourier space. For the momentum-dependent case, the author defines a new quantity called the non-analytic area density by taking the limit of a contour integral around an infinitesimal area in the complex frequency plane, and computes it by exchanging the momentum and frequency integrals. Two distinct analytic continuations ("pictures") are identified: the physical picture with a horizontal branch cut, and the continuous picture with two vertical branch cuts.

## Key numbers

- The diffusion mode dispersion relation in standard RTA is ω_H(k) = -i/t_R (1 - k t_R / tan(k t_R)) = -i (t_R/3) k² + O(k⁴), with diffusivity D = t_R/3.
- In standard RTA, the hydrodynamic pole exists only for k t_R < π/2 in the physical picture, and for k t_R < π in the continuous picture.
- The residue of the diffusion mode in standard RTA is Res(G^{0,0}, ω_H) = -χ ω_H(k) (k² t_R² / sin(k² t_R²)).
- For momentum-dependent RTA with τ_R(p) = t_R p/T, the non-analytic area density for the particle density is NAS[n] = -(π/k) (T³ / (y⁴ t_R³)) f(0, T/(y t_R), k), which is non-zero only for |Re(ω)| < k.
- For distribution functions with an exponential tail in p/T, the non-analytic density decreases as e^{-1/y} near the real axis, with a peak at y = 1/(4 t_R), indicating an effective relaxation time of 4 t_R.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of analytic structures in kinetic theory from Romatschke (2016), who found a horizontal branch cut for constant relaxation time RTA, and from Kurkela and collaborators (2017), who deformed the analytic structure into two vertical branch cuts for momentum-dependent RTA. The paper directly addresses the non-analytic region that was previously avoided by branch cut manipulations in Kurkela (2017) and was observed numerically by Ochsenfeld (2023). The new non-analytic area density method enables analytic calculation of signals from two-dimensional non-analytic regions, which is necessary for general kinetic theories where numerical momentum grids make explicit branch cut manipulations impossible. The work also connects to the proof by Gavassino (2024) that general quantum field theories have gapless non-analyticities.
