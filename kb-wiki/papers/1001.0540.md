# Electromagnetic interactions in Halo Effective Field Theory

> After a brief discussion of effective field theory applied to nuclear clusters, I concentrate on the inclusion of two particular aspects, namely, narrow resonances and electromagnetic interactions. As examples of applications, I present the details of our studies on alpha-alpha and proton-alpha scattering.

## Key claim

Halo/cluster effective field theory can describe narrow resonances and electromagnetic interactions in nuclear cluster systems, but the power counting requires fine-tuning of coupling constants to generate the large scattering lengths needed for shallow bound states and resonances. The deep non-perturbative Coulomb regime allows a low-energy expansion of the Coulomb function that simplifies the amplitude and makes the resonance physics more transparent.

## Method

The author formulates a non-relativistic effective field theory for nuclear clusters using structureless boson fields and an auxiliary dimeron field carrying the quantum numbers of two bosons in S-wave, with Coulomb interactions included via minimal substitution and the two-potential formalism. For the alpha-alpha system, the amplitude is expanded around the resonance pole position rather than zero energy, using the deep Coulomb approximation for the function H(eta) to combine strong-interaction and Coulomb contributions into redefined effective range parameters. The EFT parameters are determined by fitting to the resonance energy and width constraints from the literature, with the shape parameter obtained from a chi-square fit to scattering data up to 3 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- Alpha-alpha S-wave resonance energy: E_R approximately 92 keV (center-of-mass), corresponding to E_LAB^R = 184.15 +/- 0.07 keV and resonance width Gamma_LAB^R = 11.14 +/- 0.50 eV (Wue92)
- Alpha-alpha scattering length a_0 approximately 2000 fm, about three orders of magnitude larger than the alpha matter radius
- Effective range r_0 approximately 1 fm and shape parameter P_0 approximately -1.7 fm^3
- Low-energy scale M_lo approximately sqrt(m_alpha * E_R) approximately 20 MeV; high-energy scale M_hi approximately m_pi approximately 140 MeV
- Bohr momentum k_C = alpha_em * Z_alpha^2 * mu approximately 60 MeV, giving k_C/k_R approximately 3
- LO fit values: a_0 = -1.80 x 10^3 fm, r_0 = 1.083 fm
- NLO fit values: a_0 = (-1.92 +/- 0.09) x 10^3 fm, r_0 = 1.098 +/- 0.005 fm, P_0 = -1.46 +/- 0.08 fm^3
- Resonance width from NLO fit: Gamma_R = 5.57 +/- 0.25 eV

## Context

This work extends the halo/cluster EFT framework developed for neutron-alpha scattering (Bertulani, Hammer, and van Kolck, 2002) and neutron-deuteron systems (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck, 2000) to include both narrow resonances and Coulomb interactions in charged nuclear cluster systems. The treatment of Coulomb interactions follows the EFT formulation by Kong and Ravndal (1999) for the two-proton system. The alpha-alpha results connect to studies of the triple-alpha process in nuclear astrophysics and to the universal Efimov-like spectrum in three-alpha systems, where the Hoyle state in carbon-12 remains close to the threshold into alpha particles. The approach enables systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections with controlled theoretical uncertainties, and the formalism can be extended to proton-alpha scattering and other halo nuclei.
