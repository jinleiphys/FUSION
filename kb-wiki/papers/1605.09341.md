# Universal off-equilibrium scaling of critical cumulants in the QCD phase diagram

> Exploiting the universality between the QCD critical point and the three dimensional Ising model, closed form expressions derived (arXiv:1506.00645 ) for non-equilibrium critical cumulants on the crossover side of the critical point reveal that they can differ both in magnitude and sign from equilibrium expectations. We demonstrate here that key elements of the Kibble-Zurek framework of non-equilibrium phase transitions can be employed to describe the dynamics of these critical cumulants. Our results suggest that observables sensitive to critical dynamics in heavy-ion collisions should be expressible as universal scaling functions, thereby providing powerful model independent guidance in searches for the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that non-equilibrium critical cumulants of the QCD critical point, which can differ in both magnitude and sign from equilibrium expectations, can be expressed as universal scaling functions using the Kibble-Zurek framework, provided the system follows specific trajectory protocols on the crossover side of the critical point.

## Method

The authors exploit the universality between the QCD critical point and the three-dimensional Ising model, using closed-form expressions for the first four cumulants derived from a Fokker-Planck master equation describing non-equilibrium dynamics of critical fluctuations. They identify two distinct trajectory protocols (type A and type B) based on whether the quench rate of the correlation length or the magnetization angle dominates the relaxation rate, then numerically solve the cumulant evolution equations to test whether rescaling by Kibble-Zurek characteristic scales collapses the results onto universal curves.

## Key numbers

- Dynamic scaling exponent for QCD: \(z = 3\) (model H universality class)
- Approximate rational critical exponents used: \((\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \nu, \delta, \eta) = (0, 1/3, 4/3, 5, 0)\)
- For protocol A, the equilibrium correlation length scales as \(\xi_{\rm eq} \sim |h|^{-2/5}\) near the critical point
- The relaxation time relates to correlation length as \(\tau_{\rm eff} \sim \xi_{\rm eq}^3\)
- For protocol B numerical tests, the magnetization angle at freeze-out was tuned to \(\theta_{\rm KZ} = -0.1\)
- The quench time for protocol A near \(T_c\) satisfies \(\tau_{\rm quench}^\xi \approx \frac{5}{2}|\tau|\)

## Context

This work extends prior results by Mukherjee, Venugopalan, and Yin (2015) which derived closed-form expressions for non-equilibrium critical cumulants, showing they can differ from equilibrium expectations of Stephanov (2008, 2011) and Asakawa (2009). The Kibble-Zurek framework, originally developed by Kibble (1980) for cosmological phase transitions and generalized by Zurek (1985), is here applied to QCD critical dynamics, inspired by studies of universal scaling of correlation functions in condensed matter systems. This enables model-independent guidance for the beam energy scan at RHIC searching for the QCD critical point.
