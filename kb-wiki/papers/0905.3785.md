# Candidate MKiD nucleus 106Rh in triaxial relativistic mean-field approach with time-odd fields

> The configuration-fixed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field approach is extended by including time-odd fields and applied to study the candidate multiple chiral doublets (MKiD) nucleus 106Rh. The energy contribution from time-odd fields and microscopical evaluation of center-of-mass correction as well as the modification of triaxial deformation parameters beta, gamma due to the time-odd fields are investigated. The contributions of the time-odd fields to the total energy are 0.1-0.3 MeV and they modify slightly the gamma values. However, the previously predicted multiple chiral doublets still exist.

## Key claim

The previously predicted existence of multiple chiral doublets (MχD) in 106Rh is confirmed even after including time-odd fields, which contribute only 0.1-0.3 MeV to the total energy and slightly modify the triaxial deformation parameters, without destroying the chiral doublet structure.

## Method

The configuration-fixed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach was extended by including time-odd fields (space-like components of vector meson fields) and applied to 106Rh. The Dirac equation was solved by expanding spinors in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 12 major shells, while meson fields were expanded with 10 major shells; pairing correlations were neglected due to the unpaired valence neutron and proton. A configuration-fixed quadrupole moment constraint calculation through β² was performed to obtain the potential energy surface for each valence nucleon configuration, with the same configuration guaranteed via the parallel-transport method.

## Key numbers

- Time-odd fields contribute 0.1-0.3 MeV to the total energy for all configurations in 106Rh
- Ground state (configuration A: ν2d⁵/₂ ⊗ π1g⁻³/₉/₂) total energy: -905.33 MeV with time-odd fields, compared to -903.92 MeV without (experimental value: -906.72 MeV)
- Center-of-mass correction energy for configuration A: -7.64 MeV (microscopic) versus -6.50 MeV (phenomenological)
- Triaxial deformation parameters for configuration A: β = 0.28, γ = 24.3° with time-odd fields (β = 0.27, γ = 24.7° without)
- Time-odd fields reduce the triaxial deformation parameter γ by up to 0.5°
- Energy contribution from time-odd fields for specific configurations: -0.22 MeV (A), -0.11 MeV (B), -0.16 MeV (C), -0.25 MeV (D), -0.27 MeV (E), -0.12 MeV (F)

## Context

This work extends the configuration-fixed constrained triaxial RMF approach of Meng (2006) by including time-odd fields, which were previously neglected under time-reversal invariance assumptions. The study confirms the prediction of multiple chiral doublets in 106Rh originally made by Peng (2008) using the same approach without time-odd fields. The results enable future searches for candidate chiral doublet bands with the νh³₁₁/₂ ⊗ πg⁻³/₉/₂ configuration, as one pair of doublet bands with νh¹₁₁/₂ ⊗ πg⁻³/₉/₂ configuration has already been observed experimentally by Wang (2008).
