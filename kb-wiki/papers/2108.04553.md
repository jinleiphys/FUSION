# Advancement of Photospheric Radius Expansion and Clocked Type-I X-Ray Burst Models with the New $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)^{25}$Al Reaction Rate Determined at Gamow Energy

> We report the first (in)elastic scattering measurement of $^{25}\mathrm{Al}+p$ with the capability to select and measure in a broad energy range the proton resonances in $^{26}$Si contributing to the $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ reaction at type I x-ray burst energies. We measured spin-parities of four resonances above the $α$ threshold of $^{26}$Si that are found to strongly impact the $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ rate. The new rate advances a state-of-the-art model to remarkably reproduce light curves of the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster with mean deviation $<9$ % and permits us to discover a strong correlation between the He abundance in the accreting envelope of photospheric radius expansion burster and the dominance of $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ branch.

## Key claim

The new $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)^{25}$Al reaction rate, determined from the first (in)elastic scattering measurement of $^{25}$Al+$p$ with resonance properties measured at Gamow energies, advances a state-of-the-art X-ray burst model to reproduce light curves of the GS 1826−24 clocked burster with a mean deviation less than 9%, and reveals a strong correlation between the helium abundance in the accreting envelope of photospheric radius expansion bursters and the dominance of the $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ branch.

## Method

The authors performed the first (in)elastic scattering measurement of $^{25}$Al+$p$ using a radioactive ion beam of $^{25}$Al at 142±1 MeV delivered to a CH$_2$ target at the CRIB separator of the University of Tokyo. Recoiling protons were measured with silicon detector telescopes at central angles of 0°, 20°, and 23°, and an array of NaI detectors was used to identify inelastic contributions via γ-ray detection; the excitation function was analyzed with R-matrix calculations using AZURE2 to determine resonance parameters, and the $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ rate was derived using the broad-resonance approximation with α-spectroscopic factors inferred from the mirror nucleus $^{26}$Mg via isospin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The $^{25}$Al beam had an average intensity of $2.0×10^5$ particles per second and a purity of typically 70%.
- The $E_{c.m.}$ resolution of the excitation function was 30–90 keV (FWHM) for the forward-angle silicon telescope.
- The new $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ rate differs from the Hauser-Feshbach (NON-SMOKER) rate by a factor of approximately 10 from about 0.4 to 1 GK, and varies up to a factor of about 160 at 3 GK.
- The mean deviation between the modeled and observed light curves of the GS 1826−24 clocked burster is less than 9%.
- The inelastic scattering contribution was less than 12% of the elastic scattering.
- The NaI detector array had an average energy resolution of 13.5% in full width at half maximum for 662-keV γ rays and covered 20% of the total solid angle.

## Context

This work extends sensitivity studies by Cyburt et al. (2016) and Jacobs et al. (2020) that identified the $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ rate as the most decisive αp-process reaction in sd-shell nuclei influencing X-ray burst light curves. The new rate resolves discrepancies between the Hauser-Feshbach rate from the REACLIB v2.2 compilation (Cyburt et al., 2010) and the rate from a $^{28}$Si$(p,t)^{26}$Si measurement by Matic et al. (2011), and improves upon the first direct measurement of $^{22}$Mg$(α,p)$ by Randhawa et al. (2020) which could not measure within the Gamow window. The results enable more reliable modeling of photospheric radius expansion bursters and clocked bursters, providing constraints on neutron star compactness and the equation of state.
