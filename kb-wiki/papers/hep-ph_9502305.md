# Chiral Symmetry and Scalar Meson in Hadron and Nuclear Physics

> After giving a short introduction to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with an anomaly term, we show the importance of the scalar-scalar correlation in the low-energy hadron dynamics, which correlation may be summarized by a scalar-isoscalar meson, the sigma meson. The discussion is based on the chiral quark model with the sigma-meson degrees of freedom. Possible experiments are proposed to produce the elusive meson in a nucleus and detect it. In relation to a precursory soft mode for the chiral transition, the reason is clarified why the dynamic properties of the superconductor may be described by the diffusive time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) equation. We indicate the chiral symmetry plays a significant role also in nuclei; one may say that the stability of nuclei is due to the chiral symmetry of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the scalar-isoscalar sigma meson, while elusive and controversial, plays a significant role in low-energy hadron dynamics and nuclear stability, with the caveat that its existence and properties remain debated due to its huge decay width (400-1000 MeV) and possible mixing with glueballs.

## Method

The author uses the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a U_A(1) anomaly term to describe dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and calculate meson properties. The model is extended to a chiral quark model incorporating the sigma meson degrees of freedom, where baryon masses are computed using a constituent quark model with spin-spin interactions and confinement, and the scalar charge matrix Q_ij is derived from the gap equation to evaluate quark contents of baryons.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: M_u = M_d = 335 MeV, M_s = 527 MeV, consistent with baryon magnetic moments
- Sigma meson mass predicted: m_sigma = 668 MeV (with a second scalar m_sigma' = 1348 MeV)
- Sigma meson decay width: Gamma_sigma->2pi ~ 900 MeV, comparable to its mass
- Phase shift analysis gives alternative values: Re m_sigma = 505 +/- 10 MeV, width = 497 +/- 5 MeV for f_0(500)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: Sigma_piN = 49 +/- 7 MeV (theory) versus empirical 45 +/- 10 MeV
- Non-perturbative quark condensate: <bar u u>^NP = -(245)^3 MeV^3, compared to empirical -(225 +/- 25)^3 MeV^3
- Strangeness content ratio: y = 2<bar s s>_N / <bar u u + bar d d>_N = 0.12
- Convergence radius condition: m_NG^2 < m_sigma^2 / (3 sqrt(3)), giving critical m_sigma ~ 1.1 GeV for kaon sector

## Context

This work extends the earlier NJL model studies by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) that provided a unified description of low-energy QCD phenomena including dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, the U_A(1) anomaly, and explicit SU_V(3) breaking. The paper addresses the controversy over the sigma meson's existence, referencing phase shift analyses by Kaminski et al. that claim a pole at 505 MeV, and a preliminary KEK experiment by Shimizu showing a bump around 600 MeV. The results enable predictions for detecting the sigma meson in hot/dense nuclear matter through relativistic heavy ion collisions, where its mass is expected to decrease and its width to narrow near chiral restoration.
