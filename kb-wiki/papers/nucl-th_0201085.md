# Liquid Drop Model with Different Neutron versus Proton Deformations

> The nuclear binding energies for 28 nuclei including several isotopic chains with masses ranging from A=64 to A=226 were evaluated using the Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and the Extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. The neutron and proton density distributions are assumed in the form of Fermi functions the parameters of which are determined so as to minimize the total binding energy of any given nucleus. The present study is restricted to quadrupole shapes, but the neutron and proton density distributions are free to have different deformations. A simple expression for the variation of the nuclear energy with the neutron--proton deformation difference is derived.

## Key claim

The paper derives a simple parabolic correction term for the nuclear binding energy that accounts for different quadrupole deformations of neutron and proton density distributions, but the derivation is restricted to the Extended Thomas-Fermi approximation with the Skyrme SIII force and assumes only quadrupole shapes, so the results are approximate and may not hold for other Skyrme parametrizations or higher multipole deformations.

## Method

The authors used the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation with the Skyrme SIII effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to compute binding energies for 28 even-even nuclei across several isotopic chains from Ni to Th. Neutron and proton density distributions were parameterized as Fermi functions with independent quadrupole deformations, and the parameters were determined by minimizing the total binding energy. The binding energy was then fitted as a function of the global deformation parameter α and the difference Δβ̃ = β̃_n − β̃_p between neutron and proton quadrupole deformation parameters.

## Key numbers

- The stiffness parameter a(A,I,α) for the parabolic term in (β̃_n − β̃_p)² has the form a₀(A,I) = c₁ (A^{1/3})ⁿ with c₁ = 1.21 MeV, n = 4, and the deformation-dependent factor [1 − c₂ α (1 + c₃ I)] with c₂ = 3.0, c₃ = 3.0.
- The additional term in the nuclear mass formula increases as A^{4/3}.
- Estimates obtained with Skyrme SII and SVII forces are identical to those for SIII within 10%.
- The effect of the neutron-proton deformation difference on the binding energy was found to reach approximately 1.5 MeV in prior HFB calculations for fissioning nuclei (Be00), with fluctuations of the order of 1 MeV.
- Prior HFB calculations (Be00) showed that the relative difference between neutron and proton multipole deformations exceeds often 10% for ²³²Th, ²³⁶U, ²³⁸U and ²⁴⁰Pu.
- The radii of spherical neutron and proton distributions were parameterized as R₀ₚ = 1.237 (1 − 0.157 I − 0.646/A) A^{1/3} fm and R₀ₙ = 1.176 (1 + 0.250 I + 2.806/A) A^{1/3} fm, based on RMF calculations of Wa98.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic approach to nuclear masses by adding a term that accounts for different neutron and proton deformations, which was previously neglected in standard liquid-drop and droplet models (Sm95, Mo95, St99). The authors build on self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolubov calculations by Be00 for ²³²Th, ²³⁶U, ²³⁸U and ²⁴⁰Pu, which showed that the deformation difference can shift ground-state and saddle-point energies by up to 0.5 MeV. The derived formula enables future generalization of Strutinsky-type calculations to allow independent neutron and proton multipole deformations, which could improve predictions of nuclear masses, fission barriers, α-decay lifetimes, and spontaneous-fission lifetimes.
