# CP Violation Problem

> The strong CP violation problem has a long history emanating from its discovery 60 years ago in the decay of neutral kaons and subsequent experimental and theoretical studies over several decades. We review herein experimental data that observe indirect CP violation of the order of $\sim10^{-3}$, as well as the discovery of direct CP violation of the order of $\sim10^{-6}$. Despite improved experimental methods over the past half a century, the original CP violation numbers have remained the same. Verification of the CP violation in the decay of charged kaons was also observed. Data reflecting CP violation in the decays of $B$ and $D$ mesons have become very important and are also discussed in this review. The question of CP violation only with the participation of $s\bar{s}$, $c\bar{c}$, and $b\bar{b}$ quarks in the framework of the Standard Model or beyond it and its small magnitude remains open.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the history and current status of CP violation, noting that despite six decades of improved experimental methods, the original measured values of indirect CP violation (order ~10⁻³) and direct CP violation (order ~10⁻⁶) have remained unchanged, and the question of why CP violation is so small and whether it occurs only within the Standard Model or beyond remains open.

## Method

The authors present a narrative review of CP violation, beginning with the theoretical framework of CP parity in neutral kaon systems, the formalism of K⁰–K̄⁰ oscillations, and the CKM matrix parametrization. They then summarize key experimental results from the original 1964 Brookhaven experiment by Christenson, Cronin, Fitch, and Turlay that discovered indirect CP violation, followed by subsequent measurements of direct CP violation in kaon decays and CP violation in B and D meson decays from BaBar at SLAC and experiments at KEK.

## Context

This review extends the line of work beginning with the discovery of CP violation by Christenson, Cronin, Fitch, and Turlay (1964) in neutral kaon decays at Brookhaven, and the subsequent theoretical framework by Kobayashi and Maskawa (1973) linking CP violation to a third quark generation via the CKM matrix. The paper also discusses the Sakharov conditions (1967) that make CP violation a necessary ingredient for the matter-antimatter asymmetry of the Universe. The review enables future searches for CP violation beyond the Standard Model, particularly in systems involving s, c, and b quarks, and leaves open the question of why the observed CP violation is so small.
