# Mott-Hagedorn Resonance Gas and Lattice QCD Results

## Key claim
The paper presents a combined effective model that reproduces the equation of state of hadronic matter from lattice QCD simulations, but the authors acknowledge that the high-temperature phase of the earlier Mott-Hagedorn model is unphysical because it leaves light hadrons below a mass threshold of 1 GeV unaffected, accidentally mimicking the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of quarks and gluons. The new model introduces a state-dependent hadron resonance width to describe the Mott dissociation of all hadrons, combined with a Polyakov-loop improved Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model plus perturbative corrections to O(α_s), to achieve a physically consistent description across the transition region.

## Method
The authors construct a thermodynamic model by combining a Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas (MHRG) with a PNJL model for the quark-gluon subsystem. The MHRG component uses a state-dependent hadron resonance width Γ_i(T) based on the inverse collision time scale from a binary collision approximation with the geometrical Povh-Hüfner law, where temperature-dependent hadronic radii diverge near chiral restoration. The PNJL model is improved by adding perturbative QCD corrections to O(α_s) for the high-momentum region above the three-momentum cutoff Λ. The total pressure is the sum of the MHRG pressure, the mean-field PNJL pressure, and the perturbative correction pressure.

## Key numbers
- Mass threshold for light hadrons unaffected by broadening: m_0 = 1 GeV
- Parameters for the earlier heuristic width ansatz: C_Γ = 10^{-4}, N_m = 2.5, N_T = 6.5, T_H = 165 MeV
- Vacuum pion radius: r_π = 0.59 fm; vacuum nucleon radius: r_N = 0.74 fm; nucleon hard core radius: r_0 = 0.45 fm
- The hadron resonance gas description matches lattice QCD up to a temperature of approximately 170 MeV, corresponding to the pseudocritical temperature of the chiral phase transition
- The MHRG pressure vanishes at T ~ 250 MeV, above which quark-gluon degrees of freedom dominate
- The transition region where both hadronic and quark-gluon contributions are relevant: 170 ≤ T [MeV] ≤ 250

## Context
This work extends the earlier Mott-Hagedorn resonance gas model of Blaschke (2003) by replacing the heuristic width ansatz with a state-dependent width based on collision time scales from Blaschke (2011), and by adding a PNJL model with perturbative O(α_s) corrections to describe the quark-gluon phase. The model reproduces lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2009). It builds on the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck equation of state approach for Mott dissociation in the NJL model by Hüfner (1994) and the temperature-dependent pion radius from the NJL model by Hippe (1995). The approach enables a unified thermodynamic description of the hadron-to-quark-matter transition that properly accounts for the dissociation of all hadronic resonances via the Mott effect, rather than only heavy resonances as in earlier models.
