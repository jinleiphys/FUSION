# Electromagnetic characteristics of $A \leq 3$ physical and lattice nuclei

> We analyze the quark-mass dependence of electromagnetic properties of two and three-nucleon states. To that end, we apply the pionless effective field theory to experimental data and numerical lattice calculations which simulate QCD at pion masses of 450~MeV and 806~MeV. At the physical pion mass, we postdict the magnetic moment of helium-3, $μ_{^3He}=-2.13~$nNM, and the magnetic polarizability of deuterium, $β_D=7.33~10^{-2}~$fm$^3$. Magnetic polarizabilities of helium-3, $β_{^3He}=9.7~10^{-4}~$fm$^3$, and the triton, $β_{^3H}=8.2~10^{-4}~$fm$^3$, are predictions. Postdictions of the effective theory for the magnetic moments are found consistent with QCD simulations at 806~MeV pion mass while our EFT result $β_D=2.92~10^{-2}~$fm$^3$ was not extracted from the lattice. The deuteron would thus be relatively pliable compared to a three-nucleon state for which we postdict $β_{^3H}=3.9~10^{-5}~$fm$^3$. At $m_π=450~$MeV, the magnetic moment of the triton is predicted, $μ_{^3He}=-2.15(5)~$nNM, based on a conjecture of its binding energy, $B_{^3H}\cong 30$~MeV. For all three pion masses, we compare the point-charge radii of the two and three-nucleon bound states. The sensitivity of the electromagnetic properties to the Coulomb interaction between protons is studied in anticipation of lattice calculations with dynamical QED.

## Key claim

The paper uses pionless effective field theory (EFT) to analyze the quark-mass dependence of electromagnetic properties of two- and three-nucleon systems, but the predictions for magnetic polarizabilities at unphysical pion masses are not directly extracted from lattice QCD calculations; instead, the EFT results for the deuteron magnetic polarizability at 806 MeV pion mass (β_D = 2.92 × 10⁻² fm³) were not extracted from the lattice, and the triton magnetic moment at 450 MeV pion mass (μ_{³He} = -2.15(5) nNM) is based on a conjecture of its binding energy (B_{³H} ≅ 30 MeV).

## Method

The authors applied pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) to experimental data and lattice QCD simulations at pion masses of 450 MeV and 806 MeV. They solved the Schrödinger equation for two- and three-nucleon bound states using the effective-interaction hyperspherical-harmonic (EIHH) method and the refined resonating-group (RGM) method, with the strong interaction parameters fitted to binding energies and scattering lengths, and magnetic parameters fitted to magnetic moments and transition matrix elements. The Coulomb interaction between protons was treated perturbatively, and the regulator dependence was studied by varying the cutoff Λ from 2 fm⁻¹ to 15 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of helium-3 at physical pion mass: μ_{³He} = -2.13 nNM (postdiction)
- Magnetic polarizability of deuterium at physical pion mass: β_D = 7.33 × 10⁻² fm³ (postdiction)
- Magnetic polarizability of helium-3 at physical pion mass: β_{³He} = 9.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (prediction)
- Magnetic polarizability of triton at physical pion mass: β_{³H} = 8.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (prediction)
- Magnetic polarizability of deuterium at 806 MeV pion mass: β_D = 2.92 × 10⁻² fm³ (EFT result not extracted from lattice)
- Magnetic polarizability of triton at 806 MeV pion mass: β_{³H} = 3.9 × 10⁻⁵ fm³ (postdiction)
- Magnetic moment of helium-3 at 450 MeV pion mass: μ_{³He} = -2.15(5) nNM (prediction based on conjectured binding energy B_{³H} ≅ 30 MeV)
- Triton magnetic moment at 450 MeV pion mass: μ_{³H} = 3 ± 0.3 nNM (constraint from binding-energy correlation)
- Point-charge radius of deuteron at physical pion mass: r_ch = 1.55(24) fm (EFT result; experimental value 1.97 fm)
- Point-charge radius of triton at physical pion mass: r_ch = 1.16(23) fm (EFT result; experimental value 1.55 fm)
- Point-charge radius of helium-3 at physical pion mass: r_ch = 1.30(28) fm (EFT result; experimental value 1.78 fm)

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)), as developed by van Kolck (1998), Chen (1999), Kaplan (1998), and Bedaque (1999), to analyze electromagnetic properties of light nuclei across different quark masses. It builds on previous lattice QCD calculations by Beane (2015) and Orginos (2015) for two-nucleon systems at unphysical pion masses, and by Chang (2015) for three-nucleon systems at 806 MeV pion mass. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations with dynamical QED to be compared with EFT predictions, particularly for the Coulomb interaction effects on charge radii and magnetic properties of few-nucleon systems.
