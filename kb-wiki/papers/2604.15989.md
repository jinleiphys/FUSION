# Charged-current quasielastic-like neutrino scattering from $^{12}$C in the coherent density fluctuation model with two-nucleon emission

> The quasielastic cross-sections of charged-current neutrino and antineutrino scattering on $^{12}$C are calculated using the coherent density fluctuation model with a relativistic effective mass $m_N^* =0.8 m_N$ (CDFM$_{M^*}$). The model explicitly considers the modification of the relativistic effective mass of the nucleon within the relativistic mean field (RMF) model of nuclear matter. In addition, our calculations include neutrino-induced two-particle emission processes, which are evaluated within the RMF model of nuclear matter. Utilizing the CDFM$_{M^*}$, we provide predictions for the neutrino and antineutrino cross sections of $^{12}$C, which have been observed in accelerator experiments, such as MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA. Also, we analyze the axial form factor value for the excitation of the $Δ$ at zero momentum transfer (commonly denoted as $C^A_5 (0)$) which is important for the treatment of the $Δ$ current in the meson-exchange currents (MEC) calculation. In addition, the quasielastic results obtained within CDFM$_{M^*}$ model are thoroughly evaluated for different regions of the momentum transfer.

# Charged-current quasielastic-like neutrino scattering from \(^{12}\)C in the coherent density fluctuation model with two-nucleon emission

## Key claim

The CDFM\(_{M^*}\) model, using a relativistic effective mass \(m_N^* = 0.8 m_N\), provides predictions for neutrino and antineutrino charged-current quasielastic cross sections on \(^{12}\)C that agree with MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA data, but only when the momentum transfer \(q\) exceeds approximately 0.4 GeV/\(c\); at very forward angles where \(q \leq 0.4\) GeV/\(c\) the scaling approach is inappropriate and the model overestimates the data.

## Method

The authors compute double-differential cross sections for charged-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering on \(^{12}\)C using the coherent density fluctuation model with a relativistic effective mass \(M^* = 0.8\) (CDFM\(_{M^*}\)), which convolves the relativistic Fermi gas scaling function with a weight function derived from the empirical 3-parameter Fermi density distribution of \(^{12}\)C. Two-particle–two-hole meson-exchange current contributions are included using a semi-empirical parametrization based on the relativistic mean field model of nuclear matter, with the \(\Delta\) axial form factor at zero momentum transfer \(C_5^A(0)\) varied between 0.89 and 1.2 to produce an uncertainty band.

## Key numbers

* The relativistic effective mass is fixed to \(M^* = m_N^* / m_N = 0.8\) in all calculations.
* The Fermi constant used is \(G = 1.166 \times 10^{-11}\) MeV\(^{-2}\) and \(\cos\theta_c = 0.975\).
* The 2p–2h MEC contribution accounts for approximately 20–25% of the total response at its peak for MiniBooNE kinematics.
* For T2K kinematics, the 2p–2h MEC contribution is around 10%, with a maximum of 25% at very forward angles near the QE peak.
* The scaling model is considered inappropriate for momentum transfers \(q \leq 0.4\) GeV/\(c\).
* For T2K at \(\cos\theta = 0.99\), the condition \(q > 0.4\) GeV/\(c\) is satisfied for all muon momenta when neutrino energy \(E_\nu > 2.84\) GeV.
* The \(\Delta\) effective mass is estimated as \(M^*_{\Delta} = 1042\) MeV/\(c^2\) under the assumption of universal coupling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDFM\(_{M^*}\) studies (Ivanov and Antonov, 2024) by incorporating two-nucleon emission via meson-exchange currents computed within the relativistic mean field model of nuclear matter, rather than the relativistic Fermi gas model used earlier. The approach builds upon the superscaling analysis with relativistic effective mass (SuSAM) framework developed by Martinez et al. (2017) and Amaro et al. (2018), and the SuSAM-v2 model. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for accelerator-based oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA, though the authors note that a prospective enhancement would be to evaluate the 2p–2h MEC contributions entirely within the CDFM\(_{M^*}\) model rather than combining two distinct nuclear models.
