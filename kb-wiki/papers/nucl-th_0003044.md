# Momentum conservation and local field corrections for the response of interacting Fermi gases

> We reanalyze the recently derived response function for interacting systems in relaxation time approximation respecting density, momentum and energy conservation. We find that momentum conservation leads exactly to the local field corrections for both cases respecting only density conservation and respecting density and energy conservation. This rewriting simplifies the former formulae dramatically. We discuss the small wave vector expansion and find that the response function shows a high frequency dependence of $ω^{-5}$ which allows to fulfill higher order sum rules. The momentum conservation also resolves a puzzle about the conductivity which should only be finite in multicomponent systems.

# Momentum conservation and local field corrections for the response of interacting Fermi gases

## Key claim

Momentum conservation in the relaxation time approximation for interacting Fermi gases leads exactly to local field corrections of the same form regardless of whether only density conservation or both density and energy conservation are included, which simplifies the previously derived response function formulae dramatically. The authors show that this momentum conservation resolves a puzzle about conductivity, which should only be finite in multicomponent systems, by producing a divergent static conductivity for the single-component case.

## Method

The authors start from the quantum kinetic equation for the density operator in relaxation time approximation, where relaxation is considered with respect to a local equilibrium distribution function characterized by local chemical potential, local temperature, and local momentum of mass motion. They derive density, current, and energy response functions for an interacting quantum system respecting density, momentum, and energy conservation, and express these in terms of polarization functions and their higher moments obtained by integration over the chemical potential. The key result is obtained by rewriting the inverse polarization functions to show that the inclusion of momentum conservation adds a term that is identical for both the density-conserving and the density-plus-energy-conserving cases.

## Key numbers

- The high-frequency limit of the imaginary part of the polarization function including momentum, density, and energy conservation scales as $\omega^{-5}$: $\lim_{\omega\to\infty}{\rm Im}\Pi^{\rm n,j,E}({\bf q},\omega) = -\frac{n^2 q^4}{\omega^5 \tau m^2}\left ({1\over \partial_\mu n}-{2 E\over n^2}\right )$
- The Mermin formula (density conservation only) gives a high-frequency scaling of $\omega^{-3}$: $\lim_{\omega\to\infty}{\rm Im}\Pi^{\rm n}({\bf q},\omega) = -\frac{n^2 q^4}{\omega^3 \tau m^2}$

## Context

This work extends the Mermin formula (Mermin, 1970) for the response function in relaxation time approximation, which respected only density conservation, by including momentum and energy conservation. The authors had previously derived the full response functions with multiple conservation laws (Morawetz and Fuhrmann, 1999), and here they show that momentum conservation produces local field corrections of the same form as those discussed in the literature (Roth et al., 1999; Singwi et al., 1968; Mermin, 1990). The result enables simpler explicit formulae for the dynamical local field factor and modified relaxation rate, whereas previous work (Roth et al., 1999; Roth and Wierling, 1998) could only provide these in static approximation with complicated integrals. The $\omega^{-5}$ high-frequency behavior allows fulfillment of higher order sum rules, which the authors indicate will be the subject of a forthcoming work.
