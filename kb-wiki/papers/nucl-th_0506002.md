# Ellipsoidal flows in relativistic hydrodynamics of finite systems

> A new class of 3D anisotropic analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics with constant pressure is found. We analyse, in particular, solutions corresponding to ellipsoidally symmetric expansion of finite systems into vacuum. They can be utilized for relativistic description of the system evolution in thermodynamic region near the softest point and at the final stage of the hydrodynamic expansion in A+A collisions. The solutions can be used also for testing of numerical hydrodynamic codes solving relativistic hydrodynamic equations for anisotropic expansion of finite systems

# Ellipsoidal flows in relativistic hydrodynamics of finite systems

## Key claim
The paper presents a new class of 3D anisotropic analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics under the condition of constant pressure (the "softest point" where the speed of sound is zero), which can describe ellipsoidally symmetric expansion of finite systems into vacuum. The solutions are limited to the regime where pressure is constant and, for asymptotically large times, require setting the constant pressure to zero to avoid non-physical negative energy density.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a perfect fluid and impose constant pressure, reducing the equations to an accelerationless flow condition and an energy conservation equation. They construct an ansatz for the 4-velocity field with ellipsoidal symmetry, where the velocity components are linear in the spatial coordinates with time-dependent coefficients, and derive ordinary differential equations for these coefficients. The general solutions for the velocity field, energy density, and particle density are obtained analytically, with the energy density expressed as an arbitrary function of scaled coordinates divided by a product of time-dependent factors.

## Key numbers
- The velocity field is given by \( v_i = a_i(t) x_i / t \) with \( a_i(t) = t / (t + T_i) \), where \( T_i \) are three integration constants with dimension of time.
- The energy density solution has the form \( \varepsilon + p_0 = F_\varepsilon(x_1/(t+T_1), x_2/(t+T_2), x_3/(t+T_3)) / [(t+T_1)(t+T_2)(t+T_3)] \).
- For Gaussian initial profiles, the spatial radii at late times (\( \tau \gg T_k \)) are approximately \( R_i \approx \tau / b_\varepsilon \).
- In the non-relativistic limit (\( v^2 \ll 1 \)), the energy density takes the Gaussian form \( \varepsilon + p_0 \simeq (C_\varepsilon / \prod_i (t+T_i)) \exp(-b_\varepsilon^2 \sum a_i^2 x_i^2 / t^2) \).

## Context
This work extends earlier spherically symmetric Hubble-like solutions for relativistic hydrodynamics (Chiu, year not specified; Csorgo, year not specified) and the cylindrically symmetric, boost-invariant solution with constant pressure proposed by Biro (year not specified). The new ellipsoidal solutions address the need for asymmetric flow descriptions in non-central heavy-ion collisions, where the boost-invariant approximation fails at SPS and even RHIC energies (Akkelin, Mun, and Sinyukov, year not specified; Busza, year not specified). The solutions can be used to describe matter evolution during the deconfinement phase transition and the final stage of hadron system expansion, and serve as test cases for numerical relativistic hydrodynamic codes.
