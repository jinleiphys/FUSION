# Dynamical Properties of Heavy-Ion Collisions from the Photon-Photon Intensity Correlations

> We consider here the bremsstrahlung emission of photons at low and intermediate energies $ E_{lab}\le 1000 MeV/u$ of the projectile. and derive expressions more general than previous results obtained by Neuhauser which were limited to the case of isotropic systems. We find that the two-photon correlation function strongly depends not only on the space-time properties of the collision region but also on the dynamics of the proton-neutron scattering process in nuclear matter. As a consequence of polarisation correlations it turns out that for a purely chaotic source the intercept of the correlation function of photons can reach the value $3$ (as compared with the maximum value $2$ for isotropic systems). Furthermore even for ``hard" photons ($E_γ > 25 MeV$) the maximum of the correlation function can reach the value of $2$ in contrast with the value of $1.5$ derived by Neuhauser for this case. The formulae obtained in this paper which include also the possible presence of a coherent component can be used as a basis for a systematic analysis of photon intensity-interferometry experiments.

## Key claim

The two-photon correlation function for bremsstrahlung photons in heavy-ion collisions depends not only on the space-time geometry of the source but also on the dynamics of the proton-neutron scattering process in nuclear matter, specifically on the anisotropy of the momentum transfer distribution; the intercept can reach values up to 3 for a purely chaotic source (compared to the maximum of 2 for isotropic systems) and up to 2 for "hard" photons (Eγ > 25 MeV), in contrast to the value of 1.5 derived by Neuhauser for the isotropic case.

## Method

The authors model hard bremsstrahlung photons as arising from independent proton-neutron collisions in the early stage of a heavy-ion reaction, using a non-relativistic recoilless bremsstrahlung formula for the current. They compute the single and double inclusive photon cross-sections via a generating functional formalism, averaging over random space-time positions of the collisions and over fluctuations of the proton momentum transfer (including Fermi motion). The two-current correlator is expressed in terms of two dynamical parameters σ and δ that characterize the transverse and longitudinal components of the momentum transfer distribution, and the correlation function is derived for both fully chaotic and partially coherent sources.

## Key numbers

- The intercept of the correlation function for a purely chaotic source can reach the value 3 (compared to a maximum of 2 for isotropic systems).
- For "hard" photons (Eγ > 25 MeV), the maximum of the correlation function can reach the value 2, in contrast to the value of 1.5 derived by Neuhauser for this case.
- The energy range considered is Elab ≤ 1000 MeV/u for the projectile.
- For a projectile energy of 45 MeV/u (slope parameter E0 = 18 MeV), the characteristic stopping time is τ = 5.6 fm/c and the stopping length is l = 1.7 fm.
- The intercept for hard photons (|f̃(2k)|² ≪ 1) lies in the range 1 ≤ C₂(k,k) ≤ 2, wider than the range 1 ≤ C₂(k,k) ≤ 1.5 obtained by Neuhauser.

## Context

This work extends the earlier results of Neuhauser, which were limited to isotropic systems, by incorporating the axial symmetry of heavy-ion collisions around the beam direction and the dynamics of proton-neutron scattering. The formalism generalizes the photon intensity interferometry method (Hanbury-Brown and Twiss) to include polarization correlations and a possible coherent component, enabling experimental extraction of both the space-time geometry of the early collision stage and the dynamical parameters σ, δ, and the chaoticity parameter λ from photon correlation data and angular distributions.
