# Cluster Reducibility of Multiquark Operators and Tetraquark-Adequate QCD Sum Rules

> If connecting properties of a multiquark hadron with those exhibited by its constituents, QCD sum rules inferred along the routes of traditional wisdom necessarily involve contributions not related at all to and thus not presenting information about multiquarks. Realizing this deficiency, we propose to increase, for the example of tetraquarks, the predictive power of the QCD sum-rule formalism by disposal of all of the unwanted contributions from the very beginning; this move is easily accomplished by subjecting the contributions to our perspicuous selection criterion.

# Cluster Reducibility of Multiquark Operators and Tetraquark-Adequate QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim

Traditional QCD sum-rule approaches to multiquark hadrons necessarily include factorizable contributions that carry no information about multiquark states; the authors propose a selection criterion to discard all such unwanted contributions from the outset, thereby constructing tetraquark-adequate QCD sum rules that exclusively retain "tetraquark-phile" diagrams capable of developing four-quark singularities.

## Method

The authors analyze four-point correlation functions of colour-singlet quark-bilinear currents for flavour-exotic tetraquarks (four mutually different flavours), categorizing them into flavour-preserving (direct) and flavour-reordering (recombination) types. They apply the Landau equations to determine which Feynman diagrams can develop branch cuts at the four-quark threshold ŝ = (m_a + m_b + m_c + m_d)², establishing that only diagrams of at least order α_s² (involving two or more internal gluons) qualify as tetraquark-phile. The factorizable contributions are identified and discarded, leaving QCD sum rules that relate only tetraquark-phile QCD-level contributions to hadron-level tetraquark poles and nonfactorizable mesonic contributions.

## Key numbers

- The four-quark threshold branch point for tetraquark singularities is given by ŝ = (m_a + m_b + m_c + m_d)², where m_a, m_b, m_c, m_d are the masses of the constituent (anti)quarks.
- Only perturbative contributions of at least order α_s² (Figs. 34(c) and 1213(c)) may support four-quark singularities and contribute to tetraquark pole development.
- The strong coupling is defined as α_s ≡ g_s²/(4π), where g_s is the fundamental QCD coupling parameter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of studies (Lucha, Melikhov, Sazdjian, 2015-2021) on multiquark interpolating operators and clustering, particularly addressing the deficiency identified by Shifman and collaborators (1979) and by Coleman (1985) that two-point correlation functions of tetraquark operators receive factorizable contributions from ordinary mesons. The proposed tetraquark-adequate QCD sum rules enable future calculations of tetraquark masses and decay widths without contamination from non-multiquark contributions, and the authors expect the concept to extend to other multiquark varieties.
