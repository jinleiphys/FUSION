# Integrals of motion, supersymmetric quantum mechanics and dynamical supersymmetry

> The class of relativistic spin particle models reveals the `quantization' of parameters already at the classical level. The special parameter values emerge if one requires the maximality of classical global continuous symmetries. The same requirement applied to a non-relativistic particle with odd degrees of freedom gives rise to supersymmetric quantum mechanics. Coupling classical non-relativistic superparticle to a `U(1) gauge field', one can arrive at the quantum dynamical supersymmetry. This consists in supersymmetry appearing at special values of the coupling constant characterizing interaction of a system of boson and fermion but disappearing in a free case. Possible relevance of this phenomenon to high-temperature superconductivity is speculated.

# Integrals of motion, supersymmetric quantum mechanics and dynamical supersymmetry

## Key claim
The requirement of maximal classical global continuous symmetry, when applied to a non-relativistic particle with odd (Grassmann) degrees of freedom, naturally gives rise to supersymmetric quantum mechanics; coupling this classical non-relativistic superparticle to a U(1) gauge field can produce quantum dynamical supersymmetry, where supersymmetry appears only at special values of the coupling constant and is absent in the free case.

## Method
The author starts from a general Lagrangian for a non-relativistic particle with two Grassmann degrees of freedom and imposes the condition that local (rather than nonlocal in time) odd integrals of motion exist. This condition forces the two arbitrary functions V(x) and R(x) to be related as R(x)=W'(x) and V(x)=½W²(x)+C, yielding the classical analog of Witten's supersymmetric quantum mechanics. The author then adds a U(1) gauge coupling term ẋ𝒜(x)N to the supersymmetric Lagrangian, shows the classical systems are equivalent via canonical transformation, and quantizes the resulting Hamiltonian for the simplest case of constant gauge potential and linear superpotential, revealing that the quantum system can exhibit supersymmetry only at specific values of the coupling constant.

## Key numbers
- For the relativistic spin particle model (Lagrangian L_f), the special parameter values are ν=±1, at which the system has maximal classical global continuous symmetry and preserves P,T-invariance at the quantum level.
- For the Chern-Simons-type model (Lagrangian L_CS), the special parameter values are ν=±2, at which the system has maximal global continuous symmetry.
- In the quantum dynamical supersymmetry example with W(x)=εx (ε=1) and constant gauge coupling g, the quantum system is supersymmetric when the effective parameter ε̃=ε−g²/2 equals 1, which occurs at g=0 (free case); if instead ε=1+α²/2 with α≠0, supersymmetry appears at the two special coupling values g=±α.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier observation (Plyushchay, 1990s) that relativistic spin particle models exhibit "classical quantization" of parameters at special values where global continuous symmetries are maximal, as seen in the (2+1)-dimensional fermion model of Geyer, Plyushchay, and Soto (1997) and the Chern-Simons field model of Plyushchay and collaborators. The non-relativistic analysis generalizes the structure of Witten's supersymmetric quantum mechanics (1981) by showing it emerges from a symmetry-maximality principle. The quantum dynamical supersymmetry phenomenon, where supersymmetry appears only at special coupling values, is suggested as potentially relevant to high-temperature superconductivity via a U(1)-gauged version of the relativistic spin particle model, though the author notes this connection remains speculative and requires further investigation of classical and quantum symmetries in the gauged system.
