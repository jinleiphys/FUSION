# Chiral waves in quark matter

> A mini-review of non-uniform phases in quark matter is presented, with particular attention to the pion condensation, also known as chiral density waves or chiral spirals. The phase diagram of strongly-interacting matter may involve such a phase, placed on the quarkyonic island between the baryonic phase and the chirally-restored quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

This mini-review argues that non-uniform phases in quark matter, particularly pion condensation (chiral density waves or chiral spirals), are likely to exist in the phase diagram of strongly-interacting matter on the "quarkyonic island" between the baryonic phase and the chirally-restored quark-gluon plasma, but the existence and properties of these phases depend sensitively on model parameters whose values in medium are difficult to assess.

## Method

The paper reviews analytic solutions of the relativistic sigma-model for pion condensation, where the sigma and neutral pion fields take a periodic plane-wave ansatz with wave vector q, and the Dirac spectrum is solved exactly. The review also covers calculations in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, where the meson kinetic term is replaced by a more general function K(q), and discusses the magnetization properties of the neutral pion-condensed phase as well as the one-loop corrections to the mean-field treatment.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f = 93 MeV sets the amplitude of the periodic chiral field ansatz
- Pion condensation in nuclear matter is expected at approximately 1.5-2 times nuclear saturation density for symmetric and neutron matter (from Akmal 1997)
- The gradient expansion for the fermion loop converges for q less than 2m, where m is the constituent quark mass
- The effective expansion variable in the chiral field gradient expansion is q^2/(4m^2 + q^2)
- Magnetization from a pion-condensed quark core in stars can reach up to approximately 10^15 Gauss (from Kotlorz and Kutschera 1994)

## Context

This review extends the early work on pion condensation in nuclear matter by Migdal (1971, 1973) and the analytic relativistic solution by Dautry and Nyman (1979) to quark matter, connecting to modern studies of quarkyonic matter inspired by Polyakov-loop arguments from McLerran (2007) and Fukushima (2003, 2008). The paper enables further investigation of non-uniform phases as a bridge between the baryonic phase and quark-gluon plasma, with open questions about the stability of one-dimensional structures against thermodynamic fluctuations and the sensitivity of results to model parameters.
