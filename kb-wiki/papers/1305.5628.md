# Coherent π0η photoproduction on s-shell nuclei

> Coherent photoproduction of π0η on the deuteron, He3 and He4 nuclei is considered in the energy region from threshold to the lab photon energy 1.2 GeV. The transition amplitude is derived using impulse approximation. Effects of pion absorption are included by means of the Fernbach-Serber-Taylor model. Interaction of the produced η-mesons with the recoiled nucleus is taken into account for the reactions on d and He3. The corresponding ηd and ηHe3 scattering amplitudes are obtained as solutions of the few-body equations for ηNN and η-3N systems. Impact of this interaction on the differential cross section in the region of small relative η-nuclear momenta is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that coherent π⁰η photoproduction on s-shell nuclei (d, ³He, ⁴He) can be used to extract η-nuclear scattering parameters, but only for d and ³He where microscopic few-body calculations of the ηA interaction are available; for ⁴He the lack of such calculations and the surprising absence of threshold enhancement in existing data leave the η⁴He interaction poorly constrained.

## Method

The transition amplitude is derived using impulse approximation with the elementary γN → π⁰ηN operator taken from an isobar model dominated by the D₃₃(1700) resonance. Pion absorption in the final state is included via the Fernbach-Serber-Taylor model, using a classical damping factor with a mean free path determined from the πN cross section. For d and ³He, η-nuclear final-state interaction is incorporated by solving few-body equations for the ηNN and η-3N systems, yielding ηd and η³He scattering amplitudes; the ηA interaction is then added to the impulse approximation via a half-off-shell t-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The elementary γp → π⁰ηp cross section is dominated by the D₃₃(1700) resonance up to Eγ = 1.2 GeV; the ratio of spin-flip to spin-independent cross sections σK/σL = 0.60 ± 0.04 in this region.
- For the deuteron, neglecting the d-wave component reduces the total cross section by about 30% at Eγ = 1 GeV.
- The average relative velocities in the ³He(γ,π⁰η)³He reaction are vηA = 0.38 c and vπA = 0.83 c; the characteristic interaction times are tη ≈ 9.34 × 10⁻²³ s and tπ ≈ 1.62 × 10⁻²³ s.
- Three sets of ηN scattering lengths are used: set 1: aηN = (0.50 + i 0.33) fm (Wilkin); set 2: aηN = (0.75 + i 0.27) fm (Wycech); set 3: aηN = (1.03 + i 0.41) fm (Work). These yield η³He scattering lengths ranging from (1.866 + i 2.752) fm to (−3.767 + i 9.362) fm.
- The time delay due to η³He attraction is Qη = 6.67 × 10⁻²³ s, calculated using Re aη³He = 4 fm and E = 38 MeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of π⁰η photoproduction from free protons (Döring, Horn, Fix et al.) to light nuclei, motivated by new data from ELSA and MAMI (Krusche). It contrasts with earlier optical-model approaches for ηA systems (Oset, Maghrbi) by using microscopic few-body calculations for ηd and η³He (Shevchenko, FiAr). The results enable extraction of ηN scattering parameters from measured spectra near threshold, and highlight the unresolved puzzle that η⁴He shows no threshold enhancement in dd → η⁴He (Frasc, Adlarson) despite strong attraction in lighter systems.
