# Modeling alpha-nucleus elastic scattering using a velocity-dependent optical model

> We performed a least-square fit analysis to reproduce the elastic angular distributions for $α$ scattering on various nuclei form $^{12}$C to $^{208}$Pb for incident energies in the range 18 - 70 MeV using a velocity-dependent optical model. The model reproduced the experimental data well including the enhanced angular distributions in the large-angle scattering region, which is commonly known as the anomalous large angle scattering (ALAS). Our best-fit potential parameters are linear functions of incident energy. Although the ALAS effect is not present in the case of $α$ scattering on the intermediate $^{58}$Ni and heavy $^{208}$Pb nuclei, we considered these nuclei to demonstrate the effectiveness of the VDOM in describing the angular distributions for $α$ scattering on various light, intermediate and heavy nuclei. For $α$ scattering on $^{40}$Ca, we compared our results to two previous works that adopted the conventional optical model. One model reproduced the data better at low energies, while the other performed better at high energies. In contrast, the velocity-dependent model of this work described the data across the considered angular range.

## Key claim

The velocity-dependent optical model (VDOM) reproduces the enhanced elastic angular distributions (anomalous large angle scattering, ALAS) for alpha scattering on light nuclei from 12C to 40Ca across 18-70 MeV, but the paper's claim is hedged: the model's best-fit potential parameters are linear functions of incident energy, and the quality of fits improves with increasing energy for 12C, while for 40Ca the VDOM performs consistently well across the entire energy range compared to two conventional optical models, one of which works better at low energies and the other at high energies.

## Method

The authors performed least-square fits of elastic angular distributions for alpha-particle scattering on targets from 12C to 208Pb at incident energies 18-70 MeV using a velocity-dependent optical model (VDOM), which adds a gradient term proportional to the derivative of the nuclear matter density to the conventional optical model potential. The VDOM Schrodinger equation includes an isotropic function rho(r) with surface and volume components, and the potential V(r) contains real volume, imaginary volume, imaginary surface, and Coulomb terms (no spin-orbit term since the alpha has zero spin). Best-fit parameters were obtained by minimizing chi-squared values while requiring physically meaningful parameters that vary smoothly with energy, and the resulting parameters were expressed as linear functions of incident energy with correlation coefficients R.

- For 12C: no imaginary volume term needed for energies up to 27.1 MeV; at higher energies (48.7-65 MeV) W_v ranges from 4.1 to 5.1 MeV.
- For 16O: real volume depth V_v ranges from 240.1 MeV at 20.1 MeV to 207.1 MeV at 69.5 MeV; chi-squared values range from 53.0 at 20.1 MeV to 4.7 at 54.1 MeV.
- For 40Ca: real volume depth V_v ranges from 265.5 MeV at 23 MeV to 225.1 MeV at 53.9 MeV; chi-squared values range from 19.4 at 23 MeV to 0.5 at 53.9 MeV.

- For 40Ca at 53.9 MeV: VDOM chi-squared = 0.5, compared to COM (Ref. PhysRevC.18.1237) chi-squared = 7.90 and COM (Ref. PhysRevC.16.142) chi-squared = 141.22.

## Context

This work extends the velocity-dependent optical model (VDOM), previously successful for nucleon-nucleus scattering (Alameer 2022, PhysRevC.84.034618, PhysRevC.85.024606), to alpha-nucleus elastic scattering for the first time across a wide mass range. The VDOM reproduces the anomalous large angle scattering (ALAS) effect for light nuclei up to 40Ca, which previous models could only describe with energy-dependent performance: the conventional optical model of PhysRevC.18.1237 worked better at high energies while that of PhysRevC.16.142 worked better at low energies, but the VDOM works consistently across the full energy range. The results confirm the finding of Apell (1975) that ALAS disappears for nuclei heavier than 41Ca, as demonstrated for 58Ni and 208Pb. This work enables systematic application of the VDOM to alpha-nucleus scattering across a wide range of targets and energies, potentially replacing the need for multiple conventional optical models with different energy-dependent parameterizations.
