# Pion-nucleon scattering inside the Mandelstam triangle

> We study the third order pion-nucleon scattering amplitude obtained from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory inside the Mandelstam triangle. We reconstruct the pion-nucleon amplitude in the unphysical region by use of dispersion relations and determine the pertinent low-energy constants by a fit to this amplitude. A detailed comparison with values obtained from phase shift analysis is given. Our analysis leads to a pion-nucleon sigma-term of sigma(0) = 40 MeV based on the Karlsruhe partial wave analysis. We have repeated the same procedure using the latest solution of the VPI group and find a much larger value for sigma(0).

## Key claim

The third-order heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory amplitude can be reliably compared to dispersively reconstructed pion-nucleon amplitudes inside the Mandelstam triangle (the unphysical region), yielding low-energy constants that are consistent with previous determinations from physical-region data, except that the LEC \(c_2\) remains essentially undetermined due to kinematic suppression.

## Method

The authors used dispersion relations to reconstruct the real parts of the invariant amplitudes \(A^\pm(s,t)\) and \(B^\pm(s,t)\) inside the Mandelstam triangle from partial-wave phase shifts (Karlsruhe KA84 and VPI SP99) and Regge parameterization for high energies. They then fitted the third-order heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory amplitudes (with Born-term subtraction to correct analytic structure) to these dispersive amplitudes over small regions around \(\nu=0, t=0\) and \(\nu=0, t=2M^2/3\), extracting the dimension-two low-energy constants \(c_1, c_2, c_3, c_4\) and dimension-three constants \(\bar{d}_i\).

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon sigma-term: \(\sigma(0) = 40\) MeV based on the Karlsruhe partial wave analysis (KA84)
- Dimension-two LECs from combined fits (KA84): \(c_1 = (-0.81 \pm 0.12)\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_2 = (8.43 \pm 56.9)\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = (-4.70 \pm 1.16)\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = (3.40 \pm 0.04)\) GeV\(^{-1}\)
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant squared used: \(f^2 = 0.079\) (Karlsruhe analysis) and \(f^2 \simeq 0.076\) (VPI group)
- Using VPI SP99 phases, \(c_1\) becomes very large: \((-3.00\ldots -2.96)\) GeV\(^{-1}\), while \(c_4\) remains consistent at \((3.39\ldots 3.40)\) GeV\(^{-1}\)
- Adler consistency condition satisfied to within 1% for KA84, but violated by 10% for VPI SP99

## Context

This work extends the program of applying heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to pion-nucleon scattering, previously studied by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1997) and by Mojzis (1998), by moving the comparison from the physical region into the Mandelstam triangle where the amplitude is purely real and chiral predictions are expected to be more reliable. The analysis enables a cleaner determination of the dimension-two low-energy constants, though it reveals that the third-order amplitude is insufficient to pin down the dimension-three constants reliably, motivating future fourth-order calculations and studies of reactions such as \(\pi N \to \pi \pi N\).
