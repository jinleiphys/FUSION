# Event-by-Event Observables and Fluctuations

> In this talk the status and open questions of the phenomenological description of all the stages of a heavy ion reaction are highlighted. Special emphasis is put on event-by-event fluctuations and associated observables. The first part is concentrated on high RHIC and LHC energies and the second part reviews the challenges for modeling heavy ion reactions at lower beam energies in a more realistic fashion. Overall, the main conclusion is that sophisticated theoretical dynamical approaches that describe many observables in the same framework are essential for the quantitative understanding of the properties of hot and dense nuclear matter.

# Event-by-Event Observables and Fluctuations

## Key claim
Sophisticated theoretical dynamical approaches that describe many observables in the same framework are essential for the quantitative understanding of hot and dense nuclear matter, but the field still lacks a first-principles calculation of the initial energy-momentum tensor and a full microscopic understanding of hadronization.

## Method
The paper reviews the phenomenological modeling of all stages of heavy ion reactions: initial conditions (including nucleon-position fluctuations and Color Glass Condensate gluon distributions), pre-equilibrium evolution, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics in 3+1 dimensions, hadronization via Cooper-Frye sampling, and hadronic rescattering via transport approaches. The talk covers both high-energy collisions at RHIC and LHC and lower beam energies where finite net baryon density and conserved currents become important.

## Key numbers
- Higher-order flow harmonics of order n=2-6 are used to extract the shear viscosity over entropy coefficient from hydrodynamic response to fluctuating initial conditions.
- Initial state fluctuations occur on the scale of individual nucleons of size ~1 fm, while gluon distributions in a Gaussian Color Glass Condensate approach produce structures with characteristic size ~0.3 fm.
- The experimentally measured pion yield at midrapidity is roughly 300-350, constraining initial state parameters in the UrQMD hybrid approach.
- Proton transverse momentum is increased by 30% through hadronic rescattering processes.
- Approximately 10^6 events are needed for CPU-intensive full experimental analysis reproduction.

## Context
This talk from Quark Matter 2012 extends the paradigm shift that began in summer 2010, when higher flow harmonics beyond elliptic flow were recognized as sensitive to initial state fluctuations and transport coefficients. The work builds on prior approaches including Glauber and CGC-type initial state models, viscous hydrodynamic codes from multiple groups (e.g., Nonaka 2012, Huovinen 2009), and hybrid calculations such as MUSIC+UrQMD (Ryuu and Jeon). The paper enables future work on disentangling initial state fluctuations from phase transition and critical point signals at lower beam energies, particularly for the RHIC beam energy scan program and planned FAIR and NICA experiments.
