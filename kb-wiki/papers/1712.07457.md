# Short-range correlation in high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> We propose a new variational method for treating short-range repulsion of bare nuclear force for nuclei in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). In AMD, the short-range correlation is described in terms of large imaginary centroids of Gaussian wave packets of nucleon pairs in opposite signs, causing high-momentum components in nucleon pair. We superpose these AMD basis states and name this method "high-momentum AMD" (HM-AMD), which is capable of describing strong tensor correlation (Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys. (2017) 111D01). In this paper, we extend HM-AMD by including up to two kinds of nucleon pairs in each AMD basis state utilizing the cluster expansion, which produces many-body correlations involving high-momentum components. We investigate how much HM-AMD describes the short-range correlation by showing the results for $^3$H using the Argonne V4$^\prime$ central potential. It is found that HM-AMD reproduces the results of few-body calculations and also the tensor-optimized AMD. This means that HM-AMD is a powerful approach to describe the short-range correlation in nuclei. In HM-AMD, momentum directions of nucleon pairs isotropically contribute to the short-range correlation, which is different from the tensor correlation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the extended high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (HM-AMD) method, including up to two high-momentum nucleon pairs per basis state, reproduces few-body results for the short-range correlation in \(^3\)H using the Argonne V4\(^\prime\) central potential, but the method is tested only for central-force short-range correlation, not yet for tensor force.

## Method
The authors extend HM-AMD by including up to two kinds of high-momentum nucleon pairs in each AMD basis state, using the cluster expansion to generate many-body correlations. For \(^3\)H, they construct basis states with one high-momentum pair (single HM-AMD) and with two high-momentum pairs (double HM-AMD) by assigning imaginary centroid shifts \(\pm i\mathbf{D}\) and \(\pm i\mathbf{D}'\) to nucleon wave packets, superposing all basis states via the generator coordinate method (GCM). They use the Argonne V4\(^\prime\) central potential and compare results with tensor-optimized AMD (TOAMD) and Green’s function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations.

## Key numbers
- Total energy of \(^3\)H in single HM-AMD: \(-7.62\) MeV, underbound by about 1.3 MeV relative to the GFMC result of \(-8.99(1)\) MeV.
- Total energy of \(^3\)H in double HM-AMD: \(-8.90\) MeV, in close agreement with double TOAMD (\(-8.97\) MeV) and GFMC (\(-8.99(1)\) MeV).
- Optimal imaginary shift for the first high-momentum pair: \(D_z = 8\) fm, corresponding to a mean nucleon momentum of approximately \(3.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- Optimal imaginary shift for the second high-momentum pair: \(D' = 7\) fm, close to the first-pair value.
- Kinetic energy in double HM-AMD: \(36.89\) MeV; central force matrix element: \(-45.79\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the previous single-pair HM-AMD method (Myo, 2017) to include double high-momentum pairs, enabling a more complete description of short-range correlation. The results agree with those of tensor-optimized AMD (TOAMD) and GFMC calculations for \(^3\)H using the AV4\(^\prime\) central potential (Pieper, 2001). The method is designed to be combined with TOAMD into a hybrid HM-TOAMD framework (Lyu, 2017) for future application to \(p\)-shell nuclei with bare nuclear forces including both short-range repulsion and tensor components.
