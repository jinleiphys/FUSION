# Longitudinal short-distance constraints for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to $(g-2)_μ$ with large-$N_c$ Regge models

> While the low-energy part of the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) tensor can be constrained from data using dispersion relations, for a full evaluation of its contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $(g-2)_μ$ also mixed- and high-energy regions need to be estimated. Both can be addressed within the operator product expansion (OPE), either for configurations where all photon virtualities become large or one of them remains finite. Imposing such short-distance constraints (SDCs) on the HLbL tensor is thus a major aspect of a model-independent approach towards HLbL scattering. Here, we focus on longitudinal SDCs, which concern the amplitudes containing the pseudoscalar-pole contributions from $π^0$, $η$, $η'$. Since these conditions cannot be fulfilled by a finite number of pseudoscalar poles, we consider a tower of excited pseudoscalars, constraining their masses and transition form factors from Regge theory, the OPE, and phenomenology. Implementing a matching of the resulting expressions for the HLbL tensor onto the perturbative QCD quark loop, we are able to further constrain our calculation and significantly reduce its model dependence. We find that especially for the $π^0$ the corresponding increase of the HLbL contribution is much smaller than previous prescriptions in the literature would imply. Overall, we estimate that longitudinal SDCs increase the HLbL contribution by $Δa_μ^\text{LSDC}=13(6)\times 10^{-11}$. This number does not include the contribution from the charm quark, for which we find $a_μ^{c\text{-quark}}=3(1)\times 10^{-11}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the longitudinal short-distance constraints (SDCs) for the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment can be satisfied by an infinite tower of excited pseudoscalar mesons, and that the resulting increase in the HLbL contribution is much smaller than previous prescriptions in the literature would imply, with the total longitudinal SDC contribution estimated at \( \Delta a_\mu^\text{LSDC}=13(6)\times 10^{-11} \).

## Method

The authors construct large-\(N_c\) Regge models that implement the Melnikov–Vainshtein (MV) constraint by including an infinite tower of excited pseudoscalar mesons (\(\pi^0\), \(\eta\), \(\eta'\) and their radial excitations), with masses and transition form factors constrained by Regge theory, the operator product expansion (OPE), and phenomenology. They then match the resulting expressions for the HLbL tensor onto the perturbative QCD quark loop to further constrain the calculation and reduce model dependence, and evaluate the contribution to \((g-2)_\mu\) using the Bardeen–Tung–Tarrach (BTT) decomposition of the HLbL tensor.

## Key numbers

* The total longitudinal SDC contribution to the HLbL part of \((g-2)_\mu\) is \(\Delta a_\mu^\text{LSDC}=13(6)\times 10^{-11}\).
* The charm quark contribution, evaluated from the perturbative quark loop with mass \(m_c=1.27(2)\ \text{GeV}\), is \(a_\mu^{c\text{-quark}}=3(1)\times 10^{-11}\).
* The \(\pi^0\)-pole contribution from dispersive TFFs is \(a_\mu^{\pi^0\text{-pole}}=62.6^{+3.0}_{-2.5}\times 10^{-11}\).
* The \(\eta_c(1S)\) pole contribution, estimated using a VMD-type form factor, is \(a_\mu^{\eta_c(1S)}=0.8\times 10^{-11}\).
* The asymptotic limit for the doubly-virtual \(\pi^0\) TFF satisfies \(\lim_{Q^2\to\infty}Q^2F_{\pi^0\gamma^*\gamma^*}(-Q^2,-Q^2) = 1/(6\pi^2)\) when multiplied by the two-photon decay constant.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to HLbL scattering (Colangelo et al., 2014–2017) by incorporating short-distance constraints from the OPE, specifically the longitudinal SDC derived by Melnikov and Vainshtein (2003). It contradicts the earlier prescription of dropping the TFF momentum dependence at the external vertex, which led to larger increases (e.g., \(13.5\times 10^{-11}\) for the \(\pi^0\) alone), and instead provides a consistent treatment using an infinite tower of excited pseudoscalars. The results enable a more model-independent evaluation of the HLbL contribution to \((g-2)_\mu\) and can be combined with dispersive descriptions of other intermediate states (e.g., two-pion channels) for a complete data-driven calculation.
