# Generalization of radiative jet energy loss to non-zero magnetic mass

> Reliable predictions for jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions require accurate computation of radiative energy loss. With this goal, an energy loss formalism in a realistic finite size dynamical QCD medium was recently developed. While this formalism assumes zero magnetic mass - in accordance with the one-loop perturbative calculations - different non-perturbative approaches report a non-zero magnetic mass at RHIC and LHC. We here generalize the energy loss to consistently include a possibility for existence of non-zero magnetic screening. We also present how the inclusion of finite magnetic mass changes the energy loss results. Our analysis indicates a fundamental constraint on magnetic to electric mass ratio.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes a previously developed radiative jet energy loss formalism (which assumed zero magnetic mass in accordance with one-loop perturbative QCD) to consistently include a non-zero magnetic mass, finding that the magnetic-to-electric mass ratio must be less than one to avoid a violation of the second law of thermodynamics.

## Method

The authors start from their existing first-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss expression for a finite-size dynamical QCD medium and modify the gluon self-energy contributions. The effective cross-section for the exchanged gluon is rewritten in terms of the electric (Debye) mass \(\mu_E\) and the magnetic mass \(\mu_M\), while the radiated gluon's effective mass is assumed unchanged. The resulting energy loss integral is then evaluated numerically for light, charm, and bottom quarks under RHIC and LHC conditions.

## Key numbers

* For a quark-gluon plasma at \(T = 225\) MeV (RHIC conditions) with \(L = 5\) fm and initial jet energy 10 GeV, fractional radiative energy loss decreases as \(\mu_M/\mu_E\) increases; the loss becomes negative when \(\mu_M > \mu_E\).
* For the same medium at \(T = 400\) MeV (LHC conditions) with \(L = 5\) fm and initial jet energy 50 GeV, the same qualitative behavior is observed.
* For the non-perturbatively suggested range \(0.4 < \mu_M/\mu_E < 0.6\), finite magnetic mass reduces the energy loss in a dynamical QCD medium by 25% to 50% compared to the zero-magnetic-mass case.
* For \(\mu_M/\mu_E = 0.5\), 25% of the decrease comes from the magnetic mass in the numerator of the cross-section, while only 14% comes from the magnetic screening in the denominator.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical energy loss formalism of Djordjevic and collaborators (2010, 2009), which itself removed the static-scattering-center approximation of earlier calculations (e.g., GLV, Gyulassy and Wang, Wiedemann, AMY). It directly addresses the discrepancy between the zero magnetic mass assumed in HTL perturbation theory and the non-zero magnetic mass reported by non-perturbative lattice studies (Maezawa, Nakamura, Hart, Bak). The results enable more accurate predictions of jet quenching and heavy-flavor suppression at RHIC and LHC by incorporating non-zero magnetic screening into phenomenological models.
