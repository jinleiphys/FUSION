# An Evaluation Of The Possible Effect Of Tetra-neutron Production On Ternary Fission Yields

> In this work, we study the effect of including a resonant state of four neutrons in the low-density warm nuclear equation of state, using a relativistic mean-field formalism, where in-medium effects are considered. For that purpose, the abundances of 62 different clusters immersed in a gas of protons and neutrons, are calculated with and without the presence of this resonant tetraneutron state. Ternary fission experiments are environments where not only these clusters can be formed, but also where such thermodynamic conditions of low densities and moderate temperatures can be achieved. The calculated yields of isotopes with and without the tetraneutron included are then compared with experimentally observed ternary fission yields. While the results indicate that the tetraneutron has little effect on yields of other clusters under the existing ternary fission conditions, a possibly observable effect on yields of clusters emitted from the neck region in mid-peripheral heavy ion collisions is clearly suggested.

# An Evaluation Of The Possible Effect Of Tetra-neutron Production On Ternary Fission Yields

## Key claim

While the inclusion of a tetraneutron resonant state has negligible effect on cluster yields under the specific thermodynamic conditions of ternary fission (density ~6.7e-5 fm^-3, temperature 1.29 MeV, proton fraction 0.035), the authors suggest that observable effects could appear in mid-peripheral heavy ion collisions where higher densities and temperatures are reached.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism including 62 light clusters (isotopes of elements with Z=1-6) immersed in a nucleon gas, with in-medium effects incorporated via a binding energy shift term and a scalar cluster-meson coupling factor x_s. They calculate cluster mass fractions and effective yields (accounting for decays of excited states) as functions of baryon density, with and without the tetraneutron resonance, using the Duer et al. (2022) tetraneutron binding energy of -2.37 MeV. The calculated yields are compared to experimental ternary fission yields from the 241Pu(n_th,f) reaction.

## Key numbers

- Tetraneutron resonance energy: E_4n = 2.37 ± 0.38(stat) ± 0.44(sys) MeV, width Γ = 1.75 ± 0.22(stat) ± 0.30(sys) MeV (from Duer et al. 2022)
- Optimum freeze-out density for ternary fission: 6.7e-5 fm^-3, with fit minima spanning 4e-5 to 8e-5 fm^-3
- Temperature characterizing the neck region: 1.29 MeV
- Proton fraction in the neck region: 0.035
- At density 6e-5 fm^-3, no difference is observed between yields with and without tetraneutron inclusion
- At higher densities (6e-4 to 6e-3 fm^-3), differences between calculations with and without tetraneutron become noticeable for He and heavier elements
- At density 6e-3 fm^-3, T=3 MeV, and y_p=0.1, differences between calculations with and without tetraneutron increase with Z

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF studies of light clusters in nuclear matter (Pais et al. 2023, Natowitz et al. 2023) by including 62 clusters instead of only 2,3H and 3,4,6He, and specifically tests the effect of the tetraneutron resonance reported by Duer et al. (2022) from RIKEN SAMURAI experiments. The results confirm the earlier finding (Pais et al. 2023) that tetraneutron effects are negligible under ternary fission conditions, but suggest that mid-peripheral heavy ion collisions (as studied by Abdurrahman et al. 2024) could provide a more favorable environment for detecting tetraneutron signatures. The work also builds on the ternary fission analysis framework of Roepke et al. (2021) and the Lestone parameter systematics (Lestone 2008).
