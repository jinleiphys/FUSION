# Dimensional versus cut-off renormalization and the nucleon-nucleon potential of the effective field theory

> The role of cut-off and dimensional regularizations is discussed in the context of obtaining a renormalized nucleon-nucleon potential from the chiral Lagrangian formulation of the effective field theory due to Weinberg. Both types of renormalizations are performed for the sum of an attractive delta function and its derivatives. The equivalence between the two forms of regularizations can be established with the use of energy-dependent bare couplings and the explicit forms of these couplings are determined.

## Key claim

By using energy-dependent bare couplings, cut-off and dimensional regularizations yield equivalent renormalized results for the sum of an attractive delta function and its derivatives in the nucleon-nucleon potential derived from chiral effective field theory, resolving previous inconsistencies between the two regularization schemes.

## Method

The author performs renormalization of the on-shell K matrix for the potential V(p,q) = λ₁ + λ₂(p²+q²) using both sharp cut-off regularization (with a momentum cutoff Λ) and dimensional regularization in three dimensions. Energy-dependent bare couplings λ₁(Λ,k²) and λ₂(Λ,k²) are introduced to cancel ultraviolet divergences, and the explicit forms of these couplings are determined for both regularization schemes. The renormalized K matrix is then expressed in terms of a function Λ₀(k²) that encodes the physical scales of the system.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off regularized integrals I_L(k²,Λ) for L=0,1,2 are given explicitly: I₀ = -[Λ + (k/2) ln|(Λ-k)/(Λ+k)|], I₁ = -[Λ³/3 + k²Λ + (k³/2) ln|(Λ-k)/(Λ+k)|], I₂ = -[Λ⁵/5 + k²Λ³/3 + k⁴Λ + (k⁵/2) ln|(Λ-k)/(Λ+k)|]
- The dimensionally regularized integrals I_L(k²) in three dimensions are identically zero: I_L(k²) = -(1/2) Γ((2L+3)/2) Γ((-2L-1)/2) Re[(-k²)^((2L+1)/2)] = 0
- The renormalized on-shell K matrix is given by K_R(k²) = -1/Λ₀(k²), where Λ₀(k²) can be chosen as -1/a for a single-scale problem (scattering length a) or as -1/a + (1/2)r₀k² for two scales (scattering length a and effective range r₀)

## Context

This work addresses inconsistencies found in previous renormalization studies of the nucleon-nucleon potential from chiral effective field theory, particularly those by Phillips, Beane, and Cohen (1998) and by Birse, McGovern, and Richardson (1998). The author extends the approach of Adhikari and Ghosh (1997) and Ghosh, Adhikari, and Talukdar (1998) by explicitly demonstrating equivalence between cut-off and dimensional regularizations through energy-dependent bare couplings, enabling a consistent renormalization framework for the full nucleon-nucleon potential of the effective field theory due to Weinberg (1991).
