# Structure of Light Unstable Nuclei Studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> Structures of light unstable nuclei, Li, Be, B, and C isotopes are systematically studied with a microscopic method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The theoretical method is found to be very useful to study ground and excited states of various nuclei covering unstable nuclei. The calculations succeed to reproduce many experimental data for nuclear structures; energies, radii, magnetic dipole moments, electric quadrupole moments, transition strength. In the theoretical results it is found that various exotic phenomena in unstable nuclei such as molecular-like structures, neutron skin, and large deformations may appear in unstabel nuclei. We investigate the structure change with the increase of neutron number and with the increase of the excitation energies, and find the drastic changes between shell-model-like structures and clustering structures. The mechanism of clustering developments in unstable nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors claim that antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) can systematically describe both shell-model-like and clustering structures in light unstable nuclei (Li, Be, B, C isotopes), but they acknowledge that the simplest version of AMD (variation before projection) underestimates extremely large radii in neutron-halo nuclei such as 11Li, 11Be, and 14Be, and that careful choice of interactions and variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) is necessary to reproduce features like the parity inversion in 11Be.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations for Li, Be, B, and C isotopes, using Slater determinants of Gaussian wave packets as basis wave functions. For the simplest version, energy variation was performed after parity projection but before total-spin projection (VBP), while for excited states they used variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) with frictional cooling. The effective interactions employed were the Volkov No.1 force and the MV1 force (with zero-range three-body terms), combined with the G3RS spin-orbit force.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of Li and Be isotopes were qualitatively reproduced with both Volkov No.1 force (Majorana parameter m=0.56) and MV1 case (1) force (m=0.576)
- For B isotopes, MV1 force with m=0.576 reproduced experimental binding energies well; mass-dependent m values of 0.576, 0.576, 0.63, 0.65, and 0.65 were used for 11B, 13B, 15B, 17B, and 19B respectively
- For C isotopes, MV1 force with m=0.576 reproduced experimental binding energies well; width parameters nu ranged from 0.19 fm^-2 for 10C to 0.16 fm^-2 for 22C with interaction (b)
- The root-mean-square radii of C isotopes showed a kink at 14C and increased with neutron number for N greater than 8
- The non-normal parity 1/2+ state in 11Be remained higher than the 1/2- state in simplest AMD calculations, despite the known experimental ground state being 1/2+
- For 13C and 15C, the calculated intrinsic states of positive parity with MV1 (m=0.576) and Volkov force (m=0.60) contained little component of the experimentally known 1/2+ states

## Context

This work extends the AMD framework previously developed by Ono et al. for nuclear reactions and by Kanada-En'yo et al. for nuclear structure, applying it systematically to unstable light nuclei. It contrasts with earlier approaches that assumed inert cores or clusters, such as three-body models for 11Li (as 9Li+2n) and 6He (as alpha+2n), and extended cluster models for nuclei with A less than or equal to 10 by Suzuki, Ogawa, and Itagaki. The results enable systematic investigation of clustering structures and shell-model-like structures across isotopic chains, and the authors note that VAP calculations with appropriate interactions can reproduce the parity inversion in 11Be and the large radius of 12Be as a 2-hbar-omega state with 2 particles in sd shell and 2 holes in p shell.
