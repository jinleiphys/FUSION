# Transverse Structure of the Kaon: A light-front Hamiltonian Approach

> We employ the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework to compute the leading-twist (twist-2) and subleading-twist (twist-3) transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) of the kaon. The light-front wave functions are obtained by diagonalizing a light-front QCD Hamiltonian that includes quark-antiquark (|q\bar{q}\rangle) and quark-antiquark-gluon (|q\bar{q}g\rangle) Fock components together with a three-dimensional confinement. Using the QCD equations of motion, the twist-3 TMDs are decomposed into twist-2 constributions and genuine twist-3 terms, the latter encoding quark-quark-gluon correlations beyond the probabilistic picture. These genuine twist-3 constributions arise from the interference between the |q\bar{q}\rangle and |q\bar{q}g\rangle sectors, which are usually negelected in the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation. This work provides the first theoretical predictions of kaon subleading-twist TMDs that explicitly account for Fock-sector interference. In addition, we present results for the kaon's twist-2 and twist-3 collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs). The twist-2 PDFs are found to be in good agreement with the recent global analysis by the JAM collaboration.

## Key claim
The paper provides the first theoretical predictions of kaon subleading-twist (twist-3) transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) that explicitly account for Fock-sector interference between the \(|q\bar{q}\rangle\) and \(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\) components, going beyond the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation which typically neglects these genuine twist-3 contributions.

## Method
The authors employ the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework to diagonalize a light-front QCD Hamiltonian that includes quark-antiquark (\(|q\bar{q}\rangle\)) and quark-antiquark-gluon (\(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\)) Fock components together with a three-dimensional confinement potential. The light-front wave functions are obtained as Hamiltonian eigenstates using truncation parameters \(\{N_{\rm max}, K\} = \{14, 15\}\), with model parameters fitted to reproduce the mass spectra of strange mesons. Using the QCD equations of motion, the twist-3 TMDs are decomposed into twist-2 contributions and genuine twist-3 terms that encode quark-quark-gluon correlations arising from interference between the two Fock sectors.

## Key numbers
- Model parameters used: quark mass \(m_q = 0.39\) GeV, strange quark mass \(m_s = 0.55\) GeV, gluon mass \(m_g = 0.60\) GeV, confinement strength \(\kappa = 0.65\) GeV, oscillator scale \(b = 0.29\) GeV, and coupling \(g = 1.92\) (dimensionless).
- Truncation parameters: \(N_{\rm max} = 14\) and \(K = 15\) for the basis expansion.
- The IR cutoff is \(\lambda_{\rm IR} \simeq b/\sqrt{N_{\rm max}}\) and the UV cutoff is \(\Lambda_{\rm UV} \simeq b\sqrt{N_{\rm max}}\), both determined by the \(N_{\rm max}\) truncation.

## Context
This work extends previous BLFQ studies of leading-twist TMDs for spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles in QED (Hu 2020, Nair 2022) and for the proton (Hu 2022), as well as subleading-twist TMDs of the pion (Zhu 2023). The kaon's twist-2 PDFs are found to be in good agreement with the recent global analysis by the JAM Collaboration (Barry 2025). The results provide nonperturbative input on higher-twist dynamics relevant for future experimental studies at the Electron-Ion Colliders in China (EicC) and the USA (EIC).
