# The Impact of Anisotropy on Neutron Star Properties: Insights from I-f-C Universal Relations

> This study presents a universal relation for anisotropic neutron stars, called the $I-f-C$ relation, which accounts for the local anisotropic pressure using the Quasi-Local (QL) Model proposed by Horvat et al. \cite{QL_Model} to describe the anisotropy inside the neutron star. This study analyzes approximately 60 unified tabulated EoS-ensembles, spanning from relativistic to non-relativistic mean-field models, that comply with multimessenger constraints and cover a broad range of stiffness. The results indicate that the relationship between the parameters becomes more robust with positive anisotropy, while it weakens with negative anisotropy. With the help of the GW170817 \& GW190814 tidal deformability limit, a theoretical limit for the canonical $f$-mode frequency for both isotropic and anisotropic stars is established. For isotropic case the canonical $f$-mode frequency for event GW170817 \& GW190814 is $f_{1.4} = 2.605^{+0.487} _ {-0.459}\ \mathrm{kHz}$ and $ f_{1.4} = 2.093^{+0.150} _ {-0.125} \ \mathrm{kHz}$ respectively. These established relationships have the potential to serve as a reliable tool to limit the equation of state of nuclear matter when measurements of relevant observables are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal relations connecting the moment of inertia, f-mode oscillation frequency, and compactness (I-f-C) exist for anisotropic neutron stars, but the robustness of these relations depends on the sign of the anisotropy: positive anisotropy strengthens the EOS-insensitivity of the I-f relation, while both positive and negative anisotropy weaken the C-f and C-I universal relations compared to the isotropic case.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations modified for anisotropic pressure using the Quasi-Local (QL) model of Horvat et al., where the anisotropy parameter β_QL quantifies the deviation between radial and tangential pressure. They computed macroscopic properties (mass, radius, compactness, tidal deformability, moment of inertia, and f-mode frequency in the Cowling approximation) for approximately 60 unified equations of state spanning relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field models (35 RMF and 25 Skyrme-Hartree-Fock). The f-mode frequencies were obtained by solving perturbation equations for quadrupolar (l=2) oscillations, and universal relations were fitted with fourth-order polynomial least-squares fits.

## Key numbers

- For the isotropic case, the canonical f-mode frequency constrained by GW170817 tidal deformability is f_1.4 = 2.605^{+0.487}_{-0.459} kHz; for GW190814 it is f_1.4 = 2.093^{+0.150}_{-0.125} kHz.
- The reduced chi-squared (χ_r^2) for the I-f relation decreases from 13.511 × 10^{-6} at β_QL = -2.0 to 2.517 × 10^{-6} at β_QL = +2.0, indicating stronger universality with positive anisotropy.
- The reduced chi-squared for the C-f relation increases from 1.846 × 10^{-6} at β_QL = 0.0 to 2.871 × 10^{-6} at β_QL = +2.0 and 2.228 × 10^{-6} at β_QL = -2.0, indicating weaker universality with anisotropy.
- The GW170817 event constrains Λ_1.4 to be 190^{+390}_{-120}, while GW190814 puts a limit of Λ_1.4 = 616^{+273}_{-158} (in the NS-BH scenario).

## Context

This work extends previous universal relations for isotropic neutron stars, such as the I-Love-C relations by Yagi and Yunes (2013) and the I-f relations by Lau et al. (2010), to include pressure anisotropy using the Quasi-Local model. It builds on the anisotropic I-Love-C study by Biswas and Bose (2019) and the work of Kumar and Landry (2022) on moment of inertia constraints. The results enable future gravitational wave detections of f-mode oscillations to constrain the equation of state of nuclear matter and the degree of anisotropy within neutron stars, particularly by using the derived theoretical limits for canonical f-mode frequencies from GW170817 and GW190814 tidal deformability data.
