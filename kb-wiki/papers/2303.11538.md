# Gravitational transverse-momentum distributions

> We study the energy-momentum tensor of spin-$0$ and spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ hadrons in momentum space. We parametrize this object in terms of so-called gravitational transverse-momentum distributions, and we identify in the quark sector the relations between the latter and the usual transverse-momentum distributions. Focusing on particular components of the energy-momentum tensor, we study momentum densities, flux of inertia and stress distribution in momentum space, revealing part of the wealth of physical information that can be gained from higher-twist transverse-momentum distributions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the concept of gravitational transverse-momentum distributions (gravitational TMDs) as a parametrization of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) of hadrons in momentum space, and identifies which of these new functions can be expressed in terms of the usual quark vector TMDs. The authors emphasize that the relations hold only for the gauge-invariant canonical EMT operator, not the kinetic one, and that the number of independent gravitational TMDs reduces from 32 to 16 for spin-1/2 targets due to the particular structure of the quark EMT.

## Method

The authors define a bilocal generalization of the gauge-invariant canonical EMT operator for quarks in QCD, then take its forward matrix element and integrate over the quark light-front energy to obtain the TMD EMT. They parametrize this object for spin-0 and spin-1/2 hadrons using all independent rank-2 tensors built from the available vectors and pseudotensors, respecting parity, hermiticity, and time-reversal constraints. The resulting gravitational TMDs are then related to the standard quark vector TMDs by comparing the parametrization of the TMD EMT with the product of the quark momentum component and the vector TMD correlator.

## Key numbers

* For a spin-0 target, the TMD EMT is parametrized by 10 polarization-independent gravitational TMDs (a0 through a9).
* For a spin-1/2 target, the general parametrization involves 32 independent functions: 10 polarization-independent, 6 associated with longitudinal polarization (a1L through a6L), and 16 associated with transverse polarization (a1T through a6T and a0T^perp through a9T^perp).
* Due to the structure of the gauge-invariant canonical quark EMT, the number of independent functions reduces to 16 for spin-1/2 targets.
* The average quark longitudinal momentum is given by <k+>_q = P+ ∫ dx d^2k_perp x f1, where f1 is the unpolarized TMD.
* The average quark transverse momentum is <k^i_perp>_q = epsilon_T^{i S_T} ∫ dx d^2k_perp (k_perp^2 / (2M)) f_{1T}^perp, involving the Sivers function.
* The simple gaussian model used for illustrations assumes a typical transverse momentum width <k_perp^2> ≈ 0.6 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the connection between the EMT and generalized parton distributions (GPDs), established by Ji (1996) and Lorce (2015), to the domain of transverse-momentum distributions (TMDs). It builds on the parametrization of the quark TMD correlator by Goeke (2005) and Bacchetta (2006), and on the interpretation of EMT components in impact-parameter space by Lorce (2018) and Freese (2021). The results provide new motivations for studying higher-twist TMDs and enable future investigations of gravitational TMDs using Lattice QCD and model calculations, as well as connections to the physics program of the future Electron-Ion Collider.
