# Exchange effects in nucleus-nucleus reactions

> We present a scattering model for nuclei with similar masses. In this three-body model, the projectile has a core+valence structure, whereas the target is identical to the core nucleus. The three-body wave functions must be symmetrized for the exchange of the cores. This property gives rise to non-local potentials, which are computed without approximation. The present model is an extension of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) formalism, with an additional treatment of core exchange. We solve the coupled-channel system, including non-local terms, by the $R$-matrix method using Lagrange functions. This model is applied to the $^{13}{\rm C}+^{12}$C, $^{13}{\rm N}+^{12}$C and $^{16}{\rm O}+^{12}$C systems. Experimental scattering cross sections are fairly well reproduced without any parameter fitting. The backward-angle enhancement of the elastic cross sections is due to the non-local potential. We discuss in more detail the various non-local contributions and present effective local potentials.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-body scattering model that extends the standard Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) formalism by exactly including core-exchange effects through wave function symmetrization, which produces non-local potentials that reproduce experimental backward-angle enhancement of elastic cross sections without any parameter fitting.

## Method

The authors construct a three-body model where the projectile has a core+valence structure and the target is identical to the core nucleus, requiring symmetrization of the three-body wave functions for core exchange. They compute the resulting non-local potentials without approximation and solve the coupled-channel integro-differential system using the R-matrix method with Lagrange functions. The model is applied to the 13C+12C, 13N+12C, and 16O+12C systems using only pre-existing two-body interactions as input.

## Key numbers

- 13C+12C elastic cross sections at center-of-mass energies E_cm = 7.8 MeV and 14.2 MeV are reproduced, with the non-local calculation involving only the 13C ground state (dashed line) reproducing data fairly well
- 13N binding energy calculated as -1.90 MeV, in fair agreement with the experimental value of -1.94 MeV
- 16O+12C elastic cross section at E_cm = 23.14 MeV is presented, where a backward-angle enhancement is observed in the data from Villari et al. (1989)
- For the 12C+12C core-core potential, parameters include: depth -100 MeV, radius 5.45 fm, diffuseness 0.48 fm (real part); depth -15 MeV, radius 5.77 fm, diffuseness 0.26 fm (imaginary part); Coulomb radius R_C = 5.45 fm
- For the n+12C potential: V_0 = 62.70 MeV for even ell and 50.59 MeV for odd ell, with r_0 = 2.656 fm, a_0 = 0.705 fm, and spin-orbit amplitude V_ells = 28.406 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method, originally developed for deuteron scattering by Rawitscher (1974) and later applied to exotic nuclei, by treating core-exchange effects that are neglected in standard CDCC calculations. Prior approaches to exchange effects in similar-mass systems, such as those by von Oertzen (1970) and Imanishi and von Oertzen (1987), used various approximations; the present model treats exchange exactly. The method enables future inclusion of core excitations and provides a parameter-free framework for analyzing reactions where the projectile and target share a common core, such as 13C+12C and 17O+16O.
