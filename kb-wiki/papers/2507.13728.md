# Triply heavy tetraquarks $\bar{b}c\bar{q}c$ and $\bar{c}b\bar{q}b$ in a constituent quark model

> A systematic investigation of $S$-wave triply heavy tetraquark systems with quark content $\bar{b}c\bar{q}c$ and $\bar{c}b\bar{q}b$ $(q = u,\,d,\,s)$, spin-parity quantum numbers $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$, $2^+$ and isospin $I=0,\,\frac{1}{2}$, is carried out within the constituent quark model framework. The four-body bound and resonance states are determined by solving the Schrödinger equation employing the high-precision and efficient Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) in conjunction with the powerful Complex Scaling Method (CSM). Besides, a comprehensive coupled-channel analysis of the $S$-wave tetraquark systems is performed, taking into account meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark and K-type configurations, as well as all allowed color structures. Several narrow resonant states are identified in each $I(J^P)$ channel. In particular, resonances for the $\bar{b}c\bar{q}c$ system are found in the mass range of $8.87-9.36$ GeV, while those for the $\bar{c}b\bar{q}b$ system lie between $12.13-12.45$ GeV. Most of the predicted resonances are compact tetraquark states with sizes smaller than $1.0$ fm, although four states exhibit more extended structures with sizes around $1.3$ fm, suggesting a more loosely bound nature. Magnetic moments and dominant wave function components of these exotic states are also analyzed. The results indicate that K-type configurations play a major role in the structure of the observed resonances. Finally, possible strong decay channels (golden modes) for these states are theoretically proposed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that narrow resonant states exist in triply heavy tetraquark systems with quark content \(\bar{b}c\bar{q}c\) and \(\bar{c}b\bar{q}b\) (\(q = u, d, s\)), but the existence of these states is conditional on the specific constituent quark model framework used and the inclusion of K-type configurations, which the authors identify as playing a major role in the structure of the observed resonances.

## Method

The authors solved the complex-scaled four-body Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) combined with the Complex Scaling Method (CSM) within a constituent quark model that includes color confinement and one-gluon exchange potentials. They performed a comprehensive coupled-channel analysis of \(S\)-wave tetraquark systems, considering meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and five K-type configurations with all allowed color structures, for spin-parity quantum numbers \(J^P = 0^+, 1^+, 2^+\) and isospin \(I = 0, \frac{1}{2}\).

## Key numbers

* Resonances for the \(\bar{b}c\bar{q}c\) system are found in the mass range of \(8.87-9.36\) GeV.
* Resonances for the \(\bar{c}b\bar{q}b\) system are found in the mass range of \(12.13-12.45\) GeV.
* Most predicted resonances are compact tetraquark states with sizes smaller than \(1.0\) fm.
* Four states exhibit more extended structures with sizes around \(1.3\) fm.
* The lowest-lying mass for the \(\bar{b}c\bar{q}c\) system with \(I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2}(0^+)\) from a complete coupled-channel calculation is \(8173\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Yang, 2024) on triply heavy tetraquarks \(cc\bar{c}\bar{q}\) and \(bb\bar{b}\bar{q}\), which identified narrow resonant states in the energy ranges of \(5.6-5.9\) GeV for the charm sector and \(15.3-15.7\) GeV for the bottom sector. The current investigation applies the same constituent quark model, which has demonstrated success in predicting various exotic hadrons including hidden-, single-, double-, and fully heavy tetraquarks, as well as pentaquarks. The results enable future experimental searches for these predicted triply heavy tetraquark states and provide theoretical guidance for identifying their strong decay channels (golden modes).
