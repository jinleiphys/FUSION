# The QGP dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of partons and hadrons in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed within the novel Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for the partonic phase (DQPM) including a dynamical hadronization scheme. The PHSD model reproduces a large variety of observables from SPS to LHC energies, e.g. the quark-number scaling of elliptic flow, transverse mass and rapidity spectra of charged hadrons, dilepton spectra, open and hidden charm production, collective flow coefficients etc., which are associated with the observation of a sQGP. The highlights of the latest results on collective flow are presented and open questions/perspectives are discussed.

# The QGP dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which includes explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom and a dynamical hadronization scheme based on a lattice-QCD equation-of-state, reproduces the observed increase of elliptic flow \(v_2\) with collision energy from AGS to RHIC, whereas purely hadronic models (HSD, UrQMD) and the AMPT model with massless noninteracting partons fail to do so. The success is attributed to the increasing partonic fraction of the energy density at higher energies and the lower shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s\) in the partonic phase.

## Method
The authors employed the PHSD transport model, which propagates dynamical quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) with self-energies and spectral functions consistent with lattice QCD, and includes covariant transition rates for dynamical hadronization. They calculated the anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) for charged particles at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions across \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) to 200 GeV, using the same model parameters as in Bratkovskaya et al. (2011) without tuning. Results were compared to experimental data from the RHIC Beam-Energy-Scan program and to predictions from the hadronic models HSD and UrQMD, the partonic AMPT model, and ideal/viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers
- The average elliptic flow \(v_2\) of charged particles at midrapidity for minimum bias Au+Au collisions increases from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.2\) GeV to 200 GeV; PHSD reproduces this rise, while HSD and UrQMD remain approximately constant or decrease slightly.
- The parton fraction of the total energy density at midrapidity (from PHSD) reaches a maximum at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and decreases substantially with decreasing bombarding energy; the critical energy density for hadron-to-parton transition is \(\varepsilon \approx 0.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) from PHSD is approximately constant across \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) to 200 GeV and deviates significantly from the ideal fluid estimate of 0.5; HSD gives a monotonous growth of this ratio.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the PHSD results for \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) versus transverse momentum \(p_T\) (20-30% centrality) overestimate the STAR data by up to 20%; hydrodynamic results significantly underestimate the data.
- The triangular flow \(v_3\) from PHSD is systematically larger than from HSD (which gives \(v_3 \approx 0\) for all energies) and increases steadily with bombarding energy; \(v_3\) is compatible with zero for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 20\) GeV in HSD.
- The hexadecupole flow \(v_4\) from PHSD stays almost constant for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \ge 10\) GeV and is noticeably higher than HSD values.

## Context
This work extends earlier PHSD studies of rapidity distributions, transverse mass spectra, and elliptic flow at SPS and RHIC energies (Bratkovskaya et al., 2011) to a systematic analysis of higher-order flow harmonics \(v_3\) and \(v_4\) across the full RHIC Beam-Energy-Scan range. It directly contradicts the results of the AMPT model with string melting (Lin et al., 2005), which uses a massless noninteracting parton equation-of-state and fails to reproduce the energy growth of \(v_2\). The findings enable future comparisons with LHC data for \(pp\), \(p+Pb\), and \(Pb+Pb\) collisions, where initial glasma fluctuations may contribute to the Fourier decomposition of azimuthal distributions.
