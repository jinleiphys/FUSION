# Energy reconstruction effects in neutrino oscillation experiments and implications for the analysis

> Data on neutrino oscillation often involve reconstructed neutrino energies while the analysis implies the real neutrino energy. The corrections corresponding to the transformation from real to reconstructed energy are discussed in the case of Cherenkov detectors where multinucleon events appear as quasielastic ones. These corrections show up as a tendency for the events to escape the region of high flux, with a clear preference for the low energy side. This is an effect of the multinucleon component of the quasielastic cross section. We have applied our corrections to the T2K and MiniBooNE data for electron appearance or $ν_μ$ disappearance data. We show that the inclusion of this correction in the analysis is expected to lead to an increase of the best fit oscillation mass parameters, particularly pronounced for the MiniBooNE neutrino data. This inclusion in the analysis of the MiniBooNE neutrino data should improve the compatibility with the existing constraints.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard method of reconstructing neutrino energy from quasielastic-like events in Cherenkov detectors is systematically biased because multinucleon knockout events are misidentified as genuine quasielastic events, and that correcting for this bias shifts event distributions toward lower reconstructed energies, which in turn increases the best-fit oscillation mass parameters, particularly for MiniBooNE neutrino data.

## Method

The authors compute a spreading function \(d(E_\nu, \overline{E_\nu})\) from the double-differential charged-current cross section on carbon, including both genuine quasielastic and multinucleon (np-nh) contributions, and use it to transform theoretical event distributions expressed in true neutrino energy \(E_\nu\) into distributions in reconstructed energy \(\overline{E}_\nu\). They apply this forward-smearing procedure to the T2K muon-neutrino disappearance and electron-appearance channels and to the MiniBooNE electron-neutrino appearance channel for both neutrinos and antineutrinos, using the MiniBooNE muon flux and oscillation formulas for sterile neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- For T2K muon disappearance, the unsmeared best-fit mass is \(\Delta m^2_{32} = 2.65 \times 10^{-3}~\text{eV}^2\) (T2K two-flavor best fit); the smeared curve is compared to an unsmeared curve with \(\Delta m^2_{32} = 2.43 \times 10^{-3}~\text{eV}^2\) (Fogli 2012), showing that smearing mimics a lower mass in the first peak region.
- For MiniBooNE with \(\Delta m^2_{41} = 0.45~\text{eV}^2\), the smearing shifts the peak of the electron-event distribution downward by approximately \(100~\text{MeV}\) in reconstructed energy, and the smeared curve is equivalent to an unsmeared curve with \(\Delta m^2_{41} = 0.1~\text{eV}^2\).
- For MiniBooNE with \(\Delta m^2_{41} = 0.2~\text{eV}^2\), the smearing shifts the peak downward by approximately \(80~\text{MeV}\); the smeared curve cannot be reproduced by any unsmeared curve because the unsmeared shape saturates at the \(1/E_{\nu_e}^2\) limit.
- The MiniBooNE baseline is \(L = 541~\text{m}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Martini 2009, 2011, 2012) in which they showed that multinucleon contributions could explain the MiniBooNE axial-mass anomaly without modifying the axial mass. It provides a practical smearing formalism that can be applied to oscillation analyses, complementing similar approaches by Lalakulich (2012) and Nieves (2012). The results suggest that including reconstruction corrections in MiniBooNE analyses could reconcile the low-energy electron excess with larger sterile-neutrino mass parameters, improving compatibility with constraints from other experiments (Abazajian 2012, Giunti 2011). The method also enables future fits to T2K disappearance data that properly account for nuclear effects.
