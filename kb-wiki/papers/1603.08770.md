# Universality of anomalous conductivities in theories with higher-derivative holographic duals

> Anomalous chiral conductivities in theories with global anomalies are independent of whether they are computed in a weakly coupled quantum (or thermal) field theory, hydrodynamics, or at infinite coupling from holography. While the presence of dynamical gauge fields and mixed, gauge-global anomalies can destroy this universality, in their absence, the non-renormalisation of anomalous Ward identities is expected to be obeyed at all intermediate coupling strengths. In holography, bulk theories with higher-derivative corrections incorporate coupling constant corrections to the boundary theory observables in an expansion around infinite coupling. In this work, we investigate the coupling constant dependence and universality of anomalous conductivities (and thus of the anomalous Ward identities) in general, four-dimensional systems that possess asymptotically anti-de Sitter holographic duals with a non-extremal black brane in five dimensions, and anomalous transport introduced into the boundary theory via the bulk Chern-Simons action. We show that in bulk theories with arbitrary gauge- and diffeomorphism-invariant higher-derivative actions, anomalous conductivities, which can incorporate an infinite series of (inverse) coupling constant corrections, remain universal. Owing to the existence of the membrane paradigm, the proof reduces to a construction of bulk effective theories at the horizon and the boundary. It only requires us to impose the condition of horizon regularity and correct boundary conditions on the fields. We also discuss ways to violate the universality by violating conditions for the validity of the membrane paradigm, in particular, by adding mass to the vector fields (a case with a mixed, gauge-global anomaly) and in bulk geometries with a naked singularity.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the four anomalous chiral conductivities (chiral magnetic, chiral vortical, chiral separation, and the axial vortical conductivity) remain universal in any holographic theory with arbitrary gauge- and diffeomorphism-invariant higher-derivative bulk actions, provided the membrane paradigm is valid; this universality can be violated by adding mass to vector fields (a mixed gauge-global anomaly) or in bulk geometries with naked singularities.

## Method

The authors consider a five-dimensional bulk action with a dynamical metric, two massless gauge fields dual to axial and vector currents, scalar fields, and Chern-Simons terms that source global chiral anomalies in the boundary theory. They use the membrane paradigm to reduce the computation of anomalous conductivities to constructing effective theories at the black brane horizon and the AdS boundary, imposing only horizon regularity and correct boundary conditions on the fields. The proof shows that for any gauge- and diffeomorphism-invariant higher-derivative Lagrangian, the conserved current along the holographic radial direction yields the same expressions for the conductivities regardless of the specific higher-derivative corrections.

## Key numbers

* The chiral magnetic conductivity and chiral separation conductivity are fixed by the anomaly coefficient gamma as sigma_{J5 B} = -2 gamma mu and sigma_{J B} = -2 gamma mu_5, where mu and mu_5 are the vector and axial chemical potentials.
* The axial vortical conductivity takes the form sigma_{J5 omega} = kappa mu^2 + tilde{c} T^2, where tilde{c} is fixed by the gravitational anomaly coefficient lambda and T is the Hawking temperature given by T = (r_h^2 / 4 pi) sqrt(f_1 g_1).
* The chiral vortical conductivity is given by sigma_{J omega} = 2 gamma mu_5 mu.
* The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density at infinite coupling is eta / s = hbar / (4 pi k_B), which receives corrections from higher-derivative bulk terms.

## Context

This work extends the universality proof for chiral magnetic conductivity in two-derivative Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton theories by Gursoy and Tarrio (2014) to arbitrary higher-derivative theories, incorporating an infinite series of inverse coupling constant corrections. The results confirm that when only global anomalies are present, anomalous transport is protected from radiative corrections, consistent with the non-renormalisation theorems of Adler (1969), Bell and Jackiw (1969), and the anomaly matching condition of 't Hooft (1979). The paper also discusses how mixed gauge-global anomalies, as studied by Jensen, Loganayagam, and Yarom (2013), can violate this universality, and it connects to lattice results by Yamamoto (2011) and Fukushima, Kharzeev, and Warringa (2010) showing that chiral conductivities renormalise in theories with dynamical gauge fields.
