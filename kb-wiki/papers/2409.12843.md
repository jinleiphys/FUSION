# Flavor Hierarchy of Jet Energy Correlators inside the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Heavy flavor jets provide ideal tools to probe the mass effect on jet substructure in both vacuum and quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Energy-energy correlator (EEC) is an excellent jet substructure observable owning to its strong sensitivity to jet physics at different scales. We perform a complete realistic simulation on medium modification of heavy and light flavor jet EEC in heavy-ion collisions. A clear flavor hierarchy is observed for jet EEC in both vacuum and QGP due to the mass effect. The medium modification of inclusive jet EEC at different angular scales exhibits very rich structure: suppression at intermediate angles, and enhancement at small and large angles, which can be well explained by the interplay of mass effect, energy loss, medium-induced radiation and medium response. These unique features of jet EEC are shown to probe the physics of jet-medium interaction at different scales, and can be readily validated by upcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims a clear flavor hierarchy in jet energy-energy correlator (EEC) distributions for both vacuum and quark-gluon plasma, where the magnitude of EEC decreases and the peak position shifts to larger angles from light to charm to bottom jets; however, this hierarchy and the rich medium-modification pattern (suppression at intermediate angles, enhancement at small and large angles) depend on the interplay of mass effect, energy loss, medium-induced radiation, medium response, and trigger bias, with the small-angle enhancement for inclusive jets being attributable to trigger bias rather than being a universal feature.

## Method

The authors simulate jet production in p+p collisions using Pythia 8, then feed the partons from vacuum parton shower into the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model for evolution inside a quark-gluon plasma described by CLVisc hydrodynamics. The LBT model includes elastic scatterings (with Debye screening mass) and inelastic scatterings via higher-twist gluon bremsstrahlung, tracking jet partons, emitted gluons, recoil partons, and negative partons (medium response). Final-state partons are hadronized via string fragmentation in Pythia, and jets are reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm (R=0.4) using charged tracks with pT>1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Jet EEC in p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV is shown for three pT^jet ranges: (20,40) GeV, (40,60) GeV, and (60,80) GeV, with ALICE data for charged hadron jets used for comparison.
- For heavy quarks, the dead-cone angle is theta_0 ~ m_Q/E: 0.013 for charm quarks (m_c=1.3 GeV) and 0.042 for bottom quarks (m_b=4.2 GeV) at E=100 GeV.
- The LBT simulation uses strong coupling alpha_s=0.15, a static medium with temperature T=300 MeV for 4 fm/c in the single-parton study, and a QGP initial time of 0.6 fm/c with hadronization cutoff at T=165 MeV for heavy-ion collisions.
- Jets in A+A collisions are studied for central 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV with pT^jet>40 GeV and |eta^jet|<0.5.

## Context

This work extends the realistic simulation of jet EEC in heavy-ion collisions from Yang et al. (2023), which studied gamma-triggered jets, to include heavy flavor jets (charm and bottom) and a complete treatment of medium response, energy loss, and trigger bias. It builds on earlier semi-analytical studies of jet EEC in static media by Andres et al. (2022, 2023) and Barata et al. (2023), and on the first observation of the dead-cone effect in QCD by ALICE (2021). The results enable future experimental validation at LHC and provide a framework for probing jet-medium interaction at multiple angular scales through flavor-tagged jet EEC measurements.
