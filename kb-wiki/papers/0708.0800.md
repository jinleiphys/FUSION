# Elliptic flow in the Gaussian model of eccentricity fluctuations

> We discuss a specific model of elliptic flow fluctuations due to Gaussian fluctuations in the initial spatial $x$ and $y$ eccentricity components $\left\{\mean{(σ_y^2-σ_x^2)/(σ_x^2+σ_y^2)}, \mean{2σ_{xy}/(σ_x^2+σ_y^2)} \right\}$. We find that in this model $\vfour$, elliptic flow determined from 4-particle cumulants, exactly equals the average flow value in the reaction plane coordinate system, $\mean{v_{RP}}$, the relation which, in an approximate form, was found earlier by Bhalerao and Ollitrault in a more general analysis, but under the same assumption that $v_2$ is proportional to the initial system eccentricity. We further show that in the Gaussian model all higher order cumulants are equal to $\vfour$. Analysis of the distribution in the magnitude of the flow vector, the $Q-$distribution, reveals that it is totally defined by two parameters, $\vtwo$, the flow from 2-particle cumulants, and $\vfour$, thus providing equivalent information compared to the method of cumulants. The flow obtained from the $Q-$distribution is again $\vfour=\mean{v_{RP}}$.

## Key claim

Under the Gaussian model of eccentricity fluctuations, the elliptic flow measured via four-particle cumulants exactly equals the average flow in the reaction plane coordinate system, not just approximately as earlier found by Bhalerao and Ollitrault (2006) under the same assumption that flow is proportional to initial eccentricity.

## Method

The authors assume that the initial spatial eccentricity components in the reaction plane system follow a two-dimensional Gaussian distribution with equal widths in the x and y directions, and that the elliptic flow v2 is proportional to the participant eccentricity magnitude via a constant factor κ. They then analytically compute the flow vector distribution, the two-particle and four-particle cumulant flow values, and the Q-distribution (distribution of the flow vector magnitude) within this Gaussian ansatz, comparing the information content of cumulants versus Q-distribution fits.

## Key numbers

- For mid-central collisions (impact parameter 4 < b < 6 fm), the Gaussian fit to the εx distribution gives mean εRP = 0.1384 ± 0.0001 and width σ_ε = 0.0935 ± 0.0001; the Bessel-Gaussian fit to the εpart distribution gives mean εRP = 0.1344 ± 0.0002 and σ_ε = 0.0957 ± 0.0001.
- In the Gaussian model, v2{4}^4 = mean(v_RP)^4 exactly, meaning the fourth-order cumulant equals the reaction-plane flow.
- The difference v2{2}^2 - v2{4}^2 = 2 κ^2 σ_ε^2 + δ = 2 σ_v^2 + δ, where δ is the non-flow contribution and σ_v is the standard deviation of flow along the reaction plane axis.

## Context

This work extends the earlier approximate result of Bhalerao and Ollitrault (2006) to an exact equality within the Gaussian model, and shows that all higher-order cumulants (v2{6}, etc.) equal v2{4}. The paper demonstrates that under the Gaussian ansatz, Q-distribution fitting provides no additional information beyond the two cumulant values v2{2} and v2{4}, making it impossible to separate flow fluctuations from non-flow correlations using only the particles under study. This contradicts the approach of Wang (2006), who used a model where flow fluctuates only in the impact parameter direction and advocated spectator neutron detectors; the present model would yield zero results for that method.
