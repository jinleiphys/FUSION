# Symmetries and deformations in the spherical shell model

> We discuss symmetries of the spherical shell model that make contact with the geometric collective model of Bohr and Mottelson. The most celebrated symmetry of this kind is SU(3), which is the basis of Elliott's model of rotation. It corresponds to a deformed mean field induced by a quadrupole interaction in a single major oscillator shell N and can be generalized to include several major shells. As such, Elliott's SU(3) model establishes the link between the spherical shell model and the (quadrupole component of the) geometric collective model. We introduce the analogue symmetry induced by an octupole interaction in two major oscillator shells N-1 and N, leading to an octupole-deformed solution of the spherical shell model. We show that in the limit of large oscillator shells (large N) the algebraic octupole interaction tends to that of the geometric collective model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Elliott's SU(3) symmetry, which links the spherical shell model to the quadrupole component of the geometric collective model, can be extended to an analogous octupole-induced symmetry in two major oscillator shells (N-1 and N), and that in the limit of large oscillator shells (N → ∞) the algebraic octupole interaction converges to that of the geometric collective model, but this convergence is only shown in the asymptotic limit, not for finite shell sizes.

## Method

The authors construct algebraic generators for U(4Λ) and its subalgebras U_L(Λ) and U_ST(4) using second-quantized fermion operators in both ℓs-coupled and jj-coupled representations, deriving commutation relations for the orbital generators. They then apply this algebraic framework to define SU(3) symmetry for quadrupole deformation in a single major oscillator shell and extend it to an octupole symmetry by considering two adjacent major oscillator shells (N-1 and N), showing that the algebraic octupole interaction reproduces the geometric collective model in the large-N limit.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle dimension of the valence space is Λ = Σ_ℓ (2ℓ+1), with total single-particle dimension 4Λ for neutrons and protons including spin–isospin degrees of freedom.
- The U_ST(4) algebra has 15 generators: number, spin (3), isospin (3), and spin–isospin (9) operators.
- The quadratic Casimir operator of U_L(Λ) is given by Ĉ₂[U_L(Λ)] = 4 Σ_{ℓℓ'} Σ_λ (-)^{ℓ+ℓ'} Ĝ^{(λ)}(ℓℓ')·Ĝ^{(λ)}(ℓ'ℓ).
- In the limit N → ∞, the algebraic octupole interaction tends to that of the geometric collective model.

## Context

This work extends Elliott's SU(3) model of rotation (Elliott, 1958) which established the connection between the spherical shell model and the geometric collective model of Bohr and Mottelson (Bohr, 1952; Bohr and Mottelson, 1953). The paper introduces an octupole-deformed solution of the spherical shell model as an analogue to Elliott's quadrupole symmetry, enabling future microscopic descriptions of octupole correlations in heavy nuclei such as those observed in ²²⁴Ra (Gaffney, 2013).
