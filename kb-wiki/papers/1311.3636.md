# Multiplicity distributions in p+p, p+A and A+A collisions from Yang-Mills dynamics

> We compute transverse momentum and momentum integrated multiplicity distributions consistently in the IP-Glasma model for proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at the LHC, in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC, and in heavy ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. Several sources of sub-nucleon scale contributions to the multiplicity distributions are identified. Our results, which are constrained by inclusive and diffractive deeply inelastic scattering data from HERA, are compared to measured distributions for a range of collision energies. These results are an essential first step in quantifying the relative role of initial and final state effects on multiparticle correlations in light and heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The IP-Glasma model, constrained by HERA deep inelastic scattering data, can consistently compute multiplicity distributions across p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions, but the energy dependence of multiplicities varies by at most ~15% within the studied parameter ranges, and additional sources of sub-nucleon fluctuations beyond color charge density variations are required to describe high-multiplicity events in small systems.

## Method
The authors solve classical Yang-Mills equations on a lattice for the Glasma fields generated in nuclear collisions, using the IP-Sat dipole model to extract the saturation scale Q_s from HERA data and convert it to color charge densities via the McLerran-Venugopalan model. They include event-by-event fluctuations of nucleon positions, sub-nucleon scale color charge fluctuations, and for small systems, additional Gaussian fluctuations of the gluon number at fixed Q_s. The gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity is computed in transverse Coulomb gauge after evolving the fields to τ = 0.4 fm, then converted to charged particle pseudorapidity density using a Jacobian transformation with effective mass and momentum parameters.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at √s = 2.76 TeV, the model underestimates Pb+Pb data by about 30% at low N_part and 15% at high N_part after fixing normalization to Au+Au.
- The infrared regulator m = 0.1 GeV describes RHIC data best; a smaller mass is preferred for LHC data.
- The ratio of Pb+Pb (2.76 TeV) to Au+Au (200 GeV) multiplicities varies by ~15% depending on the choice of running coupling scale (μ̃ = 0.5 k_T vs. μ̃ = 0.5 Q_s^max).
- The cutoff distance r_max ≈ 1.2 fm (approximately twice the gluonic radius R_g ≈ 0.6 fm) is used to remove numerical noise from exponential tails of the thickness function.
- Lattice parameters: N=400 sites with a=0.03 fm (L=12 fm) for p+p and p+A; N=600 with L=30 fm for A+A.

## Context
This work extends the IP-Glasma model previously applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions (Schenke 2012) by eliminating simplifying assumptions in constructing nuclear color charge distributions and incorporating improved IP-Sat fits to combined HERA data (Rezaeian 2012). It enables systematic comparison of initial-state effects across system sizes, which is topical for understanding the "ridge" correlations observed in p/d+A collisions that show sensitivity to both initial-state (Dusling 2012) and final-state (Bzdak 2013, Bozek 2013) effects. The results provide a foundation for quantifying the relative role of initial and final state effects in multiparticle correlations, and for future work incorporating Balitsky-JIMWLK evolution and 3+1-D Yang-Mills dynamics.
