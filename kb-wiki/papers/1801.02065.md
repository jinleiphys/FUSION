# Investigation of ground-state properties of even-even and odd Pb isotopes within Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> The nuclear structure of even-even and odd lead isotopes (178-236 Pb) is investigated within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. Calculations are performed for a wide range of neutron numbers, starting from the proton-rich side up to the neutron-rich side, by using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction and a new proposed formula for the pairing strength which is more precise for this region of nuclei as we did in previous works in the regions of Neodymium (Nd, Z=60) [Int. J. Mod. Phys. E 24, 1550073 (2015)] and Molybdenum (Mo, Z=42) [Nuc. Phys. A 957 22-32 (2017)]. Such a new pairing strength formula allows reaching exotic nuclei region where the experimental data are not available. Calculated values of various physical quantities such as binding energy, two-neutron separation energy, and rms-radii for protons and neutrons are discussed and compared with experimental data and some estimates of other nuclear models like Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM), Relativistic Mean Field (RMF), Density-Dependent Meson-Exchange Relativistic Energy Functional (DD-ME2) and results of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations based on the D1S Gogny effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (Gogny D1S).

## Key claim

The authors claim that their new pairing-strength formula \( V_0^{n,p} = 7.28\,A^{3/4} \) yields more accurate ground-state properties for lead isotopes than the default constant pairing strength, but they acknowledge that the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) still gives a lower rms deviation for binding energy per nucleon (0.0028 MeV vs. their 0.0086 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the HFBTHO (v2.00d) code with the SLy4 Skyrme functional and a zero-range mixed surface-volume pairing interaction. They fitted the pairing strength for neutrons and protons (taken equal) to reproduce experimental binding energies for each lead isotope, then derived a mass-dependent formula \( V_0^{n,p} = 7.28\,A^{3/4} \). Odd-N isotopes were treated using the equal-filling approximation with blocking of quasi-particle states, and calculations used a quasi-particle cutoff of 60 MeV, 20 oscillator shells, and 500 basis states.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon rms deviation from experiment: 0.0086 MeV (this work), compared to 0.0028 MeV (FRDM), 0.0130 MeV (RMF), 0.0193 MeV (Gogny D1S), and 0.0105 MeV (DD-ME2)
- Two-neutron separation energy rms deviation from experiment: 0.9679 MeV (this work), compared to 0.2394 MeV (FRDM), 1.1343 MeV (RMF), 1.1948 MeV (Gogny D1S), and 0.9847 MeV (DD-ME2)
- Charge radius rms deviation from experiment: less than 0.024 fm (this work and Gogny D1S), and less than 0.018 fm for other models
- Neutron skin thickness for \( ^{236}\text{Pb} \): 0.308 fm (this work), compared to 0.370 fm (DD-ME2) and 0.260 fm (Gogny D1S)
- Maximum binding energy per nucleon observed at neutron number \( N = 126 \)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB studies on Neodymium (Bassem and Oulne, 2015) and Molybdenum (Bassem and Oulne, 2017) isotopic chains, where the same pairing-strength fitting procedure was applied. The results are compared against several established models: FRDM (Moller et al., 1997), RMF with NL3 functional (Lalazissis et al., 1997), DD-ME2 (Lalazissis et al., 2005), and HFB with Gogny D1S force (Amedee et al., 2009). The new pairing formula enables calculations for exotic lead isotopes where experimental data are unavailable, and the authors suggest their approach could be tested on other isotopic chains to assess its general validity.
