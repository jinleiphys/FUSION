# Nuclear Modification Factor in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.02 TeV at LHC energies using Boltzmann Transport Equation with Tsallis Blast Wave Description

> In this article, we have studied the nuclear modification factor measured in Pb-Pb collisions ($R_{PbPb}$) for $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p+\bar{p}$, $K^{*0} + \bar{K^{*0}}$, $ϕ$ and in p-Pb collisions ($R_{pPb}$) for $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p+\bar{p}$ at Large hadron collider (LHC) energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV for the most central and peripheral collisions. We have also analysed the experimental data of transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra for these identified hadrons at LHC for Pb-Pb as well as for p-Pb collisions. We have used Boltzmann transport equation (BTE) in relaxation time approximation (RTA) for this analysis. The Tsallis statistics is used as an initial distribution function and The Tsallis blast wave (TBW) model is employed as an equilibrium distribution in BTE. The present model fits the measured transverse momentum spectra, $R_{PbPb}$, and $R_{pPb}$ successfully upto $p_T$ = 8 GeV with a reasonable $χ^2/ndf$ for all the considered hadrons at various centralities. The experimental data for $R_{pPb}$ are generated using the particle yields at pPb and pp collisions where number of binary collisions are taken from Glauber model calculations. We find that the average transverse flow velocity ($<β_r>$) follows the mass and centrality ordering and decreases with the mass as well as when one move from the central collisions to peripheral collisions. These findings are inline with the results of the hydrodynamical calculations.

# Nuclear Modification Factor in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.02 TeV at LHC energies using Boltzmann Transport Equation with Tsallis Blast Wave Description

## Key claim

The Boltzmann Transport Equation in relaxation time approximation, using Tsallis statistics as the initial distribution and the Tsallis Blast Wave model as the equilibrium distribution, successfully describes the nuclear modification factor and transverse momentum spectra for identified hadrons up to $p_T$ = 8 GeV/c in both Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV, but only when the kinetic freeze-out temperature is treated as a fixed parameter (not fitted) and the non-extensive parameter $q_{AA}$ deviates from unity, indicating the system does not achieve full equilibrium.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann Transport Equation in relaxation time approximation using the Tsallis distribution as the initial distribution function and the Tsallis Blast Wave model as the equilibrium distribution function. They fitted the transverse momentum spectra and nuclear modification factors $R_{PbPb}$ for $\pi^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p+\bar{p}$, $(K^{*0}+\bar{K^{*0}})/2$, and $\phi$ mesons in Pb-Pb collisions, and $R_{pPb}$ for $\pi^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p+\bar{p}$ in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV for both central and peripheral collisions. The $R_{pPb}$ data were generated from particle yields in p-Pb and pp collisions using Glauber model calculations for the number of binary collisions. The kinetic freeze-out temperature was fixed at values taken from prior Tsallis Blast Wave studies: 110 MeV for central Pb-Pb, 96 MeV for peripheral Pb-Pb, 120 MeV for central p-Pb, and 100 MeV for peripheral p-Pb.

## Key numbers

- The model fits transverse momentum spectra and nuclear modification factors up to $p_T$ = 8 GeV/c for all considered hadrons at various centralities
- Average transverse flow velocity $<\beta_r>$ for pions in central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions: 0.639; for kaons: 0.584; for protons: 0.536
- Average transverse flow velocity $<\beta_r>$ for pions in peripheral (70-80%) Pb-Pb collisions: 0.481; for kaons: 0.469; for protons: 0.351
- Average transverse flow velocity $<\beta_r>$ for $\phi$ mesons in central (0-10%) Pb-Pb collisions: 0.423; in peripheral (70-80%) Pb-Pb: 0.268
- Average transverse flow velocity $<\beta_r>$ for pions in central (0-5%) p-Pb collisions: 0.536; for kaons: 0.501; for protons: 0.447
- Non-extensive parameter $q_{AA}$ for pions in central Pb-Pb: 1.030; in peripheral Pb-Pb: 1.047
- Non-extensive parameter $q_{AA}$ for $\phi$ mesons in central Pb-Pb: 1.062; in peripheral Pb-Pb: 1.100
- Tsallis temperature $T_{ts}$ for pions in central Pb-Pb: 0.087 GeV; in peripheral Pb-Pb: 0.033 GeV
- $\chi^2/ndf$ values range from 0.170 (pions in central p-Pb) to 5.114 (protons in peripheral p-Pb)

## Context

This work extends prior studies using the Boltzmann Transport Equation with relaxation time approximation, where Tripathy et al. (2017) used the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave as the equilibrium distribution and successfully fitted $R_{PbPb}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV up to $p_T$ = 8 GeV/c, but found that $t_f/\tau$ did not show mass ordering and $<\beta_r>$ for protons and $K^{*0}$ did not follow hydrodynamic behavior. The present work replaces the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave with the Tsallis Blast Wave model, following the approach of Che et al. (2020) who used TBW to describe spectra up to $p_T$ = 3 GeV/c in Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and Xe-Xe collisions. The results enable future investigation of the centrality dependence of the $t_f/\tau$ ratio, which the authors note does not exhibit clear centrality-dependent behavior and warrants further study.
