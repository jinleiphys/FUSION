# How the X(5568) challenges our understanding of QCD

> We discuss the X(5568) particle recently announced by the D0 Collaboration. Several types of models were proposed to explain this structure in the literature. As pointed out by Burns and Swanson (arXiv:1603.04366), none of them provides a satisfactory description of the observation. We provide additional arguments using general properties of QCD, and conclude that the observation of the X(5568), if confirmed, poses serious challenges to our understanding of nonperturbative QCD.

## Key claim

If the X(5568) observation is confirmed, it poses serious challenges to our understanding of nonperturbative QCD because none of the proposed models (tetraquark, hadronic molecule, or threshold effect) provides a satisfactory description when examined using general arguments from chiral symmetry and heavy quark symmetry.

## Method

The authors apply general QCD principles, chiral symmetry and its spontaneous breaking, and heavy quark flavor symmetry, to evaluate the viability of proposed interpretations for the X(5568) structure reported by the D0 Collaboration. They estimate the expected mass of a tetraquark state using chiral symmetry arguments, examine the chiral suppression of pion interactions for molecular interpretations, and use heavy quark symmetry to predict the mass of a charmed partner state, comparing it to known experimental candidates.

## Key numbers

- X(5568) mass: \( M = (5567.8 \pm 2.9^{+0.9}_{-1.9}) \) MeV, width: \( \Gamma = (21.9 \pm 6.4^{+5.0}_{-2.5}) \) MeV, with a significance of 5.1 sigma, from D0 Collaboration \( p\bar p \) collisions at \( \sqrt{s} = 1.96 \) TeV.
- The X(5568) peak lies about 13 MeV above the \( B_s^*\pi^\pm \) threshold.
- The predicted charmed partner mass is \( M_{X_c} \simeq (2.24 \pm 0.15) \) GeV, obtained from \( M_{X(5568)} - \bar M_{B_s} + \bar M_{D_s} \), where \( \bar M_{B_s} = 5.403 \) GeV and \( \bar M_{D_s} = 2.076 \) GeV are spin-averaged masses.
- The \( D_{s0}^*(2317) \) meson has a width less than 3.8 MeV and is an isoscalar, while the X(5568) would be isovector, so they cannot be heavy quark symmetry partners.

## Context

This work sharpens the conclusions of Burns and Swanson (2016), who pointed out difficulties in interpreting the X(5568) as a tetraquark, hadronic molecule, or threshold effect. The authors extend those arguments using chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1978, Gasser 1983) and heavy quark symmetry (Manohar 2000) to show that the proposed interpretations are strongly suppressed or inconsistent with QCD expectations. The analysis suggests searching for the X(5568) in dipion decays of excited bottom-strange mesons like \( B_{s2}^*(5840) \to B_s\pi\pi \), and notes that the LHCb Collaboration did not see a corresponding signal in their \( pp \) collision data.
