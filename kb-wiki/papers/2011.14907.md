# Spin polarization dynamics in the Gubser-expanding background

> Evolution of spin polarization in the Gubser-expanding conformal perfect-fluid hydrodynamic background is studied. The analysis of the conformal transformation properties of the conservation laws is extended to the case of the angular momentum conservation. The explicit forms of equations of motion for spin components are derived and analysed, and some special solutions are found.

## Key claim

The paper extends the study of spin polarization dynamics from the previously considered Bjorken flow (boost-invariant and transversely homogeneous) to the Gubser-expanding background (boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric with nontrivial transverse flow), finding that the angular momentum conservation is conformally invariant only if the spin tensor is antisymmetric in all indices and the particles are massless, which the GLW spin tensor does not generally satisfy.

## Method

The authors first solve the perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations using Gubser symmetry arguments in de Sitter coordinates, obtaining analytical solutions for hydrodynamic variables. They then extend the analysis of conformal transformation properties of conservation laws to angular momentum conservation, deriving equations of motion for spin polarization components in the de Sitter spacetime. The spin polarization tensor components are decomposed using a four-vector basis in the Gubser-symmetric background, and the resulting equations are studied both analytically (for massless particles) and numerically (for finite masses) before transforming back to Milne spacetime.

## Key numbers

* The conformal weight of the energy density and pressure is 4, with transformation rule: $\cE(\tau,r) = \hat{\cE}(\rho)/\tau^4$, $\cP(\tau,r) = \hat{\cP}(\rho)/\tau^4$
* The conformal weight of the net baryon density is 3, with transformation: $\cN(\tau,r) = \hat{\cN}(\rho)/\tau^3$
* Temperature and chemical potential have conformal weight 1, transforming as: $T(\tau,r) = \hat{T}(\rho)/\tau$, $\mu(\tau,r) = \hat{\mu}(\rho)/\tau$
* The conformal weight of the spin polarization tensor $\omega^{\alpha\beta}$ is 2, while the spin polarization components $a_i$ and $b_i$ are conformally invariant with weight 0
* The conformal weight of the energy-momentum tensor is 6, and the spin tensor also has conformal weight 6
* The Gubser symmetry introduces an inverse length scale $q$ that parametrizes the special conformal transformations

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization dynamics from the Bjorken flow background considered in Florkowski (2019) to the Gubser-expanding background, which includes nontrivial transverse flow. The formalism builds on the relativistic hydrodynamics with spin framework developed in Florkowski (2017, 2018, 2019) and uses the GLW spin tensor from DeGroot (1980). The results enable testing of full 3+1D numerical codes for heavy-ion collisions and describe collisions of systems with initial spin polarization.
