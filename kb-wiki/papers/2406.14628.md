# Gluon radiation inside a flowing medium

> We compute the spectrum of gluons emitted by a highly energetic quark inside a flowing QCD medium, focusing on the soft gluon limit at first order in the opacity expansion. Specifically, we derive the leading energy-suppressed corrections to the double differential final parton distribution, and show that they are substantial for both static and flowing matter. In particular, we demonstrate that the corrections due to the transverse flow become large even at moderate energies and flow velocities, affecting drastically both the shape and magnitude of the spectrum. We observe that the final transverse momentum of the emitted gluon tends to align along the flow direction, resulting in a non-trivial azimuthal distribution. These results can be directly implemented into the estimation of multiple observables to get a better understanding of the jet-medium interaction processes in HICs.

## Key claim

The authors compute the medium-induced soft gluon spectrum at first order in the opacity expansion, including the leading energy-suppressed (subeikonal) corrections, and show that transverse flow of the QCD medium produces substantial modifications to both the shape and magnitude of the spectrum even at moderate energies and flow velocities, with the emitted gluon's transverse momentum tending to align along the flow direction.

## Method

The calculation extends the formalism of Sadofyev (2021) to spin-1 gluons in the soft gluon limit (x << 1) at first order in the opacity expansion, using the Gyulassy-Wang model for the scattering potential and working in axial gauge with scalar quarks in the fundamental representation. The medium is modeled by a classical stochastic color field generated by moving quasi-particle sources, assuming transverse homogeneity and taking the broad source approximation, with the authors computing all ten diagrams (three single-Born and seven double-Born) contributing at this order and performing the medium averages assuming Gaussian statistics for the color field.

## Key numbers

- The subeikonal corrections scale as O(1/(xE) * (perp^2/(xE) * z)^n) with n >= 0, where x is the gluon energy fraction, E is the initial quark energy, and perp is the characteristic transverse momentum scale
- The LPM phases scale as (perp^2/(xE) * z)^n ~ O(1) and must be kept to all orders
- The screening scale (Debye mass) mu is controlled by the medium temperature, with the assumption that mu(z_i - z_0) >> 1 for the medium to be diluted and extended enough
- The single-source scattering potential in the GW model is v(q) = g^2/(q^2 - mu^2), where g is the effective in-medium strong coupling

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for jet-medium interactions in heavy-ion collisions beyond the static, homogeneous medium approximation, building on the formalism developed by Sadofyev (2021) and Kuzmin (2023) for subeikonal corrections. The results enable direct implementation into calculations of multiple observables to better characterize the quark-gluon plasma evolution, addressing limitations of earlier phenomenological models by Gyulassy (2000, 2001), Baier (1998, 2006), and others that relied on strong simplifying assumptions or focused solely on longitudinal expansion.
