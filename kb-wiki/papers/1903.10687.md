# Kaonic Nuclear Clusters --- a New Paradigm of Particle and Nuclear Physics ---

> Lambda*=Lambda(1405) plays an essential role in forming kaonic nuclear clusters (KNC). The simplest KNC, K-pp, has the structure Lambda*p=(K-p){I=0}p, where a real kaon migrates between two nucleons, mediating super-strong Lambda*-p$ attraction. Production data of K-pp have been accumulated by DISTO, J-PARC E27 and J-PARC E15 experiments. In the case of K-K-pp, the attractive covalent bond of Lambda*Lambda* is doubly enhanced compared to the Lambda*-p one. As a consequence, a m-th Lambda* multiplet, (Lambda*)m, with m(m-1)/2 bonds becomes more stable than its corresponding neutron aggregate, (n)m, with m=8 ~ 12. This may suggest the possible existence of stable Lambda* matter. The production of K-K-pp by high-energy pp or heavy-ion collisions is awaited as a doorway to so-far unknown Lambda* matter.

# Kaonic Nuclear Clusters, a New Paradigm of Particle and Nuclear Physics

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, interpreted as a $(K^-p)^{I=0}$ bound state, can form multi-body clusters $(\Lambda^*)_m$ that become more stable than the corresponding neutron aggregates $(n)_m$ for $m = 8$ to $12$, potentially leading to stable "$\Lambda^*$ matter." This claim is hedged by the condition that it depends on using the "DISTO" phenomenological interaction fitted to experimental binding energies, and that a serious problem remains in reconciling the observed two-peak structure of $K^-pp$ with specific predicted level energies.

## Method

The authors construct the $\Lambda^*p$ interaction from experimental $K^-pp$ production data (DISTO, J-PARC E27, J-PARC E15) and then derive the $\Lambda^*\Lambda^*$ interaction by adding one more kaon to the $(K^-p)-p$ system using a Heitler-London-type formulation. Binding energies of $(\Lambda^*)_m$ multiplets for $m = 2, 4, 6, 8$ are calculated via the ATMS variational method, and compared with Stochastic Variational Method (SVM) calculations by Nemura (2018) that include full anti-symmetrization and higher-order correlations. The authors also analyze the Bethe-Goldstone equation for $\bar KN$ pairs in nuclear matter to demonstrate the instability of mean-field descriptions.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: $\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}$
- Experimental $K^-pp$ binding energies: two peaks observed at $BE \sim 50\ \text{MeV}$ and $BE \sim 100\ \text{MeV}$
- "Chiral" theoretical predictions give $BE \sim 20\ \text{MeV}$; "phenomenological" predictions give $BE \sim 50\ \text{MeV}$
- "Chiral" calculation by Sekihara et al. gives $BE \sim 16\ \text{MeV}$
- Momentum transfer in DISTO reaction: $Q \sim 1.6\ \text{GeV}/c$
- $\bar KN$ "wound" integral from Bethe-Goldstone equation: 0.45, which is 15 times larger than that for $NN$
- For $(\Lambda^*)_8$ with "DISTO" interaction: binding energy per $\Lambda^*$ is $BE_m/m \sim 473\ \text{MeV}$; $\Lambda^*$ separation energy $S_8(\Lambda^*) \sim 945\ \text{MeV}$
- Nemura's SVM calculation for $(\Lambda^*)_8$ gives $BE_m/m = 238\ \text{MeV}$
- Hrtánková et al.'s RMF calculation for $(\Lambda^*)_8$ gives $BE_m/m = 50\ \text{MeV}$, saturating at $\sim 80\ \text{MeV}$ for $m > 120$
- RMF at $m=\infty$: 87 MeV at $k_F = 2.15\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, with $\langle T \rangle = 38\ \text{MeV}$, $\langle V_\omega \rangle_{\text{Born}}\rho_B = 316\ \text{MeV}$, $\langle V_\sigma \rangle_{\text{Born}}\rho_s = -441\ \text{MeV}$
- Relativistic effect estimated at roughly 10 MeV for $(\Lambda^*)_8$ within local-density approximation

## Context

This work extends the authors' own 2002 prediction of deeply-bound anti-kaonic nuclear clusters (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002) and their subsequent studies of kaonic-proton matter (Akaishi 2017). It directly contradicts the Relativistic Mean Field calculations of Gazda et al. (2008), who concluded that $\bar K$ separation energy saturates at kaon numbers larger than about 10 and does not exceed 200 MeV, and of Hrtánková et al. (2018), who claimed $\Lambda^*$ matter is totally unstable. The paper supports its scenario using Nemura's (2018) Stochastic Variational Method calculations, which include multi-cluster correlations that the RMF approaches neglect. The work suggests that production of $K^-K^-pp$ in high-energy $pp$ or heavy-ion collisions could serve as a doorway to exploring $\Lambda^*$ matter, and speculates on implications for primordial anti-quark survival in the early universe.
