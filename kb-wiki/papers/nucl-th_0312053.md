# Superconductivity with deformed Fermi surfaces and compact stars

> I discuss the deformed Fermi surface superconductivity (DFS) and some of its alternatives in the context of nucleonic superfluids and two flavor color superconductors that may exist in the densest regions of compact stellar objects.

## Key claim

Deformed Fermi surface superconductivity (DFS) may provide a lower-energy ground state than the LOFF (Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell) phase for flavor-asymmetric quark matter in compact stars, but this conclusion depends on the assumption of a single wave-vector LOFF phase and the specific choice of deformation parameters.

## Method

The author derives self-consistent gap equations and number-density equations for asymmetric BCS superconductors, first in a non-relativistic framework and then for relativistic two-flavor color superconductors using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio effective model. The free energy is minimized at fixed density and temperature as a function of the center-of-mass momentum of Cooper pairs (for the LOFF phase) and the quadrupole deformation parameters of the Fermi surfaces (for the DFS phase), with particle number conservation enforced throughout.

## Key numbers

- The Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit for the homogeneous BCS state is at a chemical potential difference of δμ₁ = Δ(0)/√2, where Δ(0) is the zero-asymmetry gap.
- The weak-coupling critical shift for the LOFF phase is δμ₂ = 0.755 Δ(0), compared to δμ₁ = 0.707 Δ(0) for the homogeneous state.
- For the combined LOFF-DFS analysis in non-relativistic systems, the pure LOFF phase ground state corresponds to a center-of-mass momentum Q ≈ 0.5 in units of the Fermi momentum pF.
- The pure DFS phase shows minima at relative deformations δε ≈ -0.8 and δε ≈ 0.55 for prolate and oblate deformations respectively.
- For the two-flavor color superconducting DFS phase at baryon density ρB = 0.31 fm⁻³ and temperature T = 2 MeV, the free energy minimum occurs at deformation parameter εA ≈ 0.25, with the physically relevant flavor asymmetry range being 0.1 ≤ α ≤ 0.3 for charge-neutral matter under beta equilibrium.

## Context

This mini-review extends the study of asymmetric superconductivity from metallic superconductors with paramagnetic impurities (Clogston, 1962; Chandrasekhar, 1962) and the LOFF phase (Larkin and Ovchinnikov, 1964; Fulde and Ferrell, 1964) to nucleonic superfluids and two-flavor color superconductors in compact stars. The work enables future investigation of whether the DFS phase is universally favored over the LOFF phase when more realistic lattice structures and multiple wave-vector condensates are considered, and it provides a framework for computing transport properties such as heat capacity and thermal conductivity in these phases.
