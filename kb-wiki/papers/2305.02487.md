# Light nuclei production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV from coalescence model

> The nucleon coalescence model is one of the most popular theoretical models for light nuclei production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The production of light nuclei $d$, $t$, $^{3}$He, and $^{4}$He is studied using the transport model JAM with a simplified afterburner coalescence at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV Au+Au collisions. We scan the cut-off of phenomenological coalescence parameters, the relative spatial distance $ΔR$ and momentum difference $ΔP$, for formation of light nuclei by nucleon coalescence to reproduce the light nuclei $p_{\rm T}$ spectra measured by STAR experiment. The results indicate a potential connection between the coalescence parameters and the binding energy as well as the diameter of these light nuclei.

# Light nuclei production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV from coalescence model

## Key claim
The coalescence parameters (relative spatial distance ΔR and momentum difference ΔP) for light nuclei formation show a potential connection to the binding energy and diameter of the nuclei, but the extracted parameters are only valid after applying a correction factor based on the ratio of measured to modeled proton spectra raised to the power of the mass number A.

## Method
The JAM transport model in mean-field mode (incompressibility parameter κ = 380 MeV) was used to generate nucleon phase-space distributions for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3 GeV. A simple afterburner coalescence was applied at a fixed time of 50 fm/c, scanning ΔR from 2 to 6 fm and ΔP from 0.1 to 0.5 GeV/c for each light nucleus species (d, t, 3He, 4He). The resulting transverse momentum spectra were compared to STAR data using χ² minimization to determine optimal coalescence parameters.

## Key numbers
- Optimal coalescence parameters at mid-rapidity (-0.1 < y < 0): d (ΔR = 5.6 fm, ΔP = 0.22 GeV/c), t (ΔR = 5.6 fm, ΔP = 0.22 GeV/c), 3He (ΔR = 5.2 fm, ΔP = 0.22 GeV/c), 4He (ΔR = 3 fm, ΔP = 0.42 GeV/c)
- Average values across all rapidity bins: d (ΔR = 5.78 fm, ΔP = 0.219 GeV/c), t (ΔR = 5.43 fm, ΔP = 0.237 GeV/c), 3He (ΔR = 5.24 fm, ΔP = 0.242 GeV/c), 4He (ΔR = 4.585 fm, ΔP = 0.3 GeV/c)
- Product ΔR·ΔP at low ΔP region: 1.2 for d and t, 1.15 for 3He and 4He
- Proton spectra from JAM agree with STAR data for pT < 1.4 GeV/c but overestimate at higher pT

## Context
This work extends coalescence model studies of light nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions, following prior work by the STAR Collaboration (2022) and others using JAM at √sNN = 3-200 GeV. The study provides systematic extraction of coalescence parameters for four light nuclei species, enabling predictions for other collision energies and systems. The results suggest that the radius and binding energy of light nuclei are crucial for their formation, and the extracted parameters can be used to study collective flow and equation-of-state effects in ongoing work.
