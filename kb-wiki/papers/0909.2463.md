# Distribution of Resonant Eigenvalues of Quantum Potential Scattering

> We formulate the Born approximation for finding resonance poles in the complex plane for potential scattering problems. Using the method, we study the distribution of resonance poles for several scattering potentials. In particular, we find for an exponential potential with a cutoff that the cutoff generates an infinite series of extra resonance poles below and along the real axis, which would not exist without the cutoff. We also find for a Gaussian potential that the series of resonance poles approach the imaginary axis of the complex energy plane from left. In other words, the real parts of the resonant eigenenergis are all negative.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Born approximation method for finding resonance poles in the complex energy plane for potential scattering, and shows that for an exponential potential with a discontinuous cutoff, the cutoff generates an infinite series of extra resonance poles below and along the real axis that would not exist without the cutoff, making it dangerous to introduce a cutoff carelessly for numerical calculations.

## Method

The author formulates a Born approximation for the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in one-dimensional quantum scattering, replacing the perturbed wave function in the integrand with the unperturbed wave function. The Siegert condition (zeros of the coefficient of the incoming wave) is then used to find resonance poles in the complex wave-number plane for several symmetric potentials: a single exponential, multiple exponentials, an exponential with oscillatory modulation, an exponential with a cutoff, and a Gaussian potential.

## Key numbers

- For a single exponential potential \(V(x) = -V_0 e^{-\kappa_0 |x|}\), the Born approximation yields a pair of anti-bound states on the negative imaginary axis at \(k_\text{res} = -\frac{i}{2}\left(\kappa_0 + \sigma\frac{V_0}{\kappa_0}\right)\), where \(\sigma = +1\) for even solutions and \(\sigma = -1\) for odd solutions.
- For an exponential potential with oscillation \(V(x) = -V_0 \cos(\omega_0 x) e^{-\kappa_0 |x|}\), each anti-bound state splits into a resonant and anti-resonant pair at \(k_\text{res} = \pm\frac{1}{2}\omega_0\left(1 - \sigma\frac{V_0/2}{\kappa_0^2 + \omega_0^2}\right) - \frac{i}{2}\kappa_0\left(1 + \sigma\frac{V_0/2}{\kappa_0^2 + \omega_0^2}\right)\).
- For an exponential potential with a cutoff at \(|x| = b\), the resonance poles for large \(n\) are approximated by \(k_\text{res} \simeq \frac{(2n-1)\pi}{4b} - i\frac{\kappa_0}{2}\), with the leading correction given by Eq. (5-170) involving terms of order \(1/b\) and \(1/((2n-1)\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of resonance pole distributions beyond exactly solvable potentials (Eckart 1930, Jost 1951, Nussenzveig 1959, and others) by providing a Born approximation applicable to a wider class of scattering potentials. The results are motivated by the finding of Sasada (2005, 2009) that resonance poles far from the real axis affect resonance peaks through Fano asymmetry, opening the possibility of experimentally observing such poles. The paper enables future study of resonance pole distributions for unsolvable potentials and presents two conjectures on the distribution for a wider class of scattering potentials.
