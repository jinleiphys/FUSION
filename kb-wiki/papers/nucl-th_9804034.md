# Including Pions

> Recent progress in using effective field theory to describe systems with two nucleons is discussed with particular emphasis placed on the inclusion of pions. Inconsistencies arising in Weinberg's power counting are demonstrated with two concrete examples. A consistent power-counting scheme is discussed in which pion exchange is sub-leading to local four-nucleon operators. NN scattering in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels is calculated at sub-leading order and compared with data.

## Key claim

Weinberg's power-counting scheme for nuclear effective field theory is inconsistent because it requires counterterms at all orders in the momentum expansion to absorb divergences from iterated pion exchange, particularly in the 3S1-3D1 channel; a consistent scheme identifies the four-nucleon coupling C as scaling like Q^{-1} rather than Q^0, making pion exchange sub-leading to local four-nucleon operators.

## Method

The authors computed NN scattering amplitudes in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels using effective field theory with pions, employing Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS) dimensional regularization to define the theory. They solved the renormalization group equations for the four-nucleon couplings C_0, C_2, and D_2, then computed the scattering amplitude at leading order (Q^{-1}) and sub-leading order (Q^0) by summing bubble diagrams with local operators and including single potential pion exchange perturbatively. The phase shifts and mixing parameter were expanded order-by-order in Q and fitted to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths: a(1S0) = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm, a(3S1) = +5.425 ± 0.0014 fm
- The scale Lambda_NN = 8 pi f^2 / (g_A^2 M) ~ 300 MeV, where f = 132 MeV is the pion decay constant
- For the 1S0 channel at mu = m_pi: C_0 = -3.34 fm^2, D_2 = -0.42 fm^4, C_2 = 3.24 fm^4 (fit to p ≤ 200 MeV)
- For the 3S1-3D1 channel at mu = m_pi: C_0 = -5.51 fm^2, D_2 = 1.32 fm^4, C_2 = 9.91 fm^4 (fit to p ≤ 200 MeV)
- The expansion parameter is estimated as m_pi / Lambda_NN ~ 1/2

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990, 1991) by identifying and resolving inconsistencies in Weinberg's original power-counting scheme. The new power-counting scheme was proposed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and is applied here to NN scattering with explicit pions. The results enable systematic calculations of multi-nucleon systems with controlled theoretical uncertainties, though the expansion parameter of approximately 1/2 means convergence is slower than desired. The predictions for the 3S1-3D1 mixing parameter epsilon_1 and the D-wave phase shift delta_2 at sub-leading order contain no free parameters once C_0 is fixed from the S-wave phase shift.
