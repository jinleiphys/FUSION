# The chiral long-range two-pion exchange electromagnetic currents in radiative nucleon-deuteron capture

> The nucleon-deuteron radiative capture process is investigated using the chiral nuclear potentials and the electromagnetic currents developed by the Bochum-Bonn group. While the strong interaction is taken up to the next-to-next-to-leading order, the electromagnetic current consists of a single nucleon current, the leading one-pion exchange one and is supplemented by contributions from the long-range two-pion exchange current at next-to-leading-order. The theoretical predictions for the cross sections as well as analyzing powers show strong dependence on the values of regularization parameters. Only small effects of the three-nucleon force and the long-range two-pion exchange current are observed. The dependence on the choice of regularization parameters results in a big theoretical uncertainty and clearly points to the necessity to include corrections from higher orders of the chiral expansion both for the nuclear forces and currents.

# The chiral long-range two-pion exchange electromagnetic currents in radiative nucleon-deuteron capture

## Key claim
The effects of the long-range two-pion exchange electromagnetic current at next-to-leading order and the three-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-leading order on radiative nucleon-deuteron capture observables are small compared to the theoretical uncertainty arising from the choice of regularization parameters, indicating that consistent currents and interactions at higher chiral orders are required before meaningful comparisons with data can be made.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev-like equation for the radiative nucleon-deuteron capture process in momentum space using partial wave decomposition, employing chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials at next-to-next-to-leading order from Epelbaum and collaborators. The electromagnetic current operator included the single nucleon current, the leading one-pion exchange current, and the long-range two-pion exchange current at next-to-leading order developed by the Bochum-Bonn group, with calculations performed for five different sets of regularization parameters (Lambda, tilde-Lambda) and compared to predictions using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon force plus Urbana IX three-nucleon force with standard meson exchange currents.

## Key numbers
- Differential cross section and analyzing powers computed at deuteron laboratory energy E_d = 17.5 MeV, corresponding to photon energy 11.3 MeV in the centre-of-mass system
- Differential cross section and analyzing powers computed at deuteron laboratory energy E_d = 137 MeV, corresponding to photon energy 50.4 MeV in the centre-of-mass system
- Five regularization parameter sets (Lambda, tilde-Lambda) in MeV: (450,500), (600,500), (550,600), (450,700), (600,700)
- Experimental data for comparison taken at E_d = 133 MeV from KVI data for the higher energy case

## Context
This work extends the results of Skibinski (2014) by using explicit chiral electromagnetic currents instead of the Siegert approach, following the path charted by Rozpedzik (2014) who explored two-pion exchange currents mainly in deuteron photodisintegration. The study uses chiral potentials from Epelbaum (2005) and electromagnetic currents from Kolling (2011, 2012). The strong dependence on regularization parameters mirrors findings in nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order by Witala (2014) and Golak (2014), where unacceptably large theoretical uncertainties were also observed. The authors note that a new regularization scheme is expected to cure this situation and plan to revisit the nucleon-deuteron capture and other electromagnetic processes once it is available.
