# Investigation of subthreshold resonances with the Trojan horse method

> references added, typo corrected;6 pages, 1 figure, to be published in the AIP Proceedings of Fusion06, International Conference on Reaction Mechanisms and Nuclear Structure at the Coulomb Barrier, March 19-23, 2006, San Servolo, Italy

## Key claim

The Trojan Horse method can be used to investigate subthreshold resonances by exploiting the continuous transition from bound (E<0) to unbound (E>0) states in the A+x system, provided that an open channel C+c exists at the E=0 threshold.

## Method

The authors extend the inclusive breakup theory and the Trojan Horse formalism to negative relative energies, where the channel is closed and outgoing wave functions become exponentially decaying. They study a two-channel model with surface coupling, matching logarithmic derivatives at a boundary radius R to determine S-matrix elements, and show that the product S_lc * J_l^+ leads to a finite cross section at E=0 that can be continued smoothly to negative energies.

## Key numbers

- The modified shape function S-tilde is well defined for x^2<0, with a characteristic pole at x^2=-1 corresponding to the binding energy of the (A+x)=B system (from tables 3 or 4 of Typel (2004)).
- For neutral particles, the imaginary part of the phase shift scales as delta_l^I ~ (kR)^(2l+1), leading to |S_lc|^2 ~ (kR)^(2l+1).
- The product |S_lc * J_l^+|^2 scales as (kR)^3, and the cross section sigma ~ (1/k^3)|S_lc * J_l^+|^2 tends to a finite constant independent of k.
- For neutral particles near threshold: Re q_l^+ = -l + O(k^2) and Im q_l^+ ~ (kappa_1)^(2l+1)/((2l-1)!!)^2.

## Context

This work extends the Trojan Horse method, originally introduced by Baur (1984) for extracting low-energy astrophysical cross sections, to the study of single-particle states in exotic nuclei near the particle threshold. The authors build on the formal development of Typel and Baur (2002) and the inclusive breakup theory of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (1985). The approach enables direct experimental investigation of subthreshold resonances through three-body reactions such as d + Li-6 -> alpha + He-3 + n, as studied by Auricchio (2003).
