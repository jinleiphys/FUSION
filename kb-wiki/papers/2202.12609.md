# Canonical and phenomenological formulations of spin hydrodynamics

> Two formulations of relativistic hydrodynamics of particles with spin 1/2 are compared. The first approach, dubbed the canonical one, uses expressions for the energy-momentum and spin tensors that have properties that follow a direct application of Noether's theorem, which yields a totally antisymmetric spin tensor. The other one is based on a simplified form of the spin tensor and is commonly used in the current literature under the name of a phenomenological approach. We show that these two frameworks are equivalent, i.e., they can be directly connected by a suitably defined pseudogauge transformation, only if the first framework is initially improved by a suitable modification of the energy-momentum tensor (addition of a divergence-free term that cannot be interpreted as a pseudogauge). Our analysis uses arguments related to the positivity of entropy production. The latter turns out to be equivalent for the improved canonical and phenomenological frameworks.

## Key claim

The canonical and phenomenological formulations of spin hydrodynamics are equivalent via a pseudogauge transformation only if the canonical energy-momentum tensor is first "improved" by adding a divergence-free term that cannot itself be interpreted as a pseudogauge; without this improvement, the naive canonical framework conflicts with the principle of entropy production and does not yield a well-defined initial-value problem.

## Method

The authors perform an entropy current analysis for a relativistic spin-1/2 fluid, comparing two formulations: the canonical one, where the spin tensor is totally antisymmetric as derived from Noether's theorem for Dirac fields, and the phenomenological one, where the spin tensor has a simpler form \(S^{\lambda\mu\nu}_{\text{ph}} = u^{\lambda} S^{\mu\nu}\) plus first-order corrections. They construct constitutive relations for the energy-momentum and spin tensors up to first order in gradients, compute the divergence of the entropy current, and impose the second law of thermodynamics \(\partial_\mu S^\mu \geq 0\) to identify dissipative currents and transport coefficients. They then connect the two frameworks explicitly using a pseudogauge transformation.

## Key numbers

- The entropy production condition yields transport coefficients with the constraints: \(\kappa \geq 0\), \(\eta \geq 0\), \(\zeta \geq 0\), \(\lambda \geq 0\), \(\gamma \geq 0\), where \(\kappa\) is heat conductivity, \(\eta\) is shear viscosity, \(\zeta\) is bulk viscosity, and \(\lambda\) and \(\gamma\) are new spin-related transport coefficients.
- The spin chemical potential \(\omega^{\mu\nu}\) is taken to be of order \(\mathcal{O}(\partial^1)\), while the spin density \(S^{\mu\nu}\) is taken to be of order \(\mathcal{O}(\partial^0)\), consistent with the high-temperature scaling \(S^{\mu\nu} \sim T^2 \omega^{\mu\nu}\).
- The dissipative currents are expressed as: \(h^\mu = -\kappa (Du^\mu - \beta \nabla^\mu T)\), \(\pi^{\mu\nu} = 2\eta \sigma^{\mu\nu}\), \(\Pi = \zeta \theta\), \(q^\mu = \lambda (\beta \nabla^\mu T + Du^\mu - 4\omega^{\mu\nu} u_\nu)\), and \(\phi^{\mu\nu} = \gamma (\Omega^{\mu\nu} + 2\beta \Delta^\mu_\alpha \Delta^\nu_\beta \omega^{\alpha\beta})\).

## Context

This work extends the entropy current analysis of spin hydrodynamics previously developed by Hattori et al. (2019) and Fukushima et al. (2020), who used the phenomenological and Belinfante formulations respectively. The authors show that the canonical framework based on the Noether-derived totally antisymmetric spin tensor requires an "improvement" of the energy-momentum tensor to avoid an unphysical constraint on the initial conditions, and that this improved canonical framework is equivalent to the phenomenological framework of Hattori et al. (2019) via a pseudogauge transformation. This enables a unified understanding of different formulations of spin hydrodynamics and clarifies the role of the pseudogauge freedom in relativistic fluid dynamics.
