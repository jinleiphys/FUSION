# Excited Hadron Channels in Hadronization

> The proper treatment of hadronic resonances plays an important role in many aspects of heavy ion collisions. This is expected to be the case also for hadronization, due to the large degeneracies of excited states, and the abundant production of hadrons from their decays. We first show how a comprehensive treatment of excited meson states can be incorporated into quark recombination, and in extension, into Hybrid Hadronization. We then discuss the quantum mechanics of forming excited states, utilizing the Wigner distribution functions of angular momentum eigenstates of isotropic 3-D harmonic oscillators. We further describe how resonance decays can be handled, based on a set of minimal assumptions, by creating an extension of hadron decays in PYTHIA 8. Finally, we present first results by simulating $e^+e^-$ collisions using PYTHIA and Hybrid Hadronization with excited mesons up to orbital angular momentum $L=4$ and radial quantum number 2. We find that states up to $L=2$ are produced profusely by quark recombination.

# Excited Hadron Channels in Hadronization

## Key claim
The authors show that a comprehensive treatment of excited meson states up to orbital angular momentum L=4 and radial quantum number 2 can be incorporated into quark recombination and Hybrid Hadronization, but the hedge is that states up to L=2 are produced profusely by quark recombination, while higher-L states may be less significant.

## Method
The authors computed Wigner distribution functions for angular momentum eigenstates of the isotropic 3-D harmonic oscillator potential, then used these to derive coalescence probabilities for two Gaussian wave packets (representing quark and antiquark) to form meson bound states. They implemented these probabilities into the Hybrid Hadronization framework, supplementing them with spin and color probabilities, and compiled masses and strong decay branching ratios for light excited mesons up to n=4, using estimates from linear interpolation and extrapolation for states not confirmed by the Particle Data Group. They simulated e+e- collisions at sqrt(s)=91.2 GeV using PYTHIA 8 and the standalone Hybrid Hadronization code, then analyzed the quantum numbers of mesons from recombination before secondary decays.

## Key numbers
- Simulations were performed for e+e- collisions at sqrt(s)=91.2 GeV using PYTHIA 8 and Hybrid Hadronization.
- Excited mesons were included up to orbital angular momentum L=4 and radial quantum number 2.
- The 1^3F_2 isospin triplet state (a_2(1918)) was estimated to have a mass of 1.918 GeV, with decay branching ratios of 63.3% into two 3-pion states and 36.7% into three 5-pion states.
- P-wave (l=1) and D-wave (l=2) states were found to be as numerous as S-wave states from recombination.
- F-wave states (l=3) were created with roughly half the strength of S-waves.
- Mesons with total angular momentum j=3 were more than twice as numerous as spinless mesons from recombination.

## Context
This work extends the Hybrid Hadronization model developed by Han (2016) and Fries (2019), which combined string fragmentation (as in PYTHIA 8) with quark recombination. The authors previously studied angular momentum eigenstates of the 3-D isotropic harmonic oscillator in Kordell (2021), which provided the Wigner distributions needed to compute recombination probabilities into physical excited states. This enables a more realistic treatment of hadronic resonances in hadronization models, with future plans to extend the formalism to heavy flavor mesons and baryons.
