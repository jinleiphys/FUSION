# Glueball nature of the sigma/f_0(600) from pi-pi and gamma-gamma scatterings

> We estimate the I=0 scalar meson sigma/f_0(600) parameters from pi-pi and gamma-gamma scattering data below 700 MeV using an improved analytic K-matrix model. A fit of the hadronic data gives a complex pole mass M_sigma= 422-i290 MeV, while simultaneous best fits of the gamma-gamma to pi+pi-, pi0pi0 data give a direct width of (0.13+-0.05)keV, a rescattering component of (2.7+-0.4)keV and a total (direct+rescattering) width of (3.9+-0.6)keV. "Running" these results to the physical real axis, the small "direct" gamma-gamma and the large hadronic widths at the "on-shell" mass are compatible with QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) and some low-energy theorems (LET) expectations for an unmixed lowest mass glueball/gluonium sigma_B of a mass around 1 GeV and a large OZI-violation decay into pi-pi.

## Key claim

The sigma/f0(600) meson is compatible with being an unmixed lowest-mass glueball/gluonium state, because its small direct gamma-gamma width and large hadronic width match QCD spectral sum rule and low-energy theorem expectations, but only after separating the direct photon coupling from the dominant rescattering contribution which had previously hidden the gluonic interpretation.

## Method

The authors used an improved analytic K-matrix model with a shape function to parametrize elastic pi-pi scattering below 700 MeV, fitting the phase shifts from the Roy equation analysis of Leutwyler (2001). They then applied this model to gamma-gamma to pi+pi- and pi0pi0 data from the Crystal Ball and MARK-II collaborations, introducing a single free parameter F_gamma for the direct coupling of the sigma to two photons, while the rescattering component was determined from the strong interaction amplitude via unitarity and dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

* Complex pole mass of the sigma from pi-pi fit: M_sigma = 422 - i 290 MeV
* Direct gamma-gamma width at the complex pole: (0.13 ± 0.05) keV
* Rescattering gamma-gamma width at the complex pole: (2.7 ± 0.4) keV
* Total (direct + rescattering) gamma-gamma width at the complex pole: (3.9 ± 0.6) keV
* On-shell direct gamma-gamma width (extrapolated): (1.0 ± 0.4) keV
* On-shell hadronic width: approximately 1.02 GeV
* On-shell mass from the model: approximately 0.92 GeV
* Bare coupling squared G for I=0: approximately 1.184 (in GeV^2 units)
* Shape function parameter F_gamma from combined fit: -(0.080 ± 0.014)

## Context

This work extends the analytic K-matrix approach of Mennessier (1998) by introducing a shape function to better describe low-energy pi-pi data, and contradicts the interpretation of Pennington (2006) that the total gamma-gamma width disfavors a gluonic nature. The results support the scheme of Ochs (2006) where the broad sigma/f0(600) is identified as the lightest glueball, in agreement with unquenched lattice QCD results of Michael (2003) and QCD spectral sum rule predictions of Narison (2001) for a low-mass gluonium sigma_B around 1 GeV. The separation of direct and rescattering components enables future tests of the gluonium interpretation through more precise gamma-gamma data below 700 MeV.
