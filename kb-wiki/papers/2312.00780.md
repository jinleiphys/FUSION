# Theoretical Developments in Lattice Gauge Theory for Applications in Double-beta Decay Processes and Quantum Simulation

> Double beta decays are rare nuclear processes that can occur in two modes: two-neutrino double beta decay, observed in the Standard Model, and neutrinoless double beta decay, a hypothetical process with profound implications for Particle Physics. To draw reliable conclusions from their experimental constraints, it is necessary to have accurate predictions of the underlying hadronic interactions described by quantum chromodynamics (QCD), a non-Abelian gauge theory with the symmetry group SU(3). QCD predictions require non-perturbative methods for calculating observables, and lattice QCD (LQCD), a numerical method based on QCD formulated on a finite space-time grid, is the only reliable first-principles technique for obtaining quantitative results. However, LQCD needs formal prescriptions to match numerical results with observables. This thesis provides such prescriptions for double beta decays using the finite volume effects in the LQCD framework. Matching relations that connect two-nucleon double beta decay amplitudes to quantities accessible via LQCD calculations, namely the nuclear matrix elements and two-nucleon energy spectra in a finite volume are provided. The impact of uncertainties is examined on the precision with which low-energy constants of the corresponding effective field theories can be determined from future LQCD calculations. Hamiltonian simulation of QCD is another non-perturbative method of solving QCD which can be more suitable in some cases than the conventional LQCD. The rise of tensor network methods and quantum simulation has made Hamiltonian simulation of lattice gauge theories (LGTs) a reality. Towards the goal of simulating QCD, a loop-string-hadron (LSH) formulation of an SU(3) LGT with matter in 1+1 dimensions is developed in this thesis, motivated by recent studies that showed the LSH formulation of an SU(2) LGT to be advantageous over other formulations.

## Key claim

This thesis provides formal matching relations that connect two-nucleon double beta decay amplitudes to quantities accessible via lattice QCD calculations, namely nuclear matrix elements and two-nucleon energy spectra in a finite volume, while also examining how uncertainties in future LQCD calculations affect the precision with which low-energy constants of effective field theories can be determined.

## Method

The author develops finite-volume matching relations that map hadronic double beta decay amplitudes in the two-nucleon sector to LQCD-accessible quantities, using the method of tail-singularity separation (TSS) to evaluate lattice sums that isolate finite-volume corrections from infinite-volume integrals. For the quantum simulation component, a loop-string-hadron (LSH) formulation of an SU(3) lattice gauge theory coupled to dynamical matter in 1+1 dimensions is constructed, extending previous work on SU(2) LGTs. A purely fermionic (gauge-fixed) formulation of the same SU(3) Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian is also presented for comparison.

## Key numbers

* The finite-volume residue function R for the two-nucleon system evaluates to -178.42 (dimensionless lattice sum), used in matching relations for double beta decay amplitudes.
* For the special case of equal momenta (tilde-p = 1), the lattice sum X6(1,1) evaluates to 264, agreeing with prior work by Beane (2014) to three significant figures.
* The finite-volume ground-state energy in the 1S0 channel ranges from -2.728 MeV at L=8 fm to -0.556 MeV at L=16 fm, with corresponding residue functions from 5.04 x 10^3 MeV^3 to 6.78 x 10^2 MeV^3.
* The low-energy constant g_nu^NN for neutrinoless double beta decay is determined as 1.3 with uncertainties ranging from +/-0.1 (at 1% energy uncertainty) to +/-0.9 (at 10% uncertainty on both inputs) across volumes from 8 to 16 fm.
* The infinite-volume integral J^infinity evaluated at ground-state energies ranges from 9.84 x 10^3 MeV^2 at L=8 fm to 1.43 x 10^4 MeV^2 at L=16 fm, with finite-volume corrections delta J^V ranging from -1.2 x 10^3 MeV^2 to -6.8 x 10^2 MeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism developed by Beane (2014) and Cirigliano (2019) for applying LQCD to double beta decay processes, providing explicit matching relations that connect LQCD results to physical decay amplitudes. The LSH formulation for SU(3) extends the approach of Raychowdhury and Stryker for SU(2) lattice gauge theories, aiming toward more efficient Hamiltonian formulations for quantum simulation of QCD. The results enable future LQCD calculations to constrain the low-energy constants governing two-nucleon double beta decay amplitudes, which are necessary for interpreting experimental constraints on neutrinoless double beta decay.
