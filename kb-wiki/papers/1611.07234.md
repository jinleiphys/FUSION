# Backward charmonium production in $πN$ collisions

> The QCD collinear factorization framework allows to describe exclusive backward production of a $J/ψ$ meson in pion-nucleon collisions in terms of pion-to-nucleon transition distribution amplitudes. We calculate the scattering amplitude at the leading order in the strong coupling constant and estimate the cross section of this reaction in the backward kinematical region for a medium energy pion beam available at the J-Parc experimental facility.

# Backward charmonium production in πN collisions

## Key claim
The paper argues that exclusive backward J/ψ production in pion-nucleon collisions can be described within QCD collinear factorization using pion-to-nucleon transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), but the factorization theorems for such backward processes have not yet been rigorously proven, and the charm quark mass may not be large enough for the leading-order and leading-twist analysis to be sufficient.

## Method
The authors calculate the leading-order scattering amplitude for π⁻ + p → J/ψ + n in the near-backward kinematical regime (|u| ≪ W², M_ψ²) using collinear factorization, expressing the hard part in terms of perturbative QCD and the soft part in terms of pion-to-nucleon TDAs and nucleon distribution amplitudes. They estimate the differential cross section using a simple nucleon exchange model for the π → N TDAs, with the strong coupling constant α_s fixed by requiring that the phenomenological nucleon DA solution reproduces the experimental J/ψ → p p̅ decay width.

## Key numbers
- Pion beam momentum range at J-Parc: P_π = 10–20 GeV, corresponding to center-of-mass energy squared W² ≈ 2 m_N P_π
- J/ψ mass approximated as M_ψ ≈ 2 m_c = 3 GeV
- Pion weak decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- J/ψ leptonic decay constant: |f_ψ| = 416 ± 5 MeV (extracted from Γ(J/ψ → e⁺ e⁻))
- Experimental J/ψ → p p̅ decay width: Γ_{J/ψ → p p̅} = 0.197 ± 0.009 keV
- For nucleon DAs concentrated in endpoint regions (Chernyak-Ogloblin-Zhitnitsky, King-Sachrajda), α_s ≈ 0.25 reproduces the experimental J/ψ → p p̅ width
- For nucleon DAs close to asymptotic form (Bolz-Kroll, Braun-Lenz-Wittmann NLO), α_s ≈ 0.4 is required
- Differential cross section dσ/dΔ² shown for P_π = 10, 15, 20 GeV as function of |u − u_max| for |u| ≤ 1 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the collinear factorization approach for backward processes previously applied to backward virtual Compton scattering and backward meson electroproduction (Frankfurt 1999, Pire 2004, Pire 2005), and is the cross-channel counterpart of nucleon-antinucleon annihilation into heavy quarkonium with a pion studied by Pire (2013). The reaction provides a test of the universality of TDAs, which also appear in N N̅ → γ* π (Lansberg 2012), N N̅ → J/ψ π (PsiTDA, Singh 2016), and backward pion electroproduction γ* N → π N (Lansberg 2011). The estimates suggest the reaction is experimentally accessible at J-Parc, enabling future feasibility studies similar to those performed for forward lepton pair production at J-Parc (JParc) and for N → π TDAs at PANDA (Singh 2014, PsiTDA, Singh 2016).
