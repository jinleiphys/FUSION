# Initial conditions, equations of state and final state in hydrodynamics

> In this paper we present properties of relativistic and non-relativistic perfect hydrodynamical models. In particular we show illustrations of the fact that different initial conditions and equations of state can lead to the same hadronic final state. This means that alone from the hadronic observables one cannot determine either of the above, one needs for example penetrating probes that inherit their properties from each timeslice of the evolution of the fireball.

# Initial conditions, equations of state and final state in hydrodynamics

## Key claim
Different initial conditions and equations of state can lead to the same hadronic final state in both relativistic and non-relativistic perfect hydrodynamical models, meaning that hadronic observables alone cannot uniquely determine either the initial conditions or the equation of state. Penetrating probes that sample each timeslice of the fireball evolution are required to break this degeneracy.

## Method
The author investigates two families of exact hydrodynamical solutions: a non-relativistic 3+1 dimensional ellipsoidally symmetric expansion solution (Csorgo 2001) and a relativistic 1+1 dimensional analytic solution with acceleration (Csorgo 2006). For each solution family, the temperature distribution, velocity field, and principal axes are evolved under different choices of the acceleration parameter λ, the adiabatic index κ, and the initial temperature profile, while requiring that all cases converge to the same final-state freeze-out conditions.

## Key numbers
- Non-relativistic solution final state parameters: T₀ = 200 MeV, X = 11 fm, Y = 12 fm, Z = 13 fm, with expansion rates Ẋ₀ = 0.6, Ẏ₀ = 0.5, Ż₀ = 0.7 (dimensionless).
- In the relativistic solution, the velocity field is v = tanh(λη) where λ is the acceleration parameter; λ = 1 gives the boost-invariant Bjorken solution with no acceleration, while λ = 2 gives substantial acceleration with the constraint κ = d (where d is the number of spatial dimensions).
- For the d = 1, κ = 1 case of the relativistic solution, λ ∈ ℝ is arbitrary, allowing a general velocity field.

## Context
This work extends the known degeneracy between initial conditions and equations of state in hydrodynamical models of heavy-ion collisions, building on the Landau-Khalatnikov solution (Landau 1953, Khalatnikov 1954), the Hwa-Bjorken boost-invariant solution (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1982), and the accelerating solutions of Csorgo (2006) and Bialas (2007). The results demonstrate that the hydrodynamic scaling laws observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Csanad 2005) do not depend separately on initial conditions or equation of state, implying that future work must combine hadronic observables with penetrating probes (such as direct photons or dileptons) to constrain the early-time evolution of the fireball.
