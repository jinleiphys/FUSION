# Hydrodynamics from free-streaming to thermalization and back again

> We study the evolution of the Knudsen and Reynolds numbers in (0+1)-dimensionally expanding fluids with Bjorken symmetry for systems whose microscopic mean free path rises more quickly with time than usually assumed. This allows us to explore within a simple 1-dimensional model the transition from initially thermalizing to ultimately decoupling dynamics. In all cases studied the dynamics is found to be controlled by hydrodynamic attractors for both the Knudsen and Reynolds numbers whose trajectories undergo characteristic changes as the dynamics changes from thermalizing to decoupling.

## Key claim

By parametrically modifying the temperature dependence of the relaxation time in a (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken-expanding fluid, the authors show that the system's evolution is controlled by a hydrodynamic attractor throughout the entire transition from initially thermalizing dynamics to ultimately decoupling free-streaming dynamics, with the attractor's shape for the inverse Reynolds number as a function of inverse Knudsen number exhibiting only weak sensitivity to changes in medium properties.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled evolution equations for energy conservation and shear stress relaxation using two dissipative hydrodynamic frameworks: anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) and third-order Chapman-Enskog (CE) hydrodynamics. They modify the temperature dependence of the relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) by introducing a power \(\Delta\) such that \(\tau_\pi \propto T^{-\Delta}\), studying both constant values of \(\Delta\) (from 1 to 6) and a time-dependent \(\Delta(\tau)\) that interpolates between \(\Delta_i=1\) at early times and \(\Delta_f=6\) at late times, with a transition around \(\tau_\mathrm{tr}=10\) fm/\(c\).

## Key numbers

- For \(\Delta=3\) with aHydro, the inverse Knudsen number increases as a power law \(\bar\tau \sim \tau^\alpha\) with \(\alpha \simeq 1/4\).
- For \(\Delta=4\), the inverse Knudsen number becomes approximately constant (slightly negative slope) for aHydro, while for \(\Delta=5\) it decreases more rapidly with time.
- The transition time in the time-dependent \(\Delta(\tau)\) study is set at \(\tau_\mathrm{tr}=10\) fm/\(c = 2500\, \tau_0\), with initial conditions \(T_0=2\) GeV at \(\tau_0=0.004\) fm/\(c\) and \(\bar\eta = 0.24 \approx 3/(4\pi)\).
- For \(\Delta=4\) and above, the inverse Reynolds number \(\bar\pi\) never drops below the free-streaming value of 0.25 (aHydro) or approximately 0.235 (CE).
- Around \(\tau \sim \tau_\mathrm{tr}\), the inverse Knudsen number drops steeply by almost 3 orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hydrodynamic attractors in Bjorken flow (Heller 2013, Romatschke 2017, Jaiswal 2019) and Gubser flow (Denicol 2014, Chattopadhyay 2018) by introducing a time-dependent mean free path to mimic the transition from 1-dimensional to 3-dimensional expansion seen in realistic heavy-ion collisions. The results are qualitatively reminiscent of the pattern shown in Romatschke (2017) for (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion with longitudinal boost-invariance, and the authors argue that a similar attractor should rule all fluid cells in three-dimensionally expanding fluids, enabling future checks in realistic (3+1)-dimensional simulations.
