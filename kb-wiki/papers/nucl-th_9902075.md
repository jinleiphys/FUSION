# Electron scattering on two-neutron halo nuclei: The case of $^6$He

> The formalism to describe electron scattering reactions on two-neutron halo nuclei is developed. The halo nucleus is described as a three-body system (core+n+n), and the wave function is obtained by solving the Faddeev equations in coordinate space. We discuss elastic and quasielastic scattering using the impulse approximation to describe the reaction mechanism. We apply the method to investigate the case of electron scattering on $^6$He. Spectral functions, response functions, and differential cross sections are calculated for both neutron knockout and $α$ knockout by the electron.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism for electron scattering on two-neutron halo nuclei, using the impulse approximation where a single constituent absorbs the entire energy and momentum transfer while the other two act as spectators, and applies it specifically to \(^6\)He. The authors hedge that this picture is valid only when the momentum transfer is kept below about 200 MeV/c, so that the energy transfer remains below the neutron or proton separation energy in the \(^4\)He core (around 20 MeV), guaranteeing that only the halo breaks into its three constituents without core excitation.

## Method

The halo nucleus \(^6\)He is described as a three-body system (\(\alpha + n + n\)) with the wave function obtained by solving Faddeev equations in coordinate space. Elastic and quasielastic electron scattering are treated using the Born approximation and the impulse approximation, where the virtual photon is absorbed by a single constituent (either a halo neutron or the \(\alpha\) core), while the other two particles are spectators. The differential cross sections for exclusive \((e,e^\prime n)\) and \((e,e^\prime \alpha)\) reactions are derived in perpendicular kinematics, factorizing into an electron–constituent scattering cross section and a spectral function that gives the probability of removing a particle with given internal momentum, leaving the residual two-body system in the continuum.

## Key numbers

- The charge root mean square radius of \(^4\)He (used as the core in \(^6\)He) is 1.68 fm.
- For elastic electron scattering on \(^6\)He at \(E_0 \approx 200\) MeV and \(\theta_e \approx 30^\circ\), the differential cross section is around 100 \(\mu\)b.
- The quasielastic peak for a participant \(\alpha\) particle at momentum transfer \(q = 200\) MeV/c occurs at \(\omega \approx 5\) MeV (from \(\omega \approx q^2 / 2 m_\alpha\)).
- The quasielastic peak for a participant neutron at \(q = 200\) MeV/c occurs at \(\omega \approx 20\) MeV (from \(\omega \approx q^2 / 2 m_n\)).
- A momentum transfer smaller than 200 MeV/c ensures that \(\omega\) stays below the 20 MeV separation energy of nucleons in the \(^4\)He core.

## Context

This work extends the few-body model approach to halo nuclei, previously applied to nucleus–nucleus fragmentation reactions by Garrido (1996, 1997, 1998) and others, to the cleaner probe of electron scattering. It provides a theoretical framework for planned experiments at the MUSES project in RIKEN, as described by Ohkawa (1997). The formalism enables future experimental tests of whether the charge density of neutron halo nuclei like \(^6\)He is indeed confined to the core, as predicted by the three-body picture, or spread over the whole nucleus.
