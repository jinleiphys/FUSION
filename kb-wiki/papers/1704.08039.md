# Bose-Einstein condensation and liquid-gas phase transition in alpha-matter

> Systems of Bose particles with both repulsive and attractive interactions are studied using the Skyrme-like mean-field model. The phase diagram of such systems exhibits two special lines in the chemical potential-temperature plane: one line which represents the first-order liquid-gas phase transition with the critical end point, and another line which represents the onset of Bose-Einstein condensation. The calculations are made for strongly-interacting matter composed of alpha particles. The phase diagram of this matter is qualitatively similar to that observed for the atomic He4 liquid. The sensitivity of the results to the model parameters is studied. For weak interaction coupling the critical point is located at the Bose-condensation line.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of strongly-interacting alpha matter, modeled with a Skyrme-like mean-field interaction, exhibits both a first-order liquid-gas phase transition and Bose-Einstein condensation; for weak interaction coupling (a ≤ a_s), the critical point of the liquid-gas transition lies exactly on the Bose-condensation line, a regime where the standard mean-field critical indices do not apply.

## Method

The authors model a system of interacting bosons (alpha particles, mass m ≈ 3727 MeV, degeneracy g=1) using a density-dependent mean-field potential U(n) of Skyrme form, with parameters a (coupling strength), n0 (ground-state density), and γ (stiffness). The grand canonical pressure is computed as the sum of an ideal Bose gas term (with shifted chemical potential μ* = μ − U(n)) and a field term p_f(n). The liquid-gas phase transition boundaries (binodals) are found from Gibbs equilibrium conditions, and the Bose-Einstein condensation onset is determined by the condition μ* = m. The critical point (CP) and triple point (TP) where the two transition lines intersect are located numerically.

## Key numbers

- For the default parameters (a = 3 GeV·fm³, n0 = 0.05 fm⁻³, γ = 1/6): CP temperature T_c = 11.2 MeV, CP density n_c = 0.013 fm⁻³, CP pressure p_c = 3.92 × 10⁻² MeV/fm³, CP chemical potential shift μ_c − m = −39.9 MeV.
- For the same default parameters: TP temperature T_TP = 4.39 MeV, TP density n_TP = 0.045 fm⁻³, TP pressure p_TP = 3.08 × 10⁻⁴ MeV/fm³, TP chemical potential shift μ_TP − m = −23.6 MeV.
- In the Boltzmann approximation for the default parameters: T_c ≈ 10.5 MeV, n_c ≈ 0.012 fm⁻³.
- For weak coupling (a ≤ a_s) with γ = 1/6 and n0 = 0.05 fm⁻³: a_s ≈ 358 MeV·fm³, n* = 0.0310 fm⁻³, T_BEC(n*) = 3.41 MeV.
- For weak coupling with γ = 1/3 and n0 = 0.05 fm⁻³: a_s ≈ 178 MeV·fm³, n* = 0.0315 fm⁻³, T_BEC(n*) = 3.45 MeV.
- For weak coupling with γ = 1/6 and n0 = 0.03 fm⁻³: a_s ≈ 427 MeV·fm³, n* = 0.0186 fm⁻³, T_BEC(n*) = 2.43 MeV.
- For weak coupling with γ = 1/3 and n0 = 0.03 fm⁻³: a_s ≈ 212 MeV·fm³, n* = 0.0189 fm⁻³, T_BEC(n*) = 2.46 MeV.

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of alpha matter (e.g., Clark and Wang (1966), Brink and Boeker (1973)) by simultaneously treating the liquid-gas phase transition and Bose-Einstein condensation, which earlier work on alpha matter did not address together. The phase diagram is qualitatively similar to that of atomic ⁴He (Buckingham and Fairbank (1990)), with the Bose-condensed region analogous to the HeII phase and the triple point analogous to the lambda point. The results enable future studies of chemically equilibrated mixtures of nucleons and light clusters (d, t, ³He, α) relevant for heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical environments, as noted by the authors.
