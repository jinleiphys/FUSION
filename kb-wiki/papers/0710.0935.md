# Self-consistent quasiparticle RPA for multi-level pairing model

> Particle-number projection within the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) method is applied to the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (SCQRPA), which is tested in an exactly solvable multi-level pairing model. The SCQRPA equations are numerically solved to find the energies of the ground and excited states at various numbers $Ω$ of doubly degenerate equidistant levels. The use of the LN method allows one to avoid the collapse of the BCS (QRPA) to obtain the energies of the ground and excited states as smooth functions of the interaction parameter $G$. The comparison between results given by different approximations such as the SCRPA, QRPA, LNQRPA, SCQRPA and LNSCQRPA is carried out. While the use of the LN method significantly improves the agreement with the exact results in the intermediate coupling region, we found that in the strong coupling region the SCQRPA results are closest to the exact ones.

## Key claim

The Lipkin-Nogami (LN) particle-number projection method, when applied to the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (SCQRPA), avoids the collapse of the BCS and QRPA at a critical interaction strength, producing smooth ground- and excited-state energies as functions of the pairing parameter G; however, the SCQRPA without LN projection gives results closest to the exact solution in the strong coupling region, while the LN method significantly improves agreement only in the intermediate coupling region.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the SCQRPA equations for a multi-level pairing (Richardson) model with Ω doubly degenerate equidistant levels, using the Bogoliubov transformation to a quasiparticle representation and including ground-state correlation factors and screening factors (expectation values of pair operators) in the SCQRPA submatrices. The Lipkin-Nogami method was incorporated as an approximate particle-number projection before variation, yielding the LNSCQRPA equations, and the results were compared across five approximations: SCRPA, QRPA, LNQRPA, SCQRPA, and LNSCQRPA. The coupled nonlinear equations for the Bogoliubov coefficients, SCQRPA amplitudes, and ground-state correlation factors were solved iteratively for systems with N = 4, 6, 8, and 10 particles.

## Key numbers

- BCS critical point: G_cr^BCS = 0.34 MeV for N = 10; below this value the BCS pairing gap is zero.
- RBCS critical point: G_cr^RBCS ≈ 0.38 MeV for N = 10.
- BCS1 critical point: G_cr^BCS1 ≈ 0.47 MeV for N = 10.
- Level distance ε = 1 MeV was used throughout the calculations.
- The LN method produces a smooth pairing gap Δ_LN as a function of G, starting from zero at G = 0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach of Dukelsky and Schuck (2001) and the SCQRPA applied to the seniority model and two-level pairing model by extending it to a multi-level (Richardson) model with particle-number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method. The approach enables calculations of ground-state and excited-state energies across all coupling regimes without the collapse that plagues standard BCS and QRPA, and it provides a benchmark for testing approximations against exact solutions in the Richardson model, which is widely used for testing many-body methods.
