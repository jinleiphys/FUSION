# Transport coefficients from in medium quarkonium dynamics

> The in medium dynamics of heavy particles are governed by transport coefficients. The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient, $κ$, is an object of special interest in the literature, but one which has proven notoriously difficult to estimate, despite the fact that it has been computed by weak-coupling methods at next-to-leading order accuracy, and by lattice simulations of the pure SU(3) gauge theory. Another coefficient, $γ$, has been recently identified. It can be understood as the dispersive counterpart of $κ$. Little is known about $γ$. Both $κ$ and $γ$ are, however, of foremost importance in heavy quarkonium physics as they entirely determine the in and out of equilibrium dynamics of quarkonium in a medium, if the evolution of the density matrix is Markovian, and the motion, quantum Brownian; the medium could be a strongly or weakly coupled plasma. In this paper, using the relation between $κ$, $γ$ and the quarkonium in medium width and mass shift respectively, we evaluate the two coefficients from existing 2+1 flavor lattice QCD data. The resulting range for $κ$ is consistent with earlier determinations, the one for $γ$ is the first non-perturbative determination of this quantity.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient κ and its dispersive counterpart γ from existing 2+1 flavor lattice QCD data, under the specific conditions that the quarkonium evolution is Markovian and the motion is quantum Brownian motion, and that the quarkonium is a Coulombic bound state with inverse Bohr radius larger than the medium energy scale and binding energy smaller than the medium energy scale.

## Method

The authors relate the transport coefficients κ and γ to the in-medium thermal width Γ and mass shift δM of 1S Coulombic quarkonium states via Γ(1S) = 3a₀²κ and δM(1S) = (3/2)a₀²γ, where a₀ is the Bohr radius. They compute a₀ self-consistently from the heavy quark mass and the running strong coupling, then evaluate κ and γ using existing lattice QCD data for the thermal width and mass shift of the Υ(1S) and J/ψ from Aarts et al. (2011) and Kim et al. (2018).

## Key numbers

- For the Υ(1S): a₀ = 0.67 GeV⁻¹ = 0.13 fm; binding energy E = -0.1 GeV; 1/a₀ = 1.5 GeV
- For the J/ψ: a₀ = 1.19 GeV⁻¹ = 0.23 fm; binding energy E = -0.24 GeV; 1/a₀ = 0.84 GeV
- Thermal mass shifts from Kim et al. (2018) after mass rescaling: J/ψ at T=251 MeV: (-85 ± 29) MeV; Υ(1S) at T=407 MeV: (-48 ± 16) MeV; Υ(1S) at T=251 MeV: (-30 ± 12) MeV
- Extracted range for γ/T³ for 251 MeV ≲ T ≲ 407 MeV: -3.8 ≲ γ/T³ ≲ -0.7
- Extracted range for κ/T³ from Υ(1S) at T=407 MeV: 0.24 ≲ κ/T³ ≲ 4.2, using Γ(1S) ≈ 22.3 MeV as lower bound from Kim et al. (2018) and Γ(1S)/T ≈ 1.1 as upper bound from Aarts et al. (2011)

## Context

This work extends the open quantum systems approach to quarkonium dynamics developed by Brambilla et al. (2016, 2017), which derived Lindblad equations for quarkonium evolution in a medium under Markovian and quantum Brownian motion assumptions. The determination of κ provides an independent extraction using quarkonium thermal width rather than the chromoelectric field correlator method used in quenched lattice simulations by Francis et al. (2015) or the perturbative next-to-leading order calculations by Caron-Huot et al. (2008). The determination of γ is the first non-perturbative extraction of this dispersive transport coefficient, which enables future phenomenological studies of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions using the Lindblad equation framework.
