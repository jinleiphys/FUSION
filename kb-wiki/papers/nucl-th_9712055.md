# Phaseshift equivalent NN potentials and the deuteron

> Different modern phase shift equivalent NN potentials are tested by evaluating the partial wave decomposition of the kinetic and potential energy of the deuteron. Significant differences are found, which are traced back to the matrix elements of the potentials at medium and large momenta. The influence of the localisation of the one-pion-exchange contribution to these potentials is analyzed in detail.

# Phaseshift equivalent NN potentials and the deuteron

## Key claim

Although modern phase-shift equivalent NN potentials all reproduce the deuteron binding energy of -2.224 MeV and fit NN scattering data with a χ² per datum of approximately 1, they exhibit significant differences in the partial-wave decomposition of kinetic and potential energy contributions to the deuteron, particularly in the tensor channel VSD, which are traced to off-shell differences and the treatment of the one-pion-exchange (OPE) contribution.

## Method

The authors computed the partial-wave decomposition of the deuteron kinetic and potential energy in the ³S₁ and ³D₁ channels for four modern phase-shift equivalent NN potentials (CD-Bonn, Argonne V₁₈, Nijm I, and Nijm II), using the deuteron wave functions from each potential. They also evaluated the expectation value of the OPE part of the interaction using the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function under three different approximations: the relativistic nonlocal OPE from CD-Bonn, the local approximation, and the local approximation with the Argonne form factor.

## Key numbers

- CD-Bonn deuteron D-state probability: 4.83%
- Argonne V₁₈ deuteron D-state probability: 5.78%
- Nijm I deuteron D-state probability: 5.66%
- Nijm II deuteron D-state probability: 5.64%
- Kinetic energy in the S-wave (TS) for CD-Bonn: 9.79 MeV; for Argonne V₁₈: 11.31 MeV; for Nijm I: 9.66 MeV; for Nijm II: 12.11 MeV
- Kinetic energy in the D-wave (TD) for CD-Bonn: 5.69 MeV; for Argonne V₁₈: 8.57 MeV; for Nijm I: 7.91 MeV; for Nijm II: 8.10 MeV
- Tensor potential contribution VSD for CD-Bonn: -14.27 MeV; for Argonne V₁₈: -18.94 MeV; for Nijm I: -20.82 MeV; for Nijm II: -17.63 MeV
- OPE contribution to VSD using nonlocal Bonn OPE: -21.48 MeV; using local approximation: -26.76 MeV; using local approximation with Argonne form factor: -18.28 MeV
- Cut-off parameter Λ for CD-Bonn OPE: 1.7 GeV
- Fixed momentum k' used in matrix element comparisons: 95 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phase-shift equivalent NN potentials by Sauer (1971), Baranger (1969), and Haftel (1970), which constructed mathematically equivalent scattering matrices while keeping operator structure fixed. The present study instead compares realistic physical models (CD-Bonn, Argonne V₁₈, Nijm I, Nijm II) that all fit scattering data with χ² per datum ≈ 1, revealing that even the long-range OPE part is not settled. The findings connect to observed differences in triton properties (Nogga, 1997), nucleon-nucleus optical potentials (Weppner, 1997), and nuclear matter symmetry energy (Engvik, 1997), and suggest that further constraints on off-shell behavior may only come from theoretical preferences or many-body calculations.
