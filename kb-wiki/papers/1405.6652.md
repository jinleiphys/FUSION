# Observation of a dynamical QCD string

> Mesons constructed from the quark propagators without the lowest-lying eigenmodes of the Dirac operator reveal not only restored SU(2)_L * SU(2)_R chiral and U(1)_A symmetries, but actually a higher symmetry. All possible chiral and U(1)_A multiplets for the states of the same spin are degenerate, i.e., the energy of the observed quantum levels does not depend on the spin orientation of quarks in the system and their parities. The quark-spin independence of the energy levels implies absence of the magnetic interactions in the system. The ultrarelativistic quark-antiquark system with only the color-electric interactions can be interpreted (or defined) as a dynamical QCD string.

# Observation of a dynamical QCD string

## Key claim
By removing the lowest-lying eigenmodes of the Dirac operator from valence quark propagators on the lattice, mesons exhibit a higher symmetry than just restored SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral and U(1)_A symmetries: all eight J=1 meson states become degenerate, implying quark-spin independence of energy levels and the absence of magnetic interactions, which the author interprets as a dynamical QCD string.

## Method
The study uses lattice QCD with a chirally invariant overlap Dirac operator, removing the lowest-lying quasi-zero eigenmodes from valence quark propagators while keeping gluon gauge configurations intact. The ground and excited states of all possible isovector J=1 mesons (ρ, a₁, b₁) are computed from correlation functions, and their masses are tracked as a function of the number of removed eigenmodes. The truncation energy (the gap opened by mode removal) is varied to observe the onset of degeneracies among chiral and U(1)_A multiplets.

## Key numbers
- The onset of degeneracy among ρ, ρ', a₁, and b₁ states is seen at a truncation energy of about 50 MeV.
- At a truncation energy of 65 MeV, the observed radial levels are approximately equidistant and described by E_{n_r} = (n_r + 1)ħω.
- The extracted radial string excitation quantum is ħω = (900 ± 70) MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies (Glozman et al., 2011; Denissenya et al., 2014) that used a Wilson Dirac operator, now employing a manifestly chiral-invariant overlap operator with gauge configurations provided by the JLQCD collaboration (Aoki et al., 2012; Noaki et al., 2008). The observed degeneracy of all eight J=1 mesons into a single multiplet of SU(2·N_f) goes beyond the chiral and U(1)_A restoration seen in prior work, and the resulting quark-spin independence distinguishes this dynamical QCD string from the Nambu-Goto open bosonic string, where orbital angular momentum L is conserved and M² ∼ L. The results enable further study of how confinement physics, disentangled from chiral symmetry breaking, manifests in excited hadron spectra.
