# Shell model study of isobaric analog states for $T_z= \pm 2$ nuclei using isospin non-conserving interactions

> In order to comprehend the process underlying mirror energy differences in mirror pairs, we have performed shell-model calculations for $T_z= \pm 2$ $sd$-shell nuclei in the mass range $A$= 20 to 36 and neutron number varying from $N$= 8 to 20. Isospin-symmetry breaking (ISB) is responsible for the mirror energy difference of excited states. We have investigated the {\color{black}isospin non-conserving} interactions: USDC and USDCm to explore the low-lying energy spectra, mirror energy differences, isoscalar ($M_0$), isovector ($M_1$) matrix elements, \textit{E2} transition probability, magnetic ($μ$), and quadrupole moments ($Q$) of mirror-pair and compared them with their available experimental data. The impact of single-particle states on weakly bound and unbound nuclear states are investigated, especially those of the $s$-wave. We have also analyzed single proton/neutron separation energies and proton/neutron occupancy for ($T_z$=-2)/($T_z$=+2) $sd$-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using the isospin non-conserving interactions USDC and USDCm can reproduce low-lying energy spectra, mirror energy differences, and electromagnetic properties for $T_z = \pm 2$ $sd$-shell nuclei in the mass range $A = 20$ to $36$, though the agreement depends on the specific state and interaction; for example, the USDC interaction minimizes deviation in the mirror energy difference for the $1^+_1$ state in $^{22}$Al/$^{22}$F compared to USDCm, yet the deviation remains greater than 550 keV relative to experiment, suggesting the role of continuum coupling effects is important.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the $sd$-shell model space using the isospin non-conserving Hamiltonians USDC and USDCm, which include an isospin-conserving part, an isospin non-conserving nuclear part, and a Coulomb part. The $T=1$ proton-neutron interaction term $V_{pn}$ was increased by 2.2% for USDC and 0.8% for USDCm to account for charge-dependent effects. Low-lying energy spectra, mirror energy differences, isoscalar ($M_0$) and isovector ($M_1$) matrix elements, $B(E2)$ transition probabilities, magnetic moments, quadrupole moments, single proton/neutron separation energies, and proton/neutron occupancies were computed and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For $^{20}$Mg/$^{20}$O, the $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value is 38.7 $e^2$fm$^4$ (USDC) and 38.3 $e^2$fm$^4$ (USDCm) for $^{20}$Mg, compared to the experimental value 35.4 (64) $e^2$fm$^4$; for $^{20}$O the calculated value is 4.1 $e^2$fm$^4$ (both interactions) versus the experimental 5.8 (2) $e^2$fm$^4$.
- The mirror energy difference for the $4^+_3$ state in $^{20}$Mg/$^{20}$O is -493 keV using USDC and -442 keV for the $0^+_2$ state using USDC.
- For $^{22}$Al/$^{22}$F, the theoretical mirror energy difference for the $1^+_1$ state deviates by greater than 550 keV from the experimental value using USDC, and by approximately 670 keV using USDCm.
- The single proton separation energy for $^{22}$Al is 0.040 MeV (USDC) compared to the experimental 0.001 (401) MeV; the single neutron separation energy for $^{22}$F is 5.138 MeV (USDC) versus the experimental 5.230 (13) MeV.
- For $^{24}$Si/$^{24}$Ne, the experimental mirror energy difference for the tentative $0^+_2$ state is -1.297 MeV, while the calculated values are -228 keV (USDC) and -294 keV (USDCm).
- The $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value for $^{24}$Si is 50.0 $e^2$fm$^4$ (USDC) and 48.5 $e^2$fm$^4$ (USDCm), compared to the experimental 19.0 (57) $e^2$fm$^4$.
- The magnetic moment for the $2^+_1$ state in $^{20}$O is -0.707 $\mu_N$ (USDC) and -0.711 $\mu_N$ (USDCm), in agreement with the experimental -0.70 (3) $\mu_N$.
- The quadrupole moment for the $4^+_{g.s.}$ of $^{22}$F is -0.007 $eb$ (USDC), compared to the experimental 0.003 (2) $eb$.
- The calculated half-life for the isomeric $1^+_1$ state in $^{26}$P using USDC is 44.73 $\mu s$, compared to the experimental half-life of 120 (9) ns for the isomeric state at 0.164 MeV.
- The deformation parameter $\beta_2$ for the $2^+_1$ state in $^{20}$Mg is 0.46 (USDC) and 0.45 (USDCm), compared to the experimental 0.44.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of $T_z = \pm 3/2$ $sd$-shell mirror pairs by Shukla et al. (2024) using the same USDC and USDCm interactions, now applied to $T_z = \pm 2$ nuclei. The calculations build on the USD-type Hamiltonians developed by Wildenthal (1984), Brown (1985), and Richter (2008), and the USDC/USDCm interactions introduced by Magilligan (2023). The results enable further investigation of isospin symmetry breaking in proton-rich nuclei near the drip line, particularly for states exhibiting Thomas-Ehrman shifts due to $s$-wave occupancy, and provide benchmarks for future experiments on weakly bound and unbound nuclear states.
