# Heavy-quark Langevin dynamics and single-electron spectra in nucleus-nucleus collision

> The stochastic dynamics of heavy quarks in the fireball produced in heavy-ion collisions is followed through numerical simulations based on the Langevin equation. The modification of the final p_T spectra (R_AA) of c and b quarks, hadrons and single-electrons with respect to pp collisions is studied. The transport coefficients are evaluated treating separately the contribution of soft and hard collisions. The initial heavy-quark spectra are generated according to NLO-pQCD, accounting for nuclear effects through recent nPDFs. The evolution of the medium is obtained from the output of two hydro-codes (ideal and viscous). The heavy-quark fragmentation into hadrons and their final semileptonic decays are implemented according to up to date experimental data. A comparison with RHIC data for non-photonic electron spectra is given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Langevin-based simulation of heavy-quark dynamics in a hydrodynamically expanding QGP, using transport coefficients from a combined pQCD and HTL treatment, can reproduce RHIC single-electron \(R_{AA}\) data with a moderate coupling, suggesting that collisional energy loss may contribute significantly to high-\(p_T\) quenching alongside gluon radiation.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Langevin equation for charm and bottom quarks in a fireball whose space-time evolution is provided by two hydrodynamical codes (ideal and viscous). Transport coefficients \(\kappa_{T/L}(p)\) are computed by splitting the momentum transfer into hard collisions (evaluated in pQCD from \(Qq\) and \(Qg\) diagrams) and soft collisions (evaluated in the Hard Thermal Loop approximation), with an intermediate cutoff \(|t|^* \sim m_D^2\). Initial heavy-quark spectra are generated with POWHEG at NLO-pQCD using CTEQ6M PDFs, with nuclear effects included via EPS09 nPDFs and nuclear \(k_T\)-broadening; hadronization uses a Peterson fragmentation function and semileptonic decays are simulated with PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- Initial sample of \(45 \times 10^6\) \(c\bar c\) and \(b\bar b\) pairs generated per explored case.
- Langevin time step in the fluid rest frame: \(\Delta \bar t = 0.02\) fm/c.
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: \(\epsilon = 0.04\) for charm, \(\epsilon = 0.005\) for bottom.
- Collision energy: \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 200\) GeV for minimum-bias Au-Au collisions.
- At \(T = 200\) MeV, \(\alpha_s \approx 0.6\) for \(\mu = \pi T\) and \(\alpha_s \approx 0.34\) for \(\mu = 2\pi T\).
- The coupling dependence is huge: a too strong coupling overestimates the quenching effect; the moderate coupling scenario yields reasonable agreement with PHENIX data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Alberico et al., 2009) which evaluated HTL transport coefficients for a static medium, by now using a more accurate microscopic calculation of the coefficients and a fully realistic hydrodynamical medium evolution. It contrasts with independent Langevin-based approaches (Hira, Rapp, Tea, Aic) and addresses the open question of the relative role of collisional versus radiative energy loss in high-\(p_T\) quenching, which had been mainly attributed to gluon radiation. The results enable future studies that more carefully disentangle these two mechanisms.
