# Exact solutions of the nuclear shell-model secular problem: Discrete Non-Orthogonal Shell Model within a Variation After Projection approach

> We investigate the capacity of non-orthogonal many-body expansions in the resolution of the nuclear shell-model secular problem. Exact shell-model solutions are obtained within the variational principle using non-orthogonal Slater determinants as the variational ansatz. These results numerically prove the realization of the Broeckhove-Deumens theorem on the existence of a discrete set of non-orthogonal wavefunctions that exactly span the full shell-model space for low-lying states of interest. With the angular-momentum variation after projection, pairing correlations are shown to be fully captured by Slater determinants as exemplified in the backbending phenomenon occurred in $^{48}$Cr. The resulting discrete non-orthogonal shell model developed in such variation after projection method is further examined in the case of $^{78}$Ni, an exotic doubly magic nucleus at the edge of currently feasible diagonalization limits. Its ground state binding energy is shown to converge to a lower value than the largest large-scale shell-model diagonalization ever done by the conventional tridiagonal Lanczos method, revealing an outstanding performance of non-orthogonal Slater determinantal wavefunctions to describe the eigensolutions of shell-model Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The paper numerically proves that a discrete set of non-orthogonal Slater determinants, generated via angular-momentum variation after projection, can exactly span the full shell-model valence space for low-lying states, thereby realizing the Broeckhove-Deumens theorem in practical calculations; the abstract's claim of "exact solutions" is qualified by the demonstration that this holds for targeted low-lying states of interest, not for the entire spectrum.

## Method

The authors develop two extensions of the Discrete Non-Orthogonal Shell Model (DNO-SM): a projection after variation (PAV) approach using deformed Hartree-Fock states plus many-particle many-hole excitations, and a variation after projection (VAP) approach using Thouless-parametrized non-orthogonal Slater determinants. In the VAP method, the projected energy functional is minimized with respect to both the mixing amplitudes (via the Hill-Wheeler secular equation) and the intrinsic states (via a generalized Brillouin condition), using a quasi-Newton optimization that combines a resonating Hartree-Fock treatment for newly added states while keeping previously optimized states fixed.

## Key numbers

* Ground state binding energy of $^{78}$Ni: DNO-SM(VAP) converges to -372.73275 MeV, lower than the largest Lanczos diagonalization value of -372.71668 MeV (at the 10p10h level) and the extrapolated value of -372.72850 MeV
* For $^{24}$Mg with USDB interaction: DNO-SM(VAP) achieves -87.10405 MeV (16 basis states) versus exact shell-model -87.10445 MeV (28503 basis states); the $(\beta,\gamma)$-only PAV approach gives -86.73278 MeV (16 states)
* For $^{28}$Si with USDB interaction: DNO-SM(VAP) achieves -135.86003 MeV (45 basis states) versus exact shell-model -135.86073 MeV (93710 basis states)
* For $^{48}$Cr yrast band with KB3 interaction: the $0^+_1$ state energy is -32.953 MeV (50 Slater determinants) matching the exact shell-model value of -32.953 MeV
* The full M-scheme dimension for $^{78}$Ni in the pf-sdg valence space is 210,046,691,518 (approximately 2 x 10^11)

## Context

This work extends the DNO-SM framework (Dao and Nowacki, 2022) by incorporating both particle-hole excitations and variation after projection, directly addressing the open question of whether non-orthogonal expansions can achieve exact shell-model diagonalization solutions. The results numerically confirm the Broeckhove-Deumens theorem (Deumens, 1979) on the existence of discrete non-orthogonal bases spanning the full Hilbert space, and demonstrate that the VAP approach outperforms previous methods such as the Monte Carlo Shell Model (Otsuka, 1996; Gao, 2018) and the projected generator coordinate method (Bally, 2019) in capturing pairing correlations and binding energy convergence. This enables reliable shell-model calculations for heavy nuclei at the edge of current computational limits, such as the neutron-rich region around $^{78}$Ni where shape coexistence is prevalent (Nies, 2023).
