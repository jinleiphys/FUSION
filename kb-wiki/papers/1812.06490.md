# Cluster radioactivity of $^{294}_{118}$Og$_{176}$

> According to theory, cluster radioactivity becomes an important decay mode in superheavy nuclei. In this work, we predict that the strongly-asymmetric fission, or cluster emission, is in fact the dominant fission channel for $^{294}_{118}$Og$_{176}$, which is currently the heaviest synthetic isotope known. Our theoretical approach incorporates important features of fission dynamics, including quantum tunneling and stochastic dynamics up to scission. We show that, despite appreciable differences in static fission properties such as fission barriers and spontaneous fission lifetimes, the prediction of cluster radioactivity in $^{294}_{118}$Og$_{176}$ is robust with respect to the details of calculations, including the choice of energy density functional, collective inertia, and the strength of the dissipation term.

# Cluster radioactivity of $^{294}_{118}$Og$_{176}$

## Key claim

The authors predict that cluster radioactivity (highly-asymmetric fission) is the dominant spontaneous fission channel for $^{294}_{118}$Og$_{176}$, the heaviest known synthetic isotope, and that this prediction is robust across different energy density functionals, collective inertia prescriptions, and dissipation strengths despite large variations in predicted spontaneous fission half-lives.

## Method

The authors used a two-stage theoretical framework combining the WKB approximation for quantum tunneling through a multidimensional potential energy surface with Langevin dynamics for the post-tunneling evolution up to scission. The potential energy surfaces and collective inertias were computed self-consistently using three different energy density functionals (UNEDF1$_{\mathrm{HFB}}$, SkM*, and D1S) within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, using axial quadrupole, triaxial quadrupole, axial octupole, and pairing fluctuation coordinates as collective variables.

## Key numbers

- Spontaneous fission half-life predicted for $^{294}_{118}$Og$_{176}$: $9.1\times10^{-9}$ s (UNEDF1$_{\mathrm{HFB}}$), $4.0\times10^{-5}$ s (SkM*), and $3.2\times10^{-2}$ s (D1S)
- Experimental alpha-decay half-life of $^{294}$Og: 0.58 ms (Brewer 2018)
- Heavy fragment predicted near doubly-magic $^{208}_{82}$Pb$_{126}$; light fragment near $^{86}_{36}$Kr$_{50}$
- Baseline dissipation tensor: $\eta_0 \equiv (50\hbar, 5\hbar, 40\hbar)$
- Scission condition: neck operator value $Q_N = 7$ for UNEDF1$_{\mathrm{HFB}}$ and SkM*, $Q_N = 9$ for D1S
- Gaussian convolution widths for particle number fluctuations: $\sigma_A=6$ for mass, $\sigma_Z=4$ for charge

## Context

This work extends the discussion of Warda (2018) to explicit fission yield calculations, providing the first theoretical demonstration that cluster emission becomes the primary spontaneous fission channel in a superheavy nucleus. The results support earlier semiempirical predictions by Poenaru (2011, 2012, 2013, 2015, 2018), Santhosh (2018), and Zhang (2018) that cluster radioactivity dominates in several superheavy nuclei, and microscopic DFT calculations by Warda (2011, 2018). The prediction enables experimental searches for cluster radioactivity of $^{294}$Og, which have already begun (Brewer 2018), and suggests that next-generation experiments with ionization chambers may find evidence of this decay mode.
