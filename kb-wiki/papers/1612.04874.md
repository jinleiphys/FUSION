# Shear viscosity from nuclear stopping

> Within a Boltzmann transport model, we demonstrate correlation between stopping observables and shear viscosity in central nuclear collisions at intermediate energies (on the order of 10 to 1000 MeV/nucleon). The correlation allows us to assess the viscosity of nuclear matter, by tuning the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section in our transport model to agree with nuclear stopping data. We also calculate the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density to determine how close the system is to the universal quantum lower limit proposed in the context of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Shear viscosity from nuclear stopping

## Key claim

Within a Boltzmann transport model, the authors demonstrate a correlation between stopping observables and shear viscosity in central nuclear collisions at intermediate energies (10–1000 MeV/nucleon), but note that different observables and different strategies for modifying the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section can lead to different conclusions, making the constraints on viscosity ambiguous without cross-checking multiple observables.

## Method

The authors use a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model (pBUU) to simulate central nuclear collisions, tuning the in-medium elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section under three reduction schemes (Tempered, Rostock, and Fuchs) to match experimental stopping data. They then calculate the shear viscosity coefficient self-consistently from the Boltzmann equation using the tuned cross sections, and compare the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density to the conjectured quantum lower limit. The stopping observables examined include linear momentum transfer (LMT) in asymmetric collisions and the rapidity variance ratio (varxz) in symmetric Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The Tempered cross section scheme with parameter ν = 0.4 or 0.6 best fits LMT data for 40Ar+Cu and 40Ar+Ag, while ν = 0.8 best fits 40Ar+Au data.
- For Au+Au collisions below 600 MeV/nucleon, the Rostock cross section or Tempered with ν = 0.8 best fits the varxz stopping observable.
- The Fuchs reduction yields LMT values approximately 7% higher than Rostock in the 65 MeV/nucleon region for the asymmetric systems.
- The ratio of peak Delta production and absorption rates to peak elastic collision rates varies from 0 to 0.8 over 90–1500 MeV/nucleon for Tempered ν = 0.6, with inelastic processes becoming significant above approximately 600 MeV/nucleon.
- Free cross sections yield varxz clearly exceeding 1 in the 0.09–0.6 GeV/nucleon range, while experimental varxz always stays below 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to extract nuclear transport coefficients from reaction observables, contrasting with approaches based on giant resonances (Mondal 2017, Auerbach 2009) that assume hydrodynamic descriptions valid at zero temperature. It also differs from quantum molecular dynamics studies (Zhou 2012, 2014) that noted correlations between viscosity and elliptic flow but did not validate their models against experimental stopping data. The results enable quantitative assessment of how close nuclear matter at intermediate energies approaches the universal quantum lower limit on η/s proposed by Kovtun (2005) and explored in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions (Lacey 2007).
