# Twist-3 contributions to wide-angle photoproduction of pions

> We investigate wide-angle pi^0 photoproduction within the handbag approach to twist-3 accuracy. In contrast to earlier work both the 2-particle as well as the 3-particle twist-3 contributions are taken into account. It is shown that both are needed for consistent results that respect gauge invariance and crossing properties. The numerical studies reveal the dominance of the twist-3 contribution. With it fair agreement with the recent CLAS measurement of the pi^0 cross section is obtained. We briefly comment also on wide-angle photoproduction of other pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

The handbag approach to wide-angle π⁰ photoproduction, when extended to include both the 2-particle and 3-particle twist-3 contributions (not just the 2-particle part as in earlier work), yields a consistent, gauge-invariant, crossing-symmetric amplitude that is dominated by the twist-3 contribution and achieves fair agreement with CLAS data, in contrast to the leading-twist analysis, which underestimates the cross section by about two orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitudes for the subprocess γ q → π⁰ q to twist-3 accuracy within the handbag approach, using light-cone collinear factorization. They include both the 2-particle and 3-particle (q\bar{q}g Fock component) twist-3 pion distribution amplitudes, which are related by the equations of motion, and combine them with helicity-flip (transversity) and non-flip generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton at zero skewness. The soft form factors are evaluated from GPD parametrizations extracted from nucleon form factor data (Kroll 2013) and from analyses of hard pion electroproduction (Kroll 2013, Passek-Kumericki 2013), and the subprocess amplitudes are then convoluted with these form factors to obtain the full photoproduction helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The twist-3 contribution dominates the cross section; the leading-twist analysis underestimates the CLAS π⁰ cross section by about two orders of magnitude.
* The mass parameter μ_π = m_π²/(m_u+m_d) is about 2 GeV at the scale 2 GeV, and at the scale μ₀ = 4 GeV² it is μ_π(μ₀) = 2.6 GeV (with large uncertainty; values of 1.8 GeV and 1.9 GeV are also quoted in the literature).
* The pion decay constant is f_π = 0.132 GeV.
* The second Gegenbauer coefficient of the twist-2 pion DA is a₂(μ₀) = 0.1364 ± 0.0213 from lattice QCD (Braun 2015).
* The form factors scale as F_i ∼ 1/(-t)^{d_i} with powers d_i listed in Table 2: for example, d_u = 2.25 for R_V, 2.22 for R_A, 2.83 for R_T, 2.5 for S_T and 2.5 for \bar{S}_T; for d-quark form factors the powers are 3.0, 2.61, 3.12, 3.5, and 3.0 respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier handbag calculations of wide-angle π⁰ photoproduction (Huang 2000, Kroll 2001) that included only the 2-particle twist-3 contribution (which was found to vanish in the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation) and failed to reproduce the data. It is motivated by the success of the same transversity-GPD plus twist-3 pion DA mechanism in explaining the transverse cross section in hard π⁰ electroproduction measured by the Jefferson Lab Hall A collaboration (Defurne 2016) and by HERMES (2008). The present inclusion of the full twist-3 contribution (2- and 3-particle) enables a consistent description of wide-angle photoproduction and provides a framework for analyzing spin-dependent observables (A_LL, K_LL) that will be measured at Jefferson Lab.
