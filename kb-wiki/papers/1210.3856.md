# Odd- J Pairing in Nuclei

> We point out a simplicity that arises when we use an interaction in which only an energy with odd J is non-zero. The emphasis is on J= J_{max} and in particular J=9+ in the g_{9/2} shell. It is noted that high overlaps can be deceptive. In many cases a single set of U9-j coefficients gives either an exact or a very good approximation to the wave function of a yrast state.

# Odd-J Pairing in Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for a simple interaction where only a single odd-J two-body matrix element is non-zero (specifically J=J_max=9 in the g_{9/2} shell), the wave function components of the lowest I=0+ state are proportional to a single set of unitary 9-j coefficients, but caution that high overlaps with realistic interactions can be deceptive because the resulting energy spectra are drastically different despite overlap values exceeding 0.99.

## Method

The authors perform shell-model calculations in a single-j shell for two-proton-two-neutron systems (specifically 96Cd and 44Ti), using an interaction where only the J=9+ two-body matrix element is non-zero and attractive. They diagonalize the four-particle Hamiltonian in a basis of coupled proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairs with even angular momenta, and compare the resulting wave functions and spectra to those from a realistic CCGI interaction and from other schematic interactions including J=0 pairing and J=1 pairing.

## Key numbers

- Overlap of the E(9) interaction wave function with the realistic CCGI interaction for the I=0+ ground state of 96Cd: 0.9467
- Overlap of the E(0,9) interaction (both J=0+ and J=9+ non-zero) with CCGI: 0.9944
- Overlap of the E(0) pairing interaction with CCGI: 0.9020
- Overlap of the E(1) interaction with CCGI: 0.6484
- For the E(9) interaction in 96Cd, the I=16+ state becomes the ground state at 0.0000 MeV, while the I=0+ state lies at 1.0587 MeV
- For the E(0,9) interaction, the I=0+ ground state is at 0.0000 MeV and the I=16+ state is at 2.1678 MeV
- In the g_{9/2} shell, the overlap between the [9,9] and [9,7] U9-j sets for I=2+ is 0.00025756 (with normalized states)
- In the d_{5/2} shell, the corresponding overlap is 0.0290; in the f_{7/2} shell, it is 0.002916

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Moya de Guerra et al., 2003) on odd-J pairing interactions in single-j shells, and responds to recent interest in J_max pairing by Qi et al. (2011) who proposed wave functions for 96Cd with proton-neutron pairs coupled to maximum angular momentum. The paper shows that while the J=J_max interaction yields wave functions with high overlap to realistic interactions, the resulting energy spectra are unrealistic, serving as a cautionary note for the interpretation of overlap values in nuclear pairing studies. Related work by Zerguine and Van Isacker (2011), Cederwall et al. (2011), Xu et al. (2012), and Zhao and Arima (2005) is also discussed.
