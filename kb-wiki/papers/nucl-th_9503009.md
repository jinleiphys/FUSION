# Composite vertices that lead to soft form factors

> The momentum-space cut-off parameter $Λ$ of hadronic vertex functions is studied in this paper. We use a composite model where we can measure the contributions of intermediate particle propagations to $Λ$. We show that in many cases a composite vertex function has a much smaller cut-off than its constituent vertices, particularly when light constituents such as pions are present in the intermediate state. This suggests that composite meson-baryon-baryon vertex functions are rather soft, i.e., they have Λconsiderably less than 1 GeV. We discuss the origin of this softening of form factors as well as the implications of our findings on the modeling of nuclear reactions.

## Key claim

The momentum-space cutoff parameter Λ of a composite hadronic vertex function is often much smaller than the cutoffs of its constituent subvertices, especially when light particles such as pions appear in the intermediate state; this softening arises from the nonlocality of the intermediate particle propagations and occurs even when the constituent vertices themselves are hard (i.e., have very large Λ).

## Method

The authors constructed a lowest-order composite meson-baryon-baryon (MBB) vertex in which the external meson decays into two mesons that then interact with baryons via s-wave subvertices. They evaluated the loop integral for the composite vertex function, first in the limit of infinite subvertex cutoffs (contact interactions) and then with finite monopole noncovariant form factors for the subvertices, using Feynman parameter integration and the static approximation. The momentum-space cutoff Λ of the composite vertex was defined as the value of three-momentum |p| at which the normalized vertex function R(s,p) falls to 1/2.

## Key numbers

- For Set I (m_b = m_N = 939 MeV, m_1 = m_2 = m_π = 139.6 MeV, all subvertex cutoffs infinite), the composite vertex cutoff Λ = 0.60 GeV.
- For Set II (m_b = m_N* = 1535 MeV, m_1 = m_2 = m_π, all subvertex cutoffs infinite), Λ = 1.14 GeV.
- For Set III (m_b = m_N, m_1 = m_2 = 2m_π, all subvertex cutoffs infinite), Λ = 1.30 GeV.
- For Set IV (m_b = m_N, m_1 = m_2 = m_π, subvertex cutoffs Λ_0 = Λ_1 = Λ_2 = 1.2 GeV), the composite Λ is significantly reduced relative to the infinite-cutoff case (exact value given in Table 2 of the paper).
- For Set V (Λ_0 = 0.46 GeV, Λ_1 = Λ_2 = 1.2 GeV, same masses as Set IV), the composite Λ is further reduced.
- For Set VI (Λ_0 = 0.46 GeV, Λ_1 = Λ_2 = 0.65 GeV, same masses as Set IV), the composite Λ is reduced still further.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronic form factors by showing that composite vertex functions naturally produce soft form factors (Λ < 1 GeV), in contrast to the hard form factors (Λ_πNN ~ 1.3 GeV) used in the Bonn potential (Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster, 1987). The results support findings by the Jülich group (Kim, Durso, and Holinde, 1994) and by Janssen et al. (1993) that including ππ and πρ interactions allows softer form factors, and are consistent with analyses of deep inelastic scattering via the Sullivan mechanism (Frankfurt, Mankiewicz, and Strikman, 1989; Kumano, 1991) that yield Λ_πNN ~ 0.65 GeV. The work enables a reexamination of meson-exchange potential models and other nuclear reaction calculations that have relied on phenomenological multipole form factors with cutoffs of 1 GeV or greater.
