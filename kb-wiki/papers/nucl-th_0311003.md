# State dependent $δ$-pairing and spontaneous fission

> We examine the fission barriers, mass parameters and spontaneous fission half lives of fermium isotopes within the framework of macroscopic-microscopic model with a $δ$-pairing interaction. Four different macroscopic models are applied and studied. The results are compared to experimental data and to the corresponding monopole pairing ones. The half lives obtained in the $δ$-pairing model are comparable with experimental data. The state-dependence of pairing has an important effect on the calculated fission half-lives.

# State dependent δ-pairing and spontaneous fission

## Key claim
The state-dependent δ-pairing interaction yields spontaneous fission half-lives for fermium isotopes that are comparable with experimental data, but the calculated values depend considerably on which macroscopic energy model is used alongside the pairing model. The authors find that δ-pairing works better than monopole pairing with most macroscopic models, yet the isotopic systematics of the half-lives remain unchanged.

## Method
The authors used a macroscopic-microscopic model with a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and a δ-pairing interaction (strength constants V₀ᵖ = V₀ⁿ = 215 MeV·fm³) treated in the BCS approximation. Fission barriers and collective mass tensors were calculated on a mesh in a three-dimensional deformation space (β₂, β₄, β₆) using the adiabatic cranking model, and spontaneous fission half-lives were obtained via the WKB approximation with a dynamic programming method to minimize the action. Four different macroscopic models (liquid drop, droplet, LSD, and Yukawa plus exponential) were applied and compared.

## Context
This work extends earlier spontaneous fission calculations that assumed constant pairing matrix elements (G = const), which averaged out the superconductive properties of nuclei and weakly reflected nucleon pair structure. The authors compare their δ-pairing results to the monopole pairing approach used in prior studies by Lojewski (2003) and others. The study enables systematic investigation of how the choice of both pairing interaction and macroscopic energy model affects fission barriers and half-lives, while noting that odd-multipolarity deformations (β₃, β₅) were neglected and should be included in future work.
