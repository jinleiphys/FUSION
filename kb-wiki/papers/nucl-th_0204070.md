# An Algebraic Pairing Model with Sp(4) Symmetry and its Deformation

> A fermion realization of the compact symplectic sp(4) algebra provides a natural framework for studying isovector pairing correlations in nuclei. While these correlations manifest themselves most clearly in the binding energies of 0^+ ground states, they also have a large effect on the energies of excited states, including especially excited 0^+ states. In this article we consider non-deformed as well as deformed algebraic descriptions of pairing through the reductions of sp_{(q)}(4) to different realizations of u_{(q)}(2) for single-j and multi-j orbitals. The model yields a classification scheme for completely paired 0^{+} states of even-even and odd-odd nuclei in the 1d_{3/2}, 1f_{7/2}, and 1f_{5/2}2p_{1/2}2p_{3/2}1g_{9/2} shells. Phenomenological non-deformed and deformed isospin-breaking Hamiltonians are expressed in terms of the generators of the dynamical symmetry groups Sp(4) and Sp_{q}(4). These Hamiltonians are related to the most general microscopic pairing problem, including isovector pairing and isoscalar proton-neutron interaction along with non-linear interaction in the deformed extension. In both the non-deformed and deformed cases the eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian are fit to the relevant Coulomb corrected experimental 0^{+} energies and this, in turn, allows us to estimate the interaction strength parameters, to investigate isovector-pairing properties and symmetries breaking, and to predict the corresponding energies. While the non-deformed theory yields results that are comparable to other theories for light nuclei, the deformed extension, which takes into account higher-order interactions between the particles, gives a better fit to the data. The multi-shell applications of the model provide for reasonable predictions of energies of exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fermion realization of the Sp(4) algebra and its q-deformed extension Sp_q(4) provides an exactly solvable algebraic model for isovector pairing correlations in nuclei, and that the deformed extension, which includes higher-order non-linear interactions, yields a better fit to experimental 0+ state energies than the non-deformed theory, though the non-deformed theory is comparable to other approaches for light nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct phenomenological Hamiltonians in terms of the generators of Sp(4) and Sp_q(4) dynamical symmetry groups, expressed through second-order Casimir invariants of the subalgebras u(2) corresponding to isospin, proton-neutron (pn), and like-particle (pp, nn) coupling limits. They fit the eigenvalues of these Hamiltonians to Coulomb-corrected experimental 0+ state energies for even-even and odd-odd nuclei in the 1d3/2, 1f7/2, and 1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2 shells, using a non-linear least-squares procedure to determine the interaction strength parameters.

## Key numbers

* For the 1d3/2 shell (Omega = 2), the classification scheme includes nuclei from 32S to 40Ca, with the fully-paired 0+ states labeled by pair numbers (n1, n0, n-1) for pp, pn, and nn pairs respectively.
* For the 1f7/2 shell (Omega = 4), the classification scheme covers nuclei from 40Ca to 48Ca, with multiple basis states for some nuclei such as 44Ti having two basis states: |1,0,1) and |0,2,0).
* The non-deformed pn pairing energy is given by epsilon_pn = (G/Omega) * n0 * (2Omega - n + n0 + 1)/2, where n is the total number of valence particles and n0 is the number of pn pairs.
* The non-deformed pp(nn) pairing energy is given by epsilon_pp(nn) = (F/Omega) * n_{pm1} * (Omega + n_{pm1} - N_{pm} + 1), where N_{pm} is the number of valence protons or neutrons.
* The q-deformed pairing energies expand as epsilon_pn^q = (G_q/G) * epsilon_pn * {1 + kappa^2 * (n0^2 - 4Omega^2 - 1 + (2Omega * epsilon_pn / (n0 * G))^2) / (24 Omega^2) + O(kappa^4)} for the pn limit.
* The q-deformed pp(nn) pairing energies expand as epsilon_pp(nn)^q = (F_q/F) * epsilon_pp(nn) * {1 +/- kappa * (1 + 2Omega) / (4Omega) + O(kappa^2)} for the like-particle limit.

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) seniority model (Kerman, 1961) and the SO(5) model (Hecht, 1965; Ginocchio, 1965) for isovector pairing to include a q-deformed generalization that incorporates non-linear interactions. The Sp(4) approach is isomorphic to SO(5) and provides an exactly solvable algebraic framework that can be compared to the SO(8) model (Pang, 1969) and the Interacting Boson Model (Van Isacker and Warner, 1997) for broader pairing problems. The results enable predictions of 0+ state energies for exotic nuclei and provide estimates of interaction strength parameters that can be related to microscopic two-body matrix elements.
