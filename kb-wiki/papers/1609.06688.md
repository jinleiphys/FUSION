# Analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors from light-front holographic QCD : The spacelike region

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the spacelike nucleon electromagnetic form factors and their flavor decomposition within the framework of light-front holographic QCD. We show that the inclusion of the higher Fock components $\ket {qqqq\bar{q}}$ has a significant effect on the spin-flip elastic Pauli form factor and almost zero effect on the spin-conserving Dirac form factor. We present light-front holographic QCD results for the proton and neutron form factors at any momentum transfer range, including asymptotic predictions, and show that our results agree with the available experimental data with high accuracy. In order to correctly describe the Pauli form factor we need an admixture of a five quark state of about 30$\%$ in the proton and about 40$\%$ in the neutron. We also extract the nucleon charge and magnetic radii and perform a flavor decomposition of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The free parameters needed to describe the experimental nucleon form factors are very few: two parameters for the probabilities of higher Fock states for the spin-flip form factor and a phenomenological parameter $r$, required to account for possible SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry breaking effects in the neutron, whereas the Pauli form factors are normalized to the experimental values of the anomalous magnetic moments. The covariant spin structure for the Dirac and Pauli nucleon form factors prescribed by AdS$_5$ semiclassical gravity incorporates the correct twist scaling behavior from hard scattering and also leads to vector dominance at low energy.

## Key claim
The paper claims that light-front holographic QCD, with the inclusion of higher Fock components (five-quark states), can accurately describe nucleon electromagnetic form factors across all momentum transfers using very few free parameters; however, the neutron Dirac form factor requires an additional phenomenological parameter \(r = 2.08\) to account for SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry breaking, and the model is less reliable for neutron form factors at low \(Q^2\) due to leading cancellations.

## Method
The authors compute nucleon electromagnetic form factors by mapping the AdS\(_5\) semiclassical gravity formalism to light-front QCD, using the universal confinement scale \(\kappa = m_\rho/\sqrt{2} = 0.548\) GeV determined from the rho meson mass. They include valence (three-quark) and higher Fock (five-quark) components in the nucleon wave function, with the Dirac form factor described by twist-3 and twist-4 contributions and the Pauli form factor by twist-4 and twist-6 contributions, normalized to experimental anomalous magnetic moments. The neutron Dirac form factor is modified by a free parameter \(r\) to account for SU(6) symmetry breaking effects.

## Key numbers
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: \(\chi_p = \mu_p - 1 = 1.793\)
- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: \(\chi_n = \mu_n = -1.913\)
- Universal confinement scale: \(\kappa = m_\rho/\sqrt{2} = 0.548\) GeV, from the rho meson mass \(m_\rho = 0.775\) GeV
- Higher Fock state probability for proton Pauli form factor: \(\gamma_p = 0.27\) (27% admixture of five-quark state)
- Higher Fock state probability for neutron Pauli form factor: \(\gamma_n = 0.38\) (38% admixture of five-quark state)
- Phenomenological parameter for neutron Dirac form factor: \(r = 2.08\)
- Higher Fock component contribution to proton Dirac form factor: approximately 1%
- Higher Fock component contribution to neutron Dirac form factor: approximately 2%
- Asymptotic scaling: \(Q^4 F_1(Q^2) \sim \text{const}\) for Dirac form factor; \(Q^6 F_2(Q^2) \sim \text{const}\) for Pauli form factor

## Context
This work extends previous light-front holographic QCD results by Brodsky et al. (2014) by systematically including higher Fock components (five-quark states) to describe the Pauli form factor, which earlier valence-only models could not reproduce successfully. It contrasts with phenomenological models requiring 8-12 free parameters (Gutsche et al., 2013, 2014) and with simple holographic models that fail to describe all nucleon form factor properties (Chakrabarti, 2013; Mondal, 2016). The results enable predictions for upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments at higher \(Q^2\) (up to 18 GeV\(^2\)), including measurements of the proton electric to magnetic form factor ratio, neutron form factors, and the proton electric radius.
