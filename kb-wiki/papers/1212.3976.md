# Nuclear Density Dependence of In-Medium Polarization

> It is shown that polarization transfer measurements $(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$ on a specific target nucleus can provide constraints on the ratio of the in-medium electric to magnetic form factor. Thereby one exploits the fact that proton knockout from single-particle levels exhibit a specific sensitivity to the effective nuclear density. It is shown that in $^{12}$C the effective nuclear density for $s$-shell knockout is about twice as high as for $p$-shell knockout. With current model predictions for the in-medium form factors, one obtains measurable modifications of the order of 5% in the ratios of the double polarization observables between those single-particle levels.

## Key claim

Polarization transfer measurements comparing proton knockout from the \(s\)-shell and \(p\)-shell in \(^{12}\text{C}\) can provide constraints on the ratio of in-medium electric to magnetic form factors, because the effective nuclear density for \(s\)-shell knockout is about twice as high as for \(p\)-shell knockout. The expected modification in the double polarization observable ratio between these shells is of the order of 5% under current model predictions, but this is a theoretical estimate that requires experimental verification.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Multiple Scattering Glauber Approximation (RMSGA), an unfactorized approach, to compute \((e,e'p)\) reaction amplitudes for proton knockout from \(^{12}\text{C}\). The single-particle bound state wave functions were obtained from the Serot-Walecka mean-field model, and final-state interactions (FSI) were treated with a relativistic Glauber eikonal phase that is scalar in spin-space, thus containing no spin effects. The off-shell cross section CC2 was used for the polarized electron scattering off the bound proton, and short-range correlations were included via a Jastrow correlation function. The effective nuclear density probed in knockout from each shell was computed as a function of missing momentum.

## Key numbers

- Effective nuclear density for \(s\)-shell knockout in \(^{12}\text{C}\): about 0.1 fm\(^{-3}\), more than double the density for \(p\)-shell knockout (about 0.04 fm\(^{-3}\)) at \(Q^2 = 0.4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- In the Chiral Quark Soliton (CQS) model, the electric form factor decreases by about 5% at nuclear saturation density (\(\sim 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)), while the magnetic form factor modification is smaller (1-2%).
- In the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model, at saturation density the electric form factor is reduced by approximately 7%, and the magnetic form factor increases by about the same amount.
- The expected modification in the double ratio \(\left(P_x/P_z \right)_{s} / \left(P_x/P_z \right)_{p}\) is about 5% at the proposed kinematics (\(Q^2 = 0.4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), beam energy 600 MeV, scattered electron energy \(E' = 384\) MeV, electron scattering angle 82.4 degrees, proton angle 34.7 degrees).
- The \(s\)-shell and \(p\)-shell removal protons are separated by more than 2 MeV in missing mass.
- Estimated systematic uncertainty in the polarization ratio is 2%, based on previous results from Paolone (2010), Zhan (2011), and Dieterich (2000).

## Context

This work extends previous polarization transfer measurements on \(^4\text{He}\) at MAMI and Jefferson Lab (Paolone 2010, Strauch 2002) that showed discrepancies with Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) calculations using free nucleon form factors, and could be described by medium-modified form factors (Lu 1997, Smith 2004, Cloet 2005) or by charge-exchange FSI effects (PhysRevLett.94.072303). The proposed measurement on \(^{12}\text{C}\) enables a comparison between two shells within the same nucleus, reducing systematic uncertainties compared to comparing quasi-elastic to elastic scattering off hydrogen, and can map the density dependence of in-medium nucleon modifications as a function of local nuclear density and missing momentum.
