# Correlations beyond the mean field in Magnesium isotopes: Angular momentum projection and configuration mixing

> The quadrupole deformation properties of the ground and low-lying excited states of the even-even Magnesium isotopes with N ranging from 8 to 28 have been studied in the framework of the angular momentum projected generator coordinate method with the Gogny force. It is shown that the N=8 neutron magic number is preserved (in a dynamical sense) in \nuc{20}{Mg} leading to a spherical ground state. For the magic numbers N=20 and N=28 this is not the case and prolate deformed ground states are obtained. The method yields values of the two neutron separation energies which are in much better agreement with experiment than those obtained at the mean field level. It is also obtained that \nuc{40}{Mg} is at the neutron dripline. Concerning the results for the excitation energies of the $2^+$ excited states and their transition probabilities to the ground state we observe a good agreement with the available experimental data. On the theoretical side, we also present a detailed justification of the prescription used for the density dependent part of the interaction in our beyond-mean-field calculations.

# Correlations beyond the mean field in Magnesium isotopes: Angular momentum projection and configuration mixing

## Key claim

The N=20 and N=28 neutron magic numbers are not preserved in the Magnesium isotopic chain, yielding prolate deformed ground states for nuclei at those neutron numbers, whereas the N=8 magic number is dynamically preserved in 20Mg leading to a spherical ground state. The authors demonstrate that beyond-mean-field correlations (angular momentum projection and configuration mixing) are essential to obtain two-neutron separation energies in much better agreement with experiment than mean-field results alone.

## Method

The authors performed angular momentum projected generator coordinate method (AMPGCM) calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction, with the axially symmetric quadrupole moment as the generating coordinate. Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) intrinsic states were obtained under constraints on the axial quadrupole moment, with axial symmetry, parity, time-reversal, and e^{-iπJ_y} symmetry imposed. The angular momentum projection was carried out exactly (not via the Gaussian Overlap Approximation), and configuration mixing was performed by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation for the projected states.

## Key numbers

- The study covers even-even Magnesium isotopes with neutron numbers N ranging from 8 to 28 (nuclei 20Mg through 40Mg).
- 40Mg is found to be at the neutron dripline.
- For 34Mg, the measured E(4+)/E(2+) ratio of 3.18 is cited as very close to the rotational limit.
- For 32Mg, the ratio E(4+)/E(2+)=2.6 is cited as consistent with expectations for a rotational state.

## Context

This work extends prior mean-field studies (e.g., Skyrme Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean field calculations) that had found 32Mg to be spherical, by incorporating dynamical correlations through exact angular momentum projection and configuration mixing. It builds on earlier work by the same group using the Gogny interaction in the Bohr Hamiltonian method (Rodriguez-Guzman, Egido, Robledo, 2000) and on angular momentum projected GCM studies of the N≈28 region. The results enable systematic comparison with experimental data on 2+ excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities across the Mg isotopic chain, and provide a theoretical framework for understanding the erosion of shell closures at N=20 and N=28.
