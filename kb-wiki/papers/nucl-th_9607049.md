# Breakup reactions of the halo nuclei Be11 and B8

> We calculate the nuclear induced breakup of Be11 and B8 using a more realistic treatment of the diffraction and stripping processes than in previous work. The breakup is treated in the eikonal approximation with a profile function calculated from a realistic optical potential at low energies and from free nucleon-nucleon cross sections at high energies. This treatment gives a good description of measured breakup cross sections, as well as the longitudinal momentum distribution of the core-like fragments, which is narrower than predicted in the transparent limit. The real part of the potential is found to be significant and enhances the diffractive breakup at low energies.

# Breakup reactions of the halo nuclei $^{11}$Be and $^{8}$B

## Key claim

The nuclear-induced breakup of the halo nuclei $^{11}$Be and $^{8}$B can be described by the eikonal approximation with realistic profile functions, but only if the real part of the optical potential is included at low energies (below about 100 MeV/nucleon) and the full expression for the differential cross section is used rather than the transparent limit approximation. The transparent limit systematically overestimates the width of longitudinal momentum distributions, especially for $^{8}$B where the discrepancy is a factor of nearly two.

## Method

The authors calculate breakup cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions using the eikonal (Glauber) approximation, with profile functions derived from a realistic optical potential at low energies (20-200 MeV/nucleon) and from free nucleon-nucleon cross sections at high energies (100-800 MeV/nucleon). The halo nucleus is treated in a single-particle model with Woods-Saxon potentials: an s-wave for $^{11}$Be (neutron binding energy 0.503 MeV) and a p-wave for $^{8}$B (proton binding energy 0.137 MeV). The calculation separately treats diffractive breakup, neutron stripping, core stripping, and absorption of both constituents, with the core-target interaction obtained by folding the optical potential with the core density distribution.

## Key numbers

- $^{11}$Be neutron binding energy: 0.503 MeV, reproduced with Woods-Saxon well depth $V_0 = -61.1$ MeV (radius $R=2.7$ fm, diffuseness $a=0.52$ fm)
- $^{8}$B proton binding energy: 0.137 MeV, reproduced with Woods-Saxon well depth $V_0 = -47.5$ MeV (radius $R=2.48$ fm, diffuseness $a=0.52$ fm)
- Longitudinal momentum distribution FWHM for $^{10}$Be fragments from $^{11}$Be breakup at 66 MeV/nucleon on $^{9}$Be: full calculation gives $\Gamma = 41.4$ MeV/c, transparent limit gives $\Gamma = 44.5$ MeV/c, experiment gives $41.6 \pm 2.1$ MeV/c
- Narrow Gaussian component width $\sigma_0$ for $^{11}$Be breakup: full calculation gives 22.2 MeV/c, transparent limit gives 28.7 MeV/c, experiment gives 22.1 MeV/c
- Longitudinal momentum distribution FWHM for $^{7}$Be fragments from $^{8}$B breakup at 1470 MeV/nucleon on $^{12}$C: full calculation gives $\Gamma = 88$ MeV/c, transparent limit gives $\Gamma = 151$ MeV/c, experiment gives $81 \pm 6$ MeV/c
- Total interaction cross section for $^{8}$B on $^{12}$C at 1470 MeV/nucleon: calculation gives 838 mb, experiment gives $809 \pm 11$ mb
- Proton-removal cross section $\sigma(^{8}\text{B},^{7}\text{Be})$ at 1470 MeV/nucleon: calculation gives 69 mb, experiment gives $94 \pm 4$ mb
- Total interaction cross section for $^{8}$B on $^{12}$C at 790 MeV/nucleon: calculation gives 796 mb, experiment gives $798 \pm 6$ mb
- Proton-removal cross section at 790 MeV/nucleon: calculation gives 66 mb, experimental difference method gives $60 \pm 11$ mb

## Context

This work extends the Serber model (Serber 1947) and Glauber theory (Glauber 1955) for deuteron breakup to halo nuclei, improving upon earlier calculations by Esbensen (1996) and Yabana, Ogawa, and Suzuki (1992) by using realistic optical potentials rather than black-disk approximations. The authors show that the transparent limit approximation, which identifies momentum distributions with the Fourier transform of the ground-state wave function, is inadequate for both $^{11}$Be and especially $^{8}$B, where the p-wave halo leads to a factor-of-two overestimate of the width. The results enable more reliable extraction of halo structure from breakup data and are relevant for determining the $S$-factor for the $p + ^{7}\text{Be} \rightarrow ^{8}\text{B}$ radiative capture process, as discussed by Brown, Csoto, and Sherr (1996). The discrepancy between proton-removal cross sections measured at 790 and 1470 MeV/nucleon remains unresolved and requires further experimental clarification.
