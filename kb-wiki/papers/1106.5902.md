# STAR experiment results from the beam energy scan program at RHIC

> We present the first results using the STAR detector from the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). In this program, Au ion collisions at center of mass energies (\sqrt{s_{NN}}) of 7.7, 11.5 and 39 GeV allowed RHIC to extend the study of the QCD phase diagram from baryonic chemical potential values of 20 MeV to about 400 MeV. For the high net-baryon density matter at midrapidity, formed in these collisions, we report several interesting measurements. These include the observation of difference between anti-particle and particle elliptic flow, disappearance of the difference in dynamical azimuthal correlations with respect to event plane between same and opposite signed charged particles, change in slope of eccentricity at freeze-out and directed flow of protons as a function of \sqrt{s_{NN}} and the deviation of higher order fluctuations from hadron resonance gas and Poissonian expectations. Possible interpretations of these observations are also discussed.

## Key claim

The Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at RHIC has extended the study of the QCD phase diagram to baryonic chemical potential values of about 400 MeV, and the first results show several observables that may indicate a transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom around 11.5 GeV, but the interpretations remain preliminary and require further data at 19.6 and 27 GeV for confirmation.

## Method

The STAR experiment collected Au+Au collision data at center-of-mass energies of 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-Of-Flight detectors for particle identification at midrapidity. Elliptic flow was measured via the event-plane method with an eta-gap of 0.05, dynamical charge correlations used a three-particle correlation technique, freeze-out eccentricity was extracted from HBT radii relative to the reaction plane, and higher moments of net-proton distributions were measured for transverse momentum between 0.4 and 0.8 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic chemical potential range extended from 20 MeV to about 400 MeV across the BES energies
- Approximately 5, 11, and 170 million good events collected for 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV Au+Au collisions respectively
- Chemical freeze-out temperature increases from 150 MeV to about 165 MeV with increasing beam energy
- Percentage difference in particle and anti-particle elliptic flow increases as beam energy decreases
- The difference in dynamical charge correlations between same and opposite charges almost vanishes at 7.7 GeV
- Proton directed flow slope changes sign as a function of center-of-mass energy
- Anti-proton directed flow slope remains negative for all studied energies
- Net-proton higher order fluctuations show deviation from Hadron Resonance Gas model expectations for energies below 39 GeV

## Context

This work extends the RHIC heavy-ion program from the top energy of 200 GeV, where partonic degrees of freedom were established (STAR White Paper), down to 7.7 GeV to probe the QCD phase diagram at high baryon density. The results build on lattice QCD calculations at zero baryon chemical potential (Lattice Nature, 2006) and theoretical predictions for critical point signatures (Stephanov, 2009), and enable future searches for the QCD critical point and first-order phase transition boundary with upcoming data at 19.6 and 27 GeV.
