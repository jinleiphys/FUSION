# Longitudinal dependence of open heavy flavor $R_\text{AA}$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Heavy flavor probes are sensitive to the properties of the quark gluon plasma (QGP) produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. A huge amount of effort has been devoted to studying different aspects of the heavy-ion collisions using heavy flavor particles. In this work, we study the dynamics of heavy quark transport in the QGP medium using the rapidity dependence of heavy flavor observables. We calculate the nuclear modification of $\text{B}$ and $\text{D}$ meson spectra as well as spectra of leptons from heavy flavor decays in the rapidity range $[-4.0,4.0]$. We use an implementation of the improved Langevin equation with gluon radiation on top of a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamical background for several collision setups. We find that the rapidity dependence of the heavy quark modification is determined by the interplay between the smaller size of the medium, which affects the path length of the heavy quarks, and the softer heavy quark initial production spectrum. We compare our results with available experimental data and present predictions for open heavy flavor meson $R_\text{AA}$ at finite rapidity.

## Key claim

The rapidity dependence of the heavy quark nuclear modification factor is determined by the interplay between the smaller size of the medium at forward rapidity (which shortens the path length) and the softer initial heavy quark production spectrum; the latter effect dominates at high transverse momentum, leading to stronger suppression at larger rapidity despite the smaller medium.

## Method

The authors coupled a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamical background (generated by the CLVisc code) with a modified relativistic Langevin equation that includes both collisional energy loss and gluon radiation via the higher-twist formalism. Heavy quarks were sampled from a leading-order pQCD calculation with nuclear shadowing, propagated through the medium until hadronization at 165 MeV using a hybrid fragmentation plus coalescence model, and then decayed into electrons and muons via PYTHIA. The spatial diffusion coefficient was set to D(2πT) = 7 to best describe experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Spatial diffusion coefficient: D(2πT) = 7, used for all calculations.
- Initial time for hydrodynamical evolution: τ₀ = 0.6 fm.
- Freeze-out temperature: T_FO = 137 MeV.
- Decoupling (hadronization) temperature: T_d = 165 MeV.
- Shear viscosity: η/s = 0.15.
- Rapidity range studied: [-4.0, 4.0].
- Collision systems: Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV, Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, and Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV.
- D meson R_AA results show consistency with STAR data for p_T ≥ 4 GeV at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- D meson R_AA results show good agreement with CMS data for both Pb+Pb collisions throughout the whole p_T range.

## Context

This work extends previous heavy flavor transport studies that focused primarily on mid-rapidity (e.g., Cao et al. (2013, 2015), Gossiaux et al. (2010), He et al. (2011)) by incorporating a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic medium to study the longitudinal dependence of R_AA. It builds on the CLVisc hydrodynamics code developed by Pang et al. (2018) and the improved Langevin framework of Cao et al. (2013, 2015). The results enable future measurements of heavy flavor observables at finite rapidity, which can provide stricter constraints on phenomenological models of heavy quark transport in the quark-gluon plasma, particularly when combined with flow coefficient measurements.
