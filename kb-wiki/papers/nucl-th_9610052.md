# More Effective Field Theory for Nonrelativistic Scattering

> An effective field theory treatment of nucleon-nucleon scattering at low energy shows much promise and could prove a useful tool in the study of nuclear matter at both ordinary and extreme densities. The analysis is complicated by the existence a large length scale --- the scattering length --- which arises due to couplings in the short distance theory being near critical values. I show how this can be dealt with by introducing an explicit s-channel state in the effective field theory. The procedure is worked out analytically in a toy example. I then demonstrate that a simple effective field theory excellently reproduces the 1S_0 np phase shift up to the pion production threshold.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory (EFT) for nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon scattering can successfully handle the large scattering length (a = -23 fm in the 1S0 channel) by explicitly including an s-channel dibaryon field as a propagating degree of freedom, rather than relying on a derivative expansion that would fail when the scattering length is large.

## Method

The author constructs an effective Lagrangian containing nucleon fields N, a dibaryon field φ (with baryon number 2 and mass 2M+Δ), and local contact interactions, plus explicit one-pion exchange. The scattering amplitude is computed by summing bubble (ladder) diagrams using dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction. The method is first validated analytically on a toy δ-shell potential with a tunable coupling g, then applied to np scattering in the 1S0 channel by fitting the three free parameters (C, y², Δ) to reproduce the Nijmegen phase shift data.

## Key numbers

- np scattering length in the 1S0 channel: a = -23 fm = -1/(8 MeV)
- For the toy model, the scattering length diverges as g → 1, with a = -[g/(1-g)] (1/Λ)
- For the np fit at renormalization scale μ = mπ: C = -(1/192 MeV)², y² = 1/(507 MeV), Δ = 14.8 MeV, σ = -1
- The effective contact interaction C̄ = C + gA²/(2fπ²) = (1/237 MeV)², corresponding to Λ = 749 MeV
- The fit reproduces the 1S0 np phase shift up to center-of-mass momentum p_cm = 400 MeV (T_lab = 340 MeV), i.e., up to the pion production threshold

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering pioneered by Weinberg (1990-1992) and van Kolck (1992-1996), and builds directly on the techniques of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) who expanded 1/CA rather than including an explicit dibaryon. The method resolves the problem that a naive derivative expansion of the effective theory without a dibaryon has a radius of convergence set by p ~ sqrt(Λ/a), which is poor for systems with large scattering length. The approach enables systematic treatment of nuclear matter at ordinary and extreme densities, though application to spin-triplet channels and bulk matter remains to be resolved.
