# New approach for alpha decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei and applicability of WKB approximation

> The alpha decay half-lives of recently synthesized superheavy nuclei (SHN) are calculated by applying a new approach which estimates them with the help of their neighbors based on some simple formulas. The estimated half-life values are in very good agreement with the experimental ones, indicating the reliability of the experimental observations and measurements to a large extent as well as the predictive power of our approach. The second part of this work is to test the applicability of the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation for the quantum mechanical tunneling probability. We calculated the accurate barrier penetrability for alpha decay along with proton and cluster radioactivity by numerically solving Schrödinger equation. The calculated results are compared with those of the WKB method to find that WKB approximation works well for the three physically analogical decay modes.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new approach for estimating alpha decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei using simple formulas based on neighboring nuclei, claiming very good agreement with experimental data; however, the method relies on experimental Q values and the agreement is conditional on the reference nuclei being well-measured, with notable exceptions such as $^{282}$113 and $^{279}$111 where the half-lives are underestimated by one order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors derive the half-life of a target nucleus $^{A}Z$ from a reference nucleus $^{A_0}Z_0$ using the difference in logarithms of half-lives based on Royer's formula and the Viola-Seaborg semi-empirical (VSS) formula, applied to isotope chains and alpha decay chains. For the WKB test, they calculate accurate barrier penetrability by numerically solving the one-dimensional Schrodinger equation using recursion formulas that divide the potential barrier into a sequence of square barriers, then compare these results with the standard WKB approximation for alpha decay, proton radioactivity, and cluster emission.

## Key numbers

- For alpha decay of heavy and superheavy nuclei (Z = 52 to 118), the WKB approximation underestimates penetrability by about -40% to -30% relative deviation.
- For proton radioactivity of spherical proton emitters, the WKB method underestimates penetrability by -40% to -20% relative deviation.
- For cluster radioactivity (emitted particles from $^{14}$C to $^{34}$Si), the WKB approximation shows a relative deviation of only -5% to 15%.
- The half-life of $^{282}$113 is underestimated by one order of magnitude compared to experiment.
- The half-life of $^{279}$111 is also underestimated by the method, similar to results from DDM3Y and GLDM calculations.
- An uncertainty of 1 MeV in Q value corresponds to an uncertainty in alpha decay half-life ranging from $10^3$ to $10^5$ times in the heavy element region.

## Context

This work extends previous semi-empirical approaches such as Royer's formula (Royer, 2000) and the Viola-Seaborg formula (Viola and Seaborg, 1966) by using them to relate neighboring nuclei rather than calculating half-lives directly. The method is compared with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction and the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM), showing comparable or better agreement with experimental data. The WKB applicability test provides a systematic benchmark for the widely used WKB approximation in alpha decay, proton radioactivity, and cluster emission, enabling more reliable use of the approximation in future theoretical studies of superheavy nuclei.
