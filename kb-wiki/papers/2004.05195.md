# Non-equilibrium attractor in high-temperature QCD plasmas

> We establish the existence of a far-from-equilibrium attractor in weakly-coupled gauge theory undergoing one-dimensional Bjorken expansion. We demonstrate that the resulting far-from-equilibrium evolution is insensitive to certain features of the initial condition, including both the initial momentum-space anisotropy and initial occupancy. We find that this insensitivity extends beyond the energy-momentum tensor to the detailed form of the one-particle distribution function. Based on our results, we assess different procedures for reconstructing the full one-particle distribution function from the energy-momentum tensor along the attractor and discuss implications for the freeze-out procedure used in the phenomenological analysis of ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the existence of a far-from-equilibrium attractor in weakly-coupled QCD effective kinetic theory undergoing one-dimensional Bjorken expansion, demonstrating that the attractor behavior extends beyond the energy-momentum tensor to the detailed form of the one-particle distribution function, with the caveat that this is shown for a simplified gluonic plasma with moderately large 't Hooft coupling.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the effective kinetic theory (EKT) Boltzmann equation for a gluonic plasma undergoing one-dimensional Bjorken expansion, including both elastic (2-to-2) and inelastic (1-to-2) collision kernels with dynamical screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression. They initialize the system with either spheroidally-deformed thermal (RS) initial conditions or non-thermal color-glass-condensate (CGC) inspired initial conditions, varying the initial momentum-space anisotropy and initialization time. The time evolution of a complete set of integral moments of the distribution function is tracked and compared to predictions from various freeze-out prescriptions.

## Key numbers

- 't Hooft coupling λ = 10, corresponding to specific shear viscosity η/s ≈ 0.62
- Momentum-space grid uses 250 × 2000 points in p and x = cosθ directions, with momenta distributed logarithmically in the range [0.02, 45] Λ
- Initial time τ₀ = 0.095 (ν/ε)^(1/4) for the anisotropy variation study
- Momentum-space anisotropy parameters ξ₀ ∈ {-0.84, 0, 5.25, 10.1, 24} used for initial conditions
- The timescale at which different solutions collapse to the attractor is roughly τ/τ_R ∼ 0.5
- For LHC phenomenology mapping: τ₀^hydro = 0.25 fm/c corresponds to initial temperature T₀^hydro ≈ 700 MeV with ν_eff ≈ 40 degrees of freedom
- Rescaled times τ/τ_R = {0.2, 0.5, 1, 2, 5, 10} map to physical times τ ≈ {0.32, 0.86, 1.88, 4.23, 14.1, 38.5} fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous observations of non-equilibrium attractor behavior in simpler kinetic theories such as relaxation time approximation (RTA) and Israel-Stewart-type hydrodynamics (Heller 2015, Romatschke 2017, Kurkela 2019) to the more realistic setting of QCD effective kinetic theory. The paper also extends prior studies of hydrodynamization without thermalization (Chesler 2009, Kurkela 2015) by demonstrating that the attractor behavior applies to the full distribution function, not just hydrodynamic moments. The results enable quantitative assessment of freeze-out procedures used in phenomenological modeling of ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, particularly for small systems like proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions where far-from-equilibrium conditions persist.
