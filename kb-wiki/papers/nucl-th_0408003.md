# Deuteron Compton Scattering in Effective Field Theory And Spin-Independent Nucleon Polarizabilities

## Key claim
The vector amplitude in deuteron Compton scattering, omitted in previous pionless effective field theory calculations, contributes at the same order (Q²) as the nucleon polarizability contribution and significantly affects extraction of the isoscalar nucleon electric polarizability α₀ at 49 MeV. The authors recommend future measurements at 25-35 MeV photon energy where the theory is most reliable and polarizability effects remain appreciable, with a 30 MeV measurement at 3% error constraining α₀ to about 30% uncertainty.

## Method
The unpolarized deuteron Compton scattering cross section was calculated to O(Q²) in pionless effective field theory using a dibaryon approach, with the ³S₁-channel dibaryon field and nucleon fields. The calculation included the scalar, vector, and tensor amplitudes, where the vector amplitude (from spin-orbit and magnetic interactions) was newly computed and found to contribute at the same order as nucleon polarizabilities. A χ² fit to existing data at 49, 54.6, 54.9, and 55.9 MeV was performed to extract α₀ and β₀.

## Key numbers
- The vector amplitude contributes approximately 20% to the cross section at backward angles, comparable to the nucleon polarizability contribution at 49 MeV.
- A χ² fit to existing data (49, 54.6, 54.9, 55.9 MeV) in the dibaryon formalism gives α₀ = 12.3 ± 1.4 and β₀ = 5.0 ± 1.6 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³, theoretical uncertainty not included).
- In the perturbative effective range counting, the fit gives α₀ = 14.2 ± 2.1 and β₀ = 9.3 ± 2.5 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³).
- A measurement at 30 MeV with 3% error will constrain α₀ with 3 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ experimental error, comparable to the expected theoretical error.
- The leading-order chiral perturbation theory prediction used for comparison is α₀ = 10β₀ = 1.2 × 10⁻³ fm³.

## Context
This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of deuteron Compton scattering (Chen et al., 1999; Beane et al., 2002; Rupak, 2003) by including the vector amplitude that was previously omitted, which changes the extracted α₀ by more than 50% at 49 MeV. The results are compared to potential model calculations (Levchuk, 1994) and to the extraction from Lund data at 55 and 66 MeV giving α₀+β₀ = 17.4 ± 3.7 and α₀-β₀ = 6.4 ± 2.4. The work enables future high-precision experiments at HIGS (Duke University) to extract nucleon polarizabilities with controlled theoretical uncertainty by operating in the 25-35 MeV energy range where pionless EFT is most reliable.
