# Generalized polarizabilities and the spin-averaged amplitude in virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon

> We discuss the low-energy behavior of the spin-averaged amplitude of virtual Compton scattering (VCS) off a nucleon. Based on gauge invariance, Lorentz invariance and the discrete symmetries, it is shown that to first order in the frequency of the final real photon only two generalized polarizabilities appear. Different low-energy expansion schemes are discussed and put into perspective.

# Generalized polarizabilities and the spin-averaged amplitude in virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon

## Key claim
The paper proves that, to first order in the energy of the final real photon, only two independent scalar generalized polarizabilities appear in the spin-averaged virtual Compton scattering amplitude, rather than three as previously claimed by Guichon et al. (1995). This reduction follows from charge-conjugation invariance combined with pion (or nucleon) crossing symmetry, and was previously observed only within the specific framework of the linear sigma model by Metz and Drechsel (1996).

## Method
The authors construct a general parametrization of the virtual Compton scattering tensor for a spinless target (pion) using Lorentz invariance, gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, and discrete symmetries, then apply the Bardeen-Tung method to obtain gauge-invariant invariant amplitudes. They expand the regular (class B) part of the amplitude to second order in the final-photon energy ω', keeping the initial virtual photon three-momentum q̄ arbitrary, and compare this expansion with the multipole expansion of Guichon et al. (1995) to derive relations between the generalized polarizabilities and the invariant functions f_i.

## Key numbers
- The fine-structure constant is taken as e²/4π = 1/137.
- The characteristic energy scale for low-energy expansion in VCS off the nucleon is the pion mass m_π.
- The relation linking the three generalized polarizabilities is: (e²/4π) P̂^(01,1)0(q̄) = (2ω_0)/(3q̄²) [α(q̄) + β(q̄)], where ω_0 = M - √(M² + q̄²) is the initial virtual photon energy in the zero-energy limit of the final real photon.
- The electric multipole H^(21,21)0(ω',q̄) = (4π/e²)√(8/3) ω' ω_0 β(q̄) + O(ω'²), showing it is determined by the magnetic polarizability to lowest order in ω'.
- In the real Compton scattering limit (ω = q̄ = ω'), the transverse amplitudes yield: A₁ + zA₂ = (4π/e²) ω² α(0) + O(ω⁴) and A₂ = -(4π/e²) ω² β(0) + O(ω⁴).

## Context
This work extends the low-energy theorem for virtual Compton scattering (Low, 1954; Gell-Mann and Goldberger, 1954) and corrects the multipole expansion of Guichon et al. (1995), which introduced ten generalized polarizabilities including three scalar ones. The reduction to two independent scalar polarizabilities confirms and generalizes the finding of Metz and Drechsel (1996) within the linear sigma model. The results enable future extractions of generalized polarizabilities from experiments such as those proposed at CEBAF and MAMI, and provide a framework for comparing with heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations (Hemmert et al., 1996) and the low-energy expansion of Fearing and Scherer (1996).
