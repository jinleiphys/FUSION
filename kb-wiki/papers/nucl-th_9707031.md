# Multi-scattering effects in (e,e'p) knockout

> Multistep direct scattering of the ejectile proton is considered in (e,e'p) reactions in the quasi-free region as an improvement over the usual treatment of final-state interactions by means of an optical potential. The theory is applied to 40Ca(e,e'p) as a case example. Important contributions of two- and three-step processes are found at high missing energy and momentum in agreement with the experimental trend.

## Key claim

Multistep direct scattering of the ejectile proton in (e,e'p) reactions in the quasi-free region, treated via the Feshbach-Kerman-Koonin multistep direct theory, produces important two- and three-step contributions at high missing energy and momentum that improve agreement with experimental data beyond the usual optical-potential treatment of final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors applied the multistep direct (MSD) scattering theory of Feshbach, Kerman and Koonin to (e,e'p) knockout reactions, treating the direct knockout cross section in distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) and subsequent proton-nucleon scatterings via one-step MSD cross sections computed from DWBA with a Yukawa effective NN potential. The direct (e,e'p) cross section and the MSD cross sections for subsequent NN scatterings were convolved over intermediate energies and angles using Monte Carlo integration, with bound-state wavefunctions from a Woods-Saxon potential and an energy-dependent Lorentzian width for the bound-state energy distribution. The approach was applied to 40Ca(e,e'p) as a case example.

## Key numbers

- Incident electron energy: 497 MeV; electron scattering angle: 52.9 degrees; outgoing proton energy: 87 ± 10 MeV; scattered electron energy fixed at 350 MeV.
- Two-step and three-step processes dominate over the direct one-step process at high missing energy over most of the angular range except for scattering angles γ ≤ 10 degrees.
- Theoretical reduced cross sections multiplied by a factor of 0.5 (interpreted as an average spectroscopic factor) reproduce the experimental trend of Mougey (1976).
- The effective NN Yukawa potential had a range of 1 fm; the average single-particle density used was g = A/13; the spin cut-off parameter was σ²₂ = 0.28 × 2 × A^(2/3).

## Context

This work extends the classical Monte Carlo study of multiple scattering in (e,e'p) reactions by Boffi et al. (1985) to a quantum-mechanical treatment, and applies the MSD theory previously validated for nucleon-induced reactions (Gadioli and Hodgson, 1992; Chadwick et al., 1993; Koning and Akkermans, 1993) to electron-induced knockout. The results enable improved treatment of final-state interactions in the dip region between the quasi-free peak and Δ-resonance excitation, where earlier work by Takaki (1989) and Gareev and Ponomarev (1994) indicated limitations of two-body mechanisms.
