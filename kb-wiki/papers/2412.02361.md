# Chiral Anomalous Magnetohydrodynamics in action: effective field theory and holography

> Chiral Anomalous Magnetohydrodynamics (CAMHD) provides a low-energy effective framework for describing chiral fluids in the presence of dynamical electromagnetic fields and axial anomaly. This theory finds applications across diverse physical systems, including heavy-ion collisions, the early universe, and Weyl/Dirac semimetals. Along with Schwinger-Keldysh (SK) effective theories, holographic models serve as a complementary tool to provide a systematic formulation of CAMHD that goes beyond the weak coupling regime. In this work, we explore holographic models with $U(1)_A \times U(1)$ symmetry, where the electromagnetic $U(1)$ field is rendered dynamical through mixed boundary conditions applied to the bulk gauge field and the axial anomaly is introduced via a Chern-Simons bulk term. Through a detailed holographic SK analysis, we demonstrate that the low-energy effective action derived from this model aligns precisely with the SK field theory proposed by Landry and Liu and, in fact, it generalizes it to scenarios with finite background axial field. This alignment not only validates the holographic model but also paves the way for its use in exploring unresolved aspects of CAMHD, such as the recently proposed chiral magnetic electric separation wave and nonlinear chiral instabilities.

## Key claim

The holographic model with mixed boundary conditions for the electromagnetic gauge field and a Chern-Simons term for the axial anomaly produces a low-energy effective action that exactly matches the Schwinger-Keldysh field theory proposed by Landry and Liu (2022) and generalizes it to scenarios with finite background axial field, thereby validating the holographic approach as a tool for studying chiral anomalous magnetohydrodynamics beyond the weak coupling regime.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic model in a Schwarzschild-AdS₅ black brane background with U(1)ₐ × U(1) symmetry, where the vector gauge field is made dynamical via mixed boundary conditions and the axial anomaly is introduced through a Chern-Simons bulk term. Using the holographic Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, they derive the boundary effective action by solving the bulk equations of motion in a perturbative expansion, truncating at quartic order in the fields and at order ε² in the MHD scaling regime (∂₀ ∼ ε², ∂ᵢ ∼ ε¹). The resulting effective action is organized by number of fields and compared term-by-term with the EFT proposed by Landry and Liu (2022).

## Key numbers

- Anomaly coefficient: κ = e²/(24π²), appearing in the cubic Lagrangian terms 12κ[−φₐ EᵢBᵢ − φ BₐᵢEᵢ − φ EₐᵢBᵢ + ...]
- Temperature relation: rₕ = πT, where rₕ is the black brane horizon radius and T the boundary temperature
- Quadratic Lagrangian coefficients: 2π²/β² for Aₐ₀A₀ and Cₐ₀C₀ terms, π/β² for the dissipative Aₐᵢ terms, where β = 1/T
- Quartic anomalous coefficients: θ₁ = 9(−1+2log2)/(2rₕ²) κ², θ₂ = (9log2/rₕ²) κ², θ₃ = (9log2)/(4π rₕ²) κ²
- The holographic model reproduces the EFT coefficients n, n₅, 1/μ, and all transport coefficients (r∥, r⊥, r×, κ∥, κ⊥, κ×, λ∥, λ⊥, λ×, γ∥, γ⊥, γ×) in terms of the holographic parameters α₁, α₂, and the anomaly coefficient κ

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory for chiral anomalous magnetohydrodynamics developed by Landry and Liu (2022) and the holographic approach to magnetohydrodynamics using mixed boundary conditions pioneered by Domenech (2010), Natsuume (2022), and Baggioli (2023). It complements the alternative holographic framework using higher-form bulk gauge fields proposed by Das, Gregory, and Iqbal (2022), to which it is related by a bulk Hodge-dual transformation. The results enable future studies of unresolved aspects of CAMHD, including the chiral magnetic electric separation wave predicted by Landry and Liu (2022) and nonlinear chiral instabilities, by providing a holographic model valid beyond the weak coupling regime.
