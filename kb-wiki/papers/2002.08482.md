# Assessment of the beta-delayed proton decay rate of $^{11}$Be

> The $^{11}$Be neutron halo nucleus appears to decay into $^{10}$Be with a rate that exceeds expectations. Neutron disappearance into dark matter, beta decay of a halo neutron, or beta-delayed proton decay have been offered as explanations. In this work we study the latter option; we carry out shell model calculations and sequential decay analysis examining the beta-delayed proton decay going through a resonance in $^{11}$B. The narrow energy window, lack of states with sufficient spectroscopic strength, overwhelming alpha decay branch, all make reconciling the observed rate with beta-delayed proton decay difficult.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed beta-delayed proton decay rate of ¹¹Be (half-life ~1×10⁶ s) is virtually impossible to explain via a sequential decay through a resonance in ¹¹B, because the narrow energy window, insufficient spectroscopic strength of candidate states, and overwhelming competition from alpha decay all make the mechanism implausible, contrary to the suggestion in Ayyad (2019) that a resonance at 196 keV above the proton threshold could account for the rate.

## Method

The author performed shell-model calculations in the p-sd model space using the psdu interaction (Utsuno, 2011) and the fsu interaction (Lubna, 2019) to identify states in ¹¹B that could be populated by allowed Gamow-Teller beta decay from the ¹/2⁺ ground state of ¹¹Be. A sequential decay analysis was then carried out using a second-order quantum-mechanical amplitude, where the beta-decay width was computed from the phase-space function and the proton-decay width from Woods-Saxon potential model with spectroscopic factors from the shell model, including competition from the alpha-decay channel.

## Key numbers

- The observed branching ratio for beta-delayed proton emission is 1.3×10⁻⁵, corresponding to a half-life t_{Be→βp} ≈ 1×10⁶ s (Ayyad, 2019).
- The phase-space-based estimate for the halo-neutron beta decay gives t_{Be→βp} = 2.2×10¹⁰ s (Ayyad, 2019); a more detailed calculation gives t_{Be→βp} ~ 10⁸ s (Baye, 2011).
- The Q-value for beta-delayed proton emission is 0.2807 MeV; the proton separation energy in ¹¹B is 11.2285 MeV.
- The shell model predicts a ³/2⁺₃ state at 11.450 MeV excitation (experimental) with B(GT) = 0.615, log₁₀(ft) = 3.792, proton spectroscopic factor SF_p = 0.008, and alpha spectroscopic factor SF_α = 0.038.
- The isobaric analog state (last row in Table 2) has B(GT) = 0.571, log₁₀(ft) = 3.195, SF_p = 0.230, and SF_α = 0.000.
- The author's evaluation of the principal contribution from the isobaric analog state gives t_{Be→βp} = 2.6×10¹⁰ s.
- The alpha decay width of the ³/2⁺₂ state at 9.873 MeV is 109 keV (Refsgaard, 2019).
- The proton resonance width reported by Ayyad (2019) is Γ = 12(5) keV with log₁₀(ft) = 4.8(4).

## Context

This work addresses the unexplained discrepancy between the observed beta-delayed proton decay rate of ¹¹Be and theoretical estimates, which has fueled speculation about neutron decay into dark matter (Pfutzner, 2018; Fornal, 2018). The paper extends the analysis of Ayyad (2019), who proposed a resonance in ¹¹B at 196 keV above the proton threshold, by performing detailed shell-model calculations and sequential decay analysis. The results contradict the resonance explanation and instead support the earlier phase-space estimates of Baye (2011) and Ayyad (2019) that predict half-lives of order 10⁸–10¹⁰ s, leaving the dark-matter decay hypothesis as a possible explanation for the observed rate.
