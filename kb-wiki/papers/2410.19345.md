# Locating Critical Points Using Ratios of Lee-Yang Zeros

> We propose a method to numerically determine the location of a critical point in general systems using the finite-size scaling of Lee-Yang zeros. This method makes use of the fact that the ratios of Lee-Yang zeros on various spatial volumes intersect at the critical point. While the method is similar to the Binder-cumulant analysis, it is advantageous in suppressing the finite-volume effects arising from the mixing of variables in general systems. We show that the method works successfully for numerically locating the CP in the three-dimensional three-state Potts model with a nonzero external field.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ratios of the imaginary parts of Lee-Yang zeros on different spatial volumes intersect at the critical point, providing a method to locate critical points numerically that suppresses finite-volume effects from variable mixing more effectively than the Binder-cumulant method, though the method assumes the system belongs to the 3D-Ising universality class.

## Method

The authors perform Monte Carlo simulations of the three-dimensional three-state Potts model on cubic lattices of sizes L=24,30,40,50,60,70 using a heat-bath algorithm, generating 10^6 configurations per parameter set. They locate the Lee-Yang zeros by applying the reweighting method to search for complex values of the external field where the partition function vanishes, then compute ratios of the imaginary parts of these zeros for different orders n and fit them to a finite-size scaling ansatz to extract the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling from combined fit of ratios R21, R31, R41: tau_c = 0.549379(14)
- Critical exponent y_t from combined ratio fit: y_t = 1.70(16)
- Critical coupling from Binder cumulant analysis: tau_c = 0.549382(11)
- Critical exponent y_t from Binder cumulant: y_t = 1.63(13)
- Ratio r21 at intersection: 2.408(12)
- Ratio r31 at intersection: 3.669(24)
- Ratio r41 at intersection: 4.861(36)
- Binder cumulant b4 at intersection: 1.614(8)
- Lattice sizes used for fitting: L >= 40 (excluding L=24,30 due to finite-volume effects)

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling analysis of Lee-Yang zeros, previously studied by Kenna (1993) and Bena (2005), to provide a practical numerical method for locating critical points. The method is analogous to the Binder-cumulant approach introduced by Binder (1981) but offers improved suppression of finite-volume effects from non-orthogonal scaling variables. The technique is demonstrated on the three-state Potts model previously studied by Karsch (2000) and can be applied to locate the QCD critical point in lattice simulations, as well as critical points in other systems belonging to the 3D-Ising universality class.
