# Study of Minor Actinides Transmutation in PWR MOX fuel

> The management of long-lived radionuclides in spent fuel is a key issue to achieve the closed nuclear fuel cycle and the sustainable development of nuclear energy. Partitioning-Transmutation is supposed to be an efficient method to treat the long-lived radionuclides in spent fuel. Some Minor Actinides (MAs) have very long half-lives among the radionuclides in the spent fuel. Accordingly, the study of MAs transmutation is a significant work for the post-processing of spent fuel. In the present work, the transmutations in Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR) mixed oxide (MOX) fuel are investigated through the Monte Carlo based code RMC. Two kinds of MAs, $^{237}$Np and five MAs ($^{237}$Np, $^{241}$Am, $^{243}$Am, $^{244}$Cm and $^{245}$Cm) are incorporated homogeneously into the MOX fuel assembly. The transmutation of MAs is simulated with different initial MOX concentrations. The results indicate an overall nice efficiency of transmutation in both initial MOX concentrations, especially for the two kinds of MAs primarily generated in the UOX fuel, $^{237}$Np and $^{241}$Am. In addition, the inclusion of $^{237}$Np in MOX has no large influence for other MAs, while the transmutation efficiency of $^{237}$Np is excellent. The transmutation of MAs in MOX fuel depletion is expected to be a new, efficient nuclear spent fuel management method for the future nuclear power generation.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that transmutation of minor actinides (MAs) in PWR MOX fuel is neutronicly feasible, with the hedge that the transmutation efficiency depends strongly on the initial plutonium concentration in the MOX fuel and that the addition of MAs causes a large decrement in reactivity, especially for low-concentration MOX fuel.

## Method
The authors used the Monte Carlo code RMC to simulate a 17x17 PWR MOX fuel assembly with reflective boundary conditions, incorporating MAs homogeneously into the fuel rods. Two sets of MOX fuel were considered: low concentration (4.2% Pu isotopes) and high concentration (9.8% Pu isotopes). Two loading scenarios were simulated: 1% by weight of a single MA (²³⁷Np) and 1% by weight of a mixed-MA composition (²³⁷Np, ²⁴¹Am, ²⁴³Am, ²⁴⁴Cm, ²⁴⁵Cm), with burnup calculations performed using the embedded DEPTH code and ENDF/B-VII.0-based cross sections.

## Key numbers
- ²³⁷Np constitutes 56.2% of the total MAs in depleted PWR nuclear fuel (from reference Liu2014).
- The decay half-life of ²³⁷Np is 2.14 × 10⁶ years; ²⁴⁵Cm half-life is 8500 years; ²⁴³Am half-life is 7070 years; ²⁴¹Am half-life is 432 years.
- In high-concentration MOX with 1% mixed MAs loading at 64 GWd/t burnup, the transmutation rates of ²³⁷Np and ²⁴¹Am are both around 55%.
- In low-concentration MOX with 1% mixed MAs loading at 64 GWd/t burnup, the transmutation rate is 72% for ²³⁷Np and 90% for ²⁴¹Am.
- In the 1% ²³⁷Np loading case, the transmutation rate is 58% in high-concentration MOX and 75% in low-concentration MOX at 64 GWd/t burnup.
- The lowest transmutation rate of 55% for ²³⁷Np in high-concentration MOX at 64 GWd/t is equivalent to 2.47 × 10⁶ years of natural decay.
- In high-concentration MOX with 1% mixed MAs at 64 GWd/t, about 6.3 × 10¹⁹ at./cm³ of ²⁴¹Am was transmuted, while only about 2.0 × 10¹⁹ at./cm³ was produced compared to the case without MAs loading.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of MA transmutation in PWRs, which were based on UO₂ fuel assemblies (Liu 2014; Hu 2015), by instead using MOX fuel and homogeneous MA distribution. The study is motivated by the need to manage long-lived radionuclides in spent fuel and by the planned use of MOX fuel in reactors such as the Taishan EPR. The results enable future investigations into different moderator-to-fuel ratios, varying MA loading percentages, and heterogeneous MA placement in the outer layer of fuel rods to flatten radial power distribution while transmuting MAs.
