# Refractive effects and Airy structure in inelastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C rainbow scattering

> Inelastic $^{16}$O +$^{12}$C rainbow scattering to the $2^+$ (4.44 MeV) state of $^{12}$C was measured at the incident energies, $E_L$ = 170, 181, 200, 260 and 281 MeV. A systematic analysis of the experimental angular distributions was performed using the coupled channels method with an extended double folding potential derived from realistic wave functions for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O calculated with a microscopic $α$ cluster model and a finite-range density-dependent nucleon-nucleon force.The coupled channels analysis of the measured inelastic scattering data shows consistently some Airy-like structure in the inelastic scattering cross sections for the first $2^+$ state of $^{12}$C, which is somewhat obscured and still not clearly visible in the measured data. The Airy minimum was identified from the analysis and the systematic energy evolution of the Airy structure was studied. The Airy minimum in inelastic scattering is found to be shifted backward compared with that in elastic scattering.

## Key claim
The coupled channels analysis of measured inelastic scattering data shows consistently some Airy-like structure in the cross sections for the first 2+ state of 12C, but this structure is "somewhat obscured and still not clearly visible in the measured data", the Airy minimum is identified primarily from the theoretical calculations rather than directly from the experimental angular distributions.

## Method
Inelastic 16O+12C scattering to the 2+ (4.44 MeV) state of 12C was measured at five incident energies using semiconductor detector telescopes at the Jyvaskyla University cyclotron. The experimental angular distributions were analyzed using the coupled channels method with an extended double folding potential derived from microscopic alpha cluster model wave functions for both 12C and 16O, using a finite-range density-dependent nucleon-nucleon force (DDM3Y-FR). The calculations included coupling to the 0+, 2+, and 3- states of 12C and excited states of 16O, with phenomenological imaginary potentials.

## Key numbers
- Incident laboratory energies measured: 170, 181, 200, 260, and 281 MeV
- Airy minimum identified in inelastic scattering at theta_cm approximately 87 degrees (170 MeV), 83 degrees (181 MeV), 75 degrees (200 MeV), 53 degrees (260 MeV), and 47 degrees (281 MeV)
- Volume integral per nucleon pair for the double folding potential in elastic scattering: 306 MeV fm^3 (170 MeV), 304 MeV fm^3 (181 MeV), 301 MeV fm^3 (200 MeV), 290 MeV fm^3 (260 MeV), 285 MeV fm^3 (281 MeV)
- Volume integral per nucleon pair for inelastic scattering: 303, 301, 298, 287, and 283 MeV fm^3 at the same respective energies
- Calculated B(E2: 2_1+ -> 0+_1) for 12C: 9.3 e^2 fm^4 (compared to experimental 7.8 e^2 fm^4)
- Calculated B(E3: 3- -> 0+_1) for 12C: 124 e^2 fm^6 (compared to experimental 107 e^2 fm^6)
- Calculated B(E2: 2_1+ -> 0+_1) for 16O: 7.5 e^2 fm^4 (compared to experimental 7.6 e^2 fm^4)
- Absolute cross-section measurement accuracy estimated at 15%

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nuclear rainbow scattering in heavy-ion systems, particularly the 16O+12C system where elastic scattering angular distributions had been measured from 62 to 1503 MeV by Brandan (1986), Ogloblin (1998, 2000, 2003), Nicoli (1999, 2000), Szilner (2001), and Trzaska (2002). It follows the recent observation by Ohkubo (2014) of a secondary bow in elastic scattering caused by coupling to the inelastic channel. The study enables future investigation of the relationship between dynamically induced secondary bows in elastic scattering and the structure of the involved nuclei, and may help understand molecular structure with core excitation in heavy-ion systems.
