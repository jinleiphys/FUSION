# Low-energy p-d scattering and He-3 in pionless EFT

> We calculate low-energy proton--deuteron scattering in the framework of pionless effective field theory. In the quartet channel, we calculate the elastic scattering phase shift up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the power counting. In the doublet channel, we perform a next-to-leading order calculation. We obtain good agreement with the available phase shift analyses down to the scattering threshold. The phase shifts in the region of non-perturbative Coulomb interactions are calculated by using an optimised integration mesh. Moreover, the Coulomb contribution to the 3He-3H binding energy difference is evaluated in first order perturbation theory. We comment on the implications of our results for the power counting of subleading three-body forces.

## Key claim

The authors calculate low-energy proton-deuteron scattering phase shifts using pionless effective field theory, extending the power counting for Coulomb contributions to the doublet channel and achieving good agreement with phase shift analyses down to center-of-mass momenta of about 3 MeV, whereas previous work by Rupak and Kong (2001) could not reach below 20 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve momentum-space integral equations for the scattering amplitude in the quartet channel up to next-to-next-to-leading order and in the doublet channel up to next-to-leading order, using dibaryon fields to represent the deuteron and spin-singlet virtual bound state. Coulomb interactions are included non-perturbatively by iterating the leading Coulomb diagrams to all orders and using an optimized integration mesh to handle the long-range Coulomb potential at low momenta. The Coulomb contribution to the 3He-3H binding energy difference is evaluated in first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding momentum: gamma_d ~ 45 MeV (sets the low-energy scale Q)
- Phase shifts calculated down to center-of-mass momenta of order 3 MeV (extending below the previous 20 MeV limit of Rupak and Kong (2001))
- The diagram with two Coulomb photons attached to a nucleon bubble (diagram (c)) is suppressed relative to the leading Coulomb diagram (b) by a factor alpha M_N p / Q^2
- The diagram with one Coulomb photon on the nucleon-exchange line (diagram (d)) is a seven-percent effect at threshold
- The diagram with a Coulomb photon on the singlet dibaryon (diagram (e)) is a 15% contribution at threshold (taken as the theoretical uncertainty for the doublet-channel calculation)

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT treatment of proton-deuteron scattering by Rupak and Kong (2001), who calculated quartet-channel phase shifts to next-to-next-to-leading order but could not reach below 20 MeV center-of-mass momentum and did not consider the doublet channel or the 3He bound state. It also complements the leading-order calculation of the 3He nucleus including non-perturbative Coulomb interactions by Ando and Birse (2010), who obtained the 3He-3H binding energy difference but did not consider scattering observables. The results enable future applications to three-body systems with two charged particles, such as the Hoyle state in 12C as an Efimov state of alpha particles and cluster EFT for halo nuclei.
