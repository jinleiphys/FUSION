# Electrical conductivity and charge diffusion in thermal QCD from the lattice

> We present a lattice QCD calculation of the charge diffusion coefficient, the electrical conductivity and various susceptibilities of conserved charges, for a range of temperatures below and above the deconfinement crossover. The calculations include the contributions from up, down and strange quarks. We find that the diffusion coefficient is of the order of 1/(2πT) and has a dip around the crossover temperature. Our results are obtained with lattice simulations containing 2+1 dynamical flavours on anisotropic lattices. The Maximum Entropy Method is used to construct spectral functions from correlators of the conserved vector current.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the charge diffusion coefficient D, finding it is of order 1/(2πT) with a dip around the crossover temperature, but this result carries significant systematic uncertainty from the Maximum Entropy Method used to reconstruct spectral functions and from the use of heavier-than-physical light quark masses (Mπ = 384(4) MeV).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with Nf = 2+1 dynamical flavours (two degenerate light quarks and one strange quark) using a Symanzik-improved anisotropic gauge action and a mean-field-improved Wilson-clover fermion action on anisotropic lattices with renormalised anisotropy ξ = 3.5. They computed Euclidean correlators of the conserved vector current for temperatures ranging from 0.63Tc to 1.90Tc, then used the Maximum Entropy Method to reconstruct spectral functions and extract the electrical conductivity via the Kubo formula. The charge diffusion coefficient was obtained from the relation D = σ/χQ, where χQ is the charge susceptibility computed separately from second derivatives of the partition function with respect to chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement critical temperature determined from the Polyakov loop susceptibility is Tc = 185(4) MeV
- The pion mass is Mπ = 384(4) MeV, with Mπ/Mρ = 0.446(3)
- The lattice spacing is as = 0.1227(8) fm, with aτ^{-1} = 5.63(4) GeV
- The electrical conductivity around T = 1.5Tc is of order σ = 0.2-0.4 Cem Tc, where Cem = 2e2/3 for three flavours
- The charge susceptibility at T ≳ 250 MeV reaches approximately 85% of the Stefan-Boltzmann (free fermion) value
- The diffusion coefficient is of order D ≈ 1/(2πT) with a dip around the crossover temperature

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice study (Amato 2013) which included only light quark contributions to the vector current, by now including the strange quark contribution and computing the charge susceptibility χQ to enable a self-contained calculation of the charge diffusion coefficient D. The results allow direct comparison with holographic predictions from N = 4 Yang-Mills theory (Policastro 2002, Teaney 2006, Son 2007) where D = 1/(2πT), and with other lattice studies using staggered fermions (Borsanyi 2011, Bazavov 2013) and resummed perturbation theory (Andersen 2012). The calculation enables improved modelling of charge density fluctuations and electromagnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions.
