# Shear viscosity of nuclear matter in the spinodal region

> Based on IBUU simulations calibrated by previous efforts of the transport model evaluation project, we have studied the specific shear viscosity $η/s$ of nuclear matter in the spinodal region using the Green-Kubo method. With the momentum-independent mean-field potential which reproduces reasonably well empirical nuclear matter properties and nuclear phase diagram, we have generated dynamically stable and thermalized nuclear cluster systems in a box with the periodic boundary condition. Extensive results of the $η/s$ at different average densities and temperatures in uniform and non-uniform systems are compared, and we found that the shear viscosity is smaller with nuclear clusters due to the enhanced correlation of the energy-momentum tensor and the stronger collision effect. The temperature dependence of the $η/s$ has a minimum only at low average densities of $ρ<0.3ρ_0$. The present study serves as a rigorous baseline calculation of the $η/s$ in nuclear systems with clusters, and helps to understand the relation between the shear viscosity and the nuclear phase diagram.

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity η/s of nuclear matter is smaller in the presence of nuclear clusters (non-uniform systems) than in uniform systems, due to enhanced correlation of the energy-momentum tensor and stronger collision effects; however, a minimum in the temperature dependence of η/s appears only at low average densities below 0.3ρ₀, not across the entire spinodal region.

## Method

The authors performed IBUU (isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport simulations in a cubic box of length 20 fm with periodic boundary conditions, using 1000 test particles per nucleon and a time step of 0.5 fm/c. They employed a momentum-independent mean-field potential with parameters α = -0.218 GeV, β = 0.164 GeV, and γ = 4/3, a constant isotropic nucleon-nucleon cross section of 40 mb, and a modified Bertsch's prescription for collisions to avoid spurious repeated collisions. Dynamically stable and thermalized cluster systems were generated by starting from uniform matter in the spinodal region, allowing density fluctuations to grow, then resetting the temperature to achieve a more uniform temperature distribution, and finally applying the Green-Kubo formula to compute the shear viscosity from the correlation of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E₀ = -16 MeV at ρ₀, and incompressibility K₀ = 237 MeV for the mean-field potential.
- Critical temperature T_c = 17.6 MeV, above which the mechanical instability region disappears.
- At density ρ = ρ₀ and temperature T = 10 MeV, the theoretical collision rate for a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution is 93.8 c/fm, and for a Fermi-Dirac distribution is 126.8 c/fm; IBUU simulations without Pauli blocking gave a slightly higher rate than the MB limit, while with Pauli blocking the rate matched the FD limit.
- The correlation of density fluctuations showed a cluster radius of about 5 fm at average density ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.3ρ₀ and T = 5 MeV.
- Dynamical equilibrium was reached after t = 1200 fm/c for the example system at ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.3ρ₀ and T = 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shear viscosity in nuclear matter using the Green-Kubo method, building on calibrated transport models from the transport model evaluation project (Zhang 2017, Xu 2019) and mean-field benchmarks from Colonna (2021). It provides a rigorous baseline calculation for η/s in nuclear systems with clusters, complementing earlier work by Chen (2007), Pal (2010), and Xu (2013, 2015) that observed a minimum η/s near the liquid-gas phase transition. The results enable future studies connecting shear viscosity to the nuclear phase diagram and to dynamics in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions.
