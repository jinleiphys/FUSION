# Internal color contributions to flux tube entanglement entropy

> In recent work arXiv:2410.00112, we introduced and computed entanglement entropy of the color flux tube (FTE$^2$) between a heavy quark-antiquark pair in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory. Our numerical results suggest that FTE$^2$ can be partitioned into a component corresponding to transverse vibrations of the flux tube and an internal color entropy. Further, motivated by analytical (1+1)D calculations, and SU(2) (2+1)D Yang-Mills numerical results, we argued that the internal entropy takes the form $\langle F\rangle\log(N_c)$, with $\langle F\rangle$ the number of times, on average, that the flux tube crossed a boundary between region $V$ and its complement. We extend here our FTE$^2$ study to consider different geometries of region $V$, varying the number of boundary crossings, and number of colors. Our preliminary results support the conjectured form of the internal entropy, albeit with noteworthy subtleties relating to the partial/full intersections of the flux tube with the region $V$.

## Key claim
The internal color entanglement entropy of the flux tube takes the form ⟨F⟩ log(N_c), where ⟨F⟩ is the average number of times the flux tube crosses the boundary between region V and its complement, but only when the entire intrinsic width of the flux tube lies in the region, partial boundary crossings do not contribute significantly to ⟨F⟩.

## Method
The authors compute flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE^2) on (2+1)D lattices using the replica method with Polyakov loop correlators for SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theory. They vary the geometry of region V (half-slab, double-slab, staggered slabs with and without gaps) and the number of colors to test the conjectured decomposition of FTE^2 into internal color entropy and vibrational entropy. Simulations use the standard Wilson plaquette action with Kennedy-Pendleton heatbath and over-relaxation updates, employing the multilevel algorithm for Polyakov loop correlators.

## Key numbers
- For SU(3) at fixed QQbar distance L√σ₀ ≈ 0.75, slab width w√σ₀ ≈ 0.25, and y=0, FTE^2 in the x→−∞ limit approaches 2 log(3) ≈ 2.20, consistent with internal color entropy.
- For SU(3), fitting FTE^2 data for x√σ₀ > −0.4 to an error function gives center at x_c√σ₀ = −0.27 and Gaussian half-width W√σ₀ ≈ 0.3.
- For SU(2) in the double-slab geometry at a√σ₀ = 0.112, L=10a, w=w_gap=2a, and y=0, FTE^2 in the x→−∞ limit approaches 4 log(2), the internal entropy prediction for four boundary crossings.
- For SU(2) in the half-slab geometry at a√σ₀ = 0.056, L=12a, w=4a, and y=0, the error function fit center is at x_c√σ₀ = −0.19 with half-width W√σ₀ ≈ 0.3.
- The internal entropy makes up roughly 90-95% of FTE^2 for the QQbar separations studied (up to L√σ₀ ∼ 0.8).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous introduction of flux tube entanglement entropy (Amorosso 2024) from SU(2) to SU(3) Yang-Mills theory and to geometries with multiple boundary crossings. It tests the conjectured decomposition of FTE^2 into internal color entropy and vibrational entropy, where the vibrational component follows the logarithmic behavior ∝ (1/4) log(L) predicted by the Luscher (1980) Gaussian string model. The results suggest that effective string models of confinement should account for the intrinsic width of the QCD string, consistent with prior work by Caselle (2012) and Verzichelli (2025). The method enables future studies of flux tube width and entanglement in more complex geometries and in (3+1)D Yang-Mills theory.
