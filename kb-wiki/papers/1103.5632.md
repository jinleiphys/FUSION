# Hydrokinetic predictions for femtoscopy scales in A+A collisions in the light of recent ALICE LHC results

> A study of energy behavior of the pion spectra and interferometry scales is carried out for the top SPS, RHIC and for LHC energies within the hydrokinetic approach. The main mechanisms that lead to the paradoxical, at first sight, dependence of the interferometry scales with an energy growth, in particular, a decrease $R_{out}/R_{side}$ ratio, are exposed. The hydrokinetic predictions for the HBT radii at LHC energies are compared with the recent results of the ALICE experiment.

## Key claim

The hydrokinetic model (HKM) predictions for HBT radii at LHC energies, made before the ALICE data were available, are quite close to the experimental results for transverse femtoscopy scales, but the longitudinal radius \(R_{long}\) is underestimated by about 20%, and the interferometry volume is smaller than observed, suggesting that the late non-equilibrium stage may need to be treated with hadronic cascade models rather than pure hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors use the hydrokinetic model (HKM), which combines relativistic hydrodynamics with a dynamical decoupling description based on escape probabilities, for central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. Initial conditions are boost-invariant with thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm/c, a Glauber-like transverse energy density profile, and a pre-thermal transverse flow parameterized by a linear rapidity profile. The equation of state features a crossover transition between QGP and hadron gas matched to lattice QCD data, with chemical freeze-out at \(T_{ch}=165\) MeV and subsequent non-equilibrium evolution of 359 hadron species including resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at top SPS: \(\epsilon_0 = 9\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (mean \(\langle\epsilon\rangle_0 = 6.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\))
- Initial energy density at top RHIC: \(\epsilon_0 = 16.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (mean \(\langle\epsilon\rangle_0 = 11.6\) GeV/fm\(^3\))
- For LHC predictions: \(\epsilon_0 = 40\) GeV/fm\(^3\) corresponds to charged multiplicity \(dN_{ch}/d\eta \approx 1500\)
- Best-fit transverse flow parameter \(\alpha = 0.194\) (mean \(\langle v_T \rangle = 0.178\)) for SPS; \(\alpha = 0.28\) (mean \(\langle v_T \rangle = 0.25\)) for RHIC and LHC
- Temperatures at maximal emission for \(p_T = 0.3\) GeV/c: 0.105 GeV (SPS), 0.103 GeV (RHIC), 0.095 GeV (LHC)
- ALICE data at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV correspond to \(dN_{ch}/d\eta \approx 1600\)
- HKM underestimates \(R_{long}\) by about 20% compared to ALICE data

## Context

This work extends the hydrokinetic approach previously applied to SPS and RHIC energies (Sinyukov et al., 2009; Karpenko and Sinyukov, 2010) to make predictions for LHC, addressing the "RHIC HBT puzzle" where \(R_{out}/R_{side} \approx 1\) was unexpectedly small. The results are compared with the first ALICE femtoscopy data at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV (ALICE Collaboration, 2011). The paper identifies that the moderate increase of interferometry volume with energy seen at RHIC is consistent with isentropic chemically frozen evolution (Akkelin and Sinyukov, 2005), but the larger volume at LHC may require treatment of the late non-equilibrium stage with hadronic cascade models like UrQMD. The different linear \(V_{int}(dN/d\eta)\) dependence in A+A versus p+p collisions is attributed to the dependence of interferometry volume on initial system size.
