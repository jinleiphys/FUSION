# Color coherent phenomena on nuclei and the QCD evolution equation

> We review the phenomenon of color coherence in quantum chromodynamics (QCD), its implications for hard and soft processes with nuclei, and its experimental manifestations. The relation of factorization theorems in QCD with color coherence phenomena in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and color coherence phenomena in hard exclusive processes is emphasized. Analyzing numerically the QCD evolution equation for conventional and skewed parton densities in nuclei, we study the onset of generalized color transparency and nuclear shadowing of the sea quark and gluon distributions in nuclei as well as related phenomena. Such novel results as the dependence of the effective coherence length on $Q^2$ and general trends of the QCD evolution are discussed. The limits of the applicability of the QCD evolution equation at small Bjorken $x$ are estimated by comparing the inelastic quark-antiquark- and two gluon-nucleon (nucleus) cross sections, calculated within the DGLAP approximation, with the dynamical boundaries, which follow from the unitarity of the $S$ matrix for purely QCD interactions. We also demonstrate that principles of color coherence play an important role in the processes of soft diffraction off nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that color coherence phenomena in QCD, particularly the interplay between hard and soft physics, lead to a leading-twist nuclear shadowing effect that is significantly larger than predicted by eikonal approximations, and that the effective coherence length controlling these phenomena decreases with increasing \(Q^2\) due to QCD evolution of diffractive parton densities.

## Method

The authors numerically analyze the DGLAP QCD evolution equation for both conventional and skewed parton densities in nuclei, using diffractive parton densities extracted from HERA data as input. They compare the inelastic quark-antiquark and two-gluon-nucleon cross sections calculated within the DGLAP approximation with dynamical boundaries from S-matrix unitarity to estimate limits of applicability of the evolution equation at small Bjorken \(x\). The effective coherence length is computed by determining the median value of \(\beta\) in the integral for the diffractive differential cross section at minimal momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x = 10^{-3}\), nuclear shadowing calculated using the leading-twist formula (Eq. sh1) is larger by a factor of 3 than nuclear shadowing calculated in the eikonal approximation.
- At the initial QCD evolution scale \(Q_0 = 2\) GeV, nuclear shadowing for gluons is larger by approximately a factor of 3 than for quarks in nuclei.
- The effective coherence length \(l_c^{eff}\) at \(x = 10^{-4}\) decreases from approximately \(1/(m_N x)\) at \(Q = 2\) GeV to significantly smaller values at \(Q = 10\) GeV, with the exact reduction depending on the inclusion of small-\(\beta\) contributions.
- The parameter \(\kappa\) describing the \(x_{\Pomeron}\) dependence of diffractive parton densities is approximately 0.15.
- The upper limit of integration in the diffractive cross section is set to 0.02, with variation between 0.01 and 0.03 changing \(l_c^{eff}\) by less than 25%.

## Context

This review extends the Gribov theory of nuclear shadowing (Gribov, 1969) by incorporating QCD factorization theorems and HERA diffractive data, specifically building on the work of Collins (1997) on factorization for hard exclusive diffractive processes and the aligned jet model of Bjorken as improved by Frankfurt and Strikman (1998). The results enable quantitative predictions for future electron-nucleus colliders and provide a framework for understanding the transition from generalized color transparency to color transparency as \(Q^2 \to \infty\) at fixed \(x\).
