# Nonequilibrium fixed points in longitudinally expanding scalar theories: infrared cascade, Bose condensation and a challenge for kinetic theory

> In Phys. Rev. Lett. 114 (2015) 6, 061601, we reported on a new universality class for longitudinally expanding systems, encompassing strongly correlated non-Abelian plasmas and $N$-component self-interacting scalar field theories. Using classical-statistical methods, we showed that these systems share the same self-similar scaling properties for a wide range of momenta in a limit where particles are weakly coupled but their occupancy is high. Here we significantly expand on our previous work and delineate two further self-similar regimes. One of these occurs in the deep infrared (IR) regime of very high occupancies, where the nonequilibrium dynamics leads to the formation of a Bose-Einstein Condensate. The universal IR scaling exponents and the spectral index characterizing the isotropic IR distributions are described by an effective theory derived from a systematic large-$N$ expansion at next-to-leading order. Remarkably, this effective theory can be cast as a vertex-resummed kinetic theory. The other novel self-similar regime occurs close to the hard physical scale of the theory, and sets in only at later times. We argue that the important role of the infrared dynamics ensures that key features of our results for scalar and gauge theories cannot be reproduced consistently in conventional kinetic theory frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper claims that longitudinally expanding scalar field theories exhibit three distinct self-similar scaling regimes at a nonthermal fixed point, including a deep infrared regime where a Bose-Einstein condensate forms, and that the infrared dynamics in these systems cannot be consistently reproduced by conventional kinetic theory frameworks, only by a vertex-resummed kinetic description derived from a large-\(N\) expansion at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of an \(O(N)\)-symmetric massless scalar field theory with quartic self-interaction in Bjorken coordinates, solving the nonlinear field equations of motion using leapfrog integration on anisotropic lattices. They employed two sets of Gaussian initial conditions: overoccupation initial conditions with a step-function distribution in momentum space and coherent field initial conditions with a large field expectation value. The single particle distribution function was extracted from the equal-time statistical correlation function and its temporal derivatives, and scaling exponents were determined by analyzing the time evolution of the distribution across different momentum regimes.

## Key numbers

- The infrared regime (i) exhibits an approximate \(p_T^{-5}\) power law dependence in the transverse momentum spectrum at vanishing longitudinal momentum.
- The intermediate momentum regime (ii) features an approximate \(p_T^{-1}\) power law dependence.
- The initial occupancy parameter \(n_0 = 35\) was used for the primary overoccupation initial conditions, with initial anisotropy \(\xi_0 = 1\) and initial proper time \(Q\tau_0 = 10^3\).
- For coherent field initial conditions, the coupling constant was chosen as \(\lambda = 10^{-4}\) and the characteristic momentum scale \(Q \equiv \sigma_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous letter (Berges et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 114, 061601, 2015) which reported a new universality class for longitudinally expanding systems encompassing non-Abelian plasmas and scalar field theories. It contradicts the predictions of conventional kinetic theory frameworks such as the BMSS approach (Baier et al., 2000) for the longitudinal-to-transverse pressure ratio, showing significantly slower decay due to infrared contributions. The results enable a comprehensive strategy for weak coupling thermalization where classical-statistical field theory at early times is matched to an appropriate kinetic theory at later times, as summarized in work by York et al. (2014) and Kurkela et al. (2015).
