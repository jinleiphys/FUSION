# Hadron-quark hybrid model, modular transformation and Roberge-Weiss transition

> In the framework of modular transformations, we reformulate the recently proposed hadron-quark hybrid model when the imaginary baryonic chemical potential is introduced. As a result, we can consider the torus, which is characterized by the complex number densities of baryons (antibaryons) and quarks (antiquarks). We apply this model to analyze the Roberge-Weiss transition. It is shown that the torus vanishes at the baryonic chemical potential where the Roberge-Weiss transition appears because the number density of baryons (antibaryons) is not linearly independent of the number density of quarks (antiquarks). When the temperature T is lower than the Roberge-Weiss transition temperature TRW, the torus shrinks smoothly to the one-dimensional object at the Roberge-Weiss transition point, but the discontinuity does not appear. On the other hand, the discontinuity of the geometrical object appears when T>TRW. We also calculate the modulus of the torus and transform it into the fundamental region. The transformed moduli are symmetric below TRW, but the symmetry is broken above TRW.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates a previously proposed hadron-quark hybrid model using modular transformations at imaginary baryonic chemical potential, showing that the torus constructed from baryon and quark number densities vanishes at the Roberge-Weiss transition point because the two densities become linearly dependent; the discontinuity of the geometric object appears only above the Roberge-Weiss transition temperature \(T_{\rm RW}\), while below \(T_{\rm RW}\) the torus shrinks smoothly without discontinuity.

## Method

The authors take the baryon number density \(n_{\rm b}\) from the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and one-third of the quark number density \(n_{\rm q}\) from the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, both in the Boltzmann approximation. They define the hybrid-model baryon density as \(\tilde{n}_{\rm b} = n_{\rm b} / (1 + n_{\rm b}/n_{\rm q})\), which yields a modular transformation \(\tau_{\rm h} = \tau/(\tau+1)\) with \(\tau = n_{\rm b}/n_{\rm q}\). Using the complex number densities as basis vectors, they construct a torus whose modulus \(\tau\) is analyzed as a function of the imaginary chemical potential angle \(\theta\), and then transformed into the fundamental region of the modular group.

## Key numbers

* The Roberge-Weiss transition temperature \(T_{\rm RW}\) for 2+1 flavor QCD is estimated as about 200 MeV by lattice QCD simulations.
* At \(\theta = \pi\) (the Roberge-Weiss transition point), the phase difference \(\Delta\phi_{\mathrm b-q}\) between baryon and quark number densities is discontinuous above \(T_{\rm RW}\), taking the value \(\frac{2}{3}\theta\) for \(0 \leq \theta < \pi\) and \(\frac{2}{3}\theta + \frac{2}{3}\pi\) for \(\pi < \theta \leq 2\pi\).
* Below \(T_{\rm RW}\), the phase difference is \(\Delta\phi_{\mathrm b-q} = \theta\), and at \(\theta = \pi\) the torus shrinks to a one-dimensional object.
* The nucleon volume in vacuum is given as \(v_0 = \frac{4\pi}{3} r_0^3\) with \(r_0 \sim 0.8\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the hadron-quark hybrid model introduced by Kouno (2023) by reformulating it in the language of modular transformations, connecting it to the Roberge-Weiss transition originally described by Roberge and Weiss (1986). The approach complements lattice QCD studies of the Roberge-Weiss transition (e.g., Bonati 2016, Cuteri 2022) and effective model analyses using the PNJL model (e.g., Sakai 2008, Kouno 2009). The modular transformation framework enables a topological characterization of the hadron-quark system and may provide a new geometric perspective for analyzing the phase structure of QCD at imaginary chemical potential.
