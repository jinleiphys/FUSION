# Microscopic Models for Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this paper, the concepts of microscopic transport theory are introduced and the features and shortcomings of the most commonly used ansatzes are discussed. In particular, the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model is described in great detail. Based on the same principles as QMD and RQMD, it incorporates a vastly extended collision term with full baryon-antibaryon symmetry, 55 baryon and 32 meson species. Isospin is explicitly treated for all hadrons. The range of applicability stretches from $E_{lab}< 100$ MeV/nucleon up to $E_{lab}> 200$ GeV/nucleon, allowing for a consistent calculation of excitation functions from the intermediate energy domain up to ultrarelativistic energies. The main physics topics under discussion are stopping, particle production and collective flow.

# Microscopic Models for Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model, based on the same principles as QMD and RQMD, can consistently calculate excitation functions from intermediate energy domain up to ultrarelativistic energies, but its usefulness extends beyond being a mere event-generator by allowing analysis of underlying physics input and how various components contribute to final results. The model is described as "no static structure" and serves primarily as a framework into which new transport theoretical concepts can be incorporated.

## Method

The authors introduce microscopic transport theory concepts and discuss features and shortcomings of commonly used ansatzes, first for the non-relativistic case then extending to relativistic. The UrQMD model incorporates a vastly extended collision term with full baryon-antibaryon symmetry, 55 baryon and 32 meson species, with isospin explicitly treated for all hadrons. Applications are reviewed for stopping, particle production, and collective flow across energies from SIS through AGS to CERN/SPS, comparing UrQMD calculations to experimental data from NA49, TAPS, and other collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Range of applicability: from E_lab < 100 MeV/nucleon up to E_lab > 200 GeV/nucleon
- UrQMD includes 55 baryon and 32 meson species
- Fraction of excited matter in collision system rises continuously from 20% at SIS up to 70% at CERN SPS energies
- UrQMD exhibits properties of a free hadron gas with a limiting temperature of approximately 140 MeV
- Energy density estimates for CERN/SPS region: 1 to 10 GeV/fm^3
- Dimuon excess observed in intermediate mass range of 1.5 ≤ m ≤ 2.5 GeV
- For Pb+Pb at 6.5 TeV, event-averaged net-baryon dN/dy is less than 5 at mid-rapidity
- In Ni+Ni at 2 GeV/u, above sqrt(s) = 4*m_N only 1/3 of BB-reactions are NN, NΔ1232, or Δ1232Δ1232 collisions; 50% involve resonances higher than N*1440
- Transverse flow velocity of β_T = 0.34c extracted from isotropic model for Pb+Pb at SPS
- Thermal fit temperature T_0 = 160 MeV without flow, or T_0 = 170 MeV with additional flow
- IQMD overpredicts π^0 yield for Au+Au by approximately 20% and for Ca+Ca by almost a factor of 2 at 1 GeV/nucleon
- UrQMD π^0 yield for Ca+Ca deviates less than 20% from data
- Δ resonance enrichment in nuclear matter is only about 15% for medium systems at SIS energies

## Context

This review extends the line of microscopic transport models including QMD (Quantum Molecular Dynamics) and RQMD (Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics), and discusses comparisons with other approaches such as VENUS (including multi-quark clusters), the LEXUS geometrical model of Jeon (1997), the rows-on-rows model of Hüfner and Knoll (1977), and the fireball model of Gosset (1977) and Hagedorn (1980). The work enables future investigations of color coherence phenomena, partonic degrees of freedom at RHIC and LHC energies, medium-dependent cross sections, and improved treatment of resonance lifetimes and time-delays. Open questions include the implementation of relativistic n-body forces, off-shell propagation, and the role of fluctuations in stopping and charmonium production.
