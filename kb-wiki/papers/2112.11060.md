# A study of the elastic $πN$ scattering at finite baryon density

> We investigate the elastic $πN$ scattering for the $I=3/2$ channel dominated by the $Δ(1232)$ resonance at finite baryon density $ρ_B$ for the symmetric nuclear matter, employing the effective Lagrangian approach at the tree-level Born approximation. The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and linear-density approximation are employed to describe the medium modifications for the in-medium baryon properties, such as the nucleon and $Δ$ masses, and $Δ$ full decay width. We first reproduce the experimental data in vacuum by fitting the model parameters, then analyze various physical observables including total and differential cross sections, proton-spin asymmetry in medium. It turns out that the $Δ$-resonance contribution becomes diminished with respect to $ρ_B$, due to the weaker $πN$-interaction strength in medium. Beyond the $Δ$-resonance region $\sqrt{s}\gtrsim1.5$ GeV, the cross sections are almost independent of the density. The present results will be useful to understand the role of the $Δ$ resonance in the neutron-star equation of state (EoS) as well as in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance contribution to elastic $\pi N$ scattering in the $I=3/2$ channel becomes diminished and broadened with increasing baryon density $\rho_B$ due to weaker $\pi N$-interaction strength in medium, but this density dependence is only significant in the $\Delta$-resonance region; beyond $\sqrt{s}\gtrsim 1.5$ GeV the cross sections become almost independent of density.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach at tree-level Born approximation to compute elastic $\pi^+p$ scattering amplitudes for the $I=3/2$ channel, including the $\Delta^{++}$ $s$-channel pole, neutron and $\Delta^0$ $u$-channel diagrams, and a Weinberg-Tomozawa contact interaction. Medium modifications for nucleon and $\Delta$ masses were computed using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for symmetric nuclear matter, while the in-medium $\Delta$ full decay width was obtained via linear-density approximation with a spreading width term. Phenomenological form factors with cut-off masses were used, and model parameters were fitted to reproduce experimental vacuum data.

## Key numbers

- In-medium nucleon and $\Delta$ masses decrease by about $15\sim20\%$ at $\rho_B\approx\rho_0$ (nuclear normal density, $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$) compared to their vacuum values
- Vacuum $\Delta$ mass used: $M_\Delta = 1215.5$ MeV; vacuum Breit-Wigner width: $\Gamma^0_\Delta = 94.0$ MeV
- Coupling constants: $f_{\pi NN} = 0.989$ (from Nijmegen potential), $f_{\pi N\Delta} = 2.127$ (from experiment)
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 93.2$ MeV
- Form factor cut-off mass: $\Lambda = 800$ MeV for all channels except Weinberg-Tomozawa channel with $\Lambda = 450$ MeV
- Spreading width: $\Gamma_\mathrm{sp} = 80$ MeV; cut-off parameter $\beta_0 = 200$ MeV
- Binding energy of $15.7$ MeV at saturation density used to fit QMC coupling constants: $g_\sigma^2/4\pi = 5.393$, $g_\omega^2/4\pi = 5.304$, $B^{1/4} = 170$ MeV, $M_N^* = 754.6$ MeV, $K = 279.3$ MeV, $z_N = 3.295$

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $\Delta$-resonance effects in neutron-star equations of state and relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance becomes important at finite baryon density. The authors build on earlier in-medium studies using the Dyson-Schwinger method (Ghosh, 2016) and one-boson-exchange model (Cui, 2020), as well as the quark-meson coupling model framework developed by Hutauruk (2018, 2019) and Guichon (2018). The results enable improved modeling of $\Delta$-rich matter in neutron-star cores and provide input for understanding pion-nucleon interactions in heavy-ion collision environments.
