# Timelike gamma* N -> Delta form factors and Delta Dalitz decay

> We extend a covariant model, tested before in the spacelike region for the physical and lattice QCD regimes, to a calculation of the gamma* N -> Delta reaction in the timelike region, where the square of the transfered momentum, q^2, is positive (q^2>0). We estimate the Dalitz decay Delta -> Ne+e- and the Delta distribution mass distribution function. The results presented here can be used to simulate the NN -> NNe+e- reactions at moderate beam kinetic energies.

## Key claim

The authors extend a covariant spectator quark model, previously validated for the spacelike region (including physical and lattice QCD data), to the timelike region for the gamma* N -> Delta reaction, but note that the pion cloud contribution requires a choice between two different extrapolations (a naive dipole model and a chiral perturbation theory motivated model) which yield significantly different behaviors in the timelike regime.

## Method

The covariant spectator quark model with a quark-diquark structure was used, taking only the dominant S-state contribution (magnetic dipole form factor G_M*) and setting the electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors to zero. The valence quark core contribution was extended to the timelike region by adding a finite width to the rho-meson pole in the vector meson dominance parametrization of the quark current, using Gamma_rho(q^2) = Gamma_rho^0 * ((q^2 - 4 m_pi^2)/(m_rho^2 - 4 m_pi^2))^(3/2) * (m_rho/q) * theta(q^2 - 4 m_pi^2). Two models for the pion cloud contribution were compared: model 1 used a naive dipole form factor with an added width, while model 2 used a function F_rho derived from chiral perturbation theory with a logarithmic term.

## Key numbers

* The experimental rho meson width is Gamma_rho^0 = 0.149 GeV (PDG value), used in the parametrization of the rho propagator width.
* The pion cloud parameters are lambda_pi = 0.441 and Lambda_pi^2 = 1.53 GeV^2, taken from prior work (Ramalho et al., 2010).
* The dipole cutoff is Lambda_D^2 = 0.71 GeV^2, giving a pole at q^2 = 0.71 GeV^2 for the dipole form factor G_D.
* The rho meson mass is m_rho (approximately m_omega), with the rho pole at q^2 = m_rho^2 approximately 0.6 GeV^2.
* The two-pion creation threshold is at q^2 = 4 m_pi^2 approximately 0.076 GeV^2, below which the rho width vanishes.
* For the Delta(1232) at the physical mass W = M_Delta, the allowed timelike region extends to Q^2 = -(M_Delta - M)^2 approximately -0.086 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model of Ramalho et al. (2008, 2010) for the gamma* N -> Delta reaction, which was previously constrained by physical data and lattice QCD data from Alexandrou et al. (2008), into the timelike region needed for describing NN -> NN e+e- reactions. The results are intended for use in simulation packages like those described by Frohlich et al. (2010) for the HADES experiments at 1-2 GeV and future FAIR experiments, where the di-lepton channel from Delta Dalitz decay is a key signal. The authors compare their approach with earlier models including constant form factors (Frohlich et al., 2010; Zetenyi et al., 2003), a two-component quark model (Frohlich et al., 2010; Wan et al., 2005, 2006; Iachello et al., 2004; Bijker et al., 2004), and a vector meson dominance model (Krivoruchenko et al., 2002).
