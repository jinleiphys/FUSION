# The structure of pentaquarks $P_c^+$ in the chiral quark model

> The recent experimental results of LHCb collaboration suggested the existence of pentaquark states with charmonium. To understand the structure of the states, a dynamical calculation of 5-quark systems with quantum numbers $IJ^P=\frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2})^\pm$, $\frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2})^\pm$ and $\frac{1}{2}(\frac{5}{2})^\pm$ is performed in the framework of chiral quark model with the help of gaussian expansion method. The results show that the negative parity states can be bound states while all of the positive parity states are the scattering states. The $P_c(4380)$ state is suggested to be the bound state of $Σ^*_cD$. Although the energy of $Σ_cD^*$ is very close to the mass of $P_c(4450)$, the inconsistent parity prevents the assignment. The calculated distances between quarks confirm the molecular nature of the states. Other five-quark bound states of the combination of $Σ_cD$ and $Σ^*_cD^*$ are also found in the region about 4.3GeV and 4.5GeV.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model calculation suggests that the LHCb pentaquark candidate \(P_c(4380)\) can be interpreted as a molecular bound state of \(\Sigma^*_c D\) with quantum numbers \(IJ^P = \frac{1}{2}\frac{3}{2}^-\), but the large experimental decay width (205 MeV) cannot be explained within this picture; the other candidate \(P_c(4450)\) has an energy close to the \(\Sigma_c D^*\) molecule but the opposite parity prevents that assignment, and all positive-parity states are found to be unbound.

## Method

A dynamical variational calculation of the five-quark (\(uudc\bar{c}\)) system was performed using the chiral quark model Hamiltonian, which includes confinement, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone-boson exchange interactions. The Gaussian expansion method (GEM) was employed to solve the few-body Schrödinger equation without assuming a prior spatial structure, using Jacobi coordinates and expanding orbital wavefunctions in shifted Gaussians. Single-channel and channel-coupling calculations were carried out for quantum numbers \(IJ^P = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2})^\pm\), \(\frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2})^\pm\), and \(\frac{1}{2}(\frac{5}{2})^\pm\), with total orbital angular momentum limited to \(L=0\) and \(L=1\).

## Key numbers

- The \(\Sigma_c D\) channel with \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\) yields a bound state with binding energy \(-4\) MeV and corrected energy 4316 MeV (experimental threshold 4320 MeV).
- The \(\Sigma^*_c D\) channel with \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-\) yields a bound state with binding energy \(-3\) MeV and corrected energy 4382 MeV, close to the LHCb \(P_c(4380)\) mass of 4380\(\pm\)8\(\pm\)29 MeV.
- The \(\Sigma_c D^*\) channel with \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\) yields a bound state with binding energy \(-3\) MeV and corrected energy 4459 MeV, near the LHCb \(P_c(4450)\) mass of 4449.8\(\pm\)1.7\(\pm\)2.5 MeV, but with opposite parity.
- The \(\Sigma^*_c D^*\) channel with \(J^P = \frac{5}{2}^-\) yields a bound state with binding energy \(-3\) MeV and corrected energy 4524 MeV.
- Calculated inter-quark distances: distances among quarks 1,2,3 are about 0.8 fm; distances between quark 4 and antiquark 5 are 0.6–0.7 fm; distances between the two clusters are 1.5–2.4 fm, confirming a molecular structure.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical quark-model study of hidden-charm pentaquarks following the LHCb observation of \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) (LHCb, 2015). It contrasts with prior approaches that assumed a fixed spatial structure, such as the one-boson-exchange model of Chen et al. (2015) and the Bethe-Salpeter equation study of He (2015), which also proposed molecular interpretations. The calculation enables future inclusion of spin-orbit and tensor interactions to address the parity puzzle of \(P_c(4450)\) and to better understand the large decay width of \(P_c(4380)\).
