# Isovector channel of quark-meson-coupling model and its effect on symmetry energy

> The non-relativistic approximation of the quark-meson-coupling model has been discussed and compared with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model which includes spin exchanges. Calculations show that the spin-exchange interaction has important effect on the descriptions of finite nuclei and nuclear matter through the Fock exchange. Also in the quark-meson-coupling model, it is the Fock exchange that leads to a nonlinear density-dependent isovector channel and changes the density-dependent behavior of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The Fock exchange interaction in the quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model produces a nonlinear density-dependent isovector channel that softens the symmetry energy at high densities, an effect that cannot be reproduced by simply readjusting parameters in a Hartree-only calculation.

## Method

The authors derived a set of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) parameters, including the spin-exchange parameters \(x_1\), \(x_2\), and \(x_3\), by comparing the energy density functionals of the non-relativistic approximation of the QMC model and the SHF model. They then performed Hartree-Fock calculations for nuclear matter and finite nuclei (using the ev8 SHF-BCS code) with these parameters, and compared the results to those from the QMC model and other Skyrme parameter sets (SkM*, SLy4).

## Key numbers

* The symmetry energy of nuclear matter at saturation density is about 30 MeV, which leads to an \(\alpha\) value of about \(1/6\) in the derived Skyrme force.
* The derived Skyrme parameters (labeled Sqmc) include: \(t_0 = -2648.19\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(t_1 = 371.07\) MeV fm\(^5\), \(t_2 = -121.644\) MeV fm\(^5\), \(t_3 = 15553.495\) MeV fm\(^4\), \(x_0 = 0.60146\), \(x_1 = 0.2697\), \(x_2 = -0.23701\), \(x_3 = 0.6968\), and \(W_0 = 104.498\) MeV fm\(^5\).
* The scalar polarizability parameter \(d\) is taken as 0.150 fm, corresponding to a bag radius of 0.8 fm.
* The effective masses of protons and neutrons are calculated at a saturation density of \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) for various isospin values.

## Context

This work extends the earlier comparison between the QMC model and the SHF model by Guichon et al. (2006), which neglected the spin-exchange parameters \(x_1\), \(x_2\), and \(x_3\). The inclusion of these terms shows that the Fock exchange in the QMC model produces a soft symmetry energy, consistent with recent experimental analyses, in contrast to the stiff linear symmetry energy obtained in Hartree-only approximations such as the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. The results enable a deeper understanding of the microscopic origin of the density dependence of the symmetry energy and its connection to subnucleonic degrees of freedom.
