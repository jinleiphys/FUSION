# Minijets in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> Recent results on minijet production in nuclear collisions at the RHIC and LHC energies are reviewed. Initial conditions of the QGP at $τ=0.1$ fm$/c$, especially parton chemistry, thermalization and net baryon number-to-entropy ratio are discussed. Also, contribution of minijets from a hard BFKL-pomeron ladder will be estimated.

## Key claim

Minijet production from semihard QCD processes will dominate the initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma formation at LHC energies, while at RHIC energies both soft and semihard components must be considered; the BFKL-ladder contribution to minijet transverse energy is at most a few tens of percent correction to the collinear-factorization results, under the assumption that the BFKL evolution is responsible for all the small-x rise observed at HERA.

## Method

The author reviews calculations of semihard parton production in nuclear collisions using lowest-order perturbative QCD with collinear factorization, where the integrated jet cross section is computed from parton distributions and the nuclear overlap function. For the BFKL approach, the author uses unintegrated gluon densities and the homogeneous BFKL equation to estimate the inclusive minijet cross section from hard pomeron ladders, then computes the transverse energy deposited in the central rapidity unit for Pb-Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (Pb-Pb at √s = 5.5 ATeV): average number of semihard partons with pT ≥ 2 GeV and |y| ≤ 0.5 at τ = 0.1 fm/c is 3252 (MRSH) or 5978 (MRSD-') total, with gluons dominating at 2710 and 5220 respectively
- At LHC: average transverse energy carried by these partons is 10310 GeV (MRSH) or 17580 GeV (MRSD-') total, with gluons contributing 8640 GeV and 15330 GeV respectively
- At RHIC (Au+Au at √s = 200 AGeV): average number of semihard partons is 200 (MRSH) or 199 (MRSD-') total, with gluons at 156 and 157 respectively
- At RHIC: average transverse energy is 547 GeV (MRSH) or 539 GeV (MRSD-') total, with gluons at 426 GeV and 422 GeV respectively
- Energy per gluon in the perturbatively produced system at LHC: approximately 3 GeV
- Equilibrium temperature of an ideal gluon gas at LHC from the energy density: 0.988 GeV (MRSH) or 1.14 GeV (MRSD-')
- Net baryon number density at τ = 0.1 fm/c: 0.25 fm⁻³ (LHC, MRSH) and 0.22 fm⁻³ (RHIC, MRSH)
- Initial net baryon-to-entropy ratio: ~2×10⁻⁴ for LHC, ~2×10⁻³ for RHIC
- BFKL-minijet transverse energy for central Pb-Pb at LHC: 3060 GeV (fixed αs = 0.2) or 4940 GeV (running αs)

## Context

This review extends earlier work on minijet production in nuclear collisions by Eskola, Kajantie, and collaborators (EKR, EMW, EK96) and the BFKL-ladder study by Eskola, Leonidov, and Ruuskanen (ELR96). It contrasts the collinear-factorization approach used in event generators like HIJING with the BFKL approach of Mueller and Navelet (1987) and the unintegrated gluon density method of Andersson, Kharzeev, Mueller, and Sjostrand (AKMS). The results enable estimates of initial conditions for QGP formation at future colliders, particularly the parton chemistry, thermalization timescale, and net baryon-to-entropy ratio, which are crucial for predicting signals such as thermal dileptons.
