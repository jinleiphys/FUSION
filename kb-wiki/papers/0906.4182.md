# Two neutron correlations in exotic nuclei

> We study the correlations between two neutrons in borromian nuclei $^{11}$Li and $^6$He by using a three-body model with a density-dependent contact two-body interaction. It is shown that the two neutrons show a compact bound feature at the nuclear surface due to the mixing of single particle states of different parity. We study the Coulomb breakup cross sections of $^{11}$Li and $^6$He using the same three-body model. We show that the concentration of the B(E1) strength near the threshold can be well reproduced with this model as a typical nature of the halo nuclei. The energy distributions of two emitted neutrons from dipole excitations are also studied using the correlated wave functions of dipole excitations.

# Two neutron correlations in exotic nuclei

## Key claim

The two valence neutrons in the borromian nuclei ¹¹Li and ⁶He exhibit a compact bound (di-neutron) feature at the nuclear surface due to mixing of single-particle states of different parity, and this same three-body model reproduces the concentration of B(E1) strength near the continuum threshold as a typical nature of halo nuclei.

## Method

The authors employed a three-body model (core + two neutrons) with a density-dependent contact two-body interaction to study ground-state correlations and Coulomb breakup. They computed the root-mean-square distance between valence neutrons as a function of the core–two-neutron distance, two-particle densities, Coulomb breakup cross sections for ⁶He+Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon and ¹¹Li+Pb at 70 MeV/nucleon, and the dipole strength distribution d²B(E1)/de₁de₂ as a function of the energies of the two emitted neutrons.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶He, the mean square core–two-neutron distance ⟨r²_{c-2n}⟩ decreases from 13.2 fm² to 9.46 fm² when the off-diagonal recoil term is neglected in the ground state.
- For ¹¹Li, ⟨r²_{c-2n}⟩ decreases from 26.3 fm² to 20.58 fm² when the off-diagonal recoil term is neglected in the ground state.
- For ¹¹Li, a large concentration of dipole strength appears at about e₁ = 0.375 MeV and e₂ = 0.075 MeV (and symmetrically at e₁ = 0.075 MeV and e₂ = 0.375 MeV).
- For ⁶He, the dipole strength is largely concentrated at one peak around e₁ = e₂ = 0.7 MeV.
- The Coulomb breakup cross section results are smeared with an energy-dependent width of Γ = 0.15·√E_rel MeV for ⁶He and Γ = 0.25·√E_rel MeV for ¹¹Li.

## Context

This work extends the three-body model studies of Borromean nuclei by Hagino and Sagawa (2005) and the earlier analysis of two-neutron correlations in ¹¹Li by Hagino, Sagawa, et al. (2007). It directly addresses the experimental Coulomb breakup measurements for ¹¹Li by Nakamura et al. (2006) and for ⁶He by Aumann et al. (1999), and the more recent measurements of two-neutron correlations in ¹¹Li Coulomb breakup by Nakamura et al. (2009). The results enable future studies of how the neutron–core potential, particularly the presence or absence of a virtual s-state in the residual nucleus (present in ¹⁰Li but absent in ⁵He), governs the energy distributions of emitted neutrons in halo nuclei.
