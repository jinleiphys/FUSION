# Event-by-event fluctuations of magnetic and electric fields in heavy ion collisions

> We show that fluctuating proton positions in the colliding nuclei generate, on the event-by-event basis, very strong magnetic and electric fields in the direction both parallel and perpendicular to the reaction plane. The magnitude of E and B fields in each event is of the order of m_pi^2 \approx 10^18 Gauss. Implications on the observation of electric dipole in heavy ion collisions is discussed, and the possibility of measuring the electric conductivity of the hot medium is pointed out.

# Event-by-event fluctuations of magnetic and electric fields in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Fluctuating proton positions in the colliding nuclei generate, on an event-by-event basis, magnetic and electric fields in the direction both parallel and perpendicular to the reaction plane that are comparable in magnitude, contrary to the earlier assumption that only the out-of-plane (y) component of the magnetic field is significant. The x component of the magnetic field and both transverse components of the electric field vanish only after averaging over many events, but in each individual event they reach values of order m_pi^2.

## Method
The authors calculate the electric and magnetic fields at t=0 and at the spatial origin (x=0) using the Lienard-Wiechert potentials for point charges, summing over all protons in both nuclei. Proton positions are sampled from a Woods-Saxon distribution with standard parameters for AuAu collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, with all protons moving at the same velocity along the beam axis. A cutoff radius of 0.3 fm is imposed to avoid singularities when a proton approaches the observation point, and the fields are averaged over events by first taking the absolute value in each event.

## Key numbers
- The magnitude of both E and B fields in each event is of order m_pi^2, approximately 10^18 Gauss.
- For AuAu collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV at impact parameter b=8 fm, the ratio R = <|B_y,Omega|> / <|B_x,Omega|> at the point x=0 is 1.6, and varies from 3.2 to 5.6 when integrated over a transverse domain of radius r_D from 1 fm to 2 fm.
- For b=4 fm at x=0, the ratio R = <|B_y,Omega|> / <|B_x,Omega|> is 1.14, and varies from 1.6 to 2.5 when integrated over a domain of radius r_D from 1 fm to 2 fm.
- The cutoff radius r_cut = 0.3 fm is used as an effective distance between partons in a nucleon, and varying it from 0.3 fm to 0.6 fm shows weak cutoff dependence.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Kharzeev (2007, 2009, 2010) and Skokov (2009) that considered only the event-averaged magnetic field perpendicular to the reaction plane, finding values up to dozens of m_pi^2. The authors show that the fluctuating proton positions make the x component of B and both transverse components of E comparable to the y component of B, which has implications for the chiral magnetic effect and for proposed observables such as the charge-dependent two-particle correlations measured by the STAR collaboration (2009). The results enable the possibility of measuring the electric conductivity of the hot medium created in heavy ion collisions by analyzing charge separation in central collisions, as the electric current from the fluctuating electric field may dominate over the chiral magnetic current.
