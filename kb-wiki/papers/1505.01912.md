# The radiative capture reaction rate from $ΛΛ$ to H dibaryon in the imaginary time method

> Radiative capture rates of thermal $ΛΛ+ Ξ$N states into H dibaryon are calculated in the novel imaginary time method. The H dibaryon is assumed to be a bound state of $Ξ$N with spin $J^π= 0^+$, isospin $I=0$ and strangeness $-2$. We consider $E1$ transition to H from $Ξ$N $(L=1)$ scattering states which mix with $ΛΛ(L=1)$. In order to calculate the transition rates, we formulate a coupled-channel imaginary time method by extending the one-channel formula originally proposed by Yabana and Funaki. The imaginary time method allows us to avoid the sum over all the excited thermal initial states, and thus to save computational time significantly. The transition rates are given as a function of temperature and the unknown binding energy of the H dibaryon, which we take as a parameter. It is found that the transition rate is not sensitive to the choices of the H binding energy or the strengths of the channel coupling for temperatures 3 MeV or higher.

## Key claim

The radiative capture rate of thermal ΛΛ + ΞN states into the H dibaryon is calculated using a novel coupled-channel imaginary time method, and the rate is found to be insensitive to the choices of the H dibaryon binding energy or the strengths of the channel coupling for temperatures of 3 MeV or higher, though the H dibaryon binding energy is treated as a free parameter.

## Method

The authors extend the one-channel imaginary time method originally proposed by Yabana and Funaki to a coupled-channel formulation, considering three initial channels: ΛΛ(³P₁), ΞN(¹P₁), and ΞN(³P₁). The Hamiltonian for the initial states uses G-matrix-type YN and YY interactions constructed from the Nijmegen potentials (NSC-97f for the antisymmetric spin-orbit term and ESC08c for other terms), and the final H dibaryon state is modeled as a ¹S₀ ΞN bound state using a simple Gaussian potential. The E1 transition rates are computed by solving a Schrödinger-type differential equation along the imaginary-time axis (identified with inverse temperature) using a Taylor expansion method, avoiding explicit summation over all thermal initial scattering states.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic coupling g for the sub-leading transition operator is -0.1572 μN (nuclear magneton).
- The adopted H dibaryon potential parameters are rH = 1.0 fm and V0 = -219.44 MeV, giving a binding energy of Ef = 3.0 MeV from the ΛΛ threshold.
- The imaginary-time step used in the Taylor expansion is Δβ = 1×10⁻⁶ MeV⁻¹ with kmax = 4.
- The radial grid size is Δr = 0.01 fm, and the reaction rate is well converged up to β = 10 MeV⁻¹ when using a box size Rmax ≥ 60 fm.
- The transition rate is insensitive to the H binding energy and channel coupling strengths for temperatures of 3 MeV or higher.

## Context

This work extends the imaginary time method, previously applied to the triple-alpha radiative capture process by Yabana and Funaki (2014), to a coupled-channel calculation for the H dibaryon production. The H dibaryon was originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) as a six-quark state, and lattice QCD calculations have suggested a bound or resonance state near the ΛΛ threshold. The calculated reaction rates are intended for application to heavy ion collision environments where strange baryons are produced at finite temperature, enabling experimental searches for the H dibaryon via characteristic photon emission.
