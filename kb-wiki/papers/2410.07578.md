# Investigation of Nuclear Structure and $β$-decay Properties of As Isotopes

> The nuclear ground state properties of 67 80As nuclei have been investigated within the framework of relativistic mean field (RMF) approach. The RMF model with density dependent (DDME2) interaction is utilized for the calculation of potential energy curves and the nuclear ground state deformation parameters $β_2$ of selected As isotopes. Later, the $β$ decay properties of As isotopes were studied using the proton neutron quasi particle random phase approximation pnQRPA model. These include Gamow Tellar (GT) strength distributions, log ft values, $β$ decay half lives, stellar $β$ plus minus decays and stellar electron positron capture rates. The $β_2$ values computed from RMF model were employed in the on QRPA model as an input parameter for the calculations of $β$-decay properties for 67 80As. The calculated log ft values were in decent agreement with the measured data. The predicted $β$-decay half lives matched the experimental values within a factor of 10. The stellar rates were compared with the shell model results. Only at high temperature and density values, the sum of $β$ plus and electron capture rates had a finite contribution. On the other hand, the sum of $β$ negative and positron capture rates were sizeable only at low density and high temperature values. For all such cases, the pn QRPA rates were found to be bigger than the shell model rates up to a factor of 33 or more. The findings reported in the current investigation could prove valuable for simulating the late stage stellar evolution of massive stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pn-QRPA model, using deformation parameters from the RMF model with DD-ME2 interaction, can reproduce measured beta-decay half-lives of As isotopes within a factor of 10 and log ft values in decent agreement with data, but the agreement is conditional on the specific isotope and transition, with notable discrepancies for the decay of 70As where the model missed low-lying transitions and produced less fragmented GT strength distributions.

## Method

The authors first calculated ground-state deformation parameters (beta2) for 67-80As isotopes using the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the density-dependent DD-ME2 interaction, performing constrained quadrupole moment calculations to obtain potential energy curves. These beta2 values were then used as input to the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model, which employed the Nilsson model for single-particle wave functions and BCS pairing gaps, to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions, log ft values, beta-decay half-lives, and stellar weak rates for beta-plus, beta-minus, electron capture, and positron capture reactions.

## Key numbers

* The predicted beta-decay half-lives matched experimental values within a factor of 10 for all As isotopes studied (67-80As).
* The pn-QRPA stellar rates were found to be bigger than the shell model (IPM-03) rates up to a factor of 33 or more for the sum of beta-plus and electron capture rates at high temperature and density.
* The Q-value for beta-plus decay of 67As is 6.0868 MeV, while for beta-minus decay of 80As the Q-value is 5.5445 MeV.
* The ground-state deformation parameter beta2 for 67As is -0.4226 (oblate), while for 80As it is 0.3261 (prolate), with the shape transition occurring between A=75 and A=76.
* The calculated log ft value for the 67As to 67Ge transition (5/2-1 to 5/2-1) is 5.08, compared to the experimental value of 5.44.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of beta-decay properties in the A=70 mass region, including calculations using the interacting boson fermion model (IBFM-2) by Bra (2004) and the IBFM-2-SCMF model by Nom (2022), as well as earlier stellar rate calculations using the independent-particle model by Fuller (1982) and Pruet and Fuller (2003). The findings are intended to provide input for simulating the late-stage stellar evolution of massive stars, particularly for the r-process nucleosynthesis, where accurate beta-decay rates of neutron-rich nuclei are needed.
