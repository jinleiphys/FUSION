# Temperature of the hot $α$-source: a guidance to probe the Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ clusters in the heavy-ion collision

> Recent experimental efforts were made to explore the Bose-Einstein condensation in multi-$α$ system using the heavy-ion collisions. This Letter provides a explanation why no signatures were observed and a guidance for future experiments. More precisely, a harmonic oscillator model is developed to study the multi-$α$ source at or near zero temperature. The temperature and particle population of the multi-$α$ sources observed in the current experiments are extracted. It is found that almost no $α$ particles occupy the ground state in those multi-$α$ sources. The critical temperature for the multi-$α$-condensed states are predicted, which provides a guidance for the future experiments to probe the Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ clusters in the heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the reason no experimental signatures of alpha-particle Bose-Einstein condensation have been observed in heavy-ion collisions is that the extracted temperatures of the multi-alpha sources produced in current experiments are much higher than the predicted critical temperatures for condensation; the authors provide a harmonic oscillator model to guide future experiments toward lower-temperature conditions.

## Method

The authors develop a harmonic oscillator model for a multi-alpha system, where the alpha particles interact via a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential with parameters omega and V. They calibrate the model using the known nucleon density distribution of the 2-alpha system (8Be ground state) and the binding energy of 8Be, fixing the width parameter hbar*omega_0 = 2.78 MeV and the depth parameter V_0 = 5.20 MeV. They then calculate the excitation energy as a function of temperature for k-alpha systems and compare these calculations with measured excitation energies and particle spectra from existing heavy-ion collision experiments to extract the temperatures of the observed multi-alpha sources.

## Key numbers

*   The width parameter of the harmonic oscillator potential is set to hbar*omega_0 = 2.78 MeV, calibrated from the 2-alpha system (8Be).
*   The depth parameter of the potential is V_0 = 5.20 MeV, determined using the binding energy of 8Be (B_2 = -56.50 MeV).
*   The center nucleon density of the conjugate nuclei in the alpha-cluster condensed state is 0.1 fm^-3, which corresponds to an infinite alpha matter density of 0.025 fm^-3.
*   The predicted excitation energies for the alpha-cluster condensed states are 8.48 MeV for 12C, 16.3 MeV for 16O, and 21.6 MeV for 20Ne.
*   The extracted temperatures of multi-alpha sources from experiments are: 2.47 +/- 0.47 MeV for 5-alpha (16O+12C at 10 MeV/nucleon), 2.25 +/- 0.10 MeV for 6-alpha (12C+12C at 7.9 MeV/nucleon), 4.05 +/- 0.25 MeV for 7-alpha (28Si+12C at 35 MeV/nucleon), and 4.75 +/- 0.65 MeV for 10-alpha (40Ca+40Ca at 35 MeV/nucleon).
*   The critical temperatures T_c (where 50% of alpha particles occupy the ground state) are predicted as: 1.20 MeV for 5-alpha, 1.07 MeV for 6-alpha, 0.98 MeV for 7-alpha, and 0.83 MeV for 10-alpha.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of alpha-cluster condensed states in light self-conjugate nuclei, previously studied for 2-alpha (8Be), 3-alpha (12C), 4-alpha (16O), and 5-alpha (20Ne) systems by authors such as Mohr (1994), Funaki (2002, 2003, 2008), Tohsaki (2001), and Yamada (2004, 2012). It directly addresses the lack of experimental signatures for alpha condensation in heavy-ion collision experiments reported by Bishop (2019), Morelli (2019), Cao (2019), Schmidt (2017), and Borderie (2016). The results enable future experiments to target lower incident energies and temperatures below the predicted critical values to observe Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha clusters.
