# Comparison of space-time evolutions of hot/dense matter in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=17 and 130 GeV relativistic heavy ion collisions based on a hydrodynamical model

> Based on a hydrodynamical model, we compare 130 GeV/$A$ Au+Au collisions at RHIC and 17 GeV/$A$ Pb+Pb collisions at SPS. The model well reproduces the single-particle distributions of both RHIC and SPS. The numerical solution indicates that huge amount of collision energy in RHIC is mainly used to produce a large extent of hot fluid rather than to make a high temperature matter; longitudinal extent of the hot fluid in RHIC is much larger than that of SPS and initial energy density of the fluid is only 5% higher than the one in SPS. The solution well describes the HBT radii at SPS energy but shows some deviations from the ones at RHIC.

## Key claim

The higher collision energy at RHIC (130 GeV/A) compared to SPS (17 GeV/A) does not primarily produce a higher-temperature fireball; instead, the additional energy is used to create a much larger longitudinal extent of hot fluid, with the initial energy density at RHIC being only 5% higher than at SPS.

## Method

The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equations with cylindrical symmetry, assuming a perfect fluid and a bag model equation of state with a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma. Initial conditions at proper time τ₀ = 1.0 fm/c are parameterized with a central plateau and Gaussian tails for energy density and baryon density distributions, with parameters tuned to reproduce single-particle spectra at both SPS and RHIC. The Cooper-Frye formula is used to compute particle spectra from the freeze-out hypersurface, and two-pion correlation functions are calculated to extract HBT radii.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density: 6.0 GeV/fm³ at RHIC vs. 5.74 GeV/fm³ at SPS (only 5% higher)
- Average energy density at midrapidity: ⟨ε_RHIC⟩ = 3.9 GeV/fm³, ⟨ε_SPS⟩ = 3.77 GeV/fm³
- Longitudinal extent parameter η₀+σ_η: 2.47 at RHIC vs. 1.09 at SPS (approximately twice as large)
- Total energy of the fluid at RHIC: 25290 GeV (99% of total collision energy)
- Net baryon number: 131 at RHIC vs. 305 at SPS
- Mean freeze-out temperature: 125.0 MeV at RHIC vs. 123.2 MeV at SPS
- Mean chemical potential at freeze-out: 76.1 MeV at RHIC vs. 281.6 MeV at SPS
- Mean transverse flow velocity at |η|<0.1: 0.509c at RHIC vs. 0.467c at SPS
- Total lifetime of the fluid: 18.94 fm/c at RHIC vs. 17.74 fm/c at SPS
- Lifetime of QGP phase: 2.92 fm/c at RHIC vs. 2.67 fm/c at SPS
- Lifetime of mixed phase: 12.61 fm/c at RHIC vs. 11.12 fm/c at SPS

## Context

This work extends earlier (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical calculations that assumed Bjorken's scaling solution for longitudinal expansion, such as those by Kolb et al. (2001) and Zschiesche et al. (2002), by explicitly solving both longitudinal and transverse dynamics. The model reproduces SPS HBT radii well but shows deviations from RHIC HBT data, contributing to the "HBT puzzle" where dynamical models with QGP fail to simultaneously describe single-particle spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii at RHIC. The results enable future investigations into the opaque source scenario proposed by Heiselberg (2001) and more sophisticated freeze-out mechanisms.
