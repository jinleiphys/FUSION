# Double quarkonium production at high Feynman-$x$

> In this paper we give estimates for the proton--proton cross sections into pairs of quarkonium states $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, $Υ(1S)$ and $Υ(2S)$ at the scheduled AFTER@LHC energy of $115$GeV. The estimates are based on the intrinsic heavy flavor mechanism which is observable for high values of $x_F$, a range outside the dominance of single parton and double parton scattering.

## Key claim

The double intrinsic heavy quark mechanism can be the leading production mechanism for double quarkonium states at high Feynman-\(x_F\) at the AFTER@LHC energy of 115 GeV, a region where single parton scattering and double parton scattering contributions vanish rapidly; the estimates are based on the BHPS model and the color evaporation model, and cannot yet be compared to existing data.

## Method

The authors apply the BHPS (Brodsky-Hoyer-Peterson-Sakai) model for intrinsic heavy quark Fock states to compute the \(x_F\) distribution of double quarkonium production, using the color evaporation model (CEM) to convert heavy quark pair production into specific quarkonium states. They calculate cross sections for double-charmonium, associated charmonium–bottomonium, and double-bottomonium production from the Fock states \(|uud c\bar c c\bar c\rangle\), \(|uud c\bar c b\bar b\rangle\), and \(|uud b\bar b b\bar b\rangle\), respectively, using the inelastic proton–proton cross section at \(\sqrt{s}=115\) GeV and known values for quark masses, effective transverse masses, and meson thresholds.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic proton–proton cross section at \(\sqrt{s}=115\) GeV: \(\sigma^{inel}_{pp} = 28.4\) mb.
- Double-charmonium production cross section from intrinsic charm: \(\sigma^{icc}(c\bar c + c\bar c) \approx 1.5 \times 10^2\) pb.
- Double \(J/\psi\) production cross section: \(\sigma^{icc}(J/\psi + J/\psi) \approx 6.0\) pb.
- \(J/\psi + \psi(2S)\) production cross section: \(\sigma^{icc}(J/\psi + \psi(2S)) \approx 3.9\) pb.
- Double \(\psi(2S)\) production cross section: \(\sigma^{icc}(\psi(2S) + \psi(2S)) \approx 2.6\) pb.
- Associated charmonium–bottomonium production cross section: \(\sigma^{icb}(c\bar c + b\bar b) = 0.35\) pb.
- \(J/\psi + \Upsilon(1S)\) production cross section: \(\sigma^{icb}(J/\psi + \Upsilon(1S)) \approx 14\) fb.
- Double-bottomonium production cross section: \(\sigma^{ibb}(b\bar b + b\bar b) = 0.03\) pb.
- \(\Upsilon(1S) + \Upsilon(1S)\) production cross section: \(\sigma^{ibb}(\Upsilon(1S) + \Upsilon(1S)) \approx 1.2\) fb.
- \(\Upsilon(1S) + \Upsilon(2S)\) production cross section: \(\sigma^{ibb}(\Upsilon(1S) + \Upsilon(2S)) \approx 0.6\) fb.
- Double \(\Upsilon(2S)\) production cross section: \(\sigma^{ibb}(\Upsilon(2S) + \Upsilon(2S)) \approx 0.3\) fb.
- Ratio \(\sigma(J/\psi+J/\psi)/\sigma(J/\psi)\) from NA3 experiment: \((3 \pm 1) \times 10^{-4}\) with pion and proton beams.
- Ratio \(\sigma(J/\psi+J/\psi)/\sigma(J/\psi)\) from LHCb: \((5.1\pm 1.0\pm 0.6^{+1.2}_{-1.0})\times 10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends the intrinsic heavy quark mechanism calculations of Vogt (1995) to double quarkonium production at the proposed AFTER@LHC fixed-target experiment, complementing the double parton scattering (DPS) analysis by Lansberg and Shao (2016) for the same kinematic region. The authors note that existing LHCb measurements of double \(J/\psi\) production cannot be interpreted as evidence against the intrinsic charm mechanism because perturbative QCD fails to explain the NA3 data, while the intrinsic heavy quark mechanism reproduces the \(x_F\) distributions measured by NA3. The results provide predictions for AFTER@LHC to extract the double heavy quark probabilities \(P_{icc}\), \(P_{icb}\), and \(P_{ibb}\).
