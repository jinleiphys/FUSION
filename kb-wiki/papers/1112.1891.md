# Critical nucleus charge in a superstrong magnetic field: effect of screening

> A superstrong magnetic field stimulates the spontaneous production of positrons by naked nuclei by diminishing the value of the critical charge Z_{cr} . The phenomenon of screening of the Coulomb potential by a superstrong magnetic field which has been discovered recently acts in the opposite direction and prevents the nuclei with Z<52 from becoming critical. For Z>52 for a nucleus to become critical stronger B are needed than without taking screening into account.

## Key claim

Screening of the Coulomb potential by a superstrong magnetic field prevents nuclei with charge Z < 52 from ever becoming critical (i.e., reaching the lower continuum), and for Z > 52 requires stronger magnetic fields than predicted without screening; this lower bound is universal and holds even for a point-like nucleus, unlike the finite-nucleus-size cutoff in prior work.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for an electron in the field of a pointlike nucleus, using the adiabatic approximation to reduce it to one-dimensional differential equations for the lowest Landau level. The screened Coulomb potential was incorporated via the one-loop photon polarization operator in a superstrong magnetic field (B > B0 = m_e^2/e), with the effective potential averaged over the transverse electron wave function. Numerical integration of the resulting Schrödinger-like equation was performed for various Z and B, and the results were compared with the analytical formula (Eq. 9) derived from matching short- and long-distance solutions.

## Key numbers

- For Z = 1 without screening, the ground state energy parameter λ (defined by E = -m_e e^4 λ^2/2) ranges from 5.735 at B/B0 = 10^0 to 29.54 at B/B0 = 10^12 (Schrödinger) and from 5.734 to 29.33 (Dirac).
- For Z = 1 with screening, λ freezes at approximately 11.2–11.4 for B/B0 ≥ 10^4 (both Schrödinger and Dirac).
- For Z = 40 without screening, the ground state energy ε0 reaches -0.759 m_e at B/B0 = 10^4 and crosses -m_e at B/B0 ≈ 2.85 × 10^4; with screening, ε0 freezes at -0.538 m_e (never reaching -m_e).
- The freezing ground state energy for Z = 1 is -1.7 keV (both Schrödinger and Dirac); for Z = 40 it is -787 keV (Dirac); for Z = 49 it is -1003 keV (Dirac).
- For Z = 52 with screening, the critical field B_cr/B0 = 35135; for Z = 50, B_cr/B0 = 1.14 × 10^7; for Z = 45 and below, no critical field exists with screening.
- Without screening, B_cr/B0 for Z = 52 is 3087; for Z = 40 it is 28408; for Z = 25 it is 9.27 × 10^6.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of critical nucleus charge in strong magnetic fields by Loskutov and Skobelev (1970s) and by Popov and collaborators, who showed that a magnetic field reduces Z_cr by making electron motion quasi-one-dimensional. The authors incorporate the screening of the Coulomb potential by the magnetic field, discovered by Machet and Vysotsky (2010–2011), which acts in the opposite direction. The key new result is a universal lower bound Z ≥ 52 for criticality, independent of nuclear size, which contrasts with the earlier finite-size cutoff. This enables future studies of vacuum instability in magnetar-strength fields and clarifies the regime where the Dirac equation must replace the Schrödinger equation.
