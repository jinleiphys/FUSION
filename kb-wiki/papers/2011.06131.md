# The role of quark matter surface tension in magnetars

> In spite of its key role in compact star physics, the surface tension of quark matter is not well comprehended yet. In this work we analyze the behavior of the surface tension of three-flavor quark matter in the outer and inner core of cold deleptonized magnetars, proto magnetars born in core collapse supernovae, and hot magnetars produced in binary neutron stars mergers. We explore the role of temperature, baryon number density, trapped neutrinos, droplet size, and magnetic fields within the multiple reflection expansion formalism. Quark matter is described within the MIT bag model and is assumed to be in chemical equilibrium under weak interactions. We discuss some astrophysical consequences of our results.

## Key claim

The surface tension of quark matter in magnetars is anisotropic under strong magnetic fields, with values ranging from 0.2 to 15 MeV/fm² depending on density, temperature, and magnetic field strength; this anisotropy implies that quark droplets may form elongated shapes aligned with the magnetic field rather than spherical ones, particularly at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors used the MIT bag model to describe three-flavor quark matter (u, d, s quarks plus electrons) in chemical equilibrium under weak interactions with local charge neutrality, immersed in a uniform magnetic field. Finite-size effects of quark droplets were treated using the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) formalism, which modifies the density of states to include surface and curvature contributions, and the magnetic field quantizes transverse motion into Landau levels. The surface tension was computed separately for directions parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field for three astrophysical scenarios: cold magnetars (T = 1–10 MeV, no trapped neutrinos), proto magnetars (T = 10–50 MeV, μ_νe = 100 MeV), and post-merger magnetars (T = 50–100 MeV, μ_νe = 200 MeV), at two baryon densities (n_B = n_0 and n_B = 4 n_0) and two magnetic field strengths (eB_low = 5 × 10⁻³ GeV² and eB_high = 5 × 10⁻² GeV²).

## Key numbers

- Surface tension spans values between 0.2 MeV/fm² and 15 MeV/fm² across all scenarios considered.
- At n_B = 4 n_0 and low temperature, surface tension reaches values as large as 15 MeV/fm².
- Two magnetic field strengths were used: eB_low = 5 × 10⁻³ GeV² = 8.5 × 10¹⁷ G and eB_high = 5 × 10⁻² GeV² = 8.5 × 10¹⁸ G.
- Two droplet sizes were considered: V/S = 2 fm and V/S = 10 fm, close to the Debye screening length λ_D ∼ 5 fm.
- For eB_high at low densities, relative differences of ∼10% were found between results for V/S = 2 fm and V/S = 10 fm.
- The critical surface tension for mixed phase formation in hybrid stars is σ_crit ≈ 60 MeV/fm²; all computed values are significantly smaller than this.
- The critical surface tension for charge-separated mixtures in strange stars is estimated in the range 0.5–18 MeV/fm².

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Lugones (2018) by exploring lower baryon densities (n_B = n_0, typical of the outer core) and smaller droplet sizes closer to the Debye screening length, complementing earlier work that focused on larger droplets and higher densities. The results are relevant for understanding whether hadron-quark interfaces in magnetars form sharp discontinuities or mixed phases (pasta structures), as studied by Voskresensky (2002), Endo (2011), and Wu (2018). The anisotropic surface tension found here implies that the pasta configurations in hybrid magnetars may differ from those in non-magnetized hybrid stars, and that the ground state of self-bound ultra-magnetized strange stars may require a more complex analysis than the single critical surface tension approach used by Alford (2006, 2008) and Lugones (2020).
