# Chiral nuclear dynamics with three-body forces

> We review recent progress in implementing high-precision chiral two- and three-body forces in nuclear many-body systems beyond light nuclei. We begin with applications to finite nuclei, which we study through the nuclear shell model and self-consistent mean field theory. We then turn our attention to infinite nuclear matter treated within the framework of Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids.

# Chiral nuclear dynamics with three-body forces

## Key claim
The leading-order chiral three-nucleon force, when incorporated into many-body perturbation theory, provides a mechanism for nuclear matter saturation and significantly improves the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter, though the anomalous orbital g-factor remains underestimated compared to empirical values.

## Method
The authors constructed medium-dependent two-body effective interactions from the N²LO chiral three-nucleon force at one-loop order, applied them in shell model studies of finite nuclei, and derived a microscopic energy density functional using the improved density matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner. They also computed the quasiparticle interaction in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter within Landau Fermi liquid theory, evaluating the L=0 and L=1 Landau parameters from chiral N³LO two-nucleon forces supplemented with the leading-order three-body force.

## Key numbers
- Two parameter sets for the three-nucleon force low-energy constants: (c_E = -0.205, c_D = -0.20, c_1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.2 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 5.4 GeV^{-1}) and (c_E = -0.625, c_D = -2.06, c_1 = -0.76 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -4.78 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.96 GeV^{-1})
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation density: M* ≈ 0.7 M_N
- Compression modulus with three-nucleon forces: K = (370 ± 160) MeV, compared to empirical range [200-300] MeV
- Isospin asymmetry energy: β = (30 ± 1) MeV, within empirical range [30-36] MeV
- Spin-isospin response parameter: g'_NN = 0.55 ± 0.03, within empirical range [0.6-0.7]
- Anomalous orbital g-factor: δg_l = 0.07 ± 0.02, below empirical range [0.20-0.26]
- Quasiparticle effective mass with three-nucleon forces: M*/M_N = 0.98 ± 0.05

## Context
This work extends earlier studies using only chiral two-nucleon forces (Bogner 2005) that found underbinding and negative compression moduli, and builds on the density matrix expansion framework of Negele and Vautherin (1972) and its improvement by Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner (2010). The results enable future shell model and mean field theory calculations of finite nuclei, and can be extended to pure neutron matter as a first approximation to neutron-star matter. The authors note that the recently completed subleading chiral three-nucleon force (Bernard 2008) will allow consistent calculations up to N³LO in the chiral expansion.
