# Field redefinitions and evolutions in relativistic Navier-Stokes

> In recent years the equations of relativistic first-order viscous hydrodynamics, that is, the relativistic version of Navier-Stokes, have been shown to be well posed and causal under appropriate field redefinitions, also known as hydrodynamic frames. We perform real-time evolutions of these equations for a conformal fluid and explore, quantitatively, the consequences of using different causal frames for different sets of initial data. By defining specific criteria, we make precise and provide evidence for the statement that the arbitrarily chosen frame does not affect the physics up to first order, as long as the system is in the effective field theory regime. Motivated by the physics of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions we also explore systems which are marginally in the effective field theory regime, finding that even under these circumstances the first order physics is robust under field redefinitions.

## Key claim

The paper provides quantitative evidence that the choice of hydrodynamic frame (field redefinition) in relativistic first-order viscous hydrodynamics does not affect the physics up to first order in the gradient expansion, provided the system remains within the effective field theory regime; even for systems marginally in this regime, the first-order physics remains robust under field redefinitions.

## Method

The authors perform real-time numerical evolutions of the relativistic Navier-Stokes (BDNK) equations for a conformal fluid in 3+1 dimensions, restricting dynamics to 1+1 dimensions along the x-direction. They evolve the equations using different hydrodynamic frames specified by parameters {a1, a2} satisfying causality conditions, with typical values {5,5} and {10,10}, and compare solutions for three sets of initial data: small amplitude perturbations of a homogeneous thermal state, large amplitude Gaussian perturbations, and shockwave solutions. They define three quantitative criteria to assess frame independence: (A) hierarchy of ideal, first-order, and second-order gradient terms; (B) relative size of frame-dependent terms versus the shear viscosity term; (C) difference between solutions in different frames compared to the difference from ideal hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity is set to η = s/(4π), the holographic value for large-N, large coupling theories.
- Causal frames satisfy the conditions a1 ≥ 4 and a2 ≥ 3a1/(a1-1); the Landau frame {0,0} lies outside this region.
- Typical frame parameters used for comparison are {a1, a2} = {5,5} and {10,10}, differing by at least a factor of 2.
- The conformal equation of state is p = ε/3, with energy density ε = C T^4 and C = (3/4)π^4.

## Context

This work extends the recent formulation by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha (2017-2020) and Kovtun (2019) showing that relativistic Navier-Stokes equations can be well-posed and causal under appropriate field redefinitions, resolving the long-standing causality issues identified by Hiscock (1985). It provides the first quantitative numerical study of frame independence in this context, going beyond the proof-of-principle evolutions by Bantilan (2022) and Pandya (2021-2022). The results support Geroch's (1995) argument that different formulations of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics should yield equivalent physical predictions within the effective field theory regime, and suggest that first-order viscous hydrodynamics may be a viable alternative to Müller-Israel-Stewart type theories for applications such as quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers.
