# Deciphering the dynamics of nuclear collisions with elongated structure of $^{20}$Ne

> We investigate the role of intrinsic nuclear geometry of $^{20}$Ne nucleus in particle production in small collision systems. Discrete geometrical representations of $^{20}$Ne, including bi-pyramidal $α$-cluster structure in two different configurations along with NLEFT configurations, are implemented within the Monte Carlo Pythia8/Angantyr framework. The resulting particle production observables in $^{20}$Ne-$^{20}$Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.36 TeV are systematically compared with those obtained using conventional Woods-Saxon description as well as with the available hydrodynamic model calculations. We investigate the sensitivity of charged particle multiplicity, transverse momentum distributions and mean transverse momentum $\langle p_T \rangle$ to nuclear geometry, $α$-clustering, and orientation effects of $^{20}$Ne nucleus. While explicit clustering and orientation dependence lead to a noticeable modifications in final state charged particle multiplicity, their impact on transverse momentum spectra and $\langle p_T \rangle$ remain modest in central collisions. The results highlight the role of intrinsic nuclear geometry and specific orientation of the colliding nuclei, providing insight into the dynamics of small systems in non-hydrodynamic particle production framework.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the intrinsic nuclear geometry and alpha-clustering structure of the 20Ne nucleus produce noticeable modifications in charged particle multiplicity in 20Ne-20Ne collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV, but the impact on transverse momentum spectra and mean transverse momentum remains modest in central collisions, with the caveat that these results are obtained within a non-hydrodynamic particle production framework (Pythia8/Angantyr) and may differ from hydrodynamic model predictions.

## Method

The authors implemented discrete geometrical representations of the 20Ne nucleus, including bi-pyramidal alpha-cluster structures in two configurations (Geometry I and Geometry II) and positively weighted Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) configurations, within the Monte Carlo Pythia8/Angantyr framework. They simulated 20Ne-20Ne collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV and compared the resulting particle production observables with those obtained using a conventional Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution and with available hydrodynamic model calculations from the Trajectum model. The study examined charged particle multiplicity, pseudorapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and mean transverse momentum as functions of centrality and nuclear orientation (Tip-Tip, Body-Body, Body-Tip, and random orientations).

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV for 20Ne-20Ne collisions
- Inter-cluster spacing for oxygen nucleus: 3.42 fm; fifth alpha-cluster placed at 3.6 fm from center of oxygen nucleus
- Geometry I: Woods-Saxon parameters for alpha-clusters R=0.964 fm, a=0.322 fm, w=0.517, yielding rms radius of 2.76 +/- 0.16 fm for 20Ne
- Geometry II: optimized Woods-Saxon parameters R=1.12 fm, a=0.44 fm, w=0.30, yielding rms radius of 3.00 +/- 0.20 fm for 20Ne
- NLEFT configurations: 13589 independent 20Ne configurations with rms radius of 3.08 +/- 0.28 fm
- Woods-Saxon parameters for 20Ne: R=2.791 fm, a=0.698 fm, w=-0.168, yielding rms radius of 3.00 +/- 0.23 fm
- Experimental rms radius of 20Ne: 3.0055 +/- 0.0021 fm (Angeli 2013)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of alpha-cluster effects in nuclear collisions, particularly those involving 12C and 16O nuclei (Broniowski 2013, Bozek 2014, Rybczynski 2019), to the 20Ne nucleus which has a bi-pyramidal bowling-pin structure composed of five alpha-clusters. The study builds on theoretical predictions of clustering in 20Ne from the antisymmetrized quasicluster model (Yamaguchi 2023), antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-Enyo 2020), and density functional theory (Ebran 2012), with experimental evidence from Adachi (2020) and Nauruzbayev (2017). The orientation-dependent effects (Tip-Tip, Body-Body, Body-Tip) were previously investigated in collisions involving prolate deformed nuclei such as 238U and 129Xe (Nepali 2007, Haque 2011, Bairathi 2015, Luo 2023), but this work contrasts those smooth quadrupole deformations with the discrete, granular density hot-spots from alpha-clustering in 20Ne. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming LHC data on small collision systems and provide constraints for distinguishing between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic particle production mechanisms.
