# Phenomenology of axial-vector and pseudovector mesons: decays and mixing in the kaonic sector

> We study the decays of the lightest axial-vector and pseudovector mesons, interpreted as quark-antiquark states, into a vector and a pseudoscalar meson. We show that the quarkonium assignment delivers a good description of the decays and allows also to make further testable predictions. In the kaonic sector, the physical resonances $K_{1}(1270)$ and $K_{1}(1400)$ emerge as mixed objects of an axial vector state $K_{1,A}$ and a pseudovector state $K_{1,B}$. We determine the mixing angle as $\left\vert θ_{K}\right\vert=\left(33.6\pm4.3\right) ^{\circ}$, a value compatible with previous studies but with a smaller uncertainty. This result may be helpful for testing models beyond the Standard Model of particle physics in which decays into the kaonic resonances $K_{1}(1270)$ and $K_{1}(1400)$ are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the lightest axial-vector and pseudovector mesons can be consistently described as ordinary quark-antiquark states, and determines the kaonic mixing angle with reduced uncertainty; however, the mixing angle determination relies on tree-level mixing with loop effects neglected, and two mathematical solutions exist, with only one being physically favored.

## Method

The authors construct a flavor-symmetric relativistic Lagrangian with three free parameters describing decays of axial-vector and pseudovector mesons into vector and pseudoscalar mesons, plus a mixing term proportional to the quark mass difference (m_s^2 - m_u^2). They fix the axial-vector coupling constant a from the decay f_1(1420) → K K*(892) and the pseudovector coupling constant b from the total width of b_1(1235). For decays affected by threshold effects, they integrate tree-level decay widths over relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral functions of the unstable particles. The mixing angle in the kaonic sector is determined by fitting the theoretical K*π decay widths of K_1(1270) and K_1(1400) to experimental values via χ^2 minimization.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle |θ_K| = (33.6 ± 4.3)° (corresponding to |φ_1| = (56.4 ± 4.2)°), obtained from χ^2 = 1.54 fit to K*π decay channels
- Axial-vector coupling constant |a| = (5.43 ± 0.26) GeV, fixed from Γ(f_1(1420) → K K*(892)) = (44.5 ± 4.2) MeV
- Pseudovector coupling constant |b| = (7.0 ± 0.22) GeV, fixed from total width Γ(b_1(1235)) = (142 ± 9) MeV
- Theoretical width for a_1(1260) → ρπ: (396 ± 37) MeV, consistent with PDG range 250-600 MeV and Compass result (367 ± 9_{-25}^{+28}) MeV
- Theoretical width for h_1(1170) → ρπ: (364 ± 23) MeV, compared to experimental total width (360 ± 40) MeV
- Bare masses for the favored solution: m_{K_1,A} = 1.36 GeV, m_{K_1,B} = 1.31 GeV
- Alternative solution |φ_2| = (19.0 ± 4.2)° gives K_1(1400) → Kρ width (27.3 ± 1.2) MeV, inconsistent with experimental (2 ± 1) MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-model description of p-wave mesons previously studied by Burakovsky (1998) and others, confirming the quark-antiquark assignment for axial-vector and pseudovector nonets. The mixing angle result |θ_K| = (33.6 ± 4.3)° agrees with the recent analysis by Cheng (2012) favoring the smaller angle solution over the alternative ~55° solution, and is consistent with the value θ_K = -(34 ± 13)° obtained from B meson weak decays by Hatanaka (2009). The improved precision of this mixing angle enables more reliable tests of physics beyond the Standard Model, particularly in studies of heavy B-meson decays to axial-vector and pseudovector kaonic resonances that probe CKM matrix elements and possible fourth-generation effects.
