# Production of a 4He_Lambda hypernucleus in the 4He(pi, K) reactions reexamined

> We investigate theoretically production cross sections of the $J^π=$ 0$^+$ ground state of a $^4_Λ$He hypernucleus in the $^4$He($π$,~$K$) reaction with a distorted-wave impulse approximation using the optimal Fermi-averaged $πN\to KΛ$ $t$ matrix. We demonstrate the sensitivity of the production cross sections to the $Λ$ wavefunctions obtained from $3N$-$Λ$ potentials and to meson distorted waves in eikonal distortions. It is shown that the calculated laboratory cross sections of the 0$^+$ ground state in $^4_Λ$He amount to $dσ/dΩ_{\rm lab} \simeq$ 11 $μ$b/sr at $p_π=$ 1.05 GeV/$c$ in the $K$ forward direction because of an advantage of the use of the s-shell target nucleus such as $^4$He. The importance of the recoil effects and the energy dependence of the $πN\to KΛ$ cross sections is also discussed.

## Key claim

The calculated laboratory cross sections of the 0⁺ ground state in ⁴_ΛHe amount to dσ/dΩ_lab ≃ 11 μb/sr at p_π = 1.05 GeV/c in the K forward direction, but this result depends sensitively on the choice of Λ wavefunction (Isle vs. SG vs. HO potentials) and on whether recoil effects and meson distorted waves are properly included.

## Method

The authors used a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with optimal Fermi-averaged πN → KΛ t matrices to calculate production cross sections for the ⁴He(π⁺, K⁺) reaction. They employed three different 3N-Λ potentials (Isle, SG, and HO) to generate Λ wavefunctions, and treated meson distorted waves using the eikonal approximation with averaged total cross sections σ_π = 30 mb and σ_K = 15 mb. The effective number technique was applied to compute differential lab cross sections, including recoil effects via the factor M_C/M_A on the momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- dσ/dΩ_lab ≃ 11 μb/sr for the 0⁺ ground state of ⁴_ΛHe at p_π = 1.05 GeV/c and θ_lab = 0° using the Isle potential with DWIA
- dσ/dΩ_lab = 19.55 μb/sr for the SG potential at the same kinematics (θ_lab = 0°)
- dσ/dΩ_lab = 7.40 μb/sr for the HO potential (ħω_Λ = 7.0 MeV) at the same kinematics
- Momentum transfer q = 359.9 MeV/c at θ_lab = 0° and p_π = 1.05 GeV/c, with effective momentum transfer q_eff = 271.1 MeV/c after recoil correction
- Λ binding energy B_Λ = 2.39 MeV for 0⁺_g.s. in ⁴_ΛHe
- Relative r.m.s. distance between 3N and Λ: ⟨r²_Λ⟩^{1/2} = 3.57 fm for Isle, 3.31 fm for SG, 3.23 fm for HO
- Kinematical factor α = 0.828 at p_π = 1.05 GeV/c and θ_lab = 0° for ⁴He target

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of hypernuclear production via (π, K) reactions on ⁴He targets, particularly the calculations by Harada (2006) for non-mesonic weak decay of ⁴_ΛHe and ⁴_ΛH at J-PARC. The results are relevant to planned experiments at J-PARC (P73 and P74) measuring lifetimes of ³_ΛH and ⁴_ΛH via (K⁻, π⁰) and (π⁻, K⁰) reactions on ³,⁴He targets, and address the lifetime puzzle raised by HypHI (2013) and ALICE (2016, 2018) measurements. The paper enables quantitative predictions for production yields of A=4 hypernuclei, which are needed to resolve anomalies in Λ binding energies and charge symmetry breaking effects discussed by Dalitz (1964, 1972), Akaishi (2000), and Gal (2015, 2016).
