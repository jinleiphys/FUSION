# Fluid pulsation modes and tidal deformability of anisotropic strange stars in light of the GW$170817$ event

> The effects of the anisotropy on the fluid pulsation modes adopting the so-called Cowling approximation and tidal deformability of strange quark stars are investigated by using the numerical integration of the hydrostatic equilibrium, nonradial oscillations, and tidal deformability equations, being these equations modified from their standard form to include the anisotropic effects. The fluid matter inside the compact stars is described by the MIT bag model equation of state. For the anisotropy profile, we consider a local anisotropy that is both regular at the center and null at the star's surface. We find that the effect of the anisotropy is reflected in the fluid pulsation modes and tidal deformability. Finally, we analyze the correlation between the tidal deformability of the GW$170817$ event with the anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how local pressure anisotropy affects the fluid pulsation modes and tidal deformability of strange quark stars, finding that all considered anisotropic configurations remain compatible with the observational constraints from the GW170817 event, though the specific effect on tidal deformability depends on the chosen anisotropy profile.

## Method

The authors numerically integrate the hydrostatic equilibrium equations, non-radial oscillation equations within the Cowling approximation, and tidal deformability equations, all modified to include anisotropic pressure effects. The strange quark matter is described by the MIT bag model equation of state with bag constant B = 60 MeV/fm^3, and the anisotropy profile is taken as sigma = kappa * p_r * (1 - e^{-2*Psi}), which is regular at the center and vanishes at the stellar surface. The equations are solved using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with a shooting method for the boundary conditions, and a scaling law is employed to relate results for different bag constant values.

## Key numbers

* Bag constant B = 60 MeV/fm^3 used for the MIT bag model equation of state
* The allowed range for the bag constant from Farhi (1984) is 57 to 94 MeV/fm^3
* The dimensionless tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses from LVC is Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}
* The chirp mass for GW170817 is M = 1.188 solar masses
* The combined dimensionless tidal deformability from LVC is tilde{Lambda} = 300^{+420}_{-230}
* The mass range considered for the binary components is 1.36 to 1.60 solar masses for M1 and 1.17 to 1.36 solar masses for M2
* The NEMO detector has sensitivity of 10^{-24} Hz^{-1} in the frequency range 1-4 kHz

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic effects in compact stars, such as Doneva (2012) on non-radial oscillations of neutron stars and Arbanil and Malheiro (2016) on radial oscillations of strange stars, by simultaneously analyzing both fluid pulsation modes and tidal deformability in the context of GW170817 constraints. The results enable future comparisons with third-generation gravitational wave detectors like the Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer, and NEMO, which may observe the fundamental oscillation mode of compact stars. The authors note that their finding that tidal deformability increases with positive kappa contradicts the results of Biswas (2019) and Das (2022), who used a different anisotropy profile, highlighting that the sign of the effect depends on the specific form of the anisotropy.
