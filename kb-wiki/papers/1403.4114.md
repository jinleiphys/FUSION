# Supernova matter at subnuclear densities as a resonant Fermi gas: Enhancement of neutrino rates

> At low energies nucleon-nucleon interactions are resonant and therefore supernova matter at subnuclear densities has many similarities to atomic gases with interactions dominated by a Feshbach resonance. We calculate the rates of neutrino processes involving nucleon-nucleon collisions and show that these are enhanced in mixtures of neutrons and protons at subnuclear densities due to the large scattering lengths. As a result, the rate for neutrino pair bremsstrahlung and absorption is significantly larger below 10^{13} g cm^{-3} compared to rates used in supernova simulations.

## Key claim

The rates for neutrino pair bremsstrahlung and absorption in supernova matter at subnuclear densities (below 10^13 g cm^-3) are significantly enhanced in mixtures of neutrons and protons due to the large scattering lengths, but this enhancement is strongest at low densities and decreases with increasing energy exchange, so the overall effect on the energy-averaged inverse mean-free path is less dramatic than the raw spin relaxation rate enhancement.

## Method

The authors generalized the relaxation rate formalism for the axial charge-density structure factor to mixtures of neutrons and protons, calculating the spin relaxation rate 1/τ_A from nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes. They compared results from one-pion-exchange (OPE) approximations, chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions at leading order (LO) and N^3LO, and a model-independent T-matrix approach based on Nijmegen partial wave analysis of NN phase shifts, under typical supernova temperature-density conditions.

## Key numbers

- The spin relaxation rate 1/τ_A shows a "dramatic enhancement" at subnuclear densities ρ ≲ 10^13 g cm^-3 for neutron-proton mixtures compared to OPE rates used in supernova simulations, with the enhancement traced to large scattering lengths.
- For the energy-averaged inverse mean-free path of a neutrino against pair absorption at Y_e=0.3, the enhancement is present for ρ ≲ 10^12 g cm^-3, but is less strong than the raw spin relaxation rate enhancement because the T-matrix rate decreases faster with increasing ω than chiral EFT and OPE rates.
- The T-matrix results are expected to be valid up to a fugacity z ≈ n_n λ_n^3/2 ≲ 1/2, marked by a small bar in Figure 1.
- At higher densities, T-matrix and N^3LO results agree well; N^3LO rates are typically a factor of two smaller at higher densities than the OPE approximation.
- For nondegenerate conditions and infinite scattering lengths, corrections to the T-matrix results are estimated to be of order (n/T) 4π/m √(3mT) ~ 0.02–0.07 for ρ = 10^11–10^12 g cm^-3 under the supernova conditions studied.
- The temperature along supernova conditions is taken as T = 3 MeV (ρ / 10^11 g cm^-3)^(1/3).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Bacca et al. (2009, 2012) for pure neutron matter to mixtures of neutrons and protons, and builds on earlier studies of neutrino processes by Hannestad and Raffelt (1998) using OPE approximations, Hanhart et al. (2001) using free-space scattering amplitudes from phase shifts, and Friman and Maxwell (1979) for degenerate matter in neutron star cooling. The results enable improved neutrino transport simulations in core-collapse supernovae, particularly for the density range ρ ~ 10^11–10^13 g cm^-3 where neutrinos decouple from matter, and suggest a mechanism for energy transport from hotter higher-density regions to outer layers.
