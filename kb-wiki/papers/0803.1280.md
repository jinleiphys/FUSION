# The ground state energy at unitarity

> We consider two-component fermions on the lattice in the unitarity limit. This is an idealized limit of attractive fermions where the range of the interaction is zero and the scattering length is infinite. Using Euclidean time projection, we compute the ground state energy using four computationally different but physically identical auxiliary-field methods. The best performance is obtained using a bounded continuous auxiliary field and a non-local updating algorithm called hybrid Monte Carlo. With this method we calculate results for 10 and 14 fermions at lattice volumes 4^3, 5^3, 6^3, 7^3, 8^3 and extrapolate to the continuum limit. For 10 fermions in a periodic cube, the ground state energy is 0.292(12) times the ground state energy for non-interacting fermions. For 14 fermions the ratio is 0.329(5).

## Key claim
The ground state energy ratio for two-component fermions at unitarity is computed via lattice simulations with Euclidean time projection, and the results for 10 and 14 fermions are extrapolated to the continuum limit, yielding specific values that depend on particle number and require careful handling of systematic errors from finite lattice spacing and singular matrix configurations.

## Method
The author used Euclidean time projection with four different auxiliary-field methods (Gaussian-integral, exponentially-coupled, discrete, and bounded continuous) that are exactly equivalent in physics but differ in computational performance. The bounded continuous auxiliary field combined with hybrid Monte Carlo gave the best performance. Ground state energies were computed for 10 and 14 fermions on periodic lattices of volumes 4^3, 5^3, 6^3, 7^3, and 8^3, then extrapolated to the continuum limit. The interaction coefficient C was tuned to the unitarity limit using Luscher's formula with the scattering length set to infinity and effective range parameters set to zero.

## Key numbers
- For 10 fermions in a periodic cube, the ground state energy ratio to non-interacting fermions is 0.292(12).
- For 14 fermions, the ground state energy ratio is 0.329(5).
- The lattice parameters used a fermion mass of 939 MeV, spatial lattice spacing a = (50 MeV)^(-1), and temporal lattice spacing a_t = (24 MeV)^(-1), giving dimensionless parameters m = 18.78 and alpha_t = 2.0833.
- The unitarity limit interaction coefficient C was -7.4415 x 10^(-5) MeV^(-2) in physical units, or -0.18604 in dimensionless lattice units.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice calculations by Lee (2005) that estimated xi = 0.25(3) for small fermion numbers and volumes, and by Lee (2007) using the symmetric heavy-light ansatz that estimated xi = 0.31(1). The results differ from fixed-node Green's function Monte Carlo simulations by Carlson (2003) finding xi = 0.44(1) for 5 to 21 fermions and by Astrakharchik (2004) and Carlson (2005) finding 0.42(1) for larger N, as well as from more recent lattice calculations by Abe (2007) yielding 0.261(12). The new bounded continuous auxiliary field method enables calculations at larger lattice volumes than previously possible, allowing continuum extrapolation for 10 and 14 fermions.
