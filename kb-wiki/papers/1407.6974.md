# Quasifree photoproduction of $η$ mesons off protons and neutrons

> Differential and total cross sections for the quasifree reactions $γp\rightarrowηp$ and $γn\rightarrowηn$ have been determined at the MAMI-C electron accelerator using a liquid deuterium target. Photons were produced via bremsstrahlung from the 1.5 GeV incident electron beam and energy-tagged with the Glasgow photon tagger. Decay photons of the neutral decay modes $η\rightarrow 2γ$ and $η\rightarrow 3π^0 \rightarrow 6γ$ and coincident recoil nucleons were detected in a combined setup of the Crystal Ball and the TAPS calorimeters. The $η$-production cross sections were measured in coincidence with recoil protons, recoil neutrons, and in an inclusive mode without a condition on recoil nucleons, which allowed a check of the internal consistency of the data. The effects from nuclear Fermi motion were removed by a kinematic reconstruction of the final-state invariant mass and possible nuclear effects on the quasifree cross section were investigated by a comparison of free and quasifree proton data. The results, which represent a significant improvement in statistical quality compared to previous measurements, agree with the known neutron-to-proton cross-section ratio in the peak of the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance and confirm a peak in the neutron cross section, which is absent for the proton, at a center-of-mass energy $W = (1670\pm 5)$ MeV with an intrinsic width of $Γ\approx 30$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper confirms a peak in the neutron cross section for quasifree $\eta$ photoproduction at a center-of-mass energy $W = (1670 \pm 5)$ MeV with an intrinsic width of $\Gamma \approx 30$ MeV, which is absent for the proton, but the authors note that the nature of this peak and its possible correlation with a dip in the proton cross section at similar energy remain not understood.

## Method

The A2 Collaboration measured differential and total cross sections for the quasifree reactions $\gamma p \rightarrow \eta p$ and $\gamma n \rightarrow \eta n$ using a liquid deuterium target at the MAMI-C electron accelerator with a 1.5 GeV electron beam producing bremsstrahlung photons. The $\eta$ mesons were reconstructed via their neutral decay modes $\eta \rightarrow 2\gamma$ and $\eta \rightarrow 3\pi^0 \rightarrow 6\gamma$ using the Crystal Ball and TAPS calorimeters, with coincident recoil nucleons detected for exclusive measurements. Fermi motion effects were removed by kinematic reconstruction of the final-state invariant mass, and nuclear effects on the quasifree cross section were investigated by comparing free and quasifree proton data.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy of the neutron cross section peak: $W = (1670 \pm 5)$ MeV
- Intrinsic width of the neutron cross section peak: $\Gamma \approx 30$ MeV
- Incident electron beam energy: $E_0 = 1508$ MeV (1558 MeV during part of the beam time)
- Photon energy threshold for $\eta$ production: $E_{\gamma} = 708$ MeV, corresponding to $W = 1486$ MeV
- Neutron-to-proton cross-section ratio in the peak of the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance: close to 2/3

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of $\eta$ photoproduction off neutrons by the GRAAL experiment (Kuznetsov 2007) and the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Jaegle 2008, Jaegle 2011), which first identified a narrow structure in the neutron cross section around 1 GeV photon energy. The new high-statistics data from MAMI-C confirm this peak and provide significantly improved statistical quality, enabling future coupled-channel partial-wave analyses (such as those by the Bonn-Gatchina group or the SAID group) to better constrain the isospin-dependent helicity couplings of nucleon resonances and investigate possible explanations including interference effects in the $S_{11}$ partial wave, threshold effects from strangeness openings, or contributions from intrinsically narrow states.
