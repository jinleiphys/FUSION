# Isomeric excitation of ^{229}\mathrm{Th} via scanning tunneling microscope

> The low energy of the isomeric state of the radionuclide thorium-229 (229Th) makes it highly promising for applications in fundamental physics, precision metrology, and quantum technologies. However, directly accessing the isomeric state from its ground state remains a challenge. We propose here a tabletop approach utilizing the scanning tunneling microscope (STM) technique to induce excitation of a single 229Th nucleus. With achievable parameters, the isomeric excitation rate is advantageous over existing methods, allowing the excitation and control of 229Th on the single-nucleus level. It offers the unique potential of exciting and detecting subsequent decay from a single nucleus, providing a new direction for future experimental investigation of the 229Th isomeric state.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a scanning tunneling microscope (STM) can excite a single thorium-229 nucleus to its isomeric state at a rate of approximately 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ with currently achievable parameters, which is orders of magnitude higher than existing methods per nucleus. This advantage depends critically on the STM's ability to focus electron current to a nm-scale area, yielding an electron flux of about 10¹⁰ nm⁻²s⁻¹ compared to 10² nm⁻²s⁻¹ from conventional beam sources.

## Method

The authors develop a quantum theory of isomeric excitation for an STM setup, modeling the ²²⁹Th nucleus as a two-level system with an 8.338 eV energy gap and treating the tunneling electron via Bardeen's formalism. They calculate the transition probability using Fermi's golden rule with multipole expansion of the electron-nucleus interaction, assuming an electric quadrupole (E2) transition with reduced probability B(E2) = 27.04 Weisskopf units. The excitation rate is computed as a function of tip radius, tip-substrate distance, and bias voltage for a silver tip and substrate with a ²²⁹Th atom doped in a CaF₂ crystal.

## Key numbers

- Isomeric excitation rate of 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ per nucleus for typical STM parameters: tip radius 0.5 nm, tip-substrate distance 0.5 nm, bias voltage -11 V
- Potential excitation rate enhancement to 10⁻² s⁻¹ per nucleus with further technological refinement
- Electron flux in STM: about 10¹⁰ nm⁻²s⁻¹, compared to 10² nm⁻²s⁻¹ from beam electron sources
- Isomeric state energy: 8.338 eV above the nuclear ground state (Kraemer 2023)
- Bias voltage -11 V chosen to stay within the CaF₂ band gap of 11.6 to 12.1 eV
- Photon detection solid angle in STM: about 3 sr, compared to about 0.1 sr in synchrotron radiation experiments (Masuda 2019)
- For comparison: synchrotron radiation method yields 10⁻¹¹ s⁻¹ per nucleus; inelastic electron scattering with 1 mA beam yields about 6.25 × 10⁻¹² s⁻¹ per nucleus

## Context

This work extends theoretical proposals for ²²⁹Th excitation via electrons (Tkalya 2020, Zhang 2022) by introducing a tabletop STM approach that achieves single-nucleus control, unlike synchrotron radiation experiments (Masuda 2019) or laser-generated plasma methods (Borisyuk 2018). The method enables future investigation of nuclear quantum optics and single-nucleus radiative decay detection, which is not achievable with existing approaches that require bulk samples or high-energy sources.
