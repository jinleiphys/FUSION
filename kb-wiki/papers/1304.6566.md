# Elliptic Flow from a Beam Energy Scan: a signature of a phase transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We employ a relativistic transport theory to describe the fireball expansion of the matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions (uRHICs). Developing an approach to fix locally the shear viscosity to entropy density $η/s$, we study the impact of a temperature dependent $η/s(T)$ on the build-up of the elliptic flow, $v_2$, a measure of the angular anisotropy in the particle production. Beam Energy Scan from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= \rm 62.4 GeV$ at RHIC up to 2.76 TeV at LHC has shown that the $v_2(p_T)$ as a function of the transverse momentum $p_T$ appears to be nearly invariant with energy. We show that such a surprising behavior is determined by a rise and fall of $η/s(T)$ with a minimum at $T\sim T_c$, as one would expect if the matter undergoes a phase transition or a cross-over. This provides an evidence for phase transition occurring in the uRHIC's and a first constraint on the temperature dependence of $η/s$. In particular, a constant $η/s$ at all temperatures or a too strong T-dependence would cause a breaking of the scaling of $v_2(p_T)$ with the energy.

## Key claim

The observed near-invariance of the elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) across a wide range of collision energies (from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\) GeV at RHIC to 2.76 TeV at LHC) can only be reproduced if the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s(T)\) has a "U" shape with a minimum near the critical temperature \(T_c \sim 165\) MeV, as expected if the created matter undergoes a phase transition or cross-over to a Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Method

The authors employ a Relativistic Boltzmann Transport (RBT) approach that fixes the local cross section \(\sigma_{tot}\) according to the Chapman-Enskog theory to achieve a desired temperature-dependent \(\eta/s(T)\). They simulate the fireball expansion for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV and 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, using initial conditions that reproduce the measured charged particle multiplicities and initial temperature profiles that scale with energy density. They compare the resulting differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) for several parametrizations of \(\eta/s(T)\): a constant value of \(1/4\pi\), a linear increase with temperature, a quadratic increase, and a linear increase combined with a rise in the cross-over region.

## Key numbers

* The initial maximum temperatures \(T_{m0}\) are 290 MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV, 340 MeV at 200 GeV, and 560 MeV at 2.76 TeV.
* A constant \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) throughout the evolution produces a breaking of the \(v_2(p_T)\) invariance of about 20% between RHIC and LHC energies.
* The lifetime of the stage at \(T > T_c\) is about \(4-6\) fm/c at RHIC energies and about 10 fm/c at LHC.
* The linear parametrization \(4\pi\eta/s = T/T_0\) yields an almost perfect invariance of \(v_2(p_T)\) within 5% across the three beam energies, in agreement with experimental data from STAR and ALICE.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow from a beam energy scan, motivated by the observation by the STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk 2012) and the ALICE Collaboration (Aamodt 2010) that \(v_2(p_T)\) is nearly invariant from 62.4 GeV to 2.76 TeV. It builds on prior estimates of \(\eta/s\) from chiral perturbation theory for a meson gas (Prakash 1993, Chen 2007), transport analyses (Demir 2008), and lattice QCD calculations (Meyer 2007), which suggested a low \(\eta/s\) near \(T_c\) but a higher value in the hadronic phase. The results provide the first phenomenological constraint on the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) and support the expectation from Csernai (2006) and Lacey (2006) that a minimum in \(\eta/s\) signals a phase transition. This enables future studies of higher harmonics like \(v_3\) and extensions to lower beam energies where the baryon chemical potential is non-negligible.
