# Dilepton emission as a novel probe of QCD critical point

> In this work, we propose dilepton emission rate (DER) as a sensitive probe of QCD critical point based on the extended Polyakov-quark-meson model. The model could successfully capture two main mechanisms for dilepton production, $π^\pm$ and quark-antiquark annihilations on one hand, and self-consistently account for chiral transition and (de-)confinement on the other hand. Along the chemical freezeout lines, all the moments of the DER peak show similar extremal features as those of light quark mass, thus the DER can well reflect the change of chiral symmetry and criticality. More importantly, the DER can be directly measured in heavy ion collisions: Compared to the baryon number fluctuations, the DER fluctuations are found to be more drastic in the critical region and more sensitive to the relative location of the critical point. However, recent statistics of DER in heavy ion collision is not large enough to perform event-by-event measurement, instead we propose to study the baseline subtracted DER: For a given dilepton center of mass, the largest deviation shows up around the point where the light quark mass fluctuation is the strongest.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the dilepton emission rate (DER) and its fluctuations are a more sensitive probe of the QCD critical point than baryon number fluctuations, but they acknowledge that current heavy-ion collision statistics are insufficient for event-by-event measurements, so they instead propose studying the baseline-subtracted DER as an alternative signal.

## Method

The authors extended the 2+1 flavor Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model by including vector meson degrees of freedom (rho, omega, phi, and axial-vector mesons) with quark-vector and scalar-vector interactions to maintain approximate SU(2) chiral symmetry. They computed the dilepton emission rate from two mechanisms: perturbative quark-antiquark annihilations through QED and nonperturbative charged pion annihilations through the rho^0 meson current via the vector dominance model. The quark masses, Polyakov loop, and pion mass were solved self-consistently from coupled gap equations at mean field level along two realistic chemical freezeout lines, and these quantities were used as input to evaluate the DER numerically.

## Key numbers

- The critical point in the model is located at (mu_Bc, T_c) = (635, 93) MeV.
- The pseudocritical temperature at mu_B = 0 is T_pc = 159 MeV.
- The vacuum rho meson mass is m_rho = 770 MeV, with decay width Gamma_rho = 155 MeV.
- The scalar-vector coupling g_sv = 6.07 and quark-vector coupling g_vq = 1.55 were fixed to reproduce the vacuum rho decay width and the DER peak location (~m_rho) in high energy collisions.
- The light sigma mass is m_sigma_l = 500 MeV.
- Along the freezeout line with n=2, the DER decreases with increasing mu_B, and the peak curvatures kappa_p decrease with mu_B, consistent with broadening.
- The quantity S_R sigma_R (derived from DER moments) is at least one order larger than the typical kappa sigma^2 values of around 5 from baryon number fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov-quark-meson model previously used by Cao (2025) to study the QCD phase diagram, and builds on the vector dominance model approach of Gale (1990) and Rapp (1999) for dilepton production. The authors compare their results to lattice QCD predictions from Bellwied (2015) and the HotQCD Collaboration (2018), as well as functional QCD approaches by Fu (2019) and Gao (2020). The proposed baseline-subtracted DER method is motivated by the limited statistics in current Beam Energy Scan experiments at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2021-2025), and the authors look forward to the CBM experiment at FAIR with higher lepton illumination.
