# Mass spectra and Regge trajectories of light mesons in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> We extend the calculation of relativistic bound-states of a fermion anti-fermion pair in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism to the case of total angular momentum $J=3$. Together with results for $J \le 2$ this allows for the investigation of Regge trajectories in this approach. We exemplify such a study for ground and excited states of light unflavored mesons as well as strange mesons within the rainbow-ladder approximation. For the $ρ$- and $ϕ$-meson we find a linear Regge trajectory within numerical accuracy. Discrepancies with experiment in other channels highlight the need to go beyond rainbow-ladder and to consider effects such as state mixing and more sophisticated quark-antiquark interaction kernels.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Bethe-Salpeter formalism to mesons with total angular momentum J=3, enabling the first explicit study of Regge trajectories within the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework. They find that the rainbow-ladder truncation produces linear Regge trajectories for the rho and phi mesons within numerical accuracy, but discrepancies in other channels indicate that the truncation materially overestimates binding in the tensor part of the spin-spin interaction, a different conclusion than drawn in prior work by Qin (2011).

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark bound states using the rainbow-ladder truncation with the Maris-Tandy model interaction. They construct covariant basis decompositions for mesons with J=0,1,2,3, including a new explicit basis for J=3 states using symmetric, traceless rank-3 tensors built from transverse projectors. Quark propagators are obtained by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation at complex momenta using Cauchy's integral theorem and barycentric rational interpolation to access masses beyond the standard parabolic contour.

## Key numbers

- Up/down current quark mass: m_u = m_d = 0.0037 GeV at renormalization scale μ = 19 GeV
- Strange quark mass: m_s(19 GeV) = 0.085 GeV, fixed by matching the experimental kaon mass
- Model parameters: Λ = 0.72 GeV, η = 1.8 ± 0.2, Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV
- J=3 tensor meson (3⁻⁻) mass for n̄n: 1528⁺⁷¹₋₁₈₄ MeV, compared to experimental ρ₃ at 1688.8 ± 2.1 MeV and ω₃ at 1667 ± 4 MeV (deviation ~5% for upper η-band)
- J=3 negative parity strange meson (K₃*): 1646.9 MeV (found only for η=2.0), compared to experimental 1776 ± 7 MeV (within 10%)
- J=3 positive parity strange meson: 1673.4 MeV (no established experimental counterpart)
- No bound state found in J^PC = 3⁺⁻ channel for n̄n
- Excited state masses deviate by 5-10% between the two extrapolation methods (Cauchy integral and barycentric rational interpolation)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to mesons with J=3, following prior basis constructions for J≤2 by Krassnigg (2010) and Maris (1999). The results confirm earlier findings by Holl (2004) that excited states in rainbow-ladder are generally too low, but provide a new interpretation: the rainbow-ladder truncation reproduces the contact spin-spin interaction and spin-orbit force but overestimates binding from the tensor spin-spin interaction, differing from the conclusion of Qin (2011) based on a smaller set of states. The study enables future investigations of Regge trajectories using more sophisticated interaction kernels beyond rainbow-ladder, such as those explored by Fischer (2009) and Chang (2011).
