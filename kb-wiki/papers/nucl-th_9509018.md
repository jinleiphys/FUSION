# Form factors in an algebraic model of the nucleon

> We study the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in a collective model of baryons. In an algebraic approach to hadron structure, we derive closed expressions for both elastic and transition form factors, and consequently for the helicity amplitudes that can be measured in electro- and photoproduction.

# Form factors in an algebraic model of the nucleon

## Key claim
The electromagnetic form factors and helicity amplitudes for nonstrange baryon resonances can be derived in closed form within a collective algebraic model of the nucleon, but only in the limit of an infinitely large model space (N → ∞) and under the assumption that the nucleon has an oblate symmetric top string configuration with D₃ₕ point group symmetry.

## Method
The authors construct a U(7) spectrum generating algebra for the six spatial degrees of freedom of the three constituent parts of the nucleon, using six dipole bosons and one scalar boson. They combine this geometric U(7) structure with SUₛ𝒻(6) ⊗ SU𝒸(3) spin-flavor-color symmetry, then derive closed-form expressions for the matrix elements of the electromagnetic transition operators Û and T̂₊ by projecting rotational states from an intrinsic coherent state and taking the large-N limit. The spatial matrix elements are folded with a probability distribution g(β) = β² exp(-β/a)/2a³ to obtain the final form factors.

## Key numbers
- The r.m.s. deviation of the mass spectrum description of nonstrange baryons is 39 MeV.
- The electric form factor of the proton is given by Gᴘᴇ = 1/(1 + k²a²)², where a is a scale parameter determined from the proton charge radius.
- The proton helicity amplitudes for the N(1520)D₁₃ resonance are A₁/₂ = 2iμ √(π/k₀) [m₃k₀a/g₃ - k²a]/(1 + k²a²)² and A₃/₂ = 2iμ √(3π/k₀) m₃k₀a/g₃(1 + k²a²)².
- The asymmetry parameter A = (A₁/₂² - A₃/₂²)/(A₁/₂² + A₃/₂²) changes rapidly from -1 to +1 with increasing momentum transfer.

## Context
This work extends the algebraic collective model for baryons introduced by the same authors (Bijker and Leviatan, 1995) which described the mass spectrum of nonstrange baryons. It contrasts with the relativized quark potential model (Capstick, 1992) which uses a small number of low-lying configurations, whereas the collective model produces wave functions spread over many harmonic oscillator shells. The predicted power-law falloff of form factors with momentum transfer differs from the exponential decrease predicted by harmonic oscillator quark models, and these predictions can be tested at facilities such as MAMI and CEBAF.
