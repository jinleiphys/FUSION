# Incomplete electromagnetic response of hot QCD matter

> The electromagnetic response of hot QCD matter to decaying external magnetic fields is investigated. We examine the validity of Ohm's law and find that the induced electric current increases from zero and relaxes towards the value from Ohm's law. The relaxation time is larger than the lifetime of the external magnetic field for the QCD matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The lower than expected electric current significantly suppresses the induced magnetic field and makes the electromagnetic response incomplete. We demonstrate the incomplete electromagnetic response of hot QCD matter by calculations employing the parton transport model combined with the solution of Maxwell's equations. Our results show a strong suppression by two orders of magnitude in the magnetic field, relatively to calculations assuming the validity of Ohm's law. This may undermine experimental efforts to measure magnetic-field-related effects in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Ohm's law fails for hot QCD matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions because the induced electric current requires a relaxation time (~1 fm/c) that is larger than the lifetime of the external magnetic field (~0.06 fm/c), causing the induced magnetic field to be suppressed by two orders of magnitude relative to calculations that assume Ohm's law holds.

## Method

The authors used the parton cascade model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings) to solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation for a thermal quark-gluon system with u and d quarks and gluons at T=255 MeV, combined with the Liénard-Wiechert potentials for electromagnetic fields from moving quarks and Maxwell's equations for the total fields. They considered two scenarios: a linearly decaying external magnetic field from an idealized solenoid, and a more realistic setup with two point-like nuclei (charge q=79e) moving at √s=200 GeV with impact parameter b=7.6 fm, using binary collisions with constant isotropic cross sections of 1 and 2 mb.

## Key numbers

- External magnetic field maximum: 5mπ² ≈ 10¹⁸ G in Au+Au at RHIC and 70mπ² ≈ 10¹⁹ G in Pb+Pb at LHC
- Lifetime of external magnetic field: R_A/γ ≲ 0.06 fm/c at top RHIC and LHC energies
- Relaxation time τ_j = 1.12 fm/c for σ_el/T = 0.03 and T = 255 MeV with two quark flavors
- For σ_22 = 1 mb: σ_el = 11.6 MeV, τ_j = 1.72 fm/c, η/s = 0.44
- For σ_22 = 2 mb: σ_el = 5.8 MeV, τ_j = 0.86 fm/c, η/s = 0.22
- BAMPS results show electric current peaks almost twentyfold lower than Ohm's law predictions for the realistic two-nuclei scenario
- Induced magnetic field from BAMPS after 0.5 fm/c is smaller than the maximal external field at t=0 by three orders of magnitude
- At t=0.5 fm/c, the induced magnetic field from Ohm's law is larger than the external field by two orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Tuchin (2013) and Gursoy (2014) that used Ohm's law to calculate induced magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions, and contradicts the assumption that the electromagnetic response is instantaneous. The paper demonstrates that the relaxation time of the electric current, derived from the Drude model, is comparable to or larger than the QGP formation time (~0.6 fm/c) and the external field lifetime, making the incomplete response significant. The results suggest that experimental searches for magnetic-field-related phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect (Fukushima 2008, Kharzeev 2007) and D meson directed flow splitting (Das 2016, Chatterjee 2018) may be more challenging than previously thought.
