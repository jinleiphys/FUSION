# Fate of the neutron-deuteron virtual state as an Efimov level

> The emergence of Efimov levels in a three-body system is investigated near the unitarity limit characterized by resonating two-body interaction. No direct evidence of Efimov levels is seen in the three-nucleon system since the triton is the only physical bound state. We provide a model-independent analysis of nucleon-deuteron scattering at low energy by formulating a consistent effective field theory. We show that virtual states evolve into shallow bound states, which emerge as excited triton levels as we drive the system towards unitarity. Even though we consider this specific system, our results for the emergence of the Efimov levels are universal.

## Key claim

The neutron-deuteron virtual state in the spin-doublet channel evolves into a shallow bound state, the first excited state of the triton, as the two-nucleon scattering lengths are driven toward the unitarity limit, demonstrating that this virtual state is a remnant of an Efimov level. The authors show this evolution is universal for any three-body system with resonating zero-range two-body interactions where at least one channel supports a two-body bound state, but only when the system is artificially driven toward unitarity; at physical nuclear parameters no excited Efimov state exists.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT) at leading order to compute neutron-deuteron scattering phase shifts in the spin-doublet channel, fitting the three-nucleon contact interaction to reproduce the doublet scattering length \(a_3 = 0.65\) fm at the physical point. They then artificially vary the two-nucleon scattering lengths by tuning the parameters \(g_t\) (controlling the deuteron binding momentum) and \(g_s\) (controlling the \(^1S_0\) scattering length) toward the unitarity limit \(g_t \to 0, g_s = 0\), while keeping the three-body parameter fixed. A separate halo EFT, treating the deuteron as an elementary particle, is constructed to provide a model-independent description of the low-energy scattering via a modified effective range expansion, and the analytic structure of the \(S\)-matrix is analyzed to track the evolution of poles from virtual to bound states.

## Key numbers

- Physical triton binding energy: \(B_3 \simeq 8.48\) MeV.
- Deuteron binding momentum at physical point: \(\gamma \simeq 45.7\) MeV.
- Two-nucleon \(^3S_1\) scattering length at physical point: \(a_t \simeq 5.4\) fm.
- Two-nucleon \(^1S_0\) scattering length at physical point: \(a_s \simeq -23.714\) fm.
- Doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length at physical point: \(a_3 = 0.65\) fm.
- At the physical point (\(g_t = g_s = 1\)), the modified ERE fit gives a virtual state at binding momentum \(\kappa_1 \approx -26.8\) MeV, corresponding to a binding energy \(\approx 0.574\) MeV.
- At the unitarity point (\(g_t = 0, g_s = 0\)), the ratio of binding momenta between the triton and the first-excited state gives the geometric factor 22.7 predicted by Efimov.
- The deuteron breakup momentum is \(2\gamma/\sqrt{3} \simeq 53\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the separable potential model analysis of Adhikari et al. (1982), who first suggested the connection between the neutron-deuteron virtual state and the Efimov spectrum, by providing a model-independent effective field theory treatment. It also builds on the pionless EFT framework developed by Bedaque et al. (1999) and Hammer et al. (2001) for three-nucleon systems. The results support the picture proposed by Konig et al. (2016) that nuclear ground states beyond the deuteron are characterized by a momentum scale intermediate between the pion mass and the inverse two-nucleon scattering lengths, and the halo EFT formulated here could enable model-independent extraction of doublet effective range expansion parameters for proton-deuteron scattering and for radiative capture reactions relevant to big-bang nucleosynthesis.
