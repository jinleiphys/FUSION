# Outer-Crust Equations of State for Neutron Stars

> The equation of state of the neutron-star outer crust is sensitive to nuclear mass predictions and provides a direct connection to properties of nuclei throughout the nuclide map, including those beyond experimental reach. We quantify the impact of contemporary nuclear mass models on the composition and thermodynamic properties of the outer crust, and assess the consequences for crust-dominated neutron-star configurations near the minimum-mass limit. We constructed four outer-crust equations of state based on the relativistic energy density functional and machine-learning mass model tables. The equilibrium composition of cold catalyzed matter in $β$-equilibrium was obtained by minimizing the Gibbs free energy per baryon, and the resulting equations of state were implemented in neutron-star structure calculations. The different mass inputs lead to variations in the equilibrium nuclide sequence, distinct last bound nuclei, and moderate shifts in the neutron-drip density. In contrast, the associated thermodynamic properties, as well as the minimum-mass neutron-star configurations, remain closely aligned across the four outer-crust equations of state. The model dependence of the outer crust is primarily reflected in the detailed nuclide composition and in the precise location of neutron drip. Nevertheless, the considered outer-crust equations of state yield closely consistent predictions for the relevant neutron-star observables, providing a reliable input for stellar modelling.

## Key claim
The four outer-crust equations of state based on different nuclear mass models yield closely consistent predictions for neutron-star observables such as minimum mass and radius, despite moderate variations in the equilibrium nuclide sequence and neutron-drip density; the model dependence is primarily confined to the detailed composition and the precise location of neutron drip, not to the bulk stellar properties.

## Method
The equilibrium composition of cold catalyzed matter in β-equilibrium was obtained by minimizing the Gibbs free energy per baryon, including nuclear mass, Coulomb lattice, and electron gas contributions, using four nuclear mass tables: three relativistic energy density functionals (DD-ME2, DD-PC1, DD-PCX) and one machine-learning model (ELMA). The resulting outer-crust equations of state were combined with an inner crust described either by the SLy interaction or a polytropic parametrization, and a uniform liquid core described by the DD-PC-J31 EOS, then implemented in Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff and Hartle-Thorne calculations for neutron-star structure.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-drip baryon density ranges from 2.436 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (DD-PCX) to 2.642 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (DD-PC1), a relative spread of about 8%.
- Neutron-drip pressure ranges from 4.753 × 10⁻⁴ MeV fm⁻³ (DD-PCX) to 5.047 × 10⁻⁴ MeV fm⁻³ (DD-PC1), a spread below 6%.
- Neutron-drip energy density ranges from 2.284 × 10⁻¹ MeV fm⁻³ (DD-PCX) to 2.477 × 10⁻¹ MeV fm⁻³ (DD-PC1).
- Electron chemical potential at neutron drip varies by less than 2%, from 25.992 MeV (DD-PCX) to 26.372 MeV (DD-PC1).
- The machine-learning model ELMA achieves a root-mean-square error of ~65 keV with respect to the AME2020 mass table.
- Minimum gravitational mass configurations cluster around ~0.087–0.090 M⊙, consistent with prior work.
- Deviations from the BPS equation of state remain at the level of ~5% across the outer-crust density range.

## Context
This work extends the seminal outer-crust formalism of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) by incorporating three modern relativistic energy density functional mass tables and a machine-learning mass model (ELMA), updating earlier studies that used Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations (Haensel 1989) or the 1992 Atomic Mass Evaluation (Haensel 1994). The constructed equations of state provide a low-density benchmark for future unified neutron-star equation-of-state calculations within the same theoretical framework, and the publicly available tables enable consistent modelling of crust-dominated neutron-star configurations near the minimum-mass limit.
