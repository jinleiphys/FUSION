# Correlation between the charged kaon ratio and the baryon phase-space density in heavy-ion collisions

> It is found that the baryon phase-space density obtained from the ratio of deuteron to proton yields is nearly constant over centrality in Au+Au collisions at the AGS. The finding offers an explanation for the puzzling centrality independence of the ratio of charged kaon total yields. The correlation between the charged kaon ratio and the average baryon phase-space density is studied for central heavy-ion collisions of various systems over a wide range of beam energy. It is found that the charged kaon ratio and the average baryon phase-space density both increase with decreasing beam energy, and are strongly correlated. Such study may provide a new approach to search for medium effect on the kaon mass.

# Correlation between the charged kaon ratio and the baryon phase-space density in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The average baryon phase-space density at kinetic freeze-out, extracted from deuteron-to-proton ratios, is nearly constant over centrality in Au+Au collisions at the AGS, offering an explanation for the puzzling centrality independence of the charged kaon total yield ratio. The charged kaon ratio and the average baryon phase-space density are strongly correlated across different collision systems and beam energies, but the author cautions that no conclusion can be drawn from the present study regarding medium modification to the kaon mass in Ni+Ni collisions at the SIS.

## Method
The average baryon phase-space density at kinetic freeze-out was extracted from deuteron-to-proton yield ratios using the coalescence model, with corrections for isospin asymmetry (proton-to-neutron ratio), antibaryon abundance (antiproton-to-proton ratio), and hyperon contributions (from strangeness conservation). The charged kaon ratio (K+/K-) was then correlated with the extracted baryon phase-space density for central heavy-ion collisions across systems from SIS (Ni+Ni), AGS (Si+Al, Si+Au, Au+Au), and SPS (S+S, Pb+Pb) energies, and the data were fit to a power-law function.

## Key numbers
- In Au+Au collisions at 11.6 AGeV/c, the average baryon phase-space density at kinetic freeze-out is nearly constant over centrality, with the hyperon correction factor alpha_Y ranging from 1.03 in peripheral to 1.13 in central collisions.
- The power-law fit to the correlation between K+/K- and baryon phase-space density yields: K+/K- = 1 + (f_Bth / (0.0076 +/- 0.0009))^(1.20 +/- 0.10).
- The KaoS Collaboration observed a K+/K- ratio in Ni+Ni collisions at SIS that is about seven times smaller than in p+p near threshold, and inferred a K- mass drop of 270 (+55/-90) MeV/c^2.
- In C+C collisions at the SIS, a large K+/K- ratio of 39 +/- 6 was observed (no deuteron-to-proton data available for baryon phase-space density extraction).

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Anisovich and Bjorken, and the equilibrium model studies of Bialas and Zimanyi, which relate particle ratios to baryon phase-space density at chemical freeze-out. The author notes that the KaoS Collaboration (1990s) had previously inferred a significant drop in the K- effective mass from Ni+Ni data, while different conclusions were reached by Brown and Koch depending on model assumptions. The study enables a new approach to search for kaon medium modifications by comparing the K+/K- versus baryon phase-space density correlation across beam energies, with new data from the E895 Collaboration at AGS low energies and SPS at 40 AGeV identified as needed to fill the gap between AGS and SIS.
