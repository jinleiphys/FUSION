# Neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering off Xe isotopes

> Large liquid xenon detectors aiming for dark matter direct detection will soon become viable tools also for investigating neutrino physics. Information on the effects of nuclear structure in neutrino-nucleus scattering can be important in distinguishing neutrino backgrounds in such detectors. We perform calculations for differential and total cross sections of neutral-current neutrino scattering off the most abundant xenon isotopes. The nuclear structure calculations are made in the nuclear shell model for elastic scattering, and also in the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and microscopic quasiparticle phonon model (MQPM) for both elastic and inelastic scattering. Using suitable neutrino energy distributions, we compute estimates of total averaged cross sections for 8B solar neutrinos and supernova neutrinos.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first complete microscopic nuclear-structure calculations of neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections for the most abundant xenon isotopes other than \(^{136}\)Xe, using three different nuclear models (shell model, QRPA, and MQPM). The authors note that the results for the two quasiparticle-based approaches (QRPA/MQPM) and the shell model are very similar for coherent scattering, but that the inelastic scattering results contain a potentially spurious large \(0^+\) multipole contribution that should be regarded with caution.

## Method

The authors computed differential and total cross sections for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering off the xenon isotopes \(^{128,129,130,131,132,134,136}\)Xe. For elastic (coherent) scattering, they used both the nuclear shell model (with the SN100PN interaction in a valence space of orbitals \(0g_{7/2}\), \(1d_{5/2}\), \(1d_{3/2}\), \(2s_{1/2}\), and \(0h_{11/2}\)) and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with a large model space including major shells up to \(0i_{13/2}\) and \(0i_{11/2}\). For inelastic scattering, they used the QRPA for even-mass isotopes and the microscopic quasiparticle phonon model (MQPM) for odd-mass isotopes \(^{129}\)Xe and \(^{131}\)Xe. The cross sections were then folded with neutrino energy distributions for \(^{8}\)B solar neutrinos and for supernova neutrinos (parameterized as Fermi-Dirac spectra with two different temperatures for electron and muon/tau flavors).

## Key numbers

* Coherent elastic cross section for \(^{136}\)Xe at \(E_\nu = 5\) MeV: \(6.40 \times 10^{-40}\) cm\(^2\)/MeV (shell model).
* Coherent elastic cross section for \(^{128}\)Xe at \(E_\nu = 80\) MeV: \(3.75 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\)/MeV (shell model).
* Total averaged coherent cross section for \(^{136}\)Xe with \(^{8}\)B solar neutrinos: \(1.320 \times 10^{-39}\) cm\(^2\) (both shell model and QRPA).
* Total averaged coherent cross section for \(^{136}\)Xe with supernova electron neutrinos: \(3.773 \times 10^{-39}\) cm\(^2\) (both shell model and QRPA).
* Total averaged coherent cross section for \(^{136}\)Xe with supernova muon/tau neutrinos: \(7.016 \times 10^{-39}\) cm\(^2\) (both shell model and QRPA).
* Total averaged inelastic cross section for \(^{131}\)Xe with \(^{8}\)B solar neutrinos: \(3.699 \times 10^{-43}\) cm\(^2\) (MQPM).
* Total averaged inelastic cross section for \(^{131}\)Xe with supernova electron neutrinos: \(5.414 \times 10^{-42}\) cm\(^2\) (MQPM).
* Inelastic cross section for \(^{128}\)Xe at \(E_\nu = 5\) MeV: \(1.71 \times 10^{-45}\) cm\(^2\)/MeV (QRPA).
* Inelastic cross section for \(^{136}\)Xe at \(E_\nu = 80\) MeV: \(1.67 \times 10^{-39}\) cm\(^2\)/MeV (QRPA).
* Experimental ground-state magnetic moment of \(^{129}\)Xe: \(-0.7779763(84)\) \(\mu_\mathrm{N}\); shell-model value: \(-1.360\) \(\mu_\mathrm{N}\).
* Experimental ground-state magnetic moment of \(^{131}\)Xe: \(+0.691862(4)\) \(\mu_\mathrm{N}\); shell-model value: \(+1.059\) \(\mu_\mathrm{N}\).

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA calculations for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering on \(^{136}\)Xe (Ydrefors 2015) and on cadmium isotopes (Almosly 2015) and molybdenum isotopes (Ydrefors 2012) to a complete set of stable xenon isotopes. The results are directly relevant for next-generation liquid xenon dark matter detectors (such as XENON, LUX, LZ, and DARWIN), which will soon encounter coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering as an irreducible background. The computed cross sections enable more accurate estimates of the neutrino floor for WIMP searches and can help in distinguishing neutrino-induced events from dark matter signals using directional or timing information.
