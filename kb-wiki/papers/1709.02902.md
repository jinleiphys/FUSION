# Shear viscosity of a pion gas due to $ŽüŽĆŽĆ$ and $ŽāŽĆŽĆ$ interactions

> We have calculated the shear viscosity of pion medium, where propagating pion has some finite thermal width due to interaction with the low mass resonances, $Žā$ and $Žü$. With the help of standard thermal field theoretical technique, the thermal width of the pion has been calculated from the pion self-energy diagram for $ŽĆŽā$ and $ŽĆŽü$ loops, where an effective Lagrangian density has been used for interaction part. We have found a very small value of shear viscosity by entropy density ratio ($╬Ę/s$), which is very close to the KSS bound.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (╬Ę/s) of a pion gas, calculated from the thermal width of pions due to interactions with Žā and Žü resonances, is very close to the KSS bound of 1/(4ŽĆ), but this result depends on including both ŽĆŽā and ŽĆŽü loops to avoid divergent behavior in different temperature regions.

## Method

The thermal width of the pion was calculated from the imaginary part of the pion self-energy at finite temperature for ŽĆŽā and ŽĆŽü loops, using an effective Lagrangian density with coupling constants g_Žü = 6 and g_Žā = 5.82 fixed from experimental decay widths. The self-energy results were folded with Breit-Wigner type spectral functions of the Žā and Žü resonances to account for their vacuum decay widths in the ŽĆŽĆ channel. The shear viscosity was then obtained from the relaxation time approximation using the momentum-dependent thermal width and Bose-Einstein distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_Žü = 6 and g_Žā = 5.82, fixed from experimental decay widths of Žü and Žā mesons
- Thermal width calculations performed at T = 0.170 GeV and T = 0.150 GeV, shown as functions of pion momentum
- Shear viscosity ╬Ę becomes divergent for T > 0.100 GeV from ŽĆŽü loops alone and for T < 0.100 GeV from ŽĆŽā loops alone; both resonances are needed for a smooth result
- The ╬Ę/s ratio respects the KSS bound of 1/(4ŽĆ) throughout the hadronic temperature domain (approximately 0.100 GeV to 0.175 GeV)
- The critical temperature T_c is approximately 0.175 GeV

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Prakash (1993), Gavin (1985), and Mitra (2015) which found ╬Ę as a monotonically increasing function of temperature, and contrasts with the Kubo approach results of Lang (2012) which showed ╬Ę decreasing with temperature. The results are similar to those of Fraile (2013) after unitarization, which dynamically generated low mass resonances and produced an increasing ╬Ę(T) above T = 0.100 GeV, though the present calculation gives lower magnitude and increasing slope. The small ╬Ę/s values found here connect to the phenomenological work of Niemi (2011), who showed that such temperature-dependent ╬Ę/s values are needed to fit the elliptic flow parameter v_2(p_T) from RHIC data.
