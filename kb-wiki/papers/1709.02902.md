# Shear viscosity of a pion gas due to $ρππ$ and $σππ$ interactions

> We have calculated the shear viscosity of pion medium, where propagating pion has some finite thermal width due to interaction with the low mass resonances, $σ$ and $ρ$. With the help of standard thermal field theoretical technique, the thermal width of the pion has been calculated from the pion self-energy diagram for $πσ$ and $πρ$ loops, where an effective Lagrangian density has been used for interaction part. We have found a very small value of shear viscosity by entropy density ratio ($η/s$), which is very close to the KSS bound.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of a pion gas, calculated from the thermal width of pions due to interactions with σ and ρ resonances, is very close to the KSS bound of 1/(4π), but this result depends on including both πσ and πρ loops to avoid divergent behavior in different temperature regions.

## Method

The thermal width of the pion was calculated from the imaginary part of the pion self-energy at finite temperature for πσ and πρ loops, using an effective Lagrangian density with coupling constants g_ρ = 6 and g_σ = 5.82 fixed from experimental decay widths. The self-energy results were folded with Breit-Wigner type spectral functions of the σ and ρ resonances to account for their vacuum decay widths in the ππ channel. The shear viscosity was then obtained from the relaxation time approximation using the momentum-dependent thermal width and Bose-Einstein distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_ρ = 6 and g_σ = 5.82, fixed from experimental decay widths of ρ and σ mesons
- Thermal width calculations performed at T = 0.170 GeV and T = 0.150 GeV, shown as functions of pion momentum
- Shear viscosity η becomes divergent for T > 0.100 GeV from πρ loops alone and for T < 0.100 GeV from πσ loops alone; both resonances are needed for a smooth result
- The η/s ratio respects the KSS bound of 1/(4π) throughout the hadronic temperature domain (approximately 0.100 GeV to 0.175 GeV)
- The critical temperature T_c is approximately 0.175 GeV

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Prakash (1993), Gavin (1985), and Mitra (2015) which found η as a monotonically increasing function of temperature, and contrasts with the Kubo approach results of Lang (2012) which showed η decreasing with temperature. The results are similar to those of Fraile (2013) after unitarization, which dynamically generated low mass resonances and produced an increasing η(T) above T = 0.100 GeV, though the present calculation gives lower magnitude and increasing slope. The small η/s values found here connect to the phenomenological work of Niemi (2011), who showed that such temperature-dependent η/s values are needed to fit the elliptic flow parameter v_2(p_T) from RHIC data.
