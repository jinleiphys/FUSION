# Radiation pions in two-nucleon effective field theory

> For interactions involving two or more nucleons it is useful to divide pions into three classes: potential, radiation, and soft. The momentum threshold for the production of radiation pions is $Q_r = \sqrt{M_N m_π}$. We show that radiation pions can be included systematically with a power counting in $Q_r$. The leading order radiation pion graphs which contribute to NN scattering are evaluated in the PDS and OS renormalization schemes and are found to give a small contribution. The power counting for soft pion contributions is also discussed.

## Key claim

Radiation pions can be systematically included in two-nucleon effective field theory using a power counting in the scale \(Q_r = \sqrt{M_N m_\pi} = 360\ \mathrm{MeV}\), but the leading-order radiation pion contributions to NN scattering are found to be small, suppressed by a factor of roughly \(2(\gamma-\gamma')/M \sim 1/10\) relative to NLO contributions, and can be neglected at NNLO.

## Method

The authors classify pions into potential, radiation, and soft types based on their energy-momentum scaling, analogous to the treatment of gluons in NRQCD. They compute the leading-order radiation pion graphs (Fig. 1a–f) contributing to S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering, using both Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS) and on-shell (OS) renormalization schemes, with the \(C_0\) bubble chain treated nonperturbatively. The loop integrals are evaluated in \(n=3-2\epsilon\) dimensions, and ultraviolet divergences are cancelled by counterterms involving the \(D_2\) operator proportional to \(m_\pi^2\).

## Key numbers

- \(Q_r = \sqrt{M_N m_\pi} = 360\ \mathrm{MeV}\) is the momentum threshold for radiation pion production.
- The nucleon axial-vector coupling \(g_A = 1.25\); the pion decay constant \(f = 131\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The leading-order S-wave amplitude is \(A^{(-1)} = -4\pi/M \cdot 1/(\gamma + ip)\), where \(\gamma \sim 1/a\).
- The radiation pion graphs scale as \(Q_r^3/(M^3 \Lambda_\chi^2)\), suppressed relative to the leading amplitude by \(m_\pi^2/\Lambda_\chi^2\).
- The dominant effect of the \(Q_r^3\) graphs is suppressed by a factor of roughly \(2(\gamma-\gamma')/M \sim 1/10\) relative to NLO contributions from Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998).
- The beta function for \(D_2(\mu_R)\) from radiation pions is \(\beta_{D_2}^{(rad)} = (3 g_A^2)/(4\pi^2 f^2) \cdot (M(\gamma-\gamma'))/(4\pi) \cdot C_0(\mu_R)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the KSW power counting (Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1998) for two-nucleon effective field theory by systematically including radiation pions, which have a threshold scale \(Q_r\) larger than the typical chiral scale \(m_\pi\). The classification into potential, radiation, and soft pions parallels the treatment of gluons in NRQCD (Luke, Manohar, 1992; Grinstein, Rothstein, 1996; Labelle, 1996; Beneke, 1997). The results show that radiation pion effects at leading order are numerically small and can be absorbed into a redefinition of the \(D_2\) counterterm, enabling future calculations at momenta \(p \sim 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and processes with external pions.
