# Nuclear Flow Excitation Function

> We consider the dependence of collective flow on the nuclear surface thickness in a Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck transport model of heavy ion collisions. Well defined surfaces are introduced by giving test particles a Gaussian density profile of constant width. Zeros of the flow excitation function are as much influenced by the surface thickness as the nuclear equation of state, and the dependence of this effect is understood in terms of a simple potential scattering model. Realistic calculations must also take into account medium effects for the nucleon--nucleon cross section, and impact parameter averaging. We find that balance energy scales with the mass number as $A^{-y}$, where $y$ has a numerical value between 0.35 and 0.5, depending on the assumptions about the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Key claim

The balance energy (the beam energy at which collective flow disappears) is as strongly influenced by the nuclear surface thickness as by the nuclear equation of state, meaning that realistic BUU predictions must account for surface thickness, in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, and impact parameter averaging, not just the equation of state.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model simulations of heavy ion collisions, introducing well-defined nuclear surfaces by giving test particles a Gaussian density profile of constant width rather than the usual grid-based smearing. They also developed a simple potential scattering model to understand the surface dependence, and varied the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section using a density-dependent parameter α₁ to study its effect on the mass scaling of the balance energy.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³⁹La on ¹³⁹La collisions at 800 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b=2.7 fm, the flow ⟨wP_x⟩ with zero grid size (zero surface thickness) is twice as large as the value at Δx=1 fm.
- For ¹³⁹La on ¹³⁹La, the balance energy shifts by ≈10 MeV when the surface thickness increases from 1 fm to 2 fm.
- For ⁴⁰Ar on V, E_Bal changes by only 8 MeV when incompressibility is increased from K₀=200 MeV to 380 MeV.
- For ¹²C+¹²C with a stiff EOS, E_Bal ≈140 MeV (1 fm surface) and ≈190 MeV (2 fm surface); with a soft EOS, E_Bal ≈170 MeV (1 fm surface) and ≈220 MeV (2 fm surface).
- The experimental mass scaling exponent y for E_Bal = x A^{-y} is 0.33 ± 0.04.
- For α₁=0 (no medium modification), y=0.38 ± 0.05; for α₁=-0.1, y=0.42 ± 0.03; for α₁=-0.2, y=0.47 ± 0.03.
- Best overall agreement with experimental data is found for α₁=-0.2, corresponding to a 20% reduction of the nucleon-nucleon cross section at ρ=ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends prior BUU studies of flow disappearance (MOL, BER, OGI) by demonstrating that the nuclear surface thickness has an effect on the balance energy comparable to the equation of state, a factor previously overlooked. The findings enable more quantitative comparisons between BUU simulations and experimental data from the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (WES) and other facilities, but also show that impact parameter averaging and realistic density-dependent in-medium cross sections are necessary for reliable predictions.
