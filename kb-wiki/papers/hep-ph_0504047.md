# Quantitative study of the violation of kt-factorization in hadroproduction of quarks at collider energies

> We demonstrate the violation of kT-factorization for quark production in high energy hadronic collisions. This violation is quantified in the Color Glass Condensate framework and studied as a function of the quark mass, the quark transverse momentum, and the saturation scale Qsat, which is a measure of large parton densities. At x values where parton densities are large but leading twist shadowing effects are still small, violations of kT-factorization can be significant - especially for lighter quarks. At very small x, where leading twist shadowing is large, we show that violations of kT-factorization are relatively weaker.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that \(k_\perp\)-factorization is violated for quark production in high-energy hadronic collisions within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, with the violation being significant for lighter quarks at moderate small \(x\) where parton densities are high but leading twist shadowing is small, but relatively weaker at very small \(x\) where leading twist shadowing is large.

## Method

The authors compute the single inclusive quark production cross-section in proton-nucleus (p-A) collisions using the CGC formalism, which includes multiple scattering (higher twist) effects to all orders in the nuclear color charge density. They evaluate the relevant two-point and three-point Wilson line correlators in two Gaussian models: the local McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, valid at moderately small \(x\) (\(\sim 10^{-2}\)), and a non-local Gaussian mean field (GMF) model that incorporates leading twist shadowing at very small \(x\). The violation of \(k_\perp\)-factorization is quantified by comparing the full cross-section to the factorized result obtained by replacing the three-point correlator with a sum of delta functions that would restore factorization.

## Key numbers

* For strange quarks (\(m = 0.15\) GeV) in the MV model with \(Q_s^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\), the ratio of the full cross-section to the \(k_\perp\)-factorized result deviates significantly from unity, with maximal violation near \(q_\perp \sim Q_s\).
* For charm quarks (\(m = 1.5\) GeV) in the MV model, the violation is smaller than for strange quarks; at \(Q_s^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) the ratio shows a moderate enhancement at low \(q_\perp\) and suppression at high \(q_\perp\).
* For bottom quarks (\(m = 4.5\) GeV) in the MV model, the violation is even smaller, becoming significant only at large saturation scales such as \(Q_s^2 = 15\) or \(25\) GeV\(^2\).
* In the GMF model for charm quarks at \(Q_s^2 = 15\) GeV\(^2\), the violation is relatively weaker than in the MV model at the same \(Q_s^2\), with the ratio closer to unity.
* The width of the peaks in the three-point correlator \(\phi_A^{q\bar{q},g}\) is roughly of order the saturation momentum \(Q_s\), taken as \(Q_s^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) in the figure.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of gluon production in p-A collisions by Blaizot, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2004), which found that gluon production remains \(k_\perp\)-factorizable, to the case of quark production, where violation was shown explicitly by Blaizot, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2004) and Tuchin (2004). The results quantify the magnitude of this violation for the first time, using the MV model and a non-local Gaussian model of Iancu, Itakura, and McLerran (2002) to bracket the expected behavior between the regimes of moderate and very small \(x\). The findings enable more precise predictions for quark production at RHIC and LHC, particularly in forward p-p/p-A collisions and deuteron-nucleus collisions, and suggest that isolating these effects may require simultaneous study of other final states sensitive to three-point functions.
