# Strong magnetic fields: neutron stars with an extended inner crust

> Using relativistic mean-field models, the formation of clusterized matter, as the one expected to exist in the inner crust of neutron stars, is determined under the effect of strong magnetic fields. As already predicted from a calculation of the unstable modes resulting from density fluctuations at subsaturation densities, we confirm in the present work that for magnetic field intensities of the order of $\approx 5 \times 10^{16}$ G to $5 \times 10^{17}$ G, pasta phases may occur for densities well above the zero-field crust-core transition density. This confirms that the extension of the crust may be larger than expected. It is also verified that the equilibrium structure of the clusterized matter is very sensitive to the intensity of the magnetic fields. As a result, the decay of the magnetic field may give rise to internal stresses which may result on the yield and fracture of the inner crust lattice.

# Strong magnetic fields: neutron stars with an extended inner crust

## Key claim
Using relativistic mean-field models with a coexisting phases approach, the authors confirm that for magnetic field intensities of approximately 5 × 10^16 G to 5 × 10^17 G, pasta phases may occur at densities well above the zero-field crust-core transition density, extending the inner crust further than expected. The equilibrium structure of clusterized matter is very sensitive to the magnetic field intensity, and the decay of the magnetic field may give rise to internal stresses that could cause yield and fracture of the inner crust lattice.

## Method
The authors applied a coexisting phases (CP) method within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework to describe pasta phases in magnetized neutron star inner crust matter. They imposed Gibbs equilibrium conditions on pressure and chemical potentials between a dense cluster phase and a background gas phase, supplemented by Coulomb and finite-size effects including surface tension, and considered two nuclear models (NL3 and NL3ωρ) with different symmetry energy slopes (118 MeV and 55 MeV respectively) at fixed proton fractions (yp=0.02 for NL3, yp=0.035 for NL3ωρ). The calculations included the effect of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of nucleons for comparison.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field intensities considered: 4.4 × 10^16 G, 1.3 × 10^17 G, and 4.4 × 10^17 G (corresponding to B* = 10^3, 3 × 10^3, and 10^4, where B* = B/B_ce with B_ce = 4.41 × 10^13 G)
- For NL3 without AMM at B = 4.4 × 10^17 G, the clusterized region extends to densities above 0.1 fm^-3
- A second region of clusterized matter appears at densities of approximately 0.08-0.09 fm^-3 for the two weaker field intensities in NL3 without AMM
- The symmetry energy slope at saturation is 118 MeV for NL3 and 55 MeV for NL3ωρ
- The proton fraction at the crust-core transition is yp=0.02 for NL3 and yp=0.035 for NL3ωρ
- The energy scale of AMM effects is approximately ε = |κ_p + κ_n|B ≈ 2 × 10^-5 B* MeV

## Context
This work extends previous dynamical spinodal calculations by Fang (2016, 2017) and Chen (2017) that predicted unstable regions at supra-saturation densities under strong magnetic fields, and confirms those predictions using a coexisting phases approach. The results contrast with Thomas-Fermi calculations by Lima (2013) and Bao (2021), which did not investigate the region above the zero-field crust-core transition. The study enables future self-consistent Thomas-Fermi calculations under β-equilibrium and may help understand violent magnetar events, as the sensitivity of crust structure to magnetic field intensity could produce stresses and fractures during magnetic field decay, as discussed by Lander (2016, 2019, 2020).
