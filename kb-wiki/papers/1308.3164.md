# Microscopic description of nuclear vibrations: Relativistic QRPA and its extensions with quasiparticle-vibration coupling

> The recent extensions of the covariant energy density functional theory with the quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) are reviewed. Formulation of the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) in the relativistic framework is discussed. Self-consistent extensions of the relativistic QRPA imply the QVC which is implemented in two-body propagators in the nuclear medium. This provides fragmentation of the QRPA states describing the damping of the vibrational motion.

## Key claim

This review paper describes how the covariant energy density functional (CEDF) theory can be extended beyond the Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) by including quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) in a fully self-consistent way, which provides fragmentation of the QRPA states and describes the damping of vibrational motion in superfluid nuclei.

## Method

The authors formulate the Relativistic QRPA (RQRPA) in the Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov (DHB) basis as a small-amplitude limit of time-dependent relativistic mean field theory, using the covariant energy density functional with pairing correlations described by a monopole-monopole interaction. They then extend this framework by incorporating quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) into the nucleon self-energy and into the two-body propagators via the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA), which yields an algebraic equation for the response function that contains configurations up to 2-quasiparticle-plus-phonon.

## Key numbers

* The static effective interaction between quasiparticles is obtained as a functional derivative of the RMF self-energy with respect to the relativistic generalized density matrix, with the static interaction given by Eq. (static-interaction).
* The mean-field response function propagator has the form: R^(0)_(k1k2)(ω) = 1/(ηω - E_(k1) - E_(k2)), where E_(ki) are the Bogoliubov quasiparticle energies.
* The phonon vertices γ_μ are derived from transition densities R_μ and the static effective interaction via Eq. (phonon), with the phonon frequencies denoted Ω_μ.
* The dynamical part of the effective interaction Φ(ω) responsible for QVC contains energy denominators of the form ηω - E_(k1) - E_(k6) - Ω_μ and similar terms, with the subtraction of Φ(0) to avoid double counting of QVC correlations.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic QRPA framework first developed by Paar, Ring, and Niksic (2003) on the base of covariant energy density functional theory, and builds upon the Landau-Migdal theory for Fermi liquids combined with parameter-free field theory techniques developed by Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev (2008, 2010) and Litvinova (2012). The approach enables the description of fragmented vibrational states and damping mechanisms in open-shell nuclei, going beyond the standard RQRPA which only describes collective vibrations without fragmentation.
