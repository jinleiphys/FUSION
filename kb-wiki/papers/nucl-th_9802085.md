# The Nuclear Sigma Term in the Skyrme Model: Pion-Nucleus Interaction

> The nuclear sigma term is calculated including the nuclear matrix element of the derivative of the NN interaction with respect to the quark mass, $m_q\frac{\partial V_{NN}}{\partial m_q}$. The NN potential is evaluated in the skyrmion-skyrmion picture within the quantized product ansatz. The contribution of the NN potential to the nuclear sigma term provides repulsion to the pion-nucleus interaction. The strength of the s-wave pion-nucleus optical potential is estimated including such contribution. The results are consistent with the analysis of the experimental data.

# The Nuclear Sigma Term in the Skyrme Model: Pion-Nucleus Interaction

## Key claim
The contribution of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential to the nuclear sigma term provides repulsion to the s-wave pion-nucleus optical potential, and the calculated strength is consistent with experimental data analysis. However, the product ansatz used for the NN potential is only approximate and does not reproduce the central attraction in the mid-range, so the authors give up consistency and use experimental charge densities rather than self-consistent nuclear wave functions for heavier nuclei.

## Method
The authors calculate the derivative of the NN interaction with respect to the quark mass, \(m_q \frac{\partial V_{NN}}{\partial m_q}\), using the Skyrme model with the quantized product ansatz for skyrmion-skyrmion interaction. They relate this derivative to \(m_\pi^2 \frac{\partial V_{NN}}{\partial m_\pi^2}\) via the Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner (GOR) relation. For nuclei heavier than the deuteron, they use a modified Hartree approximation with experimental charge densities from electron elastic scattering to evaluate the matrix element of this derivative, neglecting spin-spin and tensor components which are more than one order of magnitude smaller than the central part.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon sigma term: 59.6 MeV for the parameter set \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, \(e = 4.0\) (compared to experimental value of about 45 MeV from Gasiorowicz (1991)).
- Deuteron contribution from NN potential to nuclear sigma term (\(\Sigma_{^2\text{H}}^V\)): \(-3.60\) MeV.
- \(^4\)He contribution from NN potential to nuclear sigma term (\(\Sigma_{^4\text{He}}^V\)): \(-34.5\) MeV.
- Deuteron total potential plus kinetic contribution (\(\Sigma_{^2\text{H}}^{V+K}\)): \(-4.46\) MeV.
- Effective isoscalar scattering length from pion-deuteron data (\(b_{0_{\text{eff}}}^{free}\)): \(-0.0099\) \(m_\pi^{-1}\), consistent with \(-0.0077(11)\) \(m_\pi^{-1}\) from Sigg et al. (1995) and \(-0.0083(38)\) \(m_\pi^{-1}\) from Koch (1986).
- Pion-\(^{12}\)C optical potential repulsion from the sigma term contribution: approximately 8 MeV in the central region, compared to the 15 to 30 MeV needed to fit pionic atoms.
- Quark condensate at normal nuclear density including the NN potential contribution: \(\langle \bar q q \rangle_{\rho_N} / \langle \bar q q \rangle_{\text{vac}} \approx 0.95\).

## Context
This work extends the treatment of the nuclear sigma term by Cohen (1991) and collaborators, who calculated the leading order contribution from nucleons alone, by adding the contribution from the derivative of the NN interaction with respect to quark mass. It contrasts with relativistic nuclear matter calculations by Li and Ko (1994), Delfino et al. (1995), and Brockman and Weise (1996), who included derivatives of meson masses but not the NN potential derivative. The results enable a more complete calculation of the quark condensate in nuclear matter and provide a mechanism for the repulsive isoscalar component of the pion-nucleus optical potential, which had been noted as an unexplained feature in analyses by Kluge (1991) and others.
