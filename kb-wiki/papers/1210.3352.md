# Calculating the Jet Transport Coefficient $\hat{q}$ in Lattice Gauge Theory

> The formalism of jet modification in the higher twist approach is modified to describe a hard parton propagating through a hot thermalized medium. The leading order contribution to the transverse momentum broadening of a high energy (near on-shell) quark in a thermal medium is calculated. This involves a factorization of the perturbative process of scattering of the quark from the non-perturbative transport coefficient. An operator product expansion of the non-perturbative operator product which represents $\hat{q}$ is carried out and related via dispersion relations to the expectation of local operators. These local operators are then evaluated in quenched SU(2) lattice gauge theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first-principles calculation of the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) in a thermal medium using quenched SU(2) lattice gauge theory, but the result is an exploratory estimate valid only under the condition that the leading-order term in the operator product expansion dominates, which occurs for temperatures between 400 and 600 MeV and for jets with virtuality much smaller than the hard scale.

## Method

The author modifies the higher-twist formalism for jet modification to describe a hard quark propagating through a hot thermalized medium, factorizing the perturbative scattering from the non-perturbative transport coefficient. An operator product expansion of the non-perturbative operator product representing \(\hat{q}\) is carried out and related via dispersion relations to expectation values of local operators, which are then evaluated on a lattice in quenched SU(2) gauge theory with spatial size \(n_S = n_T \times 4\) and temporal extent \(n_T\) from 3 to 6, using 5000 heat bath sweeps per point.

## Key numbers

* At \(T = 400\) MeV, the leading local operator expectation value is \(\langle F^{+ i} F^{+ i} \rangle = 0.01\) GeV\(^4\).
* For a jet with \(q^- = 20\) GeV traversing a lattice length of \(10\) GeV\(^{-1}\), the maximum virtuality without radiation is \(\mu^2 \simeq 1.4\) GeV\(^2\), giving \(Q^+ = 1.4/40\) GeV.
* Using \(\alpha_s(1.4\ \text{GeV}^2) = 0.375\), the resulting \(\hat{\bar{q}}\) for an SU(2) quark is \(0.134\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at \(T \sim 400\) MeV.
* For an SU(2) gluon, the corresponding \(\hat{q}_G\) is \(0.358\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at the same temperature, obtained by scaling by the Casimir factor \(C_A/C_F = 8/3\).

## Context

This work extends the operator definition of \(\hat{q}\) from earlier effective field theory approaches (Idilbi 2008, Benzke 2012) and provides the first lattice-based estimate of the transport coefficient, which had previously only been extracted phenomenologically. The result enables future calculations in full SU(3) QCD and at higher statistics, and it opens the possibility of computing other transport coefficients such as \(\hat{e}\) and \(\hat{e}_2\) using the same operator product expansion and dispersion relation framework.
