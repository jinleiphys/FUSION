# Delta-Hole Interaction in Nuclei and the Gamow-Teller Strength in ^90Nb

> The recently obtained experimental result for the quenching of the GT sum rule in the reaction ^90Zr(p,n)^90Nb is used to extract the value of the Landau-Migdal parameter g_{NΔ}', taking into account also the effect of the finite range meson exchange interactions. The extracted value is compared to the one obtained in the π+ρexchange model by explicitly taking into account the effects of antisymmetrization and short range correlations. Although the π+ρexchange model tends to give a somewhat stronger quenching than observed experimentally, the results are consistent within the experimental error bars if the quark model value for the parameter f_Δ/f_πis used.

# Delta-Hole Interaction in Nuclei and the Gamow-Teller Strength in ^90Nb

## Key claim

The experimentally observed quenching of the Gamow-Teller sum rule in ^90Nb is consistent with the meson exchange (π+ρ) picture for the Δ-hole interaction in nuclei, but only if the quark model value for the coupling ratio f_Δ/f_π is assumed; the Chew-Low model value gives quenching that is too strong compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors performed two types of perturbative calculations of the Gamow-Teller sum rule for the reaction ^90Zr(p,n)^90Nb including Δ-hole excitations in lowest order. In "type 1" calculations, the π+ρ exchange transition potential was used with explicit inclusion of exchange terms and short range correlations via a step function f(r)=Θ(r-c) with c=0.7 fm. In "type 2" calculations, only direct terms were considered using the bare meson exchange potentials supplemented by a Landau-Migdal contact interaction, and the value of g_{NΔ}' was adjusted to reproduce the type 1 results or the experimental quenching factor.

## Key numbers

- Experimental quenching factor from Wakasa et al.: (S_- - S_+)_exp / S_{-0} = 0.9 ± 0.05
- For the quark model value f_Δ/f_π = √(72/25), the π+ρ exchange model gives quenching factor S_-/S_{-0} = 0.86, within experimental error bars
- For the Chew-Low model value f_Δ/f_π = 2, the π+ρ exchange model gives quenching factor S_-/S_{-0} = 0.805, too strong compared to experiment
- Extracted g_{NΔ}' from fitting experimental quenching with only Landau-Migdal interaction: 0.18 ± 0.09 (quark model) and 0.13 ± 0.07 (Chew-Low model)
- Extracted g_{NΔ}' from fitting experimental quenching with Landau-Migdal plus π+ρ exchange: 0.27 ± 0.09 (quark model) and 0.21 ± 0.07 (Chew-Low model)
- Harmonic oscillator parameter: ℏω = 9.23 MeV (b = 2.12 fm)
- Single particle sum rule value: S_{-0} = 3(N-Z) = 30 for ^90Zr

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Suzuki and Sakai (1999), who extracted g_{NΔ}' from the same experimental data using only a delta-function Landau-Migdal interaction, obtaining values around 0.18 (quark model) or 0.12 (Chew-Low model). The present paper shows that including finite-range π+ρ exchange potentials increases the required g_{NΔ}' by about 0.1, making it consistent with the meson exchange picture of Arima et al. (1999) for the quark model case. The results enable discrimination between the quark model and Chew-Low model values of f_Δ/f_π, favoring the quark model, and demonstrate that nuclear surface effects (attenuation factor γ ≈ 0.5) are important for extracting Landau-Migdal parameters from finite nuclei.
