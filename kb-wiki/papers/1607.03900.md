# The primordial deuterium abundance of the most metal-poor damped Lyman-alpha system

> We report the discovery and analysis of the most metal-poor damped Lyman-alpha (DLA) system currently known, which also displays the Lyman series absorption lines of neutral deuterium. The average [O/H] abundance of this system is [O/H] = -2.804 +/- 0.015, which includes an absorption component with [O/H] = -3.07 +/- 0.03. Despite the unfortunate blending of many weak D I absorption lines, we report a precise measurement of the deuterium abundance of this system. Using the six highest quality and self-consistently analyzed measures of D/H in DLAs, we report tentative evidence for a subtle decrease of D/H with increasing metallicity. This trend must be confirmed with future high precision D/H measurements spanning a range of metallicity. A weighted mean of these six independent measures provides our best estimate of the primordial abundance of deuterium, 10^5 (D/H)_P = 2.547 +/- 0.033 (log_10 (D/H)_P = -4.5940 +/- 0.0056). We perform a series of detailed Monte Carlo calculations of Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) that incorporate the latest determinations of several key nuclear cross sections, and propagate their associated uncertainty. Combining our measurement of (D/H)_P with these BBN calculations yields an estimate of the cosmic baryon density, 100 Omega_B,0 h^2(BBN) = 2.156 +/- 0.020, if we adopt the most recent theoretical determination of the d(p,gamma)3He reaction rate. This measure of Omega_B,0 h^2 differs by ~2.3 sigma from the Standard Model value estimated from the Planck observations of the cosmic microwave background. Using instead a d(p,gamma)3He reaction rate that is based on the best available experimental cross section data, we estimate 100 Omega_B,0 h^2(BBN) = 2.260 +/- 0.034, which is in somewhat better agreement with the Planck value. Forthcoming measurements of the crucial d(p,gamma)3He cross section may shed further light on this discrepancy.

## Key claim

The paper reports the discovery of the most metal-poor damped Lyman-alpha system known and uses it to measure the primordial deuterium abundance, but the abstract's claim of a "precise measurement" is hedged by the fact that many weak D I absorption lines are blended with unrelated features, making this system less ideal for D/H determination than previously reported cases.

## Method

The authors obtained high-resolution spectra of quasar J1358+0349 using Keck/HIRES (R~48,000) and VLT/UVES (R~46,000), then performed simultaneous Voigt profile fitting of the HI and DI Lyman series absorption lines along with metal lines (C, N, O, Al, Si, S, Fe) using the Absorption LIne Software (ALIS) package. They tied the DI/HI ratio to be the same across all absorption components and performed 2000 Monte Carlo simulations to ensure the global chi-squared minimum was found, with the analysis conducted blindly until finalized.

## Key numbers

- Average oxygen abundance of the DLA: [O/H] = -2.804 +/- 0.015, with one absorption component at [O/H] = -3.07 +/- 0.03
- Iron abundance: [Fe/H] = -3.25 +/- 0.11
- Best estimate of primordial deuterium abundance from weighted mean of six DLAs: 10^5 (D/H)_P = 2.547 +/- 0.033 (log10 (D/H)_P = -4.5940 +/- 0.0056)
- Cosmic baryon density using theoretical d(p,gamma)3He rate: 100 Omega_B,0 h^2 (BBN) = 2.156 +/- 0.020, differing by ~2.3 sigma from the Planck CMB value
- Cosmic baryon density using experimental d(p,gamma)3He rate: 100 Omega_B,0 h^2 (BBN) = 2.260 +/- 0.034
- Total HI column density: log N(HI) = 20.524 +/- 0.006 cm^-2
- Total DI/HI ratio for the system: log (DI/HI) = -4.582 +/- 0.012

## Context

This work extends the sample of five DLAs previously analyzed by Cooke (2014) that exhibited a D/H plateau over a factor of ~10 in metallicity, adding the most metal-poor system currently known. The authors report tentative evidence for a subtle decrease of D/H with increasing metallicity across the six self-consistently analyzed systems, which must be confirmed with future measurements. The discrepancy between the BBN-derived baryon density and the Planck CMB value (Efs15) highlights the need for forthcoming measurements of the d(p,gamma)3He cross section to resolve the tension.
