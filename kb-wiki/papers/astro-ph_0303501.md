# Differential Neutrino Rates and Emissivities from the Plasma Process in Astrophysical Systems

> The differential rates and emissivities of neutrino pairs from an equilibrium plasma are calculated for the wide range of density and temperature encountered in astrophysical systems. New analytical expressions are derived for the differential emissivities which yield total emissivities in full agreement with those previously calculated. The photon and plasmon pair production and absorption kernels in the source term of the Boltzmann equation for neutrino transport are provided. The appropriate Legendre coefficients of these kernels, in forms suitable for multi-group flux-limited diffusion schemes are also computed.

## Key claim

The paper provides new analytical expressions for the differential emissivities of neutrino pairs from the plasma process, which yield total emissivities in full agreement with those previously calculated by Braaten (1993), but the differential information about neutrino energy and angular distributions, lost in prior works through the use of Lenard's identity, is now preserved for use in neutrino transport calculations.

## Method

The authors computed squared matrix elements for neutrino pair production from transverse, longitudinal, axial, and mixed vector-axial channels of a massive photon (plasmon) in an equilibrium plasma, using finite-temperature field theory with Matsubara frequency sums. They derived differential emissivities as functions of neutrino energies and opening angle by integrating over phase space while retaining the energy delta function, and provided explicit forms for the production and absorption kernels and their Legendre coefficients suitable for multi-group flux-limited diffusion schemes.

## Key numbers

- The transverse polarization function \(\Pi_T\) and longitudinal polarization function \(\Pi_L\) are defined via scalar functions \(G(\omega_T,k)\) and \(F(\omega_L,k)\) given in Eqs. (G) and (F), with the plasma frequency \(\omega_p\) used as a first guess for iterative solution of the dispersion relation.
- The residue factor for transverse modes is \(Z_T(k) = \left|1 - \partial \Pi_T / \partial \omega_T^2\right|^{-1}\), and for longitudinal modes \(Z_L(k) = k^2/(\omega_L^2 - k^2) \times Z_L^{BS}\) where \(Z_L^{BS}\) is the Braaten (1993) factor.
- The condition for longitudinal emission is \(k \leq k_{max}\), enforced by \(\Theta(\mathcal{K} = k_{max} - E_1 - E_2)\).
- The total emissivity from the mixed vector-axial channel integrates to zero: \(Q_M = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the total emissivity calculations of Braaten (1993) by providing the differential rates and emissivities that were previously eliminated via Lenard's identity, enabling detailed neutrino transport in supernova simulations. The results are intended for use in multi-group flux-limited diffusion schemes as described by Bruenn (1985) and Burrows and Thompson (2002), and complement separate work on the photoneutrino process. The paper enables future comparisons of the plasma process with competing neutrino emission mechanisms across the wide range of densities and temperatures encountered in astrophysical systems.
