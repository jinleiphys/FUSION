# Baryon Interactions in Nuclear and Hypernuclear Matter

> The production and structure of &#923; hypernuclei are investigated in field theoretical models. The production in coherent p+A reactions is investigated by means of a resonance model. Results of exploratory calculations for associated strangeness production are presented for proton reactions on 40Ca. The target single particle wave functions are obtained from DDRH theory with density dependent Dirac-Brueckner meson-baryon vertices. The dependence of the in-medium vertices on density is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper investigates associated strangeness production in exclusive proton-nucleus reactions as an alternative to more common pion and kaon induced reactions, but notes that the calculated cross sections in the nano- to picobarn range are at the lower end of experimental feasibility and that initial and final state interactions are not yet included in the calculations.

## Method
The authors use a field theoretical resonance model where associated strangeness production proceeds via a two-nucleon mechanism: the projectile nucleon collides with a target nucleon, exciting intermediate baryonic resonances (N*1650, N*1710, N*1720) that decay into a K+ and a Λ hyperon. The target single particle wave functions are obtained from density-dependent relativistic hadron (DDRH) theory with Dirac-Brueckner meson-baryon vertices. Angular distributions for the outgoing K+ are calculated for proton reactions on 40Ca at 2 GeV incident kinetic energy.

## Key numbers
- Momentum transfer in the A(p,K+) reaction is about 1.0 GeV/c in forward direction, compared to about 0.330 GeV/c for (π+,K+) reactions.
- Calculations are performed for proton reactions on 40Ca at incident kinetic energy Ep = 2 GeV.
- Calculated cross sections range in the nano- to picobarn range.
- The ratio of Λ to nucleon coupling constants Rα = ΓαΛ/ΓαN shows reduction factors of about 50-60% for meson-Λ vertices, deviating from the naive quark model prediction of 1/3 reduction.
- Single Λ separation energies are well described for A > 40, with discrepancies for A < 16 attributed to core polarization effects.

## Context
This work extends the field theoretical approach of Shyam (2003) and Shyam (1999) for associated strangeness production, using DDRH theory developed by Lenske (1995) that has been applied to normal nuclei by Hofmann (2001) and hypernuclei by Keil (2000). The results enable future experimental investigations at COSY energy regime and suggest that in-medium meson-baryon vertices show a universal density dependence, with form factors that are almost independent of the meson channel. The discrepancies for light hypernuclei (A < 16) support a conjecture by Polls (1998) regarding additional dynamical self-energies from core polarization.
