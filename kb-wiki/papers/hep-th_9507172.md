# Quantum Corrections to the Semiclassical Quantization of the SU(3) Shell Model

> We apply the canonical perturbation theory to the semi--quantal hamiltonian of the SU(3) shell model. Then, we use the Einstein--Brillowin--Keller quantization rule to obtain an analytical semi--quantal formula for the energy levels, which is the usual semi--classical one plus quantum corrections. Finally, a test on the numerical accuracy of the semiclassical approximation and of its quantum corrections is performed.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical semi-quantal formula for the energy levels of the SU(3) shell model that includes quantum corrections beyond the usual semiclassical approximation, but the accuracy of this approximation decreases for higher energy levels because it is a perturbation theory in 1/M (where M is the number of particles) that works best when the classical limit 1/M → 0 and n_k → ∞ are taken simultaneously while keeping the action constant.

## Method

The authors applied canonical perturbation theory to the semi-quantal Hamiltonian of the SU(3) shell model, transforming to action-angle variables and expanding the Hamiltonian to second order in the coupling parameter χ. They then used the Einstein-Brillouin-Keller quantization rule to obtain an analytical formula for the energy levels, and compared these semi-quantal results to exact numerical diagonalization of the Hamiltonian matrix in a basis of SU(3) coherent states.

## Key numbers

- The semi-quantal Hamiltonian is expanded to second order in χ = MV/ε, where M is the number of particles, V is the interaction strength, and ε = 1 is the single-particle level spacing.
- The quantum corrections to the energy are given by: E_{n1 n2}^{qc}(M) = -(χ²/4M)[-1+(n1+1/2)(1/M)+2(n2+1/2)(1/M)] × [2(n1+1/2)(1/M) -4(n1+1/2)²(1/M²)+(n2+1/2)(1/M)-(n1+1/2)(n2+1/2)(1/M²)-(n2+1/2)²(1/M²)]
- The semiclassical energy formula is: E_{n1 n2}^{sc}(M) = -1+(n1+1/2)(1/M)+2(n2+1/2)(1/M)+(χ²/4)[-1+(n1+1/2)(1/M)+2(n2+1/2)(1/M)] × [2(n1+1/2)(1/M)-4(n1+1/2)²(1/M²)+(n2+1/2)(1/M)-(n1+1/2)(n2+1/2)(1/M²)-(n2+1/2)²(1/M²)]
- The unperturbed frequencies are ω₁ = 1 and ω₂ = 2, with the resonance condition mω₁ + nω₂ = 0 not satisfied up to second order in χ.
- The phase space is scaled so that (q₁²+q₂²+p₁²+p₂²) ≤ 2.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical quantization of the SU(3) shell model previously studied by the same authors (Manfredi and Salasnich, 1995) by adding quantum corrections in powers of 1/M, where 1/M plays the role of the Planck constant ℏ. The approach builds on the work of Graffi and others (1993) showing that classical perturbation theory can be used in quantum-mechanical perturbation theory for perturbed non-resonant harmonic oscillators. The results enable more accurate analytical calculations of nuclear shell model energy levels and demonstrate that for systems with a finite number of Fourier harmonics, like the SU(3) model, rational frequencies do not cause small-denominator problems up to a certain order of canonical perturbation theory.
