# Realistic Few-Body Physics in the $dd\to απ^0$ Reaction

> We use realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions in a hybrid chiral-perturbation-theory calculation of the charge-symmetry-breaking reaction $dd\toαπ^0$ to show that a cross section of the experimentally measured size can be obtained using LO and NNLO pion-production operators. This result supports the validity of our power counting scheme and demonstrates the necessity of using an accurate treatment of ISI and FSI.

## Key claim

Using realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions in a hybrid chiral-perturbation-theory calculation, the authors show that the experimentally measured cross section for the charge-symmetry-breaking reaction $dd\to\alpha\pi^0$ can be obtained from LO and NNLO pion-production operators, but only when initial-state interactions (ISI) and final-state interactions (FSI) are treated accurately; the formally leading-order (LO) contribution is suppressed by wave-function symmetries, and the NNLO terms become large enough only after including ISI and high-momentum components of the $\alpha$-particle wave function.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid calculation: pion-production operators are derived from chiral perturbation theory at LO and NNLO, while the nuclear wave functions are obtained from realistic phenomenological two-nucleon potentials (AV18 and CD-Bonn 2000) combined with the Tucson-Melbourne (TM99) three-nucleon force. The four-body scattering problem for the initial $d+d$ state is solved approximately using a truncated Neumann series of the Yakubovsky equation, which includes deuteron breakup followed by three-body scattering of the $N+d$ system, and the transition matrix elements are evaluated for the $^3$P$_0$ partial wave at a deuteron laboratory energy of $T_d=228.5$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross section: $\sigma=12.7\pm2.2$ pb at $T_d=228.5$ MeV and $15.1\pm3.1$ pb at $T_d=231.8$ MeV (Stephenson 2003).
- The one-body term alone, using realistic wave functions and including ISI, yields a cross section of $10-13$ pb, in accidental agreement with experiment.
- The pion-recoil and one-body matrix elements (including ISI) correspond to cross sections between $4.5$ and $42$ pb.
- The pion-exchange amplitude (LO) in the plane-wave approximation is $0.41\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$ (CD-Bonn+TM99) and $-0.08\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$ (AV18+TM99); with ISI it becomes $(-1.79 + i\,2.22)\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$ and $(-0.90 + i\,0.88)\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$, respectively.
- The one-body amplitude in the plane-wave approximation is $1.76\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$ (CD-Bonn+TM99) and $1.60\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$ (AV18+TM99); with ISI it becomes $(-2.51 + i\,1.84)\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$ and $(-1.94 + i\,1.60)\times10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$, respectively.
- The nucleon mass splitting is $M_n-M_p=1.29$ MeV; the electromagnetic contribution is estimated as $\bar{\delta}m_N=-(0.76\pm0.3)$ MeV from the Cottingham sum rule.
- The pion decay constant is $f_\pi=92.4$ MeV.
- The $\pi$-$\eta$ mixing matrix element is $\langle\pi^0|H|\eta\rangle=-4200$ MeV$^2$.
- The cross-section conversion factor is $\sigma=4.303$ pb $\times |\mathcal{M}\times10^4\ \text{fm}^2|^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier toy-model study (Gardestig 2005) of the $dd\to\alpha\pi^0$ reaction, which used plane-wave initial states and simplified final-state wave functions and found a cross section of only $0.9$ pb from NNLO diagrams. The present calculation incorporates realistic four-body wave functions and initial-state interactions, showing that these nuclear effects enhance the NNLO contributions by roughly an order of magnitude, bringing them into agreement with the experimental data from the IUCF Cooler ring (Stephenson 2003). The results support the chiral-perturbation-theory power-counting scheme for this reaction, but also demonstrate that a consistent treatment of nuclear interactions and production operators from the same EFT will be needed for a final analysis; the authors identify the need for new experimental constraints on $dd$ interactions near the pion-production threshold and for a reassessment of the Z-graph model used to estimate four-nucleon operators in light of recent EFT developments (Lensky 2005).
