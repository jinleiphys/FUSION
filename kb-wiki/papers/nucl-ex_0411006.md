# Parallel momentum distribution of the $^{28}$Si fragments from $^{29}$P

> Distribution of the parallel momentum of $^{28}$Si fragments from the breakup of 30.7 MeV/nucleon $^{29}$P has been measured on C targets. The distribution has the FWHM with the value of 110.5 $\pm$ 23.5 MeV/c which is consistent quantitatively with Galuber model calculation assuming by a valence proton in $^{29}$P. The density distribution is also predicted by Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation. Results show that there might exist the proton-skin structure in $^{29}$P.

## Key claim

The parallel momentum distribution of $^{28}$Si fragments from the breakup of 30.7 MeV/nucleon $^{29}$P on a carbon target has a FWHM of 110.5 ± 23.5 MeV/c, consistent with a Glauber model calculation assuming a core-plus-valence-proton structure; however, the authors conclude that a full proton halo is unlikely to form in $^{29}$P, and instead a proton-skin structure may exist, positioning this nucleus as a transitional case between exotic and stable isotopes.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the Radioactive Ion Beam Line in Lanzhou (RIBLL), where a 69 MeV/nucleon $^{36}$Ar primary beam fragmented on a Be target to produce a $^{29}$P secondary beam at 30.7 MeV/nucleon. The $^{29}$P beam impinged on a carbon target, and the parallel momentum distribution of the $^{28}$Si fragments from one-proton removal was measured using time-of-flight (TOF) between two scintillator detectors separated by 16.8 m, combined with energy-loss measurements from a silicon detector telescope and a CsI crystal. The experimental data were compared with a Glauber model calculation using Monte Carlo integration with the Metropolis algorithm, where the projectile density was treated as a $^{28}$Si core plus a valence proton, with core and target densities fitted from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations.

## Key numbers

- FWHM of the parallel momentum distribution of $^{28}$Si fragments: 110.5 ± 23.5 MeV/c, measured for the breakup of 30.7 MeV/nucleon $^{29}$P on a carbon target.
- Glauber model calculated FWHM: 91 MeV/c, for the same reaction system.
- Angular distribution width (FWHM) of $^{28}$Si fragments: 9.2°, obtained from a single-Gaussian fit.
- Single-proton separation energy ($S_p$) of $^{29}$P: 2.748 MeV, cited as the binding energy of the valence proton.
- Single-particle energy of the last proton (2s1/2 orbit) in $^{29}$P: 2.748 MeV; the 1d5/2 orbit energy is 11.585 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of proton-halo candidates such as $^{8}$B (Tanihata, 1985), $^{12}$N (Warner, 1995), and $^{23}$Al (Cai, 2002), as well as investigations of $^{26-28}$P by Navin (2000) and others, which found narrow momentum distributions indicative of halo structures in the lighter phosphorus isotopes. The measurement of $^{29}$P, with a higher separation energy (2.748 MeV) than $^{26,27}$P (0.14 MeV and 0.9 MeV, respectively), shows a broader momentum width that is inconsistent with a full halo but consistent with a proton-skin structure. This result enables further study of the transition from proton-halo to normal nuclear structure near the proton drip line around mass number 30, and demonstrates the utility of combining Glauber model calculations with SHF density distributions for interpreting fragment momentum distributions.
