# Bayesian inference of multimessenger astrophysical data: Joint and coherent inference of gravitational waves and kilonovae

> We present a Bayesian framework for joint and coherent analyses of multimessenger binary neutron star signals. The method, implemented in our bajes infrastructure, incorporates a joint likelihood for multiple datasets, support for various semi-analytical kilonova models and numerical-relativity (NR) informed relations for the mass ejecta, as well as a technique to include and marginalize over modeling uncertainties. As a first application, we analyze the gravitational-wave GW170817 and the kilonova AT2017gfo data. These results are then combined with the most recent X-ray pulsars analyses of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 to obtain EOS constraints.Various constraints on the mass-radius diagram and neutron star properties are then obtained by resampling over a set of ten million parametrized EOS built under minimal assumptions. We find that a joint and coherent approach improves the inference of the extrinsic parameters (distance) and, among the instrinc parameters, the mass ratio. The inclusion of NR informed relations strongly improves over the case of using an agnostic prior on the intrinsic parameters. Comparing Bayes factors, we find that the two observations are better explained by the common source hypothesis only by assuming NR-informed relations. These relations break some of the degeneracies in the employed kN models. The EOS inference folding-in PSR J0952-0607 minimum-maximum mass, PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 data constrains, among other quantities, the neutron star radius to $R_{1.4}={12.30}^{+0.81}_{-0.56}$ km ($R_{1.4}={13.20}^{+0.91}_{-0.90}$ km) and the maximum mass to $M_{max}={2.28}^{+0.25}_{-0.17}~{\rm M_\odot}$ ($M_{max}={2.32}^{+0.30}_{-0.19}~{\rm M_\odot}$) where the ST+PDT (PDT-U) analysis of Vinciguerra et a (2023) for PSR J0030+0451 is employed. Hence, the systematics on PSR J0030+0451 data reduction currently dominate the mass-radius diagram constraints.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Bayesian framework for joint and coherent inference of gravitational-wave and kilonova data from binary neutron star mergers, finding that the common-source hypothesis is only favored over the different-source hypothesis when numerical-relativity informed relations for mass ejecta are included, which break degeneracies in the kilonova models.

## Method

The authors extended the bajes infrastructure with a joint likelihood for multiple datasets, incorporating semi-analytical kilonova models and numerical-relativity (NR) informed relations for mass ejecta with marginalization over modeling uncertainties. They analyzed GW170817 using the TEOBResumS effective-one-body waveform template and AT2017gfo using a multi-component spherically symmetric kilonova model, then combined results with X-ray pulsar analyses of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 by resampling over ten million parametrized equations of state built under minimal assumptions of general relativity and causality.

## Key numbers

* Neutron star radius at 1.4 solar masses: R_1.4 = 12.30^{+0.81}_{-0.56} km (using ST+PDT analysis of PSR J0030+0451 from Vinciguerra et al. 2023) and R_1.4 = 13.20^{+0.91}_{-0.90} km (using PDT-U analysis)
* Maximum neutron star mass: M_max = 2.28^{+0.25}_{-0.17} solar masses (ST+PDT) and M_max = 2.32^{+0.30}_{-0.19} solar masses (PDT-U)
* Total binary mass from joint GW170817+AT2017gfo analysis with NR-informed relations: M = 2.77^{+0.03}_{-0.01} solar masses
* Mass ratio from joint analysis with NR-informed relations: q = 1.2^{+0.2}_{-0.2}
* Chirp mass from joint analysis with NR-informed relations: M_c = 1.1975^{+0.0001}_{-0.0001} solar masses
* Effective spin from joint analysis with NR-informed relations: chi_eff = 0.00^{+0.01}_{-0.01}

## Context

This work extends previous multimessenger Bayesian analyses of binary neutron star mergers such as those by Breschi (2021), Dietrich (2020), and Raaijmakers (2021) by implementing a truly joint and coherent framework rather than combining posteriors in postprocessing. It incorporates for the first time the recent re-analysis of PSR J0030+0451 by Vinciguerra (2023), finding that systematics from that pulsar's data reduction currently dominate the mass-radius diagram constraints. The framework enables future joint analyses of multimessenger data from upcoming gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observations.
