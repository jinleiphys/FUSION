# Threshold eta and eta' electroproduction off nucleons

> The electroproduction of eta and eta' mesons on the proton and the neutron is investigated at tree level within the framework of U(3) chiral perturbation theory. In addition to the Born terms low-lying resonances such as the vector mesons and J^P= 1/2^+, 1/2^- baryon resonances are included explicitly and their contributions are calculated. Results for the separated differential cross sections are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a tree-level calculation of eta and eta' electroproduction off nucleons within U(3) chiral perturbation theory, but the author explicitly states that the model should be considered a qualitative check rather than a detailed prediction, and that chiral loops and further resonances would be needed for better agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The author extends a previous U(3) chiral perturbation theory framework for eta and eta' photoproduction to electroproduction, including Born terms and explicit contributions from low-lying resonances: vector mesons (rho, omega) in the t-channel and J^P = 1/2+ (P11(1440)) and 1/2- (S11(1535)) baryon resonances in the s- and u-channels. The invariant amplitudes are calculated at tree level, and the separated differential cross sections (transverse, longitudinal, transverse-longitudinal, transverse-transverse) are decomposed into S- and P-wave multipoles near threshold.

## Key numbers

- The eta-eta' mixing angle is taken from experiment as approximately -20 degrees, determined from two-photon decays of pi0, eta, and eta'.
- The axial flavor-singlet coupling lambda = 0.05 is obtained from a fit to eta and eta' photoproduction.
- The vector meson couplings to eta are g_{rho gamma eta} = 1.46 +/- 0.16 GeV^{-1} and g_{omega gamma eta} = 0.53 +/- 0.04 GeV^{-1}, extracted from radiative decay widths.
- The vector meson couplings to eta' are g_{rho gamma eta'} = 1.31 +/- 0.04 GeV^{-1} and g_{omega gamma eta'} = 0.45 +/- 0.04 GeV^{-1}.
- For the proton at k^2 = -0.04 GeV^2 and cos theta = 0, the transverse differential cross section for eta electroproduction is d sigma_T/d Omega = 0.57 microbarn/sr (central value), with a range of 0.25 to 1.33 microbarn/sr when varying S-wave resonance couplings.
- For the proton at the same kinematics, the longitudinal differential cross section for eta electroproduction is d sigma_L/d Omega = 0.57 microbarn/sr (central value), with a range of 0.35 to 1.03 microbarn/sr.
- For the proton at k^2 = -0.04 GeV^2 and cos theta = 0, the transverse differential cross section for eta' electroproduction is d sigma_T/d Omega = 0.60 microbarn/sr (central value), with a range of 0.48 to 0.80 microbarn/sr.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous U(3) chiral perturbation theory treatment of eta and eta' photoproduction (Borasoy, 2000) to the electroproduction case, providing a test of whether including eta and eta' in a pseudoscalar nonet leads to an adequate description of meson-baryon processes. The approach contrasts with treatments that treat eta as a pure SU(3) octet state and neglect eta-eta' mixing, and the author notes that no experimental data currently exist for eta and eta' electroproduction near threshold at small momentum transfer, so the predictions await experimental verification from facilities such as CEBAF.
