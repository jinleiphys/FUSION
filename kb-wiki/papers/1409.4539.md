# Modifications of Heavy-Flavor Spectra in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=62.4~{\rm GeV}$ Au-Au Collisions

> We calculate open heavy-flavor (HF) production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$=62.4 GeV utilizing a nonperturbative transport approach as previously applied in nuclear collisions at top RHIC and LHC energies. The effects of hot QCD matter are treated in a strong-coupling framework, by implementing heavy-quark diffusion, hadronization and heavy-flavor meson diffusion within a hydrodynamic background evolution. Since in our approach the heavy-flavor coupling to the medium is strongest in the pseudo-critical region, it is of interest to test its consequences at lower collision energies where the sensitivity to this region should be enhanced relative to the hotter (early) fireball temperatures reached at top RHIC and LHC energies. We find that the suppression and flow pattern of the non-photonic electrons from heavy-flavor decays at 62.4 GeV emerges from an intricate interplay of thermalization and initial-state effects, in particular a Cronin enhancement which is known to become more pronounced toward lower collision energies.

## Key claim
The suppression and flow pattern of heavy-flavor decay electrons at 62.4 GeV emerges from an interplay of thermalization and initial-state effects, specifically a Cronin enhancement that becomes more pronounced at lower collision energies, rather than being solely attributable to hot-medium effects.

## Method
The authors employed a nonperturbative transport approach using relativistic Langevin simulations for heavy-quark diffusion through quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter, with resonance recombination hadronization at the pseudo-critical temperature. The medium evolution was modeled with 2+1D ideal hydrodynamics (AZHYDRO) tuned to reproduce pion and proton spectra at 62.4 GeV, and initial heavy-quark spectra from FONLL were modified by a Gaussian Cronin broadening with variance parameters estimated from nuclear path lengths.

## Key numbers
- Bottom-to-charm cross section ratio at 62.4 GeV: 1.9 × 10⁻³ (compared to 5 × 10⁻³ at top RHIC and 5 × 10⁻² at LHC)
- Typical Cronin broadening variance for charm quarks in central Au-Au: ~0.6 GeV² (default value, amplified by factor 2 from baseline estimate of ~0.3 GeV²)
- Typical Cronin broadening variance for bottom quarks in central Au-Au: ~2.0 GeV² (default value, amplified from baseline estimate of ~1.0 GeV²)
- Hydrodynamic freezeout temperature: 130 MeV; thermalization time: 0.9 fm/c; initial radial flow parameter: 0.035 fm⁻¹
- Bottom fraction in decay electrons reaches 0.5 at electron transverse momentum 4-5 GeV
- Resonance recombination contributes roughly 30% to elliptic flow, with ~50% from QGP and ~20% from hadronic phase

## Context
This work extends the authors' nonperturbative transport model previously applied at top RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC energies (He 2012, He 2014) to the lower collision energy of 62.4 GeV, where sensitivity to the pseudo-critical region is enhanced. The study tests whether the strong heavy-flavor coupling to the medium is driven primarily by temperature or by increased coupling strength near the transition region, and enables comparison with recent PHENIX and STAR data at this energy. The results are roughly compatible with existing data but call for higher-precision measurements to distinguish the predicted flow bump and Cronin enhancement structures.
