# Testing the handbag contribution to exclusive virtual Compton scattering

> We discuss the handbag approximation to exclusive deep virtual Compton scattering. After defining the kinematical region where this approximation can be valid, we propose tests for its relevance in planned electroproduction experiments, e + p -> e + p + gamma. We focus on scaling laws in the cross section, and the distribution in the angle between the lepton and hadron planes, which contains valuable information on the angular momentum structure of the Compton process. We advocate to measure weighted cross sections, which make use of the data in the full range of this angle and do not require very high event statistics.

# Testing the handbag contribution to exclusive virtual Compton scattering

## Key claim

The handbag approximation to exclusive deep virtual Compton scattering (VCS) is likely valid only in a restricted kinematic region where the transverse momentum transfer Δ_T is small (Δ_T ≲ 1 GeV), and the authors propose that experimental tests using weighted cross sections in the angle φ between lepton and hadron planes can determine whether the handbag dominates, without requiring prior knowledge of the off-diagonal parton distributions.

## Method

The authors analyze the helicity structure of the handbag diagrams for γ*p → γp at Born level, deriving that the handbag predicts s-channel photon helicity conservation to leading order in 1/Q. They then expand the full electroproduction cross section (including Bethe-Heitler interference) in powers of 1/Q and in Fourier components of the azimuthal angle φ, showing that different Fourier coefficients project onto different photon helicity amplitudes. They propose measuring weighted cross sections ⟨⟨f(φ)⟩⟩ with f(φ) = 1, cosφ, cos2φ, cos3φ, sinφ, sin2φ to test the scaling predictions and helicity selection rules of the handbag model.

## Key numbers

- The handbag is expected to apply only when Δ_T ≲ 1 GeV, with a more conservative criterion of Δ_T ≲ 300 MeV for constituents inside a proton
- The condition for excluding the resonance region is W² ≫ 1 GeV², equivalent to 1 - x_Bj ≫ m²/Q²
- The squared BH amplitude, BH-VCS interference, and squared VCS amplitude scale as 1, 1/Q, and 1/Q² respectively in the handbag model
- The handbag predicts ⟨⟨cosφ⟩⟩ - ⟨⟨cosφ⟩⟩_{BH only} ∼ 1/Q at fixed Δ_T, x_Bj, and y
- The handbag predicts ⟨⟨cos2φ⟩⟩ and ⟨⟨cos3φ⟩⟩ ∼ 1/Qⁿ with n ≥ 2 (power suppressed relative to ⟨⟨cosφ⟩⟩)
- For large ε (close to 1), the handbag predicts ⟨⟨1⟩⟩ ∼ 1/Q²
- The handbag predicts ⟨⟨sinφ⟩⟩ ∼ 1/Q and ⟨⟨sin2φ⟩⟩ ∼ 1/Qⁿ with n ≥ 2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for off-diagonal parton distributions developed by Ji (1997), Radyushkin (1996), and Collins, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1997), who recognized that VCS could measure generalized parton distributions related to quark orbital angular momentum. The authors explicitly distinguish their approach from the fixed-angle region calculations of Farrar, Gunion, and Sivers (1979) and Kronfeld and Nizic (1983), and from the resonance region. The proposed weighted cross section method enables experimental tests at facilities including JLAB, HERA, CERN, and the ELFE project without requiring high event statistics or binning in φ.
