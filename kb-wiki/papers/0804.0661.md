# Astrophysical Implications of Equation of State for Hadron-Quark Mixed Phase: Compact Stars and Stellar Collapses

> We construct an equation of state including the hadron-quark phase transition. The mixed phase is obtained by the Gibbs conditions for finite temperature. We adopt the equation of state based on the relativistic mean field theory for the hadronic phase taking into account pions. As for the quark phase, the MIT bag model of the deconfined 3-flavor strange quark matter is used. As a result, our equation of state is thermodynamically stable and exhibits qualitatively the desired properties of hadron-quark mixed matter, such as the temperature dependence of the transition density. The pions raise the transition density because they make the equation of state softer. Using the equation of state constructed here, we study its astrophysical implications. The maximum mass of compact stars is investigated, and our equation of state is consistent with recent observations. We also compute the collapse of a massive star with 100 solar masses using our equation of state and find that the interval time from the bounce to the black hole formation becomes shorter for the model with pions and quarks. The pions and quarks affect the total energy of the emitted neutrinos because the duration time of the neutrino emission becomes shorter. The neutrino luminosity rises under the effect of pions since the density of the proto-neutron star becomes high.

## Key claim

The authors construct a finite-temperature equation of state (EOS) for hadron-quark mixed phase using Gibbs conditions and find that including pions raises the transition density because pions soften the EOS, while the maximum mass of compact stars remains consistent with recent observations.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described by the Shen EOS based on relativistic mean field theory with non-linear self-coupling terms, extended to include thermal pions as an ideal boson gas. The quark phase uses the MIT bag model for deconfined 3-flavor strange quark matter with bag constant B = 250 MeV fm^-3 as the reference model. The mixed phase is constructed by solving Gibbs conditions for mechanical and chemical equilibrium between phases, including trapped neutrinos, for baryon mass densities from 10^5.1 to 10^17 g cm^-3, temperatures from 0 to 400 MeV, and electron fractions from 0 to 0.56.

## Key numbers

- The reference model uses bag constant B = 250 MeV fm^-3 (B^1/4 = 209 MeV)
- The EOS covers baryon mass density range 10^5.1 g cm^-3 ≤ ρ_B ≤ 10^17 g cm^-3, temperature range 0 MeV ≤ T ≤ 400 MeV, and electron fraction range 0 ≤ Y_e ≤ 0.56
- Quark masses adopted: m_u c^2 = 2.5 MeV, m_d c^2 = 5 MeV, m_s c^2 = 100 MeV
- The transition density for the model with pions is larger than for the model without pions; for comparison, a previous study (Drago 1999) found transition density ~10^14 g cm^-3 at T ~ 30 MeV
- The critical point temperature for the phase transition is estimated in the range 150 MeV ≤ T_c ≤ 200 MeV from lattice QCD and heavy-ion collision studies
- For the bag constant to yield critical temperature in this range, B must be in 210 MeV fm^-3 ≲ B ≲ 650 MeV fm^-3 (200 MeV ≲ B^1/4 ≲ 265 MeV)
- The authors compute collapse of a massive star with 100 solar masses (M_⊙) using their EOS

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hybrid stars using Gibbs conditions (Glendenning 1992) and finite-temperature EOS with neutrinos (Drago 1999) by incorporating the Shen EOS with non-linear self-coupling terms and pions, which were absent in previous hadronic models. The authors also improve upon zero-temperature Maxwell construction approaches used in earlier stellar collapse simulations (Gentile 1993, Yasutake 2007). This enables future studies of progenitor dependence and EOS dependence of collapse and neutrino emission, with the goal of probing hot dense matter EOS from observed neutrino signals.
