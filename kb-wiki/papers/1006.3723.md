# Isospin dependent multifragmentation of relativistic projectiles

> The N/Z dependence of projectile fragmentation at relativistic energies has been studied with the ALADIN forward spectrometer at the GSI Schwerionen Synchrotron (SIS). Stable and radioactive Sn and La beams with an incident energy of 600 MeV per nucleon have been used in order to explore a wide range of isotopic compositions. For the interpretation of the data, calculations with the statistical multifragmentation model for a properly chosen ensemble of excited sources were performed. The parameters of the ensemble, representing the variety of excited spectator nuclei expected in a participant-spectator scenario, are determined empirically by searching for an optimum reproduction of the measured fragment-charge distributions and correlations. An overall very good agreement is obtained. The possible modification of the liquid-drop parameters of the fragment description in the hot freeze-out environment is studied, and a significant reduction of the symmetry-term coefficient is found necessary to reproduce the mean neutron-to-proton ratios <N>/Z and the isoscaling parameters of Z<=10 fragments. The calculations are, furthermore, used to address open questions regarding the modification of the surface-term coefficient at freeze-out, the N/Z dependence of the nuclear caloric curve, and the isotopic evolution of the spectator system between its formation during the initial cascade stage of the reaction and its subsequent breakup.

## Key claim

The N/Z dependence of projectile fragmentation at relativistic energies is studied, and the authors find that a significant reduction of the symmetry-term coefficient in the liquid-drop description of fragments is necessary to reproduce the measured mean neutron-to-proton ratios and isoscaling parameters, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the charge-to-mass ratio of the thermalized sources remains that of the initial projectiles.

## Method

The ALADIN forward spectrometer at GSI was used to measure projectile fragmentation of stable 124Sn and radioactive 107Sn and 124La beams at 600 MeV per nucleon incident on natSn targets. The experimental data were interpreted using the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) with an ensemble of excited spectator sources whose parameters (mass, charge, excitation energy) were determined empirically by optimizing the reproduction of measured fragment charge distributions and correlations. The SMM calculations included a modified liquid-drop description of fragments with adjustable symmetry and surface term coefficients to study their behavior in the hot freeze-out environment.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 600 MeV per nucleon for all projectiles (Sn and La beams)
- Inclusive cross section for fragments with 3 ≤ Z ≤ 49 from 124Sn projectiles: 5.9 b
- Inclusive cross section for fragments with 3 ≤ Z ≤ 49 from 107Sn projectiles: 5.979 b
- Charge resolution of the TP-MUSIC IV detector: ΔZ ≤ 0.6 (full width at half maximum)
- Time-of-flight resolution: ≈ 250 ps (standard deviation) for lithium fragments, decreasing to ≈ 100 ps for fragments with Z ≥ 10
- Mass resolution: about 3% for fragments with Z ≤ 3, decreasing to 1.5% for Z ≥ 6
- Freeze-out density assumed: one-third the normal nuclear density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³)
- Standard symmetry energy parameter in SMM: γ = 25 MeV
- Standard surface energy coefficient: B₀ = 18 MeV
- Critical temperature of infinite nuclear matter: T_c = 18 MeV
- Binding energy of infinite nuclear matter: W₀ = 16 MeV
- Integrated cross sections recorded: 4.3 b (Z_bound ≤ 48) for 107Sn, 3.9 b (Z_bound ≤ 49) for 124Sn, and 4.6 b (Z_bound ≤ 55) for 124La
- Calculated reaction cross section for Sn + Sn collisions: σ_R = 4.8 b (r₀ = 1.25 fm)

## Context

This work extends previous ALADIN experimental studies of multifragmentation (Kreutz et al., Schuettauf et al.) that demonstrated the "rise and fall" of multifragmentation with excitation energy and showed that temperature remains nearly constant around T ≈ 6 MeV (Xi et al., Pochodzalla et al., Trautmann et al.). The reduction of the symmetry term coefficient in the low-density freeze-out environment, previously reported for 12C-induced reactions on 112,124Sn isotopes (Le Fevre et al.), is confirmed and extended to a wider range of isotopic compositions using radioactive beams. The results enable further investigation of open questions regarding the modification of the surface-term coefficient at freeze-out, the N/Z dependence of the nuclear caloric curve, and the isotopic evolution of the spectator system between its formation during the initial cascade stage and its subsequent breakup.
