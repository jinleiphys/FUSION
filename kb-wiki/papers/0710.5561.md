# Progress in finite temperature lattice QCD

> I review recent progress in finite temperature lattice calculations, including the determination of the transition temperature, equation of state, screening of static quarks and meson spectral functions.

## Key claim

The transition to the deconfined phase in 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses is not a true phase transition but only a rapid crossover, and the value of the transition temperature depends on the action used and the definition of the chiral susceptibility, with reported values ranging from 151(3)(3) MeV to 192(7)(4) MeV.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations using improved staggered fermion actions (p4, asqtad, and stout) on lattices with temporal extents N_tau = 4, 6, 8, and 10. The transition temperature is determined from the peak of the chiral susceptibility or Polyakov loop susceptibility, with continuum and chiral extrapolations performed. The equation of state is calculated from the trace of the energy momentum tensor (interaction measure epsilon-3p) using the integral method, and spatial correlation functions of temporal Wilson lines are used to extract the Debye screening mass.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from p4 action with continuum and chiral extrapolation: T_c = 192(7)(4) MeV (Sommer parameter r_0 = 0.469(7) fm used to set the scale)
- Deconfinement temperature from stout staggered action on N_tau = 4,6,8,10 lattices: 176(3)(4) MeV
- Chiral transition temperature from stout action: T_chiral = 151(3)(3) MeV
- For 3 flavor QCD with improved staggered quarks (p4) on N_tau = 4 lattices, the transition is first order only for pseudo-scalar meson masses of about 60 MeV
- Debye screening mass fitted with m_D(T) = A sqrt(1+N_f/3) g(T) T gives A approximately 1.4-1.6, representing about 40% non-perturbative correction
- At temperatures of about 4T_c in pure gauge theory, the deviation from the ideal gas value is only about 15%
- Entropy density at high temperature is only 10% below the ideal gas limit

## Context

This review extends the work summarized in earlier reviews (Sewm 2006, Lat 2006) and discusses lattice calculations that resolve the nature of the QCD transition, contradicting earlier unimproved staggered results (Norman) that suggested a first order transition for pseudo-scalar meson masses of about 300 MeV. The results enable comparison with re-summed perturbative techniques (Arnold, Scpt 1997, Braaten, Blaizot) and potential model studies of quarkonium spectral functions (Mocsy 2006, Mocsy 2007), while highlighting that previous claims about charmonia survival up to 1.6T_c (Umeda 2002, Asakawa 2004, Datta 2004) must be revisited due to systematic uncertainties in the Maximum Entropy Method.
