# QCD tests from pion reactions on few-nucleon systems

> We show on two examples, namely a calculation for charge symmetry breaking in pn->d pi^0 that allows one to extract the quark mass difference induced part of the proton--neutron mass difference and a high precision calculation for pion--deuteron scattering and its implications for the value of the charged pion--nucleon coupling constant, how QCD tests can be performed from low energy hadronic observables.

## Key claim

Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) can extract fundamental QCD parameters from low-energy pion reactions on few-nucleon systems with controlled theoretical uncertainties, but the accuracy of these extractions is limited by experimental precision and the need for additional QCD input to pin down unknown contact-term contributions.

## Method

The author presents two calculations within ChPT: (1) a leading-order (LO) analysis of the charge-symmetry-breaking forward-backward asymmetry in \(pn\to d\pi^0\), where the sum of two classes of diagrams (isospin-violating \(\pi N\) scattering and energy-dependent isospin-conserving \(\pi N\) interaction) is shown to be proportional only to the strong part of the proton-neutron mass difference \(\delta M^{\rm qm}\); (2) a high-precision calculation of the \(\pi^- d\) scattering length including diagrams up to \(\mathcal{O}(\chi^{3/2})\) relative to leading order, with systematic treatment of isospin violation, photon loops, dispersive corrections, and Delta-resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

- Forward-backward asymmetry in \(pn\to d\pi^0\): \(A_{fb} = [17.2 \pm 8\ {\rm (stat.)} \pm 5.5\ {\rm (sys.)}] \times 10^{-4}\) (TRIUMF experiment, Opper)
- Leading-order prediction: \(A_{\rm fb}^{\rm LO} = (11.5 \pm 3.5) \times 10^{-4} \cdot (\delta M^{\rm qm} / {\rm MeV})\)
- Extracted quark-mass-induced proton-neutron mass difference: \(\delta M^{\rm qm} = (1.5 \pm 0.8\ {\rm (exp.)} \pm 0.5\ {\rm (th.)})\ {\rm MeV}\)
- Extracted isoscalar \(\pi N\) scattering length (from combined pionic hydrogen and deuterium analysis): \(\tilde{a}^+ = (1.9 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-3}\ m_\pi^{-1}\)
- Extracted isovector \(\pi N\) scattering length: \(a^- = (86.1 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-3}\ m_\pi^{-1}\) (correlation coefficient \(\rho = -0.21\))
- Final value for \(a^+\) (after correcting for isospin-violating terms): \(a^+ = (7.6 \pm 3.1) \times 10^{-3}\ m_\pi^{-1}\)
- Charged pion-nucleon coupling constant from GMO sum rule: \(g_c^2/4\pi = 13.69 \pm 0.12 \pm 0.15 = 13.7 \pm 0.2\)

## Context

This work extends the program of using ChPT to bridge between experimental hadronic reactions and lattice QCD predictions, as reviewed by pipi, piN, and NN (recent reviews) and lattice review (lattice gauge theory). The \(pn\to d\pi^0\) calculation improves upon earlier incomplete treatments by van Kolck (1994) and Bolton (1994) by including both classes of isospin-violating diagrams, and yields a \(\delta M^{\rm qm}\) extraction with accuracy comparable to the direct extraction via Cottingham sum rule (Gasser, 1982) and lattice QCD (lattice). The \(\pi d\) scattering analysis resolves a long-standing discrepancy in the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant: previous GMO analyses gave values ranging from \(g_c^2/4\pi = 14.11 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.19\) (ELT) down to \(13.0-13.3\) (Arndt, 1992), while the present work with complete isospin-violating corrections and controlled uncertainties excludes values above 14. The results enable non-trivial tests of QCD dynamics at low energies through comparison with lattice calculations.
