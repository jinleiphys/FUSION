# On the Shell Structure of Nuclear Bubbles

> We investigate the shell structure of spherical nuclear bubbles in simple phenomenological shell model potentials. The shell correction energies for doubly magic bubbles may be as large as -40 MeV and probably imply a very long lifetime against spontaneous fission. Beta-stability occurs for ratios of the neutron number N to the proton number Z which differ markable from the beta-stability valley of ordinary compact nuclei. The alpha-decay probability is shown to be very small for proton rich bubbles with a moderately large outer radius. Metastable islands of nuclear bubbles are shown to exist for nucleon in the range A=450 - 3000.

## Key claim

The authors investigate the shell structure of spherical nuclear bubbles using simple phenomenological shell model potentials and find that shell correction energies for doubly magic bubbles may be as large as -40 MeV, which probably implies a very long lifetime against spontaneous fission, though the spherical liquid-drop bubble solutions are unstable against deformation and only shell effects can potentially stabilize them.

## Method

The authors applied the Strutinsky method to evaluate shell correction energies for spherical bubble nuclei, using two limiting single-particle potentials: an infinite square well and a shifted harmonic oscillator, both with a finite internal step to model reduced interior density. The spin-orbit potential was treated in perturbation theory, and the shape parameters of the shell model potentials were chosen to match the mean-square radius of the liquid-drop density distribution. The level spectra were calculated as functions of the hole fraction \(f = (R_2/R_1)^3\), and the liquid-drop energy differences between bubble and compact configurations were analyzed as functions of the fissility parameter \(X_0\).

## Key numbers

- Shell correction energies for doubly magic bubbles may be as large as -40 MeV.
- Bubble solutions within the liquid-drop model exist only for fissility parameter \(X_0 \geq 2.02\).
- For \((Z^2/A)_{\rm crit} = 51.57\), bubble solutions require \(Z^2/A > 104\).
- For nucleon number \(A=600\), the minimal charge for LD-bubble solutions is \(Z > 250\); for \(A=1200\), \(Z > 353\).
- Metastable islands of nuclear bubbles exist for nucleon numbers in the range \(A = 450\) to \(3000\).
- The surface tension parameter used was \(\sigma = 1.026\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) and the radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.2\) fm.
- The dimensionless parameter \((Z^2/A)_{\rm crit}\) was calculated as 51.57.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Wong (1973), who first investigated shell correction energies for toroidal and spherical bubble nuclei using a harmonic oscillator potential within the Strutinsky method, and by Siemens and Bethe (1967), who studied bubble nuclei using the liquid-drop model. The present paper extends the investigation to a much larger range of neutron and proton numbers beyond known nuclear species, identifying magic numbers and estimating shell energies. The results provide a first assessment of bubble stability that could guide future Hartree-Fock or Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations, such as those already finding bubble solutions with Gogny interactions (Decharge, 1997) and Skyrme interactions (Bonche, 1997).
