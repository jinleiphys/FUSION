# Positivity violation for the lattice Landau gluon propagator

> We present explicit numerical evidence of reflection-positivity violation for the lattice Landau gluon propagator in three-dimensional pure SU(2) gauge theory. We use data obtained at very large lattice volumes (V = 80^3, 140^3) and for three different lattice couplings in the scaling region (beta = 4.2, 5.0, 6.0). In particular, we observe a clear oscillatory pattern in the real-space propagator C(t). We also verify that the (real-space) data show good scaling in the range t \in [0,3] fm and can be fitted using a Gribov-like form. The violation of positivity is in contradiction with a stable-particle interpretation of the associated field theory and may be viewed as a manifestation of confinement.

## Key claim

The paper presents explicit numerical evidence that the lattice Landau gluon propagator in three-dimensional pure SU(2) gauge theory violates reflection positivity, which contradicts a stable-particle interpretation and may indicate confinement. The authors hedge that while the violation is clearly observable for all three lattice couplings and two lattice volumes considered, a careful extrapolation to infinite volume is needed to verify if the oscillatory behavior survives in that limit.

## Method

The authors computed the real-space propagator C(t) from lattice Landau-gauge gluon propagator data in momentum space using a discrete Fourier transform, for three-dimensional SU(2) gauge theory on large lattices (V = 80^3 and 140^3) at three couplings (β = 4.2, 5.0, 6.0) in the scaling region. They evaluated the discretized function G(t,a) = [C(t)C(t+2a) - C(t+a)^2]/a^2, which must be non-negative if reflection positivity holds, and fitted the real-space data using sums of Gribov-like functions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volumes used: V = 80^3 and V = 140^3
- Lattice couplings in the scaling region: β = 4.2, 5.0, 6.0
- The real-space propagator C(t) becomes negative at t ≈ 0.7 fm for all couplings
- The minimum of C(t) occurs at t_min ≈ 1 fm
- Good scaling is observed for t in the range [0, 3] fm
- From the minimum position, the Gribov mass parameter M ≈ 2.22 fm^{-1} = 438 MeV = 0.995 √σ
- From momentum-space fits in prior work (Cucchieri 2003), p_max = 0.8^{+0.2}_{-0.1} √σ = 350^{+100}_{-50} MeV
- Fits using a sum of two Gribov-like functions yield mass scales λ_1 = 1.69(1) √σ = 745(5) MeV and λ_2 = 0.74(1) √σ = 325(6) MeV
- The light-mass scale from the fit is M ≈ 0.74 √σ = 325 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies that found an infrared-suppressed Landau gluon propagator in momentum space (Cucchieri 2003, and others) and earlier indications of negative real-space propagators in 3d SU(2) (finite-temperature studies) and in 4d SU(3) for one exceptional configuration (Furui 2004). The results provide explicit verification of reflection-positivity violation predicted by Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenarios, and enable future studies of the analytic structure of the gluon propagator, though the authors note that the Gribov-like form may not represent the true analytic structure.
