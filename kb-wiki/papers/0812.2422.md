# Shear-Viscosity to Entropy Density Ratio of a Relativistic Hadron Gas

> Ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) are thought to have produced a state of matter called the Quark-Gluon-Plasma, characterized by a very small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$, near the lower bound predicted for that quantity by Anti-deSitter space/Conformal Field Theory (AdS/CFT) methods. As the produced matter expands and cools, it evolves through a phase described by a hadron gas with rapidly increasing $η/s$. We calculate $η/s$ as a function of temperature in this phase and find that its value poses a challenge for viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, which requires small values of $η/s$ throughout the entire evolution of the reaction in order to successfully describe the collective flow observables at RHIC. We show that the inclusion of non-unit fugacities will reduce $η/s$ in the hadronic phase, yet not sufficiently to be compatible with viscous hydrodynamics. We therefore conclude that the origin of the low viscosity matter at RHIC must be in the partonic phase of the reaction.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of a hadron gas in full chemical and kinetic equilibrium has a minimum value of approximately 0.9 near the critical temperature, which is significantly higher than the range of 0.08–0.24 required by viscous hydrodynamic calculations to reproduce RHIC elliptic flow data; even when non-unit fugacities are included, η/s only decreases to about 0.4–0.5, still above the needed range, implying the low-viscosity matter must originate in the partonic phase.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) microscopic transport model to simulate infinite equilibrated hadronic matter in a box with periodic boundary conditions. They extracted the shear viscosity via the Green-Kubo formalism from correlations of the shear component of the energy-momentum tensor, and computed the entropy density using the Gibbs formula with energy density, pressure, and chemical potentials extracted from the equilibrated system. The calculation was performed as a function of temperature, baryo-chemical potential, and non-unit fugacities for pions and kaons.

## Key numbers

- Minimum η/s for the equilibrium zero baryo-chemical potential case is approximately 0.9, compared to the KSS bound of η/s ≈ 0.08.
- Viscous hydrodynamic calculations require η/s in the range 0.08–0.24 to reproduce RHIC elliptic flow data.
- With non-unit fugacities, η/s in the range of hadronic chemical freezeout can be as low as 0.4–0.5.
- The speed of sound extracted from the pressure versus energy density slope yields c_s² = 0.18.
- The relaxation time for the viscous correlator at T = 67.9 MeV is 136 fm/c.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature is near T_chem ≈ 160 MeV; kinetic freeze-out temperature is T_kin ≈ 130 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior analytic calculations of η/s for simple hadronic systems (Gavin 1985, Prakash 1993, Dobado 2001, Chen 2006, Chen 2007, Itakura 2007) that included only one or two hadronic species, by using a full hadron resonance gas with the UrQMD model. The results contradict the suggestion by Csernai (2006) that η/s should decrease with rising temperature in the hadronic phase and reach a minimum near the deconfinement transition; instead, the minimum η/s in the hadronic phase is found to be too large for viscous hydrodynamics. The work enables the conclusion that the low viscosity matter at RHIC must originate in the deconfined partonic phase, possibly in the range 1 < T/T_c < 2.
