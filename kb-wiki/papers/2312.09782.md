# Response functions and giant monopole resonances for light to medium-mass nuclei from the \textit{ab initio} symmetry-adapted no-core shell model

> Using the \textit{ab initio} symmetry-adapted no-core shell model, we compute sum rules and response functions for light to medium-mass nuclei, starting from interactions that are derived in the chiral effective field theory. We investigate electromagnetic transitions of monopole, dipole and quadrupole nature for symmetric nuclei such as $^4$He, $^{16}$O, $^{20}$Ne and $^{40}$Ca. Furthermore, we study giant monopole resonance, which can provide information on the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) can compute response functions and giant monopole resonances for light to medium-mass nuclei using chiral effective field theory interactions without renormalization in the nuclear medium, but the reported centroid energies and compressibilities carry uncertainties from the many-body truncation and the underlying interaction, and the response functions depend explicitly on the chosen resolution scale Gamma.

## Method

The authors used the Lanczos sum rule method combined with the SA-NCSM to compute sum rules and discretized response functions for the isoscalar monopole, electric dipole, and electric quadrupole operators in 4He, 16O, 20Ne, and 40Ca. The response functions were obtained by folding the discrete strength distribution with a Lorentzian of width Gamma, using chiral NNLOopt and NNLOsat nucleon-nucleon potentials (with three-nucleon forces included as averages for NNLOsat). The SA-NCSM employs a symmetry-adapted basis with SU(3) and Sp(3,R) labels to reduce model space dimensions while preserving collective features.

## Key numbers

* 4He monopole response peaks at 17.8 MeV for 0+ states with NNLOopt and Gamma=4 MeV
* 4He dipole response peaks at 26.5 MeV for 1- states with NNLOopt and Gamma=4 MeV
* 4He quadrupole response peaks at 31.0 MeV for 2+ states with NNLOopt and Gamma=4 MeV
* 4He GMR centroid energy E_GMR = 27.0(1) MeV from m1=176.7(4) fm4MeV, m-1=0.242(2) fm4MeV-1, m0=22.92(4) fm4
* 16O monopole response shows a well-defined peak around 23.5 MeV with NNLOopt and Gamma=2 MeV
* 16O GMR centroid energy E_GMR = 24(1) MeV, with ground-state rms matter radius 5.51 fm2
* 20Ne GMR centroid energy E_GMR = 25(1) MeV, with ground-state rms matter radius 7.22 fm2
* 40Ca GMR centroid energy E_GMR = 27(2) MeV, with ground-state rms matter radius 9.70 fm2
* 4He compressibility K_A = 36.0(2) MeV from NNLOopt
* 16O compressibility K_A = 75(7) MeV from NNLOopt
* 20Ne compressibility K_A = 109(6) MeV from NNLOopt
* 40Ca compressibility K_A = 170(10) MeV from NNLOopt
* 16O ground-state energy with NNLOsat including NNN(0) is -127.97 MeV for Nmax=8 and hbarOmega=16 MeV, compared to experimental -127.62 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier SA-NCSM benchmark studies on 4He (Baker 2020) to heavier nuclei including open-shell 20Ne and medium-mass 40Ca, and builds on prior no-core symplectic shell model descriptions of giant resonances (Dreyfuss 2016). The results enable ab initio extraction of nuclear compressibility from giant monopole resonances, connecting to the macroscopic approach of Blaizot (1980) and providing a pathway to constrain the nuclear matter incompressibility K_infinity, which is relevant for the nuclear equation of state in astrophysical contexts such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron stars.
