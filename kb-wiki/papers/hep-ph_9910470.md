# Ferromagnetism of quark liquid

> Usually it is believed that the Hartree-Fock state of quark matter is a Fermi gas state with no polarisation of spins. We examine the possibility of the polarised quark liquid interacting with the one-gluon-exchange interaction. It is suggested that the Hartree-Fock state shows a spontaneous magnetic instability at low densities through the same mechanism as the appearance of ferromagnetism in electron gas. Metastability of the polarised quark liquid is also discussed.

# Ferromagnetism of quark liquid

## Key claim

The paper suggests that quark liquid interacting via the one-gluon-exchange interaction can exhibit spontaneous ferromagnetism at low densities through a Hartree-Fock instability, but the transition is of first order and the polarized state may be only metastable rather than absolutely stable, depending on the QCD parameters (coupling constant and quark mass).

## Method

The author applies relativistic Fermi liquid theory to spin-polarized quark matter, computing the kinetic energy density and the exchange (Fock) energy from the one-gluon-exchange interaction using the polarization density matrix formalism. The total energy density is evaluated as a function of the polarization parameter p (fractional spin alignment) for one-flavor quark matter, and the magnetic susceptibility is derived from the curvature of the energy at p=0. Critical densities for ferromagnetic instability, absolute stability, and metastability are determined by solving χ⁻¹=0, δE=0, and η=0 conditions respectively.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ ≃ 0.16 fm⁻³
- For the relativistic limit (k_Fⁱ ≫ m_q), the polarized state becomes energetically favorable when α_c > 3.23
- The potential contribution to the inverse susceptibility χ_pot⁻¹ is always negative; if α_c > 3π/2 = 4.7, χ becomes negative over all densities
- Example calculation uses s quark mass m_q = 300 MeV and α_c = 2.2 (MIT bag model parameters)
- Critical lines are presented at density n_q = 0.3 fm⁻³ in the (α_c, m_q) plane
- Estimated surface magnetic field of a strange quark star: B_max ∼ O(10¹⁵–10¹⁷) G for n_q = O(0.1) fm⁻³, using quark magnetic moment μ_q ∼ μ_N (nuclear magneton ∼ 5×10⁻²⁴ erg·gauss⁻¹) for massive quarks and ∼10² μ_N for light quarks, with star radius R ≃ 10 km

## Context

This work extends Bloch's (1929) mechanism for ferromagnetism in electron gas, where exchange Coulomb attraction dominates kinetic energy at low density, to quark matter interacting via one-gluon-exchange. It builds on the suggestion that strange quark matter can be stable or metastable (Chin 1978, Witten 1984, Farhi and Jaffe 1984, Madsen 1999) and uses the Fermi liquid theory formalism of Baym and Chin (1976). The paper enables future study of magnetized strange quark stars as a possible explanation for magnetars, and identifies open questions: the need to include higher-order ring diagrams (known to be important for electron gas susceptibility from Ceperley and Alder 1980), and the difficulty of estimating the lifetime of the metastable ferromagnetic state.
