# Probing for an IR-fixed Point in QCD by Superallowed Gamow-Teller Transitions in Doubly Magic Nuclei

> This brief note is to point out that the recent measurements at GSI and RIKEN of the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition in the doubly magic closed-shell nucleus $^{100}$Sn could give an indication for a possibly important {\it fundamental} quenching, thus-far unrecognized, of $g_A$, unambiguously distinct from nuclear correlation effects in the framework of nuclear effective field theory. The result, either confirmed or ruled out both experimentally and theory, can have strong impacts on nuclear physics vis-à-vis with nuclear effective field theory as well as on particle physics relevant for going beyond the Standard Model.

# Probing for an IR-fixed Point in QCD by Superallowed Gamow-Teller Transitions in Doubly Magic Nuclei

## Key claim

The discrepancy between the GSI and RIKEN measurements of the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition in 100Sn may indicate a fundamental renormalization of the axial coupling constant gA (distinct from nuclear correlation effects) that could probe the existence of an IR fixed point in QCD for small flavor numbers, but the Crewther-Zwicky scenario of scale symmetry breaking predicts qssb ~ 1, which would support the GSI result and rule out the RIKEN result as a fundamental effect.

## Method

The authors map the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition in the doubly magic closed-shell nucleus 100Sn to a quasiparticle transition on the Fermi surface in Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid theory, using a scale-invariant hidden local symmetric chiral Lagrangian (GnEFT) with BR scaling. They decompose the quenching factor q into a product of two density-dependent factors: qssb encoding scale anomaly effects from the IR fixed point, and qsnc capturing nuclear correlation effects. The nuclear correlation factor qsnc is computed in the Fermi-liquid fixed-point approximation using the Landau parameter for pion exchange and the BR scaling factor Phi, while qssb is expressed in terms of the anomalous dimension beta'_IR of the gluon energy-momentum tensor and the in-medium dilaton decay constant.

## Key numbers

- GSI measured GT strength: B_GT^GSI = 9.1^{+3.0}_{-2.6}, giving quenching factor q^GSI ≈ 0.73 (gA* ≈ 1.0)
- RIKEN measured GT strength: B_GT^RIKEN = 4.4^{+0.9}_{-0.7}, giving quenching factor q^RIKEN ≈ 0.50 (gA* ≈ 0.6)
- Free-space axial coupling constant: gA = 1.276
- Nuclear correlation quenching factor at nuclear density n0: q^L_snc = 0.79 at n = n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}, giving gA^L = 1.276 × 0.79 ≈ 1.0
- Shell-model calculations for A=18-39 nuclei obtain q = 0.794 ± 0.05 and 0.815 ± 0.04, agreeing with the predicted q^L_snc = 0.79
- The RIKEN result implies an additional fundamental quenching factor qssb ≈ 0.6, giving gA*^RIKEN ≈ 1.276 × 0.79 × 0.6 ≈ 0.6

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GnEFT framework (Rho 1991, Rho 2023) and the Fermi-liquid fixed-point approach (Friman and Rho 1996) to address the long-standing problem of gA quenching in nuclei (Wilkinson 1973, Suhonen 2017). The paper contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) calculations (Wiringa 2023) which find q ≈ 1 in light nuclei (A < 10) and with "first-principles" ab initio approaches that the authors argue fail to capture fundamental renormalization. The result impacts neutrinoless double beta decay searches for beyond-Standard-Model physics, where the decay rate scales as (gA*)^4, potentially suppressing rates by an order of magnitude. The paper also engages with the Crewther (2020) and Zwicky (2023) scenarios for an IR fixed point in QCD with small flavor numbers, predicting beta'_IR = 0 and thus qssb → 1, which would support the GSI measurement over the RIKEN result.
