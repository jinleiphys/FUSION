# The Soft R'egime in NRQCD

> A Lagrangean and a set of Feynman rules are presented for Non-Relativistic QCD with manifest power counting in the heavy quark velocity $v$. A régime is identified in which energies and momenta are of order $Mv$. It is neither identical to the ultrasoft régime corresponding to radiative processes with energies and momenta of order $Mv^2$, nor to the potential régime with on shell quarks and gluons providing the Coulomb binding. In this soft régime, gluons are on shell, and the quark propagator becomes static. Examples show that it contributes to one- and two-loop corrections of scattering and production amplitudes near threshold. The results are readily generalised to any effective field theory with more than one low energy scale.

## Key claim

A consistent power-counting scheme for Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) requires the identification of a "soft regime" (energies and momenta of order \(Mv\)) in addition to the previously known potential regime (\(T\sim Mv^2\), \(|\vec{p}|\sim Mv\)) and ultrasoft regime (\(k_0\sim|\vec{k}|\sim Mv^2\)), contrary to earlier claims that the soft regime could be omitted or mimicked by contact terms.

## Method

The author constructs a Lagrangian and Feynman rules for NRQCD with manifest velocity power counting by rescaling space-time coordinates and fields separately for the soft, potential, and ultrasoft regimes, following the approach of Luke and Manohar (1992) and Grinstein and Rothstein (1993). The classification of regimes is based on identifying all possible particle poles in non-relativistic propagators given the two scales \(Mv\) and \(Mv^2\), and the coupling between regimes is handled via multipole expansions that arise from the different scaling of time and space coordinates in each regime.

## Key numbers

* Soft regime: energies and momenta are of order \(Mv\), where \(M\) is the heavy quark mass and \(v\) is the heavy quark velocity.
* Potential regime: kinetic energy \(T\sim Mv^2\) and momentum \(|\vec{p}|\sim Mv\).
* Ultrasoft regime: gluon energy and momentum \(k_0\sim|\vec{k}|\sim Mv^2\).
* For Bottomium, \(\alpha_s(Mv)\approx 0.2\), providing a window where both \(\alpha_s\) and \(v\) are small.
* The soft quark propagator becomes static to lowest order, with corrections of order \(v\) or higher treated as insertions (e.g., the kinetic energy insertion is \(\mathcal{O}(v^1)\)).
* The potential gluon propagator becomes instantaneous, with retardation corrections of order \(v^2\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Luke and Savage (1997) by incorporating the soft regime previously identified by Beneke and Smirnov (1997), who demonstrated that earlier velocity rescaling rules proposed by Luke and Manohar (1992) and Grinstein and Rothstein (1993) failed to reproduce the correct two-gluon exchange contribution to Coulomb scattering near threshold. The paper provides a self-consistent low-energy Lagrangian for NRQCD that resolves these discrepancies, and the results are claimed to be generalizable to any effective field theory with more than one low-energy scale, including nuclear effective field theories where the small parameter is \(\sqrt{m_\pi/M_N}\sim v\).
