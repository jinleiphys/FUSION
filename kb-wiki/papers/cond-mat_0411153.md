# Finite-size behaviour of the microcanonical specific heat

> For models which exhibit a continuous phase transition in the thermodynamic limit a numerical study of small systems reveals a non-monotonic behaviour of the microcanonical specific heat as a function of the system size. This is in contrast to a treatment in the canonical ensemble where the maximum of the specific heat increases monotonically with the size of the system. A phenomenological theory is developed which permits to describe this peculiar behaviour of the microcanonical specific heat and allows in principle the determination of microcanonical critical exponents.

## Key claim

For classical spin models with a continuous phase transition in the thermodynamic limit, the microcanonical specific heat exhibits a non-monotonic dependence on system size: its maximum first *decreases* as the system size increases from very small values, before eventually increasing for larger systems. This contrasts with the canonical ensemble, where the specific heat maximum increases monotonically with system size.

## Method

The authors numerically computed the density of states for the two- and three-dimensional Ising models and the two-dimensional three-state Potts model using a recently proposed efficient method (Hueller 2002). They evaluated the second derivative of the reduced entropy at the pseudo-critical energy, denoted A₂(l) where l = 1/L is the inverse linear system size, for systems with both periodic and open boundary conditions. A phenomenological finite-size scaling theory was developed based on a decomposition of the finite-system entropy into regular and singular parts, assuming the singular part obeys a scaling relation with the microcanonical homogeneity degree a_ε.

## Key numbers

- For the two-dimensional three-state Potts model with periodic boundary conditions, the slope of |A₂(l)| in a double-logarithmic plot for system sizes L between 48 and 84 yields an estimate of the exponent ratio α_ε/ν_ε = 0.42 ± 0.03, compared to the exactly known value of 2/5.
- The back-bending of the coefficient A₂(l) is observed for the three-dimensional Ising model with periodic boundary conditions, where the modulus of A₂(l) first increases for increasing l (decreasing system size) and then decreases for very small systems.
- For the two-dimensional Ising model with open boundaries, the asymptotic behaviour of 1/A₂(l) follows a logarithmic law in L, consistent with the logarithmically diverging specific heat of that model.

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical analysis of finite systems previously developed by Kastner (2000) and Hueller (2002), who showed that features of symmetry breaking and divergent susceptibility appear already in finite microcanonical systems. The observed non-monotonic specific heat behaviour is a new finite-size effect specific to the microcanonical ensemble, contrasting with the monotonic increase seen in canonical treatments (Binder 1972, Landau 1976). The phenomenological theory enables, in principle, the determination of microcanonical critical exponents from finite-system data, complementing earlier work on extracting the order parameter critical exponent directly from the density of states (Hueller 2002, Pleimling 2004).
