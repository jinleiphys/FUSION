# A $U_A(1)$ symmetry restoration scenario supported by the generalized Witten-Veneziano relation and its analytic solution

> The Witten-Veneziano relation, or, alternatively, its generalization proposed by Shore, facilitates understanding and describing the complex of eta and eta' mesons. We present an analytic, closed-form solution to Shore's equations which gives results on the eta-eta' complex in full agreement with results previously obtained numerically. Although the Witten-Veneziano relation and Shore's equations are related, the ways they were previously used in the context of dynamical models to calculate eta and eta' properties, were rather different. However, with the analytic solution, the calculation can be formulated similarly to the approach through the Witten-Veneziano relation, and with some conceptual improvements. In the process, one strengthens the arguments in favor of a possible relation between the U_A(1) and SU_A(3) chiral symmetry breaking and restoration. To test this scenario, the experiments such as those at RHIC, NICA and FAIR, which extend the RHIC (and LHC) high-temperature scans also to the finite-density parts of the QCD phase diagram, should pay particular attention to the signatures from the eta'-eta complex indicating the symmetry restoration.

# U_A(1) symmetry restoration scenario supported by the generalized Witten-Veneziano relation and its analytic solution

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic, closed-form solution to Shore's generalized Witten-Veneziano equations for the eta-eta' complex, showing that the full QCD topological charge parameter A (rather than the pure-gauge Yang-Mills topological susceptibility) determines the anomalous mass contributions, thereby strengthening the argument that U_A(1) symmetry restoration is tied to SU_A(3) chiral symmetry restoration rather than occurring at a significantly higher temperature.

## Method

The authors solve Shore's generalization of the Witten-Veneziano relation (valid to all orders in 1/N_c) analytically by combining it with the Feldmann-Kroll-Stech (FKS) scheme, which uses a single mixing angle phi and treats eta_NS and eta_S as pure q\bar q states. The analytic solutions for the eta and eta' masses and mixing angle are expressed in closed form in terms of the topological charge parameter A, the pion and kaon masses and decay constants, and the unphysical s\bar s decay constant f_{s\bar s}, which can be approximated by the chiral expansion relation f_{s\bar s} = 2 f_K - f_\pi.

## Key numbers

- For A^{1/4} = 175.7 MeV (lattice value of chi_YM): M_eta = 488.4 MeV, M_eta' = 832.7 MeV, mixing angle phi = 45.07 degrees
- For A^{1/4} = 191 MeV (lattice value of chi_YM): M_eta = 503.2 MeV, M_eta' = 949.8 MeV, mixing angle phi = 50.92 degrees
- The experimental eta' mass is 957.8 MeV at T=0; the RHIC data imply this mass is reduced by at least 200 MeV inside the fireball at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV central Au+Au reactions
- The weighted average chi_YM = (0.1757 GeV)^4 used as input, with an alternative value chi_YM = (0.191 GeV)^4 from Del Debbio (2004)

## Context

This work extends the earlier numerical solutions of Shore's equations by Horvatic (2007) and provides analytic confirmation of the conjecture by Benic (2011) that the temperature dependence of the eta' anomalous mass should follow the chiral quark condensate rather than the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility. The analytic solution shows that the flavor symmetry breaking parameter X necessarily equals f_pi/f_{s\bar s}, and that the topological charge parameter A replaces chi_YM in the standard Witten-Veneziano relation. The results support the interpretation of enhanced eta' multiplicities observed by Csorgo (2009) and Vertesi (2009) at RHIC as signals of U_A(1) symmetry restoration, and motivate searches for similar signatures at finite density in experiments at RHIC, NICA, and FAIR.
