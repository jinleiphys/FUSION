# Baryons and Chiral Symmetry

> The relevance of chiral symmetry in baryons is highlighted in three examples in the nucleon spectroscopy and structure. The first one is the importance of chiral dynamics in understanding the Roper resonance. The second one is the role of chiral symmetry in the lattice calculation of $πN σ$ term and strangeness. The third one is the role of chiral $U(1)$ anomaly in the anomalous Ward identity in evaluating the quark spin and the quark orbital angular momentum. Finally, the chiral effective theory for baryons is discussed.

# Baryons and Chiral Symmetry

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry and its associated dynamics, rather than color-spin interactions from one-gluon exchange, play the dominant role in determining the low-lying baryon spectrum, the nucleon sigma terms, and the decomposition of proton spin; however, the author notes that lattice calculations with non-chiral fermions at finite lattice spacing produce results that differ substantially from those with chiral fermions, and these differences are expected to vanish only in the continuum limit.

## Method

The paper reviews three lattice QCD studies: (1) extraction of the Roper resonance mass using overlap fermions with the Sequential Empirical Bayes Method and variational calculations with varying source sizes; (2) calculation of the pion-nucleon sigma term and strangeness sigma term from overlap fermion simulations on 2+1 flavor domain wall fermion gauge configurations across multiple ensembles; (3) determination of quark spin and orbital angular momentum contributions to the proton spin using the anomalous Ward identity with overlap fermions to normalize the local axial-vector current, combined with form factor calculations of the energy-momentum tensor in quenched approximation.

## Key numbers

- Roper resonance mass from overlap fermion calculation near physical pion mass: approximately 1430 MeV, agreeing with experiment; Wilson-type fermion calculations give Roper masses approximately 300 MeV higher
- Variational calculation with large smeared sources (r.m.s. radius 0.86 fm) on Clover fermion configurations yields Roper mass of 1.92(6) GeV at pion mass ~390 MeV; with sources limited to r.m.s. radius less than 0.4 fm, the excited state mass is 2.19(11) GeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term from overlap fermion global fit: sigma_piN = 45.9(7.4)(2.8) MeV
- Strangeness sigma term from overlap fermion global fit: sigma_sN = 40.2(11.7)(3.5) MeV
- Strange quark spin from anomalous Ward identity: Delta s + Delta s-bar = -0.0403(44)(61)
- Quark spin constitutes 25(12)% of proton spin, gluon total angular momentum 28(8)%, and quark kinetic orbital angular momentum 47(13)% from quenched lattice calculation
- Quark orbital angular momentum fractions 2<L^q_kin> for u and d quarks in the connected insertion add to 0.01(10), essentially zero

## Context

This review extends the chiral symmetry program of Brown and Rho, referencing the little chiral bag model (Brown and Rho, 1979), Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991), and the Skyrme model approach to baryons (Adkins, Nappi, and Witten, 1983). The Roper resonance discussion contrasts quark potential models based on SU(6) symmetry with color-spin interaction (Capstick and Roberts, 2000) against the chiral soliton model (Mattiello, 1987) and the flavor-spin interaction picture (Glozman and Riska, 1995). The sigma term results show a two-sigma tension with the Roy-Steiner equation analysis (Hoferichter et al., 2015) giving sigma_piN = 59.1(3.5) MeV. The spin decomposition enables future dynamical fermion calculations with light quarks and large volumes to confirm the large quark orbital angular momentum fraction, which the author argues arises from meson cloud effects absent in naive constituent quark models.
