# Charmoniumlike tetraquarks in a chiral quark model

> The lowest-lying charmonium-like tetraquarks $c\bar{c}q\bar{q}$ $(q=u,\,d)$ and $c\bar{c}s\bar{s}$, with spin-parity $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$, and isospin $I=0$ and $1$, are systematically investigated within the theoretical framework of complex-scaling range for a chiral quark model that has already been successfully applied in former studies of various tetra- and penta-quark systems. A four-body $S$-wave configuration which includes meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark and K-type arrangements of quarks, along with all possible color wave functions, is comprehensively considered. Several narrow resonances are obtained in each tetraquark channel when a fully coupled-channel computation is performed. We tentatively assign theoretical states to experimentally reported charmonium-like signals such as $X(3872)$, $Z_c(3900)$, $X(3960)$, $X(4350)$, $X(4685)$ and $X(4700)$. They can be well identified as hadronic molecules; however, other exotic components which involve, for instance, hidden-color channels or diquark-antidiquark structures play a considerable role. Meanwhile, two resonances are obtained at $4.04$ GeV and $4.14$ GeV which may be compatible with experimental data in the energy interval $4.0-4.2$ GeV. Furthermore, the $X(3940)$ and $X(4630)$ may be identified as color compact tetraquark resonances. Finally, we also find few resonance states in the energy interval from $4.5$ GeV to $5.0$ GeV, which would be awaiting for discovery in future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors systematically identify several narrow charmonium-like tetraquark resonances in a chiral quark model with complex scaling, and tentatively assign them to experimentally observed states such as X(3872), Z_c(3900), X(3960), X(4350), X(4685) and X(4700); however, they qualify that while these states can be well identified as hadronic molecules, other exotic components such as hidden-color channels or diquark-antidiquark structures play a considerable role.

## Method

The authors solved a four-body Schrödinger-like equation using the complex-scaling method within a chiral quark model that includes Goldstone boson exchange, confinement, and one-gluon exchange interactions. They considered S-wave configurations including meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and K-type arrangements of quarks with all possible color wave functions, and performed a fully coupled-channel computation using the Gaussian expansion method with the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle.

## Key numbers

- Two resonances obtained at 4.04 GeV and 4.14 GeV, which may be compatible with experimental data in the energy interval 4.0-4.2 GeV
- The X(3940) and X(4630) may be identified as color compact tetraquark resonances
- Few resonance states found in the energy interval from 4.5 GeV to 5.0 GeV, awaiting future experimental discovery
- Model parameters include quark masses: m_q (u,d) = 313 MeV, m_s = 555 MeV, m_c = 1752 MeV
- Theoretical meson masses listed for 1S, 2S and 3S states of q\bar{q}, c\bar{q} and c\bar{c} mesons, e.g., D meson 1S at 1897 MeV (experimental 1870 MeV), D_s 1S at 1989 MeV (experimental 1968 MeV), J/psi 1S at 3097 MeV (experimental 3097 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' systematic investigation of bottomonium-like tetraquarks (Yang 2022) and complements their previous study of hidden-charm tetraquarks with strange content (Yang 2021). The chiral quark model with complex-scaling method has been successfully applied before to various multiquark systems including strange-, double- and full-heavy tetraquarks, and hidden-, double- and full-heavy pentaquarks. The results enable future experimental searches for predicted resonance states in the 4.5-5.0 GeV energy interval.
