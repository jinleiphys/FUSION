# Pion-nucleon scattering to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the complete $T$-matrices of pion-nucleon ($πN$) scattering to the third order in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory. The baryon mass in the chiral limit $M_0$ and the low-energy constants are determined by fitting to phase shifts of $πN$ and the experimental octet-baryon masses simultaneously. By using these constants, we obtain the pion-nucleon sigma terms, $σ_{πN}=(34.57\pm 11.85)$ MeV. We also find that a very small strangeness content of the proton, $y \simeq 0$. The scattering lengths and the scattering volumes are predicted, which turn out to be in good agreement with those of other approaches and the available experiment data. The contributions from the third-order amplitudes are discussed in detail. We find that the contributions from the internal kaon lines of one-loop diagrams and the counterterms of the third order are sizeable. In addition, the issue of convergence is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors calculate complete pion-nucleon scattering T-matrices to third order in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, but the quality of the description depends on the fitting strategy: using both octet-baryon masses and phase shifts yields a reasonable chiral-limit baryon mass and sigma term but fails for some phase shifts above 100 MeV, while fitting phase shifts alone gives a good description of all waves but produces an unrealistically large chiral-limit baryon mass and a larger sigma term.

## Method

The authors computed the complete T-matrices for elastic pion-nucleon scattering in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory up to order O(p^3), including tree-level diagrams, one-loop diagrams with internal pion, kaon, and eta lines, and 1/M_0^2 corrections from the relativistic Lagrangian. The baryon mass in the chiral limit M_0 and the low-energy constants (LECs) were determined by fitting to the WI08 partial-wave phase shifts of pion-nucleon scattering and the experimental octet-baryon masses simultaneously, using a common uncertainty of ±4% for all phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_πN = (34.57 ± 11.85) MeV from fit 1 (simultaneous fit to phase shifts and baryon masses)
- Chiral-limit baryon mass: M_0 = (963.58 ± 153.97) MeV from fit 1
- Chiral-limit baryon mass: M_0 = (1530.20 ± 290.87) MeV from fit 2 (phase shifts only)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_πN = (88.99 ± 9.10) MeV from fit 2
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_πN = (106.12 ± 7.36) MeV from fit 3 (M_0 fixed to 963.58 MeV)
- S-wave scattering length for isospin 3/2: a_0+^(3/2) = (-0.132 ± 0.042) fm
- S-wave scattering length for isospin 1/2: a_0+^(1/2) = (0.214 ± 0.066) fm
- P-wave scattering volume for isospin 3/2, J=3/2: a_1+^(3/2) = (0.617 ± 0.014) fm^3
- P-wave scattering volume for isospin 1/2, J=1/2: a_1-^(1/2) = (-0.192 ± 0.018) fm^3
- Strangeness content of the proton: y ≃ 0 (specifically y_N = 0.05 ± 0.05)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory study of pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon scattering (Huang 2017), which failed to describe P-wave phase shifts at high energies because the q·k term from one-loop diagrams was omitted. The present calculation includes these complete loop contributions and also fits to octet-baryon masses simultaneously, unlike the SU(2) heavy baryon approach of Fettes (1998) or the SU(3) extended-on-mass-shell scheme of Chen (2013) and Lu (2019). The predicted sigma term is consistent with lattice QCD results from Bali (2016) and Yang (2016), and the scattering lengths agree with experimental data from Schroder (2001) and Hofe (2015). The results enable consistency checks for more general meson-baryon scattering calculations and provide inputs for dark matter direct detection studies.
