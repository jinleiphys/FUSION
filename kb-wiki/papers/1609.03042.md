# Numerical magneto-hydrodynamics for relativistic nuclear collisions

> We present an improved version of the ECHO-QGP numerical code, which self-consistently includes for the first time the effects of electromagnetic fields within the framework of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD). We discuss results of its application in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the limit of infinite electrical conductivity of the plasma. After reviewing the relevant covariant $3\!+\!1$ formalisms, we illustrate the implementation of the evolution equations in the code and show the results of several tests aimed at assessing the accuracy and robustness of the implementation. After providing some estimates of the magnetic fields arising in non-central high-energy nuclear collisions, we perform full RMHD simulations of the evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma in the presence of electromagnetic fields and discuss the results. In our ideal RMHD setup we find that the magnetic field developing in non-central collisions does not significantly modify the elliptic-flow of the final hadrons. However, since there are uncertainties in the description of the pre-equilibrium phase and also in the properties of the medium, a more extensive survey of the possible initial conditions as well as the inclusion of dissipative effects are indeed necessary to validate this preliminary result.

## Key claim
In the ideal RMHD limit (infinite electrical conductivity), the magnetic field generated in non-central heavy-ion collisions does not significantly modify the elliptic flow of final hadrons, but this preliminary result requires validation through a more extensive survey of initial conditions and inclusion of dissipative effects.

## Method
The authors extended the ECHO-QGP numerical code to self-consistently solve the coupled system of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) equations in the ideal limit, where the electric field vanishes in the fluid rest frame. They implemented a finite-difference scheme with HLL approximate Riemann solver and a Dedner method to enforce the solenoidal magnetic field constraint, using an ultra-relativistic equation of state (p=e/3). The code was validated against exact solutions for magnetized shock tubes, Alfvén waves, and other test problems before being applied to simulate the quark-gluon plasma evolution in non-central heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- Initial magnetic field values of B ~ 10^15 T are produced in non-central Pb-Pb collisions at ultra-relativistic energies, oriented perpendicular to the reaction plane
- The system lifetime is ~ 10 fm/c
- Grid resolution of 0.0025 (400 cells per unit length) was used for the magnetized shock tube test at t=4
- The code uses an ultra-relativistic gas equation of state with p=e/3 for the heavy-ion collision simulations

## Context
This work extends the ECHO-QGP code (Del Zanna 2013) to include electromagnetic fields within RMHD, addressing the need for fully coupled simulations beyond the semi-analytic estimates of magnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions (Tuchin 2013, McLerran 2013, Deng 2012, Gursoy 2014, Bzdak 2011). The results provide a baseline for future studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and related phenomena (Kharzeev 2008, Son 2009), with the next steps being inclusion of dissipative effects and anomalous currents to enable realistic CME simulations.
