# A Global Potential Analysis of the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si Reaction Using a New Type of Coupling Potential

> A new approach has been used to explain the experimental data for the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si system over a wide energy range in the laboratory system from 29.0 to 142.5 MeV. A number of serious problems has continued to plague the study of this system for a couple of decades. The explanation of anomalous large angle scattering data; the reproduction of the oscillatory structure near the Coulomb barrier; the out-of-phase problem between theoretical predictions and experimental data; the consistent description of angular distributions together with excitation functions data are just some of these problems. These are long standing problems that have persisted over the years and do represent a challenge calling for a consistent framework to resolve these difficulties within a unified approach. Traditional frameworks have failed to describe these phenomena within a single model and have so far only offered different approaches where these difficulties are investigated separately from one another. The present work offers a plausible framework where all these difficulties are investigated and answered. Not only it improves the simultaneous fits to the data of these diverse observables, achieving this within a unified approach over a wide energy range, but it departs for its coupling potential from the standard formulation. This new feature is shown to improve consistently the agreement with the experimental data and has made major improvement on all the previous coupled-channels calculations for this system.

# A Global Potential Analysis of the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si Reaction Using a New Type of Coupling Potential

## Key claim
The authors claim that a new second-derivative coupling potential, replacing the standard first-derivative form, provides a unified simultaneous fit to elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions and 180-degree excitation functions for the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si system from 29.0 to 142.5 MeV, solving long-standing problems including the out-of-phase discrepancy. However, the authors explicitly state that "it is not possible at present to provide a solid theoretical foundation" for this new coupling potential, and that further microscopic derivation is still under progress.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-channels calculations using a deformed optical potential with a real potential of Woods-Saxon squared shape and an imaginary potential consisting of Woods-Saxon volume and surface terms. The target nucleus $^{28}$Si was treated as a rotational nucleus with a static quadrupole deformation, coupling the ground state (0$^+$) with the first two excited states (2$^+$ at 1.78 MeV and 4$^+$ at 4.62 MeV) including reorientation effects. The key innovation was replacing the standard first-derivative coupling potential with a second-derivative coupling potential parameterized as the second derivative of a Woods-Saxon shape, with parameters $V_{C_0}=155.0$ MeV, $R=4.16$ fm, and $a=0.81$ fm.

## Key numbers
- Energy range studied: $E_{Lab}=29.0$ to 142.5 MeV for the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si system.
- Real potential depth: $V_0=706.5$ MeV (standard) and $V_0=750.5$ MeV (new coupling); radius parameter $r_0=0.7490$ fm; diffuseness $a=1.40$ fm.
- Imaginary potential parameters (fixed): volume depth $W_V=59.9$ MeV with $a_V=0.127$ fm; surface depth $W_S=25.0$ MeV with $a_S=0.257$ fm; radii increase linearly with energy as $R_V = 0.061E_{CM} - 0.44$ and $R_S = 0.241E_{CM} - 2.19$.
- Deformation parameter $\beta_2$: standard calculations used $\beta_2=-0.64$; new coupling calculations used $\beta_2=-0.34$, which corresponds to the exact value derived from the lifetime of the 2$^+$ state.
- New coupling potential parameters: $V_{C_0}=155.0$ MeV, $R=4.16$ fm, $a=0.81$ fm.
- Threshold energy for irregular behavior: $E_{Lab}\sim 35.0$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous successful application of the same second-derivative coupling potential to the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg reactions (Boztusun and Rae, 2001a, 2001b). It directly addresses the long-standing problems identified for the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si system, including anomalous large angle scattering (ALAS), oscillatory structure near the Coulomb barrier, and the out-of-phase problem between theory and experiment, which previous approaches such as those by Kobos and Satchler (1984) and others had failed to resolve within a single model. The work enables future microscopic derivation of the new coupling term and its application to other heavy-ion scattering systems.
