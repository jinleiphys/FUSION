# Hyperon-nucleon interaction at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory

> Results for the Lambda N and Sigma N interactions obtained at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory are reported. At the order considered there are contributions from one- and two-pseudoscalar-meson exchange diagrams and from four-baryon contact terms without and with two derivatives. SU(3) flavor symmetry is imposed for constructing the hyperon-nucleon interaction while the explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking by the physical masses of the pseudoscalar mesons (pi, K, eta) is taken into account. An excellent description of the hyperon-nucleon system can be achieved at next-to-leading order. It is on the same level of quality as the one obtained by the most advanced phenomenological hyperon-nucleon interaction models.

## Key claim
The hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory achieves a description of the available Lambda N and Sigma N data that is on the same level of quality as the most advanced phenomenological models, but this result is obtained under the working hypothesis of SU(3) flavor symmetry for the contact interactions, which the authors acknowledge may need to be relaxed when higher-precision data become available.

## Method
The authors constructed a chiral effective field theory potential for the Lambda N and Sigma N systems at next-to-leading order, including one- and two-pseudoscalar-meson exchange diagrams and four-baryon contact terms without and with two derivatives. SU(3) flavor symmetry was imposed to relate the low-energy constants across different baryon-baryon channels, while explicit symmetry breaking was included via the physical masses of the pi, K, and eta mesons. The low-energy constants were fixed by fitting to 36 low-energy YN scattering data points plus the binding energy of the hypertriton, and the scattering amplitudes were obtained by solving a regularized coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers
- The fit used 36 low-energy YN data points from Lambda p to Lambda p (12 points from Sechi-Zorn (1968) and Alexander (1968)), Sigma- p to Lambda n (6 points from Engelmann (1966)), Sigma- p to Sigma0 n (6 points from Engelmann (1966)), Sigma- p to Sigma- p (7 points from Eisele (1971)), and Sigma+ p to Sigma+ p (4 points from Eisele (1971)), plus the inelastic capture ratio at rest.
- The regulator cutoff values considered were in the range Lambda = 450 to 700 MeV, with best results achieved for cutoffs between 500 and 650 MeV.
- At next-to-leading order, there are 8 new low-energy constants entering the S-waves and S-D transitions, and 10 coefficients in the P-waves, reduced from 20 independent contact terms for the Lambda N and Sigma N systems in the S-waves alone by imposing SU(3) symmetry.
- The coupling constants are expressed in terms of f = g_A/(2f_0) with g_A approximately 1.26, and the ratio alpha = F/(F+D).

## Context
This work extends the leading-order chiral effective field theory study of the YN interaction by the Bonn-Julich group (Polinder, 2006) to next-to-leading order, following the same scheme applied to the nucleon-nucleon interaction by Epelbaum et al. (2005, 2000, 1998). The results are compared to the Julich '04 meson-exchange model (Haidenbauer, 2005) and to the Nijmegen phenomenological models (Rijken, 1999, 2010). The approach enables future ab initio calculations of hypernuclei using techniques like nuclear lattice simulations, similar to the calculation of the Hoyle state in carbon-12 by Epelbaum (2011).
