# Charge Screening at First Order Phase Transitions and Hadron - Quark Mixed Phase

> Possibility of structured mixed phases at first order phase transitions in neutron stars is re-examined by taking into account charge screening and surface effects. Transition from the hadron $npe$ phase to the quark phase is studied. Two possibilities, the mixed phase and two separate phases given by the double-tangent (Maxwell) construction are considered. Inhomogeneous profiles of the electric potential and their contribution to the energy are analytically calculated. The electric field configurations determine the droplet size and the geometry of structures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Gibbs condition of equal electron chemical potentials in two phases does not contradict the Maxwell construction when electric field effects are properly incorporated, and that inclusion of finite-size effects (charge screening and surface tension) is crucial for determining whether a structured mixed phase or separate bulk phases are realized at first-order phase transitions in neutron stars.

## Method

The authors develop a general formalism based on the thermodynamic potential as a generating functional, from which they derive equations of motion including the Poisson equation for the electric potential. They analytically solve the equation of motion for the electric potential of spherical droplets and slab structures in a Wigner-Seitz lattice, incorporating charge screening effects and surface tension. The hadron-quark (H-Q) phase transition between normal npe hadron matter and quark matter is used as a concrete example, with the volume energy of each phase calculated using a model described in the appendices.

## Key numbers

- Debye screening length for quark matter: λ_D ≈ 5 fm
- With surface tension σ ≈ 10 MeV·fm⁻², minimum droplet radius R_min ≥ 3.1 fm
- With surface tension σ ≈ 100 MeV·fm⁻², minimum droplet radius R_min ≥ 6.6 fm (> λ_D)
- Confinement scale: ≤ 0.5 fm
- Debye screening lengths estimated as λ_D^(I,II) ∼ (5–10) fm
- Surface layer thickness d_S ∼ 1 fm, with confinement radius r_Λ ≃ (0.2–0.4) fm and nuclear diffuseness d_N ≃ 0.6 fm
- Pion mass m_π = 140 MeV, used as typical strong interaction scale

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of mixed phases at first-order phase transitions in neutron stars, particularly the hadron-quark transition. It addresses the long-standing debate between the Gibbs condition approach of Glendenning (1992) and Glendenning & Schaffner-Bielich (1999), who argued the Maxwell construction is always unstable, and the results of other works (e.g., Prakash et al. 2000, Yasuhira et al. 2000) where the Maxwell construction was found stable. The paper builds on the treatment of finite-size effects by Norsen & Reddy (2000) for kaon condensate mixed phases and on the earlier work of Heiselberg, Pethick & Staubo (1993) for the H-Q transition. The analytical solutions for charged density profiles with screening effects are analogous to those developed by Migdal et al. (1977), Voskresensky & Chernoutsan (1977), and Voskresensky & Chernoutsan (1978) for abnormal pion condensate nuclei. The results enable more reliable determination of whether mixed phases exist in neutron star interiors, with implications for the equation of state, neutrino emissivities, glitch phenomena, and r-modes.
