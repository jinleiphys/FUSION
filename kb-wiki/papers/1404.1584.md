# Covariant Spectator Theory of np scattering: Deuteron magnetic moment and form factors

> The deuteron magnetic moment is calculated using two model wave functions obtained from 2007 high precision fits to $np$ scattering data. Included in the calculation are a new class of isoscalar $np$ interaction currents which are automatically generated by the nuclear force model used in these fits. After normalizing the wave functions, nearly identical predictions are obtained: model WJC-1, with larger relativistic P-state components, gives 0.863(2), while model WJC-2 with very small P-state components gives 0.864(2) These are about 1\% larger than the measured value of the moment, 0.857 n.m., giving a new prediction for the size of the $ρπγ$ exchange, and other purely transverse interaction currents that are largely unconstrained by the nuclear dynamics. The physical significance of these results is discussed, and general formulae for the deuteron form factors, expressed in terms of deuteron wave functions and a new class of interaction current wave functions, are given.

## Key claim

The deuteron magnetic moment is predicted to be 0.863(3) using Covariant Spectator Theory wave functions from high-precision fits to np scattering data, which is about 1% larger than the experimental value of 0.857 n.m., implying that the total size of purely transverse interaction currents (including the rho-pi-gamma exchange) unconstrained by np dynamics is smaller than previously estimated.

## Method

The deuteron magnetic moment was calculated using two model wave functions (WJC-1 and WJC-2) obtained from 2007 high-precision fits to np scattering data using the Covariant Spectator Theory with a one-boson-exchange kernel. A new class of isoscalar np interaction currents, automatically generated by the momentum-dependent terms in the nuclear force model, were included in the calculation. The deuteron current was decomposed into three independent form factors expressed as integrals over bilinear products of four invariant functions (the S-state u, D-state w, and two small P-state components v_t and v_s), with contributions from the interaction currents expressed in terms of two new types of wave functions, Psi^(2) and Psi-hat.

## Key numbers

- Model WJC-1 predicts deuteron magnetic moment = 0.863(2) n.m.
- Model WJC-2 predicts deuteron magnetic moment = 0.864(2) n.m.
- Overall prediction = 0.863(3) n.m., with experimental value = 0.857 n.m.
- The missing contribution is about delta-mu_d = -0.006 +/- 0.002, less than 1% of the magnetic moment
- Model WJC-1 has 27 parameters with chi-squared/datum = 1.06 and large nu_sigma0 = -15.2
- Model WJC-2 has 15 parameters with chi-squared/datum = 1.12 and smaller nu_sigma0 = -2.6
- Wave function normalization includes a term from energy dependence of the kernel: -5.5% for WJC-1 and -2.3% for WJC-2
- Nonrelativistic D-state contribution to magnetic moment: -0.044 for WJC-1, -0.037 for WJC-2

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory calculations of deuteron form factors to a fourth generation, following the first paper (Ref. I) that accompanied this work. The calculation uses wave functions from 2007 high-precision fits to np scattering data by Gross (2008) and builds on the invariant function expansion first introduced by Blanckenbecler and Cook (1960) using the notation of Buck and Gross (1979). The results provide a new prediction for the size of the rho-pi-gamma exchange current studied by Casper (1967), Chemtob (1974), Ito (1993), and Hummel (1989, 1990), and enable future calculations of the quadrupole moment and form factor dependence on momentum transfer Q^2 in the remaining two papers of this series.
