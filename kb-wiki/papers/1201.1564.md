# In-medium hadronic spectral functions through the soft-wall holographic model of QCD

> We study the scalar glueball and vector meson spectral functions in a hot and dense medium by means of the soft-wall holographic model of QCD. Finite temperature and density effects are implemented through the AdS/RN metric. We analyse the behaviour of the hadron masses and widths in the $(T,μ)$ plane, and compare our results with the experimental ones and with other theoretical determinations.

# In-medium hadronic spectral functions through the soft-wall holographic model of QCD

## Key claim

The soft-wall holographic model predicts that both scalar glueball and vector meson masses decrease and their widths increase with rising temperature and chemical potential, but the magnitude of the mass shift for the ρ meson at nuclear matter density is only about 1%, consistent with experimental analyses that find a small or compatible-with-zero effect, contrary to larger shifts predicted by some other models.

## Method

The authors use the soft-wall AdS/QCD model with a dilaton background field to break conformal invariance, and implement finite temperature and density via the AdS/Reissner-Nordström (AdS/RN) black-hole metric. They compute the retarded Green's function from the bulk-to-boundary propagator for scalar glueball and vector meson fields, imposing in-falling boundary conditions at the black-hole horizon, and extract the spectral function as its imaginary part. Masses and widths are obtained by fitting the spectral function peaks with a deformed Breit-Wigner function, and at low T and μ masses are also computed as eigenvalues of the Schrödinger-like equation of motion.

## Key numbers

- The dilaton mass parameter c is fixed from the ρ meson mass: c = m_ρ/2 = 0.388 GeV.
- At μ = 0, the scalar glueball dissociation temperature is T ~ 0.116c (approximately 45 MeV).
- At T = 0, the melting of glueballs occurs at μ ~ 0.365 κ c and of vector mesons at μ ~ 0.499 κ c; with κ = 1/2 these correspond to μ ≃ 71 MeV and μ ≃ 97 MeV respectively.
- At T ~ 0.114c (T ~ 44 MeV) the glueball spectral function is almost flat at zero density; at T ~ 0.162c (T ~ 63 MeV) the vector meson spectral function is almost flat at zero density.
- For the ρ meson at T ~ 0 (T = 0.023c = 9 MeV), the mass shift parameter α = (m(0) - m(μ_0))/m(0) ≃ 0.012 at μ_0 = 0.209c = 81.1 MeV (corresponding to nuclear matter density).
- At T ~ 0, a 13% effect on the ρ mass (25% for squared mass) is observed at higher μ; at T = 0.1c the effect reduces to 8% (16% for squared mass).

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of spectral functions at finite temperature and zero density (Colangelo 2009, Fujita 2009, Miranda 2009) to include finite quark chemical potential via the AdS/RN metric. The results for scalar glueball mass decrease qualitatively agree with SU(2) lattice field theory predictions (Lombardo 2008). For vector mesons, the model predicts a much smaller mass shift at nuclear matter density than the Hatsuda-Lee QCD sum rule prediction (α = 0.16 ± 0.06) and the Brown-Rho scaling picture, and is instead consistent with experimental results from the CLAS Collaboration (Wood 2008) that find α = 0.02 ± 0.02. The decreasing mass trend contrasts with the hard-wall holographic model (Jo 2009) and some top-down holographic approaches (Mas 2008) which predict increasing meson masses with density.
