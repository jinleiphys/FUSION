# Subtracted dispersion relation formalism for the two-photon exchange correction to elastic electron-proton scattering: comparison with data

> We apply a subtracted dispersion relation formalism with the aim to improve predictions for the two-photon exchange corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering observables at finite momentum transfers. We study the formalism on the elastic contribution, and make a detailed comparison with existing data for unpolarized cross sections as well as polarization transfer observables.

## Key claim

The authors propose a subtracted dispersion relation (DR) formalism for the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to elastic electron-proton scattering, where the subtraction constant, which encodes poorly constrained high-energy physics, is fitted to available data, thereby minimizing model dependence from unknown higher intermediate states.

## Method

The authors calculate TPE corrections to the invariant amplitudes \( \cG_M^{2\gamma}, \cF_2^{2\gamma}, \cF_3^{2\gamma} \) using fixed-\(Q^2\) dispersion relations in the crossing-symmetric variable \( \nu = (s-u)/4 \). They obtain the imaginary parts of the amplitudes from unitarity relations for the nucleon intermediate state, using on-shell one-photon-exchange amplitudes with Sachs form factors, and perform an analytical continuation of the phase-space integration into the unphysical region. For the amplitude \( \cF_3 \), which is even in \( \nu \) and requires UV regularization in the box-diagram model, they compare an unsubtracted DR with a once-subtracted DR at a low-energy point \( \nu_0 \), fitting the subtraction constant to data.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for the elastic cut due to the nucleon intermediate state is located at \( \nu_{th} = \nu_B = -Q^2/4 \).
- The threshold for the inelastic cut due to pion-nucleon intermediate states is given by \( s = (M+m_\pi)^2 \) or \( \nu_{th} = m_\pi (m_\pi + 2M)/2 - Q^2/4 \).
- The physical region threshold is defined by the hyperbola \( \nu = \nu_{ph} \equiv \sqrt{Q^2(Q^2+4M^2)}/4 \).
- For \( Q^2 < 0.064~\mathrm{GeV}^2 \), analytical continuation into the unphysical region is required only for the nucleon intermediate state cut; above this value, the \( \pi N \) inelastic cut also requires continuation.
- The momentum transfer accessed by current experiments goes down to \( Q^2 \gtrsim 0.001~\mathrm{GeV}^2 \).
- The squared momentum transfer range studied is up to about \( 1~\mathrm{GeV}^2 \).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the elastic TPE contribution by Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2003), who evaluated the two-photon box graph with on-shell vertices, and by Borisyuk and Kobushkin (2008), who used unsubtracted dispersion relations. The subtracted DR approach aims to reduce model dependence compared to these earlier methods, enabling a more reliable extraction of proton form factors and radii from low-\(Q^2\) elastic electron-proton scattering data, particularly in light of the "proton radius puzzle" from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013).
