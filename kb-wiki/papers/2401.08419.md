# Probing the four-fermion operators via the transverse double spin asymmetry at the Electron-Ion Collider

> The chirality-flipping operators of light fermions are currently poorly constrained by experimental analyses due to the lack of interference with Standard Model (SM) amplitudes in traditional observables. In this work, we propose to investigate the semi-leptonic scalar/tensor four-fermion operators of electron and quarks through the transverse double spin asymmetry (DSA) at Electron-Ion Collider, where both the electron and proton beams could be highly transversely polarized. Due to the chirality-flipping nature of these operators, we demonstrate that their interference with the SM results in an unsuppressed contribution to the DSA, and could lead to non-trivial azimuthal $\cos2ϕ$ and $\sin2ϕ$ distributions that are linearly dependent on their Wilson coefficients. This new method has the potential to significantly improve the current constraints on these scalar/tensor four-fermion operators without relying on theoretical assumptions about other types of new physics effects, particularly for the tensor type operator of the $u$-quark. Additionally, our findings indicate that both the real and imaginary parts of these operators can be simultaneously constrained and offer a new opportunity for probing potential $CP$-violation effects. However, it is important to note that these results would be sensitive to the quark transversity distributions, which are currently poorly constrained by the experimental data, but could be significantly improved at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider. Therefore, our work opens up a new avenue to utilize this new spin asymmetry for exploring the new physics effects from the scalar/tensor four-fermion operators.

## Key claim

The transverse double spin asymmetry (DSA) at the Electron-Ion Collider can probe scalar/tensor four-fermion operators at order 1/Lambda^2 without suppression from light fermion masses, but the sensitivity depends strongly on the quark transversity distributions, which are currently poorly constrained by experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculate the transverse DSA for inclusive deeply-inelastic scattering (DIS) at the EIC and EicC, including both Standard Model contributions and contributions from the scalar/tensor four-fermion operators O_{ledq}, O_{lequ}^{(1)}, and O_{lequ}^{(3)} in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT). They define weight-integrated asymmetries using azimuthal weight functions (cos2phi, sin2phi, or 1) to isolate the real and imaginary parts of the Wilson coefficients, then perform a chi-squared analysis to estimate projected sensitivities at 68% confidence level.

## Key numbers

- The Standard Model DSA is estimated to be on the order of -(10^{-8} to 10^{-9}) at the EIC with sqrt(S)=105 GeV and Q=15-55 GeV
- For the tensor operator O_{lequ}^{(3)} with C_i=1 and Lambda=1 TeV, the DSA can reach order 10^{-3} to 1
- Projected 68% C.L. limit on Re[C_{ledq}] (Im[C_{ledq}]) at EIC (sqrt(S)=105 GeV, L=100 fb^{-1}, Lambda=1 TeV): 5.16 (using Kang et al. transversity), 4.53 (using Zeng et al. transversity), 5.12 (using JAM Collaboration transversity)
- Projected 68% C.L. limit on Re[C_{ledq}] (Im[C_{ledq}]) at EicC (sqrt(S)=16.7 GeV, L=100 fb^{-1}, Lambda=1 TeV): 34.60 (Kang et al.), 13.72 (Zeng et al.), 29.69 (JAM Collaboration)
- The analysis uses kinematic region x in [0.1, 0.8], Q in [15, 65] GeV for EIC and Q in [6, 11] GeV for EicC, with inelasticity cut 0.01 <= y <= 0.95
- Assumed beam polarizations: P_{T,e}=P_{T,p}=0.7

## Context

This work extends the analysis of electron and quark dipole operators via single transverse spin asymmetry at lepton colliders (Wen 2023) and at the EIC (Boughezal 2023) to scalar/tensor four-fermion operators. It provides a new method that is complementary to Drell-Yan measurements at the LHC, which constrain these operators only at order 1/Lambda^4 in the massless limit and cannot distinguish real and imaginary parts of the Wilson coefficients. The results enable simultaneous constraints on both real and imaginary parts of these operators, offering a new opportunity to probe CP-violation effects, though the sensitivity depends on improved knowledge of quark transversity distributions expected from the EIC itself.
