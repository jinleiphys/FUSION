# Chiral symmetry patterns of excited mesons with the Coulomb-like linear confinement

> The spectrum of $\bar q q$ mesons in a model where the only interaction is a linear Coulomb-like instantaneous confining potential is studied. The single-quark Green function as well as the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking are obtained from the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation. Given the dressed quark propagator, a complete spectrum of "usual" mesons is obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The spectrum exhibits restoration of chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries at large spins and/or radial excitations. This property is demonstrated both analytically and numerically. At large spins and/or radial excitations higher degree of degeneracy is observed, namely all states with the given spin fall into reducible representations $[(0,1/2) \oplus (1/2,0)] \times [(0,1/2) \oplus (1/2,0)]$ that combine all possible chiral multiplets with the given $J$ and $n$. The structure of the meson wave functions as well as the form of the angular and radial Regge trajectories are investigated.

## Key claim

The spectrum of $\bar q q$ mesons in a model with only a linear Coulomb-like instantaneous confining potential exhibits restoration of chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries at large spins and/or radial excitations, but this restoration is conditional on the dynamical (loop-induced) quark mass function $M(p)$ vanishing at large momenta, which occurs only when the typical momentum of valence quarks in a highly excited hadron becomes large enough that the valence quarks decouple from the quark condensate of the vacuum.

## Method

The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation in the chiral limit ($m=0$) to obtain the dressed quark propagator and the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, using a linear confining potential $V(r) = \sigma r$ with an infrared regulator $\mu_{\rm IR}$ that is sent to zero. With the dressed quark propagator, they solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation for mesons of all three categories (defined by $J^{PC}$ quantum numbers) and extracted the meson masses and wave functions, demonstrating cancellation of infrared divergences in all color-singlet observables.

## Key numbers

- The string tension $\sigma$ is the only scale; all quantities are given in units of $\sqrt{\sigma}$.
- The infrared regulator $\mu_{\rm IR} = 10^{-4}\sqrt{\sigma}$ is used as a good approximation of the infrared limit.
- The quark condensate is given by $\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = -\frac{N_C}{\pi^2}\int^{\infty}_0 dp\;p^2\sin\varphi_p$, where $\varphi_p$ is the chiral angle.
- The normalization condition for mesons of categories 1 and 3 is $\frac{3}{4\pi}\int\frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3}h(p)g(p)=1$, and for category 2 it is $\frac{3}{4\pi}\sum_{i=1}^2\int\frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3}h_i(p)g_i(p)=1$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of Wagenbrunn and Glozman (2006) that first reported the meson spectrum from this model, and it provides the full formalism and additional numerical results. It builds on the Generalized Nambu and Jona-Lasinio model of Orsay (1984) and the demonstration of effective chiral restoration in baryons by Glozman (2000, 2002) and in mesons by Glozman (2003). The model is directly related to the Gribov-Zwanziger scenario of confinement in Coulomb gauge, and the results enable further study of how the mixing between chiral representations (e.g., $(0,0)$ and $(1/2,1/2)_a$ for category 2 mesons) vanishes as the dynamical quark mass goes to zero at high excitation.
