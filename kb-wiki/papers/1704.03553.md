# Toward the description of fluid dynamical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> In this talk we present results obtained when fluid dynamical fluctuations are included in relativistic $3+1$ dimensional viscous fluid dynamics. We discuss effects of the interactions of fluctuations due to nonlinearities and the cutoff dependence.

# Toward the description of fluid dynamical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Including fluid dynamical fluctuations in relativistic 3+1 dimensional viscous fluid dynamics leads to cutoff-dependent renormalization of the equation of state and transport coefficients due to nonlinear interactions of fluctuations, but the observed corrections to average energy density and variance are only about 30-40% of what is expected from analytic calculations and grandcanonical thermodynamics, possibly due to discretization effects and total energy conservation.

## Method
The authors implemented stochastic noise fields in the relativistic 3+1d viscous fluid dynamics code vHLLE, evolving the noise fields via Israel-Stewart relaxation equations with the same relaxation time as the shear relaxation time. They initialized a homogeneous energy density of 10 GeV/fm^3 in a box with periodic boundary conditions, smoothed the noise field over a correlation length of 1 fm, and studied the time evolution of energy density fluctuations and their dependence on lattice spacing (cutoff parameter).

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density: e0 = 10 GeV/fm^3 in a box with periodic boundary conditions
- Lattice spacing Δx = 1 fm used for the time evolution shown in Fig. 1, and Δx = 0.1 fm used for variance studies with noise smoothing over 1 fm correlation length
- Variance of energy density saturates around 10 fm/c after initialization
- Correction to average energy density and variance of energy density both scale as the third power of the cutoff parameter (inversely proportional to cell volume)
- Observed corrections are only about 30-40% of the values expected from the analytic expression G^{xyxy}_{R,shear-shear}(ω,0) = -7T/(90π²)Λ³ - iω·7T/(60π²)·Λ/γ_η + (i+1)ω^(3/2)·7T/(90π²)·1/γ_η^(3/2)

## Context
This work extends prior studies of fluctuations in linearized Bjorken expansions by Kapusta (2011, 2012) and Akamatsu (2016), and linearized numerical implementations by Young (2014). It goes beyond first attempts at fully numerical implementations by Murase (2016) by explicitly discussing the cutoff dependence of nonlinear interactions. The approach is model-independent, in contrast to earlier models by Nahrgang (2011) and Herold (2013, 2016) that used effective QCD models like the quark-meson model. This work enables systematic studies of fluctuation propagation needed for precision extraction of QGP properties, particularly near the QCD critical point and in small systems.
