# Kinetic freeze-out properties from transverse momentum spectra of pions in high energy proton-proton collisions

> Transverse momentum spectra of negative and positive pions produced at mid-(pseudo)rapidity in inelastic or non-single-diffractive proton-proton collisions over a center-of-mass energy, $\sqrt{s}$, range from a few GeV to above 10 TeV are analyzed by the blast-wave fit with Boltzmann (Tsallis) distribution. The blast-wave fit results are well fitting to the experimental data measured by several collaborations. In a particular superposition with Hagedorn function, both the excitation functions of kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_0$) of emission source and transverse flow velocity ($β_T$) of produced particles obtained from a given selection in the blast-wave fit with Boltzmann distribution have a hill at $\sqrt{s}\approx10$ GeV, a drop at dozens of GeV, and then an increase from dozens of GeV to above 10 TeV. However, both the excitation functions of $T_0$ and $β_T$ obtained in the blast-wave fit with Tsallis distribution do not show such a complex structure, but a very low hill. In another selection for the parameters or in the superposition with the usual step function, $T_0$ and $β_T$ increase generally quickly from a few GeV to about 10 GeV and then slightly at above 10 GeV, there is no such the complex structure, when also studying nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The excitation functions of kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_0$) and transverse flow velocity ($\beta_T$) in proton-proton collisions show a complex structure (a hill near 10 GeV, a drop at dozens of GeV, then an increase to TeV) only when using the blast-wave fit with Boltzmann distribution under a particular parameter selection and superposition with the Hagedorn function; this structure disappears when using the Tsallis distribution or alternative parameter selections.

## Method

The authors analyzed transverse momentum spectra of negative and positive pions at mid-(pseudo)rapidity from inelastic or non-single-diffractive proton-proton collisions using the blast-wave model with both Boltzmann and Tsallis distributions, superposed with a Hagedorn function (inverse power-law) for the hard scattering component. They fitted the data using a two-component superposition (Equation 7) with two different sets of parameter values, where the soft component contribution fraction $k$ was taken either close to 0.5 or close to 1. The experimental data came from the NA61/SHINE, PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, and CMS Collaborations, covering center-of-mass energies from 6.3 GeV to 13 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_0$ from Boltzmann blast-wave fit (first parameter set) ranges from $108\pm5$ MeV at $\sqrt{s}=6.3$ GeV to $105\pm5$ MeV at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV for $\pi^-$, with a hill structure near $\sqrt{s}\approx10$ GeV
- Transverse flow velocity $\beta_T$ from Boltzmann blast-wave fit (first parameter set) ranges from $0.30\pm0.02$ $c$ at $\sqrt{s}=6.3$ GeV to $0.35\pm0.02$ $c$ at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV for $\pi^-$
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_0$ from Tsallis blast-wave fit (first parameter set) ranges from $81\pm4$ MeV at $\sqrt{s}=6.3$ GeV to $83\pm5$ MeV at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV for $\pi^-$, showing only a very low hill
- Transverse flow velocity $\beta_T$ from Tsallis blast-wave fit (first parameter set) ranges from $0.19\pm0.01$ $c$ at $\sqrt{s}=6.3$ GeV to $0.22\pm0.01$ $c$ at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV for $\pi^-$
- Entropy index $q$ in Tsallis fits ranges from $1.02\pm0.01$ to $1.06\pm0.01$ across all energies
- The hard component contribution fraction $(1-k)$ in the first parameter set is approximately 0.37 to 0.39 at LHC energies, with no hard component contribution at energies around 10 GeV ($k=1$)

## Context

This work extends studies of kinetic freeze-out properties from nucleus-nucleus collisions to proton-proton collisions, where such excitation functions had been lacking. The authors compare their results to the special energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx10$ GeV identified by Cleymans (2006) for nucleus-nucleus collisions, where the highest net baryon density occurs and a transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated final states takes place. The results enable comparison of freeze-out parameters between small (proton-proton) and large (nucleus-nucleus) systems, showing that the second set of parameters from proton-proton collisions approaches the values obtained from gold-gold and lead-lead collisions. The complex structure seen in the Boltzmann fit may relate to the critical-end-point of the phase transition from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma, though quark-gluon plasma is not expected to form in minimum-bias proton-proton collisions.
