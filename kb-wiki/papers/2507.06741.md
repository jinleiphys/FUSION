# Asymptotic behavior of the Speed of Sound in Dense Matter

> We show that a class of NJL-like models fails to reproduce the expected conformal limit of the speed of sound, making them unsuitable for analyzing the equation of state of dense matter. We then demonstrate how this issue can be resolved within a simple dynamical quark model.

# Asymptotic behavior of the Speed of Sound in Dense Matter

## Key claim
The paper shows that NJL-like models with local interactions fail to reach the expected conformal limit of the speed of sound (c_s^2 = 1/3) at high baryon densities, instead asymptotically approaching c_s^2 = 1. This failure is resolved by introducing momentum-dependent interactions that respect asymptotic freedom, without requiring ad hoc modifications such as form factors or density-dependent couplings.

## Method
The authors analyze the gap equations of the standard NJL model in the T=0 limit, deriving the asymptotic scaling of the dressed chemical potential and density to show that c_s^2 approaches 1 rather than 1/3. They then formulate a dynamical quark model with momentum-dependent interactions derived from QCD in Coulomb gauge, where the mass and chemical potential become momentum-dependent functions. Using a separable interaction with Gaussian form factor γ(p) = e^{-p^2/Λ^2}, they derive the leading-order correction to the speed of sound and compare the asymptotic formula to full numerical solutions.

## Key numbers
- In the standard NJL model, as μ → ∞, the dressed chemical potential scales as μ′ ∝ μ^{1/3} G_V^{-1/3}, leading to c_s^2 → 1 (violating the conformal limit of 1/3)
- The vector density in the NJL model asymptotically becomes n_V → μ/(2 G_V)
- For the separable model with momentum-dependent interaction, the leading correction to the speed of sound is c_s^2 ≈ (1/3) × (1 - (ω_∞/μ) × (1 + 2μ^2/Λ^2) × e^{-μ^2/Λ^2}), where ω_∞ = G_V N_c N_f Λ^3/(2√π^3)
- The Fermi momentum in the dynamical model satisfies p_F = μ′(p = p_F), with the conformal limit p_F → μ recovered at large μ

## Context
This work extends the critique of NJL-like models for dense matter equations of state, following the observation by Baym (2017) that modeling QCD at low and intermediate energies is complex. The paper directly addresses the common practice of introducing ad hoc form factors or density-dependent couplings (as done by Dutra and Bogner, 2001; Ivanytskyi, 2024) to fix the high-density behavior, arguing these lack theoretical foundation. The resolution using momentum-dependent interactions from QCD in Coulomb gauge builds on the work of Reinhardt (2017) and provides a principled framework for future studies of dense matter equations of state, with the authors noting that modeling confinement and deconfinement at intermediate densities remains an open question for enabling comparisons to gravitational wave data.
