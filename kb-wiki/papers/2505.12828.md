# Exploring the spectroscopic features of double-strangeness tetraquark states

> Since the discovery of the $T_{cc}$ double-charm tetaquark by the LHCb collaboration, the field of the theoretical research on heavy quarks has advanced rapidly, with increasing interest in exploring the light quark sector. In this study, the quark model is employed to systematically analyze the double-strange tetraquark system. Both the meson-meson configuration and diquark-antidiquark configuration are considered. The interactions between hadron pairs under various quantum numbers, as well as the possibilities of bound states and resonances, are evaluated. The results indicate the presence of two bound states, $ \bar{K}^{\ast }\bar{K}$ and $\bar{K}^{\ast }\bar{K}^{\ast}$, with quantum number $I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})$ in single-channel estimations. Additionally, by considering the channel coupling between the two configurations, a bound state with quantum numbers $I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})$ and a mass of approximately $1310$ MeV is obtained. Moreover, through the application of Resonance Ground Method, a resonance state is identified in the $I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})$ $\ssqq$ system, with an estimated mass of around 1783 MeV and a decay width of approximately 17 MeV.

## Key claim

Using the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), the authors find that bound states exist in the double-strange tetraquark system only for the quantum number \(I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})\), while for other quantum numbers no bound states are formed; the bound state at approximately 1310 MeV arises specifically when channel coupling between the meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark configurations is included.

## Method

The authors employed the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) to perform dynamical calculations for the \(ss\bar{q}\bar{q}\) tetraquark system, considering both meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark configurations. The resonating group method (RGM) was used to solve the Schrödinger equation for bound states, and the real-scaling method was applied to identify resonance states. The Hamiltonian included color-confinement, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone-boson exchange potentials, with model parameters fixed by reproducing the mass spectrum of ground mesons.

## Key numbers

* Bound state in the \(I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})\) system with mass approximately 1310 MeV, obtained from full channel coupling between meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark configurations.
* Single-channel bound states: \(\bar{K}^{0}K^{\ast-}\) with binding energy approximately \(-1.5\) MeV relative to its threshold of 1386.2 MeV; \(\bar{K}^{\ast0}K^{-}\) with binding energy approximately \(-1.5\) MeV; \(\bar{K}^{\ast0}K^{\ast-}\) with binding energy approximately \(-4.4\) MeV relative to its threshold of 1784.2 MeV.
* Resonance state in the \(I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})\) system with mass around 1783 MeV and decay width approximately 17 MeV, identified via the Resonance Ground Method.
* For the \(I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})\) system, channel coupling within the meson-meson configuration yields a binding energy of approximately \(-61.4\) MeV; full coupling between both configurations yields a binding energy of roughly \(-76.5\) MeV.
* No bound states found for \(I(J^{P})=1(0^{+})\), \(1(1^{+})\), or \(1(2^{+})\); the lowest eigenenergy for \(I(J^{P})=1(0^{+})\) after full coupling is 991.7 MeV, above the \(\bar{K}^{0}\bar{K}^{0}\) threshold of 988.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of tetraquark states from the heavy-quark sector, motivated by the LHCb discovery of the double-charm \(T_{cc}^{+}(3875)\) state, into the light-quark sector by exploring double-strange tetraquark candidates. The results are consistent with earlier one-boson-exchange model calculations by Wang (2024) and Ji (2024), which also suggested \(\bar{K}\bar{K}^{\ast}\) and \(\bar{K}^{\ast}\bar{K}^{\ast}\) with \(I(J^{P})=0(1^{+})\) as possible double-strange tetraquark states. The findings provide predictions for experimental searches for double-strange tetraquark states and advance the understanding of tetraquark formation mechanisms in light quark systems.
