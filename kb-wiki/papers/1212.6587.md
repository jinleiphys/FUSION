# QGP formation time and direct photons from heavy ion collisions

> We investigated the information carried by the data of direct photons, ie, the transverse momentum spectrum and the elliptic flow $v_{2}$ from Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76TeV measured at Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 200 GeV measured at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), in the frame work of (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamical models constrained with hadronic data. We found those direct photon data may serve as a useful clock at the early stage of heavy ion collisions. The time scales of reaching thermal and chemical equilibrium, extracted from those data, are about 1/3 and 1.5 fm/c, respectively. Thus the large elliptic flow of direct photons is explainable. High order harmonics, ie, $v_3$, $v_4$ and $v_5$, of direct photons from Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76TeV are also predicted, as a further test to compete with those who claim new sources of photons to account for the large elliptic flow of direct photons.

## Key claim

Direct photon data from heavy ion collisions can be explained by a delayed formation of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using two distinct time scales for thermal and chemical equilibrium, rather than requiring new sources of photons. The authors find that the QGP formation time (when the system reaches both thermal and chemical equilibrium locally) is about 1.5 fm/c for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, but this explanation only works when the initial hydrodynamical time is sufficiently small (0.35 fm/c).

## Method

The authors used (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamical models (EPOS2.17v3 and a Glauber-based model) constrained by hadronic data to provide the space-time evolution of energy density and flow velocity. They introduced a linear increase of quark fugacity from zero at the initial hydro time to unity at the QGP formation time, modifying the photon emission rate accordingly during this pre-equilibrium period. Direct photon yields were calculated as the sum of prompt photons (from next-to-leading order QCD) and thermal photons (from both the QGP phase using AMY rates and the hadronic phase using massive Yang-Mills theory), with the emission rate during the pre-equilibrium stage bounded by lower and upper limits.

## Key numbers

- Thermal and chemical equilibrium time scales extracted from direct photon data: about 1/3 fm/c and 1.5 fm/c, respectively, for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV with centrality 0-40%, the initial hydro time tau_0 = 0.35 fm/c was used
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c was tested; the maximum elliptic flow at p_t = 2 GeV/c reached only 60% of the measured value for both 0-20% and 20-40% centralities
- The uncertainty in the emission rate during the pre-equilibrium stage (tau_0 to tau_QGP) introduces a theoretical error less than 10% for both the spectrum and elliptic flow of direct photons

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions of two time scales for thermal and chemical equilibrium by Biro (1993) and Shuryak (1992, 1993), and addresses the puzzle that direct photon elliptic flow was predicted much lower than observed in previous calculations by van Hees (2011), Chatterjee (2013, 2005), and Liu (2009). The authors argue against alternative explanations invoking new sources of direct photons proposed by Basar (2012) and Bzdak (2012). The predictions of higher-order harmonics (v_3, v_4, v_5) provide a testable alternative to these competing models, and the results suggest that future systematic studies with AA, pA, and pp systems are needed to fully extract the thermalization and QGP formation time scales.
