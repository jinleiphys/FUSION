# Search for Higher Flavor Multiplets in Partial Wave Analyses

> The possible existence of higher multi-quark flavor multiplets of baryons is investigated. We argue that the S-matrix should have poles with any quantum numbers, including those which are exotic. This argument provides a novel justification for the existence of hadrons with arbitrary exotic structure. Though it does not constitute a proof, there are still no theoretical arguments against exotics. We then consider KN and piN scattering. Conventional and modified partial-wave analyses provide several sets of candidates for correlated pairs (Theta1, Delta), each of which could label a related 27-plet. Properties of the pairs (masses, mass orderings, spin-parity quantum numbers) do not quite correspond to the current theoretical expectations. Decay widths of the candidates are either wider or narrower than expected. Possible reasons for such disagreements are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, under standard assumptions about the analytic properties of strong-interaction amplitudes, the S-matrix must contain an infinite number of complex-energy poles for any exotic quantum numbers, providing a novel theoretical justification for the possible existence of higher multi-quark flavor multiplets such as the **27**-plet; however, this is only a necessary condition and does not prove that such poles lie close enough to the physical region to produce observable resonances.

## Method

The authors performed conventional and modified partial-wave analyses (PWA) of KN scattering with isospin I=1 and πN scattering with isospin I=3/2, searching for correlated pairs of Θ₁ and Δ states that could belong to a **27**-plet. For wide states, they used energy-dependent PWA to analytically continue partial-wave amplitudes into the complex-energy plane and identify poles. For narrow states (total width less than about 30 MeV), they modified the PWA procedure by explicitly adding a narrow resonance hypothesis and comparing fit quality with and without this addition, scanning over mass, total width, and elastic partial width.

## Key numbers

- For wide states with J^P = 3/2⁺: (M_Θ₁, Γ^tot_Θ₁) = (1811, 236) MeV from KN PWA; (M_Δ, Γ^tot_Δ) = (1600, 300) MeV from PDG values for Δ(1600)
- For wide states with J^P = 5/2⁻: (M_Θ₁, Γ^tot_Θ₁) = (2074, 506) MeV from KN PWA; (M_Δ, Γ^tot_Δ) = (1966, 364) MeV from πN PWA
- For narrow Δ-like candidates in the S₃₁-wave (J^P = 1/2⁻) from modified πN PWA: (M, Γ^el) = (1570 MeV, <250 keV); (1630 MeV, <30 keV); (1740 MeV, <90 keV)
- For narrow Θ₁ candidates in KN scattering, the modified PWA found several candidates in S, P, and D waves over the c.m. energy interval 1500 to 1750 MeV, with elastic partial widths restricted to very small values (down to 10 keV in some cases)

## Context

This work extends the search for exotic baryons beyond the antidecuplet containing the Θ⁺(1540) (predicted by the chiral soliton model of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and reported by LEPS, DIANA, and CLAS) to higher flavor multiplets, specifically the **27**-plet. The authors note that while the Θ⁺(1540) appears to be an isosinglet (consistent with antidecuplet membership), a **27**-plet would contain an isotriplet Θ₁ including a double-charged Θ⁺⁺, which could be searched for in K⁺p scattering. The results contradict theoretical expectations from the chiral soliton model regarding mass orderings and decay widths, and the authors suggest that the candidates identified require confirmation through more detailed experimental studies, particularly of inelastic processes.
