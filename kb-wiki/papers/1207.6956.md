# Nuclear reactor fissile isotopes antineutrino spectra

> Positron spectrum from inverse beta decay reaction on proton was measured in 1988-1990 as a result of neutrino exploration experiment. The measured spectrum has the largest statistics and lowest energy threshold between other neutrino experiments made that time at nuclear reactors. On base of the positron spectrum the standard antineutrino spectrum for typical reactor fuel composition was restored. In presented analysis the partial spectra forming this standard spectrum were extracted using specific method. They could be used for neutrino experiments data analysis made at any fuel composition of reactor core.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new analysis of the Rovno Nuclear Power Plant (NPP) positron spectrum data from 1988–1990, claiming that the standard antineutrino spectrum for a typical pressurized water reactor (PWR) fuel composition can be restored and then separated into partial spectra for the four main fissile isotopes, with the caveat that the separation relies on the assumption that the ratios of antineutrino spectra for these isotopes are stable and independent of the calculation method.

## Method

The positron spectrum from the inverse beta decay (IBD) reaction on protons was measured using the RONS spectrometer at the Rovno NPP, which contained 1 m³ of liquid scintillator divided into inner (target, 511 l) and outer (surrounding, 510 l) detectors. The measured positron spectrum was corrected for detector response (energy resolution and energy shift from partial absorption of annihilation gammas) using calibration data and Monte Carlo simulations, then converted to an antineutrino spectrum by accounting for the annihilation gamma energy shift and dividing by the IBD cross section. The resulting standard antineutrino spectrum for the mean reactor fuel composition was then separated into partial spectra for ²³⁵U, ²³⁹Pu, ²³⁸U, and ²⁴¹Pu by applying the known stable ratios of each isotope's spectrum to the mixture, using the fission fractions from the Rovno experiment.

## Key numbers

- Total statistics: 174,000 antineutrino events from IBD reaction on proton, accumulated over three years.
- Antineutrino counting rate: 1050 ± 7 events per 10⁵ s at full reactor power, with 50% efficiency.
- Correlated background: 215 ± 5 events per 10⁵ s during reactor stop.
- Positron energy threshold: 150 keV.
- Fuel composition (parts of fission): ²³⁵U = 0.5895, ²³⁹Pu = 0.2893, ²³⁸U = 0.0748, ²⁴¹Pu = 0.0464.
- IBD cross sections from Rovno spectra (with ILL spectra values in brackets): ²³⁵U = 6.45 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fiss. (6.40), ²³⁹Pu = 4.36 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fiss. (4.19), ²³⁸U = 9.14 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fiss. (8.91), ²⁴¹Pu = 6.28 × 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fiss. (5.77), calculated for neutron lifetime 885.7 ± 0.8 s.
- Fission energies: ²³⁵U = 201.92 ± 0.46 MeV, ²³⁹Pu = 209.99 ± 0.60 MeV, ²³⁸U = 205.52 ± 0.96 MeV, ²⁴¹Pu = 213.60 ± 0.65 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Rovno NPP experiment (Mikaelyan, late 1980s) which first measured the IBD positron spectrum with high statistics and low threshold, and previously demonstrated the effect of fuel burn-up on the antineutrino spectrum. The analysis uses the stable spectral ratios established by Kopeikin (2010) to separate the measured spectrum into partial spectra for the four main fissile isotopes, enabling neutrino experiments at any reactor fuel composition to use these partial spectra for data analysis. The resulting spectra differ from the ILL spectra (Schreckenbach et al., 1985) in the soft part, where contributions from long-lived isotopes like ⁹⁰Sr are concentrated, which is important for experiments searching for oscillations with mixing parameter θ₁₃.
