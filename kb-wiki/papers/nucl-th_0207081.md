# Y-Scaling Analysis of the Deuteron Within the Light-Front Dynamics Method

> The concept of relativistic scaling is applied to describe the most recent data from inclusive electron-deuteron scattering at large momentum transfer. We calculate the asymptotic scaling function f(y) of the deuteron using its relationship with the nucleon momentum distribution. The latter is obtained in the framework of the relativistic light-front dynamics (LFD) method, in which the deuteron is described by six invariant functions f_{i} (i=1,...,6) instead of two (S and D waves) in the nonrelativistic case. Comparison of the LFD asymptotic scaling function with other calculations using $S$ and $D$ waves corresponding to various nucleon-nucleon potentials, as well as with the Bethe-Salpeter result is made. It is shown that for |y|> 400 MeV/c the differences between the LFD and the nonrelativistic scaling functions become larger.

# Y-Scaling Analysis of the Deuteron Within the Light-Front Dynamics Method

## Key claim

The relativistic light-front dynamics (LFD) method can describe the asymptotic scaling function of the deuteron in good agreement with TJNAF experimental data across the entire measured region of negative y, but for |y| > 400 MeV/c the LFD and nonrelativistic scaling functions begin to differ substantially, with the LFD result showing better agreement with data than potential-model calculations in this high-momentum regime.

## Method

The authors calculated the asymptotic scaling function f(y) of the deuteron using its relationship to the nucleon momentum distribution n(k), where for the deuteron the scaling function reduces to the longitudinal momentum distribution f(y) = 2π ∫_{|y|}^{∞} k n(k) dk. The nucleon momentum distribution was obtained within the relativistic light-front dynamics method, in which the deuteron wave function is described by six invariant functions f_i (i=1,...,6) instead of the two S and D waves of the nonrelativistic case, with the functions calculated within a relativistic one-boson-exchange model. The resulting LFD scaling function was compared with the Bethe-Salpeter result from Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1999) and with nonrelativistic calculations using S and D wave functions from various nucleon-nucleon potentials including the charge-dependent Bonn, Argonne v18, Nijmegen-I, Nijmegen-II, Nijmegen-Reid 93, and Paris 1980 potentials.

## Key numbers

- For |y| > 400 MeV/c, the differences between the LFD and nonrelativistic scaling functions become larger, with both LFD and Bethe-Salpeter curves deviating from nonrelativistic scaling functions at this threshold.
- The contribution of the f_5 function (related mainly to π-exchange) becomes important for nucleon momentum k ≥ 2 to 2.5 fm^{-1}, where it exceeds the S and D wave contributions.
- The deviation of the total n(k) from the sum n_{12} = n_1 + n_2 starts at k around 1.8 fm^{-1}.
- For k ≤ 3 fm^{-1}, the LFD result for n(k) nearly coincides with calculations using various NN potentials, while for k > 3 fm^{-1} large differences appear between the LFD result and all potential-model calculations.
- The TJNAF data used for comparison were taken at six values of Q^2 with 4.045 GeV incident beam energy, reaching Q^2 ≈ 7 (GeV/c)^2.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic y-scaling analysis of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1999), who used Bethe-Salpeter solutions for the deuteron, by applying the light-front dynamics method of Carbonell and Karmanov (1995) to calculate the nucleon momentum distribution and scaling function. The LFD approach provides a relativistic generalization of nonrelativistic wave functions that remain probability amplitudes, and the results confirm the consistency of both covariant approaches while showing that for |y| > 400 MeV/c relativistic dynamics must be treated explicitly beyond potential-model approaches. The work enables future y-scaling analyses of complex nuclei at high momentum transfer using LFD, where proper treatment of final-state interactions and nucleon binding remains an open problem.
