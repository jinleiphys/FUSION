# A possible shortcut for neutron-antineutron oscillation through mirror world

> Existing bounds on the neutron-antineutron mass mixing, $ε_{n\bar n} < {\rm few} \times 10^{-24}$ eV, impose a severe upper limit on $n - \bar n$ transition probability, $P_{n\bar n}(t) < (t/0.1 ~{\rm s})^2 \times 10^{-18}$ or so, where $t$ is the neutron flight time. Here we propose a new mechanism of $n- \bar n$ transition which is not induced by direct mass mixing $ε_{n\bar n}$ but is mediated instead by the neutron mixings with the hypothetical states of mirror neutron $n'$ and mirror antineutron $\bar{n}'$. The latter can be as large as $ε_{nn'}, ε_{n\bar{n}'} \sim 10^{-15}$ eV or so, without contradicting present experimental limits and nuclear stability bounds. The probabilities of $n-n'$ and $n-\bar{n}'$ transitions, $P_{nn'}$ and $P_{n\bar{n}'}$, depend on environmental conditions in mirror sector, and they can be resonantly amplified by applying the magnetic field of the proper value. This opens up a possibility of $n-\bar n$ transition with the probability $P_{n\bar n} \simeq P_{nn'} P_{n\bar{n}'}$ which can reach the values $\sim 10^{-8} $ or even larger. For finding this effect in real experiments, the magnetic field should not be suppressed but properly varied. These mixings can be induced by new physics at the scale of few TeV which may also originate a new low scale co-baryogenesis mechanism between ordinary and mirror sectors.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that neutron-antineutron oscillation can occur not through direct mass mixing (which is tightly constrained) but through a two-step process mediated by mirror neutron and mirror antineutron states, where the overall probability is the product of the individual transition probabilities and can reach values as high as ~10⁻⁸, provided the applied magnetic field is properly tuned rather than suppressed.

## Method

The author constructs a 4×4 Hamiltonian describing the mass mixings between the neutron, antineutron, mirror neutron, and mirror antineutron states, including their interactions with ordinary and mirror magnetic fields. The oscillation probabilities are derived by diagonalizing this Hamiltonian and computing the time-averaged transition probabilities, with particular attention to resonant enhancement when the ordinary magnetic field is tuned close to the mirror magnetic field value. The analysis considers the constraints from existing neutron disappearance experiments and nuclear stability bounds to determine the allowed parameter space for the mixing masses.

## Key numbers

- Direct neutron-antineutron mass mixing is bounded as ε_nn̄ < few × 10⁻²⁴ eV from nuclear stability (oxygen decay limit τ_nn̄ > 2.7 × 10⁸ s at 90% C.L.)
- The proposed neutron-mirror neutron and neutron-mirror antineutron mixing masses can be as large as ε_nn', ε_nn̄' ∼ 10⁻¹⁵ eV without contradicting existing limits
- The induced neutron-antineutron transition probability can reach P_nn̄ ∼ 10⁻⁸ or larger, compared to the direct mixing limit P_nn̄(t) < (t/0.1 s)² × 10⁻¹⁸
- For the marginal case β = 1 (equal mixing), the product of oscillation times satisfies τ_nn' τ_nn̄' = 2τ², where τ > 448 s (90% C.L.) from UCN experiments under minimal hypothesis
- For non-zero mirror field B', the bound from n-n̄ experiments gives τ_nn' τ_nn̄' > (0.5 G/B')² × 95 s²

## Context

This work extends the mirror world framework previously developed by Berezhiani and collaborators (Berezhiani and Bento, 2006) for neutron-mirror neutron oscillations, and the broader mirror matter paradigm (Okun, 1980; Foot, 2014). It proposes a new experimental signature where neutron-antineutron conversion can be resonantly enhanced by tuning the applied magnetic field, in contrast to the standard approach of suppressing magnetic fields to achieve quasi-free conditions as in the ILL experiment (Baldo-Ceolin et al., 1994). The mechanism enables testing baryon number violation at scales accessible to current or near-future experimental capabilities, and connects to co-baryogenesis scenarios that could explain the dark matter abundance.
