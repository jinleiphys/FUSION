# Neutrino emissivities and bulk viscosity in neutral two-flavor quark matter

> We study thermodynamic and transport properties for the isotropic color-spin-locking (iso-CSL) phase of two-flavor superconducting quark matter under compact star constraints within a NJL-type chiral quark model. Chiral symmetry breaking and the phase transition to superconducting quark matter leads to a density dependent change of quark masses, chemical potentials and diquark gap. A self-consistent treatment of these physical quantities influences on the microscopic calculations of transport properties. We present results for the iso-CSL direct URCA emissivities and bulk viscosities, which fulfill the constraints on quark matter derived from cooling and rotational evolution of compact stars. We compare our results with the phenomenologically successful, but yet heuristic 2SC+X phase. We show that the microscopically founded iso-CSL phase can replace the purely phenomenological 2SC+X phase in modern simulations of the cooling evolution for compact stars with color superconducting quark matter interior.

## Key claim

The microscopically founded isotropic color-spin-locking (iso-CSL) phase can replace the purely phenomenological 2SC+X phase in simulations of compact star cooling evolution, because both phases produce similar suppression of neutrino emissivity and sufficient bulk viscosity to satisfy observational constraints, but the iso-CSL phase is derived from a consistent microscopic pairing pattern rather than heuristic assumptions.

## Method

The authors compute thermodynamic and transport properties of the iso-CSL phase within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type chiral quark model, solving gap equations for dynamical quark masses, diquark gaps, and chemical potentials self-consistently under compact star constraints. They derive the direct URCA neutrino emissivity from the Kadanoff-Baym kinetic equation for neutrinos, and the bulk viscosity from the flavor-changing weak processes of electron capture and beta decay, following the formulation of Schmitt (2005) and Sa'd (2006). Results are compared with the 2SC+X phase using the parametrization denoted as model IV from Popov (2005).

## Key numbers

- The gapless direct URCA emissivity of normal quark matter is given by epsilon_0 = (457/630) alpha_s G_F^2 mu_e mu_u mu_d T^6, following Iwamoto (1982).
- The integral over dimensionless variables yields a numerical factor 457/5040 pi^6 for the gapless case.
- The bulk viscosity peak for both phases is located at T = 1 to 2 MeV for a frequency of omega = 1 kHz, typical for r-mode excitations in millisecond pulsars.
- The bulk viscosity values for the self-consistent NJL model are up to three orders of magnitude higher than those in Sa'd (2006), due to the self-consistent treatment of masses, gaps, and composition.

## Context

This work extends the study of color-superconducting quark matter transport properties beyond the heuristic 2SC+X phase introduced by Grigorian (2004), which required a phenomenological X-gap for the unpaired color quarks to match cooling data. The iso-CSL phase was originally suggested by Aguilera (2005, 2006) as a single-flavor pairing scheme robust against isospin asymmetry and strong magnetic fields. The results enable future cooling and rotational evolution simulations of hybrid stars with a microscopically justified pairing pattern, and can be applied to protoneutron star evolution in core-collapse supernovae.
