# Limits on $\boldmath n {\bar n}$ oscillations from nuclear stability

> The relationship between the lower limit on the nuclear stability lifetime as derived from the non disappearance of `stable` nuclei ($T_{d}~\gtrsim~5.4~\times~10^{31}$ yr), and the lower limit thus implied on the oscillation time $(τ_{n \bar n})$ of a possibly underlying neutron-antineutron oscillation process, is clarified by studying the time evolution of the nuclear decay within a simple model which respects unitarity. The order-of-magnitude result $τ_{n \bar n} \approx 2 (T_{d}/Γ_{\bar n})^{1/2} > 2 \times 10^{8}$ sec, where $Γ_{\bar n}$ is a typical $\bar n$ nuclear annihilation width, agrees as expected with the limit on $τ_{n \bar n}$ established by several detailed nuclear physics calculations, but sharply disagreeing by 15 orders of magnitude with a claim published recently in Phys. Rev. CRAP.

## Key claim

The paper clarifies that the lower limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time derived from nuclear stability is approximately \(\tau_{n \bar n} > 2 \times 10^{8}\) sec, which agrees with several detailed nuclear physics calculations but disagrees by 15 orders of magnitude with a claim by Nazaruk (1998) that the limit should be \(\tau_{n \bar n} > 10^{16}\) yr.

## Method

The author constructs a simple two-state model for the neutron-antineutron system in nuclear matter using coupled Schrodinger equations, including an oscillation coupling \(\varepsilon\) and an antineutron annihilation width \(\Gamma_{\bar n} \sim 100\) MeV. The mass matrix is diagonalized to find eigenfrequencies and eigenwidths, and the time evolution of the neutron and antineutron wavefunctions is solved explicitly with boundary conditions corresponding to an initial pure neutron state. The decay rate of the neutron is extracted from the exponential decay of the wavefunction intensity at times long compared to \(\Gamma_{\bar n}^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear disappearance lifetime lower limit: \(T_{d} > (5.4 \pm 1.1) \times 10^{31}\) yr, from proton decay searches (Takita 1986, Berger 1989).
- Free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time lower limit: \(\tau_{n \bar n} > (2.1 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{8}\) sec, derived from the relation \(\tau_{n \bar n} \approx 2 (T_{d}/\Gamma_{\bar n})^{1/2}\).
- Antineutron nuclear annihilation width: \(\Gamma_{\bar n} \sim 100\) MeV.
- Free-space oscillation time limit from the ILL-Grenoble reactor experiment: \(\tau_{n \bar n} > 0.9 \times 10^{8}\) sec (Baldo-Ceolin 1994).

## Context

This work extends the standard nuclear physics approach to neutron-antineutron oscillations, as previously developed by Alberico et al. (1982) and Dover et al. (1983), and directly contradicts claims by Nazaruk (1994, 1998) that oscillations are not suppressed in the nuclear medium. The paper enables future reactor experiments, such as the one planned at ORNL (Kamyshkov 1997), which aim to improve the free-space oscillation limit by two orders of magnitude.
