# Doubly Charmed Mesons

> Doubly charmed mesons are studied within a quark model framework. We solve the four-quark Schordinger equation by means of a variational approach by using different interacting potentials. Our results point to the existence of a stable isoscalar doubly charmed four-quark meson with quantum numbers $J^P=1^+$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a stable isoscalar doubly charmed four-quark meson with quantum numbers J^P = 1^+ exists, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific quark-model potentials used (Bhaduri-like and constituent quark model with boson exchanges) and on solving the full four-body problem variationally; the number of bound states is "rather small if not zero" once the four-body problem is properly solved.

## Method

The authors solved the four-quark Schrödinger equation using a variational method with generalized Gaussian basis functions, expanding the radial wave function in terms of Jacobi coordinates. They employed two standard nonrelativistic quark-quark interactions (a Bhaduri-like potential and a constituent quark model with boson exchanges) that reproduce meson and baryon spectroscopy. The four-quark wave function was constructed as a product of color, isospin, spin, and spatial components, with the color states restricted to the two possible color-singlet couplings for a QQ\bar q \bar q system.

## Key numbers

- The J^P = 1^+ (I=0) cc\bar n \bar n state has a four-quark energy of 3861 MeV, which lies 76 MeV below the DD* threshold (the sum of the D and D* meson masses).
- The same state has a root-mean-square radius (RMS_4q) of 0.363 fm, with probabilities of 0.505 for the pseudoscalar-vector (MM*) component and 0.495 for the vector-vector (M*M*) component.
- For the bb\bar n \bar n system, the J^P = 1^+ (I=0) state has an energy of 10395 MeV, lying 217 MeV below the BB* threshold, with RMS_4q = 0.217 fm.
- A new bound state appears in the bottom sector: J^P = 0^+ (I=0) at 10948 MeV, lying 153 MeV below the B1B threshold, with RMS_4q = 0.330 fm.
- Unbound states (e.g., cc\bar n \bar n with I=1) have energies above threshold (e.g., +5 MeV for the (0,1) state and +15 MeV for the (1,1) state) and very large RMS values (30.492 fm and 6.856 fm, respectively).

## Context

This work extends the early proposal by Zou (1986) that doubly heavy four-quark mesons should be bound for sufficiently large heavy quark mass, and the suggestion by Ader (1982) that QQ\bar Q'\bar Q' states should be stable when the mass ratio m_Q/m_Q' is large. The authors compare their results to a simple string model studied by Vijande (2007) that considered only multiquark confining interactions. The finding of a compact tetraquark state in the cc\bar n \bar n system with J^P = 1^+ (I=0) enables experimental searches for doubly charmed mesons at facilities such as LHCb, and the analysis of hidden-color components provides a quantitative criterion to distinguish molecular states from compact tetraquarks.
