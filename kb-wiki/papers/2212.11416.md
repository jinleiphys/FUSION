# Microscopic Calculation of Fission Product Yields for Odd-Mass Nuclei

> Fission data are essential inputs to reaction networks involved in nucleosynthesis simulations and nuclear forensics. In such applications as well as in the description of multi-chance fission, the characteristics of fission for odd-mass nuclei are just as important as those for even-even nuclei. The fission theories that aim at explicitly describing fission dynamics are typically based on some variant of the nuclear mean-field theory. In such cases, the treatment of systems with an odd number of particles is markedly more involved, both formally and computationally. In this article, we use the blocking prescription of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Skyrme energy functionals to compute the deformation properties of odd-mass uranium isotopes. We show that the resulting fission fragment distributions depend quite significantly on the spin of the odd neutron. By direct calculation of the spin distribution of the fissioning nucleus, we propose a methodology to rigorously predict the charge and mass distributions in odd-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that fission fragment distributions for odd-mass uranium isotopes depend significantly on the spin of the odd neutron, and proposes a methodology to rigorously predict charge and mass distributions by combining blocking calculations in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with spin distributions of the fissioning nucleus computed from coupled-channel reaction formalism. The authors acknowledge that their approach uses the equal filling approximation and treats the time-dependent generator coordinate method with inputs from finite-temperature adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory, which has not been formally extended to finite temperature.

## Method

The authors compute potential energy surfaces for odd-mass uranium isotopes using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Skyrme energy functionals and the blocking prescription, employing the equal filling approximation to enforce time-reversal symmetry. They then simulate the time evolution of a collective wavepacket on these surfaces using the time-dependent generator coordinate method with the Gaussian overlap approximation, extracting fission fragment charge and mass distributions by computing the flux across scission. The spin distribution of the compound nucleus formed in neutron-induced fission is calculated using a coupled-channel reaction formalism with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, and this distribution is used to weight the fragment yields from different blocked configurations.

## Key numbers

- Fission barrier heights and potential energy surfaces vary substantially with the configuration occupied by the odd particle, with the spin projection values K=1/2 and K=7/2 explicitly referenced from prior work on 235U
- The level density at relevant excitation energies is typically of the order of 10^6 MeV^{-1} around the neutron separation energy
- The error incurred when using the equal filling approximation for energies of blocking configurations near the Fermi level does not exceed a few keV

## Context

This work extends prior microscopic fission studies that were mostly restricted to even-even fissioning nuclei, with notable exceptions including calculations of fission barrier heights for 235U and 239Pu by Hock (2013) and Koh (2017) using Skyrme Hartree-Fock theory, and one-dimensional fission path calculations for 235U by Perez-Martin (2009) and Rodriguez-Guzman (2017) using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation with the Gogny force. The methodology enables the first microscopic calculation of fission fragment distributions for different blocked configurations in odd-mass nuclei and provides a framework for incorporating entrance channel effects into fission yield predictions, which is important for nucleosynthesis simulations and nuclear forensics applications.
