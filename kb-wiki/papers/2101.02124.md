# QCD pressure: renormalization group optimized perturbation theory confronts lattice

> The quark contribution to the QCD pressure, $P_q$, is evaluated up to next-to-leading order (NLO) within the renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) resummation approach. To evaluate the complete QCD pressure we simply add the perturbative NLO contribution from massless gluons to the resummed $P_q$. Despite of this unsophisticated approximation our results for $P = P_q +P_g$ at the central scale $M\sim 2πT$ show a remarkable agreement with ab initio lattice predictions for $0.25 \lesssim T \lesssim 1 \, {\rm GeV}$. We also show that by being imbued with RG properties, the RGOPT produces a drastic reduction of the embarrassing remnant scale dependence that plagues both standard thermal perturbative QCD and hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) applications.

## Key claim

The renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) resummation, applied to the quark sector at next-to-leading order (NLO) and combined with a simple perturbative NLO gluon contribution, yields QCD pressure predictions that agree remarkably with lattice data for temperatures 0.25 GeV ≲ T ≲ 1 GeV, while drastically reducing the remnant renormalization scale dependence that plagues both standard perturbative QCD and hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt).

## Method

The authors start from the perturbative QCD pressure for three degenerate massive quark flavors and massless gluons, then restore perturbative RG invariance of the massive quark pressure by subtracting a finite zero-point contribution with coefficients determined from the beta function and anomalous mass dimension. They apply a variational mass modification with an exponent fixed by requiring the resulting pressure to satisfy the reduced (massless) RG equation, reexpand in the variational parameter δ, and set δ→1 to recover the massless case. The variational mass is fixed either by a mass optimization prescription (MOP) or by the RG equation itself, with a renormalization scheme change (RSC) parameter B₂ introduced to ensure real solutions; the full QCD pressure is then obtained by adding the NLO perturbative gluon pressure.

## Key numbers

- The RGOPT NLO pressure (RG prescription) at central scale M = 2πT shows remarkable agreement with lattice data from LQCD (2010) for temperatures 0.25 GeV ≲ T ≲ 1 GeV.
- The pseudocritical temperature for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration from lattice QCD is T_pc ≈ 155 MeV.
- The two-loop running coupling uses Λ_MSbar = 335 MeV for N_f = 3.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for the quark sector is P_q^SB / (N_c N_f) = T^4 (7π²/180) (1 + (120/7) μ̂² + (240/7) μ̂⁴) with μ̂ = μ/(2πT).
- The NLO gluon pressure is P_g^PT = (8π²/45) T^4 [1 - (15/(4π)²) g].

## Context

This work extends the RGOPT approach, previously applied to the scalar φ⁴ model and to cold dense QCD at T=0 (Kneur, Pinto, Restrepo 2020), to finite temperature QCD with three flavors. It directly confronts the long-standing problem of large scale dependence in standard thermal perturbative QCD (pQCD) and HTLpt (Andersen, Strickland, Su 2011; Haque, Mustafa, Strickland 2013), showing that RGOPT produces a drastic reduction in remnant scale uncertainty already at NLO. The results enable future systematic exploration of hot and dense QCD, including finite chemical potential regimes where lattice QCD suffers from the sign problem, and call for extending the method to the gluon sector beyond the simple perturbative addition used here.
