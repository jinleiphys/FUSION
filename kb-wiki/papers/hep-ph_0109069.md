# Complete analysis of spin structure function g1 of He-3

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the nuclear effects important in DIS on polarized He-3 over a wide range of Bjorken x, 10^{-4} \leq x \leq 0.8. Effects relevant for the extraction of the neutron spin structure function, g1^n, from the He-3 data are emphasized.

## Key claim

The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of nuclear effects in polarized deep inelastic scattering on He-3, concluding that the simple effective polarization approximation (Equation 2) is only valid for extracting the neutron spin structure function g1^n in the limited range 10^{-4} ≤ x ≤ 0.1, contrary to earlier claims that it works up to x = 0.9. The inclusion of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom (Δ isobar) and coherent nuclear effects (shadowing and antishadowing) are necessary for a complete description over the full range 10^{-4} ≤ x ≤ 0.8.

## Method

The authors combine the convolution approach with spin-dependent light-cone nucleon momentum distributions from the He-3 ground-state wave function (using the PEST potential with 5 channels), including off-shell nucleon modifications from the Quark Meson Coupling model. They incorporate non-diagonal interference transitions n → Δ^0 and p → Δ^+ to account for non-nucleonic degrees of freedom, and use the Gribov-Glauber multiple scattering formalism with an effective hadronic fluctuation model to calculate nuclear shadowing and antishadowing corrections, with the antishadowing magnitude constrained by the Bjorken sum rule for the A=3 system.

## Key numbers

- Effective neutron polarization in polarized He-3: P_n = 0.879 (PEST potential with 5 channels); average over multiple models gives P_n = 0.86 ± 0.02
- Effective proton polarization in polarized He-3: P_p = -0.021 (PEST potential with 5 channels); average over multiple models gives P_p = -0.028 ± 0.004
- Ratio of triton to nucleon Bjorken sum rules from experiment: g_A|_triton / g_A = 0.956 ± 0.004, where g_A|_triton = 1.211 ± 0.002 and g_A = 1.2670 ± 0.0035
- The nucleon-only prediction for this ratio (P_n - 2P_p) × (off-shell correction) = 0.921 × (off-shell factor), underestimating experiment by about 3.5%
- Required combination of effective Δ-isobar polarizations: 2(P_n→Δ^0 + 2P_p→Δ^+) = -0.025
- Shadowing correction at x = 10^{-4}: 11% (for cross-over x_0 = 0.03) to 12% (for x_0 = 0.07)
- Maximum antishadowing correction: 3% (for x_0 = 0.03) to 7% (for x_0 = 0.07)
- At x = 0.2, the Δ-isobar contribution decreases g_1^{He-3} by 12% relative to the convolution prediction without Δ
- First moments at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2: Γ_p = 0.151, Γ_n = -0.060 (on-shell); Γ̃_p = 0.147, Γ̃_n = -0.060 (off-shell, valence region 0.2 ≤ x ≤ 0.7)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear effects in polarized He-3 by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1993) and by Frankfurt, Guzey, and Strikman (1996), providing the first unified treatment combining spin depolarization, Fermi motion, off-shell effects, Δ-isobar contributions, and coherent nuclear shadowing/antishadowing over the full range 10^{-4} ≤ x ≤ 0.8. The authors find that the simple effective polarization formula used by the E154, E155, and HERMES collaborations to extract g_1^n from He-3 data is only valid for x ≤ 0.1, and that Δ-isobar contributions and nuclear shadowing/antishadowing must be included for accurate extraction at larger and smaller x respectively. This enables more reliable extraction of the neutron spin structure function from existing and future polarized He-3 data.
