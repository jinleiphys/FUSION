# Thermodynamic stability in relativistic viscous and spin hydrodynamics

> We have applied thermodynamic stability analysis to derive the stability and causality conditions for conventional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and spin hydrodynamics. We obtain the thermodynamic stability conditions for second-order relativistic hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscous tensors, finding them identical to those derived from linear mode analysis. We then derive the thermodynamic stability conditions for minimal causal extended second-order spin hydrodynamics in canonical form, both with and without viscous tensors. Without viscous tensors, the constraints from thermodynamic stability exactly match those from linear mode analysis. In the presence of viscous tensors, the thermodynamic stability imposes more stringent constraints than those obtained from linear mode analysis. Our results suggest that conditions derived from thermodynamic stability analysis can guarantee both causality and stability in linear mode analysis.

## Key claim

Thermodynamic stability analysis yields stability and causality conditions for second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and spin hydrodynamics that are identical to those from linear mode analysis when viscous tensors are absent, but imposes more stringent constraints than linear mode analysis when viscous tensors are present.

## Method

The authors applied thermodynamic stability analysis based on the second law of thermodynamics and the principle of maximizing total entropy in equilibrium states, constructing the information current \(E^{\mu}\) for small perturbations around thermodynamic equilibrium. They derived the conditions that \(E^{\mu}\) must satisfy (positivity for any timelike future-directed normal vector, zero only when all perturbations vanish, and non-positive divergence) and compared these constraints with those obtained from conventional linear mode analysis for both conventional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics and minimal causal extended second-order spin hydrodynamics in canonical form.

## Key numbers

- For second-order viscous hydrodynamics: the thermodynamic stability conditions are \(c_s^2 > 0\), \(\tau_\pi > 0\), \(\tau_\Pi > 0\), and \(1 - c_s^2 - \frac{4\eta}{3\tau_\pi(e+P)} - \frac{\zeta}{\tau_\Pi(e+P)} > 0\), identical to linear mode analysis results from Denicol (2008) and Pu (2009).
- For spin hydrodynamics without viscous tensors: the conditions include \(c_s^2 > 0\), \(\gamma_s > 0\), \(\lambda > 0\), \(\tau_\phi > 0\), \(\tau_q > 0\), \(\delta\omega_{\alpha\beta}\delta S^{\alpha\beta} > 0\), \(1 - \frac{\lambda}{\tau_q(e+P)} - \frac{\gamma_s}{\tau_\phi(e+P)} > 0\), and \(1 - c_s^2 - \frac{(3c_s^2+1)\lambda}{\tau_q(e+P)} > 0\), matching the causality conditions from linear mode analysis in Xie (2023).
- For spin hydrodynamics with viscous tensors: the thermodynamic stability imposes more stringent constraints than those from linear mode analysis, though the specific numerical inequalities are not given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic stability framework of Gavassino (2021) to spin hydrodynamics, building on prior linear mode analyses of causality and stability in relativistic hydrodynamics by Hiscock (1985, 1987), Denicol (2008), and Pu (2009). It directly addresses the finding by Xie (2023) that minimal extended second-order spin hydrodynamics appears unstable at finite wavelengths despite satisfying asymptotic stability conditions, and provides an alternative approach to derive stability conditions. The results enable more reliable constraints on transport coefficients for spin hydrodynamics used in modeling quark-gluon plasma evolution in relativistic heavy ion collisions, particularly for understanding spin polarization phenomena observed by the STAR collaboration.
