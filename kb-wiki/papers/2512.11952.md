# Renormalization Group Evolution for In-medium Energy Correlators

> We present a first-principles analysis of the renormalization group (RG) evolution of the two-point energy-energy correlator (EEC) in light-quark and gluon jets propagating through nuclear matter. Our work focuses on the analytic structure of the RG equations in the thin-medium regime, highlighting how collinear emissions in the presence of a dense QCD medium reshape the EEC observables. We work in the opacity expansion of the SCET$_{\rm G}$ formalism, where the propagating quarks and gluons interact with the medium via Glauber gluon exchanges. We compute the corresponding one-loop jet functions using the medium-induced splitting kernels at first order in opacity and perform resummation at leading logarithmic (LL) order. In particular, we identify an experimentally accessible regime of jet energies and EEC angles where one can directly investigate the medium-induced scale evolution and extract the corresponding opacity-one correction to the anomalous dimensions. Furthermore, we demonstrate analytically, using the method of regions, the Coulomb-logarithmic enhancement regulated by plasma screening for EEC. We compare our theoretical predictions with experimental data in $p$-Pb collisions and make projections for O-O collisions to test whether energy correlators could serve as sensitive probe of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) dynamics in small collision systems, offering a robust and model-independent avenue for constraining jet evolution in QCD matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the thin-medium regime, for jet energies and EEC angles satisfying Λ_QCD/p_T ≪ θ ≪ √(8π/p_T L_eff), medium effects manifest solely as a correction Δγ(α_s) to the vacuum anomalous dimension γ(α_s), enabling a model-independent extraction of jet-medium interaction strength from the modified scale evolution, rather than from fixed-order medium corrections.

## Method

The authors work within the SCET_G formalism, using Glauber gluon exchanges to describe screened-Coulomb-like scatterings between jet partons and the QGP medium at first order in opacity. They compute the one-loop medium-modified exclusive and semi-inclusive jet functions for the two-point energy-energy correlator using medium-induced splitting kernels, then derive the renormalization group equations governing the scale evolution of these jet functions. The RG evolution is performed at leading logarithmic (LL) order, with the medium correction to the anomalous dimension extracted from the opacity-one contribution. The method of regions is used to analytically demonstrate Coulomb-logarithmic enhancement regulated by plasma screening.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum anomalous dimension for quark jets at N=3: γ_qq^vac(3) = (25/6)C_F
- The vacuum anomalous dimension for gluon jets at N=3: γ_gg^vac(3) = (14/5)C_A + (2/3)N_f
- The vacuum anomalous dimension for quark-to-gluon mixing at N=3: γ_gq^vac(3) = -(7/6)C_F
- The vacuum anomalous dimension for gluon-to-quark mixing at N=3: γ_qg^vac(3) = -(14/15)T_F N_f
- The NLO exclusive EEC jet function for quark jets in vacuum contains the term (α_s(μ^2)/2π) × (3C_F/4) × Θ(R-θ)/θ^2
- The NLO exclusive EEC jet function for gluon jets in vacuum contains the term (α_s(μ^2)/2π) × ((7/10)C_A + (1/10)N_f T_f) × Θ(R-θ)/θ^2
- The hard-collinear matching coefficient for quark jets includes d_qq = C_F(13/2 - 2π²/3)
- The identified medium-induced resummation region is Λ_QCD/p_T ≪ θ ≪ √(8π/p_T L_eff), where L_eff is the effective medium length

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based factorization and resummation framework for EEC developed for vacuum collisions (Dixon 2019, Lee 2022) to heavy-ion collisions by incorporating medium effects through the SCET_G formalism (Ovanesyan 2011). It contrasts with existing approaches that rely on Monte-Carlo simulations (Yang 2023, Barata 2023, Bossi 2024, Xing 2024) or fixed-order medium corrections (PhysRevLett.130.262301, Andres 2023, Barata 2023) by providing a first-principles RG evolution analysis where medium effects modify the anomalous dimensions themselves. The work enables a model-independent extraction of QGP properties from EEC measurements in small collision systems such as p-Pb and O-O, and may explain the unexpectedly strong p_T dependence observed in p-Pb data (Liang-Gilman 2025).
