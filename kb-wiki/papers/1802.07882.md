# Production of $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$ in $Λ_c\rightarrow\bar{K}^0ηp$ $(πN)$ decay

> In order to study the properties of the $N^*$(1535) and $N^*$(1650) we calculate the mass distributions of $M B$ in the $Λ_c \rightarrow \bar{K}^0 M B$ decay, with $MB=πN(I=1/2),ηp$ and $KΣ(I=1/2)$. We do this by calculating the tree-level and loop contributions, mixing pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon channels using the local hidden gauge formalism. The loop contributions for each channel are calculated using the chiral unitary approach. We observe that for the $ηN$ mass distribution only the $N^*$(1535) is seen, with the $N^*$(1650) contributing to the width of the curve, but for the $πN$ mass distribution both resonances are clearly visible. In the case of $MB=KΣ$, we found that the strength of the $KΣ$ mass distribution is smaller than that of the mass distributions of the $πN$ and $ηp$ in the $Λ_c^+\rightarrow\bar{K}^0πN$ and $Λ_c^+\rightarrow\bar{K}^0ηp$ processes, in spite of this channel having a large coupling to the $N^*(1650)$. This is because the $KΣ$ pair production is suppressed in the primary production from the $Λ_c$ decay.

# Production of $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$ in $\Lambda_c\rightarrow\bar{K}^0\eta p$ $(\pi N)$ decay

## Key claim
The $\eta N$ mass distribution in $\Lambda_c^+\rightarrow\bar{K}^0\eta p$ decay shows only the $N^*(1535)$ as a visible peak, with the $N^*(1650)$ contributing only to the width of the curve, while in the $\pi N$ mass distribution both resonances are clearly visible; the $K\Sigma$ mass distribution strength is smaller than those of $\pi N$ and $\eta p$ despite the $N^*(1650)$ having a large coupling to $K\Sigma$, because $K\Sigma$ pair production is suppressed in the primary production from the $\Lambda_c$ decay.

## Method
The authors calculated the mass distributions of meson-baryon ($MB$) pairs in $\Lambda_c \rightarrow \bar{K}^0 MB$ decays for $MB = \pi N(I=1/2), \eta p$, and $K\Sigma(I=1/2)$ by including both tree-level and loop contributions. Pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon channels were mixed using the local hidden gauge formalism, and the loop contributions for each channel were calculated using the chiral unitary approach following the model of Garzon (2014) that dynamically generates both $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$ resonances. The primary production vertex was treated at the quark level with the $ud$ pair in $\Lambda_c^+$ acting as a spectator, and the meson-baryon scattering amplitude was evaluated using the chiral unitary approach with $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$, $\rho N$, and $\pi\Delta$($d$ wave) channels.

## Key numbers
- The $N^*(1535)$ coupling to $\pi N$ is $g = 1.03 + i0.21$ and to $\eta N$ is $g = 42.2$ (absolute value of $gG$ at the pole), while the $N^*(1650)$ coupling to $\pi N$ is $g = 1.37 + i0.54$ and to $\eta N$ is $g = 34.0$ (absolute value of $gG$ at the pole), taken from Garzon (2014).
- The absolute values of $g_{N^*,M'B'}G_{M'B'}$ at the resonance pole for $N^*(1535)$ are: $\pi N$ = 25.2 MeV, $\eta N$ = 42.2 MeV, $K\Lambda$ = 40.7 MeV, $K\Sigma$ = 3.2 MeV, $\rho N$ = 17.9 MeV, $\pi\Delta$ = 8.8 MeV.
- The absolute values of $g_{N^*,M'B'}G_{M'B'}$ at the resonance pole for $N^*(1650)$ are: $\pi N$ = 36.6 MeV, $\eta N$ = 34.0 MeV, $K\Lambda$ = 20.3 MeV, $K\Sigma$ = 31.6 MeV, $\rho N$ = 8.1 MeV, $\pi\Delta$ = 9.0 MeV.
- The $N^*(1535)$ energy is about 200 MeV below the $K\Sigma$ threshold.

## Context
This work extends the calculations of Xie (2017) on $\Lambda_c^+\rightarrow\bar{K}^0\eta p$ decay by incorporating vector-baryon channels and the $N^*(1650)$ resonance, using the model developed by Garzon (2014) that dynamically generates both $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$ through meson-baryon interactions. The study builds on the chiral unitary approach of Inoue (2001) and Kaiser (1995, 1996) for the $N^*(1535)$, and on the vector meson-baryon studies of Oset (2009) and Garzon (2012, 2013) for the $N^*(1650)$. The results enable future experimental comparisons of the $\Lambda_c^+\rightarrow\bar{K}^0\pi N$ and $\Lambda_c^+\rightarrow\bar{K}^0\eta p$ mass distributions to distinguish between different models of these nucleon resonances, particularly testing whether the $N^*(1650)$ appears as a separate peak or only contributes to the width.
