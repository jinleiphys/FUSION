# Nonlinear coupling of flow harmonics in heavy-ion collisions

> Anisotropic flow coefficients beyond triangular flow receive important contributions from lower-order harmonics through nonlinear coupling. We present a theoretical framework which allows one to quantify the contribution induced by such nonlinear couplings to any flow harmonic of any order. We show the effectiveness of this formalism through an application to hexagonal flow, $V_6$. We study, in particular, the coupling of $V_6$ to triangular flow, $V_3$, in Pb+Pb collisions at $~\sqrt[]{s}=2.76$ TeV, using both Large Hadron Collider data and event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical matrix-inversion formalism to quantify the nonlinear coupling contributions to any flow harmonic from lower-order harmonics, and demonstrates that neglecting off-diagonal correlations between nonlinear terms (as done in prior analyses) leads to a worse determination of the coupling coefficients, specifically for hexagonal flow \(V_6\).

## Method

The author decomposes a given flow harmonic \(V\) into a sum of products of lower-order harmonics \(W_k\) with unknown coupling coefficients \(\chi_k\), plus a residual \(U\) that is uncorrelated with each \(W_k\). The coefficients are obtained by solving the linear system \(\langle W_j^* V \rangle = \sum_k \chi_k \langle W_j^* W_k \rangle\) via matrix inversion of the Hermitian matrix \(\Sigma_{jk} = \langle W_j^* W_k \rangle\). The formalism is applied to \(V_6\) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV using ALICE and ATLAS experimental data, and compared to event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations with the v-USPHYDRO code.

## Key numbers

* The coupling coefficient \(\chi_{63}\) (between \(V_6\) and \(V_3\)) shows a mild centrality dependence when the full matrix \(\Sigma^{(6)}\) is used, but a much stronger centrality dependence when off-diagonal terms are neglected (Fig. 2).
* The matrix \(\Sigma^{(6)}\) is approximately diagonal, with only one tiny off-diagonal element: the three-plane correlator \(\langle V_3^2 U_4^* V_2^* \rangle\) (Fig. 1).
* The analysis uses Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of nonlinear flow harmonic coupling introduced in Giacalone (2018) and builds on prior studies of nonlinear flow modes by Qian (2016), Yan (2015), and Zhao (2017), which neglected mutual correlations between nonlinear terms. The matrix formalism enables a more accurate extraction of coupling coefficients from experimental data and hydrodynamic calculations, and the author notes that the coefficients \(\chi\) depend mildly on centrality and medium properties, leaving open the question of what specific late-time dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma they probe.
