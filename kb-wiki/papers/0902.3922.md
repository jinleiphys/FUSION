# Multiplicity fluctuations due to the temperature fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We investigate the multiplicity fluctuations observed in high-energy nuclear collisions attributing them to intrinsic fluctuations of temperature of the hadronizing system formed in such processes. To account for these fluctuations we replace the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs (BG) statistics by the non-extensive Tsallis statistics characterized by the nonextensivity parameter q, with |q-1| being a direct measure of fluctuations. In the limit of vanishing fluctuations, q --> 1 and Tsallis statistics converges to the usual BG. We evaluate the nonextensivity parameter q and its dependence on the hadronizing system size from the experimentally observed collision centrality dependence of the mean multiplicity, <N>, and its variance, Var(N). We attribute the observed system size dependence of q to the finiteness of the hadronizing source with q = 1 corresponding to an infinite, thermalized source with a fixed temperature, and with q > 1 (which is observed) corresponding to a finite source in which both the temperature and energy fluctuate.

## Key claim

The paper attributes the observed centrality dependence of multiplicity fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions to intrinsic temperature fluctuations of the hadronizing system, modeled using non-extensive Tsallis statistics. The key condition is that this explanation works only when one also accounts for energy transfer between the hadronizing source and its surroundings (spectator nucleons), introducing a \(q\)-dependent effective temperature \(T_{eff}\).

## Method

The authors replace the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential distribution with the Tsallis \(q\)-exponential distribution, where the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) is directly related to temperature fluctuations via \(q = 1 + Var(T)/\langle T\rangle^2\). They derive a relation between the scaled variance of the multiplicity distribution, \(Var(N)/\langle N\rangle\), and the mean multiplicity \(\langle N\rangle\) using the concept of an effective temperature \(T_{eff}\) that depends on \(q\) and accounts for energy transfer. The system size dependence of \(q-1\) is then obtained by connecting temperature fluctuations to the heat capacity \(C_V\) and the fraction of participating nucleons \(f = N_P/A\), using a Lindhard-type uncertainty relation between energy and temperature fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* For negatively charged particles from \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at 158\(A\) GeV (NA49 experiment), the scaled variance \(Var(N)/\langle N\rangle\) increases from near unity for central collisions to larger values for peripheral collisions, with the data described using parameters \(n_0 = 0.642\) and \(c = 4.1\).
* The dependence of \(q-1\) on the number of participants \(N_P\) for \(Pb+Pb\) collisions is fitted by \(q-1 = (1 - N_P/A)/(a N_P)\) with \(a = 0.98\) and nuclear mass number \(A = 207\).
* For the scaled variance of negatively charged particles as a function of \(N_P/A\), the data from p+p, C+C, Si+Si, and Pb+Pb collisions are described by \(Var(N)/\langle N\rangle = \omega_0 + (1/a')(1 - N_P/A)\) with \(\omega_0 = 0.79\) and \(a' = 1.0\) (solid line) or with \(\omega_0 = 0.87\) and \(a = 0.83\) (dashed line).
* Transverse momentum fluctuations quantified by \(\Phi(p_T)\) for negative particles at 158\(A\) GeV show a linear dependence on \(N_P/A\): \(\Phi(p_T) = \Phi_{N_P=A} + b(1 - N_P/A)\) with \(\Phi_{N_P=A} = 0.48\) MeV and \(b = 4.8\) MeV.
* For mean multiplicities, the linear fit using Eq. (prediction2) gives parameter \(c/a' = 2.75\), and using Eq. (prediction1) gives \(c/a = 1.8\), with \(n_0\) values of 0.559 (p+p), 0.575 (C+C), 0.657 (Si+Si), and 0.642 (Pb+Pb).

## Context

This work extends the application of non-extensive Tsallis statistics to multiplicity fluctuations, building on earlier studies by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2000) that connected temperature fluctuations to the nonextensivity parameter \(q\). It directly addresses the failure of standard statistical models and Monte Carlo event generators like HIJING, HSD, and UrQMD to reproduce the observed centrality dependence of the scaled variance, as reported by the NA49 and WA98 experiments. The results enable a unified description of both mean multiplicity and multiplicity fluctuations in terms of the participant fraction \(N_P/A\), and also connect to transverse momentum fluctuations measured by NA49, suggesting a scaling behavior that can be tested in future experiments at higher energies.
