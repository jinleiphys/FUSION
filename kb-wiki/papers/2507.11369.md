# Giant Dipole Resonance and Related Spin-dependent Excitations

> The time-dependent Hartree-Fock equation is solved by the Wigner Function Moments method taking into account spin degrees of freedom. Energies and reduced transition probabilities of $K^π=0^-$, $1^-$ and $2^-$ excitations are calculated taking $^{164}$Dy as an example. The spin degrees of freedom give rise to the electric Spin Dipole Resonance. Its properties and interplay with the Giant Dipole Resonance are investigated. The deformation-induced splitting of the spin $M2$ resonance is discussed. The results of calculations are compared with the experimental data and other theoretical studies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including spin degrees of freedom in the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) solution of the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation gives rise to an electric Spin Dipole Resonance (SDR) that coexists and interacts with the conventional Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR), but the present study is limited to first-rank tensors and uses a separable Hamiltonian with harmonic oscillator plus spin-orbit potential rather than full Skyrme forces, so the results are exploratory for deformed nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the TDHF equation using the Wigner Function Moments method, integrating over phase space with weights \(r_\mu\) and \(p_\mu\) to obtain dynamical equations for collective variables that include spin-scalar, spin-vector, and spin-flip components. They employ a separable Hamiltonian consisting of a harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit potential plus dipole-dipole, quadrupole-quadrupole, and spin-dipole–spin-dipole interactions, and linearize the equations in the small-amplitude approximation to compute eigenfrequencies and reduced transition probabilities for \(K^\pi=0^-\), \(1^-\), and \(2^-\) excitations in the deformed nucleus \(^{164}\)Dy.

## Key numbers

* GDR centroid energy for \(^{164}\)Dy: \(\bar E_{\rm GDR} = 14.94\) MeV (coupled case) or 14.75 MeV (with \(\xi_0^{K=1}/\tilde\xi_0^{K=1}=-30\)), compared to the empirical estimate \(E_{\rm GDR}=14.62\) MeV from \(80A^{-1/3}\).
* GDR splitting \(\Delta_{\rm GDR} = E_{11} - E_{10} = 3.48\) MeV (coupled) or 3.60 MeV (with \(\xi_0^{K=1}/\tilde\xi_0^{K=1}=-30\)), compared to the empirical estimate \(\Delta_{\rm GDR}=3.80\) MeV from \(E_{\rm GDR}\delta\) with \(\delta=0.26\).
* \(K^\pi=1^-\) GDR energy: \(E_{11}=16.28\) MeV with \(B(E11)=23.86\;e^2{\rm fm}^2\) (coupled, \(\xi_0^{K=1}/\tilde\xi_0^{K=1}=1\)).
* \(K^\pi=0^-\) GDR energy: \(E_{10}=12.80\) MeV with \(B(E10)=14.85\;e^2{\rm fm}^2\) (coupled, \(\xi_0^{K=0}/\tilde\xi_0^{K=0}=1\)).
* Spin-vector \(K^\pi=1^-\) excitation energies: 7.61 MeV and 12.02 MeV (coupled), with \(B(M21)\) strengths 14.89 \(\mu_N^2{\rm fm}^2\) and 1766.92 \(\mu_N^2{\rm fm}^2\) respectively.
* Spin-flip \(K^\pi=1^-\) excitation energies: 4.79 MeV and 10.61 MeV (coupled), with \(B(M21)\) strengths 212.62 \(\mu_N^2{\rm fm}^2\) and 1916.18 \(\mu_N^2{\rm fm}^2\) respectively.
* Isovector dipole constant for \(K=1\): \(\xi_1^{K=1}=0.0264\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-2}\); for \(K=0\): \(\xi_1^{K=0}=0.0162\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-2}\).
* The calculations exhaust 100% of the classical energy-weighted sum rule for \(E1\) when the center-of-mass motion energy is pushed to high energy, and 98% when the spurious state is zeroed.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous WFM studies of \(1^-\) excitations (Balbutsev and Molodtsova, 2013–2024) that neglected spin degrees of freedom and deformation, and also extends their spherical-nuclei calculations (Balbutsev and Molodtsova, 2017) to deformed nuclei. The paper builds on the systematics of the GDR established by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the empirical \(80A^{-1/3}\) energy formula, and it addresses the limited experimental and theoretical knowledge of negative-parity spin-dependent excitations noted by Morsch (1978), Sagawa (1985), Castel (1987), Hamamoto (1991), and Paar (2007). The results enable future studies of spin giant resonances of various multipolarities and their interplay with orbital modes in deformed nuclei.
