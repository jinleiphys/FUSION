# Shell-Model Description of the Isospin-Symmetry-Breaking Correction to Gamow-Teller $β$-Decay Rates and Their Mirror Asymmetries

> The isospin-symmetry breaking correction, denoted as $δ_C$, is introduced for the first time within the shell-model framework to the nuclear matrix element of Gamow-Teller transitions. $δ_C$ is separated into two components: the isospin mixing term, $δ_{C1}$, induced by the Coulomb and nuclear charge-dependent forces in the effective Hamiltonian, and the radial mismatch term, $δ_{C2}$, arising from differences between proton and neutron realistic wave functions. Consequently, the refinement strategy developed for superallowed $0^+\rightarrow 0^+$ Fermi transitions is applied to Gamow-Teller transitions as well. It is demonstrated that, to a given precision level, the shell model calculation of $δ_C$ converges much faster than the calculation of the transition matrix element. Furthermore, higher-order correction terms are investigated and considered for consistent study of our works. Various interesting properties of the leading correction terms are discovered within the two-level model and parentage expansion of the one-body transition densities in angular momentum and isospin spaces. One such property is the dependence of $δ_{C1}$ on the isospin admixture amplitude, $α$, starting from the first order, while the same model yields $δ_{C1} = α^2$ for Fermi transitions. The calculated $δ_C$ values are then utilized to evaluate the mirror asymmetry of Gamow-Teller transition strengths, which are compared with available experimental data and other theoretical calculations. Due to the refined fitting procedure of the Woods-Saxon potential parameters and the improved convergence as a function of intermediate state number, our results show better agreement on average compared to those of Smirnova and Volpe [Nucl. Phys. {\bf A 714}, 441 (2003)].

## Key claim

The paper introduces, for the first time within the shell-model framework, a formal isospin-symmetry-breaking correction δ_C for Gamow-Teller transitions, separated into an isospin mixing term δ_C1 and a radial mismatch term δ_C2; the authors demonstrate that the shell-model calculation of δ_C converges much faster than a direct calculation of the transition matrix element, and that their results show better average agreement with experimental mirror-asymmetry data than those of Smirnova and Volpe (2003) due to a refined fitting procedure of Woods-Saxon potential parameters.

## Method

The authors generalized the shell-model formalism for the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction δ_C, previously developed for superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ Fermi transitions, to Gamow-Teller transitions by replacing the τ_± operator with the σ τ_± operator and relaxing the angular momentum selection rule. They separated δ_C into a leading-order isospin mixing term δ_C1, computed using isospin nonconserving effective Hamiltonians within the shell-model configuration space, and a radial mismatch term δ_C2, calculated using Woods-Saxon radial wave functions whose depth and length parameters were re-fitted case-by-case to reproduce experimental separation energies and charge radii. Higher-order correction terms (δ_C3 through δ_C6) were also calculated exactly, and the resulting δ_C values were used to evaluate the mirror asymmetry of Gamow-Teller transition strengths for a number of p- and sd-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-symmetry-breaking correction δ_C for superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ Fermi transitions of isotriplets is generally about 1% or smaller.
- For 15 cases of superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ Fermi transitions, f_V t has been measured with a precision of 0.1% or better.
- The calculation of M_GT typically requires thousands of intermediate states, while the calculation through δ_C^A usually needs only a few hundred.
- A negative mirror asymmetry δ was found only for the pair ²⁸P(β⁺)²⁸ᵐSi / ²⁸Al(β⁻)²⁸ᵐSi, which disagreed with the previous shell-model calculation of Smirnova and Volpe (2003).

## Context

This work extends the shell-model refinement strategy for isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections, previously established by Towner and Hardy (2008) for superallowed Fermi transitions, to Gamow-Teller transitions where the correction is expected to be larger. The authors compare their results to those of Smirnova and Volpe (2003) and find better average agreement with experimental mirror-asymmetry data, attributed to their improved fitting of Woods-Saxon potential parameters and better convergence with intermediate state number. The formalism enables more precise tests of the Standard Model via mirror asymmetry in Gamow-Teller transitions and may help constrain the presence of induced second-class tensor currents.
