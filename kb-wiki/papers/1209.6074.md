# Energy Density Functional analysis of shape evolution in N=28 isotones

> The structure of low-energy collective states in proton-deficient N=28 isotones is analyzed using structure models based on the relativistic energy density functional DD-PC1. The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model for triaxial nuclei is used to calculate binding energy maps in the $β$-$γ$ plane. The evolution of neutron and proton single-particle levels with quadrupole deformation, and the occurrence of gaps around the Fermi surface, provide a simple microscopic interpretation of the onset of deformation and shape coexistence. Starting from self-consistent constrained energy surfaces calculated with the functional DD-PC1, a collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrations and rotations is employed in the analysis of excitation spectra and transition rates of $^{46}$Ar, $^{44}$S, and $^{42}$Si. The results are compared to available data, and previous studies based either on the mean-field approach or large-scale shell-model calculations. The present study is particularly focused on $^{44}$S, for which data have recently been reported that indicate pronounced shape coexistence.

## Key claim

The relativistic energy density functional DD-PC1, which was parameterized on nuclei in the mass regions A ≈ 150–180 and A ≈ 230–250, successfully predicts the erosion of the N=28 spherical shell gap and the onset of shape coexistence in proton-deficient N=28 isotones, but the authors note this constitutes an extrapolation to a very different mass region and thus a test of the functional's global applicability.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the DD-PC1 energy density functional in the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing interaction in the particle-particle channel, solving the RHB equations by expanding nucleon spinors in a 3D harmonic oscillator basis with N_f^max = 10 major shells. From the self-consistent constrained energy surfaces in the β-γ plane, they constructed a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrations and rotations, with mass parameters and moments of inertia calculated in the cranking approximation, and diagonalized it to obtain excitation spectra and collective wave functions for ⁴⁶Ar, ⁴⁴S, and ⁴²Si.

## Key numbers

- Spherical N=28 neutron gap decreases from 4.73 MeV in ⁴⁸Ca to 4.48 MeV in ⁴⁶Ar, then to 3.86 MeV in ⁴⁴S, 3.13 MeV in ⁴²Si, and 2.03 MeV in ⁴⁰Mg (Table I, DD-PC1 RHB calculation)
- For ⁴⁴S, the DD-PC1 calculation predicts coexistence of prolate and oblate minima at (β,γ) = (0.34, 0°) and (0.27, 60°), respectively, separated by a barrier of less than 1 MeV
- For ⁴²Si, the binding energy map shows a deep oblate minimum at (β,γ) = (0.35, 60°) with a secondary prolate minimum approximately 2.5 MeV higher
- For ⁴⁰Mg, a deep prolate minimum appears at (β,γ) = (0.45, 0°)
- The theoretical spherical N=28 gap for ⁴⁸Ca (4.73 MeV) and ⁴⁶Ar (4.48 MeV) are close to experimental values from neutron stripping reactions: 4.80 MeV in ⁴⁹Ca and 4.47 MeV in ⁴⁷Ar (Gaudefroy 2006, 2007)

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent mean-field studies of N=28 isotones using both non-relativistic functionals (Gogny D1S interaction, as in Delaroche 2010) and relativistic functionals (Lalazissis 1999), and complements large-scale shell-model calculations (Nowacki 2009, Caurier 2005). The results enable future generator coordinate method calculations with triaxial angular-momentum projected states, which the authors note is a more complete but technically difficult approach than the collective Hamiltonian used here.
