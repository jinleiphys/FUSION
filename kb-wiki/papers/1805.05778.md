# Deuteron analysing powers in deuteron-proton elastic scattering at 1.2 and 2.27 GeV

> The vector and tensor analysing powers in deuteron-proton elastic scattering have been measured in the forward hemisphere at deuteron kinetic energies of 1.2 GeV and 2.27 GeV using the ANKE spectrometer at the COSY storage ring. The results are compared with other experimental data and with predictions made within the framework of Glauber multiple scattering theory.

## Key claim

The vector and tensor analysing powers in deuteron-proton elastic scattering at 1.2 and 2.27 GeV are generally consistent with predictions from the Glauber multiple scattering model, though the description improves at higher energy and the 2.27 GeV data were used to determine the deuteron beam polarisation needed for a dibaryon search.

## Method

The ANKE magnetic spectrometer at the COSY storage ring measured elastically scattered deuterons in the forward hemisphere at deuteron kinetic energies of 1.2 and 2.27 GeV. The vector analysing power \(A_y^d\) was extracted from fits to the \(\cos\varphi\) distribution using only polarised mode #1 (where tensor polarisation is zero), while tensor analysing powers \(A_{xx}\) and \(A_{yy}\) were determined from fits to \(\cos 2\varphi\) distributions using pairs of polarisation modes. The results were compared with predictions from an extended Glauber single- and double-scattering model that includes full spin dependence of elementary proton-nucleon amplitudes and S- and D-state components of the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

* Vector analysing power \(A_y^d\) at 1.2 GeV: ranges from \(0.354 \pm 0.009\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 16.3^\circ\) to \(0.350 \pm 0.019\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 31.7^\circ\)
* Vector analysing power \(A_y^d\) at 2.27 GeV: ranges from \(0.256 \pm 0.012\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 17.7^\circ\) to \(0.045 \pm 0.045\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 32.5^\circ\)
* Tensor analysing power \(A_{yy}\) at 1.2 GeV: ranges from \(0.204 \pm 0.017\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 16.3^\circ\) to \(0.567 \pm 0.029\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 31.7^\circ\)
* Tensor analysing power \(A_{yy}\) at 2.27 GeV: ranges from \(0.333 \pm 0.023\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 17.7^\circ\) to \(0.380 \pm 0.072\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 32.5^\circ\)
* Tensor analysing power \(A_{xx}\) at 1.2 GeV: ranges from \(-0.25 \pm 0.15\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 16.3^\circ\) to \(-0.63 \pm 0.30\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 25.5^\circ\)
* Tensor analysing power \(A_{xx}\) at 2.27 GeV: ranges from \(-0.62 \pm 0.20\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 17.7^\circ\) to \(-1.02 \pm 0.30\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 24.0^\circ\)

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of deuteron analysing powers at ANL (Hajdas et al., 1987) and SATURNE (Arvieux et al., 1988) to higher energy and provides the polarisation calibration needed for the WASA dibaryon search (Adlarson et al., 2014). The results test the Glauber model formalism developed by Platonova and Kukulin (2010) and Temerbayev and Uzikov (2015), and suggest that the model becomes more reliable as energy increases, while also raising questions about the WASA data at 2.27 GeV in the dip regions of both \(A_y^d\) and \(A_{yy}\).
