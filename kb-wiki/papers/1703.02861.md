# Onset of $η$-nuclear binding in a pionless EFT approach

> $ηNNN$ and $ηNNNN$ bound states are explored in stochastic variational method (SVM) calculations within a pionless effective field theory (EFT) approach at leading order. The theoretical input consists of regulated $NN$ and $NNN$ contact terms, and a regulated energy dependent $ηN$ contact term derived from coupled-channel models of the $N^{\ast}(1535)$ nucleon resonance plus a regulated $ηNN$ contact term. A self consistency procedure is applied to deal with the energy dependence of the $ηN$ subthreshold input, resulting in a weak dependence of the calculated $η$-nuclear binding energies on the EFT regulator. It is found, in terms of the $ηN$ scattering length $a_{ηN}$, that the onset of binding $η\,^3$He requires a minimal value of Re$\,a_{ηN}$ close to 1 fm, yielding then a few MeV $η$ binding in $η\,^4$He. The onset of binding $η\,^4$He requires a lower value of Re$\,a_{ηN}$, but exceeding 0.7 fm.

## Key claim
The onset of η-nuclear binding in ³He requires a minimal value of the real part of the ηN scattering length close to 1 fm, while binding in ⁴He requires a lower value exceeding 0.7 fm; these thresholds are derived from self-consistent calculations that account for the energy dependence of the ηN interaction, and the results show only weak dependence on the EFT regulator.

## Method
The authors performed stochastic variational method (SVM) calculations for ηNNN (η³He) and ηNNNN (η⁴He) systems using a leading-order pionless effective field theory (EFT) approach. The Hamiltonian included regulated NN and NNN contact terms fitted to low-energy nuclear data, an energy-dependent ηN contact term derived from two coupled-channel models (GW and CS) of the N*(1535) resonance, and a regulated ηNN contact term. A self-consistency procedure was applied to determine the subthreshold energy at which the ηN interaction was evaluated, iterating until the input energy shift matched the expectation value from the calculated wavefunction.

## Key numbers
- The ηN scattering lengths used were a_ηN^GW = 0.96 + i0.26 fm for the GW model and a_ηN^CS = 0.67 + i0.20 fm for the CS model.
- For η³He with the GW model and Λ = 4 fm⁻¹, the self-consistent calculation gave B_η = +0.30 MeV (bound) and Γ_η = 1.46 MeV; for Λ = 2 fm⁻¹, B_η = -0.16 MeV (unbound) and Γ_η = 0.24 MeV.
- For η⁴He with the GW model and Λ = 4 fm⁻¹, B_η = +1.54 MeV and Γ_η = 2.82 MeV; for Λ = 2 fm⁻¹, B_η = -0.15 MeV and Γ_η = 0.34 MeV.
- The ⁴He binding energy calculated in the LO pionless EFT approached 27.8 ± 0.2 MeV in the Λ → ∞ limit, compared to the experimental value of 28.3 MeV.
- The CS ηN interaction was too weak to bind η³He and fell short of binding η⁴He by a fraction of an MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous few-body calculations by Barnea et al. (2015) on ηNN and ηNNN systems, which used Minnesota and Argonne AV4' NN potentials, to the first precise few-body calculation of η⁴He. The results are consistent with the review by K. Wiśniewski (2015) suggesting ηd is unbound, η³He is nearly or just bound, and η⁴He is bound. The findings contradict a recent preprint by Fix and Kolesnikov (2017) that concluded η³He and η⁴He are unbound, and provide theoretical context for interpreting the WASA-at-COSY search for η⁴He bound states reported by Adlarson et al. (2016).
