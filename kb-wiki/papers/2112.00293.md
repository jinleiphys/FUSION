# Charmonium Triangular Flow in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We calculate, for the first time, $J/ψ$ triangular flow in high energy nuclear collisions. The charmonium motion in medium is controlled by a transport equation with loss and gain terms, and the evolution of the hot medium is governed by a single-shot hydrodynamic equation with a triangularly deformed initial condition. In comparison with the elliptic flow $v_2$, the triangular flow $v_3$ comes almost completely from the charmonium regeneration in the quark gluon plasma and therefore is more sensitive to the medium properties.

# Charmonium Triangular Flow in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The triangular flow $v_3$ of $J/\psi$ mesons in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV originates almost completely from charmonium regeneration in the quark-gluon plasma, unlike elliptic flow $v_2$ which has contributions from both initial production and regeneration. This makes $v_3$ more sensitive to medium properties, though the calculation uses a simplified single-shot hydrodynamics with an averaged, smooth but deformed initial profile rather than full event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions.

## Method
The authors solve a relativistic Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium phase space distributions, with loss terms from gluon dissociation ($g\psi \to c\bar{c}$) and gain terms from the inverse coalescence process ($c\bar{c} \to g\psi$), using detailed balance. The QGP medium evolution is described by (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC package) with an initially triangularly deformed energy density profile, where the triangularity parameter $\varepsilon_3$ is taken from MC-Glauber or MC-KLN models. The initial charm quark distribution is assumed thermally equilibrated, and cold nuclear matter effects (Cronin effect with $a_{gN}=0.15$ GeV$^2$/fm and EPS09 nuclear shadowing) are included in the initial charmonium distribution.

## Key numbers
- Maximum initial temperature $T_0 = 510$ MeV for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV
- Triangularity parameters $\varepsilon_3 = 0.08, 0.1, 0.2$ for impact parameters $b = 3.2, 6.8, 9.6$ fm (corresponding to centrality bins 0-10%, 10-30%, and 30-50%)
- Charm quark production cross section range: $0.56$ mb $< d\sigma^{c\bar{c}}_{pp}/d\eta < 0.78$ mb in forward rapidity at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV
- Initial charmonium parameters from pp collisions: $\langle p_T^2\rangle_{pp} = 12.5$ (GeV/c)$^2$, $n=3.2$, $d\sigma^{J/\psi}_{pp}/dy = 3.25$ $\mu$b in forward rapidity $2.5<|y|<4$
- Shear viscosity $\eta/s = 0.08$, critical temperature $T_c = 170$ MeV, initial time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section $\sigma_{\text{inel}} = 68$ mb for Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV
- Binary collision fraction $\gamma = 0.16$ for the initial energy density parameterization

## Context
This work extends the transport approach previously applied to $J/\psi$ nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ at RHIC and LHC energies (Zhao 2010, Zhao 2011, Liu 2009, Zhou 2014, Du 2015, Chen 2018) to the first calculation of $J/\psi$ triangular flow $v_3$. It responds to the first measurement of inclusive $J/\psi$ $v_3$ at forward rapidity by the ALICE Collaboration (ALICE 2020) in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The single-shot hydrodynamics with deformed initial conditions follows the approach of Alver (2010) and Qiu (2011) for light hadron anisotropic flow. The results enable using heavy flavor triangular flow as a probe of initial energy density fluctuations and heavy quark interaction mechanisms with the QGP, complementing the established use of $v_2$ for studying nuclear geometry effects.
