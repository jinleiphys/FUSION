# Measuring deformed neutron skin with free spectator nucleons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The neutron skin in deformed nuclei is generally not uniformly distributed but has an angular distribution, depending on both the spin-dependent nuclear interaction and the nuclear symmetry energy. To extract the information of the deformed neutron skin, we have explored the possibility of using free spectator nucleons in central tip-tip and body-body collisions at top RHIC energy with four typical deformed nuclei. The density distributions of neutrons and protons are consistently obtained from the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculation, and the angular distribution of the neutron skin can be varied by adjusting the strength of the nuclear spin-orbit coupling. With the information of spectator nucleons obtained based on a Monte-Carlo Glauber model, the free spectator nucleons are generated from a multifragmentation process. By investigating the results from different systems and with different collision configurations, we found that although it is difficult to probe the deformed neutron skin in $^{96}$Zr and $^{238}$U by their collisions, it is promising to extract the polar angular distributions of the neutron skin in $^{96}$Ru and $^{197}$Au by comparing the yield ratios of free spectator neutrons to protons in their central tip-tip and body-body collisions. The proposed observables can be measured by dedicated zero-degree calorimeters in heavy-ion collision experiments that have been carried out in recent years by RHIC.

## Key claim

It is promising to extract the polar angular distribution of the deformed neutron skin in \(^{96}\)Ru and \(^{197}\)Au by comparing the yield ratios of free spectator neutrons to protons in their central tip-tip and body-body collisions, although it is difficult to probe the deformed neutron skin in \(^{96}\)Zr and \(^{238}\)U by their collisions. The proposed method depends on the strength of the nuclear spin-orbit coupling, which can be varied to change the angular distribution of the neutron skin, while the overall neutron-skin thickness is set by the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) calculations to obtain consistent neutron and proton density distributions for \(^{96}\)Zr, \(^{96}\)Ru, \(^{238}\)U, and \(^{197}\)Au, using a fixed symmetry energy slope parameter \(L = 90\) MeV and varying the spin-orbit coupling strength \(W_0\) between 80 and 150 MeV fm\(^5\). A Monte-Carlo Glauber model with a nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section of 42 mb was used to simulate central tip-tip, body-body, and random-orientation collisions at top RHIC energies, and free spectator nucleons were generated from the spectator matter via a multifragmentation process including cluster formation and deexcitation using the GEMINI model.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{96}\)Ru collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with \(L = 90\) MeV and \(W_0 = 80\) MeV fm\(^5\), the \(N_n/N_p\) ratio is about \(4\%\) smaller in central tip-tip collisions than in central body-body collisions.
- For \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with \(L = 90\) MeV and \(W_0 = 80\) MeV fm\(^5\), the \(N_n/N_p\) ratio is about \(7\%\) smaller in central tip-tip collisions than in central body-body collisions.
- The default spin-orbit coupling strength \(W_0\) is set to 133 MeV fm\(^5\), generally constrained within \(80-150\) MeV fm\(^5\) based on nuclear structure studies.
- The slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy is constrained within about \(30 \sim 90\) MeV from various probes.
- The quadrupole deformation parameters used are \(\beta_2 = 0.06\) for \(^{96}\)Zr, \(\beta_2 = 0.16\) for \(^{96}\)Ru, \(\beta_2 = 0.28\) for \(^{238}\)U, and \(\beta_2 = -0.15\) for \(^{197}\)Au; \(^{96}\)Zr also has an octupole deformation \(\beta_3 = 0.20\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Liu et al., 2022) which proposed the yield ratio of free spectator neutrons in ultracentral isobaric collisions as a probe of the average neutron-skin thickness, and further proposed the \(N_n/N_p\) ratio in a single collision system as a more sensitive probe (Liu et al., 2022). The present study adds the capability to probe the polar angular distribution of the neutron skin in deformed nuclei by selecting special collision configurations, building on earlier proposals for event-shape engineering in heavy-ion collisions (Nepali et al., 2007; Goldschmidt et al., 2015). The results enable future experimental measurements using dedicated zero-degree calorimeters at RHIC to study the interplay between the nuclear spin-orbit coupling and the symmetry energy.
