# A Simple Quark Model for γp -> K^+ Λ^0

> The photoproduction of the Λ^0 and K^+ from circularly polarized photons on protons is discussed in a simple quark model; we compare the result to experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a simple quark model, where only the strange and anti-strange quarks participate directly in the photoproduction reaction and the ud diquark is a spectator, can explain the near-100% polarization transfer \(C_z\) observed in CLAS data, but the model has problems because \(|C_z|\) exceeds unity at backward angles.

## Method

The authors compute the reaction \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda^0\) using a relativistic quark model where the photon couples directly to the \(s\) and \(\bar{s}\) quarks, with the \(ud\) diquark and \(u\) quark as spectators. They include rescattering corrections via scalar boson exchange between quarks, parameterized by a Gaussian in momentum space \(V = g^2 \exp(-t^2/\alpha^2)\), and integrate over quark distribution functions with a Gaussian width \(\alpha^2 = (0.55)^2\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\). The strange quark and diquark masses are taken as \(m = 0.35\ \text{GeV}\), and the up quark mass as half that value.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section at \(0^\circ\) and \(E_\gamma = 1.7\ \text{GeV}\): \(0.27\ \mu\text{b}\) at first order, \(1.9\ \mu\text{b}\) for rescattering diagram Fig. 5(a), and \(0.4\ \mu\text{b}\) for Fig. 5(b); experimentally, \(d\sigma/d(\cos\theta) = 1.8\ \mu\text{b}\) at \(\cos\theta = 0.9\).
- Differential cross section at \(0^\circ\) and \(E_\gamma \approx 2.3\ \text{GeV}\): calculated value \(2.8\ \mu\text{b}\) vs. experimental \(1.6\ \mu\text{b}\) at \(\cos\theta = 0.9\).
- Quark masses: strange quark and diquark mass \(m = 0.35\ \text{GeV}\); up quark mass \(m/2 = 0.175\ \text{GeV}\).
- Gaussian width parameter: \(\alpha^2 = (0.55)^2\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- Photon lab energy range considered: \(0.9 < E_\gamma < 2.94\ \text{GeV}\) (c.m. energies \(1.6\) to \(2.53\ \text{GeV}\)).
- Circular polarization of photons: \(0.440 < P_\circ < 0.995\).

## Context

This work extends the sparse quark-model treatments of kaon photoproduction, contrasting with the dominant effective Lagrangian approaches that use many baryon resonances (e.g., Mart and Bennhold, Mart and Sulaksono, de la Puente, Maxwell, and Raue) or Reggeized t-channel exchanges (Guidal, Laget, and Vanderhaeghen). The model is motivated by the CLAS observation that the polarization transfer \(C_z\) is approximately 100% in the photon direction, which the authors argue follows naturally from the \(\Lambda\) spin being carried entirely by the strange quark. The paper enables future improvements by noting the need to replace the scalar exchange with gluon exchange between quarks.
