# Shell structure of potassium isotopes deduced from their magnetic moments

> \item[Background] Ground-state spins and magnetic moments are sensitive to the nuclear wave function, thus they are powerful probes to study the nuclear structure of isotopes far from stability. \item[Purpose] Extend our knowledge about the evolution of the $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ states for K isotopes beyond the $N = 28$ shell gap. \item[Method] High-resolution collinear laser spectroscopy on bunched atomic beams. \item[Results] From measured hyperfine structure spectra of K isotopes, nuclear spins and magnetic moments of the ground states were obtained for isotopes from $N = 19$ up to $N = 32$. In order to draw conclusions about the composition of the wave functions and the occupation of the levels, the experimental data were compared to shell-model calculations using SDPF-NR and SDPF-U effective interactions. In addition, a detailed discussion about the evolution of the gap between proton $1d_{3/2}$ and $2s_{1/2}$ in the shell model and {\it{ab initio}} framework is also presented. \item[Conclusions] The dominant component of the wave function for the odd-$A$ isotopes up to $^{45}$K is a $π1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ hole. For $^{47,49}$K, the main component originates from a $π2s_{1/2}^{-1}$ hole configuration and it inverts back to the $π1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ in $^{51}$K. For all even-$A$ isotopes, the dominant configuration arises from a $π1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ hole coupled to a neutron in the $ν1f_{7/2}$ or $ν2p_{3/2}$ orbitals. Only for $^{48}$K, a significant amount of mixing with $π2s_{1/2}^{-1} \otimes ν(pf)$ is observed leading to a $I^π=1^{-}$ ground state. For $^{50}$K, the ground-state spin-parity is $0^-$ with leading configuration $π1d_{3/2}^{-1} \otimes ν2p_{3/2}^{-1}$.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the evolution of proton $sd$ orbitals in potassium isotopes beyond $N=28$ by measuring magnetic moments and nuclear spins, showing that the ground-state configuration inverts from a $\pi 1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ hole to a $\pi 2s_{1/2}^{-1}$ hole at $N=28$ and reinverts back to $\pi 1d_{3/2}^{-1}$ at $N=32$, with the caveat that shell-model calculations using SDPF-U fail to reproduce the magnetic moment of $^{49}$K unless at least 25% mixing with the $[\pi 1d_{3/2}^{-1} \otimes \nu (pf)_{2^+}]_{1/2^{+}}$ configuration is included.

## Method

High-resolution collinear laser spectroscopy on bunched atomic beams was performed at the COLLAPS beam line at ISOLDE/CERN, using a titanium:sapphire laser tuned to the $4s\;{^2S_{1/2}} \rightarrow 4p\;{^2P_{1/2}}$ transition at 769.9 nm in neutral potassium. Hyperfine structure spectra were fitted with Voigt line shapes to extract $A$-parameters, from which nuclear spins and magnetic moments were deduced relative to $^{39}$K. Experimental results were compared to shell-model calculations using the ANTOINE code with SDPF-NR and SDPF-U effective interactions, and to \textit{ab initio} Gorkov-Green's function calculations for odd-$A$ isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of $^{47}$K: $\mu = +1.9292(2)[58]\,\mu_N$ (uncertainty in square brackets due to hyperfine structure anomaly), with ground-state spin $I^\pi = 1/2^+$
- Magnetic moment of $^{49}$K: $\mu = +1.3386(8)[40]\,\mu_N$, with ground-state spin $I^\pi = 1/2^+$
- Magnetic moment of $^{51}$K: $\mu = +0.5129(22)[15]\,\mu_N$, with ground-state spin $I^\pi = 3/2^+$
- Magnetic moment of $^{48}$K: $A(^2S_{1/2}) = -795.9(3)$ MHz, with ground-state spin $I^\pi = 1^-$
- Magnetic moment of $^{50}$K: $A(^2S_{1/2}) = 0$ (no hyperfine splitting), with ground-state spin $I^\pi = 0^-$
- Energy gap between $\pi 1d_{3/2}$ and $\pi 2s_{1/2}$ decreases by $-3.15$ MeV from $N=20$ to $N=28$ for both SDPF-NR and SDPF-U interactions
- Energy gap increases by $+0.23$ MeV (SDPF-NR) and $+0.89$ MeV (SDPF-U) from $N=29$ to $N=32$ when filling the $\nu 2p_{3/2}$ orbital

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $\pi sd$ orbital evolution in the $Z<20$ region by Gade (2006) and Smirnova (2012), who predicted a degeneracy of $\pi 2s_{1/2}$ and $\pi 1d_{3/2}$ levels at $N=28$ based on shell-model calculations. The measurements resolve conflicting spin assignments for $^{48}$K (Krolas 2011 proposed $I^\pi=(1^-)$), $^{49}$K (Broda 2010 proposed $(1/2^+)$), $^{50}$K (Baumann 1998 proposed $0^-$ versus Crawford 2009 suggesting $1^-$), and $^{51}$K (Perrot 2006 proposed $(3/2^+)$). The results enable testing of the monopole components (central, vector, tensor) of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in shell-model calculations, and provide benchmarks for \textit{ab initio} Gorkov-Green's function approaches to nuclear structure far from stability.
