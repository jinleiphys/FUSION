# Single-energy partial wave analysis for pion photoproduction with fixed-t analyticity

> Experimental data for pion photoproduction including differential cross sections and various polarization observables from four reaction channels, $γp \to π^0 p$, $γp \to π^+n$, $γn \to π^- p$ and $γn \to π^0 n$ from threshold up to $W=2.2$ GeV have been used in order to perform a single-energy partial wave analysis with minimal model dependence by imposing constraints from unitarity and fixed-$t$ analyticity in an iterative procedure. Reaction models were only used as starting point in the very first iteration. We demonstrate that with this procedure partial wave amplitudes can be obtained which show only a minimal dependence on the initial model assumptions. The analysis has been obtained in full isospin, and the Watson theorem is enforced for energies below $W=1.3$ GeV but is even fulfilled up to $W\approx 1.6$ GeV in many partial waves. Electromagnetic multipoles $E_{\ell\pm}$ and $M_{\ell\pm}$ are presented and discussed for $S,P,D$ and $F$-waves.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that single-energy partial wave amplitudes for pion photoproduction can be obtained with only minimal dependence on initial model assumptions by imposing constraints from unitarity and fixed-\(t\) analyticity in an iterative procedure, though the method still requires a reaction model as a starting point in the very first iteration.

## Method

The authors performed a single-energy partial wave analysis using experimental data for differential cross sections and polarization observables from four reaction channels (\(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\), \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ n\), \(\gamma n \to \pi^- p\), and \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\)) from threshold up to \(W=2.2\) GeV. They imposed constraints from unitarity and fixed-\(t\) analyticity in an iterative procedure coupling a fixed-\(t\) amplitude analysis with a single-energy partial wave analysis, using three different energy-dependent models (BnGa2019, SAID-M19, MAID2007) as starting points only in the first iteration. Watson's theorem was enforced for energies below \(W=1.3\) GeV, with the unitarity constraint smoothly truncated to zero between 1.4 and 1.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Energy range of analysis: from threshold up to \(W=2.2\) GeV
- Watson theorem enforced for energies below \(W=1.3\) GeV, but fulfilled up to \(W\approx 1.6\) GeV in many partial waves
- Fixed-\(t\) amplitude analysis performed at 43 \(t\)-values in the range \(-1.00\) GeV\(^2 < t < -0.005\) GeV\(^2\)
- Single-energy partial wave analysis considered 135 energy bins in the range \(1.09\) GeV \(< W < 2.2\) GeV, with final multipoles binned in 45 energy bins
- For the \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\) channel: 600 differential cross section data points from A2MAMI-2013 in the range \(W=1075-1136\) MeV, and 6451 points from A2MAMI-2015 in the range \(W=1136-1894\) MeV
- For the \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ n\) channel: 129 differential cross section data points from A2MAMI-2004 in the range \(W=1178-1313\) MeV
- For the \(\gamma n \to \pi^- p\) channel: 746 differential cross section data points from older measurements in the range \(W=1179-1798\) MeV
- For the \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\) channel: 497 differential cross section data points from A2MAMI-2019 in the range \(W=1195-1533\) MeV (this channel was not used in the fit)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fixed-\(t\) analyticity method applied to \(\eta\) photoproduction on the proton (Osmanovic 2018) and \(\pi^0\) photoproduction on the proton (Osmanovic 2019) to the full isospin decomposition of pion photoproduction, requiring simultaneous analysis of at least three of the four reaction channels. The method addresses the phase ambiguity problem in single-energy analyses identified by Omelaenko (1981), Wunderlich (2017), and Workman (2016), and provides an alternative to energy-dependent models such as Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa), Julich-Bonn, George Washington University (GW-SAID), and Mainz (MAID). The results enable extraction of electromagnetic multipoles \(E_{\ell\pm}\) and \(M_{\ell\pm}\) for \(S, P, D,\) and \(F\)-waves with reduced model dependence, which can be used to determine properties of light baryon resonances \(N^*\) and \(\Delta^*\).
