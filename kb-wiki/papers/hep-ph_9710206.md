# eta and eta' in a coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach

> Extending our earlier treatments of pi0, eta_c and eta_b, we study the eta-eta' system and its gamma-gamma decays using a model which is a leading version of the consistently coupled Schwinger-Dyson (SD) and Bethe-Salpeter (BS) approach. The electromagnetic interactions are incorporated through a (generalized) impulse approximation consistent with this bound-state approach, so that the Ward-Takahashi identities of QED are preserved when quarks are dynamically dressed. To overcome some of the limitations due to the ladder approximation, we introduce a minimal extension to the bound--state approach employed, so that the U_A(1) problem is avoided. Pointing out which of our predictions hold in the coupled SD-BS approach in general, and which are the consequences of the specific, chosen model, we present the results for the axial-current decay constants of eta_8, eta_0, and of their physical combinations eta and eta', the results for the gamma-gamma decay constants of eta_0 and eta_8, for the two-photon decay widths of eta and eta', and for the mixing-independent R-ratio constructed from them.

## Key claim

The paper extends the coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach, previously applied to π⁰, η_c, and η_b, to the η–η′ system and its two-photon decays, but notes that the ladder approximation places this system at the "applicability edge" of the model, requiring a minimal extension to avoid the U_A(1) problem.

## Method

The authors solve the ladder-approximated Schwinger-Dyson equation for dressed quark propagators and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for bound-state vertices, using the gluon propagator model of Jain and Munczek (1993) with a perturbative UV part and a modeled IR part. Electromagnetic interactions are incorporated through a generalized impulse approximation using the Ball–Chiu vertex, which satisfies the Ward–Takahashi identities. The η–η′ mixing is treated via the octet–singlet basis, with the s¯s bound-state vertex obtained by explicit numerical solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, retaining all four scalar functions and the first two Chebyshev moments.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses from the model: non-strange (u/d) mass M_ud = 375 MeV (with explicit breaking) and 356 MeV (chiral limit); strange quark mass M_s = 610 MeV.
- Mass of the unphysical s¯s pseudoscalar bound state: M_s¯s = 721 MeV.
- Axial-current decay constants: f_π = 93.2 MeV, f_s¯s = 136.5 MeV (= 1.47 f_π), f_η₈ = 122.1 MeV, f_η₀ = 107.6 MeV.
- Two-photon decay constants: f̄_η₈ = 73.64 MeV (= 0.797 f_π), f̄_η₀ = 98.58 MeV (= 1.067 f_π).
- Ratio of reduced γγ-amplitudes: T̃_s¯s(0,0) = 0.62 T̃_u¯u(0,0).

## Context

This work extends the coupled SD-BS approach of Jain and Munczek (1991, 1992, 1993), which successfully reproduced the meson spectrum and leptonic decay constants for most pseudoscalar and vector mesons, to the problematic η–η′ system. The authors previously applied the same framework to π⁰, η_c, and η_b two-photon decays (Klabučar and Kekez, 1997; Lesniak, 1997). The results enable future calculations of η–η′ mixing parameters and provide a baseline for comparing with chiral perturbation theory results such as those of Donoghue, Holstein, and Lin (1988), who found f̄_η₈ > f_π, in contrast to the general SD-BS prediction f̄_η₈ < f_π for realistic strange quark masses.
