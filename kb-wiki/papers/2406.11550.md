# Low Energy Nuclear Reactions Through Weak Interactions

> We consider the possibility that low-energy nuclear reactions (LENR) arise due to the conversion of proton to neutron through weak interactions. The resulting neutron forms a short-lived virtual state, which then gets captured by another nucleus through photon emission. This whole process happens under the framework of second-order perturbation theory. We find that the rate of this process is negligibly small in most cases. However, in the presence of a resonance, the rate can be substantial and observable.

## Key claim

Low-energy nuclear reactions (LENR) may proceed via a second-order weak interaction process where a proton converts to a virtual neutron, which is then captured by a target nucleus; the rate is negligible in most cases but can become substantial and observable only when a nuclear resonance is present with a width that approximately matches the energy scale of the oscillations in the intermediate-state sum.

## Method

The authors model the reaction of a proton with a target nucleus X (using nickel-58 as an example) within second-order perturbation theory, where the first vertex is a weak Fermi transition converting the proton to a neutron with emission of a positron and neutrino, and the second vertex is an electromagnetic capture of the neutron by the nucleus with photon emission. They treat the initial proton-nucleus system as a molecular bound state with a Gaussian wave function peaked at typical bond length, model the intermediate neutron state using phase-shift expansions with and without resonance, and compute the transition amplitude by summing over intermediate neutron energy eigenstates. The rate is evaluated by integrating over the phase space of the emitted positron, neutrino, and photon, using shell-model parameters for the final nuclear state.

## Key numbers

- Weak interaction coupling constant: \( G_V/(\hbar c)^3 = 1.149 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{MeV}^{-2} \)
- For a resonance at \( E_R = 0.74 \, \text{eV} \) with width \( 0.02 \, \text{eV} \) (parameters \( a_1 = 800 \), \( a_2 = 8 \)), the computed reaction rate is \( 4 \times 10^{-26} \) per second
- For a resonance at \( E_R = 7 \, \text{keV} \) with width \( 7 \, \text{eV} \) (parameters \( a_1 = 1.9 \times 10^6 \), \( a_2 = 2.0 \)), corresponding to nickel-58, the rate is approximately \( 10^{-16} \) per second
- The photon energy spectrum peaks at approximately \( 3.5 \, \text{MeV} \) for the nickel-58 resonance case
- The binding energy of the outermost neutron in nickel-59 is approximately \( 9 \, \text{MeV} \)
- The nuclear matrix element integral \( I_f \) for nickel is \( -7.6 \times 10^{-11} \) in atomic units

## Context

This work extends previous studies of second-order fusion processes by Jain and collaborators (Jain 2020, Jain 2021, Ramkumar 2022, Jain 2024) which showed that such processes generally suffer from cancellation in the sum over intermediate states unless special conditions are met. The paper specifically applies the weak-interaction mechanism to LENR, contrasting with earlier proposals including screening contributions (Assenbaum 1987, Ichimaru 1993), electroweak interactions (Widom 2010), and phonon contributions (Hagelstein 2015). The resonance-assisted mechanism is argued to be applicable to a wide range of nuclei beyond the weak process considered here, and the authors identify nickel-58 as a candidate for experimental testing due to its known resonance at approximately 7 keV with 7 eV width from BNL National Nuclear Data Center data.
