# Description of continuum structures in a discrete basis: Three-body resonances and two-nucleon decays

> Weakly bound and unbound three-body nuclei are studied by using the pseudostate method within the hyperspherical formalism. After introducing the theoretical framework, the method is applied first to the $\boldsymbol{^9}$Be nucleus, showing a good agreement with the available data for its low-lying dipole response. Then, recent results on the structure and decay of the two-neutron emitters $\boldsymbol{^{26}}$O and $\boldsymbol{^{16}}$Be are presented. In particular, the role of the $\boldsymbol{n}$-$\boldsymbol{n}$ correlation in shaping their properties is discussed.

## Key claim
The pseudostate method within the hyperspherical formalism can describe weakly bound and unbound three-body nuclei, but the identification of resonances requires additional techniques such as the stabilization approach for narrow resonances or a resonance operator method for broader states, with the neutron-neutron correlation playing a crucial role in shaping the properties of two-neutron emitters.

## Method
The authors solved the three-body Schrodinger equation using a hyperspherical harmonics expansion, where the hyperradial functions were expanded in a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis. For bound states and narrow resonances, diagonalization of the Hamiltonian in this discrete basis provided pseudostates that approximate the continuum. For broader resonances, a resonance operator M = H^{-1/2} V H^{-1/2} was diagonalized in the basis of Hamiltonian pseudostates to identify localized continuum structures and extract resonance widths from their time evolution.

## Key numbers
- Ground state of 9Be: binding energy of -1.574 MeV, charge radius r_ch = 2.508 fm, quadrupole moment Q_2 = 4.91 efm^2
- Ground-state wave function of 9Be contains 52.5% 8Be(g.s.)+n and 46.6% 8Be(2+)+n configurations
- 0+ ground-state resonance in 26O: energy of 18 keV (from experiment Kondo 2016), with a second 2+ state at 1.28 MeV
- 25O d_{3/2} resonance at 750 keV (from experiment Kondo 2016)
- 0+ ground-state resonance in 16Be: two-neutron separation energy of -1.35 MeV, width of 0.16 MeV
- 15Be ground state: d_{5/2} resonance at 1.8 MeV above one-neutron emission
- 26O ground-state wave function contains only about 60% (d_{3/2})^2 configurations, with large mixing from negative-parity components
- 27F wave function has dominant (d_{3/2})^2 character at about 85%

## Context
This work extends the pseudostate method previously applied to bound systems like 9Be (Casal 2014) to unbound two-neutron emitters 26O and 16Be. The stabilization approach for 26O follows the method of Hazi and Taylor (1970) and Taylor and Hazi (1976), while the resonance operator method for 16Be was developed in Casal (2019). The results for 26O are compared with Green's function calculations by Hagino (2016) and experimental data from Kondo (2016), and for 16Be with R-matrix calculations by Lovell (2017) and experimental data from Spyrou (2012). The work enables systematic studies of neutron-neutron correlations across the neutron dripline and predictions for decay properties of unbound three-body systems.
