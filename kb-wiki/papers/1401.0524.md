# Heavy quarkonium fragmentation functions from a heavy quark pair. II. $P$ wave

> Recently, a new perturbative QCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium production at a large transverse momentum was proposed. Phenomenological application of this new approach relies on our knowledge of a large number of universal fragmentation functions (FFs) at an input factorization scale $μ_0\gtrsim 2m_Q$ with heavy quark mass $m_Q$, which are nonperturbative, and in principle, should be extracted from data. With heavy quark mass $m_Q\gg Λ_{\rm QCD}$, we calculate these input FFs in terms of non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization. In a companion paper, we calculated these input FFs in NRQCD factorization approach including all contributions from $S$-wave NRQCD $Q\bar{Q}$-states. In this paper, we calculate contributions to the heavy quark-pair FFs from all $P$-wave NRQCD $Q\bar{Q}$-states.

## Key claim

The authors calculate contributions to heavy quark-pair fragmentation functions (FFs) from all \(P\)-wave NRQCD \(Q\bar{Q}\)-states up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in \(\alpha_s\), providing the input FFs needed for a recently proposed perturbative QCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium production at large transverse momentum, under the condition that the heavy quark mass \(m_Q \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) and that the input factorization scale \(\mu_0 \gtrsim 2m_Q\).

## Method

The authors use NRQCD factorization to express the heavy quark-pair FFs as a sum of short-distance coefficients multiplied by NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs). They compute the short-distance coefficients by matching perturbative QCD calculations of the FFs for a perturbative \(Q\bar{Q}\)-pair fragmenting into a non-relativistic \(Q\bar{Q}\)-pair (the left-hand side) with perturbative NRQCD calculations of the LDMEs (the right-hand side), order by order in \(\alpha_s\), up to NLO. The calculation involves evaluating cut-diagram amplitudes with dimensional regularization to handle infrared and Coulomb divergences, using a trick to exchange the \(q_1\)-integration with the \(q_r\)-derivative and limit \(q_r \to 0\), which the authors justify for \(P\)-wave final states.

## Key numbers

*   The input factorization scale is \(\mu_0 \gtrsim 2m_Q\), where \(m_Q\) is the heavy quark mass.
*   The heavy quark mass satisfies \(m_Q \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).
*   The calculation is performed up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\).
*   For color octet initial and final states, the LO contribution satisfies \({\cal D}^{\text{LO}}_{[\cc(s^{[8]})]\to [\cc(i^{[8]})]} = (N_c^2-1)^{-1} \times {\cal D}^{\text{LO}}_{[\cc(s^{[1]})]\to [\cc(i^{[1]})]}\), where \(N_c = 3\).
*   Out of 18 possible partonic fragmentation channels for \(P\)-wave final states, 8 vanish due to Lorentz invariance.

## Context

This paper extends the work of the authors' companion paper (Ma, Yan-Qing, Qiu, Jian-Wei, and Zhang, Hong, 2014) which calculated contributions from all \(S\)-wave NRQCD \(Q\bar{Q}\)-states to the same input FFs. The results are necessary for the phenomenological application of the new pQCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium production proposed by Kang, Ma, Qiu, and Sterman (2011, 2012). The calculated input FFs, combined with the evolution kernels from Kang, Ma, Qiu, and Sterman (2011) and partonic hard parts from Kang, Ma, Qiu, and Sterman (2012), can in principle reproduce previous NLO NRQCD calculations for heavy quarkonium production in hadron-hadron collisions (Ma, Wang, and Chao, 2010; Butenschoen and Kniehl, 2010; Gong, Wan, Wang, and Zhang, 2013), electron-hadron collisions (Butenschoen and Kniehl, 2009), and electron-positron collisions (Zhang, Ma, Wang, and Chao, 2009), while also enabling the resummation of large logarithms from FF evolution.
