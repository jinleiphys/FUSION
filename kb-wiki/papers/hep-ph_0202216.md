# Electroproduction of Charmonia off Nuclei

> In a recent publication we have calculated elastic charmonium production in ep collisions employing realistic charmonia wave functions and dipole cross sections and have found good agreement with the data in a wide range of s and Q^2. Using the ingredients from those calculations we calculate exclusive electroproduction of charmonia off nuclei. Here new effects become important, (i) color filtering of the c-cbar pair on its trajectory through nuclear matter, (ii) dependence on the finite lifetime of the c-cbar fluctuation (coherence length) and (iii) gluon shadowing in a nucleus compared to the one in a nucleon. Total coherent and incoherent cross sections for C, Cu and Pb as functions of s and Q^2 are presented together with some differential cross sections. The results can be tested with future electron-nucleus colliders or in peripheral collisions of ultrarelativistic heavy ions.

## Key claim

Exclusive electroproduction of charmonia off nuclei is calculated using realistic charmonia wave functions and dipole cross sections, finding that the previously predicted nuclear enhancement of psi' production is much weaker than earlier estimates when using more realistic dipole cross sections that saturate at large transverse separations.

## Method

The authors compute coherent and incoherent cross sections for exclusive charmonium electroproduction on carbon, copper, and lead nuclei using the light-cone dipole approach with realistic charmonium wave functions and two parameterizations of the dipole cross section (GBW and KST). They incorporate three nuclear effects: color filtering of the c-cbar pair through nuclear matter, finite coherence length corrections, and gluon shadowing in nuclei, with the latter calculated using a Green function approach for the c-cbar-gluon Fock component.

## Key numbers

- Differences between GBW and KST parameterizations for incoherent production ratios are at most 10-20%
- Calculations are performed at fixed s = 4000 GeV^2 for Q^2 dependence studies
- The gluonic coherence length is approximately l_c^G ≈ 1/(10 x m_N), about an order of magnitude shorter than the coherence length for sea quark shadowing
- The parameter b_0 = 0.65 GeV is used for the real part of the light-cone potential, fixed by diffractive gluon radiation data
- Lambda = 200 MeV is used in the running coupling constant
- The onset of gluon shadowing occurs at c.m. energies of a few tens of GeV
- The transition energy regime for finite coherence length effects is estimated as s_{c-cbar} ~ m_N (M_J/psi^2 + Q^2) R_A^{ch}/sqrt(3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful calculations of elastic charmonium production in ep collisions (Ivanov et al., 2001) to nuclear targets, and improves upon earlier calculations by Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1991) that used oversimplified dipole cross sections proportional to r_T^2 and oscillator wave functions. The predictions are intended as a realistic basis for planning future electron-nucleus collider experiments such as eRHIC, and the results can also be tested in peripheral collisions of ultrarelativistic heavy ions.
