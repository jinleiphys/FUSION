# Phase diagram of interacting pion matter and isospin charge fluctuations

> Equation of state and electric (isospin) charge fluctuations are studied for matter composed of interacting pions. The pion matter is described by self interacting scalar fields via a $ϕ^4-ϕ^6$ type Lagrangian. The mean-field approximation is used, and interaction parameters are fixed by fitting lattice QCD results on the isospin density as a function of the isospin chemical potential at zero temperature. Two scenarios for fixing the model parameters -- with and without the first order phase transition -- are considered, both yielding a satisfactory description of the lattice data. Thermodynamic functions and isospin charge fluctuations are studied and systematically compared for these two scenarios, yielding qualitative differences in the behavior of isospin charge susceptibilities. These differences can be probed by lattice simulations at temperatures $T \lesssim 100$ MeV.

# Phase diagram of interacting pion matter and isospin charge fluctuations

## Key claim
The lattice QCD data on isospin density at zero temperature can be fitted equally well by two different interaction scenarios, one with only repulsive interactions and one with both repulsive and attractive interactions, but these scenarios predict qualitatively different behavior of isospin charge fluctuations at temperatures below about 100 MeV, which can be distinguished by future lattice simulations.

## Method
The authors model interacting pion matter using a relativistic mean-field approximation with a Skyrme-like Lagrangian containing φ⁴ (attractive) and φ⁶ (repulsive) self-interaction terms. Model parameters are fixed by fitting lattice QCD results for isospin density as a function of isospin chemical potential at zero temperature from Brandt et al. (2018). Two parameter sets are considered: Scenario I with only repulsive interactions (a=0, b>0) and Scenario II with both attractive and repulsive interactions (a>0, b>0). Thermodynamic functions and isospin charge susceptibilities up to fourth order are then computed across the (μ_I, T) phase diagram.

## Key numbers
- Scenario I: b ≈ 9.09/m_π² with χ²/dof ≈ 1.62, fitted to lattice data for μ_I/m_π < 2
- Scenario II: a ≈ 0.93 and b ≈ 11.39/m_π² with χ²/dof ≈ 1.35
- In Scenario II, the first-order phase transition occurs at μ₀ ≈ 0.993 m_π, with ground state density n_I ≈ 0.022 fm⁻³ and binding energy W ≈ −1 MeV
- In Scenario II, the critical point is located at T_c ≈ 0.369 m_π, μ_c ≈ 0.991 m_π, and n_c ≈ 0.051 m_π³
- The analytical approximation for T_c gives T_c ≈ 0.393 m_π, within 6% of the numerical result
- At μ_I = 0 and temperatures 120–160 MeV, corrections to the ideal pion gas pressure do not exceed 1% in either scenario

## Context
This work extends a mean-field model previously applied to pion matter at zero chemical potential by Mishustin et al. (2019) and to alpha particles by Satarov et al. (2020). It builds on lattice QCD results at finite isospin density from Brandt et al. (2017, 2018) and on chiral perturbation theory predictions by Son and Stephanov (2000) and Adhikari et al. (2019). The study enables future lattice QCD simulations at temperatures below 100 MeV to distinguish between a second-order Bose-Einstein condensation transition and a first-order liquid-gas phase transition with a critical point in pion matter, which has implications for understanding pion stars, early Universe cooling, and heavy-ion collision phenomena.
