# Analytic Solution for the Helicity Evolution Equations at Small $x$ and Large $N_c\&N_f$

> We construct an exact analytic solution of the revised small-$x$ helicity evolution equations, where the contributions of the quark-to-gluon and gluon-to-quark transition operators were newly included. These evolution equations are written in the large-$N_c\&N_f$ limit and are double-logarithmic, resumming powers of $α_s\ln^2(1/x)$. Here $N_c$ and $N_f$ are the numbers of quark colors and flavors, while $α_s$ is the strong coupling constant and $x$ is the Bjorken-$x$ variable. Using our solution, we obtain analytic expressions for the flavor singlet quark and gluon helicity parton distribution functions (PDFs) and for the $g_1$ structure function as double-inverse Laplace transforms. We also extract analytic expressions for the four DGLAP polarized anomalous dimensions $Δγ_{qq}, Δγ_{qG}, Δγ_{Gq}$, and $Δγ_{GG}$: these expressions resum powers of $α_s/ω^2$ to all orders at large-$N_c\&N_f$ (with $ω$ the Mellin moment variable). We extract the leading small-$x$ growth of the helicity distributions, \begin{align} ΔΣ(x,Q^2) \sim ΔG(x,Q^2)\sim g_1(x,Q^2) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^{α_h}, \end{align} where the intercept $α_h$ satisfies an algebraic equation. We determine $α_h$ numerically for various values of $N_c$ and $N_f$. We further obtain the explicit asymptotic expressions for the helicity distributions, which yield numerical values for the ratio of the gluon helicity PDF to the flavor singlet quark helicity PDF in the small-$x$ asymptotic limit (for different $N_f/N_c$). We find that all our predictions for polarized DGLAP anomalous dimensions are fully consistent with the existing finite-order calculations. Similar to the large-$N_c$ case, our intercept $α_h$ exhibits a very slight disagreement with the predictions made within the infrared evolution equations framework.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an exact analytic solution to the revised small-x helicity evolution equations at large N_c and N_f, where the newly included quark-to-gluon and gluon-to-quark transition operators resolve previous discrepancies with finite-order calculations of polarized DGLAP splitting functions, though the resulting intercept alpha_h still exhibits a very slight disagreement with predictions from the infrared evolution equations framework of Bartels, Ermolaev, and Ryskin.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled set of double-logarithmic integral equations for the polarized dipole amplitudes Q, G_2, G_tilde, and Q_tilde using double-inverse-Laplace transforms in the variables eta and s_10, which are rescaled versions of the center-of-mass energy and dipole transverse size. They convert the integral equations into algebraic equations for the Laplace-transformed amplitudes, solve the resulting linear system, and invert the transforms to obtain analytic expressions for the helicity parton distribution functions and the g_1 structure function. The polarized DGLAP anomalous dimensions are extracted by matching the small-x evolution to the DGLAP evolution in Mellin moment space.

## Key numbers

* The small-x intercept alpha_h for the large-N_c case is 3.66074 times the square root of alpha_s N_c/(2 pi), compared to the BER prediction of 3.66394 times the same factor.
* The four-loop GG polarized DGLAP anomalous dimension from this work is 496 times alpha_s^4/(2 pi)^4 divided by omega^7, while the BER prediction is 504 times the same factor.
* The flavor singlet quark helicity PDF Delta Sigma, gluon helicity PDF Delta G, and the g_1 structure function all grow as (1/x)^(alpha_h) at asymptotically small x, where alpha_h satisfies an algebraic equation solved numerically for various N_c and N_f values.

## Context

This work extends the analytic solution methodology of Borden and Kovchegov (2023) from the large-N_c limit to the large-N_c and N_f limit, incorporating the quark-to-gluon and gluon-to-quark transition operators derived in Borden, Kovchegov, and Chirilli (2024). The results resolve discrepancies with finite-order DGLAP calculations that appeared in earlier large-N_c and N_f evolution equations, while maintaining the same small disagreement with the infrared evolution equations framework of Bartels, Ermolaev, and Ryskin (1996) and Blumlein (1996) that was observed in the large-N_c case. The analytic solution enables precise predictions for the asymptotic ratio of gluon to quark helicity PDFs and provides resummed expressions for all four polarized DGLAP anomalous dimensions to all orders in the double-logarithmic parameter.
