# Origins of the di-jet asymmetry in heavy ion collisions

> The di-jet asymmetry --- the measure of the momentum imbalance in a di-jet system --- is a key jet quenching observable. Using the event generator \jewel we show that the di-jet asymmetry is dominated by fluctuations both in proton-proton and in heavy ion collisions. We discuss how in proton-proton collisions the asymmetry is generated through recoil and out-of-cone radiation. In heavy ion collisions two additional sources contribute to the asymmetry, namely energy loss fluctuations and differences in path length. The latter is shown to be a sub-leading effect. We discuss the implications of our results for the interpretation of this observable.

## Key claim

Using the JEWEL event generator, the authors show that the di-jet asymmetry in heavy ion collisions is dominated by fluctuations in both the vacuum-like fragmentation pattern and medium-induced energy loss, while the difference in path length between the leading and sub-leading jet is a sub-leading effect.

## Method

The study uses the JEWEL 2.0.2 Monte Carlo event generator to simulate di-jet events in proton-proton and central lead-lead collisions at a nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV. Jets are reconstructed with the anti-kt algorithm with radius parameter R=0.4 within |eta| < 2, with di-jet cuts of pT,1 > 100 GeV, pT,2 > 20 GeV, and azimuthal separation Delta phi > pi/2. The analysis compares scenarios with realistic Glauber-model production point distributions versus central production to isolate path-length effects, and uses gamma-jet events to study the relationship between initial parton properties and reconstructed jet momentum.

## Key numbers

- The di-jet asymmetry is defined as A_J = (pT,1 - pT,2) / (pT,1 + pT,2)
- In 34% of di-jet systems in heavy ion collisions, the leading jet has the longer path length
- The mean path-length difference increases from <Delta L_n> = 0.56 fm in the most symmetric asymmetry bin to <Delta L_n> = 1.86 fm in the most asymmetric bin
- The average path length for jets produced in the center is 4 fm, while with distributed production points the average is 3.74 fm
- The fraction of di-jets lost when going from p+p to Pb+Pb is 0.637 +/- 0.002 for the full A_J^(in) range, ranging from 0.623 +/- 0.002 in the lowest asymmetry bin to 0.729 +/- 0.034 in the 0.4-0.6 bin
- Transverse momentum loss in Pb+Pb events is nearly a factor of two larger than in p+p collisions
- 13% of p+p di-jet events are rejected due to contamination from initial state radiation

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet quenching observables first measured by ATLAS (Aad 2010) and CMS (Chatrchyan 2012) for fully reconstructed jets in heavy ion collisions. The results contradict the common assumption that path-length differences between leading and sub-leading jets are the primary driver of di-jet asymmetry, and instead support the picture of intra-jet coherence effects argued by Casalderrey-Solana (2012). The findings are consistent with the observation by Chesler (2015) that fractional energy loss depends on jet opening angle, and the analysis provides a methodology that can be replicated for other jet quenching observables to identify sensitivity to specific medium properties.
