# Kinetic theory with Berry curvature from quantum field theories

> A kinetic theory can be modified to incorporate triangle anomalies and the chiral magnetic effect by taking into account the Berry curvature flux through the Fermi surface. We show how such a kinetic theory can be derived from underlying quantum field theories. Using the new kinetic theory, we also compute the parity-odd correlation function that is found to be identical to the result in the perturbation theory in the next-to-leading order hard dense loop approximation.

## Key claim

The authors show that a kinetic theory incorporating Berry curvature corrections, which reproduce triangle anomalies and the chiral magnetic effect, can be microscopically derived from underlying quantum field theories by carefully integrating out the antiparticle degrees of freedom in the high density effective theory, rather than being postulated phenomenologically.

## Method

Starting from the Lagrangian for right-handed chiral fermions at finite chemical potential, the authors construct the high density effective theory by decomposing the fermion field into particle (psi_+) and antiparticle (psi_-) components near the Fermi surface and integrating out the psi_- degrees of freedom order by order in 1/mu. They then perform a derivative expansion of the gauge-covariant Wigner transform of the two-point function for the remaining psi_+ field, obtaining the on-shell condition and transport equation to second order in the expansion parameters. The resulting kinetic equation and modified definitions of particle number density and current are shown to match the previously known phenomenological forms.

## Key numbers

* Berry curvature for chiral fermions: Omega_p = +/- hat{p} / (2|p|^2), where the sign corresponds to right-handed and left-handed fermions respectively.
* Triangle anomaly from the kinetic theory: partial_t n + nabla dot j = +/- (1/(4 pi^2)) E dot B for right-handed and left-handed fermions.
* Chiral magnetic effect current: j = +/- (mu/(4 pi^2)) B for chiral fermions at finite chemical potential mu.
* Dispersion relation near the Fermi surface: epsilon_p = p - (B dot hat{p})/(2p) to leading order in 1/mu.
* Fermi velocity relation from Lorentz invariance: v_f (1 + (1/3) F_1^A) = p_f / mu, where F_1^A is a Landau parameter.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological kinetic theory with Berry curvature proposed by Son (2012) by providing its first-principles derivation from quantum field theory, clarifying the microscopic origin of the modifications to the particle number density and current. The derivation uses the high density effective theory framework of Hong (1998) and Schafer (2003), and the resulting kinetic theory reproduces the parity-odd correlation function found in perturbation theory beyond the leading-order hard dense loop approximation of Blaizot and Iancu (2001) and Manuel (1995). This enables systematic inclusion of anomalous transport effects, such as the chiral magnetic effect relevant to heavy ion collisions and Weyl semimetals, in a controlled field-theoretic setting.
