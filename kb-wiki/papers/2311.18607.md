# Effect of color randomization on $p_T$ broadening of fast partons in turbulent quark-gluon plasma

> We analyze the effect of the parton color randomization on $p_T$ broadening in the quark-gluon plasma with turbulent color fields. We calculate the transport coefficient for a simplified model of fluctuating color fields in the form of alternating sequential transverse layers with homogenous transverse chromomagnetic fields with random orientation in the SU(3) group and gaussian distribution in the magnitude. Our numerical results show that the color randomization can lead to a sizable reduction of the turbulent contribution to $\hat{q}$. The magnitude of the effect grows with increasing ratio of the electric and magnetic screening masses.

## Key claim

The paper shows that color randomization of fast partons due to gluon exchanges with the QGP medium can cause a sizable reduction of the turbulent contribution to the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$, with the magnitude of this reduction growing as the ratio of electric to magnetic screening masses increases.

## Method

The author calculates $p_T$ broadening in a simplified model where the turbulent color fields are represented as alternating sequential transverse layers, each containing a homogeneous transverse chromomagnetic field with random orientation in the SU(3) group and Gaussian distribution in magnitude. The thermal QGP constituents are treated using the model of Debye screened color centers, and the evolution operator for the transverse density matrix is computed by averaging over both the fluctuating background fields and the positions of scattering centers, with intermediate color states replaced by color singlet states after averaging over SU(3) rotations.

## Key numbers

- The transport coefficient for the thermal contribution in the static model in the leading-logarithmic approximation is $\hat{q}_{th}^{st} \approx \frac{g^4 C_R \xi(3)[C_F N_F 9 + C_A 16] T^3}{32\pi^3} \ln\left(\frac{6ET}{m_D^2}\right)$
- The transport coefficient for the thermal contribution in the HTL scheme in the leading-logarithmic approximation is $\hat{q}_{th}^{HTL} \approx \frac{g^4 C_R T^3[1+N_F/6]}{4\pi} \ln\left(\frac{6ET}{m_D^2}\right)$
- The ratio of $\hat{q}_{th}$ for the HTL scheme to that for the static model is approximately 1.19 for $N_f=2.5$
- The Debye mass for the QGP is $m_D = gT\sqrt{1+N_F/6}$
- The effective quark number density is $n_q^{eff} = \xi(3)[N_F 9 + C_A 16/C_F] T^3/\pi^2$

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of $p_T$ broadening in turbulent color fields by Mrowczynski (classical approach using Wong's equations) and by Dumitru and Muller (quantum calculation in glasma color fields), which ignored the effect of color randomization. The study is motivated by the connection between the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ and the shear viscosity of the QGP, as discussed by Asakawa, Muller, and Wang, where $\eta/s \sim 1.5 T^3/\hat{q}$. The results enable more accurate modeling of jet quenching and turbulent shear viscosity in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
