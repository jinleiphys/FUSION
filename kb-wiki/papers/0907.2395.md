# Hadron mass corrections in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering

> We derive mass corrections for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of leptons from nucleons using a collinear factorization framework which incorporates the initial state mass of the target nucleon and the final state mass of the produced hadron. The formalism is constructed specifically to ensure that physical kinematic thresholds for the semi-inclusive process are explicitly respected. A systematic study of the kinematic dependencies of the mass corrections to semi-inclusive cross sections reveals that these are even larger than for inclusive structure functions, especially at very small and very large hadron momentum fractions. The hadron mass corrections compete with the experimental uncertainties at kinematics typical of current facilities, and will be important to efforts at extracting parton distributions or fragmentation functions from semi-inclusive processes at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives hadron mass corrections (HMCs) for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) using collinear factorization, showing that these corrections are larger than target mass corrections in inclusive DIS and that they compete with experimental uncertainties at kinematics typical of current facilities, particularly at very small and very large hadron momentum fractions.

## Method

The authors used a collinear factorization framework to compute leading-order SIDIS cross sections with finite target nucleon mass M and produced hadron mass m_h, rescaling the parton distribution functions to a modified Nachtmann variable ξ_h and the fragmentation functions to a finite-Q^2 variable ζ_h, with the prescription chosen to respect physical kinematic thresholds. They then computed ratios of mass-corrected to massless cross sections numerically using leading-order CTEQ6L parton distributions and KKP fragmentation functions, and compared the size of the corrections to experimental uncertainties from Jefferson Lab experiment E00-108 and HERMES data.

## Key numbers

- At x_B = 0.3 and Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, the ratio σ/σ^(0) for charged pion production is enhanced by ≤ 20% for z_h ≲ 0.7, rising dramatically as z_h → 1
- At x_B = 0.8, the same ratio shows approximately an order of magnitude larger overall effect compared to x_B = 0.3
- At Q^2 = 2 GeV^2 and x_B = 0.8, the mass-corrected parton distribution f_q(ξ_h) is several times larger than the uncorrected f_q(x_B); even at Q^2 = 20 GeV^2 the HMC is some 50%
- For x_B = 0.3 and Q^2 = 5 GeV^2, HMCs yield upward corrections of ≤ 10% for pions, but downward corrections of ≤ 20% for kaons and ≤ 40% for protons/antiprotons over 0.3 ≲ z_h ≲ 0.8
- For the Jefferson Lab experiment E00-108 at Q^2 ≈ 2.5 GeV^2 and x_B = 0.32, the mass effects are approximately 2 times larger than the experimental statistical errors
- At HERMES kinematics (Q^2 ≈ 2.5 GeV^2, x_B = 0.082), mass corrections are generally very small compared with experimental uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach to target mass corrections previously applied to inclusive DIS by Kretzer, Accardi, and collaborators, and addresses the combined effects of target and produced hadron masses in SIDIS. Prior work by Albino et al. studied final state hadron mass effects but neglected target mass, while Mulders derived corrections for both masses without discussing phenomenological consequences; neither addressed kinematic threshold problems. The formalism enables reliable extraction of parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions from SIDIS data at intermediate energies, particularly at facilities like Jefferson Lab and HERMES, and provides a model-independent method to account for finite-Q^2 effects that become significant at large x_B and low Q^2.
