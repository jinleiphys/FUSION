# A comparison of two possible nuclear effective field theory expansions around the one- and two-pion exchange potentials

> In the effective field theory formalism nuclear forces are organized as a low energy expansion. Usually the lowest order in this expansion corresponds to the non-perturbative iteration of the one-pion exchange potential and a few contact-range operators as to ensure renormalization group invariance. Here we consider an alternative expansion in which two-pion exchange becomes the lowest order finite range interaction and as such is expected to be iterated. A comparison of this new expansion with the standard one shows a better convergence pattern for a few selected partial waves ($^1S_0$, $^3P_0$ and $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$) in two-nucleon scattering, in particular for the $^1S_0$ channel (though both expansions converge well). We briefly comment on the possible theoretical grounds for the expansion around two-pion exchange from the point of view of effective field theory.

# A comparison of two possible nuclear effective field theory expansions around the one- and two-pion exchange potentials

## Key claim
The alternative expansion in which two-pion exchange (TPE) is iterated non-perturbatively at leading order shows a better convergence pattern than the standard one-pion exchange (OPE) expansion for selected partial waves in two-nucleon scattering, particularly for the $^1S_0$ channel, though both expansions converge well. The author notes that this improved convergence may be explained by the actual numerical values of the TPE scales being significantly smaller than naive dimensional analysis expectations, with subleading TPE scales around 220-233 MeV rather than the expected 0.5-1.0 GeV.

## Method
The author constructs two power countings: EFT(OPE), where OPE is iterated non-perturbatively at leading order, and EFT(TPE), where both leading and subleading TPE (orders $\nu=2$ and $\nu=3$ in the chiral expansion) are promoted to leading order and iterated. For each expansion, the leading-order wave functions and scattering amplitudes are obtained using boundary condition methods to determine the minimum number of contact couplings required for renormalization group invariance, and subleading-order phase shifts are calculated perturbatively using distorted wave methods with a delta-shell regularization. The phase shifts are calibrated to reproduce scattering lengths from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and fitted to Nijmegen II phase shifts within momentum windows.

## Key numbers
- Scattering lengths used for calibration: $\alpha_0(^1S_0) = -23.7\,{\rm fm}$, $\alpha_0(^3P_0) = -2.65\,{\rm fm}^3$, $\alpha_0(^3S_1) = 5.42\,{\rm fm}$, $\alpha_0(E_1) = 1.67\,{\rm fm}^3$, $\alpha_0(^3D_1) = 6.60\,{\rm fm}^5$
- Subleading TPE scales extracted from chiral potentials: $\Lambda_{\rm TPE(SL)}(^1S_0) = +233\,{\rm MeV}$ and $\Lambda_{\rm TPE(SL)}(^3S_1) = +220\,{\rm MeV}$, compared to NDA expectation of $\sim (0.5-1.0)\,{\rm GeV}$
- Leading TPE scales: $\Lambda_{\rm TPE(L)}(^1S_0) = +389\,{\rm MeV}$ and $\Lambda_{\rm TPE(L)}(^3S_1) = -370\,{\rm MeV}$
- Cutoff range used for uncertainty bands: $R_c = (0.5-1.0)\,{\rm fm}$
- Fitting momentum window for subleading couplings: $k = (100-200)\,{\rm MeV}$ center-of-mass momentum, except for the singlet channel where windows include $k = (10-40)\,{\rm MeV}$, $(10-80)\,{\rm MeV}$, and $(10-200)\,{\rm MeV}$ depending on order
- Chiral coupling values used: $g_A = 1.26$, $f_{\pi} = 92.4\,{\rm MeV}$, $m_{\pi} = 138.03\,{\rm MeV}$, $c_3 = -3.4\,{\rm GeV}^{-1}$, $c_4 = +3.4\,{\rm GeV}^{-1}$

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of nuclear effective field theory power countings, particularly the Weinberg prescription (Weinberg, 1990; 1991) and the observation that the $^1S_0$ channel is well reproduced when TPE plus a contact operator is treated as leading order (Pavon Valderrama, 2019; Valderrama, 2010). It builds on the recent proposal by Mishra (2021) to consider TPE as an organizing principle for the $^1S_0$ singlet. The comparison enables future Bayesian analyses of convergence patterns in both expansions, as suggested by the author, and could inform the development of more systematically improvable nuclear forces for ab initio calculations.
