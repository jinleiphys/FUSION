# Quantum properties of a non-Gaussian state in the large-N approximation

> We study the properties of a non-Gaussian density matrix for a O(N) scalar field in the context of the incomplete description picture. This is of relevance for studies of decoherence and entropy production in quantum field theory. In particular, we study how the inclusion of the simplest non-Gaussian correlator in the set of measured observables modifies the effective (Gaussian) description one can infer from the knowledge of the two-point functions only. We compute exactly the matrix elements of the non-Gaussian density matrix at leading order in a 1/N-expansion. This allows us to study the quantum properties (purity, entropy, coherence) of the corresponding state for arbitrarily strong nongaussianity. We find that if the Gaussian and the non-Gaussian observers essentially agree concerning quantum purity or correlation entropy, their conclusion can significantly differ for other, more detailed aspects such as the degree of quantum coherence of the system.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the simplest non-Gaussian correlator (the four-point function) in the set of measured observables does not change global properties such as quantum purity or correlation entropy compared to a Gaussian description, but it can lead to significantly different conclusions regarding more detailed aspects such as the degree of quantum coherence, particularly for large nongaussianity where the Gaussian observer may see a thermal state while the non-Gaussian observer sees a highly coherent quantum state.

## Method

The authors consider an O(N)-symmetric scalar field state with given Gaussian two-point correlators and a non-Gaussian four-field correlator, and construct the least biased effective density matrix that maximizes von Neumann entropy consistent with these measured observables. They compute the matrix elements of this non-Gaussian density operator at leading order in a 1/N expansion using a saddle-point approximation, which allows them to study the quantum properties for arbitrarily strong nongaussianity. The Wigner function is then computed numerically in the large-N limit to analyze quantum coherence properties.

## Key numbers

- The ratio C_4/(2F^2) is bounded between -1 and 0, with the extremal value C_4 = -2F^2 exactly canceling the connected contribution to the correlator ⟨(φ_a φ_a)^2/N⟩
- For n ≫ 1, the purity ratio p/p_0 is bounded between 1 and √(2/e) ≈ 0.86, with the two limits corresponding to Gaussian (x=0) and extreme non-Gaussian (x≫1) cases respectively
- The condition for imaginary solutions of the saddle-point equation requires κ ≤ 3 and x ≥ 1/(2(1-κ/3)), which corresponds to n ≤ n_c ≈ e^{-3}
- The Gaussian square-mass parameter z_0^2 = ln²(1+1/n)(1-2x) turns negative for x > 1/2, marking the transition from perturbative to non-perturbative regimes

## Context

This work extends the incomplete description picture of Balian (1989) and the real-time dynamics studies of entropy production and decoherence in QFT by Giraud and Serreau (2009) and Koksma, Prokopec, and Schmidt (2009), which were restricted to Gaussian correlators. The authors build on the analysis of correlation entropy for deformations of Gaussian density matrices by Koksma, Prokopec, and Schmidt (2010), but go beyond small deformations to study arbitrarily large nongaussianity. The results enable future nonequilibrium calculations incorporating non-Gaussian observables and provide tools for understanding information spread in correlation functions during thermalization.
