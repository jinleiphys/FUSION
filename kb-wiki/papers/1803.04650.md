# Ground state properties of $^{4}He$ and $^{12}C$ nuclei at equilibrium and at large static compression at zero temperature using Nijmegen and Reid Soft Core nucleon-nucleon interactions

> In this paper, we investigate the ground state properties ($i.e$ binding energy, nuclear radius, radial density distribution and single particle energies) for $^{4}He$ and $^{12}C$ nuclei at equilibrium and at large static compression at zero temperature by using two realistic different potentials namely, Nijmegen and Reid Soft Core (RSC)potentials. We carry out the calculations in No-Core Shell Model space consisting of six major oscillator shells within the framework of the Constrained Spherical Hartree- Fock (CSHF) approximations. We find out that, the computed equilibrium root mean square radii and the Hartree Fock energies for 4He and 12C with those two different potentials are very close to the experimental values of the nuclear radii and nuclear binding energies for the same nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Constrained Spherical Hartree-Fock (CSHF) method with a six-oscillator-shell No-Core Shell Model space can reproduce experimental ground-state radii and binding energies for 4He and 12C using both Nijmegen and Reid Soft Core (RSC) potentials, but only after adjusting three free parameters (lambda1, lambda2, and hbar-omega-prime) to force agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors performed Constrained Spherical Hartree-Fock (CSHF) calculations for 4He and 12C in a No-Core Shell Model space consisting of six major oscillator shells (21 single-particle orbitals). They used two realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijmegen and Reid Soft Core) with an effective Hamiltonian that includes three adjustable parameters (lambda1, lambda2, and hbar-omega-prime) tuned to reproduce experimental binding energies and radii at equilibrium. The calculations were then repeated at various degrees of static compression (reduced root-mean-square radius) to study the nuclear equation of state.

## Key numbers

* For 4He at equilibrium: root-mean-square radius r_rms = 1.46 fm and Hartree-Fock energy E_HF = -28.296 MeV using both Nijmegen and RSC potentials, matching the experimental binding energy of -28.296 MeV and radius of 1.46 fm.
* For 12C at equilibrium: r_rms = 2.3498 fm and E_HF = -92.167 MeV using Nijmegen potential; r_rms = 2.3508 fm and E_HF = -92.174 MeV using RSC potential, compared to experimental radius 2.35 fm and binding energy -92.162 MeV.
* For 4He at maximum compression: minimum r_rms = 1.244 fm with E_HF = -11.260 MeV using RSC potential; minimum r_rms = 1.327 fm with E_HF = -20.804 MeV using Nijmegen potential.
* For 12C at maximum compression: minimum r_rms = 2.063 fm with E_HF = -49.579 MeV using RSC potential; minimum r_rms = 2.255 fm with E_HF = -82.444 MeV using Nijmegen potential.

## Context

This work extends earlier No-Core Shell Model calculations by Barrett (1996, 2000) and constrained Hartree-Fock studies by Vary (1984, 1985) to compare two realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijmegen and RSC) under static compression. The study enables future investigations of nuclear matter under extreme conditions relevant to astrophysical problems and heavy-ion collisions, and provides a benchmark for testing how the choice of NN potential affects the stiffness of the nuclear equation of state in light nuclei.
