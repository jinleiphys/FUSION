# QCD strings with spinning quarks

> We construct a consistent action for a massive spinning quark on the end of a QCD string that leads to pure Thomas precession of the quark's spin. The string action is modified by the addition of Grassmann degrees of freedom to the string such that the equations of motion for the quark spin follow from boundary conditions, just as do those for the quark's position.

## Key claim

The authors construct a consistent action for a massive spinning quark on the end of a QCD string that yields pure Thomas precession of the quark's spin, with the key condition that the parameters alpha and beta in the modified string action must both equal 1 for consistency and to obtain the correct spin precession.

## Method

The authors introduce Grassmann-valued fields on the string worldsheet and modify the Polyakov string action by replacing the derivative of the string position with a combination that includes bilinear terms in the fermionic variables. The equations of motion for the quark spin arise from boundary conditions on these worldsheet fermionic variables, analogous to how the quark's position equations arise from boundary conditions on the bosonic string coordinates. The parameters in the modified action are fixed by requiring conservation of the Dirac equation constraint and pure Thomas precession of the spin.

## Key numbers

- The parameters alpha and beta in the modified string action are both fixed to 1 (alpha = beta = 1) from consistency requirements and the condition for pure Thomas precession
- The Thomas frequency for uniform circular motion is given by Omega_T = -(gamma - 1) omega, where gamma is the Lorentz factor and omega is the angular velocity
- The string tension T and quark mass m appear as parameters in the action, with the quark momentum given by p_mu = m u_mu + i lambda P_{munu} xi^nu / sqrt(-dot x^2)

## Context

This work extends the bosonic string with a spinless massive quark end developed by Ida (1977) and the relativistic flux tube model, which produced realistic meson spectra but lacked quark spin. The authors build on the suggestion by Buchmuller (1981) that quark spin should undergo pure Thomas precession due to a purely chromoelectric field in the quark's rest frame, which is supported by experimental data and QCD calculations by Eichten and Bali (1997). The construction enables the inclusion of spin into numerical quantization schemes for the relativistic flux tube model, providing conserved momentum and angular momentum as starting points for quantization.
