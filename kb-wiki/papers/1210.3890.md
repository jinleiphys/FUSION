# Evidence for BFKL and saturation dynamics from di-hadron spectra at the LHC

> We demonstrate that rapidity separated di-hadron spectra in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC can be quantitatively described by a combination of BFKL and saturation dynamics. Based on these results, we predict the systematics of di-hadron spectra in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the combination of BFKL evolution and gluon saturation dynamics, within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, can quantitatively describe the rapidity-separated di-hadron spectra measured by CMS in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC, but the agreement requires a K-factor of unity for the Glasma graphs only after including subleading contributions, and the BFKL contribution on the nearside is taken to be independent of azimuthal angle within "ridge accuracy" as a conjecture.

## Method
The authors compute the double inclusive di-hadron yield as the sum of two contributions: "Glasma graphs" (leading and subleading in transverse momentum over saturation scale) from the Color Glass Condensate, and "back-to-back" graphs described by the leading-logarithmic-in-x BFKL Green's function with running coupling. The unintegrated gluon distributions are obtained by solving the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, and the results are compared to CMS data using the zero-yield-at-minimum (ZYAM) procedure to extract the associated yield per trigger.

## Key numbers
- The initial scale for the BK evolution is set to \(Q_0^2 = 0.168\) GeV\(^2\) for minimum bias p+p and \(Q_0^2 = 0.672\) GeV\(^2\) for central p+p collisions.
- For p+Pb collisions, the initial saturation scale for lead is taken as three times the minimum bias proton value, \(Q_0^2 = 0.504\) GeV\(^2\) for minimum bias, and six times, \(Q_0^2 = 1.008\) GeV\(^2\), for central lead ions.
- The saturation scales probed in the di-hadron studies span \(Q_s^2(x \sim 10^{-4 \div 5}) \sim 1-5\) GeV\(^2\) in the adjoint representation.
- Inclusion of subleading Glasma graphs lowers the required K-factor from \(K=2.3\) in a previous study to \(K=1\).
- The BFKL contribution requires a K-factor of \(K_{\text{BFKL}} = 1.1\) to match the awayside data.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Dusling and Venugopalan, 2012) of the "ridge" in high-multiplicity p+p collisions by including subleading Glasma graphs and a full BFKL treatment of the awayside. It contrasts with the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) evolution and with the Mueller-Navelet framework for dijet decorrelation. The predictions for p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV are intended to be tested against forthcoming LHC data, and the framework can be extended to di-jet production as proposed by Deak and Kutak (2010, 2012).
