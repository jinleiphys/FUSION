# Towards a Unified Description of Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonances in a Self-Consistent Quasiparticle-Vibration Coupling Approach

> "Why is the EoS for tin so soft?" is a longstanding question, which prevents us from determining the nuclear incompressibility $K_\infty$ accurately. To solve this puzzle, a fully self-consistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) plus quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QPVC) approach based on Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov is developed. We show that the many-body correlations introduced by QPVC, which shift the ISGMR energy in Sn isotopes by about 0.4 MeV more than the energy in $^{208}$Pb, play a crucial role in providing a unified description of the ISGMR in Sn and Pb isotopes. The best description of the experimental strength functions is given by SV-K226 and KDE0, which are characterized by incompressibility values $K_\infty=$ 226 MeV and 229 MeV, respectively, at mean field level.

# Towards a Unified Description of Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonances in a Self-Consistent Quasiparticle-Vibration Coupling Approach

## Key claim

The many-body correlations introduced by quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QPVC) shift the ISGMR energy in Sn isotopes by about 0.4 MeV more than the shift in 208Pb, and this differential shift is crucial for resolving the longstanding "softness" puzzle of tin isotopes. The best simultaneous description of ISGMR strength functions in Ca, Sn, and Pb is given by Skyrme forces SV-K226 and KDE0, with mean-field incompressibility values K∞ = 226 MeV and 229 MeV respectively, but only when QPVC effects are included.

## Method

A fully self-consistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) plus quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QPVC) approach was developed based on Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) theory in a spherical coordinate-space code. The QRPA equations were solved in the canonical basis with volume pairing forces, and phonons with multipolarities Jπ = 0+, 1-, 2+, 3-, 4+, 5- and energies below 30 MeV were included in the coupling, using a subtraction procedure to preserve the inverse energy-weighted sum rule. The constrained energy Ec = √(m1/m-1) was used to characterize the ISGMR energy, calculated over energy intervals 10-30 MeV for Ca and 5-25 MeV for Sn and Pb.

## Key numbers

- The energy shift from QRPA to QRPA+QPVC in Sn isotopes is 0.7-0.8 MeV downward, bringing ISGMR energies into agreement with experimental data along the whole Sn isotopic chain.
- The energy shifts in Ca and Sn isotopes are about 0.4 MeV larger than the shift in 208Pb, enabling a unified description across these nuclei.
- For 120Sn with SV-K226, the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) m1 is exhausted at 98.7% up to 100 MeV without subtraction, and at 106.8% with the subtraction procedure; the inverse energy-weighted sum rule m-1 is 818.45 fm4/MeV with subtraction, nearly equal to the QRPA value of 820.71 fm4/MeV.
- The best-performing Skyrme forces SV-K226 and KDE0 have mean-field incompressibility values K∞ = 226 MeV and 229 MeV, respectively, consistent with the previous constraint K∞ = 240 ± 20 MeV from 208Pb ISGMR in QRPA.
- At QRPA level, 120Sn and 48Ca prefer SkP with K∞ = 201 MeV, while 208Pb prefers SkM* (K∞ = 217 MeV), SV-K226 (226 MeV), and KDE0 (229 MeV); with QPVC, SV-K226 and KDE0 describe all three nuclei simultaneously with deviations from experiment of 0.02-0.36 MeV for 120Sn, 0.25-0.36 MeV for 208Pb, and 0.36-0.51 MeV for 48Ca.
- The EWSR for 120Sn with SV-K226 is 215185.4 fm4MeV from the double-commutator expectation value.

## Context

This work addresses the longstanding "softness" puzzle of tin isotopes, where QRPA models that reproduce the ISGMR centroid energy in 208Pb overestimate it in 112-124Sn by about 1 MeV, as reported by T. Li (2007) and T. Li (2010). Previous attempts to resolve this included exploring surface pairing by J. Li (2008) and L. G. Cao (2012), and the "mutually enhanced magicity" effect ruled out by Patel (2013) and Khan (2009). The present work extends self-consistent particle-vibration coupling approaches developed for electric dipole resonances by Lyutorovich (2012), Y. F. Niu (2016), and Litvinova (2007, 2008) to the ISGMR for the first time. The results enable a unified description of ISGMR across Ca, Sn, and Pb isotopes, providing a path to more accurate determination of nuclear incompressibility K∞ and constraining the nuclear equation of state relevant for supernova explosions and neutron star mergers.
