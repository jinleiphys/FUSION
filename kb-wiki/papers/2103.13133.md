# Updated systematics of intermediate-energy single-nucleon removal cross sections

> The body of experimental measurements of intermediate-energy reactions that remove a single nucleon from a secondary beam of neutron- or proton-rich nuclei continues to grow. These data have been analysed consistently using an approximate, eikonal-model treatment of the reaction dynamics combined with appropriate shell-model descriptions of the projectile initial state, the bound final states spectrum of the reaction residue and single-particle removal strengths computed from their wave-function overlaps. The systematics of the ratio $R_s$ of the measured inclusive cross-section to all bound final states and the calculated cross-section to bound shell-model states -- in different regions of the nuclear chart and involving both very weakly-bound and strongly-bound valence nucleons -- is important in relating the empirically deduced orbital occupancies to those from the best available shell-model predictions. Importantly, several new higher-energy measurements, for which the sudden-approximation aspect of the dynamical description is placed on an even stronger footing, now supplement the previously-analysed measurements. These additional data sets are discussed. Their $R_s$ values are shown to conform to and reinforce the earlier-observed systematics, with no indication that the approximately linear reduction in $R_s$ with increasing nucleon separation energy is a consequence of a breakdown of the sudden approximation.

# Updated systematics of intermediate-energy single-nucleon removal cross sections

## Key claim

The ratio \(R_s\) of measured to calculated inclusive single-nucleon removal cross sections continues to show an approximately linear decrease with increasing nucleon separation energy asymmetry \(\Delta S\), and the new higher-energy data (up to 1.6 GeV/nucleon) conform to this trend with no indication that the reduction is a consequence of a breakdown of the sudden approximation in the lower-energy (80–120 MeV/nucleon) analyses.

## Method

The authors reanalyse a growing body of experimental single-nucleon removal cross sections from rare-isotope beams at intermediate energies (above 80 MeV/nucleon) using an eikonal-model treatment of the reaction dynamics combined with shell-model descriptions of the projectile initial state, the bound final-state spectrum of the residue, and single-particle removal strengths from wave-function overlaps. The theoretical inclusive cross section \(\sigma_\text{th}\) is computed as the sum of elastic and inelastic breakup contributions to all bound shell-model final states, and the ratio \(R_s = \sigma_\text{exp}/\sigma_\text{th}\) is plotted against the asymmetry parameter \(\Delta S\) (the difference between neutron and proton separation energies, weighted by partial cross sections when multiple bound states exist). New data sets from measurements at 218–240 MeV/nucleon and at 1.6 GeV/nucleon are added to the earlier compilation, and all calculations use consistent optical potentials, Hartree-Fock radial geometries (SkX Skyrme interaction), and shell-model effective interactions appropriate to each mass region.

## Key numbers

- The trend line for the entire data compilation is \(R_s = 0.61 - 0.016\,\Delta S\), with a scatter of order 0.1 independent of \(\Delta S\).
- For the new \(^{25}\text{F}(-p)\) measurement at 218 MeV/nucleon on a carbon target, \(R_s = 0.48(5)\) with \(\Delta S = 10.17\) MeV.
- For the new \(^{16}\text{C}(-p)\) measurement, \(R_s = 0.34(4)\) with \(\Delta S = 18.1\) MeV.
- The collision time at 100 MeV/nucleon is of order \(8 \times d \times 10^{-24}\) s (with \(d\) in fm, typical path lengths 2–4 fm); at 1.6 GeV/nucleon (\(v/c \approx 0.92\)) the collision time is reduced by more than a factor of 2 relative to 100 MeV/nucleon.
- The fraction of the removal cross section due to elastic breakup ranges from 40% to 17% in cases where the two mechanisms have been distinguished experimentally.
- Estimated errors on \(\sigma_\text{sp}\) from radial-size uncertainties are typically 10% for strongly-bound cases and 5% for weakly-bound cases for a \(\delta R\) uncertainty of 0.1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the systematics first compiled by Gade et al. (2008) and later updated by Tostevin and Gade (2014), adding new measurements from the 80–100 MeV/nucleon range (e.g., \(^{24}\text{O}(-n)\), \(^{44}\text{S}(-n)\), \(^{34}\text{Si}(-n)\), \(^{36}\text{S}(-p)\), \(^{71}\text{Co}(-p)\), \(^{43}\text{P}(-p)\)) and, critically, higher-energy data at 220–240 MeV/nucleon (from RIBF at RIKEN and the External Target Facility in Lanzhou) and at 1.6 GeV/nucleon (from GSI). The results reinforce the earlier linear trend and rule out a breakdown of the sudden approximation as the cause of the \(R_s\) reduction at large positive \(\Delta S\). The paper discusses open questions about the role of short-range correlations (SRC), noting that neutron-proton SRC studies from electron scattering (Hen et al., Duer et al.) suggest that minority protons in neutron-rich nuclei have enhanced high-momentum components, which may reduce shell-model spectroscopic factors for well-bound proton orbitals. The work enables future comparisons with alternative reaction probes such as transfer reactions (Lee et al., 2011; Flavigny et al., 2013; Kay et al., 2013) and \((p,2p)\)/\((p,pn)\) knockout (Atar, Kawase; Ramos), and calls for precision measurements on common projectile-residue systems across these different methods.
