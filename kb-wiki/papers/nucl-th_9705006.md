# Energy loss effects on charm and bottom production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We study the effect of energy loss on charm and bottom quarks in high-energy heavy-ion collisions including longitudinal expansion and partial thermalization. We find that high $p_\perp$ heavy quarks are greatly suppressed, and consequently, high-mass dileptons from heavy quark decays are also suppressed. We consider in detail the detector geometry and single lepton energy cuts of the PHENIX detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). Because of the longitudinal expansion, the suppressions of $ee, eμ$ and $μμ$ pairs resulting from the heavy quark energy loss are very different due to the different rapidity coverages and energy cuts. The assumed energy loss rate, on the order of 1 GeV/fm, results in a large suppression on dielectrons, and dielectrons from heavy quark decays become comparable or even lower than the Drell-Yan yield. It is thus possible to probe the energy loss rate of the medium using dileptons from heavy quark decays.

# Energy loss effects on charm and bottom production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Heavy quark energy loss in dense matter, modeled with longitudinal expansion and partial thermalization, suppresses high-transverse-momentum charm and bottom quarks and consequently suppresses high-mass dileptons from their decays; the suppression pattern across dielectron, electron-muon, and dimuon channels within the PHENIX detector acceptance differs dramatically due to rapidity coverage and energy cuts, making it possible to probe the energy loss rate of the medium.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING event generator to produce charm and bottom quark pair momentum distributions in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, then applied a phenomenological energy loss model with a Bjorken-type longitudinal expansion where heavy quarks follow the longitudinal flow and lose transverse momentum in the fluid rest frame. The number of scatterings was sampled from a Poisson distribution with mean free path lambda=1 fm, and quarks whose transverse momentum fell below the thermal average were assigned a random thermal momentum from a Boltzmann distribution at T=150 MeV. Dilepton spectra from D and B meson decays were generated using JETSET7.4 with PHENIX detector geometry and single lepton energy cuts (E_e>1 GeV, E_mu>2 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Charm pair production cross section: 340 microbarns in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, giving an average of 9.7 charm pairs per central Au+Au event
- Bottom pair production cross section: 1.5 microbarns in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, giving an average of 0.043 bottom pairs per central Au+Au event
- Charm quark mass: 1.3 GeV; bottom quark mass: 4.75 GeV
- Energy loss rates studied: -2 GeV/fm, -1 GeV/fm, and -0.5 GeV/fm
- With dE/dx=-2 GeV/fm and PHENIX acceptance, dielectron yield from charm decays suppressed by roughly a factor of 100, electron-muon yield by a factor of 10, and dimuon yield by a factor of 4 at the spectral peaks
- Static fireball model gave suppression of about 3000 for all three dilepton channels
- Elastic dE/dx for a charm quark at 10 GeV with alpha_s=0.2, n_f=2, T=250 MeV: approximately -0.22 GeV/fm (compared to -0.25 GeV/fm for a massless quark)
- PHENIX electron arm pseudo-rapidity coverage: -0.35 to 0.35; muon arm pseudo-rapidity coverage: 1.15 to 2.44 and -1.15 to -2.44
- Single lepton energy cuts: E_e>1 GeV, E_mu>2 GeV
- Branching ratio for charm semileptonic decay to electrons or muons: 12 percent
- Bottom quark branching ratios: 9.30 percent to primary electrons, 2.07 percent to secondary electrons, 1.25 percent to primary positrons, 7.36 percent to secondary positrons; dielectron branching ratio from single b decay: 0.906 percent

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Shuryak (1996) on heavy quark energy loss in heavy-ion collisions by including longitudinal expansion and thermal fluctuations, which were neglected in that prior work where heavy quarks were considered only at mid-rapidity and mostly at rest. The paper builds on the theoretical framework of radiative energy loss developed by Baier, Dokshitzer, Peigne, and Schiff (1995) and by Gyulassy and Wang (1994), and on earlier calculations by Vogt (1992) and Shuryak and Vogt (1994) showing that dileptons from charm decays dominate over Drell-Yan and bottom decay contributions at RHIC. The results enable experimental searches for thermal dilepton signals from a quark-gluon plasma by quantifying how energy loss reduces the heavy-quark decay background, and suggest that dielectrons are the most sensitive channel for measuring the energy loss rate while dimuons above 4 GeV remain useful for charm observation even with large energy loss.
