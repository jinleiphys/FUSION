# Inferring nuclear structure from heavy isobar collisions using Trajectum

> Nuclei with equal number of baryons but varying proton number (isobars) have many commonalities, but differ in both electric charge and nuclear structure. Relativistic collisions of such isobars provide unique opportunities to study the variation of the magnetic field, provided the nuclear structure is well understood. In this Letter we simulate collisions using several state-of-the-art parametrizations of the $^{96}_{40}$Zr and $^{96}_{44}$Ru isobars and show that a comparison with the exciting STAR measurement arXiv:2109.00131 of ultrarelativistic collisions can uniquely identify the structure of both isobars. This not only provides an urgently needed understanding of the structure of the Zirconium and Ruthenium isobars, but also paves the way for more detailed studies of nuclear structure using relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Inferring nuclear structure from heavy isobar collisions using Trajectum

## Key claim

The authors simulate ultrarelativistic collisions of the isobars ⁹⁶₄₀Zr and ⁹⁶₄₄Ru using five different Woods-Saxon parametrizations and show that comparison with STAR data can uniquely identify the nuclear structure of both isobars, but only when a full hydrodynamic evolution (rather than a simple geometric calculation) is used; the agreement is qualitative rather than quantitative because Trajectum is tuned to LHC energies, not RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors use the Trajectum 1.2 framework with the TᵣENTo initial-state model and UrQMD for the hadronic gas phase, employing maximum likelihood settings tuned to PbPb collisions at LHC energies. They simulate 0.5M hydrodynamic events per case (5M for case 5) for both ⁹⁶₄₄Ru and ⁹⁶₄₀Zr using five different Woods-Saxon parameter sets that include separate proton and neutron distributions, quadrupole deformation β₂, and octupole deformation β₃, then compare the resulting multiplicity distributions, elliptic flow v₂{2}, triangular flow v₃{2}, and mean transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ to STAR measurements.

## Key numbers

- ⁹⁶₄₄Ru has up to 10% higher particle production than ⁹⁶₄₀Zr and up to 3% larger elliptic flow v₂, but 7% smaller triangular flow v₃ for central collisions (STAR data)
- The Woods-Saxon radius for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru ranges from 5.053 fm to 5.085 fm across cases; for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr from 4.912 fm to 5.02 fm
- The quadrupole deformation β₂ for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru ranges from 0.053 to 0.158; for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr from 0.08 to 0.217
- Case 5 includes an octupole deformation β₃ = 0.202 for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr (zero for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru)
- The inelastic nucleus-nucleus cross section for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru ranges from 4.605 b to 4.734 b; for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr from 4.540 b to 4.871 b
- The normalization of the initial energy density was reduced from 18.3 to 7.5, loosely tuned to RHIC entropy densities (underestimating multiplicity by 18%)
- A correction factor of 1.21 was applied to Trajectum multiplicity to account for insufficient tuning to RHIC energies
- The STAR normalization correction factor for the ratio was 1.00857; Trajectum correction factors ranged from 0.9777 to 1.0005

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the STAR isobar run (2021) which found no evidence for the chiral magnetic effect but revealed unexpectedly large differences in particle production and flow between ⁹⁶₄₄Ru and ⁹⁶₄₀Zr. The authors use five Woods-Saxon parametrizations from prior nuclear structure studies: cases 1 and 2 from electron-scattering experiments and the finite-range liquid-drop model (Moller and others, 1995); cases 3 and 4 from density functional theory fits (Guo and others, 2021); and case 5 from AMPT-based fits to the STAR data (Jia and others, 2021). The results agree qualitatively with AMPT studies (Jia and others, 2021; Xu and others, 2021) but provide a true hydrodynamic confirmation. This work enables future studies that include magnetic field effects for the chiral magnetic effect and demonstrates that relativistic heavy-ion collisions can serve as a probe of low-energy nuclear structure, with implications for neutron star physics.
