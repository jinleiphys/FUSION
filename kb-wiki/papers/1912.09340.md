# Postmerger Gravitational-Wave Signatures of Phase Transitions in Binary Mergers

> With the first detection of gravitational waves from a binary system of neutron stars, GW170817, a new window was opened to study the properties of matter at and above nuclear-saturation density. Reaching densities a few times that of nuclear matter and temperatures up to $100\,\rm{MeV}$, such mergers also represent potential sites for a phase transition (PT) from confined hadronic matter to deconfined quark matter. While the lack of a postmerger signal in GW170817 has prevented us from assessing experimentally this scenario, two theoretical studies have explored the postmerger gravitational-wave signatures of PTs in mergers of binary systems of neutron stars. We here extend and complete the picture by presenting a novel signature of the occurrence of a PT. More specifically, using fully general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulations and employing a suitably constructed equation of state that includes a PT, we present the occurrence of a "delayed PT", i.e. a PT that develops only some time after the merger and produces a metastable object with a quark-matter core, i.e. a hypermassive hybrid star. Because in this scenario, the postmerger signal exhibits two distinct fundamental gravitational-wave frequencies -- before and after the PT -- the associated signature promises to be the strongest and cleanest among those considered so far, and one of the best signatures of the production of quark matter in the present Universe.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a novel "delayed phase transition" (DPT) scenario in binary neutron star mergers, where a phase transition from hadronic to quark matter occurs several milliseconds after merger and produces a metastable hypermassive hybrid star rather than a prompt collapse to a black hole. The authors present this as the strongest and cleanest gravitational-wave signature of quark matter production among those considered so far, but note that only about 5% of randomly constructed equations of state actually yield any phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed fully general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the McLachlan code for spacetime evolution and the WhiskyTHC code for hydrodynamics, employing a piecewise polytropic hadronic equation of state (FSU2H) modified with a Gibbs-like construction to include a phase transition (FSU2H-PT). They simulated four equal-mass irrotational binary neutron star systems: two with total masses of 2.64 and 2.68 solar masses using the FSU2H-PT equation of state, and two corresponding systems without a phase transition using the standard FSU2H equation of state. Thermal effects were included via a hybrid equation of state adding an ideal-fluid thermal component with adiabatic index Gamma_th = 1.75.

## Key numbers

- Total binary masses simulated: 2.64 solar masses (low-mass) and 2.68 solar masses (high-mass)
- Initial stellar radii: 13.11 km and 13.13 km for the low-mass and high-mass configurations respectively
- Central rest-mass density in the low-mass DPT case reaches approximately (4-5) times nuclear saturation density (rho_0) in the metastable hybrid star
- Central rest-mass density in the high-mass PTTC case reaches approximately (5-7) times nuclear saturation density before collapse
- Relative frequency shift between hadronic and quark-dominated postmerger emission: 1 - f^q_2/f^h_2 approximately 25%, found robust at different resolutions
- The mixed-phase shell thickness is approximately 1 km
- The subdominant l=2,m=1 gravitational-wave mode has amplitude ratio |h^{21}_+|/|h^{22}_+| less than or equal to 0.1
- The phase transition develops between 4 and 7 milliseconds after merger in the specific setup shown

## Context

This work extends and completes the classification of phase transition signatures in binary neutron star mergers, building on prior studies by Most (2018) which identified the phase-transition triggered collapse scenario, and Bauswein (2019) which explored prompt phase transitions. The DPT scenario is distinct from both the prompt phase transition (where the frequency settles to a constant higher value immediately after merger) and the phase-transition triggered collapse (where the remnant collapses to a black hole). The authors note that only about 5% of over 10^7 randomly constructed piecewise polytropic equations of state actually yield a phase transition, based on analysis by Most (2018). The work enables future searches for quark matter signatures in postmerger gravitational-wave signals, particularly for events where the postmerger emission lasts long enough to show the characteristic frequency shift.
