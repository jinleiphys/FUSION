# Isospin Effects on Strangeness in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Kaon properties are studied within the framework of a fully covariant transport approach. The kaon-nucleon potential is evaluated in two schemes, a chiral perturbative approach and an effective One-Boson-Exchange model. Isospin effects are explicitly accounted for in both models. The transport calculations indicate a significant sensitivity of momentum distributions and total yields of $K^{0,+}$ isospin states on the choice of the kaon-nucleon interaction. Furthermore, isospin effects are rather moderate on absolute kaon yields, but appear on strangeness ratios. This is an important issue in determining the high density symmetry energy from studies of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The isospin-dependent part of the kaon-nucleon interaction produces a significant sensitivity in the momentum distributions and total yields of \(K^{0,+}\) isospin states, but the effect on absolute kaon yields is moderate; the main observable effect appears in the strangeness ratio \(K^{0}/K^{+}\), which retains a sensitivity to the stiffness of the symmetry energy at the level of \(\sim 25\%\) (ChPT) and \(\sim 15\%\) (OBE) even after isospin effects are included.

## Method

The authors used a fully relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport approach extended for isospin and strangeness degrees of freedom. The kaon-nucleon potential was evaluated in two schemes: a chiral perturbative approach (ChPT) and an effective One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) model, with isospin effects explicitly accounted for in both. Transport calculations were performed for central Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV and semi-central Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV, comparing results with and without the isospin-dependent parts of the kaon potentials, and using three different parametrizations of the isovector nuclear mean field (NL, NL\(\rho\), NL\(\rho\delta\)) that produce progressively stiffer symmetry energies.

## Key numbers

- The free kaon mass is \(m_K = 494\) MeV; the pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
- The kaon-nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_{KN}\) is chosen in the range \(350 \div 450\) MeV.
- The parameter \(C\) in the ChPT isovector term is fixed to \(33.5\) MeV.
- The in-medium pion decay constant is taken as \(f^{*2}_\pi \simeq 0.6 f^2_\pi\).
- The saturation density is \(\rho_{sat} = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\); the asymmetry parameter \(\alpha = (N-Z)/(N+Z) = 0.2\) corresponds approximately to a \({}^{197}\)Au nucleus.
- For central Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV, the sensitivity of the strangeness ratio \(K^{0}/K^{+}\) to the stiffness of the symmetry energy survives at the level of \(\sim 25\%\) in the IChPT approach and \(\sim 15\%\) in the IOBE approach.
- The kaon-nucleon potential at saturation density is repulsive on the order of \(20-30\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical investigations of kaon medium effects in heavy-ion collisions (Fuchs, 2006; Fang, 2000) to asymmetric hadronic matter, and builds on the authors' own prior work (Prassa, 2008) by now comparing two different models for the kaon-nucleon interaction (ChPT and OBE) in detail. The study is motivated by the need to determine the high-density symmetry energy from strangeness production, as proposed by Ferini (2006) and others. The results enable future experiments with exotic radioactive beams at facilities such as MSU and GSI (FAIR project) to resolve theoretical uncertainties in the symmetry energy at high baryon densities.
