# Quark model calculations of transition form factors at high photon virtualities

> We present calculations of $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast$ transition form factors, where $N$ is the nucleon and $N^\ast$ is a nucleon resonance, based on a covariant quark model. Our main focus is at high photon virtualities (large $Q^2$) where the valence quark degrees of freedom dominate the contributions to the transition form factors and helicity amplitudes. In that regime, the quark model estimates can be compared with the available data, particularly with the Jefferson Lab data at intermediate and large momentum transfer ($Q^2 >2$ GeV$^2$). The main focus is on the $Δ(1232)3/2^+$, $N(1440)1/2^+$, $N(1535)1/2^-$ and $N(1520)3/2^-$ resonances, but estimates for other higher mass resonances are also discussed.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, which includes only valence quark degrees of freedom, can describe transition form factors for several nucleon resonances at high photon virtualities (Q² > 2 GeV²), but the model fails for specific form factors (the Pauli form factor for N(1535) and the electric form factor for N(1520)) where meson cloud effects remain significant even at intermediate Q².

## Method

The covariant spectator quark model represents baryons as quark-diquark systems with wave functions determined by spin-flavor-radial symmetries, and parametrizes the quark electromagnetic structure using vector meson dominance. Transition form factors are calculated in impulse approximation using the Landau prescription for current conservation, with radial wave functions calibrated using either EBAC bare core estimates or lattice QCD simulations at large pion masses to isolate valence quark contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the Δ(1232) magnetic form factor G_M*, the valence quark model predicts G_M*(0) ≤ 2.07, well below the experimental value G_M*(0) ≈ 3, requiring pion cloud contributions of 30-45% at low Q²
- The d-wave admixture contributions to the Δ(1232) wave function are of order 0.7% for both states
- The γ*N → N(1440) transition form factors are well described by the model for Q² > 1.5 GeV²
- The γ*N → N(1520) magnetic and Coulomb form factors are well described for Q² > 1 GeV², while the electric form factor fails for Q² < 2.5 GeV²
- The γ*N → N(1535) Dirac form factor F₁* is well described above Q² = 1.5 GeV², while the Pauli form factor F₂* has the wrong sign
- The relation S₁/₂ = -√(1+τ)/√2 × (M_R² - M_N²)/(2 M_R Q) × A₁/₂ holds for Q² > 1.5 GeV² for N(1535)
- The neutron electric form factor second moment is r₄⁴ ≈ -0.4 fm⁴, with electric and Coulomb square radii r_E2² = 2.2 ± 0.2 fm² and r_C2² = 1.8 ± 0.1 fm²

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model calculations of Ramalho and collaborators (2017, 2018) for nucleon resonance transitions to higher Q², where valence quark degrees of freedom dominate. The results can be compared with Jefferson Lab data from CLAS (2009, 2012, 2016) and will be testable with future JLab-12 GeV upgrade data. The model enables predictions for the timelike region relevant to HADES Dalitz decay measurements and provides a framework for separating valence quark and meson cloud contributions in dynamical coupled-channel models such as EBAC/Argonne-Osaka (2008).
