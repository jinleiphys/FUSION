# Two freeze-out model for the hadrons produced in the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> An expanding fireball model with two freeze-outs, which assumes that the chemical freeze-out occurs earlier at higher temperature and the thermal freeze-out occurs later at lower temperature, is developed and successfully applied to fit simultaneously the hadron ratios, transverse momentum spectra of measured hadrons and rapidity distribution of charged hadrons measured in Au+Au collision at 200 A$\cdot$GeV by STAR, PHENIX and PHOBOS collaborations. The quality of all the fittings is very good and the resulting parameters are in agreement with published values.

## Key claim

The authors develop a blast-wave model with two distinct freeze-out stages (chemical then thermal) and claim it can simultaneously fit hadron ratios, transverse momentum spectra, and rapidity distributions from Au+Au collisions at 200 A·GeV, but the fit quality is notably worse for the Ξ/π ratio from STAR and the φ/K⁻ ratio from PHENIX, and the rapidity distribution magnitude overpredicts the PHOBOS data at midrapidity.

## Method

The model uses the Cooper-Frye formula with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and azimuthally symmetric ellipsoidal geometry. At the chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch, hadron ratios are fitted using chemical potentials that are algebraic sums of baryon and strangeness chemical potentials; below T_ch, the number of each hadron species is held fixed while the temperature drops to the thermal freeze-out temperature T_th, and the chemical potential for each species is numerically determined to preserve those fixed numbers. Resonance decays are added after thermal freeze-out, and the transverse momentum spectra and rapidity distributions are fitted using the same blast-wave parameters.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature for STAR data: T_ch = 173.4 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature for PHENIX data: T_ch = 173.9 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential from STAR data: μ_B = 18.5 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential from PHENIX data: μ_B = 26.4 MeV
- Strangeness chemical potential from STAR data: μ_s = 7.9 MeV
- Strangeness chemical potential from PHENIX data: μ_s = 6.0 MeV
- Strangeness fugacity from STAR data: γ_s = 0.986
- Strangeness fugacity from PHENIX data: γ_s = 1.01
- Thermal freeze-out temperature: T_th = 121.1 MeV
- Transverse expansion rapidity at thermal freeze-out: ρ_0 = 1.03
- Longitudinal rapidity parameter at thermal freeze-out: η_max = 5.0
- χ²/n for STAR chemical fit (excluding Ξ/π): 1.4; including Ξ/π: 2.85
- χ²/n for PHENIX chemical fit (excluding φ/K⁻): 0.12
- χ²/n for thermal freeze-out fit: 5.3
- Effective volume parameter V_eff = 15597; pion chemical potential μ_π = 115 MeV

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave model of Dobler, Sollfrank, and Heinz (1999) by incorporating a two-freeze-out scenario that had previously been implemented only in hydrodynamic hybrid models (Bass and Dumitru, 2000; Hirano and Tsuda, 2002; Song, Bass, and Heinz, 2011) and partial chemical equilibrium models (Gerber et al., 1992; Teaney, 2002; Kolb and Rapp, 2003; Huovinen, 2008). The model enables a consistent single-parameter-set description of hadron yields, spectra, and rapidity distributions, and the authors state that further studies with LHC data are underway.
