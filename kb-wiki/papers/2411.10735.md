# Self-consistent thermodynamical treatment for quark matter in quasi-particle model at finite temperature

> In this work, we have studied the medium effects in strange quark matter in the framework of a grand-canonical ensemble using the phenomenological quasi-particle model. This model is studied with proper self-consistent thermodynamical treatment by incorporating chemical potential-dependent quark mass. We have also included the vector interaction in a self-consistent way. The main aim of this work is to explore the proper thermodynamic treatment in addressing the medium effects at both zero and finite temperatures. In the case of the finite temperature, we explore the study of self-consistent thermodynamics in the isothermal as well as the isentropic processes. The effect of finite temperature and lepton fraction have been studied on the equation of state, speed of sound, and particle fraction. The $M-R$ and $M-Λ$ diagrams are found to be consistent with the observational constraints.

## Key claim

The authors propose a self-consistent thermodynamic treatment for the quasi-particle model of strange quark matter in the grand-canonical ensemble, claiming that no external counterterm is needed to maintain thermodynamic consistency when the medium effect is incorporated through a chemical potential-dependent quark mass, contrary to previous formulations.

## Method

The authors start from the grand partition function of a free Fermi gas with quark mass modified to account for medium effects via a chemical potential-dependent running coupling, add a constant bag pressure for confinement, and derive all thermodynamic quantities using standard definitions without introducing any counterterm. The model is applied to both zero and finite temperature quark matter, considering isothermal and isentropic processes, and includes vector interactions through a modified chemical potential. The equation of state is computed under charge neutrality, chemical equilibrium, and baryon number conservation for both neutrino-trapped and neutrino-transparent matter.

## Key numbers

- For the case without vector interactions: \(g_0 = 1.0\), \(\alpha_{\mu} = 20.0\), and \(B_0 = 58 \text{ MeV fm}^{-3}\)
- For the case with vector interactions: \(g_0 = 1.0\), \(\alpha_{\mu} = 20.0\), \(B_0 = 50 \text{ MeV fm}^{-3}\), and \(G_V = 0.2 \text{ fm}^{-2}\)
- Quark masses used: \(m_u = 2.16 \text{ MeV}\), \(m_d = 4.67 \text{ MeV}\), \(m_s = 93.4 \text{ MeV}\)
- Stability criterion: energy per baryon for three-flavor quark matter must be less than 930 MeV, while for two-flavor quark matter it must exceed 930 MeV
- Finite temperature calculations performed at \(T = 10 \text{ MeV}\) and \(T = 50 \text{ MeV}\) with lepton fraction \(Y_{Le} = 0.4\) for trapped neutrinos
- Isentropic calculations performed at \(\sigma = 1, Y_{Le} = 0.4\) and \(\sigma = 2, Y_{\nu_e} = 0.0\)
- The \(M-R\) and \(M-\Lambda\) diagrams are found consistent with observational constraints from PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, GW170817 (tidal deformability constraint \(70 \le \Lambda_{1.4} \le 580\)), and HESS J1731-347

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent thermodynamic treatment previously applied to the MIT bag model by Pal and Chaudhuri (2023) to the quasi-particle model, resolving the thermodynamic inconsistency that required external counterterms in earlier formulations by Chu (2021), Schertler (1996), Zhang (2021), and others. The approach enables consistent study of proto-quark star matter in both isothermal and isentropic processes, and the resulting equations of state satisfy astrophysical constraints from NICER and LIGO/Virgo observations, allowing future investigations of hybrid star structure and cooling evolution.
