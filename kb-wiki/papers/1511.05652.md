# Systematic study of $α$ decay half-lives for even-even nuclei within a two-potential approach

> $α$ decay is a common and important process for natural radioactivity of heavy and superheavy nuclei. The $α$ decay half-lives for even-even nuclei from Z=62 to Z=118 are systematically researched based on the two-potential approach with a quasi-stationary state approximation. To describe the deviations between experimental half-lives and calculated results due to the nuclear shell structure, a hindrance factor related with $α$ particle preformation probability is introduced. Our results can well reproduce the experimental data equally to the density-dependent cluster model and the generalized liquid drop model. We also study the isospin effect of nuclear potential in this work. Considering the isospin effect the calculated results improved about 7.3$\%$.

## Key claim

The two-potential approach with a quasi-stationary state approximation, using a constant alpha particle preformation probability of 0.7 and a cosh-parameterized nuclear potential, can reproduce experimental alpha decay half-lives for even-even nuclei from Z=62 to Z=118 equally well as the density-dependent cluster model and the generalized liquid drop model, provided a hindrance factor related to nuclear shell structure is introduced; including an isospin-dependent nuclear potential improves the calculated results by about 7.3%.

## Method

The authors used the two-potential approach with a quasi-stationary state approximation, where the alpha decay half-life is determined from the preformation probability, assault frequency, and penetration probability calculated via the WKB approximation. The nuclear potential was taken in a cosh parameterized form with depth and diffuseness parameters fitted to 164 experimental half-lives from NUBASE2012, using a genetic algorithm to minimize the total square deviation. A hindrance factor with five adjustable parameters, depending on proton and neutron magic numbers and valence nucleon numbers, was introduced to account for shell-structure deviations, and the isospin effect was studied by adding an isospin-dependent term to the nuclear potential.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of 0.350 for the initial fit of 164 even-even nuclei (Z=62 to Z=118) using parameters a=0.5654 fm, V0=189.53 MeV, Pα=0.7
- RMS deviation reduced to 0.205 after introducing the hindrance factor, reproducing experimental half-lives within a factor of 2 for most nuclei
- Including the isospin effect improved calculated results by about 7.3%
- Half-lives span a range from 10^-7 s to 10^22 s across the studied nuclei
- For N=84 isotones, increasing decay energy by 2.6 times results in 18 orders of magnitude shorter half-life

## Context

This work extends the two-potential approach of Gurvitz (1987) and the hindrance factor method of Zhang (2009) and Guo (2015) to a systematic study of even-even nuclei from samarium to flerovium. The results are compared with the density-dependent cluster model of Xu (2005, 2008) and the generalized liquid drop model of Bao (2014), showing comparable accuracy. The study enables improved predictions of alpha decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei and tests the next proton magic number, finding that Z=114 is unlikely to be the next magic number in this mass region, consistent with Dong (2011).
