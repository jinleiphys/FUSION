# Low-lying collective excited states in non-integrable pairing models based on stationary-phase approximation to path integral

> For a description of large-amplitude collective motion associated with nuclear pairing, requantization of time-dependent mean-field dynamics is performed using the stationary-phase approximation (SPA) to the path integral. We overcome the difficulty of the SPA, which is known to be applicable to integrable systems only, by developing a requantization approach combining the SPA with the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method (ASCC+SPA). We apply the ASCC+SPA to multi-level pairing models, which are non-integrable systems, to study the nuclear pairing dynamics. The ASCC+SPA gives a reasonable description of low-lying excited $0^+$ states in non-integrable pairing systems.

## Key claim

The authors propose a requantization approach (ASCC+SPA) that combines the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method with the stationary-phase approximation to the path integral, overcoming the limitation that SPA is applicable only to integrable systems. The method gives a reasonable description of low-lying excited \(0^+\) states in non-integrable multi-level pairing models, though the description is approximate rather than exact.

## Method

The authors first use the ASCC method to extract a one-dimensional collective subspace (plus the pair rotation degree of freedom) from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov phase space of a multi-level pairing model, which is non-integrable. They then apply the stationary-phase approximation to the path integral on this integrable one-dimensional collective subspace, using the Einstein-Brillouin-Keller quantization rule to find periodic trajectories and construct quantized states as superpositions of generalized Slater determinants.

## Key numbers

- The collective Hamiltonian is expanded up to second order in momenta, with the reciprocal mass parameter \(B^{\alpha\beta}(j)\) given explicitly for the pairing model: diagonal elements are \(2g\sum_{\gamma\neq\alpha} \sqrt{j^{\gamma}j^{\alpha}(\Omega_{\gamma}-j^{\gamma})(\Omega_{\alpha}-j^{\alpha})}\) and off-diagonal elements are \(-2g\sqrt{j^{\alpha}j^{\beta}(\Omega_{\alpha}-j^{\alpha})(\Omega_{\beta}-j^{\beta})}\).
- The EBK quantization condition used is \(\oint_{C_k} p_1 dq^1 = 2\pi k\) with \(\hbar=1\), where \(k\) is an integer.
- The ground state with \(k=0\) corresponds to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov state with a fixed particle number \(J = q^n\).

## Context

This work extends the stationary-phase approximation approach previously tested on integrable two-level pairing models by Ni and Nakatsukasa (2018) to non-integrable multi-level systems. The ASCC method was developed from the self-consistent collective coordinate method by Marumori and coworkers (1980) and has been applied to nuclear quadrupole dynamics including shape coexistence by Hinohara and collaborators (2008, 2009). The approach enables microscopic description of low-lying excited \(0^+\) states in nuclei where pairing dynamics plays an important role, such as neutron pair vibrations in Pb isotopes, and provides wave functions directly in terms of microscopic degrees of freedom with automatic restoration of particle-number symmetry.
