# Factorization in Break-up and Recombination Processes for Atoms with a Large Scattering Length

> Break-up and recombination processes for loosely-bound molecules composed of atoms with a large scattering length $a$ necessarily involve interactions that are nonperturbative in the exact 2-body interaction. If these processes involve atoms with relative momenta much larger than $\hbar/|a|$, the leading contributions to their rates can be separated into short-distance factors that are insensitive to $a$ and long-distance factors that are insensitive to the range of the interaction. These factorization contributions can be obtained from the leading term in a perturbation expansion in the exact atom-atom scattering amplitude. The short-distance factors are atom-atom cross sections at a lower collision energy. In the special case of inclusive break-up cross sections for atom-molecule scattering, the long-distance factors simply count the number of atoms in the molecule.

## Key claim

Break-up and recombination rates for loosely-bound molecules can be factorized into short-distance factors (atom-atom cross sections at a lower collision energy) and long-distance factors that are insensitive to the high momentum scale, provided the relative momenta of the atoms involved are much larger than \(\hbar/|a|\), where \(a\) is the large scattering length.

## Method

The authors derived factorization formulas by taking the leading term in a perturbation expansion in the exact atom-atom scattering amplitude, valid when the collision energy \(E \gg \hbar^2/ma^2\). For atom-dimer break-up, the T-matrix element was expressed as a product of a short-distance factor involving the large momentum scale \(Q\) and a long-distance factor proportional to the dimer wavefunction. For inclusive break-up of molecules containing \(N\) atoms, completeness relations were used to show that the long-distance factor reduces to the number \(N\) of atoms in the molecule. For 3-body recombination, the rate was obtained by squaring the T-matrix element and averaging over hyperangular variables.

## Key numbers

* The dimer binding energy is \(E_D = \hbar^2/(ma^2)\) (Eq. 1).
* For identical bosons, the 3-body recombination rate constant at zero energy is \(K_3 = {768 \pi^2 (4 \pi - 3\sqrt{3}) \hbar a^4 /m\over \sinh^2(\pi s_0) + \cosh^2(\pi s_0) \cot^2[ s_0 \ln (a \kappa_*)+ 1.16]}\), where \(s_0 = 1.00624\) and the coefficient of \(\hbar a^4/m\) ranges from 0 to 402.7 (Eq. 3).
* The atom-dimer break-up cross section at collision energy \(E\) just above threshold is \(\sigma_{AD}^{\rm (break-up)}(E) = {\sqrt{3} \over 96 \pi} \left({m K_3 \over \hbar a^4} \right) a^2 {(E - E_D)^2 \over E^{1/2} E_D^{3/2}}\) (Eq. 4).
* For atom-dimer break-up at high energy, \(\sigma_{AD}^{\rm (break-up)}(E) \approx 2 \, \sigma_{AA}(\frac{3}{4}E)\) (Eq. 11).
* In the scaling region \(E_D \ll E \ll \hbar^2/m r_s^2\), the atom-dimer break-up cross section scales as \(\sigma_{AD}^{\rm (break-up)}(E) \approx {64 \pi \hbar^2 \over 3 m E}\) (Eq. 12).
* For inclusive break-up of an \(N\)-atom molecule, \(\sigma_{AM}^{\rm (break-up)}(E) \approx N \, \sigma_{AA}({N+1 \over 2N} E)\) (Eq. 13).
* For 3-body recombination at high energy, the hyperangular-averaged rate is \(K_3 \approx {384 \sqrt{3} \pi \hbar^3 \over m^2 E} \sigma_{AA}(\frac{3}{4}E)\) (Eq. 16).
* In the scaling region, the 3-body recombination rate scales as \(K_3 \approx {12288 \sqrt{3} \pi^2 \hbar^5 \over 3 m^3 E^2}\) (Eq. 17).
* For \(^4\)He, the scattering length is \(a \approx 100\) Å, the effective range is \(r_s \approx 7\) Å, and the dimer binding energy is \(E_D = 1.6\) mK.
* At \(E=10\) mK, the scaling approximation for \(K_3\) overestimates the accurate result of Suno et al. (2002) by a factor of 28.8, and the factorization approximation overestimates it by a factor of 23.7.
* The crossover between diagonal and cross terms in the 3-body recombination rate occurs near \(E = 20\) mK.

## Context

This work extends the factorization strategy from quantum chromodynamics (QCD), where asymptotic freedom allows separation of short-distance and long-distance physics, to nonrelativistic atoms with large scattering lengths. The authors show that for collision energies much larger than \(\hbar^2/ma^2\), the atom-atom scattering amplitude decreases with increasing momentum, analogous to asymptotic freedom in QCD. The factorization formulas for break-up and recombination processes significantly expand the list of processes that can be calculated accurately using present methods, which are otherwise limited to systems of at most 3 or 4 atoms. The results can be applied to \(^4\)He molecules (comparing favorably with the VCC-RIOS approximation of Bodo et al. (1996) for He\(_2\)-Xe break-up), to deuteron production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (as observed at RHIC), and to alkali atoms near a Feshbach resonance where the scattering length can be tuned to arbitrarily large values.
