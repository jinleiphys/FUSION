# Novel Pauli blocking method in quantum molecular dynamics type models

> In this work, we propose a novel method for calculating the occupation probability in the Pauli blocking of the quantum molecular dynamics type models. This method refines the description of the Pauli blocking ratio in the nuclear matter and that in the finite nucleus. The influence of the new Pauli blocking method on the heavy ion collisions observables, such as the charge distribution, the free neutron to proton yield ratios, and the extracted physical quantities, such as the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, are investigated. For the extracted in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, our results show that it will be enhanced 1.1$-$2.5 times than that with the conventional Pauli blocking method at the beam energy less than 150 MeV/u, which highlights the importance of a refined Pauli blocking method for developing an advanced transport model to describe complex heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new Pauli blocking method for quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) type models that reduces fluctuations in occupation probability by treating each nucleon as having multiple momentum states, leading to extracted in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections that are enhanced by 1.1 to 2.5 times compared to conventional methods at beam energies below 150 MeV/u.

## Method
The authors implement an ad hoc Pauli blocking method (PB(W*)) in the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, where the occupation probability for each nucleon is calculated by summing contributions from surrounding nucleons, with each nucleon represented by N momentum states sampled from the system's momentum space. The method is evaluated by comparing occupation probabilities and successful collision rates in nuclear matter at T=5 MeV and in finite nuclei against benchmark Fermi distributions, and then applied to heavy ion collision simulations to study charge distributions, free neutron-to-proton yield ratios, and extracted in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The variance of the occupation probability P(p') is reduced by about 70% using PB(W*) compared to the conventional PB(W) method in nuclear matter at T=5 MeV.
- The extracted in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections are enhanced by a factor of 1.1 to 2.5 times with the new Pauli blocking method compared to the conventional method at beam energies less than 150 MeV/u.
- Box simulations used N=1280 nucleons in a box of length L=20 fm, with cross section sigma_NN=40 mb, stop time t=140 fm/c, and N_event=200 events.

## Context
This work extends the Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP) findings (Xu 2016, Zhang 2018, Ono 2019, Colonna 2021, Wolter 2022) which identified Pauli blocking as a crucial theoretical challenge in transport models, particularly in QMD-type models where conventional methods underestimate blocking ratios compared to benchmark values. The new method addresses the underestimation of occupation probability that causes spurious nucleon-nucleon collisions, especially in the surface region of finite nuclei as identified by Chen (2021). This enables more reliable extraction of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and nuclear equation of state parameters from heavy ion collision data, which is essential for developing advanced transport models as called for in the 2023 long-range plan for nuclear science.
