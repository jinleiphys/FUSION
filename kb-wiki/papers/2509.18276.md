# Probing Quark Electromagnetic Properties via Entangled Quark Pairs in Fragmentation Hadrons at Lepton Colliders

> Electromagnetic dipole interactions of light quarks induce distinct spin correlations in quark pairs produced at lepton colliders, favoring entangled spin-triplet state aligned along the $\hat{z}$ axis or spin-singlet state. These correlations lead to unique $\cos(ϕ_1-ϕ_2)$ azimuthal asymmetries in inclusive $π^+π^-$-dihadron pair production and in back-to-back hadron pairs ($ππ,Kπ,KK$), which are absent in the SM. By analyzing Belle and BaBar data and using ratios of azimuthal asymmetries, we demonstrate that these measurements provide robust and significant constraints on light-quark dipole couplings, insensitive to nonperturbative fragmentation functions and free from contamination by other new physics effects. This approach offers a clean and novel probe of light-quark dipole interactions in collider experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that electromagnetic dipole interactions of light quarks (magnetic and electric dipole moments) produce distinct spin correlations in quark pairs at lepton colliders, leading to unique azimuthal asymmetries that are absent in the Standard Model. The hedge is that these asymmetries are only experimentally accessible through ratios of observables that cancel nonperturbative fragmentation function dependence, and the analysis assumes that systematic uncertainties cancel in these ratios.

## Method

The authors derive the spin correlation matrix for quark-antiquark pairs produced in electron-positron annihilation, incorporating Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) dipole operators at order 1/Lambda^4. They compute the differential cross sections for both inclusive dihadron pair production (using collinear interference dihadron fragmentation functions) and back-to-back hadron pair production (using transverse-momentum-dependent Collins functions), extracting the azimuthal asymmetries A_+ and A_- corresponding to cos(phi_1+phi_2) and cos(phi_1-phi_2) modulations. They then construct ratio observables R_diFF and R_Collins from existing Belle and BaBar data to isolate the dipole-induced spin correlations, performing a chi-squared analysis to constrain the dipole couplings.

## Key numbers

- The spin correlation matrix from dipole interactions yields C_ij = diag(1,1,-1) for CP-even (real) couplings and C_ij = diag(-1,-1,-1) for CP-odd (imaginary) couplings
- The ratio observable R_diFF is approximately equal to -(s v^2)/(2 pi alpha Lambda^4) * (sum over u,d of ([Re C_gamma^q]^2 - [Im C_gamma^q]^2))/(sum over u,d of Q_q^2), where s is the center-of-mass energy squared, v = 246 GeV, and alpha is the fine-structure constant
- Current Belle and BaBar data yield O(1) bounds on the up- and down-quark dipole couplings at the benchmark scale Lambda = 1 TeV
- Projected sensitivity from the Belle upgrade with integrated luminosity L = 50 ab^{-1} provides improved constraints (shown in Figure 2 but exact numerical values not given in text)

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum spin correlations in quark pairs at lepton colliders, previously explored by Cheng (2025) for Bell inequality tests, to probe new physics in quark electromagnetic dipole moments. It contrasts with conventional probes such as Drell-Yan production at the LHC, where dipole couplings are subleading in the SMEFT power counting and difficult to isolate from other new physics effects. The approach enables model-independent constraints on light-quark dipole interactions using existing Belle and BaBar data, with the advantage that the observables are insensitive to nonperturbative fragmentation functions and free from contamination by four-fermion operators.
