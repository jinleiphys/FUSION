# Nucleon Quark Distribution Functions from the Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> We present results for the nucleon's leading-twist spin-independent valence parton distribution functions obtained from a theoretical framework based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) of QCD that previously gave an excellent description of nucleon electromagnetic form factors. We employ the rainbow-ladder truncation of the DSEs and utilize nucleon bound state amplitudes from the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation, where the dominant scalar and axial-vector quark-quark correlations are included. This DSE framework is used to numerically evaluate the first 20 moments of the valence $u$ and $d$ quark distribution functions, from which the $x$-dependence of the distributions is found to be well constrained. We find good agreement with empirical parameterizations of experimental data and make the prediction that the $d/u$ ratio in the $x\to 1$ limit, invariant under scale evolution, takes the value $d/u \to 0.087 \pm 0.010$. We find that this ratio is rather sensitive to the strength of axial-vector diquark correlations. However, contrary to a naive expectation, our result for the $d/u$ ratio in the $x\to 1$ limit does not vanish when only scalar diquark correlations are present, although it is an order of magnitude smaller than our $d/u$ result that also includes axial-vector diquarks. The valence quark distribution results are set in a broader context via a simple pion cloud model estimate of sea-quark light-cone momenta and gluon light-cone momentum.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $d/u$ ratio in the $x\to 1$ limit, which is invariant under scale evolution, takes the value $d/u \to 0.087 \pm 0.010$, but this result is conditional on the inclusion of both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations; the ratio is sensitive to the strength of axial-vector diquark correlations, and contrary to naive expectation it does not vanish when only scalar diquark correlations are present, though it is an order of magnitude smaller.

## Method

The authors employed the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) of QCD and used nucleon bound state amplitudes from the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation, where the dominant scalar and axial-vector quark-quark (diquark) correlations are included. They numerically evaluated the first 20 moments of the valence $u$ and $d$ quark distribution functions from three dominant diagrammatic mechanisms (quark with diquark spectator, quarks inside a diquark, and quark exchanged between diquark correlations), then reconstructed the $x$-dependence via a chi-squared fit to the moments using a functional form $x\,q_v(x) = N\,x^a(1-x)^5(1 + b\,\sqrt{x} + c\,x)$. The model scale was determined by evolving the valence $u$-quark distribution via NLO DGLAP evolution to match NNPDF 3.0 NLO results at $\mu^2 = 5$ GeV$^2$, yielding $\mu_0^2 = 0.14$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- The $d/u$ ratio in the $x\to 1$ limit is predicted to be $0.087 \pm 0.010$ when both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations are included.
- With only scalar diquark correlations, the $d/u$ ratio in the $x\to 1$ limit is $0.011 \pm 0.003$.
- The model scale determined by fitting to NNPDF 3.0 NLO results at $\mu^2 = 5$ GeV$^2$ is $\mu_0^2 = 0.14$ GeV$^2$.
- The scalar diquark correlation dominates the nucleon structure by contributing 70% of the strength.
- The large-$x$ behavior of the PDFs is found to be $(1-x)^5$, not the $(1-x)^3$ expected from the Drell-Yan-West relation.

## Context

This work extends the same DSE framework previously used by Cloet (2008) to successfully describe nucleon electromagnetic form factors over the range $0 \leqslant Q^2 \lesssim 20$ GeV$^2$, now applied to nucleon valence parton distribution functions. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD techniques, which are presently limited to the first few moments of PDFs (Lin 2017) and face challenges for $x \lesssim 0.2$ and $x \gtrsim 0.8$. The results enable a unified description of both nucleon form factors and PDFs from a single theoretical framework, and the prediction for the $d/u$ ratio in the $x\to 1$ limit provides a testable benchmark for future experiments and for other nonperturbative approaches such as the recently proposed spacelike correlator methods (Ji 2013, Radyushkin 2017).
