# Neutrino-nucleus interaction rates at a low-energy beta-beam facility

> We compute the neutrino detection rates to be expected at a low-energy beta-beam facility. We consider various nuclei as neutrino detectors and compare the case of a small versus large storage ring.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a low-energy beta-beam facility can achieve significant neutrino-nucleus interaction rates, but the rates depend strongly on the storage ring geometry (small vs. large ring) and the boost factor \(\gamma\), with the small ring configuration being preferred for a close detector.

## Method

The authors compute charged-current neutrino-nucleus interaction rates by integrating the boosted neutrino flux from beta-decaying ions (\({}^{6}\)He for antineutrinos, \({}^{18}\)Ne for neutrinos) over the detector volume and storage ring geometry. They consider two storage ring configurations: a small ring (150 m straight sections, 450 m total length) and a large ring (2.5 km straight sections, 7 km total length), with detectors placed 10 m from the ring. The rates are calculated for four target nuclei (deuteron, oxygen, iron, lead) at two boost factors (\(\gamma = 7\) and \(\gamma = 14\)), using cross-sections from the literature.

## Key numbers

- For \(\gamma = 7\) with the small ring and small detector (R=1.5 m, h=4.5 m): \(\nu + \text{D}\) yields 194 events/year; \(\bar{\nu} + \text{D}\) yields 2494 events/year; \(\nu + ^{208}\text{Pb}\) yields 7598 events/year.
- For \(\gamma = 14\) with the small ring and small detector: \(\nu + \text{D}\) yields 2363 events/year; \(\bar{\nu} + \text{D}\) yields 25779 events/year; \(\nu + ^{208}\text{Pb}\) yields 103707 events/year.
- The flux-averaged cross section in the forward direction \(\langle\sigma\rangle_\gamma\) for \(\nu + \text{D}\) at \(\gamma = 7\) is \(36.30 \times 10^{-42}~\text{cm}^2\); at \(\gamma = 14\) it is \(184.47 \times 10^{-42}~\text{cm}^2\).
- The small ring has \(L_{SR} = 450~\text{m}\) and \(D_{SR} = 150~\text{m}\); the large ring has \(L_{LR} = 7~\text{km}\) and \(D_{LR} = 2.5~\text{km}\).
- The parent ion injection rates are \(g_{\bar{\nu}} = 10^{13}~\bar{\nu}/\text{s}\) (from \({}^{6}\)He) and \(g_{\nu} = 5 \times 10^{11}~\nu/\text{s}\) (from \({}^{18}\)Ne).

## Context

This work extends the beta-beam concept of Zucchelli (2002) to low-energy neutrinos, as proposed by the authors in a prior study (2003), and compares the achievable rates with those from conventional neutrino facilities like the proposed ORLAND project. The results enable systematic studies of neutrino-nucleus interactions in the 10-100 MeV energy range, which are needed for interpreting supernova neutrino signals and for nucleosynthesis calculations, and complement measurements from existing experiments on deuteron (deut), carbon (expc12), and iron (iron).
