# Evolution of Hot, Dissipative Quark Matter in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Non-ideal fluid dynamics with cylindrical symmetry in transverse direction and longitudinal scaling flow is employed to simulate the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies. The dynamical expansion is studied as a function of initial energy density and initial time. A causal theory of dissipative fluid dynamics is used instead of the standard theories which are acausal. We compute the parton momentum spectra and HBT radii from two-particle correlation functions. We find that, in non-ideal fluid dynamics, the reduction of the longitudinal pressure due to viscous effects leads to an increase of transverse flow and a decrease of the ratio $R_{out}/R_{side}$ as compared to the ideal fluid approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using causal (second-order) relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies leads to an increase in transverse flow and a decrease in the ratio \(R_{out}/R_{side}\) compared to the ideal fluid approximation, but only under the specific conditions of cylindrical symmetry with longitudinal scaling flow and an ultrarelativistic ideal gas equation of state where bulk viscosity vanishes and heat flow is neglected.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled conservation equations and the Muller-Israel-Stewart second-order transport equations for shear stress in a Bjorken cylinder geometry with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry in the transverse direction, using the LCPFCT numerical algorithm. They assumed a net baryon-free system of massless u, d, s quarks and gluons with an equation of state \(p = \epsilon/3\), initialized the shear stress tensor components to \(\tau^{rr} = \tau^{\phi\phi} = \epsilon(r)/18\) and \(\tau^{zz} = -\epsilon(r)/9\), and computed parton momentum spectra and HBT radii from two-particle correlation functions along freeze-out isotherms using the Cooper-Frye prescription with dissipative corrections to the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density \(\epsilon_0 = 25\) GeV/fm\(^{-3}\) for the first scenario, with initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.1 R_0\) where \(R_0 = 1.14 A^{1/3}\) fm for Au+Au collisions (\(A=197\))
- Initial energy density \(\epsilon_0 = 100\) GeV fm\(^{-3}\) for the second scenario, with initial time \(\tau_0 = 1/(3T_0)\)
- Shear viscosity coefficient \(\eta = b T^3\) with \(b = (1 + 1.70 N_f) \times 0.342 / [(1+N_f/6) \alpha_s^2 \ln(\alpha_s^{-1})]\) for \(N_f = 3\) quark flavors and strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.5\)
- Relaxation coefficient \(\beta_2 = 3/(4p)\) for massless particles
- Transition temperature \(T_c \sim 160\) MeV from lattice QCD calculations

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional Bjorken scaling flow investigation of Muronga (2004) to a more realistic three-dimensional geometry with transverse expansion, and is the first to solve the second-order causal equations of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for such a geometry, in contrast to previous attempts that used first-order acausal equations (Eckart and Landau theories). The results enable future studies of dissipative effects in heavy-ion collisions that include hadronization and comparison with experimental data from RHIC, though the authors note that transport coefficients in the mixed phase remain unknown.
