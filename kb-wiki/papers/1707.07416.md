# Octupole deformation in the nuclear chart based on the 3D Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS model

> We have performed a systematic study of the ground state for even-even 1,002 nuclei in which 28 octupole-deformed nuclei are found. The interplay between the spatial deformation and the pairing correlation, plays important roles in nuclear structure. Our model is based on the Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS model represented in the three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate space which can describe any kind of nuclear shape. The quadrupole and octupole deformed nuclei appear in the mass region with characteristic neutron and proton numbers which are consistent with previous studies. In our results, there appear only pear shape ($β_{30}$) in the octupole deformed nuclei. We investigate the potential energy surfaces as functions of the octupole deformations ($β_{3m}, m = 0, 1, 2, 3$), which tells us that. $^{220}$Rn has also local minima in the $β_{31}$ and $β_{32}$ potential energy surfaces.

## Key claim

A systematic survey of 1,002 even-even nuclei using a fully three-dimensional Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS model finds 28 octupole-deformed nuclei, but only the pear-shaped (β₃₀) deformation appears in the ground state; the abstract does not emphasize that non-axial octupole deformations (β₃₁, β₃₂, β₃₃) are absent in the ground states of all nuclei studied, with only ²²⁰Rn showing local minima in β₃₁ and β₃₂ potential energy surfaces.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations using the SkM* parameter set, with the single-particle wave functions represented on a three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate mesh and the pairing functional treated as a constant monopole model. The ground states of 1,002 even-even nuclei (Z=6–92, between N=Z and approximately N=2Z) were computed using the imaginary-time method, and quadrupole (β₂, γ) and octupole (β₃ₘ for m=0,1,2,3) deformation parameters were extracted. Constrained HF+BCS calculations were performed for ¹⁴⁴Ba and ²²⁰Rn to map potential energy surfaces as functions of each octupole deformation mode.

## Key numbers

- 28 octupole-deformed nuclei were identified among the 1,002 even-even nuclei surveyed.
- 54% of even-even nuclei are quadrupole deformed (β₂ ≥ 0.05); among these, 70% are prolate, 12% oblate, and 18% triaxial.
- Octupole-deformed nuclei appear in two mass regions: N=84–88 with Z=54–70, and N=130–136 with Z=84–92.
- For ¹⁴⁴Ba, the octupole correlation energy at the β₃₀ minimum is approximately 200 keV.
- For ²²⁰Rn, the octupole correlation energy at the β₃₀ minimum is approximately 1.5 MeV.
- ²²⁰Rn shows local minima in the β₃₁ and β₃₂ potential energy surfaces, with correlation energies on the order of a few hundred keV.
- Selected deformation parameters from the table: ¹⁴⁴Ba has β₃=0.110, β₂=0.16; ²²⁰Rn has β₃=0.160, β₂=0.13; ²²⁴Ra has β₃=0.168, β₂=0.18; ²²⁶U has β₃=0.183, β₂=0.18.

## Context

This work extends systematic mean-field studies of nuclear deformation, consistent with the earlier survey by Stoitsov (2003) for quadrupole shapes and the review by Butler and Nazarewicz (1996) for octupole regions, but uses a fully three-dimensional representation that allows non-axial octupole shapes. The results show fewer octupole-deformed nuclei than the macroscopic-microscopic approach of Moller (2008), which the authors attribute to a stronger pairing interaction in their model. The calculated potential energy surfaces for ¹⁴⁴Ba and ²²⁰Rn provide input for reaction studies relevant to the ImPACT transmutation program, and the sensitivity of octupole deformation to single-particle level spacing with Δl=3 suggests that experimental octupole data can help constrain energy density functionals.
