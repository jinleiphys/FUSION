# A renormalization method for three-boson system with a triboson field

> A renormalization method that introduces an auxiliary field to represent a three-body bound state is studied in a three-boson system with a triboson field. A cutoff dependence in the three-boson system emerges as a limit cycle, and the cyclic singularity is renormalized by employing two methods: a standard method and the auxiliary field method. For each method, different sets of diagrams are involved for renormalization, and thus we numerically study three quantities: counter term for renormalization, scattering length of $s$-wave boson-diboson scattering, and normalized wavefunction. We confirm that the two methods would lead to the same result.

# A renormalization method for three-boson system with a triboson field

## Key claim

The auxiliary field renormalization method (AM), which introduces a triboson field to represent a three-body bound state, yields the same renormalized coupling constant and scattering length as the standard renormalization method (SM) for a three-boson system exhibiting a limit cycle, despite involving different sets of diagrams for renormalization. However, the normalized wavefunctions obtained from the two methods differ by an overall factor of about 2.36, though they share the same momentum dependence.

## Method

The author studies a system of three neutral scalar bosons of equal mass with diboson and triboson fields at leading order in pionless effective field theory. Two renormalization procedures are compared: the standard method (SM), which solves the homogeneous integral equation for the scattering amplitude and fits the three-body contact interaction using Newton's method, and the auxiliary field method (AM), which determines the triboson field parameter by fixing the pole position of the three-body binding energy in the dressed triboson propagator. Numerical calculations are performed for the counter term, s-wave boson-diboson scattering length, and normalized wavefunctions as functions of a sharp momentum cutoff Λ.

## Key numbers

- Boson mass: m = 940 MeV; two-body binding energy: B₂ = 2.22 MeV; three-body binding energy: B₃ = 8.48 MeV; diboson binding momentum: γ_d ≈ 45.7 MeV
- The limit cycle parameter s₀ = 1.0064 (dimensionless), obtained from the asymptotic integral equation
- Second binding energy in the amplitude t: B₃⁽²⁾ ≈ 1.2 GeV, appearing around Λ ≈ 1300 MeV
- First binding energy in a and Y_t for AM starts appearing around Λ ≈ 15 MeV, with B₃⁽¹⁾ just above B₂ = 2.22 MeV at the starting point
- Second binding energy in a and Y_t for AM appears around Λ ≈ 480 MeV; at that point B₃⁽¹⁾ ≈ 17.6 MeV
- Scattering length a₃ converges to a₃ ≈ -10 fm for Λ > 300 MeV
- Normalized wavefunction ratio: φ_S(p) ≈ 2.36 φ_A(p)

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary field renormalization method introduced by Hagen, Hammer, and Platter (2013) for studying electric form factors of two-neutron halo nuclei, applying it to a simpler three-boson system to test the method itself. The limit cycle behavior was originally discovered in three-boson systems by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) and in the triton channel by the same authors (2000). The results confirm that the auxiliary field method can reproduce the standard renormalization results for scattering observables, but the wavefunction normalization difference requires further attention when computing physical observables such as form factors.
