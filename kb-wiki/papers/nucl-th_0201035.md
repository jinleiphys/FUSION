# Relativistic vs. Non-relativistic Nuclear Models

> Both the relativistic and non-relativistic model explain very well low-energy nuclear phenomena, but in a physically different way from each other. There seems to be no low-energy phenomenon to answer which model is more reasonable. In order to explore a difference between two models, high momentum transfer phenomena are investigated. First it is shown that the neutron spin-orbit charge density in the relativistic model reproduces very well experimental data on elastic electron scattering which have not been explained in the non-relativistic model. Next it is predicted that the relativistic Coulomb sum value is strongly quenched, compared with the non-relativistic one. This quenching is owing to the anti-nucleon degrees of freedom, which make the nucleon size larger and equivalently the vector-meson mass smaller in nuclear medium. New experiment on the Coulomb sum values around the momentum transfer 1GeV is expected to distinguish the relativistic from the non-relativistic model.

## Key claim

The relativistic model predicts a strong quenching of the Coulomb sum value at high momentum transfer (around 1 GeV) due to anti-nucleon degrees of freedom, which is not present in non-relativistic models; this quenching provides a potential experimental test to distinguish between the two frameworks, whereas low-energy phenomena cannot differentiate them.

## Method

The authors compared relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear models by calculating elastic electron scattering cross sections for 40Ca and 48Ca using phase-shift analyses with nuclear charge densities derived from the time-component of the relativistic four-current. They also computed the Coulomb sum values for quasielastic scattering within the relativistic RPA framework, including and excluding nucleon-antinucleon excitations, and estimated the modification of the proton radius in nuclear medium due to the effective nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the effective proton radius in nuclear medium to the free-space radius is (⟨r_p²⟩*/⟨r_p²⟩)^(1/2) = 1.146, for an effective nucleon mass M* = 0.731 M.
- The effective omega-meson mass in nuclear medium is m*_v = 0.696 m_v.
- The quenching of the Coulomb sum values becomes significant above momentum transfer 0.5 GeV.
- The elastic electron scattering data were taken at incident energy 249.5 MeV for 40Ca and 48Ca.
- The neutron anomalous magnetic moments are μ_p = 1.793 and μ_n = -1.913.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between relativistic models (using the NL-SH parameter set) and non-relativistic models (using Skyrme forces such as SLy4) from low-energy phenomena, where both agree, to high-momentum transfer processes. It addresses a long-standing failure of non-relativistic models to reproduce the difference in elastic electron scattering cross sections between 40Ca and 48Ca, as observed in experiments from Saclay and SLAC. The prediction of quenched Coulomb sum values motivates new experiments around 1 GeV momentum transfer to test whether the relativistic model's anti-nucleon degrees of freedom are physically realistic.
