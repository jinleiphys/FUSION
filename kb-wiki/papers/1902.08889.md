# Charm-Baryon Production in Proton-Proton Collisions

> Recent measurements of charm-baryon production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC have found a surprisingly large yield relative to those of $D$-mesons. We propose that this observation can be explained by the statistical hadronization model (SHM), by employing a largely augmented set of charm-baryon states beyond the current listings of the particle data group. We estimate the additional states using guidance from the relativistic quark model and from lattice QCD. Using charm- and strange-quark fugacity factors to account for the well-known suppression of heavy flavor in elementary collisions, we compute the yields and spectra of $D$, $D_s$ and $Λ_c$ hadrons in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5$\,TeV. Our main finding is that the enhanced feeddown from excited charm baryons can account for the $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio measured by ALICE at midrapidity, with some caveat for the forward-rapidity LHCb data. Furthermore, assuming independent fragmentation of charm quarks but with the hadronic ratios fixed by the SHM, the measured transverse-momentum ($p_T$) spectra of $D$-mesons and $Λ_c$ can also be described; in particular, the low-$p_T$ enhancement in the observed $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio is attributed to the enhanced feeddown from "missing" charm-baryon states. We comment on the implications of these findings for measurements of $D_s$ and $Λ_c$ in heavy-ion collisions.

# Charm-Baryon Production in Proton-Proton Collisions

## Key claim
The observed enhancement of the Λc/D0 ratio in pp collisions at LHC energies can be explained by the statistical hadronization model (SHM) only if the underlying charm-baryon spectrum is augmented with a large set of "missing" excited states beyond those currently listed by the Particle Data Group, with the caveat that the forward-rapidity LHCb data are better reproduced by the PDG-only scenario.

## Method
The authors constructed a statistical hadronization model using two different inputs for the charm-hadron spectrum: one using only states listed by the Particle Data Group (PDG), and another using an augmented set of charm-baryon states predicted by the relativistic quark model (RQM) of Ebert (2011), adding 18 Λc, 42 Σc, 62 Ξc, and 34 Ωc states up to 3.5 GeV. They computed thermal densities at hadronization temperatures of 160 and 170 MeV, applied charm- and strange-quark fugacity factors, and then fragmented a FONLL charm-quark pT spectrum using fragmentation functions whose normalizations were fixed by the thermal densities, followed by decay simulations of excited states into ground-state hadrons.

## Key numbers
- Λc+/D0 ratio in the RQM scenario at TH=170 MeV: 0.5696, compared to 0.2851 in the PDG scenario at the same temperature.
- Λc+/D0 ratio in the PDG scenario at TH=160 MeV: 0.2404, close to the previously reported SHM value of 0.22.
- Thermal density of Λc+ in the RQM scenario at TH=170 MeV: 0.6613 × 10−4 fm−3, versus 0.3310 × 10−4 fm−3 in the PDG scenario.
- Additional RQM states: 18 Λc, 42 Σc, 62 Ξc, and 34 Ωc states up to a mass of 3.5 GeV.
- Fitted total charm cross section dσc¯c/dy in the RQM scenario at √s=5.02 TeV: 0.855 mb; in the PDG scenario: 1.0 mb.
- Fitted r-parameters for fragmentation: rD0=0.1 and rΛc+=0.16.
- Strangeness suppression factor γs=0.6 applied to Ds mesons and Ξc baryons.

## Context
This work extends the statistical hadronization model previously applied to light- and strange-hadron production by Braun-Munzinger (2003) and Andronic (2017), and to charm-meson ratios by Andronic (2007, 2009). It addresses the "charm-baryon puzzle" arising from ALICE measurements at √s=7 TeV (Acharya, 2017) showing Λc/D0 ≈ 0.54, much larger than the SHM prediction of ~0.22 based on PDG states. The need for additional charm-baryon states is supported by lattice-QCD computations of the vacuum spectrum by Padmanath (2014) and of thermal charm susceptibilities by Bazavov (2014). The results enable improved equilibrium limits for transport calculations of charm hadrons in heavy-ion collisions and provide predictions for measurements of Ds, Σc, Ξc, and bottom-sector hadrons.
