# 3D real-space calculations of continuum response

> We present linear response theories in the continuum capable of describing continuum spectra and dynamical correlations of finite systems with no spatial symmetry. Our formulation is essentially the same as the continuum random-phase approximation (RPA) but suitable for uniform grid representation in the three-dimensional (3D) Cartesian coordinate. Effects of the continuum are taken into account by solving equations iteratively with a retarded Green's function. The method is applied to photoabsorption spectra in small molecules (acetylene and ethylene) and inelastic electron scattering from a deformed nucleus 12C.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for calculating continuum response in finite systems with no spatial symmetry, based on a modified Sternheimer approach that solves linear response equations iteratively with a retarded Green's function; the method is demonstrated for both nuclear and molecular systems, but the authors note that the main difficulty is the heavy computational task, especially for calculations of response near threshold energies.

## Method

The method reformulates the continuum random-phase approximation (RPA) into a modified Sternheimer equation, converting the integral over the Green's function into a differential equation with an outgoing boundary condition. The single-particle potential is split into a long-range spherical part and a short-range non-spherical part, and the Green's function action is computed iteratively using three nested loops: solving the RPA equation, calculating actions of the full Green's function, and evaluating boundary values of the spherical Green's function. The approach is applied to the BKN Skyrme interaction for nuclei and to time-dependent local density approximation (TDLDA) with a gradient-corrected exchange-correlation potential for molecules.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, single-particle energies are -28.4 MeV for the 0s orbital and -16.7 MeV for the 0p orbital, calculated in a spherical box of radius R=8 fm with 0.1 fm meshes.
- For 12C, the model space is a 3D spherical box of radius R=8 fm with square meshes of Δx=Δy=Δz=1 fm; the HF ground state has an oblate shape with axis ratio approximately three to two.
- For acetylene, the static dipole polarizabilities from the IPA calculation are α∥=10.7 Å³ and α⊥=3.87 Å³; dynamical screening reduces these to 4.79 Å³ and 2.77 Å³, respectively, compared to experimental values α∥=4.73 Å³ and α⊥=2.87 Å³.
- For ethylene, the photoabsorption calculation shows a peak at ω=7.6 eV (response to dipole field parallel to the C-C axis), a peak at 9.8 eV, a small peak at 11.4 eV, and a prominent peak at 12.4 eV; the calculated spectra show about 75% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule for valence electrons.
- The numerical calculation of photoabsorption spectra for ethylene takes about 30 minutes at a single photon energy ω on a single CPU of a Fujitsu VPP700E supercomputer, and about 1.5 hours to calculate a single energy point for all three dipole directions.

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA method, previously limited to spherical systems by Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) and applied to giant resonances in spherical nuclei by van Giai and Sagawa (1981) and Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1996), to systems with no spatial symmetry. The method also extends the time-dependent local density approximation approach of Zangwill and Soven (1980) for photoabsorption in rare gases to molecules and deformed nuclei. The results enable unified treatment of response functions below, near, and above the separation energy for many-particle systems, and the authors suggest application to drip-line nuclei as a next step.
