# The cumulants of the baryon number from central Au+Au collision at $E_{lab}$= 1.23 GeV$/$nucleon reveal the nuclear mean-field potentials

> Fluctuations of the baryon number in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are a promising observable to explore the structure of the QCD phase diagram. The cumulant ratios in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies ($\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}} < 7$ GeV) have not been studied to date. We investigate the effects of mean field potential and clustering on the cumulant ratios of baryon and proton number distributions in Au+Au collisions at beam energy of 1.23 GeV$/$nucleon as measured by the HADES Collaboration at GSI. Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) and the JAM model are used to calculate the cumulants with different mean field potentials. It is found that the cumulant ratios are strongly time dependent. At the early stage, the effects of the potentials on the fluctuations of the particle multiplicity in momentum space are relatively weak. The mean fields enhance the fluctuations during the expansion stage, especially for small rapidity acceptance windows. The enhancement of cumulant ratios for free protons is strongly suppressed as compared to that for all baryons. The mean field potentials and the clustering play an important role for the measured cumulant ratios at intermediate energy.

## Key claim

The cumulant ratios of baryon number in central Au+Au collisions at 1.23 GeV/nucleon are strongly enhanced by nuclear mean-field potentials during the expansion stage, but this enhancement is strongly suppressed for free protons compared to all baryons, and the effect depends critically on the momentum-dependent component of the potential rather than the nuclear incompressibility.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model and the JAM model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of 1.23 GeV/nucleon, with 2 million events per potential setting. They implemented three different nuclear mean-field potentials: soft momentum-dependent (SM, K0=200 MeV), hard momentum-dependent (HM, K0=380 MeV), and hard momentum-independent (H, K0=300 MeV), plus a cascade mode without mean field. An isospin-dependent minimum spanning tree method (with distance cutoffs of 2.8 fm for proton-proton and 3.8 fm for neutron-proton, and momentum cutoff 0.25 GeV/c) was used to identify nuclear clusters at the end of the 100 fm/c simulation.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 1.23 GeV/nucleon (lab frame) for Au+Au collisions
- Nuclear incompressibility values: SM = 200 MeV, H = 300 MeV, HM = 380 MeV
- Total baryon number N = 394 used for binomial baseline calculations
- Cluster recognition parameters: relative distance < 2.8 fm (proton-proton) or < 3.8 fm (neutron-proton), relative momentum < 0.25 GeV/c
- Simulation time: 100 fm/c total propagation, with early stage defined as t ≤ 15 fm/c
- For small rapidity windows Δy0 ≈ 0.4, C2/C1, C3/C1, and Sσ calculated with mean field potentials are larger than the binomial baseline, while cascade mode values are smaller
- In central Au+Au at 1.23 GeV/nucleon, about 40% of protons are bound into clusters (citing FOPI collaboration results)
- The SM potential produces approximately 5 times more clusters than the cascade simulation

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon number fluctuations to intermediate energies (√sNN < 7 GeV), which had not been previously investigated, building on the HADES experiment measurements at GSI and the Beam-Energy Scan program at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2014). The results contradict the assumption that cumulant ratios are insensitive to nuclear potentials at these energies, as found in earlier JAM calculations at √sNN = 5 GeV by He et al. (2016). The study enables interpretation of HADES data and provides guidance for the CBM experiment at FAIR, showing that both mean-field potentials and cluster formation (previously studied in a simplified way by Feckova et al., 2015) must be accounted for when using net-proton cumulants as a proxy for net-baryon cumulants.
