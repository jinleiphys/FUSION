# Proton radioactivity with a Yukawa effective interaction

> The half lives of proton radioactivity of proton emitters are investigated theoretically. Proton-nucleus interaction potentials are obtained by folding the densities of the daughter nuclei with a finite range effective nucleon-nucleon interaction having Yukawa form. The Wood-Saxon density distributions for the nuclei used in calculating the nuclear as well as the Coulomb interaction potentials are predictions of the interaction. The quantum mechanical tunneling probability is calculated within the WKB framework. These calculations provide reasonable estimates for the observed proton radioactivity lifetimes. The effects of neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron rich asymmetric matter as well as the nuclear matter incompressibility on the decay probability are investigated.

# Proton radioactivity with a Yukawa effective interaction

## Key claim

The half-lives of proton radioactivity can be reasonably estimated using proton-nucleus interaction potentials obtained by folding daughter nucleus densities with a finite-range Yukawa effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (YENI) and calculating tunneling probabilities within the WKB framework, though the agreement with experimental data is comparable to that of the DDM3Y model and the calculated results show little sensitivity to the neutron-proton effective mass splitting in asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors constructed proton-nucleus interaction potentials by folding Wood-Saxon density distributions of daughter nuclei with a finite-range effective interaction containing a single Yukawa term (YENI), where the density distributions themselves were predicted by minimizing the total energy of each nucleus with respect to the Wood-Saxon parameters. The quantum mechanical tunneling probability was calculated using the improved WKB framework, and the half-lives were obtained from the barrier penetrability, assault frequency, and zero-point vibration energy, with the spectroscopic factor set to unity. The exchange part of the nuclear potential was evaluated self-consistently using the density matrix expansion with modified Fermi momenta that account for surface corrections up to second order.

## Key numbers

- For the EOS with gamma=1/2 and exchange strength parameter epsilon_ex^l = (epsilon_ex^l + epsilon_ex^ul)/3, the parameter t_0 was determined to be 481.86 MeV fm^3 by fitting to the binding energy of Ca-40, predicting a charge radius of 3.49 fm for Ca-40.
- The nuclear matter incompressibility values considered were 220, 240, and 253 MeV, corresponding to gamma = 1/3, 1/2, and 2/3 respectively.
- The slope parameter L = 3 E_s'(rho_0) was predicted to lie within the range obtained from simultaneous analysis of neutron skin thickness and neutron star properties, with E_s'(rho_0) = 21.51 MeV for gamma=1/2 and epsilon_ex^l = (epsilon_ex^l + epsilon_ex^ul)/3 at a symmetry energy of 30 MeV.
- For Cs-113 (Q = 0.978 MeV, l=2), the calculated log10(T/s) was -5.51(4) compared to the measured value of -4.777, with turning points R_a = 6.78 fm and R_b = 73.45 fm.
- For Tm-145 (Q = 1.753 MeV, l=5), the calculated log10(T/s) was -5.25(7) compared to the measured value of -5.409, with turning points R_a = 6.70 fm and R_b = 56.27 fm.
- For Bi-185 (Q = 1.624 MeV, l=0), the calculated log10(T/s) was -5.44(14) compared to the measured value of -4.229, with turning points R_a = 7.84 fm and R_b = 65.71 fm.
- Binding energies and charge radii for closed-shell nuclei were reproduced within 0.5% of experimental values for the proton radioactive nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the folding model approach to proton radioactivity using a Yukawa effective interaction (YENI), building on previous studies that employed the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction (Basu et al., 2005; Mahata et al., 2007), the JLM effective interaction (Mahata et al., 2007), the unified fission model (Basu, 2005), and the generalized liquid drop model (Duarte et al., 2009). The results are compared directly with DDM3Y calculations and the generalized liquid drop model, showing good agreement with experimental data and performance comparable to DDM3Y. The study also examines the effects of neutron-proton effective mass splitting and nuclear matter incompressibility on decay probabilities, finding that the p-N interaction potential is largely insensitive to the effective mass splitting, while lower incompressibility produces slightly extended tails in the nuclear potential.
