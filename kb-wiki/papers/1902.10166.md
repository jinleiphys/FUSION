# Electromagnetic multipole moments of baryons

> We calculate the charge quadrupole and magnetic octupole moments of baryons using a group theoretical approach based on broken SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry. The latter is an approximate symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian which becomes exact in the large color N_c limit. Spin-flavor symmetry breaking is induced by one-, two-, and three-quark terms in the electromagnetic current operator. Two- and three-quark currents provide the leading contributions for higher multipole moments, despite being of higher order in an 1/N_c expansion. Our formalism leads to relations between N --> N* transition multipole moments and nucleon ground state properties. We compare our results to experimental quadrupole and octupole transition moments extracted from measured helicity amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that higher electromagnetic multipole moments of baryons (charge quadrupole and magnetic octupole) are dominated by two- and three-quark currents rather than one-quark currents, despite being higher order in a 1/N_c expansion, and that broken SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry provides relations connecting transition multipole moments to nucleon ground state properties such as the neutron charge radius.

## Method

The author employs a group-theoretical approach based on broken SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry, using the general parameterization (GP) method to construct spin-flavor operators for electromagnetic multipoles as sums of one-, two-, and three-quark operators transforming according to the 35, 405, and 2695 dimensional representations of SU(6). The analysis includes the 1/N_c expansion of QCD to classify symmetry breaking operators, and current algebra to calculate transition multipole moments for excited states with nonzero orbital angular momentum, specifically the N*(1680) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The relation Q_{p → Δ^+} = (1/√2) r_n^2 connects the p → Δ^+ transition quadrupole moment to the neutron charge radius r_n^2 (Eq. qmnr)
- The generalized relation including 1/N_c corrections gives Q_{N → Δ} = (1/√2) r_n^2 × (N_c/(N_c+3) × √((N_c+5)/(N_c-1))), which differs from the simpler relation by less than 1.2% for arbitrary N_c (Eq. ffrel2statlargeN)
- The relation G_{C2}^{p → Δ^+}(Q^2) = -(3√2/Q^2) G_C^n(Q^2) connects the charge quadrupole transition form factor to the elastic neutron charge form factor (Eq. C2C0rel)

## Context

This work extends earlier quark model predictions by Buchmann, Henley, and others (Buchmann et al. 1997) that connected the p → Δ^+ transition quadrupole moment to the neutron charge radius, and generalizes these relations using broken SU(6) symmetry and the 1/N_c expansion method developed by Witten (1979) and applied to baryon charge radii and quadrupole moments by Hester and Lebed (2002) and Lebed (2000). The formalism enables extraction of intrinsic quadrupole and octupole moments of the nucleon ground state from measurable transition moments, with planned measurements of the Ω^- quadrupole moment at FAIR in Darmstadt providing future experimental tests.
