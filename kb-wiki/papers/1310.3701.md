# Medium modifications of photon-tagged jet fragmentation function in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Based on a multiphase transport model, medium modifications of prompt photon-tagged jet fragmentation function are investigated by comparing prompt photon-tagged hadron azimuthal correlation in Au+Au collisions (0-40\%) with that in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. The measured modification factor, $I_{AA}$, increases with the increasing integration range of the away side, which reveals a medium-modified jet shape in which the medium enhancement of soft particles is preferentially located far away from the jet axis. The $I_{AA}$ largely results from strong interactions between jets and partonic matter. However, both hadronization of coalescence and hadronic rescatterings play certain roles to modify the $I_{AA}$. These behaviors reflect a dynamical evolution of modifications of the prompt photon-tagged jet fragmentation function in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The medium modification factor \(I_{AA}\) for prompt photon-tagged jet fragmentation functions increases with increasing integration range of the away side, revealing that the medium enhancement of soft particles is preferentially located far from the jet axis; however, the AMPT model results show a much smaller angular dependence than the experimental data, and the hadronization of coalescence weakens the modifications while hadronic rescatterings strengthen them.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string-melting scenario to simulate p+p and Au+Au collisions (0-40% centrality) at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}\) = 200 GeV. A photon jet with \(p_{T}^{\gamma} \sim\) 4 GeV/\(c\) is triggered via the HIJING jet-triggering technique, and three hard photon-jet production processes are included. The model evolves through initial conditions, parton cascade via Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) with a 10 mb partonic cross section for Au+Au and zero for p+p, hadronization via a simple coalescence model, and hadronic rescatterings via a relativistic transport (ART) model. Constant backgrounds are removed using the zero-yield-at-minimum (ZYAM) method.

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses prompt photons with \(5 < p_{T}^{\gamma} < 9\) GeV/\(c\) and associated hadrons with \(0.5 < p_{T}^{h} < 7\) GeV/\(c\) within mid-rapidity \(|y| < 0.35\) at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}\) = 200 GeV.
- The partonic interaction cross section used for 0-40% centrality Au+Au collisions is 10 mb, while zero cross section is used for p+p collisions.
- The \(I_{AA}\) is shown for three away-side integration ranges: full away side (\(|\Delta\phi-\pi| < \pi/2\)), and restricted ranges (\(|\Delta\phi-\pi| < \pi/3\) and \(|\Delta\phi-\pi| < \pi/6\)).
- The initial \(I_{AA}\) is close to unity, indicating less modification from nuclear effects in the initial state.

## Context

This work extends the PHENIX measurement of the jet fragmentation function via direct photon-hadron azimuthal correlations (Adare 2012), which showed suppression at low \(\xi = \ln(1/z)\) and enhancement at high \(\xi\) consistent with the Borghini and Weidemann modified leading log approximation (Borghini 2005) and the YaJEM model (Renk 2011). The study uses the AMPT model to provide a dynamical evolution picture of the modifications across partonic and hadronic stages, and it enables future studies of medium modifications for different initial \(\gamma\)-jet-production configurations using \(\gamma\)-jet \(p_{T}\) imbalance at RHIC and LHC energies.
