# Accessing the speed of sound in relativistic ultracentral nucleus-nucleus collisions using the mean transverse momentum

> It has been argued that the speed of sound of the strong interaction at high temperature can be measured using the variation of the mean transverse momentum with the particle multiplicity in ultracentral heavy-ion collisions. We test this correspondence by running hydrodynamic simulations at zero impact parameter with several equations of state, at several colliding energies from 0.2 TeV to 15 TeV per nucleon pair. The correspondence is found to be precise and robust for a smooth, boost-invariant fluid and an ideal detector. We discuss the differences between this ideal setup and an actual experiment. We conclude that the extraction of the speed of sound from data is reliable, and that the main uncertainty comes from our poor knowledge of the distribution of density fluctuations at the early stages of the collision.

## Key claim

The correspondence between the speed of sound squared and the logarithmic derivative of mean transverse momentum with respect to charged multiplicity (Equation 2) is precise and robust for a smooth, boost-invariant fluid with an ideal detector, but the main uncertainty in extracting the speed of sound from actual experimental data comes from poor knowledge of the distribution of density fluctuations at the early stages of the collision.

## Method

The authors ran hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with smooth initial density profiles at zero impact parameter, using three different equations of state (the hotQCD lattice equation of state and two modified versions with opposite values of a parameter alpha). They evolved the fluid through a free-streaming stage, hydrodynamic expansion, and hadronic transport with UrQMD, then extracted the effective temperature and effective volume from the total energy and entropy at freeze-out. For each equation of state, they simulated 25 values of charged multiplicity density grouped into 5 sets corresponding to collision energies from 0.2 TeV to 15 TeV per nucleon pair, and extracted the speed of sound by fitting the logarithmic derivative of mean transverse momentum with respect to multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: 0.2 TeV to 15 TeV per nucleon pair
- Freeze-out temperature: T_F = 151 MeV
- The ratio ⟨p_T⟩/T_eff is taken as 3.00 ± 0.05 for LHC energies and 2.93 ± 0.05 for top RHIC energy
- The speed of sound squared extracted from the method agrees with the input equations of state within statistical error bars, with a slight overestimate of ~0.01 only for the lowest temperature (top RHIC energy)
- The relative dynamical variance σ_dyn²/⟨N_ch⟩² is approximately 2×10⁻³ in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- The correction factor for Poisson fluctuations in Equation (6) is roughly √(1 + 500/N_ch)

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Gardim et al. (2019) for extracting the speed of sound from ultracentral collisions, which was recently applied by the CMS collaboration (2024) to obtain c_s² = 0.241 ± 0.016 at T_eff = 219 ± 8 MeV. The authors reassess the validity of the theoretical basis in response to state-of-the-art calculations by Nijs and van der Schee (2023) suggesting the equations are not precise. The paper enables more reliable extraction of the speed of sound from experimental data by identifying the dominant uncertainty as the distribution of initial density fluctuations, and suggests that comparing the increase of mean transverse momentum with collision energy versus with multiplicity at fixed energy could shed light on whether the effective volume remains constant.
