# Muon Creation in Supernova Matter Facilitates Neutrino-driven Explosions

> Muons can be created in nascent neutron stars (NSs) due to the high electron chemical potentials and the high temperatures. Because of their relatively lower abundance compared to electrons, their role has so far been ignored in numerical simulations of stellar core collapse and NS formation. However, the appearance of muons softens the NS equation of state, triggers faster NS contraction and thus leads to higher luminosities and mean energies of the emitted neutrinos. This strengthens the postshock heating by neutrinos and can facilitate explosions by the neutrino-driven mechanism.

## Key claim

Muon creation in nascent neutron stars softens the equation of state, accelerates proto-neutron star contraction, and thereby increases neutrino luminosities and mean energies enough to facilitate neutrino-driven supernova explosions, but the effect is demonstrated only in two-dimensional simulations and may depend on the nuclear equation of state used.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional neutrino-hydrodynamics simulations of a non-rotating 20 solar-mass progenitor using the PROMETHEUS-VERTEX code, upgraded to track all six neutrino species (electron, muon, tau and their antiparticles) individually and to include muon and antimuon contributions to the equation of state, neutrino opacities, and lepton-number conservation. They compared models with and without muons for two nuclear equations of state (LS220 and SFHo), and also tested combinations with virial corrections and strangeness-dependent axial-vector coupling.

## Key numbers

- Muon rest mass is 105.66 MeV, which is exceeded by the electron chemical potential in newly formed neutron stars, enabling muon production.
- Peak temperatures in the proto-neutron star rise above 30 MeV roughly 100 ms after core bounce.
- Charged-muon number per baryon reaches up to Y_mu ~ 0.05 in the region between ~7 km (density ~4e14 g/cm^3) and ~21 km (density ~2e13 g/cm^3).
- In the model with muons, the peak abundance of muon antineutrinos per baryon exceeds 0.02, more than doubling compared to the standard case.
- The SFHo model with muons explodes, while the same model with standard neutrino physics does not; the LS220 model with muons explodes earlier than without.
- Neutrino transport used 15 geometrically distributed energy bins with maximum energy 380 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of muon roles in cold neutron stars (Steiner et al., 2005; Alford and Good, 2010) and the theoretical argument that muons could appear in supernova matter (Lohs et al., 2014). It directly addresses the sensitivity of neutrino-driven explosion simulations to 10-20% level effects noted in recent 3D simulations (Melson et al., 2015b). The results enable future three-dimensional simulations including muons, and suggest muon formation may also be relevant for neutron-star merger simulations and black-hole formation from hot neutron stars.
