# Determination of matter radius and neutron skin of $^{58}$Ni from reaction cross section of proton+$^{58}$Ni scattering based on chiral $g$-matrix model

> Background: Using the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model with the densities calculated with Gogny-HFB (GHFB) with the angular momentum projection (AMP), we determined the central values of matter radius and neutron skin from the central values of reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})$ of p+$^{40,48}$Ca and p+$^{208}$Pb scattering. As for p+$^{58}$Ni scattering, $σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})$ are available as a function of incident energy $E_{\rm in}$. Aim: Our aim is to determine matter radius $r_{m}$ and skin $r_{\rm skin}$ for $^{58}$Ni from the $σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})$ of p+$^{58}$Ni scattering by using the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model with the GHFB+AMP densities. Results: For p+$^{58}$Ni scattering, the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model with the GHFB+AMP densities reproduces $σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})$ in $8.8 \leq E_{\rm in} \leq 81$MeV. For $E_{\rm in}=81$MeV, we define the factor $F$ as $F=σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})/σ_{\rm R}({\rm AMP})=0.9775$. The $Fσ_{\rm R}({\rm AMP})$ be much the same as the center values of $σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})$ in $8.8 \leq E_{\rm in} \leq 81$MeV. We then determine $r_{\rm m}({\rm EXP})$ from the center values of $σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})$, using $σ_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})=C r_{m}^{2}({\rm EXP})$ with $C=r_{m}^{2}({\rm AMP})/ (Fσ_{\rm R}({\rm AMP}))$. The $r_{m}({\rm EXP})$ thus obtained are averaged over $E_{\rm in}$. The averaged value is $r_{m}({\rm EXP})=3.697$fm. Eventually, we obtain $r_{\rm skin}({\rm EXP})=0.023$fm from $r_{\rm m}=3.697$fm and $r_p({\rm EXP})=3.685$fm of electron scattering.

## Key claim

The authors determine the matter radius and neutron skin of $^{58}$Ni from reaction cross section data of proton+$^{58}$Ni scattering using the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model, but the extracted values are central values without quoted uncertainties, and the method relies on a fine-tuning factor $F=0.9775$ defined at a single energy (81 MeV) to bring the model into agreement with experimental data across the full energy range.

## Method

The authors used the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model with nuclear densities calculated from Gogny-HFB with angular momentum projection (GHFB+AMP). They computed reaction cross sections $\sigma_{\rm R}$ for p+$^{58}$Ni scattering at incident energies from 8.8 to 81 MeV, introduced a fine-tuning factor $F = \sigma_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})/\sigma_{\rm R}({\rm AMP}) = 0.9775$ at 81 MeV, then extracted the matter radius $r_m({\rm EXP})$ from each experimental $\sigma_{\rm R}$ using the relation $\sigma_{\rm R}({\rm EXP}) = C r_m^2({\rm EXP})$ with $C = r_m^2({\rm AMP})/(F\sigma_{\rm R}({\rm AMP}))$, and averaged the results over all energies. The neutron skin was then obtained by combining the averaged matter radius with the proton radius from electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- The fine-tuning factor $F = \sigma_{\rm R}({\rm EXP})/\sigma_{\rm R}({\rm AMP}) = 0.9775$ at incident energy $E_{\rm in} = 81$ MeV for p+$^{58}$Ni scattering.
- The averaged matter radius extracted is $r_m({\rm EXP}) = 3.697$ fm.
- The neutron skin extracted is $r_{\rm skin}({\rm EXP}) = 0.023$ fm.
- The proton radius from electron scattering used is $r_p({\rm EXP}) = 3.685$ fm.
- The extracted neutron radius is $r_n({\rm EXP}) = 3.708$ fm.
- The model reproduces experimental reaction cross sections in the energy range $8.8 \leq E_{\rm in} \leq 81$ MeV, with experimental data having 2.7% accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determinations of matter radii and neutron skins for $^{40,48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb (Tagami et al., 2020) using the same Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model with GHFB+AMP densities. The results for $^{58}$Ni agree with those from an independent experiment at the GSI storage ring (Zamora et al., 2017) which reported $r_m = 3.70(7)$ fm and $r_{\rm skin} = 0.03(12)$ fm from $^{58}$Ni+$^{4}$He scattering. This enables systematic comparison of matter radii and neutron skins across medium-mass nuclei using a consistent theoretical framework.
