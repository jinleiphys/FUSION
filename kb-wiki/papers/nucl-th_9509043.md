# Octupole Vibrations at High Angular Momenta

> Properties of octupole vibrations in rapidly rotating nuclei are discussed. Microscopic RPA calculations based on the cranked shell model are performed to investigate the interplay between rotation and vibrations. The ability of this model to describe the properties of collective vibrations built on the ground bands in rare-earth and actinide nuclei is demonstrated at high angular momentum. The octupole vibrational states in even-even superdeformed Hg nuclei are also predicted and compared with available experimental data. A new interpretation of the observed excited superdeformed bands invoking these octupole bands is proposed.

# Octupole Vibrations at High Angular Momenta

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that microscopic RPA calculations based on the cranked shell model can describe octupole vibrational properties in rapidly rotating nuclei, but the model's semi-classical treatment of rotation and small-amplitude nature require special attention when discussing low-spin states. A new interpretation of observed excited superdeformed bands in Hg nuclei is proposed, invoking octupole vibrational bands rather than two-quasiparticle configurations.

## Method
Microscopic RPA calculations were performed using a cranked Nilsson single-particle Hamiltonian with separable multipole residual interactions (octupole and dipole) in doubly-stretched coordinates. The coupling strengths for octupole interactions were determined by reproducing known band-head energies of octupole vibrations, or set to 1.05 times the self-consistent harmonic-oscillator values where experimental data were unavailable. Transition amplitudes were calculated using Marshalek's formula based on lowest-order boson expansion and (1/J)-expansion techniques, with generalized intensity relations applied for low-spin states.

- In 238U: pairing gaps Δn=0.7 MeV, Δp=0.8 MeV (constant with rotational frequency); ε=0.23; observed aligned angular momentum for lowest α=1 octupole band about 2.5ℏ at ωrot≥0.1 MeV/ℏ; octupole state breaks down at ωrot≈0.25 MeV/ℏ; B(E3; 3−→0+) for K=0 state: 25.2 W.u. (generalized intensity calculation), 24.2 W.u. (experiment); for K=1 state: 17.5 W.u. (theory), 7.8 W.u. (experiment); for K=2 state: 9.6 W.u. (theory), 7.1 W.u. (experiment)

## Context
This work extends earlier RPA calculations on octupole vibrations in deformed nuclei by Vogler (1970) and by Ragnarsson and collaborators (1986), and applies the cranked RPA framework developed by Shimizu and Nakatsukasa (1995) to high-spin regimes. The calculations for 238U are compared with new experimental data from Ward et al. (1995) at Chalk River, extending octupole bands up to Jπ=31−. For superdeformed Hg nuclei, the paper provides a new interpretation of excited bands observed by Crouch (1994), proposing octupole vibrational bands rather than two-quasiparticle configurations, which enables future experimental tests of octupole collectivity at high spin in superdeformed systems.
