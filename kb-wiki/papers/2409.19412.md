# Hypercontractivity and factorial moment scaling in the symmetry broken phase

> The search for remnants of the QCD chiral critical point is a central objective of current and future high-energy ion collision experiments. Previous studies suggest that a scaling law relating higher-order factorial moments of hadron multiplicity fluctuations to the second factorial moment could serve as a tool for detecting the QCD critical point. However, we demonstrate that this scaling law is not unique to critical phenomena. Instead, it emerges as a general property of distributions by extending the concept of hypercontractivity, originally applied to ordinary moments, to factorial moments. We present examples of distribution classes that exhibit the same higher-order factorial moment scaling as multiplicity fluctuations in the symmetry-broken phase. This insight allows us to explain the recent intermittency analysis results from the STAR experiment at RHIC (arXiv:2301.11062), where no indication of criticality was observed.

# Hypercontractivity and factorial moment scaling in the symmetry broken phase

## Key claim

The scaling law relating higher-order factorial moments of hadron multiplicity fluctuations to the second factorial moment, previously proposed as a signature of the QCD chiral critical point, is not unique to critical phenomena. Instead, this scaling emerges as a general property of distributions through the extension of hypercontractivity to factorial moments, and can be reproduced by non-critical distribution classes such as mixtures of Poisson distributions and the discrete Weibull family.

## Method

The authors demonstrate that the scaling relation F_q(M) ~ F_2(M)^{β_q} with β_q = (q-1)^ν can be satisfied by constructing distributions with enough free parameters to solve the system of equations relating factorial moments for q = 3, 4, ..., q_max. They construct explicit examples using uniform mixtures of Poisson distributions and also analyze the scaling exponent ν_F from a generalized Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional with a(T)|φ|^2 + b(T)|φ|^{2k} terms. Additionally, they perform numerical simulations using a cluster model for hadron transverse momenta, where events are generated with normally distributed transverse momenta around a normally distributed center.

## Key numbers

- For a mixture of 6 Poisson distributions with mean multiplicity set to 1 and μ_2 = 1.1, the solution parameters are λ_1 = 0.3568, λ_2 = 0.9048, λ_3 = 1.1248, λ_4 = 1.1248, λ_5 = 1.1248, λ_6 = 1.3635, achieving error less than 10^{-4} in satisfying the factorial moment equations up to q = 6.

- The analytically determined scaling exponent for the Ginzburg-Landau free energy with k = 2 is ν_F(2) = [ln(π) + Ψ(3/2)] / [2(ln(π) - ln(2))] ≈ 1.304, valid for 2 < q ≤ 10.

- In the cluster model simulations, for δσ_p in the range [0.001, 0.01], the estimated value of ν̄_q shows practically no variation at ν̄_q ≈ 0.69.

- The cluster model simulations used mean multiplicities in the range of 250 to 10000 particles per event, with up to 10^7 events per data set for the 250-particle case.

- In the Gaussian model simulation for the correlator scaling condition, the values obtained were a(q) ≈ 1.3, β_q ≈ 2, F_{2,m} ≈ 7, ā_q ≈ 100, and ΔF_2 ≈ 0.2, yielding 18.2 < 20 for q = 3.

## Context

This work extends the concept of hypercontractivity, originally applied to ordinary moments, to factorial moments in the context of multiplicity fluctuations in high-energy ion collisions. The authors directly address the scaling behavior previously associated with critical point searches, particularly referencing the work of Hwa (1992) on intermittency and critical phenomena. The results explain the recent intermittency analysis from the STAR experiment at RHIC (arXiv:2301.11062), where no indication of criticality was observed, by showing that the same factorial moment scaling can arise from non-critical distributions such as mixtures of Poisson distributions or the discrete Weibull family. This work enables a reinterpretation of future critical point searches by establishing that the observed scaling exponents alone cannot serve as unambiguous signatures of the QCD chiral critical point.
