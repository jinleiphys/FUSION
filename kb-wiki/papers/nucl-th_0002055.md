# Moments of inertia for multi-quasiparticle configurations

## Key claim

In the zero-pairing limit, the calculated moments of inertia for multi-quasiparticle states in well deformed A≈180 nuclei are substantially smaller than the rigid-body value (about 55 MeV⁻¹ versus 85 MeV⁻¹), and they are close to experimental values, contradicting the long-held expectation that quenched pairing should yield rigid-body moments of inertia. The moments of inertia are found to be insensitive to dynamic pair correlations, meaning that the observed low moments do not require residual pairing to be explained.

## Method

Tilted-axis cranking (TAC) calculations were performed for multi-quasiparticle rotational bands in ¹⁷⁸Hf, ¹⁷⁸W, and ¹⁷⁹W using a modified oscillator single-nucleon Hamiltonian with Strutinsky renormalization. For each configuration, the equilibrium deformation parameters (ε₂, ε₄) were determined at zero angular velocity, and the orientation angle ϑ of the rotational axis was found by requiring the total angular momentum vector to be parallel to the angular velocity vector. The calculations were carried out in the limit of zero pairing, with additional comparisons using static BCS pairing and Lipkin-Nogami pairing to assess the role of pair correlations.

## Key numbers

- Rigid-body moment of inertia for these masses and shapes: about 85 MeV⁻¹
- Typical empirical moment of inertia for the high-K bands: about 55 MeV⁻¹
- Equilibrium quadrupole deformation ε₂ = 0.23 and hexadecapole deformation ε₄ = 0.02 for most configurations in ¹⁷⁸W and ¹⁷⁹W
- For the K^π = 45/2⁻ and 25⁺ configurations (with a 1h₉/₂ proton): ε₂ = 0.25, ε₄ = 0.015
- For the K^π = 16⁺ configuration in ¹⁷⁸Hf: ε₂ = 0.22, ε₄ = 0.05
- The difference in deformation between the 45/2⁻/25⁺ configurations and others changes the rigid-body moment of inertia by 6%
- Variation of ε₂ with angular velocity stays below 0.005 (2% variation of rigid-body moment of inertia)
- Extrapolation of J₁(ω₁) to ω₁ = 0 for the 45/2⁻ and 25⁺ bands yields J₁ = 2.8 ± 0.5
- Pair gaps at band heads: Δ_p = 1.13 MeV for ground-state proton configuration; Δ_n = 0.48 MeV for the neutron configuration with one broken Cooper pair in the 7⁻ and 15⁺ bands

## Context

This work extends earlier tilted-axis cranking results (Frauendorf, 1995) and addresses the long-standing question of whether quenched pairing leads to rigid-body moments of inertia, a conjecture based on the harmonic oscillator model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1955; Valatin, 1956) and early studies of realistic potentials (Pashkevich and Frauendorf, 1975). The calculations contradict the assumption in Dracoulis et al. (1998) that the zero-pairing limit would produce rigid-like rotation. The results enable a microscopic understanding of high-seniority rotational bands in the A≈180 region and demonstrate that shell structure at mid-shell Fermi levels can produce moments of inertia far below the rigid-body value even without pairing, with implications for the interpretation of mass currents in rotating nuclei.
