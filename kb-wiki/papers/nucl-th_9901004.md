# Anisotropic $J/Ψ$ suppression in nuclear collisions

> The nuclear overlap zone in non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions is azimuthally very asymmetric. By varying the angle between the axes of deformation and the transverse direction of the pair momenta, the suppression of $J/Ψ$ and $Ψ'$ will oscillate in a characteristic way. Whereas the average suppression is mostly sensitive to the early and high density stages of the collision, the amplitude is more sensitive to the late stages. This effect provides additional information on the $J/Ψ$ suppression mechanisms such as direct absorption on participating nucleons, comover absorption or formation of a quark-gluon plasma. The behavior of the average $J/Ψ$ suppression and its amplitude with centrality of the collisions is discussed for SPS, RHIC and LHC energies with and without a phase transition.

# Anisotropic $J/\Psi$ suppression in nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The azimuthal asymmetry of the nuclear overlap zone in non-central heavy ion collisions produces a measurable oscillation in $J/\Psi$ and $\Psi'$ suppression as a function of the angle between the reaction plane and the transverse momentum of the charmonium pair, with the average suppression probing early-time physics while the oscillation amplitude is more sensitive to later stages of the collision.

## Method
The authors model charmonium evolution using a Boltzmann equation with free-streaming comover distributions approximated by Gaussian transverse density profiles. They compute comover absorption analytically for peripheral collisions and via exponential suppression for central collisions, and separately evaluate Glauber absorption on nucleons using a standard straight-line geometry model. The azimuthal dependence arises from the deformation parameter $\delta = (R_y^2 - R_x^2)/(R_y^2 + R_x^2)$ of the nuclear overlap zone, with the reaction plane defined by the impact parameter direction and the beam axis.

## Key numbers
- Azimuthal asymmetry of $J/\Psi$ suppression is 5-10% for semi-central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, more than doubling at RHIC and LHC energies
- The opacity $\tilde{\sigma}$ is approximately 0.3 at SPS, 0.6 at RHIC, and 0.9 at LHC energies for central collisions
- The relative amplitude of the oscillation reaches up to $\pm 7\%$ for semi-peripheral events at RHIC
- Glauber absorption cross section on nucleons is taken as $\sigma_{J/\Psi N} = 3.6$ mb following Gerland (1998)
- Comover absorption cross section is estimated as $\sigma_{\psi c} = (2/3)\sigma_{\psi N} \simeq 2.4$ mb
- The rms radius squared for central Pb+Pb at SPS is $R^2 \simeq 10$ fm$^2$, with total rapidity density $dN^{tot}/dy \simeq 500$
- The deformation parameter is approximately $\delta = b/2R_A$ for the initial overlap
- The elliptic flow parameter $2v_2$ is about 20% at SPS for pions and protons, with transport cross section $\sigma_{tr} \simeq 10$ mb
- The NA50 minimum bias data has relative error bars of 1-2%

## Context
This work extends the study of $J/\Psi$ suppression mechanisms at SPS energies, where the NA50 collaboration observed anomalous suppression in central Pb+Pb collisions that cannot be explained by Glauber absorption alone (Kharzeev, 1996). The authors propose a new observable -- the azimuthal oscillation of charmonium yields -- to discriminate between comover absorption models (Vogt, 1998; Capella, 1997) and quark-gluon plasma formation scenarios (Satz, 1995; Blaizot, 1996). The technique is analogous to elliptic flow measurements used by NA49 and can be applied at RHIC and LHC to study whether anomalous suppression arises from a phase transition, since the average suppression and its amplitude probe different collision stages.
