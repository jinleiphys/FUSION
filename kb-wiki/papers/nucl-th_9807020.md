# On quasi-bound states in eta-nucleys systems

> The optical potential for the $η$-meson is calculated. This is based on a new determination of a large $η$-N scattering length and recent experiments of $η$ production in two nucleon collisions. These determine the $η$ - nuclear potential well to be about 45 MeV deep and able to support S, P, D and F quasibound states. Some of the higher angular momentum states are fairly narrow and offer a chance of experimental detection.

# On quasi-bound states in eta-nucleus systems

## Key claim

The optical potential for the eta-meson in nuclear matter is deep enough (about 45 MeV) to support S, P, D, and F quasibound states, with some higher angular momentum states being fairly narrow and potentially detectable experimentally. However, the authors caution that the free-space eta-N scattering length of 0.75 fm would imply an unrealistically deep potential of -80 MeV, and that off-shell and nuclear medium effects reduce this to about -50 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculate the eta-nucleus optical potential using a K-matrix model with four coupled channels (eta-N, pi-N, gamma-N, pi-pi-N) to describe the eta-N scattering amplitude, incorporating the S(1540) resonance. They then compute the single-nucleon optical potential by averaging the eta-N scattering amplitude over nucleon states in a Fermi gas model, and separately calculate the two-nucleon absorptive potential from experimental cross sections for pp -> pp eta, pn -> d eta, and pn -> pn eta near threshold, using detailed balance relations with corrections for final state interactions.

## Key numbers

- Free eta-N scattering length: a_etaN = 0.75 + i 0.27 fm (from Green and Wycech (1997) and Batinic et al. (1995))
- Effective scattering length in nuclear medium (without Pauli blocking shift): A_etaN = 0.50 + i 0.092 fm
- Effective scattering length in nuclear medium (with Pauli blocking shift): A_etaN = 0.45 + i 0.068 fm
- Single-nucleon optical potential depth (without Pauli shift): V_N(0) = -53 - 10 i MeV
- Single-nucleon optical potential depth (with Pauli shift): V_N(0) = -47 - 7.1 i MeV
- Two-nucleon absorptive potential from deuteron channel: W_NN^1(0) = 4.2 MeV (from sigma(pn -> d eta) = 93 microbarns at Q = 56 MeV)
- Two-nucleon absorptive potential from pp channel: W_NN^0(0) = 1.2 MeV (from sigma(pp -> pp eta) = 4.9 microbarns at Q = 38 MeV), later corrected to 0.2 MeV after final state interaction correction
- Final total two-nucleon absorptive potential: W_NN(0) = 3.2 MeV
- S-state energy in O-16: -17.2 - i 6.7 MeV
- S-state energy in Ar-40: -27.2 - i 8.6 MeV
- S-state energy in Pb-208: -39.2 - i 10.1 MeV
- P-state energy in Pb-208: -31.5 - i 9.6 MeV
- D-state energy in Pb-208: -22.6 - i 9.0 MeV
- F-state energy in Pb-208: -12.3 - i 8.1 MeV
- S-state energy in He-4: -8.8 - i 7.4 MeV
- S-state energy in He-6: -17.2 - i 9.5 MeV
- Pauli blocking shift of S(1540) resonance: 36 MeV for nucleon at bottom of well, 19 MeV for nucleon at Fermi level (Fermi momentum 1.36 fm^-1)

## Context

This work extends the earlier predictions of eta-nuclear bound states by Haider and Liu (1986) and Li et al. (1987), who first noted the attractive eta-N interaction but obtained shallower potentials of about -25 MeV. It contradicts the view of Chiang, Oset, and Liu (1988) that eta-nuclear states are very broad due to two-nucleon capture, by showing that the two-nucleon absorptive potential is only about 3.2 MeV, much smaller than the single-nucleon absorptive potential. The results enable experimental searches for narrow higher-angular-momentum eta-nuclear states in medium and heavy nuclei, and support the interpretation by Wilkin (1993) of a quasibound state in the pd -> eta He-3 reaction. The authors also note planned experiments at Brookhaven Laboratory for He-6 systems.
