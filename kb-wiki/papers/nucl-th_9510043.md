# Quantum Algebraic Symmetries in Nuclear Structure

> Various applications of quantum algebraic techniques in nuclear structure physics, such as the su$_q$(2) rotator model and its extensions, the use of deformed bosons in the description of pairing correlations, and the construction of deformed exactly soluble models (Interacting Boson Model, Moszkowski model) are briefly reviewed. Emphasis is put in the study of the symmetries of the anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational ratios of frequencies, which underlie the structure of superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, the Bloch--Brink $α$-cluster model and possibly the shell structure in deformed atomic clusters.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that quantum algebraic techniques, particularly the su$_q$(2) rotator model and deformed boson methods, provide a useful framework for describing nuclear rotational spectra, pairing correlations, and the symmetries of the anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational frequency ratios; the abstract's claim of "emphasis" on the anisotropic oscillator is qualified by the paper's structure as a brief survey that also covers several other applications.

## Method

The paper reviews the su$_q$(2) rotator model and its extensions, where the Hamiltonian is proportional to the second-order Casimir operator of the su$_q$(2) algebra and the deformation parameter $\tau$ (with $q=e^{i\tau}$) is related to the softness parameter of the Variable Moment of Inertia model. It then describes the use of $q$-deformed bosons for mapping correlated fermion pairs in a single-$j$ shell, and the construction of $q$-deformed versions of the Interacting Boson Model and the Moszkowski model. The main technical contribution is the explicit construction of a generalized deformed u(N) algebra as the symmetry algebra of the N-dimensional anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational frequency ratios $m_1:m_2:\ldots:m_N$, where the $m_i$ are mutually prime natural numbers, and the derivation of the corresponding Fock space representations.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter $\tau$ (with $q=e^{i\tau}$) in the su$_q$(2) rotator model is found to correspond to the softness parameter of the Variable Moment of Inertia model (no explicit numerical value given).
- For the 1:1:2 anisotropic oscillator, the modified commutator $[{\cal A}_1, {\cal A}_1^{\dagger}] = -2 U_1 U_3 + U_3^2 + 3/16$.
- For the 1:1:3 anisotropic oscillator, $[{\cal A}_1, {\cal A}_1^{\dagger}] = -3 U_1 U_3^2 - (5/36) U_1 + U_3^3 + (23/36) U_3$.
- For the 1:1:4 anisotropic oscillator, $[{\cal A}_1, {\cal A}_1^{\dagger}] = -4 U_1 U_3^3 - (11/16) U_1 U_3 + U_3^4 + (43/32) U_3^2 + 105/4096$.
- The energy eigenvalues for the N-dimensional anisotropic oscillator are given by $E = \Sigma + \sum_{k=1}^{N} (2q_k - 1)/(2 m_k)$, where $q_k = 1,\ldots,m_k$ and $\Sigma$ is a non-negative integer.
- The dimension of each representation is $d = {\Sigma+N-1 \choose \Sigma}$.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on quantum algebras in nuclear physics, including the su$_q$(2) rotator model introduced by Bonatsos and collaborators (1991) and the use of deformed bosons for pairing correlations by Bonatsos (1992). It also builds on studies of the anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator by Louck (1965) and more recent work by Bonatsos and Daskaloyannis (1995) identifying the symmetry algebra as a deformation of u(2) for the two-dimensional case. The paper enables further development of exactly soluble deformed nuclear models and provides an algebraic framework for understanding the single-particle level structure of superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, as well as cluster configurations in light nuclei and deformed atomic clusters.
