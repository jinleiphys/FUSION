# J/psi Absorption in Heavy Ion Collisions II

> Using the methods introduced in a previous paper, we consider the dissociation of J/psi by the lowest-lying pseudoscalar and vector mesons in the hadronic fireball formed in heavy ion collisions, assumed to be a hadron gas at temperature T. Absorption by nuclear matter is accounted for as well. We compare with the S-U and Pb-Pb data presented by the NA50 Collaboration. From data at low centrality we find T=160-180 MeV, close to the predicted temperature of the transition to quark-gluon plasma and to the temperatures measured by hadron abundances. We extrapolate to higher centralities with the approximation of scaling the energy density of the fireball with the average baryon density per unit transverse area. Using the energy-temperature relation of the hadron gas made by the same pseudoscalar and vector mesons, the fall off of J/psi production shown by the NA50 data, can be marginally reproduced only for the highest temperature, T=180 MeV. If we use the energy-temperature relation of a Hagedorn gas with limiting temperature T_H=173 MeV, predictions fall short from reproducing the data. These results suggest that a different mechanism is responsible for the J/psi suppression at large centralities, which could very well be the formation of quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Using a hadron gas model that includes the lowest-lying pseudoscalar and vector mesons, the authors find that J/psi absorption in heavy ion collisions can be marginally reproduced by the NA50 data only for the highest temperature of 185 MeV, while a Hagedorn gas with limiting temperature 177 MeV falls short; this suggests that a different mechanism, likely quark-gluon plasma formation, is responsible for the J/psi suppression at large centralities.

## Method

The authors computed J/psi dissociation cross sections for reactions with pions, eta, kaons, rho, omega, K*, and phi mesons using the Constituent Quark Model (CQM) with couplings determined by flavor SU(3) symmetry and nonet symmetry for vector mesons, with real particle masses used for reaction thresholds. They then calculated thermal averages of the product of number density and cross section for each species in a hadron gas at temperature T, and combined these with nuclear absorption to produce attenuation curves as a function of fireball size l, comparing to NA50 data for S-U and Pb-Pb collisions. For extrapolation to higher centralities, they scaled the energy density with the average baryon density per unit transverse area and used the energy-temperature relation of either the hadron gas or a Hagedorn gas.

## Context

This paper extends the authors' previous work (Maiani et al., 2004) which considered only a pion gas and found T = 225 +/- 15 MeV, by including the full set of lowest-lying pseudoscalar and vector mesons, which lowers the inferred temperature to 165-185 MeV, consistent with freeze-out temperatures measured from hadron abundances (Rafelski, 2004; Antinori, 2004) and with lattice QCD estimates of the transition temperature. The results contradict the possibility that a purely hadronic mechanism explains the J/psi suppression at high centrality seen by NA50, and instead support the interpretation that quark-gluon plasma formation (with Debye screening as proposed by Matsui and Satz) is responsible, with the Hagedorn gas analysis (using the formalism of Rafelski, 2004; Cabibbo and Parisi, 1975) showing that the hadronic absorption mechanism saturates at the limiting temperature.
