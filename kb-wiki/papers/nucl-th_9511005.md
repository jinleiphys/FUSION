# $A$--Dependence of $ΛΛ$ Bond Energies in Double---$Λ$ Hypernuclei

> The $A$-dependence of the bond energy $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ of the ${ΛΛ}$ hypernuclear ground states is calculated in a three-body ${Λ+ Λ+ {^{A}Z}}$ model and in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach. Various ${ΛΛ}$ and $Λ$-nucleus or ${ΛN}$ potentials are used and the sensitivity of $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ to the interactions is discussed. It is shown that in medium and heavy ${ΛΛ}$ hypernuclei, $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ is a linear function of $r_Λ^{-3}$, where $r_Λ$ is rms radius of the hyperon orbital. It looks unlikely that it will be possible to extract ${ΛΛ}$ interaction from the double-$Λ$ hypernuclear energies only, the additional information about the $Λ$-core interaction, in particular, on $r_Λ$ is needed.

## Key claim

The bond energy of two Lambda hyperons in double-Lambda hypernuclei decreases with mass number A, but this A-dependence alone cannot be used to extract the Lambda-Lambda interaction strength because the bond energy is primarily determined by the rms radius of the hyperon orbital, not by the details of the Lambda-Lambda potential.

## Method

The authors calculated the bond energy Delta B_{Lambda Lambda} for double-Lambda hypernuclear ground states using two complementary approaches: a three-body Lambda + Lambda + core model solved with a variational oscillator basis expansion, and a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with Skyrme-like NN, Lambda N, and Lambda Lambda interactions. Various Lambda A potentials (Woods-Saxon C1 and density-dependent C2) and Lambda Lambda potentials (single-Gaussian Lambda1, two-Gaussian Lambda2 with repulsive core, and three Skyrme-type sets S Lambda Lambda 1-3) were employed, with strengths fitted to reproduce the bond energy of ^{13}_{Lambda Lambda}B (4.8 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The bond energy of ^{13}_{Lambda Lambda}B used for fitting is 4.8 MeV (from earlier experiments).
- The C1 Lambda A potential has depth V0 = -28 MeV, radius c = (1.128 + 0.439 A^{-2/3}) A^{1/3} fm, diffuseness a = 0.6 fm.
- The C2 Lambda A potential has V1 = -340 MeV, V2 = 1087.5 MeV, radius c = 1.08 A^{1/3} fm, diffuseness a = 0.54 fm.
- The Lambda1 single-Gaussian potential has range r0 = 1.034 fm; its depth V0 was fitted to -62 MeV for both C1 and C2 potentials.
- The Lambda2 two-Gaussian potential has V1 = 148 MeV, r1 = 0.82 fm, r2 = 1.2 fm; its attractive component V2 was fitted to -107 MeV for both C1 and C2 potentials.
- The S Lambda Lambda 1 Skyrme parameters: lambda0 = -312.6 MeV fm^3, lambda1 = 57.5 MeV fm^5.
- The S Lambda Lambda 2 Skyrme parameters: lambda0 = -437.7 MeV fm^3, lambda1 = 240.7 MeV fm^5.
- The S Lambda Lambda 3 Skyrme parameters: lambda0 = -831.8 MeV fm^3, lambda1 = 922.9 MeV fm^5.
- For strongly polarizing Lambda N potentials, the S Lambda Lambda 2 parameters must be multiplied by 0.33 (SkSH1 contraction) or 1.09 (R3 dilatation) to reproduce the boron bond energy.
- In medium and heavy hypernuclei, Delta B_{Lambda Lambda} scales linearly with r_{Lambda}^{-3}, where r_{Lambda} is the rms radius of the hyperon orbital.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that focused on light double-Lambda hypernuclei (e.g., ^{6}_{Lambda Lambda}He, ^{10}_{Lambda Lambda}Be, ^{13}_{Lambda Lambda}B) where the bond energy is nearly constant at about 4.5 MeV. Prior work by Ikeda and collaborators used single-Gaussian Lambda Lambda potentials, while the authors of the present paper had previously fitted Skyrme-type interactions to the boron bond energy. The paper shows that even with future measurements across a wide range of A, the Lambda Lambda interaction cannot be uniquely determined without independent information on the hyperon orbital rms radius r_{Lambda} from single-Lambda hypernuclei. The results also have implications for distinguishing double-Lambda hypernuclei from possible H dibaryon hypernuclei, as suggested by earlier reinterpretations of experimental data.
