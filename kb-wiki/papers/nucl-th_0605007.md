# Global Examination of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C Reaction Data at Low and Intermediate Energies

> We examine the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C elastic scattering over a wide energy range from 32.0 to 70.7 MeV in the laboratory system within the framework of the Optical model and the Coupled-Channels formalism. The $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system has been extensively studied within and over this energy range in the past. These efforts have been futile in determining the shape of the nuclear potential in the low energy region and in describing the individual angular distributions, single-angle 50$^{0}$ to 90$^{0}$ excitation functions and reaction cross-section data simultaneously. In order to address these problems systematically, we propose a potential that belongs to a family other than the one used to describe higher energy experimental data and show that it is possible to use it over this wide energy range. This potential also predicts the resonances at correct energies with reasonable widths.

## Key claim

The authors propose a deep, strongly attractive Woods-Saxon squared nuclear potential belonging to a different family than the UNAM potentials used for higher-energy data, and show that this potential can simultaneously describe elastic scattering angular distributions, 50° to 90° excitation functions, and reaction cross-section data for the ¹²C+¹²C system between 32.0 and 70.7 MeV, a feat not previously achieved at low energies.

## Method

The authors performed Optical model and Coupled-Channels calculations using a phenomenological complex nuclear potential with a real part of Woods-Saxon squared shape (fixed depth V=280.0 MeV) and an imaginary part of Woods-Saxon volume shape. They systematically varied the real radius (r_V from 0.6 to 0.9 fm) and diffuseness (a_V from 1.1 to 1.5 fm) on a grid to fit 18 individual angular distributions, then used linear interpolation of the three free parameters (r_V, a_V, and W) to predict 5 excitation functions and reaction cross-section data. Coupled-Channels calculations included the single-2⁺ (4.44 MeV) and mutual-2⁺ (8.88 MeV) states of ¹²C with a deformation parameter β₂ = -0.6.

## Key numbers

- Energy range: 32.0 to 70.7 MeV in the laboratory system (E/A from approximately 2.7 to 5.9 MeV per nucleon)
- Real potential depth fixed at V = 280.0 MeV
- Best-fit real radius r_V generally between 0.72 and 0.78 fm; best-fit real diffuseness a_V between 1.30 and 1.39 fm
- Imaginary potential radius fixed at r_W = 1.1 fm and diffuseness a_W = 0.55 fm
- Coulomb radius fixed at 5.5 fm
- Deformation parameter β₂ = -0.6 for ¹²C, derived from B(E2) = 42 e²fm⁴
- Linear interpolation formulas for Optical model: r_V = 0.80951 - 0.001226 E_Lab; a_V = 1.3482 - 0.00043225 E_Lab; W = -0.77098 + 0.15341 E_Lab
- Linear interpolation formulas for Coupled-Channels: r_V = 0.78664 - 0.00056643 E_Lab; a_V = 1.3476 + 0.00073848 E_Lab; W = -4.1719 + 0.209 E_Lab
- Resonances predicted at E_Lab ≈ 38.0, 50.0, and 57.5 MeV for spin values J=14, 16, and 18 respectively

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ¹²C+¹²C system by addressing the failure of previous potentials to simultaneously describe angular distributions, excitation functions, and reaction cross-sections at low energies. The authors show that the UNAM potentials of Brandan et al. (1990) and the modified potentials of Kondo et al. (1998), which successfully describe higher-energy data (E/A > 6 MeV), fail for low-energy angular distributions and excitation functions. The paper also contradicts the claim of Brandan et al. (1990) and Boztosun and Rae (2001) that coupling to inelastic channels mainly affects absorption at high energies, showing instead that at low energies (tested at E_Lab=35.0 MeV) coupling significantly modifies both the real and imaginary parts of the nuclear potential, in agreement with the microscopic calculations of Sakuragi and Kamimura (1984). This work enables future systematic studies of the ¹²C+¹²C system using a potential that simultaneously describes multiple observables and predicts resonances at correct energies.
