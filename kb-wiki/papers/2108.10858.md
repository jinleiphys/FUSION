# Sensitivity to the initial conditions of the Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory

> Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory is mathematically formulated through non-linear coupled time-dependent 3-dimensional partial differential equations and it is natural to expect a strong sensitivity of its solutions to variations of the initial conditions, akin to the butterfly effect ubiquitous in classical dynamics. Since the Schrödinger equation for an interacting many-body system is however linear and mathematically the exact equations of the Density Functional Theory reproduce the corresponding one-body properties, it would follow that the Lyapunov exponents are also vanishing within a Density Functional Theory framework. Whether for realistic implementations of the Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory the question of absence of the butterfly effect and whether the dynamics provided is indeed a predictable theory was never discussed. At the same time, since the time-dependent density functional theory is a unique tool allowing us the study of non-equilibrium dynamics of strongly interacting many-fermion systems, the question of predictability of this theoretical framework is of paramount importance. Our analysis, for a number of quantum superfluid many-body systems (unitary Fermi gas, nuclear fission, and heavy-ion collisions) with a classical equivalent number of degrees of freedom ${\cal O}(10^{10})$ and larger, suggests that its maximum Lyapunov exponents are negligible for all practical purposes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, despite the nonlinear nature of Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory (TDDFT) equations, the maximum Lyapunov exponents for realistic implementations in quantum superfluid many-body systems are negligible for all practical purposes, meaning the butterfly effect is effectively absent and TDDFT dynamics remain predictable.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of TDDFT for three strongly interacting many-fermion superfluid systems: unitary Fermi gas, nuclear fission, and heavy-ion collisions. They introduced small random perturbations to the initial quasiparticle wave functions according to a prescribed noise prescription, then evolved the systems in time and monitored the divergence of trajectories to extract Lyapunov exponents. The analysis was conducted for systems with classical equivalent degrees of freedom on the order of 10^10 and larger.

## Key numbers

- The upper bound for the nuclear Lyapunov exponent from the out-of-time ordered correlator (OTOC) formalism is λ^MSS_nuclear ≈ 0.016 c/fm = 5.33 × 10^26 s^-1, with λ^MSS_nuclear τ_cross = O(1) for a heavy nucleus of diameter ≈ 12 fm and crossing time τ_cross ≈ 50 fm/c
- For the unitary Fermi gas at critical temperature Tc ≈ 0.17 εF, the OTOC bound gives λ^MSS_UFG t_coll = O(1), where the two-body collision time is t_coll ≈ πℏ/2εF
- For condensed matter systems at room temperature, λ^MSS_CM ≈ 1.2 × 10^14 s^-1, with λ^MSS_CM τ_cross ≈ 15 for an electron traversing 100 nm
- The Bertsch parameter for the unitary Fermi gas is ξ = 0.372(5) theoretically and ξ = 0.376(5) experimentally

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of quantum chaos from the out-of-time ordered correlator (OTOC) framework of Maldacena (2016) and the Lyapunov stability analysis of time-dependent Hartree-Fock by Balian (1989) to practical TDDFT implementations. The paper addresses a gap in the literature regarding whether realistic TDDFT dynamics display sensitivity to initial conditions, which was previously only examined for specific cases by Bulgac (2016, 2019) and Shi (2020). The findings enable continued use of TDDFT as a predictive tool for non-equilibrium dynamics of strongly interacting many-fermion systems, including nuclear fission and heavy-ion collisions.
