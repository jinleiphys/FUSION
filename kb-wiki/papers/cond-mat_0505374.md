# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Regime: A Superfluid of a New Type

> We have studied, in a fully non-perturbative calculation, a dilute system of spin 1/2 interacting fermions, characterized by an infinite scattering length at finite temperatures. Various thermodynamic properties and the condensate fraction were calculated and we have also determined the critical temperature for the superfluid-normal phase transition in this regime. The thermodynamic behavior appears as a rather surprising and unexpected melange of fermionic and bosonic features. The thermal response of a spin 1/2 fermion at the BCS-BEC crossover should be classified as that of a new type of superfluid.

# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Regime: A Superfluid of a New Type

## Key claim

A dilute system of spin 1/2 fermions with infinite scattering length at finite temperatures exhibits a thermodynamic behavior that is a "surprising and unexpected melange of fermionic and bosonic features," leading the authors to classify the thermal response at the BCS-BEC crossover as that of a new type of superfluid, with the caveat that the critical temperature determined in this lattice calculation cannot be directly compared with experimental trap results due to significant differences in thermodynamic properties.

## Method

The authors placed spin 1/2 fermions on a 3D spatial lattice and used a path integral representation of the partition function, employing a zero-range two-body interaction with a momentum cutoff and a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to convert the interaction into a one-body Hamiltonian in an auxiliary field. They applied the Metropolis algorithm to sample auxiliary field configurations, computing thermodynamic quantities and the condensate fraction via one-body density matrices, with lattice sizes ranging from 6^3 to 10^3 and imaginary time steps chosen to maintain numerical stability.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the superfluid-normal phase transition: T_c = 0.23(2) epsilon_F, where epsilon_F is the Fermi energy of the free Fermi gas with the same number density
- Ground state energy parameter: xi_s approximately 0.44, where E(0) = (3/5) N epsilon_F xi_s
- Temperature dependence of energy below T_c follows E(T) proportional to T^(5/2), characteristic of an ideal Bose condensed gas, with exponent n = 5/2 determined to within about 10%
- Effective boson mass inferred from the temperature dependence: m* approximately 3m
- Condensate fraction alpha(T) is consistent with T_c = 0.23(2) epsilon_F and follows the form alpha(T) = alpha(0) [1 - (T/T_c)^(3/2)]
- System sizes: approximately 20 particles on a 6^3 lattice and approximately 55 particles on an 8^3 lattice
- Monte Carlo correlation length approximately 150 Metropolis steps at T approximately 0.2 epsilon_F

## Context

This work extends non-perturbative calculations of unitary fermions beyond the T=0 results of Baker (1999), Carlson (2003), Chang (2004), and Giorgini (2004), and addresses the finite-temperature regime where mean-field approaches based on the Eagles-Leggett wave function become questionable. The authors note that previous estimates of T_c by Wingate (2004) gave approximately 0.05 epsilon_F with an imprecise scattering length, and by Lee (2004) had large discretization errors. The results enable construction of an absolute temperature scale in the unitary regime using adiabatic tuning of the scattering length, extending the ideas of Carr (2004), and provide a benchmark for future lattice calculations with larger system sizes to resolve the critical behavior near T_c.
