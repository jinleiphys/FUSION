# Effects of Subnucleonic Fluctuations on the Longitudinal Dynamics of Heavy-Ion Collisions

> It is well understood that subnuclear fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions have an important impact on the creation of long-range correlations in the transverse plane. This is also true for the creation of particle correlations along the beam direction, which can be measured in particle detectors, e.g. through longitudinal decorrelation observables. In this work, we study the emergence of long-range rapidity structures in Pb+Pb collisions using a hybrid model connecting the 3D resolved {\Dipper} initial state model to a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamics framework CLVisc. We include different sources of fluctuations at the (sub-)nucleon level and present the effects of their inclusion on the longitudinal structure of relevant observables, focusing in this proceedings paper on charged hadron multiplicities, baryon stopping, directed flow and flow decorrelation. We find remarkable agreement to the experimental data regarding directed flow and the rapidity resolved charge particle multiplicity.

# Effects of Subnucleonic Fluctuations on the Longitudinal Dynamics of Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Subnucleonic fluctuations in the initial state of Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV have evident effects on the longitudinal (rapidity) dependence of final-state observables, and including them improves agreement with experimental data for directed flow and charged particle multiplicity, though the framework still falls short of experimental results for flow decorrelation in peripheral collisions.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid model coupling the 3D resolved McDipper initial state model (based on the color glass condensate kT-factorization framework) to the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic code CLVisc, with particlization via the Cooper-Frye prescription at an energy density of 0.266 GeV/fm3. Five different fluctuation scenarios were compared: smooth nucleons, three hotspots per nucleon, nucleon-level thickness fluctuations (sigma=0.637), and two combinations of hotspots with thickness fluctuations (sigma=0.637 and sigma=1.2). The initial conditions were evolved on an iso-tau surface at proper time tau=0.6 fm.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamical evolution: tau = 0.6 fm
- Particlization energy density: epsilon_frz = 0.266 GeV/fm3
- Thickness fluctuation magnitudes: sigma = 0.637 and sigma = 1.2 for the log-normal distributions
- Reference rapidity for flow decorrelation: 4.4 < eta_b < 5

## Context
This work extends prior studies of subnucleonic fluctuations in the transverse plane (PHENIX, 2018) to longitudinal observables, addressing the need for realistic 3D initial conditions beyond the approximate boost-invariance used in many models (Bozek, 2010; ALICE, 2010). The results enable future incorporation of valence-quark fluctuations, a realistic pre-equilibrium stage, and hadronic afterburners for quantitative precision. The framework was compared to ALICE data (2015, 2013) for charged hadron multiplicities and directed flow, and to CMS data (2015) for flow decorrelation.
