# On the Coulomb Interaction in a Nucleus for Odd J States

> With a certain approximation for the Coulomb matrix elements in a single j shell of protons and neutrons it is found that wave functions of states of odd angular momentum J in an even-even nucleus are not strongly affected by their presence,

## Key claim

Under the approximation that the only non-vanishing Coulomb matrix element in a single j shell is the J=0 two-proton matrix element, wave functions of odd-J states in even-even nuclei are not strongly affected by the Coulomb interaction because they cannot contain a component with J_p = 0.

## Method

The author calculates isospin mixing between the lowest J=2⁺ T=0 and J=2⁺ T=1 states in ⁴⁴Ti using a single j = f₇/₂ shell model with MBZE two-body matrix elements. The Coulomb interaction is approximated by retaining only the J=0 two-proton matrix element, and the mixing amplitude is computed from the wave function coefficients and energy difference of the two states.

## Key numbers

- The lowest J=2⁺ T=0 state in ⁴⁴Ti lies at 1.1631 MeV and the lowest J=2⁺ T=1 state at 5.2366 MeV, using the MBZE interaction in the f₇/₂ shell.
- The mixing amplitude between these two states is 0.09537 C, where C is the strength of the Coulomb interaction for two protons in a J=0 state.
- Taking C = 1.0 MeV, the probability of T=1 admixture in the J=2⁺ T=0 state is 0.0091.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of Coulomb effects in nuclei, specifically contrasting with the MBZE interaction used by Zamick and collaborators, which differs from earlier two-body matrix elements (Zamick, earlier works). The approximation shows that odd-J states are insensitive to the dominant J=0 Coulomb term, and that for even-J states with J greater than the maximum J for two identical nucleons (e.g., J=8, 10, 12 in the f₇/₂ shell), there is also no mixing. This enables simplified estimates of isospin mixing in medium-mass nuclei.
