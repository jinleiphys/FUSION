# Three different approaches to the same interaction: the Yukawa model in nuclear physics

> After a brief discussion of the meaning of the potential in quantum mechanics, we examine the results of the Yukawa model (scalar meson exchange) for the nucleon-nucleon interaction in three different dynamical frameworks: the non-relativistic dynamics of the Schrodinger equation, the relativistic quantum mechanics of the Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front equations and the lattice solution of the Quantum Field Theory, obtained in the quenched approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that even for the simplest possible nuclear interaction (scalar meson exchange in the Yukawa model), the predictions for binding energies differ substantially between non-relativistic, relativistic (Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front), and lattice QFT approaches, with the non-relativistic results generating "much more attraction" for a given coupling constant than any relativistic treatment.

## Method

The authors solved the S-wave Schrodinger equation with a Yukawa potential V(r) = -(g^2/4pi) * exp(-mu r)/r for the non-relativistic case, obtaining binding energies and scattering lengths as functions of a dimensionless coupling G = (g^2/4pi)*(m/mu). For relativistic dynamics, they solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation (four-dimensional, with four spinor components for J=0+) and the Light-Front Dynamics equation (three-dimensional, with two spinor components) in the ladder approximation for scalar meson exchange. The full QFT treatment used lattice techniques in the quenched approximation on Euclidean lattices of volume V = L^3 x T, computing the fermion propagator via Monte Carlo integration over meson field configurations.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling for first bound state in non-relativistic case: G_0 = 1.680 (dimensionless), corresponding to g_0^2 = 4pi * 1.680 * (mu/m); for nuclear case (mu/m ~ 0.5), g_0 ~ 3
- Excited state thresholds in non-relativistic case: G_1 = 6.445, G_2 = 14.34 for appearance of second and third bound states
- Critical coupling constant for relativistic equations (m=1, mu=0.5): g_c = 2pi for Bethe-Salpeter, g_c = 6.84 for Light-Front Dynamics
- Binding energy range in relativistic equations: B in [0, M] (bounded by vanishing of M^2 near g_c), while non-relativistic solutions allow B exceeding 2m
- Lattice results for binding energy as function of lattice size La*mu showed B/m tending to zero in infinite volume limit for the parameters studied, indicating no bound state for that parameter set

## Context

This work extends the comparison of dynamical frameworks previously studied for scalar theories (phi^2 chi theory) by Ji (1992) and Oropeza (2003) for scattering, and by Nieuwenhuis (1996) for cross-ladder sums, now applied to the fermionic Yukawa model. The relativistic results show strong repulsive effects relative to non-relativistic predictions, with the existence of critical coupling constants above which the spectrum becomes unbounded (first found by Mangin-Brinet et al., 2001, for LFD and confirmed for BS by Carbonell et al., 2010). The lattice results in the quenched approximation (which neglects virtual nucleon-antinucleon pair creation, justified by nucleon heaviness) enable future full QFT comparisons with the ladder-approximated relativistic equations, and the authors note that the spectator equation approach of Gross and Stadler, using OBE kernels, reproduces experimental triton binding without explicit three-body forces.
