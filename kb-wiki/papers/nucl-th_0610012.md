# Isospin symmetry in mirror $α$-decays

> We show that a consequence of isospin symmetry, recently discovered in mirror conjugated one-nucleon decays, can be extended to mirror-conjugated $α$-particles decays, both virtual and real. For virtual $α$-decays of bound mirror pairs this symmetry manifests itself as a relation between the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficients (ANCs) of $α$-particle overlap integrals. This relation is given by a simple analytical formula which involves $α$-particle separation energies and charges of residual nuclei. For bound-unbound mirror pairs, the ANC of a bound nucleus is related to the $α$-width of the mirror unbound level. For unbound mirror pairs we get a new analytical formula that relates the widths of mirror resonances. We test the validity of these analytical formuli against the predictions of a two-body potential and of a many-body microscopic cluster model for several mirror states in $^7$Li-$^7$Be, $^{11}$B-$^{11}$C and $^{19}$F-$^{19}$Ne isotopes. We show that these analytical formulae are valid in many cases but that some deviations can be expected for isotopes with strongly deformed and easily excited cores. In general, the results from microscopic model are not very sensitive to model assumptions and can be used to predict unknown astrophysically relevant cross sections using known information about mirror systems.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously discovered isospin-symmetry relation for mirror one-nucleon decays to mirror-conjugated alpha-particle decays, deriving simple analytical formulas that relate asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) or resonance widths; however, they note that deviations from these formulas can occur for isotopes with strongly deformed and easily excited cores.

## Method

The authors derive analytical formulas for the ratio of squared ANCs in bound mirror pairs, for the relation between a bound ANC and the width of its mirror unbound level, and for the ratio of widths in unbound mirror pairs. They test these formulas by comparing them to exact two-body potential model calculations using Woods-Saxon potentials with varying diffuseness (0.35 to 0.95 fm) and to many-body microscopic cluster model (MCM) calculations for mirror states in ⁷Li-⁷Be, ¹¹B-¹¹C, and ¹⁹F-¹⁹Ne, using both two-cluster and three-cluster descriptions with Volkov V2 and Minnesota (MN) nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- For bound mirror pairs in the two-body potential model, the analytical formula agrees with exact calculations on average within 2% or less; for ⁷Li-⁷Be the agreement is slightly worse, about 3-4%.
- In the microscopic cluster model for ⁷Li-⁷Be, the ratio of squared ANCs for the 3/2⁻ state is 1.35 ± 0.01 (analytical: 1.37); for the 1/2⁻ state it is 1.43 ± 0.01 (analytical: 1.47).
- For ¹¹B-¹¹C in the two-cluster MCM, the ratio for the 3/2⁻₁ state (l=0) is 1.60 ± 0.02 (analytical: 1.56); for the 3/2⁺ state (l=1) the ratio is (1.68 ± 0.02) × 10¹² (analytical: 1.72 × 10¹²).
- For ¹⁹F-¹⁹Ne, the ratio for the 9/2⁺ state (l=5) is 222 ± 3 (analytical: 228); for the 3/2⁻ state (l=2) it is 10.74 ± 0.04 (analytical: 10.63).
- The sensitivity of the ratio to the potential choice in the two-body model was less than 2%.

## Context

This work extends the isospin-symmetry relations for mirror one-nucleon decays discovered by Timofeyuk (2003) to alpha-particle decays, providing a framework analogous to the earlier nucleon ANC relations that achieved about 7% accuracy for bound mirror pairs (Timofeyuk 2005a) and 10% for bound-unbound pairs (Timofeyuk 2005b). The results enable prediction of unknown astrophysically relevant (alpha,gamma), (alpha,N), and (N,alpha) cross sections using known information about mirror systems, similar to how proton ANCs for ⁸B, ⁹C, ¹²N, and ²⁷P were previously determined from neutron ANCs of their mirror analogs (Trache 2003, Guo 2005, Tang 2005, Guo 2006) for (p,gamma) reactions.
