# Will protons become gray at 13 TeV and 100 TeV?

> It is shown that the regime of pp-interactions at 7 TeV is a critical one. The LHC data about elastic pp-scattering at 7 and 8 TeV are used to get some information about both elastic and inelastic profiles of pp-collisions. They are discussed in the context of two phenomenological models which pretend to describe the high energy pp-data with high accuracy. Some predictions following from these models for LHC energy 13 TeV and for energy 95 TeV of the newly proposed collider are discussed. It is claimed that the center of the inelastic interaction region will become less dark with increase of energy albeit very slowly.

# Will protons become gray at 13 TeV and 100 TeV?

## Key claim
The regime of pp-interactions at 7 TeV is a critical one, where the inelastic profile at central impact parameters is extremely close to complete blackness (G(s,0) ≈ 1). At higher energies, the center of the inelastic interaction region will become less dark (more gray) albeit very slowly, with the effect only becoming noticeable at 95 TeV or beyond.

## Method
The author uses the unitarity condition to relate the elastic scattering amplitude to the inelastic profile G(s,b) as a function of energy s and impact parameter b. Experimental data from TOTEM at 7 and 8 TeV on the diffraction cone slope B(s) and total cross section σ_tot(s) are inserted into a Gaussian parameterization of the elastic amplitude to compute the inelastic profile. Two phenomenological models (KFK and FMS) are then used to extrapolate these quantities to 13 TeV and 95 TeV.

## Key numbers
- At 7 TeV (TOTEM data): ζ = 1.0128, G(s,0) = 0.9998, B = 19.9 GeV⁻², σ_tot = 98.6 mb
- At 8 TeV (TOTEM data): ζ = 1.0189, G(s,0) = 0.9996, B = 20.4 GeV⁻², σ_tot = 101.7 mb
- At 13 TeV (KFK model prediction): ζ = 1.0524, G(s,0) = 0.9973, B = 21.35 GeV⁻², σ_tot = 109.93 mb
- At 95 TeV (KFK model prediction): ζ = 1.1497, G(s,0) = 0.9776, B = 27.10 GeV⁻², σ_tot = 152.43 mb
- At 13 TeV (FMS model prediction): ζ = 1.0554, G(s,0) = 0.9969
- At 95 TeV (FMS model prediction): ζ = 1.1231, G(s,0) = 0.9848
- The darkness at the center changes by only 6% if ζ increases from 1.0 to 1.2
- At ISR energy 62.5 GeV, the central darkness was slightly above 0.9, decreasing by half at about 0.7 fm

## Context
This work extends the BEL (blacker, edgier, larger) picture of proton interactions from ISR energies through LHC energies, building on the TOTEM collaboration results (2013) at 7 and 8 TeV. The finding that the inelastic profile at 7 TeV is completely dark up to about 0.5 fm confirms earlier expectations from studies of high-density gluon configurations (Dremin, 2013). The predictions for 13 TeV and 95 TeV enable experimental tests by TOTEM and CMS at the LHC and by future collider proposals, where precise measurements of the diffraction cone slope B and total cross section σ_tot will determine whether ζ exceeds 1 and the central region becomes more gray.
