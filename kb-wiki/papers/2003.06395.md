# Semiclassical Approximation meets Keldysh-Schwinger diagrammatic technique: Scalar $φ^4$

> We study the evolution of the non-equilibrium quantum fields from a highly excited initial state in two approaches: the standard Keldysh-Schwinger diagram technique and the semiclassical expansion. We demonstrate explicitly that these two approaches coincide if the coupling constant $g$ and the Plank constant $\hbar$ are small simultaneously. Also, we discuss loop diagrams of the perturbative approach, which are summed up by the leading order term of the semiclassical expansion. As an example, we consider shear viscosity for the scalar field theory at the leading semiclassical order. We introduce the new technique that unifies both semiclassical and diagrammatic approaches and open the possibility to perform the resummation of the semiclassical contributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Keldysh-Schwinger diagrammatic technique and the semiclassical (Classical Statistical Approximation) approach coincide when both the coupling constant \(g\) and Planck's constant \(\hbar\) are small simultaneously, with the caveat that the leading-order semiclassical term sums up certain multi-loop diagrams of the perturbative expansion, but a specific diagram (the last one in the \(g^2\) expansion) remains subleading for highly occupied initial states.

## Method

The authors start from the Keldysh-Schwinger path-integral representation for a scalar \(\varphi^4\) theory, rotate to the classical-quantum field basis (\(\varphi_{cl}, \varphi_q\)), and expand the Keldysh action in powers of \(\hbar\). They then compare the perturbative expansion in the coupling constant \(g\) (using standard Keldysh-Schwinger diagram rules with retarded, advanced, and Keldysh propagators) with the semiclassical expansion, where the leading order (Classical Statistical Approximation) involves solving the classical equation of motion and averaging over initial conditions via the Wigner function. The next-to-leading order in \(\hbar\) is handled by replacing \(\varphi_q\) fields with functional derivatives with respect to an auxiliary source \(J\), leading to coupled differential equations for the variations of the classical solution. The shear viscosity is computed via the Kubo formula using the stress-energy tensor correlator.

## Key numbers

- The "cactus" diagram contribution to the retarded Green function is given by \(G_R^{(cactus)}(x;x') = -18g^2 \int d^dy\, d^dy'\, G^R(x;y) G^R(y;y') G^R(y;x') G^K(y;y') G^K(y';y')\).
- The Keldysh Green function for a free field with initial distribution \(f_p\) is \(G_K^0(x;x') = -i\hbar \int \frac{d^{d-1}{\bf p}}{(2\pi)^{d-1}} \frac{\cos(\omega_p(t-t'))}{4\omega_p} (2f_p+1) e^{-i{\bf p\cdot x}}\), with \(\omega_p^2 = {\bf p}^2 + m^2\).
- In the high-temperature limit, the Keldysh Green function scales as \(G_K^0 \sim T/\omega_p^2\) (classical, independent of \(\hbar\)), while in the zero-temperature limit it scales as \(G_K^0 \sim \hbar/\omega_p\) (linear in \(\hbar\)).
- The next-to-leading order semiclassical correction to the retarded Green function includes a term \(-\frac{3g^2\hbar^2}{2} \int dy\, G_R^0(y,x_2) \int dz\, G_R^0(x_1,z)[G_R^0(z,y)]^3\).

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the Keldysh-Schwinger diagram technique and the classical approximation previously performed for thermal equilibrium by Aarts (1998), which demonstrated agreement of leading-order contributions at high temperatures. The paper builds on the Classical Statistical Approximation formalism developed by Mueller and collaborators (2000s) and the semiclassical expansion framework established in earlier works by one of the authors (LR1, LR2, LR3). The new unified technique enables resummation of semiclassical contributions and provides a systematic way to compute transport coefficients such as shear viscosity for non-equilibrium scalar field theories, with potential applications to early-stage heavy-ion collisions, cold atomic gases, and early Universe dynamics.
