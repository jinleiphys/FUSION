# Angular Dependence of the Nuclear Enhancement of Drell-Yan Pairs

> We calculate the nuclear enhancement in the angular distribution of Drell-Yan pairs produced in proton-nucleus reactions. Nuclear effects are encoded in universal twist-4 parton correlation functions. We find that the Lam-Tung relation for the angular coefficients of the lepton-pair distribution holds for the double-hard, but not for the soft-hard contribution. We also predict that nuclear enhancement effects at RHIC energies can be large.

## Key claim

The Lam-Tung relation for the angular coefficients of the lepton-pair distribution holds for the double-hard contribution to nuclear-enhanced Drell-Yan production, but not for the soft-hard contribution; the authors predict that nuclear enhancement effects at RHIC energies can be large, but note that the soft-hard process is negligible at those energies.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential Drell-Yan cross section for proton-nucleus collisions, including the full angular dependence, using the formalism of Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1994) for twist-4 parton correlation functions. They distinguish two types of double-scattering contributions: double-hard processes, where both QCD interactions are hard, and soft-hard processes, where the beam parton picks up a soft nuclear parton before the hard scattering. The nuclear twist-4 matrix elements are modeled using known twist-2 parton distribution functions with a nuclear enhancement factor proportional to A^(1/3) for double-hard and A^(4/3) for soft-hard scattering.

## Key numbers

- The normalization constant for double-hard scattering is taken as C = 0.072 GeV^2, with the nuclear enhancement factor C(A) = C * A^(1/3).
- The soft-hard scattering normalization uses lambda_LQSG = 0.1 GeV, with an enhancement factor A^(4/3).
- For 800 GeV protons on tungsten (W) at Q = 5 GeV and q_perp = 4 GeV, the twist-4 contribution to the single spin-flip amplitude W_Delta is much larger than the twist-2 result, with the soft-hard contribution negligible (multiplied by a factor of 10 in the figure).
- For 250 GeV protons colliding with 100 GeV/nucleon gold (Au) nuclei at Q = 5 GeV and q_perp = 4 GeV, the twist-4 cross section peaks at rapidity y ≈ -2, with a predicted count rate of about 30 events per year in a window of 1 GeV^4.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of Guo (1997) for nuclear-enhanced Drell-Yan production to include the full angular distribution of the lepton pair, and applies the results to RHIC energies. The formalism builds on the twist-4 factorization approach of Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1994) and the earlier work of Berger (1989) on nuclear enhancement. The results enable experimental tests of the Lam-Tung relation violation as a signature of soft-hard scattering, and suggest that the dominant double-hard contribution at RHIC can be measured with detectors like PHENIX, providing a test of the LQS formalism.
