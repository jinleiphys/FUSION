# Sum Rule Description of Color Transparency

> The assumption that a small point-like configuration does not interact with nucleons leads to a new set of sum rules that are interpreted as models of the baryon-nucleon interaction. These models are rendered semi-realistic by requiring consistency with data for cross section fluctuations in proton-proton diffractive collisions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a class of exactly solvable models for color transparency (CT) in which the assumption that a point-like configuration (PLC) does not interact with nucleons leads to new sum rules, but the models are only rendered semi-realistic by imposing constraints from data on cross section fluctuations in proton-proton diffractive collisions.

## Method

The authors treat the PLC formed in a hard process as a coherent superposition of a finite number of hadronic states (two-state and three-state models). They express the vanishing PLC-baryon interaction in a hadron basis, leading to sum rules that specify the form of the baryon-nucleon scattering matrix. The models are constrained using moments of the transition matrix from diffractive dissociation data, specifically the quantities ω_σ and κ_σ from the analysis of Blättel et al. (1992). The propagation of the PLC through nuclear matter is solved using a generalized eikonal approximation with a constant-density slab model and then extended to a three-dimensional Wood-Saxon density distribution.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon-nucleon total cross section is taken as σ ≈ 40 mb for the high-energy regime (p > 1 GeV/c).
* The characteristic rest frame time for PLC expansion is τ₀ ≈ 1 fm, giving τ ≈ 5 fm for presently available energies.
* The resonance mass M_N* is taken as 1.4 GeV and M_N** as 2.4 GeV as representative values.
* The constraint from diffractive data gives ω_σ ≲ 0.1 and κ = 0.2 for the lower-energy regime considered.
* The constant nuclear density used in slab calculations is ρ₀ = 0.166 fm⁻³.
* The average distance across a nucleus is taken as L = 0.64 A^{1/3} fm.
* The resonance width is simulated with an imaginary part of 75 MeV (corresponding to a typical width of ~150 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the Good-Walker (1960) cross section eigenstate formalism to color transparency, building on earlier PLC propagation studies by Jennings and Miller (1990, 1991) and the two-gluon exchange model discussions by Low (1975) and Nussinov (1975). The sum rule approach was first applied to CT in photoproduction of charmed particles by Frankfurt and Strikman (1991). The models enable calculations of nuclear transparency ratios for (e,e'p) and (e,e'N*) reactions, and the generalized optical potentials can be applied to other processes such as (p,pp) reactions and neutron-nucleus total cross sections. The authors suggest this approach may lead to a new method for relating perturbative QCD results to soft hadronic amplitudes.
