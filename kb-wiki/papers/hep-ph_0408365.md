# Double distributions for the proton

> We derive double distributions for the proton in a simple model that contains scalar as well as axial-vector diquark correlations. The model parameters are tuned so that the experimentally measured electromagnetic form factors are reproduced for small momentum transfer. Resulting generalized parton distributions satisfy known constraints, including the positivity bounds.

## Key claim

The authors derive double distributions (DDs) for the proton using a simple quark-diquark model that includes both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, but the model is only tuned to reproduce experimentally measured electromagnetic form factors for small momentum transfer, and the resulting quark distributions compare poorly with experiment at small Bjorken x.

## Method

The authors treat the proton as a bound state of a residual quark and two quarks coupled in scalar and axial-vector diquark channels, using point-like quark-diquark vertices and free-particle propagators for the diquarks. They compute the double distributions F, K, and G from the impulse approximation to twist-two operator matrix elements, employing a smeared vertex regularization scheme (with cutoff Lambda) that preserves Lorentz covariance and positivity bounds. The model parameters (quark mass m, diquark mass m_D, and cutoff Lambda) are tuned to fit the proton's Dirac and Pauli form factors at low momentum transfer, with an additional factor Z(Lambda) added to the axial-vector diquark contribution to maintain correct quark number normalization.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass m = 0.445 GeV, cutoff Lambda = 0.465 GeV, diquark mass m_D = 0.720 GeV
- Normalization N = 1.52 for the scalar diquark contribution
- Z(Lambda = 0.465 GeV) = 1.09, the regularization-dependent factor for the axial-vector diquark contribution
- The model fits proton electromagnetic form factor data to approximately 2-5% accuracy for small momentum transfer
- The dipole mass squared used for comparison is M_D^2 = 0.71 GeV^2
- The model GPDs are plotted at fixed t = -0.1 GeV^2 for various values of the skewness parameter xi
- For t = -0.1 GeV^2, the maximum skewness is xi_max = 0.33

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of double distributions in simple model scenarios by Tiburzi (2002, 2003, 2004) and Mukherjee (2002) to the proton with appropriate spin structure, including both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations. The model enables comparison between the light-cone Fock space representation of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the Lorentz-invariant double distribution formalism, and it provides a framework for constructing GPDs that satisfy positivity bounds, which are frequently violated in standard hadronic models. The authors note that the model's small-x behavior compares poorly with experiment, suggesting that for phenomenological applications the model should be augmented with realistic valence quark distributions, for example by factorization of the beta-dependence of the DDs as done by Mukherjee (2002).
