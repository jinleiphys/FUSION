# Thermal Field Theory in the Presence of a Background Magnetic Field and its Application to QCD

> This review has explored the fundamental principles of thermal field theory in the context of a background magnetic field, highlighting its theoretical framework and some of its applications to the thermo-magnetic QCD plasma generated in heavy-ion collisions. Our discussion has been limited to equilibrium systems for clarity and conciseness. We analysed bulk thermodynamic characteristics, including the phase diagram as well as real-time observables, shedding light on the behaviour and dynamics of the thermo-magnetic QCD medium relevant to heavy-ion physics.

## Key claim
This review systematically presents the theoretical framework of thermal field theory in a background magnetic field, but explicitly limits its discussion to equilibrium systems, thereby glossing over the nonequilibrium dynamics that may be relevant for the rapidly evolving quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors review the construction of free propagators in a background magnetic field and the extension of thermal field theory to include such fields. They then apply this framework to analyze bulk thermodynamic properties, the QCD phase diagram in the temperature-magnetic field plane, and real-time observables including damping rates, dilepton production rates, and heavy quark diffusion coefficients. The review synthesizes results from lattice QCD, effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and holographic approaches.

## Key numbers
- The QCD phase diagram in the \(T-eB\) plane is shown for magnetic field strengths up to \(1\,{\rm GeV}^2\), with the crossover temperature \(T_{\rm CO}\) decreasing as a function of \(eB\) (Bali et al., 2011).
- The IMC effect disappears for a pion mass of \(497(4)\,{\rm MeV}\), corresponding to a current quark mass of \(14.07(55)\,{\rm MeV}\), at a magnetic field of \(eB=0.6\,{\rm GeV}^2\) (Endrodi, 2019).
- For magnetic fields up to \(3.25\,{\rm GeV}^2\), the chiral crossover remains a crossover, though it becomes sharper (Endrodi, 2015).
- At \(eB=9\,{\rm GeV}^2\), the transition appears to become first order, with a large gap in the condensate and a jump in the strange quark number susceptibility (Delia et al., 2021).

## Context
This review extends earlier work on thermal field theory and QCD phase diagrams by focusing specifically on the effects of a background magnetic field, a topic of central importance for non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. It contrasts the earlier understanding of a monotonically increasing crossover temperature with magnetic field (Klimenko, 1991; Shushpanov, 1997; D'Elia, 2010) against the revised lattice QCD results showing a decreasing \(T_{\rm CO}\) and the phenomenon of inverse magnetic catalysis (Bali, 2011; Bali, 2012). The review enables future work on the search for a critical point in the \(T-eB\) plane and on understanding the role of gluonic degrees of freedom in driving these effects.
