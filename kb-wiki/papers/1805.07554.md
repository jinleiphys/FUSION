# Persistence of magicity in neutron rich exotic $^{78}$Ni in ground as well as excited states

> Recent experimental observation of magicity in $^{78}$Ni has infused the interest to examine the persistence of the magic character across the N$=$50 shell gap in extremely neutron rich exotic nucleus $^{78}$Ni in ground as well as excited states. A systematic study of Ni isotopes and N$=$50 isotones in ground state is performed within the microscopic framework of relativistic mean-field (RMF) and the triaxially deformed Nilson Strutinsky model (NSM). Ground state density distributions, charge form factors, radii, separation energies, pairing energies, single particle energies and the shell corrections show strong magicity in $^{78}$Ni. Excited nuclei are treated within the statistical theory of hot rotating nuclei where the variation of level density parameter and entropy shows significant magicity with a deep minima at N$=$50, which, persists up to the temperatures $\approx$ 1.5$-$2 MeV and then slowly disappear with increasing temperature. Rotational states are evaluated and effect of rotation on N$=$50 (Z$=$20$-$30) isotones are studied. Our results agree very well with the available experimental data and few other theoretical calculations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the doubly magic character of $^{78}$Ni persists not only in the ground state but also in excited states up to temperatures of approximately 1.5–2 MeV, after which the shell effects slowly disappear with increasing temperature.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic study of Ni isotopes (N=20–70) and N=50 isotones (Z=20–50) using two theoretical frameworks: the relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus state-dependent BCS approach with NL3* and PK1 force parameters, and the triaxially deformed Nilsson-Strutinsky model (NSM). For excited states, they applied the statistical theory of hot rotating nuclei to evaluate level density parameters, entropy, and rotational states as functions of temperature (T=0.7–3.0 MeV) and spin (M=0–60 ℏ).

## Key numbers
- The proton single-particle shell gap at Z=28 attains its maximum value of 8.4 MeV at $^{78}$Ni, compared to 3.5 MeV at $^{48}$Ni and 6.2 MeV at $^{98}$Ni.
- The shell correction δE_Shell at $^{78}$Ni is approximately 8 MeV, the deepest minimum among all Ni isotopes.
- Magicity in $^{78}$Ni persists up to temperatures of approximately 1.5–2 MeV, then slowly disappears with increasing temperature.
- At T=3 MeV, the inverse level density parameter K (=A/a) becomes almost constant around 9–10 for N=50 isotones.
- Deformation (β) of $^{78}$Ni increases from zero at zero spin up to 0.16 with prolate non-collective shape, then transitions to oblate non-collective shape with deformation up to 0.2.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of $^{78}$Ni magicity including shell-model calculations (Sieja, 2009), relativistic mean-field model using TMA parameter (Yadav, 2011), relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory (Meng, 2006), and First-Principles computations (Hagen, 2016). It also builds on recent experimental confirmation of $^{78}$Ni as a strong doubly magic nucleus by Olivier (2017) and Welker (2017). The study enables understanding of how shell structure evolves with temperature and rotation in extremely neutron-rich nuclei, which is relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis where $^{78}$Ni serves as a possible waiting point.
