# The Onset of Color Transparency in $(e,e'p)$ Reactions on Nuclei

> Quantum filtering of the ejectile wave packet from hard $ep$ scattering on bound nucleons puts stringent constraints on the onset of color transparency in $(e,e'p)$ reactions in nuclei at moderate energies. Based on multiple-scattering theory, we derive a novel formula for nuclear transparency and discuss its energy dependence in terms of a color transparency sum rule.

## Key claim

The onset of color transparency in $(e,e'p)$ reactions at moderate energies is much slower than previously expected, because the quantum interference (Gribov inelastic shadowing) required to produce a small-size ejectile wave packet demands coherent excitation of many nucleonic resonances, and at finite energies the coherence length limits the number of states that can interfere.

## Method

The authors formulate a coupled-channel multiple-scattering theory based on Gribov's inelastic shadowing, introducing a diffraction operator whose eigenvalues are cross sections for eigenstates of the ejectile. They derive a "color transparency sum rule" relating the wave functions of nucleonic states at zero transverse size to the off-diagonal diffraction matrix elements, and compute the energy-dependent nuclear transparency by diagonalizing an effective diffraction operator that includes a nuclear form factor cutoff for high-mass intermediate states. Numerical estimates are made using a quark-diquark model of the nucleon with harmonic-oscillator wave functions and a parametrized $\rho$-dependent cross section.

## Key numbers

* For carbon, aluminum, copper, and lead, the low-energy nuclear transparencies (with $\sigma_{in}(pN) \approx 30$ mb) are: $Tr_{C}=0.61$, $Tr_{Al}=0.49$, $Tr_{Cu}=0.40$, $Tr_{Pb}=0.26$.
* At $\nu \sim 15$ GeV, the predicted reduction of attenuation relative to the low-energy limit is $\sim 35\%$ for carbon and $\sim 25\%$ for aluminum.
* The minimal eigenvalue of the diffraction matrix for three interfering shells ($K=1,2,3$) at $\nu \sim 30$ GeV is $\sigma_{min}=17.5$ mb; for two shells it is $22.1$ mb; for four shells it is $14.0$ mb.
* The level splitting for the nucleonic resonances is taken as $\Delta m = 2\hbar\omega \approx 700$ MeV/$c^2$ (from the $N^*(1680,5/2^+)$ channel) or alternatively $\Delta m \approx 1200$ MeV/$c^2$ for radial excitations.
* The diffraction dissociation cross section for $NN \rightarrow N^*(1680,5/2^+)$ is $\approx 0.17$ mb, compared to $\sigma_{el}(NN\rightarrow NN) \approx 7$ mb; the total inclusive diffractive excitation cross section sums to $\sim 2$ mb.
* The 3-quark core cross section is estimated as $\sigma_{tot}((3q)N) \approx 0.8\,\sigma_{tot}(pN)$, with $\sigma_0 \approx 1.6\,\sigma_{tot}(pN)$ and $R_0^2 \approx 0.55$ fm$^2$ in the $\rho$-dependent cross section model.
* The condition for the onset of coherence is $Q^2 \approx 2 m_N \nu \gsim (5-10) A^{1/3}$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$, corresponding to $\nu \gsim (3-5)$ GeV $\cdot A^{1/3}$.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov inelastic shadowing formalism (Gribov 1969) to the $(e,e'p)$ reaction, providing a quantitative framework that goes beyond the classical expansion-and-attenuation model of Farrar, Frankfurt, Liu, and Strikman (1988) and the single-channel complex-potential approach of Jennings and Miller (1990). The authors predict a slow onset of color transparency, consistent with preliminary results from the NE18 experiment at SLAC, and argue that electron beams of $\sim 30$ GeV are needed to observe a clear signal on heavy nuclei.
