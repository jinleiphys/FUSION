# Skyrme-Random-Phase-Approximation description of spin-flip and orbital giant resonances

> The self-consistent separable random-phase approximation (SRPA) model with Skyrme forces is extended to the case of magnetic excitations and applied to the description of spin-flip and orbital M1 giant resonances in the isotopic chain $^{142-152}$Nd. The Skyrme forces SkT6, SkM*, SLy6 and SkI3 are used. The calculations show the onset of the scissors mode with increasing deformation. A specific three-peak structure of the spin-flip response is found and explained by particular neutron and proton spin-flip transitions. Although the employed forces provide an acceptable qualitative description, the Skyrme functional still needs further improvement to reproduce quantitatively the experimental data.

## Key claim

The self-consistent separable random-phase approximation (SRPA) model with Skyrme forces, extended to magnetic excitations, provides an acceptable qualitative description of spin-flip and orbital M1 giant resonances in the isotopic chain 142-152Nd, but the Skyrme functional still needs further improvement to reproduce the experimental data quantitatively.

## Method

The authors extended the self-consistent separable random-phase approximation (SRPA) model, previously developed for electric excitations, to magnetic modes by deriving the residual interaction from the Skyrme functional including spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms. The model was applied to the isotopic chain 142-152Nd using four Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3), with the strength function calculated for the Kπ=1+ branch using a Lorentz averaging parameter of Δ=1 MeV and a set of three input operators to ensure convergence.

## Key numbers

- The spin-flip M1 strength in 142Nd exhibits a distinct two-peak structure at approximately 4 MeV and 10 MeV, evolving into a broad one-peak resonance in 150,152Nd.
- The low-energy scissors orbital mode appears in deformed 150,152Nd and dominates at 2-4 MeV, giving a significant contribution to M1 strength at 2-7 MeV.
- The high-energy orbital strength appears at approximately 30 MeV for all isotopes, identified as the λμ=21 branch of the isovector electric quadrupole giant resonance.
- The Lorentz averaging parameter used in the strength function calculation is Δ=1 MeV.
- The RPA matrix rank for the calculations is 4K=12, using K=3 input operators.

## Context

This work extends the earlier SHF study of spin-flip M1 giant resonances by Nesterenko et al. (2009) within the SRPA model, which showed that none of 8 different Skyrme parameterizations could simultaneously describe the one-peak structure in doubly-magic nuclei and the two-peaks in deformed nuclei. The present study provides a more detailed investigation of the isotopic chain 142-152Nd, analyzing the evolution of the spin-flip strength with deformation and neutron number, and presents first results for the orbital scissors mode. The calculations enable future improvements of Skyrme forces, particularly their spin-orbit parts, by using magnetic modes as sensitive probes of spin-orbit splitting and tensor forces.
