# Long Range Azimuthal Correlation, Entanglement and Bell Inequality Violation by Spinning Gluons at the LHC

> We apply the recently developed concept of the nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) for the gluon sector to investigate the long-range azimuthal angular correlations in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. The spinning gluon in these collisions will introduce a significant nonzero $\cos(2ϕ)$ asymmetries in both Higgs Boson and top quark pair productions. The genesis of the $\cos(2ϕ)$ correlation lies in the intricate quantum entanglement. Owing to the substantial $\cos(2ϕ)$ effect, the NEEC observable in Higgs Boson and $t{\bar t}$ production emerges as a pivotal avenue for delving into quantum entanglement and scrutinizing the Bell inequality at high-energy colliders.

## Key claim

The paper claims that long-range azimuthal $\cos(2\phi)$ correlations in nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) measurements at the LHC arise from quantum entanglement of spinning gluons, and that these correlations can be used to test Bell inequality violation. The claim is conditional on the existence of sizable spinning gluon distributions and on the ability to measure forward/backward energy flows in coincidence with central hard processes like Higgs boson or top quark pair production.

## Method

The authors apply the nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) formalism for the gluon sector to proton-proton collisions at the LHC, factorizing the cross section into normal gluon NEEC distributions $f_{g,\rm EEC}$ and spinning gluon NEEC distributions $d_{g,\rm EEC}$ multiplied by perturbative partonic cross sections. They compute the $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry from the interference of double helicity-flip amplitudes in the partonic scattering, using leading-order perturbative QCD expressions for the NEEC distributions in terms of collinear splitting functions. The Bell observable is constructed from the azimuthal-angle-dependent cross section using the Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt (CHSH) inequality.

## Key numbers

- The $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry for Higgs boson production is positive and reaches a sizable value at mid-rapidity, with $\hat\sigma_2=\hat\sigma_0=\pi g_\phi^2/64$ at leading order.
- The $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry for top quark pair production is negative, with the maximum value occurring near threshold where $\hat s=4m_t^2$.
- The CHSH inequality violation is observed for Higgs rapidity $y_{\rm Higgs} < 0.5$ at the LHC.
- At $\sqrt{S_{pp}}=33\ {\rm TeV}$ without jet tagging, the Bell observable reaches $B \approx 2.36$ for $y_{\rm Higgs} = 0$.
- The maximum possible violation of the CHSH inequality is given by the Tsirelson bound $B_{\rm max} = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.828$.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) framework, previously developed for deep inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider by Liu (2022), Cao (2023), and Li (2023), to proton-proton collisions at the LHC. The spinning gluon distribution studied here is related to the helicity-flip gluon generalized parton distribution (GPD) discussed by Diehl (1997) and Hoodbhoy (1998), and to the linearly polarized gluon distribution in transverse momentum dependent (TMD) formalism studied by Boer (2010), Metz (2011), and Pisano (2013). The Bell inequality test builds on recent collider-based proposals by Fabbrichesi (2021), Severi (2021), Barr (2021), Aguilar-Saavedra (2022), and others, but claims to enable the first test of Bell inequality in confined quantities like gluons while closing the locality loophole present in previous LHC-based tests.
