# MEC-induced two-nucleon emission in neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Meson-exchange currents (MEC) play a crucial role in the nuclear response to a leptonic probe, inducing the emission of two nucleons, the so-called 2p2h excitations. Here we report the results of our recent work \cite{Belocchi:2025eix}, in which we evaluated the 2p2h contribution to the $(ν_μ,μ^-p)$ neutrino cross section off a carbon target, which involves the simultaneous detection the outgoing muon and a proton in the final state. This process has recently been measured in long-baseline neutrino experiments, but no truly microscopic calculation for the 2p2h channel existed to be compared with these data until now. The calculation employs fully relativistic two-body currents and is carried out within the Relativistic Fermi Gas framework. It provides a generalization to the weak sector of the electromagnetic inclusive model developed in Ref.~\cite{DePace:2003spn} and an extension of the calculation to enable semi-inclusive predictions. A selection of our results for the semi-inclusive cross section versus the emitted proton variables is presented, both at fixed neutrino energy and folded with the T2K muon-neutrino flux.

# MEC-induced two-nucleon emission in neutrino-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
The authors present the first fully microscopic, relativistic calculation of the semi-inclusive 2p2h (two-particle–two-hole) contribution to the charged-current (ν_μ, μ⁻p) cross section on carbon, extending an existing electromagnetic inclusive model to the weak sector. The calculation is carried out within the Relativistic Fermi Gas framework and is limited to plane-wave final-state nucleons, with no treatment of final-state interactions or nucleon correlations beyond the Fermi gas picture.

## Method
The authors compute the semi-inclusive weak nuclear tensor for 2p2h excitations using fully relativistic two-body meson-exchange currents (MEC) within the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, with a phenomenological energy shift of 40 MeV to account for binding and final-state interaction effects. The MEC operator includes pion-in-flight, seagull, pion-pole, and forward/backward Δ-resonance components derived from a chiral Lagrangian. The nine-dimensional phase-space integral is reduced to five dimensions analytically using the Dirac δ⁴ and evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum for carbon: p_F = 225 MeV
- Energy shift for 2p2h channel: E_shift^(2p2h) = 40 MeV (2 × E_shift from QE analysis)
- Fixed neutrino energy for differential cross section calculations: E_ν_μ = 750 MeV, with transferred energy ω = 200 MeV and muon scattering angle θ_μ = 15°
- The Δ-resonance current dominates the 2p2h cross section, accounting for more than half the total strength, with a peak around proton kinetic energy T_p ≈ 150 MeV in parallel kinematics
- Flux-folded cross sections are computed using the T2K ND280 muon-neutrino flux and kinematic cuts from the 1μCC0πNp measurement

## Context
This work extends the electromagnetic (e,e'p) semi-inclusive framework of Belocchi (2024) to the weak sector, generalizing the inclusive MEC model of De Pace (2003) and Ruiz Simo (2016). It provides the first microscopic semi-inclusive 2p2h calculation for comparison with T2K data (T2K Collaboration, 2018), which previously relied on the GENIE implementation (Dolan, 2019) of the SuSAv2-MEC inclusive model. The results show systematic differences from GENIE predictions, with higher strength and peaks at larger leading-proton momenta, attributed to the inadequacy of extrapolating inclusive inputs to semi-inclusive observables. This work enables improved implementation of 2p2h contributions in event generators for T2K, NOvA, DUNE, and Hyper-Kamiokande, though further inclusion of final-state interactions and correlations is needed.
