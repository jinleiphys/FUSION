# The Delta I=1/2 rule in non-mesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei

> By employing recent data on non-mesonic decay of s-shell Lambda-hypernuclei we study, within the phenomenological model of Block and Dalitz, the validity of the Delta I=1/2 rule in the Lambda N--> NN process. Due to the low experimental precision, a possible violation of this rule can be neither proved nor excluded at present with sufficient accuracy: a pure Delta I=1/2 transition amplitude is excluded at 40% confidence level.

## Key claim

Using recent data on non-mesonic decay of s-shell Lambda-hypernuclei within the phenomenological model of Block and Dalitz, a pure Delta I=1/2 transition amplitude is excluded at 40% confidence level, but due to low experimental precision a possible violation of this rule can be neither proved nor excluded with sufficient accuracy.

## Method

The authors employed the phenomenological model of Block and Dalitz, which treats the non-mesonic decay width as a product of spin-averaged rates for neutron-induced and proton-induced processes multiplied by the average nucleon density at the hyperon position. Using experimental data for the non-mesonic widths and neutron-to-proton ratios of s-shell hypernuclei (4-Lambda-H, 4-Lambda-He, and 5-Lambda-He), they extracted the partial rates for spin-singlet and spin-triplet interactions and derived constraints on the ratio between Delta I=1/2 and Delta I=3/2 transition amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Free Lambda decay branching ratios: Gamma_pi-/Gamma_Lambda = 0.639, Gamma_pi0/Gamma_Lambda = 0.358, with total lifetime tau_free_Lambda = 2.632 x 10^-10 sec
- Experimental ratio of free pionic widths: (Gamma_pi-/Gamma_pi0)_Exp = 1.78, compared to the Delta I=1/2 prediction of 2
- For 4-Lambda-He (BNL data): Gamma_n = 0.04 +/- 0.02, Gamma_p = 0.16 +/- 0.02, Gamma_NM = 0.20 +/- 0.03, Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.25 +/- 0.13 (all in units of Gamma_free_Lambda)
- For 5-Lambda-He (BNL data): Gamma_n = 0.20 +/- 0.11, Gamma_p = 0.21 +/- 0.07, Gamma_NM = 0.41 +/- 0.14, Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.93 +/- 0.55 (all in units of Gamma_free_Lambda)
- Extracted partial rates (in fm^3): R_n0 = 4.7 +/- 2.1, R_p0 = 7.9 (+16.5/-7.9), R_n1 = 10.3 +/- 8.6, R_p1 = 9.8 +/- 5.5
- Ratio R_n0/R_p0 = 0.6 (+1.3/-0.6); ratio R_n1/R_p1 = 1.0 (+1.1/-1.0)
- Predicted Gamma_n/Gamma_p for 3-Lambda-H = 0.7 (+1.1/-0.7); predicted Gamma_NM for 3-Lambda-H = 0.007 +/- 0.006 (in units of Gamma_free_Lambda)
- For 12-Lambda-C: average Gamma_NM = 1.01 +/- 0.13, average Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 1.61 (+0.57/-0.66) (both in units of Gamma_free_Lambda)
- Predicted Gamma_NM for 56-Lambda-Fe = 1.48 (+0.59/-0.45) (in units of Gamma_free_Lambda)

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analysis of Block and Dalitz (1962) for non-mesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei, which was later updated by other authors (Dover 1987, Cohen 1990, Schumacher 1992) who suggested a possible violation of the Delta I=1/2 rule but noted the need for more precise data. The study addresses the long-standing Gamma_n/Gamma_p puzzle, where theoretical calculations underestimate central experimental data points for all hypernuclei, and enables future tests of the isospin rule once more precise measurements become available from facilities like COSY and KEK.
