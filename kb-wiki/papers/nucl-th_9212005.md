# Microscopic Origin of Quantum Chaos in Rotational Damping

> The rotational spectrum of $^{168}$Yb is calculated diagonalizing different effective interactions within the basis of unperturbed rotational bands provided by the cranked shell model. A transition between order and chaos taking place in the energy region between 1 and 2 MeV above the yrast line is observed, associated with the onset of rotational damping. It can be related to the higher multipole components of the force acting among the unperturbed rotational bands.

## Key claim

The transition from order to chaos in the rotational spectrum of $^{168}$Yb, associated with the onset of rotational damping, occurs between 1 and 2 MeV above the yrast line and is caused specifically by the high-multipole components (λ > 2) of the residual interaction, not by the low-multipole components common to simpler effective forces.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a surface-delta interaction (SDI) and a pairing-plus-quadrupole force within a basis of approximately 1000 unperturbed rotational bands from the cranked shell model for $^{168}$Yb, for each parity and signature. They computed the Δ₃ statistics of level spacings and the distribution of stretched E2 transition strengths, comparing them to the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) predictions of Wigner surmise and Porter-Thomas distributions. The branching number for E2 decays was calculated to quantify the transition from discrete to damped rotational bands as a function of excitation energy above yrast.

## Key numbers

- The unperturbed basis included the lowest 10³ states, with stable results for the lowest 300 levels covering approximately 2.4 MeV of excitation energy above yrast.
- The transition between order and chaos takes place in the energy region between 1 and 2 MeV above the yrast line.
- Discrete band transitions (carrying more than 71% of the total B(E2) strength) yield about 36 predicted rotational bands, consistent with the 30 bands reported in fluctuation analysis of rotational decay spectra.
- The branching number indicates a gradual transition from discrete to damped rotation around an energy region of U₀ ≃ 800 keV above yrast.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum chaos in nuclear systems by providing a microscopic mechanism for rotational damping, building on the cranked shell model framework of Bengtsson and Frauendorf (1979) and the fluctuation analysis of rotational decay spectra by Døssing et al. (1991). The results show that the pairing-plus-quadrupole force, which has been successful for low-energy nuclear structure (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975), cannot generate the chaotic behavior observed; instead, the high-multipole components (λ up to 5) of the surface-delta interaction are essential. This enables future experimental studies of statistical properties of rotational spectra as a function of rotational frequency to probe the poorly known high-multipole components of the effective nuclear residual interaction.
