# Quarkonium In-Medium Transport Equation Derived from First Principles

> We use the open quantum system formalism to study the dynamical in-medium evolution of quarkonium. The system of quarkonium is described by potential non-relativistic QCD while the environment is a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma in local thermal equilibrium below the melting temperature of the quarkonium. Under the Markovian approximation, it is shown that the Lindblad equation leads to a Boltzmann transport equation if a Wigner transform is applied to the system density matrix. Our derivation illuminates how the microscopic time-reversibility of QCD is consistent with the time-irreversible in-medium evolution of quarkonium states. Static screening, dissociation and recombination of quarkonium are treated in the same theoretical framework. In addition, quarkonium annihilation is included in a similar way, although the effect is negligible for the phenomenology of the current heavy ion collision experiments. The methods used here can be extended to study quarkonium dynamical evolution inside a strongly coupled QGP, a hot medium out of equilibrium or cold nuclear matter, which is important to studying quarkonium production in heavy ion, proton-ion, and electron-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Boltzmann transport equation for quarkonium in-medium evolution from the Lindblad equation of open quantum systems, showing that the Markovian approximation and the molecular chaos assumption are valid under the specific power-counting scheme where the quarkonium binding energy is larger than or comparable to the temperature (\(Mv^2 \gtrsim T \gtrsim m_D\)), and the quarkonium size is small (\(rT \sim T/(Mv) \lesssim v\)).

## Method

The authors start from potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) for the quarkonium system and a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma in local thermal equilibrium as the environment. They expand the von Neumann equation to second order in the dipole interaction, take the partial trace over the environment to obtain the Lindblad equation, and then apply a Wigner transform to the system density matrix. The double time integrals are simplified by the Markovian approximation (taking the upper limit to infinity), and the resulting linear time dependence is extracted to obtain the Boltzmann equation with dissociation and recombination terms.

## Key numbers

- The dissociation rate is proportional to \(\frac{2}{3}C_F q^2 g^2 |\langle \psi_{nl} | \mathbf{r} | \Psi_{\mathbf{p}_{\mathrm{rel}}} \rangle|^2\) with \(C_F = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)\) and \(N_c=3\).
- The recombination term contains a factor \(\frac{2}{3} \frac{T_F}{N_c} q^2 g^2 |\langle \psi_{nl} | \mathbf{r} | \Psi_{\mathbf{p}_{\mathrm{rel}}} \rangle|^2\) with \(T_F = 1/2\).
- The energy conservation delta functions are \(\delta(-|E_{nl}| + q - \mathbf{p}_{\mathrm{rel}}^2/M)\) for both dissociation and recombination.
- The momentum conservation delta function is \(\delta^3(\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{p}_{\mathrm{cm}} + \mathbf{q})\).
- The dissociation rate scales as \(\sim (grT)^2 T \lesssim \alpha_s v^2 T\) in the assumed power counting.
- The environment correlation time is roughly \(1/T\), while the relaxation time is the inverse of the dissociation rate.

## Context

This work bridges the open quantum system approach (used by Brambilla et al. (2017) for bottomonium suppression without center-of-mass motion) and the Boltzmann transport equation approach (used by Yao et al. (2017) and others for coupled heavy quark and quarkonium evolution). It extends previous transport models by deriving the equation from first principles in pNRQCD, justifying the Markovian and molecular chaos approximations, and explicitly including center-of-mass motion to enable studies of transverse momentum and rapidity dependence. The methods can be extended to strongly coupled QGP, non-equilibrium media, and cold nuclear matter.
