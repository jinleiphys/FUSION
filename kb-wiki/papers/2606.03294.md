# Non-monotonicity of $p_T$ correlations from meson-baryon mixing

> The STAR experiment has recently reported data on the $\sqrt{\langleΔp_{T,i}Δp_{T,j}\rangle}/\langle\langle p_T\rangle\rangle$ charged hadron correlation in Au+Au reactions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3-200$ GeV. The beam energy dependence of this quantity is non-monotonic, showing a pronounced minimum at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7.7$ GeV, while being essentially flat at lower and higher energies. It has been proposed that such a non-monotonicity would be consistent with increased momentum correlations due to a critical point of QCD. In the present work it is shown, using a simplified model, that the observed structure can be consistently explained by the transition from a baryon dominated system to a meson dominated system and is therefore not a good observable for the critical point of QCD.

## Key claim
Using a simplified two-species model (pions and protons), the observed non-monotonic minimum in the beam-energy dependence of the normalized transverse-momentum covariance can be explained by the transition from a baryon-dominated to a meson-dominated system, without invoking a QCD critical point.

## Method
The authors construct a minimalistic model where charged particles in each event are composed of charged pions and protons, with yields taken from UrQMD simulations within mid-rapidity \(|y|<0.5\) and \(0.2<p_{\rm T}<2\) GeV. Particles are sampled independently from thermal transverse momentum distributions with different effective temperatures for pions and protons, and pairwise momentum conservation is implemented with a probability reflecting the charge-to-mass ratio of the nucleus and the neutral-pion fraction. The two components of the covariance, the variance of the event-wise mean \(p_{\rm T}\) and the averaged scaled variance of the in-event \(p_{\rm T}\) distribution, are computed separately to isolate the mixing effect.

## Key numbers
- The minimum in the scaled covariance \(\sqrt{\langle\Delta p_{{\rm T},i}\Delta p_{{\rm T},j}\rangle}/\langle\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle_k\rangle\) occurs at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7.7\) GeV, consistent with the STAR data.
- The effective temperatures used for central collisions are \(T^p_{\rm eff}=0.280\) GeV for protons and \(T^{\pi}_{\rm eff}=0.130\) GeV for pions; for peripheral collisions, \(T^p_{\rm eff}=0.230\) GeV and \(T^{\pi}_{\rm eff}=0.120\) GeV.
- The negative contribution from meson-baryon mixing is proportional to \(-\frac{N_M N_B}{N_{k}^{2}(N_k-1)}(\mu_M-\mu_B)^2\), where \(\mu_M\) and \(\mu_B\) are the mean transverse momenta of mesons and baryons.
- The asymptotic value of the root of the correlator divided by the mean of the mean \(p_{\rm T}\) is on the one percent level.

## Context
This work directly addresses the STAR Collaboration's recent data (STAR 2019, STAR 2026) on charged-hadron \(p_{\rm T}\) correlations in Au+Au collisions from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3-200\) GeV, which had been speculated to signal a QCD critical endpoint. The authors show that the observed minimum can be reproduced by a trivial mixing effect, contradicting the interpretation that it is a signature of the critical point. The analysis extends prior work on \(p_{\rm T}\) fluctuations (Mrowczynski 2004, Gavin 2016) and momentum conservation effects, and it implies that this observable is not suitable for locating the QCD critical endpoint, redirecting future searches toward more robust fluctuation observables.
