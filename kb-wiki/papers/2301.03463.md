# Analysis of realistic and empirical multipole interactions for shell-model calculations

> We perform analysis of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, as well as of empirically-corrected interactions, fitted to reproduce in detail the spectroscopic data in p and sd shells. We focus on the multipole part of the interactions, contrary to previous studies of that type which focused on monopole parts. We analyze how the different parts of the NN force (tensor, spin-orbit and central) contribute to the leading multipoles of the interaction and how do they renormalize in medium. We show that the leading coherent terms of the realistic and effective interactions are dominated by the central component as expected from their schematic operator structure. Convergence and cutoff dependence of multipole Hamiltonians is also discussed.

## Key claim

The leading coherent multipoles of realistic and empirical effective interactions in the p and sd shells are dominated by the central component, supporting the equivalence of multipoles extracted from realistic two-body matrix elements and schematic operator forms; however, the renormalization persistency of the tensor force, which holds for monopole matrix elements, does not hold for the monopole-free (multipole) part of the interaction.

## Method

The author performed a spin-tensor decomposition of the central, spin-orbit, and tensor components of the multipole Hamiltonian for effective interactions in the p and sd shells. Realistic interactions were derived from the CD-Bonn potential via the Vlowk approach with a sharp cutoff of 2 fm⁻¹, renormalized through the Q-box method with folded diagrams including all diagrams through third order and allowing 18ℏω excitations for intermediate states. Empirical fits were obtained using a linear combination method, fitting 41 energy levels in 14 nuclei in the p shell and 300 levels in 59 nuclei in the sd shell, with 10 linear combinations for the p shell and 40 for the sd shell.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of effective interactions beyond the monopole part, which was the focus of prior studies such as Otsuka (2001, 2006, 2010, 2011) and Nowacki (2007), by performing the first spin-tensor analysis of the multipole terms. It builds on the classification of leading multipoles in effective interactions by Zuker (1994) and Dufour (1995), who demonstrated the universality of multipole content across realistic interactions. The results enable future investigations into how inclusion of three-body forces would influence the couplings in the multipole Hamiltonian and whether they could explain the multipole modifications required by empirical fits.
