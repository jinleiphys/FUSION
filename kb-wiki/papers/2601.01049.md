# Neutron skins probed in proton knockout from neutron-rich nuclei

> Proton-induced quasifree knockout reactions provide a powerful probe of nuclear single-particle structure and reaction dynamics in both stable and neutron-rich nuclei. In this work we develop a unified theoretical framework for the calculation of inclusive (p,2p) and sequential (p,3p) reaction cross sections and fragment momentum distributions at intermediate and relativistic energies. The approach is based on a probabilistic extension of Glauber multiple-scattering theory combined with microscopic nuclear densities obtained from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using Skyrme energy-density functionals. We focus in particular on the sensitivity of total cross sections and longitudinal momentum dispersions to neutron-skin thickness along isotopic chains. Our results indicate that both (p,2p) and (p,3p) reactions exhibit a systematic decrease of cross section and momentum width with increasing neutron excess, reflecting enhanced attenuation and surface bias induced by neutron skins. The effect is significantly stronger for two-proton removal, suggesting that (p,3p) reactions may offer enhanced sensitivity to isovector nuclear structure. These findings establish proton-induced knockout reactions as complementary hadronic probes of neutron skins and the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy.

# Neutron skins probed in proton knockout from neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim

The authors develop a unified probabilistic framework for (p,2p) and (p,3p) reactions and show that both cross sections and longitudinal momentum widths decrease systematically with increasing neutron excess, with the effect significantly stronger for two-proton removal; however, the calculated cross sections reproduce experimental data only to within a factor of 2–5 in most cases, and up to an order of magnitude in a few cases, indicating that the model captures trends but not absolute normalization.

## Method

The authors extend Glauber multiple-scattering theory with a probabilistic reaction propagator that allows different particle propagation directions after each binary collision, combining eikonal scattering waves for nucleon propagation with single-particle wavefunctions from Woods-Saxon potentials and microscopic nuclear densities from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using 22 Skyrme energy-density functionals. The inclusive (p,2p) and sequential (p,3p) cross sections are obtained by Monte-Carlo integration over impact parameters, collision coordinates, and scattering angles, with elastic proton-proton angular distributions from the SAID partial-wave analysis and medium-modified nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness values considered are typically 0.1–0.3 fm for heavy nuclei
- The ratio σ_pn ≈ 2–3 × σ_pp for energies in the range 200 MeV/nucleon to 1 GeV/nucleon
- Calculated cross sections differ from experimental data by about a factor of 2–5 in most cases, and up to an order of magnitude in a few cases
- Pronounced reductions of (p,3p) cross sections observed around A=82 and A=101
- The Woods-Saxon diffuseness parameter is fixed at a=0.65 fm
- The isoscalar potential depth V_0 = -57.8 MeV, spin-orbit strengths V_so0 = -22.0 MeV and V_so1 = 14.0 MeV
- Pairing strength v_0 = -258.2 MeV with saturation density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Cutoff energy for quasiparticles is E_max = 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends the probabilistic reaction framework previously applied to (p,2p) reactions by Aumann (2013) and Bertulani (2021) to sequential (p,3p) reactions, building on the experimental observation by Taniuchi (2019) that (p,3p) reactions can populate states unreachable via one-nucleon knockout. The formalism is simpler than the quantum-interference approach of Aumann (2013) but goes beyond standard Glauber models by allowing lateral scattering of protons between collisions. The results establish proton-induced knockout as a complementary hadronic probe of neutron skins, connecting to the extensive literature on neutron-skin constraints on the symmetry energy slope parameter L from Centelles (2009), Roca-Maza (2015), and Piekarewicz (2012), and to neutron-star structure correlations from Lattimer (2023) and Fattoyev (2018).
