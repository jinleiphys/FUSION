# Lagrangian with off-shell vertices and field redefinitions

> Meson exchange diagrams following from a lagrangian with off-shell meson-nucleon couplings are compared with those generated from conventional dynamics. The off-shell interactions can be transformed away with the help of a nucleon field redefinition. Contributions to to the $NN$- and $3N$-potentials and nonminimal contact e.m. meson-exchange currents are discussed, mostly for an important case of scalar meson exchange. (pacs 11.10.Lm, 13.75.Cs, 21.30.-x, 24.10.Jv)

## Key claim

Off-shell meson-nucleon couplings in a Lagrangian can be removed via a nucleon field redefinition, but the resulting transformed Lagrangian is equivalent only if all nonlinear terms are retained; in practical truncated calculations the two forms are not equivalent, and the off-shell parameter \(\nu_s\) still affects physical observables such as phase shifts and three-nucleon binding energy.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian containing a scalar-nucleon-nucleon (sNN) vertex with an off-shell extension parameterized by \(\nu_s\) (or \(a_s = \nu_s g_s / m\)). They perform a nucleon field redefinition \(\psi(x) = F_s(\Phi_s(x)) \psi'(x)\) with \(F_s = 1/\sqrt{1 + a_s \Phi_s}\) to transform the off-shell coupling into nonlinear contact interactions. They then expand the transformed Lagrangian to second order in the scalar field and derive explicit expressions for the resulting triangle and bubble diagrams contributing to the NN potential, the three-nucleon potential, and the electromagnetic meson-exchange current. The equivalence of the box and crossed-box diagrams from the original off-shell Lagrangian to the sum of conventional on-shell diagrams plus the contact diagrams is demonstrated algebraically.

## Key numbers

- The off-shell parameter \(a_s\) is related to \(\nu_s\) by \(a_s = \nu_s g_s / m\).
- The scalar meson mass is typically \(m_s \approx 500\) MeV.
- The quadratic contact vertex from the transformed Lagrangian is \(\Gamma^{ab}(q_2,q_1) = -2 g_s^2 (\nu_s/m) [\delta^{ab} + i \epsilon^{abc} \tau^c (\nu_s/8m)(\hat{q}_1 + \hat{q}_2)]\).
- The leading-order three-nucleon potential from the contact vertex is \(V_{3N} = 2 (\nu_s/m) g_s^4 D(k_1) D(k_2) \bar{u}(p'_1) \tau^a u(p_1) \bar{u}(p'_2) \tau^b u(p_2) \bar{u}(p'_3) [\delta^{ab} + i \epsilon^{cab} \tau^c (\nu_s/8m)(\hat{k}_1 - \hat{k}_2)] u(p_3) + \text{acyclic}\).
- The contact electromagnetic current contribution to the charge density in lowest order in \(v/c\) is \(\delta \rho_{s,con} \simeq (\nu_s g_s^2 / 2m) \{ \hat{e}_1, \vec{\tau}_1 \cdot \vec{\tau}_2 \} D_s(p'_2 - p_2)\).

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator formalism of Stadler and Gross (1997), who found that including off-shell scalar-nucleon couplings improved the triton binding energy and NN data fit. It also relates to the relativistic mean-field studies of Zimanyi and Moszkowski, who used derivative (off-shell) sNN couplings for nuclear matter. The paper shows that off-shell effects can be reinterpreted as specific triangle, bubble, and three-nucleon force contributions, enabling numerical comparison between models with standard on-shell vertices and those with off-shell extensions. Unlike the pseudoscalar case (where unitary freedom exists as studied by Friar, Gari, and others), the authors find no such freedom for scalar mesons, so the derived \(\nu_s\)-dependent potentials and currents are uniquely determined.
