# Solving the $R_{AA}\otimes v_2$ puzzle

> For the past ten years $R_{AA}(p_T)$, the nuclear modification factor that encodes the suppression of high $p_T$ particles due to energy loss within the medium was fairly well described by many theoretical models. However, the same models systematically under-predicted the high $p_T$ elliptic flow, $v_2$, which is experimentally measured as the correlation between soft and hard hadrons. All previous calculations neglected the effect of event-by-event fluctuations of an expanding viscous hydrodynamical background as well as the soft-hard flow harmonic correlations in the experimentally measured $v_2$. In this talk I show how event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics (computed using the v-USPhydro code) coupled to an energy loss model (BBMG) is able to simultaneously describe soft physics observables as well as the high-$p_T$ $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$. Suggestions for future more differential calculations at the LHC run2 are made to explore soft-hard flow correlations.

# Solving the $R_{AA}\otimes v_2$ puzzle

## Key claim
Event-by-event fluctuating viscous hydrodynamical backgrounds, when coupled with the proper calculation of soft-hard flow harmonic correlations in the experimentally measured $v_2$, can simultaneously describe soft physics observables and high-$p_T$ $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$, resolving a longstanding puzzle. The hedge is that this solution requires both full event-by-event hydrodynamically expanding backgrounds *and* the correct calculation of the soft-hard correlation, not just one ingredient alone.

## Method
The author coupled event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics (v-USPhydro code) with the BBMG energy loss model in a pQCD-like scenario. The soft $v_2^{soft}$ was taken from best-fitting parameters in the soft sector, while $v_2^{hard}$ was computed as the second Fourier coefficient of $R_{AA}(p_T,\phi)$. The experimentally measured $v_2^{exp}(p_T)$ was then obtained by correlating the soft integrated $v_2^{soft}$ and hard $v_2^{hard}(p_T)$ via the formula $v^{exp}_n(p_T)=\langle v^{soft}_n\,v_n^{hard}(p_T)\cos[n(\psi^{soft}_n-\psi^{hard}_n(p_T))]\rangle / \sqrt{\langle (v^{soft}_n)^2\rangle}$, simulating the two-particle (one soft, one hard) correlations used experimentally.

## Key numbers
- $v_2(p_T>10\ \mathrm{GeV})$: event-by-event calculations with MCKLN initial conditions produce larger $v_2$ than MCGlauber, matching the trend seen in the soft sector where MCKLN $v_2$ is always larger due to larger eccentricities.
- $R_{AA}$ at $\sqrt{s}=2.76\ \mathrm{TeV}$ Pb+Pb collisions: relatively insensitive to the effect of initial conditions and event-by-event fluctuations, in contrast to $v_2$.
- $v_3$ at high $p_T$: MCKLN produces smaller $\varepsilon_3$ than MCGlauber, correlating to a smaller $v_3$ both in the soft and hard sectors, demonstrating that event-by-event fluctuations are needed for nonzero $v_3$ at high $p_T$.

## Context
This work extends the breakthrough circa 2010 by Alver et al. (2010) that included event-by-event calculations for nonzero triangular flow measurements, and the subsequent success of event-by-event viscous hydrodynamical models in describing soft physics observables. Previous calculations by Betz et al. (2014) and Xu et al. (2014) had systematically under-predicted high-$p_T$ elliptic flow because they neglected event-by-event fluctuations and soft-hard flow harmonic correlations. The v-USPhydro+BBMG approach enables future more differential calculations at LHC Run 2, including event-shape engineering in the hard sector, studies of temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s(T)$ effects on high-$p_T$ flow, and inclusion of hard scattering effects within relativistic hydrodynamics to study the intermediate $p_T$ range.
