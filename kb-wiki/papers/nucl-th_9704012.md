# Constraints on the relativistic mean field of $Δ$-isobar in nuclear matter

> The effects of the presence of $Δ$-isobars in nuclear matter are studied in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory. The existence of stable nuclei at saturation density imposes constraints on the $Δ$-isobar self-energy and thereby on the mean-field coupling constants of the scalar and vector mesons with $Δ$-isobars. The range of possible values for the scalar and vector coupling constants of $Δ$-isobars with respect to the nucleon coupling is investigated and compared to recent predictions of QCD sum-rule calculations.

## Key claim

The presence of Δ-isobars in nuclear matter, treated in relativistic mean-field theory, imposes constraints on the scalar and vector meson coupling constants for Δ-isobars relative to nucleons; the paper derives a "window" of allowed ratios, and finds that existing QCD sum-rule predictions (Jin 1995) fall outside this window, implying they yield too strong a deviation of the Δ-isobar self-energy from the nucleon self-energy.

## Method

The authors extend the Walecka model (Quantum Hadron Dynamics) to include Δ-isobars as Rarita-Schwinger particles interacting with scalar σ and vector V mesons, with coupling constants g'_σ and g'_v for the Δ relative to the nucleon couplings g_σ and g_v. They solve the mean-field equations for symmetric nuclear matter in chemical equilibrium between nucleons and Δ-isobars, varying the ratios r_s = g'_σ/g_σ and r_v = g'_v/g_v, and examine the resulting equation of state (energy per baryon versus density). Constraints are imposed by requiring that no Δ-isobars appear at saturation density, that any mixed Δ-nucleon phase at higher density is only metastable (not the ground state), and that the scalar interaction is more attractive and the vector interaction less repulsive for Δ-isobars than for nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.167 fm⁻³, binding energy -15.8 MeV, compressibility 290 MeV (parameters from Nakai 1984).
- The "soft" restriction from requiring no Δ-isobars at saturation density: r_s ≤ 0.82 r_v + 0.71.
- The more restrictive constraint from requiring the mixed phase to be only metastable: r_s ≤ 1.01 r_v + 0.38.
- For the QCD sum-rule predictions (Jin 1995) with r_v ~ 0.4–0.5 and r_s ~ 1.3, the second minimum in the equation of state has a depth of about 280 MeV at a density of about 3 ρ₀, meaning the ground state would be Δ-isobar matter.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Δ-isobars in Quantum Hadron Dynamics (Boguta 1982, Wehrberger 1989, 1993) by systematically mapping the allowed range of Δ-meson coupling constants from phenomenological constraints. It directly compares to QCD finite-density sum-rule calculations (Jin 1995) which predicted a weaker vector self-energy and stronger scalar self-energy for Δ-isobars relative to nucleons, and finds those predictions incompatible with the existence of stable nuclei at saturation density. The results are relevant for transport models of heavy ion collisions (Metag 1993, Fuchs 1997) where Δ-isobars are a major source of meson production, and where the coupling strengths affect thresholds for meson production and the dynamics of resonance matter.
