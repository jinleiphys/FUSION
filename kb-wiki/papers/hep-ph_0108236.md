# Supersymmetry Structure and Phenomena

> A fairly non-technical introduction to and survey of supersymmetry phenomenology with pedagogical emphasis, including exercises. Suitable for both theorists and experimentalists. The manuscript appeared as a Springer-Verlag monograph in physics. The body of the manuscript is provided here with the publisher`s permission.

## Key claim

This monograph provides a pedagogical survey of supersymmetry phenomenology, arguing that weak-scale supersymmetry is a reasonable and likely perturbative extension of the Standard Model, but emphasizing that whether supersymmetry is realized in nature, and in what form, must be determined by experiment.

## Method

The text presents a "bottom-up" phenomenological construction of supersymmetry, beginning with a review of Standard Model ingredients and the hierarchy problem, then supersymmetrizing the Standard Model with attention to renormalization, radiative symmetry breaking, unification, and the Higgs boson mass. The discussion organizes the model space by possible resolutions of the flavor problem and explores specialized topics including neutrino mass generation, vacuum stability, classifications of supersymmetry breaking operators, and extended supersymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The electroweak scale is set by the Higgs vacuum expectation value ν = (√2 G_F)^(-1/2) = 246 GeV, extracted from the Fermi constant measured in muon decay.
- The W boson mass is M_W^2 = g^2 ν^2 / 4 = (80.419 ± 0.056 GeV)^2 and the Z boson mass is M_Z^2 = (g^2 + g'^2)ν^2 / 4 = (91.1882 ± 0.0022 GeV)^2.
- For a cut-off at the Planck scale ~10^19 GeV, the Higgs mass parameter ν = 246 GeV would require fine-tuning by 10^17 orders of magnitude (and ν^2 by 10^34 orders of magnitude).
- The Standard Model has 18 free parameters (in addition to its rank and representations): gauge couplings, number of chiral families, Higgs parameters, Yukawa couplings, and the weak scale ~gν.
- Current experimental energies are √s ≲ 200-300 GeV per parton.
- The LHC was scheduled to operate by the year 2005 (at the time of writing).

## Context

This monograph extends the lecture series "Essential Supersymmetry" from the TASI-98 school and provides a pedagogical alternative to more formal treatments such as Weinberg's textbook. It is intended to prepare readers for the LHC era by connecting ultraviolet theoretical possibilities to infrared experimental implications, and it references the Super-Kamiokande evidence for neutrino oscillations as the most direct evidence for physics beyond the Standard Model.
