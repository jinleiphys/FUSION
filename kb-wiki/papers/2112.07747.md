# Jet Charge: A new tool to probe the anomalous $Zb\bar{b}$ couplings at the EIC

> We propose to utilize the average jet charge weighted single-spin asymmetry $A_e^{bQ}$ in the neutral current DIS scattering with one $b$-tagged jet produced at the EIC to probe the $Zb\bar{b}$ interactions. This novel observable is sensitive to the axial-vector component of the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling and is complementary to the single-spin asymmetry measurement at the EIC and the $gg\to Zh$ cross section measurement at the HL-LHC in the determination of the $Zb\bar{b}$ couplings. We show that the apparent degeneracy of the $Zb\bar{b}$ couplings, implied by the LEP and SLC precision electroweak data, can be broken by the $A_e^{bQ}$ measurement at the EIC. With a large enough integrated luminosity collected at the EIC, the measurement of $A_e^{bQ}$ could also resolve the long-standing discrepancy of $A_{\rm FB}^b$ at the LEP, strongly dependent on the $b$-tagging efficiency.

# Jet Charge: A new tool to probe the anomalous Zbb couplings at the EIC

## Key claim

The average jet charge weighted single-spin asymmetry $A_e^{bQ}$ in neutral current deep-inelastic scattering with one $b$-tagged jet at the Electron-Ion Collider is sensitive to the axial-vector component of the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling, in contrast to the single-spin asymmetry which is sensitive only to the vector component; this observable can break the degeneracy in the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling parameter space implied by LEP and SLC precision electroweak data, but the required integrated luminosity to resolve the long-standing $A_{\rm FB}^b$ discrepancy depends strongly on the $b$-tagging efficiency.

## Method

The authors calculate the average jet charge weighted single-spin asymmetry $A_e^{bQ}$ for neutral current DIS with a polarized electron beam (18 GeV) scattering off an unpolarized proton beam (275 GeV) at the EIC, requiring one $b$-tagged jet with $p_T^j > 5$ GeV and rapidity $-2 < \eta_j < 4$ using jet size $R=1$ and parameter $\kappa=0.3$. The cross sections are computed at leading order with the sACOT-$\chi$ scheme for heavy flavor contributions, using CT14LO parton distribution functions and two benchmark $b$-tagging efficiency scenarios: (i) $\epsilon_b^b=0.7$, $\epsilon_c^b=0.03$, $\epsilon_q^b=0.001$ and (ii) $\epsilon_b^b=0.5$, $\epsilon_c^b=0.2$, $\epsilon_q^b=0.01$. The non-perturbative jet charge parameters are obtained from PYTHIA simulations, and the NLO inclusive jet cross section is approximated as the product of the LO differential cross section and the NLO jet function.

## Key numbers

- For tagging efficiency scenario (i) with $\mathcal{L}=100$ fb$^{-1}$: $A_e^{bQ}=0.008$ in the Standard Model, with statistical uncertainty $\delta A_e^{bQ}/A_e^{bQ}=5\%$; total $b$-tagged cross section $\sigma_b^{\rm tot}=77$ pb.
- For tagging efficiency scenario (ii) with $\mathcal{L}=100$ fb$^{-1}$: $A_e^{bQ}=-0.007$ in the Standard Model, with statistical uncertainty $\delta A_e^{bQ}/A_e^{bQ}=3\%$; total $b$-tagged cross section $\sigma_b^{\rm tot}=417$ pb.
- Minimal integrated luminosity to exclude the degeneracy parameter space with $\kappa_{V,A}<0$ at 68% C.L.: scenario (i) $\mathcal{L}>0.03$ fb$^{-1}$, scenario (ii) $\mathcal{L}>1.5$ fb$^{-1}$.
- Minimal integrated luminosity to resolve the degeneracy with $\kappa_{V,A}>0$ (the solution $(\kappa_V,\kappa_A)=(1.46,0.67)$): scenario (i) $\mathcal{L}>0.6$ fb$^{-1}$, scenario (ii) $\mathcal{L}>36.4$ fb$^{-1}$.
- Minimal integrated luminosity to exclude the LEP $A_{\rm FB}^b$ measurement through WSSA: scenario (i) $\mathcal{L}>122$ fb$^{-1}$, scenario (ii) $\mathcal{L}>8583$ fb$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Yan, Yuan, and collaborators (2021) on single-spin asymmetry at the EIC, which was shown to be sensitive only to the vector component of the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling, and complements the $gg\to Zh$ cross section measurement at the HL-LHC (Yan, 2021) which constrains the axial-vector component. The proposed observable addresses the well-known two-fold degeneracy in the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling parameter space from LEP and SLC precision data (Choudhury, 2001) and could help resolve the $2.1\sigma$ discrepancy in the bottom quark forward-backward asymmetry $A_{\rm FB}^b$ at LEP (Particle Data Group, 2020). The jet charge technique builds on theoretical developments by Krohn (2012), Waalewijn (2012), and Li (2019) for flavor tagging and quark-anti-quark separation.
