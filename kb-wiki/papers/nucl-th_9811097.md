# Equation of state for finite nuclei and liquid-gas phase transition

> We construct the equation of state (EOS) of finite nuclei including surface and Coulomb effects in a Thomas-Fermi framework using a finite range, momentum and density dependent two-body interaction. We identify critical temperatures for nuclei below which the EOS so constructed shows clear signals for liquid-gas phase transition in these finite systems. Comparison with the EOS of infinite nuclear matter shows that the critical density and temperature of the phase transition in nuclei are influenced by the mentioned finite size effects.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an equation of state (EOS) for finite nuclei that includes surface and Coulomb effects, and finds that these finite-size effects significantly reduce the critical temperature and pressure and increase the critical volume for the liquid-gas phase transition compared to infinite nuclear matter, with the exact values depending on the specific nucleus.

## Method

The authors use a Thomas-Fermi (TF) framework with a finite-range, momentum- and density-dependent Seyler-Blanchard type effective interaction to calculate the energy density profile of a nucleus at a given temperature. The density profiles of neutrons and protons are determined self-consistently for different confining volumes at fixed temperature, from which the free energy and then the pressure are obtained via \(P = -(\partial F/\partial V)_T\) to construct isotherms. The EOS of infinite symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter is also calculated for comparison using the same interaction.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for infinite symmetric nuclear matter: \(T_c = 14.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Critical temperature for infinite asymmetric nuclear matter with asymmetry \(X = (N-Z)/A = 0.16\): \(T_c = 14.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Critical temperature for the finite nucleus \(^{85}\mathrm{Kr}\): \(T_c = 11.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Critical temperature for the finite nucleus \(^{150}\mathrm{Sm}\): \(T_c = 11.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Saturation density of nuclear matter at zero temperature: \(\rho_0 = 0.153\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- Nuclear matter incompressibility calculated from the interaction: 238 MeV.
- The zero-temperature nuclear radius parameter used: \(r_0 = 1.16\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends self-consistent mean-field calculations of the nuclear EOS from infinite nuclear matter (Jaqaman et al., 1983; Bandyopadhyay et al., 1990) to finite nuclei, addressing a gap noted in the literature. The results are directly relevant to interpreting experimental signals of liquid-gas phase transitions, such as the caloric curve data from GSI for Au+Au at 600 AMeV (Pochodzalla et al., 1995) and the EOS collaboration data for Au+C at 1 A GeV (Hauger et al., 1996; Ellis et al., 1996). The findings enable more realistic modeling of fragment mass distributions in statistical models by providing a physically motivated lower bound for the "freeze-out" volume.
