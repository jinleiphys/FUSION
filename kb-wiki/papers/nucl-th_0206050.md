# Local scale transformations and extended matter distributions in nuclei

> Local scale transformations are made to vary the long range properties of harmonic oscillator orbitals conventionally used in model structure calculations of nuclear systems. The transformations ensure that those oscillator states asymptotically have exponentially decaying forms consistent with chosen single nucleon binding energies, leaving the structure essentially unchanged within the body of the nucleus. Application has been made to the radioactive nuclei 6,8He and 11Li and the resulting wave functions are used to generate g-folding optical potentials for elastic scattering of those ions from hydrogen. As a consistency test, application has been made to form wave functions for 40Ca and they have been used also to specify relevant proton-40Ca optical potentials with which elastic scattering has been predicted. The need for appropriate specifications of single particle binding energies in exotic nuclei is discussed.

## Key claim

Local scale transformations (LSTs) can modify harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions to have asymptotically exponential tails consistent with chosen single-nucleon binding energies, while leaving the interior structure essentially unchanged; this provides a less artificial alternative to Woods-Saxon potentials for describing halo nuclei, though the authors stress the calculated matter densities are exploratory and not "final" determinations.

## Method

The authors apply a local scale transformation of the form \( r = f(s) \) to harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions, using a harmonic mean interpolation between the linear regime \( f(r)=r \) at small radii and the asymptotic regime \( f(r)=\gamma\sqrt{r} \) at large radii, where \( \gamma \) is determined by the chosen single-particle binding energy. They use shell-model one-body density matrix elements from large-space calculations (up to \( 4\hbar\omega \) for \( ^{6,8}\text{He} \) and \( 2\hbar\omega \) for \( ^{11}\text{Li} \)) and apply the LST with order parameter \( m=8 \) to generate transformed wave functions, which are then used to construct \( g \)-folding optical potentials for elastic proton scattering from these nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For \( ^{6}\text{He} \): LST model gives a mass rms radius of 3.21 fm (HO: 2.44 fm) and a reaction cross section of 441 mb at 40 MeV (HO: 321 mb; experimental value \( 410\pm21 \) mb at 36.2 MeV)
- For \( ^{8}\text{He} \): LST model gives a mass rms radius of 2.66 fm (HO: 2.55 fm) and a reaction cross section of 293 mb at 71 MeV (HO: 280 mb)
- For \( ^{11}\text{Li} \): LST model gives a mass rms radius of 3.99 fm (HO: 2.38 fm) and a reaction cross section of 447 mb at 62 MeV (HO: 343 mb)
- The neutron rms radius for \( ^{6}\text{He} \) from LST is 3.58 fm (HO: 2.59 fm); for \( ^{11}\text{Li} \) it is 4.45 fm (HO: 2.46 fm)
- For \( ^{40}\text{Ca} \), the LST was applied as a consistency test to form wave functions and predict proton-\( ^{40}\text{Ca} \) elastic scattering

## Context

This work extends the local scale transformation formalism of Petkov and Stoitsov (1983) and Stoitsov et al. (1998) to describe exotic light nuclei near the drip lines, specifically \( ^{6,8}\text{He} \) and \( ^{11}\text{Li} \). It builds on shell-model calculations by Navratil and Barrett (1996, 1998) using Reid93-based \( G \) matrices and by Karataglidis et al. (2000) using the Nijmegen III interaction, as well as earlier analyses of proton scattering from these nuclei that used Woods-Saxon wave functions. The approach enables a consistent treatment of single-particle binding energies for all orbits simultaneously, which is not possible with a single Woods-Saxon potential parametrization, and provides wave functions suitable for folding-model analyses of elastic scattering data from radioactive beam experiments.
