# A unitary correlation operator method

> The short range repulsion between nucleons is treated by a unitary correlation operator which shifts the nucleons away from each other whenever their uncorrelated positions are within the replusive core. By formulating the correlation as a transformation of the relative distance between particle pairs, general analytic expressions for the correlated wave functions and correlated operators are given. The decomposition of correlated operators into irreducible n-body operators is discussed. The one- and two-body-irreducible parts are worked out explicitly and the contribution of three-body correlations is estimated to check convergence. Ground state energies of nuclei up to mass number A=48 are calculated with a spin-isospin-dependent potential and single Slater determinants as uncorrelated states. They show that the deduced energy- and mass-number-independent correlated two-body Hamiltonian reproduces all "exact" many-body calculations surprisingly well.

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) provides a state-independent unitary transformation that removes the short-range repulsive core from the nucleon-nucleon interaction, producing a correlated Hamiltonian whose irreducible three-body contributions remain small enough that a two-body approximation yields ground-state energies in good agreement with exact many-body calculations for nuclei up to mass number A=48.

## Method

The authors construct a unitary correlator as an exponential of a two-body generator that shifts nucleons apart along their relative coordinate whenever their uncorrelated distance falls within the repulsive core region. The correlator is expressed as a coordinate transformation R±(r) of the relative distance, and the correlated Hamiltonian is obtained by applying this unitary transformation to the original Hamiltonian. The correlated operator is then decomposed into irreducible n-body operators via a cluster expansion, with the one- and two-body-irreducible parts worked out explicitly and the three-body contributions estimated to check convergence.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies are calculated for nuclei from A=2 up to A=48 using a spin-isospin-dependent potential with single Slater determinants as uncorrelated states.
- The correlation volume Vc is defined as Vc = 4π ∫₀^∞ dr [r − R₋(r) √(R₋′(r))]², with R₋(r) being the coordinate transformation that maps the correlated relative distance back to the uncorrelated one.
- The coordinate transformation R±(r) satisfies ∫_r^{R±} dξ / s(ξ) = ±1, where s(r) is the shift function appearing in the generator of the unitary transformation.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing idea of using unitary transformations to treat short-range correlations in nuclear many-body systems, previously explored by Providencia and Shakin (1964), Baker and Kahana (1973), and Suzuki, Okamoto, and Kuo (1994). The UCOM approach combines advantages of Jastrow-type correlation methods and effective operator techniques, enabling the use of a single energy- and mass-number-independent correlated two-body Hamiltonian across many nuclei. The method enables future calculations of spectra and transitions in a shell-model framework, which was not straightforward with earlier Jastrow-type approaches.
