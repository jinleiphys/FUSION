# Projectile $Δ$ Excitations in $p(p,n)Nπ$ Reactions

> It has recently been proven from measurements of the spin-transfer coefficients $D_{xx}$ and $D_{zz}$ that there is a small but non-vanishing $ΔS=0$ component $σ_{0}$, in the inclusive $p(p,n)Nπ\,$ reaction cross section $σ\,$. It is shown that the dominant part of the measured $σ_{0}$ can be explained in terms of the projectile $Δ$ excitation mechanism. An estimate is further made of contributions to $σ_{0}$ from s-wave rescattering process. It is found that s-wave rescattering contribution is much smaller than the contribution coming from projectile $Δ$ excitation mechanism. The addition of s-wave rescattering contribution to the dominant part, however, improves the fit to the data.

# Projectile Δ Excitations in p(p,n)Nπ Reactions

## Key claim

The measured ΔS=0 component σ₀ of the inclusive p(p,n)Nπ reaction cross section, which is small (approximately 2.5% of the total cross section in the resonance region), can be explained primarily by the projectile Δ excitation mechanism, with a smaller contribution from s-wave rescattering that improves the fit to data, particularly in the off-resonance region.

## Method

The authors calculate the zero-degree inclusive cross section σ and the no-spin-transfer component σ₀ for the p(p,n)Nπ reaction at 795 MeV by constructing a transition amplitude that includes both projectile and target Δ excitation processes (treated with NN→NΔ transition amplitudes and Δ decay Hamiltonians) and s-wave rescattering processes (treated with NNπ coupling and Nπ→Nπ s-wave amplitudes). The Δ propagator includes a width Γ(sᵢ) and a πNN form factor with cutoff Λ = 1200 MeV is used. The spin-transfer coefficients D_xx and D_zz from experimental data are used to extract σ₀ via the relation σ₀ = (1/4)σ(1 + 2D_xx + D_zz).

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for the Δ decay Hamiltonian is f*²/4π = 0.36
- The NNπ coupling constant is f²/4π = 0.08
- The s-wave Nπ→Nπ coupling constants are λ₁ = 0.0075 and λ₂ = 0.0528
- The πNN form factor cutoff is Λ = 1200 MeV
- At the resonance region, the experimental ratio R = σ₀/σ is approximately 0.025, meaning σ₀ contributes about 2.5% of the total inclusive cross section
- For the deuteron target, the R-values are larger by a factor of 2 to 4 compared to the proton target, consistent with the predicted factor of about 3 from the projectile Δ excitation mechanism
- The incident proton energy is E_p = 795 MeV

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the p(p,n)Nπ reaction at intermediate energies, which had been studied experimentally by Glass et al. (1977, 1983), Shimizu et al. (1982), Wicklund et al. (1987), and Prout et al. (1993), and theoretically by Jain and Santra (1993), Silbar et al. (1982), Mizutani et al. (1993), and Ray (1994). The projectile Δ excitation process had been difficult to test because it gives only a small correction to the dominant target Δ excitation cross section, but the spin-transfer measurements now allow extraction of the ΔS=0 component. The authors show that an alternative explanation for σ₀ from target Δ excitation using the NN→ΔN operator determined by Ray (1994) fails to fit the data, being too large by about a factor of 2 and having the wrong energy dependence. This work enables future studies of Δ excitation mechanisms in nuclear targets, where the R-values for d, ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb are found to be similar to each other and larger than for the proton target.
