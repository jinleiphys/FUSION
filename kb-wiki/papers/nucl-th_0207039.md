# On the equivalence of pairing correlations and intrinsic vortical currents in rotating nuclei

> The present paper establishes a link between pairing correlations in rotating nuclei and collective vortical modes in the intrinsic frame. We show that the latter can be embodied by a simple S-type coupling a la Chandrasekhar between rotational and intrinsic vortical collective modes. This results from a comparison between the solutions of microscopic calculations within the HFB and the HF Routhian formalisms. The HF Routhian solutions are constrained to have the same Kelvin circulation expectation value as the HFB ones. It is shown in several mass regions, pairing regimes, and for various spin values that this procedure yields moments of inertia, angular velocities, and current distributions which are very similar within both formalisms. We finally present perspectives for further studies.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the dynamical effects of pairing correlations in rotating nuclei can be quantitatively reproduced by imposing a specific vortical collective flow (the S-ellipsoid coupling a la Chandrasekhar) onto Hartree-Fock calculations, provided the Kelvin circulation expectation value is constrained to match that of the full Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov solution.

## Method

The authors compare two microscopic approaches for rotating nuclei: Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with a Skyrme effective force and seniority pairing, and Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with a double constraint on both angular momentum and Kelvin circulation (denoted HF+V). The HF+V solutions are constrained to have the same total angular momentum and total Kelvin circulation expectation values as the HFB solutions. Calculations are performed for three nuclei (150Gd, 192Hg, 254No) using the SkM* Skyrme parameterization, with Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection applied for 150Gd and 192Hg.

## Key numbers

- For 150Gd: the HF+V and HFB axial quadrupole moments remain constant over the entire band, differing by less than 2 percent around Q20 = 3900 fm^2
- For 150Gd: the HFB+LN dynamical moment of inertia reproduces the experimental decrease along the band, with theoretical values close to experimental ones; the kinetic moment of inertia agrees well with experiment when assuming I_init = 34 hbar rather than 32 hbar
- For 192Hg: the HF+V and HFB+LN axial quadrupole moments differ by less than 3 percent with a mean value of Q20 = 44 barns
- For 150Gd: the Kelvin circulation mean value in the HF case is about -1 hbar for protons and about +2 hbar for neutrons; pairing correlations shift it down by about 2 hbar per charge state at low angular velocities and by 0.5 hbar at the end of the band
- For 150Gd: the two rotational states studied correspond to I = 32 hbar (Omega = 381 keV) and I = 46 hbar (Omega = 516 keV)
- For 254No: the rotational ground-state band has been observed from I = 4 hbar up to I = 20 hbar
- Pairing strengths used: for 150Gd, G_n = 14.4 and G_p = 14.4; for 192Hg, G_n = 11.5 and G_p = 11.5; for 254No, G_n = 13.8 and G_p = 15.0

## Context

This work extends the conjecture from Laftchiev et al. (1999) that pairing correlations in rotating nuclei could be pictured as a vortical intrinsic collective motion coupled to global rotation, building on the S-ellipsoid fluid dynamics studied by Chandrasekhar for self-gravitating objects. The results confirm earlier semiclassical calculations by Durand et al. (1985) showing current patterns similar to S-ellipsoids in paired rotating solutions, and extend the work of Bonche et al. (1996) on HFB calculations for 150Gd. The approach enables a simpler description of pairing effects in rotating nuclei without full HFB calculations, and opens the way to particle-number-conserving Routhian calculations and a simple model correlating Kelvin circulation with angular velocity.
