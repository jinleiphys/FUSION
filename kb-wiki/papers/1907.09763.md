# Non-adiabatic transitions and non-equilibrium statistics of deforming nuclei

> We establish a connection between macroscopic "heating or cooling" of a finite many-body quantum system and the non-adiabatic Landau-Zener-Stückelberg transitions between its quantum states. We have considered the well-known Nilsson model for describing the single-particle states of nuclei and subject the system to a random walk in the deformation space. This subsumes modelling of an evolving many-body system where the dynamics is chaotic. We discover a universality in the distribution of final "temperatures", beginning with a canonical equilibrium at some temperature $T$. The quantum system is thrown out of equilibrium where free energy and work undergo fluctuations. These fluctuations are shown to respect Jarzynski inequality, and, the Bochkov-Kuzovlev equalities. We believe that this study will pave the way towards understanding non-equlibrium phenomena in other finite quantum systems like metallic clusters, quantum dots, and others.

# Non-adiabatic transitions and non-equilibrium statistics of deforming nuclei

## Key claim
The authors establish a connection between macroscopic heating (or cooling) of a finite many-body quantum system and non-adiabatic Landau-Zener-Stückelberg transitions between its quantum states, specifically for the Nilsson model of nuclear single-particle states. The claim is hedged by noting that the results are demonstrated for a specific model and may not apply in letter to all many-body systems, though the authors believe they are relevant in spirit.

## Method
The authors consider the Nilsson model for single-particle states of nuclei and subject the system to a Gaussian random walk of 1000 steps in deformation space. At each step, Landau-Zener-Stückelberg transition probabilities are calculated between adjacent energy levels, and nucleons initially distributed according to a canonical distribution at temperature T are redistributed according to these probabilities. After 1000 such random walks, a Boltzmann-like temperature is extracted by fitting the final distribution, and this procedure is repeated for 1000 realizations for various mass numbers A at initial temperatures of 2 MeV and 5 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperatures considered: 2 MeV and 5 MeV for various mass numbers A = 20, 40, 60, 100, 200
- For the universality scaling, initial temperatures of 1, 2, 3.5, and 5 MeV were used
- The scaling constant c in the universal distribution exp(-cT/T_i) is approximately 10, with an additive constant d approximately 8.3
- For the Jarzynski equality test at 2 MeV: ⟨exp(-βW)⟩ = 1.0001 and exp(-βΔF) = 1.0011 for A=20; values remain near unity up to A=200 (1.0214 and 1.0368 respectively)
- For the Jarzynski equality test at 5 MeV: ⟨exp(-βW)⟩ = 1.0077 and exp(-βΔF) = 1.0117 for A=20; at A=100, ⟨exp(-βW)⟩ = 995.0000 and exp(-βΔF) = 50.8406
- For the Bochkov-Kuzovlev equality (cyclic process) at 2 MeV: ⟨exp(-βW)⟩ ranges from 1.0000 (A=20) to 0.9895 (A=200)
- For the Bochkov-Kuzovlev equality at 5 MeV: ⟨exp(-βW)⟩ ranges from 0.9999 (A=20) to 0.9176 (A=200)

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of energy diffusion in evolving many-body systems by Jain (1999, 2000) and connects to the broader framework of non-equilibrium statistical mechanics established by Jarzynski (1997) and Bochkov and Kuzovlev (1981). The authors use the Nilsson model (Nilsson, 1955) for nuclear single-particle states, which has been foundational in nuclear structure physics. The study enables understanding of non-equilibrium phenomena in other finite quantum systems such as metallic clusters, quantum dots, and ultracold atomic systems, and relates to discussions of mass parameters in large amplitude collective motion (Jain, 2012).
