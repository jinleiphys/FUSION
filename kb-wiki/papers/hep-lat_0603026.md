# Electromagnetic and spin polarisabilities in lattice QCD

> We discuss the extraction of the electromagnetic and spin polarisabilities of nucleons from lattice QCD. We show that the external field method can be used to measure all the electromagnetic and spin polarisabilities including those of charged particles. We then turn to the extrapolations required to connect such calculations to experiment in the context of finite volume chiral perturbation theory. We derive results relevant for lattice simulations of QCD, partially-quenched QCD and quenched QCD. Our results for the polarisabilities show a strong dependence on the lattice volume and quark masses, typically differing from the infinite volume limit by ~10% for current lattice volumes and quark masses.

## Key claim

The authors show that the external field method can be used in lattice QCD to measure all six electromagnetic and spin polarisabilities of nucleons, including those of charged particles, but the extracted values will require careful chiral and finite-volume extrapolations because the polarisabilities typically differ from the infinite-volume limit by approximately 10% for current lattice volumes and quark masses.

## Method

The authors derive the quark mass and finite-volume dependence of nucleon electromagnetic and spin polarisabilities using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) at next-to-leading order. They extend the analysis to partially-quenched and quenched QCD, providing analytic expressions for the volume dependence of the polarisabilities in a periodic box. The results are obtained by calculating chiral loop corrections to the Compton scattering amplitude in finite volume and evaluating the resulting finite-volume functions.

## Key numbers

- The electric polarisability of the proton is α_p = 12.0 ± 0.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and the magnetic polarisability is β_p = 1.9 ∓ 0.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (experimental values quoted from Schumacher (2005))
- The electric polarisability of the neutron is α_n = 12.5 ± 1.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and the magnetic polarisability is β_n = 2.7 ∓ 1.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (experimental values quoted from Schumacher (2005))
- The polarisabilities typically differ from the infinite-volume limit by approximately 10% for current lattice volumes and quark masses
- At the lightest quark masses used in existing quenched lattice simulations, m_π ≈ 0.5 GeV, the volume sensitivity is as large as 10%

## Context

This work extends the external field method previously applied in quenched lattice QCD by Fiebig (1988), Christensen (2004), and Lee (2005) to extract electric and magnetic polarisabilities, showing how the same technique can measure spin polarisabilities and the polarisabilities of charged hadrons. The chiral perturbation theory analysis provides the model-independent framework needed to extrapolate lattice results to the physical quark masses and infinite volume, which is essential because current lattice calculations use quark masses larger than those in nature and finite volumes. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations to make physical predictions for all six nucleon polarisabilities by performing the necessary chiral and volume extrapolations.
