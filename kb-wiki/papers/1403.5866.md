# Superdeformed $Λ$ hypernuclei from relativistic mean field models

> We study the superdeformed (SD) states and corresponding SD hypernuclei of Ar isotopes with the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) models which can accommodate various shape degree of freedom. %The hyperon-nucleon $σ$-$Λ$ and $ω$-$Λ$ interactions are introduced %in the same covariant framework. We found that the density profiles of SD states in Ar isotopes show a strong localization with a ring structure near the surface, while the central part of the density is dilute showing a hole structure. This localization of SD density induces an appreciable deformation in the hyperon wave function and results in a large overlap between the core and the hyperon in the SD hypernuclei of Ar isotopes. Then the $Λ$ separation energy of SD state becomes larger than that of normally deformed or spherical ground state. This feature is different from that found in other nuclei such as $^{32}$S, $^{56}$Ni, and $^{60}$Zn in which the $Λ$ separation energy of larger deformed state is smaller. In this context, the measurement of the $Λ$ separation energy may provide an important information on the localization of the density profile of SD states.

# Superdeformed $\Lambda$ hypernuclei from relativistic mean field models

## Key claim

The $\Lambda$ separation energy in superdeformed (SD) states of Ar isotopes is larger than that in normally deformed or spherical ground states, contrary to the pattern found in lighter nuclei such as $^{32}$S, $^{56}$Ni, and $^{60}$Zn. This reversal occurs only when the SD core density exhibits a localized ring-shaped structure near the surface with a dilute central hole, which induces an appreciable deformation in the hyperon wave function and a larger core-hyperon overlap.

## Method

The authors used the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) model with the PK1 parameter set for nucleon-nucleon interactions and the PK1-Y1 set for $\Lambda$-N interactions, including scalar, vector, and tensor couplings. The Dirac equations for nucleons and hyperons and the Klein-Gordon equations for mesons were solved in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis, with pairing treated via the BCS approximation using a finite-range separable pairing interaction. The energy surfaces of $^{36}$Ar, $^{38}$Ar, $^{40}$Ar and their corresponding $\Lambda$ hypernuclei were calculated as functions of the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$, and the overlap integral between core and hyperon densities was computed to explain the $\Lambda$ separation energy differences.

## Key numbers

- $^{36}$Ar ground state: $\beta_2 = -0.212$, binding energy $E = -303.802$ MeV; SD state: $\beta_2 = 0.620$, $E = -296.670$ MeV
- $_{\Lambda}^{37}$Ar ground state: $S_\Lambda = 18.177$ MeV; SD state: $S_\Lambda = 18.524$ MeV (SD state has larger separation energy)
- $_{\Lambda}^{39}$Ar ground state: $S_\Lambda = 18.441$ MeV; SD state: $S_\Lambda = 18.858$ MeV
- $_{\Lambda}^{41}$Ar ground state: $S_\Lambda = 18.785$ MeV; SD state: $S_\Lambda = 19.070$ MeV
- $_{\Lambda}^{41}$Ca ground state: $S_\Lambda = 18.553$ MeV; SD state: $S_\Lambda = 19.242$ MeV (SD larger)
- $_{\Lambda}^{33}$S ground state: $S_\Lambda = 18.093$ MeV; SD state: $S_\Lambda = 16.364$ MeV (SD smaller)
- $_{\Lambda}^{57}$Ni ground state: $S_\Lambda = 21.906$ MeV; SD state: $S_\Lambda = 20.718$ MeV (SD smaller)
- $_{\Lambda}^{61}$Zn ground state: $S_\Lambda = 21.641$ MeV; SD state: $S_\Lambda = 20.930$ MeV (SD smaller)
- Overlap $I_\mathrm{overlap}$ for $_{\Lambda}^{37}$Ar: 0.1352 (ground state), 0.1370 (SD state)
- $\Lambda$ hyperon deformation $\beta_\Lambda$ in $_{\Lambda}^{37}$Ar SD state: 0.172
- Root mean square radius change in SD states does not exceed 1%

## Context

This work extends the study of $\Lambda$ hypernuclei from $sd$-shell nuclei into the superdeformed regime near $A \sim 40$, where SD states have been observed experimentally in $^{36}$Ar, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{44}$Ti by Svensson (2001), Ideguchi (2001), and O'Leary (2000). The result that SD $\Lambda$ separation energies can be larger than ground-state values contradicts the pattern found in lighter clustering states by Hiyama (2012) and Zhang (2012) using few-body models, and in SD states of Ca and Sc isotopes by Isaka (2014) using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). The localization of SD densities was previously reported by Ebran (2012, 2013) in the context of clusterization in mean field models. The present work enables experimental tests: measurement of $\Lambda$ separation energies could distinguish between localized and non-localized SD density profiles, and the contradictory AMD results for $^{37}_{\Lambda}$Ar by Isaka (2014) highlight the need for further theoretical and experimental investigation.
