# The Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly without rho-omega mixing

> We examine the effect of isospin-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants and of $π$-$η$ mixing on the binding-energy differences of mirror nuclei in a model that possesses no contribution from $ρ$-$ω$ mixing. The ${}^{3}$He-${}^{3}$H binding-energy difference is computed in a nonrelativistic approach using a realistic wave function. We find the ${}^{3}$He-${}^{3}$H binding-energy difference very sensitive to the short-distance behavior of the nucleon-nucleon potential. We conclude that for the typically hard Bonn form factors such models can not account for the observed binding-energy difference in the three-nucleon system. For the medium-mass region (A=15--41) the binding-energy differences of mirror nuclei are computed using a relativistic mean-field approximation to the Walecka model. We obtain large binding-energy differences---of the order of several hundred keV---arising from the pseudoscalar sector. Two effects are primarily responsible for this new finding: a) the inclusion of isospin breaking in the pion-nucleon coupling constant, and b) the in-medium enhancement of the small components of the bound-state wave functions. We look for off-shell ambiguities in these results and find them to be large.

# Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly without rho-omega mixing

## Key claim

The authors propose that isospin-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants combined with pi-eta mixing can generate binding-energy differences in mirror nuclei comparable to those previously attributed to rho-omega mixing, but only if the short-distance behavior of the nucleon-nucleon potential is sufficiently soft; for the typically hard Bonn form factors, the model cannot account for the observed binding-energy difference in the three-nucleon system.

## Method

The authors compute the He-3/H-3 binding-energy difference using a nonrelativistic approach with a realistic three-body wave function from a variational analysis of the Reid soft-core potential, evaluating the singlet S-0 component of charge-symmetry-breaking potentials derived from pi-eta mixing and isospin-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants. For medium-mass mirror nuclei (A=15-41), they use a relativistic mean-field approximation to the Walecka model, computing binding-energy differences in the Hartree-Fock approximation with pseudoscalar (pion and eta) exchange contributions.

## Key numbers

- Experimental He-3/H-3 binding-energy difference: 764 keV
- Electromagnetic contribution to He-3/H-3 binding-energy difference: 693 +/- 19 +/- 5 keV, leaving 71 +/- 19 +/- 5 keV to be explained by charge-symmetry-breaking effects
- Pi-eta mixing amplitude used: -4200 MeV^2
- Isospin-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant (g_0^pi): approximately 0.004, derived from quark mass difference Delta m = 4.1 MeV with average constituent quark mass m = 313 MeV
- With unrealistically soft form factors (Lambda_NNpi(eta) = 0.6 GeV, Lambda_NNrho(omega) = 0.85 GeV), the model accounts for only +40 keV of the He-3/H-3 binding-energy difference
- Pseudoscalar sector accounts for about 70-85% of the discrepancy between nonrelativistic theory and experiment for A=15-41 mirror nuclei
- Fock term generates a 30-40% exchange correction to the direct Hartree contribution for rho-omega mixing in medium-mass nuclei

## Context

This work extends the search for alternate charge-symmetry-breaking mechanisms following the suggestion by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1992) that rho-omega mixing is suppressed at small momentum transfers, a finding confirmed by Piekarewicz and Williams (1993), Krein et al. (1993), and others. The authors build on their own previous work (Gardner, Haidenbauer, and Piekarewicz, 1995) on isospin violation in meson-nucleon vertices, and contrast their results with nonrelativistic calculations by Blunden and Iqbal (1987), Coon and collaborators (1987), and Ishikawa and Sasakawa (1990) who found rho-omega mixing dominant. The study enables future work to focus on determining the short-distance structure of the nucleon-nucleon potential and the cutoff parameters in meson-nucleon form factors, which remain poorly understood.
