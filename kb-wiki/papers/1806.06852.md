# Dipole Polarizabilities of $π^{\pm}$-Mesons

> The main experimental works, where dipole polarizabilities of charged pions have been determined, are considered. Possible reasons for the differences between the experimental data are discussed. In particular, it is shown that the account of the $σ$-meson gives a significant correction to the value of the polarizability obtained in the latest experiment of the COMPASS collaboration. We present also new fit results for the $(γγ\toππ)$ reaction.

## Key claim
The COMPASS collaboration's result for the charged pion dipole polarizability, which agrees with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions, is highly model-dependent because it neglects the contribution of the σ-meson and does not properly account for the interference between Coulomb and nuclear amplitudes in the Primakoff analysis.

## Method
The authors review experimental determinations of charged pion dipole polarizabilities from three processes: radiative pion scattering off heavy nuclei via the Primakoff effect (Serpukhov and COMPASS), radiative pion photoproduction from protons (MAMI and Lebedev), and two-photon production of pion pairs (γγ→ππ). They calculate the σ-meson contribution to the Compton scattering amplitude on pions using dispersion relations with the σ-meson treated as a pole on the second Riemann sheet, with parameters Mσ=441 MeV, Γσ=544 MeV, Γσγγ=1.98 keV, and gσππ=3.31 GeV. They also perform a new global fit to the γγ→π+π− cross section data up to 2500 MeV using dispersion relations saturated by meson exchanges in s and t channels.

## Context
This work reviews and critiques the experimental determinations of charged pion polarizabilities, which are fundamental structure parameters sensitive to different theoretical models. The authors extend their previous dispersion relation analysis (Fil'kov and Kashevarov, 2006) by including the σ-meson as a pole on the second Riemann sheet, and they argue that the COMPASS result (2007) contradicts the Serpukhov result (1983) and the MAMI result (2005) due to neglected σ-meson contributions and improper treatment of Coulomb-nuclear interference. The analysis enables future experiments to properly account for the σ-meson contribution and to resolve the discrepancy between dispersion sum rule predictions and chiral perturbation theory calculations (Gasser and Leutwyler).
