# Nuclear tetrahedral states and high-spin states studied using quantum number projection method

> We have recently developed an efficient method of performing the full quantum number projection from the most general mean-field (HFB type) wave functions including the angular momentum, parity as well as the proton and neutron particle numbers. With this method, we have been investigating several nuclear structure mechanisms. In this report, we discuss the obtained quantum rotational spectra of the tetrahedral nuclear states formulating certain experimentally verifiable criteria, of the high-spin states, focussing on the wobbling- and chiral-bands, and of the drip-line nuclei as illustrative examples.

# Nuclear tetrahedral states and high-spin states studied using quantum number projection method

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that full quantum number projection from symmetry-broken mean-field wave functions can reproduce the characteristic spin-parity sequences predicted by group theory for tetrahedral nuclear rotors, and they formulate experimentally verifiable spectroscopic criteria for identifying tetrahedral symmetry in nuclei such as ⁸⁰Zr, ⁸¹Zr, and ⁸²Zr. However, the tetrahedral ground state of ⁸⁰Zr is predicted to be very difficult to observe experimentally because strictly tetrahedral-symmetric configurations generate neither quadrupole nor dipole moments, making them "rare events" likely not yet seen.

## Method

The authors perform angular momentum, parity, and particle-number projection from a single Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) type mean-field state using an efficient method based on the Thouless amplitude and Pfaffian overlap formulas. The Hamiltonian employs a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with schematic separable multipole residual interactions (λ=2,3,4) for the particle-hole channel and monopole plus quadrupole pairing, with some calculations also using the Gogny D1S force. A cranking term −ω_rot J_x is introduced to generate time-odd components in the mean-field state, which is essential for obtaining the correct moment of inertia.

## Key numbers

- For ⁸⁰Zr, the Gogny D1S HFB calculations give a tetrahedral deformation α₃₂ = 0.11–0.12, with energy more than 3 MeV lower than the prolate deformed minimum at β ≈ 0.45.
- The phenomenological Woods-Saxon calculation finds that when minimisation over tetrahedral deformation is allowed, the tetrahedral minima appear at α₃₂ ≈ ±0.2, about 3.2 MeV below the superdeformed minimum at β ≈ 0.43.
- For the wobbling bands in ¹⁶³Lu, the mean-field deformation parameters used are (β₂, β₄, γ) = (0.42, 0.02, 18°), with pairing gap parameters Δ_n = Δ_p = 0.5 MeV and cranking frequency ħω_rot = 0.2 MeV.
- For ⁴⁰Mg, the root-mean-square radius is √⟨r²⟩ = 3.63 fm, about 15% larger than stable nuclei in the same mass range, and the axial deformation is β₂ ≈ 0.34 despite N=28 being a magic spherical number.
- The cranking term increases the moment of inertia by 30–40% compared to the uncranked case, with ħω_rot of order a few tens of keV sufficient.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed efficient projection method (Tagami and Shimizu, 2012) to study tetrahedral symmetry in nuclei, which was earlier proposed by Dudek and collaborators as a high-rank point-group symmetry producing four-fold degenerate single-particle levels and "tetrahedral closed shells." The tetrahedral rotor spectra follow the irreducible representations of the T_d group, with the ground state rotational sequence of double-closed shell nuclei belonging to the A₁ representation (0⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 6⁺, 6⁻, 7⁻, 8⁺, 9⁺, 9⁻, …). The wobbling band calculations for ¹⁶³Lu are compared with experimental data from Jensen et al. (2002) and Hagemann (2004), while the chiral doublet band predictions follow the mechanism proposed by Frauendorf and Meng (1997). The drip-line nucleus ⁴⁰Mg is studied to examine how weak binding and neutron skin affect collective rotation, with the surprising result that the moment of inertia decreases with increasing spin, opposite to the behaviour in stable nuclei.
