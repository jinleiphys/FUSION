# Nuclear tetrahedral states and high-spin states studied using quantum number projection method

> We have recently developed an efficient method of performing the full quantum number projection from the most general mean-field (HFB type) wave functions including the angular momentum, parity as well as the proton and neutron particle numbers. With this method, we have been investigating several nuclear structure mechanisms. In this report, we discuss the obtained quantum rotational spectra of the tetrahedral nuclear states formulating certain experimentally verifiable criteria, of the high-spin states, focussing on the wobbling- and chiral-bands, and of the drip-line nuclei as illustrative examples.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that full quantum number projection from symmetry-broken mean-field wave functions can reproduce the characteristic spin-parity sequences predicted by group theory for tetrahedral nuclear rotors, and they formulate experimentally verifiable spectroscopic criteria for identifying tetrahedral symmetry in nuclei such as ‚Āł‚ĀįZr, ‚Āł¬ĻZr, and ‚Āł¬≤Zr. However, the tetrahedral ground state of ‚Āł‚ĀįZr is predicted to be very difficult to observe experimentally because strictly tetrahedral-symmetric configurations generate neither quadrupole nor dipole moments, making them "rare events" likely not yet seen.

## Method

The authors perform angular momentum, parity, and particle-number projection from a single Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) type mean-field state using an efficient method based on the Thouless amplitude and Pfaffian overlap formulas. The Hamiltonian employs a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with schematic separable multipole residual interactions (őĽ=2,3,4) for the particle-hole channel and monopole plus quadrupole pairing, with some calculations also using the Gogny D1S force. A cranking term ‚ąíŌČ_rot J_x is introduced to generate time-odd components in the mean-field state, which is essential for obtaining the correct moment of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed efficient projection method (Tagami and Shimizu, 2012) to study tetrahedral symmetry in nuclei, which was earlier proposed by Dudek and collaborators as a high-rank point-group symmetry producing four-fold degenerate single-particle levels and "tetrahedral closed shells." The tetrahedral rotor spectra follow the irreducible representations of the T_d group, with the ground state rotational sequence of double-closed shell nuclei belonging to the A‚āĀ representation (0‚Āļ, 3‚ĀĽ, 4‚Āļ, 6‚Āļ, 6‚ĀĽ, 7‚ĀĽ, 8‚Āļ, 9‚Āļ, 9‚ĀĽ, ‚Ä¶). The wobbling band calculations for ¬Ļ‚Ā∂¬≥Lu are compared with experimental data from Jensen et al. (2002) and Hagemann (2004), while the chiral doublet band predictions follow the mechanism proposed by Frauendorf and Meng (1997). The drip-line nucleus ‚Āī‚ĀįMg is studied to examine how weak binding and neutron skin affect collective rotation, with the surprising result that the moment of inertia decreases with increasing spin, opposite to the behaviour in stable nuclei.
