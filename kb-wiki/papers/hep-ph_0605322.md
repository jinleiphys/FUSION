# In-medium formation of quarkonium

> We confront preliminary RHIC data on J/Psi production in nuclear interactions with expectations which follow in scenarios involving charm quark recombination in a region of color deconfinement. The focus is on transverse momentum and rapidity spectra of the J/Psi, which carry a memory of the spectra of the charm quarks. In such a scenario, one predicts that both spectra will be narrower than those expected without recombination. Preliminary results for the transverse momentum spectra point toward a preference for the recombination picture, while the rapidity spectra do not exhibit any narrowing within present large uncertainties. We present new calculations in the recombination model for the centrality behavior of these signals, which map out the necessary experimental precision required for a definitive test.

# In-medium formation of quarkonium

## Key claim
Preliminary RHIC data on J/Psi transverse momentum spectra show a preference for the charm quark recombination picture in a color-deconfined medium, but the rapidity spectra do not exhibit any narrowing within present large uncertainties, so a definitive test requires improved experimental precision.

## Method
The author uses a kinetic model with a Boltzmann equation that tracks J/Psi formation via recombination of c and anti-c pairs and dissociation via gluon absorption, with reactivity parameters averaged over momentum distributions. Normalized transverse momentum and rapidity spectra are calculated from NLO pQCD charm quark pair amplitudes, comparing "diagonal" pairs (from the same parton interaction) against "off-diagonal" pairs (from different interactions) to isolate the in-medium formation signature. Initial state nuclear broadening is modeled with a random walk parameter extracted from pp and d-Au data.

## Key numbers
- The initial state broadening parameter extracted from d-Au data is lambda^2 = 0.56 +/- 0.08 GeV^2, using measured <p_T^2>_pp = 2.51 +/- 0.21 GeV^2 and average <p_T^2>_d-Au values of 4.28 +/- 0.31 (y = -1.7) and 3.63 +/- 0.25 (y = +1.8).
- The initial temperature parameter T_0 = 0.5 GeV is used for the kinetic model with in-medium formation, while T_0 = 0.35 GeV is used for the scenario without in-medium formation.
- The reactivity lambda_F decreases by a factor of approximately five when the charm quark momentum distribution changes between thermal equilibrium and that predicted by pQCD initial production.
- The pp intrinsic transverse momentum broadening is k_T^2_pp = 0.5 +/- 0.1 GeV^2 from a fit to dimuon data.
- For a definitive test at large centrality, a measurement of <p_T^2> with less than approximately 20% uncertainty is required.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous kinetic model studies (Thews 2005) that proposed normalized J/Psi pT spectra as a signature of in-medium formation independent of absolute production magnitude. It confronts preliminary PHENIX data (PereiraDaCosta 2005) and contrasts with an alternative scenario by Karsch (2005) that asserts no dissociation of primary J/Psi even at RHIC energy. The results enable future measurements at LHC energy to test sequential suppression scenarios, and the centrality-dependent <p_T^2> predictions provide a roadmap for the experimental precision needed to distinguish initial production from in-medium formation.
