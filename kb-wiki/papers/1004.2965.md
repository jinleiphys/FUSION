# Hypernuclei in the quark-meson coupling model

> We present results of hypernuclei calculated in the latest quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, where the effect of the mean scalar field in-medium on the one-gluon exchange hyperfine interaction, is also included self-consistently. The extra repulsion associated with this increased hyperfine interaction in-medium completely changes the predictions for Σ hypernuclei. Whereas in the earlier version of QMC they were bound by an amount similar to Λ hypernuclei, they are unbound in the latest version of QMC, in qualitative agreement with the experimental absence of such states.

# Hypernuclei in the quark-meson coupling model

## Key claim

The inclusion of the in-medium enhancement of the one-gluon exchange color-hyperfine interaction, treated self-consistently, reverses the earlier QMC model prediction for Sigma hypernuclei: whereas the earlier version (QMC-I) predicted bound Sigma hypernuclei with binding similar to Lambda hypernuclei, the latest version (QMC-III) predicts no bound middle or heavy mass Sigma hypernuclei, in qualitative agreement with the experimental absence of such states.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, where nucleon internal structure is modeled by the MIT bag and binding is described by self-consistent couplings of confined light quarks to scalar sigma and vector omega meson fields. For hypernuclei, they employed a relativistic shell model with a Hartree core and Hartree-Fock couplings for the hyperon, and included the effect of the mean scalar field on the color-hyperfine interaction self-consistently. An additional vector repulsion term was introduced for the Sigma hyperon to model the two-pion exchange process N Sigma to N Lambda to N Sigma.

## Key numbers

- Calculated Lambda 1s_{1/2} binding energy in 209_Lambda Pb: -26.9 MeV, compared to experimental -26.3 ± 0.8 MeV in 208_Lambda Pb
- Calculated Lambda 1s_{1/2} binding energy in 17_Lambda O: -16.2 MeV, compared to experimental -12.42 ± 0.05 ± 0.36 MeV in 16_Lambda O
- Calculated Lambda 1s_{1/2} binding energy in 41_Lambda Ca: -20.6 MeV, compared to experimental -18.7 ± 1.1 MeV in 40_Lambda Ca
- Calculated Xi^0 1s_{1/2} binding energy in 17_Xi^0 O: -5.3 MeV
- Calculated Xi^0 1s_{1/2} binding energy in 209_Xi^0 Pb: -15.0 MeV
- Free space meson-nucleon coupling constants used: g_sigma^2 = 8.79 m_sigma^2, g_omega^2 = 4.49 m_omega^2, g_rho^2 = 3.86 m_rho^2, with m_sigma = 700 MeV, m_omega = 770 MeV, m_rho = 780 MeV
- Hartree-Fock couplings for hyperons: g_sigma^2 = 11.33 m_sigma^2, g_omega^2 = 7.27 m_omega^2, g_rho^2 = 4.56 m_rho^2
- Additional repulsion parameter for Sigma: lambda_Sigma = 50.3 MeV-fm^3
- Bag radius in free space: R_N^{free} = 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the QMC model (QMC-I and QMC-II) by including the in-medium effect on the color-hyperfine interaction, resolving a long-standing discrepancy with experiment where earlier QMC-I predicted bound Sigma hypernuclei similar to Lambda hypernuclei. The results agree qualitatively with the phenomenological analysis of Sigma-minus atoms by Batty et al. (1994) and the experimental absence of Sigma hypernuclei beyond A=4 reported by Saha et al. (2004). The model predicts a variety of bound Xi hypernuclei, motivating experimental searches at J-PARC and GSI-FAIR, and is suited for application to dense matter and neutron star properties.
