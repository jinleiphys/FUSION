# Auxiliary field Monte-Carlo study of the QCD phase diagram at strong coupling

> We investigate the QCD phase diagram in the strong coupling limit by using a newly developed auxiliary field Monte-Carlo (AFMC) method. Starting from an effective action in the leading order of the 1/g^2 and 1/d expansion with one species of unrooted staggered fermion, we solve the many-body problem exactly by introducing the auxiliary fields and integrating out the temporal links and quark fields. We have a sign problem in AFMC, which is different from the original one in finite density lattice QCD. For low momentum auxiliary field modes, a complex phase cancellation mechanism exists, and the sign problem is not serious on a small lattice. Compared with the mean field results, the transition temperature is found to be reduced by around 10 % and the hadron phase is found to be extended in the larger chemical potential direction by around 20 %, as observed in the monomer-dimer-polymer (MDP) simulations.

## Key claim
The auxiliary field Monte-Carlo (AFMC) method reproduces the QCD phase diagram in the strong coupling limit, confirming that the transition temperature is reduced by around 10% and the hadron phase extends by around 20% compared to mean field results, but only on small lattices where the sign problem is not serious due to a phase cancellation mechanism for low-momentum auxiliary field modes.

## Method
Starting from an effective action in the leading order of the 1/g² and 1/d expansion with one species of unrooted staggered fermion, the authors introduce auxiliary fields via an extended Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to decompose the four-fermi interaction. They then integrate out the temporal link variables and quark fields analytically, obtaining an auxiliary field effective action, and perform Monte-Carlo integration over the auxiliary fields (σ and π) on small lattices of size 4³×Nτ and 6³×Nτ.

## Key numbers
- Transition temperature at μ≃0 is reduced by around 10% compared to mean field results.
- Hadron phase extends in the larger chemical potential direction by around 20% at low T compared to mean field results.
- Average sign factor ⟨cosθ⟩ is ≥0.9 on a 4³×4 lattice, indicating no serious sign problem on small lattices.
- Phase boundary on 6³×4 lattice is almost the same as on 4³×4 lattice, while the transition is sharper on the 6³×4 lattice.

## Context
This work extends the mean field studies of the QCD phase diagram in the strong coupling limit (MF-SCL) and the monomer-dimer-polymer (MDP) simulations (Karsch and Mutter, 1989) by introducing a new auxiliary field Monte-Carlo method that can include fluctuation effects beyond mean field. The results confirm the MDP simulation findings that fluctuations reduce the transition temperature and expand the hadronic phase, and the method is designed to be extendable to finite coupling (1/g²) effects, which were not included in previous MDP simulations.
