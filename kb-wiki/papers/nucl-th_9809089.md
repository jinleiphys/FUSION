# Wigner energy, odd-even mass staggering and the time-odd mean-fields

> Various properties of single-particle Hartree-Fock ground-state solutions in $N\sim Z$ nuclei are investigated. The emphasis is on a role of single-particle mean-field in odd-even mass staggering. It is shown that, unlike in traditional scenario originating from the Fermi gas or macroscopic models, the symmetry energy contribution to odd-even mass staggering is nearly cancelled by the contribution coming from the average level density. It allows to construct indicators probing both pairing as well as mean-field components to the odd-even mass staggering. The impact of the single-particle Hartree-Fock field on Wigner energy and residual $pn$ interaction in odd-odd nuclei is also discussed.

# Wigner energy, odd-even mass staggering and the time-odd mean-fields

## Key claim

Contrary to the traditional scenario from Fermi gas or macroscopic models where symmetry energy dominates the mean-field contribution to odd-even mass staggering (OES), the symmetry energy contribution is nearly cancelled by the contribution from the average level density, allowing construction of indicators that separately probe pairing and mean-field components. The paper shows that single-particle Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations generate sizable OES even with the four-point indicator that was thought to remove mean-field effects, and that this OES reaches 30% to 50% of the experimental value.

## Method

The author performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations for nuclei with 9 ≤ Z ≤ 28 and -2 ≤ N-Z ≤ 6 using the HFODD code (v1.75) with two Skyrme force parametrizations: SIII and SLy4. The odd-even mass staggering was analyzed using three-point and four-point indicators applied to the theoretical mass tables, and the results were interpreted using the Strutinsky energy theorem to separate shell-model, smooth Strutinsky, and macroscopic (liquid-drop) contributions. The time-odd mean-field contributions were isolated by comparing full SHF calculations with calculations restricted to time-even fields only.

## Key numbers

- The OES from SHF calculations reaches 30% to 50% of the experimental value, and this pattern is almost independent of the Skyrme parametrization used (SIII or SLy4)
- The contribution to OES from smooth Strutinsky energy is δΔ⁽³⁾ ≈ -18/A MeV, while the liquid-drop symmetry energy contribution is δΔ⁽³⁾ ≈ 19/A MeV, giving near cancellation
- The empirical level density parameter for light nuclei is a ≈ A/8 MeV, with effective mass m*/m = 0.76 for SIII and 0.70 for SLy4
- The empirical symmetry energy strength in light nuclei is a_I = 38 MeV
- Deformed SHF-plus-BCS calculations generate approximately 30% of the empirical Wigner energy strength in even-even N=Z nuclei
- The residual pn-interaction indicator ε_pn ≈ 500 keV for light N∼Z nuclei, with enhancement only near the N=Z line
- The time-odd coupling constant C^s_0(ρ→0) = -207.8 MeV·fm³ for SLy4, which is negative unlike most commonly used Skyrme forces where C^s_0(ρ→0) > 0

## Context

This work extends the understanding of odd-even mass staggering beyond the traditional Fermi gas picture of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the macroscopic models of Myers and Swiatecki (1966). The paper builds on the Strutinsky energy theorem (Strutinsky, 1974) and earlier work on single-particle mechanisms for OES in metal clusters by Clemenger (1985), Manninen et al. (1994), and Yannouleas and Landman (1995). The analysis of Wigner energy extends the work of Myers and Swiatecki (1966, 1977) and Jensen et al. (1984), and the shell-model analysis of Brenner et al. (1990) and Satula et al. (1997). The results enable construction of improved empirical pairing indicators and suggest that most of the Wigner energy in N=Z nuclei is beyond the Skyrme mean-field, likely due to neutron-proton pairing, while the time-odd mean-field enhancement at N=Z may help constrain the free time-odd coupling constants of the Skyrme force.
