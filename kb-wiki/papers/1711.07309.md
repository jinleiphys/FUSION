# The impact of $S$-wave thresholds $D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s}+c.c.$ and $D_{s0}\bar{D}^*_{s}+c.c.$ on vector charmonium spectrum

> By investigating the very closely lied $D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s}+c.c.$ and $D_{s0}\bar{D}^*_{s}+c.c.$ thresholds at about 4.43 GeV we propose that the $ψ(4415)$ and $ψ(4160)$ can be mixing states between the dynamic generated states of the strong $S$-wave $D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s}+c.c.$ and $D_{s0}\bar{D}^*_{s}+c.c.$ interactions and the quark model states $ψ(4S)$ and $ψ(2D)$. We investigate the $J/ψK\bar{K}$ final states and invariant mass spectrum of $J/ψK$ to demonstrate that nontrivial lineshapes can arise from such a mechanism. This process, which goes through triangle loop transitions, is located in the vicinity of the so-called "triangle singularity (TS)" kinematics. As a result, it provides a special mechanism for the production of exotic states $Z_{cs}$, which is the strange partner of $Z_c(3900)$, but with flavor contents of $c\bar{c}q\bar{s}$ (or $c\bar{c}s\bar{q}$) with $q$ denoting $u/d$ quarks. The lineshapes of the $e^+e^-\to J/ψK\bar{K}$ cross sections and $J/ψK \ (J/ψ\bar{K})$ spectrum are sensitive to the dynamically generated state, and we demonstrate that a pole structure can be easily distinguished from open threshold CUSP effects if an exotic state is created. A precise measurement of the cross section lineshapes can test such a mixing mechanism and provide navel information for the exotic partners of the $Z_c(3900)$ in the charmonium spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the $\psi(4415)$ and $\psi(4160)$ can be mixing states between quark model states $\psi(4S)$ and $\psi(2D)$ and dynamically generated states arising from strong $S$-wave couplings to the nearby $D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s}+c.c.$ and $D_{s0}\bar{D}^{*}_{s}+c.c.$ thresholds at about 4.43 GeV, with the caveat that the triangle singularity kinematics are only partially satisfied, producing CUSP effects rather than narrow peaks unless a genuine exotic pole is present.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel propagator matrix for $\psi(4S)$ and $\psi(2D)$ including bubble diagrams from $S$-wave couplings to $D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s}$ and $D_{s0}\bar{D}^{*}_{s}$ thresholds, using an exponential form factor with cutoff $\Lambda \simeq 1$ GeV. They then compute triangle loop diagrams for $e^{+}e^{-} \to J/\psi K\bar{K}$ where the intermediate $D_{s1}$ decays to $D^{*}K$ and $D_{s0}$ decays to $DK$, with subsequent rescattering to $J/\psi\bar{K}$, and compare the resulting cross sections and invariant mass spectra to Belle and BESIII data.

## Key numbers

- The $D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s}$ and $D_{s0}\bar{D}^{*}_{s}$ thresholds lie at about 4.428 GeV and 4.429 GeV, respectively.
- With coupling $g = 7~\mathrm{GeV}^{-1/2}$, the obtained pole masses from diagonalizing the propagator matrix lie at about 4.41 GeV and 4.17 GeV, close to the masses of $\psi(4415)$ and $\psi(4160)$.
- The wavefunction renormalization constant $Z \simeq 0.69$ for $\psi(4415)$, implying a molecular component probability of about 52%, i.e. $(1-Z^{2}) \simeq 0.52$.
- The cross section lineshape for $e^{+}e^{-} \to J/\psi K\bar{K}$ is calculated and compared with BESIII data measured at 14 energy points from $\sqrt{s}=4.189$ to 4.600 GeV.
- The $J/\psi K$ invariant mass spectra are shown at initial energies of 4.5, 4.6, 4.7, and 4.8 GeV, with a CUSP structure appearing at the common thresholds of $\bar{D}_{s}D^{*}$ and $\bar{D}_{s}^{*}D$ around 3.98 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach used for $Y(4260)$ and $Z_{c}(3900)$ via the $D_{1}(2420)\bar{D}+c.c.$ threshold (Wang 2013, Cleven 2013, Qin 2016, Xue 2017) to the strange sector, proposing that $D_{s1}\bar{D}_{s}$ and $D_{s0}\bar{D}^{*}_{s}$ thresholds similarly impact $\psi(4415)$ and $\psi(4160)$. It addresses the comment by Voloshin (2018) that kaon exchange might disfavor molecular formation from these thresholds. The analysis enables experimental searches for the $Z_{cs}$ exotic state, the strange partner of $Z_{c}(3900)$, in $e^{+}e^{-} \to J/\psi K\bar{K}$ at BESIII and Belle-II, and demonstrates that triangle singularity kinematics can be distinguished from genuine pole structures in the invariant mass spectra.
