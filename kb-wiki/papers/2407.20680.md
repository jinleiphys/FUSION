# The Fox-Wolfram Moment of Jet Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present the first theoretical investigation of Fox-Wolfram moments (FWMs) for multi-jet production in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this work, jet productions in p+p collisions are computed with a Monte Carlo event generator SHERPA, while the Linear Boltzmann Transport model is utilized to simulate the multiple scattering of energetic partons in the hot and dense QCD matter. The event-normalized distributions of the lower-order FWM, $H_1^T$ in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions are calculated. It is found that for events with jet number $n_\text{jet} = 2$ the $H_1^T$ distribution in Pb+Pb is suppressed at small $H_1^T$ while enhanced at large $H_1^T$ region as compared to p+p. For events with $n_\text{jet}>2$, the jet number reduction effect due to jet quenching in the QGP decreases the $H_1^T$ distribution at large $H_1^T$ in Pb+Pb relative to p+p. The medium modification of the Fox-Wolfram moment $H_1^T$ for events with $n_\text{jet}\ge 2$ are also presented, which resemble those of events with $n_\text{jet} = 2$. Its reason is revealed through the relative contribution fractions of events with different final-state jet numbers to $H_1^T$.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical investigation of Fox-Wolfram moments (FWMs) for multi-jet production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, specifically the lower-order moment \(H_1^T\). The medium modification of the \(H_1^T\) distribution depends on jet multiplicity: for events with exactly two jets, the distribution is suppressed at small \(H_1^T\) and enhanced at large \(H_1^T\) in Pb+Pb relative to p+p, while for events with more than two jets, the jet number reduction effect due to jet quenching suppresses the distribution at large \(H_1^T\).

## Method

Jet production in p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV was simulated with the Monte Carlo event generator SHERPA 2.2.11, which performs NLO matrix element calculations matched to parton showers. The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model was used to simulate multiple scattering of energetic partons in the quark-gluon plasma, with bulk medium evolution provided by the (3+1)D CLVisc hydrodynamical model. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-\(k_{\perp}\) algorithm with cone size \(R=0.4\) and kinematic cuts of leading jet \(p_T > 110\) GeV/c, all jets \(p_T > 30\) GeV/c, and rapidity \(|\eta| \le 2.5\).

## Key numbers

- The fraction of events with \(n_{\text{jet}} = 2\) increases from 0.51 in p+p collisions to 0.56 in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV; correspondingly, the fraction of \(n_{\text{jet}} > 2\) events decreases from 0.49 to 0.44.
- For \(n_{\text{jet}} = 2\) events, the average azimuthal angle \(\langle \Delta\phi_{12} \rangle\) changes from 2.946 in p+p to 2.922 in Pb+Pb collisions across the entire \(\ln(H_1^T)\) statistical region.
- For \(n_{\text{jet}} > 2\) events, \(\langle \Delta\phi_{12} \rangle\) changes from 2.829 (p+p) to 2.816 (Pb+Pb), \(\langle \Delta\phi_{13} \rangle\) from 2.539 to 2.465, and \(\langle \Delta\phi_{23} \rangle\) from 0.847 to 0.934.
- The suppression/enhancement transition for \(n_{\text{jet}} = 2\) events occurs at approximately \(\ln(H_1^T) = -4.5\).
- For \(n_{\text{jet}} > 2\) events, significant enhancement is observed in bins \(\ln(H_1^T) \in [-4.8, -0.9]\) with suppression elsewhere.

## Context

This work extends recent theoretical studies of jet event shape observables in heavy-ion collisions, specifically jet broadening \(B_{tot}\) (Kang 2023) and transverse sphericity \(S_{\perp}\) (Chen 2020). Unlike jet broadening, which gives non-zero results only for events with \(n_{\text{jet}} \geq 3\), \(H_1^T\) covers both \(n_{\text{jet}} = 2\) and \(n_{\text{jet}} \geq 3\) events. Compared to transverse sphericity, \(H_1^T\) may exhibit a larger magnitude of medium modification. The results enable future experimental measurements of FWMs at the LHC as a new probe of jet quenching and QGP properties.
