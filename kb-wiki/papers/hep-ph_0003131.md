# Excited Heavy Baryons and Their Symmetries I: Formalism

> This is the first of two papers to study a new emergent symmetry which connects orbitally excited heavy baryons to the ground states in the combined heavy quark and large $N_c$ limit. The existence of this symmetry is shown in a model-independent way, and different possible realizations of the symmetry are discussed. It is also proved that this emergent symmetry commutes with the large $N_c$ spin-flavor symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new emergent symmetry, described by a contracted O(8) group (or more generally O(2d+2) in d spatial dimensions), connects orbitally excited heavy baryons to ground states in the combined heavy quark and large N_c limit, but this symmetry only emerges when both limits are taken simultaneously and the excitation energy ω vanishes as N_c^{-1/2}.

## Method

The authors decompose the QCD Hamiltonian for the heavy baryon Hilbert space into heavy quark and light (brown muck) parts, then construct QCD-based position and momentum operators for the heavy quark, the brown muck, and their relative motion. By studying double commutators of these operators with the Hamiltonian in the combined heavy quark and large N_c limit, they show that the kinetic masses are m_Q for the heavy quark and m_N for the brown muck, and that the relative coordinate dynamics reduces to that of a three-dimensional simple harmonic oscillator with a contracted O(8) spectrum-generating algebra.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy ω scales as N_c^{-1/2} and vanishes in the large N_c limit, causing the tower of excited states to become degenerate with the ground state.
- The reduced mass μ of the heavy meson–light baryon system scales as N_c, so μ → ∞ in the combined limit, localizing the wave function at the bottom of the potential.
- The binding potential V(x) is of order N_c^0, and its spring constant κ is generically also of order N_c^0.
- The kinetic mass of the heavy baryon is M = m_Q + m_N in the combined limit, with corrections of order λ^0 (where λ ∼ Λ_QCD/m_Q, 1/N_c).
- The kinetic mass of the heavy quark is m_Q, and the kinetic mass of the brown muck is m_N, both with O(λ^2) corrections.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier report (Chow and Cohen, 1999) of a contracted U(4) symmetry to a larger contracted O(8) symmetry, and provides a model-independent QCD derivation that was previously only seen in the bound state picture (e.g., the simple bound state model of Refs. [genius1,genius2,genius3]). The formalism enables model-independent predictions for static properties such as axial current couplings and weak form factor moments of orbitally excited heavy baryons, which will be explored in the companion paper.
