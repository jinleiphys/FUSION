# Magnetic Vortex Lattices in Finite Isospin Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study finite isospin chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT) in a uniform external magnetic field and find the condensation energy of magnetic vortex lattices using the method of successive approximations (originally used by Abrikosov) near the upper critical point beyond which the system is in the normal vacuum phase. The difference between standard Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory (or equivalently the Abelian Higgs model) and $χ$PT arises due to the presence of additional momentum-dependent (derivative) interactions in $χ$PT and the presence of electromagnetically neutral pions that interact with the charged pions via strong interactions but do not couple directly to the external magnetic field. We find that while the vortex lattice structure is hexagonal similar to vortices in GL theory, the condensation energy (relative to the normal vacuum state in a uniform, external magnetic field) is smaller (larger in magnitude) due to the presence of derivative interactions. Furthermore, we establish that neutral pions do not condense in the vortex lattice near the upper critical field.

## Key claim

The condensation energy of magnetic vortex lattices in finite isospin chiral perturbation theory is smaller (larger in magnitude) than in standard Ginzburg-Landau theory due to additional momentum-dependent derivative interactions, but only near the upper critical field where the system transitions to the normal vacuum phase.

## Method

The author used the method of successive approximations (originally developed by Abrikosov) to solve the equations of motion derived from the O(p^2) chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian in a uniform external magnetic field, expanding perturbatively near the upper critical field B_c. The analysis assumed time-independence, no electric fields, and that neutral pions do not condense, then computed the Hamiltonian density expectation value over the transverse plane up to O(epsilon^4) to obtain the condensation energy. The Abrikosov ratio beta_A was minimized to determine the optimal vortex lattice structure.

## Key numbers

* The upper critical field is given by B_c = (mu_I^2 - m_pi^2)/e, where e = sqrt(4*pi/137)
* The Abrikosov ratio for the hexagonal (triangular) lattice is beta_A = 1.159595... with nu = 3^(1/4)/sqrt(2)
* The critical field H_c assuming type-I superconductivity is H_c = f_pi * m_pi * (mu_I/m_pi - m_pi/mu_I)
* The lattice vectors for the hexagonal vortex lattice are d_1 = (L_B/nu)(0,1) and d_2 = (L_B/nu)(sqrt(3)/2, 1/2), where L_B = sqrt(2*pi/(e*B_ext))

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of single pionic vortices in finite isospin chiral perturbation theory by Adhikari (2015) to consider vortex lattice solutions and their condensation energy. It contrasts with standard Ginzburg-Landau theory (or the Abelian Higgs model) by including derivative interactions that modify the condensation energy magnitude. The results enable future studies of the full phase diagram of finite isospin QCD in external magnetic fields, where lattice QCD suffers from a sign problem due to flavor symmetry breaking as noted by Endrodi (2014).
