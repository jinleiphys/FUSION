# Many-body correlations in a multistep variational approach

> We discuss a multistep variational approach for the study of many-body correlations. The approach is developed in a boson formalism (bosons representing particle-hole excitations) and based on an iterative sequence of diagonalizations in subspaces of the full boson space. Purpose of these diagonalizations is that of searching for the best approximation of the ground state of the system. The procedure also leads us to define a set of excited states and, at the same time, of operators which generate these states as a result of their action on the ground state. We examine the cases in which these operators carry one-particle one-hole and up to two-particle two-hole excitations. We also explore the possibility of associating bosons to Tamm-Dancoff excitations and of describing the spectrum in terms of only a selected group of these. Tests within an exactly solvable three-level model are provided.

# Many-body correlations in a multistep variational approach

## Key claim
The paper presents a multistep variational approach that improves upon standard Random Phase Approximation (RPA) by constructing both the ground state and excited states through an iterative sequence of diagonalizations in small subspaces of a boson space, avoiding RPA's collapse at finite interaction strength. The method is tested only within an exactly solvable three-level SU(3) model with 2Omega = 10 particles, and the quality of results depends on the number of iterations and whether 1p-1h or 2p-2h excitations are included.

## Method
The approach maps the fermion Hamiltonian onto a boson Hamiltonian using a Marumori-type mapping, where bosons represent particle-hole excitations. An iterative sequence of diagonalizations is performed in subspaces of the full boson space, each subspace containing the current ground state approximation plus states generated by acting with boson creation and annihilation operators on it. At each step, the lowest eigenstate provides an improved ground state approximation, while the remaining eigenstates define excited states via operators that act on the converged ground state. The procedure is tested for operators carrying only 1p-1h excitations and for operators including up to 2p-2h excitations, and also with bosons identified as Tamm-Dancoff collective phonons.

## Key numbers
- Model parameters: 2Omega = 10 particles, single-particle energies epsilon(1) = epsilon, epsilon(2) = 1.5 epsilon, with interaction strengths V_x(i,j) = -2 chi, V_v(i,j) = 0.5 chi, V_y(i,j,k) = -0.75 chi (all in units of energy)
- RPA undergoes collapse at chi/epsilon approximately 0.024, where the ground state energy starts deviating significantly from zero
- The iterative procedure with 1p-1h excitations produces four excited states (versus two in RPA) for chi/epsilon greater than about 0.014
- When using only the lowest Tamm-Dancoff boson with 1p-1h excitations, the working subspace dimensionality is 3 versus 66 in the full boson space
- The boson space B can contain up to ten-boson states (for 2Omega = 10), while each iterative subspace B^(k) contains only up to two-boson states in the 1p-1h case

## Context
This work extends the author's previous variational approach (Sambataro, 1999) developed for quasiparticle RPA in beta-decay physics, reformulating it in a boson formalism with an iterative diagonalization scheme rather than sequential minimizations. The method is tested against the SU(3) model used by Li, Klein, and Dreizler (1970), Matsuo and Matsuyanagi (1985), and Takada, Shimizu, and Thorn (1988) for testing approximation schemes. The approach overcomes RPA's lack of internal consistency and its collapse at finite interaction strength, while the use of collective Tamm-Dancoff bosons parallels the Interacting Boson Model of Iachello and Arima (1991). The work enables application to realistic nuclear systems by potentially reducing dimensionality through selection of only the most collective particle-hole excitations.
