# One-body energy dissipation in fusion reaction from mean-field theory

> Information on dissipation in the entrance channel of heavy-ion collisions is extracted by macroscopic reduction procedure of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock theory. The method gives access to a fully microscopic description of the friction coefficient associated with transfer of energy from the relative motion towards intrinsic degrees of freedom. The reduced friction coefficient exhibits a universal behavior, i.e. almost independent of systems investigated, whose order of magnitude is comparable with the calculations based on linear response theory. Similarly to nucleus-nucleus potential, especially close to the Coulomb barrier, there are sizable dynamical effects on the magnitude and form factor of friction coefficient.

## Key claim

The reduced friction coefficient extracted from Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory exhibits a universal behavior that is almost independent of the colliding system, but this universality holds only for center-of-mass energies well above the Coulomb barrier; at near-barrier energies, the friction coefficient shows sizable energy dependence and its magnitude increases by about a factor of four.

## Method

The authors apply the Dissipative Dynamics TDHF (DD-TDHF) macroscopic reduction procedure to head-on heavy-ion collisions simulated with a three-dimensional TDHF code using the SLy4d Skyrme effective force. From the TDHF evolution of the relative distance and momentum, they extract the friction coefficient γ(R) by assuming the mean-field dynamics can be reduced to a one-dimensional Newtonian equation with dissipation. The friction coefficient is obtained for both symmetric systems (⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca) and asymmetric systems (¹⁶O+⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca, ¹⁶O+²⁰⁸Pb, ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁸Ca, ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰Zr) at various center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- Reduced friction coefficient at high energy (well above barrier) is about β(R) ≡ 0.8 × 10²¹ s⁻¹ near the Coulomb barrier radius R/R_B = 1.
- Reduced friction coefficient at near-barrier energies is about β(R) ≡ 3 × 10²¹ s⁻¹ near R/R_B = 1, approximately four times larger than the high-energy value.
- For the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca reaction, center-of-mass energies between 55 and 100 MeV were used; the Coulomb barrier energy extracted at E_c.m. = 55 MeV is about 53 MeV.
- The mesh sizes in space and time for TDHF calculations are 0.8 fm and 0.45 fm/c, respectively.
- Initial distances between nuclei are set between 16 and 22.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the DD-TDHF method previously applied to extract nucleus-nucleus potentials (Washiyama, 2008) to now extract friction coefficients from the same microscopic framework. The results are compared with the surface friction model (SFM) of Gross and Kalinowski (1974) and with microscopic linear response theory calculations by Adamian et al. (1997); the SFM strongly overestimates the friction magnitude near the barrier, while the TDHF results at high energy agree with the linear response calculations. The extracted friction coefficients enable a fully microscopic description of one-body dissipation in fusion reactions without adjustable parameters, and the near-equality of dissipated energy and internal excitation energy provides a consistency check of the method.
