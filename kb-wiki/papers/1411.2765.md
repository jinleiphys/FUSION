# Shear Viscosity and Phase Diagram from Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model

> We discuss a detailed study of the variation of shear viscosity, $η$, with temperature and baryon chemical potential within the framework of Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model. $η$ is found to depend strongly on the spectral width of the quasi-particles present in the model. The variation of $η$ across the phase diagram has distinctive features for different kinds of transitions. These variations have been used to study the possible location of the Critical End Point (CEP), and cross-checked with similar studies of variation of specific heat. Finally using a parameterization of freeze-out surface in heavy-ion collision experiments, the variation of shear viscosity to entropy ratio has also been discussed as a function of the center of mass energy of collisions.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity η and the ratio η/s show distinctive variations across the phase diagram that can be used to locate the Critical End Point (CEP), but the quantitative results depend strongly on the assumed form and magnitude of the spectral width Γ(p) of the quasi-particles, which is not computed from first principles within the model.

## Method

The authors compute the shear viscosity η using the Kubo formalism, which relates η to the retarded correlator of the energy-momentum tensor, evaluated within the 2+1-flavor Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The spectral function of the quarks is parameterized with a Breit-Wigner form using a constant spectral width Γ = 100 MeV, chosen to approximately satisfy the sum rule Γ ≤ M. The entropy density s is obtained from the thermodynamic potential of the PNJL model, and the ratio η/s is studied as a function of temperature T and quark chemical potential μ_q.

## Key numbers

- For T = 100 MeV, η shows a jump at μ_q ≈ 280 MeV for all forms of Γ, indicating a first-order phase transition.
- The minimum of η/s at μ_q = 0 occurs near T = T_c and reaches the KSS bound of 1/(4π) (≈ 0.08).
- The CEP is located around T = 100–120 MeV and μ_q = 250–270 MeV, confirmed by a peak in the specific heat C_V/T^3 near μ_q ≈ 260 MeV.
- Along the freeze-out curve, η/s saturates to the KSS bound for Γ = 200 MeV, while for Γ = 100 MeV it saturates at a higher value; the result is close to η/s ≈ 0.09 ± 0.015 extracted from RHIC Au+Au data.
- The parameterization of the freeze-out curve uses T(μ_B) = a − b μ_B^2 − c μ_B^4 with a = 0.166 GeV, b = 0.139 GeV⁻¹, c = 0.053 GeV⁻³.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of shear viscosity in the two-flavor NJL model using the Kubo formalism (Weise, 2009) and the relaxation time approach (Sabya, 2010) to the 2+1-flavor PNJL model, which incorporates both chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement via the Polyakov loop. The results are compared with lattice studies of the gluon plasma (Nakamura, 2005) and with chiral perturbation theory for a pion gas (Chen, 2007). The study enables the use of η/s as a probe for the location of the CEP in the QCD phase diagram, which can be cross-checked with experimental data from RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions.
