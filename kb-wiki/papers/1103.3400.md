# Neutral pion photoproduction off 3H and 3He in chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate electromagnetic neutral pion production off three-nucleon bound states (3H, 3He) at threshold to leading one-loop order in the framework of chiral nuclear effective field theory. In addition, we analyze the dependence of the nuclear S-wave amplitude E_{0+} on the elementary neutron amplitude E_{0+}^{pi0 n} which in the case of 3He provides a stringent test of the prediction based on chiral perturbation theory. Uncertainties from higher order corrections are estimated.

## Key claim

The authors calculate neutral pion photoproduction off 3H and 3He at threshold to leading one-loop order in chiral nuclear effective field theory, finding that the S-wave multipole E0+ for 3He is highly sensitive to the elementary neutron amplitude E0+π0n, which provides a test of the chiral perturbation theory prediction that the neutron amplitude is larger in magnitude than the proton amplitude. The quoted uncertainties from higher-order corrections are only lower bounds, and the dominant error comes from the 5% uncertainty in the single-nucleon amplitudes.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear matrix element for threshold neutral pion production using chiral 3N wave functions at N2LO in the Weinberg power counting, with five different cutoff combinations (Λ̃,Λ) = (450,500), (600,500), (550,600), (450,700), (600,700) MeV to estimate theoretical uncertainty. The production operator includes one-body contributions (parametrized by form factors FT/L S±V) and two-body contributions from two Feynman diagrams, evaluated numerically with Monte Carlo integration using the VEGAS algorithm. The single-nucleon multipoles E0+π0p, E0+π0n, L0+π0p, and L0+π0n are taken from subleading chiral perturbation theory at O(p4).

## Key numbers

- Single-nucleon multipoles from chiral perturbation theory: E0+π0p = -1.16 × 10-3/Mπ+, E0+π0n = +2.13 × 10-3/Mπ+, L0+π0p = -1.35 × 10-3/Mπ+, L0+π0n = -2.41 × 10-3/Mπ+
- Total threshold multipole for 3He: E0+ = (-2.24 ± 0.11) × 10-3/Mπ+, L0+ = (-4.98 ± 0.12) × 10-3/Mπ+
- Total threshold multipole for 3H: E0+ = (-4.94 ± 0.07) × 10-3/Mπ+, L0+ = (-4.12 ± 0.07) × 10-3/Mπ+
- Kinematical factor K1N ≈ 1.092 for converting 1N to 3N phase space
- Two-body prefactor K2N ≈ 0.135 fm × 10-3/Mπ+
- Form factor FT S-V for 3He = 1.480(26)(3), FT S+V for 3He = 0.017(13)(3)
- Two-body form factor difference FT(a)-FT(b) for 3He = -29.3(2)(1) fm-1
- Changing E0+π0n by ±20% changes the threshold cross section a0 for 3He by about ±30%
- Extracted value from Saclay measurement: E0+ = (-2.8 ± 0.2) × 10-3/Mπ+, about 25% above the prediction

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to neutral pion photoproduction from the deuteron (Beane 1997) to three-nucleon bound states, using chiral 3N wave functions from Epelbaum (2003). The calculation enables a test of the counterintuitive chiral perturbation theory prediction (Bernard 1994, Bernard 2001) that the neutron S-wave multipole E0+π0n is larger in magnitude than the proton one, with 3He being a particularly promising target due to its spin structure. The results are roughly consistent with the old Saclay measurement (Argan 1980, 1987) but call for a new, more precise measurement using continuous-wave beams and modern detectors.
