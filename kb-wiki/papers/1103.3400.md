# Neutral pion photoproduction off 3H and 3He in chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate electromagnetic neutral pion production off three-nucleon bound states (3H, 3He) at threshold to leading one-loop order in the framework of chiral nuclear effective field theory. In addition, we analyze the dependence of the nuclear S-wave amplitude E_{0+} on the elementary neutron amplitude E_{0+}^{pi0 n} which in the case of 3He provides a stringent test of the prediction based on chiral perturbation theory. Uncertainties from higher order corrections are estimated.

## Key claim

The authors calculate neutral pion photoproduction off 3H and 3He at threshold to leading one-loop order in chiral nuclear effective field theory, finding that the S-wave multipole E0+ for 3He is highly sensitive to the elementary neutron amplitude E0+ŽĆ0n, which provides a test of the chiral perturbation theory prediction that the neutron amplitude is larger in magnitude than the proton amplitude. The quoted uncertainties from higher-order corrections are only lower bounds, and the dominant error comes from the 5% uncertainty in the single-nucleon amplitudes.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear matrix element for threshold neutral pion production using chiral 3N wave functions at N2LO in the Weinberg power counting, with five different cutoff combinations (╬ø╠ā,╬ø) = (450,500), (600,500), (550,600), (450,700), (600,700) MeV to estimate theoretical uncertainty. The production operator includes one-body contributions (parametrized by form factors FT/L S┬▒V) and two-body contributions from two Feynman diagrams, evaluated numerically with Monte Carlo integration using the VEGAS algorithm. The single-nucleon multipoles E0+ŽĆ0p, E0+ŽĆ0n, L0+ŽĆ0p, and L0+ŽĆ0n are taken from subleading chiral perturbation theory at O(p4).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to neutral pion photoproduction from the deuteron (Beane 1997) to three-nucleon bound states, using chiral 3N wave functions from Epelbaum (2003). The calculation enables a test of the counterintuitive chiral perturbation theory prediction (Bernard 1994, Bernard 2001) that the neutron S-wave multipole E0+ŽĆ0n is larger in magnitude than the proton one, with 3He being a particularly promising target due to its spin structure. The results are roughly consistent with the old Saclay measurement (Argan 1980, 1987) but call for a new, more precise measurement using continuous-wave beams and modern detectors.
