# Energy Loss in Small Collision Systems

> We present leading hadron suppression predictions in $Pb+Pb$ and $p+Pb$ collisions from a convolved radiative and collisional energy loss model in which partons propagate through a realistic background, and in which the radiative energy loss receives a short pathlength correction. We find that the short pathlength correction is small for $D$ meson $R_{AA}(p_T)$ in both $Pb+Pb$ and $p+Pb$ collisions. However the short pathlength correction leads to a surprisingly large reduction in suppression for $ŽĆ$ mesons in $p+Pb$ and even $Pb+Pb$ collisions, providing a qualitative explanation for the rapid rise in $ŽĆ$ meson $R_{AA}(p_T)$ at the LHC. We find that the size of the short pathlength correction to $R_{AA}(p_T)$ is acutely sensitive to the chosen distribution of scattering centers in the plasma. Furthermore we find that conventional elastic energy loss models, which apply the central limit theorem to the number of scatterings, dramatically overpredict suppression in $\mathrm{p}+\mathrm{A}$ collisions, calling for short pathlength corrections to elastic energy loss.

# Energy Loss in Small Collision Systems

## Key claim

The short pathlength correction to DGLV radiative energy loss is small for D meson suppression in both Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions, but leads to a surprisingly large reduction in suppression for pi mesons in p+Pb and even Pb+Pb collisions; however, this correction becomes excessively large at high transverse momentum (reaching O(100%) at 100 GeV for pi mesons in central Pb+Pb), suggesting the large formation time assumption may be breaking down.

## Method

The authors convolved radiative energy loss (DGLV with short pathlength corrections from Kolbe (2015)) with elastic energy loss (Braaten (1991) with central limit theorem assumption) following the WHDG framework (Wicks (2005)), using a realistic background with Bjorken expansion and participant density profiles from Schenke (2020). They compared two distributions of scattering centers, exponential and truncated step, and computed nuclear modification factors R_AA and R_pA for D0 and pi mesons in Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For D0 mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions with exponential distribution, the short pathlength correction is less than or equal to O(10%) and grows with p_T
- The exponential vs truncated step distribution for D0 mesons is an O(10ŌĆō20%) effect at high p_T
- For pi mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions with exponential distribution, the correction reaches O(100%) at 100 GeV
- For pi mesons in central Pb+Pb, the predicted R_AA values differ by O(10ŌĆō20%) between exponential without correction, truncated step with correction, and truncated step without correction
- The large formation time assumption breaks down at O(30ŌĆō50) GeV for gluons fragmenting into pi mesons
- In p+Pb collisions, there are approximately 1 scattering, causing central limit theorem breakdown for elastic energy loss

## Context

This work extends the WHDG convolved radiative and collisional energy loss model (Wicks (2005), Horowitz (2011)) by incorporating short pathlength corrections to DGLV radiative energy loss derived by Kolbe (2015). It addresses the challenge of applying successful A+A energy loss models to small collision systems like p+Pb, where large system size assumptions (central limit theorem, large pathlength L >> mu^{-1}) become fragile. The results enable future work on short pathlength corrections to elastic energy loss, relaxation of the large formation time assumption in GLV/DGLV, and more rigorous treatment of energy loss in small systems, potentially explaining the observed R_pA > 1 in experimental data from ATLAS (Balek (2017)) and ALICE (2018).
