# Optimal colliding energy for the synthesis of superheavy element $Z$=119

> The evaporation residue (ER) cross section of 3n and 4n channels related to the synthesis of superheavy element (SHE) with the charge number $Z=119$ in the $^{51}$V+$^{248}$Cm reaction has been calculated by the dinuclear system (DNS) model as a sum of the partial cross sections of the corresponding channels. The angular momentum distribution of the compound nucleus is estimated by the dynamical trajectory calculations of the capture probability which is considered as the DNS formation probability. The fusion probability decreases by the increase of the DNS angular momentum due to its influence on the intrinsic fusion barrier $B_{\rm fus}^*$. The range $α_2=60^{\circ} ÷70^{\circ}$ of the orientation angle of the axial symmetry axis of the deformed target nucleus $^{248}$Cm is favorable for the formation of the compound nucleus. The fusion probability decreases at around $α_2=90^{\circ}$ since the number of the partial waves contributing to the capture decreases. Therefore, it is important to calculate the capture cross section dynamically. The 4n channel cross section of the SHE synthesis is larger than the 3n channel cross section maximum value of the ER cross section is 12.3 fb at $E_{\rm c.m.}$=232 MeV.

## Key claim
The maximum evaporation residue cross section for synthesizing superheavy element Z=119 via the 51V+248Cm reaction is predicted to be 12.3 fb for the 4n channel at a center-of-mass energy of 232 MeV, but this value depends critically on the orientation angle of the deformed 248Cm target nucleus, with the favorable range being 60° to 70° relative to the beam direction.

## Method
The authors used the dinuclear system (DNS) model to calculate capture, fusion, quasifission, fast fission, and evaporation residue cross sections for the 51V+248Cm reaction. Capture probabilities were determined from dynamical trajectory calculations of the relative motion and angular momentum, while fusion probabilities were computed from the competition between the intrinsic fusion barrier and quasifission barrier using level densities. The survival probability of the compound nucleus against fission during neutron evaporation was calculated with the statistical model code KEWPIE2, incorporating deformation-dependent level densities and shell corrections.

## Key numbers
- Maximum 4n channel evaporation residue cross section: 12.3 fb at E_c.m. = 232 MeV
- Maximum 3n channel evaporation residue cross section: 6.6 fb at E_c.m. = 225 MeV
- Intrinsic fusion barrier B*_fus = 10.67 MeV for target orientation angle α2 = 30°, and B*_fus = 6.20 MeV for α2 = 60°
- Compound nucleus excitation energy range: 35 < E*_CN < 39 MeV
- Favorable orientation angle range for compound nucleus formation: α2 = 60° to 70°
- Maximum fusion probability occurs in the ranges 232 < E_c.m. < 236 MeV and 15 < L < 30 ħ

## Context
This work extends theoretical predictions for synthesizing superheavy element Z=119 using the 51V+248Cm reaction, comparing with prior calculations by Zhu (2014), Lv (2021), Wilczynska (2019), Ghahramany (2016), Adamian (2018), and Adamian (2020). The results are closest to the predictions of Zhu (2014) and Adamian (2018). The study enables experimental planning for the RIKEN facility, where beam energies in the range E_c.m. = 232–236 MeV are identified as optimal for the synthesis attempt, and it highlights the importance of accounting for target deformation orientation in reaction calculations.
