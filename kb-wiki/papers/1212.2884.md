# Direct capture in the $^{130}$Sn(n,$γ$)$^{131}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn(n,$γ$)$^{133}$Sn reactions under $r$-process conditions

> The cross sections of the $^{130}$Sn(n,$γ$)$^{131}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn(n,$γ$)$^{133}$Sn reactions are calculated in the direct capture model at low energies below 1.5\,MeV. Using recent data from (d,p) transfer experiments on $^{130}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn, it is possible to avoid global input parameters with their inherent uncertainties and to determine all input to the direct capture model by local adjustments. The calculated direct capture cross sections of $^{130}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn are almost identical and have uncertainties of less than a factor of two. The stellar reaction rates $N_A < σv >$ show a slight increase with temperature. Finally an estimate for the influence of low-lying resonances to the stellar reaction rates is given.

## Key claim

The direct capture cross sections of the 130Sn(n,γ)131Sn and 132Sn(n,γ)133Sn reactions are almost identical and have uncertainties of less than a factor of two, but additional resonant contributions may enhance the stellar reaction rate by up to a factor of 5 for 130Sn depending on whether the remaining L=1 and L=3 strength is located close above threshold.

## Method

The direct capture cross sections were calculated using the direct capture model restricted to E1 transitions, with all input parameters determined by local adjustments to recent (d,p) transfer experiments on 130Sn (Kozub, 2012) and 132Sn (Jones, 2011) rather than global parametrizations. Bound state wave functions were calculated by adjusting the depth V0 of a Woods-Saxon potential to the bound state energies, and scattering wave functions used the average potential parameters derived from the bound L=1 and L=3 states. The stellar reaction rates N_A <σv> were then computed from the cross sections and parametrized by a three-parameter parabolic fit in T9.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of 131Sn: Sn = 5206 ± 13 keV
- Neutron separation energy of 133Sn: Sn = 2370 ± 24 keV
- Most important temperature range for r-process: 0.8 ≤ T9 ≤ 1.3, corresponding to thermal energies 70 keV ≤ kT ≤ 110 keV
- For 130Sn(n,γ)131Sn: fit parameters a0 = 16811, a1 = 2291, a2 = 700 (in cm3/(s mol)) for the stellar reaction rate parametrization
- For 132Sn(n,γ)133Sn: fit parameters a0 = 16321, a1 = 2236, a2 = 870 (in cm3/(s mol)) for the stellar reaction rate parametrization
- Fit deviations are 1-2% over the full temperature range
- Total uncertainty of the direct capture cross sections is below 50%
- A hypothetical 3/2- resonance at about 73 keV with total width Γ ≈ 58 keV would have a total cross section of 4.2 mb in the resonance maximum

## Context

This work extends previous direct capture calculations for these reactions by Rauscher et al. (1998), Chiba et al. (2008), and Xu et al. (2012) that used global parametrizations and found variations over three orders of magnitude at 30 keV for 130Sn. The new results are lower by a factor of slightly above 2 than Xu et al. (2012) and slightly below 2 than Chiba et al. (2008), and about a factor of two below the highest result by Rauscher et al. (1998). The study enables more reliable r-process nucleosynthesis calculations around A ≈ 130, particularly during freeze-out when neutron capture cross sections become important, and excludes the huge enhancements of the reaction rate by a factor of 10 or even 100 discussed by Beun et al. (2009).
