# New nuclear structure features in transactinide nuclei

> The structural evolution of the heavy nuclei, with Z > 82, is investigated by looking at the differential variation of the two-neutron separation energies. It indicates, by non-monotonous behavior at certain neutron numbers, structure phenomena such as major shell (N = 126) and deformed subshell (N = 152) closures. Another interesting effect is observed at N ~ 142, which is very well correlated with a previously observed, intriguing behavior of quantities measured in alpha decay, such as relative branching ratios and hindrance factors of excited states from the ground state band of deformed nuclei in this region. Corroboration of the existing experimental data indicates another possible deformed subshell closure.

# New nuclear structure features in transactinide nuclei

## Key claim

The differential variation of two-neutron separation energies reveals a possible deformed subshell closure at N ~ 142 in isotopes with Z = 92 to 98, in addition to the known N = 126 spherical shell closure and N = 152 deformed subshell closure. This interpretation is tentative, based on correlation with alpha-decay fine structure observables and analogy with behavior at established shell closures, rather than a definitive identification.

## Method

The authors analyzed the differential quantity dS₂ₙ(Z,N) = [S₂ₙ(Z,N+2) - S₂ₙ(Z,N)]/2 from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation for even-even nuclei with Z = 82 to 104 (Pb to Rf). They compared the evolution of this quantity with the neutron-number dependence of relative branching ratios and hindrance factors for alpha decay populating the 4₁⁺ and 6₁⁺ states of the ground-state band in daughter nuclei, using data from the ENSDF database with Preston penetrabilities.

## Key numbers

- The negative bump at N ~ 142 in dS₂ₙ is approximately 150 keV deep, compared to approximately 400 keV for the N = 152 deformed subshell closure.
- The relative branching ratio for the 4₁⁺ state ranges roughly between 100 and 4000 for nuclei in the region of interest, and reaches values of order 10⁶ at the N = 126 magic number.
- The hindrance factor for the 4₁⁺ state shows a maximum at N = 142-144 for U, Pu, and Cm isotopes, correlating with the dS₂ₙ minimum.
- The Cm isotopes show a minimum at N = 144 in dS₂ₙ with practically the same depth as at N = 152.
- All nuclei in the region of the N ~ 142 bump have quadrupole deformations β₂ larger than 0.25.

## Context

This work extends the differential S₂ₙ analysis previously applied to medium-mass nuclei (Z = 28 to 80) by Bucurescu and collaborators (2012) into the trans-lead region. The observed correlation between dS₂ₙ and alpha-decay hindrance factors addresses an unexplained maximum in the 4₁⁺ state branching ratios and hindrance factors reported earlier by the same authors (2012) for U to Cf isotopes. The proposed N ~ 142 deformed subshell closure is consistent with Nilsson model calculations by Gustafson (1967) and Meldner (1967) that predict a gap at N = 142 for β₂ around 0.22-0.23, in addition to the N = 152 gap at higher deformation. The finding enables further investigation of how subshell closures in neutron number correlate with proton number and how alpha-decay fine structure can serve as a sensitive probe of nuclear structure in heavy deformed nuclei.
