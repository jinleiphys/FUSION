# Virial theorem for rigidly rotating matter

> The scaling property of the thermodynamic free energy ($Φ$) of a system at global equilibrium has been examined using a real-time method known as the virial theorem. We demonstrate these scaling properties through a derived relation based on the general structure of equal-time commutators among Poincare charges and their densities. This relation is applicable to any renormalizable fields with spin $\leq 1$, excluding gauge fields. In this particular study, we investigate a rigidly rotating solution ($Ω= \text{const}$) at global equilibrium for massless fermionic matter. It has been shown that the applicability of a hydrodynamic description requires a hierarchy $ΩR \gg Ωβ_{0}$, where $R$ is the radius of the cylindrical-shaped rotating matter and $β_{0} = 1/T_{0}$ is the inverse temperature on the rotation axis. Consequently, the thermodynamic free energy $Φ$ depends on the angular velocity through the product $ΩR$, a dependency that extends to other thermodynamic variables as well. These findings are consistent with recent lattice QCD simulation results. Furthermore, we compute the moment of inertia for massless fermions and estimate the light quark contribution to the total moment of inertia of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The thermodynamic free energy of a rigidly rotating system depends on angular velocity through the product ΩR (where R is the system radius) rather than on Ω alone, but this holds only under the condition that a hydrodynamic description requires the hierarchy ΩR ≫ Ωβ₀, where β₀ is the inverse temperature on the rotation axis.

## Method

The author derives a field-theoretic version of the virial theorem by applying a spatial scaling (dilatation) transformation to the grand canonical density operator, which includes Poincaré charges (momentum and angular momentum) and matter charges. The scaling properties are examined through equal-time commutators among components of the energy-momentum tensor and charge currents, applicable to any renormalizable fields with spin ≤ 1 (excluding gauge fields). The formalism is then applied to a rigidly rotating solution (Ω = constant) at global equilibrium for massless fermionic matter, modeling light-quark contributions to the Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Key numbers

- Angular velocities in heavy-ion collisions reach Ω ∼ 10²² s⁻¹ (≈ 7 MeV), described as the highest vorticity ever observed in a laboratory setting.
- The condition for hydrodynamic validity requires R ≫ λ_th, where the thermal de-Broglie wavelength λ_th ∼ 1/T₀ for a massless system, leading to the hierarchy ΩR ≫ Ωβ₀.
- The causal boundary constraint requires ΩR ≤ 1 for any physical description of the rotating system.

## Context

This work extends the virial theorem formalism from classical and quantum statistical mechanics (Landau 1984, Huang 1963) to relativistic rotating thermal field theories, following the field-theoretic approach of Leermakers (1985). The finding that thermodynamic variables depend on ΩR rather than Ω alone is consistent with recent lattice QCD simulation results from Braguta (2020, 2023) and Chernodub (2016). The results enable estimation of the light quark contribution to the moment of inertia of the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, and provide a framework for understanding the interplay between rotation and finite system size in thermal QCD systems.
