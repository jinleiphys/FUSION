# Finite temperature effects on spin polarization of neutron matter in a strong magnetic field

> Spin polarization of neutron matter at finite temperatures and strong magnetic fields up to $10^{18}$ G is studied in the model with the Skyrme effective interaction. It is shown that, together with the thermodynamically stable branch of solutions for the spin polarization parameter corresponding to the case when the majority of neutron spins are oriented opposite to the direction of the magnetic field (negative spin polarization), the self-consistent equations, beginning from some threshold density, have also two other branches of solutions corresponding to positive spin polarization. The influence of finite temperatures on spin polarization remains moderate in the Skyrme model up to temperatures relevant for protoneutron stars, and, in particular, the scenario with the metastable state characterized by positive spin polarization, considered at zero temperature in Phys. Rev. C {\bf 80}, 065801 (2009), is preserved at finite temperatures as well. It is shown that above certain density the entropy for various branches of spin polarization in neutron matter with the Skyrme interaction in a strong magnetic field demonstrates the unusual behavior being larger than that of the nonpolarized state. By providing the corresponding low-temperature analysis, it is clarified that this unexpected behavior should be addressed to the dependence of the entropy of a spin polarized state on the effective masses of neutrons with spin up and spin down, and to a certain constraint on them which is violated in the respective density range.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at finite temperatures up to those relevant for protoneutron stars (several tens of MeV), the metastable state with positive spin polarization (majority of neutron spins oriented along the magnetic field) previously found at zero temperature in Isayev and Yang (2009) is preserved, but only in the high-density interior of magnetars where densities exceed a threshold value that depends on the magnetic field strength.

## Method

The authors use a Fermi-liquid approach with the Skyrme effective interaction (SLy7 parametrization) to solve self-consistent equations for the single-particle energy components and distribution functions of neutron matter in a strong magnetic field up to 10^18 G. They compute the spin polarization parameter, free energy, and entropy for different branches of solutions at finite temperatures, and compare the thermodynamic stability of states with positive and negative spin polarization by evaluating their free energies.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: up to 10^18 G, the maximum allowed by the scalar virial theorem for magnetar interiors
- Temperature studied: T = 20 MeV for the main spin polarization results, with additional calculations at T = 5 MeV for the entropy analysis
- Nuclear saturation density for SLy7 force: ρ₀ = 0.158 fm⁻³
- Threshold density for appearance of positive spin polarization branches: ρ_th, which is larger than the critical density of spontaneous spin instability and depends on magnetic field strength at the given temperature
- For the branch Π₁ (negative spin polarization), the difference D in the constraint on effective masses changes sign at density ρ_s ≈ 0.41 ρ₀ at H = 10^18 G and T = 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature study of neutron matter with Skyrme forces in strong magnetic fields by Isayev and Yang (2009) to finite temperatures relevant for protoneutron stars. It contradicts the finite-temperature calculations by Perez-Garcia (2008), which found only one branch of solutions for the spin polarization parameter (the thermodynamically stable state with negative spin polarization) using the same Skyrme interaction, missing the metastable positive polarization branches. The results enable future studies of how the metastable positive spin polarization state affects neutrino opacities and cooling histories of neutron stars, and motivate extension to more realistic neutron star matter including protons with charge neutrality and beta equilibrium conditions.
