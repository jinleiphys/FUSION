# Isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass from giant resonances in $^{208}$Pb

> Based on mean field calculations with Skyrme interactions, we extract a constraint on the isovector effective mass in nuclear matter at saturation density $ρ_0$, i.e., $m_{v}^{\ast}(ρ_0)=(0.77\pm0.03) m$ by combining the experimental data of the centroid energy of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) and the electric dipole polarizability $α_{\mathrm{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb. Meanwhile, the isoscalar effective mass at $ρ_0$ is determined to be $m_{s}^{\ast}(ρ_0)=(0.91\pm0.05) m$ by analyzing the measured excitation energy of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) in $^{208}$Pb. From the constrained $m_{s}^{\ast}(ρ_0)$ and $m_{v}^{\ast}(ρ_0)$, we obtain the isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass in asymmetric nuclear matter of isospin asymmetry $δ$ at $ρ_0$ as $[m_n^{\ast}(ρ_0,δ)-m_p^{\ast}(ρ_0,δ)]/m = Δm^*_1(ρ_0) δ+ O(δ^3)$ with the linear isospin splitting coefficient $Δm^*_1(ρ_0) = 0.33\pm0.16$. We notice that using the recently corrected data on the $α_{\mathrm{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb with the contribution of the quasideuteron effect subtracted slightly enhances the isovector effective mass to $m_{v}^{\ast}(ρ_0)=(0.80\pm0.03) m$ and reduces the linear isospin splitting coefficient to $Δm^*_1(ρ_0) = 0.27\pm0.15$. Furthermore, the constraints on $m_{v}^{\ast}(ρ)$, $m_{s}^{\ast}(ρ)$ and $Δm^*_1(ρ)$ at other densities are obtained from the similar analyses and we find that the $Δm^*_1(ρ)$ increases with the density.

## Key claim

The paper extracts constraints on the isoscalar and isovector nucleon effective masses at saturation density from giant resonance data in 208Pb, finding that the data favor a positive isospin splitting (neutrons heavier than protons in neutron-rich matter) with the caveat that using the corrected electric dipole polarizability value (with quasideuteron contribution subtracted) slightly reduces the linear isospin splitting coefficient.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations for 208Pb using 50 representative Skyrme interactions to establish linear correlations between the isoscalar effective mass and the ISGQR excitation energy, and between the isovector effective mass and the energy-weighted sum rule of the IVGDR. They then extracted the effective masses by combining these correlations with experimental data: the ISGQR centroid energy (10.9 ± 0.1 MeV) and the IVGDR centroid energy (13.46 MeV) together with the electric dipole polarizability measured at RCNP. The isospin splitting was derived from the difference between isoscalar and isovector effective masses using the Skyrme model relations.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar effective mass at saturation density: m_s*(ρ₀) = (0.91 ± 0.05) m, extracted from ISGQR excitation energy E_x = 10.9 ± 0.1 MeV in 208Pb
- Isovector effective mass at saturation density: m_v*(ρ₀) = (0.77 ± 0.03) m, extracted from IVGDR data with electric dipole polarizability α_D = 20.1 ± 0.6 fm³ in 208Pb
- Linear isospin splitting coefficient at saturation density: Δm*₁(ρ₀) = 0.33 ± 0.16
- Using corrected α_D = 19.6 ± 0.6 fm³ (quasideuteron subtracted): m_v*(ρ₀) = (0.80 ± 0.03) m and Δm*₁(ρ₀) = 0.27 ± 0.15
- Energy-weighted sum rule of IVGDR: m₁ = 905.60 ± 27.03 MeV·fm² (uncorrected) and m₁ = 883.05 ± 27.03 MeV·fm² (corrected)
- Enhancement factor κ = 0.228 ± 0.037 (uncorrected) and κ = 0.197 ± 0.037 (corrected)
- Third-order isospin splitting coefficient at ρ₀: Δm*₃(ρ₀) = 0.01 ± 0.01

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to constrain the isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass, which had yielded conflicting results from optical model analyses (XuC10, LiXH15) favoring positive splitting and transport model analyses (Cou14) suggesting the opposite. The results are consistent with constraints from global nucleon optical potentials (XuC10, LiXH15) and from symmetry energy analyses (LiBA13), and agree with microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Zuo99, Ma04, Dal05). The findings enable more reliable predictions for transport properties of asymmetric nuclear matter, neutrino emission in neutron stars, and primordial nucleosynthesis, and provide a benchmark for future theoretical calculations of effective masses at various densities.
