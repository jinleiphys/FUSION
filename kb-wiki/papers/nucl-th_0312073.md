# Predictions from Microscopic Models on Particle Correlations at RHIC

> We review the recent developments on microscopic transport calculations for two-particle correlations at low relative momenta in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC.

# Predictions from Microscopic Models on Particle Correlations at RHIC

## Key claim
Microscopic transport models can reproduce the overall magnitude of two-particle correlation radii at RHIC, but the detailed transverse-momentum dependence of the $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$ ratio (the "HBT puzzle") remains unresolved, with no single model simultaneously matching all features of the data.

## Method
The paper reviews predictions from five microscopic transport approaches applied to central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{nn}=200$ GeV: a simple hadronic rescattering model; the RQMD hadron+string model; the Hydro+UrQMD hybrid model coupling hydrodynamic expansion to a hadronic cascade; the MPC model of a classical gluon gas with elastic collisions; and the AMPT model combining parton scatterings with a hadron cascade. For each model, two-particle correlation functions are calculated from the freeze-out phase-space distributions and fitted to a Gaussian form to extract the HBT radius parameters $R_{\rm out}$, $R_{\rm side}$, and $R_{\rm long}$ as functions of transverse momentum $K_T$.

## Key numbers
- The RQMD model yields calculated radii typically $\sim 1$ fm larger than experimental data, with $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$ ratios of 1.2--1.4 compared to data values of 0.9--1.1.
- The Hydro+UrQMD model overpredicts $R_{\rm out}$ and $R_{\rm long}$ by $\sim 20$--$30\%$ relative to STAR and PHENIX data; agreement improves when the transition temperature from hydrodynamics to the microscopic phase is lowered from $T_{\rm switch}=160$ MeV to $T_{\rm switch}=130$ MeV.
- In the AMPT model with string fusion and an elastic parton cross section $\sigma_{\rm part}\approx 10$ mb, the calculations come closest to the data, achieving $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\approx 1$.
- The hadronic rescattering model, starting from an initial thermal state at $\tau_{\rm had}=1$ fm/c with $T=300$ MeV and Gaussian rapidity width $\sigma_y=2.4$, produces $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$ only slightly larger than unity.

## Context
This review extends earlier work on Bose-Einstein correlations in heavy ion collisions, particularly the prediction by Pratt (1986) and Wiedemann & Heinz (1999) that a first-order phase transition would produce anomalously large $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$ ratios (5 or 10), which was contradicted by experimental results from STAR (2001) showing ratios close to unity. The paper systematically compares multiple transport approaches to address whether short hadronization times or a cross-over transition (as suggested by lattice QCD) explain the data. The findings enable future work on nonidentical particle pair correlations to independently check the space-time correlations ($x_{\rm out},t$) that differ between hydrodynamical and transport models.
