# New insights in \bar{K}N scattering and the Λ(1405)

> We discuss in this talk aspects of \bar{K}N plus coupled channels dynamics with special emphasis on the Λ(1405) resonance and isovector resonances in the same energy region. We comment on several experimental reactions giving rise to πΣdistributions with important implications on the spectroscopy. We also discuss recent theoretical models based con NLO SU(3) unitarized chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is actually composed of two distinct poles in the complex energy plane, and isovector ($I=1$) resonances also exist near the $\bar{K}N$ threshold, though the isoscalar signal is stronger and has historically masked the isovector ones.

## Method

The author reviews unitarized chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) applied to S-wave $\bar{K}N$ scattering with ten coupled channels ($\pi^0\Lambda$, $\pi^0\Sigma^0$, $\pi^-\Sigma^+$, $\pi^+\Sigma^-$, $K^- p$, $\bar{K}^0 n$, $\eta\Lambda$, $\eta\Sigma^0$, $K^0 \Xi^+$, $K^+\Xi^-$). The approach resums the unitarity cut via a once-subtracted dispersion relation to handle the infrared enhancement from large baryon and meson masses, with interaction vertices calculated perturbatively in ChPT up to ${\cal O}(p^2)$. Pole positions are extracted in the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

* Two isoscalar poles for the $\Lambda(1405)$ from Ref. [plb]: lower pole at $1379 - i\,27$ MeV, higher pole at $1434 - i\,11$ MeV.
* From Ref. [ikeda2012], the lower $I=0$ pole interval: $1381^{+18}_{-6} - i\,81^{+19}_{-8}$ MeV; higher $I=0$ pole interval: $1424^{+7}_{-23} - i\,26^{+3}_{-14}$ MeV.
* From Ref. [guo2013], the lower $I=0$ pole interval: $(1380\sim 1450) - i(90\sim 150)$ MeV; higher $I=0$ pole interval: $(1413\sim 1424) - i(14\sim 31)$ MeV.
* $I=1$ poles from Ref. [guo2013] at $1376 - i\,33$ MeV and $1414 - i\,12$ MeV in the second Riemann sheet.
* The shift in peak positions between $\pi^+\Sigma^-$ and $\pi^-\Sigma^+$ differential cross sections in CLAS photoproduction data is around 30 MeV, with peak heights changing by around a factor of 2.
* The natural value for the subtraction constant $a$ is approximately $-2$, derived from $\Lambda_{\text{ChPT}} \simeq 1$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-pole interpretation of the $\Lambda(1405)$ first indicated by the cloudy bag model (Landau, 1980s) and independently established in unitarized ChPT by Oller and collaborators (2000). It also confirms the prediction of $I=1$ resonances near the $\bar{K}N$ threshold from the same framework. The results enable sharper determination of subthreshold $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes needed for kaon properties in nuclear matter, and motivate further analysis of CLAS photoproduction data and future measurements at J-PARC E31 and IKON/KLOE DA$\Phi$NE.
