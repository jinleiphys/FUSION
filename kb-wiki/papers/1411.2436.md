# Proton-proton fusion in lattice effective field theory

> The proton-proton fusion rate is calculated at low energy in a lattice effective field theory (EFT) formulation. The strong and the Coulomb interactions are treated non-perturbatively at leading order in the EFT. The lattice results are shown to accurately describe the low energy cross section within the validity of the theory at energies relevant to solar physics. In prior work in the literature, Coulomb effects were generally not included in non-perturbative lattice calculations. Work presented here is of general interest in nuclear lattice EFT calculations that involve Coulomb effects at low energy. It complements recent developments of the adiabatic projection method for lattice calculations of nuclear reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the spherical wall method, rather than Lüscher's method, can accurately reproduce the Coulomb-subtracted proton-proton phase shift and fusion rate in lattice effective field theory when both the strong and Coulomb interactions are treated non-perturbatively at leading order, with lattice results agreeing with analytical continuum results to within about 3% or less.

## Method

The authors discretize space on a periodic cubic lattice and define a short-ranged strong interaction potential (a Kronecker delta at the origin) plus the Coulomb potential (regulated at the origin with an additional delta coupling). They use a hard spherical wall boundary condition, with the wall radius determined from the free-particle spectrum on the lattice, to extract the Coulomb-subtracted s-wave phase shift from the vanishing of the wave function at the wall. The proton-proton fusion rate is then computed from the overlap integral of the incoming proton-proton wave function (determined by the lattice phase shift) and the analytic deuteron bound-state wave function.

## Key numbers

- The lattice calculations reproduce the Coulomb-subtracted phase shift to within about **3% or less** for momenta up to **p ≲ 30 MeV**.
- At zero energy, the lattice-extrapolated fusion matrix element is **Λ^(latt)(0) = 2.49 ± 0.02**, consistent with the leading-order continuum EFT result **Λ^(EFT) = 2.51**.
- Two lattice spacings are used: **b = 1/100 MeV⁻¹** (coupling ĉ₀ + d̂₀ = -0.4047) and **b = 1/200 MeV⁻¹** (coupling ĉ₀ + d̂₀ = -0.8330).
- The deuteron binding energy is taken as **B = 2.2246 MeV**, corresponding to binding momentum **γ = √(2μB)**.
- The proton-proton scattering length used is **a_p = -7.82 fm** (with effective range r = 0 at leading order).
- The solar Gamow-peak energy is approximately **E ∼ 6 keV**, where Λ(E) deviates no more than a few percent from Λ(0).

## Context

This work extends prior lattice EFT calculations of nuclear reactions (Rupak 2013, Pine 2013) by including the long-range Coulomb force non-perturbatively, which was generally absent in earlier non-perturbative lattice calculations. It complements the adiabatic projection method (Elhatisari 2014) for calculating nuclear reactions on the lattice. The results enable future lattice EFT calculations of low-energy nuclear reactions involving compound nuclei, where Coulomb effects are important for astrophysical applications.
