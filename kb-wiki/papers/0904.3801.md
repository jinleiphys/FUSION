# On the Exact Eigenstates and the Ground States Based on the Boson Realization for Many-Quark Model with su(4) Algebraic Structure

> Based on the boson realization, the ground state and phase structure are investigated in a many-quark model with algebraic structure developed in our previous paper, in which the two-body pairing and particle-hole type interactions are active. The physical interpretation of the exact eigenstates obtained in the boson realization in our previous paper is given in a many-quark model with su(4) algebraic structure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, in a many-quark model with su(4) algebraic structure (the modified Bonn model), the ground state undergoes a phase transition from a colored superconducting state to a color-neutral triplet state as the coupling strength of the particle-hole type interaction increases beyond a certain critical value, but this conclusion depends on the specific parameterization and the assumption that the particle-hole interaction breaks the su(4) symmetry while preserving su(3) color symmetry.

## Method

The authors use a Schwinger boson realization to map the su(4) algebra of fermion bilinears onto boson operators, obtaining exact eigenstates and energy eigenvalues for the model Hamiltonian that includes both pairing interactions (with coupling G set to 1) and a particle-hole type interaction proportional to the su(3) Casimir operator (with coupling χ). They then analyze the energy minima for two classes of states, pairing-type states (where fermion number changes in units of 2) and triplet-formation states (where fermion number changes in units of 3), and determine which configuration yields the lowest energy as a function of χ and total quark number N.

## Key numbers

* For the color superconducting state (pairing correlation with n₀=0, n₁=2Ω, ρ=q'/2), the energy eigenvalue at χ=0 is E = -q'(2Ω+3-q'), where q' is the number of paired quarks with colors 2 and 3.
* For the color-neutral triplet state (τ=3/2, λ=0, n₀=0), the energy eigenvalue at χ=0 is E = -(N/3)(2Ω+3-N/3), where N is the total quark number.
* The critical coupling strength for the triplet formation case is given by Eq.(I·5·25) or (I·5·26) from the previous paper (I), which separates the regime where colored states are energetically favorable from the regime where the color-neutral triplet state is favored.
* For the pairing correlation case with n₀=0, the minimum energy is given analytically in three regimes of χ: (1) -1/6·(Ω+6)/(Ω+2) ≤ χ ≤ -1/6, (2) -1/6 < χ ≤ (1/6)(2Ω-3), and (3) χ ≥ (1/6)(2Ω-3), with different expressions for the energy E(N) and the optimal color-1 quark number n₁ depending on N.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous papers (I) and (II), where exact eigenstates and energy eigenvalues were obtained for the modified Bonn model using boson realization. The original Bonn model (Petry et al.) has su(4) symmetry and yields a color superconducting ground state, but the present model introduces a particle-hole type interaction that breaks su(4) down to su(3) color symmetry, allowing the color-neutral triplet state to become favorable at sufficiently large χ. The results enable a phase diagram for the many-quark system and provide a benchmark for approximate methods such as the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach.
