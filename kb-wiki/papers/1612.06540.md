# Magnetic fields in turbulent quark matter and magnetar bursts

> We analyze the magnetic field evolution in dense quark matter with unbroken chiral symmetry, which can be found inside quark and hybrid stars. The magnetic field evolves owing to the chiral magnetic effect in the presence of the electroweak interaction between quarks. In our study, we also take into account the magnetohydrodynamic turbulence effects in dense quark matter. We derive the kinetic equations for the spectra of the magnetic helicity density and the magnetic energy density as well as for the chiral imbalances. On the basis of the numerical solution of these equations, we find that turbulence effects are important for the behavior of small scale magnetic fields. It is revealed that, under certain initial conditions, these magnetic fields behave similarly to the electromagnetic flashes of some magnetars. We suggest that fluctuations of magnetic fields, described in frames of our model, which are created in the central regions of a magnetized compact star, can initiate magnetar bursts.

## Key claim

The paper claims that magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) turbulence effects are important for the behavior of small-scale magnetic fields in dense quark matter, and that under certain initial conditions these magnetic field fluctuations can behave similarly to the electromagnetic flashes of some magnetars. The hedge is that this behavior occurs only for specific initial conditions and only for small-scale magnetic fields (scales of 1-10^3 cm), not for large-scale fields.

## Method

The authors derived kinetic equations for the spectra of magnetic helicity density, magnetic energy density, and chiral imbalances of u and d quarks in dense quark matter, accounting for the chiral magnetic effect, electroweak interactions between quarks, and MHD turbulence effects. They numerically solved these coupled integro-differential equations for two scenarios: hybrid stars (with only u and d quarks, n_d = 2n_u) and quark stars (with u, d, and s quarks in equal proportions), using initial Kolmogorov-type spectra for the magnetic energy density and initial chiral imbalances of 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial seed magnetic field: B_0 = 10^12 G for all simulations
- Initial chiral imbalances: μ_{5u}(0) = μ_{5d}(0) = 1 MeV
- Quark matter conductivity at reference temperature: σ_0 = 3.15 × 10^22 s^{-1} at T_0 ~ (10^8 - 10^9) K
- Fermi constant: G_F = 1.17 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- Mean chemical potential of quarks: μ_0 = 346 MeV for n_0 = 1.8 × 10^38 cm^{-3}
- Effective electroweak potentials in HS: V_{5u} = 4.5 eV, V_{5d} = 2.9 eV
- Effective electroweak potentials in QS: V_{5u} = 1.1 eV, V_{5d} = 4.1 eV
- Maximal magnetic field reaches approximately 30% of the saturated value: B_max ≈ 0.3 B_sat
- Reynolds numbers in HS/QS: Re ~ 10^8, Re_B ~ 10^16
- Fine structure constant: α_em = 7.3 × 10^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the author's previous model (Dvornikov 2016) for magnetic field amplification in dense quark matter driven by electroweak interactions, which itself built on earlier work about magnetic field instability from parity-violating interactions (Boyarsky, Ruchayskiy, Shaposhnikov 2015). The paper specifically incorporates MHD turbulence effects that were previously studied in the context of the early universe plasma (Dvornikov and Semikoz 2016) and in other chiral MHD studies (Vilenkin 2017, Rogachevskii 2017). The results suggest that small-scale magnetic field fluctuations in the central regions of magnetized compact stars could initiate magnetar bursts, potentially addressing the open question of how internal magnetic energy transfers to the stellar magnetosphere.
