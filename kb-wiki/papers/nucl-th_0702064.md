# Application of density dependent parametrization models to asymmetric nuclear matter

> Density dependent parametrization models of the nucleon-meson effective couplings, including the isovector scalar δ-field, are applied to asymmetric nuclear matter. The nuclear equation of state and the neutron star properties are studied in an effective Lagrangian density approach, using the relativistic mean field hadron theory. It is known that the introduction of a δ-meson in the constant coupling scheme leads to an increase of the symmetry energy at high density and so to larger neutron star masses, in a pure nucleon-lepton scheme. We use here a more microscopic density dependent model of the nucleon-meson couplings to study the properties of neutron star matter and to re-examine the δ-field effects in asymmetric nuclear matter. Our calculations show that, due to the increase of the effective δcoupling at high density, with density dependent couplings the neutron star masses in fact can be even reduced.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, contrary to the constant-coupling relativistic mean-field (RMF) scheme where including the isovector scalar δ-meson increases the symmetry energy at high density and leads to larger neutron star masses, in a density-dependent coupling (DDRH) model the δ-field can actually reduce the maximum neutron star mass, because the effective δ coupling increases at high density while the ρ coupling decreases.

## Method

The authors apply a density-dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) theory, with meson-nucleon couplings taken as functions of the baryon density, to asymmetric nuclear matter including the isovector scalar δ-meson. They solve the mean-field equations for β-equilibrated (npe) and (npeμ) matter, compute the equation of state, and then integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star masses and radii. The density-dependent parametrizations for σ, ω, ρ, and δ couplings are taken from Dirac-Brueckner (DB) calculations and from the TW99 and related parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³; binding energy at saturation: E/A = −16.25 MeV; compressibility modulus: K = 240 MeV.
- For (npe) matter with DDRHρ (no δ): maximum neutron star mass M_S = 2.108 M_⊙, radius R = 11.00 km, central density ρ_c/ρ₀ = 6.99.
- For (npe) matter with DDRHρδ (with δ): maximum mass M_S = 2.01 M_⊙, radius R = 10.29 km, central density ρ_c/ρ₀ = 7.41.
- For (npe) matter with NLρ (constant couplings, no δ): maximum mass M_S = 2.14 M_⊙, radius R = 11.02 km, central density ρ_c/ρ₀ = 6.78.
- For (npe) matter with NLρδ (constant couplings, with δ): maximum mass M_S = 2.21 M_⊙, radius R = 11.55 km, central density ρ_c/ρ₀ = 6.44.
- For (npeμ) matter with DDRHρδ: maximum mass M_S = 1.98 M_⊙, radius R = 10.27 km, central density ρ_c/ρ₀ = 7.44.
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 31.3 MeV (for both NL and DDRH models at ρ₀).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Liu et al. (2002, 2005) that introduced the δ-meson in constant-coupling RMF models, where it increased the symmetry energy at high density and produced heavier neutron stars. The present paper applies the more microscopic density-dependent coupling approach (TW99; Fuchs 1995; Hofmann 2001) based on Dirac-Brueckner calculations (Jong 1998; van Dalen 2004) to re-examine the δ-field effects. The results show that the density dependence of the couplings, particularly the increase of g_δ at high density, reverses the trend seen in constant-coupling models, leading to softer asymmetric matter and lower neutron star masses. This highlights the need for further DB calculations of the high-density behavior of the δ coupling and for dynamical studies of isovector meson effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions with exotic beams.
