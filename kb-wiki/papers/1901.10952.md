# Future heavy-ion facilities: FCC-AA

> The operation of the Future Circular Collider (FCC) with heavy ions would provide Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at sqrt{s_NN}= 39 and 63 TeV, respectively, per nucleon-nucleon collision, with projected per-month integrated luminosities of up to 110/nb and 29/pb, respectively. This document outlines the unique and broad physics opportunities with heavy ions at the energy frontier opened by FCC.

## Key claim

The Future Circular Collider (FCC) with heavy-ion beams would provide Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 and 63 TeV, respectively, with per-month integrated luminosities up to 110/nb and 29/pb, enabling novel studies of the Quark-Gluon Plasma and gluon saturation at the energy frontier, though the performance projections assume the LHC as the final injector and include efficiency factors for setup time and beam aborts.

## Method

The paper presents a conceptual design study for heavy-ion operation of the FCC-hh, using the relation sqrt(s_NN) = sqrt(s) * sqrt(Z1*Z2/A1*A2) to derive nucleon-nucleon collision energies from the 100 TeV pp center-of-mass energy. Beam parameters were calculated for baseline and ultimate scenarios differing in beta-function at the interaction point and bunch spacing, with luminosity projections based on the LHC as the final injector and including a performance efficiency factor. Global event characteristics were extrapolated from LHC measurements using a power-law scaling dN_ch/dη ∝ (sqrt(s_NN))^0.3, and hard process cross sections were computed at NNLO using MCFM and top++.

## Key numbers

* Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39.4 TeV; p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.8 TeV
* Per-month integrated luminosity for Pb-Pb: 35/nb (baseline) and 110/nb (ultimate) for one experiment
* Per-month integrated luminosity for p-Pb: 8000/pb (baseline) and 29000/pb (ultimate) for one experiment
* Peak luminosity for Pb-Pb: 80 x 10^27 cm^-2 s^-1 (baseline) and 320 x 10^27 cm^-2 s^-1 (ultimate)
* Charged hadron multiplicity at midrapidity in central Pb-Pb: dN_ch/dη = 3600 at 39 TeV (extrapolated from 1600 at 2.76 TeV)
* Energy density at tau = 1 fm/c in central Pb-Pb: 35-40 GeV/fm^3 at 39 TeV (compared to 12-13 GeV/fm^3 at 2.76 TeV)
* Initial QGP temperature estimated at T0 ≈ 800-1000 MeV at time O(0.02 fm/c) at FCC energies
* Cross section increase from LHC to FCC: factor ~6 for beauty production, ~10 for Z production, ~20 for Z+jet, ~80 for top production
* Top quark pT reach in Pb-Pb: up to approximately 1.8 TeV/c in baseline luminosity scenario
* Higgs boson observation significance in gamma-gamma channel: 5.5 sigma (baseline) and 9.5 sigma (ultimate) in one Pb-Pb run
* Thermal charm production in central Pb-Pb at FCC: final yield larger by up to 80% compared to initial hard-scattering production
* Bottom quark pair cross section in nucleon-nucleon collisions: dσ_bbbar/dy = 73 to 163 μb at NLO

## Context

This paper extends the heavy-ion physics programme from the LHC to the FCC energy frontier, building on earlier studies documented in the CERN Yellow Report (Dainese et al., 2016) and the FCC Conceptual Design Report (Mangano et al., 2018). The work is complementary to proposed electron-hadron colliders such as the Electron-Ion Collider in the USA (Accardi et al., 2012), the LHeC (Abelleira Fernandez et al., 2012), and FCC-he, which would provide complementary kinematic coverage for studying parton saturation. The projected integrated luminosities are larger by factors 10-30 than current projections for future LHC runs (Citron et al., 2018), enabling novel measurements such as boosted top quarks for probing the space-time evolution of jet quenching (Apolinario et al., 2017) and thermal charm production in the QGP (Zhou et al., 2016; Liu et al., 2016).
