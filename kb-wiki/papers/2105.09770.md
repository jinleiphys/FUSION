# Event topology and constituent-quark scaling of elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider using a multiphase transport model

> Transverse spherocity is an event shape observable, which separates the events based on their geometrical shapes. In this work, we use transverse spherocity to study the identified light flavor production in heavy-ion collisions using A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model. We obtain the elliptic flow coefficients for pions, kaons and protons in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV as a function of transverse spherocity and collision centrality. Also, we study the number of constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow which interprets the dominance of the quark degrees of freedom at the early stages of the collision. We observe a clear dependence of the elliptic flow for identified particles on transverse spherocity. It is found that the NCQ-scaling is strongly violated in events with low transverse spherocity compared to transverse spherocity-integrated events, confirming the fragmentation-based hadronization mechanism for high-momentum partons involved in the dynamics of jetty-like events.

## Key claim

The paper claims that number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow is strongly violated in events with low transverse spherocity compared to spherocity-integrated events in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, which the authors interpret as confirmation of a fragmentation-based hadronization mechanism for high-momentum partons in jetty-like events. The hedge is that this conclusion is drawn from simulations using the AMPT model with string melting, not from experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model version 2.26t9b in string melting mode to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They classified events into low-transverse-spherocity (lowest 20%) and high-transverse-spherocity (highest 20%) classes, then calculated elliptic flow coefficients (v_2) for pions, kaons, and protons using two-particle correlation methods with pseudorapidity cuts of 2.0 < |Delta-eta| < 4.8 to remove non-flow effects. The single-particle v_2 was extracted via factorization of the two-particle flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV were simulated for centrality classes 0-10%, 40-50%, and 60-70%
- The low-S_0 cut for 0-10% centrality was 0-0.88025 and the high-S_0 cut was 0.95285-1
- The low-S_0 cut for 40-50% centrality was 0-0.71475 and the high-S_0 cut was 0.86365-1
- The low-S_0 cut for 60-70% centrality was 0-0.70775 and the high-S_0 cut was 0.87355-1
- Particles were selected with p_T > 0.5 GeV/c and |eta| < 2.5 for the correlation analysis
- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV were simulated for 40-50% centrality
- The paper reports that LHC results show a deviation of 20% for NCQ and transverse kinetic energy scaling of v_2 at intermediate/high transverse momenta

## Context

This work extends the use of transverse spherocity, previously applied to small collision systems by Cuautle (2014) and Tripathy (2019), to heavy-ion collisions for the first time to study identified particle elliptic flow. It builds on the authors' prior work (Mallick 2021) showing strong anti-correlation of spherocity with v_2 for unidentified charged particles. The study complements event shape engineering techniques such as flow vector selection used by Schukraft (2012) and Aad (2015), and tests NCQ scaling predictions from coalescence models by Molnar (2003), Greco (2003), and Fries (2003) against fragmentation-based hadronization. The results suggest that spherocity selection can isolate events where NCQ scaling is violated, enabling future experimental tests at RHIC and LHC.
