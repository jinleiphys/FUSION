# The spin-orbit potential and Poincaré invariance in finite temperature pNRQCD

> Heavy quarkonium at finite temperature has been the subject of intense theoretical studies, for it provides a potentially clean probe of the quark-gluon plasma. Recent studies have made use of effective field theories to exploit in a systematic manner the hierarchy of energy scales that characterize the system. In the case of a quarkonium in a medium whose temperature is smaller than the typical momentum transfer in the bound state but larger than its energy, the suitable effective field theory is pNRQCD_HTL, where degrees of freedom with energy or momentum larger than the binding energy have been integrated out. Thermal effects are expected to break Poincaré invariance, which, at zero temperature, manifests itself in a set of exact relations between the matching coefficients of the effective field theory. In the paper, we evaluate the leading-order thermal corrections to the spin-orbit potentials of pNRQCD_HTL and show that Poincaré invariance is indeed violated.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates leading-order thermal corrections to the spin-orbit potentials in pNRQCD_HTL and demonstrates that Poincaré invariance, which at zero temperature imposes exact relations between matching coefficients (such as the Gromes relation), is violated at finite temperature due to the presence of a preferred reference frame introduced by the thermal bath.

## Method

The authors compute the leading thermal corrections to the colour-singlet static potential and the centre-of-mass momentum-dependent part of the spin-orbit potential in pNRQCD_HTL by matching real-time propagators in pNRQCD with the corresponding expressions in pNRQCD_HTL, assuming a weakly coupled quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma at rest with temperature satisfying m >> m*alpha_s >> T >> m*alpha_s^2. They evaluate five classes of diagrams (delta V_{LS,a} through delta V_{LS,e}) that contribute to the thermal correction of the spin-orbit potential, using dimensional regularization to handle scaleless non-thermal parts and expanding octet propagators for momenta k_0 ~ T much larger than the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The hierarchy assumed is m >> m*alpha_s >> T >> m*alpha_s^2, where m is the heavy-quark mass, alpha_s is the strong coupling, and T is the temperature.
- The leading thermal correction to the static potential is delta V_s^{(0)}(r) = (2*pi/9)*C_F*alpha_s*(V_{so}^{(0,1)}(r))^2*T^2*r^2*(V_o^{(0)}(r)-V_s^{(0)}(r)), with V_o^{(0)}(r)-V_s^{(0)}(r) = N_c*alpha_s/(2r).
- The leading thermal correction to the singlet normalization is delta Z_s(r) = -(pi/9)*C_F*alpha_s*(V_{so}^{(0,1)}(r))^2*T^2*r^2.
- The leading thermal correction to the centre-of-mass momentum-dependent spin-orbit potential delta V_{LS,sa}(r) receives contributions of size m*alpha_s^5 >> delta V_{LS}*(r x P)*(sigma^{(1)}-sigma^{(2)})/m^2 >> m*alpha_s^8.
- The contribution delta V_{LS,d}(r) is suppressed relative to delta V_{LS,a}, delta V_{LS,b}, and delta V_{LS,c} by at least a factor alpha_s^3.
- The contributions delta V_{LS,b} and delta V_{LS,e} exactly cancel in the sum, as V_{so}^{(1,1)} = V_{so}^{(0,1)} from Poincaré invariance at zero temperature.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory framework for heavy quarkonium at finite temperature developed by Brambilla (2008), Escobedo (2008), and others, specifically building on pNRQCD_HTL where degrees of freedom with energy or momentum larger than the binding energy have been integrated out. The paper shows that the Gromes relation, which at zero temperature links the spin-orbit potential to the static potential and was derived by Gromes (1984) from Lorentz boost properties of Wilson loops, is violated at finite temperature, enabling future studies of how heavy quarkonium moving with a velocity relative to the thermal bath experiences modified spin-dependent forces.
