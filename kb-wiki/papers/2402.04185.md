# Probing the internal structures of $pΩ$ and $ΩΩ$ with their production at the LHC

> The strange dibaryons $pΩ$ ($^5\rm{S}_2$) and $ΩΩ$ ($^1\rm{S}_0$) are likely bound, existing either in molecular states like the deuteron or as more exotic compact six-quark states. Here, we investigate the production of these two dibaryons in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) within a covariant coalescence model, which employs a blast-wave-like parametrization for the phase-space configurations of constituent particles at freeze-out. For the molecular states, the $pΩ$ and $ΩΩ$ are produced via $p$-$Ω$ and $Ω$-$Ω$ coalescence, respectively, while for the six-quark states, they are formed through $uudsss$ and $ssssss$ coalescence. We find that the yield ratio $N_{pΩ}/N_Ω$ and $N_{ΩΩ}/N_Ω$ have a distinct centrality dependence between the molecular and multi-quark states, thus offering a promising way for distinguishing the two states. Our results suggest that the measurements of $pΩ$ and $ΩΩ$ production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can shed light on their internal structures.

## Key claim

The yield ratios \(N_{p\Omega}/N_{\Omega}\) and \(N_{\Omega\Omega}/N_{\Omega}\) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV show a distinct centrality dependence between the molecular and multi-quark state scenarios, providing a way to distinguish the two internal structures; however, the absolute yields alone are insufficient for this distinction because they depend on both the internal structure and the implementation of the coalescence model.

## Method

The authors employed a covariant coalescence model with a blast-wave-like parametrization for the phase-space configurations of constituent particles at freeze-out. For molecular states, \(p\Omega\) and \(\Omega\Omega\) were produced via \(p\)-\(\Omega\) and \(\Omega\)-\(\Omega\) hadron coalescence, respectively; for six-quark states, they were formed through \(uudsss\) and \(ssssss\) quark coalescence. The freeze-out parameters for nucleons and \(\Omega\) hyperons (FOPb-\(p\) and FOPb-\(\Omega\)) were obtained by fitting experimental spectra of protons, \(\Omega\), deuterons, and \(^3\)He, while quark freeze-out parameters (FOPb-Q) were obtained by fitting measured spectra of \(p\) and \(\Omega^-\). The cluster yields were calculated as a function of root-mean-square radius using harmonic oscillator wave functions, and the centrality dependence of yield ratios was examined.

## Key numbers

* In 0-10% centrality, the yield (d\(N\)/d\(y\)) of \(p\Omega\) from hadron coalescence ranges from \(6.41\times 10^{-4}\) to \(1.32 \times 10^{-3}\) for root-mean-square radii from 0.5 to 5.0 fm.
* In 0-10% centrality, the yield of \(\Omega\Omega\) from hadron coalescence ranges from \(6.25\times10^{-7}\) to \(1.56\times10^{-6}\) for root-mean-square radii from 0.5 to 5.0 fm.
* In 0-10% centrality, the yield of \(p\Omega\) from quark coalescence ranges from \(3.89 \times 10^{-5}\) to \(1.54 \times 10^{-3}\) for root-mean-square radii from 0.5 to 5.0 fm.
* In 0-10% centrality, the yield of \(\Omega\Omega\) from quark coalescence ranges from \(2.49\times10^{-7}\) to \(8.80\times10^{-6}\) for root-mean-square radii from 0.5 to 5.0 fm.
* At the estimated root-mean-square radii from lattice QCD, the yield of \(p\Omega\) is \(9.92\times10^{-4}\) at \(r_{\rm rms}=3.24\) fm (hadron coalescence) and \(6.77\times10^{-4}\) at \(r_{\rm rms}=1.30\) fm (quark coalescence).
* At the estimated root-mean-square radii from lattice QCD, the yield of \(\Omega\Omega\) is \(8.13\times10^{-7}\) at \(r_{\rm rms}=4.12\) fm (hadron coalescence) and \(3.45\times10^{-6}\) at \(r_{\rm rms}=1.12\) fm (quark coalescence).
* The binding energy of \(p\Omega\) is taken as 2.46 MeV (with Coulomb attraction) from lattice QCD by HAL QCD (2018).
* The binding energy of \(\Omega\Omega\) is taken as 0.7 MeV (without Coulomb attraction) from lattice QCD by Gongyo et al. (2017).

## Context

This work extends previous coalescence model studies of dibaryon production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly those by the ExHIC collaboration (2011, 2017), and uses lattice QCD results from HAL QCD (2018) and Gongyo et al. (2017) for binding energies and root-mean-square radii. The STAR Collaboration (2018) measured the \(p\)-\(\Omega\) correlation function in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, suggesting a bound state with binding energy 27 MeV, while ALICE (2020) found that lattice QCD predictions underestimate their correlation data in p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=13\) TeV. The present results enable experimental differentiation of molecular versus compact six-quark states through centrality-dependent yield ratio measurements at the LHC, and suggest that measurements of \(p\Omega\) and \(\Omega\Omega\) production can shed light on their internal structures and the nature of the strong interaction.
