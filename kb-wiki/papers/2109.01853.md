# Dark matter admixed neutron star as a possible compact component in the GW190814 merger event

> We put constraints on the secondary component of GW190814 by analyzing the observational data of the event. The relativistic mean-field models are used to calculate the mass-radius profile and tidal deformability of the compact object, considering it as a massive neutron star with the presence of dark matter particles inside it. With the increase of dark matter percentage, the maximum mass, radius, and tidal deformability of the neutron star decreases. We observe that the predicted properties are well consistent with GW190814 observational data, suggesting the possibility of a dark matter admixed neutron star if the underlying nuclear equation of state is sufficiently stiff.

## Key claim

The secondary component of GW190814 (mass 2.50–2.67 \(M_\odot\)) could be a dark matter admixed neutron star, but only if the underlying nuclear equation of state is sufficiently stiff and the dark matter Fermi momentum is in a specific range (0.03–0.04 GeV), which reduces the maximum mass from above 2.67 \(M_\odot\) down to the observed range.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with nonlinear sigma-self-coupling interactions (NL-RMF parameter sets NL3, NL3*, NL1, NL-SH, NL3-II, NL-RA1) to compute the equation of state of neutron star matter. They added nonannihilating weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs) of mass 200 GeV that interact with nucleons via the Standard Model Higgs boson, with DM-Higgs coupling \(y=0.07\) and nucleon-Higgs form factor \(f=0.35\). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius profiles and tidal deformabilities for different dark matter Fermi momenta ranging from 0 to 0.06 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The secondary component of GW190814 has a mass range of 2.50–2.67 \(M_\odot\).
- For a dark matter Fermi momentum of 0.03 GeV (DM3), the maximum masses for the six NL-RMF parameter sets range from 2.64 to 2.71 \(M_\odot\); for 0.04 GeV (DM4), they range from 2.49 to 2.55 \(M_\odot\).
- The canonical radius \(R_{1.4}\) for DM3 lies between 12.38 and 12.75 km across the parameter sets, consistent with the GW190814 constraint of 12.20–13.70 km.
- The dimensionless tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) for DM3 ranges from 784.59 to 932.99, consistent with the GW190814 constraint of \(\Lambda_{1.4}=616_{-158}^{+273}\).
- The DM-nucleon scattering cross section for \(M_\chi=200\) GeV is \(9.70\times10^{-46}\) cm\(^2\), consistent with XENON-1T, PandaX-II, and LUX limits.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Huang et al. (2020), who suggested the secondary component could be a pure hadronic neutron star using density-dependent RMF (DD-RMF) sets, but those sets predict maximum masses only up to about 2.56 \(M_\odot\). The present study uses stiffer NL-RMF parameter sets that predict masses above 2.67 \(M_\odot\) and shows that adding dark matter can reduce the mass into the GW190814 range while satisfying constraints from GW170817 (Abbott et al., 2017, 2018), NICER data (Miller et al., 2019; Riley et al., 2019), and causality. This enables future gravitational-wave and x-ray observations to potentially constrain the dark matter fraction inside neutron stars.
