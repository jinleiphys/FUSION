# On Magnetized Neutron Stars

> In this work we review the formalism normally used in the literature about the effects of density-dependent magnetic fields on the properties of neutron and quark stars, expose some ambiguities that arise and propose a way to solve the related problem. Our approach explores more deeply the concept of pressure, yielding the so called chaotic magnetic field formalism for the stress tensor. We also use a different way of introducing a variable magnetic field, which depends on the energy density rather than on the baryonic density, which allows us to build a parameter free model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard formalism for including density-dependent magnetic fields in neutron star models suffers from severe ambiguities due to both the anisotropic stress tensor and arbitrary free parameters; the authors propose that using a chaotic magnetic field approximation combined with an energy-density-dependent magnetic field can reduce these uncertainties and yield a parameter-free model for certain values of the exponent gamma.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) framework with the GM1 parametrization and include hyperons via SU(3)/SU(6) coupling constants. They compare the standard approach of adding B^2/8π to both energy density and pressure against their proposed chaotic magnetic field formalism where the magnetic pressure is B^2/24π, and introduce a new prescription where the magnetic field depends on energy density rather than baryon density. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for neutron stars with and without hyperons, and also apply the MIT bag model for quark stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass variation from standard formalism: from 0 M⊙ (Set IV) to 1.08 M⊙ (Set VI) for stars with hyperons
- Maximum mass variation with chaotic field formalism: ΔM < 0.18 M⊙ for all parameter sets
- Maximum mass variation with energy-density-dependent field (γ ≥ 2.0): ΔM ≤ 0.05 M⊙
- Canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star radius at zero field: 13.8 km; with energy-density-dependent field and γ ≥ 2.0: 13.6 km
- Upper limit for isotropic treatment of magnetic field: 3.1 × 10^18 G
- Quark star maximum mass increase: up to 17% with standard formalism, up to 8% with chaotic field approximation
- Quark star maximum mass reaches 2.03 M⊙ at B0 = 9 × 10^18 G with standard formalism

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent magnetic field approach introduced by Pal (1999) and widely adopted by subsequent authors including Mao, Rabhi, Menezes, and others, by addressing the anisotropy problem noted in the textbooks of Misner and Zel'dovich. The chaotic magnetic field formalism was previously suggested by Zel'dovich for treating magnetic fields in spherical symmetry. The paper enables a more controlled study of magnetic effects on neutron star structure and connects to observational constraints from Hebeler (2013) on neutron star radii, while also addressing the two-solar-mass neutron star observations of Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013).
