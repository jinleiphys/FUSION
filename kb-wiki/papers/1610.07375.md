# Scattering states of Dirac particle equation with position dependent mass under the cusp potential

> We solved the one-dimensional position-dependent mass Dirac equation in the presence of the cusp potential and reported the solutions in terms of the Whittaker functions. We have derived the reflection and transmission coefficients by making use of the matching conditions on the wave functions. The effect of position dependent mass on the reflection and transmission coefficients of the system is duly investigated.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for a one-dimensional Dirac particle with position-dependent mass under a cusp potential, the reflection and transmission coefficients can be obtained analytically in terms of Whittaker functions, but only under the simplifying limit where the mass parameter \(m_1\) is small (\(m_1 \rightarrow 0\)) so that terms containing the derivative of the mass in the differential equations can be dropped.

## Method
The authors solved the one-dimensional Dirac equation with a position-dependent mass of cusp form \(m(x)=m_0+m_1[\theta(-x)e^{x/a}+\theta(x)e^{-x/a}]\) and a cusp potential \(V(x)=V_0[\theta(-x)e^{x/a}+\theta(x)e^{-x/a}]\). By introducing auxiliary components \(\phi(x)\) and \(\chi(x)\), they reduced the coupled first-order equations to uncoupled second-order differential equations, which were transformed into Whittaker differential equations in the negative and positive spatial regions. The reflection and transmission coefficients were derived by applying matching conditions on the wave functions and their first derivatives at \(x=0\), using asymptotic plane-wave forms at \(\pm\infty\).

## Key numbers
- The rest mass parameter \(m_0\) is set to \(0.1\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) in the numerical examples.
- For a low mass parameter \(m_1=0.01\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), a single resonance peak appears at the transmission threshold energy \(E=m_0=0.01\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- For \(m_1=0.05\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), a pronounced resonance peak occurs at energy \(E=2.45\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The potential barrier height \(V_0\) is taken as \(0.4\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) in the main figures, and the length parameter \(a\) is varied between \(2.5\ \text{fm}\) and \(3\ \text{fm}\).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the cusp potential within the Dirac equation (Chabab et al., earlier works) and the Klein-Gordon equation (Chabab et al., 2014) to the case of position-dependent mass. It also builds on the position-dependent mass formalism introduced by Von Roos (1983) for the Schrodinger equation, which has been applied in condensed matter physics and nuclear physics. The results enable further investigation of transmission resonances and supercritical states in relativistic quantum systems with spatially varying mass, particularly for potentials that support zero-energy resonances as discussed in earlier work by Senn (1988) and Nogami et al. (1996).
