# Systematic analysis of transverse momentum distribution and non-extensive thermodynamics theory

> A systematic analysis of transverse momentum distribution of hadrons produced in ultra-relativistic $p+p$ and $A+A$ collisions is presented. We investigate the effective temperature and the entropic parameter from the non-extensive thermodynamic theory of strong interaction. We conclude that the existence of a limiting effective temperature and of a limiting entropic parameter is in accordance with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that experimental data on transverse momentum distributions from ultra-relativistic p+p and A+A collisions are consistent with the existence of a limiting effective temperature and a limiting entropic parameter predicted by non-extensive thermodynamics, but they note that the temperature and entropic parameter are strongly correlated, meaning that good fits can be obtained with different pairs of (T, q) values near the optimum.

## Method

The authors fitted the non-extensive Tsallis-type transverse momentum distribution formula (Equation pt) to experimental data from p+p collisions at center-of-mass energies from 0.2 TeV to 7 TeV and from Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at various centralities. To avoid the strong correlation between the temperature T and the entropic parameter q, they then fixed T at values between 70 MeV and 100 MeV and performed fits with only q as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- For p+p collisions, the temperature T varies between 70 MeV and 90 MeV for center-of-mass energies from 0.2 TeV up to 7 TeV.
- For energies above 1 TeV, the temperature can be considered constant at T approximately 73 MeV.
- Fits with fixed T = 90 MeV produced good results for all p+p and A+A collision systems studied.
- The entropic parameter q remains below 1.2 for all data, consistent with the integrability limit for thermodynamic functions.
- For p+p collisions at 0.9 TeV, prior work by Cleyman and Worku found T approximately 75 MeV and q approximately 1.15.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent non-extensive thermodynamic theory of strong interactions developed by Deppman (2010) and earlier work by Hagedorn using Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, which breaks down above center-of-mass energies of approximately 10 GeV. The analysis confirms that a fixed temperature can describe data across a wide energy range, enabling future studies of the entropic parameter q as a function of collision energy and centrality, and supporting the interpretation of temperature fluctuations in hadronic systems as proposed by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2007, 2009).
