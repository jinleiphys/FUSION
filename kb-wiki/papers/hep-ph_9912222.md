# Masses of Decuplet Baryons Treated within Anyonic Realization of the q-Algebras U_q(su_N)

> In the approach to hadronic flavour symmetries based on the q-algebras U_q(su_N) and proved to be realistic, the known construction of U_q(su_N) in terms of anyonic oscillators residing on 2d lattice is utilized. Anyonic Fock-like realization of basis state vectors is given for baryons (3/2)^+ from the 10-plet of U_q(su_3) embedded, via 20-plet of U_q(su_4), into the "dynamical" representation [4000] of U_q(su_5). Within the anyonic picture, we reobtain the universal q-deformed decuplet mass relation M_Ω-M_{Ξ^*}+M_{Σ^*}-M_Δ=[2]_q (M_{Ξ^*}-M_{Σ^*}), where [2]_q=q+q^{-1}=2 cosθ. Consistency with data for baryon masses requires θ\simeq\fracπ{14}. As a result, anyons with anyonic statistics parameter ν= 1/14 can be put into correspondence, at least formally, with the constituent quarks of decuplet baryons.

## Key claim

The paper rederives a previously known \(q\)-deformed mass relation for decuplet baryons using an anyonic oscillator realization of the quantum algebra \(U_q(su_N)\), showing that the constituent quarks of these baryons can be formally treated as anyons with statistics parameter \(\nu = 1/14\), provided the masses are defined in a way that eliminates the anyons' nonlocality from the final results.

## Method

The authors construct state vectors for the decuplet baryons (\(\Delta\), \(\Sigma^*\), \(\Xi^*\), \(\Omega\)) using a Fock-like realization of anyonic oscillators on a two-dimensional square lattice, where the anyons obey braiding relations with parameter \(q = e^{i\pi\nu}\). They embed the decuplet of \(U_q(su_3)\) into the representation \([4000]\) of \(U_q(su_5)\) via the 20-plet of \(U_q(su_4)\), and define the mass operator as a diagonal matrix element between a state and its dual (tilded) counterpart, using the generators \(E_3^\pm, E_4^\pm\) of the quantum algebra. The masses of the four isoplets are computed explicitly, and the constants \(M_0, \alpha, \beta\) are eliminated to obtain the universal mass relation.

## Key numbers

* The \(q\)-deformed decuplet mass relation is \(M_\Omega - M_{\Xi^*} + M_{\Sigma^*} - M_\Delta = [2]_q (M_{\Xi^*} - M_{\Sigma^*})\), where \([2]_q = q + q^{-1} = 2\cos\theta\).
* Consistency with empirical baryon masses requires \(\theta \simeq \pi/14\), giving \([2]_q \simeq 1.96\).
* The anyonic statistics parameter is \(\nu = 1/14\).
* The empirical mass of the \(\Delta\) baryon is \((1232 \pm 2)\) MeV; the \(q\)-deformed relation (27) predicts a value for \(M_\Delta\) that lies exactly in this range, unlike the average-type formula.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gavrilik (1993) and Gavrilik et al. (1997) that replaces the usual unitary flavor symmetry groups with quantum algebras \(U_q(su_N)\) to derive generalized hadron mass relations. It specifically rederives the decuplet mass sum rule previously obtained by Gavrilik et al. (1997) using the Gel'fand-Tsetlin formalism, but now within the anyonic oscillator realization of Lerda and Sciuto (1993). The result enables a formal interpretation of constituent quarks in decuplet baryons as anyons with statistics parameter \(\nu = 1/14\), though the authors note that a full field-theoretic justification remains an open question.
