# Nuclear physics from QCD on lattice

> We have presented a strategy to study nuclei and nuclear matters from first principles, namely, from QCD. We first compute nucleon-nucleon potentials numerically in lattice QCD, and then use them to investigate properties of nuclei and nuclear matter by various methods developed in nuclear physics. As a demonstration that this strategy works, mass and structure of ^4^He, ^16^O and ^40^Ca, and equation of state of nuclear matters are determined with the lattice QCD induced two-nucleon potentials in a heavy quark region as an input. We have found that these nuclei and the symmetric nuclear matter are bound at one quark mass corresponding to the pseudo-scalar meson (pion) mass of 469 MeV (the octet baryon (nucleon) mass of 1161 MeV). The obtained binding energy per nucleon has a uniform mass-number A dependence which is consistent to the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula qualitatively. The present study demonstrates that our strategy works well to investigate various properties of atomic nuclei and nuclear matter starting from QCD, without depending on models or experimental information about the nuclear force.

## Key claim

The HAL QCD collaboration demonstrates that a two-step strategy, first extracting nucleon-nucleon potentials from lattice QCD, then using those potentials in standard nuclear many-body methods, can produce bound nuclei and nuclear matter, but only at a single, unphysically heavy quark mass corresponding to a pion mass of 469 MeV and a nucleon mass of 1161 MeV, not yet at the physical point.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials from lattice QCD using the HAL QCD method, which extracts the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function from four-point correlation functions and defines a non-local potential via a Schrödinger-type equation, expanded to leading order in derivatives. They then used these potentials as input to solve the many-body Schrödinger equation for finite nuclei (via variational methods) and for infinite nuclear matter (via Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory), employing only central potentials with the tensor force partially included through an effective central potential for the \(^3S_1\) channel.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass \(M_{\rm PS} = 468.6(7)\) MeV and octet baryon (nucleon) mass \(M_{\rm B} = 1161(2)\) MeV at the lightest quark mass used (\(\kappa_{\rm uds} = 0.13840\)).
- Lattice spacing \(a = 0.121(2)\) fm and spatial volume \(L \approx 3.87\) fm for the \(32^3 \times 32\) lattice.
- Five quark masses were studied, with the heaviest giving \(M_{\rm PS} = 1170.9(7)\) MeV and \(M_{\rm B} = 2274(2)\) MeV.
- The NN potential in the \(^1S_0\) channel was extracted at three different time slices \(t = 10, 11, 12\) and shown to be time-independent, demonstrating the method's robustness against the "plateau crisis."

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii et al., 2007) from two-nucleon systems to medium-heavy nuclei and nuclear matter, contrasting with direct lattice QCD approaches (Yamazaki et al., 2010; Beane et al., 2012) that suffer from the plateau crisis in multi-baryon correlation functions. It enables future studies at the physical quark mass, where the NN potentials are expected to reproduce empirical scattering observables, and provides a framework for incorporating three-nucleon forces (Doi et al., 2012) and spin-orbit forces (Murano et al., 2014) as lattice QCD data improve.
