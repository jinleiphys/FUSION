# Effect of isospin dependent cross-section on fragment production in the collision of charge asymmetric nuclei

> To understand the role of isospin effects on fragmentation due to the collisions of charge asymmetric nuclei, we have performed a complete systematical study using isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. Here simulations have been carried out for $^{124}X_{n}+ ^{124}X_{n}$, where n varies from 47 to 59 and for $^{40}Y_{m}+ ^{40}Y_{m}$, where m varies from 14 to 23. Our study shows that isospin dependent cross-section shows its influence on fragmentation in the collision of neutron rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross-section influences fragment production in collisions of neutron-rich nuclei, but the effect of density-dependent symmetry energy on fragmentation is very small because the density during fragmentation is lower than normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of charge-asymmetric nuclei. They simulated two sets of reactions: \(^{124}X_{n}+^{124}X_{n}\) with charge varying from 47 to 59, and \(^{40}Y_{m}+^{40}Y_{m}\) with charge varying from 14 to 23, at an incident energy of 100 MeV/nucleon and scaled impact parameter \(\hat{b}=0.3\). Fragment production was analyzed using the minimum spanning tree (MST) algorithm, comparing results with isospin-dependent cross-section (\(\sigma_{np}=3\sigma_{nn}=3\sigma_{pp}\)), isospin-independent cross-section (\(\sigma_{nn}=\sigma_{pp}=\sigma_{np}=55\) mb), and a fixed cross-section of 55 mb.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{124}X_{n}+^{124}X_{n}\) and \(^{40}Y_{m}+^{40}Y_{m}\) systems, simulations were performed at incident energy 100 MeV/nucleon and scaled impact parameter \(\hat{b}=0.3\).
- The fixed cross-section case used \(\sigma_{nn}=\sigma_{pp}=\sigma_{np}=55\) mb; the isospin-dependent case used \(\sigma_{np}=3\sigma_{nn}=3\sigma_{pp}\).
- The symmetry energy stiffness parameter \(\gamma\) was set to 0.66 for the cross-section comparisons, and both \(\gamma=0\) and \(\gamma=0.66\) were tested for density-dependent symmetry energy effects.
- For \(^{124}Sn+^{124}Sn\) and \(^{107}Sn+^{124}Sn\) at 600 MeV/nucleon, the multiplicity of free nucleons, LMFs, and IMFs was studied as a function of scaled impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the role of symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross-sections on fragment production. It builds on prior investigations by Dempsey et al. (who found IMF multiplicity increases with neutron excess in Xe+Sn at 55 MeV/nucleon), Buyukcizmeci et al. (who showed symmetry energy affects isotope distributions but not mean fragment mass distributions), and Tsang et al. (who demonstrated isospin diffusion in Sn+Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon). The finding that density-dependent symmetry energy has negligible effect on fragmentation at sub-saturation densities agrees with earlier observations, while the constant difference between isospin-dependent and isospin-independent cross-sections for charge-asymmetric nuclei provides a new systematic result that could guide future experimental studies of the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
