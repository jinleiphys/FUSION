# QCD-like theories at nonzero temperature and density

> We investigate the properties of hot and/or dense matter in QCD-like theories with quarks in a (pseudo)real representation of the gauge group using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The gauge dynamics is modeled using a simple lattice spin model with nearest-neighbor interactions. We first keep our discussion as general as possible, and only later focus on theories with adjoint quarks of two or three colors. Calculating the phase diagram in the plane of temperature and quark chemical potential, it is qualitatively confirmed that the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition is much higher than the deconfinement transition temperature. At a chemical potential equal to half of the diquark mass in the vacuum, a diquark Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) phase transition occurs. In the two-color case, a Ginzburg-Landau expansion is used to study the tetracritical behavior around the intersection point of the deconfinement and BEC transition lines, which are both of second order. We obtain a compact expression for the expectation value of the Polyakov loop in an arbitrary representation of the gauge group (for any number of colors), which allows us to study Casimir scaling at both nonzero temperature and chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper qualitatively confirms that in QCD-like theories with adjoint quarks, the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition is much higher than the deconfinement transition temperature, and that at a chemical potential equal to half the diquark mass in the vacuum, a diquark Bose-Einstein condensation phase transition occurs, but these results are obtained within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model coupled to a lattice spin model for the gauge sector, not from first-principles lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for QCD-like theories with quarks in real (type-I) or pseudoreal (type-II) representations of the gauge group, using a lattice spin model with nearest-neighbor interactions for the gauge sector in the Weiss mean-field approximation. For two-color adjoint QCD (aQC2D), they derive the thermodynamic potential and use a Ginzburg-Landau expansion to study the tetracritical behavior around the intersection point of the deconfinement and Bose-Einstein condensation transition lines, both of which are second order. For three-color adjoint QCD (aQCD), they compute the phase diagram where the deconfinement transition is first order.

## Key numbers

* Pure gauge deconfinement critical temperature: T_d^0 = 270 MeV, used as input for two-color adjoint QCD.
* String tension: sigma_s = (425 MeV)^2, used as input for two-color adjoint QCD.
* Model parameters for two-color adjoint QCD: a = 670.9 MeV, b^(1/3) = 269.2 MeV, Lambda = 657 MeV, G = 25.71 GeV^(-2), m_0 = 5.4 MeV.
* Ratio of effective NJL couplings for adjoint versus fundamental quarks: G_A / G_F = 2 N_c^3 / (N_c^2 - 1)^2, which for N_c = 2 gives a specific numerical value used to rescale the coupling.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model approach, previously applied to two-color QCD with fundamental quarks by Brauner (2009) and to QCD with fundamental quarks by Fukushima (2004) and Ratti (2005), to QCD-like theories with adjoint quarks. The authors construct for the first time an NJL Lagrangian for type-I (real representation) theories with SU(2N_f) flavor symmetry, and they obtain a compact analytic expression for the Polyakov loop expectation value in any representation. The results confirm earlier lattice-based expectations by Kogut (1985) and Karsch (1998) that in adjoint QCD the deconfinement temperature is much lower than the chiral restoration temperature, and they enable future studies of Casimir scaling at nonzero temperature and chemical potential.
