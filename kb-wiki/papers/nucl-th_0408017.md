# Study of the 16O(p,gamma) Reaction at Astrophysical Energies

> The Feshbach theory of the optical potential naturally leads to a microscopic description of scattering in terms of the many-body self-energy. We consider a recent calculation of this quantity for 16O and study the possibility of applying it at astrophysical energies. The results obtained for the phase shifts and the 16O(p,γ) capture suggest that such studies are feasible but the calculations require some improvement geared to this specific task.

# Study of the 16O(p,γ) Reaction at Astrophysical Energies

## Key claim

The many-body self-energy approach to nucleon-nucleus scattering can yield reasonable predictions for the 16O(p,γ) capture reaction at astrophysical energies, but the calculations require improvement specifically geared to this task because the harmonic oscillator basis used was chosen to study nuclear interior properties and gives a poor description of the nuclear surface to which halo states are particularly sensitive.

## Method

The authors computed the nuclear self-energy for 16O using the self-consistent Green's function method within a harmonic oscillator model space up to the pf shell plus the 1g9/2 orbital, using a G-matrix interaction based on the Bonn-C potential. They augmented this self-energy with a Coulomb potential for a uniformly charged sphere and a correction potential U(r,r') that shifts the energy of principal mean-field orbitals to fit the known energy levels of 17F. The Dyson equation was then solved to obtain phase shifts for proton-16O scattering and the bound-state wave functions of 17F, from which the astrophysical S-factor for radiative capture was computed.

## Key numbers

- The correction potential shifts applied to fit energy levels: δε(1p3/2) = -2.62 MeV, δε(1p1/2) = -2.25 MeV, δε(2s1/2) = +8.52 MeV, δε(1d5/2) = -3.75 MeV, and δε(1d3/2) = +8.4 MeV
- The first s1/2 resonance was originally predicted at 6.3 MeV, approximately 0.5 MeV above the experimental value
- Asymptotic normalization constants (ANCs) predicted: C(s1/2) = 98.2 fm^{-1/2} and C(d5/2) = 1.59 fm^{-1/2}
- Relative spectroscopic factors: 0.921 for the s1/2 state and 0.878 for the d5/2 state
- Rescaling factors needed to match experimental data: 0.63 for capture to the s1/2 state and 0.49 for capture to the d5/2 state
- After rescaling the ANCs, the zero-energy astrophysical factor S(0) = 10.37 keV·b

## Context

This work extends the application of many-body Green's function techniques, previously focused on nuclear correlations as in Diba (2004), to low-energy nucleon-nucleus scattering relevant to nuclear astrophysics. The approach builds on the Feshbach theory of the optical potential as reviewed by Mahaux and Sartor, and the self-energy calculation follows the self-consistent Green's function method applied to 16O by the same authors in earlier work (Barbieri and Jennings, 2004). The results enable future improvements by repeating the self-energy calculations with a basis better suited to describing the nuclear surface, which would be necessary for reliable predictions of capture reactions on halo nuclei.
