# Polarization and dilepton angular distribution in pion-nucleon collisions

> We study hadronic polarization and the related anisotropy of the dilepton angular distribution for the reaction $πN \to Ne^+e^-$. We employ consistent effective interactions for baryon resonances up to spin-5/2 to compute their contribution to the anisotropy coefficient. We show that the spin and parity of the intermediate baryon resonance is reflected in the angular dependence of the anisotropy coefficient. We present results for the anisotropy coefficient including the $N(1520)$ and $N(1440)$ resonances, which are essential at the collision energy of the recent data obtained by the HADES collaboration on this reaction. We conclude that the anisotropy coefficient provides useful constraints for unraveling the resonance contributions to this process.

# Polarization and dilepton angular distribution in pion-nucleon collisions

## Key claim
The anisotropy coefficient of the dilepton angular distribution in the reaction πN → Ne+e- reflects the spin and parity of the intermediate baryon resonance, but the characteristic angular shapes are modified by interference with u-channel resonance contributions and the off-shellness of s-channel contributions, meaning the clean spin-parity signature is only visible for hypothetical on-shell resonances.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian model with consistent interactions for baryon resonances up to spin-5/2, using Rarita-Schwinger spinor fields and the vector meson dominance model for the ρ0-photon coupling. They computed the hadronic density matrix from Feynman diagrams and extracted the anisotropy coefficient λθ from the triple differential cross section, evaluating contributions from s-channel and u-channel diagrams at a fixed center-of-momentum energy.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-momentum energy set to √s = 1.49 GeV, corresponding to the HADES experiment energy
- Anisotropy coefficient λθ computed at dilepton invariant mass M = 0.5 GeV
- Hypothetical resonance parameters: mass mR = 1.49 GeV and width ΓR = 0.15 GeV used for spin-parity studies
- Two dominant resonances identified at this energy: N(1520) and N(1440)
- The anisotropy coefficient λθ shows a maximum around θγ* = π/2 for the N(1520) contribution, indicating transverse polarization of virtual photons emitted perpendicular to the beam axis

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous publication (Zétényi et al., 2017) on dilepton production in pion-nucleon collisions and provides theoretical support for the HADES collaboration's experimental program on pion-induced reactions. The study shows that the anisotropy coefficient can help disentangle resonance contributions, complementing earlier work by Zétényi et al. (2012) on non-resonant Born contributions to pion photoproduction. The results enable experimental extraction of resonance properties from angular distributions, though the authors note that non-resonant Born terms and model dependence from different choices of ρ-baryon interaction Lagrangians need to be addressed in future work.
