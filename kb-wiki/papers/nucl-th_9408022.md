# Quantum Statistical Correlations for Slowly Expanding Systems

> Quantum statistical correlations and momentum distributions are calculated for a spherically symmetric, three-dimensionally expanding finite fireballs, for non-relativistic expansions applying plane-wave approximation. The new concepts of the geometrical temperature as well as the thermal radius are presented for the simplest case. The symmetry properties of the correlation function are shown to reflect the symmetry properties of the emission function.

## Key claim

For a spherically symmetric, non-relativistically expanding fireball with constant flow gradient, the Bose-Einstein or Fermi-Dirac correlation function is dominated by the *smaller* of the thermal and geometrical length scales, while the momentum distribution is dominated by the *larger* of the freeze-out temperature and the newly introduced "geometrical temperature." The correlation function becomes symmetric in the center-of-mass system of the particle pair when the geometrical size exceeds the thermal length scale.

## Method

The authors model particle emission using a classical emission function factorized into a spatial distribution (Gaussian with width \(R_G\)), a freeze-out time distribution (Gaussian with width \(\Delta t\) around mean time \(t_0\)), and a non-relativistic Boltzmann momentum distribution with freeze-out temperature \(T\) and a spherically symmetric scaling velocity field \(\vec{u}(x) = \vec{r}/t_0\). They compute the two-particle correlation function and single-particle momentum distribution analytically in the plane-wave approximation by Fourier-transforming the emission function, combining the Gaussian factors to obtain effective radius and temperature parameters.

## Key numbers

- Thermal radius: \(R_T^2 = t_0^2 (T/m)\), where \(t_0\) is the mean freeze-out time, \(T\) the freeze-out temperature, and \(m\) the particle mass.
- Geometrical temperature: \(T_G = m (R_G^2 / t_0^2)\), where \(R_G\) is the geometrical radius of the source.
- Effective temperature: \(T_* = T + T_G\), which dominates the single-particle momentum distribution \(f(\vec{p}) \propto \exp(-\vec{p}^2 / (2 m T_*))\).
- Effective radius parameter: \(1/R_*^2 = 1/R_T^2 + 1/R_G^2\), which dominates the perpendicular component of the correlation function: \(R_{\text{perp}}^2 = R_*^2\).
- Out-component radius: \(R_{\text{out}}^2 = R_*^2 + \vec{v}_K^2 \Delta t^2\), where \(\vec{v}_K = \vec{K}/m\) is the mean velocity of the pair.
- Lower bound on freeze-out time: \(t_0^2 \ge 4 R_*^2 (m/T_*)\).
- Lower bound on geometrical radius: \(R_G^2 \ge R_*^2\).

## Context

This work extends the concepts of geometrical temperature and thermal length scales, previously introduced for relativistically expanding systems in one and three dimensions (Csörgő et al. 1994), to the simplest non-relativistic, spherically symmetric case. It provides an analytic framework for interpreting Bose-Einstein correlation measurements in low-energy heavy ion collisions (E/A < 1 GeV), where full stopping and spherical symmetry are expected. The results enable simultaneous extraction of bounds on the geometrical source size and freeze-out time from combined analysis of momentum distributions and correlation functions, and show that the symmetry of the correlation function (in the pair CMS versus LCMS) reflects the symmetry of the underlying flow pattern.
