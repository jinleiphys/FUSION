# Formation of light antinuclei and "dense gas" stage in heavy ion collisions

> The antideuteron and antihelium-3 production rates at high-energy heavy ion collisions are calculated in the framework of fusion mechanism when participating particles are moving in the mean field of other fireball constituents. It is shown that coalescence parameters can be found from the requirement of balance between created and disintegrated antinuclei. The explicit formulae for coalescence parameters are presented and compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The antideuteron and antihelium-3 coalescence parameters in high-energy heavy ion collisions can be derived from a balance between production and disintegration rates at the "dense gas" stage of fireball evolution, without assuming statistical or thermal equilibrium, provided that all participating particles are treated as moving in the mean field of other fireball constituents.

## Method

The author solves a transport equation for antinuclei in the fireball center-of-mass frame, where the production term uses a low-energy fusion cross section (with coupling constant g² from Landau) and the disintegration term uses the antinucleus width from interactions with fireball constituents (pions, kaons, nucleons). Particles are treated as Breit-Wigner resonances with mass shifts and widths derived from forward scattering amplitudes on medium constituents, and the coalescence parameters B₂ and B₃ are extracted from the balance condition equating production and disintegration rates.

## Key numbers

- For antideuterons: theoretical B₂ = 4.0 × 10⁻⁴ GeV² (NA44 experiment: 4.4 ± 1.3 × 10⁻⁴ GeV²); B₂ = 4.2 × 10⁻⁴ GeV² (STAR: 4.5 ± 0.3 ± 1.0 × 10⁻⁴ GeV²); B₂ = 14.0 × 10⁻⁴ GeV² (E684: 41 ± 29 ± 23 × 10⁻⁴ GeV²)
- For antihelium-3: theoretical B₃ = 3.3 × 10⁻⁷ GeV⁴ (STAR experimental: 2.1 ± 0.6 ± 0.6 × 10⁻⁷ GeV⁴)
- Normal nuclear density used: n⁰_N = 0.24 fm⁻³; acceptable range: 0.17 to 0.30 fm⁻³
- Fireball volumes: 6.0 × 10³ fm³ (NA44), 7.8 × 10³ fm³ (STAR), 3.0 × 10³ fm³ (E684)
- Antiproton width Γ: 190 MeV (NA44), 210 MeV (STAR), 140 MeV (E684)
- Low-energy coupling constant for antideuteron: g² = 128π m √(mε) × (1 − αr₀)⁻¹ with (1 − αr₀)⁻¹ ≈ 1.67
- Low-energy coupling constant for antihelium-3: G² = 36√3 (4π)³ m/Λ with Λ = 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model for light antinuclei production by incorporating the "dense gas" stage of fireball evolution, where particles interact via a mean field, building on prior results from Landau and from the author's earlier work (Shushpanov, references d and He in the paper). The approach differs from statistical or thermal equilibrium models by deriving coalescence parameters from a balance of production and disintegration rates, and it enables predictions for antideuteron and antihelium-3 yields at CERN, RHIC, and AGS experiments using only the fireball volume as a free parameter.
