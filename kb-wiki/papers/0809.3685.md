# Role of the target orientation angle and orbital angular momentum in the evaporation residue production

> The influence of the orientation angles of the target nucleus symmetry axis relative to the beam direction on the production of the evaporation residues is investigated for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm reaction as a function of the beam energy. At low energies ($E_{\rm c.m.}<$137 MeV), the yield of evaporation residues is observed only for collisions with small orientation angles ($α_T<45^0$). At large energies (about $E_{\rm c.m.}=$140--180 MeV) all the orientation angles $α_T$ can contribute to the evaporation residue cross section $σ_{ER}$ in the 10--100 mb range, and at $E_{c.m.}>$180 MeV $σ_{ER}$ ranges around 0.1--10 mb because the fission barrier for a compound nucleus decreases by increasing its excitation energy and angular momentum.

## Key claim

The influence of the target nucleus orientation angle on evaporation residue production is investigated for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm reaction, showing that at low center-of-mass energies below 137 MeV evaporation residues are produced only for collisions with small target orientation angles ($\alpha_T < 45^\circ$), while at higher energies all orientation angles contribute.

## Method

The authors used the dinuclear system (DNS) model to calculate capture, fusion, quasifission, fast-fission, and evaporation residue cross sections as functions of beam energy, orbital angular momentum, and target orientation angle. The method involves three stages: DNS formation at capture, evolution into a compound nucleus, and production of evaporation residues after emission of gamma-quanta, neutrons, protons, and alpha-particles. For the spherical $^{48}$Ca projectile, shape fluctuations due to quadrupole and octupole vibrational excitations were included by averaging over deformation parameters. The capture probability was determined by solving equations of motion for the relative distance and orbital angular momentum, with the fusion probability $P_{CN}$ calculated from the competition between complete fusion and quasifission.

## Key numbers

- At $E_{\rm c.m.} < 137$ MeV, evaporation residue yield is observed only for collisions with $\alpha_T < 45^\circ$
- At $E_{\rm c.m.} = 140$–180 MeV, all orientation angles contribute to $\sigma_{ER}$ in the 10–100 mb range
- At $E_{\rm c.m.} > 180$ MeV, $\sigma_{ER}$ ranges around 0.1–10 mb
- The quadrupole deformation parameter of $^{154}$Sm is 0.27 at ground state
- The quadrupole vibrational amplitude for $^{48}$Ca is $\langle\beta_2^{(+)}\rangle = 0.101$
- The octupole vibrational amplitude for $^{48}$Ca is $\langle\beta_3^{(-)}\rangle = 0.25$
- The critical angular momentum for fission barrier disappearance in $^{202}$Pb is $\ell_f = 82\hbar$
- The maximum Coulomb barrier is $V_B = 148.2$ MeV at $\alpha_T = 90^\circ$ (equatorial collision)
- At $E_{\rm c.m.} > 150$ MeV, the fusion probability $P_{CN}$ ranges around 0.35–0.41

## Context

This work extends the DNS model approach developed by the authors in previous papers (Fazio et al., 2004; Giardina et al., 2000) to study the role of entrance channel dynamics in evaporation residue formation. The results contradict the opinion from Nishio (2006) that maximal cross sections correspond to equatorial collisions ($\alpha_T = 90^\circ$), instead showing that maximal cross sections should be observed at orientation angles less than $90^\circ$ due to entrance channel effects on capture dynamics, consistent with earlier calculations by Nasirov et al. (2005). The findings enable optimization of beam energies and target orientations for synthesis of superheavy elements in reactions with deformed actinide targets, particularly for hot fusion reactions with $^{48}$Ca beams.
