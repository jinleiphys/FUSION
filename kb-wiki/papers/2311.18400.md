# Linear response theory for spin alignment of vector mesons in thermal media

> We present a calculation of the spin alignment for unflavored vector mesons in thermalized quark-gluon plasma based on the Kubo formula in linear response theory. This is achieved by expanding the system to the first order of the coupling constant and the spatial gradient. The effect strongly relies on the vector meson's spectral functions which are determined by the interaction and medium properties. The spectral functions are calculated for the one-quark-loop self-energy with meson-quark interaction. The numerical results show that the correction to the spin alignment from the thermal shear tensor is of the order $10^{-4}\sim10^{-5}$ for the chosen values of quark-meson coupling constant, if the magnitude of thermal shear tensor is $10^{-2}$.

## Key claim

The correction to the spin alignment (ρ₀₀) of unflavored vector mesons in a thermalized quark-gluon plasma, arising from the thermal shear tensor via linear response theory, is of order 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ for the chosen values of the quark-meson coupling constant, provided the magnitude of the thermal shear tensor is 10⁻².

## Method

The authors use the Kubo formula in linear response theory, expanding the system to first order in the coupling constant and the spatial gradient. They compute the vector meson's spectral functions from the one-quark-loop self-energy with a meson-quark interaction vertex, using the closed-time-path (CTP) formalism. The hard-thermal-loop (HTL) and quasi-particle approximations are adopted to evaluate the spectral functions, from which the correction to the spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ is derived.

## Key numbers

- The correction to ρ₀₀ from the thermal shear tensor is of order 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ for the chosen values of the quark-meson coupling constant.
- The magnitude of the thermal shear tensor is taken as 10⁻².
- The vector meson mass is denoted m_V, with on-shell energy E_p^V = sqrt(|p|² + m_V²).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of spin alignment for vector mesons beyond the nonrelativistic quark coalescence model of Sheng (2019) and the relativistic quantum transport approach of Sheng (2022). It directly addresses the contribution from the thermal shear tensor, which was previously estimated by Li (2022) using linear response theory without a full calculation of the in-medium spectral functions. The results enable a more precise comparison with STAR data on φ meson spin alignment in Au+Au collisions, and provide a framework for incorporating medium effects into spin alignment calculations for other vector mesons.
