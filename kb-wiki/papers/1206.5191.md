# Propagators and phase structure of Nf=2 and Nf=2+1 QCD

> We investigate the phase structure of QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential by solving a coupled set of truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark and gluon propagator. In contrast to previous calculations we take into account the full back-reaction of the quarks onto the Yang-Mills sector and we include the effects of strange quarks. We discuss the resulting thermal mass of the unquenched gluon propagator and extract order parameters for the chiral and deconfinement transition from the quarks. Our result for the temperature dependence of the quark condensate at zero chemical potential agrees well with corresponding lattice calculations. We determine the phase diagram at finite chemical potential and find a potential critical endpoint at (mu_q,T) = (190,100) MeV.

## Key claim
The authors solve a coupled set of truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations for quark and gluon propagators at finite temperature and chemical potential, including for the first time the full back-reaction of dressed quarks onto the Yang-Mills sector and the effects of strange quarks. They find a potential critical endpoint at (mu_q, T) = (190, 100) MeV, but note that results at large densities and low temperatures should be interpreted with great care because diquark and baryon contributions are not included.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled system of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark and gluon propagators in Landau gauge at finite temperature and chemical potential. The gluon propagator is constructed by taking quenched lattice data and adding the quark loop contribution with dressed quarks, while the quark-gluon vertex is modeled using a Ball-Chiu construction multiplied by an infrared-enhanced function. The system is solved self-consistently for Nf=2 and Nf=2+1 flavors, with the strange quark chemical potential set to zero.

## Key numbers
- Critical endpoint at (mu_q^EP, T^EP) approximately (190, 100) MeV for Nf=2+1 QCD
- For Nf=2, ratio of critical quark chemical potential to critical temperature at the endpoint: mu^q_EP/T_EP approximately 1.1
- Light quark mass fixed at m_u/d approximately 2 MeV at renormalization point 80 GeV; strange-to-light mass ratio m_s/m_u/d = 27
- Pion decay constant in vacuum: f_pi approximately 88 MeV
- At zero chemical potential with physical quark masses, T_c approximately 156 MeV for Nf=2+1
- For Nf=2 at zero chemical potential, crossover temperature T_c approximately 200 MeV
- Curvature kappa for Nf=2+1 chiral transition line: extracted from fit in region mu in [0,25] MeV; lattice comparisons give kappa approximately 0.059(2)(4) (Kaczmarek 2011) and kappa approximately 0.059(20) (Endrodi 2011)
- For Nf=2, curvature kappa approximately 0.051(3) (de Forcrand 2002)

## Context
This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger calculations for two-flavor QCD (Fischer and Luecker 2011) by including dressed quarks in the gluon loop instead of a hard thermal loop approximation, and by adding strange quarks. The results agree well with lattice data for the quark condensate at zero chemical potential (Borsanyi 2010). The calculation enables determination of the QCD phase diagram at finite chemical potential where lattice methods suffer from the fermion sign problem, and provides a first-principles alternative to model approaches such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and its Polyakov loop extended versions.
