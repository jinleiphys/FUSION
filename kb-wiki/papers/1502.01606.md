# Curvature correction term as a constraint for the Skyrme interaction

> The curvature correction term to the surface tension is used as a criterion for the efficiency of the Skyrme interaction in describing surface properties. Based on the nuclear equation of state, the curvature correction term to the surface tension coefficient is calculated for 97 standard Skyrme interaction parameter sets in the vicinity of nuclear saturation density at zero temperature. The main idea is to find those parametrizations that give the Tolman's $δ$ correction close to the available theoretical predictions from the statistical theory. Only 59 out of 97 models give satisfactory results. Comparison of the obtained results with the results of the implementation of different macroscopic and microscopic constraints to Skyrme parametrizations available in the literature allows us to select 4 models that satisfy all the constraints.

## Key claim

The curvature correction term (Tolman length delta) to the surface tension can serve as a constraint to select Skyrme interaction parameter sets that adequately describe nuclear surface properties, but only 59 out of 97 tested parametrizations yield a delta within the theoretically expected range of 0.34 to 0.68 fm, and only 4 models satisfy all constraints when combined with prior macroscopic and microscopic criteria.

## Method

The Tolman delta correction was calculated from the nuclear equation of state for 97 standard Skyrme parameter sets at zero temperature and near saturation density, using a restricted extended Thomas-Fermi approach with terms up to fourth order including effective mass and spin-orbit contributions. The surface tension of semi-infinite symmetric nuclear matter (without Coulomb interaction) was computed using a one-parameter Fermi function for the density profile, and the resulting delta values were compared against the theoretically expected range derived from statistical mechanics of ordinary liquids.

## Key numbers

- Only 59 out of 97 Skyrme parametrizations satisfy the Tolman delta constraint with delta in the range 0.34 to 0.68 fm (derived from 0.3 to 0.6 times the interparticle distance r0 = 1.14 fm)
- The surface tension sigma_infinity for the tested parametrizations ranges from 0.93 to 1.21 MeV per fm squared
- Two parametrizations (Ska25s20 and SV-min) satisfy both the delta constraint and all criteria from Dutra (2012), with delta = -0.74 fm, saturation density rho0 = 0.1746 per fm cubed, incompressibility K = 210.78 MeV, and energy per particle E0 = -15.32 MeV
- Five additional parametrizations (Ska35s20, SkT1, SkT2, SkT3, Skxs20) satisfy the delta constraint and all but one of the Dutra (2012) constraints, with delta values of -0.78 fm or -0.58 fm
- For an extended admissible range up to the interparticle distance, two more parametrizations (LNS and SQMC700) satisfy all constraints, and three more (MSL0, SKRA, KDE0v1) satisfy all but one

## Context

This work extends the systematic constraint approach of Dutra (2012) by introducing the Tolman curvature correction as a new criterion for Skyrme force selection, complementing existing macroscopic and microscopic constraints on nuclear matter properties. The study builds on the Gibbs-Tolman theory of curved interfaces, previously applied to ordinary liquids by Tolman (1949) and to nuclear systems by Brack (1985) and Jenkovszky (1994), and finds that parametrizations constructed with systematic parameter variation (such as the SV and SkS families) tend to pass the curvature correction test, while those designed for neutron matter (such as the SLy family) fail. The results enable more selective identification of Skyrme parametrizations suitable for describing nuclear surface properties and interfaces, though the authors note that no simple correlation between delta and individual force parameters was found, suggesting fundamental limitations in the Skyrme parametrization approach.
