# Chiral phase structure of three flavor QCD in a background magnetic field

> We investigate the chiral phase structure of three flavor QCD in a background $U(1)$ magnetic field using the standard staggered action and the Wilson plaquette gauge action. We perform simulations on lattices with a temporal extent of $N_τ=4$ and four spatial extents of $N_σ= 8,16, 20$ and 24. We choose a smaller-than-physical quark mass in lattice spacing as $am = 0.030$ such that there exists a crossover transition at vanishing magnetic fields, and adopt two values of magnetic field strength in lattice spacing $a \sqrt{ e{B}}\simeq 1.5$ and 2. We find that the transition becomes stronger in the presence of a background magnetic field, and turns into a first order as seen from the volume scaling of the order parameter susceptibility as well as the metastable states in the time history of the chiral condensate. On the other hand, the chiral condensate and transition temperature always increase with $B$ even within the regime of a first order phase transition. This suggests that the discrepancy in the behavior of chiral condensates and transition temperature as a function of $B$ between earlier lattice studies using larger-than-physical pion masses with standard staggered fermions and those using physical pions with improved staggered fermions is mainly due to lattice cutoff effects.

## Key claim
The authors find that a background magnetic field with strength \(a\sqrt{eB} \geq 1.5\) (corresponding to \(eB \gtrsim 11 m_\pi^2\)) turns the crossover transition of three-flavor QCD into a first-order phase transition, while the chiral condensate and transition temperature always increase with \(B\) even inside the first-order regime. This suggests that the discrepancy between earlier lattice studies using larger-than-physical pion masses with standard staggered fermions and those using physical pions with improved staggered fermions is mainly due to lattice cutoff effects, not the quark mass.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with three mass-degenerate flavors of standard staggered quarks and the Wilson plaquette gauge action on \(N_\tau=4\) lattices with spatial extents \(N_\sigma = 8, 16, 20, 24\). They used a quark mass \(am = 0.030\) (corresponding to \(m_\pi \sim 280\) MeV at zero field) and applied background \(U(1)\) magnetic fields with strengths \(a\sqrt{eB} \simeq 1.5\) and 2. The order of the transition was determined by analyzing the volume scaling of the disconnected chiral susceptibility, the Binder cumulant of the chiral condensate, and the time history of the chiral condensate for metastable states.

## Key numbers
- Quark mass in lattice spacing: \(am = 0.030\), with corresponding pion mass \(m_\pi \sim 280\) MeV at vanishing magnetic field.
- Magnetic field strengths: \(a\sqrt{eB} \simeq 1.5\) and 2, corresponding to \(eB/m_\pi^2 \sim 11\) and 20, respectively.
- At \(\hat{b}=1.5\) on \(N_\sigma=24\) lattices, the disconnected chiral susceptibility at the critical \(\beta\) is \(\chi_\text{disc}(\beta_c) = 105(11)\), and the Binder cumulant of the chiral condensate is \(B_{\psi\bar{\psi}}(\beta_c) = 1.15(2)\).
- At \(\hat{b}=1.5\) on \(N_\sigma=24\) lattices, the Polyakov loop susceptibility at the critical \(\beta\) is \(\chi_P(\beta_c) = 115(13)\), and its Binder cumulant is \(B_P(\beta_c) = 1.173(3)\).
- The order parameter susceptibility \(\chi_\text{mixed}\) divided by spatial volume at \(\hat{b}=1.5\) is approximately 0.015, constant across all volumes.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of QCD in magnetic fields that found magnetic catalysis (increasing chiral condensate and transition temperature with \(B\)) using standard staggered fermions with larger-than-physical pions (DElia 2010), and inverse magnetic catalysis (decreasing transition temperature) using improved staggered fermions with physical pions (Bali 2011). It also builds on studies of the three-flavor QCD phase diagram at zero magnetic field, where the critical pion mass for a first-order transition is estimated to be below 50 MeV with improved staggered fermions (Endrodi 2007, Bazavov 2017) and below 110 MeV with clover-improved Wilson fermions (Nakamura 2019, Kuramashi 2020). The results demonstrate that a sufficiently strong magnetic field can induce a first-order chiral phase transition even at a pion mass where only a crossover exists at zero field, opening the possibility of a critical point in the temperature-magnetic field plane.
