# Monopole giant resonance in $^{100-132}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) in spherical nuclei $^{100-132}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm, and $^{208}$Pb is investigated within the Skyrme random-phase-approximation (RPA) for a variety of Skyrme forces and different pairing options. The calculated GMR strength functions are directly compared to the available experimental distributions. It is shown that, in accordance to results of other groups, description of GMR in Sn and heavier Sm/Pb nuclei needs different values of the nuclear incompressibilty, $K \approx$ 200 or 230 MeV, respectively. Thus none from the used Skyrme forces is able to describe GMR in these nuclei simultaneously. The GMR peak energy in open-shell $^{120}$Sn is found to depend on the isoscalar effective mass, which might be partly used for a solution of the above problem. Some important aspects of the problem (discrepancies of available experimental data, proper treatment of the volume and surface compression in finite nuclei, etc) are briefly discussed.

# Monopole giant resonance in $^{100-132}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb

## Key claim

The authors confirm that no single Skyrme force from the set used can simultaneously describe the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) in Sn isotopes and in heavier $^{144}$Sm/$^{208}$Pb nuclei, requiring different nuclear incompressibility values of approximately 200 MeV for Sn and 230 MeV for Sm/Pb; however, they find that the GMR peak energy in open-shell $^{120}$Sn depends significantly on the isoscalar effective mass, which might offer a partial path toward resolving this discrepancy.

## Method

The authors perform Skyrme random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations of the isoscalar E0 strength function for spherical nuclei $^{100-132}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm, and $^{208}$Pb using the family of SV Skyrme forces (which systematically vary nuclear matter parameters) plus several other Skyrme parameterizations (SkP$^{\delta}$, SkM$^{*}$, SLy6, SkI3). Both volume (DI) and surface (DDDI) pairing options are applied at the BCS level, and the calculated strength functions are directly compared to experimental distributions from the Texas A&M University (TAMU) and Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP) groups using a Lorentz averaging parameter of 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility modulus needed to describe GMR in Sn isotopes is approximately 200 MeV, while for $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb it is approximately 230 MeV.
- For $^{144}$Sm, the force SV-K241 with K = 241 MeV gives the GMR maximum at 16.2 MeV, while SV-K218 with K = 218 MeV downshifts this maximum to 15.0 MeV.
- The energy-weighted sum rule EWSR(E0) estimated in the energy interval 9-45 MeV is exhausted by 98-105 percent.
- The difference in GMR peak energy for K = 218 and 243 MeV reaches 1 MeV across the Sn isotopes studied.
- The empirical estimation used for comparison is $E^{\rm e}_{\rm GMR} \approx 78 A^{-1/3}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies (Co08, Av13, Ca12, Ve12) that identified the inability of self-consistent models to simultaneously describe GMR across different mass regions, and directly compares calculated strength distributions (rather than just sum-rule centroids) to experimental data from TAMU (Yo99, Yo04, Lu04) and RCNP (It03, Li07, Li10, Li12, Pa12). The authors note significant discrepancies between TAMU and RCNP experimental data, particularly in the left wing of the GMR strength and the presence of a high-energy tail in RCNP data, and suggest that more accurate experiments and matching of these datasets are needed. The dependence of GMR peak energy on isoscalar effective mass in $^{120}$Sn opens an additional window for future work, and the authors suggest that richer density dependence in energy functionals (Sto10) or separate treatment of volume and surface incompressibility may be required.
