# Classical Initial Conditions for Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We construct an analytical expression for the distribution of gluons in the state immediately following a heavy ion collision in the quasi-classical limit of QCD given by McLerran-Venugopalan model. The resulting gluon number distribution function includes the effects of all multiple rescatterings of gluons with the nucleons of both colliding nuclei. The typical transverse momentum k of the produced gluons is shown to be of the order of the saturation scale of the nuclei Qs, as predicted by Mueller. We analyze the properties of the obtained distribution and demonstrate that due to multiple rescatterings it remains finite (up to logarithms of k) in the soft transverse momentum limit of k << Qs unlike the usual perturbative initial conditions given by collinear factorization. We calculate the total number of produced gluons and show that it is proportional to the total number of gluons inside the nuclear wave function before the collision with the proportionality coefficient c = 2 ln2.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an analytical expression for the gluon distribution immediately after a heavy ion collision that resums all powers of both saturation scales \(Q_{s1}^2/k_\perp^2\) and \(Q_{s2}^2/k_\perp^2\), showing that the distribution remains finite (up to logarithms of \(k_\perp\)) in the soft transverse momentum limit \(k_\perp \ll Q_s\), unlike the singular behavior of collinear factorization.

## Method

The author solves the classical Yang-Mills equations with source currents from valence quarks in the McLerran-Venugopalan model, working in covariant gauge and light cone gauge to resum multiple rescatterings of gluons with nucleons in both colliding nuclei. The analysis uses the non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams field of a large nucleus and applies Glauber-type propagators in transverse coordinate space to obtain the gluon production cross section. The calculation extends the proton-nucleus solution of Kovchegov (1999) to the nucleus-nucleus case by including all powers of both saturation scales.

## Key numbers

- The total number of produced gluons is proportional to the total number of gluons inside the nuclear wave function before the collision with proportionality coefficient \(c \approx 2 \ln 2\).
- The typical transverse momentum \(k_\perp\) of produced gluons is of the order of the saturation scale \(Q_s\) for identical nuclei.
- Using PHOBOS data at RHIC, the saturation scale is estimated as \(Q_s^2 \approx 2\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For large transverse momenta, the distribution scales as \(1/k_\perp^4\).
- The numerical result from lattice simulations by Krasnitz and Venugopalan (2000) gave \(c = 1.29 \pm 0.09\).

## Context

This work extends the classical gluon production formalism of Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995) from perturbative order \(g^3\) to all orders in the saturation parameters, and generalizes the proton-nucleus solution of Kovchegov (1999) to nucleus-nucleus collisions. The analytical result confirms the saturation picture predicted by Mueller (2000) and provides initial conditions for subsequent thermalization studies via Monte-Carlo simulations or transport theory, enabling quantitative comparison with RHIC data.
