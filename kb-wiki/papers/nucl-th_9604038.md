# $η$-Deuteron Scattering

> Eta-deuteron scattering lengths are calculated. A summation of the multiple scattering series is carried out and the result is checked against more involved calculations. The necessity to go beyond the fixed nucleon approximation is emphasized. It is shown that a quasibound or virtual state in the $η$-deuteron system may occur within the range of $η$-nucleon scattering lengths suggested by other experiments.

# η-Deuteron Scattering

## Key claim

The paper shows that a quasibound or virtual state in the η-deuteron system may occur within the range of η-nucleon scattering lengths suggested by other experiments, but this conclusion is conditional on the η-nucleon interaction being sufficiently attractive (Re a_ηN approximately 0.8 fm) and on the specific method used to extrapolate the η-nucleon scattering matrix below threshold.

## Method

The authors sum the multiple scattering series for the η-deuteron T-matrix below the deuteron breakup threshold, expressing the η-deuteron scattering length A_ηd in terms of an effective η-nucleon scattering length a_ηN averaged over subthreshold energies. The calculation incorporates both the nucleon-nucleon interaction (via the deuteron pole and continuum) and the free three-body intermediate continuum, using separable Yamaguchi form factors for both the η-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions. The convergence of the expansion is checked by comparing first-order (T^1), second-order (T^2), and third-order (T^3) approximations against more involved fixed-nucleon calculations from Rakityansky et al. (1995).

## Key numbers

- For a_ηN = 0.27 + i0.22 fm, the η-deuteron scattering length A_ηd = 0.57 + i0.97 fm (with both NN continuum and three-body continuum included).
- For a_ηN = 0.55 + i0.30 fm, A_ηd = 0.61 + i2.73 fm (with both NN continuum and three-body continuum included).
- The critical value of Re a_ηN for which a virtual state crosses over to a quasibound state at the ηd threshold is approximately 0.8 fm.
- The nucleon-nucleon scattering length used as input is a_NN = 5.405 fm, with inverse range κ_NN = 1.41 fm^{-1}.
- For the energy-dependent extrapolation a(E) = a_ηN/(1 - i q_ηN a_ηN), the subthreshold momentum is q_ηN = i 0.367 fm^{-1}, corresponding to an average recoil energy of about 4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends a multiple scattering series formalism previously applied to η-helium scattering by Wycech, Green, and Niskanen (1995) to the simpler η-deuteron system. It improves upon the fixed-nucleon approximation used by Rakityansky et al. (1995) by including the effects of the free three-body continuum in intermediate states, which become significant for large Re a_ηN. The results enable experimental searches for η-deuteron quasibound states via final state interactions in pd → pdη scattering, where relative ηd momenta as small as 10-60 MeV/c could reveal strong effects.
