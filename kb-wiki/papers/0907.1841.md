# Superscaling of non-quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering

> The present study is focused on the superscaling behavior of electron-nucleus cross sections in the region lying above the quasielastic peak, especially the region dominated by electroexcitation of the Delta. Non-quasielastic cross sections are obtained from all available high-quality data for Carbon 12 by subtracting effective quasielastic cross sections based on the superscaling hypothesis. These residuals are then compared with results obtained within a scaling-based extension of the relativistic Fermi gas model, including an investigation of violations of scaling of the first kind in the region above the quasielastic peak. A way potentially to isolate effects related to meson-exchange currents by subtracting both impulsive quasielastic and impulsive inelastic contributions from the experimental cross sections is also presented.

# Superscaling of non-quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
The superscaling hypothesis, previously validated for quasielastic scattering, can be extended to the region above the quasielastic peak dominated by Delta excitation, but only with scaling violations of approximately 15-20% that arise from contributions beyond the impulse approximation, including meson-exchange currents and higher resonances.

## Method
The authors obtained non-quasielastic cross sections for Carbon-12 by subtracting effective quasielastic cross sections (based on the superscaling hypothesis using the longitudinal scaling function) from all available high-quality experimental data. They then compared these residuals with predictions from a scaling-based extension of the relativistic Fermi gas model (SSM-inel) that uses a universal scaling function derived from quasielastic longitudinal data, and with a variant (SSM-Delta) that includes only N-to-Delta excitations with finite width. They also applied kinematic cuts on momentum transfer to isolate contributions from different physical processes.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum for Carbon-12: k_F = 228 MeV/c, with energy shift E_shift = 20 MeV used throughout
- The phenomenological quasielastic scaling function peak is about 17% lower than the relativistic Fermi gas prediction (peak value approximately 0.6 for the RFG)
- Scaling violations in the non-quasielastic region are at the level of roughly 0.1 units of scaling function, versus the quasielastic peak value of about 0.6, corresponding to approximately 15-20%
- At q = 500 MeV/c (1000 MeV/c), the transverse RMF scaling function is 13% (20%) larger at its peak than the longitudinal one
- The Delta resonance width used: Gamma_0 = 120 MeV
- The SuSA model for the Delta region agrees with data at the level of 10% or better

## Context
This work extends the superscaling analysis (SuSA) approach of Maieron et al. (2004) and the earlier scaling studies of Donnelly et al. (1999, 2000) from the quasielastic region into the Delta resonance region. It builds on the relativistic Fermi gas model and the phenomenological scaling function extracted from longitudinal quasielastic data, which was shown to be universal across multiple theoretical approaches including relativistic mean field theory (Caballero et al., 2006) and semi-relativistic approaches (Amaro et al., 2007). The paper enables improved predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the Delta region and provides a method to isolate meson-exchange current contributions by subtracting both impulsive quasielastic and impulsive inelastic contributions from experimental cross sections.
