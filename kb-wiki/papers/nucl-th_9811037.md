# Resonant continuum in the Hartree-Fock+BCS approximation

> A method for incorporating the effect of the resonant continuum into Hartree-Fock+BCS equations is proposed. The method is applied for the case of a neutron-rich nucleus calculated with a Skyrme-type force plus a zero-range pairing interaction and the results are compared with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations. It is shown that the widths of resonant states have an important effect on the pairing properties of nuclei close to the drip line.

# Resonant continuum in the Hartree-Fock+BCS approximation

## Key claim

The widths of single-particle resonances have an important effect on pairing correlations in nuclei close to the drip line, and neglecting these widths artificially enhances the amount of pairing correlations. The proposed HF+BCS method captures this width effect through the continuum level density, whereas existing Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using box boundary conditions do not properly account for resonance widths.

## Method

The authors extend the standard HF+BCS framework by discretizing the single-particle continuum using a box of large radius, then expressing the gap equations in terms of the continuum level density. They use a factorization relation that connects scattering wave functions inside the nuclear volume to resonant wave functions, allowing the gap equations to become independent of the box radius. The method is applied to the neutron-rich nucleus 84Ni using the SIII Skyrme interaction for the mean field and a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction, with resonant states included up to 5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total averaged gap for 84Ni including width effects: <Δ> = 0.51 MeV; without width effects: <Δ> = 0.72 MeV
- Fermi energy including widths: λ = -0.874 MeV; without widths: λ = -0.948 MeV
- Total binding energy including widths: E = -652.7 MeV; without widths: E = -653.1 MeV
- Pairing energy including widths: E_P = 3.4 MeV; without widths: E_P = 6.2 MeV
- Number of neutrons in bound states between 12 and 22 fm: 0.069; in resonant states: 0.037
- Width of the d3/2 resonance: Γ = 0.077 MeV; g7/2: Γ = 0.009 MeV; h11/2: Γ = 0.017 MeV
- Averaged gap for the 3s1/2 bound state including widths: Δ = 0.505 MeV; for d3/2: Δ = 0.630 MeV; for g7/2: Δ = 0.966 MeV; for h11/2: Δ = 1.227 MeV
- Cut-off radius D = 22.5 fm; results insensitive to D in the range (3-4)R where R is the nuclear radius

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatment of resonant continuum in BCS by Sandulescu, Liotta and Wyss (1997) to the self-consistent HF+BCS framework with Skyrme forces. It addresses a limitation of coordinate-space HFB calculations by Terasaki, Heenen, Flocard and Bonche (1996), which use box boundary conditions and therefore represent each resonance by a single discrete state, missing the width effect. The method enables proper treatment of pairing correlations in nuclei near the drip line, where resonant continuum contributions are significant, and the authors note that extending this to HFB with proper boundary conditions is work in progress.
