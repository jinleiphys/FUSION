# Off-Fermi Shell Nucleons in Superdense Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> Recent observations of the strong dominance of proton-neutron (pn) relative to pp and nn short-range correlations (SRCs) in nuclei indicate on possibility of unique new condition for asymmetric high density nuclear matter, in which the pp and nn interactions are suppressed while the pn interactions are enhanced due to tensor interaction. We demonstrate that for sufficiently asymmetric case and high densities the momentum distribution of the smaller p-component is strongly deformed with protons increasingly populating the momentum states beyond the Fermi surface. This result is obtained by extracting the probabilities of two-nucleon (2N) SRCs from the analysis of the experimental data on high momentum transfer inclusive electro-nuclear reactions. We fitted the extracted probabilities as a function of nuclear density and asymmetry and used the fit to estimate the fractions of the off-Fermi shell nucleons in the superdense nuclear matter relevant to neutron stars. Our results indicate that starting at three nuclear saturation densities the protons with fractional densities 1/9 will populate mostly the high momentum tail of the momentum distribution while only 2% of the neutrons will do so. We discuss the implications of this condition for neutron stars and emphasize that it may be characteristic to any asymmetric two component Fermi system with suppressed central and enhanced short-range tensor interactions between the two components.

## Key claim

For sufficiently asymmetric superdense nuclear matter (proton fraction 1/9), starting at three times nuclear saturation density, protons will predominantly populate the high-momentum tail of the momentum distribution while only about 2% of neutrons will do so, based on extrapolations from fits to experimental data on two-nucleon short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors extracted the per-nucleon probability of finding a two-nucleon short-range correlation, denoted \(a_2(A,y)\), from world data on inclusive \(A(e,e')X\) reactions for nuclei from deuterium to gold, selecting events with \(x_{Bj} \ge 1.5\) and \(Q^2 \ge 1.5\) GeV\(^2\). They fitted the extracted \(a_2\) values as a function of nuclear mass number \(A\) and asymmetry parameter \(y = |1-2x_p|\), using a factorization ansatz \(a_2(A,y) = a_2(A,0)f(y)\) with the density dependence constrained by nuclear charge density measurements. The resulting parameterization was then extrapolated to infinite nuclear matter and to superdense densities relevant to neutron stars, and the fraction of off-Fermi-shell nucleons was estimated using the relation between \(a_2\) and the deuteron momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- In the momentum range \(k_F < p \le 600\) MeV/c, 92% of struck protons emerge from \(pn\) short-range correlations, while \(pp\) and \(nn\) correlations contribute only about 4% each.
- Extracted \(a_2\) values include: \(^4\)He: \(3.51 \pm 0.03\); \(^{12}\)C: \(4.19 \pm 0.02\); \(^{56}\)Fe: \(4.95 \pm 0.07\); \(^{197}\)Au: \(4.56 \pm 0.03\).
- For symmetric infinite nuclear matter at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 1.6\) fm\(^{-3}\), the extrapolated value is \(a_2(\rho_0,0) \approx 7.03 \pm 0.41\).
- At \(3\rho_0\) densities with proton fraction \(x_p = 1/9\), half of the protons will be off-Fermi-shell; at \(\rho \gtrsim 4.5\rho_0\), all protons populate the high-momentum tail.
- Only about 2% of neutrons are off-Fermi-shell at these densities.
- For \(^3\)He, the average kinetic energies are \(\langle T_n \rangle = 18.4\) MeV and \(\langle T_p \rangle = 13.7\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of short-range correlations in nuclei, building on the experimental observation of strong \(pn\) dominance over \(pp\) and \(nn\) correlations (Subedi et al., 2008; Piasetzky et al., 2006) and the theoretical framework of Frankfurt and Strikman (1981, 1988) for two-nucleon correlations in inclusive electron scattering. The authors use the \(a_2\) parameter extracted from data by Fomin et al. (2011) and earlier work by Day et al. (1987) to extrapolate to superdense asymmetric nuclear matter relevant to neutron stars. The results enable predictions for neutron star cooling via direct URCA processes, proton superfluidity, and the equation of state, and suggest a possible universality with asymmetric two-component Fermi systems in ultracold atomic gases (Shin et al., 2006; Bulgac et al., 2006).
