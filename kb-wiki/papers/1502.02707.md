# Microscopic description of collective properties of even-even Xe isotopes

> Collective properties of the even-even 118-144Xe isotopes have been studied within a model employing the general Bohr Hamiltonian derived from the mean-field theory based on the UNEDF0 energy functional. The calculated low energy spectra and E2 transition probabilities are in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The UNEDF0 energy density functional, when used to derive the general Bohr Hamiltonian via the adiabatic time-dependent HFB (ATDHFB) method, reproduces low-energy spectra and E2 transition probabilities for even-even 118-144Xe isotopes in good agreement with experiment, *provided* all mass parameters are multiplied by a constant scaling factor of 1.3 to simulate neglected Thouless-Valatin and pairing vibration effects.

## Method

The author performed constrained HFB calculations using the UNEDF0 energy functional on a regular grid of 144 points in the (β, γ) deformation plane (0 ≤ β ≤ 0.65, 0 ≤ γ ≤ 60°) to obtain the potential energy surface and the six inertial functions (vibrational mass parameters Bββ, Bβγ, Bγγ and rotational moments of inertia Bk) entering the general Bohr Hamiltonian. The mass parameters were computed in the cranking approximation (ignoring Thouless-Valatin terms) and then multiplied by a constant factor of 1.3 before numerical diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian to obtain collective energy levels and E2 transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- RMSD (root mean square deviation) for binding energies of 16 Xe isotopes: 0.454 MeV, with the largest error Bth − Bexp = −0.71 MeV for 134Xe.
- Deformation minima βmin in the range 0.1–0.25 for deformed isotopes; 118Xe and 128Xe have slightly nonaxial minima with γmin = 8° and 12°, respectively.
- Depths of potential energy minima relative to spherical shape: less than 2 MeV.
- The scaling factor for mass parameters was set to 1.3, based on crude estimations placing the factor in the range 1.2–1.5.
- The quasi-particle space truncation for pairing was fixed by the condition Eμ < 60 MeV.
- The pairing interaction uses ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3 as the saturation density parameter.

## Context

This work extends the application of the UNEDF0 functional (Kortelainen et al., 2010) from binding energies to collective excitations in Xe isotopes, following earlier studies using the Bohr Hamiltonian with geometrical models (e.g., Rohozinski, 1977; Prochniak, 1999, 2004) and the interacting boson model (e.g., Cottle, 2011). The results enable systematic comparisons with newer functionals UNEDF1 and UNEDF2 (Kortelainen et al., 2012, 2014), which have higher RMSD for binding energies (around 1.9 MeV) and whose consequences for collective properties are under investigation. The approach is complementary to generator coordinate method calculations (e.g., Rodriguez, 2010; Yao, 2013) and RPA-based studies using realistic interactions with the UCOM method (e.g., Paar, 2014).
