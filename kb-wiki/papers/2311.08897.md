# Role of the isospin diffusion on cluster transfer in $^{12,14}$C + $^{209}$Bi reactions

> Heavy-ion collisions at near-barrier energies provide a crucial pathway for investigating nucleon correlations and clustering structures. Recent experimental results showed that the valence neutrons in light projectiles obviously enhance the $α$ transfer. This finding is extremely puzzled and fascinating, because it violates the ground-state $Q$ value systematics unexpectedly. In this work, the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach is utilized to investigate the cluster transfer. By comparing the reactions $^{12,14}$C + $^{209}$Bi, we discover that above puzzling behavior is because of the strong correlation between isospin diffusion and clustering. Our calculations clearly show that the equilibrium of neutron-to-proton ratio strongly inhibits the clustering. This work opens a prospect for investigating the clustering in open quantum system.

# Role of the isospin diffusion on cluster transfer in $^{12,14}$C + $^{209}$Bi reactions

## Key claim

The puzzling experimental enhancement of alpha cluster transfer in reactions with neutron-rich projectiles (violating ground-state Q-value systematics) is explained by the strong correlation between isospin diffusion and clustering: the equilibrium of neutron-to-proton ratio strongly inhibits cluster transfer, and valence neutrons in the projectile partially offset this inhibition.

## Method

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach with the Skyrme SLy5 density functional was used to simulate the reactions $^{12,14}$C + $^{209}$Bi at near-barrier energies. The density-constrained TDHF method was employed to calculate the ion-ion interaction potential, and the particle number projection method was applied to extract the probabilities of $^{4}$He and $^{8}$Be cluster transfer from the post-collision wave functions. Both linear-chain and equilateral triangular cluster configurations of the carbon projectiles were initialized and compared.

## Key numbers

- $^{209}$Bi($^{12}$C,$^{4}$He)$^{217}$Fr has a ground-state Q-value of $-21.6$ MeV, while $^{209}$Bi($^{14}$C,$^{6}$He)$^{217}$Fr has $-33.8$ MeV; despite the more negative Q-value, $^{14}$C shows higher cluster transfer probability than $^{12}$C.
- At $E_{\rm c.m.} = 70$ MeV and impact parameter $b = 6$ fm, $^{12}$C$^{\rm Lin.}$ transfers about two protons and two neutrons from projectile to target, with significant neutron reverse-transfer from $^{209}$Bi.
- For $^{14}$C$^{\rm Lin.}$ at the same energy and impact parameter, 0.7 valence neutrons transfer to the target, partially offsetting the reverse-transfer.
- The Coulomb barriers of the two systems differ by only 0.5 MeV, allowing direct comparison at identical incident energies.
- At $E_{\rm c.m.} = 75$ MeV and $b = 5.5$ fm for $^{12}$C$^{\rm Lin.}$+$^{209}$Bi, turning points in the potential curve appear at $R = 11.7$ fm and $R = 10.5$ fm, corresponding to transfer of two and four protons respectively.
- The $^{14}$C$_{\rm T}^{\rm Lin.}$ configuration (valence neutrons concentrated near the neck) shows about one more neutron-proton pair transferred to the target compared to $^{14}$C$^{\rm Lin.}$ at $E_{\rm c.m.} = 70$ MeV, $b = 6$ fm.

## Context

This work extends experimental observations by Sahu (2001) and Biswas (2012) showing enhanced two-neutron correlated cluster transfer in $^{18}$O induced reactions compared to $^{16}$O with $^{174}$Yb and $^{208}$Pb targets, which violated ground-state Q-value systematics. The present TDHF calculations resolve this puzzle by demonstrating that isospin diffusion (N/Z equilibration) inhibits cluster transfer, and that valence neutrons in the projectile counteract this inhibition. The results provide direct evidence that statistical equilibration processes affect clustering in open quantum many-body systems, opening prospects for investigating clustering in open quantum systems using microscopic approaches.
