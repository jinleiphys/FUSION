# Sizes and shapes of very heavy nuclei in high-K states

> We have investigated shapes and sizes of selected two- and four-quasiparticle \mbox{high-$K$} states in nobelium and rutherfordium isotopes within the microscopic-macroscopic model with the deformed Woods-Saxon potential. Excited nuclear configurations were obtained by blocking single-particle states lying close to the Fermi level. Their energies and deformations were found by the four-dimensional energy minimization over shape variables. We have selected the most promising candidates for \mbox{$K$-isomers} by analyzing the isotopic dependence of excitation energies, and compared our results to available experimental data. We calculated differences in quadrupole moments and charge radii between nuclei in their \mbox{high-$K$} and ground states and found their quite different pattern for four-quasiparticle states in neighboring No and Rf isotopes. The leading role of the quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations as well as the importance of higher rank symmetries are also discussed. The current development of laser techniques and the resulting ability to measure discussed effects in the near future is the motivation of our study.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the differences in quadrupole moments and charge radii between high-K isomeric states and ground states show a "quite different pattern" for four-quasiparticle states in neighboring nobelium and rutherfordium isotopes, with the sign of the difference depending on the element. The calculations are performed within a microscopic-macroscopic model that uses liquid-drop formulas for quadrupole moments and charge radii, which the authors acknowledge is an approximation whose usefulness depends on future experimental comparison.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic-macroscopic (MM) method with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, applying the Strutinski shell and pairing correction to single-particle levels from the 19 lowest shells of the deformed harmonic oscillator. Excited nuclear configurations were obtained by blocking single-particle states near the Fermi level, and energies and deformations were found by four-dimensional energy minimization over shape variables β20, β40, β60, and β80 using the gradient method. Quadrupole moments and mean-square charge radii were calculated from the deformation parameters using liquid-drop model formulas, assuming a uniform charge distribution within a sharp deformed nuclear surface.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the two-quasiproton Kπ=8- state in 254No is approximately 1.4 MeV in the calculation, compared to experimental values of 1293 keV (Herzberg 2006), 1296 keV (Tandel 2006), 1295(2) keV (Hessberger 2010), and 1297(2) keV (Clark 2010)
- The experimental excitation energy for the K-isomer in 252No is 1.25 MeV with half-life T1/2 = 109(6) ms (Robinson 2008, Sulignano 2012); the authors' calculation gives about 1.3 MeV for the two-quasiproton π2 8- configuration
- In 250No, the calculated excitation energy for the ν2 6+ configuration is 0.57 MeV, supporting the tentative experimental assignment of this state as the K-isomer
- The measured change in mean-square charge radius for the four-quasiparticle isomeric state in 178Hf m2 is δ⟨r2⟩ = -0.059(9) fm2 (Boos 1994), showing the isomer is significantly smaller than the ground state
- For 244Rf, the excitation energy of the π2ν2 16+ four-quasiparticle state is about 3 MeV
- The quadrupole deformation difference Δβ20 for the 4-qp state in Rf isotopes reaches up to 20 percent for the lightest isotopes

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions for high-K isomers in the nobelium and rutherfordium region, building on previous microscopic-macroscopic calculations by the same group (Jachimowicz 2018, Kowal 2010) and others using the Nilsson-Strutinsky approach (Walker 2016). The study is motivated by recent experimental advances in laser spectroscopy that have enabled measurements of quadrupole moments and charge radii in ground states of 252,253,254No (Raeder 2018), and the authors aim to provide predictions for similar measurements on isomeric states. The results enable future experimental tests using collinear laser spectroscopy on high-K isomers in very heavy nuclei, similar to the pioneering measurement on 178Hf m2 (Boos 1994), and may inform understanding of alpha-decay hindrance mechanisms for multi-quasiparticle states.
