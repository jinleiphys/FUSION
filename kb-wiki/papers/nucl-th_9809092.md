# Coalescence and flow in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Using a density matrix approach to describe the process of coalescence, we calculate the coalescence probabilities and invariant momentum spectra for deuterons and antideuterons. We evaluate our expressions with a hydrodynamically motivated parametrization for the source at freeze-out which implements rapid collective expansion of the collision zone formed in heavy ion collisions. We find that the coalescence process is governed by the same lengths of homogeneity which can be extracted from HBT interferometry. They appear in the absolute cluster yield via an effective volume factor as well as in a quantum mechanical correction factor which accounts for the internal structure of the deuteron cluster. Our analysis provides a new interpretation for the parameters in the popular phenomenological coalescence model and for the effective overlap volume in Hagedorn's model for cluster production in pp collisions. Using source parameters extracted from a recent HBT analysis of two-pion correlations, we successfully describe deuteron and antideuteron production data from Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS as measured by the NA44 and NA52 collaborations. We also confirm the recent finding by Polleri et al. that the different measured slopes of nucleon and deuteron transverse mass spectra require a transverse density profile of the source which is closer to a box than to a Gaussian shape.

## Key claim

Coalescence probabilities for deuterons and antideuterons are governed by the same "lengths of homogeneity" that can be extracted from HBT interferometry, appearing both in an effective volume factor and in a quantum mechanical correction factor for the internal deuteron structure; the successful description of Pb+Pb data from the CERN SPS depends on using a transverse density profile closer to a box than a Gaussian shape, as previously found by Polleri et al.

## Method

The authors used a density matrix (Wigner function) approach to calculate coalescence probabilities and invariant momentum spectra for deuterons and antideuterons, evaluating their expressions with a hydrodynamically motivated parametrization of the source at freeze-out that includes rapid collective expansion. They employed a Cooper-Frye formalism with a Gaussian longitudinal and transverse density profile, a scaling longitudinal velocity profile, and a power-law transverse flow rapidity profile, then performed saddle-point approximations to obtain analytical expressions for cluster spectra. The model parameters were taken from a prior HBT analysis of two-pion correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature: approximately 150 MeV (below deconfinement temperature)
- Longitudinal proper time at freeze-out: τ₀ ≈ 9 fm/c
- Longitudinal rapidity width: Δη ≈ 1.3
- Transverse size: Δρ ≈ 7 fm
- Emission duration: Δτ = 1.5 fm/c (from two-pion correlation fits)
- Deuteron binding energy: B = -2.25 MeV
- For nucleons with transverse velocities v_t ≤ 0.6c, the applicable transverse mass range is 1.0 ≤ M_t/M ≤ 1.25
- The effective volume for deuterons is about 1/3 that of nucleons: V_eff(2,M_t) = V_eff(1,m_t) / 2^(3/2)

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Mrowczynski that coalescence and other final-state momentum correlations share similar source information, and provides an analytical framework that contrasts with numerical approaches using Intranuclear Cascade, ARC, or RQMD simulations. The paper confirms the finding by Polleri et al. that the different measured slopes of nucleon and deuteron transverse mass spectra require a transverse density profile closer to a box than a Gaussian shape, and it enables the use of cluster yields for analyzing the chemical composition of the fireball at thermal nucleon freeze-out, complementing the chemical analysis of elementary hadron yields that determines the earlier point of chemical freeze-out.
