# Supershell Effect and Stability of Classical Periodic Orbits in Reflection-Asymmetric Superdeformed Oscillator

> A semiclassical analysis is made of the origin of an undulating pattern in the smoothed level density for a reflection-asymmetric superdeformed oscillator potential. It is suggested that, when the octupole-type deformation increases, an interference effect between two families of periodic orbit with the ratio of periods approximately 2:1 becomes stronger and thus a pronounced ``supershell'' structure appears.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the enhancement of the supershell effect in a reflection-asymmetric superdeformed oscillator potential arises from the difference in stability between two families of classical periodic orbits with periods approximately in a 2:1 ratio, rather than from a simple interference effect alone.

## Method

The author performed a semiclassical analysis of the quantum energy spectrum for an axially-symmetric oscillator potential with frequency ratio ω⊥/ωz = 2, adding an octupole (Y30) deformation term. Classical periodic orbits were calculated using the Monodromy Method, and their stability was characterized by computing the traces of the stability matrices. Fourier-transform techniques were applied to the quantum energy spectrum to extract information about classical periodic orbits and their contributions to the level density.

## Key numbers

- The frequency ratio of the superdeformed oscillator is ω⊥/ωz = 2, with ωx = ωy = 2ωz ≡ 2ωsh.
- Power spectra P(s) were computed for octupole deformation parameters λ30 ranging from 0.2 to 0.4.
- Peaks in the power spectrum appear at periods s ≈ π and s ≈ 2π, corresponding to the two families of periodic orbits.
- The summation in the power spectrum calculation was taken up to n = 200 quantum states.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Arita and Matsuyanagi, 1993) which showed that the supershell effect is significantly enhanced when octupole deformation is added to the 2:1 deformed harmonic-oscillator potential. The results suggest a semiclassical explanation for the odd-even effect in stability of superdeformed states against octupole deformed shape discussed by Bengtsson et al. (1981), Holler and Aberg (1990), and Nazarewicz and Dobaczewski (1992). A more detailed analysis is promised in a forthcoming full-length paper.
