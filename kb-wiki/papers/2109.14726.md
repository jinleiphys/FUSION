# Decorrelation of participant and spectator angular momenta in heavy-ion collisions

> High-energy heavy-ion collisions contain enormous angular momentum, $|\vec{J}|$, which is $\mathcal{O}(10^3-10^6\hbar)$ in the range of collision energy, $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$, spanned experimentally by the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). A fraction of $\vec{J}$ is transferred to the overlapping collision region, which is indispensable for measuring observables such as vorticity-driven hadron spin alignment with $\hat{J}$. Experiments estimate the orientation of $\hat{J}$ of the participant nucleons within the collision overlap region, $\hat{J}_\mathrm{part}$, by using that of the forward- and backward-going spectating nucleons $\hat{J}_\mathrm{spec}$. Using two models, we study the decorrelation between $\hat{J}_\mathrm{part}$ and $\hat{J}_\mathrm{spec}$, driven both by angular-momentum conservation and event-by-event fluctuations, as well as by the decorrelation between the orientation of the elliptic overlap region and the $\hat{J}_\mathrm{part}$. $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$-dependent decorrelation is observed in both of these cases and is large enough to be an important corrective factor used when experimentally observing phenomena driven by $\vec{J}$.

## Key claim

The orientation of the angular momentum of the participant nucleons in the collision overlap region, \(\hat{J}_\mathrm{part}\), is significantly decorrelated from the experimentally accessible orientation of the spectator nucleons, \(\hat{J}_\mathrm{spec}\), and this decorrelation is large enough to require a corrective factor when measuring angular-momentum-driven observables such as global hyperon spin polarization.

## Method

The authors used two models: a simple Monte-Carlo Glauber (MCG) model with Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions and a more realistic string-melting version of the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model that includes parton cascade evolution. For the MCG model, 50K events were generated at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 27\) GeV with impact parameters \(0 \leq b \leq 14\) fm; for AMPT, the same number of events were generated at multiple collision energies matching the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, using a rapidity cut \(|y|>2\) to define spectators and \(|y|<1\) to define the experimentally accessible mid-rapidity region.

## Key numbers

- The total angular momentum in heavy-ion collisions is \(\mathcal{O}(10^3-10^6\hbar)\) across the energy range spanned by RHIC and LHC.
- For the MCG model at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 27\) GeV, the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section gives \(d_{\perp} = 0.984\) fm.
- The correlation \(\langle \hat{J}_\mathrm{mid} \cdot \hat{J}_\mathrm{spec} \rangle\) for mid-central collisions differs by more than a factor of 2 between the lowest and highest collision energies studied.
- For the mid-rapidity window \(|y|<1\) typically used in experiments, the correlation \(\langle \hat{J}_\mathrm{mid} \cdot \hat{J}_\mathrm{spec} \rangle\) is suppressed such that it would translate to a correction of roughly 25% on the observable of interest.

## Context

This work extends studies of initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions (Alver 2010, Bzdak 2011, Bloczynski 2012) by applying them to the previously unaddressed decorrelation between participant and spectator angular momenta. It directly impacts experimental measurements of global hyperon polarization (STAR 2017, ALICE 2019, STAR 2021) and model predictions of angular-momentum-driven phenomena in the quark-gluon plasma, showing that corrections for this decorrelation are necessary and that using the elliptic overlap region orientation (\(\Phi_2\)) to estimate \(\hat{J}_\mathrm{mid}\) is not a viable alternative.
