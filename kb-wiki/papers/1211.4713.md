# Interplay between valence and core excitation mechanisms in the breakup of halo nuclei

> The phenomenon of core excitation in the breakup of a two-body halo nucleus is investigated. We show that this effect plays a significant role in the reaction dynamics and, furthermore, its interference with the valence excitation mechanism has sizable and measurable effects on the breakup angular distributions. These effects have been studied in the resonant breakup of 11Be on a carbon target, populating the resonances at 1.78 MeV (5/2+) and 3.41 MeV (3/2+). The calculations have been performed using a recently extension of the DWBA method, which takes into account the effect of core excitation in both the structure of the halo nucleus and in the reaction mechanism. The calculated angular distributions have been compared with the available data [Fukuda et al., Phys. Rev. C70,054606]. Although each of these resonances is dominated by one of the two considered mechanisms, the angular patterns of these resonances depend in a very delicate way on the interference between them. This is the first clear evidence of this effect but the phenomenon is likely to occur in other similar reactions.

## Key claim
Core excitation in the breakup of two-body halo nuclei produces measurable interference effects with valence excitation, and the angular distributions of the 1.78 MeV (5/2+) and 3.41 MeV (3/2+) resonances in 11Be depend in a very delicate way on this interference, even though each resonance is dominated by one of the two mechanisms.

## Method
The authors extended the standard DWBA method to include core excitation effects in both the structure of the halo nucleus and the reaction mechanism, splitting the transition amplitude into a valence term (which does not alter the core state) and a core-excitation term (which includes non-central parts of the core-target interaction). They applied this model to the resonant breakup of 11Be on a 12C target at 70 MeV/nucleon, using a particle-rotor model for the 11Be structure with a 10Be core deformation parameter β2=0.67, and compared calculated angular distributions with experimental data from Fukuda et al. (2004).

## Key numbers
- Bombarding energy: 70 MeV/nucleon for the 11Be + 12C reaction
- Resonances studied: Ex = 1.78 MeV (5/2+) and Ex = 3.41 MeV (3/2+)
- Core deformation parameter: β2 = 0.67 for the 10Be core in the particle-rotor model
- Ground state spectroscopic factor for the dominant |0+ ⊗ s1/2⟩ configuration: 0.857 (PRM model)
- 5/2+ resonance spectroscopic factor for the |0+ ⊗ d5/2⟩ configuration: 0.702 (PRM model)
- 3/2+ resonance spectroscopic factor for the |2+ ⊗ s1/2⟩ configuration: 0.737 (PRM model)

## Context
This work extends the core-excitation DWBA method previously developed by Crespo et al. (2011) and Moro et al. (2012), which had shown the importance of core excitation in 11Be breakup on protons but lacked sufficient resolution to assess the interplay between mechanisms. The calculations are compared with data from Fukuda et al. (2004) for 11Be on carbon at RIKEN. The results demonstrate that the interference between valence and core excitation mechanisms is crucial for reproducing the correct oscillatory pattern of angular distributions, opening possibilities for extracting spectroscopic information about halo nuclei from such measurements.
