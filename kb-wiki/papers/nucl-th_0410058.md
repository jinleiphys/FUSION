# Model wave functions and dynamic correlations in light-medium nuclei

> The nuclei $^4$He, $^8$Be, $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O have been studied starting from nucleon-nucleon interactions of $v_4$ type. The wave function is built as the product of three terms, a Jastrow correlation factor, a linear correlation factor and a model wave function. The correlation factors account for both the short range repulsive and the spin and isospin dependence of the nuclear potential. The model wave function is antisymmetric and has the values of the angular momentum and parity of the state under description. For the model wave function we have used two different schemes. The first one is based on a Harmonic Oscillator shell model with and without deformation, and the second one is based on the Margenau-Brink model of alpha clustering. Projection operators of parity and total angular momentum are used. The performance of these two models is studied and compared systematically. Wave functions for the ground state and some members of its rotational band and some other bound states of these nuclei have been obtained. Binding energies, root mean square radius and the expectation value of the kinetic energy and the different channels of the nuclear interactions and the one-- and two-- body densities are reported. The two different model wave functions and the effects of the different nucleon-nucleon correlations have been evaluated on those quantities. All the results here presented have been obtained by using the Variational Monte Carlo method.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares two different model wave functions (deformed Harmonic Oscillator shell model and Margenau-Brink alpha-cluster model) for light-medium nuclei, finding that the Margenau-Brink model consistently provides better variational energies than the shell model, but only when combined with both central Jastrow and linear state-dependent correlation factors.

## Method

The authors constructed trial wave functions as a product of three terms: a central Jastrow correlation factor, a linear state-dependent correlation factor, and a model wave function (either Harmonic Oscillator shell model or Margenau-Brink alpha-cluster model). They applied parity and angular momentum projection operators and optimized variational parameters using the simplex algorithm, then computed expectation values via the Variational Monte Carlo method with the Metropolis algorithm. Two nucleon-nucleon interactions of v4 type were used: the BB1 Brink-Boeker and a modified S3 Afnan-Tang (MS3) interaction.

- For 8Be with BB1 interaction, Margenau-Brink model, JLO correlation: binding energy -74.86(3) MeV, root mean square radius 2.30(4) fm
- For 12C with BB1 interaction, Margenau-Brink model, JLO correlation: binding energy -117.64(8) MeV, root mean square radius 2.44(6) fm
- For 16O with BB1 interaction, Margenau-Brink model, JLO correlation: binding energy -180.7(1) MeV, root mean square radius 2.33(5) fm
- For 4He with MS3 interaction and JLO correlation: binding energy -30.17(3) MeV, root mean square radius 1.40(2) fm
- For 8Be with MS3 interaction, Margenau-Brink model, JLO correlation: binding energy -57.6(2) MeV, root mean square radius 2.51(9) fm
- For 12C with MS3 interaction, Margenau-Brink model, JLO correlation: binding energy -87.3(2) MeV, root mean square radius 2.47(7) fm

## Context

This work extends the variational Monte Carlo approach of Guardiola (1996) and Buendia et al. (1998, 1999, 2000, 2001, 2002) by systematically comparing shell model and alpha-cluster model wave functions for A=4n nuclei. The study demonstrates that the Margenau-Brink model (originally developed by Margenau in 1941 and Brink in 1966) provides better variational energies than the deformed Harmonic Oscillator shell model, especially when combined with both Jastrow and linear correlation factors. The results enable future studies of heavier nuclei and more complex cluster configurations, and show that the interplay between short-range correlations (Jastrow factor) and medium-range correlations (linear factor) is essential for accurate description of nuclear binding.
