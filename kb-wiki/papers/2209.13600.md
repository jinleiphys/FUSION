# Medium-enhanced $c\bar{c}$ radiation

> We show that the same QCD formalism that accounts for the suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron and jet spectra in heavy-ion collisions predicts medium-enhanced production of $c\bar{c}$ pairs in jets.

## Key claim

The same QCD jet quenching formalism that explains the suppression of high-\(p_T\) hadron and jet spectra in heavy-ion collisions also predicts a medium-enhanced production of \(c\bar{c}\) pairs within jets, though this enhancement is a power-suppressed contribution to the total charm yield that only becomes significant in the small phase space region where the squared invariant mass \(Q^2 \ll \hat{s}\).

## Method

The authors compute the medium-modified \(g \to c\bar{c}\) splitting function using the BDMPS-Z multiple soft scattering formalism, expressed as a path integral that describes the space-time evolution of a gluon splitting into a \(c\bar{c}\) pair in the presence of a QGP. They then implement this modification by reweighting each \(g \to c\bar{c}\) splitting in a Pythia 8.3 parton shower by the ratio of the medium-modified to vacuum splitting functions, using a static medium of length \(L = 4\) fm and a quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) constrained by heavy-ion data.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transferred from the medium is constrained to \(2\ \text{GeV}^2 < \langle q^2\rangle_\text{med} < 8\ \text{GeV}^2\) for 0-5% most central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\ \text{TeV}\), with a narrower range \(4\ \text{GeV}^2 < \langle q^2\rangle_\text{med} < 8\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the jet quenching models in Huss (2020).
- For \(\hat{q}L = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\), the medium-modified splitting function \(P_{g\to c\bar{c}}^\text{med}/P_{g\to c\bar{c}}^\text{vac}\) shows an enhancement of nearly 100% over the vacuum baseline in a significant phase space region.
- The fraction of jets containing exactly one \(D^0\bar{D}^0\) pair in vacuum ranges from 0.2% to 1% for jets between 20 and 200 GeV.
- For the HL-HI-LHC with \(\mathcal{L}_\text{int}^\text{PbPb} = 10\ \text{nb}^{-1}\), approximately 1000 counts are expected in the \(D^0 \to K^- \pi^+\) decay channel for jets with \(80\ \text{GeV} < p_T^{\text{jet}} < 90\ \text{GeV}\).
- The probability of having more than one \(g\to c\bar{c}\) splitting in a \(D^0\bar{D}^0\)-tagged jet is below 1% for \(p_T^\text{jet} < 100\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) and the first-order-in-opacity calculation of medium-modified \(g\to c\bar{c}\) splitting (Kang 2016) to a full BDMPS-Z path-integral treatment. It enables experimental searches for medium-enhanced charm pair production in jets at the HL-HI-LHC, complementing existing measurements of jet quenching via single hadron and jet suppression (Connors 2017). Future work should embed this splitting function in a state-of-the-art jet quenching Monte Carlo alongside other medium-modified splittings.
