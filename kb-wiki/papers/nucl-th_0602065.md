# Ground state energy of unitary fermion gas with the Thomson Problem approach

> The dimensionless universal coefficient $ξ$ defines the ratio of the unitary fermions energy density to that for the ideal non-interacting ones in the non-relativistic limit with T=0. The classical Thomson Problem is taken as a nonperturbative quantum many-body arm to address the ground state energy including the low energy nonlinear quantum fluctuation/correlation effects. With the relativistic Dirac continuum field theory formalism, the concise expression for the energy density functional of the strongly interacting limit fermions at both finite temperature and density is obtained. Analytically, the universal factor is calculated to be $ξ={4/9}$. The energy gap is $Δ=\frac{{5}{18}{k_f^2}/(2m)$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the universal coefficient ξ for the ground state energy of a unitary fermion gas can be calculated analytically as ξ = 4/9, but this result depends on modeling the strongly interacting unitary limit through an analogy to the classical Thomson Problem and a relativistic Dirac continuum field theory with a hidden local symmetry (HLS) formalism, which introduces a fictive opposite-charged background to stabilize the system.

## Method

The author models the unitary fermion gas by proposing a QED-like Lagrangian where fermions carry an "electric" charge g and interact with an auxiliary Proca-like vector boson field, with a fictive uniform opposite-charged background ensuring stability. Using the functional path integral formalism in the relativistic Hartree instantaneous approximation (RHA), the effective potential and energy density functional are derived at finite temperature and density. The unknown mass gap parameter m̃_B^2 is determined by imposing a Thomson stability condition (charge neutralization via the background), which yields a result consistent with the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the Debye screening mass.

## Key numbers

* Universal coefficient ξ = 4/9, obtained analytically in the non-relativistic limit (k_f/m ≪ 1) from the ratio of the binding energy to the Fermi kinetic energy (4/15) times the free gas factor (5/3).
* Energy gap Δ = (5/18) ε_f, where ε_f = k_f^2/(2m) is the Fermi kinetic energy.
* Critical temperature T_c ≈ 0.157 T_F, approximated using the BCS universal relation T_c = (e^γ Δ)/π with γ the Euler constant.
* Sound speed v = (√2/6) v_f, where v_f = k_f/m is the Fermi velocity, indicating system stability.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical search for the universal coefficient ξ in unitary fermion gases, which had previously yielded values in the range ξ ∼ 0.3–0.6 from various approaches (Heiselberg 2000, Ho 2004, Astrakharchik 2004, Carlson 2005, and others). The analytical result ξ = 4/9 matches the quantum Monte Carlo calculations of physics/0303094 and is in reasonable agreement with experimental measurements (Bartenstein 2004, Kinast 2005, Partridge 2006, Stewart 2006). The approach enables an analytic treatment of the strongly interacting limit by exploiting an analogy to the classical Thomson Problem and relativistic gauge field theory, and can be extended to the near-unitary regime.
