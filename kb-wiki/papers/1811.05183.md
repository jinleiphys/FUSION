# New targets for relic antineutrino capture

> $^{163}$Ho has been considered as a suitable candidate for the capture of relic antineutrinos. However, the detection of the relic antineutrino using $^{163}$Ho is extremely challenging with current techniques. Therefore, we have searched for new targets for relic antineutrino detections through the resonant capture on nuclides undergoing electron capture. We have investigated nuclear and atomic properties of all nuclides. And we finally propose $^{131}$Ba, $^{159}$Dy, $^{175}$Hf, $^{195}$Au, and $^{243}$Cm as new candidates for the relic antineutrino detection, and call for high precise experiments of $Q_{\rm EC}$-values and intensities of EC decays for these new candidates.

## Key claim

The paper proposes five new candidate nuclides for relic antineutrino detection via resonant capture, but the practical detection remains extremely challenging even with these improved targets, which require orders of magnitude less material than previously suggested candidates.

## Method

The authors systematically surveyed all nuclides with available nuclear and atomic data, applying criteria including electron-capture instability, allowed beta-decay spin-parity changes, half-lives longer than 10 days, and Q-values below 10 keV. They calculated Q-values, uncertainties, and resonant conditions for each candidate, then computed the ratio of antineutrino capture rate to electron-capture decay rate and the required target mass for 10 signal events per year.

## Key numbers

- For $^{163}$Ho, using the most optimistic Q-value of 2.788 keV, the required target mass is 1.23 × 10³ kg for 10 events per year, with a rate ratio λ_ν̅/λ_EC = 1.45 × 10⁻²³
- For $^{243}$Cm decaying to ground state, the required mass is 1.71 kg with λ_ν̅/λ_EC = 3.42 × 10⁻²⁰, using Q_EC^opt = 6.133 keV
- For $^{159}$Dy decaying to excited state, the required mass is 1.44 kg with λ_ν̅/λ_EC = 5.52 × 10⁻¹⁹, using Q_EC^opt = 365.512 keV
- For $^{195}$Au₁ decaying to the 211.4 keV excited state of $^{195}$Pt, the required mass is 0.82 kg with λ_ν̅/λ_EC = 1.48 × 10⁻²⁰, using Q_EC^opt = 225.286 keV
- For $^{157}$Tb, the required mass is 2.79 × 10⁶ kg with λ_ν̅/λ_EC = 8.70 × 10⁻²⁶, using Q_EC^opt = 59.747 keV
- The recent high-precision measurement of $^{159}$Dy gives Q_EC = 364.73 ± 0.19 keV, which does not allow resonant absorption and increases the required mass to 2.01 × 10⁴ kg

## Context

This work extends the search for relic antineutrino capture targets beyond the previously proposed $^{163}$Ho (Lusignoli and Vignati, 2011) and $^{157}$Tb (Vergados and Novikov, 2014), which required impractically large target masses. The new candidates reduce the required mass by three to six orders of magnitude compared to these earlier proposals. The paper calls for high-precision Penning-trap mass measurements of Q-values and electron-capture branching ratios for the new candidates, as demonstrated by the recent $^{159}$Dy measurement (Ge et al., 2021) which may disqualify that candidate.
