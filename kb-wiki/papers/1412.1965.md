# A coarse-graining approach for dilepton production at SPS energies

> Coarse-grained output from transport calculations is used to determine thermal dilepton emission rates by applying medium-modified spectral functions from thermal quantum field theoretical models. By averaging over an ensemble of events generated with the UrQMD transport model, we extract the local thermodynamic properties at each time step of the calculation. With an equation of state the temperature $T$ and chemical potential $μ_{\mathrm{B}}$ can be determined. The approach goes beyond simplified fireball models of the bulk-medium evolution by treating the full (3+1)-dimensional expansion of the system with realistic time and density profiles. For the calculation of thermal dilepton rates we use the in-medium spectral function of the $ρ$ meson developed by Rapp and Wambach and consider thermal QGP and multi-pion contributions as well. The approach is applied to the evaluation of dimuon production in In+In collisions at top SPS energy. Comparison to the experimental results of the NA60 experiment shows good agreement of this ansatz. We find that the experimentally observed low-mass dilepton excess in the mass region from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV can be explained by a broadening of the $ρ$ spectral function with a small mass shift. In contrast, the intermediate mass region ($M > 1.5$ GeV) is dominated by a contribution from the quark-gluon plasma. These findings agree with previous calculations with fireball parametrizations. This agreement in spite of differences in the reaction dynamics between both approaches indicates that the time-integrated dilepton spectra are not very sensitive to details of the space-time evolution of the collision.

# A coarse-graining approach for dilepton production at SPS energies

## Key claim

The coarse-graining approach, which extracts local thermodynamic properties from UrQMD transport calculations and applies in-medium spectral functions, can explain the NA60 dimuon excess in In+In collisions at top SPS energy. The agreement with previous fireball-model calculations, despite differences in the treatment of reaction dynamics, indicates that time-integrated dilepton spectra are not very sensitive to details of the space-time evolution of the collision.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model to generate an ensemble of events for In+In collisions, then placed the output on a (3+1)-dimensional space-time grid with cell size 0.8 fm and time steps of 0.2 fm/c. For each cell, they extracted the local energy-momentum tensor and baryon four-flow, applied an anisotropic-hydrodynamics correction for kinetic off-equilibrium, and used an equation of state (hadron-gas EoS matched to a lattice-QCD EoS with critical temperature 170 MeV) to determine temperature T and baryochemical potential μ_B. Thermal dilepton emission rates were calculated using the Rapp-Wambach in-medium ρ spectral function, multi-pion contributions from tau-decay data, and QGP emission from lattice-QCD correlators, with a pion chemical potential extracted to account for chemical off-equilibrium. Non-thermal ρ decays from cells below 50 MeV were treated via a direct-shining procedure.

## Key numbers

- Time step size: Δt = 0.2 fm/c; spatial cell extension: Δx = 0.8 fm
- Critical temperature for the lattice EoS: T_c = 170 MeV
- The low-mass dilepton excess is explained in the mass region from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV
- The intermediate mass region is defined as M > 1.5 GeV
- The hadron-gas EoS and lattice EoS agree well up to an energy density of roughly 15 ε_0 (where ε_0 is normal nuclear-matter density), corresponding to a temperature of 200 MeV
- Kinetic equilibrium may only be reached after approximately 10 fm/c
- The parametrization of the Rapp-Wambach spectral function has a maximal deviation of up to 15% in the mass region around 0.4 GeV compared to the full spectral function

## Context

This work extends previous fireball-model calculations by Rapp and Wambach (2000s) and hybrid-model studies by Santini (2011) by using a full (3+1)-dimensional transport description rather than simplified fireball parametrizations or a short hydrodynamic stage. The approach was previously proposed by Huovinen (2002) for hadron, dilepton and photon spectra, but here it is modified to include the very initial stage of the reaction and account for non-equilibrium effects in pion dynamics. The results confirm the findings of van Hees (2006, 2007) and Dusling (2006) that a broadening of the ρ spectral function with small mass shift explains the NA60 low-mass excess, and that the intermediate mass region is dominated by QGP emission. The approach enables the application of in-medium spectral functions from thermal quantum field theory at all stages of the collision, going beyond the limitations of both pure transport models and macroscopic fireball or hydrodynamic models.
