# Physics of Strangelets

> After a brief introduction to the physics of bulk strange quark matter (SQM) this review focuses on the properties of low baryon number strangelets presently searched for in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion experiments at CERN and Brookhaven. Shell-model calculations reveal interesting (meta)stability properties in the experimentally accessible regime. A liquid drop model (Fermi-gas model) is shown to explain the overall behavior of the mode-filling calculations, leading to a physical understanding of strangelet properties, which can be generalized to non-zero temperature and pressure.

## Key claim

This review argues that strangelets (small chunks of strange quark matter) with baryon number below 100 can be metastable and potentially detectable in heavy-ion experiments, but the theoretical predictions are strongly parameter-dependent and many nearly degenerate states exist, making experimental signatures ambiguous.

## Method

The paper uses the MIT bag model to calculate strangelet properties via two complementary approaches: a shell-model (mode-filling) method that solves the Dirac equation for quarks confined in a spherical cavity, and a liquid drop model (Fermi-gas model) based on a multiple reflection expansion of the density of states that includes volume, surface, and curvature terms. The liquid drop model is fitted to the shell-model results to extract bulk, surface, and curvature energy coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For bulk strange quark matter with bag constant B^(1/4) = 145 MeV, the bulk energy per baryon ranges from 829 MeV (for massless strange quarks) to 917 MeV (for m_s = 300 MeV)
- The minimum baryon number for absolute stability (E/A < 939 MeV) requires bulk binding energy exceeding 65 MeV for A = 30, and exceeding 20 MeV for A = 370
- For B^(1/4) = 145 MeV and m_s = 100 MeV, the liquid drop energy formula is: epsilon = 852 + 83 A^(-1/3) + 241 A^(-2/3) MeV
- For B^(1/4) = 165 MeV and m_s = 150 MeV: epsilon = 985 + 93 A^(-1/3) + 265 A^(-2/3) MeV
- The lower limit on the bag constant from stability of ordinary nuclei is B^(1/4) > 145 MeV
- Stable bulk SQM requires B^(1/4) < 164 MeV for sufficiently low m_s
- The metastability window relative to a gas of Lambda particles extends to B^(1/4) < 195 MeV

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on strange quark matter by Witten (1984) and Farhi and Jaffe (1984), who first proposed that strange quark matter could be absolutely stable. It builds on shell-model calculations by Gilson and Jaffe (1993) and Madsen (1994), and develops a liquid drop model that explains the dominant role of curvature energy (previously overlooked in the Berger and Jaffe (1987) treatment). The results enable experimental searches for strangelets at CERN and Brookhaven by providing mass formulas and stability criteria, while highlighting that many metastable states with different charges and decay modes may exist, complicating detection.
