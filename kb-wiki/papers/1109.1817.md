# Back-to-Back Correlations of Di-hadrons in dAu Collisions at RHIC

> We perform a complete theoretical analysis of the azimuthal angular correlation of two-hadron productions in the forward $dAu$ collisions at RHIC in the saturation formalism, and obtain a very good agreement with the experimental data. It is demonstrated that the suppression and broadening of the away side peak provide a unique signal for the onset of the saturation mechanism at small-$x$ in a large nucleus. We emphasize that future experiments of di-hadron correlations in $pA$ collisions at both RHIC and LHC, and in $eA$ collisions at the planned electron-ion collider, shall provide us with a thorough study and understanding of the strong interaction dynamics in the saturation regime.

## Key claim

The suppression and broadening of the away-side peak in di-hadron azimuthal correlations in forward dAu collisions at RHIC provide a unique signal for the onset of gluon saturation at small-x in a large nucleus, but the agreement with data depends on using the correct distinction between the Weizsacker-Williams (WW) gluon distribution and the dipole gluon distribution, which earlier calculations had confused.

## Method

The authors performed a complete theoretical analysis of two-hadron production in forward dAu collisions at RHIC using the color-glass-condensate (CGC) saturation formalism. They calculated the coincidence probability C(Δφ) as the ratio of correlated two-particle cross sections to single-particle cross sections, using effective kt-factorization that incorporates both the dipole gluon distribution and the WW gluon distribution, along with double parton scattering contributions. The Golec-Biernat Wusthoff (GBW) model was used to parametrize the unintegrated gluon distributions, with nuclear dependence introduced by modifying the saturation scale as Q_sA^2 = c(b) A^(1/3) Q_s^2(x), where c(b) is the only free parameter fit to data.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale Q_s reaches approximately 2 GeV at x_g ~ 6×10^(-4) in the center of the gold nucleus, with jet transverse momentum k_perp ~ 3 GeV at rapidity 3.2
- The profile parameter c(b) was found to be 0.45 for peripheral, 0.56 for minimum bias, and 0.85 for central dAu collisions
- Pedestal contributions to C(Δφ) were found to be around 0.016 for peripheral and 0.018 for central collisions
- The single hadron production cross sections were described up to p_perp = 3.0 GeV with a K-factor of 0.8 for y_h = 2.0 and 0.5 for y_h = 3.2
- The GBW parameters used were Q_s0 = 1 GeV, x_0 = 3.04×10^(-3), and λ = 0.288

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Marquet (2007) to use forward di-jet production in pA collisions as a signal for gluon saturation, which was qualitatively confirmed by STAR (Braidot, 2010) and PHENIX (Adare, 2011) collaborations. It corrects earlier quantitative attempts by Albacete (2010) and Tuchin (2009) that had taken inappropriate approximations, particularly by correctly including the WW gluon distribution contributions that were missing in those calculations. The results enable future measurements at RHIC, LHC, and the planned Electron-Ion Collider to systematically map the phase structure of cold nuclear matter at small-x and thoroughly investigate QCD dynamics in the saturation regime.
