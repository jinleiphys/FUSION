# Reaction cross sections of the deformed halo nucleus 31Ne

> Using the Glauber theory, we calculate reaction cross sections for the deformed halo nucleus $^{31}$Ne. To this end, we assume that the $^{31}$Ne nucleus takes the $^{30}$Ne + $n$ structure. In order to take into account the rotational excitation of the core nucleus $^{30}$Ne, we employ the particle-rotor model (PRM). We compare the results to those in the adiabatic limit of PRM, that is, the Nilsson model, and show that the Nilsson model works reasonably well for the reaction cross sections of $^{31}$Ne. We also investigate the dependence of the reaction cross sections on the ground state properties of $^{31}$Ne, such as the deformation parameter and the p-wave component in the ground state wave function.

## Key claim
The Nilsson model (adiabatic limit of the particle-rotor model) works reasonably well for reaction cross sections of the deformed halo nucleus 31Ne, even though finite rotational excitation energy of the core nucleus 30Ne changes the wave function composition; the configuration with deformation parameter β2=0.2 (corresponding to the [330 1/2] Nilsson level) is a promising candidate for the ground state, while the β2=0.55 configuration ([321 3/2]) is inconsistent with experimental one-neutron removal cross sections.

## Method
The authors assumed a 30Ne + n structure for 31Ne and employed the particle-rotor model (PRM) to account for rotational excitation of the deformed core nucleus 30Ne. Reaction cross sections on a carbon target were calculated using the optical limit approximation of Glauber theory, with S-matrices obtained by folding densities of the projectile and target nuclei. The ground state wave function was expanded on a basis of spherical Woods-Saxon eigenfunctions and rotational wave functions, with the Hamiltonian diagonalized numerically; the adiabatic limit (Nilsson model) was obtained by setting the rotational Hamiltonian to zero.

## Key numbers
- One-neutron separation energy of 31Ne: Sn = 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV (from J07)
- First excited 2+ state of 30Ne: 0.801 MeV; 4+ state: 2.24 MeV; energy ratio E4+/E2+ = 2.80
- For β2=0.2 and Sn=0.2 MeV: total p3/2 probability in adiabatic limit is 54.9%; [0+ ⊗ p3/2] component probability in PRM is 54.2%
- For β2=0.55 and Sn=0.2 MeV: total p3/2 probability in adiabatic limit is 25.7%; [0+ ⊗ p3/2] component probability in PRM is 2.1%
- Calculated Coulomb breakup cross section for β2=0.2 at Sn=0.2 MeV on carbon target: 0.0033 b (small compared to total reaction cross section)
- Reaction cross sections for 30,31Ne increase by about 0.01 b from β2=0.2 to β2=0.4
- Total p3/2 probability in adiabatic limit varies from 54.9% to 57.0% as β2 changes from 0.2 to 0.4

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous particle-rotor model analysis of Coulomb breakup cross sections for 31Ne (Urata et al., 2011) to reaction cross sections, and complements the microscopic optical potential model calculations by Minomo et al. (2011, 2012). The results are consistent with experimental interaction cross sections measured by Takechi et al. (2012) and one-neutron removal cross sections from Nakamura et al. (2009). The finding that the adiabatic approximation works well enables future studies of the deformed nucleus 32Ne using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov methods with pairing interactions, as suggested by the odd-even staggering observed in Ne isotopes.
