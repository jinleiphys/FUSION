# Leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes of S-wave heavy-quarkonia

> The leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) of ground-state $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ $c\bar c$- and $b\bar b$-quarkonia are calculated using a symmetry-preserving continuum treatment of the meson bound-state problem which unifies the properties of these heavy-quark systems with those of light-quark bound-states, including QCD's Goldstone modes. Analysing the evolution of $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ PDAs with current-quark mass, $\hat m_q$, increasing away from the chiral limit, it is found that in all cases there is a value of $\hat m_q$ for which the PDA matches the asymptotic form appropriate to QCD's conformal limit and hence is insensitive to changes in renormalisation scale, $ζ$. This mass lies just above that associated with the $s$-quark. At current-quark masses associated with heavy-quarkonia, on the other hand, the PDAs are piecewise convex-concave-convex. They are much narrower than the asymptotic distribution on a large domain of $ζ$; but nonetheless deviate noticeably from $φ_{Q\bar Q}(x) = δ(x-1/2)$, which is the result in the static-quark limit. There are also material differences between $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ PDAs, and between the PDAs for different vector-meson polarisations, which vanish slowly with increasing $ζ$. An analysis of moments of the root-mean-square relative-velocity, $\langle v^{2m}\rangle$, in $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ systems reveals that $\langle v^4\rangle$-contributions may be needed in order to obtain a reliable estimate of matrix elements using such an expansion, especially for processes involving heavy pseudoscalar quarkonia.

## Key claim

The leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) of heavy quarkonia are piecewise convex-concave-convex and much narrower than the asymptotic form, but nonetheless deviate noticeably from the delta-function approximation δ(x-1/2) that would result in the static-quark limit; material differences exist between pseudoscalar and vector meson PDAs and between different vector-meson polarisations, which vanish slowly with increasing renormalisation scale.

## Method

The authors computed the leading-twist PDAs of ground-state ¹S₀ and ³S₁ charmonium and bottomonium by projecting their Bethe-Salpeter wave functions onto the light-front, using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation and a DCSB-improved (DB) truncation. Dressed-quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes were obtained from gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations using the interaction of Qin (2011), with two interaction strength parameters: ς_G^RL = 0.87 GeV (fitted to pion properties) and ς_G^DB = 0.55 GeV (consistent with gauge-sector solutions). Moments of the PDAs were computed via direct integration with extrapolation to remove integration-boundary artifacts, and the PDAs were reconstructed using a Gaussian-inspired ansatz φ_I(ξ,a) fitted to the computed moments.

## Key numbers

- For ς_G^DB (the more realistic truncation), the PDA width parameter a is: η_c = 1.7, η_b = 2.5, J/Ψ^⊥ = 3.0, J/Ψ^∥ = 3.4, Υ^⊥ = 5.3, Υ^∥ = 6.0
- The critical current-quark mass at which the PDA matches the asymptotic form φ^asy(x)=6x(1-x) and becomes insensitive to renormalisation scale is m^c(ζ₂) = 0.15 GeV for pseudoscalar, 0.13 GeV for vector transverse, and 0.12 GeV for vector longitudinal polarisation
- Root-mean-square relative velocities ⟨v²⟩ from ς_G^DB: η_c = 0.31, η_b = 0.21, J/Ψ^⊥ = 0.14, J/Ψ^∥ = 0.12, Υ^⊥ = 0.07, Υ^∥ = 0.04
- ⟨v⁴⟩ values from ς_G^DB: η_c = 0.16, η_b = 0.07, J/Ψ^⊥ = 0.03, J/Ψ^∥ = 0.02, Υ^⊥ = 0.01, Υ^∥ = 0.00
- Decay constants from ς_G^DB: f_ηc = 0.262 GeV, f_ηb = 0.543 GeV, f_J/Ψ = 0.255 GeV, f_J/Ψ^⊥ = 0.213 GeV, f_Υ = 0.471 GeV, f_Υ^⊥ = 0.421 GeV
- The ς_G^DB results differ from experiment by a 10% root-mean-square relative-error, while ς_G^RL results differ by 43%

## Context

This work extends the continuum DSE framework previously applied to light-meson PDAs (pion, kaon, rho, phi mesons) by Chang (2013), Gao (2014), and Shi (2014) to heavy-quark systems, providing a unified description from QCD's Goldstone modes through to bottomonium. The results contradict earlier sum-rule analyses by Braguta (2006, 2007) and Zhong (2014) and a light-front constituent quark model by Choi (2007), which reported approximately equal PDAs for η_c, J/Ψ^⊥, and J/Ψ^∥, whereas this work finds material differences between these systems. The findings enable more reliable calculations of hard exclusive processes involving heavy quarkonia, such as quarkonia production at high energies, J/Ψ+η_c pair production in e⁺e⁻ annihilation, and Higgs boson decays into quarkonia, and demonstrate that ⟨v⁴⟩-contributions may be needed for reliable matrix element estimates using non-relativistic QCD expansions, especially for heavy pseudoscalar quarkonia.
