# Multiplicity fluctuations at the quark-hadron phase transition from a fluid dynamical model

> The region of large net-baryon densities in the QCD phase diagram is expected to exhibit a first-order phase transition. Experimentally, its study will be one of the primaryobjectives for the upcoming FAIR accelerator. We model the transition between quarks and hadrons in a heavy-ion collision using a fluid which is coupled to the explicit dynamics of the chiral order parameter and a dilaton field. This allows us to investigate signals stemming from the nonequilibrium evolution during the expansion of the hot plasma. Special emphasis is put on an event-by-event analysis of baryon number fluctuations which have long since been claimed to be sensitive to a critical point.

# Multiplicity fluctuations at the quark-hadron phase transition from a fluid dynamical model

## Key claim

The authors model the quark-hadron transition in heavy-ion collisions using a fluid coupled to explicit dynamics of the chiral order parameter and a dilaton field, and find that event-by-event net-baryon number fluctuations are enhanced not only at a critical endpoint but also, and more strongly, at a first-order phase transition, but only when the system is allowed to develop spinodal instabilities after supercooling, meaning the enhancement is a nonequilibrium effect that vanishes at later times.

## Method

The authors couple an ideal fluid of quarks and gluons to Langevin equations for the sigma field (chiral order parameter) and a classical Euler-Lagrange equation for the dilaton field, using a linear sigma model with dilatons. They simulate the expansion of a spherical droplet of plasma initialized with a given temperature and chemical potential, evolving through crossover, critical endpoint, or first-order phase transition regions. Net-baryon number fluctuations are extracted event-by-event from cells within a defined acceptance region (rapidity |y| < 0.5 and transverse momentum density between 100 MeV/fm³ and 500 MeV/fm³).

## Key numbers

- The quark-meson coupling g_q is fixed to 3.37 to reproduce the vacuum nucleon mass.
- The sigma meson mass is fixed at 900 MeV, resulting in a critical endpoint at temperature T_CEP = 89 MeV and quark chemical potential μ_CEP = 329 MeV.
- At a constant temperature of T = 40 MeV, the susceptibility and kurtosis diverge around the spinodal points with critical indices of 2/3 and 2, respectively.
- The variance and kurtosis of net-baryon number are enhanced for a critical endpoint and even more for a first-order phase transition compared to a crossover scenario; the kurtosis becomes negative for both the critical endpoint and first-order transition.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics model (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013, Herold 2014) which successfully described supercooling, critical slowing down, and domain formation from spinodal dynamics. It builds on the expectation from Stephanov (2008) that moments of conserved charge fluctuations are sensitive to a critical endpoint, but shows that similar signals arise from first-order phase transitions under nonequilibrium conditions. The results enable future studies of whether enhanced density fluctuations survive freeze-out and become imprinted in final particle number fluctuations measurable at FAIR.
