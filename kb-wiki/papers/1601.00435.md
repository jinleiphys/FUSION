# Hyperons in neutron stars and supernova cores

> The properties of compact stars and their formation processes depend on many physical ingredients. The composition and the thermodynamics of the involved matter is one of them. We will investigate here uniform strongly interacting matter at densities and temperatures, where potentially other components than free nucleons appear such as hyperons, mesons or even quarks. In this paper we will put the emphasis on two aspects of stellar matter with non-nucleonic degrees of freedom. First, we will study the phase diagram of baryonic matter with strangeness, showing that the onset of hyperons, as that of quark matter, could be related to a very rich phase structure with a large density domain covered by phase coexistence. Second, we will investigate thermal effects on the equation of state (EoS), showing that they favor the appearance of non-nucleonic particles. We will finish by reviewing some recent results on the impact of non-nucleonic degrees freedom in compact star mergers and core-collapse events, where thermal effects cannot be neglected.

# Hyperons in neutron stars and supernova cores

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the onset of hyperons in dense stellar matter could be associated with a strangeness-driven phase transition, analogous to the liquid-gas transition in nuclear matter, and shows that thermal effects favor the appearance of non-nucleonic particles; however, the existence of such a phase transition depends on the specific hyperonic interaction model employed, and the authors assume it exists to explore possible consequences rather than claiming it is definitively present.

## Method

The authors employ a non-relativistic energy density functional developed by Balberg and Gal (1997) to study the phase diagram of baryonic matter containing the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) under conditions of strangeness equilibrium (μ_S = 0). They perform a thermodynamic analysis of phase coexistence using Legendre transformations to reduce the dimensionality of the system from eight particle species to three conserved densities (baryon number, charge, and strangeness), and examine the influence of Coulomb interactions by including electrons as an ideal Fermi gas. For thermal effects, they review relativistic mean field models for the equation of state including hyperons and pions at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: n_0 = 0.155 fm^{-3} with energy per nucleon of -15.9 MeV for the Balberg-Gal interaction
- Incompressibility modulus of the Balberg-Gal interaction: 375 MeV (noted as largely overestimated)
- Hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: U_Λ^{(N)}(n_0) = U_Σ^{(N)}(n_0) = -26.6 MeV, U_Ξ^{(N)}(n_0) = -22.8 MeV
- Λ-potential in Λ-matter: U_Λ^{(Λ)}(n_0/5) = -12.8 MeV (noted as too attractive compared to experimental value of -0.67 MeV)
- For β-equilibrium matter, the phase coexistence region covers roughly 0.3 fm^{-3} ≤ n_B ≤ 0.43 fm^{-3}
- Critical temperature for the strangeness-driven phase transition in the (n,p,Λ,e)-system is of order 15 MeV
- Temperature range considered for stellar matter: 0 < T ≤ 150 MeV
- Density range considered: 10^{-10} fm^{-3} ≤ n_B ≤ 1 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the phase diagram of the (n,p,Λ)-system by Gulminelli et al. (2012, 2013) to include the full baryon octet, and contrasts with the standard picture where hyperons are excluded from neutron star matter due to the softening of the equation of state. The paper builds on the observation of two neutron stars with masses of about 2 M_⊙ by Antoniadis (2013) and Demorest (2010), which constrains but does not exclude hyperonic matter. The results enable future studies of how a strangeness-driven phase transition would affect neutrino mean free paths and the hydrodynamical evolution of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, particularly through the critical behavior near the critical point.
