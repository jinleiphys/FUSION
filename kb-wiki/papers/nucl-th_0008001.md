# Kaon photoproduction on the nucleon: overview of some applications

> Some applications of the elementary kaon photoproduction process are discussed: the investigation of missing resonances in the p(gamma,K+)Lambda channel, the role of the P_{13}(1720) state in the p(gamma,K0)Sigma+ channel, and the calculation of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule. For the latter, we present an extension of our previous study to higher energies.

# Kaon photoproduction on the nucleon: overview of some applications

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the missing resonance \(D_{13}(1895)\) can explain the second peak near \(W = 1900\) MeV in the \(p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda\) total cross section, but only if the fit determines the mass, width, and coupling constants of the resonance; the claim is conditional on the specific isobar model and the agreement with quark model predictions.

## Method
The authors use an isobar model for kaon photoproduction with Born terms, \(K^*(892)\) and \(K_1(1270)\) vector meson poles in the \(t\)-channel, and resonance contributions including \(S_{11}(1650)\), \(P_{11}(1710)\), and \(P_{13}(1720)\). They perform fits to total cross section data from SAPHIR, allowing the mass, width, and coupling constants of candidate resonances to vary, and compare the results with constituent quark model predictions of Capstick and Roberts. For the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, they extend their previous calculation to higher energies by using a Regge model for cross sections between 5 and 15 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The \(p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda\) total cross section shows a structure around \(W = 1900\) MeV, which the model reproduces by including the \(D_{13}(1895)\) resonance.
- The previous GDH sum rule calculation gave \(\kappa^2_{p} (K) = -0.063\) and \(\kappa^2_{n} (K) = 0.031\) for kaon photoproduction contributions, with the upper integration limit fixed at 2.2 GeV.
- The Regge model is used to calculate cross sections in the energy region between 5 and 15 GeV, showing that contributions from higher energies are very small.
- The extracted fractional decay width for \(P_{13}(1720) \to K^0\Sigma^+\) is consistent with quark model predictions, with a magnitude almost one order smaller than the value given by the Particle Data Group.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of kaon photoproduction by Mart and collaborators, and addresses the problem of "missing resonances" predicted by constituent quark models of Capstick and Roberts (1998) but not observed in \(\pi N \to \pi N\) scattering. The investigation of the \(D_{13}(1895)\) resonance in the \(K\Lambda\) channel and the \(P_{13}(1720)\) role in \(K\Sigma\) channels builds on SAPHIR experimental data (1998, 1999). The GDH sum rule calculation extends the earlier work of Karliner (1973) and the authors' own previous study (1999) by incorporating higher-energy contributions via a Regge model from Guidal, Laget, and Vanderhaeghen. The results enable future improvements by reggeizing the \(t\)-channel intermediate states.
