# Parity nonconservation in deuteron photoreactions

> We calculate the asymmetries in parity nonconserving deuteron photodisintegration due to circularly polarized photons gamma+d to n+p with the photon laboratory energy ranging from the threshold up to 10 MeV and the radiative capture of thermal polarized neutrons by protons n+p to gamma+d. We use the leading order electromagnetic Hamiltonian neglecting the smaller nuclear exchange currents. Comparative calculations are done by using the Reid93 and Argonne v18 potentials for the strong interaction and the DDH and FCDH "best" values for the weak couplings in a weak one-meson exchange potential. A weak NDelta transition potential is used to incorporate also the Delta(1232)-isobar excitation in the coupled-channels formalism.

## Key claim

The authors calculate parity-nonconserving (PNC) asymmetries in deuteron photodisintegration by circularly polarized photons and in radiative capture of polarized thermal neutrons, finding that the threshold asymmetry in photodisintegration is insensitive to pion exchange and thus dominated by heavier mesons and possibly Delta-isobar excitation, while the neutron capture asymmetry is dominated by pion exchange. The calculations use two modern strong potentials and two sets of weak coupling constants, with the caveat that the weak Delta-nucleon couplings from FCDH are not entirely consistent with the DDH nucleon-nucleon couplings used for the other parts of the calculation.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Schrodinger equations for the parity-admixed nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-Delta system, using the Reid93 and Argonne v18 potentials for the strong interaction and the DDH and FCDH "best values" for the weak one-meson exchange couplings. They employ the leading-order electromagnetic Hamiltonian in the dipole approximation, neglecting nuclear exchange currents except as they appear through Siegert's theorem, and include a weak NDelta transition potential to incorporate Delta(1232)-isobar excitation in a coupled-channels formalism.

## Key numbers

- Photon laboratory energy range for photodisintegration: from threshold (2.22592 MeV) up to 10 MeV
- Thermal neutron energy for capture: 25 meV
- DDH weak pi-NN coupling constant h_pi^(1) = 4.6 x 10^(-7) (units of 10^(-7))
- FCDH weak pi-NN coupling constant h_pi^(1) = 2.7 x 10^(-7)
- DDH weak rho-NN coupling constants: h_rho^(0) = -11.4 x 10^(-7), h_rho^(1) = -0.2 x 10^(-7), h_rho^(2) = -9.5 x 10^(-7)
- DDH weak omega-NN coupling constants: h_omega^(0) = -1.9 x 10^(-7), h_omega^(1) = -1.1 x 10^(-7)
- Existing experimental data point for photodisintegration asymmetry: (7.7 +/- 5.3) x 10^(-6) at photon energy 3.2 MeV
- Existing experimental data point for photodisintegration asymmetry: (2.7 +/- 2.8) x 10^(-6) at photon energy 4.1 MeV
- Latest photon polarization measurement for np -> gamma d: (1.8 +/- 1.8) x 10^(-7)
- Preliminary NPDGamma result for neutron capture asymmetry: (-1.2 +/- 2.1(stat.) +/- 0.1(sys.)) x 10^(-7)
- Typical theoretical prediction for neutron capture asymmetry: -5 x 10^(-8) using DDH best value for h_pi^(1)

## Context

This work extends previous PNC calculations for deuteron photoreactions by Lee (1975), Oka (1981), Khriplovich (1984), Fujii (1984), Liu (2003), Schiavilla (2004), Hyun (2006), and Liu (2007), among others, using updated strong potentials and including Delta-isobar effects that had previously been studied only in PNC elastic pp scattering by Iqbal (1984) and Niskanen (1985). The calculations are motivated by ongoing NPDGamma experiment at LANSCE and SNS, which aims to improve the accuracy of the neutron capture asymmetry measurement to 20% of the theoretical prediction, thereby constraining the poorly known weak pi-NN coupling constant h_pi^(1).
