# BCS-BEC crossover at finite temperature in spin-orbit coupled Fermi gases

> By adopting a $T$-matrix-based method within the $G_0G$ approximation for the pair susceptibility, we study the effects of the pairing fluctuation on the three-dimensional spin-orbit coupled Fermi gases at finite temperature. The critical temperatures of the superfluid/normal phase transition are determined for three different types of spin-orbit coupling (SOC): (1) the extreme oblate (EO) or Rashba SOC, (2) the extreme prolate (EP) or equal Rashba-Dresselhaus SOC, and (3) the spherical (S) SOC. For EO- and S-type SOC, the SOC dependence of the critical temperature signals a crossover from BCS to BEC state; at strong SOC limit, the critical temperature recover those of ideal BEC of rashbons. The pairing fluctuation induces a pseudogap in the fermionic excitation spectrum in both superfluid and normal phases. We find that, for EO- and S-type SOC, even at weak coupling, sufficiently strong SOC can induce sizable pseudogap. Our research suggests that the spin-orbit coupled Fermi gases may open new means to the study of the pseudogap formation in fermionic systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for extreme oblate (Rashba) and spherical spin-orbit coupling, tuning the SOC strength alone can drive a BCS-BEC crossover even at weak attractive interaction, and that sufficiently strong SOC can induce a sizable pseudogap in the fermionic excitation spectrum even at weak coupling, a result that depends on the specific type of SOC and does not occur for extreme prolate (equal Rashba-Dresselhaus) SOC.

## Method

The authors adopt a T-matrix-based method within the G0G approximation for the pair susceptibility, extending the BCS theory to include pairing fluctuations. They solve coupled gap equations, number density equations, and pseudogap equations self-consistently for three-dimensional spin-orbit coupled Fermi gases at finite temperature, using a pole approximation for the pair propagator. The critical temperature is determined via the Thouless criterion, and the pseudogap is computed from the non-condensed pair contribution to the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- For spherical (S) SOC in the molecular BEC limit, the chemical potential is approximately -E_B/2, where the two-body binding energy is E_B = mλ² + (1/(4m))[1/a + sqrt(1/a² + 4m²λ²)]².
- For S SOC in the BEC limit, the gap squared is Δ² ≈ (32ε_F/(3π)) sqrt(ε_F/E_B) [(E_B - mλ²)/2]^(3/2).
- For extreme oblate (EO) SOC in the BEC limit, the perpendicular effective pair mass is m_b^⊥ ≈ 2m [1 - mλ²/(2E_B) - (E_B - mλ²)/(2E_B) ln((E_B - mλ²)/E_B)]^{-1}, while the parallel effective mass is m_b^∥ ≈ 2m.
- For EO SOC in the BEC limit, the gap squared is Δ² ≈ (8ε_F(E_B - mλ²)/(3π)) sqrt(2ε_F/E_B).
- For extreme prolate (EP) SOC, the chemical potential in the BEC limit is μ ≈ -1/(2ma²) - mλ²/2, and the gap squared recovers the result without SOC: Δ² ≈ (8ε_F/(3π)) (k_F/(ma)).
- At strong SOC limit for EO and S types, the critical temperature recovers that of an ideal BEC of rashbons.

## Context

This work extends zero-temperature mean-field studies of BCS-BEC crossover in spin-orbit coupled Fermi gases (Vyasanakere and Shenoy 2011; Hu 2011; Yu 2011) by including finite-temperature pairing fluctuation effects. It builds on the G0G T-matrix formalism developed by the Chicago group (PhysRevB58.R5936, 1999; prb61:11662, 2000) for pseudogap phenomena in Fermi gases without SOC. The results enable future experimental studies of pseudogap formation in spin-orbit coupled ultracold Fermi gases, such as those realized with ⁴⁰K at Shanxi University (Wang 2012) and ⁶Li at MIT (Cheuk 2012).
