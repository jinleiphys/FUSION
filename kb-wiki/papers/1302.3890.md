# Extraction of Axial Mass and Strangeness Values from the MiniBooNE Neutral Current Elastic Cross Section Measurement

> Results of the analysis of the MiniBooNE experiment data for the neutral current elastic neutrino scattering off the $CH_{2}$ target with the NuWro Monte Carlo generator are presented. Inclusion in the NuWro the two body current contribution leads to the axial mass value $M_A = 1.10^{+0.13}_{-0.15}$ GeV consistent with the older evaluations based on the neutrino-deuteron scattering data. The strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin is estimated with the value $g_A^s = -0.4^{+0.5}_{-0.3}$.

# Extraction of Axial Mass and Strangeness Values from the MiniBooNE Neutral Current Elastic Cross Section Measurement

## Key claim

Including two-body current (np-nh) contributions in the NuWro Monte Carlo generator allows a simultaneous fit to MiniBooNE neutral current elastic data that yields an axial mass value consistent with older neutrino-deuteron scattering evaluations, but the extracted strange quark contribution remains consistent with zero within large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator with a local Fermi gas nuclear model and the transverse enhancement (TE) model for two-body current contributions to analyze MiniBooNE neutral current elastic neutrino scattering data on a CH2 target. They performed a simultaneous fit of the axial mass MA and the strange axial form factor gAs to the distribution of events in total reconstructed kinetic energy of final-state nucleons, using response matrices provided by the MiniBooNE collaboration to convert true kinetic energy to reconstructed energy. The analysis treated two-body current events by summing the kinetic energies of both final-state nucleons and applying the appropriate response matrices for proton-on-carbon events with final state interactions or neutron-on-carbon events.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass with two-body current contribution included: MA = 1.10+0.13-0.15 GeV
- Strange axial form factor with two-body current contribution included: gAs = -0.4+0.5-0.3
- Without two-body current contribution, effective axial mass for carbon: MAeff = 1.47 ± 0.10 GeV (χ2min/DOF = 23.6/50)
- Without two-body current contribution, strange axial form factor: gAs = 0.24 ± 0.46 (χ2min/DOF = 26.7/29)
- Two-body current contribution amounts to approximately 15% of the overall distribution at typical MiniBooNE flux neutrino energies around 700 MeV
- Axial mass for hydrogen fixed at MA = 1.03 GeV in the analysis without two-body currents

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of MiniBooNE neutral current elastic data by Butkevich and Perevalov (using relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation) and Ankowski (using spectral function formalism), and by the MiniBooNE collaboration itself which used the NUANCE generator without two-body current contributions. The inclusion of two-body currents reconciles the axial mass extracted from MiniBooNE data with older evaluations from neutrino-deuteron scattering experiments, supporting the interpretation that the previously reported larger effective axial mass values were due to unaccounted multinucleon contributions. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments such as T2K, and demonstrate that the total reconstructed kinetic energy distribution is a more robust observable for parameter extraction than the proton-enriched sample ratio, which is highly sensitive to details of final state interactions and two-body current kinematics.
