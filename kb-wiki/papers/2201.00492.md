# Analysis on the composite nature of the light scalar mesons $f_{0}(980)$ and $a_0(980)$

> We study the weight or compositeness of the $ππ$-$K\bar{K}$ and $πη$-$K\bar{K}$ in the composition of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances, respectively. Either we use the saturation of the total width and compositeness, or we use a Flatté parameterization taking also into account the spectral function of a near-threshold resonance. We make connections and compare between these two methods. We take input values for the pole mass and width from several determinations in the literature. In addition, we take as third input either the total compositeness or the decay-width branching ratio to the lighter channel for each resonance. It turns out that for the poles considered the meson-meson components are dominant for the $f_0(980)$, while for the $a_0(980)$ resonance they are subdominant. We also provide partial decay widths and partial compositeness coefficients, so that the $K\bar{K}$ component is the most important one for the $f_0(980)$. Additionally, this study stresses the need to distinguish between the bare and dressed couplings and widths in a Flatté parameterization. We elaborate on the connection between the partial-decay widths calculated in terms of the dressed couplings and the actual measured ones. Due to the coupled-channel dynamics when the pole lies near the heavier threshold in the second Riemann sheet some changes are needed with respect to standard relations.

## Key claim

The meson-meson components (ππ-KK̅ for f₀(980) and πη-KK̅ for a₀(980)) are dominant in the composition of the f₀(980) but subdominant for the a₀(980), with the caveat that the a₀(980) may lie in a hidden Riemann sheet where the methods used cannot be applied, and the results depend on whether the pole lies in the second or third Riemann sheet.

## Method

The authors used two complementary methods: (1) saturation of the total width and compositeness relation to solve for couplings and partial compositeness coefficients, and (2) a Flatté parameterization incorporating the spectral density function of a near-threshold resonance. Input pole masses and widths were taken from dispersive analyses (GarciaMartin 2011) and unitarized chiral perturbation theory (Guo 2012), with either the total compositeness X or the decay-width branching ratio to the lighter channel as a third input. The analysis distinguished between bare and dressed couplings in the Flatté parameterization and accounted for the Riemann sheet (second or third) in which the pole lies.

## Key numbers

- For f₀(980) using the pole from GarciaMartin (2011): m_R = 996 ± 7 MeV, Γ_R = 50 +20 −12 MeV
- For f₀(980) using the pole from Guo (2012): m_R = 978 +7 −11 MeV, Γ_R = 58 +18 −22 MeV
- For f₀(980) with branching ratio r_exp = 0.52 ± 0.12 (from B decays to Kππ), the total compositeness X ranges around 0.6–0.9 within errors
- For a₀(980) with the PDG average branching ratio, the total compositeness X is significantly smaller, around 0.2–0.4, taking into account errors and variations in the method

## Context

This work extends the compositeness analysis of scalar mesons beyond bound-state formalisms to resonances near threshold, following the probabilistic interpretation of Guo (2015) and Oller (2017). It contrasts with earlier studies such as Dai (2012), where a₀(980) was treated as a Breit-Wigner resonance and f₀(980) as a KK̅ bound state, and Sekihara (2014), which found that f₀(980) and a₀(980) cannot simultaneously be KK̅ bound states. The results enable quantitative assessment of the molecular versus compact components in these resonances and clarify the distinction between bare and dressed couplings in Flatté parameterizations for near-threshold states.
