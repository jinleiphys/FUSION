# What does the matter created in high multiplicity proton-nucleus collisions teach us about the 3-D structure of the proton?

> The study of multiparticle correlations and collectivity in the hot and dense matter created in collisions of heavy-ions as well as those of smaller systems such as proton--heavy-ion collisions has progressed to the point where detailed knowledge of the three-dimensional structure of the proton is needed to confront experimental data. We discuss results from a simple proof-of-principle initial state parton model which reproduces many features of the data on proton--heavy-ion collisions that are often ascribed to hydrodynamic flow. We outline how this model can be further improved with particular emphasis on missing elements in our understanding of the dynamical spatial and momentum structure of the proton.

## Key claim

A simple proof-of-principle initial-state parton model, in which eikonal quarks from the projectile proton multiple-scatter off color domains in a dense gluon-saturated target, can qualitatively reproduce many features of multiparticle azimuthal correlations in proton-nucleus collisions that are often interpreted as definitive evidence for hydrodynamic collectivity. The authors explicitly caution that this pattern of correlations "in of itself, is not conclusive" for hydrodynamic flow.

## Method

The authors compute multiparticle azimuthal cumulants \(c_n\{m\}\) and the corresponding Fourier harmonics \(v_n\{m\}\) for quarks scattering eikonally off a nuclear target modeled by the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. The projectile is described by a Gaussian Wigner function with a single nonperturbative scale \(B_p = 4\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (fixed from HERA dipole fits), and the target is characterized by its saturation scale \(Q_{s,T}\). The \(m\)-particle distributions are obtained from the expectation value of products of \(m\) dipole operators, computed analytically for two and four Wilson lines and numerically for higher-point correlators using a systematic expansion around the last light-cone slice. For the \(v_n\{m\}\) with \(m \geq 6\), a \(U(1)\) Abelian version of the model is used to enable rapid computation.

## Key numbers

- The projectile size parameter is fixed to \(B_p = 4\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), determined from dipole model fits to HERA exclusive vector meson production data (Kowalski 2006, Rezaeian 2012).
- The transverse-momentum-dependent \(v_2\) peaks between \(\sim 1-2\ \text{GeV}\), similar in magnitude and shape to experimental data (CMS 2013).
- For \(\pmax = 10, 20, 40\ \text{GeV}\) (with \(\pmax \gtrsim Q_{s,T}\)), the two-particle cumulant \(c_2\{2\}\) scales as \((Q_{s,T}^2 B_p)^{-0.95}\) at large \(Q_{s,T}^2\).
- For \(\pmax = 3, 5\ \text{GeV}\) (with \(\pmax \leq Q_{s,T}\)), the scaling is much weaker: \(c_2\{2\} \sim (Q_{s,T}^2 B_p)^{-0.18}\).
- A real \(v_2\{4\}\) (negative \(c_2\{4\}\)) appears at a finite value of \(Q_{s,T}\), qualitatively similar to the transition from positive to negative \(c_2\{4\}\) observed experimentally (ATLAS 2017) as a function of increasing multiplicity.

## Context

This work extends the two-particle initial-state correlation studies of Lappi (2015) to multiparticle cumulants \(v_n\{m\}\) for \(m \geq 4\). It directly challenges the interpretation that the observed hierarchy \(v_2\{2\} > v_2\{4\} = v_2\{6\} = v_2\{8\}\) in small systems (proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at the LHC, and proton/deuteron/\(^3\)He-heavy-ion collisions at RHIC) is unique evidence for hydrodynamic collectivity. The authors show that their initial-state parton model reproduces this hierarchy, including the convergence of \(v_2\{m\}\) for \(m \geq 4\), and also reproduces the systematics of symmetric cumulants. The work motivates more stringent tests of hydrodynamic collectivity and highlights the need for a better understanding of the 3-D spatial and momentum structure of multiparton correlations in the proton wavefunction, including quantum interference effects discussed by Kovner (2017, 2018) and full Yang-Mills simulations (Schenke 2015, 2016).
