# Spectroscopy of the neutron-rich hypernucleus $^{7}_Λ$He from electron scattering

> The missing mass spectroscopy of the $^{7}_Λ$He hypernucleus was performed, using the $^{7}$Li$(e,e^{\prime}K^{+})^{7}_Λ$He reaction at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility Hall C. The $Λ$ binding energy of the ground state (1/2$^{+}$) was determined with a smaller error than that of the previous measurement, being $B_Λ$ = 5.55 $\pm$ 0.10(stat.) $\pm$ 0.11(sys.) MeV. The experiment also provided new insight into charge symmetry breaking in p-shell hypernuclear systems. Finally, a peak at $B_Λ$ = 3.65 $\pm$ 0.20(stat.) $\pm$ 0.11(sys.) MeV was observed and assigned as a mixture of 3/2$^{+}$ and 5/2$^{+}$ states, confirming the "gluelike" behavior of $Λ$, which makes an unstable state in $^{6}$He stable against neutron emission.

## Key claim
The ground-state Λ binding energy of ⁷ΛHe was measured with improved precision compared to the previous experiment, and an excited-state peak was observed for the first time, confirming that the Λ hyperon acts as a "glue" to stabilize a neutron-unstable state in ⁶He against neutron emission. The systematic error was reduced from 0.25 MeV in the prior measurement to 0.11 MeV, but the statistical error on the excited state remains larger than for the ground state.

## Method
The experiment used the ⁷Li(e,e′K⁺)⁷ΛHe reaction at Jefferson Lab Hall C with an electron beam energy of 2.344 GeV. Missing-mass spectroscopy was performed by detecting the scattered electron and the produced kaon in coincidence, with energy calibration based on elementary p(e,e′K⁺)Λ,Σ⁰ reactions on a hydrogen target. The data set contained five times higher statistics than the previous measurement (JLab E01-011).

## Key numbers
- Ground state (1/2⁺) Λ binding energy: B_Λ = 5.55 ± 0.10 (stat.) ± 0.11 (sys.) MeV
- Excited state peak (mixture of 3/2⁺ and 5/2⁺): B_Λ = 3.65 ± 0.20 (stat.) ± 0.11 (sys.) MeV
- Previous ground state measurement (JLab E01-011): B_Λ = 5.68 ± 0.03 (stat.) ± 0.25 (sys.) MeV
- First excited-state energy of core nucleus ⁶He (2⁺): 0.824 MeV above α+n+n breakup threshold, with decay width Γ = 0.113 MeV
- Ground state binding energies of A=7 isotriplet partners from emulsion: ⁷ΛLi (T=0) = 5.58 ± 0.03 MeV, ⁷ΛBe = 5.16 ± 0.08 MeV; ⁷ΛLi* (T=1) = 5.26 ± 0.03 MeV (derived from γ-ray spectroscopy)

## Context
This work extends the study of Λ-nucleon charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in p-shell hypernuclei, following earlier measurements of the A=4 iso-doublet (⁴ΛHe and ⁴ΛH) by emulsion experiments and the previous ⁷ΛHe measurement by the same collaboration (JLab E01-011). The results differ from a cluster model prediction by Hiyama et al. that used a phenomenological ΛN CSB potential fitted to the A=4 systems. The observation of the excited states confirms theoretical predictions by Hiyama et al. and Richter et al. that the Λ hyperon can stabilize neutron-unstable core states, and enables future comparisons with mirror hypernuclei ⁷ΛLi* and ⁷ΛBe to quantify CSB in the A=7 isotriplet.
