# Low-energy dipole excitation modes in ${}^{10}$Be

> Low-energy dipole excitations in ${}^{10}$Be were investigated based on the generator coordinate method with $α+ {}^6$He and $α+ α+ 2n$ cluster models. We obtained three $1^-$ states in $E_{x} < 15$~MeV, which show different characters in the $E1$, compressive dipole, and toroidal dipole transition strengths. We found the strong toroidal dipole transition in the $1_1^-$ state, remarkable $E1$ strength in the $1^-_2$ state, and the strong $E1$ and compressive dipole strength in the $1^-_3$ state. The $1^-_1$ is described by the $α+ {}^6$He cluster structure, where as the $1^-_2$ and $1^-_3$ states are understood by three-body excitations of the $α+ α+ 2n$ clustering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that three low-energy dipole states in ${}^{10}$Be below 15 MeV excitation energy exhibit distinct characters in toroidal dipole (TD), electric dipole (E1), and compressive dipole (CD) transition strengths, with the lowest state showing a strong toroidal dipole transition. However, the calculated E1 strength for the $1_1^-$ state overestimates the experimental value by several orders of magnitude, indicating the model captures the qualitative pattern but not the quantitative suppression of the observed E1 transition.

## Method

The authors applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) using two types of cluster-model basis wave functions: a two-body $\alpha + {}^6$He di-cluster configuration and a three-body $\alpha + \alpha + 2n$ tri-cluster configuration. They superposed parity- and angular-momentum-projected Brink-Bloch cluster wave functions with discretized generator coordinates for the inter-cluster distances and angles, then diagonalized the Hamiltonian to obtain the ground and $1^-$ excited states of ${}^{10}$Be. Dipole transition strengths were calculated using the E1 operator for isovector dipole transitions, the compressive dipole (CD) operator for isoscalar dipole transitions, and the toroidal dipole (TD) operator to probe nuclear vorticity.

## Key numbers

- Three $1^-$ states were obtained in the excitation energy region $E_x < 15$ MeV: the $1_1^-$ at 7.52 MeV, the $1_2^-$ at 10.27 MeV, and the $1_3^-$ at 13.78 MeV.
- The $1_1^-$ state has a TD transition strength corresponding to 3.4% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for the CD operator, and an $EB(E1;0_1^+\rightarrow 1_1^-) = 5.38 \times 10^{-1}$ fm${}^2$MeV, which overestimates the experimental value of $1.62\times 10^{-5}$ fm${}^2$MeV.
- The $1_2^-$ state carries 17.5% of the Thomas-Reich-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule ($S(\textrm{TRK}) \sim 35.64$ fm${}^2$MeV) for E1 transitions, while the $1_3^-$ state carries 13.9% of the TRK sum rule.
- The $1_3^-$ state has a CD transition strength of 6.2% of the EWSR, whereas the CD strength for the $1_2^-$ state almost vanishes.
- The total energy-weighted sum in $E_x < 15$ MeV is approximately 10% of the EWSR for CD transitions and 33% of the TRK sum rule for E1 transitions.
- The calculated binding energy of ${}^{10}$Be is 58.48 MeV, which underestimates the experimental value of 65.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of low-energy dipole excitations in light nuclei using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) by Kanada-Enyo (2017), which predicted toroidal dipole excitations in ${}^{10}$Be but only included $\alpha$-${}^6$He relative motion without treating internal excitations of the ${}^6$He cluster. The present calculation incorporates both two-body and three-body cluster dynamics, enabling the description of the $1_2^-$ and $1_3^-$ states as three-body excitations of the $\alpha+\alpha+2n$ clustering. The results connect to broader theoretical efforts on toroidal modes in deformed nuclei by Kvasil (2013), Nesterenko (2016), and Repko (2017), and to experimental studies of low-energy dipole strengths in neutron-rich nuclei relevant for the $r$-process as discussed by Goriely (2004). The work enables future investigations of how cluster degrees of freedom generate distinct dipole excitation modes in light neutron-rich nuclei.
