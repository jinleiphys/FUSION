# Precision calculation of threshold pi^- d scattering, pi N scattering lengths, and the GMO sum rule

> We use chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to calculate the $π^- d$ scattering length with an accuracy of a few percent, including isospin-violating corrections both in the two- and three-body sector. In particular, we provide the technical details of a recent letter, where we used data on pionic deuterium and pionic hydrogen atoms to extract the isoscalar and isovector pion-nucleon scattering lengths $a^+$ and $a^-$. We study isospin-breaking contributions to the three-body part of $a_{π^-d}$ due to mass differences, isospin violation in the $πN$ scattering lengths, and virtual photons. This last class of effects is ostensibly infrared enhanced due to the smallness of the deuteron binding energy. However, we show that the leading virtual-photon effects that might undergo such enhancement cancel, and hence the standard ChPT counting provides a reliable estimate of isospin violation in $a_{π^- d}$ due to virtual photons. Finally, we discuss the validity of the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule in the presence of isospin violation, and use it to determine the charged-pion-nucleon coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the π⁻d scattering length using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with a few percent accuracy, including isospin-violating corrections from mass differences, isospin violation in πN scattering lengths, and virtual photons; crucially, the authors show that leading virtual-photon effects that might be enhanced by the small deuteron binding energy actually cancel, so standard ChPT counting provides a reliable estimate of isospin violation from virtual photons.

## Method

The authors decompose the π⁻d scattering length into two-body (πN) and three-body (πNN) contributions, compute the three-body part using Weinberg power counting to order N²LO, and include isospin-violating corrections from quark mass differences and electromagnetic interactions at next-to-leading order in ChPT. They evaluate diagrams with deuteron wave functions from chiral and phenomenological NN potentials, and combine the π⁻d result with pionic hydrogen data to extract the isoscalar and isovector πN scattering lengths a⁺ and a⁻, then use the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme (GMO) sum rule to determine the charged-pion-nucleon coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- π⁻d scattering length three-body part: computed to better than 10% accuracy, with variation of about 5% when using different deuteron wave functions
- Pionic hydrogen 1s level shift: ε₁s = (−7.120 ± 0.012) eV
- Pionic hydrogen 1s width: Γ₁s = (0.823 ± 0.019) eV
- Pionic deuterium 1s level shift: ε₁sᴰ = (2.356 ± 0.031) eV
- Panofsky ratio: P = 1.546 ± 0.009
- Isospin-violating correction to the isoscalar scattering length: Δã⁺ = (−3.3 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹
- Isospin-violating correction to the isovector scattering length: Δa⁻ = (1.4 ± 1.3) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹
- Isospin-violating correction to π⁻p scattering length: Δãπ⁻p = (−2.0 ± 1.3) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹
- Isospin-violating correction to charge-exchange scattering length: Δaπ⁻pᶜᵉˣ = (0.4 ± 0.9) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹
- Vacuum polarization correction to wave function at origin for πH: δ_εᵛᵃᶜ = 0.48%
- Vacuum polarization correction to wave function at origin for πD: δ_εᴰᵛᵃᶜ = 0.51%

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework for πN scattering lengths developed by Hoferichter et al. (2009) and Gasser et al. (2002), and provides the technical details behind the letter by Baru et al. (2011) that extracted a⁺ and a⁻ from pionic atom data. The calculation enables a precise determination of the pion-nucleon sigma term through dispersive analyses and improves the evaluation of the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule for the πNN coupling constant, which impacts nuclear physics through the NN interaction. The authors build on prior work by Weinberg (1966) on low-energy theorems, Beane et al. (1998) on three-body operators, and Liebig et al. (2011) on the multiple-scattering series.
