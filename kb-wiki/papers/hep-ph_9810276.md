# Chiral dynamics - status and perspectives

> I review the status of chiral perturbation theory in the one-nucleon sector and give some predictions to be tested. I then discuss various methods to go to higher energies (inclusion of the Delta(1232), dispersion relations) and also to higher precision (virtual photons and isospin violation).

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) in the one-nucleon sector provides systematic predictions near threshold, but the convergence of the chiral expansion is not always rapid; for quantities like the scalar form factor, dispersion theory properly constrained by chiral symmetry is a better tool because the chiral expansion converges slowly.

## Method
The paper reviews the status of baryon chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) for pion-nucleon systems, comparing one-loop and next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculations to experimental data from PSI, TRIUMF, MAMI, and SAL. It then discusses two extensions: inclusion of the Delta(1232) resonance via a "small scale expansion" in three small parameters (pion energy, pion mass, and N-Delta mass splitting), and combination with dispersion relations to improve predictions at higher energies and higher precision, including virtual photons and isospin violation.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: F_pi ≈ 93 MeV, chiral symmetry breaking scale: Lambda_chi ≈ 4π F_pi ≈ 1.2 GeV
- N-Delta mass splitting: Δ = m_Delta - m_N = 293 MeV ≈ 3 F_pi
- CHPT prediction for the electric dipole amplitude for neutral pion production off deuterium: E_d = (-1.8 ± 0.2) × 10^{-3}/M_pi, compared to empirical value from SAL: E_d^emp = (-1.45 ± 0.09) × 10^{-3}/M_pi (overlap within 1.5 sigma)
- Delta sigma_piN at the Cheng-Dashen point from small scale expansion to order epsilon^3: Δσ_piN = (7.4 + 4.1) MeV = 11.5 MeV, compared to dispersion-theoretical analysis: (15 ± 1) MeV
- Proton sigma-term including isospin violation to third order: σ_p(0) = 47.2 MeV - 3.9 MeV = 43.3 MeV (isospin-violating terms reduce it by ~8%)
- Triangle deviation for pion-nucleon scattering at threshold: to second order Δ_piN = 2.3%

## Context
This review extends the work of Weinberg (1979) on chiral Lagrangians and the systematic power counting in CHPT, and builds on the heavy baryon formalism of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1992). It contrasts the pure chiral expansion with the small scale expansion including the Delta(1232) developed by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1997), and with the dispersion-theoretical analysis of the scalar form factor by Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio (1991). The paper enables future tests of chiral predictions in spin-dependent Compton scattering, generalized polarizabilities in virtual Compton scattering, and systematic calculations of isospin violation in pion-nucleon scattering.
