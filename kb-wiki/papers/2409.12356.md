# Magnetized neutral 2SC color superconductivity and possible origin of the inner magnetic field of magnetars

> In this paper the neutral 2SC phase of color superconductivity is investigated in the presence of a magnetic field and for diquark coupling constants and baryonic densities that are expected to characterize neutron stars. Specifically, the behavior of the charged gluons Meissner masses is investigated in the parameter region of interest taking into account, in addition, the contribution of a rotated magnetic field. It is found that up to moderately-high diquark coupling constants the mentioned Meissner masses become tachyonic independently of the applied magnetic-field amplitude, hence signalizing the chromomagnetic instability of this phase. To remove the instability, the restructuring of the system ground state is proposed, which now will be formed by vortices of the rotated charged gluons. These vortices boost the applied magnetic field having the most significant increase for relatively low applied magnetic fields. Finally, considering that with the stellar rotational frequency observed for magnetars a field of the order of $10^8$ G can be generated by dynamo effect, we show that by the boosting effect just described the field can be amplified to $10^{17}$ G that is in the range of inner core fields expected for magnetars. Thus, we conclude that the described mechanism could be the one responsible for the large fields characterizing magnetars if the cores of these compact objects are formed by neutral 2SC matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the chromomagnetic instability of the neutral 2SC phase in a magnetic field can be removed by restructuring the ground state into gluon vortices, and that this vortex formation amplifies an applied magnetic field; the authors show that a dynamo-generated field of order 10^8 G can be boosted to 10^17 G, which lies in the range of inner core fields expected for magnetars, but only if the cores of these compact objects are formed by neutral 2SC matter.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with massless quarks in the presence of a constant uniform rotated magnetic field, solving the gap equation and electric neutrality condition simultaneously to obtain the diquark gap Delta and electric chemical potential mu_e for baryonic chemical potentials from 320 to 500 MeV. They then compute the Meissner masses of the charged gluons using the polarization tensor formalism from Huang et al., and analyze the effective action for charged gluon fields in the background of the rotated magnetic field to find the vortex condensate solution and the induced magnetic field from the coupled equations for the gluon condensate and the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* For diquark coupling eta = 0.75, 0.85, and 0.95, the squared Meissner mass of charged gluons is negative (tachyonic) for baryonic chemical potentials between 320 and 500 MeV at both 10^16 G and 10^18 G; only for eta = 1.15 does the Meissner mass stop being tachyonic.
* A dynamo-generated magnetic field of order 10^8 G can be amplified to 10^17 G by the gluon vortex boosting mechanism, matching the inner core field range expected for magnetars.
* The magnetic field does not significantly affect the behavior of Delta and delta mu_e in the parameter range under consideration, as shown for field values of 10^16 G and 10^18 G.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the neutral 2SC phase in a magnetic field by Jaikumar et al. and Mishra et al., which were constrained to the evolution of the quark gap and constituent quark mass, by investigating the effect of the applied magnetic field on the gluon sector. The chromomagnetic instability addressed here was previously identified by Huang et al. in the gapless 2SC phase, and the proposed solution using gluon vortices follows the "zero-mode problem" solution found for Yang-Mills fields by Ferrer and de la Incera and for the MCFL phase by Ferrer et al. The mechanism enables a possible explanation for the origin of the strong magnetic fields of magnetars, with inner core fields estimated to range from 10^17 to 10^20 G by previous authors.
