# Study of Beta Equilibrated 2+1 Flavor Quark Matter in PNJL Model

> We report a first case study of the phase diagram of 2+1 flavor strongly interacting matter in $β-$equilibrium, using the Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model. Physical characteristics of relevant thermodynamic observables have been discussed. A comparative analysis with the corresponding observables in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is presented. We find distinct differences between the models in terms of a number of thermodynamic quantities like the speed of sound, specific heat, various number densities as well as entropy. The present study is expected to give us a better insight into the role that the superdense matter created in heavy ion collision experiments play in our understanding of the properties of matter inside the core of supermassive stars in the Universe.

## Key claim

This paper presents a first study of the phase diagram of 2+1 flavor strongly interacting matter under beta-equilibrium conditions using the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, finding that the critical endpoint (CEP) in the PNJL model occurs at a slightly higher temperature and lower quark chemical potential compared to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, with the CEP temperature lowered by about 10 MeV when a non-zero electron chemical potential is introduced.

## Method

The authors extended the 2+1 flavor PNJL model to include beta-equilibrium conditions (mu_d = mu_u + mu_e, mu_s = mu_d) and charge neutrality, treating electrons as free non-interacting fermions. They solved the coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields sigma_u, sigma_d, sigma_s, and the Polyakov loop fields Phi and Phi-bar by extremizing the thermodynamic potential at given temperature T, quark chemical potential mu_q, and electron chemical potential mu_e. The resulting thermodynamic observables (equation of state, speed of sound, specific heat, compressibility, number densities, entropy per baryon) were computed and compared with those from the NJL model.

## Key numbers

* The critical endpoint (CEP) for the PNJL model occurs at a temperature about 10 MeV lower when mu_e = 40 MeV compared to mu_e = 0 MeV.
* At T = 50 MeV, the constituent quark masses in the PNJL model show a discontinuous jump at around mu_q = 350 MeV, indicating a first order phase transition.
* The strange quark constituent mass goes down to the current mass at around mu_q = 500 MeV in both models, producing a second crossover feature.
* The speed of sound squared c_s^2 in the PNJL model starts from the ideal gas value of 1/3 at mu_q = 0 MeV and falls sharply to almost zero at the transition, while in the NJL model it starts from a non-zero value and shows a sharp fall around mu_q ~ 320 MeV.
* Normal nuclear matter density n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} was used to scale baryon number densities.
* At and above 3 times nuclear matter density, the matter appears to be always in the partonic phase for both NJL and PNJL models.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 2+1 flavor PNJL model thermodynamics and phase diagrams (paramita, 2010; anirban, 2010) by incorporating beta-equilibrium conditions relevant for neutron star interiors. It contrasts with earlier NJL model studies of beta-equilibrated matter (Buballa, 2005; Hanauske, 2000) and with the MIT bag model and color-dielectric model approaches to neutron star structure (Burgio, 2002; Alford, 2005; Ghosh, 2003). The results enable future comparative studies of how laboratory heavy-ion collision experiments (probing matter without weak equilibrium) relate to astrophysical compact star interiors (where weak interactions equilibrate), and suggest that the hadron-parton transition in neutron stars might be a crossover rather than a first-order transition.
