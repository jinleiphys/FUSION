# Quark level and hadronic contributions to the electric dipole moment of charged leptons in the standard model

> We evaluate the electric dipole moment of charged leptons in the standard model, where the complex phase of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix is the only source of CP violation. We first prove that, at the quark-gluon level, it is suppressed by a factor of $m_b^2 m_c^2 m_s^2$ at all orders of perturbation due to the Glashow-Iliopoulos-Maiani mechanism. We then calculate the hadronic long distance contribution generated by vector mesons at one-loop level. The $|ΔS|=1$ weak hadronic interaction is derived using the factorization, and the strong interaction is modeled by the hidden local symmetry framework. We find that the electric dipole moments of charged leptons obtained from this hadronic mechanism are much larger than the perturbative four-loop level quark-gluon process, by several orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The standard model electric dipole moment of charged leptons receives a hadronic long-distance contribution from vector mesons that is several orders of magnitude larger than the perturbative four-loop quark-gluon process, though both remain far below current experimental sensitivity; the quark-level contribution is suppressed by a factor of \(m_b^2 m_c^2 m_s^2\) at all orders of perturbation due to the Glashow-Iliopoulos-Maiani mechanism, a stronger suppression than the abstract's generic statement about GIM cancellation.

## Method

The authors first prove analytically, using the unitarity of the CKM matrix and Taylor expansion of quark propagators with neutral boson insertions, that the quark-gluon level contribution to the charged lepton EDM vanishes at two- and three-loop order and is suppressed by four quark mass factors at four-loop order. They then calculate the hadronic long-distance contribution using one-loop diagrams involving \(K^*\) vector mesons, where the \(|\Delta S|=1\) weak hadronic interaction is derived via factorization of the four-quark effective Hamiltonian with next-to-leading order logarithmic running, and the strong interaction is modeled using the hidden local symmetry framework for vector mesons.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to quantify the standard model background for EDM searches, following the three-loop cancellation proofs by Pospelov (1991), Booth (1993), and Pospelov (2013), and the four-loop quark-level estimates by Hoogeveen (1990). It provides the first evaluation of the hadronic long-distance contribution to charged lepton EDMs, which had never been calculated previously, and enables future work to refine the hadronic modeling using lattice QCD or more sophisticated chiral approaches to reduce the theoretical uncertainty.
