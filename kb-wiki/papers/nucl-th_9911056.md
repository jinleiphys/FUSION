# Beyond the time independent mean field theory for nuclear and atomic reactions: Inclusion of particle-hole correlations in a generalized random phase approximation

> The time independent mean field method (TIMF) for scattering defines biorthonormal sets of single-particle wave functions and corresponding creation and annihilation operators. 2p - 2h correlations can be introduced through a generalized random phase approximation; 1p - 1h contributions vanish (Brillouin theorem). While the general variational method for scattering by Giraud and Nagarajan solves inhomogeneous Euler equations by inversion of the standard, hermitean Hamiltonian, the present approach diagonalizes a non-hermitean Hamiltonian, which carries the information about entrance and exit channels.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the time-independent mean field (TIMF) method for scattering can be extended beyond mean field by incorporating 2p-2h correlations through a generalized random phase approximation (RPA), where 1p-1h contributions vanish due to a generalized Brillouin theorem. The key formal innovation is replacing the inversion of the standard Hermitian Hamiltonian (as in the Giraud-Nagarajan variational method) with diagonalization of a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian that encodes entrance and exit channel information.

## Method

The authors reformulate the scattering problem by introducing a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian H' = H + Λ|χ⟩⟨χ'|, where Λ is the reciprocal of the Green function D(z) and |χ⟩, ⟨χ'| are channel wave functions. They solve the eigenvalue problem for H' using a hierarchy of approximations: first a Hartree-Fock approximation yielding a zero-order answer z₀(Λ), then a Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA), and finally a random phase approximation (RPA) giving a second-order correction Δz(Λ). The RPA equations are solved via a symplectic diagonalization procedure, and the final result z₂(Λ) = z₀(Λ) + Δz(Λ) is functionally inverted to obtain the improved Green function. The method is tested numerically on a one-dimensional, separable two-body Hamiltonian with Gaussian interaction.

## Key numbers

- The RPA correction to the Green function is given by Δz(Λ) = ½(∑_{ν=1}^{M} E*_ν - Tr A), where E*_ν are RPA eigenvalues and A is a matrix element defined in the paper
- The test calculation used a separable two-body Hamiltonian of the form ⟨q'Q'|H|qQ⟩ = δ(Q-Q')[q²δ(q-q') - λν²qq'exp(-ν²q²)exp(-ν'²q'²)]
- The numerical test was performed at Im E = 2.0 (energy units) for a solvable two-body model with Gaussian interaction

## Context

This work extends the time-independent mean field theory of collisions (TIMF) developed by Giraud, Nagarajan, and Thompson (1984), which had been tested for 3- and 4-particle systems and applied to various nuclear and atomic reactions. The approach offers an alternative to traditional methods for including correlations in scattering theories, such as the antisymmetrization of cluster structures (Tang, 1981) and fluctuations in time-dependent Hartree-Fock (Balian and Veneroni, 1989). The method enables systematic inclusion of particle-hole correlations in scattering calculations beyond the mean field approximation, with the numerical test on a solvable model showing improvement over TIMF results.
