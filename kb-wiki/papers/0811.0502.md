# Effect of Tensor Correlations on Gamow-Teller States in 90Zr and 208Pb

> The tensor terms of the Skyrme effective interaction are included in the self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF+RPA) model. The Gamow-Teller (GT) strength function of 90Zr and 208Pb are calculated with and without the tensor terms. The main peaks are moved downwards by about 2 MeV when including the tensor contribution. About 10% of the non-energy weighted sum rule is shifted to the excitation energy region above 30 MeV by the RPA tensor correlations. The contribution of the tensor terms to the energy weighted sum rule is given analytically, and compared to the outcome of RPA.

## Key claim

Including tensor terms in both the HF mean field and the RPA residual interaction shifts about 10% of the non-energy weighted Gamow-Teller sum rule to excitation energies above 30 MeV even within the 1p-1h model space, while the main GT peak itself moves downward by about 2 MeV; the abstract's "main peaks are moved downwards by about 2 MeV" applies only to the dominant low-energy peaks, not to the average centroid which actually shifts upward.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations in coordinate space with a radial mesh up to 20 fm and a 0.1 fm step, using the Skyrme interaction SIII with added triplet-even (T=1008 MeV fm⁵) and triplet-odd (U=-432 MeV fm⁵) zero-range tensor terms. They then solved the charge-exchange Random Phase Approximation (RPA) equations for the Gamow-Teller strength in ⁹⁰Zr and ²⁰⁸Pb, comparing three cases: no tensor terms, tensor terms in HF only, and tensor terms in both HF and RPA. The two-body spin-orbit residual interaction was omitted from RPA, which the authors note breaks strict self-consistency but is justified by its small effect on GT transitions.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹⁰Zr, including tensor terms in both HF and RPA moves the centroid of GT strength in the 0-30 MeV region downward by about 1 MeV, and the high-energy peak at E ~16 MeV moves downward by about 2 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, including tensor terms in both HF and RPA moves the centroid of GT strength in the 0-30 MeV region downward by about 1.5 MeV, and the higher-energy peak at E ~18 MeV moves downward by about 3.3 MeV.
- About 10% of the non-energy weighted sum rule (NEWSR) is shifted from the 0-30 MeV region to the 30-60 MeV region by the tensor correlations in RPA for both nuclei.
- In ²⁰⁸Pb, 29% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) is shifted from the 0-30 MeV region to the 30-60 MeV region by including tensor terms in HF plus RPA; for ⁹⁰Zr the corresponding shift is 25% of EWSR.
- The tensor coupling constants used are T=1008 MeV fm⁵ (triplet-even) and U=-432 MeV fm⁵ (triplet-odd), consistent with the Skyrme force SIII.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Otsuka (2005) and Colo (2007) that added tensor terms perturbatively to existing Skyrme parametrizations, and by Brink (2007) who used SIII with tensor terms, by implementing the tensor force self-consistently in both HF and RPA for the first time. The results show that tensor correlations shift substantial GT strength to high excitation energies even at the 1p-1h level, a mechanism previously attributed mainly to 2p-2h couplings as studied by Bertsch (1977). The analytical formula derived for the tensor contribution to the energy-weighted sum rule enables estimation of the average GT energy shift without full RPA calculations, and the authors indicate that similar effects should be expected for spin-dipole and other spin-dependent excitation modes.
