# Neutrino masses from the GSI anomaly

> We investigate the influence of the strong Coulomb field of a heavy nucleus on massive neutrinos, produced in the K-shell electron capture (EC) decays of the H-like 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ ions. The corrections to the neutrino masses due to virtually produced charged lepton W-boson pairs in the strong Coulomb field of a nucleus with charge Ze are calculated and discussed with respect to their influence on the period of the time-modulation of the number of daughter ions, observed recently in the EC-decays of the H-like 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ ions at GSI in Darmstadt. These corrections explain the 2.9 times higher difference of the squared neutrino masses obtained from the time-modulation of the EC-decays with respect to the value deduced from the antineutrino-oscillation experiments of KamLAND.

## Key claim
The discrepancy between the squared neutrino mass difference derived from the GSI time-modulation experiments and the value from KamLAND antineutrino oscillations can be resolved by including corrections to neutrino masses from virtual charged lepton W-boson pairs interacting with the strong Coulomb field of the daughter nucleus, but only if these corrections are evaluated at the nuclear surface immediately after neutrino production.

## Method
The authors calculated the correction to the neutrino mass from the interaction of a massive neutrino with the strong Coulomb field of a daughter nucleus with charge Ze, mediated by virtual \(\ell^- W^+\) pairs (\(\ell = e, \mu, \tau\)) in the intermediate state. They used the weak leptonic interaction Lagrangian with W-boson exchange and the energy-dependent Green function of negatively charged leptons in a strong Coulomb field, evaluating the correction at the nuclear radius \(R = 5.712\,\mathrm{fm}\). The corrected period of time-modulation was then used to solve for the neutrino masses \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) by equating the GSI-derived \(\Delta m^2_{21}\) to the KamLAND value plus the correction terms.

## Key numbers
- The period of time-modulation for H-like \(^{140}\mathrm{Pr}^{58+}\) is \(T_{EC} = 7.06(8)\,\mathrm{s}\).
- The squared neutrino mass difference from GSI is \((\Delta m^2_{21})_{\mathrm{GSI}} = 2.18(3) \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV}^2\), which is 2.9 times larger than the KamLAND value \((\Delta m^2_{21})_{\mathrm{KL}} = 7.59(21) \times 10^{-5}\,\mathrm{eV}^2\).
- The correction to the neutrino mass from electron, muon, and tau intermediate states at the nuclear radius are \(\mathcal{M}_{e^-}(R) = -2.02 \times 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{eV}\), \(\mathcal{M}_{\mu^-}(R) = -5.16 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV}\), and \(\mathcal{M}_{\tau^-}(R) = -3.88 \times 10^{-5}\,\mathrm{eV}\).
- The resulting neutrino mass corrections are \(\delta m_1(R) = -14.74 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV}\) and \(\delta m_2(R) = -8.22 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV}\).
- The solved neutrino masses are \(m_1 = 0.11 + 4.26 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV}\) and \(m_2 = 0.11 + 0.82 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV}\).
- The mass of the third neutrino is \(m_3 = 0.12 + 8.05 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV}\), using \(\Delta m^2_{32} = 2.4 \times 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{eV}^2\).
- The sum of neutrino masses is \(\sum_j m_j = 0.34\,\mathrm{eV}\), consistent with the upper limit of \(< 1\,\mathrm{eV}\).

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Ivanov (2008) that the time-modulation of EC-decay rates at GSI arises from neutrino mass differences, and addresses the factor-2.9 discrepancy with the KamLAND result from Eguchi et al. (2003). The mechanism of Coulomb-field-induced mass corrections was calculated using Green functions from previous Coulomb field studies (Coulomb1, Coulomb2), and the results reconcile the GSI anomaly with standard neutrino oscillation parameters. The derived neutrino masses around 0.11 eV enable consistency checks with cosmological bounds on the sum of neutrino masses.
