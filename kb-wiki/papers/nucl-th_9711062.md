# Excluded volume hadron gas model for particle number ratios in A+A collisions

> We recapitulate a thermodynamically consistent excluded volume hadron gas model and examine its differences with other ``thermal models'' used in the literature. Preliminary experimental data for particle number ratios in the collisions of Au+Au at the BNL AGS (11$A$ GeV/$c$) and Pb+Pb at the CERN SPS (160$A$ GeV/$c$) are analyzed. For equal values of the hadron hard-core parameters the excluded volume model gives essentially the ideal gas predictions for the particle number ratios, which is similar to other thermal models. We observe, however, the systematic excess of experimental pion abundances compared to the ideal gas results. This effect can be explained in our model by a smaller pion hard-core volume compared to those of other hadrons. The absolute values for particle number and energy densities at the chemical freezeout are predicted with a simultaneous fit to all these AGS and SPS particle number ratios.

# Excluded volume hadron gas model for particle number ratios in A+A collisions

## Key claim

The excluded volume hadron gas model can explain the systematic excess of experimental pion abundances compared to ideal gas predictions by allowing a smaller hard-core volume for pions than for other hadrons, but only if the model is formulated in a thermodynamically consistent way that modifies chemical potentials rather than simply applying a common suppression factor to all particle densities.

## Method

The authors formulate a thermodynamically consistent van der Waals excluded volume hadron gas model in the grand canonical ensemble, where the pressure satisfies a transcendental equation \(p(T,\mu) = p^{id}(T, \mu - v p)\) for a single species, extended to multiple species with different hard-core volumes. They fit the model to preliminary experimental particle number ratios from Au+Au collisions at the BNL AGS (11\(A\) GeV/\(c\)) and Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS (160\(A\) GeV/\(c\)), using all known hadron resonances up to 2 GeV mass and including resonance decay contributions to final particle yields.

## Key numbers

* Chemical freeze-out temperature \(T \cong 140\) MeV and baryonic chemical potential \(\mu_b \cong 590\) MeV for Au+Au at AGS (11\(A\) GeV/\(c\))
* Chemical freeze-out temperature \(T \cong 185\) MeV and baryonic chemical potential \(\mu_b \cong 270\) MeV for Pb+Pb at SPS (160\(A\) GeV/\(c\))
* Effective pion chemical potential \(\mu^{*}_{\pi} \cong 100\) MeV for Au+Au AGS collisions
* Effective pion chemical potential \(\mu^{*}_{\pi} \cong 180\) MeV for Pb+Pb SPS collisions
* Intersection point of the AGS and SPS curves in the \((r_{\pi}, r)\) plane: pion hard-core radius \(r_{\pi} \cong 0.62\) fm, other hadron hard-core radius \(r \cong 0.8\) fm
* Total pion number density at chemical freeze-out for Au+Au AGS: \(n_{\pi}^{tot} \cong 0.054\) fm\(^{-3}\) (compared to experimental estimate \(n_{\pi}^{exp} \cong 0.063\) fm\(^{-3}\) for Si+Pb at AGS)
* Ideal gas total particle number densities exceed normal nuclear density \(n_o \cong 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) by factors of approximately 4 for AGS and 8 for SPS; excluded volume suppression reduces these by factors of 8 and 14 respectively

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamically consistent excluded volume model developed by Gorenstein (1991, 1993) and applies it to heavy-ion collision data from the AGS and SPS, addressing the pion underproduction problem noted in earlier thermal models by Cleymans (1993, 1994) and Rafelski (1993). The authors distinguish their self-consistent formulation from ad hoc van der Waals corrections used in other thermal models (Refs. 4-7 in the paper) and from the approach of Braun-Munzinger (1995), showing that only the consistent formulation allows hard-core volume differences to affect particle ratios. The fitted hard-core radii provide physically reasonable freeze-out densities and enable future work incorporating collective flow effects to fit hadron momentum spectra.
