# The Long Slow Death of the HBT Puzzle

## Key claim

The HBT puzzle, the failure of early hydrodynamic models to reproduce femtoscopic source sizes inferred from two-particle correlations at RHIC, now appears to be solved not by a single mechanism, but by a conspiracy of several factors (pre-equilibrium flow, a stiffer equation of state, and shear viscosity), each contributing less than half of the original discrepancy, that together make the collision evolution more explosive.

## Method

The author performed viscous hydrodynamic calculations coupled to a hadronic cascade for temperatures below 170 MeV, assuming both boost-invariant and azimuthal symmetries to reduce the model to a one-dimensional radial mesh. Gaussian source radii (Rout, Rside, Rlong) were extracted from the calculated source functions and compared to STAR data, with features added incrementally: pre-equilibrium flow (starting hydrodynamic expansion at 0.1 fm/c vs. 1.0 fm/c), a stiffer equation of state (nonzero speed of sound vs. first-order phase transition), shear viscosity (4πη/s = 2 vs. 0), and more compact initial energy density profiles.

## Key numbers

- The original discrepancy between hydrodynamic models and experimental HBT radii was approximately a factor of 2 or more for Rout, Rside, and Rlong; after including all features, models reproduce source sizes within the systematic error of the models, often stated as close to 10%.
- Pre-equilibrium flow was tested by comparing hydrodynamic expansion started at 0.1 fm/c vs. 1.0 fm/c.
- The default equation of state used a latent heat L = 1.6 GeV/fm³ with zero speed of sound (first-order transition); the stiffer case used L = 800 MeV/fm³ with cs² = 0.1 in the mixed phase and cs² = 0.3 above the mixed phase.
- Shear viscosity was set to 4πη/s = 2 for energy densities above εh + L; for lower energy densities the shear viscosity was set equal to that of a hadron gas.
- Longitudinal source sizes approach 10 fm, whereas initial nuclei are compressed to a tenth of a fm; sideward dimensions are double the initial transverse size.
- The more accurate treatment of the two-pion wave function (Coulomb and strong interactions) changed source sizes only at the level of a percent or two.

## Context

This work extends and resolves the HBT puzzle that arose when early hydrodynamic models by Soff (2000), Teaney (2001), and Hirano (2002) failed to reproduce femtoscopic source sizes, while purely microscopic simulations by Petersen (2008), Li (2008), Humanic (2006), and Lin (2002) fared better despite not incorporating soft regions in the equation of state. The solution enables future work to simultaneously address the full host of low-pt observables including elliptic flow, radii with respect to the reaction plane, non-identical particle correlations, and non-Gaussian features of the source function, with the ultimate goal of a statistical analysis of model parameters similar to cosmic microwave background analyses.
