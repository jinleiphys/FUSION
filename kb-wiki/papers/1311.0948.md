# Statistical and dynamical parts of the cumulants of conserved charges in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The Poisson-liked statistical fluctuations, which are caused by the finite number of produced particles, are firstly estimated for the cumulants of conserved charges, i.e., the cumulants of net-baryon, net-electric charge, and net-strangeness. They turn out to be the same as those baselines derived from Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model. The energy and centrality dependence of net-proton cumulants at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) are demonstrated to be mainly caused by statistical fluctuations. Subtracting the statistical fluctuations, the dynamical kurtosis of net- and total-proton from two versions of the AMPT model and the UrQMD model at current RHIC beam energies are presented. It is found that the observed sign change in the kurtosis of net-proton can not be reproduced by these three transport models. There is no significant difference between net- and total-proton kurtosis in model calculations, in contrary to the data at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed energy and centrality dependence of net-proton cumulants at RHIC is dominated by Poisson-like statistical fluctuations from the finite number of produced particles, and that the sign change in the dynamical kurtosis of net-proton seen in data cannot be reproduced by conventional transport models (AMPT default, AMPT with string melting, and UrQMD), suggesting missing correlations between conserved charges.

## Method

The authors first derive the statistical cumulants of net-baryon, net-electric charge, and net-strangeness by assuming independent Poisson distributions for each particle and antiparticle species, showing these match the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model baselines. They then compute the statistical cumulants of net-proton using the measured mean proton and antiproton numbers from STAR at three RHIC beam energies (19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV) and nine centralities. Finally, they subtract these statistical parts to obtain the dynamical kurtosis of net- and total-proton from two versions of the AMPT model and the UrQMD model at seven RHIC beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV), comparing with STAR data.

## Key numbers

- At 7.7 GeV, the mean of antiproton is two magnitudes smaller than that of proton, making all statistical cumulants mainly determined by the mean of proton.
- The statistical kurtosis of net-proton is given by \(\kappa_{p,stat} = 1/(\langle N_p \rangle + \langle N_{\bar p} \rangle)\), and the product \(\kappa_{p,stat} \sigma_{p,stat}^2 = 1\) exactly.
- The dynamical kurtosis of net-proton from STAR data is negative at non-central collisions for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 19.6\) GeV and positive at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 19.6\) GeV, with absolute values less than 0.1.
- The dynamical kurtosis of net-proton from all three transport models (AMPT default, AMPT with string melting, UrQMD) is positive at all seven beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV) and nine centralities, in contrast to the STAR data sign change.
- The dynamical kurtosis of total-proton from all three models is positive at all centralities and energies, consistent with STAR data.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of statistical fluctuations in cumulants of conserved charges, previously studied by Chen et al. (2011) and Bzdak, Koch, and Skokov (2013), by directly comparing statistical cumulants to RHIC data and by computing dynamical kurtosis in three transport models. The finding that conventional models (AMPT and UrQMD) cannot reproduce the sign change in net-proton dynamical kurtosis observed by STAR motivates further investigation into whether this sign change is a critical point signal or arises from other non-critical effects such as experimental cuts, centrality bin width corrections, or phase space windows.
