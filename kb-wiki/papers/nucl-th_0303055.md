# New J/$ψ$ suppression data and the comovers interaction model

> New data on the $J/ψ$ suppression both in proton-nucleus and in lead-lead interactions have been presented recently by the NA50 collaboration. We show that these data, together with the final ones on sulfur-uranium interactions, can be described in the framework of the comovers interaction model with a unique set of three parameters : the nuclear absorption cross-section, the comovers interaction cross-section and a single (rescaled) absolute normalization. Expectations for $J/ψ$ suppression at RHIC are also discussed.

# New J/ψ suppression data and the comovers interaction model

## Key claim

The new NA50 data on J/ψ suppression in proton-nucleus and lead-lead collisions, together with the final sulfur-uranium data, can be described within the comovers interaction model using a single set of three parameters (nuclear absorption cross-section, comovers interaction cross-section, and a rescaled absolute normalization), provided that the comovers cross-section is reduced from 1 mb to 0.65 mb compared to earlier analyses, which resolves a previous 30% mismatch between absolute normalizations in SU and PbPb systems.

## Method

The authors compute the survival probability of J/ψ mesons due to nuclear absorption and comovers interaction using the dual parton model (DPM) formalism. The nuclear absorption cross-section σabs = 4.5 mb is fixed from pA data alone, and the comovers interaction cross-section σco is determined from the centrality dependence of J/ψ suppression in PbPb collisions. The model folds impact-parameter-dependent cross-sections with a Gaussian fluctuation kernel to obtain distributions in transverse energy ET, using parameters q and a fitted to minimum-bias event samples.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear absorption cross-section: σabs = 4.5 mb (fixed from pA data, consistent with new NA50 value of 4.4 ± 0.5 mb)
- Comovers interaction cross-section: σco = 0.65 mb (reduced from 1 mb used in earlier analyses)
- Absolute normalization in PbPb: 47 (using GRVLO parton distributions) or 43 (using MRS 43 distributions)
- Absolute normalization in SU: 45, giving a 4% difference from PbPb normalization, consistent with the rescaling factor of 1.051 ± 0.026
- Parameters for ET-b correlation in PbPb (1998 data): q = 0.62 GeV, a = 0.60
- Parameters for ET-b correlation in SU: q = 0.69 GeV, a = 1.6
- Comover density in pp at mid-rapidity: Nf = 1.15 fm⁻²
- Beam energy for PbPb and SU: Ein = 158 GeV
- Contamination parameter for EZDC: α = 0.076

## Context

This work extends the comovers interaction model previously developed by Capella and collaborators (1997-2000) to describe J/ψ suppression in nuclear collisions. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the NA50 deconfining scenario, which posits a sudden onset of anomalous suppression at ET ~ 40 GeV, and the comovers scenario, which predicts a smooth suppression already present in SU collisions. The new NA50 data (2002) with lower peripheral PbPb points resolve a previous 30% normalization mismatch between SU and PbPb systems that had been identified by Capella and Sousa (2002). The model enables predictions for J/ψ suppression at RHIC, where the authors expect comparable suppression to SPS if nuclear absorption is small at mid-rapidity, and where the continuous fall-off from peripheral to central collisions would discriminate against a deconfining scenario with a flat region below threshold.
