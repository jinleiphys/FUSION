# PNJL model for adjoint fermions

> Recent work on QCD-like theories has shown that the addition of adjoint fermions obeying periodic boundary conditions to gauge theories on R^3 X S^1 can lead to a restoration of center symmetry and confinement for sufficiently small circumference L of S^1. At small L, perturbation theory may be used reliably to compute the effective potential for the Polyakov loop P in the compact direction. Periodic adjoint fermions act in opposition to the gauge fields, which by themselves would lead to a deconfined phase at small L. In order for the fermionic effects to dominate gauge field effects in the effective potential, the fermion mass must be sufficiently small. This indicates that chiral symmetry breaking effects are potentially important. We develop a Polyakov-Nambu-Jona Lasinio (PNJL) model which combines the known perturbative behavior of adjoint QCD models at small L with chiral symmetry breaking effects to produce an effective potential for the Polyakov loop P and the chiral order parameter psi-bar psi. A rich phase structure emerges from the effective potential. Our results are consistent with the recent lattice simulations of Cossu and D'Elia, which found no evidence for a direct connection between the small-L and large-L confining regions. Nevertheless, the two confined regions are connected indirectly if an extended field theory model with an irrelevant four-fermion interaction is considered. Thus the small-L and large-L regions are part of a single confined phase.

## Key claim

The small-\(L\) and large-\(L\) confined regions of \(SU(3)\) gauge theory with two flavors of periodic adjoint fermions are part of a single confined phase, but they are not directly connected unless an extended field theory model with an irrelevant four-fermion interaction is considered; the two regions are connected indirectly only for a narrow range of the four-fermion coupling \(\kappa\) between approximately 1.250 and the critical value \(\kappa_c \simeq 1.234\).

## Method

The authors construct a Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) effective potential for the Polyakov loop \(P\) and the chiral condensate \(\bar{\psi}\psi\) in \(SU(3)\) gauge theory with two flavors of Dirac fermions in the adjoint representation. The fermionic contribution includes a one-loop free energy expanded in modified Bessel functions, with periodic boundary conditions in the compact direction of length \(L\); the gluonic contribution uses a high-temperature expansion of the one-loop free energy with a phenomenological mass parameter \(M = 596\ \mathrm{MeV}\) tuned to reproduce the pure-gauge deconfinement temperature \(T_d \approx 270\ \mathrm{MeV}\). The effective potential is minimized numerically as a function of \(L^{-1}\) and the dimensionless coupling \(\kappa = g_S \Lambda^2\), where \(\Lambda\) is a three-momentum cutoff and \(g_S\) is the scalar-pseudoscalar four-fermion coupling.

## Key numbers

- Pure-gauge deconfinement temperature: \(T_d \approx 270\ \mathrm{MeV}\), used to set the gluonic mass parameter \(M = 596\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For adjoint fermions with antiperiodic boundary conditions, the ratio of chiral restoration temperature to deconfinement temperature is \(T_c / T_d \approx 7.8\) (from lattice simulations of Engels (2005)).
- For the periodic adjoint case with \(m_0 = 0\) and \(m(T=0)/\Lambda = 0.1\), the cutoff is \(\Lambda = 23.22\ \mathrm{GeV}\), giving constituent mass \(m(T=0) = 2.322\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(\kappa = 1.2653\); the critical coupling below which chiral symmetry is unbroken is \(\kappa_c = \pi^2/8 \simeq 1.234\).
- Chiral symmetry breaking persists up to \(L^{-1} = 10\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for the periodic adjoint case with the same parameters.
- The narrow range of \(\kappa\) where the large-\(L\) and small-\(L\) confined regions are smoothly connected lies between approximately \(1.250\) and \(\kappa_c \simeq 1.234\).

## Context

This work extends the PNJL approach, previously applied to fundamental fermions by Fukushima (2003) and others, to adjoint fermions with periodic boundary conditions on \(R^3 \times S^1\). It builds on the discovery by Unsal (2007) that periodic adjoint fermions can restore center symmetry and confinement at small \(L\), and on lattice simulations by Cossu and D'Elia (2009) that found no direct connection between the small-\(L\) and large-\(L\) confined regions. The model reproduces the large separation between deconfinement and chiral restoration temperatures seen in lattice studies of adjoint QCD by Karsch (1998) and Engels (2005). The results enable future lattice studies to search for the narrow coupling window where the two confined regions are connected, and clarify the role of chiral symmetry breaking in determining the phase structure of QCD-like theories with compactified dimensions.
