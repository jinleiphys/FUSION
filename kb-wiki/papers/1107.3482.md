# The low energy frontier: What is exciting about physics below the top RHIC energy

> These proceedings summarize my plenary talk at Quark Matter 2011 with a focus on the future perspectives of the low energy programs at RHIC, FAIR, NICA and CERN.

# The low energy frontier: What is exciting about physics below the top RHIC energy

## Key claim

The low energy regime (center-of-mass energies between 5 and 15 GeV per nucleon pair) at RHIC, FAIR, NICA, and CERN offers the best opportunity to locate the critical endpoint (CEP) and explore the onset of deconfinement in the QCD phase diagram, but the existence and location of the CEP remain ambiguous because different lattice QCD groups have produced conflicting predictions and recent studies do not provide evidence for the critical point below a baryochemical potential of 600 MeV.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental excitation functions (inverse slope of particle spectra, directed flow v1 slope, elliptic flow v2, non-flow contributions, and HBT eccentricities) from the NA49, STAR, and CERES collaborations across beam energies from 5 to 15 AGeV. It compares these data to predictions from lattice QCD susceptibilities (ratios of fourth-order to second-order baryon number susceptibilities) and to effective model calculations for fluctuations near the critical endpoint. The author also surveys the planned capabilities of the FAIR (SIS-100/SIS-300) and NICA facilities for future heavy ion programs.

## Key numbers

- The irregular structures in excitation functions appear between center-of-mass energies of sqrt(s_NN) = 5-15 GeV (5-15 AGeV).
- NICA will provide Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies between sqrt(s_NN) = 4-11 GeV with luminosities around 10^27 /cm^2 /s.
- FAIR with SIS-300 will allow heavy ion beams up to 35 AGeV beam energy.
- Recent lattice QCD studies by Endroede et al. found no evidence for the critical endpoint in the (T, mu_B) regime below a baryochemical potential of 600 MeV.
- The STAR data on the scaled proton number kurtosis (as a proxy for chi^B_4 / chi^B_2) confirm expectations from lattice QCD predictions.

## Context

This plenary talk at Quark Matter 2011 summarizes the experimental and theoretical status of the low energy heavy ion program, extending earlier irregularity observations reported by the NA49 collaboration (Marek, 2000s) and the STAR beam energy scan (BES) program. The paper contrasts the crossover transition at top RHIC energy (where lattice QCD predicts no phase transition) with the first-order phase transition and critical endpoint expected at higher baryon densities. It enables future searches for the CEP and Quarkyonic phase at FAIR (planned first beam 2017/2018) and NICA (physics start envisaged 2016), and calls for a unified theoretical framework combining lattice QCD, hydrodynamics, and transport simulations, building on prior hybrid approaches (Huovinen and Petrecky, 2000s) and the MADAI and TechQM theory initiatives.
