# Strongly intensive quantities for rapidity correlations of multiplicities

> Studies of the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter created in nuclear collisions are typically carried out using event-by-event fluctuations. Well-known way to disentangle statistical and dynamical fluctuations is to construct special observables named strongly intensive which are free from trivial volume fluctuations. Within the color string model behavior of the second-order strongly intensive quantity $Σ$ is completely determined by the two-particle correlation function from a single string and the string fusion mechanism. In this paper, we analyze third-order strongly intensive observable for forward-backward rapidity correlations and test its behavior within the PYTHIA8 model.

## Key claim

The paper analyzes a third-order strongly intensive observable $\Gamma[N_F,N_B]$ for forward-backward rapidity correlations of multiplicities, and tests its behavior within the PYTHIA8 model; the property of strong intensity is present up to $\Delta y < 1.8$, with possible breaking at larger $\Delta y$ due to string interactions deferred to future studies.

## Method

The author constructs the third-order strongly intensive quantity $\Gamma[N_F,N_B]$ from joint cumulants $P_{ij}$ of multiplicities in forward and backward rapidity intervals, following the formalism of Broniowski (2017). The observable is tested using the PYTHIA8.3/Angantyr event generator for $10^8$ inelastic p+p interactions, $10^7$ minimum-bias O+O collisions, and $10^6$ minimum-bias Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 900$ GeV, with charged particles selected in the transverse momentum range $0.3 < p_T < 3.0$ GeV/c. The behavior is studied as a function of the distance $\Delta y$ between interval centers for asymmetric rapidity windows of varying widths.

## Key numbers

- For p+p and minimum-bias O+O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 900$ GeV, $\Gamma[N_F,N_B]$ shows strong intensity property up to $\Delta y < 1.8$ (asymmetric intervals with backward window fixed at $y_B \in (-0.8, -0.75)$).
- In p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 900$ GeV, $\Gamma[N_F,N_B]$ increases with $\Delta y$ for forward interval widths $\delta y_F = 0.1, 0.2, 0.4$, showing only moderate dependence on interval width (backward window $\delta y_B = 0.05$).
- Baseline model predictions give $\Sigma[N_F,N_B] = \Delta[N_F,N_B] = \Gamma[N_F,N_B] = 1$ for independent particle production and for factorized Poisson distributions from a single string.

## Context

This work extends the study of strongly intensive observables from second-order quantities $\Sigma$ and $\Delta$, introduced by Andronov (2015) for forward-backward correlations, to third order via $\Gamma$ as formulated by Broniowski (2017). The second-order quantity $\Sigma$ was previously understood within the color string model to be determined by the two-particle correlation function from a single string and the string fusion mechanism (Andronov 2025, Vechernin 2023). The results enable future investigations of string-string interaction effects at larger rapidity separations and provide a volume-fluctuation-free observable for searching critical phenomena in heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as the LHC and RHIC.
