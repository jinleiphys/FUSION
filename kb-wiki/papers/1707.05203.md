# Anderson-Bogoliubov phonon in inner crust of neutron stars: Dipole excitation in spherical Wigner-Seitz cell

> Background: The Anderson-Bogoliubov (AB) phonon, called also the superfluid phonon, has attracted attentions since it may influence the thermal conductivity and other properties of inner crust of neutron stars. However, there are limited number of microscopic studies of the AB phonon where the presence of clusters is explicitly taken into account.\\ Purpose: We intend to clarify how the presence of clusters affects the AB phonon in order to obtain microscopic information relevant to the coupling between the AB phonon and the lattice phonon. \\ Methods: The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation formulated in a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell are adopted to describe neutron superfluidity and associated collective excitations. We perform systematic numerical calculations for dipole excitation by varying the neutron chemical potential and the number of protons in a cell. \\ Results:The model predicts systematic emergence of the dipole AB phonon mode, which however exhibits strong suppression of phonon amplitude inside the cluster. We find also that the phonon amplitude around the cluster surface varies as the neutron density. At higher neutron densities the AB phonon mode exhibits behaviour similar to the pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich nuclei.\\ Conclusions: The dipole AB phonon mode does not penetrate into the clusters. This suggests that the coupling between the AB phonon and the lattice phonon may be weak.

## Key claim

The dipole Anderson-Bogoliubov (AB) phonon mode systematically emerges in the inner crust of neutron stars, but it exhibits strong suppression of phonon amplitude inside the cluster, suggesting that the coupling between the AB phonon and the lattice phonon may be weak.

## Method

The authors employed the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) formulated in a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell to describe neutron superfluidity and associated collective excitations. They performed systematic numerical calculations for dipole excitations by varying the neutron chemical potential from 1 to 6 MeV and the proton number Z from 20 to 50, using the Skyrme parameter set SLy4 and a density-dependent delta interaction for the pairing channel.

## Key numbers

- The AB phonon mode appears at excitation energy E = 1.71 MeV for the Z = 28 system with neutron chemical potential λ_n = 2.0 MeV
- The neutron dipole strength B(D_n) of the AB phonon mode is enhanced by a factor of about ten compared to unperturbed two-quasi-neutron configurations
- The pair-addition and removal strengths B(P_add) and B(P_rm) show enhancement of order 10^2 relative to unperturbed strengths
- The neutron pair gap Δ_ext reaches a maximum value of approximately 2.4 MeV at density log(ρ_ext/ρ_0) ~ -1.0, corresponding to λ_n ~ 6 MeV
- The neutron surface radius increases from 5.18 fm at λ_n = 1.0 MeV to 6.05 fm at λ_n = 6.0 MeV for Z = 28
- The neutron skin thickness R_n - R_p grows from 0.35 fm at λ_n = 1.0 MeV to 0.57 fm at λ_n = 6.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends microscopic studies of the AB phonon (superfluid phonon) in neutron star crusts beyond macroscopic approaches based on effective field theory or superfluid hydrodynamics used by Aguilera (2009), Pethick (2010), and Page and Reddy (2012). It builds on the HFB+QRPA methodology developed by Matsuo (2001, 2005) for isolated neutron-rich nuclei, and complements the work of Khan (2005) who suggested a possible AB phonon mode for low-energy quadrupole excitation. The results provide microscopic information relevant to understanding the coupling between the AB phonon and lattice phonon, which is needed for modeling thermal conductivity and quasi-periodic oscillations in neutron stars.
