# What is Double Parton Scattering?

> Processes such as double Drell-Yan and same-sign WW production have contributions from double parton scattering, which are not well-defined because of a delta(z_\perp=0) singularity that is generated by QCD evolution. We study the single and double parton contributions to these processes, and show how to handle the singularity using factorization and operator renormalization. We compute the QCD evolution of double parton distribution functions (PDFs) due to mixing with single PDFs. The modified evolution of dPDFs at z_\perp=0, including generalized dPDFs for the non-forward case, is given in the appendix. We include a brief discussion of the experimental interpretation of dPDFs and how they can probe flavor, spin and color correlations of partons in hadrons.

# What is Double Parton Scattering?

## Key claim

Double parton scattering (DPS) contributions to processes like double Drell-Yan and same-sign WW production are not well-defined as independent physical processes because a delta-function singularity at zero transverse separation (z_perp = 0) is generated by QCD evolution; however, this singularity is resolved by recognizing that DPS is only one contribution to a physical cross section, and the problematic term vanishes in dimensional regularization, leading to a consistent factorization theorem.

## Method

The authors performed a QCD factorization analysis of double Drell-Yan production, studying both single parton scattering (SPS) and double parton scattering (DPS) contributions and their mixing under renormalization group evolution. They computed the evolution of double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) due to mixing with single PDFs, using operator renormalization to handle the delta-function singularity at z_perp = 0. The analysis included diagrammatic evaluation of mixing contributions, dimensional regularization of divergent integrals, and derivation of anomalous dimensions for generalized dPDFs at z_perp = 0.

## Key numbers

- The DPS cross section is of order Lambda_QCD^2 * sigma_0^2 and is Lambda_QCD^2/Q^2 suppressed relative to SPS, where Lambda_QCD is the QCD scale and Q is the hard scale
- The naive estimate for the single PDF contribution to same-sign WW production is approximately 10^{-9} sigma_0 (with alpha/(4 pi sin^2 theta_W) factors) or 10^{-7} sigma_0 (with only a factor of pi per alpha)
- The dPDF contribution to same-sign WW production is of order Lambda_QCD^2 * sigma_0^2, estimated at 10^{-8} to 10^{-9} sigma_0
- For the uu quark pair in a proton in the constituent quark model, the probability of parallel spins is 2/3 and antiparallel spins is 1/3
- The color factor C_1 = (N^2 - 1)/(4N^2) = 2/9 and C_d = (N^2 - 4)/N = 5/3 appear in the evolution kernel for generalized dPDFs

## Context

This work extends the analysis of double parton scattering by Diehl (2011) and Diehl (2011) who identified the delta-function singularity problem, and by Kirschner (1979) and Shelest (1982) who studied mixing of single and double PDFs. The authors resolve the singularity using dimensional regularization, consistent with the approach of Gaunt and Stirling (2011) who studied the singularity in gg to ZZ production and found it is not logarithmically enhanced. The results enable well-defined calculations of DPS contributions to processes like same-sign WW production, which the authors identify as a clean channel for studying double parton scattering because contamination from other production mechanisms is small.
