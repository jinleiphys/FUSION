# A Statistical Analysis for the Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay Matrix element of 48Ca

> Neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) nuclear matrix elements (NME) are the object of many theoretical calculation methods, and are very important for analysis and guidance of a large number of experimental efforts. However, there are large discrepancies between the NME values provided by different methods. In this paper we propose a statistical analysis of the $^{48}$Ca $0νββ$ NME using the interacting shell model, emphasizing the range of the NME probable values and its correlations with observables that can be obtained from the existing nuclear data. Based on this statistical analysis with three independent effective Hamiltonians we propose a common probability distribution function for the $0νββ$ NME, which has a range of (0.45 - 0.95) at 90\% confidence level of, and a mean value of 0.68.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a common probability distribution function for the $^{48}$Ca neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) based on a statistical analysis using three independent shell-model effective Hamiltonians, finding a mean value of 0.68 and a range of (0.45 - 0.95) at 90% confidence level, but this result depends on a "democratic" equal-weighting approach for the three Hamiltonians because experimental occupation probability data that could refine the weighting are not yet available.

## Method

The authors performed interacting shell-model calculations in the fp-shell model space (0f7/2, 0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2 orbitals) starting from three effective Hamiltonians (FPD6, GXPF1A, KB3G). They generated 20,000 random Hamiltonians per starting interaction by adding random contributions of plus or minus 10% to the two-body matrix elements while keeping single-particle energies fixed, then calculated the 0νββ NME and 23 other observables including 2νββ NME, Gamow-Teller strengths, excitation energies, B(E2) values, and occupation probabilities. The resulting distributions were combined using Gram-Charlier A series expansions to produce a weighted probability distribution function.

## Key numbers

- The proposed common probability distribution function for the $^{48}$Ca 0νββ NME has a mean value of 0.68 and a range of (0.45 - 0.95) at 90% confidence level
- Pearson correlation coefficient R between 0νββ NME and 2νββ NME is approximately 0.90 for the FPD6 Hamiltonian
- Pearson correlation coefficient R between 0νββ NME and the 2+ state energy in $^{48}$Ti is approximately 0.80 for FPD6
- Pearson correlation coefficient R between 0νββ NME and the 4+ state energy in $^{48}$Ti is approximately 0.78 for FPD6
- Pearson correlation coefficient R between 0νββ NME and the 6+ state energy in $^{48}$Ti is approximately 0.75 for FPD6
- Pearson correlation coefficient R between 0νββ NME and neutron occupation probability of the 0f5/2 orbital in $^{48}$Ca is approximately 0.62 for FPD6
- Pearson correlation coefficient R between 0νββ NME and neutron occupation probability of the 0f7/2 orbital in $^{48}$Ca is approximately -0.58 for FPD6
- Chi-squared values for the three starting Hamiltonians: 7.9 for FPD6, 4.8 for GXPF1A, and 7.3 for KB3G

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach to 0νββ NME uncertainties previously applied to other observables (PhysRevC.101.054308, 2020) and provides a shell-model counterpart to ab-initio calculations of the $^{48}$Ca 0νββ NME by Yao (2020), Novario (2021), and Belley (2021). The strong correlation found between 0νββ and 2νββ NMEs supports the common practice in QRPA calculations of calibrating the particle-particle interaction parameter g_pp to reproduce the experimental 2νββ NME, while the correlations with excitation energies and occupation probabilities suggest that future experimental measurements of occupation probabilities in $^{48}$Ti and $^{48}$Ca could help reduce the 0νββ NME uncertainty.
