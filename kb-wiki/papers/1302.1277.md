# Non-spherical collapse in AdS and Early Thermalization in RHIC

> In the flat space, non-spherical shells collapse to give globular cluster after many oscillations. We show that in anti de sitter space, they form black holes in one dynamical time. We propose that this is the mechanism of early thermalization in strong quark-gluon plasma in gravity dual. This is traced back to the a remarkable property of AdS : the period in radial motion is amplitude independent in spite of the NON-linearity of the equation of motion. We investigate the interaction effect numerically and observe the same qualitative behavior for the attractive forces. For repulsive interactions, particles halt at a small but finite radius for long time due to the specific structure of the bulk AdS propagator. It helps hair creation in the AdS black hole.

## Key claim

The paper claims that any non-spherical shell of dust particles in Anti-de Sitter (AdS) space will collapse to form a black hole in a single dynamical time, regardless of initial positions or masses, provided all particles start from rest. This synchronization effect is proposed as the gravity-dual mechanism for early thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC, but the claim is explicitly conditional on particles starting with zero initial radial velocity and on inter-particle interactions being negligible or attractive.

## Method

The authors treat a collapsing shell as a collection of non-interacting dust particles in global AdS₅ and in the Poincare patch, solving the geodesic equations analytically for radial motion. They then introduce inter-particle interactions via a scalar propagator derived from AdS/CFT, and perform numerical simulations of few-particle systems with both attractive and repulsive forces to test whether the synchronization effect survives.

## Key numbers

- Falling time from the boundary to the center in global AdS₅: \( T_{fall} = \frac{\pi}{2} \frac{R}{c} \), independent of initial radial position \( r_0 \).
- Period of radial oscillation in global AdS₅: \( 2\pi \) (in units where \( R=1, c=1 \)), independent of amplitude.
- For a particle in the Poincare patch with initial height \( r_0 = \epsilon \), the radial position evolves as \( r = \frac{\epsilon}{\sqrt{1 + (\epsilon t)^2}} \), with large-time behavior \( r \sim 1/t \) independent of \( \epsilon \).
- For a particle with horizontal velocity \( V = p/E \), the radial position in the Poincare patch is \( r = \frac{\epsilon (1-V^2)}{\sqrt{1 + (\epsilon (1-V^2) t)^2}} \), with the same \( 1/t \) large-time behavior.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Shuryak (2005) that radial motion in the Poincare patch is harmonic in proper time, but improves upon it by demonstrating exact synchronization in global coordinate time rather than proper time, and by removing the need for an IR cutoff. It contrasts with studies of scalar field collapse in AdS by Bizon (2011) and others, which found that black hole formation requires repeated reflections from the boundary due to wave-like bounces; the authors attribute those bounces to the effective repulsion from the uncertainty principle in coherent scalar field configurations, which is absent for dust particles. The results enable a gravity-dual explanation of the early thermalization puzzle at RHIC and suggest a mechanism for hair formation in AdS black holes via repulsive interactions.
