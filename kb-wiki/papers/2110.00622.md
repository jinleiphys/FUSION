# Strangeness neutral equation of state with a critical point

> We formulate a family of equations of state for Quantum Chromodynamics that exhibit critical features and obey the charge conservation conditions present in heavy-ion collisions (HICs). This construction utilizes the first-principle Lattice QCD (LQCD) results up to $\mathcal{O}(μ_B^4)$ by matching the Taylor coefficients at each order. The criticality of the equation of state (EoS) is implemented based on the well-established principle of universal scaling behavior, where QCD belongs to the 3D Ising Model universality class. The critical point can be placed in a range of temperature and baryonic chemical potential relevant for the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC. Furthermore, the strength of the critical features can be varied as well. This flexibility is embedded in four free parameters, that could potentially be constrained by the experimental data. We will discuss the features and versatility of our EoS, as well as present the relevant thermodynamic quantities which are important for hydrodynamical simulations of HICs.

# Strangeness neutral equation of state with a critical point

## Key claim
The authors construct a family of QCD equations of state that include a critical point in the 3D Ising model universality class while matching lattice QCD results up to O(μ_B^4), but they explicitly state that their chosen critical point parameters (T_c=143.2 MeV, μ_B,c=350 MeV) are for illustrative purposes only and do not constitute a prediction for the actual location of the QCD critical point.

## Method
The authors map the 3D Ising model onto the QCD phase diagram using a linear transformation with four free parameters, placing the critical point along the chiral phase transition line. They match the Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure order by order to lattice QCD results at μ_B=0, separating the pressure into critical (Ising) and non-critical (background) components. The full pressure is reconstructed as the sum of these components and then smoothly merged with an ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) pressure at low temperatures using a hyperbolic tangent switching function with an overlap region of ΔT=17 MeV. Thermodynamic quantities and the correlation length are then calculated as derivatives of the final pressure.

## Key numbers
- Critical point placed at {T_c=143.2 MeV, μ_B,c=350 MeV} (illustrative, not predictive)
- Angular parameters: α_1=3.85°, α_2=93.85°
- Scaling parameters: ω=1, ρ=2
- 3D Ising model critical exponents: β=0.326, δ=4.8, ν=0.63
- Normalization constants: M_0=0.605, h_0=0.364
- Correlation length scaling constant f set to 1 fm
- Merging overlap region ΔT=17 MeV
- Lattice QCD matching performed up to O(μ_B^4)

## Context
This work extends the BEST collaboration equation of state framework (Parotto 2018) by implementing strangeness neutrality and fixed baryon-number-to-electric-charge ratio conditions relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. The approach enables hydrodynamic simulations of the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC and can be coupled to the SMASH hadronic afterburner. The four free parameters in the mapping can potentially be constrained by experimental data from BES-II, and the EoS has already been used for studies of out-of-equilibrium critical phenomena (Dore 2020), kurtosis sign (Mroczek 2020), and transport coefficients (McLaughlin 2021).
