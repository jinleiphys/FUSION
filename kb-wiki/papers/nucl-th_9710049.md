# Masses and Deformations of Neutron-Rich Nuclei

> We have calculated the masses, deformations, and other properties of 8979 nuclei ranging from oxygen-16 to Z = 136, A = 339 and extending from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line on the basis of the 1992 version of the finite-range droplet model. The predicted quantities include the ground-state mass, deformation, microscopic correction, odd-proton and odd-neutron spins and parities, proton and neutron pairing gaps, binding energy, one- and two-neutron separation energies, quantities related to beta-delayed one- and two-neutron emission probabilities, beta-decay energy release and half-life with respect to Gamow-Teller decay, one- and two-proton separation energies, and alpha-decay energy release and half-life. For 1654 nuclei heavier than oxygen-16 whose masses were known experimentally in 1989 and which were included in the adjustment of model constants, the theoretical error is 0.669 MeV. For 371 additional nuclei heavier than oxygen-16 whose masses have been measured between 1989 and 1996 and which were not used in the adjustment of the model constants, the theoretical error is 0.570 MeV.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the 1992 version of the finite-range droplet model predicts nuclear masses with a theoretical error of 0.570 MeV for 371 nuclei measured between 1989 and 1996 that were not used in the original model adjustment, suggesting the model extrapolates reliably to new regions. However, this error estimate includes seven proton-rich superheavy nuclei whose masses are estimated indirectly from alpha-decay chains, with an assigned mass error of 0.5 MeV each, which could introduce systematic uncertainty.

## Method
The authors used the macroscopic-microscopic finite-range droplet model (1992 version) to calculate ground-state masses, deformations, and other properties for 8979 nuclei from oxygen-16 to Z=136, A=339. The macroscopic part uses a droplet model with finite-range corrections, while microscopic shell and pairing corrections are calculated from a folded-Yukawa single-particle potential using Strutinsky's method. Model constants were adjusted to 28 fission-barrier heights and 1654 experimentally known masses from 1989, with nuclear shapes minimized over deformation parameters epsilon_2, epsilon_4, epsilon_6, and epsilon_3.

## Key numbers
- 8979 nuclei calculated, ranging from oxygen-16 to Z=136, A=339, from proton drip line to neutron drip line
- For 1654 nuclei heavier than oxygen-16 with masses known in 1989 (used in model adjustment), theoretical error is 0.669 MeV
- For 371 additional nuclei heavier than oxygen-16 measured between 1989 and 1996 (not used in adjustment), theoretical error is 0.570 MeV
- Maximum reduction in energy from independent variation of epsilon_6 deformation is 1.28 MeV for fermium-252
- Maximum reduction in energy from epsilon_3 (octupole) deformation is 1.29 MeV for the neutron-rich nucleus gadolinium-194
- For even-even nuclei near the valley of beta-stability, maximum reduction from epsilon_3 is 1.20 MeV for radium-222

## Context
This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic approach of Strutinsky (1967) and earlier droplet model calculations by the same authors, providing a comprehensive mass table for nuclei far from stability. The 1992 finite-range droplet model improves upon previous versions by including epsilon_6 and epsilon_3 shape degrees of freedom and adjusting constants to a larger set of experimental data. The calculated masses, deformations, and decay properties enable studies of neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis and superheavy element research, and the tabulated results are publicly available online.
