# Heavy quark production in $pA$ collisions: the double parton scattering contribution

> In this paper we estimate the double parton scattering (DPS) contribution for the heavy quark production in $pA$ collisions at the LHC. The cross sections for the charm and bottom production are estimated using the dipole approach and taking into account the saturation effects, which are important for high energies and for the scattering with a large nucleus. We compare the DPS contribution with the single parton scattering one and demonstrate that in the case of charm production both are similar in the kinematical range probed by the LHC. Predictions for the rapidity range analysed by the LHCb Collaboration are also presented. Our results indicate that the study of the DPS contribution for the heavy quark production in $pPb$ collisions at the LHC is feasible and can be useful to probe the main assumptions of the approach.

# Heavy quark production in $pA$ collisions: the double parton scattering contribution

## Key claim

The double parton scattering (DPS) contribution to charm production in $pPb$ collisions at LHC energies is comparable to the single parton scattering (SPS) contribution, but only when using the dipole approach with saturation effects included; the claim is conditional on the specific choice of heavy quark masses, factorization scales, and the GBW saturation model, with an estimated theoretical uncertainty of approximately 40% for SPS and 90% for DPS predictions.

## Method

The authors calculate the DPS cross section for heavy quark production in proton-nucleus collisions using the "pocket formula" factorization, which expresses the DPS cross section as the product of two SPS cross sections divided by an effective normalization cross section $\sigma_{eff}$. The SPS cross sections for charm and bottom production are computed using the color dipole approach, where the gluon-nucleon interaction is described by the GBW saturation model, and the nuclear dependence is treated via the Glauber-Gribov formalism with a 3-parameter Fermi nuclear density distribution. The DPS cross section for $pA$ collisions includes two contributions: interactions where both gluons from the proton scatter with the same nucleon in the nucleus, and interactions where they scatter with different nucleons, with the latter enhanced by a nuclear geometry factor $F_{pA}$.

## Key numbers

- For $pPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, the SPS charm production cross section is 994 mb and the DPS $c\bar{c}c\bar{c}$ cross section is 602 mb (central predictions, Table 1)
- For $pPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, the SPS bottom production cross section is 55 mb and the DPS $b\bar{b}b\bar{b}$ cross section is 1.5 mb (central predictions, Table 1)
- The effective cross section for $pPb$ collisions is $\sigma_{eff,\,pPb} = 23.8$ $\mu$b, compared to $\sigma_{eff,\,pp} = 15$ mb for proton-proton collisions
- The DPS contribution for charm production equals the SPS contribution at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10.4$ TeV for $pPb$ collisions ($A=208$) and at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 19.6$ TeV for $pCa$ collisions ($A=40$)
- The ratio $\sigma^{DPS}(b\bar{b}c\bar{c}) / \sigma^{SPS}(b\bar{b})$ is of order unity in $pPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV for the full LHC rapidity range, but all ratios are smaller than 0.3 when restricted to the LHCb rapidity range ($2 < y < 4.5$)
- The nuclear geometry factor $F_{pA} = 28.1$ mb$^{-1}$ for $A=208$ (lead) and $F_{pA} = 3.0$ mb$^{-1}$ for $A=40$ (calcium)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of DPS heavy quark production in $pp$ collisions (Cazaroto, Goncalves, Navarra, 2015) to $pA$ collisions, following the formalism developed by Treleani and collaborators for DPS in nuclear collisions. The results are compared with NNLO collinear calculations by David et al. (2015) that used the EPS09-NLO nuclear parton distribution parametrization but did not include saturation effects. The study demonstrates that $pPb$ collisions at the LHC provide a significantly enhanced environment for studying DPS compared to $pp$ collisions, with the DPS cross section enhanced by a factor of approximately $3A$ ($\approx 600$ for lead) relative to $pp$ collisions, which can help constrain the value of $\sigma_{eff,\,pp}$ and test the assumptions of the DPS factorization approach.
