# Consistent hydrodynamic theory of chiral electrons in Weyl semimetals

> The complete set of Maxwell's and hydrodynamic equations for the chiral electrons in Weyl semimetals is presented. The formulation of the Euler equation takes into account the explicit breaking of the Galilean invariance by the ion lattice. It is shown that the Chern-Simons (or Bardeen-Zumino) contributions should be added to the electric current and charge densities in Maxwell's equations that provide the information on the separation of Weyl nodes in energy and momentum. On the other hand, these topological contributions do not directly affect the Euler equation and the energy conservation relation for the electron fluid. By making use of the proposed consistent hydrodynamic framework, we show that the Chern-Simons contributions strongly modify the dispersion relations of collective modes in Weyl semimetals. This is reflected, in particular, in the existence of distinctive anomalous Hall waves, which are sustained by the local anomalous Hall currents.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete set of consistent hydrodynamic and Maxwell equations for chiral electrons in Weyl semimetals, explicitly showing that the topological Chern-Simons (Bardeen-Zumino) contributions to the electric current and charge densities must be added to Maxwell's equations to account for the separation of Weyl nodes in energy and momentum, but that these topological terms do not directly affect the Euler equation or the energy conservation relation for the electron fluid.

## Method

The authors derived the Euler equation and energy conservation relation by calculating the corresponding moments of the Boltzmann equation from chiral kinetic theory (CKT) in the relaxation time approximation, using a local equilibrium distribution function that includes fluid velocity, vorticity, and Berry curvature effects. They then added the topological Chern-Simons contributions to the electric charge and current densities, which were derived separately using a two-band model of a Weyl semimetal (following Landsteiner (2014) and Gorbar et al. (2017)), and linearized the full set of hydrodynamic and Maxwell equations to study collective mode dispersions.

## Key numbers

- The matter part of the electric current density includes a chiral magnetic effect (CME) term proportional to \(e^2\mu_5 \mathbf{B}/(2\pi^2\hbar^2 c)\) and a chiral vortical effect (CVE) term proportional to \(-e\mu\mu_5 \bm{\omega}/(\pi^2 v_F^2 \hbar^2)\) after including the magnetization current.
- The Chern-Simons charge density is \(\rho_{\text{CS}} = -e^3 (\mathbf{b}\cdot\mathbf{B})/(2\pi^2\hbar^2 c^2)\), where \(\mathbf{b}\) is the momentum separation between Weyl nodes.
- The Chern-Simons current density is \(\mathbf{J}_{\text{CS}} = -e^3 b_0 \mathbf{B}/(2\pi^2\hbar^2 c) + e^3 [\mathbf{b}\times\mathbf{E}]/(2\pi^2\hbar^2 c)\), where \(b_0\) is the energy separation between Weyl nodes.
- The magneto-vortical correction to the charge density proportional to \(\mathbf{B}\cdot\bm{\omega}\) is found to be 6 times smaller than in Hattori and Yin (2016).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic theory of chiral electrons in Weyl semimetals by providing a consistent framework that incorporates both the "matter" response from chiral kinetic theory and the topological Chern-Simons contributions from filled states deep below the Fermi level, which were previously treated separately. The results enable the study of collective modes such as anomalous Hall waves, which are sustained by local anomalous Hall currents, and provide a foundation for future work on magnetohydrodynamic effects in Weyl semimetals, building on prior approaches by Landsteiner (2014) and Gorbar et al. (2017).
