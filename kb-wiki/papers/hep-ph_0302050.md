# Quarkonium Formation from Uncorrelated Quark-Antiquark Pairs

> The goal of this section is to assess the possibility that quarkonium production rates may be enhanced in nucleus-nucleus interactions at the LHC relative to that predicted by extrapolation of processes thought to be dominant at lower energy. This enhancement could follow from the effects of incoherent recombination mechanisms involving uncorrelated pairs of heavy quarks and antiquarks which result from multiple pair production. Two different approaches have been considered: statistical hadronization and kinetic formation. Updated predictions relevant to Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC are given.

# Quarkonium Formation from Uncorrelated Quark-Antiquark Pairs

## Key claim
Quarkonium production rates in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC may be enhanced relative to extrapolations from lower-energy processes, provided that incoherent recombination mechanisms involving uncorrelated heavy quark-antiquark pairs from multiple pair production become active. The enhancement is conditional on the existence of a deconfined medium or a statistical hadronization scenario where charm quarks can combine with any available anticharm quark, not only their original partner.

## Method
The paper evaluates two distinct models for quarkonium formation from uncorrelated charm quarks: (1) a statistical hadronization model, where charm quarks are distributed into hadrons according to thermal equilibrium parameters with a charm enhancement factor γ_c determined by flavor conservation; and (2) a kinetic formation model, where J/ψ states form directly in a deconfined medium via the inverse of gluon-induced breakup reactions, described by a rate equation with formation and dissociation terms. Both models are applied to Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, with centrality dependence calculated as a function of the number of participant nucleons N_part and initial charm pair multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- For central collisions (b=0) with dN_ccbar/dy = 25 (corresponding to N_ccbar ≈ 200), the statistical hadronization model predicts dN_J/ψ/dy = 0.625, giving a J/ψ to charm ratio of 0.025.
- The expected hadronic production ratio for J/ψ relative to charm is 0.01, so the statistical hadronization efficiency at LHC is larger than the baseline production ratio.
- For the kinetic formation model with N_ccbar(0) = 200 and thermal charm momentum distributions, N_J/ψ = 52.7 for central collisions; with Δy = 4 distributions, N_J/ψ = 17.5.
- For the kinetic model with leading-order pQCD charm distributions and N_ccbar(0) = 200, N_J/ψ = 4.0 for central collisions.
- The statistical hadronization model uses thermal parameters T = 170 MeV and μ_B ≈ 0, with a thermal density ratio of approximately 0.5.
- The centrality dependence of N_ccbar is parameterized as proportional to N_part^(4/3), while N_ch scales with exponent ≈ 1.2 based on RHIC data from Bazilevsky (2002) and Kharzeev (2001).

## Context
This work extends the original Matsui-Satz (1986) proposal that quarkonium suppression signals color deconfinement, by identifying a "loophole" where copious charm production at LHC energies allows recombination to enhance J/ψ yields. The statistical hadronization model builds on the approach of Braun-Munzinger (2000, 2001) for light hadron abundances, while the kinetic model develops earlier work by Thews (2000, 2001) on formation in deconfined media. The results enable experimental searches for the quadratic dependence of J/ψ on charm multiplicity as a signature of recombination, requiring simultaneous measurement of open charm to distinguish between statistical hadronization and kinetic formation mechanisms.
