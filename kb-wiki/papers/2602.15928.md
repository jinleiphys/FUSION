# A compendium of cold-nuclear matter baseline predictions in light-ion collisions

> The recent light-ion collision programme at RHIC and the LHC provides a unique opportunity to investigate the onset of quark-gluon plasma formation and parton energy loss in small systems. A quantitative interpretation of emerging jet quenching measurements requires precise control over cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects, which modify hard-process cross sections independently of any hot-medium dynamics. In this work, we present a comprehensive set of perturbative QCD baseline calculations for nuclear modification factors ($R_{AA}$) in proton-oxygen (pO), oxygen-oxygen (OO) and neon-neon (NeNe) collisions at LHC energies. The study includes charged hadron, neutral pion, prompt photon, and electroweak-boson production computed at next-to-leading order using a broad set of recent nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs). We demonstrate that CNM effects alone can induce sizeable suppressions in light-ion systems, with large associated nPDF uncertainties that currently limit the quantitative extraction of parton energy loss. To address this limitation, we explore a range of multi-cross-section ratios in which CNM effects and their uncertainties largely cancel. In particular, ratios of neutral pion $R_{OO}$ to prompt photon $R_{OO}$ or charged hadron $R_{OO}$ to $R_{pO}^2$ provide theoretically robust observables with substantially reduced nPDF uncertainties, thereby enhancing sensitivity to possible energy-loss signatures.

## Key claim

Cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects alone can induce sizeable suppressions in light-ion systems, but the large associated nPDF uncertainties currently limit the quantitative extraction of parton energy loss; multi-cross-section ratios such as neutral pion R_OO to prompt photon R_OO or charged hadron R_OO to R_pO^2 can cancel these uncertainties and enhance sensitivity to possible energy-loss signatures.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear modification factors (R_AA) for charged hadrons, neutral pions, prompt photons, and electroweak bosons at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD using the INCNLOv1.4 and MCFM codes. They employed four recent nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) sets (EPPS21, nCTEQ15HQ, nNNPDF30, TUJU21) for oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.36 TeV, proton-oxygen (pO) collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=9.62 TeV, and neon-neon (NeNe) collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.36 TeV, with hadrons required at mid-rapidity (|y*|<0.8) and scales set to the hadron transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron R_pO shows suppression of up to 10% at p_T ~ 6 GeV/c for EPPS21, while R_OO shows suppression of up to 15% at the same p_T for EPPS21
- R_NeNe shows at most 1% increased suppression of the central value at low p_T compared to R_OO
- Gluon shadowing in oxygen at x~10^-4 and Q^2=100 GeV^2 is about 20% for EPPS21, but less than 10% for the other three nPDF analyses
- Scale uncertainties for pp cross-section ratios at different centre-of-mass energies reach up to 10% at p_T ~ 4 GeV/c, reducing to less than 5% at high p_T
- Neutral pion nuclear modification factors agree within 1% with those of charged hadron production

## Context

This work extends previous pQCD baseline calculations for light-ion collisions by Huss (2020), Brewer (2021), Belmont (2023), Gebhard (2024), Mazeliauskas (2025), and Faraday (2025) by using a comprehensive set of recent nPDFs and a wide selection of hadronic and electroweak observables. The calculations provide no-quenching baselines to support ongoing experimental investigations of energy loss in light-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and the multi-cross-section ratios offer theoretically robust observables with reduced nPDF uncertainties that can enable more precise searches for jet quenching signatures in small systems.
