# Giant Dipole Resonance as a Fingerprint of $α$ Clustering Configurations in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O

> It is studied how the $α$ cluster degrees of freedom, such as $α$ clustering configurations close to the $α$ decay threshold in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, including the linear chain, triangle, square, kite, and tetrahedron, affect nuclear collective vibrations with a microscopic dynamical approach, which can describe properties of nuclear ground states well across the nuclide chart and reproduce the standard giant dipole resonance (GDR) of $^{16}$O quite nicely. It is found that the GDR spectrum is highly fragmented into several apparent peaks due to the $α$ structure. The different $α$ cluster configurations in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O have corresponding characteristic spectra of GDR. The number and centroid energies of peaks in the GDR spectra can be reasonably explained by the geometrical and dynamical symmetries of $α$ clustering configurations. Therefore, the GDR can be regarded as a very effective probe to diagnose the different $α$ cluster configurations in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) spectrum is highly fragmented into several characteristic peaks for different α cluster configurations in ¹²C and ¹⁶O, and the number and centroid energies of these peaks can be explained by the geometrical and dynamical symmetries of the α clustering configurations, making GDR a potential probe to diagnose α cluster structures in light nuclei.

## Method

The authors used an extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model with dynamical Gaussian wave packet widths and a Pauli potential to describe α clustering in ¹²C and ¹⁶O. They initialized the system in various α cluster configurations (linear chain, triangle, square, kite, tetrahedron) near the α decay threshold, then applied a dipole boost operator to excite the GDR and evolved the system for 300 fm/c. The dipole strength was obtained from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent dipole moment, yielding the photoabsorption cross section.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ground state of ¹⁶O has a binding energy of 7.82A MeV, close to the experimental value of 7.98A MeV.
- For all α cluster configurations considered, the GDR spectrum along the short axis shows a single peak around 30 MeV.
- For ¹⁶O linear-chain configuration, the main GDR peak along the long axis is at 12 MeV; for the square configuration, it is at 20 MeV.
- For ¹²C linear-chain configuration, the GDR along the long axis consists of two peaks around 16 and 20 MeV.
- For ¹²C triangle configuration, a strong peak appears around 25 MeV, with a lower peak at 20 MeV from the two-α mean field.
- For ¹⁶O kite configuration, three peaks are located at 12, 20, and 26 MeV.
- The smoothing parameter Γ = 2 MeV was used when displaying the dP/dE spectrum.

## Context

This work extends studies of α clustering in light self-conjugate nuclei, which have been investigated for decades using approaches such as fermionic molecular dynamics (Chernykh 2007), antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-En'yo 2012), and covariant density functional theory (Liu 2012). The authors show that GDR spectra can distinguish between different α cluster configurations (linear chain, triangle, square, kite, tetrahedron) that have been predicted for ¹²C and ¹⁶O by various theoretical approaches including the Brink wave function and the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke wave function (2001). The work enables experimental identification of α cluster configurations using monochromatic γ-ray beams at facilities such as HIγS, where the GDR built on excited cluster states could be measured via exclusive decay measurements.
