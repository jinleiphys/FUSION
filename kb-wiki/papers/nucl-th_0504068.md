# Direct Reactions with Exotic Nuclei

> We discuss recent work on Coulomb dissociation and an effective-range theory of low-lying electromagnetic strength of halo nuclei. We propose to study Coulomb dissociation of a halo nucleus bound by a zero-range potential as a homework problem. We study the transition from stripping to bound and unbound states and point out in this context that the Trojan-Horse method is a suitable tool to investigate subthreshold resonances.

## Key claim

The paper argues that direct reaction theory for exotic nuclei, particularly Coulomb dissociation and transfer reactions, can be understood through universal low-energy parameters (effective-range expansions) and that the transition from stripping to bound states and to unbound states is continuous, with the Trojan-Horse method providing a suitable tool to investigate subthreshold resonances. The authors hedge that while the foundations of direct reaction theory were laid decades ago, the new possibilities with rare isotope beams invite a revisit of the field, and that despite increasing precision of microscopic approaches, it will not be possible to predict binding energies of nuclei to about 100 keV.

## Method

The authors develop an effective-range theory for electromagnetic strength distributions in halo nuclei, applying it to the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be and comparing with experimental Coulomb dissociation data from GSI. They propose a homework problem involving a three-body system (neutron, core, and target) with a zero-range neutron-core interaction to study elastic scattering and breakup in the Coulomb field, for which analytical solutions exist in various limits (plane-wave, Coulomb-wave Born approximation, adiabatic approximation). They also analyze the continuous transition from stripping to bound and unbound states using a multichannel formalism with Green's functions and optical potentials, including a toy model with coupled square-well potentials and delta-function coupling.

## Key numbers

* For 11Be, the reduced transition probability was fitted using a cutoff radius of R = 2.78 fm and an inverse bound-state decay length of q = 0.1486 fm^-1, extracting an asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of C_0 = 0.724(8) fm^-1/2.
* The ANC for 11Be was converted to a spectroscopic factor of C^2 S = 0.704(15).
* The halo expansion parameter for 11Be is gamma = qR = 0.4132, which is less than 1.
* For 11Be, the scattering length parameters are c^{3/2}_1 = -0.41(86,-20) and c^{1/2}_1 = 2.77(13,-14).
* The electric dipole polarizability of the deuteron was measured as alpha_pol = (0.70 +/- 0.05) fm^3 by elastic scattering of deuterons on 208Pb at energies from 3.0 to 7.0 MeV (Lynch, 1982).
* The paper discusses deuteron breakup on 197Au at E_d = 12 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective-range approach for electromagnetic strength in halo nuclei, previously introduced by Typel and Baur (2004) for 11Be and applied by Nociforo et al. (2004) to 23O, and relates it to effective field theories used for the nucleon-nucleon system and halo nuclei (Bertulani, 2002). The paper builds on the textbook case of the deuteron as a halo nucleus and generalizes to arbitrary nucleon+core systems, with the square-well potential model providing analytical solutions that can be compared to more realistic Woods-Saxon models. The work enables future studies of the transition from bound to unbound states in exotic nuclei near the dripline, with implications for the astrophysical r process, and proposes a benchmark homework problem for testing numerical methods like the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method.
