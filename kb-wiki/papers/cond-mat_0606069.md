# Three-fermion problems in optical lattices

> We present exact results for the spectra of three fermionic atoms in a single well of an optical lattice. For the three lowest hyperfine states of Li6 atoms, we find a Borromean state across the region of the distinct pairwise Feshbach resonances. For K40 atoms, nearby Feshbach resonances are known for two of the pairs, and a bound three-body state develops towards the positive scattering-length side. In addition, we study the sensitivity of our results to atomic details. The predicted few-body phenomena can be realized in optical lattices in the limit of low tunneling.

## Key claim

The paper presents exact calculations showing that for three fermionic atoms in a single well of an optical lattice, a Borromean state (three-body bound with all pairs unbound) exists for Li-6 across the region of distinct pairwise Feshbach resonances, while for K-40 a bound three-body state only develops towards the positive scattering-length side. The authors hedge that the quantitative results for ground states are somewhat sensitive to atomic details (effective range effects), though the qualitative features are independent of such details and of the precise oscillator frequency.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body problem in a harmonic oscillator potential using the Bloch-Horowitz (BH) method, which diagonalizes an effective Hamiltonian in a truncated space of low-energy excitations to exactly reproduce the low-lying spectrum. They employed effective field-theory contact interactions regulated by separable cutoff functions, with the coupling determined from the scattering length. The two-body problem in the harmonic oscillator was solved exactly following Busch et al., and the BH method was applied using a Faddeev decomposition to construct the effective Hamiltonian, with calculations performed for Li-6 at a lattice frequency of 270 kHz and for K-40 at 100 kHz.

## Key numbers

- For Li-6, the Feshbach resonance positions are: B_12 = 83.41 mT, B_13 = 69.04 mT, and B_23 = 81.12 mT.
- For K-40, the Feshbach resonance parameters are: B_12 = 202.10 G with width Delta_12 = 7.8 G, B_13 = 224.21 G with width Delta_13 = 9.7 G, and background scattering length a_bg approximately 174 a_0.
- The two-body energy from the separable interaction is within 3% (or 7%) of the Busch et al. result for cutoff parameter lambda b = 100 and all scattering lengths except the tight-binding region 0 < a/b <= 1 (or 0.5).
- Typical well frequencies in optical lattices are nu ~ 100 kHz, giving oscillator length b ~ 1000 a_0, which is large compared to the range of atomic interactions R ~ 10 a_0.
- For K-40 at B_0 = 209.9 G, a_12(B_0) = 0, and the low-lying states follow E_3 - E_2,(13) - 3/2 hbar omega = 2n_2 + l_2 for positive parity.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of few-body systems in optical lattices from the two-body problem (measured by Kohl et al. 2005) and the three-boson problem (Stoll and Kohler 2005) to three-fermion systems with distinct pairwise Feshbach resonances. The results enable experimental realization of Borromean states and other three-fermion phenomena in optical lattices in the limit of low tunneling, and the authors note that the power-counting of three-body interactions in the corresponding effective field theory must change compared to free space, which may be important for a pionless EFT for nuclei in an oscillator basis.
