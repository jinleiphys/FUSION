# Signs of analyticity in fermion scattering

> We show that the signs of the leading irrelevant interactions for Dirac fermions are constrained by the analytic structure of the S-matrix. If Regge behavior obtains, negative signs indicate the presence of higher-spin bound states that spoil the convergence of the dispersion integrals and drive the corresponding operators relevant. For nucleon-nucleon scattering, the negativity of some of the low-energy interactions signals the presence of a spin-1 bound state: the deuteron. We connect the divergence of the dispersion integral to the "Sommerfeld enhancement" of the cross-section for low-energy scattering. We also discuss how this illuminates potential pitfalls in using perturbative methods to understand the dependence of the low-energy nuclear interaction on the masses of the light quarks. Finally, we suggest the possibility of applying similar reasoning to the current-current operators in the electroweak effective lagrangian, where no bound states spoil convergence of the dispersion relations.

## Key claim

The signs of the leading irrelevant four-fermion interactions for Dirac fermions are constrained to be non-negative by the analytic structure of the S-matrix, provided the relevant fixed-s dispersion integrals converge; a negative sign indicates the presence of higher-spin bound states that spoil convergence of the dispersion integrals and drive the corresponding operators relevant, as illustrated by the deuteron in nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors derive fixed-s dispersion relations for the five invariant amplitudes describing fermion-fermion scattering, using unitarity to show that the imaginary parts of these amplitudes in the t-channel are non-negative for s=0. They then relate the zero-energy values of these amplitudes (the coefficients of the effective four-fermion operators) to the dispersion integrals, establishing that these coefficients must be non-negative if the integrals converge. The analysis is extended to non-relativistic nucleon-nucleon scattering using the KSW effective Lagrangian, and the connection to Regge theory is made to explain how the deuteron bound state causes the dispersion integrals to require subtraction.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length for the \(^3S_1\) channel (triplet spin state) is given by \(a_{^3S_1} = \frac{m}{4\pi} (C_S + C_T)\), where \(C_S = -\tilde{c}_1 + \tilde{c}_2\) and \(C_T = -\tilde{c}_4 - \tilde{c}_5\) in the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme.
- The scattering length for the \(^1S_0\) channel (singlet spin state) is given by \(a_{^1S_0} = \frac{m}{4\pi} (C_S - 3C_T)\).
- The binding energy of an S-wave bound state from a delta function potential is \(E_B = 1 / (2 m a^2)\) for positive scattering length \(a\).
- The condition for the delta function interaction to become relevant is \(|\vec{p}| \gtrsim 1 / |a|\).
- The critical value of the dimensionless coupling \(x \equiv g m / \mu\) for the Yukawa potential to develop a bound state is approximately 1.35.

## Context

This work extends the program of deriving analyticity constraints on effective field theory coefficients, previously applied to bosonic theories by Adams, Arkani-Hamed, Dubovsky, Nicolis, and Rattazzi (2006) and to chiral lagrangians by several authors, to the case of fermion scattering. It connects to the earlier work of Nambu (1957) on the analytic structure of fermion-fermion scattering and to the dispersion relation approach of Cottingham and Vinh Mau for nucleon-nucleon scattering. The results enable a deeper understanding of why the deuteron bound state prevents naive perturbative calculations of the quark-mass dependence of nuclear forces, as attempted by Donoghue (2006) and Damour and Donoghue (2008), and suggest that similar reasoning could be applied to electroweak effective operators where no bound states spoil convergence.
