# Formation of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei at PANDA

> We study the formation of single- and double-$Λ$ hypernuclei in antiproton-induced reactions relevant for the forthcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR. We use the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model with relativistic mean-fields for the description of non-equilibrium dynamics and the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) for fragment formation. This combined approach describes the dynamical properties of strangeness and fragments in low energy $\bar{p}$-induced reactions fairly well. We then focus on the formation of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei in high energy $\bar{p}$-nucleus collisions on a primary target including the complementary $Ξ$-induced reactions to a secondary one, as proposed by the PANDA collaboration. Our results show that a copious production of double-$Λ$ hyperfragments is possible at PANDA. In particular, we provide first theoretical estimations on the double-$Λ$ production cross section, which strongly rises with decreasing energy of the secondary $Ξ$-beam.

## Key claim

The authors provide first theoretical estimations showing that copious production of double-Λ hyperfragments is possible at PANDA, but only if the secondary Ξ-beam energy is sufficiently low; the double-Λ production cross section strongly rises with decreasing energy of the secondary Ξ-beam.

## Method

The authors used the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model with relativistic mean-fields to describe the non-equilibrium dynamics of antiproton-induced reactions, then applied the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) for fragment formation. They simulated antiproton-nucleus collisions on a primary target at energies around the ΞΞ production threshold, and separately performed transport calculations with Ξ⁻-beams on a secondary target at incident kinetic energies of 0.08, 0.15, 0.3, and 0.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The threshold beam momentum for ΞΞ production is P_lab^thr = 2.62 GeV/c (corresponding to E_lab = 1.84 GeV incident energy).
- The antiproton-proton annihilation cross section at PANDA energies is σ_pp ≈ 60-80 mb, almost a factor of 2 higher than the corresponding pp cross section.
- The yield of light double-strange hyperfragments in primary-target reactions (p+Cu at 5 GeV) is around three orders of magnitude less than that of nuclear fragments.
- For Ξ⁻+⁶⁴Cu reactions, the double-Λ hypernuclei yield strongly decreases with increasing beam energy; the Ξ⁻p → ΛΛ elementary cross section drops sharply for momenta higher than 0.4 GeV/c.
- The Λ-hyperon in-medium optical potential at saturation density is around -38 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on single-Λ hypernuclei formation in heavy-ion collisions and high-energy proton-induced reactions (Gaitanos et al., 2010) to double-strange hypernuclei in antiproton-induced reactions. It provides the first theoretical estimates for the PANDA experiment at FAIR, which aims to produce double-strange hypernuclei through capture of primary cascade particles (Ξ) into secondary targets, as proposed by the PANDA Collaboration (2005, 2006, 2009). The results enable experimental planning for the two-target setup at PANDA and demonstrate that deceleration of emitted Ξ-particles before secondary-target interaction is crucial for achieving measurable double-Λ hypernuclei yields.
