# Medium Response and Jet-Hadron Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> High-energy heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider have evolved from qualitative understanding into precise extraction of the properties of the Quantum Chromodynamics medium at extremely high temperature. Jet quenching has offered unique insights into the transport properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) created in these energetic collisions. Apart from medium modification of jets, jet-induced medium excitation constitutes another crucial aspect of jet-QGP interaction, and is indispensable in understanding the soft components of jets. We review recent theoretical and phenomenological developments on medium response to jet energy loss, including an overview of both weakly and strongly coupled approaches for describing the thermalization and propagation of energy deposition from jets, effects of medium response on jet observables, and exploration of its unique signatures in jet-hadron correlations. Jet-induced medium excitation is shown to be an essential component in probing the in-medium dynamics of jets and a critical step towards precise extraction of the QGP properties.

## Key claim

This review argues that jet-induced medium excitation (the response of the quark-gluon plasma to energy deposited by jets) is an essential but previously underappreciated component for understanding jet-medium interactions, and that its inclusion is necessary for precise extraction of QGP transport properties, though direct experimental detection of Mach cone structures remains extremely difficult due to complications from radial flow, multi-parton shower structure, and event-by-event fluctuating backgrounds.

## Method

The authors review both weakly coupled approaches (linear Boltzmann transport models such as LBT, Martini, Jewel, and full transport models such as AMPT and Bamps) and strongly coupled approaches (linearized and full hydrodynamic response with source terms) for describing medium response to jet energy loss. They also discuss coupled parton transport and hydrodynamics frameworks (CoLBT-Hydro, jet-fluid models) that concurrently simulate jet shower evolution and medium evolution with energy-momentum exchange, as well as recent efforts to model the thermalization of deposited energy using causal diffusion equations.

## Key numbers

- The jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ in the QGP is about two orders of magnitude larger than the value in cold nuclei, as determined by JET and JETSCAPE Collaborations
- For $\hat{q}/T^3 \sim 5$ and medium temperature $T \sim 0.3$ GeV, the relaxation time $t_\mathrm{rel}$ is on the order of 1 fm
- The separation scale $p^0_\mathrm{cut}$ between quasi-particles and medium background in CoLBT-Hydro is varied between 1 and 4 GeV with little impact on final jet spectra
- The Gaussian smearing width parameters for energy-momentum deposition in CoLBT-Hydro are set as $\sigma_r = 0.2$ fm and $\sigma_{\eta_s} = 0.2$
- In the causal diffusion approach for thermalization, model parameters are set as $p_\mathrm{cut}^0 = 2$ GeV, $\tau_\mathrm{rel} = 1.0$ fm, $D_\mathrm{diff} = 0.6$ fm, and $t_\mathrm{th} = 1.5$ fm

## Context

This review extends the extensive jet quenching literature that has focused primarily on medium modification of jets (energy loss, suppression of high-$p_\mathrm{T}$ spectra, modification of jet shapes and fragmentation functions) by emphasizing the complementary aspect of how the medium responds to deposited energy. It builds on earlier work establishing Mach cone structures from single partons (Casalderrey-Solana 2004, Stoecker 2004) and extends to full parton showers (Qin 2009, Neufeld 2009). The review enables future experimental searches for diffusion wake signals using $Z/\gamma$-jet correlations and machine learning techniques (Yang 2021, Yang 2022), and motivates further development of concurrent jet-fluid simulations for precise extraction of QGP properties such as shear viscosity and the speed of sound.
