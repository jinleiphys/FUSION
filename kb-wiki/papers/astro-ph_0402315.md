# Neutrino emission in neutron matter from magnetic moment interactions

> Neutrino emission drives neutron star cooling for the first several hundreds of years after its birth. Given the low energy ($\sim$ keV) nature of this process, one expects very few nonstandard particle physics contributions which could affect this rate. Requiring that any new physics contributions involve light degrees of freedom, one of the likely candidates which can affect the cooling process would be a nonzero magnetic moment for the neutrino. To illustrate, we compute the emission rate for neutrino pair bremsstrahlung in neutron-neutron scattering through photon-neutrino magnetic moment coupling. We also present analogous differential rates for neutrino scattering off nucleons and electrons that determine neutrino opacities in supernovae. Employing current upper bounds from collider experiments on the tau magnetic moment, we find that the neutrino emission rate can exceed the rate through neutral current electroweak interaction by a factor two, signalling the importance of new particle physics input to a standard calculation of relevance to neutron star cooling. However, astrophysical bounds on the neutrino magnetic moment imply smaller effects.

# Neutrino emission in neutron matter from magnetic moment interactions

## Key claim

Neutrino pair bremsstrahlung in neutron-neutron scattering via the neutrino magnetic moment can exceed the standard electroweak neutral-current emission rate by up to a factor of two, but only if the neutrino magnetic moment is at the upper end of the allowed range (near $10^{-7} \mu_B$); astrophysical bounds that constrain the magnetic moment to $10^{-10} \mu_B$ or below imply negligible corrections.

## Method

The authors compute the neutrino pair bremsstrahlung emissivity from neutron-neutron scattering in pure neutron matter, using a one-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction following Friman and Maxwell (1979), with the photon coupling to the neutron via its magnetic dipole moment ($\kappa_n = -1.91$) and to the neutrino pair via the neutrino magnetic moment ($\kappa$). The calculation is performed in the non-relativistic limit for neutrons at the Fermi surface, assuming strong degeneracy, and the result is expressed as a ratio to the standard electroweak bremsstrahlung emissivity. Analogous differential scattering rates for neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-electron elastic scattering are also presented for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of magnetic-moment-induced to electroweak bremsstrahlung emissivity for $nn$ collisions: $R^{nn} = 2.25 \, (\kappa / 10^{-5} \text{ GeV}^{-1})^2 (1 / m_n^*)^2$, where $m_n^*$ is in GeV.
- Ratio for $np$ bremsstrahlung: $R^{np} = 0.31 \, (\kappa / 10^{-5} \text{ GeV}^{-1})^2 [(-1.91)^2 / m_n^{*2} + (2.79)^2 / m_p^{*2}]$.
- For the range $10^{-10} \mu_B \leq \kappa \leq 10^{-7} \mu_B$, the emissivity ratios span $1.86 \times 10^{-6} \leq R^{nn} \leq 1.86$ and $3.61 \times 10^{-6} \leq R^{np} \leq 3.61$, using nucleon effective masses of 0.9 GeV.
- Conversion: $1 \mu_B = 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ eV}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino magnetic moment effects on stellar cooling, which had focused on plasmon decay in red giants and neutron star crusts (Iwamoto 1995 and references therein). By computing the core emission process (neutron-neutron bremsstrahlung), the authors show that magnetic moment contributions can compete with the standard electroweak rates calculated by Friman and Maxwell (1979), particularly for the tau neutrino whose magnetic moment is less constrained. The results motivate further study of inelastic scattering rates and medium effects for supernova neutrino transport, and highlight that order-unity particle physics corrections to standard emissivities could exist but remain masked by model-dependent uncertainties in neutron star cooling simulations.
