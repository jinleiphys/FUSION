# Charmed Baryons Circa 2015

> This is basically the update of [arXiv:0809.1869], a review on charmed baryon physics around 2007. Topics of this review include the spectroscopy, strong decays, lifetimes, nonleptonic and semileptonic weak decays, and electromagnetic decays of charmed baryons.

## Key claim

This review updates Cheng (2009) on charmed baryon physics, covering spectroscopy, strong decays, lifetimes, and weak/electromagnetic decays, but notes that despite many new excited states discovered by BaBar, Belle, CLEO, and LHCb, the spin-parity quantum numbers of most excited charmed baryons remain unmeasured and must be inferred from quark model assignments.

## Method

The paper compiles experimental mass spectra and decay widths from the Particle Data Group and recent Belle/BaBar measurements, then classifies singly charmed baryons into SU(3) antitriplet and sextet multiplets. It analyzes the orbital angular momentum structure of p-wave and first positive-parity excitations using the heavy quark-light diquark picture, and compares quark model predictions (including the 3P0 recombination model and the relativistic quark-diquark model of Ebert, Faustov, and Galkin) with experimental data to suggest spin-parity assignments for states such as Lambda_c(2880), Sigma_c(2800), and Xi_c(3080).

## Key numbers

- Mass of Lambda_c(2880)+: 2881.53 +/- 0.35 MeV, width 5.8 +/- 1.1 MeV
- Ratio R = Gamma(Lambda_c(2880) -> Sigma_c* pi) / Gamma(Lambda_c(2880) -> Sigma_c pi) = (24.1 +/- 6.4 +1.1/-4.5)%
- Mass of Lambda_c(2940)+: 2939.3 +1.4/-1.5 MeV, width 17 +8/-6 MeV
- Mass of Sigma_c(2800)++: 2801 +4/-6 MeV, width 75 +22/-17 MeV
- Mass of Xi_c(3080)+: 3077.0 +/- 0.4 MeV, width 5.8 +/- 1.0 MeV
- Mass of Xi_c(3080)0: 3079.9 +/- 1.4 MeV, width 5.6 +/- 2.2 MeV
- Mass of Omega_c(2770)0: 2765.9 +/- 2.0 MeV
- Mass difference between Xi_c and Lambda_c in the 5/2+ antitriplet: 196 MeV

## Context

This review extends the earlier review by Cheng (2009) and summarizes the experimental progress on charmed baryons from B-factories (BaBar, Belle) and CLEO. It provides a comprehensive classification of observed states into SU(3) multiplets and compares quark model predictions from Capstick, Ebert-Faustov-Galkin (2011), and the Peking group's 3P0 model. The work enables future experimental determination of spin-parity quantum numbers for states like Lambda_c(2765), Lambda_c(2940), and Xi_c(2980), and motivates searches for predicted states such as the Omega_c(3/2-) sextet member.
