# Model-Independent Determination of the Tidal Deformability of a 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ Neutron Star from Gravitational-Wave Measurements

> Tidal deformability of a 1.4 $M_\odot$ neutron star provides a pivotal window into the physics of dense nuclear matter, bridging gravitational-wave(GW), electromagnetic observations and nuclear physics. In this work, we present a novel, data-driven approach to constrain $Λ_{1.4}$ without invoking specific equation-of-state(EOS) models. By interpolating directly over the mass--tidal-deformability posteriors from GW170817, we obtain an EOS-independent constraint of $ Λ_{1.4} \;=\; 222.89_{-98.85}^{+420.33}. $ We further combine these GW-based results with the X-ray EOS-independent constraint from \cite{Huang_2025}, deriving a multimessenger limit of $ Λ_{1.4} \;=\; 265.18_{-104.38}^{+237.88}, $ which remains largely EOS agnostic. This framework demonstrates that higher-order terms neglected in linear expansion methods do not significantly affect $Λ_{1.4}$ estimates under current observational uncertainties. As gravitational-wave detectors improve in sensitivity and more binary neutron-star mergers are discovered, our purely data-driven strategy can serve as a robust standard baseline for extracting neutron-star interior properties without relying on unverified EOS models.

## Key claim

By interpolating directly over the mass–tidal-deformability posteriors from GW170817 without assuming any specific equation-of-state model, the author obtains an EOS-independent constraint on the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, and demonstrates that higher-order terms neglected in linear expansion methods do not significantly affect estimates under current observational uncertainties.

## Method

The author applies a linear interpolation between the mass–tidal-deformability posteriors of the two neutron stars in GW170817, selecting only pairs where one mass lies below and one above 1.4 solar masses, to estimate the tidal deformability at exactly 1.4 solar masses. This data-driven approach is applied to three publicly available GW170817 posterior datasets: high-spin prior, low-spin prior, and an EOS-insensitive dataset that incorporates universal relations. A second scenario uses Bayesian inference to extract the conditional probability distribution of tidal deformability under the assumption that one binary component has exactly 1.4 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the data-driven interpolation method previously applied to neutron star radius measurements by Huang (2025) to the tidal deformability parameter, providing a model-independent alternative to standard linear expansion methods (PhysRevLett.111.071101, PhysRevD.92.023012) and parametric EOS approaches such as piecewise polytropes (Hebeler 2013) and speed-of-sound parametrizations (PhysRevC.98.045804). The resulting EOS-agnostic constraint on Λ₁.₄ enables direct comparison with nuclear physics constraints from parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as PREX-II and CREX, and can serve as a robust baseline for interpreting future gravitational-wave detections of binary neutron-star mergers as detector sensitivity improves.
