# Two and three photon fusion into charmonium in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions

> In this paper we investigate the production of charmonium states in two and three photon fusion processes in nucleus -- nucleus collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. Our results indicate that the experimental study of these processes is feasible and can be used to constrain the theoretical decay widths and give information on the non $c - \bar{c}$ components of these states.

# Wiki Page: Two and Three Photon Fusion into Charmonium in Ultra-Peripheral Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The authors argue that two- and three-photon fusion processes in ultra-peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies can produce charmonium states with cross sections large enough to be measured experimentally, but only if the produced state has a dominant quark-antiquark component; a large discrepancy between predicted and measured cross sections would indicate a multiquark (tetraquark or molecular) component rather than a pure charmonium assignment.

## Method
The authors use quantum electrodynamics (QED) formulas derived in earlier work (Bertulani and collaborators, 2002) for two-photon fusion into C-even mesons and three-photon fusion into C-odd (vector) mesons, with the hadronization of the quark-antiquark pair into a bound state performed via projection operators and the Van Royen–Weisskopf relation connecting the wavefunction at the origin to the measured two-photon or dilepton decay widths. The nuclear form factor is modeled with a Woods-Saxon distribution parametrized as a convolution of a hard sphere and a Yukawa function, using standard parameters for lead (R = 6.63 fm, a = 0.549 fm). Cross sections are computed for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV (and also at 2.76 TeV and 39 TeV for some states), with the photon fluxes and absorption at small impact parameters included via the equivalent photon approximation.

## Key numbers
- For two-photon fusion into C-even charmonium at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: eta_c (2984 MeV) cross section = 0.43 mb; chi_0c (3415 MeV) = 0.11 mb; chi_2c (3556 MeV) = 0.02 mb; eta_c(2S) (3637 MeV) = 0.09 mb
- For the X(6900) state (assuming interference scenario) at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: cross section = 238 microbarns, using Gamma_gamma = 67 keV from light-by-light scattering analysis
- For three-photon fusion into vector charmonium at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: J/psi (3097 MeV) = 476.5 nb; psi(2S) (3686 MeV) = 161.4 nb; psi(3770) = 19.5 nb; psi(4040) = 59.7 nb; psi(4160) = 32.4 nb; psi(4230) = 101.5 nb; psi(4415) = 36.9 nb
- For the X(3940) assumed as J=0 at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: cross section = 9.7 microbarns, using Gamma_gamma = 0.33 keV from theoretical calculations

## Context
This work extends the formalism developed by Bertulani and collaborators (2002) for meson production in ultra-peripheral collisions, updating cross sections with current PDG decay widths and including newly observed states such as the X(6900) and the psi(4230). It builds on earlier studies of exotic meson production in UPCs by Bertulani (2003) and by Vicencio and collaborators (2016), and is motivated by the recent CMS observation (2022) of the chi_c1(3872) in Pb-Pb collisions. The results provide a baseline for experimental searches at the LHC and future facilities such as the FCC; if the predicted cross sections are confirmed, this would support the quark-antiquark assignment for states whose nature is debated, while large discrepancies would indicate multiquark components, as suggested for the psi(3770) by Voloshin (2005) and recently supported by BESIII (2023) measurements of its decay to eta J/psi.
