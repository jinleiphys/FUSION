# Evolution of the statistical disintegration of finite nuclei toward high energy

> We develop a statistical approach for the description of complex nuclei formation from dynamically produced baryons in high energy heavy-ion reactions. We consider a finite highly-excited expanding nuclear system formed after central nucleus-nucleus collisions. This system is sub-divided into primary equilibrated nucleon clusters. The final nuclei are produced after the decay of these excited clusters. By the successful comparison with the FOPI experimental data we prove the possibility of such a local equilibrium in nuclear matter with the temperature corresponding to the phase coexistence region. The regularities obtained in this new nuclei production mechanism are shown.

## Key claim

The authors propose that complex nuclei in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can be formed via local statistical equilibrium in subnuclear-density baryon clusters, rather than requiring global equilibrium of the entire expanding fireball. The approach is validated by comparison with FOPI data, but the authors hedge that the final conclusion on cluster excitation energy "can only be done after a detailed comparison with experimental data."

## Method

The authors generate initial nucleon distributions using two methods: a microcanonical phase-space generation (PSG) method and a hydrodynamic-like generation (HYG) method with velocities proportional to radial distance. They then apply a coalescence of baryons (CB) model with a velocity coalescence parameter \(v_c\) to group nucleons into primary excited clusters. Finally, they use the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to describe the de-excitation of these clusters into final cold nuclei, with cluster excitation energies scaled to conserve total energy.

## Key numbers

- The coalescence velocity parameters used are \(v_c = 0.18c\), \(0.22c\), \(0.24c\), and \(0.28c\), with \(0.22c\) being approximately the Fermi velocity.
- The cluster density is taken as \(\rho_c \approx \frac{1}{6} \rho_0\), where \(\rho_0 \approx 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) is ground-state nuclear density.
- The best description of FOPI data corresponds to cluster excitation energies of 6--10 MeV per nucleon, corresponding to temperatures \(T \approx 6\)--8 MeV.
- The initial source energies used for comparison with Ni+Ni data are \(E_0 = 37\) A MeV and \(E_0 = 60\) A MeV; for Au+Au data the beam energies are 90, 120, 150, and 250 A MeV.
- The dynamical stage is estimated to occur at times around 10--30 fm/c after the beginning of the collision.

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation approach (SMM, developed by Botvina and collaborators in the 1990s) from single-source spectator fragmentation to the formation of multiple local clusters in the expanding baryonic matter produced in central high-energy collisions. It contrasts with the simplistic coalescence model of Tondeur (1983), where only nucleons forming bound nuclei interact, by instead treating all nucleons in a cluster as fully interacting. The approach enables a consistent statistical description of fragment production in reactions from Fermi energies up to relativistic energies, resolving previous difficulties in describing fragment kinetic energies at high excitation.
