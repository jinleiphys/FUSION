# Production of Light Nuclei in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7-27$ GeV from STAR BES-II

> The studies of the production of light nuclei, such as deuteron and helium nuclei, in heavy-ion collisions are essential for understanding the dynamics of nuclear matter under extreme conditions. The yields and ratios of light nuclei serve as an effective method to distinguish between the thermal and coalescence models of light nuclei formation. Within the coalescence framework, the energy dependence of the coalescence parameters reflects the effective volume of the collision system, while in the thermal model yields are governed by chemical freeze-out conditions. The significantly larger datasets from the STAR Beam Energy Scan Phase II (BES-II), combined with enhanced detector capabilities, allow more precise and comprehensive measurements than phase I. In these proceedings, we present measurements of light nuclei production, including p, $\rm \bar{p}$, d, $\rm \bar{d}$, $\rm ^3He$, in Au+Au collisions at BES-II energies of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7 - 27$ GeV. The results include centrality-dependent transverse momentum spectra and yields ($\mathrm{d}N/\mathrm{d}y$), along with coalescence parameters $B_\mathrm{A}$ and particle yield ratios. The physics implications of these results are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents new, more precise measurements of light nuclei production from the STAR BES-II program, showing that the thermal model overestimates the \(^3\)He/p ratio while the coalescence parameters \(B_A\) decrease with collision energy up to 11.5 GeV and then stabilize, indicating an expanding effective emission volume.

## Method

The STAR Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and Time-of-Flight (TOF) detectors were used to identify and reconstruct protons, antiprotons, deuterons, antideuterons, and \(^3\)He in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\)–27 GeV. Transverse momentum spectra were corrected for energy loss, tracking efficiency, TOF matching efficiency, knock-out effects, and feed-down from weak decays of strange baryons. Yields \(\mathrm{d}N/\mathrm{d}y\) were extracted by integrating the spectra after extrapolation to full \(p_{\rm T}\) range using Blast-Wave fits, with systematic uncertainties estimated by varying analysis cuts and comparing to double-exponential fits.

## Key numbers

- \(\mathrm{d}N/\mathrm{d}y\) yields for p, \(\bar{\rm p}\), d, \(\bar{\rm d}\), and \(^3\)He were measured in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\)–27 GeV, with rapidity selections \(|y| < 0.1\) for (anti-)protons, \(|y| < 0.3\) for (anti-)deuterons, and \(|y| < 0.5\) for \(^3\)He.
- The coalescence parameters \(B_A\) were evaluated at \(p_{\rm T}/A = 0.65\) GeV/\(c\) for d, \(\bar{\rm d}\), and \(^3\)He in 0–10% central Au+Au collisions.
- \(^{A-1}\sqrt{B_A}\) decreases with collision energy between \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 3\)–11.5 GeV, then remains roughly constant up to 27 GeV.
- The \(\rm d/p\) and \(\rm \bar{d}/\bar{p}\) ratios at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \ge 7.7\) GeV are well described by the thermal model, while the \(\rm ^3He/p\) ratios are overestimated.
- A hint of increase in \(\langle p_{\rm T} \rangle\) is observed for energies below 4.5 GeV, with a flat trend between 7.7 and 27 GeV.

## Context

These measurements extend the STAR BES-I results (Jin 2015, BESI_d, BESI_t) with a tenfold increase in data and improved detector capabilities from the BES-II program, enabling more precise tests of the thermal model and coalescence model for light nuclei formation. The results constrain the effective source volume in the coalescence framework and the chemical freeze-out conditions in the thermal model, and the discrepancy in the \(^3\)He/p ratio suggests hadronic re-scattering effects not captured by the thermal model. Future studies in the intermediate energy region (4.5–7.7 GeV) are needed to clarify the transition in \(\langle p_{\rm T} \rangle\) behavior.
