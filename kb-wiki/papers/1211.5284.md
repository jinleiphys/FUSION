# Ideal gas provides q-entropy

> A mathematical procedure is suggested to obtain deformed entropy formulas of type K(S_K) = sum_i P_i K(-ln P_i), by requiring zero mutual K(S_K)-information between a finite subsystem and a finite reservoir. The use of this method is first demonstrated on the ideal gas equation of state with finite constant heat capacity, C, where it delivers the Renyi and Tsallis formulas. A novel interpretation of the qstar = 2-q duality arises from the comparison of canonical subsystem and total microcanonical partition approaches. Finally a new, generalized deformed entropy formula is constructed for the linear relation C(S) = C_0 + C_1 S.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that requiring zero mutual K-information between a finite subsystem and a finite reservoir mathematically produces deformed entropy formulas of the Tsallis and Rényi type, but only under the condition that the heat capacity of the system is constant and finite; the specific q-parameter is determined by the inverse heat capacity, with the duality q* = 2 − q arising from whether one factorizes the thermodynamical or the canonical probability.

## Method

The author starts from the microcanonical equation of state for an ideal gas with finite constant heat capacity C₀, S(E) = C₀ ln(1 + E/(C₀T₀)). He then seeks a function K(S) such that K(S₁(E₁)) + K(S₂(E − E₁)) = K(S₁₂(E)) for a subsystem and reservoir with fixed total energy, i.e., zero mutual K-information. By requiring K(S) to be linear in energy (since energy is additive), he solves for K(S) and obtains the Tsallis q-entropy formula with q = 1 − 1/C₀ for the thermodynamical probability and q* = 1 + 1/C₀ for the canonical (conditional) probability. He then generalizes the procedure to systems with a linear heat capacity–entropy relation C(S) = C₀ + C₁S.

## Key numbers

* For an ideal gas with finite constant heat capacity C₀, the deformed entropy is K(S) = C₀(e^(S/C₀) − 1), leading to Tsallis q-entropy with q = 1 − 1/C₀.
* For the canonical (conditional) probability, the dual parameter is q* = 1 + 1/C₀, satisfying q* = 2 − q.
* The microcanonical equation of state for constant heat capacity is S(E) = C₀ ln(1 + E/(C₀T₀)), with S(0) = 0 by the third law.
* The thermodynamical probability for an isolated system with energy E₁ is P₁ = (1 + E₁/(C₀T₀))^(−C₀).
* The conditional probability for a subsystem with energy E₁ given total energy E is P*₁ ∝ (1 − E₁/(C₀₂T))^(C₀₂), where C₀₂ is the reservoir heat capacity and T = T₀₂ + E/C₀₂.
* In the limit N → ∞ (infinite reservoir), the canonical distribution becomes P*_i = e^(−ΔS) e^(−(E_i − U)/T).
* In the opposite limit N → 0 (vanishing reservoir), P*_i = e^(−ΔS) δ(E_i − U), recovering the microcanonical result.
* For the linear class C(S) = C₀ + C₁S, the generalized deformed entropy formula is K*(ΔS) = (C/C₁) Σ_i P*_i (1/q₁)[1 − (1 + (C₁/C) ln P*_i)^(q₁)] with q₁ = 1 + 1/C₁.

## Context

This work extends the finite-reservoir approach to deformed entropies previously studied by Campisi (2012) and by the author in earlier work on the zeroth law and asymptotic rules (Biro, 2012). It provides a physical interpretation of the Tsallis q-parameter as the inverse heat capacity of the system or reservoir, and explains the q* = 2 − q duality as arising from the two different factorization procedures (thermodynamical versus canonical probability). The results enable the construction of generalized deformed entropy formulas for systems with entropy-dependent heat capacity, such as black body radiation (C = 3S) and black hole thermodynamics (C = −2S), which are left for future work.
