# Generalized Hall current on a finite lattice

> Gapped fermion theories with gapless boundary fermions can exist in any number of dimensions. When the boundary has even space-time dimensions and hosts chiral fermions, a quantum Hall current flows from the bulk to the boundary in a background electric field. This current compensate for the boundary chiral anomaly. Such a current inflow picture is absent when the boundary theory is odd dimensional. However, in recent work, the idea of quantum Hall current has been generalized to describe odd dimensional boundary theories in continuous Euclidean space-time dimension of infinite volume. In this paper we extend this idea to a lattice regulated finite volume theory of 1+1 dimensional Wilson-Dirac fermions. This fermion theory with a domain wall in fermion mass can host gapless modes on the wall. The number of gapless fermions is equal to the integral of the divergence of the lattice generalized Hall current.

## Key claim

The paper extends the continuum construction of a generalized Hall current, which compensates for boundary anomalies in odd-dimensional boundary theories, to a lattice-regulated finite-volume theory of 1+1 dimensional Wilson-Dirac fermions, demonstrating that the space-time integral of the divergence of this lattice current equals the number of gapless fermions on a domain wall, provided one works with the singular value decomposition of the fermion operator rather than with exact zero modes.

## Method

The authors discretize a 1+1 dimensional Euclidean Dirac fermion with a domain wall in the fermion mass and a crossed domain wall in a background diagnostic pseudoscalar field, using either a Wilson-like operator (with higher-derivative terms separated by direction) or a standard Wilson term. They compute the generalized Hall current via a one-loop Feynman diagram in the continuum and then, on the lattice, study the singular value decomposition of the fermion operator to identify near-zero modes of \(\mathcal{D}^\dagger\mathcal{D}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\mathcal{D}^\dagger\) in finite volume, relating the divergence of the lattice current to the local index near each vortex defect.

## Key numbers

* For the Wilson-like operator with domain wall heights \(m_0 = 1\) and \(\mu_0 = 1\), the zero mode solution on an infinite lattice has the form \(z_0 = (1 - \phi_2)\) and \(z_1 = (1 - \phi_1)\) (Eq. 4.12), requiring \(0 < m_0 < 2\) and \(0 < \mu_0 < 2\) for normalizability.
* In finite volume on \(S^1 \times S^1\) with periodic boundary conditions, the exact zero mode of the Wilson-like operator exists when the domain wall heights satisfy \(\frac{1}{1 - m_-} = (1 - m_+)\) and \(\frac{1}{1 - \mu_-} = (1 - \mu_+)\), independent of lattice size (Eq. 4.16).
* The smallest singular value of the 0+1 dimensional Wilson fermion operator with a domain wall approaches zero exponentially as \(\sigma_0 \sim O(e^{-L})\) with lattice size \(L\).
* The continuum generalized Hall current is computed as \(\mathcal{J}_\mu^\chi = \frac{1}{\pi} \epsilon_{\mu\nu} \partial_\nu \theta\), and the space-time integral of its divergence equals the winding number \(\nu_\theta\) of the crossed domain wall configuration, yielding an index of 1 for the chosen profile.

## Context

This work extends the continuum construction of a generalized Hall current for odd-dimensional boundary theories, introduced by Kaplan and collaborators (2021, 2022), to a discrete lattice in both infinite and finite volume. It builds on the Callan-Harvey (1985) mechanism of current inflow from a gapped bulk to a boundary, and on the Goldstone-Wilczek (1981) calculation for current generation. The paper enables numerical studies of topological indices and anomaly inflow on finite lattices without fine-tuning of domain wall heights, using singular value decomposition to handle the absence of exact zero modes in finite volume.
