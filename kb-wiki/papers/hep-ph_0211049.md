# Optically opaque color-flavor locked phase inside compact stars

> The contribution of thermally excited electron-positron pairs to the bulk properties of the color-flavor locked quark phase inside compact stars is examined. The presence of these pairs causes the photon mean free path to be much smaller than a typical core radius ($R_0 \simeq 1$ km) for all temperatures above 25 keV so that the photon contribution to the thermal conductivity is much smaller than that of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons. We also find that the electrons and positrons dominate the electrical conductivity, while their contributions to the total thermal energy is negligible.

## Key claim

The presence of thermally excited electron-positron pairs in the color-flavor locked (CFL) quark phase inside compact stars makes the CFL core optically opaque to photons for temperatures above about 25 keV, which suppresses the photon contribution to thermal conductivity; however, the overall thermal conductivity of CFL matter remains extremely high, and the electron-positron contribution to the total thermal energy is negligible.

## Method

The authors calculated the photon mean free path in CFL quark matter using the Thomson cross section and the equilibrium electron density from a finite-temperature neutral plasma, finding the temperature threshold where the mean free path becomes smaller than the typical core radius of 1 km. They then estimated the thermal conductivity from Nambu-Goldstone bosons and compared it to the photon contribution, and computed the electron-positron thermal energy using Bessel function expansions. Finally, they estimated the electrical conductivity of the electron-positron plasma using classical expressions with the electron mean free path in the leading logarithm approximation, considering scattering processes and the Debye screening mass.

## Key numbers

- Photon mean free path in CFL phase is less than 220 m for temperatures above 25 keV, compared to a typical core radius of 1 km.
- Transparency (mean free path exceeding 1 km) sets in for temperatures below 23.4 keV.
- Thomson cross section used: sigma_T = 66.54 fm^2.
- Thermal conductivity of CFL matter: kappa_CFL = (7.2 x 10^31) * T_MeV^3 * R_0,km erg cm^{-1} sec^{-1} K^{-1}, where T is in MeV and R_0 in km.
- Electron-positron contribution to thermal energy is much smaller than that of massless states (photons and Nambu-Goldstone bosons) for all temperatures below 0.1 MeV.
- Electrical conductivity of the electron-positron plasma is dominated by electrons and positrons; at temperatures above about 17 keV, the expression depends on density only through the Coulomb logarithm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of thermal properties of CFL quark matter (Shovkovy and Ellis, 2002) by including the previously overlooked role of thermally excited electron-positron pairs. It shows that the cooling mechanism described in that earlier work remains unchanged, but the optical opacity of CFL matter at temperatures above 25 keV may have observational importance for bare CFL quark stars. The electrical conductivity results may be relevant for studying the dynamo mechanism of magnetic field generation in compact stars with quark cores, as discussed by Thompson and Duncan (1993).
