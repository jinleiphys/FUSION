# Hadronic Paschen-Back effect

> We find a novel phenomenon induced by the interplay between a strong magnetic field and finite orbital angular momenta in hadronic systems, which is analogous to the Paschen-Back effect observed in the field of atomic physics. This effect allows the wave functions to drastically deform. We discuss anisotropic decay from the deformation as a possibility to measure the strength of the magnetic field in heavy-ion collision at LHC, RHIC and SPS, which has not experimentally been measured. As an example we investigate charmonia with a finite orbital angular momentum in a strong magnetic field. We calculate the mass spectra and mixing rates. To obtain anisotropic wave functions, we apply the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method, where the Gaussian bases to expand the wave functions have different widths along transverse and longitudinal directions in the cylindrical coordinate.

## Key claim

The authors propose a "Hadronic Paschen-Back effect" (HPBE) for hadrons with finite orbital angular momentum in a strong magnetic field, analogous to the atomic Paschen-Back effect, where the wave functions become dominated by the $L_z$ and $S_z$ quantum numbers rather than total $J$. The effect is claimed to occur even when the direct coupling between orbital angular momentum and the magnetic field vanishes (as in neutral quarkonia), and the authors argue it can serve as a probe of magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions at strengths as low as $|eB| \sim 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic constituent quark model for charmonia, solving the Schrödinger equation with a Hamiltonian that includes kinetic terms, a confining potential, a Coulomb term, spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions, plus the magnetic moment coupling to the external field. They apply the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method (CGEM) to obtain anisotropic wave functions with different widths along the transverse ($\rho$) and longitudinal ($z$) directions relative to the magnetic field. The model parameters are taken from Barnes et al. (2005) and the spin-orbit and tensor smearing parameters are fixed to reproduce the experimental masses of the 1P charmonia.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength at RHIC is estimated at $|eB| \sim 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ at most, and at LHC at $|eB| \sim 1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ at most.
- The typical QCD scale is $\Lambda \sim 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}$.
- At SPS energies, the maximum magnetic field strength is $|eB| \sim 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ with a lifetime of $t \sim 2\ \mathrm{fm}/c$.
- The saturation of the wave functions by the Paschen-Back configurations is reached already at $|eB| \ge 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
- Level crossings occur at $|eB| = 0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ in the $J_z=\pm1$ channel and at $|eB| = 0.5$ and $0.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ in the $J_z=0$ channel.
- The mass splitting between the 2nd and 3rd states in $J_z=0$ due to the tensor coupling is about $100\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- The deformation of S-wave charmonia requires a strong magnetic field of $|eB| \sim 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, while P-wave deformation occurs at much smaller fields due to HPBE.
- The model parameters used are $(\sigma, \alpha_s, \Lambda, m_c) = (0.1425\ \mathrm{GeV}^2, 0.5461, 1.0946\ \mathrm{GeV}, 1.4794\ \mathrm{GeV})$.
- The smearing parameters for the LS and tensor terms are $(\Lambda_\mathrm{LS}, A_\mathrm{LS}, \Lambda_\mathrm{T}, A_\mathrm{T}) = (0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}, 7.3, 1.2\ \mathrm{GeV}, 1.2)$.
- The mass splitting between $h_c$ and $\chi_{c1}$ is about $15\ \mathrm{MeV}$ for charmonia, while for bottomonia the analogous splitting is about $6.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of quarkonium properties in magnetic fields, previously focused on S-wave charmonia (Suzuki 2016, Yoshida 2016), to P-wave states where the deformation arises from the Hadronic Paschen-Back effect in addition to Landau levels. The authors propose that anisotropic radiative decays of P-wave charmonia could serve as a novel probe of magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, and SPS, where direct experimental measurement of the field strength has not been achieved. The work also suggests that bottomonium systems (e.g., $h_b$, $\chi_{b0}$, $\chi_{b1}$, $\chi_{b2}$) could exhibit similar effects, with the smaller spin-orbit coupling in bottomonia being favorable for HPBE, though the smaller magnetic moment of bottom quarks weakens the spin mixing.
