# Quarkyonic equation of state with a momentum dependent interaction and neutron star structure

> The structure and basic properties of dense nuclear matter still remain one of the open problems of Physics. In particular, the composition of the matter that composes neutron stars is under theoretical and experimental investigation. Among the theories that have been proposed, apart from the classical one where the composition is dominated by hadrons, the existence or coexistence of free quark matter is a dominant guess. An approach towards this solution is the phenomenological view according to which the existence of quarkyonic matter plays a dominant role in the construction of the equation of state (EOS). According to it the structure of the EOS is based on the existence of the quarkyonic particle which is a hybrid state of a particle that combines properties of hadronic and quark matter with a corresponding representation in momentum space. In this paper we propose a phenomenological model for quarkyonic matter, borrowed from corresponding applications in hadronic models, where the interaction in the quarkyonic matter depends not only on the position but also on the momentum of the quarkyonic particles. This consideration, as we demonstrate, can have a remarkable consequence on the shape of the EOS and thus on the properties of neutron stars, offering a sufficiently flexible model. Comparison with recent observational data can place constraints on the parameterization of the particular model and help improve its reliability.

## Key claim
The authors propose that introducing a momentum-dependent interaction (Skyrme-type) between baryons in quarkyonic matter, rather than only density-dependent interactions, makes the equation of state more flexible and can adjust its stiffness without violating causality in the speed of sound, provided the confinement-scale parameter Λ_Qyc does not exceed about 210 MeV.

## Method
The authors construct a zero-temperature NDU (neutron, up-quark, down-quark) quarkyonic model where neutrons interact via a momentum-dependent potential of the Gale-Bertsch type (with two finite-range terms), while quarks are treated as a non-interacting Fermi gas. They compute the total energy density and pressure as functions of neutron Fermi momentum, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass, radius, and tidal deformability for various values of the transition density n_tr (0.2–0.4 fm⁻³) and the confinement parameter Λ_Qyc (160, 180, 200 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is used as the reference density for the interaction parameterization.
- The speed of sound violates causality (c_s/c > 1) for Λ_Qyc > 210 MeV.
- For transition densities n_tr = 0.3 and 0.4 fm⁻³, the model predicts neutron stars of 1.4 solar masses have radii of approximately 13–13.3 km, consistent with LIGO observational estimates.
- The tidal deformability Λ for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star falls outside GW170817 constraints only for the case with n_tr = 0.2 fm⁻³.
- Based on observational data, the transition density is constrained to be greater than 0.3 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends the quarkyonic matter framework introduced by McLerran and collaborators (2019) and further developed by Fukushima (2016) and Kojo (2024), which treats quarks inside the Fermi sea and baryons near the Fermi surface. It adds a momentum-dependent interaction term borrowed from heavy-ion collision studies by Gale et al. (1987) and Prakash (1997), which had previously been applied only to hadronic models. The results enable future comparisons with gravitational wave observations from binary neutron star mergers (e.g., GW170817) to constrain the transition density and confinement scale of quarkyonic matter, and the authors note that extension to finite temperature and inclusion of beta equilibrium (protons and electrons) is underway.
