# Isospin Symmetry Breaking in Nuclei - ONS Anomaly

> We study the binding energy differences of the valence proton and neutron of the mirror nuclei, $^{15}$O - $^{15}$N, $^{17}$F - $^{17}$O, $^{39}$Ca - $^{39}$K and $^{41}$Sc - $^{41}$Ca, using the quark-meson coupling model. The calculation involves nuclear structure and shell effects explicitly. It is shown that binding energy differences of a few hundred keV arise from the strong interaction, even after subtracting all electromagnetic corrections. The origin of these differences may be ascribed to the charge symmetry breaking effects set in the strong interaction through the u and d current quark mass difference. In this report, we first review the quark-meson coupling model. In particular, we discuss about the nucleon mass in nuclear medium. Then, we present details of the charge symmetry breaking in finite nuclei, especially the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly.

## Key claim

The binding energy differences of a few hundred keV observed in mirror nuclei (the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly) can arise from charge symmetry breaking in the strong interaction through the u and d current quark mass difference, even after subtracting all electromagnetic corrections, but the calculation depends on the specific quark-meson coupling model and the explicit inclusion of nuclear structure and shell effects.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which treats nucleons as MIT bags containing three quarks coupled to scalar (sigma), vector (omega), and isovector vector (rho) meson fields in the mean-field approximation. They introduced different u and d current quark masses (5 MeV and 9.2424 MeV respectively) to break charge symmetry, then solved the coupled Dirac equations self-consistently for finite mirror nuclei including shell effects, with the coupling constants fixed to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties and free nucleon masses.

## Key numbers

- u current quark mass: 5 MeV (input); d current quark mass: 9.2424 MeV (calculated to reproduce free neutron mass)
- Free bag radius: 0.8 fm for both proton and neutron; bag constant B^(1/4) = 169.81 MeV (CSB case)
- Nuclear matter saturation: binding energy -15.7 MeV at density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3; incompressibility K ~ 280 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_sigma^2/4pi = 5.40, g_omega^2/4pi = 5.31, g_rho^2/4pi = 6.93 (for symmetric nuclear matter)
- Binding energy difference for 17F-17O: 171 keV (calculated) vs 220 keV (observed)
- Binding energy difference for 15O-15N: 123 keV (calculated) vs 230 keV (observed)
- Binding energy difference for 39Ca-39K: 108 keV (calculated) vs 340 keV (observed)
- Binding energy difference for 41Sc-41Ca: 228 keV (calculated) vs 460 keV (observed)

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-based calculations of the ONS anomaly by Saito and Thomas (1994) using a more consistent shell model approach rather than local density approximation, and contrasts with traditional meson-exchange potential calculations by Blunden and Iqbal (1987), Suzuki, Sagawa and Arima (1992), and Shahnas (1994). The results enable future comparison with the rho-omega mixing mechanism for charge symmetry breaking, and raise the question of whether there is a deeper connection between these apparently different sources of charge symmetry violation.
