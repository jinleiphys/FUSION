# Monte Carlo model for nuclear collisions from SPS to LHC energies

> A Monte Carlo model to simulate nuclear collisions in the energy range going from SPS to LHC, is presented. The model includes in its initial stage both soft and semihard components, which lead to the formation of color strings. Collectivity is taken into account considering the possibility of strings in color representations higher than triplet or antitriplet, by means of string fusion. String breaking leads to the production of secondaries. At this point, the model can be used as initial condition for further evolution by a transport model. In order to tune the parameters and see the results in nucleus-nucleus collisions, a naif model for rescattering of secondaries is introduced. Results of the model are compared with experimental data, and predictions for RHIC and LHC are shown.

# Monte Carlo model for nuclear collisions from SPS to LHC energies

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-QGP (Quark Gluon Plasma) conventional model for nuclear collisions from SPS to LHC energies that includes both soft and semihard components with string fusion, but the authors explicitly state that the model's predictions which depend critically on rescattering effects "should be taken with much care" due to the extremely naive implementation of rescattering with no space-time evolution.

## Method

The model generates partonic wave functions of colliding hadrons using a quasieikonal approach, computes elementary partonic collisions with both soft and semihard components (the latter via PYTHIA 5.5 + ARIADNE 4.02 + JETSET 7.3 for gluon-gluon collisions only), and forms color strings that can fuse when their parent partons are within a certain area in impact parameter space. String breaking follows a Schwinger formula with an invariant area law, and a simple rescattering model using two-body reactions with energy-independent cross sections and a minimum density threshold is applied to secondaries.

## Key numbers

- String tension for triplet representation: K_{3} = 0.18 GeV^2
- Pomeron intercept minus 1: Delta = 0.139
- Parton-parton cross section: sigma_P = 3.3 mb
- Fusion cross section: sigma_fus = 7.5 mb (r_fus = 0.35 fm)
- Transverse momentum cut-off for hard collisions: p_T min = 3.03 + 0.11 ln(s/s_0) GeV/c, with sqrt(s_0) = 25 GeV
- Probability for two particles to scatter in a given inelastic channel: 7% (except Omega channels at 14% and nucleon annihilation at 70%)
- Minimum density for rescattering: dN/(dy dp_T)|_min = 17
- Critical percolation density: eta_c ~ 1.12 to 1.5, corresponding to 6 to 12 strings/fm^2
- Predicted central rapidity densities for charged particles: 600 to 1500 for central AuAu at RHIC, 2000 to 8000 for central PbPb at LHC
- Omega yield in central PbPb at SPS: model results are 40% below experimental data
- Phi integrated yields per event: 3.55 (without fusion), 4.20 (with fusion), 5.35 (with fusion and rescattering), compared to experimental 7.6 ± 1.1

## Context

This work extends the Dual Parton Model (DPM) and Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) approaches by incorporating string fusion as a mechanism for collectivity, similar to the color ropes mechanism in RQMD (Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics). The model is intended as a conventional, non-QGP baseline for interpreting heavy ion data from SPS through RHIC to LHC, and can be used as initial condition for further evolution by transport models such as RQMD, UrQMD, or HSD. The results are compared with experimental data from the NA49, WA97, and NA35 collaborations at SPS, and the authors note that baryon junction migration approaches (as in DPMJET or by Vance and collaborators) provide an alternative explanation for strangeness enhancement and stopping power that they do not include to avoid double counting.
