# Elastic scattering of 3He+4He with SONIK

> Measurements of the elastic scattering cross section of 3He and 4He are important in order to improve constraints on theoretical models of 4He(3He,g)7Be, a key reaction in Big Bang nucleosynthesis and solar neutrino production. The astrophysical S-factor for this reaction is a significant source of uncertainty in the standard solar-model prediction of the 7Be and 8B solar neutrino fluxes. The elastic scattering measurements reported in the literature do not extend to low energies and lack proper uncertainty quantification. A new measurement of the 4He(3He,3He)4He reaction has been made at center-of-mass energies Ec.m. = 0.38-3.13 MeV using the Scattering of Nuclei in Inverse Kinematics (SONIK) scattering chamber: a windowless, extended gas target surrounded by an array of 30 collimated silicon charged particle detectors situated at TRIUMF. This is the first elastic scattering measurement of 3He+4He made below 500 keV and it has greater angular range and better precision than previous measurements. The elastic scattering data were analyzed using both R-matrix and Halo Effective Field Theory (Halo EFT) frameworks, and values of the s-wave scattering length and effective range were extracted. The resulting improvement in knowledge of the s-wave effective-range function at low energies will reduce the overall uncertainty in S34 at solar energies.

## Key claim

The new elastic scattering measurement of 3He+4He at center-of-mass energies from 0.38 to 3.13 MeV provides the first data below 500 keV for this system, and the resulting improvement in the s-wave effective-range function will reduce the overall uncertainty in the astrophysical S-factor S34 at solar energies, though the paper does not directly report a new S34 value.

## Method

The experiment used the SONIK (Scattering of Nuclei in Inverse Kinematics) scattering chamber at TRIUMF, a windowless extended gas target of 4He surrounded by 30 collimated silicon charged particle detectors, with a 3He beam delivered by the ISAC-I facility. Elastic scattering of 4He(3He,3He)4He was measured at nine incident beam energies from 0.721 to 5.490 MeV (corresponding to center-of-mass energies 0.38-3.13 MeV), and the differential cross sections were extracted using a Geant4 Monte Carlo simulation to account for multiple scattering effects on the detector acceptance (G factor). The data were analyzed using both R-matrix and Halo Effective Field Theory (Halo EFT) frameworks to extract s-wave scattering length and effective range values.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range: Ec.m. = 0.38-3.13 MeV (from 3He beam energies of 0.721-5.490 MeV)
- First elastic scattering measurement of 3He+4He made below 500 keV (Ec.m.)
- Measured stopping power at E[3He]=1.767 MeV: 11.97 ± 0.53 eV/(10^15 atoms/cm^2), which differs from SRIM prediction (11.08 eV/(10^15 atoms/cm^2)) by 8.0%
- Effective target length: 24.61 ± 1.09 cm
- G factor at lowest particle energy (0.24 MeV): 80.6 nm
- Systematic uncertainty in G factor due to multiple scattering model choice: approximately ±1%
- Common mode errors for different energies: between 3.7% and 9.6%
- Empty gas beam transmission uncertainty: additional 3% added in quadrature

## Context

This work addresses the need identified by deBoer et al. (2014) for a new elastic scattering study of 4He(3He,3He)4He covering a wide angular range with detailed uncertainty estimates, which was motivated by discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental observations in ab initio NCSMC calculations by Dohet-Eraly et al. The improved elastic scattering data will constrain theoretical models of the 4He(3He,gamma)7Be reaction, which is a key input for standard solar model predictions of 7Be and 8B neutrino fluxes (currently with uncertainties of ±6% and ±12% respectively, as reported by Vinyoles et al. 2017), and will enable more precise extrapolations of S34 to solar energies using R-matrix and Halo EFT frameworks.
