# Heavy Quark Diffusion in Strong Magnetic Fields at Weak Coupling and Implications for Elliptic Flow

> We compute the momentum diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks, $κ_\parallel$ and $κ_\perp$, in a strong magnetic field $B$ along the directions parallel and perpendicular to $B$, respectively, at the leading order in QCD coupling constant $α_s$. We consider a regime relevant for the relativistic heavy ion collisions, $α_s eB\ll T^2\ll eB$, so that thermal excitations of light quarks are restricted to the lowest Landau level (LLL) states. In the vanishing light-quark mass limit, we find $κ_\perp^{\rm LO}\propto α_s^2 T eB$ in the leading order that arises from screened Coulomb scatterings with (1+1)-dimensional LLL quarks, while $κ_\parallel$ gets no contribution from the scatterings with LLL quarks due to kinematic restrictions. We show that the first non-zero leading order contributions to $κ_\parallel^{\rm LO}$ come from the two separate effects: 1) the screened Coulomb scatterings with thermal gluons, and 2) a finite light-quark mass $m_q$. The former leads to $κ_\parallel^{\rm LO,\,gluon} \propto α_s^2 T^3$ and the latter to $κ_\parallel^{\rm LO,\,massive}\propto α_s (α_s eB)^{1/2} m_q^2$. Based on our results, we propose a new scenario for the large value of heavy-quark elliptic flow observed in RHIC and LHC. Namely, when $κ_\perp\ggκ_\parallel$, an anisotropy in drag forces gives rise to a sizable amount of the heavy-quark elliptic flow even if heavy quarks do not fully belong to an ellipsoidally expanding background fluid.

## Key claim

The authors compute that in the regime \(\alpha_s eB \ll T^2 \ll eB\), the heavy-quark momentum diffusion coefficient perpendicular to the magnetic field scales as \(\kappa_\perp \propto \alpha_s^2 T eB\) while the parallel coefficient \(\kappa_\parallel\) receives no contribution from scatterings with lowest Landau level (LLL) quarks due to kinematic restrictions; the first non-zero leading-order contributions to \(\kappa_\parallel\) come from scatterings with thermal gluons (\(\kappa_\parallel \propto \alpha_s^2 T^3\)) and from finite light-quark mass effects (\(\kappa_\parallel \propto \alpha_s (\alpha_s eB)^{1/2} m_q^2\)), yielding \(\kappa_\parallel / \kappa_\perp \sim T^2 / (eB) \ll 1\).

## Method

The authors used leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD to compute the longitudinal and transverse momentum diffusion coefficients \(\kappa_\parallel\) and \(\kappa_\perp\) of a heavy quark in a strong magnetic field. They employed the real-time Schwinger-Keldysh formalism to evaluate the retarded gluon self-energy from LLL quark loops and from hard thermal gluon loops, resummed the self-energy into the longitudinal gluon propagator, and extracted the scattering rate from the spectral density in the \(\omega \to 0\) limit. The calculation assumed the hierarchy \(\alpha_s eB \ll T^2 \ll eB\) so that only LLL states are thermally occupied and the screening mass from LLL quarks (\(m_{D,B}^2 \sim \alpha_s eB\)) dominates over the gluonic contribution (\(\sim \alpha_s T^2\)).

## Key numbers

* \(\kappa_\perp^{\rm LO} \propto \alpha_s^2 T eB\): leading-order perpendicular diffusion coefficient from screened Coulomb scatterings with (1+1)-dimensional LLL quarks, in the regime \(\alpha_s eB \ll T^2 \ll eB\).
* \(\kappa_\parallel^{\rm LO,\,gluon} \propto \alpha_s^2 T^3\): leading-order parallel diffusion coefficient from screened Coulomb scatterings with thermal gluons.
* \(\kappa_\parallel^{\rm LO,\,massive} \propto \alpha_s (\alpha_s eB)^{1/2} m_q^2\): leading-order parallel diffusion coefficient from finite light-quark mass \(m_q\).
* \(\kappa_\parallel / \kappa_\perp \sim T^2 / (eB) \ll 1\): ratio of longitudinal to transverse diffusion coefficients in the regime studied.

## Context

This work extends perturbative QCD calculations of heavy-quark diffusion (Moore 2004, Caron-Huot 2007) to the case of a strong magnetic field, relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The authors propose a new scenario for the large heavy-quark elliptic flow observed in experiments: when \(\kappa_\perp \gg \kappa_\parallel\), the anisotropy in drag forces can produce sizable elliptic flow even if heavy quarks do not fully thermalize with the expanding plasma. This contrasts with previous studies of static heavy-flavor properties in magnetic fields (Machado, Gub) and with AdS/CFT computations for weak magnetic fields (RS).
