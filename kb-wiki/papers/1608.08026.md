# Color screening masses from string models

> We use gauge/string duality to estimate the Debye screening mass at non-zero temperature and baryon chemical potential. We interpret this mass as the smallest one in the open string channel. Comparisons are made with the results from holography and lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper uses gauge/string duality to estimate the Debye screening mass at non-zero temperature and baryon chemical potential, interpreting this mass as the smallest mass in the open string channel, but the estimates rely on the assumption that short-distance behavior of correlators can be matched to parameterizations motivated by high-temperature perturbation theory and lattice QCD.

## Method

The author computes correlators of Polyakov loops \(C_{1,\bar 1}\) (quark-antiquark) and \(C_{2,0}\) (diquark) using a five-dimensional effective string model with a charged black hole background, where strings are Nambu-Goto strings and baryon vertices are point-like objects. The Debye mass is extracted by matching the short-distance expansion of the binding free energy from the string model to the parameterized form \(-\frac{4\alpha}{3l}e^{-ml}\) for the singlet state and \(-\frac{2\alpha}{3l}e^{-ml}\) for the antitriplet state, then using the relation \(M = 2m\) to obtain the screening mass from the lightest closed string state.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient \(\alpha_{\qqb}\) in the quark-antiquark potential is \(\alpha_{\qqb} = \g (2\pi)^3 \Gamma^{-4}(\tfrac{1}{4})\), with \(\g\) a model parameter.
- The coefficient \(\alpha_{\qq}\) in the quark-quark potential is given by \(\alpha_{\qq} = -\frac{1}{2}\g\,\xi^{-\oh}B(\xi^2;\tfrac{3}{4},\tfrac{1}{2})\bigl(1+3\kappa+\frac{1}{2}\xi^{\frac{1}{2}}B(\xi^2;-\tfrac{1}{4},\tfrac{1}{2})\bigr)\), where \(\xi = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}(1-2\kappa-3\kappa^2)^\oh\) and \(\kappa = \frac{\mathfrak{m}}{3\g}\).
- The screening mass \(M\) is given by \(M = \frac{\gamma}{\pi}\sqrt{\s}\biggl(\frac{\ep^{h}}{\sqrt{h}}-\sqrt{\pi}\,\erfi(\sqrt{h})+\frac{\mathfrak{w}}{\sqrt{\s}}T\biggr)\) with \(\gamma = \frac{1}{2\pi^2}\Gamma^{4}(\tfrac{1}{4})\), where \(h = \s \rh^2\) and \(\mathfrak{w}\) is a model parameter.
- For the Schwarzschild black hole case (\(f(r)=1-(r/\rh)^4\)), the screening mass becomes \(\frac{M}{T} = \gamma\biggl(\exp\bigl((\T0/T)^2\bigr)-\sqrt{\pi}\frac{\T0}{T}\,\erfi(\T0/T)+\frac{\mathfrak{w}}{\pi}\biggr)\) with \(\T0 = \frac{\sqrt{\s}}{\pi}\).
- The value of \(\kappa\) fitted to lattice data for the three-quark potential is \(\kappa \approx -0.083\); the consistency condition \(\alpha_{\qq}/\alpha_{\qqb} = 1/2\) gives \(\kappa \approx -0.102\).

## Context

This work extends the effective five-dimensional string models of Andreev and Zakharov (2010) and Andreev (2010) to finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, using a charged black hole background. It provides a method to compute the Debye screening mass from Polyakov loop correlators, complementing holographic approaches based on gravity modes (Karch, 2006) and lattice QCD simulations (Karsch, 2002; Zantow, 2003). The results enable comparison between string model predictions and lattice data for screening masses at non-zero chemical potential, where lattice simulations are limited.
