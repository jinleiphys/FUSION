# First-order relativistic hydrodynamics with an information current

> We show that it is possible to define a timelike future-directed information current within relativistic first-order hydrodynamics. This constitutes the first step toward a covariantly stable and causal formulation of first-order fluctuating hydrodynamics based on thermodynamic principles. We provide several explicit examples of first-order theories with an information current, covering many physical phenomena, ranging from electric conduction to viscosity and elasticity. We use these information currents to compute the corresponding equal-time correlation functions, and we find that the physically relevant (equal-time) correlators do not depend on the choice of the hydrodynamic frame as long as the frame leads to causal and stable dynamics. In the example of chiral hydrodynamics, we find that circularly polarized shear waves have different probabilities of being excited depending on their handedness, generating net helicity in chiral fluids.

## Key claim
The authors show that a timelike future-directed information current can be defined for relativistic first-order hydrodynamics, but only by adding higher-order derivative terms that act as UV regulators; these terms are not fixed by the first-order truncation and must be chosen on a case-by-case basis to ensure the information current remains timelike future-directed at large gradients.

## Method
The authors construct quadratic information currents \(E^\mu\) and entropy production rates \(\sigma\) for several first-order theories by including second-order derivative terms of the form \(\partial\varphi\,\partial\varphi\) as UV regulators, while setting mixed terms \(\varphi\,\partial\partial\varphi\) to zero. They enforce that the equation \(\partial_\mu E^\mu + \sigma = 0\) holds exactly along solutions of the first-order equations of motion, and require \(E^\mu\) to be timelike future-directed and \(\sigma\) non-negative for arbitrary perturbations. The resulting information current is then used to compute equal-time correlation functions via Gaussian functional integrals in Fourier space.

## Key numbers
- For causal diffusion, stability requires \(\lambda > \tau > D > 0\), where \(\tau\) is the relaxation time, \(D\) the diffusion coefficient, and \(\lambda\) a free UV-regulator parameter.
- Causality for the diffusion equation demands \(\tau > D\).
- The density-density correlator for the conserved current \(J^0 = \varphi + \tau\partial_t\varphi\) reduces to \(\langle J^0(\mathbf{x}) J^0(\mathbf{y}) \rangle = \delta^3(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})\) when \(\lambda = \tau\).
- The field-field correlator decays as \(\langle \varphi(\mathbf{x})\,\varphi(\mathbf{y}) \rangle = e^{-a|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}|}/(4\pi \lambda D |\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}|)\) with \(a = \sqrt{(\lambda-\tau)/(\lambda^2 D)}\).

## Context
This work extends the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) approach to relativistic first-order hydrodynamics, which achieves stability and causality by adding non-Navier-Stokes derivative terms but fails to regularize stochastic fluctuations. The authors address the UV divergence problem identified by Mullins (2023) and Jain (2023), where BDNK theories produce divergent noise contributions. The construction enables a covariant theory of fluctuations via a relativistic generalization of the Fox-Uhlenbeck approach, with a follow-up paper (Gavassino, 2024) developing the full fluctuating theory.
