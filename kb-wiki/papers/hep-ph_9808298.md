# Hadronization of a Quark-Gluon Plasma in the Chromodielectric Model

> We have carried out simulations of the hadronization of a hot, ideal but effectively massive quark-gluon gas into color neutral clusters in the framework of the semi-classical SU(3) chromodielectric model. We have studied the possible quark-gluon compositions of clusters as well as the final mass distribution and spectra, aiming to obtain an insight into relations between hadronic spectral properties and the confinement mechanism in this model.

## Key claim

The authors simulate hadronization of a hot quark-gluon plasma using a semi-classical chromodielectric model, but the results depend on the specific choice of model parameters and the abelian approximation for color fields; the final state consists of irreducible white clusters whose masses are simply the sum of constituent masses, with no interaction contribution to ground-state energies.

## Method

The authors perform classical molecular dynamics simulations of approximately 400 quarks, antiquarks, and charged gluons treated as extended Gaussian charge distributions (RMS radius 0.7 fm) in a (25 fm)^3 lattice with a (40 fm)^3 computational domain. They solve the color Gauss law for the two neutral color fields (U(1)×U(1) abelian approximation) using an adaptive multigrid finite element method, while the scalar σ field is evolved via a leapfrog scheme on a 125^3 grid; magnetic fields are neglected. The simulation identifies irreducible white clusters (color-neutral with respect to both λ^3 and λ^8 charges) as hadrons at each timestep, removes them from the simulation, and records their properties.

## Key numbers

- Nonstrange quark mass: 400 MeV, yielding ρ-meson mass 800 MeV and nucleon mass 1200 MeV
- Strange quark mass: 550 MeV, gluon mass: 700 MeV
- String tension: approximately 1 GeV/fm, chosen to match quarkonium spectroscopy
- Bag constant B = (150 MeV)^4
- σ_vac = 0.31 fm^{-1}
- Lattice volume: (25 fm)^3 for σ field, (40 fm)^3 for color potential domain
- Timestep: 0.04 fm
- Charge distribution RMS radius: 0.7 fm
- CPU time per Gauss law solution: 15-200 seconds on a Pentium Pro 200 MHz machine
- Model hadron masses compared to experiment: N+Δ average 1200 MeV vs 1086 MeV (+10.5%), ρ 800 MeV vs 770 MeV (+3.9%), ω 800 MeV vs 782 MeV (+2.3%), K* 950 MeV vs 892 MeV (+6.5%), Φ 1100 MeV vs 1020 MeV (+7.8%), Ξ 1500 MeV vs 1530 MeV (-2.0%), Ω 1650 MeV vs 1672 MeV (-1.3%)

## Context

This work extends the chromodielectric model originally proposed by Friedberg and Lee, building on earlier stationary-state studies by Wilets, Ripka, Mosel, and Kalmbach, as well as real-time dynamical approaches by Vetter and Loh. The authors contrast their evolutionary approach with prompt hadronization models (LUND string model, parton fragmentation), equilibrium mixed-phase models (Toneev), and chemical rate-equation approaches (Knoll and Barz, ALCOR model by Zimanyi). The simulation enables event-by-event microscopic study of hadronization dynamics, cluster formation, and mass/rapidity/transverse momentum distributions, providing insight into the confinement mechanism until real-time QCD calculations become feasible.
