# Microscopic description of hexadecapole collectivity in even-even rare-earth nuclei near $N=90$

> We present an extensive study of hexadecapole correlations in the rare-earth region near $N=90$ and the effects these correlations have on various nuclear properties, such as the low-energy spectra, as well as quadrupole, hexadecapole, and monopole transition strengths. In order to examine hexadecapole correlations, we employ a mapped $sdg$ interacting boson model, with parameters derived from a self-consistent mean-field calculations with a relativistic energy density functional. We apply this model to even-even isotopes of Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy, and Er ($Z=60 - 68$) with neutron numbers $N=84-96$. The obtained results show a good agreement with the experiment. By comparing the results with the ones obtained from a simpler mapped $sd$ interacting boson model, we show that the inclusion of the hexadecapole degree of freedom via $g$ boson is necessary to improve the results of the $J^π \geq 6^{+}$ yrast energies in the nuclei with $N=84$ and 86, being near the neutron shell closure. The $sdg$ interacting boson model increases the quadrupole transition strengths between yrast states in the $N=90$ and 92 well deformed nuclei, which is in good agreement with the experiment for most of those isotopes. The presence of $g$ bosons does have an important effect on hexadecapole transition strengths, although experimental data for such transitions are limited. The obtained monopole transition strengths do not differ significantly from the ones obtained from the simpler $sd$ model.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the hexadecapole degree of freedom via the $g$ boson in the interacting boson model is necessary to improve the description of yrast energies for states with $J^{\pi} \geq 6^{+}$ in nuclei near the neutron shell closure ($N=84$ and 86), but the improvement is less significant for well-deformed nuclei with $N \geq 90$.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using the multidimensionally constrained relativistic mean-field model with the DD-PC1 energy density functional to obtain quadrupole-hexadecapole potential energy surfaces for axially symmetric shapes. They then mapped these microscopic energy surfaces onto the $sdg$ interacting boson model (IBM-1) Hamiltonian by fitting the IBM parameters to reproduce the SCMF potential energy surfaces in the vicinity of the minimum. The resulting $sdg$-IBM was applied to calculate excitation energies and electric quadrupole, hexadecapole, and monopole transition strengths for even-even isotopes of Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy, and Er with neutron numbers $N=84-96$, and results were compared to those from a simpler mapped $sd$-IBM.

## Key numbers

- The largest hexadecapole deformation in the minimum was $\beta_4^{\text{min}}=0.25$ for $^{152,154}\text{Nd}$ and $^{154}\text{Sm}$; for Gd, Dy, and Er isotopes the largest was $\beta_4^{\text{min}}=0.15$ in the $N \geq 90$ region.
- The $g$ boson energy parameter $\epsilon_g$ fluctuated between 1.0 and 1.3 MeV across the studied isotopes.
- For $^{152}\text{Er}$, the $sdg$-IBM predicted an energy ratio $R_{4/2}=1.54$ compared to the experimental value of 1.83, while the $sd$-IBM gave $R_{4/2}=2.14$.
- The $sdg$-IBM predicted $R_{4/2} < 2$ for $N=84$ nuclei (e.g., $^{144}\text{Nd}$: 1.78 vs. experimental 1.89; $^{148}\text{Gd}$: 1.86 vs. experimental 1.81), which the $sd$-IBM could not reproduce.
- For $^{160}\text{Dy}$, the observed $0^+$ level at 1280 keV was identified as the bandhead of the first excited $K=0^+$ band.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on hexadecapole collectivity in Gd isotopes (Lotina 2024) to a wider range of rare-earth nuclei near $N=90$. It builds upon earlier mean-field studies that found non-zero $\beta_{40}$ deformations in this region, including work by Nazarewicz (1981) using an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential, Ganioglu (2014) using total Routhian surface calculations, and Kumar-Robledo (2023) using the generator coordinate method with the Gogny energy density functional. The results enable future studies of hexadecapole correlations in other mass regions and could be extended by considering more independent Hamiltonian parameters or different energy density functionals.
