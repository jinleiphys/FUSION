# The Dipion Mass Spectrum In e+e- Annihilation and tau Decay: A Dynamical (rho0, omega, phi) Mixing Approach

> We readdress the problem of finding a simultaneous description of the pion form factor data in e+e- annihilations and in tau decays. For this purpose, we work in the framework of the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian and modify the vector meson mass term by including the pion and kaon loop contributions. This leads us to define the physical rho, omega and phi fields as linear combinations of their ideal partners, with coefficients being meromorphic functions of s, the square of the 4--momentum flowing into the vector meson lines. This allows us to define a dynamical, i.e. s-dependent, vector meson mixing scheme. The model is overconstrained by extending the framework in order to include the description of all meson radiative (V P gamma and P gamma gamma couplings) and leptonic (Ve+e- couplings) decays and also the isospin breaking (omega/ phi --> pi+ pi-) decay modes. The model provides a simultaneous, consistent and good description of the e+e- and tau dipion spectra. The expression for pion form factor in the latter case is derived from those in the former case by switching off the isospin breaking effects specific to e+e- and switching on those for tau decays. Besides, the model also provides a good account of all decay modes of the form V P gamma, Pgamma gamma as well as the isospin breaking decay modes. It leads us to propose new reference values for the rho^0 --> e+ e- and omega --> pi+ pi- partial widths which are part of our description of the pion form factor. Other topics (phi --> K anti K, the rho meson mass and width parameters) are briefly discussed. Therefore, we confirm the 3.3 sigma discrepancy between the theoretical estimate of a_mu based on e+e- and its direct BNL measurement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simultaneous, consistent description of the pion form factor in both \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and \(\tau\) decay can be achieved using a dynamical (s-dependent) \(\rho^0,\omega,\phi\) mixing scheme within the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian, but only if isospin breaking effects specific to each channel are properly switched on or off; the work confirms the 3.3\(\sigma\) discrepancy between the theoretical estimate of the muon anomalous magnetic moment \(a_\mu\) derived from \(e^+e^-\) data and its direct BNL measurement.

## Method

The authors start from the HLS Lagrangian and modify the vector meson mass term by including pion and kaon loop contributions, which generates s-dependent transition amplitudes among the ideal \(\rho^0_I,\omega_I,\phi_I\) fields. They diagonalize the resulting s-dependent squared mass matrix to define physical fields as linear combinations of ideal fields with meromorphic coefficients, and extend the framework to include radiative decays (\(VP\gamma\), \(P\gamma\gamma\)), leptonic decays (\(Ve^+e^-\)), and isospin-breaking decays (\(\omega/\phi \to \pi^+\pi^-\)). The model is overconstrained by fitting simultaneously the pion form factor in \(e^+e^-\) and \(\tau\) data, with the \(\tau\) form factor derived from the \(e^+e^-\) expression by switching off \(e^+e^-\)-specific isospin breaking and switching on \(\tau\)-specific isospin breaking effects.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(f_K/f_\pi\) is constrained to \(1.495 \pm 0.031\) from the Review of Particle Properties (2006).
* The CKM matrix element \(V_{ud}\) is fixed to \(0.97377 \pm 0.00027\).
* The parameter \(\epsilon = (m_{\pi^0}^2 - m_{\pi^+}^2)/m_{\pi^+}^2 \simeq -0.06455\) accounts for the pion mass difference in \(\tau\) decay kinematics.
* The HLS parameter \(a\) is favored to be \(\simeq 2.4 \div 2.5\) from previous phenomenological studies of pion form factor data and light meson decays.
* The paper confirms a 3.3\(\sigma\) discrepancy between the theoretical estimate of \(a_\mu\) based on \(e^+e^-\) data and the direct BNL measurement.

## Context

This work extends previous HLS-based studies of vector meson mixing (Benayoun et al., 2004; mixing, 2005) by implementing a fully s-dependent mixing scheme for \(\rho^0,\omega,\phi\) derived from one-loop effects, rather than using constant mixing angles. It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the isospin-1 part of the pion form factor in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and \(\tau\) decay, which has implications for the muon \(g-2\) puzzle; prior analyses by Davier (2007) and Maltman (2006) had questioned whether isospin breaking effects were adequately treated. The model enables a unified description of radiative, leptonic, and isospin-violating decays alongside the dipion spectra, and provides new reference values for the \(\rho^0 \to e^+e^-\) and \(\omega \to \pi^+\pi^-\) partial widths.
