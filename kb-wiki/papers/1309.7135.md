# Re-analysis of the $Λ(1520)$ photoproduction reaction

> Based on previous studies that support the important role of the $N^*(2120) D_{13}$ resonance in the $γp \to K^+ Λ(1520)$ reaction, we make a re-analysis of this $Λ(1520)$ photoproduction reaction taking into account the recent CLAS differential cross-section data. In addition to the contact, $t-$channel $\bar K$ exchange, $s-$channel nucleon pole and $N^*(2120)$ [previously called $N^*(2080)$] resonance contributions, which have been already considered in previous works, we also study the $u-$channel $Λ(1115)$ hyperon pole term. The latter mechanism has always been ignored in all theoretical analysis, which has mostly relied on the very forward $K^+$ angular LEPS data. It is shown that when the contributions from the $N^*(2120)$ resonance and the $Λ(1115)$ hyperon are taken into account, both the new CLAS and the previous LEPS data can be simultaneously described. We also show that the contribution from the $u-$channel $Λ(1115)$ pole term produces an enhancement for large $K^+$ angles, and it becomes more and more relevant as the photon energy increases, being essential to describe the CLAS differential cross sections at backward angles. Furthermore, we find that the new CLAS data also favor the existence of the $N^*(2120)$ resonance, and that these measurements can be used to further constrain its properties.

## Key claim

The authors show that including the previously ignored $u$-channel $\Lambda(1115)$ hyperon pole term, together with the $N^*(2120)$ resonance contribution, allows simultaneous description of both the new CLAS differential cross-section data (spanning a wide $K^+$ angular range) and the earlier LEPS forward-angle data, whereas previous models that omitted the $\Lambda(1115)$ term could not describe the CLAS backward-angle measurements.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach combined with an isobar model to compute the $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1520)$ reaction amplitude. They included contact, $t$-channel $\bar K$ exchange, $s$-channel nucleon pole, $s$-channel $N^*(2120)$ resonance, and $u$-channel $\Lambda(1115)$ hyperon pole contributions, fitting the free parameters (masses, widths, coupling constants, and cutoff parameters) to the CLAS and LEPS differential cross-section data via $\chi^2$ minimization.

## Key numbers

- $N^*(2120)$ resonance mass: $2135 \pm 4$ MeV (Fit II, combined CLAS+LEPS fit)
- $N^*(2120)$ resonance total width: $184 \pm 11$ MeV (Fit II)
- $N^*(2120) \to \Lambda^* K$ partial width: $30 \pm 8$ MeV (Fit II)
- $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 2.5$ for both Fit I (CLAS only) and Fit II (CLAS+LEPS)
- $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 9.9$ when both $N^*(2120)$ and $\Lambda(1115)$ contributions are omitted (Fit II)
- $\Lambda(1520) \to \Lambda \gamma$ radiative width: $0.133$ MeV (taken from PDG)
- $h_1$ coupling constant: $0.64 \pm 0.05$ (Fit II)
- $h_2$ coupling constant: $-0.38 \pm 0.07$ (Fit II)
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda_B = 620 \pm 2$ MeV (Fit II)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Xie et al. (2013) that previously described only the LEPS forward-angle data using the $N^*(2120)$ resonance, and addresses the failure of earlier models by Nam et al. (2012) and He et al. (2013) to describe the recent CLAS data from Moriya et al. (2013). The inclusion of the $u$-channel $\Lambda(1115)$ term, previously ignored in all theoretical analyses, enables description of backward-angle CLAS measurements and provides further constraints on the $N^*(2120)$ resonance properties, supporting its existence as predicted by constituent quark models (Simon 1998) and chiral unitary approaches (Gamermann 2011, Oset 2009).
