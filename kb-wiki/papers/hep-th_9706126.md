# Flavor independence and the dual superconducting model of QCD

> Baker, Ball and Zachariasen have developed an elegant formulation of the dual superconducting model of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), which allows one to use the field equations to eliminate the gluon and Higgs degrees of freedom and thus to express the interaction between quarks as an effective potential. Carrying out an expansion in inverse powers of the constituent quark masses, these authors succeeded in identifying the central part, the spin-dependent part and the leading relativistic corrections to the central potential. The potential offers a good account of the energies and splittings of charmonium and the upsilon system. Since all of the flavor dependence of the interaction is presumed to enter through the constituent masses, it is possible to test the potential in other systems. Logical candidates are the heavy B-flavor charmed system and the heavy-light systems, which should be more sensitive to the relativistic corrections. Lattice gauge calculations furnish an additional point of contact for the components of the BBZ potential. Some preliminary calculations of the energies of B and D mesons are presented and the challenge of agreement with experiment is discussed. The spinless Salpeter equation is used to account for the effects of relativistic kinematics.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the dual superconducting model of QCD (the BBZ potential) can simultaneously describe heavy quark systems (charmonium, upsilon) and heavy-light systems (B and D mesons) using a single set of potential parameters, finding that the full BBZ potentials fail to achieve such a simultaneous description when using the spinless Salpeter equation.

## Method

The author solved the spinless Salpeter equation with the central potential from the BBZ model using a Rayleigh-Ritz-Galerkin method with basis functions of associated Laguerre polynomials, then added the velocity-dependent corrections (V_{v^2}) via first-order perturbation theory. Parameters were determined by fitting the spin-averaged 1S, 2S and 1P energies of the upsilon system and the 1S energy of charmonium, and then the same parameters were used to predict masses of B and D mesons as functions of the light quark constituent mass.

## Context

This work extends the dual superconducting model of QCD developed by Baker, Ball and Zachariasen (BBZ) (1991-1995), who constructed an effective potential from the field equations by expanding in inverse powers of constituent quark masses. The paper tests flavor independence by applying the BBZ potential to heavy-light systems (B and D mesons) beyond the charmonium and upsilon systems where it was originally successful. The results are compared with lattice gauge calculations by Bali, Schilling and Wachter (1997) for the velocity-dependent potential V_c, though the lattice data have error bars too large to distinguish between different forms of the longitudinal potential V_L. The author concludes that the full BBZ potentials cannot simultaneously account for heavy quark and heavy-light systems with a single parameter set, suggesting that calculations using the formalism of Hardekopf and Sucher (1984) might resolve the discrepancy.
