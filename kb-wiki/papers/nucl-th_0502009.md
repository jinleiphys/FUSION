# J/Psi strong couplings to the vector mesons

> We present a study of the cross sections J/Psi X --> D^(*) \bar D^(*) (X = rho, Phi) based on the calculation of the effective tri- and four-linear couplings J/Psi (X) D^(*) \bar D^(*) within a constituent quark model. In particular, the details of the calculation of the four-linear couplings J/Psi X D^(*)\bar D^(*) are given. The results obtained have been used in a recent analysis of J/Psi absorption by the hot hadron gas formed in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the effective tri- and four-linear strong couplings of J/Psi with rho and Phi mesons to charmed meson pairs (D, D*) within a constituent quark model, finding that the resulting cross sections for J/Psi dissociation by rho mesons are of the same order as those for pion-induced dissociation, but the higher spin multiplicity of the rho makes its contribution important for J/Psi absorption in hot hadron gas. The authors note that the predicted cross sections show very different energy dependence and magnitude near threshold depending on the approach used, and that no experimental test can be performed on these processes.

## Method

The authors compute the effective couplings using the Constituent-Quark-Meson (CQM) model, which incorporates heavy quark spin-flavor symmetries and chiral symmetry in the light sector, with the J/Psi and rho introduced via Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) Ansatz. The four-linear couplings J/Psi rho D(*) anti-D(*) are obtained by computing a one-loop diagram where the J/Psi couples to the heavy quark line and the rho couples to the light quark line, with the loop regularized using Schwinger proper time with infrared cutoff mu approximately equal to the constituent light quark mass and ultraviolet cutoff Lambda approximately 4 pi f_pi. The cross sections for the processes J/Psi rho -> D(*) anti-D(*) and J/Psi Phi -> D_s(*) anti-D_s(*) are then computed at tree level using the extracted tri- and four-linear couplings.

## Key numbers

- Constituent light quark mass for u,d: 300 MeV (from gap equation with zero current mass)
- Constituent strange quark mass: 500 MeV (from gap equation with current mass m_0 = 131 MeV)
- rho decay constant f_rho = 0.152 GeV^2
- J/Psi decay constant f_J = 0.405 GeV
- Phi decay constant f_Phi = 0.249 GeV^2
- Tri-linear coupling beta for rho D(*) anti-D(*): beta = -0.98
- Tri-linear coupling lambda for rho D(*) anti-D(*): lambda = +0.42 GeV^{-1}
- Tri-linear coupling beta for Phi D_s(*) anti-D_s(*): beta = -0.48
- Tri-linear coupling lambda for Phi D_s(*) anti-D_s(*): lambda = +0.14 GeV^{-1}
- J/Psi D D coupling g_{J/Psi DD} = 8.0 +/- 0.5
- J/Psi D* D* coupling g_{J/Psi D* D*} = 8.0 +/- 0.5
- J/Psi D D* coupling g_{J/Psi DD*} = 4.05 +/- 0.25 GeV^{-1}
- Energy threshold for J/Psi rho -> D anti-D and D* anti-D channels: E_rho approximately 0.77 GeV
- Energy threshold for J/Psi rho -> D* anti-D* channel: E_rho approximately 0.96 GeV
- Temperature for thermal averages in hadron gas: T approximately 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Constituent-Quark-Meson model calculations previously applied to J/Psi dissociation by pions (Piccinini, Polosa, Riquer, 2004) and to rho D(*) anti-D(*) tri-linear couplings (Casalbuoni et al.), providing the four-linear couplings J/Psi rho D(*) anti-D(*) not calculated elsewhere within the CQM framework. The results are used in a recent analysis of J/Psi absorption by the hot hadron gas formed in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies (Ioni, Ioni2), demonstrating that the rho contribution is important alongside the pion contribution for understanding the hadronic absorption background to the J/Psi suppression signal in heavy-ion collisions.
