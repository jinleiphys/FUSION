# An intrinsic-state formalism for the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model with configuration mixing

> In this letter, we present the intrinsic state formalism of the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model incorporating both 0p-0h and 2p-2h (regular and intruder) configurations. The framework allows to deal with the unpaired fermion in either a single-j or multi-j orbital and is applicable to both axial and triaxial nuclear shapes. The formalism is applied to a schematic transitional region where regular and intruder configurations coexist and cross. We show that the boson-fermion part of the Hamiltonian can either enhance or suppress the emergence of a type II Quantum Phase Transition.

## Key claim

The paper presents an intrinsic-state formalism for the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM) that can handle both single-j and multi-j orbitals as well as axial and triaxial nuclear shapes; the authors show that the boson-fermion part of the Hamiltonian can either enhance or suppress the emergence of a type II Quantum Phase Transition, but emphasize that this behavior is not universal and depends on the particular choice of boson-fermion interaction.

## Method

The authors construct an intrinsic-state formalism by extending the IBM-CM intrinsic state to include a fermion degree of freedom, building a matrix of dimension 2 times the sum over j of (2j+1) that couples regular (0p-0h) and intruder (2p-2h) boson configurations. The Hamiltonian includes boson, fermion, and boson-fermion terms (monopole, quadrupole, and exchange components), and the energy surfaces are obtained by diagonalizing the resulting matrix as a function of the deformation parameters beta and gamma. The formalism is applied to a schematic isotopic chain from A=172 to A=192 with a regular U(5) spherical configuration and an intruder SU(3) deformed configuration, using a multi-j shell with j=1/2, 3/2, 5/2.

## Key numbers

- The regular configuration uses epsilon^N = 1 MeV with all other regular parameters set to zero; the intruder configuration uses kappa^{N+2} = -0.044 MeV and chi^{N+2} = -sqrt(7)/2.
- The mixing interaction strength is omega = 0.15 MeV, and the energy offset for the intruder configuration is Delta^{N+2} = 14.2 MeV.
- The boson number evolves from N=8 at A=172 to N=13 at mid-shell (A=182) and back to N=8 at A=192; the crossing of boson configurations occurs between A=176 and A=178 (N=10 and N=11).
- The boson-fermion parameters used are A_0 = 1.0 MeV, Gamma_0 = -5.0 MeV, Lambda_0 = 1.0 MeV, with chi^N = -chi^{N+2} = sqrt(7)/2 and all fermion single-particle energies set to 0 MeV.
- The occupation probability uses N_p = 7 for the proton hole.

## Context

This work extends the intrinsic-state formalism previously developed for the IBM-CM by Frank et al. (2002, 2004, 2006) and for the IBFM without configuration mixing by Levi et al. (1988) and Alon et al. (1992), as well as the IBFM studies of Quantum Phase Transitions by Iachello et al. (2011) and Petrellis et al. (2011). The formalism enables the study of shape coexistence and intruder band formation in odd-mass nuclei, and the authors note that a similar work by Levi (2025) appeared while completing this manuscript. The approach can be used to investigate how the odd fermion shifts the mass number at which a type II QPT occurs relative to the purely bosonic case.
