# Multi-channel S-matrices from energy levels in finite boxes

> We show that for a generic quantum mechanical system with more than one open scattering channel, it is not possible to fully reconstruct the theory's S-matrix from spectral information obtained in large finite volumes with periodic boundary conditions. Physically distinct S-matrices can have identical finite-volume spectra for large finite boxes of arbitrary sizes. If the theory is not time-reversal symmetric, there exists an uncountably infinite set of distinct S-matrices with the same spectra. If the theory respects time-reversal symmetry there exists a discrete set of S-matrices with identical energy levels for finite boxes. We illustrate the issue for simple quantum mechanical systems in 1+1 dimensions.

## Key claim

It is not possible to fully reconstruct a multi-channel S-matrix from finite-volume energy spectra alone, even with access to arbitrarily many box sizes; physically distinct S-matrices can yield identical spectra in large periodic boxes, and for time-reversal-symmetric theories there remains a discrete sign ambiguity in the off-diagonal matrix elements.

## Method

The authors study a nonrelativistic two-channel scattering problem in 1+1 dimensions with two identical bosons per channel and an energy offset Δ between channels. They derive a generalized Lüscher formula for this toy model, det[B_L(E) - S(E)] = 0, where B_L is a diagonal matrix encoding periodic boundary conditions. They then analyze the U(2) parameterization of the S-matrix and show that a transformation D†S D with diagonal D leaves the determinant condition invariant, demonstrating that the β parameter (associated with time-reversal-noninvariant dynamics) cannot be determined from energy levels. For time-reversal-symmetric systems, a discrete ambiguity remains under a specific transformation with χ = -π/2.

## Key numbers

- The S-matrix for a two-channel system is parameterized by 4 angle variables: φ, θ, α, and β, with ranges 0 ≤ φ < 2π, 0 ≤ θ < 2π, 0 ≤ α < π, and -π/2 ≤ β < π/2.
- The transformation χ(E) = -π/2 applied to a time-reversal-symmetric S-matrix yields a distinct symmetric S-matrix with identical finite-volume spectra, demonstrating a discrete ambiguity.
- The probability difference P_R - P_L for particle B₁ emerging right versus left in the 1+1 dimensional example equals Re(S⁺_{AB}(E) S⁻_{AB}(E)*), which flips sign if either off-diagonal element changes sign.

## Context

This work extends the foundational Lüscher formula (Lüscher, 1986, 1991) from single-channel to multi-channel scattering, directly contradicting the implicit assumption that finite-volume spectra uniquely determine the S-matrix. It builds on recent generalizations by Polejaeva (2012), Lage (2009), Hansen (2012), and Bernard (2012), but demonstrates a fundamental limitation: the sign of off-diagonal S-matrix elements cannot be extracted from spectral data. This has direct implications for lattice QCD studies of resonances like the Roper resonance and for coupled-channel scattering such as the ³S₁-³D₁ nucleon-nucleon channel, where the sign ambiguity affects physical observables like differential cross sections.
