# Low-lying ud anti-s anti-s configurations in a non-relativistic constituent quark model

> The energies of the low-lying isoscalar and isovector ud anti-s anti-s configurations with spin-parity J^P=0^+, 1^+, and 2^+ are calculated in a non-relativistic constituent quark model by use of the variational method. The contributions of various parts of the quark-quark interacting potentials including the s-channel interaction are investigated, and the effect of different forms of confinement potential is examined. The model parameters are determined by the same method as in our previous work, and they still can satisfactorily describe the nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and the hyperon-nucleon cross sections. The parameters of the s-channel interaction are fixed by the masses of K and K^* mesons, for which the size parameter is taken to be two possible values. When it is chosen as the same as baryons', the numerical results show that the masses of all the ud anti-s anti-s configurations are higher than the corresponding meson-meson thresholds. But when the size parameter for the K and K^* mesons is adjusted to be smaller than that for the baryons, the ud anti-s anti-s configuration with I=0 and J^P=1^+ is found to lie lower than the K^*K^* threshold, furthermore, this state has a very small KK^* component and the interaction matrix elements between this state and KK^* is comparatively small, thus its coupling to the KK^* channel will consequently be weak and it might be regarded as a possible tetraquark candidate.

## Key claim

When the size parameter for the K and K* mesons is adjusted to be smaller than that for baryons (0.4 fm instead of 0.5 fm), the ud anti-s anti-s configuration with isospin I=0 and spin-parity J^P=1^+ lies lower than the K*K* threshold and has a very small KK* component, making it a possible tetraquark candidate; however, when the size parameter is taken the same as baryons', all configurations lie above their corresponding meson-meson thresholds.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, confinement (linear form), one-gluon exchange, boson exchanges (scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector nonets), and s-channel quark-antiquark interactions (K and K* exchanges). They performed variational calculations for six spatially symmetric ud anti-s anti-s configurations with J^P=0^+, 1^+, and 2^+, considering configuration mixing between states with identical quantum numbers, and examined three different model parameter sets (with and without vector meson exchanges) and three forms of confinement potential.

## Key numbers

- The isovector J^P=0^+ state after configuration mixing (size parameter same as baryons) has energy 1394 MeV in Model I, 1483 MeV in Model II, and 1482 MeV in Model III, all above the KK threshold of 990 MeV.
- The isoscalar J^P=1^+ state after configuration mixing (size parameter same as baryons) has energy 1567 MeV in Model I, 1619 MeV in Model II, and 1615 MeV in Model III, all above the KK* threshold of 1387 MeV.
- With the size parameter of K and K* taken as 0.4 fm, the isoscalar J^P=1^+ state in Model I has energy 1768 MeV, which is below the K*K* threshold of 1784 MeV.
- The root mean square radius of this 1768 MeV state is about 0.57 fm.
- In the wave function of this state, the KK* component is only 5.8%, while the K*K* component is 50.4% and two color-octet q anti-q pairs contribute 43.8%.
- The interaction matrix elements between this state and the KK* channel are less than 6 MeV and approach zero with increasing distance.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-relativistic constituent quark model studies of baryon-baryon scattering and pentaquark states to the tetraquark ud anti-s anti-s system. It contrasts with earlier predictions by Jaffe (1977) using the MIT bag model (masses 1.55 GeV for isovector J^P=0^+ and 1.65 GeV for isoscalar J^P=1^+), and with Kanada-En'yo et al. (2005) who found a stable isoscalar J^P=1^+ state around 1.4 GeV in a flux-tube quark model. The results also differ from Cui et al. (2006) who found a 1347 MeV tetraquark using color-magnetic interactions, and from Vijande et al. (2004) who found no stable tetraquark. The finding of a possible narrow tetraquark candidate near 1768 MeV enables future experimental searches in channels such as K+ d to p + p + K- + T+ or J/psi (Upsilon) to K- + anti-K0 + T+.
