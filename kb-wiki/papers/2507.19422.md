# Adressing the pΩ- interaction and di-baryonic states via femtoscopy

> Motivated by recent experimental measurements of the p$Ω^-$ correlation function and the concurrent theoretical efforts to describe the strong interaction among hadrons in the strangeness sector, we present a data-driven approach for fine tuning of a meson-exchanges potential for the p$Ω^-$ system. Using femtoscopy data from the ALICE and STAR collaborations, we constrain the strength of the interaction, encoded in the tunable short-range parameter introduced in the potential. The resulting model provides a good description of the measured correlation functions and favors the existence of a bound state in the \({}^5S_2\) channel with a binding energy of approximately \(0.5\,\mathrm{MeV}\). The role of the \({}^3S_1\) channel, however, remains poorly constrained due to the absence of an accurate model accounting for its inelastic contributions.

# pΩ⁻ Interaction and Di-baryonic States via Femtoscopy

## Key claim

The authors use femtoscopy data from ALICE and STAR to tune a meson-exchange potential for the pΩ⁻ system, finding that a bound state in the ⁵S₂ channel with binding energy ~0.5 MeV is favored only when the ³S₁ channel is treated as fully absorptive; in the purely elastic scenario for the ³S₁ channel, no bound state is supported.

## Method

The authors take a meson-exchange potential for the ⁵S₂ channel from Sekihara, Kamiya and Hyodo, originally fitted to lattice QCD scattering lengths, and introduce a free tuning parameter β that scales the short-range contact term. They compute femtoscopic correlation functions via the Koonin-Pratt formula using the CATS framework, then perform a simultaneous fit of β to pΩ⁻ correlation functions measured by ALICE in pp collisions and by STAR in Au-Au collisions, treating the ³S₁ channel under two limiting assumptions (purely elastic or fully absorptive).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy B ~ 0.5 MeV for the ⁵S₂ channel, obtained when the ³S₁ channel is treated as fully absorptive (β = 1.03⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀·₀₃)
- Fitted short-range parameter β = 0.22⁺⁰·⁰⁶₋₀·₀₆ for the purely elastic ³S₁ scenario; β = 1.03⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀·₀₃ for the fully absorptive ³S₁ scenario
- Critical β threshold for bound state formation: β = 0.92 (where inverse scattering length 1/a₀ = 0)
- ALICE source size: r = 0.89 ± 0.05 fm; STAR source sizes assumed: 2.5 ± 0.5 fm (peripheral) and 4 ± 1 fm (central Au-Au collisions)
- Normalization parameters for inelastic scenario: n_ALICE = 1.14⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀·₀₂, n_STAR = 0.96⁺⁰·⁰⁵₋₀·⁰⁵
- Normalization parameters for elastic scenario: n_ALICE = 0.97⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀·₀₂, n_STAR = 0.95⁺⁰·⁰⁷₋₀·⁰⁷

## Context

This work extends meson-exchange model calculations by Sekihara, Kamiya and Hyodo and lattice QCD results from the HAL QCD collaboration, both of which predicted a shallow bound state in the ⁵S₂ channel with binding energies around 0.1–2.46 MeV. The authors use experimental femtoscopy data from ALICE and STAR to constrain the interaction, rather than fitting to lattice potentials. The results highlight that the ³S₁ channel remains poorly constrained and that future measurements of ΛΞ⁻ correlations or pΩ⁻ correlations in systems with varying source sizes (e.g., O-O or Ne-Ne collisions at the LHC) could help resolve the bound state question.
