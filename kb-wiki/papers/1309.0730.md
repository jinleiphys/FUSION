# Valence quark and meson cloud contributions to the $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1520)$ form factors

> The covariant spectator quark model is applied to the $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1520)$ reaction. The valence quark contributions to the electromagnetic form factors and helicity transition amplitudes are estimated from the covariant structure of the $N^\ast(1520)$ wave function calibrated by the experimental data for large squared momentum transfer $Q^2$. The difference between the model results and the experimental data is then used to parameterize the low $Q^2$ behavior, where meson cloud effects are assumed to dominate. This parametrization can be very useful for future studies of the reaction, as well as for the extension of the transition form factors to the timelike region.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, which describes the valence quark core of the nucleon and the $N^\ast(1520)$ resonance, underestimates the helicity amplitudes at low squared momentum transfer $Q^2$, indicating that meson cloud effects (meson exchange between different quarks, not included in the quark dressing) dominate in that region; the difference between the model and experimental data is then parametrized to represent those meson cloud contributions.

## Method

The authors applied the covariant spectator quark model in the relativistic impulse approximation, where only one quark interacts with the photon while the other two act as spectators via a quark-diquark structure. The $N^\ast(1520)$ wave function was constructed as a superposition of two configurations with core spin 1/2 and core spin 3/2, with the mixing parameter $\sin \theta_D \approx 0.1$ favoring the spin-1/2 configuration, and the radial wave functions were constrained by orthogonality to the nucleon. The valence quark contributions to the electromagnetic form factors $G_M$, $G_E$, $G_C$ and helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$, $A_{3/2}$, $S_{1/2}$ were calculated for spacelike $Q^2$, and the residual between model results and experimental data at low $Q^2$ was parametrized as meson cloud contributions.

## Key numbers

* The mixing coefficient for the $S=3/2$ configuration in the $N^\ast(1520)$ wave function is $\sin \theta_D \approx 0.1$, indicating dominance of the $S=1/2$ configuration.
* The $N^\ast(1520)$ decays to $\pi N$ (60%) and $\pi \pi N$ (40%), motivating the importance of meson cloud effects.
* The quark current parameters used are $c_+=4.16$, $c_-=1.16$, $d_+=d_-=-0.686$, $\lambda_q=1.21$, $\kappa_+=1.639$, and $\kappa_-=1.833$, with $m_v = m_\rho$ and $M_h = 2M$ (twice the nucleon mass).
* The helicity amplitude $A_{3/2}$ is nonzero at low $Q^2$, requiring $G_4 \ne 0$ in the formalism.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to the nucleon (Ramalho and Pena, 2010), $\Delta(1232)$ (Ramalho and Pena, 2011), $N^\ast(1440)$ (Ramalho and Pena, 2012), and $N^\ast(1535)$ (Ramalho and Pena, 2013), to the $N^\ast(1520)$ resonance. The results confirm that meson cloud effects are important at low $Q^2$, as was also found for the $\Delta(1232)$ in dynamical coupled-channel analyses by EBAC (Diaz, 2008) and in Dyson-Schwinger calculations (Gernot, 2010). The parametrization of meson cloud contributions enables future extension of the transition form factors to the timelike region, relevant for interpreting dilepton production data from $NN$ collisions (Weil, 2012; Agakishiev, 2014).
