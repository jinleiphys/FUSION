# Bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> In this proceedings contribution I review recent progress concerning the suppression of bottomonium production in the quark-gluon plasma. Making use of open quantum system methods applied to potential non-relativistic quantum chromodynamics one can show that the dynamics of heavy-quarkonium bound states satisfying the scale hierarchy 1/a_0 >> pi T ~ m_D >> E obey a Lindblad equation whose solution provides the quantum evolution of the heavy-quarkonium reduced density matrix. To solve the resulting Lindblad equation we use a quantum trajectories algorithm which allows one to include all possible angular momentum states of the quark-antiquark probe in a scalable manner. We solve the Lindblad equation using a tuned 3+1D dissipative hydrodynamics code for the background temperature evolution. We then consider a large number of Monte-Carlo sampled bottomonium trajectories embedded in this background. This allows us to extract the centrality- and p_T-dependence of the nuclear suppression factor R_AA[Upsilon] and elliptic flow v_2[Upsilon]. We find good agreement between our model predictions and available sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collision experimental data from the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations.

## Key claim

The open quantum system approach to bottomonium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma, truncated at leading order in the ratio of binding energy to temperature (E/T), yields predictions for the nuclear suppression factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 of Upsilon states that agree with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, but only within the theoretical uncertainties from the transport coefficients kappa and gamma.

## Method

The authors applied open quantum system methods within potential non-relativistic quantum chromodynamics (pNRQCD) to obtain a Lindblad equation for the heavy-quarkonium reduced density matrix, valid under the scale hierarchy 1/a_0 >> pi T ~ m_D >> E. They solved this equation using a quantum trajectories algorithm (QTraj 1.0) that includes all angular momentum states, coupled with a tuned 3+1D dissipative anisotropic hydrodynamics code (aHydro3p1) for the background temperature evolution, and sampled millions of Monte-Carlo bottomonium trajectories through the evolving QGP medium.

## Key numbers

- The transport coefficient kappa was varied over three temperature-dependent parameterizations: hat_kappa(T) = kappa(T)/T^3 in {hat_kappa_L(T), hat_kappa_C(T), hat_kappa_U(T)} taken from Brambilla (2020).
- The transport coefficient gamma was varied over three values: hat_gamma = gamma/T^3 = {-3.5, -1.75, 0} based on indirect lattice extractions from Brambilla (2019), Kim (2018), Aarts (2011), Larsen (2019), and Shi (2021).
- The 2S to 1S double ratio of R_AA was found to be insensitive to variations in hat_kappa but quite sensitive to variations in hat_gamma, suggesting gamma could be constrained from fits to experimental data.
- The elliptic flow v_2 of Upsilon(1S) was predicted for three centrality classes and compared to CMS measurements, with the centrality-integrated result showing small systematic/statistical uncertainties consistent with CMS data within experimental uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends earlier bottomonium transport models by Grandchamp (2005), Emerick (2011), Du (2017), and Du (2019) by including explicit quantum dynamics and internal color and angular momentum transitions, rather than relying solely on complex-valued potential models as in Strickland (2011), Krouppa (2015), and Islam (2020). The approach enables future inclusion of sub-leading corrections at order (E/T)^1, which will allow extension to lower temperatures where E/T is larger and go beyond the recoilless limit.
