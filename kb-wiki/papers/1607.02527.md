# Kaon fluctuations from lattice QCD

> We show that it is possible to isolate a set of kaon fluctuations in lattice QCD. By means of the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, we calculate the actual kaon second-to-first fluctuation ratio, which receives contribution from primordial kaons and resonance decays, and show that it is very close to the one obtained for primordial kaons in the Boltzmann approximation. The latter only involves the strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials, which are functions of $T$ and $μ_B$ due to the experimental constraint on strangeness and electric charge, and can therefore be calculated on the lattice. This provides an unambiguous method to extract the kaon freeze-out temperature, by comparing the lattice results to the experimental values for the corresponding fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the second-to-first fluctuation ratio for kaons, calculated in the Boltzmann approximation using only strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials, is very close to the actual ratio that includes contributions from both primordial kaons and resonance decays, enabling a lattice QCD extraction of the kaon freeze-out temperature. This agreement holds for lower-order fluctuations but breaks down for higher-order ratios like χ₃/χ₂.

## Method

The authors use the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to calculate kaon fluctuations, comparing two approaches: (1) the full calculation including primordial kaons and resonance decay feed-down using decay probabilities, and (2) the Boltzmann approximation where only the k=1 term in the partition function expansion is kept. They then show that the Boltzmann-approximated ratio χ₂^K/χ₁^K depends only on the combination (μ_S + μ_Q)/T, which can be computed on the lattice via Taylor expansion or analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials. The same analysis is repeated for protons using χ₂^p/χ₁^p.

## Key numbers

- At μ_B = 20 MeV and μ_B = 420 MeV, the Boltzmann approximation for χ₂^K/χ₁^K agrees well with the full HRG calculation including resonance decays across the temperature range shown.
- For χ₃^K/χ₂^K, a discrepancy between the Boltzmann approximation and the full calculation appears already at small chemical potentials.
- For protons, deviations between the Boltzmann approximation and the full calculation for χ₂^p/χ₁^p start to occur at μ_B ≈ 300 MeV, corresponding to collision energies √s ≤ 14.5 GeV.
- For χ₃^p/χ₂^p, a ∼15% discrepancy between the two approaches appears in the temperature range of interest.

## Context

This work extends the program of using conserved charge fluctuations to determine freeze-out parameters, previously applied to baryon number and electric charge in Borsanyi (2014) and Bazavov (2015), by adding strangeness via kaon fluctuations. It addresses the tension between light and strange freeze-out temperatures observed in ALICE data by Preghenella (2011) and Floris (2014), which has been discussed by Becattini (2012), Steinheimer (2012), and Bellwied (2013) but not resolved from first principles. The method enables a first-principles extraction of the kaon freeze-out temperature by comparing lattice QCD results for χ₂^K/χ₁^K to preliminary STAR data from the Beam Energy Scan, and the results may impact hydrodynamical modeling and transport coefficients.
