# Sensitivity of neutron radii in the $"" sup 208_Pb nucleus and a neutron star to nucleon-$ sigma_-$ rho_ coupling corrections in relativistic mean field theory

> We study the sensitivity of the neutron skin thickness, $S$, in a $^{208}$Pb nucleus to the addition of nucleon-sigma-rho coupling corrections to a selection (PK1, NL3, S271, Z271) of interactions in relativistic mean field model. The PK1 and NL3 effective interactions lead to a minimum value of $S$ = 0.16 fm in comparison with the original value of $S$ = 0.28 fm. The S271 and Z271 effective interactions yield even smaller values of $S$ = 0.11 fm, which are similar to those for nonrelativistic mean field models. A precise measurement of the neutron radius, and therefore $S$, in $^{208}$Pb will place an important constraint on both relativistic and nonrelativistic mean field models. We also study the correlation between the radius of a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star and $S$.

## Key claim

The addition of nucleon-sigma-rho coupling corrections to relativistic mean field (RMF) models can reduce the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb from the typical RMF range of 0.2–0.3 fm down to values as low as 0.11 fm, comparable to nonrelativistic Skyrme model predictions, but only when the experimental binding energy per nucleon and proton rms radius are simultaneously constrained.

## Method

The authors supplemented existing RMF Lagrangian densities (PK1, NL3, S271, Z271) with two new isospin-dependent higher-order correction terms coupling the nucleon current to sigma- and rho-meson fields, plus an existing omega-rho coupling term. They solved the resulting Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently for spherical 208Pb using the Runge-Kutta algorithm and shooting method, and for neutron star matter using the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations under chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. Coupling constants were calibrated by fixing the symmetry energy at an average density of kf = 1.15 fm−1 and requiring the binding energy per nucleon in 208Pb to lie within 0.005 MeV of the experimental value.

## Key numbers

- Minimum neutron skin thickness S = 0.159 fm for PK1 interaction with Λv = 0.035 and Γ1 = 0.2 (or Γ2 = −0.55), compared to original PK1 value S = 0.28 fm
- Minimum S = 0.155 fm for NL3 interaction with Λv = 0.035 and Γ1 = 0.25 (Γ2 = 0)
- S271 and Z271 interactions yield S = 0.11 fm with the new corrections, similar to nonrelativistic mean field model predictions
- Experimental proton rms radius of 208Pb is 5.45 ± 0.02 fm (Fricke)
- Experimental binding energy per nucleon for 208Pb is −7.868 MeV; equivalent value used for comparison is −7.843 MeV after center-of-mass correction
- Symmetry energy at kf = 1.15 fm−1 is 26.08 MeV for original PK1 and 25.68 MeV for original NL3, S271, and Z271

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001), who introduced nonlinear omega-rho coupling to modify the density dependence of symmetry energy and reduce neutron skin thickness in RMF models. The new sigma-rho coupling terms allow RMF models to produce neutron skin values (S = 0.11–0.16 fm) that overlap with predictions from nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock models such as those of Brown (2000), which give S = 0.16 ± 0.02 fm. The results enable future parity-violating electron scattering measurements at Jefferson Laboratory (aiming for 1% precision on the neutron radius of 208Pb) to discriminate between relativistic and nonrelativistic nuclear structure models, and establish a correlation between the neutron skin in 208Pb and the radius of a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star.
