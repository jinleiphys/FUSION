# The baryon coupling scheme in an unified SU(3) and SU(6) symmetry formalism

> We calculate the baryon-meson coupling constants for the spin-1/2 baryonic octet and spin-3/2 decuplet in a unified approach relying on symmetry arguments such as the fact that the Yukawa couplings, present in the Lagrangian density of the Walecka-type models, must be an invariant under SU(3) and SU(6) group transformations. The coupling constants of the baryon with the scalar $σ$ meson are fixed to reproduce the known potential depths for the hyperons and $Δ$ resonances, in an approach that can be extended to all particles. We then apply the calculated coupling constants to study neutron star matter with hyperons and deltas admixed to its composition. We conclude that the $Δ^-$ is by far the most important exotic particle that can be present in the neutron star interior. It is always present, independent of the chosen parameterization, and might appear in almost every known neutron star, once its onset happens at very low density. Yet, its presence affects the astrophysical properties of the canonical 1.4 M$_\odot$ star, and, in some cases, it can even contribute to an increase in the maximum mass reached.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the $\Delta^-$ resonance is "by far the most important exotic particle" in neutron star interiors, appearing at very low densities in every parameterization considered, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of the free parameter $\alpha_v$ and the assumed potential depth $U_\Delta = -98$ MeV, which is itself poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors calculate baryon-meson coupling constants for the spin-1/2 octet and spin-3/2 decuplet using SU(3) and SU(6) symmetry arguments, with one free parameter $\alpha_v$ for the vector meson couplings. The scalar meson couplings are fixed by requiring that the resulting potential depths match known values: $U_\Lambda = -28$ MeV, $U_\Sigma = +30$ MeV, $U_\Xi = -4$ MeV, and $U_\Delta = -98$ MeV. These couplings are then used in a relativistic mean-field (Walecka-type) model with an enhanced L3$\omega\rho$ parameterization to compute equations of state, which are solved via the TOV equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- $U_\Lambda = -28$ MeV, $U_\Sigma = +30$ MeV, $U_\Xi = -4$ MeV, and $U_\Delta = -98$ MeV: the potential depths used to fix scalar meson couplings for hyperons and $\Delta$ resonances
- $U_\Delta = 1.5 U_N = -98$ MeV: the assumed $\Delta$ potential depth relative to the nucleon potential
- Saturation density $n_0 = 0.156$ fm$^{-3}$, effective nucleon mass $M^*/M = 0.69$, compressibility $K = 256$ MeV, symmetry energy $S_0 = 32.1$ MeV, slope $L = 66$ MeV, binding energy $B/A = 16.2$ MeV: nuclear matter properties of the enhanced L3$\omega\rho$ parameterization
- $\Delta^-$ onset density approximately $n = 0.3$ fm$^{-3}$: the density at which the $\Delta^-$ first appears in $\Delta$-admixed neutron star matter
- Maximum masses for hyperonic neutron stars: $M_{\rm max} = 1.97 M_\odot$ (SU(6), $\alpha_v=1.00$), $2.05 M_\odot$ ($\alpha_v=0.75$), $2.13 M_\odot$ ($\alpha_v=0.50$), $2.20 M_\odot$ ($\alpha_v=0.25$)
- Radius of canonical 1.4 $M_\odot$ star: $R_{1.4} = 12.82$ km for all hyperonic cases (same as nucleonic)
- Tidal deformability for canonical star: $\Lambda_{1.4} = 516$ for all hyperonic cases

## Context

This work extends previous SU(3) and SU(6) symmetry-based coupling schemes for hyperons (Lopes 2013, Weiss 1987, Tsu 1999) to include the spin-3/2 baryon decuplet, particularly the $\Delta$ resonances. It contrasts with studies such as Constança (2021) that allowed $\Delta$ couplings to vary freely, and with Sedrakian (2021) and Duarte (2021) that also addressed $\Delta$ inclusion. The results enable future calculations of neutron star properties with a unified, symmetry-constrained treatment of both octet and decuplet baryons, though the scalar couplings for $\Sigma^*$, $\Xi^*$, and $\Omega$ remain undetermined due to unknown potential depths.
