# Bethe-Salpeter approach with the separable interaction for the deuteron

> Recent developments of the covariant Bethe-Salpeter (BS) approach with the use of the separable interaction for the deuteron are reviewed. It is shown that the BS formalism allows a covariant description of various electromagnetic reactions like the lepton-deuteron scattering, deuteron electro-disintegration, deep inelastic scattering (DIS) of leptons on light nuclei. The procedure of the construction of the separable nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction is discussed. The BS formalism facilitates analysis of the role of the P-waves (negative energy components) in the electromagnetic properties of the deuteron and its comparison with the nonrelativistic results. Furthermore the covariant BS approach makes it possible to analyze DIS of leptons from the deuteron in a model independent way and to extend the formalism to DIS reactions on the light nuclei.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a separable nucleon-nucleon interaction provides a covariant description of electromagnetic reactions involving the deuteron, but the approach is still in an early stage of development and the role of abnormal parity states (P-waves) has not yet been confronted with experimental data, though the necessity of such confrontation is demonstrated.

## Method

The authors review the construction of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for an n-nucleon bound state and derive the relation between the bound-state matrix elements of local operators and the truncated Green's function. They then discuss the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the n-nucleon Green's function and introduce a separable ansatz for the irreducible kernel, which reduces the integral equation to a system of linear equations. The formalism is applied to elastic electron-deuteron scattering, deuteron electro-disintegration, and deep inelastic scattering of leptons on light nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron bound state appears as a pole in the n-nucleon Green's function at \(P^2 = M^2\), where \(M\) is the deuteron mass.
- The nucleon mass is denoted \(m\) throughout the formalism.
- The free nucleon Green's function \(S_{(1)}\) satisfies \((i\hat\partial_{x_1} - m)S_{(1)}(x_1,x_2)=\delta(x_1-x_2)\).
- The separable kernel for the two-nucleon system is written as \(\overline G_{4}(z_1,z_2,z'_1,z'_2)= \sum^{N}_{i,j}\lambda_{ij}g_i(z_1,z_2)g_j(z'_1,z'_2)\).

## Context

This review extends the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach originally developed by Salpeter and Bethe (1951) and by Mandelstam (1955), and contrasts it with three-dimensional quasipotential reductions such as those by Logunov and Tavkhelidze (1963) and by Gross (1969). The work enables future confrontation of the role of P-wave (negative energy) components in deuteron electromagnetic properties with high-precision experimental data expected from forthcoming experiments at large momentum transfer.
