# Role of local anisotropy in hybrid stars

> Using the Bower-Liang model, we discuss how pressure anisotropies affect the microscopic and macroscopic properties of hybrid stars. We find that anisotropies affect the maximum mass, central density, and radius of the canonical stars. Anisotropies also affect the minimum neutron star mass that presents quarks in their core, as well as the total amount of quarks for the maximally massive stars. We also confront our results with standard constraints, such as the radius and the tidal parameter of the canonical star, as well as the mass and radius of the PSR J0740+6620 pulsar. We observe that moderate values for anisotropies could fulfill these constraints simultaneously. On the other hand, within more extreme degrees of anisotropies, more speculative constraints such as black widow pulsars PSR J0952-0607 and the mass-gap object in the GW190814 event can be explained as hybrid stars. We also investigate the role of anisotropies in the neutron stars' moment of inertia.

## Key claim

Using the Bower-Liang (BL) model, the authors find that moderate values of local pressure anisotropy can simultaneously satisfy standard observational constraints on hybrid stars (such as NICER and GW170817 data), while more extreme degrees of anisotropy could explain speculative objects like the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 and the mass-gap object in GW190814 as hybrid stars, provided the anisotropy parameter is positive.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star models using the enhanced L3ωρ parametrization (eL3ωρ) within quantum hadrodynamics for the hadronic phase, with and without hyperons, and the thermodynamic consistent vector MIT bag model for the quark phase, using two values of the vector coupling constant (G_V = 0.40 and 0.42 fm²) to produce weak and strong quark interactions. The hadron-quark phase transition is determined via the Maxwell construction, and local pressure anisotropy is incorporated using the Bower-Liang model, where the anisotropy parameter λ_BL ranges from -2.0 to +2.0. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved with the anisotropic stress-energy tensor to obtain mass-radius relations, central densities, and moments of inertia.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for isotropic (λ_BL = 0) pure nucleonic star: 2.30 M⊙ with central density ~0.94 fm⁻³
- Maximum mass for isotropic (λ_BL = 0) hyperonic hadronic star: 2.13 M⊙ with central density ~0.99 fm⁻³
- Maximum mass for isotropic (λ_BL = 0) hybrid star (weak quark interaction): 2.06 M⊙
- Maximum mass for isotropic (λ_BL = 0) hybrid star (strong quark interaction): 2.09 M⊙
- For λ_BL = -2.0, pure nucleonic star maximum mass drops to 1.98 M⊙; for λ_BL = +2.0, it peaks at 2.74 M⊙
- For λ_BL = -2.0, hyperonic hadronic star maximum mass: 1.83 M⊙; for λ_BL = +2.0: 2.56 M⊙
- For λ_BL = -2.0, hybrid star (weak quark) maximum mass: 1.75 M⊙; for λ_BL = +2.0: 2.51 M⊙
- For λ_BL = -2.0, hybrid star (strong quark) maximum mass: 1.77 M⊙; for λ_BL = +2.0: 2.56 M⊙
- Radius of the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star for isotropic case: 12.82 km (same for all configurations)
- Minimum hybrid star mass for λ_BL = -2.0: 1.23 M⊙ (Nucleonic+Weak); for λ_BL = +2.0: 2.21 M⊙ (Nucleonic+Weak)
- Critical chemical potential for Nucleonic+Weak transition: 1214 MeV; critical pressure: 85 MeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic neutron stars using the Bower-Liang model (Bowers 1974), previously applied to hyperonic stars (Rahmansyah 2020), strange stars (Lopes 2023), and I-Love-C relations (Das 2022), to the specific case of hybrid stars with hadron-quark phase transitions. The results are confronted with observational constraints from NICER measurements of PSR J0740+6620 (Miller 2021) and the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star (Riley 2019; Miller 2019, 2021), as well as tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 (Abbott 2017, 2018). The paper enables future work on constraining the degree of anisotropy in hybrid stars through upcoming moment of inertia measurements, particularly for PSR J0737-3039(A) and other millisecond pulsars.
