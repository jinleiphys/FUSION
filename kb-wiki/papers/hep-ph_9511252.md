# Particle Production in a Hadron Collider Rapidity Gap: The Higgs Case

> Production of rare particles within rapidity gaps has been proposed as a background-free signal for the detection of new physics at hadron colliders. No complete formalism accounts for such processes yet. We study a simple lowest-order QCD model for their description. Concentrating on Higgs production, we show that the calculation of the cross section pp -> pp H can be embedded into existing models which successfully account for diffractive data. We extend those models to take into account single and double diffractive cross sections pp -> H X1 X2 with a gap between the fragments X1 and X2. Using conservative scenarios, we evaluate the uncertainties in our calculation, and study the dependence of the cross section on the gap width. We predict that Higgs production within a gap of 4 units of rapidity is about 0.3 pb for a 100 GeV Higgs at the Tevatron, and almost 2 pb for a 400 GeV Higgs within a gap of 6 units at the LHC with 14 TeV beams.

## Key claim

The authors show that Higgs boson production within a rapidity gap at hadron colliders can be described by embedding the process into existing lowest-order QCD models that already successfully account for diffractive data, but they note that no complete formalism yet exists for such processes and that their calculation relies on conservative scenarios to evaluate uncertainties.

## Method

The authors extend a two-gluon exchange QCD model, previously used for elastic and total cross sections, to include single and double diffractive processes by introducing new form factors derived from the proton wavefunction. They embed Higgs production via an effective gluon-gluon-Higgs vertex into the s-channel discontinuity of the two-gluon exchange amplitude, keeping exact kinematics and using Sudakov variables to handle longitudinal momentum fractions. The model parameters (α_S, c, and a non-perturbative scale μ_0) are tuned to reproduce Tevatron data on total, elastic, and diffractive cross sections, making the LHC predictions parameter-free.

## Key numbers

- Higgs production within a rapidity gap of 4 units at the Tevatron: approximately 0.3 pb for a 100 GeV Higgs boson.
- Higgs production within a rapidity gap of 6 units at the LHC with √s = 14 TeV: almost 2 pb for a 400 GeV Higgs boson.
- The inelastic diffractive contribution pp → X₁X₂H is a factor of approximately 3 larger than the elastic contribution pp → ppH.
- The pomeron trajectory used is α_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25 t, fitted from √s = 5 GeV to 1800 GeV and t = 0 to -1 GeV².
- Best-fit parameters using perturbative propagator: α_S = 0.88, c = 0.61; using constituent gluon propagator: α_S = 1.52, c = 0.11, μ_0 = 1.9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-gluon exchange approach of Low and of Soper, and the earlier Higgs-in-rapidity-gap calculation by Bialas and Landshoff (1991), which relied on a non-perturbative gluon propagator and neglected longitudinal kinematics. The authors remedy several drawbacks of that earlier work by introducing new form factors for diffractive scattering, keeping exact kinematics, and showing the cross section is IR finite in perturbative QCD. The results enable parameter-free predictions for Higgs production at the LHC and can be generalized to other rare particle production channels within rapidity gaps.
