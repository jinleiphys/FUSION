# Imaginary deuteron optical potential due to elastic and inelastic breakup

> The contributions to the reaction cross section from the elastic and inelastic breakup processes, calculated within the post-form distorted wave Born-approximation theory, are used as constraints to determine the contributions to the imaginary part of the deuteron optical potentials (IPDOP) due to the breakup channels. The Coulomb part of this potential due to the elastic breakup process is seen to account for the long range absorption in the optical potential. The nuclear parts of the IPDOP due to the elastic and inelastic breakup modes peak in different regions of the nuclear surface, with the latter being almost an order of magnitude larger than the former. This makes the IPDOP due to the breakup channels determined by us stronger than those calculated earlier ignoring the inelastic breakup mode.

## Key claim

The imaginary part of the deuteron optical potential due to breakup channels is stronger than previously calculated because the inelastic breakup mode, which dominates the total breakup cross section, contributes an imaginary potential almost an order of magnitude larger than that from elastic breakup alone; the Coulomb part of the elastic breakup potential accounts for long-range absorption that conventional Woods-Saxon optical potentials miss.

## Method

The authors calculated elastic and inelastic breakup cross sections for deuteron-induced reactions using the post-form distorted-wave Born-approximation (DWBA) theory. They then used these calculated breakup cross sections as constraints in a fitting procedure to determine the imaginary part of the deuteron optical potential due to breakup channels, employing a sum-of-Gaussians parameterization and imposing the condition that the breakup potential does not exceed the total phenomenological imaginary potential at any radius.

## Key numbers

- For 56 MeV deuterons on a 51V target, the inelastic (d,p) breakup cross section is 290 mb, the inelastic (d,n) breakup cross section is 284 mb, and the elastic (d,pn) breakup cross section is 122 mb, giving a total deuteron breakup cross section of 698 mb.
- The total (d,p) breakup cross section (inelastic plus elastic) is 412 mb, in reasonable agreement with the measured value of 481 mb from Matoba (1980).
- The imaginary potential due to inelastic breakup is about an order of magnitude larger than that due to nuclear dissociation (elastic breakup).
- The nuclear dissociation potential peaks around 8.0 fm, while the inelastic breakup potential peaks around 6.5 fm; both have a width of about 2 fm (FWHM), similar to the rms-radius of the deuteron.
- The Coulomb dissociation potential has a radial dependence of approximately e^{-0.3r}, compared to the Woods-Saxon potential's approximate e^{-2.0r} dependence at large distances.
- Adding the Coulomb dissociation potential to the phenomenological optical potential changes the total reaction cross section by less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the method introduced by Shyam et al. (1980) for using unitarity to relate breakup cross sections to the imaginary optical potential, and improves upon earlier calculations by Sakura et al. (1986) and Yabuzaki et al. (1992) that ignored the inelastic breakup mode. The authors note that Christley et al. (1991) used a similar method for fusion potentials but achieved poorer fits due to using Woods-Saxon parameterizations without l-dependence. The method can be applied to halo nuclei such as 11Li and 11Be, where breakup cross sections are significantly enhanced.
