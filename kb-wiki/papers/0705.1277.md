# Strongly Interacting Low Viscosity Matter Created in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Substantial collective flow is observed in collisions between large nuclei at high energy, as evidenced by single-particle transverse momentum distributions and by azimuthal correlations among the produced particles. The data are well-reproduced by perfect fluid dynamics. In a separate development, calculation of the dimensionless ratio of shear viscosity eta to entropy density s by Kovtun, Son and Starinets within AdS/CFT yields eta/s = 1/4pi, and they conjectured that this is a lower bound for any physical system. It is shown that the transition from hadrons to quarks and gluons has behavior similar to helium, nitrogen, and water at and near their phase transitions in the ratio eta/s. Therefore it is possible that experimental measurements can pinpoint the location of this transition or rapid crossover in QCD via the viscosity to entropy ratio in addition to and independently of the equation of state.

# Strongly Interacting Low Viscosity Matter Created in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) in QCD matter must have a minimum at or near the critical temperature for the hadron-to-quark-gluon transition, analogous to the behavior observed in helium, nitrogen, and water at their phase transitions; therefore, experimental measurements of η/s could independently pinpoint the location of this transition or rapid crossover, in addition to and independently of the equation of state.

## Method
The author compares the temperature dependence of η/s for helium, nitrogen, and water at various pressures using data from the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), showing that η/s has a cusp-like minimum at the critical point. These empirical trends are then contrasted with theoretical calculations of η/s in the hadronic phase (using chiral perturbation theory and calculations by Prakash that include intermediate resonances such as the rho-meson) and in the quark-gluon plasma phase (using leading-order perturbative QCD with summation of Coulomb logarithms by Arnold et al.), with the running coupling evaluated via the two-loop renormalization group equation.

## Key numbers
- The Kovtun-Son-Starinets AdS/CFT lower bound for η/s is 1/(4π) (in units with ℏ = k_B = c = 1).
- In the low-energy chiral limit for pions, η/s = (15/(16π)) × (f_π^4 / T^4), where f_π is the pion decay constant.
- For three flavors of massless quarks in perturbative QCD at leading order, η/s = 5.12 / [g^4 ln(2.42/g)].
- The two-loop running coupling uses Λ_T = 30 MeV, corresponding to an energy scale of 2πT and Λ_MS-bar = 200 MeV.
- Lattice gauge theory for pure SU(3) without quarks gives η/s ≈ 1/2 in the temperature range 1.6 < T/T_c < 2.2, with uncertainties of order 100%.
- Classical nonrelativistic quasi-particle modeling by Gelman, Shuryak, and Zahed gives η/s ≈ 0.34 for temperatures from T_c to 1.5 T_c.
- The critical or crossover temperature T_c is approximately 175 MeV from lattice QCD, and theoretical calculations suggest a minimum near T_c ≈ 190 MeV.
- Perfect fluid dynamics reproduces measurements of radial flow and elliptic flow v_2 up to transverse momenta of order 1.5 GeV/c.
- Parametric fits to transverse momentum spectra yield average transverse fluid flow velocities of more than 50% the speed of light and chemical freezeout temperatures on the order of 160 MeV.

## Context
This paper extends the empirical observation that η/s has a minimum at the critical point for atomic and molecular substances (helium, nitrogen, water) to the QCD phase transition between hadrons and quark-gluon plasma. It builds on the AdS/CFT conjecture by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets that η/s ≥ 1/(4π) for any physical system, and on the experimental discovery at RHIC that gold-on-gold collisions exhibit substantial collective flow well-reproduced by perfect fluid dynamics, in contrast to earlier SPS measurements where v_2 was about half as large. The work enables a program to extract η(T)/s(T) from heavy ion collision data via scaling violations to perfect fluid flow predictions, analogous to how compressibility and the nuclear optical potential were inferred at lower beam energies (30 to 1000 MeV per nucleon) using transverse momentum distributions relative to the reaction plane.
