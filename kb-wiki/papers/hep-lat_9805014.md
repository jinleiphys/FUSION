# Improved Lattice Actions with Chemical Potential

> We give a prescription how to include a chemical potential μinto a general lattice action. This inclusion does not cause any lattice artifacts. Hence its application to an improved - or even perfect - action at μ=0 yields an improved resp. perfect action at arbitrary μ. For short-ranged improved actions, a good scaling behavior holds over a wide region, and the upper bound for the baryon density - which is known for the standard lattice actions - can be exceeded.

# Improved Lattice Actions with Chemical Potential

## Key claim

The standard procedure for including a chemical potential into a lattice action, treating it as an imaginary constant Abelian gauge potential, preserves perfectness, classical perfectness, or Symanzik improvement to any given order, so that an improved action at zero chemical potential yields an equally improved action at arbitrary chemical potential without introducing new lattice artifacts.

## Method

The author shows that including a chemical potential μ via the substitution Ψ(x,t) → e^{-μt} Ψ(x,t) and Ψ̄(x,t) → e^{μt} Ψ̄(x,t) (the "standard procedure") commutes with block variable renormalization group transformations. Starting from a fine lattice with chemical potential μ/(nN) and performing N renormalization group transformations of block factor n, the limit nN → ∞ yields a perfect action at chemical potential μ. Alternatively, one can construct the perfect action at μ=0 and then include μ by the same standard procedure. The analysis is demonstrated explicitly for free fermions, comparing standard Wilson and staggered actions, Symanzik-improved actions (D234 and Naik fermions), and truncated perfect actions (hypercube fermion, BA and PD schemes).

## Key numbers

- For the free fermion gas at zero mass and zero temperature, the continuum pressure is P = μ⁴/(6π²) and the baryon density is n_B = 2μ³/(9π²).
- The scaling region for truncated perfect actions is extended by one order of magnitude compared to standard actions: for Wilson-type fermions, P/μ⁴ deviates by less than 50% from the continuum value up to μ=0.8 for the Wilson fermion, up to μ=1.6 for the D234 action, and up to μ=8.2 for the hypercube fermion.
- Standard naive fermions saturate at a fermion density of n_f = 32; Wilson fermions with r=1 saturate at n_f = 16; the hypercube fermion also saturates at n_f = 32 but with significantly delayed saturation.
- The D234 action has maximal temporal coupling distance 2, which could allow n_f,max = 64, but cancellations reduce it to 32.
- Standard staggered fermions have an upper bound of n_f = 8; actions coupling over temporal distance 3 amplify this bound by a factor of 3.

## Context

This work extends the construction of perfect and improved lattice actions, previously developed for zero chemical potential by QuaGlu (1996) and others, to nonzero chemical potential, addressing the sign problem that plagues lattice QCD simulations at finite baryon density. The prescription applies to Wilson-type fermions, staggered fermions, and any improved action (classically perfect, Symanzik-improved, or truncated perfect). The results enable simulations on coarser lattices with larger physical volumes, which indirectly helps overcome the statistical sign problem identified by BG (1990) and the onset problem discussed by the Glasgow group. The approach is complementary to ongoing work on Symanzik-improved and approximately perfect gauge actions reviewed at Lat97.
