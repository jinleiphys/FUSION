# Strongly coupled plasma -- hydrodynamics, thermalization and nonequilibrium behavior

> In this talk I will describe various features of time dependent dynamics of strongly coupled plasma from the perspective of the AdS/CFT correspondence. I will take as an example boost invariant plasma flow and concentrate on the properties of hydrodynamic expansion, thermalization versus hydrodynamization and some features of nonequilibrium behaviour.

## Key claim
The AdS/CFT correspondence provides a concrete example of a strongly coupled gauge theory in which nonperturbative real-time dynamics can be studied in detail, but the qualitative relevance of results from N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory for QCD must be judged depending on the specific physical question, because N=4 SYM has no phase transition, no running coupling, and the coupling must be large for AdS/CFT methods to be effective.

## Method
The author uses the AdS/CFT correspondence to map the time evolution of strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma in a boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow to a 5D gravitational description with negative cosmological constant. Einstein's equations are solved numerically for various initial geometries at proper time τ=0, and the gauge theory energy-momentum tensor is extracted from the asymptotic behavior of the 5D metric near the AdS boundary. The effective temperature T_eff is defined from the instantaneous energy density via the N=4 SYM equation of state.

## Key numbers
- The hydrodynamic description holds for w = T_eff · τ ≥ 0.7, using a criterion that 3rd order hydrodynamic equations are satisfied to 0.5% accuracy.
- For RHIC sample initial data T_0 = 500 MeV, τ_0 = 0.25 fm, the corresponding w = 0.63, which is comparable to the hydrodynamization threshold.
- At the transition to hydrodynamics, the longitudinal pressure anisotropy Δp_L = 1 - p_L/(ε/3) ~ 0.7, indicating the plasma is still far from local thermal equilibrium.
- The late-time asymptotic behavior of the temperature follows π T(τ) ~ 1/τ^(1/3) with a fixed coefficient.
- Around 240 subsequent terms in the boost-invariant hydrodynamic expansion were obtained numerically.

## Context
This work extends the Bjorken (1983) boost-invariant flow model and the fluid/gravity duality framework to study the transition from far-from-equilibrium initial conditions to hydrodynamic behavior. The numerical results from Heller, Janik, and Witaszczyk (2012a, 2012b) show that hydrodynamization (onset of viscous hydrodynamics) occurs earlier than thermalization (isotropization of pressures), resolving the "early thermalization puzzle" as a misnomer. The approach enables semi-quantitative estimates for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and opens the question of describing nonhydrodynamic degrees of freedom from a purely 4D perspective.
