# Molière Scattering in Quark-Gluon Plasma: Finding Point-Like Scatterers in a Liquid

> By finding rare (but not exponentially rare) large-angle deflections of partons within a jet produced in a heavy ion collision, or of such a jet itself, experimentalists can find the weakly coupled short-distance quark and gluon particles (scatterers) within the strongly coupled liquid quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in heavy ion collisions. This is the closest one can come to probing QGP via a scattering experiment and ultimately learning how a strongly coupled liquid emerges from an asymptotically free gauge theory. The short-distance, particulate, structure of liquid QGP can be revealed in events in which a jet parton resolves, and scatters off, a parton from the droplet of QGP. The probability for picking up significant transverse momentum via a single scattering was calculated previously, but only in the limit of infinite parton energy which means zero angle scattering. Here, we provide a leading order perturbative QCD calculation of the Molière scattering probability for incident partons with finite energy, scattering at a large angle. We set up a thought experiment in which an incident parton with a finite energy scatters off a parton constituent within a "brick" of QGP, which we treat as if it were weakly coupled, as appropriate for scattering with large momentum transfer, and compute the probability for a parton to show up at a nonzero angle with some energy. We include all relevant channels, including those in which the parton that shows up at a large angle was kicked out of the medium as well as the Rutherford-like channel in which what is seen is the scattered incident parton. The results that we obtain will serve as inputs to future jet Monte Carlo calculations and can provide qualitative guidance for how to use future precise, high statistics, suitably differential measurements of jet modification in heavy ion collisions to find the scatterers within the QGP liquid.

## Key claim
The paper provides a leading-order perturbative QCD calculation of the Molière scattering probability for incident partons with finite energy scattering at a large angle, extending previous work that was limited to the infinite parton energy (zero-angle) limit. This calculation is intended as a key input for future jet Monte Carlo simulations and as qualitative guidance for experimental searches for point-like scatterers within the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), but the authors caution that detecting such rare events will require high statistics, judicious choice of observables, and considerable phenomenological modeling.

## Method
The authors set up a thought experiment in which a single energetic parton (quark, antiquark, or gluon) with initial energy $p_{\rm in}$ is shot through a static "brick" of QGP of thickness $L$ at constant temperature $T$, modeled as a cloud of massless quarks and gluons with Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein momentum distributions. They compute the probability distribution $F(p,\theta)$ for finding an outgoing hard parton with energy $p$ at an angle $\theta$ relative to the incident direction, by summing over all possible $2 \to 2$ binary scattering processes (including both the Rutherford-like channel where the incident parton scatters and the channel where a medium parton is kicked out) and integrating the scattering amplitudes weighted by the thermal distribution functions.

## Key numbers
* The paper considers incident parton energies of $\pinit = 25\,T = 10$ GeV, $\pinit = 100\,T = 40$ GeV, and $\pinit = 250\,T = 100$ GeV incident on a brick with $T = 0.4$ GeV and $\Delta t = 3$ fm.
* The probability distribution $F(p,\theta)$ is normalized such that $\int^{\pi}_{\theta_{\min}} d\theta\, \int^{\infty}_{p_{\min}} dp\, F(p,\theta) = N_{\rm hard}(\theta_{\min})$, where $N_{\rm hard}(\theta_{\min})$ is the number of outgoing hard partons with $\theta \geq \theta_{\min}$ and $p \geq p_{\min}$ per single incident parton.
* The authors specify that $p_{\min}$ is chosen to be significantly greater than $T$ (the temperature of the plasma).

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of the probability for picking up significant transverse momentum via a single hard scattering, which were performed only in the limit of infinite parton energy (zero angle) by Arnold (2008) and D'Eramo (2012). The results are intended to be incorporated into jet Monte Carlo calculations, such as the hybrid model by Casalderrey-Solana (2014, 2015, 2016) or existing frameworks like JEWEL, MARTINI, or LBT, to enable quantitative predictions for observables like jet acoplanarity or jet substructure modifications in heavy ion collisions. The paper provides a qualitative guide for experimentalists to assess the probability that a parton in a jet could scatter by a large angle, which is relevant for future high-statistics measurements at RHIC (sPHENIX) and the LHC.
