# Inhomogeneous chiral condensates in three-flavor quark matter

> We investigate the effect of strange quark degrees of freedom on the formation of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in a three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in mean-field approximation. A Ginzburg-Landau study complemented by a stability analysis allow us to determine in a general way the location of the critical and Lifshitz points, together with the phase boundary where the (partially) chirally restored phase becomes unstable against developing inhomogeneities, without resorting to specific assumptions on the shape of the chiral condensate. We discuss the resulting phase structure and study the influence of the bare strange-quark mass $m_s$ and the axial anomaly on the size and location of the inhomogeneous phase compared to the first-order transition associated with homogeneous matter. We find that, as a consequence of the axial anomaly, critical and Lifshitz point split. For realistic strange-quark masses the effect is however very small and becomes sizeable only for small values of $m_s$.

## Key claim

The authors find that, as a consequence of the axial anomaly (the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction), the critical point and the Lifshitz point split in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, unlike in the two-flavor case where they coincide; however, for realistic strange-quark masses this splitting is very small and becomes sizeable only for small values of the bare strange-quark mass.

## Method

The authors perform a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the mean-field thermodynamic potential of the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model about the two-flavor chirally restored phase (with light-flavor chiral limit), expanding in powers of the light and strange order parameters and their gradients up to fourth order. They then eliminate the strange order parameter via its equation of motion and derive the modified coefficients for the light-sector effective potential, supplemented by a stability analysis of the homogeneous phase against periodic modulations to locate the second-order phase boundary to the inhomogeneous phase.

## Key numbers

* The critical point and Lifshitz point split due to the axial anomaly; with the realistic parameter set (bare strange-quark mass m_s = 236.9 MeV, Pauli-Villars regulator Lambda = 781.2 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 4.90, K*Lambda^5 = 129.8), the two points are extremely close but do not coincide exactly, with the Lifshitz point at slightly higher temperature.
* At T=0, the instability toward the inhomogeneous phase occurs at a wavevector q_min ~ 350 MeV, comparable to the chemical potential at the transition.
* The vacuum constituent light-quark mass with m_ell = 0 is about 310 MeV, while the fit value with m_ell = 10.3 MeV gives M_ell^(vac) = 325 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio studies (Nickel, 2009) where the critical point and Lifshitz point coincide, to include strange-quark degrees of freedom. It is complementary to an earlier three-flavor study by Moreira (2013) that used a specific chiral density wave ansatz at zero temperature; the present analysis does not assume a particular spatial shape and covers nonzero temperature. The results enable future investigations of whether in three-flavor QCD with very light quarks the inhomogeneous phase could reach down to zero chemical potential, potentially making it accessible to lattice studies.
