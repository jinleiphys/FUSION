# The two-nucleon system in the $Δ$ region including full meson retardation

> A model is developed for the hadronic and electromagnetic interaction in the two-nucleon system above pion threshold in the framework of meson, nucleon and $Δ$ degrees of freedom. It is based on time-ordered perturbation theory and includes full meson retardation in potentials and exchange currents as well as loop contributions to the nucleonic one-body current. Results for $NN$ scattering and deuteron photodisintegration are presented.

# The two-nucleon system in the Δ region including full meson retardation

## Key claim

The authors claim that including full meson retardation (rather than the static approximation) in a coupled-channel NN/NΔ approach with meson, nucleon, and Δ degrees of freedom largely resolves the long-standing failure of static models to describe deuteron photodisintegration cross sections in the Δ region, though some discrepancies in polarization observables remain.

## Method

The model is constructed in time-ordered perturbation theory with a Hilbert space comprising two bare nucleons, one nucleon plus one Δ resonance, and two nucleons plus one meson (π, ρ, σ, δ, ω, η). Retarded one-boson exchange potentials are derived from basic XNN and πNΔ vertices via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and the Elster potential is used for the NN interaction with one-pion loop diagrams to maintain unitarity above pion threshold. Electromagnetic currents include retarded pionic meson exchange currents, static ρ and Δ contributions, and loop contributions to the nucleonic one-body current, with gauge invariance satisfied in leading order of 1/M_N.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic Δ parameters and M1 γNΔ coupling are fitted simultaneously to the M_{1+}(3/2) multipole of pion photoproduction, the P_{33} channel in πN scattering, and the ^1D_2 channel in NN scattering.
- The ^1D_2 NN phase shift and inelasticity are described well up to about T_lab = 800 MeV.
- The hadronic interaction model is being constructed to fit phase shifts and inelasticities of all relevant NN scattering partial waves for T_lab energies up to about 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the static approach of Wilhelm et al. (1993), which used the Bonn-OBEPR potential and failed to describe deuteron photodisintegration data in the Δ peak, producing a considerable underestimation of the cross section and a spurious dip structure around 90 degrees at higher energies. The present retarded approach eliminates these problems in the differential cross section, though discrepancies in polarization observables such as the linear photon asymmetry Σ and the outgoing proton polarization P_y(p) remain. The model is intended to enable future studies of the GDH sum rule on the deuteron and to be extended to electrodisintegration and higher energies by including additional resonances (Roper, D_{13}, S_{11}).
