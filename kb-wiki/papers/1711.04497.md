# Inclusive heavy quark photoproduction in $pp$, $pPb$ and $PbPb$ collisions at Run 2 LHC energies

> In this paper we present a comprehensive analysis of the inclusive heavy quark photoproduction in $pp$, $pPb$ and $PbPb$ collisions at Run 2 LHC energies using the Color Dipole formalism. The rapidity distributions and total cross sections for the charm and bottom production are estimated considering the more recent phenomenological models for the dipole - proton scattering amplitude, which are based on the Color Glass Condensate formalism and are able to describe the inclusive and exclusive $ep$ HERA data. Moreover, we present, by the first time, the predictions for the transverse momentum distributions of the $D$ and $B$ mesons produced in the photon -- induced interactions.

# Inclusive heavy quark photoproduction in pp, pPb and PbPb collisions at Run 2 LHC energies

## Key claim

The color dipole formalism, with its free parameters already constrained by HERA data, provides parameter-free predictions for heavy quark photoproduction at LHC Run 2 energies, and the theoretical uncertainty between the b-CGC and IP-SAT models is small enough that future measurements can test the universality of the color dipole description for both inclusive and exclusive processes.

## Method

The authors used the color dipole formalism to compute heavy quark photoproduction cross sections in ultraperipheral hadronic collisions, factorizing the hadron-hadron cross section via the equivalent photon approximation. The dipole-proton scattering amplitude was modeled using the updated b-CGC and IP-SAT phenomenological models, which incorporate non-linear QCD dynamics and are constrained by HERA inclusive and exclusive data. For nuclear targets, the dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude was obtained via the Glauber-Mueller approach using the b-CGC and IP-SAT dipole-proton amplitudes as input. Heavy meson transverse momentum distributions were obtained by convolving the quark-level distributions with the Peterson fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass assumed: $m_c = 1.27$ GeV; bottom quark mass assumed: $m_b = 4.5$ GeV
- Total charm photoproduction cross section in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV: $1.03 \times 10^4$ nb (b-CGC) and $1.14 \times 10^4$ nb (IP-SAT) for full rapidity range $-10 < Y < 10$
- Total charm photoproduction cross section in $PbPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV: $2.81 \times 10^9$ nb (b-CGC) and $2.75 \times 10^9$ nb (IP-SAT) for full rapidity range $-10 < Y < 10$
- Total bottom photoproduction cross section in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV: $1.31 \times 10^2$ nb (b-CGC) and $1.60 \times 10^2$ nb (IP-SAT) for full rapidity range $-10 < Y < 10$
- Total bottom photoproduction cross section in $PbPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV: $1.87 \times 10^7$ nb (b-CGC) and $1.99 \times 10^7$ nb (IP-SAT) for full rapidity range $-10 < Y < 10$
- For the LHCb forward rapidity range ($2 < Y < 4.5$), charm cross sections in $pp$ collisions: $2.08 \times 10^3$ nb (b-CGC) and $2.30 \times 10^3$ nb (IP-SAT)
- Difference between b-CGC and IP-SAT predictions for $pp$ and $pPb$ collisions: approximately 10% for charm and 20% for bottom production
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: $\epsilon_c = 0.05$, $\epsilon_b = 0.006$

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Goncalves et al., 2015-2016) on heavy quark photoproduction using the color dipole formalism, now incorporating updated dipole-proton scattering amplitudes from Rezaeian (2013) and the IP-SAT model from Rezaeian et al. (2013) that were constrained by high-precision HERA data. The paper presents, for the first time, predictions for the transverse momentum distributions of $D^0$ and $B^0$ mesons from photon-induced interactions at LHC Run 2 energies. The results enable future experimental tests of the universality of the color dipole formalism across inclusive and exclusive processes, and suggest that the large charm production rates in peripheral $PbPb$ collisions may need to be accounted for when interpreting $D$ meson production data from the ALICE Collaboration.
