# Insensitivity of the Coulomb breakup of halo nuclei to spectroscopic factors

> Exotic nuclear structures such as halos are mostly studied using reactions. In Coulomb breakup, the radioactive projectile dissociates through its interaction with a heavy target. Often, a spectroscopic factor for the core-halo structure is inferred from experimental data. In this work, we present a new calculation of the Coulomb breakup of the one-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Be performed with a coupled-channel effective particle-rotor model of that nucleus, which accounts for the excitation of the $^{10}$Be core. Changes in the spectroscopic factor have no effect on the cross sections when the asymptotic normalisation coefficient is fixed, hence confirming the insensitivity of Coulomb-breakup cross sections to spectroscopic factors.

## Key claim
Changes in the spectroscopic factor of the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be have no effect on Coulomb-breakup cross sections when the asymptotic normalisation coefficient (ANC) is fixed, confirming that spectroscopic factors cannot be inferred from such measurements even when core excitation is included in a coupled-channel description.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective particle-rotor model of 11Be that couples the valence neutron to the 10Be core, including excitation of the core to its first 2+ state. They varied the quadrupole deformation parameter β to change the spectroscopic factor in the main 1s1/2⊗0+ channel while keeping the binding energy and ANC fixed, then computed the Coulomb breakup cross section on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon using first-order perturbation theory with pure E1 transitions.

## Key numbers
- 11Be ground state one-neutron separation energy: Sn = 0.503 MeV (with footnote noting the most recent value is 0.50164 MeV ± 0.25 keV)
- ANC of the 11Be ground state: C1/2+ = 0.786 fm−1/2 (from ab initio predictions)
- 10Be first excited 2+ state energy: ε2+ = 3.368 MeV
- Quadrupole deformation β varied from 0 to 0.7, producing spectroscopic factors in the main channel ranging from 1.00 (β=0) down to 0.81 (β=0.7)
- Impact-parameter cutoff for forward-angle data selection (θ < 1.3°): bmin ≈ 30 fm
- Potential range parameter: σ = 2.0 fm

## Context
This work extends previous single-particle descriptions of halo nuclei (Capel 2006, 2007, 2018) that showed Coulomb breakup probes only the tail of the wave function (the ANC) and not the spectroscopic factor. Those studies were criticized by Byron (2007) for using overlap wave functions with unit norm. The present calculation uses a coupled-channel particle-rotor model (Kubushishi and Capel 2025) that includes core excitation, confirming the insensitivity of breakup cross sections to spectroscopic factors and enabling future work with Coulomb-corrected eikonal reaction models that include higher-order effects and nuclear interactions.
