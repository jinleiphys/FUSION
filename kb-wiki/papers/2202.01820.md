# Thermal effects on tidal deformability in the last orbits of an inspiraling binary neutron star system

> The study of binary neutron stars mergers by the detection of the emitted gravitational waves is one of the most promised tools to study the properties of dense nuclear matter at high densities. It is worth claiming that, at the moment, strong evidence that the temperature of the stars is zero during the last orbits before coalescing, does not exist. Nevertheless, theoretical studies suggest that the temperature concerning the inspiral phase, could reach even a few MeV. According to the main theory, tides transfer mechanical energy and angular momentum to the star at the expense of the orbit, where friction within the star converts the mechanical energy into heat. During the inspiral, these effects are potentially detectable. Different treatments have been used to estimate the transfer of the mechanical energy and the size of the tidal friction, leading to different conclusions about the importance of pre-merger tidal effects. The present work is dedicated to the study of the effect of temperature on the tidal deformability of neutron stars during the inspiral of a neutron star system just before the merger. We applied a class of hot equations of state, both isothermal and adiabatic, originated from various nuclear models. We found that even for low values of temperature ($T<1$ MeV), the effects on the basic ingredients of tidal deformability are not negligible. On the other hand, in the case of the adiabatic star, the thermal effects on tidal deformability remain imperceptible, up to the value $S=0.2 \ {\rm k}_{B}$. According to the main finding, the effect of the temperature on the tidal deformability is indistinguishable. The consequences of the above result are discussed and analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for isothermal neutron stars with temperatures below 1 MeV, the individual tidal deformability lambda remains almost constant despite temperature-dependent changes in radius and Love number; for adiabatic stars with entropy per baryon up to S=0.2 k_B, thermal effects on tidal deformability are imperceptible. The abstract's claim that thermal effects are "almost negligible" is qualified by the finding that this insensitivity holds specifically for low temperatures and low entropies, with deviations appearing only at higher temperatures (above 1 MeV) or higher entropies (S=0.5 k_B).

## Method

The authors constructed hot equations of state for neutron star matter using the MDI+APR1 nuclear model, generating both isothermal profiles (T = 0.01, 0.1, 1, 5, 10 MeV) and adiabatic profiles (entropy per baryon S = 0.1, 0.2, 0.5 k_B). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the tidal Love number differential equation to compute the tidal deformability lambda and effective tidal deformability Lambda_tilde for binary neutron star systems, using the chirp masses and mass ranges from the GW170817 and GW190425 events.

## Key numbers

- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star using the Lattimer and Swesty equation of state at T = 0.01 MeV: k2 = 0.1005, R = 12.21 km, lambda = 2.73 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2
- For the same star at T = 1 MeV: k2 = 0.0788, R = 12.82 km, lambda = 2.73 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2
- For the same star at T = 10 MeV: k2 = 0.0127, R = 18.92 km, lambda = 3.02 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2
- The chirp mass for GW170817 is 1.186 solar masses; for GW190425 it is 1.44 solar masses
- The component mass ranges for GW170817 are m1 in (1.36, 1.60) solar masses and m2 in (1.16, 1.36) solar masses
- The component mass ranges for GW190425 are m1 in (1.654, 1.894) solar masses and m2 in (1.45, 1.654) solar masses

## Context

This work extends studies of tidal heating in binary neutron star inspirals by Meszaros and Rees (1992), Bildsten (1992), Kochanek (1992), and Arras and Weinberg (2019), which predicted temperature ranges from 0.01 to 10 MeV during inspiral. It is the first systematic examination of how such temperatures affect tidal deformability, finding that the individual tidal deformability lambda is surprisingly insensitive to temperature for low temperatures (below 1 MeV) despite changes in radius and Love number. This enables future gravitational wave analyses to potentially neglect thermal corrections to tidal deformability for low-temperature inspirals, though the authors note that binary systems with lower chirp mass (like GW170817) are more suitable for studying these effects than higher-mass systems (like GW190425).
