# Recombination rates from potential models close to the unitary limit

> We investigate universal behavior in the recombination rate of three bosons close to threshold. Using the He-He system as a reference, we solve the three-body Schrödinger equation above the dimer threshold for different potentials having large values of the two-body scattering length $a$. To this aim we use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion and we extract the $S$-matrix through the integral relations recently derived. The results are compared to the universal form, $α\approx 67.1\sin^2[s_0\ln(κ_*a)+γ]$, for different values of $a$ and selected values of the three-body parameter $κ_*$. A good agreement with the universal formula is obtained after introducing a particular type of finite-range corrections, which have been recently proposed by two of the authors in Ref.[1]. Furthermore, we analyze the validity of the above formula in the description of a very different system: neutron-neutron-proton recombination. Our analysis confirms the universal character of the process in systems of very different scales having a large two-body scattering length.

## Key claim

The universal formula for the three-body recombination rate, \(K_3 \approx 67.1 \sin^2[s_0 \ln(\kappa_* a) + \gamma] \, \hbar a^4 / m\), accurately describes numerical results from finite-range potential models only after introducing a shift \(\Gamma_+\) in the variable \(\kappa_* a\) and replacing the scattering length \(a\) with \(a_B\) derived from the two-body binding energy.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation above the dimer threshold using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method, extracting the S-matrix via recently derived integral relations. They used a family of attractive two-body Gaussian potentials tuned to reproduce the low-energy properties (scattering length, binding energy, effective range) of the LM2M2 He-He potential, with a repulsive hypercentral three-body interaction added to reproduce the trimer ground-state energy. The recombination rate was computed from the inelasticity parameter \(|S_{11}|\) for different values of the two-body scattering length \(a\) and the three-body parameter \(\kappa_*\), and the results were fitted to a modified universal formula.

## Key numbers

- The LM2M2 potential gives a two-body scattering length \(a = 189.41 \, a_0\), two-body energy \(E_2 = -1.303\) mK, and effective range \(r_e = 13.845 \, a_0\).
- The three-body parameter \(\kappa_*\) is \(2.119 \times 10^{-3} \, a_0^{-1}\) for the TBG potential and \(1.899 \times 10^{-3} \, a_0^{-1}\) for the TBG+H3B potential.
- The shift for the recombination rate formula is \(\Gamma_+ \simeq 6 \times 10^{-2}\).
- The scattering length at the recombination minimum is \(a_+^1 \approx 121 \, a_0\) (TBG) and \(a_+^1 \approx 135 \, a_0\) (TBG+H3B).
- The scattering length at the atom-dimer threshold is \(a_*^1 \approx 14.5 \, a_0\) (TBG) and \(a_*^1 \approx 16.2 \, a_0\) (TBG+H3B).
- The ratio \(a_*^1 / a_-^0 \approx -0.32\) for both TBG and TBG+H3B potentials.
- The ratio \(a_-^1 / a_+^1 \approx -6.3\) (TBG) and \(-7.2\) (TBG+H3B), compared to the zero-range prediction of \(-4.9\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of universal behavior in three-boson systems from atom-dimer scattering below breakup (Kievsky and Gattobigio, 2013) to recombination rates above the dimer threshold. It confirms that finite-range corrections, parameterized as shifts in \(\kappa_* a\), are necessary to reconcile numerical calculations with the zero-range universal formula derived by Petrov (2005) and summarized by Braaten and Hammer (2006). The results enable comparison with experimental measurements of Efimov resonances in ultracold atomic gases (e.g., Zaccanti et al., 2009; Ferlaino et al., 2011) and can be applied to nuclear systems such as neutron-neutron-proton recombination relevant to stellar processes.
