# Hadron Correlations in Jets and Ridges through Parton Recombination

> Hadron correlations in jets, ridges and opposite dijets at all $p_T$ above 2 GeV/c are discussed. Since abundant data are available from RHIC at intermediate $p_T$, a reliable hadronization scheme at that $p_T$ range is necessary in order to relate the semihard partonic processes to the observables. The recombination model is therefore first reviewed for that purpose. Final-state interaction is shown to be important for the Cronin effect, large B/M ratio and forward production. The effect of semihard partons on the medium is then discussed with particular emphasis on the formation of ridge with or without trigger. Azimuthal anisotropy can result from ridges without early thermalization. Dynamical path length distribution is derived for any centrality. Dihadron correlations in jets on the same or opposite side are shown to reveal detail properties of trigger and antitrigger biases with the inference that tangential jets dominate the dijets accessible to observation.

## Key claim

The recombination model (RM) provides a reliable hadronization scheme for intermediate $p_T$ (above 2 GeV/c) that can relate semihard partonic processes to observed hadron correlations, with the specific condition that final-state interaction (FSI) is shown to be important for the Cronin effect, large baryon/meson ratio, and forward production, contradicting the conventional interpretation that the Cronin effect arises solely from initial-state transverse broadening.

## Method

The paper reviews the recombination model (RM) formalism developed by Hwa and Yang, which treats hadronization as recombination of partons using one-dimensional momentum-fraction distributions. Shower parton distributions (SPDs) are derived from fragmentation functions (FFs) by solving the recombination equation for mesons, then applied to baryon production without adjustable parameters. Thermal parton distributions are determined from low-$p_T$ data, and the formalism separates partons into thermal (T) and shower (S) types, with hadron production categorized as TT+TS+SS for mesons and TTT+TTS+TSS+SSS for baryons.

## Key numbers

- Baryon/meson ratio $R_{p/\pi}$ reaches approximately 1 at $p_T \simeq 3$ GeV/c in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, as shown by STAR data.
- The fragmentation function ratio $D_{p/q}(x)/D_{\pi/q}(x)$ is at most 0.2 at $x \simeq 0.3$, much lower than the observed $R_{p/\pi}$.
- Thermal parton distribution is parameterized as ${\cal T}(q) = Cqe^{-q/T}$ where $C$ has dimension (GeV)$^{-1}$.
- The hard parton distribution is parameterized as $f_i(k) = K A / (1 + k/B)^n$ with $K = 2.5$ and $\zeta = 0.07$ as the average suppression factor related to $R_{AA}$.
- The $A$ dependence of hadron production in $pA$ collisions shows $\alpha_p(p_T) > \alpha_\pi(p_T)$ for all $p_T$ measured.

## Context

This review extends the recombination/coalescence (ReCo) approach developed by three groups (TAM, Duke, and Oregon) for hadronization at intermediate $p_T$, building on earlier work by Hwa (1980s) on valon distributions and recombination functions. It contrasts with conventional fragmentation-based approaches and challenges the standard interpretations of the Cronin effect (initial-state scattering), baryon junction models for baryon enhancement (as proposed by Kharzeev and others), and the assumption that azimuthal anisotropy requires early thermalization. The work enables jet tomography studies by providing a framework to extract jet-medium interaction properties from hadron correlation data, and is complementary to the review by Majumder (2008) which focuses on higher $p_T$ where fragmentation applies.
