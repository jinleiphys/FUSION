# Electron Capture in 163Ho and Overlap plus Exchange Corrections and the Neutrino Mass

> Holmium 163 offers perhaps the best chance to determine the neutrino mass by electron capture. This contribution treats the electron capture in 163 Holmium completely relativistic for the overlap and exchange corrections and the description of the bolometer Spectrum. The theoretical expressions are derived assuming single Slater determinants for the initial Ho and the final Dy Atoms. Electron capture is proportional to the probability to find the captured electron in the parent atom at the nucleus. Non-relativistically this is only possible for ns(1/2) electron states. Relativistically also p(1/2) electrons have a probability due to the lower part of the relativistic electron spinor, which does not disappear at the origin. Moreover relativistic effects increase by contraction the electron probability at the nucleus. Capture from other states are suppressed. However they can be allowed with smaller intensity due to finite nuclear size. The purpose of this work is to give a consistent relativistic formulation and treatment of the overlap and exchange corrections for electron capture in 163 Ho to excited atomic states in 163 Dy and to show the influence of the different configurations in the final Dy states. The overlap and exchange corrections are essential for the calorimetric spectrum of the deexcitation of the hole states in Dysprosium. The slope of the upper end of the spectrum, which contains the information on the electron neutrino mass, is different. In addition the effect of the finite energy resolution on the spectrum and on the determination of the neutrino mass is studied.

# Electron Capture in 163Ho, Overlap plus Exchange Corrections and the Neutrino Mass

## Key claim

The slope of the upper end of the calorimetric spectrum from electron capture in 163Ho, which contains the information on the electron neutrino mass, is sensitive to the relativistic overlap and exchange corrections; these corrections must be included in a maximum likelihood fit to determine the neutrino mass, because the Q-value and energy resolution are not known to the required accuracy.

## Method

The authors derived a fully relativistic formulation of the overlap and exchange corrections for electron capture in 163Ho using second quantization and Wick's theorem, assuming single Slater determinants for the initial Ho and final Dy atoms. They calculated the electron wave functions self-consistently with a Dirac-Fock code, treating the nucleus with a finite Fermi charge distribution and taking the captured electron wave function at the nuclear radius rather than at the origin. The theoretical calorimetric spectrum was computed as an incoherent sum over final hole states in Dy (3s1/2, 4s1/2, 5s1/2, 3p1/2, 4p1/2, 5p1/2) and the effect of finite energy resolution was studied by folding the spectrum with a Gaussian.

## Key numbers

- Q-value for 163Ho electron capture: recommended value Q = (2.55 ± 0.016) keV, with range 2.3 keV to 2.8 keV
- Nuclear radius for Ho: R = 1.2386 × 10⁻⁴ atomic units (from R = 1.2 × A¹/³ fm)
- Excitation energies of hole states in 163Dy: E(3s1/2, M1) = 2.040 keV (Γ = 13.7 eV), E(3p1/2, M2) = 1.836 keV (Γ = 7.2 eV), E(4s1/2, N1) = 0.411 keV (Γ = 5.3 eV), E(4p1/2, N2) = 0.333 keV (Γ = 8.0 eV), E(5s1/2, O1) = 0.048 keV (Γ = 4.3 eV)
- Maximum non-diagonal overlap: <3s'|4s> = 0.023; two-hole excitation probability is more than a factor 10⁻³ smaller than single-hole excitation
- Folding with FWHM = 1 eV reduces the integrated spectrum near the endpoint by 1%; folding with FWHM = 3 eV reduces it by 3%

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic treatment of overlap and exchange corrections by Bahcall (1960s) for lighter atoms (Z = 14 to 37) and the Vatai approximation (1970s), which set all diagonal overlaps to unity and neglected exchange contributions. It also improves upon the treatment by De Rujula (1981), who used the Vatai approximation with wave functions at the origin. The authors include for the first time the multiplicity of electrons in orbital overlaps and self-consistent hole-state wave functions in the daughter atom. The results are directly applicable to the ECHo (Electron Capture in Holmium) experiment, which uses micro-calorimeters to determine the electron neutrino mass from the upper end of the calorimetric spectrum.
