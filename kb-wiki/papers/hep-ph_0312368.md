# Entanglement in condensates involving strong interactions

> We look at two well known examples of interacting systems relating to condensed matter in which we put the strong interacting parameters. At high quark chemical potentials and low temperatures we study the entropy arising from the excitation in the BCS model of superconductivity and the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of colored quark pairs. We compare it with the ground state entropy for a system consisting of two colored quarks. In the BCS model we found that the entropy strongly depends on the energy gap. Both for the very small values of the momenta as well as those much greater than the characterizing Fermi momentum $p_f$, the ground state entropy is dominant. For the BEC case we suggest a phenomenological model to build up colored bosonic quark pairs. Here the entropy entirely depends upon the short ranged repulsive interactions between the quark pairs and vanishes for large momenta.

# Entanglement in Condensates Involving Strong Interactions

## Key claim

The paper argues that in both BCS superconductivity and Bose-Einstein condensation of colored quark pairs, the entanglement entropy arising from excitations at zero temperature is bounded by the ground-state entropy of a two-colored-quark system (ln 4), but this excitation entropy is nonzero only within a finite momentum range controlled by the interaction strength or coupling; for momenta far below or far above the Fermi surface (BCS) or for large momenta (BEC), the excitation entropy vanishes and the ground-state entropy dominates.

## Method

The authors apply second-quantized Bogoliubov canonical transformations to the Hamiltonians of an interacting Bose gas of colored quark pairs (BEC) and a BCS superconductor of quarks, treating only color as the effective degree of freedom while keeping flavors and other quantum numbers identical. For BEC, they introduce a phenomenological model of tightly bound quark pairs with short-ranged repulsive interactions characterized by strength \(U_0\); for BCS, they use the standard gap equation parameterized by coupling \(G\) and solve the entropy expressions numerically as functions of momentum \(p\). The excitation entropy for each system is compared to the constant ground-state entropy \(\ln 4\) for a system of two colored quarks, which is derived from counting color degrees of freedom \(N_c^2 = 4\) for \(SU(2)_c\).

## Key numbers

* Ground-state entropy for a system of two colored quarks: \(S_{qq} = \ln 4\) (dimensionless), independent of temperature and momentum, derived from \(N_c^2 = 4\) for \(SU(2)_c\).
* For BEC, as \(U_0 \rightarrow 0\), the excitation entropy \(S_{BEC}^{ent} \rightarrow 0\); for finite \(U_0\), \(S_{BEC}^{ent}\) is maximum at \(p \rightarrow 0\) and vanishes for large momenta.
* For BCS, the energy gap is parameterized as \(\Delta = \epsilon_f \left(\frac{2}{e}\right)^{7/3} \exp\left(\frac{-1}{4G}\right)\), where \(\epsilon_f\) is the Fermi energy at \(T=0\).
* For BCS, at the Fermi momentum \(p_f\), the excitation entropy \(S_{BCS}^{ent}\) reaches its maximum value, which equals \(\ln 4\) (the ground-state entropy), independent of the coupling \(G\).
* For BCS, for vanishing coupling \(G \rightarrow 0\), \(\Delta \rightarrow 0\) and \(S_{BCS}^{ent} \rightarrow 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of ground-state entropy for colored quark systems (Miller, Tawfik 2002; Miller 2003) to the case of strong interactions in condensates at high quark chemical potential and low temperature. It applies the BCS theory of superconductivity (Bardeen, Cooper, Schrieffer 1957) and Bose-Einstein condensation to quark matter, building on earlier studies of BCS pairings and BEC in nuclear matter (Roepke 1990s) and on the thermodynamics of \(SU(2)\) gauge theory at finite baryon density (DeGrand, Karsch, Miller 1986). The results enable future work on the role of quantum entropy in compact stellar objects and cold quark matter, and suggest that the excitation entropy in both condensates is bounded by the mixing entropy of two colored quarks, providing a measure of pair-state density around the Fermi surface.
