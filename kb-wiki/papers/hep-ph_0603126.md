# Tests of Two-Body Dirac Equation Wave Functions in the Decays of Quarkonium and Positronium into Two Photons

> Two-Body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics provide a covariant framework to investigate the problem of highly relativistic quarks in meson bound states. This formalism eliminates automatically the problems of relative time and energy, leading to a covariant three dimensional formalism with the same number of degrees of freedom as appears in the corresponding nonrelativistic problem. It provides bound state wave equations with the simplicity of the nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation. Here we begin important tests of the relativistic sixteen component wave function solutions obtained in a recent work on meson spectroscopy, extending a method developed previously for positronium decay into two photons. Preliminary to this we examine the positronium decay in the 3P_{0,2} states as well as the 1S_0. The two-gamma quarkonium decays that we investigate are for the η_{c}, η_{c}^{\prime}, χ_{c0}, χ_{c2}, π^{0}, π_{2}, a_{2}, and f_{2}^{\prime} mesons. Our results for the four charmonium states compare well with those from other quark models and show the particular importance of including all components of the wave function as well as strong and CM energy dependent potential effects on the norm and amplitude. The results for the π^{0}, although off the experimental rate by 15%, is much closer than the usual expectations from a potential model. We conclude that the Two-Body Dirac equations lead to wave functions which provide good descriptions of the two-gamma decay amplitude and can be used with some confidence for other purposes.

## Key claim

The Two-Body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics produce wave functions that give good descriptions of two-photon decay amplitudes for both positronium and quarkonium, with the pion decay rate being only 15% off the experimental value, much closer than typical potential model expectations, but the authors hedge that this conclusion applies specifically to the decays tested and that the formalism can be used "with some confidence for other purposes."

## Method

The authors computed two-photon decay amplitudes for positronium (¹S₀, ³P₀, ³P₂ states) and for quarkonium mesons (η_c, η_c′, χ_c0, χ_c2, π⁰, π₂, a₂, f₂′) using the full sixteen-component wave functions from the Two-Body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics. They extended a previously developed method for positronium decay by folding the bound-state wave function with the Feynman amplitude for quark-antiquark annihilation into two photons, using a Yukawa smearing to handle singularities at the origin in the relativistic wave functions. The decay amplitudes were evaluated in configuration space by Fourier transforming the momentum-space amplitude and using the 4×4 matrix form of the wave function solutions.

## Context

This work extends the Two-Body Dirac equation formalism previously applied to meson spectroscopy by Crater, Wong, and Van Alstine (2005) and to positronium decay by Crater (1990), testing the wave functions against two-photon decay data as a more sensitive probe than mass spectra alone. The results compare well with other quark models such as those of Godfrey and Isgur (1985), but the constraint dynamics approach uses only two parametric functions (scalar and vector potentials) rather than the six or more used in other approaches. The successful description of the pion decay rate supports the authors' earlier finding that the pion's Goldstone boson behavior emerges naturally from the full relativistic formalism, and enables future application of these wave functions to reactions such as J/ψ + π ↔ D + D̅* in heavy-ion collisions, which were previously studied using nonrelativistic models by Wong, Barnes, and Swanson (2000, 2002).
