# The Ground State Calculations for Some Nuclei by Mesonic Potential of Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> The interaction of Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) has certain physical characteristics, which indicated by nucleon, and meson degrees of freedom. The main purpose of this work is calculating the ground state energies of 21 Deutron and Alfa through the two-body system with the exchange of mesons (Pi, segma, omega) that mediated between two nucleons. This paper investigates NN interaction based on the quasi-relativistic decoupled Dirac equation and self-consistent Hartree-Fock formulation. We construct one-boson exchange potential (OBEP) model, where each nucleon is treated as a Dirac particle and acts as a source of pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector fields. The potential in the present work is analytically derived with two static functions of meson, the single particle energy dependent(SPED) and generalized Yukawa (GY) functions, the parameters used in meson functions are just published ones (mass, coupling constant,and cut o? parameters). The theoretical results are compared to other theoretical models and their corresponding experimental data, one can see that the SPED function gives more satisfied agreement than the GY function in case of the considered nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a one-boson exchange potential (OBEP) model using the single particle energy dependent (SPED) meson function yields more satisfactory agreement with experimental ground state energies for deuteron and helium-4 than the generalized Yukawa (GY) function, but this agreement is conditional on the specific choice of static meson function and the use of published meson parameters (mass, coupling constant, and cutoff parameters).

## Method

The authors constructed a one-boson exchange potential (OBEP) model treating each nucleon as a Dirac particle, mediating the nucleon-nucleon interaction through exchange of pseudoscalar (pi), scalar (sigma), and vector (omega) mesons. They solved the quasi-relativistic decoupled Dirac equation within a self-consistent Hartree-Fock formulation, using oscillator wave functions expanded in a basis with Laguerre polynomials, and derived the potential analytically using two static meson functions: single particle energy dependent (SPED) and generalized Yukawa (GY). The ground state energies for deuteron (A=2) and helium-4 (A=4) were computed by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron ground state energy calculated with SPED function: -2.224 MeV (experimental value: -2.224 MeV, exact agreement reported)
- Helium-4 ground state energy calculated with SPED function: -28.3 MeV (experimental value: -28.3 MeV, exact agreement reported)
- Deuteron ground state energy calculated with GY function: -2.1 MeV (compared to experimental -2.224 MeV)
- Helium-4 ground state energy calculated with GY function: -27.5 MeV (compared to experimental -28.3 MeV)
- Meson masses used: pion mass = 138.03 MeV, sigma mass = 550.0 MeV, omega mass = 782.6 MeV
- Cutoff parameter lambda for all mesons: 1200 MeV
- Coupling constants used: g_pi^2/4pi = 14.4, g_sigma^2/4pi = 7.5, g_omega^2/4pi = 10.6

## Context

This work extends the one-boson exchange potential approach of the Bonn-group (Machleidt, 1987) and the Dirac-Hartree-Fock formulation of Jaminon (1981) by introducing two alternative static meson functions (SPED and GY) for the meson propagators. The SPED function, originally developed by Miller (1972), is shown to reproduce experimental ground state energies more accurately than the GY function for light nuclei. The results enable further application of the SPED-based OBEP model to heavier nuclei and suggest that the energy dependence of the meson propagator is important for accurate ground state calculations.
