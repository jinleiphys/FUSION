# Color screening in (2+1)-flavor QCD

> We study correlation functions of spatially separated static quark-antiquark pairs in (2+1)-flavor QCD in order to investigate onset and nature of color screening at high temperatures. We perform lattice calculations in a wide temperature range, $140 \le T \le 5814\,{\rm MeV}$, using the highly improved staggered quark action and several lattice spacings to control discretization effects. By comparing at high temperatures our lattice results to weak-coupling calculations as well as to the zero temperature result for the energy of a static quark-antiquark pair, we observe that color screening sets in at $rT \approx 0.3$. Furthermore, we also observe that in the range $0.3 \lesssim r T \lesssim 0.6$ weak-coupling calculations in the framework of suitable effective field theories provide an adequate picture of color screening.

## Key claim

Color screening in (2+1)-flavor QCD sets in at a distance of approximately \(rT \approx 0.3\), and in the range \(0.3 \lesssim r T \lesssim 0.6\) weak-coupling calculations within effective field theories provide an adequate description of screening, but this agreement is limited to that specific distance window.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of Polyakov loop correlators and singlet correlators for static quark-antiquark pairs using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with physical strange quark mass and light quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of approximately 160 MeV. They generated gauge configurations on \(N_{\sigma}^3 \times N_{\tau}\) lattices with \(N_{\tau}=4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 16\) and aspect ratios of 4 and 6, covering a temperature range from 140 to 5814 MeV, and performed continuum extrapolations using fits of the form \(a+b/N_{\tau}^2+c/N_{\tau}^4\). The renormalized correlators were compared to weak-coupling calculations from potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) and electrostatic QCD (EQCD) frameworks.

## Key numbers

* Color screening onset occurs at \(rT \approx 0.3\), determined by comparing lattice results to weak-coupling calculations and zero-temperature static energy.
* The temperature range studied was \(140 \le T \le 5814\) MeV, with lattice calculations performed at temperatures as low as 116 MeV.
* The effective coupling from the singlet free energy reaches a maximum at a distance \(r_{\rm max}(T)\) that scales approximately as \(0.4/T\).
* The weak-coupling description is adequate in the range \(0.3 \lesssim r T \lesssim 0.6\).
* The light quark mass was set to \(m_l = m_s/20\), corresponding to a pion mass of \(m_\pi \sim 160\) MeV in the continuum limit; additional ensembles used \(m_l = m_s/5\) corresponding to \(m_\pi \approx 320\) MeV for temperatures above 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of Polyakov loop correlators in SU(2) and SU(3) gauge theories and in QCD with two and three quark flavors (Karsch 2000, Kaczmarek 2005, Petreczky 2004, Borsanyi 2015) by providing continuum-extrapolated results over a much wider temperature range and systematically comparing to weak-coupling effective field theory predictions from pNRQCD (Pineda 1997, Brambilla 1999) and EQCD (Braaten 1995). The results enable improved modeling of in-medium quarkonium properties for heavy-ion collision phenomenology, where effective field theory approaches (Burnier 2007, Brambilla 2008) require knowledge of which scale hierarchy is relevant for the physically interesting case.
