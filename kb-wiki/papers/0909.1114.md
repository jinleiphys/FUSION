# Quark-hadron phase transition in a neutron star under strong magnetic fields

> We study the effect of a strong magnetic field on the properties of neutron stars with a quark-hadron phase transition. It is shown that the magnetic field prevents the appearance of a quark phase, enhances the leptonic fraction, decreases the baryonic density extension of the mixed phase and stiffens the total equation of state, including both the stellar matter and the magnetic field contributions. Two parametrisations of a density dependent static magnetic field, increasing, respectively, fast and slowly with the density and reaching $2-4\times 10^{18}$G in the center of the star, are considered. The compact stars with strong magnetic fields have maximum mass configurations with larger masses and radius and smaller quark fractions. The parametrisation of the magnetic field with density has a strong influence on the star properties.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields (reaching 2-4 x 10^18 G at the center) prevent the appearance of a pure quark phase in hybrid neutron stars, decrease the baryonic density extension of the mixed phase, and stiffen the total equation of state, but the specific parametrization of how the magnetic field varies with density (fast vs. slow increase) strongly influences the star's mass, radius, and quark fraction.

## Method

The authors used the GM1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) to describe hadronic matter including the full baryonic octet, and the MIT Bag model for quark matter with two bag constants (165 MeV and 180 MeV). They imposed the Gibbs criteria with global charge conservation to construct the mixed hadron-quark phase, assuming a density-dependent magnetic field that increases from 10^15 G at the surface to a maximum value at the center, with two parametrizations (slow: beta=0.05, gamma=2; fast: beta=0.02, gamma=3). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for spherical stars were then integrated to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field at the center reaches 2-4 x 10^18 G for the largest B*_0 = 2 x 10^5 (in units of B^c_e = 4.414 x 10^13 G)
- For Bag^1/4 = 180 MeV with no magnetic field, the mixed phase onset is at u_1 = rho_1/rho_0 = 1.605 and pure quark phase onset at u_2 = 5.618 (rho_0 = 0.153 fm^-3)
- For Bag^1/4 = 180 MeV with B*_0 = 2 x 10^5 (slow parametrization), the mixed phase onset shifts to u_1 = 1.614 and pure quark phase onset to u_2 = 5.357
- For Bag^1/4 = 165 MeV with no magnetic field, the mixed phase onset is at u_1 = 0.832 and pure quark phase onset at u_2 = 3.567
- For Bag^1/4 = 165 MeV with B*_0 = 2 x 10^5 (fast parametrization), the pure quark phase onset shifts to u_2 = 3.317
- The energy density at the onset of pure quark phase for Bag^1/4 = 180 MeV increases from 4.794 fm^-4 (B=0) to 13.017 fm^-4 (B*_0 = 2 x 10^5, fast parametrization)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetized neutron stars by Chakrabarty (1997), who used a Dirac-Hartree-Fock approach and found that strong magnetic fields reduce the maximum mass, a result the present authors attribute to an incorrect sign in the magnetic field contribution to pressure. The paper also builds on hadronic star studies with strong magnetic fields by Broderick (2000) and Prakash (2000), and on quark star studies using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The results enable future investigations of non-spherical star configurations, which the authors note should be considered when the magnetic field contribution to pressure is comparable to the matter contribution.
