# Application of microscopic transport model in the study of nuclear equation of state from heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies

> The equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter, i.e., the thermodynamic relationship between the binding energy per nucleon, temperature, density, as well as the isospin asymmetry, has been a hot topic in nuclear physics and astrophysics for a long time. The knowledge of the nuclear EOS is essential for studying the properties of nuclei, the structure of neutron stars, the dynamics of heavy ion collision (HIC), as well as neutron star mergers. HIC offers a unique way to create nuclear matter with high density and isospin asymmetry in terrestrial laboratory, but the formed dense nuclear matter exists only for a very short period, one cannot measure the nuclear EOS directly in experiments. Practically, transport models which often incorporate phenomenological potentials as an input are utilized to deduce the EOS from the comparison with the observables measured in laboratory. The ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model has been widely employed for investigating HIC from the Fermi energy (40 MeV per nucleon) up to the CERN Large Hadron Collider energies (TeV). With further improvement in the nuclear mean-field potential term, the collision term, and the cluster recognition term of the UrQMD model, the newly measured collective flow and nuclear stopping data of light charged particles by the FOPI Collaboration can be reproduced. In this article we highlight our recent results on the studies of the nuclear EOS and the nuclear symmetry energy with the UrQMD model. New opportunities and challenges in the extraction of the nuclear EOS from transport models and HIC experiments are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the UrQMD transport model, with recent improvements to its mean-field potential, collision term, and cluster recognition, can reproduce newly measured collective flow and nuclear stopping data from the FOPI Collaboration, thereby enabling extraction of the nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy; however, the extracted values of the incompressibility \(K_0\) depend on the specific parametrization of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section used, with different parametrizations yielding central values ranging from 190 to 275 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, incorporating a Skyrme-potential-based mean field with surface, surface-asymmetry, and symmetry-energy terms, plus a density- and momentum-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section parametrized by four sets (FP1, FP2, FP4, FP5). They simulated \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions at beam energies of 150, 250, 400, 600, 800, and 1000 MeV per nucleon, and compared the calculated directed flow \(v_1\), elliptic flow \(v_2\), and nuclear stopping power \(varxz\) of light charged particles (free protons, deuterons, tritons, and \(A=3\) clusters) with FOPI experimental data. Clusters were constructed using an isospin-dependent minimum spanning tree method.

## Key numbers

*   The incompressibility \(K_0\) of isospin symmetric nuclear matter extracted from the \(v_{2n}\) of free protons is \(K_0 = 240 \pm 20\) MeV for the FU3FP4 parametrization and \(K_0 = 275 \pm 25\) MeV for the FU3FP5 parametrization of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section.
*   From the \(v_{2n}\) of deuterons, the extracted \(K_0\) is \(190 \pm 10\) MeV for FU3FP4 and \(225 \pm 20\) MeV for FU3FP5.
*   Combining results from free protons and deuterons yields an averaged \(K_0 = 220 \pm 40\) MeV for the FU3FP4 parametrization.
*   The three Skyrme interactions used to study \(K_0\) (Skxs15, MSK1, SKX) give \(K_0\) values of 201 MeV, 234 MeV, and 271 MeV, respectively, while all yield similar symmetry energy parameters (\(S_0 \approx 30\)–32 MeV and \(L \approx 33\)–35 MeV).
*   The medium correction factor for the nucleon-nucleon cross section uses parameters such as \(p_0 = 0.425\) GeV/\(c\) (FP1), 0.225 GeV/\(c\) (FP2), 0.3 GeV/\(c\) (FP4), and 0.34 GeV/\(c\) (FP5), with \(\kappa\) values of 5, 3, 8, and 12 respectively.

## Context

This work extends prior constraints on the nuclear incompressibility \(K_0\) from heavy-ion collisions, notably the analysis by Danielewicz et al. (2002) which ruled out \(K_0\) larger than 380 MeV or less than 167 MeV, and the more recent extraction of \(K_0 = 190 \pm 30\) MeV by Le Fevre et al. (2015) using the IQMD model. The present study shows that the extracted \(K_0\) depends on the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section parametrization and on whether free protons or deuterons are used, and it highlights the need for further work to resolve differences between transport models (UrQMD vs. IQMD) and to better constrain the density-dependent symmetry energy at supranormal densities.
