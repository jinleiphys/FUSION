# Short-Range Correlations Between Partons in a Proton

> A principal lesson from recreating droplets of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy ion collisions is that it is a strongly coupled liquid, not a plasma of partons. The energy density and pressure of quarks and gluons confined within a proton are comparable to those of QGP at or just above the QCD transition temperature. Given this similarity between protons and QGP, we propose that the investigation of correlations between nearby partons within a proton must be a central goal for the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Here, we ask how EIC measurements can discern such short-range correlations (SRCs) of quark pairs. Doing so would characterize the strongly coupled interior of a proton, augmenting the one-parton-at-a-time understanding of protons via (generalized) parton distribution functions, and could at the same time yield a key ingredient for the microscopic understanding of the liquid nature of QGP. Motivated by the experiments that have been used to demonstrate the existence of SRCs between nucleon pairs within a nucleus, we propose using EIC observables involving measurements of a jet and a pion, together with the scattered electron, to seek and quantify the possible existence of SRCs between quark pairs within a nucleon. The pronounced isospin dependence observed in the dominance of $np$ SRCs over $pp$ or $nn$ SRCs has played a central role in establishing the importance of SRCs among nucleons in nuclei. Analogously, the QCD attraction in the $ud$ diquark channel can make the $ud$ SRC stronger than the $uu$ and $dd$ SRCs, allowing a first observation of partonic SRCs.

# Short-Range Correlations Between Partons in a Proton

## Key claim
The energy density and pressure within a proton are comparable to those of quark-gluon plasma at or just above the QCD transition temperature (T_c ~ 155 MeV), motivating the search for short-range correlations between partons within a proton. The authors propose that measuring azimuthal angle correlations between a jet in the current fragmentation region and a pion in the target fragmentation region at the future Electron-Ion Collider can reveal partonic short-range correlations, with the expectation that ud correlations will be stronger than uu or dd correlations due to QCD attraction in the diquark channel.

## Method
The authors performed a Monte Carlo study using Pythia 8 for deep-inelastic scattering at EIC kinematics, generating baseline events with no partonic short-range correlations. They then constructed modified event samples in which 5% or 10% of events had 400 MeV momentum kicks applied in opposite directions to the struck u quark and a d quark in the proton remnant, modeling ud diquark short-range correlations. The Breit-frame azimuthal angle difference phi_hJ between the struck u-quark jet (proxy) and the leading pion in the target fragmentation region was evaluated, comparing distributions for pi- versus pi+ to isolate the isospin-dependent SRC signal.

## Key numbers
- The QCD transition temperature is T_c ~ 155 MeV (Borsanyi 2013, HotQCD 2014).
- If the proton radius were 0.86 fm (charge radius) or 0.49 fm (mass radius), the proton energy density would equal that of QGP at T ~ 155 MeV or 200 MeV respectively, per lattice QCD calculations.
- Lattice QCD studies indicate ud diquark pairs within a nucleon have typical size r_qq ~ 0.5-0.7 fm, suggesting relative momentum |k| ~ 300-400 MeV (Francis 2022).
- The Monte Carlo study used kinematic cuts: 4 GeV < Q < 5 GeV and x > 0.05.
- For beam energies 5x41 GeV^2, 26.5% of target pions fall in the central detector (|eta| < 3.5), 16.8% in the B0 detector (eta ~ 4.5-5.9), and 1.0% in the forward region (eta > 6).
- For beam energies 10x100 GeV^2, only 2.9% of target pions fall in the central detector, 59.0% in B0, and 5.5% in forward.
- The momentum kick applied to model SRCs was 400 MeV, chosen based on lattice results.
- The analysis restricted to target pion pseudorapidity 2 < eta_h < 3.5 to ensure charge tagging and particle identification capability.

## Context
This work extends the study of short-range correlations from nucleon-nucleon pairs in nuclei (Hen 2016, Ciofi degli Atti 2015) to parton-parton pairs within a proton, motivated by the discovery that quark-gluon plasma is a strongly coupled liquid (PHENIX 2004, BRAHMS 2004, PHOBOS 2004, STAR 2005). The proposed observable is analogous to triple-coincidence measurements that demonstrated np SRC dominance over pp or nn SRCs in nuclei (Tang 2002, Piasetzky 2006, LabHallA 2014). The work builds on lattice QCD calculations of proton gravitational form factors (Shanahan 2018, Hackett 2023) and diquark correlators (Francis 2022), as well as the long-standing diquark model of baryons (Close 1973, Farrar 1975, Jaffe 2003). The results motivate experimental searches at the EIC and potentially in archival HERA data and JLAB CLAS12 measurements (CLAS 2022, CLAS 2026).
