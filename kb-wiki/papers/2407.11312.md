# Proton drip line of deformed hypernuclei

> The proton drip line of (hyper)nuclei is examined within the framework of the deformed Skyrme-Hartree Fock approach by adjusting the nuclear force parameters to exactly reproduce the core binding energies. The impact of adding a Λ hyperon in a s or p state is studied, and it is found that in some cases the deformation effect facilitates the extension of the drip line by an added p-state hyperon. However, no extension of the drip line is found for s-state hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding a Λ hyperon in a p state can extend the proton drip line for some deformed hypernuclei, but only when the hyperon occupies a p substate with the same deformation as the core; no extension is found for s-state hypernuclei, and the effect depends critically on the deformation of the core.

## Method

The authors used the deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (DSHF) approach with SLy4 and SkI4 nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces and the SLL4 ΛN interaction. They adjusted the surface parameter t₂ and spin-orbit parameter b₄ of the NN forces to exactly reproduce the experimental binding energies of the core nuclei, then computed the single-proton and two-proton separation energies for hypernuclei with the Λ in s[000] and p[101] and p[110] states, comparing deformed and spherical calculations.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶F core: experimental single-proton separation energy S_p = -0.53 MeV; with adjusted SLy4 force, the p[110] hypernucleus ¹⁷_ΛpF has S_p = +0.64 MeV (stable), while the s-state hypernucleus has S_p = -0.27 MeV (unstable)
- For ¹⁹Na core: S_p = -0.32 MeV; the p[110] hypernucleus ²⁰_ΛpNa has S_p = +0.38 MeV (stable)
- For ¹⁶Ne core: S_pp = -1.40 MeV; the p[110] hypernucleus ¹⁷_ΛpNe has S_pp = +0.97 MeV (stable)
- For ¹⁹Mg core: S_pp = -0.77 MeV; the p[110] hypernucleus ²⁰_ΛpMg has S_pp = +0.63 MeV (stable)
- For ¹⁷_ΛpF with adjusted SLy4 force: Λ removal energy B_Λ = 3.9 MeV for p[110] state, 13.8 MeV for s[000] state
- For ²⁰_ΛpNa: B_Λ = 5.9 MeV for p[110] state
- For ¹⁷_ΛpNe: B_Λ = 4.3 MeV for p[110] state
- For ²⁰_ΛpMg: B_Λ = 6.1 MeV for p[110] state

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of proton-rich hypernuclei by Lanskoy (2022) and Kornilova (2023), who identified candidate nuclei but could not make definite predictions due to the delicate dependence on NN and NΛ interactions. The present study improves accuracy by adjusting NN force parameters to reproduce core binding energies exactly and by including deformation effects, which were essential for stabilizing p-state hypernuclei. The results enable future beyond-mean-field calculations and motivate experimental searches at facilities such as NICA, FAIR, and HIAF.
