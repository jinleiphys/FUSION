# A microscopic approach to $^{3}$He scattering

> We propose a practical folding model to describe $^{3}$He elastic scattering. In the model, $^{3}$He optical potentials are constructed by making the folding procedure twice. First the nucleon-target potential is evaluated by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix with the target density and localizing the nonlocal folding potential with the Brieva--Rook method, and second the resulting local nucleon-target potential is folded with the $^{3}$He density. This double single-folding model well describes $^{3}$He elastic scattering from $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb targets in a wide incident-energy range from 30 MeV/nucleon to 150 MeV/nucleon with no adjustable parameter. Spin-orbit force effects on differential cross sections are found to be appreciable only at higher incident energies such as 150 MeV/nucleon. Three-nucleon breakup effects of $^{3}$He are investigated with the continuum discretized coupled-channels method and are found to be appreciable only at lower incident energies around 40 MeV/nucleon. Effects of knock-on exchange processes are also analyzed.

## Key claim

The double single-folding (DSF) model, which first constructs a microscopic nucleon-target potential by folding the Melbourne g-matrix with the target density and localizing it via the Brieva-Rook method, then folds that local potential with the 3He density, describes 3He elastic scattering from 58Ni and 208Pb across 30-150 MeV/nucleon with no adjustable parameter, provided that three-nucleon breakup effects (appreciable only around 40 MeV/nucleon) and spin-orbit effects (appreciable only at 150 MeV/nucleon) are accounted for where relevant.

## Method

The nucleon-target potential was constructed by folding the Melbourne g-matrix (an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in nuclear matter) with the target density under the target-density approximation, then localizing the resulting nonlocal potential using the Brieva-Rook method. This local nucleon-target potential was then folded with the 3He density to produce the 3He-target optical potential. Three-nucleon breakup effects of 3He were investigated using the continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with a model space including 0+ and 2+ breakup states up to 20 MeV excitation.

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross sections for 3He+58Ni and 3He+208Pb were calculated across 30-150 MeV/nucleon; the DSF model yielded better agreement with experimental data than the DF-FDA model.
- For 3He+208Pb scattering at 72.3 MeV/nucleon, the difference between results using the Melbourne g-matrix (from Bonn-B) and a g-matrix from CD-Bonn was only 5 mb.
- Spin-orbit force effects on differential cross sections were appreciable only at higher incident energies such as 150 MeV/nucleon.
- Three-nucleon breakup effects were appreciable only at lower incident energies around 40 MeV/nucleon.
- For 3He+58Ni scattering at 72.3 MeV/nucleon, the DSF model was better than DF-FDA at q < 2 fm^-1, but DF-FDA was superior at q > 2 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the model Hamiltonian approach previously applied successfully to deuteron scattering (with CDCC) and 4He scattering (Egashira 2014) to 3He scattering, demonstrating that the DSF model works systematically for these three projectiles. The approach contrasts with the double-folding frozen-density approximation (DF-FDA) model, which typically requires adjustable parameters; the DSF model yields better agreement with experimental data for 3He scattering without such adjustments. This enables future microscopic analyses of 3He-induced reactions on unstable nuclei where no phenomenological nucleon-target potentials exist.
