# Quantum interference of rho0- and omega-mesons in the pi N -> e+e- N reaction

> The study of the $蟺N\to 蟻^0 N$ and $蟺N \to 蠅N$ amplitudes below and close to the vector meson production threshold ($1.4<\sqrt s <1.8$ GeV)reveals a rich structure arising from the presence of baryon resonances in this energy range. These resonances are reflected in the interference pattern of the $e^+e^-$ decays of the $蟻^0$- and $蠅$-mesons produced in $蟺^- p$ and $蟺^+ n$ reactions. We discuss the shape and magnitude of the $蟻^0$-$蠅$ interference in the $蟺^-p \to e^+e^- n$ and $蟺^+n \to e^+e^- p$ reaction cross sections as functions of the total center of mass energy $\sqrt s$. We find contrasted results: the interference is largely destructive for the $蟺^-p \to e^+e^- n$ cross section but constructive for the $蟺^+n \to e^+e^- p$ cross section. An experimental study of these reactions would provide significant constraints on the coupling of vector meson-nucleon channels to low-lying baryon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the quantum interference between $\rho^0$ and $\omega$ mesons in the $\pi N \rightarrow e^+e^- N$ reaction is largely destructive for the $\pi^-p \rightarrow e^+e^- n$ cross section but constructive for the $\pi^+n \rightarrow e^+e^- p$ cross section, with the caveat that the model is restricted to s-wave vector meson-nucleon final states and is valid only for $\sqrt s \leq 1.75$ GeV.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic unitary coupled-channel approach to meson-nucleon scattering, fitting data on pion-nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering and meson photoproduction in the energy window $1.4<\sqrt s <1.8$ GeV. They computed the $\pi N \rightarrow \rho^0 N$ and $\pi N \rightarrow \omega N$ amplitudes from this model, then applied Vector Meson Dominance to convert these amplitudes into $\pi N \rightarrow e^+e^- N$ cross sections, including only s-wave scattering in the $\rho N$ and $\omega N$ channels.

## Key numbers

- The $\pi^-p \rightarrow e^+e^- n$ differential cross section at $\sqrt s=1.5$ GeV is less than 10 nb GeV$^{-2}$ for the range of $e^+e^-$ invariant masses considered.
- The $\pi^+n \rightarrow e^+e^- p$ differential cross section at $\sqrt s=1.5$ GeV is of the order of 0.15 $\mu$b GeV$^{-2}$.
- The Vector Meson Dominance coupling constants are $|f_\rho|=0.036$ GeV$^2$ and $|f_\omega|=0.011$ GeV$^2$, with both taken as positive.
- The coefficients $\overline{C}_{\frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{2}}$ and $\overline{C}_{\frac{3}{2}\frac{3}{2}}$ at $\sqrt s=1.5$ GeV are 1.12 and 0.02 GeV$^2$ respectively for $e^+e^-$ pair invariant masses around 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel model of Lutz, Friman, and Soyeur (2001) which dynamically generates baryon resonances in the S$_{11}$, S$_{31}$, D$_{13}$, and D$_{33}$ partial waves. It complements programs studying photo- and electroproduction of baryon resonances at ELSA, MAMI, and Jefferson Laboratory. The predictions could be tested at GSI-Darmstadt with the HADES detector, and the results would provide constraints on $\rho N N^*$ and $\omega N N^*$ coupling strengths needed for models of vector meson propagation in nuclear matter and for interpreting lepton pair production in heavy ion collisions.
