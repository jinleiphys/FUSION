# Analysis of recent CLAS data on $Σ^{\ast }(1385)$ photoproduction off a neutron target

> Based on recent experimental data obtained by the CLAS Collaboration, the $Σ(1385)$ photoproduction off a neutron target at laboratory photon energies $E_γ$ up to 2.5\,GeV is investigated in an effective Lagrangian approach including $s$-, $u$-, and $t$-channel Born-term contributions. The present calculation does not take into account any explicit $s$-channel baryon-resonance contributions, however, in the spirit of duality, we include $t$-channel exchanges of mesonic Regge trajectories. The onset of the Regge regime is controlled by smoothly interpolating between Feynman-type single-meson exchanges and full-fledged Regge-trajectory exchanges. Gauge invariance broken by the Regge treatment is fully restored by introducing contact-type interaction currents that result from the implementation of \textit{local} gauge invariance in terms of generalized Ward-Takahashi identities. The cross sections for the $γn\to K^+Σ^*(1385)^-$ reaction are calculated and compared with experimental results from the CLAS and LEPS collaborations. Despite its simplicity, the present theoretical approach provides a good description of the main features of the data. However, the parameters fitted to the data show that the gauge-invariance-restoring contact term plays a large role which may point to large contributions from final-state interactions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the main features of CLAS and LEPS data for Σ*(1385) photoproduction off a neutron can be reproduced without explicit s-channel baryon resonances, using only Reggeized t-channel exchanges and a gauge-invariance-restoring contact term. However, the authors qualify that the contact term plays a disproportionately large role, which may indicate significant contributions from final-state interactions that cannot be independently constrained.

## Method
The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach with s-channel nucleon pole, u-channel Λ(1116) and Σ*(1385) exchanges, and a Reggeized t-channel K exchange, using an interpolating function to smoothly transition between Feynman-type single-meson exchange and full Regge-trajectory exchange. Gauge invariance broken by the Regge treatment is restored by introducing contact-type interaction currents derived from generalized Ward-Takahashi identities. The nine free parameters (including Regge coupling strength, cutoff parameters, and interpolation parameters) are fitted to 75 differential cross-section data points from CLAS and LEPS collaborations using the MINUIT code.

## Key numbers
- Photon energy range: Eγ up to 2.5 GeV
- Reduced chi-squared: χ²/dof = 1.75 for the fit to 75 data points
- Fitted Regge coupling constant: f_KNΣ*^Regge = -1.22 ± 0.02
- Fitted s-channel cutoff: Λ_s = 1.03 ± 0.12 GeV
- Fitted u-channel cutoff: Λ_u = 0.81 ± 0.03 GeV
- Fitted t-channel cutoff: Λ_t = 1.45 ± 0.05 GeV
- Regge trajectory slope: α'_K = 0.7 GeV⁻²
- Kaon trajectory signature parameter: η = 1.0 (subtraction of degenerate secondary trajectory)
- Magnetic coupling constants for γΛΣ*: f₁ = 4.52, f₂ = 5.63
- KNΛ coupling constant: g_KNΛ = -13.24
- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: κ_n = -1.913
- Σ*⁻ anomalous magnetic moment: κ_Σ*⁻ = -2.43

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Σ(1385) photoproduction off protons by Yongseok Oh (2008), Gao (2010), and He (2014), and is the first application of the gauge-invariance-preserving Regge method proposed by Haberzettl (2015). The approach contrasts with earlier treatments that included explicit s-channel baryon resonances, instead invoking a "poor-man's duality" where t-channel Regge trajectories substitute for s-channel resonances. The results suggest that future experiments at JLab (CLAS) or CERN (COMPASS) with wider energy and angle coverage could resolve whether s-channel resonances are needed to describe finer structures in the data.
