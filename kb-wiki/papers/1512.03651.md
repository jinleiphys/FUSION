# Scattering asymptotic conditions in Euclidean relativistic quantum theory

> We discuss the formulation of the scattering asymptotic condition as a strong limit in Euclidean quantum theories satisfying the Osterwalder-Schrader axioms. When used with the invariance principle this provides a constructive method to compute scattering observables directly in the Euclidean formulation of the theory, without an explicit analytic continuation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that scattering observables can be computed directly in the Euclidean representation of relativistic quantum field theory without analytic continuation, provided the Schwinger functions satisfy the Osterwalder-Schrader axioms and the injection operators mapping asymptotic Hilbert spaces to the physical Hilbert space can be constructed with appropriate mass-filtering functions.

## Method

The authors formulate the scattering asymptotic condition using a two-Hilbert space framework, where channel injection operators map asymptotic particle states (tensor products of irreducible Poincaré representations) to the physical Hilbert space constructed from reflection-positive Schwinger functions. They construct Euclidean analogs of Haag-Ruelle quasi-local field operators using functions \(h(\nabla^2)\) that filter the mass spectrum, and show that the Cook condition for existence of wave operators can be evaluated using the Euclidean inner product expressed directly in terms of Schwinger functions. The invariance principle replaces the Hamiltonian \(H\) by \(-e^{-\beta H}\) to enable direct Euclidean computation of S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* The model calculation in Kopp (2011) demonstrated accurate computation of sharp-momentum transition matrix elements for energies from 50 MeV to 2 GeV using the invariance principle with a polynomial approximation of \(e^{inx}\) for \(x \in [0,1]\).
* The integrand in the Cook condition for two-particle scattering asymptotically behaves like \(t^{-3/2}\) for a model connected four-point function, which is sufficient for convergence.
* The disconnected contribution to the Cook condition vanishes identically when the injection operator includes mass-filtering functions \(h(m^2)\) that are 1 at the asymptotic particle mass and 0 elsewhere on the Lehmann weight support.

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Wessels (2003), Kopp (2011), and Polyzou (2013) for computing scattering observables in Euclidean quantum field theory. It provides the explicit construction of injection operators (Euclidean Haag-Ruelle operators) that were assumed to exist in Polyzou (2013), completing the demonstration that the scattering asymptotic condition can be formulated as a strong limit. The approach contrasts with LSZ reduction, which requires analytic continuation, and with the original Haag-Ruelle formulation (Haag 1958, Ruelle 1962) by working entirely in the Euclidean framework. The method enables direct computation of scattering observables from Schwinger functions without solving singular integral equations or performing analytic continuation.
