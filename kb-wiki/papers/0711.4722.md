# The heavy-quark potential in an anisotropic plasma

> We determine the hard-loop resummed propagator in an anisotropic QCD plasma in general covariant gauges and define a potential between heavy quarks from the Fourier transform of its static limit. We find that there is stronger attraction on distance scales on the order of the inverse Debye mass for quark pairs aligned along the direction of anisotropy than for transverse alignment.

# The heavy-quark potential in an anisotropic plasma

## Key claim

The heavy-quark potential in an anisotropic QCD plasma exhibits stronger attraction for quark-antiquark pairs aligned along the direction of momentum anisotropy than for pairs aligned transversely, but only at distance scales on the order of the inverse Debye mass; the effect is absent at very short distances and vanishes in the limit of extreme anisotropy.

## Method

The authors compute the hard-loop resummed gluon propagator in an anisotropic QCD plasma using a general covariant gauge, extending the tensor basis of Romatschke and Strickland (2003) to four independent tensors. They define the real part of the non-relativistic heavy-quark potential from the Fourier transform of the static (zero-frequency) limit of the propagator, using an anisotropic phase-space distribution function parameterized by an anisotropy parameter ξ. The potential is evaluated analytically to linear order in ξ for small anisotropy and numerically for general ξ.

## Key numbers

- For ξ ≪ 1 and r parallel to the anisotropy direction n, the potential at r = 1/m_D is V(r ∥ n) = V_iso(r) × [1 + ξ × 0.27], where V_iso(r) = −g²C_F/(4πr) × exp(−r m_D).
- For ξ ≪ 1 and r perpendicular to n, the potential at r = 1/m_D is V(r ⟂ n) = V_iso(r) × [1 + ξ × 0.115].
- In the isotropic limit ξ = 0, the potential reduces to the standard Debye-screened potential V_iso(r) = −g²C_F/(4πr) × exp(−r m_D).
- In the limit ξ → ∞, the potential becomes the vacuum Coulomb potential V(r, ξ=∞) = −g²C_F/(4πr).
- For realistic coupling α_s ≈ 0.3, the Debye screening length λ_D = 1/m_D is approximately equal to the medium scale r_med(T) ≈ 0.5 × (T_c/T) fm from Mocsy and Petreczky (2007) and Kaczmarek et al. (2004).

## Context

This work extends the hard-thermal-loop resummed perturbation theory approach of Laine et al. (2006), who derived a Schrödinger equation for the finite-temperature Wilson loop including an imaginary part from Landau damping, to the case of a locally anisotropic plasma. The anisotropic distribution models the expansion of a real quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, where shear viscosity leads to momentum anisotropy as described by Asakawa et al. (2006). The results enable future numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation for quarkonium states in an anisotropic medium and motivate studies of how the width of quarkonium states from Landau damping is modified by anisotropy.
