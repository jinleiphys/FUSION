# Nuclear deformation effects in photoproduction of $ρ$ mesons in ultraperipheral isobaric collisions

> We have investigated the $ρ^{0}$ meson photoproduction in ultraperipheral isobaric collisions between $_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}+_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}$ and $_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}+_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, employing the dipole model with the equivalent photon approximation. By implementing the Woods-Saxon distribution to represent the nuclear mass density, which is derived from density functional theory with an inclusion of nuclear deformation effects, we have calculated the transverse momentum $q_{T}$ spectra in isobaric collisions. We observe the characteristic dip behavior in these spectra, indicative of diffraction phenomena in high-energy physics. We notice that the deformation effects cause a nearly linear increase with $q_{T}^{2}$ for $q_{T}^{2}\lesssim0.015$ $\textrm{GeV}^{2}$, aligning with experimental observations. We offer a simple explanation for the observed behavior in these spectra by introducing the effective width of the nuclei in the thickness function. We also extend our discussion on the $ρ^{0}$ meson photoproduction with the targets $^{63}\textrm{Cu}$,$^{197}\textrm{Au}$, and $^{238}\textrm{U}$.

## Key claim

The ratio of differential cross sections for $\rho^{0}$ meson photoproduction in Ru+Ru versus Zr+Zr ultraperipheral collisions shows a nearly linear increase with $q_{T}^{2}$ for $q_{T}^{2}\lesssim0.015$ GeV$^{2}$ when nuclear deformation is included, matching experimental observations; this behavior can be explained through an effective width parameter of the nuclear thickness function.

## Method

The authors employed the dipole model within the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) to calculate $\rho^{0}$ meson photoproduction cross sections in ultraperipheral isobaric collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. They implemented Woods-Saxon distributions for nuclear mass density derived from density functional theory, incorporating quadrupole deformation ($\beta_{2}=0.16$) for Ru-96 and octupole deformation ($\beta_{3}=0.20$) for Zr-96, with parameters matched to flow observables from STAR (2021) and Zhang (2021). The differential cross sections were computed using the ZMCintegral package, and the ratio $R_{\mathrm{Ru/Zr}} = (d\sigma_{\mathrm{Ru}}/dq_{T}^{2})/(d\sigma_{\mathrm{Zr}}/dq_{T}^{2})$ was analyzed as a function of transverse momentum squared.

## Key numbers

- Ru-96 nuclear parameters: $R=5.093$ fm, $a=0.471$ fm, $\beta_{2}=0.16$, $\beta_{3}=0$ (deformed case)
- Zr-96 nuclear parameters: $R=5.021$ fm, $a=0.517$ fm, $\beta_{2}=0$, $\beta_{3}=0.20$ (deformed case)
- The ratio $R_{\mathrm{Ru/Zr}}$ equals $(44/40)^{2}\approx1.21$ and is independent of $q_{T}^{2}$ without nuclear deformation
- The characteristic dip in differential cross section occurs at $q_{T}^{2}\simeq0.024$ GeV$^{2}$
- Effective width $w_{T}$ values: Ru (body) = 3.628 fm, Ru (tip) = 3.372 fm, Ru (spherical) = 3.544 fm, Zr (spherical) = 3.571 fm
- Slope parameter $\delta w_{T}\approx17$ GeV$^{-2}$ for tip-tip Ru+Ru collisions and $\delta w_{T}\approx-5$ GeV$^{-2}$ for body-body Ru+Ru collisions
- For EIC targets: Cu-63 ($R=4.200$ fm, $a=0.596$ fm), Au-197 ($R=6.380$ fm, $a=0.535$ fm), U-238 ($R=6.831$ fm, $a=0.598$ fm, $\beta_{2}=0.239$)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector meson photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions, which have been extensively investigated for spherical nuclei like Au+Au and Pb+Pb by STAR (2022) and ALICE collaborations. It builds on the authors' prior work on dilepton photoproduction in isobaric collisions (Lin, 2022) and studies of nuclear deformation effects on dilepton production (Luo, 2023). The results demonstrate that nuclear structure information can be extracted from photoproduction ratios in isobaric collisions, and the approach can be extended to electron-ion collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider with targets including Cu-63, Au-197, and U-238.
