# Two-Proton Correlations from PB+PB Central Collisions

> The two-proton correlation function at midrapidity from Pb+Pb central collisions at 158 AGeV has been measured by the NA49 experiment. The preliminary results are compared to model predictions from proton source distributions of static thermal Gaussian sources and the transport models of RQMD and VENUS. We obtain an effective proton source size 4.0 +-0.15(stat.) +0.06-0.18(syst.) fm. The RQMD model underpredicts the correlation function (source size 4.41 fm), while the VENUS model overpredicts the correlation function (source size 3.55 fm).

## Key claim

The NA49 experiment measures an effective proton source size of 4.0 fm from two-proton correlations in Pb+Pb central collisions at 158 AGeV, but this result is preliminary and the extracted size depends on comparisons to model calculations using a chi-squared minimization over the first 8 data points (q_inv below 48 MeV/c), with the RQMD model underpredicting and the VENUS model overpredicting the correlation amplitude.

## Method

The two-proton correlation function at midrapidity was constructed as the ratio of true proton pair distributions to mixed-event pair distributions in q_inv, using protons identified via dE/dx in TPCs and time-of-flight, with a 2 cm pair-distance cut at the TPC midplane to remove close-pair reconstruction inefficiency. The measured correlation function was corrected for weak decay proton contamination (estimated at 30% of pairs) and finite momentum resolution, then compared to calculations from isotropic Gaussian sources with various size and temperature parameters and from the transport models RQMD v2.3 and VENUS v4.12, using chi-squared per degree of freedom over q_inv below 48 MeV/c to quantify agreement.

## Key numbers

- Effective proton source size: sigma_eff = 4.0 +/- 0.15 (stat.) +0.06/-0.18 (syst.) fm
- RQMD model effective source size: sigma_eff = 4.41 fm (underpredicts correlation)
- VENUS model effective source size: sigma_eff = 3.55 fm (overpredicts correlation)
- Gaussian widths for RQMD freeze-out protons: (sigma_x, sigma_y, sigma_z, sigma_t) = (7.6, 7.7, 6.4, 7.0) fm
- Gaussian widths for VENUS freeze-out protons: (sigma_x, sigma_y, sigma_z, sigma_t) = (3.6, 3.6, 4.3, 1.9) fm
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: 1.47 for RQMD, 2.29 for VENUS (8 data points, ndf=8)
- Combined sample contained about 10^5 pairs with q_inv below 120 MeV/c, 75% from TOF analysis
- Peak in correlation function at q_inv approximately 20 MeV/c
- Impact parameter for 5% most central events: b less than or equal to 3.3 fm

## Context

This work presents the first two-proton correlation measurement at SPS energies, extending studies from lower-energy measurements at GSI (ALADIN collaboration, 1997) and AGS (E895 preliminary) to 158 AGeV, and finds that the effective proton source size is similar across a wide energy range despite expectations from pion interferometry that source size should increase with bombarding energy (Gazdzicki and Roehrich, 1995). The comparison to RQMD and VENUS transport models tests their description of freeze-out dynamics, and the result enables future multi-dimensional correlation analyses with higher statistics to probe the shape of the proton source.
