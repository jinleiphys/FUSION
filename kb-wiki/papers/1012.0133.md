# The effect of hyperfine mixing in electromagnetic and semileptonic decays of doubly heavy baryons

> We consider the role played by hyperfine mixing in electromagnetic and semileptonic decays of doubly heavy baryons, which give rise to large corrections to the decay widths in both cases.

## Key claim

Hyperfine mixing between $S_h=0$ and $S_h=1$ spin components in doubly heavy baryons produces large corrections to both electromagnetic and semileptonic decay widths, but the effect is negligible for $cc$ and $bb$ baryons and only noticeable in the $bc$ sector due to the finite heavy quark masses.

## Method

The authors used a variational procedure to determine masses and wave functions of doubly heavy baryons in the $S_h$ basis (where the total spin of the heavy quark subsystem is well defined), then diagonalized the mass matrix to obtain mixing coefficients and physical masses for the $bc$ baryons. Decay widths were computed for both unmixed and mixed (physical) states in semileptonic $b \to c$ transitions and electromagnetic transitions, with results compared to prior calculations by Roberts and Pervin (2008, 2009) and Branz et al. (2009).

## Key numbers

- Mixing coefficients for $\Xi_{bc}$ states: $\Xi_{bc}^{(1)} = 0.902\,\Xi'_{bc} + 0.431\,\Xi_{bc}$ with mass $6967$ MeV; $\Xi_{bc}^{(2)} = -0.431\,\Xi'_{bc} + 0.902\,\Xi_{bc}$ with mass $6919$ MeV
- Mixing coefficients for $\Omega_{bc}$ states: $\Omega_{bc}^{(1)} = 0.899\,\Omega'_{bc} + 0.437\,\Omega_{bc}$ with mass $7046$ MeV; $\Omega_{bc}^{(2)} = -0.437\,\Omega'_{bc} + 0.899\,\Omega_{bc}$ with mass $7005$ MeV
- Semileptonic decay width $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc}^{(2)} \to \Xi_{cc}^*\,l\bar\nu_l) = 0.017 \times 10^{-14}$ GeV (mixed state), compared to unmixed $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc} \to \Xi_{cc}^*\,l\bar\nu_l) = 0.75 \times 10^{-14}$ GeV
- Semileptonic decay width $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc}^{(2)} \to \Xi_{cc}\,l\bar\nu_l) = 3.50 \times 10^{-14}$ GeV (mixed state), compared to unmixed $\Gamma(\Xi_{bc} \to \Xi_{cc}\,l\bar\nu_l) = 2.57 \times 10^{-14}$ GeV
- Electromagnetic decay width divided by $(M_I-M_F)^3$ for $\Xi_{bcu}^* \to \Xi_{bcu}^{(1)}\gamma$ = $248 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$ (mixed state), compared to unmixed $\Xi_{bcu}^* \to \Xi_{bcu}\gamma$ = $333.9 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the findings of Roberts and Pervin (2008, 2009) on hyperfine mixing in semileptonic decays of doubly heavy baryons, qualitatively confirming their results while finding quantitative differences of up to a factor of two. The authors claim their work in Albertus et al. (2010) was the first to consider hyperfine mixing for electromagnetic decays of these systems. The results enable future experimental measurements of semileptonic and electromagnetic decay widths to provide information on mixing parameters, though electromagnetic transitions alone require a theoretical model for interpretation.
