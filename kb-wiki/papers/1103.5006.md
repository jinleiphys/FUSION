# Gravitational Anomaly and Transport

> Quantum anomalies give rise to new transport phenomena. In particular a magnetic field can induce an anomalous current via the chiral magnetic effect and a vortex in the relativistic fluid can also induce a current via the chiral vortical effect. The related transport coefficients can be calculated via Kubo formulas. We evaluate the Kubo formula for the anomalous vortical conductivity at weak coupling and show that it receives contributions proportional to the gravitational anomaly coefficient. The gravitational anomaly gives rise to an anomalous vortical effect even for an uncharged fluid.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the anomalous vortical conductivity receives a contribution proportional to the gravitational anomaly coefficient, which gives rise to an anomalous vortical effect even for an uncharged fluid; this temperature-squared term was not seen in prior holographic calculations and was previously argued to be an undetermined integration constant unrelated to the gravitational anomaly.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Kubo formula for the anomalous vortical conductivity at weak coupling for a theory of N free right-handed fermions transforming under a global symmetry group, using one-loop diagram calculations with fermionic Matsubara frequencies and chemical potentials. They compute the retarded correlation function of the current and the energy-momentum tensor, perform the sum over Matsubara frequencies, and take the zero-frequency, zero-momentum limit after vacuum subtraction. The magnetic conductivity is computed as a trivial extension of prior work by Kharzeev (2009).

## Key numbers

* Vortical conductivity for a single right-handed and left-handed fermion with vector and axial currents: sigma_V^V = (mu * mu_A) / (2 pi^2) and sigma_A^V = (mu^2 + mu_A^2) / (4 pi^2) + T^2 / 12
* The temperature-squared term in the axial vortical conductivity is proportional to T^2 / 12, with no factor of pi appearing
* For RHIC conditions (T = 350 MeV, mu approximately 10 MeV, mu_A neglected), the temperature enhances the axial chiral vortical conductivity by a factor of approximately 10^4 relative to the chemical potential contribution
* Magnetic conductivity for vector and axial currents: sigma_VV^B = mu_A / (2 pi^2) and sigma_AV^B = mu / (2 pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the calculation of anomalous transport coefficients from chiral anomalies to include gravitational anomaly contributions, building on the Kubo formula approach of Kharzeev (2009) for the magnetic conductivity and Amado (2011) for the vortical conductivity. The holographic calculation in AdS/CFT by Amado (2011) did not show the T^2 contribution because only a gauge Chern-Simons term was included, while Neiman (2010) had argued for similar terms as undetermined integration constants without relation to the gravitational anomaly. The result enables quantitative predictions for chiral separation effects in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the temperature enhancement could make the vortical effect competitive with the magnetic effect, and suggests possible applications to lepton number generation in the early universe.
