# A Possible Boson Realization of the so(4)- and the so(3,1)-Algebra

> As natural extensions of the boson realizations of the su(2)- and the su(1,1)-algebra, the so(4)- and the so(3,1)-algebras are presented in the form of boson realizations with four kinds of boson operators. For each algebra, two forms are discussed. One is constructed in terms of two sets of the boson operators which play a role of spherical tensor with rank 1/2. The other is based on the ranks 1 and 0. As a possible application, the Runge-Lenz-Pauli vector, which is famous in the hydrogen atom, is derived with some aspects.

## Key claim

The paper presents boson realizations of the so(4)- and so(3,1)-algebras using four kinds of boson operators, as natural extensions of the well-known Schwinger boson representation for su(2) and su(1,1). The authors claim these realizations can describe the hydrogen atom and the Runge-Lenz-Pauli vector, but only under the specific condition that the Casimir operator Gamma_2 is nonzero for the so(4) case.

## Method

The authors construct two distinct boson realizations for each algebra: one using two sets of boson operators that act as spherical tensors of rank 1/2 (spinors), and another using three boson operators of rank 1 (vector) plus one of rank 0 (scalar). For the so(4)-algebra, they express the six operators (L_±,0 and M_±,0) as bilinear forms of the boson operators and analyze the two cases where the Casimir operator Gamma_2 = (1/2)L·M is nonzero or zero. For the so(3,1)-algebra, they replace the M operators with a set obeying commutation relations with opposite signs, and similarly construct two forms.

## Key numbers

- The so(4)-algebra orthogonal set is specified by four quantum numbers: λ, λ_0, κ, κ_0, where λ, κ = 0, 1/2, 1, 3/2, ... and λ_0 = -λ, -λ+1, ..., λ, κ_0 = -κ, -κ+1, ..., κ
- The eigenvalues of the Casimir operators Λ^2 and K^2 are λ(λ+1) and κ(κ+1), respectively
- For the vector/scalar boson realization (Section 4), the eigenvalue of Γ_1 is μ(μ+1) with μ = 0, 1/2, 1, 3/2, ...
- The quantum number η_0 takes values η_0 = μ+1, μ+2, μ+3, ...
- For the so(3,1) case with nonzero Γ_2, the eigenvalues of the su(1,1) Casimir operators are λ(λ-1) and κ(κ-1), with λ = 1, 3/2, 2, ... or λ = 1/2, 1, 3/2, ... depending on the sign parameter u

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger boson representation (Schwinger, 1952) for su(2) and su(1,1) to the so(4) and so(3,1) algebras, building on the authors' previous work (Nishiyama et al., referred to as (A) and (B) in the text) on boson realizations of su(M+1) and su(N,1) algebras. The formalism is motivated by the so(4)-algebraic approach to nuclear dynamics with pairing plus quadrupole interactions, as previously investigated by several authors, and by the historical application of so(4) to the hydrogen atom via the Runge-Lenz-Pauli vector. The results enable future work on the pairing plus quadrupole interaction in nuclear dynamics and on scattering problems in Coulomb fields.
