# Pion photoproduction in a dynamical coupled-channel model

> Pion photoproduction reactions are investigated in a dynamical coupled-channel approach based on the Juelich pi-N model, which presently includes the hadronic pi-N and eta-N stable channels as well as the pi-Delta, sigma-N and rho-N effective channels. This model has been quite successful in the description of pi-N to pi-N scattering for center-of-mass energies up to 1.9 GeV. The full pion photoproduction amplitude is constructed to satisfy the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity and hence, it is fully gauge invariant. The calculated differential cross sections and photon spin asymmetries up to 1.65 GeV center-of-mass energy for the reactions gamma p to pi+ n, gamma p to pi0 p and gamma n to pi- p are in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dynamical coupled-channel model for pion photoproduction that satisfies the full gauge-invariance condition (generalized Ward-Takahashi identity), not just current conservation, and achieves good agreement with experimental data for differential cross sections and photon spin asymmetries up to 1.65 GeV center-of-mass energy.

## Method

The authors extend the Juelich pi-N hadronic coupled-channel model (which includes pi-N, eta-N, pi-Delta, sigma-N, and rho-N channels) to pion photoproduction by constructing the full amplitude via attaching a photon to the dressed NNpi vertex. The amplitude includes s-channel, u-channel, t-channel, and interaction-current contributions, with the interaction current chosen to ensure gauge invariance; the model parameters are determined by fitting differential cross section and beam asymmetry data up to W=1.65 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range for the hadronic pi-N model: up to 1.9 GeV
- Center-of-mass energy range for photoproduction calculations: up to 1.65 GeV
- Eight bare genuine resonances included in the interaction kernel: S11(1535), S11(1650), S31(1620), P31(1910), P13(1720), D13(1520), P33(1232), and D33(1700)
- Total angular momentum states considered: J=1/2 and 3/2
- Three reaction channels calculated: gamma p to pi+ n, gamma p to pi0 p, and gamma n to pi- p

## Context

This work extends the Juelich pi-N model (Schutz 1998, Krehl 2000, Gasparyan 2003) to photoproduction, building on a field-theoretical approach by Haberzettl (1997) and a preliminary feasibility study (Haberzettl 2006). It contrasts with the EBAC dynamical coupled-channel model (Julia-Diaz 2008), which included two additional resonances (D15 and F15) but did not analyze the gamma n to pi- p channel; the present model achieves slightly better agreement with data for the charged and neutral pion channels. The results demonstrate that both coupled-channel effects and full gauge invariance are essential for understanding single-pion photoproduction, going beyond the majority of existing dynamical models that satisfy only current conservation.
