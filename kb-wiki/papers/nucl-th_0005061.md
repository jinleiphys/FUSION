# Pion electroproduction on the nucleon and the generalized GDH sum rule

> Results of the recently developed unitary isobar model (MAID) are presented for the spin asymmetries, structure functions and relevant sum rules in the resonance region. The model describes the presently available data for single-pion photo- and electroproduction in the resonance region below Wcm = 2 GeV very well. It is based on Born terms and resonance contributions, and the respective multipoles are constructed in a gauge-invariant and unitary way for each partial wave. The eta production is included in a similar way, while the contribution of more-pion and higher channels is modeled by comparison with the total cross sections and simple phenomenological assumptions. Our evaluation of the energy-weighted integrals is in good agreement for the proton but shows big discrepancies for the neutron.

## Key claim

The unitary isobar model (MAID) reproduces the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule integrals in good agreement with data for the proton, but shows "big discrepancies" for the neutron target, particularly for the sum rule \(I_3(0)\) which is heavily violated by the single-pion channel contribution.

## Method

The authors apply the MAID unitary isobar model, which describes single-pion photo- and electroproduction using effective Lagrangians for Born terms and vector meson exchange (background) plus Breit-Wigner resonance contributions for all major resonances below \(W=1700\) MeV, with multipoles constructed to satisfy gauge invariance and unitarity via K-matrix unitarization. Eta production is included similarly, while multi-pion and higher channels are modeled by comparison with total cross sections and phenomenological assumptions. The model is used to evaluate the helicity-dependent cross sections \(\sigma_{1/2}\) and \(\sigma_{3/2}\) and the longitudinal-transverse interference cross section \(\sigma_{LT'}\), from which the generalized GDH integrals \(I_{GDH}(Q^2)\), \(I_1(Q^2)\), and \(I_2(Q^2)\) are computed up to \(W_{\rm max}=2\) GeV.

## Key numbers

*   GDH integral for the proton over the full energy range up to \(W_{\rm max}=2\) GeV: sum rule prediction \(-205\ \mu\)b, MAID calculation including reggeized \(2\pi\) contributions gives \(-201\ \mu\)b.
*   GDH integral for the neutron over the full energy range up to \(W_{\rm max}=2\) GeV: sum rule prediction \(-233\ \mu\)b, MAID calculation gives \(-164\ \mu\)b.
*   Zero-crossing of \(I_1(Q^2)\) for the proton: at \(Q^2=0.75\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with only one-pion contribution, lowered to \(0.52\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with \(\eta\) included, and to \(0.45\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with multi-pion contributions included.
*   Integral \(I_3(0)\) from the single-pion channel: 0.59 for the proton (sum rule expects 0.45) and 0.78 for the neutron (sum rule expects 0).
*   Forward spin polarizability \(\gamma_0\) for the proton (200-450 MeV): MAID total \(-1.57 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\), Mainz experiment \(-1.4 \pm 0.3 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\).
*   GDH integral for the proton (200-450 MeV): MAID total \(-159\ \mu\)b, Mainz experiment \(-157 \pm 11\ \mu\)b.

## Context

This work extends the MAID unitary isobar model (Drechsel et al., 1998) to evaluate generalized GDH sum rules and the Burkhardt-Cottingham (BC) sum rule (Burkhardt and Cottingham, 1970) for both proton and neutron targets in the resonance region. The results are compared with experimental data from Mainz (Ahrens et al., 2000; Bragin et al., 2000) and SLAC (Abe et al., 1998), as well as with other theoretical approaches such as the dispersion-theoretical partial wave analysis HDT and the SAID solution SM99K. The findings enable future precision tests of nucleon spin structure at low \(Q^2\) and motivate improved theoretical treatment of multi-pion channels, particularly for the neutron target.
