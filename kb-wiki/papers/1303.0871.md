# On Normalization of Strongly Intensive Quantities

> A special normalization is proposed for strongly intensive quantities used in the study of event-by-event fluctuations in high energy collisions. It ensures that these measures are dimensionless and yields a common scale required for a quantitative comparison of fluctuations of different, in general dimensional, extensive quantities. Namely, the properly normalized strongly intensive quantities assume the value one for fluctuations given by the model of independent particle production and zero in the absence of event-by-event fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a specific normalization for the strongly intensive quantities Δ[A,B] and Σ[A,B] that makes them dimensionless and sets them to 1 for the independent particle model (IPM) and 0 in the absence of event-by-event fluctuations, but this normalization requires additional information beyond the first and second moments of A and B, namely the first and second moments of single-particle contributions and the mean particle number, and is not possible when the normalization factors C_Δ or C_Σ become zero.

## Method

The authors define normalization factors C_Δ and C_Σ such that Δ[A,B] = Σ[A,B] = 1 within an Independent Particle Model (IPM) whose multiplicity distribution P(N) and single-particle distribution P(α,β) are identical to those of the studied ensemble of states. The procedure involves calculating the ensemble averages of the first and second moments of A and B, then computing the first and second moments of single-particle quantities α and β and the average particle number ⟨N⟩ from the ensemble, and finally evaluating Δ and Σ using Eqs. (1) and (2) with the normalization factors given by Eqs. (12) and (13). The method is illustrated using UrQMD model simulations for inelastic p+p interactions and 7% most central Xe+La collisions across CERN SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- The proposed normalization yields Δ[A,B] = Σ[A,B] = 1 in the IPM and Δ[A,B] = Σ[A,B] = 0 when ω[A] = ω[B] = ⟨AB⟩ − ⟨A⟩⟨B⟩ = 0 (no fluctuations).
- For "transverse momentum" fluctuations with A = P_T and B = N, the normalization factors are C_Δ = C_Σ = ⟨N⟩ · ω[p_T], where ω[p_T] = (⟨p_T²⟩ − ⟨p_T⟩²)/⟨p_T⟩.
- For "chemical" fluctuations with A = K (kaons) and B = π (pions), C_Δ = ⟨π⟩ − ⟨K⟩ and C_Σ = ⟨π⟩ + ⟨K⟩.
- For partly overlapping sets (A = K, B = H⁻), C_Δ = ⟨H⁻⟩ − ⟨K⟩ and C_Σ = ⟨H⁻⟩ + ⟨K⟩ − 2⟨K⁻⟩.
- In the IB-GCE with volume fluctuations, ω[N] = 1 + (⟨N⟩/⟨V⟩) · (⟨V²⟩ − ⟨V⟩²)/⟨V⟩.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of strongly intensive quantities introduced by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (2011) by proposing a specific normalization that addresses the dimensional and scaling issues of the earlier Φ measure (Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski, 1992). The Φ measure was dimensional and did not assume a characteristic value for non-fluctuating A and B, which the proposed Δ and Σ quantities now remedy. The normalization enables quantitative comparison of fluctuations of different extensive quantities across different energies and collision systems, as demonstrated with UrQMD model results for p+p and Xe+La collisions relevant to the NA61/SHINE experimental program.
