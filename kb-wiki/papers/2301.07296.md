# Three-body coupled channel framework for two-neutron halo nuclei

> We study the Borromean nuclei formed by a core nucleus and two neutrons in a nonrelativistic effective field theory formalism considering both neutron-neutron and neutron-core interactions. We provide formulae of the charge and matter radii, and successfully reproduce the universal relation proposed by Hongo and Son based on the approximation of an infinite neutron-neutron scattering length and neglecting the neutron-core scattering. Once the realistic finite neutron-neutron and neutron-core scattering lengths are used, the charge and matter radii are influenced by the neutron-core channel in a growingly relevant manner. We obtain a relation among the binding energy of the three-body Borromean system, the ratio between charge and matter radii, and the ratio between the neutron-neutron and core-neutron scattering lengths. We find that the two-neutron separation energy for $^{22}$C needs to be $\lesssim 2$ keV in order to be consistent with the experimental constraints of the matter radius of $^{22}$C and the $^{20}{\rm C}\,n$ $S$-wave scattering length.

# Three-body coupled channel framework for two-neutron halo nuclei

## Key claim

The authors develop a nonrelativistic effective field theory (halo EFT) that includes both neutron-neutron and neutron-core dimers, showing that the universal relation between charge and matter radii proposed by Hongo and Son (2022) holds only in the limit of infinite neutron-neutron scattering length and vanishing neutron-core scattering; once realistic finite scattering lengths are used, the neutron-core channel contributes significantly to the radii, and the two-neutron separation energy for 22C must be less than or approximately 2 keV to satisfy experimental constraints.

## Method

The authors use a particle-dimer formalism within halo effective field theory, introducing both nn and An dimer fields as auxiliary degrees of freedom to describe the three-body Borromean system of a core nucleus and two neutrons. They solve the coupled-channel Faddeev equation for the Ann scattering amplitude in S-wave, extract the bound-state wave functions and couplings to each channel, and derive analytic expressions for the charge, neutron, and matter mean-square radii from form factor calculations. The three-body contact term is tuned to reproduce the binding energy, and the running of this coupling with the momentum cutoff shows logarithmically periodic behavior reminiscent of Efimov states.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18.6 fm (from Chen 2008)
- For a toy system with A=20 and binding energy B=20 keV, the ratio of couplings kappa = G_An/G_nn ranges from 3.38 (for a_nn/a_An = -0.01) to 13.7 (for a_nn/a_An = 100)
- For 6He with binding energy B=974 keV, the experimental ratio <r_m^2>/<r_c^2> = 1.59 +/- 0.07 = (0.40 +/- 0.02)A, which is smaller than the unitary limit 2A/3
- For 11Li with binding energy B=247 keV and a_9Li n = 30.3 fm (virtual energy 25 keV), the calculated rms charge radius is 0.8 fm and rms neutron radius is 5.0 fm
- For 22C, reproducing the matter radius of 3.44 fm requires a_20C n = 12.6 fm, and reproducing the matter radius of 5.4 fm requires a_20C n = 233 fm
- The 20C n S-wave scattering length is constrained to be less than 2.8 fm (from Mosby 2013)
- The two-neutron separation energy for 22C must be less than or approximately 2 keV to satisfy both the matter radius constraint of 3.44 +/- 0.08 fm (from Togano 2016) and the scattering length constraint

## Context

This work extends the universal relation for Borromean nuclei proposed by Hongo and Son (2022), which assumed infinite neutron-neutron scattering length and neglected neutron-core scattering, by incorporating both finite scattering lengths and the neutron-core channel explicitly. The formalism builds on the halo effective field theory approach developed by Bertulani, Hammer, and others for halo nuclei, and uses the particle-dimer framework from Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck. The results enable extraction of the neutron-core scattering length from combined measurements of binding energy and the ratio of matter to charge radii, and provide constraints on the binding energy of 22C that are significantly tighter than previous estimates from Acharya (2013), which suggested B less than 100 keV.
