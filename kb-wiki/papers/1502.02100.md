# Gauge Invariance and Holographic Renormalization

> We study the gauge invariance of physical observables in holographic theories under the local diffeomorphism. We find that gauge invariance is intimately related to the holographic renormalisation: the local counter terms defined in the boundary cancel most of gauge dependences of the on-shell action as well as the divergences. There is a mismatch in the degrees of freedom between the bulk theory and the boundary one. We resolve this problem by noticing that there is a residual gauge symmetry(RGS). By extending the RGS such that it satisfies infalling boundary condition at the horizon, we can understand the problem in the context of general holographic embedding of a global symmetry at the boundary into the local gauge symmetry in the bulk.

## Key claim

The paper argues that gauge invariance of physical observables in holographic theories under local diffeomorphisms is intimately tied to holographic renormalization, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the quadratic on-shell action (the generating function for two-point retarded Green's functions) is not itself gauge invariant under the residual gauge symmetry; instead, physical observables derived from it are invariant because the Green's functions are second derivatives of the full on-shell action at the zero source limit.

## Method

The authors study a holographic model consisting of a charged black hole in AdS4 with two free massless scalar fields added for momentum relaxation, described by the action S_ren = S_EM + S_psi + S_c. They introduce small fluctuations around the background solution (an AdS-Reissner-Nordstrom black brane with linear scalar fields) and analyze the gauge invariance of the on-shell action under both the axial gauge fixing condition h_rx = 0 and a residual gauge symmetry (RGS) that remains after this fixing. The analysis uses the holographically renormalized action expanded to quadratic order in fluctuations, with the counter term weight eta_c set to 1 to cancel divergences.

## Key numbers

- The background solution is characterized by three parameters: black brane horizon position r_0, chemical potential mu, and momentum relaxation parameter beta, with temperature T = f'(r_0)/(4 pi) = (1/(4 pi)) (3 r_0 - (mu^2 + 2 beta^2)/(4 r_0))
- Non-vanishing components of the energy-momentum tensor and charge density: <T^{tt}> = 2 m_0, <T^{xx}> = <T^{yy}> = m_0, <J^t> = mu r_0, where m_0 = r_0^3 (1 + mu^2/(4 r_0^2) - beta^2/(2 r_0^2))
- The horizon is set to r_0 = 1 for convenience
- Near the horizon, solutions have exponent nu_+ = i omega/(4 pi T) for the infalling boundary condition
- The residual gauge transformation is given by h_tx -> h_tx + h_0 and chi -> chi + i (beta/omega) h_0, where h_0 is a constant

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous numerical method for calculating Green's functions and AC transport quantities in systems with coupled fields and gauge constraints (Kim 2014, Kim 2015), which was based on earlier work by Amado (2009) and Kaminski (2009). The paper addresses the principle of gauge invariance that underlies those calculations, showing that the residual gauge symmetry resolves a mismatch in degrees of freedom between the bulk theory (which has only two independent infalling solutions) and the boundary theory (which requires three independent source fields). This enables a systematic understanding of how global symmetries at the boundary are embedded into local gauge symmetries in the bulk, and provides a foundation for computing conductivities in holographic models with momentum relaxation.
