# Prolate-oblate asymmetric shape phase transition in the interacting boson model with SU (3) higher-order interactions

> Prolate-oblate shape phase transition is an interesting topic in nuclear structure, which is useful for understanding the intrinsic interactions between nucleons. Recently, the interacting boson model with $SU(3)$ higher-order interactions was proposed, in which the prolate shape and the oblate shape are not described in a mirror symmetric way. This asymmetric description seems more realistic. The level evolutions, $B(E2)$ values and other important indicators showing the prolate-oblate asymmetric transitions are investigated in detail, and realistic structure evolutions from $^{180}$Hf to $^{200}$Hg are compared. A key finding is that, the average deformation of the prolate shape is nearly twice the one of the oblate shape. These results, together with the successful description of the $B(E2)$ anomaly in $^{168,170}$Os, $^{172}$Pt, the $γ$-soft properties of $^{196}$Pt, $^{82}$Kr and the normal states of $^{110}$Cd, support the validity of the new model.

## Key claim

The prolate-oblate shape phase transition in the interacting boson model with SU(3) higher-order interactions is inherently asymmetric, with the average deformation of the prolate shape being nearly twice that of the oblate shape, contrary to the mirror-symmetric description in the previous IBM with SU(3) and overline{SU(3)} limits.

## Method

The authors numerically diagonalized the Hamiltonian H = c[(1-eta) hat{n}_d + eta (-hat{C}_2[SU(3)]/(2N) + kappa hat{C}_3[SU(3)]/(2N^2))] for boson numbers N=5, 10, and 25 along the path with eta=0.5 (the blue line in the shape triangle), varying the cubic interaction coefficient kappa from 0 to 3. They computed energy levels, B(E2) transition probabilities using the operator hat{T}(E2)=q hat{Q}, quadrupole moments, and order parameters such as R_{4/2}, R_{3/2}, B_{4/2}, and aveQ to characterize the prolate-oblate transition.

## Key numbers

- The average deformation order parameter aveQ is approximately -1.2 for the prolate shape and approximately 0.6 for the oblate shape, showing the prolate deformation is nearly twice the oblate deformation.
- For N=10, the A point (crossing of 2_2^+ and 4_1^+ states) occurs at kappa=1.044 and the B point at kappa=1.23; for large N, the A and B points approach 1.305 and 1.323 respectively.
- The critical point from prolate-biased to oblate-biased gamma-soft region is near kappa=1.314, between the A and B points.
- For the oblate side, R_{4/2} is near 2.7 (not 10/3), while for the gamma-soft region it may decrease to 2.3.
- For N=10, the ratio R_{3/2}=E_{2_3^+}/E_{2_2^+} shows a rapid increase at the prolate side from 1.1 to 2.3; at the oblate side for N>=5, this value is around 2.0.
- At the prolate side, B_{4/2} is around 1.4, while at the oblate side it is nearly 1.1.
- In the large N limit, the rigid triaxial region lies between kappa=1.333 and kappa=1.362.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Fortunato et al. (2011), who first showed that SU(3) three-body interactions can describe oblate shapes and that the prolate-oblate evolution is asymmetric in the large N limit, and of Zhang et al. (2012), who provided analytic descriptions of the SU(3) degenerate point. It contrasts with the previous IBM description along the SU(3)-O(6)-overline{SU(3)} line (Jolie et al., 2001), where prolate and oblate shapes are mirror symmetric about the O(6) critical point. The results enable a unified description of the B(E2) anomaly in Os and Pt isotopes, the gamma-soft properties of Pt and Kr isotopes, and the normal states of Cd isotopes within the same SU3-IBM framework.
