# QCD thermodynamics at non-zero isospin asymmetry

> We study the thermodynamic properties of QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential using improved staggered quarks at physical quark masses. In particular, we discuss the determination of the equation of state at zero and nonzero temperatures and show results. Using the results for the isospin density $n_I$, we also determine the phase diagram in the $(n_I,T)$-plane.

## Key claim
The authors present lattice QCD results for the equation of state at nonzero isospin chemical potential using physical quark masses, but the results are limited to finite lattice spacings (primarily Nt=8) with only a tentative continuum extrapolation for the phase diagram in the (n_I,T) plane, not for the full equation of state.

## Method
The study uses improved staggered quarks at physical quark masses on lattices with temporal extents Nt=8, 10, and 12. The isospin density n_I, pressure difference Δp, energy density difference Δε, trace anomaly ΔI, and entropy density difference Δs are computed as functions of the isospin chemical potential μ_I scaled by the pion mass m_π, for temperatures ranging from 120 MeV to 165 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Isospin density n_I/m_π^3 measured up to μ_I/m_π = 0.9 for temperatures T = 124, 136, 148, and 162 MeV on 24x8 lattices
- Pressure difference Δp/m_π^4 measured up to μ_I/m_π = 0.9 for T = 120, 145, and 165 MeV on 24x8, 28x10, and 36x12 lattices
- Trace anomaly ΔI/m_π^4 measured up to μ_I/m_π = 0.9 for T = 120 and 145 MeV on 24x8, 28x10, and 36x12 lattices
- Energy density difference Δε/m_π^4 measured up to μ_I/m_π = 0.9 for T = 120, 145, and 165 MeV on 24x8 lattices
- Entropy density difference Δs/m_π^3 measured up to μ_I/m_π = 0.9 for T = 120, 145, and 165 MeV on 24x8 lattices
- Phase diagram in the (n_I,T) plane shows pion condensation boundary and chiral crossover line, with n_I/m_π^3 ranging from 0 to approximately 1.9 and T from 0 to 185 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice studies of QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential (Brandt 2017, Brandt 2018) which established the continuum phase diagram featuring hadronic, quark-gluon plasma, and Bose-Einstein condensate phases. The equation of state results enable applications to early Universe scenarios with large lepton flavour asymmetries, as discussed by Wygas (2018) and Vovchenko (2020). The phase diagram in the (n_I,T) plane provides a complementary representation to the traditional (μ_I,T) phase diagram from chiral perturbation theory (Son 2000) and earlier lattice work (Kogut 2002, Detmold 2012).
