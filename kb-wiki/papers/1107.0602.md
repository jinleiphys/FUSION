# Two-nucleon electromagnetic current in chiral effective field theory: one-pion exchange and short-range contributions

> We derive the leading one-loop contribution to the one-pion exchange and short-range two-nucleon electromagnetic current operator in the framework of chiral effective field theory. The derivation is carried out using the method of unitary transformation. Explicit results for the current and charge densities are given in momentum and coordinate space.

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading one-loop contributions to the one-pion exchange and short-range two-nucleon electromagnetic current operator in chiral effective field theory, using the method of unitary transformation to obtain energy-independent, renormalized expressions. Unlike prior work by Pastore et al. (2008, 2009, 2011) which derived only the momentum dependence without renormalization, this calculation aims to produce finite results where the low-energy constants can be taken from other sources such as pion-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The derivation begins with the heavy-baryon effective chiral Lagrangian and uses the canonical formalism to obtain the Hamilton density, then applies a unitary transformation to decouple the purely nucleonic subspace of Fock space from the rest. The effective nuclear current operator is defined as the projection of the transformed bare current onto the nucleonic subspace, and the calculation proceeds by classifying loop contributions according to powers of the axial coupling constant g_A and the type of hadronic current, evaluating loop integrals using dimensional regularization. Additional unitary transformations in the nucleonic subspace are employed to absorb divergences from pion loops into redefinition of low-energy constants, yielding finite results for the current and charge densities in both momentum and coordinate space.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is carried out at leading loop order, corresponding to order eQ with Q ~ M_pi (pion mass) referring to low external momenta.
- The pion mass M_pi appears as the soft scale, with the hard scale Lambda of order the rho-meson mass.
- The pion decay constant F, nucleon mass m (with a tilde notation for chiral-limit values), and axial coupling g_A (with a ring notation) are the fundamental parameters.
- The Lagrangian includes LECs l_3, l_4, l_6 from pi-pi sector; d_16, d_18, d_28, d_6, d_7, d_8, d_9, d_20, d_21, d_22 from pi-N sector; C_S, C_T from NN contact interactions at leading order; and alpha_1 through alpha_18 plus L_1, L_2 from NN contact interactions at next order.
- The short-range contact Lagrangian is expressed in terms of 7 independent constants C_1 through C_7 in the standard basis.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory framework for nuclear forces, originally formulated by Weinberg (1991), to electromagnetic currents at the one-loop level. It builds on the authors' previous derivation of two-pion exchange contributions to the current and charge densities (Kolling et al., 2009) and complements the calculations by Pastore et al. (2008, 2009, 2011) which used time-ordered perturbation theory without renormalization. The renormalized expressions enable the low-energy constants to be determined from pion-nucleon scattering and other independent processes, which was not possible in the earlier unrenormalized approach, and provide the necessary input for ab initio calculations of electromagnetic observables in light nuclei.
