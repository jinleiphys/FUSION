# Towards a Predictive HFB+QRPA Framework for Deformed Nuclei: Selected Tools and Technique

> Reliable predictions of the static and dynamic properties of a nucleus require a fully microscopic description of both ground and excited states of this complicated many-body quantum system. Predictive calculations are key to understanding such systems and are important ingredients for simulating stellar environments and for enabling a variety of contemporary nuclear applications. Challenges that theory has to address include accounting for nuclear deformation and the ability to describe medium-mass and heavy nuclei. Here, we perform a study of nuclear states in an Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) framework that utilizes an axially-symmetric deformed basis. We present some useful techniques for testing the consistency of such calculations and for interpreting the results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) plus Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) framework using an axially-symmetric deformed harmonic-oscillator basis with the finite-range Gogny D1M interaction can produce consistent results for spherical nuclei, but only after applying angular-momentum restoration and verifying degeneracy conditions as a stringent consistency test.

## Method

The authors performed HFB calculations to obtain the nuclear ground state, then built QRPA excitations on top using the same Gogny D1M interaction, all in an axially-symmetric deformed harmonic-oscillator basis. They restored angular-momentum symmetry using Wigner rotation matrices to construct states of good angular momentum from the intrinsically excited QRPA states. They tested the implementation by calculating the dipole response for the spherical closed-shell nucleus 16O, comparing results from different K projections and against a spherical Skyrme-RPA approach by Colò et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- QRPA calculations for 16O included N_osc = 6 major oscillator shells and 2qp states up to 60 MeV of energy
- The main peak of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) distribution for 16O is located at E = 21.65 MeV
- The Gogny D1M approach gives a GDR mean energy of 23.4 MeV, compared to 18.4 MeV for the Skyrme-Sly4 interaction and 25.4 MeV from systematics by Harakeh (2001)
- Transition strengths were spread with a Breit-Wigner distribution using Gamma = 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the deformed HFB+QRPA framework developed by Peru (2014), Deloncle (2017), Peru (2019), and Peru (2021) by establishing tools to test and interpret calculations, specifically angular-momentum restoration and verification of spherical-limit degeneracy. The comparison with the spherical Skyrme-RPA implementation by Colò et al. (2013) shows that the Gogny D1M interaction produces a GDR mean energy closer to the systematic expectation than the Skyrme-Sly4 parameterization. The techniques enable future studies of both spherical and deformed nuclei, including predictions for unstable isotopes as demonstrated by Nobre (2010), Nobre (2011), and Dupuis (2019).
