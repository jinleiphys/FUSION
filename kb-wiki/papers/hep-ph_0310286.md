# Two flavor color superconductivity and compact stars

> Baryonic matter at high density and low temperature is a color superconductor. In real world, this state of matter may naturally appear inside compact stars. A construction of a hybrid compact star with two flavor color superconducting quark matter in its interior is presented.

# Two flavor color superconductivity and compact stars

## Key claim
A hybrid compact star containing two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) quark matter in its interior can be constructed, but only if the charge neutrality condition is satisfied globally via a mixed phase rather than locally, because the 2SC phase alone is positively charged and cannot satisfy local neutrality.

## Method
The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation to describe quark matter, with a diquark coupling strength set to 75% of the quark-antiquark coupling. They impose global charge neutrality by constructing mixed phases of normal quark matter and 2SC quark matter using Gibbs conditions, and also construct a hadron-quark mixed phase using a chiral SU(3) hadronic model. The resulting equation of state is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for non-rotating compact stars.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³
- Hadron-quark mixed phase begins at baryon density ρ_B ≈ 1.49 ρ₀
- Hadron-quark mixed phase ends at ρ_B ≈ 2.56 ρ₀, where the energy density is 378 MeV/fm³
- At the transition to the normal-2SC mixed phase, baryon density jumps from 2.56 ρ₀ to 2.75 ρ₀ and energy density jumps from 378 MeV/fm³ to 415 MeV/fm³
- In the normal-2SC mixed phase, volume fractions are 0.53 (2SC) and 0.47 (normal)
- The maximum mass hybrid star has radius 10.86 km, mass 1.81 M⊙, and central baryon density 7.58 ρ₀
- The maximum surface tension allowing a mixed phase is σ_max ≲ 20 MeV/fm²; for 20 ≲ σ ≲ 50 MeV/fm² the mixed phase appears at higher densities (3ρ₀ ≲ ρ_B ≲ 5ρ₀)
- The thickness a of alternating layers in the mixed phase scales to approximately 10 fm
- Pressure difference between neutral g2SC phase and neutral normal phase is of order 1 MeV/fm³

## Context
This work extends the study of color superconducting phases in compact stars by addressing the earlier argument of Alford and collaborators (2002) that 2SC quark matter could not appear in compact stars under local charge neutrality. The authors construct a globally neutral mixed phase that incorporates the positively charged 2SC phase alongside negatively charged normal quark matter, enabling 2SC matter to exist at densities around 3ρ₀. The results for pure quark stars are comparable to those of Blaschke et al. (2000), Ruester et al. (2003), and the strange hybrid star calculations of the authors' earlier work (2003), provided the strange quark mass is not very small (m_s ≳ 300 MeV) and the superconducting gap is not too large (Δ ≲ 50 MeV). This construction enables future systematic studies of model dependence in hybrid matter with various color superconducting phases.
