# Nuclear scissors modes and hidden angular momenta

> The coupled dynamics of low lying modes and various giant resonances are studied with the help of the Wigner Function Moments method generalized to take into account spin degrees of freedom and pair correlations simultaneously. The method is based on Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations. The model of the harmonic oscillator including spin-orbit potential plus quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin interactions is considered. New low lying spin dependent modes are analyzed. Special attention is paid to the scissors modes. A new source of nuclear magnetism, connected with counter-rotation of spins up and down around the symmetry axis (hidden angular momenta), is discovered. Its inclusion into the theory allows one to improve substantially the agreement with experimental data in the description of energies and transition probabilities of scissors modes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a new source of nuclear magnetism, "hidden angular momenta" arising from counter-rotation of spin-up and spin-down nucleons around the symmetry axis, substantially improves agreement with experimental data for energies and transition probabilities of nuclear scissors modes, though the spin-spin interaction alone does not reduce the summed M1 strength as previously hoped.

## Method

The authors generalized the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method to simultaneously incorporate spin degrees of freedom and pair correlations, starting from Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations. They employed a harmonic oscillator model with spin-orbit potential plus separable quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin interactions, then linearized the resulting twelve dynamical equations in a small-amplitude approximation to extract collective mode energies and B(M1) values.

## Key numbers

- For rare earth nuclei, energies of scissors modes were reproduced with approximately 10% accuracy when including pair correlations alone.
- Inclusion of pair correlations reduced B(M1) values by about a factor of two relative to non-superfluid values, but they remained about two times too high compared to experiment.
- Calculations were performed for 26 nuclei in the rare earth region with the hidden angular momenta taken into account.
- The experimentally observed group of peaks in the energy interval 2–4 MeV likely corresponds to two different types of motion: the conventional orbital scissors mode and the new spin scissors mode.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous WFM method developments from TDHF to TDHFB equations (Malov, Urban) and from spin-orbit only (BaMo) to include spin-spin interactions (BaMoPRC). It builds on the prediction of the nuclear scissors mode by Hilton, Iudice, and Lo Iudice as counter-rotation of protons against neutrons in deformed nuclei, and addresses the long-standing discrepancy where RPA results (Zawischa, Sushkov) questioned the mode's collectivity. The new hidden angular momenta mechanism provides a tentative explanation for the experimental finding by Siem that B(M1) values in Th-232 are inverted in strength favoring the lowest state, which triaxial deformation models could not explain.
