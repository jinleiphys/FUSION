# A renormalisation-group treatment of two-body scattering

> A Wilsonian renormalisation group is used to study nonrelativistic two-body scattering by a short-ranged potential. We identify two fixed points: a trivial one and one describing systems with a bound state at zero energy. The eigenvalues of the linearised renormalisation group are used to assign a systematic power-counting to terms in the potential near each of these fixed points. The expansion around the nontrivial fixed point is shown to be equivalent to the effective-range expansion.

# A renormalisation-group treatment of two-body scattering

## Key claim

The Wilsonian renormalisation group applied to nonrelativistic two-body scattering by short-ranged potentials yields two fixed points: a trivial one for weak scattering and a nontrivial one describing systems with a bound state at zero energy. The expansion around the nontrivial fixed point is exactly equivalent to the effective-range expansion, but this equivalence is demonstrated for a specific choice of sharp momentum cut-off and the detailed form of the fixed-point potential is regulator-dependent.

## Method

The authors impose a sharp momentum cut-off on the intermediate states in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for s-wave scattering by a contact-interaction potential. They derive a renormalisation group equation for the rescaled potential by demanding that the off-shell K-matrix remain independent of the cut-off as it is lowered. Fixed points are identified as cut-off-independent solutions of this RG equation, and perturbations around each fixed point are classified by the eigenvalues of the linearised RG equation to assign a systematic power-counting to terms in the potential.

## Key numbers

- The nontrivial fixed-point potential for s-wave scattering is given by V_0(p, Lambda) = -(2 pi^2 / M) [Lambda - (p/2) ln((Lambda+p)/(Lambda-p))]^{-1}, corresponding to infinite scattering length (1/K = 0).
- The RG eigenvalues for energy-dependent perturbations around the nontrivial fixed point are nu = -1, 1, 3, ..., giving a power-counting with orders d = nu - 1 = -2, 0, 2, ...
- The one-pion-exchange coupling constant is alpha_pi = g_A^2 m_pi^2 / (16 pi f_pi^2) approximately 0.072.
- The pionic "Bohr radius" is R = 2/(alpha_pi M) approximately 5.8 fm, compared with the Yukawa range r_pi = 1/m_pi = 1.4 fm; the ratio r_pi/R is approximately 0.24.
- The perturbatively corrected short-range effective range in the 1S0 channel is r_e^0 = 2.62 - 1.38 = 1.24 fm; the distorted-wave treatment gives r_e^0 = 4.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering developed by Weinberg (1990), Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998), and van Kolck (1999) by providing a Wilsonian RG foundation for the power-counting schemes. The trivial fixed point reproduces Weinberg's power counting, while the nontrivial fixed point yields the power-divergence subtraction scheme of Kaplan, Savage and Wise. The analysis enables systematic inclusion of pion-exchange forces as perturbations and raises questions about the convergence of such perturbative treatments, particularly given the discrepancy between perturbative and distorted-wave calculations of the effective range.
