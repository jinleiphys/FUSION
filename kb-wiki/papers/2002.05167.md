# Precise determination of proton magnetic radius from electron scattering data

> We extract the proton magnetic radius from the high-precision electron-proton elastic scattering cross section data. Our theoretical framework combines dispersion analysis and chiral effective field theory and implements the dynamics governing the shape of the low-$Q^2$ form factors. It allows us to use data up to $Q^2\sim$ 0.5 GeV$^2$ for constraining the radii and overcomes the difficulties of empirical fits and $Q^2 \rightarrow 0$ extrapolation. We obtain a magnetic radius $r_M^p$ = 0.850 $\pm$0.001 (fit 68%) $\pm$0.010 (theory full range) fm, significantly different from earlier results obtained from the same data, and close to the extracted electric radius $r_E^p$ = 0.842 $\pm$0.002 (fit) $\pm$0.010 (theory) fm.

## Key claim

The authors extract a proton magnetic radius of 0.850 fm from the Mainz A1 electron-proton scattering data, which is significantly different from earlier results obtained from the same data using empirical fitting methods, but the result carries a theoretical uncertainty of ±0.010 fm that reflects the full plausible range of the dispersion analysis and chiral effective field theory model.

## Method

The authors combine dispersion analysis with chiral effective field theory (DIχEFT) to compute the proton electromagnetic form factors as dispersion integrals over the two-pion cut, using elastic unitarity, χEFT amplitudes, and the timelike pion form factor. They perform a global χ² fit to the Mainz A1 elastic scattering cross section data up to Q² = 0.5 GeV², with floating normalizations for each data set, minimizing simultaneously over the radii parameters and the normalization parameters.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic radius r_M^p = 0.850 ± 0.001 (fit 68%) ± 0.010 (theory full range) fm
- Electric radius r_E^p = 0.842 ± 0.002 (fit 68%) ± 0.010 (theory full range) fm
- Fit uses 1285 of the 1422 Mainz A1 data points with Q²_max = 0.5 GeV², giving reduced χ² = 1.39
- Fit to entire Mainz A1 data up to Q²_max = 1 GeV² gives r_M^p = 0.850 ± 0.001 fm and r_E^p = 0.843 ± 0.002 fm with reduced χ² = 1.43
- Transverse charge radius ⟨b²⟩_1 = 0.394 ± 0.002 (fit) ± 0.011 (theory) fm²
- Transverse magnetization radius ⟨b²⟩_2 = 0.531 ± 0.002 (fit) ± 0.019 (theory) fm²

## Context

This work extends the DIχEFT method previously used by Alarcon (2018) to extract the proton electric radius from empirical form factor parameterizations, now applying it directly to cross section data to extract both electric and magnetic radii. The results disagree with values obtained from the same Mainz A1 data using empirical fits by Bernauer (2010) and Lee (2015), which gave magnetic radii ranging from 0.78 to 0.91 fm, but agree with older dispersive fits by Belushkin (2006) giving ~0.85 fm. The method enables reliable extraction of the magnetic radius by using data at Q² ~ 0.1–0.5 GeV² where the magnetic form factor dominates, overcoming the difficulties of Q² → 0 extrapolation methods.
