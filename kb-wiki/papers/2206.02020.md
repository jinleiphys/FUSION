# Extended optimal Fermi averaging for near-recoilless $Λ$ production in the ($K^-$, $π^-$) reaction on nuclei

> We propose to extend the optimal Fermi averaging procedure theoretically in order to calculate an in-medium $K^-n\to π^- Λ$ amplitude for the exothermic ($K^-$, $π^-$) reaction on nuclei in the framework of a distorted-wave impulse approximation, taking into account the local momentum transfer generated by semiclassical distorted waves for $K^-$ and $π^-$ mesons. Angular distributions for the $^{12}$C($K^-$, $π^-$)$^{12}_Λ$C reaction in which a momentum transfer is $q \lesssim$ 80 MeV/$c$ at $p_K=$ 800 MeV/$c$ in the $π^-$ forward direction, are estimated by applying the extended procedure. The result shows that the calculated angular distributions are in good agreement with those of the data, and this extension is a successful prescription making it possible to describe the reaction cross sections in near-recoilless reactions such as ($K^-$, $π^-$) in our framework.

## Key claim

The authors propose an extension of the optimal Fermi averaging (OFA) procedure that incorporates local momentum transfer from semiclassical distorted waves, which overcomes the previous OFA's inability to populate Λ hyperons in hypernuclear states under near-recoilless conditions (q ≲ 80 MeV/c) where the condition q²/2Δm < Δω would otherwise prevent on-energy-shell solutions.

## Method

The authors calculate in-medium K⁻n → π⁻Λ amplitudes using a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework, where the local momentum transfer q(r) is obtained from semiclassical distorted waves for K⁻ and π⁻ mesons generated by solving Klein-Gordon equations with Kisslinger optical potentials. The extended optimal Fermi averaging procedure averages the on-energy-shell t-matrix over the nuclear density distribution weighted by a meson absorption factor, using the local momentum transfer q(r) rather than the asymptotic momentum transfer q_{Kπ}. Angular distributions for the ¹²C(K⁻, π⁻)¹²_ΛC reaction at p_K = 800 MeV/c are calculated and compared with experimental data from Chrien (1979).

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer q ≲ 80 MeV/c at p_K = 800 MeV/c in the π⁻ forward direction for the ¹²C(K⁻, π⁻)¹²_ΛC reaction
- Root-mean-square local momentum transfer ⟨q²⟩^{1/2} = 203 MeV/c at θ_lab = 0°, compared to asymptotic q_{Kπ} = 56.5 MeV/c
- For the ¹²C target, the neutron momentum distribution has ⟨p²⟩^{1/2} ≃ 147 MeV/c using a harmonic oscillator model with b_N = 1.64 fm
- The calculated angular distributions for 0⁺ and 2⁺ excited states agree with data without renormalization, while the standard DWIA with free-space amplitude overestimates data by a factor of 2.1 and with standard Fermi averaging (SFA) overestimates by a factor of 1.6
- Binding effects for a struck neutron correspond to a momentum shift Δp_K = √(2 × 494 × 19) ≃ 140 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the optimal Fermi averaging procedure previously developed by Harada (2004) for endothermic reactions (π⁺, K⁺) and (K⁻, K⁺), which had large momentum transfers of q ≳ 300–500 MeV/c and worked well without local momentum corrections. The extension addresses the near-recoilless exothermic (K⁻, π⁻) reaction, where earlier DWIA calculations by Dover (1979), Auerbach (1983), and others using standard Fermi averaging (Rosenthal, 1980) overestimated cross sections and required renormalization factors. The semiclassical distorted wave approach follows the formalism of Kawai (1962) and Luo (1991). This work enables more reliable extraction of hadron-nucleus potentials from experimental data at facilities such as J-PARC.
