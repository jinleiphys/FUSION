# Determination of the Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constant in the VPI/GW Pion-Nucleon Elastic Scattering Partial Wave and Dispersion Relation Analyis

> Our extraction of the pion-nucleon coupling constant from πp elastic scattering data is outlined. A partial wave analysis (T_π < 2100 MeV) is performed simultaneously with a fixed-t dispersion relation analysis (T_π < 800 MeV). The πNN coupling constant g^2/4πis searched to find the best fit. The result 13.73 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.07 (first error statistical, second systematic) is found to be insensitive to database changes and Coulomb barrier corrections. This value satisfies important elements of low energy QCD like the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, the Dashen-Weinstein sum rule, and chiral perturbation theory predictions of threshold pion photoproduction.

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon coupling constant is extracted as \( g^{2}/4\pi = 13.73 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.07 \) (first error statistical, second systematic), a value that is significantly lower than the older "textbook" value of 14.3 and is found to be insensitive to database changes and Coulomb barrier corrections.

## Method

A multi-energy partial wave analysis (PWA) of \(\pi p\) elastic scattering data up to \(T_\pi < 2100\) MeV was performed simultaneously with fixed-\(t\) dispersion relation (DR) constraints applied over \(20 < T_\pi < 800\) MeV and momentum transfers \(-0.3 \leq t \leq 0\) GeV\(^2\)/c\(^2\). The coupling constant and subtraction constants were treated as free parameters and determined by minimizing the total \(\chi^2\) of the fit to both the scattering data and the dispersion relations, using forward subtracted \(C^\pm(\omega)\) and derivative \(E^\pm(\omega)\) DRs as well as unsubtracted fixed-\(t\) Hüper (\(B_\pm\)) and \(C^+\) DRs.

## Key numbers

- \(g^2/4\pi = 13.73 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.07\) (first error statistical, second systematic) from the combined PWA + dispersion relation analysis (solution SM99).
- The statistical uncertainty of \(\pm 0.009\) was derived from \(\chi^2\) contour mapping of \(g^2/4\pi\) versus the subtraction constant in the forward derivative \(E^+(\omega)\) dispersion relation.
- The unconstrained (no DR) solution yielded \(g^2/4\pi = 13.66 \pm 0.18\) from the \(B^+(\nu,t)\) dispersion relation and \(13.66 \pm 0.07\) from the Hüper dispersion relation.
- Removing all charge exchange data gave a best-fit coupling of 13.65, only 0.6% lower than the nominal value.
- Ignoring Coulomb barrier corrections entirely shifted the minimum to 13.70, with individual channel minima ranging from \(\sim 13.45\) (\(\pi^+p\)) to 13.72 (\(\pi^-p\)) and 13.67 (charge exchange).

## Context

This work extends the VPI/GW partial wave analysis program (Arndt et al., 1995) by simultaneously applying fixed-\(t\) dispersion relation constraints, treating the coupling constant as a searched parameter rather than an input. It contradicts the older result of 14.3 \(\pm\) 0.2 from Bugg, Carter, and Carter (1973) and the subsequent Karlsruhe-Helsinki KH80 analysis (Koch and Pietarinen, 1980), and is in serious disagreement with np backward scattering determinations near 14.5 \(\pm\) 0.3 (Erikson, 1995). The lower value of 13.73 resolves the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy (Goldberger and Treiman, 1958; Dominguez, 1985), satisfies the Dashen-Weinstein sum rule (Dashen and Weinstein, 1969) when corrected for higher-order chiral effects (Goity, 1999), and is more consistent with chiral perturbation theory predictions for threshold pion photoproduction.
