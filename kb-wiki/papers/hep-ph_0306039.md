# Neutron - Antineutron Oscillations in a Trap Revisited

> We have reexamined the problem of $n-\bar n$ oscillations for ultra-cold neutrons (UCN) confined within a trap. It is shown that for up to $10^3$ collisions with the walls the process can be described in terms of wave packets. The exact equation is derived describing the time-evolution of the $\bar n$-component for an arbitrary number of collisions with the trap walls. The $\bar n$-component grows linearly with time with enhancement factor depending on the reflection properties of the walls.

# Neutron - Antineutron Oscillations in a Trap Revisited

## Key claim

The paper shows that for ultra-cold neutrons (UCN) confined in a trap, the process of n-nbar oscillations can be described in terms of wave packets for up to 10^3 collisions with the walls, and that the nbar component grows linearly with time with an enhancement factor of 1.5 to 2 depending on wall material, but only after approximately 10 collisions have occurred, and only for observation times less than the beta-decay time (about 10^3 s), after which a standing-wave regime might set in.

## Method

The authors model the trap as a one-dimensional potential well with optical potentials for neutrons and antineutrons, using scattering lengths for carbon (graphite and diamond) and copper. They derive an exact equation for the time evolution of the nbar component by treating neutrons as Gaussian wave packets that undergo reflections from the trap walls, with reflection coefficients calculated from the optical potentials. The annihilation probability after N collisions is obtained by summing over successive collisions, accounting for both the reflection coefficient rho and the decoherence phase theta between n and nbar components.

## Key numbers

- Mixing parameter epsilon ≈ 10^{-23} eV, corresponding to oscillation time tau > 0.86 × 10^8 s (from ILL-Grenoble experiment)
- Earth magnetic field splitting omega = E_nbar - E_n ≈ 6 × 10^{-12} eV, giving tau_B ≈ 2 × 10^{-4} s
- UCN energy E = 0.8 × 10^{-7} eV, velocity v ≈ 3.9 × 10^2 cm/s, wave packet width a = 3.2 × 10^{-3} cm
- For graphite: nbar reflection coefficient rho = 0.56, decoherence phase theta = 0.72 radians
- Collision times: tau_n,coll = 0.7 × 10^{-8} s, tau_nbar,coll = 1.1 × 10^{-8} s for graphite
- Optical potentials for graphite: U_n = 1.95 × 10^{-7} eV, U_nbar = (0.9 - i0.3) × 10^{-7} eV
- Enhancement factor for nbar production in a trap: 1.5 to 2, depending on wall material
- Linear time dependence of annihilation probability settles after about 10 collisions with trap walls

## Context

This work extends earlier treatments of n-nbar oscillations in traps by Chetyrkin (single collision only) and by Golub and Yoshiki (multiple reflections with decoherence), providing for the first time an exact equation for the annihilation probability after an arbitrary number of collisions. The paper clarifies the relationship between the wave-packet approach and the discrete eigenstate approach used by Marsh and Baldo, showing that the standing-wave regime would require times comparable to the neutron beta-decay time (about 10^3 s) to develop. The results enable quantitative predictions for future UCN trap experiments searching for n-nbar oscillations, though the authors note that reliable optical potential parameters for antineutrons remain the central open problem.
