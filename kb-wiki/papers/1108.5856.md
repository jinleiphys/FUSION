# Study of Deuteron-Proton and Deuteron-Deuteron Collisions at Intermediate Energies

> The $dp\to dp$ and $dd\to ^3He n$ reactions are considered in the energy range from few hundred MeV up to 1 GeV. The approach is based on the multiple-scattering theory, where the few-body collisions are presented through two-body ones. The high-energy nucleon-nucleon interactions are described by t-matrix, which is parameterized in accordance with the modern phase-shift analysis data. The special attention is given to investigation of the rescattering effects in these reactions. The theoretical predictions are obtained for the differential cross sections, vector and tensor analyzing powers. All results are presented in comparison with the existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including double-scattering (DS) terms in the multiple-scattering expansion significantly improves agreement between theoretical predictions and experimental data for deuteron-proton elastic scattering and deuteron-deuteron collisions, particularly for tensor analyzing powers and non-forward angles, but the approach is limited to energies where Faddeev equations are unusable (above a few hundred MeV).

## Method

The author starts from AGS-equations and iterates them over the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix, which is parameterized according to modern phase-shift analysis data. For the \(dp\to dp\) reaction, plane-wave-impulse-approximation (PWIA), single scattering (SS), and double scattering (DS) contributions are included; for the \(dd\to {^3He}~n\) reaction, one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) and single scattering terms are considered. The approach is applied to deuteron energies from 500 MeV to 2 GeV for \(dp\) elastic scattering and from 200 MeV to 520 MeV for the \(dd\to {^3He}~n\) reaction.

## Key numbers

- For \(dp\to dp\) elastic scattering: calculations are presented at a deuteron energy of 2 GeV, comparing vector (\(A_y\)) and tensor (\(A_{yy}\)) analyzing powers with experimental data from Saclay, LHE JINR hydrogen bubble chamber, and LHE JINR Nuclotron.
- For \(dd\to {^3He}~n\): differential cross section is presented at a laboratory momentum of 1.109 GeV/c, with data taken from Bizard.
- For \(dd\to {^3He}~n\): the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) is obtained at zero scattering angle in the energy range between 200 MeV and 520 MeV, with data taken from T20.

## Context

This work extends the multiple-scattering approach to intermediate energies where Faddeev equations are not applicable, building on the author's previous detailed presentations in JAPH and EURO. The results are compared with experimental data from Saclay, LHE JINR, Bizard, and T20, and the inclusion of double-scattering terms is shown to be a step toward solving the four-nucleon problem. The approach enables further theoretical development for few-body hadronic reactions at energies from a few hundred MeV up to 1 GeV.
