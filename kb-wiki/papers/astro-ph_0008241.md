# On the 21Na(p,gamma)22Mg thermonuclear rate for 22Na production in novae

> Classical novae are potential sources of gamma-rays, like the 1.275 MeV gamma emission following 22Na beta decay, that could be detected by appropriate instruments on board of future satellites like INTEGRAL. It has been shown that the production of 22Na by novae is affected by the uncertainty on the 21Na(p,gamma)22Mg rate and in particular by the unknown partial widths of the Ex = 5.714 MeV, J^pi = 2^+, 22Mg level. To reduce these uncertainties, we performed shell model calculations with the OXBASH code, compared the results with available spectroscopic data and calculated the missing partial widths. Finally, we discuss the influence of these results on the 21Na(p,gamma)22Mg reaction rate and 22Na synthesis.

## Key claim

The uncertainty on the 21Na(p,gamma)22Mg reaction rate, which affects 22Na production in novae, is reduced by shell model calculations that show the spectroscopic factor for the key Ex = 5.714 MeV, J^pi = 2^+ level is unlikely to be much smaller than one meV, thereby excluding the previously adopted lower limit for the rate.

## Method

Shell model calculations using the OXBASH code with the USD interaction for the sd shell model space were performed to calculate spectroscopic factors and radiative widths for 22Ne and 22Mg. The calculated values were compared with existing experimental spectroscopic data from the 21Ne(d,p)22Ne reaction, and a DWBA analysis using the ECIS code was performed on the experimental data of Neogy et al. (1972) to extract additional constraints on the spectroscopic factor of the 6.120 MeV level in 22Ne (the analog of the 5.714 MeV level in 22Mg).

## Key numbers

- The calculated proton width for the Ex = 5.714 MeV, J^pi = 2^+ level in 22Mg is Gamma_p = 4.5 meV, obtained from single-particle widths of Gamma_{0d5/2} = 2 x 10^{-6} eV, Gamma_{0d3/2} = 2 x 10^{-7} eV, and Gamma_{0s1/2} = 4.5 x 10^{-3} eV.
- The experimental total width for the 5.714 MeV level is Gamma = 16.5 +/- 4.4 meV (Endt 1990).
- The DWBA analysis yields an upper limit for the spectroscopic factor of C^2S <= 0.0025 for l=0 transfer, corresponding to Gamma_p <= 1 meV.
- The calculated radiative width for the Ex = 5.965 MeV, J^pi = 0^+ level is Gamma_gamma = 33.6 meV, giving a resonance strength of omega*gamma = 4.2 meV.
- The resonant part of the reaction rate is approximated by sum_i A_i exp(-B_i/T_9) with A_i = 334, 1862, 686 for B_i = 2.52, 3.95, 5.49 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jose et al. (1999), who identified the 21Na(p,gamma)22Mg rate as a major source of uncertainty for 22Na production in novae and adopted upper, recommended, and lower limits for the resonance strength. The shell model calculations provide a theoretical basis that excludes the lower limit (zero spectroscopic factor) used by Jose et al. (1999), while the resulting rate is close to the earlier estimates of Wiescher et al. (1986) and Wallace and Woosley (1986). The new rate enables more reliable predictions of 22Na yields and gamma-ray detectability distances for future missions like INTEGRAL.
