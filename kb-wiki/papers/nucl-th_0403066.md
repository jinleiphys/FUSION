# Static response of Fermi liquids with tensor interactions

> We use Landau's theory of a normal Fermi liquid to derive expressions for the static response of a system with a general tensor interaction that conserves the total spin and the total angular momentum of the quasiparticle-quasihole pair. The magnetic susceptibility is calculated in detail, with the inclusion of the center of mass tensor and cross vector terms in addition to the exchange tensor one. We also introduce a new parametrization of the tensor Landau parameters which significantly reduces the importance of high angular harmonic contributions. For nuclear matter and neutron matter we find that the two most important effects of the tensor interaction are to give a contribution from multipair states and to renormalize the magnetic moments. Response to a weak probe may be calculated using similar methods, replacing the magnetic moments with the matrix elements of the weak charges.

## Key claim

The paper derives general expressions for the static magnetic susceptibility of a Fermi liquid with tensor interactions, showing that the two most important effects of the tensor force are a contribution from multipair states and a renormalization of the magnetic moments; the authors introduce a new parametrization of tensor Landau parameters that reduces the importance of high angular harmonic contributions compared to the conventional parametrization.

## Method

The authors use Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids to derive the static response, expressing the quasiparticle interaction as a sum of central, exchange-tensor, center-of-mass tensor, and cross-vector terms, with the tensor interaction expanded in Legendre polynomials. They calculate the magnetic susceptibility by minimizing the quasiparticle energy with respect to distortion coefficients \(C_{lJ}^M\) in a basis of total angular momentum eigenstates, and evaluate the relevant matrix elements \(\langle 01|A|01\rangle\), \(\langle 01|A|21\rangle\), and \(\langle 21|A|21\rangle\) for the new parametrization. The center-of-mass tensor contributions are handled by relating them to exchange-tensor results with appropriate sign factors and treating logarithmic divergences at \(\theta \rightarrow \pi\).

## Key numbers

- For neutron matter at Fermi wave vector \(k_{\rm F} = 1.7\ \mathrm{F}^{-1}\) and effective mass \(m^*/m = 0.8345\), the exchange-tensor parameters in the new parametrization are \(H_0 = 0.529\), \(H_1 = 0.150\), \(H_2 = -0.0959\), \(H_3 = -0.141\), with higher \(l\) values decreasing in magnitude; the conventional parametrization gives \(\tilde{H}_0 = 0.665\), \(\tilde{H}_1 = 1.200\), \(\tilde{H}_2 = 1.150\), \(\tilde{H}_3 = 0.933\), showing slower decay with \(l\).
- The one-pion-exchange contribution to the exchange-tensor parameters in the new parametrization is \(H_0^{\rm OPE} = 0.403\), \(H_1^{\rm OPE} = -0.088\), \(H_2^{\rm OPE} = -0.079\), \(H_3^{\rm OPE} = -0.064\).
- The matrix elements for the exchange-tensor contribution in the new parametrization are \(\langle 01|\mathcal{F}^{\rm ex}|21\rangle = -(1/(2\sqrt{2}))(H_0 - H_1)\) and \(\langle 21|\mathcal{F}^{\rm ex}|21\rangle = H_2/10 - (1/4)(H_0 + H_1)\).
- The magnetic susceptibility expression (Eq. 44) contains terms proportional to \(\mu^2\), \(\mu\mu_{\rm T}\), and \(\mu_{\rm T}^2\), plus a multipair contribution \(\chi_{\rm M}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier generalization of Landau theory to tensor forces by Dabrowski and Haensel (1970s) and the magnetic susceptibility calculation by Haensel and Jerzak (1980s), which only included exchange-tensor contributions. It incorporates the recent finding by Schwenk and Friman (2003) that second-order tensor interactions produce center-of-mass tensor and cross-vector terms in addition to the exchange-tensor term. The results enable calculation of neutrino scattering and absorption rates in dense neutron-star matter, previously computed without tensor effects by Iwamoto (1993), and provide a framework for extracting effective interaction properties from magnetic susceptibility data such as those calculated by Fantoni (1998).
