# Soft two-meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials. II. One-pair and two-pair diagrams

> Two-meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials are derived where either one or both nucleons contains a pair vertex. Physically, the meson-pair vertices are meant to describe in an effective way (part of) the effects of heavy-meson exchange and meson-nucleon resonances. {}From the point of view of ``duality,'' these two kinds of contribution are roughly equivalent. The various possibilities for meson pairs coupling to the nucleon are inspired by the chiral-invariant phenomenological Lagrangians that have appeared in the literature. The coupling constants are fixed using the linear $σ$ model. We show that the inclusion of these two-meson exchanges gives a significant improvement over a potential model including only the standard one-boson exchanges.

## Key claim

The paper derives two-meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials from one-pair and two-pair (seagull) diagrams, arguing that these contributions effectively describe heavy-meson exchange and meson-nucleon resonance effects via "duality." The authors show that including these pair interactions yields a significant improvement over a potential model containing only standard one-boson exchanges, but note that the coupling constants are fixed using the linear sigma model and "do not have to be taken exactly."

## Method

The authors derived the fourth-order two-meson-exchange kernels for one-pair and two-pair diagrams using Feynman diagram techniques in Pauli-spinor space, with Gaussian form factors for the meson-nucleon vertices. They considered meson pairs including ππ, πρ, πσ, and σσ (where σ represents the low-mass pole of the broad ε(760) scalar meson), and fixed the pair coupling constants using the linear sigma model with chiral-invariant Lagrangians. The potentials were evaluated in coordinate space using λ-integral representations, including 1/M corrections from nonadiabatic energy denominator expansions and pseudovector vertex contributions.

## Key numbers

- The ε(760) scalar meson has a width Γ_ε ∼ 640 MeV, with its low-mass pole in the two-pole approximation at ∼500 MeV
- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 92.4 MeV
- The weak interaction axial-vector coupling constant is g_A = 1.2573
- The σ model gives the nucleon mass relation M = (g_1 - g_2 v^2)v, with v = (m_A/m_ρ)f_π and m_A^2 = m_ρ^2 + (g_V v)^2

## Context

This paper is the second in a series on two-meson-exchange potentials, extending the previous work on planar and crossed-box diagrams (Rijken 1995a) and the Nijmegen soft-core one-boson-exchange potential (Nagels 1978). The pair-meson potentials are motivated by "duality" (Dolen 1968, Swart 1989) as an effective way to include heavy-meson exchange and meson-nucleon resonance contributions without explicitly including resonance intermediate states. The work aims to develop an extended soft-core (ESC) nucleon-nucleon potential model that improves upon the one-boson-exchange Nijm93 potential (Stoks 1994) in describing scattering data.
