# Nuclear incompressibility in the quasilocal density functional theory

> We explore the ability of the recently established quasilocal density functional theory for describing the isoscalar giant monopole resonance. Within this theory we use the scaling approach and perform constrained calculations for obtaining the cubic and inverse energy weighted moments (sum rules) of the RPA strength. The meaning of the sum rule approach in this case is discussed. Numerical calculations are carried out using Gogny forces and an excellent agreement is found with HF + RPA results previously reported in literature. The nuclear matter compression modulus predicted in our model lies in the range 210-230 MeV which agrees with earlier findings. The information provided by the sum rule approach in the case of nuclei near the neutron drip line is also discussed.

## Key claim

The quasilocal density functional theory (QLDFT) can describe the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in finite nuclei using sum rules, with the nuclear matter compression modulus predicted to lie in the range 210-230 MeV, but this range is extracted from the upper and lower bounds given by the scaled and constrained estimates for different nuclei (230 MeV from Pb-208 and 210 MeV from Zr-90).

## Method

The authors used the scaling approach to compute the cubic energy-weighted sum rule (m3) and constrained calculations to compute the inverse energy-weighted sum rule (m-1) of the RPA strength within the QLDFT framework. These sum rules were evaluated using Slater determinants built from single-particle wave functions obtained by minimizing the QLDFT energy functional with Gogny D1' and D1S forces. The scaled average energy (from m3/m1) and constrained average energy (from m1/m-1) of the ISGMR were then used to extract the finite-nucleus compression modulus and to estimate the nuclear matter compression modulus.

## Key numbers

* For Pb-208 with the D1' force, the scaled ISGMR energy is 14.51 MeV and the constrained energy is 13.93 MeV; with the D1S force, the scaled energy is 13.65 MeV and the constrained energy is 13.05 MeV.
* The experimental centroid energy for Pb-208 is 14.2 +/- 0.3 MeV; for Zr-90 it is 17.9 +/- 0.2 MeV; for Ca-40 it is 19.2 +/- 0.4 MeV.
* The nuclear matter compression modulus for the D1' force is 228 MeV and for the D1S force is 209 MeV; the SkM* force has K_infinity = 217 MeV and the SIII force has K_infinity = 355 MeV.
* For O-28 (a neutron drip line nucleus), the scaled ISGMR energy with D1S is 19.3 MeV and the constrained energy is 7.3 MeV, compared to O-16 where the scaled energy is 27.1 MeV and the constrained energy is 24.1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quasilocal density functional theory (QLDFT), previously established by the same authors for ground-state properties (Soubbotin, Tselyaev, Vinas, 2003), to describe the collective breathing mode in finite nuclei. The results agree with earlier HF + RPA calculations using Gogny forces by Blaizot (1980) and with Skyrme force calculations by Giai and Sagawa (1981). The sum rule approach enables estimation of the nuclear matter compression modulus from finite nuclei and can be applied to nuclei near the neutron drip line, where Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1997) previously showed the monopole strength is affected by the low-energy threshold.
