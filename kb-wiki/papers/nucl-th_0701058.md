# What to Expect When You're Expecting: Femtoscopy at the LHC

> A huge systematics of femtoscopic measurements have been used over the past 20 years to characterize the system created in heavy ion collisions. These measurements cover two orders of magnitude in energy, and with LHC beams imminent, this range will be extended by more than another order of magnitude. Here, I discuss theoretical expectations of femtoscopy of $A+A$ and $p+p$ collisions at the LHC, based on Boltzmann and hydrodynamic calculations, as well as on naive extrapolation of existing systematics.

## Key claim

Femtoscopic length scales at the LHC will likely increase relative to RHIC values, but the magnitude of increase depends strongly on the actual multiplicity achieved; simple multiplicity scaling predicts 20-90% increases, while dynamical models predict more nuanced changes that may violate the factorization pattern observed at lower energies.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical expectations for femtoscopy (Hanbury Brown-Twiss interferometry) in A+A and p+p collisions at the LHC by examining three approaches: naive extrapolation of existing systematics assuming factorization of global and kinematic dependences; Boltzmann/cascade transport calculations (AMPT and Hadronic Rescattering Model); and 1+1-dimensional and 2+1-dimensional hydrodynamical calculations with first-order phase transitions. Predictions for pion HBT radii, freezeout timescales, and source shapes are compared across these models and with existing data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Naive extrapolation of systematics suggests dN/dy at LHC will be 60% larger than at RHIC, giving 17% larger length scales (1.6^{1/3}=1.17)
- Saturation-based calculations predict roughly triple RHIC multiplicity, giving 45% larger length scales; R_long for pions at midrapidity and low pT in central collisions would be 1.45 x 7 fm = 10 fm
- AMPT predicts transverse radii increase by 10% and longitudinal radii increase by 30% at LHC relative to RHIC
- HRM predicts transverse radii increase by 30% and longitudinal radii increase by 70% at LHC relative to RHIC
- In hydrodynamical calculations, time to cool from maximum energy density to critical energy density (epsilon_c = 1.93 GeV/fm^3) is 6 fm/c at RHIC and 20 fm/c at LHC from longitudinal expansion alone; with transverse dynamics included, cooling times become 5 fm/c at RHIC and 7.5 fm/c at LHC
- Evolution time to kinematic freezeout (T approx 140 MeV) is tau_0 ~ 12-14 fm/c in both RHIC and LHC cases in the hydro calculation
- In the NNUS scenario, R_long at low pT might be expected in range (1.2 to 1.9) x (7 fm) = 8.4 to 13 fm; average shift between pions and kaons (about 6 fm at RHIC) would be in range 7.2 to 11.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the systematic femtoscopic measurements compiled by Lisa (2005) covering two orders of magnitude in collision energy, and provides theoretical expectations for the upcoming LHC program. The paper contrasts simple multiplicity-scaling predictions (the "Nothing New Under the Sun" scenario) with dynamical model predictions from Boltzmann transport (AMPT by Ko and collaborators, HRM by Humanic) and hydrodynamical calculations (Eskola and collaborators, Heinz and Kolb). The results enable experimental tests at LHC that could eliminate some models, particularly regarding whether the factorization of kinematic and global dependences observed from AGS through RHIC will hold at higher energies.
