# Role of universal function of the nuclear proximity potential: A systematic study on the alpha-decay of heavy/super-heavy nuclei and α-induced reactions

> The idea of the universal function is fundamental advantage of proximity potential model. We present a systematic study of the role of universal function of the proximity potential model on alpha decay process for 250 ground state to ground state transitions using WKB approximation. In order to realize this goal, five universal functions proposed in the proximity models gp 77, Guo 2013, Ngo 80, Zhang 2013 and Prox. 2010 have been incorporated into the formalism of Prox. 77. The obtained results show that the radial behavior of universal function affects the penetration probability of the α particle. The theoretical α decay half lives are calculated and compared with the corresponding experimental data. It is shown that the Prox. 77 with Guo 2013 universal function provides the best fit to the available data. The role of the different universal functions in the α decay half lives of super heavy nuclei (SHN) with Z from 104 to 118 are also studied. It is shown that the experimental half lives in the super heavy mass region are described well using the Prox. 77 with Zhang 2013 universal function. In this paper, the validity of the original and modified forms of the proximity 77 potential is also examined for complete fusion reactions between α particle and 10 different target nuclei. Our results show that the measured α induced fusion reaction cross sections can be well reproduced using the Prox. 77 with Zhang 2013 universal function for the reactions involving light and medium nuclei. Whereas, the Prox. 77 with Guo 2013 universal function model demonstrates a reliable agreement with the experimental data at sub-barrier energies for heavier systems.

## Key claim

The paper systematically tests five different universal functions within the Prox. 77 proximity potential framework and finds that no single universal function works best for all cases: the Guo 2013 function gives the best fit for alpha-decay half-lives of heavy nuclei (Z=64-103) with a standard deviation of 0.49, while the Zhang 2013 function performs best for super-heavy nuclei (Z=104-118) with a standard deviation of 0.47, and the choice also depends on whether one is modeling alpha-decay or alpha-induced fusion reactions.

## Method

The authors incorporated five universal functions (gp 77, Guo 2013, Ngo 80, Zhang 2013, and Prox. 2010) into the original Prox. 77 proximity potential formalism and calculated alpha-decay half-lives for 250 ground-state to ground-state transitions using the one-dimensional WKB approximation. They also applied the same potential models to compute fusion cross sections for 10 alpha-induced reactions using the one-dimensional barrier penetration model (1D-BPM). The theoretical results were compared with experimental data using standard deviation calculations and Geiger-Nuttall plots.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation for alpha-decay half-lives of 250 heavy nuclei (Z=64-103): Prox. 77 with Guo 2013 universal function (MP-II) gives sigma = 0.49, compared to sigma = 1.17 for original Prox. 77
- Standard deviation for alpha-decay half-lives of 80 super-heavy nuclei (Z=104-118): Prox. 77 with Zhang 2013 universal function (MP-IV) gives sigma = 0.47, compared to sigma = 0.51 for original Prox. 77 (58 nuclei) and sigma = 3.59 for Ngo 80 (MP-III)
- Barrier height for alpha-decay of 201Rn: ranges from 19.61 MeV (MP-III with Ngo 80) to 21.86 MeV (original Prox. 77)
- Barrier height for alpha-decay of 285Fl: ranges from 24.44 MeV (MP-III with Ngo 80) to 27.13 MeV (original Prox. 77)
- Preformation factors used: 0.43 for even-even, 0.35 for odd-A, and 0.18 for odd-odd nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous studies on proximity potentials for alpha-decay, particularly building on the systematic comparison of universal functions for heavy-ion fusion reactions by Gharaei (2023). The authors test the same universal functions (Guo 2013, Zhang 2013, Ngo 80, gp 77, Prox. 2010) that were previously studied for fusion barriers and cross sections, now applying them to alpha-decay half-lives and alpha-induced fusion reactions. The results enable more accurate predictions of alpha-decay half-lives for super-heavy nuclei and provide guidance on which universal function to use for different mass regions and reaction types.
