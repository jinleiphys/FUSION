# QCD phase transition at finite isospin density and magnetic field

> The QCD phase transition is explored at finite isospin density and magnetic field within the extended two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. By adopting the Ginzburg-Landau approximation, we study the transitions from normal chiral symmetry breaking phase to pion superfluidity or rho superconductivity. To avoid the artificial divergence for a large isospin chemical potential, we adopt the Landau representation rather than the proper-time one for the fermion propagators in a constant magnetic field. For the Landau representation, the same cutoff to the Landau energies, rather than to Landau levels, should be adopted to regularize the divergences from the summations over Landau levels. Then, the Ginzburg-Landau coefficients for pion and rho mesons are worked out both analytically and numerically in random phase approximation. The results show that pion superfluidity is favored for a small magnetic field while rho superconductivity is favored for a large magnetic field when increasing isospin chemical potential, in line with the magnetic enhancement (deduction) of the lowest energy of $π^+ (ρ^{+})$ meson. The novel rho superconductivity phase at large magnetic field and finite isospin density implies an interesting and nontrivial interplay between QCD and QED.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the QCD phase diagram at finite isospin chemical potential and magnetic field, pion superfluidity is favored for small magnetic fields while rho superconductivity is favored for large magnetic fields when increasing isospin chemical potential, with the transition driven by the magnetic enhancement of the lowest pion energy and magnetic reduction of the lowest rho meson energy.

## Method

The authors employ the extended two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector four-fermion interactions, using the Ginzburg-Landau approximation to study second-order phase transitions from the normal chiral symmetry breaking phase to either pion superfluidity or rho superconductivity. To avoid artificial divergences at large isospin chemical potential, they adopt the Landau-level representation for fermion propagators in a constant magnetic field rather than the proper-time representation, and regularize by applying the same cutoff to Landau energies rather than to Landau level numbers. The quadratic Ginzburg-Landau coefficients for charged pion and charged rho meson condensates are computed analytically and numerically within the random phase approximation.

## Key numbers

* The critical magnetic field separating the favored phase is determined by comparing the Ginzburg-Landau coefficients for pion and rho mesons; the lowest energy of the charged pion increases with magnetic field while the lowest energy of the charged rho meson decreases with magnetic field, as established in prior lattice QCD simulations (Bali 2017, Ding 2020).
* The regularization scheme uses a three-momentum cutoff Lambda for the divergent parts, with the Landau energy cutoff condition |q_u B n_1|, |q_d B n_2| <= Lambda^2 applied to the summations over Landau levels.
* The magnetic field strengths considered span from small values (where pion superfluidity dominates) to large values (where rho superconductivity dominates), with the transition occurring at a critical field strength determined by the competition between the two Ginzburg-Landau coefficients.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of pion superfluidity at finite isospin density (Son 2000, Kogut 2002, He 2005) and the vacuum superconductor proposal for rho mesons (Chernodub 2010, 2011), which was later shown to be disfavored by the Vafa-Witten theorem and lattice results (Hidaka 2012, Cao 2019, Bali 2017, Ding 2020). The paper builds on the observation that magnetic field enhances the lowest pion energy but reduces the lowest rho meson energy, previously noted in studies of rotating systems (Liu 2017, Cao 2019, Cao 2020), and enables future exploration of the interplay between QCD and QED in the novel rho superconductivity phase at large magnetic field and finite isospin density.
