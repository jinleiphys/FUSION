# Does nuclear medium affect the transverse momentum-dependent parton distributions of valence quark of pions?

> We calculate the valence quark transverse momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) of the lightest pseudoscalar meson, pions, in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature by employing a light-cone quark model. The medium modifications in the pion unpolarized TMDs are induced through the effective quark masses computed using the chiral SU($3$) quark mean field model. The spin densities at different momentum fraction ($x$) have also been calculated at different baryonic densities.

## Key claim

The paper calculates for the first time the medium modifications of unpolarized transverse momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) of valence quarks in pions immersed in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, finding that the nuclear medium significantly alters the TMDs through changes in effective quark masses, with the magnitude of modification depending on baryonic density and longitudinal momentum fraction.

## Method

The authors employ a light-cone quark model (LCQM) with a minimal quark-antiquark Fock state to compute the unpolarized T-even TMD \(f_1(x,\mathbf{k}^2)\) of the pion. Medium modifications are introduced through effective quark masses \(m_q^*\) and energies \(e_q^*\) calculated using the chiral SU(3) quark mean field (CQMF) model, which incorporates scalar fields (\(\sigma\), \(\zeta\), \(\delta\)) and vector fields (\(\omega\), \(\rho\)) to describe the in-medium properties of confined quarks. The thermodynamic potential of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter is minimized with respect to the meson fields to obtain the density-dependent effective quark masses, which are then used as inputs to the LCQM wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass \(m_q^*\) is expressed as \(m_q^* = -g_\sigma^q\sigma - g_\zeta^q\zeta - g_\delta^q I^{3q}\delta + \Delta m\), where \(\Delta m = 77\) MeV for the strange quark and zero for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks.
- The nuclear saturation density is taken as \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), and calculations are performed for baryonic densities up to \(\rho_B = 5\rho_0\).
- For \(x=0.3\), the enhancement trend of the TMD amplitude with increasing baryonic density reverses beyond \(\mathbf{k}^2 = 0.029\) GeV\(^2\); for \(x=0.5\), this reversal occurs beyond \(\mathbf{k}^2 = 0.025\) GeV\(^2\).
- The vacuum PDFs are evolved from the model scale \(Q_0^2 = 0.23\) GeV\(^2\) to \(Q^2 = 16\) GeV\(^2\) using NLO DGLAP evolution and compared with E615 data.
- The average transverse momentum carried by the quark shows a decreasing rate of change beyond \(\rho_B = 2\rho_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Puhan 2024) which found that asymmetric nuclear matter can impact pion PDFs by approximately 10%, and is the first calculation of medium modifications to pion TMDs. Prior studies of in-medium pion properties include the NJL model approach by Suzuki (1995), the combined light-front Bethe-Salpeter and quark-meson coupling model by de Melo (2014), and the NJL/QMC model studies of valence quark distributions by Hutauruk (2019, 2021). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes in nuclear targets, and provide a framework for studying other TMDs such as the Boer-Mulders function in medium.
