# Evidence of Hexadecapole Deformation in Uranium-238 at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> There is strong evidence of the failure of hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to reproduce data on the elliptic flow of particles in relativistic collisions of $^{238}$U nuclei at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We demonstrate that this failure is caused by an inappropriate implementation of well-deformed ions, such as $^{238}$U, in the hydrodynamic framework. Past studies have identified the deformation of the nuclear surface with that of the nuclear volume, though these are different concepts. In particular, a volume quadrupole moment can be generated by both a surface hexadecapole and a surface quadrupole moment. This feature was so far neglected in the modeling of heavy-ion collisions, and is particularly relevant for nuclei like $^{238}$U, which is both quadrupole- and hexadecapole-deformed. With rigorous input from Skyrme density functional calculations, we show that correcting for such effects in the implementation of nuclear deformations in hydrodynamic simulations restores agreement with BNL RHIC data. This brings consistency to the results of nuclear experiments across energy scales, and demonstrates the impact of the hexadecapole deformation of $^{238}$U on high-energy collisions.

## Key claim

The failure of hydrodynamic simulations to reproduce elliptic flow data in relativistic uranium-238 collisions at RHIC is caused by an inappropriate identification of nuclear surface deformation with volume deformation; correcting the Woods-Saxon quadrupole deformation parameter from the commonly used value to approximately 0.25, as required by the presence of a significant hexadecapole deformation, restores agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using 21 different Skyrme energy density functional parametrizations to obtain realistic ground-state densities for 238U and 197Au. They then fitted Woods-Saxon parametrizations to these densities to extract surface deformation parameters, and used the resulting parameters in IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD hydrodynamic simulations of U+U and Au+Au collisions at RHIC to compute elliptic flow ratios.

## Key numbers

- The recommended spectroscopic quadrupole deformation of 238U is β₂ = 0.287 ± 0.007, derived from B(E2) = 12.19 ± 0.62 e²b²
- The newly derived Woods-Saxon surface quadrupole deformation for 238U is β₂^WS ≈ 0.25, significantly lower than the volume deformation
- The ratio of mean squared elliptic flow coefficients between U+U and Au+Au collisions at 0-1% centrality: model-independent estimate gives r_Au,U{2}² = 1.55 ± 0.10, compatible with STAR data value of 1.49 ± 0.05
- IP-Glasma simulations with β₂^WS = 0.25 yield r_Au,U{2}² = 1.63 ± 0.06 for 0-1% centrality
- Hexadecapole deformation of 238U from muonic x-ray experiments: β₄ ≈ 0.144 ± 0.007 (Zumbro 1984); from Coulomb excitation: β₄ ≈ 0.124 ± 0.033 (Bemis 1973)

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing tension between hydrodynamic simulations of U+U collisions at RHIC and experimental data from the STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk 2015), which previous studies such as Giacalone (2020) and Schenke (2019, 2020) had identified but could not explain. The paper demonstrates that the hexadecapole deformation of 238U, previously neglected in high-energy collision modeling, significantly affects the appropriate surface quadrupole parameter. This enables consistent interpretation of nuclear structure across low-energy spectroscopy and high-energy heavy-ion collisions, and motivates future experimental investigations of quadrangular flow v₄ as an independent probe of hexadecapole deformation.
