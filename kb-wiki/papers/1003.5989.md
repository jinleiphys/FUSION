# Heavy Quark Fluorescence

> Heavy hadrons containing heavy quarks (for example, Upsilon-mesons) feature a scale separation between the heavy quark mass (about 4.5 GeV for the b-quark) and the QCD scale (about 0.3 GeV}) that controls effective masses of lighter constituents. Therefore, as in ordinary molecules, the de-excitation of the lighter, faster degrees of freedom leaves the velocity distribution of the heavy quarks unchanged, populating the available decay channels in qualitatively predictable ways. Automatically an application of the Franck-Condon principle of molecular physics explains several puzzling results of Upsilon(5S) decays as measured by the Belle collaboration, such as the high rate of Bs*-anti Bs* versus Bs*-anti Bs production, the strength of three-body B-anti B + pion decays, or the dip in B momentum shown in these decays. We argue that the data is showing the first Sturm-Liouville zero of the Upsilon(5S) quantum mechanical squared wavefunction, and providing evidence for a largely b-anti b composition of this meson.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Franck-Condon principle from molecular physics applies to open-flavor decays of heavy hadrons like the $\Upsilon(5S)$, explaining several puzzling Belle collaboration results. The key hedge is that this principle holds only up to corrections of order $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/m_b$ (about 0.3 GeV / 4.5 GeV), and the authors provide a model-based estimate of these corrections rather than a rigorous effective-field-theory derivation.

## Method

The authors model the $\Upsilon(5S)$ and $B$ mesons using a Coulomb-gauge QCD Hamiltonian with a Cornell (Coulomb+linear) potential, treated in BCS+TDA approximations to obtain canonical wavefunctions. They compute 2-body and 3-body open-flavor decay widths using the $^3P_0$ decay model, where light quark creation proceeds via a chiral-symmetry breaking interaction proportional to ${\bf \sigma}\cdot {\bf p}$, with the vertex proportional to the BCS mass-gap function $M(k)/E(k)$. The decay amplitudes are evaluated by integrating over the internal quark momentum ${\bf q}$, and the resulting partial widths are compared to Belle data after dividing by spin, isospin, and phase-space factors.

## Key numbers

- Heavy quark mass for the $b$-quark: about 4.5 GeV; QCD scale: about 0.3 GeV
- Decay width to $B_s^{*}\bar{B}_s^{*}$: 19 MeV; decay width to $B_s\bar{B}_s^{*}+B_s^{*}\bar{B}_s$: 1.6 MeV (ratio of about 12, compared to the spin-counting expectation of 7/4)
- Model parameters: gluon mass $m_g=0.89$ GeV, strong Coulomb coupling $g_s=1.41$, heavy-quark mass $m_b=3.73$ GeV
- Dip in $B$ meson momentum distribution in 3-body decays at around $k = 600$ MeV (corresponding to $M_{bc}=5.4$ GeV)
- The $^3P_0$ vertex is proportional to $M(q)/E(q)$, with the running mass function $M(k)$ falling steeply for momenta above $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\simeq 250$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of the Franck-Condon principle from molecular physics to heavy hadron decays, building on the authors' earlier work (General:2006ed) that first recognized its relevance. It directly addresses puzzling Belle collaboration results (Drutskoy:2009ci) for $\Upsilon(5S)$ decays that had not been quantitatively predicted by theoretical approaches such as Simonov (2008). The approach enables a new way to extract structural information about heavy quark momentum distributions inside excited quarkonia, and the authors suggest it could be applied to analyze the new $X-Y-Z$ charmonium-like resonances in the 3.8-4.6 GeV range, potentially distinguishing between $c\bar{c}$ states (which would show Sturm-Liouville nodes) and exotic mesons (which would not).
