# The Properties of Radially Excited Charmonia in The Light Front Quark Model

> Investigating the properties of excited charmonia is important to clarify its internal structure. In this paper, we present the mass spectra (MS) and decay constants (DC) for charmonia up to 3S states calculated by means of the light-front quark model based on a variational approach. In particular, we consider the QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian, which includes both confinement (linear and screened) and Coulomb-like potentials. Furthermore, since the existence of the nature of heavy quark symmetry, we treat hyperfine interactions perturbatively. We developed the harmonic oscillator expansion method to approximate the wave function (WF) for excited states. We found that the results of our theoretical calculations, using screened potentials rather than linear ones, are in good agreement with experimental data. By looking at the mass and decay constant result, we found that our result on the ψ(3S) state matched the properties of the ψ(4040) resonance.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their light-front quark model with a screened confinement potential and a three-basis harmonic oscillator expansion can reproduce the mass spectra and decay constants of charmonia up to the 3S state, and specifically that the ψ(3S) state matches the ψ(4040) resonance; however, this agreement depends on using screened rather than linear potentials and on including three harmonic oscillator basis states with two mixing angles.

## Method

The authors used a light-front quark model with a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, confinement (linear or screened), Coulomb-like, and perturbatively-treated hyperfine potentials. They expanded the radial wave function as a linear combination of three harmonic oscillator basis states (1S, 2S, 3S) with mixing angles, determined parameters by variational minimization of the 1S state mass, and then computed mass spectra and decay constants for charmonia up to 3S states.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium mass spectra with screened potential: η_c(1S) = 3002.3 MeV (experiment 2983.9 ± 0.4 MeV), η_c(2S) = 3614.5 MeV (experiment 3637.5 ± 1.1 MeV), η_c(3S) = 4028.1 MeV (no experimental value)
- Charmonium mass spectra with screened potential: J/ψ(1S) = 3102.4 MeV (experiment 3098.9 ± 0.01 MeV), ψ(2S) = 3679.8 MeV (experiment 3686.1 ± 0.06 MeV), ψ(3S) = 4079.1 MeV (experiment 4039 ± 1 MeV for ψ(4040))
- Decay constants: η_c(1S) = 349.1 MeV (experiment 335 ± 75 MeV), J/ψ(1S) = 396.7 MeV (experiment 407 ± 5 MeV)
- Decay constants: η_c(2S) = 224 MeV (no experimental value), ψ(2S) = 285.6 MeV (experiment 294 ± 5 MeV)
- Decay constants: η_c(3S) = 163.9 MeV (no experimental value), ψ(3S) = 230.2 MeV (experiment 238 ± 5 MeV)
- Model parameters: constituent charm quark mass m_c = 1.61 GeV, string tension b = 0.18 GeV², strong coupling α_s = 0.402, screening parameter μ = 0.027 GeV, variational parameter β_{c\bar{c}} = 0.5417 GeV, mixing angles θ_{12} = 12.12° and θ_{13} = θ_{23} = 8.44°

## Context

This work extends the light-front quark model calculations of Choi (2015) and Arifi (2022) from ground and 2S states to 3S charmonia by adding a third harmonic oscillator basis state. The results are compared with the Godfrey-Isgur model (1985), the Relativized Quark Model of Ebert (2003), and non-relativistic QCD calculations of Soni (2017). The screened potential approach follows the work of Gao (2022). The identification of ψ(4040) as the ψ(3S) state supports the review by Mo (2010) and enables future calculations for bottomonium systems and radiative decays.
