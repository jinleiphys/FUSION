# Effect of short- and long-range correlations on neutron skins of various neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei

> We study the effects of correlations beyond the independent particle model in the evaluation of neutron skins of various neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei. We consider short- and long-range correlations to take into account the presence of the strongly repulsive core of the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction and collective nuclear phenomena, respectively. Despite the strong sensitivity on the structure of the nucleus considered, our results indicate that, in general, correlations increase the values of the neutron skins.

## Key claim

Correlations beyond the independent particle model (IPM) generally increase the calculated neutron skin values of neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei, but the effect is strongly sensitive to the specific nuclear structure of each isotope, with the only exception being the $^{90}$Zr nucleus when using the D1M interaction.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the D1S and D1M parametrizations of the Gogny effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to obtain independent particle model (IPM) ground states for five neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei: $^{48}$Ca, $^{68}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{132}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb. Long-range correlations (LRC) were treated within the random phase approximation (RPA) using the same interaction as the HF calculation, while short-range correlations (SRC) were included via a two-body correlation function based on the Argonne V8' plus Urbana IX interactions, using a cluster expansion truncated at the one-correlation-function level.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb, the IPM neutron skin with D1S is 0.122 fm; including both LRC and SRC gives 0.165 fm, a 35.2% increase.
- For $^{208}$Pb, the IPM neutron skin with D1M is 0.092 fm; including both correlations gives 0.132 fm, a 43.2% increase.
- The PREX-1 and PREX-2 combined experimental value for the $^{208}$Pb neutron skin is (0.283 ± 0.071) fm.
- The IPM $^{208}$Pb skin values lie at 2.3σ (D1S) and 2.7σ (D1M) from the PREX mean; the correlated values lie at 1.6σ (D1S) and 2.1σ (D1M).
- For $^{90}$Zr with D1M, the total correlated skin is 0.039 fm, a 23.1% decrease relative to the IPM value of 0.050 fm.
- The relative difference between D1S and D1M IPM neutron skins is 24.4% for $^{208}$Pb, about 15% for $^{68}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{132}$Sn, and 7% for $^{48}$Ca.

## Context

This work extends the study of correlations beyond the mean-field model to neutron skins, following earlier treatments of charge density distributions using the same SRC approach by Co et al. (1995) and Anguiano et al. (2001). The results are directly relevant to interpreting the PREX-2 experiment on $^{208}$Pb (Adhikari et al., 2021) and the ongoing debate about the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy, which connects to neutron star structure as discussed by Fattoyev et al. (2012). The authors note that fully microscopic calculations such as the coupled-cluster model applied to $^{48}$Ca by Hagen et al. (2016) are not yet feasible for heavier nuclei like $^{208}$Pb.
