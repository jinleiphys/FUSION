# (Phenomenology/Lattice-Compatible) $SU(3)$ M$χ$PT HD up to ${\cal O}(p^4)$ and the ${\cal O}\left(R^4\right)$-Large-$N$ Connection

> Obtaining the values of the coupling constants of the low energy effective theory corresponding to QCD, compatible with experimental data, even in the (vector) mesonic sector from (the ${\cal M}$-theory uplift of) a UV-complete string theory dual, has thus far been missing in the literature. We take the first step in this direction by obtaining the values of the coupling constants of the ${\cal O}(p^4)$ $χ$PT Lagrangian in the chiral limit involving the NGBs and $ρ$ meson (and its flavor partners) from the ${\cal M}$-theory /type IIA dual of large-$N$ thermal QCD, inclusive of the ${\cal O}(R^4)$ corrections. We observe that ensuring compatibility with phenomenological/lattice results (the values ) as given in arXiv:1510.01634[hep-ph], requires a relationship relating the ${\cal O}(R^4)$ corrections and large-$N$ suppression. In other words, QCD demands that the higher derivative corrections and the large-$N$ suppressed corrections in its M/string theory dual are related. As a bonus, we explicitly show that the ${\cal O}(R^4)$ corrections in the UV to the ${\cal M}$-theory uplift of the type IIB dual of large-$N$ thermal QCD, can be consistently set to be vanishingly small.

## Key claim
The authors obtain values for the coupling constants of the \( \mathcal{O}(p^4) \) chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian in the chiral limit (involving Nambu-Goldstone bosons and the \( \rho \) meson) from the \( \mathcal{M} \)-theory/type IIA dual of large-\( N \) thermal QCD including \( \mathcal{O}(R^4) \) corrections, and find that compatibility with phenomenological and lattice results requires a specific relationship between the \( \mathcal{O}(R^4) \) corrections and large-\( N \) suppression.

## Method
The authors start from the type IIB holographic dual of large-\( N \) thermal QCD (the resolved warped deformed conifold with fluxes), perform a Strominger-Yau-Zaslow (SYZ) triple T-duality to obtain a type IIA mirror, and then uplift to \( \mathcal{M} \)-theory in the MQGP limit. They incorporate \( \mathcal{O}(R^4) \) corrections (order \( l_p^6 \)) to the \( \mathcal{M} \)-theory metric and derive the DBI action on flavor \( D6 \)-branes. Using the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) formalism, they express the low-energy coupling constants (LECs) of the \( \mathcal{O}(p^4) \) \( SU(3) \) chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian as radial integrals over the holographic background.

## Key numbers
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \( \Lambda_\chi \sim 4\pi F_\pi \sim 1.1\ \text{GeV} \) in the chiral limit.
* At NLO (\( \mathcal{O}(p^4) \)), there are 12 coupling constants (\( L_{i=1,2,\ldots,10}, H_1, H_2 \)) in the Gasser-Leutwyler Lagrangian.
* The ten low-energy constants \( L_{i=1,2,\ldots,10} \) were evaluated at scale \( \mu = M_\rho \) in prior work (Pich); updated values are given in Ecker (2015).
* The MQGP limit is defined by \( g_s \sim 1/\mathcal{O}(1) \), \( M, N_f \equiv \mathcal{O}(1) \), \( N \gg 1 \), and \( g_s M^2 / N \ll 1 \).
* The IR cut-off is denoted \( r_0 \), and the \( D5 \)-\( \overline{D5} \) separation scale is \( \mathcal{R}_{D5/\overline{D5}} \sim \sqrt{3} a \), where \( a \) is the resolution parameter of the blown-up \( S^2 \).

## Context
This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) and the HLS formalism (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003) by using a UV-complete top-down holographic dual (the type IIB model of the McGill group, 2006) rather than a bottom-up approach. It also builds on prior calculations of low-energy constants from lattice simulations (MILC collaboration) and from phenomenological fits (Pich, 1995; Ecker, 2015). The results enable a direct connection between higher-derivative corrections in the string/M-theory dual and large-\( N \) suppression in QCD, and they provide a framework for computing mesonic observables from a UV-complete holographic dual.
