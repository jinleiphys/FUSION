# $nd$ scattering lengths from a quark-model based $NN$ interaction

> We calculate the doublet and quartet neutron-deuteron scattering lengths using a nonlocal nucleon-nucleon interaction fully derived from quark-quark interactions. We use as input the $NN$ $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$-${}^3 D_1$ partial waves. Our result for the quartet scattering length agrees well with the experimental value while the result for the doublet scattering length does not. However, if we take the result for the doublet scattering length together with the one for the triton binding energy they agree well with the so-called Phillips line.

## Key claim

The quark-model-based nonlocal NN interaction yields a doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length that disagrees with experiment when taken alone, but when combined with the triton binding energy the pair falls on the Phillips line, meaning the discrepancy is consistent with the known correlation between these two observables.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in momentum space for neutron-deuteron scattering, using as input only the NN \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) partial waves from a nonlocal interaction derived from the chiral quark model via the resonating group method. They expanded the two-body t-matrices in Legendre polynomials (12 terms, with a scale parameter b = 3 fm\(^{-1}\)) to reduce the integral equations to one continuous variable, performing a five-channel calculation for the doublet and a seven-channel calculation for the quartet scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Doublet scattering length \(a_{1/2} = 1.13\) fm from the quark-model calculation, compared to the experimental value (no experimental number given in text).
- Quartet scattering length \(a_{3/2} = 6.40\) fm, which agrees well with experiment.
- Triton binding energy \(B = 7.72\) MeV from the same model, comparable to predictions by conventional meson-exchange models (Nijmegen, Bonn) and differing from experiment by less than 1 MeV.
- The Phillips line derived from five-channel calculations with local potentials (Malfliet-Tjon, de Tourreil, Reid) gives \(a_{1/2} = 6.352 - 0.677B\), yielding \(a_{1/2} = 1.13\) fm at \(B = 7.72\) MeV.
- The Phillips line derived from 48 modern NN force models (Wir95, Mac01, Sto94) with three-body forces gives \(a_{1/2} = 7.028 - 0.756B\), yielding \(a_{1/2} = 1.19\) fm at \(B = 7.72\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the triton binding energy using the chiral quark model (Garcilazo and Valcarce, 2002) to the neutron-deuteron scattering lengths. The results are comparable to those from conventional meson-exchange models such as Nijmegen and Bonn (Schadow, 2000), and to the comprehensive calculations of Wiringa et al. (2003) that include higher angular momentum channels and three-body forces. The quartet scattering length is well described by S-wave models without three-body forces, similar to effective field theory (Bedaque et al., 1998; Griesshammer, 2005), while the doublet channel requires three-body forces for accurate predictions (Bedaque et al., 2000; Platter et al., 2006).
