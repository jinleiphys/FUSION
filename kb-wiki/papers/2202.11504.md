# Relativistic hydrodynamics with spin in the presence of electromagnetic fields

> We extend the classical phase-space distribution function to include the spin and electromagnetic fields coupling and derive the modified constitutive relations for charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor. Because of the coupling, the new tensors receive corrections to their perfect fluid counterparts and make the background and spin fluid equations of motion communicate with each other. We investigate special cases which are relevant in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, including baryon free matter and large mass limit. Using Bjorken symmetries, we find that spin polarization increases with increasing magnetic field for an initially positive baryon chemical potential. The corrections derived in this framework may help to explain the splitting observed in Lambda hyperons spin polarization measurements.

## Key claim

The paper extends spin hydrodynamics to include coupling between spin and electromagnetic fields, finding that spin polarization increases with increasing magnetic field for an initially positive baryon chemical potential, which may help explain the splitting observed in Lambda hyperon spin polarization measurements.

## Method

The authors generalize the classical phase-space distribution function for spin-1/2 particles by adding a term coupling the particle magnetic moment to the external electromagnetic field, then derive modified constitutive relations for the baryon charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor. They investigate special cases relevant to high-energy heavy-ion collisions, including baryon-free matter and the large mass limit, and study the equations of motion under Bjorken symmetries in the ideal magnetohydrodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic moment of Lambda hyperons is taken as g_Lambda = -0.6138 +/- 0.0047, with the nuclear magneton as the unit.
* The number density of spinless neutral classical massive particles is given by N_(0) = g T^3 z^2 K_2(z) with g = 1/(2 pi^2) and z = m/T.
* The spin tensor decomposition uses thermodynamic coefficients A_1 = N_(0) - A_3 and A_2 = 2[A_1 - 2 A_3], where A_3 = -2(E_(0) + P_(0))/(T z^2).
* The pressure and energy density of spinless neutral classical particles are P_(0) = N_(0) T and E_(0) = g z^3 T^4 K_1(z) + 3 P_(0).

## Context

This work extends the spin hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski et al. (2017-2021) by including coupling between spin and electromagnetic fields, which was absent in previous formulations. The framework aims to address discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental data from STAR (2017) on Lambda hyperon spin polarization, where the splitting between Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization may arise from opposite magnetic moments as suggested by Becattini et al. (2016). The results enable future studies of spin polarization dynamics in heavy-ion collisions with more realistic setups including multiple quark-like quasiparticles.
