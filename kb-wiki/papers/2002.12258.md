# Improved description of light nuclei through chiral effective field theory at leading order

> We propose an arrangement of the most commonly invoked version of the two-nucleon chiral potential such that the low-lying amplitude zero of the 1S0 partial wave is captured at leading order of the effective expansion. Adopting other partial waves from the LENPIC interaction, we show how this modification yields an improved description of ground-state energies and point-proton radii of three test nuclei.

# Improved description of light nuclei through chiral effective field theory at leading order

## Key claim

By replacing the standard Weinberg-power-counting short-range part of the $^1S_0$ two-nucleon potential with a momentum-dependent separable-plus-constant interaction that captures the amplitude zero at leading order, the overbinding of light nuclei is reduced by about 70% and point-proton radii increase toward experimental values, but the modified interaction still leaves $^6$He unbound relative to the $^4$He threshold.

## Method

The authors construct a modified leading-order (LO) chiral potential by replacing the $^1S_0$ partial-wave contact term $C_0$ with a separable short-range interaction derived from an auxiliary-dibaryon Lagrangian, which yields a momentum-dependent form $V(p',p) = (4\pi/m_N)[\Delta_1^{-1}\mathcal{F}(p'/\gamma)\mathcal{F}(p/\gamma) + \Delta_2^{-1}] + V_\pi(p',p)$ with $\mathcal{F}(x)=(1+x^2)^{-1/2}$. They fit the three low-energy constants $\Delta_1$, $\Delta_2$, and $\gamma$ to reproduce the $^1S_0$ scattering length, effective range, and the amplitude zero at $k_0\approx340$ MeV, then combine this modified $^1S_0$ channel with the LENPIC LO interactions in all other partial waves. No-core shell model (NCSM) calculations are performed for $^3$H, $^4$He, and $^6$He using the Many-Fermion Dynamics for nuclei (MFDn) code, with extrapolation to infinite model space via an exponential fit.

## Key numbers

- $^1S_0$ neutron-proton scattering length: $a = -23.7$ fm $= -(8.3\text{ MeV})^{-1}$
- $^1S_0$ amplitude zero momentum: $k_0 \approx 340$ MeV
- $^1S_0$ effective range: $r_0 = 2.7$ fm
- Fitted low-energy constants: $\Delta_1 = -58$ MeV, $\Delta_2 = 96$ MeV, $\gamma = 476$ MeV (with coordinate cutoff $R=0.9$ fm, momentum cutoff $\Lambda=439$ MeV)
- Ground-state energy of $^3$H: original LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $-11.87^{+0.04}_{-0.10}$ MeV; modified LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $-9.28 \pm 0.02$ MeV; experimental $-8.42$ MeV
- Ground-state energy of $^4$He: original LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $-48.507^{+0.001}_{-0.040}$ MeV; modified LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $-34.936^{+0.005}_{-0.010}$ MeV; experimental $-28.30$ MeV
- Ground-state energy of $^6$He: original LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $-44.9 \pm 0.5$ MeV; modified LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $-31.9 \pm 0.3$ MeV; experimental $-29.27$ MeV
- Point-proton RMS radius of $^3$H: original LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $1.3 \pm 0.1$ fm; modified LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $1.5 \pm 0.1$ fm; experimental $1.587 \pm 0.096$ fm
- Point-proton RMS radius of $^4$He: original LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $0.99 \pm 0.01$ fm; modified LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $1.22 \pm 0.01$ fm; experimental $1.455 \pm 0.011$ fm
- Point-proton RMS radius of $^6$He: original LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $1.39 \pm 0.05$ fm; modified LENPIC$^{[0]}$ $1.67 \pm 0.05$ fm; experimental $1.912 \pm 0.018$ fm

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Ref. (zero) to capture the $^1S_0$ amplitude zero at LO in chiral EFT, applying it for the first time in many-body NCSM calculations of light nuclei. It directly addresses the overbinding problem identified by the LENPIC Collaboration (Maris 2016, LENPIC 2018, Epelbaum 2018) in LO Weinberg-power-counting calculations, where the $^1S_0$ channel lacks sufficient repulsion. The modified interaction brings LO results significantly closer to experiment, enabling future work to incorporate this improved $^1S_0$ treatment into higher-order chiral potentials and to extend calculations to heavier nuclei and to three-nucleon forces.
