# Two- and Three-Point Functions in the Extended NJL Model

> The two-point functions in generalized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio models are calculated to all orders in momenta and quark masses to leading order in $1/N_c$. The use of Ward identities and the heat-kernel expansion allows for a large degree of regularization independence. We also show how this approach works to the same order for three-point functions on the example of the vector-pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar three-point function. The inclusion of the chiral anomaly effects at this level is shown by calculating the pseudoscalar-vector-vector three-point function to the same order. Finally we comment on how (vector-)meson-dominance comes out in the presence of explicit chiral symmetry breaking in both the anomalous and the non-anomalous sectors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two- and three-point functions in extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) models can be calculated to all orders in momenta and quark masses at leading order in $1/N_c$, with the regularization dependence largely isolated into two arbitrary functions, provided one imposes QCD Ward identities and uses the heat-kernel expansion; however, the authors caution that the exact identification of the quark condensate and its current-quark-mass dependence with those used in Chiral Perturbation Theory or QCD Sum Rules is not straightforward due to regularization ambiguities.

## Method

The authors generalize the analysis of Bijnens, Bruno, and de Rafael (1993) for two-point functions beyond the chiral limit, allowing for nonzero and different current-quark masses, and extend the approach to three-point functions using the vector-pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar and pseudoscalar-vector-vector cases as examples. They sum the infinite class of constituent-quark-loop diagrams in the large-\(N_c\) limit using recursion relations that relate \(n\)-loop graphs to products of one-loop and \((n-1)\)-loop graphs, combined with the relevant four-fermion couplings \(G_S\) and \(G_V\). The one-loop functions are expressed via a heat-kernel expansion, and the full resummed functions are constrained by Ward identities that relate the two-point functions of vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar currents.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass \(M_i\) plotted as a function of \(G_S\) for \(\Lambda_\chi = 1.160\) GeV; for small current quark masses \(m_i\), \(M_i\) converges smoothly to the chiral-limit value in the spontaneously broken phase.
* For the numerical fits, \(\Lambda_\chi = 1.160\) GeV, \(G_S = 1.216\), \(G_V = 1.263\), and \(g_A(Q^2=0) = 0.61\) (from fit 1 of Bijnens, Bruno, and de Rafael (1993)).
* The current quark mass \(\overline{m} \equiv m_u = m_d\) that reproduces the physical neutral pion and kaon masses with the above parameters is \(\overline{m} = 3.2\) MeV.
* The Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation for the pion mass is recovered when expanding the expression for \(m_{ij}^2(Q^2)\) in powers of \(m_i\).
* The Weinberg Sum Rules in the equal-mass sector give \(f_V^2 M_V^2 = f_A^2 M_A^2 + f_\pi^2\) and \(f_V^2 M_V^4 = f_A^2 M_A^4\), while QCD requires \(f_V^2 M_V^4 = f_A^2 M_A^4 + m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2\); the ENJL model thus suppresses the high-energy behaviour too strongly for the second Weinberg Sum Rule.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bijnens, Bruno, and de Rafael (1993) for two-point functions from the chiral limit to nonzero current-quark masses, and generalizes the approach to three-point functions, building on earlier studies of vector-pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar functions by Lutz and collaborators. The results enable a systematic comparison of ENJL model predictions with Chiral Perturbation Theory at order \(p^4\) (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984) and with QCD Weinberg Sum Rules, showing that the ENJL model's high-energy behaviour is too strongly suppressed for the second sum rule. The treatment of the chiral anomaly via consistent one-loop anomalous Ward identities (Bijnens and Prades, 1993) resolves earlier concerns about anomaly reproduction in such models (Ball, 1989) and allows the study of vector-meson-dominance in both anomalous and non-anomalous sectors.
