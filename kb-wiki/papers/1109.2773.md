# $ρ$ self energy at finite temperature and density in the real-time formalism

> The $ρ$ meson self-energy in nuclear matter from baryonic loops is analysed in the real time formulation of field theory at finite temperature and density. The discontinuities across the branch cuts of the self-energy function are evaluated for an exhaustive set of resonances in the loops considering the fully relativistic thermal baryon propagator including anti-baryons. Numerical calculations show a significant broadening of the $ρ$ spectral function coming from the Landau cut. Adding the contribution from mesonic loops, the full spectral function of the $ρ$ in a thermal gas of mesons, baryons and antibaryons in equilibrium is evaluated at various values of temperature and baryonic chemical potential.

# ρ self energy at finite temperature and density in the real-time formalism

## Key claim

The ρ meson spectral function shows significant broadening from the Landau cut contribution of baryonic loops, but this broadening arises specifically from scattering processes of the ρ meson with baryons and antibaryons in the medium, not from the unitary cut contributions which are far from the ρ pole and thus neglected.

## Method

The authors evaluated the ρ meson self-energy at one-loop order using the real-time formulation of thermal field theory, considering baryonic loops with the nucleon and all 4-star N* and Δ resonances up to spin 3/2. They used fully relativistic thermal baryon propagators including antibaryons, gauge-invariant ρNR interactions with momentum-dependent vertex functions, and convoluted the narrow-width self-energy with the spectral functions of unstable baryons and mesons to account for finite widths.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson spectral function broadens significantly at T = 175 MeV, with the dilepton emission rate showing enhanced low-mass production from baryonic loops compared to mesonic loops alone
- The NN*(1520) loop makes the most significant contribution to the imaginary part of the ρ self-energy, followed by N*(1720) and Δ(1700)
- The monopole form factor cutoff is Λ = 2 GeV
- The ρ decay constant Fρ = 154 MeV is obtained from ρ0 → e+e- decay
- The isospin factor IF is 2 for I = 1/2 resonances and 4/3 for I = 3/2 resonances

## Context

This work extends the real-time formalism analysis of Ghosh (2011) from hot mesonic matter to baryonic matter at finite temperature and density. It differs from earlier approaches by Rapp (1997) using resonant interactions, Eletsky using forward scattering amplitudes, and Post using Lindhard functions, by evaluating the thermal loop directly with the full relativistic baryon propagator including antibaryons. The results enable more complete calculations of dilepton production rates for comparison with NA60 and PHENIX data, and are relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR.
