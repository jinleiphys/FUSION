# Effect of the final state interaction of $η' N$ on the $η'$ photoproduction off nucleon

> We investigate the $η'$ photoproduction off the nucleon with a particular interest in the effect of the final-state interaction (FSI) of the $η'$ meson and nucleon $(η'N)$ based on three-flavor linear sigma model. We find an enhancement in the cross section of the $η'$ photoproduction near the $η'N$-threshold energy owing to the $η'N$ FSI. With the $η'$ meson at forward angles, the energy dependence near the $η'N$ threshold is well reproduced with the $η'N$ FSI. The cross section at backward angles can also be a good probe to investigate the strength of the $η'N$ interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an attractive final-state interaction (FSI) between the eta' meson and nucleon produces a significant enhancement in the eta' photoproduction cross section near the eta'N threshold, and that this enhancement can reproduce the rapid energy dependence seen in experimental data at forward angles, though the model cannot simultaneously reproduce the scattering length suggested by the COSY-11 experiment.

## Method

The authors employ a three-flavor linear sigma model to compute the eta' photoproduction amplitude, including Born diagrams in the s and u channels and vector-meson exchange in the t channel. The eta'N FSI is incorporated via a two-channel coupled scattering equation (eta'N and eta N channels) using interaction kernels derived from scalar-meson exchange and the U_A(1) anomaly term, with the coupling strength varied by scaling the parameter g to explore different FSI scenarios.

## Key numbers

- The eta'N scattering length for case (b) (g x 1.0) is -1.9 + 0.58i fm, with a binding energy of 9.79 - 7.10i MeV.
- The eta'N scattering length for case (c) (g x 0.5) is +0.53 + 0.042i fm, with no bound state.
- The eta'N scattering length for case (d) (g x 1.5) is -0.77 + 0.086i fm, with a binding energy of 98.6 - 24.6i MeV.
- The eta'N scattering length for case (e) (g x -0.5) is -0.13 + 2.8 x 10^{-3}i fm, with no bound state.
- The cutoff parameters used for comparison with experimental data range from Lambda_{s,u} = 600 to 680 MeV and Lambda_t = 610 to 840 MeV across cases (a) through (e).
- The broad bump structure without FSI appears around 2.6 GeV total energy W, approximately 600-900 MeV above the eta'N threshold.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the eta'N interaction in the linear sigma model (Sakai 2013, Sakai 2014), where a strongly attractive eta'N interaction was found. It provides a framework to interpret experimental data from the CLAS collaboration (Williams 2009) and complements analyses of eta' photoproduction by Nakayama (2005) and others. The results enable future extraction of the eta'N interaction strength from photoproduction data, particularly at backward angles where the cross section near threshold is sensitive to the FSI.
