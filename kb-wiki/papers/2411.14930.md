# Determining the minimal mass of a proto-neutron star with chirally constrained nuclear equations of state

> The minimal masses and radii of proto-neutron stars during different stages of their evolution are investigated. In our work we focus on two stages, directly after the supernova shock wave moves outwards, where neutrinos are still captured in the core and the lepton per baryon ratio is fixed to $Y_L = 0.4$, and a few seconds afterwards, when all neutrinos have left the star. All nuclear equations of state used for this purpose fulfill the binding energy constraints from chiral effective field theory for neutron matter at zero temperature. We find for the neutrino-trapped case higher minimal masses than for the case when neutrinos have left the proto-neutron star. Thermal effects, here in the form of a given constant entropy per baryon $s$, have a smaller effect on increasing the minimal mass. The minimal proto-neutron star mass for the first evolutionary stage with $Y_L = 0.4$ and $s = 1$ amounts to $M_{min} \sim 0.62M_{\odot}$ and for the stage without neutrinos and $s = 2$ to $M_{min} \sim 0.22M_{\odot}$ rather independent on the nuclear equation of state used. We also study the case related to an accretion induced collapse of a white dwarf where the initial lepton fraction is $Y_L = 0.5$ and observe large discrepancies in the results of the different tables of nuclear equations of state used. Our finding points towards a thermodynamical inconsistent treatment of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition for nuclear equations of state in tabular form demanding a fully generalized three-dimensional Gibbs construction for a proper treatment. Finally, we demonstrate that there is a universal relation for the increase of the proto-neutron star minimal mass with the lepton fraction for all nuclear equations of state used.

## Key claim

The minimal mass of a proto-neutron star depends primarily on the lepton fraction (neutrino content) rather than thermal effects, with values around 0.62 solar masses for the neutrino-trapped stage and 0.22 solar masses for the neutrino-free stage, but these results are contingent on using equations of state that satisfy chiral effective field theory binding energy constraints for neutron matter at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using three families of tabulated nuclear equations of state (DD2, SFHo, and QMC-RMF1-4) that all fulfill chiral effective field theory constraints for pure neutron matter. They modeled two quasi-stationary proto-neutron star stages: an early stage with trapped neutrinos (fixed lepton fraction Y_L = 0.4, constant entropy per baryon s = 1) and a later stage after neutrinos have left (Y_nu = 0, s = 2), treating neutrinos as an ultrarelativistic Fermi gas added to the nuclear matter contributions.

## Key numbers

- Minimal proto-neutron star mass for the neutrino-trapped stage (Y_L = 0.4, s = 1): M_min ~ 0.62 solar masses, with minimal radius R_min ~ 38 km
- Minimal proto-neutron star mass for the neutrino-free stage (Y_nu = 0, s = 2): M_min ~ 0.22 solar masses, with minimal radius R_min ~ 80-100 km
- For the accretion-induced collapse scenario (Y_L = 0.5, s = 2), most equations of state produce an additional stable branch with a very high maximal mass of around 3.5 solar masses, though this is identified as an artifact of interpolation at the liquid-gas phase transition
- The minimal mass for the neutrino-free case with s = 2 and Y_L = 0.4 would decrease by approximately 0.13 solar masses and shift to the right by about 10 km if a different interpolation method (linear in log(p) and log(epsilon)) were used

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of proto-neutron star minimal masses by Gondek (1997), Dexheimer (2008), Burgio (2010), and Koliogiannis (2020), which used models based on Brueckner theory or chiral SU(3) models and found minimal masses ranging from 0.43 to 0.61 solar masses for s = 1 and 0.56 to 1.07 solar masses for s = 2. The authors improve upon these by using only equations of state compatible with modern chiral effective field theory constraints from Kruger (2013), finding much tighter agreement between different models. The results enable future work to identify deviations from standard proto-neutron star evolution caused by exotic matter phases or beyond-standard-model particle emission, and reveal that tabular equations of state may produce artifacts from thermodynamically inconsistent treatment of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, requiring a fully generalized three-dimensional Gibbs construction for proper treatment.
