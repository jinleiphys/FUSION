# Rotational-vibrational coupling in the BPS Skyrme model of baryons

> We calculate the rotational-vibrational spectrum in the BPS Skyrme model for the hedgehog skyrmion with baryon number one. The resulting excitation energies for the nucleon and delta Roper resonances are slightly above their experimental values. Together with the fact that in the standard Skyrme model these excitation energies are significantly lower than the experimental ones, this provides strong evidence for the conjecture that the inclusion of the BPS Skyrme model is necessary for a successful quantitative description of physical properties of baryons and nuclei.

# Rotational-vibrational coupling in the BPS Skyrme model of baryons

## Key claim

The BPS Skyrme model yields excitation energies for the nucleon and delta Roper resonances that are slightly above experimental values, while the standard Skyrme model gives energies significantly below experiment; this suggests that a complete model including both the BPS terms and the standard terms, with the BPS terms dominating, should produce resonance energies close to experiment.

## Method

The authors semiclassically quantize simultaneous rotational and vibrational (breathing) modes of the hedgehog skyrmion with baryon number one in the BPS Skyrme model, which consists only of the sextic term (baryon current squared) and a potential term. They treat the rotation as an SU(2) symmetry transformation and the dilatation as a time-dependent scaling of the soliton, then promote both parameters to quantum mechanical variables. The resulting Hamiltonian is solved in each fixed-spin sector by expanding the vibrational mode about its ground state as a harmonic oscillator, and the model parameters are fitted to the nucleon and Delta masses.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon Roper resonance excitation energy in BPS Skyrme model: 588 MeV (experimental: 502 +/- 20 MeV)
- Delta Roper resonance excitation energy in BPS Skyrme model: 593 MeV (experimental: 368 +/- 100 MeV)
- Standard Skyrme model gives nucleon Roper excitation of 388-390 MeV and Delta Roper excitation of 290-292 MeV (both below experiment)
- Fitted product of model parameters: mu * lambda = 30.06 MeV
- Fitted combination: lambda^2 * (mu/lambda)^(1/3) = 17.79 MeV fm^2
- Static energy of B=1 soliton: E_06 = (64*sqrt(2)*pi/15) * mu * lambda

## Context

This work extends the BPS Skyrme model analysis of Adam et al. (2013) from purely rotational quantization to include vibrational modes, calculating the Roper resonances that were previously studied in the standard Skyrme model by Schwesinger (1986), Tarlini (1988), and Zahed (1988). The BPS model, which provides a solitonic realization of the incompressible liquid droplet limit of nuclear matter, gives excitation energies that are too high (unlike the standard model's too-low values), supporting the conjecture that a complete model with all four terms (sigma model, Skyrme term, sextic term, and potential) should yield accurate resonance energies. The results also enable future studies of the infinite set of vibrational modes from volume-preserving diffeomorphisms, which are expected to have relatively small energies once the sigma model term is added as a perturbation.
