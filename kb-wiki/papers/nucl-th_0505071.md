# Determination of the parameters of a Skyrme type effective interaction using the simulated annealing approach

> We implement for the first time the simulated annealing method (SAM) to the problem of searching for the global minimum in the hyper-surface of the chi-square function which depends on the values of the parameters of a Skyrme type effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. We undertake a realistic case of fitting the values of the Skyrme parameters to an extensive set of experimental data on the ground state properties of many nuclei ranging from normal to exotic ones. The set of experimental data used in our fitting procedure includes the radii for the valence $1d_{5/2}$ and $1f_{7/2}$ neutron orbits in the $^{17}$O and $^{41}$Ca nuclei, respectively, and the breathing mode energies for several nuclei, in addition to the typically used data on binding energy, charge radii and spin-orbit splitting. We also include in the fit the critical density $ρ_{cr}$ and further constrain the values of the Skyrme parameters by requiring that (i) the quantity $P = 3ρ\frac{dS}{dρ}$, directly related to the slope of the symmetry energy $S$, must be positive for densities up to $3ρ_0$ (ii) the enhancement factor $κ$, associated with the isovector giant dipole resonance, should lie in the range of $0.1 - 0.5$ and (iii) the Landau parameter $G_0^\prime$ is positive at $ρ= ρ_0$. We provide simple but consistent schemes to account for the center of mass corrections to the binding energy and charge radii.

## Key claim

The authors implement the simulated annealing method (SAM) for the first time to search for the global minimum in the chi-square hyper-surface when fitting Skyrme interaction parameters, claiming that this approach can produce a more realistic parameterization than traditional gradient-based methods, provided that the fit includes an extensive set of experimental data and additional physical constraints.

## Method

The authors used the simulated annealing algorithm to minimize a chi-square function that compares Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with experimental data for 14 spherical nuclei. The Skyrme parameters were expressed in terms of 10 nuclear matter quantities (binding energy per nucleon, incompressibility, saturation density, effective mass, surface energy, symmetry energy and its slope, enhancement factor, Landau parameter G0', and spin-orbit strength W0), which were varied within empirically known ranges. The fit included binding energies, charge radii, spin-orbit splittings, valence neutron orbit radii, breathing mode energies, and the critical density, with additional constraints requiring positive symmetry energy slope up to 3ρ0, enhancement factor κ between 0.1 and 0.5, and positive Landau parameter G0' at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy errors: 1.0 MeV for most nuclei, 2.0 MeV for 100Sn
- Charge radii theoretical error: 0.02 fm for most nuclei, 0.04 fm for 56Ni
- Spin-orbit splitting in 56Ni: 1.88 MeV (neutrons 2p), 1.83 MeV (protons 2p), with theoretical error 0.2 MeV
- Valence neutron orbit radii: r_v(ν1d5/2) = 3.36 fm in 17O, r_v(ν1f7/2) = 3.99 fm in 41Ca, with error 0.06 fm
- Breathing mode energies: 17.81 MeV (90Zr), 15.90 MeV (116Sn), 15.25 MeV (144Sm), 14.18 MeV (208Pb), with theoretical errors 0.5 MeV (90Zr) and 0.3 MeV (others)
- Critical density included in fit: 2.5ρ0 with error 0.5ρ0
- Enhancement factor κ constrained to range 0.1 - 0.5
- Saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Value of ℏ^2/2m = 20.734 MeV fm^2 used in calculations
- Proton charge radius ⟨r^2⟩_p = 0.801 fm^2, neutron charge radius ⟨r^2⟩_n = -0.12 fm^2

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to parameterize Skyrme interactions, specifically addressing limitations of the SKX interaction (Brown 1998) which was found unsuitable for neutron star studies (Stone 2003), and the SLy1-10 and SkI1-6 families (Chabanat 1997, 1998; Reinhard 1995) which underestimate Coulomb displacement energies. The authors introduce SAM as an alternative to traditional fitting methods, building on its recent use for generating trial parameter sets in relativistic mean-field models (Burvenich 2002, 2004). This enables the development of a unified Skyrme interaction that simultaneously reproduces ground state properties of stable and exotic nuclei, breathing mode energies, and satisfies constraints from neutron star models and Landau parameter stability.
