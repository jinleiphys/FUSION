# Rescattering effects in incoherent photoproduction of pi-mesons off deuterium in the Delta(1232) resonance region

> Incoherent photoproduction of pions on the deuteron in the Delta(1232) resonance region is investigated in order to study the influence of final state NN and PiN rescattering. For the elementary production operator an effective Lagrangian model is used which describes well the elementary reaction. The interactions in the final two-body subsystems are taken in separable form. We found that the influence of rescattering effects on total and differential cross sections is significant. Inclusion of such effects leads to an improved and quite satisfactory agreement with experiment. Since several experiments to measure the spin asymmetry of the total photoabsorption cross section, which determines the GDH sum rule, are now being performed or planned at different laboratories, the contribution of incoherent pion photoproduction to the spin asymmetry for the deuteron is evaluated with inclusion of NN and PiN rescattering contributions. The effect of final state rescattering on the spin asymmetry for the deuteron is found to be quite important and should be included in forthcoming theoretical studies.

## Key claim

Incoherent photoproduction of pions on the deuteron in the Delta(1232) resonance region requires inclusion of nucleon-nucleon (NN) and pion-nucleon (piN) rescattering effects to achieve satisfactory agreement with experimental data, as the impulse approximation systematically overestimates neutral pion cross sections by about 30 percent.

## Method

The elementary gamma N -> pi N production amplitude was constructed using an effective Lagrangian approach with pseudovector Born terms and Delta(1232) resonance contributions, fitted to experimental data from MAMI, TRIUMF, and TAPS. For the deuteron reaction, the final-state three-body dynamics were limited to leading-order two-particle interactions in the NN and piN subsystems, modeled using separable potentials fitted to phase shift data for NN and piN scattering.

## Key numbers

- The impulse approximation overestimates experimental data for neutral pions by about 30 percent.
- The conversion factor used throughout is hbar c = 197.32696 MeV.fm.
- The deuteron binding energy parameter alpha = sqrt(E_b M_N) = 0.231609 fm^{-1} is used in the wave function parametrization.
- The mass parameter m_0 = 0.9 fm^{-1} is used in the deuteron wave function expansion.

## Context

This work extends earlier impulse approximation calculations for pion photoproduction on the deuteron, which showed systematic overestimation of neutral pion cross sections. The inclusion of NN and piN rescattering effects follows the separable potential approach used by Nozawa (1990) and Haidenbauer (1984, 1985) for two-body interactions. The results are directly relevant to ongoing and planned experiments at MAMI, ELSA, LEGS, GRAAL, and TJNAF measuring the spin asymmetry of the total photoabsorption cross section for the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule, as the rescattering contributions to the spin asymmetry for the deuteron are found to be quite important and must be included in future theoretical studies.
