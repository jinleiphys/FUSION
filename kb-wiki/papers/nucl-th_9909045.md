# Effects of Disoriented Chiral Condensates on Two- and Three-Pion Correlations of Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Two- and three-pion correlations are investigated in cases when disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) occurs. A chaoticity and weight factor are used as measures of two- and three-pion correlations, and the various models for DCC are investigated. Some models are found to yield the chaoticity and weight factor in a reasonable agreement with recent experimental data.

# Effects of Disoriented Chiral Condensates on Two- and Three-Pion Correlations of Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Some models of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) can yield values of the chaoticity and weight factor for two- and three-pion correlations that agree with recent experimental data from the NA44 Collaboration, but the paper does not claim that the data prove the appearance of DCC; rather, the authors state that neutral-pion interferometry would be required to strengthen the case.

## Method

The authors compute two- and three-pion correlation functions using a generating functional method with c-number source currents, averaging over isotropic directions in isospin space to enforce the isosinglet property of DCC. They investigate three models: a partially coherent source combining one DCC source with one chaotic generic source (PC-DCC), multiple DCC domains with no generic source (M-DCC), and multiple DCC domains combined with a chaotic generic source (M-DCC/G). For each model, they derive analytic expressions for the chaoticity (lambda) and weight factor (omega) as functions of the ratio of generic to total pion number (epsilon) and the mean number of DCC domains (alpha).

## Key numbers

- For the partially coherent DCC model (PC-DCC) compared to NA44 data (chaoticity 0.4-0.5, weight factor 0.20 +/- 0.19), the best-fit ratio of generic pion number to total pion number is epsilon approximately 0.30, implying about 70% of pions are emitted from DCC domains.
- For the multiple DCC domain with chaotic generic source model (M-DCC/G), the best-fit parameters are alpha = <N> = 0.18 and epsilon = 0.57, giving a mean number of DCC domains of 0.18 and a generic pion fraction of 0.57.
- For these M-DCC/G best-fit parameters, the predicted neutral-pion chaoticity is lambda_00 = 0.35 and the neutral-pion weight factor is omega_000 = -0.12.
- For a single coherent DCC source, the two-pion correlation functions take anomalous values: C_2^{++} = C_2^{+-} = 6/5, C_2^{+0} = 4/5, C_2^{00} = 9/5.
- For a single coherent DCC source, the three-pion correlation functions include C_3^{+++} = C_3^{++-} = 54/35, C_3^{000} = 27/7.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of partially coherent sources without DCC dynamics (Nakamura and Seki, 1999) by incorporating specific DCC domain structure into pion interferometry models. It builds on earlier theoretical work by Bjorken, Rajagopal, and others who proposed DCC as a signature of chiral symmetry restoration, and on the isosinglet method of Kogan and Tkachev. The paper addresses the NA44 Collaboration's experimental data on three-pion correlations and suggests that neutral-pion interferometry, if feasible, could distinguish DCC from generic sources. The results enable future experimental searches for DCC using combined charged- and neutral-pion correlation measurements.
