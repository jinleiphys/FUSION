# Elastic Coulomb breakup of $^{34}$Na

> Purpose : The aim of this paper is to study the elastic Coulomb breakup of $^{34}$Na on $^{208}$Pb to give us a core of $^{33}$Na with a neutron and in the process we try and investigate the one neutron separation energy and the ground state configuration of $^{34}$Na. Method : A fully quantum mechanical Coulomb breakup theory within the architecture of post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation extended to include the effects of deformation is used to research the elastic Coulomb breakup of $^{34}$Na on $^{208}$Pb at 100 MeV/u. The triple differential cross-section calculated for the breakup is integrated over the desired components to find the total cross-section, momentum and angular distributions as well as the average momenta, along with the energy-angular distributions. Results : The total one neutron removal cross-section is calculated to test the possible ground state configurations of $^{34}$Na. The average momentum results along with energy-angular calculations indicate $^{34}$Na to have a halo structure. The parallel momentum distributions with narrow full widths at half maxima signify the same. Conclusion : We have attempted to analyse the possible ground state configurations of $^{34}$Na and in congruity with the patterns in the `island of inversion' conclude that even without deformation, $^{34}$Na should be a neutron halo with a predominant contribution to its ground state most probably coming from $^{33}$Na($3/2^{+}$) $\otimes$ $2p_{3/2}ν$ configuration. We also surmise that it would certainly be useful and rewarding to test our predictions with an experiment to put stricter limits on its ground state configuration and binding energy.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that even without deformation, $^{34}$Na should be a neutron halo with a predominant contribution to its ground state most probably coming from the $^{33}$Na($3/2^{+}$) $\otimes$ $2p_{3/2}\nu$ configuration, but this conclusion is hedged by the large uncertainty in the one-neutron separation energy (0.17 $\pm$ 0.50 MeV from one measurement versus approximately 0.80 $\pm$ 0.008 MeV from the NNDC database) and the lack of experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors applied a fully quantum mechanical Coulomb breakup theory within the post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA), extended to include quadrupole deformation effects, to study the elastic Coulomb breakup of $^{34}$Na on a $^{208}$Pb target at 100 MeV/u beam energy. The triple differential cross-section was calculated and integrated to obtain total cross-sections, momentum distributions, angular distributions, average momenta, and energy-angular distributions for the breakup into $^{33}$Na plus a neutron. The ground state wavefunction of $^{34}$Na was constructed from a spherical Woods-Saxon potential for single orbital angular momentum values ($2s_{1/2}$, $2p_{3/2}$, $1f_{7/2}$), with the deformation entering via the core-neutron interaction potential.

## Key numbers

* Total one-neutron removal cross-section for the $2p_{3/2}$ configuration at $S_n$ = 0.17 MeV and $\beta_2$ = 0.0 is orders of magnitude larger than for the $1f_{7/2}$ configuration at the same separation energy.
* Full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the parallel momentum distribution for the $^{33}$Na fragment with $^{33}$Na($3/2^{+}$) $\otimes$ $2p_{3/2}\nu$ ground state configuration and $S_n$ = 0.17 MeV: 36.82 MeV/c at $\beta_2$ = 0.0, 35.88 MeV/c at $\beta_2$ = 0.1, 34.76 MeV/c at $\beta_2$ = 0.2, and 33.83 MeV/c at $\beta_2$ = 0.3.
* Average slope of the linear fit of peak position of relative energy spectra versus one-neutron separation energy across deformation parameters $-0.3 \leq \beta_2 \leq 0.3$ is 0.9365.
* The one-neutron separation energy of $^{34}$Na is reported as (0.17 $\pm$ 0.50) MeV by Gaudefroy, while the NNDC database gives approximately (0.80 $\pm$ 0.008) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of halo nuclei into the medium-mass "island of inversion" region around N = 20-28, following prior theoretical and experimental investigations of neutron-rich isotopes such as $^{29}$Ne, $^{31}$Ne, $^{37}$Mg, $^{35}$Mg, and Al isotopes that have shown p- or f-wave dominant ground states. The authors note that $^{34}$Na is expected to be an innate fragment in alternate r-process paths involving light to medium mass neutron-rich nuclei as discussed by Terasaki et al. The calculations enable future experimental tests to put stricter limits on the ground state configuration and binding energy of $^{34}$Na, which would help determine its quadrupole deformation and its role in stellar nucleosynthesis.
