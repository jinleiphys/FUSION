# Fixed Angle Scattering and the Transverse Structure of Hadrons

> Talk presented at the 4th Workshop on Exclusive Reactions at High Momentum Transfer, May 18-21, 2010, Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility, Newport News, Virginia. Reformatted version with additional references

# Fixed Angle Scattering and the Transverse Structure of Hadrons

## Key claim
The perturbative treatment of high-energy fixed-angle hadron-hadron exclusive scattering is reviewed, but it is not really known to what extent experiments have yet reached a truly asymptotic regime, and pictures involving higher Fock states may offer alternative descriptions.

## Method
The paper reviews the classic parton-model "quark counting" predictions for elastic scattering, grounding them in a geometric picture where transverse structure plays a passive role, then introduces the Landshoff mechanism which reintroduces a dynamic role for transverse structure. The analysis shows how radiative corrections (Sudakov resummation) link the two pictures, with the full amplitude resulting from a competition between geometric enhancement and radiative suppression.

## Key numbers
- Quark counting rule for elastic scattering: dσ/dt = f(s/t)/s² × (m²/s)^(Σ(n_i − 1)), where n_i are the numbers of valence partons in each of the four hadrons
- Landshoff mechanism provides an enhancement by a factor Q² ∼ s for fixed-angle pion scattering and Q⁴ ∼ s² for proton-proton fixed-angle elastic scattering
- For pp at fixed angle: dσ/dt = f(s/t)/s² × (1/(s π R_H²))⁶
- For pp with s ≫ −t ≫ Λ_QCD: dσ/dt = F(s)/t² × (1/(t π R_H²))⁶
- Ratio of antiproton-proton to proton-proton elastic cross sections at 90 degrees: R_N ∼ 1/40 from experiment at Brookhaven AGS
- Sotiropoulos found R_p ∼ 1/30 at ninety degrees with color randomization, but only ∼ 1/3 without it, at √s ∼ 3.5 GeV²

## Context
This talk reviews classic results on hadron-hadron exclusive reactions from Matveev (1973), Brodsky (1974), Landshoff (1974), Efremov (1979), Lepage (1980), and Mueller (1981), and recalls subsequent work by Botts (1989) and Sotiropoulos (1994, 1995). The analysis extends the perturbative QCD factorization formalism for exclusive scattering and enables future tests of transverse hadron structure, including nuclear transparency for small-size color-singlet states as studied by Bertsch (1981), Jain (1995), Frankfurt (1993), and Carroll (1988). The paper also discusses the comparison of pp and p-pbar scattering as a probe of color dynamics, with experimental data from Blazey (1985) at Brookhaven AGS.
