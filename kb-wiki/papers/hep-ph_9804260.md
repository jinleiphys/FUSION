# Radial excitations of low--lying baryons and the $Z^+$ penta--quark

> Within an extended Skyrme soliton model for baryons the interplay between the collective radial motion and the SU(3)--flavor--rotations is investigated. The coupling between these modes is mediated by flavor symmetry breaking. Collective coordinates which describe the corresponding large amplitude fluctuations are introduced and treated canonically. When diagonalizing the resulting Hamiltonian flavor symmetry breaking is fully taken into consideration. As eigenstates not only the low--lying ${1/2}^+$ and ${3/2}^+$ baryons but also their radial excitations are obtained and compared to the empirical data. In particular the relevance of radial excitations for the penta--quark baryon $Z^+$ (Y=2, I=0, $J^π={1/2}^+$) is discussed. In this approach its mass is predicted to be $1.58{\rm GeV}$. Furthermore the widths for various hadronic decays are estimated which, for example, yields $Γ(Z^+\to NK)\sim 100{\rm MeV}$ for the only permissible decay process of the $Z^+$.

## Key claim

Within an extended Skyrme soliton model that includes a scalar glueball field to mock up the QCD trace anomaly, the coupling between collective radial motion and SU(3)-flavor rotations, mediated by flavor symmetry breaking, is treated canonically and fully diagonalized. The mass of the penta-quark baryon \(Z^+\) is predicted to be 1.58 GeV, about 40–60 MeV larger than in prior estimates that omitted the breathing mode and the coupling between \(Z^+\) states in the \(\overline{\mathbf{10}}\) and \(\overline{\mathbf{35}}\) representations.

## Method

The static soliton solution (hedgehog configuration \(U_0(\mathbf{r})\) and scalar field \(\sigma_0(r)\)) is augmented with collective coordinates for SU(3) rotations \(A(t)\) and a scale (breathing) coordinate \(x(t) = [\mu(t)]^{-3/2}\). Substituting this ansatz into the action yields a Lagrangian for \(x\) and \(A\); the flavor-symmetric part of the collective Hamiltonian is diagonalized for each SU(3) representation to obtain basis states \(|\mu, n_\mu\rangle\) labeled by radial excitation number \(n_\mu\). Flavor symmetry breaking (the \(s(x)(1-D_{88})\) term) is then fully included by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix in a basis of up to eight representations for \(J=1/2\) baryons and twelve for \(J=3/2\) baryons, including radial basis states up to 4 GeV above the ground states. Decay widths are estimated using a pseudoscalar Yukawa coupling whose flavor part is taken as \(D_{\phi,3}\) and whose scaling part is parameterized by a power \(x^{2n/3}\) (with \(n=2,3,4\)), normalized to the \(\Delta \to N\pi\) width of about 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- \(Z^+\) mass predicted to be 1.57 GeV (for \(e=5.0\)) and 1.59 GeV (for \(e=5.5\)); the abstract quotes 1.58 GeV as the central value.
- Decay width \(\Gamma(Z^+ \to NK) \sim 100\) MeV (the only permissible decay channel).
- Mass differences (in MeV) with respect to the nucleon (939 MeV) for ground-state (\(m=0\)) baryons at \(e=5.0\): \(\Lambda\) 175, \(\Sigma\) 284, \(\Xi\) 382, \(\Delta\) 258, \(\Sigma^*\) 445, \(\Xi^*\) 604, \(\Omega\) 730. For the first radial excitation (\(m=1\)) at \(e=5.0\): \(N\) 413, \(\Lambda\) 657, \(\Sigma\) 694, \(\Xi\) 941, \(\Delta\) 640, \(\Sigma^*\) 841, \(\Xi^*\) 1036, \(\Omega\) 1343.
- Strange content fractions \(X_S\) for \(J=1/2\) ground states at \(e=5.0\): \(N\) 0.16, \(\Lambda\) 0.25, \(\Sigma\) 0.30, \(\Xi\) 0.37; for the pure octet (flavor-symmetric case) the values are 0.23, 0.30, 0.37, 0.40 respectively.
- First excited state in the \(Z^+\) channel at 2.02 GeV (\(e=5.0\)) and 2.07 GeV (\(e=5.5\)).

## Context

This work extends the breathing-mode approach of Schvellinger (1991) and Schvellinger (1991a) from SU(2) to SU(3) flavor, incorporating a scalar glueball field to mock up the QCD trace anomaly as in Gomm (1986) and Jain (1987). It directly addresses the interplay between radial and rotational excitations that was omitted in the penta-quark analysis of Diakonov (1997), where the \(N(1710)\) was identified as the nucleon state in the \(\overline{\mathbf{10}}\) representation and only first-order flavor symmetry breaking was included. The present model predicts the \(Z^+\) mass about 40–60 MeV higher than Diakonov (1997) and finds that the \(N(1710)\) is not a pure \(\overline{\mathbf{10}}\) state but contains a non-negligible admixture of the \(2\hbar\omega\) radial excitation of the octet. The results enable future studies of penta-quark decays and the role of higher-dimensional SU(3) representations in baryon spectroscopy.
