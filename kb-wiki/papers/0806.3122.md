# Holographic Baryons : Static Properties and Form Factors from Gauge/String Duality

> In this paper, we study properties of baryons by using a holographic dual of QCD on the basis of the D4/D8-brane configuration, where baryons are described by a soliton. We first determine the asymptotic behavior of the soliton solution, which allows us to evaluate well-defined currents associated with the U(N_f)_L \times U(N_f)_R chiral symmetry. Using the currents, we compute static quantities of baryons such as charge radii and magnetic moments, and make a quantitative test with experiments. It is emphasized that not only the nucleon but also excited baryons, such as Δ, N(1440), N(1535) etc., can be analyzed systematically in this model. We also investigate the form factors and find that our form factors agree well with the results that are well-established empirically. With the form factors, the effective baryon-baryon-meson cubic coupling constants among their infinite towers in the model can be determined. Some physical implications following from these results are discussed.

## Key claim

The holographic D4/D8-brane model of QCD, where baryons are described as instanton solitons, yields static properties (charge radii, magnetic moments, axial couplings) and form factors for nucleons and excited baryons that agree well with experimental data, with most results closer to experiment than those from the original Skyrme model of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983), though the model is limited to the large-\(N_c\) and large-\(\lambda\) regime and the soliton solution is only valid asymptotically.

## Method

The authors start from the five-dimensional \(U(N_f)\) Yang-Mills-Chern-Simons theory derived from the D4/D8-brane configuration, with baryons identified as instanton configurations in the four spatial dimensions (three plus the holographic \(z\) coordinate). They quantize the soliton using the moduli space approximation, obtaining a quantum mechanics on the instanton moduli space parameterized by position, size \(\rho\), and \(SU(2)\) orientation. The chiral currents are computed by introducing external gauge fields at the boundaries \(z = \pm\infty\) and solving the linearized equations of motion in the curved background, using Green's functions that incorporate the warp factors \(h(z)\) and \(k(z)\) to connect the near-instanton region to the asymptotic region.

## Key numbers

* The model parameters are fixed to \(M_{\rm KK} = 949\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\kappa = 0.00745\) to fit the \(\rho\) meson mass and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi \simeq 92.4\ \text{MeV}\).
* The classical instanton size is \(\rho_{\rm cl}^2 = \frac{N_c}{8\pi^2\kappa}\sqrt{\frac{6}{5}}\) and the classical position is \(Z_{\rm cl} = 0\).
* The classical soliton mass is \(M_0 = 8\pi^2\kappa\).
* The baryon number current is given by \(J_B^\mu = -\frac{2}{N_c}\kappa \left[k(z)\widehat{F}^{\mu z}\right]^{z=+\infty}_{z=-\infty}\).
* The vector and axial-vector currents are expressed via the decay constants \(g_{v^n}\) and \(g_{a^n}\) of the infinite tower of vector and axial-vector mesons, with the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 2\sqrt{\kappa/\pi}\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD model of Sakai and Sugimoto (2005a, 2005b) and the baryon quantization of Hashimoto, Sakai, and Sugimoto (2008) by computing static properties and form factors, generalizing the Skyrme model approach of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) to include contributions from vector and axial-vector mesons automatically. It improves upon the earlier holographic current calculations of Hata, Murata, and Yamato (2007) by using gauge-invariant currents, and it enables systematic predictions for excited baryons such as \(N(1440)\) and \(N(1535)\) that are not accessible in the Skyrme model. The results are consistent with the complementary fermionic approach of Hong, Rho, Yee, and Yi (2007) and Park and Yi (2008).
