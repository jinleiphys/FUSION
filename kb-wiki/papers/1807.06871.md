# Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium Equation of State for Core Collapse

> Extensive calculations of properties of supernova matter are presented, using the extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium model of PRC92 055803 (2015) based on a statistical distribution of Wigner-Seitz cells modeled using realistic nuclear mass and level density tables, complemented with a non-relativistic Skyrme functional for unbound particles and beyond drip-line nuclei. Both thermodynamic quantities and matter composition are examined as a function of baryonic density, temperature, and proton fraction, within a large domain adapted for applications in supernova simulations. The results are also provided in the form of a table, with grid mesh and format compatible with the CompOSE platform [http://compose.obspm.fr/] for direct use in supernova simulations. Detailed comparisons are also presented with other existing databases, all based on relativistic mean-field functionals, and the differences between the different models are outlined. We show that the strongest impact on the predictions is due to the different hypotheses used to define the cluster functional and its modifications due to the presence of a nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper presents complete equation of state tables from an extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium model using a non-relativistic Skyrme functional (SLy4) for unbound particles, showing that the strongest impact on predictions comes from the different hypotheses used to define the cluster functional and its modifications due to the nuclear medium, rather than from uncertainties in the nuclear energy functional itself.

## Method

The authors compute supernova matter properties using an extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium model based on a statistical distribution of non-interacting Wigner-Seitz cells, where each cell contains a single cluster characterized by baryon number and atomic number. The cluster free energy combines experimental nuclear masses (AME2012) and evaluated masses from the Duflo-Zuker model up to drip lines, with a liquid-drop parametrization beyond drip lines, all using the Skyrme SLy4 functional for the homogeneous gas component. The self-consistent solution of coupled equations for baryon density, proton fraction, and temperature yields the cluster distribution and thermodynamic quantities across a grid compatible with the CompOSE platform.

## Key numbers

- The clusterized phase is computed up to maximum densities of typically 4e-2 to 9e-2 fm^-3, where the NSE procedure still converges
- Homogeneous matter is assumed to onset at n_t = 1e-1 fm^-3, independent of temperature and proton fraction
- The allowed mass range of clusters is 2 ≤ A ≤ 300
- A phenomenological pairing term Δ(A) = ±12/√A (MeV) is added, where + corresponds to even-even nuclei and - to odd-odd nuclei
- The Coulomb parameter used is a_c = 0.69
- The saturation density of symmetric matter n_0(0) is used with the SLy4 parametrization from Danielewicz (2003)

## Context

This work extends the Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium model of Raduta and Gulminelli (2015) and provides tables complementary to existing NSE models that employ relativistic mean-field parametrizations, such as those by Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich (2010) and Furusawa et al. (2011, 2013). The tables are designed for direct use in core-collapse supernova simulations via the CompOSE platform, enabling sensitivity studies on how different treatments of nuclear clusters affect electron capture rates and deleptonization dynamics, following earlier work by Juodagalvis et al. (2010) and Fischer et al. (2014) showing important effects of realistic nuclear distributions on weak interaction rates.
