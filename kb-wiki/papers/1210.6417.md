# A running coupling explanation of the surprising transparency of the QGP at LHC

> The CUJET Monte Carlo Jet Energy loss model is applied to predict the jet flavor, centrality and density dependence of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ at RHIC and LHC. Running coupling effects due to combined $x$, $k_\perp$ and $q_\perp$ evolution are included for the first time in the dynamical DGLV opacity expansion framework and are shown to provide a natural dynamical QCD tomographic solution to the surprising transparency of the quark gluon plasma produced at LHC as suggested by $p_\perp>10$ GeV $R_{AA}$ data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS.

## Key claim

The inclusion of running coupling effects in the CUJET model provides a natural dynamical QCD tomographic solution to the surprising transparency of the quark gluon plasma at LHC, but the model's only free parameter (alpha_0 = 0.4) is fit to a single LHC reference point at p_T = 40 GeV rather than being a fully ab initio prediction.

## Method

The CUJET Monte Carlo model extends the GLV/DGLV opacity series approach by computing first-order-in-opacity contributions to the jet medium induced gluon radiative spectrum, using a pure HTL dynamical interaction potential. Both radiative and elastic contributions to energy loss are included, with fluctuations of radiated gluon number incorporated via Poisson expansion, and the plasma is modeled as inhomogeneous (Glauber profile) with 1+1D Bjorken expansion. Running coupling effects are implemented at one-loop order with the scale Q varying by plus or minus 25 percent, with the coupling at the radiated gluon vertex scaling as alpha_s(k_perp^2 / (x(1-x))) being the most important contribution.

## Key numbers

- Central dN/dy = 1000 for sqrt(s) = 0.2 GeV RHIC collisions and 2200 for sqrt(s) = 2.76 GeV LHC collisions
- Initial thermalization time tau_0 = 1 fm/c and decoupling temperature T approx 100 MeV
- Free parameter alpha_0 = 0.4, constrained by fitting pion R_AA(LHC, p_T = 40 GeV) = 0.35
- Fixed coupling CUJET results used alpha_s = 0.3, constrained at p_T = 10 GeV RHIC
- Running coupling results show improved agreement with ALICE and CMS data in the p_T range 30-100 GeV at LHC
- D meson R_AA predictions at LHC are constrained by the same pion fit parameters (alpha_0 = 0.4)

## Context

This work extends the GLV, DGLV, and WHDG opacity series approaches (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev 2000-2002; Djordjevic, Gyulassy 2003; Wicks, Horowitz, Djordjevic, Gyulassy 2007) by implementing running coupling effects suggested by Betz (2012) to resolve the discrepancy where fixed-coupling extrapolations from RHIC systematically overpredicted LHC quenching. The results enable further jet flavor tomography studies, particularly for heavy mesons at both RHIC and LHC, to test the energy dependence of parton energy loss.
