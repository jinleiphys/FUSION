# Pionic Final State Interactions and the Hypertriton Lifetime

> We analyze the contribution of pionic final state interactions (FSI) in the weak decay of the hypertriton. Focusing on the $^3$He channel, we find a contribution of the pionic FSI of the order of $18\%$. Assuming a fixed value for the branching ratio $R_3$ for the decay width into $^3$He over the decay width into $^3$He and $pd$ final states, we find values for the hypertriton lifetime that are consistent with the world average as well as recent measurements by the ALICE Collaboration.

## Key claim

Pionic final state interactions (FSI) in the hypertriton decay into the \(^3\)He channel contribute an enhancement of the decay width of about 18%, but this result depends on using a fixed experimental branching ratio \(R_3\) and on an estimate of the \(\pi^-\)-\(^3\)He scattering phase shift from sparse cross-section data at a single energy.

## Method

The authors calculate the weak decay of the hypertriton into \(^3\)He and a \(\pi^-\) within an effective field theory framework, adding the pionic FSI amplitude coherently to the direct decay amplitude. They treat the elastic \(\pi^-\)-\(^3\)He scattering amplitude on-shell and include the principal-value integral of the loop momentum to define a characteristic momentum \(\bar{k} \approx 64\) MeV. Using differential cross-section data for \(\pi^-\)-\(^3\)He scattering at \(T_\pi^{\text{lab}} = 45.1\) MeV from Fournier (1984), they perform a least-squares fit to extract the S-wave phase shift \(\delta_0 \approx 11^\circ\), which enters the FSI correction factor. The full hypertriton lifetime is then obtained by assuming a fixed branching ratio \(R_3 = \Gamma_{^3\text{He}}/(\Gamma_{^3\text{He}} + \Gamma_{pd})\) taken from experimental averages.

## Key numbers

*   The pionic FSI contribution enhances the \(^3\)He decay width by 18% relative to the direct decay.
*   The outgoing pion momentum in the hypertriton decay is fixed at \(k \approx 114\) MeV.
*   The characteristic momentum from the principal-value integral is \(\bar{k} = 2k/\pi \approx 64\) MeV.
*   The S-wave phase shift extracted from the fit to \(\pi^-\)-\(^3\)He scattering data at \(T_\pi^{\text{lab}} = (45.1 \pm 1.0)\) MeV is \(\delta_0 = -11.0523^\circ\).
*   Using the branching-ratio method with pionic FSI, the hypertriton lifetime is \(\tau \approx 0.84\,\tau_\Lambda\) (where \(\tau_\Lambda\) is the free \(\Lambda\) lifetime), assuming the branching ratio from the authors' previous calculation.
*   The world average hypertriton lifetime is \(237^{+10}_{-9}\) ps (HypernuclearDataBase), and the ALICE (2022) value is \(\tau = (253 \pm 11 \pm 6)\) ps.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous EFT calculation of the hypertriton lifetime (Hildenbrand 2020), which neglected pionic FSI based on a kinematical suppression argument. It follows the strategy of Perez-Obiol (2020), who treated pionic FSI using distorted pion waves and found a contribution of order 10%. The present result confirms that pionic FSI must be included at next-to-leading order in the EFT power counting. The method enables predictions for the hypertriton lifetime as a function of the \(\Lambda\) separation energy, and the results are consistent with the world average and with recent ALICE measurements.
