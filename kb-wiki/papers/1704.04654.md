# Event-by-event $v_n$ correlations of soft hadrons and heavy mesons in heavy ion collisions

> Combining event-by-event hydrodynamics with heavy quark energy loss we compute correlations between the heavy and soft sectors for elliptic and triangular flow harmonics $v_2$ and $v_3$ of D$^0$ mesons in PbPb collisions at $2.76$ TeV and $5.02$ TeV. Our results indicate that $v_3$ is strongly influenced by the fragmentation temperature and that it builds up later than $v_2$ during the evolution of the system.

# Event-by-event $v_n$ correlations of soft hadrons and heavy mesons in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The correlation between triangular flow $v_3$ of D$^0$ mesons and the soft sector is strongly influenced by the fragmentation temperature, indicating that $v_3$ builds up later than $v_2$ during the evolution of the system, whereas the elliptic flow $v_2$ correlation shows no significant dependence on fragmentation temperature.

## Method
The authors combined event-by-event relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (v-USPhydro) with a Monte Carlo heavy quark energy loss simulation (DAB-MOD) that uses a parametrized energy loss per unit length proportional to a flow factor. Heavy quarks were sampled at midrapidity using FONLL pQCD calculations, propagated through hydrodynamic backgrounds with a constant drag coefficient, and hadronized via fragmentation at a chosen decoupling temperature $T_d$, with no coalescence implemented.

## Key numbers
- D$^0$ meson nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ computed for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 2.76$ TeV and $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV in 0-10% central collisions, with good agreement for $p_T \gtrsim 10$ GeV
- Two fragmentation temperatures tested: $T_d = 120$ MeV and $T_d = 160$ MeV
- Multi-particle cumulants $v_2\{m\}$ of D$^0$ mesons computed for 30-40% centrality at both collision energies
- Hydrodynamic simulations used $\eta/s = 0.11$ and initial time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm

## Context
This work extends the event shape engineering approach of Prado (2016) by computing correlations between heavy flavor and soft sector flow harmonics using event-by-event hydrodynamics combined with heavy quark energy loss. The results contrast with the approximate linear response relation between high $p_T$ $v_2$ and initial state eccentricity $\epsilon_2$ established in earlier work by Noronha-Hostler (2015) and Wang (2000). The finding that $v_3$ builds up later than $v_2$ enables future studies of the time structure of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma and motivates inclusion of coalescence hadronization at lower $p_T$ where the current model underestimates experimental data.
