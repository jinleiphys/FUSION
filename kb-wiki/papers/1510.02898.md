# Intermediate Mass Fragment Emission in ${^{32}}$S +${^{51}}$V, ${^{109}}$Ag, and ${^{238}}$U Collisions at E~=~31.6~MeV~A

> Intermediate mass fragment emission for reactions of $^{32}\text{S} +\,^{51}\text{V},\,^{109}$Ag, and $^{238}\text{U}$ has been studied. Double differential cross sections were analysed in terms of the generalised moving source model yielding charge distributions. Isotope ratios show strong fragment mass dependencies. The data were successfully reproduced by the coalescence model as well as by statistical multifragmentation model calculations. Quantum molecular dynamics model calculations were not so successful.

## Key claim

The paper claims that intermediate mass fragment (IMF) emission in 32S-induced reactions at 31.6 MeV per nucleon can be successfully described by the coalescence model and the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), but the abstract glosses over the fact that quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model calculations were "not so successful" and that the generalized moving source model (GMSM) showed inconsistencies when applied to data with limited angular and energy coverage.

## Method

Double differential cross sections for IMFs (Z=3 to Z=16) were measured at six laboratory angles (17°, 35°, 53°, 71°, 89°, and 107°) using ΔE-E silicon detector telescopes. The data were analyzed using a generalized moving source model (GMSM) to extract source velocities, temperatures, and angle-integrated cross sections, and were compared with coalescence model, statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), and quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 1.011 GeV for 32S13+ ions, corresponding to 31.6 MeV per nucleon
- Target thicknesses: 51V (10.0 mg/cm²), 109Ag (10.0 mg/cm²), 238U (2.9 mg/cm²)
- Solid angles: ΔΩ = 17.7 msr for most angles, ΔΩ = 9.2 msr at 17° position
- Systematic error in cross sections: 6%
- Energy calibration uncertainty: 1.3%
- Projectile-like source velocity: approximately 0.2c, corresponding to v_proj/v_beam ≈ 0.77
- Intermediate source velocity: v_int/v_beam ≈ 0.50
- Beam velocity: v_beam = 0.26c
- Fitted a(ε) parameter function: a(ε) = 1 + (0.006677 ± 0.000223)ε^(3/2)
- Ratio R = dσ(7Be)/dσ(7Li) at 35°: R = 0.407(3) - 0.0188(2)Ṽ_C, where Ṽ_C is the reduced Coulomb barrier
- Cross section power law for lithium isotopes: α = 2/3 for 6Li, α = 1 for 8,9Li

## Context

This work extends previous studies of IMF emission from heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies (10-100 MeV per nucleon), particularly building on the moving source analyses of Machner et al. (1990), Buhler et al. (1992), and Machner et al. (2006). The results are compared with earlier measurements by Lleres et al. (1987) for sulfur on silver and gold at 30 MeV per nucleon, and with studies by Kim et al. (1992) and Milkau et al. (1990) for similar systems. The isotope ratio findings connect to isospin drift and diffusion studies by Tsang et al. (2009) and to the CHIMERA collaboration results of Filippo et al. (2014). The work enables further testing of coalescence and statistical fragmentation models against inclusive IMF data, while highlighting the need for more complete angular and energy coverage to validate the generalized moving source approach.
