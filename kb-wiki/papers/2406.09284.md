# Surface and curvature tensions of relativistic models

> In the present paper, we show a simple method to obtain fittings for the surface and curvature tensions. The method uses the nuclear mass of a spherical fully ionized atom and a simple expression for the binding energy such that a least square fit is found when confronted with the Atomic Mass Evaluation (AME) 2020. The fittings are then used to evaluate the pasta phase free energy per particle, which is confronted with the one obtained with a Thomas-Fermi fitting. The results are very encouraging and suggest that this recipe can be safely used whenever the surface and curvature tensions are necessary.

## Key claim

The paper provides a simple least-squares fitting method to obtain surface and curvature tension parameters for relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, using nuclear masses from the Atomic Mass Evaluation (AME) 2020. The authors claim the resulting pasta-phase free energies are "very encouraging" and that the recipe can be "safely used" whenever surface and curvature tensions are needed, but they note that a one-to-one mapping with Thomas-Fermi results is not possible except at zero temperature and symmetric proton fraction.

## Method

The authors fit four parameters (sigma_0, b_s, sigma_{0,c}, beta) for each RMF parametrization by minimizing the chi-squared difference between theoretical and experimental binding energies per nucleon from the AME 2020 database, using nuclei with N >= 8 and Z >= 8. The theoretical binding energy is computed from a nuclear mass expression that includes bulk, surface, curvature, and Coulomb terms, with the surface and curvature tensions parameterized as functions of proton fraction at zero temperature. The resulting zero-temperature tensions are extended to finite temperature using a scaling function h(T) that depends on the critical temperature of symmetric nuclear matter, and the pasta-phase free energy is computed using the coexistence phase approximation (CPA) and compared with Thomas-Fermi results.

- For the TW99 parametrization: sigma_0 = 1.14237 +/- 0.00098 MeV.fm^{-2}, b_s = 19.81622 +/- 0.19257, sigma_{0,c} = 0.11633 +/- 0.00324 MeV.fm^{-1}, beta = 0.46713 +/- 0.00361, chi^2 = 0.76282, T_c^{SM} = 15.174 MeV.
- The theoretical error per data point used in the chi^2 calculation was Delta B_i = 0.04 MeV.
- The pasta-phase free energy per particle was compared at T = 3 MeV for IUFSU and NL3omega_rho, showing coincident transition densities from pasta to homogeneous phase.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hoa (2021) for fitting surface and curvature tensions from nuclear masses, applying it to a wide set of RMF parametrizations that are consistent with neutron star observations from LIGO/Virgo and with nuclear structure data. It provides an alternative to the more computationally intensive Thomas-Fermi fitting method previously used for specific models (e.g., by the authors in earlier work), enabling consistent surface and curvature tension calculations for any RMF model where such fittings were not available. The results enable future calculations of the pasta phase in neutron star crusts and core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly for models where only bulk properties are known.
