# Hyperon Non-leptonic Weak Decays in the Chiral Perturbation Theory II

> Hyperon non-leptonic weak decay amplitudes are studied in the chiral perturbation theory. The weak interaction vertices caused by the four quark operators are substituted by the products of the hadronic currents and by the phenomenologically introduced weak Hamiltonian of hadron operators. Our study suggests the improvement of the theoretical prediction for the weak decay amplitudes.

## Key claim

The study suggests that introducing a phenomenological "internal interaction" Hamiltonian, particularly one with V-A or S±iP symmetry, can improve the theoretical prediction of hyperon non-leptonic weak decay amplitudes within chiral perturbation theory, but the improvement depends on the specific form of the internal interaction and the fitting of unknown parameters.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective weak Hamiltonian for ΔS=1 hyperon decays by combining a current-current interaction (derived from products of hadronic currents) with a phenomenological internal interaction Hamiltonian representing two-point baryon vertices. They considered three forms of the internal interaction: a minimal form with three parameters, a V-A symmetric form with five parameters, and an S±iP symmetric form with ten parameters. The amplitudes were calculated at tree level and with one-loop chiral logarithmic corrections, and the unknown parameters were fitted to eight experimental hyperon decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The parameters a1 and a2 in the scalar current were determined from baryon mass differences as a1 = 29.1/ms and a2 = -94.8/ms, where ms is the strange quark mass.
* The effective Hamiltonian H_current + H_int^min and H_current + H_int^(V-A) each have 5 unknown parameters fitted to 8 experimental data points.
* The effective Hamiltonian H_current + H_int^(S±iP) has 9 unknown parameters, requiring the assumption h_MP = 0.0 to reduce to 8 fitted parameters.
* The amplitudes derived from H_int^(S±iP) and H_int^(V-A(2)) (where a1 and a2 were also treated as unknown parameters) reproduced the experimental data well, while H_int^(V-A(1)) (with fixed a1, a2) did not.

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Takayama and Oka (1998) which used only current-current interactions and could reproduce ΔI=3/2 amplitudes but not ΔI=1/2 amplitudes. It addresses the failure of chiral perturbation theory to reproduce hyperon decay data noted by Bijnens (1985) and Jenkins (1992). The newly introduced internal interaction terms have the same form as those in Borasoy (1998), but the present method imposes constraints between parameters by using Noether currents. The study enables future work to resolve three open questions: the origin of S±iP symmetry mixing in weak interactions, the sign contradiction of a1 and a2 parameters, and the ambiguity in the quark condensation value B0.
