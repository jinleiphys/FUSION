# Analysis of $M1$ capture in the $α(d,γ)^6$Li reaction

> An effective operator is exactly equivalent to the long-wavelength form of the $M1$ operator in transition matrix elements. It allows us to analytically and numerically analyze the $M1$ contribution to the $α(d,γ)^6$Li reaction. Isoscalar $M1$ transitions from an initial $S$ wave are shown to be forbidden in radiative capture reactions when distortion is neglected in the initial state. A calculation in a three-body model with proton, neutron, and a structureless $α$ interacting through effective forces leads to a negligible $M1$ $S$-factor at small energies. The dominant $M1$ contribution comes from transitions from an initial $S$ wave to isospin 1 components of the $^6$Li ground state. It is suggested that using this effective $M1$ operator in other models should clarify the origin of large discrepancies between $M1$ $S$-factors appearing in the literature.

# Analysis of $M1$ capture in the $\alpha(d,\gamma)^6$Li reaction

## Key claim

Isoscalar $M1$ transitions from an initial $S$ wave are forbidden in radiative capture reactions when distortion is neglected in the initial state, meaning the dominant $M1$ contribution at low energies must come from isovector transitions to isospin 1 components of the $^6$Li ground state. The resulting $M1$ $S$-factor is negligible compared to $E2$ contributions, contradicting the large $M1$ component found in the ab initio calculation of Hupin, Hagen, and Kvaal (2022) but agreeing with Nollett, Wiringa, and Schiavilla (2001) and Solovyev (2022).

## Method

The authors derived an effective $M1$ operator exactly equivalent to the long-wavelength approximation form by subtracting a term proportional to the total angular momentum operator, exploiting the orthogonality of initial and final states in radiative capture. They then applied this operator in a three-body $p+n+\alpha$ model with effective Minnesota $NN$ and deep $\alpha N$ interactions, where the $^6$Li ground state wave function was obtained variationally in hyperspherical coordinates and the initial $\alpha+d$ scattering states were described with a frozen deuteron and a central $\alpha+d$ potential. The initial and final wave functions, arising from different Hamiltonians, were properly orthogonalized, and the $M1$ $S$-factor was computed from partial cross sections for $^3S_1$, $^3D_1$, and $^3D_2$ initial partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The squared norm of the isotriplet ($T=1$) component in the $^6$Li ground state is $5.3 \times 10^{-3}$.
- The $\alpha N$ potential was renormalized by a factor of 1.014 in the $S$ wave to reproduce the experimental binding energy of 3.70 MeV for $^6$Li relative to the $p+n+\alpha$ threshold.
- The $M1$ $S$-factor is orders of magnitude smaller than the dominant $E2$ $S$-factor across the energy range studied.
- The isovector $^3D_J$ matrix elements are smaller by about one order of magnitude than the isoscalar $^3D_J$ matrix elements.
- The isovector $^3D_1$ and $^3D_2$ curves are weaker by about two orders of magnitude at the lowest energies compared to the isovector $^3S_1$ component.

## Context

This work extends the three-body model studies of the $\alpha(d,\gamma)^6$Li reaction by Tursunov, Tursunov, and Kadyrov (2016, 2018, 2020) and Baye and Tursunov (2018), which treated $E1$ and $E2$ capture, by providing a detailed analysis of the $M1$ component. The results confirm the negligible $M1$ contribution found in the six-body model of Nollett, Wiringa, and Schiavilla (2001) and the microscopic cluster model of Solovyev (2022), but contradict the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum calculation of Hupin, Hagen, and Kvaal (2022), which found a large $M1$ $S$-factor that increases at very small energies. The effective $M1$ operator introduced here, based on the formalism of Baye (2023), enables future analyses of the origin of this discrepancy when applied in other models.
