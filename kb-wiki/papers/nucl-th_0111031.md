# Recent results on hadron form factors

> We discuss the recent data on the electric proton form factor, obtained at JLab, which show a spectacular deviation from the commonly assumed dipole behavior. We discuss the implication of these results on the deuteron structure and on the neutron electric form factor: at relatively large $Q^2$ a revision of the deuteron models may be required, and the neutron electric form factor might become even larger than the proton electric form factor.

# Recent results on hadron form factors

## Key claim
The new Jefferson Lab data on the proton electric form factor show a spectacular deviation from the commonly assumed dipole behavior, but the theoretical situation is not yet clarified; the neutron electric form factor might become even larger than the proton electric form factor for momentum transfers above approximately 2 GeV², contingent on the specific choice of nucleon form factors used in the deuteron analysis.

## Method
The authors analyze the ratio G_Ep/G_Mp measured via the recoil polarization method in Hall A at JLab, where longitudinally polarized electrons scatter off unpolarized protons and the transferred polarization of the recoiling proton is measured by a focal-plane polarimeter. They then use elastic electron-deuteron scattering data for the structure functions A(Q²) and the tensor polarization t_20, combined with the new proton form factor results, to extract the isoscalar nucleon form factor and thereby infer the neutron electric form factor G_En within the non-relativistic impulse approximation using the Paris potential.

## Key numbers
- The ratio G_Ep/G_Mp was measured up to Q² = 3.5 GeV² in the first JLab experiment and later extended to Q² = 5.6 GeV².
- At Q² = 9 GeV², the electric form factor contribution to the unpolarized cross section is only about 8% of the total.
- The new G_Ep data can be fitted by G_Ep(Q²) = 1/[(1 + Q²/0.71 GeV²)² × (1 + Q²/4.8 GeV²)], explicitly showing deviation from the dipole form.
- The deuteron elastic cross section appears to scale as (Q²)^(-10) up to Q² ≈ 6 GeV².
- The reduced deuteron form factor analysis yields values of the QCD scale parameter Λ ranging from 0.032 ± 0.016 GeV (case 2) to 0.648 ± 0.228 GeV (case 3), depending on the choice of nucleon form factors.
- The neutron electric form factor G_En becomes larger than G_Ep starting from Q² ≈ 2 (GeV/c)².

## Context
This work extends the analysis of deuteron electromagnetic structure following the JLab measurements of G_Ep by Jones et al. (2000) and the deuteron A(Q²) measurements by Alexa et al. (1999) and t_20 measurements by Abbott et al. (2000). The authors show that the commonly used dipole parametrization for all nucleon form factors, previously consistent with Rosenbluth separation data from Arnold et al. (1986), must be revised. The results challenge the scaling relation for the reduced deuteron form factor proposed by Brodsky et al. (1976) and indicate that meson exchange current and relativistic corrections in deuteron models need to be reconsidered. The upcoming direct measurements of G_En by Madey et al. will be crucial for resolving the interpretation.
