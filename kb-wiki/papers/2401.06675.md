# Neutron-rich nuclei and neutron skins from chiral low-resolution interactions

> Neutron-rich nuclei provide important insights to nuclear forces and to the nuclear equation of state. Advances in ab initio methods combined with new opportunities with rare isotope beams enable unique explorations of their properties based on nuclear forces applicable over the entire nuclear chart. In this Letter, we develop novel chiral low-resolution interactions that accurately describe bulk properties from $^{16}$O to $^{208}$Pb. With these, we investigate density distributions and neutron skins of neutron-rich nuclei. Our results show that neutron skins are narrowly predicted over all nuclei with interesting sensitivities for the most extreme, experimentally unexplored cases.

## Key claim

The paper develops novel chiral low-resolution nuclear interactions that accurately reproduce ground-state energies, charge radii, and neutron skins from 16O to 208Pb, resolving the underprediction of radii that plagued the established 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction, but the authors hedge that neutron skins are "narrowly predicted" across all nuclei with interesting sensitivities only for the most extreme, experimentally unexplored cases at isospin asymmetry I greater than about 0.25.

## Method

The authors start from high-precision nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials (N3LO EM 500, EMN, and NNLOsim families) evolved via similarity renormalization group (SRG) to a resolution scale lambda = 1.8 fm^{-1}, then fit the three-nucleon (3N) low-energy couplings c_D and c_E at N2LO with cutoff Lambda_3N = 2.0 fm^{-1} to reproduce the 3H ground-state energy and, unlike prior work, the 16O ground-state energy and charge radius. All many-body calculations use the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) at the IMSRG(2) approximation in a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis with e_max = 14 and E^(3)_max = 24.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies per nucleon for closed-shell nuclei from 16O to 208Pb show deviations from experiment below 2%, with the exception of 56Ni for the 1.8/2.0 (sim7.5) interaction.
- The two novel interactions, 1.8/2.0 (EM7.5) and 1.8/2.0 (sim7.5), use c_D = 7.5 and reproduce the 16O charge radius, resolving the underprediction of the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction.
- For 208Pb, the neutron skin from the new interactions agrees with the PREX parity-violating electron scattering result only within the 2-sigma range.
- The harmonic-oscillator frequency hbar-omega ranges from 10 to 16 MeV depending on the system and observable.

## Context

This work extends the low-resolution interaction strategy of Hebeler et al. (2011), who introduced the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction that suffered from underpredicted radii as noted by Simonis et al. (2017). The new interactions improve upon the established 1.8/2.0 (EM) and DeltaNNLO_GO interactions by simultaneously reproducing charge radii and binding energies across the nuclear chart. The results enable ab initio studies of neutron-rich nuclei at rare isotope beam facilities like FAIR, FRIB, and RIBF, and provide constraints on the symmetry energy slope L that connects neutron skins to the nuclear equation of state and neutron star properties.
