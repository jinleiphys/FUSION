# A nuclear matter calculation with the tensor optimized Fermi sphere method using central interaction

> The tensor optimized Fermi sphere (TOFS) method is applied first for the study of the property of nuclear matter using the Argonne V4' $NN$ potential. In the TOFS method, the correlated nuclear matter wave function is taken to be a power-series-type of the correlation function $F$, where $F$ can induce central, spin-isospin, tensor, etc.~correlations. This expression has been ensured by a cluster expansion theory. In the TOFS calculation, we take into account the contributions from all the many body terms arising from the product of the nuclear-matter Hamiltonian $\mathcal{H}$ and $F$. It is found that the density dependence of the energy per particle in nuclear matter is reasonably reproduced, in comparison with other methods such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach.

## Key claim

The first-order tensor optimized Fermi sphere (TOFS) method, using only the central-force Argonne V4' potential and including all many-body terms from one-body to six-body operators, reasonably reproduces the density dependence of the energy per particle in symmetric nuclear matter when compared to other approaches such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method, but the calculation omits tensor correlations and is limited to first order (N=1) in the power-series expansion of the correlation function.

## Method

The authors applied the first-order TOFS method (N=1) to symmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne V4' central two-nucleon potential. The correlated wave function was taken as Ψ_{N=1} = [1 + F_S] Φ_0, where F_S is a spin-isospin dependent central correlation function expanded in Gaussian functions, and Φ_0 is the uncorrelated Fermi-gas wave function. All linked-diagram contributions from one-body to six-body terms arising from the product of the Hamiltonian and the correlation function were evaluated, and the Gaussian expansion coefficients of F_S were determined variationally by solving a system of linear equations.

## Context

This work extends the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) method, previously applied to finite nuclei by Myo et al. (2015, 2017), to infinite nuclear matter through the newly developed TOFS formalism. The results are compared with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach and other many-body methods such as Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG), self-consistent Green's function (SCGF), and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC), as studied by Baldo et al. (2012) for various Argonne potentials. The present calculation enables future application of the TOFS method to more complex bare interactions including tensor and spin-orbit forces.
