# Optimal Observables for the Chiral Magnetic Effect from Machine Learning

> The detection of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions remains challenging due to substantial background contributions that obscure the expected signal. In this Letter, we present a novel machine learning approach for constructing optimized observables that significantly enhance CME detection capabilities. By parameterizing generic observables constructed from flow harmonics and optimizing them to maximize the signal-to-background ratio, we systematically develop CME-sensitive measures that outperform conventional methods. Using simulated data from the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics framework, our machine learning observables demonstrate up to 90\% higher sensitivity to CME signals compared to traditional $γ$ and $δ$ correlators, while maintaining minimal background contamination. The constructed observables provide physical insight into optimal CME detection strategies, and offer a promising path forward for experimental searches of CME at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The machine learning optimized observables achieve up to 90% higher sensitivity to the Chiral Magnetic Effect signal compared to traditional gamma and delta correlators, but this performance is demonstrated only in simulated data from the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics framework with specific parameter choices (axial-charge-to-entropy ratio n5/s=0.1, later corrected to 0.05, and local charge conservation parameter P_LCC=0.33) and a magnetic field lifetime of 0.6 fm.

## Method

The authors parameterize a general class of P-even observables as linear and bilinear combinations of flow harmonic coefficients (v_n and a_n) weighted by powers of transverse momentum, then optimize the coefficients using gradient descent to maximize a loss function that rewards CME signal significance while penalizing background sensitivity. They generate approximately 3 million events from the Event-by-Event Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) simulation for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with 30-40% centrality, running four scenarios with CME and local charge conservation (LCC) backgrounds independently turned on and off. The optimization scans expansion cutoffs in harmonic order N=1,2,3,4 and p_T weight order M=0,1,2,3, and the resulting observables are compared against conventional gamma and delta correlators.

## Key numbers

- Optimized observables demonstrate up to 90% higher sensitivity to CME signals compared to traditional gamma and delta correlators (abstract)
- Signal significance reaches 18.7 sigma with N=4 and M=3 expansion cutoffs, compared to 8.9 sigma for gamma correlator and 13.3 sigma for delta correlator
- Background-to-error ratios for conventional correlators are 52 (gamma) and 327 (delta), while optimized observables with N>=2 show background consistent with zero
- At the lowest complexity (N=1, M=0), the optimized observable achieves signal significance of 12.3 sigma with background influence reduced to approximately 0.2
- Simulation uses approximately 3 x 10^6 events for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with 30-40% centrality
- Magnetic field lifetime set to 0.6 fm in the simulation
- Correction factor f_c = 1/4 applied to significance estimates to adjust from n5/s=0.1 to n5/s=0.05

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to detect the Chiral Magnetic Effect in heavy-ion collisions, which has been pursued experimentally at RHIC and the LHC for over a decade using conventional gamma and delta correlators (Voloshin, 2004) that suffer from substantial background contamination from local charge conservation, elliptic flow, and resonance decays. The method builds on recent machine learning applications in high-energy nuclear physics (Aarts, 2025; Zhou, 2023; Zhao, 2021) and leverages the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics simulation framework (Shi, 2017; Jiang, 2016) to generate training data. The approach enables systematic improvement through inclusion of higher-order harmonics and transverse momentum weighting, and the authors propose extending the method to experimental data by minimizing observable response in ultra-high-energy collisions where CME is negligible and maximizing signal differences between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, with a predicted scaling relation O_CME^(Ru+Ru) approximately 1.2 times O_CME^(Zr+Zr) from magnetic field strength scaling.
