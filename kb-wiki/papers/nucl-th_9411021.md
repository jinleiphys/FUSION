# On the structure of the scalar mesons $f_0(975)$ and $a_0(980)$

> We investigate the structure of the scalar mesons $f_0(975)$ and $a_0(980)$ within realistic meson-exchange models of the $ππ$ and $πη$ interactions. Starting from a modified version of the Jülich model for $ππ$ scattering we perform an analysis of the pole structure of the resulting scattering amplitude and find, in contrast to existing models, a somewhat large mass for the $f_0(975)$ ($m_{f_0}=1015$ MeV, $Γ_{f_0}=30$ MeV). It is shown that our model provides a description of $J/ψ\rightarrowϕππ/ϕKK$ data comparable in quality with those of alternative models. Furthermore, the formalism developed for the $ππ$ system is consistently extended to the $πη$ interaction leading to a description of the $a_0(980)$ as a dynamically generated threshold effect (which is therefore neither a conventional $q\overline{q}$ state nor a $K\overline{K}$ bound state). Exploring the corresponding pole position the $a_0(980)$ is found to be rather broad ($m_{a_0}=991$ MeV, $Γ_{a_0}=202$ MeV). The experimentally observed smaller width results from the influence of the nearby $K\overline{K}$ threshold on this pole.

## Key claim

The $f_0(975)$ is a $K\overline{K}$ bound state with a relatively large pole mass of 1015 MeV, while the $a_0(980)$ is not a $K\overline{K}$ bound state but a dynamically generated threshold effect with a broad pole at 991 MeV; the experimentally observed narrower width of the $a_0(980)$ results from the influence of the nearby $K\overline{K}$ threshold.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel meson-exchange model for $\pi\pi$ and $\pi\eta$ scattering using the Blankenbecler-Sugar formalism, starting from a modified version of the Jülich model. They iterated the potentials via a coupled-channel scattering equation to obtain the $T$-matrix, then searched for poles of the scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane by solving an eigenvalue equation that accounts for the multi-sheet structure imposed by the $\pi\pi$ and $K\overline{K}$ thresholds. The formalism was extended consistently from the $\pi\pi$ system to the $\pi\eta$ system, using the same $K\overline{K}$ interaction projected onto isospin $I=1$ and adding $K^*$ exchange for the $\pi\eta\rightarrow K\overline{K}$ transition.

## Key numbers

- $f_0(975)$ pole position: $m_{f_0}=1015$ MeV, $\Gamma_{f_0}=30$ MeV (on the $[bt]$ sheet)
- $a_0(980)$ pole position: $m_{a_0}=991$ MeV, $\Gamma_{a_0}=202$ MeV (on the $[bt]$ sheet)
- $\sigma(400)$ pole on $[bt]$ sheet at complex energy $(387,\pm305)$ MeV
- $f_0(1400)$ pole on $[bb]$ sheet at $(1346,\pm249)$ MeV
- $\rho$ pole on $[bt]$ sheet at $(775,\pm82)$ MeV
- In the zero $\pi\pi/K\overline{K}$ coupling limit, the $f_0$ pole moves to $(985,0)$ MeV on the real axis below $K\overline{K}$ threshold
- Breit-Wigner fit to the calculated $a_0$ cross section yields $m_{a_0}\simeq 985$ MeV and $\Gamma_{a_0}\simeq 110$ MeV, in agreement with the experimental values of Armstrong (1991) ($m_{a_0}=984\pm4$ MeV, $\Gamma_{a_0}=95\pm14$ MeV) but not with the Particle Data Group average ($m_{a_0}=983\pm2$ MeV, $\Gamma_{a_0}=57\pm11$ MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Jülich meson-exchange model for $\pi\pi$ scattering (Lohse 1990) to the $\pi\eta$ system and addresses the long-standing debate over the nature of the scalar mesons $f_0(975)$ and $a_0(980)$. The authors find that, contrary to the claims of Morgan and Pennington (1993) that a conventional Breit-Wigner ($q\overline{q}$) structure is most probable for the $f_0(975)$, their $K\overline{K}$ bound state model fits the $J/\psi\rightarrow\phi\pi\pi/\phi KK$ data comparably well, supporting the view of Zou and Bugg (1994) that $J/\psi$ decay may not be the crucial discriminant. The results enable future experimental investigations of $K\overline{K}$ production and the $\pi\eta$ system, such as those proposed at the Jülich COSY facility.
