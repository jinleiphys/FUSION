# Analytic model studies of polarized baryon production

> We investigate known exact solutions of hydrodynamics and derive analytic formulas for the polarization of baryons produced at freeze-out. Such polarization (observed in high energy heavy-ion experiments) carries information on the time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), and our results give first analytic insight into the connection between this type of measurements and dynamical properties of the sQGP (e.g. vorticity). We present results for a rotating and acceleratingly expanding solution and also give hints on how to calculate the polarization using a rotating extension of the Buda--Lund parameterization.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic formulas for the polarization of spin-1/2 baryons produced at freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions, using exact solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics under the assumption of local thermal equilibrium and the Maxwell-Boltzmann (f << 1) limit; the results are restricted to simple, idealized flow geometries and are not directly applicable to realistic collision scenarios.

## Method

The authors start from the Becattini (2013) formula for the spin vector of locally thermally equilibrated fermions, then evaluate it analytically for two exact solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics: a spherically symmetric Hubble-type flow and a rotating, acceleratingly expanding solution. They apply the saddle-point integration method to approximate the space-time averaged polarization, and also present preliminary formulas for a rotating generalization of the Buda-Lund parameterization.

## Key numbers

- For the rotating and accelerating solution, the polarization vector in the particle rest frame is **S_r.f. = (1/(8 T_0)) Omega**, independent of momentum, where T_0 is the freeze-out temperature and Omega is the angular velocity three-vector.
- The helicity of produced particles in this model is **H = (E/(8 m T_0)) (hat{p} dot Omega)**, where m is the baryon mass and E is the particle energy.
- Illustrative calculations use **m = m_Lambda = 1115 MeV/c^2** and **|Omega| = 0.1 c/fm** for the rotating solution.
- For the rotating Buda-Lund model, calculations use **omega = 0.1 c/fm**, with source sizes **X = 6 fm, Y = 8 fm, Z = 10 fm** and expansion velocities **dot{X} = 0.3, dot{Y} = 0.4, dot{Z} = 0.2**.

## Context

This work extends numerical model studies of polarization by Csernai (2014), Karpenko (2016), and Xie (2016, 2017) by providing the first analytic insight into the connection between polarization measurements and dynamical properties of the quark-gluon plasma such as vorticity. The results enable a more direct understanding of how flow properties map to observable polarization, and the Buda-Lund extension (based on the model of Csorgo (1995) and Csanad (2003)) offers a path toward realistic descriptions that include temperature gradients and acceleration beyond simple rotation.
