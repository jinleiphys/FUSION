# A full quantal theory of one-neutron halo breakup reactions

> We present a theory of one-neutron halo breakup reactions within the framework of post-form distorted wave Born approximation wherein pure Coulomb, pure nuclear and their interference terms are treated consistently in a single setup. This formalism is used to study the breakup of one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be on several targets of different masses. We investigate the role played by the pure Coulomb, pure nuclear and the Coulomb-nuclear interference terms by calculating several reaction observables. The Coulomb-nuclear interference terms are found to be important for more exclusive observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully quantum mechanical theory of one-neutron halo breakup reactions within the post-form distorted wave Born approximation (PFDWBA) that treats pure Coulomb, pure nuclear, and Coulomb-nuclear interference terms consistently in a single framework, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the fragment-fragment interaction is treated only in first order while the projectile-target interactions are treated to all orders.

## Method

The authors developed a PFDWBA formalism where the six-dimensional breakup amplitude is factorized into a structure integral and a dynamics integral using a local momentum approximation (LMA) to treat finite-range effects. The distorted waves for the projectile, core fragment, and valence neutron are expanded in partial waves, with the radial integrals evaluated using complex plane methods. For one-neutron halo nuclei, the infinite partial wave summations are handled by splitting the amplitude into a numerically evaluated part and an analytic pure Coulomb remainder expressed via the bremsstrahlung integral.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of 11Be: one-neutron separation energy of 504 keV and spectroscopic factor of 0.74
- Valence neutron rms radius: 6.7 fm; 11Be rms radius: 2.91 fm (with 10Be core size of 2.28 fm)
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution on U target: 48 MeV/c; on Ta target: 49 MeV/c
- FWHM of parallel momentum distribution on 44Ti target: 60 MeV/c; on 9Be target: 56 MeV/c
- Total one-neutron removal cross section on 197Au at 41 MeV/nucleon: 2.10 b (experiment: 2.5 ± 0.5 b)
- Total one-neutron removal cross section on 44Ti at 41 MeV/nucleon: 0.403 b (experiment: 0.55 ± 0.11 b)
- Total one-neutron removal cross section on 9Be at 41 MeV/nucleon: 0.189 b (experiment: 0.24 ± 0.05 b)
- Pure Coulomb contribution on Au: 1.88 b (approximately 90% of total)
- Pure nuclear contribution on Be: 0.181 b (accounts for almost all of total)
- Total one-neutron removal cross section on 9Be with 10 fm lower cut-off in r_i integral: 0.128 b; without cut-off: 0.189 b (approximately 70% from regions larger than 10 fm)
- LMA validity parameter eta_b at 10 fm: 185 fm for Pb target, 914 fm for Be target; eta_c at 10 fm: 108 fm for Pb target, 3892 fm for Be target

## Context

This work extends the post-form DWBA theory originally developed for breakup of light stable nuclei by Baur (1984), which used a zero-range interaction approximation inapplicable to cases with non-zero internal orbital angular momentum. The formalism improves upon prior-form DWBA calculations by Shyam (1999) and time-dependent Schrodinger equation approaches by Typel (2001) and Bonaccorso (2002) by treating Coulomb and nuclear interactions consistently within a single quantum mechanical framework. The theory enables analysis of exclusive observables in halo breakup experiments, particularly for understanding the role of Coulomb-nuclear interference terms in reactions with 11Be on targets of varying mass, and can be extended to describe (a,b,gamma) reactions and higher beam energies using eikonal expansions.
