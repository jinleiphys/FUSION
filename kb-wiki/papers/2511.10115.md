# Generalized Gross-Pitaevskii Equation for 2D Bosons with Attractive Interactions

> We introduce a generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation that provides a nonlinear framework for studying two-dimensional (2D) attractive Bose systems. Its defining feature is the logarithmic density dependence of the coupling constant, which breaks the scale invariance inherent in the standard mean-field equations. This framework allows straightforward calculations of the system properties arising from the quantum anomaly. As a first illustration, we study universal bound states in free space, commonly referred to as quantum droplets. Then, we analyze breathing modes and quench dynamics in trapped systems, paving the way for a systematic exploration of non-equilibrium phenomena in 2D attractive Bose systems. Finally, we predict the existence of universal excited states, including vortex configurations, which may be more accessible to experimental investigation than the ground state. Our results provide a robust theoretical foundation for studying both static and dynamical properties of finite systems, and offer guidance for the design of future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation for two-dimensional attractive Bose systems whose coupling constant depends logarithmically on the local density, breaking the scale invariance of the standard mean-field equations; this framework is claimed to provide a unified description of both static and dynamic properties, but the quantitative accuracy relies on the assumption of large particle numbers (N >> 1) and the specific choice of the parameter alpha = 2.607 that is fitted to reproduce the ground-state energy asymptote from Petrov (2024).

## Method

The authors derive a generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation from an energy functional where the coupling constant g depends on the local density as g = -4 pi / ln(alpha |psi|^2 / B_2), with B_2 the two-body binding energy and alpha = 2.607 fixed by the large-N ground-state energy. They solve this equation numerically in polar coordinates using an ansatz with vorticity s, and benchmark the results against flow-equation calculations for trapped systems and against known few-body results from Bazak (2018). Breathing-mode frequencies are extracted from time evolution after a small quench of the interaction strength.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state fixed point of the generalized GPE: G = -5.84, which determines the universal energy scaling E_N = B_2 exp(4 pi N / |G|) for N >> 1
- Excited-state (vortex, s=1) fixed point: G = -24.15, giving energy ratio E_{N+1}/E_N = 1.7 compared to 8.6 for the ground state
- Breathing frequency in the strong-interaction limit: Omega = 3.8 |E_N| / sqrt(N), independent of trap frequency
- Breathing frequency in the weak-interaction limit: Omega = 2 + N / (ln B_2)^2, deviating from the scale-invariant value of 2
- Critical interaction parameter for crossover: ln B_2 / N = -2.15, corresponding to the transition between weakly and strongly interacting regimes

## Context

This work extends the mean-field description of two-dimensional attractive Bose gases beyond the standard Gross-Pitaevskii equation, which exhibits scale invariance and collapse at a critical interaction strength. The generalized equation incorporates the quantum anomaly arising from the two-body bound state, building on prior studies of universal droplets by Hammer (2004) and Petrov (2024), and on beyond-mean-field approaches by Tononi (2024) and Brauneis (2024). The framework enables systematic exploration of non-equilibrium dynamics and excited states such as vortices, which the authors argue may be more experimentally accessible than the ground state.
