# Matter induced rho-omega mixing

> A novel mechanism for $ρ-ω$ mixing induced by neutron-proton asymmetry in nuclear matter is uncovered and the variation of the mixing angle with the extent of asymmetry is presented.

# Matter induced rho-omega mixing

## Key claim

The paper claims that isospin asymmetry in nuclear matter (unequal numbers of neutrons and protons) induces mixing between the rho and omega mesons, even though the interaction Lagrangian itself is isospin-conserving. This mixing arises purely from the medium, not from electromagnetic effects or quark mass differences, and the mixing angle increases nearly linearly with the asymmetry parameter.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop polarization tensor for rho and omega mesons propagating in asymmetric nuclear matter, using the Walecka model to determine the effective nucleon mass in the medium. The nucleon propagator is split into a free (Dirac) part and a density-dependent part that accounts for Pauli blocking via a theta function cutoff at the Fermi momentum. The mixing amplitude comes from the density-dependent part of the polarization, where neutron and proton loops have different Fermi momenta and therefore do not cancel. The shifted meson masses and mixing angle are obtained from the zeros of the dielectric function in the static limit.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density taken as rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Coupling constants: g_rho = 2.71, g_omega = 10.1 (from Vector Meson Dominance fits)
- At complete asymmetry and densities available in neutron star cores or relativistic heavy ion collisions, the mass shift from mixing is nearly 50 MeV for rho^0 and 25 MeV for omega from their unmixed values
- The mixing amplitude at normal nuclear matter density is ten times larger than that estimated for electromagnetic mixing
- The mass splitting of rho-omega in matter is larger by an order of magnitude compared to what is observed in vacuum

## Context

This work extends the study of rho-omega mixing beyond the conventional sources such as electromagnetic mixing (studied within Vector Meson Dominance) and the u-d quark mass difference (investigated by Cohen (1995) and others using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model). The authors introduce a new mechanism where the nuclear medium itself breaks isospin symmetry through neutron-proton asymmetry, which is relevant for neutron star interiors and relativistic heavy ion collisions. The effect could potentially be observed in dilepton or pi+gamma decay channels, though the two-pion channel is obscured by rescattering in nuclear matter.
