# Hydrodynamic simulation of elliptic flow

> We use a hydrodynamic model to study the space-time evolution transverse to the beam direction in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions with nonzero impact parameters. We focus on the influence of early pressure on the development of radial and elliptic flow. We show that at high energies elliptic flow is generated only during the initial stages of the expansion while radial flow continues to grow until freeze-out. Quantitative comparisons with SPS data from semiperipheral Pb+Pb collisions suggest the applicability of hydrodynamical concepts already $\approx$ 1 fm/c after impact.

## Key claim

Elliptic flow in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is generated only during the initial stages of expansion (approximately the first 1 fm/c after impact), while radial flow continues to grow until freeze-out; the applicability of hydrodynamical concepts is suggested already at this early time, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the model only works reliably up to impact parameters of about 8-10 fm and at transverse masses below about 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors used a hydrodynamic model with longitudinal boost invariance (Bjorken scaling flow) and solved the transverse dynamics numerically, using three different equations of state: a hard ideal ultrarelativistic gas (EOS I, p=e/3), a soft hadron resonance gas (EOS H, p≈0.15e), and a mixed equation of state with a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma (EOS Q, with bag pressure B^(1/4)=230 MeV and critical temperature T_cr=164 MeV). Initial transverse energy and baryon density profiles were taken proportional to the wounded nucleon density from the Glauber model, and parameters were fixed by fitting to central Pb+Pb collision data at 158 A GeV, then applied to non-central collisions without extra parameters.

## Key numbers

- For EOS Q, the critical temperature for the phase transition at zero baryon density is T_cr = 164 MeV, with a latent heat of 1.15 GeV/fm^3.
- For EOS Q in Pb+Pb collisions at b=8 fm and 158 A GeV: 1/6 of the final elliptic flow is generated before the pure QGP phase disappears, 1/2 in the mixed phase, and about 1/3 in the hadronic phase.
- The decoupling temperature used for freeze-out is T_dec = 120 MeV.
- The critical energy density for deconfinement is e_cr = 1 GeV/fm^3.
- Hydrodynamic model successfully reproduces data up to impact parameters b = 8-10 fm and transverse masses up to about 2 GeV.
- The most peripheral WA98 data point shown corresponds to b = 13 fm, where hydrodynamics loses applicability.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of non-central collisions at SPS energies by Ollitrault (1992) and others, and provides quantitative comparisons with data from the WA98 and CERES Collaborations (Appelshäuser et al. 1998, Aggarwal et al. 1998, Ceretto et al. 1998). The results enable the use of elliptic flow as a probe of the early collision stage and the equation of state, particularly the softening near a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, as suggested by Sorge (1999). The finding that a large fraction of elliptic flow is generated while energy density exceeds the deconfinement threshold confirms that elliptic flow can serve as a signature for early pressure development.
