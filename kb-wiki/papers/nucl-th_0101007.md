# Meson-exchange $πN$ Models in Three-Dimensional Bethe-Salpeter Formulation

> The pion-nucleon scattering is investigated by using several three-dimensional reduction schemes of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a model Lagrangian involving $π$, $N$, $Δ$, $ρ$, and $σ$ fields. It is found that all of the resulting meson-exchange models can give similar good descriptions of the $πN$ scattering data up to 400 MeV. However they have significant differences in describing the $πNN$ and $πNΔ$ form factors and the $πN$ off-shell t-matrix elements. We point out that these differences can be best distinguished by investigating the near threshold pion production from nucleon-nucleon collisions and pion photoproduction on the nucleon. The consequences of using these models to investigate various pion-nucleus reactions are also discussed.

## Key claim

All four three-dimensional reduction schemes of the Bethe-Salpeter equation can give similarly good descriptions of πN scattering data up to 400 MeV, but they produce significantly different πNN and πNΔ form factors and πN off-shell t-matrix elements, meaning the models are phase-shift equivalent while differing in off-shell dynamics.

## Method

The authors start from a model Lagrangian involving π, N, Δ, ρ, and σ fields and derive four three-dimensional scattering equations from the Bethe-Salpeter equation using the reduction schemes of Blankenbecler-Sugar, Kadyshevsky, Thompson, and Cooper-Jennings. They fit the parameters of each resulting meson-exchange model to empirical πN scattering phase shifts up to 400 MeV, imposing nucleon pole conditions and renormalization constraints in the P11 channel. The models are then compared by examining their predicted πNN and πNΔ form factors and half-off-shell t-matrix elements at relevant kinematic points.

## Key numbers

- All four models describe πN scattering data up to 400 MeV with similar quality.
- The bare πNN coupling constant g^{(0)}_{πNN} is considerably smaller than the physical value g_{πNN} in all models, with a mass shift of about 150 MeV between the bare nucleon mass m^{(0)}_N and the physical mass m_N.
- The bare Δ mass m^{(0)}_Δ is around 1400 MeV, compared to the resonance position m_Δ = 1232 MeV.
- The πNN form factor cutoff Λ_π is ≤ 700 MeV for the Cooper-Jennings, Blankenbecler-Sugar, and Thompson models, and about 860 MeV for the Kadyshevsky model.
- Significant differences in half-off-shell t-matrix elements appear at momenta k ≥ 500 MeV/c, which is close to the momentum of the exchanged pion at the production threshold.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of three-dimensional reduction schemes for NN models by Woloshyn and Jackson (1970s) to the πN system, where no similar comparison had been made. The models build on meson-exchange approaches used by Pearce and Jennings, and by the Juelich group. The results enable future studies of near-threshold pion production from nucleon-nucleon collisions and pion photoproduction on the nucleon, which the authors argue can best distinguish between the reduction schemes. The differences also have consequences for pion-nucleus reactions such as double-charge exchange and pion absorption in the Δ resonance region.
