# The nucleus-nucleus interaction between boosted nuclei

> The nucleus-nucleus interaction potential has been studied within the relativistic mean field theory. The systematics of the relativistic effects have been investigated by analyzing the relation between the potential and the bombarding energy as a function of the impact parameter. It is shown that the potential barriers are noticeably sensitive to the bombarding energy for a given impact parameter. At large bombarding energies the slope at the potential edge decreases with the impact parameter. Comparisons with a non-relativistic treatment shows that relativistic effects cannot be ignored at bombarding energies around and larger than 100 MeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper shows that relativistic effects in the nucleus-nucleus interaction potential cannot be ignored at bombarding energies around and larger than 100 MeV/nucleon, but the study is exploratory because a fully consistent relativistic theory of the nucleus-nucleus potential is still missing in the literature.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean field theory with the PKDD effective interaction to compute the interaction potential between two identical \(^{12}\)C nuclei. The projectile nucleus was boosted to the target rest frame using Lorentz transformations, and the total energy of the two-nucleus system was calculated as a function of separation distance \(R\) and impact parameter \(b\), with the interaction potential defined as the total energy minus the sum of the isolated nuclei energies. Elastic scattering cross sections were then computed using the eikonal approximation, with an imaginary part of the optical potential taken as 0.8 times the real part.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier for \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C at \(b=0\) fm shows a strong dependence on bombarding energy; for \(\gamma=1.0\) the barrier peak occurs near \(R \approx 7.5\) fm.
- The neutron, proton, and total radii of \(^{12}\)C from the PKDD self-consistent calculation are \(r_n = 2.26\) fm, \(r_p = 2.28\) fm, and \(r = 2.27\) fm.
- Elastic cross sections were computed for \(\gamma=1.05\) (approximately 50 MeV/nucleon) and \(\gamma=1.3\) (approximately 300 MeV/nucleon), showing that minima shift to smaller scattering angles when relativistic effects are included.
- The coefficient \(\lambda\) for the imaginary part of the optical potential was set arbitrarily to 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach, previously successful for static nuclear properties (Walecka 1974, Serot 1986, Reinhard 1989, Ring 1996, Bender 2003, Meng 2006), to the problem of nucleus-nucleus scattering at energies where relativistic effects are expected to be of order 10% or more. It builds on earlier studies of relativistic corrections to inelastic processes (Bertulani 2005, Ogilvie 2009, Ogilvie 2010) and on Dirac phenomenology for proton-nucleus scattering (Arnold and Clark 1979). The results enable improved interpretation of elastic scattering data at rare isotope facilities, where folding models that neglect relativistic dynamics are commonly used.
