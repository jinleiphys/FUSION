# Hypernuclei and the hyperon problem in neutron stars

> The likely presence of $Λ$ baryons in dense hadronic matter tends to soften the equation of state to an extend that the observed heaviest neutron stars are difficult to explain. We analyze this "hyperon problem" with a phenomenological approach. First, we review what can be learned about the interaction of $Λ$ particle with dense matter from the observed hypernuclei and extend this phenomenological analysis to asymmetric matter. We add to this the current knowledge on non-strange dense matter, including its uncertainties, to conclude that the interaction between $Λ$s and dense matter has to become repulsive at densities below three times the nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The interaction between Λ baryons and dense matter must become repulsive at densities below three times nuclear saturation density (n₀) in order to reconcile the existence of ~2 M⊙ neutron stars with the presence of hyperons; otherwise the equation of state is too soft to support such massive stars.

## Method

The authors performed a phenomenological analysis of Λ binding energies in hypernuclei using a mass-formula approach with three- and four-parameter fits (including terms for nuclear radius corrections and neutron excess dependence) to extract the Λ energy shift in nuclear matter (EΛ) and its dependence on proton fraction (SΛ). They then constructed a parametrized equation of state for neutron/proton/Λ/electron matter, combining these hypernuclear constraints with empirical nuclear matter parameters (saturation density, incompressibility, symmetry energy) and Monte Carlo sampling over uncertainties, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to determine which parameter combinations yield neutron stars with M > 2 M⊙.

## Key numbers

- Λ binding energy in nuclear matter: ΔE = 28.5 MeV from the three-parameter fit (range 27.5–29.5 MeV from varying fit details)
- Neutron excess parameter d = 4.99 MeV from the four-parameter fit; values outside the range −10 MeV ≲ d ≲ 15 MeV are disfavored by hypernuclei data
- Saturation density n₀ = 0.16 ± 0.01 fm⁻³; nuclear binding energy B = 16 ± 0.1 MeV; incompressibility K = 235 ± 25 MeV; symmetry energy S = 32 ± 2 MeV; symmetry energy slope L = 50 ± 15 MeV
- The density at which Λ–neutron interaction becomes repulsive is constrained to Δ ≲ 3 (in units of n₀) to support M > 2 M⊙ stars
- Λ fraction in viable equations of state is rarely larger than 10%

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Millener et al. (1988) on hypernuclear binding energies and addresses the "hyperon problem" identified in prior studies (Chen 2011, Weissenborn 2012, Dexheimer 2013, Whittenbury 2014, Yamamoto 2014, Drago 2014, Lopes 2014, Lonardoni 2014, van Dalen 2014) where the presence of Λ hyperons softens the equation of state too much to explain the ~2 M⊙ neutron stars observed by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013). The analysis uses the non-strange equation of state from Monte Carlo calculations (Gandolfi 2011, 2013) and chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2009, 2010, 2013), and finds that radius measurements of low-mass neutron stars are not strong probes of Λ–nucleon interactions in this model. The results enable future hypernuclei experiments on neutron-rich systems to further constrain the Λ energy shift in neutron matter.
