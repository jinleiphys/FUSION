# Odd-even staggering in nuclear binding and the liquid-drop model

> The trends with mass number are examined for the odd-even-staggering (OES) in nuclear binding of neutrons and protons through the conventional measures $Δ^{(3)}$. The large differences previously observed between these trends for even and odd values of these measures is found to arise, in part, from the slow variation of binding energies with mass and charge which provides a background contribution. This background is estimated with the liquid-drop model, and accounts for the greater difference found in proton removal relative to neutron removal. The differences which persist after backgrounds are removed can not be treated in the conventional liquid-drop model but require the addition of a new term. Such a term is investigated, and its effect on specific values of the OES is calcutated. The liquid-drop fitting is also applied to a set of separation energies constrained to match the specific set of nuclei used to determine the observed values for the odd $Δ^{(3)}$. The resulting fit for the pairing term is compared to the average value of even and odd measures. The effect on this value of the new liquid-drop term is observed, and the change in background when the new term is included is also used as an alternate method for determining the difference between trends of the even and odd values of the OES.

## Key claim

The large differences between even and odd values of the odd-even staggering measure Δ⁽³⁾ arise in part from the slow variation of binding energies with mass and charge (the "background"), not solely from pairing effects; after removing this background using the liquid-drop model, residual differences remain that require a new term beyond the conventional liquid-drop pairing term.

## Method

The author used the liquid-drop model (Weizsäcker mass formula) to estimate the background contribution to the second differences of binding energies that define Δ⁽³⁾ for neutrons and protons. The background was subtracted from experimental Δ⁽³⁾ values to isolate the true odd-even staggering contributions, and a new term η(A) was introduced into the liquid-drop formula to account for the residual difference between even and odd measures. The liquid-drop model was also fitted directly to constrained sets of neutron and proton separation energies corresponding to the nuclei used to determine the odd Δ⁽³⁾ values.

## Key numbers

- Observed mass trends for Δ⁽³⁾ for neutrons: (0.82 + 24./A) MeV for odd N, (0.94 + 41./A) MeV for even N
- Observed mass trends for Δ⁽³⁾ for protons: (0.75 + 25./A) MeV for odd Z, (1.11 + 59./A) MeV for even Z
- Liquid-drop background β₀ for neutrons: (-0.0066 - 16.43/A) MeV; for protons: (-0.087 - 32.79/A) MeV
- Estimated δ (difference between even and odd OES after background removal) for neutrons: (0.05 - 8./A) MeV; for protons: (0.09 - 16./A) MeV
- RMS deviation for 5-term liquid-drop fit to constrained neutron separation energies: 0.62 MeV; for protons: 0.57 MeV
- Best-fit pairing coefficient (c/A¹/² form) from full 5-term fit to neutron separation energies: 12.4/A¹/² MeV; for protons: 13.9/A¹/² MeV
- With additional Aln(A) term, RMS deviation reduced to 0.58 MeV (neutrons) and 0.54 MeV (protons)
- δ from the Aln(A) term method: (0.08 - 9.5/A) MeV for neutrons; (0.08 - 9.3/A) MeV for protons

## Context

This work extends the analysis of odd-even staggering reported in a prior study (Friedman, 2009, Ref. 1) which found large differences between even and odd Δ⁽³⁾ trends and suggested physical origins in mean-field models. The present paper uses the liquid-drop model to quantify the background contribution that partially explains these differences, and proposes adding a new term η(A) to the liquid-drop formula to account for the residual difference. This enables a more refined understanding of the pairing term in nuclear mass formulas and provides a method to extract the true odd-even staggering from experimental binding energies.
