# Exact hydrodynamic attractor of an ultrarelativistic gas of hard spheres

> We derive the general analytical solution of the viscous hydrodynamic equations for an ultrarelativistic gas of hard spheres undergoing Bjorken expansion, taking into account effects from particle number conservation, and use it to analytically determine its attractor at late times. Differently than all the cases considered before involving rapidly expanding fluids, in this example the gradient expansion converges. We exactly determine the hydrodynamic attractor of this system when its microscopic dynamics is modeled by the Boltzmann equation with a fully nonlinear collision kernel. The exact late time attractor of this system can be reasonably described by hydrodynamics even when the gradients are large.

## Key claim
The gradient expansion for an ultrarelativistic gas of hard spheres undergoing Bjorken expansion converges absolutely in a finite range of Knudsen numbers, contrary to all previously studied rapidly expanding fluids where the series diverges. The hydrodynamic attractor of this system can be reasonably described by hydrodynamics even when gradients are large.

## Method
The authors derived the general analytical solution of Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamic equations for an ultrarelativistic gas of hard spheres with Bjorken expansion, including particle number conservation. They then solved the full nonlinear Boltzmann equation numerically for the same system and extracted the exact attractor solution at late times, comparing it to the analytical Israel-Stewart attractor.

## Key numbers
- The gradient expansion converges absolutely when |Kn| < 3/(2a√b); for the 14-moment approximation values a=4/3 and b=5, this gives |Kn| < 9/(8√5) ≈ 0.5.
- The attractor solutions from Israel-Stewart theory and the full Boltzmann equation never differ by more than 20%, even when Kn >> 1.
- The first nonzero term of the gradient series is the relativistic Navier-Stokes value aKn/3.
- For λ ≠ 0, the series converges absolutely when |Kn| < 12/[a(√(18b²λ²+64b) + 3bλ)].

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hydrodynamic attractors in conformal systems (Heller 2015) and relaxation time approximation kinetic models (Denicol 2016, Heller 2016), where the gradient expansion was found to diverge. It provides the first example of a convergent gradient expansion in a rapidly expanding system, contradicting the expectation that expansion alone causes divergence. The results support the use of Israel-Stewart hydrodynamic theories in heavy-ion collision simulations even at large Knudsen numbers, as seen in the quark-gluon plasma produced in such collisions.
