# Correlations in self-bound systems of identical fermions

> A method for the calculation of translationally invariant wave functions for systems of identical fermions with arbitrary potential of pair interaction is developed. It is based on the well-known result that the essential dynamic part of the Hamiltonian for the system of identical particles is the Reduced Hamiltonian operator describing relative movement of two particles inside the system. The eigenfunctions of this operator take into account all correlations caused by interaction. These eigenfunctions are basic for the construction of the components (i.e. the functions with a lower degree of antisymmetry) of the system wave functions. The main problem of this approach appears to be antisymmetrization of the components. The developed universal algorithm for antisymmetrization gives a possibility to perform this operation in a simple way and keep numerical approximations under control.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method for constructing translationally invariant wave functions for systems of identical fermions with arbitrary pair interactions, based on the claim that the essential dynamic part of the many-particle Hamiltonian reduces to a two-particle Reduced Hamiltonian (RH) operator. The hedge is that while the eigenvalue of the total Hamiltonian can be expressed via the RH operator, practical application is complicated by the noncommutation of the RH and total Hamiltonian operators and the requirement of antisymmetry, requiring a universal antisymmetrization algorithm that keeps numerical approximations under control.

## Method

The method expresses the intrinsic Hamiltonian for N identical fermions as a sum of two-particle operators, from which a Reduced Hamiltonian (RH) operator describing relative motion of two particles is extracted. Eigenfunctions of the RH operator, which incorporate all correlations from the pair interaction, are used as "actor" functions to construct components (functions with lower antisymmetry) of the total wave function. A universal antisymmetrization algorithm is then applied, based on calculating the matrix of the antisymmetrization operator in the component basis and performing its spectral decomposition to obtain coefficients of fractional parentage (CFP), followed by diagonalization of the Hamiltonian matrix in the antisymmetrized basis.

## Key numbers

* The total Hamiltonian eigenvalue is expressed as the binomial coefficient N(N-1)/2 multiplied by the expectation value of a single two-particle operator: \(\mathcal{E} = \binom{N}{2} \langle h_{i,j} \rangle\).
* The dimension-free Reduced Hamiltonian is given by \(H = \binom{N}{2} \left[ -\frac{1}{N} \Delta_{\mathbf{\xi}} + \frac{1}{\hbar\omega} V(\sqrt{2}\mathbf{\xi}, \sigma_{N-1}\sigma_N) \right]\), where \(\hbar\omega\) is an energy parameter and \(b\) a length parameter.
* The Hall-Post lower bound for the energy is \(E_{Hall-Post} = \varepsilon_1\), where \(\varepsilon_1\) is the lowest eigenvalue of the RH operator.
* The energy eigenvalue can be written as \(\mathcal{E} = \sum_{i=1}^n \varepsilon_i \omega_i\) with \(\sum_{i=1}^n \omega_i = 1\) and \(\omega_i \geq 0\), where \(\varepsilon_i\) are RH eigenvalues and \(\omega_i\) are probabilities.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Reduced Hamiltonians introduced by Hall and Post (1967) and further developed in earlier work cited as [5,6] in the text, providing a general framework for translationally invariant wave functions beyond the harmonic oscillator basis used by previous authors (cited as [3]). The method enables systematic calculation of bound states for self-bound fermion systems such as nuclei and hadrons, where translational invariance creates additional correlations beyond those from interactions and permutation symmetry. It contrasts with approaches based on Faddeev equations, Green Function Monte Carlo (GFMC), or harmonic oscillator expansions by being free of phenomenological parameters, effective interactions, or variational parameters.
