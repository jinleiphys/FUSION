# Spectral Change of Hadrons and Chiral Symmetry

> After a brief summary of the QCD phase structure with light quarks, we discuss two recent developments on in-medium hadrons. First topic is the sigma-meson which is a fluctuation of the chiral order parameter \bar{q}q. Although sigma is at best a broad resonance in the vacuum, it may suffer a substantial red-shift and show a characteristic spectral enhancement at the 2 m_{pi} threshold at finite temperature and baryon density. Possible experimental signatures of this phenomenon are also discussed. Another topic is the first principle lattice QCD calculation of the hadronic spectral functions using the maximum entropy method (MEM). The basic idea and a successful example of MEM are presented. Possible applications of MEM to study the in-medium hadrons in lattice QCD simulations are discussed.

## Key claim

The sigma meson, which is a broad resonance in vacuum, may become a soft and narrow collective mode in hot/dense matter due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry, producing a characteristic spectral enhancement at the 2π threshold rather than a narrow resonance peak as earlier suggested.

## Method

The author reviews the QCD phase structure with light quarks, then analyzes the sigma-meson spectral function using the O(4) linear sigma model at finite temperature and the density expansion of the chiral condensate at finite baryon density. For lattice QCD, the maximum entropy method (MEM) is applied to extract hadronic spectral functions from Euclidean correlation functions obtained in quenched lattice simulations on a 20³×24 lattice with β=6.0.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 175 MeV for 3-flavor QCD and ≈ 155 MeV for 2-flavor QCD from lattice simulations
- Normal nuclear matter density n_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³
- The chiral condensate at nuclear matter density is reduced by almost 35% relative to vacuum
- Pion-nucleon sigma term Σ_πN = 45 ± 10 MeV
- Lattice spacing a = 0.0847 fm (a⁻¹ = 2.33 GeV) for the quenched MEM study
- Lattice spatial size L_s a = 1.69 fm with N_conf = 161 configurations per hopping parameter
- 12 data points in the temporal direction (1 ≤ τ_i/a ≤ 12) used for MEM analysis

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral symmetry restoration effects on hadrons, particularly the suggestion by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1985) that the sigma meson softens near the critical temperature. The CHAOS collaboration's observation of enhanced π⁺π⁻ yield near the 2π threshold in nuclear targets motivates the in-medium analysis, which prior calculations using nonlinear chiral Lagrangians with πNΔ many-body dynamics could not reproduce. The MEM approach enables first-principles lattice QCD calculations of spectral functions, previously limited by the ill-posed analytic continuation problem, and opens the way to study in-medium vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ, J/ψ, Υ) and scalar mesons at finite temperature.
