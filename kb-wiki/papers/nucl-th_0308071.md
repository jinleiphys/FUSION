# Electron screening in the liquid-gas mixed phases of nuclear matter

> Screening effects of electrons on inhomogeneous nuclear matter, which includes spherical, slablike, and rodlike nuclei as well as spherical and rodlike nuclear bubbles, are investigated in view of possible application to cold neutron star matter and supernova matter at subnuclear densities. Using a compressible liquid-drop model incorporating uncertainties in the surface tension, we find that the energy change due to the screening effects broadens the density region in which bubbles and nonspherical nuclei appear in the phase diagram delineating the energetically favorable shape of inhomogeneous nuclear matter. This conclusion is considered to be general since it stems from a model-independent feature that the electron screening acts to decrease the density at which spherical nuclei become unstable against fission and to increase the density at which uniform matter becomes unstable against proton clustering.

## Key claim

The electron screening effect broadens the density region in which nonspherical nuclei and nuclear bubbles appear in the phase diagram of inhomogeneous nuclear matter, but this conclusion is based on a linearized Thomas-Fermi approximation and the screening correction is small for typical values of the surface tension.

## Method

The authors extended a compressible liquid-drop model for inhomogeneous nuclear matter (originally developed by Baym, Bethe, and Pethick, 1971) by incorporating the electron density deviation from the constant-density case within the linearized Thomas-Fermi approximation up to second order in the electrostatic potential. They considered five crystalline phases (spherical nuclei, cylindrical nuclei, planar nuclei, cylindrical bubbles, and spherical bubbles) and solved the Poisson equation with screening for each geometry, then minimized the total energy density with respect to nuclear size, proton fraction, neutron drip density, and volume fraction at fixed baryon density for both neutron star matter and supernova matter (with lepton fraction Y_L = 0.3).

## Key numbers

- Thomas-Fermi screening length λ_TF^(e) ≈ 20 fm for neutron star matter at n ≈ 0.5 n_0, x ≈ 0.1
- Thomas-Fermi screening length λ_TF^(e) ≈ 15 fm for supernova matter at n ≈ 0.5 n_0, x ≈ 0.3
- Normal nuclear density n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Typical half-thickness of a nucleus or bubble ≈ 5 fm
- Typical half internuclear spacing ≈ 10–15 fm
- Surface tension parameter C_3 = 3.5 MeV (fixed); C_2 varied over range 0.1–10 (with C_2 = 1.0 matching Hartree-Fock results of Ravenhall, Bennett, and Pethick)
- Proton chemical potential parameter C_1 = 400 MeV fm^2

## Context

This work extends the compressible liquid-drop model of Baym, Bethe, and Pethick (1971) and the earlier pasta-phase studies by Ravenhall et al. (1983) and Hashimoto et al. (1984), which did not include electron screening. The results show that screening broadens the density range for nonspherical and bubble phases, a conclusion the authors argue is general because screening lowers the fission instability threshold for spherical nuclei and raises the proton clustering threshold in uniform matter. The findings are relevant for future quantum molecular dynamics simulations (previously performed by one of the authors) that have not yet incorporated electron screening, and for understanding the structure of neutron star crusts and supernova matter at subnuclear densities.
