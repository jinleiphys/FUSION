# Neutrino mean free paths in spin-polarized neutron Fermi liquids

> Neutrino mean free paths in magnetized neutron matter are calculated using the Hartree-Fock approximation with effective Skyrme and Gogny forces in the framework of the Landau Fermi Liquid Theory. It is shown that describing nuclear interaction with Skyrme forces and for magnetic field strengths $log_{10} B(G) \gtrsim 17$, the neutrino mean free paths stay almost unchanged at intermediate densities but they largely increase at high densities when they are compared to the field-free case results. However the description with Gogny forces differs from the previous and mean free paths stay almonst unchanged or decrease at densities $[1-2]ρ_0$. This different behaviour can be explained due to the combination of common mild variation of the Landau parameters with both types of forces and the values of the nucleon effective mass and induced magnetization of matter under presence of a strong magnetic field as described with the two parametrizations of the nuclear interaction.

## Key claim

For magnetic field strengths above approximately \(10^{17}\) G, the neutrino mean free path in magnetized neutron matter shows qualitatively different behavior depending on the nuclear force parametrization: Skyrme forces predict a large increase (enhanced transparency) at high densities, while Gogny forces predict almost no change or a small decrease at densities between 1 and 2 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The author calculates neutrino mean free paths in spin-polarized pure neutron matter using the Hartree-Fock approximation with effective Skyrme (SLy4, SLy7) and Gogny (D1P, D1S) forces within Landau Fermi Liquid Theory. The spin polarization induced by a strong magnetic field is determined by minimizing the Helmholtz free energy, and the in-medium response functions are computed in the random phase approximation (RPA) using generalized spin-dependent Landau parameters. The differential cross section for neutrino scattering via neutral currents is then integrated to obtain the mean free path.

## Key numbers

- For a magnetic field strength \(B = 5 \times 10^{17}\) G and neutrino energy \(E_{\nu} = 15\) MeV, the relative change ratio \(R_B = (\lambda_{B} - \lambda_{B=0})/\lambda_{B=0}\) shows a dramatic increase at high densities for Skyrme forces, while Gogny forces show almost unchanged or mildly reduced mean free paths at densities \([1-2]\rho_0\).
- At density \(\rho = 3\rho_0\) and \(E_{\nu} = 15\) MeV, the relative change ratio as a function of magnetic field strength reaches approximately \(10\%\) for the SLy7 parametrization, while the Gogny D1P parametrization predicts a relative change \(\lesssim 1\%\).
- The maximum magnetic field strength considered is \(B^* \approx 2 \times 10^4\) in units of the electron critical field \(B^c_e = 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G, corresponding to \(B \approx 8.8 \times 10^{17}\) G, and the maximum absolute field studied is \(B = 10^{18}\) G.
- The neutron magnetic moment is \(\mu_n = -1.9130427(5) \mu_N\) with \(\mu_N = 3.1524512326(45) \times 10^{-18}\) MeV G\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetized nuclear matter (Lattimer 1990, Chakrabarty 1996) and quark matter (quark1, quark2) by providing the first calculation of neutrino mean free paths in spin-polarized neutron matter within Landau Fermi Liquid Theory. The results show that the choice of nuclear interaction (Skyrme vs. Gogny) leads to qualitatively different predictions for neutrino transparency at high densities, which has implications for neutrino transport in proto-neutron star simulations (sim, cooling) that currently lack consistent treatment of magnetic field effects. The work also connects to earlier studies of spin-polarized neutron matter (ang2, ang3) and the equation of state in magnetic fields (prakash, chakra, vida1).
