# Search for the sterile neutrino mixing with the ICAL detector at INO

> The study has been carried out on the prospects of probing the sterile neutrino mixing with the magnetized Iron CALorimeter (ICAL) at the India-based Neutrino Observatory (INO), using atmospheric neutrinos as a source. The so-called 3~$+$~1 scenario is considered for active-sterile neutrino mixing and lead to projected exclusion curves in the sterile neutrino mass and mixing angle plane. The analysis is performed using the neutrino event generator NUANCE, modified for ICAL, and folded with the detector resolutions obtained by the INO collaboration from a full GEANT4 based detector simulation. A comparison has been made between the results obtained from the analysis considering only the energy and zenith angle of the muon and combined with the hadron energy due to the neutrino induced event. A small improvement has been observed with the addition of the hadron information to the muon. In the analysis we consider neutrinos coming from all zenith angles and the Earth matter effects are also included. The inclusion of events from all zenith angles improves the sensitivity to sterile neutrino mixing by about 35$\%$ over the result obtained using only down-going events. The improvement mainly stems from the impact of Earth matter effects on active-sterile mixing. The expected precision of ICAL on the active-sterile mixing is explored and allowed confidence level (C.L.) contours presented. ## Key claim
The ICAL detector at INO can constrain sterile neutrino mixing angles in the 3+1 scenario, with the inclusion of Earth matter effects from all zenith angles improving sensitivity by about 35% over using only down-going events. The projected upper bounds at 90% C.L. depend on marginalization over the sterile mass-squared difference and mixing angles, with the analysis assuming normal mass hierarchy for both active and sterile neutrinos.

## Method
The analysis used the NUANCE neutrino event generator modified for the ICAL detector geometry to simulate atmospheric neutrino events, then folded the events with detector resolution functions and efficiencies obtained from a full GEANT4-based simulation by the INO collaboration. Oscillation probabilities were calculated numerically in the exact 3+1 framework including Earth matter effects using the PREM density profile, and events were binned in muon energy, muon zenith angle, and hadron energy. ## Context
This work extends the study by Raj et al. (2013) on constraining active-sterile mixing with atmospheric neutrinos in magnetized iron calorimeters, using more detailed detector simulations from the INO collaboration rather than fixed detector resolutions and efficiencies. The analysis addresses anomalies from LSND (Aguilar et al., 2001), reactor antineutrino experiments, and gallium solar neutrino experiments (GALLEX and SAGE) that motivate the 3+1 sterile neutrino model. The results enable future comparisons with constraints from cosmological measurements (CMB + lensing + BAO) and other neutrino experiments like MiniBooNE and KARMEN.
