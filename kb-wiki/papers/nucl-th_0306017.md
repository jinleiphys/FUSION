# Dipole Symmetry Near Threshold

> In celebrating Iachello's 60th birthday we underline many seminal contributions for the study of the degrees of freddom relevant for the structure of nuclei and other hadrons. A dipole degree of freedom, well described by the spectrum generating algebra U(4) and the Vibron Model, is a most natural concept in molecular physics. It has been suggested by Iachello with much debate, to be most important for understanding the low lying structure of nuclei and other hadrons. After its first observation in $^{18}O$ it was also shown to be relevant for the structure of heavy nuclei (e.g. $^{218}Ra$). Much like the Ar-benzene molecule, it is shown that molecular configurations are important near threshold as exhibited by states with a large halo and strong electric dipole transitions. The cluster-molecular Sum Rule derived by Alhassid, Gai and Bertsch (AGB) is shown to be a very useful model independent tool for examining such dipole molecular structure near thereshold. Accordingly, the dipole strength observed in the halo nuclei such as $^6He, ^{11}Li, ^{11}Be, ^{17}O$, as well as the N=82 isotones is concentrated around threshold and it exhausts a large fraction (close to 100%) of the AGB sum rule, but a small fraction (a few percent) of the TRK sum rule. This is suggested as an evidence for a new soft dipole Vibron like oscillations in nuclei.

# Dipole Symmetry Near Threshold

## Key claim

The paper suggests that dipole strength observed near threshold in halo nuclei and N=82 isotones, which exhausts a large fraction (close to 100%) of the Alhassid-Gai-Bertsch (AGB) cluster sum rule but only a few percent of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule, provides evidence for a new soft dipole Vibron-like oscillation in nuclei, analogous to molecular configurations such as Ar-benzene.

## Method

The author applies the AGB cluster sum rule, derived by subtracting the individual sum rules of constituents from the total sum rule, to analyze measured electric dipole strength in several nuclei. The ratio of TRK to AGB sum rules is computed for different cluster configurations (alpha, one-neutron, two-neutron) and compared with experimental B(E1) data from Coulomb dissociation or photonuclear reactions on halo nuclei and stable isotopes.

## Key numbers

- In $^{11}$Li, dipole strength at approximately 1.2 MeV exhausts approximately 20% of the molecular sum rule, with total strength integrated up to 5 MeV exhausting approximately 100% of the cluster sum rule and approximately 8% of the TRK sum rule.
- In $^{11}$Li, the measured TRK fraction is $8.0 \pm 2.0\%$, the TRK/AGB ratio is 12 (for 2n), and the AGB fraction is $96 \pm 24\%$.
- In $^{11}$Be, the TRK fraction is 5.0%, the TRK/AGB ratio is 18 (for 1n), and the AGB fraction is 90%.
- In $^{17}$O, the TRK fraction is 4%, the TRK/AGB ratio is 18 (for 1n), and the AGB fraction is 72% for strength below 15 MeV.
- In $^{138}$Ba (N=82 isotone), the TRK fraction is $0.78 \pm 0.15\%$, the TRK/AGB ratio is 200 (for 1n), and the AGB fraction is $156 \pm 30\%$.
- In $^{18}$O, measured B(E1) and B(E2) exhaust 13% and 23%, respectively, of the molecular sum rule, and alpha widths exhaust 20% of the Wigner sum rule.

## Context

This work extends the molecular (cluster) interpretation of dipole excitations first observed in $^{18}$O by Gai (1983) and later in $^{218}$Ra, applying the AGB sum rule derived by Alhassid, Gai, and Bertsch to halo nuclei such as $^6$He, $^{11}$Li, $^{11}$Be, and $^{17}$O, as well as N=82 isotones. The results contradict the expectation that dipole strength near threshold would exhaust a significant fraction of the TRK sum rule (as the Giant Dipole Resonance does), instead supporting the existence of a soft dipole mode involving surface oscillations of the nucleus, analogous to the Vibron model of molecular physics developed by Iachello and Levine. This enables future studies of molecular configurations in nuclear structure using the U(4) spectrum generating algebra.
