# Phase Structure of Compact Star in Modified Quark-Meson Coupling Model

> The K$^-$ condensation and quark deconfinement phase transitions are investigated in the modified quark-meson coupling model. It is shown that K$^-$ condensation is suppressed because of the quark deconfinement when $B^{1/4}<$202.2MeV, where $B$ is the bag constant for unpaired quark matter. With the equation of state (EOS) solved self-consistently, we discuss the properties of compact stars. We find that the EOS of pure hadron matter with condensed K$^-$ phase should be ruled out by the redshift for star EXO0748-676, while EOS containing unpaired quark matter phase with $B^{1/4}$ being about 180MeV could be consistent with this observation and the best measured mass of star PSR 1913+16. We then probe into the change of the phase structures in possible compact stars with deconfinment phase as the central densities increase. But if the recent inferred massive star among Terzan 5 with M$>$1.68M$_{\odot}$ is confirmed, all the present EOSes with condensed phase and deconfined phase would be ruled out and therefore these exotic phases are unlikely to appear within neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for bag constants \(B^{1/4} < 202.2\) MeV, quark deconfinement suppresses K\(^-\) condensation in neutron star matter, and that only the equation of state (EOS) containing unpaired quark matter with \(B^{1/4} \approx 180\) MeV can simultaneously satisfy the mass constraint from PSR 1913+16 and the redshift constraint from EXO0748-676. However, if the massive star in Terzan 5 with \(M > 1.68 M_\odot\) is confirmed, all EOSes with condensed K\(^-\) and deconfined phases would be ruled out.

## Method

The authors use the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model, where baryons and kaons are described by static spherical MIT bags with quarks coupled to meson fields (\(\sigma, \omega, \rho, \sigma^*, \phi\)). The EOS for hadronic phase (HP) with hyperons and K\(^-\) condensation, and for unpaired quark matter (UQM) via the MIT bag model, are solved self-consistently under \(\beta\)-equilibrium and charge neutrality. Phase transitions between HP and UQM are treated via Gibbs construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius and redshift relations for compact stars.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant for nucleon in vacuum is fitted as \(B_0^{1/4} = 188.2385\) MeV.
- K\(^-\) condensation in pure HP begins at a critical density of about \(4.4\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- The mixed phase (HP+UQM) appears at a density of \(1.6\rho_0\) for \(B^{1/4} = 180\) MeV.
- Pure UQM exists when baryon density reaches \(7.8\rho_0\) for \(B^{1/4} = 180\) MeV.
- The critical bag constant below which deconfinement occurs is \(B^{1/4} < 202.2\) MeV.
- Maximum masses of stars lie between \(1.45 M_\odot\) and \(1.52 M_\odot\), all larger than the PSR 1913+16 mass of \(1.44 M_\odot\).
- The EOS of HP+UQM with \(B^{1/4} = 180\) MeV yields a maximum gravitational redshift of 0.3330, marginally consistent with the EXO0748-676 lower limit of 0.3325.
- For the star 1E 1207.4-5209, the EOS with \(B^{1/4} = 180\) MeV gives mass \(M = 0.82\)–\(1.27 M_\odot\) and radius \(R = 11.0\)–\(11.9\) km.
- A pure quark core appears when central density exceeds \(7.3\rho_0\) for \(B^{1/4} = 180\) MeV.
- The inferred mass limit of \(1.68 M_\odot\) at 95% confidence for Terzan 5 would rule out all presented EOSes.

## Context

This work extends the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model, previously used to study kaon production in hot dense matter by Zhang and Gao, to zero-temperature compact stars. It contrasts with earlier QMC studies by Panda, Menezes, and Providência (2004) where the bag constant was fixed at its free-space value, and with the FST model of Glendenning (1992) which found K\(^-\) condensation within the mixed phase at high density. The results enable future investigations of color superconducting quark phases, which might satisfy observational constraints that the present unpaired quark matter EOS cannot.
