# Neutrino-nucleus interactions and the determination of oscillation parameters

> We review the status and prospects of theoretical studies of neutrino-nucleus interactions, and discuss the influence of the treatment of nuclear effects on the determination of oscillation parameters. The models developed to describe the variety of reaction mechanisms contributing to the nuclear cross sections are analysed, with emphasis placed on their capability to reproduce the available electron scattering data.The impact of the uncertainties associated with the description of nuclear dynamics on the the oscillation parameters is illustrated through examples, and possible avenues towards a better understanding of the signals detected by long baseline experiments are outlined.

## Key claim

The treatment of nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus interactions is a major source of systematic uncertainty for oscillation parameter determinations, and no existing nuclear model implemented in Monte Carlo simulations can fully explain the measured inclusive cross sections from the MINERvA Collaboration across beam energies of 2-20 GeV.

## Method

The authors review theoretical models of neutrino-nucleus interactions developed within Nuclear Many-Body Theory (NMBT), analyzing their ability to reproduce electron scattering data as a benchmark. They compare the Impulse Approximation (IA) formalism, which factorizes the nuclear cross section into a convolution of the nucleon spectral function with elementary electron-nucleon cross sections, against the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM) and mean-field approaches. The impact of nuclear model uncertainties on oscillation parameter extraction is illustrated through examples using the spectral function formalism.

## Key numbers

- The MiniBooNE CC QE double differential cross section discrepancy was observed using a beam of average energy ~0.8 GeV in neutrino mode and ~0.7 GeV in antineutrino mode
- The mean momentum transfer for CC QE events averaged over the MiniBooNE flux is ~640 MeV; for MINERvA it is ~880 MeV
- Electron scattering data in the QE region is generally reproduced with an accuracy of a few percent by advanced nuclear models
- The θ13 mixing angle was measured to be ~10 degrees by reactor and accelerator experiments
- At momentum transfer |q| < 500 MeV, accurate NMBT calculations can be performed for few-nucleon systems

## Context

This review extends the analysis of nuclear effects in neutrino interactions beyond the independent particle model, which the RFGM represents, and builds on electron scattering benchmarks established by Benhar (2006) and Ankowski (2013). The work is motivated by the large discrepancy between Monte Carlo simulations and MiniBooNE CC QE data reported by Aguilar-Arevalo (2010), and the MINERvA finding that no nuclear model explains their inclusive cross section data (2014). The paper enables future long-baseline experiments like DUNE to better quantify systematic uncertainties from nuclear effects in oscillation parameter extraction.
