# Low-energy $^7$Li($n,ﾎｳ$)$^8$Li and $^7$Be($p,ﾎｳ$)$^8$B radiative capture reactions within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach

> The electromagnetic dipole transitions in $^7$Be($p,ﾎｳ$)$^8$B and $^7$Li($n,ﾎｳ$)$^8$Li reactions at the keV-energy region were analyzed simultaneously within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock potential model. The Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation is adopted as a microscopic approach to obtain consistently the single-particle bound and scattering states in the calculation of the radial overlap function within the potential model. All non-resonant and resonant electromagnetic dipole transitions are taken into account. The electric dipole transitions are successfully described with the slightest adjustment. The resonant magnetic dipole transitions at $633$ keV and $2184$ keV of $^7$Be($p,ﾎｳ$)$^8$B reaction, and the one at $222$ keV of $^7$Li($n,ﾎｳ$)$^8$Li are also analyzed. The astrophysical $\mathcal{S}_{17}(0)$ factor of $^7$Be($p,ﾎｳ$)$^8$B reaction is found to be $22.3$ eV\,b.

## Key claim

The Skyrme Hartree-Fock potential model can simultaneously describe both the non-resonant electric dipole and resonant magnetic dipole transitions in the $^7$Li($n,\gamma$)$^8$Li and $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B reactions with only slight adjustments to two parameters ($\mathcal{N}_b$ and $\mathcal{N}_s$) and the spectroscopic factor, despite mean-field models being often considered inappropriate for light $A=7,8$ nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme Hartree-Fock formalism with the SLy4 interaction to obtain single-particle bound and scattering wave functions simultaneously, then employed these wave functions in a potential model to calculate the radial overlap integrals for electric dipole ($E1$) and magnetic dipole ($M1$) transitions. The bound-state parameter $\mathcal{N}_b$ was adjusted to reproduce the experimental nucleon separation energy (well-depth method), while the scattering-state parameter $\mathcal{N}_s$ was tuned to match the positions of low-lying resonances. The spectroscopic factor $S_F$ was then fine-tuned to reproduce experimental cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- Astrophysical $\mathcal{S}_{17}(0)$ factor for $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B: $22.3$ eV b (present work, including both $E1$ and $M1$ transitions)
- $Q$ value for $^7$Li($n,\gamma$)$^8$Li: $2033$ keV; for $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B: $136$ keV
- Resonant $M1$ transitions analyzed at $633$ keV and $2184$ keV for $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B, and at $222$ keV for $^7$Li($n,\gamma$)$^8$Li
- Spectroscopic factor $S_F = 0.80$ for the $E1$ transition in $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B; $S_F = 1.00$ for $^7$Li($n,\gamma$)$^8$Li $E1$ transition
- For the $M1$ resonance at $633$ keV in $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B: $S_F = 1.00$, $\mathcal{N}_s = 0.67$; at $2184$ keV: $S_F = 0.10$, $\mathcal{N}_s = 1.27$
- For the $M1$ resonance at $222$ keV in $^7$Li($n,\gamma$)$^8$Li: $S_F = 0.05$ (to reproduce data from Wie89), $\mathcal{N}_s = 0.83$

## Context

This work extends the simultaneous analysis of the $^7$Li($n,\gamma$)$^8$Li and $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B reactions, previously studied using phenomenological potential models (Rob73, Hua10, Xu13, Tur21), folding models (Kit02, Anh21NPA), shell-model calculations (Ben99, Ben00), multi-cluster calculations (Des94, Sco95, Des04NPA, Des04PRC), and phenomenological $R$-matrix approaches (Bak95, Bak06, Pan19). The present approach differs by obtaining both bound and scattering wave functions from a single microscopic Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation, following the method developed in Anh21PRC103 and Anh21PRC104. The results enable more consistent determination of the $\mathcal{S}_{17}(0)$ factor, which is crucial for understanding high-energy solar neutrino production and the nucleosynthesis of low-mass stars.
