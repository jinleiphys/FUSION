# In-Band and Inter-Band B(E2) Values within the Triaxial Projected Shell Model

> The Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) has been successful in providing a microscopic description of the energies of multi-phonon vibrational bands in deformed nuclei. We report here on an extension of the TPSM to allow, for the first time, calculations of B(E2) values connecting gamma- and gamma-gamma-vibrational bands and the ground state band. The method is applied to 166,168Er. It is shown that most of the existing B(E2) data can be reproduced rather well, thus strongly supporting the classification of these states as gamma-vibrational states. However, significant differences between the data and the calculation are seen in those B(E2) values which involve odd-spin states of the gamma-band. Understanding these discrepancies requires accurate experimental measurements and perhaps further improvements of the TPSM.

## Key claim

The Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) can reproduce most measured B(E2) values for transitions between the ground-state band, the gamma-vibrational band, and the double-gamma vibrational band in 166,168Er, but significant discrepancies remain for transitions involving odd-spin states of the gamma-band, where only experimental lower limits exist.

## Method

The authors extended the TPSM to calculate B(E2) values by performing exact three-dimensional angular momentum projection from a triaxially deformed Nilsson plus BCS basis, then diagonalizing a quadrupole-quadrupole plus pairing Hamiltonian. The deformation parameter epsilon was set to the experimental value (0.320 for 168Er, 0.324 for 166Er) to reproduce the ground-state band B(E2) values, while the triaxiality parameter epsilon-prime was adjusted to match the measured K=2 band-head energy. Standard effective charges of 1.5e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons were used.

## Key numbers

- For 168Er: experimental B(E2: 2+_K=0 -> 0+_K=0) = 207 (6) W.u., TPSM gives 228.6 W.u.
- For 168Er: experimental B(E2: 2+_K=2 -> 0+_K=0) = 4.80 (17) W.u., TPSM gives 2.7 W.u.
- For 168Er: experimental B(E2: 3+_K=2 -> 2+_K=2) lower limit >12 W.u., TPSM gives 408 W.u.
- For 168Er: experimental B(E2: 3+_K=2 -> 2+_K=0) lower limit >0.19 W.u., TPSM gives 4.9 W.u.
- For 168Er: experimental B(E2: 8+_K=2 -> 10+_K=0) = 120 (50) W.u., TPSM gives 1.4 W.u.
- For 168Er: experimental B(E2: 4+_K=4 -> 2+_K=2) = 3.4 (19) W.u., TPSM gives 11.9 W.u.
- For 166Er: experimental B(E2: 2+_K=0 -> 0+_K=0) = 214 (10) W.u., TPSM gives 231.6 W.u.
- For 166Er: experimental B(E2: 4+_K=4 -> 2+_K=2) = 7.4 (2.5) W.u., TPSM gives 12.1 W.u.
- For 166Er: experimental B(E2: 5+_K=2 -> 3+_K=2) lower limit >14 W.u., TPSM gives 222.0 W.u.

## Context

This work extends the TPSM framework previously developed by Sheikh and Hara (1999) and Sun et al. (2000), which had successfully described energies of multi-phonon vibrational bands but had not yet been applied to B(E2) calculations. The results support the classification of the K=2 and K=4 bands as one-phonon and two-phonon gamma-vibrational states, respectively, in 166,168Er, which were identified experimentally by Borner et al. (1991) and Garrett et al. (1997). The discrepancies for odd-spin gamma-band states and high-spin transitions point to the need for including two-quasiparticle admixtures in the TPSM basis, and for more accurate lifetime measurements to resolve whether the odd-spin discrepancies are real.
