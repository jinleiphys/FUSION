# Hadronic Molecules and $χ_{cJ}(2P)$ Coupled States

> We investigate hidden-charm exotic mesons based on a coupled-channel framework, where the physical states are described as superpositions of a compact $c\bar c$ core (identified with the $χ_{cJ}(2P)$ states) and hadronic components such as $D^{(*)}\bar D^{(*)}$. We incorporate meson exchange potentials to describe hadron-hadron interactions and determine model parameters to reproduce the observed masses of $X(3872)$ and $Z(3930)$. The model also predicts a $0^{++}$ bound state consistent with the $X(3860)$. The internal structure is found to be predominantly molecular for the $X(3872)$, while the $X(3860)$ and $Z(3930)$ have larger $c\bar{c}$ components. The coupling between $c\bar{c}$ cores and hadronic components plays a crucial role in generating these states. The resulting wave functions provide insight into the internal structure and decay properties of these exotic states.

# Hadronic Molecules and χcJ(2P) Coupled States

## Key claim

The physical X(3872), X(3860), and Z(3930) states are superpositions of a compact cc̅ core (identified with the χcJ(2P) states predicted by the Godfrey-Isgur constituent quark model) and D(*)D̅(*) hadronic molecule components, with the mixing between these sectors being essential for generating the bound states; the model parameters are tuned specifically to reproduce the observed masses of X(3872) and Z(3930), and the 0++ state mass emerges as a prediction rather than a fit.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation where the Hamiltonian includes kinetic terms, one-boson-exchange (OBE) potentials derived from heavy meson chiral Lagrangians (including π, η, K, ρ, ω, K*, and φ exchanges), and a transition potential U that couples the bare χcJ(2P) states to S-wave hadronic molecule channels (D(*)D̅(*) and DsD̅s). The OBE potentials are regularized with a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ = α × 220 MeV + m, and the three free parameters (α, gc̅c̅, and Λq) are determined by fitting the experimental masses of X(3872) (1++) and Z(3930) (2++) for three values of α (0.7, 1.0, 1.3).

## Key numbers

- Bare χcJ(2P) masses from the Godfrey-Isgur model: χc0 at 3916 MeV, χc1 at 3953 MeV, χc2 at 3979 MeV
- Fitted coupling constant gc̅c̅ = 0.0448 (α=0.7), 0.0427 (α=1.0), 0.0409 (α=1.3)
- Fitted cutoff Λq = 2260 MeV (α=0.7), 3089 MeV (α=1.0), 4647 MeV (α=1.3)
- Predicted mass of the 0++ bound state: 3868.62 MeV (α=0.7), 3867.31 MeV (α=1.0), 3866.07 MeV (α=1.3)
- Mixing ratio for the 1++ state (X(3872)) at α=1.0: 82.03% D0D̅*0(3S1) molecular component, 12.99% bare χc1(2P) component
- Mixing ratio for the 0++ state at α=1.0: 94.36% bare χc0(2P) component, 2.53% D*0D̅*0(1S0) molecular component
- Mixing ratio for the 2++ state (Z(3930)) at α=1.0: 92.83% bare χc2(2P) component, 3.67% D*0D̅*0(5S2) molecular component

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel framework previously applied by Takizawa (2012) and Miyake (2025) to provide a unified description of the X(3872) and its spin partners X(3860) and Z(3930), which are all observed near D(*)D̅(*) thresholds. The model builds on the Godfrey-Isgur constituent quark model (Godfrey, 1985; Barnes, 2005) for the bare charmonium masses and incorporates heavy quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry in the meson-exchange interactions. The results enable future studies of decay processes such as χcJ(2P) → J/ψ V (V = γ, ρ, ω) and χcJ(2P) → DD̅, and the authors note that extension to resonant states like X(3915) and X(4010) is left for future work.
