# $Ab~initio$ description of collectivity for $sd$ shell nuclei

> In the present work, we have reported shell model results for open shell nuclei Ne, Mg and Si isotopes with $10 \leq N \leq 20$ in $sd$-shell model space. We have performed calculations in $sd$ shell with two $ab~initio$ approaches: in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) and coupled-cluster (CC) theory. We have also performed calculations with phenomenological USDB interaction and chiral effective field theory based CEFT interaction. The results for rotational spectra and $B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+)$ transitions are reported for even-mass isotopes. The IM-SRG and CC results are in reasonable agreement with the experimental data except at $N$ =20. This demonstrates a validity of $ab~initio$ description of deformation for doubly open-shell nuclei for $sd$ shell. To see the importance of $pf$ orbitals, we have also compared our results with SDPF-MU interaction by taking account of $2p-2h$ and $4p-4h$ configurations in $sd$-$pf$-shell model space.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that *ab initio* IM-SRG and CCEI Hamiltonians can reasonably describe rotational spectra and B(E2) transitions for doubly open-shell Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes in the sd shell, but they fail to reproduce the enhanced collectivity observed experimentally at neutron number N=20, indicating the need for explicit inclusion of pf-shell configurations.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the sd model space for even-mass Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes with neutron numbers 10 ≤ N ≤ 20, using two *ab initio* interactions: in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) and coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI). Results were compared with the phenomenological USDB interaction and the chiral effective field theory based CEFT interaction. To assess the role of pf orbitals, additional calculations were performed in the sd-pf model space using the SDPF-MU interaction with 2p-2h and 4p-4h excitations, with matrix diagonalization carried out using the KSHELL code.

## Key numbers

- For Ne isotopes, IM-SRG results for E(2₁⁺) are closest to experimental data from N=10 to N=18; at N=20, only CCEI shows the same pattern as experiment while all other interactions deviate upward.
- For Mg isotopes, CCEI results for ³²Mg reproduce the rotational spectrum up to 4⁺, but the 6⁺ state is very high in energy compared to experiment.
- For ²⁴Mg, the rotational spectra are well reproduced by *ab initio* interactions up to 6⁺.
- For Si isotopes, the trend of E(2₁⁺) from N=10 to N=18 is well predicted by CEFT and USDB interactions.
- With 4p-4h excitations in the sd-pf space using SDPF-MU, the occupancy of fp-shell orbitals in ³²Mg is around 2.23, compared to about 3.5 in the work of Tsunoda et al. where the gap between d₃/₂ and f₇/₂ is about 2 MeV for Z=12, N=20.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of electromagnetic properties, Gamow-Teller strengths, and spectroscopic strengths for sd-shell nuclei reported by the same group (Saxena et al., 2018; Srivastava et al., 2016). The paper directly tests the predictive power of *ab initio* Hamiltonians derived by Stroberg et al. (2017) using IM-SRG and by Jansen et al. (2014, 2016) using CCEI for describing deformation in doubly-open shell nuclei. The failure at N=20 confirms the importance of pf-shell intruder configurations for the "island of inversion" region, as previously emphasized by Brown (2001) and Utsuno et al. (2012), and the results suggest that reducing the gap between sd and fp shells in the SDPF-MU interaction, as done by Tsunoda et al. (2017), could improve agreement with experiment.
