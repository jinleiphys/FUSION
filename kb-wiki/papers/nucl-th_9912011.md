# An Isobaric Model for Kaon photoproduction

> The kaon photoproduction is analyzed up to $E_γ^{\rm Lab}$=2.0 GeV by using an isobaric model based on effective Lagrangians and by taking a cross symmetry into account. Both {\it pseudovector} and {\it pseudoscalar} couplings for kaon-baryon-baryon (baryon spin=1/2) interactions are considered with form factors. A vector meson($K^*(890)$), an axial vector meson($K_1(1270)$), nucleon resonances($J\le5/2$), and hyperon resonances($J\le3/2$) are treated as participating particles. By determining unknown coupling constants through a systematic fitting of the differential cross section, the total cross section, the single polarization observable, and the radiative kaon capture branching ratio to their experimental data, we find out a simple model which reproduces all the experimental data well.

## Key claim

The authors construct an isobaric model for kaon photoproduction that reproduces experimental data well up to \(E_\gamma^{\rm Lab}=2.0\) GeV, but the model's success depends on a systematic fitting procedure that imposes constraint conditions from approximately 20% broken \(SU(3)\) symmetry and the nonrelativistic quark model for strong and electromagnetic decay widths of resonances, and the choice between pseudovector and pseudoscalar coupling schemes yields different predictions that are compared explicitly.

## Method

The authors use an isobaric model based on effective Lagrangians with Feynman diagrammatic techniques, including Born terms, a \(K^*(890)\) vector meson and a \(K_1(1270)\) axial vector meson in the \(t\)-channel, nucleon resonances with spin \(J \le 5/2\) in the \(s\)-channel, and hyperon resonances with spin \(J \le 3/2\) in the \(u\)-channel. Both pseudovector and pseudoscalar couplings for kaon-baryon-baryon interactions are considered with hadronic form factors introduced in a gauge-invariant manner using Haberzettl's and Ohta's prescriptions. Unknown coupling constants are determined by fitting differential cross section, total cross section, single polarization observables, and the radiative kaon capture branching ratio to experimental data, with constraints from \(SU(3)\) symmetry and the nonrelativistic quark model.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers photon laboratory energies up to \(E_\gamma^{\rm Lab}=2.0\) GeV.
* The threshold energy for kaon photoproduction is \(E_\gamma^{\rm Lab}=910\) MeV.
* The vector meson \(K^*(890)\) and axial vector meson \(K_1(1270)\) are included as exchanged particles.
* The radiative kaon capture branching ratio uses a value for the imaginary part of the \(K^- p\) pseudopotential of \(W_p = 560 \pm 135\) MeV fm\(^3\).
* The model includes nucleon resonances with spin \(J \le 5/2\) and hyperon resonances with spin \(J \le 3/2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier isobaric models for kaon photoproduction by Thom (1966), Renard and Renard (1971), Hsiao and Cotanch (1983), and Adelseck, Bennhold, and Wright (1985), and addresses the need for a more comprehensive treatment that includes both pseudovector and pseudoscalar couplings, higher-spin resonances, and hyperon resonances. The model is constrained by the radiative kaon capture reaction via crossing symmetry, following earlier studies by Burkhardt (1985) and others, and enables improved theoretical predictions for hypernucleus production experiments at facilities such as TJNAF, ELSA, and ESRF.
