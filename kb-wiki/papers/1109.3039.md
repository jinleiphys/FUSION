# Rise of azimuthal anisotropies as a signature of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The azimuthal anisotropies of the collective transverse flow of hadrons are investigated in a large range of heavy-ion collision energy within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) microscopic transport approach which incorporates explicit partonic degrees of freedom in terms of strongly interacting quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) in line with an equation-of-state from lattice QCD as well as dynamical hadronization and hadronic dynamics in the final reaction phase. The experimentally observed increase of the elliptic flow $v_2$ with bombarding energy is successfully described in terms of the PHSD approach in contrast to a variety of other kinetic models based on hadronic interactions. The analysis of higher-order harmonics $v_3$ and $v_4$ shows a similar tendency of growing deviations between partonic and purely hadronic models with increasing bombarding energy. This signals that the excitation functions of azimuthal anisotropies provide a sensitive probe for the underling degrees of freedom excited in heavy-ion collisions.

# Rise of azimuthal anisotropies as a signature of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The smooth increase of elliptic flow $v_2$ with bombarding energy from AGS to RHIC energies is successfully described by the PHSD transport approach incorporating partonic degrees of freedom, whereas purely hadronic models (HSD, UrQMD) and the pQCD-based AMPT model fail to reproduce this rise; the authors argue this signals the increasing importance of partonic degrees of freedom, but note that even at top SPS energy central collisions retain a large hadronic corona fraction.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic transport simulations using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) approach, which incorporates partonic quasiparticles with spectral functions and mean fields fitted to lattice QCD results, a dynamical hadronization scheme, and hadronic dynamics from the HSD model. They calculated the Fourier coefficients $v_2$, $v_3$, and $v_4$ of charged particles at midrapidity for Au+Au collisions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ to 200 GeV, comparing with experimental data from STAR and PHENIX and with results from the purely hadronic HSD model.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature $T_c \approx 160$ MeV and critical energy density $\epsilon_c \approx 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$ from lattice QCD, used to fix DQPM parameters in PHSD
- The elliptic flow $v_2$ from PHSD shows a clear increase with bombarding energy from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 10$ GeV to 200 GeV, matching STAR and PHENIX data, while HSD predicts approximately energy-independent $v_2$ above $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 10$ GeV
- Triangular flow $v_3$ from PHSD has negative values for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 10$ GeV and increases with energy, while HSD gives $v_3 \approx 0$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \gtrsim 20$–30 GeV
- Hexadecupole flow $v_4$ stays almost constant across the energy range from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7$ GeV to 200 GeV, with PHSD giving slightly higher values than HSD
- The partonic fraction of the energy density at mid-pseudorapidity grows substantially with increasing bombarding energy, while the duration of the partonic phase remains roughly the same

## Context

This work extends the study of azimuthal anisotropies from SIS through RHIC energies using a unified transport approach, building on prior PHSD studies (Konchakovski et al., 2011; Cassing, 2006, 2007) and lattice QCD results (Aoki et al., 2010). The authors show that purely hadronic models (HSD, UrQMD) and the pQCD-based AMPT model (Lin et al., 2005) fail to reproduce the rising $v_2$ excitation function, while PHSD succeeds due to repulsive partonic mean fields and lower $\eta/s$ in the partonic phase. The results enable future constraints on partonic dynamics from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, particularly through higher-order harmonics $v_3$ and $v_4$.
