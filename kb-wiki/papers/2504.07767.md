# Radiative $帢$ capture on $^{12}$C in cluster effective field theory: short review

> Study of radiative $帢$ capture on $^{12}$C, $^{12}$C($帢$,$帠$)$^{16}$O, in cluster effective field theory (EFT) is reviewed. A low energy EFT for $^{12}$C($帢$,$帠$)$^{16}$O at the Gamow-peak energy, $E_G=0.3$~MeV, is constructed, and the theory is first applied to the study of elastic $帢$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies. The effective range parameters are fitted to the precise phase shift data of the elastic scattering and the astrophysical $S_{E1}$ factor of the $E1$ transition of $^{12}$C($帢$,$帠$)$^{16}$O at $E_G$ is estimated. For the study of the $E2$ transition of $^{12}$C($帢$,$帠$)$^{16}$O, we discuss a difficulty to determine the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the subthreshold $2_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O from the elastic scattering data, and demonstrate the difficulty with the estimate of the astrophysical $S_{E2}$ factor of $^{12}$C($帢$,$帠$)$^{16}$O at $E_G$. We discuss the uncertainty in the estimate of the $S$ factors at $E_G$ in the present approach.

## Key claim

The cluster effective field theory (EFT) approach can estimate the astrophysical S factors for the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction at the Gamow-peak energy $E_G=0.3$ MeV, but the $S_{E2}$ factor carries a large uncertainty (about 33%) because the effective range parameters for the $l=2$ channel cannot be uniquely determined from elastic scattering phase shift data alone, unlike the $l=1$ channel.

## Method

The author constructs a low-energy cluster EFT treating $\alpha$ and $^{12}$C as point-like scalar fields, with the Gamow-peak energy $E_G=0.3$ MeV as the typical scale and the $p$-$^{15}$N channel threshold difference $\Delta E=4.79$ MeV as the high-energy separation scale. The effective range parameters in the dressed $^{16}$O propagators for partial waves $l=0,1,2,3$ are fitted to precise elastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C scattering phase shift data from Tischhauser et al. (2009). For the $E1$ transition, two additional parameters (the coupling constant $y^{(0)}$ and the contact vertex coefficient $h_R^{(1)}$) are fitted to experimental $S_{E1}$ factor data. For the $E2$ transition, the difficulty in determining the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the subthreshold $2_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O from elastic scattering data is demonstrated by comparing fits with small and large ANC values.

## Key numbers

* The Gamow-peak energy for the reaction is $E_G = 0.3$ MeV, corresponding to a momentum scale $Q = 40$ MeV/c and length scale $\hbar/Q \simeq 5$ fm.
* The high momentum separation scale is $\Lambda_H = 160$ MeV/c, giving an expansion parameter $Q/\Lambda_H = 1/4$.
* For the $E1$ transition, the fitted parameters are $y^{(0)} = 0.260$ MeV$^{-1/2}$ and $h_R^{(1)} = 2.67 \times 10^4$ MeV$^3$ with $\chi^2/N = 1.73$, yielding $S_{E1} = 59 \pm 2$ keV b at $E_G$.
* For the $E2$ transition with a small ANC $|C_b|_2 = 3.24 \times 10^4$ fm$^{-1/2}$, the fitted parameters are $y^{(0)} = 1.99 \pm 0.01 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $h_R^{(2)} = 50.6 \pm 0.4 \times 10^{11}$ MeV$^4$ with $\chi^2/N = 1.55$, giving $S_{E2} = 4.1 \pm 0.2$ keV b at $E_G$.
* For the $E2$ transition with a larger ANC $|C_b|_2 = 10 \times 10^4$ fm$^{-1/2}$, the fitted parameters are $y^{(0)} = 5.8 \pm 0.1 \times 10^{-2}$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $h_R^{(2)} = 45.53^{+0.04}_{-0.03} \times 10^{11}$ MeV$^4$ with $\chi^2/N = 1.18$, giving $S_{E2} = 42^{+14}_{-13}$ keV b at $E_G$.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous cluster EFT studies of the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction (Ando, 2019, 2023, 2024) and provides an alternative to standard $R$-matrix analyses and two-body potential models (reviewed in deBoer et al., 2017). The key advantage of EFT over these methods is that it provides a systematic perturbative expansion with controlled truncation errors, while the $R$-matrix approach requires a radius parameter and summation of resonant poles, and potential models use Woods-Saxon parameterizations. The results enable future work to reduce the systematic uncertainty from the coupling constant $y^{(0)}$, which is related to the poorly known ANC of the ground state of $^{16}$O and takes significantly different values when fitted to $S_{E1}$ versus $S_{E2}$ data.
