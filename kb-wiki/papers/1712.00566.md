# The semileptonic baryonic decay $D_s^+\to p\bar p e^+ ν_e$

> The decay $D_s^+\to p \bar p e^+ν_e$ with a proton-antiproton pair in the final state is unique in the sense that it is the only semileptonic baryonic decay which is physically allowed in the charmed meson sector. Its measurement will test our basic knowledge on semileptonic $D_s^+$ decays and the low-energy $p\bar p$ interactions. Taking into account the major intermediate state contributions from $η, η', f_0(980)$ and $X(1835)$, we find that its branching fraction is at the level of $10^{-9} \sim 10^{-8}$. The location and the nature of $X(1835)$ state are crucial for the precise determination of the branching fraction. We wish to trigger a new round of a careful study with the upcoming more data in BESIII as well as the future super tau-charm factory.

## Key claim

The branching fraction of the semileptonic baryonic decay \(D_s^+\to p\bar p e^+ \nu_e\) is predicted to be in the range \(10^{-9}\) to \(10^{-8}\), but the precise value depends critically on the mass and nature of the \(X(1835)\) meson, which dominates the contribution due to its proximity to the \(p\bar p\) threshold.

## Method

The authors compute the decay amplitude via a meson-exchange model, inserting intermediate states \(\eta\), \(\eta'\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(X(1835)\) that couple to both the \(D_s^+\) (through semileptonic transition form factors) and the \(p\bar p\) pair (through nucleon–antinucleon–meson couplings). The form factors for \(D_s^+\to \eta,\eta'\) are taken from the covariant light-front quark model, those for \(D_s^+\to f_0(980)\) from CLEO data, and the \(X(1835)\) is treated as a mixed \(p\bar p\)–\(s\bar s\) state. The \(p\bar p\) couplings are fixed using the Jülich nucleon–antinucleon model, which reproduces low-energy \(p\bar p\) scattering data. The differential decay rate is integrated over the four-body phase space to obtain the branching fraction.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(D_s^+\to p\bar p e^+\nu_e) = 3.5\times 10^{-9}\) using the \(X(1835)\) mass of 1826.5 MeV (PDG average).
- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(D_s^+\to p\bar p e^+\nu_e) = 1.5\times 10^{-8}\) using the \(X(1835)\) mass of 1859 MeV (BESII fit without FSI treatment).
- The \(X(1835)\) alone contributes \(1.42\times 10^{-8}\) to the branching fraction when \(\eta,\eta',f_0(980)\) effects are turned off.
- The \(\eta\), \(\eta'\), and \(f_0(980)\) couplings to \(p\bar p\) are \(g_\eta = 2.87\), \(g_{\eta'} = 3.72\), and \(g_{f_0} = 8.48\), respectively.
- The \(X(1835)p\bar p\) coupling is taken as \(g_{Xp\bar p} \approx 3.5\) for a pure baryonium, reduced by \(1/\sqrt{2}\) in the mixed-state scenario.
- The \(D_s^+\to f_0(980)\) transition form factor is parameterized as \(F_1^{D_s\to f_0}(q^2) = 0.4/(1 - q^2/M_{\text{pole}}^2)\) with \(M_{\text{pole}} = 1.7^{+4.5}_{-0.7}\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryonic semileptonic decays from the \(B\) meson sector (where Belle measured \(B^-\to p\bar p \ell^-\bar\nu_\ell\) at \(5.8\times 10^{-6}\)) to the charmed meson sector, where \(D_s^+\to p\bar p e^+\nu_e\) is the only physically allowed channel. It builds on the hadronic baryonic decay \(D_s^+\to p\bar n\) observed by CLEO and on the Jülich \(p\bar p\) interaction model. The prediction can be tested at BESIII with its planned \(10^{10}\) \(J/\psi\) events and at a future super tau-charm factory, and it may help clarify the nature of the \(X(1835)\) state, which has been debated as either a \(p\bar p\) bound state or a pure final-state interaction effect (Sibirtsev 2005, Kang 2016).
