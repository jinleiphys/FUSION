# Role of residual interaction in the relativistic description of M1 excitation

> Magnetic dipole (M1) excitation is the leading mode of multi-nucleon excitations induced by the magnetic field, and is a phenomenon of the spin-orbit (SO) splitting and residual interactions involved. In this work, we investigate the effects of the residual interactions on the M1 excitation from a novel perspective, the framework of relativistic nuclear energy-density functional (RNEDF). The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model is utilized to determine the nuclear ground state properties, while the relativistic quasi-particle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) is employed for the description of M1-excitation properties. From the analysis of M1 mode in the Ca isotope chain, role of the isovector-pseudovector (IV-PV) residual interaction is discussed. For open-shell nuclei, the pairing correlation also plays a noticeable role in the M1 mode. The experimental data on M1 mode is expected to provide a suitable reference to improve and optimize the theoretical aspects to describe the residual interactions.

## Key claim

The authors investigate how residual interactions affect M1 excitation properties within a relativistic nuclear energy-density functional (RNEDF) framework, finding that the isovector-pseudovector (IV-PV) residual interaction and pairing correlations both significantly shift M1-excitation energies away from the single-particle spin-orbit (SO) splitting energies, with the IV-PV coupling parameter optimized to reproduce experimental M1 energies in 48Ca and 208Pb yielding a mean-absolute error of about 0.79 MeV.

## Method

The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-PC1 point-coupling interaction determined the nuclear ground state, while the relativistic quasi-particle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) described M1-excitation properties for even-even Ca isotopes from 36Ca to 64Ca. The IV-PV residual interaction term was included in the RQRPA with a coupling constant α_IV-PV = 0.53 fm^2 optimized against experimental M1 energies of 48Ca and 208Pb, and the Gogny-D1S force was employed for the pairing channel in open-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- IV-PV coupling parameter α_IV-PV = 0.53 fm^2, optimized using experimental M1 energies of 48Ca (10.23 MeV) and 208Pb (≈7.3 MeV), yielding theoretical centroid energies of 9.37 MeV for 48Ca and 8.02 MeV for 208Pb with mean-absolute error ≈0.79 MeV
- For 42-48Ca, the energy-weighted M1 sum m_1 values were obtained as 27.6, 49.7, 71.7, and 94.2 MeV·μ_N^2 respectively, consistent with the Kurath sum rule prediction of approximately 12.6·N_M1 MeV·μ_N^2
- In 42Ca (N=22), the difference between the M1-excitation energy and the 1f_7/2-1f_5/2 SO-splitting energy exceeds 2 MeV
- The M1-SO energy difference takes its minimum value at 48Ca, where pairing correlations vanish in the closed shell

## Context

This work extends the study of M1 excitations from non-relativistic frameworks based on Skyrme energy-density functionals (Vesely 2009, Nesterenko 2010, Tselyaev 2019) into the relativistic RNEDF approach, which naturally explains spin-orbit splitting through its Dirac-Lorentz formalism. The analysis of Ca isotopes demonstrates that the IV-PV residual interaction and pairing correlations break the simple coincidence between M1-excitation energies and spin-orbit splitting energies, and the results enable future optimization of RNEDF parameters using experimental M1 data as a reference.
