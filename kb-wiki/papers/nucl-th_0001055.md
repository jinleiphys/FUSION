# Hadrons in Dense Resonance-Matter: A Chiral SU(3) Approach

> A nonlinear chiral SU(3) approach including the spin 3/2 decuplet is developed to describe dense matter. The coupling constants of the baryon resonances to the scalar mesons are determined from the decuplet vacuum masses and SU(3) symmetry relations. Different methods of mass generation show significant differences in the properties of the spin-3/2 particles and in the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

A nonlinear chiral SU(3) model including the spin-3/2 baryon decuplet can describe dense matter, but the equation of state for non-strange nuclear matter cannot be predicted unambiguously because different methods of mass generation for the nucleon and Delta baryon lead to drastically different predictions for the behavior of dense resonance-matter.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed nonlinear chiral SU(3) sigma-omega model by including the spin-3/2 baryon decuplet (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega). The coupling constants of the decuplet baryons to the scalar mesons were determined from the decuplet vacuum masses and SU(3) symmetry relations, using two different approaches: one where the nucleon and Delta couple to both the non-strange sigma condensate and the strange zeta condensate (parameter set C1), and another where they couple only to the sigma condensate with explicit symmetry breaking terms (parameter set C2). The mean-field approximation was applied to solve the nuclear many-body problem, treating the meson fields as classical expectation values and the baryons as quantum mechanical single-particle operators.

## Key numbers

- The strange condensate zeta is reduced by only about 10 percent from its vacuum expectation value at high densities when only nucleons are present.
- The non-strange condensate sigma drops to 30 percent of its vacuum expectation value at 4 times normal nuclear density, and saturates at even higher densities.
- For parameter set C1 with f_K = 122 MeV, the nucleon coupling to the strange condensate is g_Nzeta ≈ 0.49, and the Delta coupling is g_Deltazeta ≈ -2.2.
- For parameter set C1 with f_K = 116 MeV, the nucleon coupling to the strange condensate is g_Nzeta ≈ 1.72, and the Delta coupling is g_Deltazeta ≈ -0.59.
- For pure sigma-coupling (C2), the mass ratio m_Delta/m_N stays constant at approximately 1.31.
- For parameter set C1, the allowed range of the ratio r_v = g_Deltaomega/g_Nomega is between 0.68 and 1.
- For parameter set C2, the allowed range of r_v is between 0.91 and 1.
- The anti-nucleon potentials do not become overcritical at densities below 12 times normal nuclear density in the chiral model with quartic vector self-interactions.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) model developed by the same group (Paper3, 1999) which described nuclear matter ground state properties, vacuum properties, and finite nuclei, by now including the spin-3/2 baryon decuplet. The results contrast with earlier relativistic mean-field models by Serot and Walecka (1986) and Boguta and Bodmer (1983) which treated the Delta coupling constants as freely adjustable parameters, whereas in this chiral model the scalar couplings are fixed by vacuum masses. The paper identifies that further experimental input on Delta production in high-density systems and theoretical investigations of resonance production in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions are needed to constrain the equation of state.
