# Elastic energy loss and longitudinal straggling of a hard jet

> The elastic energy loss encountered by jets produced in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus is studied in the collinear limit. In close analogy to the case of (non-radiative) transverse momentum broadening, which is dependent on the medium transport coefficient $\hat{q}$, a class of medium enhanced higher twist operators which contribute to the non-radiative loss of the forward light-cone momentum of the jet ($q^-$) are identified and the leading correction in the limit of asymptotically high $q^-$ is isolated. Based on these operator products, a new transport coefficient $\hat{e}$ is motivated which quantifies the energy loss per unit length encountered by the hard jet. These operator products are then computed, explicitly, in the case of a similar hard jet traversing a deconfined quark-gluon-plasma (QGP) in the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation. This is followed by an evaluation of sub-leading contributions which are suppressed by the light-cone momentum $q^-$, which yields the longitudinal "straggling" i.e., a slight change in light cone momentum due to the Brownian propagation through a medium with a fluctuating color field.

## Key claim
The paper identifies a new transport coefficient $\hat{e}$ that quantifies the non-radiative (elastic) energy loss per unit length of a hard jet, distinct from the transverse transport coefficient $\hat{q}$; this elastic loss is defined only for processes that do not interfere with radiative energy loss, unlike prior higher-twist treatments where elastic and radiative amplitudes were mixed.

## Method
The author extends the higher-twist expansion formalism for transverse momentum broadening (Majumder 2007) to a three-dimensional momentum distribution including longitudinal light-cone momentum $l^-$. The delta function $\delta(l^- - q^- - k^-)$ is expanded in a Taylor series in $k^-$, and the coefficient of the first derivative is identified as the elastic energy loss. The resulting operator product is expressed in terms of gauge fields and then evaluated explicitly for a deconfined quark-gluon plasma using the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation for the gluon spectral density.

## Key numbers
- At $T = 300$ MeV with $\alpha_s = 0.3$ and Debye mass $m_D \simeq 4\pi \alpha_s T$, the elastic energy loss coefficient is $\hat{e} \sim 0.06$ GeV/fm.
- The difference between using the HTL spectral density extended to large momentum transfers versus a separate treatment of hard momentum transfers is small ($\sim 20\%$) at large jet energy $E \sim 40-50$ GeV.
- The upper limit of the momentum integral is extended to $Q_{MAX} \sim \sqrt{ET}$ for the energy-dependent estimate.

## Context
This work extends the higher-twist formalism of jet modification (Majumder 2007, Guo and Wang 1998) to include elastic energy loss, complementing prior treatments of radiative energy loss and transverse broadening that depended only on $\hat{q}$. It differs from earlier elastic loss calculations by Wang (2006) by isolating processes that do not interfere with radiative amplitudes, and it reproduces results consistent with thermal plasma calculations by Thomas (1991), Djordjevic (2006), and Qin (2007). The formalism enables a unified treatment of jet modification in both cold nuclear matter (DIS at CEBAF and EIC) and hot deconfined matter (RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions).
