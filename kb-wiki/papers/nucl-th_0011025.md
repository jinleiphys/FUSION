# Nuclear matter properties in the relativistic mean field model with sigma-omega coupling

> The possibility of extending the linear sigma-omega model by introducing a sigma-omega coupling phenomenologically is explored. It is shown that, in contrast to the usual Walecka model, not only the effective nucleon mass M* but also the effective sigma meson mass m*_sigma and the effective omega meson mass m*_omega are nucleon density dependent. When the model parameters are fitted to the nuclear saturation point (the nuclear radius constant r_0=1.14fm and volume energy a_1=16.0MeV) as well as to the effective nucleon mass M*=0.85M, the model yields m*_sigma=1.09m_sigma and m*_omega=0.90m_omega at the saturation point, and the nuclear incompressibility K_0=501MeV. The lowest value of K_0 given by this model by adjusting the model parameters is around 227MeV.

# Nuclear matter properties in the relativistic mean field model with sigma-omega coupling

## Key claim

Introducing a phenomenological sigma-omega coupling into the linear sigma-omega model makes the effective sigma and omega meson masses density-dependent, but the model cannot simultaneously produce a realistic nuclear incompressibility and effective nucleon mass; the lowest achievable incompressibility is around 227 MeV, which is still not low enough, and fitting to a moderate incompressibility of 250 MeV yields an unrealistically low effective omega meson mass of 0.328 times its vacuum value.

## Method

The authors extend the linear sigma-omega (Walecka) model by adding a sigma-omega coupling term to the Lagrangian, parameterized by a coupling constant g_omega' and a choice of real (eta=1) or imaginary (eta=i) coupling. They solve the mean-field equations for static nuclear matter, obtaining self-consistent expressions for the effective nucleon mass M*, effective sigma meson mass m_sigma*, and effective omega meson mass m_omega* as functions of baryon density. The model parameters are fitted to the nuclear saturation point (r_0=1.14 fm, a_1=16.0 MeV) plus either the effective nucleon mass or the incompressibility as a third constraint, and the resulting nuclear matter properties are computed.

## Key numbers

- At the Walecka model limit (alpha=0.4908): K_0=553 MeV, J=20.2 MeV, L=70.6 MeV, K_s=88 MeV, effective nucleon mass xi=0.5437
- With M*=0.85M as input: K_0=501 MeV, m_sigma*=1.09 m_sigma, m_omega*=0.90 m_omega, J=13.8 MeV, L=30.1 MeV, K_s=-38 MeV at saturation
- With K_0=250 MeV as input: M*=0.938M, m_sigma*=1.295 m_sigma, m_omega*=0.328 m_omega, K_s=-31.2 MeV at saturation
- Lowest achievable incompressibility: K_0=227 MeV (at alpha=0.1037, below which m_omega* becomes imaginary)
- Standard nuclear matter inputs: r_0=1.14 fm, rho_0=0.161 fm^{-3}, a_1=16.0 MeV, M=938.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma-omega (Walecka) model, which yields K_0 around 550 MeV and M* around 0.54M, values considered unrealistic. Previous attempts to remedy this included nonlinear sigma self-interactions (Serot 1997, Reinhard 1989) and the Zimanyi-Moszkowski model (Zimanyi), which introduces density-dependent N-sigma coupling and contains an implicit sigma-omega coupling. The present study isolates the direct effect of sigma-omega coupling alone, finding it insufficient to produce simultaneously acceptable incompressibility and effective masses. The authors suggest that additional physics beyond this coupling is needed, such as inclusion of the rho meson field (which they show does not affect saturation properties but can adjust symmetry energy).
